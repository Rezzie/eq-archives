Well, a few things.

First, when I'm using ShowEQ, with my ranger or not, I always try to weave a bit, or sort of 'wander' over to where the spawn is and once I can see it visually, I usually click on it, con it, and then beeline for it, even staring at my showeq screen.

Second, if something spawns across the zone or in a place that I normally don't go, I won't go over there, or will go there in a round about way.

Third, as you've noticed from showeq, the only thing that gets updated from the servers are spawns and things that happen within a certain proximity of your location. So, stuff that's on the other side of the zone wouldn't show anyways. (I always laugh when I ask if 'x' is up on tracking when I'm with a tracking class, and they say no when I see two or three 'x's on showeq. 'why don't we mosey over on this way?' )

Fourth, tying in with third, the only way you'd see someone zooming to a spawn on showeq is if it is in close proximity to you as they won't get updated if you're far away. Of course, if they're watching real time at the servers, they know exactly where everything is.

And lastly, my question really wasn't answered. What I really want to know is when you track, do they know you're tracking? Do they know what you're tracking? Does the client send this info back. It doesn't seem that it'd need to, but who knows. I figure that in writing ShowEQ, they'd know exactly what goes back and forth between the client and servers.
