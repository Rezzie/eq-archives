In early béta, they used to separate the two values for ATK and AC (you had ATK: 128/110 instead of the stupid ATK: 228/1000 you get now), but they dropped it "because players were confused by the display" (like, "huh, my current ATK is above my max?" from stoopid people).

Basically, if what I remember is right, your AC score is composed of:

First factor: Hit avoidance (Defense skill + AGI modulated per level)
Second factor: Damage absorbtion (total AC worn modulated per level)

As for your ATK score, it follows a similar method:

First factor: Hit chance (Weapon skill + Offense skill)
Second factor: Hit damage (Offense skill + STR modulated per level)

Typically, the combat system checks your hit chance vs the opponents avoidance to determine if you score a hit, then takes base damage, modulate it by hit damage upward, by damage absorption downward (no one knows what kind of formula are used), put a low cap of 1dmg and a max cap that depends on the hit damage, and voilà.

So, when a spell increase your "AC", it may either increase your first or second factor. No one really knows which, usually
