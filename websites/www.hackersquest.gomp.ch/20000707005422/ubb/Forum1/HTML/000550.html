You should be averaging much better than a level every 4 hours. Hell, you can do that on your own.

Anyway, at level 15....hmm, essentially, what you want to do is this.

Fight stuff that is 3 or 4 levels higher than you. Have the druid damage shield you and/or your pet. What is ideal is having more than 1 mob pounding on you and your shield. Just remember to make sure you get at least one hit in on every mob as the damage shield damage doesn't count as YOUR damage to the mob. In fact, it may even be better to ditch the pet altogether, have him buff you up, and just dark stuff, and let them beat on you.

Go to places that are not highly populated and just goto town man.

5/6 reds should equal a bubble to you if you follow this advice. Any competent druid should be able to keep you alive while 3 mobs, 3 levels above you, pound on you. I know my druid friend, kept me alive easily doing this.

You should average more like a level an hour bro, maybe a level every 90 minutes. That's how long its been taking with him helping me and we are focused on what we are doing. I'm level 24 at the moment.

Slow, slow, slow...

I don't have the ideal chars handy (nice high level tank or wiz), but have been Pleveling a new char with a 30+ Nec.

The fastest way to go is to use a Nec or an Enc on a newbie level +5 non-running mob (one that won't take off at 50% health). Work the mob down to a sliver of health (less than a DD or single hit from the newbie). Use Mez with the Enc or Screaming Terror with the Nec. The mob will stand there with the hate list wiped and the next hit from the newbie will take it out and give them all exp.

SEQ helps alot to get the mob HP to exactly the right place, but it can be done if you know how much HP that mob has ahead of time (off of web lists) and do DD with fixed values to work it down.

Here is how I level my twinks: The beauty to these suggestions is that these mobs usually don't cast (with the exception of shadowed men clerics and necros which the druid should just beat down unless you are a melee), and they don't run away - i get about 3 - 4 on me and just damage shield em to death...

Keep in mind, if you (twink) pull, your druid can med while you do.

I twink myself (two accounts) so this is what I usually do.

Level:

1-5 (your newbie area, if good misty thicket not a bad idea)

6-14 (east karana east of druid ring almost to bandit camp, work from lions to carrion spiders to gorge hounds - YOU PULL)

<variant>
8-16 (unrest -- DON'T DO THIS WHEN LOTS OF PEOPLE = TRAINS = DEATHS, I like EK since druid can bind near my twink bind point, and recover quickly in case of death, whereas, unrest is a hike when people get stupid)

14-20 (east karana, crags and anything else south of highkeep ramp - watch for clops - YOU PULL)

21-30 (shadowed men, various locations -- DRUID PULLS)

Shadowed Men are neat because with a druid they are easy, are in abundance, have a fairly quick respawn, are in groups of 4-8, don't run away, and beat the hell out of you (dmg shields smack them back). You have to take care though when you do them. The druid needs to pull, usually with a snare, then waits for you to connect (melee/spell), then just melee em to death (twink), while druid sits back and meds -- casts one heal, maybe two.

The necro variety (with the book), and the Cleric variety (with the globes) I just nuke with my druid - they cost too much to kill if you aren't a melee in terms of downtime and mana IMO.

I level about as follows
1-5 - 10/15 mins per level
6-10 - 20/30 mins per level
11-15 - 30/40 mins per level
16-20 - 40/60 mins per level
20-30 - 60/100 mins per level

Not bad if you ask me... I think most of my twinks are around lvl 20-30 with max /played of 3 days.

Usually that includes practicing for spells and such.
