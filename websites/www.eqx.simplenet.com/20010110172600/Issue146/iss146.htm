ISSUE # 146

 October 21st - October 27th, 2000

Religion and role-playing games

In the wake of the Mystere banning, I received the following response on something I said in my Letters to the Editor last week. I thought the topic worthy of an article.

In response to this statement:

Role-playing games have often been the target of religious groups seeking to increase fear in their communities. The more scared their communities are about the outside world, the more that community will cling to that religion. It’s sad that some religious groups will create a paper tiger (can you say Tinky Winky?) to scare their community and will ignore the more basic tenets of their religion such as tolerance and the golden rule. It’s easy to make people fear each other. It’s much more difficult, and rewarding, to help them love one another.

    Article by Oghma
