Yvette's Tower - Part 3

He stopped and breathed deeply, and sighed. "It was my fare for the show tomorrow night, something no warrior would need. Bottled Kelpie tears if it means anything to you, ones that would imbue the drinker with the natural ability to breath water far longer than the normal enchantment." He paused, "It was my life time of work." He proceeded to untie Sabrina the Enchantress. "You mean the Kelpies actually gave you the tears? I thought it would have been impossible." Sabrina wondered as she turned to untie the rest of the passengers.

"I don't think I could repeat the process even if my life depended on it," he said. "It was a rather unique opportunity, and the pinnacle of my research." The old man sounded weak, as if his very life had been sucked from him from the event. 		Montgomery spoke up. "Absolutely fascinating," he remarked. "How was it you freed yourself?" he asked.

"No normal bindings could ever hold me, certainly not these," he smiled in the dark. "Will you help me get that box back?" he asked them all.
