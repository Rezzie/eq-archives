ISSUE # 20May 2nd - May 8th

A Bards Last Tale

"We heard it howl, but it seemed distant. It was only a moment later that it leapt from the darkness and landed on Renigald. It tore out his throat before we could draw our weapons."

She was crying and gasping for breath at the same time. I held her tightly. Her blood was welling from four deep parallel cuts in her left arm and a tremendous bite in her left leg. I could not find any way to save her. She was already near death when I heard her cry out and found her in this forsaken alley. It seemed that she only lived now to tell her tale. 		"Wren struck it several times with her daggers, but it only howled again. It was unhamred by her attacks. I started a chant, one that I hoped would drive the creature away, but it ran into me, knocking me over, and then disappeared into the shadows that spawned it. Orm would have run after it, but I begged him not to. He stopped and helped me up. Wren was crying over Rens body. They had been lovers for more than two years, unusual for Ren to become so attached to any woman. Not unusual for a woman to fall for him, though. He was the most handsome man I ever knew. And just when he finally grew up and found his heart, he was killed. Orm was furious, his green eyes flashed. My brother never allowed himself any sorrow. He used anger in its place. That was what made him such a great hero. He never gave up, even when others could not go on. His anger at Rens death was tremendous. So we began searching for the creature that killed him.

I know now that we did not have to seek it, for it was hunting us. It took Wren next. She was hidden in the shadows, as was her way. We were used to not seeing her in tense situations. But she would let us know that she was there every few minutes. After we did not hear from her, we started to call out. She didn’t respond. We started to backtrack. We found her. Her arm had been pulled from its socked and left…"

Tale by Absor.
