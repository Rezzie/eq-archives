ISSUE # 99

November 6th - November 12th


Gnomish Arts


Tick-tock, Tick-tock.
Our lives revolve around the clock,
We sleep, we wake,
We eat, we make,
Machines to help us dig the rock,
Where lies the ore,
We turn to more,
Machines that move and walk and talk,
And guard the town,
When we are down,
The mines dug deep below the chalk,
'Neath granite layers,
Where all our prayers,
Are put towards the search for gold,
To fill the home,
Of every gnome,
With riches of which tales are told.

Tock-tick, Tock-tick.
Our metallurgists know a trick,
With ancient lore,
From days of yore,
To make the hearts of machines tick;
They take their steel,
To make a wheel,
And brass to make their tiny cogs.
They stoke the fire,
A burning pyre,
Of anthracite and heartwood logs.
Pour molten gold,
Into a mould.
Turn flattened brass into a spring.
Then fit the parts,
And by their arts,
To cold machines new life they bring.

    Tale by Elegor Mandreskal of the Soerbaird
