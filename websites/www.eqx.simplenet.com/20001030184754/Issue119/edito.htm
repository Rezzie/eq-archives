ISSUE # 119

 April 8th - April 14th, 2000


Editorial - Oghma

Slight Mythology/History spoilers

Ironically, after writing on events, I was almost a witness to one. Tuesday night saw a repeat of the three dragon event heralding the possible dropping of Veeshan’s Veil. I went 3 hours without seeing a single part of the event, and logged off an hour before its conclusion.

I logged in about 8 or 9 pm EST and began playing Posy, my now level 30 wizard. Chatting with friends and guild members, I heard about some dragons in East Commons. Excited, I drop everything and teleport to West Commons, then zone. Nothing. Shouts arise saying that they are in West Commons - I re-zone. Nothing. Worried for my saftey, though, I levitate, and go invisible so I can get a high perspective on the zone.

Aided by my guildmates, I spent the next 2 hours responding to reports by players teleporting into zones and running, luckily with Spirit of Wolf, to the various locations people reported. I even stooped so low as to use out of game info in that I had a guild member that swore that since the event ended in the Rathe Mountians on one server, it would end there on ours. (It didn’t, it ended in Lavastorm).

We are on the verge (16 days and counting) of a new chapter in the history of Norrath. Each server, I suppose will be visited by 3 dragons, and following text will be broadcast to all in the zone (or is it server-wide?)

“A faint laughter can be heard upon the rising wind, a husky voice, filled with hatred and disgust.
The voice grows from gentle laughter to a roaring shout,
"Children of Scale! You shall not succeed this night. I shall make sure of it! For centuries, you have evaded me!"
"Now, this shall end. Everything that you possess... is but a waste!"
The voice dies down again, but the wind rages on, swaying the few burned trees which line the firey mountains, their trunks creaking beneath the very force of wind.
As silence descends, the voice begins to speak again, it's tone filled with mocking endeaderment.
The voice cries, "Your names, your faces and your very ways shall fade from view. Such a pity it is indeed, that the world shall know my name, and that of your Queen's fall from history and of the Gods and Mortals alike!"
The shadows quickly retreat back to the corners and stones from which they came, their tendrils rising in the air and the sound of a thousand shrieks echo across the Lavastorm Mountains.
As the shadows slither away from view, the voice can be heard, faintly hissing foul words.
"You have evaded me this day, Children of Scale, but it shall not be the last battle which you will face!"
"Rend your viel and harness your power, it shall do nothing for you, as in the end, you all shall perish, dragon and mortals alike, your corpses shall rot at my feet. For this, I can asure you."
"Untill that night comes, I bid thee all a good morrow, and watch your back.." The voice trails off into a sadistic laughter that hangs within the air for what seems like an eternity and then eventually fades away into the wind.

The speaker, I believe, is Mayong Mistmoore (who has atrocious spelling for a bard), who has been trying to stop the Ring of Scale from dropping Veeshan’s Veil, which is what keeps the planes and the world of Norrath separate. Another interpretation is that the Veil is what keeps only Veeshan from visiting Norrath. A third, and other likely interpretation is that the Veil is what keeps Kunark and the Iksar, Veeshan’s chosen race, from being seen by the rest of Norrath.

This sort of sifting through rumors and legends is what I love to see. I am firmly behind Verant’s policy to give conflicting versions of historical/mythological truths. I love the difficulty in trying to balance the different factions: is Mistmoore good or evil? Are the acts of the Ring of Scale designed to bring Veeshan to Norrath? The faction system is great, complex and interconnected like politics.

I am, however, disappointed in how I found them out - from a news page. I ache to discover these things in Norrath. Some of it is my fault, as I have recently been playing LevelQuest. I haven’t actually done a quest in 2 months. I do hang out in the cities, though, and there are very few npc’s telling news, or boards or signs. (Does anyone know if the message board is still up in the Lion’s Mane in Qeynos? Is it used?)

We need more in-game ways of discovering things. One of the ironies of EQ is that the community centers are at camping spots, not in cities. Why go into town nowadays except to bank, or do quests? Experience and treasure, the driving reason behind Level/ItemQuest players, aren’t there. The largest bazaar in Norrath (on my server, at least) is in the East Commons tunnel. We need books, dropped notes, chatty (not spammy) npc’s triggered by the proximity of faction-heavy players, and message boards in the Inns. And mostly, we need these things to matter. Your Norrathian life needs to be changed because of these things.

Did I have fun at the event that I failed to be able to attend? Yes, I did. I was excited and hopeful popping around Norrath sorting out the rumors and trying, desperately, to see one of the mystical dragons. I was frustrated in the end, but the process, the hunt, was very fun. I just hope that these things happen more, and that history will change. The world will change. If it doesn’t matter, it won’t matter.

    Editorial by Oghma
