ISSUE # 127

 June 3rd - June 9th, 2000

The Rescue

Igag woke wearily in a place vaguely familiar, his sword lying in front of him on the ground. As he grabbed his sword and felt the comfort of its grip, he saw the bottles of kalish, mead, ale, brandy and wine line one wall behind a counter. He knew he was in a familiar place… except… a strange red haze was everywhere. The air was putrid as he gasped for a breath. He grabbed a bottle of Brandy and took a swig when he heard strange sounds behind him, he turned suddenly and thrust his sword totally by instinct. It was just in time as his blade went deep into the belly of a goblin wizard. After taking another drink of brandy and cleaning his blade with a mug of ale and a rag from the bar, he remembered where he was and what he was doing there.

A few months ago, Igag and his wife Laldaena had been in the Mountains of Rathe on a hunting expedition with some friends and fellow adventurers, Askaria, Jalladar and Avidon among others. They had been hunting Giants for the honor of the tribunal, but this is another story. His friends had mentioned in passing that they had heard of some Goblins that made their home in some fiery caves in a place called Lavastorm and they had planned to venture there. Igag bid them good fortune and hoped to meet up with them again.

After a brief stint in the Lost Temple of Cazic Thule, Igag and his holy wife Laldaena ventured to Freeport for some R&R and to replenish their supplies as well as knock back several ales in the local pubs. One morning after a hearty breakfast Laldaena and Igag ventured out to the East Commons to sell the rare items they had gotten on their journeys to the collectors that seemed to frequent there, when suddenly a hand grasped at Igag’s arm…

It was Jalladar, a fine barbarian warrior. He was stripped to his loincloth and was without any weapons other than his fists and in obvious pain. While Laldaena called upon the spirits to help the poor lad, Igag demanded answers to what had happened to him.

“Gah.. Gah… Gobbys Igag! Gobbys… Avidon! Askaria!… Gobbys!” he sputtered.

Igag shouted.“WHERE ARE ASKARIA AND AVIDON NOW?!”

“Igag! Let him be! He needs rest!” Laldaena demanded.

“Give the boy some Ale and he will be fine woman!” Igag responded

“Go drink some Ale then and let me tend to him! By the Hammers, you are so stubborn sometimes!” said Laldaena.

Igag grumbled to her about the others, but it was no use and he knew it. She was a holy shaman and a woman, let alone his wife. He knew that arguments with her were futile, especially when someone needed healing. So many times had he argued with her about tending to the wounded Trolls and Ogres that they had seen in the travels to the Lost Temple of Cazic Thule and lost.

After a few days of rest and the healing from the holy, Jalladar was ready to speak. He told Igag and Laldaena of their adventures into the lair of the Goblins and how they had become captured and incapacitated. They had stripped him and the others of all their gear and were preparing them for some sort of sacrifice to something called Nagafen. Jalladar said this is what Askaria had told him, she was a holy also and had learned a bit of the gobbyspeak from hunting the caves of permafrost. Jalladar managed to escape with help from the warrior named as Avidon. Avidon instructed Jalladar to find help while he stayed to try and keep Askaria from harm.

Now Jalladar was far from being ready to venture back to this vile place but he insisted in going along as Igag prepared for the journey. Laldaena pleaded with Igag to gather a party to free their friends, but Igag insisted there was no time. He had dealt with many goblins in the past and knew he would be extremely lucky if his friends were still alive. Laldaena conceded to her husband as she could tell when his stubborn mind was made up, and she too knew that time was of the essence as she also knew the horrible ways of the gobbys. Arming Jalladar with the equipment they could, the three set off to Lavastorm with Jalladar as their guide.

Laldaena called upon the gods for assistance in the journey. Giving them great speed they made it through the Home of the dark elves and to the entrance of the cave in Lavastorm in less than a day. As they prepared to enter the caves Igag gave specific instructions to both Jalladar and Laldaena.

“Once we get to where they are holding Avidon and Askaria they will surely know of our presence. These gobbys tend to run for help instead of standing and fighting. Their strength is in their numbers. We cannot take a stand until we have Avidon and Askaria out and equipped. We will find them first then their equipment and make our retreat for regrouping. We cannot fail to get them out… we can always come back for the equipment or even buy them new. So that said… listen to me, if I say run… you run. I will be right behind you. Now take some Ale, it will clear your mind and give your steel strength.”

Igag felt as if this were his chance to prove himself to the Tribunal. A true warriors heart with a plan of action. He would save his friends and vanquish his enemy with one swing. He was truly sure that it was his destiny. With this in mind the three entered the caves. A thick red haze was everywhere as lava poured from crevices everywhere. Small bridges built over flowing rivers of lava, the heat and stench was almost unbearable. Igag thought to himself about how strong a race had to be able to survive such extremes as the freezing cold of Permafrost and the blazing heat of this forsaken place. He truly had respect for them in their strength. As they followed Jalladar deeper and deeper into the caves, avoiding patrols and killing sentries swiftly and quietly when needed, making as little noise as possible wanting not to be discovered, they came across a holy man named Yenen. Yenen, a High Elf cleric, recognized Jalladar right off. Turned out that Yenen was a part of the party that was captured. He was one of two that was able to avoid capture, the other had disappeared. Yenen had spent the last week cowering around avoiding patrols trying to formulate a safe plan to rescue the three captured adventurers. He was very happy to see that Jalladar was still alive as he had not seen him in days. Yenen had been keeping track of where the prisoners where being held as well as he could. Turned out Jalladar had escaped while Yenen slept. At least the other two were still alive and Yenen knew where they were.

“They are in the Wizard’s chamber on the far side of the bar…” Yenen told the group. “He is preparing them for a sacrifice it seems… they have been covered in the blood of the gobbys we killed. I have heard rumblings about someone or something named Nagafen. I believe they have put their equipment in a room near there.”

“Show me” Igag said to Yenen

The group, now four, moved inwards and downwards as deftly as possible to where the prisoners were being held. They came to the bar and as Igag peered in through a hole in the door. He saw Gobbys drinking and celebrating like he had never seen before. Seemed as though something big was going to happen. A sinking feeling came over Igag as he felt it had something to do with his friends… Yenen motioned them to follow him around the bar too a back room. They had found them!

They quickly undid the restraints on both Askaria and Avidon. They were both badly beaten and were in dire need of help. Laldaena and Yenen did what they could right away but they needed time and someplace safe to get it done correctly. Igag told them to get out as fast as they could… he and Jalladar would find the equipment and be out soon.

Yenen, Laldaena, Avidon and Askaria all rushed quietly and safely to the exit of the caves while Jalladar and Igag searched for the equipment. Room after room they searched, avoiding the bar as all those Gobbys were there. When Igag realized that would be exactly where they would be… like trophies from a hunt… like the mammoth heads and leopard heads that lined his own hometown bar. He would have to get them out of there, but how?

Suddenly the gobbys were yelling and screaming… evidently they had found out that the prisoners had escaped. Igag knew at this point they would be leaving the bar. This was their chance. He and Jalladar would have to stay where they were hidden until the right time when they could dart over to the bar and grab the equipment then get out.

The time was at hand. Igag saw ten, perhaps fifteen, gobbys rush past away from the bar… this was the moment they had been waiting for. He and Jalladar rushed into the bar. They found the equipment just like he had thought… mounted like trophies. They got the gear down and got Jalladar equipped. They gathered Askaria and Avidon’s gear, Jalladar with weapons and armor, Igag with the supplies. Igag grabbed a bottle of brandy and took a swig... when he was struck from behind, his sword and the brandy tumbling to the floor in front of him. Before Igag fell into unconsciousness he yelled for Jalladar to run to the others, protect them, he turned to look at his attacker and saw a young goblin with a club standing over him.

“Ahhh, the wizard and Nagafen will be pleased I got you. Don’t worry, after I tell the wizard about you I will get your friend then the prisoners you helped to escape. They think I am a kid but this will show them I am worthy.”

Igag woke wearily in a place vaguely familiar, his sword lying in front of him on the ground. As he grabbed his sword and felt the comfort of its grip, he saw the bottles of kalish, mead, ale, brandy and wine line one wall behind a counter. He knew he was in a familiar place… except… a strange red haze was everywhere. The air was putrid as he gasped for a breath. He grabbed a bottle of Brandy and took a swig when he heard strange sounds behind him, he turned suddenly and thrust his sword totally by instinct. It was just in time as his blade went deep into the belly of a goblin wizard. After taking another drink of brandy and cleaning his blade with a mug of ale and a rag from the bar, he remembered where he was and what he was doing there. He turned to the exit, hoping Jalladar had made it out. He opened the door to find more gobbys than he could count. He backed into the room until his back hit the bar. The gobbys fanned out, surrounding him, no escape. He would not be a sacrifice to this so-called Nagafen! He would fight until the last drop of blood pumped from his warrior’s heart!

“I AM IGAG! BARBARIAN SON OF HALAS! FEEL MY STEEL AND MEET THY MAKER!” Igag roared.

This first two swipes of his blade were lucky ones, the first decapitating a goblin the next penetrating deep into the chest of another. Two fatal blows. A brief remembrance of his father, Igag thanked him for the training. Igag then stumbled as he felt a blade hit him on his shoulder, taking a nice chunk of his armor away and exposing a bleeding arm. It was far from a fatal blow but enough for Igag to realize death was not far. His only solace would be as many of these foul beasts as he could take with him to the grave.

“YOU MAY KILL ME YOU BASTARDS! BUT I WILL DO MY DAMAGE! FOR THE GLORY OF THE TRIBUNAL YOUR BLOOD WILL STAIN MY BLADE! HANG THAT IN YOUR TROPHY ROOM!”

Slamming a gobby with a forearm to the face, knocking him unconscious, then slamming his sword into his skull was kill number four, and five came on a riposte as a gobby left his sword wide and an open backside which Igag took full advantage of... lunging his blade into the gobby’s kidney area. This was unfortunate at the same time as Igag was left vulnerable and a gobby shaman clubbed him to the head, taking Igag to the ground. They swarmed at this point, surrounding him… pummeling him with their fists and kicks to the face and body. No they did not want him dead… they wanted him for sacrifice. Igag fought off the darkness that was surrounding him, he felt nothing but numbness. His ears were ringing and everything flashed white. He opened his eyes one last time… he saw the goblins we no long pummeling him. He saw a flash of blue from the corner of his eye. His wounds began to heal… he felt much better. IT WAS HIS HOLY WIFE! OH MY! Igag cursed her in his mind… “Damn her! I did not want her to have the same fate as me… now she is here…. The Hammers have lead her astray as I don’t have the power to protect her now…” Igag reached for his sword and got up. Blood pouring over his face. A figure had its back turned to him. Igag took a deep drink of brandy and lunged for the figure, only to have his sword stopped by an axe! It was Avidon! He had almost killed one that he had come to save! Swinging his mighty axe back to the battle, Avidon buried it into the leg of a goblin shaman. Too his right Jalladar was kicking a goblin in the head while his axe, swinging wildly, caught another in the midsection. Askaria and Yenen were casting their mighty healing and protective spells over the warriors and Laldaena was healing Igag. Igag washed the blood from his face temporarily with a splash from the Ale on the bar, when he suddenly fell to his knees dropping his sword once more and grasping for his back. Behind him was the wizard. It was not a fatal blow that Igag had dealt, but blood was pouring from the wizard as he removed the dagger from Igag’s back and began to cast. The wizard cast a spell upon Laldaena knocking her back against the wall and rendering her unconscious. Igag, lying in a mixture of his crimson colored blood, the black vicious goblin blood, ale and brandy, saw his holy wife lying across the room, eyes glazed as if dead. Igag felt a surge of anger and power. Revenge! Revenge! Revenge! is the only thing that runs through his mind. Igag looked up to see the wizard standing over him and grabbed his sword once more. The wizard was fast. Seeing that Igag was up and able, he blocked his first swing of the mighty sword. But the first swing was the setup for Igag, as was the second… the third swing was the crippling blow that Igag had learned from his father many moons ago. A high arc starting at his feet to full extension over his head, Igag’s blade went into the wizards skull and stopped just below his waist. Igag fell to his knees and then on his face as Laldaena awoke. She immediately began healing her husband but she was weak as well.

Avidon, Jalladar, Yenen and Askaria quickly dispensed of the remaining goblins and carried Igag and Laldaena safely to the cave entrance.

The people he had come to save had saved him…

He was supposed to be the chosen one… He was the one to save others… He was the one to bring honor to the Tribunal… Igag had failed in his heart. He had fallen in battle. He should be dead. But the Tribunal did not wish him dead. He may have failed today but there must be something in his future.

    Tale by Igag
