ISSUE # 26June 13th - June 19th

Remember when you were a newbie?

My grades started to suffer so I took a break. I was at least living in reality enough to realize I needed a break. So I cut my playing time to a few times a week. Sometime during my absence, the Gods on the MUD started changing things around. They became very bossy, and annoying, constantly playing tricks on players, or taking control of MOBS and making them do unpredictable things to get players killed. The MUDS creators had been working on a second world on another server for several years and were nearing a beta. They threatened often to replace the original MUD and force everyone to play the new one. Threats of purges became an everyday thing. A lot of people started quitting. Most of my friends had left, and the game had become very boring. I left the game altogether. It was a year later I decided to go back to that same MUD. To my disbelieve they still had not finished the second world, and were still using the original code. Everyone that I knew was gone, and replaced with all new names. I think I played for about two weeks becoming very bored, and left again. I have never played there since. I still find time to play on a few other MUDS. I have written several levels for them in my spare time. I love building. It has become a nice way to relax when I'm stressed out.

My son now plays, and its interesting to watch him go through a lot of the same things I went through. Including the drop in grades, which I had to ground him for, thus taking away his computer (grin). I'm happy to report however, that his grades have since improved.

I'm looking forward to getting that newbie feeling back with the release of EverQuest. Where I can once again be a "Newbie".

Article by Razor.
