Blythe

Blythe rubbed his hands together quickly, trying to stay warm. A small fire darted in front of him, the flames rising and falling with the beat of his heart. He looked into its depths. It reminded him of the fire inside of him, the fire that had caused him to run away to this forgotten land. He could still feel the hands of his father on his chest. Squeezing the life from him. Only a slight whisper from his mother had brought the man’s anger to a close. Blythe had never imagined that the news of his marriage to Hena would bring this.
    Tale by Sfinks

A Simple Druid

I am a simple Druid, a Druid who loves Nature and her life. I cannot see myself living any other life but the one I do now. I have known many a folk in my travels and have come to understand all that I was brought up in the tree city of the Wood Elves was wrong.

I was told to hate and destroy all the Dark Elves I come in contact with. They are evil and must die. Ogres and Trolls also are to be destroyed, for they walk the same path of the Fallen Elves. I was taught that I must destroy each one of them and never allow one of them come near me. For they will kill me as soon as look at me.

    Tale by Ranalla

Journey's End?

The wisps of morning mist rolled slowly over the lake that circled the fine marble walls of Felwithe. It had been many circles since Safka had witnessed the daybreak from the towers of her home. The young cleric sighed gently to herself, she had missed much in her time away. The chance to return had been most welcome, to the tall spires of the elven lords she brought fair tidings of her engagement. The council had blessed the matrimony, be it with slight apprehension as they always opposed the diluting of the high bloodlines. Her betrothed Rhyk was of their forest dwelling brethren. Many of the most arrogant of the high council still held the views that had lead to the parting of their races in the first place.

    Tale by Safka Fairheart

The Tale of Elianna

Elianna leaned heavily against the cold rock wall and watched the door warily. The faint glow from the seams of the wooden planks confirmed that the spell was working. She breathed heavily, exhausted by the skirmish, her hand clasped over the wound on her thigh. It bled through the thin leather armor she wore, but did not burn. No poison - this time. As she watched, the planks that made up the heavy portal shook and bowed slightly between the iron bands that bound it. She would not have much time.

    Tale by Qwibble
