EverQuest E3 Report - Part 2

Class Halls

When you start a new character, you begin with a little food, a little water, class-specific items (such as a bard's lute, wizard spell, and/or a basic weapon), and a tattered note. Reading the note displays the text over your main view, just as a spellbook would. The text on this note directs you to your local class guildhall. Once you find that, you can doubleclick the guild master to begin a training session. The only time I tried this, I had five free trains available. Some skills were free, while others would have required monetary payment as well. You should be able to train the basic skills that apply to your class for free to start. I believe that every level gained will grant you more training sessions with your guild master. 		Summary

This article should exhaust my limited view of EverQuest at E3. Don't forget that this is how I interpreted the explanations and what I saw. If anything is incorrect, feel free to mention it to the editor so a correction can be printed.
