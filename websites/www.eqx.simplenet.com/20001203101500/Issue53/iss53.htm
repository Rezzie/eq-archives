The Fall of Ambi

As I was wandring to and fro, I spied a soaring sight,
A wood elf maid with hair of gold engage-ed in a fight.
A Deathfist Foot, its lips snarled back, did press her hard and fast,
And brave, brave Ambi showed no lack with sword in her hands clasped.
On hillside did the swords ring high-- Foot massive, Ambi small,
But, oh, her smalling frame was spry, and blow by blow did fall.
Ambi elf, your soul is brave! Your spirit made of fire.
You did not run, nor ground you gave as you leapt into death's pyre.

    Poetry by Jithri, Bard and Wanderer



Return of the Ancients

It has been over twenty years since the young Leif was thrown into the flames of mourning by the passing of his master Brendelson. He wanders without a cause hoping that one day he will be able to rid himself of the grief he feels. He has seen much of Norrath, gaining knowledge and experience in his travels. Despite his travels and encounters he has only acquired three true friends. Tieon, a young female elf, adept in the ways of the paladin, Yeoul, a large troll with, surprisingly, a heart of gold, and Frok, a mischievous, rogue halfling, who gets in more trouble than he's worth. None of Leif's friend know his true past and are not brave enough to ask.

    Tale by Micajah

The Prophecy

Overhead, the stars glittered across the night sky like a mural made of a million flickering candles. The artist, identity unknown, had woven intricate patterns into his work, stringing the stars along in various shapes and forms: the bear, the serpent, the wolf, and so on. The ancient elven mystics named them constellations and recorded their movements across the great obsidian stage above. For these ancient ones, the patterns offered much wisdom and foretold events great and small. And while that age had passed centuries ago, much of the old wisdom was passed down through generations and across oceans. There were still a few magus across Norrath who could gaze up and read the signs, though their numbers dwindled every year.

    Tale by Kha'Dorak
