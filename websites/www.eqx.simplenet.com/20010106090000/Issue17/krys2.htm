Krysthanir's Return

He continued to listen, and heard that many generations ago there had been a giant Wyrm named Krysthanir inhabiting the area, causing the human villages nearby a great deal of distress and brought death to those who tried to stop it. This continued for several years until a wandering paladin who left no name and carried an enchanted sword battled the beast and returned alive. Legend has it that Krysthanir was not destroyed, but merely had been imprisoned by the sword's powerful magic. However, its raids on the villages were over, and the people rejoiced, not even caring how it had came to be. The paladin moved on as quietly as he had arrived. Although the legends of the area do not tell what happened, Raylend alone knew the rest of the story. It had been his great grandfather, Yari, who battled the Wyrm that day. The sword he had carried was now sheathed at Raylend's waist, still shining as if it were new. Yari had sacrificed the magic in the sword to generate a powerful enchantment that turned Krysthanir back into its youngest form. The spell had so drained Yari, that he fell unconscious. When he awoke, the Wyrm was no where to be found. Content that the threat was over, he returned to the village. He accepted no thanks for what he had done, and merely went on his way knowing he had helped the helpless. His god was pleased. Recently, Raylend heard rumors that a large snake had been spotted again in the area, and came to investigate. His fear was that this was Krysthanir, as either a young Wyrm or in some modified form caused by the enchantment. With no magic remaining in the sword, he could not afford to wait until it was fully grown. Raylend was a paladin, like his father and his father before him, and knew it was his duty to finish the job his ancestor had begun. He knew he had to destroy the threat for good. The next morning, he set off for the mountains.

Tale by Wynn Orill.
