ISSUE # 51

December 5th - December 11th

Letters to the Editors

Actually what I have to say does not specifically concern EverQuest Express, but concerns EverQuest Websites in general.

If I was in the Beta I personally would stop reading previews and looking at screenshots. I HAVE stopped, in fact, anyway. I learned a few lessons in UO - I was fascinated with it, hungered for information, and spent time at work (where I couldn't play) cruising the net for info. The sense of discovering things for myself, exploring COMPLETELY unknown territory, coming up with clever solutions for things, which, while they may not have been new to the world, were new and satisfying accomplishments for me, were inevitably, permanently reduced by this over time.

EQ Example involving screenshots:

Case 1: What the heck is THAT!?!?! Wow, what should we do?!?! (tense, excited)

Case 2: Oh yeah, that's a Deathfist Pawn, saw it on EQVault, the best way to kill it is by ______. That wasn't so tough, ummm, you wanna go somewhere else? (satisfaction, early stages of boredom)

EQ Example involving locations:

Case 1: Where the heck ARE we? I figured we could get to Freeport this way ... Wow - what is happening around here? This CAN'T be safe for us, should we keep going?!?!

Case 2: No, stupid, EVERYONE knows you get to Freeport by taking the ________ at the _______ - sheesh. Just go to *www.link* it's all mapped. (jaded and bored) Man, newbies are so dumb. I've gotta stop wasting time talking to them.

I bet not one in ten of you would be able to follow the course I am going to. I am done with reviews, done with screenshots, and I'm never going to go anywhere near number tables or so-called "strategy guides". This game is MINE to discover and explore, and I'm not going to let short-sighted curiousity drain the excitement out of a game I'm looking forward to immensely. I plan on playing EQ for a LONG time and I'll do the exploring and problem-solving myself, thank you very much. It's hard to resist the lure of satisfaction at doing the "best" thing, of being one of the ones in the know, of making things easier for yourself, of curiousity - but I think it is more than compensated for by creating your own solutions, tension, novelty, discovery, , and wonder.

- Severian
