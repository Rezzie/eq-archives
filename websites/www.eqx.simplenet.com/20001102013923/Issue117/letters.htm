Dear Absor,

As of today, I have read every volume of Everquest Express - since number one. I have visited every website both guild and other. I have played the game for months solid. I have enjoyed immersing myself in the Everquest Experience. I look forward to the Ruins of Karnak and I try to balance on the highwire between powergaming and roleplaying.

I also have spent time in almost every chat room on each homepage where I have heard few compliments and many gripes. Even I have composed a list of gripes - which got whittled down to suggestions for Aradune; which I didn't post.

I decided to meditate on the subject and here are the conclusions I have reached re: some of the topics discussed in you last issue.

I think it's very easy to gripe and blame Verant, the Gm's, Customer Service and everything else for parts or things in the game that players feel are not right.

Like little children we clamor to get attention; yell, scream, holler, cry and harass because we believe our issues are more important than any other. And then stick all the blame on the creators of the game when we are not happy with the results. Verant at least listens and addresses many of the issue that players raise plus make fair and positive changes. But sometimes they say 'no' - which leaves more than a few people unhappy.

Aradune has it right. You can't make everyone happy all the time! Sometimes burnout will occur - and that's the time to pull back and maybe play something else, or start at the bottom and reinvent your character. Even play a race and character you hate!

I know when I get tired or frustrated, I head to my PlayStation - and when I burn out on my Playstation, I head to my computer.

This game is made for people to interact. And like most things in life, we only have ourselves to blame when things go wrong. It is up to the players to be responsible and react in POSITIVE ways. I think Everquest Express does a fantastic job trying to do just that. Keep up the good work!!

I believe that guilds could play a larger part also - I have met several wonderful people in various guilds that roleplay - enhancing everyone's game on my server. People in guilds should take their gripe(s) to the guild, hold meetings and have discussion on ways to make things better. Then the guild should make suggestions based on member response to the GM and/or Customer Support and follow up with those suggestions.

Lastly, I don't know of any other Network Mud, moo or RPG that slowly tailors itself to the majority of players. If the majority of players are levels 40 to 50, then Verant must address their issues first because the company doesn't want to loose those loyal customers who have invested so much time and energy in the game.

On the other hand, Verant does try to keep it fun, exciting and not too frustrating for the lower levels who are advancing. Put yourself in their shoes. It's a fine line they walk to keep the game balanced and fair for all. Setting the rope on fire doesn't help matters.

EverQuest players can make their adventures in Norrath what they will - be it a haven to escape daily life, to have fun and frolic.... or make it a frustrating hell. It is their choice.

In the end everyone should just remember one thing....it's only a GAME!

Well, that's my two copper for what it's worth.

Happy Hunting -

Katania EmeraldMaiden
Amazon Ranger
