I'm sure a majority of people will turn this ON eventually. Obviously this leaves you open to the game-destroying mindless slaughters and corpse looting by sticky-fingered gnomes. o User Cap

By limiting the number of people allowed per server, Sony is requiring that they foot the bill for more servers every time more people sign up for the game. This is going to limit their cash flow, which may detract from putting the money towards game development. And actually, any hit to profitability is generally difficult to push through the various layers of management, so the user cap may not even happen. It is highly unusual for a company to put quality of gameplay over sheer dollars. We have already seen the definition of the user cap change, and currently there is no limit to the number of players allowed online at any given time. During peak hours, we may very well see the same server lag problems as in every other game. 		o Variety

Whenever you have many different types of equipment, monsters, spells, and skills, along with the promise of continually adding more upon more, you run into the impossible task of maintaining balance. This has been one of the biggest challenges in running an ever-expanding MUD. What typically happens is people realize what armor or weapon is the best, and all start using it. Then, in order to increase variety again, the items are tweaked. Either that or other things become more powerful, resulting in a proliferation of numbers that radically offsets any balance achieved in the past, or anything that is deemed the "best" gets downgraded. This applies to races, classes, skills, and spells, too. Imagine picking your race and class, only to have it downgraded a month later, when you no longer have a choice. Sure, the game may start out without any problems like this, but maintaining a balance while adding new things is another story.
