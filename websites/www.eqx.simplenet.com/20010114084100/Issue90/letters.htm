Pathing bug - While pot vs pot-hole is a somewhat clever play on words, I feel that the similarity between the arguments ends with the word pot. The major flaw I found in your pot argument is this, the US government doesn't make the pot, Verant HAS made the pot-hole. Unfortunately we must enforce a strict policy to prevent players from taking advantage of these environmental quirks."

I don't cheat. I obey the rules as written because I have respect for the product and I want my character to succeed with honor. That doesn't mean that I blindly follow them without questioning their validity. It also doesn't mean that I don't have sympathy for those people who stray into an area that breaks these rules, especially when the rules are created to cover up a design deficiency (and an unapologetic deficiency at that).

EQX,

I've just read Absor's editorial on exploitation which was written as a response to a letter. I think Absor has missed the original author's point. His point is not "I think exploitation is ok", rather its "If Verant wants to stop exploitation then FIX THE BUGS!" The punishment is a side issue. The point is if the problems were patched then there would be no exploiters to punish.

The pathing problem in particular is a sore point with me and has been the subject of much discussion recently between myself and a friend. I know of two locations where casters can stand back and safely unleash damage spells with no fear of being attacked. They can even stop and meditate! Both of these bugs have been reported to Verant. In the case of the Cazic-Thule bug a Guide even came down to lay down the law. That's fine. But these bugs are not new. Yet they remain unpatched. Why? This is particularly frustrating as there would seem to be a simple solution. Implement some variant of the following.

If a target is trying to move toward you but is unable to make any progress in your direction (presumably stuck by the local geometry) then, after some small delay, it simply teleports to your location. This stops people from medding while their red-level targets try in vain to attack. It also fixes all known and unknown pathing bugs everywhere in the game at once.

My friend Highline has recently sent a similar suggestion to Verant and I expect that it will be read and at least considered by someone. Is it a good idea? I don't know. But I do think that Verant should work to get these and other exploits fixed as quickly as possible. Banning exploiters addresses the symptom. Fix the problem and the symptom goes away.

With best regards,

Luborthos Windhammer, son of Shamus son of Marcus
Shaman of the Six Hammers
Quellious Server
