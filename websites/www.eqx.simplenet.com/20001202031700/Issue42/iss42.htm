Birth of Evil - part 2

Rale shivered as something dry and rough slid across his face. He opened his eyes, straining to focus through a shadowy haze. He was lying on his back, at least he thought he was. His blurred vision gave no clue as to his orientation. He squinted and tried to lean forward. Instantly, he fell back, his motions accompanied by a familiar rustling sound. The sound of dry leaves. With a grunt he rolled onto his side and again forced his eyes open to examine his surroundings. He was lying on the floor of the royal antechamber. All around him, the floor was covered in a blanket of broad Wildwood leaves. The pile continued to grow as the leaves fell almost eagerly from the tree. The tree itself seemed beyond old now. It look nearly hollow and brittle to the touch; nothing like the vibrant life that had sprung from it earlier at Galarin's command. He started to reach out to it. He had felt its life with his druidic powers when Galarin had first cast the spell. Now it was cold and lifeless. No, not quite dead but wasting away from the inside. He shuddered and scanned the room.

    Tale by Russell Linton

Fear the Faceless - Part 4

Bulgar reached for the nearest clover sapling and pulled himself up the steep hill. His large legs were growing painfully tired and he considered resting before he approached the top. Bulgar realized that ten years ago this climb would have been much easier, but now his body was beginning to feel each aching step. After a quick glance upward, Bulgar knew that he was almost there and continued with renewed progress. With no other vegetation in sight, he continued at a severe slouch to balance his weight up the arduous hillside. Bulgar's legs alone couldn't carry the burden so he finished the last few feet in a crawling position.

    Tale by JO Cool
