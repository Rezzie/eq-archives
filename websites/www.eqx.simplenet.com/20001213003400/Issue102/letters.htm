Hello.

I am very aware of that, it is why I chose the name :). Early in the beta for EverQuest, the race/class division was not set in stone, and I happened to really like the image of a dwarf bard. I, like you, have played an Oghma in RPG's and Muds for a while (since 1988), and part of the idea of a Dwarf Bard was that Oghma is also a mighty warrior and was well known for his strength and prowess as wrestler. As Dwarves are powerfully strong warriors, I felt it appropriate. Also, as Oghma is the creator of the ogham script, I feel a strong part of the concept is to be a chronicler of the Dwarven race. This task was left to the bards and priests of the Celtic peoples, so when dwarves were not allowed to be bards in the final version of the EQ, I made my Oghma a cleric.

We all have our own image of characters, and they are all equally valid if the concept is consistent. I myself prefer Amergin for an Elven bard (he was of the Milesian wave of invaders to Ireland, who displaced the Tuatha De Danaan of which Oghma was a member), and Oisin for a human bard(he was a son of Finn MacCool, the Irish Cheiftain). But that's just me, I'm a big fan of mythology :).

Thanks for reading EQX, and for your letter! We Oghmas must stick together!

PS. Aklar even wrote a story about a dwarf who wanted to be a bard, and named him Ahgmo :).
