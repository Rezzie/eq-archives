Ziff in Rivervale

Perched comfortably in a tree, Ziff watched the goblins work. They disgusted him. Slouching green humanoids with potbellies, their entire wardrobe consisted of revealing loincloths and gaudy brass jewelry. Even from his hiding place some yards away, he was sure he could smell their vile stench.

Ziff had arrived in Rivervale barely six months ago, exhausted from his travels as a journeyman enchanter. Moving from town to town, he had earned a modest living enchanting farmer tools, blacksmith metals, and, on occasion, weapons. But rigorous travel, though ripe with its own rewards, takes its toll on the body. When he at last stumbled into the comfortable hamlet of Rivervale, he needed a break.

    Tale by Chris


ARE THRIVING ONLINE COMMUNITIES IN DANGER?

This past week a friend send me an icq message with a link to an article on a new lawsuit filed in Colorado against Ultima Online on behalf of its volunteers who supposedly were enslaved in a virtual sweatshop, laboring unpaid on behalf of Electronic Arts’ bottom line. Not long ago, I also followed the links on the Vault to articles about a similar suit against AOL alleging it too had mistreated its volunteers by not paying them in order to pad its already fabulous bottom line.

    Essay by Nepenthia

Star Wars MMORPG, Who’s ready?

Recently I had an event happen in my life. Perhaps not so recently as it was actually shortly following the Vegas EQ trip in July. My hubby’s family had a crisis and I have had to be in another city except for three or four days out of a month. This sojourn has provided me with a step back from EverQuest, and one I didn’t particularly want. When the crisis came I was becoming even more heavily involved in the community than ever before and enjoying the heck out of the interaction it provided with all of you. In fact one of my last logged times on Oxesiess can easily be ranked as one of the highlights of playing her (the “fans” were just too dang cute and great role-players that I met at the bank {waves}).

    Article by Onnie

Editorial - Absor

For a brief time in my life I was responsible for managing people. I worked as an assistant manager for a Crown Books store. Posted in the bathroom, right over the back of the toilet, was a poster that we had been given and told that we had to post where all employees could see it. It’s been ten years or more since I read that poster. But it was printed by the U.S. Government and it, basically, outlined some of the rights of employees in the United States.
