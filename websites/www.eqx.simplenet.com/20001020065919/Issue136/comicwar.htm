ISSUE # 136

 August 12th - August 18th, 2000


Is there a "Comic War"?

I guess this all started with EQVault's unveiling of their new online, "professionally penned" comic EverQuests. Now, whether the commotion was caused by the comic itself, their claim of "the first (and therefore best) adventure comic that takes place in Norrath", or EverCrest's very humorous mockery of the comic I do not know. But it seems to have turned into an all out "war". If the EverQuests comic was a tangible object, it would have been flamed to ashes weeks ago.

The arguments against the new comic go like this, with my comments about them afterwards: (Note: Opinions were gathered from flamers and decent people alike. I apologize to those who are offended by being grouped with the flamers).

-EverQuests is NOT the first adventure comic based on EverQuests. I'm a laid back guy and I'm not gonna squabble about EverQuests being first or not and what type of comic Bard's Log is (it's kinda both).

-The dialogue is cheesy and the plot is horrible. The dialogue is that of a comic book of that type. It's ridiculously clear in order to inform people who have never played EverQuest before. Anybody could pick this comic up and understand what is going on, while you have to be an EQ player to get EverCrest most of the time. As for Bard's Log, there're elements, but the characters grew into their own people and I rarely even think of EverQuest when drawing the comic.

-Dark Elf butts. Sure she's half-naked. So is the High Elf on all the EverQuest boxes though ;). Although it is offending to some, I can't help laughing when I see it, thanks to EverCrest. (Bear with me; while reading the comic, fill in the Dark Elf's speech bubble with "Look at my blue butt" whenever there's a back view. Its funny... ok, I'll stop)

-It's just "following the crowd". Maybe that's true. Maybe EQVault just thought "Oh, EQX has some comics that are very popular, EverCrest is very popular, maybe we can be popular as well. But we can be better and 'professionally penned' " but maybe they didn't, and I doubt they did. I'm sure (throw rocks at me if I'm wrong) Barry Blair offered to do a comic based on EverQuest for EQVault, and EQVault let him.

This is where it gets personal... Two things that Drysart and I have in common are: We do humor comics, we both got rejected by EQVault. Although Drysart on a much larger scale. I sent work to EQVault's "Featured Art" section and their "Race Art section" but they were never posted. I didn't know why because they never gave a reason. I didn't ask, I'm a laid back guy. I took my work to EQX and I love it there. As stated in that fateful EverCrest comic, EQVault didn't post the work because it wasn't "professional looking". I think all work should be posted. If a person has the enthusiasm in a game to spend time and draw a picture of their character (no matter how "bad" it looks. Who are we to judge?) they should be allowed to have it posted. Sure, it's EQVault's webspace and they don't have to post anything, but look where it got them with EverCrest.

Which leads me to think... maybe they scrambled to get something professional after seeing that they lost a now popular comic that they had previously rejected. I'm going to conclude with that because I'm starting to rant. This is just what's going through my head as I think about other daily stuff. Are we cartoonists fighting a war here? Back in the day, newspapers fought to get the best comic strips because comic strips got readers. Maybe history repeats itself. Ooooooooooooooooooooh Scary. Anyway, this is Sir Maximus, signing off. Flame me all you want. I'm a laid back guy ;)
