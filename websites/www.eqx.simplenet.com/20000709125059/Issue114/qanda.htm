Q:  When roleplaying, how do dwarves "speak"? It seems the universal reference in EQ is Longjohn Silver, but somehow I don't think that's correct...

Thromia

A:  "Arreeee me matie 'tis the pirates' life fer me" can be charming but in and of itself the lingo of Longjohn Silver is not really the source of dwarf speaking styles in Everquest. Pillar of Wisdom Tagad is reasonably certain that he saw a posting that the barbarians and dwarves of Norrath are based on models from Scottish and Welsh cultures and that a stylized language fitting to that background would be appropriate. In traditional fantasy settings, barbarians are often heard speaking with a Scots "burr" and dwarves are quite often heard speaking a blend of Scots with a bit of the lilt of Wales and the Yorkshire Hills of England. You might want to take a look at the traditional poetry of Robert Burns and other writers of Scotland or check into fantasy novels where dwarves use a dialect. From what I remember, Tolkien did not use one for his dwarves, however, so it seems to me there is plenty of room for you to develop the distinctive dwarf voice that suits your character.


Q:  I read your Q&A about getting married on EQ... I'm a bit puzzled. Is there anything really "official" in getting married if a cleric friend does the ceremony? Isn't it then just roleplaying out the event, but yet, there's nothing gamewise that from then on says you are married or anything is there? Unless you take the same last name, of which you said you would petition... But is that it? Or is there something here I am missing? If a Cleric friend does perform the ceremony is there anything they need to do to make it official? Thanks for any answers to my questions, much appreciated!

Alanah

A:  It's my understanding that all weddings in Norrath, whether officiated by a GM or by a player character, are roleplay and do not cross over into an "official" status. Last name changes are permitted as an enhancement to roleplay and again can be changed should the union fail and the parties resume single lives. Since it is roleplay, I think the best way to make a wedding "official" is to have a ceremony that suits the race and classes of the players being married, have the names altered, and then make sure that the story of the wedding appears to interested parties. I did check with a guide on this and he reminded me that while guides and GMs can and do officiate at weddings, they may not assist with items or transportation or even the safety of the wedding party, all of which must be arranged for ahead of time by the players. They can, though, add a great deal of roleplay fun for the occasion as I mentioned before.


Q:  Greetings, fine bard. I wish to peruse your vast amount of knowledge on a question that has been perturbing me recently. I am a Necromancer of the 7th amount of ability that has been having some trouble. You see, I am having a very difficult time gaining xp. Just tonight, I hunted for upwards of 3 hours (without a pet, mind you) and still only gained about a quarter bubble of xp. To give more detail, I have been killing white and blue cons, since I have been unable to properly destroy yellows and reds at my current level. Am I doing something horribly wrong? I have spoken to many wise, older Necromancer friends, and none can see why I am having such a hard time. According to them, I am killing the same filth that they did at my level. I currently have 18 hours play time to get to 7th level. My last incarnation (a troll shaman) had made 7th in 6.5 hours. Is there anything to help me? I have finally found a profession I love, but it is very frustrating to be unable to advance. Can you advise me?

Jhenik

A:  You don't mention if you are using a pet while you adventure. Some young necromancers miss out on this, but you are able to gain at pet at level 4, and an even better one at level 8. The pet will only take about 1% of the experienced gained you will be able to level faster. Assuming that you have been using a pet, then the next thing you might want to consider is find someone to tank for you. Killing will go much faster with another person until you are high enough level to get a really good pet. Several other factors go into the speed at which you level. You need to take your race into consideration for sure. If you are a dark elf or an Erudite, you will level more slowly than a human, for instance. There is another reality out there as well. All creatures that con /blue or whatever to you will not necessarily give you the same amount of experience, I believe. Finally, it's important to realize that certain classes and races just do level faster. Melee classes for instance almost always race ahead as they have little to worry about besides hitpoints. It seems like if you want to speed up leveling, you would probably then want to use your pet as much as possible, group with a good tank, find a decent weapon that helps you kill faster, compare experience from npcs that con the same and don't waste time on the ones that don't give decent experience, and be realistic about the experience penalties that some races and classes have. Hope this helps!

Q:  I was wondering how one could find out if a magical item with charges is rechargable and how to do so. I have heard many speak of being able to do this but no one I have seen has explained how it can be done. Thanks.

Tauithar Tausiirath

A:  The only item I know for sure that is rechargeable is the squad ring which has a questlike process for recharging. There may be others in the game but I haven't heard of them yet. Most items that have charges are simply used up over time and must be replaced. If you think the magical item you have might possibly be rechargeable, you should ask around to others who may be using the same item to see if they have found any way to replenish it. Also, you might want to spend some time doing npc questing, asking about the item and seeing if you get any response to your queries.
