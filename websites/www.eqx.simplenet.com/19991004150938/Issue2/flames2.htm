Trouble in the Commons

by Ian Ash

That next day in the commons Sphere came looking for the owner of the dagger and scroll. Sphere usually did not wear his armor when traveling inside the kingdom, but today it shined. I followed out of duty to my work. The normal pleasant folk were in the commons, and they stood aside of the armored Sphere. There stood Saturnino with his small band of men. Sphere approached within striking distance and spoke.

"You dare compare your small band of men and your meager home to my great towers by insulting my scholar's work?"

Saturnino said nothing, still angry from the comments about 'scantly clad women', and the silent retreat of the commoners from Sphere meant that Saturnino's following would not remain calm. Even the servants to Sphere stepped away. I stood my ground, paralyzed with fear of what might happen. Moments passed before one of the traveling rangers spoke up. He was a slender man, dressed in leathers and appeared to be moving between kingdoms. His name was Goral, and thus he spoke:

"Commoners, have you heard of any other towers like Master Sphere's? You seem to argue over his existence, when no one forces you to flock to his lands. If you seek a tower seek another. Alas Master Dach gave his title for a room in EQU Towers. Surely there are others?"

One of Sphere's Captains spoke, the one named Marcus Yu. "Why do you oppose EQU?"

Sphere then said: "Yes tell me why you have such trouble with the Greatest Tower and its keeper?"

Saturnino stepped closer and said "All right, then I shall build a tower greater than yours." He looked around for approval with that maverick grin "heh heh heh", hoping for a fight. Sphere's face turned red with anger.

Sphere said: "That is hopeless. Go ahead and try, you will fail. Join me instead. Bring your men and live in my towers, and work for me. I shall provide you and your whores shelter." 

I felt it time to speak up for my liege, and cast away my silence. I stood forward and as I moved to speak I could not help but fear I would be buried in the same mouse-brown robes I wore that day.

"Goral, do not let the voices you hear from others sway your mind before seeking your own opinion. Master Dach left his own tower to join us with the best interests of the people in mind. The voice of conflict rises from the opinions of only a few, not the whole gathering you see here."

Goral's reply was quick, and with it the commoners grew restless for conflict. "My opinion has been formed from what I have heard from Sphere himself, not others. Sphere squelches any who speak against him with the fervor of a child, and lacks nobility in his efforts to calm those who object to his tower. My grievance with EQU is with Sphere himself."

"Perhaps, Sphere, we should leave the commons before our names are slandered any more," I told Sphere, preparing to leave.

Sphere took it a few steps further than I intended. "Nah perhaps we should no longer support the commons! If we were to no longer come here, and no longer support the pleasant folk, surely the commerce would come to a halt."

A man in robes spoke from the crowd. He struck me as another scribe or scholar, perhaps from a different school of thought than I. His name was Absor: "That is not logical Sphere. If one seeks the shelter of your Towers they will come to the towers. The People who come here come here for other reasons, and certainly not to hear this babble."

A Crowd was gathering and I felt that it was too late to escape. Several had come within arms reach to argue now, including The Noble WarDragon and the Vagabond called 'Bloodystumps'. Surely there would be a battle, for the majority of the capable warriors were here or nearby. I trembled in fear as many spoke at once in a festival of hate.

WarDragon: "Here is an idea Sphere. Leave the commons and then you can laugh all you want if the commerce comes to a stop. And since all of the commerce today revolved around you and your tower, you might have a point. Still, we would be better off without your persistence."

BloodyStumps: "Someone knock the chip off his shoulder! The commons has been active for a long time before the tower was built, and will remain that way! I need not the shelter of your tower when Lord McQuaid provides a similar tower, and many more guards."

Goral turned to address me again, and I flinched as people turned to face me instead of Sphere. "I see no truth in insisting that Saturnino favors discussion of whores to discussions of politics. Such accusations and on-going lies are the reason I oppose EQU and Sphere. And this argument, did it not start from people growing tired of hearing about EQU? At every opportunity he beckons us to seek his tower? Your support in him is misguided, and he will only continue to embarrass you unless someone else takes the crown from his head. He is a child!"

Sphere drew his blade: "I am a child? Immature? Certainly a man who makes such an accusation is still seeking carnal knowledge and prefers to fondle himself when alone."

My mouth moved but no sound came out. The cries of outrage turned to anger quickly and steel was drawn. The pleasant folk fled the commons, while the warriors raced to participate. I would have left were my legs obedient; nay I stood immobile. Cries ranged from "That's indecent!" to "Words of experience, eh Sphere?" A combat ensued.

Saturnino, Goral and other warriors and vagabonds joined in the fray. The guards of EQU defended Sphere carefully, watching each other in fear of being stabbed in the back. Dach found himself defending against men that once looked up to him, even if they attacked with reluctance. He used non-lethal combat to subdue the opponents of EQU, in hopes of later mending friendships. Other combat was brutal, inflicting wounds in those who might be called innocent. The combat ranged across the commons, from the pub to the watch tower. Soon I was caught up in the battle, forced to defend myself. Evening came, and despite the prowess of the opposition, Sphere's potency in combat proved to be greater. It was almost if the opposition was weak with disgust. All had sustained injuries when the banner of the Lord McQuaid was spotted in the commons. It was him!

McQuaid spoke the words of a wise man losing patience. "I made arrangements to visit the Commons like of norm, and this is what I find here Sphere? A bloodbath in your name? All of you, cease this senseless conflict at once, and Sphere I will tolerate no more of you abusing those who come here."

Sphere mouthed the words "me? abuse?" without making sound as the Lord left the commons. Each party left for their own homes and keeps murmuring about the day's excitement, most nurturing wounds. Sphere left, but Dach, I and a few remained to talk.

We spoke of Dach leaving his own Tower to join EQU, and those that today warred with him forgave him, urging him to take action against Sphere or to leave EQU. The conversation lasted for hours and was civilized. I quite enjoyed the change of pace. At the same time I grew ill from talk of leaving EQU and hints at a greater conflict. I slept not at all that night.
