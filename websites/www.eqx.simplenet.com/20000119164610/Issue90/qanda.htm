Q:  Hello all I have question for you. I am a Bard and would like to know why I am auto-targeted when grouped as some party members find this a little annoying. Also I was wondering why Bards can't use bow's and Paladins can (not a very honorable way to attack) if you could enlighten me on this I would be very grateful for your assistance.
Thank you Koldar
A:  Oh dear, it's a pity they find the autotargetting annoying from a bard but not from a druid or a cleric or other spellcasters or healers? Bard songs work much the same way as a spell; if my song speeds you along your way my name will appear in your target box, for instance. It's much more noticeable for bards, however, because our songs are ongoing. You can tell them that the only time a song will auto target them will be when it is music that directly affects them thus it can be useful as a quick reference spot to check if heal is going after they call for it, etc. As for the bows, you must remember that Bards are rogues and thus go in for stealthier approaches, particularly at higher levels. No one said bards had to be honorable, particularly, but quite personally I have found bards to be honorable, clever, witty, kind, romantic, adventurous, intelligent, resourceful, and did I mention connoisseurs of fine libations. Give the Paladin a bow and give me a song, a sweet glance and a delicious cool drink any day.

Q:  Question related to last weeks editorial: What, exactly, constitutes an exploit? As a ranger, I usually carry a bow, and I have religiously avoided firing upon any creature that could not immediately pursue me and directly assault me. I was under the impression that all 'sniping' was considered an exploit. Is this the case, or is sniping allowed when the creature has no way to reach you . . .i.e. when you are actually out of reach in a location that is difficult or impossible for the creature to reach, as opposed to an easily accessible place that can not be reached because of a flaw in the pathfinding?
Sarama
A:  I think for a comprehensive discussion of exploits you might wish to visit the official Everquest Website (www.everquest.com) where under the section on "Support" you'll find explanations of exploits and other rules for play in the Guide/GM FAQ section. Quoting from there, an exploit is "abusing a weakness or anomaly in the game system. An exploit provides advantage for one or more players with the intention of profiting from them in some manner." In light of this, if you fire on a creature in a position where it cannot fight back and you can either cast on it or melee it without any damage or response back, it would be considered an exploit. If the critter can engage you in anyway, chasing you or casting spells upon you, then it would not be considered exploiting for you to use your bow to start a battle. You might have noticed recently however, that even stuck npcs seem to have a way of getting unstuck and fighting so perhaps exploiting questions will become fewer and less worrisome to you. If in doubt, though, you can petition and ask a guide or GM if a given situation would constitute an exploit.

Q:  Not really a question, but some additional info for one of your answers in Issue 89. In regards to the use of a quiver, a ranger friend of mine told me that the quiver is used as an ammo "auto-loader", that is, you can store 20 arrows in your ammo slot (max), but with a quiver, when that ammo slot drops to the last arrow, the bow will start drawing from arrows stored in the quiver, so you don't run out at an inopportune moment (unless you also use up all the arrows in the quiver).
Gatlen
Q:  : This isn't really a question, just some information in regards to a question on quivers in issue 89. The difference between a quiver and a regular bag is that different kinds of arrows can be auto-loaded from a quiver to shoot them. Where as a bag can only auto-feed arrows of the same kind. For example, if you have a class 1 arrow in your ammo slot, and in a backpack you have class 1, 2, and 3 arrows, only the class 1 arrows will be fired until they run out. Then you have to put a class 2 or 3 arrow into the ammo slot to prime the autofeed of the respective types. If you had the arrows in the quiver, when you ran out of class 1 arrows, you would continue to fire with class 2 and 3 arrows without having to place another arrow in the ammo slot. Hope this clears it up for you for future questions.
Bortoas
