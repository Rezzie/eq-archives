ISSUE # 18Apr. 18th - Apr. 24th

Yvette's Tower - Part 2

"Sure, yes. I was just thinking again." Yvette turned away from the ocean and looked at the coastline ahead. Already you could make out the gentle glow of the Qeynos walls in the setting sun. The sailors became a lively bunch, anticipating the night of frolic in the taverns at shore ahead. There was much singing and drinking as they made early preparations to land. Such was not the case on the other ship, however. Almost forgotten by the Erudite passengers, the second ship was a warrior's charter, more of a war ship than a cargo ship like this one. It had spent much of the journey at a peaceful distance away from them but as they came within a half hour of the shore it was closer, and one could now see flickering light from a fire beneath the deck. Men came rushing up from below engulfed in oil based fire, diving to their deaths in the freezing ocean waters. Spine chilling screams called for help of the cargo ship, and the crew turned to pull the people out of the water and away from the burning ship. By the time they were half way turned around the whole ship was aflame, and the women stood in awe of the helpless men in the water. Marissa looked as if she was going to be sick. Montgomery came to stand by them as they watched the sailors pull the dead and wounded out of the water. The smoking hull of the other ship sunk into the water in a mist, taking the warriors treasure with it.

Tale by Ian Ash - Fraterni Lux.
