Editorial - Issue 107 reply

You hit the nail right on the head regarding the issue of MU* players having far more patience for changes and such in EverQuest than many of the people who picked it up as a video game, not a MMORPG. Also, in addition to just playing text-based MU*'s over the years, many of us have **RUN** the games in one manner or another and have had to make the "nerfs" or mistakes that Verant is now making. We smile when we see the big company making the same mistakes we did or making ones we avoided or, even moreso, nodding when we notice they fix a problem we couldn't.

From what I can tell, _most_ of the really avid complainers haven't spent much time on MUD's or doing much online gaming outside of things like Quake, Mechwarrior or so forth. Instant satisfaction isn't something you run across when you build or manage online games and many of us "old timers" (even though most are barely or not even 30 yet =) ... ) have learned far more patience, I think, than those who require the instantaneous fun of normal video games. To me, that marks the definitive difference between the two major groups who play EQ: those who have the patiences to wait for long-term satisfaction and those who want instantaneous satisfaction all the time no matter how unreasonable that might be. We often label these people as teenaged "brats" because it fits the image but some could be 50 years old yet just far too used to being waited on or just never having interacted with this sort of medium before and now finding it inherently frustrating.

Ididar
44th wood elf bard on Prexus
