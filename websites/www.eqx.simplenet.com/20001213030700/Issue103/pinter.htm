ISSUE # 103

December 4th - December 10th


Pinter's Legacy



Ivar picked up the small, retractable telescope. It quivered in his hand slightly as he held it to his eye. "This is almost as ingenious as your underwater breathing apparatus, Pinter."

"Aye the greatest friend of a hermit is his mind." Pinter shifted his feet back and forth methodically.

Ivar nodded, "There is the beast," he whispered. "Remember, the calm defeats the storm."

"And your pockets defeat your allegiance," he chuckled. "I have your finger darts in my pouch. As soon as we bring it down, the reward is..." Ivar stopped him in mid sentence with a wave of his finger. They stared into the animal's grey hide. Pinter blinked slowly. Ivar began to summon his trusted poison tipped dagger, Riend. The sickle shaped blade drew an almost inaudible shudder from the nervous gnome. Ivar smiled. Pinter, awakening from his trance, drew two star rubies from his pouch. He handed one to Pinter. They cracked them simultaneously, so as to create the least possible disturbance. A mystical creature of Earth formed in front of Ivar. A water elemental formed almost crashing into a tree. The pets gave a hands up, meaning that they were ready to fight. Ivar manuevered his way through the thick underbrush. As soon as he could hear the rhino breathing he unsheathed Riend, in plain view of the rhino. With a reflex of years of training, Ivar drove his blade into the white part of the eye of the bewildered beast. He twisted it quickly, and only the tip of the horn slashed through his arm. He retreated to the shadows. The two elementals raced forward with a surge of courage toward the rhino. The roots of the trees began to engulf the rhino's feet. Ice pounded down from the water elemental, as the ground shook with a tremendous force. Ivar placed his hands in a semi-circle and uttered one soft word, "fire". A blazing gust raced forward. Soon after, the elementals found themselves beating on a charcoal-like corpse. Adding up the costs of the battle, he noted the earth elemental was badly wounded. He put it out of its misery swiftly, without pain. He suddenly realized Pinter wasn't there.

"Pinter, the battles over. Pinter!" He began to search the surronding area and eventually came across small footprints looking as though he had taken off at a hurried pace. They were still fresh. Further down the trail, he came across a deep chasm. On the edge was the telescope. Peering down, he saw nothing but dirt and rocks. He raised his arms above his head and quickly found himself back in Freeport. He tried not to put a second thought on Pinter's fate, but the dreams... they might haunt him.
