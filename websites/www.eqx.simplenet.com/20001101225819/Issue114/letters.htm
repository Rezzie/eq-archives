Letters to the Editors

I just thought I'd let you know that there's another game that's similar to that, but due out much sooner.

You may or may not have heard of the game Vampire: The Masquerade. It's a pencil & paper game that's grown fairly popular. Recently, a company called Nihilistic Software decided to convert that game onto the computer.

It has an off-line single player game, as well as a multiplayer mode. I believe they allow up to eight players, not counting the StoryTeller (GM). As the StoryTeller, you can completely design levels for players to play in, and put in switches and triggers that the players can set off. You can set up NPCs to automaticaly respond to a PC's questions, or you can even take over that NPC and answer them yourself. For a really good time, you can insert an NPC and attack the players.

Since it's based on a popular Pencil & Paper game, I'm assuming it'll be an immediate hit to anyone that buys it. Unfortunately, due to the company's lack of fame and money, it hasn't had much of an advertisement. Even I, an avid V:TM player, only recently heard of it.

Oh... it's coming out sometime this March, too.

For good information about the game, try http://www.planetvampire.com. They have a bunch of interviews with the creators, and little infobits and downloads to check out. There's also a great sneak-peek video to download. Unfortunately, it's rather large, and the narrorator is speaking in german. =P

Anyway, I thought you might find that interesting, as it has a LOT of similarities with Neverwinter Nights.

Kortalh
17th Necromancer
