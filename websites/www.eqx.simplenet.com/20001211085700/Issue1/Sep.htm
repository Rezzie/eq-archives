Separatism


It was not easy to come up with something to write for this fine magazine. But recently I caught an undercurrent in the ebb and flow of the Avault EverQuest newsgroup. A subtle thing that maybe only I can feel, and therefore may not actually exist. But my nerves have been raw and exposed seeking something interesting to write about. I have sensed the potential for disaster in this great new endeavor called EverQuest. A ripple in the reality into which I hope to someday immerse myself. I seek in this writing to calm the ripples, ease the rocking concern in my mind, by exposing these nerves to the community that shares my wishes for a second home in Norrath.

First be warned. I am going to talk about... THE SWITCH! I have chosen to call it the Universal Player Interaction switch (UPI) on the strength of a suggestion made in the newsgroup by someone very smart whose name I no longer remember. Sorry 'bout that.

However, I have no intention of discussing the virtues or disadvantages of such a switch <reader  sighs   in   relief>.   What   I


want to tell you about is a sense that I have that we are setting ourselves up to rend Norrath. I expected to find Norrath a place divided. But divided among the many races, professions and desires of its inhabitants. Now I fear that it will be split by external forces, us, the players.

The UPI switch will exist in our role-playing environment. By its very nature it will separate us into two groups. But that separation is mechanical and should be kept apart from the characters, left in our world and out of Norrath as much as possible. But I have sensed a trend. It seems that we are expecting our characters to treat each other based first on how their switch is set, not on their race, class, sex or point of view. I am not espousing any sort of "ism" in our role-playing. Nothing more than the roles require. But any sexism that a barbarian may feel is certainly more valid in the character than any sort of evaluation or bias that one character might feel toward another based on the setting of his/her switch.

In discussions about the potential of EverQuest,  we  have  talked   about



justice and how it should apply to our characters. We have talked about racial solidarity. We have talked about many issues. In these discussions I have been hearing, or maybe sensing, something. It sounds like all of us, deep in our subconsciences, are envisioning a Norrath where the first thing we notice about another person is the position of his/her switch. I know that I just realized this in myself. Now this, by itself, is an acceptable price to pay for the potential good that this switch can bring. But what if it goes too far? Suppose that non-UPI characters refuse to hang around UPI characters? Suppose the reverse also happens? It could. Let's imagine that we are playing a dedicated non-UPI character.

He/she may want to avoid traveling with UPI characters to avoid the risk of losing his/her non-UPI status by helping a UPI character. What if this becomes common? It could. What if UPI characters tended to avoid non-UPI characters because they would not be of any use against other PCs in a fight. Maybe this would feel unfair to the UPI character. It could happen.
