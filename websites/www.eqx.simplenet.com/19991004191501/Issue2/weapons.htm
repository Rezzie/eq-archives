Weapon Jumble and Horse Pucky


Recently in the News Group two popular issues have popped up, aside from flames: Weapons and Riding Creatures. We can agree on one thing it seems, and that is the fact "It's too soon to say for sure".

On the topic of weapons we have discussed the amount of training it takes to become potent in a weapon and how useful that weapon is in certain situations. It started with pikes and polearms, which were agreed to be weapons used against mounted troops and not very good weapons in a melee fight. Polearms, like the halberd, are useful for about one strike or less before the opponent closes within (under) the range of the sharp end.   At   that    point    a   normal

weapon could be used for combat. It could be as simple as not putting polearms in the game, but what about mounted combat?

If there are horses in the game and assuming that riding the horse gives any combat bonus at all the polearm becomes a viable weapon for defence against mounted charges. A long spear would do horrendous damage to the attacker, as well any polearm. Mounted soldiers vs foot soldiers has historically been about what weapons who was using. The same could apply to magical mounts, like Unicorns, Pegasi, Nightmares, Wolves, Griffins, Dragons, etc. I tell you I would rather fight a charging dragon with a real long sharp spear than a sword  unless  he   was  already

up close and personal.

  Also in the same discussions is the issue of training. Should a non-warrior be able to learn a weapon skill? A few weeks training in a pike or polearm is about all you would need to use it, since there is no great mastery to poking someone with it. Historically polearms were used by the non-combat troops like farmers and children to kill as many mounted enemies as possible when charged. The conventional weapons are a different story. While two weeks of training in a sword is far better than none, it takes many years to become a warrior like out of classic role playing games.
