There has been a crime perpetrated against the artistic community this week. I am speaking, of course, about the incident where a player was banned from EQ for fiction written to one of the fan message boards.
Guilt and poor judgement were in abundance this week. No surprise there. As usual, and as is appropriate, it’s impossible to get the entire story. Verant does not publicize details of their dealings with individuals, and in my opinion, that’s good policy, as long as they hold the proper communications with the person actually involved.

Flowers of Evil

A sudden uneasy silence fell between the two Teir'dal, and even the rumblings of the molten pools nearby seemed to quiet for a moment. The scent of ash seemed fresh again as if they had just arrived at this moment in time from someplace far away, even though both had been here in the shadows of the lavastorm range for most of the evening. The youthful Teir'dal necromancer sat as she had for hours, staring in disbelief at what she had just heard her companion say. The battle-hardened Sister of the Indigo towered over the frail necromancer, with a gaze nearly as gloomy as the news she had brought.

    Tale by Ian Ash

The Painting

Cameleon was an artist.

She could not remember a time when she didn't love to draw and paint. Even her earliest memories of childhood involved endless piles of construction paper and boxes of crayons that never seemed to contain the proper colors to express the visions that burned inside her skull.

Her parents, while far from wealthy, did their best to support their daughter's obvious talent. As she progressed through her teenage years, however, her burgeoning artistry continually surpassed and was limited by the rough tools which her parents could provide.

ROLEPLAY AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

If I started this essay writing in character as Nepenthia, it’s quite likely I’d describe her as frequenting an inn in Freeport, or searching for scrolls about Tunare, or perhaps even (as set out last week in that rather silly poem about dresses) as initiating a quest to marry Aradune. It would seem entirely natural to place Nep in Norrath, using the possibilities of that huge and fascinating world for her stories because that is where Nep now spends her time, meets her friends and experiences adventures. Even though Nep wasn’t born in Norrath, the place of her birth actually having been a ship that sailed far, far away and long, long ago, she has traveled through a variety of realms on her way there.

    Article by Nepenthia
