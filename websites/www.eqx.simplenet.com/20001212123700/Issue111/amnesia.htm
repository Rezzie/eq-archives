ISSUE # 111

January 29th - February 4th, 2000


Amnesia


'Twas not four score but seven long and tragic years ago
I chanced to bump my head in Freeport; how I do not know.
Was it a roguish fiend who struck me just to gain my purse?
Or was it some adventurer I failed to reimburse?
It could have been the influence of too much gypsy wine,
Or it could be a vengeful old associate of mine.
Whatever was the cause of it, I must have lost my mind
And wandered all about the region for a span of time.
When I awoke I found myself buck-naked and bereft
Of any armor, weapon, cloak; some fiendish type of theft
Had left me without any means of merely scraping by.
Crushed, I sat down on the ground and then began to cry.

Through my tears I saw a good old friend (Stormline by name);
He gave me basic arms by which all heroes gain their fame.
Then Hawkwind, an old friend I used to knew before my loss
Informed me that he held my things that I had tried to toss
While fuzzy-brained and drunk out on the streets of Freeport North.
Apparently I'd quite forgotten what those things were worth.
Starhawk, Namus, Thaylos, others came unto my aid
And furnished me that I may once again swing fist and blade.
Old acquaintances I'd long forgotten in my daze
Welcomed me with merry cheer; in truth, I was amazed
And shocked that after such a time my memory lived on
While I was languishing and stupefied hin and anon.

Again back on my feet and wandering about the lands,
I now run bardic mail from Queynos to the Commonlands.
And yet the questions plague me - what did I do unaware
And what deeds could some claim are mine while I knew not nor cared?
Did I aid wicked inkie bands and lead an evil life?
Was I enslaved to someone just to play my drum and fife?
Did some foul magic dazzle me to act against my will?
And were I evil then, am I considered evil still?
By far the most important question that I have to pose
Is this: what women did I bed without my mind and clothes?

    Tale by Calthaer
