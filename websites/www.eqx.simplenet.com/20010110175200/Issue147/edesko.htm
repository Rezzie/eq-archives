From the Editor’s desk

Issue 147

Halloween seemed to leap up and snag this issue. I spent half the week with a fouled-up e-mail server, and a Halloween party stopped me from finishing this issue on Saturday! Then Morgaelin had trouble with his e-mail! My apologies if anyone sent me mail in the middle of the week, please send it again and hopefully I’ll have no problems.

I launch myself back into the fiction with a story about my DnD group. Hopefully I’ll be able to keep this one going . Please look at “Plaguesong: the beginning”.

I’ve gotten a few letters like the one below and wanted to address them for those of you writing stories and articles about EQ or role-playing.

Hello,

I am an avid EQ player (along with several other games, this is my only online game). I started playing rpg's back in the late 70's. D&D and the later AD&D were my games of choice in my youth.

Recently I have spent a lot of time with Warhammer and Magic, the Gathering.

I'm sure that since Absor's move to VI you've received a lot of letters like this... I'm distressed that it took me this long to find you... I follow quite a few boards but somehow EQX didn't make my radar.

The reason I am writing is that while reviewing some of Absor's prior work I came across a letter suggesting that you would accept contributing articles and editorials...

I would be very interested in contributing some articles and was wondering what I should do; just send you unsolicited work? I used to write for a daily newspaper in my home state of NH, the Daily Telegraph.

Yours truly,

Greyfin Twiceborn

Take a look at our submissions guidelines. Many of our current and former staffers were folks who just started out sending stuff in. We’ll take a look at it and put it up if it's good. If you contribute good material on a steady basis, we'd definitely consider adding you to the staff. With Absor’s Ab-sence we are definitely looking to add quality writing to our staff.

Experience with different gaming systems helps, as does experience writing for other sites or employers. Keeping up with current issues in EQ or online gaming by reading the message boards or watching gaming sites helps also. The ability to look at the “big picture” and not focus narrowly on a pet topic helps.

I began writing for EQX back in Issue 7, by just sending in an article on a topic that I hadn’t seen anyone cover and I thought was important. I continued to send in articles over the next weeks, and now, almost three years later, I’ve somehow wound up with a rather large body of content. It’s amazing to think that we are fast approaching our 150th, and shortly after, our 156th or third year of publishing. We have been blessed with many talented contributors, some who have moved on to write or draw professionally based on the experience obtained through writing for us.

Enjoy this issue!
