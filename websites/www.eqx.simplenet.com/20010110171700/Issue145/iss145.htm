Game Communities in MMORPGs

By now, I think everyone realizes that although we talk a lot about game features, balance, and depth, the most important factor in the success of an online game is the people. Players are attracted to these games for the sense of community they provide, and player retention has an awful lot more to do with how tied to the community a player feels than how much they like the combat system or trade skills. Something happened in EverQuest a few weeks ago that got me thinking about communities in games. It was a small incident, but it’s generated a lot of thought and discussion.

Reviewing 3rd edition Dungeons and Dragons

Since I reviewed the 3e Player's Handbook, I've been getting in a fair amount of good old-fashioned paper and pen role-playing in. I played three times before the DM's guide came out, and I've played five times since. Anytime you start a new game system, there is a period of acclimatization that takes place, even if the game mechanics are familiar. You look at rules, you try them out and miss bits and you go back and wonder what you did wrong, or, find if it was the game system itself. As you read this, if you are not familiar with the 3e rules, peek back at my other review, since I’m referring to game mechanics I mentioned there.

Farewell EQX

So I’m sitting here in a Hawaiian T-shirt. Not because I like it all that much, it’s actually a hand-me-down from a friend. No, I’m wearing it because when I woke up this morning I didn’t have anything else to wear that was still clean. There’s something about moving that seems to make all your clothing dirty.

Yup, I’ve moved. I’ve moved about 80 miles south. I was in Costa Mesa (in Orange County, California) last weekend. Then, in a flurry of bodies and broken lamps, I’m suddenly in San Diego. I love Orange County, so moving to San Diego wasn’t something that I did to improve my surroundings. Not that San Diego isn’t wonderful; it is. But it would take a lot to get me to quit my job and move. After all, I have to drive an hour and a half on the freeway to get to my games on Saturday...

    Letter by Absor
