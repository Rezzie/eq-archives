Q:  Do you have any suggestions on where a humble Dark Elf like myself might take a nice Teir'dal lady on a date? I'd prefer somewhere in which my lady won't be eaten by goblins, orcs or lions, and a place with a nice view, preferably somewhere with a nice overlook of an area...in which humans might hunt animals that are much, much stronger than them...<cough> Please give me some suggestions for a date? :)
Eliptus
A:  Oh my, this kind of question is definitely one that interests me although I wasn't aware that Dark Elves observed the more genteel mating rituals such as dates. (I'm always glad to learn these things --- strictly as anthropological lore information obviously.) I have continued to research such matters and have come up with a couple of possibilities. The first suggestion is for the dark rainy cold nights when Dark Elf females might freeze in the outdoors. For those times, I understand there is a charming bar in Neriak Commons with dark corners near subterranean windows where you can observe a halfling perpetually swimming. While I can't promise you privacy (and suggest that you coo to each other in groupsay or tells), it is a safe and cozy nook. To round out the evening, you might then invite your date behind the waterfall also in Neriak Commons for a wee bit of private conversation (again avoiding "say" or the public channels for fear of embarassment). For when the moon rises over warm and balmy evenings, however, you might try a picnic by the cliffs near the fishing village in North Ro overlooking the sea as they seem to be relatively undisturbed. A friend suggested that I send you off to skinnydip in the lake in West Commons. It is a beautiful spot indeed but as my elven friend has little interest in preserving indigo skins from piranhas, I think I will not seriously propose that you take your lady friend there. Finally, for that special weekend getaway, perhaps you might consider the wonders of Lake Rathe. A small boat, countless private bays and soft grassy spots will enhance your enjoyment of your companion. A word of caution, however, you might wish to scope out the environment with an adventuring party first as the area does have its natural dangers. At any rate, may romance always travel the road with you!

Q:  G'day I was just wanting to get an answer on an issue that has caused strife in my circle of eq friends :). What I want to know is whether I could still be killed by DE's or other evils if I had cast illusion: DE on my self or if I was wearing a guise of the deceivers? Someone has said that the mask and illusion spells turn you indifferent to the DE NPCS.... Is this true or would I be like a DE that has killed too many guards? Please clear this up if you can.
Thanks Qaelan
A:  I was able to run a few miles alongside a fastmoving gnome and received this answer to your question to pass along to you. Basically, illusions will only change your race, they will not affect your factions or your religion. In your example, an agnostic in the form of a DE would be accepted but followers of Tunare in the same guise would be slain. However, if the agnostic killed too many guards and destroyed his faction, he too would be slain. I hope this helps.

Q:  What does it matter when the swim skill goes up? Does it increase how long you can stay under water, or how fast you swim? Perhaps it uses less stamina? I dunno... that's why I'm asking you. *grin*
Enlay, monk.
A:  My understanding of the swim skill is that as you raise in the skill you can swim faster, thus covering longer distances with the same amount of air and stamina. From what people have told me, I don't think increased skill would allow you to stay under water longer though.

Even tho I knew he was charmed,I couldn't allow him to kill her, so I had to kill him. I felt terrible, but felt I had no choice. Was I wrong to do this?

A:  It seems to me that you had the right motives and in desperation, killing the wood elf acting under the dastardly influence of a meanie npc, was the right thing to do. However, depending on your class and skills, I'd like to suggest that you try to use your skills and abilities as creatively as possible. For instance, if you are a healer you could heal your partner enough so both of you could run off, with perhaps the wood elf chasing you until his charm had worn off and he could come to his senses. However, said creative solutions are not always possible, sadly enough, and in any case the wood elf might have ended up dead anyway at the hands of his charmer. So, all in all, it would have been cool to see if you could save both, but if the odds were against it, you did the right thing in saving your partner. Now, sweet dreams and I'm sure the woodelf will forgive you over a glass of good elven spirits.

Q:  How do you make pies? I have tried the meat pie recipe, and the berry pie recipe, with no luck. I have even tried using more clumps of dough on the meat pie recipe, with no success. I had a friend that successfully made Firebeetle Eye Pie, but I haven't seen him in awhile, so I cannot ask him how he did it. If you have the answer, please give it to me.
Silversight
A:  I found a cute gnome with purple stains on his moustache to ask about pie baking and he told me that the recipes were all somewhat similar. For instance, if you wish to make rat ear pie, you will need dough, rat ears, spices, a pie tin and an oven. However, it is possible that not all the recipes work correctly, so if you are following the recipe book exactly and still do not end up with a pie, you might want to petition to ask if the recipe is broken or bugged. The gnome, quite the connoisseur of pies, said that as far as he knew, there are beetle pies, fruit pies (foraged fruit), berry pies, rat ear pie, vegetable pie and meat pie.
