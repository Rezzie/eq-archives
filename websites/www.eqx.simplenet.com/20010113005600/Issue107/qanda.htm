Q:  Dear Nep, I enjoy reading your column with much interest each week. My question -- what is your take on various guilds? I was trying to explain to a fellow traveler of Norrath the other night how to find a guild and what are up some good and bad qualities. I figured the sage of bards would have a good response. Also, are you in a guild ?

Relseg

A:  This is really a question for an entire article I think, but I'll take a quick crack at it for now. Guilds have been in Norrath since ancient times (i.e., beta when they were first developed and tested) and have espoused all sorts of reasons for existing. I think that the first step in finding a guild, unless you want to try to start a fresh one, is to first figure out what you'd like in a guild. Do you want powerlevelling and cool equipment fast? Do you want a guild involved in creative quests and roleplay? Do you want a huge guild with a lot of structure or do you want a group of dependable friends with minimal hierarchy? Once you have an idea of the kind of people you want to hang around with and the kind of playstyle you want to use, then I'd suggest you learn as much as you can about the various guilds on your server. For me, I tend to judge a guild on how they treat non-members, figuring if they are reasonably kind and informative to people outside the guild, then the atmosphere inside the guild will generally be friendly and supportive, which is something I look for. You can find out if the guild has a website and visit it and you can chat with guildmembers and see if they are taking new members and what they are looking for. Finally, you can watch how the guild as a whole and its individual members operate on your server? Does it demean other guilds? Does it create dissension and fighting over non-roleplay issues? Will it pitch in on corpse retrievals for anyone who needs it? What is its attitude toward loot and camping? Do members actively hog zones for gaining equipment to sell on EBay? Finally, what is a guild's reputation among players on your server? That will tell you a lot before you ever make a decision about joining an organization. You might even talk to the GM or older guides (you'd have to petition and ask for one), and ask them about the guild you're interested in and whether they have any kind of server-wide reputation.

For me, the ability to trust my guildmates is the main quality I look for in a guild so we can all enjoy the game and not have to worry about guild politics or greed. Also, I look for a guild whose members see me as having something to offer even though I'm not a terrific gamer. (Have I ever told you about how often I get lost?) A guild that will stop everything to help one of its members achieve a new skill or finish a quest and where everyone in the guild can feel that they can call for help and be sure of getting it, is important to me. For instance, Tagad, a Pillar of Wisdom and a leader of my guild, has spent countless hours helping people learn to play better, teaching them about their class and how to play it even when he could have been putting in all that time just leveling. And, just the other night, my fellow guildmember Sylvos went off and got himself killed and then stuck in a bugged area just because my habitual chatter and a momentary lack of attention led me off a bridge into the lava and I couldn't get my corpse back. I do hope I contribute too by being in my guild as a friend to all its members, willing to listen and chat and be there for them, even though at level 24 I don't have a lot to offer unless they want to hang around watching the little goblins chomp away at my toes and whatever other bardly parts they can reach.

Any guild that squabbles constantly over equipment or power is not going to be fun. I would definitely avoid a guild that wasn't willing to help make Norrath a better place for all who play there. I would also run screaming away from a guild where inflated egos and meanness were the norm; where blacklisting and bickering characterized its interactions with other players, and where "beating the game" was the commonly accepted illusion. After all, bards don't beat games, they just write bad poetry about them!

This has turned into a long answer indeed, but, yes, I am in a guild on Veeshan server and am very glad to be part of it. It was formed as a guild of friends and has added friends of friends, spouses and significant others, and new friends found on the server. We sometimes have squabbles and sometimes big frustrations because our members go from very low levels to 50 and grouping is hard to do, but everyone in it wants to be part of a guild that has fun, helps its members and contributes to Norrath, so I'm pretty lucky to be part of it.

Q:  Are there any low level npcs (8-12) that yield a magical weapon or armor. If there are, where do they start?

A:  I think the pickings are pretty slim, to use a colloquialism from the hills, but you might want to try some questing for low level armor from npcs. Frogloks are a good npc group for a lower level monk to fight as they do yield some magical armor for monks but the fruits of the fight are not particularly useful for other classes. Q:  Is it true that the spell Inner Fire (shaman, lvl 1), heals even more than the lvl 9 heal spell?

Mcbarky da barb

A:  It is true that the effect of Inner Fire at low levels does give you more hitpoints than a level 9 heal spell, if I understand it correctly. All good mages and part-mages will use whatever spells are at their disposal to survive however, so no one would blame you if you avoided certain death by casting Inner Fire.

Q:  I have tried to track this info down for months, but no one I know has been able to tell me how to insert a description of my character into the "examination" view. When you examine another character, there is a box to the right of the icons of personal possessions are located where you can presumably add a colorful description of yourself. I've seen it done by someone else (the description was horribly conceited and juvenile.) How do I do this? What is the name of the box? Does my description of the problem make any sense?

Liniri, Half-HUMAN Paladin of Tunare.

A:  Hidden in the depths of your online manual in the EQ folder are the directions for getting to your description box and entering your text. Here's how to do it: "To create your own description, press F9 to change to a view that you can see yourself, then inspect yourself by doubleclicking on you. The description box is now open and you can click on it and start typing." When you're done, the text in the box will be visible to anyone who inspects you. It's important to remember that this description box is not a place for offensive material like swear words, etc. so do be careful what you choose to write there.
