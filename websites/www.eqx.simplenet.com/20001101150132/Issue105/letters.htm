Letters to the Editors

Hi Oghma,

Sorry this is a week late but I have been quite busy lately and just got around to reading the 100th Issue. Congrats to everyone at EQX on such an achievement. EverQuest would not be the game it is today without your constant support. I also very much enjoyed your editorial on "Infinite and Finite Games" (..and the added bonus of having my name mentioned in it! ;) When I am asked what I am most proud of about EverQuest my first thought is always, "Hey, I named Norrath." The fact that there are thousands of people from all over the world who have embraced Norrath, its gods, geography and populace, and have used it as a setting for exploring their own creative abilities is by far the most rewarding thing about EverQuest for me. I am sure there are those who dismiss Norrath as being cliché or unoriginal but that was never the point of it. I believe we have the following we do in part because Norrath feels so darn familiar.. yet.. if you dig around a little, you discover all the little differences that make it intriguing. (Due in no small part to the awesome work done by our main quest designer, Tony Garcia) I am also a firm believer in "nebulous history" as a hook.. it allows room for conflict and debate over who knows "the Truth" and in general makes for a much more fascinating world.. Hell, if we all knew the truth, where is the room for the unbelievable? Was Befallen an Elf city?? If not, why is there an Elf skeleton who haunts it? hmm.. answers that spawn more questions = good stories in my book..

I also saw the letter from Briza X'Ris, Spurned Cleric of Solusek Ro about some info learned from a GM (loose lips sink ships ;), and would like to clarify a few things that maybe you can pass on (and will be dealt with in game in time)... Please remember that these clarifications are just what the general citizens of Norrath believe.. and faith is sometimes misplaced.

Fennin Ro is the god of Fire and rules that plane.. his son Solusek Ro rules the Plane of Flame, his focus is more the release and discovery of raw forms of power and energy, hence the strong wizard following he attracts.
The information on the Triumvirate of Water and Prexus was correct. (hmm here is that Marr surname again...) ;)
The information on The Rathe was correct
The Plane of Sky is actually ruled by Veeshan, the Wurm Queen
The Plane of Air is ruled by Xegony
The information on the Six Hammers was correct.. (Has anyone seen mention of a Seventh Hammer??) ;)
While the information about Tallon and Vallon Zek was correct in that they are in fact the twin sons of Rallos Zek, Rallos rules the Plane of War not the Plane of Power (the Planes of Power is actually the name used for the higher planes of Air, Earth, Fire and Water)
The info on Innoruuk and Cazic-Thule was correct.
Rumors of the demise of Fizzlethorpe and Mithaniel have been greatly exaggerated.. ;)

Thanks once again for your thoughtful and thought provoking editorials.

Bill Trost
Senior Game Designer, EverQuest
Verant Interactive Inc.
