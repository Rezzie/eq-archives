ISSUE # 135

 August 5th - August 11th, 2000


Editorial - Absor

I’ve been out of town a lot lately. EQG3 started it. After that I was sick for a week (staying up late and drinking a lot does that to me). By the time I recovered I was on my way back to Las Vegas on a vacation trip. When I got back I was sent to San Francisco for a week for a conference. All of it was a heck of a lot of fun, except the part about being sick for a week.

But that means that for the last month I’ve been out of touch with the game. That’s why I didn’t write an editorial last week. I was still recovering from Vegas and I only had time to edit the stuff for the issue, not to read all the boards and barf out an editorial. Despite how it seems, I try not to just write something to fill space here. I can do that, it’s easy for me. But I really want to stick to writing when I actually have something to say.

So I got off the plane from San Francisco yesterday and went to sleep. I woke up this morning and went to work. Now it’s 10:30 on Friday night and all the editing is done. All that’s left for me to do is to write an editorial. And I still haven’t read the boards. I’m still way out of touch with the community. So how can I write something worth reading?

Easy, don’t write about EverQuest current events.

As I write I’m watching one of my new DVDs. Now, don’t get all freaked out. Don’t call me names when I tell you what it is. It’s the Kindred: The Embraced television series. If you remember it, then you’re probably asking yourself why the hell I would buy such a thing.

I kinda liked it, that’s why. And I like to encourage the genre. Other than writing about it here, giving them money is the best way to do that. Yes, it wasn’t a great show. And it didn’t really manage the feel of the game that it was modeled after. But those things I can forgive. These vampires, unlike in the game, can head into the sunlight if they’ve fed (perhaps only the elders, though). This is terribly contrary to the game rules. But if they don’t then the series will be filmed entirely in the dark. It’s really not appealing to people that way. Of course I would have gotten around that problem by centering the series on the ghouls belonging to the main vampires, making their masters mysterious and often unseen. The Nosferatu are not nearly ugly enough. They’re just a bunch of bald people. Again, done probably just to make the series more appealing to a broader audience. The vampires are not nearly feral enough, they never frenzy. Again, probably done for the same motivation.

But they did several things right. The casting was good. The Ventrue prince looks just like he should. The Tremere primogen is beautiful and entrancing, as she should be. Even the Gangrel punk enforcer is right. And the Brujah primogen, who gets himself killed, is my favorite. He’s the same guy that you’ve probably seen as the alien assassin on the X-Files. Whenever I see him on TV anyplace, I am forced to call out to him, naming him “Bruhaha”...

Anyway, they cast the characters well. The script is weaker than I would like, not biting enough, not lustful or dark enough. But that’s not what the public wants. They want Buffy. They want light-hearted evil. And with characters that are naturally evil as their protagonist, they have to tread very lightly.

But now I’m seeing shows that are a bit more dark. I caught an episode of a new show while in San Francisco, the name of which escapes me... But the Internet tells me that it’s call Mysterious Ways. It deals with an Indiana Jones kinda guy, a girl paranormal investigator and a psychiatrist. They seek out paranormal stuff. In the episode that I watched, they were waiting for the dead husband of a psychic couple to come back from beyond the grave and give a sign to his wife. He never comes. I don’t think the show will make it, it’s not entertaining enough, even for me. But I’m still glad to see it.

Remember when you first got hooked on the X-Files? For me it was the middle of the second season. Finally there was a show on broadcast television that was willing to do justice to one of my favorite genres. It was dark and funny. I appreciated the freak show episode, and I laughed out loud at the Jose Chung episode. The mix of dry humor with the power of the unknown... I was in love (and I have the first season DVDs too, I’ll have you know).

And from the tremendous success of that show we got all the others, Buffy, Vampire and the rest. It really does take a big, sweeping success like that to convince the people with the money that we actually want these things.

But the follow-ups also need to succeed. Enough failures and the money people lose faith. But those failures are not the fault of the genre. I think the reason that the Vampire thing failed was because it tried to blend mainstream and hardcore horror-fantasy. That can work (see Buffy the Vampire Slayer for details), but not if it takes itself seriously. You really can’t make likeable vampires and still be doing a serious vampire show. But would the public actually like a serious vampire show?

I think so. Make the likeable characters ghouls. Let the vampires be evil, just don’t make them the primary characters. Play the drama and love and happiness in the day, and make the dark the something to fear. It could be done, and people would like it.

Just don’t stray from the true heart of the genre.

The same can be said for on-line role-playing games. I’m still waiting for the game that stays true to the genre and doesn’t dilute itself in its attempts to include mainstream needs.

If either a TV show director or a game designer needs any help with this, I’m available... :)

    Editorial by Absor
