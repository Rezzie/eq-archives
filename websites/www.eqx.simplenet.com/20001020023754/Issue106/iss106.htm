The Ballad of the Tasty Bard

The group was joined, five stalwarts all,
A cleric and a wizard tall,
Two paladins for close melee.
What's that? Oh yeah. The bard was me.

To Blackburrow our leader led.
Down ramps, round turns, our leader sped.
This speed, thought I, is passing rash.
He zigged, I zagged. Look OUT! Ker-SPLASH.

    Tale by Kianel of Qeynos

Blood Guardian
part 4

Galm felt all sorts of tension between himself and the rest of the adventurers during the last leg of the sea voyage. He heard the others laughing and joking, and could sense the way that they forced the jovial sounds from their lips. Galm couldn't bring himself to act so happy. And he couldn't shake the feeling that a lot of their laughter had something to do with him. How could he admit a desire to protect Fealwin in front of all of those strangers? How could he admit something that he wasn't even aware of until the moment he spoke? How could he allow this journey to make such a change in his life?

    Tale by Absor


Spellbounder Kapolo L'Arety: Agnostic Life

I stride through the gates of Qeynos. I am a young enchanter of the world of Norrath but the thing is, I wasn't supposed to become one. My feet, slight wobble from my hunger, carry me to the nearest vendor and I request a smattering of bread and Elven wine. Without reaction, the vendor calmly stated that they did not have any more wine and had to settle for some ale. My tastes are fine, but right now, the sweat of ogre would be pleasing.

    Tale by Kapolo
