Letters to the Editors

I was disappointed to read that Absor mentioned us in his article about Guild Names. He effectively made a mockery of our guild name with obviously little or no real information on us. He is right, our guild made a name to attempt to preserve our Tanarus name MOB... we struggled for a month to come up with a name that would match it and the best we could come up with is Mystic Order of the Brotherhood as he mentioned. Now as you can see there is a redundancy in Order and Brotherhood and thus we changed it to just the Mystical Order as is reflected on our in game name and on our domain name mysticalorder.com (no real page there right now). Also, our charter was very well written and I didn't appreciate the assumptions he made about it. Our guild stands for honor and this editorial has shaken things up in our guild resulting in the loss of a great member, XXXX, and numerous different reactions. I don't know exactly how to correct this problem, but at least please forward this to Absor so he will understand what our guild really is. If you see a problem with The Mystical Order then by all means tell me, but otherwise please don't mention us again, as it hurts my reputation as a guild member and our guild as a whole.

Hafez of The Mystical Order

Amongst the many great stories and articles, one did jump out at me that I had to mention: Expression. In particular this line rang very true, and I hope everyone takes note of this:

"Don't let game mechanics stand in the way of playing the role that you want."

This phrase is the type of thinking is one of the important elements that makes a role-playing game the *best* and most *creative* it can be. Imagine a "character" you have always wanted to play and "flesh" him/her out within the contest of the world he/she is inhabiting. Challenge your own creativity, have fun doing something unique.
