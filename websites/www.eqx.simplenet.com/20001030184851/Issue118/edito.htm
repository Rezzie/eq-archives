ISSUE # 118

 April 1st - April 7th, 2000


Editorial - Oghma

One thing I wanted to address on my topic of Norrath evolution was what is already out there and exists. It would be remiss of me to state that Verant is doing nothing as far as historical background, because they are doing a lot, and adding quests all the time. I’d like to bring your attention to a thread on EQSS called Norrathian Mythology. In it, many players contributed what they have gleaned from in the game, discussions with GM’s, and from experiences in Beta. It's not official, but it’s an awesome compilation of information, and I hope we will be able to organize it in some way and present it to you. Take a second to look through it, (or more than a second, it’s rather long) and then come back.

I’m going to change my stance on information like this being available in only game, because I think that this information is not being found enough in game, or not being found in the correct way. There are too many people willing to trot through quests and post the bare minimum and spoil it for others, and not enough folks who find the lore and study it. Does this mean that the spoilers miss out? Yep, and it means we all miss out. This is fascinating stuff! It shows many directions to go with multiple story lines. We all know there is something called a Ring of Scale, but what does it do? Who are they? What is their goal, and what does that mean to us? I also like how that thread contained conflicting facts, such as the origin of dark elves and Innoruuk’s relationship to elves. Rebel elf factions? WOW. The high elves, those snooty palefaces, are actually rebels? Bill Trost mentioned that vagueness specifically in his letter to us in Issue 105 - how important conflicting stories are, and how they add depth to the history.

EQ is focused on items and classes right now. For a while, it was necessary. Class balance is essential, it will make or break a game. The fact that there is no dominant race/class combination chosen by gamers means that the game’s balance is essentially finished by now, with minor tweaks needed every once and a while. (I believe this strongly, so responses to this statement will be ignored. If you have a complaint about your class, don’t bring it to me. )

Now it’s time to refocus. Pull some of those item creators and balance wizards out and take a look at how to take the storylines that exist and turn them into history. Kunark is a sweeping change in what is available to players, and I hope that the impact of a new land is not only felt on Kunark itself. Perhaps a new port will open on Antonica? Let’s not have an immutable history, unchanging, but a story, which sweeps us into an evolving world and leaves us enthralled once again with the tapestry that is Norrathian history.

    Editorial by Oghma
