ISSUE # 24June 1st - June 5th

Yvette's Tower - Part 3

The cries of the lone sailor thrashing in the water were muffled by the sounds of the ocean and the noisy pirate crew. The five of them had snuck onto the deck and hid at the rear of the ship behind the boxes. They whispered to each other as to what they could do to retake control of the ship without destroying it or the box. Never before had such a rushed argument about battle tactics passed the lips of any of the participants save one, the older man, who's name turned out to be Morgan. Yvette could only hear the sounds of the man in the water, and heard none of the argument. She stared out through the gap in the railing at the sailor who was swimming back to the ship with what looked like his last ounce of strength. She recognized him as the larger of the cargo crew men, she smiled as a tear ran from her eye for once thinking he looked too soft to be a sailor, seeming more like a large child's toy with cheerful eyes. She knew he was too tired to swim to shore and the others probably died in the freezing water, he would soon too but was alive due to his weight. Her eyes met with his for a short moment, and a final sound came from him before he sunk beneath the surface, thrashing for a moment. She reached out for him, tears flowing from her cheeks. Her lips moved as she sent a prayer for his soul, and then she began to cast her spell.

Montgomery gently nudged her, asking if she was ready to attack the pirate group, but she continued to stare out at the black sea, crying silently to herself. Montgomery shrugged and murmured to the others "she must not have been as ready for this conflict as she made up to be, she is lost to us." The others then gave a two-count before moving for the attack.

Tale by Ian Ash, Fraterni Lux.
