ISSUE # 101

November 20th - November 26th


Myth Server


I'm sure that you are all aware of the three kinds of EverQuest servers that exist today. There are the standard, PvP and race war servers. Each with a different set of game rules, but with the exact same world history. On some servers you can attack only a few other player characters, on others you can attack nearly any other player characters. Oddly, with such dramatic rules differences comes absolutely no in game reasoning for the way things work. We haven't really heard the reason for the fact that some people in Norrath can't hurt each other at all, and some can. I wanted to outline an idea that I know many of you have thought of. I want to talk about a server where all PvP activity is based on religious factions. It is my goal here to break the world up into religious factions (sticking to the idea that there will be 'teams' rather than every god for himself), and to provide an in-game reason for such a thing.

Before I start, I want to give some credit. I want to thank the regulars (and irregulars - so you're included too, TwoHead) on the Avault newsgroup. They inspired this idea, and gave me feedback on the silly stuff that I did. I'd name them all, but then I'd leave someone out. It's going to be bad enough that I singled our TH. Hopefully there will be a few chunks of him left after the rage and frenzy subsides…

First let's tackle the game rules part. I'm not a mathematician, so my numbers may be flawed. And I welcome comments from readers about any mistakes in my math (just don't write to give me any grief about my spelling, grammar, life-style or character flaws - stick to the math).

I started out with a few assumptions about server population, and about the goals and desires that Verant has for any sort of team PvP server.

  1. There need to be relatively safe areas for new characters to hunt.
  2. Teams should be relatively even, not only in number of characters per side, but geographically as well.
  3. Role-playing 'hooks' are a good thing.
  4. Making sense in the world is an even better thing.

Reasonable assumptions, I think.

In order to meet goal #2, that teams should be relatively even, we need to know how many characters worship which gods. And because we want to keep track of the general location of these factions, we'll need to know how many of each race worship each god. Again, I make some assumptions.

  1. The race/religion chart that I stole from EQSS months and months ago is accurate.
  2. That players will, given a large enough population, choose characters in a method that approximates and even distribution. This would mean that if three people chose to play barbarians, they will each chose one of the three classes available to them as barbarians. And it means that if twelve people chose a race to play, they will manage to select each of the twelve races available.

With those assumptions I created the following table. I simply took the smallest number of characters that we needed to create for each race and distributed them over the chart. Since the barbarians have three classes, and the most diverse one has three religions, we needed nine new characters. For the Humans, with 13 classes and 14 religions available to the most diverse one (silly bards), we need 182 characters to get at least one to fall in each combination.

Based on this chart, and keeping in mind that we want to keep characters of the same race in the same group as much as possible, I created the chart below (last chart, I promise). There are four groups of gods, and worshipers of each could be set to be +PvP with followers of the others. Some races will have a few stragglers that appear +PvP to their own people, but only in small numbers and in cases where it would make perfect sense. I kind of like the idea that Teir'Dal followers of Bristlebaine would have to watch their backs in Neriak. A few races, Humans and Half Elves in particular, will be fairly well dispersed among all four groups. I consider it the nature of the beast, for their diversity will always cause strife. Several of the gods could conceivably be moved into nearly any group (Veeshan, for example), but it also turns out that moving her would have little effect since she has so few followers. The Erudites are scattered a bit as well, but having their homeland isolated should make this of little consequence to them.

The gods could be arranged in any fashion that makes sense to Verant, all I did was try to keep the races as much together as possible.

Oh, and some of the categories don't add up to 100%. Those are all rounding errors on the very small percentages, and really should have little bearing on the overall results.

For lack of any better labeling system, I've chosen to call the first group the Neutrals (Agnostic, Bristlebane, Brell and The Tribunal), the second group the Goods (Marr twins, Rodcet Nif and Quellious), the third group Nature (Prexus, Veeshan, Tunare and Karana) and the last one the Evils.

All the rules for combat and such that exist on the current team server would work just fine on the religion server. But because of the complicated nature of the division of the team, there would need to be a few changes as well. In order to reduce the 'attack on site' nature of PvP combat, it is important to make sure that there is no external indication of who is on what 'team'. There would be no tricks like making the character names reflect the 'team color' or anything like that. This should help to reduce the number of people that get mugged just outside their own city. It might also be a very good idea to change the way /consider works in regards to Player Characters. Perhaps all PCs that you might reasonably be able to group with (changing with level as well) con as 'even'. All other characters significantly below your level would /con as 'risky', and those higher would /con tombstone. Or perhaps it's even better to remove the ability to /con a PC altogether. While this would bring some difficult role-playing problems (concerning who to group with and how to express level in character), it would certainly force people to think before they act, and maybe even force people to 'size-up' someone before attacking them.

Along with the far more interesting world that rules like this would create on their own, there would be a tremendous increase in the potential ideas for quests, both player run and GM hosted. What happens if the King in Neriak finally listens to his advisors (all priests to Innoruuk) and decides to route all followers of other gods from the city? Imagine the quest generated by the thief lords as they hustle to prove their value to the King before hundreds of armor plated, spell wielding Voices of Hate come tumbling into their lairs to exterminate them! Or what if in Halas there arises a messiah of sorts that follows Rallos Zek? He talks daily about leading the people of Halas on raid against the Humans, as they had done in the not so distant past…

I am, however, aware that there could be problems on this server. Many of the folks that have read over this proposal have had concerns about the organization of the gods. All very smart people, and there's a good chance that their concerns are valid. For example, if you'll note the numbers to the right of this last chart, you'll see the totals, the results if 100 people were to play each race. You'll note that the 'good' group is the smallest. Not only that, but the group itself consists of gods that people are perhaps are less likely to choose. This seems to make the Goods the weakest group. However, they are also the ones most likely to find allies among the other groups (and the Evils the least likely, therefore their larger size isn't as great a benefit). Also note that for some individual worshipers, even their own town might be very dangerous. The high elf Magician that worships Solusek Ro, and I'm guessing that on every server there are perhaps five of this strange souls, will find himself without allies. A tough life, yes. Will that stop those five characters from being created? Perhaps. But I suspect those individuals will still exist. They were created for their uniqueness, and would be created for the same reason on a religion server.

But there's no way to know the truth of it until it's brought to the public. It would take too many testers to test a server-wide concept like this. And I've discovered from experience that testers are not the same group entity that players are. They do things differently, and choose things for different reasons. So even a six month beta test with a server full of testers running around would probably not tell us anything about how people will actually play the game; how they will group, what class and race they will play, or how they will treat the underdog (poor Agnostic Troll).

But there's one really nice thing about this system. It can change. If it turns out that the followers of Tunare really need to be part of the good group in order to even things out, or to prevent a very bad problem, then it can be done. In fact, it would make for a great event. There could be a falling out of the gods. Perhaps Tunare grows angry with the callous outlook that her allies take toward life on Norrath (and perhaps one of the Twins has been talking to her about the death of her sons…), and she decides to join with the good. Such fiddlings would be unfortunate, and would have to be very important, but not only could they solve some distribution problems, but they serve to make the world feel alive.

Oh, and the other thing this system needs, is a myth. I hacked the official history from the official EverQuest web site and came up with the following:


From the Dawn of Time to the Present Day: The History of Norrath on the Myth Server

It is said that outside of space and time an entity known only as the Nameless exists, and that this being created all that there is and was and will be. It is also written in ancient texts that from His mind sprang not only the universe and its countless suns and worlds, but also a myriad of sentient, powerful, yet finite creatures whom one such as a man, or elf, or dwarf, would call a god.

In aeons past came one of these gods upon the world Norrath. Veeshan, Crystalline Dragon and ruler of the Plane of Sky, found this world pleasing and deposited her brood onto the frozen continent of Velious. With one swipe of her mighty claws, Veeshan opened several great wounds upon the surface of Norrath, staking her claim to this promising new world. Dragons then walked the land and flew the skies, powerful beings of great intellect, wisdom, and strength. Thus began the Age of Scale.

In time the other gods noticed Veeshan's work, and being often petty and jealous beings, they too came upon Norrath, intent on leaving their mark. Brell Serilis was first, and from his Plane of Underfoot, a dark realm of vast caves and endless tunnels, he quietly created a magical portal to a cavern deep in the belly of Norrath. Through this portal the Duke of Underfoot seeded the depths of Norrath with all manner of creatures. Brell then returned home, sealing his portal within a labyrinthine chamber of mystical Living Stone.

And when the other gods came to Norrath, Brell Serilis approached each of them, and after some time convinced them to meet as one to discuss the fate of the world. The Great Mother Tunare, and Prexus, The Oceanlord were in attendance, and Rallos Zek, the Protector, was also there, his love for his wife Tunare drawing him from his home. Brell, carefully avoiding all queries as to the origins of his information, told of Veeshan's discovery of the new and potentially powerful world in which she had deposited her brood. Words befit of the King of Thieves poured forth from Brell's lips and he proposed that they accept an alliance of sorts, to which all agreed, ignorant of the fact that Brell had already released some of his creations into the Underfoot of this new world. The planet that would be called Norrath was divvied up between these beings of power for the purpose of keeping the Wurmqueen in check. Each would create a race of beings to watch over Norrath and keep a vigilant eye on the schemes of Dragonkind. Brell claimed the bowels of the planet and created the Dwarves, stout and strong, deep beneath the mountains of Norrath. In the abysmal depths of the oceans Prexus left his children, the Kedge, hearty aquatic beings of great mental power and stamina. And on the surface of Norrath did Tunare create the Elves, creatures of limitless grace and beauty, and Rallos Zek the Giants, fierce and formidable beings, intent upon the defense of their lands. Thus began the Elder Age.

During these great acts of creation Tunare conceived her first children. The twins, Tallon and Vallon Zek became very much the children of their parents. Both were strong warriors, like their father, and both were strong of will and heart like their Mother. Tallon was enamored of the sea as a boy-god, and spent much time with Lord Prexus and his children under the waves, or more precisely, among the waves. For it was the ever-restless nature of the water that interested him. It was his nature as well. As Tallon grew he became more and more restless. And he knew desire. He wanted to be like the elder gods. He wanted the things that they had. He desired to rule a land, he wanted to create a race.

Vallon lived with his father, among the Giants. Their influence on him as a youth made him steadfast and stoic. He was very much like his father. But he, like his brother, was born on Norrath, and at the time when great amounts of life energy were present. He too was restless. But Vallon learned to control his urges. He was content among the giants.

It was inevitable that such energies involved in seeding planets with life would attract even more of the gods, and it was the Elves who drew the unwanted attention of Innoruuk, Prince of Hate. In a decrepit tower overlooking the dark decaying alleys of the Plane over which he ruled, Innoruuk waited, stoking the fire of his Hate until it was a raging inferno. He cursed his fellow gods for not including him in their pact and vowed to make them regret such disrespect. From the halls of the Elves' fair city, Takish-Hiz, the Prince of Hate snatched away the first Elven King and Queen. In his realm of pain and anger he slowly tore them apart, physically and mentally, over the course of three hundred years. He then gathered the quivering remnants of these beings of light and rebuilt them into his own dark sadistic image, a twisted mockery of Tunare's noble children. In depositing the Teir'Dal, as Innoruuk's Dark Elven creations would come to be called, back into the Underfoot of Norrath, the seeds for The Prince of Hate's final revenge were sown.

Fizzlethorpe Bristlebane and Cazic-Thule came next to Norrath, and Brell met them, concocting a second pact with these latter gods, wishing another excuse to create more peoples into the world. Rallos Zek again watched from afar, not so interested in joining another such pact. Rallos Zek chose his sons to stand for him in the council, for he could sense that even Vallon was impatient to be recognized as one of the gods, and the Protector was content with his people, the Giants. Deep in the earth did Brell return to create the gnomes, resembling dwarves to some extent, yet more wiry and gnarled, consumed with tinkering with devices more so than their cousins. On the surface, away from Elves and Giants, Bristlebane made the Halflings, short and stubby folk, agile and with a propensity to meddle and even pilfer at times. Cazic-Thule, Lord of Fear, was drawn to the swamps and jungles of Norrath and there created the green skinned Trolls and reptilian Lizard Men. Vallon, fearing that even his hidden desires would come out during such a creation process, declined to create any children. His brother Tallon, falling under the sway of the power of creation and finally with a chance to express his boundless energy made the Ogres, massive, unmovable beings of questionable intelligence, as well as the Orcs, bred for battle and singled-minded in their desire for conquest.

By this time, those who are now known as the elder races of Norrath had begun to expand and mature. Villages became cities, and cities became kingdoms. Several of the races became bent on expansion and warfare became part of their developing cultures. And of all the races, it was the Ogres who quickly proved the most interested in battle and plunder, and their empire grew outward from their mountain home until it eventually encompassed a large portion of Tunaria, largest of the known continents. Their knowledge of magic grew, as did their greed, until they became weary of only Norrath. And when they learned of other planes and dimensions, they -invaded the Plane of Earth itself. Tallon Zek watched with pride as his creations challenged the gods of that realm, and when they eventually knew defeat, Tallon himself led a second invasion. The war that ensued shook the heavens and angered the greater gods. Through their combined might, and with the aid of his very own father Rallos Zek, Tallon was finally thwarted and forced back into the oceans, where he was accepted by Prexus and the Kedge, who had not become involved in the battle. And then, in what some view as spite and others justice, the gods cursed the Tallon's creations. Thousands of Ogres were slain and their empire collapsed around them. And the goblins were also cursed, but no writings remain of their punishment as they no longer keep records of their history (which is perhaps some indication as to the severity of their curse). Thus began the Age of Monuments.

As the dust settled, the last of the gods came to Norrath. Mithaniel Marr, god of Valor, and Erollisi Marr, goddess of Love, created the Barbarians, a hardy race who settled the cold and rugged northlands, near the insular Giant empire. Being the youngest race, they were generally unwashed and rugged, possessing very few social graces. And while they too had a warlike culture, there were those amongst them who began to believe in something more. By this time the other civilizations of Norrath had either long since declined, or were well on their way, and this small minority of Barbarians saw an opportunity to triumph where the others had failed. Perhaps this was a seed of wisdom planted by the Marr Twins, or perhaps it was only by chance, but as the Barbarians spread out across the lands, warring with both each other and any other race encountered, this tiny movement continued to grow. And so even amidst desolation and war, there was hope. Thus began the Age of Blood.

The last of the old races to decline was the empire of the Elves. The Elddar Forest spread across the entire southeastern quarter of Tunaria. Cities and villages built high into the trees housed thousands of Wood Elves, and the marble cities of the High Elves were built in the forest's clearings and meadows, their white towers and spires climbing out of the forest, higher than the tallest tree. But then came the last curse of the gods, as Solusek Ro, Lord of Flame, arched the spine of the serpent mountains, bringing heat from the burning sun to the ancient forest. The rivers ran dry, it rained less each year, and while the great elven druids fought long and hard, using their powerful magics to combat the change, they could only delay the inevitable. Slowly the forest gave way to desert, and eventually even great Takish-Hiz crumbled and the elves were forced to flee Tunaria, leaving much of their greatness behind. Thus began the Lost Age.

This next period of Norrathean history as it relates to many of the races is the most confused. Because the result of these events, even the most knowledgeable historians find themselves influenced to 'remember' things as their race remembers it. It is believed that while the elder races regrouped and reestablished themselves, a small group of Barbarians were suddenly transformed both physically and intellectually. Most that so little is known about this period is that the gods wish it to be so, deciding afterwards that they would have less to do with their creations. In any case, this small and enlightened group were the fathers of the Human race, and they rapidly gained a foothold throughout the lands, studying the lost art of geomancy. The Combine Empire, as this lost race of Humans is called, spread throughout the known world, but then died even more quickly than it grew, destroyed in the last battle of the gods.

Many gods resented the strength of the growing numbers of Humans. No god would accept them as their own, none would admit to creating them. And this left them vulnerable. As they stretched their domain, they came to find the lands of the Giants. The Giants were still strong in their homeland, having isolated themselves since the Age of Blood. But the Humans failed to respect their domain, and Rallos Zek was very protective of his it. When the first Humans stepped foot in the land of his people, Rallos struck them dead with his own hand. The Combine Empire had little reverence for gods, and they resented such an act. They took it upon themselves to find this god and destroy him. Bristlebane, having been among the Humans and somewhat enamored of them, knew of this plan, and carried the information to Rallos Zek. Rallos the Protector called upon all of the gods to help him in destroying the Humans, for they dared to threaten a god! At first, only his own sons came to his aid. And, in truth, that would have been enough. For the Giants were strong and the power of three gods is something more than even a great empire can withstand.

The Giants began to march from their isolation just as the Combine Army formed. The battle was tremendous, causing destruction to any land and people swept up in the battle. Armies of Orcs, led by Tallon Zek , once again free to fight with the Conquerors blood in his heart, led the Orcs to battle himself, destroying every Human in his path. Vallon Zek grew troubled. For as he fought beside his father and his cousins the Giants, he began to realize that the war would not end until every Human was destroyed. His heart was heavy with every blow he struck. Soon, he could no longer fight. He still felt the lust, just as strongly as his brother. But his great will had been set upon restraining that lust. Vallon convinced his father to end the war once it became clear that the Humans would not be able to invade the home of his people. The Giants returned to their homes.

But the Orcs and their wild-eyed god Tallon Zek would not stop. Vallon held council with him, in an attempt to stop the conquest. But nothing could convince him. The slaughter continued. Tallon called the remnants of his Ogres into battle to replace the Giants, and the Humans were hard pressed to survive, much less to hold their ground.

It was then that the Marr twins came to Vallon. They found him weeping at the sea, his tears falling among the waves that his brother loved so much. It became clear that Tallon had become a true god, the god of Conquest. It was then that Erollisi Marr named Vallon the god of Grief, for never before had a god wept. Even Prexus arose from his depths at the taste of a gods tears in his waters. He remained near as he heard the Marr twins offer hope to Vallon, and he heard the pact that they made with him. They promised to aid the Humans. And they sought others to aid them, for their Barbarians were few in numbers compared to the Orcs and Ogres. Prexus refused to fight against Tallon, who was as close to him as any son could have been. Others refused to become involved, siting the weakness of their peoples, or that they were unconcerned with the entire thing. But soon enough, the forces of Light were gathered behind a banner of Grief.

And Tallon was captured. He could not resist the power of the Elder gods that stood with his traitorous brother.

Upon hearing this, Innoruuk, always hopeful of opportunities to strengthen Hate in the world, went to Rallos Zek. He spoke with the Protector, and told him of his sons capture and unfair treatment at the hands of those manipulative twins. With some urging, he convinced Rallos to gather a following of his own gods to free Tallon from his imprisonment, and to free Vallon from the web that he was entangled in. It was a simple matter to interest some of the gods in this idea (Solusek Ro, Bertoxxulous).

So Rallos Zek appeared among the council of Light, with Innoruuk and the others at his side, and demanded the return of both of his sons. Vallon argued that he was with the Light by choice, and that they had rallied to his banner of Grief. The end of the war should be proof to all that what they did was right. Rallos Zek grew angry, but he would not strike against his son. He returned to his home.

But Innuruuk was not finished yet. With the aid of the others of the Dark, he trapped the Protector in his castle, and, with the assistance of Bristlebane, cast illusion about it so that the Protector would not know of the world outside. They then freed Tallon Zek from his imprisonment by devious means, and set him at the head of their army. They continued the war on the Humans, with the intent of destroying them completely. The Light fought them and the destruction in Norrath was tremendous.

Tunare was lost in all of this, for war and death made her weak, as such things destroy life and love. She tried to contact her husband, but was prevented by the same illusions that kept him mollified. She called for an end to the war, but few listened. Karana rallied to her side, having always been her ally. Together they, and a few others, fought to end the war, but could never do so without destroying more then they could heal. They took it upon themselves to protect the things that mattered to them the most, actively fighting any combatant that entered their protectorates. They forced both sides to constrain their fighting, and saved what of Nature that they could.

Others, like Prexus and Veeshan, refused to be involved at all, garnering the resentment of all three of the other godly factions. But they kept their own council, and their people were left alone for fear that they might enter the battle against anyone that would try to involve them.

This time of war raged on, until one day the banner of Grief and the banner of Conquest met. The two brothers stood and watched as their troops battled. Tallon began to strike down the High Elves and Humans around him. Vallon felt helpless, as he refused to strike down anyone. He, as always, stood there and watched the battle, tears streaming from his face. Tallon saw his brother weeping, and cursed him for his weakness. He called to him, and laughed at him. And his armies began to push back the enemy. The Trolls and Ogres shoved the Humans back, but Vallon stayed, refusing to yield to the Conqueror. Soon the two brothers stood face to face as the forces of Light retreated. Vallon felt the death of every creature on the battle field. His Grief was overwhelming. His brother, standing so close, could feel that grief as well. And he Hated it. Hate that he learned from Innoruuk, combined with the restlessness in his heart, and his desire to end the grief, caused him to strike out at his brother.

No god had ever struck another. It was just never something that any of them had ever thought to do before. Only this new god, one born in Norrath, could conceive of such a thing. And only such a god could actually make the concept real. The maul, stained with the life's blood of hundreds of creatures, broke the skull of Grief. Vallon Zek fell to the ground, and with a tremendous, silent gasp, he ceased to exist.

When the god fell, everything stopped. The creatures of Norrath stopped. The gods stopped. The wind stopped blowing. Rain held it's fall. Even the oceans ceased their rhythmic slapping against the land. Time stopped. In that moment, everything changed. No one knows how long that moment lasted, but when it was over, everything was different.

It was during that moment that the alliances became a fact of life. In that moment, all allegiances between the gods and their creations were broken, and reformed. Reformed within the boundaries of the alliances. Thus the power of life was weakened between each group of the alliance of gods, and the power to destroy each other across those boundaries increased.

And when that moment was over, time began anew. Rallos Zek appeared, free of his confusion, and took his son Tallon. They left together, and Tallon was never seen again. It is believed that Rallos Zek reclaimed his son, and returned him within himself. It is said that Rallos Zek devoured his own murderous child, so that he himself could contain all of his restlessness and lust. Rallos Zek scattered his people and destroyed their palaces and temples. He never wanted to be tempted to use them as weapons again. The Giants are forever beyond his reach, and forever without a home. But not even Rallos Zek, the Protector, could contain the Conqueror, for he has become the Warlord.

To this day the waters no longer dance. They lay as still as rock. Their master Prexus gathered the Grief of Vallon Zek upon his death. He could not retain it all himself, and ordered that the sea itself should hold most of that Grief. The waves no longer care to leap and play. And if you were to meet the Oceanlord himself, you would never see his tears, for his ocean takes them from him as he cries them.

Thereafter the gods built a great barrier to the Planes of Power, denying entry to both the lesser gods and mortals as well.

The Humans survived, but their Empire did not. And while they are the ancestors of every Human on Norrath and their relics and ruins still litter the lands from Odus to Faydwer, little or their history of this period remains.

After the fall of the Combine Empire, the remnants of mankind dwelled mostly in the center of Tunaria, inhabiting primarily the vast and fertile plains of Karana. Villages appeared and prospered, several reaching the size of towns, and two even became cities. To the west a strong and noble band of Humans, lead by Antonius Bayle the First, founded Qeynos under the lofty principles of law. Freeport, to the east, became an active and dangerous port of call for all who dared to venture into the Ocean of Tears. Humanity, much to the disdain of the elder races who watched from afar, remained strong, even daring to rename their home after one of their own instead of one of the gods. The great continent of Tunaria would forever more be known as Antonica. This is not to imply, however, that humanity was at peace. Competition was fierce, and when resources grew scarce for one reason or another many groups abandoned the promises and alliances of their past and fought. A few leaders spoke out against the violence, urging the masses to remember why they had fled the cold north. Others reminded them of their former glory and the might of the Combine Empire. These leaders insisted that humanity adhere once again to those principals to which all had agreed. Explorers and adventurers returned from afar with tales of elves, dwarves, and other strange creatures, as well as descriptions of ancient abandoned cities. A few even came back with limited knowledge of sorcery and the mystic arts. And when that discontent minority of leaders heard all of this, they became both jealous and determined.

A small, frail man of great intellect called Erud led this group, and he formed them into a council. They quickly became irritated, even disgusted, by their fellow man. Leaving a small network of spies behind, the remainder of Erud's followers fled the city of Qeynos and boarded a small fleet of ships. They sailed to the west and landed upon the barren coast of the island of Odus. The land was sparse and uninhabited and quite appealing to the council and their people. They quickly built a city of their own, dissimilar in almost every way to both Qeynos and Freeport, for it was almost entirely a towering castle. Erudin it was called, and within it the scribes and scholars, who called themselves High Men, gathered and analyzed reports, captured books and scrolls, and other artifacts brought to them by their spies. The first human mages since the Combine Empire were born - wizards, sorcerers, and enchanters occupied the great halls of Erudin and grew immensely in both power and knowledge. Thus began the Age of Enlightenment.

It came to pass some years later that a small group of Erudites discovered the lost art of Necromancy. They were branded heretics and great conflict arose. For the first time in several hundred years, the Erudites fought. They engaged in a civil war not entirely dissimilar to that which they had loathed and fled from back on the mainland. But there was one very significant difference - they did not use swords and bows, but rather magic, and the result was terrible. Lives by the hundreds were lost, great buildings and structures destroyed, and eventually the heretics were forced to flee Erudin, to hide and regroup in the southern regions of Odus. In one final battle, great mystic energies were released and an immense hole leading to unknown depths beneath the earth was created. Into the sides of this chasm the heretics built their own city which they called Paineel. And while both sides still seethed with anger and hatred towards one another, their fear of what the last battle had wrought has kept any further conflict at bay. Thus began the Age of Turmoil.

It is in this age you find yourself, an age filled with wonder. The elder races have begun to reclaim their former glory. The younger races have matured, and an active economy stretches across Odus, Antonica, and Faydwer. And while conflict and battle is hardly rare, it has also been centuries since open war has plagued the lands. A myriad of alliances and factions exist, friend and foe plot and scheme, and the world of Norrath is ripe for action. Equip yourself for adventure, seek allies and knowledge, and head out into a rich world of dungeons, towers, crypts, even planes and realities beyond your imagination. Learn skills, earn experience, acquire treasure and equipment, meet friends and encounter enemies. And whether you assume the role of a noble human knight, a vicious dark elf thief, a greedy dwarven merchant, or whatever suits your desire, remember one thing:

You're in Our World Now!
