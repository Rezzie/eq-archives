ISSUE # 31July 18th - July 24th

An Event

The famed Alarik proudly strode up to the arena. Clad in golden platemail, his green cape waved with every step he took. He was the first warrior to arive at the tournament it would seem. The only other person there was a mage by the look of him. Brown robed, wearing only a somber expression on his face.

"Hail. I am here to test my mettle with the best of them" Alarik stated, with a quick glance at the sharped halberd he was carrying.

"Warriors sit to the south" the mage told him.

Alarik walked around the arena to the seats to the south and waiting for the rest to arrive. One by one, warriors arrived, along with others coming to watch the small tournament. About 25 people in all, 12 of them there to fight. People quickly sat around the arena as Alarik waited for his name to be called.

"Once they fight, we kill both fighters." Alarik overheard from an odd group of 3 standing outside the arena.

"wont we lose noto?" One of them asked.

"No, they will both be flagged criminal for fighting".

"Oh..I C" said the second man.

From another one of the seats, there was a man who must have had a mental problem.

"MORTAL KOMBAT!" He would repeatedly say over and over. the noise was so loud Alarik could not hear what anyone else was saying, and barely see anything going on either.

The tournament was able to be organized after 45 minutes of trying to get people under control. The first two fighters stepped into the ring and went to battle. It was a back and forth battle, with neither of them having the upper hand. They had crippled each other to near death. The next hit by either of them would end the fight. Alarik watched, enthralled by the great match. Then, all of a sudden, there was the sound of two arrows, and both men dropped to the floor. The 3 men that had been talking before the tournament laughed and took off running.


If you have tried to attend or put on a roleplayed even in Ultima Online, you probably have had a similar experience to that. This is what happened when I attempted to fight in a tournament, just the other day in UO (Yes, I'm playing UO, but just because I'm not in the 1st phase of EQ beta) . That story isn't even a quarter of what went on to disrupt the event. We had spammers filling up the screen with "@@@@" just trying to lag people, others trying to loot the warriors as they lost matches, and other people talking about the Simpson's, Baseball, South Park, or anything else completely unrelated.

As hard as it is to understand, people will intentionally try to disrupt a roleplayed event simply for the sake of doing it. There was an old argument, probably still occuring on message boards, about if random player killers are trying to ruin the game. Of course the PKs say they are simply playing their style and not trying to hurt anyone else's fun, and the other side screams bloody murder at them (literaly). Well, I can't get into that discussion but, it is a fact that there are a lot of people that do intentionally try to disrupt things and ruin other people's fun. We, in the Everquest community seem to be pushing for a better environment for roleplying than past experiences, but we can't expect that we won't get our share of assholes. The fact that many of us have stopped going to the irc channel, #everquest, or checking the EQVault message board (No offense to WarDragon) show this. Even the monthly chats.."Breast size" and "Beer Helmets"? Although, they have stated they will be trying to get some deterents to that behavior in the game, can it really even be done?

There are a ton of ways to be obnoxious, and the irritators will find a way. SISA has promised to be quicker to ban people than UO for bug abuse, but I don't see any evidence to show they will do the same for people that aren't allowing others to roleplay effectively. And there are lots of ways for people to cause enough disruption to keep other people from getting into character.

The mentality of these people is hard to understand. A lot of them are young kids, and there is no way to keep them out of the game. They are playing on Daddy's powerful computer and paying with his credit card, and SONY will take their money as readily as they will take ours. There is no point in trying to talk sense into them, as I remember being that age myself, and the good it would have done. And that isn't meant as a knock at kids. I know there are quite a few good roleplaying ones out there. Yet, I can't imagine many grown men or women running around in the game, calling each other "Nigga", and "Byatch", or spamming the screen just for the sake of ruining a roleplayed event.

And I have seen the other end of the spectrum. I once played on a MUD based on Tolkien's Middle Earth, that did enforce roleplaying. When you created a character, you chose to be an Elf, Hobbit, Goblin, Good Human, or Evil Human. You started in the headquarters for your race, and you were instructed to first go to your superiour and do a task for him. I started out patroling an area for goblins. It was fun, yet quite a bit restrictive as your role was really set for you from the start. If you were a disruptive person, you were simply banned from the game. Of course, that MUD was free, so they didn't lose your business by banning you, but oh how I wish for that type of response to a disruptive person.

One thing I don't understand at all, is the reluctance to have any kind of seperate servers. The idea has been tossed around a lot, but game designers always say " No way". Setting a side a few servers for people that plan to seriously roleplay, or at least sign an agreement not to disrupt roleplaying in any way makes sense to me. If they break the agreement, then SONY would not have to lose their money, they could simply ban them from those servers. Many of us truely wish to experience what an Online RPG could provide in the way of roleplaying. Please allow us to do so.
