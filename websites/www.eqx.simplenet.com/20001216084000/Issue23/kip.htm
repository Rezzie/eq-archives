ISSUE # 23May 23rd - May 29th

Kip Goodleaf

The bartender gave Kip a knowing glance as the argument turned for the worse, drawing the attention of the few remaining patrons to the table in the back corner. The group of adventurers had settled in for a fair game of kings court with the halfling, and not even twenty minutes into the night there were harsh words in the tavern. The group seemed to be composed mostly of mercenaries and thugs, with the complimentary apothecary of Bristlebane to offset the dull collection of warriors. Kip Goodleaf was a regular at this odd tavern, The Broken Wheel it was called, and was quite pleasantly in the middle of nowhere. There were hardly any regular patrons at the tavern with the exception of traders who frequent the long road between cities. The bartender was a quiet man who liked his silence, and was known for the relaxing atmosphere in the bar. The only other person that could be counted on to be found in the tavern was the half elf groundskeeper, Corwin. As the other customers in the bar started to leave, fearing a fight at that table in the back, voices were raised to try and resolve the misunderstanding, lest they be kicked out of the bar. The apothecary was the voice of reason amongst the group, being a man of faith and good humor. The others of the group seemed ready for blood. One in particular, a warrior who was not at all ashamed that he was not good at gambling because he was much stronger than anyone else at the table, and was verbally making sure that everyone knew what copper was his.

Tale by Ian Ash.
