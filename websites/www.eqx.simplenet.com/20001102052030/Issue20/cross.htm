"The Cross of Elwin"--Excerpt

A sliver of light slid across the night sky. Phinehas smiled wryly as the star winked out of existence. Funny thing about omens – one could never figure out whether they bode good or ill. Nevertheless, he could not shake the feeling that his entire life revolved around the events of this particular night. If recent experience was any indication, he didn’t think his premonition too far-fetched. Phinehas reached into the small scrip at his side, slung crossways around his neck by a thin leather strap, and fingered the rough parchment that was its only occupant. He shivered and hurried along the narrow street, moving further into the shadows to avoid the light cast from nearby windows. It was only a crude drawing that he had hastily sketched with a piece of charcoal, but… the Cross of Elwin. He shook his head in wonder. The sage had seemed certain. If Durgon or his knaves even suspected… He left the thought unfinished. Portents aside, his best hope was to return to his room at the Hind’s Foot, retrieve the priceless artifact and catch the first ship for Freeport before the sage started wagging his tongue. Phinehas knew that, in spite of the carefully crafted tale he had spun about finding the drawing buried in the dusty boxes that passed for the library of a shrine just outside of Haggada, the sage had been suspicious. He had stuck with the truth for the most part when relating his discovery of the cross, only omitting his discovery of the actual artifact wrapped in the large piece of parchment he now carried in his scrip. Experience proved that the best fiction was based mostly on fact. He frowned as he reminded himself again that his visit with the sage had been a necessary risk.

Tale by Paul Schwanz.
