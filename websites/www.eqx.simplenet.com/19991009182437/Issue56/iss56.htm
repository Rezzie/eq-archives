A Druid's Duty, part 2

It was cool inside the hollow of the Great Tree. Druids lined the walls, casting spells and talking quietly, a few with shaggy wolves panting amiably at their feet. The ceiling rose up into the tree, disappearing into darkness over Aklar's head. The sap of the living tree pulsed in the walls, a flickering light that filled the place with a soft wavering glow. As always, when she came here, Aklar could feel the harmony of nature, the loving gaze of Tunare like a palpable touch on her skin. Here, it never seemed hard to live in peace, to do no harm. Here, she could remember what it had felt like the first time she heard the Tunare's call, that soft surging like the wind, like tree sap in spring, like the flow of a stream over rounded pebbles. It was impossible not to feel humbled and awed.

    Tale by Aklar

Editorials

Well, it's been an interesting week. There have been a few newsworthy items that I want to mention. The UO lawsuit was settled. The plaintiffs are dropping their case against EA. As part of that settlement, EA will donate $15,000 to the San Jose Tech Museum. You know what, I no longer care whether or not the lawsuit was a good thing. And heck, with a result like this, I doubt that it will have much impact on EA, much less on the entire industry.

    Editorial by Absor


When I read about the PK situation in UO and the complaints of role-players and those who want to be able to enjoy the game without fear of rampant killing, I also remember that those who are true PvP warriors are not those who run around killing newbies, they are the mighty bunch who relish a challenge only provided by defeating a human opponent. Running around PK-HQ, I found there are varying levels of feeling on that, ranging from a "survival of the fittest take no prisoners and kill anyone around you" mindset to hard core Role-playing PK's.

    Editorial by Oghma
