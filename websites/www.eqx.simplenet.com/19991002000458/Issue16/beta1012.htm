ISSUE # 16Apr. 4th - Apr. 10th

Beta Testing 101
Be suicidal. Don't be afraid to die. They will be resetting our characters throughout various phases of beta testing anyway. Jump off that cliff. Slide down the steepest hill. Climb every tree. This is the best way to find places you might get stuck. If we find them now, we're less like to get upset if we get stuck in the real game.

PK. Yes I know this is a heated debate, and some people do not like Player Killers and some wouldn't play the game without it. However, the bottom line is Player vs Player combat is an excellent way to test the balance between the races and classes. Even if you don't fight to the death, at least test your strength vs a character of similar level. If it turns out you consistently get your butt kicked, and others that share your selections do too, then the balance can be tweaked to help even things out. 		Share. Find the best equipment, and tell everybody! This may not be the best way to make yourself into a superior killing force, but we need to know if there is equipment that is definitely the best, and everyone will get. If so, the price must be right, or the stats modified. Everyone will figure it out eventually, but in beta testing, speeding up the game tweaks is beneficial to everyone. Most importantly, though, a variety of equipment and lots of choice will make the game more interesting and, of course, fun.

Experiment. Try casting every spell on everything you see. Can you heal the furniture you attacked earlier? Will the NPCs kill you if you cast an enchantment on them? Try out your skills in the same way.

Powerup! Ideally Sony will give beta testers big chunks of exp in order to test out the higher levels. Be sure to get as much exp as you possibly can. It may be a while before someone in the real game gets that high, but better to find out now if it works right. 		Work together. Get everyone online to crowd into the same Tavern. It might not smell so good, but maxing out areas and pushing things to the limit like this is a good way to test the stability of the system.

Be creative. Come up with your own ideas not mentioned here to try out. With all the beta testers thinking outside the norm, we'll be much more likely to catch the problems that will arise when the gates open and floods of newbies come streaming into the game. Remember, beta test is meant for this, and the real game is where the true fun begins. The most important thing to remember, fellow testers, is that the quicker we solve the problems, the quicker the game will open!
