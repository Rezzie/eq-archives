EverQuest E3 Report - Part 2

Combat

First, you select a target by clicking on it with your mouse. It displays your current target on the right margin. Pressing 'z' will toggle attack mode. Now all you need to do is move into range of your target, and combat will begin. When fighting woodland monsters such as snakes and bats you may have to chase them for a short time in order to get close enough to start combat. I would like to see that a rear attack gives you a free attack round, but currently the monster instantly turns around and fights back the same round. Now all you do is sit and watch your HPs to make sure you don't die. If you get low, you may have to run back towards the town, where the guards destroy any monsters that wander too close. Then after healing, you can strike out and try a different monster. 		There are some combat skills, such as Kick and Bash that give you an extra attack during combat. With an external camera, you will be able to see your character swing his foot into the monster to kick it. This means there are things you can do during battle, so it is not as boring as it sounds. Archery

Bows are pretty expensive, so newbies do not have the chance to try them out. But, I was able to get a demonstration of how archery works. Bows do not currently fire in attack mode, which means you need to click to shoot an arrow. I expect this will change. You do see the arrow fly through the air, but it is pretty quick. That means you wont be able to manually dodge an arrow just by seeing it in time to react. It will be based upon your skill at dodging, just like a normal attack. However, a moving target is harder to hit. The better the archer's skill, the better he will be at leading the target. I'm guessing that to be safe from archers, it is best to keep moving side to side and changing directions.
