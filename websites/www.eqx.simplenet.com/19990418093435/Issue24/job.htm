I Need A Job!

I know some of my articles are way off the mark regarding the theme of this magazine, but sometimes I feel compelled to let you know just what a geek I am, or share some experiences that I have had, you might benefit from. Some of you are getting ready to graduate high school, or graduate from college. I want to share with you some techniques I used to find a job.

The focus will be on a job as a MIS or Network profession, but you could really apply this to any trade. 		Creating Your Resume

The first impression I have of you will come from your application / resume. Your resume should be short, no more than one page. Most of you will not have much experience to write about, so I recommend you stick to this format.

1. Your personal info
2. Your goal statement
3. Skills
4. Hobbies
 Your goal statement (#2) is very important. This is a one-paragraph explanation of what you want to accomplish by working for my company. Below is my goal statement. Notice the use of "growth". Use phrases like "high growth", "high profits", "maximize value", innovative and entrepreneurial".

Goal Statement

To find a position that promotes continual growth in Intranet networking, so that I may support that position with my growing networking experience.
