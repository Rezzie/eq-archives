ISSUE # 17Apr. 11th - Apr. 17th

The Bridge

The party had penetrated two levels of caverns before they ran across the Gnolls. It had first been a couple of sentries, then a room full of drunken Gnoll warriors, and finally had culminated in a pitched battle in a large cavern which had looked to be a training ground. During the battle, Arthedain had been separated from his companions, and had to flee for his life. Unable to find a way up towards the surface, he had gotten lost trying to find some way to leave this hell-hole which may very well soon be his tomb. Rounding the corner, he came to a huge cavern with tattered hanging banners and a bridge over a small stream. As he entered, he decided to pause for breath and check behind him. Panting, he rested against the wall and looked down the corridor he had just came from. As his breathing slowed, Arthedain was able to listen and hear the barking growls the Gnolls made as they searched for him. It seemed like they were getting farther away, the baying call of the hunt receded as if they had taken another way, perhaps even lost the track. Looking across the cavern, he saw a pair of blue posts which looked like a gate of some sort. The same sort of posts were part of the bridge which went over the stream. Perhaps the "gate" was some sort of exit of significance, maybe leading to stairs or a corridor which led up. Arthedain jogged over the bridge towards the gate.

Tale by Oghma, Fraterni Lux.
