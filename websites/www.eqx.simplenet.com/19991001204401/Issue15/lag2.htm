The Lag Monster

"Ok. So you had lag. What the heck is it".

Games on the Internet use TCP and UDP datagrams to carry information from the ‘Server’ to the ‘Client’ (the client being your home PC). Lag is the interval between the time a piece of data leaves the server, travels across the Internet cloud, and arrives, and is processed or reassembled on your PC. Let us follow this process from start to finish. I’ll try to keep it simple, so bare with me. 		You just loaded EverQuest and installed the game. YEA!!!!! (hey it’s my story I can dream if I want to!) You hit the connect icon. Hopefully by now you have already connected to your Internet service provider or ISP. This connection between you and your ISP is one hop. The Internet is full of these hops to get from one destination to another.
