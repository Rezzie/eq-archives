ISSUE # 24June 1st - June 5th

EDITORIAL

Welcome to Issue 24!

Hello again. Sorry that EQX went up late this week. We had some unforeseen problems, and the editor had some other obligations this weekend.

Recently, from what I hear, Chaosium decided to reclaim their world (Glorantha) from the clutches of Avalon Hill, to whom they sold the game several years ago. It's a pretty convoluted situation, but from what I understand Avalon Hill has rights to the game system (the Universal Roleplaying System, arguably one of the best ever created), but Chaosium still has rights to the game world and history of Glorantha (also arguably one of the best ever created). I am also quite certain that the world of Glorantha was a beloved thing by its creator, Greg Stafford. Now he, and his company, Chaosium, are creating "Hero Wars", the Glorantha RPG. There was a convention locally last weekend where they showed off the new game system that was to accompany the old game universe. Our GM was there…

And he said, to paraphrase, that the new system sucked. Now usually I take this with a grain of salt, after all, he hates everything. He's never liked a movie, never loved a book. So a remake of his favorite world and his favorite game system could never make high marks. But we talked about it a bit, and it seems he is right. They tried to create a system that did not intrude on the story telling aspect of the game (what White Wolf did so successfully). But they took it too far. They went beyond an easy to use system all the way to a nearly non-existent system (what someone did with the Amber Diceless Game). No matter how simple the system, there needs to be some depth to the rules, some clear method to proceed from action A to result B. They seem to have failed to do that.

By now you are wondering, as I suspect you usually do a few paragraphs into these editorials, what this has to do with EverQuest. Not much, unfortunately. Except that there are two factors that make any game, the system and the world. This applies to EverQuest as much as to Glorantha.

In EverQuest we will be given a world with history. We will be able to draw upon that history to create our characters and to help us find our way in the world. If that game world is half as complex and well thought out as Glorantha (though I admit that Glorantha has been built over a dozen or more years, Norrath won't have that luxury) then I will be ecstatic. I would encourage the designers to open the creation of the history of Norrath to interested and talented folks on the net and expand it as much as possible before the release of the final. An in depth world history can encourage folks that want to role-play, and inspire those that have never done it before. What always inspired me about the Gloranthan mythology was the stories about individual heroes. Small details of the worlds history that rarely, if ever, have any bearing on the current game, but always give you some insight into the ways of the world and how and why things happen. While I have been very firm about not posting URLs in EQX, I want to encourage all you role-players (and Sony design staff) to check out the world of Glorantha. You'll not find a more original and complete game world anywhere.

But the sticky part here is the system. What character statistics matter for what kind of actions (or what statistics are there in the first place)? What types of characters exist (will there be classes like D&D and EQ, or a classless system like UO and the old Glorantha)? How does a character gain in power (levels or a skill increases as it's used)? Stuff like that. The old system used for RuneQuest (Glorantha before it was sold to Avalon Hill) was very much like UO in its system. There were no "classes". Each character could learn nearly any skill or magic. EQ will be more like D&D. A level system. I like the system used by RuneQuest better than D&D. But I like the D&D model better for an on-line game like EQ.

Hmmm… too many acronyms in that last paragraph. I suspect I lost some of you in there. It boils down a few simple things. A careful examination of the mechanics of the game system is important. A level based system will work best in an on-line world because, while harder to balance, it is easier to create a diverse world where all the characters are different and they work together to get things done. On-line there is no GM to say, "No, Bob, you can not be a fighter/mage, we already have 5 in the party…"

Anyway, check out Glorantha. And when Hero Wars comes out, check to see if they have adopted the rules system that we are creating for them, even thought they don't know it yet...

   Absor
