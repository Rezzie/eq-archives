I tried to stop myself, I really did. But..I..just..can’t..help..it. This week’s clue is going out on the topic of, you guessed it, gazongas.

There was a 42-car pileup over at Lum’s this week, and like every other moron on the road, I had to slow down to look. The accident started out innocently enough, but before it ended, it had involved 6 bald guys in hot sports cars, 2 cattle trucks full of dairy cows with enormous udders, twenty-six teenaged boys driving without a license, half the cast of Cirque de Soleil, eight big-haired bimbos headed out for some hot two-stepping action, and a Shriner car full of evil midget clowns. I skipped around the threads reading various posts and groaning, laughing or dozing off in all the appropriate places. I thought the accident was over and the towtrucks were moving in. I was wrong. The official emergency vehicle finally showed up, but the driver just couldn’t stop in time. He crashed into 18 of the other cars, starting a domino reaction. The guy is currently in stable, but serious condition. Here it is in all its gory splendor.


Here’s what happened. Apparently Asheron’s Call added a new piece of woman’s clothing to the game, and it was a real cowsuit - something you have to tuck yourself back into every time you bend down to loot a critter. Some woman named Lietgardis wrote a rant about it for Lum’s site. And then the world exploded. Just kidding. Kinda. Lietgardis’ issue is at least worth a mention…I dunno if it’s worth a whole rant, but at least I understand her issue, which is more than I can say for the majority of the posters. What IS worth a rant, however, is the official response to it, which has to be the WORST official response to any gaming issue I’ve seen in at least 10 years. Barely anyone understood the rant for the first 15 pages of posts, because when it comes to computer games, people tend to get their knickers so twisted up that it cuts off the supply of blood to their brain, so here’s the rant, paraphrased and shortened, in non-game terms. Everyone remain calm and stand behind the yellow line till the short bus comes to a full stop.

I happen to be a woman, and one of my favorite recreational things to do is to go out for dinner. I go out for dinner with my friends maybe 4 times a week because I like not having to cook or do dishes after working all day. But I live in a VERY small town and there are only three restaurants here. One of them is Hooters, where all the waitresses wear horrible bright orange polyester hot pants and t-shirts that are 3 sizes too small and bounce energetically up to your table. (I think the Jell-O company owns a major share in this chain). One of them is a theme restaurant based on Comedy Central’s The Man Show, and features “Juggies” jumping up and down on trampolines as entertainment. The third one is a nudie bar, but they’ve got a pretty decent chef there. I’m not crazy about these places. Oh, they’re ok once in a while, but hell, what’s the attraction for women in a restaurant featuring naked women? There’s nothing there for ME to ogle, and there’s little chance for decent dinner conversation in a place like that. But because the food is ok, and because I like to go out to dinner, I go to them. I’ve got no choice. I guess the majority of the customers must like them pretty well, because they seem to do a good amount of business. I notice that most of the customers are young guys, some of whom are really loud and rude, but there are some other people there like me, just eating dinner and trying to ignore the comments and the occasional interruption in their meal when one of the main attractions slips its halter and flops into the soup.

One day a new restaurant opens up in my town and my friends and I go in for dinner, and I’m surprised to see that the place doesn’t feature scantily clad staff of either sex, and the décor is interesting, and well, it seems to actually be about food and conversation. I think this is great, it’s a huge relief and I begin to patronize this restaurant almost exclusively. The food’s not quite as good, and the place doesn’t have as many patrons as the other ones, but the regular crowd there likes it quite a bit. The manager tells me that he thinks it’s important that people have a place to go eat dinner that doesn’t have tatas on the menu, and I’m glad to hear it. One night my friends and I go in to dinner, and the place has changed. Most of the female staff members are now wearing babydoll t-shirts, and they’ve started selling them as well, up near the cash register. My reaction might be best described as - “Awwww, crap. Here we go again.” I ask to speak to the manager and voice my complaint and my concern that he’s blown it, he’s sold out, that there is no longer one restaurant in town where a woman can go for dinner that isn’t a boobfest. Here’s what the manager says to me in reply:


(Note: The manager in this case is Sean Huxter, the artist who designed the infamous Daemondress. Translation of his reply into the restaurant analogy is courtesy of yours truly.)


I understand Leitgardis' original post, its entire intent, and her feelings about the dress' addition into her safe environment.

I understand your point, and I know you really looked forward to eating in a restaurant without someone shoving her knockers in your face every 10 minutes.

I do NOT agree with it.

I don’t think you have the right to care if someone shoves her knockers in your face every 10 minutes.

I play almost exclusively female characters. Yes. It's true. I just happen to LIKE women more than men. Playing a female character is NOT some bizarre manifestation of some latent homosexuality, nor some cry of need for a mother I never knew or any such nonsense. I just like women better than I like men. (Sorry guys!)

I like to pretend that I’m a woman sometimes. This does NOT make me gay, not that there’s anything WRONG with being gay, some of my best friends are gay. I like women better than men. This means that I’m qualified to tell you what women are entitled to get pissed off about.

So we designed four dresses. We did three conservative dresses for the women who, while wanting to look female and feminine, did NOT want to look overtly sexual. Then we tossed one in for those who did.

And let me make this clear. THERE ARE THOSE WHO DID!

To eliminate this choice from them made no sense to me, just to ensure that the women playing who did NOT want to be taunted were secure in their non-sexual roles. Please. Go ahead and continue to be secure in your non-sexual roles by not wearing that particular dress.

We hired some waitresses from those other places and they complained that their tit..err..their TIPS weren’t as good here. If you are against this change, you are depriving them of their CHOICE to shove their tits in patrons’ faces. If you don’t want to buy the T-shirt at the register, then DON’T buy it and then you won’t have to worry about anyone making rude remarks about YOUR tits.

I also highly suspect that BECAUSE of this thread and its uproar, more people in-game are now more likely to approach someone in that dress and say "Hey, nice tits." I seriously do. Because they know they will be upsetting someone. Those people exist. Sad but true. Any hint of controversy brings them out. And this is one STINKER of a controversy - over nothing.

And now our patrons will ALL be commenting on everyone’s boobs and it’s ALL YOUR FAULT. If you hadn’t said anything, nobody would have noticed.

Somewhere recently it a great quote came to my eyes "A Puritan is someone who has a deadly fear that somewhere, someone is having fun."

This "tempest in a D-Cup" as my colleague so humorously put it -- you really should get to know this guy, he's a hoot! -- will blow over.

AC was designed from the ground up to be a game that is friendly to women. We actively seek that under-represented market. Yes. We do. We did NOT do so by just wrapping it in a pink box. (Which seems to work wonders for Mattel)

We designed this restaurant to be friendly to women! Why just look at our well-endowed waitresses. They’re women, and all our customers are very friendly towards them! We added a LOT of tits to our décor so all you women would feel right at home, cause you have tits too. We COULD have made the restaurant appeal more towards women by having it professionally decorated, and some people have had a lot of luck with this approach, but since I dress up as a woman, I know what you women really want and that’s jugs, and lots of ‘em.

The inclusion of the dress was another step FOR women. Not against them. WOMEN want this dress. For every leering testosterone-overcharged idiot who makes a snide comment about this dress, I'm willing to bet there are far more females who will gladly wear it and smile.

We did this for YOU, you ungrateful bitch. You are obviously a lesbian, since REAL women show their hooters whenever you ask them to and they LOVE It. Look at them all smiling.

You are one selfish bitch. 80% of the people are getting all the choice they want. They have FOUR places they can go for dinner and jugs now. So what if all four of the choices are titty bars? Nobody’s saying you can’t have a choice.

Am I putting words in your mouth? Yeah. But I'm simply paraphrasing your argument as I see it. And as others are apparently seeing it. Your original intent is lost within your reasoning and follow-ups. As I stated before, I fully undersand your original intent, but your words have belied it. There seems to be something far more deep and emotional about this dress that distresses you, not just the fact that you think it's going to pollute your safe environment.

I can’t see any logical reason why a woman wouldn’t want to spend all her time in a place that’s wall-to-wall tits. And all these other people agree with me so I must be right. So we’re going to try to make you explain to us why women get tired of hanging around with tits, which is a clever move on our part because then we can call you a selfish fat bitch, a lesbian, and a commienazipinko, and then make fun of you when you complain about it.
