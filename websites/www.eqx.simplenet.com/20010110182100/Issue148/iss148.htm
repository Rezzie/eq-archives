Editorial - Oghma

Recently I started a new character. It’s actually been a long time since I’ve made a new character, despite my recurring bouts of Multiple Character Syndrome. I haven’t played a lot at all really. I’ve been busy in real life, and I also have sort of lost the desire to play. I’ve been playing EQ since July of 1998, and while I haven’t gotten a character to level 50, I have a fair amount of characters at a decent level. I’ve had bouts of EQ burnout before, but this has lasted longer than my occasional week-long hiatus from Norrath.

    Editorial by Oghma

Letters to the Editors

The biggest thing AD&D does that quite a few folks find threatening is force people to think for themselves, and actually use their minds to solve problems, rather than having someone else take care of everything for them. I know of very few RPGers that suffer from the "The world owes me a living" mentality, in my opinion, because they've had it demonstrated to 'em in a fantasy setting that their actions directly lead to a end result, be it good or bad.
