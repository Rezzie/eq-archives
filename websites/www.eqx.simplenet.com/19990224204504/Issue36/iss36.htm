The Paladin

Paladins capture our imagination for many reasons. The idea of a selfless hero, a champion of the downtrodden, a holy warrior using his/her martial gifts to overcome evil, and protect the weak is an icon which brings us hope and inspires courage. Where did this ideal warrior come from? The paladin is not a typical hero, because there is an added element of selflessness and piety that a Conan or Odysseus does not have. The paladin is above all a hero above reproach, whose dedication to lofty ideals places him/her beyond the reach of the typical hero.

    Article by Oghma


The Elf-Storm

Sometimes, without much warning, a storm will brood and form its sifting self above the ocean, then sweep inland and loose its load upon unsuspecting travellers. Sometimes, the storms and weak, and hold little danger in their watery bodies. Sometimes, they are strong, and force creatures of the land to hide, and wait for the sun's return. Sometimes, they are magical.

    Tale by David Penley


A Long Rant on Online Role Playing

Role Playing meets the high tech computer game industry. Can the relationship work?

It works in text based MUDs. That feeling. You are in a social environment with friends and making new ones, as well as meeting people you might never see again, every day. It is often busier than a major city mall. People like to hang out and do nothing some times, while others are goal oriented. The setting is perfect for role playing.

    Article by Ian Ash


Dark Nights

Trynlor landed quietly on the rooftop. He was up and running the moment his feet touched the roof. He ran to the edge of the building and leapt onto the roof of the neighboring building, his thick black cloak fluttered behind him as he flew threw the air. It was long past midnight, and those that were out were either guards or drunks who couldn't find their way back to their homes. Trynlor paid little attention to any of them. He was on a mission, 4,000 platinum were riding on Trynlor's success tonight...
