A Look Inside Monks

Many role-playing games offer the choice to be a monk. They are typically great fighters with no equipment. But historically, I had always pictured monks as quiet holy men who spend much of their time meditating and the rest of their time transcribing books with beautiful penmanship and artwork. So, how did the monk come to be the elite martial artist of RPGs?

Greywolf Ridge - Part I

Chapter 1 - Kyanar Tavern

Dra'Ka pulled back the reigns on his horse and surveyed the landscape in front of him. The sky was a dim purple and the moon hung lazily in the air. Storm clouds drifted off to the west slowly. Sharp mountains shot up at the moon, their bent crags looking like some odd teeth. In front of him was a bleak forest, full of gnarled trunks and limp branches strung with leaves. The path was muddy but by the tracks in it he could tell he was near a town. The trail wove in and out of the foreboding forest, winding off into the shallow fog. He breathed in the air, which smelt of dead wood and water.

Upheaval

I awaken from my sleep. A strange sense of solitude fills me immediately as I open my eyes and look straight up. I realize I'm still laying under the branches of a medium-sized Yew tree. A slight bit disoriented, and very stiff, I gather myself and look through my belongings. All is intact, but something is noticeably strange. I notice the rumblings of the clouds above, looking almost like rain. The coulds form a most foreboding pattern as lightning traces the clouds. I try to recap my events.
