Letters to the Editors

I read quite a few of the articles you linked to on the web and they are mostly filled with conjecture, false assumptions, and bible quotes taken completely out of context. These people make money, make a living, at pointing out a common enemy and directing their followers derision toward it, rightfully or not. Freemasonry is also a common target of these people, despite the good they do for children and other charities.

However, while D&D and other FRPGs, rock music and fictional storires do not directly cause a person to become a satanist, someone with a weak mind, easily led or controlled, can take the information within these games and stories and try to incorporate it into their real world. It gives them a direction. Then again, it could have been "I Love Lucy" or the game Monopoly that directed their will.

I actually do know a person who became a self-professed Satanist, claiming that D&D "showed him the light." The way he explains it however, is that Satanism is not Devil Worship, his belief is that the vices of life are placed here for a reason, for us to enjoy to their fullest, then at the end, to return to the fold as did the prodigal son. Another example of taking a bible quote out of context, and just as foolish as the D&D detractors.

The point is, while it isn't the fault of D&D, D&D can give a real life direction to people with weak minds, and those people's parents are responsible to ensure they are not exposed to something that would affect them so. The problem is the parents would rather place the blame than take the responsibility.

Kanid of Povar
Christian and avid roleplayer
