Walking in the Light - the story of Indreju Drochnatiar - part 2

The booming sound of thunder overhead let him know that a storm was coming, but the flash of light a few yards to right let him know that it was close. Very close. Indreju cursed at the torrential rain that immediately followed, counting his luck even worse for a night that had already gone bad. His pursuers had yet to leave him; mounting horses themselves, and now if he managed to escape these men he would need to sleep outside in this rain.

Slowly he was managing to gain more distance because his pursuers had had too much to drink this night, but surely they knew the area around their home town better drunk then he did sober. To top it all off, he must return and for his crystal figurine of Fennin Ro. He could not risk loosing his new abilities just after he gained them. To be the first Elven Paladin in all of known history had not been an easy task, and he vowed not to let a group of drunkards get him in a bad standing with his god. While contemplating how on Norrath he was going to get back into the Wayward Traveler to get his figure, he realized that he had not paid enough attention to where he was going, and was lost in a small clearing. 		Looking up would do him no good, the heavy clouds prevented him from seeing the stars, and the rain kept his sight to a very limited range, even for an elf. Another deafening thunderclap overhead made it so that he did not hear his pursuers on the other side of the trees, and a small group broke out. There were only three of them, and they were drunk, but he did not want to give the others a chance to find them and help. They all drew a weapon of some sort, one had a mace, another an axe, and the third a sword. They did not wait for a formal introduction as two of them moved off to the sides to try to get at Indreju from three different angles. Resigning himself to a fight, he drew out his long sword, and made a prayer to his god Fennin Ro.

Trying to decide if it was better to try to flee on horseback, or get down so he could fight, he had a strange feeling to close his eyes. Just after closing them, lightning struck the ground all around. From the screams coming from his left and right, and the smell of burnt flesh, Indreju knew that two of his combatants had died to the hands of lightning. He opened his eyes to see the third attacker, the one with the sword, was rubbing his eyes to try getting his vision back. He spurred his horse forward, past the attacker, and slapped the swordsman’s horse on the rump to get it to move. 		Taking advantage of his newfound luck, Indreju ran his horse past another group of attackers, back towards town. If the storm would only hold up, he might just be able to get back to the Inn and get his figure of Fennin Ro tonight.

With the help of the wild storm that was throwing lightning bolts out of the sky every few seconds, he managed to make it back to the Inn with only one other minor incident. Coming around to the front of the inn he leapt off his horse and crashed through the front doors.

"Sir you can’t bring your sword into my Inn!" Screamed the innkeeper, as he started to come out from behind the bar.

Indreju drew his sword and turned on the man; "I shall wear this sword where I choose! I let you take it off of me, to find that you do not keep your Inn’s rules consistent with all of your customers. Besides I only came to retrieve something of mine." Either from the fire in his eyes, or the sharp ringing of the steel coming free of it’s scabbard, the innkeeper backed down.
