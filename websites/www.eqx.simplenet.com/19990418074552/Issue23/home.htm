Home Alone

I have the whole weekend to myself. My wife took the kids to Virginia to a wedding. This was to be a weekend of total self-indulgence! I woke up this morning feeling fresh, ready to take on my first day of solo geekness. I get my first cup of coffee and head off to the computer. I sit in front of my computer browsing through all the file directories looking for a game to play. I load Dungeon Keeper, and play it for about five minutes, quickly loosing interest. I then load StarCraft. I enter a Ladder game and after about fifteen minutes of that I’m again bored to tears. This repeats with about five other games, and I give up in disgust, loading up IRC to watch the mindless chatter in #Everquest. 1. Mech. Warrior
2. Diablo
3. Ages of Empires
4. Dungeon Keeper
5. StarCraft
6. Total Annihilation
7. A Bridge too Far
8. HellFire
9. Might & Magic VI
10. Magic The Gathering
11. Tomb Raider
12. Worms 2
13. Quake II
14. Die by the Sword
15. Links 98
16. UO (which I canceled)
17. Forsaken
 That’s $700 dollars worth of games that I cant even find myself wanting to play anymore! Actually I have to give some credit to Blizzard. I did play Diablo for over a year, and I can still find myself starting a character from time to time when I’m in just the right mood.

What do game developer’s need to do to keep our interest in a game, especially a game that they wish us to keep paying online services fees for.

What do I need to do to stop shelling out so much money on games I loose interest in less than a week?
