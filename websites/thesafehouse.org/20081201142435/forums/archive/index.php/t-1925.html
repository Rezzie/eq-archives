Perhaps that's what makes them such good games, and what makes video games one of the best mediums for storytelling.

There's no grand badass who will undoubtedly save the world, but a bunch of different vagabonds, strangers, valorous warriors, who come together and kick ass. The choices you can make in game to shape what happens greatly improves upon stories, and drags you into and involves you in it, making you care more. And the ability to almost completely shape events as in Infinity engine games (your own character class, party makeup, and play style effects things as well - though it doesn't always alter the storyline, it changes the gameplay, which is part of the storyline in a game.)

Trying to tell the story of a group of 14 adventurers in a novel would be extremely hard. You do things in a videogame you can't do in a novel. The reverse is true, but you are free to focus less on painting visuals, and you can add in content and backstory up to your eyeballs that people can freely access without disrupting the plot. (That old geezer in the corner of the Monument to All History of the Planes who you can go talk to about ancient_power03 at any time would need a specific place in a novel to fit.)
