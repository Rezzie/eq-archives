Yes, rogues can learn unarmed combat style, even now.

Fricka, I have a suggestion or two that I would like for you to pass along.

It seems to me that rogues need to fill the role of the 'utility melee' class. Along with the damage ability rogues currently have, they also need to be able to either negatively affect enemies in some unique way or positively affect allies. I would suggest that rogues be given a finishing move that is an attack speed slow, unique to the class, that would make them much more viable. The mechanics could be set up to be similar to both slice and dice and cheap shot, where the higher the number of combo counters, the longer the slow lasts, and a single monster cannot be slowed more than 3 times by a single player. Also, require that this finishing move be performed from the rear. This would make the skill much more group-friendly, and still viable for solo thanks to Gouge, but not overpowered. This also provides stackability for both other rogues and for warriors and their Thunderclap skill.

Another idea, probably not as well-thought-out, but worth mentioning would be the exchange of the Backstab and Ambush skills for Sneak Attack and Stealth Attack - instead of adding damage to a single attack that takes place off the timer, Sneak Attack would add a "buff" to the rogue for 6 seconds, with a 10-second cooldown, that would cause all attacks during that window to do 150% damage. This would allow a great deal of variety between Rogues and make both fast and slow weapons viable for different types of sneak attacks. Stealth Attack would be the same, but cause 250% damage and only be usable from stealth (and would break stealth when used). Currently there is a great deal of controversy about Swords and Daggers, and their usability for Backstab - some feel Rogues should not be able to backstab with swords, due to their higher damage. However, others feel that restricting rogues from using anything but daggers in their mainhand is a poor idea. I think that the beta period would be an invaluable time to test such an idea.
