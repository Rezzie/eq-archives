01-07-04, 06:39 PM
SWG at present has about 4-6 months worth of enjoyment if you get over the initial learning curve, then you realise it's a vacuum and quit.

That said, the post-release dev team (quite different from the original dev team) has just finished adding the features that were missing at release.

Now they have a chance to add content and make the Galactic Civil War meaningful. I was in beta and played five months after release, but I believe it will take until mid-2004 for them to achieve anything more with this game. If they do it well I might go back, but I'm not paying for an account in the meantime.

I got to know an excellent SWG PvP guild led by a former officer of a top-5 EQ guild. Being smart people they have set up a very smart player city with defenses that actually work, outsmarting the bugs. Like me they are tired of the game in its current state, but they have something pretty amazing going and if the devs deliver on the GCW (e.g. add AO-style land control with meaningful benefits for victory, beef up the Empire and give incentives for participation) I'd love to see what happens.

Right now, SWG is a great preview of the EQ2 engine (look at the screenshots, it's SWG with orcs).
