Alternity was skill-based. You abilities determined not what your armor class is or how much damage you deal, but how easily and what magnitude the success of a "Unarmed Combat - Power Martial Arts" or "Acrobatics - Dodge" skill check would be.

Nenjin, why don't you just tie matrix physical prowes to character level?

You can jump levelX10 feet without fail at will.

You can punch through levelX2Xinches of concrete at will.

You can hold your breath for levelX2Xminutes underwater before losing conciousness and drowning.

These happen without fail, no check rolls. As someone gains levels, they obviously gain inner belief in their abilities, letting them do more. Until they get to level two, they *know* that they can't jump that 15 foot space. If they try, they go splat, no die roll. Stupidity leads to character creation.

Anything you don't want left to chance, tie directly to character level in some aritmetic (or non-arithmetic) progression, and make it every clear to your players that the limits on those abilities are not up for negotiation.

As characters level, you can even give them new abilities. Starting at level 4, you can fly for level^(1/2) (square root level)X10 minutes. Or (level-3)X10 minues.

Think about what types of physical abilities you want your characters to have, define them, and then make them happen without fail, with strength/intensity/duration tied directly to level.
