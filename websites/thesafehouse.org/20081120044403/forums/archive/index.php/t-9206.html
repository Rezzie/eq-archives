Last push (beta phase 1) you could backstab with a sword if you had a dagger in your main hand (since with duel wield it meant you could backstab with a sword because duel wield was a random chance of hitting with your offhand). This is incorrect. Last push, you could backstab with a sword every time if you had a sword in your main hand and a dagger in your offhand, as backstab only checked to see if you had a dagger equipped at all, not whether it was in your main hand, whereas the backstab damage calculation used the damage of whatever was in your main hand for its actual damage. This could cause some rather interesting effects, since as long as you had a dagger in your offhand, you could backstab with anything, including items that weren't weapons. In testing this, I backstabbed things with:

my fists
a torch
a held item
a zero-damage flaming mace used for the Morbent Fel Quest

Things got particularly interesting with the mace of holy flame, since it technically counted as a melee weapon, too. Which meant you could sinister strike with it.

This current push, the code governing fighting move restrictions is very buggy. For example, if you have a sword in your mainhand, and a dagger in your offhand, you can use sinister strike, and other melee-weapon-required special attacks. However, you cannot backstab. So, swap the weapons, right? Sword in offhand, dagger in main, classic EQ rogue setup. Seems perfectly reasonable.

However, this is where things get interesting - with the above setup, you can execute backstab and ambush normally - but you cannot execute sinister strike, eviscerate, or any other melee-weapon-required special attack. This is particularly strange since two-dagger rogues can use these moves without problem, but the effect of equipping a sword in the offhand does strange things to the special attack restrictions code.
