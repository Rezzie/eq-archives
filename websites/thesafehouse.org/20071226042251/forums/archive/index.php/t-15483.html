I don't think it really matters which you see first.

Essentially, the only thing you should know at the start of the movie is that Major Kusanagi is a cyborg, almost completely robotic, but she does have human brain cells. Because she almost completely machine, she's beginning to doubt her humanity.

In GitS: Stand Alone Complex, it's an alternate reality in which The Puppetmaster (the person Kusanagi was tracking down in the movie) didn't exist. I've only seen the first eight episodes, but the Major is played with more feeling and caring (she's not doubting her humanity in the series), and interacts more freely with her team. In SAC it comes up that Kusanagi was forced into her cyborg body by a childhood illness, so she didn't actually volunteer to become a cyborg, it was a way to save her life when she was young (episode eight: 'Missing Hearts' deals with this).
