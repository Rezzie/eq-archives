Using this List

If you are just trying to keep abreast of new items as they are posted, scrolling down to the name of each zone and looking at the most expensive stuff works fine. But if you are shopping for a specific item this guide can be cumbersome due to sinple length. Here are a couple tips on quickly finding what you want.

Go to the EDIT portion on your browser, and select FIND. Then you can follow the below tips on my notation:

If you are seeking an item for you class, it is abbreviated with the standard 3 letters on most items you can use. All/All gear is marked as such instead of listing all classes. However, not all items are marked as not all reports included the classes useable.

If you are seeking an Augmentation, they are listed as "Type X". The important thing here is the space between TYPE and the number. So far I have types 1 through 7, and some "Type All."

If you are seeking armor/weapons with certain slots, I have listed the slots as "typeX, typeX, typeX" I do not group the slots (ie - 3 type1 slots) so that you can search for multiple slots faster, and all slots are numerically listed. For example, if you want some armor with 2 type 3 slots, search for "type3, type3" and if there are any such items yet, it will show you them. Similarly you can search for "type2, type3" "type1, type1" etc.

To browse all available Augments, just do a search for "Type ". Remember to place a SPACE after Type so that it doesn't stop on the gear.


Augment Levels:
Type 1 - Single stat enhancements (+5 intelligence, or whatnot)
Type 2 - Multiple stat enhancements (+15HP/Mana, +3 sv FR/MR/CR, etc.)
Type 3 - Worn effects (Haste, Regeneration, FT)
Type 4 - Weapon Procs
Type 5 - Single Weapon Damage additions (+2 Fire dmg, or +3 dmg)
Type 6 - Multiple Weapon Damage additions (+2 Dmg with + 2 cold dmg etc)
Type 8 - Time Loot (I've never seen a Type 8 augment) i heard but dunno if its true that this involves spell shield, accuracy etc and high level foci


Adventure Types:
-Assassination: kill a boss mob, it spawns when 60% of the mobs are dead
-Rescue: save an npc deep in the dungeon, he spawns right away, but will not leave until 60% of the mobs are dead
-Collection: collect 20-30 items that randomly drop off any mob
-Slaughter: kill 50-60 mobs in the dungeon

How I basically understand the merchants to work:

Points earned at lvl 65:

30 points per person for raid
51 for regular events (13 points if you finish after time runs out, but before dungeon expires)
76 for hard events (19 points if you finish after time runs out, but before dungeon expires)

The numbers tracked are these:

Total points EVER earned per dungeon type: This determines what the merchant will show you from their lists, and therefore what you can buy from them.

Total points CURRENTLY owned: This is the cumulative total of all points you ahve from all dungeons, minus what you have spent. These points can be spent at any dungeon, but only on items that they are showing you.

Guard is interesting, but a little crappy for the amount of points it costs.

Basically, it absorbs 5% off of each hit, till the TOTAL amount absorbed is equal to a specific amount.
For guard 1, its about 50, guard 2 is 100ish, guard 3 about 150, and guard 4 200.
Those numbers arent exact, as guard 2 has been fading around 112 dmg absorbed. Lasted to 107 and then faded next hit, and another time it lasted to 104 and faded next hit.

Those all sound kind of crappy, but when you look at it another way: To get the 100 or so guard 2 absorbs at 5% each hit, you need to take about 2200 damage. 2250 is my estimate so far.

You have to earn enough points at a camp to open up the list to what you want to buy...these points are tracked seperately from those you actually have to spend.

Once you have the list opened up, you can use points earned anywhere to buy things.

The list of what's available for you to buy at each camp does not reset when you purchase an item (ie: once you open up the 1492s at a camp, you will always see everything available for purchase *disclaimer: assuming 1492 truly is the cap*, no matter how many points you actually have to spend).
