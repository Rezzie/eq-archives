Lol. Yeah as people say, I am a huge figure collector. I don't like the smaller scale stuff much (Anything below 6", really.) But I, of course, pay a preminum for it. I generally stick with certain companies, I don't buy anything completely mass produced. By mass produced, I mean stuff made in almost limitless quantity for the casual fans. Most of the bigger figure companies make in somewhat limited quantity so most stuff get sold out within a few weeks.

For shounen stuff, Megahouse has never failed me with their excellent stuff. I have the entire Portrait of Pirates line (Yes, all 12 of them!) which are as the name implies, One Piece figures. The quality and workmanship on it are extraordinary. You don't see that kind of quality on Shounen figures usually. They are 1/8 scale, and so are about 9" tall.

They also make a Bleach line, which has Rukia and Ichigo on first release, Inoue and Renji on second release. I did not pick them up though, I'm not as big of a fan of Bleach as I am of One Piece, and I'm not attached to any of the characters of Naruto or Bleach, where in One Piece I'm attached to the entire crew.

For everything else, I stick with Good Smile Company, Alter, and Max Factory. With Max Factory being the best, the other two you can't go wrong with. I sometimes get figures from other companies, but not without careful consideration and checking their reputation. I know some small but high quality companies that rarely release stuff as well.

But I'm anal about my figures. Any discoloration, excess material, lines, notches, etc get a heavy scowl from me. Lately I've been spending my money more on resin figures, as I have someone building them and painting them for me now for a small fee. And free if I buy one that he's going to make for himself too.

I use figurines to supplement my anime viewing, so if I was to spend $50 on an anime character, instead of spending the whole budget on a figurine I'd get a couple DVDs or a less expensive figurine and a DVD.
