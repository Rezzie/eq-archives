June 4-6, 2004 Sony Online Entertainment invited over 60 players to a "Guild Summit" to give direct feedback about the current state of EverQuest and players' disapointment with the Gates of Discord expansion. Attendees were mainly guild leaders or their appointed officers plus representatives from player class and fan sites. A sampling of guilds included: Legacy of Steel, Township Rebellion, Triton, Silent Redemption, Cats in Hats, BotS, LoS, and other large guilds as well as some mid range guilds. Class and fan sites included The Safehouse, Druid's Grove, Steel Warrior, Shaman's Crucible, Berserker.com, EQTraders, GU comics, EQmac, Mobhunter and The Runes.net.

The weekend's structure included a tour of the SOE offices and group discussions throughout saturday with designers, developers, producers, and customer service, with some time to speak to the same folks individually during the lunch break. The evening ended with a dinner at a San Diego restaurant and attendees departed throughout the day Sunday.

I literally just got home so this report will be ongoing. Pictures, movies and much more detail will be added shortly.

Meanwhile, here is the letter passed out at dinner Saturday compiled from information discussed during the panels.

June 5, 2004

Dear Guild and Community Representatives,

Thank you very much for a great Summit! By all accounts it was a huge success. I can speak for the EverQuest Development Team and tell you that we're grateful for the input you provided. In fact, in trying to generate this list to give to you this evening, we realized there was so much to go over that wouldn't be able to get a complete list together in time. We're committed to not only listening to your feedback, but working with you to make EverQuest more enjoyable for years to come.

So, we decided to list the Top Ten themes we would address starting Monday, as well as the most common themes that we'll come back with more information by mid-week next week.

A quick discussion resulted in a list of items that we will immediately address:

- Add scripting to shut off empty instances of Plane of Time
- Raid messages of the day
- Improving how we handle betas and the testing process
- Increase the number of debuffs allowed on the MOBs
- Increase buffs on players through AAs
- Note field on raid window
- Improved consent & drag for raid, group and guild*
- Improve corpse drag radius*
- Make charms not no-drop
- Guild and individual rewards for guild beta testing
- Work on large To Do list....I know this one is a little random

Future items we will immediately begin looking at and discussions are as follows:

- Plane of Time instancing and other issues*
- Back flaggings solutions to help with High-end guild atrophy
- Improved raid and guild functionality
- Auto loot all for your corpse
- Improvements to Raid XP
- Review the Epic 2.0 quests
- No more alien NPCs ;-) (focusing more on familiar Norrathians themes)**
- Solo LDoN-stype instances or similiar content***
- Issues with item augmentations*
- Possible enhancements to Guild Wars

Of course these aren't all of the items. You brought us a CD, 2 books, 20 printed sheets and a manifesto. We'll need to go through all of those and our notes before we can really get a good tally of all of the wonderful ideas and questions that were presented.

Thank you again for your feedback and participation. We've had a great weekend and a wonderful display of things to come.

Sincerely,

Robert Pfister
Executive Producer
EverQuest

And the EverQuest Development Team!

* Safehouse submitted ideas. Additionally, I was also able to speak one on one with one of the so called "rogue guys", John Troy, Designer, and got much more covered than what made it to this preliminary list. I also spoke to the game designer who wrote the current storyline.

** This idea was submitted by the Safehouse but got a lot of support from all attendees throughout the day.

*** I brought this up but had added that the solo quests could be geared to be class specific and not necessarily entail "hack and slash" content but rely on class abilities, the addition didn't make it to the list but hopefully it is part of what they mean by "similar content".

It is to be noted that both John Smedley and Producer, Rod Humble apologized for Gates of Discord as part of their introductions before the offices tour began. An energetic discussion of how testing could be improved followed.

With the entire community giving input, you end up with not only a mish mash of ideas that lack that type of focus, but you end up breeding animosity within the community - people see that they implemented so-and-so's idea, even though they thought it was stupid, but they see their own idea get rejected, even though they think it's a much better idea.

It's a very hard thing to explain, but I'll try. In the very beginning, Everquest had a certain feel to it. Everyone played a little part in the world of Norrath. You shared things in common with people you had never spoke to. You remember that name from your starting city, or you remember seeing that person ask in /ooc for a heal at T2 in West Karana. There was really a sense of community and everyone belonged. Little by little that part of the game has disappeared. I can't even comprehend the mindset of people that are driven to get to Plane of Time or complete GoD for example.

In the old days, there was a common "enemy". Verant never listened to anything the customer said. They shaped the game around "The Vision" regardless of what the community said most times. That person next to you was your ally in that fight. But things began changing. Verant and then SOE started implementing things that customers asked for, and suddenly that person beside you became your enemy. They started looking at the things that you could do that they couldn't and started pointing fingers, waiting for Verant/SOE to either a) come down on you, or b) give them a boost. The community became fragmented and remains that way today.

Yes, it probably sounds stupid to most people, but if SOE would just stop listening to the customer with regards to encounters, class balance, etc., I think that not only the game but the community would benefit from it in the long run. Yes, it would be a very rough road getting back to that point, and a lot of customers would leave, but it would be good not only for the long term playability of the game, but for community unity as well. Someone at SOE needs to step in and make EQ the game THEY want to play. This IS NOT to say they shouldn't listen to the community as it pertains to bugs.

Maybe I'm wrong, but I see that EQ started to go downhill on the quality side when it got to the point where SOE did not have the resources to fully inhouse QA large encounters. For years now, one of the biggest complaints I've seen from the bleeding edge community is that when they get to the final zones, they are not finished (Time, Vex Thal, etc). It's because the resources simply do not exist for SOE to log in 72 testers to actually play the encounter the way a guild does. A company, and especially a software company, shouldn't ever get to the point where it develops things it doesn't know anything about.

The biggest fault I see isn't a fault at all. It was just SOE's desire to make money, and there is certainly no crime in that. Instead of being like every other game out there where the game is released, people buy it, play it, then move on to something else, SOE tried to keep those customers from moving on by allowing the game to expand to a scope that is outside of their ability to realistically control.

I'm probably completely out to lunch with this. Lately, EQ has just become more and more foreign to me. I see the game constantly becoming faster/easier/bigger and see that as being completely out of step of what it used to be. Others I guess see these things as improvements, I see it as speeding faster and faster towards the end.
