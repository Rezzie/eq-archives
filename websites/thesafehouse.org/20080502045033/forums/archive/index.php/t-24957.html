There's a new method to flash the Linksys WRT54G v5.0 routers to DD-WRT. Previously, you would have to build or use a JTAG cable which involves some hardware assembly/etc. Now you can just use an intermediate firmware that 'preps' the router to accept the DD-WRT Micro version. You can only use that version of DD-WRT since the v5 routers have minimal storage.

Here's a link to the instructions: Upgrading to Linux from a VX Works Router (http://www.wrtrouters.com/guides/upgradetolinux/)

And here's a link with some information about the whole thing: Linux hackers re-claim the Linksys WRT54G (http://www.linuxdevices.com/news/NS6352077661.html)

Here's the official DD-WRT Wiki entry on it (lots of information!): Vxworkskiller (http://www.dd-wrt.com/wiki/index.php/Vxworkskiller)
