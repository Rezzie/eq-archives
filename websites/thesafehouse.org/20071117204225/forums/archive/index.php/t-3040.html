What do you folks think? The little tagline about "not knowing to cast as an arcane or a divine" each round seems to me to be a mostly trivial issue.

I guess what I see is that there's very little downside to going this route, especially if you look later on the caster's career. Giving up 3 levels of arcane caster for the sake of some hefty cleric powers seems to me to be a gimme of a tradeoff. That is - given that someone wants to devote themselves to being a wizard, taking a 3 level detour into cleric or druid is a relatively minor hit, seeing as you would be able to still cast 9th level spells as an arcane caster later on (albeit later), and be able to pack some heavy duty divine punch as well.

It seems to me that the archetype fighter/rogue/arcane/cleric party becomes after level 6 fighter/rogue/theurge/cleric or even -theurge/theurge. The only downside is having to keep up both wisdom and int/cha. But if you figure that the cleric path will end at effective level 13 cleric, you're still only looking at needing a 17 wisdom to cast those level 7 spells, something that with upper tier magic items is amazingly doable, even with a pretty paltry starting wisdom.

general consensus is that it's a tad bit overpowered, but not as grossly as you'd think when you first look at it.

and to quote sean reynolds on some of the issues:

If you get into the class as early as possible:

1) overall you end up with more spells per day than a single-classed cleric (you start off behind, but that's just because clerics get so many spells per day)
2) you have more spells per day than a single-classed wizard (nearly twice as many when you reach the end of the class)
3) you have more spells per day than a normal multiclassed spellcaster who splits his spellcaster levels evenly
4) you have more total spell levels per day than a single-classed cleric
5) ditto for wizard
6) ditto for even-levels multiclassed spellcaster

You get the same skill points as either class (assuming Clr or Wiz) and cleric BAB.

Drawbacks: wizard saves (not as good as cleric), wizard hp (not as good as cleric), you don't get the high-level spells.

he wrote up an analysis on the class, on his board.

of course, one of the big problems is that the requirements for the class are an absolute joke. they aren't even really requirements, they're standard statistics for any multiclassed wiz(sorc)/cleric. plus, the class has absolutely no flavor to it - it just seems like a pointless powergamed wiz(sorc)/cleric prc.
