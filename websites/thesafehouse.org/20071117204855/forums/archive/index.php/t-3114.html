Evil parties die. A Lot.

Most evil personalities in fantasy worlds exist in one of a couple ways. They are uber-powerful mastermind types. They never leave the "seemy underworld area" with all their informants, bodyguards and such. Or, they stick to small stuff that doesn't register on the "good guys" radar.

Evil parties of players tend to think they are uber-powerful mastermind types and tend to do things that register on the "good guys" radar. Once that happens, their life expectancy is severely limited.

The only way I've found to make an evil party last a decent while is to make a plot device that stops them from killing/hurting/robbing/turning on each other. Had one GM bind a group of evils together by giving them collective awareness, so they couldn't plot on each other.

Also, the players need to realize that evil can mean "looks to acheive personal power without regard for whom is hurt in the process" rather than "is a raving homicidal maniac". I played a Lawful Evil sorcerer once that despised killing and wouldn't do it unless his life was threatened, but had no compunctions about charming or otherwise influencing beings into serving him in ways that were not beneficial to them...

I've ran a few evil campaigns and I've found one concept to be infinitely valuable. No matter how big and powerful your evil PC's are, make sure that they know that they aren't the biggest fish in the sea. An evil NPC overlord, or group of them, bossing the PC's around (giving them quests/missions) and putting the smack down when they get out of line goes a long way towards sustaining an evil campaign. A small clan or coven of high lvl renegade wizards should do the trick in a Dragonlance setting. Also, if you can balance their missions where they are seriously challenging and demand group cooperation, you can quell a good portion of the desire to PK other group members.
