I got jumped by lone hunters a few times when I was around level 30, all of whom had 3-5 levels on me. I got away each time (just barely).

Some things that helped me:

Sprint as soon as you see that little red arrow over your head. If you're engaged in a fight, gouge the mob first so it doesn't try to daze you.

You're probably going to be snared with concussive shot as soon as you try to sprint away. The hunter will probably hit you with a dot too.

You're going to take major damage here as these guys can really dish out from ranged combat, I couldn't have surived without a healing potion. Make sure to have some at all times. Use one as soon you get to around 30% hp.

After about 4-5 seconds snare will break. As soon as it does, sprint INTO a group of mobs. You're still going to have 10 seconds to run through them at sprint speed and by the time they aggro you you'll have already run past them. However, this'll make the hunter think twice about charging after you, especially with cheetah on.

As sprint ends make sure you're now clear of any new mobs and pop vanish. It WILL put you into stealth even though mark is still on you, dunno if this is a bug or what, but it does work. All the mobs that were chasing you will lose aggro too.

From here, just keep moving away from the hunter. Try to find a place with lots of mobs around that you can sneak around in.

You are now stealthed and he will have a hard time getting close enough to try to actually reveal you even though he can still see you on the minimap. You should be able to wait out the minute or so left on mark.

I'm not sure when hunters get flare, but this worked for me around level 30.

Thing about being marked is it isn't as bad as it seems.

You've got 1 chance.

Hit sprint and pick a direction, towards the hunter or away. I'll always run away unless I already have crippling on my weapons (although my weapons proc root, I don't rely on it).

If I do have crippling on, I hit sprint and charge toward the hunter, quickly close that gap.

Now you've got a couple more options.

Hit vanish and cheapshot (the reason why mark isn't so bad. It doesn't make it impossible to stealth and use your opener, it just means he gets to watch you CS him) if you think you can do it before he breaks your stealth or you can blind and then vanish so you're certain he won't break your stealth.

After that lay in like you would normally attack a hunter if you had the first strike.

Personally, I've never had a hunter freeze trap me in PvP, I guess they get scared that I may see them before they have their chance to attack and never drop it. So don't even worry about that scenario happening.
