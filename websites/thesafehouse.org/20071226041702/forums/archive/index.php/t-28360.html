I just watched the episode and there were a few things I didn't like about it.

First and most important was the director. I could see him in the control booth going "Okay, ready camera one, go on camera one! Okay, we've done that shot for two seconds, ready camera two - go on camera two! Okay, five seconds this time, now go back to camera one! Okay, three second on that shot, ready camera three - go on camera three!..." He probably lost is voice calling out all those camera changes over the course of the fifteen minutes or so.

In the second half of the show I started counting how long each shot was being held and I rarely got above six seconds, with the longest at ten seconds. I replayed a portion of the show and listened to it instead of watching it and found it was much better - I could concentrate on what the people were saying instead of the visual changes. Also, get mics on each reviewer instead of a group mic - some of the reviewers have lower/quieter voices than others. And clear all that crap off the table - the guys can go fifteen minutes without a drink, and if it's product placement then one of each drink is fine.

Second was the fact that there were a bunch of people talking about one anime for fifteen minutes. I think instead they should review two or three anime with one of the reviewers taking the intro portion which describes the show and what they liked and disliked, then have the rest of the crew either agree or disagree with him/her and bring up other points the reviewer might have missed. Jonny Bosch might want to play a bigger part in moderation (and get the hell off that highchair and onto the sofa with the other guys). Essentially, the director should watch Ebert & Roeper and rip off as much as he can from that reviews show.

Last was their list of top anime. 5, Mononoke, 4. Spirited Away, 3. Howl's Moving Castle, 2. Evangelion, 1. Bleach.

Aside from my personal opinion that movies and series shouldn't be judged together, Bleach has no business being number one, and I can think of many better anime that could be put on the list instead of Howl's and Bleach. Not saying either are bad, they're just not two of the three best anime ever made. Spirited Away has no business being as low as number four either.

Oh, and I don't think they should have the viewer ratings at the end - it'll encourage people to praise or bash an anime just to get their post on the show.
