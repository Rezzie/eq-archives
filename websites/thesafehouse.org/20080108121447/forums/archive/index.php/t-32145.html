It's not entirely optimal, but there's this (http://www.wowhead.com/?talent=f0eboLxZMjV0rVzxMhot).

While some mixed-spec builds are popular, they almost all use Swords for the offhand, since a Sword Specialization proc from the offhand will trigger a -mainhand- swing. If you put a dagger in your offhand, then you may as well skip Dagger Specialization entirely, since offhand crits are moderately irrelevant - they're certainly not worth spending 5 talent points on. Therefore, the build I linked has Sword Spec, but no Dagger Spec.

The one advantage of that build is that Combat Potency is massively affected by offhand speed - the faster the better, and each 0.1s counts. Generlly speaking, daggers are faster than swords, so when you're looking for the fastest offhand you can possibly find, daggers are a pretty good place to look. The Searing Sunblade (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=29275) would be the ideal offhand for that build, with a Merciless Gladiator's Slicer (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=32052) mainhanded.

berael
11-05-07, 02:16 PM
For sword spec, the bulk of your extra attacks comes from the mainhand.

You're actually off on that one - remember, Combat Potency demands the fastest possible offhand you can find, and you always want a slow mainhand regardless of the situation simply because slower weapons are always better per-hit on instant attacks due to their higher damage.

The inherent desire for fast offhand and slow mainhand means that the offhand's SS procs will be triggering many, many more mainhand attacks than the mainhand's SS procs - that's why Sword works very well for offhand in dual-specs, since you get both the benefit of SS and CP. With either Daggers or Fists offhand and a different mainhand, you'd want to skip Dagger Spec / Fist Spec, but the offhand sword rewards you both coming and going.
