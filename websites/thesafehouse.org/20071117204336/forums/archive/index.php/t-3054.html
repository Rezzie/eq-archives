This isn't going to be any help ... but playing a horror RPG is probably the most difficult thing you can try and do. The DM has to be very into it and be a great storyteller. The players have to be into it and it helps if they've all read Lovecraft at one time or another.

Ok, I actually do have some advice. Don't be too descriptive, especially about monsters (don't ever show a picture of one!). I think one of Lovecraft's high points is that he left a lot up to the reader's imagination and it works well. Of course, this only applies if you're atually trying to create a sense of horror/suspense.

Truer words have never been spoken.

I've only played CoC once...but I'm a fan of the whole horror, Lovecraftian Genre. The system is not for everyone. Some players need more hack, speed up grades, shooting two guns, flaming swords, and a snow balls chance in hell against really bad monsters. CoC doesn't really offer that.

I'd suggest really examining your players, and find out which ones actually enjoy survival/mystery horror, and which ones are just there for the ride, because they are the ones that will bring your session down, and detract from the atmosphere that is essential to running a good CoC game. If people aren't scared of the stuff you are doing to them, the no-eyed, elucidian tentacled horror from the depths isn't going to have the impact it should have. If a player fails an insanity check, and goes "aw god damnit, now what am I going to do", then I'd say he's the wrong player for the game. the Nocturnum campaign module is amazing.

Im a big Lovecraft fan, but only started playing CoC a few months before the d20 version was released, so i moved over to that immediately.

Like someone said, Horror is the hardest genre to both play and storytell, imo. I ran two campaigns of CoC that lasted probably about 10 sessions apiece, with 3 or 4 players, varying. Horror/suspense was a new genre for a couple of my players. different opinion between them and the more experienced players was a problem (a lot of "screw researching in the library all day about that creepy old house. lets just go burn the @#%$ down!")

anyways... i had just about given up on CoC until i ran across the Nocturnum campaign module. Its definately one of the best modules i have ever read. It has 18 distinct chapters (which turned into 20+ long games, so its a lot of bang for your buck), a ton of information and mood, and many many differing scenarios depending on how and when your party reacts to a situation. Things such as newspaper clippings that you can copy and give to your players as clues really add to the 'investigator' feel of it.

30 minutes into the first chapter and my players were nervous, completely creeped out, and looking at me like i was the devil. that only got worse as the night progressed its a ton of fun and i cant recommend it enough if you want to give CoC a try.
