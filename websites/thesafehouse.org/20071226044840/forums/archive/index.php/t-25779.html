Black Blood Brothers - C

The first episode is very confusing and cluttered. There was a huge action sequence in the beginning, but they never told what it was all about. Then later on there's another battle on a ship, and we still don't know what that battle is about either or anyone's motivations. I'm holding most of my judgement until later episodes come out, which should clear things up a bit.

One good thing about the show, if anything, is how the older brother beat the living **** out of his younger brother. Some one-sided sibling violence is a nice change! I actually laughed a bit in that part.


Kaleido Star Good Dayo! Goo~d! OVA - B+

I recommend it for Kaleido Star fans, but people new to the series will get the wrong impression by this OVA. And won't understand any of the character dynamics until after seeing the second season. If you haven't seen Kaleido Star, GET IT NOW! It's almost fully released in the US already!

Kikoushi Enma - B

I know next to nothing about this series. Other than that it's an OVA that just came out. It is a story about a brother and sister who are mages or excorcists of some sort. They hunt demons and get rid of them. There's some very fanservice in the show, but it is kind of amusing sort.

The art was interesting to look at, very dark/gothic. As are the character designs. I like the talking hat that's always sleeping. It has a very mysterious feel to it, a large part due to not knowing what is going on exactly.

I do recommend it to fans of supernatural anime.

Negima?! (alternatively known as Pani Poni Dash 2 B

I have a hard time giving it a better grade. First off, I advise against Mahora's sub of Negima?!, as the encoding is horribly done. Pixelization everywhere. If you can wait, let someone else sub it. I haven't seen an encoding job this horrible in the last 4 years. It's just that bad. It reminds me of the days of watching .rma...

However, I don't hold that against the series itself. The animation is decent. I really don't know where to begin with the series. It's not clearly better than the first run. The animation is very odd. It's VERY confusing and disorienting, in my opinion. I don't really know how to explain this well. However, I will compare the style to Pani Poni Dash, which it's almost a clone of. In Pani Poni Dash, the storytelling style worked well because the screen was always packed with comedy, oddity, and so on. Everything had a reason, a meaning, etc.

In Negima?!, it was mostly meaningless. The constant tolling of the bells, shifting perspectives, moving view, multiple different things going on at the same time for no reason, etc. It doesn't add any style in a good way. It just leaps out at you and disorients you.

However, it does shine at times. I don't know if this is better than the first season, only time will tell. But I'm not too impressed, especially after their claims that it would be hundredfold better.

Sasami Mahou Shoujo Club B-

I will begin by saying I'm not a terribly big fan of Tenchi. I recognize it's merits, but it never really pulled me in. The character designs in Tenchi are a big put off for me. Or at least, the hair is. As such, I've only seen Tenchi Universe.

That being said, I thought the show was only slightly better than mediocre. There's really no comparision between this and modern Mahou Shoujo series (i.e. Pretty Cure). The animation is average, and the story felt abstract. There was no clear purpose to the series, which seems to have a purpose. So by halfway through, I felt a sense of pointlessness, as though it was all over the place instead of going in a clear direction.

Sasami was my favorite character of Tenchi Universe, so I did enjoy seeing her again, but that's all that made me last the whole episode instead of dropping it.

Code Geass A

I was pleasantly suprised by this one. But it's definitely not for everyone. A lot of people will be offended by the theme of the show. In this world, the Great Britannica Empire (i.e. us) are evil corrupt beings who invade Japan and took away everything it held dear - their freedom, their traditions, and even their name. Japan was renamed to Area 44.

And so, Japan fights back against us evil people! Of course, that means super robots. What else could it be :)

However, there's a twist. A young boy (Lelouch) met a girl while trying to escape, and was given a power that nobody understood. To put it simple, it's a power not unlike Light of Death Note. By looking at someone in the eye and using his power, he can temporarily make orders of the person. Even if it's to make them kill themselves or others.

Lelouch is also a genius kid who is trying to manipulate the rebellion against the britannica empire.
