here's how MQing works. Say an Npc requires 4 items for the quest.

Item1 = flag1
item2 = flag2
item3 = flag3
item4 = flag4


Now lets say you have 2 items on 1 char, 1 on another and the last piece on another.

You hand in item1 and item3. The Npc now has set flag1 and flag3 to ON.

Char 2 now hands in item2. Flag2 is now set to ON. So now to complete the quest all someone has to do is give the npc item4 and they will receive the reward. The last person to hand in is the one that receives the reward.

You could turn in part of the items then log in another char for the rest. The only problem is if the npc gets reset (patch, zone crash, someone kills him). Otherwise the npc will be there waiting for the last item till they are reset.

Screwy things can happen when someone comes by in the middle of a MQ and turns in all the proper items. Sometimes the MQ goes fine. others it seems that the npc ignores the dup'd items from the non MQ person gives them the reward and the dup'd items just poof.

It all depends what order the items get handed in. Picture a 2-item quest. You hand in item A, then log in your alt to hand in item B. Unbeknownst to you, some guy comes by and does the quest. There are two possibilities:

1. If he hands in item B first, you'll never know it, and everything's fine. The NPC says "I have A and B, so here's your reward!". Then the NPC gets item A (since the guy gave him both) and holds onto it. When you log in, you hand over item B, and get your reward.

2. If he hands in item A first, you get stiffed. The quest NPC already had item A, so he just basically ignores it. He takes item B from the guy and gives him the quest reward. Now your alt logs in and hands over item B. The NPC wil accept it, but still wants item A before you get your reward. If you're super-lucky, item A is easy to get, and no one does the quest before you get it.
