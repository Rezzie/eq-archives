Haiku is an exceptional form, and if ye'd care ta listen, I'd like to say a few words about it... Although I mean not to critique the excellent story the Luckless Rogue wrote, Haiku is a bit different from what that poster made it. In particular,

It is a single verse
It is never 5-7-5 in English, and not always 5-7-5 in Japanese (Japanese has less information per "syllable". Haiku is about minimalism, and if ye wish to adhere to a 5-7-5, you'd find that 3-5-3 would contain about the same amount of information, as 5-7-5 in Japanese). And yes, the minimalism may leave room open to interpretation.
It always involves nature
It contains a word that informs you what season it is
Finally, yes, like the Luckless Rogue did, you may break the rules.
