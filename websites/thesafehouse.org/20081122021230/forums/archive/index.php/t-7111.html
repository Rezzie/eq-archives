You're complaining that NPCs only give certain quests if you have the necessary requirements (level, completion of previous quests, etc)? Please go back to the Old World and look around - there have *always* been level-related quests, and chained quests. Flags just make it so you don't have to carry notes from one NPC to another, or cart around a backpack full of heads, or hand in a previously quested item for proof you've completed the quest, or various faction-level subquests, or...

For the first time ever, Verant has released an expansion with an actual focus on Story. Or perhaps, this is just the first time they've been successful. The "storyline" in Kunark was in the digging up of ancient history - much of it sold in books in Cabilis. SOV introduced factions, but apart from the notable 10th ring war, the whole dwarf-giant-dragon thing was just a "watch your faction" balancing act. SOL had scraps of backstory, but it was all very disconnected.

In POP, the storyline is central - you can't get *anywhere* without exposure to it. Let's face it - there isn't much difference between "good" and "evil" in EQ anyhow. That's not what EQ's about, and that's not what POP is about.

From a true Roleplaying point of view, yes, the Planes have a lot wrong with them. On some of the new servers, specifically Legends and the like, is it the same issue or did they instate actual roleplaying quests? I'm not sure. I'm on the ancient Fennin Ro server and lie a lot of others, good and evil have kinda blurred over the years into something that holds absolutley no consequence other than getting your DE butt kicked walkin' by the pallies in North Freeport.

I do kinda wish they'd put actual good/evil/neutral quests in the game. As an evil character, your whole purpose in life is to bring about chaos, destruction or whatever wets your whistle, and actually piss off the goodie-two-shoes. I kinda like taking my usual neutral Gnome rogue (not good or bad) up into PoG and pissing off Tunare just because while I may not be an evil race or class, I'm not a good race or class either.

Kunark expanded on the racial differences that came up with the Dark Elves/Trolls/Ogres from the original release by adding a new hated race that had very little options for moving around due to how hard it was to enter any other city other than Cabilis. Still though, it was handled nicely with the ability to raise even the toughest factions with various quests.

Velious took the whole concept of Factions and kinda tossed it around like a salad. One minute you're friendly with the dorfs, then after a few kills in defense of your life and even they hate you while the obscure dragons decided for once not to eat you with ketchup. Still, players found a way around even that mess to the point of turning it into a science of juggling in order to get what armor pieces they wanted from where.

Luclin's factions aren't even hardly discernable other than Katta Castellum and Sanctus Seru. Other than that, every mob up there in Luclin seems to be either flat indifferent or ready to kill you anyway, faction or no faction.

It would have been nice to see PoP bring about the genuine roleplaying quests that you would expect from actually seeing/being with your god in his/her/its own plane of existence. I mean, if they can start a ring war in GD between the giants and the dorfs that essentially resets itself after 2 hours no matter what side wins, why can't they do that with PoD? Give some quest that allows players following Bertoxxulous to spread a disease down onto the Karanas (like the mini storyline tried to do a couple years ago) and if they succeed, everyone gets poisoned or you need an eb item to walk thru the Karanas for a couple hours til it resets.

I'm not saying they completely disregarded factions there; there's too much of the planes not even seen yet, even by the 'uber' guilds. Maybe if we're lucky something will come out of those nethermost planes to appease the RP'ers out there.
