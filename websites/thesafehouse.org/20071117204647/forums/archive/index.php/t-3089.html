Miniatures has grown on me quite a bit. I played my first game of D&amp;D back in oh, 1981 or so, and when you're 8 years old you don't have the cash to blow on minis so we just kinda kept track of things in our heads. We never really worried about any facing or placement or whatever. We just slugged it out with the baddies until they were all dead! RAR!

I just started playing again last fall after a many year hiatus, and 3.0's rules really are geared towards using miniatures. Rules on AOOs and flanking are pretty much designed with minis in mind - someone has to be keeping track of who is where - and many feats/abilities require knowing exactly where someone is relative to the baddies.

The thing that I find relatively noxious is the line of minis that they're releasing in conjunction with the new rules. They're debuting a new "campaign" run through the RPGA at gencon, and guess what? If you shell out the cash for certain minis you get in-game benefits. Oh, and they've disallowed online play for the new campaign as well - I guess people who play online won't buy their super bonus Lidda the Halfling mini. Or something.

D&amp;D the Gathering? No thanks.

Monte is aware of those issues (him writing on 3.0 and Arcana Unearthed (AU)), and he mentions those in his review.
AU was also started before 3erevised was scheduled to come out.
AU is also nothing like 3e revised, its more like the eqrpg, wheel of time , or call of cthulhu.
AU is Monte's product, unearthed arcana was a first edition AD&amp;D product released more than a decade ago, that's the diff /grin

I pretty much agree with what Monte has to say as well.

As far as miniatures go, it sort of have to goes with what Lindianae has to say about scribing spells. It's your game, you can change whatever you want. However dnd3e (and revised) was built with the idea that everybody knew how to play the same game.
In EQ everyone plays the same game, there are no house rules, thus when you talk EQ everyone is talking the same game.
So many people were using miniatures it was decided that common rules for those would be handy, especially with many of the living campaigns and RPGA -- all these people need a common reference for them to talk the same game.

You can change scribing costs, you can play without miniatures, but to create a common ground for people to start with and for all those RPGA people who basically have to play "by the rules", they came up with someone for miniatures and for something more reasonable for scribing costs.

The new miniatures they are releasing, I agree with Llabak, they are noxious. Randomly packed plastic miniatures (similar to heroclix without the rules and clicky-base). I don't want random figures. I want to pick out the one I want and move on. I'm hoping the aftermarket will provide the ones I would want for a comparable price.
