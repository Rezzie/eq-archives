2 reasons I chose not to endorse the 3e crit system

1-I'd have to make crit values and threat ranges for all weapons. I could do it, but it would be time consuming, and obnoxious.

I went with my system for crits because it allows at best for 2x base weapon damage(players don't add weapon/specialization or strength to the second die), allows for some variablity in the strength of the crit, and won't gut players when it happens to them. One second edition fumble/crit system I saw went as followed:

If you roll a natural '20' to hit, that attack hits and you get another attack upon that person.
On this second attack, a natural 20 does NOT grant another attack, nor is a 1 a fumble on this second attack.

If you roll a natural 1 to hit someone, that person gets an immediate attack against you. Further 1's or 20's on this roll also do not crit/fumble.

Gnome hooked hammers are a double weapon that is 1d6 20/x3 and 1d4 20/x4, the only standard weapon other than the scythe (2d4 20/x4) that crits for x4.

And great axes only threaten on a 20 (x3), while daggers threaten on 19 (x2).

So, it's really not a matter of weapon size that effects the threat and multiplier, it's really weapon specific.

A spear threatens on 20 and crits for x3... it makes since, because of the way you fight with a spear, and impaling someone in a vital area is gonna do serious damage. Daggers threaten on a greater range, presumably because they are good at slipping between pieces of armor. Though, the large weapons tend to have higher multipliers. Swords also tend to threaten on a larger range than other weapons (why the hell does every game cater to swords?)

You don't have to do up an entire weapon table. Just a table for the weapons your pcs are using, and make adaptations as necessary as weapons are introduced. There's very little need to convert katanas if the orient doesn't exist in your world, unless you decide they're just well made swords.

If you're interested in the 3e version of certain weapons, there are a few hundred of us reading this forum that have PHBs.

Just making sure people realize under 3e when you score a crit, you also multiply any damage bonuses (except for damage bonuses represented as extra dice, such as from a sneak attack or flaming sword) on the roll.

For example, if you crit with greataxe (X3) and normally have a +4 damage with it, you would roll 3d12+12 (1d12+4 three times).

That is a summary of Players Handbook pg 119 under the Multiplying Damage section.

I like the crit system they have in place, and as a DM you can always fudge a roll here and there if you do not like the results. However, at least for me, as the players get higher level, the gloves come off. Especially on the main NPCs. At lower level the crit system is pretty brutal, both ways.

Just a bit of clarification.
