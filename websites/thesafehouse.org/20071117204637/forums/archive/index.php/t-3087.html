Multiclassing in 3e simply lets you take your next level and apply it to another class. So if you're 2nd level, and you reach third, you can either take a 3rd level in the same class, or stay second level in the first, and take all the skills and abilities of a first level in a second class.

I'm particularly partial to fighter levels for a rogue, at least one or two, because you get nice BAB, and those bonus feats are really nice additions to a rogue's abilities.

Alternatively you could go with Barbarian levels, and get the bonus movement, rage, and extra hitpoints.

Or if you're feeling crazy and have the charisma for it, take a level of sorcerer and pick up ray of frost. Range touch attack sneak attack as a zero level spell is pretty sweet

Llabak Tharr

BAB is base attack bonus. Fighters get a bonus to attack equal to their level, and every 5 levels get an extra attack at a reduce bonus. Rogues get their bonuses at a rate slower than every level, so a level of fighter will let you get an extra bonus to your to-hit rolls that you may not have gotten with a level of rogue.

As for cleric, if you took a level of a cleric with the trickery domain, that certainly would be a nice addition to your skill set.

You can read more about the base attack bonus in the SRD that was linked to you before. Similarly, cleric spells, their domains and granted powers are available in the SRD as well. You really need to read through that stuff bigtime, because no one is going to be able (or willing) to explain to you how to play from start to finish from scratch. Answering the odd question is fine, but I don't think anyone has time to answer questions like what base attack bonus is and means.

If it's too hard to follow the SRD, and you have Kazaa, then DL the player's handbook from it in PDF format and read through it that way.
