If you pay enough attention to it in terms of slots, etc, yes. it can work very well.

In Fact, an Empathy Controller is easily a better healer than any non-empathy defender. Their heals are 75% as big as an Empath Defender's Heals. So you'd have to burn more endurance, but not a huge amount more.

The trick is that when you are doing Your Crowd Control thing, you probably won't be able to do that AND full heals for a large group at once. But in a smaller 3-4 person group, you should be good.

Several Healers in Beta decided that when live came they were going that route, thinking that would give them slightly lower damage ehaling power, but a lot more Damage mitigation power, and thus make the healing job easier.

The one downside: Aggro. If all you are doing is Healing, it is a cakewalk to avoid Aggro. If you are doing Crowd control and healing for te grou, you will be getting a large share of Aggro, which will interfere with your ability to be an effective healer, and make it much mroe risky to use Absorb Pain. AP Heals a ton, but takes like 15% of your health per cast, and makes you unhealable except by direct heals for a while. i.e. No using AP and then Healing Aura to repair the damage, you'd need someone else with a targetted heal to help you recover from that.

But It would give you a lot of versatility. Assuming both sets of powers were appropriately slotted, you could join a group and Fill a role as a Healer, or as a Crowd Control Expert, but both might be pushing it in a large group.
