Unfocused? I don't really follow. It seems as focused as any MMORPG I've played. Are you sure you aren't confusing lack of focus with am abundance of things to do? It seems I always have a quest to finish, a new area to explore, a tradeskill to work on, or a new level to earn. The primary foci are the same as they've always been in EQ, to level up and get better stuff.

As for information about classes and tradeskills, it takes a while for that sort of thing to become common knowledge. Every game I've ever started at launch has been like that (Camelot comes to mind in a big way). I think the basics behind each class and subclass are pretty well explained, but the details will only be revealed in time. For now, the important decisions (basic class, subclass, class) and starting city are the only major class decisions. Hell, in Camelot the manual outright lied and said that hunters could track and berserkers could shapechange (they couldn't at release).

Unfortunately I'm sure there are going to be roadbumps too (troubadors are better than dirges, alchemy is easier and makes more money than woodcrafting, etc etc) but that's part of what you get when you play a game from the beginning. Hopefully SoE did a good job balancing the classes and tradeskills in beta, because I'd rather not repeat my Camelot experience (started as a shaman and they completely SUCKED at launch and for a good year afterward).

If you are running an LCD display, try setting the resolution to one of the supported resolutions, meaning usually LCDs (at least older ones) have two settings they display properly, a native and a stretch.

Worth a look at least.
