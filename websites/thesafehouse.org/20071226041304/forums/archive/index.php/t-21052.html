Animal Yokocho- B

Very cute series. At first, I thought it was from the creator of Full Moon o Saga****e, but I was wrong. The girl looks a lot like Mitsuki. It's a cute show about a girl who has a door in her room to a land of animals. Based on the first episode alone, it's just about kids having fun. Not in the way Ichigo Mashimaro is, but more like, childish, for children. Not childish, for adults. I liked it. If you can stand children's show, and cutesy things, you'll like this one. I am trying to think of a good comparision, but nothing's coming to mind.

Aria the Animation- A+

The manga is wonderful. There is no real story, it's more of a showcase of beauty and emotions. The changing of the seasons, a life with slow pace, calmness and peace and tranquility. It's a show that invokes emotions, rather than thought or excitement. As the seasons change, the subtle differences occur, you learn the beauty of life.

Hopefully, the anime will capture the feel of the manga. The story revolves around Neo Aqua, a man-made planet that has a very striking, if not exact, resemblance to Veince. It revolves around a few girls who are training to become Udines. Udines are the people who row small canoes around the city to transport people, almost like a water taxi.

With the first episode, it really catches the general feel of the show, but it was a little more exciting than it usually is. But I felt content with the way they introduced the characters. The personalities fit very well, and it was very interesting to watch. But I'm hoping they focus a little less on the story like it tried to in the first episode, and more on the atmosphere like the manga does.

Black Cat- C

The manga has a hardcore cult following, but I could not get into the anime. I'll be giving it a few more episodes. It tries to be mysterious for no particular reason, which gives me the chills more than it does intruge me. But it's very typical shallow for a shounen show. It looks like it will have a fair bit of action in it. There's no particular story going on right now, so I can't really define the show yet.

Blood+ B+

The animation was good, and I have a thing for innocent girls turned bloody murderers. It's an adaptation of Blood The Last Vampire. Set in the same world, but completely different. The first episode worried me a bit because it had the same "fake mysterious" feel as Black Cat did, but it was a little more well-done. The second episode redeemed this aspect somewhat, as they moved the story along fairly fast.

The opening scene of the first episode was among the most intense I've seen yet. If you have bandwidth to spare, it would not be too bad to check the opening alone out. And the show too, if you like. They animate the fights decently, even if it's a bit fast and there's some "poofing" where they disappear and reappear elsewhere, leaving it to your imagination how they got there. Still good, though, better than most.

Canvas 2- C

I thought it was a little cute, but the story just moves at a blazing fast speed. Plot development that usually takes most anime 5 episodes, in one episode. I got a bit dizzy, I think. The show pretty much is similar to your typical bishoujo anime. Guy with many girls. A lot of little sister complex fanservice in the first episode.

But, it felt very... odd. Like the reason why the girl didn't like red. It just felt shallow and 2D, even for a bishoujo anime where 2D girls are penny a dozen. Doesn't seem great, but it's not bad either.

Cluster Edge- B-

It seems to be a bishounen show. So that scared me off, for the most part. But I prevailed and watched the episode. It seems to be a show created tomake excuses to have yaoi, but it might have something interesting to it. I gave it a B for it's potentional to be good, so it might turn bad or good here. A lot of pretty boys, a lot of story setup. It has a old world feel (no technoloogy) with some random technology thrown in for good measure.

Requires a ****load of suspense of disbelief. A pistol that can destroy a car in one shot, and they have trouble against two cars. Stuff like that. A guy goes to save another pretty boy for no particular reason, and destroys his own ship in the process. For no particular reason.

Jingoku Shoujo A

This show, I feel, has the highest potentional out of all the shows so far. It is not exceedingly amazing or intruging, but it has such a good idea for a story that, if well executed, could end up being one of the best anime of the season. The title translates to Hell Girl.

Basically, there's this girl who ferries people to hell. At midnight, if you type in an specific url, they will take revenge on someone for you. After typing their name in, they will contact you and explain the terms of the contract. They will kill the object of your revenge and take them to hell for all of eternity, in exchange for your soul. You will live, until you die until your natural causes, but you pretty much deny yourself admittance to heaven, and they get to take your soul and bring you to hell.

The animation is very beautiful, and chilling. It reminded me a bit of Gankutsuou, and Le Portrait de Petite Cossette.

Mai-Otome B+

I'll get a lot of hate for the grade, but I was not much of a fan of Mai-HiME in the first place. I like how they did this second show, though. It seems very interesting, and the characters feel very unique. I don't feel like I'm watching the same show again, even though many of the old cast are still there.

It feels absolutely the same as Mai-HiME in general, though. Can't say if that's a good or bad thing. The first three episodes have kept my interest, which says a lot, really. Check out Mai-HiME before watching this, if you like that, you'll like this. But that aside, the two show shave nothing to do with each other, so bypassing HiME would be just fine.

Shoujo-ai still going strong in this series, as it did the last. Somewhat glad to see that.

Noein- A-

When I watched this episode, I thought that Studio 4C made an new TV series. Much to my disappointment, they did not. The animation style is very remniscent of it. There's not much to the story so far, but the atmosphere and intensity is spot on. A lot of potentional.

There's not much story revealed so far. From what I can see, there is a world outside of this. And this other world is trying to take something called the Dragon's Torque. Reasons are not clear yet. Would have to watch more. But, the animation alone makes this show worth watching.

Paradise Kiss- A+

I'm certainly blown away, really. I've skimmed the first chapter or two of the manga, and didn't pick it up. The odd character designs made me not feel too interested in it.

But, the animation and design of the first two episodes really is incredible. I felt a certain nostilaga when I watched it, then I realized it was because the main character reminds me a lot of Maho from Beck. The pacing and style of the show feels very much similar.

It gives off a great atmosphere. Not too fast, not too slow. Not too serious, but not too simple. The people in the show are all very interesting, and the animation style really brings out their personalities. For someone like me, who is not interested in fashion at all, I could really see how their personalities are reflected by their clothes. That makes the show all the more better.

Seeing their very unique clothes change constantly really gives the show a new life. There are many shows that have characters change clothes a lot, but usually it's not noticeable. But you can really notice and appreciate it in this show.

All impressions of the animation aside, the story is fairly basic so far. A group of clothes designers who are trying to find someone to be their model. Typically, I would brush it off as a simple story, but I also know from experience how intense these type of shows can get. Beck is the easy one that comes to mind. It's just about a band who finds a singer and tries to hit big time. Simple, but very intense.

Given the length I am writing on this show, I can really start to see how impressed I am with the show. I sincerely hope it maintains it's pace and really does well. The one thing that worries me is the animation plays a huge part in this show. The second the clothes and designs look dull, is when the show will really turn for the worse, so hopefully they have a good budget to work with.

Overall, excellent release, one of the better ones of the season.

Ginga Densetsu WEED- B

The show certainly is fun to watch. The animation is better than average. The story is straightforward. If I had to define this show, it would be a Shounen anime like Bleach, Naruto, etc. Except, all the main characters are dogs.

So from the standpoint of a Shounen anime, it hits all marks well. It's not a bad shounen, but it's certainly no Naruto. Just very average. A big problem for me was relating to the characters. I cannot relate to dogs, obviously.

It's a story about a group of dogs who fought to reclaim their land in the mountains of Japan, and won. Eventually, a monster came and took over their land, and a young pup was taken away to another place.

It's easy to explain what will happen. The pup will grow up, become stronger (after all, he's got the blood of the Legendary dog that defeated the monstrous bear who took the land) and as he grows up, he'll eventually fight the monster and so on. It's a typical shounen story.

If you're looking for something different, or to expand your experience of anime, it's a good show. I've already watched the earlier incarnation (not a prequel, it's a side story) so I didn't feel any need to watch more of this.
