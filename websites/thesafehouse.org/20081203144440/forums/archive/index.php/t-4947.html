You can pull with the xbow but you need to pull at pretty much max range and run back about 1/4 that far as well - if the mob is not too fast then it will not get to you before your restealth timer has reset - takes some practice but if you pull past hubby then he can get agro easy and when the mob turns your immediatly behind it.

I use this as a solo technique: tag, run, turn, stealth, Perforate Artery (CS style from front).

That would allow your hubby to med up while you pull.

As for spec I can recomend 18 in Dual weild - the deagro style is not very effective to off set our masive damage but has a 'large bonus to defence', this helps you to evade which is good because it will be very dificult for your hubby to keep agro off you (or anyone bar a paladin with heal chant for that matter).

Elsebet,

I think you have a potentially powerful pair. You wanna pull with a crossbow? Go right on ahead. Like Aach says, you can run back a bit and restealth. When you run back past hubby, he can use his stun then his shout(s). This should give him the aggro since xbows do poop for damage.

Once he has aggro he should turn the MOB's back to you so you can get your backstab in without having to maneuver around too much.

Oh and about fighting "just us"....that's only if you want it to be. Infiltrators aren't too welcome in a lot of groups, but Minstrels are very welcome. His mezzing skills will be in high demand. He'll get a lot of tells asking for groups. You just go with him.

Two people working in perfect harmony can be impressive.

I play two computers at the same time, playing a mostly PAC spec'd healer and my shadowblade. I can kill reds using the same amount of setup time (less time when recovery is factored) as I would be able to kill yellows as a solo SB, and it is safer because of the mez/healing ability in those cases where things just don't fall my way.

When you get to higher levels and have graduated from the back positional openers to PA, which is a front positional opener, you may want to have your hubby pull. I don't know how useful a minstrel is at range pulling in comparison to an infiltrator, man I wish I could use X-bows heh.

I do all my pulling with my healer (mostly b/c SBs have shite for range attacks). I setup my SB in the area where I want the fight to happen. I cast my pulling spell and run my healer directly onto my shadowblade. when the mob gets there I PA with assassin and then start to maneuver the healer for casting stuns or attacking with his damage added hammer (which bothers me a little when he hits for more than my assassin, but i digress)

after the first kill it gets much easier because I don't tend to loot much so I can see the loot bag from the distance and know where I need to go (if i'm in a new area or outdoor zone).

On oranges I typically don't even have to poison to have a safe victory. Reds I always poison. I have killed purples, using 3 poisons, but those are not really worth the time involved in the fight.
