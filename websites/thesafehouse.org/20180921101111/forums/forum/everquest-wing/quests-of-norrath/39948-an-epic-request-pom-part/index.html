            I am not saying you are wrong in that a parse would give the right answer, but I do not believe that is the way that it is intended.

            The rogue "long fight" would probably be the fight with the jester. Some classes have longer "long fight" fights, some shorter. Enchanters don't even have a fight.

            The reason I don't believe DPS is the intended measure (even though it might come up with the correct answer) is that everything else with this test has a convenient in-game way of measuring.

            1. The height test you can use the brick/stone wall behind the halflings to measure the change in height.

            2. For the sound test, you can use the floor tiles. The halflings have two floor tiles between them. Go the the opposite side of the room, and you cannot hear any of them. Move forward one tile at a time and when you can hear the first one you will be about 3 tiles away from the wall. At this point, move forward two squares and listen (you will hear the first and second halfling). Rinse and repeat until you get the off sounding halfling.

            3. Pick pockets, you just have to be able to count to two.

            4. Tummy rubbing, it is pretty much one of observation.

            5. The last test definately has something to do with attacking each halfling (the video posted in the Melee section of SOE's forums shows that). There has to be something that we can detect in game that will give us the answer without resorting to tools outside of the game. I guessed on this stage and eventually guessed correctly (25% chance to be right). I suspect but am not sure, that it involves poisons or backstabbing. But what it is I am at a loss.

              I tried the poisons I normally use and tried unusual ones (even "Bixie Sting"). Unfortunately, all halflings are high resistant against weapon and poison procs. They also avoided every offensive discipline.
              So I shoved that ideas aside and focused again on the plant's hints:

              "Bixie's stinger is its primary weapon" ... "high resistance to their painful stings" ... there's no mention for poisons, just the stings itself.

              So I went for pure piercing with weapons without procs (or at least perma-resisted ones).
              I did no backstabs because high damage spikes even on the correct halfling would place this halfling between the others.

              I gave each hobbit between 4 and 6 minutes pure "stings" to get a good average DPS parse.

              Then I compared and took the one with least average damage taken.

              I don't know if there's a long-term emote like for other classes in their parts. Honestly, I doubt it because if it occurs e.g. after 10 minutes fighting the correct halfling you would need up to 40 min just on this step. Add the previous halfling riddles and the other two parts of that phase and I'm very sure you have already run out of quest time.

              Besides the facts above, I'm with you ... it's not a smart thing if you have to use 3rd party tools to figure out the correct halfling but for the moment it's the best we have ...
