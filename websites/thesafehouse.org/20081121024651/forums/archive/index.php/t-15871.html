Vanguard is sponsoring a Fantasy Fiction Contest. Basically you write a story, submit it to the Safehouse staff, we post all the stories at the same time, then everyone votes on the best one and is presented as one of the finalists on the Vanguard site. The winner gets into the Vanguard Beta and their story gets added to the game.

It is interesting to note that you can NOT use Vanguard's timeline, history or known characters, nor any other copyrighted characters, places, etc. Be sure to read the full rules below.

Dec. 8th - Submission Deadline to shmods@thesafehouse.org

EDIT: Entry update: We received 10 entries by the deadline

Dec. 10th - Stories get posted on The Safehouse and voting begins

Dec 17th - Voting closes

Dec. 22nd - Contest winner announced on the VG site

General Rules

1. Contestants must be registered members of the Official Vanguard Forums ("Forums"). All entries must be submitted and displayed under the contestant's Forum name. It is the contestant's responsibility to ensure that his Forum registration information is accurate and current.

2. Submissions must be emailed to any one of the Affiliated Fan Sites listed below. All submissions must be received by the Affiliated Fan Sites by midnight PST on Wednesday, December 8, 2004.

3. Only one entry per contestant. Contestants submitting entries to more than one Affiliated Fan Site will be disqualified.

4. The Affiliated Fan Site may determine, in its sole discretion, whether a particular submission is appropriate to be displayed to the public. All accepted submissions will be publicly posted on each respective Affiliated Fan Site on Friday, December 10, 2004.

5. Members of the community are invited to visit the Affiliated Fan Sites and vote for which submission they like the best. The public voting for the finalists ends on midnight PST on Friday, December 17, 2004.

6. The finalist with the most votes at each Affiliated Fan Site will be submitted to Sigil Games Online. The winner of the contest will be chosen from among the finalists by a panel of judges comprised of Sigil Games Online Team members. Judging will be based on originality, creativity, technique and style. Decisions of the judges are final.

7. The winner will be displayed on the Official Vanguard Site on December 22, 2004.

8. Contestant may not submit an entry to a Fan Site for which he or she works or volunteers.

9. Submission must be no longer than 1500 words.

10. All material must be original. The story should read as a work of fiction that would be told to players as a story within the game. There should be no reference to any other work of fiction nor any reference to Vanguard’s timeline, history or its known characters.

11. Any reference to any copyrighted name, character or place, or a real name or place, or any variation thereof is grounds for disqualification.

12. Vanguard is a game for a wide public audience. Please keep this in mind and refrain from using unacceptable language or questionable material. The decision to reject an entry based on content deemed unacceptable will be at the sole discretion of each Fan Site Administrator and Sigil Games Online.

13. Prize: The winning story will be put into the game for players to read at their leisure, most likely in libraries throughout the world. The winner will also receive a spot in Vanguard’s first public Beta.

14. Upon submission, finalists are consenting to their work being displayed on the Vanguard Site. Additionally, the finalist will be asked to relinquish certain rights in the submission to ensure that the story can be included in the lore of Vanguard.

The above General Rules explain the terms and conditions upon which Sigil Games Online offers the Fantasy Fiction Contest. Your submission of any fiction to an Affiliated Fan Site constitutes your acceptance to these General Rules. No purchase is necessary to enter or to win. It is expressly understood that the Fantasy Fiction Contest is a contest of skill and not a game of chance. The Fantasy Fiction Contest is void where prohibited by law, rule or regulation.

Those are the rules, now have at thee and may the best story win!

The intent of this is a common sense interpretation, I believe. Don't write a story about Drizzt (no one should, after all), or a story that takes place in Qeynos or Rohan or London. However if you write a story about "Trueheart Evilsmiter" or whatever sissy name you gave your paladin, you don't have to go on a search to find out if anyone has ever used that name in another work before. A general rule of thumb is that anything creative is copyrighted - just because it doesn't have the little (C) jobbie doesn't mean it's fair game. Don't use someone else's character.

The theme should be Fantasy, I'd expect, since this will become part of Vanguard's in-game fiction, and Vanguard is a fantasy genre game.
