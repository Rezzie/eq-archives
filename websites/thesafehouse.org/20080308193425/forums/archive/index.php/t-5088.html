If you want a crit blade, my suggestion is you start by making an LA/Envenom/Axe heavy character. Then respecing once you get to Rvr Levels. Auto training stealth is really not viable for a CS SB unless your being PL'd. If you don't have stealth there is no reason to train CS.

I am very happy with my PVE abilities even with the bonus of Double Frost being reduced from +300% to +200% of base. I would choose Norse as a race, the benefits of Dex/Quick are not out wieghed by what Con (hps &amp; damage reduction) and str offer. Also putting creation points into Con (15) and str (10). For spec Max LA, divide remaining points between Envenom and Axe evenly. Stop once you reach the template max and start saving points for stealth.

What you end up with in PVE, is a character that is easier to level. This means you will more likely get into Rvr fucntional levels faster before burning out. You can solo (piercing opponants), you can tank (piercing opponants, or Slash/Crush if you max those resistances) , and you add alot of group benefits with damage being greater than a berzerker (w/ poisens).

The exp rate gained is great because you kill things so fast. You can respec to what you want to be in Rvr later and be the best you can be in Pve now.

Although you will see pure SZ's posting they are not happy now. If you have envenom skill you are still viable. Really as a melee class you will only be rivaled by Armsman, Hero, and Infiltrators.

lot's of good info so far, except whoever said to take cs to 50, that's a bad idea IMO.

you're looking at 40st,40cs,40en,40sw

my thoughts:

stealth: 36 is all the stealth you need, +11 items +3 from RR gets you 50 eventually and anything over that is pretty well agreed upon as useless.

cs: whatever you take it to, unless you've just got extra points to burn stop it right after a new attack skill, i.e. 34 for creeping death (good) or 39 for stunning stab (ss said to suck, but can get cs to 50 w/ bonuses.)

en: my considered opinion is that 29 is where to stop env; w/ bonuses you get the 40 DOT. i think this is the best bang for the buck. taking it above 29 you have to go all the way to 34 just to get the next usefel (forget the 42 snare) poison, the 45 DOT. above that you do get a new DOT, disease and weakening poison from base env 38 (9 items, +3 RR = 50) but they're not all that much better than the 2nd best poisons and not worth the points IMO.

sw: w/ 39 base in sw (or main weap) you can get to 50, yes. most ppl agree that weapon spec > 50 is more beneficial than cs > 50, i certainly do from all i've read. for one thing, if your weap spec &lt; 50 w/ bonuses your damage will range from 75%-125%, if its >= 50 w/ bonuses you get 100%-150%, that's a difference on every swing of 25%. furthermore, if you have a sw spec at 47 or more you can get to 50 w/ just RR and maybe at the beginning a little sw bonus. if you need +9 or +11 sw items to get to 50 you'll end up using a dropped sword to get the sword bonus needed to raise spec to 50. drops are often qual 89%, vs. player crafted weaps which can be qual 99%, that's again a dif. of 10% damage on every swing; and that's a big dif.

this said i'm looking at a critblade template of:

st: 36
cs: 44
sw: 47
en: 29

38 points leftover (i think, char builder is down.) i'm not getting the last point in stealth until i see how i like this build, b/c already it would take the 2 promised respecs to go to my 2nd favorite template (5-spec: 39ax,39la,34cs,29en,36st)
