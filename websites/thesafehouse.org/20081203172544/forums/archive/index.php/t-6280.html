Here's my review so far - picked up the game Friday, and played on and off through the weekend.

I :heart creating new costumes for superheroes, lots of options - can go wild coming up with some truly unique looks. It's too bad there isn't a way to update them once you've started the game - one little error in costume design (had blended the gloves the wrong way) and I found myself deleting the character and starting from scratch. One of the ones I was working on almost looked like Seven of Nine :borg before I realized what I was doing and changed it around.

I also am having problems trying to figure out how to swing the camera in front of my 'toon so I can get a frontal screenshot - everything I've taken so far of her is of her butt. Not to say that it isn't a rather shapely butt, but I'd love to show off her glowing red eyes to my friend... speaking of which...

.tga formatted screenshots are REALLY huge - I've been too lazy to reformat before sending to a friend who might be interested in playing - so I've been sending them 1 at a time to keep my email server from barfing on me. ;) Names of characters don't get saved in screenshots - so it's a good idea to write down the name that went with the shot if it was something funny like "The Original Bling" wearing a purple pimp suit. ;)

Group battles are cool - but all of those flashy effects do get a bit disorienting and I had some trouble finding my next target - is the reason I have spell effects off on EQ raids, just gets too hard to see - I will have to look and see if I can customize my display options to clean that up a little bit.

$14.95 a month is a little pricey, but I don't expect that I'll be playing for as long as I've been playing EverQuest... I'm already at level 8 after about 12 hour played in this game - I have a feeling I might hit the level cap of this game relatively quickly if the levelling difficulty curve doesn't go up a bit more swiftly
