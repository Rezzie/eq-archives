One tip on a Stone/Stone, you may want to not get the primary skills Mud Pots, and Rock Armor instead going for the power pool stamina. Stone/Stone eats endurance like you wouldn't believe.

The damage is good but the attacks are somewhat slow to recharge, so you'll want to be using stone hands, stone mallet and heavy mallet as much as possible for damage. Unfourtunately heavy mallet drains about 15% of your endurance and that's with 2 DO endurance drain lowerer thingies.

Mud Pots is an OK skill, since it's PBAOE is decent for holding aggro, but nothing killer, plus it's going to further eat your eundurance.

Many people will scoff at not getting rock armor, since the armor set is what makes a tank, but you can get stone skin, which is an always on resistance to smashing and lethal, the same things rock armor protects against. Plus once you get up high enough to get Granite armor, you'll have a smashing lethal armor.

Of course there are skills in the secondary powerset you can not pick up. Of these I think hurl boulder is probably the best candidate to drop, followed by either fault or tremor, but I find both of these to be quite useful, that may be personal play style however.

Really isn't a matter of IT, really. Stone just looks like crap, literally. Ice also obstructs the costume. And Fire isn't as defensively oriented. So if you want to play a good tank and look good, Invul is your choice.

That's also why you see so many SS tankers. Image. SS itself isn't the best. Overall, it is mediocre. Stone does what SS does, but does it better. Axe deals damage the quickest and does the most knockdowns. Ice is probably the best defensive secondary, with slows and ice patch. Fire does more damage to groups of mobs. Energy is the master of stun, which is way better than knockback when you use US/Rooted. And so on. Not saying SS is bad, it isn't, just that the others have their own perks. SS is the classic comic book melee though. Punch stuff, no energy, no ice, no fire. And that's why it gets picked a lot.

I've got a feeling all the MUST HAVE crap will fade away as well as you level. Invul tankers take quite a few skills to develop. 3 resists, US, TI, Invincibility. That's 6 primary powers right there by level 18. And you'll probably want Dull Pain too. Add to that provoke and/or taunt, a precursor to a travel power, and a travel power, and, well, that's quite a few skills. If you want offense as well...even more skills. In the early levels, you're making hard choices. But as you get more and more power slots, more and more enhancements, you'll be able to have very nice defense and have a few good (for a tanker) attacks. Mix in SOs which make everything better still, gap widens between tanker and non-tanker.

We tankers just take time to develop.
