EZ_Lady Kaylan
06-05-03, 09:50 PM
Same as your typical RPG ... imagination, pencil &amp; paper, and bucketloads of dice. There's the main sourcebook and then shitloads of add-ons. There's basically one book for every major land mass in the world and then all kinds of other junk as well (the Atlantis and Japan books were some of my faves). The ability scores and skill system will be totally different than anything you're used to (this is assuming you've at least played D&amp;D) but it's still pretty simple to learn.

Rifts by far is one of my favorite settings / systems. One of the things I like most about it was you could combine all the other Palldium games into it. We usually played with a ton of Robotech mechs and used TMNT for mutant creation. Also bust open the Ninja's &amp; Superspies book for cool martial arts and gadgets.

I don't know where you live Tribal, but if you're interested in picking up any Rifts books (or any books by Palladium) and near a Wizards of the Coast / The Game Keepers store, they're all 70% off cover price. Can't beat that ... roughly equates to $7-8 per book.
