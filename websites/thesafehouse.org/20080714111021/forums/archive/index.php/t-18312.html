These sites depend on people running the addon to collect data, so it may or may not be indicative of the whole server population, depending on exactly who chooses to use it.

That's why warcraftrealms lists how many updates they got and how long the last update was ago - so one can get a somewhat good idea about the validity of the data.

If a server has an 2:1 A:H ratio, but there have been twice as many horde updates than alliance, it's easy to see that the ration most likely is even higher in Alliance's favor. On the other hand, if there was only one horde update, but 3 alliance, then horde might actually be more numberous than just 2:1.

Not really. Just to compare one from each side, Humans get a huge PVE bonus from the sword/mace skill bonus, whereas orcs only get it with one skill (axe). Further, orcs had the only racial "bonus" that could kill you, and another that only works versus some kinds of stuns. Humans get a passive combat, a passive reputation, a passive stat boost, and an active stealth detection. Slightly better for humans but nothing really unbalanced.

To compare two class choicse, orcs versus dwarves as hunters, I think most people would vote for orcs pre-60, and then dwarves at 60 because of how good +Skill is when you fight things on raids.

Overall the "good" racial abilities are fairly evenly distributed amongst everyone, but all of the really stupid ones seem to have gotten dumped on the horde (mostly on trolls). The Horde has the racial skill people bitch about the most (Will of the Forsaken) but they ignore that Gnomes have an equally annoying immunity skill in Escape Artist.


On the whole, the entire game really is slanted in favor of the Alliance[1], but I think that's more a symptom of the population imbalance than a cause. Far more people were Alliance in beta, so they got a ton more feedback on quest, abilities, etc. The real problem seems to be preconceived notions about what character you want to play.


[1] It seems like roughly half the interesting fights were consciously designed around cross-faction abilities, and the other half take certain Alliance-only ability combinations for granted. I'm sure it'd seem a lot more fair if Grounding Totem worked right and wasn't disabled when you need it most.
