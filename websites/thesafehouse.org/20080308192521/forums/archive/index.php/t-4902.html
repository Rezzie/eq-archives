Have to take into account the spec level of the weapon type that you're hitting with too.

Unspec'ed you'll do 25-100% damage. 2/3 spec you'll do 75-125% of base damage, etc, on up to 100-150% base damage at full spec.

Now that I think about it, not sure if you're asking about base damage to be used in the above calculation, or base damage as in normal melee hit.

Level plays into it passively. It affects the con of the mobs you fight (your level compared to theirs), and against lower con mobs you do extra damage, against higher con mobs you do less damage. Having a higher level also affects the con of the weapon you're using, and your DPS cap for the level. -- Huff McTuff, 41 Nightshade, Pendragon

It is very hard to calculate, because of AF, and how it might relate to weaponskill. The most raw form of the equation is damage * spd * qua * con, which makes sense, but isn't really provable yet. Any other modifications are really speculative, and are also very hard to pinpoint. Static tests vs exact same monster/player are really the only way to accurately test something like this, with pvp being the best option, because you know the other player's af beforehand. Unfortunately, absorb also comes into play somewhere, and its role is also questioned a lot, with many people thinking absorb is the %chance to completely absorb a blow, as opposed to a %reduction to damage dealt.

I think they have done a neat job at making DAoC harder to min-max than many people thought it would be. Dither Disgustipated
