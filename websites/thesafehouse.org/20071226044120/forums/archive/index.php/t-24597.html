Marimite definitely is one of my favorite Shoujo of all time. It was one of the few shoujo that spread out to the male-dominated otaku fandom. A suprise, considering the lack of fanservice, action, deep plot, and other shonen/seinen properties.

Even more suprising its success, being given an unplanned second season. The characters are all interesting in their own way, which I quite like. Most characters, you get a strong first impression of (in anime as whole, I mean) but the girls of Marimite were more.. Mysterious? Maybe not the right word.

Your first impression of them would be similar, no matter which girl. But you see the subtle differences between all of them that make them who they are. I loved that. Ginko of Mushishi had the same effect on me, I had no impression of him upon being introduced to him, but during the series yoiu learn his subtle hypocrisies, passion, cruelty, and everything that makes him extraordinary, but still very human.

Ah, bringing up fond memories is fun.
