Quote: Ye got that right, lad! I'll take quality over quantity any day o' the week. NWN Safehouse will be about role-playing and adventure, AD&amp;D style, and not about the latest "l33t" drop from UberMOB #1443 at spawn camp 1266, 190. Bah! *Remmi scowls*

This is something I typed up 2 years ago when NWN2 was in the early stages as a possible cool way to play the game in real RP. I still think something like this would be pretty cool, if the game design worked for it.

I originally wrote it for my guild, which is a good-alligned guild and has people who know each other OOC relatively well, to explain why I make a point of mentioning the good-evil thing

Quote:

Now I don't for the life of me know how flexible the game design will be, but if I had my drothers, this is how I think I'd like it to look.

1) Have one primary city in which much of the main STORY takes place. The city would be populated with merchants,
beggars, nobles, etc., perhaps along the lines of Sanctuary from Thieves' World (or really any fantasy city, heh). The city would have to be large enough that players wouldn't always be bumping into each other every five minutes (see 2
below for the reason why). However, the outside areas would also be available for all-around monster killing and dungeon romping.

2) There would be 3 primary factions vying for control of the city. 2 'legit' noble houses, say, and an underworld. I would propose that those who want to be "Evil" (or perhaps Chaotic) would play their characters as part of the underworld a la the Mockers in Feist's Riftwar. There would be heirarchy within it, and as characters grew, they would be 'promoted.' This would provide an outlet for those who are
interested in pickpocketing or mugging or the like. If they choose to mug/steal from a player, then there is the possibility for PvP.

The 2 legit houses would be along the lines of the Capulet/Montague relationship from Romeo &amp; Juliet. The two houses would hate each other, and would brawl if given
the chance (hence you need a large city to aviod constant braws at the one tavern, heh). The members of these houses would be fanatically loyal to their leaders, such that any character type could be played as a 'legit' character.
You want to be a thief or assassin? You're not 'evil' (or chaotic) if you think you're doing the right thing by killing someone from te opposing hosue. I would propose that players wishing to play as a legit character be assigned
randomly to one or the other house. This will a) keep cliques from forming in-game by people who already know each other well and b) encourage roleplay, since players would be encouraged to take actions against their RL friends. As
players become more involved (and RP better), then they can be awarded higher rank (beyond just level) within their organization. At some point I think it would be great if all the major heirarchy characters are player-controlled, such that the 2 Dukes, or whatever, plot against one another, design raids or political machinations, and direct other PCs to actually do the dirty work. Or even if a Duchess tries to assume the power of her house by conspiring with the other Duke to have her husband killed. Extending this, perhaps there could be 'secret' factions (again assigned, to aviod hard feelings and name calling) within the 3 uber-organizations. I don't know if you ever played Paranoia (great game), but all characters had a secret society to which they belonged,
and which really drove their actions. Perhaps all the characters could be assigned a secondary 'true' loyalty as another motivator.

I guess what it all comes down to is that I really believe that you want convincing role play beyond a bunch of people saying thee and thou while hacking and slashing for experience, the key figures in the game will have to eventually be run by actual people so that they can direct the action in a rational way. While some may find this restrictive, I believe that what most people are interested in in MMOGs is more than just getting to kill things in a group, but an actual story.

So to recap, here's what I envision: a) a central city in which the Story takes place, and the active involvement of players within the story b) outside 'dungeon' areas in which people can dungeon romp if that's their desire and c) randomly (or
non-randomly) assigned factions that hate each other enough to PvP, as well as 'true' factions that are unknown to others and which REALLY guide players' actions.
