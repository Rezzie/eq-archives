I don't believe this person's intentions were in any way noble. Yeah, he latched on to the "see how hard it is to get suspended" line, but basically he was just out for attention and a feeling of power.

In general, I love EQ's policies with guides and GMs: they are always polite; they take time and examine situations before making decisions; they try to respond to every request.

In this case, though, that system failed. They should have dealt with the situation much more quickly and decisively. We should not have seen a number of guides and GMs come and go before anything happened. I could understand following a chain of apprentice guide, guide, and GM - that's reasonable. Someone needed to realize, "Okay, this guy is a disturbance and needs to be neutrallized immediately."

He should not have had the oportunity to continue to annoy the players in the zone while each guide to come along tried to talk some reason into him. He should have been muted, or summoned to some mystical penalty box, where they could talk to him.

Say you were at a real-life play/banquet/mall, and someone started making a scene like this. You'd be unhappy if security guards came up one by one and tried to talk to him, while he continued to make a scene. You'd then be very unhappy if police showed up and wandered off one by one and tried to talk to him while he continued to disrupt your day.

(I recognize, of course, that I only have the troublemaker's own account of the situation, and that it may be misleading.)

In general, I'm impressed with Verant's commitment to a pleasant gaming experience; this situation just fell through the cracks.

I have complete contempt for narrator of the article, as well as anyone who would tout him as a hero.
