02-28-03, 02:36 PM
Druids can be useful. But you have to be willing to play them the way they are. Just remember that you are rarely going to be the first choice for the situation. Main use for a 58+ druid is as the cleric replacement. Of course a real cleric is much better at healing but if there is not one around, you can fill in under most conditions.

You do get some spells that have their uses. Hand of Ro and the Ro debuff line comes in handy. But you have to watch your aggro with them since they really piss mobs off. The Ro line has its main use in lowering the fire resist so that others can nuke for full but since it is a small amount of mana, I tend to use it also for the lower ATK / AC, even though I have no idea if it is helping at all, really.

If we have a cleric in the group, then my job becomes backing them up and being the Emergency Response Team. Usually, I keep myself at close to full mana at all times and just wait in case something goes wrong. Then I jump in with plenty of heals, root some adds and if all fails, evac.

Playing a druid has become less fun due to nerfs that have no logic behind them but it still can be fun. You just have to adjust to the fact that you are not usually the first choice for the job. If you want to play a character that is really wanted, you need to make a shaman.

Every character has their job to do and you need to pick one that makes you happy. For druids, 58+ means you mostly heal. At least the way I play. I know that the objective is to keep the group alive and my nukes and such are really too lame to make much difference, while healing the heavy DPS dudes lets us kill the mobs and survive. There might not be much glory in being healer or ERT but those I group with tend to appreciate my ubar skillz :) Fanra
