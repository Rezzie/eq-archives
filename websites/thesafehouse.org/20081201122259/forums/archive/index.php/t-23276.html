Wiz or Sorc, what's the differences?

Wizards are a swiss-army-knife of spells, but can't cast as many per rest period. Sorcerors only learn a set amount of spells but offset this by being able to cast the spells they know a lot more before needing to rest, they also cast quicker. Wizards are good for nuking, but are mainly sought after for their adaptability, they're able to swap out spells in the middle of a quest to gear up more for crowd control, AE damage, single target damage, repairing Warforged, or buffing. Sorcerors must carefully choose what spells to take because they will define themselves through these decisions. Typically sorcs are better suited for doing magical dps because their spellpoint reserves are greater and they cast their spells quicker, not to mention that wizards must 'prepare' the spells they're going to cast, where sorcerors can cast any of the spells that they know. So yeah... it can be a -really- tough choice.

Personally, I prefer wizards because of their versatility, as it's very difficult to make a 'mistake' while taking spells as a wizard because you can just add more in by buying a scroll and scribing it. Sorcerors are more dependent on using wands to fill in the gaps and said wands do not 'scale' up in damage, they do the same damage/have the same duration/repair the same amount as if you were 'naturally' casting them as the same level as when you are first able to get the spells. So some spells (Like burning hands, magic missile) pale in comparison to the damage you could do if you were casting them 'naturally' at 5th level vs. a wand doing 1st level damage. Some other spells, like (Feather Fall, Charm Person), don't scale up with level and as such, it doesn't really matter if you cast them from a wand or not.

So yeah, there ya go... a quick and dirty rundown of the arcane casters, hehe...
