I would like to expand on the ideas posted several posts up by Peter Corbin. In it, he says there is the 5-5-9-5-9 pattern, which might apply to all classes. I actually think there are 3 such schemes:

(Dawnshroud - Mons Letalis - Scarlet Desert - Tenebrous Mountains - Twilight Sea)
I: 5-5-9-5-9
II: 5-5-9-7-7
III: 5-5-11-5-7

If you observe the 15 different classes, you can roughly assign five classes to each of these three schemes.

I: BRD, BST, SHD, SHM, WIZ
II: MAG, CLR, DRU, ENC, NEC
III: RNG, WAR, MNK, PAL, ROG

Of these, at least BRD, BST, MAG, RNG and WAR are matching completely. There are some anomalies in the others:

SHD: Missing one Mob. Probably found in Mons Letalis.
SHM: Missing one Mob. In addition, there is one Mob too many listed for Scarlet Desert. Mobs are missing from Dawnshroud Peaks and Twilight Sea. Could also be made to match III, if there's a Mob too much in Twilight, and Mobs missing from both Dawnshroud and Scarlet Desert.
WIZ: At least five Mobs missing, assuming the requirements for the pants are completely wrong (error by Verant). Adding these in different ways could make WIZ fit with I, II and III.
CLR: Missing one Mob. In addition, there is one Mob too many listed for Scarlet Desert. Mobs missing from Dawnshroud Peaks and Mons Letalis.
DRU: One Mob too many in Mons Letalis, one missing in Tenebrous Mountains
ENC: Could be in I and II (see WIZ), but missing two Mobs in any case. If in I, missing one Mob in Twilight while having one too many in Tenebrous. If in II, missing one Mob in Tenebrous while having one too many in Twilight. In both cases, two Mobs missing from Dawnshroud.
NEC: Missing one Mob. Also, One Mob too many in Dawnshroud Peaks. Missing Mobs in Mons Letalis and Scarlet Desert.
MNK: One Mob too many in Mons Letalis, one too little in Tenebrous.
PAL: One Mob unknown, probably in Scarlet Desert.
ROG: One Mob too many from Twilight Sea, one missing from Scarlet Desert if it belongs to III. If it belongs to I, it has one too many Mobs in Scarlet and one too little in Twilight Sea.

I am not saying that the reported information is wrong; after all, Verant is not known to be 100 percent consistent in these things at all, as has been proven before. Either these hint at hidden 'inconsistencies', Verant side, or they are errors in the listings so far.
