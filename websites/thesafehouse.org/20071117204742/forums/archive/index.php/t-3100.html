Actually, something similar happened in a campaign I was playing in.

One of the two thieves in the party had repeatedly lifted items from encounters and kept them for himself. So while the rest of the party was using +1 to +2 weapons with maybe one effect, he had a +3 axe and a +2 katana. Same deal with armor, misc items.

So eventually, the cleric of the party comes across an item he's looking for. He makes his will save to not use it, and stuffs it in his bag. When we ask him about it, he tells us not to worry about it. Eventually, somehow or another, it gets pulled out of the bag. It's a Deck of Many Things.

So, most people in the party decide they'll have a go at drawing a card. Uber-thief draws a card, has to fight 'Death'. With his weapons and armor, it was no problem for him. Other players draw cards, get a keep, get a magic item, avoid any one bad encounter, and so forth. I for one decided not to risk it, as I was pretty happy with my character as he was. Then uber-thief decides to draw again... and loses all of his magic items.

He left early, and stopped playing the game after that.

The next card drawn was a major exp bonus.

The people that drew cards did so by choice and got what they got randomly, yet on the whole it managed to balance out the party fairly well for the DM, without him actually forcing anything on us.
