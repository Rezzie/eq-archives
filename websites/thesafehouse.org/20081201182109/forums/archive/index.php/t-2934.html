Well, I've asked all the people not IN this game, so why not try here....

A friend of mine is a long-time D&amp;D player. He has a character that has been converted through all editions, and he would like, for his birthday, to convert him to third finally and play a high level (17th) game.

I have volunteered to run this, but I have a problem. I've never really designed a game before, just run other people's.

My thought at the moment for a plot. Aging Evil Dragon is adding magic items like mad to his hoard. In fact, he doesn't care about the boring coinage and all, so he is paying wizards to summon people with magic items for him to add to his hoard, and they get all the non-magical loot.

Enter our adventurers, who wake up in various cells, having been summoned in this fashion. After they break out and take out the guards, they will eventually find a cache of items that hasn't been catelogued and sent to the lair.

Currently, I'm wanting to set this in Forgotten Realms, so I'm considering making this a Brown Dragon, which is a desert dwelling dragon, typically with a lair about 1000 feet below the ground. In this case, I'm going to have a keyed teleporter in his lair, plus a spiral staircase as means of getting out.

Questions:

1) Does this sound like a good plan?
2) What do you think would be appropriate hirelings for him? I'm thinking that for the summoning there would be at least a half dozen wizards, plus there would be some less arcane sorts for keeping an eye on prisoners. Oh, and some collection of pets who would be eating any adventurers the dragon didn't. I'm also considering including a half dragon daughter.
3) Another idea, is having a delegation from the Cult of the Dracoliche there, trying to persuade him, but only have them run into that group if they have to run up the stairs.



Phew. Hope no one minds the big post, and thank you in advance.
