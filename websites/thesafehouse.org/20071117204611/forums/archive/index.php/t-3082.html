I don't think I'd be very good at running Shadowrun.

NPCs having their own agenda is good, but I tend to do better in a fantasy setting where they tend to be much more open about things.

The way I see it, Powers in a fantasy game tend to gain their power through holdings of lands, loyalties, hardware, and prestige.

Much of what they do is done within the confines of a code of honor, and underhanded dealings (defined as dealings that violate the code) tend to hurt as much or more than they help. Sure, you can lie, cheat and steal to gain hardware or even lands. But if (and when) it becomes known that you do such things, you lose prestige and the loyalty and/or trust of your subordinates.


I also see most powers in the world as being inherently conservative. They may want to expand their own power, but they want to retain what they have even more. Risks are to be avoided, if at all possible. Safe, incremental expansion via a plethora of small actions and plots is much preferred to explosive, go-for-broke takeovers. Such takeovers tend to make other powers uneasy, and bring alliances about among them to deal with the upstart (you) who is upsetting the status quo.

Gauntlets of Ogre power are magical hand coverings, after all.

However... if the ghost is only hit by +1 weapons or better in ethereal form, the Gauntlets of Ogre Power aint gonna help.

The to hit and damage bonuses granted by the gauntlets are the way the system simulates the advantage in melee combat accrued by being immensely strong. Someone wearing Gauntlets of Ogre Power, is, in effect, the same as a person who has a natural 18/00 Strength wearing +0 gloves. There is no inherent magical attack bonus in the gauntlets, though they do count as magical for creatures hit only by magic (of an unspecified level)
