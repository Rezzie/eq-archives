Maintaining the sticky, here (http://www.thesafehouse.org/forums/showthread.php?t=24244) has proven to be more work than most of us really want to do. So I figure instead of letting it die and take up space, I'll go ahead and turn it into a more pratical use, a SH recommendation thread.

Now, please feel free to debate what I'm about to say. If you disagree in any way, we can work it out. It's just my brainstorm of sorts, and hasn't been completely thought out.

Here's what I want to do. I want to make a SH recommendation thread. However, everyone has their own niche. Everyone will think a certain title is something the other guy should see, and due to his love for the anime and participating in related fansites, threads, etc, gain a perception that everyone else agrees. When it's basically just those in similar niche.

It's hard to say which titles fall in this category, and which doesn't. I'm working on a rough draft of sorts to give an idea of what's included and what isn't, and why, which will be posted later.

I want titles that have cross-genre appeal. What's considered a cross-genre appeal in the USA and Japan are completely different, as I will demostrate.

Cowboy Bebop is undoubtly a humongous influence on the USA crowd, however it's disposable to the Japanese. Conversely, Super Dimensional Fortress (SDF) Macross is considered a revolution in Japan, but in the USA only appeals to mecha fans.

On this issue I don't have any particular feelings one way or another. So think hard about what you want to recommend - we're not just recommending to the casual anime fan, but even to the longtime fans who may have missed some titles of importance. I want to try to make it as close to an list where you can put a blindfold on and randomly pick any title, and KNOW it will be a high quality title.

I adore Ichigo Mashimaro, and think it's one of my favorite anime. But would I recommend it to ANYONE as a high quality title? Not particularly, it's a high quality title to those who are in the moe~ niche.

Conversely, Suzumiya Haruhi no Yuutsu would qualify as a high quality title that you can recommend to anyone. It gets solid marks on all fronts - animation, story, and music. The fandom speaks for itself, there is not a single person who does not know who Suzumiya Haruhi is in the world of anime fandom.

Opinions? Recommendations? Just give me ideas here. If you disagree with another poster's recommendation, don't trash the title. People don't like hearing their favorite titles suck. Explain objectively what makes the title not qualify that excludes your personal bias.

Thanks.

What's the difference between shoujo-style and shounen-style magical girl?

Shounen style, like Nanoha's, incorporates very shounen-specific traits and is marketed towards an male audience. You know, powerups, screaming, I'll never forgive you!, etc. Shoujo style incorporates girly things, like accessories, love and friendship, OUR TWO HEARTS AS ONE, PRETTY CURE MARBLE SCREW!, etc.

I'd recommend noting that Maria-sama ga Miteru isn't plain shoujo, but shoujo-ai. For people like me, that makes all the difference. :wink

Actualy, Maria-sama ga Miteru was marketed towards the shoujo crowd to begin with. Male otaku never noticed the existence of Maria-sama until the novels were well underway, it became so popular with the female crowd that it spilled over to the male crowd and got embraced by the male otaku. With the obvious exception of Sei, any -ai content is the figment of male otaku's imaginations.

That being said, it would be worth noting that it has a strong shoujo-ai feel for male otaku with vivid fantasies.

Figured I'd add a few more suggestions to the list: Millenium Actress, Jin-Roh, and Serial Experiments Lain.

The first two movies I just watched today and enjoyed both for very different reasons. Millenium Actress (http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/encyclopedia/anime.php?id=374) is a very unique movie about a director and cameraman doing a documentary about a famous aging actress. As they interview her they hop around in her movies and in parts of her real life, as if they were actually there. It's a very enjoyable movie and I really liked the ending as well.

Jin-Roh: The Wolf Brigade (http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/encyclopedia/anime.php?id=161) is a slower, graphically violent movie which deals both in a man's emotions about being an elite soldier for a government organization, and in being one of the chess pieces in a larger plot between government organizations. It's a darker, brooding style anime that conveys what it wants to very well.

Serial Experiments Lain (http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/encyclopedia/anime.php?id=166) is a series I bought a while back and I recently rewatched some of it. I love the opening theme song, and the plot about the real world and the wired world, and Lain's connection to both is interesting for those into computers and technology. It also has an unusual visual style to it that I like.
