So, a trap meant to put the entire party into the lair of a Merelith Demon was in the game I ran tonight. Basically, there was a cursed book that, if opened, would teleport anyone within 10' of the book to the Merelith's lair. The Merelith is in her lair expecting the party (and setting up traps of various types) because she just SOLD them the book for an enormous sum of gold.

Problem. The party wizard opened the book when the part was seperated (foolish thing to do; even though he was told it was safe, you STILL take precautions!) So, now, instead of a very difficult battle for the full party of 7 individuals, I'm facing a slaughter of only 3 of them in the lair. Warrior and cleric? Not there. 2 mages and a Rogue are fighting an AC 29, SR 25 demon.

So, the question is, do I let nature take it's course and have the demon eat half the party, or do I replace the demon with an Illusionist trying to scare the party? The later feels like a cop out...but the battle would be like a cat playing with a mouse. No challenge at all for the Merelith.

Anyway, anyone have any other suggestions for me? I have until Tuesday to come up with some interesting twist or another that is still a challenge, but not a cop out.

TBH, this depends on the style of play you use for your games/party.

Do characters rarely/never die in your campaign? Then use the illusionist.

You could have them fight the real demon, but the demon realises they are no match for him and just plays around with them like a cat with a mouse- maybe cages them up and waits for the rest of the group to show up? If you want to be devious, have the demon use a "lost arcane ritual" that allows it to threaten the very souls of the characters if they do not do what it wants. Then you can set up the three who were ported in as minifoils versus the rest of the party. The demon will have an incredible inside source, and you will have a new hook.

The three can't tell the rest of the party, but when they act strange (I need to step out to um, powder my nose. Hello, Ms. Boss Demon? Here's what is going on...), the rest of the party gets worried.

When the local church detects a demonic scrying link, follows it to the characters and tries to purge the evil... All kinds of devious goodness.

Of course, you would have to set a pretty tight limit on what could cause the demon to snuff them out. Their goal is to figure out how to get out from under it and extract revenge. That alone could take months or more real time to play out depending on how mean you are

Players die on occassion in this campaign, but it's not an everyday happening.

Basic story in a nutshell: The Merelith basicly runs a town on an island frequented by pirates. It's a safe port and a free city where anything and everything can be found for a price. There is one bookseller. Since most of the pirates cannot read, they tend to sell books and scrolls they capture as booty to the bookseller. This bookseller happens to be one of the Mereliths 3 "forms" (Polymorph self is so handy!)

The major temple in town is dedicated to Istishia, an evil goddess of the sea. The priests, while aware of the Merelith, are for the most part unconcerned about the fact that she, in all but name, runs the town. The Merelith has a long standing deal with the temple, and the two evil factions get along modestly well. Tention at times, but...both sides are prospering and there is little need to fight over an abundance.

Now, the only thing the Merelith is worried about are powerful priests or wizard who are not allied with her. So, she sets up an identiday as the bookseller, figuring most magic types eventually needs scrolls, spellbooks and such. Minor spellworkers, she is not concerned over, but those who have true power (such as the PCs) she uses the book to lure into a trap where she can quickly eliminate them as a threat.

Thanks for the suggestions so far! A couple ideas I can play with here.
