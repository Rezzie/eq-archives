Step 2:
Assign stats
STR* * * * 18 + 4
Strength gets the highest roll because barbarians are all about damage.
DEX* * * * 14 + 2
Dex gets roll #3 because dexterity is added to armor class, give the lightly armored barbarian better ability to shrug off blows.
CON* * * * 17 + 3
Con gets the second roll because after dealing damage, the ability to take it (ie hit points) is also very important.
INT* * * * 10 + 0
WIS* * * * 13 + 1
CHR* * * * 12 + 1

Step 3:
Choose race
Human
Racial bonuses: 1 extra feat at first level.
* * * * 4 extra skill points at first level
* * * * 1 additional skill point per level

Step 4:
Choose class
Barbarian
15 hp (1d12 + 3 from con)
Base attack + 1
Saves +2/+0/+0
skill points - 16 [(4+0) x4]
fast movement
illiteracy
rage 1/day

Step 5:
Choose feats
Level 1 feat - Bullheaded (FR pg 34) Will + 1, Intimidate + 2
This barbarian needs to be an imposing figure. High intimidation and a strong will support this idea.
Bonus feat - Thug (FR pg 3 Initiative +2, Intimidate + 2
Brabarians often hold position through power. The fast and fearsome thug is a master at this.

Ring of Bonding – These rings come in pairs or sets. (NOTE: assuming in this case the druid would have the other one)

Ring of Deflection – Adds a deflection bonus of +6 to the wearer’s armor class.
