the RT is back.

team.

repeats.

Mana will cast on raid.

Tip - don't use the above guide religiously.
It has some good information, but it is far from completely accurate.
The things you need to depend on are assists and quick heals. There will frequently be times where you'll have 6+ mobs in camp, and it can get hectic. Make sure you have as tank line set up - even if you have to throw some hybrids in there - and make sure your healers know who they're looking after. Get the debuffs in there quickly, stay away from the edges to avoid being thrown in to the lava by knockback effects, and set up a border (using corpses) as to give you enough space away from the DT drakes and backspawn, and enough space away from the ring, as to maintain some sort of control over the rate of mobs you have.
If your guild can handle 3 flurry drakes at the same time, you should be set. As mentioned previously, the hardest thing really is wave 7 and wave 15, where you'll have 2 drakes that each split in to 11 drakes, for a total of 22 mobs. Now, you have to kill those 22 mobs before the next wave (wave's usually are on a 12min or so timer). Therefore, you're going to need some fairly good DPS, around 35 - 40ppl. If not, be prepared to handle wave mergers, and that can get messy.
As mentioned in the guide above, you'll get an AE CH and mana refill after every boss, so take advantage of this. There isn't really a need to look after your mana, so let the nukers and such know this.
Apart from that, it takes a total of 3 hours, but it's a lot of fun, and Vulak'Aerr himself is painfully easy. His AE is a 55% disease based slow, cureable, but the recast is 12 seconds, so very pointless. He hits for 1k, and can be fully slowed. He's rooted in the ring, and his AC and hp are quite high, and the fight takes a while. But to be honest, he'll be no challenge for a guild that can trigger him.
G'luck. Some excellent items to be had here.
