Stratholme and Scholomance are both extremely challenging for a well-geared 5-man group of 60s; you'll want to do them in 10-person raids for loot, for a while.

Lower Blackrock Spire is a fun challenge for a 5-man group of 60s. Upper Blackrock Spire isn't possible without a full raid. I'd suggest doing Strath/Scholo/BRS raid groups for loot before trying LBRS in a 5-man.

Blackrock Depths is great for a 5-man group in that level range. Keep in mind the dungeon is huge, and split into, essentially, a lower-level, lower-loot "jail / city" section, and a higher-level, higher-loot "beyond the bar" section.

Dire Maul is split into 3 wings; in increasing order of difficulty, it's East, then North, then West. You could easily do the East wing, which is a pretty quick run and has 4 or 5 loot mobs. After going through the East wing, disband, reform with a new leader, and do it again - you'll be surprised how many good, blue-item upgrades everyone comes away with. Also, the super-rare book that begins the Pally/Warrior epic quest can potentially drop from any named mob in the instance. You could probably do the North wing as well, but the melee DPS output of some of the mobs can get nasty.
