Gots me the planescape campaign setting... 2nd edition... even tho ya can't use much of its rules for 3rd edition, the history and sociology of the place is easily adaptable...

Sigil is pretty much the meeting point of the planes... everyone and everything short of deities wanders about within its confines. Doing business with Baatezu or Celestials or even a zombie or two hundred is nothing unusual.

For the majority of trade/stuff to do within Sigil, one simply needs to look at the entry on "Union," an interplanar city much like sigil and mentioned in the Epic Characters Handbook.

Beyond the "Union" entry, its also important to know the politics of Sigil. Somewhere around 12 to 15 major factions operate in and around the Sigil area, each one focusing on different philosophies and life outlooks. Among these are the Mercykillers (hitmen/bounty hunters), the Harmonium (Respec' Mah Authoritah, Biatch!), The Sensates (ooo... the colors...), The dustmen (death death death death death death death death...), The Cyphers (Shoot first, think later), Indeps (Ain't got a job... AND LOVIN' IT!), The Godsmen (Workers of the world unite!), and so on and so forth...

But EVERYBODY pays homage to the Lady of Pain. Nobody knows much about her, apart from the facts that she can kill you just by putting you in her shadow, keeps the gods out of town, and has a particularly insidious collection of extraplanar mazes that she likes dumping troublemakers into. She does NOT like to be worshipped, and if you even joke about it, expect to be targeted.

Oh, and the town is always changing. There's a bunch of repairmen that wander around the place, performing minor fixit work 24/7 (or 10, depending on yer campaign). The city itself also exerts a limited amount of life, forming new buildings and removing old ones, and generally making it utterly impossible to keep a map for longer than a week...

Then there's that thing about the portals. They're everywhere. If there's a doorway, archway, toilet, or rathole, there's a dang good chance that something will cause that particular orifice to turn into an interplanar gate. (For example, if you grunt while taking a shite on the can, while reading a playboy mag, some poor Astral Deva's about to learn the meaning of a "Shite Eating Grin")

This can be useful, and problematic. Useful in that you can get anywhere, anytime, just by knowing where a particular portal is and what activates it; and problematic in that you might end up going to hell... for walking under a bridge while whistling dixie.

Any more questions? 'Cuz I'm Psycho Like That...
