There are about 3 stations that are listenable where I go to school. We have at least 3 rap stations, 4 country stations, 2 gospel stations 1 classic rock station, 1 "modern rock" station and one 70s &amp; 80s station. I usually flip between the last 3. The modern rock station is getting on my nerves more and more. I don't listen to the radio a lot, I think it's mostly crap. Rock has degraded to the point where it's not worth keeping up with. Yeah it all sounds the same, but that's almost always been the case. In the 80s we had hair bands and 80s rock sounded the same mostly until Nirvana came along with Smells Like Teen Spirit, and that was where (I put) the end of 80s rock. We had a decent amount of good stuff out of the 90s I think. Recently though, all rock is REALLY sounding the same. Steps to making a rock song:
1. Whiny voice, gotta have the droneing whiny voice or
1a. Screaming angry voice.

2. Power chords. Everyone has the power chords going on in the background, and I do mean background. Few and far between do you find any kind of guitar work nowadays. What do I mean? Ok, listen to the latest Nickleback song, or some Linkin Park...then listen to Message in a Bottle by the Police and you'll see what I mean. Listen to some Weezer. There is nothing going on in modern rock, it's very one-track. Does it have to be all riffs? Well heck no, but having everything be extentions of the beat is really, really irritating. Look at something inbetween, like Foo Fighters stuff. They don't have a lot going on in their guitar parts (compared with Message in a Bottle) however the seperation between drum beat and guitar is there.

3. Where's the bass? No not the thub-thub-thub, I mean the thing with 4 strings (or 5 if you're l33t). I can't hear it, do you not have a bassist? Did he quit cuz the bass parts sucked? Good bass parts I think of the Chilli Peppers and The Cure.

(Note that lyrical ability is not in there.)
Really, though. I am totally willing to admit that I have picky tastes in music, and am skeptical of anything recent coming out. I thought Queens of the Stone Age - No One Knows was decent, I liked the guitar part, I thought the style of the singing was a bit of fresh air. It just seems like the same stuff is showing up, and the only people who are actually rocking the scene have been doing so for a while now. I realize that the best stuff probably doesn't get played on the radio, but there was a time when it was! There was a time when you could be a local band and get your song on your local radio station, is that gone now too?
