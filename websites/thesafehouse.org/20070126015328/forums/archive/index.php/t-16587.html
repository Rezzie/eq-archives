Yer on yer own. No help will come to Qeynos betrayers.

I did it a few days ago at level 14 (granted, I'm a Crusader, but still...). Failed twice, then succeeded the third time. I'm not sure about how Scouts manage their skills and whatnot, but the best tips I can give are:
When you zone in, DON'T MOVE. Wait for a minute, buff up as much as you can, allow your power to regen, then attack the guards. Focus on one guard until he's dead, then work on the other one. After the two guards are dead, rebuff and rest. Once fully healed/powered, proceed up the stairs and take out the guard captain.

Super-important: Heroic Opportunities. Use them, and use them nonstop. They are the only way you'll get the edge and best the opponents. The reason I failed the previous two times was that I wasn't managing my heroic opportunities effectively to get the most available opportunities off. Make sure to use them, and you should be fine. I imagine it'll be a little harder for you than me, since my Crusader had more HP, but it should still be doable.

Also, if you have spirit shards left in the room, my suggestion is to make a kamikaze run in, and absorb all of them, aggroing whoever you have to. Just make sure you get all of them. Then, run back to the zone-in door to die (since you can't zone out). You will leave one shard there. The next time you try, as soon as you zone in, you won't have to move and aggro the guards in order to retrieve your shard and get back up to full power.

Hope some of this helps. :)
