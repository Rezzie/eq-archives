Each food/drink has a maximum amount of health or mana it will regen for you.

This is then put on a timer -- (forgive me I dont' have exact numbers for this) 98 hps in 20 seconds for example. If you remain seated while eating you will get the hps over the course of the 20 seconds (it is evenly spaced). However, you can stand up before the time period is over and only get a partial amount of the full health. Or, if you didn't need all 98 hps and you're back to top health again.

Food doesn't work the way you're thinking of. When you consume a piece of food or drink, you sit down and you get a buff. The buff is "Eating", and the effect is "Restores X [HP/Mana] per second", and it will generally last for about 20-30 seconds; the lowest-level foods that you start with are 98HP over 21 seconds, and some of the slightly-higher ones I've seen are something like 560HP over 35 seconds.

Standing or taking any other action will remove the "buff"; likewise, as it is a buff, I don't *think* that multiple foods would stack, as the second one would just overwrite the buff you have currently - but I've not tried it to check.

Likewise, there is no such thing as "full".
