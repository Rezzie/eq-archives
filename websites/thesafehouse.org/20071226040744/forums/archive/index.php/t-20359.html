Hmm, may as well start with a summary and then comments on the lastest episode.

One Piece mainly your typical Shounen anime. In terms of popularity, it has been the highest ranking show almost every week for the last five years, trumping shows like Naruto and Bleach. A large part of the reason is that One Piece is not centered around the action, like Naruto and Bleach are. It puts more emphasis on the story than the other two, and on the concept of "adventure" rather than fighting.

Due to this, it's become a hit family show that families get together to watch, rather than being a hit among younger children and Otaku in their 20's.

The basic premise of the story is about Monkey D. Luffy, (Nickname, Last name, Given name in that order) and his quest to become the Pirate King. When the first Pirate King, Gold Roger died, a era of pirates began as everyone set on a quest to find the vast treasure he left behind in the Grand Line.

Along the way, we see him pick up characters, the first two and most notable being Roronoa Zoro, a pirate hunter, and Nami, the Navigator. Along the way a few people join the crew, bringing it up to a current total of 8 as of the current fansubbed episode, 160.

One thing that is different about One Piece is the heavy emphasis on character development. I'll bring up the story arcs to explain how strong the developments are.

Captain Morgan Arc: Episodes 1-3.

Buggy Arc: Episodes 4-8

Kuro Arc: Episodes 9-17 (Ussopp Backstory)

Don Kreig Arc: Episode 18-30 (Sanji Backstory)

Arlong Arc: Episode 31-44 (Nami Backstory)

Buggy Side Story Episode 45-47 (manga filler)

Logue Town Arc: Episode 48-53

Warship Island Arc: Episode 54-60 ( anime filler)

Whiskey Peak Arc: Episode 61-67

Coby/Helmeppo Side Story: Episode 68-69 (manga filler)

Little Garden Arc: Episode 70-77

Drum Island Arc: Episode 78-91 (Chopper Backstory)

Alabasta Arc: Episode 92-130

Filler Arc: Episode 131-143

Jaya Arc: 144-152

Skypeia Arc: 153-195

As can be seen, a good amount of time is spent on character development, the only exception being Luffy and Zoro who have their story developing all throughout the series in little bits and pieces. And one current character that is being developed in the current Water Seven arc.

Starting with episode 162, we at #K-F will start using X264 to encode our episodes.

For those who don't know, X264 is a new codec which will eventually completely replace XviD. You can think of it as being higher quality for the same size file or lower file size for the same quality. There are several sites of comparison, but I won't list them here. Use Google.

X264 is harder to decode than XviD. If you cannot play the file without experiencing lag, you may need to upgrade your computer. One site recommends at least a 1GHz computer.


What do I have to do to watch X264 episodes?
First, install ffdshow.
We strongly recommend that you do NOT use the default options: Uncheck VFW interface on the first screen of options. Uncheck EVERYTHING except for the [ ] H.264, X264 choice on the second screen of options. Uncheck everything on the third screen of options. Uncheck everything on the fourth screen of options. Uncheck everything on the fifth screen of options. The rest of the installation you can decide options for yourself (it won't affect the playback).

Next, install Haali's Matroska Splitter.
This will enable you to play MP4 files in most media players (WMP, VLC, MPC have all been tested).


Why are you making me install ffdshow?
ffdshow is currently the best implementation of X264 decoding. It has several other very annoying quirks when playing other files, which is why we recommend against the default installation.


Why is there so much lag in the playback?
Chances are your computer simply cannot handle the additional processing load of decoding X264. You can probably get a better computer or upgrade pretty easily.


How do I know which version is which?
The X264 version is an MP4 file, and the XviD version is an AVI file. X264 can be placed in an AVI container, but it's easier to distinguish this way.


Do I have to install X264?
For now, we are releasing two versions of the episodes. This is only [i]temporary[\i]. In the near future we will begin releasing X264 exclusively, so you should take appropriate action before then.


Why did you decide to use X264?
This codec makes it much easier to make high quality encodes. Most people don't know this, but XviD often gives files which are too large because of the way it handles compression. I won't bore you with details, but if we set the target file sise to be 230MB, it may come out to be 240MB, which does not fit on a CD. This means we have to reencode with different compression settings. Using X264 will save us a lot of trouble.

Is X264 the same as H.264?
Not quite, but mostly. H.264 is a closed source codec produced commercially and not available to the public in general. X264 is the open source, public equivalent, a lot like XviD. ffdshow decodes both X264 and H.264. The current commercial H.264 decoder is extremely slow and would be illegal to distribute.
