One thing about APB is the surprising variety between the songs, very few sound remotely alike. Their (His? I read the biography, but I can't really tell if it's still two guys or just one) remix of Fade to Black is simply beautiful. Eclipse, spiritual reality, until the end of the world, paranoia (admittedly I prefer a remix to the original), and the rest for the most part is energizing, uplifting music. There's a few that are slow and depressing like Burning Heretics and another that I can't recall offhand, with my music collection unavailable to me :/.

VNV nation is definitely a heavier, darker tone. I don't know how I didn't list Serial Killer, probably the most dramatic song I've heard by them. Strangely, one of their older ones too. I won't claim to have heard all of either band's songs, though I think I've heard more VNV nation (through futureperfect, the newer stuff I havn't gotten) than APB. There's sort of limboy area in the chronological middle of their music, with stuff like Voice, and Cold, and a couple of other that sound nothing like the earlier serial killer or the later futureperfect and the songs you and i listed in this thread. The music instills sorrow, or anger, or loss, or remembrance of scenarios similar to the lyrics. Things that you still can't let yourself forget, even if you want to. I definitely can associate VNV nation with a time in my life, which I can't really do with APB. Overplaying music really angers me, it'd be nice if our brains forgave us and we could get that 'fresh feel' back again.

Perhaps it's the difference between loving life and praising the fallen. You need both. Likeminded emotions probably draw on oneanother, when you're happy, you don't want to be sad, and when you're sad, you want remembrance that you're not alone.
