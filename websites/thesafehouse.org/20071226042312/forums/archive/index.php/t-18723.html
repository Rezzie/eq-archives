Ah! My Goddess: Watched up to episode 17. It continues to follow the manga very closely. The two other sisters have showed up now, Skuld and Urd. It is very different from the OVA's and Movie, which did not follow the chronology of the manga. It is a slow-paced romance anime, and very sweet, not comedic like Love Hina or Mahoraba.


Akane Mainax Gaiden: This title is something of a guilty pleasure. Standalone, it is more horrible than... horrible. But I love this series. It is a Media Factory title so it is not availble on legit fansub sites (not licensed, long story). It is centered mostly around some random joe blow, but the "focus character" is Akane from Kimi ga Nozomu Eien. It is a slight parody on it, episode 2 had me convulsing in laughter. Basilisk ~Kouga Ninpo Chou~: Watched up to episode 2. It has a Ninja Scroll feel to it. But, it looks like it might have a strong story later on, so I will continue to watch in hopes of such.


Bleach: Watched up to episode 31. It continues to be a wonderful Shounen title in the same vein of Naruto, and One Piece. Even though I'm far ahead in the manga, watching the fights animated are very enjoyable, the lastest episode was especially good.

Futakoi Alternative: Watched up to episode 4. The anime is wonderful, stylish, and funny as hell. The original Futakoi was cute, cliche, and generally not worth watching. There was no fanservice, no "sexy" or anything "adult-related". Futakoi ALternative, is about as alternative as it gets. All the characters that we watched being so sweet and innocent, are suddenly turned into gunslinging hardcore girls, tons of fanservice, comedy, and sexy as hell. It is hard to go throught he first series, but I feel it is worth it just to appreciate Alternative just that much more. But you can watch the first one without watching the second, they have nothing to do with each other AT ALL except the characters.


Gakuen Alice: Watched up to episode 7. I can't really tell why I enjoy this anime. It's nothing particularly impressive, but it has the potentional to become an extremely serious and heartwrenching show. It's about a young girl who had an Alice, which could be called a superpower, and went to a school for people with Alices. Something like the X-Men school, I guess. But you cannot leave this school. None of the students are allowed to contact their family. At first it was very cute, and danced around the harsh reality very well, but the lastest episode was very heartwrenching when she became homesick. I can't spoil more, but the story seems to be turning very dark.


Gallery Fake: Watched up to episode 4. Want style? Here's a whole lot of style on a platter. It's about paintings, and a genius art salesman and his exploits. He is no hero, that's for certain. Very highly recommended.


Girls Bravo S2: Episode 7. Well, it's a far cry from being anything good. It's stupid, worthless, and a waste of brain cells. But, it's one of my few fanservice shows, so it's nice to take a break from focusing and just lay back, get a boner, and enjoy using my little brain cells.

Hachimatsu to Clover: Episode 2. It's based in college, and apparently an josei (young women's 18-25) anime. It has the most interesting male characters I've seen in a long time. I wanted to bump this thread up to mention a series I recently got into.

I was watching Major, a anime about baseball. At first, I wasn't really into it, but I started to enjoy it by episode 3. I was completely hooked on episode 6.

Anyway I hope you guys watch this with a open mind. The sports anime crowd will like the action, the drama crowd would love the drama, and it's IMO one of the best mixes of the two. It's not as exciting as Hajime no Ippo, but it can't be helped, it's Baseball, after all.

Shounen means anime aimed at young boys. I don't remember the exact ages, but it's like something that's aimed at our version of 3rd to 8th graders. Typical Shounen series would be, Dragon Ball, Naruto, One Piece, Shaman King, etc.

Shoujo means anime aimed at young girls. Girls the same age as it's shounen counterpart. It's difficult to name some Shoujo anime. While Shoujo manga is popular, there are few anime made out of it. Some Shoujo would be Peach Girl, probably Maria-sama ga Miteru. My allergies have me in a mental block right now though.

Mahou Shoujo would mean, Magical Girl. About the same age as Shoujo, but also for very young to infants. Typical Mahou Shoujo would be, Sailor Moon, Futari wa Pretty Cure, Pretear, etc.

Fanservice would mean the act of giving service to the audience. Typical fanservice is panty shots, swimsuit episodes, erotic positioning, the "KYAA!!!" moments you often see girls do in anime.

When you said you're looking for story, do you mean story within a hentai? If so I can't really help you there. I don't watch much hentai. But if it's anime... I'll also need to know if you are able to obtain fansubs (www.animesuki.com). Or if you are limited to US releases.

It's not bad to love FLCL. It was a very popular title in it's time. I own FLCL myself. It was a very unique show. The person who made FLCL, Hideaki Anno, also made the very famous title, Neon Genesis Evangelion.

If you're not a fan of Mecha, then it makes things easier for me. I'll name a few titles to get you started. It's easy to list many titles to watch, but if you're just getting into anime, it's a good idea to watch a variety of shows so you can get a good perceptive of what sort of anime you enjoy.

For action oriented anime, you would want to try Naruto and One Piece out. Both are massively popular, with One Piece currently (and for the last 4ish years) reigning supreme. But USA fans seem to enjoy Naruto more for some reason. Mostly because of the art style, and that One Piece has too much story and development.

For romance anime, there's shows like Kimi Ga Nozomu Eien and To Heart.

Harem anime (loser guy many chicks) would be stuff like Love Hina, Tenchi Muyo. But many people dislike this genre because of the loser main character they can't relate to.

Sports anime (a underappreciated genre in USA) would definitely be Hajime no Ippo, without a doubt. It's a boxing anime. It has a massive following.

Samurai anime would be stuff like Rurouni Kenshin (and the Samurai X OVA's, which I preferred far more than the series itself).

Crazy fun like FLCL would be Excel Saga, but while it's funny, you have to be very knowledgable about anime to get a full appreciation of the show, unlike FLCL.

Most of the above are just good starter anime. Everyone has their own preferences to what's the best of the genre. From there, you have classic stuff like the Leiji Matsumoto universe, World Masterpiece Theatre anime, etc. There are many anime considered classics in the USA, but usually for different reasons. For example, Neon Genesis Evangelion is a classic for being a great anime in the US, but in Japan, it's not considered a classic for being great as much as it is for defining to society that anime is not just for children to watch.

Hope I helped somewhat. It's a little hard for me to do this without feeling like I'm overwhelming you.
