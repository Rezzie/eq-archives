More tidbits from Brad: (http://www.vanguardsoh.com/forums/showpost.php?postid=76587&postcount=74)

Once you say something like the Unreal Engine, all sorts of images (good and bad) pop into your mind about the great FPS games that have been built on this technology. Then there are thoughts on how Unreal games are more often first person than third, and whether Vanguard will be too...

Speculation is fun, and by no means do I want to discourage it

But, please do keep in mind that we are using all of this tech to get us going... as crucial stepping stones to get us making the game, not the technology, tools, or engine. This is important... key, in fact: we are at the same time heavily modifying the code base to turn everything MMOG specific (and, more so, Vanguard specific).

Just for example, we added per pixel lighting, bump and specular, seamless world technology, completely re-did terrain (including building special in-house terrain creation tools), re-worked (and continue to do so) the networking layers, and more (Ryan Elam could go on and on).

I'd also point out, just for example, that Lineage 2 uses the Unreal engine as a basis, and I know they too have heavily modified it. It's a seamless or almost seamless world, and it's third person with 'Asian' style point and click movement.

Anyway, just some food for thought.
