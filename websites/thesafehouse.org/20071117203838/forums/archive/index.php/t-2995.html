Before you all gasp, let's just say that I've been around D&amp;D games and other pen and paper rpgs but I was never invited to "join those reindeer games".

So when I was invited to the Parkinsons while Shadowcross was still in the States, I gladly jumped at the chance to play with Glip, Shadowcross, Zato, Ester (formerly the Tester), Zach Parkinson, and Wotan (SH poster aka Findahl).

All I can say is: NOW I get it, hehe. I kept thinking, "ah this is where EQ got this from" etc. The atmosphere of the game was fun too. Great minatures, a large grid map for play and plotting. And, while the Parkinson's had just recently acquired the house and thus there wasn't any original D&amp;D artwork up, I did spot an original oil later on in the family room. What really made the game of course was Keith's great imagination. During our game (2nd edition) we had shadowy thieves, orcs, drums of doom, giant spiders, water pits, and of course.... the giant slugs. Unfortunately we didn't get to finish the game because Shadowcross needed to get to the airport.

It was still a blast though and I look forward to my next game. Maybe I won't be a pre-made 8' 6" half ogre female in it either

Anyway, the old Victorian was indeed once a home and was rumored to have tunnels built beneath it than ran to down close to the lake, apparently used as a hideout for escaped slaves seeking refuge before/during the Civil war. It was also reportedly a whorehouse at one time owned by gangstas, er, gangsters.

There was apparently a couple of student suicides in the 60's as a result of one of these sleepovers...

Anyway, it was quite a perfect place for D&amp;D.
