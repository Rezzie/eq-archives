Being the mother-of-all dps class is NOT what being a Rogue should be about imo. EQ1 turned the Rogue into an Assassin which while nice, was not everyones cup of tea. After that many games just followed suit.

EQ2 the realm of "Rogues" went to the Swashbuckler and Brigand who both had hybrid utility value...this list could go on and on.

Burglars do a good amount of DPS in LOTRO, nothing compared to a Hunter ofc, but as someone has already pointed out Hunters are plain sick for DPS.

We are the class who can sniff out a hidden cache of loot (Track Treasure), can study our opponents from the shadows spotting a flaw in their defense (Reveal Weakness), can confound an enemy with riddles (aka Mez) aswell as debuffs of differing flavours. With the added use of Caltrops (AoE DoT) Stun Dust and Bags of Marbles (knockdown) the class is really flavoursome for my liking.

I played a Rogue EQ1 for about 5 years, Ragebringer was the worst thing that ever happened as it turned us into a 1 trick pony, DPS. Nerf bats were continually being swung by all and sundry about Rogues and the backstab multiplier, excluding us from certain weapons because the formula couldnt handle it.
