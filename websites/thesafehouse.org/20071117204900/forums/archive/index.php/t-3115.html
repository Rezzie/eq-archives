2e babble here..

Greyhawk, Darksun, Forgotten Realms, Birthright, all of them, are total and seperate realities.

Each campaign represent the Prime Material Plane on the diagram of the universe. And each campaign setting exists as the prime material in it's own reality. It has(if you want) it's own Elysium, Abyss, Ethereal, Astral. Ravenloft is the exception. Ravenloft is actually it's own seperate plane of existance, the demiplane of dread. It mentions in the Ravenloft books that travel to other campaign worlds IS possible from ravenloft, because Ravenloft can bend reality to grab anyone from any campaign universe, conversely, it can dump them in any universe.

So you essentially have 2 options. Each universe exists in it's own seperate reality, OR, only the prime material plane exists in seperate realities, all the other planes exist simultaenously throughout reality, going to Elysium from Dragonlance and going to Elysium from Dark Sun will land you in the same place at the same time.

Now, as a GM, you can give players the power to travel through reality. A carefully worded wish spell might allow it. But there is no spell or ability in 2e that specifically lets you travel in such a way. There may be planes of existance that can be accessed by planewalk or what have you, but those planes of existance still exist within a singular reality, of which there are many.

As far as the original topic, I read it as trees centric to the FR setting. I don't dig on FR, so I kind of assumed they had their own special kinds of trees. Maybe you are just asking for fantasy type trees and wood from all sorts of stuff? Like Heartwood, and the Lorien trees from Lord of the Rings?
