Okay, We've talked time and time again about Fansubbed and unlisenced anime, not it's time to review what's come over onto the shelves here in the US. I'm slightly tipsy (you can guess why (http://www.thesafehouse.org/viewtopic.php?t=20018)) so forgive any spelling mistakes and such, ya?


Last Exile: Me like. Great CG balanced with animation typically reserved for movies or OVA's. Great soundtracks as well, Dolce Triade does some great work. Story never quite tells you everything that's ever happened, and you're often left to accept things at face value with no history given, but that should only cause a problem for people with no ability to accept the suspension of disbelief.

Elfen Lied: Still watching. Me liking. Your standard Chobits case of 'cute girl that can only say one word mysteriously shows up one day' meets Kill Bill esque massive amount of bloodletting due to her other personality happens to be a raging psychopath with spooky mind powers. Pretty dark series, some of the trauma incurred by various people makes Evangelion look downright normal. (Mommy, I don't like Daddy... WHACK) :blink
So, in summary: Chobits + Evangellion + Kill Bill = Elfen Lied.

FLCL: Yeah, it's been out for a long time, but it's still one of the most bizarrely kick-ass anime there is, period.

Read Or Die TV: Me like. Three sisters that can 'use' paper by forming it into whatever shape they want just by thinking about it go on guard duty for a Japanese author. What initally is an action series quickly becomes more as plots and characters from the OVA come into play, leading to some very well-scripted drama intermixed with some imaginitive action scenes using simple paper.

Gunslinger Girl: One of the most beautifully tragic anime, ever. A must-see.

Maburaho: Me NOT like. It's hard to think that you could actually screw up a magic shool story, but what starts out promising just doesn't go as far as it could have. If you could only use magic 8 times in your life; and you'd die upon using #8, you'd prolly not use it to, say, magically restore someone's lunch that they dropped. I mean WTF. Granted, he's also the typical 'guy that's beset upon by every woman in the school cause his child will be a super-powerful mage due to the bloodline he carries', but for some odd reason he never even tries to get busy with anyone, even his childhood friend that claims to be his wife. Can we have a character that acts like a guy at some point, and starts his own harem, please???

Haibane Renmei: Oh so wonderful story. Putting into words without spoiling anything is hard (I watched it the first time with no prior knowledge), but let me say that if you like a touching, well-told story involving people with angellic charcoal-grey wings in a warm small town setting that feels like it's out of a Miyazaki film, this is a definate must see.


Me tired. Add some more, folks.

Let's dump my Netflix queue out here and see what we've got...

Azumanga Daioh - A set of vignettes around a group of high school girls. Cute and just slightly twisted. A great low-energy comedy that produces some really memorable characters and visuals. One of the best series I've seen in a while.

Excel Saga - If you're reading this thread, chances are you don't need me to tell you about Excel Saga. Worth watching, it's got its funny moments ands Menchi is doubleplus awesome, but it also just tries too hard in a lot of places and falls flat. I think a more coherent storyline could have really pulled it all together.

Last Exile - I haven't really gotten into Last Exile like a lot of people. It's very stylish, but the story just isn't doing it for me.

His and Her Circumstances - The famous Kare Kano, Gainax does highschool romantic comedy. I've only seen the first disc so far and its hovering at the edge of a thumbs up, could go either way.

Haibane-Renmei - /second Lenilya. Beautiful and touching and just remarkable. It's better if you go in blind.

Ai Yori Aoshi - Netflix kicked this one out at me as a Recommended, but I'm not sure why. Pretty standard harem comedy so far, decent but not special.

Texhnolyze - Wow, freaky. Your dystopian future setting, dark, brooding and bloody. I found the first disc pretty blah but the action started to pick up at the end of the second. Number three is sitting on my table right now.

To me, Fan Service is when a situation happens for no other reason than to produce a sexual result as a 'service' to the 'fans' (thus, fan service. clever, huh?)

Fanservice to me at least doesn't matter, as long as it's not forced. Here's an example of the situations that would make me think wether or not something was really fanservice-based:

In Love Hina, you have a dorm at a hot spring. of COURSE there's going to be girls in towels. And, amazingly, not a panty shot in sight- even super-hyper Kaolla in a short skirt never once flashes the camera. Nothing is really 'forced' at all, execpt for the potato-stew event, and the dress Mutsumi is wearing. So, as opposed to the rest of the world, I didn't really register Love Hina as fanservice-y.

Now, in Love Hina Again, suddenly we have half naked characters wandering around for no reason, and a bevvy of panty shots where suddenly there were none. Fanservice, ahoy. That is an example of my definition. Course, It's just my opinion, so take it as you will.
