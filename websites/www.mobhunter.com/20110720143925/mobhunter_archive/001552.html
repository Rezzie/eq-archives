The game designers are obviously aware of AA's when they design the content. 1 zone level 45+
3 zones level 50+
10 instances level 50+ (with 30+ AAs)
6 zones level 65+ (with 300 AAs and gear with 100/100+ hp/mana min.)

Just lets have them be honest and let us know what the real assumptions are before we go off and give them another buck.

Comment Posted by: Redhenna on October 11, 2007 05:00 AM

Hopefully this is in time...

Quick preface: I played EQ for 6 years, and played hardcore. My account expired in April, and I parted ways with EQ, but it was an amicable parting of the ways. I am not one of those bitter ex-players, explaining at length why EQ sucks. I don't think it does suck, and if it may have moved in a direction I did not like, I can still understand some of that reason. I think the devs do increbile things, considering how difficult some of those things probably are in a game as aged as EQ. While I still believe EQ is the best MMO out there(just not the one I want to play currently...maybe 6 years of any game is just too much), I also believe there are real issues, real problems with the game. As my questions are about those problems, they kinda seem negatively slanted. This is not because I think negatively of EQ, but because I am asking about areas of EQ where I do see problems.

1: For me, and a fair number of people I knew in EQ, the number 1 problem got to be the real, hard limits in progression, especially outside of raiding. I raided hardcore(though not endgame, DP level when I retired), but that alone was not enough to keep me interested. When you cannot get gear upgrades outside raids(which is understandable, having raid gear), cannot progress your character any more through experience(expendable AA's do not progress a character), and have seen the zones, done the quests...well, it makes it hard to log on. While this is not a problem for the majority of EQ, it is a problem, and a growing one. What, if anything, are you doing to solve this problem? Especially with the longer expansion cycle, it seems likely that this problem is only going to get worse, how do you plan to counteract this?

2: With the longer expansion cycle, and with the speed that some players consume content, do you have any plans to add new content between expansions, ala EQ2 and older EQ1, where new zones where added between expansions?

3: There are basically 2 primary ways to progress a character, through experience(levels and AA's), and items(3 ways if you count tradeskills). The last 2 expansions, itemization has been, to say the least, abysmal. For every good thing(and there have been a few), there has been bad, really bad. While part of the problem may be, down the road, aleviated by the longer expansion cycle, the lack of knowledge about what players are after in items shown these last two expansions suggests there will be some real stinkers for items coming. Further, next expansion is not on the one year cycle, so Merloc will not have that much longer to work on items for SoF. What is being done to ensure that this time, itemization is at least semidone for SoF, and to ensure that those items that are not done right at launch will get fixed in a timely manner? This is quite possibly the number one issue going on the last close to a year, with players at all levels of play being usatisfied with at least some aspects of itemzation, weaponry especially. With the shear volume of issues here, something really does have to be done to adress the way itemization is done, and how itemization is fixed when inevitable mistakes do happen. How do you(assuming you do) plan to adress this?

4: EQ is plagued with stability and gamebreaking issues. Sever crashes, zone crashes, west bug, and so on. Some of these problems have been around for years, and are only now being seriously looked at(west bug). With a program as old, and probably bloated as EQ is, problems of this type are probably inevitable to some extent. What is being done to attempt to improve EQs stability, and to ensure that gamebreaking issues(any crash bug, anything that requires you to relog to fix, or renders certain aspects of play impossible(eg west bug) are fixed promptly?

Sorry this is so long, I can get overly wordy some nights, when it is late and I should be asleep.
