After Omens

by Loral on September 28, 2004

Every new expansion changes the face of Everquest. Omens is no different. I had a conversation with a guild leader today who said he had great trouble bringing his guild together to defeat raid targets in Planes of Power. Their guild focuses on reaching the Plane of Time but each day he finds it more and more difficult to gather his forces. When I asked what they were doing instead, he said they hunted in Omens. I see little surprise there.

There seem to be two attitudes towards Omens. One all but ignores it and continues progression down the same path they followed two years ago. People seek the same flags, people hunt in the same experience zones, people all but completely avoid the new worlds.

The other takes the opposite approach. They seek to tear through all of the content in Omens as fast as possible. They hunt in the most lucrative experience zones in the game until they reach level 70 twenty hours later. They earn AAs and buy the best abilities as soon as they can. The random drops of spells and equipment hold them back, so instead they switch to Epic 1.5s. I remember being asked to help with all new epic raids within three days of entering the lands of Kuua.

A healthy balance exists between these two extremes. The world of Norrath changed with the opening of the portals of Discord. Powerful equipment becomes easier to acquire. We find new levels, spells, AAs, quests, and zones. We should seek out and learn more of these new paths. We should enjoy our journeys and adventures without feeling like someone pokes a spear in our back. The journey is more rewarding than the goal.

Most of the epic 1.5 quests have already been completed. The puzzle solvers worked day and night to solve the quests and epic 1.5 spoilers now sit on almost every class forum. I didn't receive my happy clicky stick until almost a year and a half after Kunark came out. I am in no hurry now. Instead, I'll pick short term goals and begin working on my quest when my own power and the power of my friends grows.

Spells in Omens so far prove difficult to obtain. All spells except teleports come from dropped runes. While the runes ensure that no class gets duplicate spells and all spell casting classes can use these runes, they drop perhaps half the time on rare mobs.

There are two things I would have liked to have seen with spells in Omens. I would like to see one spell given out at each level off of a vendor or a short quest. A single spell at each level gives players a bit more motivation to level than just new aas. I would also like to see a more progressive, less random, quest to receive new spells.

Again, I fall back to the point system in LDON. It took 11 adventures to receive a new spell but I knew I made progress with each successful adventure. I did not compete with my group to receive it; we all earned equal rewards for our adventures. Instead of only dropping muramite runes off of random rare mobs, why not drop pieces of runes randomly off of every mob. Collect 10 pieces, build yourself a rune.

Speaking of LDON, prepare for a flip flop. Four months ago I asked SOE to add new LDON equipment to the LDON vendors. At the time it would help non-raiders access more Gates content and give high-level LDON adventurers a few more trinkets to seek. I was told at the Summit that these items would be added. Later I was told they would be added, but not until after Omens.

At this point in the game, most of the "after Omens" fixes are obsolete. Like instancing the Plane of Time, new content overshadows the desire for such a large fix. If we want higher end items than the LDON vendors, we can go to one of three instanced expeditions and earn better loot. We can hunt named in the Wall of Slaughter and Ruins of Dranik. Like the gear in Kunark, Velious, and Luclin, LDON gear becomes obsolete with age. LDON equipment works well up to 65 but when one outgrows it, one should head head to Omens.

I could be a pain in the arse and demand that SOE follow through with new LDON loot. I know many high-end raiders will still demand a Time instance even though instanced zones in Gates and Omens offer better loot without any guild-based competition. Instead, SOE should focus their resources on the problems that matter most and the features that help the game grow further.

I'd rather see them work on new LDON-style adventures with new themes, new adventure types, new beasts, and new loot than rehash the loot on the adventures in which many people have already fought. Even if they did put focus 5 items on LDON vendors, I'd probably stay over in Omens for awhile.

I would prefer SOE look at the expeditions in Omens and add more LDON-like features to them than rebuild LDON. I would like to see an incremental quest for spells in addition to random drops. I would like to see a in-game goal and a two-hour duration for each expedition. I would like to see the removal of spawns so groups crawl from the beginning to the end. I would like to see boss-mobs spawn after the party kills a number of normal beasts.

Expeditions have a lot more potential than instanced group hunting content. Expeditions are controlled environments for the developers to build fun and interesting scripts. They use some of these scripts for the Muramite Proving Grounds trials, but few will see those powerful events and far fewer will survive them.

I asked for new LDON items and better LDON adventures, but now I would rather focus on the future. I would prefer to see Omens filling in the gaps left open with LDON. Omens includes enough content to keep almost any player busy for a long time regardless of the amount of time they play or the style of play they enjoy. A few changes to the expeditions would help bring the best parts of Lost Dungeons to new and more flexible expeditions. After that, all we must fear are the sharp teeth of the Dragorns and the green gasses of the umbrella people.

Comment Posted by: Talaen on September 29, 2004 03:18 PM

Here is my take on Omens of War:

From a graphics and sound perspective, I believe the expansion was very well done. I am very happy with the look, feel, and overall atmosphere of the expansion. That said, I have some guildmates who are having serious video problems since Omens went live - to the point where they can't play. Obviously this is very frustrating for them and I hope that SOEs tech support team can find and fix the issues quickly.

On content: Most of the zones are set up very well for what would seem to be their intended level ranges. I especially like how some zones, like the causeway, get tougher the further into them you go. This allows folks to move deeper in for more challenge and more rewards.

The new epic quests which have been added are, at least from what I have seen and read, very epic. They bring back the feel of the original in that this is something that transcends the simple act of accumulating gear and experience.

However, I have some problems with the content in Omens. First, the instanced expeditions are too thin. Most players are using them simply as a way to farm experience and a few loot drops. There needs to be more to them - either some purpose to you being in that area, or more named mobs and loot. Something to keep folks from clearing them up to one particular room and then sitting there working on spell runes or whatever particular piece of gear they were after. I don't necessarily think a timer is a good idea - these zones are huge and a timer would unfairly limit less powerful groups. However, I do think that there should be much more scripting involved.

Second, it is extremely difficult for players to get involved in the storyline of Omens. The factions only halfway make sense. I think that people -should- have to pay attention to factions, but they should also be able to actually get involved in the storyline from day 1, rather than having to kill tons of things to raise a faction that may or may not make a difference in the end. As most players simply ignore faction until someone has posted a powerful quest item on the web, this really only penalizes people who want to explore for the sake of learning.

Finally, while i do very much like the new epic quests, their release has reminded me of what I hate most about EQ - and that is the tendency of most players to focus solely on the levels and the loot and not on the story or the world itself. People don't go to zones to see somewhere new, they go because it's "better experience" or has a piece of loot they want. They will happily destroy their faction in their hometown to get an item with better stats from an opposing faction's quest. To give you an example, the other day some friends and I were in Kaesora getting Xalgoz fangs for the Charasis Key quest. A group of 66+ players zoned in, and quite politely asked us which epic we were working on that we needed to get into Charasis. When we told them we just wanted to go there to see the place and find its secrets, they tried (again very politely) to tell us that we were wasting our time. This to me exemplifies what is very wrong with EQ - the loot mentality that it instills in its players is the same one that eventually burns most players out on the game.

On itemization - This could have been done better. There are some nice upgrades in Omens, to be sure. However, there seem to be two classes of gear there - either extremely nice to the point that people fight over it, or okay but not really much better than what people already had. This lack of a middle ground really hurts - it means that certain mobs and zones get camped to death while others go untouched.

Likewise, the spell runes are better than GoD, but still frustrating to many people. Along with many of the nice-but-not-awesome items, I think these should probably be changed to rare drops from any mob in their zones, rather than rare drops from named mobs only.

The biggest problem I see with itemization is that the class requirements on many items don't seem to make sense. I've been seeing a lot of gear that's listed for all hybrid classes, for example. Since I'm a hybrid, that's great, but if I as a paladin can wear a piece of armor, shouldn't a warrior be able to wear it? Or a cleric? Sometimes I really wonder if the people who do itemization for new zones and expansions at SOE really take into account anything that's gone before them in the game.

On difficulty: I have mixed feelings on this. I am happy with the challenge level of many encounters - especially some of the encounters I have had deeper in the Omens zones where you really had to pay attention to how the mobs were fighting and come up with a good set of tactics to defeat it. I also like how you have to pay attention to the environment in some places. Ideally, I think the challenge of any area should be at least partially (30%) determined by the environment - narrow walkways, water, etc.

However, you could cut the hp of most Omens mobs by 1/3 or even 1/2 and the fights would still be difficult. As it stands, most encounters basically degenerate into hitting auto-attack and waiting 5 minutes. While I understand the need for making the mobs tough enough to keep players from clearing out the zone, this is a little extreme. It's especially glaring when you have a minimal group or one that's low on damage-dealing classes, where individual fights can take nearly ten minutes to complete. This ultimately just becomes boring. Longer fights do not generally make for harder fights.

Getting to places isn't very hard once you learn the way there, but it's dangerous enough to keep you on your toes. I do think that every expansion since at least Legacy of Ykesha has been forcing players to become more and more dependant on invisibility potions, and that's a bad thing.

Overall I think Omens is a good expansion, worth the money, and is much more solid and available than its predecessor, but I also think that it could use a good bit of tweaking and rebalancing. Then again, the whole game could use that in my view. There have been so many mistakes made, and so many bandaids applied over the past few years, that it seems impossible that one expansion cold really fix much of anything.
