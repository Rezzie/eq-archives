Skill List

This list is what is available to a first level Human Paladin starting in Freeport as of May 16, 1999.

1 Handed Slash - One handed Slashing is one of the most important skills Paladins can aquire. Enabling us to wield 1 handed, bladed weapons in combat. Any number of Foes fall about Our feet when faced with an army of Sword Wielding Paladins. This skill also enables us to wield a few types of Our Holy Swords effectively.
2 Handed Slash - Two Handed Slashing is, to some Paladins, the skill that grants them the greatest power to defeat Evil in Norrath. Enabling Us to fight with Great Swords and Halberds, when used in conjunction with Double Attack, Paladins can do in excess of 60 points of damage in one round.
1 Handed Blunt - This grants Paladins the ability to fight with the same weapons our Holy Brother and Sisters, the Clerics, use. Although not a mainstay of Paladins, the Hammer can be summoned from the Gods to slay enemies when faced in a desperate situation. One of the four combat skills that should be as high as possible at all times.
2 Handed Blunt - Another combat skill utilized by Clerics, Paladins should be a Master of all weapon styles. This skill enables Paladins to use powerful, damaging weapons such as the Ogre War Maul.
Bash - Bash is a very useful combat skill that should become an integral part of a Paladin's Combat Readiness. Because we lack the ability to Dual Wield, we can take the defensive and offensive bonuses of a Shield and make them a strength. Aside from doing 6-12 damage to an opponent, a Bash can disrupt spells and stun opponents.
Taunt - Taunt is a vital skill for all of the Warrior Classes in EverQuest. The best way to keep a creature off of your spellcasters is to enrage them with your Taunting. A spellcaster that is not being hit can cast spells with interference, ending battles safer and faster. A Paladin certainly has the capability of withstanding large amounts of damage, while our frailer counterparts need the protection of Us in order to rain their Fire from the Heavens on our Foes.
Dodge - Dodge is the first skill Paladins gain as a defensive bonus. Be sure to drop one or two points into dodge at level 10 and before you know it, it will be maxed. In conjunction with all of defensive skills, a Paladin who is hit less, can fight more effectively.
Parry - A welcome bonus to your defensive skills. Parry enables you to turn the attack of a foe with your weapon. As dodge, drop a couple points into it and watch it go up. In combat, dodging and parrying begin to make the difference to a Paladins survivability.
Double Attack - Double Attack comes to Paladins at later levels but is well worth the wait. Double Attack allows Paladins to swing and hit TWICE in one round. Coupled with a Holy Sword or large 2 Handed weapon, the damage We deliver can be devastating. Coupled with a One Handed Sword, casters can be consistently interrupted as Our Blades rain down upon them.
Riposte - The second last defensive bonus Paladins earn. Riposte is another defensive tactic that keeps a Paladin fighting longer.  It is bascially, a block and counterthrust technique where you cause your target damage and block their attack.
Disarm - The last and greatest defensive bonus Paladins gain. The most effective way of disabling an opponent is to disarm him/her. When faced with a well armed foe, some Paladins have been able to disarm their opponent and retrieve the superior weapon, turning the tide of combat.
Bind Wound - Bind Wound can mean the difference between life and death for your companions. Although bandages are very expensive, a Magician can summon them, making the skill a lot more affordable. Paladins are encouraged to practice this skill and apply it as We all have a Duty to protect Our Companions.
Hand to Hand - The basic combat skill all members of the Warrior family receive. As young soldiers, We all learn the art of wrestling and punch. Although not a Paladins style, Hand to Hand can assist you if you find yourself disarmed by a superior foe.
Meditate -Meditate is a welcome addition to a Paladins Casting ability. After Healing in combat, meditating will quickly return your mana to you, enabling you to heal and fight effectively. Less downtime allows you to fight more often, levellng you faster!
Sense Heading - Sense Heading is a useful skill. When given directions or leaving a city into strange territory, it is helpful to get your bearings. Knowing where North is can help you avoid a dangerous camp or avoid ceaseless wandering.
Swimming - Swimming is a useful skill that all Paladins can use. Enabling a Paladin to swim effectively, creatures of the deep can be brought to the surface to be slain by your Companions. Swimming is also a valuable tool in skirting the shoreline of hostile territory.
