Armor of Ro:

 

4.6.1 Q – Where does the Armor of Ro quest begin?

A. – Head to Rathe Mountains on the land of Antonica.  Find the Paladin camp (loc 3000, -1000) there, and hail each of the Paladins.  Armor of Ro is the Paladin-only quested armor.

 

4.6.2 Q – How do I acquire the Breastplate of Ro?  Stats?

A. – Talk to Abigail in the Paladin camp in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Clerics of Tunare, Felwithe, is needed.  Abigail requires 2 Dark Cauldrons that come off Ancille Cooks in Mistmoore Castle to receive the Breastplate mold.  This kitchen is located near the Maid and Butler rooms.  The Dark Cauldrons are No Drop.

 

Breastplate stats are: AC 20 STR 4 SV FIRE 10 WT 5, light source, steel plate look.

 

4.6.3 Q – How do I acquire the Greaves of Ro?  Stats?

A. – Talk to Elisabeth in the Paladin camp in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Temple of Marr, Freeport, is needed.  Elisabeth requires a single head off a Nightfall Giant to receive the Greaves mold.  Nightfall Giants look like normal Hill Giants and con about the same, but only spawn randomly at night in West Commons and Southern Ro.  The head weights 20 pounds, does not fit in any containers, and is droppable.

 

Greaves stats are: AC 13 STA 6 SV COLD 10 WT 3.7, steel plate look.

 

4.6.4 Q – How do I acquire the Boots of Ro?  Stats?

A. – Talk to Nicholas in the Paladin camp in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Clerics of Underfoot, Kaladim, is needed.  Nicholas requires you to recover the plans for the Brazen Brass Kijil off a weak sand giant in South Ro in order to receive the Boots mold.  Besides the giant being very weak (around Level 26), he will say something about ‘Husam’ to you upon being hit.  The plans are droppable. 

 

Boots stats are: AC 11 HP 35 WT 1.5, gold look.

 

4.6.5 Q – How do I acquire the Bracers of Ro?  Stats?

A. – Talk to David in the Paladin in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Deepwater Knights, Erudin, is needed.  David requires you to bring back two Goblin Nets from goblin net masters in Lake Rathe to receive the Bracer mold.  They are random spawns, around level 30, have the ability to Harm Touch, have no known placeholders, and are weaker than Deep Water Aqua Goblins.  Placeholders are believed to be Aqua Goblins, Aqua Goblin Shamans, and Webclaw Murkwave.  The nets are No Drop.

 

Bracer stats are: AC 9 SV MAGIC 5 WT 2.0, gold look.

 

4.6.6 Q – How do I acquire the Vambrace of Ro?  Stats? 

A. – Talk to Marianna in the Paladin camp in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Temple of Life, Qeynos, is needed.  Marianna requires you to bring back two rotten shark portions from Plague sharks in order to receive the Vambrace mold.  Plague sharks are rare spawns in Erud’s Crossing that pass near the wrecked ship on Kerra Island.  Killer sharks will kill each other, regular sharks, and Plague sharks.  Killers sharks are about level 40, while the Plague sharks are about level 30.  You do not have to kill Killer Sharks in order for Plague Sharks, you will see or track them sooner or later.

 

Vambrace stats are:  AC 11 SV DISEASE 10 WIS 6 WT 3.2, steel plate look.

 

4.6.7 Q – How do I acquire the Helm of Ro?  Stats?

A. – Talk to Thomas in the Paladin camp in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Temple of Thunder, Qeynos, is needed.  Thomas requires 2 skulls from undead Cyclops found in the plains of Karana in order to receive the Helm mold.  Undead Cyclops have only been found in South Karanas and are extremely rare.  Undead Cyclops are about the same level as their Cyclops brethren.  There are no known placeholders.  The Cyclops skulls do not fit in containers, and are droppable.

 

Helm stats are: AC 13 WIS 8 WT 3.0, steel plate look.

 

4.6.8 Q – How do I acquire the Gauntlets of Ro?  Stats?

A. – Talk to Joanna in the Paladin camp in Mount Rathe.  An acceptable faction with the Knights of Truth, Freeport, is needed.  Joanna requires of you to hunt down Sentry Alechin in his werewolf form, and return the locket in order to receive the Gauntlet mold.  Alechin appears in either human or werewolf form, and has never been spotted morphing from human to wolf form.  Killing the human form gives you a big faction hit with Knights of Truth and Priests of Marr.  No known placeholders.  Locket is No Drop.

 

Gauntlets stats are: AC 12 DEX 6 SV POISON 10 WT 2.5, gold look.

 

4.6.9 Q – How do I fix Temple of Solusek Ro factions?

A. – One way is to kill Shadowed Men, lots of them.  You can also give Ssynthi in Mistmoore Castle the flower in the burial chamber.  If you have money to spend, you can give money to an Erudite NPC named Rathamana in South Ro.  Give him 3 plats worth of coins each time for a faction adjustment, experience, and a low level prize – usually a spell.  Consider Rathamana, since he has the same factions as the NPCs in the temple.

 

4.6.10 Q – How do I acquire Ronium?

A. – Ronium comes from combining Sand of Ro, Melatite, Soil of Underfoot, Mistmoore Granite, and 2 Enchanted Platinum Bars into the Sol Cauldron.  Lord Searfire gives the Sol Cauldron if your faction is good enough.  Ronium is not Lore, not stackable, and is droppable.

 

4.6.11 Q – How do I acquire the Soil of Underfoot?

A. – Hand in 4 Fairy Dusts from the Faeries located in Lesser Faydark (loc 1000, 3750) to Priestess Chalea of Kaladim.  Fairy Dusts are random drops off the Faeries, are stackable, and are droppable.

 

4.6.12 Q – How do I acquire the Sands of Ro?

A. – Sands of Ro is random loot off Sand Giants in Oasis.  They do not stack, are not Lore, and are droppable.

 

4.6.13 Q – How do I acquire Mistmoore Granite?

A. – You can find Mistmoore Granite off gargoyles located in Mistmoore Castle zone.  Granite randomly drops off leering, jeering, and flouting gargoyles.  It is not stackable, not Lore, and is No Drop.

 

4.6.14 Q – How do I acquire Melatite?

A. – Melatite is a random drop off clockworks located in Solusek A dungeon, within the Gnome castle.  Known Melatite droppers include CWG models MC, XB, XC, CC.  Melatite weights 5.0 pounds, is not stackable, and is droppable.

 

4.6.15 Q – How do I acquire the actual armor piece?

A. – Along with the Armor mold and Ronium you should already have, go to the nearest forge and place both of them in it.  No water is needed.  Although the smithing requirements were once very high, players have been able to make pieces with as low as 20 skill, and still receive a “trivial” message.  A few players have said there is no skill at all, and were able to make the armor with 0 smithing skill, although it has not been confirmed at this time.  Failures have happened with that low a skill though.  I would recommend getting your smithing skill to at least Below Average, just to be safe.  The actual armor piece is Magic and Lore, but droppable.

 

4.6.16 Q – How can I get to some Mistmoore Granite droppers?

A. – Here’s a nice guide from Romidar (Xegony) on the Granite camp past the hidden room that is connected to the graveyard:

 

To get to the best place to hunt granite, you'll want to go to the second floor of the castle.

Go through the "jail" or "bugged room." Beside the door that acts as a switch to open the 3 "jail cells," there's a false wall. Behind it is a set of stairs. Go up the stairs as far as you can go and you'll hit a corridor that goes to the left and right. Make a left. You'll quickly come to a door that opens into the "torture chamber."

Torture chamber has 2 spawns in it (from 30th to 37th level). Kill them, then heal and med. There is one other door in this room. If you go out through this door, there's a shorty, winding corridor that opens up into a small room where two gargoyles spawn. These will be jeering, flouting or leering gargoyles (in order of difficulty and, I think, the odds of having granite). You MIGHT be able to pull one of these alone by slowly walking up to the spawn and turning around to run as soon as one comes to attack. If two come, try rooting one and then run back to the torture chamber.

At this point, a low level group can concentrate on keeping the torture chamber and gargoyle spawn clear. A higher level group can pull at least 4 more gargoyles:

If you go back to the gargoyle spawning spoint, you'll see a set of stairs, and to the left (if you're facing the stairs) a short hallway that appears to be a dead end. That short hallway actually leads, through a false wall, to the "jacuzzi" where there are 5 spawns (one of which could be Lasna Cheroon, another of which COULD be Xicotl). If Xicotl is there, pulling mobs by the jacuzzi will probably not be possible, as he has a very wide aggro range. So, either kill him or leave for another day.

If you go down the stairs (instead of making the left into the jacuzzi), you'll come to a door. Behind it sit two gargoyles, so you can try to pull one by itself by creeping up on the door (I'll call this proximity pulling from now on). Pull these gargoyles back to the torture chamber. Once dead, come back to this room.

There are two doors in the room, the one you entered and a second one right beside of it. Open the second door and you'll be looking down a set of stairs. Go down the stairs and make a right when you reach the bottom. After a very short passageway, you'll come to yet another gargoyle spawning point with 2 gargoyles. You can pull these back to the torture chamber.

This gives you 6 readily accessable gargoyles, and the granite should pile up quickly if you can pull all of them. If you're group is 40+, you could also pull the jacuzzi, the bedroom beyond it, and then you'll come to a room with 2 more gargoyles (a little foyer before the throne room). In the throne room, *6* gargoyles spawn. If you go through the throne room, you'll come to a couple of small "guest bedrooms" where you'll find another 4 gargoyles.

I recommend camping the torture chamber until you get very comfortable in Mistmoore. This spot allows VERY easy escape (run back out of the door through which you entered it, go the very short distance to the "bridge," jump off and you'll be seconds from the zone).

Our group (5 or 6 of us: paladin, paladin, ranger, ranger, wizard, shaman), ranging from 36th to 44th level camped the entire second floor along with all the gargoyle spawns I mentioned. This amounts to 18 gargoyles every 27 minutes, and we quickly got more than enough granite (along with a lot of experience, 2 soulfire hilts, 5 pairs of diamondine earrings, rune of ivy for the ivy etched helm, etc.).
