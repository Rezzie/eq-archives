1.1.10 Q – What are some common gripes of being a Paladin?

A. –

Lay Hands not being a complete heal, and being able to be disrupted.   It was gimped because of worries of ‘exploit’.

We are more along the lines of 65% Melee and 15% Healer, not the 75%/25% that we’d like to think. 

Fiery Avenger has been teasing us since beta, yet many are wary of it’s existence even though Verant claims that it was always available to us.  The revision of this quest has been ongoing for many months and we still do not know of it’s progress or ETA. 

Lack-luster offensive performance vs. all other melee classes.  

Killing other Paladins to acquire ‘Holy swords’ and killing other races that have we have no problems with (Gnomes, Faeries).  

Broken quests and factions.

1.1.12 Q – What are some benefits of using a shield?

A. – Paladins are really the only ones to make full use of shields, despite our unimpressive cap on the skill.  The 15-25 point increase in AC can make a big difference in your defensive ability.  Many shields also come with stats or resistance that you would usually forgo by only wielding a weapon.  Shields and the ability to bash plays a big part in disrupting casters, or stunning mobs, possibly causing you and your group to take much less damage.  Bashing will not add up to be a large portion of damage given to a mob, you may also damage somewhat less with a 1-hander + shield combo as compared to a 2-hander setup.

 

1.1.13 Q – I’m Level xx, should I stick with 1HS or 2HS?

A. – At low levels, at least up to level 20, there is a damage cap for all weapons.  With a decent weapon, you may be more effective using a 1hander + Shield combo than a 2hander (i.e. BBC, SBH).  A good practice would be to keep your 1hand blunt/slash and 2hand slash up to level.  Switch off weapon types when one type is maxxed.  Some races improve upon skills very quickly do to their stats, others learn very slowly.  It is much easier to keep each skill at an appropriate level, then to start from 1 point invested in a certain category and have to struggle your way up.  The best currently known Paladin-useable weapons calls upon 1hand slash/blunt, and 2hand slash.

 

1.1.14 Q - What determines how often you a hit a critter?

A. – Direct quote from Gordon Wrinn, “Only your level, offense, and weapons skill figures into the chance to hit.”

 

1.1.15 Q – What are uses for STR?

A. – Direct quote from Gordon Wrinn, “STR determines max damage and average damage. Higher STR will raise your damage cap, and will also increase your likelihood of hitting closer to your max. The exception to this is Archery, where Dex determines this.”

 

1.1.16 Q - What are uses for DEX?

A. – Direct quote from Gordon Wrinn, “Dex determines how often a weapon will fire a random process, and also figures into critical hits for warriors. It also figures into some of the rogue skills.” And another, “…whereas dex
helps your chance of a successful ‘skill increase’ roll for parry and riposte.”

 

1.1.17 Q - Are there differences in Haste items?

A. –

Direct quote from Gordon Wrinn at Verant:

 

The deal with haste-items and haste-spells:

1. There is a hard cap on how much someone can be hasted.

2. Haste-items can stack with haste-spells.

3. Haste-items do have different speeds. As a rule, it's speed will basically be based off the level creature that it came from.

4. Haste-items do not stack with other haste-items.

5. Haste-spells do not stack with other haste spells.
