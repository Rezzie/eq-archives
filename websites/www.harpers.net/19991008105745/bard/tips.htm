Beware!

-Spirit of the Wolf (aka SoW) is a commonly sought after spell, and it is quite annoying that one of our old favorites, Selo`s Accelerando, will nullify the effects. - A Role Playing switched has been added so that players may identify other characters who wish to make known they are Role Playing. To activate the Role Playing switch, target your character and type "/roleplay", if the character is +PvP, the name tag above their head will appear as a darker shade of Red, whereas a non-PvP players name will appear purple. NOTE - There is currently a BUG with this switch. While in rp mode, you will appear as anonomous to anyone who does a /who on you. If guilded however, your guild title will still show.

General Interest

Frustrated trying to switch from instrument to weapons in battle, or vice versa? Here's the answer...Both Melee and Inventory slots can be drug down to your quick key boxes, just like spells. So...set up your quick key boxes with Both your Melee slots, and 2 Inventory slots, which are kept dedicated for weapons/instruments transfers. For Example, hot boxes 1 and 2 would be your melee slots, containing 1 sword each. Hot boxes 3 is an inventory box, filled with an instrument, and 4, an empty inventory box. Now, it's just a matter of picking up the weapons from 1 and 2, and swapping them out with the instrument in box 3. No need to transfer screens, and the switch eliminates the chance of dropped weapons, while only taking a few seconds to finish.

-Singing Selo`s Accelerando will nullify the root spell on any party member.

-Kiting is the simple act of running and using Songs (or spells) on your opponent, not weapons. Allow them to chase you, but don't stop to melee, let them feel the power of your Songs (Such as Bellow) and when you have wounded them to a degree you feel the fight is more in your favour, let them catch up and teach them a lesson in manners. I prefer to group, so I've not used this tactic often (usually when something a LOT larger than me decides I'd make a tasty snack) , but it is quite effective, and requires a bit of skill and practice to perfect.
