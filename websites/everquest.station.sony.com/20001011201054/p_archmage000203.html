Answers Volume V - 2.3.00

This issue of Arch-Mage Answers is all Bards, all the time. Each question here was taken directly from our message boards. I want to thank everyone who submitted the many hundred questions. I obviously couldn’t answer them all, but I think we got a pretty good sampling. And so, without further ado…


Q: A few months after release, there was a speed cap put on Selo's due to cheaters. Was this Intentional?

A: When our speed cap was put on, it was discovered that Bards with exceptional magic drums were breaking the speed limit that we had set back in Beta. In effect, they were moving faster then the fastest we ever planned for players to move. So yes, the speed cap for cheaters brought their speed down a small percentage.


Q: Charm limited to level 37 and below mobs. Our charm is already very limiting, why the level limit?

A: We are very careful which classes we give charm to in EQ - so much so that we actually removed the ability from some classes post Beta. Bards are the only class, other then the Enchanter, which can charm all types of creature.

We are currently kicking around another higher level Bard Charm song for one of the empty level slots. If we do put it in, it will be different from Solon’s - but still cool


Q: A Bard’s Dodge caps at 120, and they get no parry or riposte. Why? (Class Balance is a fine answer, just checking)

A: Bards have much better, and much more unique, magical abilities then those of any other Hybrid class. Because of this, their melee skills are not as good as the typical Hybrid. Bards melee still better then any non-melee class - their offense, weapon skills, hit points and two-weapon fighting are superior to that of any priest or magi type.


Q: Using some Magic instruments, for example, the Darkwood Piccolo, have no effect.

A: High Quality Instruments always bump up the effect of a song. Some songs, however, don’t have effects that can be bumped up - for example, Tarew’s Aquatic Ayre gives the ability to breathe water. Having a magical instrument will not make you breathe water “better.” The Piccolo, a wind instrument, has the least number of songs that have effects which can be increased. Most Wind songs tend to be all or nothing.


Q: I have never seen a "Your target has resisted your attempt to mesmerize it" while using lullaby, as per your post. When should I?

A: I believe that went in last patch. If not, it should be in this one. You will see the resist message when your target resists your song. This not showing was a display error - it should have been fixed long ago. It took a while because when some of us (not all of us, just some) play, we use a version of the client that provides more information/messaging. This lets us catch a lot of bugs that most people might not see. It makes tracking down bugs a lot easier, quicker, and more efficient. On the downside, it sometimes isn’t always easy to remember which messages everybody sees, one which only we see. This was one of the few cases where too much information caused the problem.


Q: Will twisting ever be coded against? I.e. is it considered cheating? If it is, I definitely want to know about it now.

A: Twisting is not cheating. It won’t be coded against. We are even looking at putting a little something extra in to help the people with slower machines twist better, while not allowing those with faster machines to twist more often.


Q: The time it takes to memorize a song should be decreased.

A: We put in some code last patch to help with that.


Q: Why don't we know what instrument type songs take when we look at them in our songbook? It's very frustrating when you do not remember which instrument is used.

A: This is something we have planned to add for a while. New display code should hopefully be going in some time soon.


Q: If any instruments raise song effectiveness, can you please have that listed when we examine it?

A: Using an instrument will always increase the effect of a song - assuming that type of song can be increased by an instrument (example: Any set of Drums will make you run faster when playing Selo’s Accelerando). Some instruments will increase your effect more then others. The same new display code that is going in to help with the Song/Skill identification will also display instrument bonus/penalties.


Q: In the expansion, the level cap will be changed and we will presumably get more songs. Will our bard singing skills have a higher skill cap? For that matter, why are our bard skills capped at 200? Songs are our lifeblood and an integral part of this class.

A: Almost every skill in EverQuest is capped at 200. There are, of course, some exceptions - but the vast majority of EQ skills range from 1-200.

That being said, if the level caps go up, you can safely assume that Bard Skills will increase with it.


Q: Is there a reason why the Bard's mana song cannot be twisted? Perhaps because of game balance again?

A: There is a common assumption that ALL Bard songs last for 3 “ticks.” This is not true. Many songs last for shorter and longer durations. The Bard’s mana song is one with a shorter duration. For more details, see Volume III of the Arch-Mage answers.


Q: While I understand the idea that Druidic and Shaman SOW effects cannot be used indoors (I even understand the Journeyman boots), I don't understand why the Bard running song not working indoors.

A: Game Balance. No spell or effect that speeds up a character will work indoors. Period.

During Beta we found that indoor use of movement increasing spells and songs led to trains on a massive scale. When we took out the movement effect songs, the amount of trains happening in dungeons significantly decreased. This was one, of many, reasons that we will never have movement increasing spells or songs able to be cast in dungeons.


Q: Denon's Desperate Dirge - is it really that "desperate?" After all, the Bard does 300 damage to himself to do 300 damage to all MOBS in a certain radius around him. That's really cool, except 300 damage is absolutely nothing. Casters have been doing that since level 30, and Bards get a song that does that at level 43. Plus, this one actually uses all the Bard's mana so it's a one shot deal.

A: The 300 damage is only done if you target something near yourself - this is an AoE song, and like all other AoE effects, if the caster stands too close, they get hit with their own effect. Ask any Wizard. As for the amount of damage dealt - Bards do not have large damage songs - their songs are more of a gradual thing, dealing damage over time. This song breaks the rules for Bards - it deals a large (for them) amount of damage instantly. This is why they can sing it very infrequently (i.e. it takes up all their mana).

Bards were actually never intended to have any songs that took mana. Using mana on this one was just a convenient way to mark off time for how often they could use the song.


Q: What kind of condiment taste best on Wood Elf Bards?

A: Around here, we go for barbecue sauce. To paraphrase one of the other game designers, “it really brings out that nice smoky wood taste.”


Q: You mentioned previously that adding an extra tick to bard DoT songs would make them 50% more powerful. Why not simply add to the damage each tick does instead?

A: Solid suggestion.

The reason that we did not increase the damage for Bard songs is that, honestly, most Bards don’t kite with their DoT songs. The Bellow is an instant - so it is unaffected by movement / kiting. Chords and such are good songs - but we do not want to increase the damage that they deal every tick for the 5% or so of the time that movement might lower their damage.

Bards are also the only class that can move and sing/cast a song at the same time. Every other class must be standing still before they can produce a magical effect. Increasing the damage done by Bard songs, purely for purposes of making them even better Kiters, is against the spirit of the reason that we put in the anti-kiting code in the first place.


Q: In 82 days of play my most ingrained behavior is to switch between weapons and instruments slowly and with the utmost care for fear of dropping something. Will a yes/no confirmation window or an item drop toggle be added to prevent us from dropping weapons?

A: We are looking into doing this. No promises, but, again, solid suggestion.

BTW - 82 days? Very impressive. (And no, please don’t everybody e-mail me your played times - we love you all regardless of how many days of play time you have).


Q: Why did you make all the weapons in plane of hate for bards pretty much the exact same thing except for its weapon type.

A: There were a few items added to Hate Plane for Bards. Three of them were originally designed to be dropped from the same monster - the three identical Bard weapons. They were made identical so that no matter which weapon a Bard had chosen to train up in (1HS, 1HB or Piercing), they would have a good weapon to chose from. During the tuning and testing phase of Hate, these weapons got split up and placed on different monsters.


Q: Why didn’t you make the attack speed songs of bards stack with other spells.

A: No attack speed spells or songs stack with each other either. Each player can h ave a total of two haste effects on them - one from an item, and one from a spell/song.


Q: Does AC matter for a bard?

A: Yes. A better AC is always worth having. It means that you get hit for less damage.


Q: Can you PLEASE make our songs attract less mobs? I cant even sing in planes during a fight because all the mobs will go aggressive on me!! So I get turned into a mediocre tank.

A: We reduced the Bard song taunt factor a bit this/last patch. Sometimes, however, discretion is the better part of valor. I know many Bards that leave their songs playing, regardless of the situation. There are times when you want to stop singing, in the same way that there are times when a Cleric may not want to attract attention by Healing, or a Wizard may not want to attract attention with their DD spells.


Q: Our dispel magic song is a very nice song, but can you make it more like nullify magic? By the time we get this song, we can’t dispel a lot of the buffs. Is there a reason for this?

A: Check the patch message - Syvelian’s has gotten a bit more powerful. That being said, all magic effects can be dispelled. Some are just harder then others. The difference in levels between the caster and the person casting the cancel does make a difference. What the “better” cancel magic spells do is add to your effective level for purposes of the comparison.


Q: Why do we not have the ability to play Invisibility and See Invisibility songs together? Or perhaps allowing our see invisible to be played on top of a casters invisible or camouflage?

A: Any effect that hits you while invisible turns you visible. We recently added the See Invisible component to the Bard’s invisibility song - this should help the problem. (Or you can just follow the cool sparkles…)


Q: Can you give us some way to increase faction (when we have a god) so that Guise is worth using?

A: Try using Cinda’s Charismatic Carillon - the song increases your faction with that of the targeted creature.


Q: Is Kellin's Lucid Lullaby (Lvl 15) supposed to have a memory blur component?

A: Nope.


Q: Ok this is a personal question and probably completely trivial, the enameled black chestplate from lower guk is a black plate that is wearable by all the plate wearing classes except Bard. I wear an inferior black plate because I like the black plate look, can this plate be made available to Bards?

A: No reason. I changed it so that Bards can use it too - should be in this patch.


Q: Recently, the range on Brusco's Boastful Bellow (level 12 song) was drastically reduced, leaving bards with, for all intents and purposes, no ranged attack. There are times when my bard cannot bellow a mob that is actually within aggro range, because it is "too far away".

A: We didn’t change it. I double checked my database just to be sure, and the range is what it has always been (half the range of a Wizard style DD spell).


Q: Are there enhanced effects for these songs when the proper instrument is equipped? In the case of Aquatic Ayre, is the enhancement range increased? For Sirens and Pixie Strike, does it increased duration, chance of success, or both?

A: Instruments only effect the primary effects of the song (healing, buff, attack speed, etc.) - not the range/duration/chance of success, etc.


Q: If a player is targeted when the level 26 screech (area fear) is started, a message is displayed that this sort of spell cannot be cast on players. Is this "feature" intended?

A: All player classes lost the ability to Fear (or anything else that causes the AI to take over your character) other PCs. This was, sadly, due to a few people who were really abusing it. They spoiled it for everybody.


Q: First I would like to know why you made bards hybrids. I mean I see rangers ( druid/warrior), Paladins (cleric/warrior), and ShadowKnights (necro/warrior), but a Bard is a rogue/?

A: Bards are Hybrids (by classification) because they more closely resemble the Paladin/SK/Ranger then any other group of classes.


Q: Can you explain your thoughts behind Psalm of Purity and Psalm of Vitality? These songs are simply useless since any 5th level druid and 4th level cleric can cure the disease and poison these songs are supposed to protect from. I mean truly 39 points of disease protection for a 29th level song?

A: These songs, in addition to protecting from poison and disease, also have chances to remove a poison or disease effect on you. They are a mixture of a cure and a resistance buff in one.


Q: Alenia’s Disenchanting Melody. It will remove all buffs/deduffs except Necromancer and ShadowKnight spells. If this song gets rid of a 49th level buff it darn sure should get rid of a 14th level infectious cloud or steal strength. Again these spells are counteracted by 4/5th level spells from a druid or cleric. Could you explain why it doesn't?

A: Alenia’s Disenchanting Melody will get rid of magical effects - but it will not get rid of poison or disease effects (no cancel magic style spell will get rid of a poison or disease effect). For that you need a Cure - or a Psalm.


Q: Do you believe Dissension the Mana Draining Song is actually useful against a Monster? What scenarios do you envision it being used and can this actually be done in game?

A: My favorite is to use it against single, large mobs that you don’t want casting lots of spells. Vox is a good example - the less mana she has, the less Complete Healing spells she can cast.


Q: I notice that Charisma affects all of the Mind based songs as far as resists are concerned. My question is what statistics affects the resists of the Direct Damage and Damage over time songs.

A: No statistic effects damage songs. Charisma is special in making mental based songs / spells harder to save against. One of the reasons that this was done was to give an extra benefit to classes / races that have higher Charismas.


Q: The radius on Selo's Accelerando should be doubled.

A: I agree. That should be in next patch.


Q: As a Wood Elf Bard, my Forage skill gets capped at 40 regardless of the fact that my heritage as a Wood Elf has trained me better. Is it intended to be so that as a bard I LOSE that heritage?

A: That’s silly. I’ll see what I can do.


Q: Why are some 2H Slashing weapons usable by bards but our skill is capped at 0?

A: Bards were never slotted to get 2H weapons. They have no ability to skill in it from a Guild Master, nor do they ever actually get the skill. There is a display error, however, at the Bard Guild Master which shows that the skill is trainable.


Q: What is the specific impact wielding a flute has on each of the following: Cinda's Charismatic Carillon, Tarew's Aquatic Ayre, Solon's Song of the Sirens, Crission's Pixie Strike, and Agilmente's Aria of Eagles?

A: Greater Faction increase, none, none, Lowers more Magic Resistance, none.


Q: I play a shaman. This is perhaps a bit off the mark, but I was wondering what are the best buffs for a bard? For example, I recently discovered … that dexterity helps a bard, as does charisma. Any other buffs that a wise bard should be asking for?

A: Charisma and Dexterity are the best Buffs. Most Bards that I know can also always use a few more points of strength - their armor usually puts them dangerously close to encumbrance. AC & HP buffs are also nice - most denizens of Norrath have an inexplicable hatred of music and tend to take it out on nearby minstrels.


Q: We have one song that only affects the bard only, called Jonathan’s Whistling Warsong. Why haven’t we seen similar songs at higher levels?

A: Most Bard songs are designed to be grant group effects rather then personal buffs. Jonthan’s Warsong was put in to give lower level Bards a boost at a time where they needed one. We could certainly add another higher level version of the song, but I have a feeling that it just wouldn’t be used that much.


Q: Because we can be interrupted while being attacked or experiencing lag, were there any thoughts on increasing the duration of levitation/invisibility/water breathing? No one in his or her right mind would ask a bard to perform these songs in combat situations. A great set of group songs that need a little more work.

A: This is something that I have been running some tests on recently. There is a decent chance that we will add a tick or so to the duration of each of these songs. It won’t overpower them, but it will make them easier to use in most situations.


Q: It seems the level 34 song Solidarity of Vision may have a use, but its duration is to short. Do you plan to increase it?

A: I think that did receive a slight duration boost. It should be going in this patch or the next one - not sure which.
