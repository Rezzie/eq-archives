In Development

The world of EverQuest is constantly evolving, and the ability to patch and update the game is one of the greatest strengths a massively multiplayer game has. The following is a description of issues and functionality that will be changed in the future — including planned new features and/or changes to existing features. .

Please note that the following information takes precedence over information that may be included in the EverQuest manual, strategy guide, and other official documents previously released.

Please also note that changes or additions are targeted to be implemented short term unless otherwise indicated, however there is no commitment to a specific date.

 

Future Development

 

 
Zones
  

Two new areas tentatively titled The Hole, and The Warrens will be accessible via Paineel and Erudin on Odus. The zones will be free of charge and available via an optional patch. The Hole is a high level dungeon which represents the ruins of Old Paineel, destroyed long ago in a war with between the Erudites and the Heretics. The Warrens is a dungeon for lower level players, and is the original home of the Kobolds upon Norrath. More information regarding these new zones will be made available as their release date nears.

 
General Issues
  

Though a majority of the EverQuest development team's time has been dedicated to creating The Ruins of Kunark expansion, we are now turning our attention to the enhancement of current game play related features, such as dungeon tuning and balancing, and quest implementation. Following are the features we plan to focus on:

- The Make Poison trade skill for Rogues will be updated.

- The retuning of Permafrost into a higher level zone.

- The retuning of Mistmoore and Gukbottom.

- Implementation of class-related weapon quests such as the Fiery Avenger quest.

 
Spells
  

Please note that The Ruins of Kunark Beta was used for testing spells. Some spells were altered for the release of the expansion. This was one purpose of the Beta - testing the viability of spells and their impact upon game balance.

Unless noted below, all spells work with their intended effects.
