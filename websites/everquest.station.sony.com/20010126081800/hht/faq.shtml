FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Harpy's Head Tavern ?
The Harpy's Head Tavern™ is an official EverQuest™ site that will provide in-character content related to EverQuest. Some of the things you will find while visiting the Harpy's Head Tavern are Historical Character Profiles, Official Historical Quest Summaries, GM Profiles, a weekly "Dear Mennix" column, an official Events section, and an In-Character forum.
 
 
What is a "Static" Quest?
A Static Quest is any sort of quest in the game that solely involves NPCs (Non Player Characters) that are controlled by the AI of the EverQuest servers. A good example of this sort of quest is the "Newbie Note" quest that all new characters can complete to obtain their guild tunic, robe or other starting item.
 
 
What is a "Dynamic" Quest?
A Dynamic Quest is a broad term that describes any sort of quest where the NPCs are played by live actors, rather then being controlled by the AI of the EverQuest servers. Dynamic Quests can range from simple invasions lasting just a few minutes to complex and intricate stories spanning the course of several weeks.
 
 
What is a Historic Quest?
Historic Quests are a special sort of Dynamic Quest. Often complex and spanning the course of several weeks, they often result in temporary or even permanent changes to the game world's story line. Historic Quests advance the ever-evolving plot line of the world of Norrath.
 
 
Can I petition in game, send e-mail or contact anybody to get a special Dynamic Quest for either my Guild, or myself?
As much as we would love to be able to be able to offer impromptu Dynamic Quests for specific players or guilds, it is something that would be impossible to accomplish.
 
 
Will Dynamic Quests ever be announced ahead of time?
Dynamic Quests are never announced ahead of time and will always be a surprise. However, frequent visits to the "Rumors and Whispers" section of the Harpy's Head Tavern may give you clues about who to look for in game and where the action may take place next.
 
 
What is the "Rumors and Whispers" section of the Harpy's Head Tavern™?
Rumors and Whispers should be visited often to give you the inside scoop on current events and the on-going changes to the game world, through the perspective of well-known residents of the world of Norrath. You'll find clues, rumors and sometimes just wild speculation that can help you to complete both Static and Dynamic Quests.
 
 
What are the "Annals of History"?
The "Annals of History" retell important Historic Quests and Events that have shaped the world of Norrath up until the present day. Here you can find information about why the world is the way it is, often told through the Historical Characters themselves, who were present as the events transpired in the evolving history of Norrath.
 
 
What is the difference between a "Quest" and an "Event"?
Events are almost always pre-announced, with rules, times, and dates scheduled by server, and are run by GM's and Guides acting as Event Coordinators. Events do not involve GM Quest actors, adventure, or historic storyline. An example of an Event would be the Test of Steel, where warriors gather to duel according to strict rules before a GM Event Coordinator. Role-playing is encouraged at Events, but GM Event Coordinators will generally not be in direct conflict with the players. Players who wish to participate in an Event should follow the directions in the Event Announcements.
 
Quests are almost never pre-announced, and usually have very limited rules for players. Quests are run with GM Quest actors, and/or controlled NPC's that can be anything from Gnolls to Dragons. GM's involved in a Quest will customarily interact with the players in a strict role-playing manner, according to the dynamic or historic storyline for the quest. This may include conflict, and GM actors may often slay, or be slain by players to further the storyline. Players may best successfully experience a Quest (and possibly obtain its rewards) by following the clues, and interacting with GM Quest actors and NPCs, as appropriate.
 
 
What are Historical Characters?
A Historical Character is a character who is intimately involved with the ever evolving and dynamic history of Norrath. Many of these characters are featured on both the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark™ box.
 
 
Will I ever run into these characters during normal game play?
You just might! It is possible that you could run into, meet, and interact with these Historic Characters in your adventures throughout the world of Norrath. You may see them in a bar or tavern reliving stories of their past over an ale or five, walking through a city street on business, or giving speeches at the city gates on the finer points of magic. Keep your ears to the ground and your eyes peeled!
 
 
Who are the different characters featured in the artwork on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark boxes?
 
 
Who is the beautiful High Elf featured on the cover of the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The blonde High Elven woman is Firiona Vie. A High-Elven Paladin, she is the daughter of King Tearis Thex and has been dubbed "The Chosen of Tunare" by Tunare herself.
 
 
Who is the Erudite in the blue robes featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Erudite in the blue robes is one known as Al`Kabor. Responsible for the creation of several wizard spells that bear his name, it is said that Al`Kabor is perhaps the most powerful wizard in the world.
 
 
Who is the female Half-Elven bard on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Half-Elven bard shown with a lute on EverQuest box and shown playing a flute on the EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark box, is Sionachie Heartsinger. She is a contemporary of Firiona Vie and one of her closest friends.
 
 
Who is the Human Paladin featured on the EverQuest™ and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark™ Box?
The Human Paladin is Sir Jevik Isqual. A follower of Mithaniel Marr, he is also among the close friends and traveling companions of Firiona Vie.
 
 
Who is the Dwarf featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Dwarf is named Thubir Axebringer. He usually goes by his nickname of "Thubr", a moniker he gained as a child due to his little sister's inability to say the "ir" sound at the end of his name.
 
 
Who is the Halfling rogue featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Halfling rogue is named Dreezil Stickens Pocketdip, usually just known as Dreezil. He became skilled at the "art" as a young child but now a little older, he has given up thieving for profit and is generally an honest, good natured and down to earth fellow.
 
 
Who is the Barbarian woman featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Barbarian woman is warrior named Dagda Icefury. A woman of strong will and straight forward action, she is an unparalleled tactician. She has pledged on her honor to defend the well being of the two Gnomes Ognit Eznertob and Dabner Drednever.
 
 
Who are the Gnomes featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Gnomes are Ognit Eznertob, a wizard and a grump and Dabner Drednever, cleric of Brell Serilis and lovable air-head. The two were rescued by Dagda Icefury, who pledged to protect the two. As Gnomes are wont to do, they often experiment with powers they do not understand, often with disastrous results.
 
 
Who is the male Wood Elf ranger featured on the EverQuest™ Box?
The male Wood Elf ranger is Lorisyn Oakwynd. One of Firiona Vie's traveling companions. He was killed by the Ogre, Ghargin Bumblok.
 
 
Who is the dark elf featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Dark Elf is a wizard by the name of Vahlai Ka`Izal. She has eternally bound the troll Rogkasth to herself, and he is now her eternal servant. She has a severe hatred of all races, including her own and has swore to destroy Firiona Vie.
 
 
Who is the troll featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Troll is known as Rogkasth Vr`l. A Shadow Knight and a cold and cunning killer, Rogkasth inherited a bit of Vahlai's intelligence through the process of her binding him and making him her slave. He is more intelligent then the average Troll and has also inherited much of her extreme hatred.
 
 
Who is the Ogre featured on the EverQuest and EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Ogre is a shaman named Ghargin Bumblok. With just enough wisdom to cast a few spells, Ghargin has the mind of a child and follows Vahlai and Rogkasth out of fear. He is responsible for the death of Lorisyn Oakwynd.
 
 
Who is the Iksar featured on the EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark Box?
The Iksar is named Danak Dhorentath. Danak is responsible for Firiona Vie's kidnapping and is one of Venril Sathir's many henchmen on the continent of Kunark.
 
 
Who is the dragon featured on the EverQuest Box?
The Dragon is named Zavo`Zatanov. She helped to recover the Crystal Claw of Veeshan from the treacherous Lady Vox, and helped to perform the ritual which tore the veil seperating the Norrath from the Plane of Sky. She often appears as a High Elf, as she finds the arrogance of the Koada`Dal amusing but respects the beauty of that "lesser race".
 
 
Who are the "Facilitators of Norrath"?
The "Facilitators of Norrath" are the avatars of the game designers, producers, artists, and anyone else who helps to bring life to the world of EverQuest. You may occasionally catch a glimpse of these people on the Test Server or at special events or see their posts on official message boards.
 
 
I sent some letters to "Dear Mennix", why doesn't he answer my question?
Mennix receives many letters each day and can only choose three to answer each week. He will keep all letters, and in time, hopes to answer as many as he can.
 
 
What is the purpose of the Harpy's Head Tavern Message board?
The Harpy's Head Tavern message board is for in-character discussion and story telling. This means that any "out of character" postings will be deleted. Try one of our other message boards for discussions about other out of character issues.
