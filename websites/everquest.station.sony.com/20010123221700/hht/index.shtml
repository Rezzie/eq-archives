Aradune on the Boards 5:00pm
Brad "Aradune" McQuaid visited the General Discussion forum over the weekend to address issues ranging from missing zones to paying to play the MMORPG.

Where are all the missing zones?
Once upon a time (early 1996, to be more precise), Steve Clover and I drew the world of Norrath on a white board in the old Sony Interactive Studios building. We added rough names, including most of the starting cities I think (Steve is really good with fantasy names, and he also came up with the name 'EverQuest', btw).

Velious planned before Kunark?
Sometime into development, however, we decided that it just wouldn't be possible to include the density of zones desired across five continents. So we decided to put more zones in less continents, and hoped we could work on Kunark and Velious later.

On "pay to play" or, MMORPG Business Models 101
Anyway, Raph Koster, SW:G's Creative Director, has made some posts that I thought warranted posting on Verant's other message boards because they're applicable to MMOGs in general. The first two have to do with why MMORGs have both a retail cost and subscription fee. In the future, don't be surprised to see me cross-post more articles, because, well, Raph knows his stuff.

January 19, 2001 - Friday

A Reminder 6:30pm
EQ Customer Service has reported that the Technical Support Representatives are being inundated with calls for in-game assistance. Technical Support can not help with any in-game related issue. Technical Support are not GM and can only offer technical support for technical issues. Any game related issue that is not a technical problem must be petitioned in game for a Game Master to handle. If a customer calls technical support for an in-game related issue such as lost items, experience, or any such issue that can be handled in game by a GM, all the technical support rep can do is advise the caller to petition in game. Please only call technical support for issues of a technical nature. They can not help with any in-game issues.

GM Staffing Changes
To keep the EverQuest community better informed of changes in customer service staffing regarding GMs and servers, we will now be posting news items when staffing changes are made. Following are the most current changes to date.

First, let’s start with the new Game Masters. Before any GM is given a server, he or she must first be fully trained as a GM and a Customer Service representative. Once all the training is completed, including time serving as a chat GM, available servers are assigned. GM Ozuri, after going through extensive PvP training will be taking up the reigns of the Rallos Zek server. GM Zrinic jumps on board on Prexus. Nangwaya is looking forward to being Terris Thule’s new GM.

Not only new GMs find themselves on new servers, some of the more weathered ones are transferred to other servers. GM Exuro, the former GM of Rallos is now taking up duties on Vazaelle. GM Imia, who served as a SWAT/Chat GM will now be looking over things on Xev while GM Lirus assumes duties on Ayonae.

There is also a couple changes to the Lead GM structure, one is a new Lead, and the other changes shifts. Congrats to GM Axgoreth for his promotion to Lead GM who will now be responsible for supervising a team of Game Masters. In order to fill the night Lead position and to provide better coverage during those late night hours, Lead GM Delanus takes over the night shift.

And last but not least, changes to GMs that most do not see because their duties are mostly performed off server. GM Lithgar, the former GM of Povar, is stepping into GM Jhek’s shoes as the Guide Coordinator while Jhek takes over duties as Guide Liaison.

“We believe it is important for the community to be aware of these changes and from now on, will be keeping you informed as changes are made,” said Head GM Elionia.

-Steve “Mennix” Fuller - sfuller@station.sony.com
