Setting up Eudora

  1. If you don't have it already, download Eudora from www.eudora.com.
  2. StuffIt, or a similar unstuffing program, should automatically unstuff the program.
  3. Once unstuffed, you can run Eudora by double clicking on the file.
  4. To configure the software, go to the Special/Configuration pull-down menu.

  5. You will see the following screen:

  6. The POP account must be in the form of username@pop.enteract.com.
  7. Your Real Name is simply your name, or how you want your name to appear on your messages.
  8. Make sure that the Connection Method is either MacTCP or TCP/IP depending on which you are using to dial.
  9. Fill in the SMTP field with smtp.enteract.com.
  10. You Return Address should be the same as your e-mail address. It will take the form of username@enteract.com.
  11. All other options are up to you.
  12. Click OK.

Checking Mail

  1. To begin retriveal of your mail, click the File/Check Mail pull-down menu.

  2. When the mail is downloaded, you will see the following screen:

  3. To read a message, simply double click it.

Composing and Sending a Message

  1. Click the Message/New Message pull-down menu.

  2. You will see another window which prompts you for who you are sending your message to and the subject of your message. In addition, there is space to compose your message.

  3. When you've finished typing your message, simply click the Send button and your message will be sent immediately.

Bug Fix for PowerBooks using the Global Village Platinum PCMCIA card with Eudora 3.0

Some of our users have found that Eudora 3.0 will not send mail via SMTP when used with a PowerBook and the Global Villiage Platinum PCMCIA card.

To fix this, go to the Global Village website and download the Ethernet Driver Update for Global Villiage PCMCIA modems. It should fix a lot of different bugs with the GV software, including this particular Eudora issue.
