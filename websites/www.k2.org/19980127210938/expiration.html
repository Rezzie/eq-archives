When do groups expire their articles?


A brief introduction to our news server: news.enteract.com is a SPARC 5/110 running Solaris 2.5 and the news server package, INN1.4UNOFF4. We have 21 Gb of striped, concatenated (RAID level 0) disk space allocated to the news spool, and an additional 4.2 Gb for the operating system and other news server files.

As of January 1997, it is typical for us to receive 5Gb of news a day in approximately 250,000 articles.

We aspire to hold the most useful articles as long as we can; here is our current expiration policy explaining at what point we delete articles from the news spool:

  • The newsgroup hierarchies comprising the "Big 8" (comp, humanities, misc, news, rec, sci, soc and talk) expire after 14 days, with the exception that articles with Expires: headers (typically newsgroup charters or FAQs) are allowed to remain in the spool up to 31 days after they are posted.

  • The hierarchies alt, bionet, linux and microsoft also expire after 14 days and articles with Expires: headers are allowed to remain in the spool up to 31 days after they are posted.

    There are some exceptions to the 14 day alt.* expiration policy, due to the huge sizes of these sub-hierarchies:
    • Groups under alt.sex are expired after 3 days.
    • Groups under alt.binaries are expired after 1 day (as of 12/31/96, 80% of our 5Gb a day of total incoming news was in the alt.binaries hierarchy)

  • The *.answers groups -- which typically hold the FAQs for groups in their hierarchies -- are expired after 31 days, but held up to 45 days if containing an appropriate Expires: header.

  • Articles in the chi and enteract hierarchies are expired after 30 days (with the exception of enteract.news, which is never expired and chi.jobs which is expired after 14 days).

  • Articles in any other hierarchy not already mentioned are expired after 7 days and articles with Expires: headers may expire as late as 14 days after they are posted.

  • A few relatively obscure foreign hierarchies (tw, de, relcom, es, fj, z-netz, maus, ncar, it, nl, ukr, sfnet, pl, prg) are expired after 2 or 4 days instead of the default 7, to conserve disk space for articles which we anticipate our users would be more generally interested in. However, if you read groups in any of these hierarchies, and would prefer that the expire time be increased, drop us a line and we would be more than happy to comply. (The rest of the obscure foreign hierarchies expire after 7 days). :-)

  • The internal news server newsgroups control, control.cancel and control.sendsys are expired after 1 day (the other groups in the control hierarchy are expired after the default 7 days.

  • *.jobs.* newsgroups expire articles after 7 days, except chi.jobs which is retained 14 days.
