Work

I am a senior software engineer for Motorola Automotive and Industrial Electronics Group in Northbrook, IL. If you want to know more about my experience and education, feel free to browse my Professional Profile. I currently spend most of my time doing the following:
  • Software Tool Development
  • Programming with Perl, Java, and C++
  • Software Configuration Management using ClearCase and ClearDDTS
  • Object-Oriented Design
  • Unix Script Crafting
  • Software Process Definition and Improvement

Check out my WWW Links for a list of more than three thousand links to software engineering and computer science resources on the WWW. I've also written a few documents and papers that you may or may not be interested in.

School

I'm working on my M.S. in Software Engineering from National Technological University. NTU provides courses via satellite from more than 45 universities across the U.S., including schools like: The University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, The University of California at Berkeley, The University of Michigan, and many others of comparable quality. I can mix and match courses from any of the participating universities towards my degree.

I attended The University of Michigan in Ann Arbor as an undergraduate student and received my B.S. in Computer Science and Mathematics in 1988.

Play

I like creating software development tools and making them freely available. Please browse my anonymous FTP directory to see if I've created anything that may be of use to you. When I'm not busy with work or school or programming, I spend much of my time (whatever's left anyway) doing the following:
  • Dancing (jazz, ballet, modern, african)
  • Martial Arts
  • Singing and playing guitar
  • Reviewing technical books for Addison-Wesley Longman
  • Author and maintainer of The Stretching and Flexibility FAQ
  • Author and maintainer of CmdLine and Options (C++ command-line parsing libraries)
  • Author and maintainer of some C++ class libraries implementing AVL Trees, 2-3 Trees, and Red-Black Trees (bottom up)
