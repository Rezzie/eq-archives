CHICAGO'S INDUSTRIAL PROPERTY DILEMMA

Thesis

Rapidly rising property values due to increasing numbers of people moving into city neighborhoods are causing a shrinkage of prime industrial zoned land near downtown Chicago. As residential property values skyrocket, developers convert previously industrial use properties into residential use properties in order to whet the insatiable demand of affluent homebuyers. Since the companies being forced out have already located themselves within the city limits of Chicago they probably wish to remain in Chicago. Otherwise they would have moved to the suburbs a long time ago. Regrettably many companies are finding a shortage of prime industrial space within the city. The preferable solution would be a creation of new industrial land within the city limits by completing the demolition of residential and commercial properties in non-performing sections of the city. In this way government can allow upscale residential growth while creating industrial corridors in areas experiencing residential stagnation. In this way the rapid increase of property values in neighborhoods like Near North will be allowed to continue while industry will have an option to move into cheap land in previously desolate communities.
