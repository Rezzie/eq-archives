Who ARE these guys and what do they WANT from us, anyways?

The Band In Question have been noodling around these parts, these parts being Chicagoland (that's what THEY call it, not us...) for the last year or so, questioning the authenticity of what it is THEY call music. Their discoveries will amaze and startle you nearly to the point of excitement! Let's take a look and see was' up!


Since before even, yes, the advent of the whell...

The Band In Question waited to become. Since then, they have been playing out constantly and expanding the overall consciousness of their audiences past, present, and future. To critical acclaim, they have received the praises of bar owners and patrons alike across North America as being one of America's "Best Kept Secrets". Playing a diverse and eclectic mix of rock, blues, and jazz styles, fused with a pseudo-folk/avante-garde twist, The Band In Question has been freaking out crowds and keeping them wondering as to what could possibly be next.

Really now, who IS The Band In Question?

The Band In Question is made up of roughly five members. They are as follows:

Questionable Personel
Bass Guitar/Vox
Paul Maylan
Guitar/Vox
Lonn Whateverthehell
Guitar/Vox/Banjo
Greg Dalbey
Drums/Vox
Billy Benson
Keys/Trumpet
Matt Johnson

The long-standing members of the band are Bill and Greg, who met during the fall of 1995 while playing for the now defuct band, Indian Summer. Since then, the two hooked up with Lonn, who joined the team the following summer. The new team, focused on free-associcative music, improvisation, rhythmic and sonic experimentation, tempered with their own unique blend of standard and contemporary blues and rock. Their peculiar voice, lending itself toward a kind of jazz sound was underscored by the arrival of Paul Maylan, who took over the bass-guitar duties from Greg later that winter.

Since then, the Band In Question have found themselves surfing the vibes around them and spreading the good vibes they catch to the music-folk in and around the Chicago area. The band is constantly exploring new sounds, so some of the members fluctuate, but Matt seems to be a staple. Matt, a one-time member of the United Stated Marines, plays electric piano and trumpet regularly with the band, adding his own freaky flare and groovy textures.
