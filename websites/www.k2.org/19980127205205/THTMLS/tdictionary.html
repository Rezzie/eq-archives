DICTIONARY

A Glossary of Terms





Web Site

    This is your page or pages on the World Wide Web. This is where most users will first locate your company's presence.

FTP Site

    Many companies wish to offer data files or program files that their customers can download and/or execute. An FTP Site gives the company the ability to offer these files.

Virtual E-mail

    With 'Virtual E-mail' you can have 'anything@yourcompany.com' sent to a specific mailbox. Most sites offer easy contact points for their customers or prospective customers. For instance, on your web page you might have the line 'E-mail info@yourcompany.com for more information.' Customers could click here to very quickly request more information concerning your products or services. 'webmaster@yourcompany.com' is used for individuals who would like to make a suggestion concerning your website. These contact points are extremely important to individuals seeking information from your firm. We highly recommend them, regardless of where you host your site!

Auto-responder

    A good way to make an impression with customers is to send them a quick response to their requests for information. An auto-responder is an automatic response sent by e-mail to anyone who sends a message to, for instance, 'info@yourcompany.com'. All customer queries are then sent on to individuals at your company for follow-up.

Mailing List

    A mailing list is an easy way for customers of your company to discuss your product or service. Essentially, you set up an address called 'yourproduct@yourcompany.com.' When a customer sends a message to this address, it will go to all of your customers who are 'subscribed' to the mailing list. Customers can easily subscribe and unsubscibe from the list. Most mailing lists involve active discussions among users, often with users helping other users solve problems!

Access Account

    In order to administer a web page (i.e. get mail, redesign a web page, etc.) it is usually necessary to have a dial-up account. Of course this account, can also be used to check out the competition and to just have fun.

Statistics and Logging

    What good is your site unless you can find out who's looking at it, how long their staying, and where they go. Basic usage reports are provided free and more complex usage reports can be generated for nominal fees.
On-line Disk Storage
    This refers to megabytes of disk storage available for HTML documents, FTP downloads, and auto-responder messages. For instance, a typical small company web-site might consist of one introduction page and 10-20 other information pages. If each page averaged 25K then this would use less than one megabyte of on-line storage. On the other hand, some large company sites might include a catalog with 1000 items and a 30K JPG image of each product. This would require closer to 30-40 megabytes of online storage.

Bandwidth

    Bandwidth is a measure of how much data is being transmitted to a customer from your site. For instance, look at the example company above, where a page averaged 25K in size. To reach 1G of bandwidth, that company would need 40,000 hits or accesses. You would need to have a pretty popular site or have a lot of graphics to exceed this!

Domain Name Registration

    Many companies would like to register their corporate identity on the Internet. For instance, the discovery channel is found at www.discovery.com and the employees' addresses read jsmith@discovery.com.

Register Site with Major Indexes

    In order for people to find your site, it is important that the major search indexes be aware of it. This is a remarkably easy process some providers charge PR firm rates to do. We'll gladly register your site in over 15 major indexes for only $60!

Forms Support

    Forms are used to collect information from your customers. The results of a form can then be e-mailed to you. For instance, your customers could fill out a form right on your web site requesting information about a specific product or service. This would generate an e-mail you could handle yourself or use an Auto-responder.
Database Capabilities
    You can use our database capabilities to allow your customers to look up data from a database you own, or to input data into a database by using a form. For instance, say you wished to provide a service where customers could lookup a physician based on certain criteria. You would need database capabilities to hold the list of physicians and forms support to allow them to lookup data from the database. EnterAct's consultants can help you set all this up for an extra charge.

Custom CGI Scripts

    These can give your web site capabilities that really differentiate it. A CGI script allows you to execute programs on our server and return results to customers. For instance, many financial sites allow you to enter stock symbols to look up. These sites are using CGI scripts to lookup the data in a database and return the data in HTML form to you.
