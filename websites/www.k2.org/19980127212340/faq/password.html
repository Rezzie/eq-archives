Change Your Password

Your password is the key to the security of your account. If your password is compromised, people other than yourself can access your account, potentially incurring extra charges or violating the privacy of your communications. It is thus important that you make sure your password is kept confidential at all times. Change your password often!

Good passwords should contain both letters and numbers. Your password should not appear in a dictionary. Examples of good passwords include things like el1t0! and ra1n+f0o. Examples of bad passwords include yellow and password. Your password should not contain any permutation of your real name or account name.

EnterAct subscribers with currently active accounts can change their password from this web page. You must know your current password to change your password. REMEMBER YOUR PASSWORD, there is no way to recover a forgotten password. If you lose your password, you can contact us via telephone at (773) 248-8511, and we'll reset it for you.

Security-conscious people should note that this web page is NOT secured with any type of encryption; passwords changed over this web page are vulnerable to interception. However, the risk involved in changing your password here is no greater than the risk of changing it from your shell account via 'telnet'.

Your account name is the name you input when logging onto the system. It's equivalent to the first part of your email address... i.e., yourname in "yourname@enteract.com".

Your password will not be visible as you type it in. If you would like to see what password you have entered after your password is changed, click "show password".
