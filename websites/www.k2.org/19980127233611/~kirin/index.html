Champions PBEM Game Center

Welcome to the Champions Play By Email Game Center. You are invited to play the hero or villain of your design in monthly tournaments against other champions from around the world. Competitions include fights, races, and contests of skill and power in arenas ranging from dungeon labyrinths to crowded office buildings. Turns are gamemastered on a daily basis and exciting news reports are broadcast each week!

Please take a few minutes to read the rules below. Next week I'll start taking applications for the first game, and in two weeks, the first tournament will begin!

Games are free for all participants.

THE RULES:

I. General

    A. A new tournament will be announced every four weeks.

    B. Tournaments will be posted with the following information.

      1. The tournament title.

      2. The rulesystem being used (Champions, Ninja Hero, Fantasy Hero, etc.).

      3. Character build points (base plus disadvantages).

      4. Specific character requirements (usually rulesystem-specific).

      5. The maximum number of players allowed for the tournament.

      6. Deadlines for character submissions.

      7. Start date for the tournament.

      8. Any additional information.

II. Game Layout

    A. Each tournament will consist of a pre-generated map of varying terrain. Characters will be magically teleported to various spots. Maps could be underground labyrinths, skyscrapers, railroad cars, demonic temples, space stations, or even underwater cities. You will not be told in advance where you will be going; therefore, it would behoove you to build a well-rounded character.

    B. There will usually be extra creatures, hazards, and encounters in addition to the other characters in the tournament. One particularly nasty creature you'll run into is the TV News Reporter. Reporters will sometimes get in your way and pester you in the middle of combat. They will also report everything they see in the weekly news. You can attack or kill reporters if you like, but you may quickly develop a reputation. Likewise, heroes that save reporters will often get good press.

    C. If your character turns out to be totally incapable of handling the environment, the Sidekick Recruitment Agency might step in and give you a small gadget to help you along. In return, the SRA will proudly feature you in one of their future commercials.

III. Character Creation

    A. You may make any character you like within the points specified and within the rules of both the system being used and the rules given here. Be sure to include all of the following:

      1. Your character's limitations, characteristics, talents, skills, powers, and point totals.

      2. A physical description of your character. This description will be used when other characters see you. Please also state what sounds your character makes (electrical noises, animal sounds, or unusual accents when speaking), how impressive he or she looks (flame auras, hovering, and the like) and any other distinguishing features. Pictures are great if you can send them! Good quality pictures often end up in the news and bring lots of fame to your character!

      3. A cinematic description of your character's powers. Be sure to explain what form (lightning bolt, fireball, laser blast), color, accompanying battle scream ("THUNDER CLAW!!!"), and other features your powers have.

      4. A short (a paragraph or so) origin for your character. This does not need to be very long, but I would like to know how your character got his or her powers. Your character can come from virtually any world. For examples, refer to the sample characters in the Champions rulebook.

      5. A paragraph or so describing your character's general personality.

      6. A paragraph or so describing your character's general tactics.

      7. Your default action if attacked. See section V., Game Play. Defense is automatic in this game. You need to tell me how your character would defend if attacked. You can specify that you would abort an action to defend (please specify which defensive action you would use), or you could just rely on your DCV. Your character will use this action when attacked. Under most circumstances, you can't specify different defensive actions for different attacks unless you have a really good reason.

    B. Do not create illegal or questionable powers. The game master will err on the side of "I don't think so." in such cases. Read the rules. If the book has a stop sign or a magnifying glass next to it, be careful and be reasonable. No characteristics in ECs, no non-limiting limitations, etc. You know the drill.

    C. If there are more players than the number of spaces allowed in the game, preference will be given to:

      1. Characters that are the most legal.

      2. Characters with clearly defined powers. (Characters that are easy to understand.)

      3. Characters that are cinematically exciting, make a good story, and make sense.

    D. RESTRICTED CHARACTER DISADVANTAGES. The following character disadvantages are not allowed in this game:

      1. Dependence with greater than a turn before taking damage (segment, phase, and turn are ok).

      2. Dependent NPC.

      3. Hunted and Watched.

    D. RESTRICTED POWERS. The following powers are not allowed in this game:

      1. Extra-dimensional movement.

      2. Faster-than-light travel.

      3. Summon.

    E. RESTRICTED POWER ADVANTAGES. The following power advantages are not allowed in this game:

      1. Indirect.

    F. RESTRICTED POWER LIMITATIONS. The following power limitations are not allowed in this game:

      1. Independent.

IV. Registration

    To register, send a typed copy of your character to me at kirin@enteract.com by the deadline. Be sure to include the following:

      A. The title and deadline of the tournament.

      B. Your name.

      C. Your email address.

      D. Your character.

    You can submit your character in plain email, ASCII format, Microsoft Word, or Microsoft Excel as long as it's legible. You will be notified within 48 hours if your character is approved. You will also be notified if your character is not approved with reasons why. You may resubmit a character while time allows.

V. Game play

    A. Correspondence

      Each day, you will be sent an email message describing your character's surroundings and what your character sees, hears, or otherwise senses. You may then react to your surroundings by emailing me your instructions for what your character does.

        1. Your instructions are due by 10:00pm Central Standard Time each day, Monday through Friday.

        2. You will get a response by 3:00pm CST the following weekday (Monday following Friday).

        3. News reports will come out over the weekend.

      Promptness is checked by the timestamp on your email. If you are late, your instructions will not count for that day. This could result in your character standing around like a goof. Deadlines are necessary to keep the game moving along for everyone.

    B. Timing

      Each real-time day equals one segment (second) in the game. You will be able to act according to your speed as per the Champions rules. Thus, if you have a speed of 6, you can act every other day. You can also hold actions by saying so in your email. You cannot act out of turn unless you normally could do so, such as to abort an action. You can talk all you like, even on turns you don't move. However, since conversation between two characters would take place through the GM, you won't get a response until at least the second after you say something.

      An instruction would be something like "I fly toward the speeding car at top speed." or "I turn invisible and hold my action." You may also ask questions of the GM like "Does it look like she saw me?" or "What is the box made of?" or "I drank what?" The more complex or numerous the questions, the more limited your answers will be.

    C. Encounters

      If you encounter someone or something, you can act according to your speed. Two characters encountering one another each have a choice: attack or hope the other character isn't hostile. You can do anything you like, including talking, running away, attacking, and so forth. Actions are not based on DEX. If you can act, you had better act. You must make your decision each segment that you can. If both characters can act and one attacks but the other doesn't, only one attack goes off and the defender will defend based upon his or her default defensive action. If both characters can and do attack, then the attacks go in order of DEX. In this case, both attacks do go off (or try to); default defensive actions do not apply. Once an action is declared for a segment, it cannot be changed.

VI. Goals and Winning

    The tournament ends when someone wins (completes the goal) or two months of real time pass.

    Your character will become aware of the goal of the scenario at the very beginning. It may be to retrieve something, be first through a maze of deathtraps, or simply to defeat all the others in the game. The winner is either the first person to do this, or, in the case of a tie, the person that does it best. In a slugfest, a desolid character may stay around a long time, but the winner will be the one that defeats the most opponents. Goals are structured so that both good and evil characters can accomplish them, though disads like "Pacifist" may be a bit too confining in this game. Mind you, however, that good and evil characters will accomplish their goals in different ways...

    Winning and non-winning characters alike will be hounded by reporters. You can reveal the secrets of your success if you want to, or you can be mysterious. Either way, you'll be the center of attention. Smile for the camera!

    Experience points are awarded at the end of each scenario. These are the only experience points that can be used in future games here and constitute the only way in which you could have a character with more than the given build points. Return characters are encouraged! However, though powers can be added to, they cannot be changed without a full news report on your radiation accident.
