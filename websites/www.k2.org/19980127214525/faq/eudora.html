Setting up Eudora

  1. If you don't have it already, download Eudora from www.eudora.com.
  2. Save it to its own folder or directory on your hard drive. You can call this new folder or directory "Eudora".
  3. Run the executable program you downloaded by double clicking on the file in File Manager (for Win 3.X) or by double clicking the icon in the Eudora folder that you created earlier (for Win 95).
  4. Double click the Eudora Mail icon.
  5. You will see the following screen:

  6. Go to Tools/Options. You will see the following screen:

  7. Fill in your POP account and Real Name as shown above. The POP account must be in the form of username@pop.enteract.com.
  8. Click Personal Information in the menu on the left side of your Options window.

  9. The POP account and Real name should already be there from the information you entered in the previous screen. You will need to enter your Return Address, which is the same as your email address. It will take the form of username@enteract.com.
  10. The Dialup username is your username.
  11. Click Hosts.

  12. The POP account will already be there. Fill in the SMTP field with smtp.enteract.com. Leave Ph and Finger blank.
  13. Click Checking Mail.

  14. Make sure that Leave mail on server is not checked. The other options are up to you.
  15. Click OK.

Checking Mail

Back in your main screen for Eudora, click File/Check Mail.

If you are properly connected to the internet, and all of your settings are configured as described above, you will now be able to get your mail off of EnterAct's server to read it.
Simply double click the message you wish to read and it will appear in a separate window.

Composing and Sending a Message

Click Message/New Message.

You will see another window which prompts you for who you are sending your message to and the subject of your message. In addition, there is space to compose your message.

When you've finished typing your message, simply click the Send button and your message will be sent immediately.

General Protection Fault in Eudora

On rare occasions, you may get a general protection fault error when running Eudora. Qualcomm, the makers of Eudora, say that this happens after receipt of a file with a "bad date". The solution is to delete the elmoff.dat file (located in the Eudora subdirectory). The only possible negative consequence to this is that if you have been leaving mail on the server, the next time Eudora connects it will download all of that mail again.
