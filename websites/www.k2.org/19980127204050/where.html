** OUR MODEM LINES **

We now offer over 100 dialup access points through the Chicagoland area. Our lines are supplied by three separate phone companies: Worldcom, TCG and Focal. The differences between each are explained below:

FOCAL: Our newest, biggest, and most stable modem pool, it supports analog modems up to 33.6Kbps and also the K56 Flex standard. The Focal dialups should be the number one choice for all dialup users (except those with ISDN equipment) for two reasons: 1) Connections to it are, in general, more reliable; and 2) Focal Communications guarantees that if you use a number designated local by the Access Locator below that they will refund any erroneous billings made by Ameritech (for details about this, read the Focal Guarantee Letter).

TCG: At one point these lines supported both analog modems up to 33.6Kbps and ISDN, but since the addition of the ISDN-ONLY modem pool ISDN is no longer supported here. The TCG lines should be used as an alternative if you can't find a suitable local FOCAL dialup, or if you're experiencing troubles with FOCAL lines.

WORLDCOM: These were our original phone lines and only support speeds up to 33.6Kbps. They should be used as a last resort if you're experiencing trouble with both the FOCAL and TCG modem pools.

ISDN-ONLY: These lines only support ISDN connects (at 64 or 128Kbps) and should be used by customers with ISDN terminal adapters or routers. **AMERITECH BILLING ISSUES**

Although the locations for the numbers listed below are to our knowledge accurate, to be safe we recommend calling Ameritech to verify your charges. You can make certain that a number is a Band A call for you by calling Ameritech at 800-244-4444. Press 0 for an agent and ask them to price a call for you. It should be noted that there are special exceptions (i.e. a band B call may be billed as band A) that these agents are unable to tell you about.

Another great source to consult is Randy Smith's Internet Service Provider locator. If you provide your area code and local exchange, it will return a list of EnterAct numbers (along with other ISPs) which are probably band A, band B or band C for your location. (It's still best to verify these results with Ameritech, if you are unsure). We are currently working on creating our very own number finder, stay tuned for more updates.

A few of our customers have reported being erroneously billed long distance type charges (per minute) for numbers which are 8 miles or less to them and are therefore local as regulated by an Illinois state tariff. We have worked with the local telephone companies to create two solutions to this problem for you.

Focal has provided us with this letter to assure our customers that they are working with Ameritech to correct mistaken information. They've really put their money where their mouth is!

We are also working with TCG to help correct the mistaken information that Ameritech operators sometimes give out. If you find that you are having any billing problems from Ameritech with a TCG line, please take a few moments to fill out our Ameritech Billing Problem Incident Report Form.
