personal stuff, in brief

i grew up in nyc, right in manhattan. people are always amazed when i tell them, but, hey, there are over one and a half million residents in the borough alone - someone had to have grown up there, right? well, i get a similar reaction when people tell me they grew up in alaska.

in 1990, i moved to the left coast, in search of good coffee, microbrews, and grunge rock. where else, but seattle. little believed fact: it does not rain a lot in seattle. actually, the total annual annual rainfall in seattle is just under nyc's. note to californians: ready, set, go! just be prepared for the endless cloudy days...

so, what brings me to the midwest? grad school, of course. currently, i am a second year mba student at the indiana university graduate school of business. my area of focus is marketing, which, contrary to popular belief, is a lot more than techniques to sell products to people who who don't need them in the first place. bloomington is a neat college town. its main claim to fame these days is the home and birthplace of john mellencamp. rumored sightings of mr. mellencamp are as commonplace here as those of the king. by the way, if you care to learn more about my professional background, take a look at my resumé, especially if you are a prospective employer. those graduate student loans are building up very quickly!

my interests? travel - near or far, backpacking or first class. i've been to 27 states and 21 countries. some of the places i would like to visit soon: costa rica, turkey, eastern europe, vietnam, and the south pacific. of course, there are many more places i would like to see. i also like to ride stomach churning, head spinning, gravity defying roller coasters! my favorite woodie would have to be the cyclone in coney island. one of the trips i have to make while i am still in the midwest is to cedar point, which appears to be mecca for coaster enthusiasts. i am ashamed that i have yet to enter its gates. while not busy riding coasters, you will catch me looking through zagats for the best places to dine. i love good food, whether it comes from the fanciest kitchen in town or the burrito shack down the street.

what's that picture?

for those of you who don't know, it is a 1964 drawing of the new york city subway train. despite the horror stories you've heard, it is a really great system that will get you just about anywhere you may want to go in the city. the picture is also linked to the wonderful nyc subway site, which provides lots of information and pictures for train buffs out there.
