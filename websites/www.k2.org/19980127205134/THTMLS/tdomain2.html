Domain Names




    Every site on the internet has an address. This address is actually designated by one of those numbers you've probably seen, that look something like: 206.54.252.1. These are routing numbers, and tell everyone on the internet how to find a specific site.

    People aren't very good at remembering strings of numbers like this, so the internet adopted a Domain Naming System. Our domain is named "enteract," and because we're a commercial site, "com" is appended to our name. In addition, prefixing our domain with "www" or "ftp" tells our server where to start looking within our domain. And further appending the name with a country, such as "uk," lets us know the site is in the United Kingdom. Note that the United States is the default, and therefore ".us" is not added to sites in the US.

    Thus, www.enteract.com.uk would mean the World Wide Web Directory in Eneract's commercial domain in the United Kingdom. If you're interested in having a domain name for your business, enter it in the form below, and we'll instantly check to see if someone's using that domain or not.
