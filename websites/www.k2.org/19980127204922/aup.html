EnterAct's Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlines some limitations on the use of your EnterAct account. Our goal in establishing this AUP is to protect both our users and the larger Internet community from the consequences of irresponsible use of EnterAct accounts and resources. EnterAct's AUP chiefly prohibits acts that are illegal, that violate generally accepted standards for online behavior, or that misuse EnterAct resources to the detriment of other users.

Violations of the AUP can result in account termination. We take violations of our AUP seriously, and as a matter of course investigate reports of AUP violations. An account may be temporarily suspended during this investigation period.

Broken down by category, actions we specifically prohibit with EnterAct accounts include:

General

  • Participating in software piracy.
  • Attempting to gain unauthorized access to EnterAct systems or other systems on the Internet, or using these systems in excess of one's authority.
  • Interfering with or denying service to any EnterAct user or host, or any other user or host on the Internet.
  • Accessing another's network sessions or files without their authorization.
  • Impersonating any person using forged headers or other identifying information.

Email

  • Sending unsolicited commercial email.
    • See Fight Spam on the Internet! for a reasoned explanation of why this is bad and acceptable alternatives for advertising your business on the Internet.
  • Sending large volumes of unsolicited email (including chain letters).
    • Is that virus warning, cookie recipe or get-well card drive a hoax? Think before you forward!
  • Collecting responses from unsolicited commercial email.
  • Sending threatening or harassing email (where harassment is defined in terms of language, frequency, or size of messages).

Usenet

  • Excessive cross-posting of the same or similar messages to many Usenet newsgroups.
  • Repeated posting of off-topic articles to Usenet groups.
    • Please see Advertising on Usenet: How To Do It, How Not To Do It.
  • Posting binaries to non-binary Usenet groups outside of alt.binaries.*.
  • Cancelling Usenet posts other than your own.
  • "Feed sucking" from the EnterAct news server.

    For general guidelines on Usenet etiquette, please see Help with Usenet, at the Usenet Info Center.

IRC

  • Using IRC bots without prior approval by EnterAct staff, or commiting any violation of any accepted policies of IRC servers (including the use of IRC bots on servers that do not allow them).

Shell

  • Using server space or bandwidth in excess of what is allowed by your account type (eg, a SoloAct account is alotted 10 Mb of server space and 250 Mb of bandwidth per month) without paying for the additional resources used.
  • Excessive use of CPU resources.

Modem usage

  • Keeping a dial-up account connected to EnterAct modems when the connection is not being actively used, or using any automatic method to avoid "time out" disconnection.
  • Using a dial-up account to make more than one simultaneous connection.
  • Using a dial-up account to run services on your side of the connection, such as a web, Quake, or FTP server.
