A Glossary of Terms

A little help to de-mystify the Internet

Web Site
This is your page or pages on the World Wide Web. This is where most users will first locate your company's presence.

FTP Site
Many companies wish to offer data files or program files that their customers can download and/or execute. An FTP Site gives the company the ability to offer these files.

Auto-responder
A good way to make an impression on customers is to send them a quick response to their requests for information. An auto-responder is an automatic response sent by e-mail to anyone who sends a message to, for instance, 'info@yourcompany.com'. All customer queries are then sent on to individuals at your company for follow-up.

Mailing List
A mailing list is an easy way to discuss a topic with a group of people through e-mail.

Bandwidth
Bandwidth is a measure of how much data is being transmitted to and from a site.
See our Bandwidth Page for more detailed information.

Domain Name
When you see something like www.enteract.com, enteract.com is the domain name. It's an easy way for people to find and refer to a site.

CGI Scripts
A CGI script allows you to execute a program, then return the results to the browser. For instance, many financial sites allow you to enter stock symbols to look up. These sites are using CGI scripts to lookup the data in a database and return the data in HTML form to you.
