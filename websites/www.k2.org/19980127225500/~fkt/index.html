Ancient Cultural Tour Of Hunza and Swat Valleys of Pakistan

Highlights

Rawalpindi, Islamabad, Taxila, Peshawar, Khyber Pass, Swat Valley, Saidu Shareef, Karakoram Highway, Chilas, Gilgit, Hunza Valley, and Khunjerab Pass

Day 1:
Rawalpindi/Islamabad

The twin cities of Rawalpindi and Islamabad are situated at the edge of Potohar Plateau and are
25 minutes apart. Explore 350 year old city of Rawalpindi in the morning and visit Islamabad in
the afternoon. This gleaming new capital of Pakistan is a superb example of modern architecture.

Day 2:
Taxila

Located 35 miles from Rawalpindi, Taxila, was once the hub of oriental culture. Originating in the
6th century B.C it was subjugated by Alexander the Great in 327 B.C and passed on to other rulers
until it reached the hands of Ashoka the Great who molded this city into a center of philosophy, art
and education. Taxila remained a prosperous city until it was destroyed by the Huns. Today Taxila is
an archaeologist's paradise. Visit Taxila museum and archaeological sites.

Day 3 and 4:
Peshawar and Khyber Pass

Peshawar is a frontier town the heart of the old city has changed little in the last 100 years. Visit Kissa Khani
bazaar, jeweler's bazaar, Mohabat Khan mosque and, Peshawar museum.

Day 5:
Swat Valley
Located 247 KM in the North East of Islamabad,
this incredible land of pristine beauty and grandeur
is the center of great civilizations. The Greeks fought
for its beauty, the Buddhists inhabited it for its peace,
the Moguls envied its green valleys and fast flowing
rivers, and today it is an eternal abode of Pathans,
Kohistanis, and Gujars- the three prominent tribes of
Swat. Visit the museum and local bazaars.


Day 6:
Bahrain Excursion

At 1400 meters Bahrain is another tourist attraction of Swat located at the picturesque
junction of the roaring Swat River. The one lane bazaar offers colorful embroideries, handicrafts and
local antiques.

Day 7:
Chilas:

A major town on the upper course of the Indus and a gateway to Gilgit, Hunza and Kaghan Valleys. Set in the middle of an arid
valley this small town lives from the irrigation water carefully channeled to it from the mountains and rivers. See petroglyphs created by the
silk traders along the Indus river more than 2000 years ago. Excursionto Fairy Meadows.

Day 8:
Gilgit

This city is situated in a wide irrigated bowl, 1500 meters
above sea level at the Eastern end of Gilgit Valley. Gilgit
is thebirthplace of Polo.The polo tournament held in the
1st two weeks of November attractsa large number of spectators.
Gilgit was once the furthermost outpostof the British Raj. Visit
silk market, Polo grounds, Chinar Bagh, and Kargah Nulla.

 

Day 9 and 10:
Karimabad

Capital of Hunza, Karimabad is nestled under the shadows of Rakaposhi (7788M). Visit ancient forts, local bazaars, jeep excursion to Nagar glacier.

Day 11:
Khunjerab Pass

The Khunjerab Pass is the continental watershed, on the Pakistan side flowing down to the
Indian Ocean and on the Chinese side being swallowed by the Taklamakan Desert in the
Tarim Basin. Traveling along the Karakoram highway we pass through the Khunjerab National Park,
the natural habitat of the endangered Marco Polo Sheep and Snow Leopard.

Day 13 and 14:
Drive back to Islamabad via Gilgit and Chilas
