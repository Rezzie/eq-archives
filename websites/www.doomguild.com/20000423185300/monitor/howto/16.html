2 . "Facion with dwarven guards"

As a level 12 gnomish necro, I recently discovered to my surprise that I raised my faction enough with Stormguard that the dwarven guards now all glower at me dubiously. This makes life in BB much more bearable.

I spent levels 6-8 in Crushbone killing orcs, and a few hours in there just killing green cents for faction raises from level 11-12.

The elven guards in Greater Faydark still KOS me (they scowl, ready to attack), and the merchants in BB still KOS me - this means that you can enter Kaladim, so long as you stay near the zone and away from the shops (fine if you're just getting bound there). Also be careful around the two houses area, as the merchants will kill you.

Not sure what you have to kill to raise faction with the merchants. Goblins, I'd imagine.

Good luck!

8 . "Faction and Banks"
 Posted by Zalamar on Jul-26-99 at 01:26 PM (EST)
When I came over to Freeport (12th) I had no problem with the Militia (East and West Freeport) or the Sirs (North Freeport - Bank)

BTW the orcs DO NOT raise faction with the Militia. I have killed many of them and had a Paladin friend of mine that became KOS in the city and the orcs are no help. They help with the Commons Residents though some of whom used to KOS me and now they just won't sell to me.

You can take the belts to the Militia and get faction, but it hurts your faction with the Sirs and you can take them to the Sirs, but same deal. The only way I have found to get faction with the Militia is either the HighHold Pass Orc Scalp Quest or the Rogue Dwarven Ale Quest.

I was able to go to Neriak without a problem and deal with Vendors and the Bank. You can also go to the Necro Guild but cast Gather Shadows to get past the ghouls. You can buy spells there, but can't get trained.

There is a Necro Guild under Freeport though that will sell and train you so that saved me the trip back to Akanon.
