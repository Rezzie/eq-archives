1 . "Kiting"
 Posted by Dumuzi on Jul-11-99 at 08:48 PM (EST)
Kiting is pretty simple. Cast a spell from your darkness line to slow the creature down and stack as many DOTs as you have on it. Then all you do is run with the slowed creature following you. If the DOTs wear off then just recast them. That's pretty much it.

I'm too impatient to kite so I usually cast a darkness spell, sick my pet on it, stack my DOTs, and then fear it. Then I just follow the creature while my pet hits it in the back. If the fear wears off I cast Siphon Life if its almost dead or recast fear if its not.

Dumuzi Eb'Sabah'Nur

 
2 . "Kiting, kiting, kiting and spam"

Ok, regardless of the title, this is just about kiting. What above poster said will work, assuming two things. A) fear WILL work and B) Said monster isn't too strong (maybe yellow? Personally I've taken reds, and sometimes the same degree of red but different TYPE of monster will kill me instantly). For true kiting, you need something big and nasty, at least compared to you, a LOW level darkness spell (lower darkness, lower resist, just trust me on this), some nasty DoTs, so get to 12 at least, heat blood does 170 overall when I tested it on myself (I'm a little crazy, ok?) but you'll have to retain a pretty good amount of distance, enough you can recast your darkness (once again, lower darkness, lower casting time, don't you just love how this works?) with Mr. Ugly running at you at full speed. Then just run in circles (big circles though. Some prefer ovals. I've even seen some odd gnomes who prefer squares :) it really doesn't matter) until said Mr. Ugly dies an agonizing death due to heated blood, faulty heart, immersion in darkness, or if you're really sick, poison bolt. It's nice to summon a pet to keep the thing distracted while you make your first stack, but you don't really NEED to. You should pick something that once a full stack of DoTs nails it, it should be wounded enough it slows a bit, which'll make the next stack so much easier. Just don't try something too much higher than you, they have this nasty habit of resisting. These are just my opinions and personal experiences, if any of you other necromancers want to call me ignorant, go ahead just as long as you point out why.

3 . "Kiting and Snailing"
 Posted by Maiya - The Rathe on Jul-13-99 at 00:34 AM (EST)
Technically... using a spell that slows down the monster is called snailing, while using a spell that speeds up your movement is called kiting.

Kiting gets its name from the concept of trying to fly a kite on a day when there is no wind... you run around as fast as you can trying to get the kite to fly.

Snailing gets its name from the concept that you are slowing down the creature, making it a snail.

Kiting generally has fewer risks, the SoW or JBoots effect doesn't randomly drop like a slow down spell (darkness or snare) might.

Another method of killing monsters is root and shoot, which is primarily a wizard mode of combat. Root and shoot is perhaps the most dangerous, because root might wear off at any time and the creature is coming at you full speed.

Shaman and Bards usually kite. Necros and Rangers usually snail (rangers use arrows). Wizards and Clerics root and shoot. Druids snail and kite at the same time (snare and SoW). Of course druids have weak DoTs, weak DD, and low mana.

Of course, a level 34+ necro with Jboots is the only one who can do all three.

Snailing is most effective when used in conjunction with the Fear spell. Once you've got it slowed down and DoTed, you drop fear on the poor thing and watch it crawl away from you.
