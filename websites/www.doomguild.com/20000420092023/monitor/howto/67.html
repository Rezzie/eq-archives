Also watch out for the Academy, they hate Necros.

8 . "Conning while Invisible makes sense"
 Posted by Therros on Tarew Marr on Jul-14-99 at 08:50 AM (EST)
Here is how conning while invisible, works, and it makes sense.

IF you click on a creature, and con it, you get the true con. Threatenly and Scowls mean it will attack. Glares Dubiously and above mean you are okay. Apprehensively and above mean they will do business with you.

Now comes invis. If you are invis, click on a creature, and con it. You will always get 'indifferent'. How do you know their true feelings when they can't see you?

(Of course, how the hell do they know we're a
necromancer just by looking at us, but that's a
different story.)

One other little known fact is that if you've
clicked on a creature... then go invis... you
can at time still get a true con. Bug, but a
minor one.

So, to safely get a true con of people, you
go invis, go up, click on them, run away, then
de-invis and con. Do remember to repeat this
for multiple guards, as in towns like Freeport
and others, guards can have different factions,
and thus different attitudes towards you.

Merchants and wandering NPC's need to be checked
as well, as they've all trained in the Ninja
Death Monk Merchant Training House, hidden
somewhere in Paineel, and all have the ability
to hit for 90 points of damage barehanded.
Beware!

for all merchants is, and where I can sign up
for training... let me know.
