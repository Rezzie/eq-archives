3 . "my thoughts on pets"
 Posted by Mortican Soulblighter on Jul-31-99 at 04:37 AM (EST)
It is my personal belief what our pets are #1 at melee, out of ALL pets. The necro pet is geered like warrior, the mage pet is a hybrid, the shaman's pet is generaly much lower level than a necro pet, the enchanter's pet seem to have 80% hp of a necro pet.

In combat, our pet out performs any other pet of the same level summoning spell. The higher level summon spell you use, the more you will notice. Double attack + duel wield actually come into play often, expecially when you cast augment dead on your pet. My pathetic lvl 34 pet takes out an NPC lvl 39 pet easily, with dagger and augment dead. 3-4 attacks a round is common, and witht he dagger, he gets up to 12 attacks in 6 secs almost regularly.

If a necro pet and a mage pet were to fight in a duel, armed and buffed, the only reason the mage earth pet will win, is because of the damage shield the mage can cast, that's it.

The shaman pet looks powerful when people see it hit for 53. The problem is, that's a lvl 49 pet, and it has close to 1/2 the hp of an mage earth elem which also hits for 53 + casts. When someone gets a lvl 49 necro pet, we'll see that it has a much higher max damage, and if the same is true for other levels, it has 70-80% the hp of an earth elemental of similar level, plus much higher melee skills.

4 . "Mage pets..."
 Posted by Etadanik on Jul-31-99 at 05:06 PM (EST)
Obviously earth elementals are the best pet tank in the game, but the magicians get three more pet types, remember?

Anybody know what a level 49 fire elemental do in terms of damage, what about its damage shield?
Anybody know how fast a water elemental heals at higher levels?
Anybody know how good an air elemental evades at higher levels?

The lack of variety is what makes us the second best pet class, even though theoritically our pets are just as good as theirs. The problem is, attacking more and doing more damage isn't necessarily good, because the skeleton takes more damage from almost everything level 10 and up that casts spells(not shaman spells), due to the fact they have damage shields casted upon them. Imagine a skeleton pet that attacks 4 times a round, and that the damage shield at higher levels do close to 30-40 damage a hit... So that would equal to the skeleton taking an equal number of damage than it deals... Plus the enemy gets to attack... Something to think about.
