This whole mana argument I find interesting.

The more we explore, the more +wis/+int items we will find.

However, given no +wis/+int, Necros have a lot more mana at their disposal. The more we find, the less we have above them (in relation to Shamans)..

On Veeshan we have Clerics at the 200 Wis range (and there are Shamans around the same point), whereas the biggest Wiz has around 220 Int.

My point? The mana difference is getting smaller, and the mana equations seem to be the same. What does that ultimately mean? In the long run we will all have similar mana.

So, negating the mana aspect of the argument, we are then left with other factors. Shamans are incredibly powerful. I have heard (and still not managed to verify) that their 49 DOT's surpass ours (being purposefully vague here - need to test more) in damage over time. Their 49th pet is purchasable and doesn't require regeants, and is 4 levels below ours (i.e. similar to our 44th pet, which as of now, no Necro on any server has managed to create, from all the ones I have talked to - over 20). Their DD is similar to ours (around 450 at 50th). Their Mana cost for their spells is similar to ours...

10 . "Good point"
 Posted by Allaxul Darksoul on Jul-26-99 at 12:22 PM (EST)
Our spells are not as effective individually. It's the combos where our true power lies. I've seen many Necromancers who want to be able to memorize one spell - a DD and sit there and click it a bunch during battle. If I wanted to do that I'd have played a wizard. Multiple effects working together to be better than the whole is where the power really lies.

Take Vampiric Curse and Call of Bones for instance - that combo costs 149 mana. Over 30 seconds or so it will give you more mana than that back and hurt the enemy. While that is going you can be transferring life to a tank (lvl 44 DoT transfer), have a DoT(s) on other enemy(ies), and be doing damage in the form of SoP, IB, or the undead DD spells if thats what you're fighting. Yes it's complicated but the total effect of all that on a fight is enormous. Then add in fear and now fear and forget for some crowd control as well and damnit - our class can rock with the best of them - it's not as simple that's all.
