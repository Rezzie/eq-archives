1 . "AC & EQ"
 Posted by Raskin on Sep-15-99 at 02:27 PM (EST)
I'll be brief since I've talked about AC before ...

I've played a lot more AC than EQ recently, generally cause its been more fun, even in its beta state.

AC has great looking outdoor areas, with kinda ugly player models.

But the two main reasons I like is AC is because there is no item camping necessary and it is skill based vs. class based.

The thing that concerns me though is the lack of depth in the game. Most everything else can be fixed with a patch. Examples would be:
No difference in the race choices except one different skill each.
Towns, no guards? Why haven't the monsters overrun them?
No faction of any kind, all merchants love everyone.
Quests are even less useful then EQ quests.
Too many rabbits.


I'm sure others testing can come up with more examples of things that the game seriously lacks. As it stands now, its fun like Diablo, but I don't see anything that would make me want to play more than 2 or 3 months, but that could change if they add a lot to the game, but there isn't much time, they plan on having it in stores by Nov. 15, which doesn't leave much time to fix bugs, let alone add new features.

2 . "The difference between AC and EQ..."
 Posted by Inraka on Sep-15-99 at 06:13 PM (EST)
is that there really isn't much difference. In AC you are in Dereth and in EQ you are in Norrath. In both games you run around and kill monster while attempting to earn XP to become more powerful and collect better items. At least in EQ it is easy to find out where your friends are and chat with them. I am not sure they could have made ACs chat interface more burdensome if their goal from the begining was to make it clunky. Not saying AC is a bad game, just saying it really doesn't seem to offer much over EQ. And if you are starting to feel boredom creep into from EQs repetiveness, it will get you that much quicker in AC.
 
3 . "adding to the debate a little"
 Posted by Aorkoleth on Sep-16-99 at 10:39 PM (EST)
I think the main problem with the game is we are not forced into PvP (except for PvP servers that don't let you loot enough). Once you get level 50 you are done. PvP adds a huge amount of danger and excitement to this type of game. UO is a great example. Yes it had its problems and I won't defend them but when we maxed out our skills and grew tired of killing the big monsters, there was always PvP. Hunting PKers was extremely fun. And if we lost, well I could go to my little shack, suit up, reg up, and go out again. I wish my guild hadn't sold off most of their accounts on UO. I find that game so much less frustrating and boring.

If you think AC will be more of the same, maybe its time to check out Shadowbane. I doubt anyone will camp one spot too long in THAT game because camped targets are easier for PKers to kill than moving ones hehe.

Gonna be killing my EQ account soon...
