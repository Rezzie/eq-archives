Editor's Note

Greetings..

Patch day, what an extensive patch indeed. Sky is far and away one of the most intruguing zones in the game. However a very alarming reality exists in the PoS: Necro Pets are useless. Read below for more info..

In other Necro news, the FD+Charm nerf has finally been implemented, and although it affects on a small portion of the Necro Community and only a few spawns in the game, it received both praise and boo's all the same.

Also the Weapon Delay nerf has made its biggest impact on the game, and is the largest Necro nerf in recent history. Approximately 8 new spells are due to be implemented to help 'compensate' for the nerf. Preliminary details below..

~Sluggo, Editor

Necro Pets in PoS

Thanks to Kefka of Veeshan for this info:

Last night whilst my guildmates and I zoned into the Plane of Sky for our first time, we noticed none other than Aradune Mithara (aka, Brad McQuaid) himself waiting to greet us.
I had the occasion to ask Mr. McQuaid about the logic behind the pet nerf: i.e., EVERY type of pet spell but necro pets (be it enchanter, magi air pets, shaman, mistwalker and what have you) summon a normal pet. Magi air pets even summon an enhanced pet with new, updated graphics. Our level 49 spell summons a level 1, green-conning, tiny skeleton. Our pet spells below 49 yield a haunting yellow message: "This spell does not work in this plane."
This is, in my opinion, a slap to the face of every necromancer in Norrath. After getting nerfed this patch and every other, this sort of treatment will NOT stand.
oh, and Aradune's comments on the subject, and I quote: "Hmm. I don't know how pets are supposed to work here. I'll ask about it."


~Sluggo

Patch Lowdown

The Necromonitor has learned the exact effects of several spells reported to be on the Test Server. These spells are expected to go live on the regular servers on Thursday, 3 February 2000.

Name: Leach.
Level: 12.
Mana: 72.
Class: Alteration.
Type: Transfer Anothers Life to Yours Spell.
Actual Effects: Target: Lose Hitpoints (72 over 45 seconds).
Self: Gain targets Hitpoints (72 over 45 seconds).

Analysis: Looks to be a newbie version of Vampiric Curse/Bond of Death. Might help lower levels but impact on existing Necros is nil.

Name: Scent of Dusk.
Level: 12.
Mana: 50.
Class: Alteration.
Type: Single Target Debuffing Spell.
Actual Effects: Target: Decrease Fire Resistance (7-9).
Target: Decrease Poison Resistance (7-9).
Target: Decrease Disease Resistance (7-9).
Stacks with Malise line and Tashan line.

Analysis: Weak Debuff that most likely wont ever be worth the rather high mana cost.

Name: Shadow Compact.
Level: 20.
Mana: 10.
Class: Alteration.
Type: General Use Spell.
Actual Effects: Self: Lose 80 hp over 24 seconds..
Target: Gain 80 hp over 24 seconds..

Analysis: Lower Version of Pact of Shadow, might be useful for low levels but again impact looks to be nil.

Name: Rapacious Subversion.
Level: 24.
Mana: 200.
Class: Alteration.
Type: General Use Spell.
Actual Effects: Self: Lose 200 Mana.
Target: Receive 60 Mana.

Analyis: First verson of the Necro-->Manastone changes.

Name: Scent of Shadow.
Level: 24.
Mana: 100.
Class: Alteration.
Type: Single Target Debuffing Spell.
Actual Effects: Target: Decrease Fire Resistance (14 - 18).
Target: Decrease Poison Resistance (14 - 18).
Target: Decrease Disease Resistance (14 - 18).

Analysis: Mana cost is exponentially higher for exponentially less effect. Nonetheless might prove a handy boost to Malise spells.

Name: Scent of Darkness.
Level: 39.
Mana: 150.
Class: Alteration.
Type: Single Target Debuffing Spell.
Actual Effects: Target: Decrease Fire Resistance (24 - 27).
Target: Decrease Poison Resistance (24 - 27).
Target: Decrease Disease Resistance (24 - 27).

Analysis: At this point if the resist rate on this line is not terribly bad, it may find its way into use as the effects should be much more noticeable at this point.

Name: Asystole.
Level: 44.
Mana: 210.
Class: Alteration.
Type: Unknown spell type.

Spell Analysis

Shadow Vortex

Shadow Vortex is a unique spell in that while it does drain, it drains AC from a target and transfers it to you. The resulting AC boost lasts for several minutes, meaning it may actually find use in your lineup. Numbers are not exact, but it appears SV drains roughly 30AC from your target and transfers 20AC to you. Exact numbers to follow soon..
This spell does not require research to be made.

Surge of Enfeeblement

Surge of Enfeeblement is a Level 34 research only spell. It is a Pointblank Area Effect spell that lowers the target(s) strength by 30. It is unclear if lowering STR actually affects a mob in any tangible way, so whether this spell will actually ever get used is improbable.

~Sluggo
