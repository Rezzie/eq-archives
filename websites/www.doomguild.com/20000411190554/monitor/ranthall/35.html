4 . "How strong is a player character?"
 Posted by Malbusia on Jul-07-99 at 04:18 PM (EST)
If I were designing how strong the necro PC should be, I would just analyze how well a moderately well-played character would do. Hit the monster with the Clinging Darkness-line, /pet kill, and then more DOT's, perhaps jumping in for a little damage and then blasting with a Lifetap series.

Then set the strength/HP of the pet to be adequate to give a reasonable chance of success vs. that monster as compared to other PC types. Notice the wide range of variables: Pet damage, pet hp, spell damage, DOT time (I understand poison DOT's were halved in time but doubled in damage rate -- the same overall damage but effectively doubling their power since you can just cast it again.)

The best thing to do is to just recast your pet until you get the topped-out one you can. I am currently level 12, and with a top pet (hits for 18) I absolutely cleaned house in the easier part of Crushbone, including the Orc Trainer's hill.

Carry a load of bone chips, find a dark spot (zone entrance tunnels are great) and give it four or five times. Yes, you may have to go out and attack simple stuff to see what your pet hits for, but when you get the maxed out one, life is good. I never keep around a non-maxed one unless I'm in a group (and can't get to a reasonable dark spot) or in a panic. You should always be able to get at least max - 1 level pet after a few casts, even in broad daylight.
