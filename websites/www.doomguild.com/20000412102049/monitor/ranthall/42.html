"Verant Coders are Incompetantly Managed"
 Posted by Ichoran on Jun-30-99 at 03:30 PM (EST)
I hate saying bad things about talented people, but I've given them plenty of time to get their act together.

Verant's ability to create quality updates sucks.

It's inexcusable.

Almost every patch, something that is announced doesn't work, or partly works, or isn't actually implemented, or something else. Someone in the dev team seriously needs to go back and look at the idea of "testing" again.

Amateur volunteer coders for MUDs--whose code runs 100,000 lines or more--can put out bug-free updates time after time after time. There is no excuse for professional programmers to be unable to do the same. Isn't this what they have the test server for, not to mention their own private machines to try things out on?

That said, I can understand them not getting to certain things. Some apparently simple problems have very complex causes and solutions, and you want to have time to do it right. But for heaven's sake, if they're NOT going to do it right (and they frequently aren't so far), at least they could try fixing everything at once!

Finally, I just want to point out that the problem is unlikely to be with any individual coder. They have to have plenty of top-notch people working there. Whoever manages the coders just needs to structure things so that you get out a tested product and not what one programmer figured, at 3am, ought to do the trick.

(Sloppiness is rampant somewhere. Remember the initial parser for strings that ate / and : and a bunch of other symbols even when they were in the middle of text, for no reason? A sophomore CS major would be embarassed to show someone code that did that, much less have it appear in an international product.)

--Ichoran

(1) Eat all whitespace at the beginning of the string (simple while loop)
(2) Parse when on first nonwhitespace character (simple if-statement).
(3) Don't parse on the remainder of the string (comment out one line of code or move it to if-statement above.)
