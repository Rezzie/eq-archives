"Multi-effect spells(ie. the darkness line)"
 Posted by Allicia on Jul-10-99 at 00:07 AM (EST)
>All DOT's with a secondary component get this - Enchanter's Choke,
Druids Immolate, etc. 35% resist by a creature white to you.
----

>With the DOT being the Necromancers main weapon in his/her arsenal, the Darkness line of spells' resistances is unacceptably high. Blue con enemies consistently resist the spell, either forcing the caster to attempt to recast and waste valuable mana, or to change his strategy in the midst of battle, often with dire consequences.
-----

One alternative to the above would be to ask that the spell not be any less resistable, but rather that the effects are resisted seperately.

So, while the slowness part of the darkness spell might be resisted, the damage might continue. Or while the damage could be resisted, the darkness might hit.

The logic behind having the entire spell being resisted if one small effect of the spell is saved against is inherently flawed.

It takes any spell with a minor side effect(say, reduce ac a little, or lower dex) and turns it into a small brown lump of cow left-overs.

I'm sure Enchanters would agree.

Why should monsters(and players) get multiple resistance rolls for the same spell?

As the darkness line stands now, it'd be better if the slowness aspect of the spell was turned off completely. At least it'd be worth using in battle then.
