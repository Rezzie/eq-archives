LoY Cartography System
Saturday, February 15th

Friday brought the announcement of the new cartography system that will be going in with the Legacy of Ykesha expansion. I haven't got the details yet, but hopefully we'll be able to transfer the maps people make around. Doing the maps still requires you to run along the walls and collect points to draw the map out. Needless to say, I've been doing this for years now, and have a program that does the same thing with the log files, and it takes hours to make a good map, so I'll guarantee few of you will be making a hobby of this new tool. However, it does add the neat feature of giving you an arrow to tell you which direction you're going, and putting your own markers on the maps.

Anyway, I'm going to look into hosting the files if they are able to be transferred around between people (I imagine they have to be somehow if you have local data), as well as possibly coming up with a converter for many of the map parses I already have collected for people to download. There will also be the GUI XML Creator for people to use if they want to include other maps (like the ones on this page) and this coming week the EQ Atlas PDF will be coming out, so stay tuned.

Frogloks Take over Grobb
Saturday, February 8th

Two nights ago in Norrath, the transformed frogloks of Guk arose and took over the Troll town of Grobb, which has been renamed Gukta, Outpost of Marr in honor of their god. Grobb is no more. This has had a ripple effect on the powers in Norrath, with the trolls fleeing to Neriak to set up their new home there, and the ogres becoming concerned that they no longer have a buffer between themselves and the humans of Freeport.

I have gone through and updated as much of the changes as I could find. Let me know if you find more.

Quick Update
Thursday, February 6th

Just wanted to let everyone know I updated the zone maps to show the portal books to the Plane of Knowledge. They are marked with a big blue K on the maps. As a general rule, they will deposit you in a safe area so that you can go through regardless of race/class. For instance, the Cabilis portal acutally dumps you in the Field of Bone in one of the valleys to the south of the entrance, and Felwithe actually dumps you in Greater Faydark some ways from the entrance. Feel safe using them.

Upcoming EQ Games
Monday, January 27th

Just a brief update on the state of EQ. First of all, the Playstation 2 game Everquest: Online Adventures has gone gold and should be out in a couple of weeks. I personally wasn't a big fan of this version, but a lot of people liked it, so who can say. In my opinion (which is admittedly cynical when it comes to the current MMORPG's out there) I thought it was like the PC version, but with worse graphics, more painful camping (which is required for quests, which are in turn required to get many spells and class-specific weapons, or in other words, every single player in the game will be doing them at once, leading to huge lines at spawn spots), unwieldy controls (cuz you still need a keyboard), high cost of entry ($50 game, $50 USB keyboard, $40 PS2 network adapter, and $10/month), and painfully steep exp curve starting at level 6 and getting worse every step of the way. On the bright side, the zones are huge, combat is less painful (no casting interrupts), and relies less on having specific classes and their skills in your group, and it's easy to get into, so it will be good for people who have never played the PC version. Or at least that's my prediction.

The other thing is that SOE announced the Legacy of Ykesha, which is a new expansion for the game coming around March (I don't think they've set a date). You can pay to download it earlier off their website (and can actually be downloading it now if you have a slow connection or don't want to wait on release day), or there is a CD that will come out after the regular release. Some of the additions they have planned are much-needed in the game to keep it on par with other games out there right now. Also, they're going to announce a cartography system on March 4th, which I'm curious to see. If that means complete maps, then selling a map book was even more of a money-grubbing scheme because this system had to have been in development long before the map book came out. If it's not, then feel free to alt-tab out and come here and get the full maps. Or maybe that's just me being cynical again... Overall, though, I think LoY will be a worthwhile purchase, although you could argue that a lot of the stuff should have been free updates to an outdated engine in order to keep it competitive.

If you're interested in either of these (I recommend LoY, but not EQOA if you already have EQ on the PC), you can get them both from my store (all sales support the site, as usual).

Lastly, I hope to have the downloadable PDF set up in the next few weeks. I haven't had a lot of time lately to do it, but it's coming. And no, it will not be free, I'm telling you now so nobody screams when they see it (not that that will work). I'm not going to make a compilation of the maps for the GUI mod, since you can alt-tab out and see them if you want to. I realize that's not perfect, but I think it works for most people now.
