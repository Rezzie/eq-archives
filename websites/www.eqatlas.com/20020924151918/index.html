EQ Atlas GUI XML Creator Released
Sunday, September 8th

I've created a new tool that I'm pretty excited about and I think a lot of people will find useful. It's the GUI XML Creator program, which allows you to take any jpg images and make a tabbed window in the new GUI to display those images. You can find it by clicking on the link above or in the menu at the left. Feel free to download it and try it out, but be sure to follow the directions, it's not fully idiot-proof yet!

This is also considered to be shareware in the old definition, which means that if you use it and you like it, I'd like to get a donation for it. Feel free to use the Paypal button at the left.

I'm hoping that people will use this as I intended, where you can download the images you need either from this site or another one and use them with the program in your game. I did not intend for people to send around huge zip files with all my maps in them, which is what had started to happen. If this works out well, this will help me determine if I should pursue things like this in the future.

Everquest Atlas Book
Sunday, August 18th

I sent out the following message to the community sites regarding the upcoming Everquest Atlas from Sony.

SOE recently began promoting their new book, the Everquest Atlas, and some of you have expressed to me some confusion regarding this. Despite the similarity between the name of this product and their use of "EQ Atlas" to advertise it on the Everquest Live site, the product is not related in any way to my site, EQ Atlas (www.eqatlas.com). While we both offer maps and descriptions, the specific content is quite different. I hope that this announcement will clear up any confusion.

Also, a clarification on the zone experience modifiers. There was a well-known Producer's Letter from about a year and a half ago that mentioned some ZEM's, these were actually changes from the prior, unpublished ZEM's. Nobody knows what the new ones are.

Check out the funky new MMOG store at EBWorld (link to right).

Some Stuff
Monday, August 12th

Ugg, been busy studying, the test is now a week away (week from tomorrow) so hopefully I'll be returning to the living world soon. Just had some time for a few bits.

People frequently ask about experience point bonuses for zones. These bonuses have never been made public. Nobody has the numbers. The ones I have on my site are wrong. In general, less populated and more dangerous zones have higher ones (like Hole and Kedge Keep) while more populated zones don't (like Dreadlands and Overthere).
