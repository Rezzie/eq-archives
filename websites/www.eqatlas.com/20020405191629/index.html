Friday, March 22nd

I've re-updated the Nektulos Forest map, I apparently hadn't uploaded it last night. Let me know what's still missing on it.

Also, Sony has announced a new upcoming expansion, the Planes of Power. You can see the press release and the official site for it.

Lastly, if you haven't heard there is a Senator who introduced a bill that used to be called the SSSCA, which got a lot of bad press, and now is called something else (CBDTPA). This legislation would require hardware manufacturers of all types to implement technology that would recognize copy protection imbedded in digital media (CD's, DVD's, HDTV, digital movies, etc) and decide whether or not to play the media based on that. Their argument is that we are not using broadband enough because there is not enough digital content that would require it, and the reason for that is that companies don't want to make digital media because they are afraid of people stealing it. The arguments against it (of which there are many) range from the electronics industry not wanting to implement expensive new technologies into their hardware that consumers are not going to want ("you mean I can't play this CD in both my car and my CD player?"), to the violation of fair use rights on this media that has been around since people started being able to record. It's clear which side I'm on. If you actually believe Napster was destroying the "rights of the artists" then maybe you'll think this bill is okay. You can see the full text of it, and the Senate Judiciary Committee has a website where you can post comments on it while they figure out what to do. I encourage you to post there, even if someone else has said exactly what you're going to say, so they know that what you're posting is a common problem. There is also a lot of other commentary floating around the web about this law (search using SSSCA) which I encourage you to read about if you haven't heard of it until now. For those of you who think this shouldn't be posted here because it's unrelated to EQ, think again. This law could be used to make your computer unwilling to play a CD that has been played in another computer or CD player, and could make it so you could only install your game on one computer, so if you play in multiple locations (like I do) or listen to music while you play, it does apply to you.
