One of the things I have started doing on CH's is running a countdown. System goes like this:

1. Sitting and medding, watching health bars. See that the tank is approaching what I consider to be CH'ing time. This is normally 40%, but will vary up and down depending on how much damage they are taking.

2. Stand up, begin casting CH.

3. Announce "CH'ing target X", at this point there are normally about 7.5 or 8 secs to go on the casting time.

4. At the halfway point type "5", meaning 5 secs to go.

5. Type "2" when 2 secs to go.

6. Spell goes off.


This system has several effects. First off it lets the tank and any secondary healers know the heal is coming, and WHEN. This branches into the following:

1. If the tank thinks they are going to die, they can back off and let someone else occupy it's attention for 2 secs.

2. If the secondary healer thinks they are going to die, they can slip in a GH to keep them healthy.

3. Everyone in the group knows I am occupied for 10 seconds and unable to perform further heals.

4. If the tank and secondary healers thinks everything's going great, they get a warm fuzzy feeling knowing that I am looking out for them. Obviously this is the one I like most!

Greetings!

I use Complete Heal lots. LOTS.

My groups are familiar with my tactics and my countdowns. For normal CH I announce '6 sec' at the 6 second mark. This lets the secondary healers toss a GH if needed or cover any other rapidly developing problem.

If the target is a ranger I will announce '3 sec' giving him time to Jolt if needed. I usually call for Jolt prior to 3 seconds if I see the HP curve won't make it.

Other tanks get a '2 sec' call at the last bubble of the cast time. That cues them to decide if they need to back off.

When a battle is close I do a '8 6 4 2' countdown, mostly to let them know I am a bit worried. If more than one person is getting a CH I name them prior to count, as in (Tanarel the Ranger and Icebeast the Warrior):

'Tan 8 sec', '6', 'Jolt', '3'
'Ice 8 sec', '2 sec'
