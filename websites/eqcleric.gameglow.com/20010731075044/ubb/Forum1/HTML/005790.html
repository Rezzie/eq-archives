When people talk of rapid leveling and such, I think that they are usually missing the point. Let me explain.

First off, my cleric is level 53, and my days played is in the high 80's. The VAST majority of that time was spent horribly unproductively though (from an xp gaining perspective anyway). Most of those days were spent wandering around trying out new places, acquiring loot (hunting and trading and questing), looking for groups or grinding in bad groups, killing time, talking with peeps, waiting around on raids, or just afk. I'd say that only a very small portion of my playing time was spent getting any REAL xp. I've probably gotten 90% of my levels in under 15 days (i usually screw around alot, then get mad that I've been at a certain level for so long, then grind solid xp until i get sick of it).

Because of this, I will make the broad generalization that SOLOING IS FASTER THAN GROUPONG 97% OF THE TIME.

Now maybe if you have a close group of friends of appropriate classes and levels, and you can coordinate well enough to nearly always be on at the same time and play together....and are on the same mindset about where to go and what to do.....then maybe grouping is faster. BUT this is rarely the case. Now I know we've all been in that 'perfect' group where xp flowed like water. Now that will NEVER happen solo, but how often are you in those groups?

Having said that, if you want to level rapidly its all about your mindset and playing style. Here is the BIG secret!!! Get ready!!

step one: Find a place where you can solo blue mobs as easily as possible (the best place will be one where the spawn rate is faster than your mana regen).....then .....most importantly.....

step two: STAY THERE!!!! No leaving for any reason. If you're a cleric, just summon food. If not, then stock up. just stay there till you no longer get xp.

step three: Repeat.

Its that simple.

I know for a fact that clerics can solo to 54 pretty easily, and I've heard very convincingly that we can solo to 60. If clerics can do it, any casting class can....just faster. All melee classes might not be able to solo to 60, but they can all solo higher than they usually get credit for (and for the time that they can solo, it will nearly always be faster than grouping).

Last I checked, I think I had over 40 days on Sonard at level 52. Created March 6, 2000. (Sad to think how much time I spent in this game). If my memory is right, I think I hit 30 with about 20 days played. I made a monk, got him to 23 in 5 days, and still did all my shackle quests, and did it totally solo (now working on fixing faction, so exp really slowing down, /rude HK guards).

Reason's my monk is leveling so much faster:
Think back to your first 10 levels playing your first charactor. You didn't know much, got lost all the time, spent a lot of time looking for that corpse when you got jumped in a new zone, endless hours hailing NPC's trying to figure out what the quest was, and who gave out quests, waiting for boats, looking for that elusive merchant that sold an item you needed (I think I spent a whole 4 hours hungry and thirsty trying to find out where to buy food in Kelethin when I got level 4 and did not get newbie food when I died), hours apon hours running around exploring new zones, sitting at a zone line looking for a group, working on trade skills, ect ect ect. Take all this out (never did any of this with monk) and you cut down ALOT of time. Don't have to be powerleveled to level quickly. You just know the game, where to hunt, and how to play effectively (what you can kill for fast exp). Other factors include little things like I am sure when you make an alt, you have a lot more idea of what stats to put points in (was totally clueless when I made Sonard), you know what equipment to put on (and can afford it), and know where to find info about your class to make the most of your playing time (learning curve a lot less).

BTW, I did not 'uber twink' my monk. I did get a pair of fighting batons from a guild member as a trade for an advisor robe, and got him 2 peices of cresent armor from Dalnir's when he was 14, and gave him +str jewelry and an exe hood (total value of equip at level 23: 1.8k approx. If it was my first char, I am sure I could have gotten him same equip alone except for cresent for another 7 levels).

Other bonuses that make alt's level faster is you never need a pickup group (twinks tend to hunt with twinks of friends), and you will probably never need to take a boat, as by this time you probably know enough druids/wizards that you can get a port for your twink when you need it. You also tend not to spend time on trade skills on alts.

Respective twinks will take less and less time as far as I have seen. And if you are making a twink because you are level 60 and your guild needs a certain key class, you can level this char at a phenominal rate.

I personally know of a level 60 necro in a large guild on my server who made a chanter. At level 4 he was constantly begged for clarity since he had the equipment better than most level 50 chanters. He also had a pet druid/chanter to Powerlevel him all the way to 60. I think he did got level 50 in something like 10 days played, but it was a while ago so I can't remember exactly what it was, but that is pretty close. I know it was insane.

Tactic to PL lowbies insanely fast: Go to newbie dungeon like runneye that is pretty much deserted, Rune and 8 pt DS and run around and agro reds till you get 10 beating on ya and sit back and rake in exp. Go to next dungeon and repeat till they start to yellow out on ya. Of course you have to stop every few levels and get skills back up so you don't hit level 50 with alteration at 10 since you never cast many spells. But skills like offence, defence, block ect go up if you are hitting mobs while being attacked. Especially defence.

So, while it's not impossible to get to high levels extremely fast, you wouldn't do it with your main char. Even if I was into powerleveling, I would never powerlevel a genuine n00b like that since he would be for all intents and purposes, an Ebay char. But if you got a char or 2 to level 50, and am not taking away mobs from real newbies (IE, fighting in deserted places), I don't see the harm.
