Really depends on what you want to do... Oh and I admit I'm assuming gear level to be pretty high.

For exp, hmm.

Classes that obviously aren't all that:
warrior and shadow knight... monk tanks blue mobs just as well and outdamages both, so if you can choose there's no reason not to pick a monk over the two
druids and wizards also come out as pretty weak, since druid can't heal by themselves without being insanely inefficient and if there's a shaman/clr in group they're reduced to nukers and wiz will own them there. Alas wiz dishes out more damage than dru so that's out with the druid... but wiz doesn't outdamage melee without manaregen help, and then we're looking at no tank or healer (unless you go for something funky like enc/pal/wiz, which admittedly might work).
Rangers are also crippled bit indoors because Verant slacks and archery isn't very reliable... so if we're looking at a 3man able to operate in any exp zone I'd have to say rangers are out too.
For exp you don't do hard hitting mobs... damshields just don't make up for lost damage that you get by putting a clr in your group. So slows > damshields+CHs -> shm > clr. Also paladin can heal pretty well now and they dish out pretty impressive dps especially against the undead.

We're left with monk, rogue, bard, paladin, shaman, enchanter, magician and necro. Bard has a significant issue with damage unless they can charm, mostly they act as a replacement healer... would never put them in group with a shm or enc. You have 3 methods of damage control and that's those 3. Get to pick one and deal with it.

Enchanters are good and bad. They become extremely powerful if they can dire charm, but as a weakness they're fragile and are really in grave danger if your groups healing method is a paladin. And even a dire charmed pet doesn't really justify having a cleric in a 3man (if we're talking bigger groups, the cleric becomes quite justifiable). All in all I don't think enc is worth the risk unless you have a very, very controlled environment. Something funky like enc/nec/pal in an area with lvl 46 undeads would be interesting, but frankly the group wouldn't be good in most areas.

Also paladin/bard/shm don't stack since they're also there for the healing purpose really. Also you do need someone tanking and it'd be monk or paladin really.

Pretty much any combo from then on owns.
Monk/shm/nec, pal/rog/mag, monk/bard/rog are all extremely potent groups.

Still like it was said before... it comes down to the people behind the keyboard. You can really rip places apart with combos like enc/mag/clr too assuming low MR mobs. Still the easy combos come down to often having a monk (best tanking + damage combo), shm (usually best endurance + damage reduction + damage combo) and a third person for damage.
