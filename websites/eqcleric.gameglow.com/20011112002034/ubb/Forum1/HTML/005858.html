The ultimate question

Hard one because there are so many different 'right' answers. I would guess the first and most important thing to consider is, what Velious factions are your friends on. To do any of the armor quests you're going to need small to medium size raids. Without friends, you're not getting any of the quests done.

First off a rough comparison of the different armors. I’ll get a little more specific afterwards. There are 4 armors in Velious. Plane of Growth, Thurgadin, Kael, and Skyshrine. The Plane of Growth and Thurgadin armor have the same AC and basically the same stats. Then there is the Kael armor. It has noticeably higher AC, but only slighty higher stats than the Thurgadin/PoG armor. The Skyshrine armor has the same AC as Thurgadin/PoG armor, but the stats are noticeably higher. As far as difficulty goes, the Thurgadin and PoG armor are much easier than the Kael or Skyshrine armor.

The first Velious armor quests are the easiest of the 3. The Thurgadin (dwarf) quests armor. The results from this is equivalent to the armor that is dropped in PoG. To complete the quests, you must first have at least indifferent Coldain faction. Next you need to obtain the correct piece of corroded plate. Corroded plate drops from giants in Kael. While almost any giant in Kael can drop it (I’ve gotten pieces from giants at the zone), it is generally believed that the higher level the giant the more likely it is to drop a piece of quest armor. Generally about 1 to 2 pieces an hour drop.

The next armor quests are the Kael armors. First you need to get up to indifferent with King Tormax faction. Once faction has been raised, you will need to obtain pieces of ancient tarnished plate. This stuff drops off of mobs located in Temple of Veeshan (ToV). These are dragons and as such require full raids of high level characters to take out.

The last armor quests are the Skyshrine armors. For these you need to get you Yelnik faction up to indifferent. Once faction has been raised, you need to obtain pieces of unadorned plate. Unadorned plate drops in the Testing Halls located in ToV. Again, these are high level mobs that require a full raid of high level characters to take out.

On all armors, once you have obtained the appropriate piece of armor, you will then need to collect 3 gems. These three gems are then turned into the quest giver along with the correct piece of armor. The gems needed are the same regardless of which quest you are doing (i.e. the same gems are needed for the Thurgadin bracers, Kael bracers, and Skyshrine bracers.) Gems needed for the cleric armor are:

Breastplate – Black Marble
Leggings – Chipped Onyx Sapphire
Boots – Crushed Flame Emerald
Gauntlets – Crushed Lava Ruby
Helm – Crushed Onyx Saphire
Bracer – Crushed Opal
Arms – Jaundice Gem

Zone that are known to have good drops of the various gems (not going into specifics as to what gems are dropped in each of the zones) are: Cobalt Scar, Velk’s Labyrinth, Dragon Necropolis, and Icewall Keep (in the cavern area). Other zones drop gems, but these seem to be the main zones. Black Marble (needed for the breastplate) tends to drop off only very high level mobs. Be prepared to take awhile getting them.

Awesome summary Kovina! =)

For a while there, Temple of Veeshan armor pieces were dropping on the first 8 mobs (drakes) in the zone, and dropping fairly plentifully. Now, however, they've moved the armor farther in and it's rather scarce.

Just remember that there are four types of armor drops - leather, silk, chain, and plate - and although the type of armor dropping seems to go in waves of one type (/cheer plate night), it is evenly distributed overall between the three types.

However, while silk only has int casters, plate has rog/shaman/ranger, and leather has monk/druid, plate drops have FIVE classes who need it - cleric, warrior, paladin, bard, SK. Add to that the high number of warriors and clerics on raids, and there is very little to go around. =(

Factor in the rarity of gems, and the result is that not a lot of Templar's armor is getting made out there.

We have gotten some good rare gems off Vaniki, Lord Doljonijiarnimorinar, Velketor, Zlandicar, and other high level Velious mobs.

In all honesty, Kael and SS are comparable. The extra resists, HP and mana in SS and the extra str and AC in Kael are nice but not enough so you can clearly point to one and say "That set is better than the other".

Odds are you're still going to have to swap out resist gear for big fights and to do that you'll have to drop a few pieces of your armor.

In reality, unless you pick your guild after they've started ToV farming, you're probably going to go along with your guild in faction choices. I know I did. It's just easier that way.

If you do find yourself staring down guild choices and a deciding factor is faction, remember Kael is neon purple or maroon (depends on race) and SS is a minty green. If you're low on str, pick Kael because while they may weigh the same, Kael has 14 str to help offset its weight while SS does not. If you want HP/mana and a bit more resist, go SS. Kael has ToV North mobs and SS has the ToV quests (slightly easier than ToVN but not as good overall).
