a pause of 10 = 1 second, long pauses will kill your raid if a fizzle or a need for a heal on others arises. The best hotkey I have found to use is this..
/cast ___ (slot CH is in)
/1 (or /ooc or whatever chat you use) CH on %T (add your number in the rotation here)
/pause 5 ( a half second pause)
/cast ___(slot CH is in)
If you DO fizzle, you will still cast the second time, if you dont fizzle, you get a dumb message that you can't use the ability, but your heal goes as planned. I leave the middle line open and stick a /say CH on %T or some other chat communication method there. Hope it helps, but I think trying to program pauses for 3 other clerics CH is never going to work.

CH rotations are easy, simply have 3- 4 clerics with good mana pool / connection for rotation.

/1 (raid channel) CH on [%t] INC

Aariis got it right, the idea is to watch for when the cleric before you says GO, cast your CH, and then tell the next cleric to GO after like 3 seconds. That's what the /pause is there for. The /pause command can cause too many problems (sputters, tanks running out of range, dead clerics in the chain), so it's not recommended that you use it to pile 15 CH's on one button.

Also, I think you can't have multiple commands per line, so /cast and /shout are incompatible, as far as I know.

So,

/cast 3
/g CH on %T
/pause 27
/g Next Cleric GO

is the basic setup.

During the fight you'll be watching for that special person who goes before you to say "Next Cleric GO" at which point you hit the button. When your heal hits, you check your range to the tank and the mob, check your mana, and sit if needed. Then you wait again.

It gets tricky when things go out of sync, so you have to have a rule that if multiple people get out of order because of emergencies, the FIRST cleric casts out of turn and everyone follows him/her again.

Otherwise everyone will look at that MT's bar, feel the need to CH, and you'll end up with 4 clerics telling each other to GO at the same time.

I actually did my first ever CH rotation this weekend (so sue me, I'm a casual player, level 60 or no ). 4 clerics & 4 or 5 groups, on Dracoliche. (hits for 600, taps, 80K hps). Not that uber but a good place to start, I thought.

I went for the minimalist approach:

/stand
/cast 1
/6 Complete Heal %t, cleric_02 next!
/cast 1

6 being the rotation-only chat channel I was used, obviously adjust to suit. Note I didn't specify an interval in the macro, the 4 of us agreed on that beforehand.

Yes, it means we had to remember the interval, so more work on our part, but that turned out to be a good thing - we started with a 4 second interval, but when we realised the MT wasn't going below 60% health, we backed off to an 8 second interval. Saved us in the end, since we had enough mana to keep going till Draco died. Worked pretty well, given that none of us had done a rotation before

A bit rambling, but the moral of the story is: don't try and do too much with macros. The important thing is that the clerics in the rotation know what they're doing.

Some people use pause, some people don't. Sometimes I use it, sometimes I don't. I kind of got a feel for what it can do, cause I did have it mess me up really bad during a raid.

It's a handle with care command.

Never ride motorcycles! Never pilot airplanes! Never skydive! Never bungee-jump! Same thing.

Average human reaction time is 0.8 seconds. Counting seconds without a timer in front of you... 20% wrong. Distractions during fight can increase that. All in all you could end up with a + or - 2 seconds delay in there, and sometimes that's alot.

Plus, if you've got a stranger cleric in your rotation, will you trust him or her with your MT's health? It's easier to trust that someone's gonna hit a button when they see a prompt than it is to trust that someone will make the right call and heal your tank on time.

And, a million things could go wrong during a fight, and a messed up /pause sequence is only one of them. Never heard anyone say "Man we tried this with /pause and we wiped, and then I told everyone to remove their /pauses and we won easy!"

Main Cleric says: "Set up a pause macro. Use delay 27."
Cleric 2 says: "Ok."
Cleric 3 says: "Done."
You say: "No. I don't like /pause and I'm not gonna use it."

Anyway, your call.
