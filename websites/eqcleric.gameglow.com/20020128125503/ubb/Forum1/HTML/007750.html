Heh. From what I've seen, most wizards never seem to get better at mana management. I've group with a wizard for over 50 levels now. Almost every fight, he's out of mana. Talked to him a while about it once and came up with a couple points.

1) Yes wizards can evac, etc., and usually keep some mana in reserve for that.

2) The majority of exp grouping is not evac's though. It's damage. And fast damage is one of the best things wizards are good at.

3) A clerics mana pool is needed for emergencies, so we tend to try to bounce our mana usage around in the upper half of our mana bar to keep the reserves up (i.e. nuke if near 100, med and just heal near 50, etc).

4) The wizard doesn't worry about health bars, and spends most of the fight just looking at that full mana bar going, "Well, I can just do a little more damage..." and uses his mana up. Then meds till he can nuke again since he thinks damage is his main job.

In a year and a half playing together, I have yet to be able to convince him that he essentially does the same damage over time if he nukes to 60 and meds up to full to nuke again vs. nuking to 0, medding to 40, nuking to 0, etc. And in the prior case, he has 60m saved up for an overpull where we need to drop a mob or two extra fast.

So we mainly do what others have done. Me and the chanter stay above 60m as much as possible and the puller just checks on us. Let the nukers do whatever.

In the defense of INT casters:

Healing is reverse nuking. When thought of in this manner, it is incredibly mana efficient. Clerics recieve even more efficiency through their natural healing bonus as well as specialized spells.

Therefore, in a theoretical group that is completely balanced, everyone contributing equal "output" over a period of 30 seconds, let's say, the nukers will inevitably be left with less mana than the healers.

This isn't an excuse for INT casters -- managing your nukes is imperative to so many aspects of keeping yourself and your group alive -- but hopefully it shows that we clerics naturally have an easier time of managing mana, simply because our spells are more efficient.

It's not as simple as saying, "I'm 80m, what's that moron doing if he's only 40m??" They spend more for the same output.

Mana management is very important to wizards. Without mana we can't contribute in any way to a group.
I like to keep a 40% mana buffer myself for emergencies, but in groups without mana regen it's very easy to reach 40% even just dropping one nuke or a couple of stuns per fight. If I have snare duty it's even worse.

As for casters going lom/oom after large pulls then surely that's the emergency you wanted them to use their mana on ? In a situation like that I'm nuking heavy to finish mobs fast, stunning and root parking when we dont have an enchanter and the mana drains away quickly. If I'm out of mana after that it's because the mobs are all dead and we aren't.
I would use the mana for an evac, but in most circumstances people aren't bothered about evacing. And there's nothing like an evac to finish a group for the night.

Wizards also worry about contributing in groups. In the long term damage stakes a group of 4 hasted tanks with an enchanter and cleric can do obscene damage. A lot of the groups I'm in tend to keep a slot free for an enchanter/bard , if a wizard is LFG he's ignored as their damage is all mana based and the group doesn't have an enchanter.
So the pressure is on to be seen to contribute in a group, which often leads to over nuking.

Also a lot of wizards solo these days due to the lack of groups. Unfortunately some wizards seem to think the "nuke it down" approach to soloing applies equally well to groups. A few of those and a lot of people prefer to go with the tank instead of the caster.

Finally remember that the reason the cleric and enchanter still have mana and the other casters don't is that the mobs are dropping faster. The longer the mob is alive the more damage it does and the more healing the cleric needs to do. And in multiple pulls the more mana the enchanter spends controlling the mobs.
It's just that cleric and enchanter mana use is way more efficient than direct damage spells, and often the benefits are more obvious too.

And most groups I'm in wait for the cleric and the enchanter, not the casters.

One important thing to realise is that cleric mana spent is dependant on enchanter mana spent.

Enchanter prevents damage though using mana while clerics heal damage though using mana.

Enchanters MUST mez and MUST buff. After that in a normal group their mana is left for damage reduction though the use of slows and stuns.An enchanter can place a 50% slow on a mob for 100 mana. Or they could put a 70% slow(225 mana) and several stuns(20 to 60 mana each).

So in an ideal situation an enchanter will stay at the same mana as the cleric. When the enchanter has more mana they prevent more damage using better slows and stuns and when the cleric has more mana they use cleaper slows and less stuns.

Also a large number of small mobs will drain an enchanter mana much faster then a small number of harder ones even though it would be the same mana expenditure for the cleric.

Other int casters should stay high mana normally and only nuke hard on dangerous mobs where their fast damage output is a distinct advantage.
