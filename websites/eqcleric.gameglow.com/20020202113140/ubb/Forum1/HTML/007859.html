At this level, your AC isn't something you should realy care about, considering that it isn't something very easily quantifiable in terms of utility. The mobs which you will be fighting will become increasingly more powerful as you level, so if you do place a high value on AC, then you will have to constantly upgrade.

HP and Mana on the other hand are something that you can easily quantify in terms of utility, and something which is more or less constant. With AC, it might help, it might not, who knows. You might get hit, you might not get hit once an entire night, there really is no way of determining just how much your AC will actually help you since you are a cleric and you're not supposed to get hit. Mana can be directly translated into a certain amount of buffing/healing and so forth, so you get a more definite and quantifiable result of adding more wis/mana gear.

Resists are somewhat something entirely different. Some spells are simply harder to resist than others, and resists work in such a broken way that you can't "really" say how useful resists are in everyday grouping. However, I doubt that you're going to be hit with a significant amount of spells in your average grouping, and if you are fighting heavy caster mobs, then just put on some resist gear if you find that it does help.

But in all honesty, you're probably going to go through quite a bit of gear between now and level 60, and once you get 46 and start to hit Hate, and possibly do some of the Velioius armor quests, then you won't have to worry so much about what to use. Of course, this is just my opinion, and everybody has different experiences. Just try a little of everything until you find what you feel works best.
