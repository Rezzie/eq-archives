Just my own opinion, by I would advise junking any complex systems, point values, etc. You guys are intelligent thinking human beings, you should be able to pick someone better than dice can, or a system can, right?

The thing is of course, the guild has to believe in your motives, that you are going to be fair. My guild does loot committee. Something drops, they debate who should get it, it is awarded. There's no absolute, its a combination of factors. You need to recognize service, and seniority somewhat, but you better avoid elitism. Levels should play a part, but not absolute by any means. Most importantly though is need, not for the individual, but the guild. Which individual should the guild award an item to so that the guild is benifited the greatest? You really have to set aside a lot of selfishness to trust in a systemless loot committee.

I think you need to let the laws of randomness work it out. Basically in my guild we let the leader determine epic loot (based upon progress in epic quest and other factors). We let people random for items which they will use. Once you win a random you're out until everyone else in your class has an item.

We ask people to be generous and defer to people on their last piece, but we don't require it.

And finally, we strongly frown on people making scenes or stirring up trouble over loot. It's grounds for guild removal.

I think point systems are too hard to track and guild outsiders don't have a means to be rewarded if you should need them. And I think loot committees will always be suspected of being biased or running a popularity contest.

I agree 100% with Caleal.

We used to do pure random - but after several pieces of very good gear ended up on people that were clearly not very large assets for the guild we changed to a system that rewards dedication/seniority/need etc.'

Currently we have 3 officers assigned to handling loot - when something drops we take tells from those that are interested, talk it over with other officers if needed and congratulate the "winner". With major pieces we favor the most deserving while we try to spread the minor pieces between everyone - so noone feels completely left out. Ie, ToV BP drops it goes to the most "deserving" - but when ToV arms drop its likely to go to someone that has gotten little loot sofar despite being less "deserving" overall. My guild uses /random. Everybody has way more than they started with 2 months ago and would have gotten otherwise. No accusation of favoritism can be made with any degree of credibility. Quest items get assigned to the person furthest along in the quest, and if two are equally far along then they random for the piece.

I made the decision to create a random-loot guild after experiencing seniority systems and becoming disgusted with corruption and favoritism, and the backbiting that comes with it from those on the short end of the stick. A random system cuts out all the butt-kissing and similar distractions that destroy many guilds.

So far it has worked excellently. In two months I've taken a bunch of demoralized pre-planar rabble and turned them into a crack raiding squad able to clear planes routinely, and every member has a full set of planar or close to it by now. Knowing exactly how loot will be decided beforehand (by random, with no bias) enables every player to concentrate on playing his best for the most overall success. It enables me as a raid leader to run raids and not answer questions about loot. Guests leave telling us how great we are rather than being secretly resentful about being treated as second-class. Participants from other guilds are routinely amazed at the discipline and good will that are hallmarks of our activities.

Oddly enough this has not caused balance problems. People in full gear still raid with the guild to get their fellows equipped. Sure, a lot of time people will be hoping that they get the loot, but if they don't get it they don't feel cheated and they don't resent the winner. And rather than angling for position with the person making the loot decision, they spend their timing thinking how to be most effective in getting the loot to drop in the first place.
