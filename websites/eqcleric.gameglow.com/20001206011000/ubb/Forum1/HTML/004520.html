Inspecting people in a virtual setting is completely different than inspecting someone in the real world.

The reason why you feel offended in a RL situation is because the other person walked up close to you and you felt like he/she is violating your "personal comfort zone". But every culture has their own definition and limits on the "personal comfort zone". For example, people in the U.S. feel extremely uncomfortable if stranger stands really close or accidentally touches your body. And you would also feel very uneasy sharing a table in a restuarant with strangers.

But people in asia are completely different. If you get on a train in India, You can literally hug a stranger because of the usual crowdedness. And nobody will feel offended.

IMHO, I think EQ is nothing more than a game, so checking what people are wearing is fine. But i always ask before i inspect.
