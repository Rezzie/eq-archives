Clerics do not get a slow spell. Neither slow as in run speed ala snare nor slow as in attack speed ala Turgxx.

Atone does a memory blur, though not always sucessful. Effectively wiping the hate list. The reason they may appear to "slow down" is that the mob has to regain agro. So if your almost out of agro range they stop and look around a second (may appear slow ala snare) or it may take a minute to gain agro of a person hitting it (ala turgurs).

I find it effective when it works. Though its a pain to try to use when in to close of quarters. Really the only time I use it is to get by mobs in dungeons and thus I have trouble with it. Either I cant get far enough away to have them NOT reagro on me or I cant see my target. Frustrating to say the least. Its a spell I rarely mem.

Atone
Wipe Hate List
Classes: Clr (L34)
Skill: Alteration
Allowable Targets: All
Range to Target: 200 feet
Resistance Check: Magic + 25
Mana Required: 60
Spell Duration: Instant
Casting Time: 4.50 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.25 seconds
Recast Delay: 12.0 seconds
Spell cast on you: You feel forgiveness in your mind.
Spell cast on someone: Soandso calms down.

While the spdat file doesn't reveal every facet of a spell, one game mechanic that's pretty fundamental is that any debuff with a duration will eventually give a "Your (name) spell has ended."

Pretty safe to say that atone does not have any lasting effect on the mob. I've atoned many mobs while passing through FM in earlier days and hunted in the zone for hours and never saw an atone wear off. A good test to be sure of this is to atone a mob and then dispel it several times. If it is a debuff it'll say that it wore off when it's dispelled.

What they probably saw was the mob lost aggro, and it took a few seconds for it to regain aggro. (20 seconds is a very long time tho, sure it was kos to that group?) In a fight situation, atone acting this way would have the same effect.
