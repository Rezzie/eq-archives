I'm the guild leader of a modest 50+ guild on Cazic Thule. We currently have about 20 members and I'm hoping to grow to about 30-40 in the near future. Prior to this guild, I was in 3 others, all of which I had incredible fun in, and made many long-lasting friendships. All of the split up, and now several good good friends of mine from these guilds are wanderring around guildless.

So I Target the 60 warriors first, and ask what their plans are. All of them reply similarly with I'm joining Uberguild "B". I ask why and they tell me, "I spent all this time getting to 60, and now I need to do the big raids." Uh-huh. So I start asking the others, all over 55, what their plans are...
and I get "I'm joining Uberguild A, B, C, or D."(currently I'd say there are four guilds on CT that are above the rest, no I won't name them)

/sigh

Does nobody enjoy the little pleasures in life? The friends and the adventure? I may only be in a minor league guild but I still do someting new every week. and the things I've done for 3-4 mos now aren't that old, and I haven't seen every kind of loot drop, and I'm not fully equipped to want to proceed too far too fast.

Why do people need to beat the game thats unbeatable? Does anyone think they're gonna kill UberMob X and get a "Coongratulations, you Win!" Message? It seems to me that if you join an uberguild, and take out al the tough stuff and get everything done... Well, then what? All of these guys are the same ones who say, " I'm bored with Kael, I'm bored with Velks, I'm bored with blah blah blah." Do they think that TOV won't get boring? Or the Ssraa temple?

I don't want to go trudging off into the end game until I want to end the game. And if you're that tired of EQ just friggin quit. I'm feeling so left out when people say they're moving on to bigger and better things when I think what I'm doing is fun enough. Yes I want to see end-game bosses and such... but that can come later, slowly, when I'm ready.

Why does everyone else want to beat themselves up and leave all their friends behind just to see a boss mob, die to it, and not get any loot thats going to senior guild members?

I miss em all.

Piikaa, I just went through the 2 pages of posts that of the message board about Hlaan leaving your guild. I was sad to say the least. Why you ask? Because he was guilt tripped into staying in the guild instead of allowed to leave on good terms and find out for himself what it was he wanted. Had he gone out and found that maybe this wasn't the best decision and asked to be reinstated as a member of your guild, this would be a different story.

Uber is but a word, I believe. It is not a level or place you reach after doing items 1 through 12 on a chart. I have been in an uber guild, I have been in a small guild. The difference and the inevitable reason *I* left my small guild at the time was because I was burnt out of being an officer through 2 merges and also I was raiding all the time outside of my guild. Why not help set up raids for my guild? Because I would, and no one would show up for them. It gets to be a little disheartening after a while and one tends to let the matter lie as is and not try and change people set in their ways.

The "Uber" guilds aren't always the bullies and push overs people paint them out to be. Through jealousy and misunderstanding do stories get told and passed around like idle gossip from pick up groups to guilds. After a while, it gets to be this image that people expect of the said "uber" guild. /shrug Be that as it may, not everyone is a saint; we all have our flaws (and bad apples in the barrel).

A lot of us have been playing this game for nearly three years. I have only been playing this game for two. Sure friends are a joy to have in your guild. Friends also don't have to wear the same guild tag in order to stay friends. (Which is why sometimes I wish my friends lists were larger so i could keep tabs on some of em hehehe).

After a while there is only so much you can do in this game. You can help other people obtain items, levels, etc. but sooner or later you're going to want to do something for *you*. It's that darned humanity factor. For a lot of people it's seeing high level encounters, it's seeing new zones that you were previously unable to see (NToV comes to mind), it's experiencing what being part of a team of people doing things with strats you never thought would work, being part of a large machine that is working towards a common goal of defeating an imposing and awesome force.

You have to look and see what type of guild you are. Are you a raiding guild? Are you a guild that plans workshops on the best methods of going about certain tradeskills? For every person there is a different directive and motivation about why they chose their classes and how they play this game the way they do. That's what makes this game such a success because it has a little bit of everything for everyone.

Your goal and/or task is going to have to be attracting the types of players you would like. If you say that you are a "friend-type guild that raids"... you're going to have to raid. And gauge it according to the responses you get. That is how you will succeed.

And Bolgor, you said you targeted 60 warriors first. Warriors and melees are VERY gear/weapons oriented people. They want to constantly improve what they have (well, doesn't everyone to a certain extent?). By joining a "uber" guild they will have effectively taken the next step to achieve that goal. Keep that in mind when they politely decline your invitation to join your guild.

Okay I think I'm done rambling now :P
