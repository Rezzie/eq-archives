It's very simple. If you make a game where a healer is required for a group, people will want to get the best healer possible, to maximize their xp. Currently this is hands down a cleric. I would say that pulls in half the camps in Seb would have to be cut in half if there were a druid instead of a cleric healing. And that is even counting a shaman slowing. There is a requirement for clerics to be in a high level group, and the shaman and druid resent that, especially when they could be the healers until 45th-ish.

Conversely, if you make a game around the fact that you don't need a healer, why would anyone ever play a cleric? If they do nothing to speed up the rate at which mobs are killed or the rate at which downtime is reduced, there is no reason for them.

VI's answer is to improve druids/shaman to be able to closely match cleric healing, but then you have cleric losing out on 90% of possible utility spells while only having a 20% increase in healing spells.

There is also the thing about rezs. As Abashi or Brad once said, it is expected that you can get a rez when you are high level. Whenever a player can't get a rez, they blame us clerics for not being at their beck and call. Given the xp it takes to get post 50, I know I would be mad when I ate a death in 54. That anger has nowhere to go except on clerics.

In the post-50 game, druids and shaman are having trouble getting groups, particularly for events/raids. Partly this is because druids and shaman are very numerous in the post-50 game, and partly because the game post-50 is geared toward unresistable wizard lure damage, melee damage, and healing.

Druids and shaman preferred, for most of their career, to be nukers/dotters, and thus specialized in evocation, generally. When they got superior heal at level 53, it wasn't very useful to them as a way to remain viable in the post-50 raid environment. The high resistance levels of the Velious MOBs made their nukes/dots/slows very unreliable. So they started to complain.

Weirdly, they did not complain much about the resistance problems that put them out of the nuke/dot/slow business, but instead started complaining about their poor healing ability.

I think they figured that Verant was not likely to retool the high level MOBs to make them less resistant, so they focused on healing as their ticket back into the big time.

They claimed that they'd always been healers (true, but by no means was it their preferred or main focus pre-50). They claimed that improving their direct healing ability would keep groups going in the absence of a cleric, while at the same time telling the whopper that filling in for a cleric did not mean completely replace. They ignored the fact that in the pre-50 game druids and shaman were more desired in groups than clerics, if the healing power was roughly equal. They especially tried to present the argument in a vacuum, comparing healing power only, as if they had no other damage-reducing or damage-avoiding abilities.

Principally, they claimed that there was a cleric shortage, and by weird logic they insisted that beefing up their healing power would solve this problem -- even though it was pointed out to them over and over that their solution would probably result in fewer clerics, not more.

They howled, b*tched, whined, and moaned until Verant caved. And they had the nerve while they were doing this to call clerics selfish whiners for attempting to preserve our principal identity as primary healers.

I only wish I could speak with the people making the class balance decisions at Verant face to face to find out what they are thinking and why they found the druids' and shaman arguments convincing. Also, I would ask why Verant continues to ignore the clerics' many requests for changes that would make the class more fun to play and thus more numerous.
