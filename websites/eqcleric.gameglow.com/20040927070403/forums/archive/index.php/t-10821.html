I haven't been on a raid yet since the changes but I have had server filtering turned on the last few sessions.

Basically I filter just about everything, all hits/misses, bard songs, everything except spell casting so I can time stuns to interrupt. I always have. Yes, bard songs still work if you do.

I haven't noticed anything weird about having the server side filtering turned on yet. One thing I did notice after the patch was a little delay when using /assist I hadn't noticed before.

You turn on filtering with this command:

/serverfilter on

There's other things that Verant has added that you can turn on/off, to assist with lag:

/dyna off (dynmaic lighting)
/reverb off (sound effects)

Some people claim turning off PC and NPC names, turning off sky, turning off all spell effects, is good in extremely lagging situations (Fear).

It seems like the most important change folks have made is increasing their datarate from the default of 5 up to at least 9.0. I have mine at 20.0 and haven't gone LD on a raid since.

To change your datarate:
click start
click run
enter "notepad c:\progra~1\everquest\datarate.txt"
change the 5.0 to 7.0 to 9.0 (or higher if you want--Verant has said above 9.0 is bad in some way, tho some people put it at 40.0)
ALT+F
S
ALT+F
X

I run mine at 8.0.

As far as settings, I think it's in flux now. Prior to server side filters, my standard procedure:

Before logging in for the raid night, I go into my eqclient and change the following: (notepad c:\progra~1\everquest\eqclient.ini)
LoadVeliousArmor=FALSE
LoadArmor17=FALSE
LoadArmor18=FALSE
LoadArmor19=FALSE
LoadArmor20=FALSE
LoadArmor21=FALSE
LoadArmor22=FALSE
ShowDynamicLights=FALSE

I have a nosound.old file I'd rename to nosound.txt. (The file just says 'delete me' inside) Having a file called nosound.txt in your EQ directory will disable all sound. Alternately you can /reverb off and/or turn sound settings way down.

In the game itself under chat filters:
Turn Other Hits to OFF (always off raid or not)
Turn PC Spells to GRP
Turn NPC Spells to OFF
Turn Bard Songs to ME (always ME raid or not--it would be OFF but I have a bard for an errand boy)

Under display settings:
Particle Density to OFF
3D Sky to OFF
PC Names to OFF
Clipping Plane all the way down and one increment inward.

As you can see, my video card is the biggest bottleneck. It's only a pci viper 550 (running on a P3 500).

When the raid is done:

Change PC spells to ALL
Change NPC spells to ON
Change particle density to LOW
Change 3d sky to SINGLE LAYER
Change PC names to ON
Put clipping plane back to 40-50%

and before I shut down my PC, I'd edit eqclient.ini and turn velious armor back on. Turn dynamiclights back on. rename nosound.txt to nosound.old.

datarate I always keep at 8.0.

My current raid setup is now:

Leave the files alone in my EQ directory.

In chat filters:
Change PC Spells to GRP
Change NPC Spells to OFF

In display settings:
Change PC Names to OFF
Pull clipping plane to 20-30%

In-game commands:
/showspelleffects off
/reverb off

And I have far better performance than I did turning off absolutely everything before. This patch was amazing. http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif
