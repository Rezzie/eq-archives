I've lived through the Ragefire camp. I've lived through Lodizal, Stormfeather, and all the others. It sucks, but not by design. It's just that Verant isn't *good* enough to create a new design or *honest* enough to admit that the current design is..well..stupid.

Everyone playing the game deserves a shot at the prize. That's what we pay the bucks for and that's the end of the argument.

But the problem is that people are just basically ****ty and have no respect for anyone else in the game. So it doesn't matter if one person or 10 groups are there in Naggy's Lair...if someone else can screw up the works..they will.

As has been mentioned (and I've seen it) one Cleric's guild clears out Fire Giants, buffs up for the big fight and Crappy Guild #2 sails in hands in the pearl and takes the prize...while the original guild is just left without.

Are there ways to solve this? To make the quest epic? To give everyone a chance..but require that they work for it? To give the little guy a shot while protecting his efforts from the big guys?

Sure there are. Verant's just too damned lazy to implement it.

Consider this scenario (not perfect as I'm pulling it off the top of my head...take the idea..the idea).

We know that they can have one monster die and pop another. It happens in City of Mist. It happens with Ragefire himself.

We know some monsters just can't be killed. Look at Drugoz in Swamp of No Hope. He heals (no spells) every tick...faster than you can take him down.

We know that a monster can "check" the level of a player and banish him. Naggy and Vox do it.

We know that an NPC can take an item and give an item in return.

So...the group clears the Fire Giants and checks to see if Naggy is up. If so..fine: You've got a Naggy raid.

Naggy dies and about 10 minutes after that (time to divvy up loot) the "Sweeper" spawns. He roams from Naggy's Lair down to the drawbridge or King's Room. He cannot be killed. He's aggro to everything. When he hits you, he ports anyone higher than level 0 out of the dungeon.

After a little while. 30 minutes, an hour, 8 hours. The "Sweeper" despawns and POOF..all the fire giants come back!

Now, let's assume that Naggy isn't up. Just the Giants. *ONE* of those giants (in the back, in Naggy's Lair) can't be mes'd, is aggro, sees invis, etc., etc. And when he's killed then one of two things happen:

1 - Zordak the Vendor spawns. Time to get ready for your Ragefire battle. You've got your forces there, so it shouldn't be much a problem. Hand him the pearl, he laughs *HANDS BACK A CHARRED PEARL* and Ragefire the Dragon pops. (more on this later)

2 - The Sweeper spawns and clears out the area again.

As for the Charred Pearl...I've had the server go down during the Ragefire fight. I petitioned and was told "too damned bad." So I got to do the whole quest again. The Charred Pearl does two things:

1 - To claim the Flail, the Cleric must turn in the Heart *and* the Charred Pearl.

2 - But if things went badly and the Cleric was ripped off (server outage or other guild KS's) he can turn the Charred Pearl into Omat and get another quest Pearl to try again.

-----------
But the important part is that the Sweeper comes along at the end of an FG / Naggy / Zordak raid and *forces* everyone out. People can't "camp" the room because the Sweeper will keep pushing people out. Once the area is set for an "epic struggle" they'll have to work through the Fire Giants again, thus ensuring that THEY and their guild is on hand "just in case Zordak pops."

Epic Struggles, everyone gets a chance, no camping, randomness, workable bottlenecks (will Zordak or the Sweeper spawn?).

Needs work..but a much better system in essence.

1. I don't see this as whining. At least not done properly. I read threads on Whineplay that were whiny but I don't see any real whining here. These are legitimate problems. If other classes were complaining about these same thing I don't think people would call it whining.

It never ceases to amaze me how when we ask for even the most trivial things it's always "stop whining". Some issues people seem to think we whined about...

Cleric Quest armor (too hard and too low in AC)
DA/DB still allowing us to be stunned
MoK (nerfed to useless)
any expansion cleric BP nerf
any mana regen requests (ie standing med)

There are people who seem to think we're just fine as-is and that "rocking the boat" would get us nerfed. Well, we didn't rock the boat and we got nerfed anyways. Other people rocked it for us. Now people are saying that if we keep pushing it we'll get the stick nerfed too.

But when exactly do you propose we ask for something to be fixed? This goes beyond the epic to the core of our class. I mean, we're not the only ones in fear of the nerf stick. Are we to allow ourselves to be Verant's doormat? The simple fact is the squeaky wheel gets the grease or replaced. The silent wheels just grind along, never mentioning anything until they totally fail. Rayne has always stood for cleric issues because we are the silent class for the most part. I, for one, will not sit by and watch us get brushed off.

2. The bulk of the fighting over Ragefire is NOT cleric vs cleric. In fact, I had some rather pleasant exchanges with fellow clerics while proceeding along the quest myself. The problem is, it's guild vs guild. While some guilds feel it's required, it most certainly is NOT and saying so to Verant WILL get it nerfed. Look at the track record here, people. Manastones, MoK... whether or not people were requiring them was irrelevant. Verant throught they were and that's all that was needed.

The problem is it's desirable period. It's the best cleric weapon in the game and one of the best stat items you can get. Regardless of how common it is, it's a status symbol. EM is common as crap now but it's also a rite of passage. Wearing the Holy Armor of Barney(tm) shows you have gone to a rough place and toughed it out to some extent because you HAVE to have a raid to get it. EM is still wanted by half the clerics on the server because it's good armor. All this despite Donal's and Velious offering better. The epic is no different. Heck, it could proc True North and there would still be fights over it. It looks unique and it has excellent stats. Why do you think guilds go after lesser epics with such fervor?

I mean, I don't see shamen using theirs a whole lot but we spent time getting them theirs. I can't say I want mine any more than a shaman wanted his/hers. Theirs looks good too and offers exceptional stats. Who cares about the effect? We got them just the same.

Getting back to my original point... CLERICS generally don't fight over Ragefire. Cleric's GUILDS fight over them. I've actually been in tells with other clerics while fights raged around us. We were all shaking our collective heads at the mess. Because it wasn't JUST the cleric that lost, it was the GUILD.

Ragefire is no different than, say, the Dain, Cazic Thule, Yelinak, Trakanon, VS or any other uber spawns that often are contested. If CT ONLY dropped the cleric helm, would it meant that fights over Cazic would be caused by clerics too? Would it be a "cleric issue"? Would it be on OUR shoulders to fix? Of course not. Likewise, it's absurd to imply Ragefire fighting is caused and/or solved by clerics. 99% of the time it isn't. I seriously doubt most clerics out there have that kind of control. Apply your "you should be calm and rational and not fight" sentiments to Burgurgle and Gimblox. Those are places where the clerics are in total control. And yet, most of the time there is peaceful resolution there...

So yes, it's not Verant's fault. But seldom is it ours either. Verant's attitude is fitting, really. It's the same attitude they've had towards in-fighting all along.

3. Lists are a house of cards. All you need is a pearl, a willing guild and the attitude of "I need this more than they do" and it all comes crashing down. I'm glad newer servers don't seem to have this problem but I fear it is only a matter of time...
