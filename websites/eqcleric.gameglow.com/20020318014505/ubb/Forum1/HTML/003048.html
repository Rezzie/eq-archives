I've been thinking about this a lot recently since I've been toying with the idea of quitting. For me what it comes down to is the friendships. After the initial exploration, EQ is basically a fairly dull game except every once in a while when you can do something really new or pull off something dangerous. But most of the time is spent waiting or doing something fairly safe and repeatitive.

So, when I think about quitting, I realize that I would really miss my friends and the few new things I am doing, but would not miss the game itself all that much.

In terms of keeping it interesting, I honestly believe that clerics have a pretty nice amount of variability. Lots of people (including lots of clerics) think it is just a heal, buff and med routine. I haven't found that to be true for me. I have played a druid and a cleric to high levels and, while I like both, prefer my cleric for his adaptablility and his importance. While druids have a easy time soloing and can offer some nice group abilities, they only have one technique for dealing with problems (running away, in various forms). Clerics, on the other hand, just start getting interesting when everything goes to hell. Plus clerics are generally vital to a group while druids are just a nice-to-have.

What you can do: try different roles as a cleric (but let your group know what you are doing). While heal/buff/med works great, so does stun/heal/buff/med - and often keeps you busier and with less total down time. Also, clerics are often the best choice for crowd control if there is no enchanter in the group (enstill and/or atone). Clerics can also taunt and (thankfully) anti-taunt. Very useful in the right place. You can solo too - all the way up to 50+. Actually the teens and low 20's are the hardest. Once you get 29, soloing is easy, occasionally interesting, and a nice change of pace if you get tired of the heal/med routine.
