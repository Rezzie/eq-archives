First I would make sure that melee people are on the rampage list. You have enough healing if you can keep the clerics standing.

One way to do that is as Vindi is running in have a group of melee plink arrows at him so they are on the rampage list rather than a healer. Just make sure that the first warlord is out in front of the plinkers to accept the first melee. You can free up one of the cycle clerics to keep the rampage list people alive.

We also don't start the first CH until the warlord is about 1 to 2 seconds from engaging. Having a CH hit after the tank has only been fighting for 3-5 seconds is rough for healer agro. We rely on all the healers except the 1st and 2nd CH cycle cleric to spam heal until the first CH hits.

Having a spare cleric keep Elixir on the MA and MoR on Vindi helps too. Make sure all melee have low agro weapons until it's their turn to tank. We lost one time when warlord #2 got agro when his willsapper procced about 5 seconds into the fight.

Vanya

Edit: Don't forget to stack any damage shield you can. Support your shaman and have all the MA's drink a DS potion before their turn. I think mage, potion, bard and MoR all stack. With how many times Vindi hits DS damage can really add up.

Apologise if any of this is obvious but here goes.

We normally fight vindi by the ramp on the left side of the arena (as in outside the arena). The pull is long but the reason for it is simple, the mobs close by are charmable - the ones in the arena are not.

You can try and time it perfectly so that you will not get any adds during the fight, but chances are you will get some - so its best to plan for that and get your enchanters to deal with anything that adds.

Vindi fight with 30 ish people should be around 3-6 mins dependant on the damage output. Delay is highly dependant on the quality of the tank but as others have said a 4 second chain with a 3 second post defensive should be more than sufficent, with 5 clerics in the chain you should be able to survive for that amount of time without too much problems.

Bard with tank group is essential - the DS + bard epic effect really help hold taunt. As vanya said getting agro due to procing weapons is an issue however there is a way that my guild deals with that. The primary tank is the only one to engage until he is happy that taunt can be maintained throughout the entire fight - it is not a set time as in 10 seconds or 20 seconds but a proc or two with a willsapper will let him call other people in.

As with any big named rampager, you have to be very careful indeed. You need to work on assigning rampage damage to someone and making it hit them. Its a difficult job specially since no one seems to truelly understand rampage agro, but needless to say you have to have tank number 2 between the clerics and the MT, and make sure that someone is on rampage heal.

One thing that often kills clerics is monk or any FD pulling. As was mentioned on another thread - a monk or whatever pulling a mob into camp then promptly FD'ing is not the right idea at all. It is probably why alot of people call for all casters to stand up, because all agro in camp is transfered onto the mob if its hatelist suddenly becomes empty.

A way round this, especially with agressive healing mobs is monk tanking at the start. The monk pulls and runs to where the MT is, hits stone stance and starts bashing it silly. Whilst the MT is doing likewise.

During this time the monk recieves no healing whatsoever. When the monk feels it prudent to FD, he does so and agro transfers onto the warrior.

Control the agro so that all melee hits are on the MT, all rampages are on the assigned person, and that pops/roamers are controlled via charm and you should be ok.

2) There is no reason to worry about healing agro cause there is none (although a cleric can sometimes get on the rampage list which = bad obviously, but thi seems to happen extremely rarely - i've only seen it once & vindi stomped the cleric & we just tightened the chain up hehe). 4) Dont bother switching tanks - its harder than just speeding up the ch rotation & my personal opinion is it takes more mana cause the fight becomes much longer due to the loss of damage during the switch.

5) Dont bother trying to work battle rezes into your tactics - vindi plows through people too quick for this to be viable. If a few people die early in the fight & there is an extra cleric with an epic, go ahead & drag & rez them, but dont put any emphasis on it.

btw, you've got the tactics down to kill him if you can get him under like <85%, which you obviously have done - the *only* thing you need to do is add dps or extra cleric mana (mod rods, another cleric, necro whatever) & you will kill him np.

Gallerus, it is entirely possible to agro vindi onto you due to lack of agro by the MT, as others have said non main tanks have got agro with lucky (or should that say unlucky) string of procs with weapons such as epics, willsapper or anything like that. It can and does happen, and will normally mean lights out to whoever gets agro.

Vindi doesn't agressively agro healing when you stand up, but meditating whilst will not get you melee agro will definitely put you on the rampage list - which is exactly what you wish to avoid, since it only takes one quad 700 to put most clerics into frenzy range and you will typically get summoned.

Starting the chain early is not a bad thing. All it means is that CH's are incoming to the MT at a set time - there is no rule that you have to follow through with those CH's. Warping can cause an issue I must admit, but in zones or situations where warping will not occur, you can judge the need for the CH to hit simply by looking around. You can go through 2 cycles of CH's and not use any mana (other than that lost in fizzles), but if the named does suddenly appear, the tank shouldn't die.
