Places my characters have gone through the 30s...

1. Cazic-Thule: The first real dungeon in the game, in the opinion of many gamers. The experiences you have here will prepare you for the challenges to come in later levels. I've played every class to at least 30, and all of them have wound up here at some point or another in their life.

2. Dalnir: This dungeon is one of the hidden gems of EQ. I went from 36 to 38 here in one day, and left with over 500 PP in loot. That should tell you about how good this dungeon is if you are willing to take the risks. Dalnir is a slightly higher level range than CT, starting in the high 20s and going up. Dalnir usually isn't heavily populated with players, but often you will find soloing monks here, monks who are falling-over happy to team up with a healer...if you are having trouble getting a group elsewhere in Kunark, try Dalnir. It's not disappointed me yet.

3. Nurga: It's in the level range, but not usually heavily populated. Getting a group could be a problem.

4. Frontier Mountains: Plenty of wanderers in the 30s level range, and of course there's the giant fort. I've taken characters from 28-34 at the giant fort, it just takes some patience. The risk is relatively low and the loot is good. You can often get a partner and pull wanderers to one of the zones for decent XP.

5. The Overthere: Low 30s is a good place to start here...it's the next logical step once you've outgrown (or gotten bored with) the Frontier Mountains. You can stay here into the low 40s...just don't expect a lot of loot. There's a tower with three sarnak spawns over by the WW zone that makes a good small-group camp.

6. Warslik's Woods: Speaking of WW, this zone is much like FM except it's even harder to navigate...you literally can't see the forest for the trees. Several goblin camps and a giant fort here provide really nice loot. Dalnir is located in this zone BTW.

7. Ocean of Tears: Several of the islands here have critters that are good into the low 30s.

8. Rathe Mountains: Bind youself just inside Lake Rathe, and then zone in. You are now in prime cyclops hunting territory, all you need is a melee partner. If you're more inclined to solo, giant skeletons are still blue into the low 30s. Don't forget to wander over to the undead ruins near Feerrott and pick up the ingot for the Caduceus of Sacrament.

9. Sol A: Good hunting into the low 40s, depending on where you go. Just watch out for the trains. Also, finding a group could be a problem these days...

10. Iceclad Ocean: The loot here sucks serious wind, but the XP is good. Get a partner or two and hunt snow cougars, dire wolves and snow dervishes. If you're feeling daring, venture into the lower levels of the Tower of Frozen Shadow. I don't recommend going above the second floor without a strong group though.
