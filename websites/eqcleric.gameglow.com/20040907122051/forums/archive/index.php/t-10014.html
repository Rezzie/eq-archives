---

I'm not going to bother posting this on the Druid Boards. Half the time I feel like I'm watching Jerry Springer when I read those posts, the rest of the time I'm torn between feeling like a masochist for not pressing the little 'x' in the upper right corner or find myself agreeing with the flickering spark of rational thought most people are ignoring or pass over. Some people bring up valid points I agree with, most do not.

I don't know about every class in the game, but as a raid leader I do as much research into the classes as I can, so I know how to effectively place/use them in a given situation/event/raid. I will never know every class intimately, nor do I claim to. So don't presume I am or I will label you incompotent and further look down on you, as I will explain a little later.

Druids do need a boost. I have one in my regular EXP group and I can tell you that when I can channel through a heal for the same amount a 56 Druid can as a 57 Paladin, there's a serious problem there. Paladins are Hybrids, PART healer. We shouldn't be healing comparable to a Priest Class of the same level. Our group heal is one thing, we needed a generic beneficial raid ability other than DS/BMB, but direct heal is something else. Know what makes it even more pathetic? We get Superior Heal at 57 but get our previous healing spell, Greater Heal, at 39. We have to wait 18 levels for our next heal, which is then followed by the best group heal in the game a level later and the level after that with a great HoT spell. When Paladins get shafted for a healing spell for 18 levels and get one just as good as a Druid's of equal level that's pretty sad and speaks volumes for the necessity of a Druid revamp. (I made this comparison so that people could get an UNDERSTANDING of how badly Druids need their heals fixed not because Paladins need to be nerfed, as I'm sure more than one person was thinking about as they read this.)

If you want to fix them, give Druids a HoT that stacks with all other HoTs (Cleric, Paladin, Shaman's Torpor ... notice that this is a new fresh idea? Creativity does wonders in this day and age and it gives merit that you're not just ripping something else off out of jealousy but looking for ways to improve YOUR class). Give them the best DS in the game--by a long shot. Give them the best group/single target regen. Give Druids the option of coming up with a list of new ideas and look it over to pick something out ... but unfortunately because of a few, this is never going to happen. Druids have shot themselves in their collective foot. Again, I will elaborate on this in a moment.

I have to apologize here, but if Druids want to be a Priest class then why are they calling for a better nuke? The last time I checked Priest Classes weren't supposed to have super nukes because they can heal. That's why Casters get all the good nukes. They can't heal. It's called Balance. (Before some triggerhappy reader decides to flame this comment because they skimmed the post, take the time to read the entire 'essay' or just read the previous paragraph and let it sink in, then try and take this all in context.)

Go ahead and flame me, but before you do, think about this: with as many druids rallying behind the CH/Manaburn post do you honestly expect to be taken seriously? You're asking for two spells/abilities that are class-defining. If you want your class defined, think up something ORIGINAL and UNIQUE to your own class. Don't ask for Manaburn and then call it Scorched Earth. Don't ask for Complete Heal and then call it Tears of Tunare or something else equally cute. And before you flame this, there was nothing to label the CH/Manaburn post as sarcastic or something to be taken as a joke.

I'm not tyring to be mean, but logical. As an English Major and writer, if someone came up to me and said, "Hey Jack! Yes, Druids need a revamp.
