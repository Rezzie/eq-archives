I agree, Korn, I also use Root almost exclusively. The main reason, however, is that I solo about 80% of my time. Root is perfectly suited to soloing in most cases.

- It's casting time is second only to Immo, necessary when rerooting mobs that are charging you.
- It's casting cost is nice when you're needing to reroot many times in a battle from mobs breaking after every DD or two.
- It's duration is moot considering I've never seen a mob hold out for more than four DD's.

The magic check is occasionally a problem, but I haven't noticed Immo to stick so much easier that the increased mana is warrented.

The only time I can see PE being useful in soloing is when I'm OOM and need to med for another nuke or two. But even then, three roots almost equal a full duration PE.

As far as grouping goes, I still use root in a well rounded group, but mostly because it always happens to be up. I agree with the previous poster who suggested that Immo is very useful when there is not snarer present.

For raiding and grouping without a mezzer, I use PE if I remember to have it up, for the long park times.

As for root parking, there is something else to think about:

The duration of root varies on the magic resist of the mob more than anything else. Paralyzing earth will give you potential for the longest duration of root. But one also has to remember that the chance of PE running the full duration without magic resist debuff is, at best, debatable.

Also, look at spdat.eff Gello posted. PE and immobilize share same type of duration formula, while enstill and immobilize have a different one. While I don't profess to know the difference between duration formulas 2 and 8, there is a strong possibility that there exists some type of difference. I almost always use immobilize - but I really haven't played my cleric since epitaph of life came out. Nonetheless, I'd probably use immobilize for regular root needs, plain old root for root/nuke, and PE if I hope I can root something long enough for me to camp out.
