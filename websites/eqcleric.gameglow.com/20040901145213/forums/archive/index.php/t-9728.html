Finally hit 44 yesterday and did some testing with my friend who's a 50 druid. More specifically I'm talking about a Druids Skin like nature vs a Clerics Resolution buff and HP/AC buffs in general.

Well my friend is a level 50 druid and has told me, along with many other people that clerics are the best at buffing and healing...I've heard this since day 1. Hell they've even told me a while ago that our Resolution Buff outdoes any HP/AC buff in the game (well this was quite some time ago when they said that --maybe things have changed). Well I received my new level 44 spells yesterday and experimented a bit with the new buffs.

The Druid Skin like nature spell adds more HP/AC than the Cleric Resolution HP/AC buff. I even spoke to a guide if there were bonuses based on the level of the caster for Resolution and they said no. (Too bad I couldn't find a 50 cleric to cast reso on me to test it out). Maybe there's something I'm missing....So it brings me to the following:

Now the break down...

Mana Costs:
Skin like Nature (Level 49 druid): 300 mana
Resolution (Level 44 Cleric): 110 mana

Are we the best at HP/AC buffing ? Our single HP/AC buff falls short of being the best, but it is more mana effiecient. Does this make our buff the best? To consider ourselves the best at buffing as some people proclaim, we have to add in the following factors/spells.

On top of resolution, at minimum:

Symbol of Pinzarn (Level 39): Costs 200 mana, casting it takes a jasper costing about 8gp and change. A pure HP buff.

Symbol of Naltron (Level 44): 315 mana, costs 11pp and change per cast a pure HP buff that gives more HPs than Pinzarn.

Armor of Faith (Level 39): Pure AC buff, 150 mana.

Shield of Words (Level 49): Pure AC buff, 300 mana.

To actually have the best "final HP/AC buff" at one given time, we have to stack our main HP/AC buff with additional HP and AC buffs. We need to add a symbol buff and armor buff.

So lets consider this setup:
We start with our basic HP/AC buff:

Resolution: 110 mana, HP/AC Buff

We add an AC buff:
Armor of Faith: 150 mana, AC buff, OR Shield of Words: 300 mana, AC buff.

We add an HP buff:
Symbol of Pinzarn: 200 mana, HP buff costs 8gp and change
OR Symbol of Naltron 315 mana, HP buff costs 11pp and change.

Basically these 3 buffs can range from 460 mana to 725 mana, and you are out the cost of either 8gp or 11 pp.

So does this really back up the claim that clerics are the best HP/AC buffing class in the game?

After hearing things from different people throughout my EQ lifespan and seeing things for myself, my personal view is that clerics have great HP/AC buffs but that doesn't make them the best buffers in the game.

To truly be the best at the HP/AC buffing, we would have to use all 3 lines of our buffing spells and as you know, you basically have to pay through the nose to be the best at it (mana wise and PP wise --don't even start the debate that money means nothing ah higher levels, because for some of us it does).

From a practical standpoint, who spends 500-700+ mana buffing someone up? (Unless of course you are off to PoF). Some of these buffs cost a ridiculous amount of mana, not to mention gem cost. These buffs are a mana nightmare if you have a few tanks in your party.

My opinion is that just because we have the best HP/AC buffs in the game when combined does not mean we are the best at, from a practicality standpoint. If we were to be the best at HP/AC buffs, our spells would be efficient and feasible to use. True, if we stack all 3 there is no match when it comes to HP/AC --we are the best in the sense that our spells can give the most HP/AC than any other class. From a practical standpoint however, is this the best ? My answer is no --the huge mana costs and pp cost for us to give the best HP/AC buffs is impractical and thats the reason why I believe we aren't the best at buffing. If we were the best, I would expect a little more mana efficiency and maybe something better then gems for casting (maybe something like a quest item you need to have in order to cast things such as symbol, but not the repeated buying of gems.)
