I had something very sad happen last night and I wanted to relate it you you all and ask a question.

I was arriving at lower guk and got a tell from a res friend.

You know, a res friend. One of those folks who calls on you for resses because you ressed him once, they always exchange buffs and such and is really nice and polite but you only know them from ressing, not from ever grouping with them. You know, a res friend. *chuckle*

Anways, this res friend sends a tell. He got killed at the lord room duing a big Dar pull and wanted to ask if he could get a res. He mixed in a little complaint that he died with two cleircs in the group. I asked about it and apparently he had two 40+ cleircs in the group and both went OOM from nuking and could not heal him while he (a chanter) got pounded into dust towards the end of the fight.

I thought this was strange and started heading down to res him.

When I arrived I find that his group has two clerics in the cleirc robes from Kunark. So I ressed him and sat next to the group a while to med up and check on a few friends to find a group for the evening.

The two cleircs did indeed dump what appeared to be all of their mana on nuking every fight. It was interesting to watch. They dressed up in robes and were acting like a couple of mages.

Anyways, I was a little suprised that anyone could die in such a controlled situation because both clerics were nuking full time with no reserve for healing. I found it disheartening.

The reason I found it disheartening is that this type of event is exactly what gives the negative "Battle Cleirc" image to most folks. Clerics can so a lot more than just heal, we are incredible sources of utility spells (stuns, roots, soem nukes, etc). But it has to be targeted to always be focused on keeping the group alive.

Anyways, I was just curious, is the "Robed Cleric" the semi-official "I'm going to act like a wizard" standard now? If you see a robe, don't expect a heal?
