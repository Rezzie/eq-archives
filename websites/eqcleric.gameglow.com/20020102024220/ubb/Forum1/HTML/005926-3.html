I'm a 56 cleric and although this is likely redundant here's my words o wisdom.

Post-50 WIS is almost useless. You're gonna want maybe 150-175 or so but its almost meaningless beyond that. 200 is too much. The reason is more wis will only give you an extra X mana. As in lets say I have 2500 mana w/ 200 wis or 2200 w/ 185. I could do the math but why bother, this is hypothetical.

I spend 1500 mana during a huge fight. 200 wis: I have 1000 mana left and 1500 to med. 185 wis: 700mana left and 1500 to med. Having the extra 15 wis did nothing to help me. The only time that extra wis matters is when you need to spend ALL your mana. If you have to spend that much you're likely over your head level-wise or someone in your group is a moron and it isn't your fault.

I currently have 189 wis and I'll be going down soon. Buffed up I have 1000 AC exactly and, to be perfectly honest, it isn't nearly enough. Next level I get Bulwark of Faith which will raise it to 1006 and a friend is selling me a Donal Bracer soon which will make it 1015 and that's not enough. Going from EM to PoG BP will be 1034 and that's not enough. PoG legs too and that's 1048. I wouldn't consider this enough either.

Basically it's like this:

Levels 1-25: Concentrate on +mana, with a slight emphasis on wis, str, and ac. During these levels you can still melee and your AC is starting to bud. By 20, start raising your wis a bit.
26-40: Wis starts to become key. AC is making more and more of a role but its still not enough to sacrifice wis for. Forget STR past encumberment at this point, you're a purple couch potato now.
41-50: AC becoming increasingly important. You can begin to drop WIS to 190-195 now in order to get some AC and resists.
51-55: AC and resists rule supreme. Get some decent resists and have at least 180 wis but past that everything is AC.
56-60: Get the minimum resists. Get resist sets for raids. Screw WIS down to as low as 175. Get AC til you can't get anymore.

With more AC you take less melee damage. You are often the only decent healer in a group so you have to save yourself heal-wise. If you die quickly the group is dead. An extra 800 mana is useless in comparison to a drastic reduction in dmg taken.

I will be trying for PoG armor to up AC and when I get 59/60 going to be going for Templars. By 59 I won't be the least bit surprised if my WIS drops to 175 or below seeing as I don't have and likely won't ever get epic.

Hope that helped.

If you are a cleric that goes on alot of high end raids, AC and HP gear is essential, along with resist gear when situation calls for it. As much as you try to avoid it, clerics will get aggroed and take a pounding. The good clerics with high ac and high hp gear will tend to survive, the low ac wisdom clerics will drop like rocks. Sure in fights where it is only one mob, or the others are mezzable, and the tanks can easily control aggro, than ac doenst matter as much. However that doesnt happen all the time in high end raids. You will have situations where there will be extra mobs that are Unmezzable, and will aggro on you for healing. Or you might just have to heal too early or too much and Uber_mob_01 will aggro on you. Not much to me is more satisfying when I channel through some serious dmg to DA, look at my hp and see Im at half a bubble of life than. That is tangible evidence that HP and AC do matter. Nothing bothers me more than to see a cleric die quickly in situations where there is no reason for them to die.

Anyway right now my raid gear is something like 207 wisdom, 1086 ac, and 2900~ self buffed. Ideally I would like to see my HP go to 3050-3100 self buffed, but thats alot of +HP gear to get
