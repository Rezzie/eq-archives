So lots of us are fed up with clericing...myself included. I haven't played my cleric in...um, at least a month. Maybe longer.

A big reason for people's frustration is the repetition and boredom of higher level clerics, yes? Well wizards do the same thing every fight. So do magicians. Monks. Warriors. EVERY class. What's the difference?

Well, I was reading the State of the Cleric post on Whineplay, and someone came up with what I think is an awesome idea that is cheesy, but would help lots. NUMBERS.

Cast a complete heal...it goes off, "Woohoo! I healed 2500 hit points this time, mwahahaha".

What do you all think of his? It won't change our gameplay at all. But seeing how much we heal for every time we heal would add an element of, I dunno...excitement, I guess? Competition?

What do you think?

I like your idea Bipplin. I also wouldn't mind having a hard number for mana and health. I hate trying to judge if I have just enough mana to cast a gheal, popping up to cast and finding that I really didn't have enough.

I would like to know, just from a mana efficiency standpoint, how many hit points my heals are healing on the tank. Am I healing too early? Would a group heal be more mana efficient? It wouldnt' have too big of an impact on actual effectiveness but it would be nice.
