I'm only 38th, but got two quick comments:

Here's the Nth vote for getting rid of Gate. Back in the late twenties, I was advised by an experienced cleric to lose it. I was skeptical, but did so. Since then (and it's been nearly 20 levels) I have never regretted that decision. The one time I needed gate (when I got trained out of an unfamiliar dungeon and couldn't find the zone), I DA'ed and sat down to mem it. DA is an anti-taunt anyway, so I was able to get the gate off afterward where I couldn't get it before.

Here's my usual spell lineup:

2 Divines (only because they can't be swapped out; in a relatively safe situation where I need lots of spells, I sometimes lose DB in favor of another spell)
Greater and Superior heal
At least one stun (the lowest level one), if fighting stunnable creatures
Complete heal (if fighting with heavy tanks mid-40's and higher)
Undead DD, if fighting mostly undead; or, very rarely, general-purpose DD
The rest will be utility spells (root, fear, extinguish fatigue, etc) - or if not used, fill up the slots with stuns. I don't nuke much, but I am a chainstunning maniac.

My most common setup now is 2 divines, 3 heals, 2 stuns, and a DD. I swap out others as needed.
