Fizzle rates are spell specific. The level of the spell has nothing to do with fizzle rate. My experience with the conjuration spells proves this.

At about 100 conjuration 40ish specialized.

Level 54 Unswerving Hammer of Faith 0 fizzles ever
Level 29 Abundant Food 70%+ fizzle rate

It's clearly something independent of spell level that is causing this to happen. The only explanation is that the fizzle rate of each spell is built into the spell definition. There may be other modifying factors but they are tiny compared to the effect of the defined fizzle rate. This is consistent with all evidence.

Why does Divine Light fizzle a lot? Because it was designed that way. Being maxxed out in skill is not going to help you all too much with this. Being higher level is not going to help too much as your skills are capped anyway.

To return to the questions:
- How can Reckoning (54 Evocation) fizzle less than Remedy (51 Alteration)?

Because the base fizzle percentage for Reckoning is significantly lower than that for Remedy.

- How can Reckoning (54 Evocation) fizzle the same as Celestial Healing (44 Alteration)?

Because the fizzle rates in the spell definitions are similar

- How can Heroic Bond (51 Abjuration) fizzle less than Remedy (51 Alteration)?

See above re: Reckoning and Remedy

- Divine Light fizzle percentages are ungodly.

True.

- Heroism (51 Abjuration) and Heroic Bond (51 Abjuration), are miles apart in fizzle rates.

Here's the clincher. Two spells virtually identical, same level nearly same effect same cleric yet one fizzles much more than the other. By any other theory this should not happen but if we assume the fizzle rates are defined in each spell we can explain this easily. Occam's razor and previous experience with Verant lead me to believe that this is just another situation where the right hand (which set fizzle rate on Heroism) had no idea what the left hand (which set fizzle rate on Heroic Bond) was doing.

Since this issue is a dwarf compared to a good number of outstanding issues I will confidently predict that it will never be addressed further.

I think that if further tests are run marking the WIS of the caster casting the same spells at varying WIS you will find that increasing WIS is the only viable way of reducing fizzle rates on undependable spells.
