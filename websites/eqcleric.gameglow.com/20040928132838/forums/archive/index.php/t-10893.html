Main tanking technique is the only way I have ever seen some serious high end mobs (Velk, CT) drop. Also works well on other strong mobs like the general in kith forest. Had a raid that tried traditional raid-style attacks on the general and he basically wiped 45 people from the map. 15 people left, and the remaining 30 switched to main tanking and took out 2 generals easily.

It keeps tanks alive long enough for that next CH to hit, and if a main tank goes down the time it takes to switch to the next can be very costly.

Umm, don't use macros with delays in them. If you start a macro with a /pause in it and fizzle the spell, you are locked down from recasting it till the /pause time is up.

One recommendation I have for setting the agro on a MT is to collect frost giant toes and have a shamen make stacks of potions for them. I forget the name of the potion, but its effectivly a 300 pt self heal. One common thing we do is CE the MT and start a CH on him a few seconds before he engages, he then rips through a few charges of the potion and everyone else engages. By the time the first CH lands and everyone engages, agro is nicely set on the MT.

Timing of the heals and smothly switching from one tank to the next is something that will take you some practice.

Greetings!

We use a variety of healing-chain macros. The basic format is like this:

/ooc CH on %t
/cast 2
/pause 35
/ooc Joebob start CH

Before a fight we announce the healing order and fast and slow delays (e.g. Heal order Thistle Joebob Jimbob Bettyjo delay 35 slow delay 60). Clerics modify the pause delay and succeeding cleric portion of their hotkeys.

The fast delay hotkey is used until the mob is slowed (if slowable), then the slow delay hotkey is used to conserve mana.

If a cleric fizzles, is out of range, or is interrupted they announce that and the next cleric begins their heal early.

If the main tank does not have aggro then the clerics duck out of the heal at 2 seconds (one casting bubble) remaining.

We normally have four clerics in the healing chain, with any other clerics attending to their groups, spot healing, or ready to replace a fallen cleric in the heal chain. For some very high damage mobs (like Lord Vyemm) we will use more clerics with a very short delay time.

I get spell recast not yet met messages occaisonally with no delay between casting attempts, so I usually leave a /pause 2 in my macros inbetween the announcement and the recast.

I also don't like timers in the heals, mainly due to lag and the fact they lock you in for the duration. Besides, a lot of being a good cleric is instinct and timing, recognizing when to play it safe, when to push the envelope and when to be a good little automaton. Timers are too rigid and inflexible, they lead to laziness and they cause confusion during those encounters when things inevitably go bad. If you're going to use timers, you may as well just calculate how long you're going to go between heals and setup a /cast 1, /pause 170, /cast 1, /pause 170, /cast 1... hotkey.
