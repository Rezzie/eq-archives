I'm still in my free trial period of EQ. I decided early on that I wanted to play a healing/priest class so I played various characters until deciding that I had the most fun with my gnome cleric. To give an idea of what I've played and the experiences, here are the others:

- Dwarf Cleric to lvl 9 (This was a cakewalk! Easiest of all the ones I tried)

- Iksar Shaman to lvl 8 (Iksars are the easiest to start with - money is everywhere! Biggest problem is that the creatures seem tougher than in other places)

- Gnome Cleric to lvl 8 (Nothing to hunt! What do I do! Ouch, never enough mana!)

- Dark Elf Cleric to lvl 3 (over fifteen deaths to get to level 3 in that forest!)

- Barbarian Shaman to lvl 3 (Neat character, but Iksar was just so much easier)

These may seem trivial to the experienced player, but for a newbie like me there are a lot of things that have to be learned (like removing the mapping on the "a" key so I don't accidently attack the merchants!)

The Dwarf Cleric was the easiest. The Gnome and Dark Elf cleric were the hardest. I eventually chose to go with the gnome cleric simply because she is so much fun!

The gnome cleric I ran into a problem between levels 5 and 6. I couldn't find enough things to fight in the Steamy mountains. I spent three days (about four hours game play each day) just killing spiders for the silk and skelletons for the weapons and bone chips. I sold the weapons, tailored the silk, and thought I'd sell the chips to necromancers who might need them. Well, I never found a necromancer that needed bone chips, so I just put the 120+ chips into my vault.

On recommendation from other players, I went and fought orcs in the Elfie forest. Best Advice ever! I gained level 7 quickly, and had enough orc belts for a quest in Kaladim (Dwarf town) to make a run over there.

While I was there, I remembered that I had all of those bone chips I'd saved and that there was a quest here for bone chips. Wow! I gained an entire level and eight platinum and a bunch of armor just from turning in those bone chips! Now my little gnome cleric is level 8 with meditate and it goes a lot easier . My biggest problem now is simply concerving mana.

Note: Gained the ENTIRE level 7 to 8 from bone chips alone, this is not an exaggeration. Didn't fight anything, just turned in bone chips. I'd just turned level 7 with barely any blue when I came to the Dwarf city). This has to be the best quest for a newbie! Good items, experience and it's easy!

Well, that about sums up what I wanted to post - what classes were easiest for a newbie and the bone chip quest. It's difficult as a gnome cleric, but it's getting better!

Hi there, I realize you are new... so this stuff may not seem as easy as some of us who know our ways around blindfolded...

Starting a Dwarf, Gnome, or High Elf cleric really gives you access to all the same hunting grounds.... you say a dwarf is easiest... in reality... they are all really the same at that level... there's not enough difference in stats to make a difference... BUT... as you said, the hunting grounds make the difference... I personally do not like Steamfont for low levels, but find it nice for 9-12ish. So for any of those 3 clerics, you can easily fight in the same areas... you just have to walk to them.

I speak from recent experience on this... if you want an easy life early on, really... Iksar Shaman (since you said priest/healer). There are some easy quests for xp/coin/faction... bone chips to a field of bone guard... hagle baron scaled wolf pets (massive xp, armor, and I think coin). Why is it so easy? Regen on the Iksar, pure and simple. Regeneration *may* not mean a heck of a lot to keeping you alive at higher levels... but at lower levels, regen is divine.

if you want a cleric? My advice is Halfling... I think it's an easier life than the dwarf, you get hide (like Dark Elves) and you get Sneak (who says you need a rogue/monk to CR Vox, I did it). Stats are similar to the dwarf... better/worse in different areas... try it out... Misty thicket is great... stay behind the wall 1-5, and outside the wall 5-10.

There's a lot of choice in clerics... different strengths and weaknesses... none of them suck (dwarves stink tho... no baths in Kaladim I hear).... some may hurt for certain stats early on (gnomes and high elves are kinda weak for example), but later in life, equipment can overcome all weaknesses. If you go cleric, choose the one the appeals to you most... sometimes the newbie area sucks, but remember... it's just that... a newbie area... once you leave it, you will not return except to bank.

I will recommend against being a "evil" cleric (dark elf) if this is your very first time playing... I'm sure you could handle it... but being KoS in certain areas really turns some people off... but you CAN fix your faction for being evil if the Dark Elf appeals to you... many Dark Elf clerics are quite happy.

If you want to go shaman... you have fewer race choices... however, they are actually quite varied in plus/minus. I'll leave it up to you to go to a shaman board and get exact details if you want them... but here is a basic rundown...

Troll: evil, high melee stats, lowest wisdom (your mana), regeneration.

Ogre: evil, highest melee stats in game, you cannot be stunned from a front melee attack (less spell interruptions)

Barbarian: good faction, 2nd highest starting wisdom, levels faster than the rest.

Iksar: very evil (only enter your home city unless you work faction), highest wisdom, lowest melee stats, regeneration, small armor class bonus.

I personally play an Iksar... I like it... but I have a Halfling Cleric which is my main so I wanted something very different in terms of race/location. If you do go to a shaman board (The Shamans Crucible is a good place, I'm sorry I dont have the URL - anyone?)... don't get caught up in item discussions.... there's a few high level items that certain shamans cannot use... don't worry about it... if you are a good shaman, you are a good shaman regardless of any one item... that logic also goes for clerics of course.

One healing class you left out was the druid... to each thier own... just be warned that druids automatically get a bad reputation when they are born, generally out of player envy or bad experiences... but if the druid appeals to you... just stick it out, and do whatever your job is well.

Finally, I recommend that you dont level on quests! You will not max all your skills... and regret it later.

Gnome clerics can be more equipment-dependant than other races because of their low base Wisdom, but they really are one of the more unique combinations - off the top of my head, I can only think of 2 higher-level gnome clerics on my server that I know by name.

Halflings stink. Who cares about stats - hairy feet are nasty. *runs and hides before Thistleburr can catch him* ;>

If you enjoy playing healer-type classes in general, then Clerics, Druids, or Shaman all work at lower levels. At high levels, tho, it's pretty much all about Clerics - just something to keep in mind for the long term. => Personally, Shaman are one of my favorite classes in the game, and I still think they're one of the most powerful...but I couldn't play one. After playing my Cleric for 52 levels now, I'd go absolutely insane trying to play a healer class without Complete Heal. My wife tells me I'm spoiled. ;p

Levelling on quests is fine as long as you don't make a habit out of it. The bone chip quest does rock, tho, doesn't it? ;> It only becomes a real problem for the people who used Tumpy Tonics to get from 1st to 30th in a day; a level or two here or there from questing is a nice way to get ahead.

If you're already in the Butcherblock area, I'd suggest finding the golden tower with the Dwarven Skeletons near it - you can mop up the skeletons there and at the nearby druid ring pretty easily. Once they're green, you can head back to Steamfont; near the rusted gears, there are 5 or 6 skeletons that spawn, around level 9-11th or so, and can all be pulled singly. Sit up on the wall to med and rest safely inbetween pulls, and you can coast through to level 12 or 13. Then you take the 8,684 bone chips you've gotten and head back to Kaladim... =>

Steamfont is good for levels 1 - 14. After 7th, kobolds stop giving experience, however if you head past the path that leads into Lesser Faydark, and just roam out in the grass, there are many pumas, skeletons, lesser ebon drakes, and the dreaded disease-ridden plague rats (which are perfect for clerics who have Cure Disease spell). That grassy area will take you to about double digit level (10ish).

From there, hunting near the minotaurs caves (or even inside of the caves) are feasible. Just beware the Minotaur Hero, or the level 20s/30s characters who pull the traitorous Meldrath and his minions out of the caves. That area is doable up until level 14.

Halfling clerics are imho the easiest to play. Hide (real useful for meditating when looking at the book), sneak (great for quests and buying from dubious merchants), lighter armor, nice round starting stats, great newbie zone (misty thicket is best newbie zone in old world imho), cultural tailoring if you're into that, 5% racial xp bonus, and not as common as the billions of dwarf/high elf clerics running around. If you plan on staying with EQ up to lvl 50+ lemme make a couple suggestions:
1. Shaman = way cool at beginning, then simply a utility class at higher levels. ie. stand, slow mob, DOT, sit, stand, buff, repeat.
2. Cleric's can be very boring. Group with ppl alot in the very beginning. If healing really gets you off, then by all means you got what it takes. If you get bored early on, just try another class. Clerics require alot of patience and fortitude to play. I guarantee you will HATE playing a cleric at high levels if you dislike it at low lvls.
3. Try a wizard. Cleric offensive spell casting is alot like Wiz.
4. Try a paladin, If you like the melee+spells aspect of the low level cleric, you may just be more suited for a Paly or even SK.

You're just starting....better to try it all now than to end up with a high level toon that you don;t enjoy playing. I've seen it happen far too many times.
