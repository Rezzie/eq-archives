This angers me as it uses up my mana on him, when it belongs to the tank....bottom line, if it comes down to it, the tank is what will keep the group alive, not the wizard so I have stopped healing him when he throws out those nukes and gets aggroed. Does this not make sense? I mean, I'm only lvl 26, but I've played the game since its beeen out just about and I don't think I'm being unreasonable here.

I had the same problem with a wizard I grouped with. Instead of trying to come at it from the mana stand point.
Try coming at it from the stand point that if he over nukes and agros the mob on him the tank has a harder time doing his job.
If an int caster agros the mob the warrior has to turn and adjust to keep on the mob this takes time away from the tank doing what he is suppose to be doing. Also if the int caster happens to be one that runs around when he does this the tank has an even harder time cause the mob is constantly moving out of range. As for not healing the wizard while it might feel good to do this I would not neccesarly let him die but let him get real low and use your lower heals.

I had a series of discussions with a very good friend of mine on this very issue.

Ultimately, in Droga, I told him that he could do whatever he wanted. We were getting multiple pulls. I then told him the order I was healing in:

Chanter
Tank
Self
Druid/evaccer
anyone else that was there
Wizard/overaggroingnuker

He stopped that day. He manages aggro wonderfully now. It is very difficult when it is a good friend. You might also try not buffing the wiz. Those int casters get their hp doubled or more by our buffs. Makes em feel like they can tank.

Good luck, you are being reasonable, doesn't mean he will agree with you.
