on 58 Cleric (cause it varies with level):

Here's my usual setup. I use heals on the right (even numbers) and utility things on the left (odd numbers).

Incidently, yes I use "/say" for all my heals. It makes it so I don't have to switch out my macros for every raid or xp group. Works good when you're saving the day on someone too.

1. Victim
/assist main_assist
/assist

2. Divine Light
/sit off
/say Divine Light --> %T
/cast 1

3. Assist
/assist main_assist

4. Stun
/sit off
/cast 2

5. TarPet
/tar pet_name

6. CH
/sit off
/say Complete Heal --> %T ... Stay in Range!
/cast 3

7. DoMoK
/sit off
/cast 5

8. Celest
/sit off
/say Celestial Heal --> %T (Heal over Time)
/cast 4

9a. Mana
/say OOM!
/say Necros dump mana!
/say Mages drop mod rods!
/say Rangers run away!

9b. Backup
/group BACKUP HEALER -- YOU'RE UP!!!

10. depends

I use ctrl-S to sit and ctrl-B for spellbook.

-=-=-=-=-=- Cleric is AFK -=-=-=-=-=-

I have always mapped autoattack to the backspace key.

You'll never hit it first off by accident!

And I don't use number keys or anything for clicking. I have hotkeys set up but I click em, it's far faster for me than trying to break old habits.

BIG RAID TIP:

In the full screen with overlay mode, use alt-M to open up the socials menu.

I have an entire socials bank with just /target hotkeys. I have one for every warrior in the guild and some others.

My hotkey bank is also open to healing hotkeys.

This way, when they say "Tank01 defensive down in 15 seconds." I can swap over to the new tank without having to type.

And if I have a sec, and someone other than the main tank needs a heal, I don't have to target them, i just click thier hotkey.
