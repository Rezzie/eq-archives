OK, By now most people know that the Paw of Opolla has been moved to Upper Guk. Having gotten three of the four rings on the weekend, here is my initial notes. I am looking for confirmation on if Vin Molter and Gynock have changed.

To get the Paw you need
-----------------------

The Container - give the Rat Ear Pie and Three bottles of Kalish to Vin Molter in Qeynos. Someone said he is now a higher level Necromancer, and killing him may be harder then you think <--- Unconfirmed

The Copper, Silver, Gold and Platinum rings.

Copper Ring : Turn Bracket Mildew (Rare drop from the Fungus Mutant in Upper Guk - about 1 in 10) to Melaxis, a named Kerran near the old Princess Mitty Castle in Kerra.

Silver Ring : Turn Faerix Spores (Extremly Rare Drop from the Fungus Mutant in Upper Guk - about 1 in 30) to Melaxis, a named Kerran near the old Princess Mitty Castle in Kerra. The Silver ring is also still spawning in the Razorgill pond in Blackburrow every four hours - I would strongly advise getting this as soon as possible

Gold Ring : Turn Degenerated Guk Weed (Very Rare drop dropped from the Fungus Mutant in Upper Guk - about 1 in 20) to Melaxis, a named Kerran near the old Princess Mitty Castle in Kerra.

Platinum Ring : Kill Gynock Molter (Rare spawn in Befallen at entrance; placeholder may spawn every 16 minutes (a rat), or may spawn every 64 minutes (a 'shouter'). As far as I know, this bit hasn't changed <--- Unconfirmed

Faction at Kerra
----------------
Most people will be Apprehensive or Indifferent to the new Kerrans. I have done the following quests to improve my faction.

Kill the 'wharf rat' that spawns in the hut near the docks and get 'A Sharp Tooth' from it. Give this to Thalith Mamluk on the Western dock for some XP, a small faction increase and a necklace of +3 Agility (Lore, Nodrop). I have done this quest about 16 times now, it doesn't seem to shift faction very much.

Or better :

Kill Sentry Creot - a 30th level Paladin who spawns outside the most North Wester hut in Toxulla forest. He uses lay hands, and has about 1300 hps. You will take a major faction hit with Erudin - I started Amiable and after 2 kills I am now dubious. He spawns every six minutes, and is often camped by mid 30's to 40th level Necromancers, due to the rapid spawn time. Turn the head into Shazad Asad in the old Clawknights room, for a major faction change (2 heads got me to Amiable from Apprehensive), and about 4 platinum, and a Sejah Ghulam Bracer (Dex +5, Sta +3, AC 5, No Drop).

Some people say kill the Erudin Emisary - I disagree. Although weaker then the Sentinel, he doesn't always drop a head, so your Erudin Faction may end up getting hurt more.

NOTE
----

It seems that Melixis has some religeon faction also : After 7 heads I am still apprehensive, and nothing I can do can shift it. (On the plus side, the best place for me to sell loot is currently Kerra Isle). I know this affects Mithanial Marr and Tunare worshippers also from a few hours of research on Friday Morning. If she refuses to give you the quest, and insults you, you will not get the ring when you hand the plant in (I tested with a spare mildew)

So, this is how I handed my three plants in :

Stand behind her. Start the trade. Put the first item in the first window. Then get invisibility cast on you, and hit complete. This may not work 100% of the time, but it worked the three times I did it. Both Enchanter Invisibility and Druid Camo worked. This will probably be fixed in the next patch, so better get in quick.

The Fungus Mutant
-----------------
The Fungus mutant spawns in a corner in the fungus farm in Upper Guk. He has about 650 hp's at most (Retribution, Force and Holy Might kills him 80% of the time, same for a single Starfire), and casts a Whirl till you Hurl spell and a poison dot. I'd think he is about mid to high 20th. Resist magic and resist poison work nicely for me at 44th level to avoid embarassing yourself in front of the assembled crowd. He drops plants about 1 in 10 kills, although I have seen 3 from 5 kills drop something.

The Camp : Let's just say the first thing shouted when zoning into upper is no longer 'Ancient Camped'. It's now 'Fungus Camped ?'. I've done this for about 15 hours, and apart from being lied to on the first day ('No, he hasn't dropped anything for the last 30 hours') It's been a pretty nice camp, normally with about 3 to 6 people hanging around

Normally you will be there with high level shamans or druids. 90% of the people doing this will be after the Guk Weed, as this is the gold ring. The best way we came up with is to figure out who needs what, and run three lists (e.g. Player 1 gets the First Guk, Player 2 gets the second guk and the First Mildew, Player 3 gets the second mildew and the first spore).

This way, if someone turns up and demands a share of the spawn, point out that the players have already come to an agreement and they are welcome to join the rotation. Most Guides and GMs will be happy knowing that the players have come to an agreement

Other spawns in the Area : Lots of fungus creatures, roaming and stationary. Expect to kill about 3 roamers to get into the spawn, if uncamped. Each has about 500 hp, and drop rusty, rare bronze, and about a plat. There is one wandering Shin that patrols through the farm, but is easy to avoid. You will totally trash your live side Frogluk faction doing this farm.

Well, that's my information from the last four days. Anyone have more info on changes in Befallen ?

Additional notes :

Fungus Mutant spawns every 18 minutes. He is a static (non-roaming) spawn, and does not pop a placeholder. It does seem that the Guk Weed may drop more on a rate with the Spore, after chatting with a few more people camping it.

Gynock Molter spawned last night an hour before I arrived, and then 45 minutes after I set up camp. That's four times I've run into him, and hopefully the last.

Vin Molter spawned in Qeynos Aqueducts when I arrived, I killed him and he dropped a cloth cord (Faction hits to Qeynos Merchants, Qeynos Guard, Antonius Bayle from memory).

So, I went and bought two Rat Ear pies, 6 bottles of Kalish and went back. I used the Word of Shadow 19th level Area Effect spell to kill the cubes, the rats, the spiders, the snakes and everything around the area where he spawns, (See Map http://2point0.com/EverQuest/images/Maps/qcat2.gif) and he popped again about 40 minutes later.

By which time, I had accidentally eaten the rat ear pies. So, I killed him again. He has about 900 hitpoints, and appears to be a monk. The person I bought the pies from tells me there are two different pies, based on Giant rats or Large rats and neither seem to work. Uncooked pies do not.

Afterwards, I went to the second dock in South Qeynos (The one to Kunark, that has the fisherman on it), dove in, grabbed his fishing box and handed it back. This has the exact opposite faction hits as killing Vin Molter, although I do not know what the degree of shift is. Tumpy kicks the fishing tackle box in once per Everquest day, I believe.

So, holding the Paw in one hand, and my shiny new Quest shield in the other (Thanks Lappen!) I officially am no longer a meleeing cleric.

But I also managed to hit AC 800 in full quest armour and shield with Paw. Wis hurts, but this is my running-around-showing-off-suit, rather then my actual work clothes.

Next : Time to ditch that Bronze Splinted cloak and go Aegis hunting, along with the rest of the Clerical population. (Yes, I am still wearing a piece of Medium Bronze at 45th).
