quote:
1: Woot mobs are in testing halls?

Halls of testing are absolutely insane. There are wurms in there with high level AEs that can rip your raid apart easily. Some of them can gate.
quote:
2: Do me need any special resists to survive there?

Everything I've seen uses a fire or ice based AE (even Acid Raid is cold) so get your FR and CR up well past 150 unbuffed for best results. MR may play a part but I jacked my MR up last time and saw no significant change in resists.
quote:
3: how high must the COV faction be to be able to hunt there?(going through the guardian)

As long as you aren't KOS to CoV, you can walk right past everything up to the Halls of Testing entrance.
quote:
4: woot lvl is recommended?

My own guild has set a bare minimum of 55 for going here since levels play a BIG part in resists. If you accept anyone below 55, make sure they know that they are at a much higher risk of dying.

I would get at least 4 groups of 55+ before even thinking about the HoT area. The AEs hit for up to 300 and some of the wurms can chain cast them. The ONLY way to survive is to beat them down as fast as possible and then do CR (cleric epic is HIGHLY recommended for this zone). Make sure you have a cleric camped for CR.

The optimum number of groups I have found for the halls of testing is 4 groups of level 55+ characters, 58-60 prefered. Less than that and you run into downtime due to lack of mana and you can have problems with some backspawn on roamers and with nameds. Getting 2 nameds that both hit for 525 damage can be a problem if you only have 2 groups.

More than 4 groups and it seems like people leave part of their brain at the zone in, stuff gets pushed around to far, folks don't listen, ext. We actually drop MOBs faster with less downtime using 4 groups than 6 *shrug*.

Fire and cold resistance is most important, magic resistance is secondary. Most of the AEs are only 400 damage max, but due to the number of hp the ancient temple guardians have and the dificulty in preventing the white ones from healing themselves, the AEs can do some damage due to the number of times they get cast.

Once you figure out how to pull the place, its easy. The hardest thing about it is that its so dark that even a DE can't see the oposite wall of a hallway when standing against one wall.

Another thing to watch out for is that the white guardians like to gate. They also are not stunable, so melee bash and moving the MOB are the only way to interupt it. After some problems while learning the place on our first trip, the only time we have had bad wipeouts was due to white guardians gating and summoning people, causing other MOBs to agro.

Oh, another thing, some of the wyverns cast a spell called Thunder Blast on people they are in melee with. This sucks very very much if you are a caster, especially a cleric. Thunder Blast is a hp/mana DoT that will drain your mana at about the same rate you meditate it with c2 on.

Good luck =)

Some spells you'll have to deal with in ToV:

Acid Jet (Area Effect) - 50 DD + 40 per tick DoT, possibly resisted with PR. If it is its pretty easy to resist cause I usually have about 40 PR in there. If not maybe its CR like Boanerges said.

Slime Mist (Single Target) - 25% slow + 40 per tick DoT, dunno how to resist this one.

Thunderblast (Single Target) - 75 hp/mana per tick DoT. Cured with level 60 enchanter Gift of Brilliance. Possibly cured with Gift of Insight as well.

The red wurms have Area Effect fire-based rains. The red wurms also hve no mana.

The White wurms have Area Effect cold-based rains. They also complete heal and gate

Black drakes cast acid jet.
Red drakes have fire breath, max 100 damage
Blue drakes cast thunderblast, complete heal and gate.
Shimmering drakes/Green drakes dont have any breaths. One of these or both cast slime mist, I can't remember.

Have your enchanters and bards mana drain blue drakes, shimmering drakes, white wurms for the entire fight and you shouldn't have any problems with gaters.
