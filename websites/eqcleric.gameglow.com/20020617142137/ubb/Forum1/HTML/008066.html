At level 60, most melees can get gear that allows them to effectively solo XP mobs with minimal downtime. The same can be said of most of the caster classes, but clerics continue to be dependant upon their mana and inefficient combat spells.

Clerics need to be given some diversity and utility across the lifetime of the character. 3.) I think most of the casters are in the same boat. Most of us are pretty well balanced, but in comparison to the way melee classes scale in the end game, we just don't rate.

4.) If I could pick ONE thing that could be changed about clerics... there isn't any one thing. We're a balanced class as far as any one thing goes, but there are several minor things that I think we can ask for.

First, make our undead DOT's a little more efficient. Give us an undead snare. Give us the ability to put some of our utility spells together to good effect.

Second, up our defense. We're a plate class. It's ridiculous to think that with full plate armor and my healing spells, I can take hits less effectively than a shaman (Of course, this is because the shaman has slows)

Make our pet static. Come on, druid pets didn't unbalance the game. Why would leaving our hammer up between fights break the game?

Scale our combat abilities better. No, we're not a melee class. I'm not asking for kick, bash or duel weild, but when I swing my hammer, let me hit a bit more than I do.

Now some of these things sound silly to you guys. You think I'm abandoning what it means to be a cleric, but frankly, I know what it is to be a good cleric. I'm level 60. I raid all the time. What I want out of the game now is the ability to relax between raids, goof around, maybe even snag a little solo XP here and there. It dissapoints me that for all of my gear and experience, going solo is nothing but a hassle.

I don't know about the pet exp thing. Giving any class an expendable tank that can do most of the work and give all the credit to their master seems a bit over the top. Currently, if the pet does 51%+ of the total damage, the pet eats 50% of the exp in SOLO situations. If the pet class is grouped the pet doesn't eat exp. Seems perfectly equitable to me.

As to what clerics and others need...

Melee

I constantly boggle at these new cleric only weapons with really good damage/delay and wonder "Does Verant know we still can't hit the broad side of a barn?"

Priests are capped at 175 in Blunt melee skills. Not sure what shaman/pure caster piercing and druid 1hS cap at but all cap WELL below even hybrids. It's a left over from the old world. When 50 was the cap, being able to melee at the same level as a level 45 character made sense. In the post 50 world it makes no sense. Even hybrids have been moved up to appropriate levels.

The reason is was never changed? Melee classes whined we should be dependant upon our spells for damage...

At the very least, priests need their 1hB, piercing, and 1hS raised to 200+. Pure casters need to be able to push piercing past 200 as well. Honestly, I would like to see us all be raised to 225 or even 250. This would not infringe upon melee at all. Melee classes have the benefit of multiple weapon types (1hB, 2hB, 1hS, 2hS) as well as focusing their stats on melee related stats (str, dex, agi) and having secondary melee abilities (dodge, parry, riposite). A cleric with 225 1hB and the best melee cleric weapon will NEVER outperform even a paladin with appropriate equipment.

We also need our Dodge skills raised from 75 to somewhere in the 150-200 range. This is something we were asking for even before Kunark. Priests, being classes that get beat on, need the higher cap.

General Casting issues

There needs to be a way to lower the 2.5 recast time ALL spells have. Either as a benefit for leveling (ie post 50 or post 55) or as an AA skill. The recast time is absurd in the post 50 realm is absurd. Again, it is a leftover vestage from the old world. It makes sense for lower level casters but in the higher areas the abilitiy to chain cast heals is important. Either this needs to be across the board or limited to utility and heal spells.

Mana pool NUMBERS. Alan stated they were rethinking this but they were still divided. My question is why are casters denied one of the most important pieces of information? Moreover, why are high level casters with large mana pools forced to GUESS what their mana is? Melee classes are told everything about melee damage, delay, etc... why not casters? The roleplay argument is old and worn out. This isn't about roleplay, this is about treating casters as equals and removing the only remaining barrier to our efficiency.

Priest issues
There is a gaping hole in the post 50 realm for heals. Clerics get their last real heal upgrade at 53 with the final HoT spell coming at 59.

There is a need for a 1k+ heal. Clerics get it at 55 and the other 2 at 60. 100 range 400 mana cost 5.0 second cast.

Cleric issues

Divine Light needs its radius increased to 200 to match Nature's Touch (60 dru).

Judgement needs its damage upgraded to match the damage/mana ratio of Reckoning. No clerics will currently use Judgement over Reckoning. And no, it's not worth it for the instant recast time.

Make the 54 pet useful as a melee DoT instead of a 600 mana sink. Absor discussed several promising ideas about this over a year ago. Mainly make the pet immune to AE spells and up its HPs slightly so it would be able to go and do damage and only die if it became agro. At present it dies on the first hit most of the time. Perhaps remove the suicide component and make it a lower level enchanter type pet.

Give us more useful CLASS AA skills. At present, only Mass Group Buff and Bestow Divine Aura are useful.

My suggestion is a class AA skill that would make cleric buffs undispellable. Would have a 72 min reuse time and possibly work with MGB. Given how much we burn on reagents, this would give us a way to save at least SOME money. Would also give clerics a unique and useful skill.

Reduce the reuse on Divine Resurrection (class AA skill) from 60 game days (72 hours RL) to something more palatable like 20 game days (24 hours RL).

Increase Celestial Regeneration (class AA skill) to 1k per tick.

Mostly druid perspective here, though I do have a 42 cleric as well. I basically see two issues, one having to do with healing and the other with boredom.

Healing is a two edged sword of sorts. Cleric healing is so good that it's the only healing that matters on an end game raid. Secondary healers are rapidly being marginalized to the point they can't even keep themselves healed, much less everyone else. Druids definitely need better healing in the end game, and possibly shamen as well, though they have Torpor which is much better than Nature's Touch. The question is how can that be done without infringing on clerics in non-raid situations.

The answer that I believe may be forthcoming is that mobs may be adjusted so that they yield experience based on difficulty rather than just level. Clerics would be required for the best experience groups in this case, since druid and shaman healing likely wouldn't be sufficient. I have somewhat mixed feelings about this as my main is a druid. Accept the truth or not, a group already takes an experience penalty for having a druid as a primary healer, and I don't like seeing that gap widened. The gap comes from both more efficient healing on the part of clerics and on the cleric's ability to rez when things go bad.

Addressing matters on the boredom front is more difficult. Clerics do have things they can do besides heal, of course. They can nuke modestly, root park, stun, fear, mem-blur, and do a few other things as well. The main issue I see here is that clerics aren't called on to do these things for a reason, and the reason isn't that they're bad at these things. No, clerics aren't called on to do these things because they're expected to heal instead. Adding something new for clerics to do may not help much. I don't think better melee skills are the answer. Priests, including clerics, are casters in the grand scheme of things. The only reason melee would even enter into a cleric's mind as a reasonable possibility is because clerics have higher AC than all other magi and priests.

Somewhat off topic for this thread, but it deserves a reply. Perhaps the answer will suggest something relevant to a cleric revamp.

quote:
Na, druids have enough of it already. A 60 druid with c2/c3 can already be primary healer for most exp groups

A cleric still does it better. Since you're assuming C2/C3 for the druid, assume the same for the cleric. The cleric group can sustain a much faster pull rate. Basically a druid can heal for a group that isn't dealing with tough mobs under the circumstances described, but the group does pay a price for that.

To put it simply, clerics aren't hurting for experience groups because of the efficiency of druid healing. If primary healer is the only consideration, groups do prefer clerics quite strongly, especially once rezzes are a factor. Druids often do play the role of primary healer, but it's because of higher availability, not because of healing capability.

quote:
if he/she only focuses on healing and maybe snares via epic or many clickable items with snare they get.

No druid worth his salt is going to be snaring with a right click item, except possibly the epic. With the epic they'll be using it for the DoT, not the snare, and a druid is definitely in trouble healing if the mob lasts for full duration on that DoT (3 minutes). Snare is a very cheap spell, not worth messing with a click item with an eternal casting time for.

quote:
Finally, i would like to see mage direct damage spells to be better than druid ones. I could never get it why druids nuke better than mages (their top nuke is not a bolt and with new 60 Luclin nuke they do more dmg than mages atm).

I'll leave it for the wizards to call you on the details of that one. Personally I wouldn't object to mages having a better nuke, but please don't frame it in such nerfage terms. You would never know it to talk to mages, but mages actually do outnuke druids by a pretty substantial amount until the mid-50's.

Druids are basically the magi of the priest classes in EQ, by the way. They have the lowest AC and the least healing capability. It's reasonable for them to nuke better than any other priest class, and for those nukes to be pretty decent nukes.

quote:
And btw, i also play lvl28 druid, which appears to be my main character now No class envy here, just sober look at things which many seem to ignore.

Come back and read your post again when your druid is level 50, assuming you ever play your druid that far. I doubt that you'll feel the same way. Being stuck with the same heal at 50 that you got at 29 has an effect on your perspective, especially when you can't chain cast it fast enough to keep up with the damage mobs are doing.

Personally I wouldn't mind seeing the cleric class opened up a bit and having a more diverse set of capabilities. However, the key problem with that is that clerics wield too much healing power. Give clerics more to do in any reasonably balanced way, and clerics will still spend all their time healing and medding. EQ is a game where you meet the demands of your group or your raid first, and meet your own desires second.

I know I won't be popular saying this, but the best way for clerics to move forward when it comes to actually being a fun class to play is to give up some of the monopoly on healing in the high end game in exchange for a more diverse skill set. Gaining the diverse skill set will either do very little for clerics or completely marginalize all the other priest classes, depending on just how much is given, so long as the monopoly on high powered heals is retained.

Complete Heal has been a thorn in our side and yet it is something we hold onto for life. At this moment, if they took CH out of the game you'd be left with a class of third rate druids with res and high AC.

The problem is... we can't solo. But Verant hates soloing. They can't do a whole lot without making it obvious they're expanding our ability to solo because then all the other classes would fall upon VI with requests for expanded solo abilities as well.

So, what do we ask for? I thought long and hard about this. When it all boils down we really need the following 2 things for our class.

1. Something beyond Complete Heal. Our greatest problem is WHEN we do this

quote:
i'd like to see a strike..
like all clerics staying of the game for a week or two... i wanna see what happens after that :P

what happens next is we are replaced by a PLed twink who can do the job that a veteran cleric can just as well.

Our entire class revolves around this ONE SPELL. Hence, we are relegated to one role and thus we are easily replaced. It takes no skill to play a cleric anymore. So the veteran clerics with lots of /played time have no distinction between the PLed twinks who have maybe 10 or 20 /played days.

It's insulting, it's degrading and it makes those of us who have stuck with this class through thick and thin feel like a waste.

No other class in the game is replaced by twinks as easily as we are. Even enchanters still require you to know the nuances of the class to play one well.

2. Make it so that groups with multiple clerics have a role for the other one. 2 druids? One nukes, the other heals. 2 Warriors? Both tank. 2 wizards? Both nuke. But 2 clerics? You can't overlap heals, your damage output is so heavily nerfed it might as well not even exist and you have no utility spells to throw into the mix.

A group with 2 clerics right now is wasting a spot. Groups won't even consider it unless there's nobody else. So most clerics rot at the zone with LFG on.
------------------------
The bottom line is this. Clerics need an offensive output increase. We need better nukes, a non-undead DoT (we're not only the only class without invis but the only one without a normal DoT as well), a melee increase and some saving throw for travel besides the unreliable root.

As to healing... well, the only true way to balance the healing would be to introduce % heals. But Verant cannot (or perhaps will not?) do this right now. So other classes are stuck with fixed heals.
