I do not know if it is safe to say that over time a person becomes bored with every EQ class they focus on. However, I do think that is the case for some people. I also know it is the case if you pick a class that does not fit your idea of fun.

I have played three classes pretty deep (paladin 47, shaman 52, monk 53). I can tell you that at some point they all became painfully boring. Recently, I started a cleric. Because the class is new to me and I've been able to level up pretty fast, I find the class fascinating. I never even think of logging on the other characters.

Eventually I will be very bored by being a cleric and may go back to playing one of the other characters or start a new one. Something that I have found is that it is very easy to level a character quickly so I do not mind putting one on the shelf. I also have much, much, much less real life time in them than I did in my first character. After the first go-around you know where the big exp spots are and if you want, you can deck out the character 40 levels better then he/she should be.

To a lot of people this is not the answer but to me, when I get bored I switch gears 100% to another character. And trust me that all classes CAN get very old.

Machia mentions a monk specifically. Despite the fact that I have 2 other characters nearly the same level as my monk and have a cleric approaching fast, had I stayed playing the monk (my first character) and never started another one I am certain he would not be anywhere near 60. It was so boring for me that I found less and less reason to play.

I was about to reply when I read Sobe's comments which mirror mine a lot.

The problem with the Cleric class IMHO is that we are too ONE DIMENSIONAL when grouped.

It is not just a problem for the higher levels. At levels 19-24 and 29-34, I have been turned away from groups because I am ONLY a healer. A druid or shaman can heal just as well and their buffs are pretty useful too. I think my undivided attention to the group's health is better, but I can't transport people to the vendors when they become encumbered. I can stun though

From what I have read, at the higher levels, we become healing and ressing 'machines'. At level 31 I am seeing this more and there is not much fun in that. If it wasn't for the joking and group chat....

If Verant gave us more abilities (fun) than we would become too powerful. I agree with this. We have a lot going for us.

Albrecht mentioned a 'fire and forget' DOT type of heal that would free us from healing temporarily so that we could do other things. This is a great idea. What other things? How about a 'Heal Shield' like the druid's damage shields. We could recieve them in the same levels the druids do and they will heal XX HP to the wearer each time they are hit. This buff should be short-lived just like the druids damage shields. I guess we would have to stand up more (whether the group liked it or not) just to keep passing out that 25 second buff on the tanks.

There are other abilities and spells that Clerics could recieve without affecting balance. Here is how- Set the healing ability/mana ratio so that it is about the same as we have now. We won't heal better but we will have more options depending on the situation. We can spend our time trying different strategies and techniques (like druids did with kiting). Not more powerful, just more flexible.... I think this is the key to making Clerics something other than healing 'machines'.

What other spells/abilities can we add to our one-dimensional class without effecting balance?

Verant won't listen until we have a solution that works.
