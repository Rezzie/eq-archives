Asking is the only way to learn. I had several clerical mentors when I switched from a warrior but still found questions to ask here. So we have all been there no worries!

Uber: Well everyone thinks of Uber differently. I consider it being decked out in the best gear, having rare spells and being really really good at what you do. I dont think Im Uber, though I now have Akkirus armor and Donals legs. I dont have my epic (Fear the dolly!) but hope to someday. I would feel pretty uber in full SS and an epic. Your goal now should be to raise your wisdom as its begining to play a larger role in your mana pool. Dont sacrifice to much AC though as clerics end up taking beatigns for heals frequently.

Mid level Guilds: Well, depending on your server, those may be hard to find. Well established guilds will do more for the 45+ crowd, new guilds may push to do raiding, again 45+. Look to small guilds or role play guilds that have a bulk of people your level. Or just establish a good bunch of friends. Try staying in one place for several weeks, helps to get familiar with people.

Multiple Characters: Bah! I used to be a warrior, got to 51 and created a cleric. I love my cleric but I have a 21 shaman, 18 rogue, 12 necro. Dont fret over a label. Play what makes YOU happy. I like the term "Alt" because to me an alternate character is just another choice to make, not better or worse.

Spells: I keep 5 to 6 heals mem'd. At 56 I know I have more options then a level 31, but I have always felt this way. The more heals I mem the more equiped I will be to heal no matter what. Keep your best heal, keep your second best, keep a fast heal, keep a group heal. But that is my opinion and I am pretty much at the mercy of my group to keep me safe. So its a trade off. My usual goal is to slip by unnoticed first off or have someone guard me. The only time I feel most equiped to cross a dangerous zone? I won't respond to several of your questions because others have done better than I will.

As for guilds... you don't say what server you're on. If you're in a guild where you're on the low end as far as levels, you're not going to get to go on many main raids until you level up. In all likelihood, you'll level faster for time spent on EQ than higher level folks, though, so it will even out a bit.

One of my guildmates is in a similar position and has come up with a wonderful solution, which she calls "Die with Aaree night." She picks a day or time when either there's no guild raid, or a guild raid that won't involve everyone, and sets up a raid. She invites friendly guilds (especially one our guild likes a lot, and which averages lower level characters). And folks who want to play their alt or twink go too, as do other folks on the lower level end of the guild. Suddenly the 30 somethings have raids, get to interact a lot more with guildies and friendly guilds, we all practice important skills AND, by learning them from another point of view, have the chance to become better players. We get a chance to see dungeons we may not have seen much (or at all) with our mains. And most important, folks have fun. She just started these, and things seem to be building.

So, let me suggest you get yourself a map of Dalnir or somewhere else of appropriate level, you learn the map and consult with your guild's raid leaders, and you arrange to lead a raid. All you need is a few folks and you can have good fun and practice invaluable skills. You'll also develop as a raid leader and raider, and that will make you an even more valuable guildmember.

Don't worry if you only have one group to start with, but give it a couple raids for people to get used to the idea, to find a time when it works well, to let folks see what fun you're having. If it works, you'll have a lot of fun at it, show your guildmates that you're interested in raiding and good at it, and learn some different zones.

If you provide the fun at a time when people can make it, and in an area that's interesting, they'll come!

Good luck, Hende

Just adding to the wealth of info already given!

Raiding doesn't really begin until level 45. Most level 1-45 zones can be completely done with a single balanced group of the appropriate level. For example Mistmoore, Infected Paw, Tower of Frozen Shadow, etc.

You could raid Mistmoore at level 25 though if you brought 4 groups. However, few guilds do that as it's *really* hard to get people who are just learning to play their class to work together as a team. You'll notice huge differences in the organization and skill of xp groups as you progress from level 30 to level 40. It's quite dramatic.

As for people coming to get you, yes that's sometimes necessary. Even a well equipped cleric complete with invis potions, jboots/tboots/sow potions, etc needs help sometimes. However, OT shouldn't be one of those zones. Mem your root, atone, and fear spells and you should be fine. Never travel without a sow if you can help it though -- 95% of all outdoor mobs can simply be outran.

One bit of advice though: print out maps and learn to use /loc. These 2 things will help you greatly.
