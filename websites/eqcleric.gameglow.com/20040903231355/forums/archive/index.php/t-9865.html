Greetings!

Illyna: 14 hour respawn time except for Draco and Cazic.

Aldur:

I have been the only cleric in Fear for 30 people on some runs, and am frequently the only break cleric. Here is how I have dealt with it.

Priority for mana on planes run is enchanter and cleric. Necros feed you on demand unless they are filling in for enchanters. Mages give you modrods on demand, druids heal you. This is non-negotiable!

Delegate all second line buffs to other classes. Druids can do Resist Magic as well as you can, Necros can get Resist Disease, etc... Let the paladins do Pinzarn (at 49) and Armor of Faith (at 53). Your buff focus is on the pull team.

Make sure that the druids understand they must concentrate on healing their groups instead of nuking. I have watched so many rangers die in druid-healer groups while their healers are dropping starfires on gorgons...

If your goal is killing Cazic then a healthy pace is needed. But mass resses are far slower than slow pulls. The puller should be frequently camping to clear aggro, and if you have healed or buffed them between clears you need to do the same. 45 seconds of clearing aggro beats a death touch/minion callout that takes 10 minutes to recover if things go smoothly.

I often clear aggro during fights, the puller and I do a camp while the tanks are taking down the mob (obviously only do this when the fight is well in hand).

Rebuffing during Tentacle Tormentor / Glare Lord fights should be limited to "freebie" buffs such as Deepwater or Donal's armor effects. If the puller is on the ball he will try to group all the dispeller pulls into a series so mana is not wasted in buffing sessions.

Obviously, heal as late as possible to maximize med time. Use your most efficient heals, including the Word series.

I often ress people during battles, if they are under control. Tell the person they will be coming into a fight so they know to withdraw from the battleline to loot/med. This gives me time to recover mana during fight and gets them out of ress effects for the next set of pulls. This is a judgement call based on the situation (as an example, safe for Fetid Fiends but not for Worry Wraiths/Scarelings that aggro on medders).

As you gain more experience in the planes, and as the pull team builds up confidence in you, you can take a more directive approach in the pace of the raid.
