The Yaulp line of spells is far from useless, as is so often said about it. The next time you're on...cast yaulp and watch your stats, specifically STR and AC.

Q: Why do you clerics want high AC?
A: So we don't get the **** knocked out of us when our heals taunt.

Q: Well how long does yaulp last?
A: 15 seconds or so.

Q: How long do mobs stay agro on you after your heal taunts a mob?
A: Oh about 10 to 15 seconds.

Q: Is it really necessary to use up a spell slot just for a bit extra AC?
A: That depends on whether or not you have a balanced crew. If you have other classes around like an enchanter to mez and stun, wizard to stun, and druid/ranger to snare then why not keep it memmed? If you're doing the atoning/ stunning/ pacifying/buffing/healing/invigoring/ AND want both divine's and a gate spell you suck anyway and yaulp won't help you.

With a good crew yaulp fits into my loadout nicely. I should also point out that I like smacking things with my "Holy Stick of Unholy Assbeating," (tm) and with shaman buffs, bard song, and yaulp my STR is 225. Without bard song and shammy buffs I usually have a STR of Earth form the ranger or druid which puts my str at a lowly 165. Hehe....so smacking stuff for 46 damage can be fun. If your crew kicks ass like mine, the chanter will cast speed on you and you get to do 300-500 melee damage a mob. (just dont forget to heal people, its addicting:)

In summary...yaulp isn't the greatest spell, but it's fun to cast and very helpful for AC when that big ass mofo gets on you as it casts almost instantly.
