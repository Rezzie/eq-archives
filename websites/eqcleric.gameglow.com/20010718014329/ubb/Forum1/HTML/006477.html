I try and be supportive with all clerics on this one because yes it is a badly designed quest (mostly at the end with Ragefire being way too accessible, I actually really liked the rest of it).

My suggestion to say 'No' to the ragefire camp is mostly for the benefit of the would be camper (its not a fun thing to do and often ends in sorrow and disputes). I know many people have had success camping him but for others it has been a nightmare. And of course there are other ways....

I started this quest with no clear idea of how I would ever manage to complete it. But I realized you can't win a game if you don't play so I figured what the heck get the thing started and see what happens.

My recommendation is: start the quest but don't obsess. You want to have that pearl on ya just in case opportunity knocks. But there is no need to knock yourself out over it. Many of the first few steps are soloable and kind of a nice change of pace from the exp or raid grind.

I would like to share my very nice and very lucky exp with Ragefire.

I get home from work and my wife tells me some of the guild is in sol B for a pick-me up Naggy raid. I get teleported to Lavastorm, manage to evade bugs and such and make it to the prep area. I find out Naggy isn't up yet and is hours over due. A GM is "looking into" the situation. So I find my guildmates and start to buff them. Next thing I know someone shouts "RAGEFIRE IS UP".
I let the leader/organizer Punish know I have a pearl and the resulting dragon has the same drop table plus some unique items as Naggy. No other cleric claims the spawn so I ask some others from my guild to come assist me.

It was very nice killed ragefire with a Naggy raid pick me up group with Zero camping time. I was so happy. The pick me up group got 2 treasure hunters satchels and a blazing BP. So I think everyone had fun. I had many people send tells to me that they were happy and glad for the chance to slay Ragefire and assist a cleric on getting thier epic weapon.

Good luck to everyone
