You need to find a chanter to hook up with your regular group. Chanters and clerics go together indoors. They make everything happen.

But, that being said, as to your armor, the cleric quest gear is very nice. It is nice AC, not much in wis, but light and has some stat or other on every piece. I think that most clerics wear at least some of the gold armor deep into the 40's and I am still wearing those boots right now at 52.

The BI look is pretty cool, but you could probably pick up 100 AC on the gold armor, and all gold armor is not bad looking either. Make sure your defense is maxed. The bulk of your AC comes off defense skill, NOT equipment. At 30, your defense should be 155. That equals approx 450 AC I think. So, make sure you are getting smacked part of the time.

Dungeons: 30 to 40 is a mecca for dungeons. Dalnir is a good dungeon, fun, small and easy to get killed in. Nurga is a good dungeon, fun, small, much easier to get to, even crappier loot, but one of the safest dungeons you will ever hunt in. Droga is also fun, also relatively easy to get to, some nice drops in there, and plenty easy to get killed in. Kaesora (you are not quite big enough yet) is more out of the way, has very nice loot, is very fun, has some of the best exp in the game, has very nice loot. That is Kunark. Velious includes Crystal Caverns, Tower of Frozen Shadows, both very interesting and fun dungeons, also, both difficult to do CR in. Old world, Cazic Thule, Guk, SolA, MM, Unrest, Splitpaw, Perma, KK.

Really, the levels you guys are at, it really almost just depends on what you are lookign for. There should be lots of action anywhere you are at. I started to really have fun with my cleric at 29. Revive is great. People knock it because it doesn't return exp, but when you are fighting in Dalnir, and one of your group dies, they are bound in FV and you are on the third floor of Dalnir, you really need that revive action. So, it is time to make sure that if everyone else dies, you at least survive somehow and can revive up your group mates.

Best way to survive? Keep the chanter alive, at all costs.

A few things to add:

Adamantite epolets: Get these right away. Great item, and very cheap. It's been a while since I hunted in Guk, but I would guess that they're practically giving these away now.

Neck: I've read that the "new" Runnyeye now drops a "black alloy medallion" or somesuch that is 6 AC and +5 int/wis. A very nice item that should be in your reach.

Chest: In my opinion, the BS tunic is a mistake. The chest slot is just too important to fill with a low AC item, especially for a lousy 4 wis or whatever. I wore an enamelled black chestplate (25 AC) in my mid-levels. Like the adamantite epolets, this should be pretty freely available in LGuk these days, or at the market for a modest sum.

Like others have said, your ranger can provide valuable assistance in doing the armor quests. If I were you, I'd start with the bracers. Just park your ranger in NK near Regis' spawn point and log on every now and then to see if he or Zahal is up.

I will answer all your questions on gear. Take your Ranger to Crystal Cavarns. It is a one way dungeon up to the dwarf city on non dwarf faction. Find the Foreman at the bottem and kill everything down there until you get the following items: Rygor Oracle Cloak +2 wis 10 mana(Oracles), Crystelline Belt +3 wis (inspector) and Herbelist Pack (Herbelist). If you fill up with sellable loot, bank in the dwarf city, repeat cycle. If people are down there, spawns are random so just go back up a ways and clear the spawns. Take your pharmed gains and buy VELIUM Jasper jewelry. Check with your smithy friend, cultural armor for Dwarves has great WIS and AC.
