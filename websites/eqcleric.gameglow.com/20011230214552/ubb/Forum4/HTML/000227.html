a couple of notes...

Clerics have a line of AC buffs, and a line of AC/HP buffs, plus symbol. the druids have their "skin" line (plus a self-only line)... there are times during the progression through levels that the druid's buffs will overwrite one of the cleric's buffs, usually at a point when druid has just earned and upgrade -- at this point the spells are pretty close to being equal (of course, the cleric can stack his/her other line on and thus end up with a much superior total enhancement. (I have a 49 cleric and a 47 druid).

I think the reason for both the spells given to the druid and the spells withheld from the cleric are primarily the desire of Verant to build interdependence. If the druid truly got the best of all spells, wouldn't you think they would be very sought after in the high end game? in fact, they tend to have some trouble getting into guilds/groups/etc at the higher levels if they haven't built up a strong reputation/circle of friends...because they have the tools to do a lot of jobs pretty well, but there is usually someone who has the tools to do it better or more efficiently, and the difference becomes more and more important in the higher levels.

My cleric was my first character, and as someone who had very erratic playing times, I would make friends but was not able to plan on regular groups or arrange to meet people (I was not in a guild at the time), so I often found myself venturing out to explore on my own ... and grew quite frustrated at my lack of "travelling" tools:

no ports, no speed enhancements, no invisibility, no levitate...not a strong enough tanking ability to rely on that...

(I'm not griping about this at all, I'm just saying that it was such a blanket shortfall that I stopped thinking it was coincidence). I think that a decision was specifically made that clerics would not be very "mobile". Verant has made no bones about the fact that they WANT classes to need each other, so they take a class that is highly needed, and in some cases desperately needed in a specific spot without much notice, and they make that class mobility-impaired.... certainly that has got to create some interdependence.

Keep in mind that druid doesn't get the low-aggro heal spells (although they do get the regen/chloro line); the druid has great mobility, but can't punt the player who needs to leave your group, or call in his replacement. he can heal, but he can't wear the armor to take the abuse when he gets aggro for that heal. (and doesn't get the lower-aggro heal spells, tho does get regen/chloro)

they can't nuke as well as a wizard or dot as well as a necro... not that they should. Just realize that when a specific job is called for, the class people are going to be looking for is usually some class other than a druid.

I enjoy playing both classes, as well as my current "main" (54 enchanter). druids can be a lot of fun to play, and really hit their strides about level 14... when I hit that level on my first druid I felt godlike. And if I'm bored and just want to wander on my own and explore a bit, nothing beats my druid. I don't enjoy soloing for experience much (i get bored way too easily), though, and the supply/demand ratio for higher level druids in groups is not in your favor...

fun to play? sure
lot of nice spells? sure
overpowered? ...I don't really think so, at least not if you want to do anything but grind out solo experience, and I don't really see that as a huge advantage.

-fergus

Even though we already pay to much in items for spells as it is.

And lastly cleric spells being based on charm has to be addressed. Either they have to change the character settings and make charm a main attribute of all clerics and therefore make all cleric eleigible characters have a base of 100 charm. This would have to be done so that they can even have a chance to cast these charm based spells. Either that or fix these spells so they are not based on charm.

If you changed the spells test to wisdom then all clerics would be able to use the , Atone/and Pacify series of spells. As it stands now none of these spells are useable by the majority of clerics except for by High Elves.

In the case of Death Pact If I was gonna cast Death Pact it would only be useable on an enchanter. And then it cost 10 plat to casts and it only last 10 minutes. There is no way this spell should work on a Agnostic , high charmed enchanter better then it works on a cleric or a Paladin from the god of that cleric. This is pure silliness. They should change it to a percentage chance per level of the person casting it, who of course is from that god.

An agnostic person recieving the spell would have a 30% chance of being revived and person from another diety would have a 25% chance of being revived. Then lastly a person from an opposing diety would only have a 20% chance of being revived with the 400 hps.

That would fix the spell so that it would be useable by clerics of the same church and if the duration was changed for something like 30 minutes instead of the 10 minutes every cleric walking the medical beat would be using that spell on everyone in their group.

This would address the clerics weaknesses now and I think all clerics should write to eq and press for these changes.
