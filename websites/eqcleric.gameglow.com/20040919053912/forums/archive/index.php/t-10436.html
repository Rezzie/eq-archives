Well, I made the trip to South Ro last weekend and it's not any more dangerous than it was before. Probably less so now that people are hunting Sand Giants. The only thing to worry about in the Oasis aside from regular madmen, ghouls and such is bad Spectre pulls, when Spectres leave their cozy island and start slaughtering people on the beach.

But back to South Ro. Various Crypt and Shriveled Mummies are good and safe experience between 15/16 and 17 if you stay close to the Oasis zone, not to mention excellent loot for that level, but Dry Bone Skeletons will give you trouble since they will be yellow-to-red to you. At 18, you may be able to take out some of them, but expect partial and even complete resists and their Fire based attacks add up. At 19, armed with your new spells and more mana/HP/Defense, you should be able to solo them just fine.

One word about the vendors in South Ro. The ones in the North-Central camp are Evil and will KoS Good races. The single vendor (and the friendly Orc who hangs out with him) in the NW corner will buy from and sell to Good races, but his prices really bite - expect a 50%+ markup/discount. You may be better off selling in the Oasis, although it is arguably more dangerous, or in North Ro, although it is a much longer run. Decisions, decisions...

One thing to be aware of if you're a Dwarf and didn't hunt dervs or orc raiders is that you will be indifferent to all the dervs and orcs in Oasis and South Ro.

This means you can pick off the wandering undead with no problem and med right in the middle of the orc spawn if you want.

Madmen will also be indifferent to you.

One thing to watch out for is the dervish thugs. They always scowl even if you have good derv faction and they will aggro the other dervs on you and then really mess up your faction when you kill them.

As a Dark Elf, this was one of the best places to hunt. With a SK, Necro and Troll Warrior and myself, we took up inside the Dark Elf Merchant encampment and had more than enough to do. We sold to the vendors not 5 feet away, while a lot of wandering creatures happened by for us to kill (sometimes too many). If there was ever a break in the carnage, the warrior would just go out and pull something in. BE CAREFUL that you don't have a vendor targeted when you attack or cast, and somemtimes the madmen sit outside the tents and lob fireballs in at you (INCOMMING!).

If you get bored there, go hunt one of the two (or three) derv camps. This zone is underhunted to the extreme, so plenty of fun can be had.

Oh, and watch out for Terrorantula!!!!
