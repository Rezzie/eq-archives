You won't see the true resist factoring in doing tests in a PvP environment and trying to use those results to apply to a PvE encounter, things in PvP work very differently than they do against mobs. For the most part (I may get ruined here by the Zek's, but this is from the tests I did) In PvP, 100 MR will prevent almost all magic based spells from landing, regardless of level, assuming they are *all or nothing spells*, and cannot hit for partial damage, eg. root, stun, snare etc. MR below 100 ends up being almost a flat percentage save vs those same spells, eg. with 50 MR, you will resist root 10 out of 20 attempts, 75 MR 15/20. Its almost scary how close the tests I did were to the numbers, in fact, they worked out *exact* with a sample size of 10 casts against varying MR, using people of various levels to compare the results.

In my experience in PvE, level is god, and I think you take a serious penalty for every level you are below the mob. I shoot for 200 resist for most things we do, unless I have a bard in group, then it goes through the roof. But for my guild, 200 is the general rule to shoot for.

Just my .02
