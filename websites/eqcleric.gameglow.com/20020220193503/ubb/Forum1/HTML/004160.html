A few thoughts:

1. I don't think that the volume of complaints on the message boards can be attributed to Abashi (or anyone else at Verant) doing a bad job. Given any large group of people, a certain fraction of them are always going to complain. If 1% of EQ players are whiny jerks, that's still 2,000 people to show up and spam the message boards. I'm not saying there aren't legitimate complaints, but realistically, a certain amount of ranting is just a given.

2. I think people have unrealistic expectations about getting changes in the game. The game didn't come out of a vacuum. It's clear to me from every post I've ever seen by a Verant rep that they truly like their game and have strong feelings about it. I'd bet that nearly every significant aspect of the game reflects a conscious, thought-out decision by a game designer. Their decision could be wrong, but they have a reason for it (good or bad), and they're not going to make a change every time Abashi tells them that some people on the message boards don't like what they did.

3. In a similar vein, I think of lot of posters "shoot the messenger" when they hear things they don't like. My class isn't getting better armor/spells/whatever? Damn that Abashi! I doubt that any big decisions are made by Abashi alone, and I doubt that replacing Abashi would effect any noticeable change in Verant's positions on the "hot topics." People love Rowyll and Ester the Tester, but they have limited areas of responsibility that usually don't touch on class balance, loot drops, etc. Put Rowyll in charge of explaining why monks don't have higher AC, and the honeymoon will be over quickly.

4. On the "censorship issue," I did disagree with the recent incident where someone got a warning for saying "Abashi doesn't count." Aside from that, however, I have yet to see any instance where someone was punished for politely stating an opinion about a game issue. Despite the overheated "Big Brother" rhetoric, Verant has done nothing more than adopt that same sorts of policies that all the good unofficial boards use (including this one).

5. Finally, Abashi has seemed a little short-tempered recently. I try to bear in mind, however, how I would feel if I was sitting at home answering questions in my free time, and I came across the twentieth "you're an idiot" message of the day.
