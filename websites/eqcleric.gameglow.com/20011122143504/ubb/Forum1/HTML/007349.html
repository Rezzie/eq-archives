Perhaps it is a bit different on my server or perhaps it is my impressions, but DAoC seems to have had a fairly moderate sized impact on Fennin Ro.

These are my perceptions as related to the active player base on FR:

Old Sebilis 4 months ago - 100+ people all the time.

Old Sebilis 1.5 months ago - 50+ people approx during peak times. Velks Lab 1.5 months ago - 30+ people approx during peak times

Old Sebilis today - 20 people or less during peak times. Velks Lab today - 20 people approx during peak times. Chardok today - 12 people approx during peak times.

Significant player base guild movement as the higher level guilds are actively recruiting and in most cases lowering their entrance requirements.

Raids that previously contained guild only people + recruiting invites now are often actively inviting *needed* classes just to fill out raids. Raiding guilds that usually raid 5+ days a week appear to only be raiding 2-3+ days a week.

The /who all [ insert guildname/number ] that previously would return the "List cut short because it's too long" message now often shows 20ish people where normal later evening activity would be 40+ (later evening = 11pm EST on for my purposes)

Guild dissolutions & Guild alliances/mergers... I have seen atleast 4 guilds that as little as a month ago had 80+ members disband. Smaller guilds are actively merging or seeking guild alliances to have raid capability where they previously could handle things like PoF or PoH internally with little or no difficulty.

Just a personal estimate... but since they took the server player numbers off the log in screen I would estimate that Fennin Ro has gone from an average of 2400-2500 during peak hours down to 1800-1900 people if they are lucky. Thats a 25-35% decline in the player base and is fairly significant. All indicators point to the recent release of DAoC although I have no empirical evidence to support that conclusion as fact.. it's unconfirmed theory. But a 30% average loss of your player base, and therefore your revenue is a significant impact.

Just my personal wild conclusions and unsubstantiated theories (ie. my 2cp)

Well I can't tell you how many people are playing EQ on the servers, but I can tell you that DAoC seems to be approaching almost 40k every night. That number seems to be reminiscent of the early days of EQ. I know my server seems crowded and it's not uncommon for people to be fighting over spawns from time to time which is also extremely reminiscent of early EQ =).

AO's problems were all self-inflicted and Dalur was correct in that there was a horrible patch, but that patch was by no means their main problem; I had left before the patch, the patch just confirmed my decision. There were numerous other problems including a severe problem with higher level content and play balance, but the bottom line is the company is just flat out amatuerish. I hope they (Funcom) survive long enough to mature as a company, but that issue seems doubtful.

DAoC is far from perfect, but the folks at Mythic used their time wisely. They learned from other companies and it shows throughout the product. I'm not interested in giving them credit for being more "player friendly" in their communications as Verant used to be more player friendly also. DAoC's game design, however, is indeed more player friendly as it's obviously been designed to remove much of the intentional tedium of which Everquest has so much. Downtime, soloing, and grouping with more casual playing friends is just nice in DAoC. In EQ my friends who didn't have the time to keep up just got left behind.

DAoC is certainly not nirvana, but it's a good game for those who are bored with EQ, yet still want to have online adventures. For those of you who haven't "finished" EQ (such as maxing your level, visiting all the places you really want to see, etc), I certainly understand sticking around. For those of you who have finished DAoC is a pretty nice alternative.
