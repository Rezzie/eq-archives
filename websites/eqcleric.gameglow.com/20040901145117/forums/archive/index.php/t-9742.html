there are two primary areas where our abilities intersect to create a whole that is more powerful than the parts:

1) Our ability to wear really good armor and also to root and nuke takes away most of the risk involved in this maneuver. I routinely attempt to kill things that are white or yellow to me using this type of tactic. If I fail I usually manage to get away anyway. The bronze armor means that carrion ghouls and drybones can't really do that much damage to me quickly and I can almost always get a gate to stick or use DA to make it to zone.

2) Our ability to buff vs magic and the elements means that npc casters are soloable, even named ones. My L19 Warrior died in under a minute trying to solo Ghanex Drah. My L19 Cleric had more than half health left after he killed Drah. The resists were the major factor here. (Well, along with the DD that killed Drah in about 60 seconds...)

I could name a half dozen other ways that we absolutely rock a melee type or Wizard into the ground but they'd all reference in some way one of the advantages (AC, DD, buffs, etc.) listed above.

What it comes down to for me is that Clerics are every bit as powerful as the pet classes but we don't have to rely on the pet for this power. This makes me nervous when I start to think of the potential nerfs that could come our way...
