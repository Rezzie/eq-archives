Manastones do NOT work anywhere in Kunark, anywhere in Velious, or in any Plane.

This really limits their usefullness past 45-50.

Now, at 36, they have uses, but those uses are also few and far between. A manastone takes 60 health and gives 20 mana. 3:1 ratio. Your best heal doesn't come close to this ratio.

If you have a druid or shaman casting a regen on you, then you can pop the stone a couple times, and regen the health back. It's like free mana. You are gonna be sitting to med, you may as well regain some health while yer at it.

Other than that, about the only time a Manastone is useful anymore is on raids like Efreeti or Dragon, when you are buffing your group, you can stone and have someone else (Ranger/Paladin/Whatever) heal you.

Post 52, when you can't do dragons anymore, a Manastone is just taking up space in your bank while you run around Velious and Kunark.

I love Manastones. Have one on both my Cleric and Shadowknight.

Manastones do indeed have limited usefulness these days, BUT that still means they have their moments.

I use my Manastone primarily when I want to XP grind and there isn't an Enchanter or Bard available. In those situations I go to either SolB (duo on LDCs or Imps works well) or the Hole.

Back in the day the Manastone was the cause of many Rayne deaths (in the 30s). "Hmm, I have 100 hit points, let me stone once to see if I can squeeze another heal." And I did, many many times. Yes, it cost me a lot of deaths, but I made a lot of saves that I couldn't have otherwise.

Once I hit 39 I could start regenning mana (stone down/complete heal). In the upper 40s that started becoming very efficient, and at 60 it's insane.

If you NEVER hit the old world zones then the stone is useless, but if you do then it's an incredible item.

The 3:1 hits to mana ratio works very very well with complete healing. You spend 400 mana for the spell and break even on mana if you stoned down 1200 hit points. Anything over 1200 hit points healed is where you get the mana regen. I would consider getting some hit point items for level 39 and then keeping yourself buffed with your best hit point buffs if you want to use this normally. Just be careful, using the stone causes agro against mobs in range, and low health increases agro too.

You pegged it Dikmer. I haven't done Kedge much since I've hit 50 (just pop in to do Phinny occasionally). The things that give me XP are too much trouble to kill for XP in that zone.

So... it's LDCs and Imps most of the time. LDCs are good XP up to 59 and can get one to 60 if soloing (er, not as a Cleric of course). Imps will get you through 60 and beyond (if there was one).

Haven't hunted in LGuk since 50, so can't comment there.

The entrance mobs in the Hole will get you to 54 and can PL a single player to 55 very quickly (great place to bring a low 50s Warrior with a snare whip). Downstairs is good until upper 50s (on the floor) and the rev tower is good to 60. I don't like the Jail for PLing, the Ratmen hit too hard, have too many hit points to do them efficiently and if you spawn a Guard just wrap it up. A good group can get incredible XP in the Hole. Loot sucks.

One day I'm going to try the bears and stuff inside Perma.... Bet they aren't camped at all =P. Just figure it'll take my SK too damned long to kill them.

I broke into the Efreeti with my SK at 58 (with Rayne healing of course). I suspect it'll be a lot easier at 59, though I haven't tried yet. The LDCs are so much easier for only a little less XP, um, provided I can get them all.

My SK is only 2+ blues into 59, so I'm too depressed to think about powerlevelling right now. I wouldn't bother, but the 60 spells are nice for SKs.

Best place I've ever seen for XP, at upper 50s, is South Wing of Charasis. Like I said earlier, the 'stone is definitely situational now, but it allows me to do productive XP hunts without an Enchanter or Bard around.

I got my enchanter from 56 to 58 in Permafrost pits. (you know, the pit someone always falls into during Vox raids) At 59, you will start seeing green mobs and experience will plummet however... (well it's 59, so experience will start getting depressing anyway!)

Getting into the pit is easy enough - only icy terrors and ice giant diplomat will see through invis 100%, and only few giants here and there will see through invis. Just check with third person view and be careful. None of the bears, wolves, and spiders will see through invis from my experience, so falling down into the pits with invis is pretty safe. At level 56 (or 57?) a careful puller should be able to get solo mobs 100% of time down in the pits. Getting out is even easier, provided you are level 53 and above. Just run into Vox's lair, and you'll get a free ride out to Everfrost. Make sure Vox is up though. :P

Loot simply does not exist down there, and if you spend any more than 4 hours there in a stretch, your mind will go numb. Good thing there are number of safe spots to camp out for breaks... I would only go there if you are really determined to level.
