Actually, if you pull Naggy to the door and fight him there, you will be much more successful. Keep the healers around the corner and out of line of sight, so they won't be affected by dragonfear or dragonbreath. Tell your healers to chain cast greater healing (nothing larger) on the tanks in their group.

Nothing too exciting but these are the rules I use for my raids: http://mav.nu/hb/naggyrules.html

Some tips that arent on that page:

Clerics stay on the stairs to the lair for the healing station. (Naggy usually ends up being fought at the door btw.) Casters stay down in that small room until time to blast.

Paladins are to use LoH as their first heal, self heal second, then come for heals (saves cleric mana).

Hrmm....basically I usually take 6 groups. All with clerics and a backup healer of some kind (clerics 44-52, with 49+ preferred, but I let the backups be higher then 53 if they want...druids with sheal are good). At least 3 wizards with Ice Comet. Group the heaviest tanks with bards. Have all casters bind there so if they die, they can just mem their nuke and run back in. Or if a healer is banished they can just gate back and go again.

Personal best so far is Naggy dead in about 3 minutes with only 5 deaths.
