I don't think there is something like a "cleric's guide" - like there is none for any other class.

It depends where you are raiding, who you are riding with (classes, level), how you are raiding (aoe, CH-rotation, group, mezzing, addtanking etc).

As for Hate and Fear I can say they don't differ much to a 2-3 group dungeon crawling, if you ever did this. (if you aren't doing AoE break in )

Most important thing: listen to raid leader's instructions and watch the battle field - try to get an overview of what is going on. Is an enchanter under attack? Help his cleric to heal. Is the MA cleric oom or down? Help keep him up. Is everything going well? Nuke :b j/k Is there a call to camp clerics? Try to find a safe spot and camp out (or try /q), manage your mana and control your aggro, and most important: have fun!

There is no rule when to start with CHs - as there is none in a group either. It depends on the mob, on the tanks level and his equipment. It is easier for you when you know the tank - if not better start early - see how much he lost during your cast and adapt. (There are mobs in ToV where I hit the MA with a CE before he attacks and start casting CH right after - and he gets healed with one bub left...)

As long as you listen to the instructions there shouldn't be a wipe out - and if there is one, it isn't your fault. There are some situation where you just can't do nothing about it - you all should learn then from the mistakes and make it better next trial. Have fun!

Before raid:

- ressearch the zone you are going to, learn where not to go (like zone edge up the wall in hate, a definite no no), print maps
- buy yourself some cloudy potions
- always have 2-3 stacks of peridots on you
- always carry your resist gear pack

During raid:

- expect to die and lose xp, and don't complain about it
- pay attention, don't go afk unless raid leader and your group leader acknowledge it
- keep the chatter down, talk less listen more
- ask question, ask other experienced clerics for pointers
- forget gate, but keep da and db up
- don't drag mob back to camp if you are moving (unless they said otherwise), just die there and ask for CR
- if you get aggro at camp spot, don't run around, stand still and let the melee taunt the mob(s) off or chanter mezz
- code your social button to target the other clerics and chanter by name, be ready to save them
- be ready to camp when raid leader said so, don't delay
- don't fight over loot
- during CR, always rezz a stick-wielding cleric first if there is one, then a bard, then a chanter, smart bards always have charm loaded in a raid, they can be very effective mob control when the chanter is manaless after a rezz
- did i say expect to die and lose xp, and don't complain about it?


Nothing beats experience. You'll be fine before you know it.

I'm in a place myself where I am learning the basics of multigroup raiding, still have a lot to learn but a few things I have noticed to be helpful:

-Make '/target playername' hotkeys for MA, backup MA (if there is one appointed), chanters, clerics. You'll probably have to use more than one hotkey bank but you can switch quickly with shift-1,2,3 etc.

-Make a hotkey for /assist and use it. Target MA, assist on him and you'll have a mob targeted. Assist on mob and you'll see who mob is beating on. Rinse repeat, very helpful way to keep an eye on who needs heals as the one who has aggro is likely to be in need of a heal.

-Make hotkeys for heals/da that send spam to a chat channel. Raids nowadays have a channel set up but you don't need to know the name of it to make the macros. That way you can prepare a few of your macros, unlike the /target ones that you won't know until raid is forming.

-When fighting mobs that dispell a lot celestial heals can be dispelled so you might not want to use those (Vox for example). Ask around if you're not sure.

-Don't be afraid to let others know that you are new to this kind of thing. At the very least let the raid leader know (through /tell). Ask what your job is. In my experience people are generally very considerate if you let them know ahead of time. Sometimes you can chat a bit with your group too.

-Try to think out ahead of time the priority of the people in the raid (maybe not everyone but the ones you have /target keys for and the ones in your group). Makes for quick decisions on who lives and who dies when mana or time is short. Be prepared to reevaluate as fight progresses. Make sure you know who the people in your group are as it is generally your job to watch their health.

-Don't let the MA die, most jobs can be switched to secondary if main dies but tank seems to be difficult.


These are some experiences I've made during my first few raids, so I might be a bit off =).

As a cleric the most important thing to learn to do on raids is to learn to assist heal.

The involves making a straight /assist key and constantly assisting the mob that is being fought at that time. This can be the difference in getting heals to the casters (who get easy aggro for a second or two and are dead) and also to which tank is generating the most aggro.

Once you master assist healing and can learn when to CH (i.e. watching your tanks HPs go down, and being able to judge when to start a heal, as opposed to just waiting for 2 bubs left...) the rest of the clerics job is easy: just follow everyone else and listen to the raid leader

Here are some basics for you:

There are some terms that you need to know..

RL - raid leader, this is the person that controls you for the next 4-8 hours or more. You need to do what they say, and not question. This may cause your death, which is sometimes unavoidable. if you have any questions about what you are told to do, write them down and ask AFTER the raid. Questions are valuable, they show that you are interested in learning and could be a valuable asset in the future, there is never a stupid question, remember that... Also, You should NEVER send Tells to the RL during a raid, unless you are sent a tell from them that requires a reply.

GL - group leader, this is the person that is responsible for the group you are in, and all communications with the RL. If a Mana, Buffs,Ready question? is asked this is who you tell your status to. If you have any questions, this is who you should ask first. The GL will coordinate what the roll of each person in a group does to make the most efective team.

MT - Main Tank, also an abbreviation of (empty) as in the mob just killed had no loots.. The Main Tank is arguably one of the most important people on a raid. A good one can make a raid run smoothly, a poor one can cause much grief. The MT and the RL work together to make the raid a success, and as such anything they say should be listened to. More about MT later...

ST - Secondary Tank, this is a backup so that if and When the MT drops, someone is there to take over. Also ST is used on some adds that cannot be mezzed.

Puller / Pull Team - The people designated by the RL to leave the camp and attract mobs to kill. If you are not on this team DO NOT leave the camp.

Camp - this is the location that the raid team waits to kill mobs. usually it will be a recognizable place, and denoted exactly where people can stand/sit/med.

Pulls - Mobs that are delibertly brought to the camp so that the raid can kill them.

Adds - Additional mobs that show up in camp, either thru natural wandering paths or by being aggro'd by the puller/pull.

Assist - If you are not puller/pull team/MT/ST this is the ONLY WAY that you should ever target a mob. If you are a melee, get rid of that attack hotkey NOW.. instead set up an /assist

and an /assist hotkeys NOW... you must use them.. if not you can easilly cause a wipeout of a multigroup raid. The MT will set up a shout hotkey that tells you when to assist, then you may hit your hotkey.. ALWAYS WAIT FOR THE ASSIST to be called...

Taunt - The MT and only the MT should taunt...

Mezzed - Enchanter can mez mobs, making them stand there and do nothing, when this happens if you attack them you will wake the mezzed mobs up and upset an enchanter, one reason that you only use the /assist MT key to target and attack...

DUMP MANA - Necros can dump mana to other casters, this is used primarilly to feed clerics to heal the tanks.

NUKE - this is the call for casters to cast DD spells at the targeted mobs. For this reason casters also must make an /assist hotkey. Casters should not cast until the mob is at least down to 75% of its life, this will help prevent ping-pong...

PING-PONG - This is when a mob bounces from one person to another, running around inside the camp, not standing in one place. This is very bad, as the Tanks do not auto-follow the mob, and must run around chasing it. This lowers the damage they deal to it, and increases the time it is allowed. If you are a caster standing far away from a mob and cast a spell, and next thing you know you are being beat upon, you just caused ping pong.

AGGRO- The MT is ideally the only one who as AGGRO on a mob, meaning that every swing/spell/effect that the mob does is targeted on the MT. AGGRO is gained in many ways, damage/spells/sitting... When a caster casts a spell and immediately sits down, that can easilly draw the attenytion and AGGRO the mob, causing PING-PONG, as we have discussed PING PONG is bad, so after casting count to 3 or 5 and then sit down, this will dranatically reduce aggro from sitting. Remeber if the AGGRO is not on the MT, then who ever is getting the AGGRO is doing something wrong.

CAMPOUT - if things go really bad, certain classes will be told to campout, clerics with noodles namely. If this is called, everyone must do all they can to get the AGGRO off of the Campers.
