Keep doing what you're doing by fighting in groups, but when you find one where things are rather calm, let the group know you want to practice your defense. Each skill up in Defense means and extra point or two in natural AC.

You can't raise defense unless you get hit; you can't raise your weapon skill or offense unless you are swinging a weapon on the front line.

I'd find a nice steady stream of blues somewhere and get a group going that has another cleric friend or healer of some type in it. Pretend your a tank and whack those beasties upside the head for a couple of nights.

Neat trick:
Root the mob and get close, and I mean REAL close. Like stand on it.
A rooted mob will always hit the one closest to it. If you need a heal, back off and heal yourself. If root breaks, your tanks should have enough aggro to make it stay on them.
If you got a rogue in your group, he will love this as that makes him able to do backstabs without fearing the mob will turn and hit him. Be sure to engage from the opposite direction of the rogue.

I found that I had to make time every level to go tank some low blues. The perfect training mob is a fast swinging, low damage, undead. If you can't find that, then find any low blue. As a rule of thumb old-world critters hit less hard than do Kunark or Velious critters.

The trick for me has been to
1, change into ac gear
2, bum an agility buff from a shaman or ranger
3, root the critter before backing of to heal myself.
4, have gate loaded and ready to use.

do NOT expect to gain experience or group with others while doing this, you really do better if you say "I will train defense for 40 minutes" and then go about your regular hunting.

If you are sorely lacking in your defense/1hb skill in your 30's/40's I have a suggestion for you that will both yield experience and your much needed skill point increases.

Grab a druid... you more than likely have alteast 1 druid friend in your level range.

If your in the mid/upper 30's you and the druid go to OT. Pull those wandering cactus plants to someplace relatively safe (i used to prefer the SW corner or the "tunnel" to FM). Then you take turns... one time the druid roots the mob, damage shields you, and heals you as needed. When the druid is OOM or the mob is dead its your turn. The druid damage shields themselves and this time you root and heal until the mob is dead or your OOM... rinse and repeat. You sure aren't gonna level at a quick pace doing this, but you will get your 1hb, offense, and defense skills up AND get some experience while doing so.

If your in your 40's grab your druid friend and go hit the Bloodgill goblins in Lake of Ill Omen. Do the same thing described above taking turns every other mob.. just be sure to pull the gobbies to land.

If you want to go hardcore then do this for as long as possible or for hours at a time. If you find that boring then do it for an hour or two a few days a week.

The other option for the less "sane" is find a place where you can get 4-6 mobs on you at once and still be near the zone line... Round them up, DA or DB, and let them pound on you while they do no damage.. you can still get defense increases while DA'd. I have found that CoM is just about perfect for this since you can easily round up the front 5 mob spawns and run to the zone to do your DA thing.. inbetween DA/DB recycle times you can solo pull/root one and just heal yourself.

those are a couple easy options for ya
