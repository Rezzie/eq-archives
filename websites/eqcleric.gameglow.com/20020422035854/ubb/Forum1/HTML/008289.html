With today's equipment, Lguk becomes pretty easy. Look at it this way: at 55, my paladin and a 55 necromancer cleared and held the dungeon from the Magus up to the Lord, and then we talked the Lord into giving up a couple yaks.

Lguk is soloable from the mid-30's, depending on where you go. Keep in mind that my pre-50 experience in Lguk is two years old at this point, so this information may be dated. If anything, you'll find more EXP giving MOBs. Standard tactic will be root and undead nuke.

Starting from the dead side entrance, you should be able to squeeze EXP from the live frogs and the wandering dead frogs until about level 38. The Bedroom will start to green out around that same time (or probably light blue now). The Spider Room will give you EXP until around 41 or 42 (again, guessing that they'll turn light blue about that time under current EXP rules). In the 40's, you should be able to kill MOBs in the undead tower (leading to ass/supp room). By 45, you can move down to the "safe hall" and single pull MOBs from around the "jail" area.

Honestly, though, I think this will be a fairly slow method of getting EXP unless you have C2 and a bard singing for you. You can melee, of course, but clerics hit about as hard as sock puppets, so you'll be a while at it. If you really want to move through Lguk, find a 30+ paladin (or two or three) who's soloing and team up with him/them. At 30, paladins get a nifty little spell called Instrument of Nife, which does up to 243 points of damage each time it procs. And it procs fairly well.

You probably won't kill things as fast as you would with a warrior or monk, but I can almost guarantee that you'll frequently find paladins soloing in Lguk. It's the secret leveling spot for paladins up to about 55.
