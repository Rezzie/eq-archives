Depending on who else is in the group, I've done a few different things (while, like you, gritting my teeth).

If there is an enchanter, I ask VERY SWEET AND NICE if the druid/shaman could PLEASE help me to keep the enchanter alive and put them in charge of healing the chanter. I still use remedy now and then but at least they have a 'job' and leave me to do mine.

I also appoint an official backup healer (if there is more than one choice) and tell him/her that when if I need to med or if I can't cast because I'm getting interupted I'll tell him to please take heals.

It seems like if you tell them what you are doing and give them a "job" they are more likely to stick to it.

I also give the group frequent updats as to how much mana I have. This lets the puller know he can pull and also lets the backup healer know he can nuke without fear of me calling on that mana for heals.

All this was flashback to DL days. Thank goodness most druids/shaman figure out by mid 40s that they can't really heal nearly as efficiently as the cleric can and they are happy to let you do it.

The druids/shaman who are jumping in and healing when the Cleric should fall into a couple categories:

1)They didn't realize that you are a cleric. Not a common occurance, but possible.

2)They are relatively new to the game and do not realize just how much better Clerics are at healing.

3)They normally find themselves in Clericless groups and are in the habit of having to play main healer.

4)They don't really CARE that you are better and want to be primary healer.

In case #1, mentioning that you are a Cleric will generally suffice to get them to switch to backup healing/offense/etc

In case #2, you have to take some time to educate them as to what your heals can do vs. what theirs can do.

In case #3, asking them to switch will work for a while... though eventually it is likely they
will revert later and need a reminder.

In the 4th case, you have essentially 3 options 1)leave the group, 2)get the group to kick the druid/shamn out, 3)play backup healer/half-ass nuker (generally NOT a desirable option).


Anyway, most of the Druids/Shaman that I know prefer to not have to do any healing so that they can use thier other abilites (myself included - when I want to be a healer I play my cleric not my shaman).
