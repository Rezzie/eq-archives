Been there, done that with my guild. I think most people have. There is only one way to combat it, lay down firm rules for rezzing. Suggested are:

1) If I am in a group, I will only rez you if you can bring the corpse to me. Even then you may have to wait for me to get the mana to do so-anything else is extremely unfair to both me and the people I am currently grouped with.

2) If I am not in a group, but on the way to join one, I will rez you if you can drag your corpse to a druid/wizard ring and if I can convince the person I am riding with to make a detour. Alternately, the entrance of the zone I am headed to will work as well.

3) If I am not in a group and working on a quest/tradeskills/etc., I will rez you if you can provide reasonable transport for me to and from your body. I shouldn't have to spend more than 20-30 minutes tops traveling. Your time is not more valuable than mine, but I am willing to help if I can.

4) If I'm just logged in and screwing around, we can probably work out a rez just about anywhere.

5) Don't ask me for a rez until your body is in a reasonably safe location. I don't want to get stuck waiting for an hour while your group CRs the bodies from the depths of Chardok.

Post um on your message board, send them out in an e-mail, put them on your web page, make a bunch of hotkeys and say them once a day in /gu. Then live by them. You'll enjoy the game ALOT more and people in your guild will learn that rezzes are something that is nice to have, but can't be counted on all the time. Plus, they will also learn how to be nice to clerics, as they will probably end up having to ask one nearby to come help them if they don't have you for a crutch anymore.
