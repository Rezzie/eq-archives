Shameless copy from my Ex-Guild website:

I'll say this very politley, and very clearly.

Unless you are a Wizard and understand how manaburn works, and the amount of effort that it takes to get, just keep your mouth shut and save yourself from looking like a complete idiot.

I realize that nobody here is ranting to get mana burn nerfed. This is a very touchy subject for me because I'm just now getting to the point where I can gain Manaburn, and the last thing I want to do is see it get nerfed because people that don't understand it, are calling for a nerf.

----

Here are some facts, Manaburn can not be quired until level 59 and even then you must spend 12 AA points in your Arch Type skills. Those 12 points MUST be put into Mental Clarity if you wish to have the fastest path to Manaburn as it requires Mental Clarity III. You can not gain Mental Clarity until level 55.

To purchase Mental Clarity III you must have 6 points in General Abilities. So at this point we are at 18 points before we can even think about buying Manaburn. So once you get to 59, you must have already put 18 points into AA, then you will need yet another 5 points to buy manaburn. Now we are talking about 23 AA pointst to gain Manaburn.

Now lets look into the amount of damage Manaburn really does.

Manaburn takes the total amount of mana left in your mana pool and multiplies that by a random number between 1.5 and 2.0. Zedrick, being a non uber wizard just barely has 3100 mana. So when I get manaburn we are realisticly talking about 6200 damage on a manaburn.

With spending as few as possible AA points on manaburn, we have not gotten Spell Casting Fury at all, so we will have about a 1 in 1000 chance of critting manaburn for 150% at most. So now we are up to 9300 damage on the rare chance that manaburn crits AND you get a 2.0 mana -> damage ratio.

So we'll now look at buying Spell Casting Fury to increase our maximum Manaburn damage. 2 points for SCF-1 (2% chance of crit, for 133% damage), 4 more points for SCF-2 (4% chance of crit, for 166% damage), and 6 points for SCF-3 (7% chance of crit, for 200% damage). I've now brought to our maximum damage up to 12,000 damage in the best case scenerio. Thats still less than 1/2 damage to a Kunark dragon.

So here we are, we've got a wizard that has taken the time to get to 60, spent 35 AA points and now has a 7% chance to crit for double damage. Lets become uber and gain Spell Casting Fury Master. 3 points for SCFM-1 (10% chance to crit for 200%), 6 points for SCFM-2 (12% chance to crit for 200%), and 9 points for SCFM-3 (14% chance to crit for 200%).

We are now at 53 AA points for a fully uber Manaburn wizard. But this still means nothing if you don't have the base mana pool for the conversion. You have to take into consideration that the wizards that are getting Manaburn crits for 18000 dmg have a mana pool of rougly 4500 mana. That is a huge mana pool. The wizards that are getting this kind of mana are in uber guilds, and that same uber guild could just come in and take down the dragon on their off time anyway. If a guild can get this many manaburn wizards together in one spot to do this kind of thing, then hell,they could do the encounter anywhere.

Do people fail to realize that Manaburn takes your mana from FULL to EMPTY in the 6 seconds it takes to cast? Do people fail to realize that this ability can be used once every 2 game days.


----

On another note, Ragefire is a Kunark dragon and only has 32k hp. He could easily be done with fewer than 12 manaburn wizards, but then again we've seen how fast guilds on Tunare can move when something they want is up and it's unrealistic to assume that just because wizards can port directly into Skyfire, that they can amass a force of Manaburn wizards (That have manaburn available to use even) in less time and simply Manaburn Ragefire.

--

People need to stop bitching about manaburn because they heard from a guildie who's got a friend on another server who knows a bard that knows a wizard that was in a manaburn squad that took down some kunark dragon and started farming him. This skill can only give SMALLER guilds like OURS the ability to do dragons we have not yet been able to do. This ability gives the middle level guilds the ability to COMPETE with uber guilds on content that has been TRIVIAL to them for over a year now.
