This isn't really a rant, but it's negative in tone so I didn't want to plague the general board with it.

I'm losing my love for the cleric class quickly. It's not because of any spells we did or didn't get...not because of equipment, or other classes or anything like that.

It's because of the responsibility level. I just finally snapped tonight I guess.

I used to love that about clerics. Along with enchanters, it was ME that determined the fate of my party. If I left, they all had to leave. If I died, they were all screwed. I had the best heals, etc etc etc. You all know the story.

Well, I don't like that responsibility anymore, it's getting too stressful to play the game ESPECIALLY with kunark out now. I can't go afk for even a few moments if I need to, and if I do, I'm made to feel guilty- because without me there, the party ends. I can't get tired and let my performance slack like I did tonight.

Most of all, I can't screw up. ONE screw up- being half a second off on timing a spell- can cost me the life of the party I'm in.

Not only all of this, but it seems to me that clerics are the most criticized class of all. It's like I'm always on performance. I have to try my damndest to do my best when in situations with strangers or people I don't know that well, otherwise word will get out that I'm a "bad cleric". Not only do other classes criticize, but other clerics are the WORST. I know this from experience. http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif I'm constantly criticizing other clerics. Not necessarily out loud, but in my head. We all know that we think we know the best, we are the best.

See tonight I wasn't feeling up to par. I played anyway, and joined my guild in a dungeon. I'll be the first to admit, I made some bad judgement calls (healing people in the wrong order, using an incorrect heal)...I didn't watch my own health (thus resulting in two deaths), and my reactions were a bit slow. No one in my group died though (except me)...but still.

If I were a warrior and I screwed up, the enchanter would yell at me and I'd be back on the right target. As a cleric though? There is no room for error period.

Am I just tired? Am I being a perfectionist? Do I suck? http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif Have any of you ever felt like you'd rather just go play a rogue or something where you just hit a-1-a-2-a-rinse-repeat until something dies?

I've been considering ditching my cleric lately in favor of a class that isn't held so responsible for everything.
