I have discovered Unrest is definitely one of those zones that you really have to be picky with who you are grouping with. I have died so many times because of bad groups in this zone! The only problem, to find the good groups you have to filter through the bad. My advice is as soon as you know you are in a bad group, disband. Don't waste your time. Here soon you can actually solo a bit there. The best time is during off hours when there are less than 10 people in zone. There are a few places that spawn only one mob that you can pull outside, or if you get a bad pull, pull to zone, and then immediately zone back in. You should be able to pick off one of the mobs running back to their spawn spot. Hope this helps!!

Well according to my experience in Unrest (lvl 16 to 18) I can only agree. You need to get a good group or you are dead (some of the times that wont help either). The worst thing with the zone is the trains, not the usual ones, but the ones from second floor that is not announced and that run out the backdoor etc. If you are luck try to sneak in with a lvl 21 - 25 grouup, decent exp if you camp the second floor, but they usually dont want low lvl in them.

I can only give you one advice on what I did when I died the tenth time in as many hours and my exp was going backwords. Go solo in SolRo and Oasis. Solo Mummies, Ghouls, Skellies if you are really careful.
If you like to travel, go to South Karana (I ran there from SolB) and join a Aviac group. If you are luck and find the hermit alone, shout for one more and camp it for the rest of your time on EQ for the day.

I camped it with a nuker and tried it with druid (you will love SoW when the gnomes attack you). The trick is easy, walk up to the house, say something like "I will kill you". He will attack you through the wall and you get him targeted. Cast fear, he runs away. But since it is a wall around the house he wont get out. You nuke him once, and cast fear again (dont root since he then will go hand to hand and he hits hard!) while the nuker takes him out. Med up and start over.
