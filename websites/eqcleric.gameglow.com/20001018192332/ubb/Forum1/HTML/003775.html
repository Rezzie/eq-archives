Hi Marley,

How did your auctions go last night?

I don't know if you saw my post on the subject, but last night was my first chance to test Clarity II. It was interesting.

I don't have a proc'ing weapon, but was fortunate enough to borrow a Yak Mace last night. Basically any time I hit 60% I would stand up and start whiffing. Things I learned:

1) *Gasp* My Offense and 1hb were NOT maxed out. They are now =P.

2) Regen rate of Clarity II is about 1/2 as effective standing vs. sitting. Don't know about the Bards new mana song as I haven't tried it.

3) On most fights I proc'd at least 2 times a fight (150 yak effect damage). Being generous I probably added an average of another 200 or so in melee damage per fight. I'm guessing (have to really run some log parsers to be sure) that I typically added 3-500 damage per fight (sometimes I proc'd 4 or 5 times on one mob).

4) While the hits in the high 20s were nice (Yak mace has lower base than Caduceus, I've had 40s with the Caduceus), it's still kind of wasted on a Cleric. Unless, of course, all your tanks have better already. They certainly did last night.

5) I suspect I would have been more effective adding a nuke or two per fight and sitting back down. I don't have Reckoning yet (*shrugs*), but even two unresisted Retributions would have easily increased the damage output I produced. Furthermore I'm positive I could have medded that back at LEAST as quickly as I gained mana while standing and swinging.

6) If I had a Dawnfire or Truesight fighting the appropriate mobs I'd definitely think my melee was worthwhile, as 2 procs per mob from those would be noticeable. The Yak mace just doesn't pack the same punch.

7) It was kinda fun to be swinging for a change, but I suspect in the long run I'd stick to the most efficient method of killing, which still seems to be, for most of us, healing/med/buff/med/nuke/med.

8) I'll certainly admit that I wasn't bored; I enjoyed watching for the procs. However, I enjoyed playing with Donal's Vambraces for a few nights after I acquired them. I don't anymore (ie. it's not efficient, and I can only kid myself so long).
