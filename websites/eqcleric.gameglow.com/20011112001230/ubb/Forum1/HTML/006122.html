Nothing to do with Clerics, but we were are having an argument.

Now we know from certain sources, that the average bank account ranges from 6000 plat on PvP servers to 15,000+ on established servers.

So each server has a fixed ratio of prices, let's take the EBoot/FBSS standard, where these items are 8,000 to 13,000 plat.

NOW, we introduce an item that takes 5,250 plat out of the game, and is not subsceptable to inflation or price reduction.

So, the actual buying value of the Platinum Piece will go UP, which means that Prices will come DOWN, under economic theory.

BUT, you'll have people who are trying to buy this fixed price item, so they will be trying to sell their items for more, so Prices will go UP.

Opposing Viewpoint : Everyone will try to raise cash, and will sell, so we will get instant drop in price, and prices will remain low due to poor buying power of the platinum piece.

Which is right ? Will prices have a short time rise, followed by 2 or 3 months down the road general price drop ? Or are we missing something else ?


Basically, if Eboots are, say, 10,000 plat now, what price will they be in

1) 1 week ?
2) 1 month ?
3) 2 months ?

What I think will happen is...

There is too much cash in the game. At the same time, people are lazy with selling and as a result, prices drop faster than they *should* by economic theory. In real life, people don't get lazy and drop prices by 10-50% on an item to get rid of it, so economic theory doesn't so much apply.

The resulting exodus of money from the economy *SHOULD* just reset the economy to what it was, say, 2 months ago. Or 5 months, or whatever. Thus, prices should hold stable on things like Efreeti boots, FBSS, etc, while prices on high ticket, newer items, will probably drop some. I can't see 5k leaving a 200k+ bank account really doing much to lower that person's buying ability, however, and since 5k per person may be millions of plat, there are probably billions of plat in the game.

A good Root or other immobilizing spell on the monster you're kiting takes care of that. It's a minor strategic adjustment for the high-level soloists and kiters who relied on their JBoots before.

Just my thoughts on the overall issue.

Bottom line on the EQ economy: it's screwed.

For starters, EQ is built around time sinks and money sinks. Where the hell Verant got their ideas from I don't know. I'm just gald that newer games are apparently learning from EQ's bad example.

Verant built the game economy...item pricing on merchants, buy-sell ratios and all that...to "encourage" (FORCE) player interaction. They failed badly in this respect on two counts.

#1 You put a bunch of people together in a game like this, they're going to interact anyway. It's human nature. You don't have to force it.

#2 Player tradeskills are useless in any sort of long-term effect on the game, except as a diversion from camping...essentially, they're just another form of time sink and money sink.

Let's look at an example of a highly successful game economy: UO. The whole game is built around player trades. Magic items from dungeons, the core of the EQ economy, are merely a sidelight in UO. It all revolves around the players, the items they craft and the gold they make. The NPC merchants help out by giving reasonable prices on both buying and selling...prices that are actually influenced by their inventory, plus you can't just walk up to any vendor and sell anything. What does a tailor want with a sword? That's a nice realistic touch if you ask me. The NPCs also sell only the basic necessities...all the good items are left to the players.

I personally am looking forward to Shadowbane and DAOC. Shadowbane will only have tradeskills as related to the main focus of the game, which they openly admit is combat. So you'll have fletching, smithing, skills like that. DAOC is going to have a more full-featured economy with player trades being very important to the game.

I'm also looking forward to the day that I can walk into a full 3D world game and make a tradeskill character that never has to pick up a weapon, or even leave town. the NPC merchants will supply the basics at reasonable prices, while I supply the good stuff. Then games will have arrived at a much more realistic level of trades and economy.
