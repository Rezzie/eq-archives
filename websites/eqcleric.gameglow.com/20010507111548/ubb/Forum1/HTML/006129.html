As a ranger, I track buffs very carefully. The worst offenders in the overbuffing department seem to be druids and shamen - I frequently have discussions with these classes to explain that I don't want buffs unless I request a specific buff. I now tell all groups I join not to buff me or cast group buffs without warning me. I send tells when the druid forgets and casts his 4 group buffs (circles, pack regen, pack SoW/WF) despite my plea to stop, and sometimes I have left groups where the others in the group wont listen to me or to the cleric.

I usually want the following buffs when available - 3 cleric buffs, Talisman, Str, Agi, Haste, my buffs (CoE, Spikecoat, SoN, CoS), SoW/WF if I am pulling, DS if I am primary tank (which I am a lot), and Clarity. This is 13 buffs with no resists. With no Shaman, I cast my own Str and Agi, so I will still have 12 buffs with no resists in lots of situations. I almost always group with a cleric (my wife) and an enchanter.

Against casting mobs I add whatever resists seem appropriate and drop my buffs as needed to keep 2 spots open (one for a DoT, one for Celestial healing). I drop Call of Sky and my Damage Shield if I need to do this. I rarely want Dexterity, Boon of Garou, Stamina or regen, and never want them over my 13 "normal" buffs.

If people seem touchy about buffs, it is because buff management is difficult at high levels. Also, smart rangers self buff first so they can recast their own buffs after a dispell or two - I usually have the top 7-8 slots filled with my buffs, so even fighting cubes in Fear I often end fights with full buffs after I recast my own.
