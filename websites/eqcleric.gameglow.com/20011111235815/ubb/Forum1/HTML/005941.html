As Rayne pointed out in another thread, NBG can be abused alot. Especially in pick-up groups. I figure we could start a thread and air it out a bit.

First, we should define NBG.

#1: If an item drops and someone in the group needs it (i.e. doesn't currently have that item or a better item) then that group member gets it, automatically.

#2: If the person who could use the item has it or something better, or there is no one in the group who can use it, then it gets lottoed to the whole group.

Personally, since I have returned, I have only received 2 items, BOTH due to NBG. Everything else I have was purchased. And NBG was not even heard of before I left.

One night, in KC, my group was fighting near zone, when somehow, someone pulled the Drolvarg Warlord (and nothing else) to the zone. We jumped it, killed it and got Donal's Boots. They handed me the boots immediatly and had no issues at all. I wore them for 3 days, and was offered 7k and a Spirit Tome for them. Seeing how I was still wearing mostly borrowed gear, I sold (way underprice, so the Cleric who bought them got a real good deal) and went shopping.

About 3 days after that, I was in the Hand room of KC and we pulled the Warlord and a pair of Donal's Boots dropped. Again, the group didn't hesitate and gave them to me. About a week later, I saw Ninda auctioning to buy a pair, and she offered 20kpp for them. I declined. I didn't need the cash, I was equipped in my own gear, and I didn't really want to part with the boots, not even for such a sweet deal. I still have the boots and will not sell until I get something better. And even then, I may just pass the boots down to a guildmember.

Were it not for NBG, I would still be wearing borrowed gear, and I would still be broke. I have watched countless high end items drop in KC that went to the rangers/monk/whoever, that I didn't even get the chance at, due to NBG, and I do not begrudge them their item at all. On all items that dropped that my group(s) rolled for, I have never won a single one. But I feel good knowing that I was only rolling for an item that no one else needed. Had I had to roll for both pairs of my boots, and lost them to another class, I would have felt cheated.

How do the rest of you feel about NBG?

How we handle things in my group (regular group, all guild mates):

-> Those who NEED the item roll. Usually it's just awarded, but if two feel they need it they can just roll. None of us are exactly hurting for loot =P.

-> If nobody needs the item then we look at the guild as a whole. If we think there is someone in the guild who would give them item a nice home we hold for later.

-> Otherwise, we all roll.

-> If someone who was awarded an item TRADES/SELLS that same item, the next time it drops they are not considered for the item unless we all roll.

The only place these rules exist formally is my head (and therefore not formal), but that's how we've handled things for a long time now.

As I said in another thread I don't do pickup groups (with an SK and Cleric I've already got the basis of a group, I can always borrow a friend's Shaman or Enchanter if I need to solo), but if I DID do a pickup group, I'd roll on anything that wasn't No Drop. If I want/need an item I get my friends to camp it for me, not 5 strangers. I've read so many horror stories, both pro/con NBG, that straight /lotto just seems the best way to deal with it.

I used to feel differently and laid out very stringent rules to strangers (ie. NBG was the rule) who joined my group. It's just not worth it to me anymore, so I don't invite strangers and don't have loot hassles to deal with =P.

As far as Dikmer's case, well, IF he explained to his group that this was his SECOND pair of boots, and why he sold his first,etc, and they STILL awarded to him, great. Otherwise, the second should have been /lotto'd, in my opinion.

I suspect my opinion on items is very jaded since I don't do pickup groups, so take what I say with a big grain of salt.

The biggest problem with NBG is, what defines Need? A cleric could argue "I need those cobalt gauntlets" based on the fact that if they win, they can then trade them for something he does need. Any item that is considered NBG for that group, can be argued by everyone as being needed by them.

Now the other side. Generally as you get up in levels, you start to get to know everyone. As such, being nice and being generous (i.e. being a good group mate) will always work in your favor. Your reputation will determine how others react to you. As such, if you get the label of greedy, many will not associate with you, invite you on raids, etc. In addition you will often find yourself grouping with many of the same people. In this situation it is to your advantage in the long run for them to also be well equipped.

IMHO there's a lot of good arguments on both sides. Many more than I stated here. As long as people state up front the method being used, as well as being honest about it, I'm happy. Hey, I'm 54th level and I'm still wearing a Foremans tunic. Equipment, while always nice, is not my primary goal.

Need Before Greed is something I try very hard to adhere to. My guild's loot policy reflect the concepts as well. Of course I rarely hunt in pickup groups. Normally I have at least one other guildmate and when our policys are presented most people like them.

Basically we have have 4 rules we follow.


  1. Loot goes to who can and will use the item.
  2. When no one *needs* the item, we either give to a guildmate that does need or for whomever's seconds need.
  3. Everyone gets one item before anyone gets a second item.
  4. Once everyone has one item, it starts back over at the begining.

We try to enforce this when we have pickup groups as well as full guild groups. On raids the policy is to have the senior guild officer attending the raid decide who gets what. Works when on alliance raids between 3 guilds as well. Though I tend to always think others will need something more then I would, so as Guild Leader I get very few items.

Our 40's crowd has been romping in City of Mist lately. Few NBG situations we had come up. First day a Goo drops Heroic Bond, which was promptly handed to me, the only cleric. (Talk about stunned!) Second day, lead by a mad bear, we take down Lord Ghiosk. Low and behold a Thorneblade drops. Thing was, the warrior (ally) didnt want it! So with group consensus it went to my warrior. Third day there with myself, guild enchanter and rogue, we pick up an ally warrior again. An apparition drops a GJB and without even thinking we handed it to the warrior. (Lucky fellow!)
From all accounts we had some amazing luck getting loot there, as most everyone walked away with something (2 rings, 2springwood clubs, 3wintry clubs, 2wrathbone hammers, 1GJB, 1Thorneblade Yak, level 52 spell and few other things I have forgotten now) in the two weeks we have been there.

Yes this has the opportunity to be abused, BUT with the rule that "Everyone gets something before anyone gets a second" helps cut down hard feelings.

I beleive that what benefits one, benefits us all. Thus should a guild warrior get some uber loot, thats GREAT that they got it! It only HELPS us as a guild be more effective.

Almost all my gear, when it becomes outdated or improved, gets donated down to whoever in the guild needs it. So should I fully retire my warrior I will pass the Thorneblade down. Course that makes me rather poor, but rich in friends.
