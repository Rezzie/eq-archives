Clerics are the masters of healing. With their great healing and resurrection spells, and devastating DD spells against the undead and stuns, they are the masters of life and death. Primary attributes for Clerics according to Verant are STR, STA, and WIS. As a WIS based caster, the order or importance for statistic value I feel is WIS/AC/STR/STA/AGI/DEX/INT/CHA.

There is a perceived difference in performance based on statistics reaching certain levels. The best known level is 75, if you have a statistic at lower than 75, you are taking a penalty in that field. The next lvl seems to be around the 100 mark, some say the 120 mark, and then somewhere between 150 and 200. I would imagine there is an additional benefit to hitting the 200 mark in any category(or there ‘should’ be. Silly Verant and their VisionTM).

WIS affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT
AC to get hit less often and for less damage
STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills
STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath
AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

For the last 3 stats; DEX is how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows: and is really only useful for a cleric that has a weapon that proc’s.
INT directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights, and CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

Mana:
Each point of WIS = 10 pts
Each point of MANA = 1 point

AC:
Each point of AC = 4 pts
Every point of AGI = 1 pt

Hit Points:
Every 2 hit points = 1 pt
Every point of STA = 2 pts

Other:
Every point of STR = 2 pt
Each point of DEX/INT/CHA = 0 points
Every 2 points of fire/cold/magic = 1 pts
Every 4 points of poison/disease = 1 pts
Haste, Enduring Breath, Levitate = 20 pts
Offensive/healing spell castable = 30 pts

Awesome job Bryll. A million thanks for your hard work.

I will point out something, which is that the stated AC on an item does not fully reflect the amount of AC increase when equipped. This is due to a bug. Stated AC for an item reflects the "avoidance" component of defense but does not include the "absorption" component, while the AC of your character is a composite of both of these (plus numerous other factors).

There is thus basically no way to tell the true effect of item AC on character AC except to equip it. You will notice that two different items with the same stats (same AC, agi, etc.) can have radically different effects on your character's AC.

I did not understand the value of STA and so have learned something theoretical from this. I would also say, personally, that +CHA is more important than INT or DEX (which are practically worthless for a cleric). At least CHA has a pervasive effect financial effect on those of us who are, er, facially challenged.

I don't see how you could have done much better really, this list is extremely useful. Clearly a list can never be perfect. Personally, because it is so rare and you'd have to completly rethink your armor to carry it, I'll just stick with my Paw and CGS.
