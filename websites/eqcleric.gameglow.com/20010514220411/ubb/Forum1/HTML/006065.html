Have you seen the stats on Imbued Dwarven Plate armor? Or even better, Enchanted Imbued Dwarven Plate armor? It's the best thing right up to (and in some cases, better) Ethereal Mist from Hate. Got a couple thousand platinum and some time to invest? Well, here's how I did it:

Total cost to me was 900pp and I didn't sell a thing to another player. I have 190wis, 69int, 120str, 80ishcha.

First thing I did was went to Kaladim (North Freeport will also work). Now, admittedly, with 4 Tinkerer Bags, I could carry alot more than the average person, so I am sure it went faster for me than most people.

Went to the mine (next to bank) and filled my 4 bags with Small Bricks of Ore. 40 bricks. Ran up the hall towards the Cleric guild and stopped at the female dwarf at the end of the tunnel. Bought 2 stacks of water. Then ran the to the forge outside of Everhot Forge (notice, this is all in the same zone).

1 Small Brick of Ore + 1 water + file = Steel Bonings.

Did that to 41 skill. Using the SAME materials, I started making Sheet Metal.

1 Small Brick of Ore + 1 Small Brick of Ore + 1 water = Sheet Metal.

This trivials at 56. Now, the kicker. Since the bank is right next to the mine, grab yourself a few backpacks and toss the sheet metal into the bank. You'll use it later anyway.

Now, once I hit 56, I went to Freeport. East Freeport. Took my bags of Sheet Metal with me, and bought a bunch of Banded Gorget molds.

1 Sheet of Metal + 1 Banded Gorget mold + 1 water = a Banded Gorget.

This will get you all the way to 102. If you run out of Sheet Metal, well the vendor that sells the molds also sells the Sheet Metal. Total time for me was 3 hours, total cost about 250pp.

I then did Banded Boots. 1 sheet, 1 mold, 1 water.
At 123 I switched to Belts. 2 sheets, 1 mold, 1 water.

At 135 I went to Mantles and Helms. 2 sheets, 1 mold, 1 water.

At 168 it is Cloaks all the way to 175. 2 sheets, 1 mold, 1 water.

Now, if you sell to players as you go, you make more of your money back, but I was in it to get to 175 asap. I did not want to invade the other lower level smiths market, so I sold my stuff to the vendor. I only actually lost money on the fails, as after gorgets, it seemed to me the items were selling for a silver or so more than the cost of the sheet metal + mold + water.

After 175 it got expensive and tedious. I'll write another guide on 175+ when I get around 200 skill. I blew about 2k making a bunch of Fine Steel (11 Bracers, 4 BP's and 3 Vambraces), and only gained 2 skill (to 177). The hardest part was killing spiderlings and animals to make the Leather Padding. VI REALLY needs to add some tailoring vendors that sell supplies.

You can purchase the MQ ore needed for fine steel plate in Steamfont at the windmills. HOWEVER, you can not purchase "blocks" only "large bricks". That means you have to add the extra step of combining "large bricks" to "blocks". This makes it more of a pain, but an option. Bind a Everhot's forge in Kaladim, run to Steamfont, buy all the ore you can, gate to forge, make sheets, get molds from Everhots has needed, sell back successes to your dwarven vendors for good prices.

If you have a second account, mules are the way to go. Various ways to use a mule to speed up the process of smithing lots of fine steel for skill gain. Seems the best way is to fill up a mule and his bank with 120 molds and some water and park him next to HH forge. If you can, get him some 10-slot storage devices for 140 or so molds. If possible, powerlevel the mule (has to be caster of course) so he can gate (and bind is helpful) to make fetching water and more molds quicker.
