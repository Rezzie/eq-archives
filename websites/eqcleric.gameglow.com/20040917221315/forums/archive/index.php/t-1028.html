I have a question. I don't want this to turn into a flame-fest, so please don't take this as monk bashing, but I really need to get some advice.

Recently, I’ve spent a lot of time hunting in Dalnir. Dalnir is a great dungeon for level 25 to 35 in Kunark. There are plenty of MOBs, lots of great spots to hunt, and the opportunity to actually crawl the dungeon (especially considering how unpopulated it usually is). BUT, (and this is the big BUT) Dalnir has TONS of monk items, including some no drop items, so there are more monks around than I’ve ever seen. So here’s my problem. I’ve never grouped with more than one monk at a time before here. And I understand that it is nice to have a monk who can FD and prevent bad pulls and split MOBs for good pulls. Let me be clear. This is not the first, or only time I’ve grouped with monks. I’ve been here in Dalnir for quite a while, rarely without having one in my group. This WAS the first time I’ve ever had three in one group. The end result was me, dead, due to the amazing amount of healer aggro I had to deal with. Chain healing three different monks is not good for the hatelist. Anyway. Any advice you have would be great. As it stands, I’m pretty hesitant about adding a monk to my group if we already have one. And at this point, will not be grouping with three of them again.

I always request/demand/beg the monk to FD when he gets back with a pull. Sometimes I have to tell them flat out that I will not heal them if they are not FD, that usually gets there attention. This practice does 2 things, one is that it gets the MOB onto someone who can be more efficiently healed. The other is it prevents me from drawing any agro from the MOB. Monks usually are beet up a bit when they get back from spliting a pull and need an imediate heal, not a good way to start off a fight if I want to be able to sit back down. =)

In situations where mana efficiancey is important, I will request that the monk FD if he starts tanking. Most good monks will understand the reasons for this if you take a few seconds to explain it to them.

To any monks out there that see this, just because your ac may be higher than that warrior your grouped with doesn't mean you are just as good or better at tanking. First of all, monks don't have near the hp as a warrior, so even if its a situation where I can consistantly CH a monk, its not as efficient as on a warrior.
Second, a lot of your ac is a different kind of ac than warriors have. There are different components to AC, the number you see as your AC is just a BS agragate number that Verant gives us to look at and compare with eachother. Monks armor and defense skills give them better damage avoidance than warriors. But the type of ac warriors get from thier armor gives them much better average damage reduction than monks have. Basicly, on average, monks will get hit less often than a warrior, but when they do get hit, the average damage they take is higher.
Not sure I stated that as clearly as I wanted, no, I am not saying there is such a thing as damage reduction from armor types, that officially doesn't exist.

Hmm sounds to me like u might have got ur hands on some pseudo monks.

Yes monks take a little more healing in the pulling phase but generally beat the dang critter to a pulp b4 it hurts them that badly again. With 2 monks u should have been going thru things like a buzz saw.

The most important thing when dealing with monks is the following question: Mend up?. Most good monks will FD pull take a bubble of damage splitting, make it back to the group and then hit mend at 2 bubbles down; pop they are healed. No cleric aggro from the massive heal u just laid on them, no fear or need to worry.

Problems occur when u have a monk who doesn't understand that mend recycles every 6 minutes its there to be used, is a twink with gear that makes them feel invulnerable , or plain just doesn't understand the monk class.

Sounds like u had multiple problems in Dalnir, starting with a full tank group. 4 tanks will tap your mana bigger time then just 2 monks. I hope that the paly was handling out of combat heals, because even with that u were gonna need serious downtime after dropping the symbol or even the ac buff cycle on the group. Also were u using GH to start off the heal cycle or throwing 2 heals? A GH at those levels is truly a way to say CLERIC over here COME HIT ME. Crowd control in that group was well in the hands of the healer hence none as u were probably in sit med heal mode. Was the puller maybe pulling a little too fast? Also where in Dalnir? the goo area? by the port pad? in the drop room? If u were in the goo caves things can really turn bad because of the closeness of the spawns.

Dunno exactly what happened to u, but sounds like a case of low mana, no back up, and too frequent pulling. Me I'll take the 3 monks and slappem into shape. One getting real low on hit points let him FD a few times clear the aggro stand up and keep pounding, no need to heal him with a biggee cause he ain't or shouldn't be getting hit. 3 monks, heh, Monk A flops to the ground and twitches a few times , critter turns to Monk B, Monk A stands back up pops the critter for more, Monk B does the gigged fish dance, Critter turns to Monk C, etc...

While I've never done a 3 monk paly I have done a 2 monk 1 warrior, and my biggest concern was time in between to get to 40 or 60 mana. 40 was pushing it but 60 left me some to spare. 30 was possible but it really comes down to communication and understanding between the puller and the healer. Yes I prefer to stand up throw some stuns, root park a critter out of the way, maybe throw some blows with my hammer, but there are times u just have to bite the bullet and go into heal med cycle.

The only other thing I might add here is that monks don't need to be healed fully. They are just fine as long as u can get back to them so that 1 maybe 2 blows won't take them down. Its the same reason why you keep sitting down when the paly or ranger or caster gets hit for 1 bubble down of health. Its more important in the long run to conserve the mana for the folks who will need it then to blow it when u low or swamped.
