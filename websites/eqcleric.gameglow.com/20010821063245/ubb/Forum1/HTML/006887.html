Without knowing anything more than what has been posted, I'll assume this is a classic "We saw Ragefire and you ran in and took him from us" whine.

First off, killing Nagafen and/or FGs doesn't entitle you to a spawn. It may be considered a dirty, underhanded trick to wait until another guild has cleared the FGs and run in, but those are the rules of the game. In this case, the FGs were dead and people were leaving when Ragefire spawned. The argument that Phantom Raiders were using the previous guild's hard work falls apart at that point becuase 1. they were clearly not there to camp Ragefire, only to clear FGs 2. if Ragefire had spawned after they were all gone the argument would never have come up.

Secondly, the guild clearly lacked the people to kill Ragefire. The winner of the Ragefire race is he who turns the pearl in first and kills Ragefire. If you have a force you think can kill him then don't wait, hand it in. If you fail you'll be at the mercy of the other guild. Like it or not, Phantom Raiders won the race. They handed in first.

Is this system fair? Of course not. It favors the fast mobilizers over the small guilds who have to call people to get them on. But that's the way things are and you have to live with it. You weren't camping Ragefire, you were clearing FGs and you're upset another guild beat you out.

quote:

To put this bluntly, there isn't. If you're not camped in the lair with a dragon force you must accept that most likely you will either run someone over or be run over yourselves on the way in to Ragefire. You either accept you won't get your epic or you accept that you will probably have someone slinging mud at you verbally in /shout and /ooc as you turn your pearl in.

I don't like it. I never have. I tried for some time to change it but Verant is satisfied with the way things are so you either play by these rules or you don't play at all.
