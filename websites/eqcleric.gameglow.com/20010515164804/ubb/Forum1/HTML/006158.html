Posted this in the developer's corner. Hope they read and respond to it.

Greetings!

I'd like to submit some comments regarding the cleric epic quest item.

First, a list of significant encounters needed to obtain the epic item:

1) A tussle with Lord Bergurgle. Not exceptionally difficult, but he is a rare spawn with a long timer. Clerics frequently spend dozens of hours at this camp.

2) A fight with a Plasmatic Priest- significant enough to demand a minimum of a full group of 50+ characters. A rapid enough respawn time to make the wait negligable.

3) An attack on Ixiblat Fer- Ixiblat is a true epic mob, far more powerful than any pre-buffed God. Taking him down will require several groups of 50+ characters all working together. Ixiblat is a triggered spawn.

4) Fighting lord Gimblox- A trivial battle for anyone that could reasonably get the epic. But Gimblox is a rare spawn on a medium length timer. Again, a long wait is usually in order.

5) Fighting the overking of Chardok-By far the most difficult encounter. Several groups of 55+ adventurers must be along to fight this monstrosity. On top of this, the overking is guarded by many guards that by themselves are extremely powerful. A VERY difficult and challenging encounter. There is a two hour respawn on the King, but it doesn't really matter, since the sheer manpower and skill needed for this encounter requires far more than two hours to plan.

6) Finally, killing Ragefire- Almost a let down compared to the overking, you must fight two succesive spawns of 55th level monsters. Still somewhat challenging to all but the most powerful groups.

The spawn time of the 6th encounter is what I would like to talk about. Presently, Ragefire is on an extremely random and extremely long (72 hour) spawn timer. Moreover, he cannot spawn while Nagafen is present, making him even more rare. The result of this is that clerics (and their guilds) have no choice but to employ silly tactics like leaving a monk permanently feigned in the Fire Giant area, waiting for the pop.

Even worse, there are many clerics waiting for ragefire, which means that once he spawns, there is a race for the spawn. Such races inevitably lead to bad blood and feuding between clerics (and guilds!). A situation like this only promotes aggravation, ill-will, immaturity, greed, and a slew of other undesirable results.

Is it really necessary to limit the number of water sprinklers in such a confrontational manner? I would hypothesize that at least two of the previous encounters serve as excellent "weeding-out" tests. Only the most skillful and most respected clerics would be capable of marshalling a force powerful enough to kill Ixiblat and the overking. And yet the final encounter is so frustratingly rare that these elite few are forced to wind up bickering with one another, when it's fairly obvious that they all deserve the weapon.

I'd ask that you review the ragefire spawn. By all means, make it more difficult- promote ragefire to 60th+ level. Give him powerful guards. Do anything to assure that only worthy clerics are able to defeat him. But make him more common, more predictable. He doesn't have to be a triggered spawn. Why not put him on a twelve hour timer? Making him more powerful but somewhat more frequent would have very much the same effect on rarity of the water sprinkler, but with far less aggravation than the spawn causes now.

Thanks for the attention.

I'm going to dodge the whole "who deserves to get an epic" issue since it's obviously something that Verant determines. However, I see two major issues with the epics in general and ours specifically:

1) The difficulty of the epics for the various classes is incredibly disparate and frankly that is just not fair.

2) Our epic is too damned easy to get to the last step, and if it wouldn't be for the incredible amount of Clerics waiting to complete the entire quest would be a breeze.

Comments on #1:
Some classes have a very easy (relatively speaking) epic to complete compared to others. I think most can agree that Rogues and Shamans go to the top of the ease list with Wizards and Mages at the bottom. I don't understand how Verant can create quests where a Mage has to get to Isle 7 in Sky to get a final item whereas a Rogue merely has to kill a mob in Kithicor, but what's new right? The Shaman quest's final mob can be duo'd by a Mage and a Cleric (I know because we did it). Wizards not only have to be in a good guild to kill VS, but in addition have to be online when the servers come up since the half-life on Venril Sathir is around 15 minutes from spawn, so tough crap if you have a normal job on the West coast. My problem with the quests in general is VS is just full of it when they say the quests are meant to be "epic." There should be absolute parity and currently there is nothing near it.

Comments on #2:
I think we need to be honest and admit except for the final bottleneck the Cleric quest is damned easy. It's boring, it takes time, but it's easy. Bergurgle, Shmendrick and the Lava Elemental can be easily duo'd, the Plasmatic Priest is a cinch to do with a Trio (Shaman/Cleric/melee) and the Overking is easy for two good 55+ groups (he is, really). Ixiblat can be tough, no question about it, but he's still doable and many many Clerics get there sceptres during an "Ixi Party."

So with the quest being too easy what happens is we get an incredible number of Clerics getting to the Ragefire point. Since we have so many Clerics reaching this point, and only 2-3 per server per week getting completed (that's when there aren't server downs) we have an increasingly growing number of frustrated Clerics. It definitely sucks to be at the final stage of a quest along with 50+ other people on your server. And to make matters worse the problem is increasing since I'd bet that more Clerics are getting to the bottleneck each week than Clerics completing the quest.

I wrote Absor a few weeks ago (I'm sure he just deleted, he never responded) outlining some problems (KSing, conflict, bottleneck, etc) along with some suggested solutions:

1) Make Plasmatic Priest much harder. For example, level 60 and totally MR would be a start.

2) Make Overking harder.

3) Make Ixiblat harder.

4) Make epic effect only work at 56+. Since I've heard that effects can only be set at 50, then make it so the quest givers only talk to 55+ Clerics. Sorry for all you lower level Clerics, but my feeling is you should be able to cast the spell before you can use the effect. Go level up a bit.

5) Leave Ragefire where he is, but add a triggerable mob (ala Feydedar) that is low 60s and requires 4-5 good groups to kill. This allows the weaker guilds a chance to get an easy Ragefire while the stronger guilds can go after the triggered mob. Put the trigger mob on a 12/24 hour timer.

Frankly I'd love to see Verant really retune all of the quests to achieve parity, but I have little hope they will do it.

I don't think it matters if we agree on how to fix the issue. While I appreciate the issue you've raised frequently, that is, the fact that Ragefire is dependent upon the level capped Nagafen, I'm more interested in how to alleviate the Ragefire bottleneck.

As I alluded to earlier I think the bottleneck is caused by two issues:

1) An increasing number of Clerics reaching the bottleneck;
2) The rarity of the spawn.

So, my feeling is Verant should take some steps to reduce the number of Clerics reaching the bottleneck while also giving us more chances at Ragefire. By giving us a much tougher, triggerable Ragefire in addition to the one that currently exists the needs of both small and large guilds are met. And by reducing the number of Clerics able to reach the bottleneck we (hopefully) wouldn't have that great an increase in the number of Cleric epics (er, after the intial backlog was depleted). Verant will be interested in keeping the numbers down.

When I play I look for an adventure, and I don't consider looking at the walls or killing greenies for hours at a time an adventure.
