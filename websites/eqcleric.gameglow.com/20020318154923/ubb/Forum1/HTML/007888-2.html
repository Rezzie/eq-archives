More 2cp from another ranger/cleric.

Adon, if you only lost the MT twice in the arena, especially pulling protectors of Zek and the like, I think you did a great job, regardless of the class of the main tank. Especially as it sounds as if that wasn't your regular hunting group.

On the other hand, the only times I've hunted in the arena, we've had either an SK OR a ranger as MT. I didn't see much difference in MT survivability rates. However, I usually am not the cleric for the MT group, and at times have even been playing a ranger, so I'm not sure how much harder the MT group cleric had to work. But the deaths were usually not the fault of a slow CH, but rather things like the puller eating a death rather than train the group with a bad pull. Or things hitting the fan, and the cleric was torn between keeping the chanter alive and the MT alive.

With respect to difficulty in playing either class, having played both, they can be both be difficult but for different reasons. On the cleric side, the level of responsibility is so high sometimes, it's almost like a job. I've already got a job in real life, I play games to relax and enjoy. And it can be tough to do that sometimes as a cleric, and what happened to Adon is a prime example of that, no matter how much you enjoy keeping people alive.

Playing a ranger is a lot more fun, although quite challenging as well. It takes some effort for a ranger to avoid aggro, even though the MT in my groups is usually a level 59 SK, and my ranger just hit level 53. While I'm trying to avoid aggro, at the same time, I'm trying to keep my aggro up high enough so that I can grab it quickly if necessary to save the chanter or cleric. I've got two systems and so can play both characters at once, but my second system is not laid out conveniently. I can play my cleric on it, but not my ranger.

With respect to the min/max thing, it's a no brainer that a heavy metal tank, one with the most HPs and ACs, makes a better MT theoretically (although I'd look askance at a warrior using a two-hander). On a practical basis, if a ranger is the MT, it's because he or she was the best available. You have two choices then. You can say "hmmm, this could be an interesting challenge." Or you can say "hmmm, this is gonna be too hard, I'm gonna find something easier to do."

Again, kudos to you, Adon, for accepting the challenge, and sorry to hear that it only caused you grief.
