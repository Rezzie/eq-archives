Hehe, now that I got your attention, I want to bring up a new style of fighting that I have not seen very often. Necros using it a lot, and some clerics use it when soloing.

It is the snare/fear technique. I have been using this the past 2 days in Burning Woods at the Giant Camp (loc = 600, 900). Basically instead of meleeing everything (my groups now usually have me, plus 3-4 tanks, with a bard) and having me pop endless heals, you get a druid/ranger to snare a mob (even a bard works in a pinch with chain), then, cast your L39 invoke fear. The lesser level ones work too, but they dont last as long. We can plow through a wurm with me maybe popping one heal (ideal situation), the party full health, and me down about a bubble of mana.

Stuns also work well, especially if another party member (paladin, etc.) is doing the stunning. Occasionally at L51 I get a resist on the fear, so I just throw a quick stun, and the fear recycles by then.

Basically we have non-stop pulls, and the only time I stop is to res other groups. In about 12 hours over 2 days, no one in my group has died, although I have performed over 10 resses. Experience after L50 is hellish (only got 1.5 bubs or so in about 18-20hrs, although it wasnt all non-stop pulls), but it is a lot of fun.

For best results, get a rogue.

Enjoy.
