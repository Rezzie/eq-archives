Clerics aren't the only class to have spells that are "must have" and very rare. For rangers, Call of Earth (26 ac, 4pt damage shield) is a very important spell. I could have camped for the spell, but that would have taken days (on average) hunting areas that don't offer much for me. Instead, I told people hunting in PoG(I won't hunt there) and Velk that I would pay 10k for the spell. At the same time I hunted the Crypt in Seb and made 3 bubs of exp in 2 days and made 20K+ in item drops (most of which I gave away in the end) and got my spell from a friend when it dropped in PoG (he actually let me have it of 5k - very nice guy).

4k is a small price to pay for avoiding the annoyance of a camp you don't want to do. If you want to camp Velk, don't pay. If you want to go elsewhere to camp, let people in Velk know you want the spell and will pay a premium (heck offer 6k - 1k for each group member). Spells, like anything else in the game, are a commodity and the only effective medium of exchange is pp. If you don't have enough money to buy/trade for the spell, you are not camping the right places. Gems drop so commonly in Seb that you are doing badly if you don't make 1k a night in gems alone if you have a decent group.

The only difference with spells is that EVERY cleric must have ONE. This means the market is constantly expanding, but at a slower rate than drops, so people will wind up with this spell sitting in the bank. If you wait a couple of months, the spell will be much cheaper, but you must decide if you will pay more to have it NOW.
