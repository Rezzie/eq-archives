This is culled from a thread on our flame boards.. thought the synopsis might be a useful topic for discussion here.
-- http://pub54.ezboard.com/fquelliousquaterstherebirthfrm13.showMessageRange?topicID=203.topic&start=1&stop=20

1) This camp can be "claimed." That is to say, one force given rights to first kill attempt and thereby, rights to the loot if successful. But it order for it to be claimed, the cleric must have a killing force present during the camp. If the force dwindles, the cleric will be given a brief period to replenish the force by whatever means within their sphere of influence. If the force cannot be replenished or the cleric leaves the zone for a reasonable amount of time, the claim is lost.

2) A single cleric at the camp has no legitimate claim rights as described in #1.

3) If multiple single clerics are contending for the camp and Ragefire spawns, whichever one who engages Ragefire with killing force first has rights to the kill. (This is effectively a race to get your people in the zone, to the spawn.)

4) Kiting does not count as killing force engagement and thus gives no right to the kill.

5) If one force fails* their attempt, they lose their claim and any killing force standing by is entitled to attempt the kill. If multiple killing forces are standing by, it would be FCFS.


Those are the rules the GM's consider when making an arbitration about contention in this spawn.

The Server GM said he and other server GM's are trying to get this quest changed yet again because of the contention problems.

---------------------------------------------

Q) In GM # 5, if the first attempt fails, but no one else engages the mob while the first triggering group recovers, can the first group re-engage when ready or do they have to wait on anyone else who wants to take a turn before taking another shot?

A.) If the first force is recooperated, ready, and able to kill and the second one is not completely or very nearly ready (like 5-10 mins from away from readiness) then yes, they may go again. If the second force is completely ready and able to kill (in which case they probably would have immediately engaged it upon the disengagement of the first force), then no, the first force must wait their turn (and they probably won't get one, but you never know).

Q.) What is "fail"? Is it merely the reduction of your killing force to the point that the kill is not possibile (only feigners or offline rezzers left alive, no one dealing damage)? Does "failure" only come when the attackers cry "Uncle" and leave?<<

A.) It would be the first case. Failure means the mob is disengaged. For example, if all that's left is feigners, camped out clerics, and a bard or druid kiting around Ragefire (remember kiting does not count as engagement) til the force can recoop, they have failed and their turn is over.

couple of notes on this for you.

Mobs that summon do not untill you have caused at least 1hp of dammage to them, via DOT, DD, mele or ranged attack.

Thus you can cause a mob to agro on you by attempting to (for example) snare them.

Weather the snare lands or not is irrelivant, that mob is now agroed on you, and will not summon you.

this does cause a problem, because then anyone attacking the mob other than you, or with your permission, is in breach of the PnP.

VI could fix (nerf?) this by making Ragefire summon regardless of damage, as soon as he has you on his hate list, he summons you.

Were this the case then no single toon would be able to live long enough to effectively kite Ragefire.

Ragefire can be killed by a single group of level 59-60 toons, equipped with ntov or better gear.

as noted in another thread, L60 war, pair of shammies with torpor, and a L60 chanter could take Ragefire although it would take an age. Add in a good cleric, and a L60 ranger, monk or rogue for damage output, perhaps replace one of the shammies with a L60 wizard or mage (Sorry, I just love the pets ;o) and you have what is in my opinion the smallest group that can effectively take on ragefire.

Logan -
Rule #3 of the Play Nice Policies
quote:
There are cases where two or more groups wish to kill the same thing. In these cases, the groups are required to compromise. If an equitable compromise cannot be reached between the players prior to EverQuest Customer Service Staff involvement, the EQCSR will mandate a binding compromise. Refusing to abide by a compromise mandated by an EQCSR will be considered disruption. It is therefore strongly suggested that the groups make every attempt to reach a compromise that they can live with prior to involving an EQCSR, who may mandate a compromise that does not suit you to the extent that a player-devised compromise would.

Note: A "group" in this case is defined as a party of one or more characters that are united in a common belief or goal and are capable of completing that goal.

The rules presented in the 1st post in this thread are what the GMs of that server will use to settle the arbitration. In fact, the arbitration is already pre-done... If you want to come to some other arrangement you can, but there is no benefit to the the group who has been holding the camp (and would under that servers rules be awarded first claim to the mob) to agree to any other arrangements...

The meaning of the Note that Verant put on this makes it pretty clear that if you don't have any chance of killing it, it isn't yours. A single person kiting the mob around while his buddies gather/prep doesn't count as having any claim to it so long as his friends aren't ready to engage...
