Just a reply about enchanters and mesmerize.

Harbron mentioned how mesmerized mobs always go after the enchanter after it breaks. Unlike atone, mesmerize does not wipe the hate list, but it is a wonderful spell to park mobs. There is a small chance for mes to clear the mob's hate list, but to do it correctly, the enchanter has to memory blur. It is very important that all the tanks understand that they taunt mesmerized mobs at least once, and preferably 2 or 3 times before finally whacking a creature over the head. The taunt of the mes is much more than a single melee attack, which means the mobs are going to try and make the enchanter lunch.

Atone, a spell for special occasions, but one of my favourites...

Use #1: Handling bad pulls.

Someone mentioned that atone doesn't work on giants - this is not the case.

I spend a reasonable amount of time killing giants in Rathe with a warrior partner (complete heal works wonders). On occasions, the warrior gets unlucky and comes back with a bad pull, maybe an extra HG or cyclops.

This isn't a problem - I root one, we move well away, and I atone the rooted HG. It's saved our lives on a few occasions.

Use #2: Keeping a mob's aggro in the right place.

Sometimes a rogue joins us on our HG killing spree. His damage output can dramatically shorten a fight, but we don't want him getting hit if possible - if all the hits are on the warrior with 2000 HP or so, I can heal much more efficiently.

Should the rogue get a couple of big backstabs in, he can seriously aggro the giant. Atone fixes this.

Use #3: Powerlevelling.

Knock a cyclops down to a sliver of health, atone it, and let your 20th level warrior friend kill it. A quick half bubble of xp

Use #4: Getting through green aggro dungeons like Upper Guk.

When you attract aggro, root, back off & atone. Saves pulling a train to the Lower Guk zone where you could end up killing someone who's just zoned out on a bubble of health.

Use #5: Dispatching pets.

Green mob pets are a pain to have to deal with. Atoning an orphaned pet makes it 32K itself.

All in all, a lovely spell. It doesn't always work, but you still get the "calmed down" message. It may need one or two recasts. It's usually possible to tell if it's worked by the stance of the mob - a rooted mob which has aggroed on you will stare straight at you. If atone takes, the mob will go back to looking around.
