quote:
we find out a little too late that the bard can't twist invis and levitate. Thats right, she can't twist 2 songs.

Did it occur to you that invis would break once she started twisting levitate?

In this specific case it wasn't her fault, but simple game mechanics you failed to understand. Start a song (even invis again) and invis breaks. It's been like that since day 1. No amount of skill will let a bard sub 51 twist invis with any other song and remain invis. At 51 they get solo's song of travel which is a combined invis+levitate and other stuff. Before that, nothing.

For those that don't know, there's no commands or magical hotkeys that lets a bard twist songs. They must start a song, stop the song, start a new one (invis breaks ALWAYS), stop new one, start next, stop next, etc. Consider it like casting a spell every three seconds. Can you remain invis undead at 47 while continually refreshing everyone's invis undead every 3 seconds while travelling? A bard would probably get fed up with your incompetence too. :P

If you only needed invis AND levitate to get through one section, then it may have been wiser to have the bard stay at the ledge (if no mobs), sing those two songs until the group got to a safe spot, then levitate down and die someplace their corpse is retrievable.

I wouldn't be too hard on the bard. My main is a bard and my alt is a cleric. As stated, no other song should be twisted with invis song; unlike other songs, when that song stops you reappear. There are a couple other things about bard songs you should know.

Mana songs are a tic-based songs that need to be on when the tic occurs. If he's twisting it with another song, you probably aren't getting a fast regeneration of mana because other other songs are playing on the tic.

Some of our fighting songs do not have an icon that you can see. Largo's and Bellow's are examples. He could be twisting two of these and one other and you would only see one icon. If you want to know what the bard is doing, you need to go to chat filter options and make sure bard songs is turned on where you get the bard messages in your chat screen.

When I fight with my bard, I typically play a haste song and a mob snare-type song. With caster mobs, I will go to instruments and buff the group with resists. If connection is good, I twist a 3rd song (depending on the situation). During downtime, I play mana song. I can make myself look good to others by twisting 3 songs that show icons on the buff screen, but they may not be the best songs for that situation. Three songs are easy to twist, but network lag can also make a bard look bad.

Lastly, if you can chat with your bard during a fight, you have a bad bard. We're too busy working the keyboard to talk. I tell my group members at the beginning of the night that I'm not ignoring them, I'm twisting the night away!

You might have had a bad bard, but I thought I'd give you the other side of the story.
