background:

in Skyfire Mts there are a set of named mobs that spawn in two cycles. any one of the mobs can spawn in either cycle. as soon as one monster in cycle #1 dies, another spawns within 30 seconds. as soon as one monster in cycle #2 dies, another spawns within one minute. no non-named mobs are in this cycle, no next named will spawn without killing one of the existing ones. all of them can drop level 50+ spells. the mobs have some variation in levels, and presumably some variation in levels of spells that can drop, but i've never seen it broken down.

question:
is "camping" a cycle here ridiculous? would a GM see a "camp" on a cycle as enforcible or is each mob first-come-first-serve?

some other zones like TT and FM have cycles as well, but those cycles have a wide variation in drops and therefore each mob in the cycle has a wide variation in desireability. i think we can agree that going to TT, letting another group kill all the foragers for example (crappy loot, unless you need the keys, and if you do than those are quickly/easily gotten), and then jumping all the nameds that are on the forager cycle that drop good stuff would be extremely rude and frowned upon. would this apply to SF?

i'm asking because of events in SF last night, and was curious to hear what other people thought.

I don't believe you can say you are camping anything in the game due to the PNP rules.

You might be sitting in a particular spawn point and somebody else can come up and insist you share that spawn point with them under the rules.

As far as camping different mobs that spawn in different points within the zone, just because they happen to be part of a cycle makes it even hard to claim ownership of.

Typically most people will be respectful if people are trying to do a cycle and try not to pull mobs that are part of that cycle unless they are attacked by one of the said mobs.

At least that is my experience.
