Here's my reply-addition on the post by Rayne on the Gamplay Forum...go post folks we need to keep that one alive.~~~~~~

Another situation comes to mind.

1- YOU DIE... and decide to log for the night and get a rez from a guildmate in prime time hours or go do the CR the next day....

2- a Roaming cleric/paladin with his newly achieved status of a Bringer of Life decides to target your corpse and cast the spell while you are offline. Spell goes off, nothing happens. Cleric/paladin wander off.

3- you log back in contact approriate friends and await your rez...
a- Corpse cannot be resurected...
b- Corpse is resurected, but no experience is returned. (flag already set to rezzed)
c- nothing happens.

All of these I have seen occur. Myself being the recipient of a guildmates rez while I was offline or someone telling me "Ohh So and So said he tried to rez you this morning, but you weren;t on"

All results are unacceptible.

The resurections already suffer heavy penalties
-ie the sickness, the 2hour limit and so forth.


Making the resurection GROUP ONLY in no way will fix this problem and will make large dungeon and planar runs a moot point. A cleric can't be in everyone's party in these large events. So this as a solution is negative to everyone in the game.

The Coding to the resurections needs to be reviewed and reprogrammed as a high priority issue. The man hours involved might be tremendous, but Cleric's need this to be a solid addition to the gameplay of others in the game.
No other class is needed as much as a cleric in the higher level areas to keep their party alive and therefore allow the parties a chance to have fun.
Fun is becoming more and more of a chore in Everquest and more and more of my friends are becoming disenfranchised with EQ-Verant's Vision. Even though the game is highly addictive and offers an alternative reality for many to play and have fun. That seems to be sliding to the wayside.
When equipment becomes more meaningful than tactics for success in any environment, people will not go there anymore no matter the rewards. Unless of course to them it is a Business. (EBAYERS)
When failure by any reason leads to drudgery of a CR (corpse recovery) that can last for over a day without a possibility of getting any of your exps back. You become angry and depressed and may walk away from the game entirely.
The "RISK" often if rife with pitfalls and bad programming, due more to lack of FORESIGHT than any "Vision" (tm).
THe "ADJUSTMENT" of a zone to make it more challenging to the newly born higher levels once again is detrimental to all the others in the game who haven't achieved that status yet and were looking forward to the dungeons "adjusted".
Verant needs to re-asses it's vision both as a business and as a entertainment provider.

I have a solution for the TIME-LIMIT for clerics that may or may not be desired or liked by the general populace but would make the 10-14 hour corpse recoveries a bit more palatable for players.

After the 2 hour timelimit, allow a resurection for exps with a item/component for each hour thereafter.
If said corpse is 3 hours active past the 2 hour point, then perhaps 3 gems could be used to refresh said corpse. IE reset the corpse timer and resurection flag.
This is possible as when a character dies a save is immediately done as the character returns to their bind points and the "timer" is started.
Suggestions would depend on the levels of said characters and time deceased.
1- A Topaz per hour past the 2 hour limit would be required as a reagent for the resurection.
or
2- A topaz per hour past the 2 hour limit for the SPELL REFRESH CORPSE (Lvl 39+) MANA same as a REZ (39) This not only would be cast targeted on the corpse itself, it would reset the "Death Clock" to zero and reset the CORPSE TIMER to Zero too. This would allow a corpse with gear in danger of decaying to have it's 24 hours online or week offline. IF using 50 topazes is a bit much, perhaps make the gem a peridot(one most clerics that level already carry) a Pearl, or a Ruby.
This is one suggestion... there are many others...
