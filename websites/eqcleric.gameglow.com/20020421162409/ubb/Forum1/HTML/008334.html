I can testify from both sides, I have a 54 (sooooo close to 55) cleric and a 49 monk. Monks make surprisingly good tanks, one of my favorite tanks for exp groups is a 57 monk in my guild. Typically until the very high end, monks have very comparable AC, but less hps. The block skill, though, is incredible, and reduces damage taken noticably. So for most exp groups, I don't really care one way or the other. But the warrior does tend to get his feelings hurt unless he's got aggro the whole time

The thing that DOES irritate me though, in a good exp group, is having the main tank be the same person as the puller, regardless of class. Two reasons: 1) If the group is functioning well, the puller should be able to leave the fight with about 25% of the mob's health left, to bring a new pull for maximum exp flow. If it is the same person, he has to wait until the fight is over, so more downtime. 2) The big one for me is that I don't like to send a puller out into a dangerous situation with less than 80% hps. If they are taking damage during the fight, all too often, their hps are high enough that CH seems like overkill (and 10secs of waiting), and remedy or Cel Heal isn't enough. If someone else had been taking the damage, I could have waited until I could have cast a more efficient CH. So from a mana management perspective, I prefer the puller and the MT to be different people, regardless of classes.

A smart monk tanks quite well. One thing I haven't seen mentioned here is how to CH a monk.

A monk has 2 tools at their disposal that make them eminently CHable. Stonestance and Mend.

The main thing that is lacking in monks as tanks is taunt.

Good thread (Blame a Cleric who also plays a Monk for letting Monks know of this thread).

I was prompted to respond because I saw a Post by Prieter regarding the Monk Mend ability and Stonestance discipline.

About Mend:
-----------
a) It has a 6 minutes recycle time (ie it will ungrey for a Monk 6 minutes after it was last depressed).

b) It allows a Monk to heal 25% of his current total HP (which equates to about 1.25 red bubbles on his HP bar).

Now, some Monks only use it as a last resort in Group fights to avoid death or give them more time to get off a successful Feign Death. Other Monks (like me) like to use it if it's available AS SOON as we're down 1.25 red bubbles (IMO, it saves you Clerics manna, and allows us to make max use of it with it's 6 minute recycle time). Also, some monks (me included) have a Group macro that sends the Group a message that we've used Mend (mine reads 'I've used Mend on myself').

I also have a Group macro that I press when my 'MEND' hotkey ungreys that reads:

'My Mend ability is back!'

I bring this up because I've had some Clerics get mad at me because when they see the 'used Mend' message, some Clerics thought I was demanding a heal from them. I have found that once I've explained why I have it, they like it because it helps them in deciding how best to manage heals if a situation gets nasty. My advice to Clerics is that when you group with a Monk, ask him if he likes to Mend early (ie as soon as he is 1.25 red bubbles down), or late (ie only as a last rtesort, etc.). That you don't waste a heal as the Monk hits Mend; or allow the Monk to die because as you wonder why he's not using Mend.

Stonestance:
------------
A) It has a base 12 minute recycle time at 51 (the Level where Monks get this), but that time decreases 1 minute for every level above 51.

B) It doesn't make us immune to damage, it just decreases the damage we take for 12 seconds (on average, a hit for 120 HPs will become a hit for 14 HPs, etc.)

C) EQ displays the following message in /say when we hit the stonestace button. For me, it would read:

'Chilau becomes one with the Earth'

Also, a Monk may use 'Stonestance' when actually split pulling a MOB from a group of MOBs, so if your Monk is doing a lot of split pulling, it's doubtful he has this discipline available during the Group fight.

BTW - I think most Monks will agree with you guys in that a Monk who doesn't learn to control his aggro deserves the scorn of Clerics, Groupmates and Guildmates alike.
