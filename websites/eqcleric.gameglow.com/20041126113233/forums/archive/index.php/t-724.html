I find alot of people play EQ the "wrong" way. Let me explain first before you all start going nuts. How many of you log on, try and find a group and then just kill things, weather it be for experience or items/money. Now obviously if you ever want your character to get better and stronger thats what you have to do, BUT how many of you have ever helped a newbie get a level or two? Ran around Norrath to find as many people as you could heal (no charge)? How many teen level clerics have sat around in Kithicore all night to see if they could survive? And not died http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif Or followed a raid into Nagafen or Voxs Lair? How many of you have bought a rediculous amount of booze and had a drinking party with anyone who would drink with you, just to laugh at how messed up your speech gets?
Do you all get what I'm trying to say? Too many people I know are SO interestead in leveling or ammassing items that it really is pointless in the end because it is a game and if you're not having fun why are you playing it? I fell into that trap for a while and stopped playing for a month. I have been playing for months now and I am level 19, my friend who started 3 weeks before me is now level 31. I am more interested in having fun and meeting people than "gaining things", whatever they may be.
It kind of reminds me of a Dragon Mirth comic I read once. There was this tall box with a slit in the side of it and two warriors standing to either side and an obviously inexperienced warrior standing in front of the box staring stupidly at it. There were all these warning signs that said things like "beware of live minotaur" and "do not feed the Minotaur" but there was one more sign above the slit in the box that said "Insert sword here for 75 exp."
And thats what it basically boils down to in the game, you go out and kill things to gain levels. Kind of oxy moronic for a cleric don't ya think? The best example I can give is the Paladin's Ghoulbane sword, it's an awsome weapon and I'll be the first to admit it, but do you know what they have to do to get it? They have to kill a Froglok Paladin! Not a Shadow Knight or a Necromancer or anything else. They have to kill a fellow Paladin in order to take his weapon. And not only is he a Paladin but he is a Froglok, a race that is supposedly "evil". This Froglok has proven his worth and his "goodness" and then they kill him, no sorry they murder him, for his weapon. And yet the questing for items continues.
Maybe they could have an experience system for doing what your class was meant to do? That would be fun, going around and healing people and gaining experience for it because thats what we are supposed to be doing.
Personally I think ALL classes should be able to solo until a very high level. But in order to balance it out make it so that grouping yields greater benefits. Why do certain classes have the ability to solo and not all, if verrant wants balance then make in balanced. It makes it very difficult to play when you only want to play for an hour and spend most of that time finding a group. But in the end we have to spend months of online time to gain items and money which takes even more time and it all boils down to more money for verrant.
