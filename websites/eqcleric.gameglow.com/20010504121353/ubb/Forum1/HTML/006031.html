Go to Lower Guk and camp the Ghoul Lord and Froglok King. Go to Sol B and do the Stone Spider room. Experience is the only thing that will make you better - gradually work your way up to harder areas.

Once you've played out guk and sol b, skip KC entirely and head to Chardok. There, you'll get some good exposure to high-intensity situations with relatively little risk if you camp the zone out. It's a good place to practice stunning gaters, an important skill for the top dungeons. Once you feel confident you can handle that, it's time to try some of the camps in Sebilis, or some of the easier parts of the Hole.

A note about KC: I don't know what it is about this dungeon exactly - maybe it's the moat/zone camps - but people who learn the ropes in KC invariably come out scarred and dysfunctional. KC is in many many ways the exception rather than the rule. It almost seems designed to get people into bad habits - when we see people not assisting, breaking mezzes, etc. we call it KC syndrome. Also the fact that it's the only in-zone pickup dungeon for 50+ means that you'll encounter way too many people who have little idea how to play in groups.

Ok onto the list point by point:

1) Buffs: Hit everyone with Reso. When you factor in cast time = not medding time, Donal's arms don't save you anything at all. That line is so wildly efficient you're only hurting yourself by not using the best all the time. Symbols on self, enchanter, and tanks - if you can save yourself money and have just one person do most of the tanking that's great. I find though if you are with 2 tanks they try and prove themselves and you really have to maintain both. AC buffs - well it's my humble opinion that you can leave these out entirely unless you have nothing else to do with your mana, but others might differ. Might be worth it on a single tank. Use the Armor of Protection spell from velious, it's also great efficiency and lasts forever.

2) Heals: You need a fast heal, a solid direct heal, and complete. Fast heals are almost exclusively for enchanters, or if you've fallen asleep and wake up with someone at a quarter bubble. I'd use GH as the fast heal if I were you... but get just one more level and Remedy does both these jobs for you for a while. Celestial and superior are redundant, they both perform the middle-heal function. I'd take the one you use more and make it your middle heal; celestial I think will be better. Complete is what in my opinion separates good clerics from great ones. Great clerics get the most out of complete heal. A lot of that is learning when you have the freedom to cast it without anyone else dying. You'll have to learn this by experience, the only advice I can really give on this is make sure your enchanter can survive while you are casting it.

3) Defense - using divines is a good sign. As you move into harder areas I recommend replacing root with atone in your regular lineup - summonners make root parking quite frustrating. Some will disagree here, it's a matter of style. I find enchanters prefer when I atone and it saves me mana healing them - and since I depend on enchanters for viability I cater to their needs first and foremost.

4) Offense: Get used to a 2 stun lineup, you'll need them to stop gaters. Level 5 Stun doesn't break mezzes and is less resisted than any other stun, something that will prove immensely valuable to you - it should be your first stun. I load up HM as the second stun because of low recycle time - until you get Reckoning at 54 you might want to play with Sound of Force since it is efficient as both a stun and a DD relative to other tools at your disposal. 54+ you can use Reckoning to blow off extra mana and speed up kills. For the most part though, clerics are defense and our role is being an enabler for others who deal the damage. Think support class, that's what we are.

5)Judgement: Communicate. Do your best to keep people alive. If you do something once and it doesn't work try something else. Nothing but experience and an open mind will help you on this one.

6)Groups: See five. Also good thing to do in groups is inform people of adds if you see them first. Make a plan for how you will kill mobs and practice on the first few that come your way. Every group will play a bit different - don't be afraid to ask people to adjust what they are doing to make things go more smoothly.

7) XP dungeons at 50 from easiest to hardest: Guk, Sol B, Kedge Keep, Hole, Chardok, Sebilis. Notice the absence of KC from this list. For your own mental health keep it that way. Get used to spending the first 30-60 min of every session arranging a good group and getting to your dungeon. It's the price you pay for avoiding the horrors of KC, and well worth it.

I think everyone has pretty much summed up my thoughts as well. You have the right ideas, now just learn from your mistakes and grow from them. You didn't list all of the tactics that you use while fighting, so I'm going to toss out some things that I do. If you already use them, then feel free to ignore, and if you don't use them, then take the advise as you'd like:

* Stuns. I know someone else mentioned this one, but I wanted to reiterate how useful they are. If you are fighting a caster, learn to stun just as they are casting, and learn the timing of their spells. You can save a lot of mana and lots of problems if you get this skill down.

* Hotkeys. Set up hotkeys for your heals, and add a message letting your group know what heal is coming, and for who. For example, something like:

/sit off
/cast 4
/g One complete heal coming up for %T!

This is just a way to improve communication with your group.

* Heals. You will always find yourself picking the wrong heal. At least I do As soon as I cast a heal, I often start thinking, "Why didn't I cast x instead?" Or "Why didn't I cast it on x instead?" With heals, live (or die, heh) and learn, it's the only way you'll find what works best for you. My tactics are similar to yours, using cel heal when feasible, complete heal on tanks when I will have time to get it off safely, and superior heal when the time factors on the other two are too long.

* But you will make mistakes, no matter how much experience you have. The other important factor is how well you recover from those mistakes. Don't let them bother you, or throw you in to a panic. A group with a calm cleric is a group that has a MUCH better chance of survival Keep your cool, and let your group see you as a confident cleric, even if you feel as though you are still learning. If someone in your group dies, don't worry about what you might have done wrong to have caused their death. Rather, immediately focus on what you need to do to keep everyone else alive.

Remember, there are up to five other people in your group that are making just as many mistakes as you are! Each person has a responsibility, and the success of the group depends on them just as much as it depends on you.

* You know how to play very well with that one person ... take the knowledge you've gained there, and apply it to the people you meet. I think after the first or maybe second fight of the group, you should be able to tell the style of each member in the group, and learn to adapt your technique appropriately. What works in one situation does not work in all, and what works in one group does not work in all.

* Don't be afraid to try something new in order to succeed. I was fighting with a group of people at the giant fort in Great Divide, I think it was all tanks, and two or three of the people were a good 8 levels below me, heh. We had a nasty pull with four giants. As soon as I saw them coming, I knew root-parking wasn't going to cut it, because it wouldn't be reliable or fast enough. So I quick flipped through my spellbook and pulled out flash of light So long as a mob isn't engaged, it causes the mob to run as if hit with fear. I kept three giants occupied using just flash of light, while they took down the fourth. Let them grab the next one, and repeat. Cost me almost no mana, and with celestial healing, everyone was okay while I was busy "flashing." In that situation, we would've been dead had I not tried something, so why not experiment and see if you can't find something that works!

Just some suggestions. Best of luck to you

Others have done a great job, explaining things, I'll just reinforce or stress a thing or two.

1)Buffs sounds good, You should always give everyone your best hp buff, in your case reso. At 52 when you get group hero, this gets easy. AC buffs, they are nice but the casting cost is so high they are optional, and the upgrades tend to be just a little better for a lot more mana, so you may want to look into only AC buffing the main tank and maybe yourself, maybe going without, or maybe even using a less expensive AC buff if mana is tight.

Symbols, everyone doesnt do it the exact same way, I will usually give myself, every tank expected to tank damage, and the chanter my best symbol. I give everyone else a "lesser" symbol (pinzarn), its cheap and low mana. For symbols, *MAKE SURE* you are getting reimbursed for them! If your in a dungeon that doesnt need many symbols, maybe scale back for dungeons like sebilis drop a ton of cash, so in seb groups I feel free to use lots of symbols, because I know the group will hand over the money at the end when I tell them how much I spent on symbols ; ) Nothing will make a cleric poor faster than letting a group get away with freebie peridot-cost symbols all night.

2) I usually keep 4 heals memmed now at 56, but at your level I used 3. Basically, you need something small and quick, you need a large heal thats less than CH, and you need CH.

CH is going to take you time, the best clerics are seperated by their ability to use CH well without getting people killed. CH is very situational. Are you in a group where 3-4 pulls are common? Is your messer seeming to have difficulty getting it all under control quickly? You use CH only when things are more or less under control and low-stress.

Also, cel heal rocks in my opinion, its a wonderful heal, but dont rely too much on this wonderful spell! Just like CH, sometimes you may get people killed by unthinkingly relying on it too much. If you have a caster or rogue or something down to 60%, not getting hit amnymore, tank has it taunted, good I'd use celheal. If your necro is getting assaulted, and you celheal him when hes down to 30%, your going to feel really stupid in about 8 seconds.

When the enchanter is getting hit deep in a dungeon, as long as you got someone else who can halfway tank, no one else matters. The enchanter's life is about as important as yours, and this is where you sometimes make tough choices. If your enchanter is 50%, and going down fast needing a heal right now, and your rogue is down to 5%, your just gonna have to let the rogue go. (depending on situation of course, lets just assume something like a 3-frog mess in seb)If he is worth a darn, he'll understand why you couldnt heal him after you explain later. This is rare though, usually I can find some way of saving everyone, but for the worst situations, sometimes you have 2 seconds to realize that no, someone is gonna die, so who do you save for the group's best chance. This is advanced stuff though, you wont have to deal with for a while.

3) Wonderful, a good sign, I've already preached the virtues of having both invulns up.

4) sounds good, though I rarely mem a nuke now, I go with the 2 cheapo stuns. stuns = very little mana = a few seconds worth of the critter not hurting anyone = little less need to heal. Just watch your own aggro, chain-stunning ticks them off. For stunning you arent concerned with damage, you just want that stupid critter stunned, especially casters, and these will go through unresisted most often, plus the level 5 stun wont break mess when your stunning something while the chanter is trying to mes.

5) You just need time, you seem to be kinda like a young pitcher in baseball. ; ) Some days your just going to do everything wrong and lose by a hundred runs, you just gotta learn what you did wrong, and then forget about it.

7) In order of progression, when you have "mastered" or "outclassed", one go to the next: Lower Guk -> Solb -> hmmm, kedge or the hole, tower of Frozen Shadow might also be nice -> Chardok. I love this zone, because it can be so deceptively easy at times. One moment you think you know everything, and then this dungeon just comes out and humbles you. : ) If it seems easy after what youve done so far, dont take it for granted or fall asleep, this place can kill you very quickly. In chardok you will either learn the joys of stunning, or youll come out bruised and broken. -> Finally, Sebilis. I'd recommend waiting till 52 or so and knowing your class very well.

First off, let me say this - you've done yourself an injustice by sticking to outdoor zones. Dungeons are lots of fun and build valuable skills. But it's easy to let happen and you can recover, and in asking these kinds of questions are off to a good start.

1. Buffs - two things I see. First is that, IMHO, Shield of words is a big fat waste of mana. Armor of faith uses half the mana for 6 less ac if I remember the discussion correctly. The only time I break out SoW is if I want the extra duration (I'm buffing a newbie and sending them off) Second, you didn't mention the ENCHANTER. High end groups have two key components - the enchanter and the cleric. Either one dies, and you're in deep trouble. You should keep full buffs on the enchanter as you would on the main tank. (As a side note, the reso/valor thing becomes moot when you get heroic bond, a great spell.)

2. Heals - remedy is an awesome spell you'll have soon. I like the fast cast and long range particularly. I will keep from 2 to 4 heals mem'd depending on things. Celestial is always one

4. Sounds good

5. Complete heal is great in very tightly controlled situations. In a dungeon it's not uncommon to have to split your attention between the enchanter, yourself, the main tank, maybe someone else. Complete heal gets tricky in those circumstances. Being pro-active with celestial can make this easier, and use of things like remedy and divine light when you get it are also good. I find myself using complete heal a LOT less than I used to, and only when the situation is well in hand, and I need high mana efficiency.

6. Mostly, you'll know it when you see it. But there are signs like good agro management, communication, etc. that will be indicators.

7. Karnors. Personal opinion time - Karnors is a dump. I don't care for it at all. Sebilis is a great place once you get your key but it will really push your skills to the limit. Similar with Howling Stones I hear. Those places might be a little rough to work your skills, so I'd suggest some old world dungeons like Sol A or lower guk.

Buffs - The calling card of a great cleric is mana conservation. Cleric downtime means party downtime, so you must learn to do all your jobs (stunner, healer, buffer) and still be ready to continue with the next pull. As suggested earlier, everyone needs Reso, but you can use Valor as a stop gap until you have the spare mana to replace it with Reso. Try to spread these buffs out and tell your party members to notify you when a buff fades. Any buff you would cast on the puller should probably also be cast on the enchanter. Dump the AC spells unless you have a ton of spare mana, I rarely use anything other than AoF (and that only because it is an armor effect).

Heals - Using CH takes experience, even the best make timing mistakes with it. Use it when you feel comfortable that mis-timing will not cause a wipe out. Make sure to take Jubal's comments to heart about types of heals.

Defense - I consider root and stun (L5 version) to be our most powerful spells. I like it when that extra mob is beating on me, its means I can root park him and it gives the enchanter plenty of time to mez it. Needless to say, AC is important to a cleric.

Offense - Level 5 stun is your friend, use it liberally. If something is beating on your enchanter, stun the mob and then start healing the enchanter. Heal the enchanter even if his life bar is not moving (that enchanter is taking damage but has rune up).

Judgement - Determine a priority list as the first thing you do in a group. Enchanter is #1, you are #2, a big tank #3, etc.. In some cases someone is going to die, your job is to make sure the right people live so that the party survives.

Groups - Remember the people that are good players, add them to your friend list. Build a collection of people you can count on to make a good group, avoid those that cause problems in the past.

Dungeons - I agree with most people, Karnor's is a place where other groups mistakes can get you killed.

Only thing I can add is an extension of what Segal said -

Learn what classes need what heals and when.

In 'high risk' encounters I will celestial the enchanter on incoming. First chance I get I will celestial the primary tank so I can attend to others, then come back with Cheal. Dump DD heals on the enchanter(s) liberally.

In 'xp-grind' encounters I choose heals based on tank. Plates will get Cheal unless they are outgunned, then a cel/complete combo. Celestial is usually sufficient for non-plate melees. Enchanters get remedy, then celestial if they need to be topped off while keeping mults locked down.

Skip celestial on mobs that dispell, unless cast before engagement.

Remedy has nice range for mobs that AE.
