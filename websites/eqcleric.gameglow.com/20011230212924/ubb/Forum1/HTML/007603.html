Personally I got tired of surname's like Lifebringer, Rezmaster and Lilheal a long time ago. I roleplayed my cleric as the son of a Stormguard warrior, skilled in combat and more interested in smiting than healing. That considerred, and adding in his devout faith in his god, I decided on Bolgor Brellwrath.

I think it adds to the rp value of the character, and to add to the storyline, I started an alt dwarven warrior to be his brother, and a paladin to be his nephew. The warrior's name is Gordor Bladewrath, and the paladin still hasn't hit 20 yet. I tried to maintain a family sense, while at the same time giving the healer and melee side of my characters' clan something to define them uniquely. Later I'll decide whether or not the nephew will follow his uncle or his father in choosing a last name, or strike out is own.

My advice is to take a few minutes and define a backround for your character and try and pull a last name from that in a roleplaying sense. Its always added to my gaming experience. But I guess I'm just an old D&D geek.

No surname is the way to go. I got rid of mine at level 50 because I was tired of it. Now I'm just plain Lifeheart. I'm unguilded at the moment, so it is pretty funny to just have 1 word over my head.

Having no surname is nice because you can put on leather gear, or just bag all your visible gear when going into a newbie zone and fit right in. It definately cuts down on the begs when people see a guy in full leather with no surname walking around.

Also, since no two people can have the same first name, no surnames are really needed to distinguish between people.

But if you really want a surname, I'd go with something that sounds Wizardly since you said you were a Wizzy at heart. What is your Wizard's last name? Would that work for your Cleric too?

I get a little tired of the class related last names, like Lifegiver and so on. No offense, but they're kind of commonplace. I like last names that make you think a bit or might strike a resonant chord with just a few people.

For instance, I have a druid whose first name is Lysteria. I thought it was pretty sounding, but it is actually a rather nasty bacteria, spelled slightly different.

My clerics last name is a particle name: Fermion. Sounded good with Electra (i.e. electron).

And my rogues last name sort of loosely translates into poison flower in french, kind of. Malfleur. Ok, that one is a little class specific.


In your case, I thought of Fist as a good last name. Why? Well, when I saw Harammer I thought of hammer. And then I thought of Arm & Hammer. I thought, shame he didn't name himself Armond as a first name and Hammer as a last name. But then thinking of the arm, I thought of a fist and think that sounds good. Nice and strong and short!

Either that or Nail.
