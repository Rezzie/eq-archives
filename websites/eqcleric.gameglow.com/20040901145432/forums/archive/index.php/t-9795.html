Hail!

I had a most interesting conversation this morning.

While camping Regis for my second bracer (last item I need for a full set), I see an OOC from another cleric asking if anyone has seen Zahal.

I gave her a tell stating I hadn't seen him, and had been checking for awhile.

She then asks me if I was camping for the bracer, which I respond to as "Yes"

"What other quest items do you need? I can tell you how to get any of them" She says.

I respond by telling her I just need the last bracer.

"You already have one bracer? You should just use a RMB as your other one. It is a better bracer"

Again, I respond that I want a complete set, plus I want the extra AC over the extra strength.

"Why? Str is better than AC. Everyone knows that."

&lt;Not everyone agrees to this&gt;

I respond to her comments like this: "Look, with all due respect, I'm a level 44 cleric, you are level 35. I think I know what is best for my cleric in the style that I play him"

"Well, I've been playing since the BETA, so I know what is best for you"

For the record, I didn't start playing in the BETA phase, but I did start the day it was released. Plus, this cleric isn't my only char. Also, there are many different ways to play a cleric.

Again, very nicely, I told her that I felt she was being rude in assuming she knew what was best for me.

I guess my rant is this: Offer people suggestions if they ask for them, but telling people what is best for them is often a matter of opinion.
