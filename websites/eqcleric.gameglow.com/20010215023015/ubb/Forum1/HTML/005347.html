Patch message

1) Superior Healing moved to level 51 for shamans and druids. casting difficulty reduced
2) New level 55 Druid and shaman spell, Chloroblast, similar to remedy for clerics.
3) New Druid spell at level 60, upgrade to superior healing, Natures Touch.
4) Paladins:
a) Add Endure Magic spell at level 30
b) Add Resist Magic at 55
c) Move Guard spell from 49 to 39
d) Move Armor of Faith from 53 to 49
e) New level 53 paladin spell, Divine Glory, clone of divine strength but 100 hp instead of 200
f) New level 59 spell, Celestial Cleansing 110 hp healdot per tick, for 4 ticks. 1.5 second casting time. Recast time 30 secs.
g) Defense skill cap made exactly same as warriors
5) Shadowknights:
a) Remove artificially inflated mana cost for pre level 50 lifetap spells
b) Move Spirit Tap to level 55
c) Move Drain Spirit to level 57
d) Add Drain Soul at level 60
e) Grim Aura added at level 22
f) Move Shieldskin to level 34
g) Mini Intensify Death pet buff added at 29, 10% (min) haste 5 str 5 ac
h) Add Diamondskin spell at 59
i) Defense skill cap made exactly same as warriors
6) Rangers:
a) Add Endure Cold at 22
b) Add Skin Like Nature at 59
c) Add FireFist at 22
d) Move Bramblecoat from 49 to 39
e) Add Spikecoat at 49
f) Add resist cold at 55
g) Add Panic Animal at 22
i) Offense and weapons skill made identical to warriors
7) Bards:
a) Cassindra`s Chant of Clarity for Bards at level 20
b) Bards defensive skill made exactly the same as a warriors

8) Magicians
a) New level 59 spell, Valiant Companion, makes pet immune to fear (own pet only)
b) New level 39 spell, Summon Companion, if pet not noticed by any hostile npcs, pet returns to you instantly.
c) Rain spells no longer count pets against total number of entities they can damage (all rain spells)
d) Expedience for Mages at level 29. Target casters pet, J Boot speed buff for pet only

9) Warriors
a) Aggressive and Defensive disciplines fixed before they would only affect damage taken and not affect damage dealt out. Now they affect both as designed.

10) New Scrolls are temporarily avaiable in East Commons

Chloroblast is a 3 second casting time 175 mana heal, that heals 408 to 428

Natures Touchs is 5.5 second casting time 400 mana heal that heals 978

20th level Bardsong is same mana regen as Breeze.

These changes will clearly create an INBALANCE between the Druid and the Cleric IF there are no improvements to the Cleric class. Obviously Druids needed an improvement to healing since Verant was determined to increase the number of viable healers besides Shaman and make Druids more group desirable. HOWEVER, IF the Druid class will ALSO get a Remedy equivalent (a spell I currently use very often at 52) a mere 4 levels later than a Cleric and then will get a Divine Light equivalent at 60, IMHO, Verant would have gone too far in 'balancing.'

Its not that a Druid will ever be AS GOOD a healer as a Cleric even with these changes. Its that a Druid will be GOOD ENOUGH of a healer that combined with the Druid's many other strenths, the class will be highly desired over a Cleric both in groups and as a class played by new players.

Of course, IF clerics get some fun spells or changes which make them more appealing to play and to groups, then BALANCE will be restored between Druids (with their new healing spells) and Clerics (with their new fun stuff). But what of BALANCE with the other classes?

I would prefer to have a win-win situation for both Druids and Clerics, but if that is not possible, then the proposed changes in Testing should not work against BALANCE. Its a pity that Verant's design when it came to classes were so far off the mark initially (their definitions of the classes were way too fuzzy) and that there knee-jerk reaction to 'fix' a class is to smash a flea with a sledge hammer.

Even if these changes go in, I'll continue to happily heal and res as always, but will wonder if I should've been a Druid instead (Brell forgive me).

Interesting discussion, but I still believe Verant went too far in their changes if they don't make postive changes to Clerics as well. Its a huge improvement to the Druid class no matter how you spin it, and its not like the Druid class was so bad before these changes.

I do agree that in a group I more often use CH than remedy (the nature of the game now, especially high end and Velious, is heavy melee for good or bad), but I do use remedy a lot when I'm not in a group (soloing or going around healin folks) because that's what a Cleric does (a support class). It seems to me that CH would be the only real distinction between a Druid and a Cleric IF these changes go in (res'n is post combat so should not really be considered), and that is simply not enough to justify a classes existance.

Give the Druids this dramatic healing boost (not slight as some suggest), but IF you do, give the Cleric class a useful Mark of Karn and Unswerving Hammer of Faith (this is what I meant by fun spells). In short, create a distinction once again between Clerics PRIMARY ability and a Druids SECONDARY (tertiary?) ability besides one spell, CH.

First of all, druids will not make clerics obsolete without CH. Add in Symbol, Celestial Elixer, and the ability to actually take a hit or two, and continue casting heals, and clerics are still the best healers.
From another point of view, who ever told clerics they'd be the only ones capable of being a healer in a group? Paladins, Shadowknights, and Warriors can all tank. There're a glut of damage-dealing classes. It's perfectly reasonable for Verant to make Druids an improved healing class... they're not a cleric, but they'll do in a pinch. God knows I wish they'd make Bards or someone else more capable of crowd control so I could form a functional Sebilis group when no enchanters are around.
