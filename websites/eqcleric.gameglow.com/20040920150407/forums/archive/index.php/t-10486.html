I went to Unrest at level 16, and have been having a great time in there -- only died once. I'm now level 17 and still hunting in Unrest whenever I get to play. With a full group, level 16-20, you can get great experience by going around the house and sitting in the back room (the one with the pillars at the entrance). This room spawns some ghouls and dark bone skeletons every 20 minutes. Once you clear the room, you can pull quite easily from the first floor, and everybody in the group should get good experience here. I went from 16 to 17 in one session (about 5 hours), plus had some bronze and FS weapons to sell. I plan on staying here for quite a few levels.

I am have been in Unrest since about level 18 and found that the most important issue for experience is the type of group you are in. Normally I play with my friends and guildmates, which means I know exactly the abilities and actions each of them possess. The biggest problem in Unrest is how people deal with being trained. If you have a cool-headed group, you can profit from trains(my friends and I have now taken to derailing those troublesome trains). My best advice is to carefully select a group, perhaps even one a few levels higher than you. Then, as long as you have some good (and knowledgable) pullers, it wont matter where you are, you will get the experience. Good luck!

I think Unrest is great for Clerics, lots of Undead, Great XP, Bronze and FS from the CGs and Drys. However I must say that until you get used to the zone and lean how to avoid trains, Unrest can be a deathtrap for unsuspecting players.

Having made 2 levels in Unrest I now always pull for the group that I'm in, Crazy you say, well not so.


You can Invis to Undead and scope out the best Mobs.

You have Endure Magic, so your less likely to get ghoul rooted
PS: I suggest you pick up a cheap Shiny Brass Shield to help with this too.

You have your Divine Armour to Save you when things really get screwed up and you need to run your train to the zone.


I recommend, if no high level players are in the zone, that you stay at the fountains, that way, although it’s harder to pull the good stuff, you have a much easier run to the zone if things go bad.
