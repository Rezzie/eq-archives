When having messages that say something. You need to think, 'what is the purpose of this message'

If you say you are CH'ing a certain person, is there a person who can make use of that information and act accordingly? If there is not then why say it?

If I say i am going to CH someone else and someone else says they will CH the same person at exactly the same time - what may happen? You have a chance that both will cancel, and the entire aim of keeping the person alive is lost.

The best form of communication is silent. If theres a certain situation, knowing what someone else will do without prompting is what you are aiming at.

I often announce im group CH'ing in group, the reason is very simply. I want people who are not tanking to look at their position relative to me and make sure they get hit by the heal.

Communication has to serve a purpose otherwise you are just filling up chat which might make a useful message get missed.

So always think - do other people need to know this to change what they are going to do. If not then you don't really have to say it.

I'm the Priest Class officer for my guild. We're a mid-sized guild (about 50 members or so), mostly 55+.

We have a permanent chat channel for all of the priest classes, and almost everyone autojoins it, so whether we're raiding, playing alts, or xp grinding, all of the clerics, druids, and shamen in my guild are in the channel at all times when logged on.

For the most part, it's easygoing. When we're raiding, I make it a rule that anyone who is on and is not at the raid leave the chat channel. All heals must be called in the priest channel and not in the raid channel (to avoid extra raid channel spam). Whether the priests want to call their heals in group is their choice entirely. No "cute" heal messages are allowed in the channel during raids, although I get a kick out of some of the ones they use when we're not raiding. Everything needs to be plain and simple (Casting Divine Light on Tank_01).

The reason that I do this is because I like to keep watch on everything that is going on. There isn't a cleric in my guild that I don't trust to make the correct decision when it comes to healing. But you learn a lot from watching who's getting healed when. You see who's getting aggro that shouldn't, you see when a cleric is in trouble because a mob flurries or enrages or AE's and they're trying to keep 5 people alive all of a sudden instead of just the MT. It stops 12 people from landing CH at once. If more than one of us casts a CH, at the same time, one of us will say "cancel" in priest chat, and once we have done so, the first person to say "cancel" ducks and interrupts. We've eliminated a lot of double CH's that way. If we see a cleric spam healing a person, all healers who aren't busy know to assist that cleric to make sure everything is under control. The druids and shamen will let us know that we can save mana on Caster_X because they can heal them or top them off. We invite our necros into the channel if they want to join. We call all post-fight mana checks in the priest channel, and I report to raid leader when all priests are ready instead of having the raid channel spammed with all of the clerics saying "fom". Overall, it's improved our communication tenfold, and I've seen major development in our lower level clerics as far as mana management and heal timing, and we rarely ever have a death on a raid (unless we wipe, but at least when we do something, we do it all the way =D ) It's made us a tight-knit group, and we've gotten a lot accomplished, and have a lot of fun hanging out in the channel together.

As far as xp groups, I always call heals in group, always have, always will. I usually group with friends or guildies, and they all trust me to keep them alive, but I think people generally feel safer when they know a heal is coming. And if you heal efficiently, you won't be spamming your group with heal messages. =D

Groups: around level 45 I stopped calling anything but Complete Heal - why? because it lets my backup healer know that they need to handle emergencies for the next 10 seconds.

My rule-of-thumb for hot-key messages (especially on a raid) is "Will it make someone else act differently as a result?".

Raids: Depends on who I'm raiding with, we'll either only call CH, or CH and DL, up to calling everything in the raid channel. Primary and secondary clerics sometimes call everything, while backup clerics only call CH's. Again, this is decided on a raid-by-raid basis, although the usual is to only call CH and DL (major mana investment spells). The other cleric that I normally work with is in constant communication with me via tells (in fact, we'll often swap duties mid-fight because one of us is getting lower on mana). The key there is "communication".

However things are setup on a raid situation, I have 2 banks of identical keys - one is setup in the "preferred" mode for that event (call on CH, call DL/CH, call it all, etc.). But I also have a 2nd bank that will switch me into call everything mode when I need to. (e.g. when the MA cleric goes down, and I need to take over.)

I see no point in a raid situation of calling heals in both groupchat and raidchat - it just makes that much more spam for people to sift through for the same amount of information. Also, I keep my heal calls extremely simple to cut down on the mental work to decipher them "COMPLETE HEAL [ %T ] 10 SECONDS!", or "DIVINE LIGHT [ %T ]" or "REMEDY [ %T ]". Short, sweet, and different enough that the other clerics can immediately tell my heals and which heal I'm casting.

Hmm, as to the case where two clerics cast CH at the same time? We discuss that ahead of time and each cleric has a area of responsibility (meaning that if you see the primary cleric for that person also casting CH, you duck and immediately call a remedy or DL). CH is one of those spells that you anticipate anyway - so it should be as simple for 2 clerics to constantly say, "40m take next CH", "okay". I have been on very few raids where one of the clerics has a "hero complex", and that quickly ends once the raid leader sees that the backup clerics are at full mana, while the "hero" is constantly low mana. The clerics that I currently work with are completely away from "this is my person, don't you dare put a heal on them", to "use your mana as you see fit, druids and shaman included, and we'll kill more mobs faster".

Things may change once we get into the upper-end zones, right now we're doing 2-6 group raids, where the MA takes most damage, but maybe we have 3-4 mobs in camp at the same time (some mezzed, some tank-mezzed). We'll have a MA cleric, SA cleric, and the other clerics are responsible for patch healing, or guarding an enchanter or slower. (I spent too much time full-mana on a few raids as the lowbie cleric, so I volunteered to keep the enchanter alive - MA cleric told me to go right ahead and they'd only step in if it looked like I needed help - now it's pretty much standard practice for us.)

Also, I added a "CORISH IS DIVINED! NO HEALS FOR 18 SECONDS!" hot-key sometime in the past few months (probably due to PoH 2nd floor). I hardly ever use it (sometimes I DA without telling a group), it's more for those cases where I am in SERIOUS trouble and someone's gonna die (and not always me!).
