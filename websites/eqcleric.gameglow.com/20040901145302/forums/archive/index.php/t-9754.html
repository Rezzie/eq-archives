If it helps, my first try at a cleric was maxed out on wisdom, but lacking in agi, str, and dex. Couldn't solo a ghoul when he was out of mana at level 18.

My current one, could solo a ghoul at no mana at level 14. Depends on what you like. My current cleric is now 27 and at about 130 wis without going wis item shopping greatly yet. (I'll remedy that later, but for now he has to tank for his group, so I have to sacrifice everything for ac, dex and str, just to survive)

Depends on what you want. If you're looking to be straight out support, work on wisdom, but leave about 100 str to carry bronze with. You won't be able to carry much loot, but you'll be able to wear a full set of bronze armor. If you're looking to be tanking/melee/healing/nuking, then even up your stats a bit. Try for 100 str, at least 90 on dex, 80 on agi minimum and toss the rest into wisdom. I've found it to be a very good balance. Tanking blues and sometimes even soloing them at 27, so it does work. I'm also seeking out some of the new cleric armor as it buffs those same stats that let me keep tanking longer. Keep in mind, in a full group, especially one that has other seriously armor-clad characters, you're primarily a healer. When you end up in a group of all casters, clerics, druids, enchanters, wizzies, you'll find you could make an excellent backup tank.

Balance is everything. No matter what is lacking there's a mob somewhere that can take advantage of it. My DE cleric has great natural agi (90) wis (118) but awful strength (65). My priority has been to get enough str items that I can wear heavy armor in all the other slots without being dangerously close to encumbered (you know, when you get that five copper split and all of a sudden You are encumbered!). In this case str is an insurance policy for my agility and my bank account (back to poverty after buying enchanted plat and level 29 spells). My secondary priority has been not wis or ac but magic resistance - doesn't matter how much mana I have if I've just been feared or nuked to cinders, and because of the cleric's advantage in resistances I like to make myself the target of enemy casters - they will do a third of the damage to me that they will do to my tank. Third priority is a balance of ac/wis items, favoring ac somewhat as I am rarely at full mana anyway. Fourth priority is pushing dex and charisma above the 25-notch levels (75,100,etc). Last thing I worry about is int since I have so much of it anyway, and it comes with a lot of +wis items as well, they will be +wis +int.

I would probably have the same priorities if I was a high elf or erudite or gnome because they have the same strengths and weaknesses. As a dwarf I would probably go for wis first to increase the tank to a respectable size, the agi to avoid getting pounded, then all the ac you can get as you hardly have to worry about encumbrance even in full bronze plate. Next would be int so that melee skills and secondary spell skills (i.e. divination) wouldn't lag too far behind.

In sum I say that whatever your racial weaknesses are should be your priority. One mob who can exploit this weakness and it doesn't matter how good the rest is. I would assume the same goes for other classes as well.
