As you know, Luc, I use complete heal on tanks, and not just "super buffed up level 50 warrior" type tanks. For complete heal to be the most mana efficient, you only have to heal around 1200 hps. I don't have exact numbers, but I imagine most lvl 45+ melee types have that much unbuffed. Add reso and even "just" pinzarn, and you've got 2000+ hps. And if you are fortunate enough to get shaman buffs like health and talisman, all the better.

Obviously, the ten second casting time requires a bit of foresight, and, personally, I look at that as a challenge -- something that separates good clerics from great clerics. Either you cast it well enough in advance so your tanks are not at any real risk of dying and you maximize mana efficiency, or you don't use it at all and revert to superior or greater heal. There's nothing that says you can't change your spell load during combat, so if you decide complete just isn't going to work in a particular situation, swap it out with something that will. Having said that, there are certain zones or areas in zones where I won't have complete loaded because I know I'd never get a cast off fast enough or without interruption.

Of course, every cleric is played differently, as evidenced by the spell loads posted on another thread. Some people load 3+ heals, some load more stuns/DDs, some load defensive spells (DA/DB/gate). With that in mind, IMO as long as you do your job and keep your party alive under reasonable circumstances, you are playing your cleric "properly" and anyone who claims that you are playing your cleric "ALL WRONG" is a pompous, closed-minded ass.

What happens if you start casting complete heal and a MOB starts wailing on a caster?

Well, there's a few solutions to this.

  • If you have a decent enchanter in the party, that's taken care of.
  • If you have a decent tank in your party, the monster will be taunted back onto the tank.
  • If you have any secondary healer (druid, shaman, paladin, even occasionally ranger) in your party, they can heal while you finish casting.

    The most important thing is learning to work with the strengths of your group. Complete Heal is too good of a spell to never use, but it has it's limitations just like anything else in the game.

    Casting CH with regards to non-aggo, is the fact that a MOB will remain on a target with 20%hp rather than 60% hp- which you would heal with sheal/gheal. So, if a mob sees you gheal the target at 60%, they run for you. If you gheal at 20%, they stay on the tank.

    Practice of CH is so important. THe best proving grounds for this is in OOT and the Cyclops. A friend (48 paladin) and I (43 cleric) soloed the ancient cyclops ph there. I would measure hit damage mitigation vs the hp of the tank. From that you can learn his breakout point when to start casting CH. It took me about 8 fights to learn how to time the CH when he was at 1 bbl of health. I believe that this is the ultimate solo technique for 2 ppl. Not very practical in solB or lguk when you have damage sharing. Nothing worse that a cleric who lets 3 tanks get to 30%hp in a fight trying to wait for CH to recycle.

    In heavy combat situations, the team will try and get 2 main tanks to hold the train, while the rest augment them. I only have 2 tanks to heal, casters don't overburn, and auxillary fighters (monks/rogues/rangers) work their magic.

    I think the most important point about this CH spell is to communicate to your group what you are doing and that you assign someone to back you up if you get interrupted.
