Few rules I live by:
1. Stand on pull.
2. Dont even think of sitting.
3. CH from as far away as possible
4. Dont even think about sitting after healing. If you even think it the mobs will come eat you for dinner.
5. Have other clerics help you CH. Take turns so you dont CH twice and move to the top of the list for the beatings.

The guild I am in has not fought the very big Velious mobs (such as Velk, Vind, Tormax, etc), but for mobs like Lord Dolj... in Velk's Lab, we have the main tank /disc defensive right away, and then have all healers (cleric, shaman, druid) spam small heals on the main tank for about 7-10 seconds. With this, we hope to spread the heal aggro over serveral healers. Once the first 7 or so seconds are up, the 1st cleric in the rotation begins the CH's. From there, we stick to complete healing, with other healers landing cover heals if needed.

The downside is that you "waste" mana in the beginning due to using less efficient heals. Plus, if only 1 tank is gaining aggro for X amount of seconds, you have healers taking rampage hits during that time.

I'm not too sure how practical this is against some of the bigger mobs, but it was enought to do Lord Dolj.

I don't have a whole hell of a lot of experience with this. But here are my observations from the experience I have.

One strategy I've seen (I realize this will not work with all mobs, especially the much nastier ones) is first tank /disc defensive and takes agro, as his disc goes a second tank starts building agro to catch up and takes over full agro (hopefully just when first tanks defensive runs out) second tank /disc defensive when he takes agro. This buys the clerics a small bit of extra time and gets more agro on the mob. Of course if your timing is off, your main tank goes down, and a rampaging mob can totally screw this strategy.

Other then that I've pretty much just done what everyone else has said, stay at max range, use a tank with defensive disc, don't sit, and pray to whatever god you happen to worship.

Because I haven't fought the nastiest of mobs (tormax, dain, etc) I've only had a severe cheal agro problem once. Puller got the hell beat of him in ToV so I started Cheal as soon as he was in range and it landed just as he came into the room before the other tanks got in. I got summoned of course and somehow popped DA with only about 100hps left. By the time it dropped the tanks had enough agro for the mob to not go back to me, but that was just drakes in the beginning of ToV, nothing serious.
