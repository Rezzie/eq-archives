A decent pet is what we need! I have a level 58 cleric so I have a good sense of the beginning, middle, and end career of the Cleric. Here is how it pans out:

START: levels 1-20 or so. Lots of options to solo or group with others. Groups can be 2 to 6. Fun factor is high. Play dynamic varied! Woot!

JOURNEYMAN: levels 20-35 or so. Options start to get compressed. Soloing becomes harder or unmeaninful at certain levels. Then gets restored at times with new spells. Cleric's fun factor is under threat at times, but overall is still there.

ADVANCED: levels 30-43. Options very limited. Fun factor very much vanishing unless you love sitting all the time and being a servant to all healing and buffing needs of others. Soloing gets very hard during this phase if you want reasonable exp for the time and risks. Clerics start to become virtual slaves to others with no alternatives. No counterbalance to this, but hey, you are a "support class" and you knew this going in right? And please forget that other support classes (a la Enchanters, Shaman, etc) can solo thanks to PETS.

VETERAN: Levels 44-50. Options open up again for varied play dynamic. You can solo in a few choice places (too few even with Kunark) and have fun (specters in Oasis, Feerott) and also have lots of invites to grouping. Power of our Direct damage spells, stuns, and roots is meanfingul and useful too! And we get the coveted level 49 ress! Of course we become most loved and most hated depending if we charge for resses or not. Will we drop everything we are doing to ress others in real or imagined crisis? Some class envy and resentment is possible.

VICAR/HIGH CLERIC: Levels 51-60
Fun factor begins to vanish and you are back to being a "support" character all the time. Lots of invites to groups, but no real variety in the role. Lots of sitting. Sometimes a thankless job since you are expected to save everyone even when the mana is gone. No real opportunity to solo any more. Terminal boredom is a real issue. Most of your combat spells (read NON support spells) are too weak or outright buggy/poorly designed (Hammer pet is an insult, Mark of Karn is nerfed, your new root actually is no better than Immobilize, your new Direct Damage spells to living and undead do too little dmg versus mobs that now have 7,000 to 15,000 hit points--let alone resists). There are also significant bugs from the old pre-kunark skill caps, so you will fizzle more on spells 51+ no matter how good your skills and specializations are.

For the sake of balance, fun, and more, we need either: A Real pet like Shaman have, OR lots more mana as we go up levels to do more with what we have and make SOLO play an refreshing alternative to being a constant server to others needs. This would probably have to be coupled with much shorter cast times as well.

I share all this to let others see what is coming. If this voting is meaninful and could influence Verant, we all need to be aware of the entire "life span" of a cleric. The simple solution and most realistic for Verant is to give us a GOOD pet between 51 and 53.

Final thought for those who have not used the Hammer Pet spell. Here it is revealed. It has no hit points (200?), the hits it does are a joke (hits are 20-45), it doesn't hit fast, it takes 600 mana to create, and ONLY LASTS WHILE ONE MOB IS ALIVE. So ya, you can pour all your mana into it with symbols, AC buffs, Frenzied Stren, etc and good luck. The pet better kill the MOB and then you have a long ass wait to sit and do nothing once more while you get mana back.
