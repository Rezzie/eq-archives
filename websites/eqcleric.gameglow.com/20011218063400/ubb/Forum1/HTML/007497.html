My first character was/is a 55 Ranger. I never worried for a second about his spellbook setup until he hit 39.

For each of my caster alts, here's my setup (my ranger's book now looks similar as well):

- Page 1&2 - 10 most often memmed spells
- Page 2&4 - 10 next often memmed spells
- Page 5&6 - Control spells and frequently used class specific spells
- Page 7&8 - Debuffs and Summoning spells
- Page 9&10 - Buffs
- Page 19&20 - Pure utility spells and PVP spells that would have otherwise been retired
- Page 21&22 - More PVP stuff and mostly retired utility spells.

I then put all my retired spells in the back of the book starting on page 50 and working backwards grouping them all by type of spell (healing, buffs, control, DDs, DoTs, whatever)

I also have a hotkey made for each category listed above. I try to keep this consistent across all my characters to make things easy and so far this method has worked quite well.

I have a 44 cleric, but have played other casting characters. My spells for all of them are set out thus:

pages 1 & 2 : most frequently memmed spells, and the order they appear here is the order they're scribed, eg root is always 3.
pages 3&4: most frequently memmed buffs & cures of the appropriate level
Pages 5 & 6: utility spells
Pages 7 & 8: combat spells - including fear and stuns (tho lowest lvl stun is on page 2) of the appropriate level.

After that each set of pages is for each level of spells. At each new set of spells, I swap some around from the front of the spell book if they've become superseded.
I have set up hotkeys to bookmark different pages by level and the different groupings (buffs, combat, utility etc) so I don't have to flip pages.
I need to redo the pages for the resist buffs but otherwise it works for me. If you're starting a character put outdated spells up the back, (in their level order), that way you've left enough room at the front for the organisation of your groups of useful spells. My shaman alt is forever having to move spells back to make room for new clusters (she has way too many buffs!)

My druid hubby has a similar method - and he has to include ring and circle and succor spells too. With hotkeys bookmarking the pages you can't go wrong.

I got sick of endlessly flipping pages, especially when I needed to swap a spell in and out really fast and this has saved me a lot of cursing.
