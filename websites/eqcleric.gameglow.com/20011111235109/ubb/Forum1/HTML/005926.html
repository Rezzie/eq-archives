As far as putting a rate on it, thats hard. Losing a point or two of wis and gaining AC is generally a good thing in my book.

If you are collecting opinions, here is mine. I have always placed a stong emphasis on AC for these reasons:

Having extra mana adds nothing if you are forced to use it healing yourself more often due to low AC.

Having extra mana will save no one if you can't cast the spell in time without being interrupted.

Those last few points of wisdom result in extra mana for you only when you are allowed to med to full before the next pull. (My regular pullers rarely let that happen) And for those big fights where I do anticipate needing every ounce of mana, I swap equipment to get it (ac shield swapped for an off hand wis item).

I carry wis items to get me to 200, but have normally equiped as little as 185 wis to keep my AC higher. Presently I am level 51 with a 194 wis and a 883 ac.

Currently I run around with 1092 self buffed AC and 214 wisdom. Jansorensen was correct in that the high end cleric gear, it is almost impossible to get rid of the excess wisdom. I no longer use any solely high wisdom items in any of my slots when there is a high ac alternative. Really the only way I could get rid of excess wisdom is by switching my Water Sprinkler for a Paw of Opolla *snicker*

If forced to choose however I would go for AC item as long as my wisdom was at a decent level. The times I have been full to OOM in one fight, where a slightly higher mana pool would of made the difference, is extremely rare. More often are the times where I die with mana, because I take too much dmg or die too fast to channel through a DA, enchanter can mezz, shaman can slow, or tank can taunt off me. Would extra ac saved me those times? maybe, maybe not. Would higher mana pool saved me from dying with mana left? No chance. Shoot for 190~ wisdom, get an enchanter to give you insight, and work on your resist, ac, hp. Eventually your wisdom will go over 200 anyway. The most important way to help others is to keep yourself alive, a dead cleric=useless cleric.
