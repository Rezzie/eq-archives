Ok, I just found this site in the past few months, so forgive me if this is a dead horse http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif

One thing has boggled me ever since I started playing a cleric. Why is it that clerics don't start out with any bonuses to resistances? Monks get +fire, rangers get +fire and cold, rogues get +poison, warriors get +magic, and specifically, paladins get +disease. Aren't paladins supposed to be warrior/cleric? So where do they get the disease bonus? Certainly not from the warrior side. Logically, it comes from the cleric side. Yet, true clerics don't get squat. I'd like to see clerics get a resistance to disease at least, equal or better than a pally.

Yes, we have the ability to buff those resistances. I'm inclined to believe that we don't always have those resistances memed. Personally, I can find a better spell to go into my slot. Besides, I'm not talking about new or existing spells.
Yes, we have the ability to cure poison and disease. Once again, not talking about spells. Personally, I'd rather use my mana on something else.
Yes, other classes, like druids and shamans, don't have inate resistances. If I were a druid or shammy with SoW, I probably wouldn't get into too many situations where the monster actually got within melee range. Casters have pets to take the disease or poison attacks. If the pet dies, they lose out on a component. If we die, we lose out on exp.

Here's a proposition. What if clerics were to get a bonus similar to warriors in that it would go up every 2 levels or so, but to disease instead of magic resistance. And it would cap at level 50.

Anyway, that's my 2cp http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif
