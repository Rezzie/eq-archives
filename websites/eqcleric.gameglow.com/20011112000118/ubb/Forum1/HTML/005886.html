Traveling alone:

1. Greater Healing
2. Celestial Healing

- Greater healing for quick remedy and celestial when I have time to regain life and I'm severely hurt.

In a group with 50- tanks playing their first character:

1. Greater Healing
2. Celestial Healing
3. Superior Healing

- Fresh tanks tend to panic too much or run out of range while I'm locked in a CH effectively killing themselves.

In a group with experienced tanks / High level pet:

1. Greater Healing
2. Celestial Healing
3. Complete Healing

- My favorite. Approximate CH to hit at around 1 bub allowing warriors to maybe get a critical in. Always keep in mind what you are fighting and what is happening around your group, switch to heals that will have your tank's hitpoints hover around 3-4 bubbles when nukers or AE dealing mobs are around.

- Complete healing is also great on high level pets. Watch out though - new server side filtering placed a greater delay on the pet health updates.


With your emergency heal (gem #1) you need to anticipate what will happen in the next few rounds. The split second you see the mob facing one of your weaker characters, you might want to start healing so that your heal lands right as the damage is done mending the wounds while you are getting prepared for the second round of punishment (depends on the mob of course). Your heals should be bound right next to your movement and sit/spellbook buttons and you should have the person who you assume will be getting hurt next targeted. Generally its a good idea to switch between group members during the combat always having the person who you think will require the next heal on lock. This reduces your response delay and sometimes saves a few lives. Often healing after you see hitpoints go down is not enough, by the time your heal goes off you will be remedying second round of combat placing target at 2-3 bubbles and falling behind, thus 3rd or 4th round will finish them off (casters mostly) if the tank fails to taunt off. Always predict what will happen next and stay in the 3rd person view (since you don't have to move around much) watching out for trains and adds.

Good luck!
