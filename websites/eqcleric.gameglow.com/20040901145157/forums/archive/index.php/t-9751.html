Hardly! How can a class suck when it defines people's play sessions? When I log for the evening my group normally shuts it down.

Clerics are the glue that holds a group together. Clerics are the enablers that allow groups to delve deep within a dungeon holding the expectations of loot and adventure, not mere survival. Late at night in SolB or LGuk the calls for "a healer in the XXX room, preferably a cleric" are common. One night I had a group &lt;b&gt;begging&lt;/b&gt; me to go to the Assassin room with them; I was NAKED at the time (corpse was stuck in the wall). After 1/2 hour of this they hooked up with a druid. Poor guys never did make it to the Assassin room (it's the very first room within the tower in LGuk's dead side.

I could continue on, but let me address your specific complaint; namely, that of equipment. Before any class had quest armor guess which class had available the widest variety of equipment. Yep, the Cleric class. We can wear the plate of a tank, the leathers of a Druid, and the chain based armor of the Shamans and Rangers. We can choose high AC, high Wisdom, or a mix of the two. We can play front-line with the tanks or hang in the back with the casters. Granted, we might not last as long as a tank, but that's a HP issue, not an AC issue. Our Defense goes just as high. Show me a pansy-in-a-robe tanking a mob and I'll show you a corpse-in-a-robe very shortly.

Don't let the quest armor get to you; the issue really doesn't affect you in the mid-30s and later. This is because you can get into higher level groups (if you wish) where BETTER armor is available.

We can go offensive (you will ROCK undead once you hit 34, and let's not even talk about 44, can you say 585 for 200?). We have damned good buffs (hps, ac, resistances). And of course, we can heal. We are 2nd to none as healers. At level 40 druids and shamans are strictly relegated to secondary healers; they cannot heal a tank fast enough to turn the tide of a battle. A level 40 tank does not notice a 300hp heal; it's less than a bubble. Superior Heal is the minimum usable heal. And if the fight is controllable, Complete Heal is super mana-efficient at 400 mana (can you imagine 1600 hps from one spell? that's Complete for you).

Do Clerics suck? Hardly!

I love being a cleric. I have grouped with all of the other classes and find too many faults in them (of which I won't elaborate on, would take too long). There are 2 classes you can't live without. One, is the tank. You just can't survive without someone taking the brunt of the damage. Second, you can't survive without a healer. And past level 34, if you aren't a cleric you aren't a healer. I don't see how you can survive without these two types of characters in your group.
Now, with that thinking in mind, how bad off are cleric? Not bad at all. Yes, our quest armor is a running gag. Yes, we seem to be in the dog house for class improvements. BUT, combat doesn't continue without heals and NO ONE else gets the good ones. Heals aren't our only attribute, but they are an important attribute. Stuns, res, and buffs help round us out (better in my opinion) than many other classes.
In my opinion and experiences, its wizards and mages who suck. They are a glass jaw with some minor pyrotechnics, easily replacable.
I dearly wish Verant would make our quest armor worthwhile but I am not going to hold my breath on that one. I just play my cleric as best I can and serve my guild with whatever tools I am given.

Unless the mob has been through the temple and gotten buffs, they usually don't resist much either. I rarely need my armor, and could probably get a group there if I was naked. Yes, the Cleric armor has about 3 less AC than the other Class armor, but it has one great advantage to poor humans like you and me. It weighs as much as small plate armor! This makes us much more comparable to dwarves Clerics when it comes to carrying capacity with better looking women. : ) Last night, I got my Cleric Boots too, I went from 8.1 weight medium bronze boots to 4.9 weight Boots of Reliant and gained 1 AC. Sure, I will probably be replacing it when I can in order to get +wisdom items, but I will still keep my Cleric armor in the bank for use in marriages and guild meetings.
