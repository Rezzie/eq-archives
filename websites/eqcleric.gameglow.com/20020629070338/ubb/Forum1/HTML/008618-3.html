The last single target direct healing spell we get is divine light, and it sucks, here's what I propose.


1) The cost and mana aren't so bad in that it scales with the spells that precede it, I would reccommend reducinging the casting time and increasing the range. Remedy just doesn't cut it when the puller has taken a few spells or HT's, or even just a massive amount of melee. Those are the two things that frustrate me the most about DL are it's cast time and range.

2) A new spell at 56
Relief
Heals 750 hps
Costs 275 mana
Takes 3.2 secs to cast
Has a range 1.2 times that of remedy

3) A new spell at 59
Holy Infusion
Heals 2100 hps
Costs 550 mana
Takes 4.5 secs to cast
Has the same range as Relief

4) Is it too much to ask that we get a group armor spell? Not all of cleric types are at level 60 and *have* aegeolism to buff their group(s).

5) Rework the AA points we get, I'm taking a hint from the other posters here that the AA Abilities we get are relatively useless with only a few exceptions. Some ideas have been suggested, but I'd like to add my own. An AA focus skill that gives you a chance of comsuming half (or none if it requires just one) of the spell components required to cast the spell.

Those are the main point i'd like to make with healing a buffing, now onto things that can make a cleric busier (and more fun) in groups and the ability to solo. If you try to find the difference between the three priest classes in a roleplay sense the are as follows:

Shamen are tribal and tied to spirits
Druids are nomadic and tied to nature
Clerics are civilized and tied to a god

The role of a traveling cleric with respect to others is to convert heretics and destroy infidels, in addition to making sure that those sympathetic to their cause, remain alive and thus sympathetic to their cause. This, of course, brings me to the following suggestion:

Let us convert our enemies

Give us the ability to "charm" a creature of 2/3's our level or lower. This would not work like a normal charm however. The creature would behave like an enchanter pet, making it impossible to command, but it would fight by your side. This would not last long however, a few minutes maximum. Once the creature breaks the conversion, it will be completely and irrevocably aggroed on the cleric (It could be reconverted though). This spell could also be cast upon any group member suffering from the effects of charm or mesmerize to bring them back to their senses.

Creatures who have good faction with your deity would not be affected, creatures with specifically bad faction would be difficult to convert.

I also like the previously mentioned idea of introducing chants at higher levels.
