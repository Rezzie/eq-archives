Va Dyn is not a hard fight at all but it's an extremely LONG fight. I think that the best I've done him with was with about 14 people. In all honesty, a CH rotation isn't needed here because his damage is pathetic when he's slow. However, he's a very good test Mob. If you want to work on a CH Rotation and get your Clerics some practice, do it with him since the fight is long and it will give you an idea of what to expect with other encounters.

I have no idea about how powerful your Guild is but Lord Bob is also a good fight to practice Main Tanking and CH Rotations on. He Flurries so you can get an idea of some large output.

After that, go for Vindi. Odds are you will not win your first, second or even third try but Vindi is an EXCELLENT mob to really see what high end Velious/Luclin mobs can do. Just make sure you have plenty of Clerics, and fill the rest of the raid force out and go for it. He takes practice but once you get the tactics down, it will make future raids much easier since you have an idea of the mentality behind such fights.

The log parser I use: http://home.inreach.com/kai/EQCompanion/

The reason a long fight vs a low dmg mob is easier than a short hectic fight is simply that the only risk is falling asleep or running out of mana - compare to a fight where the tank can actually die and you have to scramble to heal the next tank while he has to try to get aggro before the mob tears up too many of your damage dealers.

The margin of error is just alot larger on a long fight vs a low dmg mob than a short(er) fight vs a high damage mob. Combine high dmg and long fight for the truly rough times http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif Just consider that if the mob cannot kill someone in less than 12 sec you can actually react to them taking aggro and start a CH - but if they kill in 6 sec you have to start healing before they get hit.
