Repost with improved English from EQ Gameplay Board:

I'm in a bummer mood.

I've worked really really hard to get my cleric up to level 29, a level rightfully I should be proud of. I know that a lot of people have multiple level 50 characters, but for someone who is running an office and raising three children, I felt deservedly like I'd really accomplished something.

But now I find that but for the continued thrill of "leveling" that I have nothing to look forward to that doesn't depress me immensely.

I realistically can't look forward to questing for the new class-specific armor. I simply cannot camp 7-8 hours at a time for multiple items. The Bilge website seems to
suggest that people have sought him alone for upwards of 50 hours. (I guess Verant
livened up the 7-hour Kithicor stump watch quite a bit by placing undead mobs in the
zone that I cannot possibly survive for a few more levels, especially since many cleric
spells in the zone do less than full damage consistently). Real life simply doesn't allow that type of play for me, and truthfully,
even contemplating this after my massive failed Journeyman boot camp (which I wrote
about on the EQ board two months ago) is enough to make my head swim with nausea. So no cleric armor for me, at least not through quests.

I cannot look forward to obtaining the non-cleric quest items that have come to
symbolize all the hard work that one must put into this game. There are lots of reasons
for this. First and foremost, it seems that every time I get to a level financially where I feel deservedly proud (currently 600 plat in bank), I find that my hard work and savings are worthless in the player driven economy. Today, I found out that even the most modest of mid-level to high level items, gatorscale leggings, was worth 1100 platinum.
A bloodstained tunic, 500. This is of course from the couple of players who were even
offering anything for sale, as opposed to for trade. (Just for the record, I have nothing
valuable to trade, at least not that valuable). That's the second depressing part. Even all the plat in the world does not grant me access to anything that is special (to me at least, but "basic equipment" to most powergamers) in the game.

I certainly am not looking forward to Hell level 1 (level 30). I have had every one of my friends almost quit the game over levels 30-31, 35, 40 and 45. These are undoubtedly
major milestones, but if they are so special why am I dreading them like the plague?

I don't look forward to growing proportionately poorer and poorer while those who powergame and then rape the economy with delusions about what items are worth
continue to be rewarded for their powergaming, obsessive focus on playing as long as possible to acquire as many levels and as many items-for-sale-that-are-used-only-by-other-types-of-classes-as possible. I know, these things shouldn't bother me. But they do. I don't like the "rich get richer/poor get poorer syndrome" in my real life and it is chafing to an extreme in a game that is supposed to be fun for everyone. All I ask for is the ability to obtain the same reward for my $10 that others do, with reasonable effort. But it appears that is impossible, given the current structure of the game and the direction that Verant continues to travel in it with it.

And as I mentioned recently on EQ board I definitely don't look forward to the ongoing requests for multiple zone travel for revives (someday resurrection if I keep playing that long). I have decided that it would interfere with my game play too much to be /anon all the time, and send the wrong message. So I shall always be visible, and thus, always be
asked for the unreasonable.

I am not looking forward at all to soon being forced to hunt in zones where trains are
lord, all spawns are camped and no one wants to group with a cleric they don't even know (my friends are all but one significantly higher level so they hunt different places
for the most part than I do).

So what is there to look forward to? I have even started alternate characters and have 2 up to 9 and 10, hoping that the new strategies and thinking required would
respark my excitement about EQ. But all these two characters have done is painfully
remind me how difficult it will be once these characters hit level 20 or so. I get tired
just thinking about it.

OK this was admittedly a whine and a long one at that. What can be done to make it better? I'm not sure. But I do wish that Verant would address the issues raised by this e-mail somewheat in upcoming game patches. Fix the cleric quests so that normal human beings can do them. Make it so that those who farm and harvest things they neither want nor need cannot hold regular non-powergamer-players like myself over the barrel that is the relatively rare rewards in the game (in my view, the only things that justify the complete misery that many feel struggling through the mid-range/higher levels.) Look at the message that is being sent to dedicated players by the hell that is "the hell levels" and ask if it really is worth affirmatively turning people off to the game?

Sorry for the whine. Any and all thoughts appreciated (even yes, the trademark "Good!
Quit! Less Lag for Me")
