I might as well throw my two cents in. My bio... 54 epic monk, 1100+ ac/2200hp without buffs. Probably because monks and clerics are posting to this thread they have not mentioned the biggest difference between warriors and monks as MA. Defensive /disc. A monk cannot effectively tank an encounter that requires the defensive /disc. Period. Any other encounter, a well equipped monk can MA as well if not better than a warrior.

Point 1: A monk should NEVER just FD as soon as he gets the pull back to the group. By dropping himself off the hate list, the mob will then aggro on the closest toon, unless someone in aggro range is sitting. A monk SHOULD stop just passed the warrior and turn to face the mob allowing the warrior to start building aggro from behind. After X number of seconds the monk can attack off and swap aggro over to the warrior.

Point 2: To the person who will always take a ranger over a monk... I will assume you are speaking about grouping in the uber dangerous outdoor zones where you can single pull to your hearts content. I hear OT can be a dangerous place. Take your monkless group into Chardok and let your ranger pull in there. Tell me how long it took for you to wipeout. I wont even talk about the difference in damage output.

Point 3: Dont knock the class because the player is incompetent. I have met bad players driving every class of toon in the game, including clerics.

Point 4: The level of the encounter makes a big difference when deciding who can MA what. Equipment is a close second. 50+ pally with a DW bracer chain stunning can tank exceptionally well. Uses stuns to maintain aggro and lower damage taken. Epic monk doesnt need taunt. Can take and hold aggro as well if not better that a warrior. The difference of say 1000 hp is more than made up for by the superior damage avoidance/mitigation of the monk as well as higher skill caps in dodge/riposte and the block skill.

Point 5: If a slower is in the group, the monk is actually a better MA in non defensive encounters. Fungi+iksar regen+chloro=30+ regen per tick. On slowed mobs that are missing/dodged/blocked/riposted, monk is regening noticable damage during fight. Throw in a high end bard and the regen is insane. I have MA'd the King in Old Seb. I routinely tank mez adds when required.

Not all monks are equal. Some monks are better tanks than warriors. Some aren't.

As much as I am accustomed to having a warrior for a main tank, if a monk has sufficient AC and HP, I'm quite happy with a monk playing tank (as long as the other melees don't get macho and suck up aggro). I'm even happier if they are okay with using Mend freely. I'm always in a good mood when a monk uses Mend.

Most of the monks I've grouped with - particularly in Karnor's Castle - have equal (if not better) AC and HP than the warriors I've grouped with. And most of those monks end up out-damaging and thus out-taunting the warriors, anyway. So either because of their stats or their ability to hold the mob's attention, I think they're better suited to being the main tank. If the warrior isn't up to par and the monk wants to take a beating, that's cool. Complete Healing a monk makes me feel kind of odd, but again, if they've got enough HP to warrant being the main tank and can stay in range long enough for a Complete Heal, why not?

However, if I'm grouped with a warrior who has better AC/HP than the monk, and the monk is pulling, I do hope the monk has some tricks for shedding aggro when the pull arrives. A monk standing there and getting beat up while the warrior's looking useless is bad. (And no, FD instantly on arrival isn't always best to transfer aggro. Having a monk flopped at the casters' feet before any damage is done to the mob usually ends up with a bunch of cranky, injured casters.)

And there are monks (and rangers, paladins, even druids and shamans!) out there with insufficient AC/HP who just refuse to believe that a group is more efficient when a person other than them is taking the damage. It's not limited to the monk class.

If you're at your wits' end because your monk is pulling, and your warrior is trying to tank, but isn't able to hold the mob's attention, talk with the group and see what suggestions they have. It's possible your monk was better suited towards tanking than the warrior. If not, maybe the monk wasn't aware of how different his HP/AC is from the warriors, or why you wanted the warrior taking the damage. If all else fails, maybe you could root the mob on the warrior and let everyone know that it'll smack whoever's closest, so if they aren't the designated meat shield, they should back off a bit.

It's been a long time since I've grouped with a monk needing education. Most of them have been very understanding and willing to accomodate a cleric's needs. I guess I've been lucky.
