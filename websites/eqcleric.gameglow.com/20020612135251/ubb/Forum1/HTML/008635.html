quote:
Are they worth it?

My God yes! These give Clerics an ability that we've never had before. We can now self port TO somewhere and still have bind/gate to self port BACK to somewhere. A bank, peridots, invis potions, and stores are always one zone away from me now. I was bored the other day and decided to hunt Hill Giants. I filled up on Fine Steel and coin, ported to Felwithe to bank it and then gated right back to kill more Giants.

quote:
Would you buy one? If so, how much would you be willing to spend?
Yes I would have been willing to buy one. Mine cost me around 5k all told. I hit 188 Pottery and decided to play the odds. 19 failures later I still didn't have mine so I skilled up to 206. Got my Faithstone on the 3rd combine after that.

When they first came out, they sold pretty easily for 10k. I sold or gave away over 30 of them myself and I made a small fortune (more money than I had ever seen in the game). Early last week though sales started to really dry up as Clerics were getting them and other Potters entered the market which increased supply, decreased demand and drove prices down. They go for about 7 or 8k now and there are at LEAST 5 people capable of making them with a decent success rate. I expect prices to fall to about 5k and hold steady there. Personally, I feel, the Stone is worth MUCH more than 5k since it's a USEFUL utility that we didn't get before.

I'm at 218 Pottery now but I'm done mass producing these Stones. Currently I only make them to order and do commissioned work. I was getting MASSIVE headaches from people trying to screw me over and from people who couldn't afford to take 5 minutes to look on EQTraders to see how these were made. Then there were the people who laughed in my face when I told them 10k but came back 2 or 3 days later begging for one once they found out how difficult these things really are to make. In the end, I made my money, bought myself a high end white horse (plus a few twinkie items) and got out of the business about 10k richer than when I started Pottery.

Deciding to make my own Faithstone was one of the best decisions I've made. Deciding to get out of Pottery is the next best decision.
