Heh, in a couple words: Good luck! ; )

Gnomish Vanishing Devices Require a very high level in tinkering skill, and it requires a diamond among other things to attempt to make one. Out of all the skills, including alchemy and jewelcraft, I believe tinkering is the most expensive to train up out of all of them. You need mid-100's tinkering skill to have a chance at success, and that requires 10's of thousands of plat to get skill tihs high.

In short, looking at it from a profit point of view, even if gnomish vanishing devices sell for a ton, it is really not worth it for a gnome to train up tinkering high enough to get these. In order to get one you would need to 1) know a gnome who is dumping every penny he or she has into this skill for a long time as a fun hobby, and 2) That gnome would need to be a *VERY* good friend of yours.

The biggest problem with this is Verant never made a skill-training-up method that is even close to inexpensive for tinkering. When you get high level in this, it takes thousands and thousands of plat to get the skill up one or two points. If you are lucky, there *might* be 1-3 gnomes on the whole server thats anywhere close to being able to craft this device.

Now, if you somehow get a gnomish vanishing device? An awsome item, I believe it has a few charges of an invis that lasts 10-20 minutes? It is also rechargable with a class 5 battery you can purchase, and you dont need tinkering skill to recharge it.

Just a minor addition:

The diamond is not the problem. Diamonds are easy to come by compared to the real problem ingredient.

A haze panther eye.

Haze panthers are in Wakening lands and they drop eyes so rarely you'd think they were all genetically blind.

I got unbelievably lucky one day and got one from a panther that had agro'd on me as I was going by. I rounded up the other ingredients and a well known tinker on our server (Thank you Meshugugget!) put it together for me and I gave her a few hundred platinum for her time and effort. (and some white chocolate cookies if I recall)

Given the cost of the mana batteries, it's no cheaper than potions, but mana batteries stack, and it's a neat item to have.
