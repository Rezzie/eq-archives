The only dumb question is the one that isn't asked.

That being said - the procedures for ressing somebody (yourself included)

Target the corpse to be ressed, and cast spell - the person (including yourself) will receing a box with something to the affect of "somebody wisses to ressurect you - click yes or no" and you get 2 buttons - left side is yes (marked) and right side is no (marked).

Once you click the yes button you are teleported to where the body is and you are then marked with the ressurection effect. You lose half your hp, and all of your mana and you lose a significant amount of your stats temporarily. The affect of the res takes about 2 mins to disappear. You will have to loot your body to get your stuff back.

Hello there Sanaddar

1- A cleric CAN rez himself. (cool huh!)
2- You do have to target the corpse to rez.
3- The person has to accept the rez, there is a bug with that, which I will not go in detail here, but make sure the person WANTS a rez, before you cast.
4- They do have to re-equip, and not only that, they will suffer a "rez sickness", which brings your str to 0, and some other stats are lessened. Also, they will only have half health, and no mana.

Hope this helps.
