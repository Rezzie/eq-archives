Just my personal opinion:

If you weren't throwing Naltron or one of the expensive symbols, they yes the gems should be put in the pot with the rest of the loot. Jasper is so inexpensive as to not be worth mentioning and certainly the selling price of a star ruby covers those costs several times over.

I've stopped to look at WHY I symbol lately and therefore changed WHEN I symbol. There's only a couple reasons:

1. Someone needs the hp to stay alive
2. Efficiency

Number 1 covers the puller in some cases, and enchanters (some days you just can't heal them fast enough) Number 2 covers someone you plan on complete-healing.

If you're just doing routine hunting and using celestial/remedy/divine light and healing people when they get under 3 bubbles, there's really no reason to symbol the tanks. I've mostly given up on symboling myself since I don't get a lot of agro. Now if you're in a tough dungeon, then it pays to be more generous.

And of course a raid is a different matter.

For what it's worth.

From a lower level perspective, I see mention of gems and clerics as a way of training my other group members about what will be expected of them at higher levels.

Thus I will likely say: "I am symboling tonight, I will be claiming clerics prerogative", "I am not symboling tonight, I waive clerics' prerogative", or "I have covered my symbol costs, we can start sharing gems now".

In all cases, though, I am trying to get across to the group the notion that the gems and such are for the cleric to distribute. A good cleric will make sure that everyone who uses reagents is covered for them, but it is our call.
