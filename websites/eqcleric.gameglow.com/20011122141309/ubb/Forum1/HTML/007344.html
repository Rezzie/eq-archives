This is an interesting thread. My guild actually has an age requirement for admittance. Of course folks can lie about it if they choose, but we can generally tell who is and isn't of age. Being a guild of adults, we can relax more and joke freely. Granted, we normally keep the tone low-key and jovial, but a lewd comment or drug related reference can be made at times. We prefer to not make all of our members uncomfortable wondering if there is an underage player around waiting to be corrupted.

All of this being said, we invite most of our members after having grouped with them for a long time. Most of our members have grouped with enough officers by the time of invite that a fair assessment to age and maturity level can be ascertained with some degree of certainty. We have had a few underage folks slip by us in the past, but their maturity level during their 30 day trial period was enough to have them politely be removed.

We have many family members and RL friends in our guild. More than one parent/child combo and RL buddy combo makes it a stronger bond with more of a familial atmosphere.

We have folks that range from the youngest 16(being a son of a member) to the oldest (being the mother of a member) in her late 40's maybe even 50's I am not really sure. The key though is to find folks that have the maturity level to maintain adult contact and interactions. This game is supposed to be restricted to 18 and older, but there is no way for Verant to police that. The best you can do is try to surround yourself with a solid group of friends and guildmates that all have the maturity level to handle themselves as needed given any circumstance.
