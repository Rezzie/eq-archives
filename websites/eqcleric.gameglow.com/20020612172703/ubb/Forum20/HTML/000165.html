CH rotations are convenient ways of assuring a constant flow of heals to a MT. Are they absolutely needed? For most mobs, no. But they do simplify the process and are a powerful way of focusing cleric power. Also the quality of your tanks has an impact on where and when a CH chain is required.

Back to the subject... once you get Aary slowed, you don't need to CH chain the tank, one CHing cleric with a spam healer support is plenty.

Theoretically this encounter can be done with 3 clerics and 3 druids as healers... in practice you'll want more.

Another approach (basically very similar to above) is to have "spam" healers help until fight is stabilized. Get tank and CH rotation on Aary (3 second till slow in then 5 second, or if defensive up 4/8 probably ok- natural durability and how well tanks are equipped may affect these times). Tank on Aary gets heals from backup healers until CH rotation stable (should be fairly quickly). Get a tank with a cleric and mebbe a backup healer on each pet. Then EVERYONE else attacks one pet till dead- then the other pet till dead- then Aaary gets whacked. If this fight is new to you- you may benefit from an identified backup tank for each of the 3 mobs, a cleric to do heals randomly as needed, and a backup healer in each group for safety. LoH has its place on the pull until CH rotation stable (and not everyone's internet connection is as fast as NASA's so this stabilization reflects the nature of online gaming as much as skill levels).

If you are planning on chaining CHes on an MT, and you already have hotkeys pretty much set up, it makes sense to set yourselves up in a rotation rather than just hope for the best, especially if you are doing this with clerics that you don't have many months of experience playing with. If you have a team that has been together a very long time and can predict how each other will act, then go for informally staggered CHes, but you have nothing to lose and a lot to gain by picking a pause setting and going in turns.
