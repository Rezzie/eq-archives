I am a level 43 cleric.. that means I have a very nice spell that gives me 50% of my experience back when I die. I have died once since 39, and I was quite pleased to get 50% of my exp back when I rezzed myself.

Today, I am in Dreadlands looking for a group. Someone shouts, 'Is anyone rezzing?' Since I'm not doing anything, I shout, "If you can do with a 50% rez, then I am".

Now, I don't expect everyone to go crazy over my 50% rez. Its nice, but I know at high levels you lose 8 hours of experience for dying. The difference between a 50% and 90% rez can be several hours. With that said, I am not offended when my rez is refused. I even do a /who on this person to first, if the person is over 50, I usually assume that person is in need of a 90% rez.

However this person says a minute later,

"Looking for 49+ rez"

I found this extremely rude. No, "Thanks Quotison, but since I lost a lot of experience in Level X, I am really looking for a 90% rez, but thanks for the offer."

I have spent 21 days playing this character to level 43. I am not a second rate cleric. If you don't want my rez, that's fine, but don't throw my rez aside as if it was useless.
