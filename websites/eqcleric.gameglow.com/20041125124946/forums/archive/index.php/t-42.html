Hinanar
05-30-2000, 08:51 AM
Clerics fullfil a vital part in a group. In most groups clerics are irreplaceable.

Druids are jack-of-all trades. They do not fulfil a designated role, they can do bits of everything. They can heal a bit, they can nuke a bit, they can DoT and they can fight a bit (but can't tank). They also have a limited amount of crowd control (charm etc).

Clerics are specialists. We specialise in healing and keeping party members alive though a limited crowd control (atone, stun, root)

Druids in the higher levels will find it more hard to get into a group, simply because they don't have a simple defined role in a group.

Will they be asked to be a healer in a group? maybe as backup. Will they be expected to act as a wizard-type nuker? Only a little. Will they have to use crowd control? Only if there's no enchanter.

At higher levels, the difference between the hybrids and the pure classes becomes greater.

Are clerics too powerful? No. Our aim is to keep the party alive. What is the druids role? depends on what element of a group is missing. Can druids fulfil a cleric's role in a party? Only if the party is in a very safe spot and can easily cope without a healer at all.
