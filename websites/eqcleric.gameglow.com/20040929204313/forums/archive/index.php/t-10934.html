Clerics who have gone most of their EQ lives without ever being a debuffer can often make the mistake of getting agro after multiple resists.

First, remember that all these big mobs get Malo/tash/occ/malosi etc. These need to land before you think about debuffing.
....but thats often after the chanter completely dispells it.

This takes time, so it can be a while before you casting mok/mor is actually both safe and effective.

Too soon and you get resists/agro because it wont land pre-tash/malo OR you junkbuff the mob that hasnt been dispelled yet=p.

EhrnamAro
03-12-2002, 11:47 AM
MoR puts a 15pt reverse damage shield on a mob. (reverse meaning when it hits something it takes damage)

MoK puts a healing shield on a mob. The amount of damage healed per hit is relative to the dly of the weapon being used as I recall. In addition it overwrites any damage shield currently on the mob. (does not need to overwrite a DS in order to heal shield the target)
