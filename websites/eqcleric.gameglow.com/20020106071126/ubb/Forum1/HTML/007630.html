Just an FYI what I've dug up from misc areas:

Divine Resurrection is straight up a tele to corpse with 100% xp restore. Nothing more (i.e. no rez effects, etc)

Invis to Undead skill lasts a SOLID 27 minutes and can be broken by usual invis breaking things. 7 second recast on this skill.

Celestial Regen is 500hp per tick regen for 4 ticks (2000hp). Weak, except that its a nice heal when you are flat oom and need to get some kind of healing on a tank or something.

Sacred Barrier (DA) is only 18 seconds, but yes can be cast on someone. Trick? Have cleric say "15 SEC TO CAMP!" and then hit him with this to secure successful camp out if needed.

Clensing Light (Disease/Poison removal) - Will remove it - period, no matter how strong.

Here is the skill some clerics said sucked --

Turn Undead (Level 3 - 7.5 sec cast time): Unresistable (Level 51 and under i hear, cant confirm) AoE fear all undead, and DoT for 10,000 damage per mob over 4 ticks. Ummm, 2,500dmg per tick. Ouchie

Turn Undead (Level 2 - 5.0 sec cast time): Unresistable (Level 51 and under i hear, cant confirm) AoE fear all undead, and DoT for 5,000 damage per mob over 4 ticks. 1,250dmg per tick.

Turn Undead (Level 1 - 2.5 sec cast time): Unresistable (Level 51 and under i hear, cant confirm) AoE fear all undead, and DoT for 1,000 damage per mob over 4 ticks. 250dmg per tick.

Turn undead could be fun. I can see alot of level 60 clerics suddenly running around the otherwise empty zone of Unrest. Starting huge trains and send em running off to die. Has loads of fun potential. Has no practiacl usage. I may get this someday just for the fun of it

Regarding the 3rd level Turn Undead, I could see this as possibly being useful for a number AE solo or grouping situations.

First, if you were to find a zone with enough xp-giving undead to make a 20-30 mob train, with enchanters back at your camp to stun while you get this skill off, well, you certainly wouldn't need a wizard.

Secondly, as far as AE soloing goes (unheard of up till now, I would think, but feel free to correct me), with Stun Command (9 seconds and low resist, 1 second casting time), we could concievably get TU3 off in time. This is tricky, of course, as Stun Command is a target AE (and thus, possibly, restricted to 4 or 5 targets, I haven't tested this) and you'd have to be enough ahead of your train to avoid being caught in the stun, then run into their midst and start casting TU3 in the 1.5 seconds you have to spare.

The other possibility that we can only speculate about until someone tests it is that possibly TU3 will work while DAed. I know this sounds crazy but the reason I suggest it is that TU3 is not a spell, as such, it is a skill, an ability. And we all know we can bind wounds while DAed, so maybe this would work too.

A long shot, to be sure, but if it's true, 20 mobs solo every 15 minutes while waiting for DA to refresh isn't anything to shake a stick at.
