I guess here are my thoughts on the matter..

At high levels (51+), where it's all about grouping I think clerics, enchanters and true tanks: warriors, rogues and monks are the most powerful classes/contribute most to groups.

Pet casters don't contribute as much as the above unless it's call of the hero (mage) or summon corpse(necro). (yeah their pets are nice too but pathing usually isn't as kind)

Wizards provide an important role as taxi/evac but their dd's compared to the 1000's and 1000's of hp's of mobs isn't significant.

Druids/Shamans are generalists and though they make small groups shine, high level dungeons require balanced groups, reducing the need for them.

Hybrids like ranger/sk/pally/bard all have a nice mixture of skills but again contribute little to well balanced groups and have a hard time getting groups at all 40+.


So there is is the class interdependence going on that Verant loves but at high levels it's more of a dependence on clerics, chanters and true tanks than an interdependence.

To balance this perhaps one can safely say at low levels who would want a cleric when you can a druid/shaman that can heal as well and add many other abilities to the group.
