That other thread covers it pretty well, and my personal experience (admittedly levels < 15) has been good results. I was splitting the skeletons at the dragon skeleton in Steamfont regularily with it (blue and white cons), and a bit earlier was splitting goblins at the camps in Butcherblock.

If a failed Lull didn't have a chance of aggroing on you, clerics would be hands down the best indoor pullers - just keep casting till it takes. This line does not at all compare to harmony, which is unresistable and works every time (if done correctly, LoS issues taken into account).

Also, unlike harmony which renders mobs totally non-aggressive, the lull series only reduces aggro range, so in a group of mobs lull the ones in the back first - if you pull past a lulled mob it will aggro.

I normally use Pacify (off Donal's bracer) as a way to pass green mobs I'd rather not spend the time and mana to fight (i.e. specs on the way to fear portal).

I have used this line to break up dungeon rooms but only do this as a convenience; don't depend on it to work. At lower levels I'd use this mostly in places where the zone was conveniently close
