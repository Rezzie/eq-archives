I always wished there were other non-combatant ways of gaining experience outside of quests. Example - through tradeskills. Originally posted by Thistleburr:
Continuous healing buff, mana for hp (similar to Shadowbond)

Wow, now this would be cool. Maybe we could go afk for a couple of minutes in a dungeon and not have to worry about the tank dying while we're gone.

As for how to apply the healing resistance when using it as an undead nuke, I see a simple solution. Remember that heals in D&D are touched based, meaning you have to actually touch the target for it to work. My solution for EQ would be: Hold your weapon on the mouse pointer and cast the heal on yourself. Your weapon then gains a single charge of: Superior Harm (undead only) or whatever, that procs the first time you make a successful hit with that weapon. The benefit is that a couple of bad resists don't kill you since you'll do full damage with the spell each time. The downside is that you'll have to actually melee some, and you'll probably have to root and retreat for each recast. Does actually make having good melee skills important for a cleric though. Probably immensely difficult from a coding standpoint, but IMO a good roleplaying way of doing it.
