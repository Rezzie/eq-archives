I can't think of a single Chardok guide out there, but here are few pointers from my time there. (I leveled my enchanter from 58 to 60 there at zoneline / entrance fort.)

1. Expect everything to be buffed: If you have one roaming shaman mob, every single mob along the path will be hasted. If you have one roaming wizard mob, every single mob along the path will have a damage shield. This is especially bad at the entrance fort, where one roaming shaman mob can haste 6+ mobs.

2. See invis mobs: All dogs (chokidai manglers, lacerators, sunderfiends - though you won't see the latter two unless you go deep) and sarnak caedosaurs can see through invis. Dogs will cause more problem, as they can spawn as roamers and see through rogue hide/sneak.

3. Roamers that summon and gate: Fortunately, they can be stunned rather easily. But once in a while, you'll get mobs with rather high MR, so be on guard.

4. Most of the zone can be done with one group: The beauty of Chardok is that except for Royals and Korocust, all the drop are random. (and even Korocust and Royals hall is sometimes done with a single group.)

And here is the list of mobs I've personally seen near the entrance, and their loot.

Sarnak jannisary: Your standard melee mob_01. They drop silver swiftblade.
Sarnak templar, accoucheuer: Shaman mobs, so they'll be hasted and have sow. Accoucheuer can summon. Rare 1, sarnak arcane fetish; rare 2, stave of shielding
Sarnak aruspex, aruspice: Wizard mobs. Fortunately, they usually waste all their time casting force strike instead of meleeing, so they tend to be the easiest of the mobs your group will face. Rare 1, burnt wood stave; rare 2, sarnak battle shield
Chokidai mangler: As noted above, they see through invis. (can be a pain if you are doing CR) They hit kind of hard and often for a mob their level. They carry no loot, unless they have a jagged chokidai spine. (9/25 piercer)
Sarnak myrmidon: Bane of zone group hunters. They summon, hit very hard and fast. (can spawn with FS scimitar much more often than one would want) I've seen imbued ivory collar drop off them.
Sarnak caedosaur: They proc knockback spell just like tentacle terrors - be aware when pulling, as many of them spawn overlooking pits. Edge of Cabilis can drop off them. (some 2h sword)
Chardoki golem: Your standard melee mob_02. Rare 1, golem tear ring; rare 2, burnished wooden staff. (mage staff with right click bolt spell)

And I haven't seen sarnak skullsplitter and symphonic sabre drop in person, though I hear they can be found on myrmidons and dogs. And I forgot what drops a 30/40 hammer. Items I marked with "rare 2" are just that - I have seen ten sarnak arcane fetishes drop before I saw my first stave of shielding. Sarnak battle shield is much more common than that, fortunately.

I am sure there are a lot more things I am forgetting here. I can tell you that unless you are going after named, there really is no reason to leave the zoneline - it's not as if loot gets noticeably better, while the risk increases substantially. This unfortunately leads to the crowding of the zoneline, as one decent group can clear all of entrance fort, entrance roamers, and then some more. Some groups enter the tunnel on the left hand side of the fort using lev and sow to hop on the ledge. (it's supposed to be one way, but can be entered with lev.) Rarely will you see a group doing experience grind go past two sarnak caedosaurs however.
