Hi Wastral,

Whenever you do a big move, its always a good idea to camp out - and I mean everyone (no whining here from the people with slow connections hehehe). It may seem a bit overcautious, but if you have some Hate newbies with you, they may not report a Harm Touch after/during a GFlux...

Liches cast gravity flux - if anyone gets GFlux'd and HT'd - they have aggro. Anything cast on that person is going to transfer aggro to another person and so on. Did anyone get GFlux'd and HT'd?

Aggro in Hate seems to ripple through the entire zone - almost like something alive - which makes the zone even a little more evil (if you ever see mobs running for "no apparent reason", someone has aggro and doesn't know it and is about to get a nasty suprise. Mobs don't run in Hate unless they're aggro'd.)

Pathing in Hate is quite odd at times (incredibly bad on the second floor). So if someone is HT'd, its possible that it will take minutes for mobs from the second floor to get to you - or they may not even come because of the pathing (which usually seems to be the case) When casting on someone with aggro, that aggro immediately passes to you as well - for instance if a monk pulls, and gets healed without the monk first sucessfully FDing, that happy aggro that the puller had is now also on the healer)

Harm touches for 750+ is almost definately a second floor mob (I've yet to see an Ire Ghast do more than in the 600+ range), so it sounds like you got some random aggro, after the two HTs I imagine you were below two bubbles of health and the lich may have summoned you for that, who knows
