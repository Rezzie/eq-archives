First things first: Lull is NOT harmony

- Lull can be used indoors.
- If the mob is already aggroed, Lull's not going to help.
- The resist rate of Lull seems to be CHA based at best guess.
- A resist may or may not cause the mob & friends to come rushing at you, whereas Harmony never disrupts the mobs.
- Lull is NOT an AE, so you have to cast it on all the mobs that you intend to lull, not just area.
- Basically, this spell is annoying because you can't depend on it to split pulls like you can with Harmony. It might of been useful at lower levels, but at higher ones, it's just a matter of time before it gets resisted and you're dead.

The lull/soothe/pacify line can be used to split 2 or sometimes 3 static spawns. Because it has a short duration and can be resisted, casting it on 2 mobs is about the limit. Always have an out though if it doesn't work....

Occasionally, a resist results in a single pull- i.e. it accomplishes what you were probably trying to do originally.

Coupled with root and atone it can be used to descend into a dungeon without having to blast everything along the way. It's particularly useful for getting into places where you have to pass irritating agro greenies...
