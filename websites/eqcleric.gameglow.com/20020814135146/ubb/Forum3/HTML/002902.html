Hi,

I sat down and did the math on how much effective FT a 10% mana preservation item gives you. Double-checked it with a few friends. Here it is... results are quite interesting.

The effective FT of a mana preservation III item with 10% mana preservation is:

FT = 0.10n * (p/((n-m)t+0.90p))

t = Irreducible time in ticks spent performing some activity (i.e. root-dotting a mob, performing a quad-kite, casting 5 CH's, etc.)
p = Total mana spent doing the activity
m = Mana regen per tick while doing the activity (usually this is your standing mana regen, but for something like root-dot, it would be closer to your sitting mana regen, etc.)
n = Downtime mana regen per tick, i.e. mana regen while medding/recovering

In most situations, the right term is close to 1 (total mana spent divided by 90 percent mana + mana you lost by not sitting during that time). This means the effective FT is primarily a function of your sitting mana regen.

In other words, the more FT you already have, the more effective FT the mana preservation item gives you. This is actually intuitive, when you think about it, as someone with FT15 benefits more from saving 'X' mana than someone with FT2, because they regen it back faster.

I just think the result is very interesting. It means, for almost any activity (besides CH, heh), that obulus death shroud is better than the 8th shawl in terms of mana saved (since most of us have >20 mana regen sitting with c3).

For classes like necro and shaman, which have insane mana regen, the formula starts to get funky, as the right term gets to be << 1.0.

An easier way of explaining it is simply stating that the effective FT of a mana preservation is

A x B

Where A = Mana preservation chance
B = Mana regeneration rate

If mana preservation is 1-10% saving then on average it saves 5.5%.

If the average cleric has baotr + c3 + standing regen then they will have at least 21 mana regen a tick.

so 0.055x 21 = 1.155

If you med it goes up almost double, if you have alot of flowing thought gear/mental clarity or bard song also it goes up.

Maxed out with ae catana (lets not get into multiple level 32 mana songs), you are looking with ancient bulwark at perhaps a max of 52 standing mana regen a tick.

Meaning that it has the effect of 2.86 FT a tick (though mana preservation allows you to go over the 15 FT cap).

Note however if you click a mod rod 3 times in one tick. The mana regen rate even without your own is 660.

Meaning that mana preservation 3 + mod rod 2 = effective FT 36.3

Since most fights nowadays revolve around mod rod generation and use - mana preservation for non CH casters is worth more than 2x max flowing thought gear.
