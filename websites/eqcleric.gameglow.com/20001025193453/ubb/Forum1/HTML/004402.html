Well I did it guys. I went and joined a guild. The people in the guild are very nice and are fun to talk to. But I have a few issues that puzzle me.

1. The guild is a very new guild (they are still working on the website!). I did a /who all guild, and noticed that there are at least 10 to 15 people on at all times. The only thing is that they most of them are considerably lower level than me. The guild leader himself is only level 20. I think when I ding 29 tonight, I will be the hightest level in the guild. This really doesn't bother me, but I would like to group with my guild members someday!

2. I was also made an officer upon joining. I feel honored to be offered such a position, but I am puzzled by it. I suppose it was because of my level, but I feel officership is something that should be earned.

3. I am worried that if i get too high a level too fast, I'll start getting begged to powerlevel everyone. I don't think my guild would ask this of me, but you never know!

Any advice I could get would be greatly appreciated.

If you are worried that you are levelling too fast and that they might ask to ride your coattails, take a short break and do something else that is fun. Take up a trade skill, or start a 2nd character for a bit to let the rest catch up. Then again, they might be slower players that don't care to advance too fast. If that is the case (like it was in my situation) you will need to do what is best for you and continue to play and advance.

Your also about to hit those lovely hell levels. That will slow you down some, though how much depends on your playing time (and proper usage of it). I have been in the position of your guild mates most of my EQ life, with my friends always higher than me because I can't play enough. It does make for harder grouping, but sometimes having one person significantly higher can be a great advantage. For example, my friends and I were in Karnor's a couple nights ago: myself (36 cleric), a bard (41), a druid (37 I think), and a paladin (52). Our guild thought we were nuts but the pally was able to pull solo's to the zone line and we had a great deal of fun (and lots of XP as well). It wasn't easy, but no one died (or even below half life) and we got to see parts (though not much) of a place some of us couldnt get to otherwise.

Level indifference can be limiting, but it can also be a blessing in disguise. Just depends on the group.
