Sentinel - if ya unfamilar with this spell then basically its a good spell to use to annoy lots of people quickly. Functionally it is of no use, its purpose is to tell you if a critter approaches you, but it spams everyone so much it just annoys. Gonna have fun casting it 4 times around a raid party and watching the whines :>

Celestial remedy - 65 hps a tick for 4 ticks for 190 mana. Its at level 19 where the biggest heal you get (other than daring) is a single target 90 point heal with an efficency of 1.5 hp to 1 mana ratio. This spell has an efficency rating of 1.368 to 1. Therefore its worse than an instant heal, although at this level it is probably about as quick at healing. If it is to be of benifit it really needs its mana reducing to 130 so that it is a 2:1 ratio. Putting it on line with the level 24 greater heal (but over time). Even so the level 44 celestial healing is about 3.35:1 ratio in comparison to the closest non CH heal of Superior heal being 2.4. Therefore it being approximately 50% better than a lower level heal is not unbalancing. making celestial heal to be 50% more efficent than healing would put the mana cost at around 115 for a 260 hp heal. None the less it needs work.

Sermon of the righteous - not tested but its an undead dot, confirmed by trying to cast on a live mob.

Sacred word - tried this a bit, mainly cos I had it memed instead of stun command. The damage component means you have to think much more about using it. It does seem to have low resist on blue mobs.

Armour of faithful - nice solid hp/ac upgrade with a massive duration upgrade. Its a stepping stone to the one you want, unfortunately all level 50+ spells are mob drops, but initial impressions suggest spells are dropping at an acceptable rate.

Overall I must say the store bought spells are a little uninspiring. As a level 60 cleric it was the 50+ spells that were of most interest to me, but it seem that getting instant spells is against verants principal.

Unfortunately the only interesting cleric spells look like blessing of risen, group aegolism and marzin, of which only one truely gives us something new (IE mana regen).

none of the classes look to get anything entirely exciting with the store bought spells, except perhaps wizzies with their familar (will have to check out what those are like).

They are sold in Shadow Haven, the city that is right off the Nexus. There are trainers and spell salesmen for all classes in that city. The cleric spells are, erm, on the east side of the city in a big building. Eqatlas has a map that is useful.

For all classes it looks like most of the sub-50 spells are lite versions of some of the level 50 spells. They are not useful to folks who are already 50+, but will be very nice to have for the lower level folks. That said, the mana/heal ration on the heal over time does seem underpowered even for its level.

The 52 and 54 spells were dropping like candy in the fungus caves last night. They might be tweaking the drop rate soon, since 2 spells from a single level 45 critter was very generous indeed.

The combination that the high priests should be eying is group aegolism and the top-level skill that makes your group spells into aoe spells. You should be able to give aegolism to an entire RAID for 4 peridots and most of a tank of mana.
