Actually, along the same lines is the idea of no-drop "uber" spells from high level mobs. It could easily be done, and would benefit the game as a whole, as well as give something back to the casting population, as opposed to making a_warrior06 better at melee. There are multiple items in the game that give a "definite" improvement for melee classes, haste items are a good example, they increase the melee output of a melee class by a fixed percentage rate. A warrior with an FBSS is 22% better than a warrior without as far as damage output, its written in stone and undisputable. Better ratio weapons have the same effect, but there's nothing in the game for non-melee classes, and by putting in no-drop spells that did this, it would balance the playing fields a bit. I'm not talking *new* spells even, I'd be happy with improvements of *old* spells, even non-combat ones would be fine, Aegolism II that costs 10% less mana to cast, someone else mentioned group BOF, thats a fine idea as well, and by making them no drop, and come from high end encounters, it helps everyone, without hurting the game, unless they really think complete heal that costs 360 mana to cast vs 400 is unbalancing. There's spells across the board they could do this with for every class, not just the clerics. Its easily implemented because spells can already be in the loot table, just flag them as MAGIC/NO-DROP, and its all good, and add 1 entry per spell in spdat, they already have a dozen "dummy" entries in there anyhow.

Just my .02
Khalim Richelieu

P.S. I'm not going to take credit for this idea in its entirety, a lot of friends and guildmates had come up with this concept, and it would give the high end game something more for the casters out there.

On the idea of no-drop 'uber' spells...if you're willing to lose the no-drop flag, this idea was already implemented:

Reckoning - more uber than Retribution
Reviviscence (well, store bought :P) - more uber than Resurrect
Hero, BoF, Aegis, Marzin - much more uber than pre-50 buffs
Aegolism - you get the picture

Casters get quantifiable, definite improvements to them from dropped spells. Unfortunately there's a very strong feeling in the caster community that everything in spdat flagged for their class' use is an entitlement. There's a profound resentment when a caster levels and doesn't have all spells of that level scribed. (Tho casters that research may be more patient.)

Tho, now that I think of it, forcing spells to be no drop would dull this feeling of entitlement a lot. (Once the caster riots subside :P)

Aegolism improves a cleric's buffing ability arguably as much as an fbss improves a melees offensive ability. They both drop. There's not a lot of difference.

And btw, what happens when you cast an AC buff on a shaman? If mana is tight they're one of the first to always get an AC buff from me, due to aggro slow causes. Not once has a shaman ever complained, but maybe they're just being nice. What stacking issues are there?
