After regaining almost two levels lost in the Lake of Ill Omen, Neiff is a very happy cleric.

High Keep - The first level for the goblins was great at lvl 23 except that there was a lot of downtime and sometimes someone died. Once my cleric had Greater Healing at lvl 24, this didn't happen (two warriars died in the group when our monk pulled 2 warrior goblins, a couple cabilists, and some guards while I was OOM, but I'd expected all of us to die).

In a previous post, it was mentioned that a lower level party could get whiped out when pulling from the warrior room. From my experience, it doesn't work that way -
what happens is the train goes straite to the guards who kill them off while all the characters head directly to the library where a guy with flaming fists does massive damage (300+) to the invaders.

Warriors and raiders are definately the way to go during these levels, but mana is always an issue.

Money isn't always that great in High Keep. Last night in six hours, Neiff made maybe six to eight plat. I liked S.Karana better for the fine steel that drops from the gnolls.

When I can't get a group in the Keep, I head off to E.Karana and find myself a tank. I memorize endure poison, counteract poison, and we fight crag spiders. The spiders drop tons of silk and if you're not a tailor, there are enough tailors out there that will buy the silk swatches from you, at least on the server I'm playing on.

Cure poison doesn't work very well against their poison which causes a lot of damage and persists. It was taking me three or four "cure poisons" before my tank was cured while I get it once with "counteract poison".

S.Karana is also good with the gnolls, just have a hard time finding pick-up groups there. It's also a long trip to the bank, and that fine steel weighs a lot. Still, both money and experience here is great. Sometimes it's all camped out though, which means there is nothing to pull. During one of these times, I went into splitpaw itself where I promptly died from a train. The inside was completly camped as well.

EDIT:
A good mid 20s to mid 30s group (and I mean good, know how to play their class and work well together) could make their way through Permafrost or SolA I think. Some good wisdom items in both places from what I understand.

My 50+ warrior friend when he helped me in Permafrost basically just got us in. There are a lot of goblins of all types (priests, wizards, etc) that con blue to me but they swarm like crazy when walking through. An enchanter is a must, some heavy hitters, and some tanks. Our party could have broken in on our own I think, but it would have taken a lot longer than with the 50+ clearing the way before us. The first section of the area was all con green at lvl 25, with the occasional high green which would aggro on us.

I've heard that SolA is basically the same with the added danger of falling into lava. I think the goblins might be a bit tougher there just based on what I conned in Permafrost as being green/blue.
