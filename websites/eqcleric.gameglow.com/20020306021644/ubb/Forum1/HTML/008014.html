Several reasons.

We are pretty much the most linear class their is. Enc's, necros, shamans, druids and the lot all have a couple avenues of oppurtunities to them. For a cleric it's heal and buff. The only thing I've seen as a derivation from this for about the past 5 months is soloing in the Grey where dark blue (to level 60) mobs only have 5-6k hp and are undead. We can still only do them one at a time and the downtime is ridiculous but it's actually something.

About a year or so ago there was a cleric surgance. Back when rangers, necros, paladins, shadow knights, druids, and to some extent bards SUCKED they all seemed to have created clerics because everyone say us as the best class of all. Well.. couple months later what do we have? 4 clerics LFG at any point in a zone. I'm a 60 cleric and I can't often get a group to save my life.

Rezzes. We got a huge burden put on our head here, and it causes many clerics to go /role or /anon because we just simply get tired of people asking us. Then you get people who yell at you for not crossing 2 zones to rez them, etc and it leads to much of the same problem druids and wizards have with ports.

This ties into the second point, but we are the least exciting (in terms of interaction with the game) class their is. The maximum I will do in exp groups is 1 MoK per mob and an occasional CH, DL, or stun. We don't very much get the thrill of dealing damage or being in the fray of battle, etc.

Total vulnerability. There are 3 classes that still have NO method for casting infinite invises (to the best of my knowledge). Clerics, Monks, and Paladins. Yes, warriors, who were never even supposed to get spells, can invis themselves where we cannot (never understood this). Monks can feign to save their lives, and paladins stand a chance at killing something if it hits them. They also have LH and just as much stun and root power as we do not to mention lots more hp and ac and can much more easily run if they get in trouble. If we get attacked? Pray you regain your root and it isn't interrupted and it lasts long enough to run to a zone.

In summary, the overall fun of a class is it's flexibility. Monks deal good damage, but also have good damage avoidance and can FD. ENC's have the burden of mezzing but can charm and have fun soloing with pets and dots. I could continue explaining how each class have several things open to them, but the sad fact is, for the most part our linearity is our downfall.

Back when they were making EQ they had 2 spells, CH and Rez and they were probably trying to figure out how to put these in the game. They decided to give them both to us. Ever since, we were doomed to live a life of boredom.

I agree with several of the points already stated.

Finding a group is near impossible.
Soloing for exp is an absolute JOKE.
Raids are very dependant on us, so there is pressure there (this one doesn't bother me, I love raiding)

Friends asking for rezzes at inopportune times can sometimes be hard to say no (thank god I don't have any friends )

The only thing I haven't seen listed here (unless I missed it) was the pressure we (or at least I) feel when actually performing my job. If someone dies...I feel responsible. Even if there was nothing I could do to save them, I still feel that sense of responsibility, and I often replay what happened in my mind to try to see if I could have done something different. Most of the time there was simply NOTHING I could do to save them. But every now and then I know I made a mistake that cost them their lives.

There is no other class like this. We really are critical to the success of raids and even some exp groups. I know this may sound silly..after all we can rez them. No big deal. Well, sometimes it IS a big deal...either to the player that died, or to the actual raid.

If anything would make me quit playing a cleric...it would be this. But this is also the very thing that keeps me playing the class I do. We are a very specialized class, and people need us. We might not always get the praise or thanks for what we do...but the very fact that all those pressures exist should reaffirm that we are needed.

I find the people that quit playing a cleric really never liked the class to begin with. Nothing wrong with that...they should find where they fit in the whole game. Makes everyone happier.

Our class is a catch 22. On the one hand we outshine all other healers to such a great extent we are the only option for most high end raids.

On the other, we're a one trick pony. We CH and... CH and occasionally res. CH is our definitive spell and it's also our bane. Every time we are evaluated, people take one look at CH, realize just how rediculously overpowered it is, and say we're good enough.

We've been handed 3 unique spells of any real use since EQ was expanded.

The Celestial Line - Finally, an alternative to straight up healing!

Mark of Karn - Useful, nerfed, pseudo-unnerfed, semi-unnerfed. Moderately useful but still not much more than a parlor trick.

Mark of Retribution - A damage shield. A damage shield you cast on mobs! A damage shield you cast on mobs that stacks with all other damage shields!!!!!!!! I just about fainted when I realized this was something new AND useful.

So where did we go wrong? Well, it was SoV that started it really. With CH being so powerful, VI had to tailor mobs to be harder and thus be outside the reach of normal clerics. And thus was born the CH chain. You cast it on cue and keep the main tank alive. In other words, pretty much everything else we have is worthless.

So why the burnout? We've got the weight of EQ on our shoulders. We are the cornerstone of every group and raid. So we should all quit and make people appreciate us, right? Wrong. I'd say half of the clerics in EQ now are PLed twinks being run on second computers. They spam CH. Sure feels good to know all your knowledge and experience and means nothing and you can be replaced by a PLed cleric being played by a monkey who pushes a key every minute or so.

We have nowhere else to go. Verant won't give us a serious review, other classes already feel we're too powerful and won't support any expanded cleric abilities, and there's nothing else to do on raids but buff and cast CH. Look at our AA abilities. Our only true "class" abilities are a manaless HoT spell and a 60 game day 100% res.

Verant needs to take a serious look at clerics. Expand our melee to somewhere just below that of a hybrid so we can at least HIT SOMETHING. Give us some better nukes. Not the horribly inefficient Judgement but something that gets on par with Reckoning for mana efficiency. They need to do SOMETHING to give us incentive to play.

Burn out.. it comes and goes with me and my level 60 cleric with 200+ days played. Main reason I get the "blues" is basically what is mentioned above about pressure to raid, rez, and attend, and to add to that the sometimes long waits for groups, and also the reluctancy at times, of other classes to lend me a hand... such as with ports, quests, invises, or sows.. yet when any of them need a rez first thing that happens is they do a "who all cleric", and look for the highest one in the zone.

The whole set up for clerics was for us to be group based.. not RAID based.. The problem that we, and in my opinion, where Verant went wrong, was that too many of the same skills were given to too many classes. Now in the end game, druids, shammies, pallies, and even necros can heal in most exp. group situations, and they have more to bring to the table in the way of soloing, and meleeing. Our only saving grace is our rez, that is only when something goes wrong. Add to that Pallies, and necros can even do that and its importance is slighted. In the case of exp. grinding in fungus grove, even the almighty rez is normally not needed, since few deaths occur with any kind of competant group.

Our complete heal on raid situations is about the only thing that we have that comes into play that gets our class the status of being truly needed. Unfortunately these raids may have 5 clerics or so on a ch rotation, and then the mob will drop some quest piece that can be used for all classes, but with a different end result, so IF it is a quest piece that a meleer needs for a new piece of gear, and lets see the melees out number the clerics by 3 to 1, then you can guess what the odds are of the cleric getting the drop. There are some limited ways around this using various point systems, but even at that, the odds do not favor clerics.

The twink factor is also abit depressing, as being an "original" cleric, I worked my tail off to where I am at now, and I see druids, warriors, etc, that twinked there cleric up to the mid 50s in half the time or less.. When I started my cleric a 50 percent rez was uber.. So now to summarize all this, I have my toon that has 200 plus days on it, that helped the newbies back when everyone was a newbie.. and now he competes for loot with twinks that became a cleric via powerleveling. You guys getting the idea of frustration yet?? : )
The good thing about being a cleric is the fact that we can be a deciding factor on raids as much or more so than any other class.. When it comes to tackling that uber mob, there are few ways around NOT having a cleric or two there..The other classes are important, but try and get a raid together to take down Klandicar, with out a couple of us around. Not impossible, but very unlikely..Still doing a good job on an uber mob, or saving that tanks life when he was about to go under is what keeps good clerics playing.. at least that is what keeps me going.
I know this is a long post, but I have been doing eq for a long time now, since its inception, and I do see some real shortfalls in our class. I'm thru with the whine now.., so offer me some cheese. : )
Good luck to all you new clerics out there..

I havent burnt out on cleric, very much doubt I will any time. But clearly, from watching other clerics burn out, and from watching my own sense of 'getting tired of some things' i can tell you these are the major problems.

1) We're a one trick pony. We buff, we heal. In xp groups this is all we can do. We get stuns, but chanters do it better. We get nukes, but they are laughable. We get weapons, but mana regen is more important. We get MoK and its pointless. at least MoR is very useful if its a mob that's gonna die slowly. 225 mana, can add up to around 1000 dmg if its got a lot of HP. that's nice.

2) On raids, we're even MORE limited to what we can do. Mostly i'm talking about the complete heal rotation here. A trained monkey can do this. A macro can do this. people make BOTS to do this. Let me show you.

Hotkey1:
/Tar Cleric1 (lead cleric)
/assist Cleric 1 (to get tank target)
/sit off

Hotkey2:
/paus XX (appropriate pause amount)
/Cast X (complete heal number)
/g Witty Message for Rotation
/sit on

with the exception of tank switches, or being short on prop healers, its fairly simple.

3) the pressure to play is tremendous. Pressure from friends who feel they 'need' you for groups. Pressure from guildmates who 'need' you for a res. Pressure from your guild or raid leader that 'need' you for that heal rotation. This can add up to hours of online time that you would rather have spent logged off.

Now, I got it pretty good. First, I play on test, not a lot of clerics there. In fact, test is STARVING for clerics. So i'm treated really well. In addition, my guild is the most understanding people in the world. If i cant make a raid, or simply dont want to be there, that's genearlly ok. They've got a fair share of 'cleric bots' so occasionally its fine if i want to leave, check out, fall asleep, etc.

Secondly, I've got very understanding friends. If i dont wanna 'drive' my cleric, one of my friends will bot him. Hell, he'll even get me xp sometimes. On top of that, if i dont feel like playing or have other responsobilities, they suck it up.

So anyways, that's my post on why clerics burn out, hope it helps

Keys to having fun as a cleric?

1. In xps groups load Stun, and stun command, Help with the Crowd Control.

2. In xps groups, LIVE in dungeons. We are much more fun/powerful in dungeons. Outside zones we become heal batteries.

3. Get Toys!! I have a donnals bp (click CH), Templars Leggings (group heal), Donnals Boots (click AE 120 damage), Dawnfire Morningstar of light(procs 585 and I have 150 dex)

5. Get a 2nd PC and another eq account. Network them and whola.. I have a SK twink I play with when I cant find a group, or that I solo when on raids during downtime.

6. Dont let yourself feel like you NEED to do anything. Its your game, have fun. If you dont feel like raiding all the time, join a more relaxed guild. OR (against the EULA) Find 2-3 Real good friends and Make them play your cleric when you have to camp =).

7. If no 2 computers, I would suggest a twink to go to once a week atleast. Just to get a diverance =)

8. Get in a good guild with people you really like/love.
