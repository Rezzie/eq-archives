Playing devil's advocate here, the problem with this approach is this: The static nature of the EQ world means that a given encounter becomes easier every time it's successfully completed.

Now, I wasn't around in the early days, but how many raids got completely wiped out trying to kill Nagafen before HE died for the very first time? Tons and tons I should imagine. How many Naggy raids get completely wiped out these days? Not very many at all. Why? Because we as a population *know how* to kill Naggy.

Same thing with let's say Trakanon. Uberguild puts in the time and the deaths to learn how it works. Once that investment is paid, you have the answer, and Trak drops pretty much every time.

Same with VP. I do personally remember the time when the 'unkillable' Phara Dar was first dropped. It wasn't that long ago. I imagine AL (it was AL, wasn't it?) took a fair few deaths before dropping her for the first time. And now? It's pretty much expected that if you're one of the top 2 or 3 guilds on your server, you can clear VP.

Same with Dain. AL seem to drop him every week nowadays.

My point? With things the way they are in the EQ universe, the only way to regulate the rate of items flowing into the game is to regulate the spawn time of the mob that drops them. Which is exactly what VI do.
