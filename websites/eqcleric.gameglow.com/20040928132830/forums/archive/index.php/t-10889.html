Typically, in these types of fights, the clerics will be staggering Complete Heals, with maybe one designated to do Celestial Elixir. The Druids are responsible for providing backup healing (and Shamans when/if mob is slowed), as well as Paladins using their LOH as backup heals. However if you didn't have any druids or shamans to backup heal, they should have decided beforehand if you were to be doing CH or backup/filler heals.

One thing that can really help is using assist healing.

Make a the following hotkey
/assist assistperson
/assist

That will target the mob your fighting then assist him to see who he is currently attacking. Then I use CE or DL to cover heal. Basically a cover heal is a heal like Divine Light that keeps the tank alive just long enough for the CH to land.

Flurry mobs can make life interesting, 1 flurry mob can take a 6200 hp tank to under a bubble in one combat round if they get 2 flurry procs. Vs. these I use duck healing. I start a CH on the warrior, even if they are at full life. Then if 5 seconds into the fight they are still full life I cancel and start the CH over, if they are taking damage, even if only a little, I coninue casting. at 9 seconds into the cast, if the tank is down 1 or more bubbles life I finish casting the ch, otherwise I duck. On a 6200 hp tank, a 1 bubble ch is much more mana efficient than a divine light.

My guild doesn't really rely on main tanking like a lot of guilds, we have 3-4 warriors with top notch equipment, they spread to damage between them.

Thats not to mention our uber monks and a shaman that tank better than your average warrior.

Yeah primary tanking is not the only way to go unless you're the sort of guild which gives all the melee equipment to one warrior and then lets him tank everything. In general its good to just know how to control agro so you have alternatives. There are mobs that we primarytank (as an example here I'll give Kreizenn and Tormax) and mobs that we certainly don't (CT, Yelinak, Dain). It all depends quite a bit on how you're used to doing it, but in general the important thing is everyone knows when they will get agro and how to get rid of it, then you can adjust tactics for specific encounters fairly easy.

My comment was there to mostly reflect the 'eggs in one basket' approach of these guilds http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/wink.gif Hell if you don't have overflow of equipment you don't need the primary tank to LD or lagburst to kill him or whatnot, you're in some trouble simply if he doesn't log on.

We have 5 or 6 warriors with ~6k hp buffed etc, so usually we spread agro around since we can afford to, but sometimes primary tanking is just simpler. Primary tanking has inefficient healing (CH > DL always), risks a lot if you reflect the primary tanking in equipment distribution and just plain risks lots with the LDs and stuff. On the other hand it is sorta foolproof. Agro passing TOO hard hitting mobs causes its own problems especially if the target mob gets low hp agro easy... I can't really fathom Vyemm being agro passed for one.

Oh and other benefit of primary tanking is that it can increase your melee damage compared to agro passing if you do it right. Still, there's usually some death and destruction once the primary tank dies, so in a long fight it's bit questionable if you win in damage if you have deaths when a new tank steps up.

As I said, we use both, totally situation dependant. Yelinak, Dain and CT are the mobs I'd never suggest primary tanking, and Vyemm is the one I'd never try to agro pass http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/wink.gif

Greetings!

My guild uses main tanking for most mobs, including Cazic and Yelinak. The only non-trivial mob we fight with aggro spreading is Sontalak.

Main tanking allows us to make highly efficient use of healing and maximizes the effect of damage shields (we have very few bards so this is a particular problem for us).

Mobs with knockback (Vyemm, Yelinak, Cazic) pose the greatest challenge to a main tank tactic. We have special variations on main tanking that we use against knockback mobs.
