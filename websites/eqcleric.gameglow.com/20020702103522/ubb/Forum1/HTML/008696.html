There are a few things I would point out about the "difference" between NPC and PC mobs that this change makes manifestly important.

First off, casting NPCs cheat like the dickens when it comes to things like channelling. Watching gnolls in Splitpaw channel every spell over and over for three hours with two hasted melees (Monk and Warrior) hit them was disturbing.

Second, they don't have to worry about mana conservation. They can use their entire mana pool in 60 seconds and not think about having to rest. PC Casters have to worry about that, a lot.

Third, they have far more hps even than player warriors. Hit like warriors when not casting (though the casting AI is now CLEARLY attempting to choose to cast the best nuke possible far more than it will try to choose melee attacks) and generally have the abilities of super PCs with super casting abilities. Every single mob.

NPC casters don't have the same range limitations players do (if they start to cast while you are in range you could be ten miles away and they will still hit you.)

Now, while I can agree to an extent that caster mobs were underpowered to an extent in the high end game, they are now fearsome. Woe is the puller trying to get 2 casters to camp. We had a warrior (level 54 with Aegolism) die trying to pull 2 casters in Akheva. He died in about 12 seconds as they both chain nuked Ice Comet on him. No healer can ever keep up with this sort of damage rate, it was basically like he got death touched.

Make of any of this what you will. But for now, my claim is that all caster mobs are now substantially overpowered compared to non caster mobs and players alike.
