News to me. Sure we don't get to melee or mix it up too much with the combat. Yes, we are very dependent on mana. But I for one have never, ever been bored playing a cleric.

I find it amazing that even other high level clerics say that our role consists of sitting on our butts all the time and medding, only standing up to heal or buff. My gawd, if I played my cleric that way I'd be out of a job. My role is not only to heal but to prevent damage to the group. So I'll chainstun that caster or healer mob, root extras, try to cripple a gating mob when he is low on life, throw a root or stun on the thing the wizard just aggroed, atone the mob the bard charmed or the enchanter mezzed, nuke or stun the runner that just got away. All that is juggled while anticipating when heals will be needed, throwing invigor on the warrior after he's been fighting for a while, sneaking in buffs when mana is plentiful to prevent downtime later, reacting instantly to vulnerable party members losing health, judging the timing of CH, deciding in a split second who to heal first and who can wait when it gets bad, not to mention that I'm usually the one nominated to call evac. My goal is always to be efficient enough with mana that I cause the group little to no downtime, and with clarity available and in a good group it is a goal I usually achieve. We're barely done with one battle and the puller is running off to start the next one. So who has time to get bored?

Clericing for me is nonstop action and requires intense mental energy and constant attention. It is stressful but exhilarating. I boggled at the person I met recently who said they liked playing their cleric better than their warrior because they could watch TV and play at the same time. When I'm healing my attention never leaves my screen for a moment. Even a second or two is enough time to be too late to heal someone who got smacked. Last night I missed dinner because I couldn't be afk long enough to get food. I was the only healer in the group, and we were not in a safe area. Sure I am intense, and I take my job too seriously, but I have a reputation that people rarely die in my groups.

I am "only" level 50, but the people I party with most often are in the mid fifties. I don't think either my role or my satisfaction with my class will change; I am actually looking forward to getting the new spells and experimenting with new ways to make them useful to a group.

Boring? You can be an adequate cleric by medding a lot and tossing off heals. But it doesn't have to be this way, and I would argue that it is not the most effective way to play the class, either.
