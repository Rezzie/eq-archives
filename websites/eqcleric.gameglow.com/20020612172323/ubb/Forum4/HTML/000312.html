I think we're all in agreement that:

1) In a raid (or other high-risk situation), aggro needs to stay on the correct person. The cleric reserves the right not to heal you if you are intentionally and repeatedly making an arse out of yourself.

2) Not every situation is high-risk, but even so it's still not courteous to repeatedly grab aggro unless that is your job.

and..

3) It's OK for people to let down their hair occasionally. Let's face it, exp grinding can get awfully boring, even if you are a terminally cute gnome cleric like me. A little variety can keep you awake and can boost group morale.

I think the important thing is, use your better judgment AND try to have fun at the same time. Sometimes one trumps the other.

I guess I don't get stressed about people playing "correctly" unless my mana is perpetually below the 60% mark, or we are in a zone that is so dangerous that I will die within 30 seconds if I get aggro. If I am constantly under 60% mana, we are not playing efficiently enough or the zone is not the optimal zone for XP grinding (or we need a more twinked out tank). If we are in a high risk zone (a zone where a train of three will always claim at least one life), there are enough good players dying to waste any mana trying to keep the bad ones alive. Dying a lot is how a lot of the bad players become good players.

I see a lot of players who really don't know what role their class should play in a group even into their 50s. These guys are usually the classes that are better at soloing and they have never learned the discipline of being constrained to one role in a group.

I've got this theory that the first class you play in everquest will forever affect the way you play any other class, which explains the necros, SKs and druids that can group well.
