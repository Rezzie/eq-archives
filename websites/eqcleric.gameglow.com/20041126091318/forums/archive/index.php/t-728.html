BUT AGAIN this isn;t where we should be fighting the battles for clerics...
What we need is a little more edge.

We have with Rayne's help and others gotten a change that affected everyone's gameplay, htat being the change to the REZ mechanics, and the extension to the rez timer.
As clerics are want to, we do what's best for everyone as a whole. Now let's cry US too.
Not for nerfs, not for gear, not for tools to make the game better, but for a core change.
We are plate wearing class, therefore battle trained like no other caster class is in the game.
By Verant's math Paladins are a hybrid of warrior and cleric.
Our weapon skills are 75% that of a paladin effectively, but our dodge caps at less than that by a good amount. I think it may be on par with regular casters.
Now What are the dodge skills of shamans and of druids? 75? if so we really have little to complain about, unless their battle skills are capped higher than ours.
But like many dwarves, I grow tired of getting chipped to pieces so fast in battle.

100?
150?

If someone could chart out shared skills for SK's, Paladins, and Rangers. Then post the druid's, shaman's and cleric's in a table. We should be able to see where exactly the math fails and not be misguided by Verant's claim of balance.
ONE BIG THING THAT WORKS AGAINST ANY CHANGES TO THE CORE CLERIC CODE IS, IF A CLERIC LIVES LONGER SO DOES HIS PARTY, AND THAT IS UNBALANCING IN THEIR EYES.
This can be pieced together by reading between the lines of all the posts made by verant.
Self sufficiency of a cleric is directly related to the amount of time a party will be fully effective. So any changes to a cleric would affect parites they grouped with too.
So what other changes to a cleric would bypass this?
MANA REGEN is totally out, dead issue.
Blessing items...that's on a wish list, but of course lack of imagination on the dev teams seems to be the norm.
Horses? BAH No real dwarf would be caught on a horse, our legs do the job just fine.

Enumerate our desires, eliminate that which would outright be tossed aside as unbalancing overall and select one or two things we as clerics can use to make our lives easier or more fun and then pursue that avenue.
We should pick our fights and plan the battle field, knowledge is power, and as everyone knows, us Clerics are a smarter bunch than others...well maybe not smarter, but definitely wiser.
Where others throw fits and tantrums, we plan...now if Rayne and others see fit, let's plan it out here on this thread carefully before sending out feelers to other boards. If we think it out for them, they won't have to. Make it easy for them, but rememeber, it could end up biting us in the ass too.
OUR Battle should in my opinion be for raising the dodge cap.

I take great offense when people say all we are is heal, med, heal. The only difference between a cleric is the word nuke instead of med stuck in there.

Everyone wants something different. Casters wanna melee, melees wanna cast and hybrids want respect and less of a penalty for being a hybrid. But honestly, do clerics NEED to melee better? Would Bash really solve our problems? It would be something different from what we once were but it wouldn't make us "exciting". Maybe we could dual wield and cast Ice Comet. Would that be exciting enough for you?

Dodge has an excitement factor of 0. In fact, you probably won't see much a difference. What we're really after here is a piece of the balance pie. We get beat on. A lot. A HELLA LOT. It would be nice to have a realistic chance of dodging even one attack (which doesn't happen often now as-is). But it would IMPROVE us, even if that improvement is only minute. Bash is a losing battle, more and better nukes is a losing battle, even better melee is probably a losing battle, but dodge has a chance of winning because it doesn't change us all that much.
