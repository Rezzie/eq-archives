Like the above people mentioned.

The strategy I use when leading Ix raids.

Have your range (fully buffed) go to the approximate location stated on the quest to pull Ixiblat. If you are doing multiple Ixiblat's in a row, then pull Naxot to the SF zone. Works pretty well there.

Nothing spawns in that area and nothing wanders into that area unless pull. No tress in that area either so less lag due to the animations.

Make sure everybody is buffed - Have a main tank and have 2 enchanters assigned to do the rune thing on him as well as a couple healers assigned to them. The main tank is the only person that is to taunt.

Spawn Ix and have the ranger pull it to the SF zone. Have your shamans that will attempt to slow standing slightly out front. As soon as Ix gets within range, they start the attempt to slow.

Give them about 2 seconds to work on that and then send in the pets (mage fire pets) and necro pets and so forth with the main tank.

The main tank will taunt (hopefully he is high enough level to use melee disciplines).

About 5 seconds after the main tank and pets go in, send in the rest of the melee.

At approximately 60 to 65 percent health, have all casters step fairly close and start nuking the heck out of it.

At approximately 20% health, Ix will enrage. Have melee turn off attack for approximately 10 seconds while he is enraged. After enrage wears off, have melee get back on him.

You will suffer a few casualties, but the end result is you will kill him and get your sceptre.

I have done this method probably 12 to 15 times and have never been close to losing.

Make sure your melees have AC gear on and then toss them a Magic Resistance and a Fire Resistance buff. Don't wear your FR/MR gear as you will get toasted by his melee attack - 575 double attacks.
