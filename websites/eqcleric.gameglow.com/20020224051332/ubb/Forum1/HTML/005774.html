I had a very different experience than Boanarges had. I saw many of the good parts of both crushbone and blackburrow. There are some named mobs, there are healers and melee mobs, there are runners and trains. I like this.

I make a brief trip through there with a twinked level 20 dwarven warrior and my friend a level 20 troll shadowknight.

The greenies did not seem to aggro on their own, but they would join in another fight. Either tank could solo two blues.

Kobolds DO run. They run early, they run fast, and they bring friends. We figured that fairly quickly, and started bashing them as they ran and making sure we had darkness on them.

There are some large caves with a mess o' kobolds in them. I suspect that at lower levels these might get a wee bit exciting without better crowd control than most real newbies can manage. At level 20 the place was blue-green to green.

The batlings are casters. They were indifferent to my dwarf, but they were mean. They are also higher level than most of the kobolds. Next week we will be taking our level 20 Wednesday night group in to play with the batlings.

We only fought one named Kobold. It was yellow at 21 and had a moderate amount of hit points.

I did not see a single "trivial encounter" message, perhaps because I ignored the greenies unless they attacked me or healed the mob we were fighting. I did see a number of dr00ds complaining about seeing that message.

I intend to take my lizard necro here and see what it is like for lower levels. There appears to be a very nice experience multiplier in here. In addition melee types can be bound right outside the zone entrance. At lower levels where the main cost of death is the run back to the corpse, well, this is a HUGE advantage.

I think that this is a good addition to Odus, and is a nice way to take some newbie pressure off of Kelethin and Crushbone. Those zones are over crowded, even on the mature servers.

Guess most of you don't read patch messages. The "This encounter was too trivial" message does indeed mean that the mob was too low level for you...you'll get it if nobody in the group would get exp from the kill (whether everyone is too high level or the level gap is too extreme), and all special drops on the mob will change to a note that says "Naughty naughty, go camp something useful. This is a newbie zone." (Well, something like that anyway.) Mobs do tend to pop quickly, about 10 or 15 minute spawn I'd guess, so it will keep any legitimate groups on their toes to keep areas split and cleared. Very good training for future places like bugs in sol-b or just about anything undead-side of guk.
Overall, though, I like the new zone and the new loot rules. I also disagree with Bonarges, I believe that VI is making noteworthy progress with this, both by giving Erudite newbies a reason to stick around past turning in their training note and by looking for solutions for item campers.
Why did they add a low/mid dungeon? Dunno, but this area definately needed it; I think Odus was the only starting area in the game that lacked an immediate place to go for anyone leaving the newbie zones. Now they no longer have to go to Blackburrow to compete with scores of half elves, humans and barbarians.
As far as the loot goes, I killed a few things (some of it was just for giggles, some of it mobbed my friend I was helping) and saw typical newbie loot (I think biggest pot I got was 5 gold and change, off a large kobold), a small amount of weapons, and a piece or three of armor. None of the items were spectacular, considering what I have now or could get, but for a true newbie I'm sure any of it would be nice. Did not get a chance to take down any named mobs, so no idea about what sort of "special" loot there might be.

Final thoughts. IMO, this was a very good add to the area. I hope VI follows up sometime soon with some more stuff aimed to take Erudites (and everyone else who comes to the no-mans-land out there) from low 20s to high 30s or low 40s; it would be an ideal place for a Splitpaw-for-kobolds.
