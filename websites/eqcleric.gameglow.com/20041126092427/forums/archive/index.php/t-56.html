When you lose xp in level 46, it looks more because of the way you see your xp bar move. At level 46, you have just completed the bump in the curve, and your xp to next level is much less than it was at level 45. Therefore, your xp 'bar' at 46 is 'smaller' than it was at 45. However, even though your xp bars represent different lengths at 45 and 46, when you die the xp you lose will stay similar.. so.. when you lose X amount of xp in 46, it will look like a bigger chunk of the xp bar than when you lose X amount of xp in 45.

It is only the visual representation of xp that is confusing you. You aren't losing more xp when you die, your xp bar is representing a different amount of xp needed for that level. Thus death xp loss looks like a bigger chunk of the bar at 46 vs. 45.

Kind of hard to explain but I hope this helps.
