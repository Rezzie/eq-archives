For me, I like to play a little bit more of a "Middle of the Road" type cleric in terms of gem/jewelry distribution.

For example:

If I'm in Seb and the group is just forming, I inform them all fairly early, that I only want the normal gems. All jewelry and special gems are theirs. Also, after awhile, I generally will start to let them keep the normal gems to help them make some cash as well.

Sure, I generally make a crapload of money if I get in Bugs or Jail group, but I also don't want to be labled as a loot-whore either.

So I figured that I end up making (over a 5-6 hour time span) anywhere from 3-4k plat in Seb. I'm sure everyone else in my group (if all of them stay with me the same amount of time) around 2-3k plat themselves. So it's probably close to 40/60 split in my favor.

For those here that might say they'll over cover a clerics current expenses and then start randoming/alpha/whatnot the rest of the gem/jewelry to keep things "fair" for everyone.....remember this the next time you do that: If you ask for a Symbol or Aego and your cleric says "Sorry, I can't do that. I just spent the last 2 weeks in Velks and I'm broke, everyone wanted the paws for faction and I didn't get enough scalps to cover my expenses..." That'll help give you an idea of what can happen (and is fairly common) with either an un-guilded, or small guilded, cleric.

While I'm in a guild that keeps several guild banks for Peri funs, not everyone has that luxury. :P
