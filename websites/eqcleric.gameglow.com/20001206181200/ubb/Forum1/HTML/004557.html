Each Fine Steel Plate recepie requires a leather padding:

Low Quality Pelt + Silk Thread = Leather Padding

The silk thread is made by combining 2 Spiderling Silks in a sewing kit.

MQ and HQ Pelts can be "downgraded" by using a skinning knife + the pelt in a sewing kit. If you use a HQ pelt and knife, you receive a MQ pelt and the knife back. Repeat the process and you get a LQ pelt and the knife back. Any pelt with HQ, MQ, or LQ can be used in the process. Puma skins, Black Wolf skins and the like, cannot be used.

The process of making enough leather paddings to produce enough FS armor to skill up is very time consuming. Made a lot of FS so far and I am at 177 skill in smithing. 175 is the max you can get making banded.

Hope this explains it some.

Cool, something I can comment on..

Ok, let's pretend that you didn't plan
ahead and have anything prepared (cept
let's say you already have a smithing
hammer).

So, step one, get a leather backing.
As we have discussed, takes 2 Spiderlings
silks, a tailoring kit, and one LQ pelt/Skin.
[NOTE, one some servers due to the increased
demand due to the new FS, stacks of 20 of
these have been going for UP TO (if not more
than) 100PP each]. At least they only
weigh 0.1 (much nicer than the raw pelts).

Step 2. Get some water. Duh, a vendor will
do just fine.

Step 3. Have a smithing hammer. That's kinda
easy, no? Just buy it in one of the several
cities that have them. Felwithe, Kaladim,
High Pass Keep, Qeynos, Halas (I think), some
evil cities. 12PP at best (this is NOT
consumed unless you screw up..I have blown up
three already..sigh..also weighs 18! stone)

Step 4. Need a mold. Much harder to hunt
down, expensive. Best possible price for
a breatplate mold is 21PP. They go down
to 7 GP (Collar) but all others are at
least 3PP, about half above 10PP.

Step 5. The metal itself. This part really
blows. There are three locations. As a
gating class, I prefer HH. I bind myself
at the Forge in Kelethin. I get to HH by
paying 10-20PP a port (assuming it's day and
I can get through Kith quickly). Anyway, at
HH you can buy BLOCKS of MQ ore. Blocks
are good, cause one block and one water,
plus the smithing hammer, makes one folded
MQ sheet. Note that you can easily fail at
this stage (about 5%). These blocks cost
20.5PP at best possible prices. They also weigh about 45Stone each (IIRC).

If you cannot use HH, you are stuck with steamfont if you are a good race. You can only get Large Bricks here, which you need to combine three of with one water to make the block mentioned above (and yes, you can fail here too). These are 15Stone each.

And heaven forbid you are an evil race. Off to the Rathe Mountains with you for your ore.. Ow.

Now, take your hammer, backing, water, mold and between 1-3 sheets (3 for Cloak, BPlate and Greaves, 1 For Bracer, Visor and Collar, 2 for the rest) of the Folded MQ metal, get all the strenght and Wis/Int buffs you can, do whatever dance you do and combine!

If it works, good, if not...up to 85PP down
the drain (Greaves/BPlate).

And forget about getting stat increases. I am finally up to 182 after way over 200 pieces. some fun..;-)

And what do you get? Armor that is pretty
much always 2 AC better than Bronze, but
weighs quite a bit less. And looks
amazingly sharp (High Elves rock, Dwarves
are ok).

Now, all that being said. 140 for a collar is excessive. There is no realistic way to charge by the AC. On Druzzil Ro I charge
the following (YMMV):

Bracer, Visor, Collar: 75PP
Boots, Vambraces, Helm, Pauldron, Girdle: 150PP
Gauntlets: 175PP
BPlate, Greaves and Cloak: 275PP
[Cloaks fail...a lot]

Full set of small/med: 1750
Full visible (1 bracer): 1250PP
+100 PP for large

Any Dye work: 100PP per item
(75PP in quantities, always 100PP
for charcoal).

Don't even ASK how much of a hassle
Dye work is..;-) It's worse.

Hope this helps give an insight into
the FS armor out there!
