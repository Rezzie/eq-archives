Achillean-

The problem with griping about the design of the game is that the chances of you gettting the one change that you feel will make the game significantly better for you is almost certainly never going to happen (no matter what that change is). There are so many factors that go into the game that to single one tiny segment out and hope for it to change is silly... While Verant might make some significant changes to various aspects of the game, the changes they make will never be of the magnitude, or in the areas, that all players would like them to be.

Don't worry about "losing" an investment of time in the game. If you're thinking that way, then your real problem is that playing the game at all is a waste of your time. You could delete all your characters and start from scratch, and not have really lost anything: you still have made some friends, you can still start a new character, and actually BENEFIT from the fact that you know some of the tricks, you know the zones, you know some strategies to use as well as avoid.
That's why it's great that you can have 8 characters on a server... and with what, 15 or 20 servers now, that means you could conceivably play every single race/class combo, and each one would be a little bit different, maybe even enough to make it worth playing. Your wood elf druid is going to have a much different "life" than a halfling druid, etc. I have yet to play a pure caster class OR a pure melee class, and I have 4 active characters and a few that were just "dabbling" in something. That fact alone means that I can try two more styles of playing that I haven't even begun to explore yet, in almost 5 months of playing the game.

It's easy to get discouraged. In my case, getting through level 15 was about the most painful thing I've been through in the game. And it doesn't get any easier from here. But I will consider Gorgrim to be my "main" character for a long time, even of some of my other characters pass him up in levels, because it was the first character that I really nurtured into something. I have a druid character on another server that I am simply power-leveling, trying to catch up to some friends that I found out had started playing a couple weeks ago. He'll probably zip past 16 in just a couple weeks, because I learned enough from my cleric about how to build a character from the ground up. I also have an Ogre Shaman on yet another server that I started one night when I got frustrated with Gorgrim's inability to find a group and get that last bubble in 15. I intend to make that character a TRUE roleplaying character... something I started with Gorgrim, and just couldn't pull off. I can't seem to imagine a dwarf cleric in my head, but my ogre shaman is very clear, how he acts and speaks. The roleplaying is the most fun thing about that character. In fact, in the roughly 4 hours I put into that character the night I created it, I spent about 2.5 hours just looking for the Shaman guild in Oggok, and it was hysterically FUN because of the way I roleplayed it, and I wished I could roleplay Gorgrim that way.

So what I'm trying to get at is, if you're unhappy with cleric, try something else for a while, and either you will find something you really do like better, or you will get some new perspectives, and the vacation from your cleric will help you to play it again later... some of that frustration will be forgotten.

Just out of curiosity, are you one of the kinds of people who, for whatever reason, has difficulty finding a regular group? I know that since my schedule prevents me from playing at a regular time (like every night between certain hours), I tend to play at very irregular times, and even though I've made friends, they usually out-level me rather quickly, and it makes staying with a regular group rather rough. I think people that play in fairly static groups, especially people that know each other offline, and can easily coordinate their playing time, are less prone to this kind of disilliusionment with their characters, especially if their character is as group-oriented and group-dependent as the cleric. Some of the more solo-oriented classes might be more appropriate for people that aren't part of a good group or guild.

Personally, I've had a couple of guild experiences that left me with a sour taste in my mouth as far as guilds go. The ones I've been in have largely been run by very self-serving officers that only use their underlings for personal gain. I had a good friend (offline) playing a mid-high level enchantress in a guild, and she was abused so badly that I quit along with her, even though I had been treated pretty well. She had quested, on her own (since no one from the guild would help her) for the Staff of Observer, and the moment she had it, her Guild Master showed up and demanded that she hand it over, because he "needed it." That was the first in a long line of injustices done to her, but was more than enough for me to realize that guilds aren't as nice as they're made out to be, sometimes.

In Lower Guk, I find that I am usually the only Cleric in the group. The role I take depends on what the tanks pull into battle.

If it's a solo undead froggy, then I start with a Banish Undead. At 585, that's a significant jumpstart for the party. After that, I'll close in and melee just for the sheer fun of it and to laugh at my pathetic results. 1, miss, miss, miss, miss, 3, miss, 1, miss, 24 (big score!), 1, 14, miss, miss, miss.

If it's more than one, then I usually do an ASSIST on the tank, cast Banish once, and close into melee. I keep an eye on everyone's HP as well as my mana. As soon as someone needs a heal, I pull back, heal, immediately sit and med. When the first monster goes down, what I do after that depends on how well the first round went, how everyone's HP stands, and how much mana I have.

If we end up with a big mob, I'll never cast Banish. Instead, I close in to melee and let my pathetic swings add whatever they can. I melee, and heal until I'm a 50% mana, then I pull back and med and heal. If necessary, and mana and HP permit, and the group is really getting banged on, I'll cast a small spell to pull one of the monsters from the party and hold him so the group doesn't have him whacking away at them.

It all depends on the situation and mix down there. But I've gone through about 5 levels and have not had any deaths on my watch. Except for the time the Monk chased a runner, and stumbled into 3 gargoyles, an evil eye, 2 bloodthirsties, and 2 frogluk casters! Then we knew it was all over...I gated to the zone, zoned, and came back to do the res's.
