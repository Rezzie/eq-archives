My primary is a druid, but play a cleric alt, and come here to learn.

I agree on communication; when I'm first grouped with a cleric, I usually send a tell and ask at what point my panic heal won't drive them nuts. I get answers from 1 to 2 bulbs. That way I can toss my fastish greater or superior heal in and maybe give breathing space if there's lag or a fizzle.

The exceptions: clerics, if my group's cleric is getting hit, I toss a heal FAST. I want my clerics alive since they're way more important in a group than I am. I'll also toss int casters a heal if they get hit, especially if the mob is still on them, so they don't go down too fast if I can help it.

I found it a bit hard to learn to group effectively with clerics, since I'd solod or been the primary healer so often; I know my healing is inefficient, and would never let a tank my level get below half, because my best heal usually doesn't heal more than a bubble on a tank my own level. So being patient while a much more efficient cleric waits for his spells to be necessary was hard at first.

One hard part about playing a druid as I get higher level (only 51 now) is really coming face to face with the reality that I'm not the best at anything. My attitude has become, ok, I'm not the best, but I can fill in and help my group flexibly. To be honest, though, for folks who are used to soloing effectively, it can be hard to accept that. It seems like grouping well as a druid demands that one use almost the opposite strategies from soloing. I can't remember the last time I felt the need to dot in a group, for example.

I love reading this board, by the way; you folks have made me a much better druid because I've learned a little about how cleric spells work and can adapt to working with a cleric more easily.

My whole outlook is if we repeatedly waste mana by overhealing (enchanter being the exception), what happens when that add gets factored in and suddenly someone is dead because the healers are oom? When lives are at stake (or more accurately, when someone's time they spent gaining that experience is wasted) isn't it important to use mana wisely? I'm not saying druids/shams shouldn't heal, I am saying that they should yield heals to clerics as a general practice and reserve their heals for those panic moments when the cleric is yelling LOM please take over or when someone is sinking to close to 1 bulb of life (tank) or half life and being beat on (caster).

First let me make clear that my main is a 57 cleric, but I have a 32 Shammy, and a 22 Druid I think a lot of the overlapping heal trouble comes from younger times (levels 20ish-30ish), when we all have the same heal spells. Many groups at those levels prefer druids/shaman as main healers because they can SoW as well. "Backup" healers get used to being "main" healers, and it can be very hard to make the switch to back-up. I think communication in both group and tells is important. I also sent a tell saying that if their "Healing so-and-so" message came up before mine I would interrupt, but if mine came up first, I expected them to interrupt.

I can definitely see Gabri's point. My cleric is my first character and my main. I have always striven to make my healing as efficient as possible, because a lot of times the pace of the group is determined by the cleric's mana more than other caster mana. Nothing against pure meleers, but they tend to get ants in their pants during down time. This is why I can see wasted mana bugging clerics. And it is wasted, because the group is not waiting on the druid, they are waiting on the cleric. If I cast a complete heal that I didn't need to, yes, everyone is still alive, but it increases group down time.

I have made a druid alt and a warrior alt, and am now seeing their points of view too. As a druid, if I am finishing a fight with 70 mana and the cleric is 40m all the time, maybe I need to use more offensive spells so the fight ends quicker.

Playing my warrior has also helped me to learn how to communicate in a group better as a cleric. Especially at the lower levels, healers can be very enigmatic (me included). :P "I need to med," and then nothing for a while. My warrior has to guess when to pull, or keep asking mana levels, which as a cleric bugs me to no end, and puts the pressure on. After a fight now I give the thumbs up or down, and keep my group updated on when to pull again.

I guess I am lucky, because I have had no complaints with any secondary healer so far that I have grouped with. I will always announce my heals, and also my mana levels. When I say 10 or 15m, that's when the sec. healer will jump in to heal while I med for that one more complete heal that will enable us to end the fight.

I agree with previous posts that communication is the key, but I think that maybe some extra /tells are the way to go when you have a secondary healer who perhaps just doesn't have a clue yet.
