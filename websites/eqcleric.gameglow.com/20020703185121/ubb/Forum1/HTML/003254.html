Ummm I would say not a bard. It's true, a bard/cleric duo is an absolutely dynamite combination; they complement each other extremely well. But if you are going to be playing 2 characters simultaneously, you want each of them to require somewhat less than your full attention. Playing a bard to its potential requires an insane amount of RL finger-wiggling. You can't just turn a song on and swing. The more songs you can twist at once, the more effective your bard will be; the best ones twist 3 or even 4 at a time, oftentimes switching instruments for each pulse. The bard I play with most often is so tied up with his hotkeys that he can barely type 3-word messages to groupmates during combat, let alone control a whole separate character. It also requires even more split-second judgement calls than playing a cleric, if you can believe that. I think if you tried to play both at once, you'd either end up as a bad one-note bard, or go insane.

Oh, you also mentioned that the second computer is slower, and bards are probably the most hardware-dependent of all the classes. It's really hard to play a bard to its fullest on less than a state of the art machine. However, I've found that clerics are the class least dependent on hardware. Whatever class you choose for your secondary, you might prefer to have your cleric on the slower computer.

If you'd like to play both characters effectively at the same time, I'd go for a straight melee type - warrior, SK, pally, or ranger (can you say snare/fear kiting? ) Or maybe a monk or rogue, though playing those classes well requires a little more attention and finesse. If your intent is only to PL the second character though, you could go with almost anything. I've considered making a druid myself; after I hit 29 I'd be a mobile ressing service.

I would think it is based upon your gameplay.... A warrior would be nice if you are fight alot. They are obviously the best tanks and if you can lull the mobs before pulling you probably can get great pulls.

If you are after rare MOBs you need tracking ability (Ranger,Druid.) Druids give a lot of spells we lack -- SOW, teleport, tracking, evac, damage shields. They are terrible tanks, and are not made to get pounded on.

On the other hand Rangers get SOW much later, but are more a complement to a cleric in my opinion. Ranger have good offensive skill, and so-so defensive skills which means they get beaten up more. A skillful ranger can pull control mobs and healer is needed to offset his poorer defensive skills (compared to a warrior) Plus having the best tracking skills allows the ranger to find the ellusive rare spawns. With alchemy components being lowered (about 8pp, not accounting failures)you can see SOW potions being sold for 10pp to 15 pp. Just because I might want tracking ability, I would lean more towards a ranger over an warrior.
