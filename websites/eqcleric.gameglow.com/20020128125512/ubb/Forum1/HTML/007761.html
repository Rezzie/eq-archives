We dont have mandatory raids...

However, we run a DKP system where loot is based on raid attendance.

Additionally we tend to publicize the important raids a few days ahead of time...such as we are going to focus on killing mob x 0 he is due in such and such time frame.

It solves the issue rather well of having people show up to help the guild IMO, though it is a lot of maintainance on setting item values, and raid values, and keeping them balanced.

It takes a long time to build up the people/skills/equipment to do the higher end raids...so start with things that are challenges, and small to moderate upgrades to the guild...focusing on getting to epic-quality weaponry, or kunark/pog/thurg armor quality for the majority of the guild are big steps for instance...Set goals for timeframes, and plan it out...and publicize the events ahead of time...success builds upon itself.

Or you can take the zerg approach, and just throw tons of people at it, but I tend to find that much less satisfying

I'd personally hate to be obliged to attend every raid...

I am currently in a decent sized guild with fair fire power. The large majority of the guild sits right around 55 and has about 25 to 30 people in this range with about 60 people total.

Now our guild is more of a nothing is ever mandatory and you are never required to do anything, guild. But we find that generally we get the people we need for a raid since people want to go and our guild is exceptionally social.

This is not what I would call the perfect situation if you are looking to be able to tackle the largest goals and grab some of the nicest loot, but we do decently enough and are quite capable.

In my experience I have found that near dictatorships probably produce the best raid results, and mandatory attendance helps to gather the needed people, along with getting those people experience working together. The down side is that your EQ life is being run by whomever. It is definately not you.

Then there is the guild like mine where it can be difficult getting the required people all the time but we all love to group and do what we can together. This makes it hareder to accomplish certain things on any definate schedule and we often have to put certain mobs off for a time. While we can not always be guaranteed of getting a mob we need at a specific time we also can do as we like, be it leveling an alt, working on tradeskills, or whatever.

It really seems to be a trade off. Which do you prefer, freedom or loot. And I am sure that guilds falling inbetween are a mixture.
