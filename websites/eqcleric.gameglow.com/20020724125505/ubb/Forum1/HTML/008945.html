Hehe, what you think they will do. I can almost run right down that list and check most of it off as stuff I'm positive Verant won't do.

The heal-over-time on test was Verant tipping their hand big time on what they're going to do. Heal-over-time's are not effected by crit or percentage bonus. So I feel strongly that it will be Healing Gift or Healing Adept they add for clerics. Since Healing Gift is more visible and falls in line with warriors and wizards staying ahead crit-wise, it makes sense. Any real changes to healing would cause more work for them -- crit heals would be more about perception. Also, bear in mind that critical heals have no real effect on Complete Heal, a prerequisite on any change Verant makes to clerics.

I could also see Verant making Levitation and/or Everlasting Breath (the new, hard-to-find Luclin versions) cleric-usable, since damn near every caster class can use these already.

I think Verant would consider us round at that point.

etc etc

I can use an OT hammer to get to kunark (courier potion in bank in case barber selo gets me), faithstone to rivervale, thurg potion to velious, run to a spire for luclin if needed, lev, run faster than sow with a horse, invis, locate or summon a corpse all without help. Travelling is really not a hassle for clerics anymore. Faithstone alone makes travelling worlds easier. It wouldn't disappoint me to get some utility spells but I don't think it's needed nor does the cleric class suffer without.

imho the part that could use rounding out (ie, not improving what we already do well) is our offensive ability. There should be a reason for undead to fear us more than live stuff (no point to load exile undead if you've got judgement), and our melee should do more than go through the motions. But they've done a lot for our offensive ability with luclin+ (scm, mc, scf, more ft, baotr, judgement doing 925 base, improved damage, mana preservation, spell haste, double-pumping nukes, etc).

Make us able to kill low greens with melee (and putting a modest dent in blue stuff), make undead fear us more than live stuff, and I think the class would be perfectly rounded out. It'd fit with the role of the plate-wearing crusader and enemy of the undead. We'd have a greater impact in small groups where we currently have none, there'd be no impact at raid level (where we're already too powerful), and there'd be room to personalize playing styles from the tired old sit/heal/sit without becoming a druid/shaman/paladin and watering down the classes.
