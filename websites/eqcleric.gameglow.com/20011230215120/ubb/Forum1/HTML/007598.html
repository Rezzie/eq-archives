hhmmm

At 39 I was leveling with an enc fast. BUT to share what I knew then and what I have learned since then.

Enchanterless crowd control can be best done at those levels with Root.

Keep Stun and Root mem'd at all times. (I like the lowest level ones as they are fastest casting and less resisted.)

Look for groupmates like shaman or bards when enchanters are scarce. 40ish should be the time that shaman are talented at slowing to gain agro, moving away from the group, sitting to pull the mob to them, and rooting.

Any class that can cast root can crowd control to a degree. Paladins are talented at this when they stun to gain agro, then move and root. Rangers are ok at this but usually to crunchy to be mana effective for the cleric.

At 39 with your new CH, crowd control will be falling to others more and more. Though if you keep stun and root mem'd you should be able to switch on the fly and return to healing quickly.

I have learned to very quickly hit my F# key and then my /assist hotkey and stun, root, /assist (orF#) and heal.

As you level up that skill will be more ane more usefull when you do multi group romps. Assisting the mob to get the target and landing a quick heal then assisting again and again to keep bouncing heals to those in need, who may not be in your group.

You should be able to safely keep up 4 heals, 1 stun, 1 root and 2 divines mem'd to handle most group situations. When buffing needs to happen unmem the lowest heal to swap in buffs quickly.

Good luck!

I do a lot of duo'ing with monks and warriors. I've found that the rewards are much higher but the challenge is much greater.

Root and stun are necessary tools for this to work right.

If you are a high elven cleric of Tunare, get yourself the warden symbol -- that mana free root makes CC much easier!

Here's the plan - you go into a dungeon with a competent puller/meleer. An SK or monk is best. You find a camp spot and set up. Clear the room pops and start a timer. Use a stopwatch with multiple counters to set a timer for EVERY SINGLE ROOM POP. Learn the times of hte spawn. It is vital that you know this as you need to plan your mana use accordingly.

Have your puller go pull singles and doubles. Use stun on the pull in to get aggro from the extras and to stop them. Let hte puller keep coming with the mob that is still moving and start casting root to park the mob that you've stunned.

Now, check your timers and mana. If you are going to have room pops consider your mana pool - you want to only be dealing with at most 2 root parks at a time (I've lived through having 5 before - but it wasn't pretty and we where damn lucky!).

Basically, I allocate half my mana to healing and half to crowd control, damage dealing. The key is to make sure that you have the mana for what you need to be doing. This means that you have to make calls on nuking the crap out of something so that you can deal with parking the extra mobs that may be popping in the room. Thus leaving you with little mana for heals.

Make sure that your partner is well aware of mana and mob situations. If you have a monk make sure htat you communicate on their mend skill - when it's up, when it's used, how much he can do, etc.

Be prepared to root the mob he's on if he needs to FD a bit to gain time to mend if you are too low to on mana to heal.

Doing CC/healer with a partner is GREAT fun.

If you are not in a dungeon, or you don't have a camp with regular spawns, you just need to do 'best judgement.' Try to think of your mana in two piles: one for cc and one for healing. You borrow from one to use the other at a very high interest rate so try to avoid it!
