From studies done, mor on non uber encounters seems to produce very little actual benefit, mok is even worse and practically produces zero.

Firstly lets look at what we want from each spell.

Mok is a spell to help people regenerate hps. In a similar way to any regeneration spell. It has similar disadvantages to regen spells, as in it will heal regardless of whether or not they need healing.

It however does not heal everyone in the party, only melee members, and it can also be resisted. It also has to be cast on every mob, which takes up valuable time.

So lets look at the figures compared to the two popular group regen spells in the end game. It is slightly lower level but I think relatively comparable. Note that for clerics and druids, both are provided on velious bp so comparison purposes ill use the cost in mana of the spell.

Regrowth of the grove heals 15hps a tick for 15 mins for a total of 2250 hps. It applies this to all party members for a total of 13500 hps healed every quarter of an hour. It does this for 600 mana for a 22.5:1 hp to mana ratio.

Mark of Karn costs 125 mana so on that ratio, it would heal a total of 2812.5 hps.

Now I will confess to not knowing the exact break down of how mark of karn heals, whether or not the delay of the weapon is taken unslowed or slowed, but lets for the sake of arguement assign a reasonable figure of 6 hps healed ever 2 seconds, per main hand hit.

With this taken into account, using 2 mainhand swings per round (above average), it would heal 360 hps per minute per attacker. Given the total duration of mok is 3.5 mins, you would have to have 2.2 tanks attacking the mob for its entire duration in order for it to rival the healing power of regrowth of the grove.

If this was a situation (and forgetting damage distribution - this affects regen spells just as much), it would be useful to cast it, since frankly put 22:1 healing ratio, even incredibly slowly is absolutely amazing.

This is not the situation however. As you get higher the damage capabilities of players goes up, and whilst hps of mobs go up, they don't to this extent.

Warrior lizards for example in CT have around 15k hps. With just one high quality warrior only attacking, its quiet possible that it would die within 3.5 minutes. And warriors have much more hps than the average critter. Raptors for example last less than a minute, absolutely destroying the spell.

And this is a zone where everything is mean and difficult.

Translate it into a zone where its much easier and mobs easily last less than a minute and it becomes practically worthless, other than for a secondary ability to dispell damage shields, something that the spell dispell can do much better.

In order for it to be effective, either the healing rate of the spell has to raise considerable, scaling to the damage the critter is recieving, not the delay of the weapons, or it becomes a buff cast upon players. If it was cast on the player and not on the mob, efficency would be much less of an issue, resists wouldn't be a problem, and it would take less of the clerics time.

Lets look at mor. I'll be the first to say that mor is not nearly as bad as mok in usefulness, in solo tanking situations it is very useful, but in groups it is practically worthless. Calculation of damage put it that in order for it to stack against judgement, the critter must hit someone 21.5 times.

Firstly, resist rates of mor are relatively high. Judgement rarely ever gets resisted, its a shock if it happens, i am used to seeing it always hit for 925 (unfocused, and lets not even get into focus items affecting effecency). Secondly agro. In order for mor to be efficent, I have to throw it early, otherwise it makes it even less useful. This either causes agro, and potencial healing, or the inability to sit for a few ticks which reduces efficency even more. Judgement however can be cast anytime after the tank has established agro and you can sit immediately after.

The mob also has to hit the tank. Again the life span of said mob is in question, the quicker it is killed, the less it will hit the tank. Combine slow, quick kill rate and a good tank who rarely gets hit and it has been shown even in small 3 man groups, not to be efficent.

What to do?

Personelly, I think a vast majority of the clerics lack of damage adding capability can be solved with fixing this single simple spell. Rather than fix it so that it was in line with our nukes, it should be made much more powerful (but not too powerful), so that when in a group where healing is less useful, it is the spell to cast.

What would be good, is rather than giving a reverse damage shield, is if it caused a damage amplify on the said mob.

If it amplified damage by a mere 10%, lets look at some numbers. On a mob with 10k hps it would have the effect of hitting for 910 damage. For 115 mana, thats insanely efficent.

On a lower level mob with say 5k hps, it would do around 450 ish, less useful but still efficent. So when you got the spell it would be useful, and it would grow as you gained levels and attacked harder things.

In order to curb the usefulness of the spell, it shouldn't be able to land on any mob with more than 32k hps.

Also it is not like a dot, it can only be cast once, and then you have to use other spells to get the benifit, so you would only get the 9:1 ratio once per mob, limiting its exploitability.

I'm trying to think about it, and see how unbalanced this would be, and I think it would really help up the damage groups can do with a cleric, without actually improving clerics damage dealing capabilities substancially.

What do people think?
