Gello
03-11-2001, 10:24 AM
I'm a firm disbeliever in the "1100" or "1150" AC softcap. And I think anyone who looks at this analytically will agree.

The AC number we see is a composite of damage avoidance and damage absorbtion (not taking damage (ie, armor ac) vs taking less damage (ie, agi)). In early beta AC was represented by these two numbers. Verant combined the two numbers to the one we strive for today. So we have two variables making up our AC.

In order for there to be a soft cap of a composite of variables, there has to be a stacking order to cap. ie, all avoidance counts and the cap cuts off absorbtion, or all absorbtion counts, and the cap cuts off avoidance. It's possible that they've implemented it this way, but it's 1) clumsy and 2) prone to making either avoidance or absorbtion absolete.

The simpler, more elegant method for putting a soft cap on AC would be to soft cap avoidance and absorbtion separately. This is the cap I think folks are experiencing. And since we really can't tell now that the numbers are combined, there will never be a magic AC number where the diminishing returns comes to a stop.

Unfortunately agility past 75 doesn't add a whole lot of ac, so it'd be tough to experiment on this theory. It's quite possible damage avoidance has a soft cap, but damage absobtion does not. Maybe several tests with huge samples will unravel it some day, but I just log on to play. http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif

I believe that the effect of AC against someone hitting is basically an arctan curve. For example, an ac of 0-500 might be just about equal, and the increase in AC will help only marginally, and at 500 the effect of each ac will increase a lot more up to maybe 700 and then the increases will taper off again.

Against another opponent who is higher level and has better skills, the values could be 0-700 for marginal effect, 700-900 for large difference, and 900+ doesn't make that big a difference.

This would mean that although there is no strict soft cap, the gain in effect decreases against lower mobs, at least, but against red mobs at 60 it might be hard to even reach the part of the curve where AC starts to get a lot of effect (at least for clerics).

AC or HP...
HP can be affected by many mitigating factors: melee, spell dmg, environmental dmg.
