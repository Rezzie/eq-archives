quote:

How? Keep in mind that the low level rezzes restore a very small amount of experience with these changes. I think the res changes are awsome. I remember how happy I was when I hit 24 and got revive. It saved so much time being able to teleport someone back to Cazic Thule that was bound in Freeport.

The nuke changes are a nice change.

However, on the subject of balance, I feel Divine Light needs to be looked at for the following reasons (not considering CH).

1) We nuke better than we heal (using raw spdat numbers) Reckoning 670/250 = 2.68 and Divine Light 910/350 = 2.6 (not counting clerical bonus over spdat)

2) We don't heal fast enough. Divine Light 955/6.5 = 146 hps (counting the 2 second recast on all spells in cast time). This means we CANNOT keep someone alive using our fast casting heal spell against mobs that do 146 dps or more. Even entrance mobs in the new Cazic Thule do more damage per second. Our only other option is CH, and for the most part, pure casters cannot live 10+ seconds taking 146+ dps.

3) Divine Ressurection timer, 4,320 minutes. Once every 3 REAL LIFE days is outrageous. I know you want to prevent people from zergling mobs. Once every 2-3 game days is acceptable.

4) Heal Agro. Works fine for single targets, such as golems in sleepers, or other main tank mobs. However with the recent advent of Ring of Fire type encouters, clerics really eat it hard. AE Taunt helps, but with the long refresh times, and waves spawning every minute... its not practical to AE taunt every wave, where clerics are often required to land at least 1, often 2-3 ch's per wave. Clerics are invariably the first ones to die, often to be ressed back into combat, med a heal, ch, die. Is there a way to possibly reduce the Healing Agro on an undamaged mob?

5) Mob summons on RoF encounters. One of the popular tactics in RoF encounters is offtanking. Warriors 1-5 all tank off mobs, while guild kills the mobs 1 by 1. This often means you have 6+ damaged mobs. Now I CH one of these of tanking Warriors, and instantly get ping pong summoned between 3-4 mobs who each take nice chunk out of my hide and the summoning prevents me from casting DA.

6) Target AA skills. This are extremely buggy, I often have to hit Bestow Divine Aura 10-15 times before I get it to actually work. Even when I'm casting it on MYSELF, I get "Target not close enough" x 10.

Thats all the cleric related issues I can think off at the moment.

As a mid-level cleric I have to say that I will be enoying these changes. I agree that if these work out it would be a good idea to consider a similar change in cast/recast timing for the undead nukes.

I think I like the increase in partial rezzes, it takes a very long time to get from 39 to 49. I also like seeing the paladins getting their rez line so much earlier. I am not sure if a no-experience rez at 22 is too early for them or not, I think that it is not too early.

I agree with the thought that these changes are being made to encourage lower and midlevel players to get out of the outdoors grind and into dungeons, out of convenient zones and into distant zones. These are laudable goals.

The other big advantage to the low-level succor is that it means you can more easily have a group break up and reform. If, for example, two groups in Kaesora both have people leaving and they want to reform into a single group, now they can both succor to the zone, folke leave, folks regroup, and you clear back down to where you had been hunting. That will also make things much easier, especially in places like the Kunark dungeons where your choices had been to either fight out or take a long walk. Low level succor just gave me an extra 20 minutes of hunting time before bedtime.

Joining the other folks who have been asking for extensions to cleric spells - it would be great to have an improved atone in the mid 50s that would be harder for the critters to resist but that would not affect anything above level 49. This would not trivialize the aggro management game in high-end play, but it would make life a lot easier for the travelling cleric.
