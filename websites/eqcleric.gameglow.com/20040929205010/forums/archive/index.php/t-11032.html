It’s generous to let the smaller guild attempt a new mob. However, you have to ask yourself, at what cost? You have a responsibility to your guild members that are there as well as keeping the bigger picture in mind. I don’t envy your position.

Well, having been in this boat many times before both as a guild member, former guild leader, and officer at one time or another, I think it all depends.

If I run into this situation, I tend to ask a couple of questions...

1) Is this mob generally considered FCFS or FFA? There is a very significant difference between the two. FFA tends to mean whoever can engage first ala Lodizal. FCFS tends to imply whoever has a capable raid force in place at the mob (IE, sufficient minimal numbers to have a chance at the mob).

2) Who is the guild in question? Do we have good relations with this guild? Have they extended us similar courtesies in the past? Or have they been equally as happy to run over us/camp us/train us in order to get to a mob?

3) Is this a guild who directly competes with us? Guilds that are on or near your level don't need a "leg up" so to speak, they fend for themselves just fine thank you. Those that are a tier below, it's nice to give them a chance to learn and take a spin when the opportunity presents itself.

4) Are there other reasonable target available? It's easy to be generous when every mob is up, it's when you haven't seen a mob in 3 or 4 days or possibly weeks that things get dicey. I don't know about your servers, but on our server most everything is dead within 48 hours of patch. What that means is that a guild needs to be competitive if they want any mobs at all. It's nice to be generous, however, the end result of being too generous can be impeding the progress of your guild.

So, in answer to your question...

A) It depends.

B) It depends.

C) We don't team up on raids unless it's something like Plane of Fear where we want CT and the other guild just wants armor drops.

D) See C.

As for sitting behind another guild while they make an attempt and going after the mob afterwards, we rarely do that though we have done it to a few select guilds. Usually, this is to a directly competitive guild who's done this or something similar to us first.

IE, our primary philosophy tends to be, do unto others as you would have them do unto you. However, if they do something else, return the favor in full.

I hope that helps.
