I wondered a bit about having strenght add a positive score in this equipment list without taking into account the weight of the item.

For example: Platinum Armbands (STR3/WIS3/WEIGHT0.4) score 36 vs. Vambraces of the Fervent (AC11/DEX5/FIRERES5/WEIGHT4.9) score 46.

If you take into account the weight as well, say -3 score per weight unit (STR adds 3 per unit) then the Platinum Armbands score 35 and Vambraces of the Fervent score 32.

Now, I am not saying that I know what the correct scoring for weight should be, but it seems as STR is very important to some of us, scoring for STR bonus without taking into account weight is going to give slightly off results.

I am currently modifying my version of the spreadsheet to add the weight to every item, I cannot decide what the score for weight should be though.

How would you score the weight, considering that STR is a plus 3 per unit?

I had some of the same thoughts. As a high elf, all this heavy armor can be of concern to me.

But the original post didn't include weight, and I didn't want to go do the research for it.

But your point is well taken - if you consider that the only real reason for strength is to carry things (given that we will never do much melee damage unless you're Lerwin and get 5 procs of dawnfire in a fight) then some kind of weighting to take into account strength given vs item weight might be appropriate.

You can also make arguments for the usefulness of various effects and different scoring there. In the end the sheet is really a guideline, and not an absolute. If your wisdom is maxed, other things become more important. As you get higher level, resistances become more important.

For me, it sometimes helps me keep my eye on the ball as far as where I want my equipment to go.
