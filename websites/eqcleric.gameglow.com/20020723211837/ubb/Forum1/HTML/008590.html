I'm in the boat with Badany on this one, of course at level 51 all I have is Remedy. My strategy varies depending on the enchanter, how many mobs are pulled, and how rough a time the enchanter has on getting them mezzed.

When I see or know that 3+ mobs are coming into camp, there's no question, I drop CelHeal on the enchanter, then /assist stun and /assist root whatever the enchanter is trying to get locked down. Between the CelHeal pumping them with HP every tick and using SHeal or Remedy I find that I'm able to keep them standing at above 60% health. I find the Remedy first, then CelHeal feels too "reactive" and puts me at too big a risk of getting behind the curve. Most of the time the enchanter is quick enough that the CelHeal is all the healing they need to get stuff locked up.

Sure, I'm buffing the enchanter (including symbol), but there's just no way I'd CHeal a chanter during the fight. Heck, if it looks dicey, I'll often drop a CelHeal on the chanter right before starting my CHeal on the main tank. 10 seconds is just way too long in a fight (chanter's live about 5 seconds if they can't get it locked down or get off the aggro list).

I do the same things on raids (Kael arena, PoH) where my enchanter or shaman (I'm usually responsible for the slower, tasher, mezzer being one of the lower level clerics). Well I didn't read this whole thread.. but here my four half pennies.

What some people don't seem to realize is that clerics have to heal multiple characters. It's hard to CH the MT, the chanter, and still keep the shaman up that is attempting to slow. Plus anyone else who gains aggro.

In a grind situation there is almost no reason to use CH on anyone but the main tank. And even then you are probably only casting every 4 or 5 (or even more) mobs. Hell I've been in plenty of grind groups where the only person I had to heal was the MT. No one else took enough damage to warrant my attention when group regen was going to heal them up just fine. Of course those were groups with a good puller, a good enchanter, and everyone knew how to properly manage aggro.

On raids, I'm not to terribly concerned about the enchanter. I might have to throw out a DL every mob, but that is usually it unless the chanter is having an incredibly hard time tashing. What worries me on raids is when I'm responsible for healing the shaman on slows and they are below 60. At those times I don't really have the chance to complete heal anyone else when we are fighting mobs above 60.

As far as I see it, the arguement shouldn't be whether you CAN complete heal the chanter, but whether it is JUSTIFIABLE to complete healt he enchanter. People think that the ideal is to use complete heal on anyone with 3k+ hps because it is the most mana efficient, but most mana efficient does not always equal most efficient.

It's kind of like telling a wizard to only use his most efficient nuke, even if the mob has a high resistance to that nuke but not to other nukes with a lesser damage/mana ratio. Hell, supposedly a wizard's speciality is chain nuking so tell them to use one nuke regardless of resists is kind of stupid any way. Plus wizard has to deal with aggro management. The might not want to unload thier best nuke right away, because it might get them beat down.

There is a large number of variables to consider when healing a group. We all know this, we've dealt with it for a while. You get a feel for when to use what heals and on who. Many non-clerics don't realize this and think that we should just use the most efficient hp/mana spells. They don't take into consideration the cast time, damage distribution among the group, abilities of the mob, aggro management, etc.

Besides.. using Cheal on more people just increases the chances of a triage situation happening. Any cleric who has made into their 40s has probably experienced this. Here is the most common scenario I've had it in.

MT is tanking away, health bar steadily dropping. You are sitting there watching it, till it drops low enough for you to start casting complete heal. Well about 6 or 7 seconds later, something goes wrong. Maybe mezzes break, maybe adds come.. but the chanter starts getting beat on bad. What do you do?

Do you wait for the cheal to finish and hope the chanter survives long enough for you to remedy/DL?

Do you interrupt the Cheal, slap a HoT or remedy on the chanter and hope the tank is stays alive long enough for you to heal them back up some and start another complete heal?

Do you panic and just yell for an evac? hehe

Whatever choice you make, sometimes everyone lives and goes "Wow that was close, nice healing" Sometimes it doesn't work and someone dies, maybe even all of you. Or maybe you do just evac, and then everyone yells "Why the hell did you evac? Now we have to fight all the way back in!"

So in essence. As a message to all those who say how to heal the various classes.. The best way to stay alive is to let your cleric handle the situation the best they know how. If you have a good cleric, they will pull you through all reasonable stitations. If you have a bad cleric, then now ammount instruction is going to help.
