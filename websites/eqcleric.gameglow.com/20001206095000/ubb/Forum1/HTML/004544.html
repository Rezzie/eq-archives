Well you play a human caster so clearly you aren't too concerned with min-maxing stats.

My advice is this:

Any race you play will have advantages and disadvantages down the track, but they are all equally playable in the end, and you can have as much fun on any.

High elf and dwarven clerics are very common because of the good starting statistics. If you want to play them that shouldn't be a concern though. The fact that many choose them isn't a reason to discount playing them at all.

Dark elves have an advantage in the quests they can go for: some new quests like that innoruuk thingie-bob (ask a dark elf not a dwarf like me) make them more attractive than they used to be, and lets face it they look good If you play smart you can get good faction pretty early as a dark elf, there is no need to stay hated unless you want to -- you may not like the high level problems with faction killing some dungeons.

Halflings are pretty well balance, and quite common as a cleric choice on some servers I think.

Anyhow, hope I've confused you yet more

Heya!

Couple o' commments!

1. Dark Elf- the only place where banking would be annoying is on Faydwer, though you should be able to get the dwarves to like you when you kill enough goblins. Otherwise, other parts of the world aren't bad- in Qeynos, bank with the Bloodsabres. You should be able to build faction with HH fairly easily. And you'll have the advantage of having bakning facilities close to Guk and Cazic Thule, something that light races don't have.

2. Dwarves- You're analysis is right on- they're a popular choice for cleric. If you make one, be sure to put at least 5 points in agility.

3. Halfling- Well, I'm one of 'em, and I love it :-) I woudl advise putting the extra 5 points that you can't put into wisdom in strength, and always remember you have hide and sneak, both of which make life MUCH easier if you use them right. The only downside is that they don't have forgeable items- yet.

Good luck on your choice. IMO, you can't go wrong with any of these.

Sheez... ERUDITES!

Ok.. I'm biased.

OK- Erudites have excellent starting stats in terms of wisdom - we can max our starting wisdom out quite a bit (not as much as high elves and dwarves I believe, tho, about 115?).

We also have the disadvantage of no night sight, like human clerics.

However, we look stunning in plate.

Basically, starting stats are great, but there is so much equipment with stats out there that even tho I fudged it when I started (I had never been a cleric, didn't know wisdom was what made mana, so my starting INT is 110) I now have a 200 wisdom.

So play a race/deity you LIKE, and put some points into strength and wisdom (strength mainly so you can wear plate) and have fun with it.

I would suggest that you pick a race that fits your roleplay model. Max your wisdom out and, if you chose not to be a dwarf, add the rest to strength.

As you reach high levels, you will be able to max out your wisdom and obtain high AC regardless of what race you chose.

The one defining factor influencing race choice that I have found is that human and erudite clerics have difficulty seeing at night. Even so, there are numerous night vision items available (mage coldstones and caster spells) that can help to reduce this problem. At lower levels, however, the lack of night vision can be a major pain.
