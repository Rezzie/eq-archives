Mana and time wise? Heck yes it's worth it. The healing ability of SH doesn't change much with level, at best only a few hp. The healing ability of CH goes up with level exponentially as the tanks you party with get more hitpoints, and you are able to give them better buffs. SH is 250 mana for about 600 hp healed. CH is 400 mana, so if you can heal more than 1000 hitpoints with it, it's more mana efficient. This won't happen as a matter of course at level 39 when you first get the spell, but at 51 the warrior I party with the most has over 3000 hitpoints with cleric and shammy buffs. I could drain my entire mana bar SH'ing him from one bubble to full, or CH the same amount for a little under a bubble.

That being said, I don't use CH all the time. If I'm the only healer in the group and we have an enchanter, bard, or other class that draws a lot of attention and can go down in a hurry, I am very careful about how I use it. 10 seconds is plenty of time for a manasink to die. Also, if we're getting piled on and hitpoints are dropping fast, I'll probably use SH rather than trusting the timing of CH. And if I'm being pounded on, I'll cast a faster heal rather than take the chance of CH being interrupted and having it be too late to cast another. Mana efficiency isn't the only thing to consider.

But aside from resurrection, Cheal is the best spell in our lineup, and no other class gets it.

2 SH, ~1400hp, 500 mana, cast time 8.5 assuming 1 sec refresh

1 CH on a player with 2000hp at 20% health, 1600hp, 400 mana, cast time 10.0

This being your situation around level 40, if you can arrange one person to take all the damage, and you have a free hand to cast, the math is unmistakable.

At 49, even paladins and SKs have 2400hp fully buffed at a minimum. 2800hp is not uncommon. With a 45+ tank I will use CH as my main heal all the time, SH being for emergencies only (someone getting nuked and you're not sure if it's ice comets from a guk wizard or not).

On the planes, when tanks are sporting heroism+symbol+marzin+talisman+stamina you could drain your entire mana bar bringing someone to full health with SH. With all those buffs even rangers, rogues, and monks get CH. Think 3000hp on a ranger, 4500 on a warrior. That SH will heal half a bub.

Once you have CH you should find every opportunity you can to use it. Plus, it's always nice to know that someone is _completely_ healed.
