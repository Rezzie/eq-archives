Let me see if I can put into perspective what Aladain is saying...

There is a point to questing. The journey is as important as the reward.

How exiciting would Indiana Jones have been had he simply been able to take the back door into the room with the Holy Grail? What point would there be to the cleric epic if people could simply skip to Ragefire?

The point is, there is a quest behind all this and Verant has made it impossible for you to simply skip past the other parts just to get to the reward. They grav fluxed up so they could get the glory of the kill first and see how hard he was and maybe get loot. They got the first two and part of the third but they were denied what others will have to quest for.

Has Verant punished players for going outside The Vision(tm) before? Yes. Is this a case of Verant doing so? Yes. The major difference? There is recourse here. They can do the quest and go back for the loot. Nobody has been robbed of anything. In fact, if anything, it actually BENEFITS other players.

The usual scenario is that uberguilds will blow through areas and exploit the hell out of them and then get it nerfed so nobody else can do the same, thus ensuring they have an advantage (for a while anyways). For once, Verant actually LEARNED something and was ahead of the game. If anything, it was a lesson to players. Verant has woken up to the pattern and put a stop to it. Moreover, it means there is less exploiting to be done AND less "uber" people have less of a gap.

Let's say that he HAD dropped normal loot... Everyone and their dog would have been grav fluxing up and killing him until Verant either nerfed the spell, the zone or the tactic. This way, there is no nerf period and uberguilds have less of an advatage.
