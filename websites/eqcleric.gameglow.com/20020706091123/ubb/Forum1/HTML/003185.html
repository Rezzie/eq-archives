It's a known bug in Kunark. Apparently mobs can yell for help just as you or I can. What this does is copy the hate list of your mob onto any other mob with friendly faction. The problem is that the NPCs in Kunark have apparently been given the ability to yell in a HUGE radius. This means that things that you can't even see from where you are can get you on their hate list.

Case in point...
A good place to get exp well into your 40s is the forest giants of FV. In the immediate area there are Drolvargs and Kunark frogloks. Each has their own faction. Now, near the FG house there is a Drolvarg temple with lots of Drolvargs inside. Now, occasionally a drolvarg will wander by and agro onto us and we would kill it. Inevitably, a few minutes we would get another faction hit for drolvargs.

It gets worse...

The frogloks will actively seek you out. I have a friend who camped these giants for a week solid but he was ALWAYS plagued by the frogloks showing up and often times walking past the entire group just to beat on him. I ran into the same problem myself. Killed a froglok after about an hour one night and I could not keep them off me.

So I tested Gordon's theory. The Swamp of No Hope is nearby so we all zoned in and zoned back. Lo and behold, we didn't see another frog the rest of the night.

Unfortunately, nobody at VI has a clue as to why this is so until it is found you should consider it a "feature" and I would recommend your group zoning once the agro becomes that bad.

Wish there was something better but that's your only answer for now

Monsters in Kunark will also "give up" on trying to catch someone pretty easily, which can lead to problems.

Your puller, with SoW, goes out into the zone, tags a monster, and heads back to your camp. He outpaces the monster a little too much, though, and the monster heads back to his spawn point. No biggie - he pulls something else instead. Remember, though - he's *still* on that monster's aggro list! From that point on, every time you heal or buff the puller, you're putting *yourself* higher on that monster's aggro list - and since the monster has been "activated", so to speak, he's yelling for help around him, even though he's not fighting you.

So now you're at the top of this one monster's hate list, and he spreads his hate list around. If the puller comes back with anything that's been near him, they'll go STRAIGHT for you - they hate you more, after all.
