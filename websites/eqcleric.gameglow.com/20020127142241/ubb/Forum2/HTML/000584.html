Spells generally have effects that fall into a number of categories.

For instance, the courage line of spells do not stack with one another...the highest one will overwrite the lower ones.

Cleric wise, the common lines are the self buff (which shaman hp buffs overwrite) courage line, symbol(hp buff that requires a gem) and ac line, and various resists.

For the most part, the higher level versions of these overwrite the lower levels ones. At the higher end there are buffs like aegolism (which is symbol, courage line, and armor all rolled into one) which do more than one line, in order to preserver buff spots (you can only have 15 buffs on at once)

Additionally there are stacking issues with some of the new luclin resists/buffs other classes got where a more powerfull spell can be overwritten with a inferior spell (the biggest example of this is the level 60 chanter all in one buff that can be overwritten by breaze (a level 16 buff))

You have 3 stacking hp/ac buffs (4 if you count the self buff). The pure AC line, the hp/ac line and the hp line.

If your the only one buffing-

The way I found to be most efficent, buff everyone with hp/ac line, main tanks and chanter(bard) with AC. Below 40 I only symboled the enchanter(bard) and puller, after 40 anyone I might complete heal, and the chanter get symbols.

As far as what other people have that conflict with our buffs, and who should cast which-

Druids- Skin Like "X" doesn't stack with our ac/hp buff. If the druid offer's I'll let them go nut's, but they usually don't and I think ours are more mana efficent and druids have better offense and other buffs to cast. PoTG change's things at 60...

Shamen- Have Focus which doesn't stack with our self-only line. If the shamen cast's it I don't click it off, but rarely ask for it outside of raids. I think they also get a ac/hp line (skin like turtle?), whatever.. they'll never cast it with you around, and they have alot of other buffs to offer.

Enchanter- Enchanter's do have an AC line of buffs. Once or twice when my mana was hurting but the chanter's wasn't I asked him to hit the melee's off with AC... that's rare.

Mage- Their AC buff has a mana regen component (atleast at higher levels) and they can buff themselves with ac and ac/hp line that our's overwrite. I'll usually not buff a mage unless asked to, I will toss their pet an ac/hp buff from time to time.

Paladin- they get acess to all our line's of buffs. I'll have them buff AC from time to time, but it's not usual for me to have one around.
