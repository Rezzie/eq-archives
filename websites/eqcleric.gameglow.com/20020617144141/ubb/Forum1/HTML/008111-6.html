First, it's great to see clerics getting some adjustment. It has seemed, to many of us (based both on these posts and in my conversations with other clerics) that the class suffers from one huge misconception - most RPGers think of clerics in terms of the old DnD system. In DnD, clerics are nearly as good a fighter as warriors are, having only some restrictions regarding what weapons they can use (blunt vs sharp) and about 20% fewer hit points.

I, and many friends I know who have clerics in EQ, felt that the cleric would be able to melee and solo better than it has turned out - this, in turn, has made it less interesting to play clerics. They are more like priests than clerics. It would be extremely interesting to know how many clerics, as a percentage of classes, there are in the game.

While these changes are good, I think you still need to go further to start to bring clerics back up to equality with some of the super-classes (shammys, necros and paladins).

First, if clerics are "the best" undead nukers in the game (and they should be), why do the nukes and undead DoTs (which were a great add also, thank you) not do the same kind of damage that wizards (recognized as the best direct nukers in the game) can do vs. normal MOBs? Where are the 1200HP undead nukes?

Second, revise the damange cap that applies to clerics and let us melee!

Lastly, regarding the new nuke changes, they are great - but there is still one major drawback. The agro rate. Clerics already have a very rapid agro of the mob in a group, because they're healing the party. Now while the mob should be unhappy with the cleric for undoing all that damage the mob had just so viciously done to the tank, it shouldn't mean that if I start nuking a mob after the tank has built up the agro, that the mob makes me the favorite target.

This effect will multiply for those clerics who mem several nukes for "chain-nuking" - we'll build up agro so fast, that even with taunts, the tanks won't be able to pull the mobs off us (unlike other caster classes like wizzies and mages). How come this healing/nuking agro algorithm doesn't apply to shammys (who are almost as good at healing as clerics, especially when you consider chloroblast, et.al.)?

So, bottom line IMO - this is good stuff, but keep going. You have a long ways to go towards bring clerics back even to equality with other classes in the game.

Perhaps this change was not intended to balance the classes. Perhaps this change addresses another matter that VI see's as a problem, perhaps the issue of people avoiding dungeons like the plague. Hmmmm....

More than once I've said that a few changes would help to get lower levels ---even twinks--- back into dungeons. My ideas were as follows:

1. Lower the level of Rez to 14 or 19.
2. Make Summon Corpse usable at 12 or 16.
3. Make all dungeons bindable outside.
4. Expand both Summon Corpse and Rez to other classes.
5. Remove or Greatly reduce the xp penalty in dungeons.

My logic? well, there are two things imho that keep people out of dungeons:
1. You die more- therefore lose much more xp and time.
2. Most dungeons are far from anywhere you may bind--losing yet more time.
These 2 factors cut out 2 major groups of players. Powergamers won't go near them, because they lose xp faster than they can gain it, no matter how good the ZEM is. Casual Players don't use them because they take a long time to get to with no garuantee of a group when you get there, and a long, time consuming CR when they die-- If you only have an hour online, you don't want to spend 50 minutes of it traveling to the dungeon then back to your corpse.

So, imagine my surprise when I checked the boards this morning and saw these changes. I nearly fell off my chair when I saw that Rez had been radicaly lowered in level, and effectively given to another class too, given that it's at a lower level for pally's now than it started for clerics.

In fact, I don't think Rez should be a balance factor for Clerics at all-- beyond the minimal value of a fairly well used utility spell. It's like saying that Group Gates are an important aspect of Wizard balance, or Waterstone Summoning is important for Mage Balance. It has some value, but it's not a big balance issue. It DOES, however, help younger clerics with grouping, as they do have a utility function to compete with the other healers's SOW, DS, TP, Slow, and DOT. And yes, that one spell does make up for all 5 of those catagories quite nicely in my opinion.

You see, it's my opinion that clerics should be the best healers, not the only healers. Before level 39, other than lacking a bit in utility, Clerics are balanced well with the other healers. When the other healers did not get anymore healing upgrades till over 50, but clerics got complete heal, it fell out of balance and stayed there. When the balancing does come down from on high, I expect clerics will be as disappointed in their balance as warriors were when the Melee's got it. I think enchanters might feel that way a bit as well. All 3 of the Holy Trinity are so superior in thier area as to exclude others from filling the gap without them, and caster balance-- if it follows the path that Melee balance did-- will see those that are lacking brought up into line with the H3, rather than see the H3 brought down.

But, what if you were interested in balancing all 3 healing classes?

Well, first of all, you'd need to increase the healing ability of Shamans and/or Druids beyond the effectiveness of a 24 cleric after level 34 and before level 51. I don't know why the Direct Healing line was stopped at this point, but it should have been continued in regular intervals right to level 60. Clerics were balanced in healing as they were 5 levels ahead, and have an inate bonus to healing spells.

Now we get into alternate healing spells. Shaman and Druids get Regen, which needs to be balanced in the Cleric line. I'd like to see Complete Heal changed to a 5 or 10 minute Recast, but with a 2 second or so cast time. Not something you can use more than once a fight, and not every fight, but still very good. Perhaps the Mana cost could be looked at, since I expect the Direct healing line to get progressivly better in mana/healing. Possibly add another version of this spell a bit lower, Say at level 19, so they can be cycled like Divine Aura and Barrier.

Now, the Final straw, The Healing over time spells. These are where the real healing power should lie. Use the same kind of progression as the Direct healing spells-- Only less mana, more healing-- but upgrade only every 10 levels, with the Shaman and Druid always being at least 1 spell lower. Between the 5 level advantage of the direct healing, and the healing bonus, and these superior Heal over time spells, the Clerics would hold a clear--but not over powering edge. Perhaps make the Druid line only usable outdoors, but closer to the cleric line.

Now balancing is nearly complete. Look at the 3 classes in terms of Utility and Buffs. I'd say the clerics come up a tiny bit short on utility--though rez is a big one-- but their buffs are fine, with maybe a few niche buffs to make up for things like Damage Sheilds and Sow. For utility maybe we could see a bit of Self only heals, and a new Word of something series with extremly low cast times that are decent AE Debuffs for AC and STR. Possibly a short lived Silence type spell for use on enemy casters, a lower single target, and a higher level AE version would be good.

The reason all clerics do is heal is because they are the only one's who can. They should have a healthy backup role other than Master AC/HP buffer. They do have decent DD's, and even better against Undead. All they need is a bit of combat utility to round out thier aresenal to compete with Shaman Slows and Druid Evacs and Damage Sheilds.
