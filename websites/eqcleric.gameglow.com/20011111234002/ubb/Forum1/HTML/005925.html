I have a very good monk friend who taught me how to heal pulling monks. Her rule is not to heal her until she says it is ok. Or she brings it into camp. If she is dying 10 feet away from camp, then my job is to watch her. She knows which pulls she wants to bring in, and which pulls she wants to eat. She is a joy to hunt with.

I hunted with another monk in Droga one day. He pulled a number of mobs, tried to feign 15 feet away and died. I stood there and watched. Next thing I knew he was giving me a really hard time. A very fine cleric in his guild took care of the situation. I told him that if he wanted a pull, to bring it into camp, if he wanted to eat the death then he needed to stay out of camp. Still, I am very grateful to the cleric in his guild who explained the situation to him so I didn't have to.

Before I met the best pulling monk in the world, I had no idea what pullers were supposed to do. Now when I pull with my alts, I know that sometimes the puller simply has to eat the death and go on. It is part of the job. Some people of all ages are immature. Do not feel guilty because someone else doesn't know how to play intelligently.

The best way to handle that situation is to have someone else in your group step up and tell the bard to get over it. The bard wanted to fight with you. Let someone else tell the bard he is an idiot. It will diffuse the situation much more. Or, follow some of the very helpful suggestions on this thread
