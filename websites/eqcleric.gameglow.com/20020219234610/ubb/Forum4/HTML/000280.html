*notice: this is a combo rant/question.

I just dinged 35 this morning and sat to med for the first time. What a pleasant experience it is to see what is happening instead of just reading about it. I feel like I just went from Radio to TV...it seems that this level may just be the point where things really change for me as a "Drunken Dwarven Battlecleric". I see that my spells are getting stronger, but so are my foes.

What are your thoughts regarding the path to level 39? Right now, I'm raiding 1-2 times per week, grouping in guild another 1-2 times, and hunting solo or pickups another 1-2 nights per week. I have done this to get the experience of all the different styles of play, both outdoors and underground.

Now for the rant, how many of you brother Clerics get upset by Warriors telling you not to meele, even though you are at FM? I hate this, as I have an offensive skill at 173, defensive skill at 152 and dodge at 169, along with 1HB maxed out for level already (175). Heck, I even have my swimming over 160!

I have spent a lot of effort maintaining my meele skills, I am after all, a Battlecleric. What's the point of Plate Mail if you sit in the dew-covered grass, letting it gather rust? I have had to explain to the tanks many times that it does our group no good if I sit there at full mana, waiting to heal them, but not doing anything else. I can do 25-33 damage with my Dark Ember (even though it doesn't proc for me yet), or 18-22 with the smoldering brand (which does proc Burn for 14dd about every 8-10 swings). I switch back and forth, using the SB when I want to do many blows (i.e. following a runner) and + strength, or the DE for + stats and a steady-heavy damage without building too much aggro. These guys (MT/MA) also seem to forget that I can self buff to 1,250+ hp, so I can take alot of meele damage (for this level of MOB).

I know, we are healers, but why must we be forced into becoming a bunch of one-trick ponies when we really are a pretty well rounded class.

If your mana is at 80 percent or greater, its none of the tanks business what you are doing, as long as you aren't screwing him up.

You can use whatever heals you like, you can hit stuff, you can cast nukes. The only time he should offer advice is if you are consistently running low on mana, and he knows of a way to save some.

Ask him this, "Do I tell you how to hit stuff? Do I tell you when to taunt? No, you do your job well, and I respect that. I do my job well too, can't you respect me?"

The only exceptions to this would be if you were gaining aggro, and not able to cast a heal spell when needed.

My general response to meat shields that offer advice is: "Thank you."

I then think about it, and if I think their idea has a chance to speed the flow of exp or loot, then I try it a couple times.

I can say this. You are approaching the point where the amount of melee damage you can do is being outweighted by the amount of spell damage you can do. You will be better off medding the extra time, then getting off a nuke or a stun.

Play the way you like. If someone objects, then tell them to stick their opinion back IN their ass and when you need it, you'll ask them for it. Until then, STFD and STFU.

While you are no longer a viable damage-dealing class in melee, you CAN do a few different things at 35:

Heal and sit and emote funny stuff (You'll have time)
Heal/Stun to mitigate damage
Heal/Melee (if you have a proc'ing weapon)
Heal/Nuke to help kill it faster still

No matter what happens, play the game the way *you* like to play it. As long as no one dies and you've all had fun, you've done your job.
