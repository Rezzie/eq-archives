If I recall didn't use CH much at 39. It doesn't get real mana efficient till the mid 40's or so. Then you will start to see major mana savings using ch over other heals. The trick to the long casting time is you have to time the heal and start it for example when your tank is at 2 bubbles of life, then the heal will go off when he has like half a bubble or life. It takes practice to get used to.

I was really nervous when I got this spell. I take deaths in my group very seriously, so the thought that I was in the process of healing someone when they died made me not want to use this spell to begin with.

I would say, only use it in 'predictable' situations, if you have frequent adds or multiple mobs, don't even try. Constant single pulls are a great time to learn the timing on this. At first, cast early, even if it means you're wasting mana. Better to waste some than have to rez. I'd say start at half life the very first times you try it, but when you start totally depends on how quickly your main tank is being taken down. (As a side note, it was this spell that made me realize how much I love having tanks with huge AC in my group.)

Also, make a hotkey for this. Mine say something like 'COMPLETE Healing %t. 10 seconds and counting..'. It lets the tank know you're healing them, so they don't panic as much. It lets backup healers know they may need to pick up any emergency heals. And last (as I found out last night, hehe) it gives you one last chance to make sure you're actually targetting your tank, and not the mob (one to many hits of the assist key).

Efficiency wise, I think it's equal to superior healing if you get like 960 hps healed, but my math isn't the best, so don't take that number seriously. Others will have a better take on efficiency than I.

And as I've seen other clerics say before, the first time you heal a 60 warrior from a sliver to full with one spell...well, that's the kind of stuff you've been waiting all these levels for, eh???
