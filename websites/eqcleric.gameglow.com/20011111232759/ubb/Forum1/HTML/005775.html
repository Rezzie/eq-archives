Having grouped extensively with a couple necromancers and shaman recently, I've encountered a curious phenomenon that I've never experienced before. They insist that I do not heal them when they convert hitpoints to mana during downtime when I am FOM. Their basic position is twofold: I should save mana for tanks and they have their own ways of getting their health back.

The flaw in this logic as I see it is that I'm charged with healing them during combat if something unforeseen happens. If they start out combat at 80% or 60% or even (ack!) 20% health, I have less time react -- they go down faster than a wet paper bag sometimes. Moreover, during combat -- as opposed to during downtime when we are waiting for the puller to return -- is when I *have* to save mana for the tanks.

My attitude is that if I am FOM and and one greater heal will top them off (bringing me to about 97.5% mana and 100% mana by the time the puller returns), and there is no immediate threat, it makes no sense *not* to heal them.

They say they will be responsible for regaining the health, of course, but when push comes to shove and they die, they are going to feel I didn't do my job and I'm going to feel bad that I lost them.

I understand their concerns about efficiency, but to me not healing them is more inefficient. Am I missing something here?

Well, in most groups that I've been in there are really only 3 people who should ever be taking damage during a fight: tank, enchanter, shaman. Tank when all is going well, enchanter when mez breaks or he has to tash before mezzing etc, and shaman because they like to debuff early on (to avoid as much total damage as possible) or attempt to debuff multiple times when resisted. BTW I almost never group with bards so don't know too much about them.

Other than pullers who can be healed between fights, other people should pretty much never need heals. If they do, it's probably their own fault for overnuking/out-taunting the tank. When people do that I may heal them but I'm not too hasty about it. If they die, I consider it their fault not mine. If they don't like me for not healing them in those cases, they can find a different group to join. I won't miss em cause they don't play their class well anyway. If the main tank is doing an awful job taunting though, then it's kind of a different story.. Like a rogue friend of mine (lvl 56 w/ragebringer) grouped with a 55 monk as main tank.. not only did the monk not have taunt button but he used an extremely slow 2h weapon.. There was a very unhappy cleric that day.

Looks like I digressed a bit there.. Back to the topic.
Heal the shaman when they get knocked about after debuffing. Or when they are really low mana and need some health to Cann off of.
Heal the necro on the rare occasion when they don't have anything to tap (or when they are playing crowd control, which is seldom if ever).
Heal the tank.
Heal the chanter.
Heal the other classes only between fights for the most part.
Or, when Full Mana, heal anyone who isn't full health.

Oh, and don't forget that some buffs are a form of heal which has other uses (think necro who can use valor/reso/hero/whatever for total hps as well as heal).

Going this way you run the risk of losing your necro/shaman faster than you can react when mobs spawn on top of your camp. But that is rather rare in my experience and probably not worth wasting mana on. Kind of a toss up if you know you're going to have spawns but I'd lean toward not healing them.

Hmm.. Think I said everything I wanted to say.. My cleric is only 39 though.. So you might want to disregard it where it conflicts with what more experienced clerics have to say.
