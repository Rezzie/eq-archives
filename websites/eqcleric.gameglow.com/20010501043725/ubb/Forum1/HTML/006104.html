It all depends upon where you wish to fight, but those lard butts cause a lot of trouble at times.

SKs are an extremely self-sufficient class. Snare, fear, self see invis/invis/invis undead, Feign Death, life taps, pets. Furthermore, SKs (and Paladins) are uber-taunters, something that is very much appreciated at higher levels. I'd go so far to say that an SK or Paladin (but SK a bit better I think) is probably the best tank for an XP group. We pull better than Warriors, tank better than Monks, taunt better than anyone (minus Rangers, but they aren't supposed to) and only have one real deficiency: damage output. SK damage output is not impressive compared to Rogues and Monks, but is not too far behind Warriors (since SKs get some incredible weapons). But, that trio would be very self-sufficient and they could add ANY class to it and the group would work. Why? Because the SK can lock that mob onto them and they have the hit points/AC to handle it. Everyone else can just open up on the mob.

The main deficiency of any hybrid versus the Warrior is if, at some point, they decide to do UBER mobs hybrids are very weak. But for XP groups hybrids are very cool, and the SK is one I personally enjoyed a lot.

All races are good btw, play a fattie if desired, but don't lock into that "phatties rewl" nonsense.

Warrior *is* the absolute best third that could be joined to a Cleric/Enchanter combo. Has your friend played EQ before? If not, I would question the "a warrior won't be fun to play" reaction (s)he is having. If so, well that's a bit different maybe, but I would be curious as to why warrior has been deselected. Warriors aren't easy or simple to play; warriors have a *lot* to do in a fight, decision making every second in good fights.

However, assuming Warrior is a definite out, this is how I see this being left.

Cleric/Enchanter; you don't want either of those pulling, ever. Maybe in light areas, but no where serious or deep dungeon. So the third has to be able to pull. Most any class can pull if done right, but some of the classes have pulling advantages.

Tank would be my recommendation for your third; it would best take advantage of the CLR/ENC support. Since Paladins don't interface well with clerics for the most part (buffs overwritten, healing usually isn't very necessary when a competent cleric is around with a near permanent supply of breeze/clarity/clarity 2), I would say the second best choice after a Warrior would be SK.

SK has the depth of hps (when buffed and leveled, and geared up in good stuff) to work efficiently with the support characters. Especially if a Troll or Ogre SK is selected. Faction problems will be no problem because the two support characters can bank for him/her and bring back supplies. The SK will begin to lag in damage output by the mid 40s, and in the 50s will be about 30-40% less effective than a warrior in overall tank combat, but still still be the second best choice. The near constant presence of the ENC means some of the disads of the SK will be mitigated against somewhat (speed, assorted other buffs like Boon of Garou and such).

If SK isn't likable, then go with a Monk. Monks can't usually tank very well, but properly buffed and with the clarity boost on clerical mana, it should work out ok. By the 40s and 50s, though, the monk will become something of a mana sink if (s)he's the only tank in your trio. Especially if tanking 2-3 at a time, but that shouldn't be too bad (mezzes and slows from enc).

A more innovative choice would be either Mage or Necro. Either can pull if done properly, even in deep dungeons, and the pet is a disposable tank that can be buffed all to hell and back by your trio. Necro gives dots and some offensive spells, plus mana regen (lich line) and mana transfer to either of the cleric or enc as needed. Mage gives damage shields (way way useful, especially on fights that last longer than 30seconds), plus a huge variety of useful summon items. A lot of people don't work with pets often, but if you take an onlevel Nec/Mage pet and buff it fully (courage line, armor line, symbol line, speed, damage shield, mage pet buffs), it's sick. Add a pickup Shaman in for talisman, regen, and a suite of stat buffs, and that pet will probably take out an on-level unbuffed tank solo if the tank isn't heavily heavily twinked.

Don't take rogue for your trio; they tank even worse than monks and need a second tank to keep a mob busy so they can fully turn on all their damage outputs.

Rangers; they tank a touch worse than monks but have close to warrior damage output if properly equipped and played. I'd go with SK or Monk before Ranger, but it is probably a viable choice if played right and buffed correctly.

Oh well, have fun.

Rangers have to work harder to get good ac, but can keep up with the warriors if they are careful. The big problem Rangers have when tanking is the lower hit points, so expect to spend more money on symbols.

If your tank still can not decide, send them to the spell lists and to the fashion page on AllaKhazam and have them pick based on spells and looks.
