While I can sympathise with the idealists (I'm actually really impressed that some survive up to post-50s!) who think that clerics should never ask for money, I also wonder if an upfront price list isn't fairer than making folks play a guessing game after each ressurection.

My view is that most clerics won't travel to ressurect, period, unless its for a friend/guildmate, they are offered a bribe so huge the inconvenience is worth it, or they're in a particularly helpful mood.

Perhaps if their was a 'market rate' for ressurection (I'm talking travelling to ressurect, not having someone bring your their body for attention when you aren't too busy) the way there is for Druid portals, more of the Less Than Perfectly Pure of Heart cleric players would go 'unanon' like taxis, to signal availability.

On the other hand, druid friends of mine get spammed all the time with requests for portals with the assumption that all druids WILL port for the flat fee, so an upfront price list might just add to our woes!

Still -- dunno why folks seem to always attempt to shame other clerics into doing everything gratis. I mean, if no one charged, how could we get that warm fuzzy superior feeling when we turn down that huge donation, or surreptiously trade them back half of it in change?

Well I have as of yet not hit 49, so I can only speak on what I *plan* to do when I get there, and that is basically stick to the same charge plan I have now.

A: If your in my group/guild/ good friend/ real life friend =no charge.

B: If your in same zone and will drag corpse to me and I can do it without inconvenienceing my group = donations accepted but not required.

C: If your 1 zone away and I have the ability to go there (that being I am not partied, or pace is slow enough to allow it) = donation required. If you give me a crappy donation, next time the answer will be no. (Crappy = 4p, or 100gold or some junk)

D: If you are multiple zones away I will set a price depending on the distance, time involved, and danger of intervening and destinations zones. All that of course *only* if I am available. Most likely multiple zone res's are going to be *no* most of the time simply because of the million factors that could mess it up including them having decayed (for res purposes) before I can get there, having found *someone else* to do it when I'm halfway there, them logging before I get there and wasting my time, and a myriad of other factors.

When it comes down to getting a res I am finding more and more that people have *NO* sense of integrity to the commitment they are making with you when you agree to res them. You commit to taking time, resources, and putting yourself in danger to recover *THEIR* experience and they are quick to dismiss you if anything else comes up.

Having had things like this happen to me enough already.. I will definitely stick by my policy *AND* set the recovery prices for item D well and sufficient to make my efforts worthwhile.
