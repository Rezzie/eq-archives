Firstly we don't know the facts, but there are situations when clerics do need mana, and there are times when they don't.

Saying necros can heal, is only fine to a point, they are great at healing minor damage, but their big group heal spell, does it over time. Thats a healing rate of around 17 hps a second.

Unless you are in a spot where basically healing isn't needed much at all, 17 damage per second by a mob is fairly low.

So a necro can not primary heal in certain places.

That means in places where a groups speed is determined by the clerics mana, being out of mana is a serious issue. Most clerics I bet do not like being low on mana, its not a case that we will definitely need it, its that if something were to happen we can't do a damn thing about it.

Think of it like evac mana for a wizard/druid. They have to think really hard if they are gonna use that mana, and I bet they get nervous when they don't have it, just like clerics get nervous when they can't cover situations. Chances are you will not get into that situation, but if you could do, you won't have the time to respond.

The cleric is right, by the time you spot the problem - you won't be able to get the mana out in time, especially if what is required is a group heal (comparing word of restoration - around 150 ish hps a second, to the next best available, what the necro has). The mana tap is useful, but firstly - you need a mob to do that on that has mana, so if you aint got any - there goes that spell, it has issues with certain buffs (its a bit flaky - very hard to explain but sometimes it causes real issues), and its not immediate.

Necro mana is called more flexible because of the incredible rate at which they regain it. Given the fact that a necro will probably have 2x the mana regen rate of a cleric, they will be able to pump the cleric for 300 more mana in the time the cleric gets back 400, almost doubling his mana regen rate until safety has been maintained.

If the enchanter had 0 mana, would you refuse to give them mana, and insist that you would handle their job too? We all know necros can mez, though not as well.

Its not about efficency with the spell you use, its about mana transfer. If I have alot of mana, and a shaman is running dry - the cleric transfers mana via healing to the shaman, it lets them do that. Its called less downtime.

The thing that ya questioning is that you felt that downtime would be less without the cleric healing for the next fight. And for where you are, this might be applicable, but it certainly is not everywhere, and having a general, I won't twitch ever unless its an emergency policy is silly.

It's like having a no heal necro policy as a cleric, after all they can heal themselves just fine. Sometimes that just doesn't work.

And you would be surprised at what happens at the other end of the spectrum, when a cleric is full of mana, and is happy they can deal with any situation, they can be very very free with their mana. The necro can leech forever, and do efficent dot's rather than less efficent lifetaps and increase the total damage output of the group.

Remember that theft of vitae is terribly inefficent if you are only healing one person, so its not a general use all the time spell.

Practically any class's of similar level can outdamage a cleric, clerics mana isn't important other than maintaining the minimum required to heal, which differs greatly depending on where you are, but most clerics will not like being in a situation where they can not contend with it. Necro's have that power to balance mana within a party, it is foolish to ignore it.
