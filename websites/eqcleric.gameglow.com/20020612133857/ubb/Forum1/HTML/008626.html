It sounds like you located the hotkeys, and yes, you should be using full-screen mode. There are a few of them that are already made. You can scroll to the right to find blank hotkeys. Just left click (no need to hold it down very long) on a blank key and you should obtain a window with 5 lines of text. If you don't have one already, make a /assist hotkey. When you target the tank in your group, then use the /assist hotkey to target the mob he has targeted.

Here is the example of the healing hotkey I use:
/sit off
/cast 2 (or whatever spot the spell is placed in)
/g Complete Healing on %T
/cast 2 (in case first attempt fizzles)

If I am healing as part of a raid, I will add an additional line for the raid chat channel to announce that heal:
/2 Complete Healing on %T

Also, if you have not discovered you have (I think) 10 pages of 10 hot key menus on the bottom left. Hold down Shift and hit 2. You will have Hot Key menu number 2. You can use the numbers on your key pad for the hotkeys, rather than clicking on them, i.e. hit 1 and it will correspond to hotkey number 1 in the current menu.

When you begin attending raids you will find it useful to make target hotkeys:
/target Tank1
That way if you are responsible for healing someone who is not in your group, you can monitor their HP bar.

I would encourage you to start using hotkeys for all your heals when you are grouped. If you are announcing your heals you should rarely have anyone say, 'HEAL ME PLZKTHX!~", in which case you should let them die anyway. JUST KIDDING!

Good luck!

Isn't it a right click on the social?

On the "menu" screen you right click socials once to bring up the socials box. Then you find a social you want to reuse, or hit the arrow button to go to a new panel of social keys.

Right click on the social you want to edit. Edit it, hit done.

Then you left-click and hold down on the social button you just created to drag it to your hotkey area. You have to carefully fit it into a spot there, if you don't get it quite right the drag falls.

You can also use the /hotbutton command (type /hotbutton to get the syntax) and it puts the button right on your cursor. Unfortunately it only seems to let you have a one-line hotkey, but you can always go back and edit it.

Once the hotkey is in place on your hotkey panel (you have 10 of them), you can edit them by right clicking them. You can fire them off by typing their number 1-0 (0=10). Or you can use a mouse to press it.

Ok, I think that I know what you're talking about.

Some terms first..
Fullscreen Mode: The display where you see the world through your windows (windows are semi-transparant)
Window Mode: This is the old style, small box for video, parchment box for text, little graphic floating in the upper left.

In fullscreen mode, if you hit ALT-M you can bring up your main menu functions. Click ONCE on socials and it brings up the first page of 10 or 20 socials (forget which). You can leave this box up at all times, and click on any of the socials to make them work.

To move the social to your hotkey bank (for ease and speed of use) LEFT-click and hold on the social until it appears on your cursor, then place the upper-left corner of the social box over your hotkey bank and left click once, this is the same as dragging a spell gem to hotkey bank.

To edit socials, you right-click them.

Most people have the social correspond to the spell gem # that it is activating (this allows you to ALT-# to cast if the social gets screwed up. IE: if you have Complete Heal in spot #1, then put the macro that casts CH in the first hotkey box.. this means that you hit "1" to activate the social, and ALT-1 to cast the spell without activating the social.

The reason you'll see people with their full social hotkey bank up are for different socials for the same spell, or for non-casting socials.. for instance you could have socials that make you assist(your target), assist 1st tank, assist 2nd tank, assist MT's cleric, another to target the enchanter and remedy/divine light,...etc.

That's what I'm assuming you were asking for. Just remember to hotkey the most crucial socials for speed, and the other tools can stay up on the socials bank.
