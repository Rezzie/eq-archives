How many times have you been urgently called to RES someone under the premise that they’re going to lose their corpse soon only to arrive and find a naked corpse that a player has already looted – looking only to gain back XP. Sure, they’ll probably be willing to pay you a hefty sum for the RES ( I typically see 200pp being offered), but I pose this question: What does this have to do with role playing?

What is transpiring in these instances is a utilization of the game rules to benefit, in essence, the numeric value of the character. It’s a loophole that reflects an unrealistic role-playing transaction, and although Verant supposedly made SOME change to minimize it (shortened lifespan of corpse after looting, or something like that), it’s still being done quite frequently.

It just seems to me that when you’re back on your feet in full armor gaining XP through combat, the prospect of seeking out a Cleric to get back XP for an incident prior should not be allowed. It’s my understanding that the role-playing concept behind RES is to bring back someone from the dead in a certain respect. If the player is already alive again and fighting, then a mockery is being made of this concept.

How does one stop this action? Simple – loot one item off your corpse and you can no longer be RES’d. I’d also argue that you can no longer be RES’d if you get within a certain distance of your corpse.

Certainly, Clerics would lose out on opportunities to make some good money; but I feel this spell is being abused, countering the spirit of role playing. Does this make any sense?
