Supposedly there are two types of factors when determining your ac, damage avoidance and damage absorption. Your character ac heavily leans on damage absorption I believe. Basically agi and some spell effects aid in damage avoidance in that the mob would have a greater chance of missing you. While armor and straight ac buffs usually deal with armor absorption in that when you are hit, it's for less total damage on average. Your defense skill I think is the most important factor when determining your actual defense ability though.

Also there's the deal of threshold numbers for your stats. At 75 and 100 you get a large bonus for that specific stat. So someone who raises their agility from 97 to 99 won't get that big of a bonus in AC compared to someone who raises it from 98 to 100.

Shamans did a bunch of testing on this when their stat buffs usefulness came into question. I think they basically said that stats really don't do a heck of alot aside from wis and int (chr for enchanters and bards). While dex, agil and str do help in some regards, the amount they provide is miniscule. Although I haven't done any statistical analysis of it, it does seem like a shaman agility buff really helps out my damage avoidance alot, also str on a rogue is amazing when you see their backstabs.

One other thing our spell Shield of Words supposedly has some other defense factors built in. I'm not sure if it was GZ himself who said this or just a rumor that was started. I don't remember if it was that it raised our defense skill or just upped our damage avoidance along with the +6 ac raise over aof. I should go test this on ping fizzlecutter later.
