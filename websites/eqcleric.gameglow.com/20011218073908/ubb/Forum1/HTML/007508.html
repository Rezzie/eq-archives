Haven't done it either.

Probably wont.

Fishing is a required skill to do the quests. I myself have over 150 fishing, due to the pious (read: BROKE) nature of lower level clerics who couldn't afford ports. I fished while waiting for the boat(s).

Pottery is another required skill for the shawls. Well, I can see ANYONE wanting to learn pottery simply for the Thurgadin Gate Potions. Not to mention, you gotta be able to make a mug to hold yer beer

Baking? Well, who in Norrath can't use a little skill in cooking? Can't live off bread and water forever, can you? (well, you CAN, but would you want to?)

I see every reason for a cleric to be a smith (especially a dwarf cleric). We wear plate armor, we have the affinity to metals, etc etc. I am a smith (188 skill). Unfortunatly, smithing is the one tradeskill not required to do the shawls.

I see no reason, roleplaying or otherwise, why a cleric should have to be a high level tailor. Sure, we learn tailoring to make ourselves the newbie patchwork armor (at least, I did). And sure, we could have some tailoring to make leather paddings for smithing and bags. But not high skilled tailors. It doesn't fit the class. (My tailoring is 82.)

I see no reason, roleplaying or otherwise, why a cleric should learn Fletching. We can't use bows. Our religeus affiliation prohibits weapons of any kind that break or pierce the skin. It's not a viable trade skill, since no one actually BUYS arrows anymore, they just use summoned.

I see no reason, roleplaying or otherwise, why a cleric should be a jewelery crafter. We can not enchant the items we make. Therefore, it's not a viable trade skill since no one will buy unenchanted jewelery.

I am not going to dump thousands of PP into learning JC, Fletching and high level tailoring. It's just not worth it.

Different types of the same effect stack- ie, equipment, songs, and spells stack but not multiples of each.

The best effect on any of your equipment will be the one to work- any other lesser equipment effects will not stack. Then you can have a bard mana song that will stack with that, and an enchanter clarity spell that will stack on top of the other two as well.

There may be exceptions to the rule, but I don't think there are many...
