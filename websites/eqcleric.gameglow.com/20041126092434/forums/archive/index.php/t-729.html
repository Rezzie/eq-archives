This is simply an idea taking a counterpoint from Brodders post of 'whats the next battle for clerics'. I myself was unconvinced that Brodders ideas should be pressed before Verant. Thats not an insult to Brodder but just a statement that I disagree.

One issue that I see incessantly now that our pet has been nerfed .. is the pet is delegated to the 'worthless' slot.

Now I'm not sure I've ever seen a class stand together on an issue like this. (Maybe mages with some of their worthless summons is relatively close) but if we can as a majority agree that our pet IS worthless .. then...

Lets not ask for it to be changed, lets ask for it to be REMOVED.

Think about it for a minute. The whole cleric community saying "look this spell is just crap, just take it out. Its taking room in my spellbook that I would rather have blank, thanks for the slap in the face but no thanks"

Quite an interesting thought to shove back in the game desingers face the fact they threw us a peice of candy that the majority of clerics have expressed is sour to the point of not being worth having at all.

Every other class petitions for CHANGE .. they want it to be better. Why not realize that we know they don't want us to really have a pet, and let them know that we realize it. Throw it back in their face (relatively politely of course).

I mean comon? The pet dies in one round if hit, dies if it doesnt agro fast enough, dies at the end of the combat and costs an attrociuos amount of mana for what it does!

Thanks but no thanks Verant.

Don't do us any 'favors'. People are going to use this 'pet' as an argument against clerics some day I warrant and the fact is it is entirely unworthy of it. Why even have it on our list if all it is good for is some other class to use as a class balance argument against us?

Now I anticipate this idea will be to confontrational, too argumentative, to negative for the crowd here. If the idea is not accepted (as I honestly don't think it will be) perhaps at least let it awaken new thought trains on ways clerical issues can be addressed to Verant. Not every issue needs to be addressed in the form of 'upgrade this, upgrade that'.

Thanks for your time.
