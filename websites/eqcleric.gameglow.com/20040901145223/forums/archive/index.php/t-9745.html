Holy might will be resisted more than stun due to the nature of the spell.

With stun, there is one affect- stun. The mob has a chance to resist the stun, if it fails, it's stunned.

However, with Holy might, it's a dual effect- stun + damage. The mob gets to try to resist the stun, and IF it fails to resist that, it gets to try to resist the damage. If it sucessfully makes it's resist on the damage, both affects are resisted.

I suck at statics, and I know this formula is off, but you'll get the idea. Let's assume an even con mob has a 50% chance to resist your damage and debuff spells.

Stun - 50% of sucess
H Might - 25% of sucess (50% for stun, PLUS 50% for damage)

That help at all?

Oh, and with root every time you do damage to a rooted mob it gets to try to resist (break) the root spell, and from what I can tell the damage and type is a factor. Big DD will give it alot higher chance to break than meele or small DoT.

When soloing I almost never use root or stun. Root gets resisted too much and the MOB always breaks it at the first damage spell.

I just slam the MOB with the highest damage spell that I can. I hit 'em with Furor for a couple and then Strike to finish them off. Then I use the AC and melee to assist the spell in killing the MOB.

I do not use WoP when soloing. I use it in a group sometimes but it isn't all that affective. It takes too much mana and does too little damage. It does 20 dmg to all monsters but a low level paladin or warrior can do that much damage with a good weapon. Furor does 17 dmg and uses half the mana so I get 34 dmg for the same as WoP. Is it the same with the higher levels AE spells?

Prior to the battle I always cast all buffs on myself. Don't forget the buffs. When hunting blacks or yellows, buffs make a huge difference.

(Druids get more effective buff spells for fighting. Shield of Thistles, Str of Earth, Thistlecoat, and Skin like Rock are better than Holy Armor and Center when fighting.)

I can take yellows with this strategy and still have 2 bubbles of health.
