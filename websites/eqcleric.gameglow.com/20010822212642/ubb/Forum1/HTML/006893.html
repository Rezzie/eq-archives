Chest: go for the dusty or EBBP, both you can get for 500pp or less on my server

Head: Seb Scale, Velium Spiked, Froggy Crown will all add a good bit of ac, and if you need the wis the seb scale is the best option.

Ears: If the 3 wis earrings you mention are forest loops you are fine, if they are the jeweler made ones, get forest loops

Mask: try to get on some FG raids and win a polished mith mask for -1 wis but +5ac, or try for enshrouded veil or bone mask of the jarsath if you don't need as much wis

Neck: Blackened Alloy Medallion is not that bad, if you're hanging out in Frozen Shadow to get the velium helm you might venture up to the 7th floor with a group and try to get a Tserrina's symbol.

Rings: I wear 6/65s but if you still need wis try for a rune etched wedding band (once again from ToFS) and a golem tear ring (random rare in chardok, can hunt with one group for some exp and nice drops)

Bracers: trade the RMBs out for quest bracers, they will give you more ac and as a dwarf you should not need the str from the RMBs anyways.

Arms: Ulthork will give you 8ac and let you keep the 3 wis, you can easily get them killing the ulthorks in EW. Giant Scalemail will let you keep the 3wis and give you like 11 ac (can't remember exactly). If you don't need wis Donals is a good option, or you can go for the gladiator arms that drop in Kael arena.

Legs: Quest and EM are good options, as are Kromriff military legs (fairly easy quest in GD)

Boots: Quest are probably easiest option, Donals are possible if you like to hunt in KC (I hate it), but usually run at least 10k if you are trying to buy them.

Weapon: I use a Paw of Opolla personally and just summon a hammer when I get bored and want to bash things, If you must have a weapon try for a Dark Ember (not sure if that is name) thing that drops at rygorr fort in EW.

Shield: Try hunting in Velks for the crystal chitin shield or chardok for a sarnak battle shield. If you don't need wis you can pick up a prismatic shield in Hate (pick one up anyway really, you will need resist gear at some point)

Range: do the shawl quest through the 3rd one and you will get a range slot item at has lots of stats including 3ac/wis (can't remember everything else)

Most everything I listed above you can get in one group in whatever zone the item drops in or buy for less than 1k in EC. If your guild hunts in the arena regularly then go for thurg armor. No matter what try to pick of some of your EM stuff in hate as a lot of it you will use for resists even after you have other gear.

I agree with Tarf. I saw my first major gear upgrade after going to Hate. Free is free and the helm/bp/legs are especially nice.

After that, Kunark armor is available in Karnors and Sebilis. IMO skip this except for the gloves (or of course the bp/legs if you can swing it).

Velious quest armor is next, with the quickest armor to get being PoG. BPs drop off some nasty named trees that take forever to kill tho. BP AC is 42.

Easiest would be Thurg imo, easy zone to get to and from, but the quality of armor is less than the other Velious armor (about equal to PoG).

Finally Skyshrine and Kael armor require you hunt in Temple of Veeshan. NOT a trivial exercise unless you're in a large, high level guild. Armor drops off random mobs, all equally nasty. Well the wurms are particularly nasty with their 500hp AE Damage. =P Have your resist gear ready (140MR/140FR/140CR is the minimum I'd shoot for).

Kael has the best AC (AC54) but SS has the best stats. I really depends on what faction your guild is on actually.

Oh also, there is some REAL nice armor (not as good as the quest armor) that drops off dragons in Western Wastes. Easy to farm, it's only disadvantage is getting there, if you're not familiar with the way or don't have a 57+ evaccer. Still this is probably the most FUN armor to get in Velious.

Another good piece is the Emerald Dragonscale BP that is the result of a quest that spawns from a scale off Woushi. AC is 22 but the wis is a wopping 20! Woushi is a dragon in WL.

As you can see the Velious stuff requires you to be able to field a force large enough to take down some big mobs, but the armor is a good return for the effort in most cases.

Hope this helps.
