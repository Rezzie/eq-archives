The other thing to remember about stunning is that at level 15, most the cast time for most spells is not much longer than our first stun. This makes it hard to interrupt a lot of spells even with our fast casting level 5 stun unless you cast the stun immediately after the mob starts casting. As you go up in level, the mobs casting time increases and it gets easier.

The second technique, if you have multiple stuns, is to just chain cast the stuns every couple seconds so that the mob is permanently stunned and never has a chance to cast.

I am 29, and defer to the folks on this board who have been playing longer. http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif

buff healing is something I have done since I got center. I do it during battle. I usually kept center memmed (until I got better ones). What I do is buff everyone with my highest level buff. Mobs tend to get on some of your casting partners and they do not have the hp or ac as other group members. After you play a while, you will know how long your buff lasts. Every once in a while, recast the buff as a heal. That way you do not have to ever refresh that buff. It keeps your party buffed and is a heal at the same time which reduces downtime.

Root and stun are great, experiment with them all the time. I learned about that on this board. I did not find them tremendously effective till I was in my 20's. (but then I didn't start reading this board till then either). Druids like snare a lot, and actually, so do I, it can be very effective, doesn't break like root does and a snared runner won't hit your party. Rooting monsters is a great anti taunt for your caster buddies. If a monster is hitting the druid, park it and the druid can med in peace.

Using socials to cast is a really good idea, you should use them for DA and heals especially.

DA should be always memmed, and do not be afraid to use it. It comes back in ten minutes, is a great anti taunt. When hunting in unrest, my group would have to run for it, I would stay behind to loot all the corpses of the recently slain monsters and waddle to the zone. I cast DA before I get jumped and only died a half a dozen times or so out of around a hundred times doing it (my guildies still tease me about it). It is not fail safe, but it refreshes pretty quick really, and you should start using it now so you remember to use it when you need it. When you heal a tank and 4 monsters start beating on you, cast it and watch them ALL immediately leave you and get back to the business of beating on your tank.

Clerics really get pretty decent DD spells starting at 14, and you need to keep in mind at the level you are at that sometimes Clerics are great nukers. You can often save a ton of mana by nuking runners or just plain nuking. Dead monsters don't hit your friends. You can heal 100 poitns with a heal or you can smite for 80 for about the same mana. If you are hunting with a druid or ranger who snares a mob it will turn its back and not fight. If you can nuke it to that point, you can sit and med while your tanks beat on it unmolested.

My cleric is a char that I made by accident, about number 8 and I have had so much fun I can not conceive of playing another as a primary. Although the rogue and enchanter are a lot of fun.

Lastly, many of the folks on this board know a lot more than I do, I am just remembering my experience at your level and what worked for me.
