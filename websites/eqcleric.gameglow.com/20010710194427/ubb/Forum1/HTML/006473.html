Mirri, you're really starting to annoy me.

Was Conquest exploiting? According to Verant they were, and they say they have the logs of what went on to prove it. I don't doubt Verant's word on that one bit. BUT...

...I don't think that what Conquest was exploiting should have existed in the first place. Terrain effect and interaction should be the very first thing addressed in the structure of any 3D world. Remember how shocked Verant was to have to add a vertical (Z) coordinate to the world? Excuse me, but that's why they call it THREE DIMENSIONAL! The evolution of the graphics in EQ reminds me of a bunch of college kids just learning how to program. I'm sorry, but pretty textures don't mean anything when monsters can hit you through walls, and terrain doesn't act like it should.

In other words...

Is Conquest at fault for exploiting bugs? By the EULA, yes.

Is Verant at fault for the bugs existing in the first place? HELL yes.

Should Conquest have been banned? I think it's a bit extreme, but in the end, it's per the EULA.

Should Verant fix their shoddy 3D engine so crap like this isn't possible in the first place? Again, HELL yes.

You see, you have to understand the basic mentality of gamers...that is, they will use whatever advantage they can, especially in nigh-impossible encounters like Sleeper's Tomb is supposed to be. So you make sure that you streamline the game and get rid of the potential exploits beforehand, so people can't use them in the first place. I think it's crap to punish the players because some programmer doesn't understand his job.

Fixing exploits is like putting a lock on a door...it serves no real purpose other than to keep honest people honest.
