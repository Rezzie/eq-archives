Look at it realistically. For most people, more HP just means you have more time to cast heal on them.

If there isn't a reasonable chance of them dropping below 2 bubbles, then you are better off spending your mana for heals and stuns.

The story changes once you start using complete heal. Since it costs the same mana to heal any number of HP, you want the person you are casting it on to have as many HP as possible to maximize efficency.

People with low HP whom often are the targets (enchanters) also needs as many HP as possible to give you the few seconds it takes to cast on them.

About the only time I will fully buff everyone in my group is when we are breaking into a room, zone, or fighting a dragon type mob.

I'm only 37th, so I cannot speak for large raid situations, where I would imagine symbol spells to be highly important.

I don't find myself using symbol all that often. More often nowadays, but not all the time. I typically use it a) on tanks and enchanters (and everyone else if mana permits) when we are first breaking into a room that could be challenging, b) if we will be fighting a very difficult named soon, and c) small groups where the extra hitpoints are vital.

In most normal hunting situations, symbol is not necessary. If you are in a situation where you must have symbol on your group at all times just in order to stay alive, you might want to reconsider your situation ... one bad pull, or spawn in a tight mana situation, and that will be trouble, regardless of how many hitpoints people have.

You could spend your mana adding hitpoints, or maybe using stuns to reduce healing, and the occasional nuke to help quick finish something off, etc. You are the cleric, don't let people tell you what to do. Always be open to suggetions, but when it is your money and your group's life, you are the one to make the call. I see symbol as a situational spell, not a "necessary" buff to keep going.

A few things to add, plus I just want to say that the information posted here is an excellent resource. Most of the posts and replies are well thought out and informative. I am just adding to the discussion and not discrediting the ablilities of anyone that has replied to this thread or any other.

That said, I beleive that you guys are overlooking the pre-20 Clerical experiences. I have ALWAYS used the Symbol line, ON THE MAIN TANK since I had it scribed. As soon as I could I filled a small bad with Cat's Eye Agates, and Center, AC-buff, symbol were the first to go on a tank that entered group.

The response has been, the first symbol is only 37hp. That is true. That does not make the symbol useless, IMHO. That 37hp is one or two rounds of damage from mobs of equal level. I beleive the HP addition increases per level also.

That does not mean I left it up. Everyone on Rallos knows that Suudar is always broke! A techinique I use is to buff myself first, then anyone else, that way I know when to rebuff and noone has to ask. If it's not taxing my mana supply to heal the tank, then Symbol doesn't get reapplied.

Whenever an enchanter or a wizzie enters the group the symbol goes up, and stays up. Those two seem to draw unswerving attention. The added hps from the symbol give me more time to keep them alive.

People learn what the costs of Symbols are, and if you are using them, they make sure to split loots or get you some extra costs. Most tanks now carry stones with them, and I get prolly a dozen jaspers at the start of a group and a tell, "Keep my symbol up, and the extras are yours." Now, that's the way to do it, and I make sure that that person doesn't go long without symbol. Even if everyone in the group is saying don't waste mans and reagents on that rogue he's taking no damage. Heh, he gave me the stones to take care of him, he gets it, end of story.

Bottom line, the main puller, enchanter and wizard get symbol without thinking about it. The rest is per case basis. The exception being "the stone giver." What it boils down to is your best judgement as the cleric in the group.
