I never cease to be amazed by the folks who think that because you helped them once when they need it, you are their ‘friend’ and they can call on you any time, whether you are grouped or not.

Example of this is the halfling warrior I’d rezzed before, and thus had me pegged as a ‘yes of course’ cleric. ‘no’ just couldn’t seem to register. He asked me to rez his necro friend while I was in a very underpowered group for the executioner’s room in Guk. I said ‘only if he can dump mana, I’m overstretched here’. Fine, the necromancer decides he doesn’t need rezzed and I carry on just scraping through each fight mana-wise. I get another tell ‘oh I died and I’m in Freeport, can you rez me.’ I sigh to myself then say explain that I’m in a very underpowered group and that taking my mana endangers the group. With some persuasion I agree to do the one rescuscitate, due to past casual acquaintance grouping with the guy.

Sure enough, not just his corpse but those of the three groupmates I wasn’t told about get dragged to me, and I’m left telling my group to stop pulling, invis themselves and wait while I med up the mana to rez these folks one by one. The necro doesn’t show up to dump mana, and I had to choose whether to have them drag the corpses right away again or do it. After my group grumbling but agreeing, I perform the resurrections and explain again to this casual rez acquaintance that I cannot spare the mana and will not be able to do this again. I have toggled /roleplay at this point, rare for me, because I’m just not able to spare mana safely and have to take a good run up to say ‘no’.

Twenty minutes later the SAME halfling warrior comes and plunks himself down beside me. “Can you please complete heal me.”

At this point I explain that he is inconsiderately taking mana from my group and inflicting down time on my group so that he can solo. I am just BOGGLED by folks that cannot take a hint. I also wish that I could just get ASSERTIVE and not get bullied into doing stuff that makes me feel exploited.
