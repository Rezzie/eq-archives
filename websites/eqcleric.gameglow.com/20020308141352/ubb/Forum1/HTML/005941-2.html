I dunno enough about particular zones or drops to be able to argue on anything other than a general basis so...

It seems to me that the only loot system that completely avoids ambiguity or the chance of ill-feeling is full random on anything that is not NO DROP.

Hear me out here a sec will ya?

We've seen in this thread a lot of people explain what *they* mean by NBG, and we've seen situations where different people, who both favor NBG, have differing opinions on what should happen.

My thesis is that it's better to have a (possibly inferior) system that EVERYONE can agree is 100% (un)fair, rather than a (possible superior) system that people will perpetually have disagreements about.

Or to put it another way: Is NBG (in whatever incarnation) *so* much better than full random, to justify all the disputes and ill-will it creates?

I guess Need Before Greed has a range of interpretations. I interpret it as based upon the item description, especially for pickup groups.

If the item is only for a single class, e.g., Donal's armor, then only the members of the group in that class (clerics in this case) would roll for it, or if there's only one cleric, it passes by default, unless that person has one already, or something superior (my Thunder Etched Gauntlets are better than Donal's gloves, for instance).

If the item is for six or eight classes, covering every member of the group, it is lottoed to all, regardless of whether one might make better use of it than another (as is generally the case with warriors and weapons).

Interpretations trickier than this lead to trouble, I've found, unless the NBG situation involves guild groups. Remember, pickup groups are a bunch of strangers, and the groupmembers haven't much of a vested interest in making some fellow they just met richer or more powerful. They will spend very little of their game time with those people, especially in the high end game.

Simple, easy to follow NBG rules work best among strangers in pickup groups.
