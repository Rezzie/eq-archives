Whenever someone has a DI or DP on them, they have a small chance (too small, according to most Clerics :b ) to receive a heal when that person would be actually slain. These spells trigger when you are low hps, not when you would die. I find anything tat takes you from above that point to below it in a single strike (AoW 1150damage hit for example) gives the spell no chance of firing. While DP dosn't do much I always liked it because, hey 5 str =). I find DI to be very valuable. Works fairly frequently, even casts nice and fast. Had a puller in Ssra yesterday bringing in 3 or 4 snakes and getting whooped on. Instead of casting CH I tried a DI, and it almost imidiatly triggered after it landed.

In that situation, he would have died if I tried to CH (no way I was finishing a 10 second CHeal if he was triggering DI when it landed), and I didn't get agroed by 4 snakes and owned. So there was a chance it wouldn't have worked and he would have died, I doubt there was a chance he would have lived if I tried for a CHeal.

Nice atro, ballsy but very nice heh. I don't typically have DI up for exp groups though, and if it was a raid I would probably have not worried about him dying too much as aegolism costs less pp to rebuff than di does :>

From testing mikar did, DI/DP activates around the 15% hp mark, and calculates on a per damage not per round basis so even for a full hit from AoW on a tank theres a good chance of it having a chance to trigger since 15% of the typical 6.5k tank is just under 1k hps, so the tank would have to be around 1k-1.1k hps and get hit for full for it not to have a chance of triggering.

A defensive tank or one with stability 3 is I believe guaranteed to have a chance at DI triggering unless the mob hits as hard as sirrun the lunatic :>
