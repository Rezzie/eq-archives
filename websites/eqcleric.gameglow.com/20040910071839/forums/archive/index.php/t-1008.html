I won't comment on what you did or didn't do in that group. Each situation is different. Your conscience will dictate how well you performed. Learn from each encounter and bring it with you to the next.

The best way to manage a group's healing is watch what happens for a couple of battles against single mobs. Whenever I join a group, I run a checklist:

Ask for classes/levels
Buff everyone with HP and AC buffs
Ask the groupmates how many HPs they have after buffs

You know how much mana you have - break out your calculator and do a little math. You will have a good feel for how much one of your heals will improve their health bars, be it at level 10 or level 48. You also have a good sense for how much damage the group can take before you start saying "low on mana".

If my group is in a particularly nasty area, then I ask the puller to grab single mobs for starters. I like to see who taunts the best and how badly they can take a beating. Then, with this "simulator" information, I know who's life bar needs to watched carefully. I do not stun, nuke, root or atone during these first few battles. I just heal. It's not creative and borders on boring, but you get a good sense for the group's "synergy" and ability to absorb damage.

After a few battles like that, we begin in earnest - pulling the nastier critters in one, twos and threes. And it's at this point that you can begin to use YOUR style of play. Don't ask the group what they want you to do. They will be wrong every time. Here are some helpful reasons why:


When deciding between a final nuke or heal to win a battle, remember that heals are NEVER EVER resisted. (yup.. that happened to me.. tried to nuke a ghoul to finish a battle.. he resisted.. two died. Lesson learned.)

Communicate with your group. Let them know your mana state at each bubble (80%, 60%...) all the way down to 30% (my Low on Mana - LOM - trigger).

Stunning is usually better than nuking - its far cheaper and if done right, can render a foe nearly inert.

Stunning also acts as damage prevention and is MUCH cheaper than healing - using several stuns in a row to prevent damage uses FAR less mana than healing an unstunned MOB's target.

If you have a secondary healer in the group, let them throw a heal or two while you med up a bit.

As the cleric, never, ever throw a big heal on the puller if he's returning wounded. Let the support healers (pally's especially) each throw a small heal into him to keep him alive. If YOU do a big heal, YOU will aggro each incoming mob and YOU will die, followed shortly by each of your groupmates. (If the high level tank TRULY knows what he's doing and cannot shake the massive pull train he's formed, he'll stop and die alone - instead of risking the lives of all party members. At higher levels, this is a common tactic as the 90% XP rezzes make death a non-issue for most.)

If you are nuking, like other casting classes, wait until the critter is below 50% to start throwing the big bombs around. Otherwise, the MOB will aggro YOU and let you know how appreciative he is about the damage you have been dealing.

Don't be afraid to take a few shots. Sometimes, taking a little bit of a beating from one MOB while the rest of your team kills the other MOB is a sound tactic. (We are a plate-wearing class for a reason. No, we don't have the hitpoints of tanks, but we sure as heck can take a beating with the best of them.) You just have to trust that your group can kill the first one before the MOB's buddy kills you. <grin>

Make a hotkey that alerts the group that you have made the decision to ZONE. Make sure it's easy to read. And in that same hotkey, let the zone know that your group has screwed the pooch and is now dragging an Amtrak to the zone. If people stay behind after the cleric has called for a ZONE, then it is their mistake. We are the lifeblood of the group and the BEST judges of what can and can't be done.

Cautious clerics live a LOT longer than impatient ones - dead clerics can't heal. Stay alive. If you are in a situation where you think some poop may hit the fan, let the group know that you are in a purely defensive mode (no nuking... just stuns, roots, heals, fear, etc) and work SMART.

You are the group's backbone. Stay cool and stay calm and you will NEVER have an unenjoyable time in Everquest.
