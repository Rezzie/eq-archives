I replaced Gheal with celestial, it heals nearly twice as much as Sup and costs less. If I needed a baby heal I used reso, it does not overwrite the caster shielding line.

I never kept force loaded, too slow and expensive for something with 2 resist checks. If I want to stun at all I use holy might for damage mitigation. Only load the others if I'm trying to interrupt/chain stun.

I keep both DA/DB up, rarely do I ever have a backup healer...and if I do they never heal after 50 levels of kiting.

I would like to use instill but it is too expensive and unreliable. If something is on me root buys me and my group enough time.

I don't keep stun mem'd, probably should being on a Zek. Instead I keep reso in that spot. Often in a nasty battle I can go oom and not be able to use a regular heal in time. With smart tanks the desperation heal of reso can make the difference.

I only have complete up if there is a warrior around. Even then I prefer celestial, I'ld rather not flirt with death and using celestial allows me to pay more attention to the casters.

When I was that level, there was no celestial heal. But I never had more than 2 heals memed at a time, and only 1 invul spell (I never really could justify the 2 spell slots dedicated to these spells that I rarely use).

Here was my set up when I got level 44:

Complete Heal
Superior Heal
Extinguish Fatigue/Sound of force/maybe Word Of Healing
Force/Sound of Force
Rotating Buff
Divine Barrier
Instill
Retrobution/Expel Undead

Spell Slots 3 and 4 were pretty much used for spare slots, fill in the spell for the need, depending on the circumstance. But I always had 1 spell slot dedicated to buffs, slots 1 and 2 for my 2 best heals, and 7,8, and 9 were pretty much always DB, root spell, DD. I always had DB up since it takes so long to refresh, when you need it you can't wait 18 minutes before you can use it. I always kept a root memmed at ALL times because when you need it, you can't sit down and mem it. And I keep a DD up most of the time for those times I am FM and group's fully buffed. To me FM is waisted mana.
If Celestial Heal was available when I was level 44, I would have used that instead of Superior Heal (I am level 52 and JUST got it and can't believe how great this spell is, I was in a full group with no chanter fighting dwarfs in icewall and only had to cast Complete Heal 3 times in 5 hours). Greater Heal is good for topping off casters when they get whacked but I found it was pretty much too weak by this time. You should know how many red bars a superior heal/celestial heal heals on a caster after you have the spell a few days. And I never kept people full health after I got complete heal, it was a waist of mana. Just cast your lesser heal on casters when they are down enough to make it efficient. Should you get the complaint between fights that they aren't full health (especially if they are casters) ask them if they would cast a 500hp DD on a mob that only had 40 hp left. Same thing.

And as for using Reso for a heal, never did it myself, but not saying that it isn't a good thing. Just never saw the need to heal 200 hp myself.

As with everything else it depends on who you are grouped with, where you are fighting and what you are fighting.

I always group with a back up healer. After 49 levels of grouping, she always knows when to heal, when to evac, when to snare, damage shield, zing, dispell and nuke. Druids rock when they are played right. As do chanters. As do clerics. As do wizards....

Anyway, normal spell load at 49 should be as follows

DA
DB (can be switched out for a stun or zing or greater depending on need)
root (I can not think of a time when this wasn't necessary in the last 40 levels.)
stun (chanter assist or to prevent heals or gates, it is essential)
DD (switch between undead and reg, depending)
CH (if there is a tank big enough, else, a stun)
Cel Heal (if you can get it, else a stun)
Sup Heal (because greater just doesn't have enough oomph)

Those work for me, I have root and buffs on teh same page of the spell book and switch them in and out at need.

Starting around 44, greater heal just doesn't get it. Chanters are worth the extra mana of Cel Heal and the others just need to work on controlling aggro better.
