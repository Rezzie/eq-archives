The above was cut from an overview of group roles posted by a level 32 ranger in my guild. Specifically, the part of the healer. There are several issues I have with this statement.

1) At 32- what cleric was he grouping with that was "showing off a big heal?" Gheal is nothing to write home about! Good- yes, but 300 hp. And the cleric has had it for several levels.

2) I don't know any cleric that would "show off" and allow someone to die

3) This was a role discussion- and lower levels who are just getting a clue will read it and might develop a negative impression about clerics because of this. None of his other role descriptions had a mention of a death due to an error, or a discussion about someone's pompous behaviour.

4) Did he really die because of the lost heal? Why isnt the death due to an overpull? Or his own overburn? or a train? Many factors go into a death. Why did he label the death due to the cleric "showing off?"

I have jokingly labelled the 20's as the "get a clue" levels- people are grouping against tougher mobs, learning their roles and others roles. Really LEARNING not to hit the mezzed mobs, pick the right target, assist the puller, yada yada yada.
