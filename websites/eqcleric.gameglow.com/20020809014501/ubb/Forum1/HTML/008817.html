To add to the other reasons...

1) Constant pressure to attend every and all raids. one of the classes that if you dont have enough of, the raid is canceled. You need 3 warriors, you need 1 enchanter, you need 4-8 clerics... sometimes as much as 10. Its not easy being a cleric.

2) Constant res requests. This makes 'your time' other peoples time. You spend your time 'waiting' on other people who have died, and didnt bring a cleric along for xp.

3) Monotony. Where as most classes can only do one thing 'well' and can do other things with a level of mediocrity, we do only one thing. We heal and buff. We cannot melee. We cannot do much damage. We cannot solo. We cannot... well, do much else very effectively. It gets tireseome only hitting the blue button.

4) Disrespect from other players. Other players feel they are entiteled to a res, cause you're in the zone... the guild.. the server... the planet. The dont want to help pay for reagents for spells you cast on them.

5) Pressure for Success. If a raid fails, all fingers point to clerics first. often if a raid fails, its because the clerics either screwed up, or couldnt heal successfully enough anyways.

6) Lack of Gear. Where as most classes get 'toys' or get 'powerful items' clerics only get additions to their hp/mana base... and sometimes we get a weapon that actually can kill rats. Sometimes we get flowing thought items. Otherwise, we're the 'bastard' class when it comes to loot making, and decisions.

Anyways, those are my guesses. I cant really say, I put up with all that crap just fine.

Honestly, people leave guilds for alot of reasons, but being a GM of a guild and a cleric, I have seen both sides first hand.

If you are in a raiding guild that raids 5-6 days a week, it is very easy to burn out as a cleric. If you like this scenario(I do), then you are what I call a raid cleric. A raid cleric is someone that prefers to raid vs xp or tradeskills. A raid cleric online time is dominated by others. Raid clerics have to deal with alot of pressure. Some people dont like this. Some do. I have seen lots of good clerics, that were recruited for my guild end up not wanting to join after raiding for 3 weeks. They just can not do raids 5-6 days a week. They prefer a raid or two a week and the rest free time.

Burnout at high level raiding is a common problem as well. We routinely let clerics take a couple days off, so they dont get burnt out.

Loot - well in my guild, our clerics are not the step children. Most of the time I see large mana items, I tell my officers now that is a CLERIC item. But all loot needs to be done fair, which is hard to do. Also I truly believe if you have good equipped clerics, you need less, but all of them need to have approximately the same gear or mana pools. So we strive to make sure which clerics get upgrades are the biggest( as long as they play regularly.

EQ is 3 years old, if you have played since beta 3 like myself, you probably seen a few friends just retire. This is just a fact of the game. Nothing you can really do about this one.

One thing we did to help our clerics is we have a dot fund. I pretty much try to supply dots for our clerics at every raid. The dot fund basically gets money from spell sells, xtra loot not needed, and gems from raids. This i know has helped tons of my clerics from having to worry about xping for cash to raise money for dots and just plain frustration at being poor all the time. This helped to remove alot of friction.

I do not allow clerics to be blamed for failures at guild raids. This actually goes for most of the guild. I know that our job is stressful as it is, but to yell at clerics who never get praise when everything goes right, is just wrong. I know a few times this had happened and caused friction, but does not happen now. Yes i know who and when a cleric misses something. Human error, people make mistakes. I just tell my guild to focus on cr and take another shot.

Those are most of the reasons i have seen for clerics leaving the high end game. IE level 60 clerics. Those our some of the things i have done to try and keep them happy. It just seems to me that the high level 60 clerics are slowing dying. Most high end raiding guilds are constantly looking for them to join them. Hope that helped.

Flooglehammer - it's not just your guild. It is universal.

I do not know of any cleric who will stay in a guild that does not or can not reimburse reagents cast on guild outings. Going broke to buff a raid is not fun.

Clerics also tend to leave guilds when they're asked to leave the exp group they just managed to join after spending [insert inordinately long period of time] waiting for a group, just to rez a guildmate who died in [insert area at least three zones away] while soloing/farming/traveling/doing anything else a cleric can't do easily.

Along similar lines, getting a tell from a guildmate of "You're still LFG, you must not be doing anything, can you come rez our group of guildies? We already have a lower level cleric/druid/shaman but we got wiped out and want a higher level rez." The we-don't-want-to-exp-with-you-but-we-want-your-rez thing is very, very annoying.

The pressure, criticism and flak from deaths on a guild outing, regardless of whose fault it was, is also a reason. I'll make a blanket generalization and say clerics are sensitive people, and probably beat themselves up enough over any missed heals. Thus, getting crap from guildmates ("Y did u let me die!!!111 OMFG u suxx0rz!!!11") is likely to encourage a cleric to leave a guild, too. Being derided is not how anyone wants to spend his or her time.

Being told, "You need to work on your epic" by a guild leader ... well, Zzule and Altheia summed that part up pretty well.

I'm not sure what a guild could do to retain clerics in those situations, other than the peridot thing. Helping clerics advance in levels and equipment is probably also a good way to keep a cleric happy and in the guild.

Being sensitive to the cleric's time and feelings - actually, that'd go for any player, regardless of class - would go far in keeping people from leaving.

Flooglehammer, high end raiding with 30-40 people is boring and unprofitable for clerics. We usually stand around 95% of the time; most of us don't even buff anymore once someone in the guild has mass group buff.

Everyone else is dealing and taking damage (all evening long), and our biggest joy is finally pulling off a good complete heal rotation.

We aren't even able to watch the big fight because we have to face a wall and stare at the rotation messages. Are clerics essential, yes, are raids exciting, no.

And we clerics don't get to end our raid evenings with any interesting gear until the guild has reached the point when those plate armor pieces would otherwise rot.

If your playing time is limited, your cleric character stagnates, because all your time is consumed by raiding. Those relatively few clerics who can play endlessly are racking up those AA points, and the rest of the people in your guild are doing it too, but some clerics can't even find the time to scrape money together to buy a few spells.

I oversimplify, but hours and hours of standing around (yet still not daring to look away from the screen) is not fun, and pretty soon we clerics are distracted by other games, or other things to do in RL, that are a much more fun use of the evening.

I had to leave a raiding guild to regain my interest in EQ after not logging in for a month. I was tired of making no progress while watching others grow in power, in some small part due to my efforts.

To retain your clerics, make sure they are still progressing, either in loot, items, or experience, preferably in all three. I suggest swamping your clerics with lots of cash, so they can purchase what they need if they aren't likely to have the time to hunt for it because of raiding, or sell that extra loot and buy stuff for the clerics. Find out what spells they need, what faithstones they need, make sure they have the cash for things or make sure the guild buys it.

For clerics, raids can equal no character growth. If it goes on long enough, clerics leave. Make sure your clerics are progressing somehow if you want them to stay. There has to be some reward for all those boring evenings watching other people have fun.
