I wanna add something here. At 24, your symbol (Symbol of Transal?) uses Bloodstones. Now, also at level 24, you can go to Lavastorm and kill just about anything that moves, and get Bloodstones. 99% of the stuff there will not aggro you (only the occasional higher level Fire Ele will) unless you sit down close to one. I would recommend sitting at the big entryway that goes to Najena to med, and kill everything you can right outside.

When I dinged 24 (God, AGES ago), I spent 2 days in Lavastorm, walked out with about 150pp in misc junk/gems and 2 full backpacks of Bloodstones. Yes, 16 stacks. And those Bloodstones lasted me a good 6 or 7 levels. This 2 day jaunt paid for all my level 24 spells, and bought some armor upgrades as well. There is a gypsy camp right in the zone, so you can sell off the junk and buy food/water too.

Now, as far as looting to compensate for symbols, you can just stop casting symbol, wait for one of the tanks who loots alot to mention that symbol wore off, and say, "Sorry, I can't loot as fast as you do, so I can't afford to keep blowing my money to symbol you".

If that doesn't drive home the hint, then a simple "/shout level xx cleric looking for a group that isn't full of greedy assholes" usually does.

Of course, if you want my REAL advice? Get the HELL out of Lake of Ill Omen. The current crowd of twinked out asswipes do the same route, over and over. Oasis -> LOIO -> Overthere -> Dreadlands -> Karnor's Castle. They do this route every time they start a new character, that's all they know, and they never learn anything except how to be an ass. Avoid those zones like the plague.

At 18, try places like The Warrrens, High Pass, Karanas (Aviak City in South Karana is nice), Sol A, maybe even Mistmoore. IF there is anyone there, they will be a nicer class of people and will make your gaming experience better. If not, you can just grit your teeth and stick it out in LOIO until you hit 24, get your new spells and try some actual dungeons.

Selectively casting Sybol has already been mentioned so I won't add to that topic.

Playing my ranger in his 20's in LOIO (right after ROK came out so it was still a decent place to hunt) I made friends with a druid who was running into loot hungry groups. She had a little trick she used to get her share: she would wait until just before the mob was going to run (read: stand still since it was snared) and she would heal everyone she could very quickly. This way she got agro, took a couple hits and then mobs dropped right in front of her to loot. This method worked particularly well when we had 2 or 3 mobs lined up and the melees were eager to get on to the next one.
