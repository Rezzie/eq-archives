I've also used heals on the incorrect target. One thing I do to alleviate this is to use F2-F6, as has been recommended by previous posters. I also have a hotkey set up as such:

/cast 3
/g Complete Heal On %t

Since %t puts in my target's name, it's a visual reminder (since I look at my text screen more than anything) of what I have targeted. I've made one mistake since, but the secondary healer in the group noticed I stated the wrong party member and saw me duck, he immediately cast a heal on the tank that I was CH'ing. Without that %t, the tank would have died. Very nice feature, I recommend it.

I'm very conservative with CH. I've even been criticized by tanks for casting it too early. But I've never lost someone on a late CH yet, and I don't run low on mana either.


When I decide when to complete heal, I look at not only the tank's health, but at what else is going on. How much mana do I have? How fast is the health bar dropping? Which tank is taunting more? How close are we to finishing a mob off? How many are still mezzed? How long ago did the enchanter refresh mez, and when is it likely to break? Is there a danger of backspawn? Do I trust my backup healer to cover for me? The timing of all this is where my battle instincts kick in big time. My goal is to find the optimal 10 second window to cast that heal in, not just to squeeze the utmost mana efficiency out of it. That's just how I operate though

I generally don't use CH on anyone till I've grouped with them for a short while and gotten an idea of how well they can take a beating. So that means that if you're a new warrior in my group, I'll be casting a couple of remedies on you before I start in on CH; but on the other hand, I CHed my favorite shaman flawlessly during a recent battle. Made his day too. *grins*

And yes, the berserker message is a good guideline. Not foolproof by any means though.
