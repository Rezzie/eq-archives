In my mind, never! We are a plate class after all, so most of our mid-high level gear tends to be plate. Also, that extra AC is going to probably save your butt in a lot of situations. Faction can be annoying in lower levels for a DE cleric but it's really not all that bad. Being a cleric we are only hated for religion and race instead of being hit with religion/race/class hatred like necros and SKs. Most notably in the human cities we are only threatening or dubious to many NPCs that scowl or are threatening to the SK and necro dark elves. Because of this our faction seems to fix faster in some places. I can give you a rundown of my faction experiences, maybe it will help.

East and West Freeport (militia areas) are for the most part non-KOS aside from the monk guild. If you run the Janam and Rebby quests in the rogue guild a few times you will be able to buy and sell in Freeport with no problem. 2 of the Inns (Seafarer's Roost and Hogcallers Inn) have KOS NPCs in them, so avoid those and the monk guild and yo should be fine. My cleric never went in the caster guild, but she was only dubious to the casters just outside the arch. She never went into North Freeport much, but orcs will fix the paladin faction.

Highpass I believe she started off dubious, or very close to it (she killed orcs for exp for a couple nights before heading in) and she was able to buy and sell there no problem due to fixing coalition of tradesfolk faction in Freeport.

In the Qeynos area you are threatening KOS to guards, priests of life, and karana residents (dubious to merchants of course) you can fix it in Blackburrow or by killing bandits in a few hours if you want, or you can just avoid the guards. They're not too hard to get around if you know where they are. In my lower levels I fixed Qeynos faction, but then I killed it again hunting treants and the occasional Qeynos guard for fun. The corrupt Qeynos guards regard me amiably from these acts.

Rivervale you start KOS, you can fix this in Runnyeye if you want, I killed halflings instead since the zorros are fun to kill for exp in your mid levels and you need to kill the ones in Nek forest for your symbol quests.

Druids hate you, so you'll want to be invised if you get a port from a druid into West Commons, North Karana, East Karana, and definitely into Surefall Glade. I chose to show the druids how much I hated them back and later hunted them for experience and farmed the ones in EC for fine steel while watching auctions. I am now an ally to the unkempt druids in Rathe Mountains, and maxed out KOS to the rest of the factions.

You're scowls KOS to dwarves, elves, and gnomes, basically all the major cities on Faydwer (and the fairie types) but not to Crushbone Orcs. I believe this can be fixed but I never bothered. Instead I killed enough elves to be considered kindly to the orcs.

Kunark is annoying faction wise, Firiona Vie is not something that will raise easily I have heard (never tried) and you are of course KOS there being that it's an elven outpost, and it's easy to get KOS to your own outpost in OT if you hunt in zones that hurt Venril Sathir faction. If you have Luclin and don't mind sending a mule in to buy your spells in OT or FV, you can ignore this and bind in the Nexus, and take the Dreadlands spire to where you need in Kunark.

Velious is nice because you start off the same as anyone else faction wise. I went with dwarf/dragon alignment because I've always been in a small guild that currently can not do much more than the dwarf armor quests and I like the tradeskill quests like the shawl. Thurgadin also is good for tradeskilling and a lot of the old world areas accessible to a dark elf right away are not so hot in comparison.

Shadow Haven/Bazaar are also nice for dark elves, as they have a lot of vendors but are not KOS. You will have to run the trade commission Henry quest several times to get all the merchants to deal with you, however.

I have not gone there but I know that Katta and Sanctus Seru both start KOS to dark elves. Significant faction work would be required to do those quests or to visit there safely, I imagine.

To answer your second question, I still try and wear as much plate as I can at level 54. Plate AC is important for a cleric as they get aggroed from heals and need to be able to withstand a few blows while they get a root or divine aura or heal off. I would suggest that you try and get your wisdom high, but try not to compromise your AC too much while doing so.

I wouldn't worry about fixing faction in good cities for the most part. But it really is dependant on where you will spend most of your time levelling up. You can go from level 1-60 without crossing more than 3 zones from Neriak if you so desired - although that wouldn't be very fun.

Most of the old world fun dungeons and zones are conveniently located to evil banks. The only difficulty is if you hunt a lot in Unrest, which many clerics do. The Nexus portals with the Luclin expansion have improved many travel issues, so even hunting in Unrest you can get to a bank by running the short distance to Greater Faydark and taking the Scion up to Nexus, then zone to Bazaar or Shadow Haven.

Invis potions are your best friend. You can purchase them in your home city of Neriak as well as in Shadow Haven (with minimal faction work as listed above). Dark elf clerics can also HIDE. Use this to your benefit. While you cannot move while hidden, it can save you from roaming KOS mobs while you meditate for the next fight.

Good Luck!! You've chosen wisely
