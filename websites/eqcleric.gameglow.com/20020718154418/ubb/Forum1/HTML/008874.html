This is often on a very touchy subject with clerics. As a general rule, clerics make very little money on a daily basis (despite people who think we make bazillions of pp doing rezzes... a lot of us don't charge =P)

I'm sure every cleric out there has been stiffed MANY times on peridot costs. Most non-clerics don't realize the peridot costs involved in keeping 6 people symbolled for a night of xping. It's roughly 65pp an hour, using Naltron or Marzin.

HOWEVER, the cleric you met with was being totally unreasonable IMO. Sebilis has so many spell/gem/sellable item drops that a night's xp can easily net each member of a group hundreds or thousands of pp after selling/splitting.

The fairest solution, and the one I've seen the most often in Seb, is to have someone master loot, then sell & split all sellable drops after you leave the zone. Reimburse the cleric in your group (or any other class that buffed with reagents) for the number of peridots they used for the night, then split the rest of the pp evenly between all group members. Divide any nice item drops or rare gems as you see fit. (random, NBG, whatever)

My personal best was doing the Nec/Guard camp for 8-9 hours one night. We got almost 30 "rare" gems (9 BDs, 11 diamonds, 7 black pearls), over 40 other sellable gems, a lammy and an RBG, plus several of all the common drops of the named in that camp. Total pp split between 6 ppl was something like 1600pp EACH, plus we each got 4-5 rare gems and rolled on the items. After they reimbursed me for peris, of course =P

Defaulting all gem drops to the cleric is pure greed IMO. If even one blue diamond drops in a 4hr xp night, he's made his peridot cost back PLUS another couple thousand pp. (assuming BDs still sell for 2-3k hehe)

Just tell him he'll be fully reimbursed for his peridot costs before the rest of the loot is split. If he argues, ditch him, he's being greedy.

When I am in Seb (and often other places too) I usually am the ML for the group. Here is how I normally divide it up...

- PP and GP split to the group as it accumulates. I keep little to none of the cash for myself, except SP...that's drinking money. CP gets destroyed.

- Rare gems and items get rolled on, unless someone can convince the group otherwise. Usually, no one has a problem with class armor going to that class by default if they need it, but I have seen some pretty big arguments over non-specific items like Lammies; I just put my foot down and say SHUT UP AND ROLL when this happens.

- Research stuff and spells default to the class that needs them, if they're present. Otherwise the research items get destroyed, and extra spells go into the pool for rolling.

- Non-rare gems and jewelry, I keep enough of for myself to cover peridots and maybe a bit more, especially if I didn't win any item rolls. After all, if I don't make SOME profit, I'd never be able to buy spells or upgrade my gear. BUT, I am always sure to split a few trinkets to each group member as well. Everyone should walk away from my group with SOMETHING, even if it's just a gem or two. And you'd be surprised how far this bit of generosity goes in terms of keeping people happy. You should have seen the look on a certain enchanter's face when I turned around and handed her two fire opals and a couple of extra spells that no one else wanted...
