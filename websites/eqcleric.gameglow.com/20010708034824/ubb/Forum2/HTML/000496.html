The thing that can't be overlooked is the masive efficency of a Complete Heal. Something that in my mind that good clerics look at with regard to when to cast what isn't just how many HP someone has, but the Rate of Damage. If someone is losing HP very rapidly, you drop either a Remedy or a Divine Light on them. If it's a slow progression, you do a CHeal, and you an mop up or stabilize with Celestial.

For instance, we have a monk pulling on a raid. She's FD'd after a pull and at 2 bubbles of health, and stable. I could Celestial her, bring her up to 4 bubbles and say good enough, or I can hit her with a Remedy to top her off as you should on pullers. However, since she was stable, why not cast a complete heal? You aren't in danger of having her die, and she can be up and ready to pull in 10 seconds, as opposed to 4.5+24+3+ decision and thinking time.

Alternatively, if I have a SK and a ranger in my group, and they are slowly losing HP in a fight, and I feel that the fight is well controlled and the risks mitigated, then you bet I'm going to wait to CHeal them. On the other hand, it they are mezzed mobs or casters, or the possibility of adds, I'll toss a Celestial at them a lot earlier, knowing that things could get bad with little warning.

Obviously though, they are set rules as well. Chanters get a Remedy at the first HP loss. Chanters can lose SO many HP in 3.5 seconds it's sick - sometimes if I have the mana and the chanter is AOE mezzing, I'll hit them with a celestial just as the pull comes in, even though they are at full health. On the flip side, if things are controlled, I may let my tank get down to under a bubble before I even start a CHeal, particually if I'm low on mana.

Being a good cleric is a lot more than 'cheal the tank at 2 bubbles, remedy casters, celestial everyone else'. It's a combination of mana control, knowing and estimating rate of damage, analysis of the potential danger in your current combat, awareness of what might be roaming toward you from around the corner, and in case of really good clerics, proactively helping chanters with stuns and nukes to boot.

A robo-cleric can get very boring, and you can slip by to high levels just by watching those red bars. A well-played cleric on the other hand as every bit the situation awareness of an enchanter, and is almost as busy.

I use complete healing on all classes all the time, you just have to be very aware of aggro, and your group has to trust you and work with you to make sure you pull it off.

I find remedy heals an insignifigant amount, especially on anything but a wizard or a mage. Divine light is a nice heal, but a terrible spell, it still fizzles too much and it's frighteningly inefficient. Celestial is nice during downtime and to heal someone who isn't aggro'd and doesn't merit a cheal, but it's too slow to be a good combat heal most of the time.

Generally, the only heal spells I use are cheal and heroic bond. I'll also use remedy when I don't have time for a cheal, but people I group with are pretty good at managing aggro, most of the time. I keep celestial memmed for those annoying situations where you really want to cheal two different party members, but only have time to cheal one safely.
