MY epic adventure has been a series of ups and downs.

First I swore I would never do the epic. I held off for ages. Then I figured I would start it and see how it goes.

Burgurle took me like 3 hours? Wasn't long at all. 5 or 6 PH's. Had a friendly rogue in the zone help out a bit, and also helped me with the Spirit of Flame part (I was busy trying to root/atone Natasha, so the rogue killed it).

Then I went to Sol A. I took my wife with me, since she was like level 26 (shadow knight). We screwed around for like 3 or 4 hours, got Gimblox a couple times (messaging clerics to come loot) and got my wife to level 30.

So, all of about 6 or 7 hours invested in this epic. After that, We got together some people from my guild and did a serios overkill on the Plasmatic Priest. That took about 1 hour all told.

Ixiblat Fer was another story all together. We set up a raid, but only about 1/2 the people showed up. Well, we tried it anyway. Total wipe out. The word went out, and people started to flock to BW. Turns out, I was more known, and more liked than I ever thought. People came from all over the world. Our raid went from 15 people to 45 people. We had to turn people away. Took Ixiblat down for me, then took him down 4 more times for the other clerics who were there. It was a good day for me. That took about 5 hours total, all told.

So, here I am, 2 pieces short of my epic, and only about 13 or 14 hour invested.

Now, the down part. 11 times, I have been on Chardock Royals raids. 11 times, we end up summoning corpses and giving up before i get my note. That raid, from my experience, requires absolute perfection. You are allowed ZERO mistakes. All it takes is one mistake and you are on CR. 1 Sarnak gates on you, 1 broken mez not recovered in time, 1 snare resisted/wore off, 1 over pull, 1 person goes the wrong way, 1 person not using /assist, 1 mistake, of any kind, and you are doing a CR.

The only "successful" Overking raid I ever saw, was months ago, for another cleric. That raid killed the king, looted the note then summon corpses. They called that one a success, because they managed to loot a note before the wipe out. Since we were supposed to stay for 2 respawns to get my note, I was not able to get my note that night. So 1 out of 11 raids a "success" and I still need a note.

I think Ragefire will be anti-climatic compared to Chardock.
