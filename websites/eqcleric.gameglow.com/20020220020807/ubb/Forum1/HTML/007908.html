It can be a disadvantage, but you will make up for it in the end. Playing untwinked I would guess your looking at a hit of something like this (and this is from memory, I'm sure someone will know more concrete numbers)

from L30 to 51 where mana is ~10 points per wisdom point. 95 Gnome vs 118 Dwarf = ~230 mana less than a Dwarf and even a tad bit more less than a pasty faced high elf.

230 mana can be pretty significant, especially non twinked, but if you spend time making money on trade skills and such and buy gear out of your hunting range or if you plan your hunting around gear upgrades for maximum Wis/Mana bonus's you'll probably do fine.

You will eventually be able to make up for the Gnome racial disadvantage, but I would think you'd certainly 'notice' the lack of mana compared to other clerics especially until about the low 50's.

Actually, I consider a smaller starting pool of mana a bonus, not a handicap.

First, Mana and wisdom items are easy to get in the higher game, when you need them the most, so no limitation there.

In the lower end game however, a smaller mana pool FORCES you to learn how to manage/conserve your mana at a much earlier time, thereby allowing you to effectively do more with that high end mana bar that you WILL eventually get.

Having a smaller mana pool and an exp hungry group quickly shows you the benifits of such tactics as buff staggering, hyper-efficient healing, and pure guts. A high elf with 200 wisdom at level 20 may be bored in the same group that a gnome with 100 wisdom would have a blast in, as it stretches the abilities and interest of the gnome more.
