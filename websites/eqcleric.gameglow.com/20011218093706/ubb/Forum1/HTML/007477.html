I have purchased over 1.3 GB of RAM this week lol. Added 256 to my laptop (384 now), added 512 to my main desktop (768 now), added 256 to my wife's computer (384 now), and added 256 to my dad's computer (384 now). *whew*

Thankfully all 4 pc's are running better for all programs, not just EQ. I'd suggest for all those who are asking about specs, that you make sure to have at least 256 MB RAM. You'll see the most performance increase by upgrading (in this order) RAM, Video Card, Processor, Motherboard.

I'll explain the RAM difference because I think it's important. When your computer runs out of RAM memory (the chips) it begins to cache-swap to your hard drive. This is where it takes parts of the memory and saves it to a file, and then reloads the file when it needs that part of memory to compute. This is why your hard drive starts going nuts when you have lots of programs open and switch between them.

RAM speed is approx 1000 times faster than a hard drive. That's why it's such a performance killer to have too little RAM.

At today's RAM costs, even the broke college guys can do this too. From Dell the 256MB of RAM varied from $44 - $150 depending on the model (laptop memory is more expensive than desktop, and the newer RDRAM is more expensive than the older SDRAM -- check your receipt to get the specifics of what you have). Also, you can purchase it from other distributers (www.tigerdirect is a good one) and save about 40% over the brand named guys.

I went through Dell to ensure my warranty remained valid, but if you have a home-built PC then that isn't necessary. The same manufacturers build the RAM no matter whose name is on it when you get it.

I think people are miffed because going from a game that recommends 64 MB to one that recommends 512 MB is a pretty big leap. We'll forgive Verant if SoL delivers all that the hype says it will.

Lastly, upgrade to DirectX 8 (or 8.1). I've noticed a measurable increase in performance with that upgrade alone. It'll be required as of Monday anyway. You can get it from www.microsoft.com/directx
