Hello MadMan

I agree with everybody here that you have so much to learn that you will be a danger for your group for a long time.

But I don't think that will make you cancel the cleric, so to make you less of a danger, I will give you a few advices:
- Read through the descriptions of every spell, technique, etc. - everything you can find. At least here on EQcleric and on EQCasters ( http://eq.castersrealm.com/ ). You need to learn things like assist-healing, proper buffing, etc., and EQCleric is a goldmine of information for you.

After that:
- Go to a newbie area and start powerlevelling somebody. This will give you some better feeling of what the spells do and how long time they take to cast. You will also learn that some of your best buffs will not work on lowbies :-)
- Move up to a mid level area (lvl 25-40) and powerlevel some more. Could e.g. try first LoIO, and them move to OT.
- Try some soloing of light blue stuff
- Do everything else you can think of that will make you learn the spells, but not bring other people in danger.

At the same time, you can earn some money e.g. from ressing people, and buy some equipment to start with.

When you feel confident that you got the basics, go find a tank on your own level. Tell that you are a 'newbie' and if that does not scare the tank away, go somewhere and kill things. At your level, a place like the Giant Fort in BW could be good - or the spectrals in TT - or some other low risk place with easy single pulling.
After some low-risk duoing, you can e.g. try a group right inside KC, etc.
Build up your healing and buffing skills slowly that way.

Be careful when you enter a new area. You do not know which factions your cleric has screqed up (e.g. from hunting in Plane of Growth), so always approach a town carefully - conning the guards and citizens.

Last advice: For the first many days you group with people - be honest. Tell people that you are less than 100% skilled with the cleric. Their lives depend on you, so they deserve to know the situation. It will probably also make them pull a little more slowly, so you will loose less people.
