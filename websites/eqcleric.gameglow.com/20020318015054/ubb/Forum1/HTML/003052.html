Since you don't have the time for the camp, you will probably have to pay huge sums of cash to get the items/plat to trade/buy what you want. Mammoth cloaks on my server were going for about 2500. GE boots are around the 5 digit figure as is the paw. Sometimes people are willing to sell some of the individual rings needed for the paw, but usually not the tough ones. With the aegis quest, it's a lot of luck. I spent many hours there in a row camping for Grimrot. Finally I gave up camping for Grimrot and decided to get my faction high enough. Then by per chance I happened to be doing a res in the NK area and just decided to pop in on the Grimrot camp and see if I could get lucky. I was extremely lucky that day. Was another high level cleric, a low level cleric and a wizard camping at that time. The wizard was just helping the 2 clerics. Both clerics had been there earlier when 2 versions of the dead Grimrot had spawned so they both had the heart. I was lucky enough to already have the scythe, so I just hung out with them for a short period of time. We ended up killing 2 versions of the live grimrot and 1 dead grimrot about 30 mins after I got there. So a lot of the camp is just pure luck.

The best thing to do to raise cash would be get a warrior partner and go hunt giants. Giants roam around in oasis, south ro and rathe mountains. To get giants to spawn in oasis and south ro, kill the giant scarabs. They tend to make the giants spawn quicker. I've heard that killing the lizards in the rathe mountains makes the giants there spawn quicker, but that is just speculation - not a proven thing as far as I know.

Hiya, Meddik! *waves*

Sorry to hear about your guild. If you have trouble finding people to hunt with, get in touch in-game, love to have you along.

Since I'm on your server and recently went through the process of evaluating trade offers for a FBSS (I rolled a 92!!! NO WAY!!!), let me offer a couple of recent opinions for your consideration.

-The Aegis of Life really is worth the time. You can do it in short shots, don't have to get it all at once, and besides, you're not likely to get both pieces in one camp anyway. If you camp it with a ranger or druid (you do need some help, live Grimrot is a bad mutha), they can pull cyclops and the like for experience while you whack putrids for the faction you'll need for the quest. Obtaining it was perhaps the most satisfying quest I've ever finished (and it's cool to be called a Defender of Life by the paladin guildmaster in Qeynos when you're an ugly dwarf , and WHAT an item!

-You just got JBoots! Shut up! *slaps self* Sorry, my insane jealousy is sometimes difficult to control.

-The Paw seems to go for around 5K in our bazaar, but I don't see many go up for plat. Mammoth cloak is probably 3-4K if someone sells it, but they almost always go as a trade. EBoots practically never get sold. A druid friend of mine put together 15K plat and has had no luck finding a taker.

-Lower Guk has noticably opened up on our server, and clerics are always in demand. I ended up getting an abalone gorget and a Paw for my FBSS. All it takes is a little luck in the Arch-magi, King, Sage, Supplier, or Frenzied rooms (and maybe others) and you're in the money.

-Finally...I know this sounds hypocritical, but through my recent acquisitions, I've learned how hollow item attainment is as a primary objective. I netted 15 WIS and 13 AC from my new items, and you know how much mana I gained at my level (after substracted mana lost from +mana items I was replacing?) About 60. 60! Not even enough for a REGULAR heal. Honestly, it felt hollow- I wondered, was this really all there is? Then I ran into a naked half-elf ranger on the docks in Freeport. Without presumption, he shared his sad story with me, of being killed in Crushbone while bound in Freeport. Without saying a word, I abandoned my plans, gated nearby, found his corpse, and rezzed him, saving him at least 30 minutes of travel and probably a couple hours of exp. Felt 1000 times better than putting that shiny stuff on. Just go have fun with your friends- if you haven't spent much time in Guk, so try it and see if you like it. If not, go do whatever you enjoy with the people you enjoy doing it with. If you haven't done the cleric armor quests, I had a blast doing them and highly recommend them. And I got to 202 wisdom wearing a full set of cleric armor, too, so don't let anyone tell you it sucks.

Look me up anytime, friend.
