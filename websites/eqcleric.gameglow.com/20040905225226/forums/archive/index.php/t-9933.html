Well, the other clerics in your guild may prefer running a warrior alt or something during those raids. You may not know, in a big guild, which alt belongs to which cleric.

In my opinion (as a 56 cleric), raids are a lot of work, stress, and expense for clerics, without a lot of fun and reward, and if someone's primary character is a cleric, and if that primary is the only character they have that is high enough level to go on raids, then they will attend as a cleric.

But if they have a primary or alt that is not a cleric that is big enough for raiding, they will generally prefer to go in that non-support role. I believe it is less exhausting, less expensive, and more enjoyable to swing a sword on a raid than be a healing dispenser.

Just a theory, of course, but if you had the option to go as a warrior, paladin, rogue, monk, or non-cleric caster, would you? I would be tempted, myself, because except for enchanters, it would have to be rather easier and more fun to do so.

As a meleer, you've no buffing to think about, no need to stare at the health bars without daring to blink, no reagent costs, no problems taking a restroom break, no medding, no mana worries, no reporting heals, no guilt if the tank dies, etc.

Here's a suggestion: when a raid is planned, show up fashionably late. Chances are someone else will get pressed into duty (complaining all the way, no doubt) as a cleric because the guild will figure you can't attend. Flush those hidden clerics out of the woodwork.

After you've done a little "flushing", don't be surprised if the person who replaced you quickly asks to switch to an alt "now that you've shown up."
