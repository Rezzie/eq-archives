The biggest thing I have seen with Win2k and hardware is, if you use the standard settings during install, IRQ 11 will be shared by your audio card, video card, USB ports, and about half a dozen other pieces of hardware.

To correct this, I went into BIOS and set the option of "PnP aware OS" (Plug and Play aware Operating System) to "NO".

Then in the hardware manager find the item that says "Computer". It will say it is a system that uses a 4 letter acronym that escapes me right now, but I think it starts with an "A". Update the driver for that and tell it to show you all the options for that device. Select "Standard PC", and allow it to find the drivers for that, it may ask for the disk.

Anyway, after it does that, you will have to re-install all your hardware drivers, sound, video, lan card/modem, etc.

When you go back and check the IRQs, your video and sound card will be on their own IRQ and not shared.

I had to go through all this when I upgraded my CPU and sound card. I was having a horrible time staying in the game with it locking up, crashing to the desktop, or other nasty things you don't want to happen when you are staring at mobs in Dreadlands.

I wish you good luck getting things sorted out and all. I hope this wasn't too vague or hard to understand. If any part of this is over your head, find a good friend that is a techie and get him to help you out.
