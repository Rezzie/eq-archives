Get azure sleeves for the arms. 12 ac, weight 1.0 lb. Better than quest arms, and IMO the 12 ac from this is much better than the 3 wisdom from platinum armband. My personal rule of thumb is AC+wisdom decides how good an item is ( with other stats and weight being a consideration as well). But I'm an AC freak... I would definetly say that AC + 2*wisdom is another good rule to go by, assuming you aren't an ac freak. If you are losing more than 2 ac to gain 1 wisdom I think you are using the wrong piece of gear.

52 * 190 / 5 + 0 + 0 (not over 200 wis)= 1976 mana

Zsalshun,

With regards to your original quest to get more AC and Wis without squashing your fragile Tier'Dal, here are some affordable suggestions.

Get yourself a new shield. The Cracked Darkwood shield has a +9STR bonus on it. This alone may solve many of your weight issues. I used this shield until I could afford...

The Charred Guardian Shield with a WIS9. I had to take a bit of a weight allowance hit (I think I was 75/79 with no loot on me at the time) but you can easily get a STR buff to compensate. Don't let a lack of strength deter you from getting the best AC/WIS stuff you can afford.

You can buy yourself weight-reducing backpacks which will help alleviate the cost of dragging your spare equipment, supplies and loot around. Check your local bazaar for pricing on those types of items.

I would definitely look into upgrading to the 4WIS/2STR rings (Golden Ruby rings, I think they are called) which would get your stats up in both WIS and STR catagories. Most folks go for the 6WIS and as such, you can get the golden rubies for a song.

Dump the chest and belt you have and look for a Thick Leather Apron (AC8, weight 1, 30 plat) and either a Foremans Tunic (WIS3, STR3, 200 plat) or Bloodstained Tunic (WIS4, 350 plat). This combo will get your weight down and your WIS up while maintaining your current AC.

The new weapons made by smithing can produce an enchanted mace which has +2 WIS on it. It's a respectible hitter and may be worth trading in your current weapon. Look into this as it won't affect your AC.

Remember - you are a group-oriented class. With a little luck, you will find a group that is able to take care of themselves while hunting. This being the case, they should also be able to taunt things off of you if you do aggro after a big heal. AC is important, but not as important as going dry on mana while in a big battle. Try to keep your WIS as high as you can WITHOUT sacrificing too much armor. It's a fine balance but you will find your sweet spot - the point at which more AC won't be worth the loss of mana.

Now.. as far a GETTING the cash to pay for these wild-eyed dreamy items:

I know that the platinum doesn't flow like water at your level, but there are things that you can do to maximize your current income.
Here are a few suggestions for fast, easy cash at your level:

Load up on platinum (maybe 120 or so), bind yourself at a bank and go to Cazic Thule/Sol B/Mistmore/Unrest dungeons and auction "Buying Fine Steel Weapons 3 plat each". Most folks will JUMP at the opportunity to trade in the very heavy FS loot for a few platinum while not having to leave the zone. You should be able to make about 2 plat per transaction as profit since you can sell these to the vendor for 5 plat each. If you have the time, newbies will buy the FS weapons for 6 to 8 plat. Within a couple of hours, you should be able to rake in QUITE a hefty profit. (Be wary though - stay AT the zoneline and if you hear ANY mobs nearby, just zone out. At your level in those high-level dungeons, you won't last long if caught in a train.)

If you want to get a little dirty, head to Lake Rathe, just inside the South Karanas zone area. Gnolls and undead spawn at the stone ring there. They also drop Fine Steel weapons and as well as a few neat little items and the occasional bronze armor. At your level, you should be able to solo well here, burning down the undead and even getting some experience from the greater zombies and embalmers here.

I'm not entirely certain that finding a balance is ALL that tough, to be honest. In all my time played, I can count on one hand the number of times where I REALLY needed a few more points of mana in order to save a party member. On the other hand, I can't count the number of times that I've gotten EXTREMELY beat on and yet managed to survive, no doubt in part because of my Armor Class. And we're also blessed with a pretty large range of equipment that will help us get to a reasonable Wisdom level while still having a good AC.

Right now, at 50th level, I have a 170 wisdom with +95 mana and an 871 buffed AC, with no EM. I'm also missing the Aegis of Life, Strathbone Shell shield, belt of the cenobite, and a couple of other non-planes pieces that would seriously improve both my AC and my WIS.

In order to get up to 200wis, I would have to seriously eat into my AC, and my overall effectiveness would really not improve much at all:

170 wis +95 mana = 1895 mana
200 wis +95 mana = 2195 mana

That extra mana means I can cast 5.5 complete Heals instead of 4.7, or 8.8 Superior Heals instead of 7.6, niether of which has seriously affected me- the last time I lost someone because I was OOM was well before CH became pracitical to use.

If you want to apply mana to real situations, also consider this- you will be medding during a fight. Regardless of how much WIS you have, you will always med back the same amount of mana. Say, for example, that dyuring a fight your medding gets you back 300 mana or so. You can basically add this to you total:

1895 + 300 = 2195

2195 + 300 = 2495

Now, the 170 wis can cast 5.5 CH's, the 200 can cast 6.2. Not much difference, eh? Now consider this- it's general considered reasonable to assume that adding 2AC will reduce the melee damage you take by about 1%. From what I can see of my get-up, I would have to sacrifice about 80points of AC to get to 200wis, barring infusion of some uber-items. That means I'll be taking about 40% more damage on average from each attack. In other words, I've gone down from having 1600hp (my normal) to an effective 1150hp in terms of survivability. Is losing 450hp really worth a mana gain that doesn't even allow you to cast one extra CH?
