Things Clerics need to improve gameplay are not very hard to see.

Our biggest albatross is that when compared to others, we are compared as if we were in a full group, because that's where they expect us to be.

There is no arguement from the dev team that this occurs, because they cannot see any changes to clerics that wouldn't affect end game balance.

A big hurdle for them to consider, but then they turn around and "adjust" individual caster classes, and tweak their own benifits further leaving the cleric behind on the single player scale.

It's that single player scale that makes the game very tough at points, very frustrating, and many times forces folks to quit, switch classes, and in turn forcing uber guilds to powerlevel rez mules for raids.
The end game for the cleric is one of servitude by design, all the service, all the sacrifice, none of the glory.

To that end, simple requests should be tailored around perceived values that developemnt team faces. One thing we do have going for us is that there is a new "BOSS" Mob at VI and he is tasked with retaining the customer base in the face of other emerging competitors.

Clerics need to have some simple adjustments.
1- Be considered a separate issue from other casters. Period.
2- Consider all level of play, issues at each spell increase and soloability during each.
3- keep the Group enhancemnt factor that is held over their head on the shelf when reviewing the class. It's a separate issue.
4- If clerics had more flair there might be more of them. Folks level fast enough soloing so saying this would unbalance the end game is a moot point. Increase the fun factor.
5- Travel dificulties. Worst in game. Just behind Paladins in dificulty.
6- Soloing, arguements could be made, but there aren't enough undead zones in game through out all levels to make this supposed "Best at fighting undead" factor anything but a joke. Necros and any other caster can far out do a cleric vs undead simply for the fact that they have a form of mana regen, a pet, and a varied and stackeable arsenal of spells and dots available to them. Clerics have the gun, but just not the ammo, not the getaway car, so are often seen as the victim in these situations. This perception is hammered in by their other limitations and competition of other healing and enhancement classes.
7- Monotony and routine, comforting for some, tedium for others. There must be a middle ground. If a player becomes bored, he becomes a detriment to others, and the game loses luster for more than just the cleric-player. Dangerous to the survival of the game, especially when that class is basic for the higher end game.
8- Spell line up. Needs reviewed. mana efficiency is tight for clerics, with or without a chanter, bard, or necro to feed them mana. It's a limited linear line of spells. All based on one flavor of resists. So in fact clerics are in effect punished by twice if nor more than that, for this linear thinking in design.
9- creativity: Deities reflect various forms of power in Norrath. Disease, growth, destruction, decay, etc etc. A Cleric's spells should reflect this and not be a linearly limited. See below for examples. Clerics belong to one deity, not a pantheon of deities where melange magics might dominate thereby justifying one flavor of ice cream for the masses.

So this is a basic overview of some of the problems with cleics, a surface scan. Instead of argueing each and every limitation, comparing to other classes it might be better to find solutions to the whole.
Whereas countering cannot be done with a wide brush and white washed to hide flaws that have existed and been exasberated for over 3 years.

--------------------------------------------

1- Increase melee cap to 200 for 1hb by lvl 55. This is still below hybrid skill levels, and without double attack, parry, and other limitations, would be a miniscule enhancement for the class on par with the shaman's 1hp skills. (same might be considered for druid's 1hs skills)
2- after lvl 50 allow clerics to go onto the melee dmg table (original) limiting and capping dmg by class is silly. The max dmg done by the rare hit is miniscule on the whole game overview. IF not allwoing them to hit as hard, remove dmg cap, and or make for a limitation in minimum damage, so a cleric can feel he is contributing to the game when grouped.
3- Effectiveness vs greens through out the game. Green cons are the bane of clerics due to their limited capabilities in melee, travel, and adventuring-exploration. An entire facet of the game has been removed from the cleric's capeabilities by the class's limitations, further making it less desireable to play, no matter the needs for it at the high end of the game.
3a- increase cleric DODGE CAP to 150 by lvl 60. Or in addition increase Cleric defensive skills on par with hybrids.
1-50 (75 dodge) 50-55 (100 dodge) 56-60 (150) dodge.
Defense caps at 200 early in Cleric's career. Increase to 225 for more avoidance by lvl 60.
These singly or on conjunction might make surviving green cons long enough to get off a finishing spell, root, or escape via gate or running to a zone line.
4- BASH. Clerics wield a shield all their lives. They are casters too, and know the effectiveness of being disturbed mid cast by a wall of iron in their face, along with claws, fists, weapons, and magics.
At the high end of the game, half of the cleric's repetoire of magics are no longer effective (creatures are unstunnable), and already suffer the double save penalty.
To balance this out, if a cleric has to melee a caster, a backhand swing of their shield should be an option.
Shaman's already have this as an option (prekunark) as all races for shaman's were oversized and therefor had racial capabilties to stun and interrupt.
How much of a skill in bashing? 3/4 of what a Hybrid might have would be in order at least. Again this might be a life saver for a cleric in a single situation (solo), or in a single group situation, giving some flavor to the cleric's arsenal,but in no way overpowering the end game. BASH (75)
5- Flavor variations. SPELLS
5a- Classify the deities that cleric's can follow into classes other than MAGIC based.
Examples
Bertoxx-Innoruk-Cazic Thule Disease.
Brell Bristlebane- poison(earth)
Karana-Tunare- Fire(sun-growth renewal)
etc etc etc.
5b- once classified determine a single GROWTH spell- ie one spell that grows with the cleric throughout their career.
That is from fist level to 60th it increases in power level by level. For balance all damage is similarily applied and cast.
There should be one version of each type of spell for clerics, DOT-AOE-DD that grows as the cleric grows, not different spells each circle, but one spell, period. Simpler to code and simpler to apply to the current game.
DOT Example- Earth Drain (dot)(Brell,Bristlebane...Poison based) Casting time 6 recast time 6+Lvl Mana 200+(3xLvl) Dmg Type Over time DMG =(Lvlx 1.5) each tic for (lvl/3)+1 Tics SO at lvl 60 it would do 90pts per tick for 21 tick. Very high potential dmg (maximum of 1890). But Mana cost would be 380 mana.

DD Example Green Fire Strike(DD)(Nature dieties-firebased) Cast time 2 Recast time 5+(lvl/10) Mana 50 + (lvl +40)/10 (lvl 1: 54 mana to 60 mana at 60). DMG direct Fire based- Lvlx2+15 (17 to 135 max)

AOE Example Cursed Cloud (disease-Bertox-innie etc). Cast time: 4 recast time 4+ (lvl/10) Mana: 10 + 2xlvl (12 to 130 mana) DMG (from caster aoe) 10+ 4xLvl (14 to 250)

These are three examples of each type that could be added, only code changes required would be to change names, deity availability, etc. But mana, cast time, dmg, etc would be similar across the board. ONLY DMG MAGIC BASE would be changed. IE EARTH(poison) NATURE(Fire) to give each Cleric a differenct flavor.
Cursed Cloud could be Fire Cloud for Tunare, Sandstorm for Brell, etc. But all would be balanced as they would do same dmg cost same cast same way and in same amount of time. This would apply to the DOT and DD type spells too. Clerics would be distinguished by class, and be more of a tatical concern for encounters, soloing, and pvp.

------------------
Utility Spells-Self Spells

With the concerns of single players and casual players wanting to play a cleric, or with pick up groups where the maximum efficiency member selection cannot be achieved.
Clerics suffer from competitionof other healer, utility classes through out their career and this occurs drastically in two segments of the game for the cleric. This is where many clerics end up dying or going into retirement.
From 22-33 Shamans and druids often bring more to the table than a cleric, especially in outdoor situations, until the cleric gets CH. As the other healers grow in capeabilities the cleric remains linear until and IF a cleric can find a copy of a lvl 44 spell that is only dropped in dungeons way above the level they require it to remain competitive. This is Celestial healing. More of a dmg amelioration spell than an actual heal. If a spell is so rare and dificult to get it can not be considered in the balncing scheme.
Post 50 Shamans and Druids gain in power of healing and damage mitigation. Either by slowing, or other utility spells (regen, dmg shields, speed etc) That in many encounters a cleric is only needed if things go bad. The buffs a cleric has only can compete with a druid's if compared enmass, versus singly. Regen and mana regen components make a cleric's buffs non-essential in much of the game due to armor, weapons, effects and utility spells of druids and shaman.
Complete heal is a pre-50 spell and situational at best so it cannot be applied to the overview of the class disparities. There is always a way around it, except in the end game encounters, and then often, the role of the cleric may be for OOPSIES, or as a health battery chain healing and nothing else. And Players have been conditioned by the game to behave in such a way by design and limited creativity.
Solutions? Consider the following-

State of Divinity- once reaching lvl 50 a Cleric has proven his dedication to the game and to his deities. This is reflected in an aura they gain upon reaching this plateau. mana flows directly from the god to the pc.
How to apply this in game?
Perhaps a spell, or simply coding game to see a cleric standing or medding as always meditating. If a Spell it should be self only, long in duration, and increase his healing-regen and mana regen rates. The Mana cost should be negligible. Availabitlity of this spell should be common and require allied status with your own church to receive. No involved quests necessary to gain this as reaching lvl 50 and allied status is tedious enough as it is for a cleric.

Shield of Divinity- self only buff. A short duration dmg shield that reflects all dmg applied to the cleric to what ever is attacking him in melee. Dmg is still suffered by the cleric, but also by he who strikes the cleric in anger. Casteable only after lvl 50- duration should be akin to divine barrier series.

ENNUI or Sacrificial Strike- All of a clerics stamina, mana and half his remaining health go into a single blast aimed at a single target. High potential Damage, the cleric is effectively under the effects of rez type effects until spell wears off (it will overwrite any and all buffs on him up- and is non dispellable)
Done early if could do more damage to a target, but that would remove any chance of that cleric being effective in that encounter.
Damage saves would be as if a lure type spell were cast-each would be based on that cleric's faith (see above examples) Dmg applied should be on order of the level of sacrifice done. Cast time should be near instataneous. Effect massive damage strike virtually guarantees that cleric's death, if the target isn't eliminated.
COST- MANA remaining+ Stamina +half remaining health.
DMG- (MANA+ HEALTH removed + Stamina lost)/2* +100. *note: this formula should be tweaked, but the strike should be on the order of a supernova or higher( upto 3500 dmg maybe upto 5000), this is the clerics very own life force being added to the mana he channels from his God.
EFFECT- massive dmg on target. A helpless and soon dead Cleric unless target is eliminated. To make this more of a sacrifice the cleric could suffer an exp death too, which is already likely if cast on an epic encounter target already. NOTE targets likely to save vs some of the damage, but should never save versus all of the damage.

Veil of the Gods- Self only post 50 spell. A cloak of maigc covers the cleric allowing him to walk unseen(not run applies snare) through a hostile area, to succor others, or to simply travel. It would overwrite sow, jboots, tboots, and scale of wolf. Effect lvl 50 snare lvl 50 improved invisibility. Mana cost 150. Cast time 1. Self Only LVL 35+ Duration same as improved invisibility.

Conversion- An unresistable single target spell that temporarily makes that target NON KOS to the cleric. (effectively a alliance+Atone spell in one) Unfortunately the change is on the target itself not to the cleric and other in area would no longer see that target nor the cleric in a favorable light. Named mobs, gods, avatars and epic encounters would be immune to effects of this spell.

These are but a few examples of what the celric class can use, and direly needs to remain viable in Everquest and to be fun through out.
The arguement that the cleric is designed solely as a support character is not a viable arguement where in the game so many other classes have so much variance in skills, spells, or equipment that can mimic such capablities of other classes.
True the cleric is considered a force multiplier when in a group and the pace of the game can be dictated by limiting a cleric's capablities within such a structure.
But to do so is short sighted and the easy way to control the game. This makes a class dificult to play, and more dificult to enjoy in all but one facet of the game.
Faced with more emerging games and competition in the MMORPG market, EQ needs to adapt and bend to keep it's current customer base interested.
The adaptation of the Melee classes skills recently was one such effort in that direction. To make all melee classes feel and be more effective and desireable.
Clerics unfortunately have been the red-headed stepchild of eq, and suffered few gains to all their "nerfs" and limitations.
To save the game you've sacrificed part of your player population forcing others to powerlevel twinks or alts to fill in the gaps for the end game.
Diversity in capabilities and playability are essential for the cleric to survive in the evolving game. For if clerics see the game as a tedium, it will become that and more for other players in the game too.
One maxim provided by the staff on several occassions was-keep the cleric happy and the group will do well. This too should be applied to considering these proposals when balancing casters classes and tweaking their abilities.
Once again these are suggestions not demands, but common sense should prevail when they are read. At no time should any single portion be discarded, but seen on it's own merits as to how it would affect clerics through out their career in EQ from 1 to 60.
Then and only then should it be seen as to how it compares to other casters, other healers (which should be separate issues for consideration).

There are 3 core issues all casters face...

1. Post 50 mana regen is broken. With mana pools able to grow by as much as 2000 mana after level 50 this issue needs redress in the form of either a new skill or a new line of spells for casters that do not have some decent form of mana regen already.
