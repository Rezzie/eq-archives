I really just don't like Divine Light (especially in 53 because of the fizzle factor). 350 mana? Waaaayyy too much.

I keep Remedy, Superior Heal and Complete Heal up. Superior Heal IMO is better to have in your "need a largish sized heal but not a complete heal" slot. Superior is just more useful due to:

A) It *rarely* ever fizzles
B) For mana it's cheap (250)

Divine Light is only really useful in situations where mana efficiency isn't *really* a major priority, and you predict you will need alot of large sized quick heals. There is also the stamina restore factor to Divine Light, which I could imagine could be of use in some situtations, although rarely. I commented on this spell quite awhile ago, mentioning that it really needs it's mana cost reduced to 300mana to be more in tune with our other heals.

The way I look at it you only need 50 more mana for a complete heal, and when you're healing a tank what's more efficient? 350 mana moving them from 2000HP to 3000HP or 400 mana to move them from 1000HP to max4000HP? Even if you have to throw a quick remedy at a tank (175mana) to keep them alive enough to get a complete off (400mana) it's more efficient then casting 2 or 3 divine lights to get them back to full hp (575mana vs 700-1050+mana if you don't fizzle).

I use superior when I'm just "maintaining health" on a tank, or on a secondary tank that gets a little bit of pounding on them.

Divine Light just doesn't do it from me in about 80% of my grouping situations to earn a slot.

My 2cents,

I look at Divine Light as the Complete Heal for casters. If you've ever grouped with an enchanter in a high level dungeon, then you'd appreciate this spell. There have been countless times where this spell has saved lives because of it's largish HP return and it's fast casting time. Sometimes I even have to use it on tanks who are getting hit so fast that I don't have time for a CH. It is expensive to cast, but it does buy a lot of time for your party to do some crowd control, letting you move back into CH territory. It may not be useful in every situation, but it's better to be prepared when your party members life is on the line and it's your responsibility to keep them alive. Is 350 mana worth the life of a party member? Heck ya.

Well for my use I keep Remedy, Divine Light, Complete Heal and Word of Vigor memmed in Dungeon situations where Im not needed on offensive spells..

Remedy - Great "buy you time" spell to get off a complete heal on a heavy tank takin a lot of damage.. usually Remedy/CH

Divine Light - As mentioned above, Good CH for casters, saved my chanter's butt several times in Hate plane when the mobs took exception to being charmed or mezzed. Its also good as a "top me off" heal for a puller/tank who is down 2-3 bubbles but doesnt need CH. Remedy just dont cut it when a tank has 3500 hp..

Complete Heal - this one is obvious folks Although I admit to holding my breath praying it gets off in time to save the tank a few times

Word of Vigor - What can I say but I LOVE THIS SPELL! Nothing improves morale of a group in the middle of a heavy or seemingly desperate fight than seeing the entire party's HP bars zip back up to full... This spell does *at least* two bubbles of health on even the most massively buffed heavy tank. Does 2 to 2.5 bubbles on a warrior fully buffed with 3.5K HP.. and it does it to everyone. Ever been in a situation where you couldnt target/cast fast enough to get everyone.. well this spell can be a lifesaver... quite literally in the middle of a massive fight.. yeah it uses some mana but it also gives you some breathing room to med. By the time the healed damage is redone you have enough mana back to start healing again.

Just my 2CP worth
