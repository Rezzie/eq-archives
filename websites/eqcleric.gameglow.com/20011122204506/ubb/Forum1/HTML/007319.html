Wise,

To a certain extent you are right. People should be careful. It's amazing how many people aren't careful and don't take responsibility.

But there are some issues you're neglecting...

First, this is a design flaw in a program. The game is coded to drop items that attempt to go into inventory. Since No Drop items cannot be dropped, the game basically creates its own conundrum and resolves it by poofing the item. Common sense says to be more careful but coding desn't quite work like that.

As a programmer, I find I spend a large portion of time trying to make sure that the dumbest person on the planet could run it without breaking it. That's why you do things like beta testing. You let people run it for a while to find the bugs you missed. It doesn't matter how common sense can avoid the error, the fact of the matter is if Joe Stupid can screw up the software, that's a lost customer.

In this case we have a user who encounters a design flaw (it's not technically a bug, it was intentionally coded this way) and when he reports it he gets told "We meant for it to do that. You should have been more careful. You're SOL, goodbye." In other words, blame the customer for not avoiding the flaw. Not even Microsoft blames its customers for the flaws in its software.

Secondly, THIS IS NOT DOCUMENTED. There's no part of the manual telling you this. It doesn't matter how easy it is to avoid, if you don't tell people it's there then they can't avoid it. I know I'd never heard of this problem before this.

Third, there is inconsistancy here. Just about every other looting system in the game has a safety net to prevent this EXCEPT looting your own corpse. If there were no safety nets at all maybe VI would have had a leg to stand on but they don't.

The bottom line is this. CS blamed a customer for an undocumented flaw in their own software when other similar systems prevent it. It also took an act from the highest person in the company to set it straight. Somewhere, something in CS is broken. And SND.net certainly seems to have a plausible explaination why...
