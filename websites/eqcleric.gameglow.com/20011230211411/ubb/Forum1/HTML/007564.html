I'm going from Mikar's list, here:
http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/Forum1/HTML/007527.html


General Abilities:

First off, Improved Strength, Agility, Dex, Intelligence and Charisma are next to useless to 50+ clerics, so I'm going to ignore them completely. The improved saves are all nice, but 5 skill points for an extra sv 10 something is hardly exciting so I'm ignoring those too.

Improved Stamina:

For one skill point you get 2 stamina, which is about 5-6 hps at the levels you can get this skill. Plus 10 stamina is actually pretty decent, giving you 30 hps at level 60, but the cost is pretty high. You get about 30 hps just for going up a level, so unless you're level 60 already, you're better off just levelling.

Improved Wisdom:

Might be important now that the wis cap is 255. An extra 10 wisdom is now worth 60 mana, which is fairly signifigant.

First Aid:

Oooh, increased bind wounds. Next.

Improved Run Speed:

I need to see how much faster someone is with this skill before I can evaluate it, but it might be nice for indoor places where you can't use jboots.

Improved Lung Capacity:

You're better off spending a few hours camping an EB item than camping for xp to get this skill.

Improved Metabolism:

Less eating. Yay.

Improved Regen:

Nice skill this, 3 regen per tick is actually fairly nice, although it'll cost you 3 skill points.

Overall:

Only Improved Wisdom, Regen and Run Speed seem useful, with Improved Stamina a nice thing to have if you've nothing else to spend points on.


Archetype Abilities:

Chanelling Focus:

My understanding of how channelling works is that the higher your channelling, the further you can be moved from the point you started casting without being interrupted. It doesn't work vs stuns or bashes at all. The wording on this ability is ambiguous, claiming it gives you an additional 5% chance of avoiding interruption per skill level, but since channelling seems based on movement (prove it to yourself by wedging yourself in a corner and getting beat on, no amount of non stun/bash damage will interrupt you), I can't see how this skill will work. If it's a 5% increase in the distance you can move, it's worthless, since that's such a minute amount it's barely noticeable, but at 15%, it might be okay. Of course, Verant might not understand how channelling and interruptions work, and this skill could do nothing.

Healing Adept:

Only effects instant heals, not heal over times. A 10% bonus on remedy, divine light and word of restoration is pretty worthless, in my opinion.

Healing Gift:

The critical heal spell. Again, the only spells this effects are remedy, divine light and word of restoration, discounting spells a level 55+ cleric is unlikely to use. I'd say it's worthless, even if you really like divine light, that 10% chance of healing double with it is unreliable and often overkill. Useful for druids though.

Mental Clarity:

Can't argue with 3 mana per tick. Best skill in Luclin, hands down.

Spell Casting Fury:

Better DDs! 7% chance of doing 1500 with our new nuke! Well, maybe, if criticals are double and not just +10% or something.

Spell Casting Mastery:

Increased Specialization. Over 100 spec, you always get the spec bonus, and it always reduces mana cost by just under 13% (I said 17% in another thread, but that's because I'm bad at fractions, 1/8 = ~13%, not ~17%). You can test this by memming word of redemption, wrath and armor of faith. Get below the amount of mana to cast redemption, and then stand and try to cast it ever tick (without mana regen), after you finally cast it you'll have just barely enough left over to cast wrath (140 mana, you may need to wait 1 tick, as you should theoretically have 138 mana left after casting redemption from 1100 mana) and you'll never have enough to cast armor of faith (150 mana). You can do this all day, and it'll always hold true. Therefore, this skill only _maybe_ effects fizzles and your non-primary spec spells, like Aego and stuns. I'd say it's useless if it's only helping you with Aego most of the time.

Spell Casting Reinforcement:

Increased buff time. An extra 30% is nice on spells like Aego and Marzin, but this skill isn't sexy or impressive at all. It's only really useful when you're casting Aego frequently, and even then, the new group Aego might make this skill pretty worthless.

Overall:

Mmmm, Mental Clarity. The rest do literally nothing, or are completely underwhelming.


Class Abilities:

Bestow Divine Aura:

Sounds nice in theory, but when do you use this? If you use it to save an enchanter, you have no one to get things back under control (and if you needed to save the chanter, things are out of control). Used on a main tank, it instantly transfers aggro to someone else (which might be useful, maybe). Maybe it'll be useful for certain pull tricks, or posistioning, but I think it's vastly overrated.

Celestial Regeneration:

Until someone gets it, we have no idea how big it is. If it's the rumoured complete heal over time, it's at least as good as loh, but it could be pretty worthless, if it's smaller. It also requires the useless healing gift (at level 3!), which makes it a hard sell if it's not amazing.

Divine Ressurection:

Umm, yeah. Saves you 15 minutes of xping per death, assuming you can get back to your corpse from your bind point. Might possibly save someone else 15 minutes if you can go without dying for 3 real days of played time. Most of the time when I die, it's far from my bind point, and someone else rezzes me. May differ for clerics not as raid focused, so does have some use, just not for me. Also requires useless healing gift (level 3).

Innate Invis to Undead:

Thanks, yeah, I'll just mem improved invis to undead thanks.

Mass Group Buff:

Once every 72 hours you get to mass buff. Useful with group aego, but if you have at least half as many level 60 clerics with group aego as you do groups, it's just as fast and efficient to not use it. It's actually less efficient when you have more level 60 clerics with group aego than 1 per 2 groups. Really, really good if you're the only level 60 cleric in your guild, it gets exponentially worse the more 60 clerics you have on raids, becoming useless by the time you have 4-5 on the raid.

Purify Soul:

Removes everything but rez effects, charm and fear instantly. A nifty tool, but no real use for it yet, barring undiscovered Luclin mobs abilities.

Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery:

If you have the 30% increase in buff duration, you can get this and increase that to 50%. Boring.

Turn Undead:

Fun, but ultimately pretty useless. Once per 72 minutes makes it marginally useful for xping, and the ae fear part is kinda scary.

Overall:

Celestial Regeneration seems nice, and could be insanely good. Mass Group Buff has it's uses, and Purify Soul could. Turn Undead nice if you have everything else. Rest are pretty useless.

So, based on all this, here's my tentative skill path:

3 x improved regen (3 points)
3 x improved run speed (3 points)
3 x mental clarity (12 points)
3 x healing gift (12 points)
1 x celestial regeneration (5 points)
5 x improved wis (10 points)
1 x purify soul (5 points)

This could change, particularly if celestial regeneration turns out to be bad, and I can skip healing gift. In that situation, I'd go from mental clarity to improved wis, and then probably work on stamina and saves, skipping all the cleric skills entirely.

Spell Casting Deftness: (3 skill levels) - Each skill level reduces the casting time for beneficial spells with a casting time greater than four seconds. The first skill level reduces the casting time of these beneficial spells by 5%, the second skill level increases the reduction to 15% and the third brings the decrease to 25%. Bards can not gain this Archetype Ability because they do not have 'casting times' over four seconds.

Now, if clerics CAN get this ability, then EVERY cleric SHOULD. Can you say "7.5 second complete heal"? Even Divine Light would benifit since it has a 4.5 second casting time (meeting the 4 second minimum). It would be reduced by approx. 1.1 seconds. A 3.4 second (faster than remedy now) 950 point heal is a good thing.

The other ability that used to be listed as cleric useable but got moved, is:

Spell Casting Expertise: (3 skill levels) - Each skill level negates any chance of spell failure (fizzle) for spells of a certain level. The first skill level prevents failure of spells under 20th level, the second skill level prevents failure of spells under 35th level and the third prevents failure of spells under 52nd level.

I would take this second. How many times has a fizzle made you late on a CH during a rotation? And for good old exp/loot grouping, fewer fizzles + shorter cast times = more med time = more mana = you get to have fun and NUKE some.

So, if these skills are available to clerics, I would pick:

3 x Spell Casting Expertise (12 points)

then I would work on my resists.

If we can't use those 2 skills (and I would be upset if we can't), then I guess I'll just work on my wis, regen, resists, mental clarity and worry about the rest after that.
