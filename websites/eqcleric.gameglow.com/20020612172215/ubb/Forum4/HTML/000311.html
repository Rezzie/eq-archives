Ok, short and the sweet of it... I'm in a 3 person group... myself, a ranger, and a rogue, in ol' seb (ench, ranger, rogue, respectively).

We are down by the first bridge, pulling from 3 spawn jail and the front hallway... pretty much wherever we find spare mobs in the local area. I'm playing main healer atm using bedlam (mini-rune) and boon of the garou. Unorthadox perhaps but it works fine, basically just xping a little before we all went to bed.

Now, the rogue is decently equiped, has a kunark BP so if he starts taking damage after a fight he can click it back with reletive speed, but I'm still having to "heal" the tanking ranger, and although it's quite draining on the mana bar, I can handle the damage after slow well enough.

This is where the issue comes in. During this time a level 60 cleric, who is joining a pyra group (we were keeping all the mobs clear up to that point, so he was able to walk down unmolested without invis or anything), runs up and asks me for c3.

Now, I'm not stingy about c3. I'll cast it on anyone if I'm not fighting, I'll even med the mana for you if I don't have it. During fighting though, 900 mana can be a LOT, especially in the function I was playing in this small group.

My whole life as a chanter I have also "traded" buffs with folks. On the occations I soloed there were often druids around who would sow for clarity, clerics who would buff for c2, eventually wizards who would port for c3 *grin*. And being that it was common notion for me to do these trades, I figured as long as I was going to use 900 mana for this guy outside my group, I may as well ask for something small in return yes?

So, I ask the cleric for a return buff. I ask him if he will aego the 3 of us for c3... and that I would provide the peridots and 100pp to butter the deal.

Response?
"Aego costs 750 mana per cast, and I don't have the group version".

Now, I am aware of the mana cost of Aego, and I'm also quite aware that group aego is a rare spell (I didn't assume in the first place that he had it), but I thought it a decent offer still considering 1)the cleric was not fighting currently, 2)there would be no gem expendature and 3)he would regain that mana faster anyway due to the exchange.

You tell cleric,"I know it costs a lot... but I'm playing healer atm, using a lot of mana for the job, and you just asked for 900 of it"
You tell cleric,"plus you'll regen it back faster "
Cleric tells you, "Fine."
You tell cleric, "Great!"
You begin casting Koadic's Endless Intellect.
Cleric tells you, "logging, thx"
Your casting was interupted!

/ponder

So, from my POV here, he logged in a fit because I was asking for something in return for my trouble? Wouldn't you want the same? My only aim really was to maybe have a little more protection for my group out of this, under almost any other circumstance I would have buffed the guy without a second thought... but I am also an opportunist at heart, and try to take advantage of such things as they come about.

So... was I in the wrong here? Or was he? Was I really asking too much? Was his response appropriate?
