ASSIST ASSIST ASSIST!!!

That can't be stated enough.

If in doubt, ASK. Sometimes when you are going places, people will say lets move here. Then they will go.

Always announc your AFK's. Don't ever assume that because all the stuff in the area are cleared out, it's ok to run to the fridge for a pop.

Keep your group advised of mana situations. Fights in Sebilis and in Kunark in general take a lot longer than you are used to.

Keep your DA or DB memmed at all times.

Watch the path you are following. Almost all of the camps that you will go to require you to go over a bridge. There are traps or holes in the bridges where you can fall into the water below resulting in very nasty CR.

Sit where you are told. Sometimes there are strange agro bugs that you get stuff beating on you that shouldn't have.

Those are a few of the things off the top of my head.

DON'T SIT ON THE RAMP.

You will kill yourself and anyone at the zone-in with a nice big train.

Death Pact and Symbol the enchanter (good tip for any zone really but moreso for Sebilis and its nasty golems). If you don't, be prepared for nasty CR.

Keep your head. There are many times where all that keeps you alive is an enchanters mes and your heals. Just stay focused and you'll be fine. Survived many a bad pull that way.

If you find yourself the target of stuff just running up and attacking you from nowhere then camp out between fights (gotta love Kunark bugged hatelists). Better to camp unnecessarily than to have the next one show up with a dozen friends (doesn't happen often in Sebilis but it does happen).

Call of Heroes is your best friend

Imperial Crypt Golems (inside the Crypt only) can CH themselves. Be prepared to chain stun them.

Medding has always been a taunt but it's 10x worse in Sebilis since about 90% of stuff you'll fight can summon. If you find yourself being summoned a lot then DON'T MED. It's counterintuative, I know, but dying is worse than not medding.

Hope that helps.

Definitely agree with everyone else.

Remember that these fights are a heckuva lot tougher than pretty much anywhere else - most mobs past the ramp are 50+ (not all, but most). They can and DO summon like crazy.

Resists for magic fire and ice are really helpful (especially the group buffs like the enchanter's magic resist and druids fire and cold resist) when you fight wizards.

Conserve mana like you never have before. You've got to remind a tank-heavy group that there should be ONE main person the mob is beating on, and to try not to taunt off that person. One tank is easy to heal, 4 tanks gets ugly quick.

I can't stress enough that you should definitely have DB and DA memmed - one is good, two is better. With the frequency of summoning, and especially if you're the only healer in the party, you will be getting smacked. Stunning krup wizards comes to mind.

Make sure everyone knows the loot rules before you get started - it may not seem that important, but some extremely nice stuff drops in Sebilis and it is really awkward to have to explain loot rules after a nice item has dropped and everyone is clamoring for it.

Have fun. I absolutely love Sebilis and will probably rent out a condo there when I can.
