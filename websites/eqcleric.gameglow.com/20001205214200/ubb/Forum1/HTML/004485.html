Here is a copy of a post that Quellious made (54 Vicar) a while back. I though it was so good that I went back and found it. here you go:

/sit on
/sit off
/cast 1
/pause 5
/cast 1

You're probably asking, 'Why?' The /sit on is to prevent movement from interrupting my spells. Ever started casting a heal on someone.. only to find that they're out of range? Then (especially if its an enchanter) you run to range quickly because they're dropping fast... and start the spell too quickly, etc. I'm sure you get the picture .

The /sit on guarantees that you're not moving when the rest of the hotkey fires. You can have autorun on and hit this hotkey and still fire your heal.

The /pause 5 and second casting of the same spell is to help reduce the effect of fizzles. If the spell went off the first time, then the /pause 5 and /cast # will have no effect. Both will occur while you're casting your heal and just give a message stating that you can't do that while casting.

I don't know... I just found it annoying to be spamming /gsay until I got the spell off. Also, in some circumstances (in very heavy fighting with multiple groups) you might not notice that your spell fizzled right away. I rarely fizzle twice on the same heal (except Divine Light ).

I would like to know.. if its possible to put more than a single command on a line. I have heard repeatedly that its possible but, have been unable to reproduce it.

Anyway, I hope you enjoy those ideas. They work well for me!

--Naeleros (54 Vicar)
Quellious

p.s. Btw, be careful using /sit on in raid situations. You can draw extra aggro by sitting and standing all the time. I only use the sit command on Remedy and Divine Light casts. I never run to do a Complete Heal. I DO have a second attempt at the spell on ALL my hotkeys. There's no reason not to.
