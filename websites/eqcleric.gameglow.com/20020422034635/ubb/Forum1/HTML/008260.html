I've actually offended my enchanter by saying "clarity please?" in groupchat. I always ask for buffs in direct tells, and only if it seems like they're not timing them.

Simple answer:

I buff myself first, wait a minute or two, then buff the rest of the group. This applies to Pizarn Symbol (Jaspers are cheap) and any resists. The Resolution line of spells I use as a touch-up heal, so as long as people get hit often enough, their buffs stay refreshed (so I kinda just refresh whenever I have full mana on those people who haven't been getting hit).

Longer answer:

On a multi-hour crawl (e.g. PoH), I keep a sheet of paper beside me with the list of buffs that I need to time (one buff per line). Especially if I'm timing 4-5 buffs I don't want them all dropping at once (resists are a real PITA since they only last 36 minutes). As I complete a round of buffs, I jot down the time. Then whenever I have a break in the action and are at full mana, I look at my "last buffed" times to figure out if I should update any.

It works, it's a pain, but I rarely had a buff drop during the 8 or 9 hrs we were up there. I had one group member who was notorious for saying "Resist X is dropping" just as I'd refresh it on them (hehe, physic cleric!).

With updating 4 Resists on this last event, I'm considering just setting a timer to go off every 7 minutes (30 minutes / # of resists to keep updated). That way I don't get "bunched" up on resists (where 3 resists fall during a 5 minute time frame).

Fortunately, I've not had to buff groups other than myself on any of these outings, so no need to keep track of who got what buff when. (In those cases, I would just keep a seperate line for each buff denoting group 2's buff times, asking the group leader to send tells if I forget.)
