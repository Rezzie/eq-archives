I have to agree that Rathe should be your best shot. But 26 is the low end of the cyclops spectrum (cycs are about level 32). First off, with Root and Dismiss you can solo (yes, you heard me. I used the 4 letter s word in a cleric forum ) Giant Skeletons. Another option is to camp a necro tower (Findlegrob's is closest to the Lake zone). Many low blue and green critters plus the necro himself and his pet. Once you've broken the spawn (one good calm/harmony pull should do the trick) it's relatively easy to camp and you have the right tools since they're all undead (except the necro. hafta have a semi-decent tank for him). Cyclops don't become easy unless you have a group of high 20s/low 30s and a couple of them be decent tanks (keep in mind that Wrath at 29 does around 200 damage).
CT is only good for high 20s up through about lvl 38 or so (it's GREAT exp to get you out of 30 and 35 fast). The Courtyard is nice to hunt at the mid 20 level but it's also train central and you can die fast. Your best bet for constant exp, tho, is the grid (NOT the AoF group. Grid is spearate unless someone in AoF is anal) or the Lower Throne Room(LTR). UTR might be a bit high for you at this point.
Another option (surprised it's unmetioned so far) is to camp the guards outside Splitpaw in SK. They're high red but very doable with a cleric and some tanks and a caster or 2. Very good (and constant) exp. I'd recommend the front areas of Paw as well, but they are trained often as well and to be honest you really need to be 30+ to have a shot at survival in there.
Hope that helps.

26 is a difficult level because many of the camps in the world are lvl 25-30 and as you no doubt have figured out blues are alot better than whites yellows and reds for exp.
I'd forget cyclops for a start...im not a great fan of doing cyclops or giants for exp...why? because they take such effort to down and there are so few of them that you just can not get good experience of them.
What you need is a static spawn...preferably one which is easy. Why? because ya dont want to die. There are certain key areas in the game that with a bit of practice you can easily not die. I managed to go for around 6-7 levels without dying more than once. I managed to maintain a similar record with my paladin between lvl 20 and 31.
The key spawn in my opinion for this is the icy orc spawn in everfrost.
It is a bunch of 5 mobs. 4 of which are lvl 25 or so orcs with a lvl27 sk orc called redwind. It is outside so harmony works a treat.
You need a druid or a ranger to effectively get experience. A few tanks and you are sorted for the full spawn. At 26 you will need a good group to stop from dying. Soloing the orcs is not a good idea (ie someone else is harmonying but doesnt want to group so you single pull) as it can turn sour.
Once you have the spawn broken (might take a while) and provided you have a powerful enough group you can pull them on spawn (which is alot safer than harmony as it dont help when a mob spawns mid pull and believe me this can happen).
There are many key tactics here.
One of the best I had was with me 2 druids and 2 shamans. Druid 1 snares, shaman 1 malaise, I fear, then druid 2 uses highest dot , druid 1 uses the lesser, same for shamans. I also nuke it once or twice (once if ya got wrath). I can guarantee that unless its a complete burn out fear related (ie it runs off and breaks within 5 seconds) it will be dead by the time it comes back.
I have had an orc stroll all the way back after fear broke come upto me ...root me and then turn away as his hitpoints put him into the zone where they run with snare on. Was very happy.
Got tanks...aint got harmony?..Pull with fear. Let the tanks taunt them off ya and then fear all but one.
As they return fear them. I have managed to pull 3 and reduce the fight to a series of 3 on 1 fights (our favour) with fear.
Use these tactics and you can get to 36 from this one spawn alone. As you go up in level the danger reduces (very difficult to die here at 29 up) but the number of people you need in a group also reduces.

I generally consider the "bubble" next to the lake zone to be the cyclops area as only GS, and cycs spawn.
Lizardmen and GS are indeed PH for the cycs (except those by the necro tower) and HGs in the HG area. Bears are not PH. I'm sure if you asked the groups nearby to let you have the GS spawns they would as they stop giving exp around level 32 or so.
As far as Paw goes, I never said it would be easy to get a spot. It is quite popular but I've not seen many low 30s outside unless they lack the nerve to go into Paw. Better exp is earned inside Paw past 30, IF you know how to pull. That's where most people go wrong in Paw. Like any dungeon, a bad pull will train you out. My favorite camping spot is in a little alcove across from the double doors that lead to the bridge. A single gnoll spawns there but he is easily dispatched, even when in the middle of a pull. The only problem I've had there is when someone tried to pull from that room or the bridge into the two rooms past the alcove. What many fail to realize is that you might as well pull straight through the alcove group if you do this as anything from there will agro to the alcove.
/rant off
If you have a capable group and a good puller then by all means try the alcove at your level. It's good, constant exp and you can make very nice progress (and pick up some Splitpaw Hide armor to boot

There's some tricks a cleric can use to solo on the second floor of unrest, using closets and IVU
Basically, find a mob near a closet, drag it in there, close the door, and kill it. IVU, med up and repeat. You can do this with your warrior friend, but make sure you keep him IVU'd, and don't let him pull. He's got a long run back if he dies. You can bind right at zone.
Just duo the darkbones, skeletal monks, and carrion ghouls in the closets. You can solo these. Getting wolfform from a druid makes you non-aggro in the zone until you attack something. (Ever wonder why you see that lone wolf in the closet? - They're medding up)
