These come in 2 varieties, guildies and non-guildies.

Guildies:
If I'm not busy I'll try to help. This includes getting my own port and helping them locate the body. I never charge guildies, even the ones I don't like.

However, if I'm in a group or busy I'm not coming. I'm not the only cleric in Norrath, and just because you're in my guild doesn't mean I'm at your beck and call. I especially hate when I'm sent a tell in Sebilis when the other person is in KC or GD. There are some zones that *always* have a rezzing cleric there if someone either cares to look or waits 5 minutes. Why ask me to run 10 zones when they can pay someone a few plat to do it? It's far cheaper for them to pay 50pp than for me to spend 45 minutes getting to them.

I really hate when people think my time is less important than theirs or that their experience (that they lost) is more important than my experience (that I'm getting). Rezzes are only guaranteed if they died on a raid and I'm present at that raid.

Non-guildies:
My attitude is similar to most who have already posted. Although, I've stopped asking where the body is within the zone. I ask which zone and when they answer I have them drag the body somewhere. If they have the body at the DL Ridge, I have them drag it to the KC zoneline. If they have it at the KC zoneline, I have them drag it to the ridge. Making them drag the body guarantees that they know where it is, and that it's where I want it. I make occasional exceptions to those who are dead and bound like idiots on another continent.

As for charging, I'm not proud, and I'm not rich. I know that I make far better cash grouping in Sebilis than I do rezzing around the world. Therefore if they want my time, they have to pay. I don't charge to click someone, but I do charge to travel to them. I generally charge 50pp per zone if I am asked to come to them. So if I'm in West Freeport and they're in WC. That's 100pp. If I have to port then that counts as one of the zones. If they provide the porter, then they don't get charged the extra 25pp cab fare. If they are in TD, OOT, or Permafrost they had better hit the bank before sending me a tell.

I prefer to be paid in pp, gems (stackable ones preferred, rather than jewelry), or some nice peridots -- never have enough of them.

Oh, and for those that do charge to click someone -- that's cool by me. Unless the person needing a rez helped you camp Ragefire, that click demonstrates a *lot* of hard work on your part that they are benefitting from. It's like having a medical degree and charging $100 to look at someone's tonsills. It's not what you did, it's what you had to endure to be able to do it.

I have a few exceptions to charging. I never charge other clerics. My cleric is my main, and he was one broke bastard until level 56. I continued to save/sell FS weapons until 56. Therefore I know the value of a pp and I know other clerics usually don't have any to spare.

I'll rez any body I just happen to come across too. I've rezzed people who were level 10 in EC. I rez people in LOIO all the time if I'm running though there. Like I said I don't charge to click them, so if someone dies in front of me, or I just happen to find a clothed corpse in my travels I'll see if they're online and will click them on my way by.

Also, I give discounts for new excuses. They have to do better than "I died while LD" or "Some d00d trained me". I had a druid tell me that he died because "I quad-trained my own arse" which made me chuckle, so he got a discount.

Lastly, there are always exceptions. There are people in Norrath who I would drop whatever I'm doing to help out, but they are very few and far between. They are the same people who would do that for me too tho.

I like to help people. Generally I will do what I can to rez those in need. Mostly the Rez'ee needs to be polite, clear and friendly. I dont rez people DEMANDING that its my job to rez them.

I had a wonderful rez experience a few weeks ago. Playing on Veeshan as I do, we have a large Japanese population. Usually you can tell from the guild or name. I had just been ported to WC after a nice time in Seb to sell my gems and replenish my frosting supply (cali's cookies go quick in seb!).

And the conversation begins...

Sakura tells you "Are you JPN?"
You tell Sakura "Sorry friend, I only speak english. How can I help you?"

Dead silence fills the gap. Hhhmm, I dont have anything to do at the moment so I try to play "Lets figure out how to help".

You tell Sakura "Do you need a Resurrection?" (I didnt want to use to much slang and confuse the fellow!)

Sakura tells you "Yes"
You tell Sakura "Where is the body?"

Little length of silence here but Im running to Puntar for frosting so no biggie.

Sakura tells you "RathMtn"
You tell Sakura "Name, Level and Location of the person needing a resurrection please."

Im nice but was NOT going to go to RM for a 25th level!

Sakura tells you "Name, 38 ranger at zone."
You tell Sakura "Could a port for me be arranged please? To Feerrott?"
Sakura tells you "WCom dru ring"

So, I run back to WC and meet up with Sakura (who happened to be a druid!). We play the emote game /wave /bow /thank /cheer. Nice to be able to use handsignals now that we are "face to face".
So off we port to the Feerrott and we run to RM zone. I rez the ranger (whom I have forgotten his name) and healed / buffed. Sakura handed me 120pp and ported me back to WC. I gave them both cookies and thanked them as heartily as they thanked me.

Was very nice to be able to help out people that truly needed help, even with the language barrier.

Truth be told, helping them made my day. I felt like I had made a difference for them for that day. Sometimes its what makes it all worth it.

Of course there are several people that stick in my mind that I wont rez or group with no matter what. So no matter what they say or do or offer I am way to busy to be bothered. I usually take pity on people shouting for rez's too. Only if I am not busy though.

I do get infuriated though should someone have the unfortunate luck to ask me for a rez when I am sitting at a zoneline naked in need of a rez myself. No matter how they ask I usually I flame them with "I happen to be very dead at the moment and will NOT be running anywhere to rez anyone, thank you." Tunare help them if they persist!

I want a few tings in a rez request:
-Good manners. Say please, don't overdo the slang and abbreviations, etc.
-Don't assume I'm a guy. Referring to me as sir, dude, bro, or anythhing like that makes me a lot less interested in rezzing you. And unless you're a close friend of mine, don't call me hun or babe either, I won't take it from a stranger. Keep it gender neutral, or use ma'am or m'lady.
-Tell me where the corpse is. That means what zone, what loc/camp, whether or not you'll drag it. If you and me are in the same zone, I'm expecting you'll drag.
-If I'm not in or near the zone the corpse is in, I wanna know how I'm getting there. I'm not gonna pay outta my own pocket for a port to the other side of the world, or risk my skin in a zone full of aggro mobs that could beat me to a pulp. And I wanna know how I'm getting back to where I was too. Are you paying for my port? Porting me? Sow/lev/invis?
-I'd like some idea of what I'm gonna get out of this. I'm a lot more likely to take a request that's offering a donation or a fair amount of money (personally I'd say fair is 50-100pp depending on the situation) involved. If there's no mention of payment or you offer some cheap amount, I'm more likely to say no. Peridots, quest pieces, favors, and so on might or might not count for payment. If it's a friend, a guildmate, or someone who's done me a big favor in the past, I won't take any money.
-If you're below level 30, you'd better have a damn good reason why you need a rez. Sorry, but lower lvls tend to be a lot less polite, they rarely pay, and they don't lose all that much exp.
-If you've irritated me in the past, don't even ask. I do remember those who've been rude to me, trained on me, and so on, and I have no intentions of rezzing them.
-If I say no, I mean it. I do not want to be begged. Like I told one begger, you'd have to offer me 10k, your guild as slaves to do my last three epic raids, enough silk to get 200 tailoring AND some really good chocolates to change my mind. Just apologize for bothering me, thank me for my time, or if you can't be polite just keep your mouth shut and go find another cleric.
