Let me preface this by stating that I absolutely LOVE having Enchanters around. Crowd control, mana regen, and speed buffs are, of course, absolutely great. That being said, as much as I try to get my favorite Enchanter to adjust her life's play schedule to mine, it just doesn't seem to always happen =P. Therefore, sometimes I find myself attempting to hit my favorite spots without an Enchanter.

***Note: All of the below assumes the presence of a Cleric. Since I play a Cleric I'm always there =).***

The Ode to the Shaman:
And here's the "secret" (ok, maybe not so secret) to doing tougher spots. Shamans are an incredible substitute to an evening without an Enchanter. We've been doing so well with just a Shaman that we are starting to break into the tougher areas (e.g. Disco, Bartender in Sebilis, some other places I WILL NOT name =P). Granted, we still have trouble and Evac's are not uncommon, but my point is the world doesn't come to an end for us. Any time I can get premium items (e.g. Runebranded Girdle) and non-stop kills I'm pretty damned happy.

The reason behind Shamans being so useful (patience please those who already know) is called Togor's Insects (and the other variations of that spell). Severely slowed mobs just don't put that much of a whupping on tanks. Provided a group is able to pull ones/twos once both mobs are slowed the pull should be doable. Even with 51+ summoning mobs. The trick is to not damage the add. That allows for the mob to be root parked somewhere else and provided the mob has not been damaged it will not summon. Call it the poor man's crowd control.

Also, the Shaman adds 500+ hit points (Tali/Health) and some AC (Agility/Nimble) and speed buffs (Quickness/Alacrity). Once a player is accustomed to fighting with max haste (any haste item + any speed buff) fighting without less is just painful.

Pulling:
Our normal technique for pulling when our Enchanter is with us is to send out the puller and have them train the group. Our Enchanter has standing orders to the Monk/SK to NOT feign unless the pull is over five. I should state that SHE feels comfortable with a seven pull, but the rest of us aren't so we have compromised at five.

Now what do we do when we don't have an Enchanter? Ideally we send out a very high level player (59/60). It seems in Sebilis (upstairs at least) a 59 or 60 player can almost always pull singles. Yes, it hurt me greatly to watch a Druid pull while my SK watched (my pride anyway), but I couldn't argue with the results. He single-pulled us all the way to the Bartender. If someone hadn't forgotten to snare I suspect we might have worked the Chef spawn too.

In the absence of an uber-level player we usually have either a Monk or SK, so we then rely upon chain pulling. For the uninitiated, chain pulling is a technique where a feign puller aggro's some mobs, brings it close to the party, but not in aggro range, and then feigns death. When only the desired number of mobs remain another person in the group then tags the remainder and brings them to the group. The feign puller will then stay down and only return once certain that the mobs have "reset." It's very important that the feign puller wait a bit for returning since some mobs will almost never become mem-blurred and then wind up bringing the mobs anyway. I personally prefer that the feign puller just stay down. Regardless, experienced feign pullers know all this and should handle accordingly.

Oh dear, where are all the damn Shamans tonight?
When this happens I always want to curl up in the fetal position. However, here are some other ways we have salvaged the evening.

1) Feign Pulling. See above.

2) One Cleric/Tank per mob fought simultaneously. It's my opinion that a decently buffed tank of an appropriate level for the mob fought should be able to tank a mob indefinitely provided the Cleric had good mana at the beginning of the fight. CHeal goes a long ways, even without the mob being slowed. Naturally, it might be harder with some mobs (e.g. Sebilite Juggernaughts) than others (e.g. Krup Knights).

3) Multiple groups: With 2 or 3 groups tough areas can be very doable provided the groups know how to work well together. We recently had an incredible fight in the Disco room that lasted 10 minutes with about 12 mobs killed before we finally ran out of gas. The number of screwups we made was terrible (e.g. Monk brought 5 when said 3, Wizard aggro'd early and died, had a runner get away since the Wizard was our snarer, had a gater, etc) and yet we only succumbed to room respawn. We lost 5 out of 12, yet had we not made so many mistakes (either the runner or the gater) we would have finished the fight and been somewhat rested for room respawn. My point is we all gained positive XP, a few items were dropped, and I didn't have to curl into the fetal position after all. We had fun.

These are just some of the techniques we've been using so our Enchanterless evenings aren't nights to solo or go screw around in Skyfire or BW.

What do YOU do when you aren't in an Enchanter group?

Some places are going to be heavily defined by your level as you noted with the level 60 pulling singles --that can make or break a hunting spot in some cases. My opinion is, that you its not worth bothering to attempt some places without an enchanter at a certain level, much like some people won't attempt anything without a cleric (I'm talking deep into a dungeon when you are in a relatively enclosed space, lets say Sebelis or Charasis).

Without an enchanter...

On multiple pulls, bards are useful for some crowd control by charming, but keep in mind their low AC. When the mob breaks it will be all over them and will take their HPs down pretty fast. Also keep in mind a bards limited charming ability based on mob level. Their attack songs absolutely rock, and so does cantana to help with hp/mana regen and downtime.

Shammies help a hell of alot for damage mitigation and enables you to go to some places where you normally couldn't when you lack an enchanter. No shammie, you have to result to things like in the old days when there were few shammies and chanters. The good old fashioned feign pull/tag.

Unfortunately this takes out the biggest damage disher of your group as the monk can't come running back to help without risk of training the gorup. Even with a monk its not as trivial as you'll find the monk will have to /q to clear hate lists.

You could consider root parking, but what happens if your pull has multiple healers in it? Sure you have them parked, but they are still interfering with togors, dispel, DD's, healing, etc.

Necro's ST? Sometimes this works great sometimes its a death trap. I have a necro myself and have seen it ranged from 20-30+ seconds to only a few seconds. ST will buy you a bit of time, but from my experience it sure doesn't last long and the recycle time on it is big if you have those multiple pulls.

Clerics parking and atoning? Just doesn't work. I've yet to ever see atone work on a kunark mob that ever conned blue to me.

Pets help alot, but if you have a few mobs, controlling them is a problem. Hell, some places in the game you won't even use pets because of weird pathing and what not.

Anyways I'll agree there are some places you can go to without enchanters and there are substitutes, but there are also places in this game where an enchanter is a must.
