Not that this is a forum for Magicians.... but having a level 50 magician as my (currently) level 48 and quickly approaching 49 cleric....

The modulating rod is an EXTREMELY efficient device for a magician. What people don't understand about magician summonings is that these items, in effect represent stored mana. No, at 200 to summon and giving back 150 this is not an efficient "I'm going to cast this real quick so you get some mana back" device.

However, summoning a rod for each caster in your group prior to the beginning of an expedition, then medding up from the summoning, is efficient. You have, for the cost of 200 mana, stored 150 mana for every caster in your group; once medded up that cost is insignificant, and this is where the efficiency lies.

When playing my magician, I almost always do this prior to a group exercise. Our druids/clerics/buffers etc. are laying on their buffs then medding up to full, why should I sit there and do nothing while this goes on when I can give everyone in my party modulating rods, rings of levitation, summoned food and water in great abundance, daggers of symbols that in many cases are better than what they have equipped in their range weapon slots? The list goes on and merely demonstrates the wisdom of storing mana in items that can be used during gameplay after summoning and mana regen.

Of course, since the buff and med cycle is repeated many times generally in the course of an excursion, expended summoned items can be replaced during these times as well, and the mana medded back up. It amazes me that people frequently don't see the wisdom and value of these items and the wise way to use them.
