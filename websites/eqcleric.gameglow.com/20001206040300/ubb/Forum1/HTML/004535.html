Ofbac's Moral Debate of the Week –
Who is playing him/her today?

Ok, this is a philosophical question to begin a discussion on a situation in EQ. Let's have a healthy discussion of the topic and avoid flames and anger and indignation.

The Topic:
----------
What is right/wrong with characters that get played by someone else?

The Situation:
--------------
You log in and see someone you have not seen in a few weeks/months and you strike up a conversation and find that they are not the same person you knew.

Could be an account that has become shared by various guild members.

Could have become an account given to a friend (may or may not have told anyone) or traded for a different character.

Could have been an account that was sold on eBay.

Regardless of how it happened, how do you feel deep down about talking to someone you know and finding it to be someone else?

The Compelling Moral/Philisophical questions:
---------------------------------------------
1) Is it ok for other friends/guildmates to also play a character when they need a certain class as has become common practice in many bigger guilds? I'm not talking about Verant ELUA details, I'm asking about how do you morally feel about it.
2) What do you really think about someone playing a character that they did not “earn” by playing it through the levels themselves?
3) Is it really different to trade characters with someone than it is to twink an alt or merely an extension of the same philosophy?

Disclaimer:
-----------
Please realize that there is no clear right or wrong in these situations and I am merely trying to sponsor a discussion on the moral and philosophical grounds of the situation.
If your post will be a heated argument or a “just because” post then please don’t post. I want to hear well though through opinions on this, not rants and flames.

Have fun all.

I don't see anything ethically wrong with letting someone else play your character, with two caveats.

First, I think you need to be upfront with people about what's happening. Tell friends and guildmates what to expect. Maybe even post something on your server message board (although that may cause problems with Verant). If I'm going into a dungeon, I want to know if that enchanter is being played by the same guy who got it to level 45, or some new guy who's still trying to figure out what that wacky "mesmerize" spell does.

Second, I think the "primary" player should be responsible for whatever is done with the character, even when controlled by someone else. I've seen a number of situations where someone is criticized for antisocial behavior (KS'ing, ninja-looting, etc.) and responds with "oh, I've been letting my brother play with the character, that must have been him." While I don't like to see someone punished for the actions of another, I think you have to reject this defense on pragmatic grounds, or else you've created an all-purpose loophole to get out of trouble.

Interesting sub-question: Suppose the character has been loaned equipment by friends or guildmates. If he's going to turn the character over to someone else, should he offer to return the equipment, even though the character will remain? I think yes. Even though the equipment was techinically given to the character, the main attraction of EQ is that you're dealing with other human beings. If the human being is leaving the game, they should settle up their character's debts.

Generally, it's disconcerting when your "friend" turns out not to be your friend, but someone else entirely... and even someone who is unfriendly or rude! This has happened... and it was an unpleasant experience.

I personally wouldn't trust a "bought" account and wouldn't want to hunt in a dangerous place with that person until they had proven their ability with the class.

However, as my guild's senior cleric, and a member of the guild's High Council (the guild is governed by a three-person council), I have given my account information to the two other Council members in addition to my real-life partner (who is also the guild's senior wizard). I've known these folks for years (having played other online games with them) and trust them wholeheartedly. They have saved the butts of countless guildmates using my account when I wasn't available for resses.

It is an unspoken policy in our guild that whenever you are logged on to someone else's account, it is ANNOUNCED in guild-say. That way, everyone in the guild know who's behind the steering wheel. (This often leads to some good-natured teasing and joking around, especially if there's a gender-switch involved.) In addition, accounts are logged on for a definite purpose... for resses, teleports, etc., the character is returned to exactly where you found him/her, and then immediately logged off. When you are logged on to someone else's account, that account is not to be used for just hanging out or fooling around... you get the job done, and log out. We have never stated these policies, we just follow them. I think it's because we know it's a sort of "privacy violation" to be logged on to someone else's account, and the idea of trading off that person's reputation or using their advantages as your own would be unethical. For instance, if someone were to log on to my account and start charging outrageous prices for resses... that would be disastrous. It IS a written policy of the guild, however, that if a guild member wishes to sell their account, then all characters on that account would be immediately de-tagged. This situation has never arisen in our guild, but the rule is there as a precaution.

Sharing of account information is not unethical as along as the behavior of the participants remains ethical. Unfortunately, that's not always the case. Having been a GM and Guide in other online games, I've seen the "little brother" syndrome all too often. Personally, I don't let my little brother get anywhere NEAR my account!

1) If the account in question is used for utility purposes (res, port, sow, clarity), I have no major problems with it.

2) If someone else has taken over the account who has never played the game or even just that particular class, I do have somewhat of a problem with it. It is technically not cheating, but I think it's cheezy. I guess I can't respect someone who gets everything handed to them on a silver platter without any effort.

3) It indeed is. First of all, you earned all the stuff yourself (we're talking about self-twinking, not being showered by items from guildmembers or friends) so it technically is your property. You spent hours after hours camping that piece, and I think that is an investment that crosses the boundaries of virtual and real life. You (the player) wasted 11 hours in front of the computer camping, not your character. Second, I think that raising a character to the level required to obtain high end items (50+ for example) gives you a priviledge to speed up the lower levels a little bit, providing you take the time to learn playing the class properly. But again, unless you are an idiot, you will most likely learn the most essential aspects of playing any class by the time you reach level 20. Of course, I'm talking about "reasonable" twinking. If it enchances your abilities to a certain degree but doesn't make you invulnerable to mobs of your level, then I think it is ok.

Just my $0.02
