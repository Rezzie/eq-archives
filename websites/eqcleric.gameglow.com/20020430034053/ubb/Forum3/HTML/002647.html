Shield of Bane Warding
25ac 8cha 20wis 12svMagic
clr/dru/shm/rng/pal/bst
all races

I completed my SoBW quest this past monday night and I thought I would put together a small quest walkthru with my experiences. There are other walkthru's, most notably a large user collaboration one on the Safehouse boards... I am just writing my own here because it is an awesome item and not every cleric visits the Safehouse on a regular basis.

Quest starts in Katta by talking to Rowena Rhorer inside the castle (building inside the gardened courtyard near the Tenebrous Mountain zone line). Hailing her will start the quest dialog. If you ask her about the "Tormented Vah Shir" she will hand you an 8 slot container and tell you to put in the skulls of 8 Tormented Vah Shir.

The Tormented Vah Shir spawn in Tenebrous Mountains. There are 3 confirmed spawn points/location:

Location 1: NE corner of TM between the Coterie Fort and a 3 building Coterie outpost.

Location 2: SE corner virtually due East of the Grimling forest zone line.

Location 3: SW corner on top of a hill with a tree due south of the Katta entrance where the Katta wall ends.

The Tormented Vah Shir are a random spawn and, atleast in the SW corner spawn, on a 6.5 minute timer with several other mobs. I have seen it take several hours to get one to spawn and I have had 2 spawn within 20 minutes of eachother. The SW corner spawn table includes aliens and bats. They spawn between the tree and the wall.. kill them immediately. Otherwise I would highly recommend having a tracking capable friend to help you.

Once you get the 8 skulls, put them into the 8 slot container Rhorer gave you and hit combine. Take the combined container back to Rhorer and hand it in.... NOTE... this starts a battle sequence that can be very tough if you don't have friends to help you... most notably an enchanter or kite capable class. Handing in the combined box causes a courier to spawn and run to Rhorer. Once the courier talks to Rhorer he begins to run away... At this point Rhorer tells you he has been possessed and that you need to kill him and return the box and skull to her.

DO NOT kill the courier inside the castle or you risk having the reanimated tormented vah shir skeletons spawn inside the castle walls... fight the courier in the courtyard or near the TM zone.

Kill the courier and 8 reanimated vah shir skeletons spawn. They are about level 35 and don't hit too hard, but you either need to mez the extra's or kite some around. I did this part of the quest with a 55war, 55shm, and 58 cleric(me)... it was extremely close and the shaman died. We did it twice (one for me and other for the shaman).. the second time we stayed far away from the warrior (who was using a DS potion) when the 8 skellies spawned and we were able to drop them all the 2nd time much easier.

Once you have killed all 8 skeletons combine the heads they drop into the container that the courier drops and return it to Rhorer in the castle.

At this time Rhorer gives you back the box and a note. Take these to Spiritist Roleko in the Vah Shir outpost in the Grimling Forest. Handing in the note and the box will cause your second turn in fight to start. Once you hand in your items a Possessed Grimling and 3 undead Grimlings spawn inside the fort. Kill all 4 and return their skulls/heads to Roleko. NOTE: the Possessed Grimling cannot be rooted or snared so be sure to engage him first.. the pathing in here is horrible so be weary of that especially if you have pet classes helping you.

Once you kill the Possessed Grimling and the 3 undead Grimlings, turn in their heads/skulls to Roleko. Roleko will give you a note to take to Spiritist Cholsa in Shar Vahl (cat city). Go to cat city and look for SCRIBE Cholsa (not spiritist) who is in the basement of the palace. Hand her the note and she will give you back a note of instructions to kill the Rakhasa. She then tells you to gather some required ingredients and return the ingredients with the instructions to Roleko back in Grimling Forest.

Required ingredients:

Black Flame Charcoal - drops off Umbrous Toilers in Maidens Eye near the lake

Thought Leech Brain - drops off of Thought Leeches in Mons Letalis (cave of them there)

Amulet of Sun Rays - drops off "the Burning Priest" who spawns in Scarlett Desert up on the plateau behind the revenant ruins. He is a single spawn near 2 rocks between the revenant ruins and the tro jeg aliens temple thingy.

All three ingredients are fairly uncommon and will take you some time to obtain.

Once you have all 3 ingredients take them, with the note Cholsa gave you, back to Spiritist Roleko in Grimling Forest. Hand them in and Rakhasa *somethingorother* spawns.. kill the Rakhasa and return the head he drops to Spiritis Roleko.

Turning in the Rakhasa head to Roleko will get you your reward... which btw, is tradeable (not NO-DROP).


Have fun and get yourself a rather nice shield....

BTW, the shield looks like one of the large silver round Lion's head shields that the Vah Shir royal guards wear in cat city.... sexy ~

You can easily do the Tormented Vah Shir in Tenebrous solo. At 58 when i completed the quest I could kill a Tormented using 2 bubs of mana. At 52 your mana output will probably be more than 2 bubs but most assuredly less than a full bar.

The burning priest is definitely not something you, as a cleric, want to solo. Grab a 50ish+ melee or a druid/necro/wizard and he will be easy.

The thought horrors in Mons letalis can be done with you and another 50+ melee quite easily.

The umbrous Toilers in Maidens Eye... i would recommend a full group when hunting these just for the simple fact of rather large aggro ranges and the likelyhood of adds. Plus the more you drop the better your chances of getting the charcoal are.

the nicest thing is that you'll get experience at every part of this quest at 52. I know at 58 alot of the stuff was greening out to me and, except for the toilers, what was not green was light blue.

I did my quest duo (95% of the time) with a 55 shaman friend. We did enough component gathering for 2 shields at once. My advice is to find a partner, the most obvious choice would be a paladin because of their melee and the fact that a good number of pallies seem to /drool over the shield since they cannot dual wield. Oh, and pallies love to be needed *wink*

A cut/paste of my observations while doing this quest, from this thread Hope it helps, if you have any questions, feel free to ask here or make a level 1 on Drinal and hunt me down:

Did this last night and had a few observations for everyone, nothing exceptional, just some tweaking of the details.

52 cleric when I started (barely, about one gold left in it) 54 when done.

About three days spent camping skulls-plenty has been said about this part. Another three in SD camping the priest, very nice exp in 53 and 54, almost zero risk. Have been told the exp is good all the way to at least 60 (a couple guildies have dinged 60 in this camp). Alternated duoing with necro, grouping with monk, monk/cleric, cleric/mage, mage, ranger/shaman/necro-basically all sorts of weird combos of people as various guildies and friends drifted in and out of the camp. Also got some rather nice twink gear for my tank in this camp. Basically, just about any remotely viable grouping structure will work here, doubly so if fear kiting is possible. Risk vs. reward definately runs high in the player's favor in the camp.

A week spent soloing toilers a few hours a day as a 60 epic druid in near full SS. Flippin nightmare of a camp. Then I got two to drop in 30 mins /sigh. For the record, both drops were near the DP zone while the week I spent was near the lake. I saw a couple others drop over near there for other people though.

The brain was a joke after all of that hell-couple hours soloing, maybe 15 total kills. As a side note, the thought leeches are VERY resistant to changes in move speed-even though they were green and light blue to 60, I would have multiple consecutive resists to druid epic and ensnare. Root often times would break within 5 seconds...if it would stick at all. Be prepared to take some lumps if you're going to solo this part.

Skull turn-in: This part was done by me (54 cleric with Epitath of Life-750/30 sec DoT) and 60 epic druid.
Courier: was a joke. We went with the root/DoT method outside of agro range when he died so the skeles wouldn't immediately attack. We killed him out in the courtyard outside the castle, about 3/4 of the way to the hedge line. When the skeles spawned, the popped in a big cluster then moved forward about 60 game feet-at least far enough away that I could move up and loot the box with no agro.
Skeles: Blue and light blue to 54, green and light blue to 60. Druid harmonied them, but ran a test with the last two and was able to single pull them easily. Root/epic/Drone of Doom(clicky-DoT)/Breath of Ro/Epitath of Life was enough to kill each of the skeles. We both ended the fight at about 40m and 100h.

Grimling: Me, 60 druid, 59 necro, 52 tank.
Probably the hardest (??) fight of the evening. Druid, necro and I are used to working together, though. Tank died early because of a pathing bug-he was fighting on the side of one of the hills there and somehow fell for 1500 damage. That took him to about 3% health and he died before I could get more than one heal off on him to stabalize. Got the reanimateds snared and rooted and the druid and necro hate kited the possesed while I and the pet kept the skeles busy. I threw a EoL on each of them to give me the hate if root broke and druid and necro threw a dot in here and there as time allowed. Once possessed died I had one skele dead and a second well on his way. Clean-up was fast then.

Quick run to Shar Val , turn-in, gate back.

MQ worked easily on last turn-in. I had amulet and note, necro had brain, druid had charcoal. No problems encountered. Necro clocked the max hit on the Rakasha at 145. He rampaged once during the fight and I burned about 50% (from 80m to 30m) mana keeping the druid and necro alive cause they were bored and overnuked. He definately summons-heh. The druid was taking damage probably 60% of the fight, necro 20% and tank 20%. As many said before, this fight was very easy with the right people.

So, in the end, about a week of no exp, two weeks of farming, and worth every minute now that I have my kitty shield =). I'll agree with whoever said that this is probably the best risk vs. reward ratio item I've found yet, though the Cloak of Truth runs a very close second.

You can easily do the Tormented Vah Shir skulls solo.. that's easy enough
