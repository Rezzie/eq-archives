I'm idly curious, being a long-cancelled player, about how you guys managed to deal with playing clerics. Almost two years did I play, and I reached all of level 21 with my cleric, my primary character, to boot. I just couldn't take hour after hour of watching my spellbook and the health bars, so I'd spend more time exploring (and dying from it), healing newbies in distress, trying to convert heathens to the church of Innoruuk, trying to find quests, or occasionally playing other characters for a change of pace.
I know a lot of people back then would blithely suggest stuns and nukes, but, as I'm sure you guys know well, there was never enough mana in a day to keep people safe and still have the luxury of magical offense. One round of basic buffs for a party would lead to six minutes of reading a book, and every possible second's worth of meditation would have to be grasped to keep the pulls going smoothly. An enchanter or a backup healer helped immensely, but only about 20% of the parties I was ever in had a competent chanter or healer. The rest of the time I was busy pulling monsters off of the enchanter after they mana-dumped at the start of a fight. I know most of you have it worse these days with eBayed characters run by idiots, but I still put up with my fair share of non-taunting warriors, mana-dumping casters, and dr00ds who tried to be wizards.

The thought of the boredom of getting to 21 still makes me shudder, I can't imagine how any of you made it to 60. My magician leapt to 16th in comparison, would've left my cleric in the dust at that rate if I'd continued to play. So how'd you get around the boring "heal while the tanks beat on the monster, meditate, repeat" bit that I never seemed to find a way out of?

Clerics are one of the LEAST boring classes to play, as far as I can tell.

Lots of people think it's boring indeed to be a cleric. Being a level 60 cleric myself I have to say that it's WRONG! =) Sure the exp grindage camps can be boring, where there's no challenge and we just kill mob after mob when there's never any real "threat". But those camps are an essential evil of the game and are boring for everyone.

Now when you start doing tougher encounters that involve hard hitting mobs, or AE casting mobs, now THIS is where being a cleric really rocks. Ever since I started killing the old world dragons in my high 40s my vision of the class really changed, and opened up a whole new world to me. Currently, when I go with my guild in the Temple of Veeshan I have always 5 healing spells loaded, including my level 52 group heal. Those mobs hit hard and most of them have AE spells. (Like Nagafen and Vox, but they all are level 60 and up.) Mana management is crucial there, you have to time your Complete Heals correctly, use your group heals as needed yet trying to avoid it since it's such a mana hog, decide in an instant wheter you have time for a CH or if you should land a divine light first, keep everyone alive, assist on the mobs to try and keep the other groups alive as well with fast casting backup heals, etc etc... Can we say exciting nights? Oh yeah! You have to make decisions at all times, trying to maximize the use of your mana to avoid downtime. Every death avoided also means a LOT of buffing mana saved. It's a really good thrill!

Sure I am talking mostly of end game things here, but if it can give the lower level clerics an idea of what to expect later on then my work is done! =) At lower levels you can have similar thrills, just skip those easy exp grindage camps once in a while and push your limits some! You might also want to try some mutliple groups raids with your guild or with friends. One thing is for sure, while playing my cleric is by far the most fun I had in Everquest!

I have to agree with Helmgest. It's a certain type of exciting. I love playing my cleric, and I love the support role (guess there's a certain type of personality for everything, I happen to find monotony reassuring).

It's always been fun, I wouldn't have stuck with it so long if it hadn't been, but it got a lot more so when I hit the upper 30's. Up until then I made a lot of alt's for the variety and to learn about the other classes/races.

I think that the other class that is exciting (intellectually) is the necro. The hp/mana management factor is very similar to the cleric, the difference being that the necro is more mob damage oriented and the cleric is more group benefit oriented. (NOTE: This is a huge generalization- I realize that the necro can benefit the group and the cleric can damage the mob, but in my experience they are not the main roles of those classes.)

I guess my point is that every class can have its exciting side, and probably that particular exciting side that appeals to you will be the class you play most. If your heart doesn't pound when you're in a fast paced good group as a cleric, then you might want to explore other classes to see other aspects that might give you that rush.
