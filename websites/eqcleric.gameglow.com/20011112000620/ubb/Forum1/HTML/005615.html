Kadzer's guidelines for begging for "Ressurection" (tm)

(You have my explicit permission to repost wherever you find a need)

As a professional Rez Beggar it shames me deeply to know how many amateurs there are out there besmirching our good name. As a result I put together a little guide from the rezze's point of view.

Lesson #1 What do you mean you were hunting without a cleric?

If you've got the room, invite a cleric along. Except for a few exceptionally stinky dwarves I've always found it to be a positive experience.

Lesson #2 Friends give better rezzes!
No, I'm not saying go out and kiss the a$$ of every purple armoured person you see. You aren't the first, you won't be the last, it's nothing special and having someone get all smarmy with them usually doesn't warm the heart of any clerics I know. I'm saying it's always a good idea to reach out to people and to be nice. Clerics are people not rez machines. Get to know them, your EQ time will be richer for doing so AND you're more likely to get rezzed when you die. Also keep in mind that you never know if that lvl 15 ______ you helped get out of Befallen is really a cleric as an alt.

Lesson #3 Be Polite
"Rez Plz D00d it's ur job!" Ah.. no :P It's not their job, they don't owe you anything and odds are it's probably pretty inconvenient for them to drop whatever they're doing to come help you. So be polite, Ask once nicely, don't forget to say thank you and to tip well. Finally, realize that No is an acceptable answer and it's unreasonable of you to expect someone to trek to wherever you are and help you out unless it's your mom or your best friend. And yes this still applies to guild mates, friends and party members. Although in almost any case they'd rez you without a moment's thought or hesitation keep in mind that it is by their grace alone that you'll be recieving any exp back.

Lesson #4
Don't stalk the Cleric! Ask in /ooc or /shout. When you /who Cleric and start sending tells, it gives the impression you're stalking them. Let them contact you if they have the time. Remember you have 3 hours from death to get that rez. If you need to, loot all but one bag and continue fighting and /ooc or shout every few minutes. Note: Some clerics don't mind tells, but some do. If they don't have /anon or /roleplay on I'd say risk it and ask em once. If they have /anon or /roleplay on though it probably means they would rather not be disturbed.

Lesson #5 Make it easy on the Cleric! They're the one doing you a favor. Bring the body to them. If they have to cross a zone line, have the body waiting for them when they zone across. If they are getting a port, drag your body to the circle/spires. If they have a group, drag your looted (except for one item) corpse to them, let them know how much time has elapsed since you died,tip them well and ask them to Rez you when they get a chance.

#6 Learn to take no for an answer
Sometimes a cleric just can't help you. It doesn't mean they don't like you and it doesn't make them a bad person. It means they couldn't help you out because they had other commitments. Deal with it. If you're lucky someone else will come along. If not, you learned a valuable lesson about the benefits of bringing a cleric along.

Some more suggestions on how to get a res and not make clerics dispise you:

1) If a Cleric has ressed you and you feel the need to donate, don't give the cleric 500 copper and 300 silver. Not only will that encumber any cleric, but also lends the impression of "Oh, I should give him something - I'll just give him all this trash I planned on dropping/destroying anyway". It's better just offer a sincere thanks.

2) Here's a real secret - instead of offering a cleric plat, offer them 5 or 6 perdiots. It's cheaper, and the cleric (espically those over 44) will usually appreciate it more than plat. It also shows that you took the time to learn something about clerics in general.
