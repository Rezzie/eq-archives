Hey everybody. This is my first post here, and it's on a controversial topic, so I hope it doesn't anger anyone. However, I can't help but notice that a lot of people get really upset about most of the patches Verant puts out - they also seem to get upset about class balance issues.

I think I have some insight into this, both as a software developer, and as a former player of this game's main competitor, Asheron's Call.

First of all, in terms of bad patching, EQ players have little room to complain. AC is patched monthly, generally with massive new content changes, in addition to fixes to the client, and changes to the underlying game rules. Sounds nice, but in fact, it really isn't...it's part of the reason I left the game. Software development is simply too difficult to do this so frequently, and AC is generally unplayable the day of, and a few days following, each patch. In fact, they have even had to do rollbacks of entire servers several days in length - people lost items and levels - because of bugs in patches.

A related issue is the length of time it seems to take Verant to fix some things. I know it's frustrating, but things that seem simple from a user perspective can be really, really tough from a developer perspective. Even simple changes in item attributes might have repercussions that we, as users, can't understand.

Finally, in terms of class balance, I think there is very little to complain about in EQ. In AC, we had infinite character customizability, but the result of this was not more variety in characters, it was the creation of basically three archetypes (melee, archer, mage), any of which was almost totally self-sufficient. Not fun. You could make a unique character, but you were generally weaker, and no one needed your skills. This is what makes class balance so great in EQ - no matter if one class or another is more powerful, they are all necessary. Until Verant gives another class all of our healing and resurrection abilities, clerics will be needed in every group - and in fact, you seem them in nearly every group. Class balance is quite simply fantastic in this game, because there are some things that each class can do that no other class can do.

Gandwin,

I've never played AC before (only willing to spend cash for one online game, not two), so I'll take on faith your comments about AC's playability and essentially three main archetype characters.

From a technical aspect, it sounds like EQ is far better in maintaining stability in patching. Since massive online gaming is still a relatively new field, we maybe should expect to have tremendous hassles with the technical aspect, and count our blessings when it is fairly stable.

Class balance, however, is a much different issue. In a game where there is required grouping to succeed, class differences must be spread further apart (IMO). Having soooo many overlapping abilities and near-capabilities, waters down the balance until it becomes closer to AC's version.

For instance, the classic Cleric vs Druid/Shaman balance: Clerics get an early 5 level lead into healing spells, but those are mostly wasted. At levels 1 - 8, a warrior grouped with a druid or shaman will see very insignificant downtime than if grouped with a cleric, simply due to the fact the hit points are so few, that sitting and resting gets them back quickly anyhow. Perhaps at level 14, there is a minor healing difference as the spread increases, but druids and shaman both by then are far more valuable because of their huge variety of spells (shield/wolf/healing/buffs) ... the balance isn't there.

In fact, at the first time clerics should reign supreme in healing (24th) due to acquistion of Greater Healing, Shaman get regeneration which heals ffffaaarrrr more than a GHeal does. In fact, at 33rd, a shaman's regeneration heals essentially TWICE as many hitpoints for LESS mana than a cleric's heal does.

Only at 34th do clerics break away from the pack with a superior heal than any other class. So, before that level, I'll never believe there is class balance between clerics and druids/shaman.

If Verant makes clerics more viable in the early levels, then I may change my opinion.

Class balance shouldn't just be for levels 50+. But that is all Verant is addressing when they gift all clerical powers to paladins, druids and shaman.

Better a game that offers variety to all (like AC) than a game that claims to be class balanced, but is in fact only done so for a short number of levels.

Well, I think the point of the original post (or one of the points) was that "variety to all" actually leads to uniformity, because everyone gravitates toward a couple of character types. I haven't played AC, but I've heard this observation from a lot of different people who play non-class-based games -- that the ability to "customize" your character doesn't actually lead to a huge variety of character types, as it's supposed to. People come to perceive a certain character design as the "best" one, and everyone copies it.

I suppose there's nothing inherently wrong with that, but I would think it greatly lessens the social aspect of the game. If everyone's a jack-of-all-trades, self-sufficient type, I would expect a lot less interaction.
