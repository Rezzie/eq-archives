There is no way to avoid the AOE but it can be resisted.

I believe the Trak Touch is used on a set timer (45 seconds?) and I believe that it's whoever has the highest aggro. Using a larger force of people, Trak Touches are not what kills you since it sends a very small amount of people out of the fight. It's his AOE and melee (primarily the AOE).

If pulled properly, you should not have any adds when fighting him.

Finally, you want to fight him in his lair. Towards the end of the fight, he'll try to gate. If you're in his Lair, that risk is greatly reduced and even if he does Gate, you're right there to finish him off.

Fire Nukes work best.

Everyone should be casting/fighting basically under Trak's belly. If he ping pongs even once, there's a 99.999% chance that you will fail.

After first poison and once people start taking damage, get under Trak and do a WoR. Then hit DA real fast cause Trak will almost surely be all over you. Click it off once you know he's off you and you need to heal. Likely will need to do WoR right away too. Be ready to do DB again (if timed right, you can miss all the AOE's with DA/DB). Click it off, 3rd WoR... If Trak isn't dead by this point, you were going to fail anyway. He's often dead after the 2nd WoR...

Lots more info I could give, but I think this generally covers it heh.

Oh and Trak touch is on highest aggro, usually first tank and then later a rogue, ranger, or 2nd tank. Recycle time is about 15 seconds. Slow raid you will loose max 4 to Trak touch, good fast kill you only loose 2.

I just did my first Trak last night here is how it was done.

We had I think 6 groups. 4 of them were tanks, debuffers, and the highest clerics. The other two were nukers and the two lowest clerics (me being one of those).

I don't know how they got the prot single since I didn't get to witness it, but apparently one monk pulled trak back and another pulled the prot then FDed and had it peeled (the peel was to soon and we ended up with 3 Jugg adds a reet pop, but with our large force and good chanters it wasn't a problem).

After the prot was out of the way we went up to the bridge. A pally was singled out to RD pull trak back to the back left corner of lair. Pallys seem to be best for this since with DA and LoH they can last a decent amount. Pally yelled when first AoE went off which was single for 1st wave (tanks, debuffers, and their clerics to move in and engage). Then the main tank waited for next AoE and called in 2nd wave (nukers and their clerics). Trak went down just after his 3rd AoE. We were told that a 4th AoE would probably mean that everyone would die after Trak did if not before. To ensure against this one cleric stayed on the cliff across the water from bridge to run in and res if everyone died from the DoT after trak died.

When the waves were running into the lair it was important to hug the left wall tight to avoid adds. Avoiding the adds was also the reason for the pally giving his life to pull Trak back into the left rear corner.

I'm not saying this is the best tactic, since I have no other experience to compare it to. This is just the one we used and it worked well but we also had a lot of people there.
Oh yea, and as soon as Trak dies, remeber that some people are still taking dmg from DoTs so try to heal them. I lost the mage in my group post-kill and almost died myself due to lack of mana.

G'luck
