If this helps:

You do your job well. People don't say anything. You keep doing your job well. People still don't say anything. You keep doing your job well.

(This isn't where it ends) http://eqcleric.gameglow.com/ubb/smile.gif

You get asked to go places, where clerics are needed - This is a compliment, they're asking you, simply because you do that job WELL. They can ask another cleric. They don't. That tells you something.

Later on, people start getting to the point where they're used to NOT dying when you're there. Where they don't yell HEAL ME!, and they don't get upset if they do die, since the party is in godawful shape when they do...

Over time, they learn that if there's any way you can keep them standing, it would already have been done. That's about the best compliment they could pay ya, imho.

I've grouped with Machia, I was her tank once. I like tanking, and I like being a cleric, and gotta tell ya, I didn't say a word to her about her job, and she didn't say a word to me about mine, but we do discuss cleric strategy in groups, dungeons we like, different kinds of mobs, fun things, and it makes the time pass very well. She did an awesome job keeping us alive through constant pulls, we kept killing things, and that's pretty much what it's about. I would be happy with either role in the group, healing or tanking, just so long as I was having fun, and passing the time.

I guess in all my time as a cleric, I've found no comment, to be a very large compliment, if they're not commenting, you're doing something so well, they're taking it for granted. They're not checking their health. That's a pretty big compliment. Take some heart in that.
