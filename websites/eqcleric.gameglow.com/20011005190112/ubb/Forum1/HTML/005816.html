Celestial healing is not more effecient than complete heal on most targets. It heals about 900 hp over 24 seconds for 225 mana (a 4 to 1 ratio). This means you only have to heal 1600 hp to be as efficient with complete healing. The main benefit of cel heal is that it involves far less risk to use. you don't have to wait for a tank to get to 1 bubble before it's useful. Instead, you just thrown it on someone taking damage when they're down a bubble and their good to go for 24 seconds before the really start to lose hp. You can then focus on anyone else hurt or someone taking a lot of damage fast. It lets you heal so much better on multiple pulls. This makes fighting so much more relaxing that I do it sometimes just to take a break. It solves a lot of the stress issues clerics used to talk about a few months ago.

So complete is better if you're worried about efficency and is still needed if you're pulling really fast. There's nothing to equal healing 2800hp on a tank in one cast. Celestial heal is better for the not quite ticking group where you don't really want to have to stare at health bars much. It can also function adaquately if you don't have a decent group heal.

It's not quite as simple as raw mana/HPs healed. Any time that you are sitting at full mana and there is someone under full health, you are effectively wasting the mana you could be meding up.

As a pull comes in, things can be chaotic. How many mobs? Will the enchanter get mez to stick 1st time? Is the target going to focus on one tank or switch? During this phase Cel is arguably more efficient than CH. You will probably med back the mana that you used for it before needing to cast again and you aren't in a 10 second cast when a mob decides to eat the 'chanter.

Mid fight, say 2nd of 3 mobs, CH is good because things are now under control. You know who the mob hates and how fast it is beating the tank down. There is also quite a while to med the 400 mana back.

End of fight, CH becomes horribly inefficient from a time to next pull point of view. Burn 20% of your mana on 2 tanks and have them end the fight at say 80% health or 40% of you mana for two CHs and probably have one of them beat back down to 80% health before the mob dies anyway.

Ideal situation would be that the puller leaves as soon as the last mob dies and gets back to the camp with friends just asch full mana again.

Alternative is to use CH on the tank regularly taking the most damage and Cel the others. Nothing worse than having two tanks get to around 30% at the same time and have to burn 350 mana on DL.

Of course, once the tanks get to higher levels and have disciplines to play with, things change again. One of my tanks sends the group "disc" and he knows I wont start casting CH until he lands his 1st crippling blow.

Play about. Try different strategies and see how much mana you end the fights with.

Celestial Healing is the best thing to come along for clerics since pre-nerf MoK.

I'm not sure I'd use it ALL the time like Dikmer's friend but I know I use it a lot. Some good uses...

Precast for CH - toss this on a tank at 50% in high intensity situations and then follow up with a CH. You'd be amazed how much stress it can reduce when you're at 50% cast and the tank goes down to nothing and then pops back with enough to survive the rest.

Second healer - This has saved so many enchanters from doom. In the same manner it's used for CH you can toss this on casters who get beat on a lot and chain Remedy. Remedy->Ch bridge - Most clerics run around with Remedy and CH loaded. Normally you'd just chain Remedy until they were full but now you can use Cel to bridge that gap AND save mana. Remember, straight heals are ~2.5 HP:mana and Cel is ~3 HP:mana.

I wouldn't use Cel on tanks unless you're topping them off. On a fully buffed 50+ tank one Cel gives about one full bubble of health.

Celestial Healing Strategies and Usefullness

I have actually attuned myself to a strong Celestial Healing strategy. Celestial Elixir is only a 1.2 levels away and I think in general Celestial Healing has its place in a wide range of situations.

In Combination:
Celestial Healing works the best in a situation when you are Mark of Karning a target, and have a druid in group. My wife is a druid, and puts up group choro/regrowth all the time. With Mok and Regrowth, the only heals I really need to cast are typically Celestials healing on the Tank. I can keep usually near FM for the entire time we are pulling once we break.

Celestial Healin vs. Divine Light:
Much better fizzle raid then DL, and frankly its easier toe use.

Celestial healing to top off:
Celestial Healing as a caster priest healer.
Very often druids, enchanters, slowers etc will build up about 30 seconds to 60 seconds worth of aggro from Snaring, Tashing, or slowing. I have found as I begin healing they tank has regained control of aggro, its safe to CEH them up to full rather than popping them with endless remedies.

Celestial healing vs. Complete Healing

Complete heals rock for mana efficiency, but I find in normal raid situations .. WHY use them? Why build up the heinous aggro. If you are not clericing for a Main Tank or Pull team, you should be easily able to support a group with remedy and CEH only.
This kind of mana conservation keeps your aggro down, and gives you the extra mana you need to keep OTHER group tanks taking a bigger beating than yours. Where CEH falls short of course is tank healing. It's lousy on warriors and usually cannot keep up with the damage output without support spells like MoK and RoG or Pack Chloro or something.
It's still an awesome Rune Spell substitute for pullers in nasty areas .. or a good augmentation. Load up a tank pulling a nasty roon with a rune and a CEH .. gives them nigh invulnerability in all but the nastiest situations. Complete heal will never be taken over by Celestial Healing, there's simply too many situations where damage output makes it a necessity, but I often "Stabilize" a tank with a CEH before setting up the Comp Heal. Those 24 seconds of healin give me alot of damage counteraction.

So basically to stress my point: Get good using Celestial Healing, you use up alot less mana and you tend to eliminate Complete heal usage in most situations.

CEH is an excellent replacement for the crappy Divine Light spell.

You never unmem complete heal of course, but you use it alot less. Celestial Healing alot less aggro (i.e. Nearly NOTHING) vs more efficient spell with a ****load of aggro (Complete heal).

I think any cleric is shorting themselves big time, by taking a side in the CEH vs. CH argument.

The only time I cel heal a caster, i.e. chanter, is when they aren't under continuous attack. I tried to use a lot on them early on and lost them too many times. Now I use remedy almost exclusively on chanters, but use cel heal if they seem to be taking slower damage or if their charm or mez might be breaking soon.

Cel heal is nice because it can buy you more time to cast other spells. For instance, you have a ranger and a tank, cel heal on the ranger, complete heal on the tank.
