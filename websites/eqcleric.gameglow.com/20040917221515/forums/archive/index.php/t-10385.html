The problem with doing quests to level (and there are some that are very good for that) is that you're not developing your melee and casting skills at the same time. You will see a HUGE difference in how fast you can kill a rat with 1HB at (1) versus (10). Your spells fizzle constantly when your Evocation (for example) is low as opposed to having it maxed.

As for items for newbie clerics: use your first couple of gold to buy that nuke, then when you have six gold, get a backpack. Now you're able to stay in the field twice as long. Fire beetle eyes don't stack, you know. Next get cloth armor; yes, it's horrible AC but even the little piddly points that it provides add up. Someone gave me two plat when I was brand new (PLEASE don't ever /shout to the zone, "I'm a newbie gimme money") and I bought as much cloth as I could.

Next buy more backpacks and the rest of your spells. Yes, your weapon stinks but you will always have horrible weapons to fight with, and our melee skills all top off too low to be useful at higher levels. Use your nuke to fight as much as your weapon. Keep your 2HB skill trained as well. If you find a mob with a two-hand staff, keep the staff and use it. Sometimes you will see someone auction, "first newbie to /poke me at the gate gets a [fill in improved item here]." Run as fast as you can to the gate. There are always such offers going on at Freeport, one of many good reasons for starting there.
