Something tells me you should buff your primary tank some more. Use symbol and daring to max his hp's. Any L39+ tank worth his salt should have min. 2k hp when buffed.

So try learning how to use CH. Depending on the situation, I start when they have around 2 bubs of health left.

A good advice would be to make and use a macro with something like:

/sit off
/cast 2 (or whatever spell gem your CH is)
/g Complete Heal on %T

If you fizzle, just press the macro again. Your party will understand the 2nd message is because of a fizzle.

Regarding rez:
I normally never charge unless I have to travel alot by foot (unless I am very bored and just hanging around chatting with friends and buffing lowbies).

The macro I use for Complete Heal is:

/sit off
/cast 4
/g Complete Heal incoming on %T ,in 10 seconds.
/pause 5
/cast 4

The reason for the last pause and recast is in case of fizzles I will automatically recast the spell. If the spell went off succesfully when the game parses the second cast command it will say something to the effect of "You can't use this command now" but it doesn't interefer with the casting that is going on. I have all of my heals and stuns macro this way so so I minimize the amount of time I waste from fizzling. I can't take credit for this idea though I as I learned it from lurking on these boards.

If you are in a situaiton where you easily have things under control, I would suggest start practicing with complete heal now. Even at 39, I found it to be useful, and saved me time overall when used appropriately.

If you don't feel safe casting at one bubble, try casting a bit sooner. Better safe than sorry when you are first learning it. Then, as you start getting a feel for the timing of complete heal, start waiting a bit longer, until you find the maximum point you can wait safely. As you use it, you'll start to get a feel for when it is useful, when it isn't, and when you need to start casting it.

If you don't start using it now and learning it, you'll never become confident enough with the spell.

Use CH as much as possible in situations that are safe untill you get the hang of it. It doesn't have to be mana efficient when you are figurign it out. Practice is important.

Most of the time you don't have to charge for resses. People will shout the zone "Paying xxx for a exp res". Make sure they know it is 50% exp recovery. I tell people to give me what they think the res was worth. As this is often an indicator of how much inconvenience I went through as well as what they went through. If you do this, then at times you will be disappointed and will not even get a thank you. At other times, you will be pleasantly surprised.

Tell your guildies that you are busy. They can wait a couple hours till you are done, they can get someone else to res them. They should understand. If they don't, then treat them as you would any other rude person.
