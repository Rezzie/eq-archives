Theres not a doubt in my mind the best place for you to go. I went from 47 to 50 there in relativly short amount of time.
Seafury cyclops in OOT.
Heres some of the many pluses about it:
In the open.
So very few or no trains.
Sow if someone can cast it.
Exps:
Exps per mob is good or better than bats, LDC, and beetles.
Spawn time.
The 5-6 clops spawn on the island with a respawn time of 5 minutes! Plus the cyclops that hold the ancient cyclops is close by as well as Gornit the Hill giant. 2 groups can pull cyclops constantly. even with 3 groups theres usually always one up. And in my 3 levels there there was never 3 full groups pulling. So constant pulls!
Loot:
Average to very good loot. clops have about 5 plat in change and often drop rare gems (fire emeralds, fire opals, star rubies, sapphires) and jewelry (worth from approx 40 plat to 90 plat!).
No wanders.
While all the cyclops wander. There are several areas on the islands where the cyclops dont wander so you only have to worry about more than one joining in when you are pulling.
Difficulty: To me these are much easier that bat or bugs. Me and a 49th levl mage with earth pet can pull constantly!...usually it takes one complete heal on the pet per cyclops. Also me and a 50th level warrior could pull pretty much non-stop. A 3 man team should do fine.
Downsides:
No zoning. But with little chance of training this is not much of a problem. Unless you are a caster banking can be bother. But since the clops dont drop anythinng heavy its not much of a big deal.
No "good stuff".
Theres not any high level magic loot cept for the rare ancient cyclops.
Quag Maelstorm.
His a named 45th (i think) level clops and hes mean. He cast a drain mana spell, has alot more hps and hits very hard. Recommend 2 tanks.
Need to take boat.
So evils have a slightly tougher time getting there.
All in all this place is was an undiscovered gem on my server. I was hesitant to even mention it here cause its just that good. I HIGHLY recommed giving it a go. Definatly a refreshing break from the stuffy, crowded dungeons.
