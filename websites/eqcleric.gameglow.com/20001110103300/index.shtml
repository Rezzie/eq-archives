When is a bug no longer a bug?
Posted by Rayne at 8:21 AM

If you haven't heard already, Verant has finally gotten around to fixing the Max Haste bug. This bug was: any haste item + any haste spell/song yielded Max Haste (75% speed up). Therefore, a FBSS an Augmentation was just as fast as a Cloak of Flames and Aanya's Quickening. Now I must admit, this fix makes sense, oh, if it was done a year ago! This bug has been in effect forever.

Whether you have a haste item or not, once you reach the 40s possibly and certainly the 50s your melee group members will definitely have a haste item. This nerf affects everyone. Now everything will be killed slower. Sure, I can ask for SLTW (250 mana) instead of Augmentation (90 mana) to reach max haste, but then I lose about 19AC and Stamina regen, not to mention the fact that I've just asked for the equivalent of three Aug's from the 'chanter. The bottom line with this change is everyone will now kill things slower (either from slower tanks or oom Enchanters and Shamans).

I did want to touch on one point Gordon made, that is that the FBSS will lose value due to it's lower haste. Quite honestly I disagree with that statement. The FBSS's value will hold just fine. Why? It's by far the most attainable haste item in the game (yet is still a long camp). The best haste items, the dragon, god, and questable Sky items, are so rare as to not have any factor in this equation. The good haste items out of Kunark (Runebranded Girdle and Siblisian Berserker's Cloak) are very rare. I can't comment on the Sash of the Dragonborne, but I suspect it's comparable to an FBSS. The Swirlspine Belt and Hangman's Noose are SLOWER than the FBSS, so they won't further diminish the FBSS's value. Sigh, I don't see this having the desired impact. Haste was haste, the real determining factor was the stats, AC, and slot of the item, with Back slots being the most desirable.

It's hard to fault Verant for fixing an obvious bug. Fixing bugs is normally a good thing. But when a bug has been in the game as long as the Max Haste bug I'm not sure it doesn't migrate from bug to feature. Regardless, this change should have a noticeable impact on game play.
