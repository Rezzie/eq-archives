Level 51:
Death Pact
Positive - +5 atk, can restore a small amount of health to a killed player, long duration
Negative - cost 5pp component, hardly ever works, health restoration so minimal that even when it works the player is 99% of the time dead anyway, atk bonus is negligible
Verdict: Innovative and different, but expensive and largely useless.

Dread of Night
Another fear spell, just what we need. Page 46.

Remedy
Good heal spell, obsoletes both GH and SH and has improved cast time.

Sunskin
Group ITU. Only place where this spell is useful at all is in Lower Guk, dead side. At level 51 you should probably be spending your last days there if not having moved on entirely.

Level 52
Heroic Bond
Great spell! Finally we get a group courage line spell to eliminate a lot of the tedium of casting a buff on six players. Extra duration also a bonus, plus acts as a good AE heal.

Heroism
Upgrade to Resolution. Nothing new here. Unfortunate stacking problems with Heroic Bond.

Upheaval
Yet another offensive AE spell that will never be used

Word of Vigor
Impressive AE heal with very good efficiency, obsoletes Word of Healing. Invigor effect a nominal bonus.

Level 53
Divine Light
Is to Remedy what SH is to GH. Downside: at 350 mana, only in exceptional circumstances would one use this over CH.

Annul Magic
Upgrade to Nullify Magic. Marginally and rarely useful.

Yaulp IV
No use. No further comment.

Level 54
Reckoning
Upgrade to Retribution. Long cast time. Useful mostly for the soloing cleric or for the cleric at full mana.

Symbol of Marzin
Upgrade to Naltron, uses same 11pp component. Good upgrade, but nothing new.

Unswerving Hammer of Faith
Cleric Pet, suicides after every battle. Could once be kept alive with Death Pact but that was nerfed. Current version is a waste of mana.

United Resolve
Removed from game due to illogical placement with respect to Heroic Bond.

Level 55
Exile Undead
Marginal upgrade to Banish Undead with slight improvement in cast time.

Fortitude
Variation on courage line, inferior to Heroism. Unused.

Wake of Tranquility
Upgrade to Pacify. Tested results show 100% failure at 200 CHA.

Level 56
Mark of Karn
Once the Holy Grail of clerical spells, as of today has been nerfed into uselessness.

Banish Summonned
Upgrade to Dismiss Summonned. Will never be used due to efficiency conflict with Retribution/Reckoning and a lack of summonned beings.

Revivescence
Upgrade to Ressurection, restores 96% of lost experience. Effect is nearly indistinguishable from the 90% restored by Ressurection.

Paralyzing Earth
Upgrade to Immobilize.

Level 57
Aegis
Variation on Holy Armor line with long duration. Rarely useful as it is higher mana cost than the superior vendor-bought

Bulwark of Faith
Upgrade to Shield of Words.

Trepiditation
Yet another Fear spell, longer duration, more mana.

Word of Restoration
Upgrade to Word of Vigor with cure component.

Level 58
Enforced Reverence
Upgrade to Sound of Force... nominally. Actually a downgrade in terms of mana efficiency.

Naltron's Mark
Group Symbol of Naltron. Nice spell, uses only a single component, saves tedium of buffing group members individually.

Antidote
Recurring abolish poison. Very specialized usage.

Level 59
The Unspoken Word
Upgrade to Word Divine with added stun. Also known as suicide.

Celestial Elixir
Broken regeneration spell. Supposed fix reduces regen effect to 40hp/tick for 4 ticks according to best info currently available. Uses 2 emeralds per cast.

Level 60
Banishment of Shadows
Causes undead target to suicide giving no xp or corpse.

Word of Redemption
Group CH. Sweet effect, but 12 sec cast time and 1100 mana cost.

Divine Intervention
?

Conclusion

The expansion provides clerics with:

2 New spells, Death Pact and Mark of Karn. Death Pact is near useless and MoK is now useless with the latest nerf.

2 Group buff spells to reduce buffing tedium.

10 Spells that are useful upgrades of existing spell lines - Remedy, Heroism, Word of Vigor, Divine Light, Symbol of Marzin, Reckoning, Exile Undead, Paralyzing Earth, Revivescence, Bulwark of Faith.

13 Spells that are useless, upgrades to unused spell lines, or broken

6 Spells that are useful only in highly specific and very rare circumstances

1 Spell that has been removed.

Any corrections or constructive commentary are welcome.

My feeling is that the list as posted is too negative to do anything but put them on the defensive and encourage them to do nothing. As several people have pointed out in this thread, a number of the spells that Jubal thought useless do have situational use, which is similar to 50+ spells for many other classes.

The argument that I think will be compelling, if any will be, is that the original balance intended for our 50+ spells has been eroded by continual adjustments that failed to keep the forest in mind while tinkering with the trees.

A balanced mix of 50+ spells should both extend pre-50 abilities to more powerful levels AND add new abilities within the framework of the class description to extend and enhance playability of the class. If they accept that assumption, how does our current spell line measure up?

On the first count, I would say they have done well for the most part. We do get more powerful versions of all of our primary spell lines, including healing, HP/AC buffing, direct damage (live and undead), root, fear, cancel magic, and resurrection. We also get more powerful versions of some more questionable spell lines, including Lull, Yaulp, and AE damage. For me, no complaints here.

The second count is where I see problems. On the plus side, they've given us:

-Group versions of several buffs and utility spells. Really, really nice.

-Death Pact/Divine Intervention. Need tuning for success rates for DI, but still a nice new ability for a class constructed to prevent death.

-Antidote. Helpful for high-level raids on poison-based mobs.

-Banishment of Shadows. Yes, the corpse poofs, but this is consistent with all other versions of this type of level 60 spell. Marginal utility but at least interesting.

On the minus side, three real disappointments:

-Unswerving Hammer of Faith. Use of this spell, originally one of the most exciting and interesting in the entire line, is prohibitively penalized to the point of uselessness. Three factors: high mana cost, low HPs, and forced despawn after one fight. I would argue that removing either the first or third factor would make the spell balanced, requiring either very specialized tactics or high mana output for its use, without destroying the spell entirely as is currently the case. If that cannot be done for whatever reason, then loss of this spell creates a balance deficit that would suggest compensation is needed elsewhere.

-Mark of Karn. A spell which promised to liberate clerics from the chief complaint of people who play the class- the redundant sit/med/heal cycle which defines the class at high levels, and a keen disappointment in its current form. Too powerful in its original form, now modified into what is widely agreed to be uselessness. The original change was never explained beyond the unsupported assertion that the spell was too powerful. The "vote" taken by Abashi was unsatisfying to say the least, especially given his unwillingness to discuss the alternatives before putting it to a vote, as well as failing to heed the many objections that followed the vote itself. Either the spell should be modified to a level of power somewhere between where it currently is and where it used to be, or we have another balance deficit that would suggest compensation is needed elsewhere.

-Celestial Elixir. We understand that problems with spell stacking in the game engine prompted the loss of the cure component in this spell, but this creates a balance deficit in our ability to cure/heal that would suggest compensation is needed elsewhere.

If we praise what's been done right and focus on the specific and highly supportable issues I've outlined above, I think we have the best chance possible of having at least SOME of our concerns addressed. I hope.
