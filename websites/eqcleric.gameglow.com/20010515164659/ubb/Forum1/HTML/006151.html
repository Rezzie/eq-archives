So I travel for work, you see.

I end up in places that more often than not, are not very interesting.

I always have my laptop with me, and while it is capable of playing EQ (in 600x400, windowed mode) my modem connections from hotel rooms generally stink, so EQ playing is usually right out. (Although now that I think of it, it might be nice to pick up a trade skill that way, as going LD every so often wouldn’t be too much of a disaster.)

So then I discovered Wizards of the Coast. (http://www.wizards.com)

They are in a lot of shopping malls all over the US. And in many of their stores, they have in-store computer gaming. They usually have 10-15 PC’s, high-end ones, with a fast Internet connection. You can rent on one to play for a few bucks an hour. (I’ve seen prices go from $4-$7 an hour.) Only downside is that you will have to set up your hotkeys again.

First off, if you usually play on 56K dialup or worse, you would really enjoy the fast connection. And the big screens are nice to play on too! You don’t have speakers, but they have a headset if you must have sound.

What I have noticed is that almost always when I go in these places, at least half of the players are teens usually. Most of the time half the machines are playing EQ and the other half are playing CounterStrike.

Having spent a few hours playing in lots of Wizard’s stores, I can tell you that it’s really an education listening to a lot of the other players talk to one another. (As an aside, when they don’t know you, and they see you playing EQ, they always ask what server you are on. When they find out I have a level 57 cleric, they are in wonder! I have to almost always spend 5 minutes showing my character off before I can start playing! Makes me kind of proud! )

Oftentimes, I see players make assumptions about other servers that may or may not be true.

I was sitting next to some kid who was playing a Ranger in the Commonlands. He was arranging a trade (which he proudly explained to me, was completely one-sided in his favor… I asked him why he thought the other player was engaging in such a foolish trade, he just shrugged and he said “Must be ebayed, I guess!”) He then proceeded to burst out laughing because he told the other player to meet at “One Torch” and the other player didn’t know what that was. My neighbor then went on to call anyone “stupid” who didn’t know what “One Torch” was, until I let him know that in 57 levels of EQ, I had never heard of that either. (Apparently, it’s a hot trading spot in the tunnel in EC. On my server, all the trading is in Greater Faymart.)

So it does have it downsides, but it does rather make up for itself, when these other players (mostly kids) get to see stuff they have never seen before. (I never would have guessed in a million years that they would find an ordinary Dain slaying something worth watching, but when they asked what my guild was up to and I told them, they all quit playing and watched our entire raid! LOL!) You see, most of the people in the stores seem to have had real-low level character. (Less than level 20 I would say.) Although I did meet a guy who had a 44th level Cleric on Rallos Zek. We traded some war stories and I told him he should read eqclerics every day! (Shameless plug!)

Oh, and the best thing about playing in the mall?

Alliliel tells the group "No, I *really* have to go right now! The mall is closing!" LOL!

So if you ever need some EQ on the road, try Wizards of the Coast, it's definitly an education, hehe.

Yep, on the Rathe the big shopping zone is East Commonlands, specifically the tunnel. "One Torch" or "First Torch" is the first torch in the tunnel as you enter from EC...the torches are commonly used as meeting points for trades. I personally use the empty tent in the back of tunnel by the Erudite spell vendor...I consider it my own little shop.

Lesser market zones on the Rathe include Greater Faydark and Lake of Ill Omen. No real market zone has evolved on Velious yet that I know of.

But yeah, back to the original subject, I've played on six different servers...Cazic-Thule, Quellious, Test, Rallos Zek, Tallon Zek and the Rathe. You very definitely can't make any assumptions regarding the economy or traditions or player environment between servers. This is really apparent on the PVP servers, which have evolved their own set of social rules completely apart from the rest of EQ. As for the others, Cazic-Thule was dominated by merchants as of when I left. Quellious was a powergaming server. Test is, to me, a quiet, lonely place filled with a sense of foreboding...you never know when Verant will make some change that sends everything into chaos. The Rathe has been the best of all the servers I've played on by far, especially now that the uberguilds have split off and taken their bickering with them.

There is one VERY serious issue with using public machines.

Your USER name is out there for the next person to see.

Once you log off, your username remains in the log in process until someone else logs in and deletes yours.

And any hacker can tell you, getting the username is 90% of the battle.

As a victim of a hack, I urge you to find an alternative way to log in.

Well, if it really bothers you, all you need to do is logout, then log back in and type in a nonsense username, which will overwrite the stored one.
