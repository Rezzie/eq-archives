I just now hit 51 and scribed Remedy.

Until now I kept Gheal up for quick small healing on casters and Sup heal up for larger heals like to hybrids and myself (and CH for the tanks, of course).

Throughout all my levels, each time I gained a new heal spell it went in slot 8 (sup heal), the previous slot 8 moved to 7 (greater heal) and CH went in slot 6 (because 7 and 8 were spoken for by the time I reached 39th level). I always rotated out 7 and 8 each time I got a healing spell upgrade. (The slot 7 spell went to page 50 of the spellbook)

I am comparing the math on GH, SH, and Remedy:

GH: 300 HP for 150 mana is 2 HP/mana. Takes 3.75 seconds to cast so heals 0.533 HP per mana per second. In 45 seconds you can cast this spell 12 times for 1800 mana to heal 3600 HP.

SH: 583 HP for 250 mana is 2.333 HP/mana. Takes 4.5 seconds to cast so heals 0.518 HP per mana per second. In 45 seconds you can cast this spell 10 times for 2500 mana to heal 5830 HP.


Remedy: 435 HP for 175 mana is 2.5 HP/mana. Takes only 3 seconds to cast so heals 0.833 hp per mana per second. In 45 seconds you can cast this spell 15 times for 2620 mana to heal 6525 HP.

(45 seconds is least common time denominator if chain casting these three spells)

These comparisons don't just take mana efficiency into consideration, they also consider casting time. We've all been there when in the middle of casting a slow spell our chanter takes a pounding and then it's too late to save him. In fast furious battles, time and timing is everything.

Mathematically, SH is superior to GH in terms of mana efficiency but slightly inferior in terms of time.

Remedy is superior to both of them in terms of mana efficiency and time.

My question is this:

It seems I can replace both GH and SH with Remedy. So, do any of you more experienced clerics still use either GH or SH? If so, when and why?

I would love to reclaim a spell slot to permanently memorize an extra stun or some such.
