Go to The Grey.. in the middle of the airless zone (yes, you'll need an Enduring Breath device/buff) you'll see a large colorful pyramid temple. Head to the west side of it and plop down at one of the corners. This is your camp spot.

The Shissar (snake guys) laying inside the ruins nearby are all undead and can be pulled one at a time. Get your Stun and favorite ROOT loaded and prepare yourself for a non-combat soloing experience.

I found that the two Undead DOTs which I have (Turning of the Unnatural and Epitaph of Life) both stack with one another. Start DOTing and rooting to your heart's content.

If you are a High Elf of Tunare, then do the quest for that necklace (most of it takes place in Lesser Faydark) which gives you a mana-less Ensnaring Roots clickable effect. With the mana-regen component of Armor of the Risen self buff, you can pull, root and DOT these things all night long. You'll be hard-pressed to run out of mana until you've gotten your third or fourth kill. The Shissar don't have THAT many hit points. They are decent experience and VERY vulnerable to Spook the Dead if things get ugly and you need some breathing room.

I was there last night with a 60 druid and 60 warrior and we couldn't kill these things fast enough. At one point, I was rooting/Dotting them while the Druid was pulling/DSing for the Warrior, who only needed a Comp Heal every 3 min or so. It was insane XPs.

Warning: The silvery millipedes which spawn in this zone are NOT rootable. They are immune to speed change spells. If you get one or two on you, just zone it. They are not worth the trouble.

Enjoy!

Taish,

You use the DOT because in the time you can spend medding (after Turning and Epitaph are cast) you can gain some of that mana back. You're going to be sitting on your duff anyway, may as well do it more safely than if you had to nuke. And when the critter falls over dead, you still have 3+ bars full of mana left.

Sample:

Click-Root (from Warder's Symbol of Tunare quest neckpiece)
Cast Epitaph DOT
Cast Turning DOT
Click-Root again

Med till DOTs wear off.. repeat as needed.

Advantages:

You never get hit. You never have to worry about crowd control. You can actually kill 2 mobs at a time if the situation presented itself to you (but I usually stick to 1 at a time). You *don't* need help from anyone to do this. And most importantly - you can do this WITHOUT the need for clarity.
