I can't believe I am actually responding to this...

How does the sound relate to RPM? Is it louder at certain speeds? Does it go away?

Is it a constant sound or does it click or clang?

How old is the engine? Miles? Any recent changes?

Thoughts: Timing chain tensioners, water pump bearings or any accessory item bearings.

Big internal parts like Crank bearings or rod bearings don't tend to last too long after you actually hear them grind(like, seconds).

If you are trying to pinpoint the source, one trick I use is to make stethescope out of a metal wire coat hanger. Unravel it and press the hook end against your ear(plug your ear with your finger) while touching the other end to parts of the engine. Stay away from electrical stuff.

Good Luck!

Don't those have timing belts? is it like a clicking sound that gets faster with RPM's? right now I'm thinking lifters on a car with that many miles. A vibration can come from just about anywhere and you would be able to feel it at the intake. 2 things to try; try pulling off spark plug wires one at a time, pull it off with the engine off, then start it, then turn the engine off again and replace the wire and try the next one. If it goes away when a certain plug is off, you might have some major engine damage . also try taking off the belts, it may be a bearing going in your ac compressor or your power steering pump.
