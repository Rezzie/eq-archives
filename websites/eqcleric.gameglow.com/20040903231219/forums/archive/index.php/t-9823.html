I just wanted to comment on how realistic the enviroment in EQ has become over the past year.

When I play EQ these days I can easily see myself as the cleric in the fantasy epic adventure. I mean, our daily routine is just like that of the hero in any fantasy novel...

I remember when I read Lord of The Rings and Frodo was shouting 'Camp Check' and looking for a place for him and his friends to be able to be together and wantonly destory evil just so that they could frisk the corpses and get money to buy a better sword for the ranger.

I recall in the Wheel Of Time how Rand would see an evil beast and witness someone being attacked by it and how they would say "MINE!" he would away without helping so that the villagers would not rise up and accuse him of that heinous crime: kill stealing.

I remember in the tales of Raistilen how he would suddenly appear in the moment that some dark fiend appeared. He would blast them to death in an instant and then dissappear with a comment of 'see you back here in 8 hours'.

I thrill when I recall the exploits of Pug and how when he wanted to find a special item to help defeat his enemies. He went forth into the evil castle and found a strange red device. From the end a piece of parchment protruded. When he pull on the paper, it broke off in his hand and the papre said '213'. Looking up he beheld a long line of adventurers in the hallway all grimacing at him and saying "we were here first, get to the back of the line"

I was amazed at the wonder of it all when the hero would go and slay that mighty, dark beast and save the day fro all mankind. And 7 minutes later the beast was back. And the hero would attack it again muttering "better have the rare this time"

Thank you verant for making this a special game for all of us.
