"I wish I had a weapon..."

I have heard that from so many high-level druids and clerics who are carrying Cones of Mystics, Paws of Opolla (how many paws did that gnoll have anyway), Spirit Tomes and other items that I have to wonder...THEN WHY AREN'T YOU CARRYING A WEAPON!?

I still get good use out of my Dark Ember at level 53. It lets me kill annoying greens without having to waste mana. It lets me contribute damage in a fight (when my mana will allow it). It helps me tank when I have to (e.g. covering the evacer). It lets me get up and relieve the boredom of stand-heal-sit-med.

Let's examine the most common arguments that people have against clerics and such folk carrying weapons.

1. "I need the extra WIS." BZZZZZT, wrong answer, you ARE the weakest link, goodbye! There are plenty of affordable weapons out there (mostly 1HB) that add WIS. If your WIS is so poor that you need to drop your weapon to raise it, you might want to examine your other equipment for improvements first.

2. "I like the look." OK, I will grant that a high elf in full Velious armor with a CoM and Hate-plane shield looks really cool. If looks are what motivates you, go ahead, but don't complain about your lack of a weapon later. I personally think my butt-ugly Dark Ember has a kind of character of its own.

3. "I need the AC." FINALLY, an argument that actually has some validity. But again, if your AC is such a problem that you need to boost it by dropping your weapon, you might want to examine your other gear for improvements first.

4. "Clerics aren't a melee class." A meaningless point. Damage is damage, no matter where it comes from. I do up to 40 points a hit with my Dark Ember and Yaulp IV. That adds up over the course of a fight, and it costs no mana. So if I'm fat on mana, why not stand up and do some damage?

Now, with all these arguments made, let me state something.

I am NOT a "battle cleric."

I spend most of my time medding and healing. I'm specialized in Alteration. I know what my job is in a group, and I've gotten a lot of compliments on how I do that job.

I'm just tired of hearing people whine about not having a weapon when it's their own choice not to carry one.
