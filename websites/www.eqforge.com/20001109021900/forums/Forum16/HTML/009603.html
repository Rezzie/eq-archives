It largely depends on what you want your alt to be able to do.

If you're looking for the ability to solo, generally just mess around, etc, play a necro. Errrr, melee class, eh? Then either a monk or an SK would be the best choice. Both have ways of avoiding the downtime that plagues melee soloists. The SK has that nasty hybrid experience penalty though, and the monk suffers from a hefty experience penalty of their own as well as a dramatic loss of relative power post-50.

If you're looking to be a powerhouse at high levels either a warrior or a rogue would be the way to go. Nobody can touch a well-equipped rogue for total damage, and the warrior's massive hit points and AC make them the best tanks, while their damage steadily increases, rivalling that of a monk at high levels.

Neither paladins nor rangers are in as much demand as warriors and rogues, but both have their uses. In the post-50 levels, the taunt key is effectively broken (a bit of remenant code from the days when drgons and planar mobs were the only level 51+ things you would fight), making paladins and shadowknights much better taunters than warriors, which can be very useful in sticky situations.
