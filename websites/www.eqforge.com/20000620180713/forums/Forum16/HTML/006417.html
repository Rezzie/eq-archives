Regardless what race you choose, max wisdom. Then you will have 5 points left over to put into other attributes. Here are some places to consider putting those 5 points:

INT - Shamans get a buff for every single attribute except INT and WIS. Since your WIS will be maxxed, it would make sense to put your remaining 5 points into INT to help skill gains.

AGI - Although shaman get the ability to buff AGI, all you really need is 75 AGI total. This gets you over the "hump" in the curve, and gets you the most AC out of your equipment. If you are less than 75 AGI, of course you can buff it, but that's an extra buff you need to leave on at all times just to be over the hump at 75 AGI.

STA - More health = more health to cannibilize for mana! This is what I did.

STR - No real need to buff str, name one shaman that won't exceed 200 strength by the time they get their level 49 STR buff.

DEX - If you are a barbarian you might consider putting the points here in the hope some day you get the spiritist's hammer. Dex helps proc's a tiny bit.

CHA - You get CHA buffs throughout your life, no real reason to put the points here, but if you plan on doing alchemy or any other trade and don't want to fill your backpack with a charisma suit just to get the best prices, 5 CHA + your buff + Crude Stein will be enough to get halfway reasonable prices from NPCs in most cases.

greetings!

i'd choose barbarian (again, since i did the first time). being non-kos to all good cities is a definate bonus.

here's a quick list.

barbarian pros:
non-kos to good races
medium or large armour
shields finally fixed (well, my character is female so i didn't care )
excellent newbie zone
higher starting wisdom (if you dump all your points available into it)

barbarian cons:
can't see in the dark - no infra/ultravision (but you get serpent sight infra at 9 and ultravision at 29)
can't wear ivandyr's hoop (best earring in the game IMHO)
lower starting strength (i end up with lots of loot and such as i don't go back to bank for days at a time)

ogre pros:
buttscratching
can't be stunned from the front
can wear ivandyr's hoop (:P on ormak)
can do the gatorsmash quest easily at mid-20's
highest shaman starting strength for carrying loot and wearing heavy armour (not that totemic is overly heavy...)
infravision

ogre cons:
buttscratching
evil race - kos to good races
large only armour
block doorways with their fat arses

by no means is this complete. the kos element of ogres leads me away from that race for the shaman. we don't get invisibility until 29th level. i don't mind my dark elf necro being kos so much as she get an invis at 8th.

being nightblind as a barbarian is kind of a nonissue since we get serpent sight at level 9, and everfrost is pretty well lit anyway. (light reflects off the snow . if you've gotten 25th already you probably know enough to get 9th in just a few hours anyway.

as for starting points, don't do what i did: 15 sta 5 cha 10 wis. . put 25 into wisdom and the other 5 whereever you want. i'd put them into strength (for carrying more) or dexterity (for that added boost for learning weapon skills faster).

there are tons of guides available on the shaman class. check the main crucible page as well as eq.castersrealm.com and eq.stratics.com.

The best distribution for your skill points for any shaman race (except for iksar, don't know anything about them) is to put as many point into wisdom (this will be 25 points) as you can, and then put the remaining 5 whereever you choose.

If you go with a barbarian, I would recommend dexterity, to get your dex up to 75, seeing as how stamina is already at 100 and won't be affected too much from extra points there, and agility is over 75 already, so the AC bonus will be minimal for any points you put in here.

Strength is at 103 to start with, and should be fine.

Charisma is really only good for buying/selling, and we already start with only 60, so I don't think that 65 cha would be much change in vendor pricing. Besides, you can buff that when you need to sell.

Intelligence might be good, if you would prefer to learn skills faster, but in 34 levels, I can't say that any of my skills have taken forever to raise up, I am happy with their current progression.

The magic numbers for melee stats seems to be 75, 100, 125, etc, so I think that getting your dex up to 75 is the best choice.

I only wish I would have known at the creation of my shaman, when I put 20 into wisdom, and 10 into stamina....ouch

At lvl 34, I still don't notice any significant difference between my HP and the HP of same level shamans of 10 less base stamina ;(

Big differences between an ogre and barbarian shaman, and not just stats. A barbarian wouldn't be that big of a change from your ranger, in terms of exploring da world. But if you go with ogre or troll, you'll have either avoid cities or raise faction with them.

Good and bad things about the different races:

Barbarians
Good:
Good faction in most places.

Higher wisdom than ogres and trolls (only 3 points higher than ogre).

Ability to wear both medium and large armor.

Bad:
Blind as a bat. Sure, we get "see in the dark" spells, but to me it would be a pain to have to cast it every night. One less spell I have to cast, the better.

Weak. They're the second weakest shaman around. But, you can buff strength (but still weak compared to a buffed ogre).

They were skirts. =]

A barbarian shaman is about the same as a wood-elf ranger. Meaning: it's a very, very common race for the class.

Your spell vendor is on the ass-end of the continent.


Trolls
Good:
Regeneration. This is invaluable for younger shaman and I've seen it work extremely well for them even in the mid 20s. It stacks with our regeneration line of spells. After level 50, their natural regeneration gets an upgrade.

Sees in the dark.

Trolls look cool.

Are one of the "bigger" races, so you can "oooh and ahhh" newbies.

Troll shaman are the least common of the class (I like playing less-popular things, so this is a perk to me).

Bad:
Have evil faction, so you will be KOS in most cities. There are ways around this, and even ways to raise faction. Taking the time to do it is part of the downside of choosing an evil race. For me, I didn't mind this.

Experience penalty. These guys have a big experience penalty so it's harder for them to get to high levels as fast as others.

If you choose Inoruuk as your God, you will not be able to raise faction at all in some places (but have the option of getting some good deity-only items as has been recently posted on castersrealm).

Not quite as big and strong as an ogre.

Ogres
Good:
You're an ogre!

Strongest of not all the races of this class, but ALL THE RACES. Also have the highest stamina.

You're part of THE biggest race. Period.

You cannot be stunned from a mob when you are facing said mob. That's right. When a mob "bashes" you, you don't get stunned at all. Supposedly giant-type mobs can stun you, but none others can. They can stun you from the side or back though.

Infravision.

Speaking like an ogre (I consider this a perk).

We look good in any armor.

Very high "oooh and ahhh" newbie factor.

Bad:
Evil aligned, therefore KOS in most places. Possible to get around. Guards and merchants in Freeport love me to death (merchants kindly consider me) and Felwithe and Kelethin guards don't mind me anymore (got them to apprehensive).

Experience penalty. It isn't as bad as trolls, and it isn't as good as barbarians. But it is there.

Ogre women are pretty fugly.

You won't be able to fit everywhere. In dungeons and after level 19, this isn't a problem with the shrink spell.

Iksar
Good:
Highest wisdom of all the other shaman races, therefore highest potential mana pool (well, easiest race to get the highest mana pool).

High intelligence, so learning skills will take less time.

They look kind of cool, I guess.

Regeneration.

Medium sized, so can fit where all the other shamen cannot.

Bad:
Weak. Very, very, very weak. Weaker than puny elves. They collapse under the weight of their own scales.

Experience penalty as bad as a troll.

KOS everywhere. Don't know if it's possible to raise or not, or if it's the same diffculty as an ogre as I've never played an iksar (for more than 10 minutes).

Can't wear totemic armor (our quest armor), but have nice kunark-only armor options.

Too many iksar shaman twinks around. =]


Whichever you choose, I'd pump as many points into wisdom as you can. Wisdom = mana. If I could start Numbuk over again, I'd max out my wisdom. There's a saying, "We can buff everything but wisdom and intelligence." So, you might want to put some into intelligence after you've maxed out wisdom. But that's a judgement call on your side.

I hope you enjoy the shaman class. I surely do after coming from a ranger (though I was only level 11 or so before I realized an EQ ranger wasn't for me). You just have to realize that while we can hold our own in melee, we're really a casting class... so get used to relying on your spells more and more as you gain levels. But out of all the pure casters, we can deal the most melee damage and take the most too without crumpling like wet tissue paper.

Get a 2hb and piercing weapon as soon as you can and keep all your weapon skills (1hb, 2hb, piercing) maxed out as you gain levels. You'll not be sorry later on.

Never use "minor healing" to heal wounds from levels 1 - 9. Use "inner fire" instead. It heals more health points for less mana. Trust me.
