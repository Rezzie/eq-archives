The actual number of barb horsemen spawned in each horde at this "raging level" is 24.

I believe the lower levels spawn at 8 and then 16 horsemen respectively.

Did you get two hordes fairly close together, so they came from the same spawn event generation or were these two hordes spawned at time differences greater than about 10 turns?

It could have looked like two different events if one horde had to wade through a lot more jungles and mountains to get to were you could see them.

Suppressing the barb villages quickly after they appear is key to keeping them from "OVERHEATING" into a horde, but there is something in the program that I have been calling a bug or at least a really severe programing flaw because currently it seems that every barb village on the planet comes equipped with intercontinental smoke signals or earth orbitting drum sound reflectors that let all the villages all over the planet organize into one single uprising.

You can stay in close contact with your military advisor each turn, and he will tell you where the nearest barb village is located near one of your towns. The advisor will not tell you exactly where the barb village is but he will get you closer to the village. The only problem with this system is that it only focuses on the village that is mathematically nearer to one of your cities. There could be other barb villages that are greater threats and you will not know about these until you kill the first one.

You can also buy another copy of the world map for the AI civs that are located 10,000 miles away from your territory and that map will have all the barb villages located on it for you, even though the AI's have never been anywhere near your territory. Haven't quite figured out how it is reasonable for the AI's to get the location of all barbs gifted to them instantly. Maybe Tokugawa and Joan have embassies open with "a barbarian chiefdom" and that lets them buy maps of baarb camp locations for a few strings of beads and some metal buttons. ;)

I have been complaining about this simultaneous uprising bug for several months because it can really wipe you out in cases where you have a lot of exposed frontier, or when you plan scenario maps that involve all the civs starting on one continent and the "gold rushing" to find and conquer other continents that have lots of barbs.

These problems are particularly disruptive on large maps with lots of mountains, jungle, and forests because even when you know where the barbs are, it can take you more than 5 turns to wade through the jungle and get to the villages.

In my GOTM-8, I had to deal with one simultaneous uprising that produced a total of 24x7 = 168 (one hundred and sixty freakin eight) horsemen all in a single turn.

If one of your neighbors has a neglected village that get old and overheats, I think the coding in the program causes every village on the planet to go mental, ven if the village you are moving to attack has only been around for just a turn or two.

I had one village that I am certain had just appeared and went to a 24 horde almost on the same turn because I had visibility of the area off and on by virtue of two passing galleys with a small time gap of darkness.

The only way to deal with these scale of uprisings is to spend all of your gold very quickly and set research high enough to use up all of your excess cash in every turn, so you will break even and leave the barbs with nothing to steal. {editted to clarify on 6/7} In most cases you should then run your units for impassable terrain like mountains, jungle or forests. You have some chance of defending the city successfully if you have lots of military to sacrifice (but that will usually make you a target for your neighbors.) A town on hills with walls and fortified defenders will double the defensive value of your units. SO if you have 3 or 4 veteran pikemen you have about a 50/50 chance of surviving a horde rush of 24 without being sacked.
