i've seen the ai use them quite effectively actually. the zulus were stuck on england, norway and greenland on the world map, but there was still a lot of open space and a lot of unclaimed resources in the middle of asia. of course, this was fairly late in the game so by that time the world had pretty much been completely railroaded, not by the zulus but by the chinese.... even land not within their territory.

by the time my tanks got over there i found about a half dozen zulu colonies collecting not only luxuries but strategic resources from across the globe using other civ's railroad's to transport them home. there's so much dead space in asia (with mountains and tundras) that it's barely worth building there, even near resources which are quite common over there to begin with.

i was quite impressed with the zulus for doing this... they invested almost nothing in these colonies, and if they were eventually were lost, so be it, but for the time being they had luxuries and strategic resources from across the globe for the cost of one infantry man and one worker... quite cheap.

my cities eventually took the resources of course, but they probably held them for hundreds of years... and utilized the resources that whole time.
