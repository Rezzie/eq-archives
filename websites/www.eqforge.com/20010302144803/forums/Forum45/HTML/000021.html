Well, as a level 53 SK I'll give you my opinion of the class, and my experience playing about 95 days of online time now as a shadow knight.

These are just my opinions, take them for what they are - opinions.

One, at lower levels, sub 30 especially, sub 39 to an extent I feel shadow knights are underpowered. our spells are weak, its tough if not impossible to solo, etc etc. Once you hit 39 you get the pay off, with that set of spells, atleast for me I felt that I finally had some power going. Granted I could still be out damaged by most classes, and its still not easy to solo, but things were better.
Now, for those that say SKs are underpowered, let me tell you if you play an SK and your lower level, chances are yea theres another class you could be playing thats more 'powerful. If you have the time and patients to stick it out to the higher levels though the pay off is golden.
I can solo things no other melee / melee hybrid class can, I can harm touch for almost 700 damage, life tap for for 280 and stack dots for nearly 1000 damage, all this and I have a pet that can dual wield and double attack for up to 39 damage. It doesnt end there, Im a dark elf with 70 base stamina, I have 3k HP unbuffed, more then any other class with the exception of warriors, I have 984 unbuffed ac, thats more then most monks OR warriors, and AC is a big factor. my ATK is on par with all other hybrid classes. Do I think SKs are underpowered ? Hell no, at the upper levels I think an SK has more to offer then any other hybrid class, and in a lot of situations more to offer then a lot of pure classes.

Its not easy getting to the top, but when your there its worth your time ten fold. I dont know a single 50+ SK that regrets playing their class. We are the most versatile of classes, with feign death, invisibility, and invis vs undead there is no where we cant go, and with our spells and skills theres very little we cant do.

Now, as for stats that were mentioned in the first post here. All stats DO make a difference, some more then others and which are most important depends on the class you play. I feel when playing an SK all the races are fairly well balanced.
The first post mentioned STR and STA, you will not notice an impact in STR until level20, as thats when STR begins to factor into weapon damage, STAs effect increases as you level. Maximum damage also increases as you level. at level 50 with an ogres str and sta you will be hitting for 140+ damage a single hit with a 30 damage weapon while other races (Dark elfs) struggle to find tons of str equipment just to be able to hit in the triple digits regularly with that same weapon. I dont have the exact formula but around level 50 it seemed that my friend who plays a troll sk, (so assuming with equipment I have aprox 90 sta, he probably has 130, 40 difference) he had about 200, 250 hp more then me unbuffed.
Doesnt sound like a lot? it is. an extra 200hp is basicly the same thing as being permanently buffed with resolution (a high level cleric spell) with high AC your hp will go a long way.

Sooo, in conclusion, I love the SK class, and I think if you can stick it out through the hard times you will find out what a great class you really have. Our relative weakness at lower levels is just a penalty for our dominance over other classes at the higher end of the spectrum.

so just some food for though, our class isnt unbalanced, we're just late bloomers.

If you happen to play on the cazic thule server send me a tell ingame and Id be happy to help you out with any advice or anything else.

PS, Dont take this as a flame or anything, Im just giving my perspective on the SK class.

~Sylfein l'Velkyn
