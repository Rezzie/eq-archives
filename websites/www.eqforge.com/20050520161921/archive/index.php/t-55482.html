We currently have a Settler en route to the Gold Rush area south of Gorina, and another due from Penguinadua in 2 turns. As these cities will be the first to be named this term, we will have to decide if city naming will be term-based or chronological, or a combination of both.

Term-based

City naming starts at the top of Term 2's Chain of Command and works through all leaders and deputies. After this, Term 1's leaders and deputies that did not get to name a city woulld be next(according to its CoC), followed the the Fanatican citizens.

Chronological

From last term, the only elected officials that did not get to name a city were Judge Advocate Fionn McCumhall and Public Defender bootstoots. After this, there are also six deputies in the hierarchy(one deputy held a higher position and got to name a city).

Chronological would have Term 1's leaders and deputies name a city first before moving to term 2's CoC.

Combination

This would have Term 1's leaders first in the hierarchy, followed by Term 2's CoC, then Term 1's deputies and then the Fanatican citizenry.

----------

Which of these methods do you prefer? Is there an even better way? Let's try to hammer this out by the beginning of Tuesday's chat.

Whoa, Mr. President. The Historical process of city-naming has always been fairly simple and well excepted throughout our journeys through time. I suggest we stick with that routine to ensure consistant equality in the COC naming process.

In a first Term of a demogame, the people at the top of the COC are the first to name cities. After they name a city they are removed from the eligibility list for naming a city until all citizens have named a city. When Term 1 ends, the process starts over and the people at the top of the COC begin naming cities first, UNLESS they have already named a city. Then they are bypassed and then they person below them gets to name a city, UNLESS they have named a city, and so on...

If you were at the bottom of the COC at the end of the last Term and did not get to name a city, you lose out. You must place yourself again on the COC in order to be considered eligible for priority placing. Otherwise, you must wait like everyone else. Let's say you were a deputy or a Governor or whatever in Term 1 and you didn't get to name a city. In Term Two you find yourself in approximately the same level of the COC, but most of the positions above you are filled with people who have already named a city. This situation would actually put you close to the top of the city-naming COC, as most of the people above you would be ineligible to name a city again until everyone else had.

But, if the begining of the last scenario was true and someone at the bottom of the COC didn't get to name a city, and then they did not return to political office (therefore placing them in the upper COC) then they would be placed in order of their registration number, just like everyone else who didn't hold political office.

I can't see changing horses mid-stream. As we've already named a good 10-12 cities using the existing format, I believe changing now would be unfair to the masses.

Just so you know, DZ. As President of the second Term, you would be the first to name a city in this Term, UNLESS you have already named a city. But I don't think this is the case.
