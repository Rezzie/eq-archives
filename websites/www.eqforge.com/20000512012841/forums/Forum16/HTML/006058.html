I haven't dumped nearly as much money into Alchemy as the rest of you guys, but I've paid a lot and now have a skill of 76. There are a *lot* of useful potions out there.

I can easily sell SoW and Shrink potions just about any time I want to. I've never been able to sell Invis to Undead, but I'll take everyone's word for it that they do sell sometimes.

Kilva's Blistering Flesh is a great potion and I make them for myself. I'm even able to sell them periodically. If Kilva's Skin of Flame wasn't bugged (has same effect as Blistering Flesh) I'm sure I'd be able to sell and/or use those as well.

Heat and Cold are surprisingly popular, but that's probably due to the fact that my guild likes to do Dragon runs. Right now I'm only making standard versions (+15 CR/FR) but even those are useful to people who don't have 100 CR/FR before buffs. Remember that these potions stack with other buffs and equipment, so that's just that many fewer froggie crowns that your dragon-slaying group needs.

A guild bard purchased 20 (!) potions of Antibody from me because he finds the extra CHA useful when charming. I'm sure I'll be able to sell Greater Antibody to him once I can make it also.

At this point I'm probably only out 3000-4000 plat on Alchemy. I trained to 21 then spent that cash to get me up around 52. Since then, though, I have spent almost none of my own money raising my skill to 76 - I simply sell attempts and let others pay for my research for me.

It's slow, but it works. Alchemy is a secondary skill... if you try to make it the focus of your character then yeah you're going to have to pay 19,000 plat or so to turbo your skill up to max. But if you're willing to just let it be a side business it really won't end up costing you that much for a skill that really does have uses.

Oh, by the way, if you want to make money on Alchemy go to Kunark and advertise SoW potions for 20 plat. Those zones are HUGE and so SoW potions are in very high demand.

This is not a skill that you can make money that is worth your time. If there is someone who has figured out how to make an economic profit, I'd love to see how. Sure you can sell Blood of Wolf potions at a 5 to 10pp accounting profit. So, it takes 150 to 170 potions sold before you breakeven on your investment to build the skill to the point where you can easily make the potion. Then factor in the time it takes to sell the potion vs the money that you can make killing something for the coins during that time.

In the long run, it does not make economic profit for you. You may be able to make a slight accounting profit but that is again after you sell off the initial investment cost.

Now, if you want to do the skill and sell for the fact that it something different to do or fun to learn a new skill, that is one thing. But DO NOT get into it thinking that this is a way to make money.
