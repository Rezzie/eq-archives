Evening, friend,

I have spent my whole career as a ranger under the impression that Agility anf Dexterity truly make a difference in our abilities. Currently, I run about 193 Agility, something I'm very proud of. However, and I am loathe to admit this, Agility and Dexterity, by EverQuest game mechanics are basically pointless to have to the extreme.

To explain, it has relatively recently been revealed that, illogically, the Agility and Dexterity stats do not, in fact, increase the chances of a successful dodge/parry/riposte. Also, even with Agility of the my caliber, my AC falls far below other classes, as well as other rangers who opt for higher pure AC in their equipment.

Dexterity does help in procs, yes, but in combat, it seems to be strength that determines your attack power, and therefore, your chance to hit and cause more damage.

In any other game of this genre, Agility is the stat that makes you untouchable, and Dexterity makes your coordination such that you can balance upon one finger, especially with rangers. In EverQuest, they are nothing.

Hail Dark Knights,
I just wanted to teach the importance of AGI.
I just wanted to get off my chest. I often heard from people about agility that sucks and all we need is to keep it at 75 as minimum and if invest more then its a waste..

We all know that minimum AGI must be 75 at ALL the time if you fight, period.

If you just leave AGI of 75 with full battle gear, BAD idea... IT WILL decrease your survivabilty big time while you are injured badly. Why? When you are hurt badly, your AGI will drop by certain number. IT will be under 75, guaranteed if your AGI is at nominal 75. You will notice that your health drops much faster if you are hurt..

For example, my barbarian warrior lvl 30 has agi of 119 and he sure can take damage very well even he was injured very badly. Why? Cuz his AGI never went under 75 even though his health was at 1%. He often escaped to zone with many MOBS behind his back pounding at him and he lived most of the time. Nocifer did that also with AGI of 94. But his health starts to drop faster if he gets hurt so badly. My friend paladin had agility of 75, he often died while trying to escape wounded. He doesn't last long while wounded.

Soo.. AGI is important than you think. Better AGI will greatly increase your survivablity when you retreat or fight while injured.

Stamina affects strength, dexerity and agility during fatigueness..

It has 3 categories..
1st cat.. 1/2 bub. to 5 bub. No penalty..

3rd cat.. 0 % stamina.. Max penalty..
