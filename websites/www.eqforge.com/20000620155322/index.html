AC and ATK not capped part 2. - Updated by Caswallawn

This topic has been probably been covered before many times although I could not find any regarding posts. I do not know the answer to this question that has been plaguing me lately. Hopefully you can shed some light on the subject.

My AC and attack unbuffed have always been under 1000 Pre-RoK. With the exception when I had the shammy strength and/or wolf form my attack would get to the 1030ish. Not really a big deal when I viewed ATK 1030/1000 cause it did not happen that often. Since RoK has come out, I am currently at level 55 and my attack is now greater than 1000 (i.e. 1040/1000). Does this mean that my attack is capped at 1000 and only 1000 is going to be caculated? My AC is slowly approaching 1000 as well. Will this be the case for AC to?

If that is the case then please consider lifting those caps. If it is not the case, why not just get rid of the /1000 part and have a single number up there for AC and ATK?

Thank you in advance for your time.

Numbers over 1000 are indeed calculated.

Back in beta, for a time, AC and ATK were displayed with two numbers each, showing the different components of the factor. For instance, AC was displayed with both a damage avoidance and a damage mitigation factor. This was highly confusing to a majority of the players, so both numbers were combined for the purpose of display.

Basically, the two numbers were added, then divided by a theoretical max that one could attain at the time, yielding a percentage. It is this percentage that is displayed.

Now, when we brought new items into the game that raised the true theoretical max (as opposed to the calculated theoretical max), and espicially when we brought out Kunark, we could have raised the calculated theoretical max so that the percentage would always be below 1000, but that would have resulted in most people seeing their AC drop, making them think that we had somehow nerfed it. So we left the calculated max the same, yeilding numbers greater than 1000.

For actual combat calculations, the values you see are still reduced into their specific componenets which are not capped with exception of lower level twinking-impact caps that we have referenced before.

- Gordon
