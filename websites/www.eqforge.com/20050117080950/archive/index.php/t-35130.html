You are mistaking kb/s for kB/s! ;)

The standard speed of ADSL is 768 BAUD. The baudrate gives you the speed in BIT per SECOND, or more conveniently kiloBIT per second. If you want to have that in kiloByte, you have to divide by 8.
So 768 kbit/s (768 kBaud or sometimes written 768 kb/s) equals to 96 kByte/second or kB/s.

You 56k connections means 56kBaud, which equals to 7kByte/s.

This calculation does not take into account any start/stop and parity bits, which are additional to the 7 or 8 databits. So for exact calculation you need to divide by 9 or even 10, depending on your settings. That´s why modems often reach only 4-5 kByte/s, they need more error correction bits than other things like ISDN.
With ISDN you have 64kBaud = 8kByte, which can be reached at max.

So yes, ADSL broadband is more than 10 times faster than your modem, sometimes even 20x, depending on your settings. Go get it.
