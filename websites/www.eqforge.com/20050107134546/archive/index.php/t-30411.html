The illegality is not my opinion... it is a matter of judicial record. There is also a case pending in Utah's Federal Court on this very issue.

MS has supposedly agreed to correct their trade practices of OS on new systems, which is why I asked the specific questions of Blue Monday.

At this point, you are supposed to be able to buy the PC without the MS OS if you want. One illegal aspect is to tie hands of the vendor, as to the choice of OS. MS used to do this by making vendors sign an agreement that did not let them provide other OSs if they wanted to provide the MS OSs. MS, as part of their ongoing court battles, has agreed to stop this and certain other illegal practices.

Another is dumping. I won't get into it in detail, but product dumping is also illegal, MS or not. And from Blue Monday's description of the pricing structure, it sounds exactly like it could be dumping.
