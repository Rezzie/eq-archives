Just wanted to share a little trick that's served me very well from levels 26-29.

1. Go to the tundra of Everfrost, near the "Big Mountain" (mountain with the tower on top).
2. Cast SoW on yourself.
3. Hotkey Envenomed Breath, Affliction, Tainted Breath, and Sickness. All shaman DoTs are stackable.
4. Find an adult mammoth (making sure no other adults are nearby) and cast EB on it
5. Run towards the tundra. After running about 30 feet, stop and cast the next DoT on it. It may be out of range, just keep trying until it's close enough.
6. As the DoTs wear off, reapply them until the mammoth is dead.

I was stuck at level 26 for 3 weeks until I began this system. Then I gained experience very quickly. Even at level 29 they're still blue to me and give exp. They drop mammoth tusks that sell for around 10pp each, so you can make 20pp off each kill. Plus they can drop the mammoth rib bone used for our totemic.

The downside of this system is how rare adult mammoths can get. In the morning when there are few people in the zone, I find them easily. After hunting them for 2-3 hours, they disappear for a while. This is when I sell my tusks to Starn Bearjumper at the temple (he's the closest merchant). After about 20 minutes break, the adults start reappearing and it's back to work.

Root doesn't work well in this case, as the DoT damage will break it quickly. DoT and run works awesome because you can keep ahead of them and you have a lot of space to move around. That's it!

Dotting and running is really inefficient, you are much better to root and dot then med until root breaks. This is much easier when you get to 35 and get sitmed. Dots do full dmg when the mob is rooted.

I found that avocets where more common or to get a spire at the entrance to paw.

Cyclops are very hard for their con, same as giants. They will die but you either need to run med as they break root quickly, but i doubt you would have enough mana on your own to easily kill it. I used to have a wizard with me at about 29 and i would dot and run it while she nuked it down.

When you have a pet then in theory you can even take a giant down. All you need to do is to dot the mob and run, let the pet run after the mob with NO TAUNT on. You must make sure that you dont get too far away from the mob or it will turn on your pet and mince it. Very slow and doing this with a giant isnt really worth the xp time or money.

I was passing through SK when i was 34 and got jumped by an undead skel. they dont root easily at all and i had about 20% mana. i tried to dot it but not much reduced it health. In the end i just ran around in a circle shape and let the pet chew it down. Not pretty and not quick but it died.

you can stack all your dots but the only advantage is that the mob will die quicker. the older dots are far less efficient so you will actually use more mana. Once you can root dot and sitmed then often i can end up with near full mana after a fight.
