I started a bunch of games as the Iroquois over the weekend with the new patch, playing through the BC's to get a feel for the new patch and playing this Civ.

Some comments:

Map generation

Overall, the maps overall seemed nicer to play than previous versions. I tried the worst possible world settings. With one exception, no setting gave me a starting position as bad as GOTM5.

"Dry" and "Hot" gets you a lot of desert, sure, but there were rivers with floodplains adjacent to desert areas, and also more grassland than I expected.

3 billion year old world gave some areas of the map that were totally uninhabitable, but the starting positions of all Civs were actually quite productive.

"Cold" did not seem to have the really extended areas of tundra that it did previously.

The one exception - a tiny, dry, 3 billion year old world with minimum archipelago land mass. That was ugly. The starting position was an island with room for two cities, max. Tiles were mostly mountain and plains (no water source). Only one tile capable of generating 2 food on the island.

Worst map for Iroquois: archipelago with small land mass - This reduces the value of expansionist trait to almost zero; the small land mass tends to result in small cities, which reduces the need for temples.

Best map for Iroquois: Pangaea with max land mass

Raging hordes?

If the barbarian setting is at raging hordes, better build some spearmen quick. 'Nuff said.

Building a central core

Corruption seems slightly reduced, so your core area can be slightly more expansive - the pressure to build a whole bunch of cities almost on top of your capital has been reduced slightly.

If we're neighbors, let's be friends

If we have a culturally linked start, your neighbors will be the Aztecs and the Americans. The Jaguar Warriors seemed to outperform their stats in battle.

Golden Age

The mounted warrior is a very cool UU. However, if you fast-track your research to horseback riding and start fighting with them, you will have a golden age that is too early to do you much good.

Get your war paint on!

Thumbs up, ready to rock! [dance]
