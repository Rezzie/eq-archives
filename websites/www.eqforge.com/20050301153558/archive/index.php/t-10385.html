Nov 28, 2001, 07:02 AM
In my current game I had a very slight lead in tech so I went for the tech that give the Smith Trading Company (STC). It was a good decision I think. I wasn't able to get my research above 40% and I thought the extra money saved from STC would be a big help. I was right, it helped a lot. I was able to boost my tech rate to 50% (big difference, from about 12 turns per tech to about 8) and I'm really happy I did it. Also after I was about half way through building STC I decided to start selling the tech to people which yielded a couple other non-essential but helpful techs. I also gave it to France for free and it helped relations with her a lot. Later on she agreed to team up with me against Egypt. Egypt is almost dead now, I'm researching a little faster and it's all because I went for that non-essential tech.

Of course next game I might not go for it, it all depends on how the game plays out, I might go for something else instead that will give me an edge somehow.

I would recommend researching optional techs in two circumstances. If your way behind (so you can trade them to get ahead) or if your way ahead. If I think someone is neck and neck with me in research I'll stick to the straight and narrow path until I come up with a plan to best them.
