[Hopefully this will post the photo, but not sure. Here is the text of the info my friend gathered last night and has allowed me to post here.]

"Hi all,

Many of you have heard of the new and very deadly additions to lfay. Personally, I think putting them here was a mistake. Lfay has always been my favorite zone for level 10-14 (Crushbone, yuk!). Also, there are several improtant ranger quests which involve this zone, as well as quests for other classes.

Therefore, I decided to do a little research, and try to determine just how much these new additions to the darker side of the forest many of us call home will affect us. I found that that they are indifferent to wolf form, so I was able to study their habits closely.

There are three of them, and they con blue to a level 43. I did not try it myself, but the information I have is that they are KOS to any other form with the POSSIBLE exception of earth elemental form. Note: I have no evidence of any of that, so be extremely cautious. These things are deadly to anyone under level 50 or so, perhaps lows 40s in a group.

First, here is a map of lfay, detailing their routes. The map is from EQAtlas. I hope they don't mind my using it, since I am giving them credit:

The red, blue, and green lines indicate the paths that these brownies follow. As you can see, they cover a pretty wide area. In case you are wondering how I got these paths, it was simple. I followed them around on their routes, and noted the /loc each time they changed course. One of them I followed at least 3 times, so I am pretty certain that the paths they take are set, not random. I have no idea where they spawn, since they were up when I got there, and stayed up the entire time (don't count on them de-spawning). My guess would be at 0,0 since that is the spot they all seem to go to in their travels. In fact, you can often see two, or even three at once by watching this spot.

Here is what I see this does for the various hunting spots and quests in LFay: low-level camps: the nybright sisters(#14 on the map), the north bandit camp and MOST of the orc camps are still huntable, safely. At the northern bandit camp (#4 on the map), you may need to be more careful of runners, because one of them comes fairly close. It's not too close, because I talked with people hunting it last night for at least 2 hours, and they never saw a brownie. It does go within sight of the camp, however, so just don't let those bandits run! Also, one of the orc camps is probaly just too dangerous to hunt. That would be the one near 0,0(#6 on the map). The biggest problem is getting to the spot. Once there, you should be pretty safe. Just look at the map, and choose a path that keeps you away from the brownies (and the DE camp on the south wall #15 on the map).

Mistmoore: Once again, getting there is the only problem. If you use the above map to pick your route, you should be able to get to MM with little or no difficulty.

Ivy leggings: As many of you know, in order to get Ivy Etched leggings, you must hunt brownie outcasts near the pixie tower (#3 on the map) to get 6 arrows. This is still safely doable (provided you can handle the outcasts, of course). Again, just pick a safe route to get there.

Orc Impaler: The spot marked with a black X is the place recommended to wait for the shadowed leader, or his shadowed man placeholder. As you can see, it is not in a safe spot. In fact, I saw the shadowed man PH at least twice in the same screen as a brownie scout. The ONLY way to do this portion of the quest safely is to be accompanied by a 39+ druid, and do it in wolf form. The area where the ghoul boss spawns (#8 on the map) is just as safe as ever, if you are careful about how you get to it.

Thistle Underbrush: Thistle is often hunted both for the Ivy Braided Cords he drops, and for the rune of Ivy needed for the Ivy Tunic Quest. Because he is a roamer, I would NOT recommend hunting Thistle without wolf form. His path will inevitably cross the paths of the brownie scouts. Also, there are a couple of other things to consider. First, it is unknown if the brownie scouts will KOS you if they see you fighting Thistle. Who really knows, until someone witnesses it? For all I know, they may attack Thistle, or any other outcast. Or, they may attack someone who is fighting him, wolf form or not. This is unknown at this time. The danger that IS known, is that Thistle likes to root. If you get rooted in wolf form, then it drops, and the brownie scouts WILL attack you then. If you go for Thistle, make sure you have as much magic resistance as you can get. Better yet, know the area, and only attack him when you are outside the scout's area.

Fairies: Rangers have to speak with Princess Joleena for the rain caller, and paladins have to collect fairy dust, so both classes come to the fairie village (#2 on the map). Some people hunt them for XP, as well. It is plenty far away from the scouts, so just pick a safe route to get to it, and you should be fine.

I think that's about it. If I missed any areas or quests (I'm sure I did) let me know.
Good hunting! I believe Lfay can still be hunted, but you must be careful!

If you sit in the bottom of the valley facing the smaller tree (if you were coming from Gfay you should be facing it when you reach the bottom of the valley). I sat there for 9 hours total without being disturbed by them. I would get a cold shiver everytime I saw a brownie path through. The tip that I would give is that you time the spawns (every 22 minutes) and keep your eyes open because the leader paths right into the brownies. I also kited the shadowmen to near the Gfay zone to avoid any chance of them running into the brownies.
