I would have to say the best starting point on the world map is the Sioux/Americans. If you use the standard starting positions, you will only have one or two civs on the same continent with you, which you can destroy quickly within the first few turns. Plus the civs on the Euro/Asia are usually too busy exploring their own continent to come bother you, at least not until you have established a considerable lead in knowledge and technology. Also, no civ has South America as an intitial starting position so you have all that land available for development w/o opposition. Plus the twin rivers in the Central U.S. make an excellent spot for a knowledge/science city.
