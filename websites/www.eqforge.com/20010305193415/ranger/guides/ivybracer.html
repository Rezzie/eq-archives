Etched Ivy Bracers - AC 9, +2 Dexterity, +3 SV Poison

FIRST BRACERS COMPONENT: Bottom rune of ivy, from Redwind in Everfrost

http://www.everlore.com/geography/default.asp?mapID=1375

There's your map. We want to get to the point marked "statue" in the south-east corner. The exact /loc is (600, -5400). Once you get there, you'll see a huge ice giant statue (don't worry, not a real giant) and a kind of "pavilion", which you can see here:

http://www.3dnews.net/work/redwind_pavilion.jpg

Anyway, a whole bunch of ice orcs spawn there, which are shamen and cast ice-based spells at you. They have a BUNCH of hit points, and I suspect they're VERY hard for their con level. Anyway, if nobody is camping the area, which is doubtful, just harmony away and pull Redwind solo.

Redwind is a shadowknight. His harm touch took off half a bubble, and he continuously casts darkness and disease spells. He's a real pushover though. Supposedly he doesn't always have his rune, but I got it on my first try and boogied my way outta there.

SECOND BRACERS COMPONENT: Watery Ring from an Aqua Goblin Tidal Lord in Dagnor's Cauldron

This one is pathetically easy. Remember those aqua goblins you used to camp when you were in your 20's? Well, now one of 'em drops something. The Aqua Goblins are pretty much ALWAYS camped, since they give good XP for their level and are a nice alternative from Eternal Unrest. Just sit there and ask the group camping them if you can have a watery ring if it's found. The first time I did this, it worked out well. I got my ring on the first try and boogied my way out.

THIRD BRACERS COMPONENT: A Fire Emerald. These can be purchased anywhere gems are sold, some locations are North Freeport, Felwithe, and Qeynos. They usually go for around 80 platinum.
