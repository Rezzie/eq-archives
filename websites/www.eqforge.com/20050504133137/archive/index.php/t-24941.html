Zachriel,

I just find this incident with China to be the most convincing evidence that I have yet to see displayed, that confirms that the game outcomes may be more significantly impacted by the effects of the Random Number Generator than by things done by the player either on accident or on purpose.

China's position was perhaps one of the strongest early positions in the game and by all rights, India should have been consumed by Mao's industrious expansion and military might. It would take some extremely bad RNG luck combined with tactical and strategic brain damage to end up with an extinct China in the early game.

This does not mean the games are any less fun, it just means that no two players are playing the same game after it has been acted upon by a different sequence of randomly generated outcomes.

This is part of my contention as to why competitive tournament type games will evolve to set piece scenario type play where the score earned by the player will be set at some fixed time point such as 50 or 100 turns in the future. These set start games will focus the scoring on how well a player plans for and then implements specific strategies versus the current process that tends to favor combination of luck and/or tedious long term milking and micro management.

We will see these same effects in the new game types released in the XP that will emphasize games that can be completed in a smaller time commitment versus the current 540 turn time sumps that can consume several weeks of time.

Originally posted by cgannon64
how do you balance military and science

I normally go straight for currency, sell it, and build marketplaces. I trade for all techs until Republic. Positive cash flow is very important. I usually build marketplaces before libraries. With marketplaces, you can rush libraries. With libraries, you may discover a lot of improvements you can't afford to build. Also, if you fall behind in your finances, you are doomed. If you fall behind in science you can always buy the techs when they become "obsolete."

General Settings
90% cash, 10% science, 0% luxuries during Despotism
50% cash, 40% science, 10% luxuries during Republic
(+/- 10% on each value)

Another important factor, I think, is number of cities originally settled. If you do not have enough cities, you will not be able to maintain your cash flow at reasonable levels. You need to know if you have parity as soon as the map is revealed or the expansion phase completed (parity depends on level). If you are not in reach of parity, you must attack immediately. If you don't, you will just fall further behind and they will gang up on you like a pack of wolves.

http://www.crowncity.net/civ3/GOTM8/ad1250-germany-small.gif

But I figured I had the land, the opportunity for an extended peace (4000BC-1695AD), and I was playing Germany, so I saved up for Panzers. :)
