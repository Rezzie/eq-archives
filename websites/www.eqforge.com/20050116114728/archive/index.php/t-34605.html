Well, actually the most amazing difference between a classi onboard chip (generally C-Media chip) is the Midi emulation. The Philips can make a midi track sound almost like a cd quality music.. There are several drawbacks though.
_ the latest drivers seem to crash windows 2000 each time I reboot or shut down! weird. But it's not too annoying since there's a reset button ;)
_ be careful, VERY careful when downloading drivers update on the Philips main homepage, the drivers have virus! Check my thread somewhere in this forum too.. I have warned you.
_ if you set the philips control panel to its default, it will allow the sound card to take up to 70% of the processor resources to render realistic and surround sound. ok I admit the quality is amazing but 70% might look a bit too much, even for such a quality.. So the choice is yours, you can take it down to 30% without loosing a noticeable quality :) Trust me.
