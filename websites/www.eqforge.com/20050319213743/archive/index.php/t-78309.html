AL East
Boston finally beats New York just barely in the division race. The Yankees win the wildcard.
1.Boston
2.NYY
3.Toronto
4.Baltimore
5.Tampa Bay

AL Central
This is the weakest division in baseball and is pretty up in the air.
1.White Sox
2.Minnesota
3.Kansas City
4.Cleveland
5.Detroit

AL West
This is a very competitive division but a lack of offense from Seattle and Oakland means Anaheim edges them out.
1.Anaheim
2.Oakland
3.Seattle
4.Texas

AL MVP-Gary Sheffield
AL Cy Young-Pedro Martinez
AL ROY- Joe Mauer (Minnesota)

NL East
After 12 year on top the Braves finally lose the division.
1.Philadelphia
2.Florida
3.Atlanta
4.NY Mets
5.Montreal

NL Central
This is a race between Houston and Chicago. The Cubs manage to win the central for the second year in a row. Houston wins the wild card.
1.Cubs
2.Houston
3.St. Louis
4.Pittsburgh
5.Cincinnati
6.Milwaukee

NL West
This division is not what it was in the past few years. The Giants are able to get career years from many players around Barry Bonds to propel them to first in the division.
1.San Francisco
2.San Diego
3.Los Angeles
4.Arizona
5.Colorado

NL MVP- Jim Thome
NL Cy Young-Mark Prior
NL ROY-Edwin Jackson (Los Angeles)

2004 World Series
Chicago Cubs over New York Yankees

These are my predictions, but don't take them too seriously.
