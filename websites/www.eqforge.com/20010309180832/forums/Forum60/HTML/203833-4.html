A bit of topic, but these are misconceptions mentioned in this thread already that hurt your credibility:

Rogue Fixes (What rogue fixes?):

Rogues had very few changes made. They always had backstab, and players just didn't realize that at high levels they are the most damaging melee class in the game. Verant added some polish to the rogue class, that's it. (Poison, Sneak/Hide simultaneously, invis to undead hide) but that's about it.


Monks are not tanks (Dead wrong!):

This is garbage. Rangers NEVER will tank as well as monks. The reason for this is Monks are now, and have always been, the second best tanks in the game, and the run a close competition for first. Monks tank poorly against casters, with low resistances and low hit points, but against melee mobs monks take less damage than ever class except warriors, and sometimes even less than warriors against mid-blue or lower mobs. Monks have the second highest AC in the game, and tank FAR better than Paladins or Shadowknights against Melee mobs. Block is NOT the equivalent of Parry. Parry works 4% of the time tops for a warrior. Block works against 20% of attacks. THEN they add Dodge/Riposte totals. Quit comparing rangers to Monks when you talk about defensive ability or you'll never get anywhere with Verant. Monks are amazing tanks, because they just don't take much damage when under attack. Level 60 monks can solo black reavers in City of Mist with ease, while Warriors get slaughtered. What do monks give up then for their superior damage capabilities? Well, they aren't THAT superior in damage. A level 60 monk is dual weilding a pair of 14/26 weapons in their fists, while a warrior is likely using 13/26 or similar. The difference in weapon damage for available monk weapons is also relatively minor.
