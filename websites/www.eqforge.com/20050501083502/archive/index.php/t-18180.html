The restriction applies to using cities to convert workers into units to build up your military. This differs from an 'emergency pop-rush' as you are building up an offensive army not desparately trying to save a that city.

The 1.17 patch does fix this exploit mostly, but there are still ways around it.

Designating a city as a military camp and using workers or captured workers to produce units can still be very powerful. The city can produce a unit every other turn in an area that would otherwise be limited to 1 shield per turn. You can't disband the city anymore, but you can keep it as a size 1 specialist city to contain the unhappiness buildup. There really isn't a penalty if it is dealt with this way, the only loss is the 1 tile that the city uses. Even then you still get credit for it along with the specialist from a scoring standpoint.

If this restriction was removed from the GOTM's, then pop rushing would become the most powerful production method once again.

Originally posted by Caldasar
Is it a good idea at all to use workers to pop-rush the cities.. Although the workers use 1 in size when they are being built. And u will only get 1 more in population anyway....

It is a powerful exploit to build workers, join them to entertainer cities, and pop them for units. A decent city can produce workers every five turns, a food city can produce workers every three or four turns. Captured workers can also be joined and then popped to produce units. This is the most efficient way to produce military units in version 1.17f. However, it is an exploit and not permitted in GOTM.

Firaxis has several options for an easy fix to the remaining loophole. A one turn delay after joining before popping, fixes the worst of it.
