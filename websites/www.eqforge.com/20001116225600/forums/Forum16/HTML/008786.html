I am lvl 52, and a while back I powerlevel quite a few guild members up some. I have powerleveled a bard, rogue, paladin, monk and ranger, and figured out a few tips for them. First off, a druid works much better to powerlevel people up to about lvl 30,but after 30 shamans really start shine as powerlevelers.

1. Buffing. Give them about everything, tuna, guardian, dex, agl, str, sta, chloro, and speed. Note: If the person you are leveling has a haste item, use quickness instead of alcrity.

2. You pull and slow the mob. I personally like Tugur's, but you may want to go with Togor's if the person you are leveling is killing the stuff fast.

3. Use the Dog. The dog really helps alot. Remember, a person only needs to do 51% of the damage to get the exp. Plus the dog is useful for some other tactics.

Leveling by class

Bards - bards can be leveled a couple ways.
1. Melee method. just buff them, slow the mob, and let them beat on it, sent in pet after they do 40% mob damage.

2. Root method. Bard dots don't break roots, like other spells, so just malise and enstill, and let them twist about 3 dots on it. This way is about as fast or faster than beating on it, but with out the skill increases.

Rogues - Probably the easiest melee class for us to power. Rogues can evade on to your dog. You may find that you will need to buff your mutt up, and even heal him sometimes, becuase he takes most of the damage. Also, at higher levels, you don't even need to wait to send the dog in, a decent rogue will outdamage the dog every time.

Other melee classes - I am just going to lump the rest together, for with a few minor differences it pretty much the same. Just buff them, slow the mob and sent the dog in after they do about 20%-40% of the damage. Let your dog taunt, and try to get the people you are leveling to stand at maxium hit range. The dog will be able taunt the mob, and absorb a few hits.

Leveling Spots

Try to find the best exp. gain for the buck. Forget things like Seafuries, their exp. isn't that good, and they have a ton of HP, so it takes a while to kill thim. I personally like some of the kunark zones. Just remember when you pick a spot, you will want a plentiful supply of mobs. I found that when powering melee classes the speed at which they can kill stuff is limited by my mana. You can speed things up consideribly, if you have clarity and/or if another person does the healing.

If you hadn't figured out how to do it already, I hope this helps.

Instead of bothering with buffing them with anything besides maybe str (works well for very low levels), dex (for learning weapon skills) and alacrity (obvious reasons), just root the mob and stand there letting the creature try to beat on you, while the powerlevelee beats on the mobs back. When the mob gets below half, join in and help finish it off with your own melee skills (saves mana and you are ready for the next mob immediately for constant pulling and near full mana). Remember a rooted mob always attacks the nearest opponent, just reroot each time it breaks.

At these levels the mobs will have a very difficult time hitting you and if you keep your best regen on, you will be full life by the time the mob is finished off because they cant outdamage your regen.

This method is very mana efficient and worked for me very well up to level 23 or so mobs. Havent really pleveled my druid for a while, but I imagine this method will still work a while longer.

I pleveled a druid from 1 to 20 in about a day and a half this way, so I cant imagine how much faster it would be with a melee class, especially a rogue. Of course the druid threw in some of his dots and dd for practice. The root breaks enough where his defensive skills are maxed and he melee'd so much that his primary weapon skill is maxed.

The druid only died once when he was poisoned by a crag spider because I just fought a HG and my mana had just run down to nothing and I couldnt get a heal off on him, around level 17 or so.

And dungeons with higher xp modifiers are the best way to go.

Here's where I PL'd my rogue:

1-4 Everfrost (or pretty much any newbie zone)
5-10 Blackburrow

25-33 Dalnir (mostly just zone area stuff)

In Nurga, it's in the middle of the spider area, but not near any spawns. You can melee them down real low, then only use backstab to finish them. Takes a bit longer, but it's really the only way to work on backstab if you're PLing.

Takes a bit longer, but it's really the only way to work on backstab if you're PLing.

Root the mob with your shaman, and stand closer to it than the rogue you are PLing. This will keep the mob firmly focussed on you and present perfect oppurtunity for backstab practice. In addition you are taking the hits, not your PL subject - odds are chloro/reqrowth and your best AC gear will leave you only negligibly damaged. Downsides - can't have a pet - can't med while they melee. I would suggest you intersperse this tactic with normal melee to evenly train their defensives skills and backstab.

That's all theoretical btw - I have never PL'd a rogue - but I did PL an Enchanter from 21 to 31 in about 18 hours played by using that tactic with his pet (which only seems to have 4 AC and 4 HP but does hella damage - hehe much like a rogue:-)).

Anyway, root is a very powerful PLing tool at lower levels where the mobs can't hit you at all, but will tear up your PLing subject. At higher levels, go for slows and medding, and let pet tank rooted mob while rogue BS's. Summon a pet that does less damage than your PL subject of course.

Also another obvious observation - Targars is your friend. The mana cost is low enough that you can cast it all day and a mid 20's buffed melee versus half speed mobs is a chainsaw. They chew up the mobs and the 50% slowdown is sufficient till mid 30's. But then I don't pit my PL subjects against Deep reds - it's just not time efficient. Yellows and very light reds are perfect.

Quick note on skill building. You can build skills (dodge, weapons, defense, etc) by battling a pet. Just have some one summon a blue pet, and then dual them. The advantage to this, it when dualing pets will only hit for 60% of there max hit, so you take less damage, plus they hit for less then mobs of equal level. So they are good for skilling on some combat skills that are lacking.

OK done alot of PL for friends and guildies.

IMHO, Druids are better at PL'ing Casters.

Shaman better at PL'ing Tanks/Melee types.

Casters need the damage shield cause after a few levels, they AREN'T gonna melee anything down. Plus Snare/SoW is a casters best friend. Root breaks too much, so Druid Snares Mob, SoW's caster, run and nuke, run and nuke to keep spell skills high, stop and melee (well sorta) with damage shield on to try and finish off and keep defensive skills high.
Works great for Casters!

Shamans are the melee-type best friend! First, we have our AC and HP buffs as individual spells. BUFF ONLY HP. Keep the AC low so they hit alot, it wont matter as regen/chloro and heals take care of any damage. BUT, this lets them get hit alot (well, more) so that defensive skill gets improved. Offense is taken care of nicely with Alacrity.

Now, a Druid can PL a melee type and Shaman can PL a caster as well. I just think this works best is all.
