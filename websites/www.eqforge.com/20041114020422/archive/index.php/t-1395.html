What version of civ do you have? There are different versions of the patch for each version of civ. If you have MGE for example, you need to take the files that come with the patch and put them in your civ folder on your harddrive, but you need to remove the files that the patch are replacing. To get to the area where these files are stored on your harddrive, Right click on the civ shortcut icon, then left click on properties. Remove the files that are to be replaced (Rules, Icons, ect.), and put the new ones in their place. Save the orginal files in another folder that you should create for them. You can switch back and forth by simply switching the files back. If you lose the orginals, don't worry about it. Simply unistal civ, and reinstall it, as the files are hardcoded to the CD.

The Fascism modpack file is in a file with the extention .ZIP

You must first unzip the files for the modpack into your main civ directory in order to convert your regular civ to the modpack (you will need the Winzip program to do this there should be a link in our download section). I say this with quite a bit of confidence because your back up civ files are on your CD anyway.

Also, please note:

The Fascism patch you speak of is available in several formats IF it is the one by Steve Strayer. I cannot vouch for other Fascism patches by other authors, but I would like to say they generally use Strayer's patch as a guide from what I've seen.

As said before, the Fascism patch does not offer a lot of graphics changes except for one or two units, a change to two governments: Fascism (formerly fundamentalism) and Communism (some changes to the rules.txt to be more accurate), and a few different technologies.

Essentially, you will likely notice no change UNLESS you change your government and PLAY as a Fascist Government...then, you get to experience a large amount of wealth, free units, and Fascism.

Hope this can be of some help.
