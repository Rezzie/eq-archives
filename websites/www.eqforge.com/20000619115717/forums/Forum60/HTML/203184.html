With that group I'd say keep the warrior. Warriors can do as much damage in melee as rangers and they can take the damage as well. This will become increasingly important as the rogue's backstab and evade skills progress. The goal of your group will be to keep all targets locked onto you.

The other skills of the ranger are pretty much covered by the other members of the group: sow, food, healing. You'll miss track and snare, so what it comes down to is whether you want to focus on tanking really well (warrior) or tanking somewhat well and having potentially useful skills (ranger).

If you plan to always keep the same group and not group with others, then i would recommend the ranger. a ranger will give your group many more options and self reliance. tracking will be help the group immensely. snare is a godsend.

tanking is not such a problem with this combination. if you had a wizard or enchanter, then tanking may be an issue, but this group can each handle some tanking. the shaman can tank slightly and will soon have a pet that can tank. the rogue has evade. the cleric is the only one you need to worry about taking too much damage. the cleric does have their invulnerability line of spells which are great for allowing them to take punishment while someone draws the mobs attention. the cleric and shaman buffs can make the ranger a very nice tank.

if you play a warrior, you will tank better, but you may get bored with the same strategy over and over (attack, kick, bash, taunt, repeat, rinse). a ranger will give you the most option. you will feel like the group leader if you play ranger because you will be tracking all the mobs you kill.

ArnCota basicly asked this question on the Warriors board on stratics. It was phrased a little differently, and I think the phrasing there was a bit more key to the problem.

Arn is concerned that warriors are boring. My take on it is this: my warrior is approaching the level my ranger is currently retired at. At 17th, my warrior is vastly superior in combat to a 17th level ranger. I don't mean a bit. I mean vastly. Better AC, better skill levels, better weapons on average . . .

My warrior CANNOT track. He CANNOT heal. He CANNOT nuke. But, what he CAN do is put BIG holes in critters. FORCE them to pay attention to him, and generally absorb a ton of punishment. And heck, he's just a Gnome, imagine what an Ogre can take.

Rangers are astoundingly good . . . but they're skirmishers, not main tanks. In the group you describe, you'd be more efficient with a warrior. But efficiency is NOT everything in the game. Rangers are truly great to play, and have a lot of options they bring to a situation.

A ranger needs ~50% more experience per level than a "typical" warrior, so your group will have more success if you're playing your warrior. A ranger on the other hand can track, sneak, hide, and cast spells, while a warrior is able to bash (and sometimes slam) . . .

What do you want? Efficiency? Options?

Go with what you'll have fun playing . . .
