Greetings!

 

For those who do not know, as of this writing (6/21/2000), I am a level 52

Shaman with a 134 Alchemy skill. Just so you know my credentials ;)

 

I recently started selling gate potions. While this increased quite a bit

the number of questions I field everyday, I have always been asked things

about Alchemy. Also, on a regular basis, I have had to correct people with

misconceptions about Alchemy and the potions Alchemists can make.

 

This document is an attempt to address some of these in a public forum, sort

of a pre-emptive strike as it were.

 

*All player made alchemy potions have a CASTING TIME

 

**This means that all player made alchemy potions can be interrupted, just

like if you were casting a spell.

 

***However, the potion is not used up if interrupted, so you can try again.

 

*No Alchemy potions are stackable in Inventory

 

*Each potion is ONE dose, meaning that if it is successfully used, it goes

away. Just like a beer, you drink the beer, the beer goes away.

 

*Any race/class can use any Alchemy potion

 

*All potions that raise your Stats (STR, DEX, etc.) STACK with player cast

buffs. I call these STAT potions.

 

**All STAT potions have side effects, positive and negative. For example:

Greater Potion of Purity is +25 STR, +15 DEX and MINUS 20 Stamina (about

45hp for a level 50 shaman if remember right) However, if you are the dmg

dealer, and not the dmg taker, this is a great buff for warriors and melee

types with Proccing weapons. BTW, there are other STR potions with different

side effects.

 

**There are two types of potions. There are auras and then there are the

other physical stat potions. The auras are the potions with resists.

 

***Not all Aura potions will stack with the other potions. Of the ones that

can, you can only stack one aura potion with one physical stat potion. NOTE:

Purity and Power do NOT stack

 

*Potions that increase a STAT and/or resistance put up a Buff Icon. If your

screen has 15 buff Icons up already, it will not work. You will be given the

"This spell did not take hold" message and your potion will be GONE.

 

*Invis VS Undead Potion (IVU) works exactly like the player cast spell. So,

the duration is random. Also, if a MOB would see through an IVU spell cast

by a player, it will also see through the IVU potion. They are the same

effect.

 

*SoW Potions work in OUTDOOR zones only. If Shaman/Druid can CAST SoW in a

zone, then you can drink the potion in that zone. You can however drink the

potion while in an OUTDOOR zone and then go into an indoor zone and you will

still have SoW speed. Again, works just like the player cast SoW. Have been

told by customers that they last a long time tho, longer than a SoW buff

from a player.

 

*Shrink Potions currently work in DUNGEONS only (Fear and Hate zones are not

considered dungeons, although Sky is). Again, if a Shaman can cast the

Shrink spell in the zone, then you can use the potion too. It lasts as long

you stay in the zone. However, it will go away when you camp, sometimes when

you go LD, and if you change your form after drinking the potion (IOW, if

you drink shrink potion, then cast wolf form, shrink will go away. If you

want to be a teeny tiny wolf, go wolf form first, THEN drink potion) One

note on this, Verant has said that they will be making the potion work in

all zones "soon", whenever that is.

 

*Damage Shield potions are DRINKER dependant. (Level/4)+1 truncated (not

exact, but very close). So, no, you can't buy them for your level 4

character for twinking, unless you only want a 2 point damage shield. A level

48 player gets a 13 point Dmg Shield. Lasts 8 to 9 minutes and STACKS with ALL

player cast Damage shields.

 

*Potion of Frost goes to Everfrost. Have been told it drops you in the 3

Igloo area in Everfrost, near BB. If you can confirm, please do so :)

 

*Potion of Swamp goes to Innothule Swamp. You land in the Southern Hand that

sticks out of the swamp, where the Shadowmen are. Shadowmen pose me no

problems, but probably depends on your class/level. I wouldn't use Swamp

potion if you were level 20, that's for sure. The Feerrott zone is West of

the Hand

 

*The Lizard Blood Potion is NOT made by alchemists, it is dropped by a MoB.

 

*Alchemy made gate potions (Frost and Swamp) have about a 25 second casting

time (according to a customer report). Obviously not a get-away-quick

potion. It is a transportation potion. To help Tanks stuck in fear, or deep

inside a dungeon, when no porters are available, or even as a way to get to

Everfrost more quickly than porting to NK. I'm sure you can come up with

many other situations where it would be handy (Can we say Kunark? I knew you

could, hehe).

 

*The cost to a shaman to make a Potion is NOT the cost of the herbs that

make it. For example, using nice round fictional numbers that do not relate

to any potion in particular: Potion of Uberness requires Herb X and Herb Y.

Herb X costs 15pp and Herb Y costs 5pp. This means that the total cost per

ATTEMPT to make the potion is 20pp. If the Alchemists is 100% successful

(yea, right, heh) then it does indeed cost 20pp to make. BUT, if the success

rate is 75% instead, it actually costs the alchemist 26.7pp per success. If

50%, would cost 40pp per success. If 25%, would cost 80pp per success. That

is the cost to the alchemist to make the potion. From there, it is up to the

individual Alchemist to decide on a fair profit margin for himself and the

customer. But please do not confuse cost per attempt with cost per success.

I am constantly running into people who have gone on a website somewhere,

jotted down herb component prices, and now think they know how much it costs

to make the potion and try to argue with me about my prices.

 

*Alchemy skill is Level Capped and is a Shaman Only Skill. (Level-24)*5 For

example, a level 50 Shaman would have a cap of 130: (50-24)*5=130 This means

you must be at least at level 25 before being able to do any alchemy. Would

not suggest trying to make gate potions until skill of 130, although some

have started at 120 on other servers. Even if you start at 120 skill, you

have to be level 48 to be able to get a skill that high. So, no, you can't

create a mule shaman to make gate potions for you unless you are willing to

Powerlevel the Shaman to level 48-50 and are willing to spend lots of plat,

even with the price redux in herbs, will still take LOTS of plat to raise

your skill high enough after you get level 48.
