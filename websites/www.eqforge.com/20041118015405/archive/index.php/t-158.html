I've recently developed this technique to get infinite NON partisans. Here's how it happened.

I have a huge civ, and tons of money. There is a size 5 city defended by one musketeer out in the middle of nowhere. 5 units of barbarian dragoons came marching by and decided to conquer my city, but when it conquered it, about 5 NON partisans came out. I quickly recaptured the city, used one to defend, and sent the rest to explore. All of a sudden, a barbarian raiding party comes by and reconquers my city. More NON partisans come out.

So if I have a special city near a barbarian "generating point", I can eventually get thousands of NON partisans!

Andu Indorin
Mar 13, 2001, 08:27 PM
Diplomats and spies can bribe enemy/barbarian ships, as long as they are sufficiently distant from a home city. This can be done either from a shore or ship-to-ship at sea. (I always try to bribe a barbarian frigate as early in the game as possible to provide combat support for my trade caravans.) Oddly enough, you can also bombers that are airborne; talk about an ability to jump high!

As for thousands of units, the max in the game is 2046.
