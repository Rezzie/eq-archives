Each terrain entry consists of 4 blocks of data.
1. food-shields-trade paramters
2. irrigation parameters (starting with the yes/no/terrain stuff)
3. mining parameters (starting with the 2nd yes/no/terrain stuff)
4. terrain transformation (whether possible or not, if so what)

This part
"Irrigate/Mine = yes, no, or type of terrain changed to"
details each irrigation/mining block.

The special terrain entries such as wheat or buffalo only list the first block. The irrigation and mining parameters are the same as their basic terrain type.

It means +1 bonus, 5 turns to make the change, and minimum AI (computer player) government to want to make this change. The last number only affects AI players.

For more shields with mining -for instance- you just alter the figures in the "mining block" -you can figure it out if you look at the numbers under mountains and hills.

To my knowledge you cannot add extra trade, unless you make a "special" terrain for it in the next single columns. There are 8 types of terrains, then 8 first types of specials, then 8 second type specials. They all go in the same appearance, as they should in the terrain01.gif-file, if you take one column at a time.

If you feel confused, just experiment a bit with the numbers. It's not really difficult to figure out.
