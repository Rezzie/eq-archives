A starting strategry I came up for this scenario:

<u>Capturing Capua</u>

First you move 4 Roman legions onto the hill right next to Capua (the hill above the whale). The Independents will attack your legions for sure, but you will not lose any units because the hill gives your legions defense bonus. Don't attack them in turn 2 because your legions must be damaged, fortify them on the hill instead. They will attack your legions again in turn 2; you still will not lose any unit. By turn three, all your fortified units on the hill are in good health. Now is the time to attack!! Capua should only have 1 or 2 units left and you will be able to take Capua in turn 3! Use the same strategy to capture Paestum.

I play this scenario on emperor level. The year is 348 BC and I have just captured the barbarian city Mediolanum. I have a total of 8 cities. http://forums.civfanatics.com/ubb/smile.gif
