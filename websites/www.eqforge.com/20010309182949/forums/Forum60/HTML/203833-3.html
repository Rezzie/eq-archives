This is something I am posting on my boards. Please feel free to repost it (with credit to Scout Strongbow) . . .

There is an event being publicized on all the major news sites which will be happening on July 1st at 10am Pacific (server) time. It will be held on The Nameless server, where people will be creating young Ranger characters to assault Crushbone.

This event is being held in support of the issues currently being ignored by Verant Interactive representitives. These issues circulate around the post 50 (as this is when the changes could have been made) defensive abilites of the Ranger class, as well as the issues with the "Hate List" which drive Rangers to the top constantly.

I would urge all of you who have seen a Ranger in action at high levels to lend your support to this activity. It's not funny watching someone using even inefficient forms of attack still get pounded mercilessly by creatures. The sad fact is that at high levels Rangers are being relegated to a role where they can no longer properly contribute to their groups. Instead of a proud contributor, they have become a sad limitation on their companions.

This injustice is being ignored. Multiple attempts at emailing Verant Interactive personnel have been ignored. Multiple threads upon Verant's own boards have been closed without a single word, after HUNDREDS of legitimate (non-BUMP) posts. The Ranger community is banding together to try and have Verant take a legitimate look at the way combat works at high levels. We do not believe they have accurately seen the effects of having a Ranger in a party at advanced levels.

Please, I urge you to lend your support. With the recent release of Ruins of Kunark, things which high level Rangers were seeing before attaining post 50 levels are becoming only more apparent. Without any kind of legitimate evasion ability, Rangers are unable to deal with the effects of dealing damage as Rogues and Monks are. Without the higher defensive skills and armor of Warriors, Paladins, and Shadowknights, Rangers are subjected to hideous abouse at the hands of creatures in the new lands.

In fact, the defensive abilities of Rangers, previously higher than other hybrids, (arguably to account for their "chain-wearing" status) are now lower than other hybrid classes. Ruins of Kunark did not raise melee skills in line with what had been available before. The astounding ability of a Ranger to taunt creatures (even off a caster who had burned too hard, or a bard playing to many beneficial songs, or a Rogue who had done 250 points in ONE attack) is turning more and more into a death knell for the profession.

So please, the Rangers wish to do nothing more than continue to do their job. To be able to scout ahead, engage enemies, return to their group, and fight side by side with their comrades, not in the back of the pack like some crippled Druid.

Thank you for your time.

The problem is it doesn't do anything to get Verant's attention. It doesn't slow anything down, cause any disturbance, or tie up their support. It doesn't make the Guides or GM's deal with more than the usual issues, it just logs an abnormally large number of players into one zone.

In real life a sitin creates a slowdown, or disturbance, that actually affects the target of the sitin.

I suppose it doesn't hurt our cause any, but we're all rallying behind it as if it's some major deal that is going to attract a lot of Verant attention. The "Wrath of the Ranger?" It's like saying "Beware or you will feel the wrath of my flamelick spell, Lord Nagafen!" It's insignificant. An support email flood campaign, a petition flooding campaign, a /bug /feedback campaign, these would actually force them to stop ignoring us, even if they could still pretend to ignore us. This, while a nice show of solidarity, does nothing to show Verant any sort of opposition worth mentioning.

Now, if anyone wants to start an email/petition/bug/feedback campaign, I would be delighted. This? I may show up at this event, but what's its purpose? How does it really help our cause? We need so much more...this is attacking a fully armored and armed knight with a nail file.

Call it a first step that we are DEFINITELY taking farther, and I'll be happy. But expect no results out of this.
