Hiyas!

/ooc I love to just wander around and meet people and just well....experience this nifty world we "live" in.

I would like to tell a short story.

I had just finished helping a band of adventurers in keeping the dreaded Dervish Cutthroat band from doing any more harm to the locals, when one jumped me as I was on my way to the Inn for a well deserved drink. I was still recovering from helping the adventurers and so wasn't quite in top form. I turned, dodged the blow meant for my head, and returned the favor. The Cutthroat and I would have been evenly matched had I not just taken out some of his fellows. As it was he had the upper hand and was using my injuries to his advantage. Suddenly a young Kodiak bear roars and runs over to help me. The young Kodiak reached out and slashed the Derv across the back. This gave me a chance to catch my breath and regroup my resources. I then kicked the Derv, catching him in the stomach, and allowing the Kodiak to get in another good swipe with his huge claws. Together we made sure the Derv would never be a threat to anyone again.


I have to say that this experience, so far, is my most favorite memory. I truely felt like a Ranger at that moment. So the point of all this....

Don't get between Kori and her after work drink....errr...I mean, ummm, would anyone else like to share their experiences of what they feel makes them uniquely Ranger?

Proudest Rangerly thing I did...let's see:

So I joined the Shadow Stalkers of Norrath on Innorruk, which was orignally a Rogue-only guild. And then they started to recruit different classes. I was probablly the only ranger around 20's in the guild.

We were in Sol A and I was 23 and the youngest in the group. My party had a enchanger, a necro, a druid, and two rogues. We were camping both the Kobold Predator and the Forman, picking up experience at the same time. So I took up the roles of the puller and the tank.

So, I would go off, pull a couple goblins back, tank so that the rogue can backstab and the casters can cast. We finished them, and I would go off again. I controlled what to pull, when to pull and I kept my track on. As soon as I saw Kobold, I ran to his spot and pulled him. All my party just sat comfortably in the room and waited to fight and loot. Everyone walked away with either Kobold Hide Boots or Foreman's Tunic.

It was a lot of work for me and sometimes if I pulled a Wiz, I could go down to one bbl before I got back to the group. But I did not mind because I was proud of what I did. A couple guild mates told me later that I did a great job that day

My Rangerly story:

I was killing icy orcs with a druid friend in Everfrost, and it was getting late. We decided to head to Halas to sell. Now my druid friend had just reached lvl 24 and had gotten wolfform and Spirit of Cheetah. She always keeps me nicely sowed as well.

She was so happy about her new spells that she challenged me to a race to Hallas. I was in a good mood so I accepted. *Zoom* off she went. As a ranger I feel it is my duty to know how to get from A to B, so I started off on the shortest route I knew thru the icy lanes. I beat her by about 2 solid minutes -- she got lost .

Ya I've done the "find a sowable partner and track down stuff in SK" lots of times, much more fun than camping. But shhhh! don't let too many people find out! I like how there's no competition.
