AE Rain Spell Tip:
Madcat Warwiz sent in a tip for getting our rain spells to work somewhat correctly. Some may know this method and some may not -

"Recently we have acquired a really nice rain spell Tears of Druzzil and Tears of Solusek as Wizards. Some of you may have noticed that when fighting with pets, the pet tends to absorb one of the pulses making an extremely mana efficient spell become a mana hog. There is nothing more satisfying than to see a 380 mana spell hit for three pulses of 600 doing 1800 dam to the mob. As many of you know trying to root and shoot with this spell pretty much will get you killed so you need tanks and or pets to keep it in one place while you do the real damage.

So the solution we found to this dilemma of losing a pulse to a pet was to target something near the mob but far from the pet. This could be another player or even a dead corpse on the ground. It will take a little bit of experimentation on your part to figure out what is close enough to the mob so you hit it but far enough away from the pet so that the pet will not absorb one of the pulses. Using this strategy will allow us as wizards to use our best damage to mana spell in alot more situations. Happy blasting!!!

Madcat Warwiz
54 Wiz Prexus
Lords of Honor"

Thanks Madcat! Didn't GZ tell me rain spells were going to be fixed before the Kunark release?
