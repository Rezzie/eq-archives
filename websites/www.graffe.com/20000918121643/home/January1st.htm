Meram de'Thorne of Tunare wrote this editorial called "Unfair Stereotypes in EverQuest":
There are two main stereotypes in EverQuest to my knowledge. One, the kill-stealing Wizard, and two, the kiting Druid. I have not played a Wizard, but have enough Wizard friends to know that they are snubbed enough by the general EverQuest population to make it a problem. Personally, as a Druid, I have had several encounters with people who have put me in this stereotype and have treated me rather unfairly.
Yes, there are kill-stealing Wizards, and yes, there are also kiting Druids. But what you don't see/hear a lot about is kiting Bards, or Necromancers; or kill-stealing Enchanters. Do not misunderstand, I am not stereotyping these classes. Far from it, but I am saying that each of those classes have the potential to kill-steal, or kite.
For example, Bards are MUCH better kiters than Druids. Their Selo`s Accelerando song is faster than SoW after Level 6. They also have multiple DoT/DD songs, which do not require mana. Therefore, since they are faster than Druids, and have the potential to do more damage (since they do not need mana to inflict it), they are perhaps the best kiters in EverQuest.
Wizards have a horrible reputation for stealing kills with their high damage DDs. But, a Level 4 Enchanter could steal a kill from a Level 50 Wizard any day. How? Enchanter's Mesmerize-Line of spells give them the potential to steal ANY kill. They simple cast Mesmerize on the target when it is at a sliver of life, and then kill the target before the person who was fighting it before can react. Mesmerize acts as a memory wipe, and therefore allows an Enchanter to kill-steal without having to use high power DDs.
The vast majority of Wizards do not steal kills. The same goes for Druids. A recent news article on EQ Vault said, and I quote: "Why are some of these spawns EXACTLY 12 hours apart which allows kill stealing wizards/druids to teleport in 15 seconds before the creature spawns and kill it and steal it from the group that has been sitting there waiting for hours?" That is a horribly unfair statement. Excuse me? Wizards and Druids have Personal and Group Teleports, yes, but not to specific NPC spawns. We don't have a spell that is called "Personal Teleport: Froglok King" or "Personal Teleport: Hadden." Eight out of the fifteen classes in EverQuest recieve the spells "Gate" and "Bind Affinity," therefore, Druids and Wizards are not the ONLY classes who have the potential to steal kills.
My intent in writing this editorial was to try to make people think before they say, "Druids suck! All you do is kite all day," or "Your just a kill-stealer like ALL Wizards!" So, in conclusion, I ask the general EverQuest population not to judge Wizards, Druids, or any other class by what others of their class may have done, but to judge them by their actions.
-Meram de'Thorne
Druid
Tunare Server
