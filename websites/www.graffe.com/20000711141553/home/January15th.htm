Vanya Terra'Null wrote a little bit of common sense here:
"I have noticed a trend in current GM events. When players can not figure out what a GM controlled NPC wants, they turn on him and want to kill him, gathering up as many people in the zone as possible to try and pound the NPC into dust. On my server today, Quellious, there were several tiny Sand Giants that had appeared and wanted to play games with the PCs nearbye. This was real fun at first, as we played tag, got them Orcs to squish [they really enjoyed squishing things] and generally roleplayed with us. I brought them some things to kill, gave them some wine I was carrying, talked to them [with little response, as they were being talked to be everyone] and tried to have a decent time. Then after a bit some players got bored of this and wanted to get items from the little giants, since they were not giving them away, they tried to take them by force. It was a futile effort at first as they could not muster up enough dmg to make a different, but after a bit many many more people joined and started to actually hurt the playful giants, a lot! The whole time they were being attacked they asked us via shouts why were we attacking them when all they wanted to do was to play? I tried to help the giants while they were being attacked, but since I am a wizard there is very little I can do to help other than try to get the giants out of there [I gave them several warnings to run away so they would not have to die]. After about 30 mins of solid combat, some of the PCs gave up and the battle became somewhat light, this is where I was summoned to the "lead" Sand Giant. At first I thought it was part of their games, cause they did this before. [they would summon someone back and forth across the zone and roleplay that they were having a game of catch with you as the ball! :) ] After i tried to get away for the 6th or 7th time and was resummoned back time and again, I sat down and smiled at him. Then he gave me a Rubicite Helm! He said thank you for the games and the help. About 10 mins after that all the playful Giants went away. As far as I know I was one of the few people to get any item at all, and I got it just because I was nice to them. Anyway, the point of this email is that force is not always the way to get things, and when it comes to GM events, listen to the GMs and try to play as best you can with what it is they want to do. They are not looking for the perfect role playing time, but rather just to see the ingenuity of people, and to make others have a good time. Sorry this email is so huge bud, but I feel this is something that people have to try an learn if they want to participate in GM events and make them successful for all involved. Have a safe journey.
Vanya Terra'Null - Quellious."
Thanks Vanya!

With all of the conflicting reports of how many mobs a certain Area Effect (AoE) spell can hit running around lately, I figured I would ask GZ what the real issue is:
Graffe: "Is there a limit as to how many mobs any Area Effect can hit? Lately I have been getting conflicting reports on all AoE's, but mainly Supernova. Many say Supernova was 'nerfed'."
GZ: "There has been a limit to the number of mobs an AoE can hit since early Beta. The bigger the AoE, the more mobs it can effect - but it has been like that forever. It was from early beta when we'd get one person collecting monsters, and then a few Wizards casting AoE's and getting 20 or so mobs per hit."
footnote from Graffe: Keep in mind that just like any spell we cast, it is a roll of the dice on how it hits. Therefore, you may only hit a few mobs with a level 49 AoE and you may hit 10 mobs with a level 16 AoE (although I've never done it).
