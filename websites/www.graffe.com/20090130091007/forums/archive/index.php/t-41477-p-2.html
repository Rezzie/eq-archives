- Modules and ships under the tech level 2 seem to be far and few between releases while discussion about tech level 3 ships...?


Ok, this is a complicated thing to answer/clarify. In EvE there are two tech levels. Tech1 gear is the most common as the BPO's can be bought straight from the market, the plus side is that it's dirt cheap, but in EvE you generally get what you pay for, tjhese mods/ships are the most basic in the game. Tech2 items are more expensive for a few reasons, first and most importantly, Tech2 BPO's are incredibly hard to obtain, second they require things that can only be obtained from a POS to create. However, Tech2 items are usually worth it, they can give you a huge advantage no matter what you're doing. Also, there are named mods, these are considered Tech1, but are better than their actual Tech1 Counterparts. Depending on the mod they can be only a minor upgrade, to rivalling Tech2 items in power.

The tier system is really different. For example: an osprey, Caracal, Moa, Eagle, and Cerberus are four Caldari cruiser class ships. And Osprey is a Tech1 Tier1 ship because it only requires Caldari Cruiser 1 to fly, while a Caracal is a Tier2 Tech1 Cruiser because it requires Caldari Cruiser 2 to fly, whereas a Moa is a Tier3 Tech1 Cruiser because you need Caladari Cruiser 3 to fly. IMO a Moa and a Caracal are on the same footing when it comes to power, but that's because I'm a missile whore. It should be mentioned that Tier3 ships take more minerals to make, therefore making them more expensive than a Tier2 or Tier1 ship. The Cerberus and Eagle however, are extremely powerful ships because they're Tech2 ships. A Cerb is a Tech2 Caracal while an Eagle is a Tech2 Moa, these are technically called Heavy Assault Cruisers, or HAC's and, depending on the situation, can destroy Battleships.

Tiers are a complicated thing to explain, because some ship classes have 3 tiers(cruisers, frigs) some have 2(Battleships), and some have only one tier(HAC's come to mind).

Hope that helped.
