Caemien
September 10th, 2004, 02:08 PM
Between 6-6.5k you should be ok quadding without having to med. Make sure to have KEI or VoQ and FoE helps if you don't have run 5.

I'm at 8.3k mana pool and I end up with about 20-30% of my mana left after a quad.

Another note is to time your snares. You can use a timer for this, or do what I do. Cast snare, do /ti for a time check so that I know when to recast. I actually pull with snare as well, though I know some Wizards like to round up the mobs and then snare. I find it a bit safer to pull with snare. Once I get them all rouned up I recast snare on the group to ensure they are all on the same snare timer.

One thing I have noted is that snares land on the mobs always in the same order. So if you get a resist or two, note which of the 4 mobs were snared and which were not.

i.e.

You begin casting snare
A Doomfire Protector was shackled to the ground
A Doomfire Protector was shackled to the ground
A Doomfire Protector resist Atol's spectral shackles
A Doomfire Protector resist Atol's spectral shackles


In this example the 1st 2 are snared, start your timer, recast snare and ensure mobs 3 and 4 get snared. Your timer should be based on the 1st mob that got snared, not the last. I also begin my recast of snare at 2 minutes rather than waiting until the 2:30 timer is almost expired. This gives me 30 seconds to ensure all 4 mobs are snared again before it wears off.

As with other quad kiting efforts, try working on one mob 1st. If you can kill one mob with pillar of frost on your mana pool, then you can do 4. I would venture to say that 25% of my mana used is on snares and the rest is used for pillars.
