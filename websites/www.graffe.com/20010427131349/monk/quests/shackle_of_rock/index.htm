Starts In: East Cabilis
Quest NPC Initiator: Master Niska

You can only complete this quest after you have completed the Shackle of Stone quest.

Find the female monk trainer named 'Master Niska' in the East Cabilis Monk Guild -

You say, 'what about the shackle of rock?'
Master Niska strikes within inches of your snout with amazing speed. 'The shackle of rock is your interest? Then learn well our ways and prepare yourself. If the stone embraces your wrist, you are ready indeed. To complete the second rung you must find for me the [Fists of Talon].'

You say, 'what are the fists of talon?'
Master Niska points to the Court Chronicler. 'All that is known of Talon has been chronicled. Should you obtain the fists then you shall hand them to me. This shall earn you the shackle of rock.'

Directly across from Master Niska is a male monk merchant named 'the Court Chronicler'. He will sell you a book titled 'Talon Southpaw' for 3 copper (it's a no rent item). This book will describe the life of Talon Southpaw.

Book of Talon Southpaw
"Talon Southpaw was the fastest pugilist ever to walk within the Court of Pain. His speed rivaled that of the great Grandmaster Tynn. He could often be found within the local taverns demonstrating the awesome speed of his left fist.
Talon Southpaw came to rebirth within this caste. Although quick with fist and tail, the speed of his left arm was not to come to blossom for a couple of decades past his rebirth. It was to follow his captivity by the accursed sarnak.

Soon after fulfilling the Rung of Tynn, Talon went on a wandering retreat into the dangerous outlands of Kunark. He was not heard from for a total of 5 seasons. Upon his return to Cabilis he told of his captivity within an ancient city of the Iksar now claimed by the sarnak. He told of intense torture. He survived the ordeal. He escaped from the claws of the sarnak with numerous scars which proved his painful ordeal. Most of the worst scars were never seen. Talon wore a robe which he never removed. The scars underneath were for no lizard's eyes. His great perseverance gave him an inner strength no other monk could lay claim to.
He now possessed incredible, almost blinding speed with his left arm. He could trap a foe's arm with his left and strike with it as if he had a third arm! He was indeed a paragon of the Swifttails. All in the empire either respected or feared him. None would spar him outside of the taverns. With grog comes stupidity. Upon his death in Kurn, Grandmaster Tynn removed his hands in a private ceremony. Only Tynn was allowed to gaze upon Talon's body. The fists were then sealed in a canopic which would stay within the Tower of Kurn His body was dusted. This is his tale as told by Rinmark."

If you read the above, you'll learn that Talon frequented the local taverns of Cabilis. So head over to the Tink N Babble and talk to the bartender 'Klok Poklon' found on the second floor for a bit more info if you want -

You say, 'hail Klok Poklon'
Klok Poklon says 'Greetings, thirsty traveler. Pull up a stool and lay yer copper down. It's time to drink and be merry. Just don't get too merry or I will have to call the troopers.

You say, 'Who was Talon Southpaw?'
Klok Poklon feints with two quick left hooks!! 'Talon Southpaw. He had the fastest hands in the empire. I remember he once beat another lizard in an arm wrestling match. The amazing thing was, while he was gripping the croak's left hand, he left-hooked him without losing his hold!! That's quick!! I hear one of his treasured hands is in the possession of a monk called Master Rinmark.'

You can find a little bit about the whereabouts of Master Rinmark by talking to Master Bain who is found in the Court of Pain (Cabilis Monk Guild) -

You say, 'where is Master Rinmark?'
Master Bain says 'Seeking Master Rinmark? He has left the Court of Pain. Gone to focus his thoughts on the elements of wind and thunder. I believe he was headed toward the Overthere. That was the last I heard of his trek.

Now to find Master Rinmark. Even though Master Bain in the Court of Pain says he was headed toward the Overthere, it seems Rinmark ventured a bit further. He's all the way out in the middle of the Timorous Deep on top of the rocks on the island better known as 'chessboard isle' at location -5060, +2800. He's not labeled as 'Master Rinmark' but labeled simply as 'an iksar master'. Be careful out here, there is another NPC down on the sand that really does not like little iksars. Try speaking to the iksar master -

You say, 'hail an iksar master'
an iksar master is humming and chanting softly. He seems oblivious to your presence.

You say, 'Who is Master Rinmark?'
an iksar master squints one eye open, then returns to his meditation.

You say, 'What about the fists of Talon Southpaw?'
an iksar master waves his arms in the air. 'The great master. His soul is the wind. He speaks to us in words of thunder. He strikes Norrath with bolts of lightning. I seek to be as he is. I have traveled the globe in search of his fists. I have found only one. Another is said to be with [Gomoz]. I shall return it when I am finished with my seasons-long meditation or perhaps I shall find a monk to [return the hand to Cabilis].'

You say, 'Who is Gomoz?'
an iksar master says 'Disciple Gomoz was once Talon Southpaw's pupil. It is rumored that he has only one hand. Unfortunately, a horde of ogre raiders swiped his bones, along with others, from their place in Kurn's Tower. What those simple oafs plan to do with them is a mystery. Only one who has studied the arcane sciences could use them. They most likely used them as soup bones.

You say, 'I will return the hand to Cabilis.'
an iksar master hands you an old tattered sack. 'This shall be your proof of allegiance. Prove that you are strong with an intact mandible of a skulking brutling and the claws of a burynai excavator. Prove that you are a monk of fist and tail with a sash of an exiled human monk and the sparring gloves of a goblin pit fighter. Combined, these items make the sack full. This will earn my trust and the hand.'

He gives you an old tattered sack for you to put the four items in. We'll do it in the order that he gave them in.

The first item he wants is an intact mandible of a skulking brutling. The real item name is 'Intact Brutling Choppers'. These only come from 'a skulking brutling'. Not from skulking runtlings or skulking bruts. In order to find this little orange monkey, goto Warslik Woods and head over to the general area of -2000, +3000 (just South of the iksar bandit lake) and start looking. To help get the spawn rotating, start killing off goblin bonecasters and skulking runtlings. This item took me about two hours to acquire and a ranger really helps doing this part.

The second item he wants is the claws of a burynai excavator. The real item name is 'Excavator Claws'. You can easily find one of these on the burynai excavators in Kurns Tower as well as in the Field of Bones.

The third item he asks for is the sash of an exiled human monk. The real item name is 'Torn Sash' and can be found on 'a young ronin' at any dervish cutthroat camp in North Ro, Oasis, or South Ro. The best place I found to acquire this in Oasis due to it has two camps very close to each other and each camp has 3 spawns. Just keep cycle killing each camp until he spawns. You don't hail him.. you just kill him and take the sash from his corpse. This took me about 3 hours to acquire.

The fourth item asked for is the sparring gloves of a goblin pit fighter. The actual name for this item is 'Goblin Sparring Gloves'. These are also found in Warslik Woods at the goblin fortress in the Northeast area of the zone. The mob you are looking for is a goblin pitfighter. Not 'Pitfighter Dob'. In order to get him to spawn, start killing anything you can in the fortress. Everytime I have seen him, he's spawned in the tents but you might find him at another location in the fortress. His spawn point is random. You can sometimes get lucky if there are some high level players there trying to get Grachnist the Destroyer to spawn. Just nicely ask them if you can kill or loot the goblin pitfighter if he spawns.

Now that you have all four items, put them in the 'Tattered Sack from Rinmark' and hit combine. It will turn into a 'Full Sack for Master'. Now take this back to the iksar master (Master Rinmark) in the Timorous Deep and hand it over -

an iksar master bows before you and takes a wrinkled, mummified hand from a pouch at his side. 'Take the hand. Let it rest within Cabilis and be reunited with the left.'

Your faction standing with Swifttails got better
Your faction standing with LegionOfCabilis got better

You now have the Iksar Right Hand. Now you are done dealing with Rinmark, but remember how he talked about the Ogre Raiders that stole the bones? Well the Ogres are actually in Timorous Deep as well. They are found on the island that houses the dock for the evil boat at about +6000, +3000. You will need to deal with a male ogre named 'The Great Oowomp'. He is now indifferent to iksars but the problem is that there is usually a female ogre raider in the house right behind him within aggro distance and she is KOS. This really wouldn't be an issue but as soon as she aggro's you, The Great Oowomp aggros as well. So you can try a couple things -

  1. See if there are any other players camping the ogres for experience and have them kill the ogre raider behind Oowomp for you.
  2. Attempt to FD pull to a safe distance out of the camp but if you are not careful or you'll end up like me. One thing to remember if you try this method is that the skelly you are trying to get the Great Oowomp to summon is going to end up back in town where he was standing. So if you don't kill the female KOS ogre, the skelly is going to summoned right there next to her.

Once all is clear and Oowomp is alone, hand him 5 gold pieces -

The Great Oowomp flings the coins into the air and they all fall neatly into his oversized coin pouch. <Tink, tink, tink, tink, TINK!!> 'Gaze in awe at my awesome powers of the arcane!! You, a simple citizen, shall see my power. Allakabam!!'

A dancing skeleton is summoned.

You say, 'What about Talon Southpaw?'
a dancing skeleton says 'Master Talon Southpaw!! He was my master. Dead, he became. Off to the elements. I keep his special hand with me. Perhaps I should have it returned. Maybe I shall find a young adventurer to [return the hand to Cabilis].

You say, 'I will return the hand to Cabilis'
a dancing skeleton says 'Yes!! Return the hand. Here it is. Missing four it is. Within the tower of past pain and torture is where the four lie. Taken by bones similar to myself.

You now have what looks like one of the fists, but it's actually a 4 slot bag called hand with one only a thumb. With this, you will need to goto Kurns Tower and fill it with four different withered fingers from a 'fingered skeleton'. The four fingers are known as, a withered 2.5" finger, a withered 3" finger, a withered 3.1" finger, a withered 4" finger. Take note that all four of these fingers are droppable so you might get lucky by simply ooc'ing for some. To find the fingered skeleton, you have to go downstairs into the basement where the burynai's reside. I have marked this map with a bright green line to show the fingered skeleton's pathing. The fingered skeleton is on a 20 minute spawn rotation and his place holders include the Thick boned skeleton. Once you have all four withered fingers, put them in the 'hand with one only a thumb' and hit combine. You will have the first Iksar Left Hand. Remember the book spoke that he could strike like he had three arms or hands? It was true, there's one more to go.

Now for the last fist.. the 'second' left fist. This was no picnic and I highly suggest getting a ranger to help you with this part as well. The left fist is found on a Sarnak Courier in the Lake of Ill Omen. He is a somewhat rare spawn and the majority of the time he is found wandering the area just North of the Windmill near the ruins. I found him smack dab in the middle of the field at about -1500, -2500 but there are rumors he has even been seen on the other side of the zone near the goblin mines. Like I said... get a ranger to help. This part of the quest, even with a ranger helping, took me about 7 hours. Once you kill the Sarnak Courier, you will pull the last hand from the corpse.. the second Iksar Left Hand.

Now you have three hands. Take all three of them back to the female monk trainer, Master Niska, in the Court of Pain and hand them over. Take note that you only have to hand over the hands, you get to keep your Shackle of Stone this time. Hit the trade button -

Master Niska removes a crudely hewn shackle. 'This is yours. It is one of the keys to the third rung. I see that you are truly a great monk and have studied your disciplines well. I have need of one such as you. I have heard of [troubles with an outlander].'

Your faction standing with Swifttails got better.
Your faction standing with LegionOfCabilis got better.
You gain experience!!
You receive 1 gold from Master Niska.

Summary:

  • I recommend getting all of the items together before heading out to the iksar master in the Timorous Deep. This way you only have to make one trip. Get the bag, put the already acquired items in it, hit combine, hand it over. Then on your way out, stop by and do the part with the Great Oowomp.
  • You'll get frustrated at the long, boring camps for this quest but if you look at it as you can gain experience while doing it (many quests weren't designed this way) and the fact that you must do it to continue with the shackle quest series, it's not all that bad.
