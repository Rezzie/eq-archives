September 22nd, 2006, 07:15 AM
Skills that you do not possess can be purchased through the market as a book. Once you purchase the skill, you can ~read~ the book (train for that skill). The "simple" skills can be purchased through many of the School stations while other "complex" skills I believe are given as rewards. Also, many skills' difficulty are rated by a ranking number, which means a multiplier in the amount of skill points required to reach the next skill level. As an example, Engineering 1 is a Rank 1 skill, which would take depending upon your primary and secondary stats approximately fifteen minutes (or less). But Battlecruisers 1 is a Rank 6 skill, which again depending upon your primary and secondary stats, would take around six times as long to train.

Hopefully this will help a bit.

Outfit ship like this:

Standard Missile Launcher I [Flameburst Light Missile]
Standard Missile Launcher I [Flameburst Light Missile]
280mm Howitzer Artillery I [Nuclear S]
280mm Howitzer Artillery I [Nuclear S]

1MN Afterburner I
Small Shield Booster I
Cap Recharger I

Reactor Control Unit I
Power Diagnostic System I
Overdrive Injector System I

That can be fitted with a minimum of skill training. The tactic works like this.

pre) have "Keep Distance" set to max distance on guns. Should be around 21,000 km

1) Hit Afterburner immediately after warping in
2) Select closest enemy after warping in and hit keep distance
3) Now target that closest ship (never fire on this ship until everybody else is dead, this guy is the spacer that keeps you from having to worry about your kiting distance)
4) Target a second ship and hit it with all guns.
5) Target as many ships as your skills/ship allow
6) Kill ships one at a time with all fire power on single ship (try to kill elite such and such first)
7) Pulse shield booster if shield set to far down (ideally keep shields above 50%)
8) Be extremely careful to keep moving. The spacer ship does this, but it's still important to say. Let the enemy ships catch up and your a dead wizard (or you warp out).
9) Kill last ship and loot

With the above ship equipping you will be able to fly with afterburner turned on at like 900 m/s, which is faster than any NPC ship will fly in level 1 missions. Also with those long distance (for a frigate) guns and that amo you will be able to keep yourself out of your enemies optimal firing range while keeping them in yours.

It would be better to use one Auxiliary Power Control instead of the Reactor Control and Power Diagnostic System, but that requires a skill at level 4 and I was trying to keep the skills easier to acquire.

Also, one might go 3xGuns and 1xMissile, but I like missiles.
