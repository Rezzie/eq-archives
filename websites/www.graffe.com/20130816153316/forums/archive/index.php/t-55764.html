Arcomancy

Assuming the worst possible case – that you are the only one nuking the mob:

There are 32 possible outcomes over the next 5 nukes when arcomancy procs; only two of these cases allow for all 5 procs to be used (52.2% of the time). The next most common case is that only 1 proc is used (15% of the time), with the other cases ranked 2 3 4 procs used. Overall, the average efficiency of arcomancy in the worst case is ~74.2%. This means:

Arcomancy 6 = 1250 x 5 x .742 = 4637.5 damage per proc, or 4637.5 * .15 = 695.625 damage per spell cast

When using 1 magic nuke and 1 of any other nuke, the efficiency is 88.5%, so:

Arcomancy 6 = 1250 x 5 x .885 = 5531.25 damage per proc, or 5531.25 * .075 = 414.814 damage per spell cast.


The quickest possible magic/magic chain is 5.5 seconds which means Arcomancy adds ~253 damage/second at its maximal state.

In a TW+WMB chain, the dps is lower, at 102.4 damage/second.


What is interesting is arcomancy on raids is actually significantly better than it is in a group. In a perfect world, all 5 procs could be used within a single cast + spell recast, thereby increasing the efficiency of the effect to 100% or 340.9 damage/second when chaining CBB/CBS.


Pyromancy

Pyromancy on the other hand, has actually decreased in per proc efficiency post spell revamp, as the number of casts possible in 30 seconds has increased. Also, it’s maximum dps addition is 166.7 damage. EDIT - What's interesting about pryomancy is that while it's per proc efficiency has decreased, the actual dps addition has increased; Because the damage is independent of your casts, a second proc during the current damage event is not an unfavorable condition -- in fact you're significantly more likely to proc a new cast of pyromancy before the first is complete than not! So while you gain less per proc, the volatility of the additional damage is much lower. The likely case (~76%) is that you will continuously have pyromancy on the mob. Still, the cost is excessive and the dps gain is significantly lower than Arcomancy. To be competitive, Pyromancy 6 should be increased in damage by as much as 2.05x, while reducing the AA cost by about 2 per level. I'm interested in seeing exactly where the average damage of this effect sits.


Cryomancy

Cryomancy is an easy model, since the direct damage portion is always the same, and never fails to damage -- it has the highest damage volatility but a perfect efficiency at 510 damage per cold spell cast. Unfortunately this means 127.5 damage per second on average, which is significantly lower than even Pyromancy. 4637 damage per proc for rank 6 would put the damage impact equivalent to the average arcomancy case.

Tangleweave Energy

Tangleweave Energy impacts our damage multiplier:

In Focus: 2.059 x
Out of Focus: 1.884 x
Tangleweaved; 2.384 x

The difference between our normal damage cases and our Tangleweave case is:

In Focus: 2.384 - 2.059 = .325 @ 25% rate = + .08125 x; this equates to .08125 / 2.059 = 3.95% increase in the average damage of the next spell cast.
Out of Focus: 2.384 - 1.884 = .500 @ 25% rate = .125 x; this equates to .125 / 1.884 = 6.63% increase in the average damage of the next spell cast.

The problem with Tangleweave is you actually have to cast it, and it really isn't very good. It is better efficiency than any of the other "core" nukes, but that doesn't mean that it is better than the nukes we actually [should] use on a daily basis.

Your best case for TW + X, for overall dps gained is TW + Ethereal Incineration:

11565 + (19337 x 1.0663 = 20661) / 9.63 = 3346 dps -- no recourse dps of 3209, for a gain of 137 dps or 4.27% overall.
Efficiency of 32227/2163 = 14.90 damage per mana -- no recourse dpm of 14.28 for a gain of .61 dpm.

For reference, the worst case is TW + Claw of Selig, at about 1.5% increase in dps.

The problem is, there is *always* a better case for DPS and DPM for all variations on X when you're trying to maximize DPS; Adding a combination of FF/RS and WMS/WMB allow you to overwhelm any possible TW pattern except:

When you are casting TW + FF/RS or WMB. The TW + WMB case may actually be the most efficient damage possible at the DPS, I didn't try every possible case available but it seems to be that you cannot perform a spell cycle both more dpm and more dps than 2177 dps at 20.11 dpm, which is TW + WMB's damage. Arcomancy adds 102.5 dps to that making it 21.05 dpm. I didn't model FF/RS and TW, I will later maybe.

The problem I have, is trying to find a case where I'd want to maximize to 21.05 dpm. You gain massive efficiency on the way to 1550 dps, up to 30 dpm; you gain massive dps on the way down to 15 dpm, up to 4500. I just don't see a use for it, even though it is perhaps the most efficient damage you can do at a given dps.


Obviously, there are uses for TW when you want to use magic but not maximize burst -- it is the best magic nuke for efficiency by far. The only time I'd choose use magic for that purpose is if hell has frozen over, though. Arcomancy doesn't make up for the fact that our magic spells are trash.

I am going to have to disagree with your assessment of Cyromancy vs Pyromancy for the fact that you do not consider the distribution of the procs over the course of time. If you have two Pryos fire back to back the damage added by pyro mancy is 1000 points (since all it does is reset the dot timer from what I gather) however if you have two Cryos proc back to back your added damage is 3400 points. Take the following example encounter:

720 second encounter (12 minutes or 120 ticks)
200 spells casted (One spell cast every 3.6 seconds)
Average number of procs = 200*.15 = 30

Over the course of the fight, assuming the best proc distribution of 1 proc every (720/30) 24 seconds, the absolute most that pyromancy can add is (120 tics * 1000pts/tic) 120,000 points of damage.

If Cyromancy procs the exact number of times, regardless of when the procs happen, it would add (30*3400) or 102,000 points of damage.

If you don't consider when the procs are fired you would simply be able to say that Cyromancy added (102,000/720) 141.67 DPS and Pyromancy added (120,000/720) 166.67 DPS.

The previous calculations are an absolute MAX that pyromancy can put out and the average amount that Cyromancy can put out. If procs all happened back to back, although very improbable, pryomancy would only be up for 23 tics adding at a maximum of 23,000 pts of damage ... Cyromancy would still add the same 102k.

If you have an above average number of procs, pryomancy is still capped at the 120k, cyromancy remains uncapped. Suppose all 200 spells fire a mancy (again very improbable but it supports my point), pyro does only 120k, Cryomancy does an additional (3400*200) 680k.

Given the example above in order for Cyromancy to eclipse Pyromancy in added damage, there would need to be 36 procs instead of 30 over the course of 12 minutes or a proc rate of 20% instead of 15% ... anything equal to or greater then a proc rate of 20% and Cryomancy will out damage pryomancy regardless of how the procs are distributed over the course of a fight.

Don't be so quick to ride off Cyromancy as less powerfull then Pyromancy. :)
