Harvest Spell Info:
A few things all should know about Harvest -

  • Only found for sale within the Third Gate of Neriak. Just like the enchanter spells.
  • Besides using the Jboot / Staff of Temp Flux trick to get the mana to register, you can cast ANY spell in your current list and just 'duck' to cancel it.
  • Keep it loaded at all times if you intend to use it. It will take 10 minutes for it to even register on your spell buttons after you memorize it.

Harvest is Working:
In order to get Harvest to register correctly on your mana bar, you need to fire off a small spell (I used my Journeyman Boots because it uses no mana. You could also use a Staff of Temperate Flux). This is the same bug that plagued us when Verant incorporated the ability to meditate after 35 without having to stare at your book. The spell gave me approximately 3/4's of a bubble of mana when cast.
A very large thanks to Ignigena of Xegony for helping me with the debug.

A Couple Infobits:
I spent my morning pestering Geoff with a lot of questions and there was a couple things you all may find interesting:

  • Disintegrate: The level 60 spell that will destroy it's target and leave no corpse to loot. Some were wondering about experience and if it will do any damage to high level mobs like dragons or gods. I learned that you get zero experience for the kill and if the mob is too big, it just won't hit it. With this spell it's all or nothing.
  • Flaming Sword of Xuzl: The level 59 spell that summons a plain, old, two handed sword to attack the targeted mob. I asked GZ what the chances were to get a 'flaming sword' like the description said. It looks like we will be getting that new animation in a month or so (don't hold him to a month.. give him some time) and it will be different than just the normal Fiery Avenger.

Disintegrate Spell:
I know a few wizards out there are wondering what kind of damage the level 60 spell Disintegrate can do. I got word from one wizard that has cast it that it will completely destroy the target. Now when it destroys the target, it also leaves no corpse to loot. Being that the wizard I spoke to was hesitant to see if it would destroy a god or a Dragon because of losing the loot, I asked Geoff Zatkin if the spell would do a lot of damage to that type of mob or just not even effect it. He informed me that the spell has a 'level cap'. So, casting this on a very large mob will probably only ensure that you will slammed right to the top of it's aggro list and be promptly killed.
