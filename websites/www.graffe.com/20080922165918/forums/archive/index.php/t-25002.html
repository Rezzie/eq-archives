So I start replaying on the hard difficulty, and guess what? When you go looking for the story you can almost find it. It appears that you can repeatedly talk to some of the vortegaunts by hitting the 'use' key. Some of them, instead of running through a couple canned responses, will tell you bits of their story. I'll probably restart again though, because I didn't try talking to as many of the humans as vortegaunts.

Basically, it looks like the vortegaunts were mostly unwilling slaves. They negotiated some sort of surrender to a 'lesser master' and a 'greater master', who then used them as shock troops to invade other worlds. When Gordon Freeman slew the 'lesser master' at the end of Half-Life 1, and the vortegaunts saw their opportunity for freedom, and their resistance began. This also explains what that laser beam was doing to the captured vortegaunts in the citadel; it was re-enslaving them to the master. Similarly with civil defense in city 17. They would be humans who were enslaved in the same way as the vortegaunts were.

The vortegaunts also use alot of pseudo-phsyics babble to try to get around to the idea that everyone exists as an extradimensional being, interconnected to one another. They insinuate Gordon is like them. They also mention something about a guiding force that is directing them, and Gordon alike; someone/thing that watches them. Sounded a little like they were talking about the G-man.

Dr Breen, the adminstrator of Black Mesa at the time of HL1 was the one who negotiated Earth's surrender. Eli Vance will share some of that story if you look at the cork board of newspaper clippings when you first get to Black Mesa East when he tells you to look around a bit. The newspaper clippings give the overall picture of: 7 hour war, Breen negotiates surrender, etc. Can't read much past the headlines, even with the HQ textures. I'd imagine whatever Dr Breen was talking to at the top of the Citadel was what the vortegaunts call the greater master.

That's pretty much what I came up with, though granted I haven't gone past Black Mesa East yet.

Couple easter eggs I noticed: on the cork board at black mesa east it looks kinda like one of the headlines says something about 9/11. thought that was ... out of place, but worth mentioning.
Also, more interestingly, and game-story pertinent it looks to me like the G-man was one of the original black mesa scientists. When you get to the first resistance hideout, to the one with the Dr with the pet Headcrab Lamarr.
