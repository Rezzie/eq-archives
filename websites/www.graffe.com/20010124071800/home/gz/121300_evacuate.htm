Level 57 Evacuate - Words from Geoffrey Zatkin.
December 13th, 2000

This conversation took place on Tuesday, December 12th 2000. This concerns a couple issues behind the level 57 wizard spell, Evacuate:

Graffe: Hello Geoff. I was wondering if you could look at and test the level 57 wizard spell, Evacuate. Supposedly, it is to use 750 mana from the caster. I've had people report it pulling in upwards of 1200 mana from the wizard when cast. Aside from the wizard, it is also draining mana from the party members (approximately 500 mana from party members).

GZ: Heh - it has always drained mana from party members. That was built into the spell from the very beginning. It is an anti-cheese method that we put in in Kunark beta - something we had to do since the teleport wipes the hate list (if we didn't wipe the hate list, imaging the trains...). If we didn't drain mana from the party, people would drag monsters to the evac point, fight a little, and then evac. Monster wanders off, gets blasted at range, and then the party evac's again. Repeat, and you have a new in-dungeon kiting system. This spell reduces the down time from having to travel back several zones to get to where you were - but there is still down time after you use the spell.

Graffe: Then the fact that it pulls more than 750 mana from the caster.

GZ: There is a mana cost - and then, when the spell actives, a mana drain. If you still have mana left, then it drains. If you don't have the mana, the spell still goes off, and then it drains your non-existant mana. So the guy with 1/2 mana looses less mana then the guy with full mana.
