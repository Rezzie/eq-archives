General Questions

  • Q. What level must I be to complete this quest?

  • A.  The epic quests were designed to be… well… epic!  You must be 50+ to complete these quests.  You can obtain the two staves and oil, but you should not turn in anything that is hard to get or important until you are 50+.

  • Q.  What items do I need for this quest?

  • A.  Quick breakdown.  Gnarled Staff (Drop Rate: Semi-Rare | Dropped from: Venril Sathir in Karnor’s Castle - Level 55 NPC with 15k HP | Spawn Time: 2.5 – 3.5 Days | Force Needed: 18+ depending on levels and guild skill level) – Blue Crystal Staff (Drop Rate: Rare | Dropped from: Phinigel Autropos in Kedge Keep - Level 51 NPC with 13k HP | Spawn Time: 13-14 Hours | Force Needed: 4-12 people depending on levels and skills) – Green Oil a Note to Arantir and Staff of Gabstick (Drop Rate: Common | Dropped from: An Enraged Golem in Plane of Fear - Level 63 NPC with 150k HP | Spawn Time: 24-72+ Hours | Force Needed: 12+ depending on levels and guild skill level).

  • Q. I am an evil race who is KoS in Erudin, Felwithe, Halas, and Butcherblok… can I do the epic, or am I screwed?

  • A. You’re fine.  All quest NPCs are on TrueSpirit faction – which everyone starts out as apprehensive to.  Simply follow the initial steps of the epic and you will raise your faction to indifferent, at least.  If you are apprehensive, don’t worry, the quest can be finished like this.

  • Q.  We killed Trakanon and Tolapumj dropped some Mistletoe powder.  I thought it was no longer needed for the quest.

  • A.  Nope, it’s not needed.  You can, however, turn it in to receive an extra Staff of Gabstick!

  • Q.  I’ve killed <insert epic mob name here> and he hasn’t dropped <insert epic item here> once!  What’s going on, please help!

  • A.  Don’t worry, a lot of this stuff is kind of rare (with the exception of the green oil, the enraged drops that every time).  There are people who have killed Phinigel over a hundred times and haven’t gotten a Blue Crystal Staff while others get it on their first try (I HATE YOU PEOPLE – LOL).  Keep trying, and don’t give up.
  • Q.  Hmm, I have three staves but no note.  Can I spawn Arantir with a potion or ring and turn them into him.
  • A.  NO!  The version of Arantir that is spawned with the note is a different version than what you get every other time.  If you had your three staves to a version other than the note spawned Arantir you will lose everything you have worked so hard for.
  • Q.  Ok, well, can I wait for another Wizard to spawn Arantir with a note and hand them in to that version?
  • A.  Again, another nope.  As soon as you turn your three staves into a note spawned Arantir he hands you the bag and disappears.  Don't even think about trying to steal another Wizards Arantir.  Not only is this wrong and a disgrace to the Wizard community, it's also something you will get banned for.

Solomen and Camin Questions

  • Q.  I turned my note from Solomen into Camin but received no faction adjustment.  What gives? 

  • A.  Nothing gives!  On 10/03/00 they removed the faction gain from this part of the quest because it was far to easy to raise your TrueSpirit faction.  Hey, you don’t want this to make it to easy, do you? 

  • Q.  Soandso sobs uncontrollably!  Camin won’t talk to me.  I gave him the note and asked him what he was searching for and he just told me to get lost!  I’m screwed, aren’t I?

  • A.  Calm down there, young Wizard.  You most certainly are not!  Just give Camin the 1000pp and continue on from there, everything will be ok.

  • Q.  I’m poor and only have about 600pp to my name.  Do I really have to give Camin the 1000pp? 

  • A.  Some say you don’t, but I personally suggest doing the quest exactly how it was meant to be done.  I hear Gornit in Ocean of Tears is a great moneymaker.

Arantir and Challice Questions

  • Q.  Hmm, I noticed vendors outside of Halas selling the Ro’s potion needed for the epic… can I spawn Arantir in other places??

  • A.  Nope.  You can purchase the potion from any of the other vendors (located in Thurgadin or Cabilis) and turn them into Camin to be stripped, but you cannot give the stripped potion back to those vendors to spawn Arantir.  Arantir can ONLY be spawned from Dargon in Halas.

  • Q.  Ok, I’ve done the prelim and am ready to spawn Arantir for the first time.  Problem is, I’m KoS and the Shaman guildmasters will surly attack me.  What do I do?

  • A.  Don’t worry.  It seems that the Shaman guildmasters have an agro range of like 0.  You can stand on top of Arantir and hail him; the guildmasters will no attack – no matter how KoS you are.  The only thing you need to be concerned about is the roaming guards.  For more information on the two roaming guards, see this link:

  • http://graffe.com/quests/fiery_avenger_type/halas.htm. 

  • If you have any Druid friends, call them to your aid.  They can harmony the guards (and the guildmasters if you’re worried J) so that they won’t attack.

  • Q.  Ok, I spawned Arantir, but he won’t respond.  What’s wrong?

  • A.  A few things can be wrong.  First off, make sure you’re not invisible.  With KoS Wizards this is probably the most common problem. J  Secondly, make sure you’re pretty much right on top of him.  I noticed Arantir has a fairly small hail range, if you’re not close to him he will not respond.

  • Q.  Got the ring, now I’m scared… you see, I’m quite KoS to Felwithe.  Will Challice hate me?  What should I do?

  • A.  Don’t worry one bit.  Simply run directly to Challice, invisible, and you’ll be ok.  The room across from her has a Paladin guildmaster in it; if you have killed in Plane of Growth or are an evil race you will be KoS.  Don’t go into that room without invisibility up.  Challice herself is on TrueSpirit faction; you need not worry about her attacking you.

  • Q.  Bleh!  I gave the ring to Challice and gated back to Halas but Arantir had already despawned.  What do I do?

  • A.  They fixed this part of the quest a bit ago thank goodness!  If you have the proper TrueSpirit faction (apprehensive+) you can turn the ring directly into Dargon and she will respawn a new Arantir for you to chat with. 

  • Q.  Ok, well, Dargon is missing!

  • A.  No worries, I had the same thing happen to me.  You see, when Dargon despawns it’s like she was killed.  You’ll need to sit around for a few minutes and wait for her to repop.  This can take anywhere from 10-25 minutes as I’m unsure of her spawn time.  There is also another Dargon in Halas from what I’ve heard, turn nothing into this one… I’ll talk about this soon.

  • Q.  There’s two Dargons in Halas, what’s up?

  • A.  Yah, for some stupid reason Verant placed two Dargons in Halas, only one is the correct one.  The Dargon you want is by the Shaman guild and sells herbs.  Dargon is also a female with no surname – the other Dargon has a surname.

Kandin Firepot Questions

  • Q.  Where’s Kandin?

  • A.  He has a spawn time.  It can be anywhere from 24-48+ hours, just be patient!

  • Q.  I have a Cazic Skin back from when they dropped and finally got a BCS and Gnarled Staff.  Anything I can do with this or is it just a wasted item?

  • A.  No, Verant actually made this work correctly.  When you hand in the Cazic Skin (if you still had it) Kandin will give you the Note to Arantir and the Staff of Gabstick, no powder needed.

  • Q.  We killed Trakanon and Tolapumj dropped some Mistletoe powder.  I thought it was no longer needed for the quest.

  • A.  Nope, it’s not needed.  You can, however, turn it in to receive an extra Staff of Gabstick!

Broken Golem Questions

  • Q.  How tough is this golem?

  • A.  Not too hard, just a ton of HP.  He’s a Warrior-type mob (watch that enraged!) so no spells are cast.  He’s also 100% magic resistant, so don’t expect slows.  Lure of Ice seems to land fairly well, but don’t over nuke – he hits hard.  If I remember correctly, he hits for a max of 400ish but is rather slow on the delay.

  • Q.  What’s up with the broken golem?

  • A.  He has a random spawn time.  When I killed mine it was 72 hours after the last one was killed.  The golem didn’t spawn for a week after I killed mine then he spawned two days in a row!!  It’s strange.

  • Q.  How do I check on the broken golem?

  • A.  I bought a gate potion for my checks.  I went in with a Ranger usually and looked for the golem on tracking.  If you don’t have enough money for a gate potion you can kill the Cazic Cenobite in Cazic Thule.  He drops a Cazic Gate Potion (instant-cast) as his common loot.  If you’re brave enough you can train to the spot where the golem spawns (under the bell tower near zone in) and then gate away.  Periodically do a check for other guilds in Fear as well and see if they can work something out with the broken golem.  Most of the time people are willing to help, unless they need it for themselves.

  • Q.  I’ve gotten myself to the Fear part and am ready to kick some golem butt, but I hear he hits quite hard.  I’m kind of afraid to go hand it in and pull it back, any ideas?

  • A.  While you’re pretty safe to turn it in and run back with a skin up, you have another option.  See, Verant thought about this carefully and did not make the gear no drop.  Simply hand it to a_warrior02 and have him/her pull the mob for you.  It’s a lot easier and safer if you’re not willing to do it yourself.  By the way, I suggest fighting him at the ledge, and make sure you keep him out of trees.

  • Q.  Ok, I got the oil.  What is next?

  • A.  Hand the oil to Kandin and make sure you have two free spaces in your packs.  He will give you a Note to Arantir and a Staff of Gabstick.  If this is your last piece, proceed to Halas!

Final Turn In Questions

  • Q.  At Dargon and have the three staves and note to Arantir, what's next?

  • A.  Give Dargon the note directly and she will turn into Arantir.  Stand close to Arantir no and hail him, he will say some stuff.  Now hand him your three staves (or if you are multi-questing have everyone else turn in their staves then you turn in yours) and he will give you the bag with his staff and your three staves.

  • Q.  Ok, here I go, about to hand in the three sta…

  • A.  WAIT!  STOP!  HALT!  It’s VERY important you have a major inventory slot open when turning in your three staves!  The bag Arantir hands you is classified as a ‘GIANT’ item.  If you have a Tinkerer’s Bag or Skarlon Pack make sure you have an open slot in them.  If you don’t, make sure you have a free MAIN INVENTORY slot.  At this point, GMs are becoming stricter with enforcing the rules and are denying Wizards reimburses for a loss of this kind.  Please, make sure you have a major inventory slot open, it will prevent a lot of frustration.

  • Q.  Ok I got my bag from Arantir with the three staves.  Now I’m going to turn this into the NPC and get my Staff of the Four.  Who do I turn it into?

  • A.  Solomen in the Temple of Solusek Ro.  Solomen and no one else other than Solomen should be seeing that pack!

  • Q.  Do I have to say anything to Solomen before turning in my bag from Arantir?

  • A.  Nope, just give him bag and he will return to you the uber Staff of the Four.

Multi-Questing Questions

  • Q.  Ok, so I have one staff and my friend has two staves.  Can we multi-quest?

  • A.  Yes, you most certainly can!

  • Q.  Ok, so I lied.  I have one staff, my friend has another, and his friends sisters brother-in-law has the third.  Can I tri…

  • A.  Stop right there my friend!  Yes, you can tri-quest.

  • Q.  Awesome!  Now, how do I do it?

  • A.  I knew this was coming *wink* !  Here's how you do it.  Have the person who got the green oil turn the note into Dargon and spawn Arantir.  Boom, there is Arantir.  Now, have the people who are NOT getting the epic turn their staves in, then you turn your staff or staves in and you should receive the bag.  It's very important that the people multi-questing have apprehensive or higher faction.  If they have dubious the staves will be eaten.  If they are dubious have them place the staff into trade with Arantir, then cast invisibility on them.  This will give them indifferent faction.  Once invisible, have them hand in the staff or staves then you hand in yours.  It's that simple!
