Wielders of the Sword History

Chapter I - Long ago

The history of the Wielders of the Sword is ancient and shrouded in mystery. Legend states that
at one time Norrath had a sixth continent known as Noldor. On the continent of Noldor a
group known as the Wielders of the Sword was formed to destroy the wicked and evil that
were attempting to gain a foothold. The group originally formed by a Lord William and Lord
Guderian fought desperately for many generations against the brood of Veeshan until a
stalemate was achieved. In an attempt to break the stalemate and prove his worth to his
mother, a great chrystalline dragon known as Vinyamar made a daring attack upon Noldor.
Although his attack left most of the continent burning and in ruins, Vinyamar was slain by the
guild. The news of Vinyamars death soon reached his mother Veeshan. In a state of blind hate
and rage Veeshan laid to waste the continent of Noldor. The seas rose, mountains rumbled,
and volcanoes poured the fire from their bellies. When all was said and done, the beautiful land
of Noldor was a pile of ash and rubble slowly sinking into the sea.

Chapter II - The Escape

The remaining survivors of the Wielders of the Sword made it to a galleon and fled across the
sea to the northeast. The journey was treacherous but eventually the galleon found its way to a
place known today as Kunark. Being weak from their voyage, the group decided to make a
settlement and attempt to make contact with any peoples that might also be living on Kunark. A
few scouts were sent out to see who else might be living near them, but none ever returned.
Fearing the worst, the guild decided to leave Kunark. A second ship was constructed and the
party split into two groups. One group would head northeast and the other would head
northwest. The day came, good-byes were said, and the ships set sail.

Chapter III - The Present

Many generations have passed since the guild split into groups. One group landed on what now
is known as Faydwer, the other group landed on the continent of Antonica. One night an
apprentice wizard name William awoke from a dead sleep. A feeling of expectation covered
him, and he immediately jumped out of bed and got dressed. In the late hour of the night, the
world was still. Only the clacking noise of a fire beetle in the distance could be heard. William
began to pace, and finally paused at a painting of his ancestor that hung over the mantle of the
fireplace. He had examined that painting countless times before. He had always noticed the
similarities in features between him and the person in the painting. In fact, if the person in the
painting were not completely bald they could have been twins. The painting seemed different
now and something caused himself to take it off the mantle. He examined the large portrait front
and back, not sure why he was doing it nor what he was looking for. Without reason and
without conscious thought he pulled his dagger and cut the backing of the painting. William
reached his hand inside and pulled out a cylinder made of gold. The cylinder had a latch at one
end of it and he quickly opened it up. Inside he found a group of scrolls that were obviously
quite ancient. He carefully unrolled the scrolls and read the incredible tale of the destruction of
Noldor, the flight to Kunark and the breakup of the group. Also included in the scrolls were the
names of the group members that set sail from Kunark so long ago. William knew why he had
been awoken, he knew what must be done. The very next day he began his journey, he would
search out the descendants of the Wielders of the Sword. The guild will again fight for justice
and vanquish the wicked and the evil.
