Fletching Guide
Info submitted by: Guntram and Tarthis

Getting Started

First of all I would recommend the book, or print out the info from a good web site. The Fletching guide has your basic info to get started. And it is some pretty good information. After the book or you have this info, next you need a fletching kit. They vary in price. Once you have the fletching kit you can start to make arrows. I would not start making bow until 20+ in Fletching.

You need four parts for your arrow: the knock, the shaft, the tip and the groove The more expensive the different parts, the better your arrow will be. My experience is that you can combine almost any of them as long as you have one component of each category. Results may vary depening on the components you used.

To give a quik rundown for bowyers:
Basically all you need is a staff and a string. If you are playing on Antonica, Surefall Glade will be your place to go and visit. Almost any staffs and strings can be found here. Put a staff and a string in your fletching kit...that's it. Results will depend on the components you used and if you had any tools available or not (can be also bought in Surefall Glade,but are damned expensive). Even if you use just a Hickory Staff and a Silk String you'll get a Bow almost as good as a LongBow (which would cost you 4pp).

General information:
As with every trade skill in Everquest, special skills will be best trained at special places. For training your fletching skill I really recommend going to Qeynos and visit Nesiff's shop (South Qeynos) and visit Surefall Glade (Qeynos Hills). If you start as a Woodelf you are also fine on Faydwer The good thing about the flecthing skill is that you can make arrows and bows with the same skill and therefore making arrows will also help you to make better bows. Also as with every trade skill it appears that you should have a certain skill rank before you try certain tasks. So for the beginning I would recommend just doing arrows and arrows all over again. You can pratice your skill for 4 copper (each of the four parts you need can be bought for 1 copper). Once you have a fletching skill of 10 or 20 you might want to try arrows with ONE better component (e.g. a medium groove instead of a large one). If you have a fletching skill of 20+ go ahead and try your first bows, but keep in mind that if you fail all your componets - and therefore all your gold, your investment-

So start simple and work your way up just as you would do if you were leveling a character. Start with the ez stuff and work your way up :)

Making Arrows

Ready to begin your career as a fletcher? Ok then here is how you will start. Get a fletching kit. And go buy the cheapest arrow components. When you start out you can get your skill from 0 to 16 on the cheap stuff. Buy the Large groove nock, wooden shaft, field point arrowhead, and round cut fletch. Make a ton of arrows, do this buy putting one of each component in your fletching kit.

Now hit the combine button and see what you get. If all goes well an arrow should be attached to your cursor. If not, try again. After you reach skill level 16 these arrows are trivial to you (even though you will still fail sometimes). So you have to upgrade one of the components, and only one. I went with all the same stuff, but I bought a medium groove nock instead of the large groove nock. This got me up to 36, and then I used a small nock with the same components. Later I had to move on to a parabolic fletch rather then a round cut, etc.

Basically all you have to do is look for the next highest price item. For example at the beginning of fletching everything costs 1 copper. Once you get to the point where making arrows with all the 1 copper stuff is out of your league, you would want to look for something that costs just a little more. Like the medium nock, which is 4 copper, obviously you wouldnt want to buy steel arrow shafts if you know you can barely make cheap arrows, build up your skill and when it becomes trivial for you at a certain stage just look for the next highest priced component.

Making Bows

Bows are easier than arrows to make in my opinion. You use your fletching kit to make a bow. All you need is the bow shaft and some twine or something to use as the string. You will want to start out making hickory and elm bows. To do this buy either a hickory or elm bow shaft and put it in your fletching kit. Then buy some twine (the cheap stuff) and hit combine. If all goes well you have a bow that is better than a store bought one.

There are also tools you can use to help you make better bows. If you look at the stuff the bow vendors sell you will see that they sell whitling knife, standard bow cam, and planing tool. These are all tools to help you create bows. Here is what these extra things do:

  • Standard Bow cam - Used to make a composite bow.
  • Whitiling Knife - Used to make a Medium bow
  • Planing tool - Used to make a smooth bow

When you first make a bow you will probably make a rough bow, if you were making a hickory bow lets say, you would make a "rough hickory recurve bow". With a Whitling knife ( I hope I am spelling that right) it is said that you can make a Medium bow instead of the rough, I have a whitling knife and haven't made a Medium bow yet, but one thing I do know is that you dont put these tools in the fletching kit. Also my skill in fletching is now 105. I spent alot of money on an Oak bow shaft and failed at making it. So I would suggest building skill on arrows after both the hickory and elm bows are to trivial for you. There is a huge price jump between a the two low end bow shafts and the two high end bow shafts. Which means your skill probably has to be REALLY good to make one of those bows.

Advanced Fletching Guide Part - I

Once you have raised your fletching skill rank to about 40 through making arrows, you might want to try making your first Elm Bow. This bow is already better than the Long Bow, which is available at the shops. There are three different kinds you can make depending on the string you use:

Damage 13, Delay 51 Damage 12, Delay 47 Damage 11, Delay 43

The better the string you use, the better the delay rate. While most people would want the Damage 13 Bow, I like the Damage 11 the most. The lower delay rate is pretty smart if you are playing in a team and your character can use his archery skill from behind without the need to go to melee. And the effect is almost as good as if you fought with a normal 1h weapon...depending on the arrows you use, of course.

The Elm Bow should be also a first source for you to earn some money with your trade skill. Since you can customize and the bow is better than a Long Bow, you should be able to find people who will buy from you for 4pp or a bit more.

You can continue to pratice your fletching skill by making those bows or even better by making arrows. You will soon get the message that cheap component arrows get "trivial" for you to make. Just take one part that is a bit more expensive and continue to work on your fletching skill. Soon you will be able to make Class 2 Arrows (adding 2 damage to bow) and after a while you will be able to make Class 3 Arrows (adding 3 Damage to bow). By that time an arrow will already cost you like 7 silver to make, but it's still a cheap pratice compared to other trade skills.

The problem you will be encountering is that only very few people have the money to buy your expensive arrows (which you should sell for at least 1 gold). Also if you raised your skill in fletching to 70 or 80 you might be willing to try and do an Ashwood Bow. But be warned, this is no good pratice anymore. The bow will eat up like 17 or 18pp to make - even if it works the first time. And unfortunately there are again very few people who will be able to buy such an expensive bow from you (which should sell for 30pp....keeping in mind that it is a rare item and you lose lots of plat if you fail).

I recommend producing them only when ordered and you have received some cash in advance.

A word on tools

There are many people out ther, who think that a "Rough Elm Bow" isn't any good. This is wrong! To get a better bow you need a tool, like a Whitling Knife. The effect of using such a tool (has to be in kit!!) while making an Elm Bow is only little. Actually, the delay rate and the damage will be lowered - just if you used a better string. But while a better string is a couple silver, a tool is at least a couple plat. Therefore I can't recommend the "Curved Elm Bows". They will be sold for like 7pp and are really not worth the price difference to a 4pp "Rough Elm Bow".

Happy hunting,

Guntram - Xegony

It works similarly to arrows; add up the values to get the final statistics for a bow using those components.

Now, one last thing a lot of people don't realize. The overall statistics for a given bow/arrow combination (you have to have a bow and an arrow to shoot an arrow at anything) is determined by ADDING together the stats for the bow and the arrow used. So, using the cheapest arrangement, class 1 wooden point arrows and a rough hickory recurve bow, you end up with 11 damage, 100 range, and 50 delay. Using an ashwood bow and parabolic cut fletch/small nock arrows, you can get a range of 250! You may be wondering how I know that you add the bow and arrow stats together. Well, making an arrow with a large nock and a shield cut fletch results in an arrow with zero range, however you can still shoot it at monsters, so it's the only explanation that works.
