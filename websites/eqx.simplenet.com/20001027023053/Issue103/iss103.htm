Pinter's Legacy

Ivar picked up the small, retractable telescope. It quivered in his hand slightly as he held it to his eye. "This is almost as ingenious as your underwater breathing apparatus, Pinter."

"Aye the greatest friend of a hermit is his mind." Pinter shifted his feet back and forth methodically.

    Tale by Sfinks


The Day of Testing

Silak drew his cowl up above his face in an attempt to keep out the chill that was seeping into his bones. He looked to the moon in the sky to gauge the time. The time was growing nearer. Silak yawned, the chill making him sluggish. In doing so he revealed row after row of sharp pointed teeth made for tearing flesh to shreds. A sickly pink tongue rolled out of his mouth and down his chin. The moonlight shimmered off the green scales covering his face.

    Tale by Tauithar Tausiirath


Hysslak

"Arise Hysslak, we have decided."

The leaden tones of the Head Judicator resounded strangely throughout the field of bone, amplified to an otherworldly level by the hollow bones of the first children of Veeshan, their spirits passed from the world ages ago. A faint glimmer of hope flashed across the warrior's features, only to be extinguished by the somber faces of the High Council members.

    Tale by Felandriel


Encounter in Warsilk

I will not ever forget my very first visit to Kunark. During the winter, on a rainy night on Freeport, my good friend Garadeb, whom I had presumed dead, returned to Antonica and wanted me to come with him on a trip to Kunark. At the time, I did not believe him, because Kunark was a legend among the people of Norrath for seemingly hundreds of years, and the thought of a voyage to a place only heard about from childhood bedtime stories was just too much to comprehend. But I could not give up the chance of a lifetime.

    Tale by Aedar
