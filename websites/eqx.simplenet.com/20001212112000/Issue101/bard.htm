ISSUE # 101

November 20th - November 26th


The Patchwork Bard


My life is a patchwork of things I have seen,
A myriad visions of places I've been.
I've traveled through Freeport,
And then to the 'Vale,
Now on I shall wander through storm and through gale.

A patchwork collection of items I hold,
From new-crafted armour to weapons of old.
My gloves were once Waji's,
My lute came from Dain,
My cape and my axe were the Pencerdd's domain.

Amrothe the master exerted his art,
And smithied the shirt that now covers my heart.
My boots from Sephira,
My drum from a store,
My belt from a goblin who needs it no more.

One day in the commons I heard a sweet sound,
And outside the tollbooth Isara I found.
On hearing her music,
I started to sing,
Isara then gave me her magical ring.

A patchwork collection of chattels am I,
A many-hued traveler 'neath Norrath's sky.
Belongings around me,
My mind full of song,
Exploring forever the place I belong.


    Tale by Elegor Mandreskal of the Soerbaird
