ISSUE # 128

 June 10th - June 16th, 2000

The Road to Kunark
Chapter One: Starting Out.

This is the story of an unexpected journey which I participated in. Everything is as factual as my memory makes it.

It all began one day when I woke up near the woods outside Kelethin. I was very excited because I knew that today my guild, The Amazons of Vallon Zek, were holding a race and bazaar.

Looking to the sky I noticed the lateness of the day - oh my! I'd overslept. Quickly gathering my gear I began the long run to the Butcherblock Docks. When I reached my destination my ears were assailed by a loud din. As I stretched and looked around I noticed a few of my Amazon sisters nearby. Ranini and Finnie seemed to be selling raffle tickets to a lottery being held. I decided to join them.

"Greetings Sisters," I formally bow and salute each.

They answer me back, "Greetings Katania."

"How have you been?" Ranini asks concernedly.

"I am doing well, hunting is plentiful this year," I reply.

"Kat, I have some armor for you if you need it." This kind offer comes from Finnie who is also a Half Elf Ranger and understands the plight of a poor 10th circle sister.

"Many thanks Finnie! Yes - I would really appreciate it," I reply gratefully.

"Fine, we will go to the bank after Ranini is done," She replies.

As we chat Ranini finally finishes selling tickets.

"Katania, roll the die!" I gratefully comply, producing a magic die and roll it.

"The number is 105!" I shout so all can hear me.

There are more than a few groans and then a short but violent fight breaks out between the winner and a sore looser. Ranini quickly quashes the fight and we set out for Kaladim, the home to the dwarves. If you have never been to Kaladim, you should go! It is all underground and can sometime get confusing but the masonry work is beyond compare. After wandering around for a bit, we three sisters finally find the Bank.

Ranini decides to take a nap. She is very tired after all the excitement of the day.

In the bank Finnie gives me some wonderful chain mail gloves and a very nice pair of leggings. She had earned some fine new armor so was kindly giving her old set to me. I was carrying almost everything I owned on my body or on my back.

While Ranini sleeps, Finnie and I break our fast and talk about the new continent which was found. I had never visited the outpost there called Firiona Vie (after our brave Elven Princess). Finnie confided that she has traveled there many a time and was heading back. She asks if I wish to join her, and being young and stupid - I gladly agreed to go.

Hindsight is a horrible thing! Sitting here now, grateful to be alive - I realize that at this point is where I make my first mistake.

Feeling confident that my older Amazon Sister Finnie can tackle any situation, I blithely get my gear and follow her out of Kaladim.

THESE WERE MISTAKES NUMBER ONE AND TWO!

#1: As you will discover with further reading - having a high circle guild member traveling with you does not guarantee that you will meet with success or that they can save you in all situations.

#2: NEVER travel for any distance, long or short, with all your gear on you! I knew this rule but for this instance chose to ignore it because of my false sense of security. As you will see, that was not the smartest of choices.


Chapter Two: All Aboard

At the Docks we chat and wait on one of the longboats to be ferried to the Maiden Voyage - the huge ship which trades with the outpost and it's inhabitants. While waiting, Finnie and I notice that we were becoming tired and drowsy. It was very difficult to keep our eyes open. Finally the ship is sighted and the longboats head out. When the longboats get to the ship I can hardly drag myself up the plank.

Only Innoruuk (or more likely, one of his followers) knows why this terrible thing happened to me. One moment I am walking up the narrow, steep, slippery slope of the gangplank and the next I feel a hard tap and am falling through the air.

My screaming is cut short by a mouthful of water as I struggle to get back to the surface. I hear my sister shout my name as the ship pulls away from me, taking with it all chances of returning to shore. Her faint words cannot carry through the buzzing in my ears as I struggle to stay afloat.

You must remember, dear reader, I am a half elf ranger - uncomfortable out of the forest. I follow the teaching of our forest mother Tunare who I called out to and prayed to for help.

I did notice as I swam that I got better and better at staying afloat.

Once and awhile brother wind brought to me voices. I heard Finnie struggling to find out a location of an island I could swim to. I heard Ranini getting in touch with a Troll she once helped out who was familiar with Orc Island - the closest island to me.

As day turned into night I despaired of ever finding any shore. I don't know that I slept in the nightmare that had become my life. But the voices stayed with me on through the night.

The next day dawned brighter and I prayed to Tunare, thanking her for her assistance. I swam and swam and finally found the ship again! I yelled and tried to climb up onto one of the longboats but my travails were in vain. The ship left again without me and I could not board the long boat.

I knew that I had come to the end of my life. I had no hope and decided to let myself sink under the deep blue sea, loosing all I had gained in my short life span - when I heard a crude voice.

It was an Ogre! Yes, an Ogre on a small skiff! I shouted until I was hoarse but the currents were pulling the skiff quickly away. The ogre tried to help me, even gave me the star location of Ogre Island, the weigh point for the Dark Races on the trip to the lost continent of Kunark. But the Ogre (of course) could not save me. From what I gathered, the Ogre felt sorry for me and would rather eat me than see me drown (felt it was a waste of a tasty elfie).

With the star location I had hope and set out that night to swim to Ogre Island. The swim was long and strenuous. It was hard to keep going, but on the wind I heard the urging of my sisters not to give up, not to surrender. My Amazon pride filled me as I swam stroke by painful stroke.


Chapter Three: Lost in the Desert

Finally, I stumbled onto land and immediately fell into a deep, deep slumber.

Daylight woke me. I cautiously began moving around, broke my fast and began to explore the island I found myself upon.

The island was large and quite barren. Only a few ruins and some tattered, dusty tents remained. I could still hear distant voices! Imagine my dismay when I realize that I have reached the wrong island. I couldn't help but cry in rage and frustration. When my tears were used up I gritted my teeth, grabbed my pack and waded out into the sea.

This act was the bravest act I've ever committed. I have ventured into Crushbone alone to help a friend and I have saved many a life from flickering out by binding wounds and protecting the weak. I have been the bane of the Crushbone Orcs and I have faced monsters and evils too terrible to name. I have bravely stood for Justice and Honor against hordes of crazed gnome killers and countless undead monsters. But this one act almost broke me.

Again I was immersed but I continued swimming until I saw land. When I beached I was exhausted but still alive, and I could hear voices carried by the wind to me.

I found the Orc encampment and also the dark docks. I watched Trolls and Ogres unload some supplies and watched for the raft which would hopefully take me to the Oasis of Mar.

All I had to do is wait for darkness.

Darkness crept in quicker than I had expected. With my superior night vision I watched the barely pieced together raft float toward shore. I waited until the last minute before running into the surf and jumping on it. Freeport bound at last I carefully set down on the weather beaten boards to wait as magical forces draw the ragged raft and it's lone inhabitant toward a familiar but distant shore.

The raft sailed through the night - through silence and squall until at last I could see the barren stretch of coast known to all as The Oasis of Mar. I quickly stood and braced myself - for I could see figures on the shore with my sharp Elven eyes and they did not look very welcoming.

As the raft got closer to land I took two slow and deep breaths and dove off, swimming with all my might toward the shore away from the dark encampment.

I was almost paralyzed, my joy was so great at finding land. I dragged myself off the beach and hid behind a pillar of stone trying vainly to regain my strength and decide which way to head.

I truly believe that Innoruuk was following my journey that day. For with no warning I was struck from behind. I was weak and bleeding and barely caught a glimpse of my attacker before I fell into darkness. Then the desert madman raised his fist for another blow and struck me dead.


Chapter Four: A Waking Dream

All I saw was white. I felt as if two suns were glowing in my head. Bright, bright white. In the white I imagined I saw a figure - I drifted forward to see who it was, and was shocked to see my mother. Confused I looked for my father and suddenly he is there to. I try to reach them, I run and run but it feels like I am running in water. Suddenly they stand before me. They look young and happy and I want to hold them so bad - but cannot seem to reach them.

"No Daughter, that is not the way it is to be," my mother says sadly as I reach out to hug her.

"Oh Mother - Oh Father - I have missed you so! I am so glad to see you. I never want to leave you again." With tears in my eyes I try to move but I am frozen in place.

"Daughter, heed your mother for she loves you well - as do I. We have been sent to tell you that it is not your time and you should fear not death, for you are well loved by Tunare and have a long and important life ahead."

"But Father, I do not understand," I say in amazement.

"Change is coming to these lands and with change comes strife. You are needed down below to help others and continue your path. The fates have decreed that you live long and continue the path that you have chosen."

"I do not wish to go back, I want to stay here with you and mother," I plead but to no avail.

"Hold yer Weesht," my Father thunders angrily at me. Immediately I snap my mouth shut and bow my head in respect for I know I have gotten to the end of my father’s patience.

His tone softening my father continues.

"It is not for you or I to choose - your destiny was woven the moment of your birth and you must fulfill your destiny, whatever it may be."

"We love you very much daughter," my mother says with a misty smile, "but you have many tasks to complete and long journeys ahead."

Father holds mother’s hand as he finishes, "We are very proud of you and will watch over you as much as we can. Do not feel sad daughter, we shall await you here and will always travel with you."

"How will I find you?" I cry in despair as they begin to slowly fade away.

I hear my father's raspy chuckle, "Do not worry child, it is for us to find you - and the view from up here is pretty good."


Chapter Five: Freeport Bound

As my father’s last sentence fades in my mind I jerk awake to find myself back at Orc Lift.

Rubbing my eyes in amazement I stand up and look around. I am standing next the guard at the bottom of the Orc Lift. I am wearing clothes but all my gear is gone. I think, where can it be?

"What day is it?" I ask the startled guard. He scratches his head and looks around.

"Where did you come from? You weren't there a second ago." He sounds terribly suspicious.

"Never mind that good sir! What day is it?" I demand. Giving me a confused look he says,

"The day of our Lady, 167 c.e."

"Thank you kind sir." I say respectfully as I walk toward the next lift quickly calculating.

It is then I realize that I have only wasted a day! But where is my gear? I have no food, no water and no armor. It has to be somewhere.

As I reach the Lift to the Ruins I make a quick decision - I will consult the oracle in the outcast camp. It is a quick run to the outcasts encampment where I am well known.

I had traveled within the brightly painted wagons of the outcast caravan and lived with them in their sheer, brightly colored tents for most of my young adulthood. Once there I search the flamboyantly garbed travelers for my dear friend and travel mother Serean O'deli.

Serena O'deli is the leader of the outcasts. After a loving hug she quickly gave me food and water and after hearing my tale, called upon the forces of nature to help me.

"Your body is in the desert, in the place of the Mar," she intones deeply - gazing into her energy orb.

"Why yes, that is where I was last at!" I say in amazement.

"Well then," she says briskly - dispelling the orb with a graceful wave, "that is where your old body and gear must be."

I felt really dumb at this point so I thanked her quickly and went on my way.

The run through Greater Faydark and Butcherblock is very familiar to me. This is a trade route I use often to deliver mail for the Bard's guild (they need all the help they can get) and for rare items only found in Freeport - the main seaport to Antonica, the continent I needed to be on.

The ship was just setting sail as I ran to the end of the dock and hopped quickly aboard. The journey across the Ocean of Tears was quick and thankfully painless. But I still felt very thankful to be alive.

MISTAKE #3: Towns are NOT SAFE! Guards at most towns are lazy, and will not protect you unless you are on top of them.

I was walking under an arch headed toward the west gate of Freeport. Suddenly, I was hit from behind with a ball of energy. I was stunned and tried to head toward a shiftless Freeport guard. I couldn't move. I was rooted to the ground.

I heard a raspy chuckle as in front of me appeared a dark elf. With a wave of his arms a ball of flame burst on me. As I felt myself bleeding to death I managed to say,

"Please do not kill me. I have nothing."

Amazingly the dark elf immediately stopped his spells and came closer to inspect me.

"Ahh," he said in a husky voice, "you have returned from the dead, no?"

"Yes," I managed to reply.

Before I knew it, he was helping me up off the ground and dusting me off. "Poor little one, you need to have your feet bound to this place - do you not?" he said sympathetically.

I was stunned so I replied,

"Yes I do. I am trying to find my body."

He seemed to understand. He steered me toward the gate and offered, "Let me bind you to this place little one, so you will return here if you perish."

Blame the lingering effects of his spells, or his honey smooth voice - but I accepted this strange Dark Elf’s offer and allowed him to bless my feet to the ground outside the Freeport gates.

With a cheery wave and these parting words he took his leave, "Take care little one and beware, these lands are not safe for one such as you!"

And then he was gone.


Chapter 6: New Beginnings

It took until evening to find someone in Freeport who could give me directions to the Oasis of Mar. Since I was hungry and thirsty anyway, I relaxed near the cool grounds of the Cleric guild until nightfall.

At nightfall I headed across East Freeport onto the blistering sands of the North Desert of Ro.

Having been warned what dangers to expect, I stayed well away from any moving thing. It was a long run, but at least I didn't have to deal with the blistering heat of daytime. I crossed dunes and valleys. I ran around snakes and giant scarabs. I avoided Sand Giants and Dune Madmen until finally I crossed into the Oasis of Mar.

Here I had to follow the stars until I saw the pillar of stone jutting out of the dry land, where my corpse should be. And then I noticed that Desert Madman. The sun was just rising and he was sitting with his back to my corpse, gnashing his teeth and foaming at the mouth as he talked to his shadow in disjointed phrases.

There was only one thing to do.

Taking a hand full of stones found at the base of the pillar, I crept around the pillar and threw a couple up the dune. Hearing the noise, the Madman spun toward the sound and on all fours went to investigate. I snuck back to my body, and grabbing it under the armpits - drug it toward the pillar.

I had to repeat this maneuver server times until I was well out of range and safely concealed behind the pillar of stone.

Picking my gear off my dead body was easy. I quickly donned my armor and broke into my pack to quench my thirst and kill the hunger gnawing at my stomach. Then the most amazing thing happened. I glanced toward my body to make sure I missed nothing and it was gone. It had completely faded away.

After breaking my fast, I began the long run back to Freeport.

I had some close calls. But I didn't die again. All in all, I believe that my journey back was blessed. Once inside the gates of the city, I decided to camp near a wall and scribe my adventure down for future Amazons to read and learn from.

I can't say what the Fates have in store for me, so I shall take the lessons I learned from my travels and apply them. I am not happy that Innoruuk loves me well. And I never want to have such a run of bad luck again. But I wanted to share my new found wisdom with my sisters - for I love them and feel a strong kinship with them. It is a wonderful thing to find that even lost in the Timorous Deep - I was not alone. My Amazonian Sisters were there whispering to me on the wind every stroke of the way.

    Tale by Katania "OrcBane" EmeraldMaiden
