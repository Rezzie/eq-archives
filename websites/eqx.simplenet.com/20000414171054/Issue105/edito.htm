ISSUE # 105

December 18th - December 25th


Editorial - Oghma

Some of you may remember the character Posy I wrote about around February which wound up as the lead story in the EverQuest Strategy Guide. I created that character on Karana and played her the very first week of final to level 4, then shelved her for other (many other, my god!) characters. Recently I picked her up again, and have been having a great time blowing things up in North Ro. Something about a cute li'l gnome wizchick tossing around mana like grenades is really appealing to me, and I have a lot of momentum with her now and I'll ride it out until some other project hits me. Playing as a female and as a wizard brings to mind some things I wanted to share with you.

Wizards are a selfish class, and it's hard to run them as a "helpful" character. They have no buffs for others except a 2-point damage shield, their spells are really geared towards kill-stealing, so there isn't much you can do when a character runs by yelling help except steal their kill. I think that otherwise, it's a great class, and I do what little I can to help others by rooting or dumping a little fire on their foes heads without hitting them too hard. They have no heals, no pets, no armor - and I love it.

I'm 15th level now and using a cracked staff and still wearing a guild tunic. I have been given a minor gift of two +3 int earrings, but all in all this character has very little "stuff". It makes me wonder really how deathly important it is to chase down that extra item which will give you that much of an "edge". Now before you get all snippy, there are some things which are very important to other classes, like armor and a magic weapon for warriors - but it's really not worth stomping on other people at camps and stealing kills.

Otherwise, it's a very fun class, and ranks right up there with bards and clerics and druids and enchanters and magicians and … well, you see that's the problem. I like all the classes and their balance. I have played most classes to about 15, some higher in beta, and though I've barely scratched the surface of class that begin to excel in their 30's, I think that they are really amazingly balanced. This was a huge issue in beta and frankly, it's not the problem that we thought it would be. Verant continues to make tweaks and changes to the classes, and this is a Good Thing. I wouldn't want any class to be the class that someone picks because they want to get to level 50 fastest, guaranteed.
