It's been a while since I read EQX, and I just caught your letters section in the last issue and your editorial in the current. I feel they're connected somehow, so please bear with me here...

Everquest is a video game. It is not a game in which a GM will tailor the world to you and make sure everyone has fun. In short, it is not a role-playing game. It was tagged with the title of "RPG" along with several hundred other games released in 99.

I find something very odd with the complaints in both EQ and UO about the lack of role-playing. I've played Baldur's Gate, the entire FF series, and countless other games marked RPG on the box. Never once have I seen a post where someone was complaining about the lack of personalized character-based plot. Why should this game be any different? RPG is a gaming industry term for anything involving levels, fantasy, or a plot with decent depth. That's all it ever will be.

You hit the whole point of the EQ experience in your editorial. It's about having fun with others. Many, many players see it's nothing more than an IRC channel with a permanent topic and activity. Personally I was always very polite and friendly while playing, but some others were not. This is what you expect in any multiplayer game. Whether it's the spamming jerks in Quake, the newbie pks in Diablo and UO, or the consistent morons in EQ. They exist in every multiplayer game.

This all happens because it is a video game and not some sort of RPG. Anyone with $30 and a computer can join. Much like the campers in 3d shooters, they have just as much right to be there as you do. In the end, it all comes down to the people in a 3d IRC channel. Perhaps when the emulated servers start popping up, we'll all be much happier playing and less irritated by the more disruptive players.

Tolil

Yes, the RPG label is a misleading one, because paper and pen roleplayers have a much different experience in "Role-playing Games" than computer gamers. Paper and pen always gives a much better level of interaction than any computer game can offer - it's not the game designers fault, the medium is still limited in that sense, but what they offer now is not a bad compromise, to me.

More and more i think it's the community which makes or breaks the game. If a new player comes in and gets treated well, then they will enjoy the EQ experience. If they meet with harrassing Kill-stealers, well, their experience of multiplayer "RPGS" will be a negative one.

Thank you for your letter!

Oghma
