I have read Tetsujin's comments on AC and comparing it with EQ. I'm also part of the beta test, and here are a couple of observations I have made:

1. The character customization

Yes, you can customize your character, but it's quite as great as it sounds. The skins are a bit basic in AC, and the characters have a slightly cartoony appearance.

2. Creature placement

Yup, creatures are more spaced out, but to tell you the truth, I feel this is a Bad Thing(tm). In EQ, you start in a city zone, and (with the exception to Kelethin I think) you zone once, and you have an area filled with monsters. Very nice, because it doesn't mean you spend the next 10 minutes for some more decent prey like in AC (Note: I haven't been to many of the dungeons in AC, so it may be different there...).

3. The question of grouping

I have to agree on this point, solo'ing in AC is easier, but I have also just started to do some serious grouping in EQ, and it is ALOT of fun, and I originally thought this was one of the main detracting aspects of EQ...

4. Graphics

With all respect, the graphics in AC are, erm, a little on the bland side. Tree's in EQ look very nice, vary in size, and appearance. In AC, the big groups of trees are more akin to orchards.

As I said earlier, the skins are a bit basic, and the monsters themselves are very low poly meshes, making them look a little silly sometimes. No zoning is cool, but the horizen graphics arn't as good as EQ's, and EQ beats AC's weather effects hands down. Or at least I think so.

5. RPG factor

Can't really comment much on this, as I tend to avoid role playing. When in a party, talking about experiences in real life, while waiting for the HP and mana to go back up appears to more fun, and appeals to more people...

So in closing, is AC better than EQ in my opinion? No, not yet. Note the not yet. It has the potential to fix alot of problems that EQ suffers from, and bring in some new unique skills, ie. You don't get spells perse, but mix your own spells using various ingredients. This is a very fun thing to do...kinda like research in EQ, except not as expensive...:).

Still an EQ player,

Matt.
