Q:  Hail, and well met. I am a Dark Elf Shadow Knight and hated by most every creature on the face of Norrath. I find myself in a small dilemma. Soon I must make my way to the isle of Faydwer to slay Faeries for their dust, and in my humble opinion and reasoning I feel there may be an easier way to acquire this powder than taking a long boat trip and slaying winged little people. Now don't get me wrong, killing them is not much of a problem because my heart feels no pity towards those who will attack me without asking questions or detest me because of my nature. But I am curious if there may be an easier way to obtain the Faerie Dust I require other than making a Faerie village into a place bereft of life and happiness? Even I appreciate the good things in life and something about these little people brings a glimmer of warmth to my dark heart. Thank you for your time to listen to my plight.

Arack Lifebane, Shadow Knight of the 32nd circle on Prexus

A:  While, of course, such deep qualms about wreaking havoc on the delightful vale of the Faeries, warms my heart immensely, I am completely puzzled by a Dark Elf who even has the words happiness and appreciate in his vocabulary. Obviously you are one who has traveled and experienced the realms in such a way that the dark doors of hate have opened onto a more genteel perspective, for which I congratulate you. Thus I think you will be glad of my answer to your question. While it is true that faerie dust can only be obtained originally through the demise of one of the denizens of the village, the dust itself is often bought and sold in various markets. I think with some discreet inquiry and the use of the go-between if necessary, you might actually be able to purchase your dust and have no sad faerie deaths on your awakening conscience. For fear of engaging Innoruuk's interest in this discussion, I will end now and simply wish you good journeys and many thanks for not disturbing the charm of the faerie village.

Q:  Hi there :) I am a High Elf Magician and have heard all sorts of stories about how much experience a pet will take, ranging from "as a party member of equal level" to "none if it does less than 50 damage". It's all very confusing and everyone I speak to says that the other information is rumor and hearsay. Could you please clear this up? thank you very much for your time. Good Journey to you.

Jenarii

A:   I went to one of the most necroish of the necros that I know, Darnalak, and asked about this. Darnalak states that this issue is continually under debate but that in his experience, a pet will take minimal experience when the player is soloing, and none at all when the player with the pet is grouped. The wizard on a mission, Adelrune, also agreed. I, too, have heard tons of stories of pets "stealing" experience and tales of bugs whereby the pet would take experience under a given set of circumstances, i.e., the bard had her left eye closed in a wink at the cleric who was twibbling just as the npc died whereupon the pet grabbed every last point of experience. Hmmm, I think for now, pending any sort of more scientific disclosure, I'm going to go with Darnalak's and Adel's views and hope that it helps you with this.

Q:  I have a few question about jewelry making at what lvl in jewelry making can I make things that add str wis sta and all those other stats. Why do merchants cheat you when you go to them? I mean I sell them something then they charge five times that amount when selling. P.S as to that question about Asheron's Call, I don't think they have a bard class and I know the only pc race is human. Thank you.

Teare, Level 12 Paladin

A:  Rosethorn, a master jeweler and Pillar of Wisdom combined into one obliging soul, was willing to take a few minutes to go over the basic of jewelry making with me. (Would the tailors and dressmakers in Norrath be so willing to help a poor bard get a dress!) The first thing you need to be aware of is that if you are not an enchanter, you need to make friends with one right away as you will need enchanted metals for your creations and only enchanters can prepare them for you. Rosethorn said that you need to have about a level 36 in jewelry making to make the first items with added stats such as strength. There is a basic ring you can make then that will add plus 2 strength to its wearer. Jewelry making is not for the faint of heart or for the poor. It is an expensive craft to learn indeed. You find yourself able to save time and money however by asking advice from master jewelers and learning from their mistakes or by spending sometime among the tomes of wisdom found at www.eqportal.com/jewelry/ or some of the other sites out there. As for the merchants, well -- I heard that they have a rivalry each year to show who has made the best profit margin and thus buy the wares of craftsman, like the jewelers, for as little as possible in order to win the contest I believe that while selling beginner's jewelry to merchants will not add to your bankbook, you will have an opportunity in the future to at least eke out a meager profit from some of the magical jewelry you create. Q:  I just want to respond to the riddle... "Angry and hungry are two words in the English language that end in gry. There are three words in the English language. What is the third word?" The answer, in all reality, is language. Actually this is a quest in the game Planescape: Torment. The actual riddle is "Angry and Hungry are two of three words that end in g-r-y in the human tongue. What is the last word?" The answer is tongue which is the last word of the sentence. This should clear up the problem because the last answer that was given in issue 116 did not make any sense.

It's nice to be back this week after a very pleasant EQ Express vacation. I did receive a peculiar piece of mail, nicely copied onto parchment but blush a bit at the somewhat inaccurate supposition of fame contained in it. The note read, "I am an innocent l'il bard girl in Qeynos and I want to be as famous in the world as you. How do I do it?" It wasn't signed, so although I prefer not to toot my own horn unless singing for an audience, I find the only way I can answer it is here in my column. My answer is this: Dear musical child, should you seek fame, do so because your heart has a deep music and understanding to share with the world, not because you seek to emulate an old bard with many journeys and many strange experiences already past. Whether in innocence or in experience, keep your ears open and your fingers ready for your task. Stay honest as a bard can be (after all prevarication can be a lifesaver at times) and always let the appreciative member of the audience buy you a drink. If you follow all these instructions, your fame and probably your fortune will soon eclipse mine and I will be the first to cheer you on!

Hope to hear from all of you soon.
Nep
