The Voyage

Lying upon the half rotten mattress, Caleb was certain of only one thing and that was that he was dying. Throughout his life he thought that when the time came, he would fear it, fight it off, or find some way to deny that it was taking place. His life had never been one of certainty or stability… what sailors ever was? From the spires of Erudin to the piers of Freeport, there was not a stretch of ocean or isolated islet in which a man or woman could not find themselves a grave.

    Tale by Canticle


Of Reality, and Factuality
Theories and Origins

Once in a dream where I dreamt I was dreaming about dreaming a dream, it occurred to me that perhaps this dream was reality, and that reality was this dream. I looked to my left, which was actually my true right, and seen a small glowing figure, enormous and foreboding, wrapped in a blanket of darkness. He told me to strive for the worst, to know myself a fool, and in so thereby gain the confining walls of liberation. When I questioned him further, I found that his nimble and orderly mind was as clumsy and chaotic as my own, and it was only a matter of time before he slowly sped away into the distance, faster than the eye can catch, slower than the mind can see.

    Tale by Bouldershoulder
