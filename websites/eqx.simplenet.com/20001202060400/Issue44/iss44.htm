The Other

This was the last place he wanted to be, on the last day he could possibly match it with. He could not be late. On pain of death, he could not be late. Rain fell about him and on him as Rath hurried through the dark forest. All the past two days he had been moving through this cursed wood of gnarled trees with the smell of death and decay hammering his senses. It would never end, he told himself repeatedly, though he continued to place one weary, heavily sodden boot in front of the other. He looked up, trying to see any sweet sign of the golden orb. None was visible; the sky was thick with iron-black clouds and streaking lightning.

    Tale by Zaerin

Round Robin - Part 5

Something was wrong.

Call it a feeling, or a hunch, or whatever you may, but something felt out of place. Beldro could feel it in the core of his stomach. This had been too easy. The whole setup was wrong. As days went, he was not having a good one. First captured by these peasants, and then subjected to an assault from a small number of Dark Elves, lead by Priestess of Innoruk. Still, reviewing what went wrong was the best way to figure out how to do right, regardless of what Zass said about the past.
