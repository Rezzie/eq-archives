Editorials

Let me get this out of the way first. Some of you may have read my editorial in issue 41. Well, the basic idea was that I once complained that the folks that play mages aren't too bright, and tend to blast the party with their magic too often. Well, I guess I ain't to bright either. My brand new character, a brand spanking new campaign, my very first spell... Whack. Got two enemies, myself, and the big fighter type. Fire hurts. I knocked my own character to the floor, as close to dead as you can get. The fighter was hurt, but not too badly. And I got the enemies fairly well. But I still did exactly what I spend so much time complaining about. Maybe just playing a magic user causes brain damage. But I don't want to talk about that anymore.

    Editorial by Absor


Letters to the Editor

To Absor and the crew,

I believe the most excited I got about a game, previous to my awaiting the birth of Norrath, was when I first got the boxed set to 'Forgotten Realms' (TSR). I would read and reread the many character histories and be transported to somewhere far away. I would dream of becoming part (figuratively speaking) of this other world where there were heroes that you would love to follow, villians you would loathe, yet understand, fantastic adventures and treasures waiting to be won. I would take my friends with me on these adventures and try to share the wonder I felt for this new world.

    Letters to the Editor
