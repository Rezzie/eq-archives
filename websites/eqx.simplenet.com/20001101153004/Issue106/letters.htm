Dear EQX (and more specifically, Absor),

Your analogy of the situation of item removal in EverQuest to traditional paper-and-pencil roleplaying is both refreshing to see and troubling to think about. Here's why:

It's refreshing to see because it often seems that few people venturing in the online world know what the roleplaying dynamic of P&P RPing is. The cameraderie formed in a P&P group, the sense of adventure and excitement about exploring the world...there are far too many people running around their respective "Matrix" (whether that be UO, EQ, AC, or what have you) treating it like a single-player game.

On that note, that's why it's troubling. EQ is not a P&P game, nor will it ever be, for one simple reason: instead of one GM (gamemaster) to about 4-6 people, you have one GM for thousands of people. Even though only one member of your anecdotal party will get the Sliced Bread sword, your GM will probably compensate the others in different ways later on. Your ranger may be accepted into the elite forester's school, or the other warrior may find a diamond-studded shield, etc. In EQ no such sense of fairness exists because there are too few empowered people to make it exist. GMs in EQ must still pay for their accounts; they are not given free time online for their service. EQ doesn't seem interested in lessening the ratio of GM:player, either, so that there are more GM-run quests and whatnot to give everyone a shot at "the wealth."

The P&P dream is a good one, but it's one that EQ will never come close to approximating.

Calthaer

This is an addendum to my previous e-mail. (Which the editor didn't include here, this one was long enough... :P - Absor.)

First, I'd like to apologise for venting my spleen to you. I love the time and effort you spend every week on EverQuest Express. You should not have to listen to me going on about how bad some days can get on EverQuest.

I've been thinking about the situation that I am encountering. Initially I decided that the best thing to do was simply to quit - to get out before this sort of thing becomes too frustrating.

However, quitting represents a fair amount of invested time and effort that will be lost. Also I might be considered cowardly for turning tail and seeking some other past-time. After all, one should only give up on a lost cause.

I don't know what I should do. The guides that I spoke with could do nothing. I do know that as an enchanter, I have ways of really annoying the hell out of selfish players. Unfortunately, this would be considered player misconduct.

What should I do? I've always tried to play a peaceful and considerate character. I've always yielded spawn sites to lower level characters and offered to help them whenever possible. My main goal whenever I'm in a group, is to make sure everyone survives and has fun.

Recently, as I leave the newbie levels, I find a change of attitude. The attitude to power-levelling conflicts with what I try to do. It promotes hoarding of camps and bitter name-calling.

How frustrating is it? Your troll friend goes linkdead when passing under the aviak guard tower. While you are trying to persuade the two mid-twentieth level characters to leave, your troll friend comes back and the aviak guard respawns on top of him. The aviak guard attacks the troll and you help him kill it. It nearly kills the two of us while the high level players stand and watch. You are then both accused of kill- stealing and the 'good' characters now refuse to negotiate saying that the troll stole the kill.

I suppose that in the face of change, one must adapt or be destroyed. As a dark elf, I can change to deal with this sort of conflict. In a sense, my poor Ularyn will become the evil elf that everyone has labelled her.

I've decided not to quit. Getting angry serves no purpose. Getting even is my motive now.

Thank you for listening to my rant. Please, keep this between you and me.

Paul Wong,
