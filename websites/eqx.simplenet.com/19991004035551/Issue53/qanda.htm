ISSUE # 53Send Your QuestionDecember 19th - December 25th

Q & A

Q: When will phase 4 begin?
A: As much as I would like the ability to look into Brad McQuaid's head, we have no information on future dates of features or phases.

Q: When a bard plays a song, will we be able to hear the music?
A: In this phase of beta, all the sound effects and music have not yet been implemented. As of this time, you do not hear any music when a bard is singing, but you do see a text message when the song begins, when the effect triggers, and when the song ends. You also see the magical special effects, as if a normal spell is being cast.

Q: What are the penalties for death?
A: This is being changed throughout the beta process, but the bottom line is you lose a percentage of your experience points. This can cause you to lose a level if you have recently achieved that level. You also have to trek back to where you died and attempt to recover your corpse.

Q: What is the lag like in EverQuest with a 28.8 modem?
A: The EverQuest Express staff uses many different connection speeds, from 28.8 to ISDN. No one has reported any consistent trouble with lag. You may load the next zone a few seconds slower with 28.8, but it is not a gameplay altering problem. I am very impressed with how playable the game is, even when seeing moderate packet loss. Keep in mind that many optimizations still have yet to be done, so I expect it will only improve with time.

Q: How does the game run with a system that barely meets the published minimum requirements?
A: Here is where you may see some framerate hits and choppy movement, and even slower zone loading. You can certainly play the game, but you wont be running at a high resolution, and you may want to avoid mass melees. Optimizations will only help this as well.

Q: Does equipment rust or wear out over time?
A: As Brad revealed in an earlier IRC chat, no.

Q: How does intelligence affect the ability to learn languages?
A: There is no limit to the number of languages you can learn at this time. Since each language is actually a separate skill, intelligence affects your language learning the same way it affects all skill increases. The more intelligent you are, the faster your skills will increase.

Q: How do languages work?
A: Through an in game menu, you can select which known language you will speak. If anyone in the area knows the language, they will receive your message no different from speaking in Common. If someone does not know the language, the message will be mixed up and look like gibberish.

Q: How important is food and water? Can someone be a good enough forager that they never need to purchase rations?
A: Food and water is important enough that you will never want to be without them. Your character's HP and Stamina will not increase if you are starving. However, you cannot die from starvation. Characters with foraging skill can absolutely live off the land and never purchase rations again. Some magic classes can also summon food at will.

Q: Can necromancers eat the dead instead of food? Can trolls eat opponents that he kills?
A: What is with you people? That's disgusting! Although it might fit with our ideas of RP, there is no way to eat the rotting, bloodied remains of hacked up corpses at this time. Some animals do give you meat that is edible, but you do not eat their corpses.

Q: Will we be able to keep our beta characters in the retail version?
A: This has not been announced, but we expect that you will not be able to keep your beta characters after release.
