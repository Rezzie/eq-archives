ISSUE # 126

 May 27th - June 2nd, 2000

An Old Friend

“Oghma!” Posy almost shouted.

“Posy, my friend, how are you!” Oghma replied, smiling. He waved from the path.

“Oh, Oghma, I’ve been banished from the Wizard’s Guild! It’s horrible!” Posy burst into tears. It was such a relief to see her good friend Oghma’s friendly face! All the past week’s events, and her resolve to not show anyone how hurt she was, tumbled out of her in a rush, and she was unable to restrain herself anymore. She raced down the hill, threw herself into his arms and sobbed.

“That’s ok, Posy,” Oghma said kindly, “you just go ahead and cry a while.” They stood together for several minutes, the small gnome girl enveloped in the brawny arms of the dwarf, her petite frame racked with the weight of her distress and frustration. Oghma murmured soothing words and patted her shoulder comfortingly. The occasional traveler would walk past and give Oghma a questioning look, and Oghma smiled at them reassuringly.

Finally Posy stepped back, her face blotchy and tear-stained. She sniffled. She almost wiped her nose on her yellow guild robe, but Oghma handed her a handkerchief before she could.

“So, now, Posy, tell me what happened. I assume it wasn’t because of a failure in your class work, because I know you are a talented wizard!”

Posy nodded, wiping her face with the handkerchief. “No, that wasn’t it - I do well with classes and the actual training.”

Oghma nodded. “Now I did hear a tale about a little gnome wizard who replaced the standard bat wing component for Levitate with something a bit different?”

Posy giggled through her tear-stained cheeks. “I didn’t know that it would turn them upside down! I thought it would make them float longer!”

Oghma grinned. “I imagine that the classroom looked like an odd collection of tents with knobby-kneed tent poles!” Oghma laughed.

Posy laughed out loud. “It did! And they floated around bouncing into each other, like a dance festival with too much gnomish spirits in the punch!” She smiled. Tobon, the wizard Guildmaster, had reprimanded her for that, but there was a hint of a smile in his stern face.

Oghma pressed on. “How about a certain wizardly tinkering experiment with firewater and a large metal tube?”

Posy smiled, feeling a bit better already. “That one wasn’t my fault!”

Oghma looked at her steadily.

“Oh, alright, it was my idea. I just thought that if fireworks could be launched out of a small tube, that a BIG tube would be able to launch a whole person! It did work, too!”

Oghma laughed. ‘What did you do? I never heard the whole story…”

“Oh, we made a lot of firewater and put it on a big chamber at the bottom of the tube. My friend Millenthorpe stood in the tube and I created a shock of fire to ignite it.” She giggled. “He did fly! It worked!”

“”How far did he go?” Oghma laughed.

“Well, he flew from the Ak’anon entrance to the skull of the old plague dragon. There was a sharp-eyed wizard who saw him flying in, and cast Levitate on him before he landed. He was all right, but a bit blackened.” Posy said defensively, “He had great fun, he said!”

Oghma grinned. “I’m sure he did, but that was hardly the point, now, was it?”

Blushing, Posy said, “Tobon had me working for weeks in the library after that. He also forbade me to tinker for a year.” Posy smiled. “I had fun in the library, though! All that history, the stories, and the knowledge! I learned so much just carrying books around - who the great authors were, what they did. I even saw a small book by you, Oghma!”

Oghma chuckled. “Which one was that?”

“Flora and Fauna of the Qeynos Hills region, it was. You talked all about wolves, snakes, the Klaknak royalty, and a little about the Blackburrow Gnolls. I didn’t know you had traveled so much when I met you!”

Oghma smiled, “I don’t think I had been there when we met. I traveled there while you were studying.” Oghma frowned. “I do need to update my treatise on the Gnolls though. A researcher named Nalixa did some earlier work after the Splitpaw Gnolls were taken over, and I haven’t seen…” He trailed off, lost in thought, pondering the changes in the Gnoll culture. A minute or so passed.

“Oghma?”

Oghma re-focused with at start. “Yes! Sorry! Why did you get banished from the guild? You haven’t told me yet.”

Posy looked sad. “Well, I’m not really sure, but I think that I made a spell with research that they’d never seen before. They’re all upset about it. Mostly I think they kicked me out because I embarrassed them.” Posy sniffled. “I think Tobon still likes me, but I’m a ‘bad example’ for the others.” Posy’s face began to fall, as if she was going to start to cry again.

Oghma glanced at her and said, “Now, Posy - you are an original, that’s for sure - but never a bad example. I wish more people were like you, really.”

“Really?” Posy stifled a sniffle.

“Did you ever hear the story of Fibtik the wolf-rider?”

Posy shook her head.

Fibtik the Wolf-rider

Once upon a time there was a gnome warrior named Fibtik. He was a brave warrior back in the times when Ak’anon was being built, when the Steamfont Mountains were wild and untamed.

Posy interrupted, “Was he part of Edril’s band?”

Oghma looked at her pointedly. “Yes, I was just getting to that.”

Posy bowed her head meekly. “Sorry, continue.”

He was part of a band of gnomes, led by Edril, whose job it was to make the Steamfont Mountains safe to live in. No one knew of the minotaurs who lived in caves along the eastern ranges, and Meldrath had yet to come to inhabit them.

Edril’s band was a very tight little group; they followed orders very well and were highly disciplined - all except Fibtik. He would often roam alone after the band had camped, and while the band was systematically clearing areas; Fibtik would go back to previously cleared areas and run ahead to untouched areas that weren’t scheduled to be cleared. Edril always had to send someone to find him, and he was often disciplined by Edril.

One day, he roamed out to a new area and came across a band of minotaurs! They were gigantic and smelly and he overheard them talking about Edril’s band. It seems that they had been watching from a nearby mountain and were planning on ambushing them in less than an hour!

Well, Fibtik couldn’t let this happen, so he began to creep away from the minotaur camp. Right at that moment he stepped on a twig, and the sensitive ears of the minotaurs heard it. Suddenly the whole camp was in an uproar, and Fibtik found himself chased by a furious band of 8-foot tall bovine monsters!

He was determined not to lead them to the unprepared band of gnomes, so he ran into a cave in a nearby mountain. Like many caves in the Steamfont range, it was actually a warren of tunnels, meandering back in and out of the mountain’s edge.

Racing through the caves, he stumbled across a pack of wolves. Literally stumbling, too, the minotaurs hot on his heels, he tripped and landed astride the pack leader, who, being suddenly awakened from his nap, took off running.

Fibtik squeezed his legs together and hung on for dear life. The wolf ran outdoors, trying to shake off the strange weight on his shoulders. Jumping, hopping, and running flat out, the wolf soon ran straight through the gnome encampment, it’s howls rousing the resting gnomes. Edril’s band, like clockwork, organized themselves into a defensive front and slaughtered the band of minotaur when they followed the wolf into the camp shortly thereafter.

Fibtik became a hero, and Edril used him as his scout from then on. The minotaurs retreated to the caves, and it’s said that Fibtik may have gone on to found the rogues guild there in Ak’anon.

“So you see Posy, sometimes people who march to a different drummer are destined for greater things! Perhaps the great Brell has plans for you beyond your wildest imaginings.” Oghma smiled. “You may wish, however, to curb your boisterous nature a bit so as to cause less damage to those around you,” he grinned.

Posy felt better than she had in a while. “Oghma, I was on my way to Felwithe to go to the Wizard Guild there. Do you think they can teach me higher magic?”

Oghma grunted. “I’m sure they could, but the question is more - would they? The high elves don’t hold gnome magic in high esteem, especially since gnomes have delved into the forbidden necromantic magic.” He frowned. “Another thing that doesn’t sit well with them is experimentation. They almost threw my friend Mundas out of town, though that mishap had little to do with his spell craft. He tends at times to be a bit wordy. Most high elves I know could use a good dose of a sense of humor.”

Posy looked worried. “What am I to do then? There are no other wizard guilds on this continent!”

Oghma smiled, “I guess that means you need to go to another continent. Perhaps Antonica beckons you. Qeynos and Freeport both have very good wizards guilds. Freeport is where the ships dock from the Butcherblock Mountains. You could go there!”

“Among the humans?” Posy looked doubtful. “I hear they smell bad, and try to step on us gnomes.”

Oghma chuckled. “No, they don’t smell any better or worse than us, and some are quite nice.” Oghma paused. “I have a friend I’m meeting in Kelethin later today, perhaps he’s heading that way. He’s human, and a nice one. With him by your side you should have no worries.”

Oghma and Posy broke camp and headed along the path to Kelethin. On the way, they passed the Felwithe gates and Posy looked longingly at the high marble spires of the High Elven city. Perhaps later, when she was a powerful mage in her own right, she could return to Felwithe and be treated according to her due.

Posy had heard of the tree city of Kelethin in stories, but was unprepared for the real thing. Trotting along the path, she saw the giant trees of Kelethin, with their sturdy wooden platforms arching over her head. The laughter and singing of the wood elves lifted her heart, and the faces she saw seemed quite friendly. Several of the handsome young wood elves gave teasing greetings that she hoped were not meant as they sounded.

“Ho, dwarf! Brought your own date? Are the ladies of Kelethin not to your liking?”

“Fair damsel, truly, your friend needs a shave! You may want to wait though! You might not like what you see when you look under the beard!”

“Oghma!” one shouted. “You have brought me the woman of my dreams! A thousand thanks!”

Posy blushed.

“The festival here is in full swing,” Oghma chuckled. “Don’t mind their teasing. At least they aren’t as snooty as the High Elves of Felwithe.”

“Do they not like gnomes too? Do all Elves hate gnomes?”

“Elves feel close ties to nature. To them, nature is a companion, a guide, a friend. Gnomes seem to feel that nature is something to be tamed or improved. Look, for example, at the great waterwheels in Ak’anon.” Oghma paused. “Can you imagine elves building such a thing?”

“But it provides light and energy for all of Ak’anon! It’s good for the whole city!” Posy asserted.

“In the eyes of an Elf, the Gnomes have chained and sucked the life from their friend the River. It’s not to say that individual Gnomes aren’t liked - Gnomes and Elves have a long shared history battling the Orcs of Crushbone. I just believe that Elves see Gnomes as cruel to nature, whereas Gnomes believe that their clockwork creations are an improvement on nature.”

“Our cleaners keep Ak’anon clean, and our merchants and guards give us more time to pursue what is important!” Posy said defensively.

“True, Posy - but not all see it that way, and at times it may be better to not point out our differences and instead celebrate the things that are shared. Only then can we come to a more complete understanding of things.” Oghma looked sad. “Dwarves are about the worst of all. We feel we work so hard to extract the gifts of Brell from the earth and work them into wondrous items. Somehow, to us that means that no other race can match our industriousness, and therefore all others are lazy. Would you see such a festival in Kaladim? This festival lasts over a fortnight. A dwarven festival is a serious thing, with much pride and boasting, and over in two days else work would suffer. We feel we are so self-sufficient, that we need not ask for aid from any other race.”

“Do dwarves need help?” Posy asked.

“Every race needs help against their enemies. Elves, Gnomes and Dwarves all battle Kobolds, Goblins, Ogres and Orcs.” Oghma looked grim. “Ak’anon must deal with the crazed Meldrath and his minions.”

Oghma paused. “What if Meldrath enlisted the help of the Crushbone Orcs to fight the gnomes? Would the elves come running to help?”

Posy pondered these new ideas as they approached a low platform near one of the largest trees. Oghma gestured to her to stand on the platform, then lifted a wooden bar near it. Posy heard the familiar sound of mechanicals. Suddenly, the platform rose into the air and they were lifted to the tree city.

Posy looked at Oghma. Oghma shrugged. “Built by gnomes,” he grinned.

“My friend is at the Heartwood Tavern,” Oghma said. “He came for the festival, I believe he has a bard friend singing in one of the celebrations.” They strode across a swaying bridge and entered the tavern.

The tavern was filled with elves, drinking and singing. Posy looked through the gloom of the tavern and spotted a lone human, who must have been the only one in the tavern not drunk out of his mind.

Oghma walked over to the table where the human sat, slowly sipping an Elven wine.
“Cairbre, I’d like you to meet my friend Posy,” Oghma said.

The human squatted easily down to be at eye level with Posy. He was dressed in an armor Posy had never seen before, steel-looking with gold trim. Though well kept, the armor was dented and dinged with scratches and marks from countless battles. Kind eyes smiled from a battle-hardened visage.

“Hello, young Posy,” Cairbre said. “Pleased to meet you!” He thoughtfully slid a small step stool over to his booth, though you could tell that he winced slightly with the motion. “Would you like to sit with me?”
