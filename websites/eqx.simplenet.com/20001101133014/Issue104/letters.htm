When the original "Fields of Athenry" (by Pete 'Satan' St John) were published here, they swept across the land like a icy hand of death, making all music classes for students everywhere hell. Still puzzled, my cross-atlantic friend? Ah, I see you do not know about something called "The Syllabis"....

Every year, a gathering of foul daemons known as "Scruduthoir an Teastas Soisearach" (Examiners of the Junior Certificate) would meet to decide what mistempered lyrical monstrosities that year's hapless candidates would have to brave in order to ensure survival. Those who failed this often lethal test would never be seen or heard from again. Those who pleased these daemons, wether by looks or by academic acomplishments, gained what can be rougly translated from Faa'nesh, or Examiner's Tongue, as a "pass".

To cut a hideously extended tale to a thrilling demise, "The Fields of Athenry" was included almost every time.

I can still hear it now, on quiet nights, those terrible lyrics and that organ-jarringly bad accompniment....

All I am saying is, be warned. For the power behind that song is belied by its seemingly innocent exterior.

You have been warned.
