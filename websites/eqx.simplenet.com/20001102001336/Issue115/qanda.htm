Q:  Ahoy! I was recently hunting the area the Dervish seemed to have camped in the Western Commonlands, and met a fellow enchanter. This enchanter was a high elf-borne, but kept changing into a human, particularly at night. I asked him why and he said for the human's exp bonus. I had always kept my gnome illusion up til this point (in hindsight, I wish I was born a gnome, then if I needed a mount I could just charm a fire beetle and ride when my feet grew tired) and was wondering, does the human illusion give you the human's innate ability at learning from fights quickly? Also, does this mean, that a human using another illusion loses this natural "defense?" Thank you, and may your songs always be ones of joy and never of sorrow.

Dedil Sangre, Norrath's Foremost Pseudo-Gnome

A:  The thing to remember when switching to the illusion/enchantment of another race is that you will gain only the abilities of the race and the basic faction of that race. Thus, if the high elf was turning into a human, he could expect to gain any abilities of the human, but things like the experience charts would not be affected and there wouldn't be much point in it. I do understand the charm of those adorable little gnomes, however, and would love to see one perched on a fire beetle's back resting his tiny toes.


Q:  Hail fellow bard. I have a question on the lvl 11 bard song, Cinda's Charismatic Carillon. I just turned lvl 11, and I eagerly bought the new song, only to find no real use for it. Does it work, if so, how much of an effect does it have.

Tegeren, Bard of the 11th Chord, Faithful Follower of Tunare, Erolisi Marr Server

A:  Yes, Cinda's Charismatic Carillon does work. What you will need to do is target the npc you wish to have some effect on and then start your song. The music will raise your faction with 1 type of the faction carried by the mob. I think you can expect the faction increase to be one level. Reports from various Pillars of Wisdom seem to focus on using the song to increase your buying and selling power. According to them, those little faction increases can help fill your purse quicker!

Q:  I would like to know -- is there any good to drinking beer, wine, etc, in Everquest?

Facan

A:  Well, from a bardic point of view, wine flows with song, or is it the other way around? Anyway, supposing you wished to enjoy the tavern social life, then a bit of imbibing -- and making sure the bard has enough coin to keep her whistle wet -- can be a delight indeed. As for effects on your smiting, well warriors will find their strength and stamina going up with a few sips of wine for courage, but sadly, just about the time their arms are buffed up, their feet get entangled all to easily (they lose agility and dexterity). Other than that, I can tell you that getting drunk is useful if you wish to gain motion sickness. Hmmm, well at least that's what I got from it after a bit.

Q:  I have two questions actually. First I am an Erudite wizard of the 15 level and no matter what I kill my exp does not seem to increase. I have leveled countless enemies, i.e., Derv camps, for countless hours. My friend, an Erudite wizard of the same level, gets more exp than me also when he fights and when we group he still gets more. This is frustrating me greatly as I have notced I lose more exp than the people I know when I die on EQ also. Does this happen to anyone else or is something not right. (I have asked a guide about it but they ignored me enough that I gave up). Also, how do you build faction with ogres and trolls if you are a good race. I have tried to find out for a long time but I'm not getting anywhere. I am friends with every other race, even dark elf.
Thank you for your time, sighned,

Talv, Erudite Wizard

A:  Whoa, all these questions that call on expertise I don't really have. However, I did talk to the Pillar of Wisdom, Adelrune, who said that chances are you are actually getting the same numbers in experience as your friend who is the same level, race and class but that you are experiencing graphical issues with the little experience bar being not entirely accurate. As far as getting your experience, it's important to always use /con on monsters as even monsters of the same race/class may actually con differently even as they appear the same. Also, it is true that one "blue" monster will give you different experience than another "blue" mob. Usually, if I feel like I'm stuck and not making progress, I check around and try to find spots that are recommended for good experience rather than just sticking with where I am. What you might want to do, if you ascertain that you are indeed doing much worse with experience than your friend, is to write to eqtech@verant.com and send them a list of your hardware to see if they could figure out any reason for the disparity in experience. Some guides are tech wizzies and can help you troubleshoot, but many are not, so it's best to take questions like this directly to Verant to try to get some help. Okies, moving on to the faction question. Unless you are already "dubious" to good races and can enter the troll or ogre cities to do newbie quests there, my Pillars of Wisdom say that there really isn't a way to increase your faction with the trolls and ogres. Besides, if you made friends with a troll, you might get something like cooties -- or worse! Shiver!
