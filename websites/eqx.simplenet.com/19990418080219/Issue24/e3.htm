ISSUE # 24June 1st - June 5th

EverQuest E3 Report

I recently had the pleasure of attending the Electronics Entertainment Expo (E3) in Atlanta. There, I was treated to dazzling displays by the big names in computer entertainment, complete with colored lights, smoke machines, live mascots, loud music, and any method of attracting attention that could be safely executed in a crowded environment. And then, away from the noise and away from the crowds, at the end of a quiet hallway, Sony Interactive Studios America had a meeting room. It was a pleasure to be able to get a hands-on demo of EverQuest without having to shout over arcade noises and theme songs played over loudspeakers. I was even treated to hanging out while Brad McQuaid gave a couple demos, and I was able to interject some questions as well.

Let me start off this article by answering a couple burning questions. Beta has not started yet. They only have in-house testers right now. They expect beta to start anywhere from 2 to 4 weeks. Secondly, yes the game does live up to the hype. Okay, now that you feel a little better, let's move on to the details. 		Creating a Character

When you first create your character, you chose a race, name, and gender and can select one of five faces (more will be added). The faces are different for males and females, obviously, and there is even at least one bearded female dwarf. There is a fairly strict selection of classes based on which race you chose.
