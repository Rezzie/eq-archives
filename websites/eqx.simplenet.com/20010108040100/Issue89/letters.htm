Hi, nice magazine!

In response to your latest editorial,

What I think is missing from Everquest is in-game support for character individuality and creativity. There are very few ways to make a character stand out from the crowd: rare items and creative use of /emotes and /says.

Everyone of a given race has the same body type; the different faces are good, but there just aren't enough of them. Except for hard-to-get clothing, there are not enough wardrobe choices to distinguish characters. Everyone (within a given class) uses the same animated emotes, the same spell effects, the same weapon attack animations.

On a text-based MUD, /emotes and /says are more than enough -- since the whole world is text. On Everquest, you might see a group of people run past you and be gone without a single emote. The only method you have to identify any of them is OOC -- reading the name floating above their head.

What Everquest needs are ways for people to "customize" their characters in ways that have no effect on gameplay. I would love to play a fat elf, or buy a hat with a feather in it, or walk with a limp. I would love to have a parrot on my shoulder, a weatherstained cloak, or a rune of power glowing on my forehead.

What do all of these things have in common? They make other players think about the /character/. They convey, in the visual language required of graphical online RPGs like Everquest, personal information about the character.

I recently sent my level 7 high elf on a perilous trip to Erudin just so I could buy a Apprentice Robe of a different color than everyone else. I'm hoping that at least some people see him and wonder. That wonder is a better roleplay seed than any amount of background history.

Geoff Speare
(Cuelaar, High-elf Wizard on Quellious)
