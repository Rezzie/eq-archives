ISSUE # 17Apr. 11th - Apr. 17th

The Bridge

The pounding of his heart echoed the pounding of his feet in the gloom-filled tunnels. Arthedain raced down the corridor and turned into a large cavern. His entire party had been wiped out by Gnolls, and now they were chasing him through their lair, trying to catch him. So far he had been able to elude them, on occasion hiding behind the rotting banners that hung in rooms, at times fighting an isolated Gnoll or pair of Gnolls and quickly dispatching them. He had abandoned his equipment and armor long ago, to increase his running speed. All he carried now was his sword and shield, and he was beginning to have second thoughts about carrying his shield. It would be difficult to hide such a large thing, and if it was discovered, it would lead the Gnolls to him instead of helping him escape. He had already had to deal with them tracking him along the trail of dead Gnolls he left behind him. He raced though the cavern to another corridor. "This didn’t turn out like it was supposed to," he thought. The research done by the mage showed that this lair had been abandoned for years, but had once held a band of orcs. The party had gambled that this band was part of an army that got wiped out in a battle with dwarves a century ago, and that their lair would be abandoned, perhaps containing the spoils of war.

Tale by Oghma, Fraterni Lux.
