Mage Combat

Over the last while there have been some theories forming about the different magic professions and which will be better at what tasks. I gave some thought to this and by using the classic dividing lines between schools I formed a theory about mages, and came up with an interesting idea about magic combat. Not so much mages in combat, but more so mages in combat with each other. In every game in the past a huge portion of the killing power has laid upon the magic using party member once at high levels. Now in recent past in online games this makes for a twist in the Player Killing Arena, where magic is almost absolutely king of the Cobras. I think that EverQuest's magic system brings an innovation in where Asheron's Call fails so far by design. While AC has the infinite spell creation system that makes for very cool spells, I highly doubt that AC has 4 to 6 magic using professions with unique spell lists. This argument leads to "which is the best magic class in combat then?" Well, assuming that there are no balancing issues that are left unsolved, there should be a few different answers.

Wizards seem to be the kings of contemporary mage combat, having the most power in a head-on battle against any single magic using opponent, plus the spells of travel which may or may not be useful in a fight. Classically known as Invocation in AD&D and other games, it often deals with area of effect damaging spells and some awesome single target spells. I see the Wizards also having a share of utility and defending spells, which makes them overall the most straight forward killing machines in the game.
