ISSUE # 11Feb. 28th - Mar. 6th

Mirror Lake

Vagarus fell, not with one thud but with two.

Sadly, his eyes did not close on their own. I had to reach beneath his helm and force his eyelids down to cover that vacant stare.

Blood looks so black in the moonlight. Once I’d stripped my gloves off and begun to scrub my hands in the lake they were restored to their usual white pallor. These gloves would never come clean, and so I let them drop below the surface and be swallowed by the reflections of moon and mountains.

Had I always been this ghostly? I stared at my reflection. Perhaps it was a trick of the light; A prank played by the bewitching goddess of the moon or a glamour of the water sprites. But I had been born of a civilization half dead, and as such I was prone to show the defects of my parents. The white visage that blinked it’s bewilderment at me was indeed my own.

Somewhere not far off, the night beasts howled. Canines, most likely. They would be coming for Vagarus, his remains lying out in the open where I’d felled him. And I would let them have him. It would be best that no living soul know that I had taken the life of my friend; If his remains be eaten by wild animals, then so be it.

I am no murderer, or at least I had never thought myself a murderer. But the evidence lay a few feet into the bushes. And what did I have to show for my treachery? A handful of herbs, a satchel of used clothing, a few weapons which I could not use and which would fetch little in the bazaar, and that damnable ring.

I don’t know what possessed me. It was as if it had been mine, or it had belonged to someone I loved. Because it was too small for his finger, he had hung it from a small chain, to wear around his neck. And he stood there, the thing dangling in the breeze, and as I stared into the opal I saw things… Things so horrid they couldn’t possibly have happened, yet they were so real. I saw the pale faces of my people speckled with dirt as men stood about, flinging dirt into the mass grave where they lay. I heard the laughter of conquerors thunder across that place; I could smell the ash of scorched flesh mingle with the fires which claimed the village structures. 		And for a second, as I looked at that time and these things which happened long before my birth and which were passed to me only in songs of the bards, I saw someone snatch the ring from off the finger of a dead girl. "Off" the finger would be a kindness of metaphor, for he actually snapped the finger off the body and slid the finger out of the ring. And as he held up the ring before his eyes I could see his face, and it was that of Vagarus.

The rush of horror and unheralded rage took me. I am a magician by trade, and carry few weapons. I have little training in the art of the blade, but in my strange berserk state I pulled Vagarus’ sword from his sheath and swung the thing with accuracy and perhaps with more strength than I had thought myself capable of bringing to bare. His head fell from his shoulders with the same casualness with which one might take off their helm.

But this obscenity I had seen in the opal’s dark eye was impossible. My people had died centuries before Vagarus was born. And all I could think was that perhaps this thing was bewitched. I had heard many tale of cursed goods and as part of my magical study had read of how to make such a thing. But I had never encountered one.

Story by Archanalia
