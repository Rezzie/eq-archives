Q:  So I'm a 6th level human ranger and a bunch of my wood elf buddies convince me to visit Greater Faydark. The scenery is beautiful, the natives are friendly, and the school system is par exellence, however I have a problem. I can handle not being able to see half the time even with light; it's embarassing to be a ranger and get lost at night, but I'm getting confidence therapy and I think I can beat it. I understand the high prices I'm charged; I realize that designer merchandise demands a larger fee. What drives me buggo is no armor in my bloody size! I can't afford the Calvin Klien patchwork armor they try to sell me in Felwithe, the stupid Orcs keep dropping small tunics or large belts (making me wonder how they ever manage to get a tuxedo fitted) , and the tailoring guide tells me to use catskin. Great! Where are the cats? In some level 30 area in Steamfront? Please help. It's hard to be a tank without armor and my friends are gaining levels faster than I can take damage.

Tralpaz

A:  Okies, it sounds like those very aloof elves are missing a bet in fashion merchandising by not stocking medium sizes. However, all is not lost and with a little buying trip across the sea, you should be wearing suitable ranger attire soon. First, it is important to remember that if you insist on tanking, even the greatest armor in the world will not keep you from dying relatively often. Why, my Pillar of Wisdom, Sylvos, is now a highly skilled ranger with glorious duds but since he fancies himself as a tank, he still dies all the time. If you really want to level fast then, the very best armor you might seek is a huge barbarian warrior to take most of the damage while you raise those very considerable ranger offensive skills. That said, of course you need armor and there are two types you might consider at your level. If you and your friends can gather about 100 plat, you should be able to engage the monsters while clad in a full suit of banded armor and be glad of it. If poverty is still with you (as it often is with bards, regardless of level), then tailoring is the way to go. I'd suggest you get a ride of some sort over to West Karana and seek a cat party. With a few cat pelts and your sewing kit, you should be wearing patched armor, medium sized, quite soon. Leather armor is also an option, but the merchants charge a fortune for it and you gotta watch the seams. They skimp so badly sometimes on the sewing that with a tap from the tip of a decayed skeleton's sword, the entire armor falls off you leaving you quite ummm breezy.

Q:  Hail, I will just start by saying great newspaper. But I was wondering, what is the deal with charged items. How do you use one, and what happens when you use all charges? I am a magician, and I can summon a Staff of Tracing which has 2 charges of flare. I wanted to use the charge, so I kept clicking repeatedly on it when it was equipped. I eventually used it (I saw myself cast it) and it said I only had 1charge left. I repeated the same thing. When I cast the effect again, it still said the staff had 1 charge left! I did it over and over, and it kept casting the effect. When I died, I gave that staff to my new pet, and summoned a new one. I tried the same process again. This time, I ran out of charges, and it said it had 'effect:flare' with no charges. And when I equipped it, it gave me a fairly strong light source. Can you explain any of this to me? When it says "effect" of something, that means it will usually go off during battle (My warrior has a PGT, 'effect:berzerker strength'). Does that mean after I use all charges, I can still use the effect? Please, if you can help me, any information would be appreciated. Thank you for your time.

Marcamo

A:  You'll have to hope I understood what the Pillar of Wisdom, Tagad, was explaining to me and that I am relaying it correctly. Basically, items in Everquest can contain effects with charges and procs or combinations of the same. An item may have an effect that you click on with a limited number of charges; an effect that you click on with an unlimited number of uses; an always-on effect usually seen upon wearing or wielding the item (some will even have an effect in inventory like lightstones); and an effect that procs (like the PGT "berzerker strength"). If your item has charges, it may -- in some instances be rechargeable -- the charges left on the item will show just below the effect description. If the item has more than one effect, it is possible to have the charges run out on one part of it and still gain the benefit of the remaining unlimited effect. Procs are essentially unlimited charges that you don't click on that do occur under certain circumstances, usually battle. I am not sure what to say about the variable effects you got from the two different Staffs of Tracing. Perhaps they are meant that way or perhaps there is some bug at work.
