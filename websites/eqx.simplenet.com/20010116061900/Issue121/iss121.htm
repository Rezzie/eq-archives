Calling all writers!

EverQuest Express is trying to expand its staff. We’re looking for creative and intelligent people that are interested in promoting role-playing. Don’t play EverQuest? No problem. While EverQuest Express is primarily about EverQuest, it is certainly not exclusively about EverQuest. We welcome talented people from any role-playing oriented pursuit. If you think you’ve got something interesting to say about computer role-playing games, pen and paper role-playing games, the computer industry in general, role-playing in general, or living the life of a role-player, please, send us your thoughts.

Discovery of the Scale

Val Athrandir had never really cared for anyone else but himself. He had become a rogue and learned the art of thievery and assassination in hopes of making money, lots of money. He had not known his Wood Elf mother or his Human father. They had left him to grow up in the care of an innkeeper in Freeport.

Now that Val was nearly twenty-three he traveled the world on his own, not caring for anyone, not needing to help anyone, and making a name for himself in the dark underworld of rogues. For the time he resided in Kelethin, waiting for any job offers, pick pocketing if any money was needed.

    Tale by Brian Johnstone

Dynamic Roleplaying

I couldn’t agree more with Absor’s editorial regarding the Verant sponsored events. “Finally!” I say, but also I’m inclined to say, “It’s a start, not the finish line.” Planned events are just that, planned events. I, like many others, would like to see an RPG based on a more dynamic model. Of course, be forewarned, I’m a roleplayer in a pretty big way so many of my suggestions don’t match up with the MMORPG status quo.

    Article by Russell Linton

Letters to the Editors

The ongoing debate about dynamically run GM events continues.

I had the rather unusual luck of happening upon a GM event in Greater Fay earlier today. Normally, I'd be estatic. Due to my play schedule, I rarely see any of these occurring in real time (having to read about it much later online). My 15 bard had forgotten to buy a song and I popped into the zone just as the EQ populace had gotten wind of it, so my discovery was earned the hard way--sitting in chat after being booted by the server.
