ISSUE # 53

December 19th - December 25th


The Fall of Ambi

As I was wandring to and fro, I spied a soaring sight,
A wood elf maid with hair of gold engage-ed in a fight.
A Deathfist Foot, its lips snarled back, did press her hard and fast,
And brave, brave Ambi showed no lack with sword in her hands clasped.
On hillside did the swords ring high-- Foot massive, Ambi small,
But, oh, her smalling frame was spry, and blow by blow did fall.
Ambi elf, your soul is brave! Your spirit made of fire.
You did not run, nor ground you gave as you leapt into death's pyre.

But fate had chosen differently, as Ambi soon did tell
For Foot stood straight, a stone sentry, as if wounds were not felt.
Soon sweat and blood ran down her chest and mingled in her hair.
The Deathfist raised a howl to test if bravery turned despair.
But Ambi stayed so proud and true, though strength began to fade.
E'vn when the death-blow cleaved her through, her brave choice had been made.
Ambi elf, your soul is brave! Your spirit made of fire.
You did not run, nor ground you gave as you leapt into death's pyre.

Her winsome form fell quickly down in crumpled bloody heap,
The Foot took rib-bone for a crown, with blood he striped his cheek.
Till one who watched the fight took heart, though cowardly in act,
A moment late played out his part and ran Foot through the back.
But Elves don't weep, kind dwarves don't mourn for Ambi's fall this night
For she did test and was proven born with a warriors gift of fight.
Ambi elf, your soul is brave! Your spirit made of fire.
You did not run, nor ground you gave as you leapt into death's pyre.

    Tale by Jithri, Bard and Wanderer
