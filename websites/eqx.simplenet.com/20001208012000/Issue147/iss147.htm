Prologue

Oghma idly toyed with his empty mug. Age-old rings of purplish wine from the bottom of mugs made haphazard designs on the scarred oak tabletop. The tavern was quiet, and the muted sounds of those who had decided to stay in the common room and were settling down for a night’s rest made a murmuring backdrop to his thoughts.

    Tale by Oghma

EARTH TO NORRATH, EARTH TO NORRATH

Every once in awhile, or perhaps more often than that, I enter into one of those day-dreaming zones of unreality where it’s necessary for someone to repeat, “Earth to Nep, Earth to Nep! Are you there Nep?” Usually a few iterations is enough to bring my focus back into the same room as the person calling for my attention and asking me to respond to them.

    Essay by Nepenthia

Flowers of Evil, Part II

A deep fog settled into valley, shrouding the lake in the early morning hours. As the sun peeked a pale ray over the Rathe, Furlespit lay by a river reflecting over the last week of her travels. Hidden beneath the roots of a leaning mossy tree drifting through the footsteps she would take in the next few days, she started to remember times she has taken this road before. She had walked through the sands with her brother in research, Uriah. Like Beldro, he had bore witness to some terrible truth and it held his mind in chaos.

    Tale by Ian Ash
