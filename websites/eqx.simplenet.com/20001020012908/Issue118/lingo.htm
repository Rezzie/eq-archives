ISSUE # 118

 April 1st - April 7th, 2000

BARBS DON’T TALK LIKE THAT!!

I, actually Absor, recently received an email in regards to my “guide” on Common to Barbarian. Actually I am glad the individual took the time to write in because it brings up an issue that has quite a history with me and the mode of speech or “lingo” I use when playing a Barbarian. This topic, however, is not limited to just the mode of speech I use, but any mode of speech players use trying to give their characters more depth. Here’s the email:

I am not usually one to complain, but when I read the Common to Barbarian section in issue 117 I had to. It may just be a server difference (I hope). What is called Barbarian on in the article is far closer to how Trolls and Ogres speak on the servers I've been on. I spent about a month as a Troll and several more months as other races, so I should have a pretty good understanding of their language. All Barbarians that role played an language around me, even in Halas, have used a Scottish language similar to Braveheart or the appropriate Highlander flashbacks. I'm having to restrain Snuggleguts from smashing you for attributing his language to puny humans. I am sure that there are Barbarians who are equally upset about the language mix-up.

Thanks for listening to my ranting, Khamul writing for Snuggleguts, Troll Shadowknight of 14 seasons P.S. I know Everquest is just a game, but its such a great game with a rich, largely player created environment.

There’s several issues revolving around lingoes but let me address them one by one completely out of order.

I spent about a month as a Troll and several more months as other races, so I should have a pretty good understanding of their language.

I think it’s really great that people have spent quite a bit of time noticing other lingoes players try to use. That this person noted some similarities between the lingo I portrayed and Troll, yet not call it outright Troll, is very observant. Of course to my eye, Troll and my version of Barbarian look totally different. Following in line with what Khamul states he should know about Troll since he spent a month as one, I shall also state that I should know about the Barbarian lingo I use. I have been using this lingo for seven years to depict a Barbarian Drawl.

Some History

Eight years ago playing a game called Drakkar a friend of mine Zoddie, known as Zodiac and Zzerker, and I, known as Onyx and Onnie, came up with the pattern style for the lingo used in my Common to Barbarian Guide. It was developed over a period of two years playing that game. In fact Gimili, a Sysop for Drakkar who’s been with them for ages and still is, perverted the lingo even further. He’ll say “yew” while we’d say “ya’s” and so forth. The lingo was in it’s final form and as you see it now well before Ultima Online even went into it’s first phases of alpha.

When I came to the Avault Newsgroup Forums where the developers talked to the players, EverQuest was not yet in beta nor had they even put a start date on phase 0.5. I used this lingo under the persona of Onyx for posting IC statements and joke replies to messages. I was told by a couple of people that the lingo was UO knewl d00d speech and the forum was going to burn down in effigy because of it. That knewl d00d’s seeing me speak in that manner would encourage them to flock to the forum. I had no idea at the time what a knewl d00d was, nor how on earth my lingo would relate to them. I had never played UO, so I set about trying to figure out what they were talking about.

Lo and Behold, I went to a page that was listed with someone’s name, I forget now, calling themselves the Role-play Master of UO. It showed a screen shot of a knewl d00d saying something like:

“1 \/\/1|| |<1|| dat”

The only word I could understand at the time was “dat” of course. I had not been exposed to this other mode of speech. I nearly fell out of my chair laughing. I looked in other places and sure enough “dat” and “da” were all over UO. I by no means claim ownership, nor do I think Zoddie would, we never even thought of getting a copyright on it... darn it. But I did find it vastly amusing when our friends, upon being asked, told us how they started “dat-ing” and “da-ing” and soon many on the server/shard were doing it. Perhaps “ya’s gonna kill dat?” was all over another game, I don’t know. I have played LoK, NWN, etc. so has Zoddie and other friends, and I had not seen it unless we were using it. It is possible that we were the sole originators. Many people in Drakkar would use that mode of speech and it’s merely an assumption of mine that possibly that’s how it spread into UO. I could be completely wrong and it just evolved there on it’s lonesome. The point of where it came from however really isn’t an issue.

More History, but not so long ago

The first time I was in Western Karana during beta Oxesiess started throwing a fit. She had talked to Tiny Miller. I had not known until then that EverQuest had put a lingo in for Troll and Ogre speech which contained some key basics Zoddie and I used for Barb. Oxesiess’ fit was about Tiny not speaking properly. I think the Monk with me thought I was nuts for yelling at an NPC and trying to correct his speech patterns into a more Barbish style. Since that day, and even before, I have been informed by a few players or followers of the game about several things:

1) You don’t talk right
2) I can’t understand you
3) You sound knewl
4) Barbs don’t talk like that
5) You’re speaking like a Troll

Wash rinse and repeat. Of course in my mind, Trolls and Ogers speak more like Oxes, NOT the other way around, since the speech predates them crawling out of the mud, swamp, or changing from frogloks. This still however is not the main point. The main point is...

WHAT does it matter?

Should my barb perhaps use the word “to” just like the other 12 races because “ta” is reserved to Trolls and Ogres only? All the rest of us need to speak not using the word “da” because that’s reserved as well. It’s okay for us other 12 to share the word “the” but not “da”? How would you attribute Ogres and Trolls learning to talk in the first place instead of only speaking Froglok? Think the dark elves took time off from being so superior and evil to teach them? Couldn’t a character possibly with heavy human background have been involved in such a thing through their portion of the tribe? What about the barb in question coming from a tribe in Ro which supplies food shipments to Halas? This causes the barbs to heavily interact with Ogres and Trolls. The results is the barbs speech warped over time. This couldn’t have happened? There are all sorts of possibilities here, none of which are to be ruled out and I haven’t even really started racking my brain.

How many have actually sat down and tried to create an entire lingo? Honestly if anyone has I would love to hear it. I am completely fascinated by lingoes and ways to pervert speech so that your character SOUNDS like they have a drawl. It is not an easy task to have text reflect annunciation of words. A high-elf could walk up to Oxes, start speaking like Oxes, and she would be completely enthralled. She would most likely not even notice until she had “thunkered” that the elf was speaking like her. Is this WRONG or something to get upset about because the high-elf has a drawl? Absolutely not. Perhaps that High-Elf has a past history of spending childhood years with the tribe Oxes was in and grew up speaking like them vs. the parents. Makes sense since that’s what the child would have heard predominantly.

When I hear lingoes I think of it as that extra little touch people are giving their characters. It’s like wearing a wrap with that black dinner dress. I honestly cannot fathom why other players instead of trying, in character, to find out someone’s background feel the need to stand up and inform a player that they are “Doing it Wrong”.

The different modes of speech for barbs I hear not only on my server but also in the forums, range widely from heavy “Aye’s”, to Oxes “Ayeps”, to “Yah!” I attribute these to different tribal upbringings. Such as the difference you can hear from a Northern Accent in the US and a Southern one, or a British Accent and an Australian. Should I be running around telling the barb who says, “You know I really need to go educate that fellow shaman on when to blast and when to heal” that he sounds like the average wizard when he speaks? I think not.

I seriously hope to one day meet an Ogre or Troll Role-played with some snap for languages so I can get into a argument with them just for fun. Until then, let me give some examples side by side of Common, Troll, and my mode of Barbarian:

Common: Think
Troll: Tink
Barbarian: Thunk, Thunker, Thunkered

Common: We slaughtered them all perfectly!
Troll: Me barsh dem froggies gud
Barbarian: I’s done bashy good! (or) I’s made all da lil beasties DEADED!

Perhaps it’s just me but I see a pretty big difference. Here’s more.

Common: Did you see the humans around here?
Troll: Did yoose see dem humies round?
Barbarian: Did ya’s see da humans round ere?

Common: That was a powerful spell my friend.
Troll: Dats sum gud mojo yer doin dere
Barbarian: Dat was an awesome finger wiggle’n ya’s did.

Common: You shouldn’t eat those dwarves because the little bits stink in your teeth.
Troll: Yer shoudnt eet dem dorfs cuz dem bits stick in yer teeth.
Barbarian: Ya’s should’n eat da lil uns cause da lil bits stick in yer teeth.

Common: Put me in a spot will you? Prepare to die!
Troll: put inna spot? me barsh yoose
Barbarian: Put me in da spot will ya’s? I’s gonna bashy da snot outta ya’s!

Verant has not released an official language guide to this game and until they do really no one can claim a “Proper” way. I seriously doubt they will however, since it limits players in the various aspects of developing their own lingoes as added background for their characters. There are NPCs that speak with a lingo up in Halas, though most NPCs don’t. I think Verant meant to put it in the game but just ran out of time. The basis for that thought is that there’s one particular NPC that if you speak to him regarding one quest, he replies in regular English. If you speak to him regarding another quest, he replies in a lingo. Are all the barbs who don’t speak with perfect English like he does on the one quest wrong? Are all the barbs who don’t speak with his lingo on the other quest wrong in their speech patterns? I’d have to double check but I am almost 100% positive he uses either the “da” or “dat” when he talks. So, does this mean all the barbs trying to emulate a “Braveheart” sound but don’t use “dat” or “da” are wrong? Of course not.

Enjoy or have fun with the creativity people are trying to bring and the depth they try to add by opening their mouths. This applies to those that have decided to Role-play with the “Thee or Thou” to the “dat’s” to the “dorfs”. I hope anyone who has encountered the “You’re not doing it right” or “you sound like (insert), not what you’re trying to sound like” will do like me, just keep role-playing along. There are no hard lines for how we all should sound, there is no right or wrong.

There is a part in this email that’s dead on, “P.S. I know Everquest is just a game, but its such a great game with a rich, largely player created environment.” My guide was written in fun and nothing more. Something on the lighter side that I hoped people would laugh at with translations from a single word spoken, into about 9 words. The P.S. sums up the whole reason I wrote the guide in the first place, that last P.S. is so true and I hope it never stops. Particularly with encouragements like the one that follows, which have and most likely always will confuse me, “I am sure that there are Barbarians who are equally upset about the language mix-up.”
