t'Ceira and Darvinn's Love

Part Six

As Hargor began speaking the strange words that I will never understand, I looked over to t'Ceira and mouthed, "I love you, Hon." Feeling as giddy as an ogreling who just killed his first halfling. I watched the glowing lights form on his hands and then pass onto t'Ciera and then me. It seemed as though the lights and words were reaching a crescendo, my heart beating wildly as if I were riding into battle with my brothers, then a blinding bright light... Darkness... and... Quiet.

    Tale by Darvinn


Identity Crisis

After the past few articles were not well received by the EQX reading public, I sort of swore myself off of articles. Just write stories, I said to myself. But then I've had some kind of problem with the game lately and it was something I couldn't quite put my finger on. Now I sort of know. There's a lot of pseudo-psychology in here, (the only kind I practice) so be prepared for amateur theories. But then they all pertain to me, so I'm the only one I can really insult with this.

Love Amongst the Darkness

Alhazred stepped back from the freshly killed skeleton. He took a breath and then bent down to salvage what he could from the corpse. Bone chips and a few copper pieces. You will rise again my friend, thought Alhazred, but this time under my power.

It had been only a few days since Alhazred had emerged from the Lodge of the Dead. His powers in necromancy were still weak, but he could feel them growing with every new kill. The bitterness and hate within him fuelled his passion. They will pay for what they did, he thought and he rose to his feet and strode off deeper into the Nektoulous Forest.

    Tale by Alhazred

Editorial - Oghma

I logged out last night with a feeling of disquiet.

I've always been a casual role-player. I'm not much for thees and thous, nor am I a big fan of constantly monitoring in and out of character speech. I figure that most folks know which is which, and while I try to keep all my says in character, group talk and tells are often out of character. In that sense, I guess, I'm not much of a role-player.

    Editorial by Oghma
