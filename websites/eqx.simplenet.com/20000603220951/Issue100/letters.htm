After pestering a GM for a while, I have learned a little about the gods of Norrath. It seems they all have descriptions.. Here goes. Fennin Ro is the God of the Plane of Fire, and father to Solusek Ro (Son of Fire). Tarrew Marr, E'Ci, and Povar are the ruling triumvirate of the Plane of Water. (Prexus however, is a minor god of Norrath's oceans) The Rathe are thirteen identical beings that rule the Plane of Earth. The Plane of Sky is ruled by Xegony, a beautiful being with faerie-like wings. The Tribunal, despite what dark elves may think, are the six Hammers, rulers of the Plane of Justice. Vallon and Tallon Zek are indeed the sons of Rallos Zek, ruler of the Plane of Power. Cazic-Thule rules the Plane of Fear, Innoruuk rules the Plane of Hate. Brell Serilis rules the Plane of Underfoot. Rumor says that Bristlebane and Mithaniel Marr were killed in the armageddon of the Age of Beta, by Innoruuk, who himself was recently killed by mortal invaders to his Plane.

~Briza X'Ris, Spurned Cleric of Solusek Ro
House X'Ris, Neriak Third Gate

Shadoe

I just finished reading Oghma's editorial this week and I have to say "Damn! Someone else does that too?!" I play a Dwarven Cleric on Rodcet Nife and I have much the same experience that Oghma does. Maybe it's because we are clerics (the ultimate group class) or perhaps he's right and we're just controll freaks, but discipline in a group makes the game so much more enjoyable.

My problem is that just when the group is starting to get working well I have to log off. I cannot play EQ for long blocks of time like everyone else seems to and I don't have a set large group that I play with. So I have to pick up groups where and when I can. This has gotten me killed more times than I like to think about. But the feeling that you get when the group is working is better than just about anything else. Right after the fight where the puller got more than he bargained for on the run back and everybody was fighting for their lives (had two on me and was popping heals on the tanks at the same time). When everybody looks around, sees most of the group at two bubbles or less, all casters at 20% or less and the massive pile of bodies that need to be looted. The rush that says "We can take ANYTHING!!!" hits you like a ton of bricks and you think "this is why I play". Not the levels. Not the cool graphics. Not the "look what I can do". It's the well oiled team that makes this game worth it.

Just thought I would let ya know that there are others that feel this way.

Stonecrusher
level 20 Cleric of Brell
Rodcet Nife
