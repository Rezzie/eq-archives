Hi there!

Even if you're low on sleep, I think that covered most of the short-sighted complaints I've seen everywhere recently. I think your editorial demonstrates the two broad mindsets that we'll see. Those who can see the potential of so many people coming together, and those who can't see the forest for the trees. I always have trouble understanding those who refuse to consider what a multi-player game can be. Some see other players as little more than intelligent targets...no different from any other monster or NPC in the game.

Then there are those who understand the concept of community. Sure, I'm looking forward to the wonderful world that Verant has built for us. I've spent a great deal of time reading and posting in newsgroups, talking to like-minded players and frankly, I'm mostly looking forward to the interaction. I'll be excited if an when my character reaches 50th level. It's the journey to that point, surrounded by my friends and other inhabitants of Norrath that will make the whole thing worthwhile.

Eric "Mongo" Carlson

[Ed. - So much to address in this letter... I'll skip over the whole thing about how many murders we have in the United States, that's not relevant. Obviously we don't think that we took it too far. We wouldn't have put it up there if we did. EverQuest is a game where the predominant activity is killing things. I certainly understand if parents think that killing is not something that they want their children to read about or see in a game. I'm in no position to tell anyone how to raise their children, having none of my own (it's all this geekiness that drives the chicks wild...).

Look, the whole intent is to portray the evil of the Teir'dal, and the F'leurs DuMal in particular. You point out that EverQuest is a roleplaying game that includes both good and dark elements. The dark elves are the epitome of that dark element. In a way I guess your offense is a bit of praise, for we really did mean for the guild, it's activities and it's history to be very gruesome.

And, well, to be honest, I do think that there are a lot of folks in phase 4 that don't read EQX, but I'm glad to know that you think otherwise. I, however, doubt that many of them are seven years old. I'm not in the habit of apologizing for what we do, especially where I think we have done nothing wrong. And while I am sorry that you were offended, if you sent me back in time to do it over, I'd do that same thing. Of course, I'd also be a bit upset that you sent me to change a web site about a game instead of sending me back to high school so that I could have a second chance with that one lovely girl that dumped me (dang spiteful geekiness!)...

I don't mean to make light of something that seems to have upset one of our readers, but I honestly see nothing wrong with that tale. In fact, it's a great story. It really does portray the evil that is the F'leurs DuMal.

And one more thing that Oghma pointed out. This is a part of the NDA, any phase 4 tester that has played has agreed to it:

1. Accounts are available only to adults or, in their discretion, their minor child. If you are a minor, your parent(s) or guardian(s) must complete the registration process, in which case they will take full responsibility for all obligations under this Agreement. By clicking the "I Accept" button you represent that you are an adult and are either accepting this Agreement on behalf of yourself or your child.

So parents have made a decision to allow their child access to all the killing on EverQuest. We should not be put in the role of the parent.]
