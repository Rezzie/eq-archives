In support of Oxesiess:

One fo the failings of EverQuest, and several RPG's, is the concept of a common language. Granted in our own history, there have been trade languages and Latin was used for centuries by scholars. But to have every single person being able to communicate perfectly is too trite. In "Paper & Pencil" games, I would often confound the players by providing NPC's who did not speak a language they had in common.

So, in Norrath, to expect an uneducated member of an insular race (just ask any Erudite who has traveled to Halas!), especially one who is not to bright, to be able to speak fluent, grammatical Common is unrealistic. Wait, can I use that word in the context of EQ??? Oxesiess dialect of Common even feels right, if you've ever imagined the Barbarians as being almost Scandanavian.

I don't expect people to throw in "thee"s and "thou"s. I would expect the High Elves and Erudites to speak very precise Common, while the Wood Elves a bit less so. Halflings, and this is just my style, speak casual "lazy" Common. Have not given much thought to Gnomes and Dwarves in this context, but realize their homes are far from any human settlement. Where would a young Ogre even learn Common when the guards kill most humans before they get into the city?

To credit Mr Ellis, I agree that Auntie Moog gives better advice.

Also, I was glad to see the link to the previous article and found it a pleasant read. Would be quite amusing to see Oxesiess speaking with someone who had a very different accent/dialect. Imagine the misunderstandings that would arise!

Roger Skelly
