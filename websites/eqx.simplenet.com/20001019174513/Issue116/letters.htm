Letters to the Editors

Onnie's Article in issue 114...

I just want to say.. THANK you! I play on the Tunare Server, and frankly, no one does anything there! Everyone is stuck in their own circles, never hunting with anyone new, or powerlevelling secondaries...

Tunare used to be a great place. Everyone helped everyone else. Now.. most of the good people have left for greener pastures because of the rudeness and apathy there. Those of us who often try to help others usually get a rude gesture or not even a thank you.

There are rarely ever events on Tunare, because no one wants to do the leg work. Take for instance, my ex-guild. They were riddled with problems and internal strife, yet no one cared.. .escpecially the leader. It was simply put on the back burner and forgotten about. They had a couple of allied guilds, but nothing was ever done with them. The officers had their own little circle and everyone else was pretty much left out in the cold unless they were useful to them.

THIS is the mentality of Tunare. Few care about others... I can't count the times I have seen people simply watch while others die..

As for me? I'm just a simple bard with a heart of gold... I try to help when I can.. regardless of the thanks I get or lack thereof.

I wish Tunare was a community instead of a bunch of people fighting for space.

Cygnusia TempestTone
Nomad bard of the Karana Pains
Proud member of the Dynasty of Heroes

Interest article regarding reality. However, that question has been asked since the beginning of philosophy and was perhaps best answered by Descartes. In Meditions, Descartes doubted everything. However, in the end, he found the one truth, the only infallible one. Even in doubting his own existence, he is thinking. Therefore, he stated "I think, I am", which peope has always mistranslated as "Cognito Ergo Sum". That basic truth has no need of a proof. It is a self-evident truth, probably the only one philosophy has found. If this self-evident truth does not sound reasonable to you, then all I can say is, "You do not exist". To me, it sounds very reasonable. Of course, you might say, "Self-evident truth? That's BS!". However, Godel has already proven that there exist statements such that it cannot be proven neither truth or false. And in mathematics, we have "axioms" which are considered self-evident truth with no need for prove. That's what philosophy is all about, searching for that self-evident truth.
