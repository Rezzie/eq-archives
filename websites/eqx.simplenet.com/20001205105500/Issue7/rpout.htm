ROLEPLAYING OUTSIDE THE LINES

Some RPG's, like EQ or AD&D, decide for game balance purposes to limit player endeavor to certain "classes". These classes can become limiting factors in a roleplayer's desire to make a unique character. How many of us have said "I want to play an elf fighter-mage" and proceeded to allow the race/class abilities/limitations decide *who* that character is? We need to be able to think "outside the lines" drawn for us by game designers.

The first step is character concept. Who is this person? Where did they come from? What sort of character "seed" can you build from to create a coherent, believable persona? Some suggestions for character building points are:

Fiction - Have you read any good books lately? :) Believable characters abound in fiction and not only SF/Fantasy. I once used the Victorian "gentleman burglar" Raffles concept to make a noble thief who stole for the "art" of it, only attempting daring, next-to-impossible thefts.

Selfish desire - Is there something you always wished you could have done in real life, but couldn't? Act through your character concept and express yourself! 		Game design - as long as you don't limit yourself to a race/class restriction, you can certainly use the game design. I love playing dwarves, and my concept often starts out with "I wanna be a dwarf".

Power - a much misaligned character "seed". Why not want the best and the most? Make it *interesting* though. The key is to imagine yourself in the characters place, and justify those desires with who the character is and act through the character's persona. For example:

You want a character who is powerful, and can affect the course of nations. Class is immaterial, really, since all classes can become powerful. Take a "fighter" class, make him charismatic and thoughtful, and *poof* you have Alan of Battlewile, whose family was once renowned for their ability in battle and is currently in disrepute. He's the 5th son whose brothers are a bunch of fops. Disgusted by his family's lack of spine, he trains to become a warrior and sets out on his own, using the gold obtained through adventuring to hire people to do his bidding. Investing in merchant ventures, he earns enough money to create a world-wide courier organization, with fees for delivery for magical and mundane courier service. The couriers act as spies, and soon Alan is a powerful information broker. He trains and equips a mercenary army that strikes at *just* the right time to save an nation's fortunes, and is knighted by the king... 		Once you have your character concept, you then need to fit it in the existing race/class system. The character I hope to play, Oghma, is inspired by the Celtic God of Knowledge, Bards and Strength - Oghma. In that concept there are several key elements. The character is a repository of cultural information, and he is physically powerful. Since I like dwarves, and dwarves are strong, I like the idea of a dwarven bard, but bard is not necessarily the correct class. As a character he is a bard only in the "transmission of knowledge" sense. He could also be a fighter, since the bardic song/spell ability really fits peripherally into the concept. As an individual, he would train more in things outside the bardic spell realm if possible. If started as a bard, he would wind up with high smithing and say, minor wrestling skills (the Celtic god was the best wrestler of the gods), with lore and knowledge being his intellectual focus. Spell/song abilities would be secondary. He could also be a Cleric, with a "holy mission". It is even conceiveable that he be an Enchanter, going more towards showing the dwarven magic with armor and weapons... One of the things I like about dwarves is their smithing ability, a creative force much like the desire to create songs and tales. So where does this put me as Oghma?
