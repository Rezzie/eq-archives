ISSUE # 34August 8th - August 14th

ROLE AGAINST ROLL

When I first launched my alter ego into the world of Britannia with the initial release of Ultima Online, I fully expected to encounter an immersive, richly detailed world where I could submerge myself in the personae of a noble warrior seeking the way of the Avatar.

Almost a year later, I realize just how wrong I really was. Ultima Online is the greatest creation that never was, a world of potential and incredible dimension that fails to deliver on so many fronts that the one feeling almost everyone can agree on is disappointment. Whether or not you think the lawsuit against OSI is well founded or a piece of tripe (I happen to fall into the latter category), everyone seems to agree that it simply doesn't measure up to anyone's expectations.

So, why would Everquest be any different?

From the hundreds of pages of dialogue and conversation which has been passed back and forth comparing Ultima Online and the still Beta Everquest, one very simple difference has emerged. And more than anything else, that simple difference may spell defeat for UO, and victory for Everquest. Just what is that difference?

Role versus roll.

Ultima Online, at its core, is nothing more than a munchkins dream, the power gamers masterpiece made reality. The poorly designed combat system makes certain combinations of skills deadly, and others monumentally useless. Loopholes in the game system render the average player powerless against individuals who view UO as nothing more than a top down version of Quake. Worst of all, Ultima Online commits the cardinal sin of the Role Playing Game. It emphasizes statistics over game play, and offers no real rewards to taking on the role you play.

Everquest, on the other hand, offers something that UO lacks. Character. UO is a shell, little more than a graphical chat room which allows combat. The flavour and story of Britannia, so pervasive in the CRPG series, is completely, utterly absent in UO. Everquest's rich background is already evident in the races and classes which they are offering as options to play. Trolls. Dark Elves. Gnomes. All possess their own stories, background, and in many instances, gods. Deities and their worshippers will play a role in player interaction, and not just as idle participants. Every indication points to the Everquest Development team taking the background story and using it to enrich gameplay, and not just use it as window dressing.

Why is there such a profound difference between the two games? Both are backed by large corporations with deep pockets (Electronic Arts is no slouch, and Sony is…well…Sony). Both have talented programmers at their disposal. Why the profound difference in approach to an online RPG?

Attitude.

Raph Koster, lead developer on Ultima Online, has repeatedly stated his beliefs that PK switches are not an option, that players should take the lead in policing the online sociopathic elements if they want to build an online community, and constantly stated that it is not their job to provide full time quests for the players. All of this is true, if you view Ultima Online as a graphics based MUD or MOO, an online social experiment if you will, and little more.

And therein lies the problem. Role Players, dedicated gamers who want to immerse themselves in a rich gaming world, have nothing to look forward to. Player Killing is rampant, and uncontrolled by code based fixes that OSI tries to put in. Harassment is endemic, and persistent. And there is no ongoing storyline which players can feel a part of. No world in which the players can immerse themselves. It has become an overglorified chat room.

Everquest offers an alternative. A chance to play something other than a set of numbers and algorithms. You have the chance to become an Erudite Necromancer, or a nefarious thief looking out after his own back and no one else. It offers options without the burden of necessity. From all indications (slim though they are at this point) your decision to play a warrior will not place you at the complete mercy of other player types for well over a year (do I sound bitter? My warrior has laboured under the UO combat system for over a year, forever inferior). Your decision will not be based on faulty game mechanics or the latest weapon of choice. Your decision will be based on what you WANT to play.

And that, more than anything else, will make Everquest an Online ROLE Playing game.

    Article by Jeff Franzmann (Canticle)
