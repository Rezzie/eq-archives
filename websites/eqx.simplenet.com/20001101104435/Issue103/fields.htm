ISSUE # 103

December 4th - December 10th


The Fields of Karana

By a lonely prison wall, I heard a young girl calling,
"Conner, they have taken you away,
For you stole Sir Lucan's corn,
So our son might see the morn.
Now a prison ship lies waiting in the bay."

Low lie the fields of Karana,
Where once we watched the small free birds fly.
Our love was on the wind.
We had dreams and songs to sing.
Now it's lonely 'round the fields of Karana.

By a lonely prison wall, I heard a young man calling,
"Nothing matters, Sara, but you're free.
Against D'Lere and his crown,
I rebelled, they ran me down.
Now you must raise our son with dignity."

Low lie the fields of Karana,
Where once we watched the small free birds fly,
Our love was on the wind,
We had dreams and songs to sing,
Now it's lonely 'round the fields of Karana.

By a lonely harbor wall, she watched the last star falling,
As the prison ship sailed out against the sky.
Now she waited, hoped and prayed that he might return one day.
Will he walk again in the fields of Karana?

No more, in the fields of Karana,
Where once we watched the small free birds fly.
Our love was on the wind.
We had dreams and songs to sing.
Now it's lonely 'round the fields of Karana.
It's so lonely 'round the fields of Karana.

These song lyrics have been adapted from a traditional Irish folksong, "The Fields of Athenry".

    Song by Ritter (Paladin of Mithaniel Marr on Cazic-Thule)
