EverQuest vs Asheron's Call - Responses

All italicized text is taken from Wynn's article EverQuest vs Asheron's Call'.


From Jason Booth, Lead Technical Artist for Asheron's Call:

Although I normaly don't post or email EQ sites, I feel I must when they are publishing mis-information about Asheron's Call. In your AC vs. EQ article, several things are misrepresented, or completely wrong. 		Graphics section:

While graphics are stricly subjective, please keep in mind that what you are being publicly show of AC is being rendered completely in software. Until EQ releases some software screenshots (which they don't support) or we release some hardware accelorated shows (yes, we are working on it, and it looks amazing), any comparisons are pretty useless. Also keep in note that we are able to stream new environments to the user at run time, and our world is continuous, something you won't find in any other game I know of. There is no "level loading", or "please wait while downloading new areas" in AC. 		Asheron's Call requires manual aiming and manual clicking to attack. You hold down the mouse button and let the power bar reach the max. Meanwhile, you move your cursor over your target, and it has arrows telling you if you are too low, too high, or whatever. When you release the button, you attack. I think this system will have to be scrapped when they realize a little lag will destroy the whole thing. If it is lenient and lets the client report whether it was a hit or miss, they will find Diablo-like cheaters taking over.
