ISSUE # 100

November 13th - November 19th


Elven Beauty


I sit here at the tavern bar,
And pray to Erollisi Marr.
For someone else to be my friend,
Before I age and meet my end.

A lonely life I've always led,
My only friends live in my head.
But now it's tavern dancing night,
And lovely maidens fill my sight.

I see, as I put down my glass,
A lithe, curvaceous elven lass,
A wiggle here, a giggle there,
A golden orchid in her hair.

If only I were not so shy,
A question I would surely try;
I'd ask her now to dance with me,
A perfect partner she would be.

But many suitors gather round,
A lover she has surely found.
My chance is gone - 'twas ever slim -
I'm far too slow and much too dim.

I look upon her radiant face,
And see her dance with so much grace,
She steps so light across the room,
As gentle as a flower's bloom.

But now, what's this? She smiles at me,
No, no, a grimace it must be.
She sees the ugliness I hold,
Upon my face and in my soul.

Alas a monster she doth see,
'Tis true, a beast is what I be,
My mother was a sweet wood elf,
But father; king of trolls himself!

A thief he prowled the dead of night,
He took her then slipped out of sight.
She fought him hard and struggled long,
But at the end he was too strong.

So now I sit here in the shade,
My only friends my beer and blade.
Mixed race, mixed fortune is my life,
As elf, not troll, I'd choose for wife.

But smile it is, I wasn't wrong!
The elven beauty sweeps along,
And settles down right next to me,
The gods at last have heard my plea.

At first I stumble, tripping tongue,
My words all come out strange and wrong,
But soon I calm and find my voice,
And now we're talking. Oh rejoice!

We drink, we sing, we laugh, we talk,
And now amongst the trees we walk.
Her life, as well, has not been good,
Her childhood dark and stained by blood.

So many bonds, so much in kind,
So many common threads we wind.
My thanks to Erollisi Marr,
The Queen of Love you truly are.

    Tale by Elegor Mandreskal of the Soerbaird
