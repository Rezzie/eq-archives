Hile freind it is I Ommamar Satori!

You make some good points as usual, but Verrant didn't copout by saying they lack the resources to police the internet. As desire would be one of the resources and I don't believe Verrant has the desire to take on ebay. Quite simply this is a game to make money. If you spend money to legally shutdown the selling of equipment on ebay. You will also cut of a good portion of you client base.

Do I think Brad Mcquaid invisioned this when he and his crew made the game? No but the simple fact is they could not publish it themselves so enters Sony, so enters big business. I believe this game will not be a "commercial succes" five years from know.

I disagree strongly about selling any virtual equipment on ebay, but some people do. For a good majority of the people this is not a role playing game, they as you stated have turned a hobby into a job.

On your article about Ebay ;)

I'm a long-time player of online games, I even implemented a few. And even back in the good 'ol days of text base MU*'s some offline trading occurred. I have to agree with you that it's reprehensible that Verant/Sony won't come out and flat out say that selling items on Ebay is illegal.

I was a Guide in the EverQuest guide program for a very long time as well, and I can tell you that ALL Guides were incensed the first time we saw Ebay items go up. As a Senior Guide I regularly got emails about this or that item going up for trade on Ebay. Not because I asked them to but because the Guides took it upon themselves to keep an eye on it. Until we were told not to bother by Verant, that is. "Nothing we can do about it, we can't police the internet". But you know what? They could have. With well over 700 guides in the EverQuest guide program it would be very easy to do.

Now, I'm no longer a guide, I resigned over some personal issues as well as some issues with Verant, but mostly I just wanted to have a life again ;) But your article was Right On The Money (ROTM!!!). If Verant asked the players to let them know about Ebay or any other auctions. The players would do it. They'd be GLEEFUL about doing it as a matter of fact. And we'd rapidly see the end of Item farming for Ebay. Heck, we might even be able to use platinum to buy items in game again....

Anyway, kudos on the clear vision you had in that Article. Just wanted to thank you for seeing the forest for the trees.

Kevin
