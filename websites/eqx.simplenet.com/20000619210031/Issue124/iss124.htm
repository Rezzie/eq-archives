It was an early spring morning in Freeport, as the sun pushed its way above the buildings of Freeport to the East. Rudra, blinded by the morning light, stumbled upon the Jade Tavern in North Freeport. The dwarven ale he had been drinking the night before had just started to wear off and his head began to ache. His back twitched and ached, having been tossed out of another tavern near by the night before. He had spent yet another night sleeping on Mother Nature’s soil, something the drunken Gnome was getting used too.

The Train Stops Here

Well, I went and did it. Yup. Count me among the disenfranchised who yearn for a new game to play.

I cancelled my EverQuest Account on Thursday evening.

It’s not to say that it was a bad game. I think my reverence for this groundbreaking game comes through in the article below. But if it had been an exceptional game, I think I’d still be playing. I think my articles show that I haven’t been playing much, as they’ve begun to slant toward general role-playing topics rather than remaining in the realm of Norrath.

    Article by Archanalia

The Player

February 25, 1982 a child was born. This innocent baby feeding from his mother, vomiting on my clothes, and making us laugh and smile. This small child was brought into the world free of hate, opinions, or fear, which looked at the world with incorruptibility from his newborn eyes.

But who was to know that this child had another talent aside from filling diapers with green goo. That as he grew up he would take on another role. My son was a “player”.

Letters to the Editors

Greetings Absor! I just wanted to drop in and give my two cents on the faction adjustments in PvP. I don't know how many people remember what happened when they actually tried this, but the effect was terrible.

Here's an example of what happened to me. I was a ranger in qeynos hills the first day that the faction flags were in place. I was attacked by an erudite wizard who attempted for the typical root/kill method. Luckily he stayed in range of my bow and I was able to fend him off until root broke. A simple ensnare and the wizard soon fell beneath my axe.
