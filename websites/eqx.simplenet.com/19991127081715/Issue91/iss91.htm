A Troll In Kelethin

Part Two

Gorch lumbered through the Faydark, his feet crushing the underbrush on either side of the path, which was made for much smaller feet. Draped over his shoulder was the unconscious Drock, who he held in place with one hand. His mismatched armor shone in the early morning light, and around him birds chirped. Song birds where unheard of in the Innonthule swamps and to finally hear them added a spring to his massive steps. Behind him the trio of elves followed warily. Their feet never left the seldom-used path and they made no noise, marking them as experienced woodsmen. Lillyana watched Gorch carefully. His sudden rescue and willingness to help were extremely out of character for a troll. However, he did seem genuinely concerned about the dwarf he carried. Lillyana chuckled at the thought of what Drock would say when he was told he was carried to Kelethin on the back of a troll. Gorch looked back over his shoulder inquisitively.

    Tale by Ramos


My Take on Exploiters

Every online game that I've played in or worked for has had people who try to cheat, or beat the game. Incidents in Everquest are different in a few ways.

1) EQ is much more complex than an old-style MUD. The more lines of code you have, the more chance that bugs crop up.
2) EQ is much more populated than MUD-style RPGs. The GM-player ratio in Everquest is really poor. It is much easier for people to get away with things in the mass of players. Also, MUD admins were usually friendly with and respected by their players. It is easier to rationalize cheating against a company that you hate than a friend.

    Article by Patwx
