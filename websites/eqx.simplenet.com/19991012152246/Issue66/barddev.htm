ISSUE # 66

March 20th - March 26th


The Bard and the Developer

Grey walls tower'd above her, a sharp cry, a blackened door --
She shivered in the cold wind and then, she spotted Tanidor.
Warmth started to suffuse her, as it spread from limb to limb
And her batting eyelashes flirted, focusing only in on him.
She laid her arm o'er his elbow, resting fingers on his wrist,
He smiled and ponderingly asked, "Should I add a little mist?
Snuggling close along his torso, she followed him inside --
Murm'ring sweet nothings to him 'til the dungeon she spied.
She shrieked, she jumped, clamb'ring to his shoulders above,
As he analyzed the scene and said "More orcs here, my love?"

Slowly dropping to the floor, breathing heavily in fear,
She gathered her determination and nibbled at his ear
Whispering to him oh so softly, "Tanidor, now we have some time."
Then watched him kneeling to the stone asking "Should I add some slime?"
She slipped her fingers into his, skin warm and soft and moist,
But he moved along to set the trap - a heavy falling joist.
She ran her fingers through his hair, and landed a loving peck,
As he stretched his arms across the hall to find "The perfect bottleneck."

And so it went, room after room, her heart was so aroused,
As they stepped into the cavelike hall where seductresses caroused.
Surely, she thought, taking his hand to start a sweet and lovely waltz,
My lips must now attract him more than his list of zonal faults.
The music played, time slipped away, as she was encircled by his arms.
She thought, at last, twas worth the wait, now he'll enjoy my charms.
Alas, alas, it was not to be, as she pressed herself against him,
He twirled her away, turning her face to a dangling bloodsoaked limb.
He smiled, then waltzed her further on, grinning at her delight,
Ah Sweet, please next observe my perfect dolerite.
The rocks, the stones, the swords and shields, the monsters in this zone
Everything now in readiness for rampaging testosterone

At that he smirked, grabbed her arms, and crushed her to his face,
His lips so sweet, his eyes aglow, sweeping her into his embrace.
She swooned and gasped, praying that his tinkering would cease,
As Tanidor then declared, "Just one more ghoul before release."

    Poem by Nepenthia
