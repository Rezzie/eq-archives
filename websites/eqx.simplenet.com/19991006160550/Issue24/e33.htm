ISSUE # 24June 1st - June 5th

EverQuest E3 Report

Once you select your desired class, you get a number of points to beef up your default stats. Stats are typically in the 50 (very low) to 120 (very high) range, and wont be easy to increase. Then you get to chose a deity, which is restricted by your class (and maybe race). Finally, you chose the city you want to call home, depending on your race.

As we've all seen in the screenshots, different characters are different heights. This means any humans or larger wont even see a gnome standing at their feet. I was told that one of the testers likes to play a gnome and you won't even see him, so he has to jump. You'd just see his head pop up and down at the bottom of your screen. 		As a newbie, you will find it difficult to kill anything, unless you have help or are a barbarian warrior. Teaming up from day one will be a necessity. In fact, you may need help just finding your way around the city. Qeynos and Erudin are the only cities in the game so far, so most characters start in Qeynos by default. The city itself consists of multiple zones, so it is fairly large. Another reason to team up is if you die, you will have a hard time finding your corpse again. If you are in a party, you can give your party members permission to pick equipment off your corpse, and then you can meet up to get your stuff back. As a non-PvP, no one else can touch your stuff, so you'll have to find your corpse on your own! Of course, if you are a necromancer, you may be able to utilize a Locate Corpse skill to help your search. As a Newbie

I didn't get much of a chance to wander too far, since as a newbie you wont stray far from town anyway. But in my travels, I saw a beautiful clock tower in Qeynos that emits a reddish glow, which is screenshot material at night. Outside the city, they have populated it with lots of bats, snakes, and an occasional fire beetle. Once, strolling down the road out of Qeynos, I witnessed an epic battle of 4 partied warriors killing a large group of various monsters. This alone wasn't too unusual, but as I gazed upward into the rolling sky, I saw a character standing fifty feet in the air. Whether this was a bug, or a flight or levitation spell I don't know, but it was awe inspiring nonetheless.
