ISSUE # 11Feb. 28th - Mar. 6th

Mirror Lake

It hardly seemed valuable. It was a simple gold band with an opal inlay. But this was its great deception. Its maker had crafted it in simplicity. Because it was nondescript one could heap upon it any history that could be drawn out of the dark recesses of one’s soul. Surely orphaned thieves had seen their parents throats slit in it’s reflection, their murderer’s face being that of whoever possessed the ring. And noble knights had seen the destruction of kingdoms and the rape of maidens by the ring’s current owner. It was a thing of malice, crafted to create discord and anger and ultimately death. And it had succeeded in its purpose once more.

I could not bring myself to pick the thing up. I feared its powers. And so I used the murder weapon, Vagarus’ sword, to scoop up the thing by the chain. And with a swing of the blade I hurled it into the lake. 		No one should ever cast eyes upon it, and beneath the shimmering surface of Mirror Lake, no one ever would. When they glanced into it’s depths they would only see their own face staring back. I would be the last to look down upon it and see death. And any time I looked into the lake and saw my own face I would still see death.

And the beasts carried poor Vagarus away.

Story by Archanalia
