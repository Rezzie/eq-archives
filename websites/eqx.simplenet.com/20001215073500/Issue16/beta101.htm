Beta Testing 101
As the EverQuest beta test gets closer with each passing day (we hope), it is a good time to discuss what to do as a beta tester. By the time a software package has reached beta, it should reliably startup and be executable from beginning to end. It's what happens between there that remains a mystery. With any open ended game such as EverQuest, the possibilities are, for all intents and purposes, infinite.

The job of a beta tester is to make sure that when the game is stamped "complete" that it will be a fun experience for everyone. This means during beta testing we need to find all the potential things that cause the game to not be fun. This includes crashes, lockups, balance issues, and more. 		The typical adventurer will walk down the middle of the roads, open the doors, and talk to their friends. We know that much works. However, given thousands of people all in a free flowing game, you are likely to get someone who is wacked out on drugs doing the stragest things you have ever seen. Odds are someone will do something that has never been tested before, and who knows what the result will be. So, the job of the beta tester is to try everything, including doing things that only the craziest wacko would do.

Here are some example areas that we can focus on.

Be CrAzY. Attack the furniture. Talk to the monsters. Pick up as many little sticks and rocks as you can find and give them to the NPCs. Climb onto the roof of the livery stable. If all you do is play normally, you will miss the potential problem areas. Think of doing something that no else is doing. The more problems we find, the more stable (and thus more fun) the game will be. 		Be stoopid. There are an awful lot of morons out there. But more importantly, there are people who have never seen an RPG. They may randomly create a character. So, try to be that Dark Elf Paladin. Maybe an Ogre Wizard has some advantages. I'm sure there will be a lot of Elfen Rangers and Ogre Warriors.. but try something unique. It will also help determine if the game is well balanced.

Be annoying. Don't break any rules of etiquette as they are already defined. But if it doesn't say not to do it... DO IT. Some little kid is going to do it anyway, so it's better to point out the annoyance factor as soon as possible so it can be prevented. If Bard songs are monotonous, play them everywhere you go. Even if someone wants a quiet conversation. Shout as often as you can. Give worthless items to everyone you see. When they drop them, give them to them again. These are just some examples of what annoying people might do. Things like this need to be discovered so rules or auto-ignore/refuse options can be added.

Article by Wynn Orill.
