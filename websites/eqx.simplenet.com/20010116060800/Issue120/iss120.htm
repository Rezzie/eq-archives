Religion in Norrath: A view from this side of the monitor

Agnostic: a person who holds the view that any ultimate reality (as God) is unknown and prob. unknowable; broadly : one who is not committed to believing in either the existence or the nonexistence of God or a god.

Well, not really. At least not in Norrath. But then Merriam-Webster doesn’t do a Norrathian dictionary.

In Norrath an agnostic can just say “I’m not sure that there’s a god.” That would not make him agnostic, it would make him insane. The gods exist. The people of Norrath do not truly have ‘faith’ in the traditional sense. Their gods manifest, they are proven. Heck, they get killed the second they appear…
    Article by Absor

Scorpios : Dark Knight of Bertoxxulous

Dane Galliway headed through the streets of Qeynos, his head down, a large bundle clutched to his chest. He was headed to the Hall of Steel, the home of the Steel Warriors guild, within the Grounds of Fate. Brin Stolunger had ordered the bundle of supplies he carried, and as was his job for the past three years, he was to deliver it. His parents had died several years ago, and he was left in the care of his uncle, Sneed Galliway, and his wife, Tanlyn. His uncle was not a cruel man, but found the lure of a coin too hard to resist.

Record of Lodoss War

I have been an anime fan for as long as I can remember. For those of you who don’t know, anime is the Japanese word for “Animation”. So, therefore, anime is classified as a cartoon made in Japan. If you are already familiar with the genre, you can skip the rest of this paragraph. Anime is very different from American cartoons in many ways. It covers a wide range of subjects, from everyday teenage troubles, to saving the world from utter annihilation. Unlike American toons, anime isn’t just for kids, as it caters to all age groups.

Editorial - Absor

Now things seem to be moving forward!

Verant has recently done a couple of things that I like.

First, I love the idea of religion based items. Great work. What Verant plans to do is introduce new items (without touching the old items) that can only be used by characters that worship a particular god. So soon we might see a Flaming Crown of Solusek Ro that can only be worn by worshipers of that god. This is a wonderful addition to the game.

    Editorial by Absor
