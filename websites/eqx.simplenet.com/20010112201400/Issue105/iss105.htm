The Making of a Young Paladin

I can still see the look on my father's face when I told him where I'd been. The dried blood on my shirt and the dirt on my face were enough to convince my parents that their worst fears had been realized.

"You've made your choice," was all my father said upon hearing my story. "Don't expect us to like it." They turned away from me then, and I can still clearly see them walk away... My father's arm wrapped around my mother's shoulders, supporting her. That was the day of my first real battle, outside the practice room in the Defender's Hall. That was also the day I left home, and the last time I saw my parents.

Gorliss

Gorliss peeked around the massive cedar serving as his lookout post. Thirty yards away, High Elves had established a military field camp. The camp had become busy very quickly. Elven heavy infantry, mobile archer troops, and pikemen were assembling in the camp's central square, their military precision and orderly movement a thing of beauty and renown.

Orc rivers had been aggressively raiding the Greater Feydark, pillaging Wood Elf, High Elf, and human villages alike. The High Elves, a stoic and aloof race, normally left the safety of the Faydark, a massive, heavily forested area encircled by young, sharp mountains, to their wilder cousins the Wood Elves. The Wood Elves were savagely effective in their efforts against the orcs...
