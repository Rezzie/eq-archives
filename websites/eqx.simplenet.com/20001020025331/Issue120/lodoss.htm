ISSUE # 120

 April 15th - April 21st, 2000


Record of Lodoss War

I have been an anime fan for as long as I can remember. For those of you who don’t know, anime is the Japanese word for “Animation”. So, therefore, anime is classified as a cartoon made in Japan. If you are already familiar with the genre, you can skip the rest of this paragraph. Anime is very different from American cartoons in many ways. It covers a wide range of subjects, from everyday teenage troubles, to saving the world from utter annihilation. Unlike American toons, anime isn’t just for kids, as it caters to all age groups. There are children’s shows (Doraemon), and then there are more adult oriented anime (Akira). Because of the wide range, you can easily find an anime that fits what you like. There are comedies, sci-fi series, fantasy, action, historical, and most are a combination of these (For example, Rurouni Kenshin is an action/comedy/historical anime). Anime is very versatile in that even the comedies have some tearjerker scenes in them, while the action shows have spurts of comic relief. Most anime springs from manga (Japanese comics) but every once and a while, an anime series will inspire a manga series that goes with it, and sometimes takes a whole different storyline.

What I want to talk about is a particular anime, Record of Lodoss War, which is one of my all time favorites. I didn’t see the connection between it and EverQuest until I introduced it to my brother a while ago and remarked, “It’s like EverQuest; you’ll love it.” Now for the story: On the accursed island of Lodoss, which was split from the continent in a battle between two goddesses, monsters have begun to grow in number and ravage the countryside. To solve this problem, six adventurers gathered together.

Parn: The young warrior, Ghim: The old dwarven warrior, Slayn: the wise mage, Etoh: The priest, Woodchuck: The thief, and (last but not least) Deedlit: the ultra-kawaii (cute) High Elf. Sound familiar yet? Looks like they have a pretty well balanced group there. Well, they need a bard. The closest thing is when Deedlit plays a lute in one episode, so that’ll have to do ^_^

Actually based on an AD&D spin-off, Record of Lodoss War is an EverQuest fan’s dream-come-true. It consists of thirteen episodes that revolve around the six heroes and their quest to save Lodoss from the various forces of evil (Recently I discovered that a spin-off TV series was made). These include an evil witch, an ambitious king set on conquering the island, a couple of dragons, and the goddess of destruction herself. With a deep plot and excellent characters, Record of Lodoss War pulls you right in. I’ll use the first episode to explain the series because episode 1 is a prologue to the rest of it. Sort of like a commercial for the series. It starts by introducing the heroes as they enter a dwarven tunnel. Soon, the stone gargoyles around them spring to life, and Slayn uses his magic to hold them off while the others run further into the cavern. Something powerful is trying to stop them. While traveling through the tunnel, Parn and Deedlit get separated from the rest of the group. Just when they are about to reach the end of the tunnel, they awaken the sleeping dragon, and the real fight begins. The “Prologue to the Legend” does an excellent job of setting the scene, introducing the characters, and pulling you into world of Lodoss. But this one episode doesn’t do the whole series justice, which is filled with action and adventure. I especially loved the Dark Elves, and while only one had a pivotal role in the series, they add a sense of intrigue to the whole plot. You can see the years of pent up prejudice and hatred as Pirotess (The sexy dark elf) and Deedlit fight. Some other memorable scenes are the ballroom dance where Deedlit seems to be dragging a bored Parn around the room, or the inevitable battle between King Fahn and Emperor Beld for control of Lodoss.

This next part is somewhat of a warning. The series is very different from the cutesy picture I included with this article. Record of Lodoss War would get a PG-13 rating for violence, language, and partial nudity (in the end). None of these detract from the storyline or the overall experience. Record of Lodoss War is an excellent story of courage, heroes, and love. I recommend it to anybody who loves EverQuest, anime, a sirgood fantasy series, or just wants to dabble into something different. Record of Lodoss War can be found at most video stores that carry anime. I recommend the subtitled versions to hear the original voice acting, but the dubbed version may be easier for beginners. I give it a 9¾ out of 10. Only because Deedlit likes Parn and not me >;)
