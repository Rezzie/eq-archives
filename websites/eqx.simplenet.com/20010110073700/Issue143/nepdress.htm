ISSUE # 143

 September 30th - October 6th, 2000


All for the Lack of a Dress!

The trolls are growing wings and the pixies hairy toes,
Firiona is chasing Fippy, and in Qeynos anything goes.
The world is topsy-turvy, and an ever growing mess
And so you want to know the reason? The lack of a dress!

The dragons are crooning lovesongs and inviting you to tea,
The skeletons are growing skin these days, just like you and me
The sea is full of cornstalks and halflings all confess
That the world is going crazy because Nep wants a dress

The sun pierces Surefall’s shadows, waterfalls are flowing in Sky.
Now Guk is full of flowers and people are wondering why.
It’s such a simple answer, I’m sure that you can guess,
The world needs a little fashion and Nep, of course, needs a dress.

The Frontier Mountains tomorrow will open a shopping mall
and Dreadlands will become a palace for those going to the ball
Squire Wembley will plunge into lava (an act that some will bless)
And all this is so needless, if Nep only had her dress.

Her quest is long and perilous, the trail goes high and low
She searches every land and town and talks with every foe
She doesn’t want the moon I’ve heard, and planes gear even less
All darling Nep is asking for is just one lovely dress.

Now should you wonder why a dress for this half elven lass,
Simply walk a ways behind her and notice her cold bare ass.
Should anyone be so embarrassed or under such duress?
Think how happily she would sing if only she had her dress.

Elven maids can take the stage in purple, or blue or white
Human bards can choose attire but Nep faces fashion blight
So to your desks and pens and scrolls, let’s ink the letterpress!
Bombard the fashion mavens, let’s all get Nep a dress.

For now she must be content to walk in warrior’s grimy gear
And depend on mail or leather or patches to cover her rear
She isn’t going a begging though, or asking for largess,
A simple gown, a flowing skirt would indeed make her dress

Should the question then arise, what if Nep’s quest would fail?
Will she wallow, will she whine? Will she weep, or scream or wail?
Or will she stand upon the stage, singing under a lover’s moon
and vow that if she can’t have a dress, she’ll marry Aradune?

    Poem by Nepenthia
