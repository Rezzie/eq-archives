EverQuest Express Submission Guidelines:

EverQuest Express is always willing to read submissions for publication. Some of our best stuff comes from readers. But due to the fact that our editor (me) does not get paid to do this, and due to the fact that I have other things in my life besides EQX (I didn't say a LOT of things!), I would appreciate it if all articles sent in for consideration were sent with the following things in mind:


Submitting Written Work:

  1. I use Microsoft Word 97. I can import nearly anything, but Word files are the easiest. If you are real ambitious you can insert HTML codes in the appropriate places, but it's probably better if you don't since I'll likely change it to match the style of EQX anyhow.
  2. Write your article with a word processor, not directly into your e-mail software (unless it spell checks). I really don't have the time to fix spelling errors. I will, if the work is good enough otherwise. But if there are too many things to fix, I won't make the effort.
  3. Read what you've written. Grammatical errors will be treated just like spelling errors. I'll fix some if the article is good enough, but not many. Obviously this excludes intentional misspellings and grammatical errors included for characterization or to prove a point.
  4. Points 2 and 3 are given with the clear understanding that not all of our readers speak English as their primary language. Unfortunately EverQuest Express is targeted to an English speaking audience. So if you have trouble writing in English you may want to find someone to help you out before submitting your work to us (and I'm not saying that our staff won't help as well, but I certainly can't promise that we will either). We are not trying to be mean spirited or exclusive. Our editor only speaks one language, so blame it on him.
  5. I will dismiss without a second thought any submission that I find in poor taste. EQX will not be printing anything that is exceptionally vulgar or abusing. I have no problem with the occasional curse word.
  6. I try to answer all e-mails right after reading them. I will try to at least acknowledge that I have received your submission. If you don't get a response from me within a day or two, feel free to send a short e-mail asking me what I thought of your work. But under no circumstances will I be interested in your work if you pester me about it. And please don't pester the EQX staff either. Remember, we do this for fun, not work.
  7. We prefer all written work be originally created for EQX. We are not very interested in repeating work that has been seen on the net before. If it has been, please let us know where. We will not immediately refuse to print such work, but it will be considered less interesting to our readers if there is a good chance that they have read it before.
  8. EverQuest Express reserves the right to hold publication of a submission until it is needed. In practical terms this means that sometimes we have enough submissions in a week to do two issues, and sometimes we have barely enough to make one whole issue. For this reason, we will sometimes hold on to an article or story which is not on a time-sensitive topic for a little while in case we are left high and dry for content some week. If you are concerned about the disposition of something which you sent us, please send us an e-mail and we'll be happy to let you know if this is the case. Please don't go tell friends that we are going to publish something in the next issue, because you will be embarrassed when we don't.
  9. We strive to publish the best fiction and content we have available. With this goal in mind, we have, over time, developed rule-of thumb issues that will result in almost immediate rejection:
    a) Articles and stories which are less than a page long are usually under-developed and/or not thought out, so we will not publish them.
    b) Character biographies are very rarely published here, unless there is a story beyond the genesis of the character. Many character biographies are simply coming of age/getting training/killing first monster/meeting first friend, which are not enough to maintain interest.
    c) Anything that ends with "To be continued" will never get the chance to be initiated. Our early issues are full of unfinished stories. If your submission has multiple parts, finish it before you submit it.
    d) Although this is a re-statement of 2. and 3. above , it cannot be firmly stated enough. Anything that our editors find needs grammar/spelling corrections will likely not get published. If a work shows a high degree of promise we will suggest the author re-submit it after re-editing, but this is fairly rare. Please take the time and effort to proof-read your work, or have someone else look at it.

Submitting Artwork:

  1. We use a column format for EQX. We expect folks to be reading it at 1024 x 768, on a 15' monitor. All things considered each of the three columns turns out to be less than 300 pixels wide. Please keep this in mind when you send in your work. Some art does not shrink well.
  2. If you are sending in line art we will usually attempt to convert it to a transparent .gif file, thus making it appear to be drawn directly onto the background. If you scan such art it will usually appear with a very gray background. When we clean that up it sometimes changes the appearance of the art. If possible, send this kind of work with a single background color. If you scan it, please try to clean it yourself. This will give you greater control over its final appearance on EQX.
  3. Everything submitted to EQX is considered property of the artist. We will make no claim on it except as it appears on EQX.
  4. We prefer all artwork be originally created for EQX. We are not very interested in repeating work that has been seen on the net before. If it has been, please let us know where. We will not immediately refuse to publish such work, but it will be considered less interesting to our readers if there is a good chance that they have seen it before.

Becoming an EverQuest Express Staff Member:

At this time EQX is not really looking for new staff members. That does not mean that we won't take them anyway. But there are some things to consider before offering to join EQX.

  1. Being on the staff of EQX is a lot of work. It's not easy writing about EverQuest every week. It takes time to think up interesting articles or stories, and even more time to write them. EQX will not keep staff members that do not produce work. It's harsh, but it's also what makes the whole thing work. It's not fair to those that write each week when others don't write at all. If you can't produce work with some consistency then just contribute when you can, but don't ask to be on the staff.
  2. Anyone that feels that they can write well enough to do the job should prove it. We want to see several pieces of work from anyone that wants to join the staff. Send in submissions. Keep doing it. If we like your work and you seem to be able to produce with some frequency (really, that's a LOT harder than you might think it is), then we may be interested.
  3. Ask. If you are interested in joining EQX, ask the editor. It can't hurt, and it won't bother him. But if you do, make sure that you've read the two points above, and that the editor has already published at least two of your works.
  4. Remember, every single person currently on the EQX staff started as a contributor.
Thanks for you interest in EverQuest Express.
