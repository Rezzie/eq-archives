Separatism - Page 2


Now, I am not usually one who believes in any version of the "slippery slope" argument. Just because one thing happens it is not always the case that it will lead to other, more extreme things. But I also believe in prevention. So I will tell you what I see as a worst case scenario. Picture this situation exacerbated by the introduction of the inevitable random player character killers. A further separation occurs between the switches. Soon there is little interaction between the two. After a while those that are UPI switched start to feel like they are on UO. Most of the role-playing types that are UPI start over as non-UPI characters or just quit altogether. Due to the proliferation of random character killers in the UPI realm, non-UPI characters say a few I-told-you-sos          and          start         to
completely avoid all UPI characters. Soon we have a Norrath full of non-UPI characters chatting in olde English filling the taverns, and a mad genocidal killing fest in the woods amongst the UPI characters.

All right, that won't happen. But I sense that we all feel a solid separation between the two types of characters. More strongly than we do between a necromancer and a healer, an ogre and a dwarf, or even male and female. So what the heck is my point? Well, I might have some game suggestions for the designers to reduce this problem, but that is a different article. Mostly I just want to encourage all of us to ignore the switch once we are actually playing the game. If your character would heal that warrior, heal him without considering your switch implications.

If your character would attack that thief, do it, don't look at his switch. Let's find some reason other than the switch to distrust our neighbors.
