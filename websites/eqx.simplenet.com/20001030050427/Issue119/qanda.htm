I went through some posts on various message boards and I started a database with all names and stories mentioned but I need more! However, as he occupies that strange position known as "editor" (along with Absor), he might do something totally unexpected or even strange to this column in response to such a suggestion. I think I’ll just offer some possibilities for you to research the history and mythology of Norrath on your own. The best starting place, of course, is on the EQ website (www.everquest.com will get you there), where you can study the character profiles of the deities and read the historical outline given there. After that, you might branch out and search more EQ fan sites for information, but I was surprised to find (when I went to check this out) that I couldn’t quickly find a site devoted to history and religion/mythology that had anything in addition to the material offered on the official site (which by the way appears to need some updating and filling in.) I think you’ll find that whoever said there wasn’t much information in the game, is quite wrong. If you ever come across my old friend, the bard Gellor, still hanging about the musty libraries looking for the lore of Norrath, tell him Nep sent you and see if he’ll give you a friendly tip or two. I do sincerely wish you sweet fortune in your work as it seems to me we’d all like to know more about the ancient times of Norrath.

Q: Hail Nep. It's Mithycan Wolfcaller again with another little tidbit for you. You may not wish to print this however as it is a PC answer. This is an added helpful item for Elexwiz in issue #118. While all character information is held on the EQ servers, other things are not. For instance, your friends list, what hotkeys you have set and all your socials buttons are kept on your local PC in the Everquest directory (Default: c:\program files\everquest). In this directory you will find various OPT extension files with you character name (8 characters only). These are just text files and can be opened with Notepad or Edit. DO NOT EDIT these files however, as they are very touchy. The bottom line is that they are small and even 8 characters on all the servers would fit on one floppy disk. For those who are moving to another PC, playing out of town or upgrading to a new machine, it's good to copy these off and copy them back AFTER installing EQ. I have personally added them to my backup so that (heaven forbid) my PC crashes, I can come back to playing without having to redo all my buttons. One other tidbit. If you run a character on more than one server, with the same name, i.e., Mithycan on Rallos and Vallos, you will find that Mithycan has the last hotkeys you set up. The OPT files are character specific and not server specific. If you do not wish to print this could you please get it to Elexwiz? Or maybe use it for others with the same questions?
Thanks, Mithycan Wolfcaller - Knights of the Bardic Order Rallos Zek

A: Thanks very much for writing in and sharing such helpful information. It’s also good to be aware that, very rarely, a file with an .opt extension can have a problem and if you need to delete it, when you log in to EQ, the EQ computers will patch it for you. If this happens, however, you’ll have to redo your friends list and socials and hot buttons unless you have a backup of them on a disk as Mithycan suggests.

Q: What would be one of the best video cards to use with EQ. I have a V770 ( not the Ultra ) and have experienced a bad chop when entering long hallways. I have a p2450 w/ 196mb ram
Darwind Healem

A: Sadly enough, I don’t do tech! Well, anyway, if I tried to guess an answer to your question, you’d quite likely blow up your machine and not be able to play EQ at all. However, what I recommend are these two avenues: The first possibility is to go to the official EQ website and check out all the information on the video cards that are supported by EQ, then under "Contact Us" you’ll find a link to Everquest Video Technical Support. I’d start there and give them your system specs and ask if it is the card causing the problem and if there is anything you can do short of buying a new one. If you have your heart set on getting a new card, however, the second possibility would be to try posting on the EQ main website message boards under the technical section or choose your favorite EQ news site and try posting on the boards they might have for technical questions. My guess is you will get more answers and suggestions than you ever imagined possible. Good luck!
