PvP servers take the PvE aspect of the game to next level by adding a 2nd element of danger. Not just Mobs can potentially kill you in the contested zones, but now you must fearful of the Horde movements in the area as well, and vice versa. Also it is quite common especially around Instance entrances (outside of Battlegrounds) to get caught up in sweet group PvP. My groups have come across horde groups so many times when preparing to do an Instance and even Raids (UBRS, Scholo, ect...)

Also, don't forget where there is open PvP in these zones, the griefers will roam as well. Some people get their enjoyment from the game by putting in the effort to camp people, allot of the times, into really long timers (had a guildie camped to like 4 minute timer before his next res or something to that affect.). It can become frustrating too when your quest givers are killed by a 60 Rogue who is bored. Places like Aastrannar and Splintertree Outpost are constantly harassed by level 55+ Characters of their respective factions.

Words of wisdom, if you are ganked, do not post on the forums about said event. Not only will you get no sympathy (it is a PvP server after all), but some people will make it a point to gank you even more. Just bear through the hardships of a PvP server at the lower levels, and give them hell when you can at the end-gaming levels. Reaching 60 gives you a feeling of great accomplishment on a PvP server because no only did you overcome the odds and fought your way through the Environment, but now people are wearier of striking at you wherever you roam without help. Although some people still like to try.

Cenaurius
09-22-2005, 02:55 PM
you had better expect to be be camped and have enemies make you die to mobs instead of them so you receive armor damage, it simply comes with the territory.

contested zones are the zones you progress into around your high teens. in these zones, you can be killed by an enemy player at any time. by contrast, in a friendly zone, you cannot be attacked by an enemy, not even a 60, first. you have to attack them for them to be able to attack you because you are not "flagged" for combat. if you have recently entered a friendly zone after being in a contested one, you will be flagged for combat, but you'll pick up these subtleties as you go along.

basically, you need to understand that if you are seen by an enemy player, they will attempt to kill you if they have an opportunity for no other reason than because they can.
