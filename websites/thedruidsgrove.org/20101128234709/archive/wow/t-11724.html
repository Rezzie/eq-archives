What you wrote is mostly correct (but I needed to read it three times to understand what you wanted to say).

The dodge percentage in the Defenses tab is where the value that you effectively have is displayed.

Dodge rating is no independent value your character has. It is just a value on items based on which the +dodge percentage of this item is calculated (based on your level).

Whether agility or dodge rating is cheaper item-budget-wise I could not say. Looking at the information wowwiki (http://www.wowwiki.com/Formulas:Item_Values) gives it seems both are worth the same.

Do keep in mind that dodge is spiky damage reduction, while AC and stamina are consistent buffers. This is more of a consideration for your healeers than anything else. Basically, while your damage per hour taken should be relatively predictable, as you increase dodge %, you become more susceptible to spike damage, as you avoid several hits in a row and then take several hits in a row. Now, this damage is no greater or less than one would imagine for a person with less dodge. However, the healers begin to heal reactively, not proactively, occasionally letting the tank slip into a health deficit. Hence I would attempt to balance AC, Stam and Dodge percentage in order to obtain the maximal damage reduction on a consistent basis.

Yes, I realize this is slightly off topic, but I feel it's important to remember that dodge is not the be all end all of druid tanking.
