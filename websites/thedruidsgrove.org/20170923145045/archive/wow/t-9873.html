While your arguement is interesting, I find it mostly esoteric as it ignores several points:

1. All items in the game have certain stat point allocation (remember the defense nerf circa 1.7 or 1.8?), and selecting an item solely on the basis of it's Armor means one is likely sacrificing stamina, resistances, or other stats.

2. Few "boss" mobs rely solely on physical damage. Armor's mitigation affects only physical and not spell-based damage.

3. +Defense items have significant, proven benefit (speaking PvE). The most important are miss increase, crit reduction, and crushing blow reduction. Armor, by itself, does little to reduce spike damage. Against bosses, mobs that mortal strike, or simply melee-heavy elites, avoiding spike damage from back to back crits is preferable, in my mind, to reducing the number of times healers drink from every 3rd pull to every 4th.

4. Overheals - even with mana conservation tools like those in CTRA, some % of heals in a fight are extremely likely to be overheals. When you are dealing with damage mitigation of such small amounts as 10% per hit, it's possible that an average group/healer wouldn't ever see the benefit, as it is absorbed through overheals. I speak as someome who primarily uses HT Rank 6 in MC.

In conclusion, ignoring key stats important to tanking (%hit, %crit, stamina, defense, and dodge amongst them), is foolhardy. The only reason to strive to achieve 10K armor is simply the end itself.
