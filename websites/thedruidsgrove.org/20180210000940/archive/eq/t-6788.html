Oh yes, I know, there are those among you who think POP is horrid. There are also a number who think its actually fun.

Recently I have had the opportunity to get involved in some of the POP content other than the usual experience grind and I have found out why people say it is fun.

If you are, like I was, stuck with sitting at camps trying to get experience, the expansion is no different than any other. However, if you get the chance to do some of the interesting events spread all over the expansion, you find that it is really different than anything you've done up to that point.

Until POP, encounters consisted of gathering a bunch of people together and killing a mob. The bosses didn't vary much, it was basically a healing rotation on the main tank and everyone else beats the mob till it dies.

Try out some of the POP encounters, though. For example, after a bunch of our people left from a Bastion of Thunder tower raid, we ran over just to try out the Brother Mujaki event for the first time. Yes, there is a boss, yes the basic idea is to beat it down. However, you have to actually have everyone involved. You start out removing some horses, then the important thing is to split yourselves up and guard some graves that Mujaki's pals will be exiting from. Do a good job here and the end will be easier. This is different from just clearing yard trash to the boss, then beating it up.

Other events are the Thelin's Nightmare maze, the justice trials, the encounter with Terris Thule. Saryrn's encounter is extremely long but that is the single most example of the exact opposite of any raid one has done up to that point. The Zek boys are another example of having to do it different.

The point is, with POP encounters there is variety and variety is wat makes it fun. More than just the MT and cleric crew are important. More than they were important before, but now more people are directly involved.

So I see now, why people said it was fun. The only unfortunate thing about that fun is it is limited to only a few who get the opportunity to experience it.

I've been thinking about this a lot lately, without realizing it over the last six months i've gone from absolutely despising PoP to considering it the best expansion the game has ever seen. Perhaps it's in part to how disappointing LOY, LDoN and thus far GoD have been (all feel like "half-expansions") or perhaps it's partly joining a guild that's progressing thru the flag mobs, but since the flag changes for XP purposes even casual guilds/gamers get a lot out of PoP.

The variety of targets above has been mentioned a few times, it's amazing that on a server with five guilds in Time now with two others only (only, heh) missing Corinav and TRC there is still a lot of content to go around. We can backflag and find targets, we can hit mold droppers in the eplanes, catch the occasional FRo to farm or invest a little time working on our TRC strat. There's a LOT to do in this expansion, even on an overcrowded server like Karana.

For those not raiding, since the flag change there are a lot of excellent places to XP and lots of one groupable mobs/quests. Zones like HoH stand out as exceptional XP spots, really aside from soloing in PoFire or grouping in PoEarth, HoH basement camps are arguably the best XP around. Compared to Velious era gear camps, the stuff acquirable by casual players is astoundingly good, even Ornate and tradeskilled gear is quite good pre-elemental. Best of all, open raiding groups have been springing up across all servers and have been quite successful. Many of them are in the eplanes, and I hear one has even made it into time. The open raiders on Karana have been lagging behind a little but are working hard on RZek (four nights in a row now with no competition for the spawn) which will put about thirty or so of them into the 3planes.

As a tradeskiller PoP has also been an unbelievably good expansion. A wide variety of very good tradeskill pieces can be created. Everything from Hero Sandwiches and pottery made focus items to eplanes skilled armor pieces, items to fit every budget and level of access to zones. Pretty much every trade (with the possible exception of Brewing) came out a big winner. It'd be tough to go back to a world without Ceramic Totems of Xegony and Steel Rings of Earthen Resilience.

It took quite a while to work out the kinks, but I think PoP has pretty much become the perfect expansion. Tons of places to solo and group for XP from tier one thru the eplanes, loads of raid targets for guilds of all shapes and sizes, and dare I say it, the flag system.

As much as I admit I loathed it with an unholy passion during the early days (and it still burns me that I have to 15% into raids til I can get the Vallon Zek script to stop bugging out and finish all my flags *grin*) there are few feelings as good in EQ as getting another flag over with. I almost feel a tiny twinge of sadness that i'm one flag away from full eplanes, as few things manage to be quite as exciting as a shot at that one key flag you need to progress to another zone.

Bleh, long post, ending it here half finished. :P Will probably add more to the thread later...
