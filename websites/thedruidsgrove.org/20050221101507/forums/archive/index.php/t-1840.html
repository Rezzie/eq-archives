Here's the generic breakdown:
Gamecube: "Family" targeted
XBox: "Mature" targeted
PS2: "Everyone and their dog gets a license to publish games"

The XBox has the current "I'm better than you" rank for graphics, none of the other machines can come close to the awesome potential of the XBox's graphic engine. Expect this to change in about 2 years as the next-next gen consoles hit the market. Just don't expect your console to be a lasting thing, most have a "shelf-life" of about 4 years before you have to upgrade, much like computers.

The PS2 is the all out champion when it comes to number of titles and diversity of titles. Sony is very lax on their requirements on dev companies, their licensing fees are small and that encourages software companies to make games for them because they get to keep a larger chunk of the cash. However, their graphics are getting pretty dated. When you compared a "PS2-port-to-XBox" game on the XBox, you could always tell the graphics were dumbed down for the PS2.

The Gamecube seems to have won the "I have young kids" slot. My friends who have all three consoles tell me that the Gamecube has the prettiest "just for fun" based games. You won't find much blood on this console, Nintendo has taken the path straight to winning the family market, and they're doing it with great multi-player games which don't require a large time investment. In other words, you can pop in games and play just to play, rather than spending hours on back-story.

Here's a generic genre breakdown (from best to worst):
Fighting Games: PS2/XBox/Gamecube
Sports Games: PS2-XBox(tie)/Gamecube
Family Games: Gamecube/PS2/XBox
RPGs: PS2, both Gamecube and XBox far behind
Simulators: XBox/PS2/Gamecube
Action/Sidescrolling: XBox/PS2/Gamecube
First-person shooters: XBox/PS2, Gamecube far behind, but has Metroid on the horizon
Racing: PS2-XBox tie, Gamecube far behind (PS2 has the popular titles, but XBox blows away the graphics)
WTH? (Games that don't belong anywhere else): PS2/XBox/Gamecube

The real answer is that there's no clear cut winner. The PS2 is your safest bet just due to the sheer number of titles they have out, the PS2's bound to have at least one game out that people will like. The XBox and Gamecube are a little more constrained - they both have better graphic abilities but have a much narrower selection of games. Is broadband/internet play going to be important for you? If so, the XBox: Live plug in has been the most impressive so far, in my opinion. However, it does require that you have an active broadband connection (no dial up support), so you'll need to have cable or DSL already in the house.

The best bet is to go to a toy/electronics store, grab a $200-300 gift certificate and let hubby pick one on his own. That way he has no one to blame but himself.

I find that gaming consoles are a very personal choice. People are usually very brand-loyal.

Good Points:

X-BOX: Harddrive for saves (nice, no memory card), a very few nice exculsive games (Halo, Steel Batallion), Built-in ability to LAN x-boxs together.

PS2: Plays all origional Playstation games and PS2 games ( hundreds of games), Some very nice exclusive games (Tekken, GTA, Final Fantasy, Metal Gear, Gran Turismo...), The ability to play DVDs right out of the box.

Nintendo Game-Cube: This has benefits, but I dont have one so its hard for me to comment on.

Bad Points:

X-Box: Very limited software collection right now.
PS2: Lesser graphics and sound compared to the other systems. (not bad, but not the best).
Gamecube: Software seems (mostly) focused on a younger audience (15 years old and younger).

I would let your husband have a say in the decision. Let him go out and try them and pick one he likes.
