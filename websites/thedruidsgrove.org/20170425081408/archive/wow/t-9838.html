Tier 1 - Feral is your focus. Bear+bash is too good to not take Furor.

Tier 2 - You must take Improved Healing Touch

Tier 3 - Most go for Nature's Focus. Some will split points between Reflection, Insect Swarm, Subtlety, and Improved Enrage as well.

Tier 4 - You must take Tranquil Spirit. If you're using rejuv all the time to spot heal you are a grossly inefficient healer.

Tier 5 - Nature's Swiftness is too good to pass up.

You raid healing could should include reflection, insect swarm, and Imp. MotW, sacrificing Nature's Focus and Furor respectively. That's your feral/resto tradeoff.

I highly recommend +healing gear and +mana/5 gear. Wear it even if it's cloth. Spam low-rank HT.

BRD/LBRS/Scholo/Strath/UBRS raids will accept you. Ony/MC/BWL/ZG raids are much more picky.

01-04-2006, 02:50 PM
Reflection doesn't really payoff until your the encounters are very long. HT should be your primary healing spell on raids. Tranquility gives you 10% mana efficiency and that is is huge.

There are four core healing talents for long encounters: Improved HT (increases your output by about 14%), Tranquil Spirit (decreases mana cost by 10%), Gift of Nature (increases healing by 10%), and Reflection (increases regen by a small amount). You can get 3 with a 21 point build.

Full restoration builds get the extra 10% from GoN. Gear being equal you'll outlast full restoration builds with a 0/30/21. They have 10% from GoN. You have 20% more mana from HotW.

Innervate, even if assigned to a priest, increases the value of resto builds beyond that of a 0/30/21 in a healing role. If there's little risk (no AoE) and you're out of mana tricks, get in cat and dps while you regen. Bear while not tanking will is dangerous. The extra AC won't help much and an accidental growl often means death.
