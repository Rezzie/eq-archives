You wouldn't even need to parse a DS, just parse mob attack rates.

Simple models:
40 point DS
Mob double attacks 80% of the time. He bash/kick rate is 30% as often as he gets attack rounds. Attack delay is 3 seconds. He hits 80% of his swings.
That's 2.1 * .8 / 3 hits per second, or 22.4 DPS.

Mob quads 80% and doubles 80%. His bash/kick rate is 30% of his attack rate. Attacks every 3 seconds, and hits 90% of his swings.
That's ((1.8)*(1.8) + .3) * .9 / 2 hits per second, or 1.6 hits/second. A 40 point DS then does 63.7 DPS.

If the mob is slowed 50%, your damage shield DPS is halved. If the mob is slowed 75%, your damage siheld DPS is quartered. Using half speed for a mob with slow mitigation is reasonable.

If you want to parse how much DPS your DS would do in a situation, just parse the number of hits by NPCs on the PCs over time, and multiply by DS.
