While the DoN cultural is certainly nice, you really need to evaluate it on a piece by piece basis.

Many of the augs have extras like regen, DS, and +ATK, which frankly are pretty much useless to a casual druid (I'm talking Tunare here, no idea about Karana). Some do have mana regen, and that is always handy.

For the pants, I still say Ornate blows away the DoN cultural. I've probably saved millions of mana by casting free 3 minute roots.

For the boots, if I would get DoN, I'd lose FT3, so that is a consideration when making those choices. And Boots of flowing slime probably cost less than you can get a master/GM combo for. My hat has more HP/mana than the master/GM combo, and costs far less.

True, you can put more augs on the cultural, so that is a consideration. It really depends on what prices you can get the DoN cultural for on your server, and what other pieces are available to you at the time.

tatankawd
04-05-2006, 01:05 AM
Each race, class and religion has a set of armor or augments that corresponds to it. They were introduced with the DoN expansion. Your race/class combo has a particular type of armor, and your religion has a particular type of augments, with a different one for each slot. For example, WE druids have Brambleborn armor, and Tunare worshippers have "of Growth" augments. The augments are type 11, and so only fit in the cultural armor. Do a search in the bazaar, and you're sure to find some there. Unless you're a serious tradeskiller, you're unlikely to be able to make your own at the master or GM level. EQTraders has a good deal of info regarding them.
