I read the following in another thread:
<blockquote><strong><em>Quote:</em></strong><hr>So, yes, a druid can singlehandedly keep CS clear of wyverns, but, a group can do it far faster...which is one of the reasons the few groups I have seen there don't stick around long. They kill so fast and the respawn is slow, they usually end up sitting on their hands with nothing to do, thus they usually end up leaving.[/quote]
I read the statement and reflected upon how significant respawn time has been to my experience with EQ in the last four and half years. In a couple years, many players will only have known EQ with instanced dungeons. But for many of us now, our experience in EQ has been largely shaped by competition for scarce resources. Decent experience camps have been the most fiercely contested and limited in supply of the resources.

I realize that many people created druids, necros, bards, wizards or <insert class> because they wanted to be able to solo, but that isn't true for everyone. Remember when rotting, quadding and kiting were not even elements of our vernacular? I have no doubt that we would have discovered that different means to solo effectively under all circumstances, but I am equally confidant that we would not have had so many people create solo classes later on under different circumstances. I remember wondering if it was possible to kill a Snared Griffawn using my superior speed and most mana efficient spell. It wasn't a matter of knowing it could be done and doing it that way because that was what my class did. I remembering pulling 2 dorfs in BB to see if I could kill them using my otherwise never used AE spell (I think I died in the first 2 attempts to the LDs that I used to suffer so frequently back then). I did it out of curiosity because the game was still in it's age of discovery.

It didn't take long to realize though that soloing was more efficient than grouping. I would argue that it wasn't the "solo" class strengths that resulted in that inevitable truth, but it was the mob spawn times. I believe that many of the game's issues linger today because of spawn times that are simply far too long. Would the solo exp nerf and group bonus adjustment been "needed" if groups could actually benefit from their synergy that allows them to be more powerful than 6 individuals? Was it any wonder that solo exp was better when groups have to sit idle and wait time and time again to find something else to pull?

The absurd length of spawn times were the catalyst that made groups less efficient at gaining exp for six than a soloist could achieve. Sadly, I believe the lengthy spawn times have always been an intentional means of limiting the amount of exp that a group could earn in a time period. In creating the artificial time sinks, someone must've forgotten that it is not fun sitting and waiting for the puller to find another mob. Had spawn times been managed to allow groups to actually benefit from their collective strength, I don't believe we would have half the number of druids that remain playing today and I believe that other classes wouldn't give a hoot about other classes being able to solo.

Personally, I think it is nuts that some zones still have nineteen minute respawn times. It was nuts that they ever did. Imagine how easy it would have been to reduce spawn times to a third of what they are now. Imagine how efficient grouping would be if the puller didn't have run to distances beyond the group exp range to find a mob.

Solo experience was better because of the efficiency an individual enjoys in dealing with the spawn times. The recent changes did not give groups the opportunity to maximize their efficiency and earn better experince than a soloist; the changes simply nerfed solo experience and gifted more experience to groups. It still is not fun sitting and waiting for a pull in a group. It is still more exciting to be able to push yourself to your optimal limits soloing.

Fix the spawn times! Yes, I say "fix". The very nature of grouping assumes greater cooperative output than what is achievable by the same individuals operating independantly. The spawn times hinder groups from being efficient.
