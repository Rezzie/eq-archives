Yeah, as Yrys said, its best to get in with a new or low pop server, cause then there will be a lot of people leveling and in the same boat as you. So what was mentioned above basically. If you play alone start out on a new / low population server of your choice. If you have buddies you know, join servers that they play on.

Now the breakdown (or at least what I know about different types of servers). Mind you that I play on a PvP server and never really experienced other server types.

Here are the different types:
Normal / PvE: You can't get world pvp'ed unless you have your PvP flag on. Good for those who want a little bit of both worlds.
PvP: You can get world pvp'ed or 'ganked' in every contested zone and in horde controlled, alliance can only attack horde if they are attacked first and vice versa. Pretty fun but soemtimes the 'ganking' can get annoying.
RP: You have to 'play' or 'act' out a character from a particular storyline. Your character should have a name with a meaning and you are expected to be in character at all times. There is more info here @ the WoW website.
RP-PvP Same thing as PvP but you still...Role play.
