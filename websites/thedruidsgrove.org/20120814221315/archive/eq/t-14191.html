For skillups, I make a hotkey like so:
/pause 45, /cast 1
/pause 45, /cast 1
/pause 45, /cast 1
/pause 45, /cast 1
/pause 45, /cast 1

Put the spell you're using for the various casting type in spell slot 1. Adjust the pause to match the cast time/ refresh time for the spell. I set it up so the hotkey fires when I hit the number 1. Turn up the volume.
The I can read or something and hit the number 1 on my keyboard when I no longer hear the spell casting. When you're done skilling up in abjuration for instance, swap out the spell for alteration or something, adjust your pause and continue.

Not sure of your level or gear (especially Flowing Thought gear), but thought I'd share a tip:
Beastlords have an aa called Perfection of Spirit that (when maxed) gives 110 mana per tick regen for around 30 seconds. Find a friendly beasty to cast this on you in the guild hall (or in the guild lobby and zone quick to the hall!) and you won't have to worry about medding or the mana-cost of the spell you're using.
Note that there is no level requirement on this aa, unlike buff spells normally do.
I used this trick to help my baby chanter do a bunch of metal-enchanting :)

So if I don't autoinv the food will it just build up on the cursor?
Or will it prevent the spell from casting?

Yes, and yes (eventually).
If you cast summon food and don't remove it from your cursor (by inventory, destroy, drop, etc), it will stay on your cursor and sorta stack up.
If you're chain casting summon-somthing, once you have 20 (I think it's 20?) items on your cursor, you'll no longer be able to cast and will receive a message telling you this.
