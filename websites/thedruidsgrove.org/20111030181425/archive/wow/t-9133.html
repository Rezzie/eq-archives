Yeh, druids need to pick their spots, because some mobs we are good against some not. Also, we aren't great mob grinders, our strength is our versatility but not our overwhelming power in one area. So accept that you aren't going to grind as well as rogue or mage aoeing. I'm resto specced, lvl 60 and this is what I've found.

1) Can't fight healing mobs effectively, don't bother.
2) I use caster form with Omen of Clarity to cast regrowth every so often. This combo allows me to start with starfire, moonfire and faerie fire and then melee them. Whenever OoC procs I cast regrowth. When moonfire wears off I cast it again. Rinse and repeat.
3) Though killing the mob is slow, between innervate and OoM healing I never have any downtime between mobs. That is where I gain some efficiency.
4) At lvl 60, I find that I can grind the toxic horror's (Felwood) pretty well because they don't do tons of damage and I can remove their poison easily. I have grinded alongside warriors, rogues and mages and they are way faster in killing a monster but normally I catch up to them after a few because of the 0 downtime. They eventuall have to drink or eat or whatever.

If you really want to make money as a druid, hit high-level instances or use a profession. We are very good at herb gathering as we have stealth and can pick up herbs in cheetah form.

Anomoly -

SM graveyard is where you want to be, and by your description of the mob you had issues with, you were in the correct spot.

For the mob that spawns the apparition(s), go bear form and save up 15-20 rage depending on your talents. Right when the mob finishes casting the spell (you can tell by the summoning animation), hit swipe. You will kill the add in one hit every time without ever changing targets. Just remember to keep at least 15-20 rage and you will be fine.

For the apparitions that come in groups of 3, I like to use swipe again. In order to keep the rage building as fast as possible, I try to keep all three mobs alive until the very end. I will work on one mob til its down to 15ish%, change targets to the next mob til its down to 10ish%, then target the last mob til its dead, all the while swiping as soon as I have the rage. With a regrowth to start the fight, I may end up low to half health, but its a fast kill and an easy way to manage all three at once especially since they are not elite. These 3-groups seemed to me to be the easiest to kill and have a good chance at dropping greens.

Farm SM on rested xp and you will get both good grinding xp as well as good drops to sell for mount cash.

Just my tidbit.

Those ghost-spawning things in the SM graveyard: You don't even have to damage the ghosts to kill them. For less rage you can just use Demoralizing Roar and they will die.

All of our bear abilities are not really meant to do alot of damage. For the amount of rage used swipe is not very efficient at doing damage. What swipe does do, better than maul or demoralizing roar, is generate aggro. Since you're solo and therfore not competing for aggro it's just not the right tool.

Also, allowing three mobs to beat on you when you could have killed one is not the best idea. You end up taking alot more damage that way and it'll just slow you down. Yes, more incoming damage means more rage. More rage leads to more damage also. The amount of damage you take is much more than the amount of damage you'll be able to put out because of a few rage points.

If you've got 10 levels on the mobs the above strategy makes sense. The amount of incoming damage is minimal as you have more HP and higher AC. If you've got that much on the mobs you should probably be in cat form though.
