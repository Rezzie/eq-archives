Kahlia Girlie
It's worth mentioning that once you ding 56 (pretty sure this is the level it happened for me), you only need to use harmony on the Chief. Meaning you can single pull otters from the other two camps at will, and from Chief camp once you Harmony him.

Other things to consider are that the otters have about 5500 hp, the wyverns, if memory serves, are only about 4500. So do the math to figure out how many ae casts that is and you'll understand the mana requirements.

Unfortunately for you, the old harmony no longer works here. It was a lot less mana for lower druids to pull the otters pre-pop than it is now. So depending on your mana pool, you may want to skip otters till you get a couple levels.

The route I took, and the one I'd suggest to you, is wyverns to 51, then raptors to 54 or 55, then return to otters. One of the very awesome changes for quad kiting to go in is that you no longer need a boat to get into TD. Which means it takes much less time to get to raptors, and it's a LOT easier to get a cleric to res you - just drag body to docks and it's a 5 min job for the cleric.

It's also a lot less camped now than it used to be when the 58+ druids had nowhere else to xp (like PoN/PoD), so you will probably be able to get raptors whenever you want.
Tatankawd
No clue on Otter HPs, never killed one, but the wyverns are definitely in the 3000 HP range. They are all between 38 and 42, so the HPs are definitely appropriate for that level.

Another factor to keep in mind when levelling, is that there is a bonus to XP when both PC and mob are 51 or higher, and the mob is no more than 5 levels below the level of the PC. And the bonus increases for each level the gap decreases, becoming quite considerable for mobs above the PCs level.

So once you hit 51, it pays to find a hunting spot where the mobs are also at least 51. That's why PoP zones were so incredible when released, huge ZEMs, and pretty much all mobs are 51+, so people were attracted by the great XP rate.
