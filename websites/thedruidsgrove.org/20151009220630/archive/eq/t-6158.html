Funnily enough, little has changed in how I imagine a modern Best-of-the-Best tournament may take place. Now, as then, damage spells would be a waste of mana and thus casting damage (either by direct damage, or by damage-over-time), would cause you to run dry compared to an opponent that kept their mana for heals.

Now, as then, I see it coming down to a sneaky configuration of dummy self-buffs and damage shields, in order to try and keep one's opponent melee'ing into thorns.

Mana would be spent now, as then, on maintaining that sneaking buff configuration of dummy buffs and damage shields, whilst trying to keep the opponents damage shield off with dispells, and saving as much mana as possible for healing spells.

The more things change, the more they stay the same. ^_))

01-09-2004, 01:38 PM
There was no clicking items in Best-of-the-Best Tournaments, recall.

The baby innate damage shield plus whatever one can keep up inbetween low-mana, quickly-casting dummy buffs are all that one can manage, if the original rules are followed in this "What If". ^_^

Consider how many hit points, and how much mana, druids had before entering Sleeper's Tomb, which was roughly the time at which the tournaments took place (and long before the Temple of Veeshan). Then, compare those numbers with the total of a druid that is finishing the Plane of Time raids in modern Everquest. The damage:delay ratio on weaponary has gone up, but the caps on druid melee skills mean that this is still (now, as then), going to be a long, long battle. ^_))

Even damage spells which are less resistant than normal (lure-based swarms) disadvantage the caster compared to the canny opponent that saves mana for heals. Every swarm cast is another nail in the caster's coffin, even if it lands on the target!

In fact, swarms are just another dummy buff. Perhaps you might remember leaving Buff Slot 1 free in the original tournaments, so that opponents could cast a swarm into it... and then chuckle as they dispelled it soon afterwards, in attempts to remove your damage shield (a fun moment). ^_^

Despite AA abilities and recent equipment additions, I imagine that a modern-day tournament would look exceedingly familiar to those that remember the original event. ^_))

I duel quite a bit in Bazaar with friends and various random people for fun, and Lotus most certainly speaks the truth. Druid vs. Druid comes down to a mostly melee battle, with damage done through clickies (thorns/dots), and mana saved solely for heals. Hammer of Hours is pretty nice for dueling, but Dark Mace of Thought from VT is pure win (procs Theft of Thought) if you're dueling someone with a mana pool.
