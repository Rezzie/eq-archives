As a level 55 Wood Elf Druid of Torvonnilous by the name of Voronwe since July 08, 2000, I would like to add my name in agreement to this document.

As a druid, we have been lacking sorely as healers in both the Velious and Luclin expansions. Velious is at times bearable, due to the frequency of the mobs resistance to fire being quite low; however, barring soloing (which is not a fun, engaging, or interactive time, as this game was meant to be), the rare groups we manage to get as healer are in places such as Velketor's Labyrinth, a zone with a population far too large for its mob supply. We are rarely main healers in these groups, and in the many cases where there are clerics in the group, we are accepted into groups in the end only begrudgingly due to our lack of damage.
This brings me to another point: our direct damage spells are far underpowered compared to the current hitpoints and resists of mobs. With Velious, the 32000 limit on mob hitpoints was removed; with druid nukes at level 60, the main point where one enters the "end-game", we can deal at most roughly 1100 damage. These days, there are mobs with far more hitpoints than this (Va Dyn in Akheva Ruins is estimated at roughly 250000 hitpoints), which makes druid direct damage spells a far cry from effective. Wizards, with Manaburn, have been rumored to be able to deal 19000 damage with one spell. We should obviously not be able to match the wizard for direct damage, however such an inequity of damage seems slightly out of proportion. Furthermore, all save three of our direct damage spells are fire-based; we have had two ice-based spells, Ice and Moonfire, and one magic-based spell, Fury of Air. Our ice-based line, with the recent addition of Moonfire (which, believe me, we are quite thankful for), is now finally reasonably usable; however, our magic-based line of damage is far underpowered, with a single spell at level 34 given to us as little more than a toy with the opening of Velious. I would propose two more of these magic-based direct damage spells, around levels 49 and 60 as the ice-based line is. With this, our damage capabilities would be far more versatile; and a druid is nothing but versatile.

Thank you for listening to the meaningless musings of an old Druid,
