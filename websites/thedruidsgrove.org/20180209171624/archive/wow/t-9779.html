Brutal impact is definitley worth it the more you progress. Even though you may just want to focus on cat, which is understandable, do not neglect bear form. I love running in cat - almost all my feral gear is +agi +str, and I have just started seeing 1.2k plus crits, which is great fun. However, as a druid, our bread and butter is bear.

When you start running instances and fighting elite mobs that dont go down so quick, cat form for the whole fight just wont cut it. If you are in the position of soloing a same level elite mob with tons of armor and hp, cat is good for the quick dps, but eventually you will have to heal. You have two options here: Switch to bear, bash, heal, mf then back to cat to finish it off, or put points into natures focus so that you have a relativley uninterrupted heal (which I would recommend. After furor in resto, this is a must have talent imo). This is especially necessary when fighting mobs with high attack speed. And the benefits of the extra second of stun in pvp are pretty obvious. I have 3/5 in feral instinct, which allows me to prowl fine and hold aggro well. I may eventually finish this talent, but 3/5 is fine imo.

The way I understand it is, each level your stealth is improved by this talent, it's basically like you were 1 lvl higher.
For example, if you didn't have the talents invested and were lvl 25 trying to sneak around a lvl 25 mob, it would be equal to sneaking around a lvl 30 mob with all 5 talents.
Or for pvp reasons, it makes it much easier for you to sneak up on a higher lvl person for a ravage without them seeing you.
