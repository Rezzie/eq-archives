Speaking as a druid who does 2-box an enchanter let me throw in my 2cp.

* 2-Charmed pets = BAD. Maybe you can handle that level of complexity.. I sure can not since my luck requires both charmed mobs to break charm at the same time more often then not.

* Enchanter DoT :Enchanter DoTs are only of one line.. which is a magic based debuff/DoT combination. It has a pretty high resist rate since it has been balanced to "need" Tashania, and has at least 2 resist checks... Its first "tick" of damage also acts like a DD so it can break ROOT when cast.

Targeted AoE (Quad Kite Spell)... The only Targeted AoE spell that enchanters get is "Gravity Flux"... which can be fun, but since it tosses your mobs into the air it is not very effective in keeping a nice tight pack of mobs to quad-kite. It is also not as efficient as the druid Targeted AoE lines.

===

That being said.. Enchanters bring some nice benefit to a druid/Enchanter 2-box.

1. Indoor hunting. (Enchanter Charm / Debuff /Crowd Control, Druid Healing / Snaring / Nuking / DoT).

-With the Enchanter able to mez/blur.. you can get into some nice indoor hunting areas and CONTROL it.

-With the Enchanter able to charm anything within the level limit, they can always get a pet.

-With indoor being harder to RUN/Re-Charm, it is nice to be able to mez a pet that breaks charm to recharm.

2. SLOW. Enchanter slow may not be as good as Shaman slow... but it is still a huge benefit (especially in PoP).

3. Cripple/Weakness.. This debuff spell line (when stacked with Druid HoR/RI) can make a very large impact on mob damage output and mitigation.

4. Clarity. Always a plus.

==================

==================

Overall, with Enchanter-Charm being the main focus of this duo. It allows for hunting virtually anywhere for either LOOT or good experience.

I've done about ~120aa points duoing a chanter/druid, and it worked wonderfully prior to the charm nerf. After the charm nerf the xp wasn't nearly as good and I found I got better xp simply using the chanter to add damage to nuke kiting higher level mobs (yellow or red to 65).

No way in the world would I attempt two pets ... other than a DC pet on the druid which I do use all the time. But charm sometimes breaks infuriatingly frequently, causing havoc to recharm (sometimes).

Also, if using a pet to tank you do run into problems if you add more than one person to the mix (and intend to still have the pet tank) due to the way charmed pets lose agro if there are more than 4 people on the agro list. This bites because frequently my partners in Fire are other druids or wizards, who ideally would just be sitting back doing ranged damage. Alas.

For pure charm xp, the druid is the best healing partner, due to snare. Being able to (safely) haste the charmed pet that is hitting for ~650 makes an enormous boost in the DPS and kill rate. But like I said, the xp doing this doesn't really even compare anymore (haha ... who knows what it will be like after today's patch) to killing higher level mobs solo.

I'll submit a couple:

<strong>Druid/Beastlord</strong>
1. Levelling - 7/10
- BST + pet +DoTs is excellent DPS and Druid heals are pretty good excepting the 29-44 phase.
- This pair excels in outdoor zones, obviously, but can do indoor also, falling back on Lesser Succor if things get messy

2. Longevity - 9/10
- This pair can go a long way. A BST is an absolute dominant DPS dealer at 60+ AND can bring slow/haste to the equation as well (I honestly can't see how this class won't get nerfed at some point -- they're unbelievable =P).
- 50-55 can be a bit challenging (before the new melee damag table), but Druid charm should be able to close the gap for that range.

3. Ease of Use - 6/10
- Controlling a pet (or 2 if charming) can be challenging. These classes also depend heavily on lots of buffs so that gets a bit annoying.

4. Fun and Enjoyment - 8/10
- There are times when the BST feels just like a gimp monk with a gimp pet, but the closer you get to the end game the more amazing they become. This pair gives amazing levels of utility all the way up, which makes it more fun. In addition, at the high end this pair simply rocks.

5. Bragging Rights - 7/10
- This combo can do many, many encounters that would otherwise require full groups, so some bragging rights there. Most major accomplishments will come 60+, however.
- Small downside is that both are leather classes and often compete for the same armor drops.

Overall - 7.4/10

<strong>Druid/Enchanter</strong>
1. Levelling - 7/10
- Was 10/10 before charm nerf. Now it's 30% less =P

2. Longevity - 10/10
- Charming is viable all the way to 65 if you can get ahold of the spells. Root/Dotting is also nice in many cases, especially with Tash helping Druid Roots to stick much better.

3. Ease of Use - 7/10
- Very easy if Root/Dotting, much more challenging if using charm, but still not terribly difficult

4. Fun and Enjoyment - 10/10
- Amazingly fun and challenging at all levels. It makes you dissect encounters, methods and strategies to figure out the "best" way to accomplish the goal. Nearly unlimited flexibility.

5. Bragging Rights - 8/10
- Depending on access to CoT/CoD and AA setup, you can do amazing things with this combo. WW Dragons for sure plus clearing large portions of 50+ Dungeons. Some other creative stuff like AoE and quadding make for fun stories as well.

Overall - 8.4/10


Notes:
The charm nerf really hammered the Druid/Chanter combo, but it's still a lot of fun to use because of the flexibility it affords. Most other combinations only allow 1-2 techinques or methods, but Druid/Chanter opens up amazing possibilities.
