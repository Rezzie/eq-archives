I'd never go for Roots as my first spell, and I definitely would stay in Doomkin-Form while soloing.

Get the mob at maximum Range (36 meters). Then start with Wrath, a second Wrath, a third Wrath, then Moonfire and then Roots. At that moment the mob will be directly in front of you. Turn around and get a few meters away. Cast Insect Swarm while you do that (can be cast on things behind you).
Now Starfire/Wrath and the mob should be down before he gets free again.

The third Wrath in above rotation is not a must, it depends on how comfortable you are with the mob you are fighting and how fast. Usually I can get off all 3 Wraths and the Moonfire before the mob is in melee range.

Hallo new druids :D

I would suggest:

Starfire, moonfire, roots, insect swarm, wrath wrath wrath wrath

Starfire first because for the whole 3 sec the mob isn't even aware you're about to attack. Then when it hits, the moonfire comes straight away. The roots comes next, then insect swarm while you're backpeddling away from it. Then just spam wrath until it dies - it's unlikely to survive more than a couple of wraths.

I would advise against opening with roots, because direct damage such as Starfire and moonfire (the initial hit) tend to break roots early. Dots such as insect swarm, and the dot component of moonfire, are much less likely to break it. With this rotation you would be getting the majority of your direct damage out the way at the start.
I would use wrath at the end, because it casts faster than starfire (and therefore is less vulnerable to spell pushback if the mob actually manages to hit you) and is more mana-efficient than spamming moonfire.


-Annikk
