FFXI is really a spectacular game design. The visual effects are stunning and the charcter creation, and progression is intriguing. The Bazaar, and Auction system work well. The loot distribution is solid.

Some things that could use tweaking. Time to engage during large scale fights. Registering, and making the first content ID, as this process usually weeds out most people that become interested in playing.

The non customizable UI thing doesn't bother me AT ALL, I mean in general play you don't see any of the UI. Once you get to level 10 you're pretty comfortable with the controls and workings of the keyboard and mouse, just many people don't make it that far. In reality it's no different than any other game that you have to get used to the controls of.

They also have a translation system that offers keyphrases and statements that can be translated to and from Japanese/English. This really eases communication, and I'm even starting to recognize several of the Japanese characters.

In my opinion this game is just neat, if you're a Final Fantasy fan it plays just like a FF game, literally. The only difference is that other people are playing right along side of you.

woah, hey I been in FFXI since introduction to US markets. I remembered the original FF way back in, what? 80ish?

Well I am not disappointed, it is definately different. Star Wars Galaxies held my attention for about 6 weeks...burned out big time, awful experience, constant rehashing of the abilities, nerfing and unnerfing,ENOUGH!

You can be any of the classes they offer, what is more, you can have a sub class at half you main class, example: whitemage level 30/ blackmage level 15. Of course, there are the sterotypes, but in FFXI you can break the mold and really mix up classes and see how you character evolves.

This game has been online in Japan for over a year so the bugs are worked out, not a lot of apologies for fixes that do not fix, or make things worse. Sure there are Japanese who do not associate with English, but there are many more who do. While there is a translator in the game, it is not perfect but you can group with all Japanese or just a single one and still get your fun.

Very rarely have I been in a degrading group, yes there are hose-heads out there everywhere but they do not last long in the FFXI game (Hey this is a grouping game over level 15 so you remember rude people)

Most of all, I can say cooperation is the name of the game, you like to hunt in groups, up to 6 in a single or 18 in an alliance, you can relax and enjoy the world of FFXI... btw The beastmaster is an excellent solo artist for those who solo.

A new way to attack is to add attacks with skillchains, and it is a must for the higher monsters out there.
