I'll start off...

Role: Druid's Grove Admin / Moderator / Site Graphics
EQ Server: Cazic Thule
Guild Level: Emperor Ssraeshza (no VT yet :( )

General Mod Info/Style:
I am a big pro-discussion buff. I like threads where there's good communication going on, and in those threads I'm willing to go a little easy on the "flaming" rules as long as there's constructive information contained therein. My server's discussion forum is nothing but a big cauldron of flames, so my perception of a rant or flame is probably a little more lenient because of it. I have no patience for trolls, ads, whiners, flamers, spammers or anything of the like. My definition of a "troll" is a person who comes in, posts one word/sentence quips in every posts and leaves. Or someone who argues only for the sake of arguing (ie: no substance). Any of the above will find their threads/replies deleted, and banned in relatively short order.

I am tough on personal attacks - I don't feel there's any room in the forums for that kind of behavior. However, just because someone called you an "idiot" doesn't quite qualify as a personal attack to me. I'm a big stickler on sigs - keep their size within the forum guidelines and we won't have a problem. Otherwise you'll probably find your post edited and your sig removed.

Personality-wise, I feel I've been playing EQ for long enough that I know not to have a knee-jerk reaction to either players or VI. I'm pretty laid back when it comes to changes and will test things out before forming an opinion on whether I like it or not (as opposed to forming an opinion based purely by other people's feedback alone). I like to think I'm pretty open-minded about other classes' opinions, but it takes a good solid discussion to make me change my own perceptions, since I base them on what I've personally experienced. My current perception of a druid is that while we aren't as good as we could be with a few small changes, we're not as "broken" as others seem to think we are.

I have just recently joined a new Tier 1 guild having been in non-ranked guilds (yeah yeah we sucked LOL) for years. As such I tend to speak more for the casual player and I'm very pro-Roleplay.

Although I am level 60 with a few AA-points under my belt my equipment is average at best consisting of mostly UP with a few upgrades here and there. I've never had more than 10k to my name and average about 1k in the bank. As such I tend to speak more for the poor Druids. *smiles*

I love a good debate. I think I'm right most of the time *rolls eyes* but I can be convinced otherwise if you make your case. I tend to be VERY sarcastic which is not a good thing as sarcasm does not translate well into text which is why you'll often see my text littered with *smiles* or other such indications about the way my posts should be read.

I love to play devil's advocate in order to get people to see both sides. In my eyes the true sense of a good debate is if you are able to easily post on either side of the discussion. I like when statements are based upon facts and when people do their homework.

I also look at posters history to see what they are about if a matter is in a grey area. I visit other class boards - mostly Wizards and Clerics - to learn about the two play styles that are closest to Druids abilities and will post there if I feel I have something constructive to add.

I love to explore Norrath and spend much more time doing nothing or sitting in a newbie area buffing then leveling.

I research everything I can get about the zones/mobs and am VERY into computer hardware/upgrades.

I am married with two children, two jobs and live in Chicago.
