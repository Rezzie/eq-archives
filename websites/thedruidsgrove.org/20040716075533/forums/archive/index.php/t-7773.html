05-10-2004, 12:24 PM
Screenshots are indeed taken with the PrintScreen button. You can only take screen shots while in the normal game view (ie viewing the world). Currently you can't take game screen shots of full screen windows like the the Login Screen, Enhancements and your ID. But you can get around that by doing the normal Print Screen and paste the screen shot into a graphic application (CoH is quite system friendly--I've ran PhotoShop and CoH at the same time w/out any noticeable slow down).

The rest is pretty well answered by Lauran there. This game is 100% "quest" based. Don't get into the EQ frame of mind and think nothing but "I gotta level to get to the good stuff" cause you're already at the good stuff. Read your contact's missions and follow them. You can have upto three missions at a time, but you can only accept 1 mission from each contact at a time (pain in the buttocks since I have 5 contacts now at lvl 17 and trying to follow each one of them when I can only have 3 missions sucks arse).

But, follow the story line, read what your contacts have to say. There are some really interesting story lines in there. I love some of the backstabbing that different groups are doing to each other within my mid teen stories.

Oh, and also; post 10 you can start getting story arcs, which are more than just "do mission" deals, but a full story you follow and investigate. At the end of that you get a cool little sourvenier detailing the full story line you've just completed (like a journal). It's really cool! I have 6 of them so far. ;)

Did you always keep your powers upgraded with enhancements as best as possible or was there a time when you started saving your influence for DO and SO enhancements?

I do a little of both. DOs and SOs are pretty expensive, so I only really buy them for important things. I put two dmg DOs in my only attack spell (I'm a controller) so I could be more effective soloing. I bought an endurance reduction enhancement for Teleport so I could travel further. I bought some hold enhancements for Blind so I could keep them held longer. Basically, I enhanced the most important aspect of the power.

Keep in mind though that DOs are always more powerful than Training enhancments (and SO is always more powerful than DO.) I believe you get something like an 8%-13% bonus from using training enhancements, but a 15-20% bonus from DOs. So, even if your DO is below you're level, you're still getting more out of it than a training enhancement that is two over your level.

I'd recommend at least investing in some DOs. You can use them for 7 levels (I believe), so it's not like you have to keep updating them if you don't want to. They'll still be better than training ones. However, I wouldn't recommend buying a full set of them. (You probably couldn't afford it anyways.) Besides, once you get higher, DOs start dropping off mobs anyways (albeit rarely.)

Oh Pan, you may already know this, if not it may help a little :). When you are doing your missions you will sometimes be asked to arrest 5 of such and such mobs or 10 of them and return to your contact. The mobs are often found in groupings of just the right numbers. Some will run though and can leave you in a situation where you either have to wait and hope they come back or have to go and kill more than you really need. This is more experience of course but sometimes you are trying to round-robin your group's missions and just want to work through them as you can.

Some things to check for are, if the guy is the one with his arms crossed or the guy who has hold of the victim's purse, or the one nearest and threatening the victim, this is most usually the guy who will break and run when you attack. So if they are nicely numbered for your quest you most likely want that guy first and either lock him down or kill him fast so he won't break up your grouping.

When you are doing your missions you will sometimes be asked to arrest 5 of such and such mobs or 10 of them and return to your contact. The mobs are often found in groupings of just the right numbers.

Most of these missions obey the "Rule of 10" (copyrighted by me!) The rule states that everytime you have a mission to arrest a certain number of mobs, you will almost always be left with only one. So, if you have to arrest 10, you'll find 9 mobs pretty easily and have a rough time with that last one. ;)

Apparently, there is a rumour of a quest you can do at level 20+ to reset one or possibly more of your powers if you have made a bad choice. No confirmation on it yet, though.

One of the task forces is supposed to do it, but it's not in yet. The devs have hinted that it won't work exactly like it says in the guide (you complete the task force and get to respec.) They do have the intention of allowing you to do it, but it sounds like they aren't planning on making it too easy.
