As far as I know there are no hardcoded boundaries for faction. The "things I did" component of faction is bounded between -2000 and +2000. This component, added together with other factors such as race, deity, and faction spells/songs, determine your current faction. So if your starting faction is 0 (determined by all those other factors--race, deity, etc.) then the faction range is -2000 to 2000. If your starting faction is 500, then the faction range is -1500 to +2500.

Max ally is a misleading statement. There is really no fixed limit on how high your faction can get after all modifiers are added. <strong>Only your personal counter is capped.</strong>

Just like Wolf Form doesn't technically make you KoS, it just gives you a tiny -1000 modifier. :p

Your faction is simply a sum of these things:
<strong>- Personal counter.</strong> Starts at 0 and is normally capped at -2000 and 2000. A few factions have different caps. Most notably Beta Neutral which is capped at 0 in both ends.
<strong>- Modifer for Race.</strong> Can be changed with wolf form and other illusions. Big penalties to opposing races and often a small bonus to the same race. Some 'sceptical' races give a small penalty to every race other than themselves. The Wolf 'race' is normally -1000 on any new faction added since EQ went live.
<strong>- Modifer for Diety.</strong> Unchangable. Most Neriak factions give around -1000 for Tunare/Karana, while Grobb used to give around -300 or so and in Oggok they have no clue who Tunare and Karana is. Cabilis gives huge penalties to any non-evil diety, including Agnostic. Used mostly to penalize opposing dieties, but gives a bonus on a few special occasions. For example Rallos Zek worshippers in Kael.
<strong>- Modifier for Class.</strong> Unchangable. Used to penalize Necromancers and Shadowknights, and to let animal factions admire Druids and Rangers. Some guilds give a small bonus to their class. (Shar Vahl guilds are generous and give almost +1000 to their respective classes.)
<strong>- Modifiers from Items.</strong> Like the faction necklace for the Shadowhaven factions.
<strong>- Modifiers from Spells or Songs.</strong> Bards and Enchanters only.

If an Enchanter with a +500 racial and a +500 diety modifer, max out her counter, she will have 3000. Using a +300 faction spell on top of that with raise it to 3300.

Druids are still KoS to the Iksar Shamans after maxing out that faction, so we probably get -3000 or so in total on our modifiers. If we also bottomed out our counter, it would put us at around -5000.

And here is how the modifiers are applied:
- The Race, Diety and Class modifiers are hardcoded into the server files. These files are sacred and they prefer to mess up the game rules instead of changing them. ;)
- The personal counter and worn faction items are loaded from your personal record when you enter a zone.
- Swapping out faction items immediately changes your faction.
- The spells and songs modifiers are added the instant they are cast/played and are removed as soon as they wear off.

So those Wuoshi incidents was because the personal record hadn't been loaded yet. I've also heard that some time ago your faction was fixed at indifferent for a few seconds after zoning in. It may have been a quick fix for those problems until they could sort it out better.
