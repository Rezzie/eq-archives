However, when one buys Dire Charm, the level 46 limit isn't a secret - you pretty much know what you're getting. When you buy the skill, you're conscious of the fact that, with it, you'll be getting a risk-free pet which hits for around the 150 mark.

You buy it not to solo with (unless you're porcupining, but Lord does that hurt in PoP), but to get 50-75 dps constant, risk-free, extra damage in a group. Dire Charm is the grouping druid's dream come true. Using it to "ghetto-mez" a slowed add, with Savage Spirit, oft-times by the time the group turns its attention to the target, your dire charmed pet has done a considerable amount of damage already, as well as keeping it "mezzed", off the group.

There's little more "druidic" than having an animal by your side to help you, whether it be to increase your damage constantly (a nice augmentation whilst mana is low, too), whether you use it to "mez" adds, or what-have-you.

The pet has to hit for 150s - some call that "wimpy", but a <strong>constant</strong> source of 50+ dps, mana-independant, is certainly anything but wimpy, for a grouping druid.

It's a nice compliment to the soloing druids Call-of-Karana style of play, too. You can take the risk and indulge in a really nasty 255-hitting pet with a breakable charm solo, or you can go for the risk-free, mana-free 50dps dire charm if you're grouping.

That's the great thing about druids - tons of different skills, and frequently each is applicable in different circumstances. The fun part, and that which separates the veteran from the average, is learning which works out best in each situation.

I've heard that a good druid can really impress groups, and lead them to completely re-evaluate the class. I wouldn't know, because I'm a bad druid, of course. ^_))

With PoP, I think that dire charm is one of the best uses of nine AA points that you can find. Spirit of the Wood also costs nine points to max, and that's pretty darned helpful too. The instant cast really makes it the go-to spell of choice when the going gets tough, and it's certainly saved groups that I've been with a number of times. MGBing it on raids or trials, in which everyone is taking constant damage from some source, really is decent too.

If you avoid the dodgy jokes from the men in your guild about "wood", that it. 8(
