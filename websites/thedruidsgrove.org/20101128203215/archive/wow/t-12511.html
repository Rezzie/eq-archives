My main desire is to heal. I have a dps toon, and I love my hunter dearly, but I have always been fascinated by druids. I love the caster aspect, and I want to "grow" her to heal both in PvE (raiding even maybe, but definitely 5 man) and PvP. After just a couple of days work my baby droodad is level 23, with the full 5 piece "Of the Fang" set from Wailing Caverns. Starfire, Moonfire, Fairy Fire, kitty shift, claw claw, claw.. rip <I usually never get the rip off, the mob dies too quickly>.

I'm promised that if I will only spec feral, I'll level in half the time. Since I tend to be a pretty slow leveller anyway, that promise is appealing. But I'll wind up speccing Resto at 60 when I hit outlands anyway, to heal my way to 70 by running 5 mans. Is feral levelling really that much faster? First I am new to the boards, but I wanted to chime in on this question. When I started my druid, I had contemplated making the long trip to 70 while focusing on the Restro tree. Part of it is because I wanted to learn a healing class from the ground up.

Once level ten hit and I received my bear, I ended up sticking with feral and by the time I received cat form at 20, I was hooked. The leveling speed of a Feral build is hands down the one of the top three in the game.

With that being said, I made the switch at 60 since I wanted to prepare myself for healing at the higher levels. Currently I am a 24 / 27 hybrid and I am loving it.

If you are still thinking about leveling via Resto, here is a guide (http://www.resto4life.com/2008/01/19/talent-spec-leveling-restorationbalance/) from Restro4Life blog. It is a mix of Resto and Balance.
