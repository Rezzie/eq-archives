To me, it really depends on the mob and how hard they hit.

If they don't hit very hard (I self buff to just under 4k hps) - I'll ds tank them, nuke, back away to heal if I must. Root applied at all times

If they Hit harder, I root and lay 2 dots on first (normally can land the second one before I get summoned), back up, Nuke, get summoned, back up, nuke, get summoned, back up, heal or nuke, repeat. Root applied at all times. If my mana is <45% and they still have over half life, I throw in the white thing early and escape.

Mobs that I can't snare or root, normally don't get a look at long enough to see if they summon.

If there is an opportunity to charm, I will use one almost everytime and hope it don't break. When I play with charm to kill a summoner, I root my to-be pet first, charm him, then pull my target to my pet and root it on my pet. If charm breaks and I get summoned.... at least both mobs are rooted with a chance for recovery. I'll apply DS to my pet after engage.

Good luck.

It really depends on level and difficulty. Got to say, the only summoning mobs I'be soloed successfully at 62 were the corrupted brownie for the epic and the fabled chief goonda. With the brownie I just kept it rooted, threw dots on it and healed myself in between summons if I had to. You can get fast casting heals and dots / nukes off, casting time of the spell and good timing is essential there. Also, the corrupted brownie seemed to start summoning fairly late 8only at around 80% health or so), so it wasn't too bad.
The fabled chief goonda was as all the other fabled mobs not rootable or snarable, so I just ran like hell, tried to get enough distance to him with SoE to be able to get a dot off before he came into melee range, then ran like hell again. Admittedly though goonda is one of the easiest fabled as he is a caster, so he stopped fairly often to cast, which made things a whole lot easier.
Don't see me soloing any other summoning mobs any time soon. Died to the corrupted seafury cyclops once and to the fabled Proon, so hard hitting summoning stuff = bad!
