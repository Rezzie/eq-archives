It's not really a "rotation" per se... more like "spinning plates"... i'll explain:

I start with Mangle, and rake, then shred to 5 Combo Points and hit Savage Roar. Once i have that up, i hit Berzerk, refresh Mangle and Rake, and Shred to 5 CP's again and Rip. With Berzerk still up, I can shred as my primary yellow attack, but not let Mangle or Rake drop off, and then Bite. Berzerk should still be up to allow more CP's. I get them to 5 again and refresh Savage Roar, then pop Tiger's Fury. At that point, it becomes "don't let (fill in the blank) drop off" (Savage Roar, rake, Mangle or Rip), again using Shred as my primary yellow attack.

Some people like to get Savage Roar up as soon as possible, since it buffs all your attacks and bleeds. I personally like to keep it simple. If it comes to a point where someone is complaining about my dps, i'll tweak my priorities a little. I just like using the CD on Tiger's Fury as my "meter" for refreshing Savage Roar.

Hope that helps.

If you go by min-maxing, ArP is useless for a bear. You would never wear that kind of set for progression raiding. If you're doing heroics then yes, whatever floats your boat.

But tanks shouldn't be worried about their DPS. I mean I out DPS half our guild in heroics with overall damage because I just spam swipe the entire time endless and don't have to worry about mana/energy/rage so my effective DPS time is a lot higher. But on an actual boss fight they'll blow me out of the water.

If you are looking for threat pieces, gemming for ArP is not going to make or break that and there is no reason for a bear to make a set gemmed with ArP solely for threat when you can just use cat pieces. I believe that's what they meant for useless for the essence of a bear.

To the OP: At about 350ArP rating, it becomes more useful to gem for ArP for cat at the moment until they nerf ArP. Unlike every other stat in the game which scales linearly, ArP scales proportionally.

So at around 100-200 ArP, 16/20 agility will be worth more DPS than 16 ArP.

But by 350 arP rating, that extra 16/20 has more value. Basically the more ArP you get the better, but you shouldn't nerf your crit rating. You should always be at least above 50% crit.
