I know blizzard already said that multiple sets of armor are something that druids just have to deal with in WoW, and that other classes have to deal with this problem too (still havent seen a shaman with 2 complete sets of armor he actually uses), but i have an idea to fix this, at least for feral druids.

First off, the reasons multiple armor sets suck.
1. Take up space in bags
2. Multiple sets of armor means fixing more armor, thus higher repair cost
3. Instead of having one piece of armor to buy, you now need multiple, so muliple vendor cost
4. Cant switch armor during combat, so if you're caught unaware in the wrong form switching to feral form wont give you any advantage from you're armor

My idea is basically this, when you're in either bear of cat form, you dont use mana or mana regeneration, (you do gain back mana but since you cant use it unless you shift back its not helping you) so why not switch the stats for the feral forms. Intelligence could become strength, well spirit could become agility. For example, lets say in caster form my druid has 60 strength, 50 agility, 100 intel, and 90 spirit, now he shifts into bear form, and instead of the stats being the same, have his strength be 100, and his agility be 90. This would focus a druid on the 3 stats that are supposed to be good for druids, intelligence, stamina and spirit. Eliminating the need for multiple gear sets.

The numbers I used are just an example and in practice it might be better to just take a percentage of intel and spirit and switch them. Maybe 75% or so would still be better than multiple sets of armor.

Armor that gives strength and agility would not have any effect in feral forms, or it could be that they just add a percentage to the stats in feral form. I think this would work since i dont think ive ever seen a druid built around melee combat in caster form, thus strength and agility are not often used.

I personally think druids need to be redone in WoW, but this might solve one problem, feedback always appreciated, (unless its about spelling :smile: )
