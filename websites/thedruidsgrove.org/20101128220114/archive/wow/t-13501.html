You...should have been okay to tank it. I've healed tanks with considerably less HP fully buffed on the 25 man version and we were fine.

Were you and the warrior making sure to move out of the fire on the ground? (That's what did me in when I tried to tank him before, MT wouldn't move him and I got yelled at for taunting him out of the fire). If you're getting pounded by meteor fists plus have the fire debuff on you- it's a recipe for disaster.

Meteor fists hit hard- healers really need to be on the ball with them to keep up whichever tank has to eat them. IIRC- they're considered magic damage, not physical(? I could be wrong), but if so, can't be dodged anyway and maybe a little fire-resist gear could help mitigate some of that damage. Otherwise, the healers HAVE to be on the ball and focused on keeping the OT alive when those fists start falling.

It might be that the tanks was standing to far from each other. Meteorfist usually hits me for around 20k. Often a bit less with pally-aura. And as mentioned standing in fire gives a debuff that increases dmg taken? A good way to make tanking easier is to mark MT so you always stand on top of eachother.

Also notised that some healers act too slow at MF. They need to cast big heals so the land directly after MF lands. If not OT is probably going to die.
Hope this helps.

Agreed Nhex. I still think you're geared well enough to tank him.

That's really a healer fight, in all truthfulness. The healers need to be focused and getting the tanks healed quickly right after the MF hits in order to keep the tanks up.

Because the damage is split amongst targets within 10yds (I think is the radius), it's important that when the tanks are moving the boss off of embers that they move together and stay stacked. The debuff from the embers (from what I remember) seems to last a second or two after you move- so that might have been another reason you got hit so hard. It's hard to say without having been there.
