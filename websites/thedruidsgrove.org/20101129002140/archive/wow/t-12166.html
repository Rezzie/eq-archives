No clue about the spell haste thing, just want to correct the thing with armor.

What you gain from 100 armor is always the same, no matter how much you already have. Yes, the reduction shown in the tooltip is increasing less with higher values, but that is misleading.

This may not be easy to see, but a small calculation shows that.

Some basic values to start with:

AC -> damage reduction (%)
1a) 5229 -> 33,12
1b) 5599 -> 34,65
370 AC give 1,53% here

2a) 23169 -> 68,70
2b) 23539 -> 69,04
370 AC give 0,34% here

At this point it looks as if additional armor is less effecitve, the more you already have. But now to the calculation.

Assuming I have 20000 HP and no healer (and no ImpLOTP, ...), how much physical damage is needed to kill me?

The formula:
damage recieved = damage done * (1 - reduction percentage)
now transform that to get damage done
damage done = damage recieved / (1 - reduction percentage)


Using above AC numbers:

damage done -> reduction -> damage recieved
1a) 29904 -> 9904 (29904 * 0,3312) -> 20000
1b) 30604 -> 10604 (30604 * 0,3465) -> 20000
here the damage needed to kill me increases by 700 when the armor is increased by 370.

2a) 63898 -> 43897 (63898 * 0,6870) -> 20001
2b) 64599 -> 44599 (64599 * 0,6904) -> 20000
now that is the interesting part. the increase of 370 in armor increases the needed amount of damage to kill me by 700 here as well.
The odd point here and there is due to rounding.


So, in reality, the point of armor needed to get from 29999 to 30000 is as effecitve as the point to get from 999 to 1000.
