Here's my perspective to PvPin as a lvl 70 druid, assuming your oppenet is Qvic+ geared.


Do`s

*Winged death line of dots
*Keep max DS (DS hits for 100% damage in PvP)
*Use Hand of ro / Immo / Summer's line of spells

Don't`s

*snare -- wont stick on players
*root -- wont stick on players
*Vengence line of dots
*E`ci / WInters line of spells (not matter if the person has better FR than CR)*Stuns

Great Pvp items (toys)
*Orb of clinging death
*Dark mace of thought
*hammer of hours
*Greatstaff of power (p5 2hb, might have name wrong)
*blade of the tempest

Class specific tactics and hints

Clerics
try to use as less mana as posible. They'll let you get them down to 50% before they heal, sometimes if you burst dps and get lucky with crits you can kill them here. If you keep Oaken Vigor on you youll more than likely wont have to heal yourself at all, but ethierway clerics are a long kill, but pretty low risk.

Enchanters
If you have a blade of tempest, this will make your day a LOT easier. It's imparitive that you dot yourself , because once they get you locked down they take the upper hand. As long as you can avoid their mez its an easy duel.

Shaman
Expect a very very long duel. Their dps is very overrated, but their heals can heal their HP faster than a druid can drop em. If they canni 5 and get down under 50 you can burst dps and kill em if you get lucky with crits / resists. Dont worry about pets, they can be snared / rooted np.


Mage
Snare / root pet (1), this applies to any pet from any class. Once pet is locked down you got em.


Warrior
The only danger with warriors are crippling mighty strikes. Once they eat DS to 35%, back up and switch to casting, because even the highest hp druids can get eaten by this if they get lucky interupting you

Druid
If you don't have a Orb of Clinging death and your oppenet does, don't try. Also, its best to dispell their dots off of you as they land.

Necro
A necro will be a short duel no matter who wins. Don't conserve any mana at all with them, because they can conserve it better. If you get the necro to chain lifetap, their mana could very likely be going down fast than yours. Go all out dps from the start and just try your best to dps faster than their lifetaps heal.



(1) -- The resistbility of snare / root on pets might have been changed from the last time I dueled a mage (Fall 2003), though I cannot confirm it ethierway

Banishment doesn't work on lvl 61+ pets.

For any melee class, i've always just backed up into a corner and tanked them. Even with 2 or 3 melee on me, i've never found it to be a problem keeping myself healed. For a quick surprise, you can cast oaken guard and spirit of the grove together. I've seen rogues kill themselves on the DS before they've even realized their health drop. The only exception is against a monk with a tranquil staff or Ton Po's bo stick or an SK. 100 blows is annoying. With an SK, keep health over 50% at all times or you're likely get disc HTed. Other than tanking a melee, you can always use a dispell item (pvp merchant pumices are good or that lantern from the mines in Ssra) in combination with annul to remove their levi, then climb up on a tree or some other annoying place and cast on them.

Rogues and Bards have weapons that proc tendonslice, -300 MR resist snare, and berzerkers get an innate ability to leg slice or something, unresistable snare. I don't know if this is the same on non-pvp servers, but snares only last for 12 seconds or so in pvp. It's really only good for keeping someone from running away.

If it's 1vs1 against a caster, something you may want to consider memming is rain spells. You can get 1 nuke off in the middle of the rain for extra burst DPS. Of course, they need to be standing still, so you may need to time it, like maybe while they're casting a spell. If you have a ToT mace, you should be in good shape.

Finally, if you are fighting a bard, forget about it, run (if you can) or gate/port. A bard in an open zone who knows how to pvp and has ancient mez will be a lot to handle. Bard's mana drain is percentage based. Bard mez cannot be dispelled, if you are caught offguard, you're going to lose all your buffs and all you mana. Bard's can click items while running, so if they ever get low on health, they'll just run out of range while clicking heal potions or trakanon bp. If you dot or debuff them, they'll dispell it and they've probably got psalms going so likely most of your nukes will be resisted.

Mez isn't so much of a problem with enchanters, dotting yourself is fine. Only thing you'll need to make sure of is having the dot really far down your buff list. I keep 11 right clicks as the first 11 buff slots and I still have problems sometimes keeping the dot from getting dispelled, glowing lantern + recant magic + pvp merchant pumice will remove 20 buff slots in about 10-12 seconds. If you're dealing with a bard, and you've dotted yourself, he'll just run oor and manadrain and dispell you. The mez is really only an issue if you get jumped, if you get mezzed, there's probably no change to get a dot on yourself unless the person you're fighting is an idiot.

One thing i've noticed about the cold line of nukes, even if the person you are fighting has high cold resists, I've found there's more of a chance to land a partial hit than a complete resist compared with fire nukes. There's been time's I flat out fired off cold nukes and rain spells without even debuffing and got 2k+ crits.

As far as the vengeance line of dots, they're pretty hard to land in pvp unless the FR is debuffed to around 100. Against a class with radiant cure, there's no point, since the curse counters are seperate from the debuff counters, so you're hand of ro and immolation will get stripped along with vengeance. I'd say this dot is a case by case basis, not something I usually mem for pvp.

PvP against a paladin basically means treating him as if he has a hp bar 3 to 4 times longer than normal. Its a very long fight, so use DS to deal the most possible damage with the least mana expenditure. Apply the usual attack debuff to reduce his dps and wait. Heal early to give you extra chances of landing one while he stuns you. Don't blast nuke him the first two or three times his hp bar goes low. Wait till he has used up lay hands and you have drained most of his mana before applying nukage.
