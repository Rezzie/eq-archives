Callahad
Really tough question eh eh.

Imho. Inflation has come from multiple factors.

1 - the real money market of pp and EQ items has skyrocketed in the last year. Those who make a living out of doing this have purchased the high end items and removed them from the normal market, driving the market price up through quasi-monopoly of the resource. 2- at the high end, items are no drop, and there are no pp sinks left. Even tradeskills are done and accounted for. Thus you find yourself with people having lots of pp to spare, driving inflation up.
3- the recent attention on making RL money has caused a number of players to want their own slice of the pie, and they used the dupes and exploits to do just that. I am sure the problem existed before, but was not used to the extent it is today.

The casino was imho a solution brought by SoE to this inflation problem, and it might be at that (when it gets back live /smirk)

Sekira
Actual play costs have gone down while the amount of money that comes into the game has increased (or at the least stayed the same). Many of the plat sinks that were in the game just aren't there anymore. I know alot of these numbers seem small, but over time they add up.

Aego/Virtue/Etc/Symbol : Even the most powerful spells still use the 11pp Peridot. With MGB/Group versions of the spells, you can hit the entire raid with Virtue or Bot9/Symbol for what, 4 dots or 44 plat? In the old days a raid of 72 would have cost almost 800 plat to symbol everyone or 260ish with just group spells. With longer durations and duration enhancing foci you are recasting less too.

Coffins : Graveyards pretty much eliminated the need for coffins. A failed raid just waits 20 minutes and starts over.

Pearls : It is very often either impossible (spell disabled) or not necessary (it's easier just to walk/fight there) to CoH anymore.

SoFR Recharging : Along the same lines as coffins. While it may have been a raid saver back in the day, graveyards make it unnecessary/pointless so noone bothers with them anymore. Even at the increased cost since they changed it to make it really expensive to do.

Food and Water : Lots of people used to buy bread and water (or milk and muffins, my old favorites) from vendors. Now we buy Misty Thicket Picnics, Qeynos Afternoon Teas, Halas Meat Pies, Hero Sandwiches, etc from other players. The vast majority of the components needed for these are looted or foraged and not purchased so the plat being payed for them stays in the economy instead of leaving via a vendor.

The casino (if and when it comes back) should make a nice dent in removing plat from the world. I also think there should be high-end tradeskill recipies that require EXPENSIVE items that can be purchased from vendors. Sure, it might make it possible for people to buy their way through some of the harsher runs of particular trade skills, but then that money would be gone. They can already buy components for trade skill items in the bazaar, but that money stays in the economy.
