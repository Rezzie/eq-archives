This is a copy of a post I wrote for another message board - seems appropriate to reproduce it here:

Think about points in terms of two different concepts - Favor Points and Advanture Points.

Favor Points are per camp, and your Favor Rating is the total number of adventure points you have earned in that camp. This dictates the price of the items the Adventure Merchant in that camp will show you (and therefore the price of the items you may buy). Your Favor Points will only increase, they do not decrease when you spend Adventure Points.

Adventure Points are the points you earn by successfully completing an adventure, and may be spent in ANY camp.

Here's an example:

I have completed several adventures in Everfrost and Butcherblock. I currently have 200 favor points with each camp. This gives me 400 adventure points to spend at the Adventure Merchants. As my Favor is only 200 in each camp, each Merchant will only sell me items up to 200 Adventure Points in cost. However, I could spend all my 400 points in either camp. So I could head over to Everfrost and buy a 200 point item and two 100 point items, thus spending all my adventure points. However my favor is not forgotten at either camp.

If I now head over to North Ro, and earn myself another 200 adventure points, I could then head back to Everfrost or Butcherblock and spend those points there, as I still have the Favor I earned there previously...

In summary, adventure points determine how much you can currently spend, favor points determine the maximum item value a merchant in that specific camp will sell you. You can lose adventure points (when you spend them), you don't lose Favor points.

Hope that helps!
