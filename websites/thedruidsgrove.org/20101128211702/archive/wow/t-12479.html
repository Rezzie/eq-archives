Best upgrade: Earthwarden.
Second best - dragonhide shoulderguards, which are a pvp item you can buy from vendors.

Also, a side note - just because the enchantment says 'of the bear' it does not mean it's a good item for bears. You don't care about strength nearly as much as you care about agility and armor. There are a lot of upgrades from quest rewards that you can get; many guides posted on this site indicate them. Your first focus should be to get more armor.

Also note that +armor enchants are not that good for bears; they don't multiply when you change forms.

kalbear
06-04-2008, 04:08 PM
Strength doesn't affect hit rating at all. It affects AP, but in general you want to increase your crit before increasing your AP. And the best way to do that is with agility. As a rule, you don't have to worry that much about hit rating, at least not at your level. Threat is great for starting bears. What you need is not threat, but armor, stamina, and some avoidance.

For gloves, go with the +15 agility enchant.

For armor, you should get it as high as you can possibly go. The highest you ever want is 35900 in bear form, but in general here are some benchmarks:

Heroics: easy ones start at 18k and more is much, much better. Ideally this should be at least 22k.

Kara: 20k to start with if you're going to main tank anything. 18k for offtanking.

Gruul: depending on your role, 20k to 25k. The more you can do on Gruul himself the better.

Magtheridon: 24k or so.
