I assume you are talking about dueling as the real key to killing mages is to never let them get that initial barrage off by attacking from stealth with cat and keeping up those quick 1 sec attacks and faster specials to rip right through their squishiness.

In a duel I would still tryo to close on them as they won't really be able to start casting till the timer ends anyway. Dash is good for keeping on them whenthey blink. IF you want to turtle down and try to swallow the first wave (not my choice), put a HoT on yourself, then go bear for the extra health, as the timer goes down hit enrage and close to melee, he'll blink to get some range, feral charge to close again and bash, shift heal, cat rip his face off.

I did some dueling last night down in Gadget.

first I took down a warrior 4 levels higher than me. I find the key tactic is roots and spell damage. Kiting them helps a lot against their powerful physical attacks, and virtually eliminates their special attacks. Spell damage isn't mitigated by AC, so once their rooted and the SF, MF, wrath start falling they freak as their health drops away.

then I tore up a rogue two levels higher than me, the key to rogues is surving the initial onslaught. I lucked out and got a FF on him just as the duel started, then NG and MF. He attacked and got rooted, I backed off and dropped a SF, wrath, and another MF (I like to time it so the follow-up MF hits the same time the wrath arrives at the target). The next time he came at me my Green Whelp Armor got him, so I shifted healed, shifted back to Moonkin and dropped another SF on him, from there it was two quick MF's and he was out.
