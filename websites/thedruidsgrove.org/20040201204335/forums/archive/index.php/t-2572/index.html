Somewhere here someone asked a question again about number crunching some dmg spells and out of curiousity I started wondering exactly how dmg and DPM scale up with all the various mana savings and nuke increasers figured in.

I can't swear to all the details. There is a fair amount of uncertainty in the data you get just perusing boards...to whit:

I have heard that FOM has the same progression as SCF (2,4,7% benefits). I have also heard that FOM has a much lower benefit than SCF. I don't know of any certain parses on this point, but maybe I will dig around in Graffe's and figure it out.

Other biggie is the MP issue. Is it the full value (as Lucy says) or is it a random 1-x% savings as I have also read? I scanned a little info from Graffe's about that but haven't really digested it all...the data set in the pages I saw seemed to limited to be reliable. For simplicity's sake, I used the max possible mana preservation rather than an average of the 1-x%. That could also be revisited.

I may have missed some steps in the dmg focus benefit as well (that one I am pretty sure is definitely a 1-x% benefit since it is easy to see, but again I did max benefit for this).

Anyway, I was pretty impressed by how the numbers scale up as you approach high AA and Time level gear. Thought I would share. Be forwarned...there are 900 permutations calculated.

Only did Winter's Storm at this point.

Also, FYI, my assumption is that chain nuking is approximately 1 nuke within every 10 seconds for the aggregate dmg calcs (dmg over 5 minutes). Might have made more sense to calc it based on expending a full bar of mana and assuming different mana levels but dunno how much time I will spend on it.

Also thoguht about playing with the nuke haste AA's and foci, but that would have multiplied my permutations many times over so I didn't bother.
