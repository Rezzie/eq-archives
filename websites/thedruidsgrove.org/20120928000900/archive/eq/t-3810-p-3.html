/AGREE
I agree with the complaint here. Many people bring in Druids as "back up" healers but they are limited in their healing capability as well as a couple of other small oversights.

1. With the current spell distribution it is difficult for a druid to actually serve as an efficient healer or "nuker". While they are potent, they are not focused enough to properly serve their function and in the eyes of too many, are simply a portal system to different areas and not good for much else.
On a whole, Druids are barely ahead of Paladins in their Healing capacity. And, while the regeneration buffs are certainly beneficial, they do not make up for the fact that Druids gain Greater Healing at 29 and then must wait until 51 (twenty-two levels!!!) to get Superior Healing. This curses them with a huge valley of difficulties until they hit 51, and even then they are so far behind in the healing ability that they can only function for emergency heals.
In short. Druids are supposed to be nature's guardians. They are the healers of the land and nurturers of the weak and helpless. They are the oldest form of healer and know methods which Clerics could never hope to grasp.
I am not saying that Druids should surpass Clerics in the healing ability but, by all rights, they should stand right beside them in that ability.
I would suggest giving them the same HEAL spell distribution up to 53 (where Clerics receive Divine Light) and give Druids their own versions of the spells after Superior Healing, perhaps a tad less powerful but still granting them the capability to function as healers.

2. The charm animal spells are handy but they are fairly useless at lower levels. Most young Druids who try to charm animals to attack for them end up with two MOBs because the spell duration is pathetically short. On this, I really don't know what is the best way to approach it but you need to keep in mind that Druids are a part of nature and rivaled in this claim by no one... not even the Rangers (who also have a great deal of problems but that's for another day).
Animals of all kinds allow Druids to pass because of a deep seeded knowledge that the Druids are their guardians and protectors.
In old texts and most fantasy, druids are known to be followed by beasts of all kind. These beasts (wolves, bears, birds and deer) protected the Druids and would lay down their lives for them.
Honestly, I think that Druids should be allowed to have a pet similar to what the Enchanters have. A new summoning every few levels of a CONTROLLED pet. Perhaps varying the pet type according to race...
EX:
Elves would receive Wolf pets
Halfelves would receive Lions
Humans would receive Grizzly Bears
Halflings would receive Badgers or Skunks (something relative to their size)
As they increase in levels and obtain new versions of the Summon Animal spell, the animal will be more and more powerful to actually pose a threat to MOBs like the pet of any other class.
Perhaps this is just me but I think that druids need this change as well.

3. Last on my list of grievances is the Druid's (and Ranger's) limitation within an indoor environment.
I can understand the inability to cast such spells as Invoke Lightning, Treeform, Stinging Swarm and Careless Lightning in an indoor environment as these require Earth, Weather and wildlife to call on but I see no reason for them to be unable to cast such spells as Harmony, Spirit of Wolf, Wolf Form or any Wind Based spell as these are totally irrelevant. The loss of Harmony indoors is a total joke... period. As for Wolf Form, that is a shapechanging ability that is "innate" to the druid that simply reconstructs their body. Spirit of Wolf calls upon a spirit to charge someone with the dexterity, speed and agility (faster running) of a wolf... and with the amount of ghosts, spirits and spectres I have seen indoors....
I say leave the Wind Based spells and Fire based spells because they simply require oxygen, which is all around, even in a dungeon.
By taking these spells from Druids (and Rangers) you are destroying their usefulness in the indoor environments and cutting their abilities almost in half, leaving them in a bad position when they want to go after XP or items indoors. This, over all is a wholly unfair drawback that has needed to be addressed for a long while now.

I have said my piece.. I hope it helps.
