07-26-2005, 01:58 PM
Strength, without a doubt. Many people think agility is a good stat to get for cat form, since cat form mimics rogues and rogues rely on agility. However, the mainr eason rogues use agility is because their class is coded differently than others--they gain attack power from both strength AND agility. Upping agility on a rogue will increase both the damage of their hits and the frequency of critical hits. It's pretty much a no brainer for rogues that agi > str.

However, for druids, the only way to get higher damage is to increase your strength. Bumping agility will only increase you crit chance by a fairly small amount. If you're going for the big "bragging numbers", strength is still the best bet since you'll be critting off of higher base damages (a critical hit, fyi, is just a normal hit x2. Higher base hits = higher critical hits).

What I do for feral form is rely on strength/stamina gear in combination with +% crit and +attack power gear. This way you can get the best of both worlds without (unsuccessfully) pretending to be a rogue :)

This assumes that AP is a more important TLA (two-letter acronym) for bear form. Personnally, I see the primary role of bearform as soaking up damage. Therefore, I want to max my HP (obviously through STA, which has no bearing on AP) and beef up my mitigation abilities, which means more AC and dodging. Since AC is more or less constant across armor, you could choose armor heavy in STR, to boost attack (IMO, meaningless for bearform), or go for AGI, which may not yield as much a benefit for attack, but also provides defensive benefits. Also, I don't have a ton of 18 slot bags, meaning I want to minimize the amount of extra armor I carry.

So, IMHO, AGI > STR

To provide some context, I'm almost purely PVE. If I go bearform, I'm playing defense. I imagine if one PVP'ed more, a more offensive stance for bearform would be appropriate. Hence, the attack boost from STR would help, and the basic 360% AC boost of dire bear would be sufficient. YMMV.
