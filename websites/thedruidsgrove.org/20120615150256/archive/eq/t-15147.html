The effects of various types of avoidance are multiplicative. Using made up numbers, you might block 5% of swings, then dodge another 8% of remaining (non-blocked) swings, then avoid 10% of remaining swings. Assuming you have correct percentages in the first place, the order that these check (ignoring riposte since that has an added bonus) does not matter. Multiplication is commutative!

If I dodge 50% of swings, then block 50% of remaining swings, I get hit 25% of the time.

My understanding of the Combat system works as such:

EQ functions as: All Swings hit, UNLESS one of these checks passes.

EQ Checks the following (Order per Maddock - Bold apply to Druids):

Block
Parry
Riposte
Dodge
Shield Block

Now, since Druids don't gain Block, Parry or Riposte, The first check is against avoiding the attack completly. This is likely a function of your Dodge Skill, Avoidance AC, any +Avoidance Items, and any Dodge Mods you may have.

If the check fails, without Shield Block, you would get hit and EQ would process the damage.

Once you purchase Shield Block, you gain an extra check to avoid damage, which occurs once the game has determined you've failed all previous checks. This occurs from parsing I beleive 1, 3 and 5% of the time for Priests.

If Shield Block also fails, then the Game determines the Mob's Hit Score, which is a function of the NPC ATK value verus your mitigation AC. This is the place our attack debuffs come in.

The value is then modifed by any vie style effects you may have running and then applied to your health total.

Because of this AA, and the general benifits of Shield AC not being subject to Softcap returns, there is exactly no reason to wield a Non-Shield Secondary Item.

If you are not sure if the item in question is a Shield, look up the item in Lucy, Click Raw Data and look at the itemtype field. A Shield is itemtype 8.

-Fenier

After the checks for these special abilities fail, there is still another check that determines whether the mob hits or misses (presumably a mob accuracy vs player avoidance-AC check).

You can confirm this by noting that dodge and block rates are the same whether you're fighting a level 1 mob that misses almost every time, or a high level kyv that hits almost every time.

So, the misses need to be included in the Shield Block failure count, not just the hits.
