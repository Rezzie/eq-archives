Little story about Switzerland....

When I was in the Army, I was stationed in Germany for awhile.

I took some time off and we took a bus tour of Switzerland. We got into town and had some time to run around and look at stuff and I saw this neat little chair lift going up the side of a hill. It was the middle of Summer and I was wearing shorts and a Tshirt so I got in line, paid for a ticket and hopped on.

The view was amazing!

As the chair lift got higer up the hill I was looking behind me at the valley and the mountains around. These were real mountains! I mean, the kind of things that rise straight up even thought you are a few miles away from them. It made me re-evaluate my definition of "big".

After about 5 minutes, I started noticing that the chairlift was still going and it had these switchbacks that went around trees and little bluffs so you couldn't see too far ahead. I was still looking mostly behind at the valley and the snow on the mountains on the other side of the valley.

It was sometime around here that I started to notice something.

It was getting cold.

I don't mean, chilly, I mean, snow-in-the-shadows-of-trees cold.

Great, the ride was still going!

After about 25 minutes or so, I got to the top of the Mountain, and got off the chair lift There were guys there with heated big leather duster style jackets ready and they tossed one to me just chuckling. I had seriously thought about jumping off that thing like 10 times but decided it would suck more getting a broken leg from dropping off a chair lift and THEN freezing to death.

I walked around up there for a little while, looked at the Eiger and the Young frau (Virgin) Mountains with view finders, then hopped back on the lift and rode down. When I got off the thing at the bottom, I noticed a big closet they had to hang the jackets in.

Bleh, will teach me to pay attention from now on!
