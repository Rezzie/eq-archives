Tinkering skill is not acquired by gnomes until level 16. They are however all born with a skill of 50 in it to give them a head start and be able to do some "trivial" combines before then.

The big 7 I believe are the only ones referenced when they make generic statements about tradeskills specifically with regards to leveling.

Poison Making, Alchemy, Research(now big with DoD release), and Tinkering are limited by class and may have some unique rules/requirements.

For anything more in depth I would check over on EQTC. They are the tradeskill gurus of Norrath (and associated planes/deminsions/moons) and would probably have a more definitive answer.

There is no level cap for general tradeskills (Tailoring, Blacksmithing, Pottery, Jewelry, Brewing, Baking, Fletching), if anything.. the level cap should be at 200 until lvl 51 because of AAs. Meaning you can only get 1 tradeskill over 200 to 300, but for the rest you will have to wait for AAs.

Poison Making and Alchemy are level restricted though, they are class/race tradeskills. They are capped at about 5 skill ups per level.

Tinkering is only level restricted by the fact that you need lvl 16 to start skilling up...

The main think to keep in mind is that the salvage aa is very important when you are doing combines of high triv/ rare components. So a level 16 gnome is going to be able yo get tinkering 300 but he not be able to get the safety net aa of salvage.

Same thing for alchemy, poison or spell research. Just in case anyone is making choices of which character to invest with tradeskills.
