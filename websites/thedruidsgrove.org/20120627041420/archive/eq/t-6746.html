Mark NPC
Cost: 3 (3 levels)
Usable by: Group Leader or Delegate
This ability allows the leader to place a mark to indicate a specific NPC which is visible to all members of the group. Each additional skill point purchased allows another NPC to be marked simultaneously.

NPC Health
Cost: 2
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability allows members of the party to see a health indicator for any NPC which has been marked or targeted by the group's Main Assist.

Delegate Main Assist
Cost: 4
Usable by: Group Leader
This allows the leader to designate a member of the party as the Main Assist. Whichever NPC is targeted by the Main Assist will be clearly marked.

Delegate Mark NPC
Cost: 4
Usable by: Group Leader
Requires: Group Mark NPC
This allows the leader to delegate Mark NPC authority to a member of the group. This player will then be allowed to use the Group Mark NPC command to highlight targets for the group.

Inspect Buffs
Cost: 4 (2 levels)
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability allows group members to target their companions and inspect their buffs.

Spell Awareness
Cost: 6
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability allows players in the group to recognize spells being cast nearby.

Offense Enhancement
Cost: 4 (5 levels)
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability increases your group member's attack ability. Each level of the ability increases it an additional amount.

Mana Enhancement
Cost: 4 (3 levels)
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability increases the maximum amount of mana your group members may have. Each level of the ability increases it an additional amount.

Health Enhancement
Cost: 4 (3 levels)
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability increases your group member's maximum health. Each level of the ability increases it an additional amount.

Health Regeneration
Cost: 4 (3 levels)
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability increases your group member's ability to regenerate their health. Each level of the ability increases it an additional amount.

Find Path To PC
Cost: 4
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability allows group members to request a path to help them locate a group member. It can be activated by targeting the player and using the hotkey, or by using the /findpc command.

Health of Target's Target
Cost: 7
Usable by: Anyone In Group
This ability allows a player to view the health of whoever his or her target is attacking.

"By FAR the two most usefull *group* leadership abilities are Target of Target, and Inspect Buffs."

I Totally disagree with you on this one. While Health of Target's Target is very useful and I highly suggest getting this (raid leader ability for this is great) even though it is very confusing to have two health bars sometimes... Inspect Buffs is a waste of points until later on. How often will you inspect someone else to find out what buffs they are missing? The answer for me would be never. The second someones buff starts blinking, they will ask you for it. You should never need this ability.

My favorite Grp Leader AA at the moment is 'Spell Awareness'. This will show everyone in the group the name of the spell being casted. If it's an AA ability it will show you which level the person has also (i.e. PlayerA begins to cast [cure2]. cure2 being radiant cure level 2, forgot the actual name it lists), which is not only helpful for assist healing, but also can allow you to laugh at the other person for slacking on AA's! :P

Health Enhancement is great as always. I love having hp's. The more you can have the better in my opinion. Seeing 8100 instead of 8010 is much better to me :)

Delegate Main Assist - Helps out enchanter's more than anyone else, but it is useful at times and definately looks pretty sweet... Not something I would ever think of grabbing first, but eventually. ;)

Delegate Mark NPC - Helps enchanter's or CC get a better idea of what they should mez/CC. Thats pretty much the biggest use I can think of for this. You can let someone else mark NPC's with a seperate target ring. Worth getting? Maybe...

To sum it up... Spell Awareness and Health of Target's Target are definately worth buying. Delegate Main Assist is very useful but I wouldn't toss out the /assist hotkey for it. Delegate Mark NPC is more useful in a raid environment where you will be pulling multiple mobs that are high dps. Any "Enhancement" ability is adequate to get eventually just on the principle of it adds to your stats (atk, hp, etc) but I wouldn't start dropping points in this area until I had SA and HoTT.

"Target of Target, I have a question where does it display this information? Nominated Assist and NPC health add % health of mob on the end of its name is it the same as that?"

You get a seperate health bar that you have a button to toggle it with like an AA ability. You click the leadership window and select the ability and Hotkey it. You click the hotkey button and a new Target window opens up you can place anywhere on the screen. Once you select something (pc or npc) it will show what they have targeted. If you have an NPC selected it will show who it is hitting. This is great for assist healing because it auto-updates and will allow you to see which target it is switching to the very instance it does it. You then can /assist off that mob and get the heals flowing. This is completely different than NPC health etc.
