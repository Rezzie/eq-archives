I wanted to start a thread to discuss three of our healing spells. My strategy thus far is to mainly use Healing Touch because it is more mana efficient than the other two main healing spells. I use Rejuvenation when the amount needed to be healed is very low (I finish a fight down only ~25% health, or someone in my group took only a few hits). I find I rarely use Regrowth.

The only benefit to using Regrowth over Healing Touch is the lower cast time (currently 2s vs. 3.5s); the mana cost of Regrowth and Healing Touch remain pretty much the same. I don't see myself using Regrowth that much in the future, but I wanted to get some input to see if I was missing some benefit.

As far as Talents go, I'm planning to do ~31 in Restoration and 20 in Balance. I am only planning to spend 4 points in either of Improved Rejuvenation or Improved Regrowth. I'm finding it difficult to decide between the two becuase of the mechanics of Improved Regrowth. How do I know what a 10% increase in the chance to crit with Regrowth will buy me in the end for average amount healed per cast? Is it 10% + the base chance to crit (which is?) or 1.1* the base chance to crit (not as much of a benefit). At least with Improved Rejuvenation, you know exactly what you're getting (+3% healed per point invested).

I could split my points between the two; however, given that I rarely use Regrowth, I think I'll invest my points in Improved Rejuvenation. Comments?

There are many instances (Mauradon and Uldaman come to mind) where there's constant need for AE spells. Mages and Warlocks (Warlocks especially, because their AE hurts them as well as the mobs) lose a lot of health over a relatively short period of time during the 5-10 seconds of AEing. The main tank will also lose health when he AE taunts or if there is a boss mob to tank while the minions are AEd. This is where Regrowth > Healing Touch for me.

Right before the AE pull, slap Regrowths on the AEers; that way you can have a little bit of a cushion to watch the tank's health + any sudden dips in health from the casters. From there, you should have plenty of time to cast a Healing Touch depending on who is getting beat on most. Granted, you'll use up some MP, but the casters will also be spent after AE pulls so you will have time to recover it.

I'm a new druid but I've done the math. With Improved Regrowth (50% chance to crit) it's a much better heal when used regularly. It's not good for spaming on the same target because it loses some effect if you don't let the regen portion work.

The math I did showed a couple things. First, Regrowth heals pretty much the same as Healing touch, but half up front and half in regen. A crit doubles the heal. So a crit regrowth is basically a healing touch with a 21 sec regen attached. With a 50% chance to do that I will use it as a first heal ALL the time.

With Improved Healing Touch, Improved Regrowth, and Gift of Nature...

Regrowth heals 2345.7 avg (full duration) for 880 mana. That's 2.67 hps/mana.
Crit Regrowth heals 3348 avg for the same 880 mana. 3.80 hps/mana.

Assuming you crit half the time the average effeciency of Regrowth will be 3.17 hps/mana.

Average heal for Healing Touch is 2060 for 687 mana. Or 3.00 hps/mana.

Although my math on that last may be off... to take 15% off do you divide by 1.15 or multiply by 0.85? In the second case the effectiveness would still only be 3.07.

So based on these figures I plan to use regrowth as a first heal on the tank and followup with healing touch. If anyone else takes aggro they are getting a regrowth.

Incidently, improved rejuv only puts it's effeciency at 2.84 hps/mana. Not bad for a patch heal when you can't spare the casting time.

Once I get improved regrowth maxed I plan on getting the one hit wonder addon and have it check for regrowth first and if it's not on the target then cast it but if it is cast healing touch. That should keep my effeciency at it's peak.
