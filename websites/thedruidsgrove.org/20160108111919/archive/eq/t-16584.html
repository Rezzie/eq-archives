Try to stick with having your new pet attack your old pet.

You'll find that (typically) the mobs that refuse to swarm are the ones you've already damaged down to low health. Those mobs will come after you and ignore the pile on your pet. After going invis and breaking charm a few times, if you are not actually making kills then you end up with a bunch of mobs all at half life and they all just keep pursuing you. At best, you can keep one occupied with pet tank.

Make pet > incite swarm > break charm > make pet > kill old pet > repeat.

You want to keep the numbers of the swarm up, in order to damage your pets down to low life asap, but still you want to keep killing mobs dead (not just damaged). Ideally, you want the swarm mobs all close to full health and the only mob approaching low life is your current pet.

Also, keep in mind if you've got some straggler mob at low life that wants to ignore the swarm and keep pursuing you -- just walk him out of agro range of the swarm and the mob will go snare-dead and stop the chase. Kill those at you leisure.
