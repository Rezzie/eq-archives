If you remeber you can forage a cabbge in the karanas as well as other places. Historically druids started to keep one single cabbage in their inventory as a "lucky" cabbage...basically if you think it of a lucky charm. Over the years it just became a normal thing and people knew what the cabbage ment.

So we needed a single target of protection of the gades. So VI set up a quested way to get this spell and with I think some pushing from TDG called it Protection of the Cabbage after the lucky cabbage some druids keep in their inventory.

Im sure other people can expand but thats basicaly how it came :)

Protection of the Cabbage and the entire quest for the spell and earring are a direct memorial to TDG and to the historic druids that have posted here since time began.

The Original NPC in Fungus Grove is named Rebbecca Cabbageleaf and although she was in the game long before PotC came about I believe she was named in honor of our own Lotusfly. I also believe that it was Lotusfly that originally began the Lucky Cabbage prophecy.

The fact that the quest designers made this lasting in game memorial to this community is unlike anything that has been done before.

Hello Lirren and welcome to the Grove :) .

Many seasons ago Lotusfly foraged her lucky cabbage and rather than eat or destroy it, she tucked it into her backpack and began to notice a difference in her fortunes.

After some thought, she realized that since her cabbage find, her death's were happening less often. The news of the cabbage's protection was something that she chose to share with her fellow druids.

Not long after, other stories of good fortune following the preservation of the lucky cabbages, by various druids, proved that the talisman worked for many of us!

It became a part of our lore and a custom to keep your lucky cabbage always, for the protection and good fortune it provides. And to also forage and share them with close friends.

I hope that your lucky cabbage will bring you much good fortune :)

Hugs to Lotusfly for sharing. My cabbage is sweet and fresh after all this time and there is nothing that would cause me to part with it.
