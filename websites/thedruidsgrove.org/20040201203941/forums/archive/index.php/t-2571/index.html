Oldoak
Basically, it all depends.

Are you in a raid guild? Then level first, and without hesitation. You need the levels for the resists, hp, mana pool, etc, as well as to keep the officers off your back.

If you play more casually, I really DON'T think the difference between being 63 and 65 will matter to you tremendously, but certain AAXP abilities like SOTW WILL.

I can't really say what I would judge best without knowing where you are, but I will give you my own personal experience.

I leveled to 65 gradually. I hit the level neither first nor last in my guild. When POP was new, hitting level 65 (and even level 63) seemed like a great thing to get access to all those spells, right? Only, at the time, no one in my guild had access to places where they dropped with any regularity. So, unless you are in an elemental guild, what spells you might get at 65 are irrelevant to your decision. You will get them, but it will take long enough (even if you are rich in plat) that you would have time to level and AA many times over before you finish.

But...back to my experience.

I alternated between leveling and AA'ing. The reality for me at the time was that my experience time tended to be in short stints or late night, and groups were hard for me to get with those restrictions. Why spend 20 minutes looking for a group if I would only be in it for an hour? It wouldn't be fair to the people who took me in. I could get great solo experience in PON at 61, but at 65 it would begin to get pretty sucky. I also had only about 40AA at the time POP came out so even with the old skills I was missing a lot of useful stuff.

So, I would do a level, then grind AA with a specific goal in mind, usually a fun AA that felt like a real reward, sometimes more than one. I would get a level, get a fun or useful skill or three. Both improve your character, and I definitely would not underestimate how often something like Exodus or SOTW can save your ass. Other abilities like SCRM can add a good multiple to the rate you can earn experience, while simply adding another level will not.

Part of the fun for me was figuring out "ok, which AA skills will I get once I am 20% into 62, and then which ones at 63?" It really broke up the treadmill for me, and fortuitously (really an accident) it put my timing more in line with when my guild turned our attention to POP from Luclin content.

I always found the "level, level, level, then you can AA..." argument to be a little simplistic. It really all depends. But I encourage you to choose a path that you enjoy over one that the greybeards tell you is wisest.

EDIT: I just looked at your Magelo and I would add a couple more points. I hit a very similar issue when I was around 50 (well there was no AA at the time but the question of how fast should I level). I got far ahead of most of my guildmates, and got very frustrated by not being able to do level appropriate raids with my guild, etc. Ultimately I got too frustrated with that disparity and went to another guild. I have no regrets at all, but at the time it was a VERY hard situation for me. I am a pretty loyal person, and choosing what was best for me versus what was best for the guild was very hard, and it an be really hard on the friendships you form within your guild.

This is just a bit of a cautionary tale I suppose, but if you find you are 10 levels ahead of most of your guildmates and you are not interested in moving at any point, AA is a decent option. If you are thinking of trying to get to 65 and do more end game raiding, level will be the very first requirement of most guilds you try to get into.

THERE IS NO RIGHT ANSWER to this question, no matter how often people post the contrary view.
Tiniane
Oldoak hit the nail on the head.

Do what you want to do. Look at the spells you get next level or two (and your chances of getting hold of those spells), compare them to the AAs you want - which are more valuable to you?

Consider the average level of your friends and guild as well.

Personally I levelled to 60 the day before PoP came out and raised the level cap to 65. Having grinded (ground?) my way to 60 (mainly by soloing) I wanted a bit of a change, so I went through level 60 on 90% AA, only going to 100% AA when I was in danger of dinging 61. At the time my Guild was not getting many parchments, and had never seen a rune, so my chances of getting my 61-65 spells were slim.

I got enough AAs to get my Venerable title, and got Exodus as well before going back to 100%xp and heading up to level 65. By the time I dinged 61 I had all my 61 and 62 spells in the bank, as Ethereals had become more common.

Don't let anyone tell you there is a right way and a wrong way of working with AA and normal xp. Do it the way that suits you.
