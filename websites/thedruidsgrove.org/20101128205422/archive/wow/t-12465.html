Solo runs aren't going to help you learn how to tank. They'll help you learn how to solo. You need to learn how to tank by tanking actual instances.

By the time you're 60 you should have enough decent outlands gear and upgrades that you can attempt ramparts. It's a good instance with interesting pulls, some good fights and a need to prioritize things. It is great for learning how to tank. I would do it with friends and people you know, and make sure they know you need practice. And then go practice.

Another thing to do is to go on runs with someone else who is a good tank and watch what they do. Watch how they mark, how they pull, which mobs they hit first, which they CC, etc.

But really, you just have to practice.

As to what your talents should be - you should max thick hide. It's one of the best advantages a druid tank has. It's a huge talent. You should take a point out of improved motw. Maxing heart of the wild is good. After that, go for naturalist and then omen of clarity. After that it really depends; I went with Intensity, some people go with primal tenacity, others natural shapeshifter. I think it really depends on how often you're going to be tanking vs. how often you'll be a cat.

But heart of the wild and thick hide are the top needs. Get them. Then naturalist and omen of clarity.

I couldn't disagree more. Learning how to position, how to use swipe and mangle and faerie fire, how to grab more than three mobs, how to CC and what to CC - these are important skills. Just because you won't be able to tank bosses as effectively doesn't mean you can't do the rest of the stuff. And frankly, bosses are cake for druids. It's multimobs and managing threat/CC on them that's the harder skill to learn and the one that you need the most practice on to get good at.
