Discussion: Lots of nice belts with high AC, but in my opinion the Haste effect on many of the highest AC belts is wasted on druids. For the best combination of overall AC, Wis, Mana, resists, and HP, the Belt of Dwarf Slaying still takes top honors. 15 to wis, 100 mana, 100 hp, 5 vs all, and 20 AC to boot.

Lcea's new drop is a close contender, but is significantly lower in HP and Mana.

The Windraider's Belt has 15 more base AC, and an additional +2 vs all, but at the cost of no wisdom, no mana, and no hp. Sure it has haste, but so what? Not a good tradeoff for a druid.

The Belt of Marvelous Visions has similar problems. The effect does give a nice Stamina (and thus, HP) boost, but it has no resists and no wisdom or mana.

The Silver Girdle of Stability is better than either the Windraider's or BMV, with 50 mana and hp. However, the low stat pluses to wis and resists make it relatively inferior.

The Belt of the Destroyer is the belt that probably takes third place. 5 more AC than the BDS, same +15 to wis, a nice 15 to FR (but none to the others), and 75 hp, but no mana. The haste effect, however, means that druids are unlikely to ever see it.

What was second place? The White Dragon Scale Sash. 90 to hp and mana, 15 to wis, 10-15 vs all, but only 10 AC. I think the BDS is better, but the better resists on this belt make it a very close second.




After this grouping, there is a myriad of choices. Most have wisdom in the 5 to 8 range, and several have decent HP and Mana stats as well. The Belt of Inconsistency would be a good choice; while it has low wisdom, it has 75 HP and Mana (and that 75 mana is the equivalent of over +12 to wisdom).

Similarly, the Belt of Guardianship, while it only has +3 to wisdom, has 20 mana, which makes it in effect a +6 wis belt. With 12 AC, 20 hp, and +3 vs all, a +6 wis equivalent belt is very nice.

The Spiked Seahorse Hide Belt is very similar, with the 25 mana translating to another +4 in wis (so +8 in effect). And so on....:)
