Add clerics to your list, because there are times when they pull as well. :) Personally, I usually swap monks, bards, and SKs in and out of groups as pullers.

Druids can be GREAT pullers in some places, like PoFire - druids and necros rule that zone. In any other zone flagged as outdoors druids can also pull with relative ease, but you need to make sure of a few things: you have sow/soe on so you don't get caught, you pull with a non-damage spell so you don't get summoned, and you have a tank with good quick aggro ability. Snare pulling can help a lot with this; it does no damage, pulls the mob, and allows you to easily outrun it. If mobs are far away, pull with some other spell and snare them when they get close to camp, then just run in tight circles around the group until the tank has aggro.

Druids pulling in indoor zones is generally a bad idea unless mobs are very spread out, because root will be your only form of crowd control until all the mobs you aggroed are in camp (where maybe you have a mezzer).

Tips for pulling.
1) Outdoor zone? Use Harmony
2) Not a lot of wanderers? Can snare-pull them to give the tank more time to get aggro
3) With wanderers around, can use burst of flame or similar low level spells for low aggro pulls
4) If you get an add, you have a choice to make about whether or not to root CC the add.. if the group can take 2, bring the add... otherwise root it off to the side.
5) Pullers will take hits from time to time (a lot sometimes), so maxing your hp with the available buffs in group is highly advisable. Another thing to consider is that you can also heal yourself if you need to.

That is the beauty of being a druid, our versatility, which might seem limited in the higherend but it really isn't.

Harmony works great, be sure to study our harmony line first though.

One common misconception about Harmony is how it works. Our very first spell "harmony" lowers hate and frenzy on a single target and picks up some sorrounding mobs.

Our second Harmony spell, Harmony of Nature, is a single target spell. I'm not sure if it works the same but...

Our last one, Nature's Serenity is where the misconception comes forth. This doesn't lower hate/frenzy radius on mobs. It lowers assist / social assist on mobs, so you can STILL agro the mob harmonied by this spell if you get close, yet you can pull his neighbor relatively safe within a no-agro distance. This is also a single target spell, and does not pick up any others only your target.

Hope that helped a bit, as you progress in levels you might need to switch your harmonies to match higher level mobs.

For indoor pulling just use root where you'd use harmony =p

Ppl fuss entirely too much over needing clean single pulls. Root cc in most cases can be as good as you can expect a chanter to mez stuff.

If it's one mob, dispell it and pull it to camp.
If it's 2 mobs root the front one. switch target to the back one and run back to camp. Once debuffed and controled run back and either snare to be safe or dispell to pull.
If it's 3 mobs root the front one switch to one of the moving ones snare it run back to camp. Get that one taunted root the snared one as inc run back and snare the other.
If it's 4 mobs mobs, target the one you want, turn away snare run for 2 seconds, stop completely and egress... run back, your mob should be single waiting to get pulled the rest of the way =p

snare + egress can be a powerful combo in a lot of places.. if you can snare a mob, unless it or a mob in the vicinty can dispell, you can get it single in most cases. When mobs move while unaggro'd they move at walk speed which of course is slower than normal.. the way it works out is walk movement when snared = 0% ie: it doesn't move. Some mobs will sorta poof back to a spot near their bind but most will just not move at all until snare wears off so if you snare them and egress they'll be where you left them (hopefully now away from mobs).

If you want to get really fancy sometimes you can split mobs by sitting on the edge of their aggro radius. Think there's a mechanism where they'll aggro from that extra distance if you're sitting and for some reason or another the usual adds may not. Saw a pally do this in tipt for the ghosts was really neat.

You want to matain a few aspects while pulling.

1: You want to use whatever your best speed buff is, but need to be careful not to outdistance the mob so it resets.
2: You want to snare as soon as you can (I often pull with snare) to ensure you are moving quicker then the mob. If you need to pull the mob a good distance tho, use another spell to pull - however once a mob hits 97 percent it begins summoning, so careful if pulling with nuke or dot spells.
3: If you pull areas you get hit alot, but sure to invest in the CA Line of AA, as it will allow you to take a few rounds without dying as long as the mob isn't hitting drastically hard.
