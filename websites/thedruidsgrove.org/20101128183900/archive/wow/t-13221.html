I encountered a similar issue. I was using bartender since TBC, and it has never really worked correctly in WotLK, so I tried Titanpanel, and experienced similar issues with Lag and overall functionality failures (read: bars not shifting with me, stupid gargoyle blocking needed items/buttons...etc)

Finally I went back to vanilla WoW interface! For those like me, who have not used it in a few years...try it again! It is not the TBC interface I ditched a few years ago. It now is much more customizable, shifts actionbars with forms, and best of all....no additional RAM consumption. I found this by accident when I had a HD crash on my "Good" computer and then jumped on my "old" computer for a raid. When my comp with an integrated VC was out performing my $200+ 512mb VC on the other computer, I decided it was time to slim down the addons!

I strongly reccomend just using the WoW bars that come standard. You get like 6, visable (12 button) bars, I think, +4 different "Action bars" which shift with you.

FWIW, you can turn off the gargoyles with....


/script MainMenuBarLeftEndCap:Hide() MainMenuBarRightEndCap:Hide()

I haven't figured out how to make it stick, but I just made a macro and put it on my 2nd bar and click it whenever I need to see the stuff I have down in the corner of my UI (tomtom).

FWIW, I use the stock UI as well along with very few addons....

Itemrack (or is it outfitter??? I don't recall)
OmniCC (cooldown thingy)
BadKitty
Pitbull (frames, with lots of default stuff disabled)
Omen (frankly, as a tank, I'm not sure you really need this. As a DPS the builtin warnings are probably enough to let you know you need to slow down a bit)
Pawn
Gatherer
Auctioneer
TomTom
QuestHelper

I often disable the bottom 4 whenever I raid just so they don't give me any issues (not that they do, but just in case).
