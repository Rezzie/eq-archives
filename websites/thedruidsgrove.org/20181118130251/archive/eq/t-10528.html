Method 1: Charm a pet with a lot of HP (DC pets usually will not work) and keep the pet healed and charmed during the entire fight. If charm breaks, evac and try again. Make sure you do not out damage your pet or you will be summoned. Do not use this method on mobs that dispel or agro-wipe themselves. Exodus is almost a requirement for this method.

Method 2: Get all the HP buffs you can find (symbol, focus, ect) and all the regen as possible. Put your best DS on yourself, attack the mob and hope you can channel enough heals thru the attacks to stay alive. Make sure you do at least 1 point of melee/spell damage to the mob or it will not leave a corpse (DS damage is considered environmental damage). Spell casting haste (cleric buff, AA, or item) helps. Exodus is useful for this method. You may want to use Virtue/Conviction instead of your skin spells.

As a druid, there is one main strategy I use for dealing with summoners. I personally prefer this strategy against summoning mobs as well as in duels against strong meleeing classes. (There is one other strategy for dealing with summoners, but it is inherently extremely unreliable)

1. Always have an exit strategy. With summoning mobs, you can't run away so this has to be either a succor, evac or gate spell. Have one memorized as a last resort, this is your one and only lifeline.

2. Buffs. Apply your best AC, HP, DS and regen buffs. DS is important as an additional source of free damage. I personally use three items - Regal Band of Bathezid (Chardok quest item), Velium Coldain Insigna Ring (8th Coldain quest ring) and smithed Humming Orbs that give me a combined DS of 45.Together with our two DS spells, I get to deal close to 100 damage every time I am hit.

3. Root. I can't describe how important for a summoner to be rooted. It is what gives us druids breathing room to cast spells safely by simply stepping back. Always reapply root when it breaks.

4. Debuffs and dispells. Once your target is rooted, anything that can reduce your target's meleeing dps is always good. 99% of summoners do not start summoning until they are damaged a little so ALWAYS start with these spells. Once your target is sufficiently debuffed, go on to the next phase.

5. Apply DoTs. Like DS, DoTs are a good source of additional damage. Always start with your longest casting and most damaging DoT (For me its either epic 1.0 or Blade of the Tempest). You will only have a small window of a few seconds before the mob starts summoning. Usually, I would try to squeeze into this small window the most damaging combo of DoTs that I can cast.

6. Melee, Nuke, Heal. Once the mob starts summoning, I stop using DoTs as its time to ramp up the dps. I turn on auto attack (for an extra source of damage) and start nuking away. Every time the mob summons me, I step back out of melee range and nuke again, or if I'm low on hp I heal myself. I usually won't bother to refresh my DoTs or DS unless they are insta-click or very quick casting. Rinse and repeat until either your mob is dead or you are dead (or escaped).

Now, for the inherently extremely unreliable second strategy - using a pet as tank and damage dealer. You will only want to use this strategy when the first strategy fails - usually because the summoning mob has very very high dps that your own druid health bar cannot withstand more than 10 seconds. As with the first strategy, always plan an exit strategy. Now find a suitable pet, make sure you have snared and glamored it before casting charm. Now buff the pet with AC, hp, DS, regen buffs. Your pet will be the main tank and damage dealer, so root the summoner and order your pet to attack it. It is vital to always 1) Keep your pet alive 2) Keep your pet charmed and 3) Keep the summoner rooted.

Using a pet introduces two uncontrollable random factors, charm breaks and root breaks. If root breaks, reapply the root. If either charm breaks, you will get summoned. Take a step back and try to recharm your pet. Its difficult with all that interrupting but still doable. Here's the catch, its very common to have both charm and root broken at the same time. To begin with, our charm spells are inherently unstable and breaks often even with a -MR debuff applied on the pet. Secondly, since the pet is meleeing the summoner to deal damage and aggro, it will cause root breaks to occur pretty often too. And if BOTH spell are down at the same time, you will be in deep deep trouble. You will have two mobs hitting on you, one of the mobs (or possibly even BOTH mobs) will summon you and at least one mob will be one that can kill you in under 10 seconds. All I can say is cast evac, and pray.

A few things to know on summoning mobs:

-When a mob summons, it instantly warps whatever PC or NPC is at the top of their aggro list to the center of its melee range.
-A mob will ONLY summon once it goes under 97% health. To clarify, at 97% health, a mob will not summon, and at 96% health, it will. This is not a permanent status; if the mob goes down to 96% and starts summoning, but then regenerates or is healed back up past 97%, it will stop summoning.
-All mobs refresh summon on a TWELVE (12) second counter. To clarify, a mob will summon, and after 12 seconds, it will summon again.
-As long as its target is in its melee range, the mob will not summon. The 12 second counter will reach 12 and stop, and will not begin again until it switches aggro to summon another target or its current target reaches the edge of the mob's melee range.
-Summoning mobs that also cast: A mob will not summon at any time while it casts a spell. Casting a spell will not stop or reset the mob's 12 second counter, but if it reaches 12 in the middle of casting a spell, it will delay summoning its target until it finishes casting.
-When you are summoned, any spell you were in the middle of casting will be interrupted.
-When you are summoned, you retain your facing. To clarify, if you are facing north, and a mob directly to your east summons you, you will have moved a distance to your east, but you will still be facing to your north (this all sounds like complicated physics or something, but it's important to remember).
-If a mob is feared/blinded/low-health-running, it will not summon. The 12 second counter will reach 12 and stop during that time.

Keep that in mind any time you deal with a summoning mob.

The root dot method works quite wel on a variety of summoners. I used it on the corrupt elysian in UP, and fine tuned the method a lot in CT. It works very well on a lot of the OOW mobs now too.

In general this is my root rot plan:

Root mob (have clicky pants, so its mana free and has neg resist modifier)

Snare mob

Use two debuffs, ro's etc. Lowering the mobs ac, fire resist and attk all help. The attk part can sometimes be really critical-- or it may not matter, depends on a lot of variables. The main thing is it cant hurt, mob isnt damaged as of yet and isnt summoning.

Apply epic. Its a slow dot so it wont tend to start the summon cycle any sooner, and its damage will add up over the fight.

Apply first dot...use one with a fire debuff usually, just to make the good fire dot stick better.

Apply good fire dot.

At this point you may get summoned....or you may have time to sneak in a wasp type dot. You will have to learn to judge this. Spell haste helps, dot crits etc make this less likely.

Get summoned....run out of range asap, stop, partially turn to make sure casting wont be interrupted, cast second (or first) wasp dot.

At this point you just reapply dots and heal self as needed.



Things that can help:

Already be running when summoned- cant always do this, but it will cut down on the hits the mob gets in.

Be ready to reapply root if/ when it breaks. Duck out of a dot cast if need be of course.

SOTW makes a nice safety net...dont wait too long to use it tho.

As much DS as you can get and regen help too.

You dont have to have exodus to lave, egress usually will work fine if you dont leave it until the last second.
