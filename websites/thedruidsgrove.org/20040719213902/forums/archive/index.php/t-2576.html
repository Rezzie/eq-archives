Fair enough.

In Everquest, players undertake tasks with an online avatar in a persistent fantasy world to garner increases in the effectiveness of said avatar versus the environment, or to a limited extent, other players. It goes without saying that the majority of the members reading this board are familiar with the time and effort required in gaining experience, acquiring equipment, or undertaking quests. The amount of effort exerted to complete a particular task may vary from person to person, depending on a variety of factors including luck, skill, tenacity, resourcefulness, and outside assistance, but there is still some measure of exertion just the same.

My stance is that the model of "effort -> reward" is fundamental in the design of Everquest, and to insinuate that players of the game be allowed to bypass the effort and simply receive the boon is nothing short of absurd. Were we to apply this same flawed thought process universally and eliminate all challenges, then achievement would lose its meaning. There would cease to be a game.

This was the argument I was attempting to make when I drew a parallel between the concepts of spells on a vendor and gear on a vendor.

Then combine A Black Potion + Pure Blood (dropped off a Luggold Initiate in Nadox) to recieve A Glowing Black Potion.

You may be able to hand in the glowing black potion all by itself for the spell, but I handed in the "A Torn Page" (no-drop, all/all, Nadox drop) along with it to the npc and I got my Pure Blood scroll! Sadly the scroll is no-drop as suspected, I had extra parts and was hoping to hook up some guildees.

No idea if you need to turn in the torn page along with the glowing black potion or not, or which one you need. for the record my page identified as pg 129, and was given back along with the spell.

"A storage attendant" does NOT drop the gold emblem, I am convinced. It drops a golden medallion of service that looks exactly like it, but is for a different quest.

Instead, you want to kill "a storage worker" to get the one gold emblem (took me about 15 kills once I figured out I was focusing on the wrong mob), and the shady treasure sorter for the other.


Both storage workers and shady treasure sorters spawn in 3 separate locations in Dulak. These are buildings in which you can find storage workers, storage attendants, and sorters. The spawns are constant for each type, and static. One building is next to the engineer/architect building (right in front of you from the zone in), and the other two are back to the left, one in front of the third (middle) ship, the other right across the street (it has a shady sorter right outside the front door to the left). Interestingly, there is a storage attendant and one other (I think a worker) in a fourth location in the back alley behind this last building. You can only get to it by jumping up and over the fence right behind the above-mentioned shady sorter to the left of the front door. Finally finished this a while back. Here is my advice:

-Charm is your friend.

Charm the highest level boar you can (exhausted if you have CoT) and go to work with that. Otherwise (unless you're stupendously geared) this is going to be a really awful time.

While you are learning the zone make sure to pull to open areas before you move in too deep.

General charm recomendations:
-get weapons for your pet (gnoll hide lariats (or anything else that procs stun), weapons that proc slow, or just decent damage weapons like mace of the fallen crusader.
-keep your pet ensnared. A good habit is to refresh ensnare each time charm breaks. Putting glamor of tunare (animial only magic resist debuff) on every third time is also generally considered good form.
-root everything you're engaged on. this will buy you some life when charm breaks. preferably have earthen roots up for recharming, debuffing the pet and savage up for rooting mobs.
-ensnare everything you're engaged on while you're starting out. you may find this unnecessary when you get to be familiar and are keeping everything rooted consistantly, but better safe than sorry.
-buff that pet: savage spirit, str buff, skin, damage shields, regen.
-make sure to at the very minimum do some damage to each mob. ideally you will be dotting.

I borrowed this from a Cleric friend of mine:

Directions to the Nadox bits (describing without a map, bear with me):
- Enter Gunthak, swim south to beach, invis up, go through the tunnels, through the town, to the "castle" entrance to Nadox.
- Once in Nadox (still invis), take left exit from the entrance room, then go stright down the hall to the 3rd(2nd?) right (from the hall you'll see into a big room with a "fountain" in the middle, *filled* with trolls). Kill those trolls (light blue to a 65) to get the "blackened vial". Note: there is a funky respawn trap in the doorway, fight back down the hall a bit, unless you like adds.
- Once you get the "blackened vial", invis up, and go straight through the "fountain" room, and down the hall. You'll get to a "big round room" filled with trolls, and past that is a "cave with a fire" and more trolls. Kill those trolls (light blue to a 65) to get "Broken Skull Blood".
- Once you get the "Broken Skull Blood", go over the bridge in the "cave with the fire". Find Luggalds (dark blue to a 65) and kill them for the "Pure Blood". (I got mine off the first luggald we saw right over the bridge, a "lugglad abomintion", but there should be more in deeper).

The Nadox bits can be done quite easily by a group of 3, takes maybe 3 hours. The Gunthak bit can be done solo, or easily duoed (or foraged, darn druids), don't know how long that takes yet.

Easier explanation (possibly) is:

Once you cross the Gunthak beach and enter the cave, take the second tunnel to the left - that takes you to the Nadox lava zone. Nothing sees invis. Swim til you see the bridge ahead and go up the right bank to a wide open area. As you look at the bridge, the blood droppers will be just through the first entrance on the right, the vial droppers will be just inside the 2nd entrance to the right, and the luggolds for pureblood will be through the doorway straight across the bridge (left side of lava river). You can also get gunthak gulf water fishing - lucky fisherman :)
