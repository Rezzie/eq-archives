Cool, I didn't know the bloom aggro went to the beneficiary of the heal either :>

There are many awesome things about lifebloom (and some I dont even know about by the sounds of it), but here's a brief summary of why it's super-awesome.

1. Mana efficiency. As has been said already in this thread, refreshing 3 stacks of lifebloom before it's about to expire each time is the most efficient healing output possible. It means you get 7 ticks of 500+ (about 3500 healing) for only ~200 mana. This method of using lifebloom is great for using on tanks, and because it's so efficient you can just sit there doing it all day without ever running out of mana.
If there are 3 tanks, you can keep lifeblooms on all of them - bringing great stability to a raid encounter.

2. Dispel effect. If you're pvp'ing and your 5% health rogue is running away from a hunter, the hunter may be tempted to use arcane shot to finish him off. Put a lifebloom on the rog - now if the hunter does arcane shot, it dispels the lifebloom, causing the bloom to hit instantly - negating the damage of the arcane shot. You can also trick shamans into helping you heal yourself for loads - when they start spamming purge at you (as shamans are prone to doing), it dispels the lifebloom, healing you. Put on another, and it gets purged again - healing you again. The most blooms I've managed to chain together in this way is 5, but usually they figure out what is happening a bit earlier :> In pvp, it's always best to go with no more than 2 stacks if you think it might be purged. Purge (and possibly also dispel?) removes 2 buffs per cast, so if you have 3 stacks up it will simply drop it to 1 stack, and this won't create a bloom.
