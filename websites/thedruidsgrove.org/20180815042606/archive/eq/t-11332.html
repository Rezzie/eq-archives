Most of the carbs there are from a dietary fiber source.

Your body does not digest dietary fiber. Those carbs do not turn into anything, they pass on through. You do not normally count those calories either.

Another 'carb' that does not turn into blood sugar is glycerine. It does become the glycerol head of the molecule of cholesterols that flow through your body--the tri-glycerides. Glycerol is an alcohol by chemical nature-7 Calories per gram as opposed to 4 Calories per gram for carbs.

You will usually find a lot of glycerine carbs in protein diet or energy bars. And sometimes 'diabetic' candies.
