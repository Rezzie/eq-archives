According to my calculations, 1 mp5 equals 2.73 spirit.

This calculation is based on a 6 minute duration fight, with the Druid being within the FSR all the time. And, of course, with the Druid having all talents in the Resto tree that are connected to mana regeneration. It combines spirit based mana regen with the mana gained through Innervate.

I would have to agree, as a healer with lots of extra spirit, I am finding that I need to stack more mp5, because as mentioned, in a 25-man raid you are always inside the FSR.

However, as a mention to the innervate, I have a high spirit weapon/enchant and a high spirit offhand. (Literally "of the spirit" greens) that I have macro'd to swap in with my self innervate macro(yes the button has to be hit twice), so I can recoup all of my mana in one innervate, without sacrificing the mp5 to keep me going during trash and/or giving my innervate to someone else.

Also, I have seen some healer use an add-on called casterweaponswapper which will swap your weapons for a high int set, a high spirit set, a spell surge set, and a normal set. It will swap you into the high int set when your mana gets close to full, so you have extra mana at the start of fights, then switch you back into your regular set until you get low on mana then into the high spirit set to help promote outside the FSR. I found that the int/spirit switching was not as useful for me, except the other set the spell surge set swaps that weapon in until it procs, then swaps it back out until the internal cool down is up on spell surge, then swaps it again. So if you have two good healing weapons one can have +healing and the other have spell surge. However, I have yet to have two decent healer weapons at the same time to try it out.
