Stormhaven
Couple of possibilities -
1 - Don't kite and just kill the pet.
2 - Get a horse.

1 - For the best bang for your buck, snare and charm your potential pet. Snare and DoT/Debuff/Root your potential target. Send in the pet and make sure yours survives (this may mean nuking critter #2 or throwing small heals on your pet). Once the other creature is dead, debuff charm (or wait for it to wear off) and kill off your low hp pet.

2 - If your adamant about keeping your pet, get a horse (this makes sense for #1 too). Remember that most NPCs at your level will probably have anywhere from 5-10khp. That's a lot of HP to heal, and at your level, you don't have the multiple thousand hitpoint heals. If you want to keep your pet, keep aggro on yourself by using a crappy spell like flame lick and stay on your horse medding the whole time. If your pet gets aggro, you'll have to heal it, so make sure that the mob is chasing you.

That's my suggestions, but my charm kiting experiences are limited. I'm sure there's a lot more folks here with better advice.
Jarilon
You could also try agro kiting the mob with the pet chasing after it and hitting it. Use a low mana cost spell like ensnare and the level 1 fire dot - flame lick, in addition to any other dots you can manage to stack on it while it is chasing you. You won't be able to sit and med at all while your pet is killing it but you also won't have to worry about healing your pet.

Additionally, if both mobs are animals you could try using fear kiting, fear the mob and let your pet chase it while it runs away.

Either way, good luck!

Been working the same thing as you recently.. Some of the stuff I have learned through trial and error.

1. Don't heal anything. Just break charm on pet when he is too low and use the other mob (charm it) to kill your former pet.

2. Savage spirit your pet. ALWAYS!

3. If you want to keep a particular raptor, or just try a diff strategy. Try fearing the raptor your pet is beating on. With this strategy, and a hasted pet, I can clear the whole big isle and have a pet at whatever hps I want in the end.

4. Try DS'ing your pet too for more damage.

5. With the fear method I actually like to pull 3 of 4 raptors over to the corner of the island that gives me the longest fear path and "park" them there in waiting. I mean ensnare and dot and root them till my pet is ready for the next one.

All of these strategies work realy well just remember to do some damage (bracers or ceramic ring) to each target. I even went so far as to stop ensnaring my pet during the fear strategies because I wanted him coming back more quickly. If he broke charm it wasn't much to ensnare and recharm but it made the fearing strategy work a little better.

Just sharing my experience as I learn charming too.

Way I do it (for xp, not for the challenge if that is what you are after).

In POS (or wherever), snare and charm your mob, then snare and root another mob. Stick your pet on mob without any buffs or ss.

When both mobs are at about 10-15% health (assumes 2 mobs are about same level of course), cast invis on yourself to break charm and finish off the pair of them.

This way pet takes no xp, and you kill 2 mobs with very little mana usage in next to no time. Pre patch the other day, I was getting 16% aa xp in under 3 minutes this way..shame there arent more pops :)

If you're in it for the XP, kill your pet after each fight (without haste), or each second fight (With haste).

If you have one of those shells that casts savage spirit (about 10k in the bazaar), you can haste all the time, but if mana is tight, you might be better off just setting your pet on another mob, and breaking charm when they're both below 10%. Then killing them both off is just too easy. You can do it with Drones from the bracer, or use the Luminescent staff to nuke them down. (Drones is probably better, cause you can med while the damage is done)

If you have the mana to haste, kill your pet after each second fight. It should end up being near death by the time the second Raptor gets near death as well.

By breaking charm when both are near death you maximise your XP. I've been doing that almost exclusively since the charm nerfs.
