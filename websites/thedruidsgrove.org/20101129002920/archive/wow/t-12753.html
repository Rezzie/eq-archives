There aren't very many moonkins out there unfortunately, which is why we don't see them in pvp much.

When you do see them, it is often as you described - with multiple moonkins. And it is mainly because of the potential of having all those adds chopping away at the enemy.
You can even take it a step further and have each moonkin equip a trinket that summons additional adds. For example, the barov peasant caller on 5 moonkins would result in 15 treants and 15 barovs for a total of 35 possible targets for the bewildered enemy to choose from.

It's a nice strategy because it's so unusual, stylish, and frequently takes the enemy by surprise.
The downside is that it is easily countered with some AOE.


I think in general the trick to being an effective moonkin is by being a sniper. Try stealthing into the arena in cat form, and only shift to Moonkin when your team has started fighting. Play it like a sniper. If you can get off some nice starfires at the start of the match the huge dps will swiftly put the enemy team on the defensive.

I am no expert on Moonkin pvp, but from what I have seen, the best strategy is the one where the enemy is hitting someone else, and you can just sit there and dps.


-Annikk
