08-03-2009, 05:22 PM
I've found that initial rage is key. I personally spam Berserker Rage (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=18499) for more rage as warriors can never have enough (keeping Bloodrage (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=2687) for those awful avoidance streaks, changing in 3.2). Pulling with Charge (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=11578) via the Warbringer (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=57499) talent gives initial 15 rage, and with every decent prot warrior going the Deep Wounds (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=12867) side-route, the extra 10 rage in Imp. Charge (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=12697) gives flexibility with a Thunder Clap (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=11581) / Shockwave (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=46968) opener. Tankspot.com used to have a video showing all these wonderful warrior pulling tricks (http://www.tankspot.com/forums/f160/40785-warrior-pulling-guide.html), but FileFront seems to have lost it.

Speaking of Tankspot.com, I'd recommend a quick read of their priority flowchart (http://www.tankspot.com/forums/f14/41426-ideal-warrior-single-target-threat-rotation.html) as a rough guideline for good tps. For AOE situations, just like in TBC, frenetic target-tabbing -> (high threat skill) is still needed. In TBC it was mostly Devastate (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47498), nowadays it's mostly Shield Slam (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47488), both with Heroic Strike (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47450) woven as much as possible; post-3.2, this may or may not change.

As mentioned, in contrast to the frenetic key-mashing, Paladins have Hammer of the Righteous, Consecration, etc. in precise rotation. This is why I don't like tanking on the warrior at all.

08-04-2009, 02:25 PM
Cleave: A sweeping attack that does your weapon damage plus 222 to the target and his nearest ally.

Thunderclap: Blasts nearby enemies increasing the time between their attacks by 10% for 30 sec and doing 300 damage to them. Damage increased by attack power. This ability causes additional threat.

Cleave is kind of like Maul + the Glyph of Maul for bears.

Thunderclap is sort of like a Tauren's war stomp, for lack of a better idea on how to explain it. Thunderclap is a fun, flashy BOOM kinda move. Rage cost is 20, not sure if that's reduced by talents or not.

I'd imagine even if your warrior tank opens up with charge and his enrage ability- it might still be safer to hold back a bit on opening up with AoE nukefests for a second or two.
ryan4nayr
08-04-2009, 03:00 PM
Talented (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=12666) TC (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47502) gives decent threat within its 8 yards (10 yds. glyphed (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=43399), about same threat as a Heroic Strike), but during the 6s cooldown, your high-crit dps party members can quickly pass it. Cleave (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47520) is limited to 2 targets (3 targets glyphed (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=43414)); it's great hitting a different 2nd/3rd mob when tab-targeting -> Cleave.

A warrior's highest threat, (spammable) skills are Shield Slam (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47488), Revenge (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=57823) and Heroic Strike (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=47450), so tabing to different targets to apply these would be very helpful.

But patch 3.2 is running now so a lot of these could change.
