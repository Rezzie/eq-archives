PvP = fighting mobs and other players from the opposing faction. You can go into any alliance city as a horde player and attack any person you see.


PvE = fighting mobs and other players as well, but to fight opposing faction outside of Battlegrounds, you have to flag yourself with /pvp and then either kill gaurds and NPC's or wait until they flag themselves to fight you also.


Classes vary. Choose what fits you best! For a beginner, I suggest warrior or rogue. Easy to use, lots of damage.

PvP = Player V Player

PvE = Player V Environment (aka Normal)

The way I understand it, if you walk into contested lands, or the opposing side's lands on a PVP server, you are flagged PvP. That means that you are able to be attacked. You can't attack someone from the opposing city until they flag themselves by attacking you, or healing someone you are in combat with, etc. So you couldn't sneak in and gank someone in their own city unless they were already PvP flagged.
