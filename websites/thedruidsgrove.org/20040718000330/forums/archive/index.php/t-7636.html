It looks as though SOE devs are really hung up on the power of the DS.

Given this information, I believe a strictly mathematical calculation is in order. Reducing the timer from 22 minutes to 15 minutes, and applying the same reduction to the Damage Shield component would yield a damage shield reduction of:

22-15=7 [22 minutes - 15 minutes = 7 minutes]
(7/22)*55=17.5 [7 minutes / 22 minutes multiplied by 55 DS = 17.5 DS]

17.5 DS

So if you were to reduce the timer to 15 minutes, and also reduce the DS by 18 (17.5 rounded) then you would mathematically have the same outcome of aggragate DS data over a large sample.

Would you trade 18 DS (making it 37 DS instead of 55) on SoTW for a 15 minute reuse?

I'd trade away the AC part. ;) To me, I think it should be 15 mins. like the others, but the DS is very useful. I use it in 1 of three situations (well, 4, but forget PLing atm):

1. At a raid as part of Mass Wood/CR/Para chain. - There the timer is irrelevant.

2. As part of a "super" DS, mostly against a GoD named (though I do it against any named/boss at raids). - there the healing is actually almost useless and the DS is everything.

3. As an emergency heal while grouping. - Here, there is some minor benefit in being able to refresh faster.

While I do think our ability should be 15 mins like the rest of them, I would not want to see the DS part reduced or eliminated for it. Wood is more flexible in its application than CR or Para, though the effects of CR/Para are better at healing than Wood (plus para has mana regen). I personally think the increased flexibility of application (like paragon) but with less than half the healing ability of CR, balances out and our ability thus, should be on the same timer. SOE disagrees. I guess I dsiagree they are perfectly balanced out too, because I would rather have Wood as it is now than CR (though I'd probably take Paragon over Wood).

Anyway, I'd vote no on any downward change to the DS, and in fact, would almost certainly vote yes to an even longer refresh time if I could have an even bigger DS.
