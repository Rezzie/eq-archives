The 30 mana vs. HP seems completely vanilla, however.

I think the thinking is that in this day and age surviving the hits is more important than anything else.

When EQ first launched HP was everything. No one had the mana pool to do more than cast a single spell really, and at least till your late teens druids melee'd a lot. So HP was where you were at. If you could survive the skeleton hitting you long enough, chances are your melee weapon would kill it.

Later AC became king. Druids had a decent amount of HP and Mana, but the main concern was that you couldnt tank. Aggro being what it was, chances are the mob would turn on you at some point no matter what you did. AC gave you the chance to avoid the mobs hits (maybe, if it was high enough) for a few rounds while the tank taunted the mob off you. We became adept at circling around the tank rather than just running circles and calming moving the mob back into position for the tank. With a high enough AC you could take a few hits, the mob would miss a few, and you'd have time to survive. If you took all the hits, chances are you'd be dead because there was no way Druids could have enough HP to survive most PoP era mobs for 3 or 4 rounds.

By the time GoD and OoW came around mobs hit so hard and so fast that it didnt matter. Whether you avoided a few hits or absorbed them, 1-2 rounds and any druid was dead. If the mob turned on you, you died. At this point, combined with the high mana cost of spells, and the increasingly large mana pools we had at 65-70 mana became much more important. Regen was slow, sitting was bad, you had to simply have enough mana to go from start to finish. If a mob would kill you just by looking at you, AC and HP no longer mattered, so mana was everything. It was also helpful for kiting since again kiting really is all about your mana pool.

With OoC regen and large HP/AC augs, mana has become less of an issue once again. We regen mana quickly, we have Mana Pres, SCM, GoRM, and other tools to help mana conservation. When you hit 10k+ mana, you never really run out of mana in a fight. Couple this with the fact that 5kmana or 10k mana with OoC regen you regen to full in 3 minutes, how large a mana pool you have doesnt really matter much. Regen scales. Having a 15k mana pool does not necessarily give you that much bigger an advantage over a 10k one other than endurance in a pure burn fight. And with all the mana pres abilities and focii we have these days, endurance is often less an issue than DPS or heal efficiency. A 15k mana pool isnt going to help you if your heal per second ratio is too low to keep up with the mob's damage.

With the AC softcap and the ability to exceed it with normal equipment, AC has become less of an issue for Druids as it has long been less of an issue with casters. So outside of a high AC aug on your shield, it doesnt matter as much once you hit the softcap.

So it comes down to user preferance again. HP vs Mana. And these days with AA's like avoidance, shield block, and mitigation Druids can take a few hits. Depending on the mob of course. Hence, a lot of druids, not needing as high a mana pool as you can possibly get, are once again looking at HP over mana. If you can take the hits for a few seconds, then you'll probably live and continue to contribute. If you cant take the hits and the mob turns on you, you die. Without a cleric or paladin handy, your out of the fight period.

30 HP in and of itself isnt going to do much. But then again, neither will 30 mana. It's simply that much more. When you add up +30HP across say 10 augs, that's 300HP. With mitigation and avoidance, that could translate to most of an extra hit. That very well could be the difference between survival and a rez.

Sure, with a 15k mana pool I can stay in the fight longer. I can solo more stuff. But if a mob looks and me and I die, then my contribution will be less no matter how many spells I have or how much mana I have. After you hit a certain point, you just stop using a lot of mana. Well, you use a lot, but it makes less of an impact on your mana pool. Once you hit about 10k mana, you can group all day long and not really burn through it unless you have a crappy group and you are main DPS or a main healer. Neither role we are really meant to be at the top end. We can do it, but not all that well. Raid druids nothwithstanding of course.

A lot comes down to personal preference of course. If you solo a lot, mana is probably going to be your biggest stat. If you group a lot though, HP will probably mean more assuming you already have a decent sized mana pool. If your 75 and your mana pool is 8k, you have a problem you need to address. With equipment and augs. But if you already are 10k+ mana, you can afford to look ahead more.

For myself, it's all about AC (till the softcap) and HP. I can preserve my mana usage. I cant do anything about a mob turning on me. If it happens, it happens. Aggro management aside, there will be times you cant help it. You have to heal early or a lot. FD procing mobs are a pain this way too. Being able to survive for a few seconds is pretty important. I group a lot, and a death slows things down more than a caster sitting out a round every 20 minutes or so to regen. So yes, to me HP is far more important than mana these days.

Who knows how things will change in the future though. As EQ continues to grow, change, and evolve we'll have to change our playstyles to match. It's happened before and it will happen again.
