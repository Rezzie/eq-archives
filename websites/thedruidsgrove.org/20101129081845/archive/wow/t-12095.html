Do repeatable quest turn-ins until you can no longer turn them in anymore (arrakoa feathers, cenarion herbs, etc).

Do instance runs until they no longer give any rep (be sure to get the quests associated with running the instances, just don't turn them in).

Do quests.

The two reps that really matter for a feral druid are CE (earthwarden) and Lower City (for epic flight). Check out both factions and the corresponding user comments on Wowhead to get the "best" approach. The above post covers 90% of the scenarios. Basically quest always give rep regardless of current level. So, do those last.

Also, in 2.3, you only have to be honored to get your heroic key, which means lower city rep management isn't as much of an issue.

Hellfire - run Ramparts & Bloodfurnace until you hit honored, then you can do quests and I think you should hit revered or pretty close.

Cenarion Expedition - I turned in plant parts until I couldn't get anymore rep ( honored I think? ) then did quests and ran maybe 3 instances and hit revered somewhere in there.

Lower City - not many quests....Just bite your lip and run Shadow Lab to revered.

Sha'tar - similar to lower city - run instances for rep ( this was the slowest for me )

Gray quests still give you the same amount of rep.

Order of progression in terms of leveling
Hellfire -> Zangramarsh -> Terrokar Forrest -> Nagrand -> Blade's Edge -> Shadowmoon Valley / Netherstorm
