For the debate of nukes vs. DoTs...

In groups, I generally will use Vengence of the Sun if the fight is gonna last more than 30-45 sec and nuke once VotS is on. Otherwise, you don't get the full efficiency of the DoT.

Soloing, I prefer using only DoTs unless quad kiting. Mana efficiency is everything when soloing. The more free dots you have the more mana you can spend stacking other dots. Potime orb (if you have access to one) is <3 <3 <3 for soloing, and the epic 1.0 at a minimum will prevent mob regen from undoing your handiwork if you need to med on the fly.

I tried doing something out of boredom a few days ago. I did a chart that plotted time, what was cast, and how much damage was being done during a theoretical fight. Started with Wasp Swarm, added in Swarming Death, added in Vengence of the Sun, then chain nuking and refreshing DoTs as needed (VotS was the only one really). Unmodified and assuming 0 delay between a spell recast delay going off and the next spell being cast, druid dps maxed at 386dps between 17 and 47 seconds into the fight (I'm pretty sure that's the range I measured dps over).

DoT vs. nukes...

When grouping with a high end warrior dots are nice... You can't nuke while mob is just engaged because you'll draw aggro, but dots are lower aggro initially. Even though dots usually end up to be more aggro, warriors agro, unlike knights, escalates on a steep curve over a short time due to procs. Wasp swarm is nice in groups even if it doesn't get the last tick or 2 in, will still do more damage (on average) for less mana with last casting time than any nuke. Also keep in mind wasp virtually never resists.

In groups I usually do hand on inc, than click SD orb, than cast vengence of the sun than nukes. If I'm low on mana I'll do tempest wrath after sun than vengence of tunare.

On raids I don't even waste spell gems for vengence dots. I usually do immo, wasp, swarming, and tempest. All of these (save tempest) do more avg damage per cast than our nukes, under most conditions. The lower cast times are very nice as well, especially since we're usually casting damage spells between our heal and cure casts.

Tempest wind has great average damage per cast, but in xp groups it's efficiency isn't all that great. However, I usually use it on nameds where high dps is nice to have (ie MPG trials.) On raids I use it on any mobs where I want to pour on the dps, however on some raid mobs resists can be a problem - due to the fact CR debuffs suck ass.
