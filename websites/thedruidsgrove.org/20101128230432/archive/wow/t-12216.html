I'd go for 8/11/42. The Resto in my 3v3 has that build and he loves it. If you go for the other build I'd suggest putting 2 more points in it to get Nature's Reach. The increase in range is really useful for your crowd control spells.

But I'd move 2 points from Empowered Rejuvination to Nature's Focus in any case. HoTs are very nice in the arena, but when you need that big heal, not getting interrupted is more valuable than a few percent more +healing IMO.

The only reason I'm hesitant about 11/11/39 is due to the fact I'd lose a point in Empowered Rejuvenation. Regrowth is good for quick healing, but the reason Resto Druids are so strong in 2v2 and 3v3 is due to HoTs. Lifebloom and Rejuv, with the occasional Swiftmend and sometimes Regrowth, is what makes them so strong. There's rarely any cast bars, and they can kite with Travel form while casting heals.

It comes down to whether I'd want less interruption on Regrowth, or get a bigger chance for it to crit; I plan on keeping at least 4 points in Empowered Rejuvenation, if not 5. It's just too sexy for the method of healing.

Personally, I've been messing around with the 8/11/42 Resto PvP build and adjusting a few talents to what best suited me and I maxed out Empowered Rejuvenation instead of Nature's Focus (3/5). You could always go 4 and 4 to be middle of the road but if you know how to avoid getting interrupted by both melee and spellcasters/ranged, you shouldn't have to rely on a talent to get your heals off.

Another perspective is that your individual skill and smart positioning will be able to make up for the spell interrupts whereas no matter how good you are, you won't magically heal for more on your HoTs ;D

Happy Arena'ing~
