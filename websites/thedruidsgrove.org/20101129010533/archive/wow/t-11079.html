Yeah, I am a 14/32/5 (Feral DPS) druid and love it. What Trix says is so very true, for feral.

The versatility of the feral druid is great. My basic strategy if I am not already in combat is to prowl in cat and act just like a rogue finding vulnerable targets. But if I can't find someone fading back from combat to rest (thats always fun) I'll go after anyone with a ravage or pounce. Deal as much initial damage from omen of clarity procs in cat form then switch up to caster to drop a moonfire. I then cast root and back off and switch to bear form. Charge in and wear the oppontnt down. If I need health I just frenzied regen. You can also bash, go caster to root and back up for a heal. This is where Trix was talking about shifting a lot. What I just described is only one way to do it. Depending on the situation you can prowl in and stay in cat form for great dps and never leave cat.

If feral sounds fun to you, getting good dps is all just a matter of the talent build and practice with your timing and knowing what form is best for the situation.

A balance druid is a caster that primarily uses wrath, moonfire, and starfire for dps. GOing heavy in the balance tree gives reduced cooldowns, mana efficiencies, and increased critical chances.

Again, druids are so versatile that it will just be a matter of what you enjoy doing. For me, feral always looked so fun so I did it and have been having so much fun, but there are balance druids here at the grove that can go into great detail on their talent specs.

Good luck and do whats fun. DPS comes with experience and gathering the appropriate gear for the job.
