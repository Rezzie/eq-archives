No matter what the situation is, a druid should be conscious of healing, damage, and utility aspects of play. The 'role' you need to play should dictact what aspect(s) you concentrate more on, and you should be aware of how your role changes while the situation you're in changes (ie, if cleric dies) -- but there is no situation where you should completely unconscious of any aspect of play.

A dead mob or a mob running mob does zero damage, so zero healing is required. Quicker kills give quicker XP. Quicker kills means the mob has less time to deal damage to the tank which means the tank will require less heals. Is it mana efficient to sit there chain nuking instead of medding between heals? No, it isn't. But if you find the right balance of what you need to do better overall results will be produced.

Basically, I hate inflexible druids who put themself in 'roles.' /hate
