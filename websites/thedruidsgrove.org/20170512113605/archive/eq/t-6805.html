The non-Uber Guide to Gates of Discord

The purpose of this guide is to help dispel the myth that GoD has nothing to offer non-elemental/time guilds. Discord is a challenging expansion, but, it is well worth the risk for players of all styles. It won’t make casual players uber overnight, but it will allow you to get some very decent upgrades and set you on the path.

Traveling in GoD:
Getting around in GoD is more difficult than getting around in PoP. It’s old style EQ travel. You need to pass through zones full of stuff that want you dead to get to anything. Do not be discouraged. It is no more difficult getting around here as it was in Kunark, Velious, or Luclin when they first came out. They key to successful travel is not fixed duration invisibility, though that helps, or taking the direct route. The secret is to be prepared. If you are not an invisibility caster, keep a supply of invisibility potions on you. Keep them in your bank as well incase you need them to do a corpse recovery. I recommend making periodic visits to Abysmal Sea for them. The general supplies vendors across the hall from the bank sells them for under 4 plat.

Here are tips to getting around in GoD:
1. Direct routes will usually get you killed. If you zone in, cast invisibility and then proceed to make a “b-line” to the zone, you will die, possibly taking other players with you. Simply don’t do it. Take a cautious approach to your route. Stick to zone walls. In Qinimi, for example. The safest route is to follow the right zone wall.
2. Assume all Mobs between you and your goal see invis. If there is a mob dangerously close to your path, consider them. If they con anything but indifferent wait for them to path away, pacify them or carefully go around them.
3. Pay attention to “You are about to appear” messages. You will die of you don’t pay attention to it. If you do not have a fixed duration invis and you see this message, take action fast. You need to move away from any mobs. You can usually find a nearby corner that is just out of agro range and safe enough to recast invis. Better yet. Have a potion ready to click. You can cast instant invis potions while standing next to mobs without ever being seen.
4. Do not hesitate to take a detour if your path is too risky. Sometimes, there are see invis mobs up in the long Corridor leading from Qinimi in Barindu. What do you do then? You take the long way around. Qinimi, Barindu, Riwwi, and Ferubi all have connections with each other. All of these zones are relatively small. You can fit all of these zones into one the size of the Dreadlands. If your way is blocked in Barindu, Zone out, go to Riwwi, cross that zone and zone in to Barindu from there. Taking the time to travel through Riwwi is a lot faster than doing a corpse recovery because you wanted to try to sneak by that wandering See invis mob.
5. Hate them or love them, Download maps. Use them. They will help you plan your routes and show you where zone lines are. Sure, some feel it’s cheating, but, They are invaluable.

But the mobs hit too damn hard!
There is one thing that can’t be denied about Gates of Discord. The mobs hit like freight trains. SOE decided to take a different approach this time. The mobs in this expansion hit much harder than anywhere else, but, they have far fewer hitpoints. It is common to hear terrifying tails of trash mobs hitting for 1200. This combined with the challenges to travel and no graveyards keeps non-ubers away from these zones. In reality, not all zones are stacked with mobs quadding for 1k plus.

Single groups should avoid Ferubi, Vxed, Tpit and the Purifying Plant. It is pretty easy to get overwhelmed in these zones if you are not equipped with at least Vex-Thal quality gear. On the other hand Qinimi, Riwwi, and some of the Sewer zones like The Crematory are decent spots. Experience is on par with LDoN runs but the real reason to go to these places is to get better gear. It’s not a fast process, but, it is definitely worthwhile. In all of these zones very nice items can rarely drop from trash mobs. Most of these items are BoT quality or better. Items from the sewers are easily Vex-Thal quality.

But aren’t the sewers trial zones?
Yes they are. Gates of Discord has 6 zones designed for trials to get access to Kod’Taz. The High Priest in Barindu starts you off on your way. You do not, however, need to use these zones as a trial. There is a NPC named Gamesh standing behind the pyramid in Barindu. He will gladly spawn any of the four sewer zones. They are identical to the trial zones but will not get you a flag. The Crematory is a good starting point. Mobs are moderately harder than LDoN normal mobs and eventually ramp up to hard mode strength deep inside. If you can handle Bastion of Thunder, you can handle the Crematory. Casual guilds can raid too!
Uber guilds are uber for one reason. They raid. The traditional problem that smaller guilds have had is that they can’t commit the kind of time it takes to get keyed and flagged for places like the Elemental Planes and Vex-Thal. This has caused massive disparities between gear between ubers and non-ubers. Believe it or not, GoD can help remedy some of that. Barindu and Ferubi are two excellent zones that non-uber guilds can raid in. Both of these zones have named mobs that a decent raid force of non-ubers can handle. Ferubi is particularly good for this since it has a very fast respawn. If it cons yellow in Ferubi, your raid can kill it. If it cons yellow and you kill it, you will be rewarded. These mobs drop everything from AC15 and 25hp/mana/endurance augmentations to loot that is Vex-Thal quality or better.

Barindu is also a good choice. Named drop very good gear and random unnamed can drop outstanding loot as well, such as:

Conclusion
After reading many boards it has become apparent that people have been shying away from awesome potential. There seems to be a general “this expansion is for the smallest portion of players.” sentiment. This is just not true. GoD is much more challenging than what most are used to, but, it also holds greater potential for advancement than any other expansion to date. This is the first time non-uber players will be able to significantly increase their power in the form of gear. If used properly, GoD can allow players to gear up to the point where they have a much better chance at the rest of EQ.

If you are satisfied with grinding in Bastion of Thunder and Halls of honor and aren’t interested in actually advancing your character, then GoD isn’t for you. If you are tired of the same old grind and are looking for adventure, challenge and very decent reward, then push forward.
