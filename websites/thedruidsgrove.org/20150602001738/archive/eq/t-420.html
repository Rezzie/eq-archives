Stat-wise and innate skill wise, Halfling is the winner. He has the best stats, sneak and hide, two skills druids don't get and sneak is a very powerful skill as it can let you shop and do quests you normally couldn't.

As for cultural armor, Tunarean Soldier armor can be worn by all druids who worships Tunare and Stormreaper Boned armor can be worn by all druids who worships Karana. However, only Wood Elf Tailors can make the Tunare only armor and only Halfling Tailors can make the pieces.

Half-elves has the worst stats, and no skills as far as I can remember. (I'm sure half-elves will correct me if I'm wrong. *grin*)

Wood elves have hide and forage, but you gain forage as a skill. Stats are comparative to both Haflings and Humans, Wood Elves are in between.

Humans are pretty much the... basis race. No innate abilities, they have cultural, but it's smithing-related, no infravision, which can be a bit of a pain if you can't turn your gamma up, I gather. They have more str and sta than Wood Elves.



That being said there's the question of what your goals are. if you're starting fresh and you don't care about looks, but just want the most powerful race, go Halfling. Other than that, the differences are so small that by the time you hit 50-60, your wis will be maxed anyway, so you're really choosing for looks.

Now, if you're aiming at a high-end raiding druid, and you have the gear and the cash to support it. I'd say go Human or Halfling and dump as many points as you can in STA, because STA has traditionally been the hardest stat to max for druids.

ADDED:
Humans and Half-elves can choose between Tunare and Karana. There are a couple of Tunare only and Karana only items in the game, but the consencus is that the Tunare only items are far better than the Karana only item(s?).
