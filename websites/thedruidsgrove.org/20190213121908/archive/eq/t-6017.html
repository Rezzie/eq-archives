Tough decision to make. Especially since you're right at the beginning of starting your AAs.

You gotta ask yourself what role you think you're going to have in raids, or what role you WANT to have. Also, where do you think you'll be doing most of your raids.

First off, without a doubt, get SCM3. Less mana for spells means more mana in the long run.

Now, if you're doing Luclin zones, then Radiant Cure would be beneficial, if not... you don't really need it. Tier 1 and 2 PoP encounters have seldom use for it.

If you plan on sticking to the healer role, then you may consider getting Healing Adept and/or Healing Gift maxed as well as their PoP AA equivalents. Add to that SotW3 and you'd be set.

If you think you might want to contribute to the DD effort, then by all means go with SCF3 and FoM3. Just go with what feels best for you. You may change your mind along the way a few times like we all have.

Whatever you decide, two additional AAs that are a must are Exodus and Innate Camo. These two alone will allow you to save your party (or yourself when you solo) more times than you can count and also allow you to do recon into zones, single-pull (if assigned to main puller) and travel just about everywhere with a bit more ease.

If your WIS isn't as high as you'd like it, you might want to invest a few AAs into raising it, or help your mana regen with MC1&2. If you're over-maxed on WIS and want to benefit from it, you can go with Innate Enlightenment or Planar Power.

There's really so many choices before you, it's hard to really plan out a definite path to take.

One last piece of generally Druid-agreed upon advice, a few AAs that really are NOT worth the effort to obtain initially are:

-MC3 (6AAs for 1 more point of mana/tic is a bit outrageous)
-Enhanced Root
-Viscid Roots
-Improved Natural Durability
-Quick Direct Damage

You should achieve a balance between raid AA's and your solo/group AA's. So, this is what I recommend, with reasons why.

SCM3 - mana savings
MGB, sotw1 - contributes to raid heals, you can get sotw3 later (there are other things more useful than sotw2,3)
radiant cure1 - removes many afflictions for groups, get RC2 RC3 much later, 100+aa's
Those are the must haves. Now for some others:

innate cameo - invaluable AA, you'll be amazed with this ability and wonder why you waited so long to get it.
exodus - good for groups, ldon, solo exploring.
Healing AA's - Healing adept3, Healing gift3 (then do the Advanced levels). You have to decide how important healing is to your raid contribution. But realilze that druid blast heals in a raid are often on the rampage tank, or on assist heals. I would do the Adept AA's first (more healing for the same mana), then Gift AA's (critical heals).
Spell Casting Fury - lots of satisfaction here, being able to cast crits.
Innate Enlightenment, planar progression - put AA's into these abilities as your wisdom rises with better gear.

Pure Blood - while this is a spell, you should do the quest for this spell. Very handy of the course of your raiding life.
RGC - Very useful in some raids, but can be difficult to obtain.

Scm1, MC1, scm2, scm3 first and foremost.

Then it depends on your soloing method. If you are charming, I suggest Innate Camo, then SCF1-2-3. Quad kiting, I would skip innate camo and jump straight to SCF1-2-3. Root-dotting and grouping I would go straight to more raid-friendly AAs.

So I would suggest Healing gift and healing adept be maxxed. Then FoM 1-2-3.

If at any point you find difficulties surviving, get ND1-2-3 somewhere in there.

Finally, to finish it off, CA3/CS3/ID5/LR5. After this it really doesnt matter much eh. 'course you likely by then have 250 AAs ;)
