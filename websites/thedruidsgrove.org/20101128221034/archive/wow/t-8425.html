Well, I'm feral (56 druid). 35 feral, 10 balance (NW), and 2 resto (MoW). I have 8894 armor in bear.


Usually, I solo with cat because I can take down mobs pretty fast.

But if I get ganked by adds I switch to bear in which I can take on and defeat 3-4 mobs 1-2 levels higher than me at a time. The thing about bear form is that swipe becomes more and more awsome the more mobs you fight. Everytime you get hit, you generate rage. So the more mobs beating on you, the fast you generate rage.

And because swipe hit's multiple mobs, your odds of gettting a crit are really high. (I have 21% crit chance). Each time I crit I get 5 rage. So getting rage while swiping and attacking and getting more rage by being beat on = as much rage as I need.

Against mobs of my level I have about 65% damage reduction (not counting demo roar). When you have like 5k HP and nearly 9k armor, mobs can barely touch you. I take something like 27-33 damage from standard melee attacks from mobs.

Then there's frenzied regeneration, healing 200 HP per second is insane. And means that I'm pretty much never in danger when fighting multiple mobs.

So all in all, I favour my bear form, but I still use cat for soloing the majority of the time.

Bear hands down.

I'm level 52 not 60. This is my experience so far.

I was a long-time cat fan (from lvl 20 til mid 30s), because of the DPS, stealth, and feline swiftness.

But then I started MT'ing, and I realized just how effective bear is at tanking. Bears are excellent at holding aggro, and they can take a pounding in dire bear form.

This game is centered around instancing and raiding. And a group with a good tank has a much easier time than one without a good tank.

Bears, esp feral-spec'd bears, make excellent tanks.

Bears have the same threat generation (130%) as a warrior in defensive stance. And most feral-spec druids will take at least 3/5 in Feral Instinct, boosting threat up to 139%.

My ability to hold aggro and survive is better than the majority of warriors that I've grouped with. Partly because of spec (most warriors are not prot-spec'd), partly because of gear, and partly because the majority of warriors don't understand threat mechanics. I see a lot of warriors trying to tank:
1. not in defensive stance, and/or
2. without a 1-h and a shield, and/or
3. without using threat-generating skills like Sunder Armor

Anyway, I've come to love tanking. The ability to keep a pack of mobs glued to you while your group members can attack is awesome.

I have read that cat form is not very good end-game. At that point, rogues far outshine us in terms of DPS. Cat DPS doesn't scale, whereas rogues continue to get higher and higher DPS weapons. And rogues can dump aggro better than we can.

I've read that bears can tank very well through most lower-to-mid level raids, until Naxx. At that point, warriors can get the Dreadnaught set, and by the numbers, warrior tanks have a clear advantage at that point. But getting to Naxx is a longgggg road.

If you haven't tried tanking, I recommend reading this awesome writeup from Hugehoss:
http://www.nightelfcollective.com/library/DruidBearTankingBasics

Bear tank FTW!
