staff is always good for restro druids... it has all the right stats usually... int, spi, stam, and healing... maces are better for bear druids because they're the ones with the usual str and stam stats for feraling (i don't know if i just made a word there)... or daggers for cats with their agi boosts athough i rarely see a dagger druid...

Actually, I'd say that strength > agi in cat form and agi > strength for bear. The reason ist that cat needs a lot of attack power and esecially with HotW (which I assume you are going for when you are mainly playing in cat form) strength is the way to go. You being only level 11, plan for your future and use them all. Rotate them around so as to keep them all leveling. You never know what you will end up with down the road.

That being said, for melee most druids tend to lean to 2handers. This usually being maces. This is because the druids nature, the stat distribution for melee tends to favor 2 hand maces for the heavier stat allocation than 1hander. There are some good melee 1 hand maces which can be acquired once you start raiding however. The only problem is that there is little for feral in ways of offhand.

But like I said you never know what you will use. A good staff with melee stats will suit you just as well, and the new feral weapon that everyone is drooling over is actually a staff.

Anyways, most of the time you will melee in cat or bear. But do take the time to duke it out in caster a bit in order to keep you weapon skill increasing. Later on you may have to use that skill while playing a casting role.

i just wonder what is the best feral weapon?

While leveling, for feral based gear find anything that is from the "of the Bear" line. Tiger(STR/AGI) and monkey(AGI/STA) are also suitable, though Bear (STR/STA) is the best bet for leveling. There is not best weapon while leveling. Just find suitable stats and go with it, you will outlevel everything before too long.

Once you start grouping more try and get some INT items too, for when you need to cast more.

Later you will have more gear options available. Like I said start with some good STR/STA items. Work from there. There are also plenty of threads listing numerous items available. Just do some research and go where you need to get them.
