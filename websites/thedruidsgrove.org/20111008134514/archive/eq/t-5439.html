Guild had a thread going on about this topic, kinda interesting read. So I thought I might ask here for your best newbie memories ! Here is my.

Well, my newbie experience is really wonderful; one that I won’t ever forget about it when thinking about EQ. Kane Bayle is my first server ever. I choose KB cause on the server select screen Kane Bayle was the first server on the population list. I thought cause Kane Bayle had the most people, so I choose it cause I really like servers with lot of people. =p The truth is kinda exactly the opposite. Anyways, I created a Wood elf Druid; I think I put most of my newbie points in int lol. I had totally no idea what I should do etc..

I had a different computer back then, one with some crappy Video card. Everything was pitch black, totally. After I created my character, I couldn’t see anything at all. I could see the chat windows and little bit of players. (Least I can tell if they moving or not =p) I had no idea how to use tells, ooc, shout, auction and others back then lol. Only channel I know how to use is /say. So I would use /say to ask for help, or hailing players for no reason. =p I think I even posted on EQ boards asking for help on the gamma problem. After spending a few hrs standing around I finally figured it out. Ok hmm iirc I learned how to use /shout and asked around for help lol. Some stupid questions I asked was like how to get spells and where to get money.

Back then, EQ was totally different. There were many new players and a lot of helpful ones. I got a few people willing to help me, one of them gave me 2 pp iirc. That was a lot for a newbie at that time. That player told me to get my spells using that 2 pp. I had 0 idea on where the heck I am supposes to get my spells. I had him showing me the way iirc. Anyways he taught me how to use tells =p I was going to ask him where the mobs are but then I fell off the platform on Kelethin. So I kinda found out where the mobs are =p

I traveled around for a bit. Totally like lost. I got so lucky that time, no orc pawns or any kos mobs saw me =p Somehow I got to Felwithe without dying! I zoned in and /shouted around for help cause I was lost. (How silly I was!) Then a gnome poped out and saying he is willing to help! I had no idea what gnomes looks like back then =p So I am like turning around and around looking for him. Lol I guess he figured out that I was a newbie and had no idea where he is. He told me to look down and I am like omg ! So short ! He helped me half way to Kelethin before I died and poped at my bindspot ! ( Lol my bindspot is right beside Kelethin and I had no idea!)

I played around for a few hrs and one of the spells require a fire beetle eye ! (How annoying was that ! No mobs in Greater Faydark like drop it ! ) I ooc’ed for help iirc and someone said it only drops in Freeport area. I had no idea where is that place so I asked around to see if anyone is willing to help me to get to Freeport! I got so lucky and a level 20 bard said he is willing to help me. So he picked me and a level 2 wizard up and zone’d into Butcherblock Mnts. I was like wow I never thought EQ is such a huge world. We took the 15 minutes boat to Freeport from Butcherblock ! After we arrived there then the bard left us. A year and a bit after, I still wondering what his name is =p Anywas, I got a beetle eye and some xp from the newbie zone. I got back to Kelethin somehow. Had some great times from then. Meet a lot of friends and had a ton of fun. From a raid attempt on Befallen to a successful 7 person raid on Emperor Crush when I was level 5. =p Most of my friends from when I was a newbie quit though, kinda sad =/ Newbie time is something to be proud of in my EQ career I guess !

Just the things that I still remember...


* Wandering around and actually making it alive to right near HHK back when I was level 4. Walking up to the three thieves near the town to hail them (thinking they were players) and not being alive anymore. Lost my leet newbie gear and my oh so important Tome of Discord.

* Thinking for the first few nights that if I killed too many goblins and orcs their people would, in fact, hunt me down. I stopped killing them for a while. Knew I could not survive the orc death squad that was surely on its way.

* Making it through Kithicor (which I actually didn't realize was a dangerous thing to do) and seeing the planes of the commonlands for the first time. Hard to imagine now but I was overwhelmed by the immersiveness and beauty of it...those really looked like lions! Look at those spiders! Look at that sky!

* Running all the way to everfrost because I wanted to see if there was really snow like the map suggested. Got there hmm around level 5 for the first time, but went back to Blackburrow to level up a bit before I spent any time (oh a lot of time) in Everfrost.

* Having a high level player buff me and not knowing what they had done. Worrying that I had been attacked (my only prior MMORPG experience was in UO so....).

* Dieing horribly to Froon or Broon or some such and having them sit on my corpse. Trusting a high level (level 12 omg!) enough to /consent them, back when consenting meant that you could loot anohter player's corpse. Yep, my tattered leather was ubah.

* Busting my halfling ass for faction so I could have faction with the Guardians of the Vale. Thinking that surely being a Guardian of the Vale would add the title after my name (ie like a guildname). And definitely wanting that uber rechargeable heal ring.

Oooh hooo mem-or-ies...like the corners of my mind....

Ty for the trip down that lane Noliniel. Very timely for me to be reflecting since I am kind of on the point of quitting the game now, and it is nice to remember the innocence of Oldoak's youth ; ).

I remember just starting out as a ranger in Greater Faydark. Someone cast a buff on me.. ah.. shield icon, ok, got it.
And I couldnt die, I kept on killing, and my red bar never went down. Wow, this is great.
Eventually, the buff faded. I had no clue how to check out what kind of buff this was (must have been skin like nature or something like that)
Some time later I get another buff, yay, that cute shield icon again, now I am invincible again. Well turns out, not all shield icon buffs are the same... hehe

Nearly crying after I lost a tarnished rapier on a corpse near orc hill, just couldn't find it in time (30min corpse timer below lv5 back then).

Standing at orc lift, I see this gnome in a distance, beeing chased by an orc. Pulling out my trusty bow I just bought for 2gp, I shot at the orc. Only to receive some mean tells about kill stealing, kiting. What the hell was he talking about ? I was just trying to help

Speaking of that tells he sent. He had a pretty long name, and I kept typing /tell Soandso everytime I replied..... later I found out about the reply key.

Never seen this blunder before., but I stayed in the newbie field a long time. Yay, those bats are finally green, and I can kill them easily now. Hmm... level 5 sure takes a long time.. wonder if thats one of those hell levels they kept talking about.
Took me a while to notice the lack of 'you gain experience'. I was so busy looking at everything, keeping my eye on the fight and possible adds, that I didnt notice really I didnt gain exp off them anymore :P

I would give a lot to get that intense feeling of innocent amazement back. Just beeing in the world... amazed at everything, not caring and knowing about stats, classes, nerfs, items. You know.... just beeing in a magnificent fantastic world.

Even though I dont play EQ anymore, I truly thank the DEV's for letting me have that great innocent fun.
I have played, and still playing, many other MMORGP, and though I am having fun, I never felt the feeling I had when I first stepped into the lands of Norrath.

My fondest newb memory is the tale of two rangers, a cleric, and a plan that actually worked...

those orcs just along the hill from the merchants in lesser fay, see, they needed to die. my friends gruffyd (ranger) and ghimkyllian (cleric) were just the folk to help make it happen. the problem was our plans for splitting, peeling, or attacking never seemed to work... and ususally resulted in our own deaths.

this time, the problem was, see, that big guy was mighty tough. the other four or five in the camp were no problem whatsoever, solo.... so we scout the camp... we find we can pull an oracle out solo.
dead orc
we find we can agro another through the tent, without actually seeing him.
dead orc
we scout some more... mind nobody has invis... we try ranger sneak.
dead ranger
we try to pull the corpse...
dead ranger
we try to pull both corpses...
dead cleric, but whew, got them both pulled back out of the camp.

so we discuss at length, while getting our bodies back (this was long before any zone had a roaming cleric able to rez...)

the plan was simple, we'd run though the camp (mind, nobody has sow), agro everyone to the merchants (and npc rangers nearby!), and gruffyd and ghim would peel the big guy off and work him over, while i helped (yeah, right) the npc's dispatch the rest of the pack.

ok, we dispatch the oracle near the tree again, the other two move to the far side of the camp and wait in ambush... a war cry is called, and within 2-3 minutes of fighting, the whole camp was dead, but we. yes, WE! had killed our prey.

ah, the good old days....
