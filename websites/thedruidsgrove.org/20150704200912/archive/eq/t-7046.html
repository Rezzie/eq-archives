I know it is not my choice, and there is no chance something like this would ever be implemented by the current development staff, but this is what I would do.

First of all I would restrict access to the Plane of Knowledge to those of 40 seasons and older. Perhaps even 45 seasons and older. Before that players really have no business going there, seeing that it is the stepping stone to areas that you can't access anyway until you are well advanced in seasons. In addition I would keep the same number of entrance points (though perhaps with more interesting graphics than a silly book), but I would make only a single return portal, that would function much like a gate.

Next I would give druids some more circles and rings to the new lands. Not everywhere, but enough to make it reasonably convenient to go most places.

Wizards would lose most of their current porting spells, but would gain a few to go to places where there is no good IC reason to have a druid ring. In exchange however, they would get a series of "blink" spells that are short distance, line of sight ports. As they grow more powerful with advanced seasons the wizard can learn to not only blink himself to some other place he can see, but he can also learn to blink other to him, blink group members to a different place and eventually blink strangers (after confirmation) and even monsters around for short distances either towards him or away from him.
Mages would get access to the same spells but at a later age, and not the most powerful ones.

Shamans, and possibly some other classes, would also get access to teleport rings so that no single class can have a monopoly on teleporting others. The idea is that even if druids can go to the most places of all classes, they can not go everywhere and for every place there are at least two classes that can teleport there. If we do not want a single class to monopolise healing we can not allow it for transporting either.

All this hopefully will encourage players to stay close to their starting locations and move through the progression of quests, rather than zipping around the world for the most convenient place to powerlevel. However, to help out the players creating their 50th character I would also give them the opportunity to create characters at 60 pct of the levels of their oldest character on the same server. Those characters would start with a reasonable learning for their age. I.e. as if they had been progressed normally, if quickly, through the seasons that got skipped. Probably even given a focus on certain skills over others so that not all have been maxed out for the level.
That would remove the need for much of the powerleveling that is both mindboringly numb and detrimental to the players who want to progress their characters normally. These characters would not get appropriate equipment since they would get twinked regardless, and we might as well prevent even more items and money from entering the game. Given that Sony seems so intent on trivialising the game for all but the most powerful characters they migh as well go the whole way and entirely remove the low end game for those so inclined.

One last thing I would do, though that has even less to do with teleporting, is to remove the experience bonus from all zones. If a zone can not attract players on its own merit then it is time to reevaluate the content, not to artificially make it more attractive in a way that only attracts powerlevelers and actually devaluates the game for other players.
