Druid aa have to be examined in light of what role you fill. If you are mainly soloing then you are going to want to maximize your solo killing ability: dot crits if you root rot, nuke crits if you quad etc. If you are usually a healer for your groups or raids...then healing aa jump way up the list.

For raiding druids MGB and silent casting are important, for nonraiders they are weak buys. Would swing from top ratings to bottom ratings for these kind of aa.


To really rate druid aa would require a split rating system. The aa that any druid should have, such as SCM etc. The aa that no one should buy until they are running out of aa to buy, such as packrat etc. And then all the hard ones in the middle that would have to be evaluated for the different roles we druids fill.

It could certainly be done, but it would be a fairly complicated process for druids since we do so many different things.
