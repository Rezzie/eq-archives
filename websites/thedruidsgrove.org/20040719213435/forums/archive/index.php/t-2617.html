I would start with advising to get 65 first of course, but I don't think it was the point of your question :p

There are already a few topics about AA choices, so you can prolly find good information there.

It depends a lot on whether you want to optimize your character first or have fun. If you just want to have fun, just take whatever you want really, in the end it will be the same anyway. If you really want to make sure that you make the best choice though, get SCM3 first. Getting SCM1 alone isn't really worth it until you plan to get the 2nd and the 3rd afterwards. And SCM3 is really great, the best AA we can get.

I don't think that spell casting fury 1 is worth it, to be honest I really started seeing a fair amount of criticals with fury of magic 1 - I stopped there because I think that the number of points required afterwards is too high. SCF1 is very disappointing, criticals are just very very rare. Same for SCF2.

Healing adept works on TR and KR, healing gift only on fast heals like nature's touch and nature's infusion. Both HA and HG work with nature's infusion and nature's touch.

I got both. Healing adept first then healing gift. Healing adept is nice but I totally fell in love with healing gift. The problem is that it's pretty useless until you get nature's infusion in my opinion. That spell is I think the best reason to get 63, and once you have all those healing AAs maxxed healing more than 4k in less than 4 seconds is wonderful.

That being said, I'm almost sure than criticals (heals and nukes) do not give more aggro than a normal cast. But NI is pretty bad aggro by itself anyway :p

In your case, I would get healing adept first, and not worry about getting healing gift until you get 63 and nature's infusion.

"SCM is decent, although I have yet to decide if it really gives you mana saving per cast or just the 30% extra chance of a specialisation as it states."

Every person who has ever tested this has come to the same conclusion: SCM1/2/3 saves an extra 2/5/10% of the base mana cost of the spell, each and every time.

In addition, pre-AA, everyone who ever tested spec savings concluded that there never was a "chance" to get the spec savings. You get the same fixed mana savings each and every cast, depending on your spec level in that skill. A spec of 50 saves about 3% of the base, and a spec of 200 saves about 11%.

Many archived threads can be found here, at Graffe's, and other boards detailing the testing methodology carried out.

I can't, FOR THE LIFE OF ME, figure out why after all this time, SoE hasn't updated the description of these AAs yet.
