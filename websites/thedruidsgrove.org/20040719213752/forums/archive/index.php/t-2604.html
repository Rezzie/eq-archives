Aquila, I'd go for MC1, SoTW3, SCF3, FoM3, HA3 and then re-evaluate my destination.

SoTW3 is the final stage of the druid group heal. On big pulls its frequently the only thing between living and dying. Your AC jumps up, your DPS jumps up, and your entire party regens 250hp/tick for about a minute depending on your AA's and gear. That mob cast an AE nuke? This is your AE heal. You can really feel the power of this AA fighting a mob like Aerian Darr (sp?) in PoValor with a raid. Position yourself in the pile of waiting melee's and hit this with MGB as the first AE goes off. You just healed the entire raid for that event. Sure you mostly group, but AD is a raid mob a lot of grouping people have done...

RC3 the final stage of our group cure. RC1 worked just as well. Spotty piece of crap skill I'd like a refund on. Pureblood and RGC/RC all the way.

MC2, MC3 This increases your ability to handle pulls by nuking, dotting or healing. Oh joy, sure it takes 2 more points of mental clarity 200 ticks to get that next nukes worth of mana up. It reduces rebuff time and works pretty good on corpse runs. This is a priority after you get your 15 points of flowing thought gear.

SCF3/FoM3 Having to do this over, I wouldn't have gone past FoM1. SCF3 though is important, you'll crit approximately 7 percent of the time and there's always damage to be done so you make use of it. I prefer this one of the crit healing AA because its so much more effictive. With FoM3 I crit about 13 percent of the time. This equates 1 crit every 7 nukes. Or in a bad situation about 370 mana free every 10 ticks or FT 37ish. (370 mana for a nuke, 8.5 second cycle time * 7 nukes = 60 seconds or 10 ticks) Sure that doesn't include SCM3, Specializing, or MP4, but its a starting point. (those all reduce the FT#, to say FT20ish, still a friggin bargain)

HA3 Percentage heal increase, this is a good AA. I did it later, after ND3/CA3 and LR5. I felt after finishing off FoM3, I needed more survivability in POP.

ND3 Increases your hp. This is a raiding druids AA to me. A grouping druid will hit evac rather than stand and take it. If your not raiding that extra 150hp or so you gain is not worth it.

HG3 Critical Heals. I'm about to buy the third rank of this to finish off all my archetypes. I quite honestly, do not like this AA. Why? Player Characters have less hp than NPC's. I see your toon get about 2k in damage I'm hitting natures infustion, your healed. If it crits, that extra is lost. Most toons you can't wait until they need 4k in a heal as their in the bloodlust range then. This AA really could use some improvement, IE a hp check to see if the target needs a 4k heal and double the percentage chance it'll fire. But NOOOOOOOO. Lets make it lame and random it when they only need a 500 hp heal. I can count the number of times on one hand a crit heal saved mana in the last 2 months let alone prevented a wipe.

Planar Power/Innate Enlightenment - These two skills increase your hp, defense skills, and mana pool. The decision between them is easy. Look at your stats. If more than your wisdom is maxed out when you have your typical buff stack, go Planar Power. It will increase your survability with more hp, agility, dexterity. If your wisdom is the only one capped, go Innate Enlightenment. Every point of Planar Power = 5 wis or about 25 mana. Every point of Innate enlightenment = 10 wis or about 50 mana. Combined you get about 375 more mana, or a heal/nuke.

Metabolism - This is an underated AA. I routinely use stat food and forget to force feed myself. Misty Thicket Picnics run like 8pp each on thule so I just spend the cash. So this AA saves me cash.

Nuke Haste - I already overaggro when nuking, doing it faster just means I die more. This AA only saves like 10 percent of the casting time on slow nukes. You can easily aquire 18 percent nuke haste in the bazaar and spend those AA's on something that is not storebought.

How do you easily get 18% nuke haste in the bazaar as a druid? I don't see any tradeable Speed of Solusek items...

Keep in mind spell haste from an item and spell haste from AA's stack. Quick Damage is (slightly) more effective if you have a spell haste item already, because the percentages are of the base cast time. (Taken to an extreme, ignoring caps: suppose you had 80% spell haste on an item, and you were casting a 10 second nuke. Your cast time would be 2 seconds, plus the 2.25 sec recast time. Quick Damage would take off another 10% of the base cast time of 10 seconds, for a resulting cast time of 1 second. This lets you nuke 30% more often.) That said, quick damage probably shouldn't be a priority. I got mine around AA 300; doing it again, I might bump it up to 200 or so, but no further.

For spirit of the wood, the gains between level 1 and level 3 aren't huge. (Same goes for radiant cure.) Level 1 is a top priority, but I'm not sure level 2 and 3 should be.

As for ND3...maybe I'm in the minority here, but I made hp a very high priority soloing and grouped as well. I guess it's largely because I spent a lot of time charming in both situations. I bought ND3 pretty early on.
