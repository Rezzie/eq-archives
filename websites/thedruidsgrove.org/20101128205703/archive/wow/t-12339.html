The change I recently made was with the use of Maul. Maul does not trigger the global cool-down and will activate (be expended) on the next normal hit. Previously I tended to use it only as part of the pull, now it gets used much more frequently.

What I do now (in infinite rage situations such as bosses) is use Maul as soon as it has been expended. Use Mangle whenever it's available and lacerate whenever the GCD allows. The maul will quite happily sit there waiting to be used and will not be affected by the Mangles or Lacerates.

I would estimate that it increased my threat by 10%-20%. The only actual figures that I can quote are from the damage I was doing. Previously 50%-55% of my damage was 'normal hits' not that has dropped to 25% with Maul being 35%.

For non-boss mobs I can't do this so much as I tend to run out of rage.

Maul is a rage-nerfer, to be sure.

The problem arises because a lot of the rage a bear generates comes from their white damage. When using maul, the white damage attacks become yellow damage maul attacks. And when the maul hits, you don't gain rage in the same way that you do when you hit a mob with white damage.
Instead you even lose some rage - Maul costs rage too.

So it's best to only use maul if you have plenty of rage. If rage is not abundant, lacerate and mangle are your best bet.

I only Maul if I have well over 50 rage. As soon as it drops below 50, I stop Maul'n. Even in times of excessive rage, using Maul every time it is available can suck the rage out of you. So, use Maul as needed, but keep an eye on the rage bar so you know when to stop.

I guess this plays into the "too much mitigation" discussion. At some point, you could have too much mitigation such that your rage generation drops and resulting in a loss of TPS (can't use maul) and dead healers/DPS'rs.
