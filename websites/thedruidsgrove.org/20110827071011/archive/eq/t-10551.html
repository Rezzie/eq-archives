From what I read on the Steel Warrior, shielding takes a straight percentage off the minimum damage (hit amount = minimum + {1..20} * multiplier). I haven't done a controlled test, but just from observation it's obvious that shielding isn't a straight percentage off the total amount -- e.g., I noticed that 7% shielding only reduces max damage by 2%. Therefore, the more you get hit for the minimum, the stronger the effect, up to a theoretical maximum of just under the % amount listed on the armor piece.

This is why it's normally much better for plate tanks... to really make shielding worth it you need the mitigation and ac to move the avg hit from mobs towards the lower end of the hit range spectrum consistantly. Taking consistant dmg also works to the same effect.

It's something that's probably well to have but unless you specifically MT a lot in grps / off raids it's not something I'd focus to hard on... AC, avoidance,and defensive aa's (and oaken guard) work better towards general surviability on raids.
