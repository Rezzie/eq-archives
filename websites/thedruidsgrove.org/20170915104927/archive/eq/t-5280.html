Well, I was going through the Recent Updates section of the EQLive site, and was surprised to see all that I had skipped in my hasty reading of the patch message.

There's a ton of fixes in there, kudos! Love 'em! Some could be construed as nerfs though (for certain play styles).

The ones I noticed the most are...

1)
POM Revamp
Many people liked it the way it was (or at least the exclusiveness of it). I'm mostly indifferent right now. Gotta see what it's like with the change.

2)
Merchants now separate items with different charges in their list. For example, 9 dose and 10 dose potions will show up as separate items on the merchant's list. Items with the same amount of charges will be stacked. NOTE: This will remove the ability to "recharge" items by selling them to a vendor and buying them back.

No more item recharging through vendors. I must admit, I've got an item that I would semi-regularily recharge. But, I suppose we can't have it both ways. Can't sell items with different charges as a merchant in the bazaar AND expect to recharge items on vendors... if they use the same code.

Still, seems like they removed a Plat sink when it seems they are looking for ways to create more Plat sinks.

:eek: Now there's an idea! Make a vendor that will recharge items for Plat!

Anyway... one last one:
3)

Frenzied Burnout will no longer last indefinitely on a charmed pet.
I personally figured that the "most powerful pet class" should have this particular ability left in place. I heard that the only lingering components were the flurry and rampage, and that the haste 'buff' actually did drop correctly. /shrug
