I recently made a post on SoE forums about how damage spell lines don't progress at high enough rate to keep their relative power through new levels.

http://forums.station.sony.com/eq/po...opic_id=116964 (http://forums.station.sony.com/eq/posts/list.m?topic_id=116964)

For druids, this is relevant because all our damage spells are from old spell lines except for NBW and Spurs. Even though Winter's Flame is a new line, it is balanced off of our normal nukes, so that doesn't count.

Personally, when I went all out vs a mob in TSS (before NBW came out), I did about 40% more dps than I did under comparible conditions in OoW. On the same token, warriors (a melee class that didn't recieve any dps disc upgrades between OOW and TSS) do about 175% more dps nowadays than they did in OoW.

Back to casters -- The reasons why (ie) wizards and necros are able to do double+ the dps nowadays than they did during OOW is because they often get new spell lines added, which boosts their damage greatly. If necros and wizards were reliant on old spell lines to produce damage, their damage would be subpar.

If other people agree, maybe further investigation can be done.
