Not sure if it's ever been done before, but for my final epic fight, we killed the spawned Faydedar with three people...me, a 62 cleric, and an uber 65 warrior. Not too surprising really, but just thought I'd mention that it can be done easily with the right characters.

In fact, I didn't really do jack in the fight. I landed two heals, but the cleric ended with 40m anyway-and he tried a few nukes (that didn't land). However, we were using buffs from my alt enchanter. So it was really two people, with buffs from two more. In fact, the tank was two boxing the cleric.

Before spawning Faydedar, we put 8 or 9 junk buffs on each raider, buffed with KEI, VoG, and GRM, and cleric and druid buffs. The tank had two different insta buffs things he was using to keep his buffs up. His important buffs lasted the duration of the fight (a few minutes). The cleric had a horse, and some FT (not sure how much).

Neither me nor the cleric could land any damage. I gave up trying to dot after three tries or so. Fay was not debuffed at all the whole fight.

For those on BB, the tank was Zeniff, from Ring of Valor...thanks again Z!

To add some more info to setups you can use for fay:

We did it tonight with the following,
62 monk (me)
61 druid (me)
61 shaman
61 bard

None of us are in a guild or uber my any means but decent gear for our levels.

Was easy and no one was close to dying. Bard was definately the key, the ae's were probably greater than 90% resisted or uneffective so was basically a melee fight.

Bard used puretone disc and everyone's resists were over 250 in fr/mr. Never got feared and didnt take much damage from ae nuke.

I mini ch'd with druid and ended fight with 80% mana still with kei. Shaman was unable to land slow so he used pet and spot heal.

Just some more data on what you might need to take him down these days.
