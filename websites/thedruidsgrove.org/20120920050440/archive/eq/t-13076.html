In my experiance, what happens is this.

I will use Uqua as my example

Say you have Aura of Destruction in your first slot.
In some slot under that, you have tash.

Aura of Destruction has the following counters:
<table class="spellview" cellpadding="3" width="95%"><tbody><tr><td class="spelllabel" align="right">10: </td> <td>Increase Poison Counter by 99</td></tr> <tr><td class="spelllabel" align="right">11: </td> <td>Increase Poison Counter by 99</td></tr> <tr><td class="spelllabel" align="right">12: </td> <td>Increase Poison Counter by 99</td></tr></tbody></table>
Resplendant Cure applies all 25 poison counters to Aura of Destruction.

Since this is not enough to cure Aura of Destruction, no remaining counters carry over to try and cure Tash.

What I typically do to avoid this, is load a instaclick in my initnal slot.

Once you zone in, and the zone wide AE goes off it should be in your last slot.

Click off the Instaclick buff, so tash will always land in the top of the buff bar, and thus curable without having to cure Aura of Destruction.

Its been awhile since I have done Bloodeye, but it is likely a similar issue.

-Fenier
