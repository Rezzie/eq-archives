Flip through the pages on this forum, there are lots but most are based on being lvl 60 and fighting 1v1.

Group pvp is much trickier because you have to decide when tog et into bear form, and when to shift and heal your partner. And when you do shift to heal, you have to make sure you don't get interrrupted/killed before your heal gets off.

The basic rule is stay in bearform as much as possible, when you need to heal use bash on your enemy, then shift and cast regrowth, then shift back to bear right away. If you have improved bash you can do all of this while he's still stunned :)

Druid is all about adapting to the situation. If the enemies start focusing on you (which is likely if you are the main healer) then throw up barkskin and go bear, your friends wont be taking any damage and so should make short work of the other players. If they don't go for you then stay in caster and heal/tangle/ff/mf, basicly keep your friends alive. If you have a priest in your group then you can go cat and have some fun :). I find most of the time a rogue goes for my throat in group pvp. Best thing to do then is to use natures grasp and keep the rogue out of the fight.

I wouldn't worry about healing your group in PvP unless you have swiftness up or everyone is ignoring you. Best tactic for helping out your group in PvP is rooting one of their melee classes, and going bear to occupy one of their rogues/warriors while the rest of your group burns down their priests/casters.

As for rogues, sword specced rogues will always be a problem due to their damage output, if you can get a root off an keep him rooted (after he pops vanish) you should be able to beat him. Get stuck in caster form an cheapshotted/stun locked though, you're gonna have it tough no matter what happens.
