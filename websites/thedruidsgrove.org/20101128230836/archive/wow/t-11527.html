Anything that says "Chance on hit:" or enchants who does that (crusader, lifesteal, fiery etc etc) will not work in feral forms.
Stuff that says "Equip:" does work in feral forms, even if it has a "chance on succsesful melee attack to..." part.
An example is the Darkmoon Card trinkets, Warlords which basically is chance on hit to heal yourself, or elementals which is chance on hit to inflict nature dmg.

As for the actual answer to the original question, no enchant weapon impact/striking has no effect on feral forms. Feral damage is given a defined damage and weapon speed that is only affected by your stats such as ap, agi, str. The speed and damage of your weapon make no difference in feral damage.

The enchant you speak of ups the overall damage of the weapon when it is swung. It is not a chance on hit proc. It is a constant damage increase to use of the weapon while in caster form.

However some nice enchants to put on a (2H) weapon would be the new AP to weapon (90) or 35 agility. I prefer agility as it adds crit, ac, ap, and dodge in cat as well as crit, dodge, and ac in bear.
