From your magelo it would seem your farming time regluarly. The next step for most guilds is Ikkinz, assuming your following a more common raid progression.

Spirit of the Grove is very useful healing wise when maxed. Depending on targets and how you handle those targets will determine how often you use it as a AE Heal however.

I am a huge fan of defenisve AA. If you are moving into upper content avoiding a single attack could save you 1500+ Hitpoints. It is very possiable you will still die, however the extra levels help in people patching you/letting a tank take aggro.

Since you claim to do healing and dps, I would probly finish out DF3 first - just to have it finished, then move onto SoTG/RM.

Extra Buff slots may or may not be a huge issue for you, depending on which buffs you have, and how many clicky buffs you tend to use.

I only have 350 or so aa, but I would suggest getting at least up to SOTG1 and getting RC5 or 6 to help with the cures of AEs on raids for raiding purposes, also, since many times, mgb ostw/sotg chains are used, if you only have wood and the sotg from the previous druid has not fully faded, then you waste yours. I find that for many AA that have multiple levels, I find it better to get all the levels of it then move on to the next one. Of course, there are times you want to get another FT, or stat boost and get just 1 level at a time.

If you raid a lot, then I would do these (you stated that you mainly heal, so you should put the emphasis surviving AEs, and on curing and healing):

chaotic potential (only if you have the sta gear to get the hps)

more buff slots

resplendent cure 3 (the more your guild progresses, the more you will need this)

quickened curing 3 is useful, and will shave 54 seconds off the reset timer.

SotW3 is fine, you really don't need higher levels until you pick up other AAs.

Get the defensive AAs that follow lightning reflexes.

Finally, I would pick up crits near the end.
