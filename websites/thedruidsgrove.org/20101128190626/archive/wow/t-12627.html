For me the druid made it hard for me to play other classes. Most other classes feel like they are dumbed down compared with the versatility of the druid. Some of the key points:

1. Who else can, tear a mobs face off, switch to tank and then in a pinch pop out and heal the entire group before they wipe.?????

2. Druids can spec one of three ways with three distinctly different play styles.... all of which are greatly desired by raids.

3. Who else can have 50% of my bag slots filled with extra gear I keep for when I raid????? AND I MEAN LITERALLY 50%. In the bank and on my character. I have sets for damned near everything....

Some things to bear in mind :p (pun intended)
1. Druids 1-20 is PAINFUL. You get only caster form till lvl 10. The NE starting area can be a pain if you are alliance.
2. Druid 10-20 is a pain because you only have bear form (oh and plucked demon chicken form at 16) Killing things takes time and because of your quests for curing poisons and other things you'll have to be subjected to the NE lands longer if you are alliance.
3. For feral, lvl 20 is a defining moment. Once you get cat form, things start to DIE FAST. Initially they don't have a ton of abilities, but by lvl 30, you'll have 90% of your core killing abilities.
4. Mangle IS WORTH IT. As soon as you can get it, GET MANGLE if you are going Feral. Don't listen to those people who say to get Omen of Clarity first or something else. Everyone I've told to get Mangle by 50 have suddenly looked at me and said OMG is Mangle insane!!!!!!

Mangle's 1.6xAP vs Claw's 1.0xAP means Mangle quickly separates from Claw as you get more AP, but that doesn't happen until you get some nice Outland gear. By, then, you'll have OoC.

So, at lvl 50-60, is an extra claw (from OoC) > than Mangle without OoC? I say yes.
