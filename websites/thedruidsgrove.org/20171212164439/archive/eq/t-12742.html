It was a warping mob... the way the path across the bridge can be tricky. If you pull them such that they path off the ledge they'll either pop back on bind or pop on you. If you run to far ahead of them while running down the bridge I believe what happens is you seperate the vertical distance too much and it thinks you're trying to use an exploit / get into a safe spot so it warps onto you.

Normally when I do those for the first mob I'll stand to the left side of the bridge (so it doesn't try to cut across gap and fall off) and cast immo to get it's attention then run down the bridge... about half way I'll turn around to make sure it's not gonna run off and start to try and get snare on before it catches up. You have to get used to it as you'll have broken line of sight depending on where the 2 of you are.

I have done a little soloing of these guys, and have figured out a science to the pull. Essentially, the mob must follow you painstakingly across the bridge in order to avoid a warp.

If you walk the mob off the bridge, it will warp onto you. This is so regardless of where you are standing. If you are levitating, and walk even slightly off the edge of the bridge, even for a second, the mob will warp to you, hit you, and fall to the floor beneath. When it hits the ground, the mob will then warp to your location again, and slap you around some more. Good luck surviving a string of hits over 1k in damage :)

The only safe way I have found to pull them is to ensure both you, and the mob, are on the bridge at the same time. As it's a zigzag bridge, this limits the amount of distance you can put between you.

Having said all that, I can't solo there reliably because the casters are too tough. They gate when you get them close to dead, and I can't come up with a way of reliably stopping this (while solo).

Another tip, gating mobs won't gate if near their bind, so if at all possible... fight them rooted right next to or on top of their bind point. If the mob is bound right next to 3 other mobs, either be prepared to root CC all of them (it's fun sometimes, try it!) or don't pull it. Patience really pays off on pulls.
