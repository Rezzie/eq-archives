Well - the issue is quite frankly the current arena ruleset is in theory against the entire ideal design of the druid class.

Limited room to kite, burst dmg, cd based etc etc...

Also group utility wise what we offer in a raid (lotp, moonkin aurora, tree form aurora with hots) isn't as viable in arenas.

Hot's are VERY VERY good now - especially lifebloom and from my resto friends we are a bit more viable in arenas now due to that and obviously cyclone.

But even as a druid if you really think about it - why would you want to bring us to a 5v5 instead of a rogue with sap/blind? Warrior with ms? mage with sheep?

Arenas are the epitomy of min/max and thus as a hybrid we do get hurt by that ideal.

NOW - that is not to say druids are totally useless in arenas. I generally do 2v2 and 3v3's because we are a bit better in that smaller setting (and I don't like being just a cyclone bot in 5v5)

Rogue/Druid combo is nasty.
Warlock/Druid combo is also nasty.

My highest raiting was around 2k with those setups - granted after I got my gladiators maul we started getting alts in the team to gear them up on the side so the rating dropped fast.

The druid is a great class - but the arenas as I said is just designed soley around min/max group setups and thus as a hybrid designed the way we are - we suffer from it.

Although with the new lifebloom buff I am hearing good things about hots from my friends who are resto druids in arena teams.

The issue I have with hots though is arena gameplay it totally based off Burst DPS. So while the hot regen atm is very insane it is still not enough to counter combo's like 2 AP/Pom/Pyro mages etc etc etc. Add into that fact our primary heal healing touch even talented is still a 3 second cast.

The same problem remains though and the OP is correct. Competitive teams don't even have to kill the resto druid healer right off the bat - although many do.

The only real defense a resto druid has is bear form. Barkskin is nice but doesn't cut it alone.

Sick a high dps melee class on the resto druid and make him go into bear - at that point his usefullness to that group and healing ability goes from 100% to zero since in bear he can't heal himself or his group mates at all and loose 2/3 of his total abilities.

Only takes one well played rogue , feral druid or even a hunter to totally shut down a resto druids healing ability and make him go into bear while your other team mates have their way with the rest of his team.

Sadly enough that's how easy it is to counter a resto druid in arenas.

Now I'm not saying it isn't possible to do well in the arena as a resto druid at all. But what I am saying is the odds are stacked against you moreso then other healing classes who have it MUCH easier.
