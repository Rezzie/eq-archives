Levelling is easy as long as you're either (1) good at soloing, or (2) can find people to hunt with on a regular basis. As you get closer to level 85 you should find it easier to get pickup groups. Use the LFG window! When you can't find a group, try charming--specifically, charming a pet, sending it to attack another mob, breaking charm when it's low on health and then finishing it off with a nuke or two.

For UI modifications, the best resource is EQInterface: http://www.eqinterface.com/downloads/index.php

Slow is still mitigated on nearly all mobs, but it's quite useful anyway--just not as critical as it used to be.

Our level 76+ heals and nukes have been boosted substantially, and we have several new tricks. Our healing power has never been as close to that of a cleric as it is now. We finally got a respectable group heal, plus an emergency group heal that only works on people that are low on health. Level 76+ nukes have a short cast time, with a longer recast to make up for it--this allows us to alternate between nukes if we want to burn a mob down quickly.

Maps: http://www.mapfiend.net
Quests: http://everquest.allakhazam.com, http://home.comcast.net/~mathadon/Guides/index.html, http://mobilis.wordpress.com/

As for AAs being easier to grind at 70 or higher levels, it depends on finding people to hunt with. It's hard to say. The time needed to get an AA is going to vary significantly, but I'll go with 15-20 minutes (not counting Lesson of the Devoted) as a typical rate. It is possible to get AAs much more quickly than this in a good group.

Important AAs that you don't have: Gift of Mana, Shield Block, Quickened Radiant Cure, final rank of Healing Adept, final rank of Mnemonic Retention, additional ranks of Healing Gift and Radiant Cure, Fortified Survival (once you're level 78), Shared Camouflage, Entrap, Spirit of the White Wolf (at least the first rank). Other recommendations might depend on play style: for example, if you find yourself charm soloing a lot, Mastery of Nature will be important.

I don't think you can get the kind of countdown timers you're looking for. However, if you click on a grayed out ability you'll typically get a message that tells you how much time is left. Clickable items with reuse timers are displayed with a portion of the box shaded, giving you an idea of how long you have to wait (as a fraction of the total reuse time).
