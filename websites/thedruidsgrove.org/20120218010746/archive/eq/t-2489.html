Well, I still think the fastest is the hybrid approach. Your best efficiency mana-using spell is FoK, BY A MILE. But, free DoTs are free DoTs =) So quad til low on mana, and then root/rot the rest with free DoTs only. When approaching 80-85% mana, start rounding up more.

And regarding the horse, the hybrid approach still allows you the medding benefit while free-rotting during med time, and if you lead the mobs far enough while quadding, you will catch a med-tick while casting FoK. If you look at a full cycle (from starting the roundup of mobs through quadding, through root-rotting the rest, and back to gathering), you are probably catching 60-70% of the ticks standing still, so getting the med benefit. If just root-rotting, you still have to move once in a while to loot, or to go get more mobs if no wanderers come by to keep you busy. so you get the med benefit probably 80-90% of the time. Not THAT big of a difference.

As I said, for where I'm at, I max out at 29 mobs/hour (at toilers) just multi-dotting and using WD on the mobs, but can easily do 36 mobs/hour using the "quad + free DoTs only" approach.

Relevant specs, for comparison:

Level 60, 3650 mana pool (including KEI), epic, DoD bracer, MP3 focus item, EA3 focus item (WD and DoD get an extra tick), SCM1+2, MC1, FT1, PotG, MotH, horse with models off, Evoc specced. I have no SCF AAs.

Granted, quadding efficiency is much lower for Alter specced, and of course, the 3 or 4 Conjuration Druids in EQ do a LOT better root-rotting =)
