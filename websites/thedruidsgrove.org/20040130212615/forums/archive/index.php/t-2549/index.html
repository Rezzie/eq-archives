Bismuth
WotW certainly isn't our best AA, but it might help to put it into perspective. If you take a look at the mana:damage ratios on one of our PoP nukes, the damage produced from WotW3 is equivalent to three or four points of flowing thought (assuming you continually have a target on which to apply it).

For a druid in the business of dealing damage, 9AA's invested into WotW3 will be more effective than spending those same points into Mental Clarity.

Of course, extra points of flowing thought are a bit more versatile for how the mana can be used. Anyway, I think people are coming down on this skill a bit too hard, since just about every skill after the core few (SCM3, SotW3, perhaps ND3, etc.) are about as marginal an upgrade as this one is. It really comes down to what your playstyle is and how many points you want to invest into one skill.

It's great for any sort of farming, including soloing VT shards, tradeskills, or plan ol' plat farming. I also use it to finish off MoBs that I am root/rotting and they're almost dead, and all my DoTs wear off. Instead of reapplying DoTs, I just walk to them and they finish themself on my Wotw. I also notice that, as always, tanks appreciate a 650 hit out of the blue.
