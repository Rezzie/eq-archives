As of the 3.0.8 patch, this quest is optional. You can still do it if you'd like, or you can learn Aquatic Form from any druid trainer at level 16.

This quest is from the same NPC as the first two druid quests, Mathrengyl Bearwalker in Darnassus, and can be started at level 16.

Mathrengyl yet again sends you to Dendrite Starblaze in Moonglade. Dendrite will first ask you to find a bauble at the bottom of Lake Elune'Ara, and then take it to the Shrine of Remulos and use it. The bauble doesn't actually look like a bauble ... it's more like a wooden block (http://www.thedruidsgrove.org/wow/forums/attachment.php?attachmentid=154) or something. It's also a bit hard to see, you'll have a 30 minute time limit, and may be competing against other druids looking for it. Having fun yet? :) Be sure to look in the deeper parts of the lake, where the bottom slopes down into little hollows. (You can see the locations of the Shrine and Dendrite on the Moonglade map (http://www.thedruidsgrove.org/wow/forums/attachment.php?attachmentid=153).)

After finding and using the bauble, talk to Tajarri by the shrine. She'll send you to find the two halves of the Pendant of the Sea Lion, and tell you to ask townsfolk in Nighthaven for clues. The two griffon masters will each tell you the location of one of the half-pendants.

The first part, Half Pendant of Aquatic Agility, can be found in Darkshore. See the Darkshore map (http://www.thedruidsgrove.org/wow/forums/attachment.php?attachmentid=142) for its location (the glowing dot). The box containing the pendant is between the northernmost two fangs. You'll probably have to fight a couple level 17-18 threshers, but this half is overall pretty easy. (If you're using a map addon, the loc is 49,11.)

The second part, Half Pendant of Aquatic Endurance, is in Westfall, and a bit trickier to get to. Swim out into the water near the location marked on the map, and be sure to watch out for the reef sharks that can be swimming nearby. Eventually, you'll find a ledge with a bubbly fissure that will allow you to catch your breath (it's also just inside the fatigue area). The pendant is in a chest right next to the fissue, as noted by the Westfall map (http://www.thedruidsgrove.org/wow/forums/attachment.php?attachmentid=141). (If you're using a map addon, the loc is 18,33.) Ignore the ship; the pendant used to be near it, but no longer.

With both halves of the pendant in hand, head back to the Shrine of Remulos and use one of the halves to combine them. Take the completed pendant back to Dendrite, and then back to Mathrengyl in Darnassus. He'll give you aquatic form and a pretty nifty belt (http://wow.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?witem=16608)!

Please note that you need to be a registered user to view the attachments!

I finished up this quest yesterday and wanted to add a couple of things. I got a loc for the fissure, but not one for the chest (sorry). I'll add that when I get home, it's on a screenshot. One thing to note, the fissure is pretty small, and it's very, very deep under the ocean. It's not quite the bottom of the ocean, there's lots of valleys under the water - the fissure was located more along the top of a lip rather than the bottom of the valley. You'll also get a "popup" if you mouse over the proper fissure - it should say something like "bubbly fissure". There are no mobs in this part of the ocean.

I had a priest along with me, so I wasn't too concerned about dying.

Like Yrys said, what you're looking for is a huge boat (like the ocean crossers). Staring out to the west, the boat should be directly down and on your left (south) if you're at the fissure. The boat looks like a crash, the front of the boat is inside of the valley wall and the back is kind of tilted a bit more upwards. The chest is towards the back of the boat and looks like one of the lockboxes, not a full sized "battered chest".

If you're swimming at normal speed and no breathing enhancers, you cannot make it from the fissure to the box, unlock the box, and back to the fissure. However, you should be able to swim close enough to the fissure that if you have a rez'er, they can reach and rez you (apparently priests cannot rez while fatigued, so they have to be able to rez you from the "protection" of the fissure or other non-fatigue water spots). You can't take more than about three "ticks" of drowning damage - it's a lot like the old EQ drowning - fast and deadly. Best bet is to loot the chest and TP:Moonglade or have some of the alchemy swim+breathing potions on you.

I believe the pendants were 750xp and then the spell awarding itself was 1150xp.
