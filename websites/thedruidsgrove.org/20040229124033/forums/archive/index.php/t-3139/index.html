Tudamorf
Duradam says: When the two are adveraged together, taking acount of how much time a druid does each, whats the result in terms of FT

Depends. In a fast-paced group, you will probably be sitting less than half the time due to casting and aggro issues. Here is where the horse really rocks, because you can just stand all the time and meditate without worrying about aggro.

Solo root/DoT, the other end of the spectrum, you'll sit maybe 90% of the time, so you'll see less benefit to the horse. Solo charming is somewhere in between the two.

But most of all, a horse (used to) be a great liberator, because you never had to worry about sitting or standing. This is a huge benefit when you're watching TV or a movie, catching up on some reading, or playing another character at the same time. Without the horse, I would just end up standing all the time to avoid the attention necessary to hit the sit button at just the right time.
TeriMoon
This is just me probably, but I find I use the horse much less when soloing or duoing with charm, simply because I need to be able to react quickly when charm wears off, and this is more difficult to do with a horse. Other than that, I love it. I don't need to worry about sitting/standing, and I can avoid much of the aggro associated with sitting, so its much easier to switch out spells, etc.

Its something one adapts to the longer they use it, and finds the right circumstances for its use, according to individual playing styles. You really almost have to try it to see if you like it.
