Improved Innate Run Speed
This ability will slightly modify your base run speed. This modification does NOT stack with movement rate spell effects.

Is it just me, or does anyone else feel like their class-based movement buffs are being continually marginalized, almost trivialized, by Innate Run Speed AAs?

It was bad enough that two plate classes gained 'steed' AAs which allow them to travel almost DOUBLE the rate of speed granted by our best movement buffs. Further disappointing how that rate of speed could literally be bought with platinum by any of the remaining classes (or obtained for free, through bridles dropped by several luclin mobs).

Maybe I'm oversensitive to this because it's part of a larger trend in which Druid abilities have been made available to other classes. For example, Teleportation (as a class attribute) has suffered decreased significance with the introduction of Nexus spires, PoK portals, Faithstones, and area-specific gate potions, in addition to existing items with gate procs.

Keep in mind, this is not about "porting for plat" -- but rather about class attributes. Being one of only two classes who could move people around was originally described on many occasions as part of our overall balance with other classes. Well, granting easier travel to other classes was good for the game perhaps, but nothing new was given to offset the loss of that 'niche' for the two porting classes.

Another example was the implementation of Graveyards, which detracted from the significance of situational abilities such as evacuation (dru/wiz) and corpse summoning (nec/shd). These were rarely so vital that groups would seek out a class based purely on those abilities, and true it was only considered 'very desirable' in a few zones, but again they were benefits of convenience for those classes and those who'd group with them; a small part of their overall balance.

Yeah I know, the game changes and evolves, yada yada. I'm just disappointed that some of our utility, which were repeatedly used by VI as an excuse to restrict us in other areas, have all but eroded away. Knowing our core attributes -- healing and damage-dealing -- would always be inferior to specialists in their respective roles, these other 'little things' were the mortar that bound those attribute bricks together for the Druid.

Now with the game's current encounter and zone designs, along with a top-heavy 65th level population on most servers, players usually have the luxury of picking whomever they believe will fill a role "best" without regard for convenience -- which is now largely provided by the game itself rather than by players. So where does that leave the few classes for whom these conveniences were part of what made them special and balanced?

~Firemynd

Druids already had concerns in this regard, because most of our utilities are shared with classes who *do* bring something unique to groups.

Sadly, every time we see class "specialties" or prime skills given out to others, I see EQ enthusiasm of the longer term players wane, but it's a double edged sword because it also helps make all the classes more playable. Kinda takes the "tough love" out of playing any character class now.

Giving all 51+ characters free access to what was essentially 'built-in' SoW didn't really make classes more playable; it simply further marginalized an advantage that a few specific classes previously had over others. (Reason I say 'further' -- Even before AAs and horses, all classes already had access to jboots, tboots, and sow potions.)

Why have these new marginalizations affected us more than others? Part of the druid's class balance was its abilities to get away quickly and to escape. This was evident in design, not only by the druid gaining all lines of "movement" spells, but seeing continued advancement in each. Other SoW-casting classes only received one or two lines of movement spells, with lesser advancement in each.

While giving other classes access to travel conveniences, SOE gave us ways of enhancing our 'port' advantage -- exodus and quick evac. My gripe is that they did nothing to maintain our 'movement' advantage, and in fact a couple other classes (knights) actually leapfrogged ahead of us in outdoor travel.

In my opinion, as other classes edge closer to our 'old' spell-based run speeds, there should be druid AAs to retain somewhat of an incremental advantage.

Soul of the Wolf: movement modifier (i.e. stacks with sow, soe, etc).
- Passive, innate.
- Target, self only.
- Ranks 1 to 3, for +5, +10, +20%.

Soul of the Cheetah: movement modifier (i.e. stacks with sow, soe, etc).
- Activated, hotkey.
- Target, group.
- Duration 60 seconds.
- Refresh timer 15 minutes.
- Ranks 1 to 3, for +50, +60, +70%.

Spell: Earthen Affinity: +30 movement modifier added to each target's own innate runspeed, indoor only, does not stack with spells.
- Level 51
- Targetable group buff.
- Duration 30 minutes.

These abilities would not be overpowering, but would serve to maintain a slight lead in what has traditionally been a specialty of the druid class.

I have to comment on the basic rationale of the post. First, let me say I can understand why the changes might have a negative emotional impact. However, on logical grounds, the argument isn't rational. The fact that the game was "orginally" a certain way is not an argument at all that it should remain that way (it's the "genetic fallacy"). If you stripped away ports and sow from druids, they were still one of the most powerful classes pre-Kunark. Further, travel for everyone else was too burdensome - one of the first time sinks (though it at least did give you the impression of the size of the world).

In other words, the game has improved for the most part due to things like innate run speed and the PoK stones. (You can make an argument that the game was "better" without them, but since we're talking a subjective measure you have to take into account that most people certainly seem to enjoy it more now than then, due to those aspects.) Adding soulbinders was another case of "watering down" the game by giving PC abilities to NPC's - but I can barely express what a positive change that was.

Play balance should be based on how effective a class helps their group to be, not on how convenient some aspects become.

That being said, allowing movement buffs to stack - at least in some fashion - with movement AA's would have been a nice thing to do.

A lot of these type of abilities don’t have a direct impact on kill speed, but they do have an indirect impact.

In LDoN for example a group finishing an adventure with a druid in the group can port straight to S Ro, and quickly get transferred to the appropriate adventure camp. It saves some time running and gives more time killing so it will increase the group xp. If a SoW allows someone to run away without dying then it saves the group downtime and hence means more time for killing. An evac in the right circumstances can save a huge amount of downtime, leading to more xp.

Rezzing falls into the same category. It wont directly lead to faster kills, but by getting the group moving after a death much quicker it means more time for killing (on top of the actual xp given back). Hitpoint buffs don’t directly lead to faster killing, but again less dying = more time killing.

For most of these abilities it is impossible to quantify just how much extra killing the group gets to do, but there is some benefit to them. Some of the time they may have no value (e.g. evaccing ability when the group never wipes) but potential value is still useful. No xp group is risk free to negate these abilities entirely – I’ve been in a PoTime group slumming in normal LDoN that would have wiped but for an evac (gater brought back a dozen or more mobs).
