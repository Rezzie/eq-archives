You should plan to do a bit of switching, assuming you have the AA skills you want from SoL already.

In general, level to 61, do the AA XPing necessary to get those AA skills you deem most helpful for that level (e.g., you can boost Fury of Magic at 61, 63, and 65), then go back to leveling.

There's no point in trying to reach 65 too quickly, because at 61 many of the XP giving mobs in the Tier 1 planes will be green or light blue to you. You can't get to the Tier 2 and 3 planes without completing the events with a group of friends or guildmates. And when you do get to those planes, you will want your levels AND your good AA skills with you.

Of course, those with 30 AA points banked are sitting pretty and can just blast up the levels...:)

Are you hunting only in newer lands Scirocco?

Yes, in the tier 1 planes. Disease, Justice, and Nightmare are good for a druid soloing. Innovation has nasty summoning mobs near the zone in, including a randomly spawn pseudo-named. Although where I hunt may change depending on the crowds. I hope to get to a tier 2 plane in the next day or two, if enough folks in my guild have gotten PoP.

I definitely recommend gettting Run 3. In fact, it was the exception to my earlier recommendation of getting to 60 first, earlier on.

SCM 3 is the most important skill for a level 60 druid to get. Along with Exodus and Innate Camo. If you do nuking or quadding, then get SCF 3. If you do a lot of healing, then get HA 3. I wouldn't worry about whether you do this at 60 or 61, since the opportunities to get XP aren't that different in either case.

At 61, I have purchased Fury 1 (I have SCF 3) and Radiant Cure 1. The later is a must have, in my opinion. It cures 9 counters of curse, poison, and disease for a group, and the reuse time is superfast at 3 minutes (people were worrying about 15 min to an hour before). This makes GRC less of a bottleneck for some SOL encounters.

Up to 60, I'd say that leveling wins out. Getting through 60 should be fairly easy (at least it was pre-PoP), so getting to 61 for the vendor-bought Replenishment (25 hp/tick regen) is worthwhile. But get the above skills at a minimum before going on.

And yes, filling up the AA XP will be easier at 65 than it is at 60. Just like filling up the AA XP is substantially easier at 60 than it was at 51. But getting to the "substantially easier" part requires access to advanced planes, and also the use of some of those lower rank AA skills. Thus, the bit of switching I mentioned above. Get the most important skills when you get to that level, then focus on leveling.

And yes, you should be able to go back and pick up skills at any time. The level 61 requirement is a minimum level requirement, not a limitation saying you can only get that skill at 61.
