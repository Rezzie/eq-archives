Magic Information from Everquest Extreme
I found this information on the EverQuest Extreme web site regarding the effects of a particular magic spell in Everquest.
  • EQ was ran on Windows 95 with Voodoo2, there was no crash expect a reboot from a Voodoo2 bug.
  • Expect slight video jerks when entering the game, but EQ was ultra-smooth after that; no video lag at all.
  • NPCs did not do much, but guards seem very functional. There were guards in some cities, in which two stands by the city gate. Guards will also patrol on trails. The three guys in golden plate armor from Next-Generation's new screen shot are in fact guards.
  • Magic was awesome. For example, here is the casting process of a fireball spell:
    1. The user will put his arms to his side and then focus...
    2. Flames will leap out of his hands and sit in his hands doing what flames do
    3. Then he raises his hands and throw it
    4. When it hits it makes a large explosion
    5. Very very cool
  • From what he saw, camera views seem unlimited
  • Control were good, although underwater controls need more work. Mouse Look was like that of Quake's
  • When I asked him how EQ compared to AC (yes, he did play both games :P), here is what he had to say : "Its like comparing mario 1 with mario 64 =)". Mario 64 being EQ of course :)

NGO Article
I found this article on Next Generation Online. I basically says the same thing that a lot of the new articles out there say... Sony must have issued some sort of official press release that everyone is working off of. Heres the article:
Sony has designed a huge new PC RPG, designed to be enjoyed by communities of up to 100 players per server. EverQuest is quite a departure for Sony and is being developed by the newly formed 989 Studios. The environment is the fully three-dimensional world of Norrath. This world spreads over five continents with 30 adventure zones and 12 major cities. Also, 1,000 players can interact, form alliances or battle each other across this vast virtual landscape.

It's due to launch this October, with a number of features unseen in previous Rags of this kind. This includes the ability to view the action from multiple angles and the chance to lock yourself into or out of a kind of deathmatch mode called "Pkilling".

It's certainly an ambitious effort, and while it looks great in the graphics department, online games of this skill have to be tested live and by large numbers of players.

Ultima Online Gold?

Ultima@Scorched has posted another update. Chris Bailey talks about Ultima Online Gold, his chats with Richard Garriott and Ed Del Castillo, and a surprise at the EverQuest booth:

...The literature states that the Gold Edition "captivates new users with it's simplified interface, expanded help code and complete documentation. For the current player, it offers uncharted territory, Lost Lands filled with dangerous new creatures, secret temples, and untold wealth. The Ultima Online Gold Edition also enhances it's robust community by providing vastly improved communication tools like text and voice chat. The game has been expanded to better support international players through in game localization and web site translations."

...I did chat briefly with Richard Garriott regarding UO Gold. He stated that the main focus of the product is to improve accessibility and cater more to international users. This will be accomplished by more extensive help screens, a demo tutorial, and the establishment of foreign servers.

I did get to talk to Ed DelCastillo at length today about many of the issues surrounding Ultima: Ascension. Ed is very passionate about his plans for the game, and has some specific goals in mind for the game's completion. Most of these involve maintaining the underlying elements of a traditional role playing game, but making them transparent or invisible to the person playing the game...

The final surprise from today was stopping by Sony Interactive's area of the show and witnessing Richard Garriott playing Everquest under the guidance of the game's lead programmer.

More E3 Coverage from Everquest Extreme
A few of our contacts from E3 reported several things to us reguarding the demo at E3.
  • The Erudite cities have just been brought online, and a ferry runs a regular cycle between there and Qeynos, with a short stop at an uncharted island. Takes approximately 30-45 minutes for each leg of the trip.
  • There will be RT(roundtime) for swinging swords and casting spells. This will be dependant on the weight of the weapon and your agility, the spell RT will probably be based on the level of the spell and its difficulty.
  • "the graphics are GREAT, better than screen shots show","they have mastered the 3d looks"
  • "The game is still FULL of cheats" - by this he means not actual cheats but the ability for brad to change lots of stuff from his console, much like the rcon command in quake and quake2
  • Brad could morph into any monster at any time, and even change its height and weight, for example he at one time was a 90ft cyclops, and also a 3ft cyclops
  • "The UI(user interface) is not compoletely intuitive yet, combat was simplistic, you click on a monster to "target" then hit a key to start "fighting" and it just scrolls results messages"
  • Race greatly effects what classes you can be, as well as how good you are at being that class.
  • You start off with only a few items, a magician for instance has a staff, shirt, pants, and 1 spell in his book.
  • Ogres cannot be monks.
  • Lighting, weather, and sky effects are great, but not completely functional yet.
  • "aradune was a beholder and a cyclops" (beholder was called "Evil Eye")
  • Naked elves appear to be wearing leotards (isnt that usefull info?)
  • The game is all around stable with very little lag to the point of almost non existance(take note only 19 people were on the server)
  • Combat and functionality need work.
  • "monster IQ is decent... upoin attacking one skeleton, 2 more came running to assist from off screen"
  • 3 kinds of viewing modes:
    1. regular: stuff everywhere
    2. full screen: everything... just used for screen caps
    3. large: "full screen" which is a HUD kind of feel
  • Brad wouldent release much info on magic and alignment, but the fireball spell needed to be scribed into a book then memorized before it could be used, quite a lengthy process.
We will release more info as it is made available.
NOTE: none of the info is official and has not been released by Sony, who as rumor has it has changed its name to Studio 887 or somthing like that(somone email me the correct name)

8:50 Pm PST
EQ Coverage From E3
Vault Networks roaming reporter at E3, Mahrin Skel has reported back with this:
Absolutely stunning, that's the only way to describe EQ. With Brad McQuaid as my guide, I toured Norrath for the better part of 2 hours. The level of detail is simply amazing, the user interface is, for the most part, very slick. A few rough areas like a patch of water you can fall into in town and can't climb out, remind you that this is still a pre-beta.
A few new bits of info:
1) That dragon we saw was a young Lava Dragon, as in it's much smaller than the full-size version.
2) The Erudite cities have just been brought online, and a ferry runs a regular cycle between there and Qeynos, with a short stop at an uncharted island. Takes approximately 30-45 minutes for each leg of the trip.
3) Magic system is still top secret. Brad showed me a few of the spells we've already seen, but all details of the system are still under wraps.
4) Ogres can't be Monks, but can be Shadow Knights.
5) Darkness is dark Stock up on torches, guys.
Levelling or even leaving Qeynos right now is very difficult, as the game is in the very early stages of balancing. Basically, you can't outrun anything, so if you start taking the short end of the fight, you have to run for the guards and hope you live long enough to reach them.
All of the races and classes are present, but many are very limited because of the current state of development.
Overall, a very promising beginning. Brad made it clear that he wants this to be a place the "Hard Core" RPG'er can enjoy (but doesn't like the term, because it is synonymous with "Unprofitable" to some marketing types. EQ is looking very much like it will be able to deliver.

Brad McQuaid On DevBoard
For the past week or so, much discussion has been going on on the DevBoard concerning the possibility of different types of dragons with different alliances. Here is what Brad McQuaid had to say about this:

It is our view that dragons are very exotic and rare creatures. Because of this, we are not going to list different types, nor their different attacks, alignments, colors, etc. Coming face to face with a dragon and doing combat with it (regardless of whether you live or die) will be something you can be really proud of. Many people who play EQ will never see a dragon -- they'll only hear tales and such. In fact, from a purely gamers standpoint, I wouldn't have wanted to even show screen shots with the lava dragon. It's even my hope that those who do encounter dragons in EQ will keep the details to themselves... in a MUD I used to play quite often the ultimate foe was Tiamat, and I remember asking people about the dragon... where it was, how did it fight, what treasure it guarded, and I received very few, and then always vague, answers to my questions. And then, well over a year later, when I finally was able to face the creature, the awe and honor was that much greater.

-Brad
