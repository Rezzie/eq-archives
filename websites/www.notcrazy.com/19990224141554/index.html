    Brad Confirms Going Gold
    20:19:13 PST
    This juicy tidbit came from Altered Worlds.

    This is true -- a couple of days ago we went gold (actually, silver -- I think gold writable CD-ROMs are a thing of the past now).

    So what does this mean? It means the graphics are final (well, as final as an ever-evolving dynamic online RPG can be, meaning it's our intent to add graphics and other new stuff after release to keep the game exciting). It doesn't mean, however, that EverQuest is done. We are still fixing bugs on both the client and the server, and tuning and adjusting all the data that makes up the game. Obviously, there will be a patch when people log in for the first time, which will consist of the client software and a few other small files. And there will be patches after release as we continue to make this a great and dynamic game (which I think is one of the greatest strengths an online game has -- the ability to adapt and evolve). Phase 4 continues, and as you can see from our latest patch message, we are hard at work squashing bugs and getting this game ready for March 16th. I remain confident that we'll have a solid game by release and urge all the beta testers out there to hang in there as we head into the final stretch.
    I really hope they know what they are doing. While the game is more ready for realse than UO was before just before its release, I think there is still a lot that needs to be done.

    Test Server Patch Info
    13:25:26 PST
    NEW PATCH ON TEST SERVER ------------------------
    The patch on the Test Server includes the following fixes: 1) Problem with people unable to enter the game after getting disconnected - We found this problem and we believe we have completely corrected it.
    2) Seeing your own Hit Point bug - We corrected several problems in this area. You shouldn't have any more problems seeing your own Hit Points any more. In addition, you shouldn't have any more problems seeing the Hit Points of your own party or monsters you target. This should fix the Paladin - Lay Hands on problem as well.
    3) Charm Spell problems - We should have fixed all the problems related to this spell, particularly regarding Hit Points and other associated problems.
    4) Problems with falling thru the World while zoning - We believe this problem has been fixed. If anyone still encounters this problem, please email us immediately.
    5) All Bards should now get Dual Wield.
    6) Rangers, Shadow Knights and Paladins should all get channelling.
    7) Several Crash Bugs have been fixed on both the zone and world servers.
    8) Several Combat bugs have been fixed regarding damage.
    9) You should now be able to target translucent creatures (also see note below).
    10) You can no longer trade with NPC's from any height.
    11) If you zone while anonymous, you will still be anonymous when you reach the new zone.
    12) Sound - We have finally activated music. You will only get music if you have a Sound Font capable sound card. These cards include - Awe 64 Gold and Value, Awe32, SB Live - In the next few days we will be activating the music on ALL sound cards. As this is code hasn't been in before, please let us know of any problems you encounter.
    In order to run on the Test Server, run TestEverQuest.exe. If the above fixes are stable, we will be releasing them to the other servers tomorrow morning around 10am pst. Other Notes: We are currently working on the following problems:
    1) Boats - We will be fixing the problems regarding boats very quickly. This is a HIGH priority item.
    2) Targeting - We are working on major improvements to the targeting system. We have listened to our playerbase and are making many of the changes you have all reccomended.
    3) Losing Newbie Guild Tunics upon death.
    4) Invisible Players/Monsters/Corpses
    5) General NPC Pathing problems
    6) Various NPC's in the world that appear as humans.
    7) Various NPC's in the world that have the "can't open textfile.txt" problem.
    We believe the above items should all be taken care of pretty quickly (we are hoping this week). We are also aware of other problems, and are working hard on many things. The above items represent what we feel are the major problems.

    EQScreenshots.com Closes

    EQ Screenshots Closes

    Below is the letter I received this evening from Brad McQuaid. My intention when starting this site, was to generate interest in the game through the use of it's stunning visuals. I really felt as though this would be helping the game, not hindering it. However, after reading this letter, I may indeed be hurting the vision that the creators of EQ have worked so hard to preserve. I do not feel that I am qualified to decide what is, or is not a 'spoiler', nor do I wish to ruin the experience for anyone, so I will no longer be sponsoring this site. I apologize to all those who have submitted wonderful screenshots, and who were so excited about contributing. Perhaps at a later time, a site like this will be practical and possible. For now, I trust in the creators of this wonderful game, as only they know what the spirit of Everquest shoud be.

    Thank you,

    Willowsong

    as the game progresses, please do me a favor and don't post 'spoiler' screenshots or screenshots of higher level areas, and areas that are difficult to get to. this can spoil the discovery and diminish the pride of seeing a new area for the first time.

    Check out: Producer, EverQuest www.everquest.com

    Brad on EQ
    18:44:24 PST
    A Disgruntled EQ player recently made accusations towards EQ stating that UO was a better environment for roleplaying. Of course, Brad can't let this be! He must retort.

    UO -is- better for roleplaying. Hands down. End of discussion.

    Obviously I'm biased, but overall I'd have to disagree.

    Can you lay out a feast (or any other roleplaying event, for that matter)in EQ? No. In EQ you can lay out food under your feet in a small, brown bag. In UO you can prop up furniture, music stands for bards, tables, food. Have a good time.

    True.

    Can you roleplay to your fullest extent in EQ? No. You cannot engage a person in combat if he insults your mother, or insults your honor. Dueling? Only if he consents. In UO you can plan out that murder, poison your weapon, poison his food, or even hire someone to do it for you.

    It's not just dueling, it's regular PvP for those who choose to PvP, and it's anyone within certain level limits on the PvP server.

    Ever play on Catskills in Ultima Online? It's amazing. Warring families, extremist radical factions bent on revolution, organized battles, regular weekly gatherings. You name it, it can be done in UO. Cannot say the same for EQ.

    Actually, I can. You can certainly do this, especially on the PvP server. In fact, the level limits to PvP aren't present in guild wars (which warring families and factions would fall under).

    Don't even try to say "EQ goes much further in that department than other games.." It limits it.

    Well, I suppose one could list all the things EQ has that promote role playing, like the races, separate starting cities, global chat, etc. I could also mention how the percentage of people choosing to play on the PvP server is but a fraction of those on the other servers.

    -Brad

    PS. It is absolutely not 'group or die' once you get to your 20s. As I've always said, some race/class combos are more suited towards solo play and others (most) towards group play. But it is and will remain possible to solo all the way up to 50 (and perhaps one day beyond). It takes more time, and is more challenging in one sense (although working together in a group environment certainly emcompasses its own tactics and skills), but definitely doable.

    -Brad
