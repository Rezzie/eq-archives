Todays Updates
21:3:40 PST
Just got this in from Smedly concerning the updates that have taken place today.

Hello All,

I waited until it was 7:30pm pst tonight to write this update. I wanted to make sure things were stable before I wrote this. Lately every time I give an update something bad happened right after I sent it.

Here's a summary of what was done in the last 2 days

1) We did a major hardware upgrade to our primary router here in San Diego.
2) We worked with CERFnet to do a router upgrade to their facility here in San Diego.
3) We moved Karana over to CERFnet
4) We brought up Rodcet Nife

I think you will find now that the packet loss should be basically gone... at least on our end. We were definetely causing some of the loss before (only when we hit major numbers) but now things are very, very clean running and that is with 23,582 people on just as I write this (a new record).

Does this mean that EVERYONE'S Packet Loss is gone? Absolutely not. BUT, I did some surveys in most of the worlds and about 75% of the people told me their Packet Loss was great. If you are having problems now, do a traceroute to chat.everquest.com and send it to the email address traceroute@verant.com and we will take a look. We take our customers connections very seriously, and if a whole lot of people on one network are having a problem we will do our best to look into it. It doesn't mean we can always get the reaction we want (some networks are just plain bad) but many times we can help find a legitimate problem and help correct it.

Some of you may also not realize what the network statistics we have in game actually are for:

The left hand number is your ping to the server in milliseconds. The left-middle number (percentage) is your packet loss to the server The right-middle number (percentage) is your packet loss from the server The right hand number is the data rate. THIS IS NOT THE PING. - I love hearing about the "terrible" pings in game of 3000.

It is my strong belief (forgive me for this rant) that EVERY online game should have this type of meter so that you are getting real feedback about the network status.

John Smedley

0:18:4 PST
This patch message showed up on the servers today. Hopefully this will solver most peoples problems.

Server Update 4/27/99 7:30 pm
Citizens of Norrath,

We are pleased to announce that the hardware upgrade that was performed on our router this morning has appeared to resolve the severe packet loss problems that many players were encountering over the past week. We have been, and will continue to monitor the load on the new hardware to assure that the packet-loss problems will no longer occur on our end. During the router upgrade we transported the Karana server farm to the AT&T CerfNet facility to further reduce the load on our router. Also, the new "Rodcet Nife" server was just brought up to accommodate the additional users that have joined over the past few weeks. After thoroughly testing changes made only for Rallos Zek (PK server), we have activated the new code for the PK server ONLY.

The changes include the following;
1) Pets will no longer kill themselves if their master has invisibility cast on them.
2) Pets will no longer attack other players while they are in guard mode if their master goes link-dead.
3) Pets will now attack opposing combatants while their master is dueling.
4) NPCs will no longer respond to invisible PCs.

Other fixes and changes include:

1) The corpse class tag will not be listed as "Unknown".
2) Players will be given text messages for items that they cannot obtain while looting a corpse, instead of the item disappearing.
3) A number of quests and combat related exploits were fixed.
4) Characters are no longer able to use the disarm command while using feign death.

At Midnight, on Wednesday the 28th, the servers will be brought down for an update.

- The EverQuest Team

The Bradman had the following to say about the "quest" in EverQuest, I'd say more but hell you can read it for yourself :)



Quests are something that are constantly being added to the game. They may or may not be balanced correctly the first time. Also, while one reward will be great for one player, another might think it wasn't worth doing the quest. Another might think the reward was too great. This is just the nature of the beast.

Game balance is very important to us, and I think we've done an overall great job at it. But in that quests are constantly being added, I don't expect them to be perfect the first time. This is a price we all pay for having a dynamic evolving game. Not only that, but we will purposely move, modify, or take out quests that may have been 'fine' just to keep the game interesting and changing.

The bottom line is if you like doing quests, go for it. If your only incentive for doing the quest is to get some great reward, then you probably ought to just hack monsters. Doing quests are supposed to be fun in and of themselves. My quest team has done a great job of integrating the detailed story behind the world of Norrath into these quests -- heck, they've spent well over a year doing this and continue working on them every day. You learn about the world doing quests and people have a great time in the process of doing them. But quests aren't for everyone, nor are they meant to be.

Looking through some of the quests you listed I certainly found some that seemed to have rewards a bit on the low side. And there will be some on the high side. And they will change and are part of our constant tuning and balancing.

I do NOT however consider these 'broken' quests that need to be 'fixed'. Broken quests are ones that simply don't work. For example, perhaps you hand the final item to the NPC, he says 'thanks, here's your holy sword of slashing' and then you receive nothing. Fixing those if there are any left would be our priority, btw.

Brad McQuaid

Brad on PVP
7:48:55 PST
Brad talks about PVP, also a brief question on ideas I like. A possibility of a PVP server where Humans can't kill Human's but they can kill Trolls. How would you like to have your PKing limited to out-of-race kills? Well there are ups and downs on this one, read on.
Everquest is overwhelmingly of course a player-versus-environment game. But there are those of us who see player-versus-player as the only real way to experience a role-playing game. Indeed, I would never play this game without the ability to fully experience a dynamic environment in which the great story of racial and factional conflict you've created couldn't be lived without the challenge of roleplayed PvP. While you've one server dedicated to us now, I believe that in time you will find more and more players either turning on their PK-flags or moving to PvP-dedicated worlds. The reason for this is simple: the challenge and fun of fighting monsters and questing inevitably ends or grows uninteresting, even with so-called "expansion packs." In 6, 8, or 12 months when many players have done and seen "it all"- or enough to see a deadend- they will crave new options, and this usually means PvP and/or advanced crafts. Rallos Zek is not only the best roleplaying shard in my opinion (the messageboards don't correctly show the real situation), but unlikely the switch servers, when lots of players reach rank 40+, Everquest will just be beginning for us; whereas, for those on switch shards, the game will just be ending.

I do have to disagree here -- we are confident we can keep PvE interesting up to level 50 (and even perhaps beyond :)

Presently however PvP has a fair number of bugs and exploits which if you check the PvP and Rallos Zek messageboard are fairly well and completely discussed in various posts. It'd be nice if you'd at least recognize these problems and let us know that in time you intend to fix them. Do you have other plans of enhancing more player-driven structures in the game beyond PvP as well?

Absolutely. The PvP server is very important to us and we are proud that we can support different playing styles within the same game (using different servers as well as the PK switch).

As John and I have mentioned, we are changing it such that if you are killed by an NPC, only you are someone to whom you give consent will be able to loot your corpse.

Invisibility will be a consent spell like Bind Affinity, such that you have to be grouped with someone before they can cast it on you (this will eliminate the need to delete a player's pet).

Also, we will be adding factions for PvP in the future.

Lastly, we are tossing around the idea of another server where different races are either allies or enemies. For example, humans couldn't PK humans, but could trolls, and vice versa. Please let us know what you think about this.

-Brad
