The Spoiler Bandwagon

After seeing that Brad McQuaid had something to say about how EQ Vault stands on the issue of spoilers and other information that ruin the mysticalness of a game, it seems as though every major EQ site, and most of the minor ones, have jumped on the Spolier Opinion Bandwagon. Probably in hopes of getting a letter from Brad telling them how much he respects their site for not posting spoilers, etc. I am not going to do this.

Spoliers ruin games for people thats a given. Anyone who likes a game, and has any respect for a game whatsoever, probably would not post spoilers. This is unlike those people who post all the spoilers for a game on their page, and then load the page down with banners in order to make a quick buck. I have too much respect for EQ, 989s, and the EQ fans who frequent the news sites and #everquest in IRC, to post these spoilers. I should not be having to say this. In fact, anyone with any respect for a game, should not have to state their opinions on spoilers, nor should their opinion be asked for. It should be a given. Anyone who even begins to consider wether or not they should post a games spoilers should ask themselves these questions: "What attracted me to this game?" and, if applicable, "What keeps me playing this game?"

People who have considered posting spoilers and then decided not to make me think wether or not they truly respect a game, of if they are running a page simply for the extra money or the ego boost.

Posting spoliers should not be an option to a true fan. Quite simply it is a matter of respect to both the game and its players.
