. Sega Ages Review
Classic Sega fun on Saturn can be had in various forms, but the easiest way is by playing the one-stop disk Sega Ages. Dimension S recently took a new look at this little gem and wrote up a review:

Three of Sega's most successful arcade titles from the '80s have been converted to the Sega Saturn as part of the "Sega Ages" series. Originally released as separate Sega Ages editions in Japan, Afterburner 2 (1987), Outrun (1986) and Space Harrier (1986) were bundled together by Sega for release in the European market. Working Designs, ever watchful for publishing opportunities such as this, quickly snatched up the rights to the compilation for release in North America.

. CPM Arena
Challenge.World reports that arQon has put together a Arena CPM modification.

So, why am I doing this? Well, I really like Clan Arena. It's what I play 99% of the time these days. OSP CA with CPM enabled rocks my world. But a lot of the CPM players out there are into duelling, and so server admins have to make a choice: run OSP and a map rotation of Overkill/NateDM1/DM6/etc, or run a CPM server with CPM1/CPM2/CPM3/etc. Given the hassle and the cost of running a server, admins should be able to get as much out of one as they can. It's a better arrangement for the gamers too: if you and a friend go to a server for a little duelling, why wait in line to play on a 1v1 map when you can just go to a different arena on the same server and start playing immediately? That's the basic premise behind CPMA, and I think it's a good one.

. Dreamcast Emulator: First Pictures
When it comes to late-breaking emulation news you have to turn to the best emulation resource online: Vintage Gaming. To close out this week of emulation, Dave and the Tux-man deliver direct grab screenshots of the world's first working Dreamcast emulator. Don't start jumping to conclusions just yet though, this puppy is super-early, will no doubt require a monster machines to run games properly (when/if it actually does this), and yes, it only runs home-made Dreamcast demos so far. Nevertheless, this is very exciting news as it will eventually allow amateur Dreamcast developers to work on their projects that much more efficiently...

. New Review
Extreme Hardware has updated there site with a review of the MPTrip Discman. Here is a snippet:
With a 650mb capacity, the MPTrip offers hours upon hours of music on one CD, making it ideal for long car/plane trips. With the MPTrip, no longer is an expensive CD changer needed. In fact, the MPTrip’s storage capacity easily exceeds its battery life of 3-4 hours. Its ability to also play regular CDs also greatly helps its value. To top it off, the MPTrip comes with all the features we would expect from a good CD player, including a 50-second skip protection, frequency manipulations, both headphone and line out jacks, a hold button, and a bundled AC adapter. Additionally, the MPTrip comes with a built-in battery recharger, and the option for 500-second voice recording, provided you make the effort to carry a microphone around.
