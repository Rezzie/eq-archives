A Different Bard Guide

Suggesting a choice of beginning statistics and play (in progress, btw)

Bristlebane Server   Luclin Server    Prexus Server    Druzzil Ro  

 

Disclaimer:
Most Bard Guides, in various websites, are geared towards information that is, probably, not applicable anymore as Verant, has grown, and its game has changed. With those changes, came many things that are expected and many things that are not expected. Without sounding paranoid, the original statistics that this game started with (as suggestions) for any Bard are now inferior and not exactly up to par to the changes and direction that the game has taken.

This Bard Guide is based on my observations taken from having played over a dozen Bards in as many servers, and taking them to a level that tends to indicate that the numbers that were suggested originally, are no longer efficient, or helpfull. One major example, is STRENGTH. The game, at higher levels, when the Bard is not counted on for much except to play one song, used to be the one statistic that everyone went for, (after all this is a game where you have to fight), but today, the game's designers are changing how things work, and I think that the days that Guilds will have nothing but tanks taking down the Gods, will be coming to a crawling end. This will be a very important moment for all the other classes in the game, that supposedly can not do any damage, or provide any defense. The Bard, will then, be able to do much more than simply be a mana battery, and we can only hope that Verant is willing to take the game into this direction, so everyone's secondary accounts, won't be a tank, but a different class.

 

Statistics and the definitions: In relationship to a Bard.
This game is too smart, to have a starting set of statistics, to make use of two of them, and ignore the other five. It is said that some only need Intelligence, and that others only need Wisdom. And then others need Strength. The one thing to remember, is that the Bard is usually considered a "jack of all trades and master of none". If this is so, then, a Bard's set of statistics at the creation of the character are most important ... unless you know the role you want to play, you must, realize that this definition suggests that all statistics will play a role in the playing of the game, sooner or later, and then some. The question is how.

Strength
How much you can carry
Determines average damage
How fast you learn Offensive skills

Stamina
How many hit points you receive
How long you can hold your breath under water
Ratings that involve a "length" of time

Agility
How quickly you learn/earn defensive statistics
How difficult it is to hit you
How much damage you will take

Dexterity
How quickly a Bard learns weapon skills
How often weapons and other items will proc
How quickly you learn the external skills

Wisdom
Amount of mana that priest classes have
How quickly you learn skills if higher than Intelligence
Apparently has no effect on Bard's whatsoever

Intelligence
How quickly you learn most skills
Determines the amount of mana for Bards and Mages

Charisma
How will vendors and NPC's react to you in exchanges
Affects the success of several Bard songs


When looking at these base definitions, the choices that one would like to place the starting points onto would normally lead you to Strength, Dexterity and Charisma. The only problem is that if you place your points in Strength and Dexterity, it is safe to say that you will, in all likelihood, be a Bard that wants to mix it up with weapons, and fight some. And your aim through out the playing of the Bard should be to make sure that you can get equipment that adds to that equation ... so you go for the AC in everything, and hope that you can be twinked fast enough so you can be strong and play like some sort of a tank ... right! If you do this, in the later levels you will be the main user of the Cleric's mana that you are trying to help your group with!

But, if you have seen other Bards playing, you will notice that they can use some instruments for some very different types of work and play. This is called the "Magical Bard", and it is the rarest form of Bard playing that you will find. It is, at the same time, the most frustrating and difficult to learn, as it is to play it. The needs for this type of Bard, are Charisma, Agility and Stamina. Charisma, because there are several Bard songs that use it, and are the basis of the "Magical Bard". Agility, so you can hope not to take as much punishment as you are going to otherwise take. Stamina, is a bit of the odd one here, but the extra points here would help, just so that the times you have a weapon in hand, you do not stink up the neighborhood (hear me Wood Elves). This also factors in the equation that adds up your hit points total.

Or, you may want to be the "hybrid" Bard. Meaning that you average out all statistics, to make sure there are no serious imbalances. There are serious debates as to the real value of Intelligence and Wisdom in a Bard's life, but it is no secret that these appear in several items and instruments and that it should be inconceivable that they do not affect things in one way or another. But, knowing the strength that programmers have, and the clever way this game is designed, it should be a rather interesting test to find a Bard with all Wisdom and Intelligence ... more on this later.

 

Decision time
What kind of Bard do you think you can be? You do not start the "Magical" Bard stuff until level 8, when you really get the first instrument that allows you to play with it, and be effective, and this is the String skill with the Lute or any other stringed instrument. Or do you want to just hit and hit and hit ... because of you do, the Bard is not your style. The song add-on to the fighting is nice, but hardly anything like a Paladin, or a Ranger, or a Shadow Knight, the so called hybrids. Your damage output, with weapons on, will be somewhere near 60% of the damage these can do. If you are thinking that the use of instruments is better than weapons, then the Bard is for you. If not, please stick to a fighting class, because the Bard's "ok" melee skills are not enough to make a difference in battle. Their instruments, however, ARE enough to make a difference in ANY battle. A difference and MEAN IT! Or as I say, "with an exclamation point!"

 

The classes and the statistics
There are three classes that have Bards. They are Human, Half-Elf and Wood-Elf. All of them have their advantages and disadvantages. The question is, what is best for you, and what are you adept at?

Human
Starting statistics for a Human tend to lean towards the Strength side of things. At the expense of Charisma. This class figures to make a good fighter if mixing it up is what you want to do. I call this a "front end stats bard".

Half-Elf
Has statistics that lean towards the average. Meaning that this particular one should be able to fight as well as do the other magical stuff, if properly attired and designed.

Wood-Elf
Statistics are centered in the "back end" in that this is the weakest of the Bards in terms of fighting skills, but it is also the best suited to magical stuff. It comes with higher Charisma and Dexterity/Agility than the others, and these can play a major role in your growth.

 

Where should the bonus points go?
This is the subject of much discussion, and a part that is in constant flux in this game. And we must remember that.

At one time, it was nothing but a hack and slash game (it still is!), but these days, more care is being taken so that a critter will require more of one thing or another, rather than the hack and slash attitude of yesterday. The days that a major Guild runs into a place, brings its Wellingtonian strategy to bear, and then walks in for the big prize, are quite numbered. It's a matter of time. If so, adjusting your statistics to fit the new, on-coming styles, is probably a very important thing to do. The only thing that we know, is that the game will not return to what it was once before, and the programmers are not interested in copying themselves much ... so improvements and change are the most important thing to think about. And you have to remember that unlike all the other classes in this game, this is one thing that the Bard in this game is capable of doing, although I think that a Human Bard may have a harder time adapting for a Magickal style, or that a Wood Elf Bard would have a harder time fighting ... specially in the newer lands of Vellious.

If the Bard, is the very first character that you will come up with, then it is important that you have, in the least, an idea for what you would like to do with your character ... weapons or instruments? ...

If you choose weapons, then you need to center your points around the statistics that help the hitting that you are going to do. These should be the Strength, Stamina and Dexterity. Dexterity helps the weapons "proc" in the higher levels, so one will need to help that area as much as possible. It also helps you learn your hitting skills a wee bit faster. Strength always helps. Basically, one never has enough of it. Stamina is also an issue, since you will miss a lot, and that affects the total outcome of any fight. Most Bards just don't like to see all those misses while fighting, but, this is a way of life. You have to remember that with the best weapons that you can carry, you never will reach even 60% of all the damage a Paladin, or Ranger can do with them. Your song add'ons, will give you 10/20% of that total, but all in all, you will still always be in the lower scale when it comes to total damage ... and this is important to remember if you want a Bard to be involved in Duels all the time. Personally, I recommend a lot of Stamina, since you can raise the Strength easily enough via items, and the rest divided between Dexterity and Strength. 

But the choice of "offensive"  in terms of weapons, is not necessarily a good idea, in this present evolution of this game. When you do a lot of damage, critters like to pick on you, and with your song added, you could become the life ... the death of the party starting with you. Thus, you will need to always make sure that your AC is high, and you have decent equipment to be able to last out in various fights. Hopefully, the Guild you join will be strong enough to help you get your items, or at least collect some money and free loot to buy them with.  As an example, at level 30, I once saw a level 33 Bard that had all the Lambent stuff and the great weapons ... and in the end, this Bard was draining the group of more mana for healing (It was playing the Bard's Mana song) than any other player. This clearly is an indication of points in Strength, and nothing else to back it up. If you are gonna be strong, make sure that you STAY strong, and Stamina is a part of this equation. If you are going to do damage, make sure that you can do enough damage to help the criters die BEFORE you do.

Thus, an "average" stat distribution would be something like this. In a Half Elf Bard I like to have the Stamina higher than Strength and Agility higher than Dexterity. With this combination, you will get the Defensive numbers rising up at a reasonable pace. A Wood Elf Bard tends to have nice numbers for Agility and Dexterity, but bad ones in Stamina and Strength, and you will probably shore those up. On a Human, you probably should add some to Stamina, to bring it in line with the Strength, Agility to bring it in line with the Dexterity, and the rest in Charisma, as this one has the smallest amount of it.

So far, Strength, Stamina, Agility, Dexterity and Charisma are the main Statistics that show up. What about Intelligence?

Well, this is the one Statistic that most folks like to tell you that you don't need. Basically it dictates the pace that you can learn all the other skills that you have in the game, except one ... Alcohol Tolerance, since the basis on this one is the opposite ... the bottom end of the Intelligence scale. And it is great fun, but not something that many players enjoy, since the visual nature of it can get you into a dizzy quickly and ... we don't need to discuss that. BUT, it is hilarious and a lot of fun!

Intelligence is one Statistic that I have been studying real carefully. In one of my Bards, I purposedly exagerated this number, and the results are that almost all Statistics develop significantly faster than otherwise. By taking that "ratio" and comparing it to my main Bard at level 30, I became inclined to think that the extra points in Intelligence WILL NEVER hurt you. In fact, at level thirty, which takes about 10 hours to play through in a reasonable group, my weird algebraic comparisons seem to indicate that a 10% increase in Intelligence at the start of the game, is almost equal to half a bubble (10%) of experience n the whole level. What does this mean? To me, it means that you can learn most your skills FASTER, and there fore you are more efficient, with less misses all around in songs, and anything that you do. A lot of the RESIST factors in a song, or spell, are based on your abilities, and if it is top'd, then it usually only fails 5% of the time. If it is not top'd, the difference to the top gets added to this number, and the scale for Critters resisting things of a magical nature tends to double up in this area, meaning that with a skill level lower, you will not be as efficient as you would with a higher skill level factor.

Solutions vary, but I am not sure that any of us know the answer. I carry on my main Bard, several Intelligence items, which I put on so that I can practice various skills at certain times -- and these items are expensive, let me tell you! But I am not sure that the "occasional" rate of my working on these skills, is as efficient as they would be if these 5/10 points were placed in Intelligence at the start ... I have not had the guts to create an Intelligence and Charisma Wood Elf Bard as yet -- notice that the other two would not be suited for one of these tests, since we are trying to maximize things to see what the reactions are on the growth of all these skills ... but I do know that in the couple of Bards I have added Intelligence to, that skills take off on level 1 and not on Level 3, and this has been an important factor, and I think one that merits more discussion and evaluation.

But there is one problem ... All these skills top off at Level 40. Then you go to Level 50, and a new set of skills arrive, and additions to these skills are now added. In other words, at Level 41, your hitting skills will not gain anymore after they are top'd, until you reach Level 50, when a new scale defines your skill raising. The other problem is that with each "hell level" these skill improvements take longer by a factor of 10 to 20%. Used to be that at 25 you could max out the hitting skills in the first bubble. At level 40, you will be lucky to get these top'd before the 3rd or 4th bubble, and here I think that the use of those few points in Intelligence are very important, and helpfull. When you need the skills the most, you are missing a lot in your fights, and I think that it should be a priority for any Bard, to work on top'ing off their skills ASAP, and  as efficiently as possible. Since a Bard can not load much of anything to the secondary slots (Range) and there are no Range items that have Intelligence statistics in this game (makes sure that Casters do not stack up mana), then you have to settle for something like the McVaxious Horn on your hands to help. And while a nice boost, this is not enough ... at level 38, I have a heck of a time getting my Swimming skill past 170, just to give you an example.

Comparatively speaking, I believe that we lose efficiency in a lot of the fighting we do as we rise up in levels. What hides a lot of this at higher levels, is that you get buff'd nicely by an Enchanter, or Shaman, in a big dance, and the end result is that things happen so fast that it is impossible to tell anymore if you are efficient or not on your hitting. The only way to tell, is for you to duel someone with the weapon, and "nekkid", so you can parse out the damages and the misses, and the improvements in your skills, if any. But at 25, your skills flew by in the first bubble and your hitting efficiency was better (UNBUFFED) comparatively speaking, than it is at level 35 on the 1st bubble, as the skills are not top'd out. I only wish that I could come up with a log of all the damage I did, and when the skill moved, so I could chart this out. I believe that the skill raising thing at higher levels is slowed down intentionally to cover for the massive Weapons and Items that were coming out of Kunark, the power of which was causing the game to lose control over its "equality" and "balance". Making these skills not be efficient, helps the Power equipment be a little less efficient .. still more than what you had before, but probably not as much as one thinks. Once again, it is important to realize that at higher levels the buffing that you get is incrredibly good, and it works too well in disguising the real skill level that you are at. However, as I said before, with the same weapons for the last 15 levels, I can tell you that my efficiency has dropped dramatically, and that it appears to be on par with the skill level that I have for it. I have kept track of all my skills since level 25 or so, and the Excel Spreadsheet (thanks Rokk'y) is fantastic for it ... and you can see things move, or not move.

 

So here are a few suggestions, for each type of Bard, and a little blurb on what this Bard MIGHT be most usefull at in later levels ... which is the key. Do remember that I am doing this from the perspective of Level 38, and from what I have seen in the skill sheets for some 20 Bards in this game (same name except one mispelling). I imagine, and expect, that these skills continue on the basis that they have shown in those charts, with a few usual modifications here and there, but all in all the curves tend to go in one direction only.

 

The Suggestions

Race Str Sta Agi Dex Wis Int Cha Modifiers
Wood Elf 70 65 95 90 80 75 85 25

To make a fighting Wood Elf:                              15 in Strength and 10 in Stamina.
In this suggestion, you would be making a Bard that has a little Strength and is capable of backing it up, with some Stamina. Generally, what will happen is that the Stamina bar will drop seriously and the weapons will begin missing a lot in any prolonged fight. For a Bard this may not be so bad, when they can add the Song #3, but generally, the idea behind a fight, for this Bard, would be not to stink up the neighborhood with their fighting. Light weapons are recommended and the faster they are the better. The 75 Stamina will be short and then some, when it comes to Dual Wield, and one might consider one Necklace to help support that. Do remember that Strength items are easy to get in this game, and later on you get so much Haste material that it almost makes these numbers redundant.

To make an instrument wielding Wood Elf:           5 in Agility, 10 in Intelligence and 10 in Charisma.
This should get a few folks ticked off. Two things are needed to be able to hold the instruments, and play them in the Rathe Mountains, and beyond, and use the likes of Song #27 (Charm). One, is the ability to take a little punishment, which should only happen when the songs miss (often!), and when one is tied up in situations where Lag (both Server and Internet Latency) comes into play. Often you won't see the first or second hit until you notice the bar has dropped one bubble. Agility, is a bit of a Defensive thing, and dictates the amount you get punished for. Were it not that you have to maximize the amount of time that the Charisma songs last, I would say that the higher this number is, the more efficient things would be. A Bard with very high Agility is tough to bring down, as would be a Ranger if they placed points there. Charisma is needed to help, and you have to cover the 130 spot and 140 or so. With two Bracers, a Mask and 2 Rings, you will be at 134 and that will get things going. You can also get 2 Earrings and make it 144 or so. This will carry you very well in the Rathe Mountains. Charisma items are not that hard, they are out there, and 6 of these are Jewel Craft items. The Mask comes out of Lower Guk and you have to get help to acquire it. The earlier the best.

 

Race Str Sta Agi Dex Wis Int Cha Modifiers
Half  Elf 75 70 90 95 60 75 85 25

To make a fighting Half Elf:                          10 Points in Strength, 10 in Stamina and 5 in Agility

The hard thing about a fighting Bard is that no matter what you do, the numbers will always be weak. As such, in this example, merely adding all the points into Strength is not going to do much. You will dish out the damage, but your skills won't improve at a better pace than usual, and neither will your Defensive abilities. I find the lack of Defensive ability a problem, since one of the things that you can do later is use Charm to protect group members from certain death, although I have seen many Bards simply say "I gave the healer mana" and leave it at that ... meaning that the Bard just kept on hitting and ignored the group's status. As such, I feel that this type of Bard playing really defeats the purpose of a "Bard" in a group, other than a simple Mana Battery which almost all fighting Bards are. The main question will be what happens as the game changes from a Strength minded game into something else where Magical and Charisma numbers are the most important thing to work with. The game in Kunark introduced hard critters that had very important Resistances and Magical abilities and the Bard was needed to help protect the players. In Vellious, things are too new to check out yet, but a strong Magical Resistance is mandatory, it seems, and this means that this Bard will have to use a song and instrument at a certain point, and this may not be the best attribute this Bard has. As the game develops, the Bard merely playing the Mana song, is coming to a close. Clerics and Enchanters nowadays will proudly say ... "don't need mana".

To make a Magical Bard:                                 5 in Agility, 10 in Intelligence and 10 in Charisma

The importance of the Intelligence here, is to help the learning of all skills move faster than usual. Not to have a skill drag on forever to improve, is an important thing for this type of Bard. At level 35 or so, the raising of skills drops off considerably, and top'ing off any of them is a problem. You can counter some of that with Intelligence. Remember that with a Charisma Bard, you have to be able to get the Instrument skills picked up. Your Wind and Brass skills need to be at the top, to ensure that they only miss the usual 5%, not more than that at all, if possible.

A lot of folks, would take the 5 poins in Agility and add them to Charisma, and I think that is a good idea, were it not for the fact in the Agility test that I did all the way to level 10 in many Bards, the Defense ability rose much faster if the Agility number was higher, as it would in a Monk, for example. Thus I like, personally, to add some points to Agility. Many will disagree here.

 

Race Str Sta Agi Dex Wis Int Cha Modifiers
Human 80 75 75 85 75 75 80 25

To make a fighting Bard:                                         10 in Strength and 5 in Stamina and 5 into Dexterity

This is the toughest one to try. I have seen a couple of these Strength minded Bards, and they were all punishment Bards, and I think the reason for it is the really low Agility. But this Bard, to do damage, only needs Strength and some Dexterity to help the weapons it carries do a little proc'ing. Personally, I am not sure why would anyone select a "fighting" Bard, when there are other hybrids that can do better damage, and get better attack ratios. This Bard, at its best in Weapons and such, will not come close to even 85% of what the instruments and songs can do. But for its life, it is a great support fighter and can play a mean Mana song. Personally I prefer the ability to help other players in the group, and this Bard is not exactly suited to that at all. The other thing that is noticeable here is the low Intelligence number, which will cause many skills to lag seriously behind, other than the main fighting skills. Instrument skills? What's that?

I can not say, that these suggestions are perfect, and that they are perfectly well thought out. These are based on my many small level Bards, and I wish there was an efficient way to take them to level 20. I can tell you that the two toughest Bards to bring down (not my main one, unffortunately) are the ones that have Agility in them. In the Povar Moshkiae, at level 20, it has little equipment, but lots of Agility and it is capable of holding itself rather well at the Keep/Highpass one level too soon. I hope to take this one to level 30 to find out a little more about it. Skills are in excellent shape though, as I added some Intelligence to it.
