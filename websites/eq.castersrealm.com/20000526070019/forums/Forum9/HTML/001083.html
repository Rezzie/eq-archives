I find the elemental and werewolf illusions to be extremely useful for one reason -- you don't change your vertical point of view when you sit and stand. If you're like me and you meditate at every possible moment (e.g., in between stuns), this prevents you from getting seasick.

Some illusions also make you smaller and therefore get you out of the way, so your group members can better target your enemies.

I also like the air elemental illusion for navigating certain dungeons, since it's a free levitate that doesn't require me to carry around components.

I started a thread over in Strategies and Tactics a while ago about Tactical uses of Illusions. Had some pretty good suggestions in it.

Personally, when I am fighting in dungeons, I always keep Illusion Air Elemental on myself. This provides me with several advantages.

> When you med, there is no annoying point of view change when you sit.

> For running around your group and retargeting mobs for mes, this is great. You are floating over and around the battle and can move freely without having to move around the tanks, you can take the more direct route. This is probably the most important reason I use it.

> PArty members can see thru you. So in those tight corridors such as the AM spawn or really any area in Guk, their view is not obstructed as much as it would be.

When I start getting encumbered and no group member has a str spell, I use werewolf. Encumberance makes your agility go to crap and you start getting hit more and harder. Remember to cast Augmentation on your self for that matter. I used to not do this, but I tried it once and it works great. I can get my agility up to 137 with it on and this helps a lot when a mob starts beating on you.
