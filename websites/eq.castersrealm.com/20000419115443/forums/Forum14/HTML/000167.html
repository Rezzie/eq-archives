A very good question this one...

And from my experience, my priorities are CHA, AGI then INT. I am also level 40 atm, just passing, not setting up camp, mind you.

When I am in combat, I usually have 201 Cha, 170 - 180 Agi and about 160 Int. I found that Agi is more important than Int for me, as it means that I am much more likely to get a spell off with a mob beating on me. In fact, at 38, I was camping the Ancient Cyclops in OOT with a 49 Shaman friend. I had a Red Cyclops beating on me and he missed 8 times in a row, then hit for 16 and 32 before dropping dead. Admittedly, I had 200+ Agi, as my shaman buddy has an awsome Agi buff. With less HP's than a tank, Agi is a winner...

I like the idea of Int, as 5 int will give you roughly your level in Mana, e.g. at 40, 160 int will give you +1280 mana and 200 Int would give you +1600 mana. But in practice, especially in L Guk, Sol B and the like, I'd rather have any agro mob miss me than have mana for an extra spell or two that I couldnt get off (Unless Enc's had Evac perhaps!).

I have had numerous situations where I know the extra Agi has saved my life... for example, in one situation I had chain area mezzed about 6 or 7 SK/GK's in Sol B thanks to a train... and we were working them down. Now the group who were running from the train came back and decided to POKE my mezzed mobs... in fact they all managed to poke a different one... great group coordination!

Yes, they were trying to help us, but after 2 or 3 mezzes, the mobs only saw the little green enchanter and started beating on me. With 5 angry mobs on me, I still managed to cast enough to survive, despite the new group continually waking them up again. I Love AGI!!!

So for me its CHA, AGI then INT... But thats just my opinion, try a few tests out for yourself

Come on Tudamorf i would welcome your input on this one as im getting a bit closer to 200/200 char/int and want to know whether to go agi, ac or mr(75 now).

Only test I did on AC versus agility was a long time ago for the shaman board, and it involved comparing the +45 AGI shaman buff with turtle skin, a shaman +AC buff that adds an identical amount of AC (both added 10 AC IIRC). The test involved getting hit about 300 times and comparing the average damage and % misses.

Anyway, the post and the analysis are long gone, but I remember the result was that there was less than 0.5% variation in the mean damage between the two, and there was a slightly lower (but not statistically significant) percentage of misses with the AGI buff over the AC buff.

My overall conclusion after the test was, wild rumors notwithstanding, there was no detectable difference between +45 agility and +10 AC on the test subject.

Here's a probability distribution of hits that's still lying around on my web host, as you can see the distribution did not change in any noticeable way with agility versus AC:

So my general thinking on AC is don't bother with agility at all (unless it's under 75), and just use whatever equipment raises your "AC" figure as high possible. Mine is a little over 600 now (buffed) and I'm pretty happy with that. If I cast shiftless deeds first, I can fight a seafury cyclops with my pet, and tank the whole fight (yes, from beginning to end) without dying, and without using any Rune/Berserker spells mid-fight. Also, when shifts and skews get resist-happy, I don't have to worry about 3 or 4 dogs or frogs beating me to a pulp. Sometimes, the total damage doesn't even make it through Rune III.

On CHA, I haven't done any tests (yet). I'm starting to get really bored again with EQ so I might do a CHA test next, but from the other tests I've seen, it appears that (a) CHA in the low to mid 100s range is noticeably better for mesmerizing than CHA below 100, (b) CHA above 150 or so does little or nothing to improve the success of mesmerize. Overall I think most enchanters greatly overrate this stat, even though they really have never done (nor seen) a test that demonstrates that CHA needs any special attention.

As for MR, I wouldn't focus on that either. With your inherently high magic resistance as a caster, arch shielding (always used, gives +15 or +20 MR -- forget which), and group resist magic (max +55 MR), you will already have MR well over 100 at level 50, and that's plenty. Remember that magic resistance will only protect you against magic-based attacks -- e.g., root, fear, and certain magic-based spells (enchanter spells, wizard's rend, etc.) -- and that's only a small part of the spell-based attacks you will come up against.

Instead of MR, I would focus on fire and cold resistance, which are the two main forms of spell-based damage attacks you'll be facing, and the two areas where enchanters are pretty weak. I would strongly recommend a crown of the froglock king at higher levels, because it has amazingly high AC (for casters), provides +20 cold/fire resist, is extremely light, and has no graphic (yay!). This is undoubtedly one of the best pieces of headgear for casters at later levels, on par with the insidious halo.

Anyway, here's a list of items I often see on enchanters, but that I wouldn't touch with a 10-foot staff:

  • silk evening tunic -- 1 AC? get real

  • the +7 CHA jewelcraft bracers -- wrists are an important AC slot, and wearing two of these means 0 AC gain for those slots. If you want, wear one, and wear a nice +AC piece on the other (I use a ring of the ancients)

  • the +9 CHA jewelcraft veil -- if you're wearing this over the incandescent mask, which has 3 AC, +5 INT, and +7 CHA, well then you're a lost cause

And here's a list of equipment that most enchanters don't think about, but which I think represents equipment that provides the best benefit for the given slot:

  • froglock crown -- see above, this thing is amazing

  • platinum fire wedding rings -- +5 AC +55 HP will keep you alive longer, especially if you have two

  • wrist pieces with AC (e.g., ring of the ancients, gold chitin bracer, vox bracer) -- the wrist is a major source of AC, don't waste it

I'm sure I'll think of more later.
