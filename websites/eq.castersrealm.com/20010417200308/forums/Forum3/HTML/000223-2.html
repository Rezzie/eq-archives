Well, I lack the perspective of a higher level enchanter, as I have only reached level 16, but I have seen some requests in this thread for the opinions of lower-level enchanters, so here is mine:

This nerf doesn't affect me at all.

Personally, I gave a dagger to my pet maybe 5 times from level 1 to 16. Most of the time it was killed instantly by something. From that, the trouble of buying them in town, etc. I just stopped doing it. I ALWAYS get rid of my animation if I am in a group, so that I can have Charm available if I need it. I do not use Charmed monsters as normal pets, I simply use Charm as a stopgap method to remove one monster from the attacking group and use it to dish some damage out to its companions. As for my personal opinions on the matter, I think that animations should be weak, barely strong enough for us to solo with. These are not elemental forces from another dimension, or even foul undead. It's a silly little shield and a weapon. I was suprised it worked as well as it did. As for enchanters not dealing out much damage, and having one of their major sources of damage taken away, again, I feel this is not a concern. As an Enchanter, I feel my role is not doing damage but preventing it. Nearly all our spells revolve around preventing damage, by weakening or slowing a monster, stunning it, or removing it from the fight entirely. If there is any other damage caster in the party, I drop Sanity Warp so I can memorize another debuff. And I have yet to see why Whirl TYH is such a bad spell. This is probably my second-most used spell. About 1/3 of the time it takes the monster out of action for the rest of the fight. I have NEVER had it last less than twice the duration of the AE stun, unless someone in the group is silly and bashes it or stuns it. And it costs 5 more mana than Languid Pace. Languid is a nice spell, but in terms of sheer numbers of attacks, it would have to be a much longer combat than mine typically are to effectivly remove as many attacks as Whirl does. I have actually been in several groups with a second enchanter, and from my experience they tend to rely on their damage spells. I see lots of Choke, lots of Chaotic Feedback. I have been with enchanters who didn't have their AE stun or Mesmerize memorized, and who have never cast a single debuff while I grouped with them. Now granted, they may not have needed to after they realized I was more than happy to take over this role. In any case, the point of all this was to say that in my fairly low-level opinion, Enchanters are by FAR the best at damage prevention, with so many different ways to stop it. Shamans are second-best, but they don't get as many options as we do. Nobody else even touches it. I really enjoy doing this, and that is why I play an enchanter. I don't want to sell enchanted weapons or items and make a ton of money. I don't want to run around with a mega-pet and solo yellows. I want to keep my group from getting hurt and ease the burden on the healers in the group.

Althien of E. Marr

Oh, one minor thing I forgot to mention. Would you like it if they allowed the speed increase of a weapon but decreased the damage accordingly? For example a pet with a dagger might hit fast but for 50% damage, while a pet with a two-handed sword might deal 150% damage, but hit VERY slowly. This is probably FAR too much trouble to insert into the code, and thus would never happen, but would allow pet users to customize their pets MUCH more. What do you think?

Abashi's attitude about enchanters sickens me. His arguments include only what he wants you to know, and conveniently leave out the points he doesn't want you to know.

Case in point: if enchanter pets were exempted from the weapon-speed nerf, "they'd be able to put out more damage than the necro pet can currently. Enchanters have the ability to cast better speed buffs than either of the true pet-using classes."

Let's analyze how ridiculous this statement is:

1. What difference does it make if enchanter pets put out a bit more damage IN A GIVEN AMOUNT OF TIME than any other pets? Their ridiculously low amount of hps guarantees that the necro and mage pets will live 3-4 times longer, so over the long haul, the necro and mage pets VASTLY out damage enchanter pets. Our pets NEED to do major damage during their short lifespans, so that we have some kind of chance of finishing the critter off when they die. Funny how Abashi points out the damage comparison to other pets, and conveniently leaves out the fact that this barely makes up for the other glaring deficiencies of our pets, the net result of which is that our pets were ALREADY by far the weakest pets pre-nerf (which they should have been). Now they're even worse.

2. Abashi points out that enchanters have the best speed increasing spells in the game, and that this will replace the loss of the weapon speed reduction, as though we didn't use speed spells AND the weapon speed reduction. HELLO, IS ANYBODY HOME IN THERE? *shouts in Abashi's ear, waves hand in front of Abashi's glazed eyes*. Enchanters used BOTH the speed increasing spell line, AND the dual wield speed reduction, and these two things together barely allowed our animations to survive a battle some of the time. Often, both together STILL weren't enough, and we still had to zone or die. Abashi's comment is like telling someone, "You don't need the socks I just took from you, you have a pair of shoes you can use instead. Your feet will be fine in the snow", as though the person had never worn their shoes AND their socks at the SAME TIME.

What an idiot. Or maybe he thinks WE are the idiots, and won't notice the glaring omissions, contradictions, and inconsistencies in his statements. More likely, he just doesn't care.

Abashi likes to point out that enchanters have a greater variety of spells than any other class, and on this point, he is correct. Too bad that half of them are completely useless due to bugs, or they literally have no use. Some examples, you ask?

1. Who has ever used a single one of the sight line of spells? I never have, and none of the many enchanters I know ever has (and I was the leader of an enchanter only guild on MM for months, we had around 50 members).

2. What about the Whirl line of spells that are totally worthless, since anything you do in combat breaks them almost immediately (Verant claimed this was a bug and promised many months ago to fix them, but we still wait...).

3. Has any enchanter ever successfully used a spell from the lull line without pulling an entire camp on your head? I rarely have, and I'm 39th level, I quit using them altogether after a half dozen deaths and no successes.

4. Then we have the ever popular dual lines of spells to remove enchantments. Is it me, or does it seem like they only remove a harmful enchantment after removing EVERY GOOD ENCHANTMENT FIRST?

5. Then there's the incredible line of illusion spells that do ABSOLUTELY NOTHING USEFUL with only 3 exceptions I can think of: Water elemental gives water breathing, fire elemental gives fire shield, and troll gives regen. The others are nearly worthless, they often give no noticeable faction increase with the race in question, and they DON'T HIDE YOUR RELIGION!! What the hell is up with that?

And what's up with this incredible magic resistance that enchanters have on every spell in their books? I grouped with a cleric 6 levels lower than myself last week to kill troll guards, and after tashani, he was having a 20-25% resist rate on his damage and stun spells, while I had a 90% resist rate on EVERY KIND OF SPELL I TRIED TO CAST!! This included DoT, DD, Debuff, Stun, Mez, EVERYTHING!! What the hell is up with that? The only value I had in the group was pulling with Tashani, and then taking most of the damage after every single spell I cast aggroed the guard on me, even though 90% were resisted. Hey, but at least I found use for the damage shield of the fire elemental illusion. Without the cleric chain casting heals on me, I would have died in nearly every combat. But I guess that makes sense, since I actually did a few points of damage on every third or fourth guard, unlike the two tanks who were each double hitting for 50-100 points at a time...

Don't get me wrong, I'm not one of those people who claim that every class is better than mine, and my class sucks, and is unplayable. I have always loved the enchanter class, and found many versatile ways to play it. I just felt the need to point out the many bugs, useless spells, outright problems, and oversights involved with the enchanter class, because when you look at the nerf to animations in this context, it becomes clear that it is MUCH more of a hit to our class than Abashi wants anyone to believe. Personally, due to my ability to only play an hour or two at a time except very late at night (or early in the morning, depending on your point of view), I have always found it difficult to get into a good group and still have any time to do anything before I have to log off. Without my pet, I would never have made it to level 29, I soloed about 90% of the time before 29th (as I said, this was out of necessity, not choice).

It's time for enchanters to stand up and demand that for once, we should get compensated for this nerf to our class! We should also demand that Verant stop giving our unique abilities to other casting classes (lowering MR, mezzing, etc). At the very least, let's demand that if they're going to continue bending us over a barrel, they at least better use a little Vaseline! =P
