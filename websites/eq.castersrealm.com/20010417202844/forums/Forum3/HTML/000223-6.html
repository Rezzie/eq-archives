Baelish,

I would like to add my own comments to the ones you added to Sethilins's
regarding Verant's recent changes to pets and other adjustments that
have either by design or oversight narrowed the role of enchanters to
simply being an auxilory group support character class restricted to
mezz, charm, stun, clarity and minor buffs and debuffs.

First I'd like to make a brief statement about why I chose the Enchanter
class. Before playing EQ I was very actively involved with a card game
call Magic the Gathering(tm). Without going into a long winded tutorial
on the game the idea was that two magicians practicing any of 5 types of
magic lets call them light, dark, fire, nature, water would duel each
other to the death. The idea was that two mages would sit opposite one
another and randomly suffle their spell book each in turn drawing spells
from their book and casting them so as to do 20 life points of damage to
the other. The first mage to have his life points fall to zero or less
at the end of their turn or be unable to draw a spell from their spell
book (deck) would lose the duel.

Each class of magic therefore had to have ways of dealing damage and
defending against taking damage. However, if one was to analyze the
five types of magic, blue (the magic of water) had the least number of
direct or indirect damage spells. As a result in the early days of the
game few people bothered to build a spell book using any blue magic.
However, what blue did have was a lot of spells that denied other
casters from casting their spells and redirecting the damage of other
spells onto the caster and spells to allow the blue caster to cast more
spells per turn. Blue was the magic of denial. I won many tournaments
playing primarily blue magic based decks. In one extreme case I
constructed a deck with only 1 in 60 cards that could deal damage. Yet
I won and I won A LOT!
