Was the enchanter nerf a good thing? Yes, for two reasons.

1) Logic - giving a pet a low-delay weapon SHOULD decrease damage output at the same time that it increases attack speed. This was not the case, and Verant addressed it properly (though somewhat rudely).

2) Role - get this through your skulls, enchanters: you are NOT a solo class. Your pets were far too powerful considering your primary spells. Imagine yourselves lucky for your short time "off the leash".

Should enchanters be compensated for what they lost? I don't think so, for two reasons.

1) They didn't deserve to have what they "lost" in the first place.

2) Unlike the necromancer, who lost some soloing capability and required additions in order to become more group-friendly, enchanters were already (and remain) very desirable to any intelligent and well played group.

However, regarding what enchanters should rightfully have, when the hell is Verant gonna get off its duff and put in item buffs for enchanters?! Makes perfect sense! Let enchanters cast buffs directly on items (weapons and armor mostly) to add special effects to them. Resistance and ac buffs for armors, delay and damage buffs for weapons, plus special properties for items of all types. Allow enchanters to add lesser sow and regen effects to boots and chestplates respectively, to apply one time dot or dd procs to weapons, etc, etc. The buffs should have limited durations roughly on par with current buff averages. These item buffing capabilities would go a LONG ways towards both fulfilling the enchanter's role and increasing the class's viability. I maintain that a well-played enchanter is still the nastiest non-npc you will ever encounter in EQ, but they should start enchanting items in addition to other beings.
