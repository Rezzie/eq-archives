Just a couple of notes on the scanning issue...

1) It will in no way hinder DoS style attacks. They are apparently referring to some hack to the client that causes a server glitch. That is easily stopped merely by checking the game files (like a CRC check.) DoS attacks will still rock them if they aren't properly firewalled (and even if they are given enough skill.)

2) The ONLY real way for them to check for ShowEQ (or winshowEQ) is to see if you have a NIC in promiscuous mode on your network. This is illegal and doesn't really prove anything since that is a common practice. The tasklist can be modified to not show the program at all if you are dumb enough to run it on the same box. Not a whole lot to scan for, now is there?

3) Even if they do scan your tasklist, how do they know what stuff is? There are tons of things that could be running in your system. Verant can't possibly know what they all are. Personally, I use a lot of nonstandard apps like an alternate shell. How can we expect them to have a clue what they are looking at? So they can ban me on a whim because I run some unrelated program and they don't know what it is? Thats BS.

4) This whole situation came about because Verant didn't encrypt the packets in the first place. They knew this would happen. It only became an issue when a distributable version of the program was released. They failed to ensure their security from day one and now they want us to give up ours to cover their mistake.

5) Your IP is public. It's known by every system you connect to, just like your home address is public. Your data is not public just like your mail isn't. There is a huge difference here.

6) Can you trust Verant? I dunno. I'm inclined to believe that GZ etc don't have ulterior motives, but they aren't the company. Your data is worth a lot of money. Your email addresses, what software you have installed, etc can be very tempting to lift for a company. And just because they are being honest today (that is still not a given) doesn't mean they will be tomorrow. But if you let them in without keeping an eye out, you'll never know.

7) Even if Verant does stay honest, its a precident. That means the next great company can push even further and just farm your data. If you give up a little bit of your privacy and/or rights every time someone asks you to, you eventually wont have any left.

8) The EULA is not legally binding yet (they are trying to do this in some states...very bad stuff) so you can click agree and it doesn't really mean much in court.

I will gladly allow Verant to sniff around my system if they agree to allow me to do the same to their systems. I have a feeling they don't want me in their systems any more than I want them in mine. Do they have something to hide? Are they running ShowHydian? Of course not. They want their privacy just like I want mine.

I think ShowEQ sucks as much as anyone. I wouldn't imagine using it. Thats not the issue here. What is the issue is your privacy and your rights to protect your network and data.

Anyone who is so willing to sell themselves out for a game, please send me an image of your hard drive and give me root access to your machine so I can get violate you too. While you're at it, send me a copy of your house keys. You might as well get it over with now.
