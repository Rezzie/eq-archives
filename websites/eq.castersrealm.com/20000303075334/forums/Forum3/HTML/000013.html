Author Topic:   A Disturbing Trend Discouraging New Players
Neydenya
unregistered 		posted 12-11-1999 06:20 PM           
Ok here's my beef about the many patches that have shown up in the past few months:

There is a disturbing trend that shows a great bias towards new players. Namely, certain powerful or useful items that characters started early will probably have are almost impossible if not impossible to obtain for anyone started after these changes.

This rant relates to 3 particular items: the manastone, the j boots and the guise of the deceiver.

1. The manastone - it doesn't drop anymore! I know there was some talk of a possible quest but at this point its not in the game anymore. Priest classes without a manastone are much less effective than priest classes with a manastone. And I noticed the HUGE difference a manastone can make for an enchanter as well. They spend a lot of time buffing and I watched the other day as one single enchanter buffed a whole dragon team (about 40 people +)with SLW and Rune 4 and clarity and the wis/intel buffs using his manastone because the clerics healed him. My enchanter doesn't have SLW yet but i know its about 300 mana - someone starting a new enchanter or cleric or druid or shaman is at a severe disadvantage compared to people who started their characters early and were able to obtain this item.

2. J-boots: Ok I know there is a quest and I know they still drop for people under about level 35 but what about the people who have to do this quest? My main character just missed the change and was a bit too high to camp at Najena for them. He's very unlucky LOL. I have tried to find the ancient cyclops for over 2 months to no avail - I know where he spawns and i've even brought ranger friends to track for hours but he's never shown up. At least with the old hours of camping one could be assured that they would get a pair of J-Boots if they put in X number of hours and waited in the long lines. Right now all my spellcasting friends can solo guards in their spare time or when they can't find a group and have got a number of levels ahead of me doing this. Again, people who did not start their characters in March or when the game first came out have been discriminated against I feel. At this rate, all my friends will have been 50 for months when I finally get there and I still won't have j-boots.

3. The Guise (or mask) of the deceiver. This item although annoying - i'm an enchanter and didn't like all those dark elves running around - WAS A GODSEND TO THE LARGER RACES. I really pitty the troll or orgre or barbarian who has got to about level 30 or so and who did not get one before they were changed. SoLB - one of the best experience spots in the game for 35+ is basically off limits to them now unless they can find a shaman to shrink them and this isn't always easy to do. I'm sure many other places are too small too but at least on my server shamans in 35+ dungeons aren't all that common.

Thus, there is a disturbing trend that I see discourages players who did not start their characters early - or have not started them yet - from playing. Granting players who have played longer special abilities that new players cannot hope to ever get (with the exception of j boots - i know i'm just unlucky about that one) seems incredibly unbalancing.

To conclude: imagine 6 months from now ... 2 guild with two full groups in fear. One guild is an old guild - all the clerics and classes who benefit from them have manastones. The other guild is a newer guild everyone is 50 but NONE of the priest classes or buffing classes have manastones. A train comes on these two groups who are sitting medding just after a huge fight. Everyone is down to below 20 mana. The old guild uses their manastones to get out a few quick heals but find that its too much. The wizards use their manastones to get the mana for evacs and they escape without any deaths. The new guild uses up all their remaining mana trying to heal. The wizards are just a bit short of mana for the evacs and they all die. THAT'S IMBALANCING! THAT PREVENTS NEW PEOPLE FROM WANTING TO PLAY OR NEWER CHARACTERS FROM WANTING TO CONTINUE THEIR CHARACTERS.

So to Verdant/Brad/whoever can help: Please do something about these issues with manastone being the first priority followed by the guise of the deceiver followed by the virtual impossibility of getting j boots if you haven't and you are over 35. That's my rant sorry I ran on so long but I like the game very much and I see this very disturbing trend which I don't like.

Just a few points I'd like to make.

If wizards are going oom on the planes, then they deserve to die. If a wiz gets to the point where a battle is so close that they have only enough mana left for an evac or a nuke, they better be picking the evac if they want to keep their friends. After all, what happens if that final nuke that is going to save the party from death is resisted?

The same goes for clerics. There is only one way possible that an entire party has pretty much zero health and the cleric is oom, and that's if the cleric was burning during the battle. Same thing goes for them as the wiz. If they don't know how to play their class, then bye bye. If a battle gets to the point where a cleric HAS TO burn all their mana on nukes for whatever reason and the entire group is pretty much dead, the smart wiz that left enough mana for an evac better be casting it. Sure it may be boring, but a cleric that plays their role to perfection should be medding during the battle and healing after it, not burning and then plopping down to med with everyone else after the battle. Some classes have jobs in the beginning of battles, some have jobs in the middle, some have jobs at the end and some have jobs after the battle. Personally, I'd rather group with a Cleric that sat on his or her butt during fights (as long as the battle wasn't turning decidely against us), a Wiz that never goes oom rather than a wiz that's always oom at the end of a fight and a cleric that has a "I'm a Wizard!" syndrome.

The guise used to let large races get into small areas. I have no problem that this is gone, BUT there *should* have been areas designed where only large races could go that smaller folk couldn't get to. Because of the dynamics of the game, this probably will never be possible however. This isn't a good enough reason to bring back the guise though. I blame Verant for their short sightedness on not designing "large race only" areas to compliment the "small race only" areas.

The game is called Everquest, not Evercamp (though it appears that it should be most times). I agree with the idea of removing the J-Boots from Najena and making them a quest. They unfortunately moved the camp from Najena to SRo though. This is a prime example as to why Verant needs to implement a random quest generator. You find Hasten, hail him, he gives you a random quest. Once this quest is fullfilled, you give him the items and get your J-Boots. At any one time there can be 100 people questing for J-Boots, but they are not all sitting in SRo waiting for an Ancient Cyclops to spawn. Some people are killing a griff in NK, some are killing HG's in Rathe Mountains, etc. At most you are competing against 5 or 10 other people for an item rather than a mile long wait to nuke the next Ancient that pops (if it even does for that matter). This would also help *gasp* grouping! 6 peole go to SRo to get a ring of the ancients. All 6 want J-Boots. Who gets the first one (or second, third)? Fine, you have a loot order worked out. The person at the end probably isn't too happy with waiting for 3 months till their turn to loot comes up. Plus, how long are the other 5 going to stick around after they've had their J-Boots for the last 2 months? Alternatively, first we go kill the Griff in NK that person 1 needs the xyz from, then we'll head to SRo and kill the Sand Giant that person 2 needs, then we'll go to Rathe Mountains and kill the Hill Giant that I need xyz from. No competition and it probably won't take 3 months for everyone to get the items they need for their INDIVIDUAL J-Boot quests.

I dunno. Just how *I* see things. The way I look at it it's Verants game and they can do whatever the xxxx the want with it. We as consumers can look at the individual changes and say "That's ones fine, that one is ok, I don't like that one, This one really xxxxxx me off!" and decide at that point whether we want to keep giving them money or not. For me, the changes, while I may not agree with some of them, aren't enough to make me want to quit. That being said I stop worrying about it (the change) and continue paying them for a great game. When it gets to the point where a change does make me want to quit, oh well. I've got my moneys worth from Everquest.
