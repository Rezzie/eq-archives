Well, I currently have a 50 shaman, 41 warrior, 40 paladin. I've played all of them extensively in groups and solo. I am sort of on the fence on this issue. Imagine what EQ would be like if EVERYONE was able to solo. A dozen level 50s would be able to camp everything in lower guk. I think the overcrowding issue would magnify.

There are also advantages to being in a group. You can handle multiple pulls with less risk. There is less downtime, so the number of kills would balance out the the experience for the single kill when soloing.

By far, the shaman I have is the most powerful solo, disregarding level (I was soloing pairs of minos in lower guk at level 35, my 41 warrior still can barely solo one).
Take this shaman to a dragon raid or planes raid and he is pretty much a buff machine with minimal damage potential.

What I'm saying is that there are pros and cons to both sides. If tanks were buffed up to the soloing capabilities of casters, there would be a lot few casters going on big raids. Imagine a group of 4 upgraded warriors with a cleric or 2, or an optional enchanter. Who the heck would want magicians or wizards in a group anymore?

I've played lots of classes to a respectable level and I'd say each one has it's strengths and weaknesses. There could be some things done, I don't think everything is balanced as far as classes go, but the discrepancy isn't as great as most people perceive in my opinion.

- Casters can solo yellows for more exp per kill

- Casters can attack higher level mobs than an equal level melee for more exp per kill either grouped or solo

- Casters don't die nearly as often as melee and recover from death quicker (gate/bind). Not dying is more important at higher levels where you lose so much exp per death

- Casters are effective naked and can buy spells, melee has to camp equipment and is ineffetive naked.

I play a rogue part time while my friends catch up in level to my mage (hint: we started at the same time). I would NEVER play melee as my primary character and will probably quit my rogue once he needs to travel simply because he cannot bind in convenient locations.

Even amoung melee classes there is little balance. Rogues cannot solo at all because they need a tank AND a healer to use their backstab. Pickpocket exludes them from groups (and now can get you banned!). But among melee only monks and rogues do enough damage fast enough to be even marginally useful in a group with casters. Warriors are nothing more than manasinks.

The reality is that melee is NOT needed in this game. They deal damage too slowly; they require to much mana to support; they take too long to recover from deaths. Mage and necro pets deal and absorb damage just as well and don't need a healer/buffer.

If you look at how the game is played, the ONLY role for melee is pulling, but in most cases groups camp a site and are prefectly capable of pulling with spells back to the waiting pets and DD mavens.

The IDEAL group? 2 mages, 1 necro, 1 enchanter, 1 druid and 1 bard. Gives you massive DD, 3.5 tanks, mana regen, evac, crowd control, buffs and all the healing that low hp casters require. Indoors this group rocks, but outdoors and it is even better.

I've primary tanked the AoF with only 3 90 points heals... 270 points of healing with me taking the beating from the AoF for 90% of the fight (didn't take the HT though)... and this when I was in my low 30's. The casters couldn't cast because of a few other mobs that wandered in. Anyone but a warrior would have crumpled and died. My warrior lived. That's the power of the melee classes. The ability to take a pounding. Yes, you can't go at it alone against any but light blues. Yes, pets are nuts. But in a balanced group, tanks can fight non-stop like no casters can. It's all plus and minus. Short fight goes to caster. Long fight (with healing of course) advantage is to tanks. Chance of surviving one mob... advantage caster, chance of surviving multiple mobs beating on you, advantage tank. Used to take my enchanter into Cazic... in the CY, if a train, I'd be dead before I reached the doors. My warrior would routinely make it out from further in, taking the time to turn the attention of as many mobs as possible onto himself and off the casters (basically always one of the last ones out), and he'd make it out. Again and again and again. The only times I didn't is when I'd get stuck (lizards blocking the doorway leading to TR1, the spiraling swim up from the SG).

I play both caster and tank. I play each for different reasons. I play my enchanter because I love the class, but only if I trust those I'm with... and I'm the first to die if things go wrong. I play my warrior because I love the class. I take him anywhere, will tank anything. If I die, I'm the only one to die (barring idiot casters that run around screaming "get it off me, get it off me!")... but most of the time I won't. I'll make it out with a half or a quarter of a bub of hp. And that's with mobs beating on me the whole way.

From the beginning, with my warrior, I accepted that I wouldn't be solo'ing at higher levels. I wanted some ability to do so though, so I did take troll to reduce healing time. And, yah, I've partied in groups with no casters... relying either on hybrid healing spells or natural healing/spreading damage around.

I see others commenting here too, about rogues being worthless (not true, they do damage right up there with monks). About wizards solo'ing with root (high level nukes break high level root spells pretty much every time). I see comments about how high level warriors can't solo greens without getting clobbered (my guess is you have to be 45+ to be here... though I'm pretty sure I'll be solo'ing xp giving blues at 42 still). A warrior in my guild (50th) can and does solo HG's. Of course, he's got all top of the line gear... but then, a tank's power is so gear dependent.

Is everything perfect? No. I wouldn't mind seeing healing kicked up another point at 40... if only for tanks. I wouldn't mind seeing a caster class get a bind II that will let them bind anyone anywhere but in dungeons. I wouldn't mind some way to make it easier to find groups. But overall the balance is there. Don't forget that against some mobs, all us casters can do is help the tanks.

I wanted to state a word or two concerning the perception of "balance" between melee and caster classes. Not too long ago, I was
playing a 13th level ranger (on the Nameless)
. I was in SRo (a dangerous place for me)
and I watched as a spellcaster (whom I did
a /who on to discover that he was two levels
LOWER than me) sent his pet to attack a
monster that could easily have killed me --
if I recall correctly, it conned yellow to
me. While his pet kept the monster occupied,
the caster nuked it with spells. I didn't
time the combat, but it was relatively brief.

I haven't played a caster, so I speak from
a position of ignorance, but I fail to see
how the caster's position and my Ranger's
are balanced. As far as I could tell, the
risk to the caster's life was minimal -- he
risked his pet's life, and he also lost mana
to cast spells, but the exp/treasure gain
more than compensates for this.

In my Ranger's situation, I can't simply solo
the monster in melee. I might be able to
cast 'Snare' so that I could kite him for a
bit, and that helps, but not nearly enough.
He will still catch me and kill me when the
snare wears off.

So I was lucky this time -- I was able to
zone out and escape. I've got maybe a bubble
of health left, I've shot 15-20 arrows (which
cost money, and I can't go back and pick up
off the ground). I might have gained a few
skill points, but that's all. The caster,
meanwhile, is recovering his lost mana, and his pet is presumably recovering lost HP.
In his case, though, he killed it and gained
exp/treasure and whatever skill increases
may have accrued.

My conclusion from this (given my limited
information) is that it is absurd to claim
that this comparison can be called in any way
"balanced".

I like my ranger, and will continue to enjoy
playing him. But given the information I
have, there is no way this is balanced.

I'm not hoping for an upgrade, a nerf, a
patch, or anything else. I just wanted to
express my feeling on the matter and see if
anyone else felt the same way.
