The Original argument to refresh everyone's memory:

Baelish Responds
On this note I think I should point on behalf of the "Quiet
Enchanters" some things Sethlin missed. Now this may have a
"Grass is Greener" feel to it, but that is intentional. Verant
are forcing Enchanters to stick to their primary role - crowd
control. More and more of the damage role has been taken away from them since release
and they are being left only with a grouping role and I think we should evaluate
that a little.

<Pets have been weakened. They are the weakest of all pets in the
game, but unlike the Necromancers or Magicians they are meant to be
ancilliary damage, even a "sacrifice' to allow enchanters time to
contol overpulls. They have no hp, they cannot be controlled, and they
are the only class without a true pet heal. That said if this is the
case animations should be made to have 100% taunt factor, always
pulling any and all mobs off an Enchanter instantly for as long as they
live.

Note that this doesn't solve the primary issue that because a pet can't be
controlled it is useless if you get overpulls since what you mez gets auto
attacked once it has hit you once. There is no way to prevent this,
and the only solution is to let the pet die and then waste enormous mana
resummoning, equipping and rebuffing before the next fight. Let me
tell you right now, between doing the crowd control and this as well - you
just don't have the mana.

So, why not charrm? Simple. You cannot charm 95% of mobs in any of
the planes as Allure is capped, and it is totally random in duration.
If Enchanters are going to be told to rely on charm then Allure has to be upgraded
to be castable on all NPC's (bar Dragons and Gods) and set durations have to be implemented.

Ok forgetting pets, are Enchanters still the most powerful class in buffs and debuffs?
Wrong again. With the increasing number of haste items in game the
need for Swift, and even Celerity is highly diminished. I
suggest you read an
on this for further information.

The only thing Enchanters have better than any other class is:

Haste buffs (which I have just mentioned the problem with)
Group Resist Magic.
Hate List Wipes.
Clarity.
AE Stuns.
Mesmerize Spells.
High Level Charm.

If Enchanters are going to be reduced to Charm, Clarity, Mez and Stuns
then they need some upgrades:

The cap on Allure upped, and random times removed.
Better clarity for grouping.
Unresistable/near unresistable mez spells.
Longer duration, less resisted AE stuns.

If it is not Verant's intent to reduce Enchanters to this role then they need to be givien some kind of combat upgrade. Possibilities include:

Stronger pets with commands.
More efficient and group friendly DD and DoT spells.
Enchancements to their slow and stat debuff spells to make them the
leaders in this area.

I am not saying Enchanters are useless, but they are being forced
into a role which they are becoming very quickly ill-equipped to handle
in High Level zones.

On top of this are the little things for Enchanters:

Berserker Spirit being nerfed silently last patch losing 20% of its
protective skin.
Still no full implementation of Item Enchantment after being told it
would be included in March of 1999.
Mana Sieve & Curse of the Mind are for all intents useless on NPC's.
The Lull Series has a resist level so high it is a suicide spell on
anything white or above.
Illusions now being for all intents purely for fun with the removal of
the Enchanter scouting abilities. The bonuses on the illusions aren't really of any major benefit.
WTYH and DDD. These spells offer nothing a cheaper mana AE stun can't provide. And the AE stuns are resisted much less, and last much longer. They were meant to be fixed months ago...

I point all of this out to show that there are class balance issues
everywhere. Enchanter's are just one class which is need of
retuning. Rogues, SK's, Paladins, Necromancers are all below what they should
be at this moment as a result of recent patching and the game evolution.
All we can do as players is keep the issues in Verant's face until something is
done.

Because I am on a roll and I have to post!

Pets are not our "core competency" and never were. Before the "nerf" I was able to solo a seafury with one bubble of mana using a pet. After the nerf I was able to solo a seafury with two to three bubbles of mana.

Point being, I can still solo a seafury. Enchanters were one of the first classes, if not the first class, to do this successfully, and still the only class that does it efficitntly. We're still powerful... what's the point of this thread then?

Enchanters are not nukers. We have the worst dd and dot damage at level 50. In many situations pet damage output along with our support spells makes up for our lack in DDs and DOTs. So pets ARE important. But...

ALL of our spells can be considered support since no one area will get us very far. In concert we excell, and a small adjustment to our pets won't affect this in the long run.

HOWEVER.

Necromancers have much much more damage output than we do. Their pets are better defense and offense. They have health to mana conversion and taps that make them uniquely independant. Even after the intentional reduction of their pets' speed they remain one of the strongest classes in the game.

When their pets were "nerfed" they were given three new spell lines that not only increased the necromancer's value to a group but further strengthened their independance.

After this change it was discovered that enchanters' pets had also been "nerfed" on par with the necromancer's. A change that was largely unnecessary but an "acceptable" loss.

Our last new spell line was the mana spells designed to replace our ability to directly enchant: thicken, clarify, etc... these have yet to bear any fruit.

There have been no new spells or spell lines for enchanters forthcoming.

As we become more powerful we accelerate in relation to other classes until we seem to be one of the strongest in game. This applies in Lower Guk, Solusek B, versus giants and cyclops, and... well that's about it.

Take that to the next level and the trend reverses dramatically. Kedge, the planes, dragon raids.

Mesmerization does not apply in most cases in these zones. Resist rates are enormous and crowd control is a lucky break rather than the rule.

Lull is as pointless in high level dungeons as it is anywhere else.

Charm falls under the mesmerization category only with a much lower operating window. It works... sometimes... on some of the mobs. And is often fatal to the caster.

Crowd control is NOT a strategic function in the planes. Most of us have trained ourselves in this mode, however It's clear from this that moving forward Verant does not intend for us to fulfill that role.

Our damage spells are in the "utterly useless" category in the planes. Even with Tashania resists are absurdly high.

Debuffs don't really accomplish anything... most planes mobs, even if the debuffs stick, still hit close to maximum damage. The one exception to this is our "slow" spells, which do not stack with shaman slow spells, which are marginally better than ours.

Mind and mana debuffs are generally useless.

What's left for us? Buffing the group. Group Resist Magic is great, everyone loves it. Clarity is great everyone begs for it. Augment is great, all the melee classes demand it.

...

That's it. There is nothing whatsoever left. That's not enough to leverage us into a group. That's not enough to decide a battle.

Yes, when dazzle works it's awesome. When it works. Control is reduced to one mob per enchanter.

Having said all that, what's our function? As a class we're orphans. This is what I want to see addressed.

And to the person (or people) who think we're whining over this, you've obviously never sat down and talked to a necro after their pets were nerfed. Or a druid when dots were nerfed. Enchanters were affected by EVERY one of those nerfs to the exact same degree. We didn't complain about it until we were openly dismissed by Verant... chew on that.
