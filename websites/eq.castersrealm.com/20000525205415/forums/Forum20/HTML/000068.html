Level 30.

Caster types:
Tashani. Enthrall. Eye of Confusion. WTYH. Chaos Flux. Enthrall. Chaos Flux. Enthrall. Chaos Flux. Enthrall. (sometimes I'll use Mesmerize because of the lower mana cost, but with Enthrall you can actually Meditate between blasts.)

Melee types:
Tashani. Color flux. Color shift. Get in close and keep Whirling and color stunning. You will not have enough mana to solo finish a warrior of equal level. If you have a partner of any class, let them do the damage while you disable. Whirl and Eye of Confusion are the best for this. If you are alone, you'd need to Charm a pet or make a run for it. Str Debuffs and DOT also work well here because you aren't using Mez against the melee types.

Not a very big PVP Player myself (as I'm on Rodcet), but don't worry about not being able to do damage later. If your opponent is under open sky, Gravity Flux will do far more damage than you'd think. Here's the details:

Gravity Flux is an AOE spell that does ~200 damage and tosses the target(s) into the air where they will fall for X damage. Against critters, with no equipment, this equates to +100 falling damage. Against players the damage goes up as their encumberance goes up. Under open sky, I fell for 297 damage having just a 50 encumberance. Now, as an additional deterrent to would-be PK's, death by falling damage causes loss of XP... Rogue/Monk/Bard targets will mitigate some of the falling damage with their Safe Fall skill.

Just thought I'd throw in my little bit. Of course Gravity Flux is research only, so it's a pain to get at 39th..

Hey Syndur, I have had lots of experience in PvP as that is a big part of the guild i was in (Warriors of Hades). I am currently level 35 and use a large variety of tactics in pvp, mostly due to experimenting with spells. The druids I do happen to fight alot as I am in the light race zones and get attacked alot. Right now they are fairly easy to kill (given that they are around the same level). The key for me is to strip their preps as they have lots of them. Using strip enchantment a couple times will get rid of wolf form, HP preps, damage shields and stuff like that. Once those are down then the entrall/blast combo takes them down fast.

Against warrior type classes I haven't had much experience as most of who I fight are casters but I like to reduce their str and then use soffocate. This again drops their strength and they are majorly encumbered. Of course this means nothing if they have pumice.

There are a lot of goofy things you can do, but it essentially boils down to a few things:

If the other guy is actually prepared (has an evenomed heal potion to prevent mes, or self-dot, etc) ... you're essentially going to have to just root; hope the guy pokes you to agg your pet, then stun stun stun.
