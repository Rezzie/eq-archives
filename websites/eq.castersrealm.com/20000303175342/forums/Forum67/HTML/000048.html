Sorry to inform you, but wisdom is the key to success rate at trade skills. Intelligence is the key for faster progression and has nothing to do with this. I have a 200 skill and 162 wisdom when doing jewelery and I rarely fail on anything but platinum. With platinum on the low-end gems, I get about an 85%-90% success rate. On the high-end gems, like ruby and sapphire, I get about 45%-50% success.

So, if you want to work jewelery successfully, or any other trade for that matter, invest in wisdom. You'll definitely notice a difference.

To muddy the waters a bit more...

First off, I dimly remember the news article that you are referring to, but I couldn't find it in the archived news section. Are you sure GZ was talking about fizzles and not recovery/channeling? If you find it, can you post a link?

This is my understanding of how wisdom affects your success rates: When you attempt anything in the game a skill roll occurs. If you fail the initial skill roll, sometimes a "second chance" roll will occur. Wisdom normally affects that second chance roll.

What they did was to substitute intelligence as the determining factor for second chance rolls involving spell fizzles for INT based casters. This was probably done because they thought wisdom based casters were somehow "unbalanced" because they had an innate ability to fizzle less.

When they made this change it is possible that they accidentally changed it so that INT is the determining factor in ALL second chance rolls for INT based casters, but I doubt it. If so, it would be a bug in my opinion.

Or maybe I'm biased because went with a High Elf enchanter for his extra wisdom.

Oh - and my personal experience seems to indicate that I succeed more when I am wearing all of my +WIS stuff. I too, would like to do a study to try and determine this once and for all but unfortunately I'm too poor now.
