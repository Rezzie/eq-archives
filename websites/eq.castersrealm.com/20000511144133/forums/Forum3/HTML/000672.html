My enchanter is currently Level 33. I have noticed a gradual increase in the resists I get as I level up, but they're by no means worse than what any other class faces. (Well, at least not wizards anyways)

Sure, enthrall is resisted occasionally. Just keep in mind that it puts a mob out of the battle for a good long time when it does work.

Tashani doesn't make spells unresistable, but it certainly helps. If I'm facing a group of several reasonably resistant mobs I'll often get at least one or two resists per AE mez. If I plan to keep those mobs out of commision for very long I'll usually fit some tashani's in between mes's. By the time I've got them all tashan'd I get a lot fewer resists and can start medding in between casts. Once you can start medding between mes's you can keep a group of mobs out of it for a *very* long time, if not indefinately.

Beguile is an excellent spell. I typically use it to speed up fights which I have under control. If my group fights one mob and leaves the rest for me to control I will typically charm one mob and sick it on another while they're all still mes'd. Mobs do take commands while mesmerised. They just won't execute them until unmes'd. After that I'll entrall the remaining mobs if there are few enough, or just keep mesmerizing.

Bad charms will happen. In such cases my group might have to throw a heal my way before I get the charmee and his friend under control again. When a good charm happens your group will return only to find two nearly dead mobs. Definately not bad. =)

Dyn's is sort of Fubar'd. You can't expect every spell in that L.A. telephone directory enchanters call a spellbook to be useful. Curse of the simple mind is at least entertaining. Making that Hill Giant look stupid can be very entertaining.

As for 29+ enchanters being no good at crowd control... I disagree totally. Just because turning a train into a nice steady flow of exp takes more than one spell on average doesn't mean enchanters can't do crowd control. It just starts to become a bit of a challenge. Just ask the group I was with the other night when we took down a train of 14 blues. That's not a total of 14 mobs including stragglers, that's 14 mobs that came through the door all on the heels of our druid at once! We had five people in the group and the druid freaked and gated out as soon as he was able to. So that's 14 blues with a 4 man party really... Show me a 4 man party that can take 14 blues in a cramped dungeon at once without an enchanter if you can!

If you're unhappy as an enchanter then EQ simply isn't the game for you. The class is plenty powerful and simply a blast to play. Go play a wizard if you want to know how it really is to be useless to a group.


I'll just close off with something I think a lot of tanks don't realize...

Grouping with an Enchanter for Tanks: Tip of the Day

The following assumes a party of average composition. I.E., only one or two meelee'rs. In a tank-heavy group things are a bit different.

Q:When an enchanter has two mobs fighting each other (one is charmed), which should you attack?

A:Neither. Why should you just stand there you ask? If you help kill the pets target faster you'll probably taunt it onto yourself rather quickly. Once the first mob dies you'll have to kill the former pet too. In effect, you will have to tank two mobs and will, as a direct result, probably need at least a bit of healing from your cleric. Now, if you were to just let the two mobs duke it out until near death, and then finish the non-pet off you would only have to smack the former-pet a couple times before it died. Your cleric might not have to heal you at all.

When you tank for a charmed mob you're cutting the benefits of charm in half. Keep that in mind. Be ready to step in if the charm breaks prematurely, but don't get your hands dirty until you have to.
