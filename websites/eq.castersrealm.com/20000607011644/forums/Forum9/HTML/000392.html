The solution to firegiants depends on your level and group.

Stuns have no effect on giant sized NPCs -- this is a side effect of making them immune to bash.

However, stuns WILL still interrupt casting even though it doesn't stop melee. That means you can stun if you see a fg wiz casting, for instance. That's why you don't see a resist message unless they actually resist.

That being said, your best tactic with giants are to debuff with shiftless (shiftless nearly halves their swing rate, meaning your party takes half damage), and incapacitate, which makes it easier for your tanks to hit white, yellow, and red giants (at level 50).

Once you have Allure, lock in that charisma, and charm. Ideal use of charm is to have your group tank a giant, while you charm it. Then you tell your group to switch and attack the other giant while you either occupy a third (if you have 3+) with your charmie, or you put your charmie on the fresh giant (if you have 2). Then debuff the giant your group is working on.

The reason for letting your group tank your charmie a bit is that he's less likely to pound the hell out of you if he resists the charm. Remember you need to be in melee range for spells to affect giants.

With a good group and careful combination of bardic lulling and good pulling, and charming, a single group of 6 can (and has) killed the fire giants.

Plus it kicks ass to be a gnome and do "/pet who is your leader" and see "fire giant wizard00 says, 'Hika is my leader'."

It kicks even more ass to be the enchanter with the fire giant pet that summons another giant, so everyone in the zone laughs when they see "fire giant wizard00 says, 'You shall not evade me, fire_giant_warrior02'"
