Forgive me for being wordy, but this rant has a point. I began playing everquest over the summer, on a friends account. My first character was a troll shadow knight, who has since fallen by the wayside, but my second was a little gnome by the name of Professor. I was shocked when I discovered the name wasnt taken, it seemed perfect. Initially I played as a wizard, but swapped to an enchanter. I intended to make 20, and take the surname Arcanum. Professor Arcanum, a name sure to bring fame and glory. For there are many enchanters out there folks, and a reputation is very important among such a class. Some would argue that the name doesnt make the character, the player does. This is true, yet let me pose a question: who would you rather have (bearing in mind that both are played the same) Professor Arcanum or Xvizxraix? I have a sneaking suspiscion that Professor would get invited to more parties and high level functions...
Anyway, I had to start the character over upon getting my own account, and leveld a bit... then the dreaded naming policy came out. Thats right, it forbid titles. My name, my identity was at stake. I wasnt offensive, i hadnt hurt anybody, and now, because of an oversight at verant months in the making, I would be forced to give up my name. I logged on, adn to my surprise, Professor was sitting pretty. I played, and for months my little gnome wandered around, talkign to others. 10 days played, and only level 13. This says something about the effort i put into playing my little pedogogue. Well, one day last week, I decide to make a stein of mogguk. Some newby is pestering clurg as i'm about to hand in the compendium, and clurg attacks. I am screwed out of my compendium as I hit 'trade'. I petition, and Brother Xave arrives. He is curt, terse, but creates me a new compendium. I get my stein, and am about to be on my way, when I hear. 'I have to change your name'. My ehart sinks. I point out that I have friends out there, and that most everyone seems to love my name. Its not Azzkikr McKewld00d, or anything. Xave is firm in his decision, and fits me with a temporary name. Hooray! my hopes of fame and prestige go up in smoke. And for what? Because some piss poor server administrator can flex his power? If xave made the server interesting, I could deal. BUt he doesnt, he flat out sucks. NOTHING ever goes on on Mith Marr. Forgive me for being pissy, but I feel that if you were the first to pick a title, you should get it. It would be different if there was a system to gain one (ie, gain level 50, slay a dragon, do a quest, etc). But there isnt. I should have been grandfathered in. So instead, i get to pick from the variety of uninteresting names leftover from release. I didnt want my enchanter to be just another guy. I wanted him to be SOMEBODY, and I had a good enough name to ensure that I could be famous, if I played my cards right. DOes verant or some gm have the right to take that away? This goes to Knight (level 50 eru paladin on my server), Xave can take what you have created as well. I personally feel screwed, and incredibly cheated. Any thoughts? Or new name suggestions? lol, If the current holder of Mastermind would like to give it up, you'd be my hero...

All it takes is one /petition regarding a name that is in violation of the policy to cause a GM to change it. Perhaps the "newby" who was pestering Clurg did a /petition?

Although I empathize with your desire to be grandfathered in, it could make Customer Service a nightmare. Let's say once a month someone objects to you having a title when they can't. They /petition. The guide then checks your creation date and tells the /petitioner "yes it's in violation of the policies, but it was grandfathered". It leaves you wandering around with a title others can't have, and each and every time, a guide has to take time to research and respond.

I truly empathize with your frustration, but I would rather see consistency in applying the rules than exceptions that have to be investigated and explained over and over and over ad nauseum.

Perhaps if you work through a thesaurus and use an imaginative spelling, you might be able to come up with an acceptable compromise that gives you the scholarly name you want?
