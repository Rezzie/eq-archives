Hmmm, since you're asking, I'll assume that you are not twinked in any way and do not have twink money laying around. If that's the case, then good for you - you'll likely be a stronger enchanter by coming up the hard way. No offense to those enchanters who have re-rolled, of course.

At 14 you're probably a de-buffing/dotting/meleeing kind of character. If you're playing root and nuke then you should think about learning new techniqes, as you will be less and less effective as you level. At 16 you will get a research spell (mesmerization) that will make you pretty effective in groups and camp-breaking, but for now I'm guessing you spend at least 50% of your time soloing (with a pet). With that in mind, you should prolly have:

armor: all silk - it may seem expensive now, but the reduced weight and the slightly better AC (compared to cloth) will make a difference. And you may be wearing some of the silk for a long time ...
feet/belt/cape: bear or wolf hide from the Millers in North Qeynos
robe: your Sr. Apprentice robe, a silk robe (lighter, but turd ugly), or the +5 cha tunic
jewelry: two +2 INT rings, two electrum jade bracers, two +3 INT earrings (Cubert quest)
weapons: GSF or low delay dagger for piercing, Runed Totem Staff for magical mobs, cracked for 1HB (or one of the Crystal Staves if you want to splurge, but you'll be dumping it before long)
off hand: Stein of Mogguk - do the quest as many times as you can stand it and keep one for yourself
ranged: Dragoon Dirk (also a nice piercing weapon). If you happen to be in Faydark anyway, getting a group of six level 14's together for the D'vinn camp will be very good for your enchanting skills. Chances are you'll be dumping this before long, as well, so don't go nuts and spend the 80pp that they seem to be selling for (at least on Nameless).
futures: at 16 when you're grouping more and mesmerize is your money spell, you'll want a Crude Stein. Go to The Feerott and buy it there - high level players are killing Ogre Guards and you shouldn't have to pay more than 10pp, if that. You'll also want to dump the INT rings and get some CHA stuff. Easy enough to afford if you're willing to put in the time on wisp isle.

That's a pretty cost-effective setup. The most expensive items are the bracers, and I think the whole deal should be less than 75pp TOPS (assuming no Crystal Staff or Dirk). If you have a friend doing silk tailoring cheap and you can sell off your Skull of Jhen'Tra (cute, but dump it for the Stein), your net should be closer to 50-55pp.

The mistake I see most enchanters (and casters in general) making is wasting money on a robe. Unless the look is very important to you, stick with the recommendations above. Look at the armor tables on this site and see what Robes are available - the cheapest one there that adds any stats at all, the Robe of the Keeper, will at least double the cost of the entire setup. And for what - +2 INT? Save your money for good jewelry and spells. When you have money to spend, there are robes that will do you some good.

MrP

PS While you're in Feerott getting your Crude Stein, pop into Cazic Thule and see if you can loot the Radiant Gloves. They're very nice on their own, and are part of the dead easy Incandescent Glove quest. If you can loot the gloves off of a shadowed man (try South Karanas), the rest of the quest is cash-only.
