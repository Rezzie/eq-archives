Torpor is currently a mediocre spell, unworthy of its level 60 status. I explain my reasoning why in my post at http://eq.castersrealm.com/forums/Forum12/HTML/001035.html .

It's important to try to understand the PURPOSE of Torpor as it was originally conceived. I believe that Torpor is supposed to be like falling into a healing trance, where you cannot change your position and you attack veeerrryyyyy slloooooow. The original spell did that through the following:

1) a very manaefficient heal
2) heal a lot of damage
3) make the target worthless in melee combat
4) take a bit of time to complete, but not a LOT of time
5) be unexploitable with manastones/lich/cannibalize.

By manipulating those five goals, we can come up with some very different versions of Torpor for different situations.

Ways to change manaefficiency
- make the spell cost more or less mana
- make the spell drain more or less mana from the target

Ways to change how much damage it heals
- change the duration (versus superior heal... faster duration is more healing over time)
- change the amount of healing per tick
- change the casting time and recovery time (also affects healing over time versus superior heal, but for multiple targets)

Ways to make the target worthless in melee combat
- totally stop movement (root)
- slow movement (snare)
- slow attack speed

Ways to affect duration
- change the duration

Ways to stop the spell from being exploitable
- make the spell cost more or less mana
- drain mana from the target

It's also important to look at the USE to which the spell will be put, and then to optimize the spell towards that use.
- downtime reduction for tanks
- downtime reduction for casters
- combat healing for tanks
- combat healing for casters

Here's a couple of examples... my favorite is the "Fast Tank Downtime Reducer I", here. God, that's a mouthful, eh?

As usual for me, this post is VERY VERY VERY LONG!

Better in Combat I
Mana cost: 200 mana
Spell Duration: 24 seconds
Casting Time: 6.0 seconds
Spell Recovery Time: 0.0 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- remove the root effect
- keep the attack speed debuff (30% slowdown)
- keep the mana drain (50 mana * 4 ticks = 200 mana drained)
-----
Thus the spell will be usable in combat. It won't be GREAT in combat since attack speed is still debuffed by 30% and all speed buffs are removed, but it'll be usable in emergencies.

Better in Combat II
Mana cost: 200 mana
Spell Duration: 24 seconds
Casting Time: 6.0 seconds
Spell Recovery Time: 2.50 seconds
Effects:
- change the root to a snare (55% movement speed debuff)
- remove the attack speed debuff
- keep the mana drain (50 mana * 4 ticks = 200 mana drained)
- keep the healing unchanged (300 healing * 4 ticks = 1200 healing)
-----
Again, this makes the spell worthwhile in combat but not GREAT in combat, particularly outdoors as it cancels Spirit of Scale. It will still be mediocre for downtime reduction compared to Superior Healing, particularly on casters.

Better in Combat III
Mana cost: 300 mana (changed from 200 mana)
Spell Duration: 24 seconds
Casting Time: 6.0 seconds
Spell Recovery Time: 2.50 seconds
Effects:
- remove the root effect
- remove the attack speed debuff
- change the mana drain to 100 mana (25 mana * 4 ticks = 100 mana drained)
- keep the healing unchanged (300 healing * 4 ticks = 1200 healing)
-----
These changes would make Torpor great in combat to heal tanks and only decent for downtime reduction, although a bit better for casters.

Unfortunately it also takes away from the uniqueness of the spell-- why call it "Torpor" if the only negative effect is 100 mana drained? This version is about equal to the clerics' Celestial Elixir, except that it drains 100 mana from the target and heals 200 more. I doubt this version would happen... and quite frankly I don't think it should.

Faster heal, slightly better in combat
Mana cost: 200 mana
Spell Duration: 18 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 4.50 seconds (changed from 6.00 seconds)
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- keep the root effect
- change the attack speed debuff to 5%
- 400 healing per tick (400 healing * 3 ticks = 1200 healing)
- 66.67 mana drained per tick (66.67 mana * 3 ticks = 200 mana drained)
-----
With these changes, the spell will do its job in two thirds the time, making it a slightly slower heal than Superior Healing, and tanks will still be able to fight defensively. They're still rooted and all attack speed buffs will be canceled, but their attacks are only slowed by 5%.

Fast Heal I
Mana cost: 200 mana
Spell Duration: 6 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 3.00 seconds (changed from 6.0 seconds)
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- keep the root effect
- keep the attack speed debuff
- heal 600 damage instantly and heal 600 damage after six seconds (1200 healing total)
- drain 100 mana instantly and drain 100 mana after six seconds (200 mana total)
-----
This is definitely overpowered.


Fast Heal II
Mana cost: 200 mana
Spell Duration: 12 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 3.00 seconds (changed from 6.0 seconds)
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- keep the root effect
- keep the attack speed debuff
- double the healing per tick (600 healing * 2 ticks = 1200 healing)
- double the mana drained per tick (100 mana * 2 ticks = 200 mana drained)
-----
This is a great downtime reduction spell for tanks. It's basically the current Torpor with the duration cut in half. The annoyance factor is cut down with the shorter duration, but spirit of scale and any speed buffs will still need to be renewed. I think this one is a nice compromise.

Slow Tank Downtime Reducer I
Mana cost: 300 mana (changed from 200 mana)
Spell Duration: 36 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 6.00 seconds
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- remove the root effect
- increase the attack speed debuff to 200%
- heal 450 HP per tick (450 healing * 6 ticks = 2700 healing)
- drain 150 mana per tick (150 mana * 6 ticks = 900 mana drained)
-----
I doubt even hybrids will want THIS spell cast on them... but if you've got a warrior in your group, this one WILL kick some ass for downtime reduction. Probably just a bit overdone.

It's nice because it doesn't cancel spirit of scale and the 200% attack speed debuff makes it worthless in combat.

Slow Tank Downtime Reducer II
Mana cost: 150 mana (changed from 200 mana)
Spell Duration: 36 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 4.50 seconds (changed from 6.00 seconds)
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- remove the root effect
- increase the attack speed debuff to 200%
- heal 250 HP per tick (250 healing * 6 ticks = 1500 healing)
- drain 50 mana per tick (50 mana * 6 ticks = 300 mana drained)
-----
This is a less overdone version of the "Slow Tank Downtime Reducer I". Even in Kunark, it's doubtful that most tanks will need 2700 healing between normal fights! 1500 healing ought to do it, and few casters will accept the 300 mana drain either... but hybrids probably will.

This spell heals a bit more than the original Torpor and drains a bit more mana from the target. It also doesn't cancel spirit of scale, but with the much longer duration, the 200% attack speed debuff makes it absolutely worthless in combat. I like this one a lot.

Fast Tank Downtime Reducer I
Mana cost: 100 mana (changed from 200 mana)
Spell Duration: 18 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 3.50 seconds (changed from 6.00 seconds)
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- remove the root effect
- increase the attack speed debuff to 200%
- heal 350 HP per tick (350 healing * 3 ticks = 1050 healing)
- drain 75 mana per tick (75 mana * 3 ticks = 225 mana drained)
-----
This spell heals a bit less than the original Torpor and drains slightly more mana from the target. However it is finished in less time and is thus more efficient than Superior Heal for downtime reduction. This one is my favorite.


Fast Tank Downtime Reducer II
Mana cost: 125 mana (changed from 200 mana)
Spell Duration: 18 seconds (changed from 24 seconds)
Casting Time: 1.50 seconds (changed from 6.00 seconds)
Spell Recovery Time: 0.00 seconds (changed from 2.50 seconds)
Effects:
- remove the root effect
- increase the attack speed debuff to 200%
- heal 450 HP per tick (450 healing * 3 ticks = 1350 healing)
- drain 100 mana per tick (100 mana * 3 ticks = 300 mana drained)
-----
This version heals slightly more than the original Torpor and drains 50% more mana from the target. Again, it's finished in less time and this one is markedly more efficient than superior heal for downtime reduction while being worthless in combat and non-exploitable with manastones/cannibalize/lich.

The structure of the spell is very interesting combining a very powerful positive effect with very powerful negatives.
Another long post in reply, primarily on the tactics of using the spell. My argument is that the positive advantages provided by the current spell do not outweigh the negatives and I would not use it in its current form. A key element is moving the negatives to the caster, not the target.

The key benefit of Topor is clear - extremely fast hit point recovery over time. Topor gives shamen a clear secondary healing advantage over druids. However, one thing to remember is that this spell is still currently less than a complete heal (not the spell) and as such, shamen will not replace clerics as primary healers. Overall it is nice, but not devastatingly powerful beyond the casting of 2 Superior Heals. The casting of 2 Superior Heals is an important for comparison with Topors in measuring effectiveness primarily because of the lack of powerful negative effects.

The key problem for this spell is the movement inhibition, much more than the reduced attack speed. One illustration of this is how many people buy bark potions? Almost none. Why? The reason why is that mobility = life in EQ. For a movement effect to be a component of a spell, the positive effect must be extremely special. For a bark potion this is clearly not the case, so Topors must provide a significant advantage to offset this effect.

In addition to the movement effect is the irritation factor of Topors. I discussed this a bit with respect to the Cannibalize spell, but why add such an effect to irritate the target of the spell? A shaman chain casting Cannibalize irritates group members enough with the constant spell messages, why give them more reason to complain by adding powerful negative effects (especially mana drain, but movement and attack speed) to their buffs? Individuals receiving spells really do not care about the mana cost/effects to the caster, just their result. No one has ever asked me how much mana it takes to buff and how many I can toss around, they just want to be more effective after I cast a spell upon their character. The addition of significant negative effects
is a reflection on my role as a shaman. If anyone is to have a negative effect from my buff, make it to ME, not my group members. Remember, I can always cast 2 superior heals to raise their health for a higher mana cost to me, or I can be more efficient but negatively impact my group member. I would choose to use the heals and spend my time meditating for the extra mana with a happy group member ready to fight.

I propose a different combination of features for Topor.

1200 hit point regeneration (300 for 4 clicks)

200 mana cost

*change* SELF ROOT for 2 clicks =12 seconds

All self movement spells are removed

Caster can still cast spells or Cannibalize.

NO additional mana loss while rooted (but you lose the opportunity to meditate while rooted)

Zero or minimal attack speed reduction
on the caster.

These are significant negatives:
1. Lost meditation time
2. Self root can be risky (12 seconds)
3. Need to recast Spirit of Scale or equivalent should be considered part of the mana cost.

These changes make the spell much more attractive and useful in group settings.
Soloing gives shaman a mana efficient heal as they can still cannibalize while the spell is in effect. I will let Sam do the analysis to see whether it is worthwhile with the current spell costs.

Overall, Topors could be made into one of the defining shaman spells. Switching the negative effects from the target to the shaman is probably the way to go.
