Walking Sleep only affects attack speed, it provides no 'Snare' effect.

I have not found Disempower to matter significantly in most cases. It seems better to spend the mana on a DOT, which will also end the melee sooner and result
in less damage taken over all.

Strength bonuses to damage are not significant until level 20 and big jumps seem to occur below Str 75 and over Str 100. The spell could theoretically be applied most effectively against such mobs at such break points (assuming you know, or can guess the approximate Str of the mob).

I would presume using Disempower against a mob below 20th level, where the Str effect on damage is negligible, would be a waste of mana.
