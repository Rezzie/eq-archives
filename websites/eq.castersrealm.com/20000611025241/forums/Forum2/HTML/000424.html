Many necros have expressed a wish for new pets beyond
the same old "pile o bones" that we always get...

Verant has consistently said that this cannot be done
because of zone limits on texture memory..

well I saw a post on one of the necro one list boards
that got me thinking that this problem has a perfect
solution...

Simply allow a high level necro spell to "re-animate"
any dead monster. This does a few things...

1) it gets us a ZONE based, included in CURRENT texture
memory, varying pet.

2) they could use "tinting" to wipe the color palette for the mobile to a gray palette to indicate this was a pet not a real monster. (or they could leave it alone and let us scare the newbies when our sand giant tries to follow us into town)

3) the pet could be given the correct level (thus not affecting its attacks based on the current necro pet level) (granted we might lose dual attacks this way because some mobs re-animated might not be able to wield..but some would)

4) this would add some interest in the playability...hmmm...I no longer just have this sack of chips..I have to FIND and KILL a monster before raising it. (keep the option of normal pets...make this an ADDITIONAL SPELL -- ie. dont nerf ADD to the game instead)

Wouldnt it be neat to have a reanimated kobold for a pet in solB? or a bat? --- or how about a swordfish pet in kedge? There are so many good things about this and so few downsides...(easy to program, large variation in pets, interest and INVOLVEMENT on the players side (hmm...which mob should i kill for this zone today to make my pet), fits in CURRENT texture memory with variations by zone, can be ADDED instead of NERFED, I could go on but you get the idea....

anyhow maybe verant will read this, and I encourage you to post it to every place and feedback in game about the idea...lets get some fun back in the game!

Here's another way this could be done that might be neat, if not in EQ, certainly in EQ2.

Target a corpse and cast reanimate. That corpse comes back as an undead version of its former self that is perhaps less powerful than in life, but more powerful than your typical pet.

Here's the sticker... The reanimating force will only last so long. After a certain amount of time the mob will just fall to the ground again. However, there is a chance that you might have insufficient will to command the monster for the full time it is reanimated. If you lose control the mob may turn on you, striking out at the being that forced it out of its eternal rest.

This spell would be similar to charm. It would probably last a bit longer on average and a reanimated pet may simply die again instead of turning on the necromancer. The trade-off to this is that only corpses can be used and the reanimated mobs are never as strong as they were in life. I would also set the spell up so that a corpse can only be reanimated once, and only shortly after death. If used on a formerly reanimated body or an old corpse the energy needed to reanimate the creature would simply be too much for a necromaner to put out.

It seems that two ideas are being espoused here (and I'll add a third to muddy the waters further);

Some of the later posters think that it would be neat if higher level pets resembled zombies (8th-ish), ghouls (20th-ish), mummies (16th-ish), ice bones (?), dry bones (?), skeletal monks (?), barbed bones (?), spectres (34th+), etc. Some go so far as to speculate if the higher level pets might have those creatures special abilities (a Ghoul pet that roots, a Mummy pet that diseases, a Spectre pet that kicks major booty...).

Conceivably, special undead might exist in the 40th+ Kunark zones that could lend special animations to Necromancers over 50th level, such as a 'Whirling Bonestorm' (looking like the spining blades monster of Najena or borrowing from the animation of ones pet sitting down to create a storm of sharp and knobby bones), a 'Rotting Colossi'
(a group of 3 to 6 people stitched together ala Frankenstein) or a 'Giant Spectral Skull' (resembling the big green skull that appears around a Manastone user).

This would lend individuality to almost every level of Necromancer, since the Ghoul pet of a 20th level caster could not be mistaken for the Decaying Skeleton pet of a 1st level caster, nor would anyone mistake either for the Spectre tagging along behind a 39th level Necromancer...

This is a GREAT idea in some regards, but apparently older systems in EQ1 can't handle the graphics required. (But pets in Kunark could be so modified, which would certainly have Necros flocking to the new continent in droves, since their pets might look or act differently there! Mage pets could be visually enhanced there also, perhaps have legs and walking with a Giant-like booming noise or looking like moving flame instead of levitating paraplegics.)


The original poster has a rather innovative spell that might function like a form of 'Charm' in that it allows the Necro to create a pet from the corpse of a fallen creature and use it's animation to represent his pet (perhaps washed out in color a bit or renamed (or remaining named 'A Cyclops Corpse' while shambling along...)). Playing within the limitations of some minorities limited graphics cards, this is a fine suggestion, one which I find quite creative and interesting. It would replace the Charm spell that was taken from Necros quite some time ago. (The reanimated dead might be slower (Drowsy) or weaker (Disempower) in some way than the in-life mob it was generated from, making this effect noticeably weaker than an Enchanters Charm, or the creature might take damage as a weak poison (or simply never heal) throughout it's existence to represent it's dead and crumbling nature or the degenerative effect of the magics sustaining its movement.)

Yet another great suggestion, perhaps being a simple stand-alone spell that exists alongside the current spell complement.


My own muddying of these fertile waters would be to suggest that the Necromancer might be able to summon special pets by using some of the special bone chips found on mobs throughout the game. If he were to have Warbone Chips in an upper inventory slot when he cast Restless Bones, his pet would remain at the level he normally summoned, but have the abilities of the Skeletal Monk that dropped those chips. Were he instead to use Barbed Bone Chips, the pet would have a damage shield, like the Barbed Bone who donated the chips. Finally, where he to use Crystallized Marrow from an Ice Bones, his pet might include the icy attack of a true Ice Bones.

Additional special bone chips could be included as uncommon drops off of Dry Bones or Putrid Skeletons to create pets with those abilities.

Few animation changes would be required (since each of these undead basically looks identical, unlike the Ghoul, Zombie, Spectre idea above), so graphics memory should not be significantly affected.


All in all, some exciting ideas for Necro pets. Now if only a Mages earth pet had *legs*...
