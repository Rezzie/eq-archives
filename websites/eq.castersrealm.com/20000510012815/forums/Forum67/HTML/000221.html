As a general rule I sell enchanted jewelry 50% higher than it cost me to make. I do however watch the auctions of other jewelers to be certain my prices are not below theres, I want to drive the prices up not down.

Furthermore I NEVER negotiate prices, others can either buy them at what I am asking or not get them, nor do I trade for jewelry (with a few very rare exceptions and for items I really wanted).

If someone has convinced me to be nice and accept his junk in trade for my jewelry I offer him 50% of what I believe the item is worth in trade value.

Though it can be said that my prices may tend to be a bit higher than others I freely admit, I want to raise the value of jewelry, not lower it. Just be patient, people will buy your wares.

The prices vary greatly from server to server because the markets change as a server becomes older. On Vallon Zek (came online in August) I can usually sell any of the popular items for 2x the cost of the materials for any items that are not Platinum (that sells for about 3-4x the cost).

My best advice to you would be to contact a local enchanter on your server and ask him if there is a general rule he/she uses to price his items. After speaking to a few Enchanters you should have a fair idea of what constitutes a fair price on your server.

Check out Maximum EQ site. They have a large section, by server, on what the going prices for items are. The few times I've checked it the prices for my server (Brell) have been reasonably to close to what I have seen being auctioned.

I was taught to sell for roughly 2x cost to make an item (ie: silver ruby veil costs me 131 to make, I sell for 200). prices are not negotiable and I only make on request. Also, I never advertise the price of anything I am selling. Of course, I only have mediocre skill (only 20th level) so I am not bothered to much by requests.
