We went to see new and horrible Paw. It was too easy for 40-45 team. So we decided to test something out.

We were at the "lake" which has the waterfall. Nearby the gate which operates by latch... there is large open area there. Our puller fetched a mob. Our enchanter attached his pet to it. Then our cleric feared the mob and we waited to see what would happen. I had seen train screenpics. The "train" that hit us... had about 15-20 gnolls. I stood in the middle with the enchanter. I chained Thunderclap and Cast Force, he chained his two stun lines. Having decent agility must have some effect (175). Cleric healed us and tanks were beating anything that moved. We survived with ease.

I have previously stated that 864 damage for 175 mana is great. Now I have to say that 2320 damage for 175 mana is even better.

We did that trick again after medding to full. Oh yes, I oomed in that battle, no one else did. And second time was as fun. Third time brought is slightly harder pull, but our cleric oomed at the last group heal... so real danger was far.

Some of the mobs were green, yes. But with those three pulls, I got about 60% of a bubble xp, which isn't bad for 3 bars of mana. And we had fun. Gnolls dropped like flies.

Why we stopped? Server crashed and we had to wait for GM xpress for 10 minutes. Then we realized that it was 4:30am and sleep would be good option, since Paw will be there tomorrow too.

"Lesson" of the story: Pets are most qualified to pull good hordes to be handled by AoE spells. AoE spells are great, mana efficient, etc.

And from log file, I logged those three fights:
54 spells cast
1 fizzle
4 interrupts
5 resists
12 regain concentrations
kills 23
number of hits on me 101

total damage made: 36231

My personal opinion is that this is good average for 44th level wizard. At least the xp it rolled in was great. And I would recommend the AoE's for every wizard to learn out. Maybe the pet-puller isn't the best way to start experimenting with AoE, but we had lots of fun and also excitement, since you didn't know what would come

Yes, pointblank AE spells are remarkably effective.


Little EQ history lesson:

Once upon a time, AE spells had no limits to how many mobs they would hit. A rain spell would hit 50 mobs all 3 times, etc.

Once upon a time, a handful of people various names such as Asmonious, Lopp, etc used a certain geographic spot in Gukbottom to effect dozens of mobs per cast. They would use a tank (sometimes a cleric w/Divine) to pull a ton of mobs into an area that the AE casters could stand where they could cast on the mob, yet the mob could not reach them.

Once upon a time, these people levelled VERY fast.

Once upon a time, it was suddenly a bannable (beta) offense to cast Rain spell sindoors and Gukbottom was closed.

Once upon a time, AE spell damage was halved.

Once upon a time, limits on the numbers of MOBs effected were placed on Rain and Column type AE's... but not Pointblank.


Because of this, Pointblank AE spells are remarkably effective. Far more then the other types of AE. However, the situations that allow for pointblank spells are even less then other AE.

For one thing, you'd never do that in say, Fear or Hate. You'd get shredded. In fact ANY AE is a big no no in Hate, and only under very controlled circumstance in Fear. Crowd control is an absolute necessity, and AE spells break most methods of crowd control.

On the other hand, Supernove is the absolute best AE spell in the game. I can clean out Blackburrow like nobody else.
