Ok, now I must confess I was a bit concerned when looking at the new spells of the magician, especially when compared to the new DD spells the druid gets. That might sound selfish and maybe childish, but it remains a groupgame where we all fullfill a certain role within a group. Our importance would be greatly reduced if we went from the
second-best nukers to third-best, especially when you consider that the new second-best
is the druid, a class that already has very good groupspells.

In summary, this is why I'm concerned :

Currently (pre-level 50) the magician's shock-spell (DD) does slightly less damage than the druid's DD, has a slightly less good damage/mana ratio, but is magicbased, instead of firebased or icebased (druid, the icespell is not always usuable as a nuke, btw, i wont go in details) and magicians get it 1 spell circle earlier. In addition to that, magicians have a bolt spell (not usuable in all situations) that is firebased, has a better damage/mana ratio than the druid's DD and does about 30 % more damage. All in all, not quite bad, and that makes a lvl 50 magician a better nuker than a lvl 50 druid.

Post-level 50 however, is a different story. The magician's shock spell (still magic based) does 22 % less damage than the DD of the druid (only firebased this time, their
icebased DD does a lot less damage), magicians get it two levels earlier this time and the ratio is slightly less than the DD of the druids. Our bolt spell (firebased) has the same damage/mana ratio as the shock this time and does 14 % less damage than the damage of the druid.

Now should we get concerned ? I think not, and these are my reasons:

1. This spell-list isn't finished or definitive.

2. I've read that one of the Verant boys said that the balance between the classes would stay the same as the balance is pre-level 50. Just a look at the new damages show that the balance between a lvl 60 druid and lvl 60 magician is very different than between a lvl 50 druid and a lvl 50 magician. I trust that Verant will notice this too.

3. The new damageshields. If these stay as they are in the spell-list now, magician
would be absolute king of damageshields (at level 50, the difference is barely
noticable). The 33 point groupdamage-shield (PLUS fireresist, very usefull, since this shield probably will last quite some time AND stacks with other resistbuffs) kicks ass. I think this is the groupspell we all have been waiting for.

One last point, you must know I will probably never get a magician to level 50.

Assuming the DD spells we know about are going to end up about the same (no way to know for sure until release), I don't really know how I feel about sacrificing DD to get seriously burly pets. The reason goes back to game balance; with the difference between our DD and the new druid DD, there would have to be a pretty sizable increase in the damage our best pets can dish out. But can you imagine the uproar from the tanks if, post-expansion, we can summon behemoths that can be buffed to 15 or so delay, that cast spells, and now quad-hit in the 80's? And with our insignificant gains in the DD department, that's roughly what I'd want to compensate for the fact that a druid can now out-nuke me by a pretty large margin. Hell, hits in the 80's at least are what I want anyway.

I played a ranger to 44 on my old server, and while I was never really bothered by the amount of damage necro and mage pets could do, I definitely got hit by the old inferiority complex every once in a while. Verant has said that they've got a lot of plans for buffing up melees in the expansion, so hopefully both our pets and our friendly tanks will get a similar increase in damage output.

As far as ideal DD goes, I think Boogle has it pretty much right on the mark: a conjuration-based Shock spell that does around 1200-1300 would probably satisfy everyone, and still keep up us far enough behind wizards to justify their existence as anything but taxis.
