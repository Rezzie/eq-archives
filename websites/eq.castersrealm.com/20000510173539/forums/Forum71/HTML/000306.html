There will definitely be a few thousand pissed off magicians about the summons spells in the expansion. Verant is basically saying to mages, "Summon a bunch of stuff that you can't even GIVE away to other players. This is how we make you more attractive for groups to invite. At really high levels, we'll let you summon a full bag of this stuff at one time, instead of having to cast four times for it."

Just a little tweaking to summoned items would make them conform to Verant's original intent. Make dimensional pockets/holes not disappear until 30 minutes after you log, and they will be very popular. People won't touch them with a ten-foot pole now though.

Just a slight enhancement to the stats or procs of the existing summoned items is still called for. Verant took a big step in that direction a couple of months ago, but it doesn't seem to have quite reached the point they wanted in order to make these items desired by anyone. I can only speak from my own experience and what my friends tell me, but here it is. I've been able to use the Summon Spear spell a total of twice and have had it for 15 levels now. I can't give the summoned swords away (though you'd think people would be interested so they can practice dual wield). Summoned staffs are only useful from when I die until I get back to my body, and even then I don't use them, they're just comforting to have. Summoned arrows? Been able to give them to people less than ten times ever. (I suggest increase their range or their damage.) Summoned throwing daggers, maybe five times ever. Bandages? Maybe given them to people two dozen times since 4th level. Etc, etc, etc. This is just my personal experience, maybe other mages are doing better. OTOH, I am constantly trying to convince people they want some of these things, always educating people. What more can anyone be doing to give this stuff away? Pretty close to the mark, Verant did succeed at getting coldstones to be mildly popular (I can at least give them away about half the time), but heatstones forget it. Water stones are popular in one dungeon for 35+ (it's a 20th-level spell). And for the very few (high level) visitors to the cave in Everfrost. Oh, is the continuing lack of a summoned 1hand blunt intentional? Perhaps if the 2hand blunts were made extremely lightweight, or enhancing Agility or something...

My bandage suggestion is to make Bind Wound work above 50% health, but at diminishing effectiveness. Everyone I've mentioned this to was struck by well balanced an idea it seems to be. For instance, under 50% health a bandage does normal healing amount, up 75% or 80% health it does one half of normal, above that it does say one-quarter or one -tenth normal. Regenerating health without magical aid will still be slow and tedious, but not to the extreme it is now. And customers will feel a *lot* less frustrated than they are now.

Three other points about expansion spells for mages.

(1) It doesn't seem appropriate for only one of the 50+ spells do DD to be Conjuration based. They're all Evocation. Come on, we're magicians, not wizards. You can do a lot of damage to someone by conjuring up a whole batch of things immediately around them, under them, above them, or hurtling towards them. It just seems more appropriate to the class.

(2) Should druids really be wildly superior at killing summoned beings? Doesn't it make sense that magicians be one spell rank ahead of druids on the Dismiss Summoned line of spells, instead of the other way around? There are a lot of complains about druids being overbalanced, and a lot of druids bragging that they are the most powerful. Nobody is complaining/bragging the same about magicians. Making the switch seems like a good idea. To avoid nerfing druids and causing hate and discontent among their numbers, try this suggestion. Don't change what level druids get these spells. Make mages get the spells two spell ranks sooner than we do now. The relatively high mana cost will limit the young mages from doing anything abusive with the spells, their ability against summoned creatures will gradually grow as they level up. It should be pretty self-balancing, due to the mana required.

(3) Since no mage in their right mind will summon a lower level pet when they could summon a higher level one, the new spell list (as listed on Casters Realm) severely limits high level mages to one or maybe two kinds of pets they can summon as they level up.
