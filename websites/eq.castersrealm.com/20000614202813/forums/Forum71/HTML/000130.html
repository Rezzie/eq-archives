Talgar, looking at the big picture does not mean forgetting about class balance. If a class is too powerful it does not translate into a better grouping experiences and more fun for everyone.

As an example, let's say wizards would get an instadeath spell. Heavily resisted, but instadeath. Now, would you feel good fighting alongside a wizard, and just waiting for the spell to hit? Very safe for you, but it kinda makes your whole class obsolete, especially if you cannot heal. Heck, groups with wizards would "rule", and take down every bloody mob there is, but would you personally have fun, since you were not needed anymore? If you felt they would just group with you out of pity?
If a class doesn't feel like it is pulling their own weight anymore the players of that class do not have much fun anymore.

Another example: Let's say druids would get the same DD as wizards at 60. Sure, grouping with a druid would mean a safer group for all, but the wizards would be rendered obsolete. Not very fun for anyone.

Take that big picture into account. Not every boost to a class helps all classes. Sometimes it destroys other classes.
