Grimstaff:
What do you mean about the "implementation of role playing"? The implementation of Role Playing lies with the players, not the game.

The only way Verant could "implement" role playing onto us characters would be to seriously gimp our modes of communication, and that would REALLY piss everyone off. So long as we can freely communicate anything we want, freely, people will either choose to communicate in a role-playing fashion, or they won't. There is no way Verant can fairly "reward roleplaying", as has been suggested many times.

And as far as the class-based system vs point system, there is a good reason they chose the way they did (not that I agree with them 100%, but their reason is valid). With a skill-based or point based system it is inevitable that someone, or some group, will eventually converge on a mix of skills and points that will result in the practical "best" player. It is almost inevitable in such a system that eventually most of the people have figured out how to mix and match skills to have the MOST powerful character when matched against the typical encounters that are common in the game.

Verant does not WANT there to be a "best" character. When people can figure out how to make a Best Character then everyone starts doing it, and pretty soon everyone is the same. Think about quake. The only thing differentiating different players (besides their names or their skins) is the manual dexterity and hand-eye coordination (and speed of cpu and graphics card) and the armor and powerups the player has accumulated. And ANYONE can theoretically gather up the same powerups, so everyone in theory can be running with exactly the same character, just with different names and skins.

Verant wants a mix of characters, and they achieve this by giving the various classes differing abilities, then attempting (not the qualifier) to balance the game such that effective groups NEED to include characters of the different classes.

That approach has a lot of merit in my book. It is dissatisfying in many people's books not to allow us to make an ubercharacter that can do it all, but it is good for the game in the long run, at least that is my opinion.
