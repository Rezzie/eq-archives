Ryan Ingram
unregistered 		posted 04-04-2000 10:15 PM           
As for the EBay change; I'm indifferent. Yes, people farming items are a problem. But on the other hand, EULA/TOS aside, people feel that their characters are their property and as such feel that it's their right to do with it what they want, including sell it.

The other change I feel is totally unacceptable in its current form. To quote: They can take from your computer any "(ii) data relating to any program that we, in our rasonable discretion, determine interferes with the proper operation of EverQuest". This is -incredibly- broad.

The way Smedley speaks of these changes is also misleading. Especially the following: "We have developed the technology to check for these hacking tools/programs and report that fact back to us." I am a developer; I've never used ShowEQ, but I've read its code and I know how it works. There is NO possible way for them to detect that ShowEQ or any other packet sniffer/hack program running on a separate computer. What they have done is to add a very simple way to detect people who are running Windows ShowEQ or other programs on the same machine at the same time as Everquest.

My problem with their methodology is that it they don't limit their checks to known hack programs; they are ACTIVELY searching out people who are using standard development tools, and sending themselves a significant amount of information about your system's setup beyond that of their program.

An example: Do you feel it is Verant's right to know that you have MS Visual Studio, the most popular Windows development environment, installed on your system?
What about the fact that they were ALREADY doing these checks before the license agreement was changed?

To those of you who would dismiss me as a "disgruntled hacker": Do you write all your mail on postcards? Why use envelopes if you have nothing to hide? I don't have anything to hide, but I use envelopes to send mail nonetheless--I simply feel strongly about my privacy rights.

I understand they wish to have a way to check for people running ShowEQ or other hack/cheat programs on the same system... and people stupid enough to do that deserve to be banned, IMO. But this clause is far too broad; I'm sure something more narrow would suffice. If anyone would care to suggest a better wording for (ii) that gives them a more limited ability to search and/or forces them to document and report to us (via the patch message and their web site, for example) what data they actually obtain and send.

I would have no problem with them sending a message to their server that says "I have detected ShowEQ running on this system". I do have a problem with them sending (as they have done previously) a message detailing a commercial non-hacking program is installed and running on your machine.

EQ can search my hard drive, for all I care; the second it sends that information to Verant or anyone else is what I don't want. If they want to do something like that-- simply make the EQ client quit: "EQ has detected a possible software conflict with your system. Please contact Verant Technical Support". VI has no right to know what else I have or am running on my system.
