Trooper, I wanted to address yours first. I'm confused as to why you think AC is empty? If you define empty as in "I've been hunting around, and OH S*IT, WHERE'S TOWN??, yes, you can definitely feel an emptiness there. I know I feel it when I'm standing on a high hill, looking all around me for miles. With no zones to mark your travel with, sure, the world is a big empty place because coming up on a town is just that...there's no "zoning" into a town.

If you define empty as a lack of intra-zone complaints, auctions, ooc's, and other such talk, you got me there, too. You can only see what people say that are in your general area. So out in the middle of the woods, who can really hear you scream? (ok, had to throw that one in there.)

If you define empty as lack of things to fight? Well, on that I have to disagree with you. In EQ, I have waited for nearly half an hour for a kodiak to show up, only to have three spawn in mid-air and stay there for another 10 minutes before being split up enough to attack. No fun there. It hurts worse when you wait and wait for that spawn, only to have someone come by and outboom you. No experience, no loot...nothing for your time. In AC, there are spawns all over the place. There are dozens of dungeons with great loot, and campfires in the woods with monsters huddled around them, and so much more. I'm sorry, I can't agree with you there.

If you define empty as lack of questing and in-game events, then again, I have to disagree with you. Every month, there's a large scale storyline that gets added to....usually it ties in with the last month's event. Like right now....last month, large spires of shadow "appeared" from the ground, and shapes of a demon in the lightning began to appear in the storms. This month, I have seen players speaking of the shadows that have begun to appear. And it wasn't "Hey, you see those shadows there? Wonder what they drop?", it was more "Have you seen the shadows that lurk near the spires?" "Yes, I have..and I have warned the towns"....THESE PEOPLE ROLEPLAYED! And they did it on their own...because a story is lurking in the background that they can attach themselves to.

If you define empty as "lack of players compared to EQ", well, ok..you've got me on that one for sure. There isn't any crowding issues I can see, and there are fewer servers. But, you know...I got tired of running all the way over to a place, only to find that it's entirely camped, and nobody wants to share. As for why all of this is here? Simple. Verant has fallen into the Hollywood style gaming company mentality. You guys are getting shafted big time, and I just want you to see that. This is coming from a former HARDCORE Eq lover...not some pansy "I played EQ for a month, ugh!".

As for the "Grass is always greener", yeah, you always gotta watch out for that. I think I have. I compare AC to EQ and UO, and even stack it up against games coming out. I know it's not the best (there are downsides, like no on call GM's in-game...if you have an issue, you send an e-mail. I don't like that.), but the game design is much more thought out, and it's so easy to get in there, look the way you want, fight the way you want, and have some really great adventures and roleplaying experience...especially with the patron system.

Anyways, that's my rant. Just think about it, is all I ask.

Ok, good point..I like the chocolate analogy, myself . But I did want to correct you on one point. Although it's true that in AC you can create a much more soloable character, you're not omnipotent as you might think. What they've done in AC is make dungeons level specific only. This keeps the newbies out of the advanced dungeons, and the powergamer away from farming items from lower level dungeons. I like that thought. As for interclass dependancies, you should see how happy the melee classes are with you when you've enchanted them with sprint and quickness, enchanted their weapons with blood drinker, enchanted their armor with invulterability, etc....to be a magic user in AC puts 1600 spells at your disposal, spread across 4 different schools of magic...to train in a school of magic gives you access to hundreds of spells..the ones you pick are up to you. Personally, I have an archer with creature magic so I can enhance myself and others, and life magic, to heal myself and others. Later, if I want to learn war magic, I can, and choose from their spells...or item magic...it's all up to me. My class is my vision of the character. I like that.
Another example is the warrior I created...skilled in swords and unarmed combat, she also trained the ability to use healing kits, of which there are various types to heal various amounts of damage. She's in plate mail for the most part, and she carries five swords: one lightning, one fire, one ice, and one acid, and one "regular" sword that hits for nice damage". I can use these swords for different types of monsters, because some monsters are more prone to different types of attacks than others. I hope to someday have an Atlan sword, because that has a container for a stone, of which there are 4 stones spread across the land. When you need a special type of attack (fire), you put in the fire stone...and now you have a nice sword that does fire damage...etc. When a character goes into attack mode, their character not only stands in an attack pose based on the type of weapon they have, but their body shifts about...ready to attack in any direction. With the ability to adjust the power and height of your swing manualy (if you choose), and the variations of attacks based on power and distance from the target, you really feel like a warrior. There's also collision detection in the game, and so houses are safe areas, and arrows hit tree branches and fall to the ground. Arrows and war magic spells are line of sight..so no arrows doing 90 degree turns.

I guess what I'm saying, and the whole point of this, is that despite what you may have seen in a brief look at AC, there's alot of well thought out magic and melee going on in there. These are things that EQ could have put in, but don't....and they keep coming up with excuses on why they can't keep this promise,and how this feature that other companies come with STANDARD for any rpg, don't fit into Verant's "Vision" of a game. I'm here to warn you to NOT get sucked into believing that you should get any less than what these gaming companies OWE you in a game you pay for. If they're the only gig in town, maybe. But they're not. AC is out, others come close behind. Dark Age of Camelot, Horizons, Deimos, Awakening Project...they're all coming...and ALL of them are coming with the features that AC has as standard. If Verant is too proud to keep with a standard vision of RPG and offer at least the same features as their competition, why get sucked in with them?
