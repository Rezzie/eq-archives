Hmm, I think I like the idea of a set number of available points that can be distributed between disciplines through practice. That is, you practice Discipline A up to the max available points (100), and then start practicing Discipline B. Discipline B gains 1 point, and A goes to 99.

This allows a melee char to create their own strategy, to choose between available specialties, to become wickedly offensive or frustratingly defensive (or any combination).

The important thing is that it would introduce an element of STRATEGY and customization that has been sadly lacking in melee classes. Up until now it has been just wading in an hacking away. Casters can choose to be offensive or defensive by choosing which spells to cast and when. Melees have been one-trick ponies, beyond crude boolean tactics (taunt/no-taunt, timed kicks, etc).

A set number of points total points and changing distribution based on usage means that the player actually gets to choose HOW his melee char acts in battle, rather than WHEN.

So, you can work on as many of the skills as you'd like, but can never have more than 100 pts in a skill at 60. And I'd make the effect of a particular skill VERY pronounced at about the 75 pt mark (earliest level 58). This way 'specializing' or 'focusing' on one discipline will have a MARKED gain over keeping several of them active.

Next, I'd allow them to switch them as needed in combat. I would however suggest that they make them 'combat' skills so they can be placed on buttons as needed. At level 50, in BGH's, if anything I'd say casters (dmg casters) are at a SERIOUS disadvantage to melee'ers. By damage casters, I mean mages, wizards, and necromancers. There is a semblance of balance at work here, because we have MUCH more usefulness against standard Mobs.
