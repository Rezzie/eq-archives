I think their "not focusing on the numbers" is ridiculous.

Things that are represented by numbers:

Hps
AC
Attack skill*
Character level
Character stats
Skill increases**
Melee damage
Spell damage
Non combat damage: (falling/drowning)
Mana requirement for spells**
Casting time
Weapon damage
Weapon delay
Stat bonuses
Item charges
Weight

...and I'm sure there's a few more.

Things that don't show numbers:

Total mana
Damage done to you by other's spells
Damage done to NPCs by other's spells
Skills ratings
Level difference between players/NPCs
Faction rating

...and possibly a couple more.

* I don't know why this is even shown at all. They show attack rating with a weapon, but not your skill level with it? Say what??

** Another more direct contradiction that flies in the face of their logic. WHY show mana usage or skill increases if these relate to some "mysterious" element of the game??

If I can tell what my skill is when I get an increase, why can't they show what it is when it's static? Either they should say "your skill level in X has increased" with no numeric value or put one in the skill list. Their explanation is so half baked it's incredible!

To a lesser degree this holds true in spellcasting. I could cast a low-delay, low-refresh spell over and over to find out how much mana i basically have. They should either take out the mana cost or give me a numeric value for mana.

The only thing I can deduce from their lack consistency in this is they didn't get something finished or had it 99% coded and, true to form, decided it would take too much effort to fix - hence the "mystery" excuse.

Sorry if this sounds irate, but even fantasy worlds have some semblance of logic (e.g. Star Trek), but EQ falls on their face here. Either show them all or hide them all, but be consistent!!
