Well, I guess it's time for me to be my normally opinionated ornery cuss. (This is a slightly off topic rant, but this looked like the best place for it.)

I think Verant dropped the ball with the trade skills. Baking, brewing, and pottery are all pretty much useless. Blacksmithing and Tailoring are usefull until you reach the low teens. Jewelcraft produces expensive items with low stats. Fletching is moderately usefull...for people who use a bow (of course). Alchemy was broken for the longest time, and produces outrageously expsensive items. Tinkering is just beginning to get some usefull items implemented. These skills were supposed to add flavor to the game, well the flavor is quite bland as far as the trades are concerned.

A while back, I had read that the reason why the trade skills didn't produce anything magical, and why it's taking so long to get the Enchanted items in the game, is that they didn't want these items to be so powerfull that people would spend there time making and selling these items, rather than hunting and questing and such. I think this line of thought is bass ackwards.

Here's the line of thinking I believe Verant would be better served using. Come up with an item to be implemented in the game. Now, what's the best way to introduce the item? Would a mob drop be ideal for it? Put the item on a mob. Think you can use it in a quest? Come up with a quest and put it in. Have something that doesn't quite fit well with those two options? Concider letting/making players make the item.

For an example of a player made "magic" item, I'll use the Lockjaw Hide Vest. How do you get one of these vests? Answer: You kill Lockjaw the giant crocodile. Let me get this straigh, you kill a crocodile and magically you get this vest? How? Is LJ wearing this vest, made of his own skin? Before you kill him, does he stop and say, "Hold on for a moment.", sew the vest together for you, then say, "Okay, you can kill me now"?

What if LJ dropped, of all things, a "Lockjaw Hide" (orignal name, huh?) You take this hide and put it in your sewing kit with a pattern, and possibly a "special" sewing needle and thread, which have to be either bought or quested for. Hit the combine button and Viola, you either have the vest or the opportunity to go kill LJ again, if you know what I mean. Lockjaw doesn't spawn any more or less often than before. It doesn't drop the "vest" any more or less often than before. The vest is nor more or less "special" than before. There aren't going to be any more or fewer people hunting for him either. What you will have, is a lot of players, in their low 20s, working on their tailoring skills.

Finally, as a side note. Am I the only person who thinks it a little odd, that most of the Enchanter's Temple of Ro quests involve taking an item to an NPC Enchanter...to get it enchanted?

First off, I recall someone telling me that the arrows summoned by magicians are NOT magical. If true, forget that idea in the post above this one.

As for enchanters or no enchanters, I have to say I was pleased to hear that you would not need to get an enchanter involved for magic arrows.

First off, many people feel the enchanters already have too much of a lock on some trade skills. Jewelcraft can certainly be done by a non-enchanter but you are completely beholden to an enchanter to make your metal. And a number of enchanters developed one or more trade skills when the wu gauntlets were finally fixed. To require every single magic item to come through an enchanters hands is basically to give them domain over the trade skills and that's not very fair.

And it's also true that you would probably be annoyed keeping up with the demand, and not want to do it, at which point demand would dry up and nobody would use them. So it would all be for nothing.

Enchanters can enchant items. But they don't have to enchant ALL items.
