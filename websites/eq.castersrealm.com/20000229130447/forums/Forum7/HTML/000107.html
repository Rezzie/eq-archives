The advantage to the rain spell is that if there are 2 targets both will get hit twice. 3 Targets, then 2 will be hit once and one twice. Low and behold if there is one target it will get hit 3 times. 3? Yeah 3. Why is that you might ask? Do the math. If the Rain spell could hit the one target 4 times it would have a quit superior damage to mana ratio Vr DD agianst one target at several lvls. Usually that would happen 2 or 3 lvls after you get the Rain spell. Where a normal DD gets like 3 more damage per lvl the rain is getting 3 more PER hit.. Thus two lvls later the rain spell is doing 24 more damage total if it gets 3 more damage per hit per lvl. When you add that to AoE spells inherantly better or at least equal damage to mana ratio you get some unbalanceing ratios.

Now if you are casting agianst 4 targets this means that you really don't care if they move after you hit them once because the spell is already used up. SO you can use Rain spells to group kite same as a Piller spell.

Nope, I've tested this against three targets,
a Rain spell will pulse ONCE.

You actually thought Verant would round UP?

The only time you get the "max" damage cap of a rain spell is against multiple of TWO.

The PROBLEM with rain spells is that when you do target TWO or FOUR targets the spell is no longer as cost effective compared to a targettable AE, ie. Circle of Force, or pb radius, ie. Thunderclap.

--------
Hard numbers: (All 34 spells)
TWO targets.

Lavastorm
Cast time: 4.5
Refresh: 12.0
Mana: 200
Damage: 2*128 = 256 each

Circle of Force
Cast time: 4.5
Refresh: 6.0
Mana: 175
Damage: 216 each, I don't think CoF hold time is 6.0, but if it is, you can see it makes the "Rain" spell look even worse.

Thunderclap
Cast time: 3.1
Refresh: 12.0
Mana: 175
Damage: 232 each
--------

At FOUR targets the numbers skew even more towards Circle of Force and Thunderclap

Lavastorm
Damage = 128 each

Circle of Force
Damage = 216 each

Thunderclap
Damage = 232 each

Rain spells may have the "potential" to do more damage (WITH TWO OR LESS TARGETS ONLY), but it's too little to offset the slower casting and refresh times, and the increase in mana cost.
