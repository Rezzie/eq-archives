I think another aspect of why the stats remain such a mystery is because if we knew just how little they did, many would probably not bother getting items.

I'd wager the mana equation is similar to:

Mana = Class level + (stat bonus)*level

where class level is the amount of increase a certain class gets per level and (stat bonus) is the equation used to determine mana given by items (+mana or +int) with a max value of 1 which gives a total mana equal to the same class unbuffed one level higher.

Maybe it's not quite that bad, but when I take off all +int and +mana items and med up, put them all on I see very, very little white space yet to med up.

Maybe all stats aren't quite so limiting, but i'm betting that the difference between having the best items and having basic, easy to get items would have very little difference in comparison to the effort it takes to get them.

I've argued before - why show numbers on every stat including level, individ. stats, attack stat, AC, etc. if you do very little to quantify their overall effect? The only answer i've can logically come up with is that it gives you numbers to chase even if they have very little overall effect.
