b. the subject at hand. this bullshit line about wizards being the best at DD. fine, so i can do tons of DD. really really fast. i ask, do you think they stopped to consider this: in order to live up to the reputation of doing the most damage in the shortest amount of time a wizard must do what? constantly recast his highest level damage spell. what happens to the little wizard who makes this mistake? anyone? anyone at all? they become a fine paste when the monster they're fighting decides to be your best friend. at one time it MIGHT have been useful for a wizard to be able to do this. it still is. for killstealing. granted, it's nice when you have to finish off something that's running away and you don't want a train. to say that a wizard is useless is kinda silly. but. to get back on topic: if a wizard is dumb enough to burn a monster as hard as he can he's dead. yup, great way to specialize in a way of doing damage. instead, if you feel like taking part in a battle you are likely to be the clean up crew. if you really feel like taking part you have to hit the monster every once in a while so your tanks/other casters have time to deal a bit more damage in order to distract them. there was a time when root worked. there was a time when you could root something and then spank the shit out of it. if you want to call that the reason why we were the masters of DD, then fine. i can agree with that. the problem being that it doesn't work that way anymore. what happened the last 20 times i've tried to root something and cast a DD spell on it? it broke root and came looking for my sorry ass. you want to know what happened the rest of the time? root got resisted. vs. a white goblin in perma. even the blues root far too often in my opinion. perhaps the resist rate will change. but the rate at which they break root seems to be pretty constant. they stand in once place until you try to do what you're SUPPOSED to do and cast a DD spell at them. yup. that makes a whole pantload of sense...
