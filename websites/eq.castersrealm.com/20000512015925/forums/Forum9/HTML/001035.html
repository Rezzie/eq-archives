There has been some recent debate over whether humans actually do get any exp bonus for our lack of night vision and mediocre stats. It seems that the PR folks do not wish to disclose if this exists (and in fact refuse to talk about it at all really), so my questuion must go out to the player-base.

IF there is a human exp-bonus, do we LOSE it if we use an illusion? Has anyone tried to evaluate this? I was recently told not to use illusions such as Fire elemental or Earth elemental because I would lose the exp bonus of my race.

If this is the case then it will really be a tough call about illusion use whilst fighting. It will be ok to still use them for travel, but in the case of EE or FE I am not sure I ought to be using it.

Anyhow, this, in a way, brings up the whole SOW debate in that it is forever unclear what, exactly, illusions ARE. Do we actually turn into the critter? Or do we just look like it? My feeling is that if it is a true transform, then we OUGHT to get sow for wolf and werewolf form, but NOT get the vaunted "human exp bonus". If we just look different then the exp should not change.

Any thoughts?

Humans, for the most part, have no exp bonus. It's a load of you-know-what. Does the hint book (yes, I mean the official verant approved hint book) say that humans level faster? Yes it does. But that is a load of nonsense.

Here's a chart that you can look at to see what I mean:
http://members.theglobe.com/minnex/

These numbers were not gotten using showeq, by the way. They are strictly tests based on the number or rats to reach level 2 or 3 by class/race. But the findings have been more-or-less confirmed by showeq, so i am pretty confident they are accurate.

As you can see, human casters level at exactly the same rate as other races. And it's pretty much the same for other classes as well.

If I had chosen to be human because I wanted to powergame my way to 50 faster than my friends, I might be pretty upset by this. But I just happened to think humans looked cool, and I was tired of seeing all those cookie-cutter high elf or erudite casters. I picked it to be different, so, this doesn't bother me all that much.

As for getting the racial exp benefits of being human, it's kinda a moot point since there doesn't seem to be a lot of difference among the races. I highly doubt there is an exp advantage or disadvantage to being in illusion form.

As for our illusions, I agree to a point. BUt you know and I know it's never going to happen. Druids and shamen would scream bloody murder if we got sow with our wolf illusion.

Anyway, we already have sow. All I gotta do is shout "trading clarity for sow" and i have a zone full of shamen and druids running to find me.
