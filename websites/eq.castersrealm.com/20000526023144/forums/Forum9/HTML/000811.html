Mesmerization, Mezmerize, Memory Blur Charm, Color Flux, Whirl till You Hurl, Fear & Chaotic Feedback.

Q: What do all of these spells and their respective lines do?

A: They put a monster(s) out of a fight for a variable amount of time.

From a 0 second stun with the Chaotic Feedback spell, to interrupt a caster while smacking the creature with a bit of damage and making it hate you a bit more, to making the creature go back home with Memory Blur, we can makes the monster:

(a) stop casting
(b) stop fighting
(c) fight for us
(d) go home
(e) forget who it hates
(f) forget everything and go home
(g) all of the above =)

The other aspect of control, which is arguably just as powerful as stunning/mindwiping, but may not be as obvious is messing with it's hatred list. With a Chaotic Feeback (or part of the line) or two, one can easily pull a mob off of the wizard that's trying like hell to cast that Evacuate Spell. Sure, you could have mesmerized, stunned, feared, whirled, etc, but whether or not, the spell is resisted, the monster STILL hates you a lot. (This being said, there are a lot of different things you can do, some better in some situations and some worse. It matters that you know all your options at the split second that you need to make that crucial decision.)

While I'm on my high horse, let me touch upon another, even more discrete method -- buffing and debuffing. There is nothing like making your fighters faster, stronger, and quicker, while doing the opposite for your opponent.

Some methods might work better than other for you or you might not be as successful at one or the other. Just remember that your skills and statistics effect your this success rate. Don't buff your charisma up the wazoo if you're going to be a buff/debuffer. You need intelligence and mana and if you have to aggrivate the monster, you might want to look into agility and armor class.
