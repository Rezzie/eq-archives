Here's how you beat him

1) Have as few people as possible.. yes the more you have the more he can AE lifetap.

2) Have everyone who is NOT a tank stand very far away from the battle.

3) The only magic Maestro is susceptable to is fire (necros cannot ignite bones, but they can ignite blood). So everyone nuke with their fire spells.

4) The enc mana sieve secret works like crap here. He still casts the spell because it's an ability for him and not a mana using spell.

5) Do NOT use pets unless you have the focus item for your pet. This is meant to insure only big nasty pets and fighting and cuts down on the number of ae lifedraws that Maestro gets. We killed him without pets after the patch with about 20 people. We got killed the first time with pets... go figure.

If you follow these steps, you should be good for the go. ;-)

Maestro killed!

Last night on Cazic-Thule, the new and improved Maestro died a slow death. The fight started with about 45 people and after the 3 min fight about 34 were still standing.

Tactically, the casters (and clerics) were warned to stay wayyyy back because the Lifetap AoE spell (song?) has an enormous radious. The casters did not cast (or move) until given a signal when Maestro was between half and third health. The clerics such as myself instructed the tanks to come away from battle and back to the SW corner for complete heals. Thus all casters remained outside the AE range.

An interesting thing did happen which might have helped. About 15 seconds into the maestro fight the SW corner spawned one cleric. Half the tanks fought and killed the cleric while the other half engaged Maestro up the west wall a bit. The cleric fight fortunately was outside the AE spell range of Maestro. So in efect we ended up with two waves of tanks, which I imagine helped decrease the lifetap effect.

Our group did an excellent job of letting the tanks melee for the first 2 minutes. This kept Maestro in one spot (not up the wall). It is very important that he does not move so the melee types can surround him. When our casters started their damage, Maestro did start moving around and took out a few unfortunate ones. But those were his last breaths of life.
