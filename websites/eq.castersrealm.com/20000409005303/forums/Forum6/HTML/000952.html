Kedge is great Make researching your fire pet your highest priority. Go down to kedge, summon a fire pet, and do a /pet guard here near the zone line, then swim up to the entrance of the first room and take a look around.

The fish in there are VERY aggressive and VERY nasty... it's very common for one of the regular lancers to double attack for 60+. The trick is to have patience, and to try and pull one when it's by its self. Be sure to avoid the impaler that hangs around near the back of the room.

I usually try my best to look above and below the entrance (check the walls above and below the fallen pillar, just inside the room), and pull any single lancers that may be floating there. At first I used Burst of Flame to pull them since its casting time was so fast, but now that I have the timing down a bit better I use Malise.

Once you've successfully pulled a single one, give it maybe 10 or 15 seconds to get a few good jabs in on your pet so that it'll be mad at your pet and its fire shield, and then lead in with a heal. Alternate between pet heals and your DD (use your best judgement) until the fish is dead. When I first started, it would normally take me about half a bar of mana to kill one, which was discouraging because of the downtime... once you get used to it though, it should only take you around 2 bubbles to solo one fish (which is about what it takes me now, with an INT of 179 for comparison).

The zone is almost always empty, except for maybe one group of 40+ players deeper in the dungeon. However, sometimes you'll get lucky enough to have a ranger or another mage there (seems to be the only 2 classes I ever see soloing in Kedge) who will offer to pull for you. You can learn quite a bit about pulling them just by watching what they do.

Hope that helps

I spent levels 35-42 in Kedge Keep. Do NOT use a fire pet here unless you are in a group. Do NOT park your pet and swim down to any rooms. 90% of the time I spent in Kedge was by myself.

Stay by the zone and always make sure you have a waterstone in a slot to use when your air runs out. Use an earth pet and park it right at the zone. Carefully swim a little out into the main room and look for LANCER SWORDFISH. Be careful as sometimes one may be hovering above you near the ceiling. Use malise to pull. Try to single one out. At times 2-3 may come. If this happens zone! Don't even try to take on 2-3 until you reach low 40's.

Don't worry about the Stamina. The only way to remedy this is to have someone throw a stamina buff on you.

Someone asked the same question a while back and Dhimmid of Bristlebane posted a good guide on how to work the front room there. I followed it using a fire pet and got VERY good XP there. I am reposting it for your enjoyment

--------------------------------------------
The trick is patience and spacing. One lancer will swim straight up to sit on the fallen pillar. Safe single pull.

There are 3 spots the fishies can be just under the ledge with the fallen pillar. Any of these will aggro on you just for sitting on the pillar.

At the same time, there are 2 spots directly above the fallen pillar (past the ceiling edge, I mean). These spots will also aggro on you for sitting on the pillar.

So the trick is to wait for the first to come to the pillar (if you don't want to wait, swim up/look down or swim down/look up to scout the spots I just described. Pull the single in front.

Then start clearing the spots I just described that are close to the front ledge. This involves patience, because you have to wait for all but one lancer to move to the far side or rear spots. The wanderers (pacing back and forth will rarely aggro when pulling from the front).

OK, now the front spots are cleared, pull from any spot that looks somewhat separated from other lancers and is on the OPPOSITE side the piercer/impaler is sitting on.

Wanderers: These guy's are very tricky to pull without aggro'ing the piercer/impaler. The trick is to time a little DD. Wait for the piercer to move to the left (or rear) spot. Now target the wanderer that is moving right to left. Sit and wait a sec, then cast the spell before you can even see him. If you time it right, it will hit him WAY over in the right hand covie just as he is appearing again.

The same holds true for the left to right wanderer. Wait till piercer/impaler is on the right. Move to the right a bit, then time your shot to hit him JUST as he's about to appear.
