I'm a Rallos player, with occasional time logged on Tallon, and the first thing you have to understand is the PvP servers are nearly a different game with the same mechanics. It's definitely not for everyone. I started on a blue server, decided to test Rallos one day, and ever since have been nothing but bored if I tried a blue again. Like Lestat said, there is just much more of a rush to facing down a player.

Kill-stealing is in fact a much lower problem. A wizard does not want to go low mana stealing a kill from a meleer because it would probably cost them their ass.

The only twinked items you'll see are pretty much weapons, as they are unlootable now. Other characters have twinked equipment, for sure, but not for long.

On the cooperation thing. You will find that people will respect each pretty often, for the obvious reason that they have the ability to hurt each other if someone starts mouthing off. 85% of people on Rallos are not not random player killers and will work with you. Beware the other 15% as they often try to blend in with the 85%. And never trust a naked druid.

The down side to the PvP servers is oppurtunity attacks. That being people who attack you when you're low on health/mana and/or engaged with a mob. This can usually be avoided by grouping, though. Also, the bottom levels are hard. The newbie killers on Rallos are the eternal 5th-10th levels who like to execute lowbies. You'll find them around every major city. Just pay attention to the shouts and you'll know who they are. But this also directly leads to one of the most fun concepts of the PvP servers - payback.

As a final note you're going to be killed on the Zeks. It's a fact. But if you play smart and are not afraid to get your hands dirty you'll be able to give at least as well as you receive. And a mage is a PvP terror if you know what you're doing.
