I understand your basic reasoning here, but something one has to remember is that this game is more like apartments than a home. With that in mind I present the following edited letter:


Dear Mr. and Mrs. Remotsuc,

Thank you for your interest and suggestions about renovations for your apartment. Sadly, we here at the firm of Tnarev Architecture, Inc. do not feel that the changes you've suggested are in keeping with our vision for the structure as we designed it. After meeting with all designers involved, we have decided that to add a sunroom to the living area as you've proposed would ultimately decrease the enjoyment of the homes of other tennants, and would decrease the overall satisfaction of the majority of our residents, and therefore we cannot permit it. As for your second request, I'm afraid we cannot permit you to have access to the blueprints for the structure, as we feel at this time that attention to technical details (structure, wiring,plumbing, etc.) would undoubtedly lead to the exploitation of the structure (i.e. theft of electricity and cable services). We assure you that the water you assert (as yet unconfirmed) is leaking from your dining room ceiling is not due to any errors on the part of our crew, and may be an unintended side effect of maintainence performed on a neighbors residence; we are looking into it.

Please do not attempt to determine the locations of pipes yourselves, as this will violate the terms of the lease agreement signed by all parties when you took residence last March.

Thank you for your continuing support.

Trarev Architecture, Inc.

Ok, so I couldn't let the fix one thing break another part slide, but overall I think it more reflects the situation.

If this was meant to be purely humorous, it succeeded. If it was meant to reflect your views, I urge you to read mine and tell me what you think.

You have some points Ammudana; it is everyone's game, and I don't think anyone but the most selfish of us would want to see changes made or information given that would spoil everyone else's fun.

I think the real crux of the original post though is that even changes that a seeming majority of EQ players want are ignored when they interfere with the so-called "vision."

Same goes for info. I cannot imagine how knowing the particulars of how all stats (including exp) work in the game could possibly be a detriment to anyone's play. For a lot of us, stats are at the root of RPG's; it's one of the things we enjoyed (or still do) about pencil and paper games. And Brad says that EQ is supposed to bring to the computer as much of the pencil/paper feel as possible. Fiddling with stats, noticing the difference a new piece of equipment because we can see it directly everytime we roll the 20-sided die and now only need an 11 to hit armor class O instead of a 12. The fact of the matter is that changes in stats in eq are so incremental, and it takes so long to get items, that it's impossible to "feel" any differences between one item and the last one you used. Those of us who are stat-hungry want to be able to choose which gear we're wearing based upon some kind of statistical certainties, not upon the "feel" of the item, which is unreliable and prone to variations in the random numbers spit out in any given fight. To this day, no one knows whether 10 str is better than 10 ac, and thats ridiculous. Why give us numbers at all if we can't even use em?

If people don't want to focus on stats, fine, they don't need to. The precise explanation of stats doesn't need to be given away in game, we're asking for it out here so we can use it to whatever degree we choose. It's obvious how this will make some people happier, but totally unclear how anyone would be made unhappier by having this information.

My fear about the stats is that they do so little, or that Verant has made such incredibly large errors in implementing them, that they don't want us to see what they did, knowing full well the uproar that's going to ensue. The halfling vs. human exp thing, for example, which is CLEARLY a mistake, but isn't being admitted as such, because they realize just how drastic the fix would have to be. We have ALWAYS been told since the beginning of beta that humans level faster, which is their benefit for inferior stats and (!!!) no night vision. Verant says they want to preserve the mystery of the game, and yet many major, major game bugs affecting balance have been discovered by players digging into the nuts and bolts of the thing using log parsers and other 3rd party tools(mage pets not dual wielding, for example).

Verant, stop being so damn defensive of your screwups. We know humans make mistakes. We expect them. We forgive them. Some of them are going to be large. But bending the rules in order to disguise the mistakes as intended features is childish. When you do something like the halfling/human exp thing, just put it back the way it's supposed to be and say "whoops!"

Your relationship with your customers is like any interpersonal relationship. When you err, we'd rather you just said sorry and we all move on compared to making excuses and making us all feel like the relationship is strained.

And we're going to find out when you've made mistakes. Why not let us help you find them, and fix them now, rather than later.
