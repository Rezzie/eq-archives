Of all the problems currently faced in EQ, I find nothing worse than the overcrowding. It leads to more problems between players than just about anything. The problems faced in "Play nice rules" and what not would be moot if Verant would take some proactive stance in crowd control.

Being on Fennin, I found the split a good start, but slowly but surely the population at peak times has crept above 1900. It was around 1600 when the server first split. The bottom line is splitting alone isn't worth the time and effort that goes into it if Verant is going to absolutely nothing (what they've done so far) in keeping the population levels at a decent level. To me decent is as low as possible, but at 1500 or lower is best. Even at their target of 1800 it can be very difficult, or possible with extensive travelling, to find a decent spot to hunt or camp.

I also don't see how it fits their vision of "server community" and building personal reputations if they're going to have to continually split servers because they won't stem the tide of incoming players.

Most of the new players I talk to don't have a clue to the overcrowding problem, and many have said that they chose Fennin (when it was the largest) because they thought it must be the best if there were so many people. *sigh* I can't blame them or their logic when they have no experience in trying for hours to find a spot. By the time they're mid-to-high level and find out for themselves, they don't want to lose all the time invested.

All of this could be nipped in the bud if Verant would do SOMETHING to prevent it rather than their typical option of damage control. It's one of the chief, if not THE chief problem people complain about and yet they seem to do NOTHING to prevent it. WHY?
