On various servers I have been playing an enchanter for about 6 months and a lot of that time was before I even discovered I could use daggers to augument my pets abilities. After the most recent nerf, I will be putting my enchanter on the shelf until enchanters are addressed and compensated for their loss, but I would like to pass on some of the wisdom I gained in my time as an enchanter to help anyone new to the class.

Summon the best pet you can

I frequently see enchanters just summon a pet and go with it, I shake my head in sadness at this. It may take a while to summon the best pet you can for your given level, but this is even more essential now than it was before. A good pet will hit harder and with greater accuracy, have a few more hp, and have better ac.

Memorize your pet spell and reclaim energy. Recalim energy is not incredibly effective, but we dont have a pet suicide command and its a lot quicker than zoning.

When you reach a new spell circle, test your new pets on some green cons you know you can survive. Take note of what the pet cons to you and what your max damage is after about 5-10 mins of use. Do this a few times until you get an idea what your max damage pet cons to you and strive to get that con and damage each pet casting.

Always buff your pet

Strength is so cheap its laughable, and it actualy does seem to make your pet hit for max damage fairly frequently.

Your haze line does cost a bit more, but the duration is decent and seems to go up with level and every ac you can give your pet counts.

Your quickness line is significanly less cost effective, so you will need to weigh its benefit with how long you expect the fight to last. At 18th it lasts about 4 minutes for me. If I expect to be fighting 2 or more mobs consecutivly, I will use it. If I will onyl be facing one mob, I'd rather save my mana for other spells.

Buffing yourslef

With the exeptions of short duration buffs, it is very helpfull to buff yourslef with the same buffs you give your pet. While it does not hurt to have the buffs on yourslef, the main reason for this is to know when they wear off on your pet so you can keep it buffed.

Controlling your pulls
(These tactics are targeted at solo play, but can be adjusted to grouping once you have a feel for your capabilities)

I look for camps that have no more than 3 mobs that con high blue or less. You mgith be able to take even higher mobs, but at even and above, you will notice much more frequent resists and dying is somethign I'm hoping to help you avoid here.

Once you have found such a camp, take note of if you are running distance of a zone or guard.

If you are in range of a zone or guard.
Start with a lull line on the weakest con in the camp. If it resists you will have the whole camp on you. These spells have a VERY short duration, and not too reliable, so you need to act quick. Next target the most powerfull mob in the camp and mez it. If all goes as planned, you shold find the remaining mob charging at you for solo confrontation. Be sure to back off a fair distance fromt he camp to deal with him outside of the camp agro range.

If you are NOT in zone or guard distance, you will need to be able to deal with a 2 on one charge. These occasionaly result in death and are a lot more risky. In thsi case, I mez the most powerfull mob in the camp with brings the 2 weaker ones at me. I let one hit me to get my pet on it then back up to isolate the other one. Once he is a perceptible distance from the one the pet is engaged in, root him (root casts quicker than mez) and back up. At this point, you need to determine if your pet is in danger or not. If your pet IS in danger, DoT or debuff the mob that is on him and tank for him (and pray that root lasts). If he is not in danger, go ahead and mez the rooted mob then help out your pet. After you finish off the initial mob, evaluate your mana. If you have enough to finsih the seocnd mob, do so. If you are low on mana, you should have tiem to run to guards or zone even if its a ways away. If you were able to finish both mobs, CONGRATULATIONS on a successfull camp break up. If you were not successfull, look for a slightly weaker camp and repeat.

Duo playing

While I despise grouping with large groups (chaos and disorganization are more hassle than they are worth), playing in a small 2 player group can be very beneficial.

If you are solo at a camp and a player within a reasonable level range approaches, or if you approach a camp with a solitary player, consider offering to group with them.

The tactics outlines above will work even better with someoen to back you up, and since if a pet does more than half the damage in a solo kill you only get half exp (which will usualy be the case), you do not loose on the exp end since pets in groups do not take nearly as much exp (the group bonus should nulify the pet loss).

Dual enchanters.
Take advantage of being able to cast two mez spells at once!

Enchanter and a mage or necro.
Keep your own pets buffed as normal, but consider using your speed buffs on their pets instead of castign offensive spells. You will be most effective as buff/debuff/crowd control here

Enchanter and wizard
People say enchanters shouldnt nuke, but in this case you may want to consider it. Aside to contributing some damage, your nukes will out taunt the wizards nukes and you have a pet to help out a bit too. Rely on quick damage but a bit more downtime for mana.

Enchanter and Druid
I havnt tried these tactics, so this one is theoretical. Have the druid sow your pet and snare the mob. Fear the mob and both you and the druid dot the mob.

Enchanter and cleric
Another theoretical one. Clerics are probably THE buff kings, so it would make sense that with their buffs, your pet might actualy compare with a mage or necro pet that can actualy tank for you while being a good DoT as well. Assuming the pet is healable, this has a lot of potential.

Enchanter and shaman
No experiece here, not even enough to get theoretical.

Enchanter and mele types
When a mele type approaches a camp, I offer to let him take the ones that are green to me in a less traditional rotation. If the mele type disagrees to this, I just sit back and let him try to take the whole camp by himslef, and when he gets himslef killed (even if the camp is already broken, mele downtime on anyoen other than a pladin will pretty much gaurantee that the camp will unbreak gradualy in their rest times (I might even buff him to give him a false sense of confidence and make him think I'm not as evil as I am)), then I resume my normal play (evil grin).

Some specific spots to try

The level listed is the minimum level I reccomend for trying these spots, but feel free to try earlier, or with a duo.

Level 4
Steamfont: kobold camps.
There is a camp near the newbie pits that has 3 in the camp and a solitary 4th one nearby. The is antoher kobold camp further north you might want to try around lvl 6 (preferably with a duo as there are some higher mobs that will agro on you if they are near a fight).

Level 8
Steamfont: By the windmills.
This area is often but not always crowded. You cna usualy find solo mobs that will give you some exp here.

Misty thicket: north side of the wall.
If you have what it takes to endure the journey to get there, this is a wonderfull place to exp from about level 8 all the way upo 14th. The actual camps here are more of a waste of time due to all the solo mobs wandering the area. Only the gobs, YKs and the rare undead will agro on you if you stay clear of the camps.

Level 12
Steamfont: dragon skeleton ruin.
At the head end of the dragon skeleton is a solitary skeleton spawn. If your not comfortable with the belwo strategy even that one spawn can be a good one.

At the tail end of the ruin, 3 skeletons spawn. This is a dangerous pull, but it can be VERY rewardign exp wise if done correctly. You are a fairly short runnign distance from the town guards, USE THIS TO YOUR ADVANTAGE. Summon a pet and mez one of the skeletons then IMMEDIATLY run to the guards. If they do catch upto you, your pet shoudl help slow them down. After the guards have killed your persuers, return to the camp and you should have one skeleton at the tail you shoudl be abel to dispatch and rest. When the other 2 spawn mez one to get a 1 vs one confrontation and you will have broken the spawn.

Level 14
Butcher block mountains: Orc tower.
Although there are 3 of them, there is a good chance of one of them being green at this level. If they are all blue, simply forget about it, or look for a partner. If one is green, mez one of the blues and the other blue should attakc with the green staying behind.

level 16
Lesser faydark: the non-sister bandit camp.
A similar approach to the orc camp above, but even if the green ones will occasionaly agro on a mez pull. However, you should be strong enough at this point to handle a 2 on one. Now if your mez happens to go bad (rare but I've had it happened), your essentialy dead. On thsi camp I liek to keep color flux and gate memd when doign the intial pull. If the pull goes bad,I can often save myslef by color flux and gate.

Level 18
Rathe mountains: Orc camp by the paladins
This is ONLY doable if you a good race.
On thsi one we fallback to the skeleton strategy mentioned above. One of the nice things bout this, is your only about a minute from a food/water/bandages vendor!

South Ro: Western derv camp.
A bit risky due to the wandering undead in the desert, but overall less difficult than the orc camp in rathe mountains. A nice bonus here is that the derv thugs occasioanly drop pages for research (rare drop, but if the first time I've had the fortune of finding a mob I can solo that drops pages)

Good luck to you, I hope that these words will be of some help to the newer enchanters out there.
