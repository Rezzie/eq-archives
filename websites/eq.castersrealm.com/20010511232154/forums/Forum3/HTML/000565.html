Well some are better at grouping and some are better at soloing, what's the point in trying to balance this? Every class has its specialities and its down sides. The curse of being a meleer is that you won't be able to solo as much as the caster, but similarly the caster's inaptitude is in his or her inability to take damage. Granted, pets nullify this statement altogether, but pet users pay for their abilities as well. Magicians will never equal to wizards/necros/enchanters or even melees in a one-on-one duel, and that goes the same for the other pet-using classes.(Enchanters can still function without pets, but they are easily beaten by a skilled caster or meleer. Monks can solo a hill giant(of all the melees, I think monks can do this alone with ease), can a paladin or warrior? You gotta ask yourself that question when you talk about balance. There is no TRUE balance in a class-based system(hence is why I like a skill-based system, but AC just isn't my cup of tea). Look at AD&D, are casters and meleers balanced? I think the main problem is that we, the customer, don't happen to agree with their form of balance. We look at the game from an individual basis, me versus them, whereas they look at the game from an overall "gameplay" perspective. They want each class to be "useful" which, frankly, they are, but we all, playing the game, want to be able to do much the same things as everyone else. We realize that some classes will be better, or worse, at various things but we don't think any class should be TOTALLY unable to fight a mob for exp without needing a group. Even if it were low blues/high greens I'm sure many classes would have no real problem with the balance issue but its that fact that even mobs 15+ levels below a melee class can kick their ass while casters are taking on even cons without really breaking much of a sweat.

From an overall perspective, no way is the game able to function without warriors/paladins/shadowknights/etc. But, the problem is, in this day of overcrowding and it being so difficult to find a group or place to hunt with your group many people are forced to look to soloing. The casters have this advantage and can gain exp while waiting for a spot in a group whereas the melee class is stuck and has to sit down and wait, wait, wait for a group to accept him in.

Its that tedium that drives the melee class up the wall that the casters can neatly avoid. The caster generally just has options to exercise in a crowded game where the melee class is stuck relying on other people to get him anything. As a bard I haven't had this problem since I got my fear song but I'm not a typical hybrid/melee class.

Either way, its something they SHOULD be addressing but that they refuse to. Is it going to lose them customers? Yes, definately. Is it going to significantly impact their profit margin or the long term health of the game? I don't think so, no. Its a question of balancing overall gameplay versus individual gameplay and something GM's and players will never agree upon.

I don't see a way they could fix the problem without causing serious problems on the high end. If you allow the melee/hybrid classes to stand toe-to-toe with blue-con mobs you then make it that much easier for a group to slaughter blue con mobs. If you downgrade casters to match the miss/hit rate of melee classes you end up seriously compromising any amount of high level grouping because no way is Nagafen going to fall if the casters only hit about 50-60% damage from what they do now.

I think that when Verant looks at it they see things from a "top down" perspective and not from actually in the game. Its fine to sit in a tuning meeting and say "all the classes fill the roles we wanted" but if those roles are making many upset, well, what's the point? The caster/melee disparity -is- a problem with the game as it stands right now and with the new PNP it becomes an even bigger issue where the soloing caster is afforded the same rights at a spawn as an entire GROUP of melee/hybrid. When that occurs, when you give the soloing caster the same portion of anything as six hybrid/melee classes you are obviously being unfair. Maybe not their intention but the inevitable result. No way should one caster, due to Verant's vision of how classes work, be able to get half the mobs from an entire group of six people who can't solo. Its basically stating that those six people are, to Verant, worth more when it comes to being "fair" about sharing spawns since they have that Verant-given ability to solo.
