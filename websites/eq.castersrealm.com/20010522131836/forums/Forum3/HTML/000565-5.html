I have very little problem with the balancing in damage dealt by casters vs. melee types. People on this thread have been comparing soloing abilities of the different classes, but I notice that one thing people are doing that is not quite fair is lumping all blue monsters together, as if they were the same. Blue monsters are DIFFERENT. There are some types that can take out casters easily (Saw a lvl 23 caster being taken out by the Hermit in SK that my Pally could take out at lvl 21 -- the Hermit's rapid attack interrupted her spells). On the other hand, my pally has no magic resistance and get wiped out by creatures with like 5 hp and no AC that can charm her and run her into nasty mobs...

Most classes can solo IF they choose the right kind of monster to go after...and if they want to...I personally thing soloing is B-O-R-I-N-G.

Regarding the binding issue, though, one thing that would help both make people happy and reduce the overcrowding problem would be to make a few more bind points for the melee types. I don't mean allow melee types to bind everywhere, just make a few more spots in neutral locations where people can be bound. Maybe teleport towers could be binding areas, or if the programming is set up such that a zone is all-or-nothing -- i.e. bind everywhere or nowhere -- then make a couple small zones where people can be bound... I would recommend a neutral bind point every few zones, so that people don't have to run through more than 3 zones to get to their corpse...that would help spread the load. As it is, the zones that are most crowded are usually those with bind points 1-2 zones away, and it isn't a coincidence (High Keep, for example, and Mistmoore, and Unrest...)

Just my 2cp worth...

I am a 41st level monk on Rodcet Nife and I've been in many a battle, so I feel I am well experienced in the way of Monkly combat and have learned some of the more advanced strategies not seen at lower levels. After playing for so long, I really know all the tricks inside and out, so I'll post them here for you to learn from.

Ok, one of the most common situations you're going to run into is when you are fighting a monster one on one. What I like to do in this situation is punch it a whole bunch until it dies, and then if one of his friends come, I punch him too, until he dies. Sometimes, when I'm fighting something fairly weak, I like to go up to it and punch it a bunch. Then if I go up against something tougher, I go up and punch it. At times, you may find yourself up against 2 or more things at once. Now this is a very advanced strategy in terms of melee combat, so pay attention. First you have to choose one of the multiple monsters. This can often be difficult since when you're fighting them they turn into this meshed ball of monster, all 3 or 4 of them occupying exactly the same space and it is nearly impossible to select any of them but the one closest to you. So instead of making a rational decision on which to fight, I like to hit the closest npc button and punch that one a bunch.

Now, here are some tricks that I've picked up along the way. Make sure that you're quad attacking instead of only once per round. You get double attack at level 15 and dual wield at level 1. Most monks don't make very good use of these skills until higher levels and I'm not really sure why. If you're following your advanced strategy and punching the monster, and your life seems to be going to run out before the monster's is (using advanced mathematics you can extrapolate your and the monster's time of death and make your decision based on that) then it might be a good idea to run. Usually run to the zone, guards or other players and yell for help. You'd probably be better off to run to a caster, since they can either heal you or cast one of their numerous spells (they are really quite versatile, unlike us lowly melee classes). Some good phrases to get help with could be "HL:JP ME" or "HLp" since it is hard to run and type at the same time. You could make a macro for this, something like "HELP ME FOR THE LOVE OF GOD HELP ME I RAN UP AND PUNCHED IT LIKE I ALWAYS DO AND I"M LOSING!". Monks also have feign death which comes in incredibly handy, since the running up and punching the baddy a bunch doesnt always work, you can feign death instead of running for help. Mend comes in handy as well, you can wait for your life to get down a couple bars and click the mend button and your life goes up some, that way you can continue your advanced 'click auto attack and tap your special move every few seconds strategy'. I feel really sorry for warriors, because all the strategy that I can think of for them is run up and slash the baddies a bunch. Maybe they're better at running or something than monks, there must be some balance.

Now young meleers, this may sound like I'm trying to say playing a melee class is boring! Not so! Not only do you get to use the above strategy (its really only one
strategy isnt it? run up and punch?) you get to SEE high NUMBERS when you punch! Thats right, you do more damage and boy, that sure is fun, doing more damage than a caster in melee, especially since melee is so much FUN, you click that ol' auto attack button and whew boy! watch the fun begin. And its also
more fun because you have more hitpoints, and more armor class. Its even MORE fun to see those monsters miss, oh man, I get excited just thinking about it, seeing all those monster misses, or better yet being blocked or dodged by me, and high damage numbers when I attack. What fun! Its even more fun since its pretty much hands off, I get to just sit here and click my dragon punch every few seconds and watch the fun scroll by, and prepare for my advanced run like a girl(sorry Moogie) strategy if things get bad.

Ok, now some of you may have detected a hint of SARCASM in there, if so, good work, you've just been promoted to Master of the Obvious. Anyone that has played a melee class knows that it is BORING after a while. I mean, I've always played a melee class, I like the idea behind them, and it was ok, I was having fun,
right? Then Verant deleted my little Quaid, and I was forced to try out a caster class. Good lord! I was having fun (fun!) at first level! I was a cleric, not only could I
SMACK things, but I could also do 2 OTHER THINGS! I could A) cast my heal spell or B) buff myself with courage. What fun! 3 THINGS to do in combat. The options were endless, I could smack, heal, buff, I could buff, heal smack, I could smack, smack smack, heal. I mean, wow, I had OPTIONS here. Then I went and got more spells and my options were increasing factorially. I mean, that may not sound like much, but jesus, I was a meleer and you do nothing but attack a monster and wait. I propose that Verant give us SOMETHING to do while we fight. Now this may be some rampant game redesigning to make melee different, or maybe tetris on a sidebar to keep us entertained while fighting. What I do suggest is that we have more moves in combat, maybe they wont have a real huge effect on outcomes but at least we'd have some options. I mean, what if I could do a choke hold, that does damage over time and acts as root, and uses my stamina the longer I hold it. How about being able to aim for their weak spot, like try to punch them right in the eye, gut or groin (only effective vs male npcs of course) and doing increased damage if I succeed, at the risk of doing less damage. Why not? I mean, its already boring as hell. Now sure, there may be more to melee strats, like CHOOSING which one of those monsters you want to fight first, or if one is attacking your caster switch to that one and try to get him to attack you. But it really boils down to that there arent many options for you in melee combat, unless you're very creative, and then caster classes have the exact same options plus many more. Just food for thought guys, I know I'm getting kind of bummed playing my monk, as powerful as I may be and as many HIGH numbers I get to PUNCH for

(ooh, the thought of it makes me want to go play right now!) Tell me your thoughts, thanks for reading my looong post. Later

Quaid Brown
41st level PUNCHER, Rodcet Nife

PS Before you flame me, I know that pets aren't as useful as a melee class because: 1) A pet can't think and doesn't listen to the caster always (not that a melee class does either, I'm almost as effective afk as I am at the keyboard).

4) Melees are better at taunting than pets are. Any wizard knows that they are the masters of damage in a short amount of time as Verant so recently announced and when they take advantage of this ability and nuke the mob til they're oom the friendly warrior has no problem getting that pesky mob to stop smacking the wizzy with a taunt and a kick or 2.

OK, I could go on and on and on and on and on X 10^45 and on about this but I'll stop now. I think I'll go invest another 45 days on a different class as Abashi so wisely suggests to anyone who makes a valid point. Feel free to make your arguments .
