"With Paladins getting such great heals, who needs a cleric?"

"With Necros getting such great spells, who needs a cleric?"

"With Rangers getting such great spells, who needs a cleric?"

"With Druids already having such great spells, who needs a cleric?"

Well guys, people keep asking that question over and over and over, but let me ask you, have you EVER seen anyone throw out a group's only cleric to invite, here goes, a Paladin, a Necro, a ranger or a druid?

For that matter, have you ever seen a group throw out its fully-equipped warrior so they can invite a mage and its pet? Or a necro and its pet?

I for one haven't. I love warriors, and I get them into my groups every chance I get.

My dream group for most situations would be one enchanter, one cleric, one shaman, and three fully-equipped warriors.

My second variation on this theme includes an enchanter, a cleric, a shaman (or druid), a mage, and two fully-equipped warriors.

Note that neither of my dream groups includes any hybrids. There is a reason for that, and it is a sad one. The exp penalty plays into it significantly, but sadly enough, I find that the hybrids aren't as effective as either of the purebred classes they embody, and since there is almost always a pure cleric or something in the group, the hyubrids healing powers go to waste, and they simpply can't tank as well as a warrior. So they aren't as useful to me. A pity that Verant thinks them so overpowered that they have this 40% experience penalty.

I have begun to take most of this "With class XXXX getting such great skills, who needs a class YYYY anymore?" with a HUGE grain of salt.
