I basically agree with Tudamorf here. I will add that you should never charm a creature with a known high MR (e.g., scorn banshee) ESPECIALLY if it's white or higher. That was a judgement error. If the scorn banshee was a revultant rat or similar then I wouldn't have a problem with you using charm. The other problem is that charm is severely bugged. There are probably a half dozen or so bugs with it. The plane of Hate has very bad pathing. This makes for a dangerous combination. Most enchanters I know will only use charm in Hate as a last resort. If you have nothing to lose, then why not? In your situation you had everything to lose because one spite and one banshee should be easy to handle even with a fairly small party without using charm.

As enchanters we don't have the luxury of taking these risks since we're largely responsible for the health and well being of everyone there. If we screw up, people die, and usually lots of em. Remember what you're there for.

Anyway, once your pet ran off you should have ordered everyone but a few tanks to camp. The tanks can keep the spite busy while everyone gets out, thus avoiding a major ressurection session.

Live and learn err, die and learn

One note on charmed pets: if you already have enough people kill the mob they are on , use /pet follow me and it will not run away into unknown. If you don't give any pet commands, charmed pet will wander off after a while. /pet sit down don't work, but /pet guard me or /pet follow me will. /pet back off or /pet calm down is a good way to disengage presuit and then followed by /pet follow me to bring it close to u. This at least solves the pet wander off problem with charm

As a few others have stated, it really wasn't necessary to charm the Banshee. In the time I've spent in PoX with my Enchanter, I do not think one time I've felt the need to use Allure, ever. With only two mobs being there, if I -had- charmed the extra instead of mezing it, I would have merely had it guard a spot, so it was not moving, but not actually make it attack (do this frequently with Fire Giants).

Since you are the Enchanter, and over-see crowd control, YOU should have called for everyone to camp via /ooc or /shout (whatever channel was being used). You saw the pet run out, that is the first thing you should have called done. At that point, it would have been wise for you to head the direction your charmed pet did, and request that no spells be cast on you (and tried to camp out). Sure a few tanks would have died, since they couldn't camp with the Spite around, but at least the clerics, casters, and some of the others left over could have gotten out, thus limiting your downtime.

Last thing, you may try using Entrance next time you head to a Plane. The duration is not nearly as long as Dazzle, but that shouldn't make to big of a difference. The difference between the two lies in the recast time. I may be wrong on this, but I believe that Entrance refreshes a bit faster than Dazzle, thus giving you more chances to mez a mob that may be hitting you (may be, as it depends on how wise your tanks are ). It's been a while since I've used Dazzle, so correct me if I am wrong.

Oh and I second Tudamorf, when he stated that if you have to wait 30 seconds to make sure people are assisting, you need to have a talk with them. When you are of the level to be in Planes, everyone there should have a clue as to what Enchanters can do, and they should all know about assist. You even stated that before you had the Banshee charmed, one tank was already dead, if everyone was in sync, the banshee would have been mez'd long before it had a chance to attack any of the tanks.

Ok that's it, really.
