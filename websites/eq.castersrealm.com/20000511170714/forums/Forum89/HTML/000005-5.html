As many have said before, this is a game and is for entertainment purposes. Now to me, this means that if there's something in the game, it should, eventually, and with an adequate horde of people, have the possibility of being killed.

For those of you who maintain that a god is all powerful and should only be able to be slain by another god, or with the help of one, this of this: that is going by your personal or cultural definition of what a god is/isn't.

Remember that catchy phrase "You're in our World Now"? That's exactly it. You're in their world. Maybe their definition of a god is a character of immense knowledge and power. Well, I'm a 12th level Paladin, and to me, a 50th level is a being of immense knowledge and power.

Granted, I don't think level 50, or 60 or whatever should be considered god-like, but at least demigod-like. If enough demigods decide to take out their god (or a rival one perhaps) it should at least be possible.

In the reality of EverQuest, level 50's are essentially people who have devoted their entire lives seeking knowledge to better themselves. Furthermore, they are also the few who have the immense talent necessary to learn and use this knowledge. It is feasible that after a lifetime of practicing one thing (necromancy, wizardry, ect) whoever did so is going to be fairly proficient with anything pertaining to that field of knowledge.

A real world example for you: Musashi. Revered as the sword god of Japan. Most samurai lived short lives (in approx 80% battles, both parties would die from wounds), yet he won all 60+ duels he fought in, bested numerous masters of different schools of martial arts, participated in a few wars, and lived to write A Book of Five Rings. In EQ, he would be perhaps a level 30-40 warrior.

In the end, however, one must realize that we're in their world now, and if they decide gods are simply beings such as ourselves who have grown from infancy (ie level 1) to their current selves, then so be it. We really can't expect our definition of a god to translate into a fantasy RPG where the point is to level up your character. So embrace Verant's definition and enjoy the game, or not.
