Banshee Aura

Skill : Abjuration 

Mana : 60

Speed : 5.0

Damage : 8 points of damage per attack taken for 20 rounds

Research or Purchase : Research only

Research Material :  Words of Derivation and Eradication

Effect : Adds a damage shield to the caster

Comments:

Skyshade - Deals 8 damage to an opponent each time it attacks you. I had three problems with this spell. First, its duration is very limited, lasting less than 1 minute. Second, it deals only 8 damage to your opponent, and only if it does attack you. That is not the case most of the times since you want your pet to take the hits the largest part of the battle (specially a few levels past 16). And third and most important of all, having Banshee Aura on seems to make the creature real mad at you. This means it will forget about your pet and focus its attack on you. Having a creature go AGRO on you for only 8 damage per round is not that great a deal. Some use only at lower levels, as soon as you have access to Banshee Aura. None later on. 
Lifebane - Some Necromancers swear by it and others despise it. The spell will require you to possess a pearl on you, the pearl will not be expended so can be reused for casting the spell indefinitely. Once cast, the Necromancer is surrounded by a screaming aura (caster only), or known by many as a damage shield. Anything which touches you will suffer 8 damage per touch. This can quickly add up and cause quite some damage on an opponent. And since many necromancers are still quite the melee fighter at this young level, this spell can be quite beneficial. The drawback of the spell is that your opponent tends to get more and more angry at you for harming them in this fashion. So, taunting them off of you becomes near impossible, which can be quite dangerous. Since pure spellcasters drift away from melee as they progress in power, this spell becomes less valuable. 
Maiya Samsara - This spell starts to lose its usefulness around level 24. Up to that point, use it when soloing. It's the difference between life and death in many situations. For 60 mana I was doing over 80 points of damage with it. Combined with Harmshield it's just plain amusing. (Take no damage, deal out 8 points a hit. Zone to get Harmshield back.) This is one of those spells that should be made castable on others. 8 point damage shield for 60 mana is worse than a druid's or magician's damage shields. Banshee aura never increases the amount of damage it does either.

Feign Death

Skill : Abjuration 

Mana : 60

Speed : 1.5

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Play dead to an opponent

Comments:

Skyshade - Once cast, you will look as if you were dead and you have a good chance of fooling your opponent. If you do so, the attacker will abandon battle or will concentrate his effort in killing your pet. Yes, your pet does not die once you cast Feign Death. This extremely useful spell will save your life plenty of times. Two important notes. On the bright side, Feign Death is very fast to cast so you have a very good chance to get it off. On the low side, it may not work. You might play dead but simply not fool your opponent. Against mobs below level 35, Feign Death always works. Against mobs above level 35, chances of succeeding increase with each time you cast it. 
Lifebane - Hmm.... Against MOB's lvl 35 and below, you must first successfully cast spell and feign death....if successful then these MOB's will forget you 100% of the time. Anything over level 35 will have a chance of forgetting you when you stand back up. So can be risky against higher level MOB's.  NOTE:  recent patch/nerf put a time limit or duration onto feign death.... now lasts approx 15 minutes before you quit feigning.



Heart Flutter

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 80

Speed : 2.8

Damage : 15 points of damage per round for a maximum of 10 rounds

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Lowers your target strength (17 points) and AC (47 points) as well as deals damage over time

Comments:

Skyshade - Even though some might disagree, I believe this is the most powerful DoT spell a Necromancer has at level 16. It might not deal as much damage as Heat Blood (16 damage per round for Heat Blood and 15 damage per round for Heart Flutter), but it greatly reduces the attack and defense capabilities or your target. Buy it. 
Lifebane - This is your pets favorite DOT. Cast it on an enemy and you will more then make up for the Heat Blood's greater damage with the increased effectiveness of your pet.

Shieldskin

Skill : Abjuration 

Mana : 41

Speed : 3.5

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Magical shield that absorbs about 80 points of damage (requires a Cat's Eye Agate to cast)

Comments:

Skyshade - If you cast it and do not get hit, it will stay on for quite some time, I believe as much as Shielding. The major downside is that it requires a component to be cast, cat's eye agate. It can be found in most if not all necro guilds and costs quite a bit of money for a reagent, 2.5g.  Nevertheless, it can save you if things get a little out of hand. 
Lifebane - First in the series of "skin" spells and typical for most protection spells, this is an Abjuration based skill (so keep it trained high!). Casting of this spell requires a Cats Eye Agate Gem to be expended each and every casting. It is a new form of shield spell, unlike any other the Necromancer has had up to this point. It does not give AC, not HP, nor Resist bonuses. What it does is simple and effective. It sets up a damage sponge of sorts around the caster that will absorb the first 100 points. Consider it a few extra seconds to get off that escape spell, final attack spell, or dash for the zone. There is a big hole in this defense... MAGIC! If while protected by the Shieldskin spell you have any offensive magic cast on you, the shield goes POOF instantly. So be aware of the abilities of what you plan on fighting, after all, why cast it on yourself if the NPC magician will pop it off of you within 2 seconds *grin*. The Cats Eye Agate can likely be found in any jeweler, and can Definitely be found in the Neriak Necromancer Guild.

Voice Graft

Skill : Divination 

Mana : 10

Speed : 6.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Research and purchase

Research Material : Words of Possession and Spoken

Effect : You can speak through your pet

Comments:

Skyshade - Combined with Sight Graft, they are probably the two least useful spells in the necromancer arsenal. The only thing you can do with Voice Graft is scare one of your friends. Or at least make him laugh at the waste of money you had from buying this spell. 
Lifebane - Well this is a spell that some find entertaining. The spell is in my book however, only because I found the correct words to research it! With this spell, you target your pet and then cast voice graft on it. Now all you say while targeting the pet will appear to be coming from the skeleton pets mouth.... (example... "Jabartik00 says: hi I am a dumb skeleton slave") the annoying thing is that it adds "00" at then end of their names... bah! I would not advise buying it unless you know of a Roleplaying reason for it... The most I have done with it is "possess" my skeleton and have it insult the warrior that hides behind it so that he takes less damage. Was good for a few laughs... nothing more.
