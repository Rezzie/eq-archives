Strategy

Ratraz is the Troll standing guard in The Maiden's Fancy in Neriak Third Gate.  I consider this the premier mob for levels 18-20.  He drops a Warclub every time he is killed, which with a charisma of 60, sells for 4pp 8gp in the same zone.  He will sometimes drop Words, gems, and cash over 1pp.  He gives good XP as well and has a fairly fast respawn rate, approximately 6-7 minutes. <EQN note: spawn has been raised to 10 minutes>

 

The strategy that has worked best for me is to summon the best pet you can, which at level 18 will be your level 16 pet that hits for 20, then set him on guard near Ratraz in front of the merchant in the corner of the shop.  Buff him with Spirit Armor and buff yourself up with Shielding and Spirit Armor as well since Ratraz hits very hard for his level, usually 40 points.  Shieldskin may and should be used to help you.  You can run next door to The Bauble and get some Cat's Eye Agate stones.

 

Stand near the door on the right side for the safest area to start casting.  Once you and your pet are buffed and you are full of mana, cast Dark Pact to start regenerating mana. Now this is where some people might alter their strategy.  If you cast Engulfing Darkness on Ratraz first and then have your pet attack and then drop Heart Flutter and Heat Blood, Ratraz may become aggro and come after you, chasing you around the room, trying to hit you for 40 points of damage.  Even with your Shieldskin and buffs, you cannot take many of these hits, especially at 18.  So, for the inexperienced, have pet attack first, then drop all your DoTs.  He will less likely aggro on you and you can control the fight better.  I vary sometimes with this, but only because I have killed him so many times.  He seems to resist Darkness a lot, and even Heart Flutter.  Make sure you recast these two and get them to stick.  Heart Flutter helps your pet, while Darkness slows him down a bit so you can run away if he aggros on you.  Once you have all Dots on him, you may want to fear him.  He has a set path when running away from you.  He will go out of the shop, around the fountain, and head for the library. Use Lifedraw when needed.  Don't worry about wasting mana nuking him. You will be waiting another 6 min or so for him to respawn.  It is better to nuke him as fast as you can so your pet survives.

 

He causes faction hits with Dark Bargainers, Inner Guard, and Outer Guard.  These are the merchants and guards in Neriak.  A lot of people will not attempt to kill him when I tell them this, but you can correct it.  Take red wine to the necro in the bottom floor of the library.  He is in a closed room.  Give him 4 red wine bottles at one time and you will get XP (not much at this level) and it will raise faction with the above 3 factions I mentioned.  I want to stress this clearly: DO NOT attack Ratraz unless he cons at least amiable to you.  It is harder to raise faction if you kill him and he goes to indifferent or less.  If he cons indifferent, then do the red wine quest until he cons amiable.  It usually takes 40 bottles of red wine to go from indifferent to amiable.  Since I was amiable anyway, it didn't matter.  I usually gave the necro enough to get to kindly considered, which was another 40 bottles of red wine.  If you want, you can keep giving red wine until you are warmly or fondly admired.

 

It can be a boring camp at times, but in between fights, med up, go sell the Warclub, deliver red wine, go buy spells, or help newbies find the necro guild =)  By the time you get back, he should respawn.  This is a wonderful money maker...how does 40-50 pp an hour sound to you?

 

Enjoy!

Additional Comments

Anon

I just wanted to add to this guide that when Ratraz gets low on HP, he flees the battle so don' t worry if your pet dies.  The DoTs and your pet plus your Lifetap will get him low and running.  I love this guy, great XP and huge money.

Skyshade Make sure you don't kill Ratraz too many times in a row.  Even though your faction standing might still be acceptable, the red wine quest might might not be an option if the Necro in the Library does not deal with you.  If that is the case, try turning in the wine and before you hit "give", have someone cast invis on you.  Your faction will turn to indifferent and you can complete the quest.

Zaarin

There is a bard downstairs in the same tavern that you can pull without anyone coming. Great XP to add while camping 'traz, or good for a 15-18. Her name is Tayla Wind"something" and she is 15-16.  Very very similar to Trissa except she has multiple attacks.  If you manage to get Banshee Aura at 16, you can fight her without the pet for very good XP.  Pull her with Darkness from her room.  When she gets up top you can get off one DoT.  I prefer doing Heart Flutter, and getting off a Poison Bolt during the fight (~40dmg pretty quickly.  Works great for the low HP NPC) So long as you have Banshee Aura and get lucky with some taps, she's all XP!  <No faction info yet>

Mortiis (Bertoxxulous)

Just wanted to add that Ratraz can be soloed as low as level 16.  Also, if you're fully recovered and Ratraz still hasn't returned, then you can go downstairs and kill Tayla Ironforge, or you can kill the DE Rogue, Molon something or the other (Affectionately referred to as Moron).  Killing Moron will lower you faction with the Neriak Rogues guild, but also raises your faction with various "good" groups such as the Guardian's of the Vale.  You kill Moron enough times, you'll have to stay away from all DE Rogues, but at least you'll be tolerated in Rivervale. (Don't ask me why anyone would want to. Personally, can't stand the annoying little furballs... but they do make a good appetizer if roasted properly with a nice High Elf blood sauce).

Burrning (Tallon Zek)

Just wanted to note, that if you do lose your pet to Ratraz, but still have some mana, run to the Temple of Cazic Thule. Swim through the moat and climb onto the side of the temple and watch Ratraz's pathing. He only crosses the moat midway between the zone to the, Commons and the tunnel toward the rest of 3rd Gate. By swimming across the water, you'll have plenty of time to lay down another DOT. Then swim back to the other side, rinse, and repeat. Just make sure Ratraz isn't in the water when you cast on him, or the spell won't hold....just wasted mana.
