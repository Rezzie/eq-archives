What Lifesuker is refering to is the nerf/fix of pact of shadow. It is the upgrade to shadow compact, and could be canceled before the spell completed. The trick is the way the game handles the spell: It treats it as 2 different DOTs, one on you (harmful), and one on your target (healing). When you cancel the harmful side, there is no way for the game to relay to your target that the DOT on YOUR end has ended, so you could basicly cast the spell, cancel it, and cast it again on another target, etc. etc. People were raving on how they could heal almost as well as a cleric. This of course got back to Verant by way of boards just like this. So... they fixed it. To fix this, they made the spell none cancelable by clicking on the icon. They also put a 12 second recast so that you could not chain cast it on lots of people.

So I guess what Lifesuker is saying is that if something is too good to be true and you have to ask if you should keep it quiet, you probably should. I doubt the pact of shadow bug/feature would have gone on forever, but it probably would have gone on much longer.

There was also a simmilar happening with pottery for those of you that have been around for a while (though directly with Brad, and not through a message board.)
