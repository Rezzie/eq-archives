Drain Soul, Augment Death, Venom of the Snake, Dooming Darkness, Invoke Fear, Ignite Bones

Strategy

Pretty simple but there is one trick to the whole deal.  First start out with hitting the pet with Augment death.  Then hit Styria with Dooming Darkness.  She usually resists it 1 time out of 20.

 

Now its time to use the trick to killing her off...

 

Straight up from Antinime is where her hut is, but what about all them other huts and other level 24 or so sisters?  Well, if you are facing Styria's hut looking in through the small window anticipating the kill directly behind you is a very tall tree.  While you pull her to the tree, hit her with Venom of the snake! =) It is not far a walk unless you are Styria and have darkness slowing you down. =)

 

You must pull Styria pass that tree before you fear or she will go towards the huts, causing a little more difficulty (pet usually wipes them but make sure you pull him off Styria so the weak sisters don't come charging right away).

 

Ok now comes the easy part...

 

After Styria gets past the tree you can send pet then Fear her.  Then from there just Ignite Bones and Drain Soul.  Occasionally, like 1 out of 10 times, she will resist Venom of the Snake or Invoke, but turns on the pet very easily.  Usually after she gets a few doubles of 108 on you).  When she resists VoS, she will usually keep resisting it in a row unless you first Fear her (go figure).  Then she is dead.

 

Ok for the loot.  She is usually poor, but does have a 10 pp sword (I usually just destroy them unless I am leaving shortly so I don't have to make multiple runs or be fighting encumbered).  The XP is pretty good... and she is a lot easier then Seafuries.  Her spawn is about 11 minutes and as for downtime, you will be FoM before she spawns.  Usually she is dead by the time I am at 40% mana.

 

But one warning.  Sometimes, if Styria spawns early before you get full mana, she may disappear and have to spawn over.  This happened to me a few times when I helped a friend out and was a little lower on mana then usual.

 

Well that is about it.  Have fun and level fast!

Additional Comments

Aeron Sley

I attempted this at level 42 and found it to be VERY impractical. Her resist rate to the Darkness line is too high before you hit level 44 to be anything other than a death trap. If you wish to try this before level 44 (At which point she becomes much easier) then remember to FD early if she is resisting. She hits fast for 108's, so she will plant you fast. Please note also that the level 44 cut start time was at the recommendation of a level 49 necro that was there when I arrived. I am level 42 and found her to be unsoloable.

Kierlak SoulReaver (The Rathe)

I agree that she is impractical, if not impossible, to solo before 44.  Tried her at 43 and it was a no-go.  Pet lasted about 10 seconds before she was gunning for me.

 

I also learned the hard way that if you feign to her she may remember you despite Verants claim that mobs always forget you when they return to their home spot.  She was whooping me, I feigned, she went home, I stood up, and she came roaring back.  I feigned a second time, but she saw thru it and smeared me all over the island.

 

Try her pre-44 at your own risk.

 

My advice is to remove her information from the hunting guides prior to level 44.  It will save a lot of lives and experience.
