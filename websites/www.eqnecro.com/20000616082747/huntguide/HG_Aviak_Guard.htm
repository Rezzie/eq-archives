Strategy

After creating a pet in the alcove of the arena entrance, cast Darkness to draw one Aviak Guard.  Then follow with pet attack, Boil Blood, Heart Flutter (if you have it), and Fear. After Fear wears off repeat and rinse.  Don't let the Aviak run too far down the hill towards his dock or you will have a few of the minor Aviaks that will help it.  No big fight in them, but for some reason the big ones don't give faction hits but the little weaker ones do.  Pretty soon you will be KOS with Aviaks and that's not a good thing if you want to hunt the birdhouse in the future levels.


After 24th level you can hunt here easily without a pet for some pretty good exp also.

 

Also one thing to note is that you will be fighting RIGHT next to the zone border, always a good thing when you need to retreat.

Nergal

Here are the 5 spawn points in Lake Rathetear for Aviak guards and what they are commonly called:

0,-1000 1-spawn, solo-spawn, or "the diving board"

0,2500 2-spawn cove or "the cove"

1000,1000 2-spawn island or "1000 island"

2300,1100 3-spawn, "bird island", or "the tower"

?,? arena zone

 

At both arena spawns and (obviously) the diving board you can pull the birds singly. At the other 3 spawns you'll pull them all. At level 20 (with the new pet) you can break a 2-spawn solo. Here's how. Note: this is easiest done at 1000 island. Summon a pet until you get the highest /con...you'll need it. Park the pet as far away from the spawn as possible, run up and Darkness a bird. Run back and sick the pet on the bird. Load it up with DoTs, as many as you can, then try to get in a few lifetaps (you may want to use Lifedraw for the faster casting time). At this point you should have one bird dead or nearly dead, and you're probably hurting. Time to feign. If possible it is preferable to wait until the first bird dies, but if not your pet still should be able to finish it off. The second bird will pummel your pet, then wander back to its roost. The spawn is now broken but you aren't out of the woods yet. These birds re-spawn fairly quick, so summon a new pet (don't worry about it's /con), use Allure to med back up to about 60%. Pull the second bird, send the pet and let them go at it a bit to make sure the bird sticks on the pet, then lifetap the heck out of it to get your HPs back. Now the spawn is completely broken. You can med up to 80-100% with Allure and pull the birds as they spawn. Note: contrary to what you might think, you cannot split the spawn by fearing one of the birds away. Aviaks seem to be almost completely immune to fear. I've had green birds resist fear 5 times in a row, and when it does stick it rarely last more than a few seconds. For necros over level 20 pulling single birds I suggest pulling with Heart Flutter, sending the pet, then laying on Shadow Vortex. With just these two spells you can debuff the bird enough that a good pet can solo all but the toughest birds, and you don't build up enough hate to make the bird aggro on you. That means you can sit and med while boney does the dirty work, making bird hunting very mana efficient. You may want to throw on an extra DoT or toss some lifetaps to help finish the bird off faster.

 

Birds drop up to 2pp in cash and occasionally words and bronze weapons.  Happy hunting.

Buldar (Bristlebane)

This place so far has been AMAZING exp.  Since I started here at level 17 with about 2.5 more bubbles to go until I hit 18. I go over there and the first thing I con is yellow, I think well, this might be a challenge, but eh, there is a zone near, it shouldn't be a problem.  So I go to the right tower, target it and run back into the cove a ways.  Then cast Darkness, which although I hear is resisted a lot, it is successfully cast for me about 75% of the time.  After Darkness, cast the Leach, send in pet, and then Heat Blood.  By now its already down to half life and pet is just starting to go after it.  As soon as pet gets on him, I Fear and he looks real scared and starts to slowly walk away.  Pet beats on him some more, and usually I don't even bother with Lifedraw or anything.  By now I'm at about 50% mana or so, and Aviak dies. And oh yeah, remember this was a yellow con, pet full health, me full health and med for 2 minutes, go after the other tower.

 

It is great, easy XP, and even thought there is a benefit of a near zone, so far I haven't needed it.  I started there, as I said at 17, put about 8 hours on in, I'm now about a bubble away from 20.  Add onto that great XP some decent loot, and got a GREAT camping area.  Hope this helps, good luck to all my evil friends out there>:)

Vargis (Tarew Marr)

Simply pull with Heat Blood, then upon its decent cast Heart Flutter and Leach. Quickly send your pet to intercept and step way back. Getting hit by these things is no thrill, so you will want to keep quite some distance between you and the mob. After the DoTs sink in a little the Aviak will begin to try to catch you but your pet will continue to distract it. In some rare instances I have had to use Feign Death to allow the DoTs to finish it off.
