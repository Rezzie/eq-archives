Ok, so you want start a necromancer character and is looking for a few tips.  Well, hopefully you can find answers to some questions you might have in this guide.  You can also check the FAQ section for issues not mentioned here.  Comments, suggestions and contributions are greatly appreciated.  Just click on the submit link in the main menu.

The first thing you should do when starting a new character is answer the following question: Do I want to be a PvP player or not? The reason I say this is some classes are a better fit to PvP than others.  But a Necromancer can be a real powerful opponent. You simply have to play it right.  Only use DoTs that deal fast damage, try to have items that raise your magical resistance, have a fast weapon when dueling other casters so you can try and interrupt them, cast Drain Life spells all the time, and so on. Dueling is a lot different than fighting a mob and requires preparation.  But hey, Necromancers were never an easy class to play and I am pretty sure you will learn how to beat each class.  We are Necromancers and fear no death. 

In any case, the Necromancer is an excellent class. One of the best in the game, you will be able to solo a number of creatures that other classes have difficulty dealing with by themselves. Our ability to have a pet, deal DoT damage, and deal like no other class with undead creatures also make us necromancers a party member anyone should want in their team. Necromancers are also the only class in the game where you can fight a creature of your level and walk out of battle with full health.

So, without any further delay, lets get into some of the specifics in order to create a necromancer character.  Walk in darkness my friend.

Which one should you choose?  No one but yourself can answer that question.  It is a matter of personal preference and the difficulty level you are looking for.  Humans are the base class in Norrath and should make a little bit easier to play a necromancer.  On the other hand, Dark Elves are the evil class of Norrath and a Dark Elf necromancer will be most definitely hated throughout Norrath.  Besides that, each class has its own peculiarity.  Dark Elves are very agile and intelligent; Erudites are very intelligent and have a better charisma then all other classes but humans; Gnomes are also very agile and intelligent, as well as pretty dexterous; finally, Humans, are the base class and even though are not bad in anything, are also not good in anything.  Thus, choose wisely and take into account not only the advantages each class possess, but also its disadvantages.


 
Necromancer's Bonus Point Distribution

The next step in a character formation  is to distribute those 30 bonus points you receive.  Once again, this will be dictated by your own preferences.  I can give only give you one advice.  What will be doing 15 levels later? Will you be relying on other people to sell things for you?  Will you be a master jeweler?  Will you be a better than average melee fighter?  Or will you be a pure spellcaster that avoids melee combat at all costs?  These are some questions you should try and answer in order to better split your bonus points.  Even though you should know this, below is a description of each attribute.

Strength: Raises your attack power and dictates how much damage you will inflict when in melee combat

Stamina: Raises your maximum Hit Points (HP)

Agility: Dictates how often you will get hit in battle

Dexterity: Dictates how often you will hit your opponent when in melee combat

Wisdom:  Dictates how successful you are when using a trade skill such as jewelry or spell research

Intelligence:  Raises your maximum mana points and dictates how fast you will learn new skills or improve old ones

Charisma:  Dictates how will people treat you and how much they will pay for things you want to sell or charge for things you want to buy

My recommendation is to put some points in intelligence (the most important one as you want as much mana as possible and learn new skills faster), stamina (a few to raise your max HP), wisdom (if you are going to take on a trade skill), and charisma (make yourself a little more friendly looking and get better prices with merchants).  But choose as you might and be aware that these attributes will remain constant for the rest of your life (with the exception of magical pieces that can modify them).


 
 Necromancer's Deity Selection

Not much to say here.  With the exception of Humans, other races have no choice in what deity they will follow.  Dark Elves must follow The Prince of Hate, Erudites must follow The Faceless, and Gnomes must follow The Plaguebringer.  Humans can choose either The Prince of Hate, The Faceless, or the Plaguebringer.  This choice will affect how Dark Elves and Gnomes necromancers will view you.  If you choose the deity a specific race follows, they will accept you more as a friend.
 
  

Necromancer's City Selection

Once again not much to say.  As a necromancer, you will never get the chance to choose the city you start in.  Dark Elves start in Neriak, Erudites in Erudin, Gnomes in Ak'Anon, and Humans in either Freeport or Qeynos.  Thus, if you have a specific preference for a city, take that into consideration when choosing the race you will be playing.
 
 

I have create my character, now what?

Ok, so there you are, pretty much naked and all by yourself in a city you might not be familiar with.  What should you do?  Well, the first thing you should is memorize the two spells you have with you.  Heheh, I guess you knew that, so now what?  Well, look out for the necromancers' guild in your town.  Remember, you are a necromancer so your guild should be pretty much well hidden or even outside of town.  Ask around and you should be able to find it.  It is there that you should hand over your letter (read it first to see the name of the NPC you should give it to) and receive your first XP and a robe.  You are now ready for your first battle.  Well, not really.  A necromancer is pretty much defenseless without a pet so check out your guild and see how much the spell Cavorting Bones costs.  Of course you won't have money to buy it but there is something you can do and that I always do if I see a young necromancer around.  Check who is in your zone at that time and see if any higher level necromancer is around.  If so, ask him if he could be so kind as to buy you that Cavorting Bones spell.  Who knows, you might get lucky (after all, 1G is nothing even for a level 8 necromancer).  If you get the spell, great.  If not, then start saving those silver coins until you get enough to purchase the spell.  It should be the first thing you spend your money at.

So now you are ready for your first battle.  If you not only new to the necromancer class but also to the game, you should learn the consider command.  Always consider a creature before attacking.  Just target that creature and hit C.  The message it gives you will pretty much dictate the outcome of the battle.  You will soon learn what all con replies are.  At first, the easiest creature should read that it would be an even fight.  Those are the ones you are looking for.  But before engaging in combat, make sure you know your way around and how close you are to a safe spot.  Try fighting at first in places close to a zone or local guards.  Those are two good resources for times when things might get complicated.  Once you are familiarized, attack and hopefully get your first kill.  Start saving those loots and once you are full, head back to the necro guild and sell it.

One important thing about the first battles is realizing that you will die, specially if you don't have a pet.  There is something you can do to make the recovery of your corpse easier if you do not own the locate corpse spell yet.  If you realize death is a possibility you might not be able to avoid, type /location or /loc or /l before dying.  It will give the coordinates of your corpse in case you die and make it easier for you to get back to it.  But do not be too afraid of dying as it will happen.  Often.  And remember you do not loose XP if you die until you reach level 5, so just go there and fight.
 
 
 Any other tips?

I will be adding more and more tips for newly created necromancers as I get contributions, but here are some to start with:

   You can move your corpse to a different and safer location by typing /corpse

  Partying with people will make you get less XP, but it will also make it safer for you, so try partying every once in a while to take on tougher creatures

  Do not party though with anyone 3 levels above or below yours.  A three level difference is the maximum that will provide you XP.

  Get to know the place you are.  Go to the zones nearby whenever you can.  Travel and adventure as much as you can.  You will never know if the place you currently are is good or not if you don't know any other place you can compare it with.  Ok, so that kobold cons green but still gives you XP, but it is so little it is not worth the time and effort you are putting into it.

  Don't be afraid to ask questions.  Most people are happy to pass on the knowledge they acquired previously throughout their lives in Norrath.

  Clouds in Norrath always move from east to west.  So if you are lost and that damn sense heading skill constantly says that you have no idea which direction you are facing, try looking up the sky.

  But do use Sense Heading as often as you can.  At least until it maxes out (max goes up by 5 each new level) so when you reach a higher level your skill will be in good shape

  Do not use practice points in skills you can learn during battles or in the field.  Save those points for skills that are either expensive or difficult to learn.  Initially, I would only spend practice points in channeling since that will make your spells fizzle a lot less.  Do not spend more than 10 points though.  Once you reach level 4, spend some in meditate as it will speed up your mana recovery process.  All other skills will be learned in the field.  Note, the first spell that requires the evocation skill is Ward Undead at level 8, so you might want to spend a few on this skill also in order to speed up the learning process.

  You can do your own /loc databank.  It can make your traveling a lot easier.  Whenever you are in a location you know you will come back, type /loc and write down those coordinate.  Or you can use the Location Database from Eqnecro

  You can customize any command you want and put it into one of the 6 hot buttons accessed by the numbers 1 through 6.  To do that, go to the socials menu and right click on a button, for example Hail.  That button is useless right now and you can change it for a more useful command.  Once you right click a button, the customization screen should appear.  Choose a name for that button and type the command you wish to store (e.g. /pet attack).  You now have your customized button.  Just left click and hold and put it in one of the hot button boxes to the left.

  The commands to control a pet are:  /pet attack, /pet follow me, /pet guard me, /pet guard here, /pet as you were, /pet sit down, and /pet stand up, pet health report, /pet back off, /pet who leader, /pet target, /pet taunt, /pet no taunt, /pet get lost

  Get to know people throughout your adventures as chances are you will be meeting them over and over again.  As a necro, the more friends you have the easier things get for you.  You will find yourself in situations where help might save your life or satiate your hunger.

  On a similar note, help people as much you as you can.  Help young necromancers as you were in that situation before and if you are like me, you know how hard it can be.  I have donated countless platinum pieces to many people that I might never see again.  But sometimes you do see them and they will remember what you did for them in the past.

 If you want to become a PvP player, all you have to do is give the note you start with to the local Priest of Discord.  He will make you a PvP.  Be prepared for the consequences though as you might loose that 1 prized possession (if you loose in a battle, the one that defeated you is entitled to loot 1 item from your corpse).

  Explore, adventure, meet people, try new things, and have fun. That is the one tip you should really take into consideration.
