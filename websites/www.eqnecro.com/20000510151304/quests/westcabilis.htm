Keeper Bile

Julak - You say hail Keeper Bile.

 

Keeper Bile bows before you as a gesture of kindness and respect. Greetings. I am Bile, Keeper of the Apprentice ranks. My knowledge is that of the first circles of necromany. I was once a great necromancer, but now I have a greater calling to protect the words of Kotiz. I also conduct many tests to insure the spells are deciphered correctly. I am in need of an apprentice to [collect components].

 

You say, ‘I will collect components’

 

Keeper Bile takes out a tatterd scroll and looks upon it’s words. Hmm. Yes. I will need the following; One Large Scorpion Pincer, One Sabertooth Cub Canine and Two Brittle Skulls. Bring these to me and I shall pay you and if I also have an overscribed spell, I shall give you that also.

 

Keeper Bile is found near the Arena area in West Cabilis. He sells the level 1 necro spells.

 

The Large Scorpion Pincer can be obtained as a pretty common drop from Large Scorpions in the Field of Bone. They are No Drop and the Large Scorpions are level 4-5. The Sabertooth Cub Canine appears to be a common drop of the Sabertooth Cubs in the Lake of Ill Omen (they are around level 3-4). And lastly, the Brittle Skulls are in fact the Brittle Iksar Skulls dropped by Skeletons of varrying variety found in many zones. The easiest place to aquire these skulls is in the Lake of Ill Omen off Decaying Skeletons, who appear to be pretty easy to find there.

 

The reward for the quest is some silver (I got 12) and the Coldlight spell, which is good for practicing conjuration, but other than that, it’s worthless. It can however be sold for some extra cash =)

 

Outcome: Some silver and a level 1 spell

 

 

Master Xydoz

Julak - Master Xydoz says ‘What is it that you seek within the tower? Could it be that you are a new apprentice? If so you are required to don the [Apprentice Skullcap].

 

You say, ‘what Apprentice Skullcap?’

 

Master Xydoz says ‘All new members of the Brood of Kotiz are required to don the [Apprentice Skullcap]. To earn one, a new apprentice is required to fetch four brains for further experiments. Not just any four brains, but the brains of [Sarnak] hatchlings. Often, they have under developed brains which are useless to us.

 

You say, ‘what Sarnak?’

 

Master Xydoz says ‘Sarnak?!! Do not speak loudly, that name. If you seek information on the Sarnak then read the tome of this tower. The tower librarian should be found within.

 

Master Xydoz can be found in the Necro Guild Tower where the guildmaster is located. He’s on the second to the highest ledge in front of a rack of brains in jars.

 

This is a pretty simple quest. You must find Sarnak Hatchlings in the Lake of Ill Omen. They are around level 3-4 and drop brains semi-often... I believe they are No Drop. If you get any Tapestries, save them. You’ll need one Ripped and one Torn Tapestry for the second rank skullcap, so it’ll save you the trouble if you just start saving them now.

 

Outcome: First Rank Skullcap, AC 2

 

Second Rank Skullcap Quest

 

You say, ‘What Second Rank Skullcap?’

 

Master Xydoz says ‘Looking for the Second Rank Skullcap? Look no further, but you shall earn it. I seek a [Faded Tapestry]. Now too, so do you.

 

You say, ‘What Faded Tapestry?’

 

Master Xydoz says ‘I have heard reports of such thing found upon sarnak hatchlings. They must have scampered from the safety of their dwelling with their masters property. I would like to see this tapestry, but only when it has been mended. I need to find a necromancer who is [adept at tailoring].

 

You say, ‘I am adept at tailoring.’

 

Master Xydoz says ‘If you are a member of the Brood and wish to assist, you may seek out this tapestry. Find the Torn and Ripped pieces and take them both to a sewing kit. Return with the Mended Tapestry and your First Rank Skullcap then I shall see that you are rewarded with coin and a Second Rank Skullcap.

 

After completing the First Rank Skullcap quest, Master Xydoz will mention the Second Rank Skullcap. If you took my advise above, you saved one Ripped and one Torn Tapestry from the Sarnak Hatchlings in the Lake of Ill Omen (they are around level 3-4 for anyone that might be reading this but hasn’t read the one about the first rank skullcap). Placing these two objects into a tailoring kit and combining them will yield a Mended Tapestry (NO DROP, so you, unfortunally, must put some practice points into tailoring... shouldn’t take much). Turn the Mended Tapestry and First Rank Skullcap into Master Xydoz for the Second Rank Skullcap!

Outcome: Second Rank Skullcap, AC 3

 

Master Rixiz

Julak - Master Rixiz says ‘You are on the grounds of the Brood of Kotiz. If you do not belong then leave at once. There shall be no [Third Rank Skullcap] for you.

 

You say, ‘what Third Rank Skullcap?’

 

Master Rixiz says ‘I offer the Third Rank Aprrentice Skullcap to those who wear the Second. If that is you then you will do the [bidding of the tower].

 

You say, ‘I will do the bidding of the tower’

 

Master Rixiz says ‘Take this Glass Canopic. Within it you shall place a brain for me. The brain I seek is of a Sarnak Crypt Raider. Any shall do. The ones we seek should be near the Lake of Ill Omen. When you obtain the brain you must quickly put it into the Glass Canopic with [Embalming Fluid]. When these are combined the canopic shall seal and if you return it to me with your Second Rank Skullcap then I shall hand you the next and final skullcap.

 

You say, ‘what Embalming Fluid?’

 

Master Rixiz says ‘Embalming Fluid is created through brewing, but do not drink it!! You can find the process of brewing on our grounds.

 

Master Rixiz is again found in the Necro Guild Tower, on the second floor, again, in front of a rack of brains in jars. Apparently you must do the first 2 skullcap quests before he will even respond to the "I will do the bidding of the tower" phrase. This sounds at lot easier than it really is, unfortunally. The Sarnak Crypt Raiders can be found in the Lake of Ill Omen, I assume. I’ve heard they are around level 6-8.

 

Embalming Fluid, as Master Rixiz mentions, is brewed, thus forcing you to take up brewing as well. I assume a low skill, like 5, will do. He also mentions that the process of brewing can be found on their grounds. Apon exitting the guild tower, turn left and cross the wooden plank to the other side. Go in the door immediately on the other side and go up the stairs. There you will find the Mortician. He sells the recipe for Embalming Fluid for free. The ingrediants are: Rubbing Alcohol, 2 Bog Juice, and Bone Granite Powder. The Mortician sells the Rubbing Alcohol, and the Bog Juice can be brewed with a really low skill (Snake Scales, Water, and a Bottle. The Snake Scales can be found in the Swamp of No Hope from one of the venders near the city zone line. Make sure to buy water, not foraged, summoned, or that special type of water, I believe it’s called Broodwater or something like that, which is sold commonly in Cabilis). The Bone Granite Powder presents quite a problem however. Try asking the Mortician about it:

 

You say, ‘Where can I find Bone Granite Powder?’

 

a mortician says ‘It is as it sounds. Two of one and a pile of the others.

 

Hammered in heat until they are one.

 

Now, this leads me to believe that this stuff can be smithed, which most likely requires us necros to take up blacksmithing as a trade (although, I’m sure a low skill will do) as it is probably no drop. The bones are most likely Bone Chips (you’ll see why when I explain possibility number 2 below) and the Granite can be quested for in the form of a Pile of Granite Peebles. So, I assume this means that you will need two bone chips and one pile of granite peebles because the mortician mentions a "pile". In East Cabilis, half way between the bank and the shaman guild (that big castle looking thing in front of the zone into the Field of Bone), there’s a small building. Just inside the door is a lizardman named The Toilmaster who mentions slaves crushing granite. Apparently monks can get him to talk a little more if they ask, "What about the granite peebles?" He says he will give you a pile of granite peebles for every two Broken Shackles you bring him. These can be found off of Escaped Frogloks in the Swamp of No Hope (around level 5, and are uncommon spawns). Aquire a pile of granite peebles and two bone chips and combine them in a forge (there’s on the East Cabilis, as well as a brewing barrel) for the Bone Granite Powder.

 

A second possibility (assuming Bone Granite Powder is not no drop) would be to get a 20+ shaman to make the powder for you via Alchemy. Two bone chips and 3 piles of granite peebles I believe it was. However, this seems unlikely since the mortician speaks of heat and a hammer. That sounds more like a forge to me and I’m willing to bet the Bone Granite Powder is No Drop.

 

However, assuming you can obtain the Bone Granite Powder, combine that, 2 Bog Juice, and Rubbing Alcohol in a brewing barrel for the Embalming Fluid. The Embalming Fluid and the brain from the Sarnak Crypt Raider into the jar Master Rixiz gave you, and the combined jar and your second rank skullcap to Master Rixiz for the third rank skullcap, which is most likely an AC of 4. This allows you to continue on to a (theoretically) must better skullcap which the guy in front of the pool of blood on the bottom floor of the necro guild tower speaks of.

Julak - I've been trying to do the 3rd apprentice skullcap quest in West Cabilis and have found out a few more things that you might find useful...

 

First, based on your suggestion, I tried 2 bone chips + pile of granite in a forge Result - "those items do not combine in those quantities to produce anything"

I also tried 2 bone chips + pile of granite + 1 flask of water since a lot of blacksmithing requires water.  Same result.

 

After chatting with some additional NPC's in the city I did find out another useful tidbit.

 

There is a merchant named Jondin in a hut in West Cabilis who offers a quest for "bone granite powder"  However, when you ask him about it, he gives a DIFFERENT formula than the one from the mortician.  He says that it's made using alchemy and you need 3 bone chips + the pile of granite.  I'm not sure if this powder is the same as what you need for the embalming fluid and unfortunately I don't know anyone with alchemy, so I haven't been able to try it out. 

 

Also, getting the pile of granite is a real pain... You have to kill uncommon/rare spawn escaped frogloks in the Swamp of No Hope and it can take awhile to find them.  Give 2 of the broken shackles they drop to the Toilmaster in East Cabilis and you get the pile of granite.

 

I haven't found any of the NO DROP skeleton bones yet, or I would have tried 2 of those plus the granite in the forge.

 

Sethra - I tried the following to get the Bone Granite Powder and it worked: 2 bone chips, 1 pile of granite pebbles, 1 forging hammer (bought from a weaponsmith in the Warrior's Guild).
