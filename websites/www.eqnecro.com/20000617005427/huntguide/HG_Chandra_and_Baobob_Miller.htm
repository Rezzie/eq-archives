Strategy

Warning: This requires SoW or Jboots.  If the spawn hasn't been broken up, this can be a bit tricky, and will require very efficient and careful use of your pet.

 

To start, Darkness both Chanda and Baobob.  Cast all DoTs on whoever you cast Darkness last on.  Then, re-Darkness both and DoT the other one all the way.  The first one should be about 2/3 health by this time.  Sic your pet on this one, and run around with them both chasing after you... don't run too far away or else they will quickly destroy your pet.  Re-apply your DoTs and pet as necessary to finish them off, and now the spawn should be broken up sufficiently so that you can grab one, kite them, med up and then kite the other, etc.  If you have two people, well obviously just have each of you take one.

 

Not bad resist, although Chanda seemed to resist Darkness more often than Baobob... And whatever you do, DO NOT let Chanda get close to your behind.. she backstabs for 100+.  Also, don't Fear in this zone... it's really buggy, they can end up teleporting all over.

 

They drop average 1-2pp and a bronze weapon you can sell to... umm, I dunno, I am KoS everywhere.  But they sell for a couple pp.  Ok, about the danger of this zone, which is why I gave it a 4 safety rating: You have to watch out for Paladins, guards, Cros Treewind, Holly Windstalker, and the occasional dread corpse and mangy rat =)

Additional Comments

Sayleth

This spot is probably doable in some form or another from 24 on up. However, at that level, I was, and you should be, in an EK Druid group making lots of plat and superb xp. The druids there are only good through 31 though, and the XP can slow down before that depending upon the size and makeup of your group.  So, with that in mind, my comments will be directed at the 29+ necro who goes to Qhills searching for easy xp and decent loot.

 

Preface

Depending upon your server, the Millers may or may not be camped. On Tribunal, it isn't too hard to get a spot there, but it is pretty hard to get them both to yourself. YMMV. Your biggest competition for them will be...Druids. No surprise there. They really arent worth it, IMO, if you can only get one. Your time is better spent killing one of the many 30-31 NPCs around Antonica.

 

The Pull

Get your top, or close to it, 29th level pet. Depending upon how long the Millers have been sitting there, they might have got up from the fire and gone to wander about. Chanda usually wanders to the nearby ruins, with Bob usually taking up watch on one of the two hills south of the Surefalls entrance. If they are spread out, great, as this will allow you to separate the spawn easily. If they are both at the camp, Leatherskin yourself, intensify Jobab, and get to FM. Cast screaming terror on Bob. It will most likely stick. If it does, don't cast or send pet on Chanda (she will aggro on you with the terror cast) until she is out of Bobs aggro range so you can allow the memblur component of terror to split the spawn for you. Once you have her where you want her, send pet and cast VC. Wait a couple rounds, then cast SoP or your top tap if you need some health. If you have a good pet, and you waited long enough, this won't aggro her on you. Then immediately after the first DD cast, follow up with one more. If you got close to 300 points of damage on them with your 2 DDs, stop casting here. This will aggro the Miller on you. Tank for your pet and Chanda will be dead inside a couple of rounds. She can really do damage quick (she dual wields, and can hit for full with her offhand. Neat trick), so feel free to cast another tap if necessary. However, what you are trying to do is get in a position where you can pull as they spawn. Wait a few minutes, renew bones on pet as needed, and repeat process with Bob. I also cast an intensify on my pet before one of the pulls, and if the spawn is timed right, it will last thru both fights. Now, going back up to the top, what happens if Bob resists your terror? Well, no big deal really. Simply send pet on Chanda, and terror him again until it sticks. Once it does, nuke Chanda to death, Bob will be waking up just in time to hear his sister's dying screams. Now, take Bob a little slower, so you can space out the spawn. I suggest a VC and a BB with heals for pet. Once spawn is broken, go back to the topics above, 1 VC and ~300points DD damage per Miller, pet do the rest. If you do it right, you can be killing a Miller every 4mins, with no need for allure of death, or ever being below half mana.

 

XP

This can be a bubble an hour at 33. So I would have to give the XP portion a 9.

 

Loot

The Millers drop up to 3pp and change. Not too shabby. The also drop level 24 research components which you should hang on to and distribute to your younger brothers, free of charge. Additionally, Chanda drops a rusty dagger and   bronze short sword every time, and Bob drops a bronze scimitar. Surprisingly, the short sword doesn't even yield a plat. The scimmy will get you 2+ though. Problem is, where to sell? Your best bet is tempting one of the newbie loot whores who will inevitably surround you. Get them to sell them for you for a cut of the action. I personally, like to hang on to the scimmys to give to Jobab. He looks cool dual wielding them. I give the loot a 7.

 

Safety

Even though Cros and Holly, and a couple of wandering guards are in the zone, you have nothing to fear from them, unless someone trains you. You do however, need to keep an eye out for a bastard paladin named nicclaus ressnin, or something close to that. He is mid to high 30s, and hits for 80+. Your only warning will be the standard NPC battlecry before he starts wailing on you. His path takes him thru the ruins to the gates of Surefalls. So he will sneak up on you if you are camped to the east of the fire, as you should be. Still, you probably wont see him more than once in a 4-6 hour period, so it isnt a big deal. If he gets on you, send pet. Step back and gather shadows, this will get you past the guards, and zone into surefalls. Safety is a 9. No way in hell you should die here.

 

Miscellaneous comments

A ton of newbies will bug the hell out of you. From outright begging to those trying to be genuinely helpful. Try to be patient. Also, Chanda and Bob hand out bear and wolf hide armor. If a PC asks you to let them do the quest, you should let them. Tell them to be quick, and your spawn will most likely remain broken up. Keep your ears pricked for ANY mention of Pyzjin. If you hear her name, chances are she is at the ruins a short distance to the East of you. Immediately bolt towards the ruins, unless you are fighting Chanda, as she will backstab you for 140+. A single SoP will take Pyzjin down. I killed her once while there from 31-33, but no GBS. Chances are good that some bored high level will be sitting near the ruins camping her anyway.

 

Good hunting!
