Only 1 chance to save.

A spell may have multiple effects, but the spell itself only has one Resistance check save.

The thing is, that save check could have some modifiers.

Ignite Bones is Heat based, so your Heat Resistance (or the mob's Heat Resistance) is checked. The effect of the illusion or the stun or whatever has no bearing on the spell. It's simply more complicated from a programming viewpoint.

What I can understand is, the programmers put a +25 to the save. If they think a spell should be resisted because it has multiple effects, then they do that kind of modifier. But it is still one save.

But, have the mob debuffed with your Scent line and some other spell that lowers Heat resistance and your Ignite Bones will most likely land.

Also, mob level relative to you affects the modifier.

I don't know the exact details, but I can make a pretty good guess that all mobs 6+ levels higher than you get an +500 to save against all resistance checks when you cast on them. Lifetaps get a -300 check modifier. So even if the mob is only 5 levels (but not yet 6) the drain is practically unresistable. (GZ mentioned a -50 save, but my numbers seem to make more sense and be easier on the programmer, hehe; could have been a +5000 and -3000, but it doesn't matter, as long as it is large enough to not be affected by anything else as far as drains are concerned.)

I'd also guess that Harmshield gives you something like +32000 AC and +32000 skin, but Cazic Thule's Death Touch gets a -32000 magic resistance save modifier for 10000 damage. (Of course, the text message would be different, but whatever.)
