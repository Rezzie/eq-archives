Strategy

The Gnoll camp by the South Karana zone is a walk in the Gnoll-infested park by day, but when the sun comes down the Undead come out.  Here you'll find Dry Bones Skeletons (18-24), Greater Zombies (18-24), Exhumed Gnolls (18-22) and Skeletons (14-18).  They tend to spawn quickly, as they are only out at night. The spot, even when heavily camped, will get you a couple of tougher Undead if you're vigilant.

 

I have found that using DoTs on these things is close to useless.  They resist about 33% of the time, and the last thing you want is a Greater Zombie double-attacking you for 46's while you try to re-cast Heat Blood or Heart Flutter.

 

Until 24th level, it is best to tackle this place with at least a partner, preferably one who can taunt effectively.  Simply pull the Mob with Expulse Undead or Siphon Life and send your pet and partner in at the same time.  Sit back and pound it with one Expulse after another until it drops.  The Mobs here are VERY aggressive, and the chance of starting a Dry Bone or Greater Zombie train is very high if you're not careful.  Numb and Rest the Dead are gambles at best, as every Undead in range will try to help out his friends.  It is almost impossible to kill a DBS or GZ without getting one or two skeletons to boot.  If your pet drops when you're out there alone, even that DBS with 33% HP left can kill you before you do enough damage to send him back to his final rest.

 

The damage they deal is fast and hard, and will disrupt your spells easily.  The added benefit of hunting here is the South Karana zone is right around the corner, and makes for an easy escape if need be.  The treasure is pretty good off the DBS's and Exhumed Gnolls.  Low-value gemstones, fine steel weapons and bone chips are the typical drops, as well as 3-10gp in coin.  If you see a DBS with a weapon in hand, odds are it's Fine Steel.  Skeletons and GZs have rusty weapons typically, along with the occasional gem.
