Allure of Death

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 5

Speed : 3.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Exchanges HP for mana at a rate of 8 HP points for 8 mana

Comments:

Skyshade - It can be useful if you have a healing effect on you, speeding your healing process. If that is the case, you can have a lot more mana in combat. You can also cast Allure of Death and constantly drain life from your opponent with Siphon Life to replenish the lost HPs. Good spell to reduce downtime. 
Lifebane - This is an upgrade to the Dark Pact spell. It costs a meager 5 mana to cast and is another one of our many Alteration magic based spells. This spell drains 8 life from you every round and exchanges it for 8 mana. Now what good does damaging oneself for mana have? A bit suicidal perhaps you think? Well we Are Necromancers! As your health withers away and your mana returns, start using your Lifetap line of spells and heal up. There are 2 particularly nasty combinations with this spell beyond the lifetap casting. 1) Group with a Bard, their heal song should offset your damage taken so that you in effect have a super mana regeneration rate so long as bard plays and you have allure up. 2) Get Regeneration cast on you by a Shaman.... same effects as #1 above. And to confirm this, Allure of Death does NOT turn you into skeletal form, though once rumored to do so.

Dominate Undead

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 100

Speed : 5.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Charms an undead creature

Comments:

Skyshade - Not a bad spell, but with limited use. To cast it, you can't have a pet, which is quite dangerous, specially after the charm effect is gone. But it can be used when hunting in undead infested areas such as Unrest. In that case, don't bother getting a pet and simply charm an undead if your party is fighting against 2 or more of them. 
Lifebane - This level 20 spell is the first of our Charm Undead spells. It has a success rate based on the difficulty of he target. Charming a decaying skeleton is trivial, charming an Ice Skeleton is considerably tougher. Duration of the Charm's effect varies, BUT..... the higher the level of the undead... the quicker it will break control. The largest problem with using this Domination is that once the effect wears off, well... suffice it to say that the Undead that was recently under your power will not be too happy with you after that little domination trick. You had better be ready for it when it turns on you, for it WILL turn. And for the showoffs out there... run around Rathe with a Giant Skeleton as your pet... too funny.



Expulse Undead

Skill : Evocation 

Mana : 60

Speed : 2.8

Damage : Maxes at 95 points of damage

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Deals direct damage to an undead creature

Comments:

Skyshade - The upgrade to the level 8 spell Ward Undead, Expulse Undead is useful but not as good as its predecessor. Assuming that your Evocation skill is maxed by level 20, Expulse Undead will rarely fizzle. But its usefulness is somewhat reduced when compared to Ward Undead as the amount of damage it deals is not that high.  You could de dealing around 70 points of damage with Siphon Life and gain life in return. And the difference in mana cost is very small. Nevertheless, it helps kill an undead creature faster.
Lifebane - Not much better then lifetap at this level, but still saves mana and does a bit more damage. Get it and use it! 



Harmshield

Skill : Abjuration 

Mana : 85

Speed : 1.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Research only

Research Material : Words of Refuge and Absorption

Effect : Makes you invulnerable for a few seconds (around 10 seconds)

Comments:

Skyshade - Awesome spell that will save your life more times than you can count. Once invulnerable, no spells or attacks will damage you, but you also can't attack or cast. In addition, a mob can still stun you. Once cast, it will take Harmshield about 7 minutes to load again. You can speed that up by zoning.
Lifebane - What can I say... awesome spell! You will end up saving you life countless times using this spell. A few seconds of time to run to zone or to hope your DOT finishes off the creature or to let your group members kill it off without you getting dead.

Damage :

Shadow Vortex

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 40

Speed : 1.5

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Research and purchase

Research Material : Words of Dissolution (I) and Imitation

Effect : Reduces the AC of a target by 17 points and adds it to yours

Comments:

Skyshade - When I tested this spell, there was not even one single battle where it seemed to make a difference. 
Lifebane - Have only used this spell once or twice myself. It does not seem very noticeable at high levels so I do not use it. The effects may be notable at around level 20 or so, but at level 40+ the effects are insignificant. 
 Maiya Samsara - This is a spell for grouping with tanks. I don't use it unless there are at least 3 melee types in the group. It will stack with heart flutter. I'd say I get about 10% faster takedowns by casting Heart Flutter and Shadow Vortex and meding. The best thing is that Shadow Vortex has a very low damage rating, so you're not likely to aggro a mob with it. 
Brollywacked - I find that for the mana, this spell is good in a group situation. Because the Mobs tend to go down so fast when beat upon by 3 tanks + my pet, I find DoTs to not have enough time to take effect. I stack this spell with Heart
flutter as an extra debuff as I am waiting to cast my next Shock of Poison or Life tap series. It also seems to not pull the mob onto you.
