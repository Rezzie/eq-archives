Clinging Darkness 

Skill : Alteration

Mana : 20

Speed : 1.8

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Makes a creature move slower and deals Damage over Time (DoT)

Comments :

Skyshade - A good spell for combat initiation, Clinging Darkness can also buy you some time to run away. The damage per round it deals is quite low, but it is persistent and slowing your opponent down is invaluable. Specially if you want to kite it.

 Lifebane - I still use this version of darkness at level 50. Quick casting and minimal mana expended. Expect spell to be resisted often, so why blow lots of mana on an upgraded version of this spell? An upgrade that takes more mana and has a much slower casting time? It gets resisted and you are out of mana and need to recast the slow spell. but now the MOB is aggro on you. Ack! This is my preference anyway.

 Fear

Skill : Alteration

Mana : 40

Speed : - 3.5

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Makes an NPC run away

Comments:

Skyshade - Lots of uses for Fear. First, it can be used to do the famous kiting. Simply cast a DoT spell on your target and then Fear it. It will run away, take the damage from the DoT spell and your pet. Of course, that worked great until the recent changes to reduce our kiting ability.   Nonetheless, still a very useful spell that you shall use until you get its upgrade at level 34.

Lifebane - You will learn to love this spell. Good for crowd control, good for using in conjunction with DOT's for more damage, and even good to make a MOB run off and let you escape. Just be careful, any "friendly" MOB's your feared opponent runs by will likely attack you in defense of your first opponent. Definitely not something to use in a dungeon.

 

Gate

Skill: Alteration

Mana: 70

Speed: - 5.0

Damage: -

Research or Purchase: Purchase

Research Material: -

Effect: Takes you back to your home/bind point

Comments:

Skyshade - Initially, your bind point is your home town, but you can change that by casting or asking someone to cast on you the spell Bind Affinity (level 12). There are two very useful ways you can use Gate. First, you can speed up the traveling process by gating back to your desired destination. And second, you can use as a last resource in a battle. Hopefully your pet can hold your attacker long enough so you can gate and safely abandon that battle

Lifebane - Finally! No more having to run back to hometown to sell things when your bags are full. Gate back to town, sell, buy, then run off to slay some more. And definitely a good escape spell if time permits you to cast it before getting thrashed.

Numb the Dead

Skill : Abjuration 

Mana : 20

Speed : - 2.0

Damage : -

Research or Purchase: Purchase

Research material : -

Effect : 

Reduces the chance an undead will attack players they would normally attack
Comments :

Skyshade - Numb the Dead is sometimes useful when dealing with 2 undead creatures at the same time. You can make the combat a lot easier if one of them is not attacking you. Not a lot you can do besides that. 

Lifebane - Numb the Dead is the First of our Undead controlling spells. It is attained at level 4 and can be very useful around that time in a young necros life. This spell, though not granting control over the undead, will cause the undead to become less aggressive. What this means is that it will not automatically attack as it would normally. Example: you happen upon a skeleton spawn point and see 2 skeletons, you and your friend decide you can handle one but not both.... normally you attack one then both will come. Well "Numb the Dead" on one, then cast an attack spell on the other. If the Numb spell was not resisted, only the skeleton you attacked will come... the other will ignore the attack on his skeleton pal. Excellent way to get more "controlled" battles at lower levels. At higher levels there is an upgrade called Rest the Dead.

Speed : - 1.8

Damage : - Deals initial 5 points of damage and 8 points of Damage over Time for a max of 10 rounds

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Poison attack that deals damage over time

Comments :

Skyshade - A nice spell that you can combine with Clinging Darkness to deal a fairly nice amount of damage to the target creature. Unlike Clinging Darkness though, Poison Bolt was resisted quite often. Nonetheless, Poison Bolt is a nice spell for low level necros and you can start using the DoT/kite strategy.

Lifebane - Nice little DOT for this level. Clinging, Poison, Disease Cloud, then fear with pet on their backside... mean combo for this level.
