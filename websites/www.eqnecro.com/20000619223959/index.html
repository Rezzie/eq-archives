Kunark Loot
[Quote from original post: The drops in Kunark of cash are pathetic, every decent item costs THOUSANDS of plat on Veeshan, and there is NO way to get this kind of money together after nerfing the dwarfs, except by camping rare spawns or HGs, and competeing w lvl 30 characters for kills. I have hunted blue con mobs in almost every outdoor kunark zone and they all drop liek 3sp and a TON of cp drops, WTF is that when you kill 10 lvl 40 mobs and get 11cp a piece?]

It is true that cash drops in general are getting lower. In addition to the Kunark zones all being set up to drop less cash, other zones are likewise being modified. "Cash Cows", such as the dwarves in Butcherblock that used to drop an average of 27pp every six minutes, are being identified and adjusted.

Good items cost "thousands of plat" because of the amount of excess cash in the world. As the money sinks take their toll, the price of the items will drop to a level of equilibrium with the current cash drops.

It shows that money is becoming more valuable.


Skyfires & Coin Looting
As I commented in another thread today, coin loot is indeed lighter for many areas of the game. Skyfire included.

In other zones, the "big" outdoor critters are an exception to other things in the zone, allowing for more loot to be handed out since there are less of them to go around. However in Skyfire, *everything* is pretty nasty, with those encounters being the rule rather than the exception. Many groups can hunt there nonstop and never run out of things to kill. Given that, they just can't drop the 10-30pp per kill of other encounters.

As for the XP, the mobs in Skyfire give more experience than mobs of a relative level in any other outdoor zone in the world.


Make the Sense Spells Make Sense
[Quote from original post: Anyway, I have noticed that no one really uses these spells very much. The reason for this is because with all the Necro's, Mages, and Enchanters around, you can't really get any use out of this. What would be nice is that instead of the current way that these spells work.. would be if they would work somewhat like Tracking does, bringing up all of the mobs in a certian radius, of the type mentioned.]

Though I appreciate your concerns and suggestions concerning the "sense" series, we wouldn't want to dilute tracking by extended "undead tracking" or "summoned tracking" to other classes. I know that it's been suggested before that we shouldn't include pets as a valid target of a "sense" spell. Perhaps that might be something I could pursue. I'll check into it.
