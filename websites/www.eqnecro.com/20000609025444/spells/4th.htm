Engulfing Darkness

Skill : Conjuration 

Mana : 60

Speed : 2.5

Damage : 10 points per round for a max of 10 rounds

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Reduce target creature's movement and deals damage over time

Comments:

Skyshade - Engulfing Darkness is not that great in terms of dealing DoT, but it is excellent to in reducing the target creature's movement. If under the effect of this spell, the creature will not catch up with you in case you need to run. In addition, when cast in combination with Heat Blood, the other DoT spell from level 12, you can deal a substantial amount of damage to a creature simply by watching it. Very useful in large areas such as the Karana Plains where you can cast the spell and then run away for some time until the creature has taken a good amount of damage.
Lifebane - One thing to note: DOT changes made some time back made the above method described by Skyshade less effective damage wise.  Your best bet is to cast all of this on them then Fear them, a creature running away takes full damage, not partial. Just be careful of fearing your prey into an area where prey can get help!

Sight Graft

Skill : Divination 

Mana : 10

Speed : 5.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Allows caster to see through his/her pet's eyes

Comments:

Skyshade - I have not yet found a situation where doing this can be useful. Since we have a very limited control over our pet, you cannot send him to a potentially dangerous area to do some scouting. And even if you could, in case the area is actually infested, your pet would get attacked and you would soon be next. A waste of money.
Lifebane - Hmmm.. maybe good for looking around corners if pet is parked there looking in that direction. Otherwise, worthless *grin

Madein - I have found Graft Sight (12th level spell) can be used in an interesting way,
based on certain behaviors of the game and your pet.   1) You Pet always faces the closest MOB.   2) You can cast spells on creatures you see through your pet.   So, when hunting, you have your pet guard near a traveled area. You pet always looks towards the closest monster. You sit a ways behind your pet, and can target the monster through your pet's eyes. You cast your spell and send pet in to attack.


 Spook the Dead

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 10

Speed : 2.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Makes the undead run away

Comments:

Skyshade - An upgrade to the level 4 Fear spell that works only on undeads. As it is true with Fear, Spook the Dead is good to make a battle more even for a short amount of time. Best when used in a party so you can control who stays and who goes. But can also be used when solo hunting. Just cast some DoT spells on your target and send it away. It will not only take some damage from the DoTs, but also from your pet and even your drain life. And the best thing is if the creature is running away, it will not fight back. 
Lifebane - Spook the Dead is another Undead only spell. This one is obtainable once you reach level 12. It allows you the ability to FEAR off undead creatures. It does seem to be more effective against undead then regular old 4th level fear spell is. One of the tactics I have used and seen used often is to find a good undead spawn point (ruins). Numb the dead on one, spook another and fight the third. By the time the spook wears off, the first undead you were battling is likely dead so kill the spooked one. Then when done... take a quick breather, then go finish off that last undead. It is a very useful undead control spell. And hey, remember that you have a pet and darkness right? Spook one, darkness on it, have pet beat on its slow running butt as it runs in fear. A later version of this is Panic the Dead.
