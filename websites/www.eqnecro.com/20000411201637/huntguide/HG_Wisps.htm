Strategy

Erud's crossing is a small zone, so finding the main wisp hunting spot should be easy.  Just walk straight from the dock and wait until you see some people on a hill hunting wisps or meding.

 

Up to 4 wisps can spawn there at a time, but that is pretty rare, usually 2-3 at time, and it is pretty fast so you won't be waiting for your turn in the rotation.  You can hunt either here or wander around looking for wisps, they are very common on this island.  Be careful though, you need magic to hurt wisps.

 

When fighting wisps, make sure you Darkness first.  I advise you don't kite or fear it, there is a good chance you will run into another wisp that doesn't like you beating up on it's friend and you will have 2 wisps on your tail. There is no escape from this island except for the occasional boat.  Then you would sic pet then heat blood, then let the wisp beat on you occasionally to save your pet from dying and Lifedraw to get some life back.

 

I find the wisps don't stand much of a chance and at level 14 I could take 3 at a time with a little luck.  Before level 12 though, you have no magic hitting pet, a 40dmg DOT, and a 10dmg Lifespike spell, so you aren't very effective against wisps until level 12.

 

Willowisps are a great source of cash, but it varies.  I ,once, on a bad day, only made 2pp in an hour's work.  Usually I make 10-20pp in an hour's work.  And, twice, I made 40-50pp in an hour's work.  I consider this VERY good for cash except for that one occasion when I made only 2pp.  The reason for this excellent source of cash is that Willowisps rarely drop Greater Lightstones, which usually sell for 8pp each to a person, or you can make a trip to North Karana and turn them into a Gypsy and get a little XP with it too, but you get less cash that way, at least for an Erudite Necro.  Most of the goody people get 8.5-9pp when turning them in, so they very willing to buy them for 8pp.  Willowisps also drop Lightstones which sell for 8gp to the near Kerran merchant. You can also sell GLS's to this merchant, but you only get 3.5pp for it.  And last, and definitely least, you can get Burned Out Lightstones, which are No Drop and sell for nothing at merchants. LS's and BOLS's are about even in occurrence, and GLS's are semi-rare.

Additional Comments
Tsin At the lower levels where wisp hunting may be desirable except for lack of a magic-hitting pet, I have found an easy solution.  Before you leave Qeynos on the boat, find a friendly mage and get him to summon you a few daggers.  When you give these to your pet, he now hits magic! It is now possible to take wisps singly as low as tenth level if you are careful.
