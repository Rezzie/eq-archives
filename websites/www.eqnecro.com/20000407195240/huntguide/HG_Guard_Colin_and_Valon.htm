Strategy

The first thing you must know about hunting Guard Colin and Guard Valon is that they fight slightly above their con. Therefore while they will CON even-red to you at about 29, you will most likely need a group to kill either one, unless you have SOW and a good pet (and a lot of mana).

 

To start off, you should Scream one of them and pull the other one with Engulfing Darkness at max range (doesn't really matter from what direction).  He will come running toward you, and the tank of your group if you have one should engage him a few yards from you. DON'T let them hit you. These suckers can hit you for 50-70 points of damage, and once Guard Valon doubled me for 66 and bashed me for 19 when I tried to be funny and run him past our druid, and got a little too close to him, for a total of 151 points of damage in a round.  Ow.  Keep casting Engulfing on him until it sticks.  This should not take too long...even at level 29, when Guard Valon was red to me, usually he only resisted Engulfing once and the second time it almost always stuck.  After that, hit him with Boil Blood.  Or Heat Blood if you don't have Boil, but I must remind you hunting these guys before level 29 is very risky.  Remember if you are dealing with both keep the one you are not fighting screamed.  Two of these guards can cause real problems to even a full group of 30 level characters.  Then, let the rest of your group do some serious damage to him and after about thirty seconds start SoP'ing.  They should drop fast. (This is the tactic a level 26 druid, who was around just for healing, a 30 warrior, and me used.  I was 29, and we had very few problems after we broke the spawn).

 

One thing to remember is DO NOT LET THEM GET AGGROED ON YOU.  Ergo, after you hit them with your DoTs, WAIT before you start to cast the rest of your damage spells. It is very hard to pull either guard off a aggroed person.  I made this mistake a few times when I hit them with my ED and BB and then cast a SOP about ten seconds later. Fear is a good spell to use after you get them with your DOTS as well.  They don't resist it too often and their speed as they "run" away with ED on is completely laughable.  Also, Fear does not get them insane aggroed on you.

 

Some more information before you try to take on these guys.  In the group you will need a tank, and a healer.  As I stated before these guys hit very hard, and your pet just can't tank them for too long even buffed and when you have a good one.  The experience is very good...I gained about a bubble an hour in the above party.  The loot is very bad though, at the most they will have a plat on them. The last thing I wish to include in this form is that Guard Valon is stronger than Guard Colin.  At 29 Colin conned even to yellow to me, while Valon was usually yellow and sometimes red.  It shows, too.  Valon is a lot harder to beat than Colin is.
