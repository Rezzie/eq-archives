Strategy

I'm level 22, and I've died a couple of times trying to solo him, but get about half a bubble for doing so.

 

I start off by buffing my pet with spirit armor, then buffing my self with Shielding.  Sometimes I use other buffs just to be safe.  First, send your pet in and cast Engulfing Darkness so he runs slow (usually resisted, don't panic).  Then, cast Heat Blood if he resists the Engulfing (usually), then cast Fear.  When he is running, I cast Heat Blood (if I haven't done so already), and Heart Flutter. After that, I nuke with my Drain Spell  and if Fear runs off, I Fear him again and keep nuking.  At the end you can use Lifespike if needed.

 

To recap, the order of spells I use are: pet, Darkness, Fear, Heat Blood, Heart Flutter, Lifedrain, Lifespike.

 

He spawns about every 20 minutes, and if you are in trouble, there is the zone near by.  Guards don't seem to attack when you hit him.

Additional Comments

Nergal

Travis is exactly a 21 minute spawn.  He resists darkness fairly well, but soaks up fear like a sponge.  Even when you nuke he seems to stay fairly well stuck on the pet too, so he's really a push over.  He does however, hit like a freight train, so its important to keep him feared or he'll plow through your pet in a hurry, and then you'll be zoning... He gives incredible XP.  At 24 I estimate I get a bubble of XP about every 6-7 kills.  The down side: he drops almost nothing.  Cash ranging up to 2pp and in 10 kills I've gotten one word off him.  Nothing else. Go out, have fun, kill a bard...and remember bards are good (if properly seasoned and slow roasted *cackle*).

Kronos (Lanys T'vyl)

Guards do not aggro against you for hunting him, unlike other zones. I almost soloed him at 20th with a Gnome Necromancer and ~150 INT.  I got him down to the T in Travis, but fear broke and I was OOM, and low on health.  I had a blue (dangerous) pet at the time with a giant snake fang as a weapon. I can see him easily duetted from 20th-22nd for adequate experience.
