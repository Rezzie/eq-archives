Strategy

The South Karana Treants are one of the most popular and most rewarding targets for characters in their 20s.  To hunt them, you will need a 4 or 5 Necros in their low 20s or 2 level 24+ Necros.

 

The strategy is the same regardless of how many people you have in your party.  Two Treants spawn right next to each other and your entire party should target one of them.  Send in your tanks and any pets your party has available and cast Direct Damage spells.  Do not cast DoT spells as Treants die fast and that is how you want it.  So, use your Drain Life spell or Shock of Poison (if you have it).  Once the first Treant is dead, move to the second one.

 

Treants cast area effect fire based spells as well as DoTs.  By sending your pets before casting, the Treants will focus on pets instead of you.  Beware though that since it is an area effect spell, any tanks or pets close to Treants will take damage.  And it is quite a lot of damage so make sure you kill the first Treant as fast as you can to reduce their power to half.  Also, make sure the members of your party are not too close to each other.  That way you can avoid the area effect spell (only one or two would be hit by it).

 

Killing Treants is as simple as that.  Send tanks/pets and burn them.

 

Treants drop up to 5 plat per kill and often a Great Staff, a normal staff that is worth around 15 plat (you can unload these at the Centaur Village located nearby).  Research material is not an uncommon loot and you might find a few level 24 Words here.  Only problem with the loot is that a large portion of the cash is dropped in silver/gold pieces.  Thus, expect to go over your weight limit very fast.

 

XP is also pretty nice.  With a good party going, you will have very little downtime since Treants spawn every 4 minutes or so.

 

The only bad thing about it is the faction hit.  Killing Treants will lower your faction standing with the League of Antonican Bards, Guards of Qeynos, and Jagged Pine Treefolk.  Unkempt Druids faction is raised.

 

Enjoy!

Amotd Innoruuk

There are 2 Treants who spawn at this spawn about every 4 minutes. Usually this is camped by a group of 6 and a list is kept to call the next lucky person when someone leaves. Very Very rare for someone to die if a full group. Biggest drawback is the loot. Drops per Treant. 0 to 5 p. 0 to 50g. 0 to 90s. 0 to 90c. You can also find random Words, and a Great Staff worth up to 17p depending on Charisma.  With the number of coins you become encumbered very quickly.  Innoruuk folks actually come by and act as bankers for the group for a small fee.  Will usually make 1 bubble of XP every 90 minutes. Loot is no split , loot both.  No particular strategy.  Tanks tank and casters do DD.  NO DoT, they die too fast.  Centaur Village is about 30 seconds away and will trade with all.

The spawn rate is about 3-6 minutes and there's two spawn points.  Loot ranges anywhere from small amounts of silver, to 10 platinum or so.  They drop Great Staves every now and then which can be sold for about 14pp depending on CHA.  They also drop a lot of silver, so be prepared to dump it, or be encumbered. :)

Gorathilnaur Mormegil (Erollisi Marr)

You can group with others for good XP on treants pretty much soon as you get to 21, though occasionally you will get a resist.

 

It is a good idea to make sure you have Shadow Compact, especially pre 24 when you get Shock of Poison. Occasionally the treants throw out a nasty set of creeping crud and an ice DD that will leave a player or two (especially tanks) very low on health. Your ability to share some health about (after all, you are going to get it back in a few minutes) will ease up the load on any Shamans, and make a huge difference if theres no healer. Almost unheard of for Clerics to be at the camp. Buffing with Resist Cold will also help. Tanks, and your lead nuker need that.

 

At 24, you should also have Rapacious Subversion. Again, you can act to help out an OOM person, say a wizzy without clarity, or the aforesaid shaman whos just buffed the group. You won't use this one as often, the efificiency is a bit dodgy unless you are FM/FH, in which case allure on, and subvert away.

 

Just these two things will make the group remember you - and want you next time. A major asset at this coveted spot.
