Engulfing Darkness, Heat blood, Spook the Dead, Heart Flutter, Ward Undead

Strategy

There is a valley that separates South Ro into two parts, desert and grassy area.

 

Stand on this hill and look out over desert area.  When you see a Dry Bones, go in and Darkness him, run a little bit towards hill and Heat Blood.  Run back to the hill, but not over.  Wandering mobs from desert won't go over this hill unless agro'd.  They get confused if they are too far behind you and get lost.  Once you have gotten the Dry Bones out of the general lanes of traffic, send in pet, Heart flutter, then Spook the Dead.  Once he turns, Ward undead until he is dead.  Not too difficult, but mana intensive.

 

This can be a dangerous zone to someone new to the area.  The desert area has two Dervish camps, on opposite sides and the rest are wandering Undeads, Madmen, and Mummies.  I love this zone.  Madmen (waterboys) will be green to you and not difficult to kill, as will be Mummies.  Most will be green, with the occasional low blue.  The only real thing to worry about is the wandering mob issue.  A green agro'd on you while your fighting a yellow can be just enough to turn success into defeat.  Scope the place out for a few minutes, learn the area and always try to pull Dry Bones towards the edge of the desert, next to to a wall or close to the grassy area.

 

And now to the very best part about hunting this zone.  Dark Elf merchants in the center will buy your stuff.  No zoning and losing your pet just to unload all those Raw-hide leggings (1+ plat).  Grobb is only two zones away, so there is a bank not too far away. About equal to hunting in East Commons.

 

On Tribunal, I have hunted this zone in the past all by myself.  I think this zone is under appreciated by far, especially to those of us who RULE the dead.

Pay head to Malnutrion's guidance and always pull to the area of the desert that runs into grass.  I made the mistake of sitting to the side of the zone and the Skeletons, Mummies and Madmen have a habit of pathing to that area and interrupting your meditation.  Also beware of the occasional group taking down Sand Giants - one romped through my camp and wiped out my pet in 2 seconds.
