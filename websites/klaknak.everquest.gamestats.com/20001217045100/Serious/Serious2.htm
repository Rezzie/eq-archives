Kiting and Proposed Changes - by the Cackling Klaknak

My opinion about kiting is lukewarm at best.

The first time I saw kiting, I did not know what it was. I saw this bard fellow running around in Northern Ro with an Orc or something hot on his tail. Being the noble cleric I was, I chased after the orc and began frying it with spells. Eventually, I killed the orc and went to check on the fleeing bard.

Imagine my surprise when he called me an "afjklsf." Or maybe it was "Ifjkd," I am not sure. After a few perplexed minutes, it finally occurred to me: Oh, so this is what kiting looks like.

The bard then asked me if I was against kiting, and without even thinking I said, "Nah. Not really."

After thinking about it some more, I still don't think I am against kiting.

Why am I not against kiting? Well, basically I just don't give a damn if someone wants to run around for several minutes in order to get a kill. I think they look completely silly and, although I have never done it, I am not sure I understand where skill comes into play, but if they want to do it, I say let them. It's not harming me or anyone else, that I can see.

Two key objections seem to come up over the whole kiting thing:

1. From the purists, you get the (legitimate) argument that kiting is a gaming loophole ... a phenomena not intended by the developers and therefore wrong.

2. From certain powergamers, you get the (maybe legitimate) argument that kiting creates class imbalance. It is not fair ... blah blah blah ... that ... blah blah blah ... can do ... blah blah blah ... but my character can't.

These arguments are strong, but I come from a different point of view:

Perfect game balance is impossible. A few classes will always be able to solo better than others.

Therefore, my response is a big fat "SO WHAT."

I play a cleric, arguably one of the worst soloists in the game, and I love (almost) every minute of it. I simply don't give a rat's ass if DoT's give other classes advantages over me. I wonder if it is only the powergamers (and I believe Brad is one) who are really concerned about this.

Of course, just because perfect game balance is impossible doesn't mean certain glaring problems should be ignored, but I think (again) that kiting is a big fat "so what."

I do have to end, however, with a stab in the back for all you kiters out there. Any player with even the tiniest slice of intuition HAD to know that Verant would eventually do something about this. I think most people have played EQ, UO, and other ORPGs long enough to realize that if something is too easy, it is going to eventually be nuked. So, don't come crying on my shoulder if you have built up a 30+ level character on kiting and now you don't know what to do.

My advice: Play a cleric. Our skills are so anti-levelling, we won't be nerfed ... ever.

(Who can spot the "famous last words"?)
