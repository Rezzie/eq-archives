Gotta Maintain

"Welcome to Everquest," my half-eaten rice and salad sandwich said.

Ok. Fine. I gotta maintain. Just maintain until the effects of Zoloft, 3 year old beer, 3 year old pot and 10 year old car carpet wear off.

"Gotta maintain," I repeated out loud.

My sandwich snorted in derision.

"Ok smart ass," I said, scolding my sandwich. "What exactly is Everquest."

A beautiful voice chimed in my head.

"EverQuest" she said, "allows thousands of gamers from all over the world to adventure solo, at great peril, or join forces with online allies to fight off enemies together. The Station@sony.com is the official provider of EverQuest software updates and news about the game, message boards and other community features, as well as an easy-to-use billing system for EverQuest’s monthly subscription fee. 989 Studios, a Sony Computer Entertainment Group Company, is a wholly-owned division of Sony Computer Entertainment America Inc. and is the third-largest PlayStation® software publisher in North America. Headquartered in Foster City, California, with additional development studios in San Diego and Santa Monica, 989 Studios develops software for the PlayStation game console, personal computer and online markets. 989 Studios is a recognized leader in the videogame industry, with top franchise titles such as NFL GameDay™, Twisted Metal®, Jet Moto™ and Cool Boarders®. "

"I see," I replied.

For the first time I noticed that the City of Elves was built across a series of gigantic trees.

"And what is this place again?" I asked. But the voice was gone.

"This is Kelethin," my sandwich said. "City of the Wood Elves."

"Ok."

I took a deep breath and began rummaging in my pockets for some cigarettes. Without surprise, I noticed that I had no pockets and was wearing some sort of form fitting cloth thing.

"And why am I here?" I asked.

"You are now a Wood Elf," my sandwich said. "You are a Wood Elf Ranger in the city of Kelethin."

"I see," I said. I began looking around nervously, ready to run in case the Lenin Leprachauns tried to sneak up on me.

"By the way" my sandwich said. "Your name was rejected."

"Eh?" I asked, desperately searching the side of my pants for some cigarettes, wary of the leprachauns.

"Your name ... Ralph ... it was rejected. You are now known as Gayfeather."

"Ok," I said. "Gayfeather, fine."

Secretly I cringed. But then I remembered what Jim had said several months earlier after I had hallucinated riding a single prong fork across a moon prairie:

"Never try and interpret your dreams, man" he said. "It will freak you out, guaranteed. Just go with it."

"Gotta maintain," I said.

Something was chafing my back. I reached back and found that I was wearing a small animal skin pack, so I flung it off my shoulder and began rummaging around in it. It contained several small chunks of yellow food, a skin of water, a sword, a scroll and a book.

"Gotta maintain," I said.

"Look," said my sandwich. "I realize this must be hard for you, but I think it is important you realize how lucky you are to be involved in such an amazing adventure. I mean, Everquest is overseen by Sony, for christ's sake."

"Yes," I said. "I guess ... Is Everquest a game of some kind?"

"Yes it is," said my sandwich. "It is an online multiplayer universe."

"Ah."

I rummaged again for some cigarettes.

"Why don't you try foraging," my sandwich said. "You are a ranger after all."

"Ok ..."

I began rummaging around on the ground. I found some worms.

"You have foraged some fish grub!" my sandwich said, with great excitement.

"Ah," I said.

When I was 18, some friends and I ate some peyote buttons and sat around eating off a huge block of chocolate all day. When we came out of the trip, our couch was gone. Perhaps this fishing grub was a dehallucinator or something. I ate them.

There was no effect.

"Ok," I said, wiping some goo from my lips. "So what do I have to do to get out of this game?"

"Simple," my sandwich said. "You must kill the Lady Vox."

My stomach grumbled.

"Excellent," I said. "Will we be done in time for dinner? I'm hungry."

My sandwich sighed.

"No," it said. "It will probably take you a while to do that."

I sighed.

"Ok, what do I need to do?"

My sandwich was suddenly very excited.

"Ok," it said. "First you need to deliver this note to your guild master. His name is Maesyn Trueshot and he is in Faydark's Champions straight ahead. Let's go."

"Guildmaster, right" I said. If there was one thing I had learned after several years of abusing my body, it was to go with the flow. If I just relax, and let the world come to me, everything would be just fine. So I picked up my pack, put it on my back and walked inside the guild hall.

Inside, several people stood around casting spells and there was a beautiful butterfly looking thing in the corner. This was more like it.

I also noticed that there were names floating over the heads of the "elves."

"Go with the flow," I said. "Go with the flow."

I found Maesyn.

"Yo, G, what up?" I said.

"Um, Roger?"

"Yes, sandwich."

"You may be a wood elf, but you are still white."

"Oh, right" I said. "So how do I talk to this guy?"

"Just say Hail," he said.

"Ok. Hail Maesyn!"

Maesyn's voice boomed inside the small guild hall. I nearly had a heart attack.

"'Welcome to Kelethin Gayfeather," he said. "I am Maesyn Trueshot commander of Faydark's Champions. We are the finest marksman in all of Norrath. With our trusty Trueshot Longbows we can miss no target no matter what the distance or the conditions."

"Ask him what a Trueshot Longbows is," my sandwich said.

"What is a trueshot longbow?" I asked.

Maesyn stood silent, staring into the distance.

"No, ask him what a Trueshot Longbows is. Use the plural."

"Oh, sorry," I said. "What is a Trueshot Longbows?"

Maesyn again exploded giving me another near fatal heart attack.

""The Trueshot Longbow,"" he said. "is created by my famed father, Eldin Trueshot. It is quite accurate and takes a rangers skill to wield. We use our new recruits to gather materials needed by my father. We shall soon begin to release it's formula to all good elves so all may fletch such a bow.

"Tell him you will gather materials," my sandwich said.

"I will gather materials," I said.

"'Take this pack," he boomed. "You will go to Kaladim and find Trantor Everhot and ask for Dwarven Wire. Then go to Freeport to meet a Jyle Windshot, search the inns, ask him for Treant Wood. Then collect some Spiderling Silk from spiderlings and Finally in Steamfont, we have the permission of the dwarves to use any Micro Servos we find while destroying rogue spiders. Combine them all and return the pack to me."

"Oh that won't be too hard," said my sandwich.

A sarcastic sandwich. This was a world class hallucination.

"Now give him your letter," my sandwich said and I did, but I was feeling very impatient and hungry and started feeling that emotion known in the Crayola box of emotions as Low Blood Sugar Surly.

'Hail Gayfeather, and welcome.." said Maesyn.

"Yeah, yeah ... hail. Ok, give me something," I said.

"I am Maesyn Trueshot, leader of Faydark's Champions."

"No shit, really? Is that why you have that name floating over your head?"

"I will teach and train you, as I have done for many others."

"I can be a Jedi, Yoda. I know I can! I can do it!"

"Let's get started.. here, put this on.."

"Shove it up your ass."

"It'll help protect you against the elements."

"Ooo, a shitty green shirt. Thanks, master."

"You look pretty bright.."

"I'm so bright, my parents call me son."

"Train hard, and I'm sure you'll do well."

"Yeah, right back at you fruitcake."

Maesyn smiled. I gave him the bird.

"Let's go," said my sandwich.

"Where are we going," I asked.

"The hell out of here," said my sandwich. "This place gives me a headache."

It was then that I noticed for the first time the incessant chatter filling the air. Grabbing some of the yellow globules of food from my pack, I listened more closely as I stuffed my face.

"Providing binds for phoney tuff and the crazy brave at the POD"

"Small PW / tattered armour for sale. caps, sleeves, gloves, boots - 3ac - 3gp. shoulders - 2ac - 2gp"

"Wcn dod kvir` ole `emp oe"

"How much do mistmoor shilds cost?"

"What is loc of BB entrance?"

"Will somone in greater fay help a crazy ass lvl 1 ogre with some stuff?"

"Providing binds for phoney tuff and the crazy brave at the POD it is just another wonderful thing provided by Viriti"

"Yeah zmog you want a fs two hander?"

"I doubt youd give it to me but waht the hell sure"

"Where are ya?"

"Providing binds for phoney tuff and the crazy brave at the POD it is just another wonderful thing provided by Viriti"

"Bless the le people and the medium craces of norrath that fury footed halfling is a true man"

"Let's get the hell out of here," I said. "I am starting to freak out."

"We're off to Freeport," my sandwich said.

And so we were. After several days of wandering around, my sandwich and I finally found the elevator down to the forest floor and we ran through various "zones" until we found a dock in the land of the dwarves. Freeport was the city of humans, according to my sandwich, so I felt confident I would be able to buy some cigarettes and porn there to help me sit the hallucination out. I was in good spirits.

If I only knew then what I knew now ...
