A Passion for Incompetence - by the Cackling Klaknak

I have played computer games for damn near my entire life. So has my beloved brother The Gun Wielding Klaknak. In many ways, our different tastes in games have summed up our differences in personalities. I have always gone for RPGs and he has always gone for blowing shit up.

Recently, however, thanks to a little game called System Shock 2 I have become enamored with first person shooters. I played System Shock 2 (my favorite game from last year) until I was nearly dead from fright and adrenaline. System Shock 2 was such a great game, I went through a several month period where I bought several games and couldn't play any of them. I wanted System Shock 3 and I wanted it right then.

Finally, about a month ago, I broke down and bought Unreal Tournament for myself and for my brother. I also bought a little known title called Half-Life.

First, I tried Unreal Tournament. Needless to say, I was extremely bad.

While I was playing System Shock 2 and reading various people talk on various message boards, I decided that most single-player player styles can be broken down into two distinct groups:

1. Rabid, hard core gamers who run and shoot until everything is dead.

2. Methodical plodders who savor every bullet as if they only have one for the entire game.

I most definitely fall into the latter group. In System Shock 2 (and especially in Half-Life) I was always at my limit for ammo. I hardly EVER used ammo. I methodically and, I think, skillfully walked through each level and did the bare minimum to make my way through. My favorite weapon by far was the crowbar (it was a crowbar, right?). Even in Half-Life, I tried whenever I could to sneak up on unsuspecting grunts and bash their brains in with the crowbar instead of using any precious ammunition.

But when I started playing Unreal Tournament, I had to . ahem . change my approach. I was astonished . nay, I was aghast that ammunition and weapons were so plentiful.

What kind of freaking game is this, I thought. Where is the skill? You basically have an unlimited amount of weapons and an open arena. Who couldn't win this game?

Oh boy.

Last night I played my very first multiplayer first person shooter. I was so extremely bad, I almost broke down into tears. No matter what map I chose, I was quickly and mercilessly eliminated by every single person on the map.

I mean, man. Listen to me for a second. Let this seep into your mind and bones for the full effect: I WAS EXTREMELY EXTREMELY BAD.

My first impression was to simply dismiss this multiplayer madness as some sort of mindless crap for adrenaline idiots, but underneath the plastic exterior of my ego-saving persona, I realized that I was just shit bad and I needed to get my act together if I ever hoped to beat even the whoriest of bad players out there.

So what do I plan on doing?

Fix some chili.

What does this have to do with Unreal Tournament? Absolutely nothing.
