Business Travel - by Toraque

Travel and the experiences it can bring are rewarding in many ways, not only in the knowledge of different ways of life, but also in the personal growth one can see by reaching new horizons. Unfortunately, it can also keep one from Everquest.

1: LOGGING ON TO EVERQUEST Difficulties exist in the attainment of a connection to an Everquest server that do not occur at one's home. Typically, a business traveler carries at best a laptop, which lacks the horsepower to run Everquest at anything approaching the playable:

WARRIOR: Here's the plan--I'll pull the room. Enchanter, you mesmerize the two guards on the right, Druid, I want you to kite the one on the left for a minute and then bring it back, Ranger, you shoot arrows at anything that moves and keep an eye out for big things heading this way.

CLERIC: What’s the Business Traveler going to do?

WARRIOR: Well, he hasn't moved for five minutes, so probably not very much.

As an alternative, one could pack a more powerful desktop computer, but that would require either paying expensive air freight or excess baggage fees, or cutting down on other luggage you might otherwise bring with you.

BOSS: So it looks like we can revitalize the contract if we can talk the home office into accepting a ten percent downturn on the . . . Weren't you wearing that shirt yesterday?

EMPLOYEE: Uh, yes.

BOSS: And the day before that?

EMPLOYEE: err . . . I left all my clothes back home so I could bring my computer. But I made level thirty!

BOSS: You're fired.

One could also choose to rent or buy a computer while in country, but aside from the expense, this option has several problems of it's own, which brings us to:

2: FOREIGN LANGUAGE BARRIERS The sad fact remains that most countries in the world do not have English as the primary spoken language. This alone is enough to deter many of those who would otherwise be able to log on to EQ, especially if they have chosen to acquire a computer while in this non-English speaking land.

CLERK: Sawadee.

BUSINESS TRAVELER: I need to rent a computer to play Everquest.

CLERK: Pah-sah, mai bin lai?

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Err, Computer? (mimes typing on a keyboard)

CLERK: (Looks confused, then hands him something.)

BUSINESS TRAVELER: No, that's a typewriter. Computer? Microsoft? Bill Gates?

CLERK: (Looks confused, then hands him something.)

BUSINESS TRAVELER: No, that's a live squid. Never mind.

Even in countries that speak a majority of English, Everquest can be blocked by local customs:

CLERK: G'Day, mate!

BUSINESS TRAVELER: I need to rent a computer to play Everquest.

CLERK: Computer? Why? There's a pub, mate, let's go get pissed!

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Err, okay.

(next morning)

CLERK: G'Day, mate!

BUSINESS TRAVELER: (Hung over) I need to rent a computer to play Everquest.

CLERK: Computer? Why? There's a pub, mate, let's go get pissed!

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Uh, okay. (next morning)

CLERK: G'Day, mate!

BUSINESS TRAVELER: (Extremely hung over) I need (urk) to rent a computer to play Everquest.

CLERK: Computer? Why? There's a pub, mate, let's go get pissed!

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Uh, I think I'm going to vomit.

3: DIFFICULTIES ARISING FROM THE TRAVEL ITSELF

Should a method of connecting to an Everquest server be determined, the combined results of jet lag, local alcohols and fatigue can often result in tragic consequences.

(After a tough fight)

RANGER: Well, we probably would have survived if you hadn't kept casting 'Bravery' on that fire goblin.

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Did I do that?

ENCHANTER: At least you only did it twice. Then you knocked me in the lava.

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Err . . .

RANGER: Moron.

4: EXPECTATIONS OF ACTUAL WORK

Often, once an employer has gone through the expense of purchasing the airfare, hotel reservations, outlaying the per diem and all of the other costs involved with running a project halfway around the world, they have some concrete beliefs concerning what exactly you should be doing while you are living on their expense voucher.

BOSS: So once we get system installed, we can deliver your report and make sure we can get the funding the second phase of operations. Is your report ready for the customer?

EMPLOYEE: Well, I'm still in the, err, conceptual phase and, uh, waiting on, uh . .

BOSS: Waiting on?

EMPLOYEE: Err, waiting on the, uh, concepts to, uh, phase . . .

BOSS: So basically you haven't done any work for the whole time you've been there.

EMPLOYEE: Pretty much.

BOSS: You're fired.

5: LOCAL APPEARANCES OF THE FOUR HORSEMEN

Sadly, much of the world is prone to periods of famine, murder or war. Murphy’s law dictates that wherever you go will be the place that riots, a coup d'etat, massive currency devaluation or outbreaks of plague will happen.

(True story)

BOSS: One of our guys caught Typhoid. We need you to go replace him.

EMPLOYEE: Wait a minute, we find out the hard way that the place you want me to go is disease-infested, It's already put one guy in the hospital, and you want me to go there? Do I even get hazardous duty pay?

BOSS: No. Pack your bags.

EMPLOYEE: Shit.

Aside from this is fact that, in many places of the world, Americans are not highly regarded.

BUSINESS TRAVELER: Take me to a computer store. I need to rent a computer to play Everquest.

LOCAL DRIVER: (Nervously) We go to hotel now, yes?

BUSINESS TRAVELER: No, I need to a computer store to--what are all those people over there shouting?

LOCAL DRIVER: Ahh, they shout, 'Death to Clinton and piss on fucking Americans!' (Mob begins to burn a US flag.)

LOCAL DRIVER: You are American, yes?

BUSINESS TRAVELER: (Sweating bullets) Uh, Vat, me? Me Sveedish! To Hotel vee go!

On final analysis, it appears that while it is possible to play Everquest while on a business trip to a foreign country, it's not without difficulty or in extreme cases, assorted dangers. With this in mind, I would give Business Travel four out of five stars for being able to prevent someone from playing Everquest. Toraque Hirsuane, The Rathe Ranger and Business Traveler Darwin, Australia (Currently)
