Lock me up for my own protection - by Tallisker Oban, mage of the 31st circle, Tribunal, White Wolf Clan

I was in my 27th season in my career as a magician, and was feeling quite proud in my flashy new Robe of the Elements. Having finished my business with Joyce in the Temple of Solusek Ro, I thought I would check out Najeena.

I ran into a monk friend inside and we proceeded to kill tentacle terrors for awhile. This was pretty easy, so we decided to go deeper. Being unfamiliar with the dungeon, I followed the monk, who led us right into a pitfall down to the basement. Well, surprise surprise, as soon as I hit the floor in the basement a named ogre guard started pounding the pudding out of my skinny Erudite butt. In a panic, I started to run, but was dead before I had gone far. It was late, and I was bound far away, so I decided to log and get my corpse the next day.

The next day I got a map of Najeena and logged on. The monk had already retrieved his corpse using feign death, but he had died several times in the process. But I had nothing to worry about -- invisibility and summon corpse! So I disappeared and went down to find my corpse. Uh oh, where the heck is it? I don't see it anywhere. Wait a minute, what is that speck of red I see inside that door? It looks sort of like a jail cell. Ah, there is my body! How did it get in there? Oh well, I'll go in and get it.

Hey!! The door is locked!!??! Ok, I'll summon it. Oh oh. . . . Ok, this was a bug, if that door is locked there is no way I could have run in there last night -- I will petition. But then the monk figured it out -- the mobs inside the cell opened the door to come out and kill me when I fell into the basement, and in my panic and confusion I ran through the open doorway right INTO the jail cell. Well, then the fun really began. I borrowed keys from everyone who had any, but none of them fit. I died once or twice when my invisibility wore off in a bad place, and got a good ribbing from my guildmate when he saw 2 of my corpses in the zone. My rogue friend ran all the way from Everfrost to pick the lock, but it didn't work. Eventually 7 people had come to my aid, and we were able to kill the mob who had the key to the mob who had the key to . . . and so on, until I finally got the right key.
