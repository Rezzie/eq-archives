The presidential race (RANT!!!). (2/15/00)

There is oh so much I could write about the race so far, but I am sleepy, so I want to focus on one issue. Let's examine The Wacko Conspiracy Response.

Before McCain clobbered Bush in New Hampshire, things were fine. Bush didn't have to go on the offensive much because he figured he could ride the dollar wave to the convention hall. Then, lo and behold, McCain not only beat Bush but beat Bush like the draft dodging mama's boy that he is (oh, right ... Texas Air National Guard ... whatever ... when you are poor you dodge the draft by going to Canada. When you are wealthy, you get daddy to put you in the guard.)

Anyway, after the New Hampshire debacle, all of the wacko conversative radio hosts and pundits suddenly came upon the real reason McCain won New Hampshire. It wasn't because people LIKED McCain, no. It couldnt be that. They decided that McCain was using a brilliant strategy that I will call the Wacko Conspiracy Response. Here is how it goes ...

1. McCain couldn't possibly win because people like him because if people really liked him, then the Republican establishment, in all of its wisdom, would have picked him as their poster boy, not Bush. After all, the Republican party has their finger on the TRUE pulse of the people.

2. So, since people aren't voting for him because they like him, they must be taking part in a great left wing conspiracy.

3. What the conspiracists are doing is this: They will vote for McCain so that the weakest Republican will go up against Gore in November and Gore will defeat him in a landslide. McCain is that weak Republican.

I will point out to you in a moment why this conspiracy theory is INSANE, but before that, I should point out that Bush is now proposing this EXACT SAME THEORY. He has claimed in recent speeches that democrats are voting in the republican party without any intention to vote for the party's nominee. The underlying message, of course, is that those accursed democrats are trying to blow the election for the republicans.

Now, Bush has bought into the Wacko Theory! I can't believe it!

And why is it wacko? I will give you two reasons. The first reason is a good basic argument against any kind of conspiracy (the "which is more likely?" argument). But the second argument ... oh mama ... just proves how big an idiot these wackos are.

1. This conspiracy theory pretty much assumes that all citizens who vote are just as politically astute, cruel and evil as the politicians themselves. It assumes that farmers, bankers, housewives, etc. sit around in their house and conspire against members of the other party. What these theorists dont realize is that most of these people DON'T GIVE A RAT'S ASS about the internal machinations of party politics. They don't give a shit, generally, what party wins as long as the person who holds their views wins the election. Granted, most people belong to certain parties because those parties share a lot of their views, but your average Joe Bloe owes much more allegiance to the welfare of his job, his family, etc. than he does to some ass sucking corporate political party.

So, which is more likely?

a. Dairy Farmer Joe, scheming amongst his cows, voted for McCain because he can't wait for Gore to beat the shit out of him.

or

b. Dairy Farmer Joe thinks McCain will be the best president for the country.

 

2. Ok, maybe that doesn't convince you. But I know this will. Consider this little tidbit of information:

If the presidential race in November is between McCain and Gore, McCain will beat the ever living shit out of Gore.

If the presidential race in November is between Bush and Gore, it is a toss-up.

Have no doubts about it, people, and you can take this to the bank. McCain, a loud mouthed, anti-establishment, ill tempered war hero will walk up to the schmoozy, scandal plagued "no controlling legal authority" Gore on debate night and rip Gore's eyes out and feed them to him. McCain will CRUSH Gore and Gore knows it (and the polls "prove" it). Hell, I bet Gore will vote for Bush in the primaries to better his chances in the fall!

So, keeping this in mind, why oh why would the great left wing conspiracy vote McCain into November since they all know (and they all know it) that McCain will wipe the stage with Gore's pasty white carcass?

Why?

Because they are not. There is no great left wing conspiracy. Gore is scared to death of McCain; and, lo and behold, the voters in New Hampshire (and elsewhere) have voted and will vote for McCain not because he is the pawn in a democratic power play, but because they think McCain KICKS ASS.

Thank you.

(Oh, by the way, I still think Bush is the odds on favorite for the nomination, mainly because many of the states are closed primaries and caucuses and because many social conservatives like McCain less than Bush, but I believe that if you threw away the parties today and held a three person general election, McCain would win in a landslide. People are sick of the Clintons (ergo Gore) but they are also wary of putting mama boy Bush (who, interestingly, sounds a lot like Clinton with all that compassionate baby slobbering crap) into office. Enter McCain. Americans, as a rule, love big round balls).

 

 

The contest.

Ok, so my hometown rag is sponsoring a fiction contest. I will never win for two reasons:

1. The stories that always win are some sort of despairing suburbanite story involving two paragraph long descriptions of an oppressive vagina and/or father figure. Or they will be a long diatribe about how ironic the author feels it is for him, a one time progressive, to own a SUV. The story will be called "Snot Wheels" and will make clever references to Ken Kesey and the Penthouse forum.

2. I don't take myself way too fucking seriously.

Ok, so this inability to win gives me a wonderful sense of freedom.

But here is the rub: The story must contain the line "Love is an untamed bird" somewhere in the text.

I have thought long and hard and I think this will be the basic outline of the story (these are my ideas! Stay away from them!):

a. The scene opens with the hero in a waiting room. Ever since the beginning of Spring, a crow had been roosting near his home, attacking him whenever he exits. The hero think his sole remaining tuft of red hair on his otherwise bald head may be responsible.

b. While in the office, the Hero sees a pamphlet called "Are you Suicidal?" and he puts it in his pocket.

c. The doctor tends his wounds.

d. When the hero comes home, a TV news crew is waiting on his doorstep. The nosy neighbor had told them about the bird and thought it would make a great human interest story. The hero refuses.

e. The next morning, the hero awakens to find several news crews on his front lawn. They all begin offering large sums of money to get him to leave the house with cameras rolling.

f. While inside, he reads the pamphlet, takes the quiz, and realizes he is, in fact, suicidal. He decides to commit himself.

g. He leaves the front door and the bird tries to attack him but is knocked cold by a wayward boom mike.

h. He drives to the local institution and while inside, he flips through a copy of Reader's Digest. Inside are the winners of the Annual Children's Poem contest. The winner is a poem called "Love is an untamed bird." The poem upsets him.

i. A Christian outreach woman comes up to him and tries to convert him by saying "Jesus loves you." He punches her out cold.

j. He is committed and charged with assault. The papers pick up on the story.

k. Several months later, the hero is discharged and goes door to door selling magazine subscriptions as part of a Felon Rehabilitation Program.

l. The end. The title of the story will be: "Sinners in the Hand of an Angry God."

Update: Hmmm .. I have thought about it for a bit and I wonder if it would be better this way: As he walks into the clinic to commit himself, he sees a local renowned crazy dressed in a white dove costume (with a strip of kudzu stapled to the beak). The dove crazy person hands the hero a pamphlet that says: "Hell is HOT HOT HOT!" The hero punches her out. I am not sure where I can fit the "love is an untamed bird" in.

 

 

Something of interest has happened in my little community that all stock market users may be wise to research. Value America, an online shopping mall that sold everything from computers to dehydrated soup, has laid off hundreds of employees in an attempt to make itself profitable. It has also decided to stick with selling electronics only. Value America lost close to 50 million dollars this last year and with the market already glutted with computer vendors, one could reasonably expect it may lose the remaining 80 million in its bank. Analysts predict that at current trends, the company will be bankrupt by July.

But that isn't really the interesting thing. Here is the interesting thing. The two owners owned all of the shares of stock when the company went public. The public, IPO delirious and technology hungry, bought shares bringing value of the stock to 70 bucks (bought from the owners, of course). The two owners continued to sell stock throughout the year and the shares are now worth 5 bucks. The last massive share sell off came only a month before the mass layoffs. One local paper here is accusing the company of breaking insider trading rules, altho (to be fair), this progressive newspaper (winner of a Cornell journalism award) tends to believe all business is inherently evil, so caveat emptor.

Whether these owners broke any rules or not, it does raise an interesting question (one that more business savvy people may already know): what the fuck? With the stock market as delirious as it is right now, the owners of a business really shouldn't care if their business fails or not. The two owners of Value America are multi multi millionaires. They started an online company, the stock market went nuts, they then sold a huge portion of their shares, laid off close to 600 employees and then ... and here is the good part ... they resigned. They now live on several hundred acres of prime real estate in the horse country of Virginia.

Hmmmmm ... Will this be a trend of things to come? Or is Value America merely an aberration? I honestly don't know the answer but it has me concerned.
