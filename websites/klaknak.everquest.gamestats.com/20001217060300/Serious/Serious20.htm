The Art of Not Being Funny - by the Cackling Klaknak

Where I help Machiavelli reconcile his pro-republic sentiment with his need to keep his job, meet my true love, and explain why I haven't written anything Everquest related in a while.

I had been waiting for the last couple weeks for the first criticisms to come rolling in and finally, yesterday, they appeared on the message boards. Let me quote some:

First of all, Taib ... you are banned from the site.

Second, he has basically summed it up nicely.

I try my best to update the webpage every day (except on weekends when I am working like a dog), but sometimes I simply don't have the muse on my side. This has been especially true since my love for Everquest has dissipated somewhat. Of course, I still have a wart on my fanny about a lot of other things (politics, the Christian Coalition, flavored coffee), so whenever I cant think of something funny to say about Everquest, I simply jump to other topics.

I don't feel really bad about doing this, but I do feel a trifle guilty. I don't think, even on a theme page, that the author should necessarily be confined (confin-ation being the death of all creativity), but I can understand if people coming for EQ humor are a little put off when I start ranting about Ralph Reed.

So let me explain a little bit why I have fallen off the EQ wagon. I have tried to overcome some of these issues, but so far I have failed.

1. Character creation. It is simple, really. I am really tired of playing a spellcaster but I cringe at the idea of not having gate or bind. I don't use gate or bind (usually) to escape monsters; I use these spells so that 80% of my limited playing time is not spent travelling to and fro. This drove me absolutely nuts when I was in a guild. I would come on and everyone would be in Befallen. So I would travel to Befallen. Then I would not play the game for a couple days (or even one day). When I came back, everyone was in Paw. Or the Gorge. Or Freeport, wherever. I found that I was spending all my damn time running back and forth so I could group with people. That is just flat out boring. So what character should I choose? I dunno. I want to whack things with some serious authority, but I don't want to spend all of my time running everywhere.

2. Grouping. I hate grouping. There, I said it. The traditional RPGer inside of me wants to say: "Oh, hey this is neat ... it encourages comradeship, etc." But it is time for me to be honest. I hate grouping. I am a rugged individualist and I resent having to group with people against my will. I want to have the choice. Of all the characters I have considered playing next, the Ranger has been top of my list because I think the Ranger represents what I want my character to be all about. But please see #1.

3. Player Run Economy. I have already beat this horse into the ground, but let me just say it again. The player run economy is stupid.

So there you have it. I am really kinda down on Everquest right now and am trying desperately to rekindle some kind of flame. We will see what happens.

Also, as you all know, Asheron's Call is coming out, but I wonder if my problems with Everquest are going to be found there too. In truth, I wonder if my problems with Everquest are really problems with the multiplayer genre in general. It just seems to me that all the craziness and stupid code changes that come about are not due to the ineptitude of the designers but are due to the greedy idiocy of players.

Yeah, that's right, I said it. I am beginning to wonder if Everquest and its ilk are nothing more than experiments in crowd control. It just has to be so very hard to create a game based on creative choices when you have thousands of people trying to do everything they can to screw the system.

So shame on you!
