Interview

Ok, I have been reading a lot of Brill's Content recently and I decided I needed to get Verant's side of the GM story in order to be fair to both sides (and in order to maintain my account). Shortly after I published the Patrick GM Abuse story, Elder Guide Semiramis emailed me and said he was available if I had any questions. So I asked him several questions and we soon decided it would be better to handle this in interview form. So here it is. Enjoy.

Caveat: This isn't a parody.

The Cackling Klaknak: Who are you and what do you do?

Semiramis: I am Elder Guide Semiramis, the non-Verant volunteer head of the EverQuest Guide Program.

My main duties are to provide Program wide direction and oversight for the Guide Program as a
whole. I also work closely with Verant on Guide Program issues and the overall development of
the Guide Program, and act as a counterpart to the Verant GM-Liaison to facilitate communications
between the Guide Program and Verant.

CK: Could you please outline the guide chain of command?

S: There are four basic layers within the Guide Program. These are:

Apprentice Guide - Apprentice Guides are new to the Guide Program. During their apprenticeship
they are trained and observed by the Guides and Seniors on their server. The training period
typically lasts from two to four weeks after which the person is evaluated and either promoted
to Guide status or released from the Guide Program depending on how they perform. Apprentice
Guides do not have access to the commands that are granted to Guides and higher and must work
under the supervision of a Guide or higher. They are generally limited to answering questions
and learning how to handle player petitions.

Guide - The Guide or "Full Guide" is mainstay of the Guide Program. A Guide is given access
to the bulk of the "tools" that are available to the Guide Program volunteers. These tools
include the ability to perform corpse retrievals and resurrections as well as other common
tasks the Guides are called upon to do on a day to day basis. The Guides handle immediate
issues, answer game play questions, and are responsible for the resolution of common problems
that players encounter. They also mentor and train Apprentice Guides and assist in the
execution and administration of dynamic quests.

Senior Guide - The Senior Guides are the server level management of the Guide Program. A
Senior Guide is a proven Guide that has shown good judgement and is willing to invest additional
time in the Program. Senior Guides handle the more difficult problems that players encounter
on the server including problems escalated by the Apprentices and Guides on their server. Seniors
handle investigation of exceptional incidents such as reports of exploitation or harassment.
The Senior Guides also work as an administrative team for their server providing a communication
focal point for their server. Many of the Senior Guides also participate as a member of a
focus group or team within the Guide Program that concentrates on providing a more streamlined
decision making process on a specific area of concentration within the Program.

Elder Guide - The Elder Guide is the consulting and Program level management of the Guide
Program. In this role I work to provide direction and oversight at a Program wide level.
I work directly with the Verant Customer service management and Lead GMs to ensure the
welfare of the Guide Program as a whole. The Elder Guide is the highest point of escalation
within the Guide Program.

CK: What do you think is behind the perception that many GMs abuse the system?

S: Unfortunately, I think that simple misunderstanding of the roles of the Guides and Verant GMs
can be attributed to this misperception. At times I also think that the players sheer passion
for the game can lead to an unfounded perception that the GMs are abusing their power.

One of the first things that we have to be aware of is that there are two different
organizations that are commonly referred to as "GMs". There is the Guide Program which is a
large organization made up of volunteers from the EverQuest player base and there is the
Verant GM organization. Verant GMs are paid employees of Verant Interactive that provide
customer service. These two organizations work very closely together as one large team to
provide the customer service within EverQuest.

CK: How valid is this perception?

S: I would have to say that there is very little validity in this claim. Both Verant and
the Guide Program are very serious when it comes to reports and cases of abuse of power in
the GM and Guide ranks. If there is any report of abuse against a GM or Guide it is investigated
thoroughly and if found to be valid, the individual perpetrating the abuse is removed from their
position.

CK: Will you take any steps to help eliminate this perception?

S: Understanding seems to be the key to the whole perception issue. Hopefully through interviews like this one will help clear some of the misunderstandings.

CK: If a player believes a GM is abusing the system in some way, what is the exact procedure he needs
to follow to report this abuse?

S: In any case where a player believes they have been witness to a GM abusing the system, the
player should use the /report command to capture any text that may provide evidence of the
abuse. The /report command sends the last 10 lines displayed on the text window to a file
on the server, ensuring that this text cannot be tampered with or modified in any way. These
files can only be accessed by Verant staff so that they can be used as evidence. Screen shots
can not be used as evidence due to the ease with which they can be forged.

Once the player has captured the text using /report they should then /petiton the support
staff to make them aware of the situation.

CK: Ok, I am going to be a hard ass. Are you basically saying that if a GM aids a player in any way that is not loggable, there is no way in the world he would ever be caught to the degree needed to confirm the action?

S: Actually, it is far from that. There are logs of all transactions between characters. There really aren't any activities that cannot be verified by Verant.

CK: What are some of the more common complaints you receive about GMs?

S: I would have to say that the most common complaint I see is along the lines of "GM or
Guide X did not replace my lost items when I died due to Y." The variations in "Y"
are absolutely huge!

CK: What helps you judge whether or not a petition against an alleged GM abuse is valid?

S: There is usually a large amount of investigation involved when a complaint about a
GM or Guide is received. This investigation includes contacting both the customer and
the GM or Guide in question and gathering information on both sides of the situation.
Log files that are maintained by Verant are also used to validate the claims.
This is a tremendous over-simplification of the process.

CK: Would it be possible for a rogue GM to give an apprentice more powers?

S: No, this isn't possible without changing the Guide's designation in the /who all
listing. If you see a Guide that is listed as an "Apprentice Guide", that defines
the limit of their abilities. This is also true of the other levels within the
Guide Program.

CK: Have any GMs ever been disciplined?

S: Yes, we have had cases of Guides being disciplined and even removed from their positions.
I will note though that these cases are relatively rare. As far as I know there has never
been the need to discipline a Verant GM for an abuse of power or authority.

give them some information that might help them view future cases such as this in a

When I read the e-mail I found that I had several questions, many of which were also

CK: Why was Verant's naming policy recently rewritten?

S: As EverQuest evolves and the player base grows we find that some policies need to
be revised, updated, or even completely rewritten. EverQuest is quite unique in
that it is a constantly evolving entity. A living world as it were. This fact
alone will bring about policy changes as we move forward.

CK: What are some of the major changes or revisions?

S: The main changes were put in place in an attempt to make the rules less ambiguous.
The rules were rewritten to be more clear and concise.

There were also rules added to govern when a name or surname can be changed by a
customer service representative (Senior Guides or higher) and to give some basic
rules for surnames.

CK: Do you basically just ignore bad names until someone petitions them?

S: We do not actively hunt down bad names. This is not to say that if a Guide happens to see a person with a name that is in blatant violation of the naming policies and no one has petitioned about that name they won't contact the person and change their name.

CK: Do you think Euthanasia is an offensive name? Does it violate the common word rule?

S: We stumble into a very gray area here. Is "Euthanasia" offensive? That question will be answered differently by different people, as you well know. Personally, I do not find the word offensive. Do my views on the word preclude it from being offensive? That is a resounding no. The diversity of the players in EverQuest is one of its most amazing qualities. This diversity also has to be taken into account when we handle a situation such as this. In a case like "Euthanasia" the Guides would usually discuss this before making a name change. If there was a consensus that the name could be found offensive, the player's name would be changed.

S: I actually have a couple that I really like. I am a Ranger at heart. My first
character in EverQuest, back in beta, was a human Ranger. I also like playing
High-Elf casters. Lately though, as I said earlier, I've been playing a gnome
Mage who I've become quite fond of!

S: It was found that pottery was the craft that was going to take Norrath by storm.
I hear rumor that Bill Gates was actually talking about investing heavily in
Norrathian pottery and that...

On a serious note, if there were one word associated with EverQuest that I
would love to see abolished it would be the word "nerf". Verant is constantly
faced with the inexorable fact that you cannot please everyone. I've been playing
EverQuest since early Phase 1 beta and I can honestly say that I have never seen
a change in the game that was made to specifically target a class or skill for its
destruction, contrary to popular belief. The changes they make in the game are
always made in an effort to improve the overall game play and player experience.
If a change seems to effect your favorite class in a negative way, you might want
to broaden your view of the game a bit and see what impact that change has on
a more global scale.

CK: Are there any other issues you want to address?

S: One thing that I see quite a bit of is that the Guides and GMs are rarely seen in a
good light. When a player petitions a Guide or GM they are usually not happy. In
the vast majority of the cases, when their petition is resolved, they walk away happy
with the resolution and nothing is ever heard of the situation. If you take the number
of petitions handled each and every day by the Guides and GMs and compare that number
to the complaints you see on the message boards and web sites, you'll find that the
ratio of positive to negative experiences is very high. Happy players are not vocal
players.

If you have a positive experience with a Guide or GM, please, voice those experiences
as well. The volunteers in the Guide Program are there because they like to help
other players enjoy EverQuest, they do this out of a true passion for the game. Let
them know when they are doing things right too, it is definitely appreciated!
