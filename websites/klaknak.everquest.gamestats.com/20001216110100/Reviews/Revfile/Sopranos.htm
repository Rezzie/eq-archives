The Sopranos

Before the HBO show "The Sopranos" hit the airways, there was only one reason to have cable: ESPN and Emmanuelle in Space. Now, HBO has introduced us to what one of my friends called "arguably the best television show of the decade, maybe of the century."

I am not sure if it is the best show of the century, but it is most certainly the best television drama of the decade. For those who are somehow deluded into thinking that ER or the Practice is a great television drama, watch a couple episodes of the the Sopranos to realize how truly crappy they are. With the Sopranos, we are seeing the dawning of a new age of quality television. People are finally ... finally ... realizing that for a show to be truly great, it needs to be able to expand beyond what network rules and small minds allow. HBO is leading the way.

It is hard to accurately describe why this show is great, but I will give you two reasons:

1. James Gandolfini - For those who have seen True Romance, Gandolfini was the ever smiling yet deeply menacing hitman who confronted Arquette in the hotel room towards the end of the movie. In the Sopranos, he carries himself in a similar vein. On the surface, he is almost a Sicilian Santa Claus with jovial dimples and a twinkling eye. But when he gets pissed ... shit. It is a thing to behold. Gandolfini won the Television Critics Award for outstanding achievement already, and if there is a God, he will win the Emmy as well.

2. The final scene of the first season's last episode. I will not ruin it for you out there, but Gandolfini's confrontation with his mother, all hyperbole aside, is one of the best scenes ever to be printed to film. Period.
