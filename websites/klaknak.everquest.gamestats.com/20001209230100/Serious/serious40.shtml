The Klaknak's Easily Digestible
Guide to Astute Politicking

by the Cackling Klaknak

"All life is politics."

What does this mean? This means that politics is really nothing more than human stupidity writ large (with colorful stickers).

So, before you start yawning and reaching for the back button on your navigator, at least consider that by understanding idiocy on a grand scale, you may come to understand idiocy in your own neighborhood and in your own mind.

And so, without any further muckety muck, The Klaknak's Easily Digestible Guide to Astute Politicking (for the newbie political junkie).

1. Read Michael Shermer's Why People Believe Weird Things: Pseudoscience, Superstition & Other Confusions of Our Time.

Commentary: The obvious reason to read this book is because it is basically a primer on the science of logical fallacy. When I read this for the first time, I felt philosophically refreshed and began viewing things with a renewed skepticism that I had somehow lost since graduating from college. The not so obvious reason to read this book is to read Shermer's scathing indictment of Ayn Rand and her cult of objectivists. If there is anything I hate more than reading the self-righteous, hyper-defensive chunky spews of a christian fundamentalist, it is reading the self-righteous, hyper-defensive chunky spews of objectivists and other paragons of bubble gum "rationalist" philosophy who claim that reason is capable of explaining everything and yet cannot explain why reason is not capable of explaining anything. Shermer really is quite the subversive, because he lures these hyper-rationalists to his book by discounting UFOs, superstition and the like and then he utterly destroys these soppers of unoriginal thought by calling Ayn Rand the leader of a cult every bit as suspect as Heaven's Gate or the most snivelling of snake handlers.

2. Watch Inside Politics (5:00EST, CNN)

Commentary: Inside Politics is fairly bubble gum for the hard core, but it boasts some of the best talking heads in the mainstream political commentating business. Here is a breakdown:

Bernie Shaw: a muffin head

Judy Woodruff: another muffin head

Jeff Greenfield: this guy should become a high school civics professor because that is all he is good for. Here is a selection from one of his biting political commentaries on "the uneasiness factor": "What's most intriguing is the possibility that the uneasiness factor might play a significant role this year. So far, Americans have expressed a level of confidence and contentment rare in recent decades. If the stock market roller-coaster continues, it may make that factor the single most significant element in November." This insight is akin to a geologist going on the Discovery Channel and claiming that "rocks are hard." I understand that CNN is trying to reach a mass audience, but jesus christ, Greenfield must die. He is also a pompous ass. William F. Buckley can get away with this because he is pretty goddamn smart. But a pompous buffoon is one of the most tragic creatures in the known universe. It's sad, really.

Robert Novak: I just really like this guy. I hardly agree with anything he says, but his raccoon complexion and extremely white teeth is an icon of US Politics in all its horrific glory. He also has the terrible habit of often being right and he is one of the few Republican pundits who will admit openly that the Republicans have been fucking up royally for the last 5 or 6 years. I have often wondered myself if Clinton could be called a political genius when his opponents are political morons.

Margaret Carlson/Tucker Carlson: Margaret is your basic knee-jerk liberal. Tucker is your basic smirking, bow tie wearing conservative Yalie. But when they come together, truth almost always overcomes ego. Margaret will say "Bush really screwed up" and Tucker will say "He sure did." And vice versa (inserting Gore where appropriate). Wow.

Bill Press: He only rarely appears on Inside Politics, but he typifies the kind of partisan analysis that typifies most political programs. Al Gore could take a hamster and bite its head off on public tv and Bill Press would defend it after proving it never happened.

Bruce Morton: He is the Kuralt of politics looking for weird stories and angles and often finding truth therein.

I want to close this discussion with CNN's two crown jewels: Bill Schneider and E.L. Dionne.

Bill Schneider: Bill Schneider's Political Plays of the Week is some of the most insightful commentary you will ever hear in mainstream political discussion. I cannot praise this guy enough.

E.L. Dionne: Dionne is a GOD. Even though he is a pretty proud liberal, he is extremely fair and believes, as I do, that politics can (CAN) be a noble endeavor. When commentators ask Dionne questions, he doesnt start spewing verbal farts out his ass, he actually delves deep into statistics and facts and does so calmly and without even the slightest shred of an ego. If pompous idiots are the sum total of human fallibility, then unassuming geniuses like Dionne are treasures we would be stupid to ignore.

3. Force yourself to watch one hour of a Senate subcommittee meeting a week.

Commentary: If this doesn't piss you off about the impotence of our political system, nothing will.

4. Read as much Michael Moorcock as you can.

Commentary: Sure, this guy writes fantasy novels, but they are overwhelmingly political. Moorcock has done more to shape my political views than almost anyone else. A common theme: Most of the world's great holocausts and stupidities (in both fantasy and real world realms) are the result of a people's or a hero's blind, unbending devotion to a theory. In our rational world, theories are so deified that they can justify the most horrible acts ever committed by one man against another. Moorcock also believes that human happiness and order must be a balancing act between absolute freedom (chaos) and unbending doctrine (order). This may seem overly obvious, but I think this is absolutely the core issue that drives every single debate in America today.

5. Watch the History Channel.

Commentary: I would much rather you read a good book on history than watch a 60 minute documentary, but I am a realist (and a trifle bored with 2000 page histories myself).

* * *

Finally, I want to include the three most important resources on my list. Even if you don't do any of the other things, at least do one of the following:

6. Read P.J. O'Rourke's Parliament of Whores.

Commentary: No book best exemplifies the phrase: "it's funny because it is true." O'Rourke says he is a conservative (although he sounds more libertarian to me), but if you think he is one of these moralistic conservatives we see running around today, think again. How many moralistic conservatives would name an entire section: "Our Government: What the Fuck Do They Do All Day, and Why Does It Cost So Goddamned Much Money?" Here are some other chapters from his book (with subtitles). Doing the Most Important Kind of Nothing - the Supreme Court Graft for the Millions - Social Security Protectors of a Blameless Citizenry - The Bureaucracy Defense Policy - Cry "Havoc!" and Let Slip the Hogs of Peace Poverty Policy - How to Endow Privation (bwahhaha!) And finally, one of my favorite quotes from his book: "(commenting on the 1988 presidential primary race) And Jesse Jackson - if you listen to what he says rather than the way he says it - sounds like Fidel Castro's Jiminy Cricket."

7. Read E.J. Dionne's Why Americans Hate Politics.

Commentary: This book is freaking brilliant. I am in awe of this man. Some people may find this book a little dry, but I have read no better commentary on why absolutely nothing of any import ever gets done in Washington (hint: nonexistent divisions on the issues are made existent for the sake of political parlay ... woo hoo!).

8. Find one issue and become active.

Commentary: I am the hardest of the hard political junkies and I cannot even keep up with a fraction of the stuff that goes on in politics. I can only imagine how overwhelmed the politically bored must feel. But surely you can find at least one issue and start studying it and start acting on it. Try to avoid the big, Time magazine issues like abortion, gun control, etc. if you can and look for issues that are equally important but often slip beneath the radar. Naturally, if these big issues are the things you are passionate about, dont let me stop you, but if you think certain issues on abortion or gun control piss you off, you aint seen nothing yet. These issues are out in the open where politicians must be circumspect and coy. It is not until you delve into the world of pork, political financing, agriculture, tort reform, and other issues that you find the true source of ... christ, there is not a word foul enough to describe what goes on in these areas of our government.

Enjoy.
