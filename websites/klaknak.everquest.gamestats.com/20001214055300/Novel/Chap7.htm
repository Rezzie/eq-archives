Career Choices

I had passed out again. Vaguely, I wondered if passed out was spelled "passed" or "past". I seemed to recall "past" relating to time and "passed" relating to "passed my cultural diversity requirement by having a psychiatrist who was from Spain" but ... I have lost my train of thought.

I had passed out.

Again, visions appeared within my mind.

Vision 1:

We are driven by the belief that people of faith have a right and a responsibility to be involved in the world around them. That involvement includes community, social and political action. Our goals include: Strengthening the family; Protecting innocent human life; Returning education to local and parental control; Easing the tax burden on families; Punishing criminals and defending victims' rights; Protecting young people and our communities from the pollution of pornography; Defending the institution of marriage; Protecting religious freedom

Vision 2:

In many large cities around the world, you'll find a community of people who groove on Balkan music and dance. In Seattle, they meet at 9 PM every Friday night at the Greenlake VFW, 7220 Woodlawn Ave NE. There are usually 30 or more friendly people for Balkan dancing, and there's live music of some sort about once a month. $4 donation to pay the rent. Visit if you're in town!

Vision 3:

I was part of something larger than myself. I saw in front of me a billion singing spheres, silently singing "My Mother Cricket" to a chorus of celestial harmonicas. And yet, even with the infinite number of spheres, the vibrations seemed to create a kind of simpler pattern, just as spinning tires can sometimes create the illusion of movement in the opposite direction. On my left ... a black sphere. On my right ... OUCH! What the fuck!

"Wake up!" the voice commanded me.

I opened my good eye and saw a pair of finely tailored brown slacks. They appeared nice enough to wear to a sherry party at the home of the local university president's fundraising barbecue and yet casual enough to use as a filter for pure grain alcohol shots.

"Wake up, my fine warrior."

It occurred to me that I had misplaced my sandwich.

I started to pass out.

As I tried to slip into oblivion, two muscular hands grabbed my lapels (umm ..) and pulled me onto the deck of a small, but elegantly blue deck.

"Ah, thanks ... I ..."

The warrior in brown slacks hit me hard in the stomach.

I fell over and began to ...

"Oh no you don't ..."

And the Warrior in Brown Slacks grabbed me and forced my head into the lap of a nearby Lap Fish.

My head cleared.

The Warrior threw me to the deck.

"I am sick of your snivelling!" he bellowed.

I started to pass out ...

He punched me in the stomach again.

"Stop it!" I yelled.

"You are the Fungible Champion!" he shouted. "It is time you started acting like one!"

"Whoa," I said. "What?"

The Warrior in Brown Slacks sighed.

"Perhaps I have spoken too soon," he said.

"Where is my sandwich ..."

"SILENCE!"

The Warrior sat on a small chair near ... the part of the ship that ... um .. juts out.

"You are the One Who Travels the Multiverse," he said. "You must fight for the cosmic balance. Sometimes you fight for this side, other times ... for that side. It is a lonely business."

I considered this for a minute.

"Yes," the Warrior said, pulling a long piece of information technology from his pocket and lighting it from his tinder box. "You are the warrior who helps determine the cosmic balance."

"The cosmic balance?" I asked. "What is that?"

"Precisely," he said.

"Eh?

"You are the warrior who strives to achieve the balance between This and That. It is an important task."

I frowned.

"I don't understand. This and That? What this and that?"

"This and That!" he said. "It couldn't be simpler!"

"What do you mean?" I asked.

The Warrior smirked.

"Well, if you don't know, I don't think I could tell you ..."

He then laughed, and millions of light years away, crowds of idiots cheered.

"But that doesn't make any sense," I said. "This and That? What the hell?"

The warrior sighed.

"Millions of philosophers throughout the millenia have tried to reconcile difference of all kinds concerning any number of subjects," he said. "They all thought their material was well thought out and of universal importance. But they were wrong. The only thing that matters ... is This and That. You are either for This or you are for That. Everything else ... is window dressing."

I looked for my sandwich.

"And you are the one charged with balancing the two," he said.

"AH," I said. "And how am I to do that?"

The Warrior frowned.

"I am not sure," he said. "The Interesting Ones pulled you from your world to enter this one ... as Gayfeather ... but you have failed totally."

"Agreed!" I said. I pulled some letters from my pocket.

"We haven't decided yet how you can achieve your goals here in the land of Norrath. We are not sure what is the This and what is the That."

A .. B ... C ... bwah. I can't believe I agreed to deliver these things ...

"Those vowels are valuable," the warrior said, eyeing my letters greedily.

I put the letters back in my pocket.

"Oh no," I said. "You won't get these until you explain to me what I should be. What I am. What I am supposed to do."

"I told you," the Warrior said gruffly. "You are to make the balance between This and That. Gayfeather is obviously a failure. Perhaps you should seek a different identity."

"A different identity?" I asked.

"Yes," he said. "A different identity. Trust your fellow man, he said. Ask them what they think."

I sighed.

"How do I do that?" I asked.

The Warrior in Brown Slacks smiled and looked up at the sun.

"Ask!" he said.

I looked into the sky.
