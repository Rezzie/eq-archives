"Dear Ms. Thunderhammer
I write to you regarding a problem plaguing many females of Norrath. Being a wood elf warrior, I yearn for nothing but to prove myself to my peers in battle. However, I've noticed a large number of male warriors do not take me seriously, and, often, make crude comments about my appearance. I opted for a bronze breastplate early in my life, thinking the high neck would deter some comments. Might it be the leather pants? Or do human men have a thing for the pointy ears? My question is, Tall One, how may I prove my might as a warrior among a gender-biased community?
signed
pretty warrior"

Dear pretty warrior,
First off I will try and contain my jealousy about how good you little wood elves look in your outfits. As far as proving yourself in battle, I do have some advice for you and this is from my experience. There are a couple of ways to change the opinions of the men of Norrath to put fear in their hearts instead of fire in their loins. The first is to prove yourself given the game mechanics of a warrior. Nothing makes a guy say, ‘Holy Crap!’ like a big critical for 100’s of points of damage or tanking a hill giant. I get exclamations all the time when people see me critical or shrug off 100 point double attacks. Secondly, wield the biggest, scariest weapon you can find. A lot of the new kunark weapons are truly frightening in both their appearance and damage dealing capabilities. I get comments all the time on how scary my executioner’s axe looks. Third, prove yourself in battle like a true warrior. Take command of any battle, issue orders, protect those wussy casters, and always be the last to flee or zone. The guys will note your courage in battle, believe me. My motto on my character bio is ‘I take the damage so you don’t have to.’ Lastly, and this may be the most difficult, is to not act feminine. I mean, honestly, what true warrior born, male or female acts feminine? Leave that stuff to the casters. Swill beer between battles, curse a lot, do a lot of offense emotes, and tell potential suitors that you will only accept a man if he can best you in a duel. I guarantee that this will eliminate almost all of the lame pickup lines, come ons, and offensive emotes you receive in-game. And even those that you do receive will be done with respect and just a little fear. And as all women know, having your man fear you for more than just a couple days a month is a good thing.
- Cyanna Thunderhammer
