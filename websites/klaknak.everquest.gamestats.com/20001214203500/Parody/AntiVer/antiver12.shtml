Answers Volume V - 2.3.00

This issue of "Arch-Mage Answers" is all Bards, all the time. Each question here was taken directly from our message boards. I want to thank everyone who submitted the many hundred questions. I obviously couldn't answer them all, but I think we got a pretty good sampling. And so, without further ado

* * *

Q: A few months after release, there was a speed cap put on Selo's due to cheaters. Was this Intentional?
A: When our speed cap was put on, it was discovered that Bards with exceptional magic drums were breaking the speed limit that we had set back in Beta. In effect, they were moving faster then the fastest we ever planned for players to move. So yes, the speed cap for cheaters brought their speed down a small percentage.

Editor's note: we wouldn't want people to move around at a REASONABLE pace, that would take the challenge out of the game.

* * *

Q: Charm limited to level 37 and below mobs. Our charm is already very limiting, why the level limit?
A: We are very careful which classes we give charm to in EQ - so much so that we actually removed the ability from some classes post Beta. Bards are the only class, other then the Enchanter, which can charm all types of creature.

We are currently kicking around another higher level Bard Charm song for one of the empty level slots. If we do put it in, it will be different from Solon's - but still cool

translation: we nerfed all the caster classes after beta, we'll blow some smoke up your ass about a better charm song so that you stop annoying us.

Q: A Bard's Dodge caps at 120, and they get no parry or riposte. Why? (Class Balance is a fine answer, just checking)
A: Bards have much better, and much more unique, magical abilities then those of any other Hybrid class. Because of this, their melee skills are not as good as the typical Hybrid. Bards melee still better then any non-melee class - their offense, weapon skills, hit points and two-weapon fighting are superior to that of any priest or magi type.

translation: bards don't use mana, because of this their melee skills suck ass. Bards melee better than wizards, they also get kewl fighting animations.

Q: Using some Magic instruments, for example, the Darkwood Piccolo, have no effect.
A: High Quality Instruments always bump up the effect of a song. Some songs, however, don't have effects that can be bumped up - for example, Tarew's Aquatic Ayre gives the ability to breathe water. Having a magical instrument will not make you breathe water "better." The Piccolo, a wind instrument, has the least number of songs that have effects which can be increased. Most Wind songs tend to be all or nothing.

translation: we thought it would work, but after we spilled coffee on the software for the piccolo before uploading it, we had to come up with some bullshit effects fast to compensate.

Q: I have never seen a "Your target has resisted your attempt to mesmerize it" while using lullaby, as per your post. When should I?
A: I believe that went in last patch. If not, it should be in this one. You will see the resist message when your target resists your song. This not showing was a display error - it should have been fixed long ago. It took a while because when some of us (not all of us, just some) play, we use a version of the client that provides more information/messaging. This lets us catch a lot of bugs that most people might not see. It makes tracking down bugs a lot easier, quicker, and more efficient. On the downside, it sometimes isn't always easy to remember which messages everybody sees, one which only we see. This was one of the few cases where too much information caused the problem.

Q: While I understand the idea that Druidic and Shaman SOW effects cannot be used indoors (I even understand the Journeyman boots), I don't understand why the Bard running song not working indoors.
A: Game Balance. No spell or effect that speeds up a character will work indoors. Period.

During Beta we found that indoor use of movement increasing spells and songs led to trains on a massive scale. When we took out the movement effect songs, the amount of trains happening in dungeons significantly decreased. This was one, of many, reasons that we will never have movement increasing spells or songs able to be cast in dungeons.

translation: if you don't have feign death your screwed

Q: Denon's Desperate Dirge - is it really that "desperate?" After all, the Bard does 300 damage to himself to do 300 damage to all MOBS in a certain radius around him. That's really cool, except 300 damage is absolutely nothing. Casters have been doing that since level 30, and Bards get a song that does that at level 43. Plus, this one actually uses all the Bard's mana so it's a one shot deal.
A: The 300 damage is only done if you target something near yourself - this is an AoE song, and like all other AoE effects, if the caster stands too close, they get hit with their own effect. Ask any Wizard. As for the amount of damage dealt - Bards do not have large damage songs - their songs are more of a gradual thing, dealing damage over time. This song breaks the rules for Bards - it deals a large (for them) amount of damage instantly. This is why they can sing it very infrequently (i.e. it takes up all their mana).

Bards were actually never intended to have any songs that took mana. Using mana on this one was just a convenient way to mark off time for how often they could use the song.

translation: bards should wait 30 minutes before doing 300 damage, to do otherwise would be unbalancing

Q: You mentioned previously that adding an extra tick to bard DoT songs would make them 50% more powerful. Why not simply add to the damage each tick does instead?
A: Solid suggestion.

The reason that we did not increase the damage for Bard songs is that, honestly, most Bards don't kite with their DoT songs. The Bellow is an instant - so it is unaffected by movement / kiting. Chords and such are good songs - but we do not want to increase the damage that they deal every tick for the 5% or so of the time that movement might lower their damage.

Bards are also the only class that can move and sing/cast a song at the same time. Every other class must be standing still before they can produce a magical effect. Increasing the damage done by Bard songs, purely for purposes of making them even better Kiters, is against the spirit of the reason that we put in the anti-kiting code in the first place.

editor's note: thou art a scurvy gnave, thou shalt repent for taking away kiting!!
