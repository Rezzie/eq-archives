Verant: On Crack or Taking Charge?

by the Cackling Klaknak

This hasn't been the best month for Verant.

Although there are some players who think Verant has been the embodiment of evil since Everquest became a germ of an idea, most believe, as I do, that Everquest has been a pretty awesome experience overall.

But what has happened recently?

While I was running around to different websites hocking my latest humor material, I came across all kinds of EQ craziness I had neglected to notice before (due to my recent Asheron's Call play).

Here are some recent events for you to ponder

1. Verant starts off the month of hell by announcing that its new EULA will give Verant the right to sniff our computers for programs like ShowEQ. Many customers, myself included, went absolutely apeshit ballistic. Verant recanted.

2. Verant also declares all Ebay-style auctioning a no-no. This displeases some, but pleases most, myself included.

3. As part of an April Fool's joke, Verant nerfs the test server. Mostly, some losers get really upset, but secretly some people (including myself) wonder if Verant should be working on EQ's numerous problems instead of screwing around in the test server with April's Fools jokes. The actions also seem to be a not so subtle finger-in-the-eye to those players who take issues like game balance very seriously. It seemed ... a little vindictive ... instead of funny.

4. The minions at ShowEQ discover that humans do NOT get a racial experience boost, contrary to what the Official guidebook states. The powers at Verant reply in a very Clintonesque way, saying that they had no control over the guidebook. Eventually, Smed (I think) said that humans may seem to level faster because they dont suffer racial penalties which makes sense to me, at least. I just kinda wonder why he hadn't mentioned it earlier.

5. Player accounts are banned for improper station names. According to news reports, the players were not given the opportunity to change their name. Verant says, once again, that this was not really their mistake but that they are looking into it.

6. Players are banned for selling their accounts and whatnot on Ebay and related services.

7. And finally, in a crushing blow to Verant, it was revealed that some customers would not receive their upgrade shipments on time. Verant promises to give these people a free month of EQ but some question whether this will recoup shipping costs.

So, let's return to the previous question.

Is Verant on crack or are they taking charge?

I think it is easy to look at these past events and see them as some sort of incredible meltdown in the glorious home office, but I think it is important to consider a few things.

First, not all of these issues are really branches from the same tree. Some of the problems were the result of really bad PR work, some were from Verant's good faith attempt to lock down on exploiters in the game and some were just flat out bad mistakes. Now, you could reasonably argue that since it is the same people running this crazy farm that all of these problems are the same, but I think in some cases Verant is acting in good faith and in other cases they are not. I think there is definitely a difference, although if I was a hoary old school stock holder I would probably be screaming for Smedley's head on a plate right about now.

I think Verant's attempt to crack down on hackers or losers with stupid names is a good faith effort to keep EQ from turning into the sewage dump that is Ultima Online. OSI really didn't give a rat's ass about most of these issues and as a result it saw its populations slowly fall as other games came available on the market. Verant is trying to protect EQ from a similar fate. Do you think it is a coincidence that all of this new stuff is going on at the same time UO is releasing its new, "people friendly" expansion to UO? I think not.

The problem, however, is that the PR people, who happen to be the game developers and servants, must have learned PR work from a team of gorillas. Never in my life have I seen so many gross misjudgments and missteps. I find it almost impossible to believe that someone on the team didnt think that the new EULA clause regarding sniffing might cause a little bit of a ruckus. Is Verant that out of touch with everyday events? In a year where Amazon.com was universally booed for patenting its one click schtick and congresses and federal agencies the nation over are seriously looking into how companies may be violating consumer privacy, Verant charges ahead on this sensitive issue, and ends up getting clobbered. This was one of the most braindead decisions I have seen in a long time.

The latest mistake (and possibly a deathblow for some customers) is this shipping mistake. In normal companies, this would signal the end of the careers for probably half of management. But in game companies, I am not sure how this works. This is just such a collosal (albeit more traditional) business screw up that Verant will be paying for this one for months. Everytime a little problem comes up from now on, this shipping fiasco will be used, justifiably or not, as an example of Verant's continued foibles.

Because what Verant is suffering from, more than anything, is a horrible image problem and some of this image problem is actually pretty justifiable. It wasn't losers alone who complained about the EULA and it isn't losers alone complaining about the shipping problems. Now, I am the first person to admit that most people are probably happy with the game and the vocal minority are the ones trying to bring it down, but with the EULA and now the shipping fiasco, I wonder if some of this discontent is seeping into the warm and fuzzy majority that never speaks.

Finally, the real shame is this: Verant has basically ZERO room to wiggle if Kunark has even the slightest problem when it goes live. People will already be hypersensitive to even the smallest snafus; and a big snafu, like a major server outage, could curse EQ for months and months to come. If Kunark turns out to work fairly well, I think most people will forget the events of the last month and focus on the new continent. But if there is a major outage or problem, many will probably wonder, as I will wonder, if the people who run Verant really are incompetent, like the vocal minority has been suggesting for months now.
