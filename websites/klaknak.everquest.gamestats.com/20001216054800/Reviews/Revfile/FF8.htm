Final Fantasy 8 by Gavin Sith

Final Fantasy 8 Review I got FF8 on 9-8-99 (Babbage's is the ultimate gaming store, they get everything a day early), and I have been dying to do a nice, lengthy review for it. Well, I concede to you, O Amazing-Klaknak-Whose-Comments-On-Satanic-Computer-Games-Had-Me-Sprawled-Out-On-The-Floor-Laughing, please publish this review of FF8, for we must warn everyone of the dangers it brings (dangers such as eating away your EQ'ing time ).

Anyway, as I said earlier, I am writing a lengthy review, and those who don't want to read it all can immediately scroll down to the bottom for my two word review. If I don't forget that is, see, this will take me a little while to write this and I most likely will forgot everything I said up here, but we'll see.

Now, onto the game itself. Personally, FF7 was my second favorite console game ever (Metal Gear Solid was my first). I loved the materia system, the story, the characters, the battle engine, everything about it, and I am happy to report that FF8 both improves on FF7 and innovates and creates many new features that I think everyone will love. First, I'll start with the story, as in necessary of every review in existence. You play Squall Leonhart, a student at a place called Garden. There you learn about combat and train to be a mercenary. If you pass your field exam where you actually are sent on a mission, you become a SeeD, a person who can be contracted to do mercenary work. I don't want to spoil it so I'll about leave it at here, but along the way you meet many colorful characters as well as the woman who captures your heart. One quick note though, I love the story much more than FF7 or nearly any other RPG (Xenogears and FF8 are about equal storyline-wise). It has a very military theme with the mercenary aspect, and it appeals to a much more mature audience, in my opinion.

Anyway, with that aside, on to the game! Second, the graphics. Wow! Amazing! Fantastic! Great! Incredible! I'd say more but I have a crappy thesaurus and my mind works better thinking of synonyms for words and phrases such as "inkie," "foul beast," and "corrupt militia." Anyway, the graphics are great. I think they are the best pre-rendered backgrounds on PSX (for the un-technically enlightened, those are the backgrounds that look real pretty, and they don't move because they just stay there, which is why they can be so pretty), and while the polygonal characters are great, Legacy of Kain: Soul Reaver has to set the new standard for 3D graphics on PSX. Back to the polygonal characters for a sec, though. Guess what? Squall, Seifer, Rinoa . . . they look like HUMAN BEINGS! Amazing, ain't it? Gone are the super-deformed heads, short bodies, and hair two times the actually height of the person (the subject of many of my nightmares)! It also makes the story much more believable, because I can understand the sorrow, love, and real human emotions that people who look like human beings could be facing. On the other hand, when you look like you just came out from being under an iron, it's much less serious.

Then there are the FMV's. Squaresoft is the FMV master. An FMV is obviously a priority to them, and it shows. Bah to those who don't think FMV is immersive! I think it is MORE immersive! I stare at the TV with my mouth gaping open, and if it is a particularly long RPG, a puddle of saliva is usually at my feet. When it is an in-game cut scene or I am doing it, I actually have to think about what I am doing, and sometimes I feel it detracts from the experience, I can't concentrate what's at hand. While discovering the story through dialogue is wonderful, I still love FMV as much as the next gamer.

Third, the sound. Pretty well done. I'm not going to stay on this topic long. Some music I really like, other I hate. I liked FF7's music much more though.

Fourth, gameplay. Ah, the real meat of the game. This will keep you interested for a long time. We all have different descriptions of what this is, and for me it is the battle engine, control, magic system, etc. The battle engine is the basic combo of real-time/turn-based. You can't cast, attack, use item, etc. until your little bar fills up. Sometimes I would revert to my other RPG's and concentrate on a well-thought out attack plan when an enemy attacks and I go "Huh? What? Oh, right, this is FF8." I like both systems a lot, though, and a happy medium of both is nice. The magic system, now THAT is something I like! While the Draw System (brief explanation: magic is treating as an item basically. You get magic from enemies: use the draw command and get a random number [up to 9] fires, thunders, cures, etc. depending on what you chose to draw or get magic from a draw point) is pretty nice (just stack 100 of everything, by the way, 100 is max capacity, and you can usually find monsters that you can draw the basics [cure, fire, thunder, blizzard, and fire] from between each mission), I still miss the amazing customization of materia. Though I do enjoy never running out of MP and having to use an Ether in the middle of a boss fight . . . as long as I have some magic stocked up, I'm fine.

Now what I really like is the Junction System. Let's take a character and I'll explain. How about . . . Squall. Your basic Squall can attack. That is it. No other commands. But if you junction a GF (stands for Guardian Force, they are basically summons) to Squall, many new realms of possibility open up. You are allowed to set ability commands (item, draw, GF, and magic), which brings up a tough question, since you can only set 3 commands. Do you want draw, magic (having draw and magic is a must), and item, or draw, magic, and GF. Really does make you wonder what is necessary. So, with this GF junctioned to Squall, I can also do other stuff. I can junction magic to certain things. Def, Atk, and Mag. Def increased HP, Atk makes you do more damage, and Mag (I am not positive though) makes your magic stronger. If I junction fire to Def, it will give me a little HP, if I junction Cure to Def though, my HP will go up a lot. Same as putting Fire, Thunder, Blizzard, etc. on Atk makes you attack much better then putting Cure on it does.

Also, if you have enough GF's (you can junction more than one GF to someone), you can do elemental attacks and defenses and status attacks and defenses. If I junction Fire to elemental attack, then I get a percent and that is the chance I have of my attack being an attack that is fire-based. Basic fire might increase the percent a little, where is Firaga (an upgraded Fire) will make the percent go up more. Same thing works for elemental defenses, junction Firaga to it, and you could half every fire hit you take. If you get it to 100%, you take no damage, and any higher than that and you will absorb every hit you take of that type. Same with status attacks and defenses. Junction blind to status attack and you have a percent chance of your attack put the effect blind on them. Same works for status defense. It's customization, and I love it.

But that's not all! Your GF's can also learn abilities. Set the GF Shiva to learn SumMag20%, and when she learns it, when you summon Shiva, she will do 20% more damage than the base Shiva would. GF's learn abilities by gaining AP (ability points). After each fight, you usually get some AP. A basic battle with a crappy enemy will garnish 1 AP, a boss fight might get you 6. These abilities are pretty varied too. You can learn commands that let you add another ability to your ability list (*groan* more decisions . . . item or card . . . hmm). There are also abilities that let you take items and refine them into magic. Use the RF-Fire (not the right letters, I know) ability and use up 1 Magic Stone (or something like that) and you have 10 Firagas. The Junction system is great and I wish Square kept some of their old systems in each new FF game.

Fifth, replay value. I personally don't have the time to play a 40+ hour RPG more than once. But plenty of things along the way can make this 40+ hour RPG into a 60+ hour RPG. There is the obligatory raising of chocobos from all FF games, as well as the mini-game "Triple Triad" that is a card game which is a constant theme throughout the whole game, and because it IS the mini-game, it is complex and comprehensive, unlike the multitude of simple mini-games in FF7. If you have the free time though, go for it and you will probably replay the game two or three times.

Sixth, Limit Breaks. Well, the gameplay section was so huge I thought that I just make a section for Limit Breaks. For those who played FF7, you will remember that when you got beat-up enough, you could fight back with a super-attack that essentially "layed the smackdown" on either a single or all enemies. Limit Breaks are a bit different this time around though. You can only do a Limit Break when you are in the yellow (not sure what the exact percent and below of HP that is), and they all involve player input. For Squall's, you have to hit the R1 button at the right time to use the "trigger" on his Gunblade weapon. For Zell, you have to input commands like a fighting game because he is a hand-to-hand kind of guy. For Rinoa, you teach her dog (named Angelo) tricks, and they restore health, revive KO'ed guys, damage the enemy, etc. I actually like them more than the FF7 limit breaks. They are more of a last resort this time, which is what I think they should be.

Final Verdict? This is an absolutely amazing game and most likely will become my Number 1 console game as I get further into it. I have heard lots of complaints about no innovation and that this is FF7 1.5, but I don't know what they are talking about. I see FF8 as a completely new game with new innovations, magic systems, and very few things borrowed from FF7. The other complaint is too many random fights, but I think that's a basic part of RPG's we'll always deal with. I give FF8 a 99% because I think no game is in a sense "perfect," and the 1% off is for too many random fights, which I agree with to a sense.

Oh yeah! The running guy thing (by the way, who remembers Running Man with Arnold Schwerzanagger (major, major, major sic), didn't that movie rock) . . . umm, I give it a 4, and if EQ is down and you go to play it, it gets a 5 because once you start playing you WILL forget about EQ until your power goes out right before the save point stupid hurricane .
