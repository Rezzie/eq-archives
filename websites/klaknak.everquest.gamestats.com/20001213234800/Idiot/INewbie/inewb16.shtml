That awkward stage

by Anhedonia

I had a little experience last night that I thought you might like for your idiot newbie files, or at least for a personal laugh.

Being a level 9 Necromancer, I am just at "that awkward stage" where the young character is still undeniably a newbie but desperately wants to act otherwise, like an annoying younger sibling. The rest of my group was not due to arrive for another couple of hourse, so I wandered morosely through Nekulos Forest, killing the occasional decaying skeleton or fire beetle, and wondering where I could safely kill time on my own. I recalled someone telling me a few days previous that there was a gypsy camp in Lavastorm that sold earrings of fire protection...I could use those. I know, of course, there is no way I can fight my way through Lavastorm alone. But maybe I don't have to fight...

Upon appearing in Lavastorm, I quickly cast Gather Shadows on myself and strike out for the camp, chuckling smugly at my own cleverness. I arrive and realize I must become visible to deal with the merchants. "Ah well, this will only take a few seconds" I think to myself. "Hail!" "I don't sell to dark elves like you!" Well, phooey. You'd think my friend would have mentioned that point...but then you'd think a handful of merchants living in an infested magma wasteland wouldn't be that picky. Oh well, home I go. I turn to start casting Gather Shadows.

A fire imp smacks you upside the head for an obscene amount of damage!

Uh oh...since I was running invisible I didn't have a pet with me, and I'm being hit too fast to cast anything. This is bad...

A fire imp socks you in the nose for half of your HP! A fire imp backhands you for the other half of your HP!
LOADING, PLEASE WAIT...

Well, don't I feel foolish. I guess I'll have to do another invisible run, loot my corpse and come home. What a wasted trip. I arrive back into the camp and kneel down next to my body and begin grabbing armor & money.

A fire elemental kicks you in the butt!

What the...they can't see invisible! You mean I lose the spell if I loo- "ARRRRGH!" I attempt to flee for the zone, and moments later realize I could not recall what direction it was in. The fire elemental had called a couple of friends over for a Dark Elf pinata party and proceeded to beat me silly. I just reached for location when-

LOADING, PLEASE WAIT...

Well, I don't know where that body is. Phooey. "That's okay, I have Locate Corpse," I think calmly. I head back into Lavastorm, cast Gather Shadows, cast Locate Corpse and noticed I was visible again...goodness, this spell is persnickety! I recast, become invisible and autorun towards my body. Half a second later, plummeting to my death off a cliff, I rethink the wisdom of this tactic.

LOADING, PLEASE WAIT...

Back at Neriak. "Well, the good news is I don't have to loot THAT one." I am desperately grasping for some kind of silver lining to this experience. I head back into Lavastorm, past a few people meditating at the entrance who are snickering to see me run by half-naked for the fourth time. I call to my guild for help and thankfully someone comes to my aid as an escort. We get to the camp and I manage to loot the body left there. Whew! Now for the other...wherever it is. My guide says he will wait for me here, and I should drag my corpse back with me. I Locate Corpse, become invisible once more, and trot off SLOWLY. Locate Corpse wants to lead me directly through a lava pool, so I pick my way around carefully to the other side. Locate again, become invisible again. Head off, now Locate wants me off a cliff. I begin to wonder if someone can be KOS to their own spells. After a dozen repeats of appear-locate-invisible I find the body. Looking around, nothing is in the area...I duck down quickly to loot the few pieces of armor and become invisible again. Success! I happily trot back towards the camp and my escort, when invisibility begins to fade. Not a problem...

Your spell fizzles. Your spell fizzles. You do not have enough mana to cast this spell!

Okay, problem! Of course, I immediately hear the flap of heavy wings approaching directly behind me. I duck behind a nearby rock, doing my best to crawl under it completely. I sit and meditate frantically, then the wingbeats carry an utterly disinterested fire drake by. I manage to scramble back to my escort and safely back to the zone entrance, and let out the breath I have been holding for 20 minutes. Feeling very much like Peter Rabbit, I ran back to Neriak and collapsed, my ego now well-tempered with humility.

-Anhedonia
