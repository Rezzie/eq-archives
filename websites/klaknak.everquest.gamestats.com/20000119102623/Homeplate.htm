Joseph Heller = dead

For those of you who think this is off-topic, forget-about-it. Along with Monty Python, Douglas Adams and Ronald Reagan, Joseph Heller has had the greatest impact on my humor (and the humor, no doubt, of countless others who are far funnier and far more famous). Along with the people listed above (and Carlin, Pryor and countless other), Heller showed a person can be both intensely funny and intensely topical and intelligent at the same time. I think Catch 22 (or Lolita by V.N.) may be the elusive Great American Novel.

Anyway, he is dead.

Here is an excerpt from his novel Catch 22:

"You mean there's a catch?"

"Sure there's a catch," Doc Daneeka replied.

"Catch-22. Anyone who wants to get out of combat duty isn't really crazy."

There was only one catch and that was Catch-22, which specified that a concern for one's own safety in the face of dangers that were real and immediate was the process of a rational mind. Orr was crazy and could be grounded. All he had to do was ask; and as soon as he did, he would no longer be crazy and would have to fly more missions. Orr would be crazy to fly more missions and sane if he didn't, but if he was sane he had to fly them. If he flew them he was crazy and didn't have to; but if he didn't want to he was sane and had to. Yossarian was moved very deeply by the absolute simplicity of this clause of Catch-22 and let out a respectful whistle.

"That's some catch, that Catch-22,"he observed.

"It's the best there is," Doc Daneeka agreed.
