What causes these shootings?

Before I give you my answer (which is: "everything"), let me tell what is wrong with our discourse.

Whenever an issue creeps up in America (and possibly everywhere else), there is a tendency to believe that every effect has a single cause. There are two reasons for this:

1. human nature - Back when I spent more time in the psychology lab than I do today, I studied human reasoning, and if there is one near certainty, it is that people are sloppy thinkers. Whether this is due to our inherent stupidity (some people's view) or our inherent ability to maximize mental efficiency due to limited space (my view), people are prone to take complex issues and distill them into easily digestible, but overly simplified clumps. So, with the shootings problem, many of us believe that there is a single cause.

2. politics - Politicians, pundits, activists, etc. know us better than we do. They know that people make decisions based on nuggets of overgeneralization. So, the conservatives come on board and say the shootings are the result of a godless country. The liberals claim they are the result of an out of control society of guns. What the shootings really are to them (to overgeneralize a bit myself) is a battlefield on which to continue to wage their war against the opponent. The arguments are always the same, but they continue to wage anyway, because if they started to consider more complicated solutions, they would lose their soundbites (see Media Criticism below).

Now, I think that a lot of sensible people DO realize that the shootings are a complicated issue, but I think it took a string of shootings for us to figure that out. As sad as it is, I think it took several shootings by a wide variety of people under a wide variety of circumstances for people to finally sit up and say: "Whoa ... dude. Like, that guy in Atlanta didn't play Doom. Maybe it isn't just video games."

So, let me say that EVERYTHING causes these shootings and the causal attribution is probably different for each shooter. But let me break down some of these probable necessary (but not sufficient, true believers!) causes of shootings from which we get to choose before assigning blame.

(in no necessary order)

1. The Christian Church as Seen on TV. Back when I was more atheist than not, I used to think that all Christian churches were bastions of hypocrisy. Never in my life have a made a more woeful and idiotic overgeneralization. Through my work in the community with mentally retarded adults, I have found that some of the best, most kind people in the community are devout Christians.

But then you turn on the TV. And believe it or not, I am not talking about big haired yo yo's on the Christian channels, but I am talking about the well coiffed yo yo's that appear on the news whenever any issue comes up. I am talking about the Falwells and the Reeds and the ... cackle ... Quayles and Buchanans.

Now, stick with me here, but I am going to digress a little bit and then come back to prove a point (I hope).

There is no absolute moral truth.

Those who believe there is an absolute moral truth are extremely dangerous.

Now, you may disagree strongly with my initial premise (there is no moral truth), but there is something very very scary about these yo yo's and their belief that only a Christian God can provide the absolute moral answers to all of us whether we are Christians, Jews, Muslims, Buddhists, athiests, Wiccans, or whatever. And the fact that they plan on carrying these issues through into the making of public policy positively turns my hair white at night.

What does this have to do with the shootings? Plenty:

a. When they say that the shootings are the result of a Godless society (sorry Jews, Muslims, etc.), they are not providing an answer. They don't really have an answer. Or, if they do, their answer doesn't make any sense. Let's break down what they are saying:

(1) The shootings are the result of a Godless society.

(2) Therefore, the shootings can be eliminated by transforming the country into one of total Christianity.

I really don't think this is an overgeneralization folks. I have been listening to these guys for years, and I honestly feel that this is what they want to do. Scary, especially if you aren't a Christian. And since this is their solution, and since it is absolutely ludicrous to believe it is obtainable, this "solution" is a non-solution. It is a preachy rant and nothing more.

b. I am a Christian and I think the Christian faith has great value for those inclined to listen to it (by their own personal choice). But I think that the yo yo's are doing more damage to the Christian Church than good by being ... by being yo yo's. Maybe if those Christians in our society were stronger Christians, maybe (maybe) our society would be less susceptible to the woes we experience. But I think a vast majority of the lukewarm Christians out there (including myself) are not more involved in their own faith because of the yo yo's. Nuff said.

 

2. Mental Illness. Gawd, I could talk for years about this, but let me be quick:

(a). Awareness of and treatment for mental illness is getting better, but it is still extremely bad (especially for people in the low socioeconomic status category).

(b). Self-help books. If we took all the money spent on producing bullshit self-help books and spent it on trying to find real solutions to the really hard core psychological problems and the education of people about these problems, our society would probably be a little better off.

(c). Get the psychologist yo yo's off television! They use their faulty studies (correlation does not mean causation, you psycho freaks) to argue their personal agendas as outlined in their twenty four dollars a book self help manifestos.

I think psychology as a profession suffers from the same problems as Christianity, so I will ask you to review my Christian rant above and insert "Psychology" into all the appropriate places to see how these tidbits fit together.

 

3. Guns.

a. I hate to say it (as a life long liberal) but the NRA has a point when it says that we should prosecute laws already on the books. If we run out of prisons, we can turn Idaho into a penal colony.

b. The NRA is wrong if they think we can allow people to self-regulate themselves at gun shows and flea markets.

c. No one (and I mean no one) needs a machine gun for personal use. Are you a gun collector? Tough. A sportsman? What about "sport" don't you understand? Unless you are hunting a platoon of Iraqis for sport, you don't need a machine gun. Tough. Sorry. Tough.

 

4. Media. Whoa Nellie, this is my favorite topic, but I will try to control myself and keep it short. Yes, I do believe that whenever a massive shooting occurs, the media should report it. This is a vital part of public discourse.

But the Columbine shooting coverage was way out of control. And I am going to be extremely cynical, but I have to say it:

Why did the ratings go up, encouraging all the major news outlets to cover every inch of the tragedy? Was it because the tragedy was so large? Partly. Was it because the OJ Simpson tragedy and the Princess Diana tragedy raised the bar on coverage? Partly. Was it because the teenager demographic is one of the most highly sought after advertising groups in television.

Hmmmm.

Nah, that is too cynical. I sound like a yo yo myself.

Maybe.

Anyway, the coverage of the Columbine shooting was so overblown, it just begged for copy cats. Is there anything scarier than a screwed up teenager feeling that his feelings of low self esteem won't be assuaged until CNN covers his actions 24 hours a day for a solid couple weeks? It keeps me up at night, let me tell you.
