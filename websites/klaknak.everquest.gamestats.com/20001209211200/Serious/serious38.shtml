Re-Response on ShowEQ

by Lore 38

Hallo.

I read your article on ShowEQ. OK, I read it after just waking up with a tremendous headache, PMS and a growling stomach (more room to digest facts?), so my judgement may be a little bit impaired. But I've been thinking about this for a bit, and here is my comment:

ShowEQ, in my opinion, is not wrong. It gives players who can afford the software an advantage, just like training for a race gives you an advantage. The people who are selling this are marketing a product, just like Verant is doing, making money off of it and having their own little business. I don't think it nerfs Rangers or Druids, since they still have their abilities. It's just that I can too. Also, it may spread out the population, clearing up camp sites and spawns.
So what keeps me from using it?
Fear.

Plain old, simple fear. I know Verant is scanning my system, and all I want them to find is EQ, pictures of The Tea Party and an mp3 player. I do NOT want to get in deep shit for this, and simply seeing that mob A gives more experience and loot than mob B will NOT make me risk the wrath of Verant. Now, I have, like everyone else I assume, looked at a bunch of code in my EQ file, wondered at it, and wondered if I altered it a bit, would I end up with an Oracle Robe? But the same fear is preventing me from doing that, fear that the great gods Verant may find out and attack me.

So what's my smart-ass idea of dealing with this? Verant should buy the rights to this thing, and market it. Simple, eh? It is a product, like so much other stuff, and that product should be marketed. It does good and, hey, a Ranger can still range, but my Enchanters can't enchant. Fi they are so concerned with a supposed nerf to Rangers' tracking, improve the tracking.

/em gets out of "Rant Mode"
/em casts "Endure Flame"

lore 38
