Might and Magic vs. Baldur's Gate - by the Cackling Klaknak

[Editor's Note: (dons his flame retardant dungaroos)]

Let me start off with my thesis statement: Might and Magic 7 is more fun than Baldur's Gate.

Caveat: But Baldur's Gate is a better game.

So: Let me explain.

A little while ago, back when CRPGs were still struggling to emerge from the RPG dark ages of the early 90's, two cheesy looking games from New World Computing emerged to plague my days: Heroes of Might and Magic 2 and Might and Magic 6.

For someone who has not played these games, it is a little difficult to convince him of how fun these games really are. Many people (including one of my close friends) look at the cartoon style interface of Heroes of Might and Magic 2 and just can't overcome their 3-D bigotry. Other people (including some of those above) look at the (horribly) dated graphics of Might and Magic 6 (and therefore 7) and can't play long enough to realize how completely addicting the game is.

In the end, however, if you give these games a chance, they are a lot of fun. I won't discuss the Heroes series now (since it already receives well deserved praise) but I do want to turn your attention to Might and Magic 6 (and therefore 7) to you and suggest that it is a lot more fun to play than Baldur's Gate. I will do this by pointing out what makes an RPG an RPG (for me) and show how Might and Magic 6 (7) wins on the fun level even though Baldur's Gate is a technically more sophisticated and better game.

Story

Baldur's Gate: What story? Baldur's Gate is elegant but it is without a soul. It is like a shiny clean robot whereas Might and Magic is like a very ugly, but gregarious dog.

Might and Magic 6...? It is true that Might and Magic 6 lacked something of a story, but for those who have played the Heroes series and some of the earlier Might and Magic series, the lack of a game specific story was not so marked in the context of the overall mythology. And Might and Magic 7's story is a huge improvement, both over MM6 and Baldur's Gate.

Statistics

Baldur's Gate: I know this is blasphemy, but ADD rules doesn't transfer very well to solo playing. When you are doing pen and paper, the fact that you can spend months on a character to get to level 3 is tempered by the fact that you are involved in a very interactive, dynamic system of play. When you play by yourself, however, the hard core, almost tyrannical ADD style rules set is oppressive and tedious. When you play solo games (or multiplayer games, for that matter), levelling plays a primary role in invigorating the player.

Might and Magic: Might and Magic gives out levels and statistics like a champ. It is just flat out fun going to the training ground and finding out you have levelled once again. It gives you a lot more flexibility to muck around with your character instead of being terrified that one tiny mistake could wreck months of work (see Baldur's Gate).

Spawn

Baldur's Gate: BG's spawn rate is absolutely adequate and its monster AI is very good.

Might and Magic: The monster AI is weak, but ... damn ... there are monsters EVERYWHERE. Everywhere, man. You can't walk 10 feet in any direction without being clobbered by something. With this many critters, your enemy can have the AI of Pong and still provide adequate challenge.

Spells

Baldur's Gate: The spells are absolutely superior.

Might and Magic: The spells are the weak link, I think.

The X factor

Baldur's Gate: By the time my party finally arrived in Baldur's Gate, I was sick of the game. I don't know, Baldur's Gate just eventually came to be a chore for me. If a game is to be primarily a solo game, its physical size needs to be limited by the game system's ability to sustain enjoyment.

Might and Magic: When I first played Might and Magic 6, I was pretty skeptical until I noticed that I spent most of my waking hours thinking about it or playing it. It has become one of my favorite games to play for reasons totally unknown to me. It's cool, man.

I wish I could write a long, well written, logically clean thesis on why I think MM6 and 7 are more fun to play than Baldur's Gate, but I think the bottom line is that it is a personal thing. Baldur's Gate was incredibly important and is essentially the Quake of the RPG genre, but something about it just left me feeling a little empty. Instead of a spark, it only provided me with a cool, methodical example of a well designed game. In the age of game slop, this is not an unimportant detail, but in the end, I want something that keeps me up a night. Might and Magic does that for me (oh yeah, and *(%$ Everquest).
