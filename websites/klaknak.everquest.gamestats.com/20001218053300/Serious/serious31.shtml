ShowEQ - Activist Tool or Hacker Evil?

by Cackling Klaknak

[with some post-hangover notes]

[Note: I wrote the following essay while suffering from a severe tequila hangover. As such, I have now included a few embarrasing admissions and caveats. Sigh. Live and learn, man. Just live and learn.]

For those who don't know, ShowEQ is a program that allows users to see the spawn, experience, and loot (I think) of MOBs in a zone in real time. It also allows you to find out exactly how much experience you get when you group, how much experience you get with a pet, and the exact numeric value of faction hits.

I have to admit that, even though I am fond of Everquest and do NOT think that Brad is satan, I kinda chuckled deep inside when I heard of this program. To me, I view hacking a lot like I view organized crime: I know it is probably wrong, but secretly I think it is kind of cool.

However, being the responsible website running person that I am, I would like to respond to a lot of the debates out there concerning this product. The number one caveat I can offer is that I know very little about how these programs actually work, so take any of my technical mumblings with a grain of salt.

And so, without further ado, the great ShowEQ debate!

 

A. The Generic Hacker Apology

From my meagre knowledge on the subject, there seems to be two kinds of hacking: plain ole mischief and hactivism.

I think many people think that all hackers are just into plain ole mischief. Just as our fathers may have raced souped up cars to the edge of cliffs in order to score points with Mary Rottencrotch, these modern day wahoos, the myth goes, hack into programs and networks just to show they can and to score points with women who have 458 body piercings and a taste for oriental fem punk bands.

On the other end of the spectrum, you have the hactivists. These are the people who let themselves into Uncle Mao's computer network in order to spread anti-chinese propaganda. Their main purpose, supposedly, is a new kind of civil disobedience.

I was reading an interesting article on this very subject the other day in Spin magazine. According to some critics of hactivism, the "activism" part is merely a front for what is basically a bunch of people interested only in plain ole mischief. So, for example, a person may hack into McDonald's website and claim, in retrospect, that they were protesting the killing of third world babies to make the chicken mcnuggets, when in reality, McDonald's was the only one they were successful with, and they threw on the activism after the fact.

Even though I must rely solely on the author of this Spin article as far as the Truth goes, this hactivism critic IMMEDIATELY reminded me of the Generic MMORPG Hacker Apology which I will detail below.

 

B.The Generic MMORPG Hacker Apology.

Even though I suppose this is not "hacking" in the pure sense, when people create a website with a list of bugs in a game, they almost always, invariably, say this:

"It is our intent to discover bugs in EQ/UO in order to help the companies make better games."

Indeed, this sounds a lot like hactivism. But remembering the Spin article's critique of certain kinds of hactivism, let's examine a few "details" of these websites that seem to undermine their altruism:

1. Money dupe bugs are found in abundance whereas bugs that dont provide any kind of cheap money or experience are mysteriously left out of the website.

2. On many of these sites, you cannot view the bugs unless you are allowed membership into the "secret area". Membership usually requires some sort of fee or contribution in the field of hacking.

3. Many of these sites actively seek out people who can hack the game code. Again, I am not a technical wizard, but neither am I naive. What, are these people going to hack the code so that they can help EQ/UO make a better game? Hell no.

In other words, to sum things up a bit, people who say "we are doing this to help the game" are full of shit and they know it. As does everyone else.

However, does this mean that ShowEQ is wrong?

I don't know. I am decidedly very mixed (although I would never use the product). I think "wrong" here is a very relative question.

 

C. ShowEQ is wrong: the arguments.

1. ShowEQ is wrong because it essentially nerfs Druids and Rangers. This is because even the frumpiest low level wizard is now able to find spawn, with the aid of the program.

Someone on one of the boards made a very interesting comment about this. He said that he was glad that it nerfed druids because he felt that druids already had too much power. This, it seems to me, is a "hactivist" type statement. But he also said that it really bothered him that rangers were nerfed. I mean, let's face it, a ranger without ... ranging .. is a seriously nerfed character indeed.

Ignoring all the moralistic and legalistic arguments, I think this is the strongest argument against ShowEQ. The absolute last thing this game needs is an added advantage to those people who would run around camping the best critters as soon as they spawn. ShowEQ creates, in a way, a kind of dynamic camping system, and ... christ ... we just don't need that (unless ... hmm ... more on this in a moment).

However, for those who like ShowEQ because it nerfs Druids, I can't honestly say that I am not on your side. It is not that I am a proponent of druid nerfdom (I know very little about it), but I am constantly perplexed about EQ's power balancing decisions (more on this in a moment) and I think a lot of players are sick of feeling that they have absolutely no say when confronted with Verant's Secretive-Hammurabi's-Code-on-Crack mode of making balancing decisions. I realize that, to Verant's credit, they have become much more vocal about game decisions than in the past, but, as politics has taught us, the mere flapping of the lips does not endear one to the unwashed masses. In fact, I think it serves to infuriate our skanky sensibilities even more.

So I am not sure how I come down on this one, but the dynamic camping aspect and the nerfing of rangers really bugs the hell out of me.

2. ShowEQ is wrong because when players know the exact numeric values of the game, they will powergame even more.

When I first started EQ, I think I remember this being Verant's standard answer to any number of questions like: "Why can't we know how much mana we have?" or "Why can't we know our exact experience count?" etc.

In theory, this sounds really nice. It sounds really Lord British vision-esque.

In reality, this just simply doesn't wash at all. Can anyone out there seriously say with a straight face that knowing these exact numbers will somehow revolutionize the gameplay in EQ? I seriously seriously doubt it. If anything, knowing the exact experience count may serve to spread out the population a little more.

As EQ stands now, most people tend to camp the same spots from knowledge that has been handed to us by countless websites. What if it was suddenly known that critter X, Y and Z, critters no one has camped, has decent experience without all the camping headache? Well, it may just be that people will start moving away from the traditional spots (like Oasis, for chrissakes) and start ... gack ... hunting in other parts of Norrath. Isn't this the whole point of EQ according to Verant. Sure, they may want people to do this out of a lust for exploration rather than a lust for stats, but here we see a perfect example of how "vision" seems just a little too goddamn pompous for a computer game.

I do have a slightly more cynical comment to make, however, and I think it is the most interesting question that ShowEQ raises.

3. ShowEQ is great because it makes Verant accountable.

All other arguments aside, this is absolutely the most seductive aspect of ShowEQ.

If I can digress for a moment, I am absolutely SICK of spin and doublespeak. I am SICK OF IT! SICK OF IT! I am not talking about just Verant, I am talking about our whole freaking culture. For those of you who think Clinton somehow ushered in an era of lying and subterfuge, you are full of it. America was built on money and money is built on the glorious deification of the product that must be protected with every kind of lie the courts will allow you to tell (or will allow you to tell if you can bankrupt your complainant with tons of motions and hearings). Here is a perfect example of this:

There is a certain graham cookie product made by a certain big company. On the side of the box, it shows a food pyramid and says something like "by eating graham crackers, you are fulfulling the need to eat all of this bread-like product at the bottom of the pyramid, as mandated and legitimized by the US gov'mint." What the box DOESN'T tell you is that these glorious cookies are loaded with sugar and fat, which the pyramid tells you to eat only very sparingly.

Now, Klaknak, you say. This is silly. Sure, ok, maybe it is ... but before you start spouting off some sort of pro-capitalist papal bull, let me tell you this. This cracker company is DECEIVING THE PUBLIC in order to MAKE MONEY. And if you think this stops with crackers, you are full of it. I only have two words for you: tobacco and the Pinto.

Now, naturally, if Verant is shaping the truth to fit their needs, it is not something I am going to cry about. This is, people, just a game. However, it is also a game that I spend a lot of my time playing. If Verant tells me through some sort of doubletalk that something is a certain way, but it turns out that Verant is basically lying, then I want to freaking know about it so I will stop wasting time dealing with this lie.

Already, there is a certain rumor floating around in the ShowEQ community that is a perfect example of this. Some say that humans, whose only real advantage is that they level quicker than anyone else, DO NOT ACTUALLY LEVEL QUICKER THAN EVERYONE ELSE.

If this is true, holy shit. We aren't talking about locations of MOBs here, folks. If you chose a human based on this little bit of info and this little bit of info is wrong, you have a right, whether this is a game or not, to be extremely pissed off ... especially if you have spent several months working on this character.

There is also a rumor that ... gasp ... having a full group does NOT give you bonus experience. Is this true? I don't know. But if it was true, do you think Verant would admit it without a hacker program breathing down their neck? Hell no.

[Post-Hangover Note: As a few irate consumers of the Klaknak product have noted, the full group = bonus experience rumor is itself a .. rumor, and an incorrect one at that. I am pretty damn embarrassed. I cant spin that, man. (It was the tequila!)(Shut up, Bill and Al)].

Again, I will repeat this again ... these are only rumors ... but this is a perfect example of why I cannot totally scorn ShowEQ. I am sick of being lied to by companies of all shapes and sizes and if Verant is lying to me, I want to know it and I want to know it NOW. If they are telling the truth, then that is great. I really do think they run a pretty good ship over there, in spite of all my little problems. But if they are lying, I might seriously consider trashing my characters right now (Diablo 2 anyone?).

In other words, if ShowEQ delivers some kind of bombshell about how Verant has been lying to us all this time, and people leave in droves as a result, there may be a good result after all:

"With the recent EQ fiasco, companies have now learned to lie to their customers at their own risk."

Is this a bad thing? Do the ends justify the means? I dont know. Since we are just talking about a game and not some world crisis, I kind of think so.

[Post Hangover note: Ok, here is the tequila talking. Do I think ShowEQ is going to cause some sort of mass revolt on EQ? Probably not. In fact, my statement here is in direct contradiction to statements I have made in other places that it is the silent majority, and not the outraged minority, that keeps the EQ boat afloat. Still, if there are any major embarrassing things out there, and ShowEQ discovers them, Verant will suffer something of a public relations nightmare ... at least with vocal fools like me who cant even get their *($# facts straight.]

So there is my little ranticle on this ShowEQ stuff. If you have any intelligent comments, please send them in.
