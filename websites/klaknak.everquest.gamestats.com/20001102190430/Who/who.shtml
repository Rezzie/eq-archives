Who is ... the Klaknak

 

The Klaknak after the nerfing of player trade skills.

I was born in 1971, the by-product of an unholy union between a vienna sausage and a nerf football. My life is long and storied, so here is a useful FAQ:

1. Klaknak, I have noticed that your site is full of drug references. Do you do drugs?

Yes and no. I love scotch. Scotch is alcohol. Alcohol is a drug. Ergo, yes I do drugs, but in reality all I do is drink a glass of scotch every week or so.

As for real drugs, I hate to disappoint, but I don't do them. I don't do them because I don't really like them. I experimented (along with 99.99% of the population and presidential candidates) in college, but it never really took.

2. Are you high right now?

Indeed. I accidentally slept late (and missed work), took my daily regimen of Rogaine, Xanax, and stress tabs, ate a few italian sausages and posted a lot of great stuff on the site ... so ... I am high on life.

3. When did you start playing RPGs?

In the 70's (80's?) I played Dungeons and Dragons for about a summer or two. Then, my friends and I discovered that HBO showed skin flicks in the middle of the day. Ask me about the time my friend's drill seargent father caught us.

4. What happened?

He yelled at us.

5. Um ... have you played any other games?

I played the Ultima serieses until I graduated from high school. Then I went to college and discovered that if you join a fraternity, you get to drink a lot of beer and sleep with girls. I didn't pick up gaming again for 6 or 7 years. Several years ago, I bought a new computer and then bought Daggerfall. Since then I have played nearly every RPG offered, including the dreadful Battlespire (shudder) game. I have also provided technical aid for various hintbooks by Prima.

6. What online RPGs have you played?

I played Gemstone for about a year and loved it. I think the Temple of Voln concept is something that all current ORPGs lack. Unfortunately, Gemstone's servers sucked big large juicy eggs, so I had to quit. I then picked up Ultima Online. Ultima Online is nearly a perfect game except for two HUGE utterly HUGE flaws: PKing and low spawn. I now play Everquest and not much else.

7. What do you do for a living?

ARGGGG!!!!! (CK runs screaming from the room).

8. No, really.

Oh. I am sorry, I thought you said "tool." Well, presently I am a counselor for mentally retarded adults. I used to work in a psychology lab, but I quit that just a few days ago. As for what I plan to do, let me give you a wish list:

a. Design computer games (utter pipe dream)

b. Write for a computer games mag if it pays over $100,000 (utter pipe dream)

c. Get my PhD in clinical psych specializing in mental retardation.

In all seriousness, I would love to somehow be involved in the gaming industry (like everyone else), but I honestly feel the work I do with MR people is important as well. What will probably end up happening is that gaming will be a hobby and I will have a very satisfying career helping out people who really need it.

What the Klaknak really does for a living.

9. What happened yesterday that is so damn special?

A few weeks ago, I looked after this woman's cat. She gave me a bottle of 12 year old scotch as a thank you present.

10. Why did you start the Cackling Klaknak?

When I played UO, I posted several humorous tidbits on a bulletin board. I thought about starting a UO humor website then, but my desire to play UO was quickly waning. When I bought EQ and played it for a few weeks, I knew I had to make a humor site dedicated to it.

11. What is the best thing about the website?

The best thing is that a majority of the submissions are awesome. When I first started, I was afraid that I would get tons of idiotic email containing fart jokes and whatnot. But I think I scared off these guys by referring to Hume a lot.

12. Why are you interviewing yourself?

I have a HUGE ego.
