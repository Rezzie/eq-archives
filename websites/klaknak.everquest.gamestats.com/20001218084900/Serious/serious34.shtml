Response to ShowEQ Article

by Daedlus

Dear Cackling Klaknak,

I found your article on the pros and cons of "ShowEQ" to be very well written and well thought out. However, I would like to bring to light a couple of points that I feel you have left out.

1. First off, hackers cannot be divided into merely two groups. Yes, the vandal hackers (the ones who just like to cause mischief) and the hactivists are two very valid types, but there are many, many more. There are computer hackers who specialize in theft of funds, industrial espionage, program code cracking, encryption cracking, registration code theft and cracking, credit card fraud.the list goes on and on. And, truth be told, computer crime, which has been dubbed by popular culture as "hacking," is a very lucrative business. I believe that ShowEQ is a form of electronic theft. This person or persons who has designed this program is after one thing: benefit for himself or herself.

2. I have to disagree with your closing statement:

"Is this a bad thing? Do the ends justify the means? I don't know. Since we are just talking about a game and not some world crisis, I kind of think so."

The reason for this is plain and simple. Sure, this program, in theory, could keep Verant "honest." But there is one little catch. It is unfair, and I am not speaking of game mechanics (negating rangers and such) The fact that this program has been designed and used has caused Verant , in an effort to be fair to all it's players, to have to change a few things about the game.one of these things being the procedure which the game uses to move a character from one zone to another. Now, this is not a simple thing to change, and it has caused many problems to arise. People have lost characters, had zoning problems, been removed from guilds, and had to petition for corpses to be looted. Now some might say that these things are the fault of Verant for releasing buggy code. I would dispute that fact. I work for a software company, and I can tell you this: when you design and implement a game or program of this size, BUGS ARE INNEVITABLE! Even with the most thorough design and testing, things of this sort happen, especially when a large and often used portion of the game, such as the zoning procedure, has to be changed. Nothing is perfect, as much as some people would like to believe. So, here is the bottom line:

What happened? Someone has designed a program (ShowEQ) to reveal secrets about EverQuest that are not meant to be revealed in such a manner.

Why? To be able to cheat the system, which is only made worse by the weak excuse of keeping the game makers (Verant) honest.

Result: Verant, in an effort to be fair to it's players, has had to make some major changes in the game to negate this program (ShowEQ) which allows a select few people access to information that makes them very powerful in the game at the expense of players (many of whom still have unresolved problems), the GMs (who are already over-worked and under appreciated, have to work to help the players with problems) and Verant (who now has to pay programmers, GMs, and technical support representatives to work to fix this problem)

My take: Any time you make a large amount of trouble for a large amount of people purely for personal gain, you are in the wrong. I'm sure that if any anti-Verant wacko read this, his or her comment would probably be something akin to this: "Verant deserves it for making such an unbalanced game!" Well, I have only one thing to say to that. Using ShowEQ as a way to keep Verant honest is taking the law into your own hands, also known as vigilante justice. And last time I checked, vigilante justice was illegal. No man is above the law. We have law systems, and rule systems in place for a reason. I think that Verant has come a long way toward making the game more balanced. Making everyone absolutely equal in a world as large, complex, and containing as many variables as Norrath is a near impossibility, and certainly isn't the type of thing that happens over night. It is a work in progress. Things such as this are detrimental. How is Verant ever going to have time to work on class balancing if they keep having to make huge changes due to a few greedy ingrates? It always happens, a few numbnuts ruin it for everyone.
