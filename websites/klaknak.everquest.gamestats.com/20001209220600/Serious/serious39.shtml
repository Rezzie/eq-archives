Re-response to ShowEQ

by Person X

Did you know that Dorothy from the wizard of Oz is James Bond's mother? The tin man didn't have a heart but man was that sex scene steamy.

anyways, I am writing to respond to a response to a response etc... respond to a response that was made by the greek guy that tries to fly. I read his letter and was astounded at some (most) of the things that he said. I'll start by saying:

"Dear Cackling Klaknak,
I found your article on the pros and cons of 'ShowEQ' to be very well written and well thought out. However, I would like to bring to light a couple of points that I feel you have left out." (pp. 45)

This is the general libertarian (sp?) start (yes, I used to listen to Rusch (spII?) Limbah (spIII?)) This person starts by flattering you, but then cuts to the chase fairly quickly.

paragraph 1:
He starts off by saying that hackers can be anything evil. What about good hackers? Now I know you are saying, "WTF are good hackers?" now, but there are. These are the people who take viruses and analyze them to make the internet safer for you by (passive!!) creating anti-viruses(viri?) He then continues to say that ShowEQ is theft. The last I checked theft meant that you take something that doesn't belong to you. ShowEQ may be vandalism yes, damaging property that doesn't belong to you, but theft? What kind of crack was he on? Cookie Dough ice-cream is good. Anyways, this person, using blatant lack of vocabulary, has proven their response unworthy of credit by saying that ShowEQ is theft.

Second paragraph:
He starts this paragraph like a libertarian(sp?) again. He claims that your closing statement is wrong, which can be read two ways:

1: Is this a bad thing? I kind of think so.

OR

2: Do the ends justify the means? I think so.

Now responding to BOTH of these possible meanings can be a tricky thing. Why include the one when all you need is the other? Surely this person wasn't thinking when they read it.

He then discounts vigilante justice, which in my political views, is perfectly ok. Though I am an anarchist and pray to the lord that my views are never put into action, (anarchy would work if people weren't idiots) something that is just a game shouldn't be taken this seriously. Certainly this is technology misuse, and breaks a legal contract, but calling it vigilante justice is like pulling someone over for speeding and adding wreckless driving on top of it. Sure they were speeding, but how reckless can they be if they thought they could go so fast? Obviously not very (sorry, that is how I look at things as an able, intelligent person and try to think of people as the same. People who know me call me dopey)

Then he goes on to tell us how a few people ruined it for everyone. I agree. Spending 2 and a half hours trying to get across the OOT wasn't my idea of fun, especially after having to make a petition that took about 3 hours to get answered (i had petitioned earlier about the game crashing while I zoned) and then finally having to be teleported to the damn docks after a rather rude (on my part, but who wouldn't be after a 2 and a half hour boat ride?) argument with the guide. But this is life, a few people (idiots or cruel and vindictive people) WILL ruin it for everyone, and though it makes everyone mad, those people will always be there.

Person X
