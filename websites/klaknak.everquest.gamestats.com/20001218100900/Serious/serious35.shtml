ShowEQ update

by the Cackling Klaknak

ShowEQ update:

Some of the information gleaned from ShowEQ has caused a stir with some of the more vocal members of Everquest. Most of the controversy surrounds the widespread information (as confirmed in the Verant sponsored Everquest hint book) that humans gain more experience than any of the other races. As you know, this is basically one of the few advantages humans get (other than being able to play a monk, I suppose). Yesterday I posted a letter from one of my fans, but I will post it again here. He originally posted it on the Everquest Gameplay board.

"Howdy,
I have posted on this subject before but I thought I would try again to get an answer from someone official.
When I bought the game and the Hint book last April, it stated that Humans level faster than other races, their only listed race benefit.
So I dealt with being blind, having completely average stats, etc knowing it would pay off eventually that I could obtain level 50 someday a bit easier than someone who played a superior race for my classes.

Now I read everwhere about this being a false statement from Verant, mostly from ShowEQ type sites (which I do not approve of) but even the Klaknack at in his recent ShowEQ editorial mentions it.

Can we, your customers, please know if this was another incorrect statement in the manual and Hint Guide? That Humans in fact level at the same rate as most of the other races? That in actuality there is no bonus to being human at all other than faction and even stat distribution?

Please respond to this so I do not have to take the word of people using a third party program to determine my trust in you as a company.

Thank you,

Brother Chuy; Solusek Ro"

* * *

In spite of a great deal of bumping on the message board, Gordon took a long time to reply. His eventual response was less than enlightening:

This has been bumped a number of times, evidently looking for some sort of response from me. I thought I'd jump in and just remind everyone that we don't comment on the validity or lack thereof of data pulled by an illegal source.

-Gordon

As I have mentioned several times, I am far from being a vehement, rabid critic of Verant, but this obsfucated response would put Dick Morris to shame. Forgive me if I suggest that Verant's policy on responding to "illegal" software sounds extremely post hoc. And likewise forgive me if I suggest that this utterly fails to answer the issue at hand.

To Gordon's credit, however, he did respond again on another thread with a slightly less spin dried, but still unsatisfactory answer:

I've never bothered to look at the hint book, and really am not concerned with what it may or may not have said. I'll take your word for it that it says what you say it does, and decline to comment further. Whatever the experience requirements are, that's what they are, that's the way it is, and it won't be changing. The contents of the hint book aren't going to change, and neither are the experience requirements to put them in line if any discrepancy exists.
As we didn't have creative control over any of this documentation, it's very likely that it contains information that might not be entirely correct. It is also very likely that it contains information that we didn't want released. The hint book itself was written in late beta, and since the game has continued to evolve after print, I'm sure that there are many things in it that are obsolete.

As for the question in general, there's really nothing wrong with it, I just don't want to be roped into answering all of the other questions that would result from it. My recommendation to you would be to kill a few things and draw your own conclusions.

-Gordon

* * *

As I said, the truth is slowly beginning to seep out here, but I think my response to Gordon pretty much sums up my feelings (and maybe the feelings of others as well):

Gordon:
Thanks for the straightforward answer.

I guess the sticky question is now this:

If there is a rumor that most of EQ believes to be true, supported by an official hintbook (that was licensed to another company by your company), shouldn't you at least look into it and comment specifically on it?

Let's say that there was a widespread rumor that a full group gets bonus experience over and above the additive value of adding members. Wouldn't you want to clear that up? Didn't you do this very thing in a Q and A session (not you, necessarily, but Verant you).

You mention that the hintbook may contain things that are incorrect because it was constructed during beta. I have no problem with this. But we arent exactly talking about an incorrect AC number here. We are talking about something that affects every single Norrathian human out there who believed this information was true. I dont think it should just be ignored as a little publishing snafu.

I understand that Verant is reluctant to talk about numbers because Verant believes that EQ should remain mysterious in this regard. But geez, man, the cat is really out of the bag on this one. Are you willing to let one of your sanctioned products (the EQ hintbook) continue to mislead anyone who picks it up (do you guys get royalties on those books?)

- CK

* * *

Anyway, just as I explained in my earlier essay, there seems to be this plague in the world of business where the truth is to be avoided like the bite of a bubonic dog. In the pre-internet age, this "never complain, never explain" philosophy may have worked, but you can forget about it now.

More later.
