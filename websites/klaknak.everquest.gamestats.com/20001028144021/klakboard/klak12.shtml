Verant Takes Over

by Cyanna Thunderhammer


I work at a large insurance company in the IS department. Recently we got a memo saying that our company had been purchased by Verant. All of the executives were let go and the big three, Brad, Smed, and Gordo took over. The memo then went on to discuss changes that were going to be made to bring the company in line with the new Vision ™. Here is a copy of that memo.

Patch Update: July 17th, 2000.
Greetings all! We want to welcome all of you to the Verant family. We know rumors have been circulating about the recent take over and we want to assure everyone that nothing bad is going to happen and that we take care of our employees just like we do our customers. However, the old management was based on ideas and rules that do not conform to those of the Verant Vision ™. So we will be making some changes as follows:

1. We are doing away with specific cubicles. This will encourage people to get out and explore the building more. Terminals and phone will be placed all around the building and people can log on and use them wherever they are. The exception to this will be IS people. They can only log onto specific terminals placed in out of the way places and they cannot log on themselves but must ask a coworker from another area to log on for them. This will encourage people to socialize more, thus increasing morale.

2. Since this company is new to the Verant family we are going to reduce all salaries across the board. However, companies previously under the Verant umbrella will not be affected by the salary decrease. We have no justification for this except we like to screw with you.

3. All departments will now have factions. If you are working on a project that requires the aid of another department, you must do quests for that department to build up faction before they will help you. For example, if you are in Accounting and you need IS to make some changes to one of your reports, you must go to the appropriate programmer and give them donuts until they view you warmly. This will also have repercussions for lowering faction. In the above example, if you persist in calling the network admin because you forgot your password for the hundredth time you may become KOS (i.e. “You idiots bring out the worst in me. Prepare to die, A_Stupid_User01”). One note, Auditing will be KOS to all other groups.

4. All office supplies may be ordered through the new company store. The store will be on the Intranet and supplies can be ordered from there. The store will be up for 5 min, during which we will lose all of the orders placed at that time. After that, you must purchase all of your own supplies with your own money. No ETA on when the store will be back up.

5. Design documents for new projects will no longer be available from the users. They can only be obtained from high level executives who you must find and then beat the design specs out of them. However, only about .0000000001% of the executives will actually have the specs. It is best to corner them in the following zones: “Country Club”, “Board Room”, “Golf Course”, and “Mistress’ Bedroom”.

6. We have decided to redo the promotion process. If you apply for a promotion and you do not receive it, you will actually be demoted to a lower salary grade. This adds fun and excitement to the process since you never know if you apply for that supervisor job, whether you will get it or wind up plunging toilets.

7. Just to show that we can be bastards to everyone equally, we are going to restructure the executive level positions. Formerly an executive would get a raise every year if they performed their job at a satisfactory level. Under the new plan, every high level executive will only get a raise every 2 years at first and then increase a year thereafter (i.e. first raise at 2 years, next raise at 3 years, next at 4 years, etc). This saves us money and makes sure they keep working since they cannot afford to retire.

8. We understand that due to staffing problems in the past, certain people were “cross trained” to handle multiple tasks. We felt this was not fair to the people who are too stupid to learn new things so we felt we had to balance the situation. If you are capable of doing your work plus someone else’s, you will now be penalized by having to work twice as many hours for the same amount of pay. So those “hybrids” will be slaving over their work, while the incompetent morons head out to happy hour.

9. We found that too many people where making enough money to retire early. This is unacceptable. We have instituted a new rule such that you only get raises every four years, followed by a two-year period of no raises. Again, this saves us money and keeps the employees working.

10. We have noticed there are 2 kinds of employees, those with social lives and those without. In the past, preference was given to people with spouses, children, and social obligations, but no more. Unless you have the time to work for days on end without stopping for sleep, food, social interaction, etc, you will never get a raise, bonus, or recognition of any kind. Also, we are switching out everyone’s computers with IBM XT’s with 640K of RAM and changing our current network server to an Atari 2600. This insures that unless you can devote at least 20 hours a day to your job, you will never get any projects finished.

11. It has come to our attention that certain individuals are working so efficiently that they accomplish their tasks without the aid of coworkers. This is unacceptable since we want people to have to rely on their lazy, ignorant, incompetent coworkers to get anything accomplished. These people will undergo a “brain nerf”. Where applicable, you will be notified of the time and date of your surgery. The Auditing department is exempt since everyone hates them anyway.

12. We have decided to replace all managers with lazy, uncaring, sadistic bastards. These new managers will ignore all your pleas for help or information, and never let you call in sick, since all employees are lying, lazy jerks anyway. However, if you happen to belong to the Payroll department or any other large, powerful group, you will be given special perks, like bonuses, parties, and the key to the executive washroom.

13. In the past, whenever there was a disagreement between coworkers, or groups of coworkers, a manager would step in and resolve the conflict. As stated above, the new managers will be too lazy and apathetic to do this anymore, so all conflicts must be resolved among coworkers. The exception are those employees who belong to the large groups discussed in #12 above for whom the manager will step in and give them anything they want, including firing the offending coworkers.

14. All paychecks will be given out by a single person in payroll named Joan Smith. Joan only works for 1 hour a week and her schedule is secretly changed every week. Anyone wanting their paychecks must wait outside her office until she arrives and even then she only passes out a few of them if any.

15. We realize the company has been growing and that there is a shortage of office space. We have decided to open a new subsidiary in another state to help ease the overcrowding. A bus will be available to shuttle employees back and forth, but it will break down at least 95% of the time. The new subsidiary will offer many advancement opportunities, but the salaries will be below the industry and company averages. We expect people to flock to the new area.

16. We have decided to change the IS policies and procedures to match those of Verant’s. Therefore, all testing is banned, interoffice communication is to be stopped, and all software changes will be done with making things as inconvenient to the users as possible in mind.

17. In order to make your jobs as exciting as possible we have decide to institute random firings. High level executives will roam the cubicled halls firing people on a whim. We feel that this will add fun to your job, and with the also mentioned salary cuts, make the risk vs. reward of this company a place you want to work.

18. We realize that there may be some problems with these policies. If you have any questions you can either be ignored by your lazy, apathetic, manager, or send an e-mail to autodelete@verant.com.

Thanks and remember, “You are working for our company now.”

-Gordon
