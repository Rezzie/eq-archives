The Last Nerf - by Cyanna Thunderhammer

'Twas the night before Christmas', when all through the house,
not a thing was moving but my right hand and mouse.
The house, all dark, by my monitor was lit,
In hopes that level 30 soon would be hit.
My hubby was asleep all snug in the bed,
While sounds of his snoring rumbled in my head.
Across my keyboard my cat, Bear, did splay,
As I settled down for a long night's play.
While from the message boards rose such a clatter,
I logged onto EQ to see what was the matter.
I clicked on the icon and it came up in a sec,
since my new cable modem was as fast as all heck.
When, what to my wondering eyes should appear,
But a message of patching, which filled me with fear.
There was a message, and assertions were made,
I knew in a moment it must be McQuaid.
More rapid than eagles, the changes they came,
And he lied and he boasted, and called them by name:
"Nerf Kiting! Nerf feigning! Nerf a high level pet!
Nerf trade skills, Nerf items, Nerf all spells that you get!
You used to level and solo, What gall!
Now nerf away! Nerf away! Nerf away all!"
"From Qeynos to Freeport you used to could fly!
Now to try it you surely will die!"
So as the patch started, my modem was flashing,
As files were loaded and my hard drive was thrashing.
And then, in a twinkling, the patching was done,
and I knew that McQuaid may finally have won.
I logged into EQ with nary a sound,
Afraid of the nerfings that soon would be found.
My armor was nerfed, from my head to my foot,
And items were removed that I used to, could loot.
The spells in my spellbook were all changed or gone,
And I knew the improvements were all just a con.
I started to solo making sure to be wary.
For with all of these nerfings, it was sure to be scary!
The first mob I found was an orc conning green,
Who ripped out my lungs and munched on my spleen.
The stump of a my arm he held in his teeth,
And my intestines encircled his head like a wreath.
He hit me and kicked me right in the belly,
He squished me and squashed me like a bowlful of jelly.
And saying he hated me for being an elf,
Although I'm a barbarian, I thought to myself.
A flick of his wrist and a twist of my head,
And the next thing I knew I was lying there dead.
He spoke not a word, but went straight back to his spawn,
And then I was back on the Temple of Ro lawn.
Angry and frustrated I started to camp,
I rose from my chair and turned on the lamp.
A letter I wrote to Verant with a sigh,
I was sick of fighting greens only to die.
I canceled my account and went to the mall,
"Goodbye to EQ, Hello, Asheron's Call!".
