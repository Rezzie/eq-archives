This guide started when I posted an HTML file of frustration. I, like many people, hate grouping ... not because it is not a good idea ... and not because it can't be fun ... but because too many obstacles have to be overcome before grouping can be fun.

The obstacles:

1. Maintaining a steady group of friends.

a. Time zone differences and RL obligations create problems.

b. Some people or characters level faster than others.

2. Game mechanics

a. The grouping paradox: If a level 10 group needs another member, and you are level 12, they may (may) not want you because it hampers their experience. If a level 12 group needs another member and you are level 10, you may (may) not want to join because it hampers your experience.

b. Loot. Alpha loot, equitable currency distribution ... it's all a bit of a pain when you are in a group.

3. People are idiots.

a. Chances are ... if you find a group that is made up of strangers ... or if you are a stranger in the group ... you will find that most people don't know how to group effectively for shit.

b. Even effective group combatants are always finding ways to stall gameplay: 1) a character runs out of food; 2) a character is selling items as he fights ("hold on, guys ... someone wants to buy this derv ring I am selling"); 3) the player has to eat/sleep/pick-up-his-kids/have-sex-with-his-wife etc.; 4) link death, etc.

Granted, many of these items can be avoided if you are lucky, but odds are that you will not be able to avoid all (or even most) of them unless you are in a very well run guild or you play EQ with your RL friends.

The solution: Be capable of soloing if you have to.

But again, I was frustrated. Which classes are the best classes to solo?

Without a doubt, this is one of the most often asked questions on bulletin boards, and a couple guides have been established in an attempt to clear up the confusion.

Well, here is another guide. Rather than write the guide myself, I have instead included comments from people (from all over the world) who send email to my Klaknak site.

All of these are opinions, of course, and many of my emailers disagree fundamentally, so I think this guide may be very useful. Rather than stating the opinion of one person, it states the opinions of several ... enjoy.

A very important note: When I posted my whine, I made the following conditions: I had to have bind and I would prefer to have pets and SOW if possible. This will explain what some contributors mean by "your criteria."

Another note of some import: These advices were sent in just prior to Verant destroying the Necromancer, Druid and Bard classes, but with the imminent destruction in the air, so do not be surprised if you don't see much comment on these previously praised classes.

In General

"ALL classes can be soloed with little problem (yes, I said ALL) if you use your mind. Think about it for a second. Now I know what you are thinking, nothing can solo perfectly, but that's exactly correct. Nothing can solo perfectly, BUT everything can be soloed. Soloing is all about tactics, which way to approach your prey, how to get it's attention, how to keep alert of wondering mobs while attacking, etc..." - Kekvit
