February 10, 2000

It is obvious that one of Verant's worst customer relations problems is with its GMs and Guides. Let me offer a simple solution that would alleviate this problem and give the players a greater say in the evolution of this game. In cases of disputes involving game play situations such as kill stealing, harassment, naming, illegal looting and such let the concerned parties have the option of either accepting the GM or Guides decision in the matter or taking it to a jury of their peers.

This jury should be selected from a group of players of every level on the server, providing of course they are not connected to the players involved either through guild membership or immediate grouping. As in a normal judicial procedure give each play the right to exclude two members of the perspective jury and then let them present their case before them with the GM acting as judge and or prosecutor. Then allow the jury to decide and in consultation with the GM arrive at a punishment providing guilt is established. In this way much of the arbitrariness of GM decisions would be removed, players would be give new input into the workings of this game, and a very real problem would be solved. Moreover, a greater sense of fairness would be added to the administration of the game and some of the distance between the players and staff would be removed.

To expedite matters a time limit could be placed upon the procedings and appeal could be made the the Senior GMs in case the players involved felt they were unfairly treated. This, of course would not apply to technical issues such as overt hacking and other forms of electronic cheating except in cases where the player felt that they were unfairly accused of such actions and then a form of grand jury could be convened to determine if there was resonable cause act upon this matter, and if there were turn it over to the staff at Verant for a final disposition.
