"Dear Denizens of the Holy Klaknak:
It has been a while since I have posted anything of any substance, so I would like to take this opportunity to talk about what has been going on.

1. Everquest.
As we all know by now, I have a very deep love/hate relationship with Everquest and I think I am still a little bit in hate mode right now. The Nuggets of Justice was an absolute riot but then I hit level 16 with my necromancer. Level 16, for whatever reason, seems to be a very hard level for me to pass. I think it is at this time that I start getting really frustrated with the kill, sit, kill, sit, kill, sit thing. Anyway, I hit level 16 and found myself just kind of staring at the screen a lot. This was complicated by the fact that my dear Aunt Pali was spending more time with her Ranger on Prexus than with her Necro on Luclin.

One day, I decided to create a Barbarian shaman so I could eventually team up with her Ranger and run around killing everything.

Through a not so atypical series of events, that turned out to be the straw that broke the camel's back.

First of all, I absolutely hated being a newbie again. I realize that level 16 is not exactly the pinnacle of EQ accomplishment, but compared to being a level 1 nitwit again, it is nirvana.

Anyway, on one particular Saturday, I decided I would start powerlevelling my dude.

Whether through accident or design, Verant was in the throes of getting Kunark online and, whether causal or not, the servers were jumping and lagging and dying at an alarming rate. I think I lost connection that day close to 10 times. On top of this, some high level jackass was completely farming the newbie area killing pretty much everything in site. As any barbarians out there know, the barbarian newbie area can be quite perilous and a level one guy needs to pretty much camp as closely as possible to the guards to keep the deaths down under 3 or 4 an hour. Well, thanks to jackass, that wasnt possible, so I ended up having to venture way out in the wild. With a combination of level-one-ness, disconnects, lag and mobbing polar bears and wolves, I was dead more than I was alive.

I became completely fed up and nearly uninstalled EQ from my computer.

Instead, I started playing Asheron's Call.

2. Asheron's Call.
I had tried playing Asheron's Call when it first came out, but a few things kept getting in my way. First of all, the learning curve can be a little daunting. Granted, it is not too hard, but for someone playing casually (as I was), it took a while to understand the optimal way to create a character, learn new spells, etc. etc.

Second, the graphics really do kinda suck.

But after a while, I totally fell in love with the game and I am still in love, as far as that goes. The big reason for this is the total absence of idiots. Or, rather, the mostly total absent of idiots with freedom to completely avoid them (nay, to completely avoid the entire society of fellow gameplayers if need be). Asheron's Call is good at a number of things, but first and foremost it is utterly utterly utterly huge. The time it takes to run from West Freeport to Qeynos in EQ would maybe cover an inch of map territory in Asheron's Call. Huge. What this means is that you can always find a place to hunt that is completely free of people. And it's not like you are going to be missing out on awesome special items either. At the lower levels at least, I have found some really kick ass weapons and armor just killing random critters in the wilderness. Oh so nice, my friends.

3. Employment.
For a few months there, I was very broke and I am only now realizing how extremely taxing that was on my ability to function as a human. Last night I paid all of my bills and still have money in the bank and money to use for food. That is a good feeling.

The new job is going really well, in spite of the fact it is a nine to five job. I think a lot of this is because a) I am playing games and 2) my bosses aren't jackasses who use their positions of "power" to meet the needs of their fragile egos. I think too many of my earlier bosses in other 9 to 5 jobs took out their feelings of inadequacy on their employees. In fact, I now wonder if our entire American corporate culture - with all the buzzwords, idiotic policies, and bullshit - is nothing more than a kind of passive aggressive neurosis writ large. I dunno.

Anyway, as I said, the new job doesn't have this bullshit, so that is cool.

4. Women.
The women thing hasn't been going well at all, but again I am beginning to understand that being broke had a lot to do with this. While visiting my brother in Atlanta and going to various parties and whatnot here, I had ample opportunity to try my wooing skills, but with very mixed success. To sum up, I think I was just really fucking frazzled the whole time and my confidence was therefore kinda shaky. I would start talking to someone and my mind would just start wandering. Every beer I bought created stress akin to buying a home because I simply didn't have money to buy beer.

Also, as I have mentioned, a friend of mine is going through a separation with her husband and that has just really depressed me. Divorce just sucks. It has a way of weighing on my mind when in rutting mode. It just makes me wonder if all relationships, no matter how happy in the beginning, can eventually just grind down into nothingness. I am kinda down on that shit right now.

5. Daikatana.
Speaking of nothingness, I bought Daikatana.

6. Thief.
But I also bought Thief and I am really enjoying that right now. I have also been playing some Nox. Nox is a surprisingly addictive game. It is incredibly linear but I think I am actually enjoying it more than I enjoyed Diablo when it came out. That is blasphemy, I know, but there ya go.

7. Humor.
Humor will once again spring from my head, but I think it is obvious now that my humor output is directly tied to how much I am enjoying EQ at the time. I have always thought I would eventually make CK into a larger humor site (covering more games) and eventually I will (with the help of others), but with my new job and some pretty stringent contractual obligations, I have a lot of crap to work through first.

Woo hoo! Keep up the good fight!

- CK
