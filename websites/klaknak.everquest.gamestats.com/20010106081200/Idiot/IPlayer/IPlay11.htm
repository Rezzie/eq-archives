The Manor of Unrest - by Jamilyn Skyheart

The Manor of Unrest It was dark, the night -- the perpetual night. I approached the house known as the "Manor of Unrest." The rain and wind was getting worse, and this house was the only sanctuary to be found in this Gods-forsaken Hellhole known as the Cauldron. I was about to knock on the front door when a bolt of lightning struck a nearby exploiter. Boy, that was a close call! In the instant of that flash, I thought I saw a ghost through the window in the door. The feeling in my gut told me to run away, but I knocked on the door anyway. The door opened.

Once inside the vestibule, I was disturbed to find that no one was there. I was about to turn and run, screaming "TRAIN TO ZONE!" when the door slammed shut behind me. I knew that there was no turning back now, so I reached into my pocket and pulled out my magic gnome cookie. While it looks ordinary, it actually has the power to turn anything into a bottle of milk or a muffin. I knew this would come in handy. As a big flash of lightning lit up the night, the torches went out, and the house went black.

Alone in the dark and just a little afraid, I decided to have a look in the basement. Had I ever been in the Plane of Fear, or I never would have done that. The old steps creaked as I went into the basement. My glow-in-the-dark rusty short sword, which I always keep with me, gave off enough light to see by, so I pressed onward. Suddenly, from the farthest corner of the basement I heard a sound, no -- a voice that sounded suspiciously like Ambassador Dvinn's. Thinking fast, I used my magic gnome cookie and turned the nasty Dvinn into a large bottle of skim milk. I was so scared that I turned and ran up the stairs and toward the front door, almost soiling my poor tattered patchwork leggings. I went to open the door, when I realized that I was not clicking on the door, but on my spells. Instantly, I had rooted myself.

Desperate, I kept trying to open the door, screaming for help -- but no one was in the zone. Again, my guided hand slipped, and I cast flash of light on myself. All I could do was scream, as skeletons crowded around me and began to bash me. Once I regained my sight, I quickly cast Divine Aura. The roots had disappeared, and I ran for the corridor, until...a skeleton bashed me! The invulnerability quickly faded, and I fell on the pavement, bleeding to death. And then I awoke again, under the close care of the Priests of the Temple of Marr. ((based on a semi-true incident that happened to me around level 10))
