A note from Garg: "Me like thank Peregrine for good guide. It help Garg. Garg know who to eat now and who to save for later. Garg think K3wlDood taste like Froot Loop. Role player make good pudding. Powergamer give Garg gas but provide Garg with valuable fiber."

Everquest message boards can be a confusing place for a newcomer. This guide is meant to help straighten things out, by helping you identify the types of people on a board and some of the lingo you may hear.

First, some common acronyms and terms you may come across.

"IMO, and IMHO" -- These mean a person is indicating that they are right, and everyone else is wrong.

"wtf?" (This is always lower case on a message board) -- The poster is confused about what something means.

flame -- Normal reply

rant -- Normal post

Twink -- Anyone who has better equipment in-game than you.

Verant -- Deity of the message board, alternately worshipped and cursed at.

Powergamer -- Everyone on the board except you.

Now, let's discuss the common types of posters on the message boards:

Kewldoods-- These posts will be difficult to read, and will revolve around the letter u, as in "F*** u," "U suck," and "I ht u." Don't worry about trying to correct their grammar or change their ways, junior high school will do that for them.

Whiners -- These posts will begin with "I'm tired of...," "We should petition Verant for...," and "Class X sucks because..." These kind of people will see the bad side of any situation, and yet attract a large following from otherwise sensible people.

Roleplayers -- Their posts will always begin with "This is an IC message board, your post is OOC, you kewldood loser." Roleplay and in-character behavior are very important to them on the board. In-game they will usually be kewldoods (see above) or twinks (see above.) [Editor's Note: Ouch!]

Newbies -- These are people new to the board, who do not have this training. Their posts will begin with "I don't have the game yet, which class is best?" or "I don't have the game yet, but how long will it take for me to camp for JBoots and then move on to camp the GCS for the RLT, which is better than the XYZ, but I also want an ATR."

Merchants -- These people are interested in buying and selling items. You can identify their posts by the fact that they never have text, only a subject, which is usually, "Where can I get rubicite for my level 35 dwarf warrior?" or "Selling account, Lvl 35 dwarf warrior."

Jokers -- Most of their posts are jokes or parodies of EQ. You can never read them, because within 10 minutes, the threads have 600 replies of "LOL!" The best people, really, especially The Cackling Klaknak.

Bulk of the players of EQ -- Don't post at all, because they don't have Internet access from work.
