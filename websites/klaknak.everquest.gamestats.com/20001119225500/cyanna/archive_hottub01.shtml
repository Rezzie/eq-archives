"Recently, I have been hunting the realms with a long-time friend, Ms. Briolique. Things always went well, and we worked very well together (she is a gnomish cleric, and I am a hafling warrior). We have hunted quite succesfully together all across Norrath, from the Aviaks in Lake Rathetear, to the undead in the Estate of Unrest.

However, as of recently, her sister (who I will call "Stormmy") has been tagging alone. Oh, she works great in the group, she's a wizard, really balances things out with that whole generic D&D thing. She also has a way of getting anything that she asks for, and I mean ANYTHING. FBR's, Staffs of Writhing, you name it, and she's got it for free.The problem is though, is that she just won't be quiet! She talks constantly, talking about things that she obviously knows nothing about. She shouts rather than tells across the zone to talk to her high level friends, just so everyone knows that yes, Stormmy knows high level people. She butts in on things that she knows she has no business in. Worst of all, she's started calling me in real life asking me for rides to places!

My problem is, I want to tell Stormmy to bugger off, but at the same time I don't want to upset Ms. Briolique. Any help?

Sincerly, Helpless Hafling in Heavy Armour"

 

Dear Helpless Halfling

It seems you have 2 problems here, one in game and one in RL. As far as the game goes, I have a possible solution. I’ll take Stormmy off your hands. I would love to hang with someone who could get me high level loot for free and had high level friends to powerlevel me. Or, you can do what I used to do with my brother and pay people to play with him, thus keeping him out of my hair. (Sorry, bro). Which brings me to an interesting point. Stormmy sounds like a youngster by her immature acts (and the fact that she can’t drive). I have never met a pair of young siblings that did not get on each others nerves. My brother and I fought all the time. Maybe you should say something to your friend. I bet Stormmy tagging along both in RL and in a game, must be too much for her too. As for the RL problem of hitching rides, I have a possible solution. I used to car-pool with my brother, and he would get on my nerves from time to time. I knew that he absolutely HATED country music. So every time he was in the car I blasted country music until he refused to ride with me anymore (sorry again, bro).

- Cyanna Thunderhammer

Dear Solo,

I must admit I am a bit confused. One of my best friends in EQ is a druid and she has no problem with people liking her. She even stays anon most of the time because people are constantly bombarding her with requests for SOWs, binds and teleports. And I must admit, as a warrior, I would want a druid over any other class if I could pick one adventuring partner. I am so freaking slow I almost have to have sow and teleports to get anywhere. Plus they can heal, the damage shields are great, and evac is a must. So I would suggest finding some slow-ass, beat to hell, warrior who would find your unique abilities a godsend and the two of you will kick some serious butt. Believe me, for a 2 person group a warrior would be nuts to pick anything but a druid or shaman. And you eventually get regen too which is the clarity crack for warriors. Once you get a taste of regen you cannot live without it. I suggest hanging out near a dungeon and saying ‘Casting Regen. First hit is free.’ BTW can I get a port to North Karana?

- Cyanna Thunderhammer

Dear CK…umm…I mean, total stranger,
Have you tried an ice pick? Seriously (yeah, right), although stalking works in some cases, it usually results in criminal charges and restraining orders. Let’s for the sake of argument, say the object of your affections is a gnome necromancer. I suggest, my favorites, flattery and gifts. For example, say, ‘You know you look beautiful under the dim red glow of a fire beetle eye’ or ‘Your eyes are like pools of water filled with piranha’ or ‘I love the stench of death you are wearing’. And if you are on a tight budget, expensive gifts can be replaced with sentimental ones. Nothing says I like you, better than a stack of bone chips and a bottle of Kwalish. If that does not work, you can always try and get her drunk. Or as a final act of desperation you can try honestly telling her that you like her. I would take her to Highpass to the inn called the Tiger’s Roar. It is a beautiful place with a nice view of the waterfall. Buy her a couple of drinks (preferably the expensive ones from upstairs), and then tell her. And if things work out, there are rooms for rent right upstairs. Trust me, it works.

- Cyanna Thunderhammer

I think wood elf harrassment is the exception rather than the rule. So I do not think it is you. I am sure you are just as good looking as the other thousands of wood elfs that you exactly resemble. However, I try not to just give advice, but to also fix problems. So I’m sending over Psychlowombat, a master of sexual innuendo, to follow you around and ask you for a topless brush job.
