Poor Newbie's Almanac - by Mike Blinde

Greetings. I am Beenjammin Frankly, scholar, inventor and diplomatist, and I have composed a number of sayings that I believe may help young adventurers in the world of Norrath achieve wisdom and success.

I present them to you, styling them my Poor Newbie's Almanac, that you might glean enough knowledge to survive the harsh world and become a success.

* 'Tis hard for a corpse to stand upright, unless you're a necromancer.

* If a man could have half his wishes, he be half clothed in rubicite.

* People who are wrapped up in themselves solo a great deal.

* Magicians, shamen, druids, rangers and clerics summon feasts, and everyone else must buy their damn food.

* Glass, china and reputation are easily cracked, while full dungeon spawns are not.

* Love thy neighbor, but do not let him kill-steal from you.

* They that won't be counseled can't get into groups.

* An open foe may prove a curse; but a train of foes is much much worse.

* A bandage in time saves 9 hours of playing to get that exp back.

* Many complain of their class's nerfs, few of their advantages.

* He that lives on hope will not make it to his next experience level.

* Be slow in choosing a guild - a lot of them suck.

* Early to bed and early to rise, makes a man fall behind in his friends in exp. .
