A Week in a Newbie's Life

(or, rather, Death)
by Vargo


Most of this stuff is probably pretty common and not very new or original, but I had told these stories to others and they liked them, so I thought I would send them, just for the fun of it. The thing that may separate them from other stories is that I am not really recalling these things from long ago. I am very much a newbie, having been playing EverQuest for a little over a week. Just a way to see through a true (and very frightened) newbie's eyes. So, here we go, in no particular order (just as I remember them).

All of these took place when I played on the Saryn server as Kilgallen, Gnome Rogue (all my C's are Gnome Rogues) except for the one about how I fell in the water. I've already forgotten his name.

* * *

The very first time I played EverQuest, within a few seconds of starting in Ak'anon, I walked through the waterfall and fell into the water and was lost for about five minutes. I finally got out, but it was still too dark to see and I continually fell in. I finally decided to not be stupid and turn up the gamma correction. By the time I had done that, my swimming was pretty good.

I have actually had about four or five characters. The reason I started over was because I lost the letter I started out with that I was to give to the Rogue Guild boss. Little did I know, when I was looting my dead bodies, I was looking at the wrong slots. Instead of seeing the pretty obvious loot slots, I saw my current inventory slots and decided, 'Hey, I guess it's gone.' I think I went through three guys before I figured that out.

One of my first fighting experiences as Kilgallen was in the mines of Ak'anon (I love that place), playing with a newbie necro I had met. He had conjured up a skeleton with some bone chips I thankfully had on a previous dead body. So, we decided to take on some of the decaying gnomes down there. Good idea, I said. We looked around until we had found one, and attacked. The necro's skeleton buddy went down, one hit, I went down, one hit. When I spawned again, there was my necro friend. I have no idea what happened. I think one of the necro guild guys had made that skele super, but I still don't know. It didn't end there. For those that have never been to Ak'anon to get to the mines, you have to go down a pretty steep little slope next to a cliff. Usually I just drop off the lip and land close to the top of the slope, then sort of slide down to the mine. On the way back to get my stuff, I dropped a little too far down the cliff, and missed the slope. I was dead again. On my way back, the server died. My friend and I decided that my stuff wasn't really worth it anymore.

We had decided to go outside after that and fight in the Steamfonts, where there were rats we could fight. I think I died a few times, but nothing special. Now, I'm not sure of the order, but some nasty things happened to me out there.

I was fighting with another guy (I think) up by the druid ring and all of a sudden, this huge troll comes out of nowhere (how easy is that for a troll to do), comes running up to me, and as I'm blinking, slices me down like nothing. It was Bugglebup, and it is some sort of quest monster. I was reading about it and people said they could never found it. Well, my level one guy found it alright. That was a scary experience, tell you that much. I was deathly afraid of going back to get my corpse. But my friend confirmed it was gone, so I (slowly) went to fetch it.

Another occurrence with the same guy was when he decided to fight an infected rat. I jumped in. I got diseased and died. If memory serves me, I had gone back and gotten my stuff when all of a sudden, a kobold shaman jumped out of nowhere. I was hit with a few spells, and I took off for the guards. Nothing happened for a while, so I figured I was outrunning him and would be soon safe. Wrong. Blam, a disease spell hit me out of nowhere, and the sweet LOADING... message came up. Once again, I spawned and creeped out to my stuff. Luckily, the shaman was gone, and once again, my level one guy learned a lesson in destruction.

I've had problems, being a newbie, with how things work, the keys, etc. Once I auto-attacked a Gemchoppers Guild guy while trying to talk and he destroyed me. I decided that the book was right about re-mapping that key. Another bad thing was a mix-up with the 'Run' button. I was fighting a spiderling at...oh...probably second level. Of course, a buddy comes to help him. I wasn't doing so well with one, and this other wasn't helping. I decided to run while the running was good. So I took off. But I kept getting hit. I figured the spiderlings were faster than I thought. When I died, I noticed that I was still walking. Another lesson learned. Never walk away from a fight.

There was only one time that I ever successfully reached the guards. I wanted to try my second level skills on some runaway clockworks. I started fighting one. And, as per usual, a pal joined him. This time, I was set to run, and I ran like the wind. I regenerated as I went, going for the guards, when I noticed that I should have seen the tunnel to Ak'anon, where the guards are, already. Luckily, in my haste, I had run quite a ways away from it. So I pulled off a U-turn, giving the clockworks some time to bit me up all nice and raw again, then ran and ran. I saw a house, so I knew I was close. Then I saw the lovely Watchmen, waiting for my newbie self to bring some foe to them. I got there after an eternity and they took care of it, but it was a close one. -Very- close. And I will always love the guards from this day forth.

Well, that's about it. Hope you found it insightful or at least a little enjoyable. Maybe both. A lot can happen in a week, and this I know. But, strangely, no matter how grisly my doom, it's still fun. And I keep trying.

Vargo, Gnome Rogue of the Luclin Server (formerly known as The Short Guy Called Kilgallen)
