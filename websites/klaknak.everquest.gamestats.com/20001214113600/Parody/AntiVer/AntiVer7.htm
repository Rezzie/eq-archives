Peregrine Interviews the EQ Banker

To better serve the fine people of Norrath, I arranged for a private interview with Bungle Holefiller, Assistant Deputy Chief Manager of Operations for the Bank of Ak'Anon. I thought it would be a good way to get to know the people behind the counter and clear up some mysteries and rumors about the way banking works on EQ.

Peregrine: Sorry to interrupt your busy schedule. But I have to ask, with all those clockwork bankers out front to deal with the public, what do you and your staff do all day?

Bungle: Well, we fill countless ledger books with doodles, take long lunches, and make play-forts out of platinum pieces, mostly.

Peregrine: Err, okay. So, is it hard keeping track of the money and items of people from all over Norrath? I know my question is, how do you sort out whose money is who's?

Bungle: We don't, normally. Just take it out back and throw it on the pile. Then when people want to make withdrawals, we just summon some more up at the counter.

Peregrine: *blinks in disbelief* Are you saying that you can magically summon coins? You don't bother actually using all the coins you have?

Bungle: Oh, yes, been doing it for years, my boy. Came down from the top, part of the Player-Based Economy. The lads in that part of the office have a saying, "Spend all you want, we'll make more!" Works out pretty well, really.

Peregrine: And how do you store all the items? The bank doesn't seem big enough to hold all the lockers and drawers it must take for that.

Bungle: Well, it isn't. We use a big summoned dimensional pocket, that takes care of most of the items. Some of the lighter stuff we just throw away.

Peregrine: A summoned pocket? What if, umm, the summoner logs off?

Bungle: Oh-ho! We'd be busy then, wouldn't we! Never happened before, guess we'd have to blame a bug. Good thing is, no matter what happens to the stuff we hold, if it's lost, we blame a bug. Let the GMs take care of all that mess!

Peregrine: It doesn't sound like a very good way to run a business. So the items just sit in the pocket until the person needs them?

Bungle: *lights a large, expensive cigar* Well, no. They're usually on loan. To our clients.

Peregrine: On loan? *greedy look in his eye* Can you...erm...loan me some rubicite then?

Bungle: *laughs* No, no, no! Not to you, our other clients. You know, the orcs, goblins, lizard men, what have you.

Peregrine: Why would you give them items? Aren't they our enemies?

Bungle: You have to understand, son, that there aren't that many magic swords and pieces of armor in the world. Have to do a little recycling, you know. You put a runed totem staff in the bank, after a few days it goes out to the gnolls in Paw, and eventually it will end up back here! Hell, if it weren't for us, there'd be nothing for you heroes to fight! You wouldn't believe how much it costs us a month to keep the orcs and minotaurs in business.

Peregrine: So you're saying that you, the banks, control the monsters we fight? If it weren't for you, the world would be peaceful now?

Bungle: Sure. But peace is boring, we'd have warriors brawling in the streets, wizards holding up vendors, dynamic quests being run all the time, what confusion! Better this way. The way it has always been. The great circle, we call it. You wouldn't be, ah, causing any trouble for us after this, would you?

Peregrine: No, of course not! I wouldn't...I mean, who would believe it?

Bungle: *opens his ledger book and reveals a Doodle of Power, putting it in front of Peregrine's face* There's nothing unusual going on here, son. Just your friendly neighborhood bank, doing normal business. You go tell that to all the good folks.

Peregrine: *a glazed look in his eyes* Of course. Nothing going on here but normal business.

In conclusion, friendly neighborhood bank. Everyone should come and deposit all their possessions.

So, there you have it. My thanks to the busy folks at the bank for letting me talk to Mr. Holefiller. It's quite a job keeping up with all that money, but they have secure facilities just for that purpose. I say, good for them! So remember, deposit all your possessions at your friendly neighborhood bank.
