Chapter One - The Half Eaten Sandwich

I hate nature and everything in it.

I didn't realize this until I woke up one morning not as a Temporary Resource Technician at the state university psychology department but as a wood elf ranger in the city of Kelethin, land of Norrath.

But I am getting ahead of myself.

My name is Ralph Nike (no relation). I am single and live in Plankton Triangle, Virginia, a suburb of Newport News.

A couple nights before the day after, my friend Jim and I were surfing through the channels on our apartment television when we noticed that the cable man had accidentally added HBO to our basic cable package. And as if this boon was not enough, that very night HBO was showing a Tales of the Crypt marathon from 6PM to 6AM the next morning.

Needless to say, we were thrilled.

Unfortunately, since both of us were broke, we didn't have any drugs.

A couple weeks earlier, Jim tried to score some by going to the local psychiatrist complaining of "global night vision," but all they gave him was a box of Zoloft samples. So, caught between taking laxatives and popping Zoloft, we decided to take Zoloft as the marathon began.

***

During an episode where a man has feline hormones injected into his brain, Jim started getting a little ... moody.

"Get off my cloud, man!" he shouted at the television. "Get off my cloud! Your hormones don't fool me."

"Dude, shut up," I said, wiping raspberry fun syrup from my leutinator.

"This ... man ... he is on my cloud, man, and I want him off!" Jim shouted again.

"He's not on your cloud, Jim. Sit down."

"Hey, I didn't major in chemical anthropology for nothing," he said. "His feline hormones don't scare me."

"Jim ..." I said, trying to pull his ass down.

He would have nothing of it. He dove for the television but missed, gashing his head on the entertainment center. Jim screamed, scaring away the Lenin leprachauns.

"I have to plug the blood!" he screamed and ran into bathroom.

Disentangling myself from the couch, I followed Jim into the bathroom where he was stuffing all of our porn into the toilet, clogging it up nicely.

"I have to plug the blood!" he screamed. "Before the feline hormones escape!"

"Dude, man," I said. "You're wasting good porn ..."

But he would have nothing of it ... and the fun syrup found me again. I passed out.

***

The next day, I woke up to the sound of plunging. Bleary eyed, I stumbled into the bathroom where Jim was vigorously trying to plunge several months worth of porn out of our toilet.

"It's hopeless," he said.

"What got into you last night?"

"I don't know," he said. "I had to clear my head a bit."

I tried to nod, but my chin hit my chest and I was unable to lift it. In the corner of my eye, a shiny object caught my attention.

"What's that?" I asked.

"Oh, that!" Jim said, leaping to his feet. "I think it is the key to my car's trunk!"

"No way," I said. "You lost that 3 years ago. I think there is some beer in your trunk!"

"I know, I know," said Jim. "Let's go see if it's still there."

I tried to nod again, but my chin was still stuck to my chest. Jim popped me on the forehead, moving my head back.

"Let's go," he said.

***

A few hours later, we were sitting in the front of the television drinking a case of three year old Milwaukee Best and smoking some pot we had scrounged up from the carpet of the trunk.

"Are you sure that carpet is safe," I said, trying to talk, spit out carpet, and hold my shit in at the same time.

"<schuh schuh>," coughed Jim, trying to keep his shit in too.

***

When I woke up again, it was the middle of the night. An advertisement for Nick at Nite screamed from the television set and my stomach was wracked with a severe case of the munchies.

"Jim," I said.

No response.

"Jim."

No response.

I grimaced as I stood and walked into the kitchen. I opened up the cupboard:

1. minute rice.

2. lettuce.

3. bread

4. A packet of soy sauce

"Ah," I thought. "That would make an excellent rice and salad sandwich."

Drooling with hunger, I began putting the sandwich together. If I had only turned on the light, I would have realized that it is not good to keep lettuce in the pantry. But I didn't and I hadn't. I passed out again.

***

When I woke up, I was standing in a courtyard. A garish sign greeted me:

Welcome to Kelethin: City of Elves

"Elves," I thought. "I told Jim we shouldn't smoke that car carpet."

I lifted the rice and salad sandwich to my lips.

"Welcome to Everquest," it said.

It was going to be a long day.
