The Verant Philoosophy (sic) by Rendr.

We all pondered, all of us, about the moronic decisions that Verant makes. The seeming stupidity, casually ignoring the protests of its customers, disdainfully shrugging off petitions or ignoring them altogether. It seems as if there is neither rhyme nor reason to any of it. "It is contrary to game philosophy," or some similar phrase is always given as the reason. Sometimes it is "bad for the player-run economy," but it all really leans to the same issue. I wondered and pondered, frustrated at the apparent stupidity of it all, and then suddenly I was enlightened. It all became clear to me in a peaceful flash, and I haven't been the same sense. Verant's game philosophy can be summed up in just a few words. Knowing this, it explains everything, makes all clear, penetrates to the heart of all motives. Simply put, you have to die. You must die as often as possible but continue to struggle for the accomplishment you will never achieve.

The best way to illustrate is to take example of the changes that have been made. Let's begin with the enchanter. Memory blur was not resistible, and it cleared the monster's hate list. Well, we can't have that. If the big monsters that can kill you cannot remember who they hate, then you would get to live. That's contrary to game philosophy. You HAVE to die.

Nerfing kiting? Anyone? It's simple. If you are kiting, then the monsters you worry about can't catch you and kill you. If you can kite, you aren't dying, and of course, that's contrary to game philosophy.

Ever wonder why in every zone, Verant put at least one highly aggressive monster that is far more powerful than any player who can productively hunt there? Griffins in East Commons, Hill Giants in West commons (not to mention the dark elf dragoons for you good races), young Kodiaks in Nektulos, Spectres and Sand giants in RO, and Oasis. We could also talk about the Paladins in Innothule, the vengeful soloist in Everfrost. It goes on and on. Why? It's so simple, it means that at any moment, you might have to die, and there is no safety. Better yet, there is absolutely nothing you can do about it because they will spawn right on your head if they need to. Everybody dies. It's a perfect implementation of game philosophy.

Ever wonder why Verant not only made merchants virtually invincible but also aggressive to the wrong height or skin color or religious affiliation? Bigots at Verant? No. no. no. It means that if you wander, WHEN you wander, once again, you get to die, and you are completely powerless to keep it from happening. You are tempted to death in the hope of selling this worthless junk you have picked up. Enticed by the dream that you may get wealthy enough to not die anymore, but Verant has your lesson ready for you. Game philosophy is inescapable.

How about those enchanters again? Let's talk about Enchant Silver. Is that a good opportunity to die or what? They put the only copy as deep as they possibly could into the one city on Norrath guaranteed to kill you on site. Neriak. Now all they have to do is make invisibility drop without a seconds notice... Oh yeah, they could also have a lot of guards wandering around the merchants... Wait, a necromancer just one room away would help... with a pet! That's brilliant, a skeleton pet... And they have to try this at 8th level? It's positively brilliant. They could not kill you more effectively without camping your spawn point. It's almost as entertaining as having a guide /kill you and giggle when you respawn.

What about those pet spells guys? Is that not sheer genius in game philosophy application? Take the single most necessary spell of the pet caster and bury it into one of the nastiest, most vicious locales known to Norrath, and make them run it starting at 8th level. High Hold pass. If you are 8th or 12th level, that is some real death for you. Or better yet, chicken out, don't go get it. Live with that lvl 4 pet and try to keep going. Good heaven is THAT ever some death. Always remember. You HAVE to die. It is required.

Want that glowing stein? OOOoooohhhh, yes. Pretty. So tempting. All I need is that Koalindl fish... Uh oh. It's so simple and elegant. If you kill the fish in Queynos they just dispatch a guard to kill you. Then you die. Perfect. Or you can kill the dog in Halas, and the barbarians hate you, and kill you. Perfect. Then take your final stuff to Ogguk where those Ogres are just going to love you and hug you, and make you die. It's great.

But wait a minute. Damn! Those enchanters can do it without dying. And it's easy for them. Well nuts. That is just an unacceptable violation of game philosophy, so Verant will just make it worth less than half of what it was, and now it doesn't matter at all. Oh well, have to put in some more killing next time.

What about that economy? Isn't it great? If everyone is equally poor, then it is child's play to make everyone die. They are all the same, and easy to predict. The monsters can be built, and sure as hell they'll kill everyone. If trade skills worked, or if something valuable were easy to get, then only some of us would die. If someone could become rich, or if his wealth made him powerful, he wouldn't die. It's contrary to game philosophy. Everybody MUST die. It's so simple. I don't understand why I didn't see it before.

Similarly, if you come up with a really clever tactic to get an edge, or if you are able to hone your skills and timing to perfection and make yourself a little better than your fellow, you are in clear violation of game philosophy. You're not dying. That's exploitation, and an offense that they will terminate your account for. So make sure you post your excellent ideas to message boards where Verant can read them and nerf them. That way everybody gets to die again, and the philosophy is restored to its former greatness once again.

Ever been killed through the wall? Through a door? Had an invincible merchant teleport through his own shop to slay you in a single blow? It's all great for maintaining game philosophy. If he hits you through a wall or through the ceiling, or around a corner where you can't even target him back , then it makes it even more likely that you will contribute your corpse to the betterment of the game. Make certain that you do not do the same in return. If you find a way to kill a monster where he cannot hit you back, that is clearly an exploitation of game geometry and absolutely intolerable. If you do that, you won't die, and you HAVE to die. It's required.

GM events. This is where Verant trains it's people on game philosophy. They come running into a zone as a group where each member could easily slay every player currently in the zone, and then they do just that. It's glorious. Bodies are everywhere. Even after the greatest of warriors shows up, there will still be casualty after casualty, and if there isn't. If someone just actually walks up and kills the GMs, then that is a poor end. Very disappointing. While they are at it, they make sure to reward those that die the best. If you are the 50th level wizard that finally brought the beast down, you are lucky is you get a polished copper, but if you were that low level shadow knight who flung himself not only into death, but certain death, happy to accept it, you may find yourself one of the richest newbies in the game. Won't do you any good, I've seen that newbie in rubicite running from a moss snake, but it will make you feel great about game philosophy.

If one death is good, how about multiple deaths? Isn't if great that is you are smashed by something way out of your league, now you get to go back stark naked to try it again? He'll almost certainly be waiting for you, and the better you did before dying, the deeper you traveled, the more certain it is that you will die not only once but twice, and mayhap even more? That of course presumes that the wandering death machine for your zone doesn't intercept you and increase the body count even further.

But more insidious yet, since you are certain to die, all those little green beasties that can't kill you anymore get another shot at it, and stark naked they will probably take you out, but on the off chance you beat them, you certainly won't get any xp. And if there were anything just too huge for you to even have a chance at anywhere near your body, you can bet as sure as the sun rises that it will be camping your corpse, knowing full well that you are WILLING to die to get that stuff back.

Game philosophy is in ultimate triumph. Do you see it now? Verant isn't stupid. They are precise, methodical, and they have a simple, highly maintained system for maintaining game philosophy. The skill with which they make you die is absolutely commendable. They are intelligent, very good at their jobs. The ranks of apathetic game masters and guides are eagerly in training to be of such quality as their forbears, and skilled they are becoming. It looks likely that Verant may have generations of people to come who are capable and willing to enforce the noble quality of Everquest Game Philosophy.
