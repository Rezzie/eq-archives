Basketball - reviewed by Derrick W.

This sport COULD drag you away from Everquest were your computer or brain permanently damaged. But just barely.

Why do I say so? Well, this is coming from someone who devoted four years of his life to the sport in High School. It... um... like taught me life lessons and stuff and made me a stronger person and... Okay it sucked... No, I'm actually kidding, I DID get alot out of basketball. But to compare it to EQ? Thats just crazy talk! Lets break it down, shall we?

If you STOP playing basketball, your skills begin to atrophy. If you stop playing basketball in order to play everquest, your MUSCLES begin to atrophy. This is bad when you try to play again. You may find that, whereas before your shots toward the basket actually clanged off the rim (WOO HOO! wait, why is everyone chanting 'brick'?), your shots now fall, light as a feather, into the arms of the 8 foot bohemeth standing beneath the rim. Everyone knows, though, that you can stop playing EQ and come back to it with almost all skills relatively intact. (lets camp for rubicite!)

While playing Everquest, you are frequently forced to group with scantilly clad female wood elfs. Darn! The monsters you fight are tough, but they never get TOO close to you... and until Microsoft perfects Smell-o-vision (coming in DirectX 8!) you don't actually smell them. In basketball, you group with people who sometimes have only the most rudimentary understanding of the human language ('dish the rock!' 'pardon?' 'THROW ME THE DAMN BALL!' 'Why didn't you just say that?'), and you are thankful when they are NOT scantily clad (I don't care if we ARE skins, I'm wearing my damn shirt!). The people you fight are just as intense upon your destruction, but... they touch you. They get their sweat on you. You have to rub up against them. It is a health hazzard (ed note: sic. Not to be confused with "duke of"]. They also have a tendency to smell (especially Mr. 8 foot bohemeth), and that tendency requires no special hardware for you to get the full effect.

In Everquest, you can TRY to roleplay. Sure, some people may not be receptive (kewl, dood, we got sum rolplay putz). But you can seek out those of like mind. Ever try roleplaying on the hoops court? 'Pardon me, but wouldst thou pointest me the way to The Hole... my holy quest is to deliver this magic orb there!' You'd be lucky to walk out alive.

The only UP side to playing hoops over playing everquest is the relative lack of equipment needs. Say you are a warrior type. You lose your gear in Everquest, you are basically screwed. You lose your ball while playing hoops, you can easily get another, or ask someone else to bring it next time! If your a mage type, don't even walk in the gym. They could smell you coming.
