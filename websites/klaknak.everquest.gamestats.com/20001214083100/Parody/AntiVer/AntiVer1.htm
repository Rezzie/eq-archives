What should have been on the EQ box - by Kropdekoms

Superior AI

Our computer-controlled characters boast artificial intelligence second to none. You will witness guards exhibiting emotions ranging from laziness to apathy as they stand around idly while hostile creatures roam freely about the cities. Monsters know precisely how do defeat you each and every time: once their health is down to 50%, they will flee. You won't be able to follow them, because they will utilize Verant's patented "lag evasion" technique, disappearing into thin air. Ten minutes later the same monster will return, at full health, and slay you while you're sitting to rest.

Massively Multiplayer Means Massive Human Interaction

You will be immersed in a world populated by thousands of other real, live people. Whether they play a Human, a Gnome, or an Ogre, you will experience the thrill of witnessing the poorest spelling and grammar and the coarsest profanity of people all across the globe. Thanks to the /shout, /auction and /ooc functions, being miles away from someone speaking will not prevent you from discovering that "Alanon is gay" or "Macros steals kills."

More Realistic Than Real Life

In order to provide a worthy challenge for even the most seasoned adventurers, our realism exceeds the real life properties defined by God Himself! Experience being attacked through walls by monsters. Witness the awe of super-intelligent bats, rats and spiders which are capable of opening doors. Want to run for days? No problem. You'll never get tired! Want to swim across the ocean? You can do that, too. Take a trip on the boat across the sea and amaze your friends by walking up through the floor!

Three Huge Continents and Dozens of Meticulously Detailed Zones

Marvel at the snowy peaks of Everfrost. Wonder at the magical woods of Faydark. Slosh through the vile swamps of Inoruuk. Each of the three continents of Norrath features dozens of zones (except for the Erudin continent, which only contains two!). Ludicrous load times between zones will allow you ample time to eat, since you won't be doing much of that once you become addicted to EverQuest!

Breathtaking Graphics

With the proper hardware, you will enjoy all that EverQuest has to offer - two layer 3D skies, awesome particle effects, mediocre rendered lighting and fancy weather conditions (note: "proper hardware" excludes video cards that were designed earlier than the February, 1999).

Play Any of 12 Races and a Wide Variety of Distance Classes

With the exception of the High Elf and Wood Elf races, which are essentially the same races with different skin textures, each of the 12 races are more or less unique. Play any character from a Halfling to a Barbarian! Many class differences are so cleverly subtle that people may not have any idea what class you belong to! The classes are also well balanced, except for enchanters, who got the shaft. Good luck trying to solo with your enchanter, losers! We even went so far as to force you to choose a deity to worship. If anyone meets another player who even remembers what deity they chose when creating their character, we (Verant) will pay that person $5000!

A Multitude of Possibilities

Be a fisherman, a tailor, or a blacksmith. It's wide open. (Note that you will have to devote a very large portion of your life to EverQuest to become even slightly proficient in any of these skills.) Thirsty? How about some water? Or maybe some milk, if that suits your fancy! The possibilities are limitless! You can play on Rallos Zek, our PvP server (on which every single player in the game will slaughter you for no reason) or one of the other servers (on which mortal enemies such as Dark Elves and High Elves are forced to coexist in perfect harmony). Players who have invested 500+ hours into a character will be rewarded with a character powerful enough to obtain unique clothing, so that you may distinguish yourself from every other member of your race and class!

A Living Game

In our valiant haste to make this groundbreaking game available to the general populace, we were forced to overlook certain issues. Enchanters, don't plan on enchanting anything items anytime soon; necromancers, don't expect to concoct any potions through alchemy. But rest assured that we've thought about all that! Every Monday we'll update the game at midnight; we'll also make numerous other alterations during the rest of the week to repair any serious glitches that might have been caused by the update on Monday. Don't worry, we promise by the time EverQuest II is released we'll have EverQuest I pretty much complete! You're In Our World Now!(tm)
