EQ Glossary - by Awynder of Tunare

EQ Glossary Bind - n. The spell that sets where your character reappears after death. By default this will be the furthest possible point from wherever it is you feel like hunting at that particular moment.

Brad McQuaid - n. Head of Verant and chief designer of EQ. Has a level 113 ranger wielding a paladin-only superweapon. Can’t understand for the life of him why the masses think the game has problems. See also Marie Antoinette

Camping - v. One of the truly lovely aspects of EQ. The process of making your virtual character sit on his virtual butt for up to 100 hours of online time waiting for a virtual frog/goblin/gnoll/etc to spawn so your party can kill it and begin squabbling over whose turn it was to loot the rare item. In the end most people forgo camping and simply buy the item off of Ebay. The people from whom they buy these items are those who make camping their profession. This means camping and selling the items on Ebay is the only trade skill in EQ known to work at this time. Verant is currently in negotiations to buy Ebay so they can nerf this process

Con - n. An NPC’s or MOB’s level relative to your own, color coded for easy and fast determination of your chances against it in battle. Cons go from green to red like thus: Green - Probably will kill you Blue - Very likely will kill you White - LOADING, PLEASE WAIT Yellow - LOADING, PLEASE WAIT Red - LOADING, PLEASE WAIT

Firey Avenger - n. Rumored holy sword that can only be obtained by: slaying all six dragons (four of whom don’t exist in-game yet) - three of them solo and unarmed, memorizing the names and stats of every character on every server, mowing Brad McQuaid’s lawn once a week, and successfully making a class 3 steel-shafted blade-tipped arrow with a fletching skill of 1. The last part of the quest (believed to be impossible) is to successfully find the Drunkard in Kelethin and finally shut up all the shouting newbie idiots. Rumored stats are unknown, but my brother’s friend’s nephew’s next door neighbor once heard a guy say that he saw on a web site it’s 1H slash 500 dam / 10 delay and casts ice comet every round. A paladin-only sword. Also wieldable by rangers provided they run very large gaming companies.

Guard - n. A special type of NPC that stands around alternately glowering dubiously at some players and smiling warmly at others. The job of these level 45ish characters in full plate armor is to protect their cities from being invaded by giant skunks and wasps and to kill newbie bards who just bought Chords of Dissonance and forgot to turn it off. Have a nasty habit of being AWOL when a player needs help with some pissed-off MOB on his tail, only to appear suddenly ten miles from nowhere to "save" you from a yellow con you had down to 5HP.

Infravision - n. An attribute possessed by some races in the game whereby they can see in the dark almost as well as a legally-blind real life human with one glass eye wearing a blindfold in a closet. Races without infravision do not have this helpful ability and suffer a bit during the game’s night periods.

Kill Stealer - n. Any caster that successfully kills a MOB, anywhere, any time, even if he/she is the only one in the zone.

Kite - n. A combat tactic that allowed certain classes to succesfully kill MOBs, something Verant apparently is against. Has been nerfed.

LD - n. Linkdead, crashed, went offline, lost connection suddenly, etc. Usually happens in one of two situations: 1) camping really tough rooms just as three level 35 mobs spawn (usually in this instance it’s all five of your group mates that go LD) and 2) just as your character is running for the zone border with a hill giant on your heels. Verant has now added a thoughtful "So-and-so has gone linkdead" message when your group mates drop, so you no longer have to wonder if they’re really LD or if they’re just standing around watching while you solo those two spectres.

Loot - n. Money, weapons, magic items, etc. that can be found on the corpses of MOBs. Unfortunately, MOB corpses are not just lying around - you have to manufacture them yourself (see con). v. To acquire loot from a MOB’s corpse. This often requires an agreed-upon order beforehand to avoid arguments over who has the rights to the goodies. Many parties use the alphabetic system, which is why you see so many characters nowadays named Aaaaaamandarn and Aaaaaaaaaaaaaaandrielle. Some parties use a random process. In either case, as soon as the rare loot appears, it is the job of one person in the party to loot it out of turn, and offer "Hey, I haven’t looted anything in ten minutes!" as an excuse.

MOB - n. Generic term for monster. Singular. It is believed to be mandatory that everyone use this term even though no one can begin to explain what it means.

Nerf - v. The act of reducing the effectiveness of a class or skill when it becomes apparent people are succeeding at it. This process happens to every class and every skill at some point. It is believed that by the year 2003 all the classes will have only hand-to-hand combat and no spells except Identify Item, and will be able to use Sense Heading only, at which point the game will be declared perfectly balanced. After the following patch, in the name of game balance, Identify Item will be removed from all spellcasters.

NPC - n. Non-player character. Can be distinguished from player characters by the fact that NPCs will actually appear to speak and spell correct English much of the time.

Powergamer - n. Term used with derision to describe anyone who levels faster than you do.

Trade Skill - n. A "career" that a player can take up in-game to earn money. The moment the player discovers a trade skill that allows him/her to make money, the skill is nerfed and the process starts over.

Train - n. A flock of angry MOBs chasing some poor character to the zone border. The group of monsters picks up others along the way until an entire line of them is chasing the poor sap. The "trainer" may or may not warn his unsuspecting fellow players (the "trainees") at the zone border of the oncoming slaughter via a /shout (don’t bet your life on it). Some zones in the game are so frequented by trains that most of the MOBs are permanently at the border, making the depths of the dungeons often the safest places to be for some peace and quiet. See Unrest, Mistmoore, Cazic Thule, Runnyeye, Crushbone, Befallen, Guk, Najena, Solusek, Permafrost, Blackburrow

Twink - v.. The act of giving newbie characters, usually the twinker’s own, very high-level equipment or armor for the express purpose of allowing those characters to level quickly to the point that they can go get more of the same armor to give back to the original character (or something like that). Also a character wielding weapons/armor he/she is too low or lazy to get for him/herself. See also Brad McQuaid
