Hackers Respond

by the Cackling Klaknak

After several hours of basically begging ShowEQ supporters to defend their position, two finally agreed to answer the questions I posted on their message board. In the spirit of intrigue, I will name them "Mr. X" and "Mr. Y" (for no real reason whatsoever ... their answers can be found on the site's bulletin board).

Interview with Mr. X.

Mr. X: [to another member of the message board; one person didn't want to visit my site because he was afraid I would log his visit and turn him in]. First, let me address the issue of having and IP address logged. What are you talking about? If you think this is the only way that someone can get your IP address, you have some serious misconceptions. Second, have you ever heard of NAT? I share a handful of external addresses with out 1000 other machines. So please post how this is relavent to anything hanz...Now, as far as the article. For someone who has his article posted on a humor site, you seem to be pretty serious. I thought the article was half a joke. I guess I was wrong. So let's talk about your issues.

CK: Some say that this program unfairly nerfs rangers. What is your response?

Mr. X: Have rangers and druids been nerfed? I don't think so. Showeq has not taken their abilities away from them. A nerf implies that their abilites have become less effective. They still have their abilites and they are still helpful in a group. Showeq users can't join a group as a warrior and start leading their party around without giving away he is using showeq.

CK: Some say that this program gives unfair advantage to people who are able to meet the hardware and software requirements and know-how to run ShowEQ. What is your response?

Mr. X: Showeq absolutley gives us an advantage over people who don't have it. What is it that Verant keeps preaching? Norrath is a dangerous and unforgiving world where things aren't always fair? Besides, have you ever tried to get customer service from Verant? It's non-existant. I lost about 600pp in items due to a game crash during a trade. I petitioned and sent e-mail for 2 weeks and nothing came of it. The only responses I did recieve told me to prove that I lost the items during the crash. So showeq, helps me recover some of my losses by killing MOB's with good loot.

CK: Why are you doing it? Ego? Activism? Both? None of the above?

Mr. X: I think this is explained in [the answer above].

CK: Do you think this program is legal? Why? (I am not talking about the User Agreement .. I mean, legal legal)

Mr. X: Do I think this program violates the user agreement? Yes. Do I think it is legal asside from the user agreement? Yes. These packets are traveling across my network. I have a handful of machines on a small LAN and if I want to look at the data traveling across that network, then that's my choice.

CK: Some say this program keeps Verant from taking care of more important issues. What is your response?

Mr. X: I don't see how this question is really relevant. Who can really answer for Verant on this? If this is taking Verant's time away from other issues, then Verant needs to address this internaly. They are the ones that need to make custome service decisions. Perhaps they need to ask questions about why poeple are using showeq( server overcrowding, camping sucks, lack of customer service, etc... ). Besides, Verant has made statements that they have devoted next to no time in during their encoding of packets..

CK: If you think this product is fine and justified, why are you so defensive about it?

Mr. X: I don't know how you arrived at the conclusion that everyone is so defensive about it. I don't think I have really seen much of anything on these boards about the ethics, morality, or legality of showeq. I think your questions and my answers have been the largest discussion I have seen on the issue. Perhaps I am looking in the wrong place. If so, please point me to text that contains the defensiveness you describe.

 

Interview with Mr. Y

CK: Some say that this program unfairly nerfs rangers. What is your response?

Mr. Y: How? I don't see this affecting rangers at all, but extending similar (but not identical) abilities to other classes. In addition, it's also a bit more difficult to pay attention to two monitors at one time, whereas a ranger just has to look at one. Perhaps that balances things a bit. Either way, the rangers/druids tracking ability remains unaffected.

CK: Some say that this program gives unfair advantage to people who are able to meet the hardware and software requirements and know-how to run ShowEQ. What is your response?

Mr. Y: Of course it does. Higher IQ gives some people unfair advantages in gaining employment as engineers instead of janitors, and the point being what? I'm sorry, I don't play this game to be exactly like everyone else. This game is about the aquisition of loot, fame and fortune. Socializing too. ShowEQ gives people an edge, why should they deny this?

CK: Why are you doing it? Ego? Activism? Both? None of the above?

Mr. Y: None of the above. I almost quit EQ several times before ShowEQ came out, but I have a renewed interest now, gives me somethin to look forward to and something I can contribute to. I can't do that with Verant directly.

CK: Do you think this program is legal? Why? (I am not talking about the User Agreement .. I mean, legal legal)

Mr. Y: In no way is this program illegal. I don't even know where people get this goofy idea? It is against the TOS of EQ, but it does not violate any US or international law. Where did you get this idea? What aspect of the program do you think could even conceivably violate a US or international law?

CK: Some say this program keeps Verant from taking care of more important issues. What is your response?

Mr. Y: Laff ... was Verant ever taking care of more important issues before ShowEQ? The answer is an emphatic NO! There have been a miriad of pathetic, known problems that Verant STILL hasn't dealt with, yet they add useless and trivial things like baking and pottery, etc... They constantly focus on things no one cares about, the small, easy things, and leave the hard decisions and problems to rot. ShowEQ does not take time away from anything important, they simply choose to ignore important issues until it's convenient. As an aside, Smed stated that they only spend 5 or 10 min here and there working on the encryption properties of the datastream to disable ShowEQ.

CK: If you think this product is fine and justified, why are you so defensive about it?

Mr. Y: I think we need a defintion of "Fine and justified" that we both can agree on. In all honesty, I rarely even use ShowEQ for spawns. I am extremely well to do EQ wise, all of it obtained long before ShowEQ. I don't really have a need to camp wandering spawns for any reason. I primarily use ShowEQ for the numbers. I love to know what my exact stats are, what the mobs HP is, what experience I am receving or have lost due to a death. I absolutely love it. If the mob SPAWN data was never sent, but I could still see the monster HP and the rest of my personal stats, that would be enough for me.
I like to see exactly how close Nagafen, Vox, Maestro, Innoruuk and CT get to death when my entire party and everyone else is dead.I like seeing that the Maestro starts lifetapping like mad at 4200hp, but not before. I like the numbers... that's why I use ShowEQ. If Verant would put a numbers switch in the game, that would go a long to eliminating a lot of SEQ users.
The only other reason I use ShowEQ is the for the map. Yes, the map. I like seeing exactly where I am, and the fastest way to get to the next zone. Even a year after playing, I have found that I have been taking round about ways through some zones.
It's all in the numbers. Verant spouts on about "Oh we want to keep the mystery of the game there" It's a #$@%%@#%ing mystery to me why monsters should be able to attack through walls, why they know EXACTLY where you are in the zone, no matter where you are, why they know EXACTLY where you are even if you are invisible, why they can cast and move at the same time, why they don't follow the same LOS rules on spells, why they hit 10 times harder at the same level, why they teleport on top of me if they happen to get stuck, why we can't use terrain to our advantage like anyones common sense would dictate, etc... THAT is the mystery of EQ, keeping the numbers from the players is not enhancing mystery, just fostering frustration... guess what, I'm a lot less frustrated now!

the end.
