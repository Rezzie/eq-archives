Monk Headbands

The monk headbands are status symbols for the Silent Fist monks of Qeynos. All of the quests come from the monk guildhouse in Qeynos. To do these quests, you need good faction with the Silent Fist (turning in your guild note at level 1 should do it). The headbands themselves don't have the most impressive stats, and most monks will abandon them after they get access to the Executioner's Hood and the Crown of the Froglok King. However, they are necessary for the Robe of the Lost Circle quest.

  • White Headband - Requires four gnoll scalps. You cna get these from gnoll pups in the newbie zone, Qeynos Hills, or Blackburrow.
  • Yellow Headband - Requires three putrid bones and the white headband. The putrid rib bones come off putrid skeletons, which spawn at the undead ruins near Blackburrow.
  • Orange Headband - Requires two gnoll pelts from gnolls in Blackburrow, and a gnoll skin off a elite guard or commander. Also requires the Yellow Headband.
  • Red Headband - Requires the head of Shen, the skull of Dareb, and the head of Drah. All of these can be found on named splitpaw gnolls in the Karanas. The only spawn I know of is Ghanex Drah, who spawns in the undead ruins in South Karana, near the bridge to North Karana. Also requires the Orange Headband.
  • Purple Headband - Requires the Dagger of Marnek off the Necro Thaumaturgist in the bottom floor of befallen. Also, the Skull of Jhen'tra off gnoll embalmers in Lake Rathe. The hardest piece to get, however, is Zaharn's Coronet, which comes from the Permafrost king room (spawn's name is a 31 cleric named High Priest Zaharn). Finally, requires the Red Headband.

Monk Sashes

The monk sashes are status symbols for the Ashen Order monks of Freeport. All of the quests come from the monk guildhouse in Freeport. To do these quests, you need good faction with the Ashen Order(turning in your guild note at level 1 should do it). The sashes themselves don't have the most impressive stats, and most monks will abandon them after they get access to the Thick Banded Belt or Flowing Black Silk Sash. However, they are necessary for the Robe of the Lost Circle quest.

  • White Sash - Requires 1 bone chip, 2 orc scalps (from Deathfist Orc Pawns), and 1 snake fang.
  • Yellow Sash - Requires the white sash, a giant snake rattle (off asps and darkweed snakes), a Chitin (off desert tarantulas in North Ro), and a deathfist sashed belt (off Orcs in East Commons). The deathfist sashed belt is acually called a deathfist slashed belt on the item name.
  • Orange Sash - Requires a legionairre bracer from Crushbone, a Greater Lightstone (from a wisp), and a Cutthroat Insignia Ring (off dervishes in North Ro). And of course, the yellow sash.
  • Red Sash - Requires the wand of marnek (called a "blackened wand", comes off Priest Amiaz, an alternate spawn for the Thaumaturgist in 3rd floor of befallen), and the sapphire of souls (called a "blackened sapphire", comes off a rare spawn rogue that is in Rathyl's room in Najena). Incidentally, these two items are also required for a Shadowknight quest originating in Freeport, which is to assemble the wand of burning.

Collar of Neshika

This quest was formerly to obtain the Treant Fists, but now yields the Collar of Neshika, a mostly useless collar for monks. At the same level to complete this quest, a monk can easily get a collar of undead protection, which is arguably a better item. This quest takes place in Kithicor forest, and you'll most likely want a ranger to help you out with it.

In Kithicor, after entering from the west commons zone, if you follow the path, the first house on your left will have a few people spawn in it. One of the spawns will cycle between a ranger, a gnome, and a monk. The monk's name is Grim Oakfist, and he's looking for help from a willing monk.

If you talk to him and follow the bracketed keywords, you'll find that he wants you to help him out by hunting three tigers which killed his wife. He can't do this himself because the tigers are protected by the rangers. The tigers have odd names, and run at the speed of SoW. Track down and kill these tigers and each will drop a pelt. It's best if the ranger helping you doesn't assist you with killing the tigers, as otherwise he'll lose faction. Return all three pelts to Grim Oakfist, and he will give you the Collar of Neshika.

I forget the exact tiger names, but one is Eenot. They are around level 20 or less, and are pretty easy to kill once you've found them. The dangerous part of the quest is that Kithicor is now haunted by undead, so it can get dangerous if you do this at the wrong time.

Incidentally, asking Grim about [Treant Fists] will direct you to Freeport to start the Treant Fists quest.

Treant Fists

This quest is originally initated by asking Grim Oakfist about [Treant Fists], but you can bypass this stage completely. Grim tells you to talk to Puab, a guildmaster in the Ashen Order.

If you ask Puab about [Treant Fists], he will direct you to Kerra Isle, where you must talk to a banished kerran monk. This Kerran can be found in the dungeon of Kerra Isle, on the island that houses the Heretics. Puab gives you a silver coin to give to the banished kerran (his name is Clawfist, but his spawn is simply called "a banished Kerran".

When you give the coin to the Kerran, he will tell you he needs your help in collecting the remains of his friend who died in the Runnyeye area. He gives you a container in which to combine all four parts of the friend and sends you on your way.

In the runnyeye area, you must collect four items:

  • Humerus Handled Mace - From the Gorge of King Xorbb (beholder's maze). It spawns on a rare spawn Goblin Alchemist, who spawns near the zone to Runnyeye, and wanders.
  • Ribcage Chest Armor - From Misty Thicket, a rare spawn Goblin Alchemist, who spawns in the place of Mooto, and wanders between the three goblin camps.
  • Catskull Cap - From the Gelatinous Cube in Runnyeye, who spawns in the underwater passage near the bank and the goblin king.
  • Some Bone Weapon - Can't remember what this item was called, but it's a drop off a common spawn called a "Goblin Captain" who spawns in the room behind the goblin king.

After combining these four items in the box and returning it to the banished Kerran, he will give you a note to take back to Puab. Give the note to Puab, and he will give you the Treant Fists. The Treant Fists are one of the best monk gloves in the game, arguably up to the Basoon Haste Gauntlets or Gauntlets of Fiery Might.

Robe of the Lost Circle

This is the most in depth monk quest known in the game, and probably one of the most frustrating. The reward is the Robe of the Lost Circle, a good monk chest piece. In the process of doing this quest, you also become an honorary member of the Monks of the Whistling Fists, a lost brotherhood of monks. This makes for some good roleplaying oppurtunities if you are into that sort of thing.

The robe also has a unique maroon look, which will earn you lots of inspects, at least for a few more months till the robes are more common.

The quest is started by talking to either Brother Qwinn in South Karana or Brother Zephyl in Rathe Mountains. Both of these monks are rare wandering monk trainers. They belonged to a lost order called the "Monks of the Whistling Fists". In order to become an honorary member of the Whistling Fists, you must perform tasks for these two monks.

Brother Qwinn requires the "Code of Zan Fi", a book dropped by Targin the Rock, a rarish spawn in the king room in Solusek B. He also asks you to turn in your purple headband to prove that you are an adept member of the Silent Fists. Turning in these two items will give you the Needle of the Void.

Brother Zephyl asks you to return "the Idol", an idol dropped by Raster of Guk. He can be found in, of course, Guk. Specifically, Lower Guk, in the Minotaur Elder room. He spawns at the top of the stairs in that room. He is an extremely rare spawn. His placeholder is a greater minotaur. If you turn in the Idol and your red sash, he will give you a rare robe pattern. Finding raster is by far the hardest part of this quest.

Combining the robe pattern, the needle of the void, Jonathon's Whistling Warsong, and a swatch of shadow silk together in a tailoring kit will create the Robe of the Lost Circle. I made the robe with a tailoring skill of 27, and it was trivial for me. Another monk reported creating the robe with a skill of 21 and failing, destroying all the components in the process.

Jonathon's Whistling Warsong can be purchased for a small amount from any bard guild, and shadow silk can be made by any decent tailor (Shadow Wolf Pelt, Silk Swatch, Scroll of Gather Shadows (low-lvl Necro invis spell scroll)). A silk swatch can be made by combining two spider silks in a tailoring kit.
