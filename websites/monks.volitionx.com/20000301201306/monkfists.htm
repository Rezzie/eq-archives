Fist Damage Delay Statistics

Our fists are operate as a standard weapon, based on Hand to Hand skill. This weapon is considered one handed (of course), and it's delay is always 36. (Note: Yeti of Karana has recently done tests which seem to indicate that our delay is now 35.5. If this is true, it will skew these numbers a bit more in favor of our fists, but only slightly). The damage on our fists is ((Level/5) + 4) rounded down. So here is the statistics on our "fist weapon" from level 1 to 50. Note the ratios posted are Delay/Damage, of which lower is better.

  • Level 01 Fists - Damage 04 Delay 36 (Ratio:8.00)
  • Level 05 Fists - Damage 05 Delay 36 (Ratio:7.20)
  • Level 10 Fists - Damage 06 Delay 36 (Ratio:6.00) (Close to Tentacle Whip)
  • Level 15 Fists - Damage 07 Delay 36 (Ratio:5.14) (Close to Crysknife)
  • Level 20 Fists - Damage 08 Delay 36 (Ratio:4.50) (Close to Mino Axe)
  • Level 25 Fists - Damage 09 Delay 36 (Ratio:4.00) (Close to Barbed Leather Whip)
  • Level 30 Fists - Damage 10 Delay 36 (Ratio:3.60) (Close to Silvery War Axe)
  • Level 35 Fists - Damage 11 Delay 36 (Ratio:3.27) (Close to Painbringer)
  • Level 40 Fists - Damage 12 Delay 36 (Ratio:3.00) (Close to Ykesha)
  • Level 45 Fists - Damage 13 Delay 36 (Ratio:2.77) (Close to Amygdalan Tendril)
  • Level 50 Fists - Damage 14 Delay 36 (Ratio:2.57) (Close to Scimitar of Mistwalker)

One Handed Weapon Comparison

So, at level 50, our fists are better damage/delay (This doesn't include procs obviously) than any dual wieldable double attacking 1 handed weapon except:

  • Fanged Skull Stiletto (Warrior/Ranger/Rogue, 1.83, GM Quest Only? PoF?)
  • Spined Dragon Claw (Warrior/Ranger, 2.10, Dracoliche?)
  • Blood Fire (Warrior, 2.18, found on Cazic Thule?)
  • Bone Razor (Rogue, 2.20, found on Cazic Thule?)
  • Crystalline Spear (Warrior/Ranger/Rogue, 2.31, Vox?)
  • Eyerazzia (Rogue, 2.40, Fear, Common drop?)
  • Scimitar of the Mistwalker (Ranger, 2.5, Vox)

Weapons like Darkmetal Holy Water Sprinkler, Ghoulbane, Six Note Blade, Evensong, and the Tunarian Scimitar don't really count since they can't be used by classes that can dual wield and double attack, so they might as well be 2 handed weapons as far as damage goes.

Of course, including procs, many other weapons could probably be comparable to our fists, but it's doubtful (The Ykesha and the Electrum Plated Koshinagta are the only ones that are close, and the Koshingata is very specialized, and the Ykesha is only 70point dd).

Still, a warrior, ranger, or rogue with lots of dragon/plane equipment can have better weapons than our fist (especially something like Blood Fire, which procs a 600point dot). The only compensation that we get is that our skill caps are higher, especially our dual wield, so chances are, at level 50, we can probably still manage to outdamage even an Eyerazzia/Bone Razor combo. With Flying Kick to balance, we might even do more damage than a Blood Fire/Spined Dragon Claw combo. But there's just no way we can outdamage someone who has two Fanged Skull Stilettos (which are the most unbalanced weapon I've seen in the game yet). However, I doubt that many people will ever acquire those.

Conclusions

So overall, yes, we are the masters of damage, but just barely, and only at the high levels. Looking at weapon equivalencies at early levels, you'll see our fists are well under the standard equipment for those levels nowadays. Even at the high levels, I don't know if it makes up for our lack of hps, stun, taunt, and 14 weight limit.

As a side note, interestingly enough, if you wanted to twink a monk, two Sharkbone Warhammers (3.25) at weight 3.0, would help a monk up to level 40. One of the few weapons worth using for a monk at the high levels. However, if you do decide to do this, I suggest you maintain your Hand to Hand skill as well.

Two Black Enamalled Maces (3.5)also work well up to level 35, but their 4.5 weight might be hard to stomach, and Cold Iron Morningstars (3.89) work till level 30. Before level 20, a Scepter of Flame (4.14) will help out, as well a Scepter of Rahotep (4.22). Everything else is generally too heavy to be wielded by a monk.
