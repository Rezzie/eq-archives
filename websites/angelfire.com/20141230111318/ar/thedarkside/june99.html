June 30, 1999

Verant paid some attention to the necromancer class in the latest upgrade patch to EQ.  Here are the changes that affect us:

- Due to level limit on the Charm and Beguile spells, they have been taken out of the game until the next update patch.  The undead charming spells will not be affected by these changes.
- Vampiric Curse / Bond of Death (formerly Circle of Death) now work with their new effects.
- The Dark Pact series has had the token AC component removed to make it stack better with other spells.
- Poison spells have had their damage doubled and their duration cut in half.  They will do exactly the same amount of damage, but in half the pervious amount of time
- Disease spells have had their duration reduced.  Their damage has been upped to compensate.
- Hungry Earth is now researchable

Also, Verant made a few changes to Nagafen's Lair and Permafrost, revamping the areas with new items, treasures, and more.  In addition, changes have been made to enhance NPCs spawning within them.
Besides finally fixing the problem with Hungry Earth, it is interesting to know that Verant is improving one of the main weapons at the necromancer's arsenal, the DoT (Damage over Time) spells.  By making it faster, DoT spells become more effective, specially when hunting in a party.

I also added a Research Inventory Sheet to the Research Guide section.  It is a Word document that is useful for keeping track of how many of each Word you have.  I did it on Word since that way you can change it to fit your taste.

  Quick Tip: Do not forget to check on your specialization progress.  Know which skill you want to specialize in and make sure the others are not evolving faster.  If so, stop and cast a spell that uses your favorite skill on yourself.

June 25, 1999

Hello fellow necros.
I added a few minor changes to the spell list and the necromancer guide.
I want to try to keep loading times to a minimum possible.
Besides that and among other things, I am currently working on the zone guides to the Karana Plains. If anyone would like to send me ideas, please do so here.
Finally, I just would like to express my own personal opinion on the roleplaying issue.  As a roleplaying game, I guess it expected from EverQuest players to act as his/her character would.  The only problem I have with that is the following, you are a necromancer.  To roleplay a necromancer, you would have to pretty much hate everyone in the game, except other necromancers. You would not be able to party with anyone that wasn't evil in nature.  Even though necromancers excel at hunting solo, that is not always possible.  The most interesting places and dungeons in Norrath require that you be in a party and I intend to visit all of them.  Thus, I do not feel we can fully roleplay our characters.  Well, just something I wanted to say.

  Quick Tip: If hunting in a party, try to learn a new language whenever possible.  Have a hotkey button ready for that.  Just add five lines of text to a hotkey and you are ready to exchange languages.  It can be useful in the future.

June 20, 1999

Greetings fellow necromancers and welcome to The Dark Side.
I have finished the first phase of this site and it is now open for your enjoyment.  I decided to do start this site because I believe that we, necromancers, still lack a site with useful information to help us thrive in Norrath.  Necromancer is a tough class to play but it can be very rewarding if you do not give up.  Overall, I believe you will find some interesting things here and I sincerely hope you enjoy it.  Make sure you send me comments on things you would like to see included here or things that could be done in a different way.  I still have quite a lot of things to work on but be sure I will get everything the way it should be in no time.  I would also appreciate contributions to the Zone Guide section as I have not yet visited in much detail all regions in Norrath.
Without any further delays, enjoy and come back soon.

  Quick Tip: A good and safe way to raise your evocation skill is to cast a spell such as Ward Undead over and over again on your own pet. He will not suffer any damage and you will improve the same way as if you were casting on any other undead creature.
