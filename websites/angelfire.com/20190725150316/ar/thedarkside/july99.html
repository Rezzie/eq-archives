July 29, 1999


Well, I am finally back. Plenty of things to talk about.  First, the new patch is up and running.  Here are the changes that affected us necromancers.

 

- The Plane of Fear is now online (looks very nice)
- Pottery has been modified (no more easy money)
- You can report a player who is harassing other players by typing /report. This will save the last 10 lines of text
- /time now reports the Earth time as well as EQ time
- If a character is resurrected, a spell will be placed on that player for a short duration once the resurrection is complete
- /surname allows players level 20+ to issue his/her last name (finally)
- If a character is killed by someone with his or her PK flag ON (+pvp), they will return from death with Zero Mana.  This is to address the 'naked mage syndrome'
- PvP Faction Hits have been removed until we can implement an aggressor flag (IF Verant decides to)
- If a character is knocked unconscious and has no stamina, the player will still regain consciousness
- Pets can no longer open locked doors
- Players are no longer able to cast Shadowstep next to a zone point and teleport to an area past the teleport region, which is inaccessible to other characters
- If a player is too far from a corpse while attempting to perform the /loot command, a message will now be given
- Players may now target Drakes much easier
- Several  "Safe Zones" have been Fixed
- A number of quests have been both enhanced and added
- If a character is blinded, the chat box will no longer flash
- If a shuriken or throwing knife is equipped, then unequipped, the weapon will no longer remain on the character model
- Necromancer research components that did not previously stack should now stack (almost a great fix...)
- The Bug that allowed Screaming Terror to work Outdoors has been fixed (BUG?)
- Vampiric Curse damage has been upped
- Hitting F1 twice, or /pet target will now target your pet (it actually does)
- A Quest to help casters with research has been added.  Good aligned races should check North Karana, while those with a more malign disposition should check South Ro

 

There it is. I do not feel that necromancers gained much with this new patch.  They were either minor changes or fixes to earlier patches.  There are some good things though.  The new quest to help casters with research might prove useful.  To take it, go to South Ro (loc +100 +500) and talk to a vendor inside a tent named Smith Tu'ysa. You should give her lightstones and greater lightstones in order to get the 3 different components for the quest.  A lightstone will give you either one of the research books that contains recipes required for the quest.  A Greater Lightstone will give you a Concordance of Research, a container piece used in the quest.  To complete the quest, you will have to create runes with items such as weapons and gems.  Once I get more info on this I will post an update.

 

The other great thing is the Plane of Fear.  I believe high level players were missing an area entirely devoted to them.  Well, here it is.  Click here to see some screenshots and my impressions on this new zone.

 

But there were also some terrible things.  Gather Shadows still doesn't blink before fading.  Screaming Terror has been officially renamed Summon Train (I don't know a situation when terror will be useful inside a dungeon).  And the worst thing is the Words problem.  They made our Words stackable now, which is great.  But the problem is the new patch simply disappeared with Words people already had.  I am included in that category and lost over 20 Words I needed.  I hope they can fix this, but so far a GM told me to write to jellis.verant.com and report my losses.

 

Now for a few updates.  I added a few more items, including the Manastone and the Sacrificial Dagger, two very useful items.  There is a lot of talk about the potentially exploit bug with Manastone, but I am not sure how to make it work.  Mystic Doll has been updated (thanks Mortado).  I also added a few ways to increase faction in Freeport and Ak'Anon (thanks Shaithis and Xakthan).  Finally, I made a few minor changes to the Spell List.

Sorry for the long update.

So, when exactly does your pet get experience?  Well, pretty much always.  I asked this question to about 5 different GMs already and the answer has always been the same.  Your pet shares experience with you anytime it does any amount of damage to a creature of makes the kill.  Thus, anytime your pet is involved in the battle at any point he will get experience.  Telling him to back off will not help...

One of the most boring but easiest ways to learn a language is to find a friend who knows at least the basics of the language you want to learn.  You may also spend one practice point to learn the basics of it.  Then, form a group with a friend and teach him that language or have him teach you.  The one who is learning will learn the basics and a little more (one more point).  You should now both zone and whoever learned teaches the other.  He will increase his skill by two points.  Zone and keep repeating the above until you are both excellent in that language.

Once you reach level 16, you will be able to start researching a few spells.  Research is necessary for some mid and high level spells.  To research a spell, you need the Book of Dark Bindings (found at most necromancer guilds, with the exception of Erudin's - thank you Saboth) and the Words necessary for that spell (found on creatures).  For example, the research only level 20 spell Harmshield requires Words of Absorption and Words of Refuge.  Put those two words on your Book of Dark Bindings and hit combine.  Hopefully you won't fail...  I would strongly recommend spending as many points as you can on research to reduce chances of failure.  Spell recipes can be found here or in the Diablerie books (also found at necromancer guilds).  To read the Diablerie books you must know the language it is written on, such as Old Erudin for Diablerie 1.
