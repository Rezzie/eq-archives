Nagafen Raid Update
The groups are full. I have added a fifth group to accomodate guildmembers whose enrollments I had misplaced. I have removed the cleric from the Pets Group to replace with a necromancer. The Pets Group should have two Shamans, which will hopefully suffice for healing. See the new Groups List.

November 19th, 1999

Nagafen Raid Update
The groups are full. If you still wish to participate, I can add you to the standby group to fill in for no-shows. We have 5 standby slots open (see the groups list).

We still need more clerics, however. As we get more clerics, they will replace the druids in Groups 1-4. Those druids will become part of the Corpse Draggin' Group (see the groups list). The Corpse Draggin' group will be a non-combat group. 36 people will be the limit on combatants versus Nagafen. Any more would risk a zone crash, which we cannot have as people will be bound there. Please send an email if you want to participate.

I wanted to post an explanation for the level limits and size limits on the Nagafen raid. However, Fitz beat me to the punch in his post on the BBS. Here is an excerpt from his post...

"The level 49+ is cutting out a lot of KEGgers as well, and though I can't say that I am fond of the idea of not being able to help on the raid, I do completely understand the rationale for it. 2 most dangerous X factors when taking on dragons are LAG and Dragon Roar. It is a known fact that less people = less lag thus the reasoning for ONLY 6 groups. Secondly, level 50s have the highest possible chance of resisting Dragon Roar...if you dont know how devastating dragon roar is, I can tell you that in game. Needless to say, no chance of resisting dragon roar means no chance of helping the group. Snaggle and I have talked about the tactics and I have to admit they are very sound."
