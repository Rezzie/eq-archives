glide == dead, and here is why jackalo.

there are 2 prominent API's for drawing 3d graphics.

1) Direct3d (microsoft)
2) OpenGL (SGI)

When a card manufacturer boasts that they support OpenGL for example, that means that the card, in hardware, supports the API (and hence is accelerated since it can do the work on its own).

Now take this, A full OpenGL 1.1 or 1.2 ICD. Now strip it down so it cant do everything OpenGL(tm) can, so that your engineers can be lazy and not have to implement everything. Now, take this half assed implementation of opengl, and instead of calling it "HalfGL", call it "Glide" and market it to the consumers like its a good thing!

Glide was decent in its time, because no one supported a full opengl icd. Glides partial implementation was better than none, but once cards started showing up fully OpenGL compliant, whats the reason to buy glide? Luckily for 3dfx their product loyalty lasted quite a while, and you guys kept buying the cards. But unlucky for you, the person sill holding onto a 3dfx card, you didnt see the light as soon as your bretheren did, and waited too long to jump ship.

And, if you are going to compare glide to anything you would be better off not comparing to a game with an engine as horrid as EQ. I would say tribes2, but i forgot, you need a full OpenGL implementation, glide (aka halfGL) wont cut it anymore. damn dynamix for not supporting glide, you should petition them too.
