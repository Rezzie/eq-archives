Hehe, thanks for posting Bixie's Corpse. They're actually called EverWalk and EverType. EverWalk will simply take a loc input (you type something like /em wants to go to location 100 -100) and it will do some nice math to make you walk to that location. Pathing is interesting, you ill most likely NOT walk in a straight line. It's tough to explain why. EverType is very complex and you could set it to recast spells on fizzle or have you start medding and say oom if you're OOM. It will actually be "smart" enough to battle for you if you have a single (or maybe 2) mob spawn that you can handle. Here's my advisory though: do NOT use this while you aren't at your computer. Just grab a book and sit by your computer to make sure you don't die (or get yelled at by a GM). In theory you could also set up EverType to say something like "I'm sorry but my mom says I have to go, I'll be able to talk when I log back on." then it types / Q <enter> if, for instance, a guide says "Hail sir, Senior Guide Stoopid here, we've received reports that you're macroing, is anyone there?". It would trigger on the word guide or macro for instance. EverType will have 20 customizable text triggers with 10 commands available underneath as well as 5 timers that can each be triggered by text or that continuously loop. EverWalk will have 10 locs that can be entered (actually 11) and they can each be called to be the destination loc with simple commands that way EverType can call them easily. It will also deactivate if it zones either accidently or if you enter a stupid loc. EverWalk takes complete control of your text-enterbar-thingy in EQ so you'll actually have to make a hotkey to say the command to stop walking, that's because it repeatedly types / L <enter> and presses the arrow keys.

Expect this software to be finished by Sunday. The website is
