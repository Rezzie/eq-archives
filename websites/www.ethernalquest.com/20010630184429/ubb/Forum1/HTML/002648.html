Before I started using EQ Macros (now xylobot), I used a Logitech Wingman and customized the trigger to follow the key sequences I wanted. With a rubber band wrapped around the trigger, it would repeat indefinately. It worked fairly well, but could only do keystokes.

Because of the addition of recording and playback of mouse movements and well as keystrokes, I recommend xylobot highly.

Some of the ways I use this program:

1) Press "Bind Wounds" key, wait till the button pops out, then press "Sense Heading". You need to wait till bind is done before pressing the key again, or it will begin the new bind and never end the previous one. Since the program removes any pauses at the end of the program, you need to finish with another keypress (you could just end with a "F1", or something else that has no effect). When you're playing an Ogre warrior with a lot of +HP eq, binding back up can take quite a while .

2) Keep pressing "Kick" or "Slam"/"Bash" in combat. Some combats can last quite a while...

3) Learn trade skills. If you use one backpack for each component you're using, you can execute 160 (or even 200) trade skill attempts just by starting a macro. A few caveats: A) You need to plan for what your macro does with the results of the attempt, and make sure that the macro works on success or failure. B) I've found that I need to be very deliberate and careful when recording mouse clicks. Press very firmly, and hold the button down for much longer than normal if you want your macro to stand up to multiple iterations.
