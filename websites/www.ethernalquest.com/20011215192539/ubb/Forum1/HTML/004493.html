I normally don't stick my foot into any of the "This is better than That" or "You Sux0r, I'm l337" discussions, but I'm gonna do it here....
What is with the Windows vs. Linux stuff?
Both OS's have their place.
I'll agree that I'd like to see Microsoft lose some serious market share, but that is because I dislike big business, and Microsoft in general, not because I favor some other OS.
Windows is good for the people it caters to: New computer owners who don't know jack shit about computers.
Linux is good for people who know the ins and outs of computing.
Before Linux can "take over" there are still a few simple facts that will be hard to change:
1. Because of the nature of open source software, installing the simplest application under linux is a chore that most AVERAGE computer users will not be willing to do, while Windows makes it a fairly simple and brainless task.
2. Linux is free. This means they will never have the kind of financial backing that feeds the Microsoft marketing engine, and thus will never reach the sheer exposure audience that Microsoft reaches. Also because of the exposure that Microsoft has, more developers will develop Windows applications and games because it will give them more market exposure and more money. More "standard" applications will develop under Windows (Like Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator being the standard graphics apps for publishing houses, and Office being the standard office suite for businesses).
My point here is that each OS is good for the people it targets, nothing more, nothing less.
Those of you out there that think Linux is the absolute best OS out there should imagine your mother trying to use it. Would she know what to do?
