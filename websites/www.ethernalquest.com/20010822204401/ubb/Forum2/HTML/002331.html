Hey guys (and gals). Just wanted to drop you a line and give you an update. We've got Zordon working with us on the EMU now. We have a few different (and working) login and world servers and we're still hashing out design details. Ashran, Zordon and I have all come up with really good design ideas and we have been in the process of hashing them out and throwing out some while refining others. I think what we'll end up with will be the best of 2 very fine minds and the mind of one savage beast. I'm not sure which one of us is the savage beast, but I'm sure the outcome will be good.

A few things that I think have been getting asked.

The new EMU will be released in a multi-phasic approach. The 1st phase will have minimal functionality while having a really cool rock-solid design. Most probably, no source code will be released with the 1st phase. Sucessive releases will begin to fill in the areas of functionality missing and we will begin releasing source (probably under GPL) for the modules that we feel is appropriate.

Some things you will not see in the EMU: the source code to the registration and login servers, this will be closed. All data that is sent encrypted, will appear in the database encrypted (and probably encrypted differently). The reason for this is that we will not be allowing people to exploit EthernalQuest (and our reverse engineered code) to decode intercepted login packets from live eq session and gain access to accounts on Verants computers. Not only would this cause unneeded tensions and anger on Verant's behalf, but we would have to deal with Verant changing their encryption. Now some other person may create (and may already have) decryption that they use for this purpose, but if it happens, it wont come from us.

But the login server or even world server isn't what people will need the source to. The zone server will be where you will modify code to change the game. That and the data that feeds it.

I had been wondering that myself Ghallant. Here is what I could come up with after speaking with a few family members that are in the legal field. Keep in mind Internet and computer law are a bit up in the air right now.

Hackersquest is creating a product from scratch that is simply a MMORPG server. (they didnt steal the original source code, They generated their own code via reverse engineering.) Reverse engineering is common practice in many industries.

Said server does not require any software from Verant to run. The server ALLOWS the Verant client to attacht to it. But does not REQUIRE the Verant client in order to run.

A non Verant client product could be made that would attach to the server. I could if I had the time and know how create my own client. I won't, but I could.

Because of the above Hackersquest simply needs not call it an Everquest emulator, but give it a name such as Hackersquest MMORPG Server, or HackerMUD, etc. This makes their product a world/mmorpg/MUD server of their own creation/design. Yes it has many features that EQ's servers do, but you can't sue because somebody created a similar product. Yes it allows an individual to attach an EQ client to it. But that is between the individual and Verant. Not between HQ and Verant.

The only clout Verant has is money. They have the money so some hosting companies, etc will allow them to shutdown websites hosting EMU creators, etc. If your hosting company does not care about threats from a deep-pocket and won't be paid off to shut you down. Then there is nothing they can do to stop you from creating a server product of this nature.

Look closely at all of the EMU's for UO. UO realized they could not stop the servers. UO still has a policy on their website that states logging into a EMU will get you banned. But UO has no policy for running a server or creating a server that other people may or may not attach the UO client to. i.e. You can run/create a UO EMU server, you just can't login to it with your UO client.

I doubt UO or EQ will want to attempt to sue each and every person that logins into an EMU with their client(s). That's a ton of money to spend on lawyers to sue individuals that even if you win the case won't have the money to pay the court decision let alone your legal bills. The worst they can do is ban your account. But if you have an EMU that you are playing on, why do you even need your account?

Hope this perspective brings forth good ideas and comments about EMU server creation.
I am interested in hearing other peoples thoughts and research on this same topic.
