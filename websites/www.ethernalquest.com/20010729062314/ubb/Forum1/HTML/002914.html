Nos, the problem with integrated crap is you never know if it can *really* be disabled until you have already bought it.

I agree that with one machine, it's cheaper to go integrated, but in the long run, it's cheaper the other way around because you can upgrade and shuffle components around to other machines with less headaches.

Since everyone is giving hard drive opinions, I like WD and Quantum, dislike Seagate and hated Conner(remember Conner?)

Lot's of people don't like Quantum, but I had one go through hell and come out OK years ago, so I like them. I took my PC in to get a new mainboard (in the old days, before I would consider doing something like that myself). When hooking everything back up in the case, the tech got the power plug for the harddrive in upside down! Yeah, I know you're asking "How the hell did he manage that?!" Well I have no clue how he did, it, but when he powered up the system it went *poof* and clouds of smoke began rolling out of it. Removing the hard drive you could see that the entire circuit board on the underside had *melted*. Since I didn't have a backup of my data (see, it IS a good idea to back up) all seemed lost. I suggested he remove the circuit board from the drive and put on one from an identical drive he had on the shelf. He did it, and the drive worked with no loss of data! That drive still runs to this day in an old PC I have sitting around!
