Jafo
Zombie 		posted 04-13-2001 07:29 PM         
Ok, here's is what slows the game down when you have those damned trees onscreen:

1) Every tree requires several transforms in order to wave. This requires D3D to set up in the neighborhood of 5-10 (guessing) 4x4 matrices per tree, all but one of which have to be multiplied by the root matrix. That's a lot of math per tree, especially if you're running on a low-end machine. If you're running a GeForce, those matrices also need to be pushed across the AGP bus, at 64 bytes a pop, not including command/header info. You also need to flush the vertex pipeline every time you have a new transform, which is a serious hit.

2) EQ uses semi-transparency, not cookie-cut (alpha-test), for those tree limbs. This means each of those relatively-huge polygons that swish around must be drawn in read-modify-write mode, which on most video cards is dog slow. Also, most developers read but do not re-write the z-buffer value when using an alpha gradient like this, so you get no benefit from z-occlusion of things drawn in the back of the "forest".

In short, anyone who says the big, wavy trees have no effect on performance is talking out of their uneducated, ignorant ass.

Jafo

Jafo
Zombie 		posted 04-17-2001 08:32 PM         
1) Vertex animation is slower than bone/transform animation. That doesn't mean they're not doing it, but all other animation in the game is by bone or texture animation. Even characters use bone animation, not vertex. Vertex animation is hideously wasteful of space and it's unlikely they used it. In fact, I know they used it because if you rapidly click through characters in the select screen you can see them blink for one frame into the classic default pose (arms straight out, legs out at 45 degrees) typically used in bone animation.

2) I checked some trees in the game before making my comment about alpha gradients. They most certainly are there. It could be a difference in video cards that you're not seeing the gradient. Get up close to a wavy branch polygon (for instance, one of the big tris anchored at the top of a conifer in the dreadlands which angles out toward the bottom with an image of branches on it). Please note that I mean the dark/bare branch polys, not the overlaid greenery polys found on some trees, which may indeed use alpha test.

Jafo
