Although Bards Tale COULD be "BUS SNIFFED", it would be easier to use a debugging cartridge and do a raw edit of memory (one of those cartridges with the POP into monitor buttons).

Or, for a tip, the game encrypts your saved-game data as follows :
For each byte in the sector, the original value is XORed (exclusive ORd) with the byte position (starting at zero). Now, keep in mind that the first two bytes in the block are not data, but are pointers to the next track and sector on the disk. That's normal for the 1541's internal OS; the C-64 and 128's both used intelligent serial drives. These were drives that had their own CPU's in them as well as a ROM operating system and (I think) around 2-4K of space.

Now, since XORing (exclusive ORing) is elegantly complimentary, if you XOR a value with 5, then just XOR it with 5 again to get the original value back again.

Ok, Bards Tale II or 3, can't remember which, had a checksum... SO, here's my suggestion, if you add 30 (decimal) to one byte, subtract it from a subsequent byte (when possible). It's clearly a simple SUM checksum BUT I could never find precisely WHERE the checksum was stored.

Or maybe I found the checksum and forgot where it was in the like 8million years since I hacked that game.

Now, Wizardry, for the Apple was a real trick to get into. They re-arranged the bits in an attempt to both compress and... well, never mind.
