Zombie 		posted 06-05-2001 09:37 PM         
your cable sharing device has a 4 port switch built into it, doesn't it?

well, showeq will can not sniff packets over a switch, due to the inherint design of a switch (only 2 ports are actively transmitting data, the source and the destination).

a hub on the other hand will fix your problem. it is a repeater, anything that comes in on ONE port, is sent out to all others... and guess what... you need that information that is being sent out to all ports on the hub...

so go buy a $30 hub, hook it to your broadband sharing device and plug your computers into that instead.

also! pay attention to one thing... if you linux boxes is ONLY 10mbit, and your windows box is 100mbit, you STILL won't be able to sniff packets if you get a 10/100 hub because it is really 2 hubs connected by a switch...

so, 10mbit traffic is on one hub, and isn't seen by the 100mbit hub UNLESS it is destined for something on the 100mbit hub....

prolly just really confusing i guess.... so make sure your showeq box and your eq box are BOTH 100mbit or BOTH 10mbit (100mbit is my suggestion, faster).

Zombie 		posted 06-07-2001 02:19 AM         
doing ANYTHING on the internet, 100mbit vs. 10mbit will not make ANY difference to 99.9% of users.

the main reason being, your internet connection is probably at MAX 1.5mbits/sec. that is WAAAAAY below 10mbit/sec.

anyway, you won't notice any more lag, and your internet download speeds will not be decreased by using a 10mbit hub.

what it WILL effect is file transfers on your local network. exactly like sodom said. if you happen to run a file server which has ALL your mp3's on it, and you play them from 2 or 3 other computers... 100mbit will help. if you run an ftp site off of a different computer than you play eq on... and you have large files on it *caugh*mp3 albums*caugh*isos*cough* that you need to swap back and forth, 100mbit will help you a lot ;)

it comes down to this... if you are poor as dirt, stay with the 10mbit hub... if you have 30 or 40 bucks burning a hole in your pocket (and for most people, thats a pretty small hole) then go get a 100mbit hub :)

u can get an 8port 10/100 hub for some pretty small change, maybe $50. room to grow eh?

personally, i started out with a 4port, got a 5port, now i have an 8port and a 5port :)
