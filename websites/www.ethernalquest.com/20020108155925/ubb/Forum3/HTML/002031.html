well, you could probably goto google.com and search for "what is a switch" and "what is a hub" and find out pretty easily, but i'll do a quicky for you.

hubs are dumb, switches are smart. a hub sends anything it gets to all ports hooked to it. a switch directs traffic between the two ports needed. you HAVE to have a hub for showeq.

my recommendation for setting up your network: goto freesco.org or .com (.net for forums) and try it out. its a floppy router that will run on VERY minimal hardware. this will let you do what ever you want on your showeq box with out killing your internet connection :)

basically... your "internet" plugs into your router, the router (on a second network card) plugs into the hub... and all your computers plug into that hub. the "internet" would be either via DSL, cable, T1, modem, etc...

And so you don't have to wonder, your ShowEQ machine and your EverQuest machine have to be on a HUB together because if they are on a SWITCH, information sent to the EverQuest machine can't be seen by the ShowEQ machine.

The most common setup for people with cable/DSL is to have a single NAT device - normally switched - serving their network, and an inexpensive hub branching out of their switched network supporting an EverQuest machine and it's ShowEQ "watcher."
