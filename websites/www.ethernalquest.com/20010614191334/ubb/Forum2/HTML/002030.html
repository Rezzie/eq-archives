Here are two documents that would be very helpful: http://www.geocities.com/ultradildo2000/eqprotocol.txt (information about the general EQ packet headers and structure) and http://www.geocities.com/ultradildo2000/everquest.h.txt (everquest.h from the ShowEQ source, contains most of the specific packet structures for client/server communication). Looking at eqprotocol.txt, the structures contained in everquest.h would be in the OpCode/data segment of the general packet structure before the four byte CRC check.

You WILL need a packet sniffer of some sort if you want to write an emulator, be it ShowEQ or some generic one. Writing the emulator would not be hard overall, however there would be one challenge: figuring out how to use the encryption from the server side. I don't believe the source to libEQ.a would be of any help even if you had it because that is only for decrypting and not for encrypting. You would have to find the packet where the client sends its four byte public key to the server, take that key, and encrypt the packets that would normally be encrypted, most notably, the spawn packets. However, you can write almost all of it without knowing how to do the encryption. If you do start seriously working on it, you could probably get help on the encryption from one of the people who helped get ShowEQ working.
