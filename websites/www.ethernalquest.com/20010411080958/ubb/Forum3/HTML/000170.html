Well there are several problems going on with your set up.

First off, when you connect with dialup networking, the dial up adapter is being assigned a public ip address. The rest of your network cards (win98 and linux) are being asigned a private ip. Without some sort of NAT or proxy service going, this is not going to work. Second problem I see is that a win98 machine has no real way of binding the same protocal to 2 NICs at the same time and even if you did, it has no ability to route between them. Probably your best solution would be to get a copy of sygate (www.sygate.net i think) going on the win98 box. This would preform the NAT service you are needing. I question if this will totaly fix it, but it should give a inet feed to your linux box at least.

IN the long run, the best solution is to use a DSL router(I use a cisco 675), these are great as they have built in NAT and DHCP services.

Once you get NAT running, set ShowEQ to snif the dynamic ip that is assigned to your dial up adapter(use winipcfg to get this info).

Good luck and hope this info was helpfull.
