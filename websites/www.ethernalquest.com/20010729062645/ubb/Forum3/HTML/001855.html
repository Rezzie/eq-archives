A hub transmits data to every device on the network, and the devices figure out what packets they belong too.

A switch transmit data to ONLY the device it is suppose to go to, that's why you can't sniff packets through a switch vs through a hub. (also why Switchs make better network performance).

You RedHat box isn't getting any EQ data if it's plugged into a switch. Hard to sniff packets you're not recieveing.

Stop the presses!!!

If the 3Com switch you represent in your network diagram (immediately above) is the 3Com OfficeConnect 10/100 then a hub is not required.

The 3Com OfficeConnect 10/100 is a switch, but only isolates by speed, rather than port. I use this switch successfully.

All you need to do in *this* scenario is make sure you have your SEQ box and EQ Client systems running the same speed NICs (all set to 10mb or all set to 100mb).

When I first brought up my installation, I had my EQ client running 100mb and my SEQ box at 10. TCPDump showed minimal traffic. After moving my EQ box to the 10-side, everything worked. I then installed a 100mb NIC on my SEQ box, switched my EQ Client back to 100mb and everything works fine.

If you are running another 3Com that isolates by port then this will not work for you.

I would suggest using TCPDump to monitor traffic to check your switch or see the documentation.

I hope you have the same success I did.

Best of luck!
