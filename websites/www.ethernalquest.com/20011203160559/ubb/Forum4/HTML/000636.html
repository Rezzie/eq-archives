Zombie 		posted 11-16-2001 03:30 PM         
A few things to point out... As one MMORPG dev has said (somewhere, LTM or SND mabye?), "The Client is in the hands of the enemy."

1. The alogrythm and key exchange methods can be determined with a combination of reverse enginering and traffic monitoring. This is simply a function of time.

2. The decryption key(s), worst case, can be pulled out of the client's memory, if needed. Some experiments with this were done when the encryption was changed last time, though it's degree of 'detectability' is yet to be determined on a wider scale.

The issues at work here are somewhat simple:

1. Risk/Reward (Money spent, vs. abilities disables)for making, possibly drastic, continued changes to the network code, structure or encryption. A significant encryption increase would undoubtedly require hardware upgrades to the servers, or a re-optimization of the number of players on a physical server at least.

2. Acceptable Loss. I presume that the map/GPS feature of SEQ is not on Verant's priority list to disable. Why? It's been working almost non-stop for such a long time. What information loss (to us) is acceptable to Verant to pass the Risk/Reward check above?

Anyways, it will be a tug-of-war with Verant as it has alwyas been. They kick us out, we adapt, find more information, they change more stuff, we fix it, etc etc etc...

One thing I find surprising is everyone talking about major drastic changes with the new SoL client. Personally I would find it very odd to see all these new changes _just_ with the SoL client, as it would (probibly) require 2 seperate network protocols, OR a major update to the Older clients (Velius/Kunark/Old World) in addition.
