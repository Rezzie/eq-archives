I know alot about trojans. The program most likely made a copy of itself in your windows, root, or system directory. On ctrl+alt+del it probably has a system name (aka windows, taskbar, etc). Check your win.ini in the run section, be sure thats empty, and then go to regedit in HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run and HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Runonce, look for anything unfamiliar with you and get rid of the mysterious programs that are set to run on logon. Then wammo your system's free.. however you should kill the program before ya take it off of the registry and win.ini. Some people make their trojans smart and make it so that they write to win.ini and the registry every time they close =P
