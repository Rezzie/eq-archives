Zombie 		posted 01-24-2001 04:13 PM            
Ok what to do if you are not getting Link Lights on your hub.....

1. I konw it sounds stupid, but make sure the cables are plugged into the back of the Computers.

2. Verify that the patch cables you have work. Either use a cable tester or you can swap them with the patch cable another computer is using and see if you get a link light on the hub.

3. Make sure you are not plugging your network cables into a special port on the hub. Most hubs will have at least one port for an UPLINK, but I have seen some with other ports labled MID-X oor something like that. Make sure you are not plugged into on of these ports.

4. Windows ME and I think 98 SE looks for connection to a network on bootup. If you are not plugged into the network on bootup it will disable the NIC card until you reboot your computer. Make sure all cables are plugged in when you bootup.


Whitefire

casey
Zombie 		posted 01-25-2001 01:41 AM            
the problem people are experiencing with 10/100 hubs occurs when the EQ and SEQ box are using different speeds. A hub works by just broadcasting the data everwhere, but you cant run it like that without a unique speed, and in _all_ 10/100 hubs, you realyl have 2 hubs, a 10mbit hub and a 100 mbit hub. To communicate from one speed to another, the segments are switched, and that is why you cant sniff across different speeds, 100mbit ports only broadcast to other 100mbit ports, and likewise for 10mbit.

10/100's are fine, just make sure both boxes are operating atthe same speed.
