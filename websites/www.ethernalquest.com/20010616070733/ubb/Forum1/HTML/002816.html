Melchior
Decaying Skeleton 		posted 06-01-2001 09:07 PM         
ElBigmac: At 37 with a full mana dump I had little trouble killing 4 sabretooth tigresses. My wisdom at the time was in the 170's or 180's, with only a little bit of +mana gear. With 200 wis I'm sure it could be done at 36.
Oh, and my main specialization was alteration. An evocation specialization would make it even easier. Not that that makes it a better choice, I'm glad I chose alteration...I rarely solo anymore except when collecting tailoring components. When I'm grouped I usually take the role of backup (or even primary) healer.

As for what to do...it's hard to beat the exp from quad kiting if you succeed every time, but it'll FEEL like you're levelling faster if you group. It'll take just as long but it'll be a heck of a lot more fun.

If you still want to solo, yes, give root/dot a try, especially if you're at a level when dots give significantly better mana efficiency (right after getting a new nuke but not a new dot this may not be the case). It lets you solo in smaller areas, both indoors and outdoors, and meditate during most of the time that you're fighting so you have less downtime. It becomes better and better compared to kiting--45 gives you ES vambraces for a mana-free 360ish dot, 49 gives you a 3 minute root, 52 and 53 give you new dots that stack with your others and each other. (Not sure after that, my druid's 53 right now. :P ) Most importantly it's a lot safer than quad kiting, particularly with an unreliable connection. Nearly every time I've been disconnected while quad kiting I was killed, while when root/dotting most of the time I survive if disconnected. And in the end how often you die is really the most important part of determining how fast you level, especially at high levels. It really sucks to have to spend two hours finding a resurrection or lose several days worth of exp.

Actually, I think my fastest exp has come from pairing up with an enchanter for fearkiting. Might want to give that a try...

Another suggestion...try running another class! I started a bard for a change of pace when running my druid got slow. There's nothing like running around whacking orcs, meleeing for a change, and levelling every hour or two if you're getting bored with your druid.

Heh, this ended up a LOT longer than I expected.
