Cryonic: If they raised the mob level, it would hit harder, get improved attacks and spells, maybe innate see invis. Too many things regarding the mobs are keyed directly off the level for them to do it that way. Which is sort of a programming compromise, but saves them having to spend hours detailing every mob's abilities. This way, all they need do is define what the mob looks like (the Skin), what class it's based off (most Melee mobs are Warrior), form of attack the mob has (hit/sting/whatever) (just for messaging purposes), chance to stun, HP factors (generally, mobs of a particular type have HP based on baseline+mult*(levels above base level), AC, and whether it needs magic to hit. A handful of numbers that define a whole class of mobs -- quick and easy, just what you need when defining a whole world. Of course, you do occasionally have stupidities associated with that, such as the lvl 30-ish Mechanics in Ak'anon with lvl 10-ish mob HP, which is why you see a lot of very sad people camping those...
