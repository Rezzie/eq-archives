think about it.. if you bought a car that never broke down or needed repaired, would you ever buy a new one? maybe if one came along with a bigger engine.. but you could buy that with all the money you *didn't* spend repairing your old one.. and then you could sell your old one, in good as new condition, to somebody with an even earlier model.. or even give it away..

equipment only leaves the game by decaying on corpses, being sold to merchants, or being destroyed by PCs.. similar for cash, although tradeskills are good money-sinks for the purpose of keeping the plat economy alive.. as people upgrade their equipment they hand their old stuff down to 'lesser' players through sale or gifts..

I can guarantee that it's not the GMs doing it; Guides don't have the power, even senior ones. It has to be done from the server console. And the GMs on the PvP servers are watched like hawks; nobody gets to GM there without a thorough looking-at.

Every non-newbie item that was ever introduced into the game is likely still there; every Ghoulbane, every piece of Rubicite, every Dragoon Dirk. This sends the economy of the game into a death spiral, where every newb will eventually have a full suit of fine steel plate and a Ghoulbane-level weapon. There simply won't be any point in playing a year from now, no matter what they do. And it all could have been fixed if they had added item decay to the game at the start.
