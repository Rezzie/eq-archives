FYI, I don't know what router you have but if it is a switching one it won't work. I have a Netgear CABLE/DSL Gateway Router (it switches traffic so only those packets designated for the specific IP get the info. It needs to be broadcast traffic for the others to see it. To fix it, I have a Netgear 4 port Hub that I connect to one port of the Router. Gave my Win98 and Linux boxes ip's 192.168.0.2 and .3 pluged them into the Hub, pointed the ShowEQ.conf to the x.x.x.2 and voila. WOW! What a tool..

Make sure that the hub has either a link switch/button or you use a crossover cable to the router.

hopefully i can help you clear up some dhcp confusion that appears to exist. the only thing i am unsure of is exactly what dhcp means, but here is my shot at what it stands for "Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol"

if i'm wrong, so be it. i know how it works and what it does though.

a.) you have dhcp clients, and dhcp servers (dhcpcd is the client in linux usually and dhcpd is the server)

b.) it is merely a way of obtaining an ip address. the dhcpd (server) has a configuration file with a few interesting items in it (your dns servers, the gateway for your network, your netmask and any other goodies you can toss in there). the other thing it has is an ip range (say, 10.0.0.5 - 10.0.0.100).

c.) the client software interacts in a generic sort of way (if that makes sense) when ever it is used. every time a windows machine boots, its dhcpcd (client) says "hey, is anyone out there a dhcp server?" if there is a dhcpd running (keep in mind that if you put more than one dhcpd on a network, u can fuck things up) it say "yeah, i'm here, take these settings and this ip for xxxx time"

so, there is your little lesson in dhcp. basically, it saves you from having to type in your dns address, your gateway, your domain, and your ip/netmask into the setup of your windows or linux box.

it will NOT effect weather or not you can sniff your everquest traffic.

the thing that is in all likely hood stopping you from doing that is a switch (probably built into the router device?)

do what the other guy suggested, go buy a HUB (usually they are 20 - 50 bucks cheaper). it will make it work. also, make sure your network topology is correct (although it looked correct).

ALL your computers should be hooked to a hub (or switch) and the uplink to the hub should goto your router. some routers have switches built into them, in this case even though you are hooking it directly physically to the router, from a network standpoint it is still a switch hooked to your router AND all your computers... know what i mean?

well... i hope u fix your problem :)

Nugget - I assigned all my IPs from the CPU's. That way they always have the same IP address and any IP address that you put in the Showeq.conf file will remain the same. And the default gateway is the Routers internal IP address (192.168.0.1).

My configuration is as follows.
Cable Modem ->[Router]
[Router]->Win98 (192.168.0.3) Wife PC
[Router]->[Hub] Netgear 4 port (using the link port)
[Hub] -> Win98 (192.168.0.2) EQ
[Hub] -> Linux (192.168.0.4) SEQ (sniffing 192.168.0.2)
