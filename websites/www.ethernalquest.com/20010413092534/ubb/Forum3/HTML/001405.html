Decaying Skeleton 		posted 03-16-2001 09:15 AM         
Yes you will need 1 NIC for 2k and 1 NIC for linux connect them with a crossed over cable.

Cat-5 cables contain 4 pairs of thin cables inside, only 2 pairs are needed for data transmission. Also, there are 8 'slots' for RJ-45 connector. Normally we plug 2 pairs of thin cable into slot 1,2,3,6 of RJ-45 connector. lets say Pair A to slot 1,2 and pair B to 3,6.

Straight cable means both ends of those 2 pairs of cables are parallely connected to slot 1,2 and 3,6. To make a cross over cable, we plug pair A to slot 1,2 and pair B to 3,6 but the other end of cable we connect pair B to slot 1,2 and pair A to slot 3,6.

The cable must be crossed if we connect 2 NICs together. A straight cable is used to connect to HUB or stuff like that, well there are exceptions though.

In fact, to get SEQ work, you need to connect the dsl modem to Linux box and share the connection using IP Masquerading, not shared by win2k. If your Linux box doesn't have a USB port for your modem, hmm too bad you need 1 more NIC for modem connection if your modem has ethernet connector.. so in this case, I totally agree with what adenine suggested, connect your modem, linux and 2k to hub.

BTW my SEQ works fine under Mandrake 7.2, just read your other post =)

Hope you understand what I say cos my mother language is not English.

Ok Samba hehe, in simple, with samba, you can browse your linux shared stuff from network neighhouhood on win2k box or vice versa. Therefore this is kind of off topic. Well Samba is a nice protocol but if you want some data transmission between both machines, you can use ftp protocol instead cos Samba isn't easy for nOOb, just my 2 cents..
