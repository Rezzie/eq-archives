Yeah. Trade skills. They're probably exaggerating a little bit with how much you can make, and it is certainly click intensive, but if you want to sit there for hours on end clicking, you may be able to clear 100 plat per hour. That is of course after you have built up the skills required.

Basically the guide tells you how to raise appropriate trade skills with little starting capital, and which items to craft and how to sell them in order to make profit. If you look around a bit on the web, you may be able to find it posted on a free site. I did. At least half of it has been nerfed, but from what I can see, there has been an upswing of people using this method to fund their newbie gear. If it gets much worse, Verant may decide to nerf the rest of it. Fierce Brew, Fish Stew, and Pie Tins used to make money, but were nerfed when they started being badly abused.

In my opinion there are better ways to make money. Kill a few Hill Giants, for example.

By the way, you can probably figure out most of it by doing some real research into trade skills. A lot of us have known about the methods for over a year. If you read the forums on the trade skills sites, some people aren't even shy any more about posting better methods than these guys are selling.

This is how it works now, but you get very little in return.

Make a DE Enchanter, put all of his points into CHA and the spare into INT (if you have spare INT/CHA items, give it to him.. if not it doesn't matter).

"Twink" him with a spit, a bunch of backpacks, and some plat. Fill a few of the backpacks with Batwings, which can be bought in FP at the casters guild.

Once you give the chanter the stuff, hand his guild note in (better faction), and put all of his 5 points into baking. Then goto "Neriak Down Under". In there you can find a baker merchant, buy some frosting.

Step 1 - In the spit 1 batwing + 1 frosting = Batwing crunchies

Sell them back to the merchants at the Enchanters guild (more money).. You will NOT make any money, but you will get some back for step 2.

THIS takes you upto skill level 46ish

Step 2 - 1 fresh fish + jug of sauce = TWO Fish Fillets

Buy the jugs of sauce from the baker merchant.. And buy the fish from a merchant also in Neriak Commons (not in Neriak Down Under).. I'm not exactly sure where he is, but he's near a water thing (not a mass of water, it's more of a foutain).

Sell the fish fillets back to the merchants in the chanters guild. You should get about 1pp 1gp 8sp per stack.. You get a LITTLE bit more money back than what you did.

This takes you upto around skill level 82.

Step 3 - 1 batwing + 1 fresh fish = 1 fish roll. These are a BANQUET size meal... Just make these and don't sell them back, save them for your main, they also only weigh 0.1.... Great for monks.

This should take you to about skill level 132. I do this step to make the fish fillets fail less.

Final Step - Keep making fish fillets and sell them back to the merchants.

-------

It took me about 3 and a half hours to get to skill level 116.

--Helpful tip--

When baking, put something heavy on your Ctrl button. Doing this makes you pick up 1 thing from a stack at a time. This way you can work the mouse a lot easier, it save you a lot of time also.

I made this myself, a few people I know found it out. With the exception of bat wings, Neriak Commons is the BEST zone for this. You are within half a minute from each merchant/banker you need to goto.

This gives you VERY LITTLE money, but it can help out a bit. If you do it all day long you can make a couple hundred plat.

I mainly just make fish rolls now, because they last forever =) Have fun killing your wrist
