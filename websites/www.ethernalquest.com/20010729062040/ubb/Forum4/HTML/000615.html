Its not really a bug persay. What happens is a mob gets beyond the full screen map, so SEQ shrinks the map a little to be able to show all the mobs at max zoom out.

A tip to make the map resize to the original if it starts to get too messed up is change the display points option under the map heading, then change it back how you like it.

I did a more extensive test last night, and here is what I found.

First of all, you are right that this happens when/because a mob goes out beyond the border of the screen. What is different in 3.0.2, however, is that once the mob goes out of bounds, the map doubles in size, and STAYS doubled.
In older versions of showeq, the map would linearly grow larger, just expanding enough to accomodate the mob as it went further and further out of bounds. Secondly, once the mob got cleared, or went back to its normal place, the map would bounce BACK to its normal size.

So the difference seems to be
1) The map now increases in size by doubling rather than increasing linearly
2) Once the map size is automatically doubled in this way, it says doubled, effectively rendering the map visually unusable until the map is manually reset.

I would propose the following solution.
When the map size increase from the player manually manipulating the map size, that the change be made permanent. However, if the map size is changed due to a mob going out of bounds, that the map size only change temporarily, until the mob is cleared. The map should "spring back" to the normal size.

I would also propose that the map expand linearly, rather than double.

This would also fix another minor inconvenience: When I am zoomed in the map in an area around myself, the map will zoom out when a mob goes OOB, and I have to rezoom in to where I was. I have to do this every five minutes, which is about how often a mob goes OOB. What I'd like is to have showEQ know that I selected this zoom focus, and that I don't want the map to change size at all.
