The good gnomes of Ak'Anon used their tinkering skill in an effort to see what was going on in Norrath. They came up with a system of satellites using magic and gnomish engineering to orbit Norrath and see what was going on. The gnomes were proud that the project was a success. Unknowingly, rogue gnomes were spying on the project and hacked into the satellite grid. As a result they were able to see everything that the good gnomes saw. In an effort to gain a profit from their newfound technology, the rogue gnomes went to sell the satellite hardware and frequencies to the rogues of other races. Each deal resulted in a double cross, the other rogues with the technology and the gnome rogues dead. As a result of these 'trades' every race that could be evil in one way or another have gained access to the satellites, including secret satellites that spied on the alter planes.

Now, how about this.

The gnomes have a system of satellites up, but I can only access the sat for the zone I am currently in.

Keeping in mind reasonable limits on resources, what do you think of this -

I'm waiting on a rare spawn (for this example I'll use Bilge) named Bilge. I don't want to sit in zone for days waiting on Bilge.

How difficult would it be to dump spawn lists from SEQ to a "secret" location that everyone running SEQ can poll for specific spawns. That way I can shoot back to DC when Bilge's name pops up *assuming* someone running SEQ is in DC and Bilge pops.

It could either be implemented in SEQ (from a viewing perspective) or I could point my web browser at a URL.

The only data sent to the "secret" location should be spawn-data. That eliminates the question of privacy for the person running SEQ.

It would let me quickly scoot to a zone and catch a rare spawn.

If no one with SEQ was currently in that zone, then the information for that zone would be null.

Just a thought. I'm hoping the developers'll look at this and comment.
