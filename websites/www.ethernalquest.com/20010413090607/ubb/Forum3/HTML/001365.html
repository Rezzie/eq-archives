Hi Dam,

This is normal, here is the explanation;

- When you zone, SEQ needs "a certain time" to crack the key for decoding player and mobs information of the new zone. This can take from a couple of seconds up to a quarter or so. It depends also on the power of your CPU, the fastest it is the faster you get the things cracked. In the meantime, SEQ is able only to show you the updated position of the mobs, not knowing what they are really, hence this list of "unknown" mobs. Once the key is cracked, SEQ retrofits the decoded information to the mobs list and to the map.

- While SEQ is cracking the key for decoding mobs information, new mobs information are flowing in (spawns, etc..) an some of these are not encoded. SEQ can thus show them immediately.

- Finally, since a couple of months, the real amount of HP of NPC's is not sent anymore to the client but only percentage. So, the HP value is a percentage of full HP for NPC and real HP value for PC.


Hope it clarify SEQ's behavior...

P.S: I have no Idea about your Velious problem...

Well actually your CPU speed doesn't really affect the decoding more than a few ms; the only thing that really affects it is how quickly a player or NPC spawns in the zone. So if you're zoning into an empty zone alone don't plan on it decrypting too quickly

There's no way to show the actual HP value for an NPC (unless you have a Guide or GM flag, in which case, the actual HP data is sent for both NPCs and PCs). In addition, the percentage of HP a mob has is not even reported until you get close enough to it I believe. I think its the same for players too not positive though..
