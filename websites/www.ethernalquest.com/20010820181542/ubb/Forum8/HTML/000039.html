Well, for starters, I underestimated just how much of the game is server-side.

At first, I figured all I'd need to would make a server that keeps tracking players going in and out, where they are in the world (figuring the buildings, for example, for static enough that there would be some kind of magic 'map file' that was with the client), and what they're doing and how that affects the world. Ta da.

My first snag was when AO was looking for Funcom-made servers - there's a computer it connects to to get a list of all the AO servers that are possible. So I thought maybe I could fool it or redirect it or something (I'd still settle for offline AO for now, just to goof off a bit), but it's pretty hard coded in there to both the launcher program and the client. They're both expecting some kind of data, something along lines of a secret knock, so that it knows the server data's coming from a place it considers 'Funcom Made'.

My second snag was just how little of the game I possessed! I have sound effects, various character and item models, data on how they interact, textures, and config stuff ... but everything that makes the WORLD - where the buildings are, where the characters are, keeping track of the accounts, even the time of day in the world ... is all done server side ... I can't find one, lousy, shread of anything that might look like a map. It's like everytime you 'zone' in AO (go to another location), you're actually temporarily existing in that place. If you leave it, you really never have access to it - your character doesn't, your computer doesn't. The metaphysical implications are interesting enough ... but I can maybe see why this might be so. The good news is, maybe Funcom wants every AO server it makes to have a unique world. In UO, for example, that Britain Bank isn't going anywhere. You could play on almost any shard, and 99% of them will have a Brit Bank. So in AO, maybe you won't even find yourself in the same part of Rubi-Ka depending on the server ... kinda EQish. The bad news? You want to make a server emu? Start making some way to place little buildings on a map for your players to run around in, other wise they're going to be connecting to a whole lot of nothing.

It's not impossible ... just a really big pain in the ass. A bigger pain, and perhaps a bigger ass, than I thought it was going to be.

Well the whole idea behind emulating is that it [the emulator] sends down a replica of the real server's datastream. So just sniff the datastream of zone information coming down from their servers, and have the emulator send the exact same thing. Same goes for the login server. The whole system seems pretty similar to EQ's with the login server sending down a list of available servers for the client to connect to and the multiple zone server type architecture, and it obviously didn't take much to get EQ connecting to non-Verant servers; I don't think it would be too different for AO. You just need to get (a) programmer(s) who are willing to work on it.
