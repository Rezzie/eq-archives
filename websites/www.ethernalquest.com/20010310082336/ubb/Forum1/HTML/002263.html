Best money making scheme so far?

Velious spells.

The best ones (improved invis., monster summoning 2) sell for 1k *easy*, and
they drop off of level 44 MOBs.

The level 39 spells also should sell for a lot, they drop off flying monkeys in
Wakening Land, they're like low 30s. Really easy stuff.

The problem with most tradeable gear is that you waste LOTS of time trying to
find a buyer. With spells, however, players feel compelled to buy them.

I have low 50s mage friends who have made dozens of thousand of pp in a couple
of days just farming spells. They bind in EC, at the tunnel, and don't mind
paying 200pp to get ported back to Velious.


Anyway, it's way out of reach for you. What I can recommend if you REALLY want
to make money, is to become a trader.

How does that work? You go to places where stuff drop, you buy the stuff that
drops there from players farming the spawns, and you go sell it where stuff
sells ... AKA East Commonmart or Greater Baydark.

For instance, I bought a BCC for 70pp in Guk. It sells for up to 400 in EC. You
do the math.

If you want to farm for money, at your level your best bet is CB or DF belts. A
couple PP each.

"Can anyone tell me how to make atleast 100pp in a week. I'm a level 10 ranger... I've been searching all week for an answer to this. I just wanna know how to make money faster than my current trade "selling CB belts" Please gimme some info, Thanx"

Yes, 100 plat in a week is trivial, even at level 10. He says in a later post he does not want to cheat and remember he is level 10 so why post about hill giants?

Here is a way off the top of my head, you are a ranger so track bears. A good spot might be misty but a better one is mtn rathe at the hill of druids where bears often are. West commons by the zone line to kith is another good bear killing zone at about 10 and a bit later.. see that corner nearby? That is a pretty safe "pocket" nothing wanders there.

Basically on a level 10 character like you I'd pick WC by the zoneline into kith kinda. You can pull bears to that pocket there and be pretty safe and run for the zone if in trouble. If you do die coming back from east commons is fairly short (get bound there)

If you are like my druid (and you probably are, being a ranger) you do not get aggroed by the druids there (in mountain rathe) for killing the bears. In fact, as a druid, I charmed some of the bears to help kill some other ones. The only trouble i had was that the npc druids HELPED me and killed the bear that was hitting my charmed bear and me. That makes the druid hill in mountains rathe a great safe (unless you get too many aggro bears at once) spot to kill bears. I am not sure if ranger is the same as my druids.. but the npcs there never hurt me even killing a bear right by them. If you have a friend who can port you back near there you might want to test to see if they do. If they don't, the volume of bears there make it a gold mine. Problem: its out of the way and not near a bind spot for a ranger that low level really.

What you do is save all high quality bear hides (now can drop from kodiaks and grizzlies also, as well as the black and brown bears like always) Those sell to tailors for backpack making for 20 plat or more each. Never accept less than 20 plat, someone will pay 20 plat or more.

A good thing to do is for you to WAIT until someone is auctioning to buy them, instead of you auctioning to sell them. The person auctioning to buy them is DESPERATE. You might be able to arrange a package deal like 6 high qual bear hides for 150 plat or someting. Depending on your server, he is gonig to tailor them into a 10 slot backpack he will then sell for 50 - 100 plat.

Just selling the high quality hides you can make 100 plat in a week, easy. However here is another good low level way to make more. You will also get some low quality hides. You yourself can tailor these into something.

What you do is once you have made some money from selling the high qual hides to greedy tailors, you vendor delve. Vendor delving is when you buy out crappy low priced stacks of things on the lower player sold vendor spaces until the slot is empty, then you ESC and refresh (reopen) the vendor. The spot is filled with something new the vendor has. Good way to do it is with a large sewing kit in your first slot and combine (destroy) the stuff in it while delving the slots of the vendor then refresh several at once. Set a limit before you start. As a level 54 my limit is like 5 gold per item. If you exhaust the cheap things and the stuff you want is not showing, don't get greedy, move on.

Big vendor delving clue: When you empty a slot to a vendor, if you then sell it what you are looking for and it fills the slot.. forget it... that is the only one the vendor has. If it DOESN'T fill the slot.. the vendor has more. At least one more the vendor will have than the one you sold, probably more (or it might workthe opposite way, but I think it works that way.)

Why am I off on this tangent? Cause the other use for your large sewing kit besides vendor delving is buying the spiderling silks (make sure SPIDERLING) from the vendor you are delving. In newbie areas around small spiders you can uncover a vendor's slot with nearly unlimited silks.

So how does that make you money? Okay. You combine 2 of the silks in the sewing kit, you get a thread. Keep doing that. One time saver is that in inventory view, while buying you can combine 2 silks as you get them, then click your mouse pointer on to your picture in the upper right hand corner of the screen (after putting at least one or more threads in seperate piles in other containers earlier) then even though you don't see it.. the combined now "silk thread" will drop into your inventory on the other piles. What that means is that you can continue to buy and make silk threads never leaving the buy screen. This all sounds complicated but is easy once you get the hang of it.

Okay, now after you have TONS of silk threads and the large sewing kit.. and adding tailoring points if you can, you move on to the low quality bear hides you have been getting. Another thing is if you buy a skinning knife from someone, you can make any medium or high qual bear hides into low. Put the knife and the hide in your sewing kit, combine, you get the knife back and the next lower hide. THEN you take the low qual hide and silk thread and combine and make your BIG money maker in volume:

Leather padding.

This is used for various cultural smithing as well as fine steel. BONUS: the leather padding is low weight and STACKS. Prices depend upon your server, but a stack might sell for 20 plat each or whatever some desperate lazy fool will pay.

For verification of various info or recipes, go to www.eqtraders.com. There you will see first hand how desperate some of the tradespeople, or your "marks" are.

As you may imagine, while delving vendors for the silk threads you will find other things you can sell.. like bone chips. I have got as many as 150 bone chips this way from a single vendor. Just so you remember none of this is done by cheating or via any programs, which I don't use.

The key is to find a vendor in an area you KNOW must have the things you want, then delve and do so a while AFTER a patch. If I hear that a patch day is coming up i will go then and hit certain vendors like the gfay newbie lift vendor lady, the fat ogre vendor in freeport (hard to miss) etc. Newbies sell EVERYTHING, no matter how many people auction they are paying a bunch you will ALWAYS find many of these valueble things on vendors. Things which you can sell for worthwhile money at level 10 are like:

The leather paddings you make($$$$$$$$)
Stacks of silk swatches (2 Spider silk)

Stacks of the silk thread (but more if you make paddings than all by selves.)

High quality bear hides (I've actually found these on vendors by delving.. amazing as much as they are auctioned for)

Low and medium quality whatever hides (skin the medium with the skinning knife, skin high quality cat and other pelts with it also to make low) then of course make leather paddings.

Bone chips and snake eggs, which people also often auction for.


Sorry for the long babbling nature of this post. Using the methods I've described here, starting at killing bears and sellng the high quality pelts for seed money you can make not 100 plat a week but more like 500 or a few thousand plat a week depending on how much you get the hang of doing these things and just how resistent you are to carpal tunnel syndrome (boy I wish they'd put in shift click to buy 20 items already)

I'd reccomend buying a couple wrist supports of the anti carpal tunnel variety BEFORE starting this stuff. Someone bought me a pair for XMAS (kinda late now.. some of the damage already done) by some company named "Mueller" Get the kind that have some metal thing inside that keeps your wrist from bending while typing.. vendor delving, etc.

If all of this seems daunting, here is the simplified way again:
Kill bears in wc near zone to kith, in that lil pocket right by the zone (pull to there)
save high quality bear hides. Sell the high quality bear hides to tailors wanting to make backpacks. Sell for at least 20 plat. Get uber newbie rich.
