This doesn't happen all that much, but with certain zones it would be very helpful.

I am currently working on finishing up maps for Skyshrine. There are a couple parts of the zone that are vertically oriented, containing two or three overlapping areas.

Normally I like to have a map of the zone for each level, so the lines don't get confusing. For example, I created a "Sebilis" map for the top part of Sebilis and a "SebilisSewers" map for the lower part. I do a File-Open and open SebilisSewers if I'm down there.

For Skyshrine I will end up with at least three maps, perhaps four.

I was wondering if you guys could program a standard into SEQ for this. For example, if I had four maps for Skyshrine, my maps would be named:

Skyshrine
Skyshrine2
Skyshrine3
Skyshrine4

"Skyshrine" would load upon entering the zone. Then to get to the other maps you would have a single keypress, for example, PgUp and PgDn. PgDn would cycle up in number, and PgUp would cycle backwards in number. For example, if you had just zoned in and Skyshrine loaded, and then entered the tower section of the zone, you would just press PgDn and SEQ would open the "Skyshrine2" map.

Not a huge thing, but would really make moving thru multiple maps more efficient.

Zombie 		posted 04-06-2001 11:12 PM         
Not off the top of my head...

Do a search on these forums, or the SourceForge forums for depth filtering or just maps.

Something may or may not come up, I'm not sure...

Just a brief run down, however:

When you create a map in SEQ, it stores the Z location. The map depth code uses that value to determine if the line should be displayed or not.

For instance, when I'm in OS and I turn on filtering and I'm hanging out in the Jail, all I see is the jail area and some other rooms across the zone that are on the same plane / elevation as the room I'm in. I can do this for mobs as well.

You can increase/decrease the tolerance, revealing more or less of the upper/lower parts of the map at will.

Fading will fade the map in and out as you acend or decend.

I can think of a _FEW_ places where this might not be suitable and a few circumstances where this might not be suitable... but for those few I don't know if it's worth the trouble to go through to put in a next/last option that probably wouldn't be used.

I'll try to answer any questions you may have, if I can.
