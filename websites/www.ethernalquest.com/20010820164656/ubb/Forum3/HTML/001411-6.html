a_necro52
Zombie 		posted 05-17-2001 07:37 PM            
Shagrath, this actually wasn't done to disable sniffing so much as to increase network performance. A hub routes all packets where a swtich will route only packets addressed to the ethernet card that it is attached to on that port. Hubs allow for sniffing, but if you have a large network with no switching, you end up with a large number of collisions that cause poor network performance.

adenine!
Zombie 		posted 05-21-2001 10:20 PM            
The problem is unrelated to simply having a switch somewhere on the network. I haven't seen anyone specifically say that the setup you have wouldn't work. The people who have problems with switches just plug the two computers and the cable/DSL interface into the switch. This will not work. Instead of explaining the details of networking and describing the process of setting up IP masquerade, it's much easier to say "you need a hub, not a switch". When dealing with newbies it is generally a good idea to keep it simple as possible. It can also be generally assumed that if the person being told a switch will not work is in fact experienced with networking, they wouldn't be asking the question in the first place because they would know what the problem is and how to fix it.
