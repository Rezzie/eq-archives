Heh, NT *is* "enterprise orientated", it just does a really shitty job at doing it.

A year or two ago, the company I worked for was hosting the Barns and Nobles website. They were using some pretty beefy machines, all running NT. They had an Oracle backend, IIS, the whole works. I don't remember the specific hardware vendor, but it was definetly to be considered an "enterprise solution".

At any rate, their software configuration turned up a memory leak somewhere in NT. When queried (and I recall it took at least a month to get Microsoft to admit there was a problem), they said there was no bug, and if it became a problem, just reboot the machines every day.

That was their SOLUTION. Reboot the machine! In a production environment.

Again: Enterprise orientated? Yes. A smart move? No.
