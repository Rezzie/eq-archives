The way that packet sniffing works is this:

when a packet is sent accross a hub each system looks at the packet and if it is not for them they ignore it. A switch on the other hand routes packets to specific machines. so on a hub a packet will hit all the computers plugged into it, but when a packet hits a switch the switch says oh that computer is on port 4; and sends the packet directly there

This is the reason you want a hub, not a switch or a router when setting this (unless your router is the sniffing machine)

everyone got that?

Hope it helps you to understand a little better

Most hubs and switches now come with an uplink port. This eliminates the need for crossover cables. A good thing since crossover cables aren't as available as straight through cables.

All the uplink port does is switch the wires the same way a crossover cable does.

For anyone who can not find a Crossover cable I will the pin out so you can make it yourself.

1 Orange/White ----- 3 Green/White
2 Orange------------ 6 Green
3 Green/White ------ 1 Orange/White
4 Blue-------------- 4 Blue
5 Blue/White ------- 5 Blue/White
6 Green ------------ 2 Orange
7 Brown/White ------ 7 Brown/White
8 Brown ------------ 8 Brown

As you can tell the only differance between a Crossover cable and a Straight through is the fact that pins 1 and 3 are crossed and 2 and 6 are crossed.

If you have a straight through pacth cable that you want to make a cross over cable then go to the computer store and get a few RJ-45 connectors for the end of the cable and a pair of crimpers. All you do if cut the connector off of one end of the cable and switch wire 1 and 3 and then do the same for wire 2 and 6. Then put the RJ45 connecter on the end and crimp it.

If you guys have any other questions about this let me know.
