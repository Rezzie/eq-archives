==========
Constructive Criticism of EQ Hacker's Realm
==========

As I attempted to access EQ's Hackers Realm, their server-sided scripts apparently barred me from accessing their site. Although I appreciate the effort to make a Web site devoted to EverQuest, I do not think it's smart, market-wise, and user-friendly to banish the most commonly used browser: Internet Explorer. Over 80% of the Internet users (according to Netcraft's Novemeber 2001 survey) use Internet Explorer.

In addition, corporate network administrators usually do not allow its employees to install programs with permissions set to disallow installation of other browsers.

Finally, Internet Explorer 6.0 is much more reliable than Netscape. Although I do support the Netscape cause and the open-source cause, I don't find it supporting all of the standards of the W3C nor do I find it a reliable browser: I've had it crashed many times. I refuse to use a browser that crashes more than twice in an hour--what if I'm writing an e-mail? Internet Explorer 6.0 supports all of the standards of thw W3C.

Limiting users to one browser is a very bad practice. You significantly limit the number of users to access your site, thus discreasing publicity. IF YOU ARE GOING TO MAKE A WEBSITE, YOU SHOULD MAKE TWO VERSIONS: ONE TO SUPPORT INTERNET EXPLORER, ANOTHER TO SUPPORT NETSCAPE-BASED BROWSERS. Go to CNet.com, and you will find that you should create two versions of your website. Good look with your Web site.

Limiting users from accessing your site is as if you're preventing me from bringing my own bottled water into a restaurant. It's a very bad practice, businesswise.

--Darkweaver.

That's because Netscape messed up pretty big. This is how it works. Netscape runs an open source project called "Mozilla". When Mozilla was at about version 0.6 Netscape took that code, made a few changes, and released it as "Netscape 6".
