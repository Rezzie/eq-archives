hmm, heres some info
.. First look if you can see anything in plain text (ie.: you read it, and its english text or whatever) ..
If its not, it gotta be either encrypted, or compressed, or both .. (or you just didnt look hard enough)
If its encrypted / compressed .. you need to use some disassembler to figure out how its encrypted / compressed .. then write your own packets sniffer that decompressed / decrypts the stuff for you ..
Then you just look and look and look and think .. at one point you figure out what one of the packets is doing .. maybe because it contains some text, or maybe because its sent every time you do something specific, or something specific happens ..
Then you gotta look at the event that happened, and think what they'd have to transmit to make it worthwhile info sent ..
Taking the movement packet as an example, they gotta transmit some movement vectors, and maybe your current x/y/z coords to sync up....
Now you look at the packet and you try to find the bytes =)
Now you've got one packet less to worry about

Hope this helps you a bit
