qalnor, stuffing files into jpegs and other similar tactics isnt all for flash. Its the only really ture way to hide your data. When the FBI comes knocking on your door and looks on your computer, they see 1 of 2 things. Encrypted data, or plaintext data. If the see encrypted data, you give up keys or go to jail while they crack it. If they see unencrypted data, they just look at it. When they look at your jpegs or whatever, they see pictures, and rarely would look deeper into them, thus truly hiding your data.

There are also better methods that stuffing files into pictures or sound. The method i like is a filesystem patch you can get for linux. You make the filesystem, and give it differing levels of access, with passwords. You first mount up the partition with no passwords, and put in filler data, then you mount up the partition with a password you want to use, you then write your sensitive data.

Now, when someone comes looking, you have the filesystem mounted as normal, and no one but you knows that you can mount it with a password option and get a different interpretation of the data off the same device (your sensitive info), since the device looks exactly like any other ext2 filesystem.
