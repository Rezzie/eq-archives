I have a sky "debug" texture which I used to study the sky system. Basically, the sky is a large sphere with a texture map on it that is tinted when necessary. The pieces themselves are tiled together to make the sky. For a two layer sky, you have the sphere as mentioned above and a simitransparent tinted cloud layer on top of the first cloud texture. These layers are offsetted so that it doesn't look like just one layer. For the moons, planets, etc, those are just sprites placed on the top of the sphere's textures and are rotated on the sphere according to the in game clock.
