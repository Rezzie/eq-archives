All the packets that are passed back and forth encrypted. The chances of having your packets captured, decrypted and your password stolen is very small. With as many packets as are passing around, you would almost have to be targeted by someone who knew your IP, and was on your segment.

Your password would most likely be stolen in other ways. Someone using a trojan horse, or tricking you into giving it to them.

Older cable modems can be hacked to allow you to see other people's packets.

Newer cable modems are much tougher to do this too (I don't know how nor have I heard of someone doing it).

Cable modems are typically bridges. They typically only pass packets whose destination matches your MAC.
