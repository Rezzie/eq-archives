I poked around a bit, and found what I believe to be the way Initative affects attack and recharge delays.

Let the players AggDef be -100 to 100, where -100 is max defense and +100 is max offense. All delays are calculated in 1/100 second.

ComputedAttackDelay=(weapon.attackdelay + 100) - (player.initiative(weapon.initiativeskill) * 0.5 * 0.333333);
ComputedAttackDelay-=player.aggdef;
if (ComputedAttackDelay < 100.0)
ComputedAttackDelay=100.0;

ComputedRechargeDelay=(weapon.rechargedelay + 100) - (player.initative(weapon.initiativeskill) * 0.5 * 0.666666);
ComputerRechargeDelay-=player.aggdef;
if (ComputedRechargeDelay < 100.0)
ComputedRechargeDelay=100.0;

In other words...
Setting your aggdef to maximum subtracts one second from each of recharge and attack, setting it to it's minimum adds one to each.
Having 600 in the initative skill will reduce attack speed by 1 second and recharge speed by 2 seconds.
The effective attack delay and recharge delay can never be less than 1 second.

So at HIGH levels, you're probably better off going after weapons with high delays as your skills will offset them

Now, I don't know how the server does things, but this is how the client calculates how often you should shoot... Odds are the two are the same
