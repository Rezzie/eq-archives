You are getting confused.

It is NOT INT+2 so you're worries that the cap is 198 is unfounded.

use this formula:

(INT * (LVL/5)) + (LVL * 2)

It works out nicer, because it lets you see how much a point change in int will affect your mana pool (assuming INT < 200)

lvl 10 : +1 int = +2 mana
lvl 20 : +1 int = +4 mana
lvl 30 : +1 int = +6 mana
lvl 40 : +1 int = +8 mana
lvl 50 : +1 int = +10 mana

I like to think of it that way becuase it helps me evaluate which equipment to use. I have some equipment that have higher INT that I keep in the bank because my lower stuff has +mana bonuses and even though I have less INT, I have more total mana.

I can state these things flatly, with complete authority:

Yes, I am, once again, trying to dispell the myth that you get +1 mana for +1 int post 200. You do not. There is absolutely, positively, without a doubt NO, NONE, ZERO, ZILCH, NADA, NIL, NOTHING, bonus to your mana pool over 200 INT.

Let me say this again. THERE IS NO MANA INCREASE TO YOUR MANAPOOL > 200 INT

I can also state that I have and can use over 3600 mana in my pool. I have found no cap on the total amount mana you can have.

Now... some interesting things I've found out. Instead of giving off the typical response, I actually logged in and ran some tests, here's what I found:

Everything I'm putting forth here holds for Level 60. It may or may not be valid for previous levels... but really, who cares about anything lower than 60

108 INT = 1440 Mana (13.333 mana/int)
110 INT = 1462 Mana (13.290 mana/int)
117 INT = 1539 Mana (13.153 mana/int)
198 INT = 2452 Mana (12.380 mana/int)
200 INT = 2475 Mana (12.375 mana/int)
202 INT = 2475 Mana (12.375 mana/int)
212 INT = 2475 Mana (12.375 mana/int)

This is revealing an alarming trend. Your mana / int goes DOWN the more INT you get, up until the cap of 200. Why is this? I have a couple theories:

1. Verant just wants to fuck people and give them less mana.
2. Mana gear : INT gear ratio scales inversely to the amount of mana gear you have. (IE - More mana gear = less Mana/INT you get)
3. Total mana pool is taken into account when figuring how much mana/int you get.

This was a somewhat impromptu test, with the gear I had on my person and in my bags. I think it warrants further exploration. I would like to test out adding +INT items ONLY (some of the gear I tested with had +mana in addtion to +int, I subtracted the +mana values). I do not believe this is the case, however, as the mana/int values went down even when I used a few pure INT items.

So, as you can see:

The formulas given are not exact
201 > INT gives you absolutely no benefit
Mana / INT value decreases the higher INT value you have (Trade out that INT For mana stuff!)
