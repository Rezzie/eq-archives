Sounds like you've made some good progress. I'll make a few suggestions and see if that helps you.

I know you've set up ppp, have you checked /etc/ppp to see if you have a firewall-masq file? Are you using IPChains? Have you checked the informative, but very confusing HOWTO, on IPChains?

You have 2 NICs in your linux box, right? Let's assume eth1 is 192.168.2.1 on the hot side and eth0 is 192.168.1.1 on the private side. Can you ping 192.168.2.1 from any system on the private side? This will tell you your firewall is actually forwarding IP like it's supposed to.

Have you verified your linux box can ping the internet as well as your private network?

What does ifconfig say?

What is the result of issuing the following command exactly as I type it on the line below:

ipchains -L

Have you verified connectivity on your private network? How?

Answering these questions should lead you in the right direction if not fix your problem. If it doesn't fix your problem, post here and answer *each* question completely and I'm sure I or someone else can assist you.

Good Luck!
