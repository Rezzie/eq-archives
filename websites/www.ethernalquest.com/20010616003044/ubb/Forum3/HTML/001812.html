You are getting stuck at the ./configure. The process is cumulative. If you have errors at step 1, subsequent steps cannot successfully complete.

There are good instructions here on how to install if you do some searching and browsing. You probably have the default qt installed that comes with Mandrake 7.2. This is the wrong version of qt.

The secret is to get the pre-reqs, and then get the appropriate lib. I've posted on this. If you just look up my previous posts, you should find many of the answers you need. I spent quite a bit of time browsing to get a feel for the install.

If you run into trouble, re-post the instructions you are following (as well as any modifications you may have added) and I'll have a look. I'm running on Mandrake 7.2.

BTW - FYI - you don't need to compile your own qt if you see my note on "why is QT so hard?" -- I haven't done that yet, but I'm also not using CVS. Don't use CVS for your first install. I'm suggesting you skip CVS for now because based upon your question it sounds like you need to move in small steps, and CVS for Mandrake can be a big step unless you are very comfortable with sysadmin and compiling source you download (based upon my exp and conversations with adenine!).

Good Luck!
