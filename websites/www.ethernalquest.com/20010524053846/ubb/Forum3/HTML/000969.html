This is my observation from personal experience:

The mobs do know your level, and it does affect how they react to you. Well, except the undead. Most mobs will not aggro on you if you are high enough a level that they wouldn't stand a chance even if you were half dead already. Also, your relative level often influences when they run. Facing a yellow mob alone, it will seldom flee unless a higher level assists you. If you attack a low blue, they will start running at about 1/3 health. If you attack a deep green, they tend to run after 1 or 2 hits. Of course, skeletons and some other undead will attack you regardless of level and never run.

Zenon, THERE IS NO DIFFERENCE.
When a mob spawns it's stats are set. Plain and simple. Your level affects your combat ability and thus your ability to trounce (or get trounced by) that mob. Uhh, Greg, as much as I hate contradicting people I have to say it. You are wrong.
Loot is determined at the SPAWN time. That's why rusty, bronze, and FS weapons are always wielded by the mob, and why some items (like the gossamer robe) are actually worn.
In SEQ, you can even tell if a wisp has a GLS because of the type of light source listed.
On top of that Verant has clearly stated that it is determined at spawn time, when the issue of bottom-feeding was brought up.
The only variable in loot tables is how often a mob is killed in a set time, as a RUMORED part of anti-camping code, and it still takes effect at SPAWN time. How long it takes to kill it, how many people kill it, the level of the people killing, don't matter. Loot is predetermined. Rogues can steal the cash and any gear the mob can use it WILL use (that's why deacaying skeletons that drop cloth armor are harder to kill, because they're using that armor).
