Ok, a couple of questions. I have cable at home with three PCs, one acting as a gateway/firewall, all connected to a 10/100 autosense switch. I want to set up ShowEQ and I'm trying to figure out how I can leave the switch in place. If ShowEQ only sniffs packets from the game PC, then I should be able to connect the game PC and the ShowEQ PC to a small hub and uplink the hub to the switch. However, if the ShowEQ PC also needs to sniff incoming packets from the gateway, perhaps someone can recommend another solution. I am trying to keep the gateway connected to the switch at all times.

Well, the way ShowEQ and your network work is like this:

1. Incoming packets come from www to your gateway.
2. If gateway == EQ Machine, ShowEQ will not detect packets. If not, packets are broadcasted to all computers on the hub connected to the gateway, or to the computer they were meant to if the hub is actually a switch. If its a switch, ShowEQ will not work.

The way you explained it, it seems as though you have a gateway to the internet that sends packets over a switch to another subnet's gateway, which is on a hub. On this hub, you have your EQ and ShowEQ boxes. If this is the case, you should be fine.

You can do what you are suggesting. As long as the seq pc and the game pc are on a hub together, seq will work.

seq does need to see traffic coming from the gateway, but only traffic destined for the game pc. Since you are going to have the game pc and the seq pc on the same hub, the seq pc will be able to see all inbound and outbound packets to the game pc.

You're golden.
