Veeshan's Peak Loot Follow-up
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:03 PM PDT
Brad wrote a follow-up to his Veeshan's Peak loot post. Here's the entire message:

    The following was my third post regarding the subject prior to the thread corrupting (*sigh*):

    I don't mean this in a condescending way at all, but I honestly wish some of you would at least try to put yourselves in our shoes.

    What options do we have in these situations?

    First, let's establish a few facts:

    1. This is a dynamic game. It may not be as dynamic as some would like with massive invasions and huge volcanic eruptions and entire NPC governments taking action that affect players' lives, but it DOES change. We add to the game, we change the game, and some times we take away. (Actually, it makes me worry for when the technology is such that we can do all of that stuff -- how will people respond... 'That volcano created a mountain where my favorite hunting grounds were and blocked the entrance to my favorite dungeon and now I have to walk farther to the new entrance at the top of the crater! You nerfed me!').

    We cannot have stagnant games, yet when the status quo changes, SOMEBODY always get's upset, especially if the change is not universally positive (and what change is?).

    2. We're not perfect! We will make mistakes. We will continue to make mistakes as long as we continue to add content, and things will be out of balance in the future.

    3. We learn and get better. For example, lots of universal class non-specific loot like Rubicite is bad.

    So, what options do we have:

    1. We could just leave mistakes and aspects of the game that didn't turn out as planned. I think that leads to stagnation. I also think it leaves areas of the game untouched... empty zones, ignored quests, etc.

    2. We could upgrade the VP items that are weaker to what they should be. This doesn't address the other items that are too powerful relative to their difficulty to obtain, but it would address one side of the problem. The problem is that they'd be comparable then most likely to the other broken items. Many players would then simply try to obtain the broken items since they are as good as the upgraded VP items but easier to get. And we'd still have broken items.

    3. We could upgrade the VP items and make them better than the non-VP broken items (and not adjust encounter difficulty). Here's where the MUDflation comes in. Now, what have we done to all the other items in the game... they're all indirectly weaker now. And, we've set a new (lower) standard of risk vs. reward. The game becomes less challenging, etc.

    4. We could upgrade the VP items and make them better than the non-VP broken items (and then re-adjust NPC encounter difficulty throughout the game as well as other treasure tables everywhere, raising the entire risk/reward ratio but keeping it balanced). This, by the way, is what the infamous mud wimping article would have us do -- either that, or nothing. Basically, it's a ton of work and would probably introduce a whole slew of new problems and potential for mistakes. Bluntly, we'd rather allocate those resources elsewhere.

    So, our solution is to identify the non-VP items that are out there that are too powerful and probably move them to VP. And then take some underpowered VP items and move them elsewhere AND/OR make some new items to replace the over-powered ones.

    I also want to emphasize that there really aren't that many items. Also, just to let you know, MORE of this is going to occur when EQ Live starts revamping old world zones (Permafrost, Najena, etc.) We will be adding new content AND moving existing content around.

    I know this isn't compatible with some players' feelings that how and where things are in the game the day they started playing MUST also be the same 3, 6 or 12 months later. But, with all due respect, that's simply NOT how MMORPGs work. As mentioned earlier, if anything, MMORPGs will become MORE dynamic and less predictable/dependable in the future.

    In closing, I'll quote from one of the EQ Live game designers emails to me regarding some of the items that very well might change -- I want to emphasize again that there aren't that many we're talking about:

    "Broken Weapons include: Blade of the Black Dragon Eye, Warding Star, Runed Fighters Staff (Somewhat easy to acquire, godly proc ratio), Moss Covered Twig (we all know this one), etc.

    Those are a few examples of broken items which have been acquired by players and severely hamper our attempts to create new and interesting loot. The players should be made happy soon however. We just went through and nerfed the drops of those broken weapons and "added new content" -- replacement new items. Near copies of some of the broken weapons are being placed into VP." -EQ Live Game Designer

    -Brad

    p.s. I know this might encourage people to camp more short-term, but I wanted to be up front and detailed with all of you nonetheless. Heh, everything's a trade-off.

  • Class Changes
    We have nothing in particular planned right now. We do plan on retesting all of the classes to ensure that they fall where we feel that they should prior to freezing the classes. Most of these tests have already been completed for most of the melee classes, with only a few more steps necessary to finalize those.

  • More on the Story Elements in EQ
    On one hand we have players asking for more of a story line to be overtly implemented into the game. On the other, there's also those who prefer an open "floorplan" if you will. In either case, we want to provide for both players.

    "Story" requires that some limits be placed on the breadth of things that players can do. There's really no way around that. Whether the story is done in short bursts, such as quests, or in large multi-zone epics, there is some limit on what you can do to take part in the story.

  • Determining Balance of the Classes
    As the game is and always will be primarily PvE (Player versus Environment), balance in this area has always been the chief concern. Even on the PvP servers, PvE is still the main focus there and affects everyone whether or not they engage in PvP regularly.

    That said we also try to keep things in mind for PvP. For instance, the damage reduction for spells cast by players upon other players is one of the PvP balance mechanisms.

    [Quote from original post: I believe Abashi or Aradune said that there would be a way for verant to relay storied to the players in the future well it must be the DISTANT future because it hasn't happened yet.]

  • Abolish Disease

As it is designed, you must often make a choice with what class you want to be near. If you are going up against something with really strong poison, you certainly want clerics around. For disease, you'll probably want to add a shaman. If you are looking for something a little more general purpose, there's the druid, with respectable DD, DOTs, healing, buffs, and ports.


Discussion of the Week: Dynamic Events
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 2:58 PM PDT
Gordon started this week's Discussion of the Week on the Gameplay Board. Here's the complete post:

    I think that there’s a common misconception going around that, “All dynamic events consist of GMs going on a killing spree.” This isn’t the case, as there are many different types of dynamic events, ranging from the typical raid, to story-telling events, to epic quests with a vast story-line behind them.

    Raids are typically short on story and last anywhere from a half an hour to an hour or two. They usually consist of trio of actor NPCs or so entering an area and running amok until they are killed by a sufficient force of players.

    Story-telling events typically have little-to-no fighting of any kind, and are usually used to tell a story leading up to or concluding an epic quest.

    Epic quests often run several hours and boast a slew of actor NPCs that both tell a story and engage in combat at some point during the quest.

    In addition to these, there are also “Invasions” which are more static in nature and deal with a temporary data reset of the zone. Examples of this have been the period undead invasions of Qeynos Hills, or the recent one in Najena that was launched with a small epic quest.

    We realize and agree that there have been problems publicizing the story behind dynamic events, and will be launching a new section of our website in the next couple of days to deal with that issue. However, the question I would like to pose is, what do you want to see in the way that dynamic events are handled? It seems that no matter the event, or the story behind it, there are always some people who felt that the event was nothing but a “killing spree”.

    What can we do to make these events more enjoyable for the players?

    -Gordon

  • How do you pronounce Kunark?

  • European Players & Overcrowding on Karana
    European players do not measurably contribute to server crowding at peak times due to the fact that their prime-time is different from that in the states. I'd attribute crowding on Karana to be more due to the fact that most really don't want to leave. The last split offered to Karana was prepared to move 1200 people, yet only 139 actually requested a move.

    I'm not really sure how to go about splitting up the server further when the community has become so strong that no one wants to leave. We're willing to discuss suggestions of course.

    First, CS Reps do not have access to account passwords.

    Second, I'm not sure why you see this as an age discrimination issue. In situation number 1, if the person proved that he bought the accounts, they would then be banned. In situation number 2, we get people calling us all of the time telling us that their account was stolen. They are required to prove ownership to get the account back. If it turns out that they did sell it, and the buyer calls us up, it becomes issue #1, and the account would be banned. In neither case does age come into the equation.

    Original owners are always required to prove ownership, and are given the account back if they can (via a password reset). Buyers are also required to prove that they bought it, however once they do the account it banned.

    We do not allow transfers of accounts, and will not support it nor open ourselves to getting involved with the fraud associated with them.

  • Problems With EQ: Crashing

  • Don't Roll For Loot!
    I'd have to agree with the original posters feelings, though somewhat more politely :) I'd never consider rolling for something that my character could not use if there was someone in the group who could .

  • Reducing Player Burnout
    I believe that the requirement to look at the spellbook goes away at 32 rather than 35. That said, arguments could always be made to have it go away sooner. It's a matter of providing rewards for continuing to gain levels. People gain levels for those rewards, and this is yet another goal that people can reach. We could lower it to 30, but I imagine that we'd see the same requests to lower it to 28, 25, or get rid of it altogether. In this case we've decided that 32 is a good level for that requirment to be overcome.

  • Enchanters Without Breeze

We did, however, decide that the spell would not be NODROP, thus allowing someone to carry them back to the mainland and sell them there for a profit. In my opinion, it has worked out pretty well in implementation.


Big News For Rangers
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:39 PM PDT
The Ranger class will be upgraded says EverQuest Producer Brad McQuaid. Here's the formal announcement from the Gameplay Board:

    After a LOT of internal testing, interacting with the community, and major debate, we've decided to upgrade Rangers as follows:

    We will be upping their dodge and parry to near Warrior levels. I don't want to get into specifics yet because we are still fine tuning, but the end result is that a Ranger should be significantly more survivable.

    I'm personally happy with the solution because, rather than just increasing defense and making them more Warrior-like, we've upped skills that are more traditionally associated with Rangers (the view that they are nimble, chain-wearing fighters who often avoid attacks).

    I'd also like to pre-announce a future goal and policy change:

    Soon (we'll announce a date in the future), we're going to completely stop adjusting game balance as it relates to tweaking classes. Recently, we've made more changes than usual due to the level limits being raised from 50 to 60. Soon, however, we're going to come to a point where it stops.

Our network affiliate PC.IGN has published a new preview of the Scars of Velious. The article comes complete with several screenshots and concept artwork sketchings. Here's an excerpt:

    Unlike Ruins of Kunark, Scars of Velious will, for the most part, be a land upgrade. There won't be any new races or classes added to the game...just more landmass. The Scars expansion will feature a new continent that will increase the playing field of the game with 16 new zones suitable for players level 30 and up to explore and hunt. "Why 30 and above?," you may ask. Well, according to producer Jeff Butler, the vast majority of EQ's player base falls between 30th and 40th level, so this expansion gives them a fresh continent to explore as well as giving newbies something to strive for.

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:32 PM PDT
Since the first infobit post today, Gordon Wrinn has made the following new posts:

  • Melee Tests
    [Quote from original post: Would you please make a statement about the defense tests you are/were conducting for melee classes.]

    Jeff Butler is personally investigating the situation by running those tests, and to my knowledge no final determination has been made yet. Jeff just came back from Europe where he attended ECTS (The European E3). This is slow going as we certainly don't have any knee-jerk reactions, so I appreciate the patience.

  • Bad Names Ignored?
    [Quote from original post: Why are silly names no longer being changed in EQ? I remember hearing that when EQ was to come out on the market character names would have to be in theme. I see names everyday that are not.]

    Names are being changed when they violate the naming policy. Unforunately "silly" is subjective. Of the name examples you mentioned, only one would be immediately ruled inappropriate. "Twomoons" would only create a problem if one tried to get a last name of "Overmiami" or something of that nature. "Physikal" would only be a problem if the last name was something along the lines of "Attraction".

    That said, a name has to be noticed and escalated when a GM or Senior Guide is online to get it changed. The obvious exception to this is if a name is horribly vulgar.

  • Guide Banning Closed For Discussion
    As a persons account status is a private matter between them and us, even if they choose to publicize it, we won't be discussing the matter further on the boards. As it is a privacy issue, posts in regards to that subject will be deleted. Please do not open additional threads on the subject.

  • Posting on the Official Forums
    We're quite pleased with the direction that the board has taken since we implemented the changes only allowing active EverQuest account holders the ability to post. It's caused the overall tone here to change greatly for the better, allowing us to raise the quality and quantity of our communication with the customers.

    That said, there's been some slippage over the past day or so, leading to 15 account warnings, 4 suspensions, and 2 bannings for posts made in the past 24 hours. There is still too much baseless flaming, personal attacks, insults, and general discourtesy going on. We’d like everyone to feel free to discuss opinions and issues, however there is no opinion worth stating that must contain discourteous content in order for the point to be made. People who cannot post without engaging in such discourse, who also want to remain EverQuest subscribers, should not do so on these boards.

    I want to reiterate our commitment to maintain these boards as a viable medium for communications with our customers. If they cannot serve that purpose, and if it is not advantageous to have them (both for you and for us), they won’t be something that we’ll continue to have on the official site.

    We appreciate everyone’s cooperation in keeping these forums civilized and respectful.
