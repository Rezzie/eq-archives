Gordon Infobits
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:39 PM PDT
Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits, a compilation of quotes and excerpts made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • 20+ Fallacies I See Every Day
    Wow, thanks for posting this. I've been guilty of a few of these myself from time to time. I hope everyone reads this and keeps it in mind, it should help us communicate and debate more efficiently in the future.

  • Outposts
    In the first line of the quote, I should have said "outposts" (meaning both FV and the evil outpost), not "outpost". The guild masters and bankers were placed in the outposts during beta since the Kunark beta testers were not allowed to leave to go back to their home towns.

    These should have been removed before it went live, but that didn't happen. In any case, as I said before, we probably won't remove them at this point.

  • Experience - Outdoors & Dungeon
    [Quote from original post: There is a multiplier for xp. Outdoor xp is much lower than indoor or dungeon xp]

    Actually I thought this was fairly well known. I wouldn't say "much lower", but it is lower.

    [Quote from original post: The guards being changed from 6 and 8 minute spawns to 20 minute spawns is purly in the interest of Verant wanting people to team in dungeons and play their game the way they see it. This nurf has nothing to do with newbies or protecting younger players]

    I never said that the spawn time change had anything to do with newbies. That's what the gate-guard change is for. The spawntimes were changed to be based upon level rather than a default 6-8 minute timer because inner cities were more attractive for hunting than dungeons were.

    We don't mind if you hunt in the cities, but it shouldn’t be more lucrative than hunting in a dungeon.

  • Changing Titles
    [Quote from original post: An enchanter gets to go through Illusionist (which isn't that bad, except we don't get any illusions after lvl 44), Beguiler (which is something we do, but sounds real stupid), and finally to spend the rest of our time in eq at lvl 60 as a Phantasmist (this title is neither pleasant nor befitting the class at all).]

    We actually though Phantasmist was one of the coolest level 60 titles out there.

    [Quote from original post: Is there a chance that the Titles for 51+ can be changed?]

    We're considering making some gender-specific changes, but that's about the extent of it. This is one of those subjects that is very subjective, and I doubt that we can give everyone exactly what they are looking for.

  • Evolving Factions
    [Quote from original post: Could it ever be the case that factions change? Couldn't it stand to reason, that at some point, the dark elves would form an alliance with the Iksar. Or, even better, how about a quest, which, when completed, allows faction alignments to be dynamically altered. Factions are fine, but for them to NEVER change is just not realistic.]

    We have the ability to do this, but the door would swing both ways and people get very upset when they become KOS in an area that they used to be able to go through unmolested. As a result, we've left the global faction issues the same in most applications.

    The closest thing we've done to changing something in this fashion was the update to Kithicor.

    [Quote from original post: Also, When a god such as Cazzic or Inorruuk is killed, shouldn't those characters that worship him suffer in some way-such as a innate decrease to magical resistance, or stats, etc. Shouldn't there be vast worldwide consequences when a god dies?]

    Possibly, but before we could do that we'd probably have to put up a "Plane of Love" or something along those lines to give people another option.

    Also keep in mind that Innoruuk is the god of Hate. What better way for a follower of hate to prove himself than to slay his own god? :)

  • Does a NPC being immune to magic also make them immune to rogue poison?
    Yes it would. It would also make them immune to melee attacks that empart a magical consequence. For instance, "Instill Doubt" might land, but the "fear" part of it would be resisted.

  • Immune to Magic
    [Quote from original post: I would like to know what exactly Abashi meant when he said they were making the newbie guards "Immune to magic". Does this mean they can't be hit by Magic based spells only? Or does this mean that they can not be hit by any spells, i.e. no spells casted on them will take effect?]

    They will be immune to all harmful spells. Another passing NPC could certainly cast buffs on them though.

  • Group Linking & Experience Sharing Not Impossible!
    I didn't say that it wasn't possible, just that it wasn't feasible. Given that there is no way that we can implement every suggestion that is made, and given that we have to prioritize where we want to concentrate our efforts, sometimes it makes more sense for us to concentrate our efforts in other areas, such as adding content/quests/etc., to the game. This is espicially true when the effort required to implement a particular suggestion is in excess of the benefit that it would bring.

    We don't want to link groups for the reasons above. The amount of effort that it would require to make that change, including redesigning the entire grouping engine, experience allocation routines, and accounting for every permutation of things that can happen in relation to the group is time that we would rather spend doing something that would affect more people than this will. The entire game is built around groups of six, with very few encounters requiring larger groups. Furthermore, the fact that any shared experience at the level that people typically attack things that require more than one group would require dozens of slayings of those particular encounters in order to move the experience bar with a group size of say 12. The benefit for doing this is much less than the effort that would be involved to implement the suggestion.

    [Quote from original post: Abashi in 19 of 20 posts i can read that you have decided this or that without ever asking any player out there ]

    People post their opinion on this board. For cases where we are willing to consider doing something based upon player input, we frequently ask for specific opinions here on the board. Quite often, if it will enhance gameplay, and we've decided to do it, we'll just wait until it's implemented before announcing it. For example, the change to meditation so higher level magic users don't have to look at their spell books any longer was a good example of this. Messages to the caster that indicate spells wearing off was another.

    However, there are suggestions that are made that we have no intention of implementing. This does not mean that the suggestion isn't any good, quite frankly they are often very good. However given that resources are finite, and that the core engine of the game is "done", there's really no point to solicit specific opinion in these cases.

    Please, by all means continue to make suggestions. If its a good idea, and it's something that we can reasonably plan on and put in EQ, when we can seriously consider it. If it's a good suggestion but there is no way that it will work for EQ, we'll keep it in mind for future products that we develop.

  • Enchanting Items Issue/Question
    [Quote from original post: So in essence if I am reading this right Enchanters will NEVER be able to enchant items to proc a level spell etc. So are you going to let us do ANYTHING to items or is Verant planning to never give Enchanters the promised ability of item enchantment?]

    It's certainly possible that we could enhance trade-skills to make use of enchanted metal or the vials of mana (from the thicken series) like Wu's Fighting Gauntlets, however the ability to take a mundane item and magically imbue it is not planned at present.

  • Pet Request
    [Quote from original post: It would remove lots of guesswork on a pet's level. It would be most helpful when you're facing a 'high risk' creature such that a good quality pet will give you a definite edge and there is no 'greenie' creature for you to determine the quality of the pet.]

    There's supposed to be some guess work involved. That's why the summoned pets have a level range that they can be. If we wanted everyone to be able to determine with certainty what level their pet is, we could just double the mana requirement and have it summon the best pet every time.

  • Experience Penalty
    I've answered a large number of posts on this subject. It's a revolving topic, much like melee binding, that comes up about once a month or so.

    Some classes require more experience than other classes to level, just as some races require more experience than others. There are a few reasons why this is done, but it basically comes down to a way to discourage everyone from playing the same race/class combo.

    Experience requirements are what they are, and are not subject to post-production changes.

  • Spell Fizzling
    [Quote from original post: Hi Abashi or whomever at Verant can find the time to help me out with a question.I am a level 52 cleric with every single skill maxed out. But all of the new spells fizzle much more than it seems they should. Remedy is finally fizzling less after I hit level 52, but it still fizzles more than seems appropriate.]

    The spells aren't fizzling any more than the level 49 spells did on the level you got them. On the level that you get any particular spell, your chance to fizzle the spell is pretty high. That chance decreases dramatically, but never goes away entirely, as you level.

Gordon on Melee Binding
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:25 PM PDT
Gordon gave what is presumablely the last word in the "melee-paying-an-npc-for-a-bind" issue. Here's the word from the Gameplay Board:

    I'm sorry guys, but the binding issue is absolutely a dead horse, which is why I'm not jumping in the threads to remind everyone about that more than once a month or so.

    The game has been out for over a year. In that time, melee characters have survived and prospered, even though the binding arrangements haven't changed. Melee characters will continue to thrive without implementing something such as this that would drastically change the pace of the game, game balance, class interdependency, and the penalty for death.

    Binding is an advantage of being a pure caster in the same fashion that the ability to dish out melee damage, wear protective armor, etc., is an advantage of being a melee player. My advice to you is to make friends with clerics, wizards, druids, and anyone else who can make your travel go quicker.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:44 PM PDT
Here's another round of posts and excerpts by Gordon Wrinn taken from the Gameplay Board:

  • No More SoulFire?
    Lucan could be killed before the change without killing the gate guards, you'll still be able to do it. Guards that are meant to be killed for the purpose of a quest haven't been changed.

  • Spells & Drop Rates
    [Quote from original post: Please increase the drop rate because of the 170 NPCs you claim CAN drop the spell, it seems that none of them are doing so.]

    Yes, I said that over 170 NPCs have the ability to drop the spell, but I didn't say that people were killing the correct 170 NPCs :)

  • Client Issue
    [Quote from original post: I've tried to figure out the timing--ie if weapon delay is 40, and bow delay is 45, I give it extra time before using the bow after turning off attack Still, though, I've found that I have to wait at LEAST one full recycle before I can use the bow again--even if the bow has a faster delay than my weapon! (ie, a Brutechopper with a 1-cam darkwood.)]

    Interesting problem. It sounds like a client issue. I'll mention it to the client guy when I see him.

  • Grouping and Taking Down Creatures
    There aren't many creatures out there that require more than one group to kill.

    Even with those that exist, there's not really enough of them to make a noticeable impact on the experience bars of the people who do get experience. The experience from one (or even 10 kills) at the level that you are hunting critters that require more than 6 people really isn't going to make much of a difference.

    People hunt things that require more than 6 people to kill for the excitement and the loot, not really for the experience. I guess the point I am making is that we are probably better off focusing our efforts on developing new content and addressing some outstanding problems rather than allocating resources to implement something that will only affect a small group of players, and even then, only affect them marginally.

  • Foraging
    [Quote from original post: Normally I would agree that "you gotta look around" however since I can forage rabbit on the boat in the middle of OoT I should be able to find berries while sitting on my butt.]

    ACK! You've found the dreaded aqua bunnies! Seriously though, forage is intended to be a bit of an active skill, so you have to be standing :)

    [Quote from original post: Besides who's to say I didn't look around to find a berry bush to sit next to?]

    Well you couldn't have found the bush without foraging, but once you've foraged then there's no more berries on the bush for you to pick while sitting :)

  • Spell Scrolls
    [Quote from original post: Ok the real point of this topic. If at level 12 a magic user could scribe a bind scroll he could sell it to warriors so they could bind themself in town only. And could only carry one because of it being lore. This would still promote the interaction with charecters, but one take some of the hassle out of getting a bind.]

    Spell scrolls don't work this way. What you are basically describing is how a potion works, and potions can only be made via the Alchemy skill. Though it may not contain all of the potions that people wish for, it's the only way expendable spell-casting items can be created.

    Since we don't allow anyone to create anything of value without some effort, opening some way for magic users to create expendable spell-casting items would basically have it working in the same fashion as alchemy, a skill that we want to keep as a benefit only for Shaman.

PvP Guide Research
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 7:32 PM PDT
From Zifeer:

Help wanted!

I was browsing through the http://www.RallosZek.net OOC board, over the last couple of pages and it occurred to me that although lots of folks regularly discuss PvP Tactics at great length, no one had ever sat down and tried to write some sort of definitive PvP Tactics Guide.

So I thought maybe this would make a useful resource, both for newer players to PvP, and for more experienced folks wanting to start new characters with unfamiliar classes.

Now I'd be the first to admit, I suck at PvP. Plus, I only have a basic familiarity with a few classes and how they work. So what I'd really like, is for those of you out there who know which end of the sword goes in the enemy, to lend me a hand.

I'm looking for maybe three or four smallish (Around or under 500 words) guides to PvP combat, for each class. (Warrior, Druid, Shadowknight, etc) These should include tips and tactics on how each should best deal with the various other types of opponent (Healer, Tank, Caster, etc) and how to cope in a variety of situations, (Ambush(Either end of), Duel, 1 on 1, 1 on 2 or Pets, group PvP, etc)

I'm not so much interested in what Verant should do for the Warrior or the Wizard so much, but more how best to use what each class has as things are now. I'd ideally like something to cover different level ranges too; I imagine a level nine Monk fights quite differently to a level forty-nine Monk. Some variety here would be useful too, allowing for a Beginners Guide and a Masterclass for each, perhaps.

Group Experience
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:10 PM PDT
Experience in groups was the subject of a recent response Gordon Wrinn posted on the Gameplay Board. Here's the post:

    There seem to be a couple of issues here in the thread, the first being linking groups for shared experience, and the second being linking groups for enhanced communication. In both of these cases, we've weighed the various pros and cons of each situation and decided against them.
    Linking groups for shared experience isn't feasible from a technical standpoint. The code that determines how experience is split up, and the group tracking mechanisms in game are part of the central core of EverQuest, something that we would rather leave intact and instead focus our time on adding new content to the game.

    Shared communication has been ruled out both for that reason (though communication would probably be easier), and to increase the challenge of encounters that require multiple groups. One thing that is difficult about these encounters is that communication is difficult, where it should be. You have 30 people in a room all going after the same critter, and it takes real skill to keep everything sorted out, even with everything filtered out via the chat filters.

    -Gordon

Brad on Clipping Plane
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:43 PM PDT
Brad posted to the Gameplay Board giving us an update to the Plane Clipping problem that's been around for a while. Here's his post:

    We're still trying to isolate the problem.
    We've not been able to reproduce it in-house.

    We are trying to see if it's related to certain video cards, but have yet to see a pattern.

    Here's how the clipping plane works in EQ:

    Each zone has information about it in a 'Zone Header' -- this includes the type of zone, the name of the zone, the chance of rain/snow, etc. It also includes a min and max view distance (clip distance).

    Your clipping plane slider bar works on a percentage basis. So, let's say you have it set in the middle, approximately at 50%. One zone might have a min of 500 and a max of 1000 feet viewable distance. In that zone, you'd see 750 feet. Another zone, however, might have a min of 500 and a max of 1500, in which case you'd be seeing 1000 feet unless you adjusted the slider.

    There are two factors related to how we set the maximum distance: 1. ambiance and 2. how the zone was created. Some zones, for example fog enshrouded forests, have a max clip purposely set lower for effect. Most zones, however, have the max very close to the drawing distance set by the zone creator when he exported the zone. In other words, if we set it any higher you'd see visual anomolies or simply not see the world at all.

    In any case, you should see some change when you adjust your slider. The more variance between the min and max, the more variance you'll see.

    We will continue to try to isolate why there are some reports that the final 25% to 50% of moving the slider does nothing.

    -Brad
