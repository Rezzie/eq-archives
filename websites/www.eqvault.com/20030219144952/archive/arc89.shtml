Gordon Infobits
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:32 PM PDT
Gordon has been making his normal rounds on the Gameplay Board this afternoon. Here's a rundown of all of today's posts:

  • More Deletions on Offical Boards
    [Quote from original post: I, for the life of me, can't see how "board" posts, have ANY THING to do with "game conduct" though]

    The ability to post on these boards is one of the privileges that go with having an EverQuest account.

    [Quote from original post: If you conduct yourself poorly here, why allow you into the game where you will conduct yourself poorly infront of even more? Flaming and name callers here arent welcome here nor ingame. Seems fine to me.]

    That's basically where we are coming from.


  • How is Damage calculated?
    [Quote from original post: 1. We know each weapon has has a damage and a dely
    2. But we also know there is a level bonus that affects each hit.
    3. We also know that str makes max damage go higher and possibly make you hit for max damage more often.
    What we dont know is exactly how these fit together.]

    That is as intended. As I've said before, we're not going to publish the formulae used to determine what happens in combat. If you need to know more, you'll have to extrapolate from the data that you do have.

    [Quote from original post: I think casters would be shocked if their 1600 hp nuke actually only landed for 800 and cost twice as much mana]

    Casters can see how much DD spells hit for per hit, in the same fashion that melee players can see how much their sword hits for on each hit. Information is given equally in that respect. If the casters however wanted to see the formulae that determines things such as fizzle and resist rates, we'd have to decline to give that info out as well.

    DOTs are even more mysterious than weapons or DDs. In fact, the only reason people have a good idea of the way that most of these work is due to the parsing of the spell data file. This is obviously something that we didn't intend. Since that information is gathered via unauthorized means, we're not going to extend that out to a purposeful action such as handing out formulae.

    [Quote from original post: Weve come a long way from damage/delay, but some exact formulas for calculating damage would be appreciated.]

    I'm wondering if this is leading to something else, as I know you've been here on the boards long enough to know what I'd say to this request :)



  • Retroactive Nerfs
    [Quote from original post: Are Retroactive nerfs EVER justified?
    I used to agree with the idea of using nerfs to maintain balance in the game....but lately they seem to do more harm than good, (i.e. moss-covered twig / manastone / rubicite issues / Jboots) ]

    Rubicite and Jboots were not changed retroactively. The manastone only had a small part of it changed retroactively (see below).

    [Quote from original post: Heh heh....Verant seems to like retroactive nerfs....]

    Actually we really dislike doing them, however sometimes an item is so unbalancing that it's necessary. Here's some examples of where we haven't done a retroactive change:

    Manastone (One component was retroactive with this, it being disallowed in the Planes)
    Box of Abu'kar (This one started as a retroactive change that was then changed back to the way it was)
    Guise of the Deceiver
    Lusterous Russet Armor (We announced an intended retroactive change, then changed our minds)
    Rubicite

    Likewise, there are cases where a retroactive change was necessary.

    [Quote from original post: I'm starting to think that VI should just leave whatever mistakes they made in the game and end the drops if necessary. It just seems like something is innately wrong with retroactive changes, and they usually come much to the chagrin of the players.

    What do you guys think? For better or worse, I'm of the belief that the policy should be no more retroactive nerfs]

    I think that you'll find people on both sides of the issue. Many felt that the Manastone and Guise should have been retroactively removed. Many feel that the twig and other low-delay weapons shouldn't be retroactively changed. I guess it depends on whether or not you are going to be a recipient of a retroactive change.


  • An Explanation
    [Quote from original post: So agian I was just wondering what is the different between the song and this.]

    The difference is that the NPC is moving. It is carrying out its commands and making progress towards that goal. Movement introduces a variable that injects some risk into the situation. Now where these particular people were fear-kiting, perhaps there is a pathing issue that reduces the risk, but that's something associated with a specific point in the world, not an ability that can be taken anywhere.

    The "Fear" spell tells the NPC to flee. It's the same "flee" that they do when they run low on health and try to get away. They can't really *get* anywhere, so the only reason they flee is to inject additional risk.

    An NPC that is fleeing, and is then rooted, will turn around and fight. An NPC that is fleeing, and then snared, will not turn around and fight, even if their movement rate is reduced to zero (an effective root). "Effective Roots" don't have the drawbacks of a root (e.g. fighting NPC, unpredictable breaking of the spell).

    Our choices in this case were: Make it so all NPCs will fight back if their movement speed is reduced to a certain point, or, limit the ability of snare spells to reduce their movement rate to zero. We did the latter. I'm sure that some could make a case for the former based upon situation.


  • We Need More Abashi's!
    [Quote from original post: Anyways do you think with all that EQ income that the players generate you could hire some EQ know it alls to respond to more posts??]

    It's not so much answering questions about how things work in EQ as it is understanding why things were set up as they are in the first place. It's my hope that some day I'll be able to convince everyone of our goals and get complete buy-in. I know that's not really possible, but I can hope :)

    [Quote from original post: Ive posted many times here, sometimes griping, sometimes praising, and sometimes role playing and my post has never been answered by you or any staff, it kinda makes me feel very small and unimportant, especially when i put alot of time and thought in a new idea or gripe or whatever.]

    The board gets 400-500 new threads per day with about 10 times that many posts. I think that the point that we can start answering everyone's posts will be about the same time that EQ has lost popularity. Until then we'll try to address issues if not individual posts.

    Thanks for posting :)


  • Autosplit Problems
    [Quote from original post: 2) convert P to G and resplit (what they obviously don't want to do);]

    Autosplit used to work like this (beta), and people *hated* it. They always ended up with far too many of a lesser valued coin. Not to mention, it doesn't make sense that change can be made out in the wilderness.

    [Quote from original post: 3) remove "feature" (what they should do).]

    Why remove a feature that people can just choose not to use? Personally, I've never had a problem with autosplit, as it works out in the long run.

    Things you can do:
    -Master loot. Have one guy loot everything with autosplit off. Split after you all are done adventuring.
    -Alpha loot. Take turns looting with autosplit on. Everything will even out in the long run.
    -Alpha loot with split off.

    -or.. Just don't worry about it. A coin here or there isnt' really that big of a deal, and if it is for you, you can still choose not to use autosplit.


  • Anti-Roleplaying Server Request
    [Quote from original post: My server is tyring to change to all RPing. Well i dont agree with this. i like to powergame and not thing.
    anyway i personally dont like to RP. i RP myself and enjoy it sometimes. but i feel out of place sometimes
    Anyway. since my server is one of the newest two. (morell thule, torvantilus (sp)) i was curious if it gets to bad if i could switch to a more powergaming server like xegony. NOOO im not going to start over and play the next 5 months getting to where i am. i have 43 levels under my belt. if im not happy its not that hard to do. i mean the internal staff does it all the time.]

    Honestly this is one of the most unusual requests I've seen.

    Frankly, we move characters during sanctioned server-moves. I don't think that we would want to expand our current moves to include those that want to move because there is "too much roleplaying" on their current server.

    [Quote from original post: "I assume you are speaking of the low-delay weapons? Items attainable in the mid 30s should not be the preferred item at 60."]

    Oops. I meant weapons, not items in general. Many items have varying degrees of usefulness throughout the life of the character.

    [Quote from original post: ENTIRE shiverback debate was due to this exact fact]

    I'll agree that Shiverback isn't as good as some of the other more mundane stuff that is out there. However, we're all but certain here that the problem is not necessarily with Shiverback being poor, but with some of the mundane stuff being too good.

    Jeff Butler is running some extensive inter-class defense tests, and the initial indication is that any defensive increase (including AC) for monks would not be appropriate.

  • Thousands of Twigs/Whips?

    [Quote from original post: Ive read a lot of the recent discussion about camping and item drops and I wanted to propose an idea I have had for awhile now. The proposition is to have in game quests which follow that same layout as current quests do but instead of being constant, they can only be completed one time only ]

    I assume you mean once per server rather than once per player. That's probably not something that we would do. We'd rather that one-time quests be handled on a dynamic basis, with actors playing the NPCs present in most of the quest. They involve many people overall, trying to get the biggest "bang for the buck".

    It wouldn't be a very good use of time to have several game designers spending a week or more of their time to input a quest that operates in the standard way, only to have it enjoyed by 3 dozen people.

    [Quote from original post: Every patch seems to either make dying easier or gaining exp harder with a net result that it takes a very long time to gain 1 bubble of exp never mind 1 whole level!]

These same people spend 12 hours per day in game doing what they come here and complain is boring. We're just trying to remove what is being cited as boring.     [Quote from original post: Why do you want us to all die Abashi (Verant)?]

    The game should be fun. One of the ways that the game provides fun is to provide challenges for you to overcome. Challenges require risk to be meaningful. The risk in this case is the chance of failure. The penalty for that being death.

    If there was no death then there would be no risk which means that there would be no challenge and thus no fun. The FAQ explains this in more detail.

  • Why Ban Players
    [Quote from original post: Why not "Exile" their accounts to a "bad boy" server. Disable their account from access to anyother server, and transfer their chars to the new server.]

    A server really has to have a minimum number of people on it before it breaks even in terms of depreciation and operations. Certainly we'd save some money from an operations standpoint by not having to provide GM support on a server of this nature, however it would have taken so long to populate the server that it would never have been profitable.
    --

    It might sound strange, but 90% of GM and Guide time is taken up by 10% of the playerbase. It's the same 10% daily. The real problem here is that when someone in the other 90% needs help, they are often in line behind several people of the 10%.

    That's why we've posted from time to time that certain issues shouldn't be petitioned while in game.

  • Guild Logo
    Though this is a great idea that we'll certainly keep in mind for the future, it's not something that we would see in EQ for technical reasons. This is explained pretty well in the FAQ.
