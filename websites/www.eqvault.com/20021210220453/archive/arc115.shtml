Rumors of Nyplid's Disappearance - Fennin Ro
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 5:50 PM PDT
Azarr Windtree brings us the following announcement:

Hail and Well meet fellow Norrathians,

Allow me to introduce myself I am Azarr Windtree Mage of the Mysts and
member of the Great Atani Guild. I come here to inform you all of a great
saga just begone. The saga began one night hence as I traveled through West
Freeport, I happened apon a small band of my fellow Atani at the Theatre who
sat quitely listening to a man named Hicor, so being the curious mage I am,
I to stopped to listen to the Hicor's tale. He spoke of his friend Nyplid,
who on the very day of his wedding had been manhandled and spirited away.
Nicor told of how he had investigated Nyplid's disapearence. His
investigation had of course begone were Nyplid had last been seen and soon
came to believe that the dark races of Norrath may of had something to do
with Nyplid's disapearence. Though he found no strong clues he did find a
note posted in one of the local taverns that spoke of a Shadow from another
realm. The Shadow could not come into our realm nor could he take a person
from this realm to his. Yet the Shadow had followers here in Norrath, ones
of the dark races. Two of which the names are known, Drodak a Troll and
Androdameia a Shdowknight. Turning up know other clues, Nicor decided to
investagate the sewers of Freeport as it is rumored that the dark races
inhabit them at times. Nicor said he began his search in the sewers and came
apon a Darkelf, but though questions many times the Darkelf only ignored
Nicor. After more investigation of the sewers he had come apon a piece of
tatered cloth, the cloth had strange symbols as some sort of writing. He
himself could not translate it to the fullest yet know that the cloth had
something to do with the Shadow.

We had all sat quitely listening to the occurance of poor Nyplid's
kidnapping and of the search for him when Hicor asked that who among us
would help to locate Nyplid and return him safely home. Out of the Atani
that were assembled there four responed I being one of them. The others were
Granaut A dwarven cleric who liked his ale, Nacacia a halfelf rogue, whos
bueaty hides her deadly skills, and lastly Lowelf a druid, a blessing to
know. After the four agreed to help, Hicor handed me the tatered cloth and
told us to seek out a friend in Felwithe named Delcaraa, but that she might
be hard to see as her mother was sick and that she had left her post as
Cleric for the fireworks company Kaboom to tend to her, but we might seek
out Cahboom who also worked for the comapny and he might aid us to find
Delacaraa. Hicor did warn us not to let Cahboom see the Cloth as he would
destroy it, why I am not sure. Hicor then left after thanking us for the
help we offered. The four of us sat a moment and talking about what to do I,
Nacacia and Granaut decided to set sail on the next boat that left for
Faydwer. Lowelf decided he would go alone, as most druid seem to be loners
most of the time, but he would keep us informed if he found out anything. We
arrived shortly at Faydwer and headed to Felwithe, at the great wizard
spires we meet the Gnome Kahboom who was interested in hiring our dwarven
cleric friend as a replacement for Delcaraa until she could return to work,
of which Granaut agreed, "so if by chance you are in Faydwer and here a loud
boom know that Dwarven ale and fireworks do not mix well." After talking to
Cahboom he agreed to lead us to were Delcaraa lived, apon ariving and
introducing our selves she asked that the others leave and of course I asked
that Cahboom leave as well so that we could talk in private, but unknown to
Delcaraa, Nacacia had used your stealth to sneak back in. I then took out
the tatered cloth from my backpack and showed it to Delacaraa, she stood
back in amazment. Asked if she might have the cloth to investigate it. I
agreed, she took the cloth and said that it would take at least a week befor
she could tell me more of what it was and perhaps how it fit into Nyplids
disapearence. We left her to her studies "I was thankful to get rid of that
piece of cloth if made me fell very uneasy holding on to it."

Knowing how well my drawven friend likes his ale we decided to head to the
local tavern in Felwithe for a few rounds and some discussion of the events.
We all drank to the day and to the hopefully safe return of Nyplid, not
knowing what the future would hold. We all agreed on meeting one week hence
to find out what Delacaraa had to say. I look forward to what lies ahead.

Azarr Windtree
Mage of the Mysts Azarr Windtree
Atani - http://www.atani.com

Rogue Weapon Bug
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:50 PM PDT
Gordon replied to the following post on the Gameplay Board regarding a bug with one of the Rogue's new weapons:

    I finally got what turns out to be an extremly nice rogue dagger (locustous *spelling sorry*) 8 damage 17 dealy with Effect: walking sleep (level 5ish shaman). Now I was thrilled that my friend actually got this for me. I couldnt wait to try it out. OMG...... I was imediately told to put it away by the shaman and enchanter in my group. It cancles there high level tugurs/slow spells. Since when can a level 5 spell cancle a level 40+ or higher spell out. So basially since most groups i get in either have a shaman or enchanter in them ill never be able to use this weapon. Well there is the off chance that I might just go out soloing with it (not). Please if you could either put a different effect on this weapon, take the effect away, or make it so the effect doesnt write over the higher level spells.

    We've acknowleded the bug. The spell supercedence rules try to figure out what spell is "better" and causes it to overwrite the inferior spell. It assumes that "higher values" = "better". The attack speed debuffs are the only spell where lesser values in the database yields a better spell.

    It's on our list, but I don't have an ETA.

    -Gordon

Gordon on Usenet
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:58 PM PDT
Gordon made a few posts recently on the Usenet group alt.games.everquest. Here's the first post on EverQuest sales:

    Wow, 300k sold and 200k still playing! That is excellent! Yeah, despite
    all the cursing I've done at Verant they must be doing something right.

Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:17 PM PDT
A few posts were made recently on the Gameplay Board by Verant's Gordon Wrinn. Here's the skinny:

  • Kerra Faction
    [Quote from original post: Befor the Kerra Isle remake when you killed them you got several positive faction bonuses. This allowed a Erudite SK i hade made gather enough faction to walk thry Erudin and make it do the boat. Well they no longer give faction positives!!! so my new Necro cant increase his faction. Please return the faction to where it was before. Thanks!]

    The only positive faction that they gave before was something like "High Guards of Erudin". However, no NPCs were ever assigned this faction, so there wasn't a benefit from building it up. I'm not sure how your SK got to the point that he could walk through Erudin, but there are other means of raising faction with the townfolk.

  • Shacke of Dust & Corpse Decaying
    These chain-quests are designed to reward those who protected the item from inception and through each step of the quest. I would recommend that people put the shackle in the bank until they get to the point that their corpse stays around a while.

  • Kunark Sound Issues
    [Quote from original post: Just a quick question: When are the sound issues on Kunark going to be addressed? I'm sure I'm nto the only one who's sick of hitting just about anything and having it grunt like a human...]

    They've been addressed in code already, we just need to get them over the live servers. It's looking like the week after next for the next patch.

  • Gameplay Board Rules
    That type of conversation has no place on these boards, and the moderators will delete them as we find them. We can certainly ban the station names that post this stuff, of course they'll just be able to create another until we get the code in to only allow EQ subscribers to post here.
    --
    I assume you speak of the post at http://boards.station.sony.com/everquest/Forum4/HTML/039436.html

    It's on page 5 at the time that I'm answering this post. This board uses an index system where the most reacently posted-to thread is moved to the top of the list.

    We don't remove posts just because we don't like the contents. If we did that, I think that the majority of the posts on this board would disappear.

  • Lag Spikes
    I'm sorry that I didn't get back to comment on this issue earlier. We've been looking around for things that could be causing what the players have been pointing out. We did find one issue that could cause something like that for a period of a few seconds, and that was being exacerbated by a network problem on a couple of servers (Quellious and Morrel-Thule) that has been fixed. But we haven't been able to find anything that could cause the 5-20 minute periods of this that some people are claiming.

    I'm willing to keep looking into the issue.

  • Gordon's Customer Relations Skills
    I normally don't like to get involved in this type of thread, but in this case I think I should since the way I phrased the comment can so easily be taken as a flame.

My intention was to try and explain the situation to the person I was replying to to their satisfaction, which wasn't done in the first post of his that I answered in the Kunark Faction thread. More Gordon Bits & Pieces
[15 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:26 PM PDT
The posting machine known as Gordon Wrinn made a few more posts in the last hour. Here's the info ripped straight from the Gameplay Board:

  • More on Solusek Ro Faction
    [Quote from original post: Although the blurb seems promising, the deity related items are presently few and far between. Further, there is a nasty SR faction problem in Kunark. This may be addressed (it should be next patch) but you are likely best off playing the middle and remaining Agnostic]

    The original author mentioned that he was playing a high elf. Just to be clear, the Solusek Ro faction issue at the evil outpost would affect a high elf. He'll be fairly well hated there regardless what deity he chooses. But yes, we will be changing the faction offset for the Dark Elven worshippers of Solusek Ro and Bristlebane in the next patch.

  • Compelling Reasons
    The "compelling reasons" why someone might choose to play one class, and another person a different one differ from person to person. I'll take a stab at how we look at it. Here's an example of two melee classes:

    If a monk had the hitpoints of a warrior, could equip all of their armor, and didn't have the weight restriction, what would be the point of playing a warrior? The monk has a bunch of nifty skills and can do more damage with their bare hands than the warrior can. There's be no compelling reason to play a warrior if the monk could do everything better.

    Now monks and warriors primarily compare damage dealing potential, but you also have to factor in other skills. The monk for instance can feign death. This also contributes to balance, even though it has nothing to do with offensive power.

    Every class has something that they can do better than some other class. That does not necessarily mean that they are the *best* at that particular thing, just that they can do *something* better, and also do something worse. As an example, a cleric can heal better than any other priest class, yet their offensive spells are not as good as the other priest classes.

  • Invoke Fear Bug?
    [Quote from original post: While feared, pet kills her. I receive no XP. There seems to be a problem that when feared, you will not obtain the XP for the kill.]

    This is true, and it's not really a bug per se. When feared you are basically an NPC. When your pet kills her the system looks for where to assign the XP and doesn't find a valid PC. This isn't something that can be changed without making major changes to the core architecture.

    Because we didn't want to put more orders through the system until we were sure all of the issues were worked out.

    [Quote from original post: Could it be that Verant is trying to force the players who decided to wait into the local software stores? Why would they DO such a thing?]

    We wouldn't.

    [Quote from original post: Because the retail ROK cost's $40, (but it comes with a brand new copy of EQ!!!) and Verant and their distributors need to move these things off the shelf]

    I'm very sorry that you are upset about the store being down, but there is no conspiracy. We took the store in order to be sure that we could serve our customers better. The higher price of the ROK retail box has nothing to do with it, we'd rather you buy the expansion directly from us anyway.

    [Quote from original post: Wonder how long the Online Store will be down....]

    I just got an update on it today. Things are starting to look better, but we won't bring up the store until we're sure everything is working ok. As soon as we can give you an ETA we'll post it.

    [Quote from original post: Harvey, the expansion IS a brand-new copy of EQ... you can either use the CD key to enable a new account, Kunark Enabled and all, or you can use that key to upgrade your existing account.]

    Just to clear this up, no it isn't. The key that comes with the expansion can only be used to upgrade an existing account. The key that comes with the retail version can be used either to upgrade or to create a new account.

  • Balance Not About Equality
    Though I do thank your for taking the time to post your opinion, balance is not about equality. It's not about making everyone able to do the same thing in the same time with the same amount of risk.

    The equality that you suggest is incompatible with the concept of a class-based system with class-interdependence. There are a few games out there that support class-less system which you may find more to your liking.

  • Pick Pocketing Questions Answered
    [Quote from original post: Just wondering if you can pickpocket something that someone else is fighting?]

    Yes.

    [Quote from original post: And if you can would that not be about a the same as Ninja looting, except you do it before the creature is dead.]

    Not really, the ninjalooting restriction primarily applies to special items. Since rogues can't PP anything but coin and the odd non-magical item, it's not really a major concern.

    [Quote from original post: Oh one more thing is thier any way to tell if a rogue comes up and steals all the loot off of what your fighting... that is if he can?]

    If the critter scolds him perhaps :)

  • Equipping Weapons on an NPC
    [Quote from original post: If you're going to nerf us, nerf us all the way.

    Before the delay necromancer pet nerf, giving a pet a halberd, and then giving them a dagger would make the pet equip the dagger rather than the halberd.

    This was because the pets preferred lower delay.

    Now that their delay has a minimum of 30, they still seem to prefer low delay weapons. In other words, giving the pet a short sword, then giving the pet a halberd, the pet would keep the short sword.

    If youre going to nerf, clip all the attached strings.]

    It's not a bug. All NPCs use the same code to determine whether or not they will equip a weapon and what weapon they will equip. The fact that the lower delay doesn't work for pets doesn't change that. I'd recommend that you don't hand your pet a dagger after you give him the halberd if you want him to wield the halberd.

  • Shaman Spell Question
    [Quote from original post: We get this spell at 29 and it now has a lock so you cant charm anything higher than 26??? PLEASE EXPLAIN HOW THIS CAN BE USEFUL????? AND WHY IT WAS DONE?]

    With levels going above 50, we had to put in code to catch caps that were controlled by the level ceiling before. One of these was a safety net for charm, which caught this spell. Assuming that they don't have it already (my spell list is dated), we'll be giving Shaman the higher level animal charm spell with a higher cap.

    I apologize for this not being in the patch message.

  • Level 51+ buffs do not work on Players under 39?
    One of the limiting factors on what spells could do before was based upon what it would do if used as a twinking mechanism. The level 49 spells put us right up against this ceiling. When we created the new buffs, we decided to put the cut-off. You can still use your level 49 series on people a ways below you in level.

  • Earth Elemental Damage Question
    [Quote from original post: WHY does my level 41 EARTH Elemental HIT HARDER then my level 43 ONE?]

    It's not a bug. There's more to the "power" of a pet than how hard it hits.

  • Play Nice Rules
    There are a number of reasons why someone might choose not to group, and we certainly can't compel anyone to join one. For instance, perhaps this guy doesn't like Dark Elves? If he had been a high elf enchanter, perhaps the claim would make more sense, but it is nonetheless valid. There's also the possibility that you and he had some history making him unwilling to group.

    In any case, it would not be reasonable for us to force people to group with other specific people, especially in the case of a 2 giant spawn, where the ability to split off and take your own spawn is just as viable.
