Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:53 PM PDT
Gordon was once again back on the boards answering questions and talking about many different EverQuest issues. Here's the latest from the Gameplay Board:

  • Buffing Your Allies
    I understand your argument, however the only reason we were able to make the 51+ buffs as powerful as we did was based upon them not being able to be cast upon drastically lower in level. If you are over 51 and you wish to group with someone out of the range for some reason, you can feel free to buff them, however you'll still be limited to the 49 and lower spells.

    Some of the 51+ buffs have serios increases to duration, with some lasting a full 2 game days. That's some serios power, and we don't want it being used to enhance buff-twinking.

  • Is Kunark in retail stores?
    Yes, it is available in the stores. Until we can get the online store back up, that is your best option. Just make sure you pick up the box with the lizard on the front :)

  • Kunark Items
    This is another case where it is not our goal to make everyone the same. We don't want everyone of a given level to be clad in the same equipment. Equipment that is able to be widely used by a large number of race/class combos also encourage camping, something that we would like to see go away.

    Restricting items by race and class works towards this goal.

  • Zone Misconceptions
    [Quote from original post: Lavastorm and Sol A/B have some very small hallways, very poorly designed. Shrink is Verants fix for this, but only shamans have it and it only works in dungeons. So the hallways in Lavastorm really suck.]

    You've got it a bit backwards. The zone was designed specifically to give you a hard time because of your size. That size, and the stats and abilities that come with it, isn't supposed to be without its disadvantages.

    As for the subject of this thread, we did put in a shrink-anywhere potion, yes?

  • Lure Series
    You are missing the elegance of the Lure series.

    The Draught series of spells is about fast-casting good-ratio mid-range damage spells. They let wizards deal damage in smaller chunks making it less likely that the target will want to play a game of eat-the-wizard.

    For the elegance of the Lure series, please allow me to quote a section of your original message:

    [Quote from original post: I've casted it quite a few times now and have cosistently gotten 640 damage]

  • Mana Regen
    The mana-regen rate is constant regardless how much mana you have left when you start medding. I think what you might be observing is an issue with the resolution of the mana bar. For instance, I've always felt that the last 1/2 bubble took longer to med than any of the others.

  • More Iksar/Plate Issues
    [Quote from original post: I was wondering is the inability for Iskar warriors and sk's to wear plate a matter of not having the skins completed yet, or as a further balancing issue? If so how is not letting us wear plate types armor explained?]

    The inability of Iksar to wear plate is in the original design doc for the expansion. It's *not* a matter of skins not being "done", but because that it was specifically planned that way.

    The reason that they cannot wear plate is that it chaffes their scales. But fear not, those same scales also give them an innate AC bonus :)

  • Iksar AC Bonus
    Yes, Iksar have an innate AC bonus based upon race. Monks also get an innate AC bonus based upon class. The Iksar monk gets both the race and class-based AC bonuses.

  • Summoning Mobs
    [Quote from original post: Wouldn't it make sense for mages to be able to summon mobs to them. I think give mages a spell line that allows them to summon(teleport) a mob to the front of them would not take a way from any other class and just make the game better.]

    Doing something like this would have bad effects on the game. It would trivialize cases where creatures are meant to be encountered in groups when it succeeded, and would cause awful trains when it failed.

    Though we apprecaite the suggestion, it really wouldn't work out well for EQ.

  • Fizzling Rates
    There have been no changes to fizzle rates. If you've been following the boards here for a while, you'll notice that this subject pops up about every 3-5 weeks. The last time that I'm aware of fizzle-rate changes taking place was late beta when fizzles were made to happen less.

  • "Get Out of Jail Free" Spells
    Nothing is meant to be a get-out-of-jail-free card. Even "Gate" has the opportunity to fail. Spells can also be resisted. Feign Death failing upon a successful cast is basically the same as a creature resisting your spell.

  • Shaman Spell Bug Report
    I've forwarded this to GZ. I'm not sure what the deal is with them, the one with the higher slowing effect should overwrite the others. It sounds as if there might be a problem with whatever determine whether higher or lower numbers is better.

  • Realism
    Realism must sometimes be bent in the interest of gameplay, balance, and hardware requirements. Not to mention that I think that people might get upset if we got rid of magic, and everything besides lions, wolves, and bears....

    (Say it with me)

    Oh my! :)

  • Balance and Fun
    [Quote from original post: Verant cares more about BALANCE than FUN]

    We don't view these two concepts as mutually exclusive ideas. We instead believe that balance is necessary in order to promote fun, and thus the longevity of the game.

    If all avenues to promote "fun" were pursued without regard to balance, we believe that people would get bored with the game and quit.
    --
    [Quote from original post: How about, If all avenues to promote balance were pursued without regard to fun, I believe that thousands of people who bought this game would quit out of frustration and leave nasty posts about Verant's "vision".]

    Absolutely. The door swings both ways.

    [Quote from original post: Guess what, it's happening.]

    But no, it's not. EQ has consistently gained subscribers every week since it was release.

    One thing that I think most people understand about MMOG's is that people who leave them tend to leave because they are unhappy with something or bored. It happens, but unlike in singleplayer games where people can win them, then put them in the closet never to pick them up again all the while remembering them fondly, it's usually some event that causes people to leave MMOG's. This leads to unhappy people posting on the boards, and would happen regardless what the Vision(tm) was.

    That's not to say that peoples complaints are invalid. They are far from that. Everyone has different reasons for playing these games and everyone has a reason that is at least valid to them when they quit. Though we can't apologize for the Vision(tm) itself, since it is what makes EQ what it is, we definitely apologize to those who left the game due to issues that we can all agree were bad, such as overcrowding or periods of excessive downtime.

  • Kunark Refunds
    The refunds are being processed as quickly as possible and emails are being sent when that occurs. There's a fair amount involved in the whole process, such as verifying that the original order was placed and that it hasn't already been refunded. We appreciate your patience.

  • A Visit To Your Local Dungeon
    Thanks for telling the story. It's nice to read these once in a while, and also nice to hear about people "doing" dungeons.

    The aggro range is what it is due to the design of the tower. It makes the dungeon a little bit easier than we would like, but increasing it causes some really nasty trains.

  • Customer Service Editorial
    I'd like to thank you for writing this wonderful editorial. Both the positive comments and well-deserved criticisms are well received.

  • Why can't NPCs tell us more stories?
    Definitely a good idea. We do believe that the world could use more inert content (content provided for the sake of content). One of the concerns is that people often expect any talking NPC to lead to a quest. In the case that you suggest, the only purpose of the story would be the story itself, and some people might get frustrated by that thinking that they had to search for some keyword to get a quest.

    The EQLive team has come up with some wonderful inert content ideas, and you should start seeing things happen soon.

  • Old Producer Letters
    Links to the old producer letters are supposed to be listed at the bottom of the current one. I just looked at the one we have up now, and this was apparently overlooked. I've asked the web team to pop the links for the old ones on the page. Thanks for letting us know.

  • Artifact Questions
    [Quote from original post: ARTIFACT means that there is only one of these items in the world (server) right? How could an Artifact drop from a MoB that respawns then?]

    The item that was recently found (a paladin sword) was marked as an artifact by mistake. It will be changed the next time we do items.

    [Quote from original post: Can you give ALL No Drop items to NPC's?]

    Yes, but it's meant to be for items associated with a quest.

    [Quote from original post: If so does killing the NPC let you loot it?]

    Nope. This was a nasty little nodrop transfer bug that we stomped sometime back.

    [Quote from original post: Giving NPCs some items does leave that item on their corpse.]

    It used to do this, I don't believe it does any longer. However it's been a while since I handed something to an NPC and later killed it :)

  • Item Tag Listing
    Here's a list of the current item tags as listed in the EverQuest ROK Manual Supplement:

    MAGIC ITEM: This means that the item is imbued with magic. If this item is a weapon, it will have the ability to damage creatures which can only be harmed by magic/

    LORE: This means that you may only have one of these items in your possession at any one time. If you attempt to acquire another, you will not be able to pick the item up. This will check your bank account.

    NODROP: This means that the item cannot be traded to another player nor sold to a merchant. However, you can hand the item to an NPC, which is necessary for many quests.

    ARTIFACT: This means that the item is unique upon the server that you play, and that there will never be another one given out. Artifacts are usually earned through participation in dynamic quests that occur in game regularly. Artifacts however remain so rare that most people may never see one, much less own one, which is why they are called Artifacts of Great Power.

    A Deities name: If a deity’s name is listed upon the item, it means that only followers of that deity may equip the item or use its effects.

  • Firepot Exploiters
    [Quote from original post: To allow these early exploiters to remain bound in the FirePot room is unfair to Wizards, Druids, all melee classes, and every- one who did not receive their Kunark CD in a timely manner. It also sends the message that it is ok to exploit known bugs, as long as you do it quickly.]

    We really don't have the capability to move everyone out of there all at once. For those who actually are bound in the room, I don't think that it will be very long before they change their bind points voluntarily. Also, I really don't think that it is fair to call those that bound there "exploiters". There's no reason to expect them to have known in advance that we didn't intend it to be like that.

  • Guild Tag Loss Issue
    The guild-tag issue has been a very elusive problem, and we've been unable to duplicate it on demand in the test environment. The programmers have been looking for what might be causing it. If anyone has any way to reproduce this problem on demand, we'd love to hear about it. You can email me directly at gwrinn@verant.com

  • Problem With Kunark Zones
    [Quote from original post: There are lines...I can take a step backwards in places in FV and the mob I am fighting toe to toe will wander off(usually by poofing) and usually back to a pile of his friends I step back over the line and wait a few minutes and slam the baddie and eight of his friends attack. I know exactly where this line is. ]

    Sounds like a pathing issue where the creature loses track of the best way to get to you, tries to run to a new patch, which ends up taking them out of pursuit range. The fact that they come back after you just means that you are back in pursuit range and are again a valid target. You don't actually leave the hate list, and aren't meant to. The pathing issues are something that needs to be fixed. If you can get specific LOCs of places where this happens (they turn around and run off), please let me know and I'll get them to the team.

  • Bad Paladin Piercing Weapons
    The item database is huge and complex, containing tens of thousands of items. Mistakes do happen.

    What's the name of the particular piercing item that has paladins listed? Also, if you all want to post items with blank effects, I can get those fixed pretty quickly.

More Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:03 PM PDT
Gordon's been at it again making more posts on the Gameplay Board. Here's the scoop:

  • Simple Fix to the Killstealing Issue?
    [Quote from original post: A very simple way to fix this is increase the spawns. More often than not, I find that the people in my group have full mana and full health. The problem is, that we have nothing to do with this mana and health, because there are never enough mobs to kill.]

    Increasing spawns does not decrease overcrowding, it in fact encourages it. If we keep increasing spawns as more and more people enter the zone, sooner or later things are going to break because there's nothing to encourage people to leave the zone in search of more fruitful hunting ground.

    We don't want to encourage people to jump on a never-ending hack-and-slash conveyor belt, we'd rather encourage them to explore.

  • Finding Spells Beyond 50
    [Quote from original post: I've yet to see anyone casting a spell past 50 they didn't buy at their vendor. In almost 3 weeks of playing, I've yet to hear of a single post 50 spell dropping off a mob.]

    I've got the EQLive team checking on this. I'll get back after I get the report from them. Just one note, Kunark has only been live for a week and a half :)

  • My hard earned faction went from Amiable to Threatening!
    This does indeed sound strange. Are you sure that you haven't done anything (quests, etc.) that would cause something like this? You seem to know your way around factions pretty well but I have to ask.

    Anyone else have anything like this happen recently?

  • No Experience from Blues?
    This doesn't sound intentional. Is there any other information that you can give that can help us track the issue down? Is anyone else having the same problem? It's possible that we might have an unaccounted-for overflow somewhere due to to the size of the numbers that we are working with now. Any additional information would help.

  • Shooting Arrows
    Shots with projectile weapons are allowed or disallowed based upon the delta-Z (Difference in the value of the Z-coordinant) of your feet versus the creatures feet. It was primarily done to disallow some exploits that are possible in the event that there is no control. The downside is that if you are standing on a hill, and for instance a sand giant is at the bottom of a hill, you might not be able to fire at it even if it is at eyelevel with you.

    We could look into increasing the maximum allowable delta-z if that would be helpful.

  • Group EXP Screwy?
    There are some limited cases where something like this can happen due to the way the group aggregate damage system works. Due to the way everything interacts, changing the system further probably isn't feasible.

    On an opinionated note, it does seem like a bit of poetic justice to hear about melee types accidently KS'ing from casters :)

  • Meshing Zones
    [Quote from original post: Are there plans in the works to add more content to the zones other than Kunark to "mesh" the old world with the new? For example, an NPC in Neriak that wants you to get something from a zone in Kunark, or a quest from someone in Overthere Outpost asking for supplies from "home".]

    We are constantly planning an implementing more content, quests, items, etc. There are already many quests in that "link" the two continents. I've heard that there's even an Iksar prisoner in one of the old dungeons :)

  • Feign Death
    Well, I suppose pretending that you are someone else and convincingly pretending you are dead are two different things.

    Really though, we didn't want to hand out Feign Death to everyone, and not everyone can have every skill, even when it's cool and incredibly useful (especially when it's cool and incredibly useful). When we started looking for a way for rogues to reduce their "hate" value, feign death was an option but we thought it would be cheesy to just duplicate another skill. That's how we came up with Evade.

  • Spell Balance
    These spells are balanced. I think that once people have an idea how they work, they'll pretty much agree. Until then however I'll leave you to debate them :)

  • Rules of Conduct
    [Quote from original post: The Basic Rules of Conduct are agreed upon when one logs in. Period. Correct?]

    Yes, that is correct.

    [Quote from original post: These rules are in place to protect players from the abuse of the Basic Rules of Conduct. Correct?]

    This is also correct.

    [Quote from original post: Those rules, when logged in and playing EQ are a contract between Verant and the subscriber. Correct?]

    Well, they are a contract between the publisher of the game (Sony Computer Entertainment America/989 Studios) and the player. Verant develops and operates the game for the publishers, who authorize us to set policy and enforce the rules of conduct.

    [Quote from original post: Are players protected by the Constitution of the United States and the First Amendment "Free Speech" or are they in a private fantasy world, governed by the basic rules of conduct put into place by Verant and agreed upon by the player?]

    The latter. EverQuest is a private service and as such is not governed by the Constitution which protects people from the government. By using our service players agree to abide by the rules of acceptable conduct that we require for continued use of our game.

    [Quote from original post: If players are protected by the rules of conduct, and players file /reports and petitions about player abuses of the basic rules of conduct, how are players to know how many /reports or petitions have been filled against an abusive player?]

    Players have no method of acquiring this information. It is for the private use of the EverQuest customers service staff. I have heard a few suggestions that we should implement something along the lines of a public Feedback system used by Ebay, but that would have the potential for abuse.

    [Quote from original post: If players and Verant are in a contractual agreement when playing EQ to play by the rules of conduct. Is Verant in breach of that contract if "abusive" players are allowed to continue to play after numerous /reports and /petitions have been filed?]

    No. We have specific rules for dealing with issues and define what exactly is entailed by the term "abusive". We typically need concrete evidence that the rules of conduct have been broken (such as a /report or the incident being witnessed by a CS rep), and then act, at our discretion, based upon the severity of the infraction.

  • More on Spectre Model
    [Quote from original post: Before, the excuse was used that enchanters couldn't get any more illusions b/c it would take too much memory to add another file to the global index. This spectre image has been added to the global file, and there is no reason not to give this to us.]

    I think I might have mentioned this in the other thread, but the spectre model has too many poly's to have two classes able to create spectres on demand. System resources are unfortunately finite, and we have to keep EQ usable by people with Voodoo1 cards.

    There isn't a 49th level song at this time, and it looks like there might not be one. We've used the rest of the cool ideas that people had on the 51+ songs, where bards get two per level. I’m certainly willing to listen to suggestions though and talk them over with GZ. As it is now, we could throw a song together just for the sake of putting one in, but it wouldn’t be cool.

  • Wolfform Issue
    Cros and Holly shouldn't be jumping on Druids in wolfform. I've asked the faction designer to take a look.

    Cros and Holly are Protecters of Pine, correct?

    [Quote from original post: Can you add in that if a caster and a warrior is in an argument over a warrior weapon camp, that the warrior has priority over the caster? Chances are the caster has already farmed it a few times and saw how easy it is to sell so he keeps coming back, where the warrior is just trying to get one on his own. Comments?]

    I don't think that we want to get into situations that we are policing camps based upon what drops. Camps are for whomever is willing to spend the time at them.

  • More Memorization Slots
    [Quote from original post: I'd like to be able to have more spell slots so I don't have to memorize spells everytime my buffs run out.]

    No plans to do this. The limitation to the choice of spells available to you at any given time promotes strategy. Some classes have a harder time with this than others. Wizards, for instance, usually don't have a hard time making their choices, where enchanters (and necromancers from personal experience) will always have an ongoing debate as to what the best spell lineup is.

  • Why do other classes have a level 55 spell and Paladins don't?
    Just checked my list. There's a spell on there. If there's not really one in game at that level, there's probably a typo. I'll check with GZ next time I see him.

  • Drops in Kunark
    The updates that we made to item drop percentages and locations were not directly based upon complaints from people about loot being too comon, but rather economic reports that we run to determine the health of the economy and the rates at which items enter the world.

    Basically, once we got 100K people over in Kunark, we'd have found the problems regardless, so it isn't the "fault" of the players who brought the issue to public light.

  • Compass Training
    Well we don't have any way that we could easily implement skill-increase potential based on what is in your inventory on a per-skill basis. I suggest you train the skill up a bit. The way skill increases work is that they require a successful skill check before they'll roll for a skill increase.

  • Server Situation
    There's really no situation that could cause everything on the server (including NPCs) to "hang" for an amount of time. If there was a network connection issue, PCs could lag, however NPCs would go about their normal business. If it were a server problem, it wouldn't affect everyone on the server, just everyone in a particular zone.

    If the server were to have a problem causing the zone to hang for a bit, spells ticks wouldn't expire either. I imagine what happened in your case was a network problem.

  • More Communication Ideas
    The Guild MOTD sounds nifty. I'll pass it around.

  • Hotkey/Mouse Problem
    [Quote from original post: I'm just curious about the fact that pressing the keyboard shortcut for a hotkey doesn't work while medding. Is this a bug? You can press the hotkey button with the mouse and that works fine. Is there any chance this could be fixed? My carpal tunnel syndrome is getting worse because of this 8-).]

    Since we allow the buttons to be pressed with the mouse, I'll check with the client programmer to see if we can also allow the key to be pressed as well.

    Factions are based on four components:

    3. Your class

    [Quote from original post: Many items are NO DROP that, once something better is found, sit in the bank and rot. Example, Razing Sword of Skarlon, great sword, collecting dust.     Blanket communication channels tend to reduce the sense of community in game, rather than enhance it. Instead of dealing with a specific person, and forming a relationship with them, it ends up pretty cold. This is somewhat apparent with zone-wide communications such as /shout and /ooc, however it's not as bad as a server-wide mechanism would be. In addition to this, there are some technical issues surrounding this idea (e.g. the number of additional packets to broadcast) which make the idea unattractive.

  • Splitting the Loot
    [Quote from original post: Every day, I get into groups with greedy master looters. When I master, I split after every battle and sometimes short myself just so the group gets an even split (i.e. if I loot 5 pp 4 gp in a group of 6 I /split 6). I just think it's fair.]

    We did the loot items due to the number of complaints about the ninjalooting of rare and expensive items. Coin really doesn't fit this description, so the best suggestion that I can offer is that you only master-loot with people that you trust.

If you are masterlooting, autosplit can work very well providing the person doing the final /split is aware that coins are not automatically reduced to even the split. If you are not masterlooting, an extra coin here, one coin less there, isn't a big deal and evens out in the long run.

  • Does Grouping Give You an EXP Bonus?
    There is both an XP bonus assigned per kill based upon the number of people in your party, and it is also expected that you can take more critters in the same amount of time in excess of what you could do solo.

    For instance, if a solo person can kill 1 blue mob every 2 minutes, then a group of two might be able to do 3 in that same amount of time, yielding 50% (plus the bonus) more experience per person per time unit.

    I don't recall anyone saying that. What I do recall saying is that the reason we don't have any +skill items out there was to reduce the impact of twinking. Stats really don't affect twinking as their effect is scaled based upon the level of the recipient. For instance, a level 1 wizards with 200 INT has roughly 1 mana point more mana than one with 100 INT.

    One other thing, I've seen a rumor going around that the effects of stats were drastically reduced at some point in late beta. Nothing like that happened.

Gordon Infobits
[13 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:53 PM PDT
Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits hot off the pages of the Gameplay Board:

  • Damage Bonuses
    [Quote from original post: Is there any reason why for melees who dual weild, the level damage bonus is only calculated on your primary hand?]

    That's just the way that it was set up for balance reasons. The enhanced melee damage is only applied to the primary hand, not the off-hand.

  • Tishan's Relocation = Mass Suicide?

  • Why do snakes kick?
    The skills that NPCs have are based on level and class, not on the model that they use. For instance, that puma that you are fighting might really be monk. I suppose that it might have been nice if we had a way to suppress the use of certain skills based upon body type.

  • Mana Regen Nerfed?
    There has been no intentional change, however due to the number of people reporting it I'm not ready to dismiss it just yet. However, I highly suspect that the observations are due to a number of things, not related to the actual rate. For instance:

    1. Perception: Mana above level 50 continues to increase at a rate much like it was before, however the mana regen rate does not increase on the same curve, giving the perception that it takes longer to med, even though you are still getting the same amount of mana back per tick. This is something that people also notice and claim as they level up or gain +INT/WIS/MANA items. They increase the amount of mana that they have without increasing the regen rate in a linear manner.

    2. Watched Pot: The addition of Kunark has caused people to spread out more. The net result of this is that the ratio of people to targets has improved. Where a group used to be at full mana the next time it was their "turn" to kill a particular spawn, they are now staring at their mana bars while a surplus of critters walk by that they could be killing.

    3. Placebo Effect: The "Placebo effect" also causes the "Watched Pot". Someone, perhaps due to one of the reasons mentioned above, perceives that the mana regen rate has changed, and comments on it. This causes other people to become sensitive to the issue, causing them to pay close attention to their mana bars, giving the impression that it is taking longer to regen.

    4. Of course there is always the good old fashioned "bug", which shouldn't take too long for us to figure out as soon as I can corner the programmers :) I'm looking into it this afternoon.

  • Loot Tables Changed: Who Gets Hurt?
    [Quote from original post: Loot tables changed - Who gets hurt... Those that had delayed shipments. They should have stayed the same so everyone had the same opportunity... What about the people still waiting for thier CD's?]

    If "fairness" is the main concern here, there's really no way to make these changes "fair". Certainly we could have waited until everyone (within reason) received their CD's, but the same subject of fairness would come up for those who have had their CDs and just now decided to make the trip to Kunark.

    Yes, the 10s of thousands of people entering Kunark did bring a few loot issues to light. There were cases where loot was dropping in the wrong place, and it has in most cases been moved to a more appropriate location. We even had a few dragon/god items dropping on level 40 creatures (though at very small percentages), and the people who were there first certainly lucked out. Unfortunately at some time we have to fix these errors, and making it fair in everyone's eyes probably isn't possible.

    Please, lets not look at this as people who got there later getting hurt, but perhaps as people who got there sooner getting lucky.

  • Killing Guards
    [Quote from original post: It is getting alittle tiresome in Butcherblock trying to find a guard being that everyone is camped and murdered the minute they spawn. You can see the bodies of newbies at the guard stations because they were foolish enough to believe that they could get some back up there with a battle gone bad. In some cases it's apparent that this may not have worked just like we would have liked it to, and we're looking for different ways to respond. We'll probably look at making some of the guards a little less appetizing.

    One thing to remember is that the goal of the faction system is to embrace the idea that no one really believes that they are evil. For instance, the dark elves do not think that they are evil, they are just uncompromising in their application of their morality. They probably feel that the high elves are evil.

    The buttons are a bit messed up due to a graphics file that went in last week. We've got it corrected, but it's a pretty sizable download (about 1.5 MB) so we wanted to hold off a little while.

    [Quote from original post: Aside from the ones that you can buy from Kunark vendors, where are these new spells?     50+ spells are either obtained via merchants, quests, or loot drops. They are out there, but you'll probably want to make your way to an area that is a little more ripe with magic.

  • Who Ya Gonna Call?
    [Quote from original post: When we can prove the troubles are on Verant's side...can we do the same and expect a response?]

    Of course, but the nice (and bad thing) about running a destination service where 25-60K people are connecting at once is that when we have a problem, we know it because we lose a good number of people at one time. We have alarms set up internally that polls the number of people connected and lets us know if a number of people drop all at one time. Aside from the zone server, where we could lose only a small number of people, any other error that we have control over would affect thousands of people.

    Source services (such as ISPs) don't have that advantage (and disadvantage). ISP XYZ might only have a few people connecting to the EverQuest servers, so when they have a problem with the route to us, they only have that small number of people who call in.

  • What is balance?
    Balance is about there being a compelling reason to choose one class over another aside from the desire to roleplay a particular type of character. It really isn't about relative power compared to another class, nor would we claim that that relative power is meant to be fixed from levels 1 to 60.

    Offensive power is only one equation in determining "balance". Other factors are usefulness to a group, and special skills or abilities. For instance, though a wizard's teleporting capability does not at all affect their offensive efficiency, it does contribute to other factors of "balance". A bard gives up much offensive prowess in exchange for being a very desirable member of a group.

    By "compelling reason", we mean that one class does not invalidate another. For instance, the monk can do more offensive damage at a give level than any warrior. Now, if they had the hitpoints of a warrior, could wear their armor, and carry as much weight, then there would be no compelling reason to play a warrior other than roleplay. The monks weakness opens the door for another class to be viable.

    Now any time I speak about "compelling reasons", posts on the board follow along the lines of "Just what is the 'compelling reason' to play a [insert class here]." All I can say in all of these cases is that "compelling" is in the eyes of the beholder, and I would encourage everyone to find that out on their own. Our opinions might differ from yours, but we believe that there are compelling reasons to play every class in the game, which is why we consider them balanced.

    [Quote from original post: I have been killing Gnolls in BB for almost a total of 15 hours and have yet to see a faction change. I would thhink I would decrease to atleast threateningly by now.. but nope. Any general idea of how many hours I will need to spend in BB total to get good enough faction to enter Qeynos alive being an Iksar Monk of Cazic?]

    Some factions have thresholds for how good or how bad they can get. I wouldn't be able to comment on whether or not it was this that you are hitting here (haven't checked to be honest, but wouldn't be able to say if I had), but it's possible.

  • The Timorous Deep
    I'm sorry guys, but I really can't speak further about this issue until the zone is widely known. To speak about it further before then would be tantemount to releasing spoilers. If you'd like to bring it back up in about a month or so, I'll be happy to discuss it then.

  • Hacking an Account
    I'm really not sure how to respond to the question. The only way that someone can "hack" your account is if they obtain your login name and password. We've seen many email scams sent to people attempting to gain access to that information, which is responsible for some "hacking", we've also a huge number of auction scams where "hacking" is claimed.

    For obvious reasons, I can't go into our internal security proceedures, nor the investigation process. We know that your data is safe once it enters our facility. We really have no means to take responsibility for anyone who gives out there SN and password, but we nonetheless investigate claims. We won't knowingly let someone keep playing on an account that we know to be stolen, however the ultimate protection of the account is in the hands of each customer.
