    Gordon Infobits
    [Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 7:28 PM PST
    Verant's Internet Relations Manger, the one and only Gordon Wrinn, made several posts today on the Gameplay board. Here's the scoop:

      Journeyman Boots
      What's happening is that he's getting stuck on a rock, and due to that he's not finishing his script that allows him to depop. So yes, killing him is one way to get him off the rock. A GM or Guide should be able to lure him off the rock as well assuming that he's stuck. Usually, after being stuck there long enough he'll find his way off the rock.

      Of course the long term plan is for us to make it so that he doesn't get stuck on the rock. I'll see what we can do along these lines.

      Server Transfers, Server CapsWe are absolutly opposed to capping server population, as we feel that it does more harm than good. New people join the game and want to play with their friends, and they can't do that if we've capped the server.

      Secondly, as people leave a new server, people have to be allowed to move in and take their place. A healthy server economy needs newbies, mid-levels, and 50s. Yes, I'm basically saying that the economy isn't healthy on a brand new server until we see the proverbial bell curve. Luckily the "Racers" move in and help a new server obtain that balance within a couple of months.

      Age and Maturity (Brought up by someone wanting a server for more "mature" players)
      Age isn't exactly a reliable factor to determine maturity in an environment with a high perception of anonymity. I've met 12 year-olds more mature than some 40 year-olds.

      The "Next" Patch Day
      Probably not next week, but the week after. Don't quote me on that yet.

      Lullaby Bugged?
      [After reviewing some evidence posted on the thread] I spoke to Geoff about the original author's tests. It looks like there might be *something* strange going on, but we'll have to run some more tests in House. Geoff will take another look at it after he gets done with the first draft of the expansion spells in the next few days.

      The Phantom Armor Series
      Poor GZ was unavailable for a good deal of time last week. He was in the Hospital from a spider bite. In fact, if anyone going to the gathering in St. Louis this weekend would be so kind as to tease him with a rubber spider, I'd be eternally grateful :)

      Anyway, we do have the ability to make the spell make you look like you are wearing plate, but that would just lead to every magician above the required level looking like shadowknights or paladins.

      GZ did say that he'd be willing to look into applying another effect to the phantom series to make it more worthwhile.

      Kunark Box Issues
      There are actually two versions of the box. The first run had a typo and said "five", the second run is correct, and says "three". The number of zones in the 3 continent world is actually higher than what was planned for the 5 continent world. This was all decided about May or June of '98.

      So no, the expansion is not "bogus". It contains additional content that wasn't included with the original game.

      Druid Expansion Spells
      I should mention that it's entirely too early to be screaming "rebellion!". Spells for the expansion are a Work-in-Progress and hardly a complete list.

      I won't be commenting specifically on them any time soon.
      --
      Of course you all can expect that the spells will be adjusted prior to the release of the expansion. That's the purpose of the beta. If anyone screams "NERF!" when an unofficial, unreleased, beta spell that no one has and only knows about through the use of one of those parser programs, I'm going to bang my head against my monitor.

    More on Server Transfers
    [Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:01 PM PST
    Gordon Wrinn responded to a post on the Gameplay forum regarding the upcoming server transfers. Here's the thread:

      One question that comes to mind about bringing in players from Solusek Ro and Xegony to Druzzil Ro is that of duplicate character names. There will very likely be a few duplicate names found from server to server, and this may create problems when migrating accounts from servers other than Fennin Ro.

      Yes, we are aware of the issue of possible duplicates, and that will be addressed in the patch message that will go up soon. Basically, it's announced that people who choose to move might need a name change on their character to resolve a duplicate issue, and if this occurs, an "X" will be appended to their name. In the event they have an "X" appended to their name, they may contact a GM in-game and get the name changed to something they pick.

      Also, the original Fennin split was capped at 1200 accounts. Will tonights registration use different, lower caps? (ie, 300 accounts from each?).

      The number of people we are moving is much smaller than it was yesterday. Ideally, yesterday 100% of the people we moved would have been represented in peak usage. As a side effect though, people who played off-peak logged on and executed the command. In addition, there are also people with multiple accounts that executed the command for each account. As a result, the amount of "peak usage" that was moved was less than we would have liked. Comparing the number moved to the amount of peak usage moved allowed us to estimate the outcome future moves and help us move the right number.

      Keep adding new non-split servers (300 copies of the game sold each day -> new servers every other week just to keep up).

      It doesn't quite work like that. First, of the new users we attract, roughly 30% play at peak. There's also the fact that people also leave the game.

      FINISH the Fennin split first.

      Once that split is DONE, then see if there's more room for overflow off other servers.

      The problem with this is that each split means that the server must be down for an extended period. This goes for the source as well as the destination server. We want to limit the number of people negatively affected by the split, hence we are doing a few at once.

      -Gordon

    Thanks Andrew!

      Preface: I had to think about whether to respond again to this thread, because trying to communicate one of the points I'm trying to make (I touched on it in my second post on this thread) may be futile.       But, what the heck, I'll go for it. You guys can agree or disagree, but this is my analysis of the MMORPG burnout phenomenon:

      First, a few assertions:

      1. Game lifespan. Games like EQ, UO, the better MUDs out there, etc. generally hold people's attention MUCH longer than conventional games. I'm talking about the players who enjoy the game initially, of course, not the ones who decide they don't like it in the first week or so and then return it/shelf it. Generally, the longer a person is associated with something and the more time invested, the more attached he or she gets. So it's pretty safe to say that players who really get into MMORPGs become VERY attached to them, and the longer they play, the more attached.

      2. Patching. MMORPGs can and are patched. They evolve and change. When you buy a conventional game you know that what you bought is pretty much what you have. There may be a few maintenance patches that fix bugs, and there may be an expansion in the game's future if the game is successful, but still, overall, the game is the game. Not so, of course, with MMORPGs. Features are added, content is added, game balance is adjusted (be it via enhancements or 'nerfs'), etc. The player KNOWS that the game COULD be, over time, made into something EXACTLY what he or she is looking for. At least in theory an MMORPG could evolve over time into the 'perfect' game, at least for one player (obviously not for everyone, because one player has different needs and wants than another player -- the old 'you can't please everyone all the time' adage applies here).

      3. 'Familiarity breeds contempt'. People get burnt out of just about anything in life. Some things take longer to grow weary of, but eventually just about everything grows old. As another poster wrote, and I paraphrase, 'no game has infinite playability.' I'm sure different people have different expectations, some realistic and perhaps others not so realistic, as to how long a game like EQ 'should' hold their attention, but, regardless, nobody expects EQ or any other MMORPG to hold their interest forever. It's also pretty safe to say that a flaw in something or someone can remain hidden, overlooked, or flat out ignored if the overall package is generally a good one. For example, if I buy a new car and it has a few problems, but overall it's what I wanted, then I'll probably ignore the problem (at least initially). But, over time, as the newness of the car wears off, as I get accustomed to the 'good' the car offers me, the problem(s) tends to get more and more annoying, until eventually, sometimes, it's all that's on my mind. After a while, every time you hop in your car that damn lump in the seat becomes more and more uncomfortable. Well, perhaps that's not the best analogy of all time, but you get the picture.

Criticize, flame, yell and scream, but we're here, trying. (Disclaimer: I know there are other developers out there making non-MMORPGs who post, do chats, write .plan files, etc., so please don't take offense at this assertion - none is meant - I'm definitely generalizing).

He's still addicted, he's invested a LOT of time into his character and the items his character possesses, but at the same time he's becoming more and more frustrated with the developers and maintainers of this game, because, after all, they COULD address his issues. And, of course, every time he experiences one of the problems he has with the game, whether it's a bug or something intentional he deems a design flaw, it bothers him more and more. He probably didn't notice the problem at first, or if he did, he ignored it in that it was dwarfed by the overall newness of the game. But now… now it's really on his nerves.

This phenomenon is therefore relatively new (although it's common with MUDs and has been happening for years, MUDs have never been mainstream and I'd say probably 95% of EQ players, for example, have never played a MUD, much less been addicted to one). It's new, but it's not going away. As I mentioned in my previous post, this genre is still in its infancy. EQ, UO, AC, etc., I still consider FIRST generation MMORPGs. And while they're all great games I think it's safe to say that the second and third generation MMORPGs will blow away the earlier ones. We will learn from our mistakes, we will have better technology, we will have more support and bigger budgets, etc. And because of all this, I think we can somewhat reduce this burnout phenomenon, or at least make it such that it takes LONGER for the average player to become burnt out or disenchanted.

All in all, people LOVE EQ, they can't stop playing it, and they tell their friends to buy it.

      But yes, there are problems. The game is hardly perfect. There are game mechanics that could and will be improved over time. There are overcrowding issues that will, over time, be addressed. There are the changes we'll have to make in the future to maintain balance and the overall health of the game that will upset some players, affecting them directly and adversely. And there are people both leaving the game as well as those who haven't left but who are becoming burnt out and disenchanted, either with the game or with us, or both. And many of this latter category post on this message board.

      So what are we going to do about it? Well, let me assure you that while I think this phenomenon is in many ways inevitable and unavoidable, we're still going to do everything in our power to minimize it. As I mentioned in my earlier post it is absolutely in our interest that as many players keep subscribing as possible. You can interpret that as base greed, self-preservation, the desire to be able to keep making games like this and fulfill our dreams, or all three (which is probably closest to the truth), but it's true nonetheless. We want to make as many of you happy as often as possible and for as long as possible.

      So here's the plan: we will continue to listen, we will continue to post, we will continue to try to explain our positions and to gather great ideas from our player base. We will launch servers as fast as we are able in order to keep overcrowding down (and no, regardless of how hard we try, that probably won't be fast enough for many of you). We will launch our first Expansion in late April, increasing the size of the world and hopefully breathing a breath of fresh air into the world of Norrath. We will continue to adjust, maintain, balance, and add features to the client and server. We will continue to add new quests, items and NPCs. We will re-tune and revamp existing areas and zones in an attempt to spread people out and entice them to enjoy ALL of Norrath's adventure areas. And, longer term than that, we have some other pretty nifty EQ related projects in the works, whether they be expansion, sequels, or what have ya. Verant is definitely in this for the long term. I hope you all hang with us and keep the faith. I hope that you keep playing EQ and enjoy the upcoming Expansion. But if you need to take a break, please do. And if you want to try our competition, please do. By all means play the game you have the most fun with - as I posted some weeks ago, life is too short to play a game you're not really enjoying. No game is right for everyone, and no matter how hard we try there will always be people who disagree with the direction we are taking the game or the decisions we are making. With 195,000+ players I'm sure statistically there are hundreds, if not thousands, who are burnt out and disenchanted. And that number will probably grow over time. And I think that's at least somewhat inescapable, even though we're going to try our damndest to make that statistic as low as possible.

      Ah well, that's pretty much it - we're doing our best, and we'll get better. If you still enjoy EQ, stick around, 'cause you ain't seen nothin' yet. But if you're starting to get that sour taste in your mouth, letting the game affect you personally, or getting steaming mad at us, well then, step back, think about what I've said please, see if it's applicable to you, and make your decision. Maybe understanding why you're feeling what you are will help, maybe not. Heck, maybe you think I'm just full of it :)In any case, keep the feedback and the constructive criticism coming, 'cause we're both listening and not going away :)

      'nuff said.

      -Brad
