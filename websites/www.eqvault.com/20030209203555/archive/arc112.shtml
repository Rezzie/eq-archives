Tuesday, May 23, 2000


Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:20 PM PDT
Gordon was up again late last night answering more posts on the Gameplay Board. Here's the scoop:

  • More Ranger Discussion
    Elderan,

    We can sit here and throw each others words at each other over and over again, but the end result is the same. You yourself talk about how you cleared all of those dungeons with your group, which you couldn't do if you were the dead weight you mention that you are.

    richard-rhal makes some very good points where he comments (paraphrasing) that one cannot properly judge what it means to be a level 60 ranger over the course of a few days. I'm not at all putting down your skills as a ranger. I'm saying that it takes more time for *anyone* to adjust.

    EverQuest is primarily a group game, and the full power of a class is determined when all members of a group are operating at peak efficiency. I don't think that you can honestly that you gave it a fair shot. I also think that you went in hunting for problems and as such are having problems seeing the benefits because all you wanted to look for was problems.

    It's clear that there's no way I can convince you that your class isn't broken, so I'm not going to discuss the issue further. I sincerely do wish that there was some amicable ground that we could reach, but we absolutely cannot make upgrades to a class where they are not needed.

  • It's So Unfair![Quote from original post: Gordon,

    There are several issues that you need to be made aware of. In the interest of game-balance I would like you to look into the following:

    1. My chair at work is made from some type of imitation, leather-like, plastic substance while my boss's chair is made from the softest, most supple, imported young-calf leather you could imagine. Regardless of the fact that he has been with the company for 27 years and I am a newbie I demand that my chair be upgraded or his chair nerfed so our seating conditions are equal.

    2. While I am at work I usually spend 90% - 95% of my time reading various Everquest message boards in anticipation of actually playing when I get home. Due to this my eyes are under a terrible strain and sometimes when I get home I can only manage to play Everquest for 9 or 10 hours before it is time for me to be late for work. This is unacceptable. There are two possible fixes for this: Either enhance/replace my eyes or enhance my job so that I am payed for staying at home.

    3. My roommate's puppy crapped on my carpet. Every time I tried to kill it I almost died myself. Now, the puppy cons green to me and it is extremely unfair for a pet to be stronger than me. The only logical solution is to nerf the hell out of the puppy or make me better at melee.

    4. I recently ran some errands for my neighbor who is an invalid. I ran all over town collecting various items for her which took the better part of a day. Upon returning and giving her the items all she gave me was a "Thank You" and an offer of some cookies. No experience for this quest? No money or loot other than cookies? I was infuriated so I killed the old bag figuring I could handle the minor faction loss and would recieve some good experience.     Thank You]

    ACK! My chair!

    Thanks for the laugh guys

  • Different Colors in Armor
    Given the way that color palettes work, you can't tint something white, as white is a lack of color, except in the cases of light where white is all colors, but lets keep the focus on pigment colors at present. The generic steel armor is as "lacking color" as those particular textures can get. There's no way to remove more color from those textures, you can only add color to them by tinting.

    Creating armor that "looks" white would require building new skins for all of the races that can wear it, and those new skins would have to be loaded in the Global, which doesn't have room given texture memory limitations of old Voodoo1 cards.

  • Gordon the Tireless
    [Quote from original post: Concordance News

    The issues noted in the original post have been fixed on the Test Server. Most of it will go live on Monday as we patch zones, some of it requires an update to items, which will probably happen next week.
    **********************************************
    The above is a quote from Gordon regarding when a fix is going in, taken from EQVault.com. Notice the day he says...MONDAY !! That is Memorial Day..a National holiday.]

    Easy there. Please note the day the original quote was written versus what day it ends up on the news sites. I wrote that sometime over the weekend. By "Monday" I was speaking of *Today*. (Note from Aurum: And by "Today" he meant yesterday, since he posted it late, late last night. Some of us mere mortals have this weird habit called 'sleeping' which is why it didn't end up in the news until today. :))

  • Who at Verant has played a Wizard?
    A common theme here on the boards is something along the lines of "If only someone at Verant played a [insert class here], then they'd see what we mean." The truth is that we have people here who play the game as religiously as some of the players, and every class is represented.

    The next comment that is usually made is that we twink our characters and play with GM powers. We don't. It's not fun and there's no point to it. Playing EQ with "powers" is roughly equivalent to playing an FPS game in "god mode". It gets old really quick, hence our firm stance that challenge is necessary in order for a game to be fun.

    I can understand how people might think that there is some "if only" condition that would lead to instant agreement on all of the issues, but there really isn't. We form our opinions based upon our desires for the game, our personal experiences, and feedback received from the players. As much as we'd like it to be so, sometime our decisions are not what everyone wants to hear.

  • VenrilSathir Faction in Kunark Dungeons - Part 3
    It's not being ignored per se, I just didn't have anything new to say on the issue.

    Given time for the faction issue to sink in, it appears that people are starting to learn how it works and deciding on their own whether or not they want to maintain the faction.

    It's not unusual for people to violently oppose a change that they view will have some negative consequences when we first make or propose it. However usually, given time to get used to the idea, many learn to accept or even like it. This faction issue is very much a situation like that, a resistance to change. But games of this nature *must* change in order to remain interesting and challenging.

    The 3 dungeons will continue affecting the Venril Sathir, and the evil outpost will likewise remain on this faction. I do apologize to anyone who finds this unacceptable and ask that give it another chance.

Monday, May 22, 2000


Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:52 PM PDT
Gordon's posting up a storm on the Gameplay Board tonight. Since the first infobit post he has responded and posted even more. Here are the latest quotes and excerpts:

  • Spell Gems: The Coloring
    Thanks for reminding me, I'll go remind him that I sent it. He was out of the office for nearly 3 weeks and came back to find over 500 emails waiting for him.

  • Is Cann2 Quest in now?
    Yes, it should be in now.

  • Do Spectres talk or am I hallucinating again?
    I'm surpised someone didn't point this out sooner :)

  • Another Hacker Scam?
    The StationID field just stores the name of the last person to log into the account from your computer. It's stored locally in a text-file (EQUname.txt). The only way what you explain could happen is if we sent that file down as a patch (we didn't) or if someone else tried to log in on your system, or someone manually edited the file or sent it to your computer across the network.

  • Are we going to get free accounts for star wars or pay and extra 10 bucks?
    It's still too soon for us to comment on pricing plans at this time. We'll be sure to announce the information as soon as we are able.

  • Names, Names and More Names
    We set up the alternate class-names as a benefit or a reward per se for those who got up to the higher levels. I'm sure that your suggestion would be neat and well-received, but we wouldn't want to cheapen the reward to the higher level folks.

  • More on Spell Drops
    No, we're still looking at the issue but we're leaning in the direction of it being ok as it is. We really don't want them to become so common that we get to the point that that it isn't a big deal and we might as well put them on the vendors.

    The core spells for each class is sold on vendors. The dropped and quested spells are pretty much for flavor. I realize that the previous way spells were done made them appear as an entitlement, but we're checking out the idea of making them like cool loot items.

    We'll keep an eye on it for a while and run some reports and let you know if anything is changing.

  • Why is there no music in Kunark?
    We'll be patching music files for all zones that currently do not have them in the near future. This will enable swiming, combat, and boat music. It will however probably be a while before we get some "mood" music assigned, and even then we'll be limited to the music that's currently in game.

  • Is the Shackle of Rock doable?
    I'm checking into this. I'll try to get back tomorrow.

  • Timorous Issues
    [Quote from original post: Great, I was hunting Raptors then all of a sudden I was kicked out of the game - no warnings whatsoever. The Timorous Deep zone is unavailable. I reconnect and get pounded on by about 4 raptors and a "Dinus". Yes, that's right, they're aggro now. I die in about 2 seconds, as I didn't even have gate memorized before.]

    NPCs, their relations, and statistics change frequently. Nothing can be counted on to remain 100% safe all of the time. In fact we routinely search out conditions that are 100% safe in order to make them a little more risky.

    I am sorry to hear that you may not have been given a warning as to the zone coming down. Any time we bring down a zone intentionally, there should be a warning to that effect.

    [Quote from original post: At least they gave the Iksars fair warning that their faction would be changed, I'd have appreciated the same.]

    The particular nasty critter that you ran into was always part of the zone, and wasn't "put in" to catch people binding there post-production. It just isn't around all the time.

    In other words, there was really nothing to warn about here.

  • Why do GM's not answer their mail?
    I'm not sure who you've been emailing or what issue you need assistance with, but you should direct your customer-service related emails to eqmail@verant.com.

    I should also mention given the tone of the posts here that though we understand that people do get upset, and are happy to whatever work we can to get CS issues addressed, we don't ask the GMs to reply to direct insults, flames, or posts laiden with profanity. Please keep your mail to that address civilized and professional.

New Story: In The Company Of Woe, Part One
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:28 PM PDT
An exciting new story has been published in our Fan Fiction area entitled In The Company Of Woe, Part One. We would like to thank it's author Lane Boyd for writing this story and allowing us to publish it. Here is an excerpt from this tale:

    Faces of soldiers drifted in and out of the mist on the path leading north out Kelethin. Dour men and women returning home silently passed Wigglewood by; others sat in make-shift camps on hillsides and under trees, watching nothing or eating or wiping their weapons clean. The halfling couldn't take time to stare, pressed as he was to keep up with Nyaleen's quick pace further into the Faydark woods. He wasn't doing so well, hampered by chainmail armor that laid over him like a net. She'd given it to him when he arrived in the elven city, but he never wore it. It was heavy. The tunic was bad enough, the leggings pieces and the coif were plain cruel. He slogged over the mossy ground, trying to keep sight of the warrior one hilltop ahead. She moved surpisingly fast in all that metal.

    A howl of pursuit severed Wigglewood's progress as Nyaleen once again disappeared over a crest. A tattered soldier broke from the trees onto the path in front of him. The halfling veered to give the man room to run, but a second figure, then a third and a fourth pierced the veil of mist, each futilely striking out at their elusive quarry. Scrambling to gain ground, they pushed Wigglewood aside. All the halfing saw was black blue fur, vicious teeth, and monstrous arms, hunched as they trampled him, intent on the soldier ahead.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 5:33 PM PDT
Hot off the pages of the Gameplay Board here is today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Creating Weapons
    One point of order. We don't create our items with random generators. They are all done by hand by the game designers :)

  • Relying on Others
    Causing characters to rely on other classes or strategies to make the most of their spells doesn't make a spell useless. For instance, you could get someone who has the ability to slow the critter (and hopefully its target) down to give you time to get the spell off. You could jump in and see if you can get off a stun. You could mez both the target and former pet, recharm, and hope your mez breaks before the charm does again.

    There are many strategies available to you, which is one of the things that makes enchanters so cool.

  • The Lumberjack's Cap
    [Quote from original post: Anytime someone in a zone 1)Loots one, 2)Auctions on being for sale, 3)Auctions wanting to buy one, 6)Inspects someone and notices they are wearing one, or 42)Remembers they themselves are wearing one, a lone shout (that will quickly bring about the crashing of the zone as assuredly as something that always happens) doth ensue: I'm a lumberjack and I'm OK......." ]

    ACK! I'll bring the issue to the attention of the nerfing team, errrrr, tuning team at once! :)

  • Fishing Facelift Thread Closed
    [Quote from original post: BOO!!! Abashi closed the thread.]

    The thread got too large. The UBB script starts choking when thread size gets too large. When that happens, the guys who look after the health of the board would just delete the thread causing the problems. I thought I'd close it before that happened.

    [Quote from original post: C'mon, Abashi.... can we get something a little more than "we will keep them in mind" and a thread closer?]

    Right now we have a huge list of *good* suggestions that we'd like to implement at some time, probably enough to keep 20 people busy for a few years. This is in addition to all the things that need to be done to keep the game running smoothly and keep it interesting. We of course can't implement every suggestion, or even every good suggestion, so sometimes "we'll keep it in mind" is the best response that we can offer.

  • Q&A on Bard Issues
    Ok, I've forwarded this to the tuning and live teams and I hope to get back to address everything in a day or two. There are a couple of issues that I'd like to pull out and comment on now though:

    [Quote from original post: * Angstlich’s Appauling Screech (26) - Fear does not interrupt casting.]

    Fear (no matter who its cast by) has never interrupted NPCs who are casting spells.

    [Quote from original post: * Still missing 49 song- thanks for asking for our input. Lots of good suggestions out there.]

    This probably shouldn't be included in a list of "bugs". One day we might put one in, but we don't have anything planned for this slot at present.

    [Quote from original post: * Item Switching: No other class switches items as much as bards - period. Bards are constantly switching between instruments because of song requirements or increased effects. Why should the interface be such a pain or cause carpal tunnel?]

    We've spoken about several options including a drop confirmation, and a /command that would make you swap out your items in specific inventory slots. I'm not sure when, or if we will something implemented here, but it's on our whishlist.

    [Quote from original post: * Any possiblility for a /twistsong ####, where # is the gem #? Would make twisting considerably easier than hitting keys twice every 3 seconds.]

    Probably not. We'd really like to see bards actively played, rather than passivly sitting there after typing one command. The more dexterous the person behind the keyboard is the more benefit that a bard can impart to the party. A person who can compose a song of 3 different melodies and change instruments at the same time is a formidable bard indeed :)

    [Quote from original post: * Sing emote. Nice for roleplaying.]

    Possible, but it would be low priority.

    [Quote from original post: * Magical bard musical instruments give no indication of their effect on the player (+5% String, +20% Percussion, +10% to Brass, etc). It would be nice to either see this info with a right click or when using the identification song.]

    The bonus varies on a per song/per instrument basis. I'd doubt that we could come up with a concise representation, and I'm also not sure that we'd want to. There are other held items in the game that increase the effectiveness of spells and do not specify by how much in their description.

    [Quote from original post: Oversight: Bards are now using mana on 2 songs, but do not get benefits of clarity or mana song. Why are bards the ONLY casting class that do not get mana regeneration benefits?]

    Not an oversight at all. We needed a method to place hard limits on how well, how often, and how long a specific song could be sung. We decided that we didn't want anything to adjust that limit. We have offered to switch DDD to a resing timer (same as a recast timer), but the charm song will remain on mana.

  • Medding in Combat/Taunt Nerf
    We haven't changed the way this works since the original AI for sitting was put in. It's not an auto-taunt, and assuming that the warrior-types have done enough damage to the critter they won't necessarily switch to the sitting person.

    I've always thought it was as if the NPC was saying, "How DARE you hurl a fireball in my face and then have the gaul to sit down!!!" :)

  • More on New Enchanter Illusions
    [Quote from original post: What about the Gender Illusion spells??]
    Actually we are working on something of this nature.

  • Melee Class Issues
    [Quote from original post: Abashi is the Dev team EVER going to fix the melee class stats? Make em MEAN SOMETHING. They work for NPCs but for PCs the stats are useless. Don't you think it's about time this was fixed?]
    As both Brad and I have mentioned before, stats matter to varying degrees depending on your class. Some stats matter more than others, and they work as designed.

    We've no plans at present to expand on the use of stats beyond what they currently mean.

  • Resurrection Bug?
    I believe I mentioned before that this isn't a bug. Player characters get one chance to accept a resurrection. If they refuse, they can still be transported to the site with a resurrection, but won't get experience.

  • Clerics and Plate

Beyond Level 50: Let it Ride
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:24 PM PDT
Gordon made an interesting post on the Gameplay Board in regards to some people complaining about how slow it is to level past 50. Here's the post:

    There's something that I'm wondering about while reading this thread, and I hope everyone here can help me understand the issues that are at work.
    When the characters were 50th level, and 50th was the cap, players accepted the fact that they weren't gaining levels and proceeded across the world to adventure and have fun.

    Now that we've raised the cap to 60, people can once again gain experience, levels, etc. Why "struggle" through levels? Why not simply play the game as you were at 50th and let whatever happens, happen? Since you can gain levels, you'll certainly gain them faster than you did when you were 50th and capped.

    I get the impression that there are a number of people who want to "hurry up and get to 60th so they can relax and have fun". Though there might be some worry of being outleveled by your friends, no longer being able to group, that concern doesn't exist from 50th to 60th.

    -Gordon
Here's the second post in this thread:
    [Quote from original post: 2. Past Mindset. I think people have been in a mindset that you need to be a certain level to do a certain area. There werent many lvl 36's in sol b until the zone was crowded and safe. Most people want to know that their group will survive the first time they go to a place so they level up to make sure they can do it. ]

    I have noticed that past-mindset seems to be a driving issue. One thing that we've noticed about the general playerbase is that they tend to fight outdoors until they outclass an area, then move in to fight. In other words, if we've designed a dungeon to be 35-45, the majority of the people in there are 45+. Without putting entrance caps in, which we definitely do not want to do, it would be interesting to debate how this could be addressed.

    [Quote from original post: 3. Perception. As long as verant hides the way the mechanics of eq works people will have their own biases and perceptions. "Oh a lvl 53 just isnt high enough to help us level 59's where we are going." ]

    As a general rule, if the lowest-level person in the group is blue to the highest level person, everyone will get experience from a kill. The actual rule is a bit more lenient than that, but that's a rule that will always work. I should also mention that everyone will either get their full share of experience or will get nothing. Basically, if you get the "You have gained experience" message, you have gained all the experience that you are otherwise entitled. In other words, we don't just give you a token amount of experience to make the message show.

    [Quote from original post: I'll just answer for myself. I play everquest to level. Becoming more powerful is what's fun. Getting new spells is what's fun. If my exp bar isn't moving then I'm wasting time. I'm burnt out on sitting around chatting. ]

    That's how some people play the game certainly, and it's a playstyle that we gladly support. These are the people who typically build one character up to max then turn around and build another. For them, the leveling is the "fun" that the game provides. I just got an email today from a gentleman who has 4 character over 50th level, and I would guess that he falls in this playstyle.

    Other playstyles do exist.

    For instance, a common playstyle is the guy who hates to level, but wants to be as powerful as possible, so powers up his character to max and then goes off to have "fun". These are the people who have one high-level, maybe a low-to-mid level secondary, and primarily play their high-level character.

    There are other people for whom the concepts of leveling and power are only a secondary part of the game. They play first to have fun and worry not about maximizing their XP acquisition rate. What this type of gamer and the one above find "fun" are often the same thing, however the one above doesn't begin to have this fun until the character is maxed. We typically don't know what level these people are as they rarely talk about it, and if queried, usually have a higher p-time to level ratio than the other types.

    So, to recap, if I had to place labels on each type of gamer:

    1. The Powerleveler (Leveling is fun)
    2. The ???? (having trouble coming up with a label) (Doesn't like leveling, thinks adventuring, socializing, and exploration is fun, but can't have that fun until the character is maxed.)
    3. The Social Gamer (Is not concerned with leveling, and thinks adventuring, socializing, and exploration is fun).

    Note: Let's not confuse this with "The Roleplayer" which really isn't a playstyle in this regard. Most roleplayers would fall under #3, but I have met many 1s and 2s as well. Also, the playstyles aren't firm, but more of a scale that wraps back to #1 after #3. Players do fall in between the different numbers. In addition, there are a couple other less common playstyles, such as the Experimenter, who has a bunch of characters, low-to-mid level.

    Giving levels 51-60 a steep experience requirement should increase enjoyment for those close to a #1, would not affect those close to a #3, and I can see how it might make things difficult for those who tend towards #2. Our assumptions would be that there are more people who have tendencies towards #1 than #2.

    -Gordon

  • /Loc Issues
    /loc is a very artificial, out-of-character, and therefore undesirable method of direction-finding. It was supposed to be taken out when EQ first went live, but as I understand it it was left in due to public opinion. We really wouldn't want to expand on its use in this manner, as it would cause it to become commonplace.

  • The Risk
    Every player has the ability to choose what level of risk is palatable to them. Those who choose to take on greater risk should be able to expect greater rewards and less restrictions upon what they can or cannot do. For example, some people are so opposed to risk that they'll only fight in a dungeon if it is crowded and there is no chance of death, while others will run headlong into certain death all for the prospect of the excitement that comes with the battle.

    Experience loss is part of that risk and part of what creates excitement by making character death something that you definitely don't want to happen.

    The death penalty is one of those things that virtually no one likes, yet is part of what makes the game fun and exciting.

  • Use of Triggers in EQ
    [Quote from original post: For example you manage to kill the level 50 lords, now, deeper into the dungeon (behind you is great) 5 lvl 45 body guards come running to the scene... lets say it takes them 1 minute to get to the party.     [Quote from original post: Well you fixed the fingers.. but obviously you didn't want to increase the total number of mobs in Plane of Fear. So what did Verant decide to do? Yeah, they removed 4 of the 8 boogeyman spawn.]

    Actually that's not entirely correct. In the Plane of Fear, there are a total number of creatures possible that can spawn. For each one, there is a percentage chance that a given mob will spawn. This means that the number of creatures of a given type that can spawn on a complete repop of the zone varies. For instance, it is possible that 50 boogeymen could spawn, just as it's possible that zero could, but on average it is usually a given number.

    For the purpose of raising the spawn percentages for the frightfingers, we reduced the percentages of Phoboplasms (which can drop any of about 8 different class-inspecific items), not boogeymen.

    Geoffrey Zatkin aka “Geoffrey Z” aka “GZ”
    Game Designer
    3 years at Verant
    B.S. in Psychology (not computer science)
    Works with the other game designers at Verant Interactive (so can ask questions around)
    Specialty of the EQ Magic System
Topics in this edition range from the Jammin Geoffrey Doll, to the portals in the Timorous Deep. Even if your not a caster or hybrid you'll find it an enjoyable and interesting read. Here's an excerpt:
Naming pets is a cool idea, and one that we have talked about. There are, however, several problems. We already maintain a live staff that checks names as the go in. Adding something that let our staff check pet names as they come in to the world would be using too many real world resources for such a small payoff (adding more staff simply to let players name their pets just won’t happen). If a player could only pick one name, and their pet got named that every time would be more feasible - but that would mean that your fire elemental and your air elemental would both be named “flamtar” or some such. We would probably have to make pet names unique at that point - since we don’t want two pets having the same name for player recognition reasons.
Once again I would like to thank Geoffrey for taking the time to do this column. Thanks GZ!
