Gordon Infobits
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:11 PM PDT
Hot off the pages of the Gameplay Board, here is today's regular edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Aegis of Life is broken due to faction. Will it be fixed soon?
    The faction thresholds on it are set a bit too high at the moment. It should be fixed on test now and will probably go live in a week or two.

  • Why are artifacts NO DROP?
    Characters are required to earn artifacts. They are given as a reward for completing various tasks. They are badges of honor.

  • How's the Pony model coming?

    Fighting with Milo now on the model. He thinks that it should have fangs, claws, and bat wings :)

  • Verant Software
    Our company is founded on the concept of massively multiplayer online games, and the ongoing revenue that they generate through player subscriptions. We don't intend to release any single-player games, or server-software to allow people to set up their own games. To my knowledge, I believe that another company is producing a game that will allow you to do as you ask.

  • Shrink Spell Changes
    This spell was changed indirectly by a fix that was made in another area. I have heard people ask for shrink to be done in stages before, but I wouldn't mind hearing everyone's thoughts on this.

    As for the shrink potions, we'll probably make them shrink to gnome-size in a single charge.

  • Getting Level 51+ Spells
    Some level 51+ spells will be buyable, some questable, and some will drop as treasure. As a general rule, spells which are core to the abilities of a class (such as a clerics heal spell) will be purchasable.

  • Will we ever see capes on characters?
    In a nutshell, no. The back and shoulder slots (places where you'd expect to equip a cape) are not visible slots. We have no plans at present to rebuild all of the character models to support visible capes.

  • Server Splits: Character Transfers
    If you have characters on more than one server destined to be split, and those characters all have different names, you could have them all moved to the split server where they would all be accessible to you.

    However, if any of those characters have the same name, you'll want to leave them on their current server, or pick one and delete the other. If you try to have characters moved from more than one server, and a name is duplicated, the last one to be copied will overwrite the first. We might look into disallowing a move in the event of a duplicate, but that isn't yet finalized.

  • Tumpy Tonic Quest Nerfed
    We reevaluated our earlier position upon continued concerns posted by the customers. In addition, we received several testimonials from people who were able to level using it at a rate far (10+ times) exceeding our designed pace for the game. Finally, I myself was sent a few screenshots of over a dozen people standing around the guy handing in tonics.

    It was essentially buying levels for money, something that we don't want done in EverQuest.

    As a note, the quest will be changed and the experience given by it will probably be increased beyond what it originally was.

  • Charm Spell Bug?
    This isn't a bug. When a creature dies, the system checks to see what entity gets credit for the kill. If that entity is a pet, the credit for the kill goes to the pets owner. Since charm broke, and the entity that did the most damage is no longer a pet, you don't get the experience. Your choice in this case is either to kill the former pet first, or recharm it so that it is your pet when the first critter dies.

  • Kunark Issues
    [Quote from original post: My question about the expansion is that, with characters going up to level 60, will the two classic dragons that are out there now simply be camped constantly by 60th levels who want to twink their new characters with dragon droppings? Is there anything special about their locations or zones to prevent this?]

    We're still considering these issues. Though we don't want to see level 60 folk bottom feeding on dragons, we don't necessarily want to buff them all up either. We'll come up with something, but everyone will have to wait for the release of the expansion to see what that is :)

  • Spirit of Cheetah Spell Bug
    Bypassing the recast time of a spell by zoning was a bug. The fact that we fixed it should have been listed in the patch message, and I apologize that it wasn't. Apparently we had to spend time on the reset-on-zone issue in order to implement some of the cooler spells and disciplines for post-50 play.

  • Breaking into the Planes
    Though we understand the feeling that people have concerning their effort in breaking a plane giving them "rights" to the area, other people have a right to be there as well. Our feeling is that the effort and deaths expended to break into the plane was evidently "worth it" to those breaking in, and that the reward for doing so was more or less "first dibs" on the creatures required to break the plane.

    Though we fully intend for there to be areas and creatures in the game that require large organized parties, guilds, etc., we won't deny access to an area based upon membership in a guild.

  • Why By Kunark?
    [Quote from original post: Why should I pay up to $30 for ROK when I really know very little about it?]

    It could be aruged that this is precisely why you might consider purchasing the upgrade, as it is comprised of new things that you know very little about.

    We believe firmly in the ideal that players should be allowed to explore areas, discover new spells, quests, dungeons, etc., without getting the information handed to them from outside sources. Yes, I understand that within a few months, everything about Kunark will be up on the spoiler sites, but before that happens, everything will be new to everyone. That's priceless in my opinion.

  • Classes and Combinations
    Actually I said that adding a new class is highly unlikely for a multitude of reasons. As for race/class combos, we recently added Erudite SKs to the list.

  • Root
    [Quote from original post: Maybe also the treants figured out that the druids root spells now work better then bevor and like to use it now?]

    Strangely enough, that is very close to the truth :)

  • Instill Doubt, Magical Kick, and Magical Boots
    You make some good points. I'll mention it to the guys. Perhaps we could make the monk kick automatically magic at a certain level, or add a craftable magic foot item as suggested.

  • Spawn Rates
    Please keep in mind that though the spawns were fixed, it doesn't mean that they are always around, just that they spawn at the rate at which they are intended. The Undead Cyclops spawn was one of those that we looked at when reviewing all of the spawn times, it was spawning improperly and should be working appropriately now. We also made changes to the Netmasters, Cooks, Clockworks, and Plague sharks.

Verant to Sponsor Player Events
[16 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:20 PM PDT
In a very interesting turn of events, after months of not supporting player-based events Verant has announced that it will begin working with the players in developing them. Here's the post Verant-Thom made on the Gameplay board:

    Greetings!

    We want to start implementing more Verant Sponsored Events in the near future. Below is a list of the current Events that will be going live to the servers shortly, so if you have comments about these events, or have an idea that we are not touching upon, please let your opinions be heard.

    Tournaments

    Test of Tactics

    This Six vs. Six-player competition is designed to be the most intense 30 seconds possible in Everquest. Two opposing teams facing each other in the arena, confined to the raised platform area, completely de-buffed before match fight in single elimination, team style combat. Has been run on Xegony and Veeshan to this date. Other servers will participate in this event in the near future. Prizes have been extreme weapons such as the legendary “Fanged Skull Stiletto” and items removed from the game such as the beautiful “Cryosilk” Robe.

    Test of Flight

    This is an archery tournament designed for all levels. The archers will fire upon a GMPC that will match his level to the level of the Archer. A flight of 20 arrows at 100 paces, most arrows landed wins the event. In the case of a tie, the archers will each fire a single arrow, and the archer doing the most damage wins the tiebreaker. Preliminary testing completed on Xegony, about to be started on all servers.

    Test of Steel

    This is a single melee vs. melee combat with very simple rules. You must hit with steel, and you cannot cast spells. Preliminary testing still in progress on Veeshan, prize is set to be the infamous “Gleaming Bastard Sword”. All Skills such as “Lay of Hands”, “Harms Touch”, and “Mend” will be allowed. Test of Faith

    This is another single competition designed for any class that is capable of casting “Greater Heal” before level 50. This is still in its design stage, and a server will be designated shortly for preliminary testing. Test of Mystics

    This is open to any class, but rules forbid melee. This is still in its design stage, and a server will be designated shortly for preliminary testing,

    Other Events

    City Invasion – Qeynos

    The ultimate challenge for a guild, the City of Qeynos still has not fallen to this date in Everquest History. Background is that Antonious Bayle has left for a diplomatic meeting in High Pass Keep, and a guild of players is going to sack the city. To achieve victory, there are 10 designated NPC’s that must be taken by force of arms within 4 hours (real life time) before the return of Antonious. These 10 are the 7 non-primary caster Guildmasters (Q-Cat is not part of this Event), the 2 Lieutenant Guards, and the Corporal. Since this is a signup event, a guild must prove ability to repel such firepower and slay the Lieutenant in Northern Qeynos (No small task I assure you). Primary Testing on Xegony and Veeshan for this event has already been accomplished, and the first attempt at this event should take place within the week. The Leader of the Guild that takes Qeynos will receive a title. The title will be “The Conqueror” or a very similar title.

    Rules for other cities may follow in the near future.

    This is what we have planned in the near future. If you have other ideas for events, we would like to hear them for all levels of play. Let us know what you think.

    -Aikbach

After reporting hundreds, even thousands of events in my time this is the first time I've ever seen something like this happen. There are more logistics to figure out, but this could be big!

Thanks James!

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[13 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:04 AM PDT
Gordon Wrinn went on a late night posting spree last night, so finish waking up, grab some breakfast and here's this mornings edition of the Gordon Infobits for your reading pleasure:

  • Inte's Super Duper Sword of Death
    Yeah that 150dmg 15 delay sword was our fault. We wanted Inte to be carrying a flaming sword, and one of the game designers figured that no one would ever be able to kill him and made the sword a little unruly. We did have the forethought to make it NORENT though, and changed the stats in the item database to something a little less nasty, but we went so long without patching items that someone actually managed to kill Inte two days before the patch.

    That'll teach us to go around calling NPCs unkillable :)

  • Spell Casters have GZ, but what about Melee concerns?
    I do agree that casters have received a good deal of information from GZ, and has done a very good job addressing casters concerns. This was one of the reasons that I first started trying to address stat issues, and also one of the reasons that we later decided to give everyone a concise explanation.

    I'll pay special attention to try and service melee concerns, but it is very difficult to get into some issues while trying to maintain some sense of adventure and exploration. Melee stuff is typically pretty straight-forward, and to delve into it any deeper might end up on the virge of spoilers.

    I am happy to say that we have gotten several melee issues addressed since I came on here. As for things I've had a primary hand in, the Paly/SK boosts, rogue hide/sneak (versus undead as well) and evade, monk built-in haste, and many item changes. There are many other things as well. So I'm not ignoring your posts and I apologize if that is the impression you've gotten.

    As for questions about items such as this, the game designers take into account many things when creating items. For instance, many casters would like to have higher strength or agility, but are unwilling to sacrifice INT/WIS to get it. This item is a shoulder item, and as many people know, there aren't too many +INT/WIS shoulder items in game that are available to casters. It's just not meant to be a melee item.

  • Patch Question
    [Quote from original post: The Necromancer spell Hungry Earth was included in the original request as well because it roots the undead and then hurts them. It was scheduled to be fixed as well, has it been fixed or is it still being worked on?]

    This should have been done as well. I'll check with GZ tomorrow, as I'm about to hit the sack now.

  • Great Quiver Idea
    [Quote from original post: Make quivers lower the delay of your bow, assuming that arrow is coming from a quiver.]

    Hmmm, that's an interesting idea and it might even be possible. I'll chat with the guys about it tomorrow. I think we'd jump at the chance to have quivers useful for something :)

  • Raising your Install Doubt Skill
    [Quote from original post: Why is Instill Doubt the hardest skill in the game to raise?]

    Well the technical reason is that the skill-increase roll is only made when the skill-success roll succeeds. This can get a bit frustrating at times I imagine. I certainly remember it from when I raised sense-heading from 0 a few times :)Recently we put in some code that should be allowing faster skilling in ID. It's nothing dramatic, but it should save you a few hundred clicks :)

  • Typo
    [Quote from original post: "CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you;"

    Now isn't "affects amount YOU will be paid" and "how much they will pay YOU" the same thing? Haha Brad is this just a typo? Might wanna go over it befor its published :)]

    ROFL. Feel free to give him a hard time bmcquaid@verant.com :)

  • Login Screen Artwork
    [Quote from original post: Dumb question but on the Login screen art, who is the big green guy, you also have one like him on the original box, At first I thought troll but the nose is wrong as is the body shape and what not(not fat enough to be a troll) Then I thought Goblin or Orc but he is huge even compared to the Barbarian chick in the corner.]

    He's a Troll. The original sketches that were provided to Kieth Parkinson were based on our old character-models, back before the engine change that occurred in about March of 98.

  • Not Allowing a Change of Faith
    The decision to disallow a "Change of Faith" was made during the early design process for EQ. At that time it was decided that we didn't want people changing faith as a matter of convenience, to get whatever benefits that a particular religion might grant at that specific time.

    With that in mind, several other design decisions were made for EQ that now makes changing faith impossible even if we decided to allow it through a complicated quest or what have you. It's burried too deep into the core faction code for all of our work since then to be unraveled.

    Your initial faction is set through your choice of race, class, and deity. Other factions that you affect as you build that character are affected based upon your own faction. The entire faction system would basically have to be redesigned to support it, and with thousands of other NPCs and factions to consider, the work involved and the potential for problems makes it prohibitive.

    I still maintain that this deity-specific items issue is no different than if we added new items restricted by race or class. Sure, I've seen people say that if they ever had the idea that weapons or items would be restricted by deity, then they would have chosen one. However, knowing all the potential aspects of the game prior to playing it is not required in order to play the game. I'm sure that many people started the game with a limited or non-existent understanding of game mechanics.

    Though I understand the viewpoints that have been presented, we are firm on this design aspect of the game.

  • Lavastorm Design Issues
    The gator model has been there forever as a rare spawn.

    So, when planning on putting more creatures in a particular zone, the game designers are limited to the models and textures that already exist in the zone. Yes, it could have been cool to stick a wandering fire-giant in the zone, or something like that, but if the model isn't there, you have to work with what you have.

    We'll probably explain what disciplines are in the near future.

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:32 PM PDT
Gordon has been posting up a storm today on the Gameplay Board. Here's excerpts and quotes from his latest round of posts:

  • When will the Hate drops be fixed?
    In the data patch tomorrow. (Note: this was posted yesterday)

  • Future Servers
    Yes, 4 new servers are planned in the very near future. Our current plan is for two of these to be split-servers (like Druzzil) and the other two to be clean-slate.

  • Camping Big Spawns
    I really don't think that this is possible any longer. Some time ago we put in code that added a random component to the spawn timer for the longer spawns. Though I can't specifically announce the size of the random component, I can say that the window for a dragon-type spawn is in excess of 24 hours. That means that even if they had it timed before we did this, they'd have to sit around a LONG time.

  • Patches and the Expansion CD
    The expansion CD will include all of the patches made through about the middle of last month when the master was burned.

  • Adding New Classes
    Adding new classes is a very complicated process. It raises all sorts of balance issues that have already been dealt with concerning the original 14.

    Except for the art, new races are comparatively easy.

    In any case, we have no short-term plans to add new classes at this time.

  • Will there ever be Agnostic-only weapons?
    No plans for this. We really don't see it making sense in the grand scheme of things.

  • Will we ever be able to change religions?
    No plans for this either. It's really no different than someone being unable to wield/wear an item due to their selection of race or class.

  • Removing Rare Spawns from Tracking
    It has long been our stance that the ability to track down rare spawns is a benefit of having tracking. Removing rare spawns from the tracking list as you request would negate that benefit.

  • Logging out in the PoH
    [Quote from original post: My question is this: when i log in today, should i expect to instantly die to a horde of new roamers you put in there? that would, to say the least, suck...]

    Well I of course can't comment on any specifics, but I would recommend that you arrange for a suitable army to meet you upon login.

  • Qeynos Hills to Western Karana Zone Bug
    It hasn't been overlooked, it's just difficult to fix without requiring a 4+ MB download for everyone. It's on the list for repair.

  • Patch Message Inaccurate?
    [Quote from original post: Also, for enchanters, DDD did NOT have its casting time reduced. The RECAST time was reduced. I'll post up a message about getting this changed in a minute unless Gordon reads this one.]

    Doh! It's right on the list that GZ gave me, I apparently wrote it down wrong. I'll see about getting it updated after we wait a little while to see if I missed anything :)

  • I cancelled my account, are my characters still there?
    They are probably still there. To my knowledge we haven't yet had to clean up old accounts. That's not to say that we won't do it some day (I believe that we are only required to hold them for 90 days), but drive space hasn't become an issue at this point.

  • Changing the Terror Forged Mask
    The same day that the terror mask was first found, we announced that the spell effect on it was a mistake and that it would be changed back. In addition, the NPC that hands it out had his quest dialogue changed to tell people acquiring it that the mask would soon lose its current spell effect and gain the power of fear.

    Though it is true that we try to keep existing items as is, there are cases where an item is so severely overpowered that it must be changed in order to maintain the integrity of the game. This was such an example.

    All of that said, I'm sorry that you are upset. It is next to impossible for us to insure that every player is made aware in advance for every issue in the game. We rely heavily on the news sites to help spread the word when something interesting happens.

  • Patch Issues
    I'm chatting with the team about these issues now. Of the people that have noticed a problem with footsteps getting louder, what sound card(s)? Also, is it your footsteps, or others footsteps?

    As for "No change to Kerra Ridge", it was patched incorrectly and is being redone now. We're still looking into the beggar.

  • Why does root and/or snare not have a "message" when it breaks like the DOTs and charm?
    As they are offensive time-based spells, they should be issuing a message when they break. I've asked the programmer to look into it.

  • Blackburrow's History
    Hahah, I remember when Blackburrow was the only dungeon. Dang, that was nearly 2 years ago.

  • Gating Spells
    Good thought. We just gained this ability (a cast-on-others "go home" spell) recently. I've also raised the idea of a "Group Gate to the Wizard's Bind Point". I'm not winning that argument though, so please don't hold me to it :)

  • Deity Restrictions
    By choosing "agnostic" a person makes a choice to avoid both the positive and negative aspects associated with choosing a deity, in the same fashion that his selection of a race or class allows him to avoid the positive and negative aspects of another class. By the same logic that we do not allow people to change their race or class selection based upon new items or spells in the game, we likewise won't be allowing the change of deities based upon the same.

    EverQuest is a game that is subject to change, and when a person makes a choice that choice not only affects them in the present, but for the future as well.
