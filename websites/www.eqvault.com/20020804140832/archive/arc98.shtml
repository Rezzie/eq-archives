Gordon Infobits
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:41 PM PDT
Gordon was busy on the Gameplay Board today as always. Here's the latest from him:

  • Level 53+ Players
    [Quote from original post: Verant don't give a rats @SS about 53+ players]

    Yes we do. Of course we also care about 52- players as well. This is a case where the needs and desires of one group are directly contrary to that of another group. Our decision will in any case be bound to upset someone.

    Leaving Vox/Nagafen as they were would sooner or later (if it hadn't already) require someone to be level 60, or at least in a guild with a bunch of level 60s in order to participate in raids upon them. At least this way, the lower-level folk still have a chance. As I mentioned in another thread, I've seen multiple reports of people being invited on Dragon runs just because they happened to be on the server at the time and another body was needed. We didn't hear too much of that before.

  • "Could you comment on existing poisons (i.e. asp poison) reduced in damage?"
    The old poisons themselves haven't been changed, though that is the effect.

    What did happen was that a fix was implemented for an existing problem. Previously, poisons would do their damage based off of the level of the rogue using them. Now, they do their damage based off of the level (e.g. strength) of the poison.

    When poison was revamped in this patch, that effect was applied to all existing poisons as well.

  • Unannounced Nerf: NPCs
    Well we generally don't announce changes to individual NPCs, much less when we increase the combat skills of a merchant, but seriously, this guy was being used as a practice dummy.

  • Karana/Bertoxxulous Split Question
    [Quote from original post: I think this has been answered before, but I can't remember-when we execute the /movelog command, does it automatically register all characters you have on that server, or do you have to log on each character and execute the command?]

    All characters on the account are covered by the registration of one /movelog for the account. People with multiple accounts will need to log in one character from each account to register should they want to move.

  • Posion Upgrade
    [Quote from original post: My 40 INJECTED POISONS (~250 damage DoT) that I had saved in the bank waiting for our 'infamous' patch have been nerfed to the introductory version I (~65 damage DoT).]

    The old poisons themselves haven't been changed. What did happen was that a fix was implemented for a problem, of which old poisons were affected. Previously, poisons would do their damage based off of the level of the rogue using them. Now, they do their damage based off of the level (e.g. strength) of the poison.

    When poison was revamped in this patch, that effect was applied to all existing poisons as well.

  • Why only two servers for a transfer at a time?
    [Quote from original post: But the newer transfer servers peak at 1000 players.]

    That's because the number of people that we transfer is a bit of a guessing game. The purpose of the transfers is to fix a problem. That problem is: Overcrowding at peak hours. To fix this problem, we must move peak population from the crowded server to the new server. However, there's no metric that we can use to define "a peak player".

    Regardless when we open registration for the move, there are always people who don't play at peak who want to be included in the move. Overall, this is expected and is actually necessary, since if this didn't happen, new split-servers would be near-vacant in the off hours. However the percentage of those moved that are represented in peak vs. off-peak varies.

    We got the best percentage of peak players when we did the first split. This was because we didn't preannounce when registration would be, and just broadcast on the server during peak hours. Though this led to a much higher percentage being moved at peak, it did upset a number of customers, which is why we moved to announcing it a couple of days in advance.

    [Quote from original post: When transfer servers come online to elevate crowding why are they only open to two established servers?]

    Actually, in the first split we did only one. Subsequent splits all used two servers. The reason that we limit the number of "source" servers is that we need to accomplish an identifiable goal in the move, meaning that we have to fix crowding on the source servers. For every server that we open to the split, we dilute the possible effect. For instance, if we take 1000 people off of each of two servers, and that reduces peak load by 300 on each of those servers, the change is noticible. However, if we open it up to 4 servers, we can only take 500 from each, reducing peak load by a mere 150, which wouldn't affect peak populations to the same extreme, and thus have a lesser chance of fulfilling the original goal of the split.

  • Rogues
    [Quote from original post: Well lets count this off now. A Rogue thats one spot]

    If going someplace that you must get behind locked doors, sure. Of course, the rogue is a fantastic group member in my opinion regardless what is to be encountered that day.

    [Quote from original post: Ok now you need to keep that group alive. Well shoot gotta have a cleric for that I suppose]

    Considering that people in the "death penalty is too severe" threads cite that clerics are sometimes hard to find, it sounds like groups are getting by with other classes that can heal.

    [Quote from original post: What about crowd control you say? Ahh the enchanter class comes into the group for that.]

    For the best crowd control, perhaps. Other classes can also fill the role to varying degrees.

    [Quote from original post: And if its a bad area you need a puller, plus another damage dealer wouldnt be a bad idea. Go get a monk for that. So now a possible 5th slot gone.]

    I dislike the idea of "pulling", particularly feign-pulling, so I won't comment on this one.

    [Quote from original post: So now whats left? Well my basic math skills say that we have 1 to 2 slots left for 6 to 7 other classes. You are forcing the gruops to have the first four classes, and totally shorting any of the others.]

    With 14 classes, and only 6 slots in a group, not everyone can be in the first 6 choices of every group for every situation. There are other classes that have the ability to fulfill the roles you speak of to various extents. I've seen all-hybrid groups kick some serious tail.

    [Quote from original post: Do I feel that Rogues dont belong in groups? No not at all. I just dont think that with a group limit of 6 people that we have to have yet one more slot literally taken from us as a choice to group.]

    Only for certain encounters, which means you are left with the ability to choose not to engage in those encounters.

  • No Dragon Changes Yet
    We're still considering what to do about the issue.

    On one hand, the login poll that we ran showed that the people who liked it versus those who disliked it were evenly matched. Actually there was nearly a 1000 vote difference in favor of the current arrangement, but with over 180K votes total, that's well within the margin of error. This wasn't quite what we were expecting.

    On the other hand, we've been hearing some good reports from the servers about the change. People who had never been on dragon runs, even ones not associated with guilds, have contacted and asked to go on dragon runs. In one case that I heard, a mid-30s cleric was invited along because they needed some extra healing power.

    We're still keeping this matter open until we see how it plays out in game. We'll let you know when we have a firm decision one way or the other.

  • ETA on Shrink Anywhere
    The change was made in the database, but after we froze it for the code change. As such, it won't go in until the next item patch. We have a short week next week (being off Monday and Tuesday) so its likely that we may not do a big patch. Most likely we'll be looking at the week after.

  • Kunark Framerates
    We're consistantly enhancing the performance of the EverQuest client from week to week to improve framerates by a frame or two here and there. We'll move these changes to live as we are happy with their effect upon performance. Right now, we're restricted from making the latest client-changes live due to the fact that we are working on a new sound engine for EverQuest. Hopefully we'll be able to promote the newest client within 2-3 weeks.

  • Safe Fall Bug
    That's an interesting one. Damage from falling shouldn't be impacted by what view you are using. I suppose we might be looking at an issue that's being complicated by framerate. In any case, it's the first I've heard of this one, and I'll forward it to the Test team.

  • Stop Duel Messages
    We've been considering some action to reduce the number of "showoff" duels that are occuring out there. One thing that we've considered is putting a minimum level-limit on dueling, or on the duel broadcast. Something along the lines of level 5 or so.

    As for putting in a filter for duel messages, we're not considering that option at the moment. The messages themselves do have a function in the server community, and provide a good way to "break the ice" so to speak. It's not unusual for people dueling to get /tells of congratulations and such from others in the game.

    It might be prudent to mention though that "showoff duels" (Duels between inappropriately named characters) can be considered a disruption of other's gameplay and could very well result in action upon your account.


Few Notes on Spell Drops
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 5:31 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn made the following post on the Gameplay Board concerning Spell Drops:

    Hello all,

    I've noticed a number of threads here talking about spell-drops in certain areas of the game, and I thought I'd clear up a few misconceptions.

    1. The chances of getting a spell are higher in some zones than others. Some zones (Karnor's comes to mind) don't have spells because there's enough loot there already.
    2. Spell-drop chances are not completely random. In other words, you may have to go to several different zones to get all of your spells.
    3. Not all spell drops are on the "random" tables. What I mean here is that sitting at a dungeon enterace or exit and pulling from the closest room will not guarantee that you get all of the spells in the zone/dungeon sooner or later. You might have to venture in further.

    I hope this might clear up some of the ideas about spells out there. If anyone has any other questions or concerns, please post in this thread and I'll see if I can address your issue.

    -Gordon


Posion Q&A
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:29 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn cleared up a few misconceptions about the posion upgrade on the Gameplay Board today. Here's what he said:

    The "system" of poison itself wasn't changed, and I apologize if people out there thought that it would be. What there is is a greater variety of poisons, which are cheaper, and have more plentiful components. I've gotten a number of emails today asking various quesitons:

    Q: Why aren't the poisons themselves stackable.
    A: The item system does not allow items with effects to be stackable. Alcohol doesn't apply, as it's covered with item-specific code. We cannot feasibly add item-specific code for poisons and potions due to technical concerns.

    Q: Why don't poisons have multiple charges?
    A: This is a function of the item engine again. They cannot have multiple charges.

    Q: Why don't poisons last through zoning?
    A: The game engine doesn't allow it, and spell-code doesn't apply.

    Q: Why do poisons take time to apply?
    A: Balance. It's necessary for it to take time so that the effects aren't used multiple times in the same combat encounter without stepping out of combat. Poisons are meant as a pre-combat preparation. Keep in mind that rogues are extremely powerful as it is.

    Q: Why can poisons be interrupted?
    A: Game engine again. To be honest, I'd like to see the chances of this reduced significantly. I'll look into seeing what we can do.

    -Gordon

[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:10 PM PDT

  • Negative Posts
    [Quote from original post: Have you ever noticed that almost all the topics in this forum are negative! Nothing seems to be done. Its always complaints, or people saying they're quitting because of poor customer services... What do you think about US? I just dont get it...]

No one feels any reason to keep responding to praise threads because they feel that nothing good will come of them, afterall, their issue is resolved.

    This is just the way human psychology works. We do address a great number of issues from week to week. It's just hard to get people as worked up about being happy.

  • "What does it mean to play EQ?"
    [Quote from original post: First, what is VI's "vision" for EQ? Personally, I have no idea what it is. Evidently it is important to VI, so why are they so shy about fully and completely saying what it is? Shouldn't it be in the manual? What about on this site?]

    Well other people actually mention the "vision" more often than what we do. It's not exactly something that can easily be quantified and placed on a web-site for open viewing. It's the culmination of everything that we've planned, expected, and learned about EQ throughout development and later into production. It's how we want EQ to be.

    People often say that ClassX should be able to do "X, Y, and Z" (what X, Y, and Z are varies from class to class and person to person). In some cases, these ideas are contrary to what we had planned for the class (e.g. our vision for the class). There's no document that we can open up that tells us what we can or cannot do in all cases. Sure, we have the several hundred page EQ design doc (which will never be public), but it of course doesn't cover anything.

    When an issue comes up that hadn't been previously considered, we (the design team) sit down and decide whether that issue merits some kind of action, based upon whether there is a problem to be solved, whether a solution is viable, and whether that solution fits into what we had in mind for the game. If we can come to some sort of agreement, we modify the "vision" (e.g. In this case, our thoughts and feelings about the way things should be).

    A lot of people who read the board and the news sites on the Net probably can give you a pretty good idea as to what our response will be to any particular issue. Overall, we want to provide people with a challenging and fun game, to the extent that it encourages them to play the game for a long time.

    [Quote from original post: Second, what does EQ want to be when it grows up, besides being EQ2? The issue here is it a FPS with some community interaction or is it a CRPG?]

    We're obviously going for RPG :) But being an RPG doesn't mean that we can force people to "roleplay", as people have differing ideas as to what "roleplay" is. Furthermore, there are players that no matter what environment they are in, will never "roleplay" as a matter of choice. What we can do is provide everyone with options. Whether or not they pursue those options are up to them. I would love to see a world where everyone acts "in character", I just don't think that there's any way that this could be enforced.

    [Quote from original post: Roleplaying requires a certain amount of interaction beyond "Where do you want to camp? ]

    I would certainly agree with that. Of course, people have to make the decision engage in things other than camping. If everyone chooses to play the game in the fashion of choosing the quickest path to advancement regardless of the amount of fun that they have *during* that process, then that is their choice to make. You, as a roleplayer, can choose not to camp. You can choose to take actions other than that being taken by the people who are only interested in advancement. Sure, both options are valid gameplay styles, and we support it for that reason, but at some point people have to be responsible for thier own choices.

    [Quote from original post: There is no real growth in a character that I can associate with camping on trainer hill in CB waiting for the SBS to drop ]

    The easy solution here is: Just don't do it. Choose to take actions that support "real growth in a character" in your opinion.

    [Quote from original post: Well, you say, what about the powergamers? What about them? The game as it currently is is geared towards them. To them, EQ is an FPS. More involved than most, but still just a hack-n-slash for exp and loot. ]

    Only if people *choose* to play it that way. Again, it's in your hands. Choose to play it in a different manner. MMORPGs are not single-player games that can provide a single plot with some minor subplots, and expect everyone to follow them. MMORPGs have to provide all possible content up front and allow people make their own decisions in regard to how they are going to choose to play the game.

    [Quote from original post: If they want to develop a true roleplaying community, they desperately need to add some human intervention on the back side. What this means is that GMs and/or guides establish quests and storylines and get people involved in them. ]

    These can and do occur, quite regularly. Of course, unlike pen and paper games where four or five people are sitting around a table with one guy directing the dynamic content, EQ has 265K+ subscribers in nearly every country in the world. Though we'd like to be able to provide a "DM" to every group of people for the purpose of generating dynamic content directly for them, finances don't allow it, at least not for $10 per month. The Holy Grail for every MMORPG, both present and future, is to give people the feeling of a dedicated DM. Small MUDs can sometimes do it, but no game of this scale really can. This is one of the reasons that player-run events are so popular. The player can create their own dynamic content for themselves and their fellow players. Check out some of the news sites. There are usually a dozen or so player-groups doing this every week.

    [Quote from original post: Next, hybrids. Why is this concept so difficult in EQ? When I first signed up, it never even occurred to me to be anything but a ranger. Ever since I started reading the boards, I have seen very little to show me that this was a smart choice. ]

    I'd recommend against letting others on the internet decide for you how you feel about things. I read a post from a guy here a couple of weeks ago. He'd played the game for three months, had a wonderful time the entire time he was playing. Someone referred him to the boards, and a couple of weeks later he quit. He got the impression that somehow something must have been wrong with him judging by the amount of negativity on these boards. He was having the time of his life, until he let someone else tell him how he should feel. Decide for yourself.

    [Quote from original post: Nerfing - Why does this even occur? If there is a problem with a certain class/object/spell/race, fix it. Lessening a skill/ability/person because there is a problem does not address the problem.]

    Sometimes nerfing is the *result* of fixing the problem. Nerfing is simply making something less powerful or useful than it was in the past. Though painful, it is sometimes necessary. As game designers, though it would be more popular, we cannot allow ourselves to get into a situation where we exclusively *UP* the power of things in the game in order to make up for an imbalance. If one item is too powerful, we often won’t get into a situation where we have to A) provide weapons of higher power to everyone else in the game B) then up the power of the NPCs in the game to make up for the fact that everyone is now more powerful. Nerfing is sometimes the best choice for the health of the game. At other times, the best choice is enhancement.

    We will do whatever we feel is the best choice for the issue at hand, and it will usually be the choice that affects the least number of people.

    As an example of how perceived “mudflation” occurs, take a look at the “rangers plight”. Six months ago, everyone (meaning, other melee characters) were all complaining about how powerful the ranger was, and how it was our “pet class” since it’s fairly well known that Brad plays one. Six months later, rangers are now complaining about how bad they are. No negative change has been made to the core ranger class. What happened? Some other classes received enhancements whereas the rangers didn’t. This “imbalanced” the ranger class in the eyes of many who play them. They feel that they’ve been nerfed even though no negative change has been made to the class. They now want to be enhanced based upon the perception that they are no longer as powerful in relation to other classes as they once were. This is the danger of mudflation.

    [Quote from original post: Customer service - It seems to border somewhere between being a sick joke and an active campaign by VI to tick as many people off as possible. I got stuck in a doorway in Felwithe because of some glitch. I could not do anything. I petitioned and it took almost 1/2 hour before a GM showed up. They did nothing. ]

    If a GM showed up and “did nothing”, there’s a good chance that it was an apprentice guide, since they do not have the powers that a full Guide or GM does (e.g. the power to unstick you). I do apologize for it taking a half an hour to “show up”. Keep in mind that there are a bunch of people on the server, all of them expecting the same level of service as the next guy. Though you’ve only petitioned two times in your EQ career, there are many that petition hourly for one issue or another.

    We do the best that we can to provide you with the best customer service we can for a $10 per month fee, and we continue to improve the service regularly. Though I wish we could change this, there will always, however, be people who feel that they have received poor service, and will take their issue to the public. Many of these situations arise out of unrealistic expectations about what GMs and Guides can or should do. I think that perhaps one of the more pressing issues to be addressed by customer-service at this time is to help the players understand the situations in which they can expect customer-service help. Hopefully if we can do this, we can cut down the number of requests that we have to deny, giving more time to address the issues that we have some control over.

    [Quote from original post: is not meant to be flamer, just honest criticism and a search for answers. ]

    And I do indeed thank you for posting your thoughts. I hope that my responses have been helpful.

  • Unforseen Consequences
    [Quote from original post: Unforseen consequence of high lvl spell restrictions? Question: Does the Divine Aura effect of a Shiny Brass Idol have a lvl restriction?]

    We have the ability to attach level restrictions to effects on items, whether those items proc (fire randomly, like when a weapon strikes the target), or the item is activatable (like jboots). The "effect limit" on items is seperate from the level requirements (and race/class restrictions) on the underlying spell.

  • Pain & Suffering Messages
    The "pain and suffering" messages indicate that you are dying from an indirect source, such as by bleeding to death or by a DOT spell. For instance, if a necro loads up Call of Bones, and lets it kill him, the "Pain and Suffering" messages will precede the death.

    It's not a bug, just how we portray indirect deaths.

  • Lycanthropy
    [Quote from original post: Remember the old RPG "Daggerfall?" If you were attacked by a werewolf and survived, you were infected with the disease of lycanthropy, and every full moon, changed into a werewolf. That would be pretty nifty.]

    We played with the idea of Lycanthropy in early beta, but decided not to implement it in game at that time. It would be neat though :)
