The titles awarded again will be 51st level title for the winners of the server competition, the 59th level title for the runners up, and the 60th level title for the grand champion of that specific class Norrath-wide.

This is so that all levels, races, and classes get to have fun with the events. When it comes to the game-wide championships, those will be dedicated only to the 55th level or higher players. Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:36 PM PDT
Here's the latest quotes and excerpts from Gordon Wrinn found on the Gameplay Board:

  • Casual Players
    I see your point Twigglin, but what do you propose? It does take time to set up for a group. That time is pretty much the same, regardless of the amount of time that you play in a day. Why is this a penalty? How do you suggest that we "fix" it? We can't *force* people to take you into a group in 10 minutes, nor can we reduce your travel time (other people have that ability).

    Now we are implementing the LFG (Looking for Group) flag. Hopefully this will allow people to determine where a good place to look for a group is, and maybe even arrange the group prior to arriving. This could effectively cut down time 1 & 2, as you know where your going and there's a group there for you.

    I wouldn't say that any of this constitutes a "penalty" for the casual gamer, but perhaps the person who plays 10 hours per day does indeed get an advantage. I doubt, however, that many groups would stay together for 10 hours without undergoing a period of looking for additional members, which affects them as it does the casual gamer.

    In short, I really don't think that what you are citing is a problem that needs to be "fixed".

  • Recent Rubicite Drops
    [Quote from original post: This could be the revamp. You dont know.]

    This is not the revamp. There's some story behind what's happening now, so you might want to look around a bit to see if you can find it.

  • "Desperate Cry For Mages"
    I'm not sure where you've been hunting, or if it's been in a few specific areas, but you might consider trying another areas out. There are some spells, for instance, that don't drop in all areas. If you've been hanging out in a few that have higher spell drops for the other classes, perhaps you've missed any areas that might have higher percentages of mage spells.

    [Quote from original post: Now, is this intended, or am I the unluckiest mage in EQ?]

    That's a possibility as well :)

  • NPCs and "Comparable" Weapons
    heatsink, check out the section of the FAQ that deals with why NPCs must be stronger than PCs in most cases. The short of it is:

    1. It's a group game. If the NPC were no stronger than a player of a particular level, it certainly wouldn't be a challenge for two.
    2. You are smarter than the NPC. The NPC gets some extras for being computer-controlled, and therefore not as intelligent or adaptive than the average human.

  • Wizzies and Group Friendly Spells
    [Quote from original post:Would it be asking too much to give Wizards ONE group friendly spell?

    I'm with Lelenae on this one. Ports are *very* group-friendly spells. Though they do not enhance the offensive power of a group, they can be very handy for getting out of trouble.

    [Quote from original post:1- Warriors, Monks, Rangers, SKs, Paladins, etc.. are stuck. They have to run forever.

    That is indeed a danger.

    [Quote from original post:2- We lost our spot.

    Don't camp. Don't fight some place where you need a "spot". Go someplace where there is no waiting and you can wade in at your own pace. There's tons of them in game at every level. Just say no to camping.

  • Guild Question
    [Quote from original post: Or to pay 10,000pp or more. I was told this by a guild today, that at high levels I can not do parts of the game (for which I paid $50 for) unless I join a guild. I feel it is not fair, I do not have 20 or more friends to get to do plane raids, or other high level things.]

    I'd have to go with DarckHellfire's response. The game is indeed a group game, and the size of a group needed to do certain things goes up as you advance. Most people should have formed relationships by reaching those levels that provide the much needed support. Those who don't form those relationships are usually the ones who solo exclusively or have shown themselves to be a burden to groups through reputation.

    You don't *need* to be in a guild, however guilds are a nice way of making it easier for a large and extended group of friends to recognize each other. It's also useful when it comes to getting some pointers on how to do certain things. Some areas of the game have a bit of a learning curve, and it's sometimes nice to cut off the bottom portion of that curve.

  • Rallos Zek Issues
    [Quote from original post: 1. Is there any plans on changing the looting system?]

    No.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Will there ever be a chance to move to another server?]

    Maybe. We're still considering the issue. Nothing is likely to be decided within the next month. I'd really like to see a server-specific polling mechanism put in, but it will be at least two weeks before we can do work on that. It would be at least a week after that before something could go live, and a few more to set up and implement any kind of meaningful poll. That's basically the plan at the moment. Of course it's all subject to change.

    [Quote from original post: 3. Do you have any other options you are working with other then the two mentioned above?]

    Not really. I think that the thoughts posted here have determined without a doubt that there are many different opinions on how PvP should be handled. I don't think that there is a solution that will please everyone, or even most everyone. We're a bit nervous about making any more major changes to the environment going forward.

    [Quote from original post: My question is WHY HAVE TWO IDENTICAL PVP SERVERS? Would it not make more sense to have:]

    Because there are enough people to support it. More people enjoy playing Teams than they do on an open PvP server like Rallos. Items being allowed, it still makes more sense for it to be allowed on Rallons than on the Teams servers due to the level range.

    [Quote from original post: BTW, any word on returning pumice stones to merchants as self-only??]

    Yes, we've ruled it out as an option, and decided that the current stone is appropriate.

  • Scale of Wolf Update
    [Quote from original post: - Scale of Wolf and Spirit of Scale will no longer be canceled by being the target of a melee attack. Ranged attacks and spells will still cancel the spell.]

    Thanks for looking up the patch message, I thought that was the case.

    [Quote from original post: Um, Gordon, you guys had better get a list of patch messages as well as a list of changes you *didn't* tell us about (like the thing you all denied about the FD changes at the end of 1999) and post it where you and your comrades from VI to post here so you don't *have* to guess.]

    I don't know why you found this so funny Mynn. I said in my message that I wasn't sure and would check into it. I clearly indicated that it's possibly been that way the entire time. In any case, I was at home when I posted. The patch messages are on my system at work.

  • GM Events
    [Quote from original post: How do I find out more about GM hosted events? I have been playing EQ since release but i always missed those events. Is there anywhere i can find out when is the next event on my server (Mith marr). Thanks]

    "Events", meaning things like the tests, will soon be posted on the EverQuest.com web page. Quests, however, are another matter, as they are never pre-announced. You'll only be able to find out about them by either being on and involved at the time, or by reading up on them afterwards. We'll soon have quest-stories posted on the official website.

  • Kunark Casters Loot
    Casters have gotten tons of items all over Norrath. Sure, there is a lower concentration of them (found, anyway) in Kunark, but I'd hardly say that they have "gotten the shaft" as you put it. There's more than enough items out there for casters. Kunark fauna just happens to be more melee-centric than other places. Perhaps something else might be observed elsewhere.
    --
    I should also mention that we're about at the point that we're running out of "useful" things to do with caster items. There's only so many different ways to do +int and +mana before it's all the same stuff, and we all know that only +int and +mana stuff is considered "useful" by the casters at large.

    We can do so much more with melee items.

  • Accusation Response
    You might want to read the sentence you found issue with again:

    "The worst ***armed monk*** versus the best ***armed warrior*** still favored the monk by 10%"

    I added emphasis to the appropriate places. The case where the warrior outdamaged the monk was when the monk was ***unarmed*** and the warrior was armed with is best weapon. Every case where the monk used a weapon, he outdamaged the warrior.

    I understand that this confusion was accidental, but in the future I would like to politely request that you be certain before breaking out accusations of lying. It's very offensive.

  • Banned
    It's easy to come here to the boards and assume that something bad is going on behind the scenes because we don't come provide public "proof" for the account actions we take. We will discuss these issues, but only with the account holder and only via email. We don't drag these issues out to the court of public opinion.

    [Quote from original post: BANNINGS should require some solid evidence - they are, after all, permanent.]

    Bannings do have solid evidence, or we don't do them. It would make sense for us to systematically kick customers off the system, and thus off of our balance sheets, by doing bannings without proof. Please don't assume that just because we won't come here to the boards to "tell our side" that the proof doesn't exist.

    [Quote from original post: Had they said in that email, "You've been banned because a senior guide was appointed to monitor your activities. This senior guide watched you make a deal with person_x for 15 fbss's and 15 golden efreeti boots after giving person_x only one of each. Person_x has a reputation for duping items. We've banned you both." ... or something like that.]

    We won't even give this level of detail to the person banned, let alone state it publicly. I'm sorry, but this is necessary in order for us to conduct business. If we divuldged in detail all of our investigative abilities and powers, we'd open ourselves to those who would search out potential loopholes.

  • Limits on Equipment
    [Quote from original post: Why not put level limits on some equipment?]

    This issue is covered in the FAQ at http://everquest.station.sony.com/forum_faq.html.

  • What is /voice?
    [Quote from original post: The message said something about *Voice Control ON* and that it would be active the next time I stared the game. Fearing that it might be a not-fully implimented feature, I turned it off again (it's a toggle). My question is: How will this work? What kind of commands will we be able to use, and what hardware will we need?]

    I can't really talk about this beyond saying that its for something that we're working on for the future. When I can release more information, I will.

  • Problems With Shaman Charm Animal
    [Quote from original post: When shaman befriend animal was nerfed, you said that you would give us equivalent spells for higher level monsters. I saw that Charm Animals was added to the game. There is a serious problem with it.

    Charm Animals does not want to seem to charm animals above a certain level (34?). If it is 34, then this is not even above the level of the spell.
    We went from a spell that would charm every level mob to 2 spells that could only do up to a certain level (34?). Is this a replacement?]

    I know, and agree with you that there has been a net loss in overall usefulness, however, the lack of a level cap on befriend was a mistake. It had to be corrected for balance reasons. Giving the Shaman spells that allowed them to do the same thing wouldn't have addressed the balance issue, which means that we might as well have left it as is.

    I'm sorry, but the change was necessary, and we've compensated shaman to the greatest extent possible.

PvP Teams Revisited: Thoughts on Crossteaming
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:54 PM PDT
A new thread was started by Verant's Gordon Wrinn this Sunday centering on the issue of PvP Teams. Here's the entire post from the Gameplay Board:

    Hello,

    I posted this in another thread but it seems to warrant its own.

    The biggest difficulty in regards to PvP in EverQuest is that EverQuest is, and will remain, a primarily PvE (Player versus Environment) game. The addition of PvP to the game should not change the basic fundemental mechanics of EverQuest. PvP in EverQuest has to result in "EverQuest with PvP" rather than "PvP EverQuest", if you get my point.

    One of the largest issues on the PvP Teams servers is the subject of cross-teaming. Players of different teams get together for battles. Due to the PvE focus of the game, this has to be allowed for sake of game balance. Though some have suggested that cross-teaming, either in groups or guilds, not be allowed, it's something that will need to remain for the PvE game.

    The problem comes in when cross-teaming is applied to PvP. Cross-teaming creates a situation that is often referred to as the "invulnarable healer" scenario. Basically, one group of PCs (Team A) attack a group of people on Team B, while supported by a healer from Team B. In this fashion, you have a situation where a member of one team is taking an active role in the deaths of members of their own team.

    There is another "invulnarable healer" scenario that is more common and often just as bad as the cross-teaming scenario. This one revolves around a group of players from Team A attacking a group from Team B, while supported by a healer/buffer who is far superior in level to anyone from Team A or Team B. For example, a group of level ~20 trolls go on a killing spree in Greater Faydark, while supported by a level 50 healer/buffer. The problem in this case is that someone out-of-range is directly contributing to the deaths of players below their range.

    On Friday, I spoke with one of the programmers about possible solutions for the PvP Teams servers, assuming that people in general believe these issues to be a problem. Suffice it to say that there is no easy solution. Each one has its disadvantages and "loopholes". For an example of some of the solutions:

    1. Disallow cross-team buffing/healing
    2. Disallow out-of-level (+/- 8) buffing/healing
    3. Combination of 1 & 2.
    4. Redefine "Teams" servers to GvE (good versus evil) servers and implement idea #1 on that basis, rather than on cross-teaming.

    The problem with all of these solutions is that they have an undeniable impact upon the PvE aspect of the game.

    The final and best idea that we were able to come up with allows things to continue as they are now in regards to PvE, and only places restrictions on the basis of PvP. In this idea, any player who has taken damage from another player would not be able to be healed/buffed/receive any beneficial spells from anyone who wasn't on their own team or in the +/- 8 level range for a given amount of time (a few minutes), or until they zoned.

    In the cross-teaming "invulnarable healer" scenario, the person from Team B would be able to support people from Team A for PvE engagements, however once a member of Team A took damage from another team, they would not be able to receive support from anyone on Team B for a few minutes. In a real game scenario, the maurading band of trolls in Greater Faydark couldn't be healed/buffed by a high-elf assuming that they were taking damage in their attacks.

    In the out-of-level scenario, that same band of level ~20 trolls wouldn't be able to receive support from a 50th level cleric.

    There are some disadvantages to this, such as in the following:

    4 Trolls are grouped with a high-elf fighting PvE encoutners in a dungeon. Humans come in and "slap" the trolls every so often thus keeping the high-elf from assisting them. Perhaps this should just be a disadvantage for cross-teaming in general. Perhaps this is just a risk that people take from grouping with the enemy.

    What are your thoughts on these ideas? What other issues do you see causing problems in regard to this idea? Do the benefits (e.g. reaffirming the servers as PvP Teams) outweigh the potential disadvantages?

    -Gordon

Abashi on the Official Boards:

  • Gordon Thank you for doing a test for us.
    For the past several weeks, the complaints by monks here on the board (most of the complaints anyway) have been that they no longer outdamage warriors. The tests that I did were only to confirm that that wasn't true. Yes, I conceed that monks cannot takes hits as well as warriors, but that's never been where the disagreement has been. When people said, "Monks no longer outdamage warriors", I had to disagree, which is why I ended up testing it out. However, when people now say, "We can't take hits like a warrior", I agree with that.
    Dealing out more damage than the warrior doesn't mean that you'll beat the warrior in a duel, nor that the ratio of damage dealing to damage taking capability is constant. The benefit of extra damage has more weight than the disadvantage of being defensively inferior.

    I'm sure that some would say, "If a warrior can solo creature X in two minutes and be almost dead at the end, then a monk should be able to do the same in a minute and end up almost dead". This would be an example of constant ratio between offense and defense and the two classes. This however isn't the case. If the ratio were constant as suggested, there would, again, be no reason to have the warrior, because the monk's other skills would make them preferable in all cases. Not to mention, there's an added benefit any time a creature is killed faster than normal, a benefit that can't be measured directly against the power of the class, but more against its usefulness to the group. Would a group, for instance, like to kill things in 2 minutes and end up OOM and near death at the end? Or would the rather do it in 1 minute and end up the same way? They'd want it done faster in all cases. That's what monks and rogues add as melee classes.

    Concerning everyone saying that the test wasn't "valid", I fully agree with that from a scientific standpoint. Many more combats would have to be carried out than the ones I could do over a couple of hours yesterday. To be honest, its not really my job to test things of this nature, I just do it as needed so I can speak with first-hand experience. Though the specific percentage point differences can't really be considered averages, I do think that the test fairly well answered the question: Do monks outdamage warriors?

    There was one interesting thing in the test that I didn't mention in the post yesterday, concerning the one time that the warrior outdamaged the monk. The warrior was using his best combo out of the 5 weapon combos I tried, the monk his worst (bare knuck). The damage ratios were determined by seeing how long it took for the character to kill the NPC in question. The monk completed the task in under two minutes in every case but one, where it took 2:03. The warrior was over two minutes in every case, with its fastest time at 2:01. The monk's best time, by the way, was 1:30.

  • Would a second arena with pets be possible?
    Pets work in the Arena. Initially when the Arena was put together, pathing data wasn't included. That pathing data was put in a few months ago, and since then pets have worked properly.
