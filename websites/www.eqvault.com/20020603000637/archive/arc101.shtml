Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:39 PM PDT
More posts were made today on the Gameplay Board by Gordon Wrinn. Here's the skinny:

  • Wizard Runes
    [Quote from original post: We have two runes of Fulguration, both marked the same but for different spells. We have two runes of Attraction - same issue.]

    We should probably mark these runes Azia and Beza and update the research text accordingly. I'll see what I can do.

  • Solusek Ro Server
    Several servers can make very logical cases for a split. The ones that we do will be based upon the need that presents itself. I have noticed a fairly large off-peak population on Solusek Ro, indicating many international players on that server, but that of course isn't peak usage, and isn't part of the problem.

    Next week after we take care of the TZ/VZ ruleset issue, we'll look into further population balancing methods across the servers, starting first with the servers with the highest at-peak population. We'll talk more about our plans then.

  • Camping
    [Quote from original post: If i want to sit at one spawn site and camp a npc anywhere in the freakin world,i should be allowed to do so.]

    But when it's the same people who are choosing to do that that go around to the boards and various gamesites declaring how EQ is boring, we have to ask why they choose to do that.

    In general, we've found that a non-trivial population will take the quickest path possible to leveling, regardless of the tedium that that particular path might present. The way to avoid tedium is to see that risk and reward are in balance. If we give these solitary and stationary fast-spawns spawn-times equivalent to the level of the NPC, then doing the boring camp for hours on end isn't as quick as getting into an underutilized dungeon with a good group.

  • More on Karana Server Switches
    We'll probably split it to one server, not several. It is problematic as you mention to do something along the lines of splitting 500 accounts to one server, and 500 to another. That said, if we do pick up 1000 people and move them to Saryrn or 7th (most likely possibilities), history has shown that only about 40% represent peak usage. We'll also still be unable to guarantee that guilds stay together.

  • More on Server Switches
    No, your current characters on Tallon will not be lost. Your Vallon characters will just be added to the account. Please keep in mind that if any of the characters have the same name, you'll end up with an X after the duplicate name.

  • Swapping Characters Between Servers
    It won't be possible to swap characters between the servers. If you register /movelog on both servers, all of your characters will end up on whichever one is done last. If they total more than 8, the ones over 8 (by alpha) won't be accessible until you delete other characters.

  • Strength
    Strength not only raises max damage, but average damage as well. 20 per hit in this case translates to a good deal more damage per minute than with lower stregth.

    Stats, all of the stats, and set up so they provide a reasonable advantage without becoming the "only" advantage.

  • EQG3 Question
    Just interested in hearing who from the boards here will be attending the gathering in Vegas in July. I heard yesterday that well over 300 people are signed up, which should lead to over 600 people attending if the walk-in percentage is anywhere near what the St. Louis Gathering's numbers were.

    I'm looking forward to putting some faces to the names here.
    --
    The website for the gathering is at http://eqgathering.bizland.com. Officially it's on Sunday July 9th, but there are other events going on that whole weekend.

  • Shaman and Charm Animal
    [Quote from original post: Shamen are not at one with nature, they already have a pet. What on earth was the rationale behind giving them Charm Animal?]

    Basically, shaman had charm for a while after release. We then removed it from them and gave them Befriend animal instead, based on the idea that they are a nature type moreso than not. When going through checking caps on certain spells in preparation for the launch of Kunark, we discoverd that Befriend Animal wasn't capped like other charm spells. So we capped it, leaving shaman unable to charm a lot of things that they could.

    Charm animal, the upgrade to Befriend, was given to them to give them a portion of that power back.

  • Skill Check on Tailoring: Robe of the Lost Circle
    I agree that the skill check is a pain. Let me see what I can do.

  • The View of a Warrior
    BosDragon made some excellent points in this post. I recommend it as a good read.

  • Add New Corpse Recovery Commands
    This issue comes up from time to time and as we've said, it is not a change that we are planning on pursuing at this time. When /corpse was introduced, it was put in to give people a way to get their corpse out of a wall or tree without requiring them to wait for customer-service assistance. The concept of "dragging" corpses around for people was not the original intent, however it did present another way for someone to interact with their fellow players, so we continued to allow the ability.

    Conning people into giving consent and then stealing their belongings is a very quick way to get an account canceled. We can't however guarantee that the victim will get their equipment back in these cases. This is a risk, and is the only risk involved. Like so many other things in the game, if the risk of getting your stuff stolen is too great, don't /consent someone. Players are fully responsbile for who they choose to /consent. Yes, we will pursue and ban people taking advantage of people in this manner, but players need to bear some responsibility for the choices they make.

    Do not consent someone who you do not trust.

  • More on Bannings
    [Quote from original post: What is the official procedure for suspensions and bannings including what avenues of appeal do you have when you feel that you have been unfairly punished . Thankyou in advance for your time]

    It depends on the specific situation.

    Depending upon what the person involved has done, there may or may not be a warning, or warnings issued prior to taking more severe action.

    Suspensions are documented and approved by a Lead. Bans are documented and approved by the head GM. In either case, word of the action is sent to the email address of record, the one they set within the EQ account data interface. The appeal route is different for each instance and is explained in the email that informs them of the action.

    I hope this answers everyone's questions. If you need more info, please let me know.

  • Root Spell Series Not Fixed?
    I checked entrapping, engorging, ensnaring, engulfing, and grasping last night. I didn't find any problem.

    The defined problem that was fixed was the spell was breaking at the time that it hit. This is evident when the spell break message is printed before the "feet become entwined" or similar message. This did not occur in any of my tests.

    The roots can still break quickly, and the occurance of that is not a bug as long as the break message occurs after the effect message.

  • A Court-System for Bannings
    [Quote from original post: Why not have in game trials for those that are being banned. GM's could send serverwide announcements with the name of the player pleading his case and randomly select people that are online asking them if they wish to be a juror.]

    I really don't think that people would go for the idea of other players (who are paying customers just like the accused) deciding their destiny. People often think that there is favoritism in the current system, I can only imagine that that feeling would be *supported* by doing something of this nature. Imagine it on a PvP server.

    The entire disciplinary process needs to be held to the highest standard of integrity. That cannot be done by turning it into a public spectacle where popularity would often determine outcome.

    I'd ask everyone to consider for a moment that we have *nothing* to gain, and everything to lose by banning someone unjustly. If we ban someone unjustly, we not only lose a good paying customer, but we lose everyone that could buy the game and play it based upon their recommendation. We also stand a good chance to lose current customers over the situation as well.

  • Seven Player Groups Popping Up
    It's a bug, and use of it is considered an exploit. We're looking into ways to fix it now.

  • Relaxed Stance for Female Models?
    I'm checking to see if there's a bug or if the animations do not exist. And yes, we've heard of the female bard animation issue as well :)

Gordon on Banning Policy
[18 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:04 PM PDT
The issue of speaking publicly about player bannings was raised again recently on the Gameplay Board. Here's the first post followed by Gordon's:

    Perception.

    It's all about perception.

    In just the last few minutes, I've seen a couple of nasty stories about "unfair" bannings.

    One about somebody who was given an item from a gm that he wasn't supposed to have. Maybe he should have questioned more about where the item come from, but did he know the other person was a gm? I honestly can't say I would have done anything different, looking a gift horse in the mouth and all, ESPECIALLY at a time when lots of people knew that drops in Kunark were much much better than they were supposed to be. (Ok, we didn't know they weren't supposed to be that way, but we did know they were much better than most things on the original continents)

    A second story is about somebody who claims to use a spawn timing method that I actually thought was quite ingenious, using only the tools that are given to you in the game. (/time and the command history, available using shift-up) Maybe the person was using a packet pusher, maybe they weren't.

    A third story is about somebody who ran around dragging a corpse behind them, probably using a hotkey for /corpse. (You did know you can do this, can't you?) Why provide the hotkeys if we're not meant to use them?

    Yes, all of these stories could be incorrect, and surely they're only one side of the story, but you know what? If the people reading this board only can see one side of the story, and that side sounds reasonable (which in all three of these cases, they do), guess what people are going to believe?

    If you have reasons to believe that the person with the timing method was using a third party program, then post them. If you have reasons to believe the person dragging their corpse was using a third party program, then post them.

    Cause from the player's perspective, right now you're starting to look more and more like ogres. (no offense meant to those of the larger, uglier races)

    I understand your opinion, but individual account issues will not be discussed in the court of public opinion. If the owner of the account wants to contact the Head GM and then post the communications, that is their choice to make those communications public.

    People can say anything that they like here in public about why they were banned. Sure, we could get in here and argue each issue, but doing that would be fruitless. There is no information that could be placed on this board that someone couldn't claim was "manufactured", leaving the other side with no further avenue to argue the issue, and having the whole thing devolve into a case of "Yes you did/No I didn't".

    -Gordon

    Player classes are not balanced in terms of PvP offensive power. What I mean by this is that we didn't throw each class in a pit and hold one on one battles in some effort to create equality. Classes are balanced primarily for Player versus Environment (PvE).

    We've had to make a few changes to the way things work in PvP in order to deal some balance issues that pop up. For example, in PvP, spells do 2/3rds damage versus another player. This is because the spells are designed to be effective versus NPCs, and are much too strong versus players who often have far less hitpoints than an equalently leveled NPC.

    June 6th, 8:00am
    -----------------

    *Patch Day*

    Greetings! The live servers have been updated to reflect the newest data on the Test Server:

    - Some new spells are available for the hybrid classes. Check your Kunark spell vendors.
    - Found and corrected a problem causing people to fall to their deaths upon zoning. The majority of these issues should no longer occur.

    *Items*

    - A problem with the spell effect for the Luminary Two-Handed Sword. Current owners of this weapon will need to contact a GM to trade their sword out for a working one.
    - The Gunthak Harpoon is now rentable. It will remain in your possession when you leave the game.
    - A couple pieces of Blood Ember armor have been reduced in weight.
    - Reduced the weight on several Monk weapons found in Kunark.

    *PvP Teams Servers (VZ/TZ) and Illusions*

    Due to an outstanding bug that allowed a player to permanently mesmerize or stun opposing team members when coupled with an illusion, illusions will now automatically cancel when the person wearing the illusion casts an offensive spell upon any player-character. Players should keep this in mind before attacking with spells in an area where their faction standing is less than desirable.

    Illusions are defined as any spell that makes a character look different than they actually are. This includes all racial illusions, as well as other illusions such as Treeform, Wolfform, and the Call of Bones series.

    *Risk and Reward*

    The concept of risk and reward is very important to the game of EverQuest. Ideally, situations that provide a greater reward should also provide substantial risk. Over the past several weeks, we've been identifying areas in game where risk and reward are at a disparity, and making necessary adjustments.

    In most cases, those adjustments are in favor of increasing the reward for a specific action. For example, we recently increased the experience awarded in many Kunark zones to offset the sometimes-considerable risk associated with adventuring there. We also do this quite frequently by increasing the reward associated with a quest by enhancing the item, as we did last week with the Burning Rapier quest.

    However, in some cases there are instances where reward is far in excess of the risk involved, and we will be correcting these issues like those above as we come across them. For example, there are many cases where a solitary and stationary outdoor creature has a "fast spawn", yielding the reward of fast experience-gain without the commensurate risk.

    Though it is not our policy to announce changes to individual NPCs, we did want to let you know that you will see risk/reward disparities corrected over the next several weeks.

    *PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Character Transfers*

    As many people playing upon the PvP-Teams servers are aware, Vallon Zek and Tallon Zek have different rules regarding looting rights from PvP combat. Vallon Zek allows the victor to loot all coin plus one item from the fallen, whereas Tallon Zek allows only coin-loot with no right to loot items.

You can register for the move by executing the "/movelog yes" command during the registration window. There is no limit to the number of people that we will allow to move between Tallon and Vallon Zek, so there is no reason to rush to enter the command.

    Please remember that once you register for the move, your decision can not be rescinded. The actual character-move will take place sometime next week, at a time to be announced later. Please keep in mind that corpses are not moved in a character-move, so we'd recommend against leaving unclaimed corpses around after Monday of next week if you've asked to be moved.

    Questions about the move process will be addressed in the Gameplay Message Forum available via www.everquest.com.

The most important aspect of a successful guild is their recruitment policies. Guilds should make sure that the guild and the player are compatible before allowing membership. Post-Hell Death Penalties: Bug or Design?
[19 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:12 PM PDT
While browsing through the pages of the Gameplay Board, I stumbled across the following post that Sony Online's Gordon Wrinn replied to:

    It is well known that there is double-exp loss associated with post-hell levels (31, 36, 41, 46). I suspect there is a bug somewhere, however; consider the following scenario:
    -----------------------------------

    A char is L44, and notices that when they die they lose about 1/2 bubble of exp. They make it to L45 (a proven 'hell' level, taking about 2x exp to get through) and when they die they see 1/4 bubble loss, which is expected since the bubbles represent double the exp of a normal level. They then level on to L46; when they die; they lose about 1 1/4 bubbles. When they make L47, exp loss is back to about 1/2 bubble per death.

    Here is the rub: if a char dies just after making L46, they will lose a full 1 1/4 bubbles in level *45*. Since L45 is a double-exp level, they have lost 2 1/2 bubbles of exp (half a level) with a single death. This 'quadruple penalty' seems to be a bug, as they lose much more exp in L45 if they die at the beginning of L46 than if they die in L45 itself.

    -----------------------------------

    So...bug or design? I understand that we should not be so concerned by numbers, but a half-level of exp in a single death seems *quite* severe.

    I see good reason to require more exp to go through the hell levels. However, I don't see why post-hell levels should have the extreme penalties for dying that they do. The penalties do not serve to keep groups of friends leveling at the same pace, but rather frustrate a single player who dies at the wrong time.

    Experience loss is calculated as a percentage of total experience, not the experience required for a current level. The number of bubbles that are lost really doesn't matter. This is just the way that the math works out. You don't lose a higher percentage of total experience, it just happens to be a higher percentage of experience required for the current level.

    -Gordon
