Sullon Zek: Watch Your Language
- Aurum @ 7:14 PM PDT
Alan popped this on the Developer's Corner as a reminder to players to watch their language on Sullon Zek:

    With the popularity of our new server, we are encountering a few problems as well. These problems revolve around the rules for the server.
    For those of you that are unaware of the rules for Sullon Zek, please read them here: http://everquest.station.sony.com/guidebook/sullonzek.jsp

    One portion of those rules that people seem to be misunderstanding is this:

    13. Rules such as the Play Nice Policy do not apply to this server. Characters have the ability on this server to deal with their conflicts through combat. Causing experience loss by 'training' NPCs on other characters is not an offense that would warrant a warning this server. HOWEVER: All rules regarding general decency still apply. Cursing, threats and other such things will be dealt with sternly, and are still unacceptable behavior on any server.

    Unfortunately, we have been forced to warn, suspend and banish a relatively high number of players (over 60 suspensions since the server opened) that have chosen to ignore the part of the rule after the bolded word HOWEVER.

    Remember, we will continue to enforce rules about common decency. We will not allow any sort of swearing or out of character racial slurs. We will not allow names that violate our naming policy (http://everquest.station.sony.com/support/policies/naming.jsp).

    Yes, we expect players on this server to duke it out for their team. But we still insist that you treat your fellow players with respect.

    - The EverQuest Team

Rune Event - Fennin Ro
- Aurum @ 7:32 PM PDT
Dynamite sent word of a new event on Fennin Ro:

Hail my fellow wizards of Fennin Ro!!

As you know, in past months, I have hosted a rune trade faire for
wizards, that we may help each other further our research and
development of spells. Last month I was not able to host the faire on
account of my writing my final exams, but this month the faire is on!

As in past months, the idea is simple: wizards of all levels meet
together and bring their runes. If you need a rune, ask for it, and if
anyone has it, they will give it to you. Conversely, if you have a rune
someone else is in need of, that you yourself do not need, then it is
expected that you shall give it to them freely. This has been a great
success in past months, and I'm looking forward to this month being even
better!

I have had requests to postpone the faire past this weekend as I am
told a number of guilds are having events that would coincide with the
faire were it held this weekend (I understand one guild is even having
an all-weekend scavenger hunt!), and even though that would mean putting
the faire past the end of the month, I see no reason why not to postpone
it. As such, the faire will be held at the following place, date, and
time:

Where: Toxxullia Wizard Spires
When: Monday July 2nd
Starting Time: 3PM PST (6PM EST)
Ending Time: When all is said and done, and we have finished having fun!

As always, I ask that if you ply a trade in which others may partake
of your wares and enjoy them, please feel free to bring some of your
fare with you! I myself brew, and always ensure I bring enough of my
wares to keep everyone happy. If you would care to bring some of your
own stock, or if you bake and would like to share your wares, then by
all means, bring them with you! These are always welcome!

While this is a faire geared primarily at wizards, I invite everyone
to attend. It's good fun and you can get to know your community better.
Since I started hosting this faire, I've gotten to know my fellow
wizards much better, and am constantly conversing with them... I like to
think that this has helped me gain many friends, and will help to gain
many more. For this reason alone, even if you aren't a wizard, or if
you're a wizard that cannot yet research, or has no more use of
research, you should attend anyway! Especially if you are beyond the
point that research can advance you, as you can help your fellow wizards
who are not quite as advanced as yourself research their spells. Higher
level wizards have always been at hand in the past to help younger
wizards research their spells, and there is no reason for this to change
now.

If you have any questions, suggestions, or comments, by all means,
either send me a tell in-game, or contact me at fenninrunes@hotmail.com,
and I will get back to you as soon as possible.

I am using the faire this month as a guage of sorts. I intend to use
its success in order to determine how popular the faire really is, and
whether it should continue. So the more people that attend, the more
likely it is that you'll see another faire! I hope one day to limit the
faire not only to wizards, but to all casters to come together and share
their research, help each other, and get to know each other in the
community.

I look forward to seeing you there this Monday, my friends, and I
hope this will be the greatest faire yet!!

Dynamite Shortfuze
Fennin Ro
Thanks!

    [Quote: ----
    Players who are invulnerable won't be the primary on the hate-list for NPC's any longer.

    Does this mean warriors will fall off of the hate list when they use DISCIPLINE OF FURIOUS against melee only mobs?

    Again, I urge you all to try it out, see how it works in game.

    Question: In MMORPG's such as Everquest (or in any of your other creations for that matter), I'm rather curious as to how much of the character's 'minds' arescripted or true AI. Sullon Zek Point System
- Aurum @ 8:23 PM PDT
This afternoon Alan announced initial plans for the Sullon Zek point system. Here's the scoop:

    Many of you know that we've been planning to add a point system to the deity team PvP server. The idea is to track the characters that are doing the most for their team, and to allow everyone to see what the activity is like on the server.

    We're working on that system now. The 'points' are already being counted, have been since the server went Live. Here's what we have in mind (and we'd appreciate any feedback on this).

    Points are only gained if the target/victim is 20th level or above.
    Points are only gained for killing characters within 5 levels of you.
    For each kill you earn 100 points.
    But for killing the same person a second time within a 60 minute period, you only gain 50 points.
    For the third kill on the same person in a 60 minute period you gain 25 points.
    For the fourth, you gain no points.
    For any over four against the same character in a 60 minute period you lose 25 points.
    Being killed by someone within 5 levels of you causes you to lose 25 points.
    Insignia are worth points as long as they are in your inventory or bank (anywhere in your character's possession.) when points are tallied.
    Lesser Insignia = 1 point
    Normal Insignia = 5 points
    Greater Insignia = 10 points
    Superior Insignia = 25 points

    Right now we are planning on daily or weekly updates (depending on the statistic being updated) of stats like the top 20 over all and for each team, perhaps the top 20 guilds over all and for each team.

    Keep in mind: This is all subject to change!

    If at some future date we feel that the point system is not working out, or if we decide to change the values of points gained or other such things, we will. Obviously we don't plan to make any changes once it is all established but keep in mind that this is an extraneous feature, a for fun thing, and it's not inviolate.

    So send in your comments and suggestions.

    Alan

Patch Message: 06-27-01
- Aurum @ 6:01 PM PDT
New message is up on the Patch Server:

    June 27th, 3:00 am
    ------------------------------


    ** Gameplay Changes **

    - Aggro Changes: We've made an adjustment to the way that NPCs choose their targets. It's a rather simple change, one that makes a lot of sense. But we want you to be aware of it so that it won't surprise you too much, and so that you won't think that it is a bug. NPCs are now much more aware of the vulnerability (or, more precisely, the invulnerability) of their targets. NPCs will now be very unlikely to waste their time attacking a target that they can't harm.

    - Spell and Item Interruption: Ducking will now interrupt a spell instantly. You will no longer be able to sit while using items or casting spells. This will not affect bard songs, which will still work as they always have, but will affect all items, even those with song-like effects.


    ** Spell Changes **

    - Skin Like Nature and Natureskin should stack with more spells now (though not with each other).

    - Aegolism can now be clicked off.

    - Imbue Ivory has had its casting level lowered to 29.

    - Shard of the Core will be tradable now.

    - Shroud of Hate and Visions of Grandeur should stack properly now.

    - Several new Translocation spells have been added.

    - Call of the Hero will no longer remove the target from NPCs' hatelists if the target is not summoned far enough.

    - Egress will now work indoors.

    - Wind of the North, Wind of the South, Markar's Relocation and Tishan's Relocation have had their additional restrictions removed, and can now be cast from any zone where other similar spells can be cast.

    - Summon Companion can now be used by Necromancers, Shamans, Enchanters, Shadowknights in addition to Magicians.

    - Deadeye should now stack with Dead Man Floating, but should not stack with Acumen.

    - Plainsight will now stack with Dead Man Floating, but not with Acumen.

    - The Great Divide portal spells are now 39th level instead of 44th level.

    - Spirit of the Oak will no longer be overwritten by any beneficial spells.

    - Paralyzing Poison spells should no longer be broken by direct damage spells.

    - Hsagra's Wrath and Porlos' Fury now have a 5 second recast delay.


    ** Item Changes **

    - The effect on the 10th ring should have fewer stacking conflicts with haste buffs.

    - The effect of the Monk epic, Celestial Tranquility, will stack with the higher end haste spells. Keep in mind - the highest haste will always win out. This means that the spells will stack, the haste components will not.

    - Speed of the Shissar has had its duration increased from 20 minutes to 30 minutes. This effect should no longer conflict with Call of Earth.

    - Bone Ring of Condemnation will no longer be usable in the secondary slot, it will only be wearable on the finger.

    - Druid Skyshrine Quest armor has had a color change so that it will look more unique.

    - Circlet of Shadow will no longer drop in-game. The Circlet of Shadows, can now be found in its place. This item differs from the original only in that it has a five-second casting time. Existing Circlets of Shadow will retain the same functionality as before, though we may change existing Circlets at some future date.


    ** Quest Changes **

    - King Tormax is now a little more particular about who he gives quests to.

    - The Iksar Shaman 6th Skull/cudgel quest should be working now.

    - Rumor has it that there is a new source for green dragon scales in Norrath.


    ** PvP Changes **

    - If you go link dead on a PvP server and are killed you can still be looted.

    - When a player becomes invisible (or hides successfully) every other player that had them targeted will lose their target. This is true for all servers, but has the greatest implications on PvP servers. The use of spells that allow you to see through invisibility will prevent you from losing your target if that target hides or goes invisible.

    - Faction hits on Rallos Zek: Killing a person in one of his or her home cities will cause a faction hit with residents of that city.


    ** Miscellaneous **

    - We've addressed a lot of spelling and grammatical errors over the past few weeks. For example we have corrected all misspellings of "deity" in messages to the player, changed Cantana to Cantata and others.

    - The 'Fab Four' in Dagnor's Cauldron will no longer assist Undertow Skeletons.

    - Instill Doubt/Intimidation can no longer be used while sitting, and sitting after starting it will cancel it.

    - Mastery of language skills now occurs at 100, not at 101. This was a problem for masters of a language, since language skills could not go over 100.

    - The messages for eating and drinking now say "Chomp... Chomp," or "Glug... Glug..." so that they can't be copied with emotes.

    - Helms on Barbarians, Trolls, and Ogres should no longer block the first person view when in combat.

    - Sirens Grotto has been changed a bit. It should be a more interesting zone now.

First Kill Of RiverSource - Tarew Marr
- Aurum @ 8:12 PM PDT
Dragons sent in a detailed report of the slaying of RiverSource on Tarew Marr. It was a huge battle so read up:

In the zone of the Warrens, Verant has added a mysterious new mob. It
is known merely as the "River Source". As the name implies, its an
invisible mob at the source of the river in the warrens. What made
this interesting was when a GM stated it had never been killed before.

Upon hearing that, The Syndicate
decided the mob had to be killed. Being as it was in the Warrens one
might assume it is a quick and easy midlevel kill. That is not the
case. Although the mob is a midlevel one and doesnt hit hard, it is
definitely not a quick kill. This mob appears to have more HP than any
other mob in game. It had anywhere from 1,000,000 HP to upwards of
5,000,000 HP. A constant beating for hours on end is what it took for
this mob to die. Thousands of HP damage per minute and yet it still
took hours to die. Movement of the health bar was imperceptible except
over time. Exact HP numbers were difficult to judge over that length of
time of constant attacking.

Oddly, when it died it left no corpse and a new one spanwed nearly
instantly. It is unlike Verant to add a mob that serves no purpose.
There must be some purpose for this strange mob with a million or more
HP. What that purpose is we dont yet know. Here is a screencap of the
River Source during the attack:
