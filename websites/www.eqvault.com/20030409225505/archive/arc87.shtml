Gordon Infobits: Sunday Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:39 PM PDT
Gordon "I don't need vacations or weekends off" Wrinn tackled a few new subjects in recent posts made today on the Gameplay Board. Here's the lowdown:

  • What is Gordon?
    What am I? Nerfed liver?


  • "Thorough File Check" Violation of Personal Rights?
    [Quote from original post: The program is more than likely doing a full CRC of every file in the EQ directory, and checking it with a server version of the CRC. The complexity of the check is likely the cause, and it could also be your computer's speed]

    Indeed. Pressing the button issues a full CRC check of every file on the required files list for EverQuest and downloads a new version if the local and remote CRCs differ. We found that a few people were suffering some file corruption that wasn't being noticed by our normal method of version control, so implemented this method for those cases.

    [Quote from original post: Starwars... whats going on?]

    Can't really say due to our agreement with LucasArts. However, we are planning on begining a co-sponsored information release program in the near future. We're still working the details out with our contacts over there, but we should be able to start giving out some info soon.

    [Quote from original post: goto bed your supposed to be on vacation]

    Well not so much vacation as it is "home for the weekend". My wife works nights (nurse) on the weekends and goes to school during the week. So I'm really just passing the time :)


  • New Armor (Click link to see pic)
    Mmmmmm... Nice new armor....


  • Thoughts on the 3D Engine
    [Quote from original post: Since the graphics engine should be entirely abstract from any gameplay and networking (for the most part) issues why cant the engine simply be upgraded and the old engine still be available for those who use lesser hardware]

    It's not so much "can't" as it is "can't feasibly be done with reasonable time and resources". The art used in EverQuest represents nearly 4 years of work for a team of artists. Going through and revamping all of EQ proper with updated textures would take roughly half that time for a full team. Now, if you are talking about a completely new engine, it would then take additional man-years of work by programmers.

    New zone geometry, though not being possible for a platform that has to be backward compatible for people using the current engine, would have to match up in every way at every coordinate, so there wouldn't be much of a benefit there either.

    Basically, it's not feasible because by the time we'd get EQ rebuilt in the suggested fashion, all of the next-generation games would be out, and we'd have an old game with a fresh look. We'd rather, when that time comes, have a new game with a fresh look.


  • Necros and Lifetaps
    The resistability or lack thereof of any spell is subject to the immunity of the target. In other words, even the "nearly unresistable" lifetap isn't going to work against a creature that is immune to magic.


  • VI = Very Irresponsible?
    [Quote from original post: My compnay let us off early on Friday for the long weekend, so I went home and played some Everquest before dinner ]

    My company let us off early for the weekend as well :) I believe that the only patch that went up was a new patch program to enable some more verbose file checking.

    Previously, we'd heard of some people experience some file corruption problems that aren't caught by the standard version-control procedure. This patch gives an option for a more thorough check (a full CRC check) on the installed files. In other words it should not longer be necessary for tech support to recommend a full re-install to people who are reporting strange problems.

    The patch shouldn't at all affect gameplay, so there's really no issue with the timing for when it was put up. I'm sorry you are upset, but did this patch reduce your ability to play the game at all?


  • "What could we do to improve your game experience?" Poll Question Suggestion
    The poll question you suggest is pretty broad and would be hard to cover in a multiple-choice poll. Of course we could open it up to text, but that's basically what these boards are used for.


  • Quest Issue
    [Quote from original post: This would be fine by me if Hasten were not a PH for Brother Zephyl. Both of these mobs are required for major quests, they should not be placeholders for each other.]

    I do understand the desire to complete both quests as soon as one has all of the quest items for either. That said it's not really something that we're planning to change. People are completing both quests, and the items to be garnered from either are nice enough to warrant an added level of difficulty.


  • Boats
    Even though he posted it in jest, Dranthor's reply is pretty much right on target. The length of the boat rides are indeed related to adding some realism to the process. If we'd wanted something akin to teleports from one continent to the other, we would have went that route. The second thing about class-interdependence is also right on target. If the boat rides are too long, you have a method at your disposal to reduce the amount of time taken for travel.

The End of the Smurfs?
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:58 PM PDT
While mostly focusing on Ultima Online's volunteer policies, I thought it might be interesting to point out AVault's recent article on the effects an AOL lawsuit is having on the MMORPG genre. For those who haven't been following it, this exceprt will help bring you up to speed:

    No one is more reliant on volunteer labor than the massively multiplayer online games. The three largest online fantasy role-playing titles, EverQuest, Ultima Online and Asheron's Call, use them as their first line of customer service. But maybe not for long. On Monday, August 28, representatives of Ultima Online's makers, Origin Systems Inc. (OSI), announced the termination of free game account privileges for hundreds of community volunteers. In a letter to the community, external support coordinator Matt Gutierrez wrote: "Due to changes in the industry regarding volunteers, effective Sept. 30, 2000, OSI will no longer provide free player accounts or other added value to members of all [Ultima Online] Volunteer Programs."

    While company representatives have not said so outright, it appears the move to eliminate what amounted to a $10 a month gratuity for volunteers is related to a recent New York class action lawsuit, brought by former volunteers at America Online (AOL). The plaintiffs are claiming that AOL's practice of relying on what it calls "community leaders" to produce, maintain and moderate online content was tantamount to employment, and hence is covered by American minimum wage laws. (Origin's parent company, Electronic Arts, has exclusive rights to AOL game content.)

  • Recipes
    The recipes themselves are indeed the simplest part of the process. It is the many factors that play into balance and any extra coding that needs to be done that is the most time consuming. Once the properties, economic values, availability and difficulty of the product and its components plus the impact of the final product on existing items are determined the recipe table can be produced and placed for testing. This is why some content with very low balancing issues can be implemented quickly and others, blacksmithing for example, with high balance issues are very time consuming. For instance, the silk thread, bandages, small bag, and silk cord, all of which had extremely minimal balance issues were placed on test server the same day I saw them on the wishlist, but of course for them to appear on the live servers they must go through testing and wait for an available patch.
