New Patch Message Up
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 10:02 AM PDT
News on the Karana/Bertoxxuolous server splits can be read off the most recent message posted on the EQ Patch Server. Here's the complete message:

    July 5th, 4:00pm
    -----------------

    *Karana, Bertox, and Test Server Split*

    Hello all,

    At some time between 6pm and 8pm PDT (GMT-8), we will open /movelog registration on Karana, Bertox, and Test, to allow players on those servers to request that their account be moved to the new split-server, Terris-Thule. Players on the affected servers will be informed via server broadcast when registration is open. Players wishing to have their characters moved to the new split server will need to run the command "/movelog yes" after registration is opened.

    Notes:

    - /movelog only needs to be run from ONE character, and will count for all characters on that server on that account.
    - Players who have requested a move should make sure all of their corpses are looted by the time the server is brought down tomorrow morning (9am PDT). Corpses will not be duplicated in the new world, and anything left on a corpse left behind by anyone who moves will be lost.
    - We may or may not be able to move everyone who requests this action, based upon the number of total requests that we receive.
    - The GM/Guide staff will not be able to tell you whether or not you have been selected until after the move is actually completed.
    - Test Server Players with artificially buffed characters on their account *MUST* delete those characters prior to registering for move. Having artificially buffed characters moved to the live servers will result in action against the player's account.


    *Item Changes Pending in Next Patch*

    In the next patch, keys for Charasis, Sebilis, and Howling Stones will all be made NODROP if not currently set with that flag. This means that people holding more than one key will not be able to hand out their extras after the patch.

    At the same time that this change is made, we are also implementing a new change that "binds" these items to your character. Should you die with one of these keys in your possession, it will not be left on your corpse, but will stay with you when you return to your bindpoint.

    This change was made in light of the difficulty of the key-quests, in order to eliminate the need to do the quest multiple times prior to entering the dungeon.

    -The EverQuest Team


The Life of an MMORPG
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 10:00 AM PDT
Gordon spoke about the "finite" life of EverQuest and a little about the history and future of the genre in the following thread found on the Gameplay Board:

    i know you can cite the number of accounts verant currently has. but what are you going to do when EQ finally has competition? you do realize how many customers you are going to lose dont you? is the company policy "rake it in while we can"?

    How many are we going to lose? That's hard to say. We all know that EQ has a finite life. Newer games will come along that will grab peoples attention, just like we've grabbed the attention of the current players. This is how the gaming industry works. You grab their attention and keep it as long as you can.

    I don't think that this qualifies as a "rake it in while we can" policy, at least not in a negative sense. We provide a service offering and people decide whether or not they want to be our subscribers. This service offering is flexible, but only to the point that it remains the same offering. Given infinite resources, sure, we have tons of things that we could do for EQ, but will only do those things that make the game "EQ". We won't risk completely changing gameplay to the point that we'd lose the magic that is responsible for EQ's success.

    But as for us not having "competition", we currently enjoy about half of the marketshare among those playing the 3 MMORPGs out there. That's fairly indicative of some current competition. If you recall, EQ was supposed to "Kill" the top MMORPG of the time (UO), and was supposed to be "Killed" by the next (AC). Niether of those things happened, as we all continue to co-exist peacefully. We each provide a niche that a varying and increasing number of subscribers enjoy.

    -Gordon

    -Gordon

    Why do we even have the option to walk? Everyone agrees that the toggle should be set to RUN as a default, but why do we even have the option to move at a slower speed?

    -Gordon


Illusions & NPCs
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:57 PM PDT
How do NPCs see through illusions and can tell what deity you worship? This question was addressed in the following post Gordon Wrinn made:

    Illusions hide your true race only. They do not hide your complete faction (race, class, deity, and what you've done). Though it might make more sense for it to completely hide your true identity, this is one case where the need for balance outweighs realism.

    Without limitations to how well illusions can work, factions would be meaningless for the classes capable of using them.

    -Gordon

"The Source of Verant's Problems"
[30 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:18 PM PDT
I spotted a new reply Gordon posted on a thread titled The Source of Verant's Problems on the Gameplay Board. Here are excerpts from the original post along with Gordon's response:

    Now Abashi has stated that rogues are "the necromancers of melee" and later clarified that he means that they are on the upper end of the balance scale, which would seem to indicate that he considers necromancers to be nearly perfectly balanced

    Actually, no. I would consider necromancers to be "on the virge of being overpowered". By "overpowered" I do not speak of pure offensive power, but everything that makes up a class that figures into the balance equation. "Perfectly balanced" lies somewhere between "overpowered" and "underpowered" in this sense.

    By saying that rogues are the "necros of melee", I was not speaking of versitility nor solo capability, but rather that rogues are on the virge of being overpowered every bit as much as necros are. The original vision for rogues was more of a "thief" than they are now. However inability to fully implement some of the skills we had planned (sense/disarm traps), we decided to fit them more into an "assassin" role.

    Now since they could not fit Verant's niche monks invented a new, valuable and effective niche for themselves and became EQ's master pullers, bringing themselves back into balance with the EQ's environment

    I've taken quite a bit of heat from some players about some comments that I made about not liking feign-pulling. Quite simply, when feign-pulling, a monk uses a skill that we created for something other than its purpose. Feign Death was supposed to be an escape skill, not a skill that allowed easier breaking of camps. Also consider that feign-pulling doesn't make sense from a roleplay perspective (we like it when things make sense in this fashion). Can you see a creature saying, "I'M MAD AT HIM, I'M GOING TO KI...wait... he's dead now.. I guess I'll go ba...AH HA! HE'S NOT DEAD, I'M GOING TO KI...wait...no... dead again....(ad nauseam)"

    Verant needs to realize that they have not created the perfect gaming environment, and therefore need to alter their perception of some of the classes and bring them in line with the world they have created, not the world they had envisioned.

    We're well aware that the game isn't perfect. Afterall, what game can be? That said, what good would we do as game designers if we did not have some idea for the way we wanted the game. People will continue to make the decision as to whether the game we offer is one that they are willing to play.

    -Gordon

Hybrid Experience Penalty

Gordon spoke on the hybrid experience penalty in a recent response he posted to the Gameplay Board. Here's the thread:

    The hybrid experience penalty

    1. Based on your statements hybrids receive more experience than group members for the same kill due to their experience penalties.

    2. Based on your statements you lose experience based on your total experience points at the time.
    Therefore logically you go by a percentage of your total experience. If this is true a lvl 60 half elf ranger who has about 55% more experience as a lvl 60 halfing warrior (about 780 million Vs about 430 million - dont have exact figures in front of me) would lose more experience between the two.

    3. Upon Res the half elf ranger would receive numerically more exp points back but would still have lost (numerically) more experience and therefore is basically penalized twice upon death.

    Tell me my logic is flawed Abashi

    If it is flawed tell me where please
