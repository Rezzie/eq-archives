"Exciting" New Changes Announced
[15 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:30 PM PDT
Gordon made a big announcement this morning on the Gameplay Board regarding new changes coming to EverQuest. Here's the post:

    Hello all,

    I thought I'd drop a line and let everyone know about some exciting changes that we are working on:

    First, the next EQ client will, unless a problem is discovered in testing, include a slash-command that will allow players to turn off display of corpses. This will be a client-side option that will just repress the rendering of the corpses themselves. This will allow corpse-related actions (like sense-corpse) to be taken even while corpse display is repressed.

    The command (/hidecorpses) will have three possible parameters:

    None - Shows all corpses
    All - Hides all corpses but that belonging to the player.
    All but group - Hides all corpses but those belonging to the player and group members.

    As asking people "Has anyone seen my corpse?" is an issue that we feel builds the feeling of community and encourages people to help each other out, the command will reset to showing all corpses upon zoning. Also, as a note, in its current implementation, any corpses created after the command is run will be shown.

    This command should help people who experience framerate problems after zoning in to areas known to cause a large number of corpses.

    The second change has to do with quested keys for entrance into the keyed dungeons on Kunark: Sebilis, Charasis, and Veeshan's Peak. It's apparent that the risk of dying in these zones is too great to risk going with only one key. Furthermore, it's become standard practice for people to leave a corpse at the entrance of these dungeons with the spare key.

    As we feel that the idea of dueling and getting killed just to leave a corpse for a bank isn't in the best intrest of roleplaying or our vision for the game (e.g. It's cheesy), we've decided to nerf this practice Our plan is to have keys for these three dungeons stay with the owner when they return to their bind point. This new ability that we are working on also gives us the opportunity to explore "soulbound" items in the future, but no promises just yet on that.

    -Gordon
Gordon made a second post in this thread clearing up an issue one of the players had with his original post:
    that when you die in sebelis that the key to sebelis (and charlis, and howling) will stay with
    you? That way only one key is needed?

    Judging by the responses, I apparently didn't explain it very well.

    When you die, instead of appearing at your bind point naked, you'll appear there with your key. So yes, only one key would be needed, and there'd be no reason to kill yourself off outside the dungeon to hold your key.

    -Gordon

Quest on the E'ci Server
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:08 PM PDT
An event announcement was sent to me by Eric:

I will be running a GM type quest with pretty good loot as rewards for those
who complete it in the given time period. This is just a test to see if
there is an interest in this type of thing, and depending on the outcome of
it, I may run others in the near future. This quest will have no entry fee,
but for me to boost the quality level of the items I will probably charge a
nominal fee per person/party that joins if this continues beyond the test
run. If you aren’t interested in role-playing and would prefer not to take
part in this, there is no need to read any further. For those of you who
would like a break from the hum drum beating on mobs in the eternal search
for that next level, please read on. Also, for anyone who has problems with
me using Freeport as the city name and Lucan as the boss, please forgive me.
It is a must though because using the word “city” and labeling the guards as
he/she/they throughout the tale would have lost a lot. This is however open
to any race though some may find it more difficult than others:

You feel as though your lungs will burst if you take another step, but
fearing your pursuers are still on your tracks you dare not rest. How could
this have happened to you? Once a respected citizen of Freeport, your name
has been disgraced and your own city has turned on you. As you escape the
boundaries of the city and look around you, you see that the guards dare not
pursue you further. Gasping for breath you think back…

Just two nights ago while enjoying a healthy cup of mead you were approached
by a sinewy man that claimed that he knew you. Having had perhaps a bit too
much ale, you humored the rather sly looking creature and asked him if it
was your fame or you good looks that he had heard about. Apparently not
getting the joke, he came closer and said, “You are the one. The one that
was foretold.” He spat at your feet and walked off, but turned with a
menacing glare and said, “Don’t think you can live out your prophecy, for I
know your heart well and will see you burned before I allow you to destroy
us!” then disappeared into the darkness of the night. You shared a laugh
with the bartender and asked what the mad man was having because you could
use a cup or two…

The encounter seemed odd, but hardly worth wasting one’s time contemplating
as angry and bitter people frequent bars as much as hardy adventurers. One
deluded soul was unlikely to be the turn of your fate and he was as likely
as not passed out in a ditch somewhere by now anyway. Sadly, you could not
have been more wrong…

As you left the bar severely intoxicated, you were greeted by the local
militia. “Stop right where you are,” one of the guards demanded. Being in
no shape to argue you froze. “Is this the man that you saw?”

A voice you have never heard before but that made your skin crawl to listen
to it stated, “Yes, he is the one. Arrest him before he brings anymore harm
to the people of this town!” With that you began to feel the world shift
underneath you as you were grabbed, thrown around, beaten, and finally taken
into custody. As you awoke the next day you could hear the screams of those
who had been placed in the “care” of the Freeport militia before you. Still
a bit hazy from the night before you screamed for a guard…. No response.
Laying back against your cell door a bit exhausted and for some reason in
pain, you drifted off again.

TAP TAP TAP

Awakened with a start, you found a guard bouncing the flat of his sword
against your head to get you off the bars, “Listen up prisoner… I
personally thought we should have killed you for what you have done when we
found you, so get out of the reach of my sword before it slips and hurts
someone! We don’t take kindly to kidnappers here. Tomorrow when Lucan
holds your trial, you can expect to meet with swift punishment, so you
better enjoy this fine cuisine like it was your last!”

“Kidnapping?”

“Heh, yeah your damn right. Kidnapping the child was worth slaying you
over, but executing the rest of the household will insure that you hang.”

“Wait, but I didn’t do anything!”

“Yeah, yeah… That’s what they all say. If I had a copper for every time I
heard that line I wouldn’t be working here! Sweet dreams kidnapper. Take
whatever spiritual precautions you feel the need to now, because in the
mornin’, you’re going to meet justice.”

The night came and went, but this time it wasn’t spent passed out or in a
stupor. By daybreak you had filed away the bars and were well on your way
out of the city. Such things as missing bars seem not to have gone
unnoticed though as you picked up a large train of pursuers and barely made
it out with your life. Thus you have ended up here…

While your life is safe for now, your name has been destroyed. But by who?
And what would they stand to gain by destroying you? Could this all have
been coincidence? That seems very doubtful. Clearly someone has made an
attempt on your life, and the only way to meet such an assault is with the
proper defense of your name and deeds.

Where shall you begin this trek though? Freeport is not open to you and
would likely cost you your head if you returned. Perhaps sending another to
scout out the information… No, this is your fight and you don’t need to
bring anyone else down this dark road. There is one who would be able to
help… A sagely monk by the name of Cheng Meng’te is known to reside deep to
the south beyond Oggok and perhaps he would be able to see with his
clairvoyance who the cause of this great evil against you was. With that,
you set out determined to bring your name justice, and to slay those that
would attempt to have you slain you no matter the cost!

When: Friday, July 7 at 8:00 PM EST is when this quest will begin and it
will last until Sunday 8:00 PM that same weekend.

Where: E’ci West Freeport near the wagons/vendors is where the quest will
begin but you may find that the travel involved in completing this quest is
in abundance. Don’t worry, at no point will I hand you a note and tell you
to take it to the Bard’s guild in such and such zone, so this is NOT like a
regular EQ quest.

Who: You are allowed to do this quest with 1 – 5 players. Please, no more
than 5 as depending on how far into the quest you get it may require that I
add one of my own characters or a friend’s into your group.

Cost: NOTHING but your time. There are prizes along the way, and prizes
for finishing the quest, but all I ask of you is that you enjoy the ride.

Requirements: Evil races will run into a bit of a problem with a few of the
encounters and may want to bring a high level friend or two to help defend
them from beatings. Be prepared to duel. Almost all combat involved in
this quest will involve other PCs and for that reason when one prompts any
member of your group with a /duel you must accept it. No waiting around for
buffs, no healing up before you accept. You should have done those prior to
the encounter. If you at any point find the urge striking you to /duel
someone, feel free to. These characters for the sake of this quest are NPCs
and if you prompt to attack them, then they will accept and the battle will
proceed from there.

Rewards: Rewards are based on a few things. The primary deciding factor is
how far you get in the quest. Some will not reach the objectives at all,
some will complete a portion of it and assume they are done, and others will
complete the whole quest. All I can suggest for completing this quest is BE
ALERT. It isn’t over until the fat lady sings. You will receive rewards at
the end of the quest based on the speed with which you accomplish it, and
the fun that you provided along the way. Role-play if you want to get
anywhere.

Registration: Please inform me ahead of time if you are interested and we
will prepare for you. All registry will actually take place at the actual
meeting place in West Freeport on Friday though and at that point you will
give me a group name (doesn’t have to be creative. Could be group X for all
I care) and a group leader which will actually be the person the above story
describes and thus the person that the entire quest revolves around. If you
choose to go solo, that is OK as well, but you will still need a group name
for referencing you when you reach certain spots.

NOTIFICATION: I know that only a small percentage of the population of E’ci
reads this board, so please notify your friends if you have any that are
interested in this sort of adventure. NOBODY at any point should share
information with other groups as YOU are the one on this quest and all other
groups are not any of your concern since as far as your group is concerned

all your tales about it at 8:00 on Sunday rather than during the actual

Thanks for reading this lengthy post and for those of you who do take part,
I hope you like it.

    Players from Test and Torvonnilous
    [11 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:51 PM PDT
    Gordon announced some big changes for those Test Server players transfered to the newer servers over the last couple of days. Here's the announcement:

      Hello all,

      Many of you are probably familiar with the situation that resulted in characters from Test being moved to Torvonnilous and other servers. For those who are not, let me do a quick recap:

      A few weeks ago we did a character wipe on the Test server in order to cleanse the testing platform. After doing this wipe, it was apparent that many people on the server did not have a reasonable expectation that this was a possibility. As a result, in the interest of providing good customer service, we decided to back out the player-wipe, change it to an item-wipe, and give the players on Test an option to move to one of the production servers.

      During this process, some mistakes were made on our part. In these plans, we neglected to place several “exceptions” for valid destinations for Test characters. We moved around 20 characters to Torvonnilous, a “young non-split” server. People often start on new non-split servers because they want a fresh environment. There is often a “race”, so to speak, shortly after the server comes up to be the first character or group to reach high levels, get good equipment, and do the dragons and planes. There are many people who enjoy this fresh environment as it gives them the opportunity to become a well-known and respected person on their server.

      It was inappropriate for us to inject established higher-level characters into the young environment on Torvonnilous. Transfers of this type, though not particularly relished by the people on the destination servers in general, should be limited to established and “older” servers, including split-servers, that are not in the midst of establishing their “server community”. A well-established community on older servers can take an injection of new people without a harmful effect upon that community, however the community on the younger servers is fragile and deserves protection.

      In a previous message on the subject that I posted earlier today, I commented that it was “too late” to address this issue. However, for the reasons above, we’ve modified our decision in regard to the Test move. We will be removing the characters from Test that were moved to Torvonnilous, and placing them back on Test for the time being. Owners of those characters should be contacted by email in the near future.

      We’d like to thank everyone for their patience and understanding in this matter.

      Thank you,

      Gordon Wrinn
      Internet Relations Manager
      Verant Interactive, Inc.

As I've mentioned before, this doesn't mean that we won't create some at some point, but it's not as high on the list at the moment as some other issues that we are addressing.

      And yes, I do understand the desire to have a different appearance as has been mentioned here on multiple occasions.

For every server that we open to the split, we dilute the possible effect. For instance, if we take 1000 people off of each of two servers, and that reduces peak load by 300 on each of those servers, the change is noticible.     Posion Q&A
    [4 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:29 PM PDT
    Gordon Wrinn cleared up a few misconceptions about the posion upgrade on the Gameplay Board today. Here's what he said:

      The "system" of poison itself wasn't changed, and I apologize if people out there thought that it would be. What there is is a greater variety of poisons, which are cheaper, and have more plentiful components. I've gotten a number of emails today asking various quesitons:

      Q: Why aren't the poisons themselves stackable.
      A: The item system does not allow items with effects to be stackable. Alcohol doesn't apply, as it's covered with item-specific code. We cannot feasibly add item-specific code for poisons and potions due to technical concerns.

      Q: Why don't poisons have multiple charges?
      A: This is a function of the item engine again. They cannot have multiple charges.

      Q: Why don't poisons last through zoning?
      A: The game engine doesn't allow it, and spell-code doesn't apply.

      Q: Why do poisons take time to apply?
      A: Balance. It's necessary for it to take time so that the effects aren't used multiple times in the same combat encounter without stepping out of combat. Poisons are meant as a pre-combat preparation. Keep in mind that rogues are extremely powerful as it is.

      Q: Why can poisons be interrupted?
      A: Game engine again. To be honest, I'd like to see the chances of this reduced significantly. I'll look into seeing what we can do.

      -Gordon

    • Shaman Root Nerfed?
      It hasn't been modified, changed, or otherwise nerfed. Bad rolls can happen from time to time. NPCs also have a chance of rolling for better than normal stats (including resists) at character creation.

      Since we get accused of nerfing root every few weeks, I'll promise you that if we do, we'll let you know.

      [Quote from original post: Here's something you don't see very often on this board -- a complaint that something is too easy to kill.]

      Hehe, yeah, that is fairly new :)

    • More on Backing Up Characters
      Well it was supposed to be 90 days, but someone put 6 months in the FAQ, so I guess it's six months now :) In any case, as I've mentioned before, we really haven't deleted any inactive characters at this point. It's just an option that we have open as a matter of technical prudence.

    • "Will we ever have a totally no drop server?"
      In a word, no. We believe that a player-driven economy is of paramount importance in a massively multiplayer game.

    • Will roleplaying get you banned?
      Teerog's explanation is about as simple as it can get. Roleplaying crosses the line, so to speak, when the actions of one character start to negatively impact the gameplay experience of the user of another character.

      As an example, refusing to group with dwarves is fine. Following an Erudite around using real-world racial slurs is not.

    • Guildwar/Duel Experience Loss
      [Quote from original post: Just a question. Are there any plans to address this in the near future (DoT deaths, bleeding to death, etc)?]

      We have no short term plans to address this issue further at this time. It's caused by the core architecture and how damage from indirect sources is calculated. We've "fixed" the issue on the PvP servers (where most PvP takes place) by eliminating experience loss from all indirect sources, such as from the environment (falling, drowning, etc.)

      On the PvE servers, experience loss is still a possible outcome from a PvP encounter. I should stress however that intentionally causing XP loss during PvP encounters is frowned-upon by the GM staff.

    • High Level Problems
      I just wanted to put some things in perspective here:

      [Quote from original post: You are now at a level where you are cut off from an entire section of the game.]

      You are cut off from participating directly in combat for 2 NPCs.

There will always be other, in fact far more, dragons that other higher level folk can attack.

      Secondly, we keep characters for inactive accounts a minimum of 6 months.
