He IS refering to Guide (volunteers from player base) and GM (paid Verant employees) characters only. When you apply to be a guide, you must list the servers you play on. When you are accepted, you are assigned to a server other than one you play on and are forbidden to have any play characters on that server. Guide and GM characters are not allowed to be part of a player guild on that server. Guides play characters are allowed to be in player guilds. When not being a guide you are a normal player with everything that comes with it (yes including getting the same frustrating answers from guides that you hate giving players yourself). Most GM's that I've met play on the Test server. There are actually a few all guide/gm guilds on the Test server that are "normal" characters (they are not empowered with the guide/gm commands).

When you are a guide, you are not supposed to tell anyone that you are. The mechanics of the chat room make that impossible for any guide who has a play character in a higher lvl guild that uses chat to coordinate events. Guides are given blue names in chat, and cannot turn that off. Verant says, that when in chat, you are on-duty and to be answering questions. They are currently working on a solution to that so guides may enter the chat room as players and not strictly guides.

Synic, the Guides (not GM's) were removed from the guide program about a month ago for blatantly violating the rules of being a guide. I was still in the program when that happened and bore witness to the whole thing. They were suspended just long enough to have Verant remove the guide characters from their accounts. They kept their play characters.
