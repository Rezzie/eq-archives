Rights, under the current gaming environment, are not that important. However, virtual games are only the first step. This is what the author was trying to say in the end of the document. What happens when your interaction with the physical world is only possible by your interaction with the virtual world? At some point, the phrase "virtual reality" will be interchangeable with the phrase "world interface". It is already happening. Credit/debit systems, billing networks, atms, hospital records, social security numbers, etc. All of these things are characteristics of the digital primordial ooze which will eventually evolve into complex entities in the not-so-distant future.

Or even the telephone company? I realize it is a stretch but it is related. They provide a service which we have an investment in maintaining. Someday soon these issues will be a lot more complex and inter-related.
