New File: EQ Log Splitter v1.1
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 5:54 PM PDT
EQ Log Splitter v1.1 (250KB) us now available in our File section. Here's a clip from the readme file:

    Fix/addition: Ability to track money received on a per character basis, either cumulative or per session.

    This program was designed to separate an Everquest log file into one log file per character.

    As long as your character was online long enough to be "saved", it should work okay.

    The new files will have the original filename plus "for " plus the character name.

    Simply place this executable anywhere you like, wherever you keep your eq log files is obviously the easiest.
    A DLL will be extracted the first time this program is run, and a simple INI file will be created.
    No other changes will be done to your system.

    This program is a bit slow, but works great as far as it's been tested.

    It appends to any preexisting files, so if you run it twice on the same log file each character file will have the same bits twice.
    I thought about putting separators when a character is restarted, but I decided against it unless someone particularly wants it.
Enjoy!

Gordon Infobits
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:18 PM PDT
Here's the latest posts made by Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • Zone Names
    We've no plans to change the file names for the zones (which is what controls what's in the /who all list). Some of the earlier zones did use a strange naming convention, but for the most part, most of them are pretty close to it's RP name. The strange ones are easily memorized after a bit of play.

    qey2hh1 is West Karana, qeytoqrg is Qeynos Hills, qrg is Surefall Glade. qcat is Qeynos Catacombs.

  • Top 10 Biggest Improvements in EQ
    Thanks Hooml2, I've got a few myself off the top of my head. Not sure where'd I'd fit them in:

    Rogue hide/sneak vs. living and dead
    Rogue evade
    Shrink
    Clarity
    Sit-n-Med
    Halfed experience loss on death
    Additional HP regen for characters based on class and level.
    HP's upped for a bunch of classes
    /corpse
    Contextual randomization of Kunark loot.
    Emote anims.
    Jewelrycraft revamp (my vote for "EQ's Best Tradeskill")
    Ok, I'm tired now. I have a complete list at the office, but not every change, even if for the better, could fit into the top 10.

  • Test of Mystics
    [Quote from original post: Your pet doesn't go poof like that. You can bet as an enchanter I'd summon my pet and Aanya's him. Of course, if it is held in the arena, pet suicides anyway.]

    Not any more, we ninjaenhanced it :)

  • Another Broken Quest?
    I believe this quest was reported in the "Broken Quests" thread started by QwinnofTunare. One of our testers is all over that thread and hand-carrying the issues there up to the designer who can fix it.

  • Gordon on Huge Wizard Thread
    Before I close the thread due to its size, did want to make a small comment:

    I'm sorry all, but the wizard issues brought up in this post and others have been addressed in the past. Wizards are not broken, and I am indeed sorry if you do not agree with our stance.

  • Rez Bug
    Though there was a problem a few months ago that had GM resurrections (and all other resurrections for that matter) returning less experience than they should, it was corrected.

    I do apologize to those who had to deal with the Rez bug, even when getting rez'd for a bug.

  • Crescent Armor in Kunark
    Armor in Kunark was typically only made NODROP if it was to be used as a component in a quest. Now I don't know off the top of my head if that is what was planned for this armor, but I'll look into it on Monday.

    The designers of Everquest had the best intentions when creating the world

  • I lost my identity!
    These problems seem to run in spurts.

    I've coordinated with the Head GM on the issue and your first name will be changed back. Please accept our apologies for the incident.

  • Bugged Deaths
    There are a couple of issue. Please keep in mind that in order to do a rez for a bug death, the GM or Guide in question has to be able to verify that that is what occured, so something like this wouldn't result in a rez.

    Secondly, this bug itself wouldn't result in a rez even assuming that it was verified. NPCs attack. They attack without warning and provocation. The fact that an NPC somewhere had you on the hatelist, but was too far away to consider chasing you, and was propagating that hatelist to new NPCs that spawned who *were* in range to chace after you, is a design issue that players just have to deal with at the moment. Everything that is happening in this case is *intended*, it just *sucks*. Sometimes things that "suck" are described as bugs, but aren't bugs in sense of something that we can fix readily, or that we would give a rez for.

    For example, NPCs aggroing through walls/ceilings/ and floors in some cases suck. It's not a bug. It's not something that we can fix without breaking a ton of other things. It often becomes a known danger of an area, and therefore a risk that is assumed for fighting there. Yes, as I said it sucks. Death happens.

    In nearly 40 days of playtime that I've put in since August of last year, I died 3 times due to something out of my control. Twice was by disconnections, traced to my ISP, and once was to an honest to god bug, where I was feared by an NPC and promptly fell for 10K.

  • Hunting Solo Spawns
    [Quote from original post: What incentive is there not to hunt solo spawns?]

    There's no problem with "hunting" solo spawns. Camping the same spot, killing the same NPC over and over again, because it's faster than doing anything else, and provides little to no risk, is the problem.

    Solo stationary fast-spawns provide a really high reward for little risk. That is the core problem that we were addressing.

  • Overcrowding
    If a server had 2200 people on it at peak before Kunark launched, and has 2200 people on at peak after Kunark lauched, then the crowding situation has to be improved, even though the number of people on at peak is still a bit high.

    The expectation of course is that people have to spread out to the new zones. If that isn't happening, we need to come up with ways to encourage people to move into them.

  • I was suspended for a "bad trade."
    Giving everyone the benefit of the doubt, I'm happy to investigate issues like this, but I need the proper information. If you have the "ample proof" that you suggest, please email it to me at gwrinn@verant.com. Please also doublecheck the GMs name. We don't have a GM using that alias. We had a Guide a while back that had a name very close to that, but he resigned a few months ago.

Please note also that we won't dupe an item to fix a situation. Until the item can be removed from the person who did it, it's out of play.

    The point I am making is that yes, we do everything within our power to keep people from using dishonest and illegal tactics to disrupt the enjoyment of the game for others, but the responsibility for it happening lies with the person committing the act.

The latter often contains a good deal of RP content, and is run across all servers.

    Events, on the other hand, are typically player-initiated and organized affairs. Most of these can be done without GM assistance. Some of them, and I've seen some pretty complicated and intriguing events, require GM assistance in one form or another.

    For "Events", we'll have an email address available soon, along with guidelines for requesting assistance to pull these off.

    Well, bards have always been pretty much in their own category, making something like this a bit difficult. Every melee capable class can out-melee them, and their songs aren't really appropriate to put up versus spells. Bards are probably the best example of a class that is balanced by usefulness to the group as a primary concern, with offensive power being further down the list.

    If anyone has any ideas how some type of test for bards could be laid out, feel free to post your ideas here. I'll be happy to forward them to the Event Master.

  • Naming Incident
    [Quote from original post: Well, I had my first experience with the naming commandos recently. It appears a Guide on my server just happened to be a history major, as he told me my name, Temujin, was spelled so closely to Ghengis Khan's birth name (Temojin, or some such) as to violate policy.]

    Like any situation that requires human judgement, there will be cases where mistakes are made and where the goals of the underlying policy are not met. That's what happened here.

    The spirit of the name policy, particularly the section about names of historical significance and from popular media, is to judge names based upon knowledge that an average and reasonable person would have. This covers the ever-so-popular names like Gandalf and Merlin.

    In your case, the change was definitely unjust, and I've coordinated with the Head GM to restore your chosen name. We sincerely apologize for the incident.
