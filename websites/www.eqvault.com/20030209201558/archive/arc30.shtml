- ChrisLTD @ 4:12 PM PDT
Alan on the Developer's Corner Forum:

In your last post a player made a comment about how it takes player exploit before you fix a bug. I found it unreasonable that you dodged the underlying question. I believe the intent of that player was to complain about the fact that existing bugs are almost entirly ignored until people find a way to use it to their advantage. There exist many bugs that have no exploitable factors (because they are broken beyond use) that have been around since the creation of the game. If we found a way to exploit the ability to agro Pain and Suffering in certain zones it would be fixed next patch. If penalty levels caused people to /petition hundreds of times a day you would pay attention. If the bug that makes your hat dissappear when an illusion wears off caused you to become invincible there would be an emergency patch. However, instead of fixing these things we get updates to the manual and silly games like gems.

The accusations of you of acting like an out of control gm is because most exploitable bugs are discovered when rare encounters are pulled off more easily then you intended and you take action. If this problem was only being exploited in West Commonlands then it would never be discovered and if it was there would most likly be no action because its low on the radar.

Why not give us some legitimate bug fixes instead of new buggy code that nobody asked for.

That's a hard appearance to avoid or to dispel.

You are right, most notable exploitable bugs are discovered when rare encounters are pulled off more easily than intended. That is because most bugs are caught in-house and never make it to a live server. The ones that do tend to be the ones that can only be discovered by the intense scrutiny of a few thousand dedicated players. And when we fix those bugs the fix is usually accompanied by a reaction similar to the one we're seeing now about Kael.

The reality is a little different than the appearance.

We've known about and have been working on the pet aggro thing for a while, long before our change to Kael. Our schedule for fixing it hasn't really changed because of anything done in Kael. It's a bug, and it's being fixed.

We are just as likely to take a similar action for a bug that is causing problems for the player. But nobody talks about those. If charming things in Kael was causing the charmer to suddenly explode, we could do something similar to what we did here and make it so that charm can't be cast in the city. But players would stop charming things in Kael even if we didn't change the code in that case. And if we turned off charm in the zone to keep player characters from exploding there wouldn't be a big ruckus about it. Most players wouldn't hear a word about it (except from those that had exploded – they'd have a right to complain…).

If a bug like this was being abused in West Commons and we heard about it, we'd take the same action. But you are right, if we don't hear about it, we can't fix it. There is a tendency for folks to not tell us about things that are broken in their favor. I guess that's human nature. And maybe that's where the sense of conflict comes from. We want to fix all the bugs. But people are more inclined to tell us about the ones that affect them negatively and not the ones that make things easier for them.

I understand that it's probably easier to see a bug that affects you negatively than one that helps you. And most certainly in this case there was plenty of poor communication on our part to make things worse. Again, I'm sorry about that.

But that doesn't mean that this isn't a bug. That doesn't mean that we shouldn't fix it. And that doesn't mean that we should allow people to use a bug to their benefit. Though maybe it's really the last part that some players disagree with us about, and maybe that's where the accusations of pettiness come in.

But we feel that it's important that bugs don't affect the game one way or the other. We're always going to be upset when we let a bug into the game. But we're not going to be upset when someone accomplishes something in game. I can't stress this enough, but we build these encounters to be used, not to be impossible. And we want people to do the hard things in game. But at the same time we want those things to be accomplished without the use of bugs.

I don't know that I can explain it any better than that.

And, well, Auric and I put a lot of work into that manual…

Infobits: What is going on in Kael, Part II
- ChrisLTD @ 9:39 PM PDT
Alan on the Developer's Corner Board:

"Most of you are aware that there is still a bit of a bug with the way pets work in the aggro code."
Until this moment, no one I know was aware the pet problems were actual bugs, and not intended by Verant. The current pet aggro code is the result of a re-patch to repair a problem introduced several patches ago. There have been a couple patches since then, and the aggro code has not changed. How are we to know that Verant considers these bugs?

Since Verant has not announced that the pet aggro code was bugged, I do not feel that anyone using the current aggro can be considered to be exploiting.

The dictionary definition of exploit is "To make use of selfishly or unethically." It is arguable that any tactic that kills any MOB is inherently selfish since killing a MOB denies it to everyone else, so the only relevant aspect of this definition is the "unethically" part. The only way someone can do something unethical is with prior knowledge of a standard. Since Verant has never announced that pet aggro was bugged, then no one could know that using pet aggro features was abuse.

We don't have the benefit of a "list of bugs" that you in Verant do. Unless you tell us, we can never know if one thing is a bug, or working as planned. Please don't assume that we know things are bugs unless you have alerted us to the fact.

You are correct.

And that is exactly why we chose to turn off the ability to 'exploit' this bug and have taken no action against those that were using it. Because it's easy to see how a player might not have known there was still a bug, we are assuming that nobody did, and therefore we aren't accusing anyone of any wrongdoing. If I somehow implied that, I'm sorry. That wasn't my intent. I certainly didn't use the word 'exploit' in my post, for exactly that reason.


Why is it that on a continous basis players have to use a change VI makes to their advantage before it is considered a bug? While necromancers and mages has been complaining since the agro change patch (June?) that pets over agro. Something that has been fixed in several patches since then with little to no change. Yet, now a guild managed to use this change (not a bug as you said, but an INTENTIONAL, and announced, change on your part how pets work), it is instantly changed so the tactic can not be used.

May I suggest using some sort of version control software and revert the changes back to how it was before the pet agro change? Seems to me that would change the problem in Kael.

This is where a lot of the confusion seems to occur.

We've been aware of and working on the pet aggro bug since the last patch where we thought we had it fixed (which, as others have pointed out, was July 10th). We haven't increased our work on fixing the bug because of anything going on in Kael. Sometimes it just takes a while to nail down and fix something like this, especially when so much else is going on.

As for players having to 'use a change' we make to their advantage before we'll consider it a bug, that is indeed sometimes the case. There are times when we aren't aware of a bug until a few hundred thousand people start looking for them. Yes, when we find players doing something using a function of the game engine that we hadn't anticipated, we have to evaluate it and see what we think.

Let us imagine that there was a weapon that proc'ed a new spell effect that turned the enemy purple. Internally we test that weapon and find that it does indeed turn the enemy purple. We also test it in combats here and there and nothing odd happens. After all, it only turns someone purple, how much trouble could it cause. So it goes in game.

Then players find it. At first only a few have it and they use it rarely. After all, it only turns enemies purple. But when more of them come into the game, someone uses it against a snow orc. He's already purple. So maybe the spell code decides that if something is already purple, it will become invisible when affected by this new spell.

Ok, not likely, but this is just an example.

We hear about it. Now, this is obviously a bug, the intent was not that the weapon would make snow orcs invisible. But we could decide that it is indeed a feature and leave it in.

But until we know that this weapon makes snow orcs invisible, we can't make that decision.

So there are certainly times when we declare something a bug only after players discover something. If the sword instantly killed any purple enemy every time it proc'ed, then calling it a bug might appear to be taking some 'clever' trick away from the players. In reality, it's still a bug.

The pet aggro thing is a bug. The fact that players have found a way to use it to their advantage doesn't make it less of a bug. And it still needs fixing.


My only request is that you overcommunicate major changes like this, particularly if they are done in a hurry. It is critical that the player base have a sense that things are done for a reason and not whimsically.

Well, we most certainly should have mentioned the change. But you and I could probably debate the notion of 'major changes'. This is one zone among more than 100. The change is only going to affect a small number of players, and only for a short time. I don't disagree, we should have mentioned it. But this isn't any sort of major change.

If we wanted something to be unkillable, it would be unkillable. I've read a lot of accusations on the Internet and comments to the Developer's Corner that we are like a bunch of pre-teen D&D Dungeon Masters that take the death of some NPC or another personally and feel the need to strike back.

That is, frankly, a load of …

We've certainly made some NPCs very hard. That was intentional. But we're not going to take anything personally about the death of that NPC. Not only is that just silly, but it's not a very reasonable assumption.

Keep in mind that we aren't a team of one person. There are a bunch of us here. Things like the change to Kael aren't up to one person to do. A designer and a coder, minimally, would be needed to make the changes we made to Kael. If this mythical juvenile game designer were actually working here, he'd have no chance to affect his petty changes.

Yes, we put a lot of effort and thought into creating the zones, NPCs and quests for players to experience. And, yes, there is a sense of pride when something is done well. But we build this stuff to be interacted with, not just be looked at. We want players to experience the content that the team slaved so hard to build.

And the assumption that this change is only about the Avatar of War is misguided. The problem in Kael extends to any NPC in that zone, not just the big ones.

Regardless, we don't want the Avatar of War to be harder than he was intended to be. We just want him to be as hard as he was intended to be. There's no real reason for the challenge level of any given content to be changed by a bug.

Nobody is forcing anyone to try to kill him. There's nothing wrong with an NPC being exceptionally hard to kill. We build the encounters. It's up to you to decide if you want to tackle them. And, well, heck – he is the Avatar of War. He's the essence of combat. He's supposed to be extremely powerful.


Two small notes: It sounds like you are misusing the word 'bug'. A 'bug' is different from a design problem. The charm code works normally. The pet code works normally. Everything works like it's supposed to, but you think it's unbalancing, so that would be a design problem, not a bug, right?

And secondly, it seems to most of us non-ubers that you are focusing 85% of resources and planning on 10% of the population. Time and time again, the most powerful groups in the game do something which you think abuses the game design somehow, and your reaction is to make that tactic impossible in order to protect the game. I'm all for protecting the game, but the high-level game is already too hard for everyone who isn't in an uberguild.

Maybe you should just start putting in situations and NPCs to DIRECTLY counter large groups of uber folk. Spawn a GM controlled (or normal) ubermob to specifically frustrate ubergroups doing things you want to prevent. Heck, just pop unmezzable, uncharmable high HP mobs randomly in the zone anytime an adventure zone gets more than 10 lvl 56+s.

You're right. Charm works as intended. Pets do what they are supposed to do. But the aggro code is not working as intended. This is, indeed, a bug.

As to your second point, we're not. In the same period that we made the change to Kael we've also done a few dozen other things. This apparent focus on the higher level stuff isn't coming from us. We make similar changes to things that come about from issues with lower level characters. But it's the players with the higher level characters that are also the more vocal.

The interesting thing here is that this "tactic" was only made possible by a bug in the code that we managed to add while trying to adjust something. This is a new thing, something that just didn't exist a few months ago. And yet, somehow, our attempts to return the code to something more like normal, like it was a few months ago, is now being called a 'nerf'…

Even more unfortunately, is that everyone seems to be ignoring the fact that the bug fix will be beneficial to pet classes all over the place. And, though I'm still receiving comments about that, the focus is suddenly all on the assumption that we're ruining a tactic used on a very few high level raid situations.

In the end, this is a bug fix. The "tactic" that we will be removing has only been possible because of this bug. The one does not exist without the other.

Infobit: What is going on in Kael
- ChrisLTD @ 7:19 PM PDT
Absor on the Developer's Corner:

Any chance there will some explanation of why giants in Kael can no longer be charmed? Seems odd that the zone would come down out of the blue and giants no longer charmable. Crowd control? I personally think it was a bad move since giants cannot be charmed... and I have an idea it was due to Avatar of War being killed, but I'm sure if a comment was mad it would clear things up.. Thanks!

I'll do my best to explain the situation.

Most of you are aware that there is still a bit of a bug with the way pets work in the aggro code. In Kael there are a lot of NPCs that can be charmed that are significantly more powerful than any pet. Some folks were using this combination of bug and situation to make encounters much more trivial than they were intended to be.

Fixing the bug will require a full patch, and a largish one at that. We weren't willing to make a large emergency patch, taking down all the servers and requiring all that downtime for our customers to fix a bug being abused in one zone.

This left us with pretty much three options.

1) Allow folks to continue to use the bug to their advantage.
2) Begin punishing people for using the bug until it could be patched.
3) Remove the ability to use the bug in the situation where it was most abusable, Kael, and then fix the bug with the next patch and remove the temporary fix.

We chose the third option.

We don't want people using bugs to their advantage. We feel that it harms the game integrity and the gameplay of those that don't use bugs to their advantage. We couldn't take option one.

We could choose option two, but policing such things requires resources, and banning people isn't something we like to do. This is a new bug, and perhaps people were unsure that it is indeed a bug. And in most circumstances this bug only changes the play style of some people. Only in a few circumstances does this bug become abusable. So fixing the specific circumstance is better than giving warnings to players.

I know the next question, and let me answer it right now. This is a new bug, and it could be argued with some conviction that players using it to their advantage were unaware that it wasn't purposeful code change. In the case of the abuse of safe zones, it is well known that using such locations is against our rules. It can't be reasonably argued that any player with a character over 50th level doesn't understand that.

In any case, we plan to fix the bug with the next patch. Until then, the NPCs in Kael will probably remain uncharmable. We understand that this adversely affects single and double group raids in that zone, and for that we are sorry. But things should be back to normal soon. Assuming anyone knows what normal is anyway…
