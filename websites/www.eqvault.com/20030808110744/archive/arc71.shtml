It is true that we are more likely to respond to posts where we see an error so that we can correct it. The Success of EQ
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:01 PM PST
Gordon spoke about the success of EverQuest in response to a post blasting the game for it's lack support for the casual gamer:

    There's no question that "success" in EQ revolves around building a proper network of friends. That however applies to those who play 2 hours every other day as well as those who play 10 a day. Those who play less often just define success differently.

    I'd have to take issue with defining the casual gamer in the same fashion that you have in your post. Rather than define it on the basis of how much time one plays, I'd rather define it based on the playstyle of the person playing. It's perfectly possible for someone who's a 10 hour a day player to be a casual gamer, as much as it's possible to define a 6 hour a week player as a powergamer.

    EQ supports the casual gamer just fine, as the focus of the casual gamer is not consumption of content, leveling, and item acquisition; It's to "have a good time", by adventuring with friends, exploring areas, and overall experiencing the game. Leveling and item acquisition is ancillary to the goal of having fun; It just happens as a result of their adventuring.

    -Gordon

GameSpot Velious First Impressions
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:16 AM PST
The folks over at GameSpot have kicked up their first impressions of Verant's Scars of Velious expansion. Here's an excerpt:

    The first thing we noticed in Iceclad Ocean was that there is plenty of snow - snow is everywhere. It covers the land and trees, and even the fauna such as snow cougars, snow gnolls, and dire wolves are snowy white. We wandered around this first zone for a while and came across the first building, the Tower of Frozen Shadow. There were plenty of animals wandering around, as well as shadow guardians, a new type of monster that resembled a whirlwind of bones.   • Absor Back on the Scene
    Well, I only just got back into the office today. I was in Vegas at the launch party.

    We certainly don't have a problem with you showing our game to your friends. Heck, thanks for doing it. And if he played on it a bit, that's fine too.

We don't want our boards to end up in such situations where cursing is an effective way of communicating.

We have no problem with off-topic, civil communications. But this board does have a purpose, and that is to talk about EverQuest. We can't have that communication cut off because the recent election was so darn close. It's certainly a worthy topic of discussion, but we can't let it take over these boards.

    Planetside Interview
    [6 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:02 PM PST
    I wanted to quickly point out an interview Daily Radar did today with Verant's Kevin McCann. For those who don't know, this month's issue of PC Gamer contained the unveiling of Verant's Planetside, a game Verant is billing as the world's first persistent massively multiplayer first person shooter. Here's an excerpt:

      Daily Radar: Tell us about Planetside. What's the setup for the game? What's going to draw players in?

      Kevin McCann: We're aiming to create a massively multiplayer tactical first-person shooter. Versus an RPG, the storyline plays less of a role because I'm trying to make sure that the game stays true to what it's trying to be -- a first-person shooter. We have some role-playing, but it's very light. People aren't going to be coming in here to socialize, they're going to come in to fight.

      In the game, you'll ally yourself with one of four corporations, which are sort of your top level causes. They give you job assignments, character growth opportunity and professional advancement.

      You have access to abilities and implants as you advance. These are more like support skills. They don't impact you actual reflex performance -- you're not getting more hit points, you're not necessarily getting auto-aim. This is a first-person shooter, so you don't want players having an unfair advantage. There are, for example, electronic skills which allow you to hack into terminals or gain certain medical abilities.
