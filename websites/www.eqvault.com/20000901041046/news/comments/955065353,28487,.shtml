Gordon Infobits
- Aurum @ 4:55 PM PDT
Moving straight on to the Gordon Infobits, here's all of Gordon Wrinn's current posts on the Gameplay board:

  • The Hole
    [Quote from original post: "We want people do 'do' dungeons instead of camp"
    -Verant

    The following is an proposal for the zone called "The Hole", which I feel would add a much needed level of excitement and skill to the mid-level Everquest game.

    We've all heard of the Planes, the high-level, ultra-hard zones that require teamwork, efficiency, and have some lovely loot. However, being level 46-50 is not something that we can all experience, sadly.

    Now, most of us have heard of Hell Levels. They are a result of a mathematical inaccuracy in the experience tables that require you to gain a lot more experience than intended to level up. 30, 35, 40, and 45 are by definition, "Hell Levels".

    What I propose is a place where mid-level characters could get pre-plane experience in a similar environment, a high-risk, high-reward dungeon that would be open for mid-level people only. There would be more experience per mob killed due to difficulty and to make leveling through Hell a bit easier. Of course, people die in The Hole, so, as I said, it's Risk vs. Reward.

    The zone could be open for players level 34-41, which encompasses 2 hell levels, 35 and 40, respectively. The limits are in place so that the people intended to be there are there, no high-level campers, no powerlevelers.

    The loot would be nice. Something for every class, I hope. Specifically, I would like to see a Shadowknight weapon of Ghoulbane strength if not better. Components for nice quests could be found here, perhaps some rare poison for the rogues, a new spell for the casters. Wouldn’t you like to see a spell earned instead of bought?

    In a nutshell, the entire point of this proposed Hole is a proving ground for sub-planar characters. It’s to get them off their camping arses and into a big, scary Hole. I’m done now, but below were several other ideas I considered, that may or may not be “over the top” for something like this.

    OTHER IDEAS-

    The Screams of Pain- Based on a random timer that spans at least 6 hours, every so often a high-pitched screaming noise can be heard coming from the depths of the Hole. This quickly acts as a warning to any people currently adventuring in there, as they have 60-80 seconds after the screams begin to act. When the 60-80 seconds are gone, full zone repop. This does two things,

    a. Makes the dungeon even more challenging, scary, and unexpected.

    b. Prevents camping and farming of the Hole. The intention is to earn what you get down here; respawn camping is lame.

    The Boss- The Hole is a physical hole ripped from Norrath in a great war of magics. The “Boss” mob should be tough enough to reflect this. Since the proposed level range for the players is somewhat large (7), balancing a Boss is difficult. Making it too high would make casters useless unless they were about to leave the zone (upon reaching the higher limit). Level is really just a number, so a level 38 Boss buffed to ungodly amounts for that level. Cazic Thule, Inoruuk, and the Dragons reportedly have 32,000 HP, so I would think that around 14,000 would not be unreasonable.

    The Boss Room, an alternative to The Boss- The “Boss Room” would be very unique, and if possible would greatly enhance the experience. For every person in The Hole (about 30 for an average raid, let’s say), then there are 1/6 mobs created. If there are 30 people raiding, there are 5 monsters, very tough. Buffed to extremes, but not quite as much as The Boss. They would vary in class and loot. Of course, I don’t know if the technology exists to allow this kind of spawning.

    The Boss, part II!- In an effort to torment players, Bruce Springsteen music is repeatedly played while in the zone. As you break the zone, jam to the classics like “Born in the USA”, and “Glory Days” The Boss is The Boss himself, a quad-hitting bard in a leather jacket. (I’m sorry, couldn’t say Boss that many times without somebody talking about Mr. Springsteen, so I went ahead and did it for you.)

    That about wraps it up. Please give your input on this. Personally, I’m sick of camping, let’s make something new. Thanks for reading, and good night.]

    These are some interesting ideas. I'll forward them to the world-builder as well as the people who'll be responsible for populating the zones.

    Though I can't comment on any of the specifics of how this zone will be designed, I can say that the dungeons that have been built since release have been designed with the knowledge of how players in the game react to certain encounters. I would say that the dungeons built since release are all *better* in that regard.

  • On Mages
    [Quote from original post: 1) Thanks for looking into phantom armor. Would it be possible to change the name to "summonedleather/chain/plate" for role playing sake when you rework it? [it would be wonderfull if it wasmade conj as well, butI know it won't happen]]

    We still like the 'Phantom' name. The regen component that we are adding to the spells might not fit 'Phantom' too well, but at least it sounds cool.

    [Quote from original post: 2) Voicegraft, please? It's a RP spell plain and simple and wouldn't affect balance at all.]

    I'll mention it to GZ :)

    [Quote from original post: 3) The spear of warding / sword of runes lost there procs sometime in jan. Was this intentinal? Any plans to be fixed/replaced?]

    Talking to one of the item guys about it now. Does it just "not proc", or does it say something like "Item is out of charges"?

    [Quote from original post: 4) At some point there was a silent change in that damage shields cast on pets do not last 3x as long as on people anymore(which hurts your mana-efficenticy a bit when soloing). I had allways thought that the effect was a bug [as damage shields last forever on most mobs], but a silent nerf like this is still a bit disheartning. Was that the case?]

    NPCs were treating buff spells differently than PCs. The point was to keep NPCs in cities from constantly casting buffs on everyone and everything. Though this part of the code was maintained, special code was put in so that buffs would wear off of pets at the proper time. We probably should have announced this.

    [Quote from original post: 5) GZ once said that summoned items are no-rent because you don't want them to have unlimited effect with a 1 time mana investment, and that if there was some limit on how much say a sword of runes could be used it would be rentable. Then could all consumable summoned items loose the no-rent flag? Waterstones, ammo, food/water & the ring of flight, come to mind. Pockets, weapons coldstones ect are all fine as no-rent.]

    We don't have a way to put a time-limit on items, beyond either making them NORENT for the term of a login session, or until you zone, so changing this really isn't an option at this point. It does have the added benefit of requiring that people seek out someone to summon their items every time they log in. Take the dimensional pouch as an example.

    [Quote from original post: 6) Could invis to undead be added to the air elemenatl's invisibilty spell?]

    I'm sorry, but the ability to be invis to both the living and the dead is and will remain a rogue-only ability.

    [Quote from original post: Best solution : "Voice of Zomm" - Allows a mage to dirrectly order his elementals moves for a period of 30 seconds, just like the eye, only works *only* with an elemental. [Yes, that's a long rant about one insignificant spell. Give a mage 3 ways to cast a spell and they start to think it should be usefull/fun]]

    Good point, I'll mention it to GZ. I'm not sure if this would be possible with limited coding however.

    [Quote from original post: 8) At what level do focus items begin to affect your elementals?]

    We leave things like this to the players to figure out.

    [Quote from original post: 9) Whas making all the pets delay uniform [after the duel wield patch] intentionial or a bug? Will this be changed?]

    When we finally allowed them to start dual-wielding, we had to reevaluate the differing attack speeds. We found them to be too powerful overall. Rest assured that your pets are more powerful than they were before since they dual-wield now.

    [Quote from original post: 10) Known bug - Burning out or fire-shielding an invisible elemental often results in it becomeing *permentily* invis.]

    This must be a real pain for PvP. I'll send this one to the test team to be sure that it is in the database.
    --
    Follow up:

    We found the problem with the Sword of Runes, and it should be fixed the next time we do data. As for the spear of warding, it doesn't have a proc and never has according to our records.

  • Gameplay Engine Question
    [Quote from original post: Why is it that when you are inside a cottage, house or building creatures can attack you through the wall even though you cant see them. This basically means in places like East Karana you cant take refuge almost anywhere. Why shouldnt you be able to get in a house and hide w/o a lion invisibly attacking you through the wall? this applies to griffins and other creatures as well. Other buildings in other zones too!]

    Not really a bug. In order to reduce the amount of "lag" that can happen due to internet latency, then engine handles object collisions client-side. Most houses and such are objects (rather than world geometry) and are subject to this.

    It also has the benefit of eliminating a good number of issues that would have to be dealt with in order to limit possible exploitation. For instance, if this wasn't the case, someone could sit inside a house and cast upon a creature that couldn't fit through the open door.

  • Corpse Decay
    [Quote from original post: I had a corpse with almost all my stuff on it when the servers came down yesterday. Do I loose all the stuff I had on it?]

    Corpses above level 5 remain for 24 online hours or one week offline, whichever occurs first. In other words, your corpse should be fine.

  • Spellcaster Issues
    [Quote from original post: I see right now that you have some GREAT spells for necros. No matter what criticism you get, keep them this way. You have kept wizards bad, no reason not to keep necros good.]

    Like other spell issues, we still can't comment on them. But I think it might be prudent to remind everyone that anything we put in will be balanced.

    [Quote from original post: Also, being a former wizard, I was inclined to check out their spells. The alleged wizard pet spell on lvl 59, Flaming Sword of Xuzl...when you kick the letters in Xuzl back 6 places it spells "ROTF"....Bad joke, guys...]

    ROFL! Or should I say Xulr!

  • Clerics and Ressurection
    [Quote from original post: This may be a pointless topic, but how can clerics ressurect themselfs after they died? Its like having your own corpse ressurect you while your dead. anyone agree?]

    From a fairness standpoint, we decided to allow this. If we didn't, in single cleric parties, the cleric would be the only one subject to full XP loss on death.
