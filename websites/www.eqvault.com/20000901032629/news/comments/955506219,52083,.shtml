Zanth sent me the following:

Zanth log:
Tuesday, April 11, 2000

There had been rumors of a new evil coming from Ak'anon, centerof the Gnome Kingdom. The rumors from weeks ago about the darkness near Erudin had been true. But there haven't been any more reports of strange happenings from Toxxulia Forest since we killed the Overlord in the Paineel. It seemed strange to hear of a 'new' evil from the heart of the Gnome Kingdom. The necromancers have always existed in Ak'anon and they are still no threat to Norrath...

This is what I can recall from the day the Gnome King fell.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

..Outside of town we prepared for our journey into Ak'anon. I glancedat the night sky and took a deep breath. Full moon, figures. Dear Karana,let us die well this night, and quickly.

Only five of us, not enough, not enough! The four others with me arepraised by some, unknown to most. They are good and I have great faith.The night passes quicky, we must go, time is of the essence.

..Inside we have problems early. The troll Uglar does not know when to sheath his blade and when to lay low. Perhaps it is the magic of the bloodfirethat drives the madness into him. Looking at his eyes you can see... small firesburning. Blade and whip swinging furiously, Uglar takes out bot after bot.He is not alone tho, next to him the barbarian Carressa shreds bots into scrapmetal. The first battle is over quickly; the two warriors had been hungry for some killing.

Quickly we moved towards the Palace. The whole way I had to listen to theranting of the drunken cleric Bannek. "Whars the ALE!" he says... "HAR HAR!" hecackles gleefully. If we didn't need him, I might have slit his throat. Damndrunk dwarves.

..There! The palace is in front of us. With the troll masked through druidmagic so he is not seen, the guards do not pay attention to him nor the others.It is common for barbarians, halflings, and dwarves to visit the Ak'anon.Through the front doors we walk in, smiling to the receptionist. "Are youhere to see the King?" she asks. I respond, "Why yes, we are," as I barelyhold back an evil grin.

Down, down, down we go, deep into the Ak'anon Palace. My heart is pounding now,glancing around you can not help notice the platemail wearing guards lining every corridor. _gulp_ ...Finally! We are before the throne room.

..Still hidden by the druid magic, the troll scouts about the throne room entrance.He's back and grinning. Trolls have really bad breath. "What are you so happy for?"I ask. Uglar says, "I found a torturer, here is proof of the evil!" "Lets kill it"We enter the torture chamber and lo and behold, one gnome torturer and two gnomeguards. Out come the swords and whips and the two warriors proceed to teach thegnome a little on the proper way of torturing. I'd hoped to get some informationfrom the gnome about the evil engulfing Ak'anon. Unforunately in the heat of battleCarressa put a six inch cut into his neck. Damn, the cut is too deep for the shamanwith us to heal the gnome. We decide to speak directly to the King.

..Hail King Ak'anon. King Ak'anon says, "Its druids like you who have ruined yourown lands! You will not ruin mine!" ACK!!!!!

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The battle that pursues is intense. The exact events I can not recall. When all was done, only 5 adventurers stood in the throne room, alone. At their feet liesthe corpse of King Ak'anon. With the evil King defeated the 5 friends looked at eachother, shrugged, and headed out after...

Out of character discussion post-

One thing I always laugh about is how some players will try to excuse their actions by deeming it fun and apart of the game. This excuse can be used just about anywhere and anytime, but its one of the weakest excuses to use. Of course this is a game, do we really need to be told what is fun and what isn't? For example, I can walk up to a player on a pvp server, kill him and start slaying him over and over at his spawn spot. If anyone asks why i'm doing it... "Hey, this is a game. I'm just having fun!"

Anyway, back to the subject. I do agree roleplaying is what you make it, but lets not forget what kind of fantasy world we are trying to roleplay in. =) Certain things need to be taken into account when a person tries to roleplay:
-The character's religious beliefs
-The character's cultural beliefs and traditions
-The racial outlook of his character
-And most importantly, the character's background history.

You can add or subtract to that list, but those are the most deciding factors why a certain player may act the way he does. (If he's roleplaying of course) Now lets look at the story:

The druid and his band of unlikely adventurers are going to assassinate the King of Aka'non because he's... hmm, I guess because he's evil. Not much explaination there I guess. Now for the most part, Druids are a priestly class. They worship a select few gods (depending on their race) but are mostly true neutral in their behavior. Meaning they understand the balance of evil and good, law and chaos, as they understand the balance of nature. For a druid to go and slay the king of akanon because he is just evil is very out of character. Even more odd is that a dwarf would accompany this band and slay his racial cousins instead of the racial enemy troll who is next to him instead.
This is why I call this a powergamer's pathetic excuse for roleplaying. They are basing this whole story on real life friendships that normally would not happen if they stayed in character. I have plenty of friends who are of all races in EQ, but thats out of character. When it comes to being in character with Thurgrum, i'm a volatile, easily-angered dwarf who is extremely racist and prejudist. I can clearly make the distinction of my character's personality, and my real life personality. But can this group of players who claim to be roleplaying do the same?

In truth Vallidus, i'm not trying to impress anyone here, but trying to point out that this group of players assassinated the King of Akanon because they found it fun and maybe, even profitable for their exp bars and item collections. To call it a good roleplaying story is to imply that these players know how to roleplay, which in fact, they don't.

Just my two coppers. =)

#21   Name: Thurgrum Ogrebane (varno123@aol.com)   Date: 4/12 @ 21:25 PM PST
OOC-

Perhaps I'm being a bit over-critical, but I think this story is nothing near extreme roleplaying. The only reason I voiced my opinion pointing out how little the whole thing had to do with roleplaying was when I saw the last line: "The Mayor of Rivervale (The TRUE Evil!)"

That led me to believe that you and yer band of "crusaders" would next assassinate the Mayor of Rivervale. I just assumed that either this story was a joke, or this band of do-gooders were comitting random acts of murder and evil upon the very targets they claimed to be evil. (Can we say hypocrisy?)
Either way, I still think you should come up with better material than to say you killed so-and-so because he/she/it was evil. And try doing certain quests and events that you'll eventually write about in an RP fashion just for the sake of roleplaying. Don't kill an important figure and then write up a short roleplaying story trying to justify your actions.

On the matter of individual background history for everyone's characters and such-
I don't doubt that there are always exceptions to every race. You won't always find an evil troll, you won't always find a drunken dwarf, you won't always find a pacifist elf, etc. It's true, there are always exceptions to this type of style of roleplaying, but I think its so damned played out its becoming to be too common. It's the "Drizzt Do'Urden renegade" style of roleplaying. Choosing a certain race/class to play, but portraying attitudes and beliefs that are completely opposite of the defining norm. It's an interesting concept when its rare and played off well, but it's revolting to keep hearing about every character resembling this style most times.
It's just too damn common for most players in Everquest. And the reason I dislike to see it so much is because I don't think most of these "renegade" players are actually doing it for the sake of roleplaying an interesting character. They are doing it for convenience. A troll who roleplays to be evil, chaotic, and bloodthirsty will often find himself not being able to get into certain groups and participate in certain events. But hey, if he says he isn't at all like his kind and he's some kind of benevolent troll who enjoys peace and serenity, he's got that part covered! It's just a crying shame to roleplay when it suits you best and is convenient. I am a firm believer that you should pick a class/race combination that you would like to roleplay, and suits you best. Stats and abilities should be secondary.

Now on the other hand, let's look at the more logical point of view when it comes to a character's roleplaying attitudes and beliefs. We have to assume that the majority of players on Norrath originate from their home cities usually around their kin. Most players would infact, have the attitudes and beliefs of their race and class instead of being a renegade. To do this, it means you have to take a stand. You have to roleplay your race and class well, and that may also mean you'll have to sacrifice some aspects of Everquest's levelquest. I admire and respect that type of roleplayer much more because not only do they know how to roleplay their character well, but they aren't afraid to do it. What's a couple of stat points, experience points, or items to a true roleplayer?

I'll leave you with these words taken from the Collective of Old Fen Ro web site about roleplaying:

"We choose to define role-play as follows:

Role-playing is the art of embodying a character, and then acting through that character based on the views, opinions, history, and abilities of the character, and not the view and opinions of the player (you).

Role-play consists of finding reasonable in-character motives for doing the things you do. Or, more truly, doing the things you do because you were already acting under the motives of your character. To role-play is to become a character in a fictional setting, and to do your best to be that character, whatever happens."


It's a shame most people don't roleplay in Everquest. It's an even bigger shame that some of those who do, can't do it very well.
