Player View Discussion: No Need for Downtime!
[47 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:39 AM PDT
Further addressing the topic of "Too many patches," Gordon posted the following in another thread:

    Would you prefer that the game never be changed? No more quests, no more items, no new areas, no more fixes, etc?

    -Gordon
Now if only for the shake living up people's mornings, I've started a special Player View Discussion Thread on our General Board. The purpose of this thread is to share thoughts and ideas on how to make downtime more painless.

People have a right to be somewhat upset with all the recent downtime. By my rough estimate the servers have been down for over 26 hours alone this month. (and that's using the estimated downtime, not always the actual downtime experienced) At the same time Gordon has a very valid point, EverQuest would be ruined if it was not properly maintained.

Some initial thoughts and suggestions come to mind:
  • Give people more than 24 hours of advanced warning - It stinks to suddenly have to deal with downtime. Announced it a week a head and post a few friendly reminders.
  • Compress the downtime - Aside from non-emergency, game threatening issues have a regular "Patch Day" on a specific day of the week/month. That way people will learn to better work around it.
Feel free to jump on the thread and post your thoughts as well.

    3) During our peak hours, we're seeing around 62,000 people playing at the same time. Even more interestingly we're seeing our MINIMUM number of people rising to around 16,000 people. More Gordon Infobits
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:36 PM PDT
Gordon has posted more to the Gameplay Board this evening. Lets get caught up:

  • Thousands of Twigs/Whips?
    I would think that the sheer number of people here on the boards complaining about it illustrates that there are far more in game than some here think. I've seen dozens of people complaining about it, and considering the percentages of people who post on the boards, the number in game can be extrapolated.

    Let me ask a question: Can anyone here say that those weapons are appropriate equipment for the mid-30s?

  • Unique Quests
    [Quote from original post: Ive read a lot of the recent discussion about camping and item drops and I wanted to propose an idea I have had for awhile now. The proposition is to have in game quests which follow that same layout as current quests do but instead of being constant, they can only be completed one time only ]

    I assume you mean once per server rather than once per player. That's probably not something that we would do. We'd rather that one-time quests be handled on a dynamic basis, with actors playing the NPCs present in most of the quest. They involve many people overall, trying to get the biggest "bang for the buck".

    It wouldn't be a very good use of time to have several game designers spending a week or more of their time to input a quest that operates in the standard way, only to have it enjoyed by 3 dozen people.

  • Verant wants us to die more?
    [Quote from original post: Every patch seems to either make dying easier or gaining exp harder with a net result that it takes a very long time to gain 1 bubble of exp never mind 1 whole level!]

    I'd say that very few patches do either, really. Perhaps if someone is dead set on camping one fast-spawning NPC, and we change the spawn time on that NPC, that might be construed as the former, but we're just trying to inject challenge into the game.

    Every day people post here on the board that the game is "nothing but camping" or "nothing but hack and slash", yet that is all they choose to do while in game, when there is so much more there. These same people spend 12 hours per day in game doing what they come here and complain is boring. We're just trying to remove what is being cited as boring. Now obviously we can't please everyone in this process, for instance, those who wouldn't be happy unless there was absolutely no downtime of any kind, yet we have to try to address the issues that we can.

    Just to answer your question:

    [Quote from original post: Why do you want us to all die Abashi (Verant)?]

    The game should be fun. One of the ways that the game provides fun is to provide challenges for you to overcome. Challenges require risk to be meaningful. The risk in this case is the chance of failure. The penalty for that being death.

    If there was no death then there would be no risk which means that there would be no challenge and thus no fun. The FAQ explains this in more detail.

  • Why Ban Players
    [Quote from original post: Why not "Exile" their accounts to a "bad boy" server. Disable their account from access to anyother server, and transfer their chars to the new server.]

    A server really has to have a minimum number of people on it before it breaks even in terms of depreciation and operations. Certainly we'd save some money from an operations standpoint by not having to provide GM support on a server of this nature, however it would have taken so long to populate the server that it would never have been profitable.
    --
    [Quote from original post: I don't know, Abashi. If you ban the bad boys and morons, I think a new server would fill up real quick.]

    We might be able to get rid of more of your averagely anti-social people (KS, ninjalooting, etc.), however 75% of the GMs' time these days is taken up investigating scams of one kind or another. Maybe if these slow down a bit...

    It might sound strange, but 90% of GM and Guide time is taken up by 10% of the playerbase. It's the same 10% daily. The real problem here is that when someone in the other 90% needs help, they are often in line behind several people of the 10%.

    That's why we've posted from time to time that certain issues shouldn't be petitioned while in game.

  • Guild Logo
    Though this is a great idea that we'll certainly keep in mind for the future, it's not something that we would see in EQ for technical reasons. This is explained pretty well in the FAQ.

Gordon Infobits
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:13 PM PDT
Here's more quotes and excerpts that Gordon Wrinn made today on the Gameplay Board:

  • Lesbian Wedding Event
    [Quote from original post: I am going to have a lesbian wedding on EQ. I was wondering if anyone thinks the GM's would do the name changing and support it. Hopefully a GM will reply with a good answer.]

    In-game weddings are not among the "official" services provided within EverQuest, and as a company, we have no stance one way or another on the issue. If a GM or Guide wishes to perform a service and make the name changes requested, that is up to the individual GM or Guide.

  • Two Accounts Canned
    [Quote from original post: This is an outrage and I won't stand for it! I have two accounts, I cancelled the one that
    wasnt banned but I couldn't even access the account info on the one that was. How in the
    hell can I cancel it? Does payment stop when I am banned? Whats going on Gordon? If you think I'm going to sit around and wait for a GM to get back to me I have some bad news for you.]

    The account in question has changed hands so many times that it's hard to be sure of who to send the email to.

    You'll want to contact eqmail@verant.com to get more info.

  • Tracking
    [Quote from original post: In my experience, tracking players from short distances has never been much of a problem. However, tacking players that are half way across the zone is when the trouble begins. Here's why: I think the server only sends players information about other player's locations on some time interval that I am really not sure of.]

    That would be correct. The problem that you are mentioning has to do with the fact that NPCs are far more predictable than players, and data outside a radius around the player is based off of prediction. Data within the radius is updated more often, but that outside gets old.

    [Quote from original post: Purposed solution: Send *group* member’s location information more often. Simple as that! I can’t see there being much of a performance hit from that. And it will completely fix player tracking (if my logic is right)]

    That would dramatically increase the amount of bandwidth used. It wouldn't be only for those who are tracking, but for everyone.

  • New & Improved Quit Command
    [Quote from original post: People are using the "new and improved" /quit command to avoid death, since it kicks the player out so quickly. You just hafta do it when you have enough hitpoints to live for a few moments after /quitting yourself out.]

    It's a bug. It will be fixed tomorrow.

  • Level 1 Character Wipe
    [Quote from original post: Is there any way so that when we log on to the server screen, we can see what servers we have characters on?]

    No, as each account logging in would have to query all of the world servers. I imagine that at some future point we might be able to put something in client-side, but it would be very low priority. I imagine that people generally don't need help remembering where their characters are.

    [Quote from original post: Do we just need to log in as our lvl 1 for a very short period of time and be able to log right back out to avoid the wipe? This guy is my mule for cryin out loud : )]

    All of the information you need is at the top of the patch message. You can open EQNEWS.TXT in your EverQuest directory as well if you wish. However, to answer your question, you just need to log in, do something that will require your character be saved (move, trade, kill something, etc), and camp normally.

  • More Duping
    [Quote from original post: You say verant have fixed all known duping bugs, but its evident duping is still rampant.]

    The issue that existed was very recently fixed. It might be a little while before for the effects to be noticed.

    [Quote from original post: There have been no patches since the players on the nameless raised this issue, so I can not see how verant can possibly have fixed this.]

    It was fixed before it was brought up here on the boards, and to be honest, The Nameless did not have the worst case of it.

  • Torpor Spell
    [Quote from original post: But my question is how are we supposed to use this level 60 spell?
    Post Rez? Sure, though Donal's BP will complete heal for free much better. Still though post rez is good.
    During combat? Not sure here, the caster won't want mana sucked out of them, the tanks won't want to be slowed, and no one likes to be rooted!]

    How about between combats? It's a fairly efficient healing spell when used this way on most targets. I can also see it being usable in combat in certain cases.

  • More Q&A
    [Quote from original post: 1. Would it be possible(however great an idea this is) that the Quiver would fit in the Ammo Slot? Just seems to make sense.]

    Yes, it would make sense, but unfortunately isn't possible due to the way inventory works.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Could this be a possible solution to your never ending Rallos Zek Coin/Item loot question(I know you dislike me for bringing this up but I think its a good idea if its feasible). Create a command similiar to the /movelog "yes/no" command only make it /coinloot "yes/no". ]

    We do plan to implement some kind of server-specific polling mechanism, however it is not likley to result in a looting change for Rallos. Though coin-only people might be in the majority there, there are enough people who want item loot across the servers that their wishes should be addressed as well. Item loot makes more sense on Rallos due to the reduced level-range for the purpose of PvP.

    The fact also remains that people who chose to play on Rallos did so knowning the item looting rules, and it's been basically the same since release.

    [Quote from original post: 3. This one is more of a logic question. Why can't rogue's track? Being assassins and all, you'd think they'd be able to pick up the trail of any target they're after. Right? Just a thought! =) ]

    This is covered in the FAQ under "Q: Why can’t my class [insert skill here]?"

    [Quote from original post: 4. This question is more of a gripe, but I still see a lot of players with this issue. The Lord Grimrot spawn. He is VERY rare. I've been camping him for 3 weeks and I've yet to see the dead version of him, and I've already killed the live version. Can you at least tell me if there is a trick I'm missing. ]

    No, I wouldn't be able to tell you that. You might want to ask around.

    [Quote from original post: 5. Late night GMs. I understand you're working towards creating more night based customer service, and I'll say I've seen a few more guides on in the late night then I have in the past. What about GMs? I've run into problems that required a GMs asssistance but was unable to get it due to none being on at those hours? Are you also working on the GM scenerio for late nights? ]

    They are around, and there is usually one in Chat to queue requests for specific servers. You might consider dropping by there.

    [Quote from original post: 7. Wedding Rings. From what I understand GMs can create No Drop Wedding Rings that have no attached stats. That is all well and good, but like myself, higher level characters have no use for a ring that sits in your fingerslot but has no extra effect. Is it possible to have player made wedding rings made into No Drop status so that we can keep our wedding rings from being looted? ]

    This is one for the wishlist. There are all kinds of things that we can do with items, and a lot of suggestions have merit of one kind or another. This is probably a case where the benefit to be gained from the time to implement it is pretty small, so it would be a very low priority request.

    [Quote from original post: 8. Just as a side note back to the Grimrot spawn, I think that the Aegis of Life quest may be broken, at least on the Rallos Zek Server. The paladin that gives the quest won't respond to me when I speak with him, and this is happening with 3 others I know doing the quest. ]

    There might be something more to it. The guys tell me that the quest is working fine.

    [Quote from original post: 9. I dunno if this was brought up to you, but what is the feasiblity of putting a new druid/wizard teleport site near the Qeynos hills/everfrost area? ]

    None. We feel that there are too many teleports as it is, which is why they are a bit more limited in Kunark.

    [Quote from original post: 10. I'm a level 42 cleric and I got my butt damn near killed by a orc warrior cuz I couldn't out melee him. I lived w/ about 50hp and no mana..whats up with that? Those guys have been green since level 20! =) ]

    I'd have to see it to believe it. What level was the orc?

  • /Q Bug
    [Quote from original post: /q take max 5 sec now :) just /q on every bad situation, thats work at 90% of all time.]

    That's a bug by the way. It will be fixed in tomorrow's patch.

  • Q&A
    [Quote from original post: 1. Why not make all pets be named after the character? NPCs have pets named after the owner. So, if your character name was Merlin and you summoned a pet, the pet would be called Merlin's Pet. It would be MUCH easier to buff player's pets if you knew which pet in the group was theirs.]

    There's a little more flavor in this implementation, not to mention, the conversation between people to determine who's pet belongs to whom isn't necessarily a bad thing. Not to mention, it's been this way since release and has worked well. I doubt that we'll change this.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Why do pets owned by PCs (not charmed but summoned) show up on tracking? There is no usefulness to this and they should be filtered out. I know they are treated as NPCs, but that still does not answer my question. Even when you filter out players with "/trackerplayers off" they still show up. I would rather track players than player's pets. PLEASE remove them from tracking and make rangers, bards and druids happy!]

    They show up because they are NPCs. Regarding your suggestion of a filter, it's possible that we may implement something like that in the future.

    [Quote from original post: 3. The solution to making the tracking window more efficient is to simply add a scroll bar. A lot of people have said this for quite some time. Filters are not necessary.. just add a scroll bar that will show everything that is in the tracking range of the player.]

    We have some things in mind already for tracking, but thanks for the suggestion.

    [Quote from original post: 4. When will the bard songs lullaby (AOE mes song), chains (level 23 non-exploited snare song) and pixie strike (target mes song) be fixed?]

    Ester is looking into lullaby and pixie strike, but we've had some other things pop up over the past few weeks that have taken priority. Regarding chains, it's working as it is supposed to. I understand that the claim is that it doesn't work as well as it did before NPC change that wasn't necessarily supposed to affect it, however upon looking into it we've determined that the way it works now is sufficient for the spell.

    [Quote from original post: 5. Why are players allowed to remove coins from the trade window?]

    This is on the list. It amazes me the lengths that people will go through to scam each other. The sheer number of the reports of scams of this nature is astounding. GMs litterally spend 3/4ths of their day dealing with scams.

    [Quote from original post: 7. The Play Nice Policies state that killing green mobs is "Not Playing Nice."]

Perhaps its the fact that the BLUE servers are so crowded and more people are being less friendly. In any case, the one thing that keeps me from playing PVP now is the same thing that always has. There is no penalty for killing another player. There is a penalty for dying though.]

    Play on Teams server (VZ or TZ). Both servers sport faction penalties for killing other players. They are pretty severe. Usually within 4 or 5 kills you'll be KOS to the hometown of the target.

    [Quote from original post: My suggestion is to allow players to turn DROPPABLE items into NO DROP items through an NPC and make them unsellable]

    The way items work in EQ would not support this. The items are not objects in and of themselves, but rather a reference (by number) to a central database. That means that we can't set attributes such as nodrop on a per-item basis.

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:37 AM PDT
Gordon made a few late night posts on the Gameplay Board recently. Here's the skinny:

  • Alchemy
    Wow, good thread. I'm glad that I finally happened across it. I'll forward the bugs that were mentioned to the testing team, and the ideas over to the guy who's handling the revamping of all of the trade skills.

    To be honest, since Alchemy was one of the most recent ones to get a revamp, first for items and availability, and later for cost of produciton, it's probably not going to be at the top of the list for enhancements going forward. However we can probably make some headway pretty soon here on the bugs.

    I guess we should also pop up the price of fish gill extract as well :)

  • Lack of CS
    I'm not sure how to respond to this. You shouldn't give your account info to other people. Yes, what someone did to you is wrong, and if we find them we'll surely take action, but it was perpetuated by you giving out your account info, which is "expressly forbidden" by the EQ EULA.

    If players reap consequences by engaging in activities that are forbidden by the EULA, they need to be held accountable for the consequences of those actions. Our GMs are too involved taking action in cases where issues are out of no fault of the player to compell them to "hand out items" for everyone who gives out their account info. Do not give out your account information to anyone, and you won't have these problems.

    I do apologize to the original author for apparently getting the run-around over this issue, as he should have been told up front that the problem was out of our hands.

  • Low Delay Weapon Debate
    [Quote from original post: Thousands of low delay weapons? Thats 100S3 >>>Twigs, 1 Whip on Luclin Where the hell do you get 1000's?]

    From a database query.

    Why am I seeing so many posts along these lines? We do have ways of determining what is in game and in what numbers. Truthfully, if it were only a matter of a couple on each server, we would have written them off as lucky and left them in game as is. But that isn't the case. There are quite a few of them on each server, though each poster here might only be aware of a few. There are roughly 8000 accounts playing on each server, half of which have characters capable of acquiring those items where they existed. No one person can say with certainty how many there are without direct access to our database.

  • Math and Logic
    [Quote from original post: NO Abashi, its still only a 20% chance, since each spawn is independant of each other. ]

    You are indeed correct that each roll has absolutely nothing to do with the other. However, the percentages are based upon individual term binomial distribution probability. If you have a 20% chance of success (loot) per trial (kill), the chances of at least 1 success in 6 trials is just under 74%. However six trials remains constant. If after the first trial you did not get a drop, your chances of getting one in the next 6 is still 74%. If after five more trials you still haven't gotten one, your chance of getting one in the next 6 drops is still 74%.

    The probability is determined before the trails, and does not improve as the trials are completed (actually gets worse). For instance, lets say that we have the situation above. 20% of Frogs will drop the item. Lets say you decide that you are going to kill 6 frogs:

    Before you kill the first frog, your chances of getting at least one item in the next 6 kills is: 73.79%
    After you kill the first frog, your chances of getting at least one item in the next 5 kills is: 67.23%
    After you kill the second frog, your chances of getting at least one item in the next 4 kills is: 59.04%
    After you kill the third frog, your chances of getting at least one item in the next 3 kills is: 48.80%
    After you kill the fourth frog, your chances of getting at least one item in the next 2 kills is: 36.00%
    After you kill the fifth frog, your chances of getting at least one item in the next kill is: 20.00%

    Previous rolls are never taken into account, since they do not impact future results. However future results are impacted by the probability success versus the number of trials.

    You can set up a spreadsheet to calculate these probabilities using the BINOMDIST function in Excel.

    [Quote from original post: Yes bad logic follows bad logic. Please take a intro class to statistics to learn how bad of a job you guys are doing of making what you want happen ]

    I might ask the same of you before you come here to flame.

Gordon Infobits
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:59 PM PDT
Gordon stated he had too many espressos last night as he went on an early morning binge posting on the Gameplay Board. As always, here's the scoop:

  • Gordon: What are you doing up this late?
    I made the mistake of indulging in one-too-many espressos when I got home this evening. It has me up a little later than normal :)

  • /Who Question
    No /who listing will ever give level if a character is /anon or /roleplay. You'll have to rely either on what they say or the return from /con.

  • "Why can I not see my fire ball when I cast "Fire Bolt"?"
    We moved missle creation server-side recently. That probably has something to do with the problem. I'll look into it Monday.

  • Rogues Killing Greenies: Playing Nice?
    [Quote from original post: Where do the rogues stand on the play nice issue of killing multiple green mobs to obtain poison components.

    I'm asking because a player complained they were going to report me for killing greens that would have granted them EXP. I didn't kill steal any mobs they attacked but i didn't really leave much for em to kill.]

    Unless there's something more to this to which I am not privy, there's generally no problem with you killing whatever you wish providing that you aren't stealing the kills.


  • Corpse Question
    [Quote from original post: Why in world is there a zone line that leads to instant death and an almost unrecoverable corpse?]

    The corpse is not unrecoverable. You can zone into the "preferred" entrance to the Hole and get a necro to summon your corpse.

    As for why it's like this, that is just how the Hole is designed, and your death is the price of the knowledge that there might be a better way to go about what you are trying. Many things in EverQuest have to be learned, and are likely to cause death until they are. You should enter new areas cautiously until you learn how general rules and physics apply there.

    Falling into an instant death situation is really no different than turning a corner and finding an NPC against which you have no chance given your current group makeup. Things of this nature need to be discovered and learned.

    When the Hole first opened, we did put a short, somewhat vague, message in the patch message, since jumping in the Hole previously teleported people out to Toxxulia. The message was "GMs will not recover corpses for people who jump, fall, or levitate into the Hole".

  • Bracer of Vajeen: Why can't Halflings wear it?
    I'm not sure about this particular item, however if halflings were accidently excluded, we'll see about getting them put back on. I'll have to check into it on Monday. It's possible that they may have been purposely excluded.

  • "Why GM Events Don't Work"
    [Quote from original post: GM events have always involved killing something.]

    Not true. There have been many events that have taken place as stories to be witnessed and participated in. There have also been events where "killing something" has only been a very minor part of the event, rather than the central focus. Of course, we've always had the problem of getting people to refrain from killing the story-tellers, but we've taken steps to prevent that. Read below.

    [Quote from original post: Who are the people in the game who can always kill something regardless of lvl? The lvl 50+s. So when you make a GM event and have most of the Mobs be around lvl 35, the people who are gonna kill them are 50+s.]

    The majority of events deal with actor npcs (npcs played by gms) rather than spawned NPCs (npcs that are still under the control of the servers). We've implemented the ability for the GMs to place a maximum level on who may attack them. That solves the majority of the problems that you cite.

    [Quote from original post: As for risk vs. Reward, these lvl 55s have NO risk killing lvl 30 mobs, and are getting all this ph4+ l3w+.]

    Finally, I should mention that actor npcs hardly ever carry "phat lewt". We abolished that practice some months ago in order to avoid having the reward go whomever could kill the actor first.

  • "Ack! Too many spell book pages!"
    Actually we're implementing a command that will let you flip to a specific page of your spell book.

  • Howling Stones & Dungeons
    When dungeons or new areas are designed, they have to be implemented as if they always existed. As such, it's possible that the quest would "require" some bottom feeding by people who have already out-classed the dungeon where the quest components are located. However, someone who is in the process of building their character could very well happen across the components during normal adventuring.
