The Lost Scroll - Mithanial Marr
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 7:30 PM PDT
Lefourgue informed me of the following:

THE LOST SCROLL
A Roleplay event on Mithaniel Marr
When: Friday the 20h of October, from 3 PM to 6 PM (EST)
Where: East Commons, on Mithaniel Marr
Who: Lvl 6-10 characters

From Lefourgue Beauregard, head of the Lodge of Bayle:

Our investigation on the rumoured return of Miragul led us to study ancient
texts and records, relating the tragic tale of the woman who opened her arms
and her books of arcane lore for him. She was a Teir'Dal sorceress, the
first of her kind to tread so far on the forbidden path of Necromancy.
Her name was Argarwaen. A daughter of Neriak's royal family, she had no lust
for secular power, only for the surge of tainted mana and the thrill of
playing hide and seek with Death itself. Unwilling to play the political
game she was taught as a potential heir to the throne, she left her hometown
for newfound Erudin.

Her true nature disguised by powerful illusions, she learned much from the
Human mages. To those who had a dark shard stuck in their hearts, she taught
the rudiments of her own art. Thus, she built a court of followers. It is
well known how Miragul joined this secret college of outcasts. But few are
those who know that he became the lover of the Teir'Dal princess.
When the war broke out between the Erudites and the Heretics, Miragul fled
north. As for Argarwaen, she left Odus and built herself an underground
palace in what is now known as the Commonlands. There, our sources diverge.
Some scholars pretend she became mad out of waiting for her lover's return,
others hint that she was cursed by Miragul himself because she had failed
him in one way of another, and a third school of thought suggests that Death
is not without its sick sense of humour, and that the Great Harvester of
Souls refused to sever Argarwaen's lifecord, sentencing the Dark Elf to an
eternity of torment. However, all agree on their conclusion: Argarwaen's
spirit never found peace.

What is the relevance of this story with our current research you may ask.
Well, learn that our Erudite brethren from Paineel have unearthed a long
forgotten scroll which could allow us to summon Argarwaen's restless ghost
and ask her about Miragul's whereabouts!

Alas, the scroll has been stolen by a perfidious shadowknight named
Filsdebayle. We tracked the traitor, cornered him in East Commons and killed
him, but the precious document was nowhere to find. Further investigation
led us to believe that it is now in the hands of the Deathfist Orcs, with
whom Filsdebayle had contracted an alliance...

/ooc GameLoft, a French site dedicated to EverQuest, invites all level 6-10
characters to attend its third in-game event, which will start in East
Commonlands, and culminate in Befallen. We promise you thrill, romance,
blood, spectacular lightshow and free beer! More precious gifts will also
reward the heroes of the event. Departure will take place at the Nektulos
pass in EC, but if you are late, you can join us at Orc 2 (until 4 PM).
To join in, please indicate your class, race and level to Radharc
[eobert@gameloft.com]. Veterans, you are welcome if you wish to help us
protect and reward the participants!
Thanks!

  • Recent Bard Tournament
    Do I expect a level 29 to defeat a level 39?

    No I do not.

    Do I expect a level 29 to enter the event for knowledge, learning, and to have fun, so that in future events he can do better?

    Yes I do.

    We are trying to make things fun for as many players as possible. This means that there needs to be a wide spread of levels. While I would love to keep it +/- 4 levels (Rallos Zek range), we are trying to get the excitement out to everyone.

    We are aware of the level differences involved in the match. This is the best we can do with still having a decent size event, Im sorry.

    We are aware of the twinking, and how they ruin events, but unless the items are obsene for their level, we are leaving them alone. Again, my appologies.

All communication with me, as the raid organizer, will be via /tell, leaving the public channels open for use by the battle commander. Producer Letter: Wrinn Promoted, Absor New PR Rep
[24 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:22 PM PDT
A big announcement was posted this evening to the Gameplay Board by Verant Interactive's Gordon Wrinn. In this post he announces his promotion to Associate Producer of EQ Live and the Scars of Velious as well as welcoming Alan “Absor” VanCouvering, formly EQX's editor-in-chief, as his replacement. Here's the complete letter:

    Hello all,

    We've got a new producer letter going up here in the next few minutes. I thought I'd stop by here and post it special for those camping the boards

    -Gordon

    ------

    Hello all,

    It seems like only yesterday that I came to Verant to build our community relations by acting as a two-way conduit of information between the players and the development team. In fact, it’s been nearly a year since I began representing the company to the players, and in return, the players to the company.

    We’ve certainly had some bumps in the road, but overall I think that the development team and the community of players have grown much closer over the past year. We’ve addressed a lot of issues, solved a number of problems, and overall improved the game as a result of our relationship. We now have more development team members posting on the boards than ever before and our response time to issues in game has dropped dramatically. Furthermore, we’ve become much more flexible in terms of addressing everyone’s issues. We’ve seen a number of old bugs addressed, and implemented numerous enhancements, both those related to a particular class, and to the players as a whole.

    Many of you have noticed that I’ve been around a little less than normal over the past couple of weeks. Some have gotten the impression that we’d become less resilient in our goal to maintain a strong community. I can now let everyone know that this is not the case, that our commitment to our players is stronger than ever, and that we remain committed to continually improving our relationship with EverQuest players.

    Today I am pleased to announce that Alan “Absor” VanCouvering, formerly of EverQuest Express (eqx.simplenet.com), has joined the EverQuest Team. Alan will be taking over my position as Community Relations Manager of EverQuest, and will be responsible for taking our community relations to the next level.   • (Client Warning) What is it?
    Sounds like a sync problem.

    Not everything in the game is either on the client or the server, some things are done on both. For example, if something is controlled server-side, the server has to tell the client what is or what is not legal (requiring network traffic). If it's controlled client-side, the client tells the server what is and what is not illegal (also requring network traffic).

    In cases where something is on both ends, both the client and the server decide what is legal, and the only time there is network traffic regarding the illegality of an operation is when they are in disagreement.

    Spell-casting is a good example of this. The client knows the delays of spells and makes buttons visible or grays them out based on this. Someone can hack their client to make it ungray spells sooner than it should. However, in case someone does that, the server also knows the delay of spells and will "speak up" should someone send bad data. Overall it results in far less network traffic than having everything controlled on one side.

    The lag that you experienced in this case appeared to cause out-of-the-ordinary delays that caused the server and client to lose sync.

Gordon Infobits: Saturday Edition
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:42 PM PDT
The following is this Saturday's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Epic Quests
    [Quote from original post: Frankly your odds of actually getting the loot needed are slim to none when every other mage on your server is vying for same thing. ]

    As I mentioned in another post, this is how it's supposed to work. We *understand* that everyone wants these items. We created them in order to ensure that that would be the case. However we cannot *give* them to everyone without there being serious reprocussions on the balance of power in the world.

    Remember that once these items go in, unless we create UberEpic quest items at some point in the future, they represent the most powerful item for your class in the game. Other held-items pale by comparison. We just can't have everyone running around with them and maintain some semblance of a game.

  • DirectX Upgrade
    [Quote from original post: While visiting test, I noticed that the frame rate does tend to get a bit jerky]

    There are some DX related framerate issues on Test that we're working on, and intend to fix before bringing the new changes live.

  • "Putting Two and Two Together"
    [Quote from original post: -Brad has often said that more and more people are coming into the game.
    -Gordon has often said that they wish to limit the rate at which rare items enter the game.

    Well, maybe, if they put their heads together, and thought about it, they would see that they need to UP THE RATE RARE ITEMS ENTER THE WORLD AT!]

    Your point suggests that new people are all joining and wanting a piece of finite server resources, which isnt' the case. This is already happening. To use your two points above, we've added more servers, so technically items are entering the "game" faster, just by virtue of there being new servers.

    Now on a per-world basis (which is what is important since the economy is measured on a per world basis), the rate at which items come in has also gone up, not by means of percentages, but as a result of item-carrying creatures being killed at a faster rate.

    [Quote from original post: Frankly, I see NO advantage to restricting the rate at which powerful items enter the world, except to keep people playing for longer, which is, of course, the Vision in a nutshell. ]

    Let's imagine a world where: Everyone has everything. Everyone starts at level 60, has an unlimited supply of platinum, and can buy everything off of vendors. Who would play on this server? A lot of people in the beginning, but that would soon drop off to nearly nothing when people realize that there is no challenge in obtaining anything. Item value, and in this case that value is the prestige in overcoming a challenge and having something that isn't too common, is inversely proportional to the availability of the item. This same formula can be applied to levels as well, which describes the "race to level" that exists on new servers.

    So technically, yes, limited item availability is about getting people to subscribe to the game longer. Just as the experience curve does the same thing. But neither of these have a direct correlation. Item availability and speed of advancement correlate directly to challenge, and the prestige associated with overcoming that challenge. It is the challenge that directly correlates to the length of time that you play the game.

    Games are about overcoming challenges. If we cease to provide you challenges to overcome, you will not play the game.

  • Karana
    Karana was split very recently in the grand scheme of things. As it turns out not nearly as many people chose to split as we would have liked, even upon subsequent movelogs where we let it be known that we had tons of room and would be able to service all-comers.

    Secondly, the "2500" figure that was being reported wasn't accurate. There's a problem that we're trying to nail down that causes the world server to stop releasing people when it is supposed to. When this happens, people can't get back into the server, and each attempt causes the population to increment even though they aren't successfully getting back in. We're trying to find out what causes this. It currently happens on one server once or twice a week, making it pretty intermittent.

    October 13th, 7:01pm
    ------------------------------

    *Important System Configuration Announcement*

    As many of you know, over the past couple of months we've made numerous enhancements to the EverQuest client. In addition to things such as new /-commands and other changes of that nature, we've enhanced the UI itself to allow moveable and resizable windows, among other things. At the same time we've been working on a brand new sound engine, and have been further concentrating on optimizing and expanding the capabilities of the EverQuest client.

    For most applications, the feature set of a version of DirectX is limited to the lowest common denominator between the version that the software is compiled under, and the version running on the client. This means that though you may be running DirectX 7.0a already, and are receiving advantages associated with the more advanced code, the feature-set of DirectX used when in EverQuest is limited to version 5.2.

    This limits the type and breadth of enhancements that can be made to the client. Given that we feel that EverQuest players would enjoy continuing to see new enhancements for the client, we will soon begin compiling the client under DirectX 7.0a, and implementing the feature-set available within that version. When we do this, everyone will need DirectX 7.0a (or higher) to play EverQuest.

    Here are some of the possible benefits from DirectX 7.0a:

    - Better performance (Graphics and Sound)
    - Gamma Correction working in D3D
    - Proper Mip-Mapping in D3D
    - Fixes fogging on some cards. (Some cards have no fogging at all and look pretty bad)
    - Allows us to use newer interfaces to support such features as full-screen anti-aliasing, and alternate Z-buffering methods, which tend to look better.

    Changing to DX7 does have a potential downside. Those using older or discontinued video or sound cards that do not have DX7 compatible drivers may need to upgrade to continue playing. However, we feel that the benefits that will come from upgrading to this newer version of DirectX outweigh the potential problems.

    We plan to begin requiring DirectX7.0a when the EverQuest client is next patched, in two or three weeks. We wanted to be sure to give everyone enough time to check their version of DirectX and install the newest version. You can run DXDIAG.EXE in your Windows\System directory to check your DirectX version and get the newest version of DirectX from http://www.microsoft.com/directx if it is needed.

    Please keep an eye on the patch message for further information regarding the planned implementation of DX7.

Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:57 PM PDT
Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Deleted Posts
    [Quote from original post: When customers have legitimate concerns, do you just delete posts at a whim?]

    The thread had far too many personal attacks in it. New posts to the thread were either additional personal attacks or complaints about the personal attacks. Though that might not have been the original subject of the thread, that's what it turned in to and why it was deleted.

    I'm not going to spend a whole lot of time sending individual posts to the moderator for cleaning. If a thread seems to attract more offending posts than proper ones, we'll just delete the whole thread.

  • Halting a Spell
    While I've never heard of a 2 second pause being insufficient, I have seen this effect before and imagine that it has something to do with the way prediction is implemented. The client and server do not send position data constantly while you are running, but instead update position data either at a regular interval, or when something significant changes. Basically, the server thinks you are still moving even though from your point of view you are stopped.

    The trick here is to be sure that the server receives the "I stopped" packet before it gets the "I started casting" packet. The best way around this I've found is to turn slightly upon stopping. I always had a much easier time of getting my spells off once I started doing that.

  • Spellcasting Bug
    This has happened a few times in the past, and is caused by a network problem on our end. It's a fairly quick fix not requiring server downtime. We apologize for the problem.

  • Working as Intended
    [Quote from original post: It procs a dmg absorption shield and Berserker Strength. When the shield is used up, the Strength componenet is still active.]

    You might want to take another look. The berserker strength spell does include a "skin", however the strength portion does not remain when the shield is used up.

    That said, we are kicking around the idea of removing that component from the bard songs based on it being a bad combination.

  • The Wishlist Board
    I've posted before on the subject. Though people here read those boards regularly, we'll rarely respond to things on the wishlist board. Wishes, by their nature, are things that are unlikely to happen. Responses on that board would typically be along the lines of "I'm sorry but..." or "That's a good idea but..."

    The board would become a negative focus because we would constantly be justifying why wishes either don't fit into EQ, or are good but not something that we are likely to implement. Right now those boards are a good idea resource, but I fear that they would degenerate if we were over there responding to posts regularly.


Xegony Guild Council Creation
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:08 PM PDT
The following information was sent in by Ilboris:

This is an open invitation to create a Xegony Guild Council for all guilds on
xegony. All are welcomed. The idea is borrowed from the similiar Karana Guild
Council on Karana Server which works very well.

The main idea is to have bi-weekly meetings to discuss all things that
concern us, to set up and schedule hunts, events, and raids and voting for
anything needed to be voted on for all those who wish to join this. A
different guild gets to host the meetings every 2 weeks. Hopefully someone
can create a web site for use with the Council's own message board for the
members but that will come later.

If you are interested in such a undertaking please ask your guild leader and
a guest to come to the Arena zone in Lake Rathetear on October 21 at 9 PM
Eastern Time. This is open to all guilds big and small. Everquest staff is
also welcomed to attend.

I hope to see everyone who is interested there next Saturday.

Ilboris
Legacy of Lycanthropes
Xegony Server
