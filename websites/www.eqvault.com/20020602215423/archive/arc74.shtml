I Flee, Therefore I Am
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:27 PM PDT
Gordon talked about NPCs fleeing in the following thread he started on the Gameplay Board:

    Hello all,

    Last week while optimizing code, one of the programmers fixed a variable initilization problem that resulted in the fixing of a bug that had been around a long time in regards to when NPCs decide to flee. As a result of this, NPCs became much more likely to flee in combat. The response to this has been mixed, with roughly an equal number of people on both sides of the issue.

    Now that the problem was fixed, we've reevaluated the the bravery code and determined that they flee too often now. As such, that potential to flee will be reduced in tomorrow's patch, though they will still flee more than they did before.

    That said, the same change also had a decidedly negative effect: Charmed creatures began fleeing. I think everyone can agree that this part of it was pretty well objectionable, so we're going to stop charmed creatures from fleeing.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition

The following contain all of last night's late posts written by Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • Mages and Regeneration
    [Quote from original post: First I'd like to say that giving Regeneration to Mages was a bad idea and addition to the Mage class. Yes I am aware that Regeneration was added to the Phantom line even prior to RoK, but why?, is my question.]

    We wanted the Phantom armor series to be useful to the mages. We didn't want to resort to increasing AC as in order to make it useful in that regard, the AC value would have had to be more than those of other classes.

  • Closing Threads
    I'm sorry cthon, but we need to encourage new ideas here on the board rather than revisiting the old ones over and over like a skipping record. We have these boards because they are a benefit to us. They can only be a benefit to us if they are a benefit to the players, which is the same thing. These boards cannot be a benefit to the players if we're constantly stuck in neutral. The point that they cease to benefit the players they cease to benefit us and they will be closed down. Let's see that that doesn't happen.

  • Starting Out on New Server
    You must be quite the adventurer Azrael!

    Though I wish you well on your journey, I might remind you that it will be some time before anyone will be capable of binding you. Though I think that it's possible for you to make the entire trip once, a few newbie deaths before anyone can bind could make things difficult.

  • Abashi Biased?
    I knew this would come up. I almost put a disclaimer at the bottom explaining that more classes than the necro have fear spells, and that the suggested problem is pretty black and white making it a simple test, but I thought better of it.

  • Necros
    [Quote from original post: Why isn't this game called "letsseehowlongittakestogeteveryonetostartanecroQuest" ]

Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:57 PM PDT
Here are the posts Gordon Wrinn wrote this afternoon on the Gameplay Board:

  • Mark of Karn - What went wrong?
    Before I close the thread, I'd hardly say that the issue regarding Mark of Karn qualified as a "fiasco". It was an overpowered spell that we addressed. End of story.

    Yes, it should have been balance-tested (different from bug testing) under more diverse circumstances, but it wasn't, and we apologize for that. That being the case, it didn't become evident until more than a few clerics picked up the spell that it was a problem.

    And just a note, the balance problems with this spell was brought up more by clerics than other classes.

  • Mark of Karn Post
    There's no "politics". The spell was too powerful in its original configuration. Given that, the change to its current state was made. At that point we put together another option that we felt would balance the spell and return it to usefulness again. The players here on the board accepted that option as better than its current state. Returning it to its old state, or anywhere near that state, was not one of the options that we made available, as it would be unbalancing to the game.

  • Invisibility
    [Quote from original post: It's reliable enough. It's a rare occasion when your invisibility breaks precisely at the wrong moment and gets you killed. At least you get a message now, something that didn't exist before. You have about 3 seconds to duck of of sight and recast it. I think it adds spice to the game. Otherwise you could just stroll through dangerous dungeon just like it was a park (and not central park). That kind of "sure thing" is against the vision TM]

    Yes, I'd have to agree with Licinius here. The invisibility spells are not completely reliable, nor should they be for what they grant. As he said, the times that it breaks at precisely the wrong moment are pretty slim in the grand scheme of things.

  • Begging
    Beg was an idea that didn't work well in early implementation, so we decided to leave it without much use. It was a good idea, but relatively impossible to balance concerning the focus of the game.

Events and Quests
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:11 AM PDT
Lady Dae elaborated a bit on the difference between events and quests. Here's the post:

    There seems to be a misunderstanding between Events and Quests.

    Events change Character (Player) and Server history, Quests change Norrath's history.

    Events are run with GM's and Guides being GM's and Guides, Quests are run with GM-NPC's that can be anything from Gnolls to Dragons.

    GM's that are involved in a quest usually are designed to be slain by players. GM's that are involved with an Event are never designed to be slain.

    The only difficult point is the creation of true NPC's which both can do to make the Event or Quest better.

    99 percent of Events take place in the Arena. I have never seen a Quest run in the Arena.

    Events are almost always pre-announced, with rules, times, and dates. Quests are almost never pre-announced, and usually have very limited rules for players.

    Hopefully this will clear up the misunderstandings between what the "Event Staff" and "Quest Team" are.

    Lady Daegarmo
    Online Events Coordinator
    Verant Interactive

    One clarification to this statement:

The Return of Lanys T'vyl
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:32 PM PDT
Lanys T'vyl has returned to Norrath. Our first report is from Kathai:

Last night, Emissary D'Veiu of the Tier'Dal nation called forth
throughout Nektulos forest for the assistance of those loyal to The
Dead. Her call was answered by many dark elves, who offered their
assistance in the bringing of Lanys T'Vyl to Neriak on order of Queen
Cristianos Thex. No harm was to come to Lanys, on punishment of death.

Through a blood ritual performed with the assistance of a shadowknight
named Kaydan, Lanys was summoned and bound to Nektulos forest at the
wizard pyramid. There Lanys was offered sanctuary one last time, before
the Teir'Dal armies of The Dead were brought to force against her
selfish plans. It was revealed that there is now a New Chosen, a young
child named Valizrae D`Tora. This child had been captured by Lanys,
with the intent of torturing and ritually sacrificing the child so as to
prevent her from stealing Lanys' position as the Chosen. However, the
Emissary revealed to Lanys that the location of the child was already
known by The Dead, and armies were already set in place to strike
against Lanys and her followers. Despite this, Lanys refused sanctuary
and set herself irrevocably on her destiny to war.

The prophet Ashteth T'Dral appeared during the bitter war of words,
speaking against Lanys' foolishness. The hatred flowing in dialogue
between the two sent a chill into even the most hardened Teir'Dal
watching. In the end, Lanys escaped the power of the binding and left.
The Emissary left to report to the Queen, leaving Ashteth to warn those
watching - a war is coming soon, and the full glory of the Teir'Dal
nation will soon follow.

Plane of Sky Raid - Tallon Zek
[16 Comments] - Aurum @ 12:16 PM PDT
I received the following news from Aeeleb:

On Saturday September 23rd and continuing into the 24th, brave adventurers
from numerous guilds of the light race teams on Tallon Zek - led by Darkoan
of the Vale - stepped into the Plane of Sky. Their goal was to reach and
clear the 3rd island, with the second island never being passed before by
any team on Tallon Zek.

After a quick recovery from a defeat on Azerack island, they continued on
past that island and setup for what was the hardest adventure many of them
had ever faced. Precision and skillful pulling on the 3rd island lead to a
near flawless defeat of this island, and keeping deaths in the single
digits.

Once the 4th island was reached Gunzo Vanderhain became the first person -
and for quite awhile longer, the only person, to wield the Fiery Avenger on
Tallon Zek.

Having accomplished many firsts for Tallon Zek, many of the adventurers went
on that night to do what we do best, kill inkies :)
