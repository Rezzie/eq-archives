  • Verant's Attitude Toward Solo'ers
    [Quote from original post: What he means is that if you have solo'd your entire career, you will not be making all of the connections with other characters. If I don't know you from Adam and no one else I know does, the odds are we're not going to be to thrilled with the idea of letting you into our group where you may kill us.]

    I'd say that's an accurate description of the issues. Grouping is a skill that needs to be developed early on. Certainly, some classes support soloing, but we've always said that soloers not be able to go everywhere and do everything. If going everywhere and doing everything is important to the person who plays a soloable class, I'd certainly recommend that they work on forming relationships early on and throughout the leveling process.

  • How do you pronounce Kunark?
    Koo-nark :)

  • European Players & Overcrowding on Karana
    European players do not measurably contribute to server crowding at peak times due to the fact that their prime-time is different from that in the states. I'd attribute crowding on Karana to be more due to the fact that most really don't want to leave. The last split offered to Karana was prepared to move 1200 people, yet only 139 actually requested a move.

    I'm not really sure how to go about splitting up the server further when the community has become so strong that no one wants to leave. We're willing to discuss suggestions of course.

  • Age Discrimination?
    First, CS Reps do not have access to account passwords.

    Second, I'm not sure why you see this as an age discrimination issue. In situation number 1, if the person proved that he bought the accounts, they would then be banned. He would not be given access back to them, and it would be up to him to pursue action against the person that sold the account. In situation number 2, we get people calling us all of the time telling us that their account was stolen. They are required to prove ownership to get the account back. If it turns out that they did sell it, and the buyer calls us up, it becomes issue #1, and the account would be banned. In neither case does age come into the equation.

    Original owners are always required to prove ownership, and are given the account back if they can (via a password reset). Buyers are also required to prove that they bought it, however once they do the account it banned.

    We do not allow transfers of accounts, and will not support it nor open ourselves to getting involved with the fraud associated with them.

  • Poor Customer Service
    I do agree that the way the CSRep phrased his response was poor, and I'd like to extend you our apologies.

    In regards to your query as to whether the decision to change the item to offhand only is final, it indeed is. Though I understand your feelings regarding this issue, the encounter from which this item was obtainable and the quantities that it dropped are not sufficient for the power of the item.

  • Problems With EQ: Crashing
    I've heard of heat-crashes before, and it sounds like this is what you may have been experiencing. Kunark is more processer-intensive than the mainland, so would cause more heat to build up. I can see an overclocking your CPU causing a problem like this.

  • Don't Roll For Loot!
    I'd have to agree with the original posters feelings, though somewhat more politely :) I'd never consider rolling for something that my character could not use if there was someone in the group who could .

  • Reducing Player Burnout
    I believe that the requirement to look at the spellbook goes away at 32 rather than 35. That said, arguments could always be made to have it go away sooner. It's a matter of providing rewards for continuing to gain levels. People gain levels for those rewards, and this is yet another goal that people can reach. We could lower it to 30, but I imagine that we'd see the same requests to lower it to 28, 25, or get rid of it altogether. In this case we've decided that 32 is a good level for that requirment to be overcome.

  • Enchanters Without Breeze
    [Quote from original post: When you added the Breeze spell to the Enchanter series, I don't understand why you made this spell only purchasable in Kunarak. Since this spell is such a well known Enchanter spell shouldn't all Enchanter guilds sell it?]

    The spell was added as part of the Kunark expansion. Kunark expansion spells are available only in Kunark. We did, however, decide that the spell would not be NODROP, thus allowing someone to carry them back to the mainland and sell them there for a profit. In my opinion, it has worked out pretty well in implementation.


Big News For Rangers
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:39 PM PDT
The Ranger class will be upgraded says EverQuest Producer Brad McQuaid. Here's the formal announcement from the Gameplay Board:

    After a LOT of internal testing, interacting with the community, and major debate, we've decided to upgrade Rangers as follows:

    We will be upping their dodge and parry to near Warrior levels. I don't want to get into specifics yet because we are still fine tuning, but the end result is that a Ranger should be significantly more survivable.

    I'm personally happy with the solution because, rather than just increasing defense and making them more Warrior-like, we've upped skills that are more traditionally associated with Rangers (the view that they are nimble, chain-wearing fighters who often avoid attacks).

    I'd also like to pre-announce a future goal and policy change:

    Soon (we'll announce a date in the future), we're going to completely stop adjusting game balance as it relates to tweaking classes. Recently, we've made more changes than usual due to the level limits being raised from 50 to 60. Soon, however, we're going to come to a point where it stops.

    We'll continue to add content (i.e. items, spells, etc.) and game balance will still be tweaked (and will continue to be tweaked as long as we do add content). But class balance changes as a whole will cease.

    The Ranger upgrade is part of this. I'd say that hopefullly by early 2001 we'll be done.

    Abashi or I will post more on this as we work out the plan in detail internally.

    -Brad

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:03 PM PDT
Hot off the Gameplay Board, here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • NPC Bards
    [Quote from original post: Why don't NPC Bards use their songs?]

    There are targeting problem. NPC bards singing an offensive AoE song would also affect any other NPC in the area. In many cases, it would cause those other NPCs to turn on the NPC bard.

  • EQMail Bug Reports
    The request for bug reports to EQMail and the award program only applies to Dupe Bugs. I don't expect it to be a very large volume at all. In fact, if the past two days are any indication, hardly any volume.

    It won't take time away from helping with CS issues.

  • Follow-up to Discussion of the Week
    I think that if anything, the thoughts presented supported my original post where I spoke about the diversity of EverQuests players, and the fact that many people see different things as fun. Some spoke, for instance, about trade skills being fun, while some others said specifically that they weren't fun.

    The task before us is to provide more. More options. More content. More things to do. We need to provide as many avenues to fun as possible, without infringing on other players' idea of fun. For instance, there are those who find it fun to cause other players grief. We do not wish to support that playstyle.

    EverQuest already has many avenues to fun available to the players, leaving it up to you to decide which avenues to take. We can always provide more, as long as those avenues do not cause other avenues to break down as mentioned above.

  • Water Races
    [Quote from original post: There is already such a race in EQ: The Kedge.

    Of course, They went extinct centuries ago.]

    Well not completely, as Phinegal somehow keeps rising from the dead. But they are huge anyway :)

  • Balancing the Classes
    [Quote from original post: You missed the point completely ! There is a huge amount of work that still needs to be done in balancing the classes.]

    That's certainly a valid opinion, but it remains an opinion. As I've mentioned before, regardless the status of the classes, and no matter what changes we make, there will always be people who have opinions regarding the relative power of the classes. The only way to avoid this would be to make all classes the same, at which point we wouldn't have a class-based system and thus the game wouldn't be EverQuest.

    As you mentioned in your post, nothing is exactly black and white. The concept of balance fits this perfectly. Balance is not a point that we aim for, but a range. Some classes might be at the bottom of that range, other classes might be more towards the top. It could be said that we should keep making adjustments until everyone is in the middle, but it should be obvious to anyone looking at these boards that we're not going to get universal agreement regardless.

    Right now our biggest concerns at higher levels is can you get a group. No class is so disadvantaged that they cannot get a group, providing that they've cultivated the friendships necessary to do so. Does this mean that a character that has soloed from 1 to a high level might not be able to get a group? Certainly, but that is OK. That is one of the prices of choosing to do nothing but solo.

    I haven't yet heard from anyone who will say that they have a bunch of friends in game, yet all of them say "sorry dude, I can't group with [your class] any more." People choose their groups not only based upon a specific need, but based upon who is "cool" and whose company they enjoy.

  • Monk Speed
    We'd decided long ago that a faster running speed wouldn't be a part of a class-specific skill, but would be handled via spells and such.

  • Age Matters?
    [Quote from original post: I am not bashing younger people with this comment. It is mostly the young that come up with innovative ideas. But without a certain degree of guidence from more experienced folks...innovation becomes idiocy.

    I have said it before....I saw the clips from the EQ gathering...and from the look there is not one Verant VIP over 25.]

    Age isn't important. It's ability. That said, I'm not sure where you got the "25" figure, but a good number of people are over that age.

  • No Response Bias
    [Quote from original post: Some people on this board are using the posts of others to show how bad one thing or the other is. How many of us that read these boards and are somewhat happy with what Verant has released. It is time for our voices to be heard. Verant has released a good product. I am not saying that it is perfect because everything has room for improvement. I may just be feeling this way because I am in this profession as well and realize the amount of work that has to go into even some of the littlest of tasks. I just want to encourage everyone who is happy with what is happening with Everquest to start to post. You may start getting more if the programmers feel like the work is appreciated.]

    Thanks for the note doc, and you are correct in that the programmers enjoy feeling appreciated. I and everyone else wish that players would go on and on about things that please them, but understand that it is not exactly human nature to do so.

    We're going to try again this Tuesday, with the implementation of another stunning UI enhancement, the biggest yet, care of Rowyl. I don't want to talk much about it yet until I'm sure that it will make that patch package, but it should be very well received.

  • Corpse Location
    [Quote from original post: 1. get someone and pay that person alot of money to help me find my body.
    2. Lose my body (fat chance)
    3. Attempt to locate a gm and ask for their assistance to locate and perhaps retrieve my body.]

    Don't forget bards, necros, and shadowknights. All three have the ability to locate your corpse, and necros can summon it if you can come up with the cash.

    /loc is an extreme out-of-character tool that was left in game under some objections upon lauch. It wouldn't be appropriate to expand its use in this manner.

  • Memorized Spell Limit
    [Quote from original post: So, the question is why have only 8?]

    It also has the effect of creating risk since you could have the wrong spell lineup ready for an unexpected encounter. Better get someone to watch your back while you remem :)
