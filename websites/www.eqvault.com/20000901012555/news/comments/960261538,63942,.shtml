Camping is a disgrace. I hate it. But worse is I hate the FACT that camping is forced upon us at times. Some items cannot be bought. Whether you agree with Saxxon or insult him, the FACT is, and the state of many aspects of this game are, he had to camp to complete a quest. Where is the quest? How can one possibly equate luck as in right place at the right time as being a quest? I'm not an advocate of straightforward quests, but I am an advocate of reduced wasted time and increased enjoyment.

People who camp for the safeness issue such as in South Karanas are pathetic. However, when there is only an hour available or groups hard to come by or trying to regain lost experience, then South Karanas fits the bill quite nicely. It is safe, easy, and convenient, and for short durations is quite fun. From an outside perspective this person may appear to be camping, but from this person's playability it is not camping and is fun. However, spending 8 hours at a time days on end, well that is really idiotic and I agree with Kathel.

But having to camp to complete a quest? Anyone remember the fiasco with Gull Skytalon? Apparently it isn't as bad but is still a waste of time. The ancient cyclops and Jboots quest, I don't really have too much of a problem with this long spawn time. It has been advertised to an outstanding degree that if someone wants Jboots they must waste their time and camp. It is an item that can be had for those that want to waste their time. But this in no way equates to Saxxon's quest (I don't even know what that quest is). I can't spend 8 hours in one sitting camping an item. Heck, my playing time limit is about 4 hours maximum and then I am tired of playing. Even if we are doing a dungeon. I've had enough and it's time for me to log. The time and effort put in by Saxxon certainly seems to be excessive. IS the quest about wasted time and who can do it the most? I don't share that viewpoint.

Those who are an advocate of purchasing everything. Where is the fun in that? Completing quests and seeing if you can kill a creature and complete a quest is a heck of a lot of fun. That is the appaeal of this game. Forcing people to purchase an item from someone who is camping the quested creature or has camped the quested creature removes a large part of the enjoyment of the game. How many aspects of this game must be forbidden or forever removed from people because of this camping issue. Off the cuff remarks about purchasing said items (if possible, but many items are no drop and can't be purchased) is not fair for the majority of players in the game. The perspective one must take on this issue is not the personal perspective but the perspective that takes the community as a whole; the perspective that involves everyone and every aspect. From this perspective it is easy to see how the whole aspect of camping is just plain wrong and silly and un-called for. However, this seems to be how most people spend their time. Obviously, that is THE problem and THAT is where effort should be placed to solve the problem.

Camping is not the issue, but rather, why do people camp and is this what EverQuest is all about and is this what it is supposed to be all about? From my perspective, this has been the failing of EverQuest, if a failing label is to be applied to the game. I think it is fair to comment on the game and give pass and fail. It is in no way a complete success and it is in no way a complete failure. People move on and always will because they are dissatisfied with their present situation. Success or failure, it is always interesting to read others' thoughts. In this case, the failure in the aspect of the issue of camping.
