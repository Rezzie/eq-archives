Nerfing Definition & Expansion Pet Issues
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:43 PM PDT
The definition of nerfing was defined by Gordon Wrinn in a recent response he posted regarding some expansion pet issues. Here's the post:

    Nerfing?

    My understanding is that the generally accepted definition for a nerf is "changing something in game after initial implementation in a manner that the players generally view as negative".

    Pets past 50 are more powerful than the 49th pets, perhaps not so much in the max-damage/hit or hitpoints, but they are higher level and have better combat skills. They are overall more powerful, and haven't been made less powerful since implementation, so there was no nerf.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits
[14 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:07 PM PDT
Here's all the latest from Gordon Wrinn on the official EQ Gameplay Board:

  • Final Fix to Overcrowding?
    [Quote from original post: The FINAL fix to overcrowding, soloing, aggroing greens, and all other problems! Make greens give experience.]

    Soloability in its current state is as intended. The classes who were designed to solo can do so efficiently. Some other classes have developed strategies that allow them to solo under certain circumstances, but not as efficiently. Finally, most if not all of the classes have the ability to kill an occasional experience-giving critter, but not to the extent that they could really say that they are "soloing".

    My point here is that since soloability isn't broken, giving every class the ability to go "soloing" (Meaning efficiently killing creatures for the purpose of advancement) really wouldn't fix anything.

    Finally, we really don't want to encourage people to bottomfeed (killing things much lower level than you, thus denying the people who should *really* be killing those things the experience of doing so). Bottomfeeding is a fairly present problem these days, to the extent that we've actually considered making it so that things that don't give experience won't give loot either.

  • NPCs
    I have to go with Xvarious here. NPCs finish their casting cycle and only then turn around to flee. It has to do with the way NPCs process commands. They have a list of commands they execute, and only certain things can interrupt that list. In casting, the only thing is a bash. Even death causes them to finish their spell as I recall :)

  • AE Spells on PvP
    Though this explanation doesn't apply to RZ, on the Teams servers, it is an incentive to group within your own team. By choosing to group outside your team, those spells become less effective.

    I will however chat with the team on Monday about it. As I recall, of the 3 types of AE spells, one of them (radiate out from caster) doesn't affect group members. The other two (radiate out from target, and rain spells) do.

  • Signs EQ is Rotting Your Brain
    I've a couple:

    1. I was intently concentrating while playing the game one night, when my wife came over, gave me a kiss, and told me that she was going to the store. I asked her if she'd pick up a couple of stacks of jasper while she was there.

    2. My wife would take insane joy in talking to me while I was playing EQ (again concentrating like mentioned above). On more than one occasion, I responded in /gsay to things that she said. My group thought I was losing my mind... If they only knew :)

  • Even More Monk Issues
    [Quote from original post: Why can Monks get weapons that are stronger than their fists?]

    Simply, because if we provided monks weapons that were not as good as their fists, there wouldn't be a point to them.

    [Quote from original post: Correct me if I'm mistaken, but I thought the idea of a monk is that they fight best with their fists, not with a weapon. This basically makes monks blunt-wielding warriors that hit harder and can't take as many hits.. Variety is a good thing, and the more the better.]

    Monks do wield a considerable amount of power with their fists, giving them an advantage over all of the melee classes when naked. The difference in power between the fists and the weapons is not so great that a monk would be at a *huge* disadvantage by choosing to shun weapons.

    Though the weapon damage on the fist does not go up, they do get better damage tables, thus yeilding more damage. In other words, a level 60 monk will still hit harder than a level 50 monk.

  • 3 Million a Day for Verant
    Though I do recogize that this is a humorous post, it is strangly in line with some serious suggestions that I heard a few weeks ago.

    At E3, I was asked a number of times by other game developers why we don't do something along these lines. Some suggested that we should sell the ability to level faster, others suggested that we should sell items and characters.

    Though we could certainly make more money doing something along these lines, we really don't feel that doing something of this nature would be fair or good for the game. We feel that people should be able to expect similar rewards for similar time invested and similar risk taken. Doing something along these lines would just reduce the only deciding factor to those with a bigger wallet.

Finding a spell will just make it that much easier, in the same fashion that a melee character doesn't have to have the absolute best weapon in existance in order to be effective.

Gordon Infobits
- Aurum @ 6:24 PM PDT
Here are the latest posts made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • Blade of Passage Nerfed
    That one was changed in the last item patch. Due to the number of changes that were made, I only announced the cases where a change would result in a non-negligible change in power for a player. Items that players could carry, but that they didn't have any skill in, were announced in a blanket statement rather than being mentioned specifically.

  • Verant at e3
    Hey, thanks for posting this. Over all, the whole EQ team had a great time at the show. It's really nice to get out there amongst your peers and chat about each other's games. In many booths that I walked in to (including booths for some future competitors with games in development), I was greeted with, "We're all die-hard EQ players here!" It's definitely a nice compliment.

  • Dragon Loot
    [Quote from original post: However, when the dragon is slayed, it must actually drop some loot!!! I mean something like 5000pp, a few spells or spell components, some armor (3-4pieces), weapons (3-4), a scale or two, some rare gems (1-2), and perhaps a few quest items (Paly books or others), and some random items like a head, torso, sachels, potions, or food items.]

    The loot the dragons drop is sufficient for their difficulty. Platinum-wise, we don't want dragons to become a bank that would bring 10K more in platinum into the game. Item-wise, it is true that they don't drop something for everyone in the party, but they really shouldn't.

    [Quote from original post: I was wondering if anyone at verant has ever considered making the dragon spawns at random. IE, Vox, Naggy and the various other dragons in Norrath would never be up at the same time, and you would not know after killing one which one would be up next or when it would be up (Stagger the next spawn between a few minutes to a few hours).]

    Though they do spawn immediately when the servers are brought up, subsequent spawns have a random component that keeps them from coming back at a predictable time, even if they are both killed at the same time.

    [Quote from original post: With this kind of loot everyone would walk away with something]

    That's not really what we were going for. They aren't supposed to be the equivalent of taking out an armored car.

  • More Boards
    I am looking at getting up 2 or 3 more message boards in the near future providing that some technical specifics can be resolved in the near future.

    One board, which we'll call something along the lines of "The Vision" (heh), will be for us to post our stance on persistent issues that keep coming up (Melee bind for instance). Another board (Announcements) will be for transient issues (Such as Quest X being repaired). Both boards would allow new threads only from official types like myself, Brad, Smed. The announcements board would allow replies from anyone.

    Both boards would direct people to a general discussion board for debate purposes.

    As far as creating another board for Venting, I suspect that it wouldn't work as envisioned. Since most people venting want to be sure that we are aware of their issues, they tend to post whereever it appears that read the most. That's why gameplay has become more of a general discussion forum than anything else.

    I have considered putting strong moderation in place on these boards, deleting flames, personal attacks, and messages with profanity. That might be a better method of promoting good debate here on the boards.

  • Concordance of Research Patch
    [Quote from original post: First, let me say Gordon is a GAWD. He walks on water and all that he pronounces is, and should be, the law of the land!]

    Well in that case, I might take a crack at this post :)

    [Quote from original post: Can you tell me if this means that the "missing" components now drop off of those things that would logically drop them or the receipes have been changed to reflect items tht have always been dropped in the past?]

    The former. The recipies remain the same, but the missing components now drop. Also, one recipe required fine steel or blunt weapons, and one weapon (FS Great staff) wasn't working. That was fixed as well.

    We've ruled out allowing this due to the excessive customer-service impact of opening up another name assignment that would have to go through an approval process. We have two situations like this at present (first and last name), which is as difficult as we want to make it at present.

  • Spell Effects on Armor Items
    The spell effects on those armor items aren't unbalancing. To my knowledge, there isn't a spell effect greater than level 16 on any of the activatable (right-click to activate) items. All of them should require that the piece be worn to activate the effect, and all should have casting times double to quadruple that of the actual spell.

  • Corpse Loss
    [Quote from original post: I'm sorry, the difficulty of corpse recovery should have *nothing* to do with the (in)efficiency of the server code. It should have to do with Vox sitting on your corpse's chest.]

    As an engine programmer, I'm sure that you are aware that any difficulty in retrieving corpses from areas with tons of corpses is caused by framerate from having to draw the corpses, not anything to do with server code.

    This problem can be reduced by turning the clipping plane all the way down, turning off the sky and particle effects.
    [Quote from original post: Address the real issue.]

    Can you concisely state what you believe the "real issue" to be? It's a fairly common occurance here that I'm accusing of not getting the point, or avoiding the "real issue". I do think that part of this is due to the fact that the "real issue" is often subjective in nature.
    ---------
    Just a follow up here. I have spoken to the client programmer about the possibility of implementing a switch that would suppress the display of corpses for cases such as this. He's looking into the issue now, as it would probably be a worthwhile feature addition.

  • The Customer is Not Always Right
    [Quote from original post: i called the tech support line and didnt even get my problem solved and the next month when the bill came in... $ 13.00 dollar bill.....now that does NOT make any sense....]

    I assume by this you mean the phone bill, since we don't charge for technical support. It is the phone company that is charging you for calling the line, not us. It's not uncommon these days for technical support to be given via a toll number. We want to retain your business, and as such we provide a phone line for you to call if you need support to get EQ running on your system.

    [Quote from original post: What happend to the customer is always right?]

    On a broad scale, the customer in this case is nearly 250K people, all of whom have differing desires and expectations, which are often in direct conflict with the desires and expectations of another customer. For example, if one customer says, "He ninjalooted", and the other says, "No, he ninjalooted", which customer is "right" in this case?

    "The Customer is always right" is a business philosophy that works well in situations where the service rendered to one customer does not affect the service that will be given to the next. The fast-food analogy in this thread (that I've been guilty of using before) doesn't apply because one customer ordering "extra pickles" will not cause all future orders to contain "extra pickles". What I mean is that in that analogy, the service organization can fulfill the needs of each individual customer impacting the next customer in line.

    What the customers *are* entitled to is courtesy, respect, and fair treatment, even when their specific needs cannot be fulfilled.

    That said, if you (gemini453) have any specific customer-service issues that haven't been resolved, I'll be happy to discuss them further here or pass them on to someone else if it is an issue that I cannot address. Please don't just say "you care so little about your customer satifaction" and not give us any guidance into how to improve our service.

    [Quote from original post: Is it me or do the resists seem higher after this last patch It sure seems like im getting resisted even more now . On three seperate occassions today i had strings of 5 or 6 spell fully resisted two of these instances were blue pickclaw mobs in HighKeep note i was using magic , fire and ice based spells.]

The Tangrin Slain - Tarew Marr
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:13 AM PDT
Uglar sends the following report:

Greetings all.

While roaming through Field of Bone today I happened accross a
vile creature named The Tangrin. I saw this creature from a far and he
seemed harmless enough. Upon closer look I relized that this happy
monkee type creature was a little less than happy to see me, he suddenly
raised his paws in anger at me, showing me that he could swipe me down
with little effort on his part.

I now realize the pain this creature must have cause many a
traveler and I set forth to rid the lands of this evil vile being. I
brought in backup to assist in the second attempt at slaying. With me
and Zaknafain raising our swords high with honor we struck hard at this
beast, Starweaver and Velven using their mojo struck hard and with
vengance in their hearts. The beast struck back HARD slaying sheading
first blood on me, then shortly there after slaying the mighty
Zaknafain.

After Rokglo used her special talent to resurrect us we decided
that we needed to call in the troops. The Tangrin was a bigger opponent
than we had first thought.

With the help of my wife Carressa, and many many others from the
Dead on Arrival clan we were able to destroy the beast with great honor
and vengance. It was a bloody battle but in the end we were the
victorious ones and payback was served. I give props to Tangrin, as he
was a worthy opponent and his bloodshed brought many a smile to the
faces of the residents of Cabilis.

finally take this beast down. Tangrin is a lvl 54 Monkee who resides on
the isle in the Field of Bone. The battle was fought by the members of
DoA and friends. Loot on The Tangrin, well unfortuately it seems Verant
forgot to stock this creature with any goods to be passed out to the
brave folk that slayed this creature. Many a thanks to the people that
came from a far to help take this beast down - it was a great battle!

Much has been said in the land of Norrath regarding those with gleams in
their eyes towards loot and plunder, intent on self-gain and gratification.

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:08 AM PDT
There are a few more posts Gordon posts made late last night and early this morning on the Gameplay Board to report. Here they are:

  • The Quest in EverQuest
    [Quote from original post: I know, I know. Wizards are catagorized as "Low Priority" at Verant but there's a quest that has been broken since ROK went live.

    It's called the BRAIN BITE QUEST. Heard of it? Probably not unless you're a wizard dying to get a spell that just might add some balance to the class.

    I wouldn't bother bringing it up since Verant has a very strict "see no evil, hear no evil" policy towards wizards, but this *is* a QUEST.

    Could you fix it please?]

    Is this the one with the NPC that spawns in an invalid location in a particular zone? That's being fixed in the patch tomorrow.

  • Disciplines in Spellslots
    [Quote from original post: It would be great to place the disciplines in spellslots as i just dont have any more room in my hotkeys or in my socials either. The slots are currenttly not used and the disciplines would be easier accesible as well as you would always be aware of the discipline status. (highlight the active discipline black all others grey and inactive disciplines grey until you can use the again, for example)]

    Though we really don't have a problem with the idea from a philosophical standpoint, the interface doesn't currently allow this, or even anything close to this that could feasibly be implemented in a reasonable amount of time.

  • Cannabilize II Update
    [Quote from original post: Why haven't you gotten back to us like promised Abashi?]

    Because I was just able to get the info today. There will be a quest going in the next time we patch data that will allow Shaman's to obtain this spell.

  • Race War Server Mishap
    There is a faction penalty for killing players on the racewar servers. Though I'm sorry that you are inconvenienced, it falls upon the players to learn the dynamics of how things work on the PvP servers.
