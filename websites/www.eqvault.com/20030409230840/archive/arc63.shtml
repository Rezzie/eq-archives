  • What happened to the blue names of VIPs?

  • On Server Dups and other Issues
    Nobu-Kai wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "im pretty sure that the mass LD's are directly related to plane raids with lots of people all in the same area. 40 people all in the same room with say a dragon or inny, all casting spells fighting, moving etc...is like a black hole of sorts for bandwidth."]>

    Not really. It's a black hole for framerate, not bandwidth. And we've added the ability to turn off the dynamic lighting in those situations, and that helps a lot. This is all a problem on the client side, where there is too much stuff for you video card to render quickly. This is a framerate problem, not a lag problem. That's not server-side nor Internet lag. Too many people in a zone could cause server-side lag, but that requires in the neighborhood of a few hundred people, not forty.

    [Quote from original post: "As Sophist said in his Very cogent post on the Technical Problems board, this IS one possibility. HOWEVER, Occam's Razor tells us that the simplest answer is usually the right answer.     It could be that we are seeing a problem with the infrastructure that delivers the Internet to us, and therefore us to you.

    I don't know. I'm going to try to find out.

    But during the last few days I've seen the servers up when folks can't connect…

Patch Message: 12-12-00
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:42 PM PST
A new message is now appearing on the EQ Patch Server:

    December 12th, 2:00pm
    ------------------------------

    *New Velious Subscribers*

    Please don't forget to run the "Velious Patch" off of the patch program.

    *New Server*

    We have a new server that will be coming up next week before the holidays. It will be a split server for Solusek Ro, Karana, and Cazic-Thule. Solusek Ro (which had about 200 people moved off about 9 months ago) and Karana (whose last split about 6 months ago did not go very well) will be split again with Cazic-Thule.

    Though the population of Solusek Ro and Karana at peak usage times is not excessive, those two servers actually peak about 6 hours before the other servers due to a large overseas population. This is why they are being split as opposed to other servers.

    /movelog registration for the three servers being moved will open this Saturday at 10am (GMT-8). It will remain open through 12 AM Monday, a total of 38 hours. We apologize for the short notice but to delay the move any longer would mean putting it off until well after the Holidays.

    By typing "/movelog yes" within the registration window, you indicate that you would like all characters on the existing account moved to the new server. The moves themselves will take place on Tuesday of next week.

    We also have a new non-split server planned for the very near future as well. It will most likely not go up until after the Holidays. If that changes, we'll be sure to let everyone know.

    - The EverQuest Team

Absor on Naming Policy
[22 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:11 PM PST
Alan addressed the latest controversy to grace the pages of the Gameplay Board last night:

    Last night on Morell Thule, the guild Tolkien's Revenge was disbanded after being around for almost half a year. The reason submitted for being disbanded, copyright violation. Give me a break, almost everywhere you look, you will see character names and guilds borrowing from popular fantasy literature. Is Verant gonna disband every guild they see for copyright violation? Are you gonna disband Fires of Heaven for being a copyright violation of a book in Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time series? I doubt it... I really do. Of all the lame excuses for disbanding a guild, this has to be right up there. If you are interested in any screenpics of a conversation with a guide about the disbanding of Tolkiens Revenge, go to http://www.wyrmfire.net/deleted/deleted.htm

    Let me wade in here with my opinion.

    First off, keep in mind that any naming convention must be ruled over by people, and judgement calls must be made.

    Tolkien's Revenge, in my opinion, as a guild name clearly violates the naming rules. Tolkien is a famous figure in fantasy literature. Arguably the founder of the whole genre (I've read that it was because of The Hobbit that book stores created fantasy/sci-fi sections in their stores). Using his name in a guild name is similar to using our president's name (even if we don't know who he is for sure).

    There is no question in my mind that the guild name violated our policies, in fact and in spirit.

    Alan

PlanetEverQuest Opens

From Aracnakat:

As the dust settles and the fog clears, shapes begin to take form within the clouds. Small, bearded forms with larger hammers sit in a circle patting each other on the back in congratulatory praise. It has been a long time in the making, but some sort of shrine needed to be built and who better to craft one than the dwarven workers of Kaladim.

The spire shot forth into the sky in splendor and magic seemed to be naturally imbued in the stonework as a blue glow emanated from the structure. Along its base were chisled one word which seemed to thrive with life - PlanetEverQuest.

Aracnakat, the dark elven cleric of other renowned spires such as Cazic-Thule Corner & EQ Corner, contracted the dwarves for the new project. After much protest and even more ale their will had been broken and Aracnakat's vision began to take form. It was with great pleasure that the spire was completed for all to see. The only thing left to do was to dispose of the dwarves.

OOC: PlanetEverQuest has officially launched with the release of Scars of Velious. Please take the time to come give us a look and let us know what you think on our message forums. It's taken awhile, but we're happy to be here!
