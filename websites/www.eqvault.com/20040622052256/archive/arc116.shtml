Group Experience
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:10 PM PDT
Experience in groups was the subject of a recent response Gordon Wrinn posted on the Gameplay Board. Here's the post:

    There seem to be a couple of issues here in the thread, the first being linking groups for shared experience, and the second being linking groups for enhanced communication. In both of these cases, we've weighed the various pros and cons of each situation and decided against them.
    Linking groups for shared experience isn't feasible from a technical standpoint. The code that determines how experience is split up, and the group tracking mechanisms in game are part of the central core of EverQuest, something that we would rather leave intact and instead focus our time on adding new content to the game.

    Shared communication has been ruled out both for that reason (though communication would probably be easier), and to increase the challenge of encounters that require multiple groups. One thing that is difficult about these encounters is that communication is difficult, where it should be. You have 30 people in a room all going after the same critter, and it takes real skill to keep everything sorted out, even with everything filtered out via the chat filters.

    -Gordon

Afternoon Gordon Infobits
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:51 PM PDT
Gordon made a few posts this afternoon on the Gameplay Board. Here's the lowdown:

  • Level 51+ Spells Not Dropping?
    [Quote from original post: Why isn't Abashi replying?]

    Because either I or Aradune has replied to several other threads. With 40 threads liable to pop up on any given subject, we usually don't copy/paste our replies to each one.

    The original author is incorrect in his assertion that "Across ALL servers, not ONE level 51 - 59 spell has been dropped. Not ONE, not even ONE, ZERO spells have been dropped. Zilch, zip, nada, nothing."

    They are dropping.

  • How long was Kunark beta tested?
    [Quote from original post: How long was Kunark beta tested? Why is there a test server? What purpose is a test server when you don't test things or the things you do test still end up "wrong"?]

    I'm sorry, but it happens. It is true that a hundred-thousand people can find more problems in less time than 500 external testers, and that's the easiest way to explain it. There are certain things that testers won't do that live customers will. As much as we'd like to have everything 100% perfect 100% of the time, it's probably not a realistic goal that can hold up to having 200K separate personalities thrown at it.
    --
    [Quote from original post: Why did you answer someone who posted to my post and not address any of the questions I asked? That wasn't very nice. Sorry, not a flame, I am usually your fan...]

    His quote was the most concise one in the thread that basically asked the same questions as yours. There were some problems with the loot that was dropping and we needed to fix them.
    --
    [Quote from original post: I don't like the fact that it was NERFED. I don't like the fact that stuff of Kunark mobs (not in dungeons) is better than most planes gear.]

    Don't these two quotes contradict each other? You don't like that some of the stuff is better than planar stuff, yet you don't like that the drops were nerfed.

    [Quote from original post: If they were to revert to the pre-Kunark nerf stage for 2 months, I guarantee that many of the problems that have popped up as the result of player interaction will slowly dissipate. However, I can guarantee they won't because they are afraid at a little experimentation.]

    We had god items dropping off of level 40 outdoor critters. It had to be fixed, and no, we won't purposely reintroduce the problems to "experiment". We don't have the option of doing a P-wipe in two months when it becomes obvious that the entire game economy is broken, so no, we won't take that chance. What would be the benefit of doing this? Player satisifaction? Players would be satisified in the short term just by virtue of getting a bunch of cool stuff that they couldn't normally get. Brad on Frogloks
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:49 PM PDT
A question asking why Frogloks are in Kunark was answered today by Brad McQuaid on the Gameplay Board. Here's the dirt:

    2 resons, really.

    1. We wanted to nice mix of old and new models in the Expansion -- blending familiarity and newness has always been key to EQ (including the setting, the creatures, etc.)

    2. There's always finite time in creating a product. Would I like a new froglok model like there's a new goblin model? Sure! Heck, I'd like there to be tons more models... but one has to draw the line somewhere in terms of how much content you can get into a game in so much time.

    Overall, I'm very proud of how many distinct models are in EQ, both in the original release and in the Expansion -- I don't think any other 3D game even comes close to the variety we have. The EQ character model team has a LOT to be proud of.

    -Brad

Brad on Clipping Plane
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:43 PM PDT
Brad posted to the Gameplay Board giving us an update to the Plane Clipping problem that's been around for a while. Here's his post:

    We're still trying to isolate the problem.
    We've not been able to reproduce it in-house.

    We are trying to see if it's related to certain video cards, but have yet to see a pattern.

    Here's how the clipping plane works in EQ:

    Each zone has information about it in a 'Zone Header' -- this includes the type of zone, the name of the zone, the chance of rain/snow, etc. It also includes a min and max view distance (clip distance).

    Your clipping plane slider bar works on a percentage basis. So, let's say you have it set in the middle, approximately at 50%. One zone might have a min of 500 and a max of 1000 feet viewable distance. In that zone, you'd see 750 feet. Another zone, however, might have a min of 500 and a max of 1500, in which case you'd be seeing 1000 feet unless you adjusted the slider.

    There are two factors related to how we set the maximum distance: 1. ambiance and 2. how the zone was created. Some zones, for example fog enshrouded forests, have a max clip purposely set lower for effect. Most zones, however, have the max very close to the drawing distance set by the zone creator when he exported the zone. In other words, if we set it any higher you'd see visual anomolies or simply not see the world at all.

    In any case, you should see some change when you adjust your slider. The more variance between the min and max, the more variance you'll see.

    We will continue to try to isolate why there are some reports that the final 25% to 50% of moving the slider does nothing.

    -Brad

More Gordon Bits & Pieces
[15 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:26 PM PDT
The posting machine known as Gordon Wrinn made a few more posts in the last hour. Here's the info ripped straight from the Gameplay Board:

  • More on Solusek Ro Faction
    [Quote from original post: Although the blurb seems promising, the deity related items are presently few and far between. Further, there is a nasty SR faction problem in Kunark. This may be addressed (it should be next patch) but you are likely best off playing the middle and remaining Agnostic]

He'll be fairly well hated there regardless what deity he chooses. But yes, we will be changing the faction offset for the Dark Elven worshippers of Solusek Ro and Bristlebane in the next patch.

  • Compelling Reasons
    The "compelling reasons" why someone might choose to play one class, and another person a different one differ from person to person. I'll take a stab at how we look at it. Here's an example of two melee classes:

    If a monk had the hitpoints of a warrior, could equip all of their armor, and didn't have the weight restriction, what would be the point of playing a warrior? The monk has a bunch of nifty skills and can do more damage with their bare hands than the warrior can. There's be no compelling reason to play a warrior if the monk could do everything better.

    Now monks and warriors primarily compare damage dealing potential, but you also have to factor in other skills. The monk for instance can feign death. This also contributes to balance, even though it has nothing to do with offensive power.

    Every class has something that they can do better than some other class. That does not necessarily mean that they are the *best* at that particular thing, just that they can do *something* better, and also do something worse. As an example, a cleric can heal better than any other priest class, yet their offensive spells are not as good as the other priest classes.

  • Invoke Fear Bug?
    [Quote from original post: While feared, pet kills her. I receive no XP. There seems to be a problem that when feared, you will not obtain the XP for the kill.]

    This is true, and it's not really a bug per se. When feared you are basically an NPC. When your pet kills her the system looks for where to assign the XP and doesn't find a valid PC. This isn't something that can be changed without making major changes to the core architecture.

[27 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:23 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn made a big post concerning various faction issues in Kunark on the Gameplay Board a few minutes ago. Here's the post:

    With the number of posts about dungeon factions in Kunark, I wanted to take an opportunity to address them all at once:
    Yes, it is correct, fighting in several dungeons in Kunark will lower your faction with VenrilSathir, and thus make conducting business in the outpost in the Overthere a bit rough. Being KOS in the evil outpost does not put the spells that are available there out of your reach. You certainly have the ability to have someone else, who is not KOS there, complete the quests for you. Evil races are KOS throughout Faydwer as it is, and people get along fairly well there. Kunark is not meant to be an encapsulated environment that people never leave. We expect people to have to go back to the mainland from time to time to get supplies and such. If you make yourself KOS to the outpost, there are other ways for you to get into Kunark without going through it.

    So just to summarize, the factions are intentional. I suggest that everyone consider their options carefully before making a decision on how to tackle the continent.
