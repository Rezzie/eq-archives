Patch Message: 6-15-00
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:11 PM PDT
Here's today's message following the patch that was implemented today:

    June 15th, 8:00am
    -----------------

    *Patch Day*

    Hello everyone,

    Over the past few weeks, a portion of the EQ team has been working on The Hole, in preparation of its release in the near future. Even so, a number of changes and additions were made in today's patch. Most of the changes are in regards to quests:

    - The "Fleshy Orb" quest has been completed
    - We've received word that "Fabian" has been spotted somewhere on Norrath.
    - Froglok Foragers and Hunters should been seen more regularly.
    - Lord Grimrot should be seen more frequently.
    - Skill in tailoring is no longer required to craft The Robe of the Lost Circle.

    A few items have also been changed this week:

    - The Robe of the Lost Circle has been enhanced.
    - The Staff of Temperate Flux and the Rod of Insidious Glamour now have a short casting time associated with their activatable effect (See Below).
    - The Gunthak Harpoon is again NORENT, meaning that it will disappear when you log off (See Below.)


    *Casting Times on Staff and Rod*

    We received word a few weeks ago that some people were using these items to "spam" other players with their spell-effect messages, causing them to disconnect. Though /ignore will allow you to filter out text messages caused by people on your /ignore list, it will not allow you to filter out spell-effect messages for spells cast upon you.

    The short casting time should prevent the problems associated with the targetable and activatable effect on these weapons, however you will have to stand still to use them now.

    *Gunthak Harpoon*

    In the last item patch, we made the mistake of making this weapon "rentable", meaning that it does not disappear upon logoff. Basically, one of our game designers heard that the weapon was NORENT, concluded that it must be a bug, and took the initiative to fix it. Unfortunately, the stats on this weapon, despite its disadvantages of weight and its non-magical composition, make it much too powerful for its location and difficulty in obtaining it.

    For these reasons, the weapon is once again NORENT, and will disappear the next time that you log out. This is a retroactive change and will affect weapons already in possession. We do apologize to those who acquired it within the last week expecting that it would be a permanent weapon.

    *Chat / Login Process*

    Due to the rapidly increasing number of people playing EverQuest, the Chat
    and Login processes have been moved to more robust servers. This should
    increase the capabilities of both servers and help alleviate the congestion
    experienced by some during periods of high utilization.

    - The EverQuest Team
Thanks Alexisi!

Player View Discussion: The Locked Doors
[24 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:38 PM PDT
I was recently sent an email concerning one of the most (if not the most) controversial class in EverQuest, the Rogue, but more specifically about their Pick Lock ability. To start off with I'd like to post that letter I received:

    The rogue community may soon come to a close over a issue concerning Locked
    Doors and Verant.

    Rogues have for a long time dealt with the issue that the class was never
    fully finished and that some skills would never be used in the game (Locate
    and Disarm Traps). Verant told them it was a dead issue and the skills
    would neither be implemented, replaced, or remove from the class as it would
    be "to difficult". The other list of skills for the rogue class has always
    been questionable by those who play it. Poison has been almost a year on
    the dev team's desk, waiting for bugs to be fixed that had been reported
    since it's implementation. It was added to the list of useless skills.
    Today the rogue community has learned that Door Exploits are now allowed.

    Pick Locks has always had a shady history with Rogues. The lack of locked
    doors in Ever Quest, and the doors wich were locked, but unpickable in
    dungeons like Najena had always never made sense. They did make the skill a
    integral part of the Burning Rapier quest though when they demonstrated they
    had the power to put a locked door anywhere they felt necessary. The door
    contained within a essential part of the Quest.

    The skill package of the rogue still left much to be desired though, and
    Verant realized this. They promoted and sold expansion to the rogue
    community on the fact that they had a better designed world with more locked
    doors to be use with Lock Picks. Now that Kunark is here though, and the
    majority of players have bought this expansion, Verant has decided to pass a
    ruling that effected the use of this skill, and the rogue class itself.
    Door Exploits are allowed, and you do not require a rogue to proceed past
    these locked doors in Kunark. Pick Locks has now become another skill in
    the list of abilities for rogues that serves no specific purpose.

    Gordon has stated that it is more reliable to have a rogue open the door,
    but that it is not necessary.

    It is now a acceptable practice to open these doors in Kunark with specific
    lock pick ratings (average of a 201 which requires a minimal of 20 hours in
    befallen of constant clicking to raise the skill. this is not a
    exaggeration.) with methods other than a key or set of lockpicks. The use of
    AoE spells, pets, and manipulation of NPCS are now all approved for use if
    you do not feel you need a rogue.

    I write this as calmly as possible, but due to Verant's lack of willingness
    to fix this issue they have in effect killed a class that they have showed
    their unwillingness to deal with in past issues. I retire my rogue, and
    step away from EQ with a sad heart knowing that I can no longer be a part of
    this when even the creators encourage cheating. I know I do not walk away
    alone though.

    Tarkien Motiff
    Rake of the 52nd Dagger.
    The Nameless

Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:51 PM PDT
Gordon has been busy on the Gameplay Board as usual. Here are the latest posts:

  • Music Problem
    [Quote from original post: So, I am wondering why I can't get ANY music in Everquest at all. Now, recently, we were sent all those .Xmi files I had to sit through,]

    They were pretty small, it shouldn't have taken too long to download them.

    [Quote from original post: and then deleted anyways for fear of having the camping issues. Either way, I have yet to get music in the game.]

    You'll only get music if there is an XMI file for the zone that you are in. If you've deleted all of the XMI files I doubt you'll get any music. Now if you didn't get any music even when you had XMI files, there's probably a problem with your sound card configuration.

  • Illusion/Faction Issue
    [Quote from original post: Why does faction(good and bad) transfer to my"true self", when I have any Illusion on? Example: I use my DE illusion when I do the stein of moggok quest in Oggok. My true self is no longer KoS there now. I use my DE and Troll illusion in SK to kill treants. My "true self" is Kos to druids and all of Qeynos now. Is it a bug or are my illusions just kinda worthless now? I would really like to hear from you on this, Abashi, if at all possible. ]

    It's not a bug and it's always worked this way. Deed-based faction (faction based on what you have done) is attached to the character, not the race that you happen to look like at the time. The only faction that illusions can affect is that of your race.

  • Item Specific Issues
    Though I appreciate everyone's concerns on some of the item-specific issues that have come up lately, some of this stuff is just the way it works.

    I would like to come up with a better way to say that, but sometimes that's the simplest explanation for issues such as this.

  • FA Quest Question
    This is the first report I've heard along these lines. The quest components are pretty rare drops.

    That said, I do believe that the quest was revamped less than 4 months ago, so your data might be a bit off.

  • Unblockable Spam
    I'll talk to the guys about including that in the /ignore filter. In the mean time, please /report anyone being anti-social in that fashion and /petition to let the GMs know that you sent the report.

    Though we do like to retain our customers, we'd be happy to do without those who purposely try to ruin the game for others.

  • Bug Report List
    [Quote from original post: L56 Mage spell Muzzle of Mardu is bugged and can't be used by any race at all.]

    Not a bug. Give it to your pet.

    [Quote from original post: Mage spell L56 Dyzil's Deafening Decoy have a spell that was meant to be an AE tauntspell (atleast what I heard), it ain't working, it can't taunt a mob of a L53 Airpet.]

    This should be pretty straight forward, I'll see if test can take a look.

    [Quote from original post: Mages normal focus items don't work on Pets over L51, this is a huge bug or a secret nerf.]

    Neither. They just aren't implemented at this time. That's not to say that we will want to implement them though, as pets are about as powerful as we want them to be.

  • Suggestion for Vallon Zek
    [Quote from original post: I was wondering if the CHEST piece could be made no drop on VALLON because its the most important piece for melee types and if some naked caster pkes it off ya when at 2 bubles of health the efficiency of that char goes down alot casters got nothing to lose when they die.]

    We really don't have any plans of making items NODROP to eliminate them as a loot possibility on the PvP servers that allow item loot.

  • Undead & Nightvision
    [Quote from original post: Abashi, Why do undead not get Night vision? Is this a bug or a feature?]

    Like some other things on the board today, this is just how it is. When creating the illusions we decided what benefits an illusion would have. Night vision wasn't one of them.

  • Fun Ideas for High Keep Hold
    Though in the original design, meta-games (games within games) were later scrapped in favor of working on other and more pressing issues. We do not have any immediate plans to revisit them at this time.

  • Complement
    I think that there's more than a few out there like you, and I appreciate the fact that you posted here on the boards.

    I'm glad you are enjoying the game.

  • Team Servers/Rallos Zek & Faction
    The teamservers have faction hits for PvP. Rallos Zek, on the other hand, does not have those faction hits. That's just how the servers are set up.

    Plenty of people still engage in PvP combat despite these restrictions.

  • New PvP Server?
    We bring up new servers when the population on existing servers gets to the point that a new server is needed. All three of the PvP servers are currently underutilized, leaving no reason to bring up another at this time.

    [Quote from original post: Plus im sure a huge majority of people would love to start fresh and not have 1000 people higher in level.]

    This happens after the server has been up a few days anyway. Unless you are the one who's highest in level, there will always be people higher than you.

  • Inspecting Players Suggestion
    [Quote from original post: I've posted about this before, and I've also suggested maybe making right-click player inspection re-mappable to a keyboard key, but maybe the simplest way to do this would be to just make player inspection during combat not possible. Accidentally right-clicking on other players, and seeing their stuff while I'm getting wailed on is annoying. I can't be the only person who has this problem. Thanks.]

    Sounds simple enough. I'll ask the client programmer if he can take a look at it. No promises though.

  • Falling off Boats
    Thanks for the info. Until we can get it fixed, I suggest you jump over that area with the collision problem.

  • Server Issues
    [Quote from original post: Case in point, after 5 days hunting in Hate during normal prime time hours, little was available to hunt as the non-peak crowd from over seas had largely cleared the zone during the "day" here (late aftrnoon here). This leaves many guilds in peak time at a loss for hunting for those items specific to these types of locations.]

    We can't start restricting use of a server during specific times of day on the basis that other people can play when you can't. It could very well be the other way around, with non-peak players citing how peak-players kill everything in these areas.

    The servers are up 24/7 excluding scheduled and emergency downtime. Nothing is on a perfect 24 hour or 12 hour spawntime, so sooner or later the critters in question will pop when virtually anyone is online.

  • Druid Quest Armor
    [Quote from original post: The last thread had a lot of good ideas and was completely ignored. Let's try again]

    This issue comes up about every two weeks as people who bring it up move on, and new people come here to the boards. I know that I've addressed it probably a few dozen times in the past 6 months.

    That said, we are aware of the interest in a mid-level quest armor. We haven't ruled out the possibility of putting some in in the future, but we don't have any specific plans to do so. We recieve hundreds of requests for items, quests, and content of various types daily. We have to decide what is *needed* versus what is *wanted*. Right now there is no pressing *need* for a mid-level druid quest armor based upon the fact that there are many other items in game that druids can wear, and that provide stats and bonuses in excess to what any class-specific armor would.

    Hopefully we will do it some day.

  • Blood Ember Armor
    The guys have looked the issue over. The blood ember armor weight is intentional. According to the Live guys, it's due to the fact that the armor is easier to get than the other armor types in Kunark.

    I've had them look at the issue twice now, so it's pretty firm at this point.

  • EverQuest Issues
    I had a few comments on some of these:

    [Quote from original post: 1) GM's will almost always take the side of the higher lvl player during a dispute. The high lvl players are usually friends with the GM group. They have been around the longest and have probably have even grouped with GMs.]

    I prefer to spend more time on issues where forward progress can be made rather than the ones that are entirely subjective in nature I'm really not here to win.

    True. People twink. Most people don't have a problem with it. We've tried to design the game in a fashion that the effect of twinking in minimized. The main issue left is envy, which I'm not sure if we can solve. Someone will always be able to get more things faster than someone else.

    [Quote from original post: 5) There is a 'experience range' around the monster when it is slain. The closer you are to it when it dies, the more exp you get.]

    Though there is a range to get experience, the amount of experience you get is not scaled by distance from the creature, damage that's been done to it, or how many spells have been cast during the fight (some of the more popular myths).

    Sometimes a cigar is just a cigar... - Freud

    Maybe they are just trying to see where it goes? I usually follow the path in Nektulos. I get turned around in that zone so easily.

    [Quote from original post: 9) Kiting was nerfed, but not how you was told. Way back when, Verant decided to nerf kiting (dot and run). They told us that if the monster is moving, it will not take damage by the dot at all. Only if it is stopped, then it will be harmed. This is not true. What they did, is decrease the time the dot is present.]

    Huh? Kiting was addressed, but not in the way that you mention here. Duration is a constant. The variable is damage per tick. If the critter is moving and chasing (but not fleeing) at the time that the damage is applied, it will take 2/3rds the damage that it normally would. At the time this change was made, all DOTs had their duration upped slightly.

    We originally considered not applying any damage in the event that the creature was moving, but scrapped that idea due to public opinion.

  • Kunark Loot
    [Quote from original post: You have reduced coin-loot to lower than typical levels on most mobs in Kunark as part of your effort to prop up the value of plat?]

    The idea was to redefine, worldwide, the definition of good coin loot. The problem is that certain static costs in game remain just that, static.

    We're going to discuss the coin drop issues, and other things related to the economy (trade skill costs, component costs, etc.) in the tuning meeting next Monday.

  • Level Limits
    We really don't intend to close off areas of the game based on people being too high in level. There are a number of reasons for this, not the least of which is that some high-level areas require passage through lower level areas.

    Lower Guk, for example, requires passage through upper Guk. If you are in Qeynos Hills, EverFrost and Permafrost require passage through Blackburrow. There's also the fact that we'd have a few problems coming up with an RP reason to restrict higher level people from entrance into lower level areas.

    We only put the level restriction on the Planes because we wanted the items there to be a badge of honor for the person wearing them. When the plane of fear was first opened, people were taking level 1 characters up there to loot things that people in the group could not otherwise use.

    This limit also let us bend some of the previous game mechanics that were required in order to insure that twinking does not have a huge impact on the game.

  • Class vs. Skill Based Systems
    Actually I think that most people would agree that the class-based system is one of EQ's greatest strengths, even if they don't agree with the particular way we've implemented it in regards to a class or two.

    Skill-based systems, in my opinion, inevitably lead to all of the characters migrating slowly to whatever combination of skills is deemed "most powerful" at the time. The only safeguard against that would be a skill-based system that doesn't let you switch to the skill-lineup du jour, but of course, then it would really be a class-based system.

  • Are Hybrid Hogging Experience?
    Experience allocation hasn't changed, and is how it always has been, so nothing has changed. The specifics surrounding how experience is passed out within the group isn't something that we plan on releasing.

    I'd try deleting the XMI files for those zones. We created and distributed a bunch of them and it caused a few problems. We're working on them now.

  • Stance Against Responding to Class-Envy Posts
    Thanks for your comments MrDete. Your advice is well-received. Please also understand though that everyone can come up with well-grounded issues for each class, which they honestly feel is a deal-breaker. The downside of this is if we could wave our hands and resolve all of the issues raised, the resolution of those issues would create other issues that other people would likewise feel need to be resolved.

    Suppose for an instant that class A feels that they are less powerful than class B. Class B might at the same time feel that they are less powerful than class C. The process of elevating class A, in a manner that they feel would "balance" them with Class B would often leave Class B feeling that they are overpowered by A and C.

    This process is played out over and over here on the boards, and will probably be a source of debate for years to come. Often the best course of action is not to engage in discussions where we are faced with the unenviable task of repetitively telling players that their specific concerns are not something that we consider to be an issue. It just creates bad feelings.

    Indeed, you are our customer, and your business is very important to us, but to no greater or lesser extent than the business of everyone else playing the game. We'd really *like* to be able to make each person or group feel that they are more important than all of the others, and our marks in customer service would probably improve if we could. However, in our business, the level of attention or service given to one customer affects that given to all of the others.

    It's a fine line. We do the best that we can and constantly strive for improvement.

However in Skyfire, *everything* is pretty nasty, with those encounters being the rule rather than the exception. Many groups can hunt there nonstop and never run out of things to kill. Given that, they just can't drop the 10-30pp per kill of other encounters.

    As for the XP, the mobs in Skyfire give more experience than mobs of a relative level in any other outdoor zone in the world.

We didn't used to make norent items disappear on linkdeath, but many people then would go linkdead as the preferred method of leaving the game for the purpose of saving the bags. Also, once a character is out of the world, we don't maintain a record of them to allow removal of the bags after, for instance, an hour.

    As far as moving the items out of the bags, that would present some major problems in the event that inventory is full.

    Given these issues, the chance of losing the items on linkdeath will probably remain one of the risks of using the summoned bags.
