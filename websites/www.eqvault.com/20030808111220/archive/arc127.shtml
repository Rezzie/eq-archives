Travels of the Scale - Bertoxxulous
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:31 AM PDT
Suineko sent me the following report of a GM event:

Long ago, while working my rounds purging the temple in the Feerott of
Thules scaled worshippers I heard news of another scaled beast via
the bards of Norrath... A singer had been seen in steamfont, accompanied
by a dragon, and she told of Veeshans coming return.

Again, word flew the taverns and bars of the world, and it was told that
she and her dragon had been slain at the hands of Mayongs man, who
then sent a pawn to the hell hole known as Dagnors Cauldron to employ
a dwarf of almost godlike skills to retrieve an... item... from the
ice Wyrms lair, Lady voxs own private treasures.

When for a third time rumblings of the scales awakening reached by ears
I abandoned by Crusade against the heretic lizards, and joined my
companions in a rush to Permafrost. We arrived to find a young, frail
rogue leading a High elven Lady of beautiful elegance and her company
of guards, trolls and the like, deep within permafrost to Vox's
nest... where we found the Dwarven rogue making his way out, carrying
a shard whos loss had raised the Dragons ire such that her screams
and roars shook the walls and caused the ice of the caverns itself
to fall.

The rogue, skilled as he was, was flushed with his success and
fearful of Mayongs temper, and wished to leave quickly. That fear, perhaps,
his downfall, as the Lady we were with began to seduce him, flattering
his skills, speaking of his feat, until at last she held the Shard
in her hands for "just a moment". She did not lie, for that moment ws
all it took to seal the dwarfs doom as her guards moved with inhuman
speed and slew him.

Vox roared again. Her goblins cowered. The intruders in her realm had
her treasure and vanished before she could rally her still considerable
might. One of the less... bright of the races in the Ladys guard let
slip a few words, enough to lead us to the Rathe mountains.

We arrived to see a Dragon, blue as the sky, its wings stretched
in triumph over the mysterious company. As we hurried to its place
we caught the Lady thanking her guide, the rogue, as two more of her
company shed their forms and took on wings... 2, then 3 dragons
soared above us, exultant in their triumph, the shard itself a shard
of Veeshans own claws, a relic of great power.

As she thanked the rogue, shadows gathered. Mayong, his prize torn
from his grasp had not been idle as the dragons wheeled in the sky.

Suddenly one fell, followed swiftly by its comrades, as the Undead
lord who aspires to godhood slew all those he could see in the air.
The lady, still concealed from him in elven form although doubtless
a Child of Veeshan herself, spoke swiftly to the rogue.

A sacrifice was required. Blood of one of the lesser children to open
the plane of sky and allow Veeshans kin ingress to slay those who would
usurp her and their rightful place on the world. He would die, of his
own choice, by the shard, and rise again as their champion, soul claimed
by the Wyrm Queen.

Despite my fears, and warnings, the rogue took this path, perhaps
flattered by the celestial attentions, perhaps hungry for reward and
power, maybe even merely fearful of the consequence should he refuse.

But he accepted, and the shard turned in his hands and slew him. When
he rose he was known as the Claw of the Scale. The remaining children
of Veeshan fled then before mayong could penetrate their cloak, and
left their new catspaw on Norrath, the shard still in his possession.

War is brewing, and this time I need no bards to tell me of its
consequence. Two sides, neither with the interests of the free peoples
at heart, struggle now for dominance, Mayong who pledged the death of
dragons and mortals all alike, and Veeshans children, returning to claim
the surface of Norrath from the usurpers who walk on two legs.

Be careful who you aid friends. Our fates are in the balance, and before
long we may find our only defence against these powers is in unity
ourselves. There may not be reward in refusing to serve these powers
beyond freedom for ourselves, but that is a powerful thing itself.

The gateway to Veeshans domain, the plane of sky, is crumbling and
this blood sacrifice may have torn it entirely asunder.

Mayongs power now extends far beyond the boundarys of his citadel, a
location perhaps more feared even than the lairs of the Ring of Scales
outcasts.

Do not mistake beauty for goodness friends. Be wary, and fearful.

OOC:
A GM event on Bertoxxulous. It was good, and nicely played, but packed.
As usual there were crowd control problems - I think maybe GM's should
be given an AE sit down and shut up audience spell or something ;) - but
it went off pretty well. I missed the beginning and a bit in the
middle as I travelled the long way from permafrost to Rathe, but its
mostly in there I think.

We also possibly missed the end on Bertie - finally too many people had
gathered, some intent on disrupting the play, and the GM characters left
some threads hanging that they obviously intended to finish. I think
we pretty much got it all though.

I'd post the logs but I kept getting disconnected so they're kind of
scrapp; overall a few ice giants were killed but this was mostly a
non-participation event, like a play staged for us.

And the three dragons were awesome ;)

Love,

Suineko
Suiddaru Fallenstar, 36th ranger and wannabe cyclops, Bertoxxulous

Intelligamer Editorial
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:17 PM PDT
A unique editorial was published recently by Intelligamer. This focuses on the "Vocal Minority" of EverQuest. It explores several apsect of this crowd, including that of the board scene. Here's a quote from this article:

    If this is all you ever read about the game, you'd get the impression that the game was incredibly bad and that you should have nothing whatsoever to do with it. But that's not the case at all. The real situation is that a very vocal, very passionate, and really very small group of players has taken it upon themselves to tell Verant how to design, upgrade, and fix the game. They do this at high volume as often as they can in as many ways, shapes and sizes as they can. And the truly amazing thing about all this is that the very same people who hate the game, hate the developer and will go out of their way to tell everyone in the world their opinion also continue to play the game day in and day out.

    At last count Everquest has more than 200,000 active subscribers, most of whom play daily. That's 200,000 people paying $10 a month. You'd figure that if the game really sucked that that number would be falling. But it isn't. It keeps rising, even in the face of new competition from Asheron's Call.

    A lot of people have said that because you invest so much time in a game like Everquest you tend to get passionate about what you like and dislike. And you tend not to want to take the drastic step of canceling your account after the, literally, weeks or months of your life you invested in improving your character. All true enough.

Brad McQuaid Q&A
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:14 PM PDT
Brad McQuaid, Vice President of Verant Interactive, tried a daring experiment last weekend. Here's his announcement straight from the Gameplay board courtesy of Anders:

    I want to try something. I don't know if it will work well, but what the heck.

    I want to spend some time today answering questions. Let me lay down some guidelines on the questions though:

    1. Keep them short -- one or two sentences max. Short, succinct, to the point, etc.

    2. No nasty flames, loaded questions, veiled insults, etc.

    3. Nothing situationally or contextually too specific. Keep questions to a more general level. No 'why does item (or quest or spell) a with stats b & c vary from item d'. To be honest, I don't know a lot of the game at that level of detail (no one person can, and my job is to understand the game as a whole more than to have memorized every quest, spell, and item), and that's not what I want to focus on here anyway.

    Keep the questions at more of a global level. Ask me about the Vision(TM), or the philosophies behind game mechanics, etc. Maybe we can put together a FAQ IF this works out (I know stephenmcleod tried to do something like this recently). I think the key here is to keep the questions short and the answers fairly short as well. My answers will be pretty blunt and matter-of-factly as opposed to responding with a detailed essay.

    I will try to answer as many questions as I can. This is really an experiment to see if I can be of some assistance in explaining issues, positions, viewpoints, etc. I can't guarantee I will get to every question, nor will I be able to focus on this thread after tonight.

    Also, in addition to being unable to answer the really specific item/quest/spell questions, I may also be unable to answer some questions about the future (for example, what all will the EQ team work on post-expansion, or will there be an EQ 2, etc.) or about areas that we've chosen to keep private (for example, what is the exact topology of an EQ server).

    Anyway, enough disclaimers and explanations. Let's give this a shot :)

    -Brad

Brad on The Nameless
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:20 PM PDT
EQ Producer Brad McQuaid talked about the downtime the Nameless server experienced this weekend. Here's the first post:

    Actually, I think this has happened 3 times in the history of EQ... maybe 4.

    It does suck. Thank God it happens very rarely thanks to the RAID setup on our machines. Basically, 2 drives have to go bad around the same time for this to happen.

    The chances of that happening, of course, is very small, which is why it's only happened 3-4 times in a year.

    Typically a single drive goes bad, we bring down the server, put a new drive in, and rebuild it.

    But if the RAID controller and/or 2 drives go bad, it's restore from backup time.

    I assume this is what happened on the Nameless recently, though I've not talked to anyone one at EQ Operations.

    In any case, what's with the 'it's happened twice on the Nameless!!' stuff? Do you guys think we're picking on servers now? Nerfing hardware instead of classes?

    Yeah, we're putting substandard hardware in the Nameless because, well, um, we hate the Nameless! Er, no, um, we're stealing Nameless CPU cycles to reverse to reverse engineer a UFO found in Verant's basement!

    The truth is out there!

    -Brad

Here's Brad's second post on this:
    tpowell2: rebuilding while keeping the server up creates an unaccpetable performance hit.

    Senlaan: Yeah, I'm at home, it's the weekend. I'm spending my free time posting here. I'm not at work talking to the EQ Operations team.

    In any case, my post responded to two things:

    Contrary to what csorrows posted, people wanted more info, and I gave it to them. I explained what can cause, albeit very rarely, data loss.

    I addressed, using comedy to put things in perspective, some people's assertions that we are targetting, or neglecting, or maliciously messing with the Nameless server.

    -Brad

    Emergency Patch April 1, 2000
    -------------------------------------

    The EQ servers were quickly brought down this morningfor an emergency patch update. We'd apologize once againfor taking the servers down, but what would be the point?

    The following changes were made:

    1.) Wizards - We've finally given Wizards a few quick"Insta-Death" spells. Our hope is that this will makeall the wizards shut up for a little while, until theystart to complain about lack of plate armor for their class.

    2.) Druids - Because of the over-abundance of Druids in Norrath, the next timeyou login one out of every three Druids will be randomly reassigned a newclass.

    3.) Warriors and Monks - These melee classes have been given magical abilities at last.They now can bind, gate, heal and summon food.

    4.) Magicians and Enchanters - New pet spell added: Nagafen. We've given these two classes a fine new pet to helpthem in their everyday struggles in Norrath.

    5.) Rogues - We feel this class is our crowning achievement. We believe it has been perfectly balanced, and as suchwill not receive any modifications for the rest of EverQuest's life.

    6.) Hybrids (Rangers, Paladins, Shadow Knights) - After careful data analysis we've come to the conclusion that simply giving these classes a few of their spellcaster counterparts spells is not enough. So thus we've given them all the spells they would normally receive had they been a Druid, Cleric or Necromancer.

    In addition to these changes, the Necromancer and Cleric classes will be overhauled in the near future! Unfortunately if you already have a character that is one of these two classes you will loose all your items and be randomly renamed.

    Thanks for your patience!

    -The EverQuest Team

[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:02 PM PST
Good grief has Gordon been busy today. In the past 24 hours Mr. Wrinn has made over 60 posts on the Gameplay board alone. Here's a rundown of some of his latest:

    Quest Structure
    We're not at all opposed to quests giving out real (e.g. tangible) experience, however that's really not going to be possible given the current architecture. The problem is that we need to have the ability to store completion-info as part of the character. This would keep the same character from doing the same quest over and over again, which is what keeps us from doing things like giving out half-a-level for a tough quest. Tracking this unfortunately isn't possible without doing a player-wipe. The Quest engine could be improved in many other ways, but they all require the ability to know exactly what a character has or has not done.

    Hybrid Debate (Click here for original post)
    Wow! These are probably some of the best arguments I've seen. I am impressed...however...

    Hybrids don't get teleports, not even Gate. Only two classes get teleports, and even though rangers are a hybrid of one of those classes, there's really no compelling reason to make an exception.

    Money and the PoS
    If you are paying 3Kpp per person to go to the Plane of Sky, there's only one reason: You want to. The zone need not cost a cent after the first trip in. Deaths can be handled with resurrect. Heck, you can even get by a few of the key requirements using death, resurrect, and summon-corpse (this one costs money though).

    Now of course if you want to bring more people than there are keys, or skip by the first island, it's going to cost you. We want the Plane of Sky to remain challenging regardless of how many warm bodies you can throw at it.

    Skill Caps and Individuality
    [Quote from original post: What about giving characters one generic skillcap point per level that they could assign to any skill? Or… implement some quests that can raise skillcaps for different skills. A 50th level character could have assigned 50 skillcap points to one skill raising its effectiveness by 10 levels. If this is too much there could be a skillcap addition cap for certain skills.]

    First, just because you stop gaining in a particular skill doesn't mean that your skill level does not go up. For instance, a level 50 person with a 200 skill in 1HS does not hit like a level 40 person, they hit like a level 50 person because their is more to calculating it than just the skill.

    Secondly, please keep in mind that all of this stuff is balanced based upon the caps and how they work now. We just can't arbitrarily decide to start letting people increase their skill-caps on a whim. Sure, it would be neat, but since things are currently balanced, the only way that something like this could go in is if we substantially nerfed the skills so that any net gain would be zero.

    Finally, enabling what you ask for here would not diverse characters. Skill-based systems never do. Everyone gravitates towards having whatever skills are perceived to be ideal. This has been persistent flaw in every skill-based MMORPG architecture that has been developed. That’s why we chose to go with a class-based system.

    Drops in PoH
    Just trying to verify your logic: The drop percentages in Hate are low so people with armor in Hate will play longer? I suppose that means that since the drops in Fear are higher, we must want everyone with armor there to quit.

    Please... We have Hate updated on the Test server and hope to patch it live the next time we do data. Probably next week.

    Bonus Skill Points
    Well I can say that we don't have any intention of including bonus points. It's certainly possible for people to work up any skills that they need, the inclusion of more points would just serve to save them some time, and go a long way towards making point meaningless in the first place. Save them up!

    Kunark and International Orders
    Our plan is to have the Expansion in everyone's hands prior to the official launch, even the overseas people. That's our plan, and it is looking good so far. However we really won't be able to nail it down further for a bit yet.

    (Click here for original post)
    Unfortunately, this is the way resurrection works, and it's not considered a bug. If the resurrection doesn't go through on the first time for whatever reason (e.g. Zone, cancel, LD, etc.), the experience cannot be restored.
    [Quote from original post: I disagree with the behavior not being considered a bug. A res should give the exp, period. No, two reses should NOT give any more exp than one res.]

    I understand your opinion, however when making coding decisions, the programmers have to decide how many contingencies they are willing to trap. There are functions that you can perform in the game that account for all different kinds of things that can go wrong, such as everything that can go wrong in a trade.

    Resurrection is one of those things where not everything possible is trapped. It makes resurrection fairly black-and-white, but has the side-effect of missing them under certain circumstances.

    Resurrection Bug
    [Quote from original post: I too recently lost the exp from missing the first res attempt. What happened was, I was waiting a while on the cleric to med and res, and going through inventory. The window popped up as I clicked on an inventory item - and clicking on the item made the res window disappear!!]

    Yeah, I'll buy that that is a bug with the confirmation-box code. You would think that a confirmation box wouldn't disappear unless you actually click on something. In *most* cases, it's safe to assume the answer is "NO", which is how the item-destroy confirmation works, but I can see this being a problem in reference to a resurrect. I'm not sure if this can be worked around, but I'll bring it to the Team.

    Fear and Charm in PvP
    Fear and Charm spells have been disabled in the PvP environment for some time due to abuses that surround them. They made it possible for all kinds of bad things to happen, including intentionally causing experience loss and they allowed people to bypass the PvP level limits.

    When this was done, AE Fear spells continued to work due to how those spells interact with the players. This latest patch merely fixed that issue, causing PCs to be immune to them as well as the single target spells from before.

It's one thing for us to talk about the positive aspects of EQ when people haven't tried another game. It's another thing when they've tried the other games and then affirmatively choose EQ.

    Thank you for continuing to choose EQ.

    [Quote from original post: When it breaks fear tho the mob runs for me at full normal speed, as for some weird reason whenever fear is broken or wears off, the movement inhibiting portion of the "darkness" line of dots seems to wear off as well.]

    What about Pet Bashing in this patch?
    Well it's not just pets, it's all NPCs are refusing to bash without a shield. And yes, that will be corrected in this patch.

    Can EQ Be Made Into a Better game?
    [Quote from original post: At this point in development, with EQ having been out for a year and over 200,000 installed copies of it on the market Verant would be making a bad business choice to rework every system that we, as players, recommend to them to be changed.]

    That's something that we struggle with here on a near-daily basis, and it comes up every time we discuss any planned enhancement or "nerf" to any particular aspect of the game: Do the ends justify the means. Will the changes we are considering really do anything for the game? Will it make people stay longer? Will it prompt them to quit sooner? Will the people who will stay longer make up for all the people that will quit instantly because of a specific change? Do we really want to risk making vast sweeping changes to an already successful game?

    In a system with over 200K active subscribers, we can pretty much bet that any change we make to the game will cause people to quit sooner, stay longer, quit instantly, resubscribe, and do nothing at all. All of these actions are represented.

    So, to the people who think that we just run around arbitrarily making changes to the game, I'd like to point out that there is a whole lot more to it than that.

    The Odds
    [Quote from original post: Anyone have the odds that the servers will be down for more then 2 hours.]

    I'll give 3:1 again :)

    I can confirm this. I believe that someone suggested it on the boards yesterday, and I said I'd take it to the team. Well the programmer who does things like that regularly reads the board here. He grabbed it and ran with it.

    One additional note that's not mentioned at castersrealm is that pets should now tell their owners when a beneficial spell wears off.

    EverQuest and Realism
    Sometimes we have to put some limitations on realism for the sake of gameplay. This takes many forms, some of which are considered both good and bad. As an example, it's "unrealistic" for someone to heal from near-dead to full simply by sitting on your can for 20 minutes. Even in game-time, that only accounts for about 8 hours. Embracing realism in this case would lead to tedium in the game.

    The same philosophy is true for things such as archery. Though realistically, a person with a really good bow could kill something in but a few shots, from a gameplay standpoint that would be too powerful. Though tedium isn't an issue with this, the sanctity and meaning of gameplay (e.g. challenge) is at stake.

    GM Events Involving City Assaults
    We'd like to see it too, and have tried it on occasion. Unfortunately it's always followed by people coming to the boards and complaining how we get our kicks out of killing newbies. After about the 3rd or 4th time we decided to reevaluate our plans and try to hold these types of things at least one zones from a newbie zone.

    Harmony and Feign Death
    Harmony and Feign Death cannot be compared directly. The spell and skill have two completely different uses. Harmony, for instance, is an outdoor-only offensive spell, and Feign Death is an escape skill that works everywhere.

Doing a worthwhile update for poison is far more difficult than putting in something like Evade or the hide/sneak/undead thing. It will take a good deal of effort to get it right.

He just happened to appear on track so I went to check him out.

What you need basically is the Solvedi Scimitar and the Treant Staff, both quests in Rathe Mtns. Then you need the testimony & the head of a fallen druid, named Jordynn or something like that, who is a new rare spawn in SK now.

Turn these 4 items in to him and you'll get the scim. Luckily I did the solvedi scim and treant staff quest long ago and had them wasting away in my bank. Jordynn I found in SK sort of near the pyramid. I think maybe talking to Oaksword made him spawn since I've spent lots of time in SK with many different characters and never ever saw him before. Jordynn coned blue at 48 and was pretty nasty.. I'd say a 45ish druid, but I had a full group and we were able to take him down :)

I have screenshots of what he was saying that you can see at http://www.tarewmarr.net/shots/oak.jpg and also one of me holding the scim at http://www.tarewmarr.net/shots/scim.jpg

So, go get one :)

Wow, everyone must check this out!
