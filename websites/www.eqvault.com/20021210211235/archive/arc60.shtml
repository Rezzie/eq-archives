Shaman Talk
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:41 PM PST
Back on the Gameplay Board, Alan made one huge post about the recent issues troubling the Shaman class:

    Hi everyone,

    I've taken a bunch of topics out of various posts and cobbled together this monster. I ended up not getting the names of the original posters, I hope you can forgive me if I don't attribute your posts or your ideas. I guess I'm more interested in addressing the ideas than giving credit…

    First, some news about a few things:

    1) Despite what I previously wrote about our satisfaction with the 55th level pet spell, we are still looking at it. I'm not promising any changes. But the debate continues…

    2) The Rage Line: All the spells in the line should be working along the same lines as Rage now. But we did find an error. Fury was (is) giving a dex debuff instead of a dex buff. That should be fixed with our next patch (though there is no date set for a patch yet). There were some stacking issues to consider. We've looked them over, and they are working the way they should work. So, yes, it does overwrite some stat buffs and not others. That's the way it's going to stay. Changing anything about the way they stack would undoubtedly cause other stacking issues, and nobody wants that. :)

    Ok, on to the various post snips. Keep in mind that this is sort of a first run through. You're getting my ideas and comments here, not company policy. I'm trying to get my ducks in a row so that I can present cogent ideas to the team.

    [Quote from original post: Icons for group disease and poison buff - when applied, the icon appears exactly the same as if you are poisoned or diseased, would it be possible to tint the icons blue so people know its a buff and not a dot. This also applies for the druid group resist fire and cold.]>

    Actually, I think a lot of icons probably need to be redone. It's something that I really would like to see get a revamp. But, really, this isn't a Shaman thing, it's something that everyone would probably like. I'm not ignoring this idea, just moving it off this thread and into my want list.

    [Quote from original post: Alchemy - 2 things

    1) NPC merchants will only buy back potions for between 9 gold and 2 plat, regardless of the cost to make the potion. Could this be made more in line with jewlry, maybe were merchants can buy back potions for 80 - 90% of the cost. Currently, the cost of practicing is enormious with no chance of profit in the end. I spent 2500 pp to practice the other day, and went from a skill of 50 to 90, to practice above this level will cost between 15pp - 200pp per attempt.]>

    I'll ask into it. But keep in mind that trade skills are designed to only be profitable when items are sold to players. But if the return isn't at least reasonable when selling to vendors, I'll see if I can find out why.

    [Quote from original post: 2) Currently only SOW potions have any demand at all, and occasionaly gate potions if people can afford them (cheaper to pay a druid or wizard). Could new gate potions be added to the new expansions zones?]>

    I'll look into it. But my first thought is that Velious is designed to be hard to get to, so that's unlikely. And, well, it might not be the intent of Alchemy to reduce the need to have a wizard or druid to zap you around the globe.

    [Quote from original post: Torpor – Doesn’t stack with chloroplast/regrowth, spirit of wolf, Turgur’s Insects, Ignite Blood, Plague, Celestial Elixer. None of these cases make sense except for SoW. We are not asking for Torpor to cancel DOTs, just stack with them, as well as CE and regen. In addition this spell hardly compares to other class Lvl 60 spells, with only 1200 hp total heal and all the negative effects, feel free to make it useful.]>

    Well, stacking regens is always going to be very limited. And it makes some sense to me that you can't go into torpor while under a DoT. It's a lot of healing for little mana. It greatly reduces downtime. Personally, I think it's fine as is. But feel free to try to convince me otherwise.

    [Quote from original post: Avatar – Does not stack with Focus of Spirit and Wolf Form. Even if you want to make some of the components (str for example) not stack please make the overall buff and non-redundant parts of these stack. This is not the main problem however, which is that for 325 mana, a 1 minute duration and a 3 minute recast, with quite questionable benefits (for example this is not anywhere near what 70% haste spells do), shamans do not use this spell at all.]>

    Again, it seems like a great spell to me. Perhaps others can wade in here and explain to me why this isn't a good spell. Keep in mind that stacking is a very complicated issue.

    [Quote from original post: Talisman of the Serpent – Most shamans consider this spell nothing less than an insult. Please just remove it. If you are feeling generous replace it with something a little more useful, a group Talisman of Kragg would be OK.]>

    Not all spells will be 'A' list spells. There's no real reason to remove this spell. Heck, if you're playing with a lot of bards…

    [Quote from original post: Stacking of Curse of Spirits, effect on epic weapon Spear of Fate- Splurt, Choke, Ignite Blood all overwrite it. It does not take on someone with Skin Like Nature series buff on. These are all obviously oversights on your part please correct.]>

    I'll look into it and see if these are, in fact, oversights or if they are as intended. It's not so obvious to me…

    [Quote from original post: Tigir’s Insects – Range of the area effect is pretty darned small. As this spell is so rare most shamans don’t have it yet to complain about this, but they will.]>

    Well, there are a fair number of spells with a very small area of effect. It takes a bit of skill to land on a lot of targets, but it can be done. Keep in mind that monsters don’t have much, er… volume, so they can be stacked very close together. This doesn't seem like something that needs to be changed.

    [Quote from original post: It's pretty obvious from the original spell listing that shamans were designed as a generalist priest class with the following features:
    1. Buffs far beyond any other class
    2. Debuffing abilities somewhat better than an enchanter.
    3. Second-tier healing. Similar to druids in straight heals, with regeneration spells to help.
    4. Strong, but limited (only two) Damage over Time lines.
    5. Use of a second-tier pet at higher level
    6. Slightly better melee skills than the other priests.
    Sound about right?]>

    Sounds right to me, for the most part. I have a disagreement with point #2, but I'll get to that in a second.

    [Quote from original post: On the face, it sounds like a good plan. The realities of play at level 50 had some interesting effects:
    1. Stats were never implemented in a manner to make stat buffs overly useful. The ratio of their effect to mana expendature make them iffy to use in a lot of circumstances. The lines of buffs that did really matter (AC, HP, melee speed) were done better by clerics, and about equivalently to enchanters.]>

    And later you mentioned something about buffs not being mana efficient either. Honestly, mana efficiency isn't the end-all and be-all of determining the value of a class. It's a consideration, but it's not the only thing that matters. The ability to do something that is useful might fully overpower the idea that you can't do it efficiently. Tell me if I'm wrong here, but shaman do have the most efficient buffs (with perhaps the noted exception of AC and HP by clerics and attack speed by enchanters). You must be comparing efficiently, I guess, to just blasting things. Keep in mind, though, that blasting things may not always be an option. Buffing the melee types in some cases might be more efficient.

    But I'm willing to accept your comments as they are, even if I might not believe in them as strongly as you do.

    [Quote from original post: 2. This worked pretty much as planned. Enchanters did most of our abilities at a slightly lower level, and mages could do resist debuffing at a lower level of effectiveness.]>

    Actually, I had always thought that enchanters were supposed to be the masters of debuffing. For me, the current setup seems just right on this account.

    [Quote from original post: 3. Worked as planned.
    4. Worked as planned, but got hit by a nerf aimed to stop kiting, which was never our forte anyway.]>

    Good enough.

    [Quote from original post: 5. Went okay here. The only real problem was our pet got hit by some changes aimed at reigning in the power of the pets that WERE problematic, namely necro and mage pets.]>
    Goodness, pets again! :)

    But, essentially, pets for shaman work as intended, right? They add a bit of damage and tanking, and they look cool.

    [Quote from original post: 6. True, but largely irrelevant since at higher levels, necessity really dictates the higher efficiency of medding over melee in most situations, and we were never close in combat effectiveness anyway.]>

    But not at all irrelevant to those in the level to use it. The ability for a shaman to melee pretty well makes the earlier levels a fairly easy go for them. Again, I think this works out well enough.

    [Quote from original post: So we got hit by a couple nerfs aimed at others, and the "king of buffs" title was both misleading and largely useless, but all in all, it was a good class. What happened with Kunark? Well, look at the spell list as a whole:

    1. We got a TON of buffs. All 5 stats get a bigger version AND all 5 get group versions. About a third of the RoK spell list is buffs.]>

    I gather you disagree with the continuance of buffs as the main focus of shaman. I think I understand this, and I'll bring it to the team. Don't, however, expect a revamp of all the spells post 50. It's not very likely that we'll be suddenly changing all buffs to DD spells or something wild like that. As you stated, up to 50 shamans are pretty darn great. I'm of the opinion that buffs are part of that greatness. But I think I see what you're getting at.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Nice slow upgrade at 51, with still a slim lead over enchanters, but the Mage Mala line is much closer to our Malo than previously. In fact, from 51 to 59, mages have the same single-target debuff spell as shamans.]>

    I guess this means that you're ok with the progression, and just upset that magicians have gained that type of spell. I'm not a big fan of the spreading of abilities over classes either.

    [Quote from original post: 3. Worked okay. We got superior heal finally, as well as Regrowth and Torpor (once in its current version) to help. Fizzle rates are insane on superior heal, but the design idea was right.]>

    All 'secondary' powerful spells like that have a bit more fizzle to them. You're not alone in that. And it is, frankly, just another drawback to the spell. Personally, I'm not sure that shaman should have superior healing… it's not unlike giving mages Malosi…

    [Quote from original post: 4. Necros get our prized #1 DoT spell (Envenomed Bolt) at 51, then several upgrades. Druids get two DoT upgrades by 54. Shamans don't get their DoT updates -- and somewhat disappointing ones -- at 56 and 59!]>

    Confusion! You don't get he upgrades at all, or you're disappointed with them…

    I looked it up. They seem to be there. In fact, here's what Everlore says about Pox of Bertoxxulous:

    "This is believed to be the best DoT in game. It is the last Shaman DoT, doing close to 2100 damage."

    Not that I hold everything on spoiler sites to be the gospel truth… :)

    Please, give me some detail as to why these upgrades are disappointing.

    [Quote from original post: 5. The pet upgrade is only minimally better than the 49 pet while hitting a HUGE cost increase. Our second-tier pet actually becomes third-tier as by level 60, Shadowknights have better pets.]>

    I know… I know. We're still looking into this one.

    [Quote from original post: Now, as I mentioned, at 50 shamans were a pretty strong class, and it's probably true that a slight cut in overall power relative to the other classes wasn't out of line. But virtually every defining factor of the class was given a terribly minor update, save one. And, in a lot of cases, we were matched or surpassed by other classes in areas we'd been the specialists in.]>

    I disagree with the minor update thing. And, well, if you agree that shaman were perhaps on the top few rungs at 50, then how else would you suggest we upgrade them?

    [Quote from original post: The one that takes up over a third of our spell list: buffs.
    It's really puzzling. One gets the feeling that the live team doesn't really quite grasp the role of the shaman in high level play, and although they think they're giving us key improvements in our major areas, we see them as near-worthless toss-ins that add to the pile of low-value spells that take the place that more interesting or useful spells could go. What's more, impinging on our REAL defining abilities by other classes is okay because they're not viewed by the design team as being shaman core abilities.]>

    It seems that it's worthwhile to have a talk about stat buffs in house. But, well, I'm not convinced that they rate as… how did you put it… near-worthless. As for giving out core abilities to other classes, well, I don't like that either. It's always a fine line, though, between making classes playable and keeping things distinct for them as well. This is something that I also want to bring up to the team.

    [Quote from original post: A prime example of this would be the abilities of the class breastplates. In Kunark, the shaman BP casts a damage shield. This is really puzzling since apart from alchemy, we don't have damage shields, and the damage from it (5 points) is tiny... the real effect is the AC buff it provides. In Velious, the shaman BP casts a stamina buff. While not totally useless, it's hardly what we'd expect, when comparing our effects to say that of clerics (who get Complete Heal and Celestial Elixir).]>

    I don't want to make this into an item discussion… I really think those sorts of things are best served in a different thread. But I'll mention this to the team as well.

    [Quote from original post: I'm not talking about the strength of the spells. It's that the clerics (and other casting classes) seem to be getting spells that are central to their function in game -- primary healing. Whereas an ac buff+damage shield and stamina buff seem to say a shaman's primary function is to buff. When it's not true at all -- our primary function is generally to DEBUFF. More appropriate spells would be things like Turgur's Insects, Malosi, or Cripple.]>

    Well, this seems to be where you and I depart philosophy. Maybe it's just me, I don't know. The damage shield obviously isn't part of your core function as a shaman. But buffing really is. Debuffing is a secondary thing. But, as I've said, I'll talk this over with the team. Contrary to popular belief, we do have people that play shaman on the team…

    [Quote from original post: I think this is the primary obstacle between the designers and players -- as far as the players are concerned, shamans just aren't there to buff. We may do it, but it's not why we're needed in the high level game, and it's not our major contribution to the group. With the game mechanics as they are, this is not likely to change. What DOES need re-evaluating is the priority that function is given when designing shaman spell lists and assigning appropriate effects to class-specific items.]>

    Again, perhaps… Buffing is certainly something that is a primary focus at lower levels. Obviously, as you've stated, you think that focus changes post 50. And, again, I'll talk to the team about it.

    Wow. I'm sorry this thing ran so long. I'm just probing for ideas and clarifications, and letting you know where I stand and what I think. Feel free to try to change my mind. It doesn't really seem that, reading over all the threads, there is all that much that you're unhappy with. I guess I'm a bit shocked to see so many posts about shamans lately…

    Alan


Absor Infobits

Alan made his daily visit(s) today to the Gameplay Board. Here's what he said:

  • Does Absor Revist Threads?
    I revisit threads when I see a need to. I'm certainly willing to discuss things that come up after I've posted.

    And, sure, I do end up repeating myself in seperate threads sometimes. Usually I do that when there's a point I want to make.

  • Getting a Response
    Well, the point of posting them to this board is because I also want people to respond. How would moving my post to a special board help that?

    And, even if we did have a board as you suggest, we would still be asking people to keep to one thread here as well, so that wouldn't change much.

    I'll mull the idea over a bit and see what I think. :)

    Thanks.

  • Overpowered
    Quinn wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "No, it wasn't an exploit. I don't consider that anyone who used this was exploiting, nor do I think Verant thinks so either.

    It's not an exploit issue, it's a balance issue."]>

    Exactly. Nobody 'exploited' anything. We put in an item that was too powerful, and we've adjusted it.

    We certainly don't like doing that sort of thing. But we'd rather have a balanced game that will still be fun years from now and take the heat for making the mistake of putting that item in as it was in the first place.

  • /camp server and /camp desktop
    It seems that we didn't get the code in for these commands in for the last patch. We will get them in for the next.

  • Where has Brother Qwinn Gone?
    One of our programmers looked at the files for that zone, comparing those on Rathe with another randomly selected server. They match perfectly.

    Whatever's wrong would have to be happening on all servers, if it was due to the software. Reports in this post suggest that this is not the case.

    Another theory was proposed. One of our guys is an ex-GM. He's seen things like this happen. It's possible that someone is killing this NPC during non-peak times, perhaps a European player. Since this person plays on your off times you never hear from him or her, and so your server community (those on mostly at the same time as you) might think it's broken.

    I just wanted to summarize what is happening and what has happened with Lure spells (hopefully I understand it).

    "After looking closely at the way saves are done when the target is higher level than the caster, it appears that Lures would get saving throws in situations where they should not. I'm fixing it now, it should go on test shortly.

    Currently on live some NPCs are getting saves versus lures that they should not be getting."

    This change, however, will not take effect until we patch next. With the holidays on us, it's hard to tell when that will be.

    December 19th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Patch Day*

    Today's patch primarily consists of tuning of Velious zones as well as content and other additions. In addition, the following changes have been implemented on the Live servers:

    - High-end items that have a activatable (right-click) healing effect have in several cases had their casting times increased.
    - Factions for some of the higher end encounters in Velious have received some adjustments. Basically, "bad acts" receive more weight than "good acts". For instance, if "A" hates "B" and "B" hates "A", killing "A" will hurt your faction with "A" more than it will help your faction with "B".

    - The spell attached to the "Fiery Defender" can now be partially resisted instead of being "all or nothing". The net result is that the average damage will be higher.
    - The Paladin "Holy Forge" discipline will now give paladins triple the normal chance of scoring a critical hit. Each critical hit has a percent chance to be scored as a critical blow.
    - Added parameters to the /camp command (the command that will cause you to camp out if you are already sitting). "/camp server" will take you back to the server selection screen. "/camp desktop" will take you out to the desktop following a successful camp. Despite popular request, "/camp spawn" will not be implemented.
    - Walruses now indeed sound like they should.
    - Added the /chatfontsize [0 to 5] (default is 1) command. This command will allow players to adjust the size of the font in the chat window.

    - The EverQuest Team

[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:12 PM PST
A site, which mimics the look and content of the official EQ site, was recently discovered to contain an ActiveX backdoor Trojan Horse. For your reference I will post a copy of this url in plain text (with a few X's placed for caution) and not hyperlinked, DO NOT visit it:

    xxsephiroth.xoasis.comxx

Here is a little more information from EQ Live's Associate Producer Gordon Wrinn:
    Now, if you've curiously gone to the address anyway, you should be able to
    easily deinfect yourself by deleing EA.HTA from your system (just do a
    search, it can be found in your C:\windows\start menu\programs\startup
    directory) as long as you do so BEFORE YOU REBOOT.

    If you've done gone and rebooted anyway, you'll need to go the long route to
    remove it (see below). In any case, please feel free to send out the alarm.
Gordon also included some important information to tell if you have it, and how to remove it:
    **HOW TO TELL IF YOU ARE INFECTED**
    Go to your start button, press run. Type in msconfig and hit return. Click
    on the startup tab. Look in the list presented for an instance of ;
    run= onz.exe
    If you have this entry, you will need to manually remove it. To remove this
    back door;
    1) Uncheck the check mark next to the run= onz.exe line and then click the
    apply button at the bottom of the window.
    2) Click on the general tab then click on advanced on the bottom of the
    window. Check the "enable startup menu" check box, click ok. Click ok again,
    and allow system to reboot.
    3) DO NOT let the system boot normally. You MUST select "Safe command
    prompt" from the boot menu.
    4) At the DOS prompt, type in "cd windows" (or the path to your window
    directory), press the enter key.
    5) Type in "del onz.exe" and then press the enter key.
    6) Reboot your system again.
    7) Select "normal" from the boot menu.
    8) If you get a window stating that the system can NOT find onz.exe, click
    ok.
    a. Click the Windows Start Button and then click Run.
    b. Type in "system.ini".
    c. You will now see a line that's looks like this; shell=Explorer.exe
    onz.exe.
    d. Remove the instance of onz.exe so it looks like this; shell=Explorer.exe.

    e. Save the changes to the file and then reboot once more.
    You have now removed the trojan from your system.
As long as you haven't stumbled onto this site in the past, or visit it now of course, you should be fine.

Shadoe's Corner: The Delicate Art of Storytelling
[12 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:43 PM PST
Shadoe has written his latest editorial entitled Role-Playing: The Delicate Art of Storytelling. It's now available to read in Shadoe's Corner. Here's a clip:

    Those of you who follow EverQuest Express every week know by now that the story I've been working on for the past year has finally crawled out of its primeval soup and seen the light of day. The prologue and chapter one have at last been published, and each successive week will reveal another chapter. Having this, my first real fictional piece, out in the open for everyone to see is an enormous step for me emotionally. But I'm eager to share it with you all, as I surely think it's a story worth telling.

    Reading the first chapter again at EQX has given me time to reflect on where this all began, and how it evolved into a novel-length tale. Initially, the plot began with two characters my wife and I created on the Test Server. As time went on, more and more plot segments fell into place until I was presented with an actual story to tell - something I could work with to bring the world of Norrath alive for myself.
