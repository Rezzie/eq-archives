#8   Name: Khaavren (wilc(at)goldinc.com)   Date: 4/5 @ 15:10 PM PST
To Yeldarb:
Mithril Two Hander: This was a mistake on my
part, this was supposed to be listed under
forbidden weapons. The page should now be
correct.
As to me reading and following quests as they
are written. First, that was very offensive
of you to say. Let me also be just as rude.
If *you* would quit surfing the web and reading
spoilers to quests, how would you know about
the nitrate quest (which is an alternative way
of getting the Bayle list from Mammoth) ... but
more on that later. Also, doing the Soulfire
quest *as it is written* and role playing a
Knight of Thunder, you would not immediately slay
the Militia person. However, after they show
their true colors, you would (judge by word and
deed, from the Code). The problem is, that by
that time, it is too late ... the Miltia person
despawns. You have to do the first part over
again. *However*, I see your point, and many
others seem to agree, and therefore I have
decided to move Soulfire back under accepted
weapons. Also, the Brilliant Sword of Faith
will be moved under accepted weapons, as well -
it was listed as forbidden since it is part of
the Soulfire quest.
Mammoth: I may be missing something here. Here
is why it is listed as forbidden:
I first encountered Mammoth at about level 22.
I had admiably or better faction with all the
residents of Surefall, as I was still trying
to uphold honor with my character back then.
Mammoth, however, was aggressive, and he attacked
me on sight. Perhaps this is changed. I know
about the nitrate quest which makes Mammoth
regurgitate the Bayle List, but how can a Paladin
complete the quest when Mammoth aggros him? You
can not enlist another's aid, either, as the list
is no-drop. Perhaps Mammoth is no longer aggro,
it has been five or more months since I did that
quest. ALSO, a Paladin, following Runethar's
instructions (Toe needs the Bayle list) would
not know about the nitrate quest - his directions
are to slay Mammoth, per Frenway, who is the
contact person that Runethar sends you to.
Granted, a person could choose not to do the
quest, and then honestly do the nitrate quest
(without using spoiler web sites) and get the
list and thus be able to finish the quest. But
how does one overcome Mammoth's KoS attitude to
even level one Paladins of Karana (who
have unmodified base factions)? If someone can
confirm that Mammoth is no longer aggro to
Paladins of Karana, I will *gladly* move this
quest and the reward weapon back into the
accepted list.
Armour of Ro: Read my Code again. One of the
(many) reasons that Ro is forbidden is that Lord
Searfire requires fairy dusts and melatite, both
of which "can only be obtained by thievery or
murder of peaceful, non-aggressive races". This
IS the primary reason it is forbidden to a Knight
of Thunder. Another strong reason it is
forbidden is that Karana and Ro are almost
enemies. Thus, your reasons for Ro being
forbidden ARE the same as mine. But I also think
Ro would be unacceptable even if it did not
require fairy dusts and melatite ... there is
only one Paladin of Ro on each server,
Ortallious the Orc. Why would other Paladins
wear the Armour of a God they do not follow and
whose practices are evil, or at least of
questionable morales?
Race specific abilities: They weren't meant to
be race specific. They were meant to be GOD
specific. As it so happens, dwarven paladins
can only worship Brell, and High Elves can only
follow Tunare. Perhaps I should make that more
clear on my page.
As far as class balance: the ideas I listed were
merely examples. The point I was trying to make
is I (and I think many other paladins) would be
MUCH happier if their choice of God meant
something more than initial faction settings.
I'm not looking to make the Paladin class more
powerful; hell, if a Paladin of Karana would just
do a proc every once in a while that does NO
damage but causes a thunder flash, I would be
EXTREMELY happy (that is randomly during combat
the sound associated with thunder would play).
Anyway, thanks to you and everyone else who has
commented, and sorry if I got on the defensive.

Khaavren

#10   Name: Khaavren (wilc(at)goldinc.com)   Date: 4/5 @ 18:01 PM PST
To Lwedari:

You state that many people (and imply that I myself am one of them) have absolutely no idea what Paladins were like or were all about. First of all, there never *were* Paladins in the real world.

You imply that Paladins in Everquest are best compared to Templar Knights of the real world's past. What makes you think this?

To what real world organization would you then compare Enchanters?

You state that the Paladin's one and only goal was to destroy enemies of their faith. What Paladins are you talking about? People who organized or participated in the Crusades were *not* Paladins.

Here is the indisputable definition of a Paladin, straight from Verant's official EverQuest Page:

"Paladins are the very picture of a holy knight, fighting for the cause of good in all aspects of his life. They share some of the power of a Cleric, and a good deal of the fighting ability of a Warrior, but have a few powers all their own"

No where in that definition are Templars mentioned. No where are crusades mentioned. I am truly sorry, but your post was not only rude, but terribly incorrect. The only thing stated in Verant's definition is that Paladins fight for good in all aspects of their life. I would say you know less about role playing a Paladin than most because you are assuming that ALL Paladins should act like the Templar Knights; which is, simply, incorrect. Note that I think it would be quite acceptable to role play a Paladin character like a Templar Knight - but just because the Code of Karana does not match the definition of a Templar Knight in no way makes it less a viable role playing option.

Khaavren
