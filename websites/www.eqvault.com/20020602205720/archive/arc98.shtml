Brad on the Clipping Plane
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:48 PM PDT
Brad McQuaid was on the boards today answering a post concerning the clipping plane issue. Here's the original post followed by Brad's response:

    I've been saying for MONTHS that the clipping plane slider bar is bugged. But Verant has yet to fix it. They say they have moved out the file header to max on the server, but since this is a client side bug, its still not fixed. I finnally got fed up at being believed by everyone BUT Verant, so I took the time to makes these pictures.
    As you can see, as the rain subsites, it moves off, reveiling the real max clipping plane, but when the rain actually stops, the haze sets in, breaking the clipping planes max distance.
    Abashi said that the clipping plane on each zone was set to max. Well if it was set to max, why is the rain able to go farther out then we are able to see?
    I also made a comparison picture so you could see the difference.
    ----------------------------------

    Here we have a normal day of EQ (typhoon)

    http://members.home.net/fireflyis/OverthereRaining.jpg

    ----------------------------------

    Here we have the rain moving off, and as you can see, it reveils a lot of open land. This wasn't even the farthest it goes, but its hard to take a pic at the right time when you only have 2 seconds.

    http://members.home.net/fireflyis/OverthereRainStopping.jpg

    -----------------------------------

    Here is what we normally see. Lots of sky, not much land. The above might not look like a lot of extra land, but when you take the time to study it, you can see that it really can ammount to a LOT of extra distance.

    http://members.home.net/fireflyis/OverthereRainStopped.jpg

    -----------------------------------

    Here is a comparison of the 2 views. I didn't want to confuse anyone and have them say that "They can't see the difference"

    http://members.home.net/fireflyis/OverthereCompare.jpg

    -----------------------------------

    Any Questions?

    First, thanks for the screenshots -- it helped us understand part of this issue.
    Jeff Butler and I looked into this personally today and came to some conclusions.

    But first, some info into how clipping planes work.

    1. When an outdoor zone is exported from the tools we use to create it, the artist chooses a maximum clip plane. This setting affects how far out visibility is calculated, and can affect file size and frame rate.

    2. Most old world zones were exported with a much lower number than most Kunark zones.

    3. Each zone has a minimum and maximum clip plane number associated with it server side. These are the numbers you move between when you adjust the clipping plane slider in-game.

    4. The slider bar is supposed to work as a percentage. In other words, in one zone if the min is 1 and the max 1000, were the slider in the center, you'd be at 500. But if the min was 1 and the max 100, say for another zone, you'd be at 50 with the slider in the middle.

    Ok, so, after looking into this and checking out the screenshots referred to in this thread we were able to determine the following:

    1. For some reason (we'll ask the programmer tomorrow), when rain stops, the clipping plane moves out beyond the max briefly and then settles back at the max. For example, I went to Overthere and verified what the screenshots showed: the Overthere's max clip is 1800 feet. It looked like the clip went to 2000 or so briefly when the sky was still gray and then returned to 1800 when the sky was on again.

  • Rare Spawn Complaints
    Most of the complaints I see in regards to these rare spawns for quest components is related to the general feeling that there is an "entitlement" to these items. There isn't. No one is entitled to any particular item.

    You can't fix a situation where everyone is camping by making what they are camping for more plentiful. That just encourages camping, as the camp duration gets shorter, and more people feeel that the camp time is worth the effort. As it is now, maybe people will get the idea that the camp is too long, and just choose not to camp. Then perhaps someone happening by can get lucky and find the spawn in question. If people are still choosing to camp it, perhaps we need to look into making it even rarer, closing the quest, or look into creating other items that represent reasonable alternatives to the item that people are choosing to camp.

  • EverQuest & FPS Games
    I completely agree that Doom and other First-person shooter type games are very fun, despite the lack of a penalty for death. However, those games are not about building and molding a character to your desires. The worlds aren't persistent. There's is nothing that can be achieved by playing for a year that can't be achieved in the first few minutes of each life.

    The games are completely different and cannot be measured against each other. The concept of "Challenge" is implemented in different ways. EverQuest implements challenge by providing risks. In order to provide risks, there must be some downside that will be realised should a task not be completed. That downside can't be enjoyable, or it wouldn't be a risk.

    I do wish that I could explain this to everyone's satisfaction. If anyone can offer any suggestions to this end, I'd certainly appreciate it.

  • Gaining Experience
    Experience given by a creature is based upon its level, and the zone you are in. It is not based upon its level in relation to you, other than at the point that you stop receiving experience from killing a particular level creature.

    In other words, if you are level 50, a level 49 creature will give more experience than a level 48 creature, even though they are both blue.

Gordon Infobits, Part 2
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:32 PM PDT
As promised, here is the second part of today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Explain ATK and AC x/1000 Caps
    Numbers over 1000 are indeed calculated.

    Back in beta, for a time, AC and ATK were displayed with two numbers each, showing the different components of the factor. For instance, AC was displayed with both a damage avoidance and a damage mitigation factor. This was highly confusing to a majority of the players, so both numbers were combined for the purpose of display.

    Basically, the two numbers were added, then divided by a theoretical max that one could attain at the time, yielding a percentage. It's this percentage that is displayed.

    Now, when we brought new items into the game that raised the true theoretical max (as opposed to the calculated theoretical max), and espicially when we brought out Kunark, we could have raised the calculated theoretical max so that the percentage would always be below 1000, but that would have resulted in most people seeing their AC drop, making them think that we had somehow nerfed it. So we left the calculated max the same, yeilding numbers greater than 1000.

    For actual combat calculations, the values you see are still reduced into their specific componenets which aren't capped with exception of lower level twinking-impact caps that we've referenced before.

  • Speed Advantage
    Well with any internet-based game, a better connection never hurts. However EQ is designed to mask some of the typical problems experienced by slower connections. For instance, some games won't allow you to move unless packets are flowing. EQ does, and sync's up with the server regularly to see that you aren't doing something illegal.

    The biggest difference experienced is usually the time it takes to zone.

  • Server Population Cap
    As I've mentioned before, capping server population is not something that we consider to be a valid option, and certainly wouldn't help retain players. People would get upset and leave if they suddenly found themselves unable to play that character that they have months of playtime invested in. It would be an all-too-regular occurence if server population were capped.

    Also, people new to the game often have friends that already play. We'd be shooting ourselves in the foot if we just told all of these new players that they couldn't play with their friends.

    As for your assertions on what would be fixed:

    [Quote from original post: Camping would be lessened]

    No, it wouldn't. People still camp on the test server which only has about 500 people on it at peak. They camp because its an easier and preferred method to get experience and loot. What there *would* be is less people waiting to join a particular camp. We're always working on this issue by trying to get people to spread out, moving loot around, randomizing drops, etc.

    [Quote from original post: Class envy would be less because competition would be less.]

    Class-envy will exist as long as some class can do something that another class can't. It's not related to competition for most.

    [Quote from original post: Finding groups would be easier]

    Actually we've found that smaller populations have more problems finding groups than larger ones. With more people on the server, there are more groups, and thus more groups that aren't full, leading to more groups that need people.

    Servers with smaller populations (PvP servers, Test) often have people who cite problems finding groups, as well as problems getting services such as binding or resurrections during off-peak times.

  • Customer Support Helping Retrieve Corpses
    [Quote from original post: I am curious, what bad aspects are there in helping me find my corpse?]

    Corpse recovery, in this case, is an intended part of the game. The Guide probably went to your corpse, found it in an open and accessible area, and then determined that he couldn't help you.

  • /ignore & Spells
    [Quote from original post: I am a lvl 50 wizard on the Prexus server and I got my Temp Flux Staff at level 20, for 30 levels I have used it as a pulling and distance gauge. To suddenly change it as a quick fix to a "so called" spam problem is totally unfair. A better fix would be to make the /ignore work with spell effects...]

    /ignore does work on spell effects, but not affects upon YOU. In other words, you won't see "IgnoredPerson begins to cast a spell", but you will see "You feel friendly", because it is an effect upon you.

    At the point that you are feeling the effects of the spell, the game doesn't know who cast it. Changing the game on a broad scale to include that information for the sake of two items really wouldn't be a good use of time.

    I do apologize to everyone affected by the casting times.

    Though I appreciate the effort to form an argument and draw a parallel between the two issues, looking at a spellbook and being able to bind anywhere aren't related.

    It's not a bug, but rather it is based upon the level that you get the spell in question. Since hybrids get the spells post-50, they are treated like level 50+ spells of other classes.

    *Casting Times on Staff and Rod*

    We received word a few weeks ago that some people were using these items to "spam" other players with their spell-effect messages, causing them to disconnect. Though /ignore will allow you to filter out text messages caused by people on your /ignore list, it will not allow you to filter out spell-effect messages for spells cast upon you.

    The short casting time should prevent the problems associated with the targetable and activatable effect on these weapons, however you will have to stand still to use them now.

    *Gunthak Harpoon*

    In the last item patch, we made the mistake of making this weapon "rentable", meaning that it does not disappear upon logoff. Basically, one of our game designers heard that the weapon was NORENT, concluded that it must be a bug, and took the initiative to fix it. Unfortunately, the stats on this weapon, despite its disadvantages of weight and its non-magical composition, make it much too powerful for its location and difficulty in obtaining it.

    For these reasons, the weapon is once again NORENT, and will disappear the next time that you log out. This is a retroactive change and will affect weapons already in possession. We do apologize to those who acquired it within the last week expecting that it would be a permanent weapon.

    increase the capabilities of both servers and help alleviate the congestion
