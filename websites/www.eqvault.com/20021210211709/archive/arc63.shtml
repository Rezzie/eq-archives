Absor Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:58 PM PST
Here is today's edition of the Absor Infobits:

  • When is a Bug a Bug?
    Well, the reasoning is pretty simple.

    If someone died because an NPC killed him, that NPC does not want him to come back to life. The only reason that someone would drag off a corpse would be to restore his life. Friend or not, that NPC doesn't want you to do that. Just because you are a friend of the NPC and the dead guy is your friend, that does not make the dead guy the friend of the NPC. That is an illogical conclusion.

    But the real reason for the corpse dragging thing is because people could use this feature to bypass parts of the game that a feature isn't supposed to let them pass. The choices were, basically, remove the feature entirely, never implement interesting new gameplay that the feature would make worthless, or modify the feature. The right choice was to modify the feature.

  • Client Font Size in EQClient.ini
    The rumor is that font sizing is something that we'll be adding in the very near future.

  • CD Key
    Just to repeat what Bloodsage said, it's a very good idea to keep your CD key (the whole CD, really). We do use that to verify your account if we need to.

  • On "Gift of Magic" Spell
    It works. It's a great spell. But it doesn't do what you think it's supposed to do…

  • Petition Reponse Times Too Slow

Updates to Disciplines
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:56 PM PST
Alan announced some new changes to the disciplines today in an announcement posted to the Gameplay Board. Here's the complete post:

    We've decided to change a few of the new disciplines. These changes will go into effect with the emergency patch (though this is not the reason for the patch, just something that we are slipping in while we're at it).

    Resistant – At 30th level the resistance gain will be +3. This will increase at increments until 50th level, where it will reach its maximum of +10.

    The Ranger True Shot – The chance of hitting with a bow while using this discipline will increase, along with the other benefits.

    Shadowknights Unholy Aura – along with the 25% increase in damage, this discipline will also make the Harm Touch less resistable, along the lines of a Lifetap resistance.

    Paladins Holy Forge – the ability to critical with this discipline in use will be doubled. It will, of course, still only affect the undead.

    Alan


Innate Resistances Update
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 3:54 PM PST
Creating a new thread on the Gameplay Board, Alan gave the following update concerning Innate Resistances:

    Another change that is going in with this patch (again, not the reason for the patch, just an extra), will be a change to the way innate resistances work.

    Some of you may remember about a year ago when we boosted the innate resistance for certain classes. It turns out that, because these were created before the ability to gain levels beyond 50, these resistances were not being calculated properly for most classes as they passed level 50.

    This means that, once this is fixed, all classes with these resistances will see an increase in their innate resistance bonus that may be as high as 11 points.

    The exception is warriors, whose resistance bonus was working just fine.

    Alan

Absor Infobits: Evening Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:22 PM PST
Alan made many new posts on a variety of issues today on the Gameplay Board. Here's the scoop:

  • Can't Get to Patch Server
    I had that problem for a few minutes myself.

    Try now, it's working for me.

  • Post SoV Bugs in this Thread
    Fair enough. Despite the obviously sarcastic intent of your post, let's use this space to discuss bugs that you find in Velious. I'll pass them on to the team.

  • Can't Take Screenshots of SoV
    As some of you may know, we disabled the ability to take screen shots during the testing of Velious.

    Unfortunately, we forgot to re-enable screen shots when the expansion went live. This means that those of you with Velious accounts can't take screen shots right now. We will turn them back on with the patch this Thursday.

    Sorry about that…

  • Inaccurate Info on SoV Box
    Yes, we noticed that too... a bit too late. If you switch those too RAM numbers it's correct... :)

  • Summon Corpse Disabled?
    Well… no.

    There will be some places where summon corpse is restricted in its use, and perhaps others where it won't work at all. But, for the most part it works just fine in Velious.

  • Inaccurate Number of Subscribers Reported?
    We really have no reason to lie about our numbers to you.

    Sinseraph wrote:

    >"why do you keep painting a picture that EQ is great and loved by 300,000+ people when in reality half of those have cancelled those subscriptions since release? you say EQ is so great and full of good challenge, content, and expansions, then shouldn't those cancelled subscriptions have been anticipating teh next big release? the next big challenge as you so thrive on? huh, well where are they? they must be missing out eh?"

    We do have over 300,000 active subscribers. That's true. We also believe that having that many subscribers does indicate that a lot of people are enjoying the game. The only place where your figures were correct (actually, a bit high) is when you calculated that about 76,000 people are playing EverQuest during peak hours. I think that's a pretty large number, and that alone indicates that EverQuest is a successful product.

    And, yes, we think that EverQuest is a great game full of good challenge, content and expansions.

    As for those that are anticipating the 'next big challenge'… I was just at an EB today that had sold their 120 copies of Scars of Velious in only a few hours.

    I had nothing to do with the building of the game. If I had, though, I would be extremely proud of that kind of response from the customers. I think the team has created a great product. I'm honored to be associated with it, even in this small way.

  • Quests and Inventory Slots
    Something to keep in mind. Some quests will give you an item that requires a whole inventory slot (one of your main slots). If you do not have an open slot the item will fall to the ground. We all know that items that fall to the ground are not secure, and are not subject to reimbursement. So PLEASE make certain that you have an open inventory slot (at least one) when attempting quests.

  • Bug Discovered: GUI and Alt-M
    We've got that one fixed. It should be fixed with the patch on Thursday.

  • "Bringing the Game back to the Game"
    Winter is a very smart woman.

    Thanks for the comments, Kildian.

  • Lack of SoV Manual
    All sorts of nifty stuff can be discovered if you read the fiction…

    But keep in mind, Velious is for people with characters of about 30th level and over. By now you should be aware that we don’t spell everything out for you. We give you enough information to discover the answers on your own. We encourage you to explore Velious and learn about the place, it's inhabitants and their treasure (which might become your treasure) on your own.

  • On Mage Epic Quest
    Just FYI: the pet doesn't do Dispel.

    I'm glad you all like this item, I sure do.

  • Rogues and Dragging Corpses
    Demonspawn wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "1. You are added to the nearby mobs hate list. In this case hide/sneek won't mean diddly. You will be attacked. Invis means nothing to an agroed mob.

    Not based on what I posted.

    Invisibility and hide and all other such things will work just fine, even if you are dragging a corpse in a place where the new /corpse coding is in use. That is, dragging a corpse will not harm your invisibility or hiding.

  • "Censoring" Threads
    Actually, there are rules against such things. We don’t allow people to flame each other. Calling someone a nazi is a flame, it's a personal attack that I won't allow, directed at me, Verant, our customers or anyone else for that matter. I have no problem with people discussing issues and disagreeing with our policies. But I won't allow people to attack each other that way.

    You may call it censorship if you like. But it is against the rules set up for this board.

  • Stacks
    Soon is an interesting word…

New Producer Letter: Velious Live!

    Hello all,

    On behalf of the entire EverQuest Team past and present, and everyone here at Sony Online Entertainment, I proudly announce the launch of the second EverQuest expansion - The Scars of Velious.

    Created in little over half the time of the previous expansion, and with a significantly larger development team, The Scars of Velious adds 19 challenging new zones to the world of Norrath, including two new Planes. In addition to the new zones, Velious includes over 150 new armor textures, helmets, and weapons for player characters, and an enhanced user interface. Also introduced are many new creatures and monsters, most notably the Coldain dwarves, Giants, and Dragons of Velious. The expansion is specifically designed to challenge the majority of EverQuest players, who are between 30 and 60th level of experience.

    It seems that just a short while ago that I had the pleasure of announcing the launch of the first EverQuest expansion. Our desire to surpass the success of The Ruins of Kunark spurred us to set ambitious goals for The Scars of Velious. As the months of development passed the team overcame what seemed at the onset to be a daunting series of challenges, not the least of which included a tight schedule. We are humbly pleased to present The Scars of Velious without compromise or concession, and we hope that it meets and exceeds your expectations for value and quality in an EverQuest product

    Returning for the second expansion were several experienced members of the original EverQuest and Ruins of Kunark teams. Their expertise combined with that of the veteran EverQuest Live team, made this challenging project possible, and allowed us to take this expansion to a new level of immersive detail. I would like to personally thank them for their unfailing dedication to excellence. It has been an inspiration and an honor to be a part of The Scars of Velious team.

    We encourage you to be among the first in the unfolding history of Norrath to board the amazing Gnomish Icebreaker ship and venture to the frozen continent of Velious. There you will be exploring a land where no adventurer has ever before set foot, where you can choose sides in the struggle between titanic forces locked in an ages-old conflict and view wonders never before seen by the inhabitants of Norrath.

    We expect that the launch of The Scars of Velious expansion will proceed smoothly, but we will maintain our Customer Service staff here at Sony Online Entertainment around the clock to insure that everything performs as expected. As we anticipate customers in record number over the next few days, we ask for your understanding should any problems arise. We will do our utmost to resolve any issues quickly, and with a minimum of downtime or inconvenience.

    In closing, I would like to again thank our loyal players for their support. The ongoing development of EverQuest and its expansions has been a profound pleasure for all of us here at Sony Online Entertainment, and we hope that you will join us in The Scars of Velious, and as EverQuest’s future unfolds. As always, we look forward to seeing you in game.

    Welcome to EverQuest: The Scars of Velious! You’re in our world now!

    For the EverQuest Team

    Jeff Butler
    Producer

Brad on MIP Mapping
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:42 AM PST
Brad quickly responded to a post concerning the status of MIP Mapping. The following is his complete response:

    With the last patch (or maybe the one before that), we decided to turn off mip mapping for everything but the world (in other words, it's not on for characters and objects).

    We did this because mip mapping all those textures was causing performance problems and pauses for many players. That, and we really think subjectively that the advantages of mip mapping are for the most part apparent and beneficial only on world geometry.

    That said, you seem to disagree. We'd love to hear some other feedback on this. Perhaps we could provide several options (for example, no mip mapping, mip mapping world only, mip mapping all textures) were there a demand.

    Let us know, and maybe Rowyl can look into it and let us know if such options are possible.

    -Brad

  • Corpse Dragging is instant argro in SoV zones?
    Ok, here's a bit more information.

    The idea is that there are some places where, with a good faction, characters can travel unharmed. Some of those places are very, very dangerous to those that are not aligned with the folks that live there. These will be major adventuring/hunting zones for those that don’t mind not being able to wander around free there and talk to the locals.

    We really like it that way, but setting things up like that leads to a simple problem. Folks that are hated by the natives can make assaults on them and have their 'friends' who are well liked by the locals simply drag their corpses to safety, right in front of the people they were trying to kill. There are other potential abuses of this situation that I won't describe…

    So, to maintain the risk of fighting in these difficult places, we had to have these mobs get upset when you drag their kills off. And, yes, rogues and invisible types will have to be a lot more careful doing their recoveries once they actually lift that corpse up on their shoulders and start to scamper away.

    You will not have a problem dragging your own corpse (at least not any more of a problem than you would normally have). So if you accidentally fall to your death and you are friendly with the natives, you will be able to drag your own corpse all over the zone with no additional problems. But then, you won't need a safe spot either…

    This will make corpse recovery a little bit more difficult in the few places where this rule is used. But we see this as a necessary and useful inclusion. We wanted to tell you about it before hand to help reduce the number of people surprised by this addition.
    --
    Yikes!

    Correction on my last post.

    Dragging a corpse will not make you more visible. So if the creature sees through invis, it will still see you dragging a corpse. If it does not, dragging a corpse will not increase your visibility to that creature.

    Sorry for the goof on that. Hopefully I caught it fast enough.
