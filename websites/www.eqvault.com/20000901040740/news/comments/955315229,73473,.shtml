Brad on International Shipping
- Aurum @ 2:20 PM PDT
EverQuest Producer Brad McQuaid addressed the high cost of international shipping of Kunark in a recent response to a post made on the Gameplay board. Here's the thread:

    Hi,

    All prices here will be in US. Destination is Australia.

    I can order the full priced box of RoK from www.dragon.ca for $37.95. The shipping and handling, Air Mail (7-21 days), costs $7.95. The last game I ordered from them took 7 days from shipping to delivery. This is a total price of $45.90.

    Purchasing "direct" from you via your agent will cost $16.95 plus $57.90 S&H for a total cost of $74.80.

    With this in mind:

    1. Is there a possibility that VI/Zomax could change its delivery method?

    2. If not, is it possible to cancel my order?

    3. Can you explain the disparity between these costs (I.e. Why Zomax are charging so much for delivery)?

    4. Did you ask the company to deliver before the 24th of April with delivery cost in mind, or as quickly as they possibly can sparing no expense?

    5. (Just for the record) Do Verant, Sony or any other company owned/operated by them have shares or holdings in Zomax or their chosen shipping company?

    I find it absolutely amazing that I can get 2 copies delivered from an independant reseller for $10 US more than 1 copy from the producer/manufacturer.

    Don't you?

    The intent was to give international players an option to get RoK quickly, preferably on or before the 24th, so they could play right away.

    In the past international players have had to wait quite some time to get EQ.

    We wanted to give them the option (buy directly, albeit at a high price due to the quick method of shipping, or wait for the boxes to get to the various countries).

    The prices are 'high' for two reasons:

    1. The shipping is fast so you get it on or around the 24th.

    2. We don't do a ton of international shipping and therefore don't have access to big discounts you might find at amazon.com or some such. We basically had to use dhl/ups type methods.

    I think we should have been a lot more clear about this, and I apologize for not explaining this stuff and our intent beforehand.

    -Brad
