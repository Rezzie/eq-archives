#15   Name: Valentinian ()   Date: 7/6 @ 13:47 PM PST
I'd personally love to see a RP server up and running. But as it stands now... it would prove most difficult.

They all are right in the fact that it comes down to the players, not the system to enforce RPing. and to what degree of RPing is involved.

There in lies a problem tho. As we see here.. we have atleast 2 different levels of RPing. Now who's to say who's RPing and who's "Just Playing the game"?

The upside to Pen and Paper or even MUD's is that rules are clearly stated and followed. The "GM's" of the game monitor and interact VERY closely to what's going on.

EQ is not so fortunate. The RP server would need the GM's to basically act as Demi-Gods and interact quite heavily and respond quickly to situations. As such, it would need a massive army of GM's to maintain the atmosphere of a RP server.

Somehow, I can't see Verant being willing to sink a ton of GM's, Guides, etc into just one server.

Now if stepping out of character came with some kind of reprisal from a GM or the gaming community (ie. Getting the boot) then it would be more feasable to carry out a RP server. You'd need very strict rules of what RPing is, what is tolerated, what is not. Names would have to be closely monitored, as would conversations, and actions.

As for PvP. That goes without saying, to be RP.. true RP... it would have to be PvP. A human Play is gunna beat the crap out of a DE SK on site if he can. It's part of his religion, his upbringing. He sure as hell woudn't group and hunt with him. and if he saw one running from a bear, he'd prolly trip the DE and laugh.

So, there's the problems as I see them.. the most basic of them.

I'd love to see a RP server, but it would take a great deal of resources, time, and dedication of all three major parties involved: The players, the GM's, and Verant.

The other problem is getting people to start anew. Those of us who do have High level characters would be most vexxed to leave them behind. If a /movelog command was opened up so that RPer's could move their characters from servers, to the RP server, it would also be helpful. But again, you'd need to verify that they ARE RPers and not Power leveling, Loot mongering, Kewl Doods. and if one snuck through, he/she would HAVE to be removed from the new enviroment. #16   Name: Maelyn Stark ()   Date: 7/6 @ 15:06 PM PST
A valiant goal, but one, I'm afraid will not happen on EQ. As many have pointed out, the way the game is set up just doesn't handle certain things to retain that atmosphere.

There also have been many, many pushes and drives to get a RP server. If my memory doesn't fail me, Fennin Ro (my home server) was supposed to be the Roleplay server.

Look at it now.

Also, I would like to point out that not everyone roleplays all the time. Especially for slow typers. In the heat of a battle, you just don't have time to type out what you could/would say. This has led to the rather unfortunately use of (some rather strange) abbreviations.

These abbreviations have migrated to outside combat as well. It seems to be the nature of the beast that people are lazy, and that is why you get the 'sow plz' or 'tp plz' messages. Laziness, pure and simple.

Though, there is an element of pen and paper games that you left out - the out of character banter that goes on between players sitting around the table. The (semi-)snide comments, the friendly jibing, the cheer over a good roll, or the collective moan over a bad turn in the game, the requests for people to 'pass the pizza', etc. To me, it just wouldn't be a good game without that banter. =)

Of course, that's just my view.

When I'm not in a hurry, or intent on doing something else, I roleplayed a lot in Norrath. You'ld be surprised at how some of the d3wdz respond. Some, of course, will blow you off, or make fun of you, but others, will attempt to RP on back. And for some, that's their first step into a different world.

Once, way back when, one time hunting outside and just inside of Crushbone, someone made not of the meditiation emote I frequently used. He commented upon it, and deviced a similar emote (well, he was a warrior, so it was a 'prep to do battle' and not meditate emote). Months later I met up with him while I was attempting to hound those vile goblins in their Citadel of Runny Eye. He noted my emote, and remembered me. I was surprised to find that he had since decked himself out with a whole suite of emotes, and was actually 'getting into the character'.

=)

I think I'm through with my ramble for now, just remember, be positive to others, have fun, and don't anger a hungry Ogre!
