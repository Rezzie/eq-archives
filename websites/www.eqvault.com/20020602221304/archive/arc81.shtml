GM Event on Povar
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:08 PM PDT
The following report was sent in by Ganzon:

This occured at 8/29/00, around 2AM EST.

-GM Event on Povar-

While hunting Sand Giants in Oasis, I came across two
named Sand Giants standing near the orc highway. They didn't attack onsight,
and just stood there for a few minutes. Reports that other named Sand Giants
appeared at the docks were confirmed, and after a moment Harziz Garnt, a
midget Sand Giant (about the size of a human) began to kill at the docks.

He had a partner who was about the size of a gnome casting wizard spells.
Reports of his brethren on a killing spree at the Orc Highway were also
confirmed, and that was when we got smart and decided to retreat to the first
pillar at the water to regroup. The Giants were killing off everyone,
disregarding race and religion...they had to be stopped. We formed assault
groups and hunted down Harziz Garnt, who, when attacked, called for his
gnome-sized friend to cast a devestating area of effect-rain spell in the
dead center of our mass of about 30 people. My party dove out of the assault
group in time to avoid being hit by the second and third rains, but most were
not that lucky.

We regrouped again at the first pillar and buffed up while
the Giants were enjoying themselves by killing weaker people. After everyone
was at full mana and full health, we went after Harziz again who had taken
the gypsies hostage with his gnome-sized friend. We pulled him away from the
gypsies and proceeded to beat him down, Fearing his friend so that we
wouldn't be hit with anymore devestating area spells. After a few minutes of
nuking and melee, we got him on the run, and eventually killed him. We had
very little time to celebrate because the other assault group was being
wrecked by the gnome Giant. He went down fairly easy though, once we got
enough melee people in there to interrupt his castings. The third giant to
die was one of the large ones, Nirziz Pire, who ran faster than a Shaman with
Spirit of Cheetah.

I myself didn't get to see the fourth one go down, or even
if he did, but I remember hearing his shout about how we "won the sands this
day."

/OOC As far as I know no one was able to loot anything, and none of the
Giants were part of a GM quest. This was all a big fun thing, and no one lost
exp for dying at the hands of the Giants.

Ganzon Actueras,
35th Shaman of The Sentinals.
Thank you.

Gordon Infobits: The Revenge
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:04 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn, aka Abashi (aka Top Secret Goverment Message Board Posting Machine), let loose another round of board posts on the Gameplay Board. Here's the scoop:

  • Thicken Mana Line
    [Quote from original post: Why not make the mana vials expendable items that recover a certain amount of mana when consumed? The higher up the mana vial spell is, the more mana is regained by drinking the vial. You can keep down the abuse of this item by making it so they cannot be placed in any containers. That and the high level ones will be VERY expensive to make (4 rubies for the highest level mana vial).]

    I'm sorry, but we've ruled out creating "mana batteries". The greatest extent that we can implement a mana battery is done with the modulation rod (which eats hitpoints and is lore) and with your friendly neighborhood necromancer.


  • Smokers Getting Damaged While Taking Breaks
    You could just turn your autoattack function on and back up against a zone wall. Anything coming after you would then most likely be in front of you, be auto-targeted, and attacked when it approached. The best suggestion I can offer is of course that you camp to take your breaks.


  • Disillusioned Enchanters
    [Quote from original post: Why are enchanters not given the ability to cast these very same race/creature illusions upon either the group as a whole, or individuals in a group? (You already HAVE the code for specific-case faction issues with the already in- game illusion series of spells)]

    I agree that the code exists, is in game, and would be easy to do. Our reasons for not implementing illusions on others via enchanters is that its not what we had in mind for them. It's part of the premise for the enchanter class.

    [Quote from original post: Are you explicitly saying that in order for an enchanter to cast any illusion spell on themselves/others that a faction adjustment MUST take place and that for this reason, Verant would not attempt to try to code a spell allowing an illusion change to any NPC model in a particular zone? Or would Verant consider allowing enchanters to create illusions with no faction adjustments?]

    Illusions always have faction adjustments. They adjust the "race" portion of the faction formula. The game/faction engine does not allow someone to appear as a different race without adopting the faction settings for that race. As such, special allowances have been made for every race that can be illusioned. Opening more races to this would require a massive revamp of the faction engine and how it interacts with illusions. That revamp would constitute a major change to what defines EverQuest. Furthermore, the diversity the various areas in the game would surely lead to some bugs that would be implemented in the course of the revamp. Given these reasons, a change along these lines does not have benefits that outweigh cost.

    [Quote from original post: Aside from faction issues, are you saying that by essentially allowing the 'minor illusion' spell to also include NPC models, in addition to the myriad of other object models in a particular zone, that this creates some sort of a problem? If so, could you please elaborate?]

    I think that I covered this one above. If I need to elaborate further, please let me know.


  • Harmony Upgrade
    [Quote from original post: Level 24 would be perfect for a harmony upgrade that could be castable indoors. I think this would greatly increase numbers in mid level dungeons, were crowed control is needed most.]

    Harmony is too powerful to extend to the indoors. We'd have to make it resistable and give a chance for the whole group of targets to come running in order to balance it.


  • More Hybrid Issues
    [Quote from original post: Abashi you said that hybrids spells are largely useless in a well balanced group http://boards.station.sony.com/everquest/Forum2/HTML/009260.html ]

    I just reread my post to that thread, and I believe that I indicated that the spells aren't useless even when in a group where the parnet class is present. I certainly didn't say that "hybrids spells are largely useless in a well-balanced group".


  • Rowl's Day Off: Bug Found!
    Oops :)

    Rowyl's off for a couple of days but I'll mention it to him when he gets back. I'd agree that that is pretty useless until we get the change made. Unless if course if you want to type the emote out every time. Sorry about that.


  • Control Item Farming
    [Quote from original post: I will use the FBSS(flowing black silk sash) for my example. Consider if that only ONE FBSS can be looted by each player on a server. Each account allows 8 players per server. That means you could only loot 8 FBSS's per account, then you would have to purchase any others you might want(for whatever reason) from other players.]

    This is much like the "Lore for Life" suggestion that was posed here several months ago, suggesting that once an item was in a characters possession, that they could not get another one, whether it be via drop, or trade, or possibly a combination of one or another. It's an idea that I like, however not something that's compatible with EverQuest's core mechanics. It might however be applicable to future products.


  • Can you riposte a riposte?
    [Quote from original post: This can be fun in an arena environment between two melee classes who use their 100% riposite chance disciplines at the same time.]

    Yeah, I was so worried about that little feature when it first came out I grabbed a tester and had it tested right there for fear it would crash the server :)


  • Velious Questions
    [Quote from original post: Velious Questions

    Your questions really aren't related to Velious, but are more along the lines of general gameplay questions.]

    [Quote from original post: 1) Will playes every be able to pick the handiness of their character? i.e. Left handed players
    2) Will anything be done to make Ogres look less goofy? A way to turn
    the butt scraching off would help, I think the best way would be a long grey beard, but somthing needs to be done ]

    [Quote from original post: 3) Will players be able to customize thier clothing? i.e. Dyes, or maybe an option for what clothing will show up and what doesn't, like
    the goofy looking bronze helm for Ogres ]

    To a certain extent, something along one of the lines you've suggested is planned for the future.

    [Quote from original post: 4) not related to Velious, but Next time there is an expantion that includes a new race, PLEASE take a poll on what new race you guys will make. Everyone I've talked to likes this Idea. ]

    It is very unlikely that new player races will be forthcoming in EverQuest.


  • Overlapping Mezes?
    The stacking of mez spells works as intended. They are not designed so that more powerful mez's overwrite weaker ones.


  • The Rathe In Distress
    [Quote from original post:From a player's perspective, a multiple Hate or Fear zone system would be just fantastic. A player would just select PlaneFearA or PlaneFearB and so forth when trying to zone in. This would not ruin the economy as the items are already no drop nor would it throw the armor rate off balance as it already has been off balance negatively for months. Bottom line - no matter how you implement it, either the plane armor drop rate has to increase - or the number of places where you can get it has to increase. Either duplicate Fear and Hate, implement new planes or increase drop rate.]

    We've addressed the "multiple copy" proposals before. In short, this is not something that we would implement. It would indeed mess up the economy, create unmanagable server load, and be inconsistant with our ideals for the world. There are more than enough areas in the game to support everyone who wishes to adventure at every level, and we're about to add an entire new continent.

    [Quote from original post:The guilds on the Rathe server favor a reservation system]

    That aside, I have to put my "reservation system disclaimer" in now. We do not support nor endorse, nor will we enforce any type of "reservation system" in use on the servers for the purpose of scheduling dragons, planes, other zones, or other encounters. Compliance with them is entirely voluntary on the part of the groups involved.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:58 PM PDT
Big day on the boards so far for Verant's Gordon Wrinn. Here's what he's posted today so far on the Gameplay Board:

  • Enchanter Questions
    [Quote from original post: Will Enchanters ever get the ability to Illusion others? I know this MAY raise some faction issues, but then again so do the Druids wolf illusions. Anyhow, At higher levels, Illusion other: (insertrace here) would make up for the lack of illuions ant the higher levels. After all Enchanters are the "Master of Illusions" heck, we even get called illusionists at high levels. Please let me know what u think of this idea. I am rather curious]

    There have been several posts as of late concerning other illusions. One limitation is that certain models are only available in certain zones, where other models are available globally. Illusions are for the most part restricted to zones in which the model exists. It has been suggested that we should allow enchanters to illusion to the "nearest animate entity" in the zone, however that would indeed present faction issues.

    Each illusion requires specific case-coding to handle the faction issues associated with each illusion. The number of different NPC body-types in the game would make that difficult, and in many cases impossible.


  • /guildmessage Suggestion
    Hey there, just dropping a note to let you know that we've seen this request *many* times here on the boards. It's something that we may be able to do at some point, but we really can't promise it as a coming feature.


  • Rangers
    [Quote from original post: 4. Spells givenn to Rangers are useless in groups and outside of groups do not allow Soloing exp giving mobs.]

    Out of your four points, I'd agree with all but #4. The spells aren't useless, and are only eclipsed when fighting in a group where there is a druid. Even then, the rangers mana can be used to do things like buff pets and help out between fights, so they aren't total invalidated.

    Also, as I've mentioned before, the soloability argument isn't indicative of any problem, as the class is not designed to solo. Finally, your other three points simply state the design, and are also not indicative of any problems.

    [Quote from original post: Archery is a Joke.]

    I disagree, especially after the double-damage upgrade above level 50.

    [Quote from original post: And yes we do less damage Dual Yielding than Warriors.]

    There are many weapons out there that can be used by both warriors and rangers. Ranger damage output is within a percentage point or two of a warrior wielding a weapon of the same quality.

    [Quote from original post: Do a who all Ranger 50-60 on Cazic thule with 2800 people on line and you will see 20-25 and who all Ranger 40 50 you see 20 more any day of the week. This should be telling you something.]

    Actually, people didn't start complaining about drops in ranger numbers until the "rangers suck" drive started shortly after Kunark was released. It is, in my opinion, the same thing that happened in regards to rogues complaining on the boards about how bad they were. I think that the community is starting to realize how valuable rogues are at this point, so the numbers will start rising again.

    Rangers might have a bit more of a difficult time ahead of them, as recovering from a "we suck" drive is very difficult. Remember that when a bunch of people get on the board and complain about how bad they feel their class is, other people read those posts and begin to believe that it's true. It's a bit of a self-fulfilling prophecy.


  • Ralls and PvP Issues
    The GM probably shouldn't have stated it in such absolute terms, but no, there are no plans to implement a move at present. That however does not equate to "There will be no move".

    The quotes posted by Nanuvis-RZ on the first page of this thread still apply. And I do apologize, but this is not an issue that I'll be able to give periodic updates on, as we've been shifting priorities a bit here.

    The PvP servers are still primarily PvE, like all of the other servers. PvP is there as an addition to the environment. As such, if we have to decide between changes that will improve things across all servers, or only on the PvP servers, we'll have to go with the one across all servers first.

    My recommendation would be that if you absolutly cannot stand playing on Rallos any longer, you should create a character on one of the other servers and play there. As long as the population on Rallos continues to be strong, we're not going to assign priority to drastically changing the server environment. I'm not trying to be short or gruff, but we are going to need some type of sign that drastic changes are indeed wanted on that server beyond what's on these boards to provide us with a business reason to assign it some priority.


  • Crashing Bug
    [Quote from original post: Anyone else having problems with the game crashing to their desktop from the character select screen? the first time it happened was when i tried zoning into WC,then i crashed,then i tried to enter norrath then it crashed again. Anyone else having this problem ?]

    There was a crash bug fixed in this patch that would cause people who had exited the game to crash upon trying to come back in, but that bug shouldn't have caused you to crash out of the game itself. We also included a bunch of code-optimizations in today's patch, so that might help out with some of the random crashes we've heard of.


  • Blacksmithing Question
    I have a note in to the designer responsible for tradeskills to find out if this is an oversight or by design. I hope to have word back later today or tomorrow. You might also consider posting about this on the tradeskills board, which that designer will visit regularly.

There was no balance issue or gameplay design that necessitated completing the key-quest twice.

    Though we certainly won't make all nodrop items soulbound, we do indeed now have a way to add additional value items that are awarded.

I mean, only say that if it is true, but give me some clue as to how close I was when I said most everthing in game (save Planar/Veeshans Peak) has been solved.]

  • Idea Suggestion

  • Linkdeath
    I appreciate the time that you took to write this up. Let me see if I can explain the current technology is as much detail.

    Pets can only exist in the world when their owner also exists in the world, meaning that once the owner disappears, so does the pet. At the same time, the character goes linkdead, that character cannot reenter the game until they are removed from the world.

    Once a character is removed from the world, the world has no knowledge of the existence of the character. As such, the world no longer processes data relative to the characater, since the character does not exist. That is why NORENT objects are removed as the character is being removed, since the world has no knowledge of the character once they are gone.

  • Discussion of the Week: Fun
    Let’s talk about “fun”.

    Last week I queried everyone’s thoughts regarding a “Discussion of the Week”. Though the number of different responses was almost as great as the number of posts, many of them dealt with fun, or with concepts related to fun.

    I think that it can generally be agreed that EQ’s customer base is very diverse, and with that diversity comes many different types of players. EQ strives to provide as many different avenues for “fun” as possible, leaving it up to the player to make decisions regarding what avenues they wish to pursue. Some find the social aspect of the game to be the most fun, while others have a good time by advancing in levels. Some measure it in items, while others prefer character development along the lines of mastering trade skills or languages. Still others want to personally witness as much as possible within game.

    Our goal with EverQuest is to entertain you for the greatest amount of time possible, and to do this we must provide activities that the players consider “fun” in nature. I’ve spoken many times about the concepts of risk and reward, but those concepts can be broken down to the perception of fun. Setting pure socialization aside, as it is one way of having “fun” that does not require a challenge, the goal is that “rewards” provide excitement, entertainment, etc. (cf. Fun), to the players that overcome challenges.

    Last week we discussed camping, how it impacts the game, and what the driving forces behind it are. I think that from the responses to that thread, we can agree that camping relates to the concept of “fun” in one way or another. Players either enjoy camping itself, or enjoy something that camping will provide, if not the process itself. The social gamer might enjoy camping because of the opportunities for socialization provided in the downtime between kills. The leveling gamer might enjoy the prospect of raising in level. The item gamer looks forward to the items. More indirectly, perhaps the exploring gamer doesn’t enjoy camping for experience or loot, but gets fun out of exploring new situations, for which they need the levels and items to do, without explicitly caring for those levels or items.

    The questions I’d like to pose are:

    What is it about EverQuest that entertains you? If you had to label yourself as a particular type of gamer, what would that type be? Though I’m sure that everyone has particular issues that they feel could use improvement, everyone either has fun in game, or foresees the prospect of having fun sometime in the future. For instance, there are players that feel that once they reach their particular goal (whether it is one of items or levels), they *then* can go have fun.

    If you are of the type that does not feel they are having fun now, and foresees fun once reaching a specific goal, what is that goal? What is it about that goal that makes you feel you can only have fun once you reach it? Internally, we refer to it as a “comfort-level”. What defines that comfort-level to you? What do you foresee will be “fun” once you’ve reached that comfort-level?

    When Kunark came out, we raised the level cap. If you define yourself as one who must achieve a comfort level prior to having “fun”, did the raising of the level limit also “raise the bar” of your comfort level, effectively putting you far below it? Or did you enjoy the level-cap increase? I ask because before the level cap was raised there were thousands of people who were out having “fun” in game. Once the cap was raised, the focus for these people quickly shifted back to the “must-level-fast” philosophy.

    Assuming that you have “fun” outside the tangible effects of levels and items, do you feel that “fun” is a just and suitable reward for a risk? Do you have fun outside of the scope of items and levels? Will you give some examples of where you’ve had fun without a tangible reward? While I understand that players who value only items and levels typically do not have fun outside of the acquisition of those rewards, I would imagine that most fall somewhere in between one player-type an another rather than directly in the middle of one of the ones I’ve defined.

    If you could make any change to the game, feasible or otherwise, to increase the amount of entertainment you get or fun you have in the game given your particular gaming style, what would that change be? Other thoughts? I’m looking forward to seeing discussions between the players on their various perceptions of fun.

    This should make for a pretty good discussion. Often here on the boards, we fail to get a good impression of the different types of gamers out there. It’s not unusual to see two posts on the opposite side of an issue related to “fun” claim to both represent the majority. I really doubt there is a “majority” for most issues in EverQuest, because as in life, very few things are black and white.

    "Concerning items in combinable containers, I think that I'm going to chat with the programmers Monday and see if they can make the containers eat any items in them upon logoff/Linkdeath. I think that there'd be no question of the intent of those bags then."

    After reviewing this post that I made earlier, I do agree that it appears to be a threat when viewed out-of-context, and understand the general reaction that followed. The point of the post was to respond to multiple posts from people earlier in the thread who had no idea that they shouldn't use combinable containers, particularly free bags given by NPCs to complete quests, for general item storage.

    I'd thought that the number of people using the bags for this purpose were the exception rather than the rule, an observation that was apparently mistaken given public response to my post.   • Cut EXP Rate
    [Quote from original post: Lets halve XP and Double Heal/Mana Regen It would allow you to take more mobs faster... that means more moving around in dungeons... less down time. more mobs dead. More play time... less down time. You would have to use your time a bit more wisely to make up for the exp cut. You would need to kill things a lot faster to keep the same rate of gain.]

    Unfortunately, though the idea looks simple at first glance, it's not that simple. Doubling mana regen rates would at least double the potential killing rate. That means that we'd have to increase spawn rates. At the same time, we'd have to halve loot drop rates for both coin, mundane, and special items. The doubled spawn rates and doubled killing rates would use more processing power and bandwidth on the servers, which isn't feasible from a cost-perspective.

    Finally, the suggested change would only bolster the claim that we "force" people to kill non-stop. I don't believe that is true currently, however might be were we to reduce the time available for social interaction within the game.

  • Gambling in EQ
    Though these are good ideas and we have given tremendous thought to them, we elected a while back not to pursue gambling within the game. Frankly, the biggest hurdle is a legal one. Since people are selling in-game items and platinum for real-world cash, it would allow someone to convert their gambling winnings to cash, and cash into a gambling medium. That would leave it open to being argued that gambling in EverQuest is gambling in the real world.

    Though we are leaving the option open for future products, and looking into the legal issues involved, it's not a possibility for EverQuest going forward.
