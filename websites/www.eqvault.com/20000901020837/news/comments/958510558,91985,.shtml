I'm going to apply the recent logical fallacies list to this arguement. Unfortunately, most of them have been used, which means that people are argueing for the sake of argueing, rather than actually viewing the topic for what it is.

Now what is funny is that most of these arguements have been issued by Gordon!

1. False Dilemma
"We can't just let everyone bind themselves"
FACT: Nobody is saying that melee players should be able to bind, only that they should have ready access to being able to be bound.

2. Arguement from ignorance
"The game has been out for over a year. In that time, melee characters have survived and prospered, even though the binding arrangements haven't changed."
FACT: Gordon, please. Your argueing that just because we haven't proved that warriors need to have better binding arrangements, that our premises must be false. It is a matter of convenience, not necessity. Nobody ever blamed the players at 2:34am because he was the only warrior in Qeynos at the time, we cursed Verant for not providing us with a quick way to be bound when nobody else is around.

4. Appeal to pity
"We are tired of having to deal with this topic"
FACT: Maybe it's because your finally running out of excuses.

5. Appeal to Consequences
"Melee characters will continue to thrive without implementing something such as this that would drastically change the pace of the game, game balance, class interdependency, and the penalty for death. "
FACT: Prove it. I dare you. Put it on the test server. Fact is that you are simply using that arguement to try and put an emotional one over on us.

6. Prejudicial Language
"Gordon is so stupid"
FACT: Ok, I'm coming out on Gordon's side with this one. I don't think he's stupid, your just flinging an insult. I think he's sometimes misinformed, perhaps brainwashed, and arrogant. But he has some smarts. Unfortunately arrogance + intelligence = ignorance.

7. Appeal to popularity
"70% of the players want this!"
FACT: Gordon is right when he says that they cannot just appeal to the popular opinion. Unfortunately this is the ONLY factual arguement he has on this subject, and it doesn't even deal with the subject itself"

11 and 12. Hasty Generalization and Unrepresentative sample.

I'm taking this separate because I think that polling 10% of the population is at least enough to get a general idea for a sample. While an arguement can be made that others might feel different, it is also just as likely that the poll is correct within +-10%

13. False Analogy
"If melee'ers can be bound at any time then melee'ers will become too powerful"
FACT: Your comparing convenience to power? What? The fact is that the only difference between an NPC binder and a player binder is cost and availability. The NPC is a backup that costs a lot of money (say 10-15plat). I believe that most people would be more inclined to have a player bind them for 1 plat then an NPC which costs 10x as much. In addition, the NPC would cost too much for low level characters so they would still require player assistence.

14. Slothful Induction
"An NPC binder would destroy game balance"
FACT: This has not been proven. You have not proven that the NPC binder would change the current binding arrangement. The only difference is who bound you.

15. Accident
"We want people to depend upon each other"
FACT: This only works if there are people to depend upon. This is a special case, there is a need for NPC binders at late night when fewer people are online and often you don't see a caster within 2 zones of a city for 20 minutes or more.

17. Coincidental Correlation
"Binding is an essential part of the caster's part in the player economy"
FACT: Binding MUST occur no matter what. Even you agree with that. Nobody would be able to adventure very far if you had to restart in your home town when you died. Now your trying to say that because binding must occur, and that casters can bind, that binding is an essential part of the player economy?

18. Complex Case
"Melee players shouldn't have any problem getting bound. You just need to interact more often"
