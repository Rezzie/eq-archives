    Server Populations and Economies
    [Quote: Subject: a few questions...

    (sorry for bad english :))

    I would like to know about a few issues...

    1)
    I'm not sure if this is for the Developer's Corner, well... . Could you please tell us what the average number of players is you think to be OK for a server. The point is that i am concerned this amount is quite high for the servers, and I got a feeling there are some bad effects developing from this crowding (don't want to see the servers at peak-time though, more than 2000 people, geez). Besides intensive camping with long waiting lists there are especially quite rude manners in the newbie zone of Kelethin regarding kill stealing.

    2)
    It seems to me that there is a constant inflation of equipment (and money too?) in the game. Example: weapons and armor from Crushbone can be found at NPC-vendors in Kelethin. Equipment from monster-killing drops in price generally. Could it be this is due to the fact that there is no effective item sink balancing the amount of things? Is this intended to be as it is? Or am I even wrong?

    3)
    What is your idea of the development of the server population? As far as I can see the level of the average character will constantly grow.     I'll just answer your questions by the number.

    1) Actually, 1,500 is a pretty low number of people on a server. I can't imagine that this was a peak time number. If a server were to have only 1,500 players on at peak times, then during off times it might be nearly impossible to find a group. Though crowding certainly wouldn't be an issue, when the server population gets too low there is a legitimate concern about under population. Though with only 1,500 players on finding more than 10% of them in one zone would be shocking. That's got to be a pretty rare occurrence (not the number of people in Greater Faydark, that's number doesn't surprise me if a server has 2,500 people on line at the time).

    With the addition of Velious (and in relation to your third question), I think 2,500 players on a server at peak times isn't too unreasonable.

    2) I'm not an expert on MUD or MMORG economies, but I'll tell you what I see. Yes, there will be a decrease in the value of items over time in EverQuest. Items don't generally leave the economy. This was a known side effect of the decision to not have items decay or get damaged. For the most part, people don't really enjoy doing maintenance on items. It can become a tedious part of gameplay. And for item decay to really have an effect on the economy, to have it effectively keep prices for items stable, it has to eat up a lot of items. This upsets people and makes gameplay less fun. So given the choice to either have items decay (by whatever method) to 'help' the economy or having them remain as relatively permanent things, the designers of EverQuest decided that relative permanency was the better choice.

    So the relative value of older items, ones that have had more time to become more common, will decrease as time passes. Debates have raged over whether this is a good thing or a bad thing, and they will continue to rage. Personally, I think it's a good thing, as an acceptable alternative to the other options.

    3) Yes, there is a growing population at the higher levels on mature servers. Similar to the point about item decay, the only way to prevent this 'problem' would be to have levels 'decay' by some means. That's also not really going to be popular with players…

    We've added content for the growing population at those levels. And that helps a lot.

    But if you start a new character on even the oldest of servers, you will notice that the newbie zones are not empty. The influx of new players has been steady. Players that have played for a while will start new characters. People will try playing on a different server to see what the atmosphere is like there. The newbie zones aren't empty at all.

    /Assist
    bago-ED wrote:

    [Quote: The assisted person does not need to have auto-attack on for /assist to work.

    Does this mean that if I am main assist and I am targeting a mob, anyone who assists me automatically attacks that mob? If so this will be a big problem.     Yes, this is how it works by default. If you /assist someone, their target becomes your target and you begin to auto-attack.

    If you don't want to have auto-attack start when you use /assist, you can turn that off in your eqclient.ini file. Just open the file with a text editor and change this line:

    AttackOnAssist=TRUE

    To read:

    AttackOnAssist=FALSE

    Do this and /assist will not turn on auto-attack.

    A Change To Trade Containers
    I'm sure we've all done it once or twice (or more). You start looking through your bags to see where that item that you've been carrying around for a month is hiding so that you can finally sell it. But instead of closing your tailoring kit, you combine it. Suddenly you've lost some stuff that you really wanted to keep.

    Well, since EverQuest opened we've been warning you that this was one of the risks that you took when using lightweight trade containers for their carrying capacity rather than just using them for making things.

    We've decided to make a rather dramatic change for the better when it comes to trade containers.

    With this upcoming patch, trade containers will no longer combine items that are not a valid and complete recipe.

    This means that if you accidentally hit combine, the container will not destroy your items. This also means that if you make an error and put the wrong item in the container while trying to cook something up, it won't destroy those items either. This will be a huge boon to craftspeople all across Norrath.

    But this removes the only drawback to using trade containers as backpacks. Their reduced weight and often larger carrying capacity made them more desirable as bags, with the risk that you might just lose something if you made a mistake.

    So, with the logical change in the way combinable containers work (I mean, really, how often does a sewing kit eat a halberd?), we are also going to make them less desirable for just carrying stuff around in. We will be increasing their weight rather significantly. Where many of these containers weighed under 1 pound, they will now weigh as much as 4 pounds for the largest sewing kit.

    Along with this we are making a change to tailor made backpacks. In case you didn't know (and I sure didn't), most tailor made packs have a built in weight reduction of 5%. We are raising that to 10%. We will also be displaying the weight reduction of all containers with the information shown on a right click. It seems that so few people knew about the weight reduction of tailor made items, even at Verant, that it seems that this bit of information should be shown to the players.

New DX Initialization Code
- Aurum @ 7:41 PM PST
A few Direct X issues were addressed by Brad McQuaid and Rowyl and the Developers Corner recently. The following excerpt is a complete copy of Brad's post:

    Yes, we did make some DX intitialization code changes that should enable us in the future to take advantage of newer 3D cards and their feature sets.

    There are a few problems associated with this code that are affecting some players. Addressing these issues and getting a new client up is a priority and we appreciate everyone's patience in the matter.

    -Brad
Another important post in this thread was from Rowyl:
    RE: new features available as a result of the new DX initialization code.

    Please note, these features only work with D3D, so don't try them if you are using Glide.

    32 Bit Color Rendering
    If your video card supports 32 Bit color 3D rendering then you can turn this on. Currently, the way to turn this on is via editing your eqclient.ini file. Look for the line that reads VideoModeBitsPerPixel=16 and change the 16 to a 32. Not only does this use 32 bit color rendering, but it utilizes 24 or 32 bit Z-Buffering (depending on your card) which improves the visual quality of many zones. Cards that I know will work are ones based on the nVidia TNT or Geforce chipsets, ATI Rage 128 and Radeon cards, and 3Dfx Voodoo 4 or 5 cards. There are others, you can check your video card manual or box to see if it lists 32bit color rendering as a feature. Cards that we know do not support 32bit color rendering are the Voodoo 1, 2, or 3, and nVidia Riva 128 based cards (like the STB Velocity 128 and others).

    DirectX Texture Compression
    This feature will work with any system or card. When enabled it will compress the textures at load time greatly reducing the memory needed to run without swapping to the hard disk. However, it will increase your load time, primarily between server select and character select. If you have a system with more then 128 MB of ram then you probably don't need to turn this on and suffer the longer load times. However, if you are running with 64 MB or less then this could help with your in game performance at the cost of a little extra load time. You turn this one on by editing your eqclient.ini and changing the line that reads UseD3DTextureCompression=FALSE to be TRUE instead. Also, note your video card does NOT need to support DXTC for this feature to work, however if it does you will gain a little more performance from it.
Thanks Skip!
