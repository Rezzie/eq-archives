#10   Name: BodyART (bodyart@dragonslave.com)   Date: 4/22 @ 6:42 AM PST
Anime? Hardly. I agree that a few of the pieces under the Anime feature has anime traits and stylings, but this last darkelf/ red haired dude pics doesn't qualify. Anime to me has a well balanced blend of the following attributes:

1) Exagerated sense of proportion. The human body does not have to have the same proportions of reality. This is most commonly seen as SD (super deformed) characters.
2) Trademark eyes. Anime eyes are typicly large, expressive, SD, and is one of the most recognized feature of anime artwork. This of course is not always mandatory, as some great anime TV series such Legends of Galactic Heroes does not employ 1) or 2)
3) Hair variants. Any possible pigment is a viable anime hair color.
4) Simplified styling. Classic Anime characters are not usually highly detailed or shaded. It is the emotion, action, and general layout of the design that makes it's mark, not the talent of a person to computer shade / color a picture.
4) Exagerated range of motion. Anime and action are synonomous. All these static pics are boring.

There are a few notable exceptions I have seen posted to the featured art section, but few, very few even qualify to be called ANIME. Anime-Inspired maybe, but not true blue Astroboy / kimba anime.

As for the quality of the rest of the artwork here, I have seen some amazing things. The current feature artist has a good deal of artistic merit. I have enjoyed most of the artwork she has displayed. Just please stop calling it anime.

Bodyart.
