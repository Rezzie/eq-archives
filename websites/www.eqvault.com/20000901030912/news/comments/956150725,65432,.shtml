the service. No /ooc and no /shout (only for help but that's another command).
Oh and the messageboards should fade after a while, there should be several
for specific things (like one near the weapon stall for selling a weapon
the people at the stall don't want).

encouraged, perhaps a little penalty if you do it elsewehere? nah).
A cool barkeep who knows rumours on quests (perhaps a GM, or an NPC who looks

suited to that person, this would be an incentive to go to the taverns
anyway).

3) Tells will take a long time to arrive, longer if you are farther away.

daggerfall).

Parties and camping:

4) Penalties for not being in a party? nah not really needed probably (as

are in one area, the less monsters (to make it a less attractive hunting place)

camping.

6) Heralds (who can shout, perhaps NPC, perhaps a guide/GM) that announce events

camping, and increases realism. There should be consequences if
you kinslay (faction), and bonuses if you kill your hated race.

http://www.stack.nl/~michield/eqmp2.html

Important! these penalties and bonuses should be switched of altogether in
three situations: in the arena, when /duel -ling and in bars
(roleplaying needs drunken barfights!!!)

9) When you die or zone, be a "ghost" (invisible, and you cant be hit or hit someone)
for a while, this cuts down a lot of problems. Also creatures should give up
a chase sooner if you are near the zone boundary (so you won't have to load zones

#11   Name: Cerra ()   Date: 4/19 @ 9:23 AM PST
Roleplaying is: Assuming and acting out a role different from your own that is consistant with the supplied environment. It's that simple.

Some ideas for a roleplaying server:

Have unseen guides give out bonus experience to people caught roleplaying outside a combat area (such as in a Tavern). This works in other games quite well. Even powergamers will roleplay if the best way to powergame IS to roleplay.

Have everyone in a group recieve the full experience from the creatures the group kills instead of just a split, and reduce the normal experience each creature gives when killed. This eliminates any benifit from soloing and encourages grouping (as it only gets easier when grouped and you get the same reward as if you soloed).

Set a maximum amount of experience that anyone can recieve from each mob. Once you have killed a goblin 100 times, you don't get exp for them anymore. Ideally the experience you get would decrease a bit each time you killed the same mob until you get zero. This encourages people to wander the world in search of new prey and discourages camping.

Make all magic or special items that are not available for purchase from a merchant LORE. This still allows you to trade them, but you can't sit and camp a bunch and then go sell them.

I have more ideas but it's time for a meeting (bleh)
