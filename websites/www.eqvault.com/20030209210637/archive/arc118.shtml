Smed on FTC Rules
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:26 PM PDT
John Smedley responded to another post regarding the FTC rules on the EQ Gameplay Board today. Here's one of the first posts followed by Smed's response:

    The lowdown on the FTC rule is...

    If you order goods by mail, phone, computer or fax (other than photo development, magazine subscriptions, seeds or plants), the Federal Trade Commission's Mail or Telephone Order Rule requires that the seller ship to you within the time promised or, if no time was stated, within 30 days.

    If the seller cannot ship within those times, the seller must send you a notice with a new shipping date and offer you the option of canceling your order and getting a refund, or accepting the new date. If you opt for the second deadline, but the seller can't meet it, you must be sent a notice requesting your signature to agree to yet a third date. If you don't return the notice, your order must be automatically canceled and your money refunded. The seller must issue the refund promptly--within seven days if you paid by check or money order and within one billing cycle if you charged your purchase.

    I guess Verant needs to learn that when it comes to violating consumer's rights...they're in our world now.

    -archtype

    I for one don't believe archtype doesn't have a right to be angry. In all honesty, I would probably be angry myself. We aren't running, hiding, or ducking any kind of responsibility in any way. We messed up and we have to make it right. It's as simple as that. Also, just because it's a "game" doesn't mean we don't have to deliver things on time.
    With all that said, I can tell you that we've been doing virtually nothing but rectifying this problem. Also, I'd like to point out that we have extended our refund policy.

    Archtype, do me a favor and email me all your info (including the tracking number Zomax gave you) and I'll be happy to check it out when I'm at work tomorrow morning.

    John Smedley
    President and CEO
    Verant Interactive, Inc.

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:35 PM PDT
Gordon made a few more posts since the first of today's regular Infobit post. Here's the newest posts:

  • A 'No Nerf' Server
    [Quote from original post: Why can't we have a server that does not have anything to with balance of power. This server will just add fun content. I mean if some class comes up with a good idea then they just add it. No more hearing that it will make things unbalance.]

    One of the foundations of massively multiplayer gaming, and gaming in general for that matter, is that there must be challenge in order for the game to be fun. In this game, game balance is required for there to be a challenge. Implementing suggestions without concern for game balance would lead to there being less "fun" in our opinion.

    Most of the people, even those who feel that there class is horrendously underpowered, would agree that if every suggestion, even every "good" suggestion, was implemented, that the game would be trivialized. In the end, we'd be constantly tuning things up, trying to reach a point where each class felt that they were powerful enough, yet when each individual class was enhanced, other classes would still feel that they get the short end of the stick.

    Though it's somewhat taking it to extremes, the end result would be a game where every NPC had 1 hitpoint. At that point, every class would finally be immensely powerful, yet the game would be awfully boring.

  • Spell Casting & Animation
    [Quote from original post: My character casts all his spells the same way (the same animation for casting). Since we have all these new emotes around, would it be possible to integrate that into the spells?]

    Funny that you should mention that. Keep an eye on the gestures made by some of the higher level casters :)

  • NPCs and Taunting
    Some NPCs aren't tauntable. However, given the fact that you play on Morell-Thule, I'm pretty certain that you haven't reached many of those yet.

    Taunt only makes the target hate you slightly more than it hates the next character on the list. It might be advantageous for the person you are trying to taunt the critter off of to back off a bit and let you get a bit of a head start before jumping back in.

Brad on Swaying Trees & Preformance Issues
[23 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:44 PM PDT
EQ Executive Producer Brad McQuaid produced the following post on the Gameplay Board regarding some RoK speed issues:

    Just to clear up this rumor:

    The trees swaying have NOTHING to do with framerate.

    Two things affect framerate: number of polygons drawn, and fill rate (the number of objects in front of other objects in front of other objects, all of which have to be drawn).

    Framerate goes down in a forest because of the additional polygons needed to respresent all the trees, as well as the trees behind the trees behind the trees having to be drawn.

    Anyway, there are three general ways to increase your framerate (starting from easiest to hardest):

    1. Adjust your clipping plane down under the display options section.

    2. Buy a faster 3D card.

    3. Upgrade your CPU/Computer

    The level of 3D sound can also affect overall framerate, depending on your system's configuration (which sound card, etc.) so you might want to experiment with those settings as well.

    Bottom line, Kunark pushes the EQ engine a lot harder, hence the better visuals. Even though this was perhaps a non-issue for you with the rest of Norrath, when you're on Kunark you might now need to adjust your display settings.

    -Brad

    ps. just to be very clear, the animating trees do not measurably affect your framerate.

    pps. framerate is how fast your screen is updated and has little to do with 'lag', which is generally another word for 'latency' which has to do with internet performance (timing issues, sending data between your client and our servers). hope that helps :)


Gordon on Kunark's Preformance
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 5:36 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn had the following to say about the preformance of Kunark hardware-size on the Gameplay Board:

    As Brad mentioned in another thread, the swaying trees really have nothing to do with the framerates that people are getting. What is probably more responsible is the fact that the zones are very large and that we allow you to push your clipping plane out quite far.
    The beautifully crafted landscapes of Kunark push the abilities of your graphics card and CPU further than the lands shipped with EQ proper. The outdoor zones are also densily populated with NPCs.

    Bringing your clipping plane further in should help the framerate issues. We are also looking into seeing if we can make any further performance tweaks, but the issue is primarily caused by there being so much more to see on Kunark.

    -Gordon

The Quest for Kunark, Part V
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:58 PM PDT
After Firiona Vie's kidnapping, Galeth Veredeth, Sir Jevik Isqual, Dagda
Icefury and Thubr Axebringer made their way back to Faydwer with an
Initiate of the Arisen, one of the Iksar involved with her abduction.
Taking the Maiden's Voyage, they landed in Butcherblock and began their
journey to deliver the prisoner to King Tearis Thex in Felwithe and to
bring him the terrible news.

Gathering a large crowd of curious onlookers, they arrived at Felwithe.
Upon entering the King's chambers, Galeth began to explain what happened.
Shocked and insulted by the presence of an Iksar in his chambers, the King
drew his sword and demanded an explanation. Galeth explained that the
Iksar was involved with the kidnapping of his daughter, Firiona Vie. The
King flew into a rage and slew the Iksar on the spot, his mind clouded by
grief and anger. Not wishing to further anger the King, Galeth told him of
how the Iksar known as Danak Dhorentath opened a portal and taking Firiona
Vie by surprise, pulled her through.

The King charged Galeth with returning to the new lands to build a
stronghold there to spearhead her rescue. The King gave Galeth two years
to redeem himself in his eyes and the eyes of his nation by returning the
kidnapped Firiona Vie to him. As a final punishment for his failure, he
stripped Galeth Veredeth of his knighthood. Galeth returned immediately
back to the elven stronghold, now named Firiona Vie in honor of the
Princess, to begin his task.

    Since nothing that regens mana will help bards, and they only get 1pt/tick back, it takes a LONG time for them to recover from singing this song.

    We've recently acquired the ability to place a song-specific recast time on the songs. We're considering replacing the mana requirement on the song with a 15 minute re-sing time.

    Thoughts?

    -Gordon

    Interesting. How much mana would be recovered in 15 minutes though?

    It currently takes a bard 80 minutes to recover the amount of mana required to sing DDD again. Basically, the proposed change would pretty much help everyone. The only people who might dislike it are the ones who have enough mana to sing DDD twice in a row. They wouldn't be able to do this if we make the change.

    However, if we make the change everyone would be able to sing it 4 times per hour, which is more than anyone can sing it now.

    First, is the intent to make DDD a "lay hands"/Discipline you can use every 15 minutes? Will this change require the bard to have the song memorized for 15 minutes before it becomes available the first time?

That's why we are making this an option.

    Second, with this, will we also see a change that will allow bards to regain mana faster through mana song/clarity?

    No. Per the above, we'd still be using the mana as a balance mechanism for a very powerful song.

    Alrighty... let's sum it up for all the bards to comment on=) Are you willing to accept a 15 minute recycle time (that means it will have to be memmed for 15 minutes before you can use it) on DDD in exchange for having it use 0 mana?

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:29 PM PDT
Gordon responded to a few more board posts late this evening on the Gameplay Board:

  • Veeshan's Peak
    Just for a bit of clarification, back when the planes were brought online, John Smedley commented that people just don't die in planes, they die hard. Veeshan's Peak makes the planes look easy. There exists a non-negligible chance that not only will you die there, but you might not be able to raise the necessary forces to allow recovery of your corpse. It is *very* dangerous.

    In order to avoid any appearance of impropriety in the zone, as sometimes follows when we've assisted in corpse recoveries in other zones due to claimed bug-deaths, we're not going to risk that for Veeshan's Peak. Veeshan's Peak is incredibly dangerous, and in order to guarantee that everyone is operating under the same set of conditions, we've elected to make the zone "enter at your own risk".

    That said, there are no known bugs in the zone that would cause the death of a character, and it was tested extensively in the Ruins of Kunark beta. We will certainly investigate and eliminate any bugs that are reported, but we won't be reimbursing items or experience, nor will we be moving corpses or people within the zone.

  • Sharkjaw Baby

    We've postponed the server split for a bit as we didn't want to go taking down the servers for a while. When the split is being done, both the source and destination servers need to be offline. We'll probably do it by the end of this week.

    I agree, unfortunately phone lines and personnel are finite. There are just a bunch of people trying to get through to both places. This should clear up by the end of this week. We appreciate your patience.

  • Split Servers
    The split process is a bit more complicated. Not only do we have to estimate the number of people on at peak, but we have to come up with a figure for how many people executing the /movelog command are actually peak players. That's why we open registration during peak hours, but it never stops a non-negligable percentage from off-peak from logging in to run the command.

    It also doesn't take into account the number of people who have more than one account who'll run it for all of them. I know a few people who have upwards of 4 accounts, who only play one at a time. Of course I know two people who have 4 accounts and play all four at once too :)

    There's a bit of trial and error involved. When we did the first split we took less people than we are taking with these. We now figure that we can safely move more people without risk of overcrowding the destination servers. We'll use data obtained in this process to further refine it for any future splits.

    The optional patch is used to patch new content that everyone will have access to. The "Kunark patch" button is used to patch content that only kunark-authorized players can access. The button that you press will depend on where the new zone is. For instance, when we launch The Hole, it will be patched via the optional patch.

    We aren't anti-kite per se. We are however against situations where characters can reap rewards substantially in excess of the risk associated with them.

accessed through having the appropriate "key", and inserting it (or
using it) with the appropriate "firepot" in a Norrathian city, to be
instantly transported to another city of Norrath, without the need of
spell, Wizard or Druid (shudder!).

Knowing that most ancient magic was not related to the evils that plague
Norrath, I have long discounted the likelihood that such devices, if
they existed, were for evil races' exclusive use. Evil races are prone
to destruction, rarely creativity of any sort! However, it has come to
my attention, that such teleportation devices do, in fact exist, and can
be utilized by all races. However, their utility is, at this stage of
my investigation, limited to travel from one hub point to twelve (12) of
the cities of Norrath.

This hub point is deep under the Timorous Deep, located at -12260,
+4365, -277. It came to my attention, that the top of a classic Combine
Era Wizard Spire, with its familiar four tines, was observable above the
waves in this depth of Ocean. If you swim down directly between the
four tines, you will find a square opening on the ocean floor.
Descending, you will find yourself suddenly in air, in a square room,
with three firepots along each wall. In the center burns a magic fire
of blue, such as can be seen at certain ancient Elvish and Combine
towers (e.g. Oasis). Note that the descent from the surface is one way,
and I was unable to find any way to leave the room by normal means. No
monster nor person disturbed my researches that followed.

Binding myself to the locale, I commenced to mediate on the twelve
firepots. It was immediately apparent that the style of each was
different, and each was familiar to a particular culture of Norrath. I
proceeded to test my hypothesis, and touching the rust-colored low
firepot, similar to many in the city of my youth, found myself, being
dusted off by a very surprised guard (my second cousin, twice removed),
in Northern Felwithe. Without answering my startled cousin's queries
(he knows I tend to gate around hastily...), I immediately gated back.
I tested all twelve pots, and here is the key of their wisdom:

North Left:(fire on floor)Grobb (entrance)
North Center:(6 side gray)Ogguk (entrance)
North Right:(6 side tall)Erudin (near dock)

East Left:(6 side gray)Neriak Commons (entrance)
East Center:(4 side wood)Greater Faydark (under Kelethin)
East Right:(6 side rust)Northern Felwithe (entrance)

South Left:(Brown box)Halas(near dock)
South Center:(Dark with blue)East Freeport (on dock)
South Right:(tall gold vase)Cabilis West (entrance)

West Left:(White marble)South Kaladim (entrance)
West Center:(Lava Lamp)Akannon (entrance)
West Right:(torch stand)Rivervale (entrance)

It is to be noted that "the Black Hole", Qeynos, and Highpass, are not
represented here.

An intriguing question, beyond the origin of these firepots and their
creator's identity, is: "Are there other pots out there that bring you
to The Timorous Deep?"

Respectfully submitted,

Mandos Turambar, Wizard Peregrinator of the 48th Degree
Treasurer of the First Chapterhouse of the Seekers of Lore (Tarew Marr)

Couple Kunark Issues
[17 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:36 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn just posted the following post on the Gameplay Board:

    There's a couple of issues that have several threads running behind them. It might be easier for me to address them at one time:
    Spirit of Scale:

    We'll be making this spell group-only (e.g. must be grouped with target) shortly. This should alleviate people's concerns about it being used offensivly to harm other players.

    Backstabbing Pets:

    Up until the level 50+ pet spells, all pets were based off of warriors. We now have the ability to base pets off of other classes, and that's what's been done here. There are now rogue pets, wizard pets, and monk pets, which represent additional abilities for the pet-users above 50th level.

    Rogues need not worry that a backstabbing pet somehow reduces the desirability of their class. First, the pet does not have the backstab skill that a rogue of that level would have, so they won't hit as often nor will they do as much damage. Furthermore, they don't have all of the stat bonuses that increase the rogues effectiveness. Certainly they can be buffed up, but they'll never come close to having a real rogue. Finally, rogue pets will never have the ability to double-backstab as rogues do.

    Rogue pets won't be picking locks either, and there are quite a few places to do that in Kunark.

    -Gordon
