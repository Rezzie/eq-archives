A lot was talked about in this edition of the Absor Infobits:

  • Scam Alert
    It seems that once again someone is trying to obtain EverQuest passwords through the use of a backdoor program. Please, if you receive an e-mail claiming to be an EQ2 trailer, do not open the attached file. Just delete it. Here is a copy of the text from the e-mail in question:

    “cool, you play everquest? everquest is such a cool game, its so addicting =)
    so what server is your char on and whats its name? Im on Fennin Ro and my
    char is named tersik. Send me a tell if your on that server. Did you hear
    that there gunna release an EQ2? I found the trailer for EQ2 on the net. If
    you wana check it out i sent it to you. whats your race and class and what
    lvl are you? ight well i got to go so ill talk to you later.”

    So, once again, please do not open or save any EQ2 trailers, because there is no such thing as an EQ2 trailer. It is a scam in attempt to steal access to your EverQuest account.

    Thanks to Catalyst from Everlore for bringing this to our attention.

  • More Necro Issues
    Tamria wrote:

    [Quote from original post: ”I'd like Absor to answer two things:
    1). Are you still reading this thread? heh
    2). Please clear up the confusion regarding why ubermobs resist lifetaps. Some thing it's purely MR related and others (myself included) think it's because ubermobs are tagged as 'untappable' (much like some mobs are tagged 'unmezable', only they give you the courtesy of telling you this when you try and cast on them...).”]

    Still reading. And I’ve stated the way I understand it, Lifetaps are resisted as magic, not on some special resist. And there is no Lifetap flag. But I’ll double check that for you.

  • Details on Tomorrow's Patch
    We’ve got a lot of changes planned for the next patch. Changes for hybrids, a change to the way that movement spells work (specifically Root), and a bug fix for Lure spells (among many other things). First and foremost I want to explain the fix to Lures.

    Right now (before the patch), creatures that should not get a save versus Lure spells are getting saves based on their levels. This has been broken for a while. We fixed it on test before Christmas, but this is the first patch since then, and the fix should go live with the patch. Basically, Lures will become less resisted after the patch.

    The changes to hybrid disciplines and powers are as follows. Hopefully these will be the last.

    1) Resistant Discipline will last five minutes instead of one minute.
    2) Holyforge Discipline will lasts five minutes instead of two minutes.
    3) Sanctification Discipline will last fifteen seconds instead of ten seconds
    4) Lay on Hands will have increased healing for each level after forty of the paladin. After forty it will do approximately twenty points per level more (for levels 41-60).
    5) Normal Harm Touches will save as all or nothing. There will be no partial resists.
    6) Harm Touch will increase ten points per level for levels one through forty. From levels forty-one to sixty it will increase thirty points per level instead of twenty
    7) The Harm Touch discipline will increase Harm Touch damage by fifty percent instead of twenty-five percent
    8) Weaponshield discipline will last twenty seconds instead of fifteen
    9) Leechcurse discipline will last twenty seconds instead of fifteen
    10) Deftdance will have its duration increased from ten to fifteen seconds
    11) Puretone will have duration increased from two to four minutes
    12) Hybrids will no longer reset weapon timers after casting a spell (further explanation below)
    13) Trueshot discipline damage calculations will be changed (further explanation below)

    We will also be giving Hybrids a new spell at level fifty.

    Now for the further explanation:

    Trueshot damage was messed up. Basically it was giving low damage bows too much damage and high damage bows too little damage. We are making the damage more in line with the base damage of the bow. This will reduce the damage of the discipline for some and increase it for others, depending on the type of bow you were using.

    As for the timer/casting thing… As it is now, casting a spell while using a weapon will cause your weapon swings to be delayed by the casting time of the spell. So, making up numbers here, lets assume you would swing your weapon every ten seconds. If you were to cast a five second casting time spell directly after a weapon swing, then your next swing would occur fifteen seconds after the last one (adding the five seconds for the spell). For hybrids, this will no longer happen. So if your weapon takes ten seconds to swing, spell casting will no longer increase that swing time.

    Movement Rate Changes:

    We have made some changes to the way movement enhancement/reduction works. Essentially, Root and Snare are now completely different spells. This means that Root will suppress snare, but it will not overwrite it. That's the primary change. But there is also a side effect to this change.

    Bard songs will no longer break root. Root will also suppress songs that enhance movement.

    We are aware that this will be seen as a 'nerf'. However, I'm pretty sure that most of you understood that bard songs were never intended to break root, and this is, in fact, essentially a bug fix.

  • Shaman Talk
    How about a 'hang in there'?

    Changes are not made over night. We're looking at a lot of issues. Your concerns have not been ignored.

  • Hate List Suggestion
    I'm here. I'll ask about this.

  • Defoliation - Necro Spell & Plane of Growth
    Well, in the case of discussing what are and are not plants, there are two things to consider. The first is, could it be seen as a plant by some use of the word plant (as the discussion about fungi does), and also whether or not it’s a good idea for the game to consider such things plants.

    Obviously if a treant is a plant on Norrath, it’s probably still a plant on the Plain of Growth. But we also have to consider the gameplay repercussions of allowing them to be considered plants on that plane. It’s easy enough to justify not having Defoliation work on them from a roleplaying standpoint (they are at the heart of Tunare, and are therefore more than mere plants). The question there is, would allowing that spell to work on them be too much from a gameplay standpoint? I don’t know the answer to that. I’ll ask.

    As for fungus… I hear that some fungus are affected by defoliation and some are not. We’ll probably look into that.

New Nercomancers on Raids Thread
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:18 PM PST
Alan summed up where things are at regarding the intense Necro discussuions taking place on the Gameplay Board:

    The old threads were getting a bit nasty and were wandering. Let's not have that happen here.

    Let me sum up my thoughts on this whole thing.

    Necromancers are just fine, in all aspects of the game, with one possible exception.

    On raids they can find a lot to do up to and after the encounter with the boss mob. But in that encounter some necromancers don't feel that they have enough to do, or feel that what they can do isn't what they want to be doing.

    In this thread I would like to keep to one narrow focus. What things could be done to give necromancers more to do in that one encounter, without improving their power in other situations?

    It's a very narrow question, and a very hard one to answer. Here are the ideas that I've seen that won't work, or won't happen:

    DoT stacking: We're probably not going to allow more DoTs to stack than already do. And this would have an impact far beyond the scope here, it would increase the power of necromancers across the board.

    Unresistable Lifetap: Probably never going to happen. I understand the whole concept of Lifetap to power mana transfer. I'm just telling you with a pretty firm tone, that it's really not likely to happen.

    Mana Transfer: The rate will probably not be improved. As I've said before, currently necromancers can convert 1 hit point into 1 mana. Right now they can give that 1 mana to a cleric (as an example) for the cost of about 2.5 mana. The cleric can convert that mana into about 2 hit points. That's the right ratio, really. There shouldn't be a time when the amount that can be returned is higher than the amount that is given out.

    Poofing: with the next patch, this should be pretty much fixed… let me copy one of my other posts:

    "Ok, this whole thing was Alan getting confused.

    There was special code put in several months ago that prevented nearly any possibility of a corpse poofing if the NPC is over 54th level. I was right there.

    But recently, a few weeks ago, we added some code to deal with a unique NPC situation (that I'm not going to go into…) that might have been causing corpses to poof is the kill was assigned to a Link Dead PC over 54th level. This will be changed with the next patch.

    I was confused and thought that this change was already in.

    I'm sorry about the confusion.

    Alan"

    In any case, let's leave this one off the list until we see how things go post-patch. I have noticed, though, that the only complaint I've seen was about Ragefire… I wouldn't be worried about poofing due to DoTs anymore. At least try it out.


    Issues that we can discuss:

    Ignite Bones: I'm willing to suggest some change to remove the skeleton. I think to do that we need to have something that does the same sort of thing, but I don't know what that is. This spell will probably never be changed into a standard direct damage spell. I like some of the ideas (like changing the tinting on the target), but unfortunately most of them don't seem possible technically. I'll keep thinking up ideas. Post any you have here.

    Group Feign Death: I haven't mentioned this idea to the team yet. I suppose that there might be a lot of reasons not to implement this idea…

    Making pets immune to fear: This probably won't happen. Then we'd have to do the same for magicians (not an inherently bad thing, but it has broad repercussions), and there's PvP to consider. But I think I have some interesting ideas along these lines that might work out. I want to here other ideas along these lines.

    Possessing the pet: that's a cute as heck idea (maybe 'cute' is the wrong word…). Another one that I'll mention to the team. But keep in mind that I'm not really looking for totally new stuff. I'm not advocating that we start making up new spells and whole new concepts. It would be hard to justify spending a ton of time making new concepts at this point. But it's certainly interesting…

    Essence Emerald: I'm kind of in favor of using this device as a way of changing things. It's a very expensive casting cost… For example I kind of liked the idea of allowing the mana transfer spell to be usable on others, considering the cost.

    Unrelated Issues (meaning that I'm mentioning them here so that you don't have to bring them up…) :

    I'm aware that Track Corpse doesn't work. I'm going to ask about that.

    The epic weapon. It's not really related to this anyhow, and I am already very aware how you all feel about it.

    Defoliate: If someone wants to start a new thread about which things they think should be affected by this spell, go for it. But it's not part of the focus of this thread.

    I may have missed some…

    Keep in mind that I understand that many of you are not happy with a support role on raids. I'm fairly convinced that it is perfectly fine for a necromancer to act in that role during the final battle of the raid. But I'm open to ideas (barring the ones that I've already vetoed) that would make them more aggressive at that one final battle.

    Alan

All About Downtime
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:57 PM PST
Brad started a new thread on everyone's favorite EQ experience, server downtime. Here's his post directly ripped from the Gameplay Board:

    I'm bumping a couple of older threads in a sense, because I'd like more discussion and brainstorming on downtime in MMORPGs in general and EQ specifically. The following are three posts I made that might have been missed because the threads became pretty large.

    The first deals with why downtime is used as a penalty for death and inefficient grouping, the second deals with travel, and the third talks about why downtime has nothing to do with us 'making more money.'.

    Anyway, let's see if we jumpstart this discussion again... I'd love to see this debated here and elsewhere.

    ---------

    The fundamental advancement mechanism in MMORPGs is Time Invested; therefore, Time Lost needs to be the fundamental death penalty if the principle of risk vs. reward is to be preserved.

    The time lost can be in the form of experience loss, an experience debt (a cool idea, btw), items lost (providing it takes time to acquire items), corpse retrievals, etc.

    As a corollary, being efficient in terms of time spent is the quickest way to advance (assuming advancing quickly is your goal, of course). Downtime, which is likewise often debated and criticized, is also a penalty based on the Time Invested/Time Lost principle. Minimizing or even eliminating downtime using intelligent tactics in a well-balanced group is an efficient time investment. Failing to do so incurs a Time Lost penalty, though probably not as severe as dying.

    -Brad

    ------

    Seriously, as I posted recently, downtime is most often used as a penalty (for death, inefficient grouping, etc.). A challenge within the game, therefore, is to be efficient and to minimize downtime.

    This isn't as applicable to travel (well, unless you consider teleportation spells), but please realize that there is a balance we've tried to achieve between tedium and losing how big a world feels and the sense of accomplishment from travel.

    This probably opens up a new area of discussion and debate, that being how does one create a virtual world in an MMOG that is large, where exploration is important, where players feel accomplished after traveling great distances, yet isn't tedious when it comes to frequent traveling....

    In a recent discussion an example came up: Frodo traveling to Mount Doom was exciting and perilous the first time, but if he had to go there over and over again, it would get tedious. So, is it possible to set up a system such that the fun and accomplishment of traveling to that far away, exotic land is maintained, yet repeated travel isn't tedious?

    I think we got closer to this goal with the way the dragon teleportation works in Velious...

    Anyway, just some food for thought.

    -Brad

    -------

    Would you guys stop with the 'downtime is there to keep people playing to make VI more $'... sheesh

    The way we keep people playing is by making a fun and challenging game that people feel compelled to play... by focusing on character development, an immersive and interesting world, and by creating an environment where people can easily develop online relationships.

    Downtime is there as a penalty (please read my other posts). It's not supposed to be fun, and the challenge is to avoid it.

    If people thought there was too much downtime and that the majority of their experience playing wasn't any fun, they'd leave. It wouldn't KEEP people playing and make us money, rather it would have the OPPOSITE effect.

    Some of you might find this hard to believe, but the EQ teams are a bunch of gamers who want to make a great game. We NEVER talk about money and profits and revenue at tuning meetings. We talk about what would make the game better, or what needs to be addressed to fix balance issues, etc.

    In addition, there are no corporate suits breathing down our necks, pushing profits and such. That didn't happen when we were part of 989, nor did it happen as Verant, and it's not happening at SOE.

    Why? Well, most good game companies know to leave their creative people alone as long as they're making great and profitable games. And guess what? EQ continues to be a huge success.

    As a final note, just to really illuminate how ludicrous the 'downtime = more bucks for VI' argument is, let's talk a bit about bandwidth. It's really, really expensive. Were we purely out to make the maximum dollars possible, we'd scheme and plan to make a game where you hardly logged on at all, but still paid us ten bucks a month. That's right: the most profit comes from a subscriber who plays only occasionally. The guys who play crazy hours every day cost us more due to bandwidth used.

    That's right, casual gamers, we make the most money off of you! *evil cackle*

    -Brad
