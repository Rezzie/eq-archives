To post #2 - Your post is what I would expect that Abashi mutters to himself each time he tries to compose a civilized response to questions asked on EQ's message boards.

This was my first attempt at posting a reply to a news item here on EQ Vault, and I'm thrilled to know I could both get at least one supporter and get under at least two thin skins out there.

I guess I will have to assume innocence until guilt is proven - and regard poster #'s 2 and 6 as folks who have never whined or complained about anything Verant has ever done to alter the game world of EQ. In which case I would be more than inclined to believe that they are being covertly given free subscriptions to EQ by Verant in exchange for their strategic placement of pro-Verant messages across the internet. Am I joking? Maybe it is as much of a joke as post #6 was - so take it for what you will.

Sure - the game was advertised as an ever-changing world. To some degree this is a good thing. I have no clue what the Vision (tm) is supposed to be, although Abashi specifically makes a reference in his post to a document that COULD be posted for viewing, but won't be.

A lot of changes have been made, both for the positive and for the negative in terms of what could be considered as "what the majority wants", and Abashi has already said to a great extent that player majority will never override the Vision (tm). If EQ can hold 200,000+ subscribers from now on through infinity, we will see how this statement weathers the test of time and better games.

My original statement was a bash against Verant based upon their reaction time to remedies in the game environment. If a GOOD player spends hours and hours of play time finding a number of ways to use the rules LEGITIMATELY (I stress this word again) - rules pertaining to the use of items, abilities, interaction with NPCs, etc. - to succeed at a rate unexpected by Verant, then the dedicated player isn't rewarded for their efforts. Instead, the playing field is "levelled", or "enhanced", by eliminating these strategies, and in more cases than not, they are eliminated due to a response fom a faction of the player base jealous of what seems to them to be a meteoric rise they themselves can't live up to. These are some of the folks that puff up Verant by telling them what a great job they've done. These nerfs are handled in quite the expedient manner, and two of the BIG exceptions to this expediency were with the ability of FEIGN DEATH, and the practice of KITING. And it becomes clearer to me that the reason these "fixes" took so long was because it took them a heck of a lot of behind-the-scenes programming to do away with, or reduce the effectiveness of, these skills.

On the other hand, consider Verant's reaction time to correct major game flaws when they are presented to their attention. Gee, what examples could I use here? Ok, how about ALCHEMY? They themselves have admitted they dropped the ball on this one. How about a few ROGUE SKILLS? It only took a dozen months or so to get poisons working, and only now do we find rogues in places like Befallen working on their pick locks skill, since it now might actually have a use.

I would be the first to admit Verant has done some very good things to the game since it's release. However, I've also seen some sloppy work as well - dropping items into the game and immediately taking them back out or nerfing them (a la paladin helm), putting half-completed quests into the game (no reasonable excuse exists for this lack of judgement), and on and on.

A player base of 200,000 is going to push the limits of the rules and find advantages of loopholes. Verant seems to be more eager to conquer every loophole than to fix legitimate bugs in the game, and sometimes it seems they are doing it with spite against the more clever players.

Just an opinion, but it's mine, and if you don't agree with it i don't care, because that's YOUR opinion. :)
