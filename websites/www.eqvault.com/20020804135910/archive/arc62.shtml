Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:55 PM PST
This is today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • "We're locking threads..."
    Posts that are monopolizing the front page with a particular issue are being locked. That's clearly defined as against the rules in the board FAQ. Chances are that if an issue is that important it's already been addressed a number of times. The mage pets issue that's currently hot falls under this definition. We've announced exactly what changes were made, and no changes other than what we've told you have been made. We've continued "looking into" the issue over the past several days and can find absolutly nothing to suggest that any other changes have occurred. If something changes we'll let you know, but until then the issue is closed.

    Posts that are rude or antagonistic are being locked. See the FAQ.

    Posts asking why threads are being locked are being locked. Posting threads of this nature is essentially spamming the boards, so please don't do it.

    Thank you for your cooperation,

    -Gordon

  • Pets
    I did some testing, specifically with 49th level necro and mage pets, and compared them to my results from early this year. Both pets were naked, no buffs, and both had similar results to what occurred earlier this year with the same tests.

    I'm not sure what I can say to put everyone's concerns to rest.

  • Verant Ignoring Reasonable Requests?
    This issue has come up a number of times and I've explained our position. We do not do ad hoc moves between servers.

    The sheer number of requests along this line that would come in would keep a staff three times our size busy 24 hours per day. Remember that there are over 300K people playing EverQuest, and number of characters are well into the millions.

    Now it has been suggested that we should charge for the service, but we feel that server communities are important, and that characters should have to live with their reputations.

    The short of it is that it is not economically feasible to do ad hoc moves, and even if we were to set it up so that it was, we'd still be dealing with the issue of the importance of server communities.

  • Endless Threads on Same Subject
    Endless new threads on the same topic: Why they hurt everyone...

    It's simple. People go looking for "official" posts on an issue, and instead of having to look at two or three, they have to filter through dozens. The ones with the official replies are missed and end up down on page 10, while post after post gets bumped to the front page asking "Why are you ignoring this". Please, do everyone a favor and use the search tool to look and see if there's a post on your issue that may have been answered already. I've personally answered 3 of these pet posts today, and read at least half a dozen threads asking why we haven't responded to the issue since then.

  • More Q&A
    [Quote from original post: Did you say that DEX was much lower for pets now ? ]

    No. The Dex of pets is not lower than it ever was. What is lower is their proc percentage chance based off of that dex. It used to roll of the NPC table and now rolls of a PC table.

    [Quote from original post: Does DEX affect the fact that an Attack lands, and also affects damage ? ]

    No. It does not.

    [Quote from original post: Did you also lower AGI on pets now ? ]

    We have not lowered any pet stats.

    [Quote from original post: Now... I have the same example in about 4 or 5 threads of a battle with a blue croc in other posts. My pet was getting hit for more damage, and was hitting less often for less damage. Coincidence ? How can 2 hours of trying the same thing be coinsidence ? I played in Oasis for a few hours prior patch and it wasn't that way. ]

    You aren't dealing with constants when fighting things in Oasis. NPCs have level ranges. Pets have level ranges. Hit and miss percentages have ranges. Damage dealt and damage taken have ranges. A combination of good or bad rolls on one side or another can make it appear that things have changed in one way or another, but they haven't.

    I've been back and forth with several programmers over the past two weeks about this, in addition to running my own tests and having them run theirs. The only determination that we've made is that aside from the procing weapon issue, which I think people understand as a bug fix at this point, is that they have not changed.

    Perhaps the best way I can "prove" to everyone that they haven't changed is by pointing to the lack of people with concrete figures here. When we *really* change something, the board is flooded with a certain type of player making complaints. These are the people who log and parse everything, and can find changes with the combat engine down to single percentage points. Those people are not here. What we have are people who "get the feeling that" something is different.

    Trust me, if there was something different with the basic way pets were in combat, there would be players here with exact figures explaning exactly what changed.

  • When will SoV go live?
    Providing that retailers don't sell it too soon, it will be live on the servers by the time you get home from the store with your box.

  • Patches and Bugs
    We correct outstanding issues with nearly every patch. As much as I'm sure everyone would like to see every outstanding issue addressed in one patch, it's not realistic for us to do that. Just because we don't fix all of them in every patch, or don't reply every time they are reposted on the bug board, doesn't mean that we aren't listening.

    By the way, could you explain #2 a little better? I can't make sense of it.

  • Polls and Statistics
    My first instinct was to pass this post up because it uses the "pull numbers of out thin air and call them facts" method of debate. The only way to "address" a post of this nature is to spend a day researching all of the correct numbers for the purpose of showing what really is fact. That would take too much time and only validate this method as a way to get a response, which is why we pass these posts up.

    That said, there is one claim I would like more information on:

    What server is this that you play on that you've noticed a 200-300 player drop at peak over the past year?

  • Female Rest Animation
    We don't like the way that the stance looks. Perhaps we'll go through and revisit it in the future, but no promises at present.

    Let me summarize, for convenience sake, the changes that we have made to pets over the course of the last few patches.

  • The Point of AFK
    Sinneed's response was pretty concise.

    Basically, there's no "pause" button in a persistant world, at least not ours. The only way we would implement a "safe AFK" would be if there was absolutely no in-game benefit to using it. That means that we'd have to make you blind and deaf as well as stopping any type of regeneration and skill-use countdowns.

    At that rate, the question indeed becomes, why not just log off?

  • Magical Damage
    This keeps coming up as an example, though I've addressed it before.

    What does "magical damage" mean to you? To you does that mean that any spell that does damage available to a wizard of a certain level should do more damage than any other spell available to any other class of a particular level?

    If so, that's not the definition we use. Our definition is damage over a time period. A DOT that does more damage overall than ICE comet does not do it in the short period of time like Ice Comet. Of those two spells you mentioned, one wizard and one mage, the wizard spell is faster and more mana efficient.

    Wizard spells across the board have usually have either better casting times, better mana ratios, do more damage, or a combination of the three than those of any other class (harmtouch excluded) ***over a period of time***. That's how it works, and wizards fit that definition.

  • More Druid Forms Needed?
    "Require" is a pretty strong word. While I understand that you *want* more forms, I don't think that they are required. As such, it's certainly good content for the wish-list, but it's not something that I can say we will or will not do at some point in the future.

    The exclaimation point is indicative of an NPC that has been "permakilled", which usually happens when a problem is found after the live servers come up that is severe enough to justify removing the NPC from the game until they can be patched.

Brian Hook Leaves Verant
[27 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:38 AM PST
Brian Hook, one of the most recognizable people in the gaming industry, resigned from his post at Verant Interactive yesterday. The guys at Voodoo Extreme caught up with Brian today to get the full story behind his depature. Here's a snippet:

    VoodooExtreme: Why did you leave Verant?

    Brian Hook: First, let me state up front that this was a completely amicable departure. Verant was very good to me. Smed [John Smedley, Verant/SOE Executive VP] and I have a good rapport, and he’s a genuinely good guy that I like a lot.

    But to answer your question, I was just getting uncomfortable with the very corporate environment there. All of a sudden I was working for this gigantic company based out of Japan instead of a relatively small game developer, and that just wasn’t as enjoyable for me. This isn’t a slam on Sony, it’s just that I’m a lot more comfortable working with a small group of people without a lot of corporate “stuff” to deal with.
