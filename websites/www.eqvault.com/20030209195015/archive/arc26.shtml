Scaled Wolves
- Aurum @ 6:37 PM PDT
The Scaled Wolves was one of the latest updates to be made on the Developer's Corner today. The original question and Absor's response is included in the following text:

    Aegwyn wrote:

    Please bear with the story or skip to the end for my suggestion/question.
    Growing up in Cabilis I had never seen a furry animal except one that came across my dinner plate. Then when I began my Shaman training I saw my masters summoning wolves, yet these weren't the wolves I hunted almost daily to supply Klok Mugruk with scales for armor. These wolves had fur and were soft. I was naive enough to ask one of the Shamans why he couldn't summon a wolf native to our lands, I was backhanded for my questioning of a superior but I believed at the time it was more because he was not powerful enough to summon one of the greater scaled wolves of Kunark. But now that I have increased my abilities beyond many of my former teachers I find I am unable to summon a wolf more suited to Iksars. I have talked to the Scaled Heirophants in the temple in Cabilis and even travelled to the great Plane of Cazic Thule, its only called Fear by those who have reason to be afraid, to seek audience with the great one himself. I have yet to recieve an answer to my questions on this subject.

    Is there anyway to make it possible for Iksar Shaman to summon a wolf that has the look of the Scaled Wolves well known in Kunark?

    I've heard a similar request from folks that play Iksar Necromancers. They would like to see their pets look like the Iksar skeletons that we see on Kunark.

    Unfortunately, in order to allow such a pet to be summoned by those spells we would have to make those models available in all zones. This would mean adding them to the 'global' graphics file. Right now this file is pretty much as large as it can get, and we can't add anything to it.

    Obviously we'll probably be doing something to accommodate new graphics for Shadows of Luclin. How that will affect our ability to add those (and other) graphics isn't really clear right now.

    In short, try asking me again after the start of next year and I'll see if we have a different answer for you.

    Alan

On Graphic Bugs
- Aurum @ 8:28 PM PDT
In the final new post on the Developer's Corner the issue of graphic irregularities is discussed:

    Hello,

    I noticed with the last patch, the Boots of the Vindicator were changed. They were previously a silver, original non-velious texture, pretty much looking like fine plate. Now, they seem to be a dull gold/brown color. Any chance we could get these changed back? One of the reasons that I wanted these boots was for the plain look they had along with great stats. I miss my old boots!

    -ebob

    We changed the look of a few items with the last patch. While your preference obviously would be that this particular item remain as it was, we thought it better that it look a little more unique. A few other items were cosmetically changed as well.

    In the long run, we understand that it's just as likely that people will prefer the old graphic as it is that they will like the new one. I think the old saying goes something like this: I don't know art, but I know what I like. We have to go with our instincts on this sort of thing.

    Alan

Complete Patch Message
- Aurum @ 8:20 PM PDT
Also new to the Developer's Corner today is a complete copy of the latest patch message as posted by Absor:

    September 11th, 3:00 am
    ------------------------------

    ** Spell Changes **

    - Changed all enchanter mesmerization spells so that they overwrite Screaming Terror

    - Changed Shaman epic effect (Curse of the Spirits) so that it would not conflict with certain Damage over Time spells.

    - The Emissary of Thule Necromancer pet is now immune to fear

    - Beneficial buffs that don't have an ability drain component on them can now be clicked off.


    ** Miscellaneous Updates **

    - The server selection screen now only gives information on whether or not a server is up instead of providing player counts. In addition, the "count" option on the /who command has been restricted to internal personnel. Servers on the server selection screen are still ordered from least-populated to most populated in order to assist players in choosing the least populated server.

    - Harla Dar has been repaired (this happened sometime earlier than today, but we wanted you to know).

    - Reactivated broken zone line between Qeynos to the Sewers. A dead halfling was stuck in the drainpipe and has been removed.

    - Fixed a long-standing bug that was causing Feign Death to fail when several characters were on the NPC's hate list along with the character using Feign Death. We'd like to extend much thanks to the monk community who helped us track this one down. Also fixed a problem with the monk's 'Block' skill which inadvertantly was recapped at a lower than normal skill level in recent months. Kudos to "Faned" of the "Monkly Business" bulletin board who brow-beat Gordon with the facts until he gave in and looked at the code :)

    - As announced earlier this week, the Belt of the Cenobite was made useable by some inappropriate classes/races in the last patch. This has been corrected.

    - Several quest components in zones with the Trivial Loot Code will now be lootable regardless of level.


    *** Norrathian News ***

    Boisterous shouts can be heard across the lands as carriers herald the arrival of the latest volume of their city's newspapers. Citizens rush to get a copy of these publications to read the featured articles, latest gossip, and community news for their hometowns.


    *** Announcing Test of Tactics III ***

    Yes you heard right, Test of Tactics will be making its return to the lands of Norrath in October and November. This event will present teams of 18 members in a battle to the death. Only the strong of body and mind will survive and come out of the arena victorious. For more information on the event and sign up for please look on Harpy's Head Tavern at http://everquest.station.sony.com/hht/events/.


    - The EverQuest Team

More on Terrorist Attacks
- Aurum @ 2:09 PM PDT
Normally real-life news issues aren't covered on this, or any other gaming site. We'll call this one the exception. First off, I wanted to include a few excerpts from the always-up-to-date guys at Voodoo Extreme:

  • World Trade Centers collapse after two hijacked planes hit, 10,000 emergency workers head to scene

  • Hijacked plane hits Pentagon, part of the Pentagon has collapsed.

  • American, United Airlines both confirm losing two planes each. The American planes alone held about 150 people each.

  • Federal buildings, United Nations evacuated

  • FAA grounds all U.S. flights, sends trans-Atlantic flights to Canada

  • Sen. John McCain calls attacks "act of war"

  • Israel evacuates embassies

  • U.S. stock markets close after the New York attacks.
With that said, I want to add in my own two cents here.

We all need to take a deep breath and relax a little. What has happened is tragic. But we absolutely must not let the terrorists demoralize us. If we do than we make ourselves even bigger targets in the future, as well as handing the opposing powers an added bonus.

Secondly, everyone take another deep breath. I'm now hearing reports that the price of gas is soaring in some cities because of the number of people panicking. I've heard similar things with some supermarkets. This is irrational behavior, I'll go as far as to even say it's Y2K on steroids.

The United States of America is not fading away anytime soon. There's no mistaking it, we've been hit hard. We've also survived much, much worse over the last two centuries. Naturally, we will recover from this, but how soon is up to us.

So please, everyone keep their heads on. If we do we can overcome any obstacle the world throws at us.

    p.20: The bottom half of this page outlines corpse decay times based upon level and login status. The times provided are incorrect. The player-corpse decay times are as follows. Please note that all times are in real time, not Norrathian time:

    Level 1 – 5: 30 minutes
    Level 6+: 24 hours of online time, or 1 week of offline time (or some combination of both).

    Level 30+ : 3 hours (total, online or offline).

Mechanics of the Sullon Zek Control Map
- Aurum @ 3:44 PM PDT
Are you wondering how the new SZ control map works? Surprise, surprise, Alan announced that SOE/Verant would probably not be releasing any technical information about the map design. Here's their reasoning:

    Just another reminder. We still don't know how the map is maintained.

    -Welfin

    That's true…

    I've been thinking about that since we put the maps up. I'm not so sure that announcing how control is determined is such a good idea, for two reasons.

    First, it's not really an exact science. We're limited by a few things, not the least of which is that the more things we keep track of to use for creating stats and maps and such, the bigger load we put on the server.

    But more importantly, if we announce how it's done that might make it very easy for some one (or some group) to tweak the system and give us false numbers through some sort of trick that would invalidate the results.

    I am curious, though, as to what method you folks think would work best. Though we're not really ready to invest time on changing the current system right now, we might be able to do so in the future, and any interesting new ideas would be welcome.

    Just keep in mind the restriction that some of the wilder ideas are going to be impossible…

    Alan

Absor Talks about Corpse Decay
- Aurum @ 3:36 PM PDT
Corpse Decay was a recent issue tackled by Alan in this Developer's Corner update:

    There seems to be some confusion regarding how long naked corpses last before they poof. From the Feb. 21, 2001 patch message: "Naked PC corpses that have experience and are above level 30 will now last 3 hours instead of 3 minutes."

    I take this to mean that if you die naked, in order for your corpse not to poof in 3 minutes, you must have earned some experience since the time of your last death. To prevent this, I often bum a copper or rusty weapon from someone while performing a coprse retrieval. However, many people state that putting something in your inventory is not necessary to prevent naked corpse poofing, since your character has experience (unless it is level one).

    A little clarity, please?

    Jolla Petbane
    E'ci

    It means that if you can gain experience from a resurrection done on that corpse, then the corpse will remain. I can't think of any examples of corpses that won't give experience back from an experience giving resurrection (perhaps sacrifice victims...), but if there were any, then they would only be around for 3 minutes if there was nothing on the corpse.

    Here's the blurb from the Supplemental Manual:

    -----------------------------------
    quote:

    Player Corpse Decay Times

    p.20: The bottom half of this page outlines corpse decay times based upon level and login status. The times provided are incorrect. The player-corpse decay times are as follows. Please note that all times are in real time, not game time:

    Level 1 – 5: 30 minutes
    Level 6+: 24 hours of online time, or 1 week of offline time, whichever comes first.

    *Note: Empty corpses that are level 30 or above characters will remain in game for 3 hours. It is best advised to get your corpse right away. If that is not possible, remember that one-hour in game is the same as seven hours out of game. Also, be advised that your corpse decay is still advancing if you leave your computer at the character select screen or play on another character on that same account.
    -----------------------------------

    I hope that clears things up a bit.
