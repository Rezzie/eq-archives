Clarification on Pet Changes
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:06 PM PST
Pets have been a hot issue lately on EverQuest boards across the net. In the following thread Alan summarizes things up:

    Let me summarize, for convenience sake, the changes that we have made to pets over the course of the last few patches.

    Pets wielding weapons will now proc with those weapons just as frequently as a player character with that weapon and the same stats. This has reduced the frequency of weapon procs from pets. To make this change we simply removed an artificial multiplier to pet proc chances, no stats or attributes were changed.

    Pets wielding the Sword of Runes will proc with that sword as often as they do with any other procing weapon, but it will only proc against its intended target, summoned creatures.

    Magician and necromancer pets (and because they use the same spells, shadowknight pets as well) now have the ability to duel wield without a weapon, if they are powerful enough. This will happen with some regularity with pets cast from 39th level (52nd for shadowknights) spells and above.

    No other changes to the duel wielding ability of any other pets was changed. This ability was not given to any other pet because magicians and necromancers are primary pet classes, the others are not.

    Alan

  • On the Reason for Nerfing
    An interesting thread. Perhaps a bit over my head, intellectually…

    I think it's wrong to imply that you can only do one or the other, either increase the power of the monsters OR decrease the power of player characters, if your goal is to make the game harder (and that's IF…).

    The real world answer is that you do both as the situation requires. In the case of one bit of the picture being off, say an item or a spell that makes one class or character type or tactic too powerful, relatively, you obviously reduce the power of that item. If you want to make the world as a whole more difficult for the heroes in it, then you can do either, really. Obviously the preferable method is to increase the AI for the monsters, or find other clever ways to make them more powerful.

    That being said, don't go overboard. If we do something that reduces the power of an item or spell or somesuch, it's done in consideration of the whole. I like to picture class balance as a sort of sea (ok, I've never pictured it that way until just now…). Waves are expected, even desirable. But when you see a tsunami, what do you do? Do you raise the ocean or lower the wave? Keep in mind that if you raise the ocean you'll drown the world…

    I love bad analogies. :)

  • Downtime Affecting Users Across World
    After having addressed many times I wonder if I should keep trying or give up, but I'll give it another short:

    EverQuest is played all over the world. ANY time that we take the servers down will be SOMEONE's primetime. We try to take them down when the fewest number of people will be affected, that will mean taking them down during off-peak time for the America's.

    It's a matter of math. How can we affect the fewest number of people possible? If *peak* was at 4am my time and the low point were at 8pm, we'd take them down at 8pm even though it was primetime here.

    I realize as an off-peak player you don't like this, any more than Americans who play during off-peak like it, but until peak time moves to a time other than the time it is, our scheduled patches will be in the general viscinity of when this one is taking place. I'm sorry, but there is really no other option that makes sense. I've seen people argue that we should stagger patch times so that it falls in someone elses peak every time, but that does not meet the goal of affecting the fewest number of people possible.

    Now, that said, no spawn is "exactly 7 days", nor is it exactly "three days". Any spawn over two hours has a +/- modifier that adjusts its spawn time. That means that the seven day spawn could very well pop up anywhere from six to eight days after it was killed.
    --
    It's plus or minus a percentage of the total spawn delay. That means that a two-hour spawn would have a variation of minutes, whereas a 7 day spawn could very well have a variation of days days.

    [Quote from original post: What we want to know now is this. Will enchanter pets be made to dual-wield automatically at the levels you mentioned, just as mage and necro, and shadowknight pets now do? ]

    In a word: No.

    The enchanter pets consist of a floating sword and shield. If you want them to be two floating swords, you'll need to hand them a weapon.

    - Random-effects on weapons carried by pets will now behave as if the pet is a player character rather than an NPC. The change last patch reduced them to a rate below player characters. As part of this patch, target-specific random effects will only work if the target is the intended type. For instance, a weapon that processes "Dismiss Summoned" will only "go off" on a summoned NPC instead of everything.

    - Added some code that should address if not eliminate a problem reported by people whereas the client would "freeze" for a second or two every few minutes. For those who do not have IRQ sharing and/or driver problems, this should address the issue.

    - Made final positive modifications to several Epic Quest rewards. Included in these changes: the SK weapon will now allow you to bash while carrying it.

    - Fixed a problem that gave people problems when exiting the game and attempting to get back in.

    - Added some code to address reports from people who find exiting from the character-select screen back to the server-select screen rather slow when mip-mapping is enabled. Most of this problem is due to routines in DX required to release resources consumed by mip-mapping and may not be addressable until the next version of DirectX is adopted.

    - Changed customizable key mappings so that the ESC key will clear the "NOKEY" setting instead of the "Backspace". This means that you can now assign "Backspace" to a function in game.

    - Corrected a sound problem that would cause certain sounds to be played "rapid-fire" under some circumstances. There is still a potential for this to happen with some other sounds that will be addressed in a patch in the near future.

    - Corrected a problem in Kedge Keep that made the top encounter more difficult than intended after the server had been up for a while.

    - Higher level Magician, Necromancer, and Shadowknight pets now have the ability to dual-wield without being handed weapons. The calculation used to determine whether or not this ability exists takes several things into account, but for the most part 39th level and above summons (higher for the Shadowknight) will have this ability. It is possible that you could get a "good" 34th level pet that will, or a "bad" 39th level pet that will not have this ability.

Verant Vs. Players
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:27 PM PST
A lengthy post was made in response by Gordon to a thread entitled "Verant vs. Players" found on the Gameplay Board.

    This is the retail version of EQ, the classes and races should have been balanced before you released it.

    That's easy to say, and for the most part it was true. However we *have* been adding content to the game with every patch, including tons of new zones that over doubled the worlds size since release. Every time we add something, we run the risk of creating an imbalance that needs to be addressed, or else we have a broken game that people will not play. Make no mistake: People will not play a broken game.

    All the patches should only be adding content and bug fixes, not plugging up ingenious strategies which the players found and which you didn't think of.

    It goes without saying that 300K people can discover strategies that weren't found by the 40K or so that were in all phases of the beta, and the 2000 people that were in for all but the last month of beta. Additionally, many of these "ingenious strategies" that you speak of were created by us attempting to add more content by way of items, quests, abilites, etc., to the game.

    We players are not here to find crack's in the design of EQ for you.

    No, you are here to have fun. However that aside, you will still find cracks in the design of EQ for us throughout that process. I'm thankful we have a dev team working to address those cracks as they are discovered, or again we'd have a broken game.

    If you truely don't like how we players play this game, or the strategies we come up with, then keep them in mind for when your designing EQ2, but dont punish us here and now.

    Again you seem to be saying that it would be better to leave gross imbalances in game that cause people to leave rather than correct them. I have to respectfully disagree, and say with some authority that this will not be the case with any game we make. These games are designed to last in the marketplace for a number of years, and need to last that long to cover development costs, both past and present. If they do not draw enough subscribers or last long enough, the game fails and future development is hurt by it. We won't sit around and watch a game fail when we can do something about it. Now EQ was never in jeopardy of that because we've always addressed these problems as they came up, but if we left every problem, or tried to address them all in a "positive" manner by increasing the power of "A" through "Y" because Z is overpowered, it would not be long before everything in game was elavated to broken proportions.

    Maybe you should realize that this game you created has taken on a life of its own when boxed it and sold it.

    Actually it took on a life of its own over a year before release when the community was formed by people interested in the game. As with anything alive though, things are in a constant state of flux. Things change, and we have to change with them to be sure that everything stays in line.

    thats exactly my point. Don't do anything. So what if warriors found a way to solo blues up to 60. So what if every other warrior is doing it 3 weeks later. How does it hurt anyone?

    So what if all classes found a way to do it? Should we leave it then? Suddenly you have everyone in the game running around without a need to group. Classes become meaningless as one class can do pretty much anything that the next class can. Class A is no longer dependant on class B for good or services in order to advance. That alone would reduce the social impact of the game by making it unnecessary for people to meet each other. Suddenly we'd have 300K people playing a single person game on our servers, with nothing really to hold them together. Aside from the hardcore players, the game would be dead within 6 months.

    Always ADD never TAKE away because when you take away (nerf) you punish.

    And *never* make a policy absolute, with exception to the policy of *never* making a policy absolute. Once you completely block out a particular course of action you limit yourself for no good reason other than it being "policy".

    As mentioned above, the policy of buffing up A-Y every time Z is overpowered is not at all suited to this environment. We would be constantly increasing every classes abilities, or the power of their spells or items, because of "inequities" cited by one class or another.

    Can we agree that as long as we have a class-based system, where classes have complimentary strengths and weaknesses, that there will always be people who believe that the strengths are not strong enough and that the weaknesses are too severe?

    If Verant really does really not like this new strategy, then they should tip their hat to the player who discovered it, and remember to make that strategy impossible in some future EQ game.

    That's certainly the policy advocated by the ever-quoted "mudwimping" article, an article that I personally disagree with. As I mentioned above, absolutes are dangerous. Completely ruling out one reasonable course of action and exclusively increasing power or abilities of the game as a whole is a BAD thing to do. It's as bad as exclusively decreasing power or abilities. Neither is an appropriate course of action. That article gets a lot of mention because it suggests a course of action that is popular with socieity: more, more, more, never less.

    There's a quote that suits this situation quite well, so I'll paraphrase: What is right is not always popular, and what is popular is not always right. It basically speaks to the fact that people do not always make the choices that are best for them. I'm sure that we could adopt the suggestions in the mudwimping article and please a lot of people in the short run, but as soon as something came up that completely unbalanced the game, invalidating a class or two, and we decided to leave things as is or enter the "enhancement death spiral", there would be no peace.

    Let me string a series of analogies together. I'm sure they all have their flaws, but I think that the point is sound. Imagine if:

    - banks have a policy of *always* taking the customers word for it in a disputed account.
    - paying taxes was voluntary (some argue that it is, but still)
    - we released all of the "innocent" people from jail.
    - *everyone* has a college degree
    - *everyone* has at least a million dollars

    These are situations that are pleasing and popular to those who are to be affected, but what happens when they are applied to everyone across the boards? Would any of this be right? Yes, if *I* have a problem at the bank I'd like to know that they'd just take *my* word for it. Yes, *I'd* like paying taxes to be voluntary for *me*. Yes, if *I* was in jail, I know *I'd* be innocent of any crime that would put *me* there so *I'd* like to be released. Yes, *I'd* like to have a college degree. Yes, *I* would like a million bucks.

No, I wouldn't like the idea of banks taking everyone's word for it, as I know that fraud would increase to the point of critical failure and that the entire economy would collapse. No, I wouldn't want paying taxes to be voluntary, because then very few would, government programs would fail, we'd have no standing army, and the government would collapse. No, I wouldn't like everyone who say's that they are innocent to be released from jail. That's why we have a court system. No, I wouldn't want *everyone* to have a college degree, because then a college degree would be meaningless. No, I wouldn't want everyone to have a million bucks, because inflation would run out of control, milk and bread would cost thousands of dollars per unit, and the money would all be in the hands of the top 1% within a month anyway, putting them that much further out of *my* reach.
