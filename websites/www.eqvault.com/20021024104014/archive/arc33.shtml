    [Quote: Any comments on the fact that the majority of players would rather see an anti-summoning spell a lot more than they want to see an anti-gating spell?

    [Quote: Will this change effect the bard songs?     This won't affect bard songs. In fact, it gives a little bonus to bards.

    Here's how it will work, as I understand it.

    When you are casting a spell or using an item that casts a spell or a song, you will no longer be able to sit down. You will still be able to sit while singing. In fact, you will now be able to start singing while already seated.

    Ducking will immediately interrupt the casting of any spell or the use of any item. It shouldn't affect songs at all.

    Over all this will make things easier for casters of all sorts.

    Werdse_Wisdem

    "The main reason for this change is to fix an 'exploit'. "

    [Quote: I'm guessing you're referring to being able to activate an item, then sit until just before it goes off to meditate, then stand for the resolution.

    Yes, that's what we're changing. As for punishment and 'exploit' and all that, don't worry about it. It won't be possible to do it after the patch anyhow, so it won't really matter one way or the other.

    NPCs and Illusions
    [Quote: Ok, wait a minute. You're talking about it giving us to much freedom of movement. How would it be any different than if we had invisibilty cast on us?     Kishan ]

    Ruckuses are great. I've got no problem with that.

    But an illusion is different than invisibility. There is no 'see invisibility' for illusions. The places where we've purposefully staged NPCs that you just can't sneak by would be compromised by such spells. NPCs treat different body types differently, and an illusion essentially gives you a body type.

    Yes, we could spend a lot of time working it out so that such a spell could exist, making special exceptions in code for each illusion in each zone. But that's not practical, it just isn't justifiable to apply the resources to create a single spell and then the resources to fix all the bad situations that such a spell would invariably create.

    Nobody denies that it's an interesting idea. It's just not something we're planning to do.

    Xombie wrote:

    I hate to say that sometimes decisions come down to matters of best bang for the buck. But sometimes they do.

    Faerenka wrote:

    [Quote: I'd like to point out that the reason of not giving us moster illusion, that we would have too much freedom to roam monster areas, is somewhat silly. Yes, sure, we could probably design some seriously complicated code that would allow a spell to work, but the effort would be pretty serious, and we're not ready at this time to trade that effort for this one spell when it might buy us better things.

Abashi (Gordon Wrinn) Speaks Out On PvP Misinformation
- Aurum @ 4:57 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn fired off a new update on the Developer's Board tackling PvP issues that are being "misinterpreted" by other sites. Here's the complete post:

    Hello all,

    It’s apparent that as a result of the Fan Faire this weekend in Minneapolis, inaccurate information regarding our PvP plans for the future were released and/or misinterpreted.

    That has always been one of the difficulties of having a real-time interactive discussion where one doesn’t have the time to think about the precise wording of their answers. However, what we gain through the interactive forum makes it worth the effort, even when things appear to come out wrong like they did in the PvP panel this weekend.

    For the most part, regarding the information that has since shown up on web sites, it appears that when it was mentioned that we “discussed” an issue, it was interpreted as “we are doing this”. The fact is, we discuss a TON of things when we’re driven to improve one area of the game. If you name it, we’ve probably discussed it at one time or another. That doesn’t necessarily mean that what we discussed has either been decided or shot down, just that we’ve given it some measure of consideration in the past.

    So, with that in mind, here’s the quote-by-quote clarification of the story on Castersrealm (eq.castersrealm.com) and RallosZek.net. (www.ralloszek.net).

    Quote from Article: In approximately one week, PvP 'rules' will be as on Sullon Zek. In other words, there are no rules with respect to PvP. Standard blue server rules apply and sexual/racial harassment will not be tolerated. Everything else (training/bind point camping/exp killing, etc) will be completely legal. No PvP related petitions will be handled by the CS staff. The standard blue server type problems such as lost corpses, bugs, lost items, etc will still be handled.

    Clarification: Sure, we discussed it, much like we discuss anything. As we were putting together the ruleset for Sullon Zek, someone suggested that we role it out across Sullon first and see how that goes and role it across the other servers if it works out. Will it go across the other servers? We don’t know. We’ll have to see how it goes on Sullon first.

    Quote From Article: ShowEQ is actively being pursued to be 'fixed'

    Clarification: As actively as “bang for the buck” allows. We really need to do something revolutionary and complex in our next attempt to address the problem. It’s just a matter of having enough time to implement and test that. Timeframe? Mid-term, probably prior to Luclin’s launch.

    Quote from Article: Classes are being re-examined for viability in a PvP environment. Lady Daegarmo specifically mentioned rangers as being one of the classes that is woeful in PvP.

    Clarification: …with nothing specific planned and nothing beyond minor tweaks, certainly nothing class-redefining planned. Regarding rangers there was talk about improving archery, but that was spoken of with plans to put something out across all servers rather than just as a PvP-related improvement. The truth is, the classes are not balanced for one-on-one PvP, no more than they are balanced for one-on-one PvE (Player versus Environment). If you have to fight as part of a group to be successful in PvE, you’ll most likely have to do so with PvP. Yes, this does mean that a solo melee player will be at a disadvantage when meeting casters, especially those configured for solo play, and yes, that is part of the plan.

    Quote from the Article: Lady Daegarmo is pushing for the return of egg shaped pumice on vendors for approx 200P each

    Clarification: Oh it’s been pushed, but has yet not been taken up by the dev team for consideration. We’re kind of like the supreme court that way :)

    Quote from Article: It was noted that Harm Touch and Lay Hands are on the same spell function with opposite effects so there's no way to give Lay Hands back to paladins on death while restricting the same recycle on HT

    Clarification: Not entirely accurate. The reuse time is not tied to the spell effect, not to mention that each ability is treated as a skill by the game and uses its own reuse timer. The reason it’s not being done is not a matter of technical unfeasibility, but rather a specific design decision not to. Both are easy to exploit. The level to which they can be exploited is acceptable on the PvE servers and not acceptable on the PvP servers.

    Quote from Article: Cross teaming is coming to an end. Teams (see later notes) will be locked down. There will be no ability to crossteam at all. No grouping, guilding, buffing, etc with non-team members. This lockdown is probably 60 days out. (I stated that the Development team would make an official date on the lock down in which they would give a minimal of 30 day notice. I also stated that I doubted you would see these changes within the next 2 months.)

    Clarification: Yep, we’re planning on addressing cross-teaming, probably through locking down the teams. However, the specifics on how this will be done, along with what if any beneficial abilities will be allowed to cross any lock down is still being discussed. Opinions are welcome.

    Quote from Article: The lockdown will entail a guildwipe. All guilds will be deleted and must be reformed. There will be extra staff for the first few days after the wipe to assist in this endeavour

    Clarification: If we decided to do the most severe lockdown (no cross-guilding), then this would probably be the case. However something like this won’t be decided until we decide what kind of lockdown we’ll do.

    Quote from Article: It is highly likely that common (the language) will be removed from the racewar servers for general communication use (i.e., public channels). Training in non-team languages will also receive very low caps.

    Clarification: No. While this was one of the many thing suggested as part of a panel discussion, it has only been discussed as one of the many possible options for an “RP Server”, for lack of a better term, by the development team. The “RP Server”, by the way, will not be PvP. Those looking for PvP rules similar to an “RP Server” should look towards Sullon Zek.

    Quote from Article: Strong possibility of adding a long (40 or so hours) quest to change your team. Certain classes/races would not be allowed to join certain teams (such as a Paladin joining the evil team).

    Clarification: No. It was one of the suggestions brought up by players in the panel discussion, and has not been discussed by the development team. However, I can say with some certainty that this probably won’t happen.

    Quote from Article: TZ likely to be changed to a 2 team server (Good vs. Evil). VZ may be changed to a 3 team server

    Clarification: These were ‘off the cuff’ examples of how a lock down could possibly be accomplished, rather than an indication of the direction planned. To clarify further, we previously decided not to implement a 2-team system, and are considering a 3 team system, by race, due to the percentage breakdown of players on each team currently.

    Quote from Article: Rallos will always remain coin + 1 item loot. Code for rain spells is currently being changed to not effect your pet or your immediate group

    Clarification: Yep and Yep, but no ETA.

    Quote from Article: Sullon Zek will be live within 3 weeks

    Clarification: Probably within a week actually baring major problems coming up.

    Quote from Article: There's discussion about adding a new Guildwar server with no 'teams' but lockdowns based on your guild.

    Clarification: This was previously discussed and ruled out by the development team, as it would be insufficiently differentiated from the current servers. In other words, most of everything that would be doable on the “guildwar” server is currently doable on Rallos, or one of the existing teams for that matter.

    Quote from Article: On Sullon, the new summon corpse mechanism for good races will be obtained via a quest which likely involves PvP/crests of fallen opponents.

    Clarification: This is accurate. In fact I’ll tell you now that Mages are in line to gain the ability on Sullon. And no, we won’t roll it out that way on other servers :)

    I hope this gives everyone a better idea of what we have planned for the near and mid-terms. We’ll work to be a little more explicit about what is currently planned or not planned to avoid misunderstandings in the future.

    Thanks,

    Gordon Wrinn
    Associate Producer, EQLive/Luclin
    Sony Online Entertainment

    And so on, and so forth, etc. I have always had an interest in playing non-standard characters, because standard characters are...well...standard. And to me, "standard" equals "boring."

    "Roleplaying requires a setting. You can't roleplay
    characters without a universe for them to exist in.
    Characters, therefore, should be a logical outcome of
    living in that world (and of course that world needs

    Allow me to point out two things.

    1) Verant defines the fiction. If Verant had created the Iksar race and said "There is a heretical sect of good-aligned Iksars hidden within a secret camp in the Field of Bone. Iksars who follow this sect can be Paladins." then *voila*, that's the fiction. That's what they did with Erudites. Erudites are noble and good -- except for those Heretics in Paineel. So you can't really justify your argument with "Well, that's not fiction." You work for the company that creates the fiction, and they can define any fiction they choose if it suits their needs.

    2) Having said that, yes, I agree that setting is important. And, for better or for worse, Verant has set the initial fiction in stone. Iksars can't start off as Paladins. Ogres can't start off as Bards. Erudites can't start off as Warriors. And I agree that it would be kinda silly to have completely unrestricted race/class combinations at character creation. I mean, I just can't see an Ogre Paladin guild in Oggok.

    But.

    People (and Iksars, and Ogres, and Erudites) can change, if they want. Who's to say that an Ogre Warrior, travelling outside of Oggok for the first time, might not run across a bard in the Feerrott, and be entranced by the songs of the bard, to the point where he becomes determined to be a bard himself? Who's to say that an Erudite, after visiting Qeynos for the first time, becomes filled with visions of martial glory after visiting the Grounds of Fate, and becomes determined to be a Warrior?

    So for me, I'm not irked by the fact that my beginning class selection is racially restricted. But I do think there should be a way to change to a different class via a quest.

    Before you start shaking your head and saying "No no no, out of the question," hear me out. I have what I think is a perfectly workable (in the sense of "won't unbalance the game and won't break fiction") idea to allow in-game class changes.

    1) For every class in the game, create at least one avenue where anyone of any race/class/deity combo raise faction with a guild of that class. It doesn't necessarily have to be an exciting or easy way to raise faction. Maybe it's tedious and boring. And make it so where it doesn't raise faction with anybody else but the guild in question.

    2) Create an NPC that gives out a "change of class" quest for that guild. The quest should be challenging on the order of the Crafted Armor quests.

    2a) Make it so where you can't switch classes if your race could have been that class from the beginning. So if you're a human, you can't switch to any class but Shaman (since humans can be every other class).

    3) Require the player character to be at least 20th level and have "Ally" level faction before the NPC gives out the "change of class" quest.

    4) Once the character completes the quest, the character undergoes a ceremony which transforms him/her into a 1st level character of the desired class -- with the same stats as a 1st level character of the desired class. All those weapon skills you've been raising for 20 levels? Reset to zero. All those other skills you've been learning for 20 levels, like Bind Wound and Baking? Reset to zero. All those hit points you had as a 20th level character? Gone. You're a 1st Level character with the same HP as any 1st level character would have based on your class and STA. You may have been a 20th level Ogre SK with 1000 hp before, but now you're a 1st level Ogre Enchanter with 30 HP. All the equipment you had on you? Gone. You're now the proud owner of 5 biscuits, 5 flasks of milk, and the standard Newbie weapon for your class. (Sure, this could be bypassed with the help of a friend. But then it's just like twinking, and that's pretty common in the game. So you're not really making the situation worse.)

    4a) The character's deity is also changed if the new class is incompatible with the character's original deity. The new class/deity combination will not improve faction with anyone. Everybody who disliked you before still dislikes you. But faction can get worse, depending on the nature of the deity change.

    5) This is a one-time only quest. Once you've changed your class, you can't change it back, or change to another class. (Word gets around that you've changed your class once, and other guild leaders will refuse to accept you on the assumption that you're a dilettante.)

    So, to illustrate an example:

    Throckmorton the Troll wants to be a Ranger. To become a Ranger, Throckmorton has to first find a way to increase faction with one of the Ranger guilds in the game (Kithicor Rangers, Protectors of the Pine, or Champions of Faydark) until he is considered an "Ally" of this guild. Note that being an Ally with one of these guilds doesn't necessarily convey the automatic ability to wander nonchalantly up to the guild trainers. For example, if Throckmorton raises his Protectors of the Pine faction without raising his JaggedPine Treefolk faction, then his trip to see Hager Sureshot in Surefall Glade will end the minute he runs across one of the druids in Surefall.

    Once he has found a way to increase faction to Ally, and found a way to approach the "change-of-class" quest NPC without getting killed, he then has to wait until he's 20th level before he can even do the quest. Once he hits 20th level, he's given a quest along the lines of "Go get a common drop from a rare MOB and bring it back." Once he completes this quest, he is transformed into a 1st level character of the new class. He has the original STR, STA, DEX, etc stats that he started with initially, but all his combat skills are reset to zero and his HP is calculated based on his new class and current Level and STA. So before the change, he was a 20th level Warrior with several hundred HP. Now he's a 1st level Ranger with 30 HP.

    Since he can't be a Ranger and worship Cazic-Thule, he also had to switch deities. So he picked Karana. Alas, the only people who've heard about his conversion are the people in Neriak, Grobb, and Oggok. So anybody he was KOS to before, he's still KOS to now. Plus he's now KOS in places he used to not be KOS in.

    The reason I think this will work is because:

    1) It won't be a cakewalk. Being allied with the Bard Guild in Qeynos doesn't mean a thing if you're KOS to the Qeynos Guards and the Qeynos Merchants. So if you're an Ogre who wants to be a Bard, you'll either have to find some way to sneak in to the Bard Guild without being seen by any of the merchants or guards, or you'll have to raise faction with the Qeynos Guards and Merchants as well.

    2) It's not easy to abuse. Requiring that someone be Allied faction *and* 20th level before even being given the *quest* to change class will keep a lot of folks from doing it, because they won't want to go through all the tedium of raising faction for 20 levels, only to get to start all over at level 1 and go back through the first 20 levels again.

    3) It's not unbalancing. Your faction won't be any better after the class change than it was before. However, it might very well be worse. And being an weak Erudite Warrior or a dumb Troll Enchanter will confer its own challenges.

    4) It's consistent with existing fiction. Yes, in the world of Norrath, most Ogres are mindless brutes who eat dwarves and beat each other with swords just for funsies. So no Ogre can *start* as a Bard. That's just not part of the Ogre culture on Norrath. But if an Ogre goes adventuring, meets a Bard, and decides that he RILLY RILLY RILLY wants to *be* a Bard, he can, if he's willing to pay his dues.

    5) It encourages creativity and roleplaying. People will be encouraged to come up with reasons for why their Gnome is a Shadow Knight, or that their Ogre is an Enchanter, and this is a good thing. And, for the most part, I think that the majority of the people who would even bother doing this will be those people who are inclined towards roleplaying anyways.

    Now, obviously, this may not necessarily be an easy coding job. But from a gameplay, game balance, and game fiction standpoint, I think it makes a whole lot of sense. I can't think of any negatives that would occur from a system like this.

So I'm asking you to please please PLEASE look this over and don't reject it out of hand just because you don't personally agree with it. Even if this isn't something you would want to implement on all the servers, I think it's something you could easily implement one one server. So those of us who wanted to be unique characters could do so, while those of you who think it "breaks fiction" wouldn't have to play on that server and wouldn't have to be confronted with High Elf Monks and Dwarf Magicians.

    My response to you is the same that it was to the last person. In a world with no restrictions, you can't be an iconoclast.

    I love to play the outcast. I love having some sort of stigma attached to my characters. In a recent D&D campaign we were to play members of a once-great society that had fallen to a plague.     I wouldn't have chosen to be an outcast if it wouldn't have been a hard character to play, and I certainly wouldn't have done it if I thought that the majority of the group would have done it.

    But that's a small group, and a group that knows that when our GM says "playing an outcast is going to be HARD", he means it.

    When you're talking about thousands of players, a large number of which are fans of the same fantasy authors that we're all fans of, you'll find that a large number of players will want to play the outcast.
