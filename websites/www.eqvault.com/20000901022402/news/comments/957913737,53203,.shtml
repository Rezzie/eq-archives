#27   Name: Thurgrum Ogrebane (varno123@aol.com)   Date: 5/11 @ 22:00 PM PST
I'll first comment on what I believe a powergamer to be. (Note that not all powergamers are like this. I often consider powergamers "kewl dewds" as well. I do have some powergamer friends, but they aren't the same type I meet most of the time hunting guards)

Powergamer: The player who sees Everquest as a game, and nothing else. Thus, he/she will often act in ways that'll only fulfill their own desires. They view the world of Norrath as a place to compete with their character against others, and will go through long periods of playing and number crunching. (Leveling in other words)
Powergamers aren't evil or bad people, just players who often care for very little that doesn't affect them in the fantasy world of Norrath. They do not see Everquest as a roleplaying game, and will often times not roleplay at all. They choose their race/class combinations based upon the statistics and numbers, and not upon what the race/class combination may represent in a fantasy setting. Powergamers will strive for material gain, and care very little for a life beyond gaining levels and items.


Now I don't really care if players want to choose the shallow life of a powergamer. That's fine, and yes, they do pay 10 bucks a month for it. But I'll criticize them often, and discourage against that type of playing. Everquest is a great opportunity to have a wonderous roleplaying experience. It's too bad roleplaying is quite rare, and powergaming is common.


Now as for the "I pay 10 bucks a month. I can do whatever I want" reason for killing guards... It's true, we all pay 10 bucks a month for Everquest. And most times, we can often kill and hunt what we want. But thats not to say other players won't try to get in your way. (After all, they pay 10 dollars too) To the powergamer, he won't really care about killing his own race's, or a friendly race's guards. To him/her, it's a great source of exp and loot. But to the roleplayer, who will hold some sense of morality for their actions because they want to roleplay in a fantasy world, they'll find it insulting that their brethren are being slaughtered. Of course, not all roleplayers will act in the same way. Some roleplayers will be doing it for a reason, others might look the other way because their characer would act that way.
But to the powergamer, its all about getting those numbers. I know I'm making an unfair generalization of the powergamers, but I've found very few of them who won't act like the next.


Onto the guards who give out quest items- I think Gordon once commented that those special guards have not been changed. I could be wrong, but I am pretty sure he announced this not too long ago. He also stated that the guards INSIDE the city have no modifications to their strength. So if a player really wants to kill guards, they can enter a city. (Oh, but wait... That would pose too much danger, wouldn't it? They can earn much more safe exp outside the city where there are obvious design flaws with the faction consequences, and little risk to themselves for camping guards.)
