Information About Horses
- Aurum @ 5:28 PM PDT
Alan unloaded a bit of information about the horses that will be available in the upcoming Luclin expansion on the Developer's Corner:

    I may be repeating things that some of you have already read about elsewhere, but it won't hurt to repeat some information here.

    Twink_a_Saurus wrote:

    I have a few questions about horse.

    First, I know they will be an extension of your body. Can you enter old world dungeons with them?

    If so will you have to be shrunk to fight cast etc? A man on a horse in a tunnel in Sol B won't cut it.

    When you die does the horse die too?

    Once you buy the horse can you ever dismount and feel the grass between your toes?

    Just wondering.

    Ok that's all for now.

    Most likely horses will not be allowed to function indoors. There are just too many situations that could make problematic for horses indoors, not the least of which might be stuffing the rider into the roof of a low ceiling. They will probably disappear when you zone into an indoor location.

    When you die the horse will probably disappear. He will also probably disappear when you dismount, but you will indeed be able to dismount. You'll be able to 'call' your horse back when you need it (which will also cause you to mount the horse).

    Palmetris wrote:

    I was just wondering if horses can be mounted anywhere in the world or just on Luclin.

    If you purchase Shadows of Luclin then you will be able to ride your horse in any outdoor zone, old world or new. However, those that do not have Luclin and all the new graphics it includes (as well as those that own Shadows of Luclin but choose to disable the new graphics) will not see your horse. Only those using the new expansion will be able to see horses.

    Where are the new Dwarves? Also, will they be riding full size horses, scaled down horses (ponies?), or some other animal altogether?

    Aalek
    51 Dwarf Paladin
    Torvonnilous

    We'll have ponies for those that can't reach up to a regular horse's saddle.

    Oh! I also wanted to respond to those that had thought that the horses in some of the earlier screen shots looked a little odd. We had noticed that and had fixed them up just about the time that those shots came out (remember, print magazines have to get these shots a while before the magazine hits the stands). I'm sure you'll be seeing more horse shots before too long, and you'll be able to see the difference. I must say, I was impressed with the very detailed diagram sent in detailing horse anatomy… I'm sorry that I can't find the diagram or the name of the person that sent it in. I was impressed with the effort you put into it. :)

    Alan

EverQuest.com Gets A New Look
- Aurum @ 8:37 PM PDT
The relaunch of the official EverQuest website was the subject of one of Alan's latest posts:

    I'm sure by now you've already seen the revamp of EverQuest.com.

    One of the things I do here is try to keep my nose into the web development for EverQuest.com. We have some very talented web people and some fantastic artists working on the site. I know this because they're smart enough to ignore most of my ideas… :)

    We'd really like to get some of your comments and suggestions about the site. But before you send in your comments, let me try to explain what it is that the new site is designed to do and a little of the logic behind how we're trying to do it.

    When someone hears about EverQuest from their friends and is curious to learn more about the game, the first thing they are most likely to do when they get to their computer is to type www.everquest.com into their browser. We want those new potential customers to be able to learn the basics about the game without the clutter and complication of the stuff that experienced players are interested in.

    Those of you that post to and read the Developer's Corner are probably familiar with the old EverQuest.com. That site basically caters to the experienced player, it's where we talk about new features, gameplay and stuff like that. A lot of that information isn't really useful to a potential new customer. When I want to learn about a game, I want to see screenshots, I want to get a feel for it. That's what we're trying to achieve with EverQuest.com. I've read comments on the net suggesting that it is just a marketing site now. That's almost true. We certainly want it to be appealing to potential players without blasting them with complicated concepts like melee balance changes when they just want to see if they are interested in the game at all. We do indeed want to sell the game to potential players through EverQuest.com.

    So we renamed the old EverQuest.com site, it is now EverQuest Live. For those of you that are experienced EverQuest players, EverQuest Live is probably the site you want to bookmark (www.everquestlive.com).

    We're not done fiddling with the web site. I guess with an ongoing project like EverQuest we'll probably never be done adding things to the site. The web development folks have some really great ideas for the future of the site. But before we roll forward with new changes, we'd like to hear your comments about the changes we just made. So please reply to this thread with any comments or suggestions and I'll make certain that the web team sees them.

    Thanks.

    Alan

Brad McQuaid (Aradune) Leaves Verant/SOE
- Aurum @ 1:39 PM PDT
GameSpy Daily is reporting that Brad McQuaid, EverQuest's long time Executive Producer, has left Verant Interactive/Sony Online Entertainment to pursue other interests in the MMORPG industry. According to the press release John Smedley, who currently serves as SOE's Chief Operating Officer, will assume Brad's responsibilities. Here's part of the press release couresty again of GameSpy:

    SAN DIEGO, CA - October 12, 2001- Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), announced today that Brad McQuaid, one of the original creators of EverQuest, will be leaving the company after five years of service. Mr. McQuaid was instrumental in the launch and success of the world's largest subscription-based online game and was a key spokesman in the online community that nurtured EverQuest's growth.

    "After five years of building the EverQuest franchise for Sony Online Entertainment, it's time for me to explore other opportunities in the creative development of massively multiplayer games," said Brad McQuaid. "I wish SOE the greatest success with all future releases and I have the greatest confidence in the ability of the development staff that we've assembled over the past few years to continue expanding the EverQuest experience."

    "Brad's contribution to gaming philosophy that evolved into EverQuest has been recognized throughout the gaming world as one of the premier accomplishments of the past decade in the business," states John Smedley, Chief Operating Officer, Sony Online Entertainment. "It's been a pleasure working with Brad for the past few years as a colleague and a friend and I'm certain that he will continue to contribute innovative and exciting game technology in the future."
    "Brad's passion for excellence in all things EverQuest has made a material impact in the success of this groundbreaking title," said Kelly Flock, President, Sony Online Entertainment. "I know he will continue to be a force in this emerging market and all of us at Sony Online Entertainment look forward to the possibility of working with him again in the future."

    John Smedley, Chief Operating Officer and a partner in the conception and production of EverQuest, will assume Brad McQuaid's responsibilities.

Important: DirectX 8 and EverQuest
- Aurum @ 7:40 PM PDT
Alan VanCouvering unleashed some important news on the Developer's Corner. Catch the full story here:

    In the next month or so, prior to the release of EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin, we will be moving to DirectX 8. This will be true not just for those purchasing Shadows of Luclin, but for everyone playing EverQuest.

    In fact, some time soon, prior to the new interface rollout, we will be giving everyone the DirectX 8 version of the graphics engine dll. This will give players the option of using the new dll to help us find any hardware incompatibilities prior to the time we move to DirectX 8 game-wide. We'll be testing as many configurations as we possibly can in-house, but with the huge number of possible hardware configurations we can't test them all. The plan here is to nail the problems down now and have as many of you updated to DirectX8 as soon as we can before we make the switchover.

    Why are we doing this? Well, you'll need it to use the new interface and we need it for all of the good things it lets us do for the game, primarily improving framerates (yes, even in the old world and even if you don't buy Shadows of Luclin). For a good idea of the new features we'll be able to implement with DirectX 8, check out our latest In Development (the EVERQUEST TECH DOCUMENT at the bottom).

    Upgrading to DirectX 8 is pretty simple. Here's what you do:

    1) Download DirectX 8.0a. Store it in a directory so you can find it when you need it in a few minutes, but don't install it yet. You can find them on Microsoft's site. Make certain to get the driver that is appropriate for your operating system.

    2) Locate the most recent drivers for you video card and download them into the same folder (this makes them easier to find). Don't install these yet either. Do this even if you have the latest drivers installed. If they have a text file with installation instructions, you might want to get that too (or print it out).

    3) Locate the most recent drivers for you sound card and download them into the same folder. Again, don't install these yet, and go ahead and download them even if you have the latest drivers installed. You might also want to get any installation instructions for the drivers. You're going to need to reinstall them shortly, so it's best to have them on hand.

    4) Set your video adapter to the Standard Display Adapter (VGA)
    The steps required to do this will probably vary for each of you a little bit. But here are the steps I followed to change this on my computer (keep in mind that I am using Win 98 and the procedure will be different if you're using Win 2k or XP):
    a) Click Start, then Settings, then Control Panel.
    b) Double Click on Display
    c) Click on the Settings Tab. Then click on the Advanced Button.
    d) Click on the Adapter Tab, then choose Change.
    e) Click Next.
    f) Select the radial button (the little circle) next to the option for Display a list of all drivers and click Next.
    g) Select the button next to the option for Show all hardware.
    h) Scroll to the top of the list and select (standard display types). Then from the window on the right choose standard display adapter (VGA) and click Next.
    i) It will ask you if you really, really want to use this adapter. Choose yes.

    5) Now install DirectX 8.0a by finding it in your folder and double clicking it. Follow the instructions for installation.

    6) NOW you need to reinstall your video drivers. Use the new ones that you saved in that folder. Follow the instructions for installation given by the manufacturer.

    7) Install the latest sound drivers for you sound card, also following the instructions given by the manufacturer.

    That should be it. You may want to reboot after this if the last driver installation didn't already force you to do it, just to make sure.

    NOTE: It is important to reinstall the latest video and sound drivers after you install DX 8.

    Yes, if you read that carefully you noticed that I said something about a new interface. We will be releasing a new and impressively customizable interface to all EverQuest players with the release of Shadows of Luclin. I don't have a lot of details for you about the new interface at this time. I can tell you that we think you'll be very impressed with it. The main feature of this new interface will be the customizability that it gives the players. We're still building it, so I can't really elaborate, but there is no doubt in my mind that you'll be impressed, or in the least intrigued. :)

    We wanted to let you know about this as soon as we could to give you as much time as possible to make the upgrade. Please consider updating to DirectX 8.0a as soon as possible. EverQuest runs fine with DirectX 8 now, so you shouldn't see any problems running EverQuest after the update.

    We will be double-checking our audio and video support page to make certain that it is up to date. If you need help with audio or video problems, including finding a driver for your cards and identifying your cards, check out our Audio and Video Support page.

    Alan

- Aurum @ 8:29 PM PDT
The melee changes that went into the last patch received some additional clarification from Alan. Here's the two posts from the Developer's Corner:

    Obviously there have been plenty of questions regarding the changes to melee and magic post 50 that we introduced with the last patch. The man with his hands on the pulse of the melee changes is Rich Waters. He was instrumental in figuring out what needed to be done and implementing the needed changes, so it's probably best that he be the one to answer questions about those changes.

    quote:

    Before level 51, paladins, shadowknights, and bards are all on the same damage table as warriors, and always have been. This patch, we changed it so that they remain on the same table all the way up to level 60. Just to make sure this is clear, here is a list of classes-

    Pre-level 51 there are two damage tables, the "standard table" and the "high damage table"
    1-50 Standard Table - Warrior, Rogue, Paladin, Shadowknight, Ranger, Bard
    1-50 High Damage Table - Monk

    Before this patch when characters hit level 51, some of them would move to a higher table. This is how things were prior to this latest melee patch.
    51+ Standard Table - Paladin, Shadowknight, Bard
    51+ High Damage Table - Monk, Warrior, Rogue, Ranger

    With this patch, we added two new totally new damage tables, and moved classes around a bit. Here is how things are on live servers right now-
    51+ Standard Table - No one.
    51+ High Damage Table - Warrior, Rogue, Ranger, Paladin, Shadowknight, Bard
    55+ Monk Table - Monk
    60+ Monk Table - Monk

    Also, please note that damage tables are not always a big damage increase. Moving from the standard table to the high damage table is a fairly large increase, but moving from the high damage table to the 55+ monk table is only a small increase.

    Another important thing to know about damage tables is this - they only affect the amount of damage you do. Damage tables do not affect the chance to hit or miss a monster, your weapon skills, or anything except the amount of damage you do. Damage tables specifically affect your average damage per hit and how high your hardest hits can be.

    quote:

    Far from killing the effectiveness of knight epics, we actually improved the damage they do by a large amount. This seems to be a common misconception with the new melee patch, and one that's important to clear up. This question is really more about the quality of two-handed weapons than it is about paladins and shadowknights, but it's important since they have two-handed epics.

    When we decided to upgrade two-handed weapons, we looked at both the formula we were using to calculate damage, and also the two-handed weapons available to players. When we looked at epics in particular, we took the amount of damage a warrior would do if he was dual wielding his epic sword pair. Our research showed that this was a good, balanced amount of damage for an epic weapon, so we used it as a basis for comparing the other epics.

    We applied this information to tune our two-handed damage formula, until we were happy with the amount of damage a shadowknight or a paladin was doing with his epic, when compared to the dual epic wielding warrior. The result was an increase in damage done with the knight epics, without needing to change the damage/delay stats on them. You should see that you are doing significantly more damage now with your two-handed epic than you were before the patch, and this increases the closer you get to level 60. Epics did not need their damage/delay stats changed, because they are doing appropriate damage with just the new two-handed damage formula we implemented.

    You've probably read by now that a few very powerful two-handed weapons had their damage/delay stats improved this patch. The reason for this is pretty simple: post-epic quality one-handed weapons were available that had much better damage/delay stats than epic weapons, but the two-handed weapons available after epics did not improve much. For example, if we made a one-handed sword that did 20% more damage than a warrior epic, we should include two-handed swords that do 20% more damage than a SK epic to keep things balanced. So, some of the very rare and powerful two-handed weapons in the game got a boost in stats, to put them on par with the one-handed weapons available in the same areas.

On Global Resist Changes
- Aurum @ 8:09 PM PDT
Alan add this update to the Combat and Spell System Enchancement thread on the Developer's Corner:

    Elans_corpse0 wrote:

    I read in your latest response that none of the global resist changes have any effect for damage over time spells.

    Perhaps I am missing something, but it seems that the Necromancer class is largely left out from these beneficial changes since 90% of their offensive spells are DoT. Can you help me out here and show me what I'm missing?

    Sure.

    All high level casters have benefited rather dramatically from the change to magic that we made, even if they don't primarily cast direct damage spells.

    We made several changes, reducing the resistance of high level NPCs to direct damage spells was just one of them. We also reduced resistances in general for many of those high level NPCs, making it so that all sorts of spells will land more easily. Let me isolate the important paragraph for you here:

    NPCs now have less of a chance to resist any direct damage spell caused by casters above level 50. Super-high level NPCs have received a large reduction in the level-difference bonus they receive to their resists based upon the level of the attacking player character. We’ve increased the effectiveness of resist debuffs against a large number of higher level NPCs in regard to damage spells being cast upon them. We’ve also reduced the resistances on a wide range of NPCs, especially for NPCs in Velious. Finally, we’ve given our designers more control over the ability to customize any particular encounter. For example previously, if a designer didn’t want a creature to be mesmerisable, he had to give it high magic resistance. Now they can set that feature individually while keeping magic resists reasonable so they don’t take a bunch of classes out of the picture through the need for one feature.

    Overall, from reading the class-specific boards, I think all casters are seeing how useful this change has been for them. You might want to dig up the URL for a necromancer site and see what your fellow gravediggers think… :)

    Alan
