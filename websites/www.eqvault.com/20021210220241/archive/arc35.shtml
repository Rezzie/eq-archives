    Well, as I've said, it is entirely possible that Evil will be strongest on this server. But not so much for the reasons you've stated.

    The number of race class combinations really doesn't matter. People will decide their team before they choose a race or class. The fact that there are more combinations for one or the other probably won't influence this decision. The only time it will matter is for those that prefer a class over all others. Those that want to be monks, for example, won't be able to be evil. But if they want to play a cleric, they can be on any team. The trick is that most of the classes are available to all teams.

    It is, however, a much bigger issue that the evil team has more security, more unity. They will rule three cities completely, and one more almost completely. That will have a large bearing on their success as a team.

    But, really, it will come down to how many players on a server will want to play on each team. If the majority of people want to play good, then good will have an advantage, regardless of the situation.

    Some of the solutions that I've seen, like moving Rallos to the neutral team, or Karana to the good, won't work. That sort of change would really mess up the class availability. Making Karana good would mean there are no neutral rangers or druids, and that seems wrong on a lot of levels. Making Rallos neutral would fragment the evil teams, leaving them worse off than any other team (as opposed to better off like they are now), and it would mean no evil bards, so no tracking…

    I'm real convinced that the current organization is the best available, even though it wasn't my design. :)

Brad on Exploiting Bugs
- Aurum @ 7:34 PM PDT
Brad spoke on exploiting bugs in response to more feedback and letters received about the banning of the guild Conquest. Here's the update from the Dev Corner:

    Hello all,

    Recently I've not only received emails specifically regarding the Conquest guild issue, but also about exploiting in general.

    First, some people are confused as to what an exploit is and how they're to know what an exploit is. Some are also afraid they might accidentally exploit and then be disciplined.

    An exploit, quite simply, is using a bug in the game to your advantage. Most of them are obvious: attacking NPCs that are stuck, or the ability to duplicate items or money. But we do realize it's not always easy to tell.

    To this end it has been and will continue to be our policy to warn people when they are caught exploiting. It basically works like this:

    1. You are caught exploiting, you receive a warning and the exploit is explained to you.
    2. You are caught exploiting again (this includes either literally being caught or caught and we have evidence you were aware it was an exploit -- in other words, premeditated exploiting) and you are suspended for a period of time.

    Shark Meat
    Breno wrote:

    [Quote: yea just want to know if VI is going to allow the selling of shark mea back into the game i have a bard that all he does is skill skill skill master baker potter tayler and the other day i found a recipie for smoked shark but when i arrived in hurugan in velious and went to morgans meat where i was told sold the suff to my suprise it had been taken out do to druids and ppl exploiting some ques that they ended up nerfing anyway so why take the shark meat away when he quest was nerfed. some ppl need the stuff for making good food for parties

    just wanted to know

    thanks ]

    The short answer is, no.

    Here's the series of events that lead up to that decision.

    Some time in the far distant past, someone created a quest that required shark meat. At that time the only source of shark meat on Norrath was living sharks with sharp teeth. The quest rewards were balanced based on the facts of the day.

    Recently we added a bunch of new recipes for bakers. Shark meat was used in one (or some) of those recipes. So shark meat was added on a vendor or two to facilitate the use of that recipe.

    This made the original quest extremely easy for people that could transport themselves. People used this fact to gain rewards beyond the intended difficulty of the quest.

    Sure, we should have caught it. But with the huge number of quests and the large increase in baking recipes and items, we missed it. To fix this we had, really, two choices. Remove the quest or remove the meat from vendors.

    We chose to remove the meat. Bakers don't suffer in their ability to make skill, there are other recipes that will get them through those skill ranges. All this does is make shark dishes a rarity, and that's actually something that I think is a good thing. If someone gives you food with shark meat in it, you know that they've gone to some trouble to make that food.

Unfortunately an invisible pet owner could, basically, send an endless chain of pets at an NPC and never be at risk themselves.     ----

    Welcome to Sullon Zek, the first Deity Team PvP server.

    Important Notice:

    This server will only be featured on the Test EQ Patch for some time for testing purposes. You will need to run testeverquest.exe to access this server. While this server is featured on the Test Patch, all the conditions that apply to the Test Server also apply to this server. There may be cases where the server comes down multiple times a day for updates. There is also a small chance of corpse or item loss. There will be very little to no initial customer service support. When testing of this server is complete and the server is ready to be introduced to the Live Servers, all characters will be wiped. Below are the new rules specific to this server. But also be aware that these rules may be changed as we feel the need to adjust them.


    The Sullon Zek server is a new server with a new rule set. The rules for this server are very different than those on any other EverQuest server. In general the server is based on the concept of a PvP teams server, like Tallon and Vallon Zek, but with the teams chosen based on which god the character worships rather than the race of the character.

    This document includes the PvP rules for Sullon Zek, and a list of the teams for the server. The 'teams' will be referred to as 'alliances' in this document, as they represent an alliance of the gods, rather than some sort of sports team…


    Sullon Zek rules:

    1. Player v. Player (PvP) combat can occur between any characters that are not on the same team, regardless of level. A level 50 character can attack and kill a level 6 character if that character is not a member of his alliance. But rules 2 and 3 apply.

    2. Characters will be immune to PvP combat until they have gained enough strength to survive the struggle. Characters below 6th level are not able to participate in PvP combat. The first time a character zones after gaining 6th level he or she will then become PvP enabled.

    3. Characters will lose experience when killed in PvP combat if the character that killed them is within 5 levels. The amount of experience lost will be the same amount that is lost if that character is killed by an NPC. Your corpse can be resurrected by a cleric to partially restore lost experience.

    4. Characters will only be able to loot coins from other characters, and then only if the victim was within 5 levels of the killer. Only the character getting the killing blow will be able to loot the corpse.

    5. Players may only have one character at a time on this server. This means that you will have to delete the existing character in order to create a new one. Because we are introducing a new class and a new race with the release of Shadows of Luclin we plan to make allowances at that time for those that wish to explore these new characters.

    6. Characters will not be able to group with anyone that is not on their Deity Team.

    7. Characters will not be able to invite someone to join their guild that is not on their Deity Team.

    8. Combat skills' effective value is capped at 2 levels above a players current level.

    9. Resistance debuff spells have a 50% increased effectiveness against player characters.

    10. /consider returns only three kinds of results when used against player characters. 'Green' means that the character is below your range. 'White' or 'black' means that the character is within your range. 'Red' means that they are above your range. 'In range' means +/- 5 levels.

    11. /consider will also display a message indicating what team the target is on. Members of your own team will be displayed as an 'ally', members of another team will be shown as 'threateningly'.

    12. Faction hits for PvP will be taken for killing characters within their racial home towns. Killing a human in any human starting city will cause a faction loss with the locals. Freeport, Qeynos and Surefall Glade are considered starting cities for humans. All of Greater Faydark and Felwithe are considered the starting city for all elves (high elves, wood elves and half elves), except dark elves. Paineel and Erudin are both starting cities for erudites, and killing one in either city (regardless of their place of origin) will result in a faction loss with that city. Other cities are not considered home towns, despite being run and controlled by a certain race. For example, HighHold is not a human home town. Thurgadin is not a dwarven home town.

    13. Rules such as the Play Nice Policy do not apply to this server. Characters have the ability on these servers to deal with their conflicts through combat. Causing experience loss by 'training' NPCs on other characters is not an offense that would warrant a warning on PvP servers. HOWEVER: All rules regarding general decency still apply. Cursing, threats and other such things will be dealt with sternly, and are still unacceptable behavior on PvP servers.

    14. Beneficial effects can not be used on members of another team.

    15. No character will be able to bind in dungeon zones.

    16. Beneficial spells will not affect characters more than 20 levels lower than the spell level. Existing spell restrictions on spells over 50th level will supercede this rule.

    17. There will be 'insignia' dropped on the corpses of characters over 20th level that are killed by another character. We plan to allow those to be turned in for a reward of some sort. What that reward will be is undetermined at this time, but we are leaning towards a team reward rather than a personal reward.


    The 'Teams':
