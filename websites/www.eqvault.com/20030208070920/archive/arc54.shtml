This is today's edition of the Absor Infobits:

  • More Paladin Talk
    Remf wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Guess this is the appropriate place to mention it; the defense cap seems too high (i.e. unbalanced).]

    I don’t agree at all. The only thing that the paladin has defensively that equals a warrior is the defense cap. Warriors have better armor, skills (like parry and riposte), and hit points. There is no doubt in my mind that a warrior will still be better defensively than a paladin after these changes.

    Kalorad Valrexxaeus wrote:

    [Quote from original post: 1) We need a useful, big encounter, uber-mob purpose. We have none at the moment. My preference would be a defensive discipline such as riposte. We are by definition defensive tanks. Give us this ability.]

    I know that just a few lines up I said that paladins, even with the defense cap increase present on Test, are not the equal of warriors defensively, I am also not certain that they need any other defensive power. I’ll add this item to the list. But I am also going to suggest that we make no further changes in this area for a while, until we get a chance to see what affect the current improvements have.

    [Quote from original post: 2) Fix the level 60 Divine Strength buff, our ONLY useful high level buff, to stack with the level 50 Ranger buff. We need ONE good buff that makes us needed and makes us unique in that no other class has it. It has to stack with everything.]

    Well, I disagree. The stacking of this spell is going to have to be kept in line with the other spells. Making it stack with everything just to make it ‘special’ for paladins isn’t a good reason.

    [Quote from original post: 3) We need the ability to Stun high level mobs. We are the masters at stuns. No mobs above level 55 or 56 are stunable, i.e., the message says they are stunned but they do not stop beating on anyone, nor do they stop their spell casting.]

    I can add this to the list. Perhaps allowing more creatures to be stunned would help a few other classes at the higher levels, without being to unbalancing.

    [Quote from original post: 4) Raise our BASH skill. Also a disc to make BASH an unresistable stun IF it lands, i.e., to interrupt caster mobs, is highly needed.]

    I’m thinking that adding more disciplines isn’t a great idea. I’m reluctant to start stuffing in disciplines to fix things. But I’ll add it to the list, assuming that we do not do something like your number 3.

    [Quote from original post: 5) Reduce the reuse time of LOH post level 50. Make it gradually less until level 60. Make it a Complete Heal at level 60.]

    No. Look, LoH is good as it is. Perhaps an increase in its healing power is in order. But reducing the reuse time isn’t appropriate (it’s kind of the basis power for recast times), and Complete Heal is out of the question. I’ll add a suggestion for an increase in the healing power of LoH, but I think it’s fine as is.

    [Quote from original post: 6) Add Yaulp IV to our spells at level 59.]

    Hmmm... I’ll add it. But there is probably a reason it wasn’t added with the last changes.

    [Quote from original post: 7) Raise our TAUNT skill. Again, we are defensive tanks. It is currently too hard to taunt mobs off of classes which deal much more damage than us.]

    Every fighting class seems to want a better ability to taunt. Folks, taunt is a group thing, not an individual thing. Your group needs to be controlling their aggro. And we just can’t be setting it up so that any character can flip a switch to gain the attention of their enemy.

    Again, I’ll add it to the list. But we’ll certainly have to look at this is relationship to everything else.

    [Quote from original post: Where do i begin. Lets start with the appearance no offense but pally's dont look "epic" with just a hilt. The proc rate is for some reason lower than before and i have even saw procs as low as 1 and 2 damage. Something needs to be done.]

    The hilt thing is already on the programmer’s to-do list. I’ll add it to this list too.

    I don’t think we’ve changed the actual proc rate of the weapon.

    I’ll ask that someone look at the damage on this item. I’ll also add to the list of a suggestion that perhaps we change this effect to something defensive.

    Scrith wrote:

    [Quote from original post: - raise the Paladin (and SK) Bash skill cap as high as Warriors. Rangers are as good at Archery as Warriors, why shouldn't Paladins be as good at Bashing? You can even argue that they should be better, since Paladins use shield MUCH more often than Warriors (unlike Archery, which is used by both Rangers and Warriors, yet they share the same skill).]

    Not better than warriors... but I can add that it be raised to match a warrior.

    [Quote from original post: - give Paladins special shields which PROC something. Much like there are Ranger bows that proc when they hit, why not give Paladins shields that can proc?
    - give Paladins critical hits on Bash. Rangers have it on Archery, how about finally giving Paladins (and SKs) a chance to get critical hits by putting them on our Bash skill?
    - increase the likelihood of Bash stunning high level mobs. One of the biggest problems facing high-level Paladins (lvl 55+) is that our beloved Bash skill, which we have used and depended on so much since our earliest days, is no longer effective against high level mobs.
    - please increase the AC on our shields...many of us are still using the same AC 25 shields we had before Kunark was released. Why have weapons (and armor) improved so dramatically since then, yet shields are still providing about the same AC?
    - how about a new skill, called Blocking? When using a shield, it gives you a chance of blocking an opponent's attack (much like parry and dodge work). This would give Paladins (and SKs) a unique defensive skill that relies upon their role as shield-bearer.]

    In fact, most of this is similar to what shadow knights suggested. It certainly makes sense to suggest that these two classes have the same sort of skills.

    ebob said:

    [Quote from original post: One thing that concerns me is the recast times on certian spells. The one that I've had experiance with is Wave of Healing, our Level 55 Group heal spell. Why is the recast time on this spell 1 minute? I would see the reasoning behind a 1 minute cast time if the spell were to heal more than 200hp, but at 55, 200hp is 1-2 rounds of combat from any mob. I belive that this spell should have its recast time significantly reduced, or the amount of the heal increased.]

The added 30 second recast time just makes it even less useful. This spell should either cost very little mana, or heal for quite a few more ticks.]

    I can also add this to the list. But keep in mind we have a delicate balance to maintain here. We are already planning to look at upgrades for the two secondary healers. Too many healers will really impede the viability of clerics.

    Guardian334 wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Skills/Abilities
    1) Defense Skill to 252 (matching Warriors, up from 225). This narrows the AC gap between hybrid and parent class.

    Spells
    1) Move the Armor line down: Guard to 39, Armor of Faith to 49.
    2) Move the Yaulp line down: Yaulp II to 30, Yaulp III to 39, Yaulp IV to 49.
    3) Move Superior Healing from 57 to 53-54. Reduce fizzle rate in line with other previously noted comments on SH fizzles.

    7) Addendum to #6 above, also add a Regeneration effect to the Divine Strength line. 3HP/Tick at 50, 5HP/Tick at 55, 7HP/Tick at 60. Maintain stacking with all current spells, including Natureskin, Divine Purpose, and druidic/shamanic Regeneration lines.
    Equipment:
    1) Add a ?Lichbane? 45-50+ version of Ghoulbane, appropriate for the level range it would be used in. Basically, an upgraded version of the popular mid level Paladin sword. The post that mentioned this idea and suggested it be yielded by questing to turn in Ghoulbane and Soulfire was a good way to go about it. If the ?sub-quest? to upgrade the sword idea could be implemented as well (where you do a quest for each temple in Norrath and get slight upgrades to your Lichbane, that?s a great idea that would end up with a final version Lichbane perhaps 10-15% better than the unquested version).
    2) Implement additional Shields in game to address the rather sparse shield choices available to Paladins and Shadowknights who, again, are the only tanks using them. Between four and eight new shields would be excellent, with one to three of them coming in at or over 30 AC. Some of the new shields should be questable, some should be drop; hopefully there will be a division in the method used to introduce them into the game. Hopefully at least two or three of them will have useful and functional effects to consider as well.
    3) Examine the class-specific weapons available in the game and note the numbers. Paladins are lacking in class-specific (1hS and 1hB especially) weapons compared to options available to other tank classes. A little on the quality side, but mostly on the availability side of the question. Broader selection would really help shake out the equipment options for the bulk of the Paladin class population.
    4) Reexamine the Paladin Epic; is it comparable to other tank epic weapons? Why is it 2hS when many Paladins are using shields? Various suggestions already made adding additional quests that can be done to transfer a 2hS major quest or epic Paladin weapon into a sword/shield combo would be great additions after balance issues with the current offering are considered.
    Combat Problems:
    1) Unstunnable mobs are a real brick wall for Paladins. The Stun line is our single most combat effective line, the one that gives us the most mileage. Some encounters should be retained unstunnable for encounter/world balance reasons; others are perhaps able to be tuned down to keep Paladins in the game in these situations. Speaking personally, fighting mobs that cast but are unstunnable and uninterruptible generate intense feelings of hopelessness. To make an encounter difficult and require challenging use of skill is one thing; to make an encounter unalterable through any means is another.
    Put another way; sometimes it?s good that you know the mob will get the complete heal off anyway. Other times, it just leaves you feeling useless at your inability to have any chance of doing something to counter that which is leading to your downfall.
    2) The proposed spell on Test, Celestial Cleansing (110hp heal-over-time for four ticks, totaling 440hps healing at the end of the spell, with an additional tick waited before recasting) is a great way to let a Paladin heal while still staying engaged in the fight (skip a melee round to get the heal off on your fellow, then start swinging again the next while the healing kicks in). The problem are the numbers involved. At 59 mobs double for 110 on a really, really bad roll. The well documented severity of Velious raid-level encounters should adequately showcase the disparity between Test?s Celestial Cleansing and the amount of damage it?s being called upon to heal. The spell idea is solid however, if implemented in two or three versions and spread across the spell options for the Paladin. Move Celestial Cleansing to 50 or 51, where it can make a more measurable impact. Put in a 170-200hp/tick version at about 55, and a 225-300hp/tick version at about 60. The Mana/HP efficiency should be very comparable to that of ?normal? heals, as you?re trading away time to get more HPs later, and that should be a tradeoff respected in the costs associated with the spell.]

    I'm also kind of interested in the idea of a discipline that would allow the LoH ability to work on the whole group. It's interesting. Probably not going to happen, but it's still interesting.

Donal's Breastplate - Additional Clarifications
- Aurum @ 6:53 PM PST
Brad McQuaid shed some more light on the changes coming to Donal's Breastplate in this thread he created on the Gameplay Board:

    I just read through Absor's post and the replies to it.
    I wanted to clarify some things.

    First, it didn't take 6 months and 300,000 people to find the problem. The problem was only made apparent recently, with Velious and the removal of the HP cap. The breastplate didn't appear as a problem until encounters started lasting much longer than they had previously. We made one attempt to address the issue, found out it wasn't nearly enough, and now are faced with having to do this change.

    Removal of the HP cap added more variety to the game -- it changed strategies and tactics. No longer were we forced to simply make mobs kill in a very short period of time. Overall, this is a very good thing for the game -- variety and encounters that require differing tactics is good.

    That said, whenever we do change or add behavior or other significant aspects of the game, other unforeseen problems often surface. It's the nature of the genre -- these game's change and evolve, and every change, small or large, can have unexpected ripple effects.

    A second clarification: less than 5 or so mobs in Velious were created assuming the breastplate would be used, and this was mostly likely in error (i.e. we shouldn't have made that assumption in those cases). We will be addressing those mobs and tuning them down accordingly.

    In summary, I'm sorry we goofed up -- it does happen. It will happen again. It's the nature of the beast, but we will always do our best to minimize these occurrences. And we will also do our best to react as fairly as possible when we are forced to act, attempting to do the right thing for both the individual player as well as the game as a whole.

    It is unfortunate that the item was created in the first place, given its potential for problems, and it's also unfortunate that we didn't realize how much more magnified that problem would become when the HP cap was lifted.

    That said, again, we're not perfect, and we will continue to make mistakes as long as this game continues to grow and evolve.
