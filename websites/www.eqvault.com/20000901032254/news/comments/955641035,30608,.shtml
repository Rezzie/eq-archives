#27   Name: A former Senior Guide (na@na.com)   Date: 4/14 @ 10:32 AM PST
Greetings all.

I am writing this letter with a heavy heart. It's not something I decided to do lightly. I tossed the idea around back when Verant unilaterally declared war on our elders and decided they had to take a "more active" role in running "Their" guide program. In Short they wanted to run the program and toss out 12 months of work by the Guide program to develop a solid team of professionals who were dedicated to nothing less than the customer. This "Re-organization" of the Guide program started last October. I left in disgust in November, but I took no direct action to sabotage the guide program by asking others to leave as well.

A little history on "Their" guide program: The Guide program existed long before Verant even had ONE Customer Service Agent. When 989 Studios was beta testing EverQuest there was no customer support. No such thing. There was some tech help but even that was limited. Ozymandius, a beta .5 player known as the Traveler, took it upon himself to help to educate players. To help them with problems and to give advice when he could. Thus was born the first guide.

Over time others joined him. Eventually they formalized the process. Thus creating the EverQuest Guide Program. Something funny to note is that the head of Verant Customer Service was himself a member of this group before being hired by Verant. Yes, Jeff Butler AKA: Ifurita, was a guide before going to work for Verant. Verant, knowing there was no way they could support this game adequately themselves, encouraged the Guide program. Giving us powers and items to help us to do our jobs better. But along with those tools came greater responsibilities. We were forced to sign in 10 hours a week as guides, and Seniors were known to work well in excess of 20 hours a week, both online and off. I myself clocked about 50 hours a week running my server.

We were not allowed to tell our friends we were guides. We were not to adventure as guides. All of these things we did. And all were for good reasons. But over time all guides noticed that Verant communicated with us less and less. They treated us with tremendous respect during Beta and even in early final. But that quickly degraded. Eventually we were treated like what we became, indentured servants. "We're letting you play for free" was actually used as an excuse for this treatment by one GM at one time.

Now Verant has come full circle. Berating guides who were only doing what they were told. What they (the guides) did was wrong. I'll admit that 100%. However, they did so at the direction, and request, of their Senior Guide. Therefore the blame falls, 99%, on their Senior. Who was correctly punished. To remove those guides from the program and to suspend their accounts (in excess of 5 days), in order to remove their powers, is unconcionable. It takes exactly 10 minutes to de-flag a guide. I should know. As a Senior Guide I had to have my fair share of guides de-flagged and removed from the program. Verant is able to de-flag a guide in minutes. Not days. These guides, people who gave their time money and effort, were treated like common criminals. They were insulted, personally, by a Verant GM (who herself was once a guide, shame on you Majara! (Cailleach)) and then suspended from the game until Verant could get around to removing their powers.

I am hereby advising all people who are, were, or were thinking about being guides to leave the program and do one of the following, whichever applies to you:

a) Forget about joining, Verant has shown they are not worthy of our help or assistance. The longer Cailleach goes unpunnished (and this is not the first time I've seen her do something like this) the stronger the signal from Verant that they just don't care about their guides.

b) Leave the program and post exactly why you are doing so. No more people should leave and say "I'm too busy now" unless that really is the truth. Some left for "personal" reasons when I was a Senior. I cringed every time because I knew just how much wasn't being said.

c) Let any friends you have that are in the program or thinking about joining it know about what went on on Lanys T'Vyl.

As you can see I feel very strongly about the way that Verant handled that situation. Unfortunately, during the time I was a guide, this is not the first time I've seen them show such poor judgement and such a horrible lack of professionalism. Good luck to each and every one of you, no matter what you decide. I love the guides. I loved the Guide program. But there comes a time when a decent human being should stand up for their rights and say "NO! I will not tolerate being treated this way".

What the guides did was wrong. But they didn't deserve to be treated the way they were. Unfortunately what happened on Lanys T'vyl was not unique, nor uncommon. I call on all who respect themselves to help spread the word. It's time for Verant to learn to respect the Guides. I think it's also time for them to re-learn just how much they NEED those guides, those people, that handle every little customer service issue for them every day. Who are _required_ to spend 10 hours a week, of their time, FOR FREE, doing something that most people are paid at least $6 an hour to do (and usually far more).

Yes, the guides volunteered for this. Yes they volunteered to take on the responsibility. But nowhere in the Customer Service Policies and Procedures does it state that Guides gave up their rights to be human beings. Their right to self respect and their right to be treated as the mature adults they are.

Sincerely,

A retired Senior Guide
