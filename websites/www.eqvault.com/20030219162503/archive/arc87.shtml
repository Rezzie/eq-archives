The End of the Smurfs?
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:58 PM PDT
While mostly focusing on Ultima Online's volunteer policies, I thought it might be interesting to point out AVault's recent article on the effects an AOL lawsuit is having on the MMORPG genre. For those who haven't been following it, this exceprt will help bring you up to speed:

    No one is more reliant on volunteer labor than the massively multiplayer online games. The three largest online fantasy role-playing titles, EverQuest, Ultima Online and Asheron's Call, use them as their first line of customer service. But maybe not for long. On Monday, August 28, representatives of Ultima Online's makers, Origin Systems Inc. (OSI), announced the termination of free game account privileges for hundreds of community volunteers. In a letter to the community, external support coordinator Matt Gutierrez wrote: "Due to changes in the industry regarding volunteers, effective Sept. 30, 2000, OSI will no longer provide free player accounts or other added value to members of all [Ultima Online] Volunteer Programs."

    While company representatives have not said so outright, it appears the move to eliminate what amounted to a $10 a month gratuity for volunteers is related to a recent New York class action lawsuit, brought by former volunteers at America Online (AOL). The plaintiffs are claiming that AOL's practice of relying on what it calls "community leaders" to produce, maintain and moderate online content was tantamount to employment, and hence is covered by American minimum wage laws. (Origin's parent company, Electronic Arts, has exclusive rights to AOL game content.)

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 11:22 AM PDT
Here's ths morning's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Boards, Board and more Boards
    [Quote from original post: Abashi has the board really changed at all?]

    Actually, yes, I believe that they have. It really wasn't our intention to suppress the expression of ideas, but rather the way those ideas are expressed. I'm happy to see cases of name-calling, profanity, and general flaming severely reduced, as those are NEVER necessary in order to express an idea.

    The fact that the boards have been clearing up has let us do some things that were impossible before: Get more Verant people here posting. While I remain the spokesperson if you will, I'm happy that we're able to get Rowyl, Ester, Hobart, Chris, Yakat....howeveryouspellit, here helping answer questions and such. Overall I think it's been a real boon for communication.


  • /Rand Can Be Hacked?
    I'm not sure when this happened, but you might want to look very close to whatever text string was coming up and compare that to the real thing. People usually use a long /say or /emote to give the impression of a /random. The programmers have made /random 100% serverside, which means that it cannot be hacked.


  • Rogue's Weapons
    Dazzler, there's nothing wrong with the weapons usable by rogues. As for that caster weapon you mentioned, it's a joke, and I think everyone is well aware of that. There is no pure caster who's coming anywhere near any melee class in melee damage. I'd hazard a guess that your rogue is outdamaging some casters in average combat.


  • "Where have all the dragons gone?"
    [Quote from original post: Guess what? The dragon just despawned! Right into thin air... Humm.]

    It think that it will take a bit of strategy, not to mention alacrity, to take on these encounters. Yes, they are rare spawns, and they depop if they haven't been killed in a certain amount of time. Don't worry, they won't depop while they are fighting.

    If anything, I consider this format better for all than that of Vox and Nagafen. Vox and Nagafen are routinely killed as soon as the servers come up which makes it hard for some people to participate.

    [Quote from original post: What for? Are my $10 a month not equal at 53+level?]

    There's four dragons outdoors and more than that indoors in Kunark. Given that there's only two in EQ proper, I think that it's a pretty good trade.


  • Items Missing After Patch
    [Quote from original post: 1. How on earth do such things happen? What sort of baroque database design would allow character attributes (name, level, skills, etc.) to be retained, while "forgetting" all possessions?]

    One of the good (and bad) things about running a system with 300K users is that if we have a problem on our end, it's never just one person experiences it. I've never heard of this happening before. My first guess at what may have happened is that someone your friend gave his login info to took the stuff and destroyed anything NODROP. Every case of a wiped character that I am aware of could be tracked back to this with enough effort.

    In any case, though I've never heard of it before, GMs still need to investigate every claim that they receive and act on them in short order.

    [Quote from original post: What's worse, his first responses from /petitions have been, "it'll take up to four weeks to research this and possibly restore your items, and anything you gain between now and then will be lost."]

    I'm dismayed to hear that any GM would say anything of that nature since they all know that immediate investigations and responses are mandated by company policy. Please have your friend forward the GMs name to gscotto@verant.com along with a description of events.

    I think that I've answered all of the questions you've posed in this thread. Please let me know if you need more info.


  • Roleplaying Server
    We cannot enforce a roleplay server. Left unenforced, the environment on that server would be no different than any other server.

    I think that it's the second sentence that you disagree with, but that is all there is to it. Roleplaying is a personal choice that everyone makes. If people wanting hardcore roleplay were to stick to their guns and do nothing but on any server out there, they might be able to turn peer pressure in their direction. However if they won't do it on a normal server, there's no reason to suggest that they'd do it on a server labled as a "roleplay" server.

    Furthermore, any new server is going to attract a large and diverse number of people regardless of any label on the server. It would also definitely attract the people who would go there just to give the roleplayers a hard time.

    I'm sorry that you don't agree, but the issue closed. Like melee binding and experience systems, this is as firm as it gets.


  • Necro Nerfs Make Gamer Easier?
    Itchy,

    You're grasping at straws here. Please read the quote again:

    "neither I nor other members of the tuning team actively seek out ways to make the lives of the players more difficult."

    We don't sit around trying to come up with ways to make the LIVES of the players more difficult. We don't have discussions about the best way to go about upsetting the players. We address issues in game. If there's an imbalance, we'll fix it. We fix it because gameplay mechanics must be balanced in order for the game to be challenging. If it weren't challenging, no one would play.

    Sure, this means that certain aspects of the game are made more difficult in nature. An encounter might become harder to overcome. A weapon that does far too much damage may be weakened. A pathing issue that allows people to slaugther without risk will be fixed. Though these things might make one particular aspect of gameplay more difficult in nature, that doesn't translate to making the LIVES of the player more difficult.

    Changing an aspect of gameplay in a way that has no positive side makes the LIVES of the players difficult. The "necro nerfs" that you mention in the subject certainly had a positive side, and doesn't qualify.

    August 30th, 8:00am
    ------------------------

    *Today's Patch*

    Today we modified the EverQuest client to implement the following changes:

    - Corrected a bug that would cause players to crash if they entered the
    game, exited to the server selection screen, and tried to reenter the game.
    - Corrected a bug that caused delays when switching between UI modes due to
    the text wrapping change implemented last week.
    - The /book command (/book #) now accepts pages 1 through 50, and will
    switch to the proper page.
    - Opening your spellbook will automatically cause you to begin meditating
    providing that you have the skill.
    - Removed the /meditate command since it is no longer necessary.
    - You can now activate a hotkey while the spellbook is open by pressing the
    corresponding number-key.
    - The "/target [name]" command can be used to target other players by name.
    Please note that this ability is subject to the range to the target.
    - EverQuest will now write the original default colors when creating the
    EQCLIENT.INI file, rather than the new colors that were put in a few weeks
    ago.
    - The @-character can be used in emotes to indicate a target. For instance,
    if you target "a gnoll" and type "/emote laughs cruelly at @", the output
    will be, "Playername laughs cruelly at a gnoll".
    - People with the /LFG (Looking for Group) flag set will now have their
    over-head name prefixed with an asterisk. Remember that you can search for
    people looking for a group by using the "/who" and "/who all" commands.
    Typing "/who all ranger 40 50 lfg" will return a list of all rangers between
    40 and 50 that are looking for a group.
    - The "Sense" series (Undead, Animal, and Summoned) will no longer point you
    towards people's pets.

    *Note on Voice Recognition*

    Last week we implemented voice recognition technology in EverQuest. To use
    this new feature, please refer to the EQROKManualSupplement.doc file in your
    EverQuest directory. Please note that you must already have a voice
    recognition engine that is compliant with the Microsoft Speech API (SAPI),
    version 4.0. The voice recognition technology was tested with Dragon
    NaturallySpeaking(r) v4.0. Check with the developer of your software to
    find out if it is SAPI 4.0 compliant.


    - The EverQuest Team
