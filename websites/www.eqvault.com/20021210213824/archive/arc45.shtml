Absor Infobits

These are the latest posts Alan made in response to player-submitted comments on the Developer's Corner:

    Trade Skill Boards
    [Quote: I was wondering if since the Trade Skill board will not be reopening (per the letter stated) will Yak be reading and answering posts on this board or where can we submit official question/comments about Trade Skills. I have noticed that in the past even though there was a trade skill board Yak would not post responses often and I was wondering if the trade skills concerns will be looked at? Please quell this poor tinker's fear that our concerns are going to fall by the way side with this new system of message boards.

    Thank you very much for taking the time to read this.

    Gnormey ]

    I'll still be forwarding questions to Yak. He may or may not reply here personally, but any responses that I make about trade skills will be information that I've gleaned from him. Keep sending in your trade skill questions along with the others. Your concerns will not fall by the wayside.

    Alchemy Items
    I've had a few requests about two alchemy items.

    The Field of Bone Gate potion does not yet have a 5 or 10 combination version. This is being added, and will hopefully be in the next patch. For now, don't try to combine these potions, since you will be trying to create an item that just doesn't exist yet.

    Kilva's Skin of Flame has the same effect as Kilva's Blistering Flesh. This is also being looked at right now, and hopefully will be fixed for the next patch.
    --
    [Quote: Subject: Shaman FoB Gate Components

    As of now, the shaman FoB gate component Sandalwood is not available in Cabilis or Halas. Is this intentional?]

    That was unintentional. We'll try to get that fixed and updated for the next patch.

    Coldain Tailoring Kit vs Large Tailoring Kit
    [Quote: In the last patch, is was made so that Velious tailoring recipies can only be made in the Coldain Tanning Kit. Prior to this, tailors had been using the Large Sewing Kit for both Velious and old world recipies.

    The Coldain Tanning Kit will currently combine both Velious and old world recipies. Is this intentional, or should we hang on to our Large Sewing Kits a while longer? ]

    Yes, specialty kits should work for basic recipes. Any recipe that doesn't require a special container to combine in will work in any container of the same type. But if it requires a special container, then you will need that special container to combine the recipe.

    Queen in Chardok - Ancient Blade
    [Quote: For warrior epic the bottleneck is the queen in chardok. it takes massive hours to get to her and kill her. She is no pushover either. It takes great dedication and pullers who know what they are doing. The risk is very great because of trains there. Currently the blade is very rare.

    i would appreciate if this issue was brought forward to the dev team. thank you

    Dindol Phoenixfyre
    57 Dwarf Warrior
    Druzzil Ro ]

    Adamite Bo-Stick

    Frostfist Coldeve
    Monk of the 44th season,
    Fellowship of Luclin ]

    All area of effect spells will damage the caster. With this weapon you get a trade-off. You suffer some damage when it procs, but you get to damage multiple opponents. There are certainly places where this weapon would be exceptionally useful.

    Yes, this was intentional. As one of the designers put it, this item is 'balanced differently'.

    Roleplaying or just playing?
    Which is it for you?

    I asked members of the team what it was that they would want to know from our customers. Bill Coyle, generally cool guy as well as an Assistant Producer for EverQuest, had this question:

    "I'd be really interested to know how many of our people actually enjoy roleplaying their character, you know, Paladins that would never group with a dark elf, etc."

    Since we've been talking about roleplaying servers, this seemed like a good question to pass on. But this isn't really a poll. I would rather generate a bit of a discussion on the topic of roleplaying based on Mr. Coyle's (he makes me call him mister Coyle) :) question than just getting yes or no answers.


    Do you enjoy roleplaying your character, even if it might come at the expense of losing out on opportunities, like your paladin not grouping with a Dark Elf? If so, maybe you can explain to us how it is that this is 'fun', rather than a restriction on your gameplay.

    If not, why not?

    Myself, I've 'compromised' the role of my characters on occasion to meet a personal need. But that doesn't happen very often. Some of the most fun I've ever had playing a computer game have been generated by seemingly innocuous encounters with other roleplayers. I've played a dark elf that has spent his time doing his duty as a follower of Hate by criticizing those that help lesser races. The conversations, discussions about the nature of 'hate' and the quips that have come from it have entertained me many dark nights in Neriak.

    So send in your comments to the questions through the submit comment form. Make certain to make the subject Roleplaying or just playing? so that we can sort them out properly.

    Obtaining "Mastery" In Racial Skills
    [Quote: I am noticing more and more quests and trade skills using foraged items. I am a wood elf rogue and I had a 50 skill in foraging when I was born, and I'll get to level 60 without once improving my skill. This seems odd in a role playing sense. I should get at least a little better over time.

    I am not requesting much of a change, more like only 2 points of skill per level starting at 0. Hence, my first possible increase would be at level 26.

    Level 25: Skill 50 (min)
    Level 26: Skill 52
    Level 30: Skill 60
    Level 40: Skill 80
    Level 50: Skill 100 (max)

    I don't see any imbalancing issues since foraging is a marginal skill to begin with, and the special items foraged only occur at skill level 100, so only the most talented high end wood elf will benefit.

    I don't see why this cannot be attributed to other racial skills such as halfling's hide ability.

    Thanks for your time and consideration.

    Vaslin Slickfinger
    Tarew Marr ]

    For the Wood Elf Rogue the forage skill is a freebie, a racial bonus. Similarly the hide ability for all Halflings that don't get it from their class. While I suppose that allowing those racial skills to raise to a 'mastery' level might not unbalance the game, it would reduce the value of those skills for those that get them as part of their class.

    Posion
    [Quote: Most of the 'useful' rogue poisons (level 50-60 useful) never become trivial. Is this by design, or a bug? ]

    This was intentional. The best items that any craftsperson can make should always be a challenge to them. These are the pinnacle of the craft. This applies to the art of poison making as well.

    Colorblind Players
    [Quote: I believe I asked about this before but it got lost in the meriad of useless posts. As far as my research into configs for the game goes, there does not seem to be any way to change the color of the target's health bar. Currently it is a (or so I'm told) a dark red color. This is a serious problem for me when I am in areas with a somewhat dark background, including almost all interior dungeons. I can't distinguish the target's life bar at all. Would it be possible to put in a config to change this life bar to some RGB color code?

    thanks, and kudos on the new forums.

    --Gotte, Druzzil Ro ]

    Right now the target's health bar is indeed dark red. You should be able to make it much more visible if you change the color of the background for the target box to something solid and light. Use the /wincolor command to accomplish this. You might want to use something like /wincolor target 100 100 100 4. This will make the box a sort of solid light gray. You can increase the first three numbers and the color will appear more white. The basics for the /wincolor command are this: "/wincolor" is the command name. The first three numbers are RGB color numbers. Try different ones to get some idea how they work (like 0 0 100 is dark blue). And the last digit is the transparency value. 4 is solid, 1 is very clear. If you type /wincolor in game with no parameters it will give you more information.

    I hope that helps. I don't know that we'll be changing the color of the health bar itself, since this feature has the same effect.

Ester The Tester hit the Quality Assurance area today and offered these comments:

    Timing Issues
    Email:
    [Quote:Any news on the "Spell recovery time not yet met!" bug? Playing both an enchanter and a Paladin, I happen to see this one quite a bit...]

    This deals with Mend, bandaging and any other issues that deal with timing. They are still working on fixing it completely.

    Resists
    This is my first one of these so bear with me!

    Email text:
    [Quote: This issue has come up before, but I never saw an answer. There is a widely held perception (especially in very powerful guilds) that resists over a certain amount (the amount cited varies, but usually above around 220) are considered to be more harmful than good. I have particular interest in this as a bard, as resists is one of the few things that I offer powerful guilds on big raids.
    As anecdotal evidence that this perception may be true, on a trakanon raid I recently attended, I was in a caster group. Four of us in the group had 210-250 poison resist with my songs up. The other two were testing this theory, and left theirs at around 180 with my songs. After Trakanon fell, we compared notes, and all four of us with 210+ PR got poisoned. The two that had the lower resists did NOT get poisoned.
    Obviously, this is a small sample size, and could just be strange luck, but it concerns me that it is a widely held perception, and would like a statement that it has been tested and is in fact false, or it's been tested, and true, and we're fixing it. :)
    Thank you for your time.
    Laluni Songhammer
    52 Bard of E'Ci. ]

    The perception is indeed false. The problem is that the saves against dragon weapons are different than the saves in the rest of the game. With a resist that would make you basically involunerable against a 'normal' (ie non dragon) MOB a dragon is going to be able to still hurt you.

    Most of the time people max out their resists only when fighting mobs of this level, so I understand where the perception comes from. However we have tested this and the stats and they do not roll over.

    Thanks!

    How This Works
    Hello everyone!

    As you have seen we have made some significant changes to these boards. Abashi has posted the changes and the reasons behind these changes.

    What does this change mean? Well it means that I and my team will be taking the emails that you send us and posting some of them along with answers and updates. It means that it will be significantly easier for you to find the answers to your questions. It means that the spam, flames and other messiness will no longer be here.

    You will not be able to post on this forum. When you fill out the form it sends an email to me. Please be polite and informative. I need locs, zones and all the information necessary for me to reproduce your bug.

    It also means that because I do not have to moderate a board I will have more time to answer questions. It is still not my primary responsibility, but I think that more questions will be answered and more information will be available.

    What we ask you to do is to read the posts to see if your question is already answered, and if it is not please use the form provided to email us. We will take the most commonly asked questions and post them and the answers. Absor is doing this to a much more extensive scale in another section.

    Please only send bug related questions. Anything development related or customer service related will not be answered. Also, anything vulgar or inappropriate sent could have consequences on your EverQuest account, so please be polite. Remember that I have to read these emails.

    We hope that this new format will let us communicate with you guys more clearly. By not having to dig through hundreds of posts we hope you will find the information you need more quickly and find that information more concise.

    Please do not be offended if your question is not answered. I or my team read each email but we do not have time to respond to every email.

    We are excited about this new system and look forward to answering your emails.

    Crashing Today
    If you are crashing because of this latest patch please be paitent. We are working (and have been since last night) on a fix.

    I will post more if we need more information, but we have isolated the problem and Rowyl is working on a solution

Gameplay Board Closed!
- Aurum @ 7:01 PM PST
A big announcement was posted by Alan VanCouvering this evening. Sony Online Entertainment has shutdown the Gameplay Board and have created a different solution for submitting feedback. In this post Alan gives his reasons for the change:

    Hello all.

    We've made some changes to the message boards, as you can see. The letter below is from the team.

    Alan


    ------------

    Hello all,

    Upon EverQuest’s release, Sony Online Entertainment, then a separate company, came to Verant Interactive and proposed that they set up and host community message boards on the EverQuest site. The purpose of these boards was to provide a place for EverQuest players to hang out, get to know each other, and basically do what EverQuest players do in any of dozens of message forums hosted by EverQuest fan sites.

    As they grew more popular, a culture of dedicated and knowledgeable players formed, and the forums quickly evolved into a place where game design concepts were discussed freely by a relatively small group of people. As a developer always open to new ideas, the boards did not go unnoticed by the EverQuest design team, and we began commenting on issues there regularly.

    As the weeks went on, some EverQuest players heard that the “Official” EverQuest forums were the place to go if you wanted to speak with the EverQuest Development Team. As issues were addressed, it was apparent that the boards were a phenomenal tool for collecting player feedback, listening to issues, and occasionally to release information.

    In late 1999, we decided to even further our commitment to communicate with players about development issues – a philosophy that remains true to this day. And it seemed the boards were the best place to do this. To that end, a full-time Community Relations Manager was hired to not only disseminate information to the public via the boards, but also to bring the public’s issues to the development team such that valid player issues would be addressed and not overlooked – the latter part being key.

    Fast-forward to today: Unfortunately, the EverQuest message boards are often a hotbed of exaggerated negativity where only through one-upmanship, melodramatics, and dauntless persistence can one hope to create a big enough thread surrounding an issue as to have it addressed. Posters with the opportunity and time to engage in these tactics at least get to see an Official tell them “Yes” or “No”, while others with equally valid stated concerns and the same desire to make the game better for everyone see their posts fall off within minutes, only to be overlooked or missed in the end. That, or they simply don’t post at all, avoiding the boards and the negativity found therein (we’ve received a lot of player feedback via email and at Fan Faires attesting to this phenomenon).

    Well, this is simply not acceptable. Our original goal for the boards was to have them serve as a place where valid player issues would be addressed and not overlooked. We need and want to hear the feedback of anyone willing to send it. You deserve to know that your feedback is being read, that your issues are being considered by the design team, and that the chance of having your thoughts heard is not tied to your ability to be outspoken and persistent in a public forum. That is one thing all of our goals have in common, and one thing that we need to improve such that we are able to give players the input and feedback they deserve – the current “public” process.

    To that end, we’re making a number of changes, both to the message boards and the procedures for sending feedback. First, you’ll notice that the Harpy’s Head and the Official Message Boards have been shut down. This is a temporary measure to allow people to time to read up on the new processes and rules in place prior to posting on official EverQuest forums. Some boards will be reopened in one-to-two weeks time under the new rules.

    The Harpy’s Head boards will reopen under their existing rules (in-character), but will require an active EverQuest account to post. The Wishlist, the Newbie Zone, and the Customer Service/Technical Help forums will also reopen at the same time, but will have very strict guidelines concerning the type of content that is allowed in those forums. Other former forums, including the server and class forums, will not reopen.

    Two new forums, “Developer’s Corner” and “Quality Assurance”, have been added. These forums will take the place of the former “General Discussion” and “Testing” forums. The difference here is that attempting to post a message or reply will take you to a special form where you can “Submit Comments” to the Development and Quality Assurance teams. Representatives from each team will read all of the comments submitted and choose several daily to which to reply publicly. Those representatives will then quote your comments if chosen in individual posts under the appropriate read-only forum. These forums will also be used for special announcements, inquiries, and the release of important information as it is appropriate. And we also hope it will be much easier for players to keep track of what is going on in EverQuest.

    It is very important to us that we are able to address the needs of our customers. It’s not only important from the perspective of our goal of providing good service, but also for our primary goal: To be the number one online gaming company in the industry. Player feedback is so important to this process that we are taking these steps in order to gather feedback from more people more effectively. We are committed to responding not only to more issues, but also to more issues of concern and interest to a broader range of players. Finally, we are committed to giving our playerbase more input into what we do, and more insight into how and why we are doing it.

    These changes are a sincere attempt to make all of that more possible. Please join us in ushering in a new age of communication and collaboration and visit the EverQuest Message Boards to submit your comments today.

    - The EverQuest Team
Obviously this is big news and your free to comment on it on our General Board. I'll have a new poll up later this evening for additional feedback.

Server-Side Filter Clarification
- Aurum @ 7:07 PM PST
A note clarifying a few issues surrounding the Server-Side Filter was posted this evening by Alan:

    The implementation and effect of these filters are actually rather simple. But I've done a less than adequate job of explaining them, so I'm going to try again.

    If you use the Chat Filter options in game to turn off OOC, Shout, Auction, Guild Chat, Damage Shields or any form of combat miss, this filtering will occur on the server automatically. This means that once you turn them off, these messages will not even be sent to your computer.

    All other messages will remain filtered on the Client Side (by your computer rather than the server) even if you turn them off, until you type /serverfilter OFF. When you do that then they will be filtered at the server. This may, however, prevent you from seeing certain spell particle effects and possibly some damage animations. /serverfilter ON will allow these messages to be sent to your computer once again.

    The advantage to filtering things on the server rather than at your computer is that it reduces the amount of data that needs to be sent to your computer. For people that don't have the best connections to the Internet, and/or for those in locations where a lot of data is being sent (such as in raid situations), server-side filtering should make the game more stable.

    Alan

    There's always been an "In Development" section, we just haven't done a very good job of keeping it up to date. Now thanks to our spiffy web-content team, we should be able to do a much better job.

    As for driving forces behind decisions we make, we don't sit down as a team, contemplate other games, and let said contemplation drive our decisions. We do what we do because we feel it's the best for the game and the customers.

  • Customer Support : More GM Issues
    I'm up right now so please allow me to answer.

    Inconsistancies in the rules are due mostly to the fact that many of these things require judgement calls on the part of the person witnessing the events. Any time you inject human judgement into an equation, things have the potential to become inconsistent. The fact is, the more we allow the GMs to step in for, the more inconsistent things become.

    However, to answer the question, the PvP changes we have in mind will also include addressing the current "rules" as they exist from a CS perspective. We even plan on posting them.

  • GMs, Events, Customer Service
    [Quote from original post: How about just HIRING TWO PEOPLE to ONLY perform the responsibility of being a GM and not a Customer Service GM !]

    The focus of the GM position is to provide customer service on the servers these days rather than implement game content such as the pen-and-paper counterpart, the DM. While they do help with these types of things from time to time, it's not part of their normal thing.

    For that, we have a team of Event Masters, and yet another team of Quest Masters. They are responsible for injecting dynamic content into the world. The Event Masters have been holding the Best of the Best and Test of Tactics type tournements and other such things on the servers, while the Quest Masters are responsible for historic quests and persistent characters within the world.

    Essentially, your suggestions are already implemented. We are however working to further organize all of the "behind the scene" type stuff to get more in game.

  • Customer Serivce: Account Problem
    Unfortunately it's the same story we hear over and over gain on a daily basis: Account stolen by someone in the guild with the true account owner. The ONLY time items are ever replaced in this case in when we can tell where the items went and can get them back. We won't inject new items into the economy due to someone being careless with their account information.

    I indeed wish that there was some way to get people to stop giving out their account information. Accounts don't just "get hacked". They only "get hacked" when the owner gets a little careless. Hold on to those account names and passwords like they were your ATM card and PIN number.
