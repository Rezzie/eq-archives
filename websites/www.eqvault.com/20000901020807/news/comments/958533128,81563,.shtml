I have to say, when I initially started playing my character(paladin) it was fun. After a short time, I became extremely disheartened, felt I couldn't get anywhere and created other characters to try out.

After some time i had reached a few walls with those as well and went back playing my paladin. It was then, with a new focus, and obviously a lot of time spent, that I began to truly attempt at understanding the class.

I believe it is well for an individual to find a class that fits ones personality, then to find that respective class' skills, limitations, and play within those as guidelines. Then to take things a step further, to take your class to the limits and see just what you CAN do with that character. Practice different techniques and strategies, learn what works and doesn't.

I know this sounds like a lot of time, but if you intend to play a character upto 60.....well, it would be wise to learn the character you're playing =) No need to rush, learning is part of the fun.

The absolute most important thing in my opinion is to have fun. I love my paladin, its an awesome class, its suits my personality and play style best, and I play him well.

Alot of individuals complain about nerfs and class limitations, and I find some of it with due reason, but most of it to be because people want things a particular way, as opposed to learning the system and living within those means.

All in all, have fun with your character. If you don't like it, try others till you find which suits you best.
