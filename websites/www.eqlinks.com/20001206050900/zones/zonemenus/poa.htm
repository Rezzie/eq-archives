  • The keys range in price from:
    420 - 500pp for the Key of Swords.
    210 - 250pp for the Key of Misplaced.
  • Every player must purchase their own set of keys. Some portals lead to other portals, and without both keys you get stuck fast.
  • If you are patient however, and not wanting to waste money, you can kill the Thunder Princesses on the starting isle and turn in a small sword they drop to the Keymaster for Keys. (Info courtesy of Genma Stonedreamer)
  • Not all mobs are KOS. In fact many are indifferent.
  • Not many new animations. New monsters include: Bladestorms, Spirocs, Gorgalasks, Windrider Drakes, Bzzazzts, Greater Sphinxes, Azaracks, Essence Harvesters and Soul Carriers. There's also a range of drakes, elementals, harpies and the like.
  • Boss Mobs include the Spiroc Guardian, the Sister of the Spire, and supposedly Veeshan (I couldn't find her).
  • NPC's range from level 49 up.
  • There are also some quests available within the zone, some of the NPC's will talk with you (details on these to come).
  • You cannot duel on the Keymaster Island or the Thunder Princesses will smack you down hard and fast.
  • The Keys dissapear when you zone out.
  • Some of the islands have strange mechanical devices whose purpose is currently unknown.

SKY PLANE QUESTS

We have just finished the marathon haul and now have the Plane of Sky Quest Area online.

Loot in the Plane of Sky is unlike any other zone. To obtain an item you must undertake a test. Then you must go forth and kill mobs until you obtain the items for a given quest. Some of the items do not last if you leave the zone. However, almost every mob drops at least one item. Don't be misled though, killing just one mob isn't easy, let alone all of the mobs required to obtain your items.

We have a complete list of the current working quests in the Plane of Sky, which will be constantly updated to keep them accurate. You can also add comments on the quests as you discover information on them to help enable you pass your tests of class supremacy.

Thanks to Gendal Concorden who helped fill in some gaps we had in our lists.
