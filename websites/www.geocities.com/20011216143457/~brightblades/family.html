Basically, I'm about as happy as one can be. The only complaint I have is that Brooke won't let me play on the computer as much as I want to. But then, who can blame her.

A little more about us. We met in Dayton, Ohio, in January of 1990. Brooke was working at Chi Chi's Family Resteraunt, and I was stationed at Wright-Patterson AFB. We got married on September 19th, 1992. Since Wright-Patt, we've lived in Del Rio, Texas, at Laughlin AFB; Merced, California, at Castle AFB; in Wichita, Kansas, at McConnell AFB; and we now live in Bellevue, Nebraska, at offutt AFB. Our daugter Megan was born on June 25th, 1996. Brooke used to work as a receptionist and insurance claims representative for eye doctors here in Wichita (and in Dayton), but now she's a stay at home mom (and if you ask me, that's tougher than working). We figured out it would cost us more for childcare than she would make. Besides, we want to raise our children instead of a stranger, or babysitter.

I just got stationed at Offutt AFB, in Omaha, Nebraska. Megan is starting pre-kindergarten now at St. Mary's School. Brooke also got a part time job there as a school secretary, so that works out great for Megan. The cool thing about Megan going to that school is that she's already getting a great education. We're not even catholic!

Poor little Megan has bad allergies though. This year she is actually going to kindergarten, but we decided to send her to the local Public School instead. The air quality there is much better than it was at St. mary's (i.e. they actually have an air handling system!). It's amazing all the things Megan is learning! She is showing a real interest in reading now, and is already sounding out words all over the place. Brooke stopped working also. It was interferring too much with our lives, and certain people drove her away (long story). Brooke will be taking classes starting in January at Bellevue University. She's excited about that. Seems that all of us are persuing education at one level or another.

Well, I'll keep updating this as things happen. Check back often, and if I have the chance, I'll see about posting more pictures.
