My Links


Links, glorious links. Don't get lost.

You might want to bookmark my homepage in case you get lost.

Here are a bunch of links to other peoples pages, and to pages on the web we thought were cool. Most of these links are about swords, fantasy, role-playing games, the SCA, and... Well, you get the picture. Some of them have links back to me, but some of them don't. Feel free to surf the web, and hopefully you'll find some other things of interest. So far I have given a top recomendation to four sites. I will limit myself to five Top Recomendations. These will be what I consider the five best sites on the entire World Wide Web about swords (even my own site doesn't meet the criteria for this!).

  • The Highlander Sword Shopper's Guide
    This is a FANTASTIC page. Even if you aren't a Highlander fan, it has a ton of really good information on what to look for when you buy a sword. It also talks about the different types of metals used in swords that are out there. Receives my top recomendation!

  • The Mediæval Sword Resource Site
    This is a FANTASTIC page. A noncommercial site that has a ton of information for the collector, sword buff, or just about anyone with questions about swords. Also receives my top recomendation!

  • Sword Forum Magazine Online
    This is a FANTASTIC page. Brought to you by the same folks who brought you the Highlander Sword Shopper's Guide. Except here you can post messages and ask questions about swords. It features a discussion forum where industry leading custom swordsmiths meet they've got some really big names on there. Tell them I sent you. Also receives my top recomendation!

  • The Japanese Sword Index.
    This site will tell you just about anything you've ever wanted to know about Japanese swords. This site also receives my top recomendation!

  • NetSword: The Internet Sword and Medieval Weapons Discussion Group
    Another forum site. These guys have 9 forums though, and they not only discuss weapons and swords, but also talk about history and such. Deffinetly worth a look.

  • Old Swords
    A site offering many valuable resources for tracking down the history of a sword (I get a lot of e-mails asking me about the history of a sword, and I'm just a hobbyist, so this is what you need). Use the site and sign up for the services.

  • Rotating Sword GIF courtesy of Vialardi di Sandigliano Foundation. Thank you!

  • Knife Outlet - Knives, Swords, Kitchen Cutlery, Sharpeners, and more - A commercial site that traded links with me. I like what they offer.

  • C.A.S. Iberia
    One of my former distributors. Since they have a web page, I thought it would be appropriate to link to them. You might want to visit them in case they've posted a new product that I haven't been informed of.

  • The Historical Armed Combat Association
    These people do "live steel" and combat the way it really was in those days. If you want to learn more, please give this site a visit.

  • The Society for Creative Anachronism
    This organization also teaches about life the way it used to be, but also focuses on other aspects of life aside from combat. The combat is more simulated than with the HACA. Still worth a look.

  • Babes with Blades
    With a title like that, You just have to go to this page. I haven't seen any of their shows personally, but from everything I've heard, I think I'm missing out on a great show. Look them up on the net and in real life. Well worth the time!

  • Custom PRG Art
    This guy makes custom pictures of your RPG character. Reasonable prices, and he's good too. Really adds that extra dimension to your character.

  • Empire of Chivalry and Steel ~ Flumen Borealis.
    An SCA type site of rich detail and chock full of information. It also introduces you to a whole new system of ranking combat and such. Check it out.

  • Illusion Armoring
    Illusion Armoring are the people who made my SCA legal suit. I love it, and I'm sure they wouldn't mind hearing from you.

  • The Renaissance Faire Junkie
