Uhh..NO!
First off, AC does not cap at 1000. I know it looks like that, but I know several 55+ warriors who hit 1100 AC with buffs.

Second, you will not hit 1000 ac with crafted.. at least not at level 52 anyway... my 52 warrior with some indicolite, cobalt, and rare armor pieces usually ends up around 950ac while fighting. And thats with nothing on him that doesnt have AC.

In regard to the original question the reason warriors duel wield at high levels instead of using a shield is simple. Duel wield kicks in so often that you will do over 50% extra damage while duel wielding versus a 1hs weapon only.

Killing the mobs that much faster reduces the number of times you would get hit by them. The 25 extra ac from the shield wouldnt compensate for the increased exposure time to the mob hitting you.

When you get to the planes and the mobs are hitting for 135+ you want them dead as fast as possible. Plus you wouldnt taunt worth a damn using only a 1hs weapon and a shield.

Ok first off AC can go over 1000. Its hard but it can be done. Second crafted does not even get close to 1k ac. Full crafted, on a warrior with maxed defense is about 800. That does not take into account other armor slots that crafted does not provide a piece for (neck, back, belt, etc.)

Second high level cleric, shammy, and druid buffs do give a nice AC boost. However at high levels a 25ac shield is really about 40 ac or so once the adjustment goes into effect. I have the warrior Fear Plane shield (ac25, +5 str/sta) It really boosts my ac up. It sits in the bank though. Why?

It can be argued that with say a Wurmslayer and a shield, a warrior can still pump out decent damage. And from a numbers standpoint that is correct. However it seriously effects the ability to taunt and total damage output.

There was a old philosophy that melee does not do that much damage compared to casters. That was true a year ago when most people where in their 30-40s and Kunark and it's amazing weapons did not exist.

The game has changed since then. At higher levels melee does a LOT of damage. Monks and rogues beat out warriors in damage output, but not a by a lot. At 49 I do the same damage the Shaman 49 dot Ebolt does in the same amount of time. 1400 damage in 1 minute. That is serious damage that will only get better as I upgrade my weapons and haste items, increase my skills, and get disciplines. yes a wizzie can outdamage me for the first 90 seconds.. then he goes OOM and I keep doing constant damage.

It has been stated by the 55+ crowd that melee do the bulk of damage in a fight.

And then take the taunt factor into account. The hate list appears to work based on the speed of attacks as much or more than the damage per hit. A warrior needs to dual wield to do that sort of speed damage. There are high end 2hd weapons that probably do outdamage the best one handers. But they do not work as taunts as well as the one handers. Hence, warriors use the one handers to keep taunt working.

So yes, a shield would help. But at the expense of damage output and the ability to taunt. Perhaps in some situations a shield would actually be worth the output lose. Not sure what those situations would be though.
