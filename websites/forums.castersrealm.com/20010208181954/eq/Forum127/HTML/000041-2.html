Finding Polzin is not difficult. Basically you enter the first tower and keep going down. This is the Hole right. When you first enter tower you'll encounter some resistance. After the first series of stiars you'll come to a wide open room with a building in the middle. Fast spawn here. Kill/Clear move quickly. Go to entrance on other side of this room and go down next set of stairs. This will put you in front of the city entrance. From here you just fight striaght back to the shop where Polzin resides. Just move opposite the city entrance till you get to him. Note: One of the component requirements if off a ghost deeper in Hole and this is where my warning applies.If you get to Polzin you might as well go all the way if you got a good group. Dont go further down unless you have two groups. Its easy to get peeps for this since the paladin and shaman epic quests require components from same location. From Polzin I believe you head west to get to the final tunnel and further down. Once you get close to the undead building beware.

I have no doubt in my mind that his info has a huge clue in it; for the life of me, I can't discern it...

'The lost scroll contains the magical incantation I need to help you out. It was penned ages ago by the Elves. It was lost along with its protector during one of the raids on the Outpost in Firiona. I fear it may be lost for good.'

There's a number of "possible" subtle clues in that sentence. He uses penned as opposed to written or scribed. Literally taken, it could mean the scroll ITSELF was put into some kind of protective device like a pen or vault. Lost along with it's protector. This could simply mean the location of the scroll, was "lost". Lost doesn't always mean lost in the world. Lost can mean lost to outside forces. Also note he uses lost twice, maybe emphasizing this? I fear it may be lost for good. Could the clue or scroll be in Fear plane? Small chance, but who knows. Lost for good. This could mean lost FOR good, ie. never coming back or being found. It can also denote the people losing it were "good", ie. he could have said "I fear it may be lost for evil."

Anyways, most of Mizzle's items have required some ingenuity (enlightenment, the moment when you find the item and go "AH HA. Well, DUH, of course it's here!"), and in all the cases, the item's location or carrier was hinted at in the information. Things to ponder...
