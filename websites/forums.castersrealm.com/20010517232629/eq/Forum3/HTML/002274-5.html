The way I see it, the proposed change to this breastplate is a HUGE step in the way Verant is choosing to deal with changes in general.

My first reaction when I started reading this thread was: "Oh great, they're going to give the breastplate a 10 minute recast time." By my way of thinking, that means that they essentially get ONE free Complete Heal every ten minutes. Now, my cleric is only level 22, so I'm in no danger of getting complete heal OR this breastplate for a long time, but it would seem to me that a cleric with Clarity (II) would recover mana fast enough to cast multiple (ie: more than one) CH's in any ten minute period. Thus, changing the breastplate to allow only one cast in 10 minutes would really be a slap in the face and be, in my limited opinion, a fairly significant downgrade of the ability.

Instead, much to my surprise, they proposed an intelligent and decent fix. Leave the recast delay the same, and make it so you can only cast it on any single player once in the refresh period. Heck, they're even talking about adding a buff effect (regen) to the casting, and have stated that it will stack with everything (okay, granted, it will probably take two tries to get it right, but it's the thought that counts). This means that the cleric can continue to play as normal and have "an ace card" for when there is a bad pull or whatever. A free complete heal for the enchanter when things get messy. A free complete heal for the ranger during an extended fight. A free complete heal for himself without having to sacrifice group mana pool to do it. All only 30 seconds apart.

I think that is a pretty awesome suggestion, as opposed to the normal Verant modus operandi which is err on the side of broken to prevent things from working too powerfully.

No, I'm not a level 60 cleric who has had this breastplate for 10 levels. I've never even seen one. Yes, it is a shame that it has to be changed at all and that it wasn't caught before it was put in the game. But yes, I personally feel it is a reasonable solution. Would I feel different if I had one and was facing the possibility of it changing? Probably, but this option is better than the alternatives.
