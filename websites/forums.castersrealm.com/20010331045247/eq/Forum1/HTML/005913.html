If people do not comment (complain as you put it) on how they perceive their class, then how do you know something is wrong?

Verant (actually Brad himself) had recently stated that they will never find all bugs in testing before they go to the live servers, and that the player base is used to find as much of the major bugs as possible.

Although this is unfortunate, it is a reality of playing video games. There will always be bugs, and the more content, items and abilities you introduce, the more bugs you will have.

I have played alot of different classes in Everquest to reasonable levels, and I currently play a Wizard.

He is level 34, since last night, but I do read the boards for most of the other classes too. In regards to balancing the game, Wizards are fine pretty much until they take a part in the post 50 (i.e.-Planes and Vellious, etc.) game.

To rephrase what a forgotten poster stated on the EQ message boards I was reading earlier...

The spell resistance level of Vellious mobs is so high that Wizards truly bring very little compared to any melee class to the table with regard to the post 50 game.

This is because Verant has made the game revolve around the amount of high level melee equipment currently in the game. For instance, a melee class can equip themselves with the latest gear from Vellious and the planes, and make a huge difference in the group/raid with that equipment. However, because of the soft cap on int, pure casters especially Wizards, have no way besides mana items (which won't add more than maybe 1 cast Max of your best spell) to add to their effectiveness after they get 200 int.

There are no items in the game that grant faster mana regeneration that will stack with other mana regen spells/songs. (except one new robe dropping rarely off of Cazic Thule, who just got a huge upgrade) There are no items that will increase your casting time, your recast time, your fizzle rates, or the amount of mana used to cast spells.

Wizards have no way of denting the MASSIVE amounts of HPs that Vellious mobs have and are not needed, except to teleport people around.

Because of this, the game is all about melee now, and the casters who support them (Clerics, Shamans, Enchanters).

Until this changes, Wizards, Mages and Necros will have little to do on large raids, and that is where the post 50 game is.

Once a Wizard reaches 200 int, why would they need anything else?

This is why Wizards aree upset at least, and I cant really speak for anyone else...
