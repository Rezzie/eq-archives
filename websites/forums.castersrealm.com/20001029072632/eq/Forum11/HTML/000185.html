I got through 35 FAST in cazic thule. Try the SR room (side room). Plenty of stuff in there is blue to a 35. The pyramid thingy there(it's been a dozen levels) can also give you exp there at 35 and higher.

But at 35, the overthere is great for multi-kiting, which is ANYTHING but boring. In fact, it's quite terrifying. Be sure you have decent wisdom for your level, but you don't at all need to be twinked. I wasn't even close to twinked. Use lightning strike to hit four snared mobs at once. Run them close to each other so they're overlapping. They should all be the same speed mobs -- chickens and cacti are the same speed; start with them. You have to be very precise with this so it's a BIG challenge, but you can see your experience bar jump at level 35 or over when doing this. Making one run of four and medding up to the next will take about 20 minutes. You can get a level a day and have fun doing it. You can do the same thing in fironia vie. You can also try the Crypt of Dalnir off of Warsliks Woods in Kunark. A coveted drop there is the fighting baton, which every monk in the entire game wants. Some people sell them for 1200pp, but I've sold four for 1500pp no problem at all. There is simply no other weapon of a comparable level that a monk wants, and every monk of every level can use it -- can use two, in fact, since it's a 1-hand blunt. The only upgrades to this weapon cost a fortune.

The giant fort in the Frontier Mountains in Kunark is some of the best fun I've had. That's where I was from 35 to about 37, but I could have stayed longer if I wanted to. This is a GREAT loot zone. Much of the loot is lore, so you can only get say one hammer of flattening or legchopper sword per person, but as a druid, you can just port out, sell, and come back. You can easily make 100's of plat a day here. If you're lucky, you can get yourself a forest loop earring off the giants (random off any of them), a plus 3 wisdom plus 3 dex plus 3 agi earring that beats the heck out of your jeweler-made ones, if you can afford not to sell them for 1000pp to 1500pp depending on your server. The agi stat helps you get hit less, always a plus.

Also, giants run all over dreadlands and frontier mountains. You can solo the weak ones while waiting for a group. And they are very fun to kill. Big, dopey, quick-spawning, and they have a very low aggro range. Run for a while on SOW and they forget you were there. Come back later in safety. Make lots of money while you're doing it and have fun grouping (which most druids don't do nearly enough).

So whether you want to solo for the best experience any class in the game gets by multi-kiting with lightning strike or want to solo or group for giants in frontier mountains at the giant fort, you have very fun and challenging options.

It sure doesn't hurt that now that you've hit 35, you can watch all the action while medding instead of staring into a spell book. Even your down time is comparatively fun now! 35 isn't a level to be bored with, I think -- it's a level to be thrilled with; and your 34 and 39 spells are what finally starts to distinguish you as a druid. You're coming into your own now!
