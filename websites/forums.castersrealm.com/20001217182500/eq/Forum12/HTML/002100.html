For my shaman, I ditched 1HB, and only use piercing and 2HB. Most of the good shaman weapons, and the epic weapon, are piercing, so thats a must. I use 2HB because there are some 2HB weapons that can do some massive damage, which I find fun every once in awhile. Also, there are some good 2HB weapons for shamans, but they are hard to get(mortificators staff is one that comes to mind).

3 Weapon skills are kinda hard to keep up with for a priest class, so stick with 2 or 1. Alot of the 1HB weapons suck compared to some of the piercers. The only reason I can see using 1HB, is that you can get a screaming mace or cold iron morning star fairly early on in your life, where as a fine steel spear will be the best piercer you can get til you are 25+ (assuming you started from scratch).

I play on the new server with my shaman and I current have a couple pieces of banded, a FS spear and Runed Totem Staff (2HB), which are great weapons for a level 12, and also cheap.

Delirium,

If you're a barb shammy, there's a great newbie quest that you can easily do (at your level) that'll net you the Initiate Tribunal necklace. It's a +1 WIS, +3 Mana necklace that has unlimited charges of Hammer of Wrath. So you'll get free magic 1hb weapons forever. Plus it's a light source!

You'll need 2 items in Halas: mammoth hide parchment from Cindl, and ink from Greta. Give these to Jinkus, and you'll get something from him. Take this to either of the guards outside of the bank in South Qeynos. You'll get a most-wanted poster in exchange. Return this to Jinkus to get your reward. I suggest you do this after you get SoW, it'll be much faster. And get yourself binded to either Halas or Qeynos in order to reduce the number of trips.
http://eqdb.allakhazam.com/quest.html?quest=749

Right click on the Tribunal Initiate Symbol necklace after it's been equipped. You'll see your character start casting. Once done your cursor turns into a hammer, meaning basically you just "picked" up a hammer. This hammer is magical, no-rent, no-drop, and also I think lore (can't be sure on the last part). So what it means is that it can hit mobs that can only be hit with magical weapons [ie willowisps], and because it's "no-rent" means it'll last as long as your current session. Once you camp and log out the weapon's gone. No biggie... You have unlimited charges anyway! The only unfortunate thing is that it's no-drop, which means you can't give it to anyone else.

Piercing, 2HB and 1HB in that order of priority. Prod is a great weapon for many levels. So's an Ogre War Maul or Midnight Mallet.

This is a very good thread--lots of interesting insights have been posted.

Kudos to all posters here!

My experience as a shaman:

Started with EB Mace and Charred Guardian Shield. Great combo but no wis with weapon.

At lvl 12 I looted the Iksar Beserker Club on a guild raid. OMG--this weapon is awesome. Easily the best 1hb you can have for a Shaman.

I ended up selling the IBC at 24 and got myself a Jade Chokidia Prod. A little more wisdom is added but not near the weapon the IBC is.

Why then did I do this? Simple, because the Shaman Uber Weapon is a piercing weapon and the fact that as Iksar I will probably not melee too much cause I dont have the HP for it. I do not miss the IBC too much as I hardly ever melee anymore so any greater wisdom enhancing item you can have is better in my opinion.

There are some great weapons out there for 1hb, 2hb, and piercing. However I suggest maxing out piercing simply cause this is the uber-weapon for shamans and the fact that there are plenty of nice shaman piercing weapons to be had.

In the long run, many a shaman are very effective with any of the skills used. In the upper levels--especially after level 34--you will no longer need to melee as you will have a wolf pet for soloing and your spells do much more damage than you could melee for, or you will be group healer, buffer, DoTer, or nuker.

The Shaman is a multi-functional character and this brings up the problem of what to use and how to use it. Don't worry too much about that as you will find what you are comfortable with as you gain in level.
