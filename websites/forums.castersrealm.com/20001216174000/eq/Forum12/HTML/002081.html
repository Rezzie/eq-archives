I made the run from Grobb to Neriak many times without any assistance, its quite simple. When running at level 1, everything is red.

Once you come out of Grobb, turn left and keep on the wall. There is a 30ish bard near a pool, will have to run around him. Just keep an eye out for kobolds and frogs, they dont frequent the wall on this side.

Follow the wall past the Feerrott zone and contine to the SRo zone. This is longer than running past Guk, but you wont make it that way.

Once in SRo turn right (east) and follow the wall until you loc counter reaches zero. This will make you run through both the druid and wizard circles, there are no orc camps if you follow this line north.

Once you hit the sand turn west and run along the edge of the sand to the wall then follow the wall all the way north and just keep an eye out for anything close to the wall, there rarely ever is anything there that will auto attack you. Once you hit the north wall turn east again and enter the gap into Oasis.

When entering Oasis you have to look out as sometimes groups hunt spectres near the zone. If its clear then run to the huts near the lake when the coast is clear, follow the lake around until you see the coast line and head for the coast. Follow the coast all the way north to Nro.

Madmen and Dry Bones alost never spawn or wander too close to the water, there are usually so many players in the zone that they dont hang around either.

When doing the run with a level 1 I take the longer safe route. That is follow the coast up to the barbarian fishing village (dont get too close) then turn west and follow the sand/grass border all the way west to the wall, you then only have a short run to the Ro tunnel minimising the chance of running into a shirveled or crypt mummy. From this side you view of the tunnel is not obscured too much either and you can see any that happen to be close to the entrance.

When in East Commonlands as a Troll both the militia and knightsoftruth are usually kos to you so running straight across from the tunnel to nektulos risks you coming in contact with Sergeant Slate (also known as Sergeant Slaughter for the low level players). If your kos to him he has a decent agro radius, not so bad if he is only threatening.

I turn right from the tunnel and head towards Freeport, run on the grass side as skeletons, pumas and elementals can spawn in the sand side which will all auto-attack you. Once you hit the sandy part which is the zone to Freeport then turn north and follow the sand to the opposite wall, you then turn west and have a clear run to the neriak zone.

The only thing that might spawn close by infrequently are black bears, but you will be able to see them well in advance and move around them.

Once in nektulos you have two options. Either follow the road to the bridge and dodge wandering bears, skeletons and kodiaks or wait for the wandering guard to come to the zone and follow him back to the bridge. Same thing on the other side, follow a wandering guard that heads from the bridge down to the Lavastorm zone. From there you just turn right and follow the wall to the entrance of Neriak, no mobs spawn between Lavastorm and Neriak.

I play lots of Trolls I have at least one of each SK, Warrior and naturally Shaman and have done this run in both direction many, many times usually without incident. The run is easy during peak times when mob counts are low.

The most dangerous is the run through Sro, do an /ooc and see if there is a group hunting the Ancient Cyclops, if so they will be slaughtering all the mobs in the desert side which is the place you are most likely to die if you get caught.

Mobs to dodge at all costs. Casting Madmen and Dry Bones, even when sowed their channel skill is so high they can pretty much almost run and cast at the same time. The madman which has druidic spells can root and cast careless lightning which does around 100hp a pop.

The run from Halas to Oasis is relatively routine even at level 9 with sow if you have a pretty good knowledge of the zones. If you dont know them well then i wouldnt try it. And i wouldnt run through HHP and Kithicor as a level 9. I'd turn into SK and go through Feerrott since the guards are easy to avoid if you know the zone well.

My halfings that run from Freeport to Rivervale frequently can vouch for the frequency of undead that wander close to the wall. If its a caster then its game over.
