Here's the deal with Agility and Dex (no spiffy graphs ). Agility is your EVASIVENESS with a given armor. It's a lot like strength in that only the "10" boundaries are the breaking points for noticeable changes. You most definitely do NOT want a melee class with an Agility below 75. The Agility stat is really how it is feasable that Halflings can be just as competent at "tanking" as Ogres/Trolls/Barbarians who all have a MUCH higher Stamina. The reason being the Large races have higher Stamina (higher hp), but the Halfling (or gnome/Wood Elf/High Elf/Dark Elf) compensates their LACK of Hit points by EVADING more attacks. That means that you will see the skills dodge/parry/riposte go off more often, and that way you actually avoid taking damage in the first place.

Dex on the otherhand, you will only see making a real difference as you begin to consistently use proccing weapons. Dex affects proc rate, and therefor, the extra damage or special ability of the weapon itself becomes part of your total damage out put. Make sense?

Okay, here is the deal with AGI and DEX. Straight from Verant, posted on the boards a long time ago, actually.

Agility affects the following:
a. A small direct bonus to AC (read: ac plummets if agi falls below 75, thus bad idea)
b. Affects speed of increase in skill level of defensive skills; including dodge, riposte, block, parry, disarm (I think), and a couple of others I probably forgot.

***it does NOT affect the chance for any of these skills to go off in combat! That is a random factor based on skill level and mob level***

Dex affects the following:
a. Chance to proc a proc'ing weapon, dependant also on the individual weapon.
b. Chance to score a critical hit or crippling blow, also calculated off the mob stats
c. Speed of increase of several combat-related skills: Dual wield, backstab, the special monk attacks (round kick, flying kick, etc) and most of the rogue-specific abilities like pickocket and disarm traps.

***Another popular misconception is that DEX affects chance to hit. It does not. Chance to hit is based upon a bunch of things--mostly, offense skill, weapon skill, and mob level and stats.***

I think what's largely being overlooked here is the *cough* magic number for ATK.

Nobody disputes that a Higher AC results in you taking less damage from mobs. Let's assume we're talking about very high level mobs for the sake of this argument.

If you're meleeing Krup Knights in sebilis and you are standing there with your 600AC naked and someone AC buffs you to 620AC it doesn't help for spit. You get more buffs to AC 650 and you're still taking full damage on nearly every single swing these guys dish out. You will notice, with great variance, from monster type to monster type there is a magic number with AC.

When you AC crosses over this threshhold you suddenly start taking dramatically less damage. I've often seen this with ranger tanks who do fine until we get up against a very potent monster and his 50-100pt AC deficit compared to the warriors suddenly has him sponging huge amounts of damage.

I'm convinced there is a very critical ATK vs AC comparison that determines which table/chart is checked for damage distribution.

I think when most str buff tests are done we're talking about people fighting creatures that are multiple levels blue to them and where their ATK score is significantly above the mobs AC.

Here's my theory on the STR buff debate:

Against very tough (for your level) mobs str gear and str buffs allow you to get a situation of ATK > AC and do consistant damage as charted by Ruathi.

If your STR is low enough to cause a ATK < AC situation I think you get a significantly worse distribution on damage.

Ever try attacking a very red mob with a low level character? You score 1pt hits and complete misses like mad.

So maybe STR buffs are only important fighting yellows/low reds/dragons/gods etc. I'd really appreciate it if you'd run my theory through the number grinder Ruathi.
