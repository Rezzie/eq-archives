I got news for yall about compairing monks and warriors:

Given equivelent or slightly worse equipment a monk will outdamage a warrior guaranteed. No contest. If you don't believe me just find a monk your level warriors and duel him/her. Be sure your both using weapons that are close to the same in power.

I can say this from personal experience: I grouped with a monk with some good gear. He had higher AC and Attack than me when we were on equal levels. His AC was significantly higher and so was his attack. The only reason that his hitpoints went down faster or may have seemed to is because he had less hitpoints than I did. The equipment I was wearing btw...was my class quest armor...yeah crafted. And I had the +5AC +55HP rings and some other decent stuffs like +3ac earrings
Coller of undead prot.(+5AC), +5AC face piece. In general I had real good AC gear. Best you can get without spending a lot of PP or a lot of time. Oh I had the Cloak of shadows too btw. Which I am borrowing from a friend. AC 12 back slot...very nice. The monk I was with had this too.

I personally feel that warriors get the shaft when compaired to monks. That is if you have some good AC gear on your monk. Monks will hands down always do better damage than warriors. If you don't believe me ask monks and warriors in game and go look at the weapon damage ratios under monk and warrior on this sight. See for yourself. Furthermore
Monks can FD pull which is a must at higher levels for some situations or is at least really nice and the prefered way to pull in many situations. Especially if you don't have an enchanter and you have a monk with some good AC/HP gear to do the FD pulling. I maintain that the only real bonus that a warrior gets is more Hitpoints!!(yeah and the Magic Resistance too, it can come in handy, but its not that crazy of a difference. Its not going to make or break anything) If not for that fact, the warrior class would be lost completely. Though I must admit that warriors can eventually get better AC than monks can. But this requires a lot of questing and prolly planes armor to accomplish a significant AC advantage over a monk.

Furthermore, on a raid and in groups more than a few warriors is a big waste in efficiency becuause a mob can only beat on one meatshield at a time. Yes if/when one of the warriors gets killed you need another warrior to take over the punishment takin, but thats the only reason you would need more than one warrior. Where as because of monks damage dealing ability, more monks in a group is must less wastefull.

That was long and perhaps not as well put together as I could have done. All that said Warriors do have their place. As follows: (With damage taking and pulling as the top functions)
1.) Taking damage
2.) Keeping mobs off other fragile party members. (course the other members should do their best to never get the mobs attention off of the tank in the first place, but...)
3.) Pulling mobs to the group.
4.) Dealing damage to the mobs. (Keep in mind that as a warrior you will NEVER out damage a rogue or monk, with equivalent gear. Provided that the rogue can stab at the soft backside of the target. Also due to the new proc spells that rangers will be getting, a warrior will NEVER be able to outdamage a ranger either if the ranger is using these spells and the warrior and ranger have equivalent gear.)

*in this case equivelent gear means gear that someone of that class would be expected to have at that level and or with weapons that have the same dam/delay ratio.* (for those of you who want some kind of definition of equivelent gear.)

Ultimately Warriors are HIGHLY dependent on other classes. For example: Healing, Fast Transportation (ie: teleports), Killing anything (once your higher level anyway, and provided that you don't have crazy soloing gear for a warrior, which would be really expensive).

So before you go and play a warrior understand what your getting into. Make an informed decision.

At mid to upper levels, monks are always going to be out damaging warriors. They can also feign death. However, what alot of people dont seem to grasp when comparing the two is that warriors are going to be able to take ALOT more punishment than a monk. Monks will get hit less than a warrior, but that doesnt mean as much as youd think. A warrior will be getting hit for less and will have much more hit points than a monk of similar level. Second, monks dont have taunt. While they dont really need it, their attacks do their taunting for them, even their high damage output isnt always enough to get a mob off a caster that is near death in time for them to survive(and they may get into trouble even if they do get the mob off the caster). Basicaly, a warrior is their to soak up damage and keep the mobs focused on them or other tanks, while a monk is there to do damage and to pull useing FD. Two pure melee classes that are completely different is what sums it up best.
