I for one always laugh when I see 4 or 5 people in the same zone all shouting LFG. What to do, is instead of waiting for an invite, simply form your own. Pre-50, mage and any other caster class make a NASTY combination. Pre-50 we can outdamage pretty much anyone, short or long term.

Post 50 we lose a little of our luster, and end up changing our role in a group a little bit, but in my experience, are still a highly wanted class in xp adventuring. We're still a very potent class at high levels, but have a bit of trouble fitting into the melee/cleric combo necessary for uber-encounters.

There are very few true newbies in the game, so most of the people you'll run into are probably twinked a little bit, but not everyone is in a rush for the high levels. Just have to find the people with similar playstyles.

Research can be a bit of a pain yes. Best idea is to find a mage 4 or 5 levels above you and ask them if they have any extra components or spells for sale. Ask but not bother. =P

If your time is short, then a mage is definitely worth continuing. A mage's power is incredible for many many levels and can be very fun along the way, especially if you take your time to make good friends, see the world, and not get frustrated over the little things.

Wow great replies ... I couldn't agree more with Nyalria. Up to this point I have never had much trouble finding a pickup group. Usually the only time I don't get an invite to a group that needs another member is when that group has a specific need (usually healer/enchanter/tank). If a group has one of those needs then you usually won't want to be in it anyways ... it's just asking for trouble.

We add alot of damage and some utility to a group's makeup. Our role from a damage standpoint is usually pretty simple ... /assist puller or main tank ... send in pet and nuke a bit. But when there is an Enchanter in the group, that's when a good Mage really shines. Things like holding the pet out of a fight until the Enchanter gets the room controlled, hitting the /pet back off key when a MOB breaks mez and runs over and whacks you (wait until the Enchanter re-mezzes it of course ), and coordinating with your other group members a stun on a MOB that gets on your pet so you can back the pet off allowing the Enchanter to mez it.

These are just a few of many examples, but playing your class well (ie handling your pet) will get you invited in groups quite often. Sure there are alot of greedy powerlevelers out there, just ignore them if you must. There are still plenty of people out there looking for a good group and a fun time.

If they have an opening (when not searching for chanter/cleric/shaman), I'm usually accepted into their group. Who knows if I am doing the most dmg, or such, I just DS the tanks, send pet in, nuke a couple times if need be, rinse and repeat.
