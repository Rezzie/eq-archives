It comes down to this:

Mages mana is only efficient (post 50, or maybe post 55) in the use of pets, damage shields, and malise (up to malisement). Our DD's, err, all DD's in the post 50 world are very ineffective with the tanks of today, except in rare occations, which you must recognize (having a rogue with poor tanking armor and a ranger tank means you should nuke to save your cleric mana dumping). Once we've used our mana on the basic strengths of our class, we have spare mana.

This spare mana we must make choices with. If we have good tanks that will stay alive and do good damage, yet you can't pull constantly due to the cleric running out of mana now and again, you should feed the cleric mana. If you are in a group with what I call, squishy tanks, then you will help more by nuking to end the mobs life, because if you don't, your cleric will have to mana dump.

The point is, we have our core spells (pet, DS) and our secondary spells. Once we do our main job, we much make choices on what the rest can help with.

In my groups, I always DS and use a pet. I usually malisement (the higher level ones are too expensive for barely any improvement). After that, I spend about 25-50% of the rest of my mana toward AE (90% of my groups since 53 have used AE to some extent, weither we just deal with 4+ pulls with AE, or pull trains). After that, if we have weak tanks I nuke heavy, if we have strong tanks I feed our cleric mana to keep them going even longer.

Everyone notices the difference. I frequently am asked to give our cleric mod rods be other members of the group. It's not just the clerics who feel mod rods help them.

---------------------------------------------

My definition of what Mages do: Give constant damage through their pet and damage shields with the ability to lower a mobs spell resistance. They can suppliment mana to those who need it or supply fast damage through DD's or AoE spells. Their pets offer support for the tanks by taking hits themselves or stunning.

I didn't say a mage was worth half a cleric. I said that the notion that the best use for a mage is to just sit around feeding mod rods to a cleric would, if true, would mean that a mage was half a cleric. It's a conceptual problem with mana transfer spells in general. (Necros face the same question.) Occasional use, to balance things out, sure; obviously handy. Used to turn a class into a "mana battery", it's a symptom that something is unbalanced with the design.

I can hope mages find something to do other than just crank out mod rods on a raid. Maybe I'm wrong, and mages are only worth half a cleric after all. But I think there's more to it than that, and that mages can and should be something more than a mod rod vending machine for the clerics. If not, then the class is broken as far as raids are concerned.
