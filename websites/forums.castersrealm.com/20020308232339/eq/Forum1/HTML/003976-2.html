AE'ing is great on greenies. You can drop dozens of them in no time. They're really good when you need to go into a green area to help a friend, or get a quest item.

AE'ing blues is guaranteeing some quick experience, and also guaranteeing death. The combination of the two, especially at 50+, even with a 49 or 56 rez, means you end up with about the same exp rate as standard pulling.

If nothing ever got resisted this could be great! But AE stuns get resisted. And worse, PB AE gets resisted. And resists still aggro the MOB big time.

As Wizzies, you know what it's like to aggro one mob on you in a single pull situation. Chanters know the same thing. If AE'ing could kill everything in one cast, it would be great. But it takes easily 4000-10000 HP or more to bring down just one of these MOBs. And at 700DD (give or take) per AE cast, it's going to take a LOT of casts, and generate a LOT of aggro, and give a LOT of opportunity for resists. Having a single MOB aggro you is bad enough, having multiple MOBs aggro you is death. Guaranteed. For the chanter first, then the casters.

So it's fun. It does get the adreneline pumping. You can get some nice screen shots (which were multiple AE pulls btw, not single pulls, and I'd be curious to know the level of the AE'er that did that). You can get some nice experience in spurts. But you'll die much more often, and you'll end up with roughly the same experience gain overall (and likely even less experience sice the downtime of rezzing is huge). And you'll only be able to effectively do this against medium to low blues.

I say this to bring some reality back here. I know Wizzies are looking for a way that they don't suck. I like Wizzies. One of my best in-game friends is a Wizzie. But Wizzies are broken, and Verant doesn't seem to want to fix them. Too much resisting, not enough mana (or not fast enough mana regen). The Epic item fixes some of this, the VP robe fixes some of this, but very few Wizzies will get the Epic or VP robe. So you all are currently left to being second class casters with TP. I hope Verant does something more to fix you soon.

Those are great screenshots!

I'm a level 52 wizard on Tribunal and have been having fun with AE teams for a little while now, but I've found that there's a reluctance for players to give it that first try. Usually, teams that like to AE are of people who have done so before together, and will likely find something else to do if their usual group members are not all online.
If, by chance, you decide that you'd like to give it a try, and you're lucky enough to find others to team with for it, these few tips should help you get into the swing of it...

- Team should be puller, enchanter, cleric, ae caster, ae caster, ae caster (or other). For puller I prefer a bard going around with ae song, mana song, and selo's if outdoors, but that's just me.

- After the puller gathers the mobs, the enchanter will take over with the ae stuns. After the first stun, cleric watches everyone's health but mainly enchanter. AE casters do their thang.

- AE casters will be chain casting and may get mobs on them. If this happens, DON'T PANIC! Run the mob/s to the enchanter and wait for the next stun. If point blank ae and the mob gets on you, just move closer to the enchanter; it may be out of range of the stun.

- Remember to target the enchanter, not the mobs. It's hard to break the habit of assisting, but you'll forget all about it in no time.

- If the enchanter dies, chances are so will you. Make sure everyone has a good connection with low linkdeaths and be sure the cleric knows what to do.

- If you're an AE caster, don't hit yourself with your spells!! Stay far enough away that pillars, spirals, rains don't getcha.

- Keep the puller buffed. More common sense here. If the puller doesn't live to pull to the enchanter, no AE for you!

- Don't take more than you can handle unless you're doing it with PB spells better than supernova. Supernova is a mana drainer and it's probably better to use the other AE spells in your arsenal instead. Just keep an eye open as to how efficient your spells are.

- Be prepared for your heart to beat faster, your hands to shake, and to break out in a cold sweat. It's a serious adrenaline boost to have multiple mobs attacking all the time. I can't imagine it getting dull.

There are probably more things I could mention, but as I said, I've only done it for a short while. If you're under level 51, it may be different, but not impossible. It will just take a little practice, and maybe more than a few deaths to get the hang of it.

AE teams are a heck of a lot of fun and very addictive. Get a group of people you're comfortable with and get to it! It's a... blast.
