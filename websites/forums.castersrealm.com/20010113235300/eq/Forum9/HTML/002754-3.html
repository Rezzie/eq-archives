The bulk of the high-level enchanters on Bristlebane are either Erudites or high elves. (More Erudites than high elves, I think.) I like being among the rare dark elf enchanters on the server; it makes me more distinctive. I picked dark elf for role play possibilities (long since abandoned) and for Ultravision.

Besides Hide and Ultravision, however, the benefits of being a dark elf include a great newbie starting areas (look Ma! No ubermobs running around slaugthering newbies!). The disadvantage to Nektulos is no vendors in the zone, but the advantages include no ubermobs (Freeport enchanters are screwed) and an extremely low train frequency. Trains which do occur are from random mobs -- rarely more than a couple -- not camps of, say, Crushbone orcs. Oh, and, the dark elf guards are *much* more responsive to mobs than those darned wood elf guards in Kelethin are. In fact, at times, the dark elf guards are a bit *too* hasty in slaying mobs.

And no one looks better in a Flowing Black Robe or a Froglok Bonecaster Robe than a dark elf. And I'll bet that the dark colors of the Insidious robe look nice on a dark elf, too. It'll be awhile before I know for sure.

High elves, on the other hand, are the only race that I've seen which looks reasonably OK in that green Kedge robe.

Erudites... I like the appearance of some of them. +5 SvMagic is nice (with a -5 SvDisease tacked on, unfortunately). If you wanna have the largest mana pool fastest, you can't beat an Erudite. But at higher levels, my mana pool is almost irrelevant. It's my mana regen speed that matters.

The role-playing possibilities for Erudites, however, are a natural match for the enchanter.

As for WIS with Jewelcraft... *grin* Over a year ago I tried to figure out what the difference was between another enchanter (Erudite) on my server and me -- he was much more successful with JC than I was. The big difference that I saw in stats was that he had a 140+ WIS. So, after playing around with it (but not with the meticulous attention to numbers which Tudamorf wants), I posted on this board about it, noting that it wasn't exactly conclusive, but a possibility. (IIRC, I was also hesitant about posting the data for fear that Verant would nerf it.)

The controversy still rages...

Two days ago I was walking through EC and got asked for the WIS buff to "reduce failures" (for archery, I believe). I explained that there was no conclusive proof that WIS reduces failures... he said that he sure noticed a difference. So I said "OK" and cast the spell on him.

Maybe it's just the placebo effect.

Pick whatever race you want. It really doesn't matter. Most people say humans are the worst, stat-wise, but a nominally equipped human enchanter of level x+1 is more effective than a nominally-equipped level x enchanter of any other race.

If stats keep you up at night, pick an Erudite or High Elf. If lack of night vision is a going to be a problem for you for the hour that you're under level 4, pick a DE. If you're worried about faction, don't - just play as Agnostic.

The DE hide is handy. High Elves have good starting charisma. Toxx is a bleak noob zone, but hey - you've got the place pretty much to yourself. Gnomes can have targeting problems because of their (lack of) height, but you're probably going to be using elemental illusions in battle later on anyway. What I'm saying is that there is no "power" race in Enchanters. You will never be excluded on the basis of race from any group you'd want to be in.

Having said that ... start an Erudite . They look better in robes than any other race, and high-value charisma items are cheap. Make your way to Paineel and enjoy one of the best noob zones in the game. Level up to 4 or 5, then head for Qeynos. Just make sure you stock up on tiny daggers before you go.

Choosing against Erudite based on hunting options is silly. Tox is a horrible place to start, thats true. However, the beetle and spider hunting is every bit as good in Tox as it is in any other newbie zone. That will get you to level 4 or 5. If my level 1 monk can go from Freeport to Qeynos without any trouble a level 4 enchanter with invis can do it with ease.

Of course, thats assuming your dead set on going to Felwithe. I personally think that would be a mistake. You can level (or even power level) just as fast in the Qeynos area as any other. Between the Qeynos Aquaducts, Qeynos Hills, Blackburrow, Karana, and the Isle in Eruds Crossing you should be able to make it to level 16 in 3-4 days /played time (from my experience). All these zones are under-utilized and that means the hunting and exp come fast. You don't need 10 bazillion places to hunt just one good one for each level. Once you hit 16 go anywhere you like.

Back in the beginning when nobody knew what stats did ... we were told that Wisdom helped resists (who knows ... maybe it does).

High Elves start with the highes wisdom of the races. This seemed an important stat.

Next ... if you are starting from scratch with no existing resources to draw from Charisma is by FAR more important than Intelligence in your early career as an Enchanter. More Intelligence does very little good in the early levels.

Next ... You get AC boost with 75 Agi. This has been mentioned several times. What hasn't been mentioned is that you get an HP boost at 75 Sta. Erudites are down in both stats (High Elves are down in Sta). It is much easier to only boost one "non critical" stat than two.

In the long run race isn't important and you should just pick the one you really want to be. But there are definitely defferances between them.
