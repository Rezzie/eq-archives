>>What lets this tank do what he does? AC and HP. Warriors have more HP.

>>AC, though, is determined by two things. Armor and defensive skill. PoG armor for hybrids is almost exactly the same as it is for warriors in terms of AC. The balancing factor has always been that post50 warrior defense caps higher than hybrid defense, and thus warriors generally had more base AC. Not any more. That's what worries me.

It doesn't worry me. I outtank higher level sk's/paladins with better AC than me all the time. Why is that? Because CH only cares about how often it must be cast. And that is determined by level, ac, hp, and avoidance skills. 3 of those categories we lead in. You can write off the hp, Daimyo. I won't. I've got a troll warrior. I've got a gnome one. And the troll warrior is significantly tougher for 1 reason: HP. The fact that the gnome had about 15 higher AC didn't matter. The troll had 150 more hp. And that did. Past tense, that was at 43, the gnome warrior is 48 now, would be higher, but I've been tinkering with my main instead.

Now, you can talk about boss mobs. Against those, the hybrid's spells are useless. So what do you end up with? A guy that has worse weapons with lower combat stats and lower hp that has sub-par weapon skills. And he's got no disciplines. In normal xp combat, paladins/sk's do 70-80% of warrior damage. Against boss mobs, this drops. Against boss mobs, you can parry, riposte, or dodge a 700 point hit that they won't. That's a huge dif. But it comes down to two things... they deal less damage and still die easier. Given the constraints of high level encounters and link death problems, why would you possible want a hybrid when you could have a warrior/rogue instead? Mass encounters means the parent classes are there which means the hybrids main advantage, flexability, goes bye-bye.

So, no, I'm not worried. I know we are better. And I'm happy that they are a bit closer. It's better for the game overall.

Ok all you pissed off warriors, there is something that you fail to realize about tanking skills:

There are 6 factors to consider in determining what makes a good tank:

Parry, Dodge, Ripost, Hp, Ac, and Defense

Now lets run down the list.

Parry: Warriors have a big advantage
Dodge: Warriors have a big advantage
Ripost: Warriors have a big advantage
Hp: Warriors have a big advantage
Ac: Warriors have a big advantage
Defense: Warriors and pallys/sk's are equal

What are you worried about? Also, people have said that thanks to the new PoG armor, the ac difference is diminished if not removed altogether. Let me ask you this: how many people are actually gonna get that armor?

I have also heard it said that the hp difference between a pally and a warrior 50+ is only 400ish. Yes, this is a mere one or two melee rounds of difference, but if you are grouped with that same pally, he will have put his buffs onto you (unless he doesn't know what he is doing), and as pallys have very few self only buffs, you will easily have 1k+ more hp than the pally. This difference will become even more pronounced if you add in a cleric to the equation.

So don't be mad, be happy. You have lost nothing and gained a more powerful group mate. What is wrong with that?
