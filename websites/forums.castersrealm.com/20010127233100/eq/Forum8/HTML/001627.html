We are absolutely worthless on a dragon raid and only marginally useful on a Planes raid. The only reason to go on such raids is for the loot (in Hate you can get the book off of Inny, and in fear you can get blighted armour). I'm not sure that there is anything that drops from a dragon that necros need. Our value on raids is definately less than almost any other class given that normally you can't use a pet and you can't dot. Now that SKs have summon corpse we aren't needed for that service either.

The only contribution that we have left, and if the raid is led well won't come up, is our ability to cast Convergence after level 53. If the raid goes bad and all the clerics get killed (this means bad raid leadership because they didn't tell the clerics to camp far enough in advance when it was obvious everyone was going to die) we can usually feign death along with the monks and not die. Then after the area clears we can use Convergence to ressurect a cleric, who can then take care of everyone else. That's our only remaining valuable contribution.

For high level raids the desired classes are enchanters, wizards, clerics, monks (for pulling), a shaman for buffs, and the rest tanks. A raid will generally take as many enchanters and clerics as they can get, suplemented by as many tanks as the clerics can effectively keep healed, a couple of wizards for quick damage and evac, and a a few support classes (e.g. shaman, bard). They used to take one necro in case they needed a corpse summoned, but now that SK's can do that too, the SK is more useful because he can tank as well. Rogues and necros are at the bottom of the barrel as far as class desirability on raids is concerned.
