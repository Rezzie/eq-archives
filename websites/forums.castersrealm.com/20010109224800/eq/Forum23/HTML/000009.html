Ala'Kan stood on top of his tower in the sky, watching the chaos and discord that infested the lands below him. He had not seen this much destruction since the dragon wars of his youth, and this looked all too like it.
The dragons have forced the weaker creatures to flee from their home and these have in turn been forced to flee from theirs. It is a wonderful tactic used, they kill anything weaker then them in their path and when they get to the new place, they are unfamiliar with the territory and are often famished and starved, leaving the dragons with almost no opposition to their rule.
The council of mages has long sworn not to interfere in the affairs of men, but these dragons threatened to destroy the world and all of its inhabitants, and that they could not tolerate.
Meanwhile, In the air off the coast of Odus, the elder dragon Bloht was flying towards Erud threatening to destroy all the sacred texts in the Great Library and the beautiful Palace. This act is what finally drove the stubborn council to act.
The Arch Mage sent the cry to all the circles of the tower, the call those walls had not heard in millenia, the call of War.
This was no ordinary tower, only the elite mages of the realm who have retired from adventure and seek to live a quiet life in the company of their fellow mages stay here, yet even so, they numbered a thousand strong.
Reactions were mixed, some did not know what the call meant, and stayed staring around befuddled, others knew and started to panic not knowing what to do as it had never happened during their lifetime, but a few, the mages who never truly stopped lusting for adventure, they knew exactly what to do and dropped to the floor whipping out their dusty spellbooks and memorizing long forgotten spells. It was these mages who organized the tower and sent them to the porches on which one could see half the world from the height of the towers levitation.
Closer and closer, Bloht seemed to be flying with tremendous speed, and now was already flying over the beautiful beaches of the south-western penisula of Odus. Here, Bloht took the time to ravage the small villages and torment their inhabitants. It was this that gave the mages time to plan the attack.
The Arch Mage Ala'Kans voice boomed throughout the tower, "Mages! Forward the Elements!". With this single command, a thousand beings took flight towards the dragon, an army of elements, forming a multi-colored quilt of death floating towards its victim. Again the Arch-Mage boomed, "Mages! Loose!", and the tower glowed red from the spell casting, the red of blood was seen in the sky for hundreds of miles, but what was even scarier to the onlookers, the glow suddenly ceased, and it seemed to someone looking from afar that a bridge was growing from the tower to the dragon, so many and great were the bolts sent that day.
Rooted and stunned the dragon could do nothing but watch as a thousand meteors crashed into him, exploding in a horrific explosion, making a crater on the whole peninsula. When finally they deemed it safe to enter, the mages were horrified at what they had done. Nothing remained, nothing. Many broke down and weeped, swearing never to use their power again. Some of the mages were embarrassed by their mistake and left their pets to forever guard the area, killing anything they see so that word of the horrible deed could not be spread throughout the world.

That was many years ago, and now the dragons come again...
