Ok.

1) The Necromancer has been a most enjoying class to play. If you are looking for overall pure God like power, here you are. If you are looking for a class with the ability to do more than just this or that, you are looking in the right direction. If time is a main concern, then IMHO the Necromancer would be the best class choice. As everyone knows, our downtime is by far the least of any. We can solo right through, and for the most part quite easily at that.

I have absolutely _no_ regrets about my character choice. I played a Magician well in until I finally tired of the tedious downtime and the lack of options when stuff goes sour. I chose an Iksar Necromancer due to the _minimal_ downtime they have, their natural regen. makes quite the difference. Only bad side to that is of course the faction. Some see that as a problem, others find ways around it.

2) Grouping. Granted, you most likely will not see many shouts saying "Group LF Necro!!"
anytime soon, (which is unfortunate, for them anyways) but you will run into your educated players. You will meet those who know the ways, and you will learn yourself what duo or trio combo you work best in. I haven't done any extended amount of grouping, but when I do I prefer no more than one or two others. I have been hunting with a Troll Shammy and a Troll Warrior on occasion, and I absolutely enjoy myself to the max in that setup. I think Necro / Shammy / Bard would be the best trio, but that's aside from the point. Yes, after 45 you can still solo as many do. You will most likely find yourself more prone to group at that time though. I haven't done any as of yet though.

About the Raids. ::sigh:: Don't worry about that just yet, there will be changes made eventually but do not refuse a class just because there are some shady issues involving them and doing Planar Raids. I would some day like to go on one, but I am still enjoying what the non-planar realms have to offer.

Other than that, you won't really get a feel for the Necromancer's true power until 29. (Or so I did) After that, it just keeps getting better and better. Hope I was some help, if you happen to start a char on Terris-Thule, give me a tell.

- Arachtyr

"I know necro's are a dime a dozen, I still do not see alot of 50+ necro. I see a ton of 20-40 but for some odd reason I still dont see a "ton" of 50+ necros... granted plenty but compared to other classes (warriors clerics) its still a fairly decent number."

Necros are not a slime-a-dozen. They were a year ago when they were "overpowered". But the stigma of an overpopulation of Necros is just false. Do a /who all Necro at any time during the week and you'll find they come close in numbers to all other casters except Wizzies. Surprisingly, Warriors make up the most played class followed by Druids.

"I am really concidering a necro as my main as it is one of the only classes I've not played past level 12.... I hear alot of things about necros about how great they are and what fun they are..."

This is absolutely true if you feel the Necro class fits your play style. Necros are one of the few classes that start powerful and get even more powerful as they level. There is no power "drop-off" as exists in many other classes.

"I hate dying, and I like helping people.. I think its awsome I can look cool, help someone, powerlevel someone if need be (feigndeath, how sweet), and be a fun person to be around. A necro seems to be all of these things in one way or another..."

A Necro will still die. The only way around this is JBoots. But my consensus after getting JBoots for my Necro was that Necros are Gods! And I really mean that. Necros can be a LOT of fun to be around, because it's one of the few classes that is designed properly... so Necros are not stressed out like the other half of Norrath. You will get a big kick out of Summon Corpse (if you evern get it in a timely fashion), but in all honesty Necros are not extremely helpful to others due to lack of buffs.

"There seems to be alot of complaints lately about Uber Necro's having so many concerns over plane raids and that is a tad troubling as this is the first time in awhile that necro's might have a major disadvantage..."

Necros have poor loot drops in the planes. And furthermore, most guild leaders just want Necros to a) sic their pet, b) lifetap, and c) pawn their mana to the Clerics. Large Plane raids are not really that fun for Necros... but believe me, Plane raids are NOT that much fun! I really can't understand why people like spending 9 STRAIGHT hours getting little to no xp, and no loot.

"1: Has the necro been a pleasure to play for you and would you recommend it to someone who needs a good class with not alot of time on my hands"

Iksar Necro is the fastest leveling class in Norrath. I forget my time to 52 but it was so low that people didn't believe me until I sent them screenshots. So if you have little time to play and still want to level, a Necro is the way. It is also the most fun class I have ever played... and I have played them all.

"2: How bad is the grouping problem for necros? I've only played up to 32 ( and a bard for that matter ) so my 45+ exp is lacking.. how do you feel about this?"

Necros do not need groups. That's a given. If you want to find a group there are plenty of reasons for them to want you; especially at certain key levels. Where it kinda falls apart is on large raids; but again I hate large raids so I don't care. In a small group in a hard zone, Necros can *really* help out a group with a quad 50 hitting pet, a short mes, and deadly short DOTs.

"and the last thing is, On the complaints about the necro's.. is it "just" the uber mobs that there is a problem? In other words, necro's are useful on other parts of the raid up until Maestro and Innoruuk? (examples)"

On all plane raids there are magic resistant MOBs. So those don't count. Lifetaps stick OFTEN if not always. And although Necro undead DD is not great it's nothing to sneeze at. Furthermore don't let anyone tell you pets don't belong on raids. That only happens when a noobie raid-leader takes the reigns. However, see my upper comment about raids. You certainly do contribute (especially on break in) but you do not compare to the other classes in terms of importance except in rare cases.

"I have a real desire to enjoy the upper level life...but I just dont think i've found my class yet.."

Upper level life is fine for Necros IMO. I was soloing Mature Wurms in Skyfire at level 50 with no problems whatsoever. Mages, Wizards and Enchanters of the same level didn't even try those stunts. If you're aiming for plane raids though and want the most valuable class there, the Necro is not it. But you may be disappointed to see how boring raids are. At least with a Necro you have options if you don't like it.
