IMHO
---Melee---
At dealing raw damage: Monk
At taking melee hits: Warrior

---Caster---
At raw damage: Wizard
Most Powerful Solo Caster when fighting single mobs: Necro

Most powerful in terms of controling the scene: Enchanter

---Healer---
Most powerful Soloer: Druid

Most powerful leverage-wise (psychologically speaking): Cleric (Hahahah a cleric is VERY powerful when someone is begging him/her for a res!!)
Most powerful damage sponge: Cleric (plate-wearing class)


To reiterate...most powerful HOW? I think every class is the MOST powerful in its own category. You're going to get a lot of mixed responses because of different interpretations of "powerful," not because of personal preferance or other opinion.

Power being defined as best dueler is not realistic. You don't gain much killing another player nor do you lose much dying to another player.

Power should first be defined...

Avoiding exp loss
Ability to solo
Ability to deal with 'surprises' ie wandering mobs
Ability to travel safely from point 'A' point 'B'
Ability to contribute and enhance power of group
Ability to prevent damage
Ability to cause damage
Ability to take damage
Ability to cause mana-free damage

Power should be reproducible and not contingent upon fixed scenarios.. like outdoor kiting would be useless in a dungeon and kiting in general exploits an predictable MOB AI.

Killing guards and merchants in newbie zones is not 'powerful' either.

A group or individual who could move through a place like Dalnir for the first time without losses and of an appropriate level for that dungeon would be 'powerful' IMHO.

It would be interesting to have a tournament where the objective is a group to move through a dungeon and kill the boss mob. The fastest time to get boss drop would be winner. Probably suggested before by someone but I still like the idea.
