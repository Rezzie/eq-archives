This strategy is for level 34+.

This is generalness so feel free to break from this slightly in varied circumstances but this strategy im about to tell you easily lets me defeat MANY 10 lvl higher people ass assuming you can get them to agree to not run off from the duel location to med/heal.

Get a pet, give it MR and speed. Buff yourself with like jboots/sow first (unless you cant recast then put it toward the end) always if you have it, then see invis and augmentation and AC and maybe ultravision first whatever you think the buffs you will need least will be, then put stuff after that which is of medium importance like radiant visage or insight next, and then follow that with the real stuff like rune, shielding, MR, clarify, etc. Make sure to get a lot of resist gear because they will probably bring a lot of MR gear with them to use for this type occasion.

If they can summon a pet they probably will when they are preparing for the duel as well.

Once they buff and stuff tell them you are going to give them the /d now and then for them to wait till you are ready. give them the /d and then target their pet and run to max spell range and say /ooc ready. As soon as they hit /d start casting mez on their pet. They will probably attempt root or some such on yours. The pet with 20MR spell has effectively around 65MR and if you give it Resist Magic that jacks its MR up to around 85. Roots should not stick. And if they do it wont have a long duration at all.

If root sticks dont worry you are fine, they more than likely wont kill you before the pet will break root no matter how high of level they are.

Depending on what class they are and your resists you can opt here for a lot of different methods of attack.

If your resists are low you can chaotic feedback/sieve them to interrupt their spells and hurry up and get their mana low.

If the spells you are going to be hit by are high damage and you dont think you will be able to take a few hits with them try the chaotic feedback range game. Stay at max spell range and when they cast either chaotic feedback to interrupt or step back out of spell range to mess up their cast and force them to run forward to get back into spell range.

The real fun starts once your pet starts hitting them, it shouldnt take more than like 15 seconds to get to this stage of the duel. The pet is on them and you have free reign to control the flow of the battle from the outside. The battle isnt you vs them anymore its your pet vs them with you assisting the pet.

Here they will have to deal with the pet again and this means attempting more roots and such. The spells to use in this stage are Sieve, Strip Enchant or pillage, and chaotic feedback, also rune self if it is gone.

This part might take a bit for them to break out of since they gotta get rid of a magic resistant pet which is in melee range hitting them. It should give you easily enough time to sieve once pillage once and rune yourself again. While they are running away from the pet sieve them again. When they stop and attempt to cast another spell on you give em a chaotic feedback. Another sieve follows this. They will then hit you with some other spell so let the rune soak it up if you have to. By now they will be damn near OOM and resistance will get harder still as it goes on.

Depending on how long the fight has been going on, if it took a while to root them or something you might need to refresh mez on their pet and now wouldnt be such a bad time to do that. If they still got mana left and start casting on you chaotic feedback and give them another sieve and let youre HP bite down on it. Here they should be OOM and you should have at least 30-40%hp if you had the right resists. From here on out all you gotta worry about is keeping their pet mezzed while yours kills them. They will be manaless so it is just a matter of time.

You should also still have 40% mana or so and take time here to strip off their buffs a few more times to make sure they are all gone and maybe try for some high power DD's if you want to try to end the fight faster and you got the mana. Make sure to rune youreself as well again, effectively sealing their coffin.

This strategy has allowed me to win against nearly every caster I have fought regardless of their class or level. All the fights where I said I killed HUGE people with HIGH MR i did this. the 49wiz at lvl 44... the 53 cleric at 43... the 56 druid at 45...
This strategy effectively breaks the spell sieve allowing you to win every fight purely because of attrition they just run out of mana and CANT finish you no matter how far they got down your hp. It also severely punishes spells that dont hurt you, basically making them a waste of mana for the opponent to cast. Examples of wasted time/mana versus you are: Any buff cancelling spell or crystallized pumice. These are free sieves for you. Any sort of movement reducing spell on you, aka, root/snare we wont be doing much of that anyway. Another free sieve. Any sort of opponent self protection (cleric invulnerability especially) just duck and cancel the spell you were casting, there is a good chance they will not know they can or want to cancel the effect and just wait the whole 18.0 seconds for it to go away. Start casting sieve at the 16 second mark and its a free sieve. If they end the protection and begin casting then give them a chaotic feedback and they didnt gain anything from it.

Notice I didnt say to rely on any resistable spells to win. This means the MR of the opponent doesnt matter in the slightest. Also necros and shamans will DOT themselves to break the mez they expect you to cast. This strategy doesnt use mez so it is not a problem either in the slightest. People ask me how I kill people 11 levels higher because they will have such huge level based magic resistance and I laugh at them and say something like Tishan+DD/mez.

What this does rely on though is your willingness to lose actual coins or items to defeat the opponent. If you dont want to waste a 13p peridot casting rune4 on yourself to stop you from losing the duel then you shouldnt be duelling in the first place. Go out and get all kinds of duel only things and always carry them with you they will help add the icing on the cake. Some of these items to look for are Mystic Cloak (10 free rune 3's) Shiny brass Idol (if you are at max range they might not know that you are invincible and waste all their mana trying to kill you), Poison cure potions - shamans make these and they are like 10p max to make buy at least 4. Disease resist items - These spells are generally easy to resist much more so than poison in any event so get high DR (mogook has 25DR as well) other items are collar of undead protect and the 8DR earrings. The combination of cure potions and disease resist will pretty much win all fights with necros and shamans. Magic resist is easy with buffs but make sure to have a few MR items also like the 7MR wrists or 9mr neck to seal it. Fire resist - Frog crown and stuff like SMR and the 6FR wrists and 10Fr rings are musts vs mages, druids, wizards etc. We also get dryboneform for 25 extra fire resist always use this. Cold resist is the same but dont worry so much about it because it is the less used longer casting time and more inefficient of spells and most players opt fire over it as a rule of thumb. Keep ability to get 80CR though. The 10CR rings and frog crown and wolfform will help a lot with CR, also vox bracers.
