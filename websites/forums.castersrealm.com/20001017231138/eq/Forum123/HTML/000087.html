At the time that I started my warrior (back in August of 1999), all of the stuff that we know about how warriors work and how melee works wasn't yet known. People were sacrificing huge amounts of AC to get bigger, better stats. People were convinced that stats made the warrior. People were convinced that Gnomes would simply suck as Warriors because of their low stats.

My opinions were similar to these. And I picked a Gnome warrior anyways. Why? I wanted a challenge. I wanted to play a character that would have serious obstacles to overcome, so that when I eventually reached 50th level, I could say "Well sure, you got your *Barbarian* warrior to 50th level. But that's easy! I got a *Gnome* warrior to 50th!"

It wasn't until I was well underway that we started learning how melee works in EQ. And we learned that playing a Gnome warrior isn't a whole lot tougher than playing a Barbarian or an Ogre warrior. (And in some places, like Sol A and Sol B, it's actually easier to be a Gnome than an Ogre or a Barb.)

As for solid statistical evidence to support that Gnomes don't make bad warriors, all I can say is: I'm 46th level. I'd be higher if it weren't for the fact that (A) I took a 4-month vacation from EQ; and (B) I've been futzing around with my alt characters lately and haven't been playing my warrior as much. But when I do play my Warrior, I do just fine.

So if a Gnome warrior can get to 46th level, then we've got to have *something* going for us.

People think warriors just hit the auto attack button and that's all their is to it.

Yes many people think that but there is MUCH skill in playing a Warrior. I can attest to that as an Enchanter... maybe more than any other class. Grouping with good Warriors is the reason I started my Warrior.

Here are a few things that come to mind, from an Enchanter's point of view:

What type of mob to go for first? Not such a problem you might think, but it might be crucial which mob you go for so that your groupmates don't die. Example: Efreeti Raids .. IMP PROTECTORS FIRST! The Imp Protectors are HARD TO MEZ, but the Lava Guardians are easy. Save your Enchanter his life, thus saving your whole group's life.

What to attack and when. I seriously doubt that there isn't an Enchanter out there who hasn't been killed when the Melee broke his mez.

How to save the casters when they get attacked. You can take the blows .. so taunt that thing off the casters! I was so impressed with a certain warrior on my server who I grouped with in my high 20's to low 30's. Talk about perfect timing, that guy knew what to look for, when to attack, when to back off... He knew to look for the 'Bick begins to cast a spell' and even had a good grasp of the timing of my spells to know when to stop attacking, so as to not keep attacking through my mez, breaking it, which would then aggro the mob on me, killing me. I dropped my jaw to the floor a few times watching him work.

When to throw in the towel. I've been in whole groups that were wiped out because the Warrior/Tank couldn't suck it up and just die away from the group because of an overpull.

What you can expect from your casters. Yesterday my Enc was killed in Karnors because the Warrior pulled too soon after I was rez'd. He didn't understand that since we were in close quarters (skelly captain) I needed certain buffs, namely my MR buffs so I wouldn't get caught in my own AOE mez's. To him, I had medded up enough, and I certainly had if the spawn wasn't in such a small area.

Hmmm, I can't think of any others, but I'll say this about tanks. I like Warriors best . When I want a tank I look for a warrior. Best hps, best ac, great dmg output. Come, let me Swift Like the Wind you my Warrior friends
