Alright, here goes. I started playing today after the new patch where pets don't eat up AE waves. I set out to really test these out, to see what the AE rains are really made of. Here are my findings.

Zone: Sebilis "upper zone out" and "d1"
mobs:
-----
froglok kor shaman
froglok zol knight
froglok dar knight
froglok jin shaman
froglok jin wizard
froglok bok knight
froglok bok wizard
froglok bok shaman
sebilite golem
pox scarab
various named mobs (lv53)

0 waves landed: 3 times
1 wave landed: 19 times
2 waves landed: 94 times
3 waves landed: 76 times
4 waves landed: 2 times

Casts of Sirocco: 194
Waves attempted: (194 x 3) = 582
Waves landed: 443
Percent of waves landed: 76.1%

(Almost all mobs had either Malosi or Malosini (same thing really) cast on them before casting Sirocco)

The total damage possible is 1890. At 76.1% efficiency, this is 1438 damage. In reality, not all waves that land do full damage, however most do. For argument's sake, multiply by 0.9 and get the ACTUAL MANA RATIO on the spell Sirocco, which is (1294 / 399) = 3.24.

This is the ACTUAL ratio observed in 12 hours of play, taking into account everything. This means for every point of mana you spend casting it, you should do 3.24 points of damage.

Now compare to Char whose ratio at lv56 is (700 / 260) = 2.69. This is under the most ideal situations, no resists at all, everything lands for full damage. In reality, this is not the case, and although I haven't done the extensive research on Char as I did for Sirocco, I have spent the past 2 months casting it and I can tell you the ACTUAL MANA RATIO would be about 2.2, figuring in resists.

3.24 / 2.2 = 1.47

Conclusion: I am 47% more efficient by using Sirocco than by using Char, when the situation allows it (which it usually does).
