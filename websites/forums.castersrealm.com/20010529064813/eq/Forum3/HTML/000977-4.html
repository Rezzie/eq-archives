That 8 hours turns into 80 because of the 10 other people in line in front of you)

Unfortunately, I think that the game is tuned so that by the time you are sufficiently powerful to get those items, they are considered mid to low end items for you (i.e. quest rewards are really designed for higher level chars to do them & sell to lower level chars).

Certainly some classes are more item dependent than others - ALL melee types require the best weapon(s) and armor they can find.

Clerics (and to a lesser extent other healer classes) require the best armor they can get because of healing taunts. I know about this one personally, because my original character is a 15th cleric who has banded and a few worse pieces, who died frequently as a result. My 20th druid, in full reinforced and 2 jagged bands with a cracked darkwood shield dies MUCH less frequently.

Enchanters HAVE to have a decent amount of charisma to get any of their spells to stick, though a few hundred PP are sufficient to get you there. In addition they (along with wizards) probably need the best armor of the int casters, since their spells taunt so much.

All else is relatively optional. Int and Wis casters can always use more mana, but mana recovery rate is far more important than total mana in general, except when soloing. I read once that if you're constantly draining to OOM you're doing something wrong.

With all the new items from Kunark coming into play, I think a lot of the old stuff will devalue considerably, making it fall more within the price range of the people who can actually still get some value out of it.
