unregistered 		posted 06-18-2000 09:42 AM              
Most things you say here or even on the EQ forum wont change anything. People have long abandoned the idea of this being a cooperative effort between the players, staff, and creators to make this game the best it can be. It is now "us vs them" to all concerned. Gordon says something that turns out to be wrong, and players call it a lie. Players attempt to call attention to a situation that is in error, and verant thinks this is some elaborate group ruse to work around some designed game methods. Creation of new items/abilities/spells appears to go like this:
Someone creates the "thing", they make it what they feel to be appropriate and worthwhile, but they do not pay attention to balance and exploitation issues specificly.
The "thing" gets handed off to the "eq security team". This is the equivalent of hiring hackers to test system security. These guys look for any way, no matter how remote that the "thing" can be used to exploit, or alter the functionality of the game. They place restrictions on the "thing" with the only requirement being to remove the exploit/advantage they find could potentially be improper in any situation. They do not look at the original intent of the "thing" in any way. This isn't their job, it was the job of the person above.
The "finished" product is not re-examined for usefullness, original purpose, or even errors/bugs. The first process ensures the item exists. The second ensures it wont be exploited. The item may or may not be usefull. It has an average to high chance of still retaining its usefullness (with less chance as the "thing" becomes more powerfull in design), but as long as it A) exists, and B) cant be exploited - they are covered.

If the first team gets lazy, the item never exists, so that's not a worry. If the second team gets lazy you have things like the robe entering the game. This robe has the potential to alter the classes ability in a specific set of situations (situations where a healer is not available).
I think we would all agree that this is not and can not be a replacement for a healer. Any situation without a healer you might consider this a drastic decrease in downtime. Consider, however, that monks post 50 can bandage to 70% health, and then mend bringing them to 90% health. Mend recycles fast enough that sitting, bandaging, and mending will outdo fungal regrowth and feigning by far. This leaves us with the scenario where a monk must be feigned (and thus cant bandage). Ignoring that this robe probably has a cast time long enough that it's measured with a half-life which in itself makes this basicly useless in this situation, you also have to wonder exactly what a monk would be able to do with this regen.
This regen is 15 per tick, the monk is feigned so there is no appreciable regen aside from this (what? maybe 1 pt?). A monk with 1500 hp requires 100 ticks to heal fully at 4 secs per tick. Say 80 ticks to heal 80% health (since a monk with 0% health isnt healing anything :P). this is 320 secs, or over 5 minutes to heal back to full. Have you ever let a monster sit for 5 minutes? Unless you manage to proc a few dozen ice comets while doing the normal massive monk damage to something like cauldronbubble (lots of hp, slow regen) any monster will be back to full health long before you are. This isnt going to let you regen faster than monsters, and it isnt going to let you solo anything you otherwise couldnt. If you're trying to escape - face it. Monks are probably the masters of escape. This might let them do a feign-run-feign escape sequence out of hell in 4 minutes instead of 20, but even without it they WILL get out. Just sit tight and mend up.
So why are people pissed over this? The same reason I'm pissed over my temperate flux staff's emminent nerf. It's a toy, a status symbol, a little bit of use if you are intelligent enough to know when and how to use it. And the same reason verant has removed the effect: Like such items as the Necklace of Superiority, the rod of annihilation, and the staff of shielding - from the surface this seems amazingly powerfull. When you put it in perspective with the workings of the game and other items at this level however, it is clearly not the most powerfull, and it's debatable wether it would give monks any advantage at all (aside from saving them the cost of 5-10 bandaids).
Abashi, like most people, has his limits on how often he feels the need to admit he's wrong (read: relate to the people). So even if he agreed with these arguments completely I doubt that he would be ready to champion the cause and put forth an effort to get it fixed.
The effects on deepwater are fairly cheesy - mainly because the class already has those abilities, and the limitations of having a cast-time make it downtime only - which they can already do with their own spells (the med time of a claritied paladin is dwarfed by the med time of a claritied + bard song pure caster).
The effects on cobalt are much more powerfull from a balance standpoint. At one point rangers were considered to trade defensive ability for basicly sow, invis, snare. Warriors have 2 of those abilities on items now (snare sword, invis .. legs is it?) Add a pair of jboots and you've outdated rangers. To top it all off, these items are fairly easy to get. Random zol frogs in sebilis drop the legs (maybe others as well). If you're going to alter the design of a class even as slightly as this, it should require a hefty amount of work.
Casters have a lot of items that seem to be subject to a mysterious rule of "do not outdo any of the previous, instead make them different but equal". This extends to spells as well. There appears to be a great reluctance to admit that for non-melee classes, 2 hand weapons must be at least twice as powerfull (in purely non-melee terms like stat bonuses) as their 1 hand counterparts to be a viable alternative. I find it ironic that the best caster weapon in terms of non-melee stats is approximately equal to the staff of wheel - and yet is 2 handed. But remember.. we have a lot of items.. like our spells. Based on this you all should expect to get 3 weapons and 3 sets of armor. Each set will give resists vs fire, ice, magic respectively, and have perfectly identical stats otherwise. Although it offers no real additional advantage, one set will be insanely hard to get, one set will be medium difficulty, and one set will be least difficulty. Still they will all be very rare.

The items on kunark have me convinced that this is a situation where one hand doesnt know what the other is doing. The people making melee items seem more concerned with keeping players interested. The people making caster items seem more concerned with avoiding exploits at all costs. Of course, it seems the later group has final say on all items - and one of that group really didn't like fungal regrowth.
