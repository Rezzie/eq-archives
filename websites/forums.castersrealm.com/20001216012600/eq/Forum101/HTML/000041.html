To all those out there who believe Paladins suck...

I hear ya, and totally agree... to a point.

The role of a Paladin is really tough to stand and even harder to play. You don't really do anything better than anyone else which makes soloing nigh impossible. To really dish out damage you need to use a 2H weapon, which negates the AC bonus AND bashing benefits of a shield. We have NO offensive spells or pets as do Shadowknights to offset this. Our alternative is defense.
You'll need high Agility to defend the multitude of attacks you will need to withstand, but of course you need strength to carry the heavy armor and effectively damage opponents. Oh, and Wisdom to cast your spells. Oh and don't forget Dexterity so you aren't missing all the time, Stamina, cause what's a good tank without Stamina, and to top it all off, the game FORCES you into a high charisma. Something is going to suffer.

So how do you deal with all these forces working against you? Items.

Paladins get to use some of the coolest, most powerful items in the game. So plan your new Paladin around this. I can tell you for a fact that it is very easy to find WIS and STR items for your Pally, most of you know this, therefore DO NOT PUT ANY POINTS INTO WISDOM WHEN YOU START. I can't say this enough, but you'll thank me later. Paladin spells are an afterthought, something to regain health and buff away from combat. If I have only one word of advice to newbie Paladins... juice up your AGILITY and DEXTERITY. You are fighters. Hook up an erollsi bracer, Spiked collar, and Bloodstained mantle (all pretty cheap in the EQ economy) and you've +13 STR. Don't be a human. The infra-vision is a far better benefit than fast leveling. You ain't gonna kill nothin if ya can't see nothin. Dwarfs are the best Pallies, if you pump EVERYTHING into Agility, but don't forget Half-elves. They start with tremendous AGI and DEX.

Now I'm going to talk about my experiences as a paladin...

Now alot of you non-paladins will say, "But Pallies have LAY HANDS, so you have to kill them twice". True, but alot of mobs I've met my level DON'T SEEM TO MIND THE EXTRA WORK. Also consider the fact that you can only do this once every 90 minutes real time. I often found myself regaining my hands ability by death rather than by over time. OK, true story...

A friend of mine and I have been playing this game for awhile (7 months, not as long as many others, but long enough) and we started with a Paladin and a Ranger. We would run through N.Karana and say, "Oh look, that Griffawn is white to us, we should be able to take'im". "Are you ready Ranger"? "Yep, you got your hands available Paladin"? "Yep, lets do it"!

30 seconds pass... CRACK CRACK CRACK...
HANDS ME HANDS ME!!!

WWWWAAAAAOOOOOWWWWAAAAAAHHHH

Thanks man...

CRACK CRACK CRACK!

RUN! RUN! RUN!

This would happen even when they conned blue, sometimes green. Now the Griffs are a special case, but this would happen against orcs, gnolls, big-ass spiders, you name it.
It took us 5 months to reach level 20. After that, we leveled even slower, cause now you have to fight really big nasty things.


So... we started over. I took a druid, he took a magician.

VERY, VERY, EASY.

But I'm not here to talk about that. All I want to say is this. If you are on EQ to make friends, fight in groups, join a guild, then Paladin is a great class to play. A party always loves a Paladin. They can take a TON of damage. Spellcasters LOVE Pallies to death. The Paladin taunts and literally gets torn a new bunghole, while the caster stands back and delightfully blasts mobs to puddy. If someone is about to die, a lay-on-hands saves the day. The few times I was able to save someones ass, I can tell you that there is no better feeling in the game than really saving a player's life, especially when they are much higher level than you and are grateful enough to give you a black pearl in return for you spending your Trump card on them. You are the savior, you sacrafice your life for others. You are usually the first to die in a party, and everyone will love you for it.
HOWEVER,
You're dead, and you have a long run back to your corpse...

My solution....................

I have pondered this for a long time. Several weeks have I sat in my room thinking up how to solve this with a minimum of game changes.... OK, so it came to me while browsing a D&D book, so what!

one new spell, level 15.

SUMMON STEED.

A cool graphic change would be required to put a horse under the Pally, but it can be done. Movement would equal that of a druid in wolf form (lesser). A Paladin could not attack while on the horse. If he rode in, and attacked, the graphic would show him dismounting on the fly and swinging away (bandwith permitting), the horse disappearing. If he is hit while on the horse, he is knocked off (falling damage if necessary) and the horse disappears. It's a travel only spell, and that alone I think you incite more Paladins, and lessen the burden of being one. It also does not fall completely outside a Pallies desired skill set.

I welcome any replies to this. Please support this idea if you feel it deserves such praise.

Thanks for listening.

I'm a twinked 11 paladin as of this Monday morning and I wanted to point out one glaring problem the topic poster might be having. He's using a Blackened Iron Bastard Sword pre-leve 20. I made the mistake of buying one at level 8 after oohing and aahing over the 20/47 dmg/delay ratio. After meleeing with it for about three fights, I recalled something I'd learned about tanks as an enchanter - the damage cap. I hit the damage cap consistently every hit. That cap was about 21 damage at the time. Well, I'd been hitting that cap (not quite as consistently) with a Fine Steel 2 handed sword for at least 1 experience level, and it is 12 dmg 43 delay. So i am actually doing worse with the 47 delay BIBS. You will not be working at peak performance with that weapon until the level 20 damage cap lift, when you will start slicing through mobs at 40+ points per hit, rather than the 30ish you're probably getting. Do yourself a favor and get an Axe of the iron back (what im currently using, hitting for 29 consistently at level 11 and only 120 str) or some other low-delay 2h slasher and you'll be hitting just as hard, but more often. An axe of the iron back is 16/37 delay, meaning you swing much faster than a BIBS (10 less delay) but the lost damage from the damage cap pre-20 is virtually non-existent. Bank the BIBS for a couple levels. Then when you hit 20, laugh all the way to the bank as you get ready to chop everyone up at 40+ damage a hit.

Yes, the level 10-19 damage cap hovers around 30. I'm doing the same damage with my 37 delay axe as you were with your 47 delay sword at level 11, and you're level 19. It's all because of the cap. When you hit 20 the damage cap lifts and the BIBS starts doing 40+ damage at the max per hit, and your str begins to affect your max damage, not just your average damage. Every few levels post 20 your weapon skill and offense will increase to the point that you will be able to do another point of damage here and there over and above the damage cap lift at level 20. Bottom line, you may find it worth your while to dust off the BIBS after 20.

I am extremely suprised to see how many people say paladins stink at solo'ing. But there are a couple things that I'd like to express.

I'm a 38 paladin on Druzzil-Ro. I can solo most any blue with exception of GIANT mobs, like HGs, Griffons, and some SGs. This wasn't always true by any stretch of the imagination. The number one most important STATs that a tank can have? AC, AC, and some AC. That couple more AC on a face piece could mean the differece in you winning the fight or not.

Forget about the WIS and STR, get the AC. Lose the Plat ruby viel, get a dented mask. The 4ac will prolly mean you ending the fight with 2 bubs of health as opposed to dead.

Take this into consideration. Your max hit with your weapon is what? Now think how many times during the fight you actually hit for that amount? What is the percentage of max damage hits? 5% maybe 8% ?

With that said, how much more damage does a couple more STR equal to be? 5pts, 10pts? and those 5 or 10 pts are NOT going to hit every round.

AC does what? Decides how hard you are hit and IF you are hit at all. Once you get to a certain level of AC you will notice that things miss more, hit softer, and generally have a harder time making contact with you.

As far as weapon choice; I have to say a 1hs and a shield. I had a BIBs for a long time. Was a great weapon. I actually used it a little too much.
