So, what keeps ME interested? Many many people gripe and complain about the fact this game is so group oriented. That you cannot get anywhere soloing, etc, etc... Well, I've played this game longer, have put in more time than ANY other game in the history of me gaming (.. that's about 16 years). I credit that to the fact that the game IS in fact dependent on the group dynamic. I've played many many games by mysely and do they ever get boring after a while. "Predictable" is the word I'm thinking of. But these days, I find the strategy of getting the most out of each person's talents awesome. It's the "people element" that keeps it all interesting.

There will of course be times when I feel a little burnt out on what I'm doing and so I just take a break... but no longer than a week or two later and I'm back. Even recently I (much to my surprise) found fun in trade skills *gasp*. But, as it has turned out, I had great fun haggling over prices with folks, trying to hord the most cash I could, then go out and buy myself something nice (again, haggling for the best price I could get). Who knows why. Just my mood I guess.

I suppose when your ultimate goal isn't to get to level 60, you open up other possibilities. Ya, lots of folks would find trade skills boring, fine, we aren't all alike (duh). I found them boring for a long time and instead found entertainment in playing multiple characters. It greatly reduced the "monotony" factor by allowing variety in play style.

It has been said many times by many folks, but this type of game allows a great many avenues for enjoyment. Exploration (wander into that dungeon and see what's there), experimentation (combine that newly introduced gem with that metal and see what happens), and opening up to a variety of goals have proven to keep my attention a long while.

Just a couple thoughts,
