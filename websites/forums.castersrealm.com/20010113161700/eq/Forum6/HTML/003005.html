Mages have options, because they can solo if they want to, and group if they want to. I spent most of my character's life from 1 to 24 soloing, and then from 25 to 44 grouping, and from 44 to 45 (plan to do a few more levels like this) soloing. I got a good mix of both.

If you want to be in groups at higher levels, learn to control your pet and make him work FOR the group and not AGAINST the group (i.e. attacking mezzed mobs out of confusion). I learned this early on, and never have problems controlling Jobabner. I have grouped with other mages (in full groups) with less than stellar pet control, and they give mages a bad reputation and this may be why we are passed over sometimes (the assumption of "dont get the mage, his pet will cause a huge train")

Someone mentioned that your reputation and friends are important. This is true. I don't know how much your friends will powerlevel you, but at some point you'll have to start doing things on your own, and if you haven't met many people outside of your powerlevelers, it'll be a bit tougher. Alot of times I shout for a group and a friend in the zone tells me "hey, come join us over in [wherever]".

It all boils down to other's perceptions of mages. The facts are this: mages, when played correctly, are the best raw damage dealers in the game. However, the perception among people (mostly tank types) can sometimes be this: that pet's max damage isn't very high, it might pull us a train, I don't see how much he's nuking for, and he can't heal, so is he really all that good? If you're with people that understand and appreciate a mage's abilities, you will be "wanted". If you're with people that don't really understand mages and think of them as halfass wizards, you won't get much respect.

You said you were a power gamer. You'll like the mage.

A couple of things I try to do to help the image of mages when in groups (other than pet control) is always cast Damage Shield on tanks. You'd be surprised how many mages don't ... too much hassle they say. Tanks *really* love this spell ... they can see that constant message of damage to the mob hitting on them.

I also make it a point to Malise and have tied a /g message saying "%T is Malised" to a hot key to the spell. This lets casters see why they are getting less resists. I remember once leaving a group at the end of an eve and as I was headed back to FV to sell before I logged, I got a /tell from the wiz in the group saying he could tell the difference now that I was gone *beams*

As the previous poster mentioned, there can be a lot of negative feelings among players caused by a few bad mages out there. But we can do our bit to counter that if we want.
