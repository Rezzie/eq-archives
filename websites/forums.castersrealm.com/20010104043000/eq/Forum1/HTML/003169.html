The following are a few excerpts from Derek Sanderson a member of the OWO: Origin MMPORPG developmen team dated September 29, 2000:
(the entire text can be found at http://www.uo2.com/comments-dev_25.html)

"First, you will generally not die immediately in Origin when your hits points are reduced to zero, but will instead fall unconscious and need to be struck at least once more before dying. This unconscious state gives us a great deal of flexibility in how we design other systems; for example, we plan to have most low-level monsters leave unconscious newbies alone, rather than striking a killing blow against them. This will allow new player to learn the finer nuances of combat without experiencing the frustration of dying over and over. Unconsciousness will also allow players to duel ‘to the pain’ without having to kill each other, or players to organize their own arena fighting or group warfare.

When you die, you will remain in your body for a period of time so your fellow adventurers can have the opportunity to rescue you. You may always, of course, choose to leave your body at any time and resurrect at a bind point (generally one of the many inns scattered throughout the lands) you chose previously.

Your first death in a single day will have no other system-generated penalties associated with it - mistakes happen, and we decided to give everyone a ‘freebie’ to cover them (we can make combat that much harder because of it - muahaha!). Subsequent deaths will give you an ‘experience debt’, a separate pool of experience that must be worn away by normal experience gain. The higher your debt, the greater percentage of experience you gain that it absorbs.

Why give you a debt instead of taking away experience you have already earned? Because we believe that taking away something you have already earned is frustrating, while giving you an obligation to meet is not. In addition, giving you the debt allows us to conduct an experiment that we hope will narrow the gap between ‘casual’ and ‘power’ gamers: Your experience debt will go down over time, whether you are online or not.

This enables the casual, hour-a-day gamer to concentrate on having fun in his daily play session, because taking a death won’t mean a huge loss of time and effort he has already expended on his character. If he dies, he can log off and come back the next day, fresh and ready to fight. Power gamers will still advance more quickly, of course, since they will be able to pay off their debt earlier through actively gaining experience - we don't want to punish power gaming, after all; merely to make Origin equally enjoyable to the casual, part-time player."

I find this a sensible, reasoned, well thought out approach to the concept of death in a MMORGP. It's pretty obvious that they've been watching the players vs. "The Vision (tm)" on this one
