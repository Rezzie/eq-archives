Ello All..I've been reading the boards here forever but this will be my first time posting..I'll probably sound like a neewb and to some degree I am..but I thought I'd toss in my 2 cents.

The reason I play my enchanter almost exclusively (except for my Warrior when things get Intolerable) is because I enjoy the versatility I have...its a rare day that I don't switch my spell list to match the needs/Wants of my group. I'm not a post 50 Enchanter like most of you so I don't know much about that (well...just what I've read here ) But I will say that in some areas chain Stunning with Color Lockdown and AoE is a pretty nice trick..but ONLY in very exacting circumstances.

First you really need to know your color stuns and how they work. To Stun lockdown you need 2 stuns which we all have after 20 (People say 3 stuns can provide 100 percent lockdown but since I can do 85-90% with 2 stuns and some nice timing..I don't really see the point.) Stun durations DO stack over eachother because they are technically different spells to the system. The trick is to time it so your stacking your second 6 second stun...on the tail end (the last second or 2) of your first stun....that will provide you with mob stunnage while you wait for your stuns to refresh.

Ex. I'll color flux..wait a 4 count..and color shift...both grey out but by the time the stun wears off (or 1 or 2 seconds later) the color flux is back up.

Second you need to realize that this method is EXTREMELY mana efficent...at least around the 20-40 range. I can stand...slow a mob...Stun and still be at 60 Mana...medding between pulls and mezzing if things get crazy. Add in a Wizz Nuking the crap out of the mobs I'm stunning with Mana Efficent AOE and you get a pretty powerful combo.

Alas..all does not come up roses here. There are a few MAJOR cautions you need to watch out for.

First, If your wizzie or cleric are not Top Notch Players...The Wiz will die and you will follow shortly. We all know that AoE has that horrible tendency to Aggro 5 or 6 baddies on you. Your going to want a wiz who knows When to AoE to keep his Aggro to a Minimum because if all the mobs get on him he's dying whether your stunning or not.

Second your cleric MUST watch your back. Moreso than usual this strategy puts you in a sickeningly large amount of danger of an untimely death. If your cleric snoozes on the job it will be more efficent to just mez and pull slower. Dying is not good XP

As a side not you didn't mention your level but I've found with the right group this kind of strategy can be EXCELLENT Xp on the hard to mez break shadowed men camp. They spawn so fast you can't really break the spawn at the level they give XP...but with some AoE Power (You..Wizzie..Shaman...Druid..ect.) And a cleric watching out for all of you you can RAKE in the XP.

WOW..this was a lot longer than I thought it was going to be...Hope it helped/was coherent.

Amaar,

Levels 50+ is a whole 'nother ball game and I've heard marvelous things about the AEs wizards get at those levels.

I'm not sure about your level--so I'm talking about levels 20-35 or so (maybe higher, haven't experimented too much yet). At those levels PBAE killing is pretty darned fun. All you need are your first two PBAE stuns to get going.

One thing about AE killing--you have to plan for it. And, you typically don't use it when you have tanks. Stun-locking REQUIRES you to be in the middle of things. The best (and safest and most efficient) AE for this job is PBAE damage. So you need groups made up of PBAE damage dealers--essentially, wizards, magicians, clerics & druids. You don't want targeted AE--because it has a 4-5 target max and it can pull foes AWAY from you (bad!).

You want to check the spell lists for the above classes and see what levels they're within 0-5 levels above a new PBAE damage spell (period of relative efficiency). If they have 2 or more to choose from (even lower level==like 10-12 levels lower), they can mem both to 'chaincast' a bit faster.

You'll want to summon a pet. You'll want any mages to summon a pet. They're good for cleaning up efficiently. (If you have pets, do NOT let any of the pet owners pull or draw any attention until the mob has surrounded you--especially if there are spell casters). Pull in such a way that you get the biggest mob of blues you can manage and still survive. It is disastrous to end up with one or two of your foes off to the side and not within the PB radius--so your intial energy must be on rounding them up tight.

Use hotkeys like this:
/g Long Stun...
/cast 3
AND
/g Short Stun...
/cast 4

Have your beloved and other group members use hotkeys like this:
/g Casting PBAE 'Whatever it's named'
/g cast 8

It's a good idea to have your healer (you should have one for situations like link-dead magicians whose pets go beserk and a string of stun resists) to hotkey something like:
/g Healing %t
/cast 2

These help you and your group get a feel for timing and EXACTLY how much PBAE it takes to kill the stuff--and how much damage has been cast so far. You'll get good at seeing stuff thru the spam.

That's planned AE for me--and I love it. It's a little crazy--but it's darned efficient.

The only time I've had a group that used impromptu PBAE was when I was grouped with a cleric and druid and we were using my pet to tank. We knew we couldn't take 4 or more one by one because it was terribly mana-inefficient--so we resorted to PBAE whenever the druid brought back more than 3. It worked great--but they had just gotten new PBAEs and we were fighting light blues, so it was probably a special case. (They are also my 'regulars'--so we trust each other far more than any pickup group would.)
