The reasonn pets break mez(switch targets) is because they get hit.

The key is not letting them get hit but why do they get hit? The reason pets get hit is because they are the closest thing to a non mezzed mob. Mobs decide who to attack based on distance.

The key is to stay out of the way of the pulls and don't sic your pet until everything is mezzed. This way, if they come out of mez they will be so pissed at the enchanter they will ignore your pet.

I've been doing a bit of grouping in at the Frenzied and AM camps in Lower Guk ... and more often than not there are multiple MOB pulls. When there is an Enchanter in the group I usually do not send the pet in until I see that all of the MOBs are mezzed. Then I hit my hotkey for /assist and /pet attack. If you have a good Enchanter he/she will usually do a good job keeping those MOBs mezzed thus not having one break and get on your pet.

If a MOB breaks mez and comes over and whacks you ... wait for the Enchanter to re-mez then immediately hit your /pet backoff key and then send the pet back in on the original target. If you don't ... the pet will sprint over to the MOB that hit you and engage it after the other is dead.

Also move your pet around the room depending on which direction the pull is coming from. By doing this you will have less risk of one of the pulled MOBs jumping on your pet.

There is no way to avoid your pet getting on a MOB that you didn't want it to all day long ... but talk to your Enchanter ahead of time and get used to the timing of hitting /pet backoff right when the Enchanter casts his/her mez.

These tactics have helped me alot ... and I still learn alot every time I play. The key is experience in different situations.

All good advice! Nice to see more mages get better and pet control.

Talking to your chanter and tank is very useful if they aren't familiar with pets. Everyone needs to understand that yelling for a mage to back a pet off a mob that is beating on it is IMPOSSIBLE. The tank needs to taunt the mob off the pet, or the chanter needs to mez and we need to back pet off with good timing (usually takes a few times in a group to get timing of the chanter down).

So make sure everyone understands that you can't back a pet off that is being beat on. I never hit pet back off in those situations since all that does is cause the pet to get beat up without hitting back. I wait for the tank or chanter, then time the back off.

And I always hit pet back off just before the end of a fight to be safe, and send the pet back in.
