I was just wondering if all "buffs" were supposed to go away on logout or LD cause the magicians buffs do.
Let me explain....

Magicians have given up the "typical" caster buffs for the ability to summon anything they or their group might need. When I say "typical" caster buffs, I mean damage absorption spell, invlunerability spell, etc. We get the ability to summon food/water/weapons/vision enchancing/weight reducing bags etc. These are our buffs. Currently these items go poof when we logout or go LD.

Keep in mind that a magician has no defensive spells or abilities and thus must rely on his/her buffs which include the pet to survive. We have no other means to survive.

If we go Link Dead, we come back to NO buffs! We have no means to survive an encounter even with a green con'd mob. No some of you might be saying well now you know how it feels to be a melee character or . Well, my response to you is that the difference between a magician going toe-to-toe with a green con'd mob and a warrior going toe-to-toe with the same mob is that the warrior has 2, 3 or maybe even 4 times the hit points that a magician has. Not only that but they also have higher defensive skills not to mention the much higher AC. All other classes faced with this situation will have a much higher chance of survival than the magician. Dont forget, magicians do not get root, snare, stun, damage absorption, invuln, Feign death, etc. We only have our "buffs"(ie summoned items including our PET), and our nukes(which have an average casting time of 5 to 7 seconds) to survive.

Ok fine, no defensive spells or abilities is the price we pay for the ofensive damage out put that we as a class are capable of. I can deal with that. That is fair and balanced.

However, what I do find unfair is that our buffs dont have a set time before they expire. Ours are only there as long as we are able to maintain a constant connection to the server. If we lose connection to the server for whatever reason be it LD or logout our buffs are gone!

This is where IMHO the magicians need to be adjusted/tweaked!

THE POSSIBLE FIX:

Change the summoned items from semi-permanent "no rent" items to temporary buffs! Summoned items IMO should have a chance to decay anyway as they are summoned out of thin air! Allow the summoned items to stay for one week of game time! This still gives the risk of using summoned items and cuts out the LD issue all together.

The RISK is still there because if you dont keep up with the time you will still lose your items in the summoned bags as well as your coldstone and even the pet. I dont think losing the pet after so much time will go over well with some magicians but I cant say that I truly know of one single magician that has kept their pet for a solid "in-game" week.

Please consider my suggestion to correct this inbalance to the class.

Thank you,

Vondu Thinnaire

I am not sure if he meant 1 week real time of game play or 1 week of game time which would be a couple hours(??) of real time. But if you made the "buffs" summoned stuff have a real time duration of 1 real life day of game played then your summoned stuff would not decay in a fight unless you were not being very careful.

Look at it this way. As mages, we are use to summoning a lot of stuff when we get on. Well with this change it would be the same.

You first log on and replace any summoned items in your inventory with newly summoned items. Now during your next 8 (however long you play) hours of play, if you go LD you just log right back on with no worries. All your summoned stuff is there.

Of course is you play more than 24 hours straight you will need to make a point to stop and re-summon before you hit the 24 hour mark. But given the LD issue you would still be re-summoning less this way then we have it now. And the biggest point is we would control when we summon stuff not LD.

One of the great things about our summon stuff over other's buffs is their duration given no LD/camp. With this solution our summons will still last as long if not longer than we are use to. (with the exception of hard core players that play 24+ hours in a row with no LD)

This is a very good idea. To push it we would need the following. (feel free to add what I have forgotten)

1. Mage community support:

Do the majority of mages agree this is a good idea? Would the Mages be willing to support a well thought out and written post regarding this issue?

2. Before posting on an EQ forum or email Verant with the idea, we need to come together on the specifics of what we want.

Verant can easily dismiss an idea for a change/fix if some mages want it one way and some want it another. Verant can use the reason for each side not to change/fix the other sides request. I have seen this a lot already and I am still pretty new to the forums.


3. Have solutions in the request to overcome Verants standard replies to most request.

Verant has several standard replies that are used a lot. Whether it is "class envy", "engine can't support that", or "working as intended", we need to overcome these hurdles before we bring Verant to the table. Otherwise your request falls before you even get a chance to plead your case.

4. Nominate one person to organize the request. One person with very good writing skills to write it (I'm easily out of running for this . All the mages to give support to the thread when the leader of the request says to attack. hmmmmm think of it as a raid on Verant.

5. Do not compare classes.

Try not to put other classes down when putting the request together. You would be amazed at how much it would help our cause if you had many of the other classes posting to support our request. And it has been proven that saying "Mr. Necro or Mrs. Enchanter gets this so why don't we" just doesn't work and it shouldn't. There is a reason I picked a mage instead of a necro or chanter.

6. Do not put Verant down.

Ok, you own the company and write the code. Customer A tells you "I really like the game and you are doing a great job". Customer B tells you "I hate this game and you are so stupid for making such a crappy game". Now, Customer A and Customer B submit a request to have something fix. Tell me....do you work on Customer A's problem first or Customer B's problem first?

7. Pick your battles.

Concentrate on what we think we can really get changed and what we have full support from the mage community. I have seen very nice request be overlooked because Verant went after another request that was easily shut down. Verant gets the kudos as to say they view mage's request but at the same time side stepping request they may not be able to avoid so easily.


There is more but time is short. I hope someone can be nominated as a mage figurehead and pull us together. I see many bright minds on the mage boards and I believe we could do wonders.
