I'll reply...

I play a 52 druid on Morell-Thule. The guild I play with is very organized, and fairly adventurous (plane of hate, kedge keep, the new kunark and velious dungeons).

These raids are very organized, with each member/class playing specific roles. My role is usually to do damage shields, regen, heals, and to keep evac up. This is done well because our enchanter keeps us with clarity constantly.

I have to say that in a group indoors, the druid's role changes to more of a maintenance role. That damage shield on your group adds up to huge amounts of damage and it totally unresisted. Regen on warriors can and will save their lives. Evac is a 7 second cast that can make the difference in whether or not you're doing corpse recovery.

But you're not always with a group...and so you solo. Soloing is great. Ensnare lasts 9min, with form of the great wolf, you easily stay ahead of your target, while makeing sure you keep your target walking towards you on a path that will not attract attention from other mobs. With drifting death, breath or ro, and immolate, it's nearly dead by the time the spells wear off, perhaps a starfire to complete the job. This is on blue formidable Drolvarg Ragers...I tend to leave the greenies alone.

Also keep in mind that harmony is one of your best friends. The knowledge that you can hit a target in the group of mobs, and only one will come is invaluable. Outdoors, your crowd control rivals an enchanter pre-pull.

Some things that I think could be different with the class is the fact that when we charm a pet, it turns aggro on us when it breaks. Every fibre of my roleplaying being says that animals would be glad to help one who is in tune with nature alongside them. Now, to be fair, you get a spell at 55 where the animal DOES NOT aggro you after, but you cannot use this spell on anything higher than a level 35 animal. By then, that is completely useless to you. You also finally get a pet at that level, but it does the least damage of all the pets in the game, equal to perhaps a shaman's first pet...without the dual's.

But, there is justice. You have more options for travel than anyone in the game, I believe. Sure, the wizards can get you to the planes, but you'll never just go up there by yourself anyways. Planes are well calculated procedures taking several hours. BUT, we can go anywhere we want anytime we want. We are free to fight all over the land at any time we decide. With tracking, we can always find people in our group from anywhere in the zone as well as the prey that will give us the most experience.

To sum it up, I don't think there is a better class in the game. Big damage spells, dots, movement control, tracking, traveling, heals, group buffs and damage shields, selective pulling outdoors. With Kunark and Velious, there is absolutely no reason you can't fight on your own or with a group all the way to 60.

-Sienne

Little bit of clarifying info on druid pets that were mentioned in the first reply.

I'm a level 55 druid on Fennin Ro and a member of a somewhat contriversial (to put it lightly) guild that does quite a few raids, on one of the raids we picked up the druid pet, which I got, the pet does 28 double attack, 10 kick and 10 bash max. That is, of course, with full buffs. Only thing is that the pet has about 700-800 hp with sln on. As far as my feelings about being a druid at high levels...it's a pain. Most people, usually melee types that have never played a caster or know very little about our role, look at us as a weak wizard, which is so far from true. The more 'enlightened' people will typically invite a high level druid only if they can't get a shaman as we can't slow, we can't mez, we can't haste...all we can do is buff and heal and throw and occasional nuke or dot. The thing that makes all that even more depressing for me is the fact that there are very few places that you can actually solo at that level. Skyfire comes to mind, dreadlands is completely green with exception to Gorenaire and the Dread Widow or the very rare ravishing drolvargs....even burning woods greens out at about 54-55 to the point that you spend more time killing greenies that jump you than gettin xp making soloing less benificial than grouping. It's unfortunate that our experience gain is hindered by ignorance of others rather than having to gain more exp to level like say a troll sk. I know I sound like I'm down with my druid, but I'm not, only down with poor knowledge of our class and people thinking that all a druid can do is solo, which at this level is no where near easy or benificial...and to whoever posts after me saying 'quad kite raptors in TD', guess what, even the raptors are about 50% green to a 55 :-)

One thing to remember about being a druid, thre are few areas where we excel. We're lousy wizards, pathetic clerics, and have zero melee ability. However, we have an extensive line of spells and can fill a lot of roles in a group. We can pull singles using harmony, we can park 'adds' to offer some Crowd Control, we can nuke (which becomes fairly powerful in the 40's, and by 53 our DoT's stack for a huge amount of damage. We can regen, heal, buff attk speed,
etc. It all depends on what kind of character you want to play. I 'll tell you this, play a druid to post 50 and trying to play another class full time will suck. SoW and Teleports alone would be reason enough to stick to a druid. And as someone has previously mentioned, on most high level raids people need 'bodies', so forget about trying to justify your usefulness..even tho in our case it's not hard to do at all. And by the way, I got through level 45 very quickly using charm in 'overthere'. Just snare them first so when charm breaks you don't have your butt chewed off. Works well in 'east wastes' too.
