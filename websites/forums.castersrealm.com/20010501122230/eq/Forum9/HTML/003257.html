Okay, campers... I said I would put some research time into this, and I have. I've seriously debated not posting this, as it inevitably starts the flames up, but a promise is a promise. This is my preliminary report of results found, may or may not be correct scientific proceedure, but only did one thing for me:

Satisfied my question as to whether mezzing is aided by high Charisma.

If you don't want to peruse the research to see it yourself, here's the big question:

Will higher CHA help me mez?
Yes. Higher CHA will yield less resists when mezzing.

Now if that peaked your interest, here is the tests:

I started off just mezzing myself, because it's safer. I hit 49th, told my guild I was going to Erudin for a year, and to leave me alone as I'd be busy. I arrived by the bank, sorted all my CHA and MR gear out, and got to work.

The 1st Test:
1. Cast Suffocate on myself (allowing me to break mez within a tic for 108 seconds.
2. Cast Mesmerize (level 4) on myself repeatedly, in batches of 25 casts, recording the results.

I only tested two Charisma levels, my high and low, for the first batch of research, to decide if it would be worth further exploration. Low was 133 CHA, high was 217.

I tested vs three MR ratings, 25, 50, and 99.

I tested each category 100 times, as a preliminary study was just seeking if this was worth delving into.

133 CHA vs Level 49, 25 MR
100 casts yielded 10 resists
217 CHA vs Level 49, 25 MR
100 casts yielded 7 resists

133 CHA vs Level 49, 50 MR
100 casts yielded 40 resists
217 CHA vs Level 49, 50 MR
100 casts yielded 36 resists

133 CHA vs Level 49, 99 MR
100 casts yielded 90 resists
217 CHA vs Level 49, 99 MR
100 casts yielded 82 resists

After getting some sleep, I logged back in and reperformed the tests using Entrance, getting the following results:

133cha/25mr - 10%
217cha/25mr - 8%
133cha/50mr - 41%
217cha/50mr - 36%
133cha/99mr - 88%
217cha/99mr - 81%

I went to sleep again, as this took forever with the increased mana load.

Then I called upon my guild to assist me. I found a level 50 guard, and had my guild sitting around to bail me out. I do NOT know what the guards magic resistance was, but I mezzed him 100 times (believe me it took all day).

At 133cha I had 45% resist rate.
At 217cha I had 37% resist rate.

I then repeated the process keeping Tashania (-33MR) on the guard.

At 133cha I had 15% resist rate.
At 217cha I had 10% resist rate.

(By the way, if you want to try this, have a guild chanter with you to hold it mezzed while you med back up to full... eventually you will run out of mana unless you let mez go to at least half duration, which blows.)

At this point, I stopped my research. For me, my question has been answered. With a higher CHA, I had less resists EVERY test when mezzing, so I won't attribute it to a fluke. However, the impact was so small, that I can't warrant scrounging up 40 points of CHA gear and keeping Adorning Grace up all the time.

To be blunt, I had to make little hatch-marks on a piece of paper for days to see the difference.

A side proof gained by this study is that droppng a mob's MR is a MASSIVE assistance to mezzing.

In no way have I found the soft-cap, the exact numbers CHA helps, or even checked vs many varying cons... or even found what the minimum resist chance is. Plus, I didn't try Entrance or Dazze on the guard--I was just too sick of mezzing.

And I don't care. What I HAVE done, is prove to myself that CHA isn't worth the cut in AC/HP/Mana to have it high. I carry my CHA gear around, but it's for charming, not mezzing.

The next study (I'm abandoning this one to seek a relationship, my notepad and I have decided to see other stationery) will be CHA effects on Color Stuns, and shortly after on fearing. My lovely in-game wife has volunteered to get the piss stunned out of her for a few days then feared into a wall for a decade or so. Everyone please take a moment to thank her.

Once all that's done I'm going to charm some mobs for a few decades and see how large the impact is there. I know I can see a difference with higher CHA when charming at least. At 133CHA I'm scared to charm for more than 30 seconds.

As for AC/hp - well, if you have 200 int (trivial to do), you have a reasonable cha in the 110-150 range (again, trivial), then what do you aim for in equipment? There's not many choices really - mana, ac, hp, resists, str. Any other options (note that agi == ac and sta == hp)?

I would say that mana is pretty useless. How often do you go from FM to OOM with 200 int with no chance to regen that mana? Yes, having a larger mana pool is always nice, but I'd bet that you will receive negligible advantages. Plus there are still rumors that there is a max mana cap even for high levels.

While resists are good, really high resists are really only important against certain ubermobs. The fact of the matter is that enchanters are pretty useless against most uber mobs. I tend to hide or camp out for most of them, simply because I'm of more use alive afterwards than I am trying to affect a dragon. For things where rune-ing makes sense, I'll do that, but those are the same things where it's melee damage not AE spell effects. So resists, while nice, seem to be a wash for enchanters.

I simply don't see any real point behind raising str, or at least focusing on it as something to raise.

That brings us down to AC and HP. Enchanters get beat on. We get beat on a LOT. We tend to be mana sinks for healers to pour into. What is the real difference between 600 and 800 AC? Good question. If someone is willing to test it, please do so (must be a non-green mob, must spawn at same level everytime - good candidates are guards and juggernauts). The effect of HP is more obvious. Two Velium FO rings add 130 hp. Yes, that's less than one hit at high levels, but consider that if you have 2800 buffed hp without them you are adding nearly 5% to your HP's. If you're looking at unbuffed hp, you're adding closer to 10-15%. I'd say that's significant. The key to the HP/AC strategy is to live long enough that a healer can get a cast off on you. I know many times I've been sure I was dead with no visible red left on my bar when a druid's superior heal hits me followed by a divine light from the cleric.

Hopefully I'll have the chance to get uber buffed this weekend. I'm pretty sure I'll break 3000 hp and have 750-755 AC. And this is at L57 with several pieces of equipment to improve yet.
