I've actually been making the claim that hybrids "steal" extra experience in a group, but based on the logic of what Verant has said about how experience is divided, and the fact (as mentioned) that Abashi has ALWAYS avoided the question - except to say that the experience penalty "goes away" for the hybrid when grouped (which, obviously, means that someone else pays it).

For many (likely most) players at high levels, the game is about slowing adding up those pixels of experience (and/or camping some item for endless hours). When you're sitting on your butt killing the same 6 mobs every 22 minutes, you -care- how fast experience is adding up.

(If you don't like to play that way, then that's all well and fine. Once you've done every quest for your class, travelled to every zone in the game, sat for hours buffing newbies, etc. you do run out of things to do; as someone else stated, if you have goals to get to the planes or to kill dragons, then it matters how fast you get experience. The most fun times I remember in EQ are when my ranger buddy and I roamed the world, just exploring - but eventually the world is explored.)

Unliked the person who posted the editorial, my belief is that this is another place where the experience penalty is an unjustified pain. Yes, a hybrid does bring some nice perks, but they are no nicer than, say, a warrior's ability to tank MUCH better than any hybrid. Especially at very high levels, specialization is what matters.

Someone else questioned what would happen if you had more people in a group - that will depend on the group's members. If almost all the members are "pure" (i.e., no penalty), then, yes, the penalty of the hybrid is spread across more people. Here are some simple numbers (hypothetical, but they demonstrate the mathematical principle):

Case 1:
--------------------------------------------
2 non-hybrids with the same amount of experience (1000 each) kill a xvart worth 100 experience points. Since they have the same amount of total experience, they share equally (each person gets 50 experience).

Case 2:
--------------------------------------------
1 hybrid (1250 experience), 1 non-hybrid (1000 experience) kill the same xvart. The hybrid gets 1250/(1250 + 1000) or 55.5% of the experience, so the "pure" class gets only about 44.5 experience points instead of 50 now.

Case 3:
--------------------------------------------
6 pure classes (1000 exp each) kill the same xvart. Each of those folks get 1/6 of the 100 exp, or 16.7 experience each.

Case 4:
--------------------------------------------
1 hybrid (1250 exp) and 5 pure classes (1000 exp each) are grouped. The hybrid takes 1250/(5000 + 1250) or 20% of the experience. Each of the other folks gets 1000/(5000 + 1250) or 16% of the experience. They each lost only about 1 point of experience that they would have gotten if the hybrid was replaced by a pure class.

You can carry this example out and change the size of the group to see what % exp each person loses:

Group Size.......Lost EXP with 1 Hybrid
---------------------------------------
2......5.5% exp loss
3......2.5% exp loss
4......1.5% exp loss
5......1.0% exp loss
6......0.7% exp loss

Remember, that's if you have only one hybrid and the rest are folks who have 0 penalty or bonus.

Let me give you the group that -I- was in 99% of the time:

paladin
paladin
ranger
ranger
wizard
shaman

4 hybrids and 2 pure classes. Using the hypothetical numbers above, each hybrid would get:

1250/(4*1250 + 2*1000) = 17.9% of the experience.

Each non-hybrid would get:

1000/(4*1250 + 2*1000) = 14.3% of the experience.

In other words, each hybrid gets about 18 experience from the kill, each non-hybrid gets 14 experience. If they were all non-hybrids, each person would get 100/6 or about 17 experience. In other words, a group full of hybrids can really hurt. If each has a 25% experience penalty, having 4 hybrids means it's like giving a full person of experience into the ether (for the non-hybrids).

This, of course, does not just apply to playing with hybrids, but you can extend the thinking to playing with folks of a higher level, folks who have gone through a hell level (e.g., you're 44 and they're 46 is going to be a lot worse than when you were 41 and they were 43).

The reason this questions is more bothersome is that hybrids are -usually- not better than pure classes at the same level (e.g., a 45th level paladin does not add more to a group than a 45th level warrior). Very early on, it -was- the case, and that was one of the reasons for the penalty. With many tweaks to other classes (warriors, especially), the "advantage" of being a hybrid disappeared - you do get spells and a couple of abilities, in exchange for things like much lower AC, lower fighting skills, lack of dual wield, etc. etc. (depending on hybrid class). If a paladin was a warrior + spells + lay hands, then a group SHOULD pay a premium in experience to have him there. If the group can get along better with a warrior than with a hybrid, then the experience penalty is flawed and the group would be better served (statistically, not in terms of fun, necessarily) to get a warrior.

Some folks seem to miss the point a bit, though (including the author of the article). If you're -sole- purpose is to level, then yes, avoid hybrids. Hybrids don't "destroy" group experience, they take a small amount (which can add up over months of play, tho) from each kill. I was very good friend with the people in my group, and, as you can see, we didn't have an optimal group by any stretch - there were lots of things we couldn't do because of it, but we did have fun and we did make it to 50.

The problem is this: you shouldn't have to play with a friend IN SPITE of an experience penalty that is not justified. Verant has refused to remove the penalties, claiming that it would mean that people wouldn't suddenly gain a lot of levels. This is poppycock. It -would- be some programming work to fix the problem (e.g., store each person's level, how far they are into that level; change the experience tables; give the person the amount of experience that would put them were they were, level-wise), but they don't see it as an issue that is worth fixing when it is only hybrids complaining about it.

The funny thing is that when I first started posting my hypothetical mathematics to show that hybrids were probably "stealing" experience from their groups, people on the paladin & ranger boards (at EQ.Stratics at the time) pretty much thought I should keep it to myself as it could make it harder for hybrids to get groups. My feeling was, and is, that this would be the only way to get the penalty removed - if people stop playing hybrids because they can't get groups at high levels, then it would affect Verant in the only way they seem to be affected - how many people are playing each class.
