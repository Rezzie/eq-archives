Hell Levels (30, 35, 40, 45, 50-60)
Typically during these levels, you need anywhere from two to three times the amount of experience that you normally need to level. For example, I play a wizard who is level 44, and a cleric who is level 40 at the same time on two different accounts. In the time my wizard gained 5 blue bubbles of experience (one orange bubble), My cleric only gained two blue bubbles. This was while I had my two characters grouped together killing a static mob. Basically, during a hell level, it takes a 'hell' of a long time to get out of it.

Post Hell Levels (31, 36, 41, 46)
These levels you will find that you actually get through them quickly. The catch here is that if you die during these levels, you lose anywhere from two to three times the amount of experience you normally lose when you die. Whenever I'm in a post hell level, I play the game in 'safe mode.' That means, I travel only to places I know very well, and fight mobs I am very familiar with. At level 31, I died with one and a half orange bubbles of experience into it. That one death sent me back to level 30 with half of an orange bubb to get in order to get back to 31. That wasn't fun.

Hell levels are a side effect of a multiplier Verant uses. When you enter a hell level you have to make up for that new muliplier all in that level rather then doing it over the next 5. Effectively you need twice as much exp to get through a hell level than a normal level. A side effect of this is when you die your exp bar appears to go down less when you die (about 1/2 as it works out). Another side effect of this is that in the levels after a hell level (which requires about 1/2 the exp of that hell level) it will appear that you loose more exp when you die. In truth however you always loose the same percentage of exp when you die.

Hell levels are

30 35 40 45 54 59

Every level after 50 is NOT a hell level, it is merely a 10% increase in required experience over the last level. Hell levels are much more demanding than that.

Level 50 is not a hell level.

The original reason for hell levels was a bug I believe. At each of these levels, 30 35 40 45, your xp modifier goes up by 0.1 ...10%. Way back when EQ was starting, someone probably mistyped a line of code and suddenly at each of those levels you had to gain 2* as much xp as you normally would have.

The way I understand is that by the time they got around to fixing the bug, they decided they liked it or just didn't feel like fixing it and left the hell levels in.

Level 54 and 59 I can only assume were created on purpose, to continue the line of hell levels and make level 55 and 60 bigger accomplishments when reached.

A side effect of hell levels are the post hell levels, also known as the Purgatory levels. These are 31, 36, 41, 46, 55, and 60. When you die in purgatory, you lose a larger amount of xp than normal.

When you die in EQ you lose 10% of the experience it would have taken you gain the previous level. IE: say level 34 takes you 1000000 xp to complete. So if you die in level 35, you lose 10% of that...100000 xp.

Now lets say in level 35 it takes you 2100000 xp to level...1050000 times 2 because of it being a hell level. So if you die in level 36, you would lose 210000 xp...a very large amount compared to how much you would lose if it wasnt a purgatory level.

The last two purgatory levels, 55 and 60, have a xp cap on how much xp you can lose from death due to the extreme difficulty of gaining xp at those high levels.

There you go, the as-complete-as-I've-heard-so-far explanation of how Hell and Purgatory work in EQ.


Edit: actually i left something out, which i know someone would point out if I didn't add it. You do not lose 10% of the xp you needed to gain your last level when you die...you lose 10% of the xp a human warrior of the same level as you needs to gain your last level.

Confusing isn't it...I'll try to explain it.

Say you're a level 10 erudite cleric. You die. To gain level 9, you had to earn 1200 xp. BUT a human warrior only had to earn 1000 xp to gain level 9. Because your xp loss from death is based off of the human warrior model, you only lose 10% of 1000 xp...100 xp...instead of 10% of 1200 xp...120 xp.

This is a little off base...

First of all hell levels are 30 35 40 45 51 and above.

Second of all, you are correct. All levels above 50 are a +10% exp. But... that is what a hell level is. There is a formula for exp needed for the next level (I am sorry - I do not have it on this computer, or I would post it here). The formula has a multiplier on the end if it. The multiplier is 1.0 for levels 1-29. At level 30 the modifier becomes 1.1. This means you have to earn an extra 10% of the TOTAL experience you have ever earned for levels 1 through 29 in addition to the normal experience for level 30 and the +10% for level 30 itself. The modifier stays 1.1 for levels 30, 31, 32, 33 and 34. Since you have already done the 'makeup' experience on levels 1 through 30, you only have to earn this extra 10% experience for the current level. At level 35 the modifier becomes 1.2. This is another 10% of the total exp you have ever earned for levels 1 through 34. At 40 the modifier becomes 1.3 - at 45 it becomes 1.4 - at 51 it becomes 1.5 - at 52 it becomes 1.6 at 53 it becomed 1.7 - at 54 it becomes 1.9 (notice level 54 is a DOUBLE hell level and all levels above 50 are in fact hell levels) - this continues and level 59 is another double hell level.

Hell levels were never a bug. Before the game was released they thought people in alphan and beta testing were leveling too fast after about 30th level. They added the multiplier at that time. They notices it made a big jump in experiance at those levels, but did not think it was a big issue.

Non-exact example to illustrate what's happening:

You already know about the xp multipliers so I'll skip that. Now, let's say from 30 to 31 takes 2000xp and from 31 to 32 takes 1000xp

One bubble (one fifth of total xp to get from the last level to the next level) of xp in 30 is 400xp and one bub in 31 is 200xp.

Therefore...

if you die in 30 and lose 100xp you appear to lose 1/4 of a bubble

if you die in 31 and lose the same 100xp you appear to lose half a bubble

You in fact lost the same (or in a real EQ example using the correct figures you lose the same portion of xp) but it just looks like you lost a different amount.
