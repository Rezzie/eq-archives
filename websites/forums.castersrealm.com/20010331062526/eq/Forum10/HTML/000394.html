Proactive parking, If your puller brings
three or four and you don't have crowd
control, start rooting the extras before
you cast any heals. Then, they will not
all jump you after your first heal because
they are already root parked. This does
piss them off for being rooted and they
will still come for you when it breaks but
solves part of the problem of two or three
going for you at once unless you have a
bad string of root durations.

Option two is to group with higher caliber tanks. I was grouping with several talented monks for quite a few levels and monks don't have taunt. So a talented monk will deliberately peg every single mob pulled one time to put himself on all their hatelists so they don't peel for you at the first heal. A talented warrior will taunt every mob. I have a warrior twink and I commonly
change targets and hit taunt then switch
back to my original target when I know there
is no crowd control. They should also
taunt mezzed mobs twice before breaking
mez when you do have an enchanter.

Also remember that if your own health drops
below two bubbles left then mobs may
frenzy on you and no amount of taunting
will pull them off you then so be sure to
keep your own health at half or better and
have a divine up. I used to hate popping
divine aura and firmly knowing at times
that it was only delaying my inevitable
death by 18 seconds as I fully knew they
were going to jump me as soon as it dropped.
Divine barrior's extra healing can take you
out of frenzy zone but the longer casting
time and higher mana cost made me not
switch to using it till level 45 or so.
I only keep one divine spell up ever.
There are too many other usefull spells to
keep up in your limited 8 slots to waste
two of them on divine.

Consider increasing your AC and actually encouraging spares to attack you whilst the tanks take care of the others. I will often use root on some and then aggro others to get them off the tanks and onto me. This is less and less as mobs hit harder but can easily be used at level 28 if you have decent gear.

Make sure tanks are topped up on hps before they go to pull so that they dont need a heal so quickly. Use a lower level heal instead of your best heal (ie the caster heal).

Why is the other tank in trouble? Only one tank generally should be taunting the other should only be assisting the main tank. The secondary tank should only come and help you by taunting a mob off you (or the enchanter). Too many tanks at your level think that they need to mash that taunt button as much as possible without realising what troubles it causes.
