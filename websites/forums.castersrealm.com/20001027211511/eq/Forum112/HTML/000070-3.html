Actually the link above made some bad conclusions about groups not alleviateing exp penalties. HIs conclusions directly contradict his statement about how exp is divided.

According to him if you have a warrior and a TSK at the same level and tehy kill something worth 100000 exp, then the SK will get most of that exp.

Example: lvl 40 warrior has 20,000,000 exp total.

lvl 40 SK has 40,000,000 exp total.

They group get the 100,000 exp. 66,666 will go to SK and 33,333 will go to warrior. Simple as that.

Now with a group with two SKs both with 40,000,000 exp they would each get 50,000 exp from the same kill.

This is all from what he said about how exp gets divided between group members.

Thus in groups your exp penalty is shared by the group members. Personally I have absolutely no problem with this.

I play a level 37 IKSAR SK and have been practicing with fear a lot. If you find a safe place to do this such as level 1 of Dalnir next to the zone, do it. Through a couple of DOTs on the MOB then fear it - attack it while it is feared and remember to recast fear before it would normally wear off - If u do it right u can kill the MOB with almost no damage and only take 3 or 4 min to regen mana. Learned this trick because I had a coerced SK-type cast fear on me til I was dead. BTW this is the best way to kill casters in Dalnir, especially those pesky female gnomes. I have also done very well with this at the giant forts in Warslick's Woods and Frontier Mountains.

I love my SK, except the EXP penalty. I can become invisible, fear MOBs, Feign Death, DOT, Locate Corpse, cast Vampiric Embrace, have a pet, Tank, Lifetap, heal by transfer of HP via Dark Empathy, and more. All this by level 30.
