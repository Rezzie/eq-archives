Jeez, necromancers are by far the whiny-est class around (yes, I play one...but I also play other characters).

1: Did you have fun for the first 50 levels of your character's "life"?

2: If you're not having fun now, why are you continuing to play?

3: Verant is under no obligation to give you a refund because you're not having fun.

"A group of Necro's can't even do there own epic quest together, I haven't seen such a lack of planning in long time from company."

There are no epics that can be completed by a group of that class. The bard epic requires a white dragonscale (used in the White Dragonscale Cloak), a red dragonscale (used in Red Dragonscale Breastplate AND the warrior epic quest), and a semi-rare drop from Trakanon.

The shaman epic requires the shaman to go DEEP inside The Hole, certainly not something a single group (of any one class) could accomplish.

There is no NEED to complete the epic weapon. If the necromancer WANTS to complete the epic, then by all means they should try. However, epic weapons are meant to be unatainable by the majority of players...that's why they're called EPIC. As it is, for those classes that require a god or dragon drop, no more than 52 players a year (per server) will complete their epics. Among those are: bards, magicians, necromancers, warriors, rangers...I'm not familiar enough with the other classes' epic quests.

"Since the EQ staff is ignoring all our issues with the Groupability of the Necro class..."

The "issues" the necromancer class has are extremely minor compared to those suffered by other classes. Necromancers are extremely useful in a group situation, but usually those who have solo'd for 55 levels are unaware of their role in a group. This does not mean they are useless in groups however.

"points to the fact that they want all the old Necro players to simply leave EQ."

Stop being so self-centered and take a look around. Necromancers are far from broken, and, despite adjustments, are still extremely powerful...arguably more powerful than most other classes. Verant doesn't want any class to leave EQ, they all have their roles in the game. *IF* they truly wanted any one class to leave they would do far more than adjust a couple facets of that class by minor degrees.

My suggestion is, if you are that unhappy with the necromancer class, play something else. If you're that unhappy with Everquest as a game, simply stop playing. Yet to demand a refund, for a product that you've used (and presumably enjoyed) for the past two years (assuming you bought it on day 1), is completely ridiculous.
