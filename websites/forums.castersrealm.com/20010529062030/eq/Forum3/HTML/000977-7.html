Author Topic:   The Casual Gamer

I just had to write this. It is fantasy. But I was prompted to analyze the current camping situation for nice items and the impact it had on the casual gamer. Anyway, here goes…

I had a friend wait on the Frenzied Ghoul list for 14 hours this weekend before he finally got in. He made the decision that he wanted an FBSS, so decided to suck it up and take the plunge. That is 14 hours to GET IN the group. I don’t know what the chance is that he will actually get an FBSS drop, but I would suspect that it is low, so he will be forced to come back weekend after weekend and wait 14 hours. What is a casual gamer supposed to do? How many other MOBS drop haste equipment?

Well, as many people have pointed out, casual gamers don't want to have the best equipment, we just want the chance to find useful equipment without having to wait on a list for 20 hours. Statistically, Verant has said that the people that play the most deserve to have the best stuff. I totally agree. It would be crazy to think otherwise. However, I think the definition of best shouldn’t be an all or none proposition. So, I come back to the FBSS.

Why aren't their baby versions of this in the game? Why is the Frenzied Ghoul the only spot to get one outside of the planes (I don’t know what haste items drop in Kunark)? Why shouldn’t the FBSS drop with a great variety of haste bonuses? Those that drop from the Frenzied Ghoul range from +15% to +25%, with the +25% being quite rare. Thus, Joe Casual gets in the Frenzy camp, a +15% FBSS drops. He either wins the lotto or Sam Power says, “I have a +20% one already. I am here for the +25% one, so go ahead and lotto this one amongst you casual gamers.” Great! Casual gamer is happy because he has his FBSS, which provides functionality he can use. It may not be the best, but he didn’t have to wait for freaking ever to get it. Sam Power is happy when his +25% finally drops after camping for 250 hours because he knows he has received something that no casual gamer will most likely endure, thus maintaining his status as one of the elite.

Better yet, why shouldn’t there be a chance of finding a +4% to +14% FBSS (with the +14% being very rare) while hunting Goblins out in the Frontier? Let us suppose that I had a +4% common drop FBSS. Boy, finding that +6% FBSS would be nice. Maybe I would get EXTREMELY lucky and get a +9% FBSS drop. Maybe MOBS in the City of Mist have a chance to drop a FBSS that ranges from +10% to +22%.

As a level 40 warrior, why shouldn’t I have the option of having some haste item already? Is that out of line? Would having a 10% haste item be that unbalancing?

I am not sure what this would do to the Frenzied Ghoul list. Part of me says it would cause more camping because every power gamer would be shooting for the +25% version. Part of me says that more people would rotate in and out because they understand how it takes exponentially increasing amount of time to get the FBSS with the better haste % and leave long before they would of under the current drop pattern. Yes, drop rates would need to be increased to make this work.

I would expect Verant to argue that then everyone will have a haste item because the power gamers will throw away +12% haste items left and right. Guess what? No matter how hard you make the drop % or the spawn rate on items, at some point, most will become ubiquitous that their rarity is no longer debatable. Everyone has an SSB, BEM, Quest Armor or Exe Axe these days.

This could be extended so much farther. Any weapon that drops could have variable damage/delay. Any item that drops could have a chance of procing some set of spells or most likely, none at all. Any item that drops could have variable stats. Maybe the Warrior could trade his Dagger X that has a +2 INT bonus with the Necromancer who happened to get a +3 STR drop version. There is the extremely rare possibility that the Dagger could drop with +3STR, +3INT and +3DEX. It would be quite exciting to not know who was going to drop what and in which variety it would come. I really hate how static the drops are. Most MOBS seem to have a common and a rare. WOW. 2 items! If the rare is a 1:25 chance, you basically know what you are getting when the MOB spawns. YUCK!

I would guess that the current item-encoding scheme that Verant uses would not allow them to use an item template with the detailed modifiers stored on each character. Thus, in order to get the item variations, they would have to manually create a separate item for each variation in their database, which would cause the database to become HUGE very quickly.

Anyway to recap…

* Variety is good. This method of populating item characteristics makes an infinite number of item drops possible.
* With an infinite # of item drops possible, you will NEVER know before hand which random numbers are going to be generated and thus determine the nature of the item. You may have some idea of the class of item, but not its detailed information.
* Power gamers continue to get the best stuff, because they may see Item X 100 times, thus by the laws of probability, they have a greater chance of seeing the random numbers come together in such a way as to produce an exceptional item.
* While the best variations of item X are just as rare, if not rare, casual gamers have the chance to get and item formally reserved for the power gamers, but generally in a less powerful form. Item drops would need to be increased.
* Let the power gamers battle it out for the best variations of item X. We casual gamers would be happy with any incarnation of item X.

Let me finish up that I think Kunark is a step in the right direction. At 35+, finding places to get xp was getting very difficult back on the mainland. So, I think the casual gamer has his choice of where to go to get xp now without waiting on some damn list. Now, they just need to fix it so you don't to be on some damn list to get item drops.
