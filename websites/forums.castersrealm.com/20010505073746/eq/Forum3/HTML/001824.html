It has always been standard practice of mine to just cast Augmentation on those wearing FBSS or better, or wielding a Mith 2Hander.

I guess that will have to change. Dangit, all it does is increase the amount of mana I have to blow to keep these guys up to speed.

It won't do a damn thing to devalue the FBSS, as Abashi insinuated. Haste items that include good AC components will always be more valued than haste items with little or no AC components. All this does is make the enchanter do more work. The bards always would sing the best haste song they have anyhow, so no change there.

This is a nerf mainly of enchanters. Why? Without an enchanter present, the FBSS was as fast as an FBSS. No change there, as the speed of a "raw" FBSS hasn't changed. With an enchanter present, the FBSS was a lot faster. The previous behavior just meant that enchanters could get away with casting just Augmentation on someone with an FBSS, now to get the same haste effect an enchanter will have to cast Celerity or Swift Like The Wind, which take more mana, don't last as long, and don't add the nice +agi and stamina regen components of Augmentation.

Shaman would cast their good haste spells anyhow. If a shaman only casted Quickness because they could get away with that and still get to max haste with an FBSS then they better be prepared to recast it all the frigging time. The higher haste spells last significantly longer, so for shaman they are more desirable. But Augmentation lasts the longest of them all, and it was very nice the way it worked in the past.

So to recap, the only class whose haste spells conveniently allowed for a lower level spell to be used with an FBSS for full effect were the enchanters. The bards don't use mana anyhow for their haste songs, so there is no reason for any bard to sing anything but their highest and best song available to them. Shaman could theoretically use a lower level haste spell than their best, but in reality that is more of a pain in the butt than it's worth because the lower level shaman haste spells last for a very short time and hence would have to be reapplied much more often. That leaves just Enchanters.

I suppose one more group hurt by this is groups including twinks who have an FBSS and an enchanter of lvl 16 and less than lvl 29, where the twink's FBSS plus the enchanters Quickness or Alacrity would in the past stack with the FBSS for a full 70% haste, where now they will only stack for whatever they stack for after the patch. Thus twinks with FBSS get less of a benefit from haste spells.

To answer Baelish's question as to whether this will increase the value of haste-casting classes the answer is NO, it will devalue them. If a shaman casting Alacrity on an FBSS wearer previously got the wearer to 70% haste, and a shaman casting Alacrity on an FBSS wearer now gets him up to only, say, 50%, then the shaman has added LESS value now than they added previously, hence they (not the FBSS wearer) has been devalued after this nerf.

In reality the way the haste spells stacked with FBSS and other haste items should never really have worked the way it did. That being said, the whole game was balanced over the last year and a half with this behaviour in place. Changing it now just lowers the overall effectiveness of the haste-casting classes. If the game were balanced up till now, this necessarily means that balance has been thrown off by a small amount. If Verant were worried about supposedly "better" haste items not being considered valuable enough, I can think of many ways to make them much much more valuable. Just add AC and STR. Those two stats seem to be something no Tank can ever resist. Just ask those lucky enough to loot a Thick Banded Belt what they are worth. The answer is - A Lot. Make a Thick Banded Belt with the haste of an FBSS and you have the makings of a *very* valuable item, much much more so than an FBSS itself.

Just goes to show how reactions to things like this can differ. Most of the bards I've heard from so far consider this nerf to ultimately favor enchanters. *shrug*

Here's the reasoning: bard hastes are substantially slower than enchanter hastes of the same level. (Our level 50 song hastes by 30% while the level 49 chanter spell gives a 60% boost.) Until now, tank heavy groups would just as often take the bard haste, as it still moved everyone up to the cap. With this new patch, groups might just pass on the bard haste and have him/her sit in the corner the entire time pumping mana so the chanter can do the job right. Manaless or no -- though I never liked that reasoning; many other bards have covered the potential energy nature of caster mana much better than I could hope to here -- most groups will want the best buffs they can get their hands on. And in this case, it would be chanter haste.

The complaints I hear are that this change again limits a bard's possible role in a group and passes the primary burden onto the chanter. As happened with crowd control. And as is now happening with haste. In the end it proves to be a bad thing for both the bard, who feels that much closer to being a walking mana battery, and the chanter, who feels that much closer to being a walking mana sieve, what with having to take on all these mana-intensive roles in a group.

But who's to say? Let's see how things shake out after the patch...

Yet, here we are, about to have it "fixed" (you call it a "nerf", but it's actually just being fixed to work the way it was *meant* to work, so it's not accurate to call it a nerf).

What we have here, clearly, is a failure to maintain a standard when it comes to fixing things in the game. It seems like any time a "bug-type" issue is brougth forth, Verant will only fix the problem if it benefits the players. If it hurts the players, they leave it alone. I don't like that....

I certainly don't have a problem with Verant fixing things in the game, but I wish they would apply some consistancy to their decision making process.
