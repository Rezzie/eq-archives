I just wanted to say that I agree with what Logix said. You can solo as an enchanter, but it isn't usually worth the risk and trouble compared to what you can achieve in a good group. The key is finding the right hunting areas for your level. Examples: 20-26, hill groups in Mistmoore, 27-34 in Cazic-Thule. A good group in these places can fight nearly nonstop. I don't think there is any way to achieve a similar rate of experience soloing. Again, I'm only speaking from the perspective of playing an enchanter.

Real satisfaction from playing an enchanter comes from the things he/she does for a group. If you really feel that you will be soloing most of the time, especially past level 20, I would suggest choosing a different class.

By solo, I assume being able to solo xp mobs from 0-50/55, after that all the classes need to group for xp mobs. Getting SOW or any other buff isn't soloing.
Soloers - any pet class (ench mag nec sham SK), druid, cleric vs undead, any caster/priest with jboots.
A melee with 5 billion arrows and jboots.
Monks at lower levels.
There are rumours of monks soloing high levels with rare equipment.
Shadowknight can solo, but has a great deal of downtime between fights.

Now a few classes can actually solo effectively, as compared to 'just barely.'
So that leaves ench mag nec dru sham, though you must really know what you are doing with ench.

But soloing is only something you do when you can't get a group, or when the groups you find are complete idiots, or when killing easy mobs for specific loot. A series of resists/bad rolls, linkdeath, some surprise additional mobs can result in several hours of effort wasted, in which a regular group could cover for. And of course a group can kill effectively enough to camp spots (and therefore loot) impossible solo.

I recommend at least 2 characters per person, one being able to solo well for those particular times. Having characters of races that hate each other also simplifies buying and selling with merchants abroad.
