A couple thoughts
unregistered 		posted March 23, 2001 02:20 PM              
I am just wondering about a couple ideas I have that might make the logistics of moving around and interacting in the world of EQ a little better.

1) How about some marketplaces set up specifically for auctioning? That way I wont go LD from lag when I need to use the bank in NFP (or whatever the auction place is for you). There could be a couple of zones set up that would each have a bank and would have auctions that might vary based on the value fo items. That way if you are looking for items to help out your level 15 character and have about 100 pp you wont go somewhere and keep asking everyone how much they are selling things for when everything they are selling they want in the 1000s of pp for. Just one idea.

2) How about a better transit system at a number of levels? First, maybe we can have more ships. Now perhaps some ships would cost money, so if you were young you might not be able to afford convenience; but, if you were a higher level you could use additional boats that might require purchase of a "key" to get on the ship. I have a hard time understanding why the NPC world of EQ wouldn't capitalize on the waiting passengers for ships. Second, maybe some NPC locations where you can purchase gates or tps. Now before those that make their living get in an uproar, let me say that the cost could be like 25pp for most sites and 100pp for say DL. This would be more than the amount that most people would charge so it shouldn't hurt business for those that want it. It would stop all the begging of high level players that might actually be trying to play. Also, it would mean that for poeple that play at odd hours when there might not be transportation available through other players they could still pay to get somewhere. Third, how about the ability to purchase SOW? Again for a sum that would still promote interaction and community. This dovetails into the next idea.

3) How about NPCs that sell buffs? If I were a true capitalist I might decide to just camp in a zone where everyone wanted clarity and set up a little booth. Well, why not have some NPCs in the game where you could go buy buffs. You could even make them interesting and fun, e.g. wandering druid X likes muffins. Give her a muffin get a random buff. Again the NPCs could want more than other players might so wouldn't detract from those trying to earn money for spells, and certainly wouldn't stop the trade sow for breeze type interactions. This would further provide a standard of valuation for other players time if they are helping those around. That way a ready response to someone asking for (or more problematic - demanding) a buff could be, "hey, go check out how much it would cost you from so-and-so". That being said I support and almost always freely buff and help other players when I am in the modd to do so and I am not engaged in my own adventures.

Well, I am interested in your responses.

I believe I can answer your questions:

1) Marketplace for auctioning - this could easily be solved (the lag part I mean) if they implement the server-side switches, so if you have /auction turned off the message won't get to your client at all (whereas right now, even if you have it turned off, your client still gets it but simply refuses to display it). Doing this should cut down on lag tremendously.

2) A transit system? Verant already had enough problems with just two boats. From what I understand there were technical reasons why. Of course, they could just SPEED the damn thing up. Having a teleport center in the middle of a city seems like a cool idea, but then it would limit the need for wizards (if you don't believe me then look back a few months ago with the kunark firepots that could teleport to whichever city you clicked on - wizards raised a storm over that one, and it was just in the middle of the ocean! Imagine if they put something like this in the center of each city.... the whining level would increase to insane levels from the wizards and druids).

3) NPC selling buffs - nope, won't work. Again, you must remember the whiney nature of a lot of the EQ players. See the reason why they do whine is because when you take something already in the game and you change it that much, then it is changing the nature of the game. Meaning say for example the game started out with teleport circles in every city that you could buy a teleport from an NPC to another city for 50 pp, then people would look to it as something nice - if it existed in the beginning of the game's lifecycle. If you implemented something like that this late in the game though there would be no end to the amount of complaining and arguing from pro- and con- transport/buff people. All the people that couldn't buff before would be in the majority for pro-, and all the people that could buff/transport would be in the con-.
