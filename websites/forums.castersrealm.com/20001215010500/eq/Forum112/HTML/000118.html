(by the way there are about 6 other topics almost identical to yours which have been active for the last 20 days. Thank god for cut and past

In short, SK's are a warrior with a selection of weak though useful, spells with some gems like darkness and fear. As such, big races are STAT wise, probably the best choice and that goes with all melee classes. Most high lvl warriors and SK's I have found to be either ogres or trolls. Also, slam should be taken into consideration. Big races get it, others don't which equates into a nice extra attack and stunning ability for trolls and ogres without having to use a shield.
There is only so much a SK can do with the spells it has purely because of the spell selection given to us (this goes with all hybrids). Most spells that we get are equivalent to the spells a necromancer half our level has and would probably not even be using anymore. This being said, no matter how much mana we have access to(meaning erudite/dark elf mana pools compared to ogre/troll/human/iksar mana pools), casting during melee or otherwise will take a back seat to pure hacking and bashing. I suppose mana or the amount thereof does effect the way you play but only very slightly such as how long you can fear kite or whatever. The above reasons are why sites all suggest you put points towards making your shadowknight as melee orientated as possible (except in the case of trolls and ogres as their points in int are really substandard) as it is really the only way to go. Our spells are only to support our melee ability and they do this well.

To address your other concerns, there are problems with the large races' manueverability in dungeons but a shrink spell or potion from a shaman will fix that. This is only a minor problem but still a hindrance.

Secondly, HP's are determined by your sta (very little influence but I suppose it helps a bit at high lvls) and your class. I'm really thinking about whether or not race has any significance other than different sta stats but I really don't think so and even if it does, the difference will be negligible.

Lastly, concerning your worries about xp, all hybrids (SK, Paladin, Ranger) have a penalty bonus but as for penalties due to race, I found this on another forum on everlore...it looks right so here it is:

40% experience modifier of shadowknight class

00% experience modifier for dark elf

00% experience modifier for erudite

00% experience modifier for human (yes none)

15% experience modifier for ogre

20% experience modifier for troll

Also from memory, a nice indicator of the very real exp requirement difficulties is the common comparison that it takes a troll SK roughly twice as much exp to get to 50 as a halfling warrior.

By the way, ogre's aren't the ones with regeneration, trolls are and all big races also get slam which is great for SK's as we never get dual wield so 2H weapons are the best for damage dealing with double attack.

Some other things to consider: Humans don't have night vision. MORE than 50% of your playing time will be during night or night like conditions in dungeons...even though sk's get infravision spells, poor humans generally have a crap time trying to navigate in the pitch black with or without a lightsource.
Also, while having an exp penalty, In my opinion big races are the way to go though that's only looking at abilities and stats. There also aren't that many Ogres or Trolls running around so playing one is a nice change and makes us more appreciated heh.

Good luck and have fun

Speaking of woh woh ...
Balance isnt everything. Your two most important stats are strenght and int. Stamina is also nice. Ogre has the best int str summ, and troll and erudite are tied for next. Human lags behind. As long as ag is 75, you dont take any ac penalty. High ag is great for dodge, but its still a secondary stat after int and str. Dex is nice for procing, but again int and str more important. Now the hardest stat for a Sk to raise is int. Many of the ac producing items we need to wear also include a strenght component, so for an erudite by maxing str first, then int, you can still easily start to build your str to a very acceptable range as you gather equipment. Human has what, 15 more pts of strenght at the cost of 32 pts of intelligence, thats a trade id make. Dark elves are more on par with erudites than the other races, but id give up the ag and dx for higher int and stamina - close call though. But when you look at the differences in faction, erudite is better over de. Depends on whether you want to be able to travel freely or not.
Erudites have one of the best starting locations in the game. Granted I work on faction from 1st lv so I get bound in quenos without even going to my guild in erudin, but the zone is still nice.
You say your most important pt is that humans "gain exp the fastest of all the races". Where did you get that. Human and Erudite have no xp penalty. I dont think de have one either.

Any race can be a good SK, got a couple human sks in my guild. But human is definately not the best choice.
