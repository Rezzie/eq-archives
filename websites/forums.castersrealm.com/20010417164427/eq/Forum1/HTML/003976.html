BUMP again... ok, the ABCs of how AE groups work.

The basic makeup of an AE group is going be something like: puller, chanter, cleric, AEer/AEer, other. This isn't set in stone and can be tailored somewhat, but the group MUST include a chanter/cleric/heavy AE casters (at least 2) in order to be successful. I've had all kinds of tanks pull for AE groups, and even a druid in Kanors a time or 2.

The idea is for the puller to get buffed up as much as possible, then go pull either the biggest train he can or whatever the group feels it can safely handle. After the puller makes the pull, he runs back to the group and stands a little ways off from where the rest of the group is and waits for all the mobs to arrive. At this point the cleric is going to wait as long as possible before popping the heal in order to prevent getting whacked on, but if the heal needs to be done, so be it. As soon as all mobs are present everyone runs in to do their thing. The chanter leads with AE tash if they have, if not, starts to chain stun. Cleric keeps everyone alive, but keeps a close eye on the chanter...if all is going well midway through there may be time for an AE too . When the first stun by the chanter is popped it's game on...everyone else with AE goes to town. Game over 15 secs later. It seems to me that either pulls of 4 (for pillar AE) or pulls of >8 (for point blank area of effect) are the way to go.

One last thing...if a true tank is pulling there should be no true downtime as singles can be done between pulls. At 51 mages get a nice AE, and can augment the group nicely with the pet to assist during the "downtime" as well as mod rods, etc. Any extras that come along during the "downtime" can be brought to the pull point and mezzed for the next AE pull.

That's my 2cp...I hope more people will give it a shot as it's a blast, and VERY mana efficent. People seem to dislike change tho.

GL and happy AEing!
