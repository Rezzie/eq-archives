Actually the link above made some bad conclusions about groups not alleviateing exp penalties. HIs conclusions directly contradict his statement about how exp is divided.

According to him if you have a warrior and a TSK at the same level and tehy kill something worth 100000 exp, then the SK will get most of that exp.

Example: lvl 40 warrior has 20,000,000 exp total.

lvl 40 SK has 40,000,000 exp total.

They group get the 100,000 exp. 66,666 will go to SK and 33,333 will go to warrior. Simple as that.

Now with a group with two SKs both with 40,000,000 exp they would each get 50,000 exp from the same kill.

This is all from what he said about how exp gets divided between group members.

Thus in groups your exp penalty is shared by the group members. Personally I have absolutely no problem with this.

The bottom line is measuring the class strength to the expectations created when Verant officials claimed that the 50 plus SK would be the most powerful of all classes, hence the experience penalty. Granted, one would have to go back to the time when the servers first came online to verify such claims. If someone could argue without emotional attachment that the SK is the most powerful class above level 50, then the experience penalty is justifiable.

Just because a character is fun to play and has some redeeming qualities does not mean that the class is performing as expected or presented. Those are completely different issues. It is not illogical or fair game for flames when someone raises questions about the disparity between a class's penalties and rewards and still says they enjoy playing their character.

Personally, I can appreciate the disappointment shared by the older shadow knights who took on the challenge when the world was unknown and the best known weapon was the Mino Axe. The game is infinitely easier now for lower level characters for a variety of reasons. I applaud those SKs who accomplished the high levels during the time of discovery. They didn't all develop the same perception that their penalties would pay off in the long run without reason.

I created my SK with the benefits of many items and information that was unavailable to those "1st generation" SKs. Just because I know that the class is not what it was originally presented to be doesn't mean the older SKs do not have a legitimate gripe.

I am hopeful that Verant will justify the experience penalty. Although, I am skeptical since Verant rarely admits to errors, changes their vision regularly and often pretends a previous vision never existed.

I have a high level druid, enchanter and necro. Comparing my experiences with those and of my mid-level shaman and SK, the SK tanks a little better than my necro pets of the same level. Admittedly, my SK is only 30 and hasn't reached the 50 plus arena where he was originally supposed to shine. But, I think that amidst all the disagreement here, we can say that SKs hardly shine above 50.
