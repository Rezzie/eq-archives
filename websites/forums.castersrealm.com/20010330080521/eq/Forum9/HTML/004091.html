1. Mezzing:
Sounds right to me. Consider rooting after AOE mez so you don't get a pile of critters all break mez and charge you at once. Also consider using blur on critters you have repeatedly mezzed so when the party finally gets to it, they can't get you off the top of the hate list.

2. Targeting:
F8 is closest target. F1-F6 are party members. I usually try to nail the incomming MOBs with a mez before they get to the party. The tank may have to switch targets but at least the party doesnt take damage from unmezzed mobs while I try to sort out which one they are fighting.

3. Single Pulls:
Your jobs in most dungeons are crowd and aggro control. If you find that you have mana left over due to good pulling, consider debuffing or nuking. If fights last longer than 30 seconds, I would seriously recommend you slow critters. If not, nuking is OK. Always keep a large mana pool in reserve for crowd and aggro control.

4. Specializing
Alterion is the choice most enchanters make since most of our mana hungry buffs and debuffs are in that school. When you put a point in specialization, it will begin to increase as you use its skill. The first skill to reach 50 will be your skill of choice.

5. Spell Line Up
Ugh. There is no ONE spell line up that works all the time. It depends on what you are fighting, your group make up, the size of the pulls, and several other factors.

Most of the others have answered what I would have except for on this point. Tash first yes. Then you should be nuking when mana permits, not quickness. Quickness should ALWAYS be cast on the melee types. Breeze should ALWAYS be up on the caster types. Mobs should SOMETIMES be slowed. What would determine the sometimes is if the battle is lasting long enough to make it mana efficient. If the slow goes off and they are at 1/4 health, it is not really worth slowing them. Use your judgement here, but 2 seconds of slow is not really helping much. Forget Disempower (will explain later). After all these conditions are met, is when you should determine if you have enough mana to nuke or not. If you are going to have to casting quickness soon, and you are at 10% mana, don't cast the nuke, sit and med. The melee types hitting more often are going to do much more damage to the mob than your nuke.

Disempower/Weakness line of spells have been determined to be pretty much useless except in pvp modes. From what I can gather, the spells will affect anyone who encumbered greatly. Mobs are carrying no weight. So let's say a mob starts out at 0/70. He gets hit with a weakening spell and loses 10 points of str. Now he is at 0/60 or so. He still has 60 points before he becomes encumbered. He still has 0 weight carried.

>>) Mezzing: What I do when there is an ADD I manually target and Mez or AOE Mez if I cant target and if there is 3 or more pulls I AOE Mez then continue with Enthral.

Yup, about right. You can root the mobs after the AE mez and then enthrall them at your leisure. You can also use the target one, mez it, clear target, pb ae stun(color flux and/or color shift), mez the one that hits you routine. A mob engaged by the tanks will typically continue to hit the tanks. This is a great way to target the unengaged mobs. Also, you can mez as the mobs are incoming... pick one off before the puller gets into the camp.

>>2) Targeting: I always manually Target or I hit the Assist key to see what the puller has targeted and then I go the opposite.

F8 targets the closest mob. This can work well with jumping around. Targetting can be a pain. Myself, I try and pick a mob off on incoming, I use the stun to annoy the unengaged method, and I click until I get a mob that's swinging with full health. Your tanks should move a bit after each mez to spread the mobs out some.

>>3) Single Pulls: At my level what spells do I use on single pulls? This is what I have been doing. First I always cast Tashan then I either Choke or Nuke then I stun as mana permits, I also cast quickness on melee's as mana permits and I cast Disempower if they con red or I know it will be a long fight.

At your level, it is probably a good idea to tash if you intend to cast other spells or someone else is casting magic based spells. Typically you are better off hasting the tanks than nuking/dot'ing. Our dots suck. Slow typically is pretty pointless at that level, the slow percentage is tiny, the fights are over so quick. You are better off dropping a nuke in to shorten the fight. However, it really comes down to balancing mana. You want to optimize everyone's use of mana... so if the healer is having problems keeping up, then slowing to help might be a good thing. Quickness is a pretty pathetic spell, giving only 15% haste with a short duration. It's useful if your group is fighting constantly or if your tank needs a bit more edge to taunt. You'll have to judge for yourself if the mana is worth it. Once you get alacrity though... always keep alacrity up.

>>4) Specialization: From what I was told by a couple of high level Enchanters I should specialize in Alteration, is this correct?

The primary benefits of specialization are reduced fizzles (fizzles take mana) and reduced mana cost. You'll find that most of your mana is gobbled by alteration spells. For this reason, most of us go alteration.

>>5) Spell Lineup: As I hit 20 how should my spell lineup be? It gives me a headache when I get my new spells as an Enchanter so I thought it would be easier to ask you guys.

The key to being a great enchanter is to be flexible and adaptable. There is no set spell line up. Many of us use our own methods and they all work, some better in some places, some better in others. Find one that works for you...but if you can't adapt, can't figure out when to use what spells, well, play a paladin or something

Well, I will respond to your post with a small miniguide I wrote for the rest of my guildies, to help them get a better grip on how I, as an enchanter, like things to flow. Hope it helps...


------------------------------------

Ok, one could say that the only one who should know how to play a class is the class itself... Well, I partly agree. BUT after playing with Sappo over a LONG time, and being spoiled with him being so well educated on how I want things to be, I would like to teach everyone else to on how to please your enchanter, wich will result in a smooth gameplay and also will keep the group alive.

I will address this in 5 different topics, first explaining the type of spells we have at our disposal, and then the scenarios where YOUR action can be of BIG difference. Note that this document only reffers to MY playstyle as an enchanter, and I hope the rest of the guildcanters can comment on this small miniguide so that we continue and improve our grouping.


1. Areamess
The areamess, or messmerisation, is aquired at level 16. What it does is that it makes target stand drooling for a period of time. The problems with this spell is that it CAN and WILL affect the enchater if he / she is in the area of affect. Another problem is that it lasts for a relatively short period of time, and on top of that, you cannot owerwrite it with a singlemess (wich lasts longer). It of course also builds alot of aggro on the creatures messed. I only use this on pulls that are more then 3, or if there are MANY people involved in the fight, or for some other reason when it is hard to target. Now for the importan stuff, WHEN i have to use this, I allways have TARGET on the main assist in the group / raid and cast the spell on HIM, in hope that I will get all the munsters in one cast. Why is that important? Well, the big reason is that I want the MAIN assist to stay AWAY from me. I dont want him running TO me all of a sudden when I decide to renew the mess, which will cause me to mess my self = Dead chanter.

2. Singlemess
Single mess have longer duration then areamess, and you dont risk messing yourself. That is, if you do not target yourself, wich have been known to happen. This one really need no description, that is apart from that you should never hit a messed mob unless you are main assist. You can cast ALL spells on it as long as they dont do damage. It is allways good to debuff a target while it is messed. Debuffs will never break a mess.

3. Memblur
Aggro... the enchanters worst enemy. We have a spell called memblur, this also come in a AOE form. I allways have it memmed. Not the Areaone becuse it is so expensive to cast in Mana. It is great to use in several situation, not only to save my own skin, but alo to save that cleric who just casted a Complete heal, more on this subject in the Breaking Mmess part lower down...

4. Pulling - outdoors
Ok, now we get to the essentials... how should you act as a puller. We start with outdoors. I will use the spectres in oasis as an example here. Sappo goes out to the island and pull, he allways gets 4 in the first pull. The rest of the group stays back while me and Sappo bring order into the situation. It is important that the rest of the group stays out of aggro. Ok, Sappo starts running the spectres back an forth the beach, while I pick them out on at a time and Mess them down. This will hopefully result in a nicely spread lined up thread of spectres. If you are a puller, I for one, really appriciate if we can spread the mobs out. It will be easier to pick a single one out before you break the mess, and I have a easy time to remess them since I know in wich order I messed them in the first place.

5. Pulling - Dungeon
This is a bit different from outdoor pulling... Of course there is not the option of running the mobs back and fourth. But there is something you CAN do as the puller / main assist in dungeons that will help the chanter VERY MUCH. Ok, if you bring in a few mobs, less then 4, the chanter will probably try to singlemess them. SO, you are standing there with 3 mobs beating on you, and suddenly you see the 'Have been messmerised' message, and one mob gets that 'imbicil-look' on it's face. MOVE BACK a few steps. That way, the chanter can easely see which mob is messed, and try to target a new one. The other situation is when the chanter will use area mess (some enchanters do that even with a 2-mob pull). Ok, you see multipble 'Have been messed' lines here. Same thing goes here... move back a few steps and let the mob you are hitting follow. It is now easier to control that nicely packed crowd of messed monster witout so much fighting going on in the middle.

6. Breaking mess
Well, so you killed mob number one and are going to kill mob number two (out of 4) now. You make SURE that the chanter know wich one you are going to barsh. Stand clearly ahead of the mob you are going to kill, and press H for hail. If there are lots of monsters with the same name, please try to communicate with your chanter WHICH one you intent to kill. Ok, now try to give this time, and taunt twice before hitting it. WHY? THere are two reasons for tauning, partly to get some aggro built up, since the mob is probably now thinking in about 1000 different way that it would like to tear the enchanter up. The second reason is that the chanter as soon as he sees your hail will try to target the mob and cast a memblur on it. That process takes about the same time as it takes for you to do two taunts. You see this with the message 'A_Danish_Mage_00 blinks a few times'. Problem with memblur is that there is NOW WAY you can now if it got resisted or not. Either it worked or it did not. But it is allways nice to at least TRY, so, after your HAIL do some taunting while you wait for the 'blinks a few times' message...


Ok, so now you at least know how Catapult works as an enchanter.... of course there is a lot more to it than this, but I did'nt have the time to go into more details. I also want to keep some trick-of-the-trades to myself of cousre. But remember, if you keep the above in mind when working in groups, you will please your enchanter, and you will also keep the 'flow' better in the pulls, and also keep your group alive longer.
