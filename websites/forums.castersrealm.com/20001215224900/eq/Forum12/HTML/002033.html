(Yes, i like to talk) My favorite class to group with is, no less, Shaman. We have a knack for looking out for others, and most of us are very polite. VERY. I enjoy my fellow shamans' company tremendously.

However, yes, we, too, have our limits. I don't give newbies anything when they come to me "do you have stuff for me? I'm a newbie" Main reason being that I am a newbie, this is first and only char. When I was a true newbie, not starting a new additional char, I had no clue to run around begging. I was too busy trying to make it on my own and figure out what OOM meant. So most 'newbies' seeking handouts aren't newbies.

In addition to that, I am not mommy, I am not EXPECTED to "take care of [them]" Another reason is that it is very abrasive. I buff and give loot or old armor freely when i see a naked and confused little newbie working their butt off to make it. Chelia helps those who help themselves, and if they are polite she helps them a lot.

I am always polite still though, "sorry just sold loot and bought new spells" /sigh broke again." I just can't bring myself to be as impolite as they. With one exception. That 'give me sow', 'sow me', or 'hey sow now i got CR'. Then I can say 'sorry OOM' and stand there and buff myself right in front of them. Rude? So are they. It is my mana that i need to protect myself or my group, so they ARE taking something from me.

When even a little level one druid buffs me, I kneel and thank them very much. Of course then i click it off so i can use inner fire, but it was the intent that mattered. They used precious mana that they might need to save themselves also.

I can't teach other people manners, or choose how they ask me for buffs. But by golly I don't have to OBEY anyone. So I agree with the philosophy of 'be VERY polite to everyone.'

I have lots of stuff that has been given to me by high level characters whos new character I helped. It pays to be kind and polite, especially when you do it just because you ARE kind and polite. To get stuff from me, that is the only way to be.

I hope i am not breaking any time-honored Shaman tradition here by not obeying EVERY request for sow.

We are a great class, guys, and even better company

Towards the end i was getting soft in my old age and helped people alot, I'd mainly go to low level areas and buff up the newbies.

The other week I sold off a spare yak and spent the proceeds on low end gear at a trade bazzar and spend the following few days running around newbie areas helping out real newbies and giving them gear.

Its nice. I was also given a few odds and ends when I was younger so I think it will help the next generation of players think there are nice people out there and they too will go around and help future newbies someday when the camping and the powerleveling gets old and the treasure begins to loose its luster.

Just remember folks, its only a game. I've lost count of the people I havent helped because they were rude, but I think its important to keep a positive outlook on things.

Sometimes they arent horrible people, just come off the wrong way. If I am bored and feeling good, I will chat with them a while, explain how if they approach people in a more pleasant less demanding manner then they are more likely to get what it is they are after.

A few weeks ago I was wandering around GF and was approached by a few level one players asking for handouts to purchase their spells. Some of them are scamming, some dont really know that their 1st level spells generally arent even worth buying but some just dont know better. They see the big 60 on the screen and mistake the big fat troll for Santa Clauss. I generally dont help those kind of players with money, but I give them great advice.

I tell them if they really need money and are not just lazy then they can do the bard quest to deliver mail between Felwithe and Kelethin and get up to a couple of gold pieces a trip. After an hour or so of running they can make all they need to buy their spells and probably enough experience to give them a level or two. I give them a sow and wish them luck.

Moral of the story is that not everyone is rude, sometimes they are young and dont know better and come off as being rude. Sometimes the beggars arent scamming, they just dont know they are better off without, or they can easily get it themselves. So whilst its easy to step on that soap box and mount that high horse, I think the EQ community would all benefit if you took the time to take one of these people under your wing every now and then.

Talk to them, show them the right thing to do and give them some advice. If they seem sincere then you may even want to help them out. It will make you feel good, they will feel good. Its a win-win scenario. The next generation will be a better generation if it is educated.

I've never asked for anything, not even a resurrection. I accepted what was offered, but its the type of person I am. I dont expect everyone to be like me though and I dont pass judgement... well, not anymore.
