Gordon and/or Abashi... I have nothing but the utmost respect for you guys. I have worked with game development in the past and empathise with you guys - you have a very difficult job. The games I worked with were not a MMORPG, so you guys take way more of the "this sucks", "Verant hates us!", and the most famous of all "We pay for this game so you owe "it" to us." and "The players make the game." than I had to.
Well... I don't want to burst anyones bubble but Everquest doesn't "suck", and to all the people that post that I have this to say: Everquest doesn't suck. If it did nobody would play it, and for some reason most of the people who come in saying it sucks are people who are level 50+! Anyone care to explain why the people who play the most Everquest of all claim it sucks?
As for the people who claim "Verant hates us." I am pretty sure the only thing Verant hates is people who piss and moan all the time! If you don't like it, don't pay for it and don't play it!
Now, last but definitely not least, for all those who yell about how "We pay for this game, so you owe *it* to us" and "The players make the game.", I only have this to say: When you paying $10 a month you are paying for the ability to play Everquest. If that is an ability you don't want, then don't pay. Also, Verant owes you nothing. Once again, I will say if you don't like the way Verant conducts its business then don't pay for a service that Verant provides. That's the beauty of the free enterprise system. And another thing (just to continue my rant) while the players actions add to the game experience of the players they interact with (and note these actions are sometimes good, sometimes bad), players need to remember that the team of people working on Everquest are the CREATORS of the game, and therefore, entitled to make the final decision regarding suggestions and requests from players. There are a number of constraints and realities that they have to deal with that. Issues relating to play balance, keeping the system running, financial considerations, to name but a few.
Anyway, to Gordon and Abashi, I applaud your patience with those who would like nothing better than to just post and post about how much Verant and Everquest sucks for whatever reason and always treating them with a certain level of respect that they have not earned. Well, that about wraps it up so I would once again like to say that both of you do a great job and keep up the good work!

-Travis Day

Verant should have a 'lost and found' department, where you can only get through by phone line or e-mail where your claim is gone over there, NOT in game. Let it be known that the ONLY way to get an item lost due to a bug is through them, NOT GMs.

The major complaint seems to be that it takes so LONG for a GM to respond, but if Verant would get rid of experience rezzes for GMs, and use a 'lost and found' hotline/e-mail system that response time would go down DRASTICALLY.

Third- MISTAKEN IDENTITY
Verant should make it CLEAR that GUIDES are just players like ourselves and that what they say about the mechanics of the game are person OPINIONS not neccessarly what actually happens. While GMs are employees of Verant and in many cases the DO understand the mechanics, they are under NO obligation to reveal them to the player, and in fact are asked NOT to tell how quest are done, or what is the place holder for what, etc.

An example of the above point:
I wanted to complete the Jboots quest for the first time. Back then very few (if anyone) truely understood about the concept of placeholders (yes, this was back in the DAWN of EQ ). After getting the ring and the other items for Hasten, I headed for Rathe Mountains to wait for Hasten. When I arrived I found that there were 2 other players already there who had been waiting for 24 and 72 straight HOURS ahead of me. One had petetioned already to say that this was ridiculous. A guide showed up and said that we should kill as many low level mobs that we could find, and that other than that, there was nothing they could do, other than contact a GM. When the GM showed up she said that she would check Hasten's 'folder' to see if there was a problem with Hasten's spawn times. Now, mind you, at the time a MOB called 'a drake' was flying around right in front of us (one of the placeholders for Hasten). The GM returned and said that Hasten has been spawning regularly and there was nothing wrong with the spawn. Upon hearing that the player who had been waiting for 72 went into a tirade at the GM and logged off. I politely asked is there ANYTHING we could do to stimulate the spawn. The GM said that her suggestion was to find out if there was any mob around with either a 'simple name' or a definate name around and kill it (if we could) it MIGHT be a placeholder for Hasten, then she left.
Needless to say, we later spotted 'a drake' killed it, then killed another one that spawned behind it. This produced 'Brother Zephyl' which we promply left ALONE! It despawned later, and to make a long story short, HASTEN later spawned about 3hrs after our conversation with the GM.
