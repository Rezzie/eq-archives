I think this boils down to the economy.

If you could make a bow by harvesting raw materials, then sell that bow to a vendor, you've brought money into the world without taking any out. Such actions cause heavy-duty inflation. Money would not be worth anything because people could get as much as they want if they were willing to spend the time farming. And there *would* be people willing to spend hours upon hours chopping trees.

You may think this is how it happens in the real world, but it isn't quite. In the real world, the shop owners have finite money. They wouldn't buy 80 bows from you @ 1pp each unless they thought they could sell them and they had the money to pay you.

The dynamics of economies are very difficult, if not impossible, to model in a game, so developers must make decisions resulting in gameplay superceeding realism.

You seem to have really missed the boat on bandaging. Honestly I think 50% is too high if you're shooting for realism. The idea that you can restore yourself to half health from near death by simply wrapping yourself in bandages is insane. Bandaging while fighting or running is just stupid.
