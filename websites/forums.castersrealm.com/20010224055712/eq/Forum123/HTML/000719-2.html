We get bind wound capped at 100 pre50, and monks get 200? huh? they get mend and they can out bind us? that makes sense.

We warrriors get ONE thing in this game. Equipment. Without an Equipment upgrade every few levels, we become stagnant. Every other class, except possibly Rogue's, get's better with age, as they get more spells or skills to boost their abilities. We only have equipment to do that. And that equipment is barely achievable without massive outings.

Notice how many warriors buy thier upgrades rather than win it in lotto's? ever wonder why? granted not all of them, but many do. We just want a little compensation for being the most group requiring class there is. We can't solo, travel, recover corpses, anything, without a group. All we can do is stand around getting beat on. At some point, we'd like more. Maybe a few skills or choices during a battle rather than mashing 4 or 5 buttons constantly as they reset every couple seconds. Tuant, Bash/kick/Slam, Sense Heading, Disarm (which is useless, but it's something to do), taunt, SH, Taunt, Kick/Bash/Slam, rinse and repeat. That's our life. No chioces of any kind to make. No when to use my emergency group saving feature, no way to get away if things go wrong, no way to get back without help if they do go wrong, etc.

you get the idea. we're not far off, but don't discount us as perfect. warriors need a little tweaking too, just because we are the baseline does not mean we're perfectly balanced.

I must admit, I am somewhat worried about what's going on in test right now, as well.
The fact that this stuff is happening on TEST, however, should give most of you people saying that you're going to quit your warrior some pause. VI won't release these new class balancing features unless they feel sure that no one class will be the worse for it.

That said, I am disturbed.

The one and only defining characteristic of the warrior that makes the class desirable is the ability to tank better than anyone else. Damage dealing is nice, to be sure, but in any experience group, and most raids, our ability to absorb damage is far paramount to our penchant for dealing it out.

Our ability to take this damage is based on 3 things.
a. equipment.
b. hit points.
c. defensive skills

For point a, warriors are still top of the heap--but not by much. When cobalt came out with Kunark, warriors were the undisputed kings of the ac game--none of the hybrid class armors came even close.
With the release of Velious (and specifically the PoG armors), that gap has been narrowed consideraby. Berserker/cobalt are still the best pure AC armor sets in the game; but the gap between them and, for example, the SK-Specific armor has been dangerously reduced.
b. Hitpoints are something warriors will always have more of than anyone else. The simple fact is, though, that they don't have enough more to make that large a difference at high levels. Consider that unbuffed warriors at level 60 generally have between 3000 and 3500 hp, while an unbuffed 60 SK has somewhere in the range of 2400-2900. Less? Yeah. But by how much? 600, maybe 1000 at the very outside?

How much to the most powerful NPC's in the game hit for? Velketor doubles for 800; Nexona and Phara Dar both triple on the 4-600 range, as does Hoshkar (VP dragons, if you didn't know). Some of the Western Wastes 'normal' mobs can triple for 200 or more.
Given this insane power, how much of a difference does that HP make? Against uber mobs, about 1 hit. Against normal Mobs, maybe 3 or 4. Again, getting missed means a lot more than being able to take that one last hit.
So, the final item. defensive skills. Particularly, the defense skill affects AC. Warrior defense caps have always been higher than anyone else's, because it is their AC that makes them the best tanks. Not the hp, not the parry/riposte etc etc, but the AC. Any board here has the 'ac is king' line on it somewhere.

So, this patch on test is raising pally and sk defense caps to match warriors. Their AC, then, will also approach warriors. Will it be the same? No. Warriors get more innate ac/defense point, and warrior armor is better. However, in the long run it seems clear that the gap has been lessened considerably.

Now, none of this is necessarily bad. I am 100% for making hybrids better tanks in relation to the insanely powerful new mobs.
What bothers me most is Absor's comment that "warriors are a base class--and one that we cannot change a great deal because of repercssions affecting other classes."

So, basically, the other classes get upgrades that make them closer to us in power and ability, but we stay the same? Great. So the hybrids will be tanks approaching our abilities (we are still better. I know. But not by as much as I think we should be). AND they'll have their spells.

I think this should be balanced by some warrior-specific combat abilities which I'm too tired to think up right now.

>>Meaning that when two lvl 30's group they recieve equal exp rather then giving the hybrid 40% more because they had to get 40% more exp to reach 30.

Let's say a 30 warrior and 30 paladin group. They kill a mob worth 200 xp (with the 2% grouping bonus figured in). They each get 100 xp. The paladins is then multiplied by 40%. So the warrior gets 100 xp. The paladin gets 140. What this means is that the paladin xp penalty is gone, but they don't have to go through and modify the xp on every character.

However, the warrior does still have a 9.00 factor. So if the paladin needs 1000 real xp to level (1400 on character), the warrior would only need 900. Assuming the same racial penalty, of course.
