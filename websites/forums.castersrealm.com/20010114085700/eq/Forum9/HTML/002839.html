On another thread, I asked the question how other enchanters were timing the duration of their mesmerizes / enthralls / entrances / etc. I noted I was thinking about getting a timer or three to put on my desk.

In a full group, I'm doing mostly mes's, so I stand mes, sometimes debuff or root, then sit back down to med until it's time to stand and cast again. I'm almost embarrassed to admit it, but I've taken to counting them time using the old "one-thousand, two-thousand, three-thousand" method. Usually, I can keep them in control that way and re-mes before it breaks.

But when add ons show up, it gets confusing remembering which is which. What is everyone else doing?

From the other thread, I saw some people using the "internal - gut feeling" method, while someone mentioned they do keep a stopwatch at their desk.

What's the general consensus?

Gut feeling method is the way I go. If you have played a lot you learn to KNOW how long 120 seconds is or 30 seconds, or a half hour. There have literally been times I have said: "Speed buff should be going about now" ... sure enough, the warrior's like: "Mine is flashing." You get a /really/ good sense of time as an enchanter and its a key to being successful. As for mezzing? Well, I just drop my mez on and wait till the time is almost alotted, then BOOM drop another mez on it. You can keep the mob held indefinitely. I once parked a spectral curate in Karnors next to me for 2 and a half hours and used it as a mana battery with minimal breakage.

There are all sorts of programs out there and stopwatches that can allow you to measure time, but, as in life, its always best to do things "naked". After all, your enchanter doesn't have those aids in-game does he/she!? =D
