I've had a couple of ideas as to how they could balance us (spellwise- skillcap wise I'm with that other bloke who posted)

1) Why not have it so that when the hybrid reaches its' spell level- it gets about half of the spells that a parent class of the same level has. So a lvl 9 hybrid would get like 4 of its' parents' level 5 spells (if you think- a level nine druid (for example) would still be using his level 5 spells- so the hybrid gets the level 5 spells and skips the level 1 set of spells altogether, because if you think- a hybrid is 75% warrior anyway, so it'd be proper to have less spells- but spells which were more useful- it should be quality not quantity where our spells are concerned).

For instance:

A lvl 9 paladin would get light healing because a cleric of the same lvl has it (think- by then minor healing is a bit naff anyway), and lets say it gets one or two buffs too. I'd rather have about three current spells than have a lot of outdated spells. Not that I'm not grateful for what we do have- I'd just like what I cast not to be laughed at as much. Let the fact that we have less mana than the parent limits us instead. (Parent classes will angle their stats towards int or wis, hybrids will aim for strength- so we limit ourselves before we even start playing)

A lvl 9 ranger would get say- the same nukes as a druid (and maybe a heal the level after- because with the ranger the emphasis is more on snaring, doting and nuking rather than the healing like a paladin).

A lvl 9 SK (bare in mind I've never played a necromancer past level 3) would get a better pet and a better lifetap etc etc (I have to admire SK's for being able to control and keep their pets alive for so long, it takes some skill ^_^).

2) OK, my other idea was to keep things as they are, but allow the hybrids' buffs/DoTs/debuffs to stack with anything the parent class casts.

For example:

Paladin:
A cleric casts all his/her decent buffs on a target, a paladin of the same level casts his/her lower level cleric buffs on the target and they stack, giving the target the benefit of both sets of buffs. (now that would be nice ^_^ esp on a raid or three)

Shadow Knight:
A neccie casts darkness on a target, the SK adds his/her darkness to it- it stacks reducing target movement even more and doing more damage along with it ^_^

Ranger:
Again Druid and a Ranger- the druid casts snare and a DoT, as does the Ranger- the effect is even slower movement rate and more DoT.(even though the DoT's would stack- because a druid would be using his best dot and the ranger would be using a lower lvl druid DoT)

This would not make hybrids essential (which they aren't now anyway), but rather- somthing nice to have in addition. Also you'd see a lot more hybrid+parent groups going this way.... but this is the way it should be- both the parent and hybrid have the same drives and beliefs from a roleplay point of view. You'd get all evil classes together and all good classes together... so from a rp point of view it's a great idea... and it may also go someway to solving the problem necromancers have with their use in a group :)

(a group of cleric, paladin, necromacer, shadow knight, druid, ranger would be very interesting ^_^)
