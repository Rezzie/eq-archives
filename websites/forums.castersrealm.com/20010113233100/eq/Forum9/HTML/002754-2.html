Actually Malleable, wisdom does affect your jewelcrafting.

Trade skills increase in skill points based on your intelligence, but the number of successful attempts is mitigated by your wisdom, so whereas a human with wisdom 75 might fail 3 of 4 attempts, a high elf with wisdom of 100 (pretty easily attainable)might only fail 2 of those 4 attempts.

It doesn't make a huge difference when you're working with lower metals, but when you start to dirty your hands with platinum at 105 plat per bar, you can burn through a lot of money in a hurry.

Every successful attempt can make you a decent profit, and make you foolishly believe that you'll actually get that carrot that verant is dangling in front of your face!

Ah, let the rumors fly.

Miscellaneous quasi-facts:

1. There is no evidence that WIS affects jewelcrafting provided your INT is higher. In fact, all the evidence points to the contrary (as well as the "official word" from Verant).

2. Only dark elves get ultravision. Gnomes and high elves get infravision.

3. Dark elf hide is a racial ability with a low set skill amount, so hide fails quite often, making it less wonderful than you'd think for breaking charm.

4. Erudites get a +5 racial bonus to magic resistance.

5. The starting area for Erudites -- counting Paineel -- is arguably far better than the starting area for high elves.
