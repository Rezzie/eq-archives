All the PvP servers are different now, and i'm just wondering how much these differences effect the game:

Rallos Zek: 4 level limit, item + coin loot, no PvP faction hits.

Vallon Zek: 8 level limit, "team" limit, item + coin loot, PvP faction hits

Tallon Zek: 8 level limit, "team" limit, coin loot, PvP faction hits

Here's how it is on Rallos Zek:

At high levels (46+), roleplayed PvP is virtually non-existant. The majority of PvP exists between guilds. They get upset over something and fight until one guild's members all leave. This has worked extremly well keeping the giant guilds under control... there is no "uber guild" on Rallos Zek like I have seen on blue servers. Just a bunch of small to medium sized guilds. The amount of PvP you experience is pretty much based on your guild and your attitude.

Then there are the level 50 ebay PK's .. about 10 of them exist. They seek out people wearing nice equipment in any zone (even cities) and kill them. A couple days later you can usually find the item they stole for sale on Ebay.

The lower level scene on Rallos Zek is a little more roleplay oriented.. with a tight RP community that makes up cool little quests & storylines posted on ralloszek.net. They are outnumbered by random jackass PK's though.


So how is the atmosphere on Tallon & Vallon?

Dotty
unregistered 		posted 04-07-2000 05:40 PM              
There is absolutely no doubt that people fight 10 times more on Tallon than on any other PvP server. There is also no doubt that there is more stupid, pointless and lame PvP on Tallon than on any other server, since coin-only loot removed almost all element of risk in PvP. Corpse camping, bindpoint camping and intentional xp-loss is rampant, an coin-only loot is mostly to blame for it. Tallon is not for the weak-of-heart. And unlike Rallos, PvP increases with level - lvl 50s spend a significant portion of their time hunting each other.

On Tallon, people fight for zone control and for prestige. Even though the dark team isn't the largest by any means, the darks' superior organization means that they dominate heavily at higher levels. Lower Guk is owned by the dark team, as is Solusek B unless most of the darks are doing PoF/PoH at the moment (when the cat's away...). The dark team is the only one to have slain dragons and gods. The lights are growing in numbers and organization though.

Unlike Vallon, crossteaming (darks and lights teaming up) is absolutely not tolerated on Tallon. Crossteam and you become a pariah, everyone will make it their mission in life to make your life miserable. There was a powerful (i.e. large) crossteaming guild on Tallon called Reformation but it was routed and broken up through the concerted efforts of the strong all-dark guilds.

I play on both Vallon and Tallon. Mostly Vallon. Out of the two, Vallon is funnest for me. More people seem to role-play than on Tallon, and you can take precious items that people worked very hard to obtain ;-)

In response to Dotty's statement, "There is absolutely no doubt that people fight 10 times more on Tallon than on any other PvP server." I haven't experienced one PvP encounter on either of my 2 characters. (They are fairly low in level: Erudite Magician Level 3, and Iksar Warrior Level 8) I know Iksar reside in a fairly secluded area, so that explains one issue, but on Vallon the Qeynos area (and most other major cities) is full of PvP action. With my Magician, the only team outside of the blue team I've ever seen is the yellow team (elf team.)

As far as my experience goes, (I've had other characters on Tallon in the past) even before coin only loot, Tallon was plagued with corpse/bind/zone camping bastards.

On Crossteaming:
[Tallon] -- I haven't had much experience with crossteaming, so Dotty would be way more informed about the consequences than I.
[Vallon] -- One of my characters is a member of a rogue only guild (the only one I know of on the server) and see a lot of his Dark Elf guildmates shunned by the Dark Race community for being involved with a guild that is "largely lighty" (almost 50% of the guild is actually Darky last I knew), but when it all comes down to who's gonna keep them alive? the rogue or the extra warrior/shadow knight, the smart ones choose to secretly abandoned their guild's charter and select the crossteaming rogue who can kill that mob before the train begins. I'm not saying it's easy, but people can survive being crossteamers. On the flip side, lighties aren't really punished for grouping or helping the dark side, mainly cause they ones who care just don't know about it ;-) Of course, my characters crossteam, but they do it in a role-playing fashion and never bring up the terms "crossteam" or "pk" over the say or shout channels. What ticks me off is the people that don't roleplay 1 single bit and get all huffy puffy about crossteaming. They don't make a drop of sense to me and I just wanna slap them across the back of the head with a mallet or carve out their kidnies with some sort of blade.
