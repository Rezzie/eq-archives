In regards to Dynamic Content...I think the people in Verant are a bit confused in the definition of this term.
Making changes to the game system, game rules and/or game mechanics is NOT the definition of Dynamic Content.
Dynamic Content is the ever changing history, storyline and overall content of the IN-GAME world. Quests that, when performed and completed, change the world structure around us. Since I started playing over 2 years ago, the guards in front of Qeynos have been muttering something about their leader. Why hasn't anything been done? The political structure remains the same. Nothing in the world has changed...save the addition of new, static continents to explore.

If you want to change something in EverQuest, change the IN-GAME things, and stop tinkering with the parts of the game system that work!

No, this is wrong. If you play any game, the basic rules never change. If you get a spell, it will always do a set range of damage based on your abilities. If you get jboots, they always probide the same effect. If you fall 200 feet, you will take the same rule of damage based on your encumberance. These are static effects which should not change.

You know if you swim underwater and run out of air, you will take damage, drown, and die.

If you killed Droga 6billion times, and won 6billion times, and then suddenly Droga kicks your butt forever, how is that to be explained? Did Droga suddenly become a god?

I think the dynamic world is a cool idea, but verant's vision doesn't match the ideal.
By dynamic -- the creatures are unchanging. If Sir Lanthin hits for 90 damage a hit, and after a few months hits for 300 damage per hit, that is wrong. If however, he begins practicing his combat tactics he could improve gradually to 300 -- say for the first week he goes to 95 damage, second week 100 damage, etc etc.

People can't just go from 90 to 300 in one day of game time.

You can't just say ok, from today on, the sense magic spell you had is morphed by the gods and converted into true north -- a spell you've never seen before.

***********************
By dynamic world -- have the world dictate the changes, like a meteor crashed into the ground and aliens are invading...or gnolls...same difference. And keep it fluid, while remaining believable.

The changes that have gone into the game are too drastic, too fast, and extremely unbalancing to the entire land of Norrath. Remind me when the game is done with its beta testing. Sadly I believe that time will come when EQ2 comes out so we can go through the whole thing all over again.
