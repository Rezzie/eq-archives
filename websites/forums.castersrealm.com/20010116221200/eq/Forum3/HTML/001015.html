Camping and random spawns is Verant's dull-witted and completly unimaginative answer to the problem of limiting access to nice items. The fact that it keeps the subscribers from "finishing the game" too quickly is a definite plus to the product design.

I decided early on that I was not going to play the game with a loot-hound mentality. First of all, I'm not impressed by loot; I'm impresssed by people who know how to play their character. Second, as someone has said, having the 99th percentile stats has far less impact on your effectiveness than level. Knowing that, where is your time better spent: leveling up or camping that uber-item? Finally, just knowing that you will eventually get the item if you're willing to tolerate the tedium is, for me, de-motivating enough. It's not as though it's a real challenge, it just a question of "are you willing to put in the time?" The satisfaction that comes from getting a rare item is nice, and (for me) lasts a few hours of game time. But if I have to put up with a no-exp 40 hour camp to get it, then the math gets a little fuzzy and I start to think my time is better spent actually doing something in-game.

If Verant wants to make the game more interesting and challenging and "Quest-friendly", then they should make most Uber items attainable (key word) only by a massive Quest. A Quest that may take even longer than a camp in game time, but one that requires moving around, maybe seeing new places and getting side-tracked, going through the game in a non-linear fashion. And break it up so that you can complete it in segments and you don't (as with camps) have to forego personal hygiene for more than 12 hours at a time. There are already a few items in the game like this, I think there should be many more.
