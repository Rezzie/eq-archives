Friends, Norrathians, Countrymen (and women, and things)...Lend me your ears.
I come not to praise the death of Role Playing on EQ, but to bury it....

Sad but true. It is soooo hard to maintain the Fanstasy Role Playing experience these days. To wit: the incessant "woot"-ing every 21 seconds in /shout; the flat-out unheroic begging for heals, buffs, equipment and cold hard cash; not to mention the (hopefully Bristlebane-only) spate of folks still /shouting "Who let the Bards out?" about 120 days after it ceased to be novel (...was it ever...?)

Oh, how far we have strayed from this game's fantasy roots. After Bilbo Baggins found a certain magic ring, did he "woot" the entire mountain (zone) announcing to every goblin in earshot of his rare find? Would Conan have waited at the entrance to Yag-Sotoh's Temple for hours hoping that a weakly finger-waggling spell-slinger would come by and "buff" him before he challenged the dangers within? When Elric blew the horn that signalled the End of The World, did Moonglum rise from the ground and yell at the top of his lungs "Who let the bards out?" (yeah, yeah, yeah, Elric is more of an SK than a bard, so sue me)

(oh, and if you have no idea who any of the above mentioned charcters are, why the heck are you playing this game?)

Yes, sometimes its cool to pal around and talk RL with your EQ buds, but when its done so that all can (or MUST) hear it, it really ruins the flavor of the game sometimes.

Thus, to come close to anything remotely approaching the immersive FRPG experience EQ should be one has to turn off just about every chat channel besides /group and /tell. Thats not right.

Whats the answer? Well, maybe somehow, somewhere the powers that be could create a role-playing server and advertise it as such, just like they do with the various PvP servers. I know, its a pipe dream. But hey, I can hope, wish, wonder and imagine, can't I? I mean, EQ IS a fantasy.....right?

In sum, the illusion of being in an adventurer in the land of Norrath, and not some slob sitting behind a computer in 21st century Earth, can be killed quicker than an orc pawn facing Lady Vox. Please realize that some folks like that illusion. Please show a little consideration.

Thanks

Channel guide 101

/ooc - as it's name implies, this is intended for when a roleplayer needs to speak out of character. It supposes that everyone is otherwise roleplaying. Instead it turned into one more channel with the marginal excuse that everyone and their aunt could talk on it about whatever they like and have the excuse 'but look, it's ooc so I'm [B]meant[B] to fill it up with mindless banter for no good reason other than I can'

/shout - desirable to some extent but overall a lazy implementation of a useful RP feature, the ability to communicate over longer distances without it constantly being set to 'on' and annoying everyone in shout distance with /says. The very existance of an /ooc channel implies that shouts were intended to be in character. How it turned out is a ffa babble about anything and everything from 'DING!... Congrats, woot!" to 'I just got a new pc! Woot!'

/guildsay - this is a tool. RP down the line with guildmates if you like or talk about what's on tv if that's what you want. You cannot escape what it is though, a tool for organisation.

/tell - like /guildsay, simply a tool. It's meant for everything you wish to chat about that isn't game related because presumably if it's not game related why'd the rest of us want to know? If more idiots used /tell EQ would be a lot better on the immersion front. The number of conversations occuring over /shout or /ooc that are strictly between two people amaze me. Take it to /tells for crying out loud, especially when half of these people have the audacity to tell anyone else joining in or adressing them to butt out /boggle.

/auction - another tool, designed to facilitate smoother player trading by limiting auctions to one channel all by itself. Does it get used properly? Hell no. 'But if I don't /shout what I want to buy or sell then people who have /auction switched off might miss it. Well, dah, that's the idea brightspark_01 LOL

Well that's my considered opinion of what the channels are (were?) meant to be used for. People don't use them for what they were intended. The original point by Chupackabra I think

By making the environment less flimsy and open to 'abuse' is the short answer.

In EQ the economy is a joke. Everything will eventually trickle downward and the only time it stops is when Verant introduce band aids to the flaws - eg lore and no drop tags. Had they done the economy correctly from the start then RPing would have had more fertile ground to flourish. Inflated money, poor drops on mobs, twinkable items leading to nerfs and band aid fixes. They all decrease immersion.

EQ enforces grouping with a stick rather than a carrot. Grouping is desirable by Verant so Verant make it a certainty for most classes. When a couple of classes prove more capable of exp gaining while solo they rightly (to stick with the overall spirit of the game) have to nerf. Had they done the carrot grouping approach then players would have felt like they had a choice abeit one which Verant were steering. No choice = less ability to play a role how you want to play it.

Anti-grief measures in EQ are a joke too. They detract from your feeling of being in control and by any reasonable definition were simply tagged onto the game much later. Leave the door wide open for abuse and abuse will happen widely. What they needed to do is lay ground rules from day 1 and enforce them fairly and evenly to everyone. PnP is a huge deterant to RPing all by itself because it allows griefers and others to force you out of RPing the character you want to play.

RPing tools were put into EQ. Languages, races, religions, classes, sexes and a whole host of other things. None of these are used much for RP though. Why play an Erudite paladin when a dwarf paladin has 'better' stats. Not my opinion but certainly this is how people think. Balance is the key and encouragement to use the tools given. There was nothing stopping them from a simple change like defaulting language to your native one for example. It wouldn't have stopped common language at all but it shows pro-RP thinking on Verants part. They didn't and won't do it.

Class/race balance in EQ is achieved by making stats worthless to the point where everyone is the same. Skills in the game are avaialble to everyone of a certain class equally. There is no variety. Variety in EQ comes from the equipment you wear and eventually, due to a crappy economy, everyone will even have the same gear in the end.

Verant OOG policy stinks where RP is concerned. They use RP only as an excuse to change something. Otherwise it doesn't exist. Considering all the fan fiction out there Verant could have implemented their own site for this. Not game related exactly but it would show encouragement for pro-Rp ideas again. Verant always state that RP cannot be enforced. True but it can be steered. They do it everytime they give an RP-like excuse for not implementing an idea. They do it everytime they state categorically that we cannot see numbers for mana etc. That is enforcing an immersive environment. They just don't see that is what it is and they don't do enough of it. Not the mana number thing exactly but the way of thinkiing, RP-like.
