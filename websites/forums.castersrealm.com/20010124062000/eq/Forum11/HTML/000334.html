There are two equally valid answers as to which to specialize in, depending on your style of play. ALT will give you a better ability to be group healer/support - Heals, Regen/Chloro, Stregnths will all benefit from ALT, so do roots+snare.

However if you are primarily a solo/nuke'em down type, EVO is a very good choice, especially when you into into quad-kiting territory.

I went for ALT as I'm quite social and like to group, and even when I do decide to solo, root+DoT is my preferred option for hard blues (so I gain on roots). I've not had any mana problems on quad-kiting so far, though sometimes I feel it'd be nice to save some mana when I'm doing this for several hours.

I don't know anybody who has specialized in CON, although I guess its worth consideration on the DoT front.

Misata, basically here is how it works.

You can specialize in one or all magical skills, but just one - your primary and first skill to 51 - can exceed 50. It IS worth investing a practise point in ALL skills, because whenever a spell is cast, a roll is thrown to see if it receives the specialisation bonus (reduced chance of fizzle + reduced mana cost). The higher your specialization skill, the greater the chance of hitting the bonus. Whilst your primary has greater chance of getting the bonus because it will be higher than your other skills, you are still ahev a chance of hitting the bonus on the other skills - they will rise to 50 quite quicky.

Ok, which skill to go for? Hard call. What I'd recommend is skip through the spell list and try and see which spells you'll be casting, but here are the options:-

EVOcation
Affects DD, AoE, anti-summon and occasional DoT spells (Immolate, Breath of Ro etc).

Great for soloing when you are burning things down with DD or when you quad-kite, but this is not always possible on critters with high HP, and when you do the root+DoT thing, you lose out too.

ALTeration
Affects, heals, sow's, harmony, charms, lulls, fears, snare, ensare, roots, cure disease/poison, gate, bind, teleports/evacs, enduring breath, strength buffs, stamina boosts, levitation, wolform, share wolfform, treeform, pet buffs, regeneration/chloroplast/regrowth, invis, debuffs, change whether spells.

You will be casting many of these spells alot, but many of them have low mana requirements anyway. However, if you expect to do doing the group buffing thing alot, give this ALOT of consideration, heals are mana expensive, group chloro is 400 mana a pop and fizzling on an evac does not bear thinking about

CONjuration
Affects, summoning food/water, summoning visual aids, most DoT's, occasional DD's (Whirling Wind DD+Stun line), summoning pets.

If you plan to do the roo+DoT thing alot, which is the most mana efficient method of killing, CON can make alot of sense are you stand a good chance of saving on the big mana DoT's which you'll be using alot. This also helps if you are whacking summoned critters like shadowed men as the Ward/Dismiss Summoned line is very mana efficient at higher levels.

The problem with this kind of decision at lvl 20 is you just have no real idea which spells you are going to need for the next 40 levels (up to lvl 60) - try questioning some high level druids.

However you can change your primary specialization skill with a quest in the Temple of Solusek, so if you do think you've made a major error you can complete a quest to reset all your spec. skills back to 49.
