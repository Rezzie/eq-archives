The problem is once your first pet dies you are at the top of the aggro list because of the amount of damage you've done. Your new pet then has trouble getting the mob off you (It's really mad at you!) Try this:
-Cast a pet
-med up
-Burnout pet
-med full
-go pull a mob with Malise and get back to your pet.
-Flameshield your pet
-sit
-watch pet health
-When you know he's gonna die begin casting new pet (you know the drill... you've chaincasted already)
-Send new pet on mob
-wait till new pet has mob's attention and try nuking just once really hard near the end.

All you have to do is outdamage the SECOND pet and you get the exp. You make adjustments as needed depending on the mob so that you nuke as little as possible while outdamaging the LAST pet you need to summon to kill a mob.

If it takes 3 pets chaincasted to kill a mob you only have to outdamage the LAST one to get the exp. So sit and let the pets take a pounding. I usually only BO and shield the first one... the rest are 'disposeables'. Keep in mind if you have the mana to spare that the damage shield damage gets credited to nobody.

You forgot a key part of this stategy and that is to use /pet get lost.

The only part of chain-casting that requires practice is your timing. When summoning your second pet as your first pet is being attacked, click the /pet get lost hotkey just before the new pet comes in. If you don't use this command, your timing might be a little off and your second pet won't come into play (You'll get the "you can only have one pet up at a time" message)

You need to be in range of pet commands.

/pet report, /pet get lost.

Keep doing /pet report till pet is 20-40% health see how much mob does 1%-5% per tick.

Summon a new pet. /pet get lost right before you complete the cast.

Some mobs have huge attack range, Some mobs hop around trying to get close to you.
Basically if you get your timing perfect, you wont get hit or get hit few times and hope you can continue to cast.

Watch out for lag, wandering mobs, it can throw off your chaining.

really chain-cast is only about skill, it has nothing to do with lag or any other factors. It is very mana-efficient, You can med in between summons , with damage shield on pet only takes 320mana per pet (200+120 ds) at 49th level.

You get 2700 hitpoint pet, you can summon around 10+ pets thats 27000+ hitpoints.

You can kill anything that doesn't summon you, go try it on gullerback (that doesn't summon) Mature wurms, Dino etc...

Even though bard, druid, enchanter, necro, can solo these mobs at 50+, we can also solo them using our trusty, safe chain-cast method.

You can solo Dino at 50th level chain-casting around 10+ pets, At 60th level you can solo Dino chain-casting around 10+ pets.

Your skill with chain-casting would have gone up alot from 50th to 60th, so you will have 100% success rate at 60th level.
