To recap for those still confused. They are nerfing the "feature" where a haste equipment (hangman's noose, runebrandt girdle, flowing black silk sash, etc) would stack with a haste spell (augment, augmentation, quickness, swift like the wind, etc) to raise the haste cap of the person with both the equipment and the spell casted on them to the maximum haste cap regardless of whether they were below or above level 50.

haste item + haste spell = max haste

It is now being changed I assume to haste equipment stacking additively to the haste spell to produce the amount of haste being applied to the person being casted on.

haste item + haste spell = added total haste

I think this is a bad move on the part of Verant. As many have stated this "feature" has been a part of the game so long it's not even funny. To say this is not a nerf to haste casting classes is funny. Before I hit level 51 (very recent) I would go in some dungeons and the pulls would be so fast that just doing crowd control and buffing I was running at a very very low rate of mana and sometimes oom while there were 2 or 3 mobs still to kill. This was using augmentation and not slw (swift like the wind). Now that I have to use slw or something better all the time it's gonna really really drain my mana.
Bate stated that the higher level haste spells will actually do something now and that's true but you never heard any enchanters complain. They were still useful for casting on pets. But to suggest that the way it worked prepatch nerfed enchanters? That's a load of bull. EVERY enchanter loved the way it worked and EVERY bard loved the way it worked even more since bards do melee and singing a spell with an fbss or other haste item raised their underpowered backsides tremendously. Anyone who has reached level 45+ will know this is a MAJOR nerf to all classes that benefited from this.

My one teeny tiny hope in all of this is that they will at least give us a small bonus of perhaps 5% or so if both a haste item and spell are present on the one being casted on. This shouldn't be too hard to program in and would at least benefit those with both spell and item present on them. I highly doubt they will change it back to the way it was prepatch.

Say the FBSS hastes by 24%. Say that Augmentation hastes by 30%. Well, since they stack, there are two ways in which it would be logical for them to stack. The first way is additive. That is, 24% + 30% = 54% haste. The second logical way is multiplicative, that is, 124% x 130% = 161%

These ways are logical and make sense. The thing is, it didn't work this way. The way it worked until today was that *any* haste spell in combination with *any* haste item would automatically bring you up to the maximum allowable haste level.

The result of today's nerf is not to lower the effect of an FBSS, it is rather to lower (primarily) the effect of the lvl 29 enchanter spell Augmentation on those wearing an FBSS (or wielding a mith 2hander or whatever).

While today's nerf brings us to what is in actuality logical, it is a fact that Norrath was balanced up till today with the previous, illogical stacking behavior. Changing the stacking behavior now invalidates the level of balance, and creates a new level of balance which is more in the mobs' favor than things were yesterday and the past year and a half. That is what we are griping about.

Also, given the illogic and downright hubris of Abashi in dealing with some other stacking anomalies, eg: Mez spells of a higher lvl not overwriting lower level mez spells, and the whole "it's a bug, no it's not a bug" fiasco, it was not entirely wrong of us to assume that Verant *meant* for haste items and haste spells to stack in the old way.

Just thought I would attempt to clarify this for those who weren't aware.
