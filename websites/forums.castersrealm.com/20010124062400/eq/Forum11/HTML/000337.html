Personally, I find shaman as fun as druids, they have some fun utility spells just as we do. On a more serious gameplay front, shamans are much harder to progress at lower levels, where 99% of the spells you get are buffs.

Things do turnaround at lvl 19, and get much better with each new set of spells, plus it's fair to say that at higher levels, shamans are much more in demand than druids - particularly so if there are many tanks in the group, or if the raiding party is hitting a dungeon.

On the surface, druids and shaman seem very similiar, but underneath there is enough variety and differences in play to warrant playing both classes to high levels. In terms of damage dealt, shamans leave us standing, their DoT's are at least on parr with necro's at medium-to-high levels and often better.

The only heavier downside to a shaman over a druid is the often lower base WIS stat, shaman typically coming from the thickest races on the planet (no offence!).

It really depends on what you want.

I am a Shaman, so I'm biased on which is better. Both have their strong points, and I can only speak of my experince to my current level, 19.

Druids are great at Soloing. They can Kite incredibly well, much better then Shamans. Once you hit the 20's, it's great to be able to teleport yourself anywhere, and later port others. Not getting enough experince somewhere? Just port yourself elsewhere. Druid casting ability, while it doesn't reach the sheer damage potential of the Shaman's DoT's, it's still rather high, and you should have more mana to make up for the less damage. While you can Tank, it is not your strong point, and you won't be as effective at it as a Shaman. I really feel that while Druids are cool in a group, Shaman's really tend to outshine them here. It makes sense though, since Druid's outshine Shaman's in soloing. You also won't be able to wear as good armor as Shamans, and they'll have more hitpoints then you.

Shamans, on the other hand, can wear nice armor, do nice damage, and have plenty of hitpoints. While we get nice spells, most of them are at later levels, so soloing between 10 and 20 is difficult and frustrating at times. We really shine at grouping. We can fill the roles of Tank, Caster, or Healer easily, and have no problems with Crowd Control. Most people love having a good Shaman in the group for buffing and healing.

Unfortunatly, most Shaman I've found don't seem to use their skills to their full potential, which gives us good Shaman bad names.

My second character I ever played was a shaman...I had a human enchanter as my first one until I found out Verant lied about humans and the enchanter class. Anyway, I played my shaman and made 25 after a year of fairly casual playing. I didn't have that much trouble soloing most of the time, being able to stack 2 or 3 dots after debuffing a mob was easy, but the low wis inherent in all shaman races (pre-iksar) did make things a bit dicey. Everfrost as a newbie zone excels which makes things easier when you start.

By the middle ranges, a pattern had formed. For soloing: I would spell (Rooting and DOTs) till OOM, and then tank to finish off the last 1/3 health the mob had.

After making 24, downtime was greatly reduced thanks the wonderful spell combination: regeneration and cannibalize. Not the most efficient, yes that's true, but a heck of a lot better than nothing. The only other problem now that I face is having to run everywhere...but at least we get spirit of woof at level 9.

Last month I decided to start a druid for the heck of it. Let me tell you that druids have a much easier time of it compared to most of the other classes I have played (I have played all of them.) Snare + fear on animals at low levels...then once you get DOTs: snare, DOT, nuke, nuke, nuke...thorns on self and tank.

As for minimizing downtime...if someone is on the other side of the world you NEVER have to walk all the way. Spirit of Woof at 14 (later than shaman yes) takes some of the pain out of running but at 19 you get those wonderful portal spells. Just portal over and you have saved at least half the trip most of the time. No more waiting for the boats, no more long boatrides

Of course I still take the boat for fun

Doing a quest that requires 4 zones all over the continent? Port, port, port. Are you in Everfrost and there's a group forming in butcher? Port...boom saves about 2 or more hours. In kunark and need to get to the karanas? Port...saves 2 boat trips plus the nasty run through Kithicor.

If however you are in a great guild that regularly offers free portals than the usefulness of this diminishes. Odds are however you are going to have to run more errands the higher you get and it is nice to have this ability.

Oh yeah, by the way, after about a month and a half...without twinking, without powerlevelling, I have made level 24...soloing about 95% of the time. I haven't grouped for long because every group so far I have been in: 1) gave less xp per time and 2) honor loot = ninja looting. Of course this may vary from server to server or if you have some good friends already. About the only real downside I have found to the class is no ability to increase mana recovery. But they get a form of self-regeneration long before any other class (treeform at level 9), which can help you conserve mana.

So the gist of this long-winded post? Druids are easier. I still play my shaman though, because I like certain role-playing aspects of it.

Good luck whichever you choose.

Cheers.
