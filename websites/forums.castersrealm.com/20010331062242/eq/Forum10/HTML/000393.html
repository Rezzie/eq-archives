Innoz the basic aggro for the heal spells does indeed work on 1 point healed = 1 point of aggro.

Your DoTs taunt so well because they do more aggro than the damage they do, and along with attacks and taunting move you to the top of the hate list. They also add a little aggro each tic while the spell lasts which helps taunting in the long run.

The cleric problem occurs when the cleric heals somebody for a lot of hit points. The aggro for Complete heal is capped on players below 51 at 800 aggro points. For those 51 plus it's goes up to a believed level of 2000.

From what I've read just being near a mob generates about 120 approx points of hate. But the heal aggro is like casting an area affect spell. Imagine what would happen to a wizard casting a 700-800 point area effect spell on a group of mobs. You'd expect them to die wouldn't you if the circumstances weren't right ? Same with the cleric healing.

At the high levels it's worse. Assuming the 2000 cap is correct thats probably more hate in one spell than any direct damage spell that can be cast. And that's an area effect 2000 aggro too. Also for a fully buffed high level tank 2000 hits might only be half their hit points. So even if the cleric waits to cast Complete Heal they still get aggroed on because no single tank has done enough aggro to beat the aggro the heal spell creates.

Mezzed mobs also get heal aggro assigned to them, as do root parked mobs. And as the Memblur and Atone spells aren't 100% at higher levels even with crowd control a cleric can soon find themselves swamped.

Of course there are ways of handling aggro in normal xp groups. The new heal over time spells help (they should be available as vendor spells in my opinion) , and groups have managed to hold their own with changes in tactics.

However for the epic mobs multiple problems occur.
First of all numbers often have to be limited to prevent link deaths.

Then for the mobs over 32K hits points the most efficent way to kill them is a lot of tanks. Mana based damage is inefficent and resists often mean most spells don't land.

So you'll need a lot of tanks. And as these mobs do horrendous damage the tanks need to be Complete Healed. There's no other way to prevent the tanks death due to the speed of the damage.

So the cleric has to cast a Complete Heal. Way before the tanks have done more aggro than the heal spell. The mob sees the spell, and being high level summons the cleric and starts attacking. The tanks can't taunt as high level mobs are currently almost taunt proof. No spells will land which rules out Shadowknight taunting, and even then the aggro they do is far short of what the cleric has produced.

The cleric then has to use a Divine spell to become invulnerable. But the tanks still can't match the aggro of the heal so when it drops the cleric is toast. Assuming the cleric can even get to cast the spell.

And assuming the tanks can get it off the cleric if the cleric has to heal again this starts all over.

Now they plan to change the aggro system again soon (hopefully this upcooming patch). We'll have to see how it goes from there.

I should point out that my knowledge of the high level (51+) situation has been gleaned from posts since the aggro change. Once I get my cleric into the 50s I'll soon see for myself.
