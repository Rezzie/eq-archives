What if I said neither is a good option?

Questing is a very underutilized aspect of the game. By that I mean we as players gain stronger and better items from direct non-quest-related kills than we do virtually any non-epic quest in the game. Then there remain a few items in the game that are virtually impossible to quest for because the target NPC to kill or speak with is camped religiously.

An area to explore on questing is a rotation of the items that the NPC may request at any one time as well as what item you might get in return. Today he may need a red dragon scale and three diamonds.. tomorrow he may need your wurmslayer and a dragon scale... another day he desire 5000 platinum and Cazic's head. The possibilities go on.. but I am sure you can see the direction this is taking.

I love going on raids where organization and a team effort are required. I would prefer to see more no-drop quest rewards in the game that surpass the items I see bombarded in the various zones traders thrive in. Bring quests to a level that cause players to choose playing over trading... provide actual REWARDS instead of the typically inferior items we see.

The quests could be an alternative means to getting existing items that are only available on raid level NPCs... or the quest model could accomplish other goals by requiring the player perform a certain task at each level (powerleveling services might deminish if epics required this approach).. again.. these are minor diversions.. but they point to the same direction.

Safe-questing helps with silly and sometimes costly mistakes... and I actually like the idea of the items being returned. But the problem isn't so much that we turn in improper items... but that in order to get items we have to buy them from players or camp more vs play less. Questing should be a fun and enjoyable avenue for us where the challenge isnt sleep deprivation.. and it should not include the burden of hearing your quest item being auctioned off because groups or quilds have it timed to the minute and can expedite the kill before you can even pluck an arrow off.

I can never decide if I consider multi-questing a scam or a sham... but I will let you choose which. I agree with the person who wrote something to the effect of "The quest was given to YOU.. not your guild or your friends..." I might.. MIGHT... actually see the beauty behind multi-questing if I didn't things like the Wurmslayer scales for sale... or part of the JBoots quest for sale. The concept of HELPING someone with their quest is fine... get in their group.. go on their raid.. let that person working on the quest loot what they need. But don't cheapen the experience of what it took to create the quest or to complete the quest. And there should be a way to prevent players from profiting in this way.

I am unsure if this is possible but will mention it... Is there a way to mark a player who has a certain quest and only a person with that particular mark is able to loot items that are used in the specific quest? Just a thought.

So what is the solution.. well.. I don't know.. I mentioned a few items earlier that I like.. while Safe-questing seems good it has a cheesy aroma.. but Multi-questing reaks of abuse and problems.
