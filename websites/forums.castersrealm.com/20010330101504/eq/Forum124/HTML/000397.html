There are so many considerations to take into account on this issue that it almost reverts to a situation-by-situation basis. Me, personally, I have played 6 characters past 30 without twinking (Shaman, Warrior, Ranger, SK and Necro) because I always felt that you became a better player by doing it the "natural" way. However, I am now starting a halfling rogue on my primary server, and I am going to be twinking it as much as possible. The main points against twinking I have seen are:
1. Twinkers don't learn their characters abilities and therefore make bad group members - depends on the person. I have played many classes and I have researched the Rogue class before I am going to start playing, so hopefully I will be able to learn while I am cruising through lvl 20. I never intend to trumpet the fact that I am a twink, and will be a good party member just as I have been with my past 5 characters.

2. Twinkers take all the good loots from high level characters to twink out their alts - I have always gone Need before Greed in any party I have been in. Most of my items I will be twinking my rogue with have been bought with money gained from smithing or adventuring.

3. That character didn't earn the item so they don't deserve it - this sounds like jealousy to me. I have earned the rights to use those items with my other characters. It is either use them myself or be greedy and sell them to other players, and I really don't see that as a better option. Besides, a lot of time spent on Everquest is wasted camping certain items. By buying those items, I not only prevent myself from wasting that time, but I free up those camps for people wanting to loot that item. Another issue is that on many servers it is very much easier to get high end weapons than on others. Getting a Blood Point on Mith Mar will be much easier than getting one on Vazaelle. Therefore the actual definition of twinking can vary from server to server unless define it as transferring any items whatever from one of your characters to another (which I really don't see anything wrong with).

These are just a few of the discussions that can go on with this issue. There are more, but a full discussion would take longer than I have to write.
