The key to powerleveling fast is a large damage shield, high HP buff, and heals, and the ability to travel to areas with things that are around 5 levels higher than you. That is why high level druids are in such high demand -- they are the ultimate powerleveler.

You can take on 4 high reds at a time, but you must do a minimum damage (I think it is 4 hp to each) to get xp. The reds just kill themselves on your damage shield as you stand there. Each red is worth a 1/2 bubble of xp at lower levels, so you can gain 2 bubbles of xp within just a minute or two. Unrest is an ideal place to powerlevel from 1 to 30. My friend got to 17 in 4 hours and 27 in 14 hours there with just one druid powerleveling him.

At higher levels, other techniques can be used, like friends that hit things down to 1%, then zone and you kill it. It can be done at any level, as long as you have enough people delivering the experience to you at a steady rate. Add in a high level cleric as a helper and you never lose XP from dying.

Do you believe it now that you see how it can be done easily?

I don't think you would want to group with a person that did this, especially an enchanter

If I remember the details of the post about experience, the class that needs the least experience to level is warrior, and the race that needs the least experience to level is halfing, so a halfling warrior should be able to powerlevel faster than any other character. Also melees should be able to powerlevel faster than casters because their swings will land more often, doing the minimum damage needed to get xp when using the damage shield method.

It think without doubt the druid is the best to Plevel at lower levels. With a Skin like nature/chloro/damage shield and heals you run virtually no risk of dying.

Best zones to Plevel players are where the mobs don't run, chasing a snared mob 5 levels higher than you is a pain in the ***, the way around this is for the druid to root mobs ontop of you to stop them running and keep them aggro.

Because you have to do at least some damage to the mob (If the Dshield kills the mob without you touching it, the mob poofs and no xp or corpse), tank classes are probably the easiest to level.

I can see at higher levels tho where a 25pt damage shield is really little use at all (mobs having 1000's of hp), that you would need a team of players including a chanter to mem blur to give the best xp income.

Personally i levelled my main past 50 and enjoyed the low levels and experience i gained from it - i still laugh when an obvious Plevel or Ebay player without any EQ knowledge and at lvl 50 doesn't know the /assist command
