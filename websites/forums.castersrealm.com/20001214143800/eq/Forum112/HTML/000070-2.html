I only have a relatively low level troll SK, and as far as melee goes, there isnt a huge difference between a SK and most melee classes. All have strengths and weaknesses, warriors have slightly more hp, ac and can critical now and then, SKs get spells and a pet.

One of the biggest problems we have is this totally uncalled for experience penalty. It makes soloing pointless because we suffer the whole penalty ourselves. At higher levels groups will refuse to take SKs, Paladins and Rangers because the experience penalty the individual has chosen is passed onto the group. I know this from experience. I've been there with my druid and shaman and know what people say when a SK wants to join a group. Why pay through the nose when there is no need. FD pulling isnt really necessary if you have an enchanter in the group, lets face it enchanters are breeding like rabbits nowdays.

Most high end groups have clerics and enchanters in them, our strengths are obsolete when others do things just as well with no extra experience loss for the group.

Let me show you why:

A group consists of a level 45 Dwarf Cleric, Halfling druid, Highelf Enchanter, Woodelf Rogue and a Human Warrior. Currently the experience is distributed 20.6%, 19.6%, 22.7%, 18.6% and 18.5% respectively.

They feel they really need the services of another 'tank' class and to deal with the really tough mobs. Bob, a 50th level Troll SK and Jo, a 50th level Dwarf Warrior are looking for a group. Who will they add?

If they add in Jo, the warrior, their share of the experience drops to 16.1%, 15.3%, 17.8%, 14.6% and 14.5% respectively. Jo's share of the experience would be 21.6%. Jo would only be earning 3.8% more than the highest previous party member.

If they add in Bob, the Shadowknight, their share of the experience drops to 13.6%, 12.9%, 15.0%, 12.3% and 12.2% respectively. Bob's share of the experience would be 34.0%. Bob would be earning 19% more than the highest previous party member, more than double the experience he receives.

How many intelligent people would figure in that situation that the SK is worth a third of the group experience? How many would choose the SK over the Warrior? Answer is not many, and alot of high level SKs spend hours looking for groups.

There is no reason to have the experience penalty. Warriors, Rogues and Monks are more than viable and comparable to Shadowknights in offensive capabilities with their disciplines. It is the only reason why SKs suck, otherwise they are fun to play.
