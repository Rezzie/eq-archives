In general, this method isn't much different from doing crowd control in a regular group. The primary difference is that you have an unwilling tank, who could turn on you at any moment, and no backup when resists happen.

Hmmm... only five spectres to deal with? Cast Mesmerization on all of them, then charm one. Then re-cast Mesmerization to hold the uncharmed four. Move a bit away, and when the spell break message (for mesmerization) appears (meaning that your charmed spectre is no longer mesmerized), sick him after one.

Then kite-enthrall the others while the first two battle it out. (By kite-enthrall, I mean enthrall them while keeping yourself out of their melee range.) Or just keep holding them with Mesmerization. Med in the meantime.

While meditating, stay away from the two who are currently fighting, and stand up and look at what's happening between mana regen ticks (unless you're already 35th level...). Don't trust the "your charm has worn off" message, as it sometimes appears sometime after you're being smacked around by your former pet.

Add a DD or two to the first non-charmed spectre to help ensure that your charmed buddy wins the fight.

If charm breaks early, liberally use enthrall or mesmerization or color flux (if you're unlucky enough to be in melee range with a spectre) to restore control. Just don't get yourself caught in the area of effect for mesmerization if you can help it.

Once the first spectre is down, once the other spectres are completely under your control, enthrall the injured one, charm a different spectre, and send the new charmee to fight the formerly charmed, quite injured spectre. Charming the others in turn helps guarantee that you get all the XP for all the kills, since a full-health spectre will probably defeat a well injured spectre, which your formerly charmed spectre should be.

You can also work on spreading the spectres out by allowing them to break mes/enthrall without re-applying it; then individually stop them, some distance from each other, and then use Memory Blur to make 'em forget and go back home (if five at a time is too much...). Just beware that you could get lots of younger characters killed when the spectres start wandering if you do this too near them (or when the spectres start pathing all the way around the lake...).

Separating them like this would be important for the purpose of keeping them from re-aggroing each other if the Memory Blur doesn't work on one or more of them. Then again, if you're expert enough to separate them like this, you probably won't have much difficulty taking on all five.

The biggest cost of this method is the charm spells themselves; you would ordinarily only need one or two DD's on the first spectre to be slain in order to guarantee that the charmed spectre wins; after that, by charming different spectres to use on the injured spectres, no additional DD is required.

To avoid the problems associated with insta-break charms, wait until the spectres are already mesmerized/enthralled before charming. This means you have a few seconds less on your charm duration, but an insta-break charm is very recoverable and results in no damage.

Of course, keep your best Rune spell up. The loot from the spectres will more than cover the costs.

And, since you are in Oasis, you should have absolutely no problems trading Clarity for SoW.

Just adding a few lower lvl strategies, so everything doesn't evolve around specs and spirocs ;-)

From 16 and onwards to 29 you can charm solo for increadible exp in Lake of Ill Omen.

1) Enter zone
2) /OOC Breeze for SoW
3) You feel the spirit of the wolf enter you
4) Search the zone for an even/yellow con
5) Mez/Tash/charm target
6) Search out another even/yellow con
7) Pull with tash
8) /pet attack
9) Root and Choke non pet
10) When non pet is at 20% health, mem blur and nuke it for full exp.
11) /pet guard here
12) Hide/invis
13) Mez, root, mem blur, nuke
14) You have just gained exp from 2 yellows at the cost of a half mana bar.

If anything goes wrong and you go below 50% health with both mobs still up, I would advise you to mez/mem blur and med up for another try.

3 minutes extra med time beats the loss of half a bub of exp.
