Gee, after some of these excellent posts I don't know how much I can add.
1. NO class is perfect--get used to it.
2. Mages can outdamage any other class in the game over time--with the exception of necro DoTs.
3. Mage pets use NPC calculators for power. A 50+ pet (fire is a bit different) can almost always solo any tank it wants to. Without you ever casting anything on it. Fire is different because 50+ flames take a reduction in hp and power in exchange for high 30-ish wizzie spells. Dispell magic, the force shock line, a flame dd, stuff like that. Kick's ass against PC's, not so good against MoB's. Fairly useful in groups, sometimes.
4. Mage DD is not terribly inferior to that of wizards at high levels, and nothing else comes close. The allways-mentioned IC only does about 300 damage more than the most powerful mage dd spell, which I believe is lava bolt. This is pre-50+, mind you. After that, the gap widens a bit. Not too much, but enough. Necros, again, can outdamage with DoT's, but in a group setting quick damage is usually more useful.
5. Mage summoned stuff is nice. Sword of runes? Goodbye Mob elemental. People will PAY for this stuff. Mana for money is almost never a bad thing. 20 food? you got it. 20 water? cool. Proc'ing weapons? Coming right up. Levitation? Water breathing? Ultravision and AC? Mages got all o' dat. The weapons won't be as good as what tanks will have by then, but your pet will use them happily.
6. Did I mention that mage pets are the best in the game, with absolutely no room for argument? Deal with their insane superiority. Mages are really one of only three classes that can effectively solo past about 35 or so. (the other 2, druid and necro, of course). Plus, you get options. RE: But do have to admit that the blades/swords line has a very low resistance rate in my opinion and i am a lv 51 magician.

I learned this, as soon as I got the first in the line, and understand the why of it as well. So for those younger mages and other classes who don't understand it, I'll explain the why.

Our shock line, as well as the Necro's Life Tap line, are magic based damage. Therefore only the target's magic resist can effect it. However, the other lines, such as flame bolt and shock of flames, are based on heat and magic. So the combined effect of heat resistance and magic resistance effect our spell's chance of being resisted.

On a side note. I heard Necros complaining a lot about their spells being resisted. All the while their life taps rarely are. The life tap used to be unresistable.
