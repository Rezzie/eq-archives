>>I just picked up EQ and i will be playing a monk, but i know nothing of EQ and how to
start a character and what to concentare on as a starting character. <<

First off let me welcome to you to the world of Everquest. I have been playing it for almost two years now and I love it! Be careful though, as the game sucks your very soul from you. You'll find yourself ditching class or calling in "sick" to work just to play the game. Don't cancel a date to play it, though, as that can be detrimental to your health.

>>where to put skill points and what i need to learn.<<

Regardless of which race you choose to play (Human or Iksar) I suggest that you pump all 20 bonus points into STR. There are lots of dex/agi gear out there that you can outfit yourself with. You will be primarily a melee class so you would want to hit hard (and, hopefully be hit for less and survive longer). You should focus on getting your AC , str and HP high. Although agi and dex are great to get high (don't neglect these) AC is far more important.

>>i also could use some advise on starting a human monk or an Iksar...i dont know in the long run which would be more benificial...regeneration and infravision sound pretty cool but i dont know if its all that and a bag of chips...<<

You're forgetting the naturally high AC that iksars have. They have a great newbie area as well, Field of Bone. You can solo there and Kurn's Tower until level 18 or so, then hop two zones away to Lake of Ill Omen and work your way into the high 20's. You never have to leave Kunark, you can get level 60 there easily. Being KoS really isn't that much of a hindrence, unless you leave to the old world. However, I love the old world and the dungeons there (SolB, LGuk, anyone?) and as it is hard to play a KoS race I find the challenge somewhat fun (my FIRST character was an Ogre warrior, Barshem gud). Humans can't see at night but they don't have the 20% experience penalty and after 50 and in a group with a healer, Iksar and human are pretty much equal anyway as your HP will be constantly healed to full. I suggest you go with Iksar but both races are equally good choices.

>>but i would appreciate any advice on where to start, server, town etc. and if the KOS Iksar factor may not be in my best interest to play for the long haul....
Thanks<<

I don't like playing on the older servers (Fennin, Karana), so pick a fairly new one. I switched to Tribunal when it first came up and have been on it every since. I don't want to leave because of all the great friends I've made. Keep in mind that EQ is a community. REAL people are playing those animated, pixellated characters you see on your monitor. Server preference is really up to you. Iksars can only start in Cabilis, but if you do chose to play human I suggest Freeport. It's a fairly simple city to navigate and East Commonlands is a GREAT zone as well because it's an auction zone (at least on Tribunal anyway) and high level characters are there and readily available to buff/save your butt as needed.


>>Will choosing a religion matter and how much affect does religion play in game or is
agnostic the way to go???<<

Religion affects only two things: What armor/weapons/equip you can use (Very few items, now) and your faction.
If you choose to play an Iksar, you have to have Cazic-Thule as your diety and as a human you have two options: Agnostic or Quellious. As choosing Quellious doesn't effect your factions that much, I suggest going with that. They may introduce Deity specific items which only people following Quellious can use and if you're agnostic you won't be able to use those items.

Well, I hope my post has helped you much. If you have any other questions, just post them and I'll be more than happy to reply.

I never considered playing an Iksar simply because of the KoS hassle. As far as religion is concerned, I believe there are only two choices - Agnostic and a deity. My character chose the latter. She started in Freeport for the first four levels and has moved on to BB to hunt Orcs and wisps (for $$$).

Something you may want to do as I have found to be quite productive for earning cash (for low level) is to keep as many batwings as possible and resell them in stacks (of 20) for the standard 5 pp. There are plenty of rich and powerful casters who constantly need them and 5 pp to them is like 25 cents to us. Most of the time, they throw in buffs as well.

Also, BEFORE you sell any rusty weapons you get from killing MOBS back to the merchants, sharpen them first (with sharpening stones) then sell them. It won't bring more money BUT it will improve your blacksmithing skill. Why is this important? Because you can then make toolboxes (with enough skills earned from sharpening weapons) which weight 0.4 stone. They have the same carrying capacity as a backpack (eight) BUT 2.6 stones LESS! And weight means everything to a monk. But just don't hit combine on the toolbox though!

Hi! Welcome to EQ! The first thing you must know is that monks have become the single most heavily "twinked" class in the game (a twink is a low level character with high level/expensive gear). Be prepared to see the majority of monks at your level with much better gear than you have. I started the game playing a monk and this was certainly frustrating...I thought I would NEVER get such sweet items!!! To put it very simply, I was wrong. I can honestly say that I now have nice stuff.

Anyway...it really doesn't matter what race you start as...humans at high levels do slightly more damage with their fists then iksar but then again, regen and nightsight is pretty groovy. Being Killed On Sight in most "good" cities is a pain in the butt...but you can do quests and kill certain monsters that will raise your faction with "good" cities so that you won't be KOS anymore.

One thing I would DEFINITELY recommend is putting ALL your initial skill points into strength. The reason for this is simple: monks are NOT tanks. Even if you put ALL your skill points into agility, dex and/or stamina, you still wouldn't have the hit points or armor class to be able to soak the damage that a warrior or even a hybrid (sk or pally) can. The monk is a damage dealing class, not a damage soaking class. Therefore, put your skill points into the area which will allow you to do more damage: strength.

If you decide to go with human, start in Freeport. Period.

One more thing: When you get a new combat skill, put only 1 skill point into it at the monk guild. The skill will then rise quite nicely on its own through practical combat use. One exception to this: Feign Death. This skill is without a doubt the most important and vital skill the monk posesses. You get it at level 17 and if I were you, I would throw ALL of my skill points that I've saved up into this skill...FD has saved my butt literally hundreds, perhaps thousands of times. It is KEY!!!

Oh...start on the Tribunal Server...it's fun and I'm there and I would love to help out if I can...send me a /tell

Ok, as was stated before you want to save most of your practice points. If you don't get any new skills when you level, just save them all.

Monk are the only class in the game that start out with dueil wield and level 1 but each skill you gain along the way is important.


Level 1 - Duel Weild
This is THE monk skill, allowing you to attack more then 1 time per round. Make sure you always have both hands free. That means no holding the newbie candle

Level 1 - Mend
One of the most helpful skills you will ever get, will heal 25% of you hit points, and once the skill gets over 100 it will NEVER fail. Make sure you are always using this even though it will fail most of the time, until it get to around 80 skill.

Level 1 - Kick
Your basic attack, adding an extra attack ever time it refreshes. You will always replace your attack skill when you geta new one

Level 1 - Dodge
Your first devensice skill it will go up naturally in battle, cauing you to dodge enemies attacks

Level 1 - Bind Wound
This is a great skill, allows you to heal yourself. However it requires bandages which are expensive at low levels. But if you can practice it all the time as it is very helpful. It allwos you to heal up to 50% of you total hitpoints, and after level 50 up to 75%

Level 3 - Safe fall
Put 1 point into this, it will take away damage from falling once you get good at it.

Level 5 - Round Kick
This is your new attack skill you will start using this instead of regular kick which you had at level one (use 1 practice point)

Level 8 - Sneak
This skill can be usefull sometimes, but really not a skill that you need (use 1 practice point)

Level 10 - Tiger Claw
This is your next new attack skill, it will replace Round kick (again use 1 practice point)

Level 12 - Block
This is a defensive skill as it rasies you will start to see yourself block enemies attacaks. (use 1 practice point)

Level 15 - Double Attack
This is a prime skill, once you start getting better at it you can attack twice with both fists so up to 4 attacks per round (use 1 practice point)

Level 17 - Feign Death
Finally probabally the most usefull skill you get in the game. This is cause to you fake death, and mosters will stop attacking you if you are successfull. Put ALL those practices you have saved into this, and practice it all the time, makeing sure to keep it maxed out at all times.

Level 20 - Eagle Strike
Another new attack, this will replace the previous one. (1 practice point)

Level 25 - Dragon Punch
Your next attack, replacing Eagle strike (1 practice point)

Level 27 - Disarm
Kind of a neat skill lets you disarm an enemy wielding a weapon, it then drops the the ground (1 practice point)

Level 30 - Flying Kick
Your last attack skill, starting out can do around 60 points of damage per hit, and once you get higher over 100 (i would put all practice points into this since it is your last attack, and you will use it throught the rest of the game)

[b]Level 35 - Riposte
Your last skill and a new defensive skill kind of like dodge. (use 1 practice point)


Well, thats about it, i hope it hels in answering your questions about traning, just remember to keep trak of when you get your skills so that you don't forget to train in them, as all of them are a vital part in your survival and success
