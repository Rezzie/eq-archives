I can see why Verant have nerfed this BP again however I strongly disagree with the reasons. Nerfing items is intrinsically wrong. It is rather like setting someone a target to achieve and then moving the finishing line. Nerfs should only be used as a last resort to prevent widespread and frequent abuse of an item. We all make mistakes and some items that have been brought into the game needed to be nerfed. This BP in it's orginal form was a good case in point. It was far too powerful and easy to abuse even taking into account the rarity and difficulty in obtaining this item. The moss covered twig was another good example.

Firstly Verant released this BP without adequate planning or forethought. Big mistake, however we all make mistakes. The first nerf on the BP to a 30 sec delay was probably a reasonable responce on the face of it. In Verant's defense this nerf was sufficient to prevent abuse by all but the most efficient groups of players. Most players felt an initial annoyance about this nerf but accepted it as reasonable.

As I see the situation now we have one guild (FoH) who are obviously expert players have worked out a tactic to defeat a high lvl dungeon that Verant did not want defeated so easily. WTG FoH! Verant's response is a knee jerk reaction we have to re-nerf the BP again. This responce makes the BP virtually useless in a serious combat situations for the vast majority of players, who are not as expert as FoH. This second nerf is wrong as it devalues a hard won prize massively! To my knowledge this is not an issue of widespread abuse but one or two expert groups of players who have the skill to use this item to it's fullest potential. Nerfing the BP should not be on the agenda at this time unless there is clear wide spread and frequent abuse.

In cases where a skilled party or parties have managed to circumvent the game design I have a solution to prevent the major feeling of outrage experienced by the vast majority of your customers. The response should be: "ok how can we alter the world to defeat this new tactic!" This should be the first thought in any situation like this. Not lets nerf this item.

Nerfing upsets people who spend days or weeks attempting to get an item only to have it devalued by the whim of Verant. It would be far better to look at the dungeon where this occured and put a high level MR mob or two there to interupt any cleric trying to use this tactic. Something similar to the lizards in CT who absolutely hate healers springs to mind. As for solo high lvl mobs which can be taken down by a warrior and a cleric with this BP, just place a couple of interupter mobs with this named mob to prevent the "undesired" tactic. Verant should have learned by now that nerfs upset people big time so instead of nerfing, review where the problems have arisen or are likely to arise as a result of a new tactic and counter this by clever game design. Your customers would welcome additional challenges of a few extra mobs to make a break or kill harder however they hate nerfs. When will you learn this! I dont see this suggestion happening as it is far easier to nerf than actually use some grey matter and defeat the intellect of your customers by clever game design.

Verant You cant close Pandora's box you have made this problem and so instead of taking the easy way out and nerfing invest some time effort and money in putting your mistake right without annoying your customers!

I am awaiting the third nerf or the deletion of this BP. Cos as sure as Bears XXXX in the woods FoH are going to work around this latest nerf too! And as a result of this latest nerf they are not going to tell you how they did it!

I haven't read the whole thread but read the comments Alan made on castersrealm.
The question whether it's justifiable to change or even remove an item from the game that has been there for quite a while is fairly simple to me.
Yes, they are. The reason is easy to find. Consider the makers of the game as the GM's of everquest. If an ordinary pen & paper a GM makes a mistake by giving an item to his heroes of which he could not forsee the consequences, he has the right to either take it away or decrease it's power. It's his obligation to adapt the game in such a way that it stays reasonable and fair.
I am not saying that clerics that obtained this item shouldn't be compensated in one way or another. They did put in an honest effort without the breastplate in the first place to loot it.
Suggestions for this problem are of several different categories.
1) Leave the item as it is.
2) Change the item. Problem is here the technical aspect of the game. Is it possible to implement. The 7.5 min. solution is good but can't be realized. I'm not sure if you could let the item still proc and at the same time let it proc some sort of penalty on the cleric. Mana penalty of some kind. (eg. loose mana half of a full heal spell or so)
3) Remove the item and give clerics another breastplate in exchange.
4) Change the mobs. This is maybe to hard since it will probably bring along some more problems that will affect other classes.

Conclusion: Admit the mistake and do something about it, you have all the right to do this. Give the (un)lucky clerics some compensation for the changes made to this item.
