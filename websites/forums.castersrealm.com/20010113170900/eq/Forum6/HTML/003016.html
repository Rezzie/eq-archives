You can solo from level 1 to 60 if you'd like.

You have to learn to know your limits. Just because you're a level 30 mage doesn't mean you can still run out and kill those reds and yellows like you used to. It's the same with necros, even though it's common knowledge that necros solo easier than magicians in certain situations and vice-versa.

Some of the weapon summons ARE useless, but every class has a few useless spells. There are spells that you'll learn to love. Rod of Modulation is *quite* useful in certain situations, most often in groups. I've kept these things flowing like water to shamen before, likewise with clerics, they love them.

Magicians don't use their summoning line of spells? Every time you make your food, water, bandages, heat/coldstone, swords, daggers or arrows for pullers, lev rings, rods of modulation, etc, you're using your summoning line. Just because you don't make a staff of tracing anymore doesn't mean the whole line is worthless. And that's not to mention that summons are only one of our spell lines. There's also our pets, dd's and bolts, malises, damage shields, and various utilities (bind, [see]invis, etc).

I think that judging a certain class's value to groups by reading their spell list is overrated. The funny thing is that mages don't sound very group friendly on paper, likewise necro's sound very group friendly on paper. In-game it's often the opposite. But who cares? In reality, groups form because people are there to fill up the spots. Granted there are classes that are "required" (clerics, enchanters), but the rest of the spots are often times filled up by people who are "just there and looking for a group." This includes mages.

Finally, you're at a level where many mages get frustrated. You're using your piddly level 24 nuke until level 34 and you're starting to get into the whole hell-level thing. There are still *plenty* of places to solo at for great experience. At that level I was doing treants and aviaks until 32-ish, where I went to kedge keep and soloed the top room for 10 levels and made every ranger, necro, cleric, and wizard who tried to solo that room jealous.

Stick it out until 34, from then it only gets better and better Oh, and make an effort to group when you can, just because you can solo doesn't mean you have to.
