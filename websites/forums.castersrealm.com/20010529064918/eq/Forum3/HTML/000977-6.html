I agree with SeaOfHoles, one fun aspect of EQ should be finding items one can use (and not just sell to vendors) on a regular basis (even small improvements or tradeable items are welcome).

The flawed item design in EQ is that it forces one to either hunt the many nameless MOBs for items to sell to vendors (or much lower levels if you have the time) for cash, or spend hours of CONTIGUOUS time camping to complete a quest or kill a named MOB for highly desirable items that one can wear or trade. The latter is not an option for the casual player, and there is no middle ground (wearable/tradeable but not stellar items found on nameless MOBs).

Put another way, if one plays a third as often over a year (as I have) as a hardcore player playing over 4 months (effectively the same amount of time played online), the hardcore player will have much better equipment (and will have learned alot from all that reading while camping), all else being equal.

Equipment is not the end all of the game, but Verant should not deny that their design caters heavily to the hardcore playing style. We are all having fun with the game, but it can't hurt to improve the item discovery aspects of the game and make quests with more discrete steps and less camping.

So here are a couple of key points from the perspective of the casual player PAYING for a service.

1) Is the power-gamer's money better than mine, such that those who wish to dedicate their lives to EQ are the haves and the remainder of us with real lives are the have-nots - when the only distinction is that they have the time (as raagnor so eloquently put it) to sit on their ass at a campsite? I know Kunark has introduced the random drop concept (thank you, Verant - a START in the right direction), so someone at Verant realizes there's a problem here.

2) Should my (the casual player) gaming experience be penalized at the expense of keeping the power-gamer interested over the longer term?. There are 2 considerations here, the philosphical and the business. Philosophically, I say no - I pay just as much for the same gaming experience as my power-leveling counterpart. From a business perspective, I guess Verant has determined more of it's revenue comes from the power-leveling community, so it must make more "nice" items extremely difficult to obtain to keep this community paying their subscriptions.

From the perspective of a customer paying for a service, and not able to take as full as an advantage of the service as others, I feel cheated. Is that Verant's problem from a business perspective? No. Can they better structure their product so that ALL their customers can get EQUIVALENT levels of satisfaction from the service we pay for? Absolutely.

As a good example, the Mystic Cloak quest. This quest is broken up into segiments that a more casual user has a shot at doing (even though the damn Priest of Najena is a pain in the butt slow camp) that yields a relatively nice item. Bad example - numerous, such as camping the artic polar bear in Everfrost caves. Not only is it a rare spawn, but there are 2 different classes of player that need drops and the spawn is in the area of an extremely high level, high agro spawn (who thinks this crap up, anyway?).

There should be more quests like the former - nice items should be more acquireable through a more complicated sequence of short duration events/camps rather than uber-camps for uber-mobs. Granted, the god armor and equipment will always come from the uber-camps, but the casual player should be able to acquire reasonable good items (class armor at a minimum) over a flexible span of NON-CONTIGIOUS TIME. This would definitely make me happier that I at least have an opportunity to equip my 40s level character with something better than what most power-leveling 20s characters are wearing.
