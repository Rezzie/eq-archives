Okey, here's how it works...

Hybrids (Paladin, Ranger, Shadow Knight) get an experience penalty for being a hybrid. Probably not "fair" but what can we do?

I believe the xp penalty for the Non-Troll/Ogre/Iksar SK is 30%.

When you group, that 30% is taken away from your group members. So if you have a group with 5 other people, they will each suffer a 6% xp penalty.

Does this system make sense? No.

I don't know why the SKs, Rangers, or Pallys should have a penalty anyway. I can accept the fact that Trolls/Iksars get a penalty, because of their ability to Regen, and I can accept the fact that my Ogre SK has a penalty because he has OUT OF THIS WORLD stats, but I don't know why hybrids all suffer a penalty.

I have done a little bit of research on this via others posting and what I have experienced in a group. I too have been rejected from a group becuase of being a 'fatty hybrid'. Here is my understanding.

Based on a post I read that included a quote from an Everquest representative, XP is divided based on the amount of xp that it took to get to your current level. The following numbers are not exact but the ratios are correct so they will give you the idea.

Example:
Group Members
Monk(human) lvl 30 (XP : 3,000,000)
Mage(human) lvl 30 (XP : 3,300,000)
Paladin(human) lvl 30 (XP : 4,200,000)
Warrior(halfling) lvl 30 (XP : 2,565,000)
Shadow Knight(troll) lvl 30 (XP : 5,040,000)
18,105,000
Percentage of XP awarded
Monk (3,000,000 / 18,105,000) : 16.57%
Mage (3,300,000 / 18,105,000) : 18.22%
Paladin (4,200,000 / 18,105,000) : 23.19%
Warrior (2,565,000 / 18,105,000) : 14.16%
Shadow (5,040,000 / 18,105,000) : 27.83%

If this group was to group all the time they would all increase in level at about the same rate. In theroy they should all ding at the exact same time every time. I would not count on them changing much in the game to correct for this issue. So what do we as Shadow Knights, fatty or not, do to deal with this?

My personal approach (others ideas may differ):
(1) When trying to join a large group try to select an zone that you are near the bottom or middle of the character levels. It is much less of an issue when grouping with higher level characters.
(2) If you want to hunt a zone where you are near the higher end of the level range, form a smaller group and kill fast. Select people that allow you to use your Shadow Knight spells and skills to your fullest ablility. (I team up with a mage, enchanter, necromancer, or bard and fear kite if possible. I don't know a rogue but would love to try this with them. Fear kiting is not just a soloing tactic.)
(3) Shadow Knights are one of the better pulling class in a dungeon being that they get both lines of invis and can feign death. A group that can use these skills will have to pay the penalty.

There is no way to get every person to group with you. Some people are more interested in the numbers of the game then just playing the game. The best thing to do is just try to enjoy the character class and try to find other that enjoy what they play and have a good time.

I know that this post really does not do much to help you with the problem. But I hope that the information helps you find your own way to deal with it.
