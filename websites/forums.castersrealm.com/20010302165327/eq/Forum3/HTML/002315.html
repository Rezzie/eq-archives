Author Topic:   What kiled my 50th + lvl guild
Tvar
unregistered 		posted February 25, 2001 08:47 AM              
Over the last 3 months my guild has slowly died, killed by the disease called real life(RL). Day after day, week after week, RL repeatedly struck at the very foundations that are required to play this game.

My first character was the third or fourth level 50 ranger on another server. I logged more then 70 days of played time on him before Kunark came out. My current character is a level 59 cleric with over 90 days of played time. Do I know what I’m talking about? You tell me. I was an officer in my guild since it was founded, back when the server first opened. I can still remember sitting in the chat room to make the guild. Three weeks ago I took over guild leadership mainly because our old GL couldn’t make enough of an online presence. I was the only person that asked for the job. I’m fairly certain that there is only one guild older then mine, and they are nowhere near as “powerful” as mine was. Apart from a few twinks moderately played, all 40-50 active members are 50th level or higher, with around a third of the guild ranging from 55-60. We have 6-7 epics in the guild, mostly the easy ones though, cleric and monk.

We struggled quite successfully through the low levels to become one of the most powerful guilds on the server, three months ago. I can remember when the flames and rants message board exploded with arguments between my guild and the only other guild powerful enough to challenge us. They passed us by very quickly. Now? My guild has little presence on the message boards, we simply haven’t been doing anywhere near enough high lvl activities to get in anyone’s way. Overall we were moderately successful in our raids, obtaining some uber gear.

As our activities moved from newbie things to the harder challenges that Verant throws at us, 50th plus activities, RL reared its ugly head:

Our guild leader went from being a full time player to having a full time job that directly conflicted with cst playing time.

Another solid member had technical problems that shut down her computer for 2 months.

I have racked up hundreds of dollars in long distance phone bills, because in and out of game communication is simply too inefficient.

One of my best friends, someone who I spent uncountable enjoyable hours with leveling to close to 60 with, quit because we weren’t able to hit the higher level mobs. She joined a larger guild, made new friends and now all we do is log on and say hi to each other. When she left I not only lost a good friend, but the guild lost an integral class in the high levels, one we couldn’t afford to lose. Why did she leave? Keep reading.

Not really RL related I guess, but a huge cancer in the guild was travel time. I have a screenshot of a member saying he couldn’t make a Skyshrine raid due to the fact that he only had three hours to play. Are their zones so elite you need to travel for hours to play there? It took us over three hours to assemble less then twelve people in western wastes from various places across Norath. I habitually found EQ to be nothing other than a chat room for up to several hours after logging on. Is this what Verant intended for us in their post 50th level game? I shudder to think of the casual player in the high level game.

The interpersonal problems within the guild ground operations to a halt for weeks at a time. It took weeks for problems to be discussed and solved on the guild board. Our Guild size of 40-50 people was such that if even a few of the key members didn’t make the raid, the raid failed or fell apart before starting. Over my short three-week tenure as GL (I’ve been an officer since formation), I’ve spent almost as much time reading, replying and posting on the member board as I have playing. Almost none of my posts dealt with issues that would further the guild, things like planing raids and recruiting. They all were posts trying to keep the guild together, to try and settle some dispute between members, because we were simply to small to lose yet another high level member. The first reaction to almost everyone involved with a problem was “Well I will just leave the guild then” We had two members who both left the guild because of each other. Anyone else but me see the problem with that? Everyone had lots of ideas on how to help solve the problems we were facing, yet when I asked a raid of up to 24 people “everyone gtg?” all of three people would reply at all. I did laugh my ass off when a level 2 ogre, after learning we had problems in the guild, offered to take over leadership from me.

Our class balance was completely out of wack for months. Regardless of how this game actually works, common perception is that to do anything at all in the high level game, 1/6th of all characters must be enchanters, another 1/6th must be clerics. I read a post from a Verant spokesperson on this subject, where he claimed that a group of 6 hybrids could group effectively. Regardless of if this is possible in the high level game, if everyone thinks it’s impossible, it is impossible. How many classes are there in EQ, 14? More then two out of 14 members in our guild was an enchanter or cleric, yet we never had enough. Did Verant intend this as well?

Some of our high level players have less then ideal computers and/or Internet connections. A few key classes link die in any number of high level encounters and the result is hours and hours of mind numbingly boring corpse recovery, where everyone HAS to sit at their computers able to do nothing at all. Does Verant factor in link death and long load times in their risk vs reward equation?

Why am I posting this? My guild still exists, yet I have given up all hope in it. And I hope that the rest of the guild members give up hope as well. I think ultimately that a guild size of 50-60 people doesn’t work in the high level game. At least that’s my perception of why I failed as guild leader, of why my guild failed as a whole. To do the high level encounters you need a good 50% more members then is necessary to win the raid. When Sally doesn’t make it because of any number of RL issues, or Gill LDs its no big deal, you can simply move on, with the amount of overkill needed to get the job done. You can deal with the missing or LD people later, without making everyone at the raid wait. I encourage all my ex guild mates to find a similarly sized guild, merge with them, and move on. A larger guild size will kill the sense of family we once tried for, but its an acceptable loss to solve the problems we faced. Sure there may be more interpersonal problems, but when one or two members leave because of it, the guild strength wont notice.

I also hope that someone in Verant manages to read this and that even the smallest of things is done to correct any of the issues brought up in this essay. Everyone knows that Verant wants their customers to keep paying for as long as possible, Verant is a business and they are out to make money. They keep us paying by challenging us with hard encounters and punishing us with hours of boredom if we fail, and fail we do. I could not conceive of playing EQ without a television or a good thick book in the same room. Death itself is meaningless since the introduction of the cleric epic, who cares about the four percent of xp you don’t get back from a 56th lvl rez? So consider this idea:

1)Reduce the mana cost for summoning corpses to 50. Allow corpses to be summoned through zones only if the caster and target are in a city.
2)Allow tanks to bind anywhere casters can.
3)Any level 51 or higher player should only receive 20% (or less depending on overall balance) instead of 96% for a level 56 rez. 49th and lower level resurrections would be scaled accordingly (but only to 51st and higher level targets)
4)Make it impossible to lose a level to death.
5)Reduce the rez side effects duration.

Under the current system, failure is punished by waiting for hours at your computer unable to do anything or even go afk in most corpse recovery situations.

Under my system, failure is penalized by forcing the player to re-earn lost xp. I usually have fun earning xp.

What is the difference to Verant’s ledger if the punishment for failing a high level raid is to sit in front of their computers for hours at a time bored to tears, or if they are sitting at their computer for an equal amount of hours having fun? Im sure there are overall balance problems with my idea, but something along this idea needs to be implemented.

If anyone currently designing competition to EQ reads this, please take these factors into consideration when designing your game, because I will pay you for it.

Tvar, level 59 burn out, Luclin Server.
