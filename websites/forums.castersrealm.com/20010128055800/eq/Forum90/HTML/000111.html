Before anyone reads this post, I want to stress three things… first this is a long post, second I and am just trying to gain more insight, and third it is not meant to be a flame. I am hoping to increase my knowledge base about rangers, so please save your “necros suck, rangers rule” posts. What I am asking for is for my thoughts to be challenged, or for ideas that I have mot considered to be offered.

The more I think about the ranger, the more I puzzle about what a Ranger’s benefits actually are. Now that bards have proven themselves to me (in my mind bard’s win for most underrated class), I can see the point of playing any other class out there. I questioned the need for wizards once upon a time as well, for the simple fact that they are not mana efficient. For the same mana a wizard is expending nuking things into oblivion, a cleric could keep two or three tanks healed, and in the long run massively out damaging a wizard. But the wizard’s advantage is speed. There is simply no other class that can inflict more damage in a brief period then a wizard. So the value of a wizard is established. A shaman was also a question mark in my mind. I thought they were a druid, racially handicapped with an incredibly low starting wisdom. In fact that is nothing like the truth. With their DOTs, fear, mana for health, fine array of buffs and melee skills, they become group friendly mini-necros. So bards, wizzies and shammies are in, but there is still this ranger question.

My very first character was a ranger, as I wanted to roleplay a ranger in the “Lord of the Rings” style. I had also thought of getting a bow, shooting something, run, shoot it again, run and repeat until dead (longbow kiting?). This proved to be impossible given the game parameters. Archery does not inflict enough damage. Try to imagine taking down a Kunark creature with longbow kiting. First off it would take you an hour to do (that is if you did not agro everything else in the zone). And arrows are expensive, so that theory was screwed. I found it very difficult to gain a consistent flow of experience from anyplace I tried to hunt. Then I tried a necro and never looked back.

Which brings me back to my original question, what benefits does a ranger have? Of the melee classes, they deal a lesser amount of damage. Of all of the melee classes, they have the second lowest AC (but monks can deal massive amounts of damage, so they are excused). Druids healing is pale in comparison to clerics, and rangers are a 9 level step down even from that. Same goes for the Ranger’s offensive array of spells. Rangers get SOW at a much higher level. But at this level, what is SOW for? It is used for solo blast kitting (not a strong suit of a ranger) and for pulling (ranger’s cannot feign pull or fully tank at this level, so they are poor pullers). The real group benefit I can see from a ranger is through heavy use of the snare spell, to help avoid a nasty train situation. Also, the combination of dual wield, archery and offensive spells might allow them to act as a peeler, by quickly getting mobs off the casters and healers.

I think ranger’s only unique class trait is there critical hit with archery, which is something I question the usefulness of. The ability to track at low levels is also questionable. The true benefit of tracking is for getting quest mobs (I imagine that tracking the Ancient Cyclops in South Ro is gravy). However, quests are really not to worthy until level 24 or so, as the lesser quest items are well… lesser quest items. The only decent low level quests which come to mind that might need tracking are the Bloodforge Hammer and Trueshot Longbow quests.

I am asking for a bit of enlightenment. I can understand a person wanting to role-play a ranger (all true role-players should be praised). But, on a purely analytical basis, why should a person choose to play a ranger? Are there enough benefits of the class to outweigh the hefty XP penalty? What roles do rangers fill that other characters cannot fill more efficiently (either solo or grouping)?

Ancagalon

Here is a brief list of ranger strengths and weaknesses, I'll let you make up your own mind.

Strengths

Damage: Rangers are the 3rd best melee damage dealers in the game. With the exception of monks, rogues, and warriors, no other melee classes can come close to a ranger in damage. (and with spells we are slightly ahead of warriors) We also out damage ALL the other melee classes when doing damage from a range. This means that if we are forced to back off from combat for whatever reason, we can still contribute.

Taunting: Very few classes can taunt as well as a ranger can. My motto when playing my ranger is that I can taunt anything off of anyone, and believe me, those wizzies and enchanters really appreciate it.

Snare: Saves mana for the druids. Always nice.

Other spells: While many of them are overridden by the druid line (and the new velious spells suck), there is always something that we can contribute. Be it capping off higher level heals, rooting for crowd control, or throwing on a damage shield or buff.

Pulling: we are also one of the best pullers in the game. Using our harmony/root and zone technique, we can pull almost as well as a character with feign death. Even if the mob's are clumped.

Track: Rangers can track far better than any other class in the game. This is invaluable, especially in outdoor zones looking for that random location spawn. Or in dungeons so you can tell when so-and-so pops and adjust your actions accordingly.

Weaknesses

Low ac and hp: A tanking ranger is a dead ranger, especially at higher lvls. We did get a nice bonus to our parry and dodge caps which makes us much more survivable, but we still just don't tank well.

High aggro: Thanks to our high damage output and annoying spells like snare, we are never far from the top of the aggro list, and taunting off us can be a problem. This, combined with our low ac and hp, can give clerics a real headache keeping us in the game. We can back off from direct combat, however, and still play a role with our strong distance damage. High aggro can also be a good thing as well.(see above)

Spells: as previously stated, many of our spells are overridden by druids.

Stigma: There is a negative stigma attached to the name "ranger". Whether it is true or not, it is a disadvantage when trying to get groups.

I will let you make your own decision, but I firmly believe that a well played ranger can make an invaluable addition to any group, and that our strengths far outweigh our weaknesses. I hope that you will come to the same conclusion.

The point to having a ranger around? Hmmm...

Well, the first point is that rangers are not a solo class. Not past about 20th. So the question is, what do we contribute in a group?

Rangers are good pullers, for a couple reasons -- we get harmony, which lets us break up clumped spawns. Any ranger who knows his class should almost never come back with an uncontrollable pull, which is more than I can say for most warriors/pallys/SK's. By pulling with either arrows or damage spells we can get the mob back to the group without taking much, if any, damage. Even at low levels, with no SoW, a ranger can get pull a mob and get back to the group at full health by pulling with a snare. Our DD's aren't much, but they're enough to soften the critter up some, and make sure it doesn't get lost on a long pull.

If a pull does get out of hand, or some wandering monster decides to pile in on the fight, we can root-park it -- other classes can do this too, but it saves mana for the casters and lets them concentrate on the fight at hand.

We save healers mana -- by healing up after a fight, that's one or two heals less that the cleric has to use.

We can pull mobs off casters like nothing else in the game. As a ranger, I can control where the monster turns his attention -- pull it off the casters, and as necessary root it, back out and let a real tank take the abuse for a while. And even once I do that, I can still contribute some decent damage by shooting and casting. (The thing I miss most when I go play my monk is that kind of control -- the only think she can do is beat on the mob until it turns on her, and that's not always fast enough to save someone.)

We can serve as a backup tank -- I may not tank as well as a warrior, but taunting the mob onto me for a few rounds may well save his life. I can, in a pinch, even serve as primary tank, with a good cleric and preferably a shaman as well standing behind me (the difference the shaman slow spells make in the damage done to tanks is amazing, but that's a whole different topic).

We're better at dealing out damage than taking it, really -- monks and rogues do more damage, but I can easily outdamage a pally or SK of my level, and maybe a warrior as well. We get a lot of very fast weapons which can really pile on the damage.

With a good ranger in the group, you should never see a mob run. They'll try, but by that time they should be snared and not going anywhere. Alternatively, if things go really, really bad, a snare or a root will give the group that much more time to run.

And the last thing...track. If you want to find a rare spawn, get yourself a ranger. Track isn't perfect, especially in crowded areas, and it occasionally gets really confused when you're trying to find other players, but it's a whole lot better than wandering around for a couple hours trying to spot that named whatever. This also goes back to the pulling thing -- I can go out and pick and choose from the available mobs a lot easier than anyone else.

I guess the point of all this is that rangers do a lot of things that other classes do better. I often see my role in a group as taking some of the load off the other players -- healing after the fight to save the cleric's mana, snaring so the druid doesn't have to worry about it, sharing out the damage so the tank doesn't die. I also play my ranger differently depending on what the group looks like and what it needs most at that moment.

I have a lot of fun playing my ranger -- I get to run around pulling, I'm right in the middle of every fight, I scout new places and find new and interesting monsters...and I love all of it!

Just an example where a ranger can be useful:

Let's say that as a necro you are soloing in Skyfire Mountains. Your poor pathetic ranger friend shows up and you decide to show pity on him and let him group with you. You sit and med while the ranger goes and pulls a mob. You fear it, out your pet on it... the same stuff you always have done, only this time you have a ranger there too.

Now, sooner or later, you pull a summoning mob. This mob summons you, the necro, and you can't get a spell off. The ranger peels it off you and saves your hide. The ranger takes some damage sure, but you probably would have ended up dead. No one can out taunt a ranger if he/she wants the mobs attention, and the ranger simply cast a few heals when it was over and went out after the next mob.

You continue on, noticing that since the ranger has track, you can be selective about what you hunt and still have mobs as often as you want them, which is pretty often.

During one fight, a wanderer comes by and suddenly you have 2 mobs. The ranger roots one while you fight the other. What should have been hard was no big deal.

=========

Rangers can be useful to have around in many ways. There is not set rules that a ranger has, things to do that are always right. Everything is situational. What works grouped with certain classes fighting certain mobs isn't right for other times and places. There are times when a ranger may not seem to add anything to a group, but one thing that is always there is more options if things go terribly wrong, whether those options are being used at the moment or not.

Don't underestimate a ranger, or any hybrid for that matter. If they know what they are doing, they can save the entire group when things don't go by the script. Where we shine is doing things no "one" else can do.

Perhaps the single biggest detriment to rangers and hybrids in general is everyones "knowledge" of how things should be done. Everyone knows what classes are needed and what roles are to be filled. That has pigeon-holed hybrids to some extent into being "backup" this or that.

Where I have most fun as a ranger are in groups that would typically be considered unbalanced. In those groups, there are no rules and you get to have fun with the abilities you have. As a matter of fact, I once was the healer of a group of 3 shadow knights and a necro in lower guk. Now THAT was exciting.
