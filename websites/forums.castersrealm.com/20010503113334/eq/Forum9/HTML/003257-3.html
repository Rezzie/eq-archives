My own views on this as a 59 enchanter:

The resists matter. Every 3 seconds I have to spend mez'ing one mob is another 200-300 points of damage taken by the group... per mob. Often, I will be casting while getting beat on. It is vital to get the spell off. And on hard to mez mobs (or when I'm spending mana on something like glamour of kintaz), resists are not an option. Additionally, I don't bother to tash. I'll trade the hp's for needing less time/taunt.

When I'm controlling a train in hate, I can't afford resists. The whole raid's survival hinges upon me at that particular moment... resists just are not an option. Can't have a tank dieing and losing all his buffs. Can't have a cleric dieing. And can't have me dieing/going OOM. I need mez to stick, first time, without having to tash first. That first shock as a train hits... that time it takes to bring it under control, that's when you'll lose the most people. Anything that reduces that is good.

On AC:
It's worthless. 600, 700, it doesn't matter. We're hopelessly outclassed in this area against the mobs we face. Sure, in the early 40's, it's still doable. But in the high 40's and 50's, it just doesn't matter anymore. Just like you reach a level at which your melee doesn't matter anymore, there's a level where your AC doesn't make a dif except against greens. For this reason, I place AC at the bottom of my priorities, behind hp and resists (200 int/cha are so easy to get, I don't really make them a priority, they just happen).

Now, we're enchanters and we all do whatever we think will enable us to survive... my passion is hp and maintaning an agil that reaches 200 shaman buffed... and it probably isn't much more effective than AC. The only real advantage is you can say "I lived with 30 hp left, my ring saved my butt!" and that's harder to do for AC We pretty much all pick a few things that we focus on besides int/cha.

And we might as well face it, the only thing that really, really matters to our survival is the speed with which we get healed and our level.

To answer the above posters question. There
are two components to getting hit and your
AC. One is damage avoidance, in other
words getting a miss/dodge/riposte and the
mob not hitting you at all for that attack.
This is what agility helps improve. The
other factor is damage mitigation, A mob_00
can hit for a max damage of 130 but if your
AC is high the mob_00 will only be hitting
you for 60-80 a pop instead of 130 every hit.
If you compare monks versus warriors for
example, monks tend to avoid getting hit but
when they do get hit it is for high damage
while plate wearing warriors tend to get
hit more often but for less damage.

However, the interesting thing that came to light out of some related tests is the idea that maybe strength makes up for having a skill that is too low. Now, if it lets you come up to your maxed skill, then it wouldn't be too useful once you max your skills. If it is a simple level based system (such as calculations are made and then capped at some number), then strength could potentially benefit people that have low skill caps (like casters or paladins/sk's) more. But none of this stuff has been tested.

Other things that have not been tested:
2 parts of offense + level vs 2 parts of defense + level...

From my experience, the conflict between these two leads to reaching a breakpoint of sorts, similiar to 100 dex, 125 dex, etc. Get a certain amount of AC and suddenly tanking that mob becomes much easier. It's also been my experience that stat debuffs are most effective when they take a person who is barely below the breakpoint and alter the equation just enough to put them on the other side. Hence, why at the low levels, as an enchanter, stat debuffs worked very well for me, but as I levelled, they became less and less effective, especially as tanks became more and more heavily armored. I fell behind the curve too much for the stat debuffs to help me... the tanks either ignored AC and were too far behind or were far enough ahead that it didn't matter.

Anyway, what has never been tested is the implications of being below, significantly below the equation on the AC side. Does the avoidance part of AC help more then? Or the absorbtion? We don't know. Does agil work in a fashion similiar to strength to help make up for low defense skill? Again, we don't know.

All these things haven't been tested. Some of them are very hard to test.

So, yes, until these specifics are tested, we can't go by more than gut feelings. Until we take an int caster with high agil and compare him to a high AC one and compare him to a high hp one and chuck in a balanced one into the mix as well, we don't know. Until we throw those guys up against reds, against low blues and against whites, we don't know.

All we have are tests on other things that people just have a "feeling" applies to these things as well.

In short, don't say "this is the way it is" without having tested the specifics as they apply to these cases. You'll only end up misleading others.
