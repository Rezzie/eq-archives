I have no problem with a master looter, as long as the person is honorable with a good reputation, or is a member of a guild with a good reputation. The master looter is frequently a warrior who can handle the weight of the coin.

Usually, a master looter of items is in a dungeon where merchants are close, so that they can dispose of items easily and quickly.

If someone has to leave, the master looter sells and splits the money before the person disbands and a new person joins. This continues throughout the ebb and flow of a group.

It does seem a little unusual to have only 12P total be all that was looted in 25 kills, but I've been in situations where I've only looted silver and copper from level 30+ Kunark MOBs, so it is conceiveable.

But you are surprised that you were disbanded after deliberately breaking the group's looting rules? You violated an agreement you made. If you thought you were being cheated, why didn't you disband yourself from the group rather than trying to make your own rules without anyone else's consent?

I've found FFA is a touchy subject, especially in pick up groups. There always seems to be some selfish ***** that thinks "one for you (player1), one for me, one for you (player2), one for me,..", etc. is perfectly fine for FFA. That or they loot everything, even during a multi-mob fight, with no concern for others. (Nothing will get your ass booted out of my group faster than looting during a fight!)

Alpha is more tedious to keep the list in order when you have fast & furious pulls all the time, but works fine for something like treants or gnoll spires in SK.

If you're hunting as a group and can bank in the same place, then the best alternative is to have a charisma character sell off all items (FS, rawhide, etc) and give the money to the designated looter - who starts off with no coin at the beginning. This player then goes to the bank and changes it all to coppers - then splits among the players. The players can then bank their share to pp, gp, etc. This seems tedious, but nobody is short-changed in the long run.
