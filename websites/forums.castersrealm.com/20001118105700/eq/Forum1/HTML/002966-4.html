This would appear to be a classic dichotomy: Twinkers can easily justify their twinking to themselves (and to other twinkers); Non-twinkers can easily explain why twinking is "wrong" to likeminded anti-twinks; but never the twain shall meet.

There is a fundamental philosophical chasm between the 2 camps that will probably never be bridged: Let us make one of those classic sweeping generalisations, shall we, (The sort that leaves one open for a broadside of flaming) and divide the whole EQ community into 2 camps:

The "TWINKIES"
Percieve EQ as a race with level 60 as the finish line, or a competition to see who has the biggest/baddest/best gear and who can kill the biggest/baddest/best mobs. Twinking is both justifiable and desirable. Other twinkies striving to "win" the same "race" can appreciate your twinking, because they are trying to do the same thing, and sure, grouping with other players will help you win the race if they are "assets" (aka twinked)

The "RPGers"
"The journey is more important than the destination". "Its not what you do, its the way that you do it". To a RPGer you must EARN that Rawhide fur-lined codpiece AC 1 casts "Itchy-crotch" DoT on wearer saves +1 versus numbnuts. Playing a character of low intelligence means that you must do STUPID things - even to the detrement of your level/experience (especially to the detrement...etc). Knowing how to be the perfect TANK is of no use if your character is fundamentally stupid - the character has to do dumb stuff, otherwise you aint roleplaying. Seeing a twink is like finding a dead rat in the lunchbox - It does not fit in. To a RPGer a level 10 player in full UBERtwink gear is like a slap in the face, because it "cannot be so" (like turning up to your 8 year old daughter's school sports day and finding out that Marion Jones is entered in every event), it just couldn't shouldn't happen and therefore detracts from the all important "experience".

What I am trying to say is that the two philosophies are mutually incompatible. "I am right in my world and you are right in yours" We could keep posting on the topic ad infinitum (rather than ad nauseum; which has already been achieved) without achieving a consensus.

Yes, every time someone is against something, they're jealous because they can't do it.

I would like to beg to differ. Just because someone is against something doesn't mean they are jealous that they can't do it. I am totally against Farming not because I can't do it (my main character can do it if he wanted) but because I am tired of going into dungeons and having a 55+ person farming all the good stuff while my group could be getting good exp and loot from these mobs. That doesn't include Jealousy and I don't like it.

I used to be against Twinking because before I got my main character up to the point of being a almost high level I didn't understand that these were just items that were being cycled down to their lower level characters (or at least thats how I twink, find a cool item that I don't need for my main or don't want to sell, give to one of my other characters to hold), I wasn't jealous that they could twink and I couldn't, I could give a crap less if I could twink, I just didn't think it was right for a 2nd level warrior to be outfitted in all crafted at the time but now I know better.
