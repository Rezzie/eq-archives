Brem- yes, having a faster offhand weapon does mean more frequent hit. But if you are interested in doing the most damage with the offhand over time, you should still pick the better ratio weapon, regardless of the delay rating alone. Ratio of damage to delay by definition accounts for delay also. Remember that delay does not affect the proportion of swings that will be hits, that is the same regardless of weapon speed. The only reason faster delay is better in the primary hand is because of the damage bonus, otherwise it would be solely a matter of ratio for that weapon too (with regards to damage output, not taunting).

From all my experiences, I believe that the primary weapon's delay must be equal to or slower than the offhand's in order to take advantage of ALL (key word here) possible dual wielding procs. I have talked to several people about this and it seems that all attack is based of primary weapon delay. So lets say you have wurmslayer (25/40) and a yak (8/24). In this case every 40dly units, you will get an attack round. During this attack round, the battle program rolls to see if you get a dual wield, and a double attack for each hands weapon. So in this case, if the dual wield procs both weapons swing. In 24dly units, the yak is ready for a new combat round, and in 40dly units the wurm is ready for a new combat round. Since the yak is lower delay and ready, it will always be able to swing during a consecutive dual wield proc. In the opposite case, lets say a yak (8/24) and a bbk (10/35), if the dual wield procs 2 rounds in a row, the first dual wield will swing both weapons, but the next round will not because the bbk was not ready when the dual wield proc'd.
