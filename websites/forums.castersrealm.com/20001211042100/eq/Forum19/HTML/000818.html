Pretend you are level 39 and just got a new spell. For arguments sake I will say that for each 10 damage you have a 10% chance of having it interrupt you. This would be around 80% chance to get interrupted if you get hit for 80-89 damage. This sounds right in actuality if you go get a level 39 spell and try to cast it while being beaten on. If the mob doubles you have almost no chance at all to cast through the hits because of the high amount of damage taken.

Now lets say you are lvl 39 and you want to cast a spell like mez. This spell is level 4. The level difference between youre current level (39) and its level (4) is 35 total. For arguments sake we will assume that for each level you are higher than the spell it subtracts 1%. In this case if you got hit for 80 damage and you were 35 levels higher than the spell you would have 55% chance to be interrupted or 45% chance to channel.

This is all speculative but thats basically how it works.

For all you idiot flamers, no I did not test this. My info is purely experience and information read by credible sources.

Could folks please enlighten me what this "bonus" is?

A simple search of the news page would've yielded you the answer.

Anyway, here it is -- note the underlined portion:

GZ ON CASTING RECOVERY, JANUARY 4, 2000

Here it is as much information as I have been able to acquire in relation to spell recovery. All information has been confirmed by GZ.

  • Your chance to recover casting is calculated solely on your Channelling Skill.
  • Dexterity only affects Channelling in relation to raising your skill in it. How quickly your raise your Channelling ability is determined by a combination of Dexterity and Intelligence.
  • Dexterity does not affect your chance to actually recover casting.
  • Agility doesn't affect Channelling, or casting recovery in any way whatsoever.
  • If you cast a spell which you are 5 or more levels higher than when you obtained it (eg: root is a level 4 spell, so if you are level 9 or higher) you get a Channelling boost, ie: a higher chance to recover casting if interrupted.
  • Player controlled movement will, in most circumstances, break a spell with no chance to recover.
  • Movement caused by a monster hitting you while you are casting a spell gets a chance to recover the casting.
  • Your skill level in a specific type of spell (eg: Evocation) doesn't affect your chance of recovery. However, this will affect your chance to fizzle the spell.
  • Any Specialization skill you have in the type of spell being cast will not affect recovery, however it will have a chance to reduce mana cost, and lower the fizzle rate.

"Player controlled movement will, in most circumstances, break a spell with no chance to recover."

One thing I've noticed about this is that it doesn't seem to care how much you've moved, but your position when you finish casting relative to your position when you started casting. I've started casting a long cast time spell, run fowards a long way, run back a long way and stopped almost exactly (as close as I can get) where I started casting. Doing this I can channel a spell through quite often. Much more often than just running in one direction (almost never channel it if I go far). Not sure if there's any pratical application of this information, but it's interesting to know.
