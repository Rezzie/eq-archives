Your EQ experience and tactics are going to vary depending on your group composition.

The enchanter that always has gate loaded and is willing to use it when things look bad may be the one that levels the fastest in pickup groups full of others who will likewise gate out. Other pick-up group oriented people will not blame them for their acts of self preservation.

The enchanter that does Crowd Control until death will probably make the impossible look easy with a well organized and courageous group of close friends or guildmates.

Take either type of enchanter and stick them into the opposed situation and they will do poorly. The gate-happy enchanter will piss off a group of friends who play together regularily and they will shun him. The CC-till-death enchanter will have a very warped perception of "when to run" and will die often and hard in pickup groups.

I am lucky enough to have grouped with the same bunch of RL friends since level 1. I also have a wonderful guild to draw upon. I do not play in pick-up groups often because of this. The result is that I don't think I've ever had gate loaded if it precluded the use of another, more useful, spell. I might have stopped loading it at level 12, but I honestly forget. It might have been earlier.

I have been most fortunate, and I recognize that the enchanters out there who do keep gate memmed probably don't have the same caliber of players to group with. My only suggestion is that they try to piece a regular group together. It is highly rewarding and well worth the effort. I used to play a wizard in pick-up groups, and having switched to guild/friend groups, I can honestly say that there is just no going back.

Stuns do two things beyond letting you escape beats:

1) Prevent damage to others
2) Interrupt casters

1) Number 1 saves your cleric mana, which means your group can do more. The cleric and enchanter are usually the two mana bottlenecks of the group. One prevents damage, and one reverses it. When you have more mana than your cleric, you want to spend mana to help him save mana because if he goes OOM, the group could be in serious trouble. Stuns are a great way of doing this. Turn "others hits" on in your log filter for a bit so you can see the difference a bit of chain-stunnage makes on even a slowed mob. Then go examine the healing ratios of your cleric's spells and work out the mana usage. Heals are *not* cheap. If you're wasting mana on nukes and coming out of a battle with 80m while your cleric is OOM, then your group is going to be sitting on their arses more then necessary.

2) Casters can be troublesome. The ones that heal themselves are bad, and the ones that gate are worse yet. Even with two monks (with Tranquil Staffs) and a warrior pounding on that Sarnak Aruspex, it still has a good chance of getting a gate off. Hell, we've had it happen with an enchanter *and* a cleric chain-stunning. (a bad string of resists can happen) If you have only one stun loaded in such a situation, you are going to have gaters. That means lots of time wasted camping out, or worse yet, trains and evacs. A train of level 50+ sarnaks is not fun to be in. One stun and gate are not going to get you out, let alone your group.


I'm not saying you need multiple stuns up in all situations. I have very different spell load-outs for different dungeons, different parts of dungeons, and hell, even for different kinds of pulls. All I'm saying is that none of my load-outs has room for gate.

Your math's a little whacked there. You forgot casting time. (you start casting *after* the hold time is up) Add 2 seconds onto the 4 seconds, and you have a bare minimum of 6 seconds that the critter isn't stunned. Of course, that's actually longer because of refresh time, lag, etc.

That means that with just color skew, assuming zero resists, you can keep a mob stunned a little over half the time. Half damage is great, but that mob is still doing plenty. I will concede that with just one stun you can help your cleric out, but throwing more stuns in will certainly help. 6+ seconds is more than enough for a mob to gate in or heal itself too.

In reality, resists happen. They happen *especially* at the wrong times. As I have said, even with a cleric and an enchanter both chaining 3 different stuns each on a sarnak aruspex in Chardok, the odd one will get lucky on resists and gate out. It is rare, but not nearly as common as it would be if you tried using fewer stuns. With Color Slant and Color Skew you can keep a mob stunned almost all the time if you get no resists. I still use the older 3 stuns for stopping gaters because I can get more stuns off and reduce the chance of a resist or two letting the mob gate.

3 slots devoted to stuns is a lot. If you devote them to stuns 100% of the time you're nuts, But don't be afraid to swap them in when the situation calls for it. They *are* useful.
