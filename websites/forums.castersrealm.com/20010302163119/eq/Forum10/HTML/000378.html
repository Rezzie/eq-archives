I originally was just about ready to quit my cleric, but I've been thinking about the changes from the last patch, and trying to figure out what was going through Verant's collective head. And I think I've figured it out. It's not all because Verant hates clerics, or anyone else. Call me crazy, but in fact it may be actually BETTER in the long run. Read on, I'm laying it out point by point.

1. Aggro was increased at the same time that the underused heal-over-time series was made more effective. Think about it...we get more aggro, but now clerics have a line of healing spells that generate less aggro...a line that the other WIS classes DON'T get. It's too good to be a coincidence.

2. Aggro was increased at the same time that shamans and druids were given their new healing spells. There's several sub-points on this...

- "You want a piece of the cleric's pie, then have a big scoop of aggro with it!" It's a subtle bit of revenge for them getting better healing. I bet someone at Verant plays a cleric.

- The aggro changes emphasize the defensive differences between a cleric and the other WIS classes. Clerics are much better at handling aggro than the other WIS classes, particularly druids. Druid gets aggroed and dies, cleric gets aggroed and res's the druid. This also has the side effect of enouraging druids to solo more instead of competing for healer slots in groups.

- With the increased aggro, a backup healer (meaning a shaman or druid) is much more desirable to have in a group. This mitigates the problem where druids and shamans were feeling left out of groups at high levels.

3. Necros (another left-out-at-high-level class) also get a piece of the grouping pie, incidentally, since their abilities (which include healing and crowd control) are now more desirable for group backup.

4. Aggro was increased at the same time that the tank classes got a significant boost, in the form of increased skill caps and new spells. I think the idea here was to balance out the increased ability of the tanks with increased danger to the rest of the group. I'm not sure on how balanced this point is yet, but we'll see.

5. Finally, EQ was getting stagnant. The same class roles, the same tactics, the same old grind, every day and every group. Now, things are a bit different. Groups will function differently due to increased danger to ALL members. People will be forced to think, to come up with new tactics, to dig into their spellbooks, to find new roles for not just a few but for all the classes.

What do you all think?

Well speaking from someone who has played almost 2 years now with a very high aggro very high taunt class, a Ranger, and who has only recently picked up the role of cleric here is my take on the situation.

I don't pretend to guess why they made the changes, though given that I have a low opinion of Verant my guesses would not be positive ones, however the effects can be summarized in several categories.

In the regular every day groups this change is not especially bad. Before lv. 34 to 39 it really is not anymore noticable than it ever has been anyway. I.e. a non-issue. After this when you start using Superior Healing and Complete healing it can be a problem. Even then, it does not seem to be too much at this stage in your life.

Now get yourself up to lv 50 to 55 and things get really screwy with the hate list and aggro. Suddenly Mobs have several multiples of thousands of HP and it now takes quite a long time to bring things down. It also means that more heals are needed. This is when the aggro from the heals starts to add up a lot and you get what we are seeing now. Also, due to the way the taunt skill works (which is 3 ways of screwed up to begin with) it is much easier for some actions to place you at the top oif the Hate list.

But this is not impossible to work around. As a Ranger, I had to learn how to control my aggro and rate of taunt. Sometimes I had to keep myself at the bottom of the Hate list. Other times I had to know how to crank it up and get myself all the way at the top in a real hurry. I became very good at both. Clerics are now in a similar position I believe. MUCH experimentation wil be needed to find out exactly how to control this and still perform your job function of keeping everyone alive. I believe it took us about 3 months of constant experimentaion with spells equipment and so on to figure out what worked and what didn't.

I think one of the best tools in managing your aggro is the Celestial Healing spell as a lot of clerics have already discovered. But keep in mind this is only one tool. A single tool is never enough to cover all situations so other methods need to be researched and found and practiced.

So, basically for the exp grinding groups this wont be a crippling change. Annoying yes. And ot a change that I think is good for the game but it is not the end of the world either.

Now on the BIG raid encounters, however, this change was indeed crippling. Minimum level for most of the super high end encounters is about lv 55. Any lower than that and your chances of survival are pretty much nill. Even at 55 they are not that good but at least it is doable. Now lets take a decently equiped lv 55 warrior. Lets disregard AC because in these encounters AC means next to nothing anyway. You could have a 3000 AC and still get smacked around the same as the Rogue in chain armor next to you. Instead lets focus on HP. This same warrior fully buffed out the ears probably has about 4200 HP. Now lets take a look at some of the bigger nasties and how much they hit for.

Take Velkator for instance. Hits for 850's multiple times in a round. Exactly how long is that 4200 hp going to last under that kind of punishment? About 3 rounds at the most. To put that in terms of total time I will estimate that equates to about 6 to 8 seconds. Roughly. This means that whoever is assigned to healing this person is going to be casting lots of heals.

Now lets look at how much HP Velk has. Over 100K. How long does it take a raid force to eat through all that HP? A LONG time. As a Ranger if I can cast my spells and get them to land for damage, and I use my dmg shields etc. (basically all my tools) I can do about 7 to 9 K damage per minute. In an average encounter I will usually do about 11 % more damage per minute than a warrior and as long as I have a Clarity I can keep that damage output sustained indefinatly. I fall about 15 to 20 % behind rogues and Monks in sustained damage output. Now there is no way I am ever gonna land a spell on Velk so that cust my damage down to about 3 to 4 K per minute if I am really lucky. Chances are it will be less than that because my hit % will not be very good against him either. So for arguements sake lets say that a Ranger will do about 2-3 K per minute on Velk, Warriors will do approx the same, Monks will do about 4 K per min, and Rogues about 4.5 to 5K per min.

Lets say you have about 18 such tanks on your raid (when you add in the other classes your total raid force is probably gonna be around 36 people. Anymore than that and your are screwed by mass LD). Lets factor in Paladin's and Shadowknight Damage here too, though their damage output is minimal. They can take the hits but they sure have problems dishing it out. (I really feel sorry for these classes on the Big Raids). They will do about 1 K per min or so. Add in say 4 of these. Now add up all your combined dmg per minute: you now have 61.5 K per minute. Roughly.

Velk has over 100 K as I said. So this means you need to fight him for roughly two minutes assuming you can keep up your rate of damage and you don't go massively LD from that many people in one place at one time. Now, when you tanks are getting pounded and losing around 1200 hp a second how the heck are you going to keep them healed even with Complete Heal? Well usually you take one bigg bad warrior buffed to hell and have him solo the mob to start the fight. As soon as he starts the fight about 5 clerics all start casting CH on him in waves. I.e. the first one starts casting, 2 seconds later the next one starts casting, 2 secinds later... etc. etc. Once the warrior has enough aggro on him alone the rest of the force jumps in and the clerics spend all their time chain casting CH on the one warrior. This was about the only way some of these bigger mobs could be taken down.

NOW, with these new changes those same CH's will have the result of a field of slaughtered clerics and then the rest of the raid force will soon follow. Superior Heals wont cut it for this encounter. He hits for more HP than the Heal will replace pere hit. Celestial Healing and the upgrade are out because they don't heal enough fast enough.

So exactly what do you do?

This is what some people are asking themselves and they are not coming up with any answers that work yet. In fact, Verant has also stated that there are some mobs that are physically impossible to kill now because of the recent changes.

My final opinion? Everyday play has not been drastically affected. It might be a tad bit too much aggro and might need to be scaled down just a little bit but generally this is not the apocolypse. For the high end raids things have now become impossible though, and this is specifically where things need to be addressed, and completely redone.

Just the ramblings of a 50 something Ranger, who sometimes happens to be a Cleric too.
