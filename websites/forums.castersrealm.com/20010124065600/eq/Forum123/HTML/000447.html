For damage output, up until the 30's or so they are about equal, although 2 handers probably have an edge. At this point, the damage bonus for 1 handers starts becoming significant. Even though it is smaller than the 2 hander damage bonus, it is applied more often netting slightly higher damage. Many also claim that it is much easier to taunt while dual wielding because the frequency of hits is more important than the overall damage. I believe this is true to some extent, although under level 50, taunting using taunt skill makes up for this difference.

Verant seems to have realized that 2 handers had become underpowered compared to fast delay 1 handers and released some extremely impressive 2 handers with Velious. I believe this pushes 2 handers slightly ahead of dual wield for damage output again.

In the end, both are important, so keep all the necessary skills maxed. In different situations, each has its advantages:
1) dual wield- slightly better taunt factor is good when you are in a caster heavy group and you are the primary tank
2) 2 handers are the best option in a tank heavy group when damage output is your goal (this, of course, is only true at certain levels, or when wielding some of the new awesome ratio weapons).

I try to keep both 1HS and 2HS maxed. They're good for different situations.

1HS is great for taunting and fighting casters and general havoc-wreaking, when you want to see your screen scrolling with hits.

2HS is good for when you don't necessarily want to be taunting as much (eg in mutli-tank groups where you aren't primary tank, or when your HP is low and the other tank is trying to taunt off you) or when you're faced with a damage shield. Damage shields SUCK when you're throwing 4 attacks a round...carrying egg-shaped pumice helps with this, but so does using 2HS...you aren't hitting as often, so you take less damage from the shield, and with reasonable weapons you should be doing comparable damage. It's also nice for when you're just in the mood to see huge criticals

AAAAHHHHH, THIS IS NOT TRUE!!

Dual wield is no longer better than 2handers for damage output with the new Velious weapons!!!!! I have said this several times, but now I will back it up with the numbers.

As an illustration, I will compare dual jade maces (9/18) to a tempered velium claidhmore (32/35), assuming a haste item of 21%, wielded by a lvl 50 warrior with maxed skills and a str of 200.

First:
%success of double attack= (lvl+DA skill)/500 = (50+205)/500= 51%

%success of dual wield= (lvl + DW skill)/600= (50+200)/600= 41%

DMG bonus for 2handers = (lvl-25)/2 = (50-25)/2= 12

DMG Bonus for 1 handers= (lvl-25)/3= (50-25)/3= 8

According to Ruatha:

Min hit = (weapon rating which depends on dly of weapon + lvl bonus

for jade mace the weapon rating is 1, for the temp vel clai it is 2 (actually it may be 3, but I'll use 2 so as not to maximize my side of the argument)

Magic number = (2*wpn dmg)+ dmg bonus

Max hit = (str+skill)*wpn dmg + dmg bonus

According to a Ruatha post even prior to EQ Geekery (and by just looking at the data in EQ Geekery): About 10% of hits are for the max damage. About 20% of hits are for the magic number, and about 15% are for the min damage. The remaining 55% fall between the min and magic number and to a lesser degree between the magic number and the max damage. This can be compensated for and averaged into 1 fairly accurate number by the averages as follows:

min hits 35%
magic number 55%
max hits 10%

Thus, AVERAGE damage per HIT= (min*.35)+(magic number*.55)+(max * .1)

Now lets look at the average damage for a round of attack with each weapon by the character described above:

NOTE: look above to remind you of teh formulas if needed

***Tempered Velium Claidhmore***:

Min= (2+bonus12)= 14

Magic number= (2*dmg32)+bonus12= 76

Max Damage= (str200+skill200)/100 *dmg32 + bonus12= 140

Thus average damage per hit = (.35*14)+(.55*76)+(.1*140)= 60.7

Each attack there is a 51% chance of double attack having the same avg damage, thus each round of attack the AVERAGE DMG= DMG + (.51*DMG)= 60.7+(.51*60.7)=
SO...Total AVERAGE DAMAGE with double attacks is 91.657 per round of attack

***Jade mace in primary hand***

Min= (1+bonus8)= 9

Magic number= (2*dmg9)+bonus8= 26

Max Damage= (str200+skill200)/100 *dmg9 + bonus8= 44

Thus average damage per hit = (.35*9)+(.55*26)+(.1*44)= 21.85

Each attack there is a 51% chance of double attack having the same avg damage, thus each round of attack the AVERAGE DMG= DMG + (.51*DMG)= 21.85+(.51*21.85)=
SO...Total AVERAGE DAMAGE with double attacks is 32.9935 per round of attack

***Jade mace in the offhand***

Note these are the same numbers minus the damage bonus as this is not applied to offhand hits

Min= (1)= 1

Magic number= (2*dmg9)= 18

Max Damage= (str200+skill200)/100 *dmg9 = 36

Thus average damage per hit = (.35*1)+(.55*18)+(.1*36)= 13.85

Each attack there is a 51% chance of double attack having the same avg damage, thus each round of attack the AVERAGE DMG= DMG + (.51*DMG)= 13.85+(.51*13.85)= 20.9135

Now we have to account for the fact that dual wield goes off only 41% of the times the primary hand swings so offhand AVERGAE DAMAGE per round = (.41 * 20.9135)= 8.57

SO TOTAL DAMAGE PER ROUND WHILE DUAL WIELDING JADE MACES TAKING INTO ACCOUNT DOUBLE SWINGS IS: Average primary hand damage 32.9935 + average offhand damage 8.57= 41.5635


To Wrap this headache up, the average damage per round of attack is:

Temp Vel Claid= 91.657

Jade Maces= 41.5635

Now the hasted delays are 27 and 14 respectively, meaning that in the time you can attack once with the Temp Vel Claid, you can attack 1.92 times with the jade maces so:

TVC= 91.657

dual JM= (41.5635 * 1.92)= 79.80192

In the same period of time, 2.7 seconds (dly/10) the TVC does 91.657 points of damage while dual wielding jade maces only do 79.80192!! This doesn't take into account crits and crips, which would widen the gap.
