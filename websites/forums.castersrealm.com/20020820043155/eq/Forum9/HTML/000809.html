I spent a couple of hours testing Entrance with varying amounts of charisma tonight. We all know that charisma affects the mez line of spells, but to what degree is still a mystery. Here were my results.

Test Subject:
Guard Jendl at the eastern N/W Freeport zone line. I chose him because I could repeat the tests on the same exact NPC and had a zone nearby. Also guards have high MR's and Guard Jendl seems to be a consistant level 40.

Me:
Level 41 Enchanter with charisma set to exactly 100, 150 and 200. My intelligence varied (more with 100 cha, less with 200), but most other stats were the same or very close.

The test:
Cast Entrance over and over on Guard Jendl without casting tashan or any other debuff on him. I did this for 3 full loads of mana at each charisma level resulting in around 50 casts of the spell on Jendl per test.

Assumptions:
-Casting entrance on an allready entranced NPC makes no difference.
-No other stats effect mez other than your level relative to the npc, the npc's mr and your charisma.

Results:
-Test 1 at 100 Charisma
27 resists
26 sticks
----------
51% resisted

-Test 2 at 150 Charisma
18 resists
28 sticks
----------
39% resisted

-Test 3 at 200 Charisma
17 resists
28 sticks
----------
37% resisted

Conclusions:
While it's obvious that charisma has a significant effect on your mez rate the ammount of charisma you NEED is still in question. Obviously if you can get 200 charisma 200 int you really don't have to worry about this. But for those of us who are still making decisions on how we gear up, these results might help you decide differently. I can easily get either 200int or 200cha at level 41, but I can't get both yet. I admit that the data set isn't huge, but its large enough to base some assumptions on. Additionally it's possible that a red or yellow conning creature would less easily resist me at 200cha than 150cha. If anyone is interested in doing more testing on higher level NPC's or other levels of cha I think we would all love to see the results. Do with this information as you wish, but I'm going to change to a 200int 175cha setup over a 165int 200cha set up for now. I'll see if this change has any effect on my regular dungeon diving in LGuk and Kedge.

Knowing how Verant like to do many of these things, I have always gone by the assumption that there is a certain threshold where the law of diminishing returns kicks in.

You will notice that with your test data there was indeed a difference in the number of resists going from 100 charisma to 150, but the difference from 150 to 200 charisma fit within the definition of statistical noise. In other words, it is not clear that that additional 50 points of Charisma did ANYTHING for you, whereas at lvl 31 50 more points of INT would have gained you over 300 additional mana, which is 6 casts of Enthrall, or 3 whole casts of Chaos Flux for 450 damage, or almost 8 casts of Color Shift. Those are real, tangible benefits.

When I fight nowadays (I am lvl 31) I generally have my int at around 192 (with an additional +100 mana). I can get my int up over 200, but I prefer to wear some mana items instead of int because they have other bonuses as well (I still wear my gold jaded bracers for example, instead of my PBBs, which I only wear while doing jewelry, and I wear a plat jaded ring instead of a plat star rose quartz ring for the same reasons). My charisma, wielding my Incandescent Rod and my Stein of Moggock, with Sympathetic Aura up, is around 126. I can swap in my Crude Stein and my Rod of Insidious Glamour for an additional 27 charisma, but to be honest I haven't noticed a need. Last night in Cazic I enthralled a LOT of lizards, and I never seemed to have a problem enthralling them, nor holding them enthralled. I like the occasional Color Shift proc from the Incandescent Wand, particularly if a Shaman will buff up my pitiful dexterity and increase the proc frequency.

My strategy is to try and Tashani them first if at all possible. This is often possible if, for example, two or three lizzies are fighting my group and I stand back a long ways off. The aggro factor from my Tashani with me standing 75 feet away will often not overcome the hatred for my groupmates that are standing right there with them, and so I can tashani and THEN enthrall. I almost never get resisted this way. Even without Tashani I usually succeed with enthrall. I don't know _exactly_ what my success rate is, but it is high enough that I have not been concerned with resists. And when I DO get resisted it is usually a simple matter to color shift the mob, then enthrall again. At higher levels this may get harder to do, but at least on most mobs I have fought recently it works quite well.

And when I enthrall without tashaniing first, I will often Tashani afterwards and then re-enthrall. I don't know if this makes a difference, but that's what I do.

When there is a need for crowd control I generally focus only on that (when we pull singletons I tend to nuke and stun a lot), and I will often remez with Enthrall every 20 seconds or so whether the mob twitches or not. I don't know if this helps, but I will tell you a mob will almost never EVER break my mez this way. Last night we had a few four and five mob pulls, and I held all but the currently fighting one mob mezzed for the whole battle, the last one having ended up mezzed for like four minutes or so. Sure, reapplying Enthrall several times uses up mana, but hey, that mana is worth it, and with 192 int plus and extra 100 mana, at lvl 31 I have around 1350 mana, and with Enthrall only costing 50 mana to cast, plus me having constant Clarity, I can Enthrall all day long. It is worth it for the damage I prevent.

So I guess you could say I take the brute force method of crowd control. I debuff the one stat that I KNOW effects mezzes, ie Magic Resistance, and then I use brute force to keep mezzing the mobs till it is their turn to die. I don't really care what my charisma is, since at 126 it seems to be high enough, and if I start getting more resists I can easily add more charisma.

Mana is king in my opinion. Once we get the spawn broken up we pull a lot of singletons and sometimes doubles, and then I just toss in one or two Chaos Fluxes and stuns. For that occasional double or triple pull my charisma seems to be high ENOUGH to keep things under control, and for the singletons I become a nuker and buffer. And for that you need mana. Besides, when I hit 34 and start using Radiant Visage (a copy of which I already possess) my cha with the same equipment as I now use, will go up to 135. In a tough crowd control situation I will swap in my crude stein (I do this sometimes) and my charisma will go up to 150, which is precisely what you show (not scientifically speaking) to be statistically as good as 200 cha.

The bottom line is, I have my doubts as to the effectiveness of each point of charisma above a certain level, and I don't think that level is as high as many people think. However I have perfect faith that each and every point of INT not only gives me more mana, but the effect is the same all the way past 200 int. Also, up to 200 int some calculations of chance will be increased in my favor, such as my chance of specialization kicking in.

Just a couple of comments -

1 - Verant seems to put absolute caps on many rolls (i.e. 5% chance to fail or succeed regardless of skill).

2 - Much like other stats, int has plateaus of usefullness before 200. If you can't reach 200 int, 175 int is nearly as good (however the difference between 165 int and 175 int is huge). I would encourage people to calculate their mana at different setups to make their own choice.

3 - The important difference between int and cha is this: Mana is like a battery, you have the energy and you spend it over time. For any particular battle that you are prepared for, an enchanter will most likely have more than enough mana to cast whatever spells are necessary to conquer. The primary usefullness of having a 'bigger battery' is that you are able to smooth out the downtime periods because you have a bigger reserve for crunch time. However, cha is a battle success stat. In other words, if you have 1 chance to charm or mes a light tombstone mob, you want to make sure you have the best chance possible. The number of times I've been beat down because of resists far outnumbers the number of times I've died because of being oom.

BTW - Its pretty easy for an enchanter to calculate mana: cancel clarity :-), load up all the mes spells you have (assuming you spec'd alteration) and cast the most expensive one can until you can't even cast lvl 4 mes any more (you can also cast tashan for 10 mana to really empty yourself). Check the scroll back and count casts, etc.
