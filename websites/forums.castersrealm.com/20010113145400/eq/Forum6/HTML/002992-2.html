Can I add something too?.. This is again directed to the kiting issue, which I think people like Krilen are so enthusiastic about proving their point, that they miss the following.

Kiting as we do it now:

1) /pet no taunt
2) Pull a mob with malosi/the biggest effective nuke you got (medium taunt)
3) stick pet, and let the pet hit/backstab whilst you run
4) as soon as he turns on your pet, nuke him again, and let the pet chase ya again, repeat till dead.

Kiting with the DoT's:

1) /pet no taunt
2) Pull with malosi/nuke/DoT, low/medium/high taunt respectively.
note: a) high taunt of the DoT = he's madder at you as when you should pull with a nuke, which means he'll chase you longer.
b) DoT is still doing dmg for the next 30 seconds which adds to aggro more every tick during those 30 seconds.
3) aggro being *much* bigger than usual (DD's) with the DoT on him, keeps him chasing you for much longer, which means you don't have to cast on him, during that time the pet also does more damage on him, so instead of the mob turning on the pet after he is down to lets say 3/4 life, he only turns on to the pet when he's 1/2 life.
4) You recast the dot when he's lets say at 1/2 life, then when he gets to around 1/4 life he auto-fears, and you can med for the last part of his life..
5) thats 2 DoT's casted on mob X to have him dead, compared to 3-4 DD's to have him dead.

Now i'm not saying it will be at a proportion of 2(dot)/1(DD), but the dot will be overall a better ratio than the DD, although individual casts of the DD/DoT, the DD may prove to be more efficient, overall efficiency will be better with the DoT's.
