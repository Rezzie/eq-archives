You have no idea how hard it is to find groups at later levels, or how much people want your mana, dead man floating, or for you to summon their corpse, yet want you last on the list for a group. I admit it is partialy because most people dont use necromancers to their full extent. I am the only pure caster that can heal, I am the only CLASS other than an enchanter, or a bard who can mez,and I do it much better than bards. I can dot, my pet is almost as good if not nearly equal to a mage, I have some useful buffs, and I can even toss in some direct damage (when the tanks dont yell at me for makeing the mobs skellies) I am a master of corpse recovery, and if I like you, in a few levels I could even give you a nice experience rez. Yet no one wants me, they would rather have a wizard, or a mage, another tank, or a shaman for buffs. I am also sad that most necro's seem to forget that we can mez or heal, or dont pay enough attention to their pets, upseting the poor enchanter. But all I have to say is that when I am in a group, I try very hard to be benificial. I will give you an example, I was in frenzied ghoul in Lguk... big nasty pull.. I directed my pet, gave the enchanter mana to mez when he ran out, healed the tank when the cleric was getting to many intterupts, mezzed a frog or two so the enchanter could get off his better mez, dot'd the monster that the tanks were on, and I have 50% of my mana left over when it was said and done. Then enchanter never could have used ae mez without the mana I gave him, the tank would have died if I hadnt healed him, and who knows what else would have happened without my extra damage from the dots. Why is it so hard for us to get into groups? With the way that they are changeing things, soloing isn't as great as it used to be, and if we are the master solo class, where does that leave us?

SOLOing begets SOLOing

I agree with the utility of the necromancer. Mine is lvl 54, and we are truly the only jack-of-all trades class. A necromancer in the early and mid-game, however, has a tendancy to solo. During this time other classes are working together in groups, getting to know other players, and forging alliances. When people get into the 50s and the game is MUCH harder, and mistakes are much more costly, people are invariably likely to look to their friends lists and to people they have grouped with for a long time before considering anyone else for a group. Those who predominantly solo their way up to level 50 are simply going to have difficulties finding groups, for while others have spent countless hours in lower guk together forging friendships, the one who solo'd often must start at square one and forge those friendships possibly for the first time.
