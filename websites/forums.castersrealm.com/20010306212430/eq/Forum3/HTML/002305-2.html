As I read through this discussion, I am stunned by the effectiveness of Absor's spin, and how little attention is truly being paid to the crux of this little situation.

To refer to this change as "dynamic content" is ludicrous. If you logged in one day to find North Qeynos destroyed by and overrun with Blackburrow Gnolls, then you would be able to classify THAT as dynamic content. EQ, as programmed, is unable to make such changes. Therefore, EQ is unable to provide dynamic content. Norrath was designed to be, and shall always be, a static world.

Referring to the change as an upgrade to mob AI is probably a bit closer to the mark. And some of Absor's later comments even hint that this was the reason for the change. And from a logical standpoint, I can even accept this point of view with some dispair.

But... this is NOT the FIRST reason that was given for the change.

Quoting the February 21 patch message: "We've redressed the amount of hate generated by heal spells. Previously, and *partly* due to a bug, the high level heal spells such as superior heal or complete heal would generate no more hate than greater heal."

The REAL, CONCRETE reason why this change was made was a BUG FIX.

EQ has been live for almost two years. If you count the beta cycles, it's been around for even longer. After all this time, after two years of live service, incorporating tens of millions of manhours of "release playtesting", they choose to fix this bug now?

Thy only question I'm left with is "Why"? When you add up the staggering amount of time that this game has been played, why make such a fundamental change now?

The only answer I can even remotely think of is "because they can".

To wit, my reply is simple: Just because you CAN do a thing doesn't necessarily mean that you SHOULD do a thing.

Absor has done a good job as Community Relations Manager, and as such, has soothed many wounds that were inflicted by his predecessor.

But changes like this show that the EQ Live team is STILL, after all this time and after stepping into so many quagmires, STILL infinitely more focused on the game than the people playing the game. I feel pretty safe in saying that no one ASKED for this change.
