The choice really depends on your style of play. But one major need for a Human Bard is a Greater Lightstone ... and unless you can have someone get you one before level 6, I would not play another one of these.

I have made 11 Moshkiae's and only one is a Human, and until recently I had a real hard time with it, until I could raise enough money to get a Greater Lightstone ... it is easier after that.

I like the Half Elf, because it has the best average of stats ... nothing really "down" in critical areas, or anything, thus making it easier to subsist and play. The Wood Elf, has excellent DEX and AGI, but it is seriouosly lacking in STR and STA ... and tires hopelessly in long fights, thus making it a perfect candidate for "magical bard", or one that yields more instruments than weapons ... but it also does better with Piercing weapons than any other ... first order of business is see if you can get a Dragoon Dirk, off some high level camping D'Vinn in Crushbone! You will use this weapon all the way into the 30's and then some ... that's how good it is.

If you take a Woodie, I would make sure that you even out the STR/STA stats. If you do a Halfie, I would add the AGI/DEX/CHA part of things. If a Human, most tend to take the STR/STA part of it, and make these good tanking Bards ... which is basically untrue, as this Bard also takes more punishment than the other two ... it just balances it out better with the hitting it can do. A very good weapon is important here.

In my book, a bard that takes way too much punishment will be a detriment to the group, regardless of the fact that it may have 5 or 6 Lambent pieces ... I have been with a 33 Human Bard in the Keep, and that one did nothing but the weapons and crack song ... and it took the most punishment in the group, even though it had a Ykesha ... I played all instruments the whole time, but more mana was wasted on that Bard than any other player in the full group ... but we did well, as we were handling both the Raider and Warrior rooms at that time, so no complaints here.

I am of the opinion that no matter your dress, if you do not have the AGI/DEX part of things, you will be hurting way too much, unless you have the CHA up high and can learn the clever stuff real fast ... which is hard to learn to work with, and much too intimidating, and more than half the Bards you will ever meet, never use it.

Being that I have "even stats" all around, makes me good for one thing ... I can switch personalities from one to another ... here is the examples:

If I am needed for hitting -- lots of casters and such: 2 rings, 2 bracers and collar that have STR on them.

If I am needed for some help with crowd control, meaning that we have a Cleric/Healer in party, and good hitters, then I switch the same five items for CHA items ... now I plug the Flute and I can Charm all day long with 114 CHA ... if I ever get the Mask, it will be a cynch ... I already can do Picklaw Raiders and I am only level 30!

So, you can do a lot of things, to help along. I am a proponent of averaging out stats some, since one that is critically low is not good ... one member of my Guild in Veeshan had all the points in AGI/DEX and CHA, and his STR was only 70, with 75 STA I think, the base for Wood Elves ... I had to go get him 2 STR rings (5 each) and will look to get him 2 Jagged Bands real soon ... it is that bad. He doesn't get hit, but he also can not hit! He is an excellent group player, though, and rarely the first one to go down! He is too tough to hit.

The only question remaining, is, how much of all this fun stuff will you be doing after you get the Level 32 song? ... without any backstats, you won't be doing much of the far out stuff, and let me tell you that crowd control is a good thing in dungeons and many places in Kunark ... and often an Enchanter is not enough!

Moshkiae
