I occasionally feel that way about druids, and I play one...Povar (where I play) has a fair number of anti-social, guard-camping, and generally less-than-pleasant druids.

There are nice druids out there -- I know several, and I try to be one. I think part of the problem is that there are a lot of people who play druids because 1.) they solo well, and 2.) they get some nifty abilities...and once they've soloed for 30-odd levels they forget their social skills. Or something like that.

That, and I think a lot of them get really sick of being asked for SoW's, TP's, etc. -- I've had people show up while I was grouped with a druid and start begging for TP's while we were very obviously in the middle of fighting something. (As a note, high-level shaman get the same thing from people wanting buffs -- my husband has been known to go anon and invis before going through newbie areas just to avoid newbies begging for buffs.)

A good, trustworthy druid is a great group member -- I've both grouped with druids and played one. I admit that one of the reasons I play a druid is because she can solo better than any of my other characters, but I have a lot more fun in a good group.
