Sorry, but there is some misinformation in the above post.

Dex does not help with interrupts while casting. That is PURELY a function of channeling skill.

Dex does not help with the percentage that you hit a mob. That is strictly based on level and weapon skill.

Dex will help with slightly more critical hits, crippling blows, weapon procs and mellee skill improvement. However, dex only helps weapons proc in your MAIN hand.

I agree with Lyfe. After a lot of testing Dex is shown to be limited in effectiveness...

Dex effects procs, crits, and crips. We know this from lots of testing. Dex is believed to effect melee skill increases, but there is not proof to that belief. Dex does have a slight effect in bow and thrown weapon damage. Its very slight in the same way strength has a minor effect on melee damage output. Again this has been shown by log tests over the past year.

Stats are less meaningful than they should be. Agl and Sta are more important for warriors than dex or str. A lot of people believe (and I am one of them) that dex really just needs be above 100 to get the crit and proc bonuses.

Triberian said:

> Dex does a a few things actually:
> 1) Used for keeping your spells from being > interupted while you are getting hit.

Not true. Only channelling does this. According to GZ: (See http://eq.castersrealm.com/viewarticle.asp?Article=607)

Here it is as much information as I have been able to acquire in relation to spell recovery. All information has been confirmed by GZ.
Your chance to recover casting is calculated solely on your Channelling Skill.
Dexterity only affects Channelling in relation to raising your skill in it. How quickly your raise your Channelling ability is determined by a combination of Dexterity and Intelligence.
Dexterity does not affect your chance to actually recover casting.
