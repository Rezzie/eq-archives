I'd have to say enchanter....there a lot of combos that will be effective and playable, however, but enchanter/druid has the fewest downfalls.

For instance:

Druid/Necro: Good combo with fear/snare/pet, and the necro will always have tons of mana from your regen spells combined with their lich line, but this causes an imbalance in mana levels. Your dots stack with theirs, though...necro is a close second to enchanter but not quite there.

Druid/Tank: Skin buff and thorns make for a good tank, but your little heals can't really stand up to a true warrior's hit points. Works ok with a monk or ranger, but they get pounded so badly you may run out of mana healing them just as quickly as a warrior.

Druid/Cleric: Kind of a waste of time. You might as well solo, the cleric won't add much effectiveness. The only upside is that you've got a free rez if you die.

Druid/Shaman: Not a bad combo....your buffs combined with the shamans on his pet make it pretty stout, and all your dots stack with each other. You can both cast SoW so your partner won't be screwed if it runs out or you get separated.

Druid/Wizzie: Very good combo for snare and burn tactics. You can take down some really tough mobs this way. Downtime kinda sucks though.

Druid/Mage: Kind of a mix between the shaman and wizzie combo...you can make their pet more effective, and they nuke well, but the downtime's kinda rough. You'd both be better off soloing as you're not going to be killing things much faster.

Druid/Rogue: This one's actually decent for a druid/melee combo, but it's hard to make work. You need to build up serious aggro on the mob then let the rogue run behind and backstab it will you burn it. If you get too far away though, the mob will turn and start whacking the rogue, so timing is everything.

And finally
Druid/Enchanter: With your snare and dots, and the enchanters fear, dot, and pet, reverse kiting is a piece of cake. Tashan helps lower the resist rates on snares, dots, and nukes, and clarity reduces downtime for both of you. All the good parts of the necro combo with none of the bad.


This is just from my exerience...your mileage may vary. As always, play whats fun for you. The games about having a good time.

I have necro and a druid and when playing one class I actively seek out the other as a partner because the druid/necro class combination is very very hard to rival.

Both can heal themselves and others, both can root, snare, DoT (which stack nicely) and at higher levels, DMF and SoW makes traversing even the most horrible terrain a breeze. Stick regen/chloro on the necro, let it become the mana battery and dump mana straight into the druid with the subversion spells.

I started my druid aftering grouping with so many as a necro; when you combine the two best solo classes in the game in one group, there is very little you can not handle - it's also reassuring to know that if things get sticky, that your partner is equally capable to look after themselves.
