Ack , trying to use our pet like necro's or magician pet is a waste of pet, and you will probably have to zone. Our pet has very low HPs, low ac and low agility, so this pet has EXTREMELY limited tanking abilities. So is our pet usefull , HELL YES!!!

The best use for our pet is to treat him as a long DOT, do most of your damage with your DOTs and when low on mana run by your pet (who is parked away from the main action with /pet no taunt) ... then just run the mob around with your pet biting at its heels.

The secret to shamans solo is this:

Pre fight buffing:

- Make sure you have SOW on at all times, don't forget to sow your pet if fighting Giants or any FAST mobs (this is obvious for all shamans , just want to mention it for completeness)

- Buff yourself with AC line, stat buffs if you want (AGI and STA are good , more hitpoins and better AC, also STR and DEX if meleeing or looking to improve melee skils), always have Talisman HP buff on (Tnarg, Altuna)

-Summon the highest level pet if possible, buff him with AC and HP buff, haste buff if mana permits, stat buffs are useless on pets, don't waste your mana

- ALWAYS HAVE Regen/Chloro on you so that you can cannibilize while medding!! (endless source of mana... regen/chloro/regrowth +can)

Solo combat:

1. Debuff the target (you can skip this if the target is known to have very low MR and is blue)

2. Pull the target to a safe area to avoid the attraction of other MOBs, learn your surroundings!

3. Root the target

4. Pull away to a maximum range and cast your DOT (you can cast disease first or poison, 2 dots work faster, I like the disease line , some hate it and stick to poison only)

5. If root breaks root again , try to keep the mob stationary at ALL times

6. Meditate (and cannibilize every 6 seconds if low on mana and high on HPs) while MOB is rooted, when you get the message that one of your DOTs has worn off, reapply the DOT

7. Rinse and repeat steps 3 and up, when MOB is under 50% health that means that it WILL NEVER ATTACK YOUR pet until it dies , so you can pet kite it til death ( you must stay within range while kiting otherwise the MOB will turn on your pet), however this is not the most efficient way. Sometimes it is better to use the pet when low on mana or for the last 20% to 30% of MOBs health.

This is the only technique that will work solo on DEEP reds, meaning 5 or more levels above you (don't go too much above 5 though)

As a level 39 shaman I was able to kill Bloodscale the Viscious (this was a lvl 45 spawn , verified by a lvl 45 cleric , conned even to him) , this MOB has over 4500+ to 5000 hps. There was lvl 43 druid who attempted to kill him 2 times and had to zone, ran out of mana with Bloodscale around 60% health due to resists.

Well it took a LONG time almost 20 minutes but without a pet this is NOT doable solo at this level, I'd like to see another lvl 39 solo this mob (necros mighnt be able to pull it off, but without a MR debuff it would be tough), Bloodscale hits in the 120s.

Another way to fight with a pet is to slow down the MOB with Togor line and tank for the pet, this will work for a lot of blue mobs, but on yellows and reds you will be TOAST!

That is exactly the mentality you need to have if you ever want to effectively manage your mana. A shaman pet is NOTHING more than a reusable DoT. Turn off taunt, slap on quickness and get into a group with a warrior or monk.

For your 450 mana at level 39, you get a pet that will do relative damage of about 200 damage per fight. Now of course this is dependent upon size of group, type of mob, etc... I am using my experiences from Dreadlands beasts. Mostly in the upper 30's level mobs. I can get Thousands and thousands of damage out of a pet in an hour. This is hands down THE most effective and mana efficient means by which a shaman can deal damage to mobs. Forget DoTs and DD spells if you are in a large group. If there are casters that is their job. If there are meleers that are killing quickly you cannot get the damage in from a dot save poisons.

My role in a group (normally) is quickness on meleers, talisman, back up heal, debuffs and buffs. Period. Rarely will I have mana that allows any more than this.

So if I want to contribute to the damage dealing end of things I make a pet. I spend the 450 mana ONCE and I get tens of thousands of damage for that mana if you manage it properly. Of course I am talking over several hours and hundreds of mobs. In DL we pull constantly one after another. We figured out once that we easily destroy 30 mobs per hour on average. Figuring my pet does at least 400 damage to every single mob, I can roughly estimate 12,000 points of damage per hour are doled out by rover.

If you are in group where you or the pet do not ever tank (save a few hits), you never even need to buff pet except for Quickness.

After 34, grab a necro or enchanter or SK and fear kite them spectres. Insanely powerful pets when fear kiting. INSANE I SAY. (foghorn leghorn voice)

I think the damage estimate above is rather low - I know that my 49th pet contributes well over 1k in damage most real fights.

Since when soloing I keep my AC max'd my usual is to keep myself at full stat/hp/ac regen buff - right before I pull I alac myself and the pet. Park the pet at the pull spot and go looking.

Unless the target is known for good mr or has buffs up I pull with a Slow spell. (If it is I use malosi or nullify respectively)
Bring it back to the pet - park VoS and EB on it and tank it for a bit. If it lasts long enough that I have to reapply the dots - I root it and let the pet finish it.

This has served me well in most outdoor areas of kunark.

My 49 pet tanks great on most blue mobs. The 44 was a bit weaker, but with slow, my pet tanks fine and can do so for a majority of the battle. I keep an eye on his health, but letting him take as much damage as possible without dying is the most effective use of a pet. More damage to him equals less damage to you equals more cannibalizing and less down time.

I always keep the mob rooted, thus the mob attacks the nearest opponent. When my pet gets below one quarter health, only then do I begin to help it tank, by stepping closer to the mob than the pet. The only buff my pet ever gets is Alacrity, unless Im on the move, then he gets sow.
