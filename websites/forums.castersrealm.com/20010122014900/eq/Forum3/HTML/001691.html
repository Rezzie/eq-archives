Since there are so few people in the zone to begin with, it would stand to reason that people see the risk/reward equation as being out of whack for the zone. In other words, it isnt worth hunting in. In order for verant to attract people to any given zone they can do a number of things. The two with the most direct impact are to either increase the chance of getting good loot, or the amount of experience given throughout the zone. Changing either the loot or the experience would be simply treating the symptom, not the underlying cause to camping which is too few monsters for too many people.

In order to conserve bandwidth, possibly to promote 'grouping' as well, verant went with the more is less mindset. Fewer monsters, but each one being tougher than the average player. Just My View
unregistered 		posted 08-27-2000 08:29 AM              
The EQ dev team keep working on ways to make the game a better roleplaying experience and encourage "the preferred method" of playing as opposed to camping. The preferred method is the traditional explore-and-discover style of play we remeber from D&D or similar games. Problem is, EQ is NOT a roleplaying game, it is a tactical game, and thus people will always approach play as a tactical exercise more than a roleplaying experience. Hence the obsession with always having the best equipment, and the whole camping mindset. Camping is very attractive tactically. It gives a (more or less) controlled engagement with (more or less) predictable opponents. If EQ were a roleplaying game, nobody would EVER camp. Can you imagine a less enjoyable roleplaying experience than camping a spawn? For that matter, the whole idea of "spawns" is bad for roleplaying, because it destroys any sense of persistence in the world. How many times can you kill mob "x" before you quit thinking of it as a Dragon or a Wizard, and just think of the proper technique for reducing its life bar faster than it can reduce yours? Don't get me wrong, EQ is a very GOOD tactical game, and I enjoy it immensely, but it is not and never can be made into a roleplaying game. The gaming engine simply is not set up to encourage roleplaying. Maybe EQ2 will be different, but until and unless that happens, people WILL camp, because it just makes good tactical sense.
