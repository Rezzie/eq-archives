Now, I am using my main role as crowd control, duh! I move up and try to spilt the pulls as they are coming in. I have been trying to keep quickness on the two main tanks and Breeze on all casters. I also tashan the mob we are attacking.

What other things should I be doing for my group. It seems like sometimes I am just hanging out not doing much. Are there other things I can do to make me a better enchanter.

Well I imagine your are doing the right things. The sanity warp I wouldnt bother with. Mezing mobs as they come in is a good idea to keep them spread out a bit. AE Mez is also very helpful. Use of Memblur on mobs after AE Mez and normal Mez will keep them from aggroing on you. One of the best spells around pre 35.
I always keep rune up so that I can cast through getting beaten on, but this might not be as important for you and might be a financial constraint. Rune III is 7gp, II is less, I is cheap I think.

Honestly ench do sit around a bit. We buff pre combat. Mez the mobs. Slow if necessary. Most the time to minimize cleric healing, but if they falling pretty fast (3 or 4 tanks) then you prob dont want to waste the mana. Then Med. Time keep track of when the mezes will wear off and insure the nontanks arent getting attacked (blur/remez them).
I try to keep my buffs spread out. I clarity a diff character every pull. Then aug a diff character every pull. Round-Robin till everyone is done. You wont run low on mana this way.
Charm is really dependent on the situation, and how much you trust your group. At 18th you wont be fighting stuff that can kill you in two seconds, so charming is pretty safe. Only problem with charm is that it is a mana hog, so it is often better just to keep things mezed rather than charming at all. On bad pulls however a good charm can really save the day.
At your level I was in HighHold Pass on Orcs.
20th-27th Mistmoore is great
22nd-27 Unrest is good, and close.
24th-27th Splitpaw inside is good too (not past doors)

There are also some places in Kunark that I hear are great for you level, havent done them myself yet though, but my new erudite enchanter probably will soon.

Good luck and enjoy the class.

Well met, and congratulations on 18th level.

"Just hanging around and doing nothing" ?
The next time your friendly neighborhood mob decides to show up with his Uncle Bob and three or four of his drinking buddies, and you mez / charm the adds, turning Doomsday into Experience Points, remind yourself that you're "just hanging around doing nothing".

The next big fight you're in, watch the damage your tanks deal out, and remember that a fair amount of that is due to the Haste you buffed them with, remind yourself that you're "just hanging around doing nothing".

The next time the Nuke specialist drops The Big One on a mob, or the cleric saves somebody's anatomy with a timely heal, ponder the extra mana that your Breeze is giving them, and remind yourself that you're "just hanging around doing nothing".

I think you've got your role nailed down...buff the tanks, control the crowds. Enchanters are very powerful, as you're probably finding out, but our power is a subtle one. Anyone who accuses you of doing nothing needs to run in a group without an Enchanter. They'll see the light in short order.

If I were you, I'd drop the nuke in favor of Languid Pace. The combination of a hasted tank and a slowed mob is a delight to watch. I'd also keep a color stun somewhere so that when the mobs come after you (and they will), you can stun-park them and sort them out without needing to yell for help from an already busy tank.
