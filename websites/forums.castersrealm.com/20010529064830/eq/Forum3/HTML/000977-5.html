I think it's pretty funny how people perceive the fact they "can't" play without good gear.

As any old-timer can tell you, there was a time when we simply had no idea where the good gear even was, or even WHAT it was. We thought combine swords rocked, and the BBC was nice, even if it had a bad agi penalty. Bronze or store bought armor was the best anyone had, until we hit levels sufficient for Cazic and the infamous rubicite.

My point is that at level 32 when I quit EQ originally, I was wearing a FBR, and the Oracle robe had just been discovered. We had, at a level spread of 30-40 max on the server, just begun to find the items that are so commonplace now.

Know what? We still kicked ass. It's just that now that everyone knows about this 'l33t g34r', everyone thinks they should have it. And there is a large enough proportion of people playing long hours that the economy has a significant number of these items available. I for one like good loot as much as the next guy, but it matters not a bit if I don't have as good loot as others my level, because I can guarantee you they in most cases did not get it with the character wearing it.

I sort of chuckle at low level characters in great gear. It robs much of the game's progression. I was so happy when I actually got my first FBR, because I got it myself. If a guy is given an Oracle robe, great, but he will be wearing it for 30 more levels or so until he can get a new one. I will take a stat/ac hit at lower levels for the chance to advance through gear naturally as well as levels.

I think the game has aspects that are designed for the casual gamer, but a large portion is not. The comment about a 72 minute spawn being ok is fine, if the item in question is just loot dropped by a specific mob. If the item is part of a quest, then I think the spawn time is MUCH too long for a casual gamer.

I had an experience that I think is exactly what the EQ designers want with my druid finding the Skull of Jen'tra [sp?]. I stumbled on the mob and was in a fight that was a bit too tough for me at the time. Luckily someone helped me finish it off and I got the loot. I was very surprised and delighted to find the Skull. I never went out of my way to find it, I just stumbled on it while exploring. That is the way "random" loot should be hunted, and I don't mind week-long spawn times for such mobs.

However, I've now started an Iksar Necro and am doing the low-level skullcap quests. One of them requires me finding and killing an apparently rare mob. I've spent days searching for it. I've asked people in the Zone if they've seen it and have searched web sites to find the specific area in the Zone to search. From what I've found, the creature is an uncommon or rare spawn in an area that is much higher level than the mob in question. I spent hours searching that area, and I can't do anything else since the other mobs are too high level to fight. This is very boring. In addition, another part of this quest involved killing an uncommon mob that other classes are hunting for. Because we all have to kill multiple of these creatures, people spend hours or days just trying to get enough kills. Again, that is boring.

I think that quest items should be on very short timers. While it is nice to say you should play and find what you can rather than hunting for specific items, the nature of a quest is to hunt for a specific item. To make that item then be on a long spawn is annoying at best. In addition, collecting all the no drop items you find in the hopes they may be needed in a quest is a waste of vault space. :-)

One additional comment, I'd like to be able to sell those no drop items (especially the ones that vendors give a price for) for the cash once I realize I won't be able to use them (a big problem in the Kurnak newbie areas). Just make the vendor not resell them.
