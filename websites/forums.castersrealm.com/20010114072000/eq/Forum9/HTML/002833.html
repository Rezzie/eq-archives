Slow leveling can have a lot of reasons. Three hours per bubble is not uncommon, and if you had a lot of fun then I wouldn't worry about it. If you main goal is fast exp you can do better. Here are some common causes of slow leveling.

1) Your level is low compared to the level of the group then your getting a smaller share of exp.

2) If even one member of the group is several levels above you but has serious exp penalties (i.e. Troll SK) that can noticably affect your exp rate.

3) If your strategy is to fight fewer higher level mobs your going to level slower than mowing though low blues. This is because the extra mana and HP it takes to kill the higher level mobs is not paid back by proportionaly higher exp.

4) If your group is not working at optimal efficiency then you can level *a lot* slower. The most familiar example to most enchanters is a group that doesn't understand crowd control or how to fight effectively and insists on fighting every mob in the pull all at once rather than letting you mez and fight one at a time.

5) Camping a spawn past the point where your keeping the group challenged and busy also results in slow leveling. If the group spends a lot of time at full health and mana waiting for something to spawn its going to affect leveling rate.

My guess its not the mobs, its one or more of the above. My advice is to not get overly concerned about one session of slow leveling. I also wouldn't worry so much about leveling that you forget to have fun. Some of the most fun times players have is not in situations where they are going for max exp.

One last note. I've historically found that I get much more consistent leveling rate solo than with a group. With groups there's a broad range and sometimes exp creeps along and other times it blows away what I could do solo. I usualy find I do better than average solo when compared to the run of the mill pickup group. In a group where I've been with the people before and they work well together and are in a good spot I almost always get better exp than solo.
