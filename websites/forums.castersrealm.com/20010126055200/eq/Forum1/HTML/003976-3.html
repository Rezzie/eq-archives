Jobber,

Ok here is how real ae works in say Karnors from the healers perspective.

Scenario 1

Puller comes back with 1-2 mobs.
Mez them and puller goes out for more.

Scenario 2

Puller comes back with 3-4 mobs.

1st option - preferred- Mez and go out for more.
2nd option -
As puller comes in I stand about 3 feet in front of chanter and I drop a fairly major heal on the tank, Remedy, and aggro the pull to ME. The reason for this is I wear plate, and as such, I dont die as fast as that pyjama caster Minki or our many lovable paper sack wearing armor enchanters. I take a few hits as the mobs group up and the chanter starts chain stunning. Note at this point, most mobs are still mad at ME. The wizzies/mages/druid then cut loose and stuff starts to die. I now start chaining Remedy on the chanter as he is usually taking some serious punishment if the mobs are borderline too high (ie, hands in karnor at lvl 52) but with no hands at 52, its usually a walk in the park... Few stun resists, wizzy are shooting with pillar of frost and lightning depending on level and mobs sometimes drop without hitting anyone cept the puller and me.

Scenario 3 -- my personal favorite

Puller runs in the Karnor basement. Starts to pull a train (about 5 mobs) I stand at the top of the stairs and HEAL him with Remedy (gotta love that range). I run back to group and mobs WARP to me ~ 10 to 17 i take damage like nobodies business as the first couple of stun hit home, heal myself then hit the chanter with remedy as fast as i can. all 3 wizzy (you NEED 3 for this to work =) cut loose with Static pulse or better PB AE spells and you quickly kill off the crap mobs (ie pets, low greens etc) and this reduces the mobs to a resonable number ~ 7 to 12. Then you see yellow spam all over as about 8 mobs die at once =) and you can finish off the rest with your puller and some single nukes.

The key thing to remember is that the less time the mob lives, the less time it can do dmg to your group. So NEVER let a mob pound on someone just to group them up, stun them and the ones beyond stun range will catch up. Dont worry about aggro, stuff wont live long enough to kill you and if one runs to get you, run to the "safety" of the chanter and cut loose a big PB ae while you are there =)

your tactic refers to the commonplace group AoE usage. heres how i explain it, although i am a wimp and do it when theres one tank per mob pulled. for some reason, the secondary mob's aggro is attached to the first mobs. this is usually what happens when a tank just charges into the sarnak fort or what have you and comes back with an army on his tail. oddly enough, you dont add aggro to the extra mobs unless you take the aggro on the primary mob. yes, it makes no sense. no, it doesn't always hold true, and yes, i have done this many times and seen good results.
