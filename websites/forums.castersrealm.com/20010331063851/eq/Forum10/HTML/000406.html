These are some things I've found that work.

As a cleric your job will be to keep the group alive. This is your most important responsibility. If anyone needs to ask for a heal, then you aren't doing your job. (of course there are always those cases where the tank starts screaming for a heal when you're in the middle of casting it.)
Keep everyone informed of your mana status. At your level you might want to put the pullers on a leash, since they tend to think a full group means they can pull 6 mobs.
So make sure everyone understands that oom means no heals.
Let everyone know that it's very hard to heal if you're being attacked. While you can probably get away with meleeing some at your level, by the time you reach lvl 14 or so, you should give it up. Meleeing the mob that went after you for healing the tank makes it that much harder for the tank to taunt it off of you.
Don't be afraid to keep everyone buffed. Some preventive maintenance can be a good thing.
Be sure to keep yourself buffed too. Those extra hitpoints can make a difference.
Good luck to you.

I have found in addition to the above that if you tell the tanks in advance that you will not heal during a battle until they get down to 40%, then they will not get so antsy and you will not get aggroed near as much.

Also, be firm with casters that continue to get aggroed at the beginning of a fight. Let them know that they have to control their nuking so they do not get aggroed. When a pull goes bad, nothing will kill the party faster than the cleric going OOM because he had to keep a claster alive with 3-4 heals because he over-nuked and got aggroed.

Case in point, at around 28, a warrior, a shaman, a paladin, a druid and myself were in the well in CT killing alligators. On 4 seperate occations, we got 3-4 at once and had no problems. The durid kept the warrior thorned and snared the tgt'ed mob, the shaman slowed the mobs and parked the ones that aggroed on him. the paly assisted and LoH on the warrior when I was oom (I usually would get oom on a pull of 4).

Enter another druid. The first pull of 4, he starts chain nuking, gets all 4 aggroed on him, I go OOM and got aggroed trying to keep him alive and after he dies, the rest of us died because I was OOM and we still had 4 healthy alligators.

I warned him that I would not heal him again if he got aggroed that fast for doing something that stupid again. I would save my mana and save the rest of the party.

Well it was not 30 minutes later at the pyramid that we got 3 frogs at once. Well, this time the same druid get in the middle of the mobs and casts an AoE spell and aggroed all three on him again. He died in seconds and I did not even attempt to save him. The rest of us survived and had a good laugh at him.

Do not waste all your mana and jepordize your group healing dummy's. Instead, talk to them, train them, make them understand the importance of not getting aggroed, or just let them die if they do not (or will not) learn.

I think my best advice would be to start grouping in a dungeon as soon as you can. While it is possible to solo undead throughout a cleric's life, nothing will teach you faster than focusing on grouping. Afterall, as a cleric, you form an essential part of any mid to high level group. Make friends with enchanters you trust at low levels, they will make grouping with at later levels an incredible experience. Half of the people on my friends list are enchanters and I can tell you a group that contains a cleric and a chanter is something that the mobs fear.

Learn to use your level 5 stun spell. later on you can save insane amounts of damage done to group members by interupting casters and stunning mobs so your chanter can mez them. I'm only level 40 so I can't comment on the high level game, but I think that stun is one of the best spells in our lineup.

Always keep Divine Aura memorized. However, don't use it to flee when everyone is sticking around. When the group decides its time to zone, I usually try to aggro a couple of them, hit Divine Aura and run like a druid to the zone. You'd be surprised how far you can get in 18 seconds of being invulnerable

Hotkeys are another great thing to develop. My heals are all hotkeyed...they go something like this...

1) /sit off
2) /g Healing %t (this will tell your group you are healing whoever you have targeted)
3) /cast # (where # is the spell slot of the spell 1 through 8)

Basically this will allow you to med and when you feel its time to heal, just click on the person you want to heal and then hit this hotkey.

Learn to control how mad mobs get at you. I am the type of cleric that waits until the tanks are low on health before healing (this becomes more applicable in the 30s). By that time, the mob is so ticked off at the tank that you healing the tank for 600 points doesn't bother them quite so much as it would if you open with a heal.

If you have spare mana and see somebody getting whacked, toss a heal their way. Or some poor tank trying to solo, toss a buff or two their way. You'd be surprised at the great people you can meet if you try helping out whenever you can.

Try and find a group of people that play at a similar time as you and try to group with them as much as possible. When I am grouped with people that I trust and that trust me, we can accomplish twice as much as a group that doesn't know their roles.

Nothing irritates me more than a tank that is constantly yelling "HEAL!" I usually respond to that "You do your job I'll do mine" and that usually shuts them up

Well that got rather long, but I hope theres at least one useful point in there Toss a /tell my way seeing as I play on Sol Ro.
