Well, I got my hood on the third day of camping the yard trash (YAY!)

A few notes: Quid is *tough*! He was double and triple-puching me even when slowed, and he maxed at 77 each punch (needless to say, enchanters will see the max quite often). I thought I'd be able to take him easily at level 39, but I died when he first spawned. Luckily, I'd bound myself in the valley and went back to try again. This time I drank a SoW potion first, and was able to escape to Feerot with one bubble of health. He resisted just enough stuns to keep himself healed even though he'd already used his LoH. Luckily, a druid I knew was in the zone and was able to help me out. He brought along a mage in his guild and the third time round Quid fell in under 30 seconds, and dropped the hood . Maybe the Quid that spawned for me was a slightly higher level (still blue at 39), or more probably I just suck at soloing.

When I first saw him, he was in the valley just south of the paladin camp. I followed him for quite a time while I was waiting for my friends to arrive, and he wandered around the area east of the paladin camp, going from the ruined village to the inn to the north. I was worried about him despawning, but he didn't respond to hails (apprehensive to me as a high-elf, I didn't try any other races as I was trying to regen at the time). There was plenty of other yard-trash around at the same time (including fire beetles), so he isn't an all-or-none spawn like the ancient croc.

Yes, that leather-look is ugly, but after around 12 hours camping him, I wear it with pride . I'd advise those contemplating this camp to combine it with bone-chip gathering. I got enough to go non-KOS with the crusaders in Cabilis, which was an extra bonus.

Finally, this was a good example of the benefits of being liberal with your clarity. You never know when you might have to call in a favour .
