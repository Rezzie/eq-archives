I have a few uses for the memblur spells.. I'll list them hopefully to allow others to see the usefulness of these spells.

As we all know many of our spells are "mega-taunts".

Situation 1: Crowd control.
Your group has encountered 2-3 mobs. Your task is to mez the extras. After each mez simply cast memblur. Without the benefit of statistical analysis that some people are using I have found that this will reduce the incidence of the mob attacking me after the mez is broken.

Situation 2: Debuffing.
Tash and slows seem to have high "mega-taunt" ratings. To be most effective these two spells should be cast at the beginning of combat. By seperating these 2 spells with Mem Blur you can reduce the chance of the mob attacking you.

Situation 3: Charming.
This is the only time I will use RA memblur. Lead with this spell. Then you cast Tash to allow your Charm a better chance of success. I have found that by doing this I am able to cast Charm without being aggroed after Tash.

Situation 4: Nuking
Between nukes (yes Enchanters can nuke and need to do so on occasions) use memblur. With the long cycle time on our nukes you are able to cast Nuke, Blur, Nuke, Blur...

I would suggest that MemBlur is our most powerful spell line. This spell has the ability to change a battle dramatically.

I have seen mobs turn and walk away after a memblur even after they have been mezzed, debuffed and taunted by tanks and then hit to break mez.

Just as an Enchanter allows a group to exceed its potential. This spell allows Enchanters to exceed their potential by stacking spells which on their own would taunt a mob to attack us.

There are only 2 spells which I always have space for. Tash and Memblur.

I hope my own experiences can help others.
