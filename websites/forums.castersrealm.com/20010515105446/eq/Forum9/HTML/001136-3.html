Two big mistakes you made, Tud, were in your methodology (I do complement you, but there are 2 mistakes).

#1: There is NO test done on the effect of the FBSS ALONE!

Reason this is important:

What if the FBSS ALONE gets the user to the MAX haste cap (weather it be based on lvl or weapon)?

This would push the user past the haste cap NO MATTER WHAT!

#2: User perception is not what necessarily happens IN GAME!

Reason this is important:

Results in game may not be triggered constantly. This means that while YOU may see the hits every 1.2XX seconds, they may actually not be applied until 1.4XX seconds. This could even work if timed (or logged) by multiple people. The server might retain this information and apply it when the correct time has been achieved.

Why this would be done:

If the game is timed on two fronts (Server and Client side), then the effect may be shown on the client, but would not actually be applied on the server until the ACTUAL time has elapsed. So while you may see and even log attacks faster, they may not do ANY DAMAGE (or miss) until the 1.4XX seconds has elapsed.

THIS IS THE VERY REASON THAT CLIENT-SIDE TESTS MAY NOT BE ACCURATE!

Just a little critical thought (BTW, I think that you generally come up with the correct methodology).

I don't think so. If so, how come I was consistently getting a maximum of 69-70% haste on the 21 delay weapons, yet I was able to get 73-75% on the 50 delay weapon? I think there is more to it than just an absolute cap.

Tudamorf, you're running into an idiosynchrasy with weapon delays in the game engine. The actual weapon delay is the listed delay + 0.5. So a 50 delay weapon is actually 50.5, while a 21 delay weapon is actually 21.5. Once you correct for that, your 21 delay results in a 72% speedup. Well within the margin of error of 73%.

I avoided this problem by also timing the delays of the weapons without any hastes whatsoever. When I did my first preliminary timings, I found it odd that all my x10 timings were ending in .5 seconds, so I did a couple really long timings (on the order of 5 minutes) to verify that this was going on. If you look at the eqsongs site, you'll see that my punches (35 delay) timed out to an effective 35.54 delay, and the FS dagger (19 delay) timed out to an effective 19.46 delay.

For archival purposes:

The haste stacking bug was recognized and addressed in the patch of 19-Sep-2000.

Here is an excerpt from the patch message:
========================
*Attack Speed Buffs*

In this patch we corrected a bug that would cause any "Haste" item combined with any "Haste" spell to automatically yield maximum haste, regardless of the level of "Haste" granted by each individual component. This fix increases the usefulness of better "Haste" equipment and spells. The downside to this is that those who usually have both items and spells active will see a reduced attack-speed unless outfitted with the best equipment and spells.
=======================
