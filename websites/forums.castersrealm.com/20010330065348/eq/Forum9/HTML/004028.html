Verant has basically made it possible not to step into a dungeon until lvl 50. And looking strictly at experience, it is much faster and safer to adventure (and I use the term rather loosely) outside. If one needs loot, hunt at FM Giants, Rathe Mountains, etc.

This is because Kunark and Velious is aimed at a wider audience and were designed to get people to keep playing the game by getting them hooked by thinking they have accomplished something by levelling.

Dervs in West Coomons, Orcs in Oasis, Highpass are harder places to fight than in Overthere. 6 people kill a tiger, rhino, etc. Everybody has SoW. Essentially an upper level newbie zone.

Unrest - place empty or bad puller will get you and have the party killed.

Mistmoore - empty except for campers/raids.

Cazic Thule - One is able to find a group on an irregular basis, but lack of a good bind spot for good races and great exp at Overthere makes this dungeon underutilised. I spent parts of 29 and 35 there, but not many groups.

SolB was apparently crowded in the old days. Not any more.

Dalnir, Droga, Nurga, Kaeosra - underutilised. Crystal Caverns is also pretty empty, except for raid groups, campers.

Upper Guk actually has some druids killing spiders and few others, but I did not want to kill my Lower Guk live side faction so I never fought there there.

Blame it on the Lake of Ill Omen, Overthere (or East Wastes) - Dreadlands cycle.

My enchanter is lvl 37 and I have to join a guild, else I will never be able to find a dungeon group on a semi-regular basis. (I have been invited to a few but have postponed the decision for some time now.) I have rediscovered my ranger while waiting for groups at Cazic Thule, Dalnir, Crystal Caverns and Upper Guk.

I really don't feel like putting together my own group made up of a bunch of strangers - most of whom have never stepped foot in the inside of the sarnak fortress - never mind a real dungeon, getting them to come to the dungeon and then - have a key member leave after 2 minutes for whatever reason, etc. (happened in Crystal Caverns).

I guess I have to join a guild and drag some people to a dungeon.

Well for you chanters that are bored with the Overthere, I have your cure. Become the group puller.

With a little practice you can pull all day and will only get hit if you get a bad resist. What I did, is pull with tash(sow'd of course), and run back behind my group. Stop, turn slighly to make sure you are sationary and start hitting the enthrall (lvl 16 mez) spell gem. Usually you will have to wait for the mob to come into range. Mez the target and let your melee do the standard mez break routine, while you are off looking for another pull.

The end result is that there is always a mezzed mob ready to be fought after the current mob is killed, melee characters get no downtime, and you only stop if the healers or you are low on mana. Not to mention you are too busy pulling to be bored.

To conserve my mana, I used the cheap lvl 16 mez, and I also only auged the melee players. That way I was not having to stop ever 5-10 minutes to haste the melee.

Don't be fooled thou, this will put you at more risk and you have a better chance of getting killed. The flip side is that you get a constant, and fast flow of exp. Note that you good races can illusion DE as to not agro the Dragoons, which are the bane of the zone.

Basically it comes down to a few things

1) People don't like dying and losing xp.

2) People want to start playing as soon as possible.

3) People want to level.


Dungeons are at odds with all the above.

They're inherently riskier than outdoor zones.

They contain groups in certain camps and if those spots are camped and the groups are full you're out of luck.

Experience can be just as good or better in outdoor groups due to a regular supply of mobs.

Outdoors groups allow for more tactics and spells than dungeons (SoW,fear kiting etc).

Outdoor groups allow for more class diversity.

After my cleric did 21 to 40 in Lake Ill Omen/Frontier Mountains/Overthere I came back to Sol B and Lower Guk specifically to get some dungeon experience.

I managed to get a couple of decent groups but after spending over an hour in both zones one night shouting for a group I gave up and headed back to the Dreadlands. All the camps that might have taken me were full, and there were never enough people in the zones looking for a group to form one.

I can get a group in the Dreadlands within 15 minutes, can cover the cost of gems, make a little profit and have a death free night.
