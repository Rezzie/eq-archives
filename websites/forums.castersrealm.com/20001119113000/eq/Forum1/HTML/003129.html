Back when EQ was first released, many zones were not safe. Griffins in East Commons usually numbered around 2 or 3 at any time. West commons were crawling with giants. The Karanas were dangerous to cross without SoW and/or invis because of the mobs there. There was even a time when sand giants were common in North Ro.

At some point, the spawn tables were adjusted to be a little more friendly to the lower level people who were consistently eaten by griffins or stomped by giants.

Then server populations matured. There were suddenly enough people to cleanse entire zones of the nasty 35+ mobs and keep them that way, often solo. Travel was no longer that dangerous, just tedious. There really isn't an outdoor zone in which I have anything to fear (save Kunark).

Maybe this addition of mid-to-high level mobs is a poorly guided attempt to reintroduce the danger of travel again. Really, these new mobs will just become like the griffins of old, with constant status reports being /shouted across the zone as to it's current whereabouts.

But 45+ agro-everything mobs in zones where the average level is around 5-15 is probably a bit excessive. Especially if it's an isolated zone like Qeynos hills.
