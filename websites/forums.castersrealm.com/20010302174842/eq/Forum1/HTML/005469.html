Just to be picky, what do you mean when you say "dungeon"?

A dank place for holding prisoners? An ancient ruin filled with secrets long ago lost? An indoor zone?

Technically CT is a Temple and tagged as an outdoor zone because you can use outdoor only spells in there. Sure, it does exhibit some indoor features - below ground/within structure areas and the zone is confined with an enclosed space. It's still an outdoor zone by virtue of those spells you can cast.

The concept of a "dungeon" is misleading in EQ. While some areas are definately classic PnP dungeons the term in PnP was invented to mean something other than it's original definition anyway. EQ adds a few more problems to that loose definition by defining the concept of where certain spells can function. Blackburrow for example is an outdoor zone while PAW is not. They are both brimming with gnolls and both mainly underground complexes. What makes them different? The fact that one has an area open to the sky while the other does not?

"Dungeon" is just too lose a term to use in EQ. Idoor/outdoor or wilderness/man-made are more accurate terms to use. Dungeon just doesn't mean anything specific enough to describe any zone on EQ.

Amber, a dungeon in EQ is a tagged area. Other tags are outdoors and cities. They are not mutually exclusive, as is evidenced by Karnor's Castle in Kunark...the druid Wind spells work there (which means has dungeon flag) yet SoW also works (which means has outdoor flag).

Dungeons have a better xp modifier than outdoor zones in general.

Melee can be bound in cities.

Outdoors are needed for some spells (Harmony, SoW, druid stuns (sans fury of air))
