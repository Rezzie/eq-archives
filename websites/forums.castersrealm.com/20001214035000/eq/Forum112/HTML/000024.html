Before you spend much more time playing your iksar sk, let me tell you a few things I've picked up.

1. They can't wear any form of plate that I've seen, including darkforge, umbral (plane armor), blood ember (post plane armor), or even rubicite. They do have a higher inate AC due to their scales, but that does not make up for the stats from umbral, and I don't see how it could even touch the massive ac from the higher quality kunark armor.

2. They lack STR and STA.....say what you want but those are THE KEY stats for warriors and hybrids. HP gain per level is based upon your natural STA, low STA, low HP at the top end. Add that to the fact that they can't wear plate....low HP + low AC = SK who can't tank. The biggest part of a hybrids job is to tank, if you can't do that.....well, you aren't of much use to your party.

3. Large races can NOT be stunned from the front, ie a mob bashes you, you are stunned, a mob gets some free hits on you while you are powerless to attack back, you are unstunned. This may not seem like a big deal, but consider fighting a mob that stuns you 10 times durring a fight, at 2 seconds a stun, thats 20 seconds that you are just standing there not attacking. Compare that to an Ogre or Troll, you are attacking 20 seconds more a fight. More attacks, more damage. Small races do not make good tanks.

4. Small races also do not get Slam. If you want to slam, that means you have to use a shield and bash, which means you have to use a lower damage 1h weapon (until you get to 50+ and get one of the new SK maces). So you either use a shield so you can bash and lower your over all damage, or use a 2h and not slam. Slam will interupt a caster 100% of the time (when it hits), slam will also keep the monster stunned and not attacking for several seconds during the course of the fight.

I have a lvl 48 DE warrior, and a lvl 45 Ogre SK, so I feel I can speak with some authority on Large vs Small melee types. 3 & 4 may not seem like much of an advantage, but they really do make a difference at higher levels. When I got bored with my warrior I made a DE SK, played until about lvl 10 or 11, then tried an Ogre SK. While the DE had trouble soloing even 1 blue, the Ogre could mow thru 2 yellows before having to sit and heal.....deleted wimpy little SK, never regreted it. So if you still think size doesn't matter....make a large SK and play him for a few levels, you will see the difference.
