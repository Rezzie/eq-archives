Yes, they get you to the CURRENT cap.

What part of this are you having trouble grasping?

As such, there will be a reason to cast the higher level speed spells on players.

A little more clarification here, until RoK, there has always been a second factor on haste effects to consider:

In addition to the absolute percentage of haste, there is a minimum weapon speed cap, set at 14.

In essence, haste is capped at 175% of normal attack speed, OR 14 effective delay, whichever comes first.

A 21 delay weapon bangs into that 14 delay cap pretty fast, where a 50 delay weapon does not, and can take full advantage of the spell/item effects.

Even though there are a few weapons in the game that have delay lower than 14, it's hardcoded to make them swing no faster than once every 1.4 seconds.

This too was intentional, to both limit the absolute effectiveness of 1h weapons, and to add value to the 2h weapons, and giving you a greater range of tactical choices to make.

Expansion will change this minimum delay as well, for some classes.

Nice work, Tudamorf.

However, some people are claiming this is not a bug. It is obviously a bug. There are two sane ways to combine haste effects and that isn't either of them.

I am assuming that the haste values are stored internally as percentage multipliers (125 for a 25% increase in speed).

If you were going to combine a 25% increase with a 20% increase, the reasonable ways to combine would be addition:

125 + 120 - 100 = 145 for a 45% increase

or multiplication:

125 * 120 / 100 = 150 for a 50% increase

You'll notice both of these include a normalization factor. I am betting that the guys at Verant failed to normalize the haste value and ended up with:

125 + 120 = 245 or 125 * 120 = 15000

Then they failed to check their math to figure out why the tanks were hitting so darn fast and decided they needed a haste cap. Hopefully they will fix their math and remove the cap some day.

It all makes sense if they are adding removed delays, not rates.

Making Sense of it all:


               Observed Haste  %Delay Removed
Augmentation:             26%             21%
Alacrity:                 40%             29%
Celerity:                 50%             33%
SLTW:                     61%             38%
FBSS:                     22%             18%

Adding Augmentation+FBSS = 26% delay removed + 18% delay removed = 44% delay removed.

1/(1-0.44) = 1.785, or 78.5% haste by removing 44% of the delay, which is above the cap.

The reason 2 different haste values are seen for the 2 weapons would be due to rounding. For example, if we are trying to remove 21% of the delay (augmentation) from the 21 delay weapon, that would put the delay at 16.59. Rounded up to 17 delay, we have only 19.04% of the delay removed due to rounding, or 23.53% haste.

What this means for expansion:

Alacrity + FBSS = -29% delay - 18% delay = -47% delay = 88.7% haste
Celerity + FBSS = -33% delay - 18% delay = -51% delay = 104% haste
SLTW + FBSS = -38% delay - 18% delay = -56% delay = 127.3% haste

If this is right, I would wager that even Celerity+FBSS will be above all expansion caps, with Augment (expansion upgrade to Augmentation) becoming the optimum haste buf.
