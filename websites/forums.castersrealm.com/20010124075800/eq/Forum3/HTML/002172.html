A post I made on whineplay follows, which gives some simple, realistic solutions to the balance issues, rather than these ridiculous spells they're trying to add:

During the design of RoK, Verant clearly overestimated the amount of improvement Warriors needed and greatly overpowered them with respect to hybrids. The result since then has been that most high level hybrids have either quit playing or changed classes.
I have heard that Verant is going to look at class balance on last time before locking up classes forever, so I thought I'd take a stab at my ideas for making high-level hybrids viable again.

I am posting this here, rather than in the Hybrids forum where it belongs, because Verant has NEVER replied to a single thread in the Hybrids forum (thanks guys!).

Paladins/SK: Offense and Weapon Skills raised 10 to 235
Ranger: archery raised to max (252?)
Paladin/SK: defense raised 10 to 240
Ranger: defense raised 30 to 230
Paladin/SK: bash raised 10 to 210
Ranger: kick raised 5 to 210
Paladin/SK: parry/dodge raised to match Rangers
Paladin/SK/Ranger: capable of critical hits with primary hand weapons
Ranger: significantly better (Epic class) bows added
Paladin/SK: significantly better shields and 1-handers (Epic class) added
Paladin/SK: trade in 1 discipline for a defensive (always riposte or parry or dodge) discipline

Would the very fabric of EQ be forever warped if these changes were implemented? No. Would nobody ever create another Warrior, a class that would still have the best offense, defense, armor, weapons, and disciplines? No. Would high level guilds overflowing with hybrids start dominating the best mobs in the game? Hahahaha, of course not (even if all the hybrids who have quit playing since RoK was released returned to EQ).

Would hybrids feel a little better about themselves and their fighting abilities? Most definitely. Would hybrids finally be returned to their status of being almost as effective in melee as Warriors, as they were before RoK was released? Yes, at last. Would most of the whining you are hearing from hybrids stop? Yes, I believe so. Would high level groups welcome hybrids at last as worthwhile contributors against the very toughest mobs in the game, in the toughest zones? Yes, thankfully.

The ball is in your court, Verant. Your loyal hybrid-loving customers, the people who bought EQ so they could play a noble Paladin, a great Ranger, or an insidiously powerful Shadow Knight, and who have stuck with their beloved characters throughout your many, many improvements to other melee classes, are awaiting your response.
