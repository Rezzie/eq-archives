>>>> you can't put many skill point into it because you automatically have a skill equal to your level when you put your first point into it (this excludes trade skills/weapon skills since you start with 0 points and don't need to put points into it) <<<<

I've heard this said before, but I beleive this assessment is incorrect.

There's a subtle, but important difference between what youve said and how it works. When you put your first training point into a skill you get a skill equal to, not your level, but rather, the level you acquire the skill at.

For instance, you get Research at level 16; when you first put a training point into reasearch you get a skill of 16. At level 17, when you first put a training point into reasearch, you get a skill of 16. At level 40, when you first put a training point into reasearch, you get a skill of 16.

I could be wrong, it's not something you can easily and repeatedly test, but I haven't seen anything to contradict this understanding since I heard about this summary of the system, and also, it makes alot more sense than the alternative.

Summoned and charmed pets take 50% of the experience if they do over 50% of the damage. Always.

The thing with enchanters is we have mezes and memory blurs. Mezes have a blur component. If you are mezzing a target your pet is beating on you will sometimes also be blurring the target. This leads to the belief that a 'killing' blow is all that is needed to get full experience. Or leads to the belief that our animations don't take 50% of experience.

You must always outdamage your pet to receive full experience. Use blur/nuke or simply nuke enough to make sure you have done over 50% of the damage.
