Very few of the posts here are about balancing enchanters (either balance vs other classes or balance vs environment). Rather they're about things we'd like to see fixed or improved... things we don't want to be forgotten before the big freeze. That's good too, but let's try to distinguish between those two issues. My list includes most of the ideas from this thread, as well as my own.

Enchanters are, on the whole, pretty well balanced, and are even in demand for groups (but not so much at lower levels). But remember... enchanters die more often than any other class and level slower than most. Don't go thinking everything's just fine. Still... almost all of the changes suggested are to make things more convenient, not to make enchanters more powerful in any real sense.


Five actual balance issues:

The Tashania line should have less taunt and more effect. Unnerf these spells enough so that enchanters can reduce MR more than shamans. Compare both the taunt and the effect to the shaman spells... I dare ya. This has special importance on PvP servers.

Too many high level creatures(53+) are immune to charm and mez, especially in Velious.

Our pet spells at 34 and 39 are too weak. The pets at other levels may or may not be balanced, but these two are worse than the rest.

The ability to both give and take mana from groupmates. It seems like some class ought to be able to 'heal' mana in the same sense that clerics heal health. Currently necromancers do that better than enchanters... just seems wrong.

Enchanters need armor with more AC (and HP). We need it more than necros need flayed skin armor (and certain other items with AC tuned to shadowknights but still usable by necros).


Four spell lines that suck:

Lull line. It may have been too powerful in beta, but it's too weak now. Enchanters, being the only class with an uncommandable pet, will find pulling almost impossible unless they play without their pet. So an enchanter already has to make a tradeoff to even use these spells. Perhaps that gives you a little leeway to make these spells more useful, without considering it unbalancing?

Whirl/Dyn's/Largarn's. Once upon a time (long ago) these were found to be overpowered. So they were made essentially useless. Isn't there anything in between? Maybe you could replace these with the cleric's line of stun spells. Clerics actually complain about their stuns, but it'd still be better than this line of spells.

Taper Enchantment. Not the whole line, just this one particular spell. Its only purpose it to confuse the young enchanters. Don't improve it, don't fix it, don't replace it, just delete it from the game. Please.

Weakness spell lines. They just aren't powerful enough. When we talk about stat buffs and debuffs we always end up talking about shamans more than enchanters, and that's only proper. But still... these spells are too weak as is. Languid Pace and the other attack-slowing spells are so much better.


Two things still missing:

The ability to create (and perhaps recharge) magic weapons and armor and wands and such.

More illusions. Many ideas on this... I'd particularly like to be able to choose which face I get (and perhaps what type of armor I seem to have) when I illusion to one of the player races.


Twelve things that just don't work quite right:

Higher level mez spells should overwrite the lower level mesmerizes (especially the AE mesmerization).

Illusion spells shouldn't fade on linkdeath. I know it's a longshot, but maybe the fix to the delayed-faction-when-zoning bug will open an opportunity to fix this too.

Charm spells should have a minimum duration. This one little change might transform charm from a soloing spell to a grouping spell. Really consider this.

Make charm more group-friendly 2: Not to make the spell more powerful per se, just a little more convenient. How many seconds does a pet class have to wait after issuing /pet back off before he can issue the next /pet attack command? It should take just as long for a charmed mob to get its bearings when charm wears off.

Make charm more group-friendly 3: A visible indication that a mob is charmed... perhaps the name above its head should change to a different color.

Yet another charm change: There should be a /pet command that ends the charm, so we can stop casting invisibility on ourselves to
break charms.

Reoccuring Amnesia spell: Doesn't reoccur.

Tiny daggers still aren't sold at the magic guild(s) in Qeynos.

It's too hard to target particular creatures when there are several crowded together. We need more targeting features. For a start, perhaps you could show the _01 number at the end of a mob name so we know which one we're aimed it. This actually is an enchanter issue... everyone else can use /assist.

Religion faction adjustment can never be hidden, not even by illusion. Seems like it should be possible... then maybe enchanters wouldn't have to be 99% agnostic.

Faction hits on some PvP servers make certain very important enchanter quests impossible... fix the quests.

On PvP servers illusions drop when casting an offensive spell. Fix the bug and remove this 'temporary solution'.
