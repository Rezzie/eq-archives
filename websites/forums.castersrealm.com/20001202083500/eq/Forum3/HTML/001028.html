I think someone (a software engineer) needs to take a look at the code. All evidence points to the following for melee taunting (talking about spells, the taunt skill, or other parts is not really directly relevant).

each time a player swings:
hateinc = potinc + actinc
where
hateinc = increase in that players hate rating on the list
potinc = amount based on potential damage done (misses count here)
actinc = amount based on actual damage done

Now actinc works just as Abashi states, bit the problem is potinc. There is nothing wrong with using potential damage to affect hate, but the amount it does seems to be wrong. They should try something like:

hateinc = (50% potinc) + actinc

to reduce the taunting factor of potential damage. As it currently stands, the potential damage factor is completely washing out the amount due to actual damage. This is probably seen most at the highest levels where players miss a lot more, as their skills are capped comparatively more compared to the mobs they are fighting.

In addition, there is a bug in general which is easily seen with large groups (say 18 people) fighting one mob. It seems the size of the damage done array is smaller than this, and of fixed size. This causes the group to get the exp to have VERY LITTLE to do with who did the most damage, but much more to do with which group got the most melee hits in at the end. A group with a lot of fast attackers will get the EXP every time, even when another group nuked for 80% of the damage. This bug MAY be affecting the problem with too fast weapons taunting too much, seeing as they are both caused byt fast weapons having an uninteded effect.
