I've played an enchanter, and loved it. I've played a necro, and loved it. I've played a shaman, and liked it.

Necros are excellent at soloing. Their entire spell arsenal is designed to maximize their damage in long, drawn-out solo battles. They have other spells that can help when they're grouping, too.

Enchanters and Necros are apples and oranges, they have vastly different playstyles. The "badass necro" isn't playing a necro like a necro. The "egotistical enchanter" isn't playing an enchanter the way they're supposed to be played.

Well I guess I'll add my 2 cents. I have played both classes and have to say they're both fun, but they require different outlooks on what you expect from EQ to enjoy fully. I truthfully can't think of 2 classes more completely opposite in terms of playstyle. It's true what someone said, I log my ench (54) on and within 5 minutes I usually get invited via /tell (often from someone I don't know) to go to Sebilis/Fear/Hate/Chardok/etc. My necro has a tough time getting in groups anywhere. Soloing? It's not even close. At 54 I have trouble soloing anything over level 44, and even then it's lousy exp due to downtime and expensive since I'm runing my pet two or three times per fight. Just because enchanters "can" solo doesn't mean they should or do at high levels. And as for charm soloing, please spare me :P Yeah I can do it successfully maybe 20 times in a row, but that 21st fight everything goes wrong, I die and am right back to where I started.

Now, on to the nitty gritty of each class. Enchanters are not an easy class to play well. It takes quick thinking, the ability not to panic in the face of extreme odds, the acceptance that you will often be the first to die, and the pressure of knowing that often you will be the difference in whether your group lives or dies. I've grouped in Karnor where our impatient monk pulled the whole skel tower on us at once when I was 50% mana and despite holding 7 of 8 mobs mezzed long enough for us to get them down to the last two mobs, I ran OOM and four of us died. Result? Grumbles from my groupmates and the implication that I'd got them all killed. This kind of thing is rare but be prepared that the pressure is on you to keep your group alive in the most heinous situations, and not everyone likes that kind of "fun" (though I do). If this appeals to you, you will love playing an enchanter.

Necros are far "easier" to play, especially solo, and therefore might appeal to someone who doesn't like having a million things to do during a big fight. I do get annoyed sometimes at how overlooked we are in group situations, but fortunately I have great friends and a great guild I can group with anytime. My advice if you want to group as a necro is to steadily make friends as you level up and join a guild. If you're discouraged that groups of 5 aren't inviting you, find a rogue to partner with. Rogues love grouping with necs because it's backstab paradise and they can't solo. No rogue around? Find a nice tank to help your pet hack down your victims. There are always options like this. Yes, you will find it tough getting in groups later that are going after the phat l3wts, but this is where getting in a guild early on and making friends (and showing them what necs can do) will help a great deal.

One other thing, you will level your necro to 50 in easily half the time it will take your enchanter, if that matters to you. Necros are masters of soloing not just because of our offensive power, but because we have very little downtime thanks to our incredible line of mana regen spells. Personally I enjoy both classes depending on my mood at the time.
