You guys think Chamelot looks cool? Well, you'll think Shadowbane is a gift from God then. Shadowbane is roughly the same general idea as Chamelot but here's the twist,(and its a great one), the players run and defend the cities/castles. Huge guilds are formed by players, and these guilds, if powerful enough, control cities, castles, and the strongest ones control even empires stretching across the giant virtual landscapes. The "job" system is also similiar to Chamelot, in that you can make changes as you progress. You start out as a healer, fighter, thief, or mage and then pick a more specific catagory (paladin, warlock, warrior, bard,assassin, etc.). And on top of that, there's even further specialties to choose from. You can choose 3 specialties or none. The more specialties you pick up, the less you specialize in each one. And listen to this list of just a few of the specialties. Shapeshifter (wolf, bear,or rat.), dueler, swordancer(specializes in duel wielding), wurmslayer, giant slayer(dwarf only), undead slayer(yes, there's vampires!!!), bounty hunter, gladiator, and the list goes on and on. The shapeshifters by the way are all different, wolf is bad, bear is good, rat is for thieves that wanna sneak up on someone to slit their throat. Its got a slighty darker look than most online RPGs (EQ, UO), and places a larger emphasis on waging war instead of small squirmishes between 6 person groups. Generals command armies and large amounts of people either fight on the open field or siege other guild's castles. There's even siege gear like giant catapaults. Its all PvP, but more precautions are taken to keep low level players from being PKed by high levels. Its also targeted towards fighting other clans for experience than computer controlled MoBs (none of that lame AI). The graphics look more detailed and less cartoonish. Besides from the run of the mill races to choose from (elves, barbarians, dark elves, dwarves, etc.) some more exotic races are thrown in and a few may only be played after reaching certain levels with the prementioned races. Minotaurs, Centaurs, and Aviaks are a few that can be played after compleating the restrictions. The guilds and their leaders set the prices that the merchants sell goods in the city, and expansion and world conquest is a major goal.I've seen some screenshots and let me tell you, they look awesome, huge see monsters, giant temples, dissappearing assassins taking out guards on the look out towers of castle walls. I suggest everyone checks out Shadowbane's website at Shadowbane.com or use the link at the bottom of Castersrealms main page. SB also utilizes roleplaying quite a bit more than EQ. GMs control characters that tell story line if you happen to be around when they show up. Some control petty thieves gossiping, merchants doing the same, or legendary warriors that have made a name for themselves. It looks epic!

I gave up EQ today after 47 Levels. All the fun's gone out of it, and I've never been one to lust lewt or exp. Its going to be a 9 month break from online gaming, then I'll try Shadowbane.

Now there's a game that, at least in philosophy, is the antethesis of everything I hate in EQ. The main things I like about it are:
1) You can mould your own character. How powerful (or useless) he is depends on your choices.
2) You don't need to chase experience. Once you reach lev 20 (estimated time = 1 month for the average gamer) you will be useful to the guild. Above this level, there are exponentially diminishing returns for advancement.
3) Skill is the most important factor in success. Level difference and equipment run a distant second.
4) Because of the above reasons there is little incentive to camp exp or loot. You play for fun/challenge.
