Here's the real thing about twinking -- it's basically a kind of power-levelling, and causes the same kind of problem: the twinkers end up stealing from the other players in the area.

How's that? Verant tried, i'd like to think, to balance the areas of a zone to match the characters expected to fight there. They took into account downtime (medding & heals), and populated the zone accordingly.

Now comes some twinked character. Inordinately more powerful gear means less downtime and less medding, means they can kill more often, more quickly, than a non-twinked player.

In any halfway crowded zone (and any place you would notice a twink is [1] low level and so [2] crowded), the twink will advance more quickly (which is the point of PL and Twinking). That means they'll be taking more than their expected share of the MOBs, because they're not experiencing the difficulties Verant designed into the area for the regular players. Has the same effect as a character being powerleveled, and that is that twinkers can basically gut an area, leaving nothing for the other players.

It's like fishing with dynamite; you may have fished the day before with worms, but that doesn't give you the right today to blow it all up because you feel you've "paid your dues".

'Course, Verant could have avoided this all by placing level limits on items like everyone yelled for at the beginning of the public release. They talked about how unfair that was; will be interesting to see just what they've learned by the release of EQ 2.
It also would have helped if you could have had only one character per server.

How many Verant designers does it take to change a lightbulb? None -- it's working as intended!

BTW, anyone else notice that once the threat from Diablo II was over, we lost that cool Saturn-like object in the night sky. Guess we can expect it back around the time of Baldur's Gate II?
