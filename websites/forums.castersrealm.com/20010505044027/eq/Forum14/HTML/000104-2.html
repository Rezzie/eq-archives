In my questing to be removed from "Newbie Poster" status on this board, I thought I'd ask a quick question... (okay so its not so quick =P)

Why is it that many enchanters are concerned with raising their AGI after CHA and INT hit the 200 mark?

Most of the melee websites are filled with posts of people dumping their AGI and DEX equipment for AC, STR, and Resist items. Verant has publicly stated that attributes like AGI and DEX only factor in skill gain, not skill execution.

In other words, having a high AGI would help you learn how to dodge faster (combined with INT as always) But, once your skill was capped, AGI has no role whatsoever in wether you dodge or not.

I've played around with AGI and AC on my 32nd level rogue character and there is NO contest. My rogue gets hit FAR less often after I swapped out his AGI equipment for AC. He dodges the same amount, but gets hit less and for less damage. The safehouse rogues agree with these findings.

Granted a higher AGI will give you a few extra AC points, but putting on an AC item gives a much more dramatic increase. For example (and I'm not using hard numbers here, just estimates) putting on a Gold BP Choker (+7 AGI, +7 DEX) might give you an extra 3 points to your AC, while puting on a Plat Topaz Necklace (5 AC, 13 Save vs Disease) would raise your AC by 10 or more AND give you a nice boost to your disease resistance.

So, what am I missing here? If I had to increase a stat other than CHR and INT, I'd probably go with STR (carry more) or STAM (more hps to survive with... again some of our jewelry would give more straight HPs than the STAM item might)

But if I'm worried about getting hit, I'd go with AC items (Azure Sleeves instead of the meager INT bonus of the Gator Arms for example)

If I'm worried about getting hit with magic, I'd opt for the Resist/AC jewelry.

But why AGI?

While like most things i have seen no hard numbers to back this up, it seems from my experience, that agi helps you not get hit. I know verant says that it has nothing to do with skill execution, and i know that it doesn't make much of a difference in the number called AC. Verant has also stated though that AC is a combination of two numbers, damage avoidance and damage mitigation. Using this information, it is possible that the two numbers end up being quite different in size, and when shown to us are simply added. (THEORY WARNING) For example, if my avoidance is 5 and my mitigation is 50, if increase mitigation by 20% it will show my AC as 60 instead of 55, but if i increase avoidance by 20%, my AC would show as 56. If this theory holds true, then adding agility could make a subsantial increase in avoidance ability without having a proportional effect on AC as it is shown. Keep in mind this is my theory, but it does fit in with everything verant has said publicly that i have seen.
Personally, if i could, i would drop all my mitigation points for avoidance (AC items for AGI) we're casters, how often do you get hit for less than max damage anyway?
