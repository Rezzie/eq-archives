There are quiet a few methods of kiting. It's up to you which you want to use, some of these is more based off of preference, then a real huge change in damage to mana ratio.

The first method (and probably the most popular) is to snare,dot,dd(repeatedly), then either meditate for more mana, or damage shield and go to melee to finish off. The DoT is optional, some druids don't like to use it because of the 60% (per tick)drop to damage they do, if the critter is running (or walking) toward you. Personally, I still DoT. Even with the 60% mana drop the spells are still pretty mana efficient, and once it's in effect the mob can not resist it.

Another method only works if you can fear the mob. If you're soloing, this will work on animal mobs only (because of our fear vs. animal spells). the pattern goes snare, fear, DoT, and DD/meditate. The fear will cause the animal to run from you, making the DoT do 100% damage per tick, while the snare keeps it from running far. Basically it's a reversed kite. Instead of us doing the running they are.

The final method (I'm going to list) is not as popular as the other 2. It's probably more potent to use this at lower levels (16ish and below). Basically goes Root, DoT/DD meditate, then damage shield when almost out of mana, and go to melee to finish off the MoB. The main reason for it's lack of popularity is that our Ensnaring Roots Line of spells have a tendancy to wear off at the worst of times. (especially if you are casting DD's at the critter) In my opinion the root seems to last longer if you DoT once, and meditate until it wears off, then reroot, and reDoT. DD spells have a tendacy to knock the critter out of the root, while DoT continues to do damage, but not kick off the root.

I really hope this helps out you younger Druids out there. :-)

I think there is some confusion here. The question asks about rain spells. Those are not the spells used in quad kiteing (Lightening Blast is the spell in question here). Rain spells work as follows. When you cast them everything in their area of effect gets hit for damage. Every tick there is another 'wave' of damage that strikes in the same place. Rain spells have either three or four waves of damage typicaly.

The problem with this line of spells is that unless rooted, the mobs try to run out from underneath the rain spell. Usualy you only get one or two of the waves of damage in makeing these spells horribly inefficient. They also tend to be graphicaly intensive and can cause lag.

As a result, they tend not to be used much. The part about casters pets is that they count as a monster to the spell and are hit by it, useing up one of the attacks the spell could have made. On top of this you end up injuring pets that others in your party control. This renders them even less mana efficient.

I never use my rain spells and have them in my book only in case Verant fixes the spells some day, and for completeness sake.

"I don't see many people saying that AoE spells are crappy... they are saying that the AoE RAIN spells are crappy.

Look at the facts about them...
A rain spell will hit 4 targets maximum.
It will hit 1 target up to 4 times
It will hit 2 targets twice
If 3 or 4 targets, it hits only once
If you have party members or pets in the effects range, they count against number of targets effected.
So, in a nutshell, this spell is less mana efficient in a group situation.

So now, lets take a look at other alternatives...

Kiting - Target (s) gets hit once before moving out of effect of spell. (So you need to kite 4 mobs at once to make it effective)

Rooting - Nope to this one, each time it gets hit for a wave, there is a chance that it breaks root which again it only takes partial damage from the spell as it moves out of the AoE. (Which root and DOT is so much more mana efficient because you can meditate while it dies as well)

So, with all the information, there is only 1 instance where these types of spells are more mana efficient and that is doing something which Verant never anticipated druid doing... and don't want us doing (hence the DOT nerf on moving targets)
