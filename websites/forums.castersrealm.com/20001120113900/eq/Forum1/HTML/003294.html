The cleric is a must. The enchanter is a must. The warrior is good. The druid, however, can be swapped out for something else. Human Warrior
Half Elf Warrior
High Elf Enchanter (me)
Halfling Cleric
Iksar Warrior

The clerics sole job is to heal. I keep him breezed and he does an excellent job. The cleric keeps the tanks buffed and I make sure we don't get overwhelmed by mobs (mezz, etc.). We're all in our low 20's and we had no trouble running up and down the hallways in the sarnak fortress in LOIO. At that level, with 5 of us in a group, we average at or above a bubble of exp an hour with good loot... And most importantly, ITS FUN.

So our group, 3 tanks (hasted up), A cleric and a Chanter is a pretty damn good one.

To answer your question though, you may want another tank, or maybe a mage who can summon a strong pet - because when 3 or 4 mobs run up to you, your casters will most likely die. I hope your friend is a good enchanter.

Most importantly, play something that you'll have fun with!

In my opinion you've got probably the best group.

1) Chanter
If you want to be self sustainable you have to have a chanter, at later levels this makes so many places accessible by a small group that would otherwise take a full well balanced group if not more. his haste buffs, slow debuffs and clarity type spells also mean your tank(s) will be more efficient and your casters have more mana.

2) Cleric
The best healers in the game. Its worth noting that druids/shamans rival Clerics at lower levels (upto 34). However past this, and i'm assuming your looking at long term a cleric is simply the best healer in the game, he gets great HP buffs (well its his speciality right ?)

3) Druid
Teleports, Sow, Nukes, Dots and damage shields, Resist buffs. You have your enchanter to slow/haste your single tank, the only class _in the game_ to out nuke a druid with a DD spell at very high lvls is a wizzy, you have to ask yourself - do you want to loose your SoW, damage shields and secondary healer abilities + Dots for a little extra nuking power ? Oh btw, Snare is one of the greatest spells in the game, make sure whatever you do - you don't miss this spell.
If you take a shaman, you loose your teleports, if you take a wizard, you loose sow and gain better nukes.

4)warrior - as you're only going to have one tank, don't pick a paly/SK - there damage output isn't on par with a warrior/rogue or ranger. A rogue would have a problem in a single tank group as it will be awkward for him to get his backstabs in, a ranger will loose ac but would have spells - however there is really no need for a ranger if your having a druid as his spells are so much better. You could maybe choose a monk over a warrior, but in my opinion if you're having one tank you've already picked the best class.

At the end of the day i'm assuming all of you have played EQ enough to know a classes benefits and downsides (those that have them), i'd recommend you play something that appeals to you all so you'll not get bored by the time you all reach your first hell level.
With this combo the only people you will need to invite into your group are another 2 tanks, or maybe a caster with a pet if you can't find a real tank.

Historically, the Paladin class added a defensive bonus to their group by just being in the group. (this is an example, because I know mostly about paladins first hand)

In EQ there is no reason to pick a Hybrid over a warrior in a group when there is a "parent" class already there. The Hybrid adds no more defense to the group than a warrior (especially Ranger), the warrior actually adds more. Plus the Hybrid can rarely output the same damage as the warrior. The warrior gets ever-increasing resistance to magic, while the paladin gets no more disease resist (class specialty, paladins are supposed to be immune to disease much in the same way warriors are immune to magic); I don't know what the SK and Ranger resists are supposed to be, but I would venture them to be Poison and Fire, respectively.

I thought of a few possible solutions to the fact that there is no point to pick a pally/SK/ranger over a warrior.

Overall, they could make it so that the presence of a matching Hybrid (pally/cleric, SK/necro, ranger/Druid) increases the output, or mana ratio, of the 'parent' classes spells 200%; this would be explained as the Hybrids 'assisting' their fellow 'parent' class in their activities. I would tend to think that this would make Clerics/Necros/Druids overjoyed if they were 200% better when their fighting brethren were in the group.

The other possibilities I have thought of are as follows:

a) Paladin buffs stack with Cleric buffs; or give an automatic AC and HP bonus, that increases with level, to all group members to represent their superior leadership and defense tactics.

b) SK DoT's stack with necro's; or that SK's give an ATK rating bonus to the group to represent their love of death and destructive battle tactics

c) Rangers DoT's and buffs stack with Druids; or give an AC and HP bonus to the group in outdoor zones, on the level of the paladin's HP and AC, that does not stack with the Pally HP and AC bonus, to represent their superior woodsman and outdoor skills

This is true, roleplaying reasoning, according what the class is supposed to represent.

The reasons these would not be unbalancing are:

a) paladins would still suck at damage output and clerics would still be used for their awesome buffs; a group would choose a paladin for defense, but a warrior for their combo of AC and damage output or a monk for damage output and pulling ability

b) SK's would still suck at defense and Necros would still have the more powerful DoT's; a group would choose a Warrior for their combo of AC and extra HP, a monk for their *better* FD pulling and damage output

c) Rangers would still have suck AC and HP and druids would still have superior buffs and DoT's; a group would choose a warrior for his combo of AC and HP, and better weapon and ATK ratings, a monk for damage output and FD pulling.

As it stands right now, once you get a Cleric, Warrior, Enchanter/Necro, Shaman in the group, you just want to fill the two other slots with Warriors or Monks. I rarely see anyone say that a Paladin/SK/Ranger would add more to the group than a Warrior EVER would. Even in the topics in this thread NO-ONE has mentioned having a Paladin in their group, nor a Ranger (I think). SK got mentioned once to be a good taunter, but that was it.

I think it says something about supposed "class-balance" when no one would pick certain classes to perform any tasks within their groups.
