I'm sure it was an unpleasant surprise to attempt to pleasantly greet a GM only to be told that your name needed to be changed. Unfortunately, your name violates at least two of the naming standards. Yes, it's very frustrating that you reached such a high level before being caught, but even if you were level 60, it is still in violation of their policy.

quote:
10.Names containing titles within them, such as, but not limited to: The, Lord, Lady, Master,King, Knight, Sir, Father (e.g. Sirtallon, Lordeagle, Mothermaggy).

12.Popular and easily recognized names from existing media (e.g. Merlin, Gandalf, Belgerath,Tanis).

Since "father" was specifically excluded, "papa" seems to be a clear violation as well. Smerph is an obvious respelling of "smurf", the popular children's cartoon of several years ago.

Frustrating, absolutely, and I empathize with your situation. But a violation of the policy? That too.

People tend to forget that EQ is not a bunch of guys (and girls), sitting in the kitchen, throwing dice and reading AD&D books. This is a multi-million dollar company that has to cover all bases.

I know it seems a trivial issue but, believe me, it is not. The naming policy is a mixture of common sense AND protectivism. To keep continunity the 'Sirhavealots' and 'Lordbottomtush's' have to be somewhat controlled. Hey a few slip by, but in general people bide by the 'rules'. To protect itself (and its young customers), other names are FOBOTTEN! Names that MIGHT cause a legal problem due to copyright laws are nixed, and names that have obvious (and NOT so obvious) sexual, racial, or religious denotations AND conotations are also not allowed.

I am also sure that if 'Wells Fargo' wrote a letter to EQ complaining about Wella Fargo the banker (not that they would, after all it is free advertisement) the name would be changed immediately without ANY whining from EQ. They would do it and move on.

Frankly, if I were the head of a multi-million dollar coperation I would do what was ever necessary to protect myself against ANY chance of backlash over a players name.

Personally I have NO idea if anything is wrong with Papa Smerph (other than the obvious link to the cartoon show), but if a large enough group of, lets say, short people made a large enough noise, stating that it was derogatory, hey, I would have it changed, and keep the money rolling in.
