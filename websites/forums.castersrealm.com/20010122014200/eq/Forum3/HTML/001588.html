I personally have done an 18 hr camp. Over a period of days I have done 70hrs in one spot in the vain hope of the AC ring dropping in South Ro.

Rumour has it that you need to kill a number of mobs at certain times to "make" the Ancient Cyclops spawn. I have therefore spent days slaughtering green mobs to make this happen.

The only way to stop this is to make drops far far more random and NOT increase the spawn times. Increased spawn times do not work to stop camping. They DO increase conflict and kill stealing! Even if a spawn is measured in days people will still camp the spot in the hope that that item will spawn.

Ok I agree that the Ancient Cyclops has to drop the ring to make the quest hang together but why does he always have to pop in S Ro. Cyclops are found in a number of zones why cant this lost Ancient spawn there as well. The total number of times he spawns in any given period could be maintained however if he also spawns elsewhere then it would truely be random good fortune that you happened to be in the right place at the right time to kill him.

In dungeons even if you reduce the numbers of times a particular named mob spawns people will still sit at that spot for hours and even days in the chance that the mob will pop at some time.

The only cure is to change where and the way mobs spawn. In a dungeon for example Why should the Arch Magi ghoul alway pop at the same place in Lower Guk. If he popped in the corridor outside Sage for example you would kill off the whole idea of camping once and for all. It would also add significantly to the risk and unpredictability of an otherwise rather predictable game.

If you cant have a randomish spawn sites why not grant named mobs the ability and desire to gate off to another part of the dungeon prior to being engaged. If you even provided 3 or 4 spots to which the mob could gate to this would again kill off the whole idea of camping. The mob could then wander back to his/her orginal spawn site. After say 5 secs it would gate again. This would greatly increase the chance of a random encounter of a named mob in the corridors of a particular dungeon. It would mean that a party would have to respond to the shouts of glee and anguish as the Arch Magi popped in his spot but then gated out to Sage or Ass Sup to wander back through the corridors of Lower Guk.

All in all this would make for a much more dynamic game and put and end to camping once and for all.

I am sure that certain items/mobs would fail to meet the fairly easy solution that I have suggested to the problem of camping. However sometimes in life and EQ u may just have to sit and wait for an event to occur. Overall this idea would dramatically reduce camping and greatly improve the excitement of the game. Frankly increasing the spawn time to weeks will not work as someone out there will be prepared to sit for weeks if necessary to obtain that item that they really want. It also leads to major disputes as to who is camping a particular mob allegations of KS are common in S Ro this is as best irritating at worse it actually is detremental to the spirit of the game.

Verant this may take a little work and time to implement however it would improve the game by a quantum level. At the moment if you want a particular item the game it involves camping in a particular small room of a dungeon for hours or days. Frankly the game in some respects should be called EverCamp not EverQuest.
