In my experience the Mesmerise line almost invariably wiped the hate list BUT

1) It has a very high aggro effect on the friends of the mob you mes.

2) The mem-blur comes in when the mes first sticks and thereafter the mob builds up a hate list.

3) Many sorts of mobs share their hatelists around -thus making one dislike you will make others dislike you as much. (I think they pick up the hatelists of nearby friends when they come out of the mes)


The upshot of all this is that when you have more than one mob to mes they will pass their hate back and forth then all come and kill you.

IF however you memblur them all after you mes them then they will finally forget you.

(alternatively you can string them out with mes and you will be ok -mes one; move away and mes the other; move again and remes the first when it comes for you)

A mesmerized NPC will build up hate, even after a memblur is applied, if you are close enough to it.

Let's assume the following:

Two dark elven guards, on the same faction, standing next to each other.

You mesmerize one. The other attacks you. You root and nuke it next to the mesmerized one. The mesmerized one, when mez breaks, will come and kill you.

You can observe this at will. Go to a dungeon where you're not KOS. For me this would be Karnor's. Bring two of the big Drolvargs. Mez and memblur one while your party fights the other close by. Con the mezzed one to ensure it is no longer mad at you. (shouldn't be conning threatening).

Now, do something bad to the one your party is fighting (such as casting Tashania on it).

Now con the mezzed, memblurred NPC. It'll be mad at you again.

This doesn't happen if the mezzed NPC is far enough away from the unmezzed NPC before you start to attack it.

Mesmerize might reduce an NPC's "awareness" radius to zero for its duration, which would mean you could walk past a kos, but mezzed, NPC, without fear of it remembering you when mez breaks. But that'd have to be tested.
