Regarding Cazic Thule:

The experience/risk is not bad. The key is not camping in the courtyard. Experience does not slow down at 29 it actually picks up. I stayed there until about halfway through 35, and I still could have stayed for a few more levels at least, but I wanted some new scenery. There are a lot of areas there:
the Throne room, 4C(top and bottom), Sewers(gators), Avatar of fear, Silvered Temple...

All of those will take you well into your mid 30's, and quickly. With an enchanter in the group, there is far less down time, and of course far fewer trains. With good groups you can have non-stop pulling, and the experience just rolls in. I spent 8 levels there, and died maybe 3 times. Just be wary of groups that don't know how to group with an enchanter. I found that most there did, but you get occasional ones that may need educating. If for some reason they don't want to listen, leave and join another group. People love Enchanters there, you will always find another group.

If you camp in the courtyard you may get hit with a lot of trains from other areas. It is best to avoid that area, and the area near the zone. A lot of the younger groups tend to camp the CY because they can pull the lower level lizzies from the maze, and it's close to the zone, but that's where all the trains will converge.

I have found that it's better to get into groups where the average person is a little higher than you, or at the very least even. If you get into groups where most of the people are lower level, experience will be much slower, and you will have longer downtimes.

I agree with going to Cazic Thule at 25+. I went there at around that level and stayed to 34. XP is fabulous IF you get into a good group that knows how to work well with a chanter. Most any of the camp spots are good, though I do not recommend doing the sewers as the corridor that you are forced to fight in is difficult to move around in, let alone mez. I don't recommend going to the Avatar of Fear camp until 34+, but you may think otherwise when you get a feel for the place.

Chanters are a welcome commodity in CT. While I was there, if there was an open spot, I was always invited in and could often pick and choose where I hunted.

Cazic Thule is also a good area to "cut your teeth" in the crowd control business. If you're new to mezzing multiple mobs and holding them for a long period of time, here is a great place to develop that skill.
