Non-Twink- I have not run into the problem you mentioned about having a hard time taunting mobs off twinks (my warrior is my primary so isnt twinked). Maybe you should try some faster delay weapons and hit your taunt button right before you are about to swing and/or kick. This will help some. For a great description of how taunt works see the copelands analyzer page (http://www.geocities.com/copelandeq/). While faster and more damage will surely raise the twinks taunt factor, your taunt button should more than make up for this.

This is just a side note and not really relevant, but the rogue in the situation you described in your last post wasnt playing the character properly if you were having a hard time taunting the MOB of him/her. They have a skill called evade, which causes them to be dropped lower on the hate list (this probably isnt the exact mechanism by which evade works, but the final result is the same).

In the end, if this is becoming a major problem in a group you are in, you can leave the group. On the other hand, if you want to stay in the group, explain the problem to them. All they have to do is turn autoattack off for a couple seconds and you will be able to taunt the MOB. Then they can start swinging again (a technique Bards have to learn early on with their singing skills because they have such a high taunt factor).
