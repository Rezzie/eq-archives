And the argument against that way of thinking is to point out that:

1. Game companies don't simply let anyone wander in without some consideration. How many players they will allow per server is a consideration. So is ultimate control over game content and how the game works to encourage/discourage certain actions.

2. You can only go on ad infinitum if the game company panders to the practice by adding more content rather than varying what they already have. You can't know everything in a totally dynamic game world. That's not possible to achieve but the point is that making a game world more dynamic will lessen how much you the player can possibly know. And making the world more dynamic is possible.

For example, degrading items that need to eventually be replaced. The truly powerful items would then be obtained by fewer people since the need to replace those mediocre items is paramount to participating at the same level of effectiveness. Items can be evolved and changed with almost not negative impact because they'd wear out anyway and it's not as if you suddenly find your weapon is something else forever - it wouldn't have lasted forever. New items can still be added but you wouldn't need as many so therefore they are potentially more interesting. That's three means of making the game world more dynamic for the price of one. Lots more small 'quirks' like that could be done to improve how you see the game world, how you behave in it and what you know outside of it. If something is more dynamic then the information about it is usually out of date and therefore useless. You play the game rather than pause to look up information elsewhere.

Ultimately the responsibility for how players 'ruin' a game is the responsibility of the company running that game. It's their fault it happens in the first place. Nope, no easy solutions to combatting this but they are there. After years of online gaming products being available we all know the pitfalls. You'd think game companies would not only know them but figure out ways of avoiding them. As a player you can blame other players - you don't know any better. Game companies (and anyone who does know better) can only blame themselves.
