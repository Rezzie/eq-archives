THE HISTORY

The beggar appears

A mad beggar appears in Freeport, shouting rants and lunacy. Saavedra pays him heed and is given a mysterious foretelling. She is told to "Lead" and to "Choose well". After the prophecy, the beggar goes completey mad and hurls himself to his death from atop the Theater of the Tranquil.

A journey to Halas

Saavedra and her group decide to go to Halas to enlist the aid of the shamans. After a perilous trip, where they were almost devoured in a Griffon swarm, they reach Halas and consult with Kaanen, elder seer of the shaman.

Kaanen consults the spirits, and is told that the beggar marks the coming of a dark time, where the world will be divided by the demon Bhjaad. Saavedra is named a 'Chosen' and is called to warn her people of the Sundering.
The beggar reappears to Mystykyl

The following day, the bard Mystykyl hears the voice of the beggar in her head. His rants are only slightly clearer this time, but he seems to know something of her future. He tells her to go to Halas. So, Mystykyl, accompanied by her husband Ursin, travels to Halas and consults with the seer Kaanen.

Kaanen shares with her the prophecy, and when the spirits are consulted, she too is named a 'Chosen'. With more questions than Kaanen can answer, Mystykyl departs in search of the beggar.
The naming in Rivervale

Mystykyl tracks the beggar to Rivervale, where he is about his usual ravings. Soon, Saavedra meets her there, and together, she and Mystykyl try to get the beggar to speak sense. Finally, the beggar agrees to answer three questions of each of the chosen.

Five chosen are named: Saavedra, a barbarian shaman; Mystykyl, a half-elven bard; Praxi, an erudite enchanter; Viyy, a halfling raider; and Tassader, an elven wizard.

The chosen are instructed by the beggar to warn people of The Sundering which is coming, and to call people to follow their cause. The beggar also speaks of the demon Bhjaad who walks the earth in green armor carrying a flaming sword. The demon, he says, will cause The Sundering. Before the beggar can answer more questions, he is taken by a bout of madness and drowns himself in the river.
Mystykyl speaks in Felwithe

Mystykyl immediately carries the news to Felwithe, where she addresses all who will listen. She speaks of the Sundering, and the prophecy that only a few from each family will be able to be carried into the new world. Many take up her message, and follow her cause.
Tassader speaks in Qeynos Tassader calls a meeting in Qeynos where he addresses a crowd gathered at the city gates. A throng of people gather to hear facts about the Sundering, hoping to still rumors that are being spread in the streets. Tassader speaks, and Viyy and Saavedra stand by his side, adding their support to his proclamations.
The Warbringer

At the close of Tassader's speech, a barbarian knight clad in blackened plate armor appears suddenly, shouting a challenge to those gathered around. He tells Tassader that soon a 'river of blood' will be spilled from the Chosen and their followers, and speaks of a coming war.

The Warbringer also proclaims the coming of the Begotten--five who have been called by the dark powers to stand against the Chosen and lay waste to the good races. At the end of this dark proclamation, the Warbringer vanishes.
Dhuratan and Ghuratan

Another shout is heart as the Warbringer disappears. A mighty human warrior named Ghuratan the Doombringer asks who will follow the Chosen. After a few bold answers from the crowd, Ghuratan announces that he is a Begotten and will soon be spilling the blood of the Chosen and their followers.

Suddenly, Ghuratan's twin brother, Dhuratan appears and challenges his evil brother to a duel, for Dhuratan is a paladin and serves the cause of good. As the two brothers swing mighty blows at one another, the onlookers begin to aid either side with healing and beneficial magics. But darkness would win this night, and soon Dhuratan is forced to flee.
Viyy speaks in Rivervale

The following day, Viyy addresses the halflings, or Tal'kein as he calls them, in Rivervale. He petetions them to the cause of the Chosen, so that the Begotten may be stopped. At Viyy's side is Verdura, a mighty druid of the Vale who has been named Rak'ak'Tal, or protector, of Viyy the Chosen. She stands next to Viyy in the form of a spirit-wolf, a challenge to any evil one who would seek to harm him.

At the close of Viyy's speech, many proclaim support for the cause of the chosen, kneeling and pledging to serve their cause.
The Beggar reappears

Once more, the beggar reappears, this time lurking around the cave entrance to Rivervale from Kithikor forest. The beggar ambushes the Chosen Praxi as he emerges from the cave, falling on Praxi from above. The beggar taunts Praxi into a duel. Praxi easily binds the beggar, and knocks him down after a few solid blows. The beggar crumples. Praxi, thinking the beggar dead, returns to Rivervale to rest and recover from the fight. The beggar lies motionless until Praxi leaves, and the gods announce that the beggar had beaten Praxi in a duel.

Mystykyl, who was nearby with Ursin, ran to see what happened, angered by the trickery of the beggar. The beggar was gloating of his victory, and on sight of Mystykyl, charged her with a raised pick-axe. Ursin stepped in swinging his broadswoard, and in a single stroke laid the beggar to rest, ending his maddening rant one more time.
The Chosen Rally

Jythri, his heart moved by the cause of the chosen, begins rallying the good people in Rivervale. Following the Chosen Saavedra, the group travels to Qeynos, after joining with another Chosen, Viyy, and his group at the Whitebridge. During the trip, a pacifist monk named Quietar follow the group, preaching against the coming violence. Jythri, with a bard's tongue, debates Quietar, challenging the goodly people to defend their homes, the innocent, and the cause of the just.

A large group of supporters rally outide Qeynos' gates, when Saavedra hears the voice of the spirits summoning her to Halas. Saavedra and most of the followers travel to Halas, where she again calls the shaman seer Kaanen to consult with the spirits. Kaanen has a vision and tells Saavedra and Viyy that the Begotten walk the land and prepare for battle. Kaanen sees a conflict on the sandy plains of Ro, and encourages the group to take heart and take up the call of the pure of heart in defending against the Begotten.

The Final Days of 3.5

The forces of the Chosen assembled on the plains of Ro, expecting the battle foretold of the seer Kaanen. Unfortunately, like many prophetic visions, Kaanen could not separate the pre-Sundering events from the Post-Sundering events to come. The war he saw will come, but it was not what was to happen before The Sundering.

The mad Beggar appeared and taunted the Chosen and their followers, finally revealing himself as the spawn of the Begotten. After a time of his ranting, Mystykyl, Viyy, and Saavedra destroyed the beggar, ending his madness.

The Begotten did not show. In their stead appeared the prophet Rezzak who had seen that the Begotten were sent (by who's orders are unknown) to seek an artifact of great power to use to eliminate the Chosen and their followers. It was said by him that one Begotten would come: Ghuratan to seek his brothers blood. Not long after Rezzak left it was he who did appeear--Ghuratan, the dark brother, and the leader of the Begotten. Again, he rained down curses on the Chosen, and demanded that they divulge the whereabouts of his brother Dhuratan. Mystykyl confronted Ghuratan asking what he would do if Dhuratan did not come. Ghuratan said that he would then slay the Chosen and their followers one by one until his brother was handed over.

Quietar, the pacifist monk, approached Ghuratan insisting that he end his violent tirade. Ghuratan would have no ear for the monk, and, to set and example to the rest of the assembled, slew Quietar. Quietar did not even lift his staff in defense as Ghuratan's killing blows fell on him.

With Quietar killed, Dhuratan appeared, not wishing any more harm upon his brother's enemies. Ghuratan and Dhuratan once more dueled, but this time, Dhuratan bested his brother with the help of those sympathetic to the cause of the Chosen. With his brother barely clinging to life, Dhuratan emplored him to fight the maddness that had befallen him. His pleas fell upon hate deafened ears. Dhuratan felled his brother. As he wept over his brother's dead body it sunddenly vanished! A short while later, still kneeling on the spot his brother had fallen, his sorrowed eyes came to meet the horizon. In the sun he could see the sillouette of a robed figure moving closer to the place where he knelt. The Wizard Vesuvian hailed "Dhuratan I would speak to you", and they did so speak... Vesuvian told Dhuratan that his brother was not lost... if he sought to save his brother he would journey to the Plane of Fear, and it is there that Dhuratan must go to rescue his brother from the evil that besets him.

Vesuvian would reveal one more dark truth--the Begotten knew of the death of their leader, and would also be going to the Plane of Fear to reclaim Ghuratan to their own. Dhuratan, his hope for his brother rekindled, resolved to beat the Begotten to the Plane of Fear and save his brother's soul.

Who will be the first to the Plane of fear? What will become of the Chosen after the Sundering? Will the Begotten recover their leader, or appoint one anew? All these questions will be answered in Chapter 2...
