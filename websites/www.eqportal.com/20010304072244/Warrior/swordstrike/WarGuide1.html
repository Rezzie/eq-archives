The warrior class is rather simple, but when played well it is very fun and popular. To understand how to play a warrior, one must first choose which race you wish to be… In popular opinon the large races make the best warriors, seeing how they have the lowest int and char, and highest str and stamina… The 3 large races are:

  • Ogres are the best warriors statistic wise, they have the most base stats. Ogres already have such a high str, and stam, they can devote almost all their training points for agility and dexterity (see important statistics) Ogres are at a disadvantage though because they can only wear LARGE armor, and are an evil race and thus limits their bindable cities.
  • Barbarians are the most popular large race warriors, because they are an accepted race, have high str and stamina, and have the best newbie area in the world.
  • Trolls have all right stats, but are not better than ogres, but are above barbarians, trolls also have a special regeneration ability, which allows them to gain 4hp instead of 1 like the other races, this is really the only reason to play a troll. This will greatly reduce your downtime after a battle, but then again you could just bring a cleric along : P
  • The other races will be added later…

The statistics warriors need are often overlooked, and thus can be a major disadvantage at higher levels. Verant highlights Strength and Stamina as the primary statistics of a warrior, but Agility and Dexterity are just as, if not more important than str and stam…

Strength directly effects the amount of damage that you deal when hitting a mob,  higher str higher damage… Str also directly effects your weight allowance.
Stamina directly effects your hitpoints, and chances of hitting, if it is low
Agility directly effects your armor class, as if you were dodging swipes…
Dexterity directly effects your speed of attack, and chance of hitting, it is VERY important, although often overlooked.

Many have argued that warriors are not the most exciting class to play, seeing as casters get a whole new set of spells every 6 levels, and hybrids (paladins, sks, rangers) get new abilities and spells, but in contrast, warriors are always in the front of the battle, and NEVER have to meditate! Warriors also get the glory of battle, seeing as the cowardly wizard is blasting the monster while the warrior is doing his/her best to battle the mob and keep it on him, rather than going off killing the cowardly caster.

 The downside of warriors is that you won’t be soloing much at later levels. Warriors will never be able to kill steal, and you always will have to beg for a teleport from your more privileged casting friends, who will think of you as a nuisance… On a side note, almost all the casters you will encounter never have played a melee class, thus they don’t understand the incredible distances that you have to cover to get from dungeon to dungeon. Once I was even told to learn how to teleport myself by an arrogant wizard who wasn’t so arrogant once he didn’t have a tank :-)

Traditionally warriors are the leaders of a group. They are the ones who brave the foes first. They are the first ones who take the damage, and often they are the first to die. A warrior must have faith in his party members. A warrior needs to have the courage to stand and fight until his healers get enough mana to throw him a heal. A warrior must have the heart to stand in there while the wizards get the mana to deal the death blow. As a result of this a warrior is often the one who makes an impression on the crowd.

Warriors aquire all the skills of a melee class, and get them around 5 levels before their hybrid cousins, who also don’t get as many, warriors skills:

Level 1: Kick, this skill will not be important for the larger races because Slam allows you to bash the monster, and stun them, also interrupting their spell, but the med and small races will love it, because it does not require a shield

Level 5: Bind wound, you will most likely only use this skill when your early on, when warriors are still soloing, lvl 1 – 10, but it is nice to help your friends heal, when there are no clerics nearby

Level 6: Dodge, this skill allows you to have a chance to dodge, and increases your armor class…

Level 6: Bash(if your not a large race), this is another damage dealer, like kick, and the 2 cannot be used at the same time, bash is superior to kick from its extra stun and interrupt, but for the smaller races it requires a shield… stick to kick unless your large

Level 10: Parry, another ac buffer, like dodge

Level 13/15 These are the warriors power levels. At level 13/15 you get your two most important skills: Double attack and duel wield. Level 13 gives dual wield and level 15 double attack. It is wise to save up most of your training points to pour into these two stats. Double attack allows you to hit twice per round. Duel wield allows you to have the option of using two one handed weapons and thus theoretically be able to get up to four attacks per round. These skills go up slowly and are not overly effective at first, but once they start gaining in skill you will notice a huge difference in effectiveness.

Level 25: Riposte, Another AC increaser, like Dodge and Parry

Level 35: Disarm, allows you to knock a weapon out of your opponents hands(although it very rarely works). This can accomplish two things. First off it can get you a nice weapon if you are lucky. Secondly a monster with a weapon hits quite a bit harder than a monster without one.

Warriors do not make great solo players for several reasons. The first one to become obvious is the fact that monsters run. At the first couple levels you will be able to finish these runners off in a swing or two and it will not be that big of a deal. As the monsters get tougher however they will require quite a few hits to finish. In a dungeon environment this is especially dangerous because they will lead you into more monsters, who will most likely join in, causing a train, which will not only make you rather unpopular, but also cause your death. In a party you will usually have casters who can root the monster or finish it off with a direct damage spell.

The second weakness that becomes apparent around level 5 is that warriors heal slowly. Warriors have more hit points than any other class yet they heal at the same rate. At higher levels you can expect to rest for up to 20 minutes if you are fully damaged. You can invest in bark potions or quest for a Squad Ring (unless you are an evil race) but these can be expensive or hard to achieve. This factor probably more than anything else will lead a warrior to team with some sort of healer.

The third weakness with a warrior is the amount of time it takes him to kill monsters at higher levels. A properly equipped warrior may be able to kill the same monsters as a casting class of the same level but it will usually take the warrior much longer. This is to be expected. We hit for less damage and can absorb much more. However in the amount of time it takes you to finish off your opponent the chance continues to grow that another monster will respawn or walk accross your path thus spoiling your fun and possibly forcing you to run. This can be quite frustrating to fight a monster for a couple minutes only to be forced to run empty handed and then rest for 10-15 minutes as a result of it.

The fourth glaring weakness of a warrior is the lack of his ability to cure himself of diseases and poisons. This becomes evident as you begin to gain levels and more or less makes entire dungeons (such as Befallen, or Unrest) completely impossible for a warrior to solo in. Some diseases can last for hours and in the meantime you will be unable to heal (or even worse you will be losing hit points) until some nice cleric comes along and cures your disease.

All in all, warriors are a GREAT class to play, if played correctly. So if you like being the group leader, being in the thick of the fighting, and depending on others to keep you alive, the warrior class is for you! Warriors are defiantly my favorite class, but we will never have the quick kills like wizards…
