DD post on new Rep system!! Whoop
Bout time is all I can say! I am greatly looking forward to this as I haven't touched my character for a while. Read on brave soldier:

We're finishing off the basic code for the new rep system, and we arrived at the conclusion that a single number for tracking both your good/evil nature and your fame isn't enough. So we're dividing it into two, which will together make up your "reputation" as displayed in a title.

Karma (these are internal names only btw) will be a positive and negative scale like notoriety. It will track good and bad deeds from the perspective of NPCs. This is what will determine things like whether you get spat at when you come into town, what sort of discounts shopkeepers give you, and so on. It is what players would be able to give each other points in, and it would be affected by various actions ranging from necromancy to healing to generosity, etc.

Fame will be a positive scale only. You gain it through advancement in skills, and questing, and other deeds. It decays away to 0 over time. And It affects things like whether NPCs bow to you, etc.

Bob Hanson's got an email with further details on this, and I am sure that FAQ updates will be forthcoming. The reason I am here now is to ask you about titles. :)

OGR with Win 98 goods
Since this is extremely important news I thought you needed to know. Yes that sweet ass OS called Win 98 is coming soon....very soon. I tell you, ever since I installed this joy filled software I have had no problems, crashes, or slowdowns at all! MS really listened to what we had to say and it looks like Win 98 is everything 95 should have been.
(footnote #2: I am not, and have never been an employee of Microsoft :) )

Microsoft announced that it will be shipping RC0 (Release Candidate 0) of its Windows 98 software to about 30,000 beta testers within the next week or so. The Release Candidate stage of previous Windows versions has lasted for several iterations before the product was finalized and went to gold master. Microsoft is finalizing some features relating to power management and driver management before the RC0 release is shipped.

It's also been rumored that the ship date of Win98 has been pushed up to May 1, taking some distributors by surprise. While this hasn't been confirmed as of yet, it is possible that we'll see the new OS before the summertime. While DirectX6 won't make it in at shiptime, we should see that new release of the gaming API later in the year. Windows 98 will offer USB, FireWire, WDM (for shared drivers with Windows NT), DVD and (well possibly) integrated Internet Explorer 4.0 and Active Desktop.
