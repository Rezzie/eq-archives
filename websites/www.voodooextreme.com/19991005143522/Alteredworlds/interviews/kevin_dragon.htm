In flight w/ Kevin Oxland

by Sacrates (April 31, 1998)

An interview Sacrates got a chance to conduct with Kevin Oxland of Grolier Interactive the creators of the upcoming The Chronicles of Pern:Dragonflight

Sacrates: Just to get the ball rolling...could you spit out to our wonderfull
readers what your position is and how far into development you are. (% wise)

Kevin: I believe in releasing a game when its ready.


Sacrates: Will you be providing Voodoo support into the game? And if yes, will you be focussing on utilizing Voodoo2 technology?

Kevin: Yes we are supporting Voodoo2 and we're supporting other cards also. But the main thing is the gameplay, lets not lose focus on that.
.

Sacrates:  What type of lighting will be integrated into the game? (Use of big words that none of us understand is always a crowd pleaser, plus the chicks dig it! ;)).

Kevin: Tim, our lead programmer says...'It's very complicated, lets just call it magic!'
.

Sacrates: How much input does Anne put into the game itself? Mostly story based and or other.

Kevin: She has major input, we keep her up to date on everyting we do and she has even given us a couple of cool game ideas also. We are going big on depth and detail, so to keep it all in check we keep Anne up to date with everything. Shes a very cool lady.
.

Sacrates:  Will there be many NPC's in the game? And if yes, will there be a lot of interaction with them.
.
Kevin: Yes there are, and yes there is.
.

Sacrates: Will we have a choice of characters in the game and will they be customizable?

Kevin: Yes.


Sacrates: How much content will be shown at E3? Will there be a demo etc?

Kevin: Yes there will be a demo but not that one you can take away! We'll show a location so you can wander around and really get a good feel of the game without giving too much away.


Sacrates: Will combat be done in real time and will it utilize natural physics etc.

Kevin: Yes. The thread battles are also in real time. There will be some hand to hand but how that works is a secret.


Sacrates: Will the game follow closely to the novels or will it be a different timeline etc?

Kevin: It has characters from the books within the game and we've borrwed some elemnets but all in all its a whole new story.


Sacrates: Possibly one of the more important questions is will the game have multiplayer support? Or will it be strictly a single player experience?

Kevin: It is at the moment still single player. We are working on the  design for a multiplayer Pern game but it will be very different, although we are still using the same technology.


Sacrates: How will character experience be handled? By levels or skills?

Kevin: We have levels but you can also learn many skills. Many, You want to be a chef, then a chef you can be. You want to learn a craft then take your pick. It is very deep.


Sacrates: If you do have 3D support built in...what types of affects can we look forward to seeing? One glance at Foresaken is enough to dassle anyone. Will there be a WOW factor involved with Dragonflight also?

Kevin: We have everything that you would expect to see in a 3D game, realtime lighting, transparency, fogging ON/OFF, great looking waterfalls falling from the cliffs in the canyon, you name it, it's in there. Although it does look cool, we are focusing on gameplay, this is the key element for us.


Sacrates: Will there be weather affects and environmental affects like night and day?

Kevin: Yes, we have complete control over the Ambient light settings so we can simulate night and day, we are still experimenting with weather.


Sacrates: What types of people do you expect Dragonflight to appeal to? Will it be leaned toward the hard core RPG gamers or will there be much action etc?

Kevin: We are staying true to Annes Pern but also focusing on making a great RPG so hopefully it will appeal to both Pern fans and hard core RPG's. It's not like Diablo where you just hack and slash, its more down the Ultima road with depth and a great storyline.


Sacrates: And finally, when do you expect the game to be released? Basically since you are early in deveopment just spit out a relative time.

Kevin: We hope to finish at the end of the year (xmas 98) so it will probably get released first quarter 99.
