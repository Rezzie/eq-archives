For some specs on the game, it will use 640x480x256 colors (or 65535 colors).  The engines are still underway, and no word from them yet. 

    Each character with the ability to cast magic, will have their own class of magic.  Each class will be signified as a special color of magic.

  • Fight

  • Combination (this will allow player to band 2 or more characters together for major striking         damage)

  • Magic Attack (special magic-forged weapons attacking with physical and magic damage)

  • Item

WOT day
I think I will proclaim Monday's the official WOT day! Looks like the update hit right on schedule and it's always a good thing for WoT fans like myself. - Sacrates

In rummaging around in the official WoT bag of tricks, all I can find is the development update again this week. I'm not swearing off the Viewer Mail or Counter Contest sections, but CGDC and E3 have made it impossible to write any more than this. I hope you all understand that--as Ian so eloquently expressed it--the less I write here, the more devoted I am to building the game. It's rough to be the critical path on a project AND be the webmaster of the official site. How did I let this happen?

Of course I wouldn't give it up for the world, but I am looking for ways to make it easier on my schedule. In addition to my weekly content, this site needs a major overhaul--but there's no way that I can spend the time to do it. So I'm speaking with GT to find ways that they can help with the site without distancing me from the readership. I think the combination can actually work out well. If I can focus on the stuff thatmeans a lot to me (the updates) and let a dedicated webmaster spruce up and maintain the site, that is--in my eyes--a pretty good deal.

Now let's get down to it. I just returned from the Computer Game Developers' Conference. Although there were over 7,000 people there (almost double last year's attendance), the new convention center housed them with room to spare. I met a lot of old friends, talked with some new ones, attended a bunch of seminars and round tables, and generally was reminded that--despite all of the project-related hassles and problems--creating games is pretty cool. We're damn lucky to be making a living doing something that we enjoy so much. Apart from the Spotlight awards (the comedy interludes were so bad that the performers were booing themselves), I had a lot of fun and even learned a bit. Of course, what made it all worthwhile were the countless free T-shirts (with an extra mousepad for totally annihilating my opponent at Forsaken--the Unreal machines were filled). Who needs to go clothes shopping? I've got a whole new wardrobe!

The project went ahead without me while I was gone (thank the Gods). I've just laid my eyes on some more incredible architecture and textures for the Fortress of the Forsaken. These chambers just keep getting more elaborate and detailed. I hope to show some of this off at E3 and, perhaps, release a few more screen shots following. Keep your eyes open. On this topic, Julie--our new texture artist--just started work today. She'll be diving right into the Forsaken's House of Fun and I'm positive that her work will absolutely blow me away. Welcome aboard, Julie!

Magic effects keep pouring in (that's what the programmers are focusing on right now). We've got some that you have to see to believe. Again, we'll be revealing a lot of them at E3.

And so I'm back in the thick of it, killing time until Sabrina arrives in early June--after which she'll be sticking around until further notice. Sometimes, things just come together (so to speak). Feel free to congratulate me. :-)

I'm really happy about The Wheel of Time's progress, both in terms of development and perception. Its profile is constantly rising (I only met a few people at the conference who hadn't heard of it) which can't help but continue after the GT marketing machine kicks in. And the game is simply fantastic and only getting better.

Until next time!

BG Town Hall news
Finally, the smack starts flowing from the guys working on Baldur's Gate. At the WebRPG Town Hall meeting this was said: (VN) - Sacrates

The ID (interactive demo - Ed.) will be a scenario unseen in the actual full game. We will be using art from the main game, but the data for that area will be completely changed, forming a small encapsulated adventure. You will play 3 PC/NPCs in the ID. There is no primary character, just a party, and they will be pregenerated. In the full game you can modify some of your PC's and NPC's AI. This can be done by loading pre-written scripts for the characters (i.e. 'agressive', 'defensive'), or by modifying the existing AI scripts. (Chris Parker, Interplay)

The NID (non-interactive demo - Ed.) is suffering right now. Although the graphics test works well for many of you, there was quite a response, and we have more problems than we had originally thought. We are currently trying to figure out if we can deal with these right now or whether we have to wait for manufacturers to fix their drivers. As it stands, nobody here is comfortable releasing the NID until we can say (at least) when the problems will be addressed. I am currently trying to get it released with the problems and a big warning for people with graphics test problems not to download it, but so far I've been blocked on that. I'll keep you posted. (Chris Parker)

You have two separate sections on each character to toggle whatever colour you wish, basically the main body of the outfit and the accents. The look also changes with whatever type of armor you are wearing. In addition, you also have hair colour and skin colour to select separately at character creation. We decided against facial hair and different body styles(weights, heights), and instead concentrated on having the six different player races. While I suppose it is a loss that you cannot choose between a fat, hairy human, and a buff, well-shaven human, we hope that the ability to play either humans, elves, dwarfs, half-elves, halflings, and gnomes more than make up for it. (Luke at BioWare)

Some areas will be locked off until you complete certain quests (obviously, you may need a pass-key or other item to enter some locales). But this isn't usually the case. You can wander about 80% of the game in the first chapter if you really want to. I don't think you would want to though, because many areas will be geared for higher level characters and you're likely to get yourself killed. (Chris Parker)

3rd World 411...
Noticed on the swankiest site Gamepen's RPG site that those cool cats doing Third World have been posting info on their discussion boards about 3 things... style of play, multi-player and their combat system.. hmm let's take a look shall we :)

[about gameplay style] There's nothing released about that yet. Making a comparison between Third World and other games is difficult, we're creating a game type that doesn't really exist yet. However, the closest comparisons that come to mind are the turn based tactical games like X-com or Incubation, but played out in real time with a completely customizable team of characters. Everything about the characters is customizable, their race, stats, class, armor & weapons, inventory, even their faces. The game is going to have a lot of cool tactical combat in it, but that's not its only focus. You'll also be solving logic puzzles, managing your team and trying to unlock the mysteries that threaten Third World. This is an entirely different experience.

I can't really comment on our exact plans at the moment, but I can assure you that the multiplayer aspect of the game will appeal to both Action/Adventure RPG fans and Strategy fans. The "how" of that is the cool part, but I can't, at this time, explain that. E3 is at the end of the month, so we'll have a lot more information for you folks after that.

...this is a place where the world isn't all happy and pretty. It's dirty, it's dark, it's gritty and it ain't a tourist attraction. It's a diverse place. You'll see everything from the blasted plains, ruins and deserts of the wastes to the hyper-industrialized urban city. It's all there. Imagine places that have so many factories that the sky literally burns at nights. Imagine places that have been worked so hard and mined so much that humans can't even live there anymore. You'll see it all, and fight through it all but you'll be doing a lot more too. This isn't a game where super fast reflexes are going to save your ass. You've got to think and think fast.

On another note, the combat system in the game is versatile. It's not just about big guns, in fact, you'd better hope you're packing a knife or two when you go through Third World; sometimes a gun is just so much metal...

hmmm.. sounds swank to me :) - Kairn

Switch in Time...
Well.. I managed to get the skinny on this little RPG in the works from a company by the name of Mystical Ages Software.  They are working on a console style RPG for the PC entitled Time Switch.  Here is what James Kurth CEO of Mystical Ages Software sent to me in reply to my question about Time Switch: -Kairn

We appreciate your questions about our new project Time Switch and we are more than happy to give you as much information as possible, without spoiling you or others.  First off, Time Switch is an RPG which will resemble much like console RPGs.  Time Switch hopefully can get finished soon.  We have no real deadline, but we hope to get this game completed before Christmas.  Most software industries have long deadlines from which they started, like two years to complete a game or something.  We, at Mystical Ages Software think that it's not the length of time required to do a game that is important.  But it's the progress of the game within a period.  So, if all goes accordingly at a faster pace, Time Switch could be finished before 1999.  That is no definite guarantee though.

    Time Switch is a secret project, underway in development.  What had started as an idea has lead from one thought to another.  And instead of implementing the storyline into a novel, we decided to make it interactive and build a game into it.

    Time Switch was developed in June, 1997 from my game storyline idea.  The story brings the player into a vast world to explore.  The storyline is about a comet coming to threaten the world of Tinari.  While the asteroid cycles around Mars, Tinari's gravitational force pulls the huge asteroid toward Tinari, but is too weak to pull the asteroid fully into Tinari's atmosphere.  Instead, the asteroid, with mass energy grows as its cycled course was shifted.  The residents of Tinari experience volatile atmospheric radiation as clouds cover up the skies.  The asteroid soars off into open space with more energy than ever, forming a comet out of it.  While this all was happening, because of the military and health departments being funded to research atmospheric pressure and radiation, the astronomy department of research lost their funding.

    65 years later, the comet returns in a direct intercept course for Tinari.  While everyone hopes that military can dispose of the comet, a young hero with the name of Kaire steps in to save the world from mass destruction through travelling in time to get the astronomical research department funded.  What seemed like an easy plan backfired, creating a set of doors leading to different realities, as an Empire is on the edge of world domination.

    Can Kaire and his pals restore peace in both eras of time?

*You decide....    Time Switch.    Coming soon to PC*

More ME
The Gents over at VN updated with another tidbit on a mini FAQ (for Middle Earth) that has been posted on the Realm Board. Stephen Nicols posted it.  - Sacrates

Q: Is Middle Earth using the same graphics engine and look the same as The Realm?

A: No. The engine is much improved. Animation quality is 1000 percent better. The basic interface will reamain similar, but the client is totally reworked.

Q: Is Middle Earth going to be real-time 3D?

A: No. We researched using real-time 3D for Middle Earth for quite some time and decided that the quality of real-time 3D characters was below the level of quality needed to do the Middle Earth environment justice. All artwork in the game will be rendered in 3D instead of the cartoony style of The Realm.

Unknown no more
Looks like the boys at VN have found some smack on the upcoming game called Unknown: Quest for the golden Dragon. Here are some highlights from it that make me warm all over. BTW they snagged this from Alpha Beta hot news. - Sacrates

First the Unknown is two separate games in a box. The Unknown will have a multi-player and a single player option within its box. These two games will have different Plots, and locations. So someone that plays the Single player thinks he/she can just enter the multi-player world they will soon learn the game is vastly different. In a way you are getting two games in the same box.

It is a game that supports both alignments. We have designed plots, goals, and even locations that benefit the forces of good and evil. Guilds are being set up not to just to hunt evil characters but those who have not strayed in life. To continue in this format, spells items and even shops have been designed to benefit both good and evil.

Plots: Many. The overall plot will not be known until you enter theworld of the Unknown. Think of yourself as someone who as just finished training by his/her master. Once you enter the Realm this is where you will learn of the overall plot that has fallen upon the lands.

- Light mapping and shadow mapping support.
- Open GL and 3dfx support
- 320x400, 640x480 and 800x600 support (We are also attempting 1024x768)
- Color light mapping
- Multi-player (Internet connection required)
- Skills broken down by each class and race
- 100's of items some worthless to those that are priceless
- Actual party interaction. (One person in your party, depending on skills might only hear certain noises)
- Timed events (Shops will open and close)
- MIDI, CD Audio and soon 3d sound
- Multiple camera angles (You are no longer seeing just though the eyes of your character)

Q: What has influenced you to create Shadows of Reality? Is there any previous game, movie, book, etc., that we can look at as an influence?

David Gallay: My influences are pretty much seeing the way the world is going, how the power is being taken form the individual and going to the large mega-corporations. Stephen Adkins: What influenced me was Gibson and Blade Runner, but also Neal Stephenson's "The

described by Gibson.
