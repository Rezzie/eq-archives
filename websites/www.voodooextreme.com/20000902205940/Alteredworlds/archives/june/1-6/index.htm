Fallout 2 dev info
looks like post E3 news keeps flooding in faster than I can keep up to all the girls asking me for sexual favors. Check out this Fallout 2 news that will make you feel special. - Sacrates

Just got back from E3, and everybody seemed to love the game. I talked to Desslock, the guys from Avault, Vault Network, Cindy from Strat +, Mike Wolf from PCGamer, and a whole bunch of other people. And I, nor any of the Black Isle producers, threw up. A real achievement with the parties that were going on.

The game has been going pretty well over the past month, since my last real update. The Lead Programmer has been working on Combat AI, and a lot of the new Partymember AI is in the game already. A lot of the AI is now dependent on the NPC, Partymember, or Monster's Perception and IQ - and we are probably going to be putting more of that in. One of the guys on the team got his character killed this morning because he thought the Combat AI was going to act like it did in the first game.

The art continues, and work has started on the intro movie. The last couple of areas still need art done for them, and a couple of the artists are going to take about a month to go over each of the areas to make sure they all look original.

There are only about three areas that are left to design, about six or seven to script. So QA is going to be moving over by the team on 6/15 and we start fixing bugs. Most of the designers have already been testing their areas.

Updates will be coming more frequently now that the month of the CGDC and E3 is over.

Ray on BG
Looks like Ray just slapped up some Baldur's gate sweets. Read on and see what these guys are doing. (VN) - Sacrates

Things are a-hopping here at BioWare and at Interplay. E3 went very well and a lot of positive reviews are showing up on the Internet now, but we are only more aware of the many remaining problems with the game that still have to be worked out.

Chris is busy in the sound lab down in California, making sure that the sounds and musics for the game are ready. We're writing the manual rough draft now, and this is looking to be a lot of work; we're using a lot of the good manuals from RPG's of the past as standards we have to exceed in writing this.

All game data and art is now entered (first pass). We're busy trying to get the first three chapters finalized, while at the same time identifying art errors that still remain (there are a lot, in a world this vast), as well as data entry errors and programming bugs. To make this happen, Ben is putting together a testing plan that will allow us to comprehenively test everything in the game, and more importantly, help us to have a standard procedure to evaluate and fix bugs that have been so identified.

There are a few remaining program features and GUI's that are being finalized - for example, the options page, map screens, and a few multiplayer features still remain. After this point, and once the testing plan is locked down, the game will go into full QA down at Interplay and here at BioWare.

One last point - there's a new newsgroup dedicated to Baldur's Gate that has been set up by one of the fans who created an unofficial site (Luminiare) - alt.games.Baldurs-Gate. We will start to cross post there when we post messages to alt.games.ibm.pc.rpg.

Tucked in a small back room of the GT Interactive booth, we were treated to a preview of what looks like one of the more original first-person shooters we've seen. The Wheel of Time is based on the popular fantasy novels penned by Robert Jordan...the game is actually set before the action in Jordan's world. Although the action takes place in a (beautifully rendered) fantasy setting, it plays like a shooter (utilizing the Unreal engine). Instead of bullets and grenades, you've got over 50 spells to cast on your opponents; but what really sets The Wheel of Time apart is the strategic role each player takes in preparing the others' challenges. Your character must go out into the world to seek power-ups and two mystical seals to make your collection of four complete. You have to leave your two existing seals behind in your palace, however...to defend them, you can set traps and deploy troops. Each player has different types of grunts to place around his or her palace and semi-intelligent captains, whom you can give simple orders, to boss them around. It was one of the better demos seen at E3...we're crossing our fingers that The Wheel of Time lives up to its initial promise.

WoT at E3
The boys over at WOD have found some good WoT news posted over at E3.net. And me being the WoT fan I am thought I would pass along some of this smack your way. I am starting to become a WoT druggy, I need a good WoT fix each day now. Luckily I have the books to give me just that! Read on.. - Sacrates

Being a fan of the novels, my first priority was to see Wheel of Time, being shown only behind closed door. This one is finally taking shape. It's another first person action game, but I wouldn't call it a shooter since all combat is handled through magic (or channeling as fans of the series would call it). Legend Entertainment is trying to keep things loyal to the books, but that's not an easy task, so they decided to keep the game taking place just before where the first book takes off.

You'll want to search the game's universe looking for artifacts because these precipitate into tools to promote your cause, weapons or otherwise. You start the game with two 'seals' in your possession, and need to find two more, so in a radicle twist to first person games, while you're raiding someone's castle to get their seal (should it even be there!) someone could be sieging your domain. And for that reason, you'll need to prepare. Before leaving, you'll need to set traps and troops to keep your enemies out. I was amazed to see this process done so simply - it's no Quake editor - simply click on the spot on the wall that you want a spear to fly out and it's there. Amazing innovation here.

Also impressive is the use of AI tactics like enemies running away to call for reinforcements, something you don't want to let happen. You'll defend yourself with such cool oddities as homing decay spells and such. This one has spent a long time in design, but it's not yet close to release, and Legend is looking ahead to Q2 '99.

Just noticed that EQVault has a few tidbits on EQ from Windwalker up. I should also have my thoughts on this game up later tonight. Basically I agree with much of what he says here and yes the walls don;t look pretty up close. However the day/night affects were killer! You defenitely need some good lighting at night! This game is so close to beta it's not funny. It was extremely stable etc...but i'll detail that later tonight with my E3 Review on the Big guns. - Sacrates

- All races and classes are implemented to some degree.
- Combat is functional, but not complete. Armor and weapons do show up in world when worn.
- I fought a Giant Beetle, a Giant Rat, a snake, a Wolf (awesome gfx), and a Brown Bear.
- I was assured that a newbie would not have to fight Bunny Rabbits for a week to gain skill. :)
- Shopkeepers are functional for buying and selling weapons and armor
- Day and night scenerio changes smoothly on the fly, absolutely stunning GFX.
- The old fuzzy anti-aliased walls are not yet a thing of the past :( Closing in on stuff sucks on detail.
- Underwater rocks!
- Weapons I saw were a Short Sword, a Dagger, a Throwing Knife, and a Shuriken (throwing star)
- The chat interface is much like IRC, and is well suited for efficient communication.
- Archery is not yet in the game, but plans are to get it in before beta.
- There are areas in town to Duel legally, but no skill gain
- Non PKers can't be looted anywhere, unless they give a person permission to do so
- Guards do not teleport in

Magic:
- The only spells I cast was a burnging hands type spell, and a healing

- Magic animations are awesome
- No reagents! It's all mana based.
- You memorize a spells by meditating, but its NOT like the SSI games, so hear me out. You memorzise the spell, but it's permanent in your memory until you die. After memorizing a spell, you only need mana to cast it.
- I was told there would be over 600 spells in the game!

The game never crashed on me, which was surprising. The guy I talked to claimed that they have an unbelievably good budget for the game, which will allow them to start as many servers as they need, at a moments notice. He claims that the code is so dynamic, that they can literally get an EQ server going in less then a day. Several members of the EQ team have been playing UO for months, so they know much of our pain. :) They seem to be a very dedicated and hard working team, that is out to prove themselves. Once mouse control is implemented, I believe it will be a lot more fun to play. Moving with the keyboard was kinda hard, eventhough it wasn't choppy or slow.

  • easier access to mountains: can get up into them via paths

  • new dungeons

  • the landmass is designed for the players and such institutions as new towns

  • you should be able to freely gate and recall in and out (except those without gold of course)

Switch in Time
Well... received and e-mail from James Kruth over at Mystical Ages Software, it seems they are make a bold move... readon my friends...

We at Mystical Ages Software are happy to tell everyone of the new changes of Time Switch.  First of all, we are now making this game Windows 95 compatible.  In addition, since it's Windows 95, we are using DirectX 5.0 for Time Switch. 

So, what we had planned a long time ago has changed, as here are some the new features used in Time Switch:

  • All Battles will have perspective shifting. (Camera rotation)

  • Everything will be 3D with special zoom-in/out functions that FF7 lacked. (We can make FMV-like scenes out of moving the views and zooming in or out)

  • 3D accelerators will be required for this game, but it will surely speed up the game.

  • Better music and audio.

Another thing I want to talk about is a celebration for anyone to attend.  It will be a celebration to celebrate the beginning of the implementation process on the Time Switch project.  I, will host this celebration, and anyone who wants to ask questions about Time Switch are more than free to do so.  I will answer any questions that I think could be given out to the public at this time.  Some topics could include:

Characters, vehicles, length, format of the game, specs, plot, conflicts, etc.

The celebration meeting will be hosted on IRC, server: undernet.  The channel will be titled: #TimeSwitch.  The meeting will begin on June 21, 1998 at 1:00pm Pacific Standard Time.  This will be the only time when we'll answer any question about the game.  So if you have a question, or would like to learn more about our game, Time Switch then please attend this meeting.

Well.. I slowly forgot about the game, and started to think vaporware (....Gasp).   Then Quake 2 came up in November, I believe, and that was "the shit", loved that game the graphics everything.  Then in February,  Sacrates pointed me to screenshots for this game I quit watching for months (and I mean months) earlier... there's that name again Unreal. I'm sorry I love id software's stuff (ok so Quake was boring), but this game kicked their ass'!  If only I could have stuck with the game all the way from it's infancy... I feel bad about that, too...   But in the end, Epic Megagames and Digital Extreme have kicked our ass' with the best 3D FPS to grace our computer screens... Ok if you were to pick up just one game, I would have to point you too Unreal.. as Nike so eloquently put it "Just Do It"!

Ok.. enough of my ramblings... let's get back to the news at hand... RPG news that is.... - Kairn
