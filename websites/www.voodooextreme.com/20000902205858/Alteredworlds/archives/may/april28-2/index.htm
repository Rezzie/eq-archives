You are what you wear The inventory box hasn’t been finalised yet but there will definitely be more slots for equipping items. Equipment will also have class-specific attributes, so although a Plate may have some tasty attributes they may be much better suited to a class other than your own. For your character to reach its full potential means trading or selling that piece and continuing your search for a more appropriate item.

Wizardry 8 goodness
Since we haven't heard from this game for a while, we thought it would be kind to let you know that Charles (Of Sir-tech) just made a post on VN's Wizardry 8 board. Scope out this sweetness yourself:(It's about the title of the game) - Sacrates

The reason? Our previousl method of labeling boxes was too confusing. BANE and CRUSADERS didn't have numbers on them, which made it hard for the customer to figure out which was the most recent. And the long subtitles are hard to remember (We get tons of letters and e-mail where people get the subtitles wrong. My favorite is "Cruisers of the Dark Servant.")

More seriously, the games would often show up with several different entries in the databases used by software chains to track inventory. For example, CRUSADERS might show up as 3 entries: "Wizardry 7", "Wizardry: Crusaders of the Dark Savant", and "Crusaders of the Dark Savant." This is very, very bad, as splitting the sales figures among three titles makes it look like the game isn't selling as well as it really is. ("Well, Bob, I checked the figures, and Wizardry 7 isn't selling that well. But that 'Cruisers of the Dark Servent' game is going like wildfire!")

The Begotten cometh
Another one of those games with cool names is The Begotten (Ward Siz Enterrtainment). Jonrik over at our beloved IGN affiliate site VN had a small chat with the boys working on it and we thought we would give you a small piece right here. For the full deal head over to VN: - Sacrates

VN: Where does development stand right now?

Jason: We're doing pre-production and building tools and technology. The first elements we're assembling are the graphics engine and the "world" engine.

The graphics engine will, of course, be really nice and have all the expected bells and whistles like dynamic colored lighting, bump mapping, advanced character animation system using inverse kinematics (bones), full hardware acceleration support (quite possibly requiring it), 16/24-bit rendering, beautiful outdoor landscapes seamlessly integrated with indoor areas, and the list goes on. Most of the core graphics engine is done, with the exception of integrating the terrain rendering with the portal-based interior rendering.

VN: So you'd say graphics are a major feature of the game?

Jason: Major, yes - but graphics are not THE selling point. The interaction is. The "world" engine consists of several systems that, together, will allow us to create cinematic experiences with unparalleled interactive depth. So, while there will be action elements to spice up the gameplay, the game is a role-playing game at heart since the player will have stats, relationships, quests, and an epic story to follow. Sounds like "yadayada" but we're approaching the game on a very deep and meaningful level, where we hope to take a step beyond offering a transitory, forgettable waste of time to players and entering the coveted realm of Art.
