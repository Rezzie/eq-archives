U:A Novel
Well, not quite that long, but close. There is a doozy of a post on Sith Dragon's homepage. Lord British (Richard Garriott for those of you more firmly based in reality :) has a 5 page splurge on everything Ultima:Ascension...wow... - Bowmeister

Action! No, we are not creating an action game. When people saw "action adventure," on some of our E3 materials, this choice of wording left many with an unintended impression. We are creating a game with a high pace of play and constant tension element. What I mean by this is best described using Ultima Underworld as an example. One of my favorite features of Underworld was that sense of tension at all times. NPC and monster threats were closely interwoven. It took place in a dungeon after all. Right outside the door of where an NPC might have been located could lurk a monster, so you always had to be on your guard. In many "lineage" Ultimas (what I call Ultimas with a # after them), we have created sedentary towns surrounded by often gigantic, aimless wanders to get to the deep dark dungeon and back. In Ascension we are trying to weave the tension into all aspects of the game as we did in Underworld. Ultimately, we have created a world that has such strife within it, it is reasonable that monsters or at least bad guys might be right around the corner.

BG Random Bits
The always informative Baldur's Gate Chronicles has the latest installment of Random Bits of Information™ up. - Bowmeister

Anyone with "leader" privileges can give any player "dialog" privileges. Any character controlled by a player with "dialog" privileges can talk to NPCs. It was decided that this should be a right assigned by the people with "leader" privilege because dialog pauses the game, and the leader players are responsible for controlling the flow of the game.

There are new multiplayer dialog panels going into the code as I type this. Right now, the game saves the last 30 chat messages, and can display them in any "chat" window, so you don't have to worry about missing any messages. There is currently no interface to save the chat messages. In all likelihood, we will allow you to choose who to send "private" chat messages to by selecting portraits.

BG Bits...
Baldur's Gate Chronicles posted their latest installment of the "Random Bit's of Information". Check the ou the goods below. - Bowmeister

The mouse is the main interface device. There are also keyboard "hot keys" that can be assigned to whatever you want. Party members (other than your own character) are controled much the same way that the main character is. They have their action icons, can cast spells and find traps (if the class allows of course). They do, however, have personalities, which shine through at times. They dislike people (a thief and a paladin probably won't get along), have a morale (they can get scared and run away), and in a few instances go kinda nuts (there is a beserker that can join). You can override most things, except the above mentioned.

During game play, characters are highlighted with various colored circles. This is a list of what those different colors represent.

Red = enemy

green = party member

blue = neutral

yellow = panicked (failed morale)

If anyone is in shadows and commits a offensive action, he/she becomes visible. The ranger (or thief) can attempt to hide in shadows again, but chances are "they know where you are".

If your main character dies, the game is over. You have to reload and try again. This is because of the story of the game. One of the main quests revolves around your main character.

Elves cannot be rescued using the Raise Dead spell, but they can be saved using the Resurrection spell. 5th level Raise Dead requires much of the corpse in reasonable condition, as it does not restore massive damage. The creature cannot have been dead for too long (varies on level of the caster). The raised creature permanently loses a point of constituion. 7th level Resurrection requires little more than a tooth, and raises the creature to perfect health. It ages the caster 3 years, and puts him out of action for a time depending on the level of the creature raised. Raise Dead is available, for a price, from various temples in the game. Resurrection is not. Sorry elves.

You cannot keep pets. You cannot ride any flying creatures. Not even horses and caravans. Perhaps in future chapters.

Still more BG Random Bits
Baldur's Gate Chronicles has thrown up yet another batch of goodies on the CRPG to end all CRPGs. Can't wait to check this one out. - Bowmeister

Treasure on monsters will be determined randomly, within basic guidelines (their weapons and such). However, many of the items in the dungeons and other places are placed there purposely, especially the more powerful ones.

There are pre-set monsters and random encounters. All of these you see on the screen, so you can run if you want. There are also random encounters when you travel from area to area, or when you rest in an unsafe place.

The Bard's ability to counter magic musical attacks is not in the game but their ability to inspire with a "battle song" is. Also, Bards will be singing strictly a capella. There will be no instruments for Bards in Baldur's Gate, but there is a good chance they will be in the expansion pack.

Some abilities will be disabled while wearing equipment that is not useable by the class with those abilities. Items that are forbidden will not be able to be equipped at all. Items that deliver penalties will cause the appropriate skill buttons to become shaded. This goes for all of the classes. Mages cannot wear armor at all. They cannot equip it. Fighter-mages can wear armor but cannot cast spells while they are wearing it. Quickspell slots will be shaded to indicate that they are inactive while armor is being worn. The manual will explain the weapon and equipment restrictions but there will be in-game help as well.

You can gain experience by completing a chapter without loading the game because you died. Along with monster killing and quest completion, that is how your character advances in levels. You do not get experience for finding treasure unless it's part of a quest.

Characters will be able to be "ported" into single player from multiplayer and vice versa, but they will be stripped of their possessions. BioWare is currently working on this to ensure that game balance is maintained.

You cannot choose height and weight attributes for your characters.

Unarmed combat specialization is currently not in the game. However, it may be added in a future expansion.

Raging Mages
Computer Games Online threw up this preview of Monolith's Russian import, Rage of Mages. This one looks good, kindof a Warcraft II/Diablo thing going, blending RPG and RTS combat. Check out the preview and screenshots here. - Bowmeister

Originally published as Allods by Buka Entertainment, the game is something of a hybrid real-time strategy/role-playing game. Teleported to a strange new world, the player chooses one of four character types—fighter, archer, wizard, thief, or their equivalents—and sets off on a series of missions. There are more than 30 of these branching quests, ranging from escorting people to curing plagues to wiping out various and sundry bad guys. Combat plays rather like Warcraft II, but with far fewer units and much more detail when the main characters are involved (ultimately, you can control several heroes as well as mundane troops). In town, you can shop for weapons, armor, magic and the like at well-stocked stores, upgrade your heroes' abilities through training and generally do other RPG-like things. Over time, the main characters can develop into powerhouses, and it would seem the missions get increasingly difficult as well.

More BG Random Bits
The guys over at Baldur's Gate Chronicles threw up some more tasty tidbits on Interplay/Bioware's upcoming RPG. - Bowmeister

Baldur's Gate allows you to pause the game in order to give your party members commands as often as you like. However, there is no autopause feature implimented at this time. BioWare may consider adding this option if they find a need for it during QA testing.

Thieving is possible for thieves and other characters who possess the thievery skills (which includes picking pockets). A random item is selected from the hapless victim if you succeed. If you don't succeed, they may become hostile toward your character.

Your party can split up within an area, including building interiors, unless the building is large enough to be its own area, but there are only a few of those (dungeons, caverns and such).

If you walk away from the NPCs, they will just sit there patiently and wait for you. However, they will leave your party if their morale gets too low.

The primary music will be streaming audio that changes as you do different things, such as starting a battle or changing areas.

More RAM allows for more animations, backgrounds, and other vital program data to be kept in memory rather than having them cached to the hard drive.
