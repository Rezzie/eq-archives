Valhalla w/ Ryan Dueckman

by Sacrates (May 24, 1998)

Sacrates does it again! You guys want more info on Valhalla? You came to the right place. We stuck everything but the kitchen sink in this Interview and came out with some grade A unhomogonized smack!

Sacrates: Well let's get this started with the perfunctory introductions etc. Who are you, where do you come from, Social Security number, What you do and a small sample of DNA for the books.

Ryan: Dammit, start out with the tough ones! :) The name's Ryan Dueckman, I'm from the outskirts of Vancouver up here in beautiful BC, Canada. When I'm not cruisin' around in my decked-out '92 Geo Storm (Where the hell is my Farrarri?!), I work as game designer and animator for Delphic Oracle Entertainment.


Sacrates: Ok with that out of the way...on with the show. Valhalla, not only a cool name but very nice looking game. Could you give us an idea of how far into development you are % wise?

Ryan: About 10% at this point. (Don't start lining up outside the EB's quite yet!)


Sacrates:  Lets hit a few Engine related questions. Is this a hardware only based game or will you have Software support in it also? If it's hardware, what types of nice stuff do you have in store for Voodoo 2 owners?

Ryan: OGRE is a hardware-only engine. Part of the reason we're so early on in the development was the decision to scrap software entirely, and rewrite the engine from scratch to be fully hardware dependant. As for nice stuff for Voodoo2 owners? The OGRE engine is designed to support ANY feature a new graphics card can throw at us. Materials in the game consist of over 12 different properties, including reflection maps, bump maps, opacity maps, and any other properties you'd find in a professional rendering package like 3D Studio MAX. Supporting a new feature in a new card is as simple as turning it on in the driver. Curved surfaces and terrain are also tesselated in real-time to achieve the highest possible polygon count while maintaining a high framerate. You've seen screenshots of it running on a P5-133 with a Rendition 2100 card at 25 frames per second, imagine how it'll look on a PII-400 with a Voodoo 3! (We can only dream :) )



Sacrates: I noticed in the shots that the sky looks incredible. Will you have day and night affects in the game also? Or maybe rain and snow etc? Basically what types of environmental affects will be in?

Ryan: The gameworld is a totally dynamic one. Weather changes take place as you play, the sun moves slowly across the sky, day changes to night... We're trying to make Midgard feel as real as possible.

Sacrates: Also there are quite a few shots with Water in them. Will you have simulated waves or rippling affects in the water like Unreal?

Ryan: Both will be present. Larger bodies of water(Such as the ocean) can only be realistically represented with large waves, whereas with smaller bodies(Lakes, ponds) we'll probably go for a ripple/reflection map.


Sacrates: The main character bears a striking resemblence to a well known Plumber  we know for consoles. Is he an earlier descendent of this character? (If he is...please don't say he has Butt Stomp capabilities or I will stop this review here and now!) Basically what I am saying is since the game has a more animated light hearted look to it, will it be more serious action or a deep story to it?

Ryan: The "cute" character you see in the screenshots is just there as a placeholder until we get the animation system fully implemented. The characters in the final version will probably be rendered more realistically.

The story behind Valhalla centers around the oncoming of Ragnarok,which is by definition grim, violent, and apocalyptic. However, this is above all a GAME, which means it has to be FUN, so we try to deal with the subject matter in a suitably light-hearted way.


Sacrates: I also hear we may be able to form a group of people or a party in this game? Will we have NPC's with us on this adventure and will we be able to do it online also?

Ryan: Hey, Vikings NEVER travelled alone :).. In normal single-player mode, you can have up to 7 NPC Vikings that you can order around using simple goal-based commands, or you can hook up to the internet and have 7 friends travel with you. This also brings up some very interesting multiplayer possibilities. (Clans anyone? :) )


Sacrates: Basically I jumped the gun with that last question. What types of Internet support will you be adding to the game?

Ryan: Cooperative is a definite must. A game like this BEGS to be played with others :).. Deathmatch is also planned, and we're looking at the logistics of setting up a virtual online world, where clans of vikings can travel from town to town battling for supremacy against other clans. That's something I'd definitely be interested in.


Sacrates: There is also the fact that we can use anything as a weapon in the game? So with that said...will I be able to chop off a good friends arm in Multiplayer and use it to bludgeon another good friend?

Ryan: It's very possible. :)


Sacrates: What style will combat be? Will there be any sophisticated styles of attacks like Chopping, jabbing, Slicing, parrying etc.

Ryan: Play balance and control is something we're spending a lot of time developing right now. No third-person game on the market to date really has what I would say to be "perfect" play control, so that's something we'd really like to remedy.


Sacrates: What kinds of physics can we expect to see in Valhalla? Will I be able to get into combat and knock someone down? Or since this little Viking guy is fairly stocky will he trip and roll down a hill?

Ryan: You will be able to throw an axe, see it arc through the air, hit a peasant on a hill, and you will laugh as the peasant flies through the air. The peasant will land, roll down the hill, and come to rest at the bottom. The blood will stain the ground. How's that for physics?


Sacrates: What type of storyline do you have set for the game so far? Will it be a story based game and will we talk to any NPC's etc?

Ryan: The game is story based, although we're not about to release the details of the story quite yet.(I think I said too much in an earlier question already :) ). The game plays very nonlinear, with multiple distinct endings, and any number of paths to take along the way.


Sacrates: Will there be anything to ride in the world? (Horses, Ostriches, other humans etc?) And since it will be one large world....just how big will it be?

Ryan: There will be of course a Viking Longboat for you to carry your crew around in(After all, it would take more than an hour to walk in a straight line from one corner of the world to the other!) There are some others, but I think our programmers would lynch me if I said any more :)


Sacrates: What type of gameplay can we expect? Will there be a lot of combat or more puzzle solving etc?

Ryan: There will be a balance between combat and exploration. One of the goals in the game is to model any major structures after their real-life counterparts, to give players a feeling that they're exploring a real location rather than just hacking their way through a random maze. The difficulty doesn't come from finding Key B to open Door X to enter Corridor W, it comes from working your way through a real location to where the treasure would be stored in it's real-life counterpart. The game's big enough without having to worry about getting lost!


Sacrates: Will we be able to sing happy Viking songs about Death, Destruction and Raping and pillaging towns in the game? Or will that just be your background music?

Ryan: Absolutely! Death, destruction, raping and pillaging towns are all integral to progression in the game! (Well, except for the rape part, I don't think we really need that). And hey, if we get enough people asking for it, we may even throw a big musical number in there somewhere!


Sacrates: And finally....when, since it is still fairly early in development, can we expect to see this in stores? Just spit out a relative time.

Ryan: We're hoping March '99

Thanks for the Interview Ryan. We hope to see and hear much more about this game soon! With that said will you be at E3? And do you by chance have a spare Pic or two gathering dust somewhere in your vaults that we could toss  up with the Interview? Anyway, great looking stuff and I can;t wait to get my paws on it!

Hey, I'm just glad to be able to finally talk about it! Unfortunately, we probably won't be at E3 this year(Unless we can find ourselves a publisher willing to foot the bill). I can't get you a new screenshot right now because we're right in the middle of throwing in a whole slew of new effects, but if you can wait a couple weeks I'll see if I can get you some exclusives..
