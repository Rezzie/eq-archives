A Conversation w/ Paul Turbett

by Kairn (March 31, 1998)

I had a chance to talk to Paul Turbett of Silver Lightning the creators of the upcoming RPG entitled Ancient Evil. This is a game in the vein of Diablo.  From the look of the test release they supplied the internet this game looks to have allot of promise!  So I was happy to have this chance to talk to Paul about his baby... so here ya go...

Kairn: Hey Paul, thanks for finding enough time out of your schedule for this interview. Let's get our loyal readers some info on you, who are your and what is your responsibilities on the Ancient Evil project?

Paul: I've been writing computer games for about 12 years. I started doing it as a hobby, then full-time about 3 years ago. I started Silver Lightning to release a shareware game in 1992. Our first
commercial game was Rally Challenge, a rally driving sim. Personally, I've always liked RPG's. The game I've probable enjoyed most was Eye of the Beholder, which was released in about 1990/91

My responsibility for Ancient Evil..I designed it and did all of the programming, expect for a 3DS Max plug-in, which the lead artist wrote.

Kairn:  What is your history in RPG's? How did you get started on them and what was the first CRPG you ever played?

Paul: My first RPG was Ultima III on my Apple II. I didn't finish it, but it was great fun. The Eye of the Beholder I & II were fantastic. I played Ultima Underworld a fair bit, too.

Kairn:  What inspired Silver Lightning to create Ancient Evil?

Paul: We wrote a game called Trial By Magic in about 1992. I was only ever released in Australia ( where we are from ). It was an OK game, so I figured we should update it a bit. Originally, we were just going to update the graphics, and re-write the lighting. As the game progressed, we went a bit nuts and added real-time shadows, line-of-sight lighting, new monsters, new weapons, different spells, 3D models.....

Kairn: How long has this project been in the making?

Paul: As Ancient Evil, about 12 months.

Kairn: Paul, from the test release what kind of feedback have you received from the users out there? What type of changes are you making to the game to make it more "user friendly"?

Paul: The main comments from the test related to the user interface. It was too clumsy. As the developer, you don't always see these things, you get a bit too close to the game. I've played it daily for the last year, so I'm totally used to it. The feedback makes you step back, and see it from a different angle.

We make it totally point-and-click. The mouse cursor is 'intelligent' it changes as is passes over things, and we've improved the pathing algorithm. The interface has been refined a lot.

Kairn:Now let's get a little info on the game. What is the background of AE? Where does it take place? Do we have a central "Continent or Town" where we start our quest from?

Paul: The game takes place in the Crypt of the Ancients, where legend states the creators of the world rest. The Crypt is controlled by Alaric, a very old mage. He offered a prize of gold for adventurers who enter the Crypt and return alive. Over the centuries, the contest has gain a huge reputation and only one person, Jetraal the Heroic, has ever survived it.

Jetraal have been dead for about 300 years, but his ghost has been seen warning travelers of a rising evil.....and that Alaric must be stopped.

Kairn: Who is the main character? Doe we have multiple characters to choose from? What is his/her history within the game?

Paul: As the player, it is up to you to enter the crypt, and discover the nature of the evil Jetraal warned of. There are multiple character classes (Warrior/Spellcaster/Ranger/Thief ) and each has abilities, but this has been underplayed a bit. I've designed the characters so that the skills that you use develop, and the skill they don't..don't. So a player might start as a thief, but develop into a spellcaster if that use a lot if magic.

Kairn:Will we get to interact with NPC's? If so, what are some of their duties in the game?

Paul: Yes, there are NPC's. Some are just for decoration, others are for story telling and sub-quests.

Kairn: Do you plan on adding Multi-player abilities to AE? TCP/IP or LAN?

Paul: Ancient Evil was designed as a single player adventure. We have no plans for multi-player in this version. However if AE II eventuated, I'd say the multi-player would more that likely be an integral part.

Kairn: How have you handled combat?

Paul: Combat is real-time. The AI of the monster means that different strategies are necessary in different situations.

Kairn: What kind of weapons can we expect? Are there magical weapons?

Paul: The usual swords and axes, as well as crossbows, knuckle-dusters and a few others. There are magic weapons for increased damage and accuracy.

Kairn: Is there a magic system in Ancient Evil? What is the system based on? (Scrolls, Talismans, Books etc.)

Paul: The magic is rune based. Rune stones are required to memorize a spell, which as cast later as required.

Kairn: Since this is a 3D based game, do you plan on supporting 3D
Accelerators? If so, which ones are you leaning towards? (Voodoo, Voodoo2 etc.)

Paul: Only the characters and monsters are 3D. 3D acceleration would come in the box, as the potential gain is that big. The 3D characters don't take much CPU time, so shifting them to a co-pro is not going to produce much of a performance increase, and being fairly small, won't affect the appearance.

However I *might* do a patch, if I get time for my own interest.

Kairn: Are there any realworld physics involved?

Paul: We've made things as realistic as possible. Especially, if you include light as part of physics.

Kairn: You say there is dynamic lighting effects and shadows, can we expect to see colored lighting in any way?

Paul: Not colored, but still true line-of-sight. Light doesn't go though wall in this game. The shadows are generated in real time form the light sources, so that add to the over all atmosphere.

Kairn:What do you feel are some of the outstanding qualities in Ancient Evil?

Paul: I like the lighting, the real-time special effects, the CD music and the variety of color in the levels. There is a lot of variety from one level to the next, each level has something of special interest. There is quite a lot to do, apart from the main quest, lots to explore. I also like to overall atmosphere of the game. I think atmosphere is important in a game like this.

Kairn: And our final question, what is the future potential of AE? Do you see an AE II?

Paul: AE2 ? If people like AE1 enough, then sure. Time will tell.

We  would like to thank Mr. Turbett for taking time out of his already busy schedule to answer these questions, all the way from down under!  We really enjoyed your answers, believe me.
