Sorcery w/ Magnus Högdahl

by Sacrates (May 10, 1998)

An interview Sacrates got a chance to conduct with Magnus Högdahl of Starbreeze Studios the creators of the upcoming RPG entitled Sorcery.. so everyone grab their grimoires and huddle by the campfire  for some learning...

Sacrates: Just for the books and the Altered readers out there, could you tell us who you are, and why you do what you do.

Magnus: My name is Magnus Högdahl and I'm the lead coder and the head of Starbreeze. I was previously one of the guys in the former 'Triton Productions'. (developer of "Into the Shadows", which unfortunately was terminated.)


Sacrates: Lets dive right in shall we? First off, let's hit the engine related questions. Like will this game be optimized for Voodoo 2 cards more than Voodoo 1's? Will you have extra bells and whistles for V2 owners or will it simply be a performance boost.

Magnus: Voodoo2 (and other next-gen-accelerators) owners will be able to use features like bump-mapping and detail-mapping, while Voodoo1 & Riva owners will probably chose to turn them off.


Sacrates:  Obviously you have the real time lighting in, but type of lighting will you be using? What can we expect to see?

Magnus: Fully dynamic colored lighting with bumpmapping. Dynamic shadows (from the environment, on the environement) is used where it makes sense. (It's too slow to use on everything, I give you that.) Characters and objects cast shadows on the environment.


Sacrates: What type of physics are in the game? Will arrows arc through the air when shot. Will the gravity be realistic in the game?

Magnus: Things behave like you expect them to behave. Some examples:

  • If you drop a hammer in the water it sinks. If you drop a piece of wood it floats.

  • A raft sink deeper the more load you put on it, and is affected by any currents in the water it is in.

  • Things close to an explosion react to the shockwave produced by it. The effect on the objects depend on their size, weight, distance to explosion, etc.

  • Surfaces have different friction.

  • Object's spin is taken into account when calculating results of impacts. (If you drop a spinning ball on a flat floor, it will bounce off in the correct direction and the spin is reduced as a result of the impact.)

Yes - the arrow will arc.


Sacrates:  Is there any type of environmental affects planned such as Rain, snow, day and night etc?

Magnus: Probably rain. None of the others mentioned is planned


Sacrates: We have seen a few of the awesome screenshots of the engine at work already. Do you plan on releasing any in the not-too-distant future that will show enemies or NPC's in the scene, and will they blend well.

Magnus: Various characters are in the works


Sacrates: One of the big boy questions here...do you plan on supporting Multiplayer in the game? If so what type? Will it be co-op, deathmatch, etc. Will we be able to see other players and what they wear and carry in the game?

Magnus: Multiplayer is the heart of the game. It's the real purpose of 'Sorcery'.Besides from 'DeathMatch', Co-op and some other game styles, etc. , there will be a built-in skill-system for the purpose of character-developing & ranking when playing on-line. Server-owners in a particular area, for example Sweden, may choose to connect their server to a swedish character-server.

A character-server have information about all players playing on all connected servers. Players have an overall skill, a ranking skill, that can be reviewed on the character-server. A player may have one or several characters for every character-server registered on.Additionaly, there's skill in every spell a player can cast, which controls the power of the spell. The more skill a player have, the harder it is to maintain the skill-level, since skill is proportionally increased and decreased depending on how well the player perform.

Yes - You will see what other players are wearing.


Sacrates: Will this game be more action based or puzzle solving etc? Will there be much interaction with NPC's etc?

Magnus: We consider it an 'action-rpg'. The main part is the multiplayer character-/team(guild) developing. Action-based, I'd say, though there will be a solid story, NPC's, mini-quests, etc...


Sacrates: What can we expect to see in terms of combat? Will it be a lot of projectile based stuff or will we get to see hand to hand types of sword fights.

Magnus: There will only be magic-based combat for player-characters.


Sacrates: How can you describe the atmosphere of the game itself? Since what we have seen is still early it is hard to tell, will you have large outdoor areas in the game like in WOT?

Magnus: There will be outdoor scenes, not as vast as, for example, "Arena - the eldes scrolls", though.


Sacrates: If you do have large outdoor areas in the game, what should we expect to see? Will it be more organic looking than most 3D games like Quake 2 and Hexen 2 etc? (Will water ripple, leaves blow in trees, birds fly by etc)

Magnus: We'll do our best to make the world look alive. All mentioned included.


Sacrates: What are a few of the particularly cool features in the game you would like to express to the wacky readers out there?

Magnus:

  1. 'Deep' character-development and item-management

  2. Awesome spells

  3. Detailed character-animations. (Like the ITS-demo, but spiced-up for the 3D-accelerator-age.)


Sacrates: Now for the perfunctory final question: When do you expect to have this bad boy coming our way? Probably not in 98 I would assume?

Magnus: My best guess is late '99.


Anyway, thanks for the interview and we hope to get you guys a ton of good exposure from it! Also....if you have a screenshot or two or three sitting around gathering loads of dust, could you toss them our way so we can post them with the Interview?

Thanks again my friend, and we hope to see and hear much more about this game soon. BTW, one final question, have you started to settle down into a name for the game yet? Or is it still just a code name called Sorcery?

It's still just 'Sorcery', for working title.
