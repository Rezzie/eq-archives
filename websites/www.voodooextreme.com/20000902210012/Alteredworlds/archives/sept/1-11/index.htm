BG Release Date
Here's a short clip from the latest news on the Official Baldur's Gate site at Interplay. - Bowmeister

Just to iron out an issue that has come up on many message boards and on various sites recently, the game will be done when both Interplay and Bioware are happy with it. Bioware may be sending us a "final candidate" soon. If they do, this means that they feel the game is the best it can be. At that time, Interplay and Bioware will both put it through the most objective evaluation we can to make sure that it really is the best it can be. If we aren't all 100 percent happy with it at that time, then we will need to make more changes and adjustments.

This game is big. It's taken a lot of work to get it to where it is, and it'd be a waste to ship it early after all this work. Nobody is going to hold up the game if its fun and its clean -- but we aren't going to ship it until both those conditions are met.

Chris at Interplay

Planescape Preview
Desslock's RPG News has a preview up of Interplay's next AD&D adventure, Planescape Torment. Based on a modified version of Bioware's Infinity engine (Baldur's Gate), Planescape takes you into the weird world of the Inner and Outer planes, instead of BG's more mundane world of the Forgotten Realms. Tons of info and cool screenshots to check out here. - Bowmeister

Although some aspects of Planescape Torment will be very familiar to AD&D veterans, such as the traditional system of attributes (strength, intelligence, wisdom, dexterity, constitution and charisma), many of the gameplay elements in Torment will be as new as the setting. Like Fallout, Planescape Torment will be a very character driven game. In fact, instead of beginning the game in traditional AD&D fashion by generating a first level Mage, Fighter, Paladin or other conventional character class, you are given a rather unique protagonist. The player's character in the game is an immortal who has, during his extended existence, acquired god-like abilities in virtually all of AD&D's character classes. But there was a cost to your character's' immortality. In the past, whenever the character received wounds that would have killed him if not for his immortal status, he awakened some time after the incident in a state of amnesia, unaware of his former identity or abilities. As a result of this "tormented" existence, the player's character has acquired great abilities but he will be unable to use them until he becomes aware of his past and his former powers. He's also acquired a "Frankenstein's Monster-ish" profile of scars. No Fabio here.

BG Update
Ben from Bioware snuck in an update on his BG page, Ben's Officially Unofficial Website. Looks like they're in the home stretch for bug squishing. He also threw in a tip on how to make that 1st level mage useful for something other than cannon fodder. - Bowmeister

We killed a whole swak of bugs over the weekend. Over 400 bugs met their demise over the last 3 days. The #'s are as follows: 799 Active bugs (that's bugs pending and on peoples desks) 1061 Inactive (we say they are killed and Interplay is verifying) and 1317 Killed (dead ,dead, dead!). Needless to say we are making good headway. Something new I want to try is to post neat tips and tricks to playing BG. Tip1: Your first level mage is kinda weak and some say useless. Bah! I say. Try this trick. Memorize the spell grease (level 1 spell) and keep your mage in the back ranks. Equip a goodly amount of bows (or other ranged weapons). When a group of monsters approach, pause the game, launch that grease spell. The area of effect is pretty large, so watch out. Un-pause the game. All creatures in the area of effect slip and slide, movement is halved. It's pepper the bad guys time. It takes twice as long for them to get to you, ergo, you get twice the "free" ranged attacks. This works great with non- ranged enemies! More to come.

1) Will characters be able to buy drinks at taverns? If so - will this effect the way they respond to a players wishes?

2) Will characters always walk around with weapons in the ready position?

Like Underworld, I assume? Not yet. It's on our extras list, so there is an outside chance, but it depends on time left.

BG bits
Over on the Baldur's Gate board there was a little something on Auto Pause that you spudmonkeys may like to know more about. After playing it you almost have to use this feature. I love real time combat and totally absolutely hate turn based crap, but in this game it is highly recommended that you use this pause feature. At times totally necessary...especially when your party gets larger. Read on. - Sacrates

Just so everyone knows, we're going to implement an autopause feature (totally optional, hence you turn it on in the options menu), which is configurable. You can enable automatic pausing after the end of every characters' rounds (i.e. effectively making the game turnbased), or when characters are hit, die, weapons break, etc.

It was something that we've been thinking about for a while, but only recently looked at in detail - it was easier than we thought, and everyone wanted it, so we're putting it in.

So, you can play BG in three ways: real time, pseudo turn-based (with space bar pausing), or in turn-based mode (with configurable autopause).

EQ goodies
Just to keep you guys spiffed up on EQ, Brad spoke more words of wisdom on the AVault Forum. I got this little goodness from EQVault. - Sacrates

I was just looking at the pics (don't ask, I've been gone), and noticed at night, one warrior, (I can't remember the name) was wearing a blue plate armor. Is this a night effect, or is it possible to buy colored armor? And if so, is there a skill to do it, or a NPC that you can go to, to have it done?

In EQ, color is based on the item. Some magical and rare items are colored... like the blue platemail you saw in the pic. These items are found or quested for (in other words, you don't go to a tailor and arbitrarily make something blue).

An example I often give when explaining this would be Red Dragon Armor. Let say, for example, you slay the Red Dragon, and from his corpse you retrieve Red Dragon Scales. Then you take those scales to an ancient dwarven armorer, who resides in the depths of a dungeon. After fighting your way to him, you finally find him, hand him the scales and a fee, and then he gives you Red Dragon Platemail. Well, it would probably appear red when you put it on :) (And one would hope it would have some magical properties as well -- perhaps it would increase your resistance to fire, as well as have great armor class).

(note, this is not an actual quest, but an example of how a quest for some exotic item might work)

-Brad
