Yessir...

Finally got my bundle of goodness from EA Direct. Upon shredding the packing that confirmed that yes, Canadian customs does approve of mind altering video games if not substances, I found the following...

   - A piece of cardboard...somehow, in the years to come, I think I will treasure this most of all...

   - A registration card...they already have my name, my address, my credit card number...what the hell else do they need :).

   - Some nifty "pre-order now" propaganda for Ultima:Ascension (as soon as it hits stores) and Wing Commander Prophecy Gold (umm...no).

   - An Installation Guide. It's pretty good as far as installation guides go, but they still had to reprint the important bits on the patch update screen cause who the hell reads installation guides anyways?

   - A Quick Refernce Card. Umm...been playing since the beta...umm. Will be cool when the box version (UO & UO:T2A together) comes out...

   - A License Agreement. I couldn't find an "OKAY" button to click quickly so I actually read it...only fell asleep twice. Err, does the "You must be at least 18 years old to accept this agreement part" mean the apparent intelligence/maturity of half the players in UO will go up?

   -  A nifty heavy paper map with Britannia on one side and the new lands on the other. Cheap bastards, I really wanted another cloth map with a game. Seriously, I hate using old t-shirts for dusting.

   - The UO-T2A Upgrade CD. Yep, it's better than 350 floppies...doesn't even have any new artwork.

   - A shipping bill. Woohoo...this is the fun part. $6.99 for the upgrade, and $15.00 for 3-5 day shipping? Glad I didn't get next day shipping. (Note, I'm only bitter till I get my Mastercard bill and see what the Canadian exchange rate was that day...then I'll actually be unhappy :).

   - A MANUAL!!!!!! This alone was worth the $6.99. Very well done, explains all the basics and some of the not so basics. Complete with maps of the cities of both Britannia and the new world. Excellent job OSI!!!
