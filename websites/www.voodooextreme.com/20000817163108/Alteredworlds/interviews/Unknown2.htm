Unknown:Quest for the Golden Dragon Interview
Part Two
By Certis
With: Kevin Ross

While the big players of the industry continue to develop and hype their products Ross Systems quietly goes on plugging away at what may be one of the best RPGs this year. This is a follow up to an interview conducted previously here at Altered Worlds, click here to read the old one. Sit back and enjoy...

Certis: Where did Ross Systems come from? How/When was it formed?

Kevin Ross: Ross Systems came from Myself, Kevin Ross. The company originally started doing PC/Network Repairs, and then a sub section spun off to do Game Development. We did this for numerous reasons, the biggest being me. I am a avid player of RPG games. I was tired of playing games where the plots were non-existent, that were copies, or had a world that forced you to play only one way. I also did not like fighting a Orc that looked like a lizard man with  just a different skin and so on. I also have some nagging from some friends who stated, 'hey, your RPG modules are great, why not spin them into a game so we can all play?' So I began the task of doing this. The project first began as a trainer for the World, then it was put to a ray casting type engine and now a fully online, 6DOF BSP engine. Each step involved re-writing the graphics and code but always insuring the core rules stayed the same. This has worked to my advantage.  The actual structure of  Unknown has never really changed, the data is the same as when I started.  The graphics, and technology have just become more intense. I feel that the graphics and the language need to work around the world. The world should not be controlled or limited by the language or the system using it.

Certis: How many people does Ross Systems employ?

Kevin Ross: This is hard to answer since we have two sides to Ross Systems. The Development section has 10 people working on the project at this time. We also have 2 Modelers from Kaid Software assisting us on animating models and also creating skins for us. We have hired a studio to help us finish up textures for the Unknown as well. I have purposely kept the team small to insure quality in the game. Each person knows his/her section inside and out. Because of this we have been able to keep the same look and feel throughout Krythoth, adhere to the core rules and the novels of Krythoth.

Certis: Do you have a publisher for the game or are you looking for one?

Kevin Ross: No, we plan to look for a publisher once the official beta test begins. We do not want to be pressured by a publisher asking us 'are you done yet?' or 'is this done yet?' I want this world to be done when I say so, not when the public or the publisher want it done. If a publisher with the same thinking as us here stepped up we would be more than happy to talk to them.

Certis: Are you or anyone from Ross Systems going to E3?

Kevin Ross: Yes, 2 people from Ross Systems will be nosing around. We were going to set up, but with some internal problems (lawyers issues), we did not. We are looking into the next major conference now. This would be our first public release of the Unknown. 

Certis: How far into the Alpha test of Unknown are you? How many people are involved so far?

Kevin Ross: We are into what we want to call prealpha40. Since the conception of the Unknown, we have had between 114-150 testers. These are testers within a 50 mile radius of Ross Systems. It has allowed us the ability to hold meetings, user groups and so on to work out issues in a group and not over email. We meet at least 1 a month, sometimes twice to 4 times a month to get problems worked out in a group. They have been great supporting the Unknown, and a great asset to the us here. It has allowed my team and myself to concentrate on the lands and not testing. We have written DEBUG commands and taught the testers on how to use them which has actually allowed them to assist us on fixing problems. 

Certis: MMORPGs released in the last year have not been as story intensive as some would hope. You mentioned plots in our last interview, could you explain exactly what plots are and how they will be implemented in both multiplayer and single player?

Kevin Ross: Plots and quests are actually two different entities in the Unknown. Quests are as they sound.  A task is given to a player or players to complete and then receive an award. They are done in simplest terms. Plots on the other hand are actual programs built into the world to be played out. We have over 200+ plots inside the Unknown. These plots can spin off from quests, or even from other plots. The staff of Ross Systems will play some of these plots, while others are played by the NPC’s. They are actual events of the lands. Before you ask , here's a small example. Orcs will begin to raid the city of Aquatish. The plot might be to find out why; a quest might be for you to rescue the city. We will not spawn 100 Orcs to attack the city, they will actually gather up somewhere before the attack.

Certis: Developers have often talked about what a large task it is to create a compelling multiplayer experience. How has developing both a single and multiplayer component with such a small team affected development?

Kevin Ross: To be honest, the development of multi-player and single player has not created much of a problem at all. We have always looked at the Unknown as a single player game first. Multi-player came afterwards. We do strongly believe to have a great multi-player game you have to have a great single player game. You must have a base to start on and work off of.

Certis: There are a few features you mentioned in our last interview that left me quite excited. Owning a house, purchasing a guild hall, riding a horse (big one there) and flight. Are these features still being implemented? 

Kevin Ross: Owning a house is still inside the world. We have a finite amount, so get them while you can. Guild homes can still be purchased. Riding a horse is still supported. And yes, flight is supported also.

Certis: In our last interview you were unable to disclose any information about the game engine. Can you give us any details on its capabilities?

Kevin Ross: We can go into a little detail about our game engine. We have signed NDA’s with key people not to discuss core abilities of the engine. We have taken a core engine and actually written 100's of API's of our own within it. Overall it is a 6 DOF engine, with almost every feature underneath the sun. We do support colored lighting; colored FOG, alpha transparency, multi-screen resolutions, surround sound support, MP3 support, 3ds models importation, model shadows, multi-layer skies and so on. We do plan to release a world editor and model editor within the engine. We feel this will allow the players the ability to edit their homes, or player skins, to allow for more customization within the game.

Certis: How will Unknown distinguish itself among the slew of new online RPGs being released in the next year?

Kevin Ross: I believe what makes the Unknown different than other games is a few things. One is that we are Unknown. We have kept ourselves small and quiet. This has allowed us to devote our time in the world’s development. What is going to make us stand out is our attention to detail, and the way we listen to users. Another is that your actions, no matter how big or small, could have dire effects upon the entire world of Krythoth. From the person who draws the wand of G’thol to the person who finds the Hall of Heroes, the world will always change, not just by the plots that are going on within it but by what the users request us to do.

Certis: Which features in Unknown are you the most proud of?

Kevin Ross: The features we are most proud of the unknown are our models and stories. We actually use 28 different player models and over 100+ monster models within the Unknown. Our player models exceed almost 1200 frames of animation each and some of our monsters exceed 2000 frames, not counting the NPC models you will see throughout the Unknown. The Unknown plots and stories are the strongest you will have ever seen in a RPG world. We have 5 novels written, which allows us to shape and prepare our coding to the next chapter. The world has a future.  It is not being made up or created after the fact but was all laid out to begin with.

Certis: Personal question, when do I get a crack at this bad boy? ;)

Kevin Ross: We are finishing up our last major problem - our Plot problem. A dedicated server that actually controls plots within the Unknown worlds is key to the actual development of the Land of Krythoth. During our last test, we actually turned the server on to insure that our plots would start. We found a serious bug that allowed the users, if cunning enough, to find out about plots before they could happen. So we are re-coding this area now. At this time I am 75% done.  We need at least another 4-5 weeks to finish up.

Certis: The compulsory question: How much of Unknown is complete right now?

Kevin Ross: We are over the 65% mark.  I can say this with ease. We are actually heading down the home stretch. Not as fast I would like, but our motto has never changed. It will be done when I say it is done. This world is one never seen before and we want to set the new stage of RPG world creation. A new standard has to be set forth, and we believe the small guys can do it.

 Big thanks to Kevin Ross for taking the time to answer our questions! Now back to Altered Worlds!
