Okay...

Cracked open the Jewel case, re-enabled auotplay, and popped the upgrade into my CD drive. Away we go...got a nifty little Upgrade, Uninstall, or Exit window. Not wanting to go back now, I clicked Upgrade...

And got a "Not enough free space on drive D:" message. Don't panic. From what I've heard this is very common. After verifying that I did indeed have 3+ gigs free (sigh) I continued on... Just a note here, you may want to back up your UO directory before upgrading...I got about half way through and had a blue screen of joy "fatal exception" pop up. Probably cause I overclock but you never know. Rebooted and the next install went through perfectly...

You are then instructed to go to "www.ultima-registration.com". You get stuck with this fun filled task whether you upgrade or buy the full box version. On the plus side, the 2+ hour wait to get into the site seems to have disappeared after that first day...

Heeding the warnings to wait 15 minutes before logging in to the new lands, I kick back with the new manual and UO Stratics to plan my entrance. After logging back in, and experiencing a complete lack of success in getting in to the new lands, I discover that you should log in and out once before you try to get into the world of The Second Age.

Bingo...(By the way, an easy way to tell if you can get in is to look at the nifty new chat button on the paper doll. If it's grey, you aren't up and running yet). Since I usually base out of Britain, I head for the sewers...

(Another note here, you may want to check around as some entrances lead to one of the two new villages, while the rest lead to somewhat inhospitable regions. I didn't have any problems, but I have heard that PK's are starting to hang around some of the entrance points in the new lands waiting for prey). I'm not even going to go into the 15 minutes I spent trying to get to the end of the Britain sewers...suffice to say get a rune to the Moonglow Mage shop entrance. It pops you out in the village of Papua, nice and safe in a guard zone.

So I finally wind my way through the Britannian sewers (bloody frogs) and step out into the swamps of the new lands...

First impression? God damn the ground is bumpy.

Next - The Flora is nice, the Fauna is a wee bit nasty...
