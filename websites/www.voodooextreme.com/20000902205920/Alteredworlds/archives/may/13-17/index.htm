FAQ

New - Is there going to be a Beta test group?
There won't be a Beta test for Fallout 2. RPG's in general don't lend well to Beta Testing. They are generally done for strategy or action games.

New - When is the Demo going to be released?
Doing a demo would delay the shipping of Fallout 2 significantly, therefor we will not be doing one. Doing a demo for a complicated RPG isn't a simple process, it would delay Fallout 2 by many months.

New - I hear you can pimp your spouse. Is this true?
Yes, this is a dark world. Since you can play either a good or evil character, there are a lot of "bad" things you can do. It's up to you to role play your character to the light or dark side. ; )

New - If I attack one guard, will everyone on the map know immediately?
No, as mentioned. The AI is getting a very large improvement in many areas. Guards will now have chance of hearing another guards cry for help depending on where they are on the map and weather or not they are in a building. Line of sight and hearing are being expanded and improved.

New - How much bigger is Fallout 2 going to be?
Fallout 2 has been advertised as being 50% bigger, but it is actually much bigger than that. There are many more quests, maps, NPCs, weapons, monsters, and all around things to do.

New - Will Fallout 2 have expansion packs or add-ons?
There are not any plans at this time, but we are making the modifications to the engine now, so that we will be able to in the future. This does not mean that we will be making expansions, it just gives us the ability if we want to.

Ray on BG
Lot of good talking out there today. Ray just hit the official BG Board and spit out an answer to someone talking about the game being pushed back. Personally I am glad they pushed it back...it will just make the final product that much sweeter! Read on chipmunk: - Sacrates

I don't want to start a flame war, but I feel that I must answer your criticism in some way. To begin with, I must acknowledge a couple of things:
Certainly the game has been greatly delayed from the original release date we were aiming for last fall. Certainly the NID wasn't put out when we had hoped.

But, bear with me while I try and answer your post:

First off, if you are unhappy with the way that the game has progressed, don't blame my team, blame me. I am directing the production of the game here at BioWare and if any promises were broken, it has been my fault. No one has lied about anything that I am aware of, however.

Second, we made a conscious decision many months ago that we were not going to cut features from our original design for the game, grand and over-ambitious though it was. Instead, we have committed to make the RPG that we have always wanted to play, and to take the time required to complete it and remove all known bugs, and balance it for as long as necessary.

Interplay has supported us in this decision and for this we are grateful.

Third, the NID has been delayed by Interplay because they were concerned that a lot of the video cards' DirectX drivers had problems under 16-bit and higher modes (few games have used these to this point). We are trying to resolve these problems so that as many people will be able to look at the NID as possible.

Finally, we are working hard to complete the game - my team is pouring their heart and souls into Baldur's Gate every day. It is the game that we have all always wanted to play. If you are unhappy with the game, return it. If you want to wait until a friend recommends it or a reviewer you trust likes it, please do so. But wait until the game is out - we think it will be a great game.

BIG fat BG post
Scott over at Bioware has just made a good sized post on the progress of BG and the NID. Sit back, relax, and read on: - Sacrates

Warning! The following is my opinion only, and does not reflect the opinions of BioWare or Interplay etc.

I thought that I would give you guys an update on what's happining with the NID....

----------

Well I just finished some bug fixes with the NID and have sent it off to Interplay for more QA. Hopefully I won't see it on my desk tomorrow with "just one more little problem". Since it should be out "any time now", I thought that I would share some of my thoughts on the overall quality of the NID vs. the final game.

The NID was mostly finished last september, but was delayed while we waited for, sounds, voice acting, and some other misc. stuff. The biggest delay was the voice acting. Since the sound studio and actors are hired on a daily basis, it didn't make any sense to rent the studio for the few sounds that the NID needed. As such, the NID had to wait until we were ready to record the voice acting for the main game, and for Interplay's sound guys to be free. After that, we had to make some minor changes, a graphic test, and send it through QA.

I don't want to sound like I'm making excuses, but I don't want you to think that we've just been sitting on it for 7 months, while snickering "hee hee, Let's make them wait just a little longer..." We have been trying to get this out a fast as posible without slowing down the production of the real game.

---------

Ok, enough rambling. The point I want to make is the NID is based on a 7 month old version of the engine and we have make a lot of improvements since then.

In case you are curious, here is a list of some of the improvements that we have done in those 7 months....

1. Character Animations: We have redone a lot of the character animations since then. The mages have better looking robes, and the kobolds and gibbering have redone animations. Overall, the animations are noticable better.

2. Lightning: In the NID the lightning is drawn with line drawing routines. We have since replaced this with a nifty glow effect. If have seen the screen shot on bgchronicles, you will know what I am talking about.

3. Rain: We have redone the rain since the NID. It looks more like real rain falling now.

4. GUI's: The GUI's are still a work in progress. We are constantly updating the functionality and art.

5. Spell Effects: We have redone almost all of the spell effects since the NID. We have added some really cool glows effects and have removed most of the pixel effects. We did keep the sparkle trail though.

6. Sound Effects: While we have most of the voice acting, Interplay's sound wizards are still hard at work, tinkering with final game sounds. From the little that I have heard, the final sounds should be top notch.

7. Voice acting: Yes the characters in the final game do say more than "For the gloy of Helm!!". Just be thankful it's not the place holder sound that Ray had screeched into the mike.

8. Music: While the music in the NID is really good, it's not the real music for Baldur's Gate. I have heard a rumor that it will be recorded by the Prauge Symphony Orchestra, but this is unconfirmed. Any guesses on where the NID music came from?

Hmmm... it, looks like we have been tinkering with almost everthing since the NID was made.

So I hope that you enjoy the NID, and keep in mind... If you liked the NID, the final game should blow you away.

Scott Greig
Lead Programmer for Baldur's Gate

8. Can someone please explain how the multiplayer is going to work? I've seen so many different postings, I'm not sure if they all are correct or if they are all wrong. IP addresses, servers, etc.

This is a complex topic. Essentially, a player spawns a multiplayer game. This player is the "leader," and he or she controls things like what is importable (stats, stats plus exp and levels, or stats, exp/levels, and items) by other players, what is visible to other players (inventory, character record sheets, etc.). Other players join in via a GameSpy-like interface (we may use GameSpy, actually), importing as many characters or creating as many characters as they have been assigned slots by the leader (i.e. players can be assigned more than one slot if the leader so desires). They then play through the same storyline as in the single player game, cooperatively. Hope this capsule summary helped!

Gamespy? Interesting indeed.....This little program is in store for some major changes in the future it seems to me.
