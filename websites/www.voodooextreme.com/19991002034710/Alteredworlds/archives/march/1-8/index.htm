Update on the project...
Looking Glass Studios, has updated their sites with 2 new screenshots, and not just your regular screens, OpenGL screens and are they sweet!!


These are sweet shots, and can't wait to get my hands on this puppy... also on this subject while I was at Psychonews they have a little blurb about the development:

".. The design team has mainly been mocking up levels and brainstorming mission designs, now that we have our prototype missions to feed our ideas and confirm or disprove our suspicions. We've got a lot of good ideas, and a few crackpot schemes. Only some of them are stolen from System Shock, Terra Nova, and Ultima Underworld, and the ones that are we promise to disguise so it's harder to tell where we stole them from.

Kate's been integrating her creature motion tools better into our level-editor, one small effect of which is that it will be easier for us to change creature models and generally get more critters types in our draft levels (and screenshots). Chris is working on the player-character body physics, which is all wrapped up in implementing crouching, crawling, impact whiplashes in combat, maybe leaning, eventually climbing ... basically all movement and maneuver modes where the PC doesn't move like a tank. A side effect of this is that it's really easy to loosen the player-character's neck until his head bobbles around and you get seasick. Can't argue with fun like that."

Gothica....
I just noticed that Tinman Studios has updated their new page with an development update for Gothica...

We've been working hard on the Necromancer area in Gothica. This is a very
large dungeon that is strewn across an ancient, ruined landscape. The action
takes place both inside and outside of these ruins. Dilapidated rope bridges
made of bone shakily span pools of stagnant sludge. This is the home of the
Undead.

This new area includes a lot of cool interactive features. The rope bridges
actually stress under the weight of your character. Skiffs can be used to
float along some of the larger water areas. We've been working on a healthy
set of monsters for this area as well. Right now, we will have zombies,
skeletons, mummies, flying bat skulls, undead knights, and skeleton spiders.

All of these new changes in Gothica are very extensive. The Necromancer
area looks so good and so cohesive that all of our current screen shots have
been put to shame. Along with a new user interface system, we've been able
to bring the game up another notch in quality. We are so excited about these
changes that we are preparing a big update to the web site with new screen shots... "

And when that update happens.. you'll be sure picken that you'll see the screens here..

Crank that Engine
Looks like Christopher Delay (from Dungeoun Keeper) is making a new RPG based engine. Head on over to the Vault Network to get the full story.  Just to keep you happy i'll toss this small blurb your way:

- Impressive lighting effects, complete with correctly placed shadows (pre-calculated) and radiosity.
- A high level scripting language ( based on LISP ) which runs the actual game.
- Sound support for .WAV and .MOD
- An editor (ShadowED) which is used to construct all of the levels.

 
New 3D RPG coming soon
Just got this little piece of news about a new RPG called "Escape or die trying". Here is a quote:

One of the most interesting new titles shown at Psygnosis' recent Psybadek showcase to the press was Escape Or Die Trying. Featuring four different characters to play, Escape ODT is an RPG which sees gamers trying to escape from a tower which wouldn't be out of place in a Riana Rouge bondage fantasy. The tower is a full 3D world made entirely of polygons, and initial hype is promising level bosses comprised of more than a thousand polygons. A thirty frames per second frame-rate is promised, and the game is to be released on PlayStation and PC, with the PC version replete with multiplayer options.
