E3

Well here we are, E3 is all finished and we are left with a ton of goodies from the convention. Let me set this up for you and I'll start at the beginning. First my Father pulled out his Plumping tools and used them to fix my Mother up....oh wait wrong story. Luckily I live only a few short hours drive away from Atlanta so we jump in the Altered Mobile and head on over. First thing I do is pick up my pass and hit the convention going in no particular direction since this place was huge. When you first walk in one of the Wings, you are totally assaulted by one thing......noise! And lots of it. Huge Flat screens hanging on walls showing new games, scantily clad women hanging around doing nothing but looking good, and the best part.....games everywhere. So without further ado let's see what we have here in no particular order:

Thief: The Dark Project:
I saw enough of this game to give an assumption that it should be good. However the AI seemed really bad. When the main character shot arrows at some of the swordsman they wouldn't really react and walk in some predetermined path. I didn't really get to talk to any of the programmers on it either. But the graphics looked clean and the idea seems very cool. It's all about Stealth and hiding in shadows etc. Overall once they kill these few bugs it should be a solid game.

Revenant:
In the Eidos section, behind all the Tomb Raider 3 hoopla lurked Revenant. The graphics looked slick for a 2D game no doubt. They had a semi playable demo there which you could fight a creature or two and talk to an NPC mage. Combat was still in it's infancy but really seemed like it could be fun. Very authentic feeling when in battle with the creatures. Also movement needed much work considering your main character seemed to float across the floor at times. Also talking to NPC's wasn't so much of choosing dialogue but listening to them. However I only met the one Mage so I can't really comment too much on that. Hopefully they will have the corridors and environments filled with more nasties when this game hits the shelves later this year. But from what I saw it looked visually better than other games of this type and perspective with the exception of BG. However this game should be very nice when it's released.

Diablo 2:
Probably the most disappointing game for me at the show. If you were a fan of the original then you'll love this. From what the programmer I talked to said, it will be more RPG like this time around which can only be a good thing. There is a run feature built in which was nice. And the Night affects were also well done especially with the new lighting. But overall this game is identical so far to the original except with outdoor scenes. When I first started to talk to the programmer he showed me around the charred remains of Tristram (SP?) which looked great. You could also run in and out of buildings too. The creatures had better death animations and combat was identical to the original. One thing this guy was vehement about was that Diablo 2 wasn't just more of the same. So I assume there is a LOT of more development to go yet on this title. And I do mean a lot. I was hoping Diablo 2 would be a bigger change but from looking at it you couldn't tell the difference between it and Diablo. Again, it's still too early to say though since the Interface hasn't been worked on much yet etc. But this is Blizzard so we can almost expect it to be great.

King's Quest: Mask of Eternity:
I talked to one of the Programmers on this for a little while. And also saw Roberta Williams playing it the day after. Anyway, he showed me around and answered all the questions I had about it. The graphics were extremely clean and well done. It really had a nice animated look to it that wasn't too childish looking. This game will be much more action oriented this time around for sure. I asked him if he had played Unreal yet and he said of course. With that I asked if they would have any cinematic touches to the game similar to Unreal's. Of course he showed me quite a few and were they nice. There will be a lot of in-game cinematics as well as cut scenes. In one particular scene he killed a Skeleton King on top of a tower and it toppled over the side after a few words to the main character. Overall this game should be a definite winner. Especially since you can toss a rope up a wall and scale it up and down! Little touches like this make it an especially cool looking title. I have a strong feeling this will be the best of the King's Quest games for sure.

Drakan:
Oh yeah! This game literally rocks the house down. The music playing in the background was great and had an incredibly adventurous feel to it. Then you see the screens displaying Drakan being played by the programmers and you finally realize why 3D is here to stay. I talked to one of the programmers (Hans) about it and he actually let me play it a bit until I crashed his PC. ;) Anyway, the graphics in this game are nothing short of phenomenal. The combination of flying on the dragon's back and walking around fighting is exhilarating to say the least. The mountains in the background are textured extremely well, and when you see the running water leading into waterfalls you will not believe your eyes. This was the most believable experience of actually flying on the back of a Dragon you will ever have. The Dragons are absolutely amazing when breathing breath weapons. Fire is the most common, but I saw Ice also! Hans said that Lighting is going to be added also and will have reflective powers which searches down all live enemies and kills them. Multiplayer is already in and I saw 3 of the programmers hook up for deathmatch of sorts. If you consider flying on the back of a Dragon a deathmatch. One of the coolest affects that I saw was when you killed another Dragon....it would go into a sort of spasm in the air and take a dive into the ground. You could even follow it down by taking your own Dragon into a powerdive! This is one of those games that needs to be seen to be really believed. Hopefully we'll get our chance this year?!

Alright, that will wrap up part one of E3. I just wanted to get a few of the RPG's out of the way before I dove into the biggies. In the next review of E3, I am going to go into a bit of depth on Everquest and Baldur's Gate. I will also talk on Ultima 9 and Asheron's Call which was one hell of a surprise for me! Needless to say I'm saving the best for last! I should have that up tomorrow! Keep your eyes peeled.
