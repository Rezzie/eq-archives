Vampire:The Masquerade - Redemption
by: hoochie galore , girl gamer

  As a fairly new player on the gaming scene - a fledgling, if you will - I don’t have a large repertoire of played games to compare Redemption to.   Nonetheless, I’ve picked up an appreciation of what makes a good RP/adventure game: engrossing storyline, unobtrusive and handy interface, character development, and of course eye candy.  Vampire has all of these.  It is a quality game that I am quite enjoying playing - when I can rip Certis away from it.  In the midst of all this loveliness, however, there are a couple of things that detract from the experience.  

In-Game Dialogue
   The rather florid voice-overs and cut-scene dialogue remind me of Soul Reaver to a large degree, as though the key word ‘vampire’ triggers some kind of Poe-esque quality in the souls of writers.  The bad guys especially are afflicted with staggeringly over-written lines such as ‘I think it best to bury you in a shallow grave with oaken stakes to pierce your hearts, where grave worms shall hollow out your noble brows and robust cheeks.’  I mean, really.  The love scenes between Christof and whatsername, the nun, were really painful.  Improper use of olde English, repetition of one idea over and over again - how many times do we really need to hear Christof say, ‘Then I am truly damned!’  Yes.  Yes, you are. Though it was rather entertaining to hear him groan the way he does when nun-girl confesses her love for him.   If that’s damnation, serve me up some.  Hopefully the dialogue will get better when I get to the modern-day half of the game.

The Save Set-up
At the beginning, this bothered me a fair bit.   Unlike, say, Half-Life, in Redemption you can only save at set save-points or when you load from one area to another, at which point it automatically overwrites the last load-save.   As I understand it, the game was designed this way to increase the tension of game-play.  It makes things a lot more nerve-racking when you get to the last bit of a level and realize that dying now means you have to trek through all those crappy rats again to get back.  And then Christof gets Embraced (how nasty was Ekaterina the Wise’s mouth? Ekaterina the Fugly would a more appropriate title.  Blech!) and I realized that the ‘Scroll of Walking the Abyss’ basically serves as not only a mobile save point (loading through to the haven is an auto-save) but also a way of dumping your junk into the vault so you need not leave any goodies behind.  At about 67 gold - my Christof is a sexy bugger and gets the best prices from the old biddy in Golden Lane - it is also fairly cheap.  There are some compensations for doing a level over, too, as the loot seems to get better each time you have to redo a section.  I didn’t test this thoroughly, but Certis had to repeat much of the first dungeon, and the general quality of treasure did go up.

   Other than these little quibbles, the game is excellent.  The environments are absolutely beautiful, and the use of light effects very nice.  The torch is a fine example of this - the effect is so good that you hardly notice it, which to me is the highest praise.  IMO, a game environment should be immersive, not ostentatious.  A’course, that could just be my ignorant self.  Certis tends to drool over the look of a game a lot more than I do.  The AI of the enemies is wonderful, not too smart, not too dumb.  With the control set up the way it is - you click on a point in the landscape and Christof trots on over by himself - poor pathfinding could have been a real nightmare for the gamer, but Christof hardly ever gets hung up on corners, and better yet, neither does his group.  The only place I really noticed a problem was on the stairway at the University of Prague Haven.

   All in all, a quality game with few bugs - compare it to, say, Drakan’s release and you’ll see what I mean.   It’s the little details that make all the difference.  Such as the expression on Christof’s face when he’s sucking someone’s blood.  Again, damnation looking pretty good from over here...

   Look for a full review when I finish the game.
