A campfire chat w/ Jason Struck

by Sacrates (March 28, 1998)

Here you have a campfire chat with one of the many folks involved with getting the anticipated Gothica out on the store shelves.   Sacrates had a chance to conversate with Jason Struck co-producer of the upcoming hit! Well let's hear what they have been saying:

Sacrates: To start, lets go through the perfunctory introductions shall we? Please give our Altered readers your name, and what you do.

Jason: I am Jason Struck, the art director and co-producer of Gothica here at Tinman Studios.

Sacrates: Lets go over a few Engine related questions. Will your 3D engine have any kind of natural physics to it? (i.e. getting knocked down while in battle or arrows arcing throught the air etc)

Jason: Yes we will have lots of natural physics in Gothica. Arrows will arc through the air. Barrels, crates and wooden doors will splinter or catch on fire and burn when smashed with a weapon or hit with magic. Monsters can be dismembered. Body parts will bounce off of walls and heads will roll. You will also be able to jump and pull yourself up onto ledges.

Sacrates:  Will there be any kind of dynamic lighting affects similar to those found in the new FPS style games like Unreal and Quake 2? Will we see lights trail along after a flaming missile? (Or magic)

Jason: There will be tons of dynamic lighting effects in Gothica. All of the magic in Gothica will use dynamic lighting. If your character is carrying a torch the suroundings will light up as you walk around. Using a fire wand or casting a fire spell will have a glowing effect on the suroundings.

Sacrates: What types of environmental affects are you planning on using? Rain, snow, night and day etc. And will they affect combat or movement?

Jason: Traveling through water or bogs will be slower unless you have a boat. It will rain in Gothica.

Sacrates:  What type of interaction do you plan on using in the game? Will there be designated combat areas like in Diablo or will you be walking through a town and get into a fight?

Jason:  Combat can take place anywhere. There are no safe havens in Gothica. Towns will have guards so fighting in towns will be difficult.

Sacrates: Will combat be a point and click kind of deal or will you be able to use various styles. Such as slashing, chopping or jabbing with swords?

Jason: The game engine is designed to arcade standards. Combat will be fast and furious. You will be able to attack your opponents in many ways.

Sacrates: How many players do you plan on supporting in Multiplayer and how will connection be handled? (via Gamespy etc)

Jason: To be determined.

Sacrates: Finally, what will set Gothica apart from other games? What is it's biggest selling points?

Jason: Gothica is very complex yet very easy to use. Average players will find it enjoyable as well as hardcore RPG fans. Gothica also has tons of replayability.

Altered Worlds would like to thank Jason Shruck for taking the time in his busy schedule at Tinman Studios to do this little interview with us!  We really appreciate it Jason and thanks again.
