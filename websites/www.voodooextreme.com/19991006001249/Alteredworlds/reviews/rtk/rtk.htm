Return to Krondor
By Bowmeister

There are two ways you can pretty well guarantee a good reception for a CRPG - set the game in a well known fantasy world that already comes with a large fanbase, or release a sequel to an already succesful game. Return to Krondor manages to accomplish both in one box.

Return to Krondor is based in author Raymond E. Feist's world of Midkemia. Midkemia is a world filled with magic and intrigue, along with a rich religious and political history. If you've managed to somehow miss his books, do yourself a favor and pick up Magician:Apprentice - you won't regret the read (When you get done with R.E.F.'s works, also pick up his collaboration series with Janny Wurtz that looks at the goings on in the world connected to Midkemia, Kelewan). RtK is also the official sequel to the incredible Betrayal at Krondor - one of the games that redefined the Computer RPG field.

Having said that, you can definately jump into RtK without playing the first game or reading the books. It's nice to have that wider picture of the goings on in the world of Midkemia, but RtK does bring you into the world and get you up to speed on the major players with a minimum of fuss...the excellent introductory sequence sets a dark and sinister tone to the story, as you watch a late night pirate raid on an Ishapian trader go wrong. Despite taking the vessel and a fortune in gold, the pirate captain, one "Bear", is furious when the ship sinks before he can locate the item he wanted.
