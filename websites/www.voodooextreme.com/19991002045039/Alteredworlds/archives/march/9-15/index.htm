Something wicked this way comes
While this is not RPG related it will have a HUGE impact on how we play our games and especially RPG's in the future.

At the Future Trends Conference on London yesterday, Psygnosis used its invitation to display the firm's first DVD-based game. Called Lander, it's an updated version of C64 classic Thrust, in a full 3D world. Psygnosis displayed the DVD capabilities such as multiple camera angles, multiple languages on one disk, MPEG 2 FMV and music in Dolby 5.1 digital sound. Companies in attendance included the likes of Dolby, Creative Labs and Toshiba.

Personally I think the sooner the better for DVD. This is what we really need for the high performance and storytelling ability of an RPG. CD Roms simply can't cut the cheese in that depatment.

March 10, 1998
We're busy trying to put the final touches on the game. Hence the long delays between What's New sections - we literally don't have the time free to write a lot of stuff, even though (paradoxically) more stuff is happening now than ever before. Here's the scoop on what we've worked on over the past few weeks:
End movie is now finished aside from adding in sounds. It actually looks better than the Intro movie, if you can believe it.
Data entry and clipping of the terrain art moving forward and should be complete in 2 weeks or so.
Doing fixes of clipping bugs.
Most of the GUI's now work - You can actually play the game! Creating a character is fairly slick.
Data entry of the first two chapters is ongoing - we're entering game data chapter by chapter, linking areas together, populating areas, bringing monsters and critters to life slowly but surely.
Sounds received for most of the voices in the game, and are being entered into our resource database now being mastered down at Interplay. It's fun to actually hear the voices of the characters that we have labored on for so long - they really come to life!
network code is still being worked on, as we develop the message layer and network layer code.
ray

Rune Controversy
And still the debate rages about Runes and their purpose. UOVault has a letter from DD on this very subject that may put more fire in the kettle. Read on oh gentle reader:

Here's what I said before, only in more detail. There are too many objects, and when many players have literally hundreds of runes, that's a prime candidate for reduction. And that is the motive at this time. Yes, we want to curtail use of recall, blah blah blah, but if we wanted to curtail the use of recall, my favored route is to charge mana based on distance and get rid of the current dungeon restrictions. We could go other routes. We could make it so you can only have one rune on you, or five, or whatever. We could make them "unstable entities" so they tend to go "pop" if they are unused for too long. Whatever. But zapping them when used is simple, fairly foolproof, and still lets you use them exactly as you do now. (Always mark a rune each time you recall). Some people won't do this for reasons of [time/mana/effort/carelessness], and hopefully rune hoarding will be reduced.

The immediate goal is item reduction, folks, not recall curtailing. Recall curtailing from this is at best a side effect. As I posted before, I feel that recall curtailing will only work once there's enough to do in the space that you tend to skip over now--enough adventure on the road that you don't mind walking through it. And before talking about recall curtailing, I'd rather talk about ways to make that journey more fun. On the day we manage that, then people will walk everywhere because it's cooler to do so. :)

If you have better ideas on how to keep players to fewer runes hoarded, please pass 'em along. We're not wedded to this solution, it just seems the clear way to go at this time.
