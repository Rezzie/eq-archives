Functional description

Columns are targeted range spells, damaging immediately up to 3 mobs (or 4 with difficulty) in a moderately wide radius around the target. You will never hurt yourself, unless you targeted yourself. The cast and recast times (6 seconds) are moderately long. The resistance pattern is standard, similar to that of Shocks.

Visual EFX

Columns generally create lasting vertical orange or blue flames around each affected mob, and represent a heavy trial for the graphic cards.

Critic

Columns are staple AE damage spells, the most genuine, straightforward, all-purpose, predictable, easy-to-cast AE damage spells for casual use in group. They also have a maybe-overpowered application which is "quad-kiting" (see below). Unfortunately, there is no Column available between levels 34 (Circle of Force) and 51 (Pillar of Frost), and this is a wide gap.

Columns have good (and relatively even for their level) strength and damage concentration: that is, compared with DD, they inflict respectable damage to each mob (they can kill blue mobs with repeated use), and they provide a damage/mana bonus versus DD even if they hit only 2 mobs (they don't devastate the mana bar tickling the mobs, like Al'Kabor spells do). Columns pay off VERY nicely on 3 mobs.

Casual rewards

- In the early fight, pull the additional mob away from the fighters with the Column damage, and park it with Root (or, if Bonds of Force was used first, pull the additional mob and walk it around, possibly testing your skill by means of homing spells on the main mob while walking the additional one).

- Gamble with your damage/mana ratio in easy multiple-mob fights. Especially, the ratio improvement is much welcome post-50, because post-50 DD has disappointing damage/mana (most level 52+ Wizards use Rains instead of Draughts as their main damage spells, but Columns still compete effectively with Rains on 2-3 mobs).

- In the late fight, help finish the main mob, while starting to proceed with the next.

Advanced tactics: "quad-kiting"

"Quad" stands for "quadri" (I think), that is, kiting up to 4 mobs at once. I suspect that quad-kiting was not possible, or hardly, when the game was first released, because Columns had a much narrower radius than they have now. The radius was increased in autumn 1999 (for Columns only) and in early 2000 (for all AE spells), and, as an outcome, quad-kiting became a reasonable challenge. When Kunark was released and the strong level 51 Pillar of Frost became available, some level 51 Wizards with more power than knowledge, who had never considered AE damage, suddenly went shouting throughout Norrath that AE spells were tremendous (although they could have quad-kited with the level 34 Circle of Force when they were younger). The ability to quad-kite (ability shared between Druids and Wizards) creates legitimate class envy (many classes can't even solo one blue mob effectively, let alone 4) and I believe it's all a big blunder from Verant. I suspect that Al'Kabor spells were initially given awful damage/mana precisely because their wide radius created a multiple-kiting threat, and the original designers of the game didn't want multiple kiting to be possible (the awful damage/mana ensures that even high-green mobs survive the kiting). Verant now seems to tolerate that kind of near-abuse, as long as it's only the feat of a tiny number of players. I find quad-kiting slightly distasteful, and I also believe that the time and effort to find and regroup the mobs (and Snare them all while they run) isn't worth the increased mana-efficiency in most cases; I find single-mob kiting, on an occasional basis, much more versatile and time-saving in the long run.

Alendine Gast made a very complete page describing quad-kiting. In short, it is necessary to find the right mobs, in the right zone. The mobs must be low-blue or medium-blue; a green mob in the pack will die for no benefit, and a high-blue mob (or white or yellow or red) will not die. Timing is more important than for single-mob kiting, and Spirit of Wolf (or Journeyman's Boots) is a must. The mobs must be tightly packed, or the Column will miss some, so, they must be all Snared, and they must also be of the same type, so that they have the same running speed; running in circles around them to regroup them is necessary before Snaring. More castings are required to achieve the kill than when using DD, so, Bonds of Force will tend to have insufficient duration.

Requested improvements

Columns are near-perfect and don't need any improvement (allthough it can be noted that the level 16 spell is a bit weak, and that there are no Columns at levels 39, 44 and 49). If a change comes, it is more likely to be a radius nerf than some form of improvement.
