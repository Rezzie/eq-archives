Functional description

Al'Kabor spells (including the mysterious "Ap'Sagor" spell) are targeted range spells, damaging immediately (and easily) up to 5 mobs in a wide radius around the target. You will often hurt yourself with the spell at short (or almost-medium) range. The cast and recast times (9 seconds) are long. The resistance pattern (except for the level 39 spell) is most probably standard, similar to that of Shocks, but it might be a bit more favorable (full damage scored more often) - this is difficult to confirm (at any rate, Al'Kabor spells don't allow to overcome exceptional resistance to a particular energy type). The level 39 spell is all-or-nothing, highly resisted; this is a drawback, with no compensation beside a split-second stun of questionable value (all-or-nothing spells are generally acceptable only if their stats are nicely swollen, or if their non-damage effects are bright).

Al'Kabor spells have a variable pushback effect which may interrupt spellcasting. Beware that NOT ALL the spells have this effect, although you will often hear that they have: the level 12 spell doesn't push anything; the level 20 spell pushes players but not mobs; the level 29 spell has a dubious push; and the level 39 spell has a definite push like Force Shock has. I doubt that the level 49 spell has any push.

Visual EFX

Al'Kabor spells create a brief nebulous burst of blue sparks around each target, and represent only a light trial for the graphic cards.

Critic

Al'Kabor spells are extremely lousy cousins of Columns.

The benefits versus Columns are hardly worth mentioning: the wider radius, the possibility to hit 1-2 more mobs, and (maybe) the slightly lesser resistance, are a kind of "guarantee" that a certain amount of damage will be dealt on the whole, but this guarantee is not very valuable (if you want "guaranteed" damage, use DD); and, given how mob hate lists work, you may provoke 3 mobs with a Column in casual play with tolerable exposure, but provoking 5 mobs at once with an Al'Kabor spell is probably foolish unless you have a special plan for surviving. The alleged pushback effect is not very valuable either, because some PB spells will do the same trick for much cheaper (although not at range).

The drawbacks versus Columns are heavy: besides the long cast and recast times, and the possibility to hurt oneself, Al'Kabor spells are VERY WEAK. Many of them do markedly less damage per mob than a lower-rank Column, and this alone would make them unattractive (size matters to a Wizard). Al'Kabor spells also have a very low damage concentration: you can't hope to kill mobs (even high-green) with repeated cast, because mana will run out. This limitation makes sense, being probably meant to prevent the kiting of multiple mobs; but it should be compensated by other benefits, which are nowhere to be found. The post-50 Al'Kabor spells have a slightly better damage concentration, and might allow to kill some very-low-blue mobs with repeated use, but post-50 Columns still beat them hands down. In other words, the Al'Kabor spells devastate your mana bar instead of devastating your enemies. In a different perspective, they are poor for damage/mana gambling: you need to hit 4 mobs to earn a noticeable damage/mana bonus over DD (or hit 3 mobs, for the post-50 spells), whereas you would need to hit only 2 mobs with a Column to earn the same kind of bonus. Al'Kabor spells are a bad bargain, a mockery better forgotten, and it's a great pity, because they are the sole Wizard-only AE damage spell line, and we could have expected it to be the shiniest, instead of being the lousiest !

Rewards

Al'Kabor spells have no identified application, whether casual or specially planned, although many Wizards have tried to put them to use. The less absurd things which I heard, or can think of, are:

- Use the wide radius for an "efficient" initial pull of a 4-5 mob group; but you'd better pull them in some safer way.

- Cast blindly Shock Spiral of Al'Kabor (the least expensive spell with pushback against mobs) in a crowd of at least 3 mobs including casters, for the hazardous benefit of some spell interruption without mana waste versus DD. But, for that kind of benefit, you might as well stand in the middle, and use a more cost-effective PB spell instead, such as Cast Force or Thunderclap.

- In PvP, cast blindly Fire Spiral of Al'Kabor (the least expensive spell with pushback against players) in a crowd of enemy caster/healer players (possibly including some friendly fighter players, who will be less bothered), for the hazardous benefit of some spell interruption. Note that you can hit any number of players that way (the spells have the capacity to hit 5 mobs plus any number of players). This might be the least grotesque application of Al'Kabor.

Requested improvements

In summer 2000, I tried to lobby (to no avail) for the two following improvements for Al'Kabor spells:

- To make them less ridiculous versus Columns: 10% more damage for the mana (for the pre-50 spells), and a mana cost increment of 75 (instead of 50) for the level 20-29-39 spells.

- To make them worth playing, besides being less ridiculous (for all the spells including post-50): either a VERY short cast time, with quasi-chain-casting (so they would be a mass taunt / finishoff red button), or a lasting debuff against the energy type of the same-rank DD.

If interested, you may check if the lobbying post still exists, and add your word: Repair pre-50 Wizard AE !
