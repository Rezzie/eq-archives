Book List - G through L

William Gibson

1)  Neuromancer :  This book almost singlehandedly started a new sub-genre of SF called cyper punk.  I don't know if it was truly the first, but it is definitely one of the best.  A cool romp in a world of technology that in only the few years since the book's release is already closer to reality.

Terry Goodkind

1)  The Sword of Truth:  I actually make it a point not to even start a series until all of the books are out, because I hate waiting several years to find out what happens next.  This is a rare exception.  This is a dark fantasy series which is just plain intense.  The novels are self contained enough that you won't mind too much that the series is not yet over.  I have to say though that this author has a seriously warped imagination.  (I mean that in a good way).  Wizard's First Rule ;  Stone of Tears ;  Blood of the Fold ;  Temple of the Winds  ; Soul of the Fire (hardback)

Barbara Hambley

1) Darwath Trilogy:  Very nice fantasy trilogy, with all of the classic elements you expect and a few surprises.  There were some follow-up novels, which I have to admit I never read.   The Time of the Dark ;  The Walls of Air ;  The Armies of Daylight

Robert Heinlein

The Granddaddy of all Science Fiction Writers.  He often churned out some real fluff and had some pretty whacked out political views, but when he concentrated on it he could create a masterpiece.  If you want to truly exerience the best of science fiction, try one of these novels:

1)   Stranger in a Strange Land Your mom and dad read this novel.  The ultimate 60's peace and love science fiction novel, it actually introduced a new word into the English Language (Grok).  Despite being somewhat dated, this is still an engrossing read.

2)   The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress An excellent political thriller that introduced artificial intelligence long before Bill Gates got out of diapers.  This one also showed some of Heinlein's more unusual sexual philosophy.

3)   Time Enough for Love This novel stars Heinlein's most unforgettable character in Lazarus Long, a long lived and likeable scoundrel who founded a dynasty.  Fun to read.

4)  Glory Road :  While this is technically science fiction, it is closer to fantasy.  This is a fun adventure story which is written in Heinlein's typical style.  Fantasy by science fiction's best author.  You won't be disappointed with this.

5) Double Star :  An interesting story about an actor who becomes a politician (couldn't happen could it?) in order to impersonate the sick leader of the world.  Fascinating characters and story that is a little dated, but still fun to read.  I believe this won Heinlein a Hugo award.

6) Starship Troopers :  Don't judge this book by the mediocre movie based upon it.  The book is actually a very entertaining look at military training and the role of duty and honor in society.

7) Starman Jones :  I read this book many times when I was a kid and thought going into space was probably the coolest thing you could ever do.  Heinlein captures that feel and writes a great book about the kid who lives his dreams.  Very good reading even now.
 

Frank Herbert

1) Dune Series:  These are considered classics for a reason.  A masterpiece of vision, philosophy and adventure.  These novels can be a little tough to read, but are well worth it.  Dune ;  Dune Messiah ;  Children of Dune ;  God Emperor of Dune ;  Heretics of Dune ;  Chapterhouse : Dune.  And if you liked these, his son recently released a Dune prequel called  Dune : House Atreides
Tracy Hickman/ Margaret Weis

1) The Dragonlance Chronicles and Dragonlance Legends Trilogies:  Sorry Tolkein fans, but these are the best fantasy novels ever written!  If you have not read them, just buy them now.  You won't regret it.  The characters in these books are so vivid and real, that I feel like I know them, and the world is as complex and well thought out as any ever devised.  If you are playing a halfling in EQ and don't know about Tasslehoff Burrfoot, you are missing out on part of your heritage.  They are technically two trilogies, but the six novels really form one set.  Did I tell you these are really good? The Chronicles: Dragons of Autumn Twilight ;  Dragons of Winter Night ;  Dragons of Spring Dawning .  The Legends:   Time of the Twins ;  War of the Twins ;  Test of the Twins

2) The Darksword Trilogy:  Hickman and Weis almost match their Dragonlance novels with these.  Good dark fantasy, with an odd SF twist. Forging the Darksword ;  Doom of the Darksword ;  Triumph of the Darksword

3) The Death Gate Cycle:  Another excellent collaberation between these two authors.  I guess this is technically science fiction, but it reads more like fantasy.  This is definitely not your standard sword and sorcery epic.  It is complex and well written.  I thought a few of the middle novels dragged a little at time, but that will often happen in a series this long and complex. Dragon Wing ;  Elven Star ;  Fire Sea ;  Serpent Mage ;  The Hand of Chaos ;  Into the Labyrinth ;  The Seventh Gate
 

Robin Hobb

1)  The Farseer Trilogy:  Very nice trilogy in the classic fantasy tradition.  Somewhat different twist on a lead character.  Worth reading. Assassin's Apprentice ;  Royal Assassin ;  Assassin's Quest

Robert E. Howard

The creator of Conan the Barbarian.  There are a whole lot of Conan books out there written by other authors.  Some of them are even pretty good.  Howard's stories are the best.  Years ago, someone collected all of his short stories into a single 12? volume set.  Alas, the set seems to be out of print, so you'll have to go find them in a used bookstore somewhere, assuming anyone was willing to give them up (I know I still have my copies).  Fortunately, Howard's best Conan story, and the only full length one, is still available in Hardback.  This is Conan at the height of his powers, after he becomes king of Aquilonia and a legend.  If you like fantasy sword and sorcery novels, you have to read this one.  Like Tolkein, this was the basis from which all others drew their inspiration. Hour of the Dragon

Other Conan books:

    a) Robert Jordan: Of all the authors to write novels starring Robert Howards legendary swordsman, Jordan is the best.  Jordan is a good writer, as is witnessed by his immensely popular Wheel of Time series, and does a good job with the character.  A good one to start with is a hardback compilation of three of his best Conan novels, that is almost cheaper than if you bought all three separately, called  The Conan Chronicles .  Other Jordan Conan novels worth reading are: Conan the Victorious ;  Conan : The Destroyer

    b) John Maddox Roberts:    These are not great literature, but they are still fun to read.  Good books to read on a beach, where reading comes second to other pleasures. Conan the Bold ;   Conan the Rogue ;  Conan the Marauder ;  Conan and the Manhunters

    c) Leonard Carpenter:  This author has made a living by writing pulp Conan novels.  While I have read most of them, I have not read them all.  However, I am including them all here for the sake of being complete.  Again, not great literature, but fun to read.  Conan is just a cool character no matter who writes about him.   Conan the Hero ;  Conan the Great ;  Conan the Outcast ;  Conan the Savage ;  Conan the Warlord ;  Conan of the Red Brotherhood ;  Conan Lord of the Black River ;  Conan the Gladiator ;  Conan the Outcast

L. Ron Hubbard

1)   Battlefield Earth This is a vastly entertaining science fiction novel with a completely original concept.  I read that John Travolta is making it into a movie.  With a book this complex, I can't imagine how this could be a movie without being at least 12 hours long.  Pure Science Fiction.

J.V. Jones

1)  The Book of Words:  Standard fantasy concept that is very well conceived and written. Nice characterizations The Baker's Boy ;  A Man Betrayed ;  Master & Fool

Robert Jordan

1) Wheel of Time:  Many people have written to me to include this series.  It is obviously very popular, and many feel it is one of the best fantasy series ever written.  I have to say that the first few books in the series were incredible and well worth reading.  However, I think it got very mediocre after that.  Some of the books can really drag, and the last few just go nowhere.  Also, it is not yet finished, and despite there being eight books out, the ending seems a long way away.  I personally think Robert Jordan decided he was just going to live off of writing these novels until he died and started stretching it out in mid series.  This is not a bad fantasy series, and sometimes can be pretty great, but go into it expecting some extensive prose.  Eye of the World ;  The Great Hunt ;  The Dragon Reborn ;  The Shadow Rising ;  The Fires of Heaven ;  Lord of Chaos ;  A Crown of Swords ;  The Path of Daggers (Hardback only)
 

Katherine Kerr

1)  The World of Deverry:  A finely conceived world and interesting series of books.  The novels lean heavily on Celtic mythology and portray a very interesting world.  This author is good at portraying the big picture of war and tragedy, as well as the more commonly portrayed small adventures.  The only problem is that the novels, while not technically a series, run in sequence with each other and are therefore still really unfinished.  I believe I have the series in the correct order here:   Daggerspell ,  Darkspell ,  The Bristling Wood ,  The Dragon Revenant ,  A Time of Omens ;  A Time of Exile ;  Days of Blood and Fire ;  Days of Air and Darkness

Stephen King

1) The Dark Tower Series:  Yes the horror writer can write dark fantasy.  I would never have considered reading these books if they hadn't been sent in as recommendations by several site visitors.  The story is not finished, and I normally hate to read unfinished series, but these are great books.  I personally don't like horror, and thus have not read much of King's other works, but I can see why so many of his novels have been made into movies.  He can paint a breathtaking picture of the action taking place that really makes you feel like you are right there.   The first three books are in a boxed set.  The fourth needs to be ordered separately. The Dark Tower : The Gunslinger, the Drawing of the Three, the Waste Lands ;   Wizard and Glass: Dark Tower IV

Katherine Kurtz

1)  The Adept:  For some reason the first on this series is not available on amazon.com.  That's a shame.  You don't really have to read the first one to enjoy the rest, but even though the novels are seperate, the characters are consistant, and there is an assumption you know things from the prior books.  This is a unique set of novels that takes place in modern times, detailing the life of a scottish magician whose job it is to fight evil and protect all of us ignorant types from being overwhelmed by black magic.  It is pretty light reading, but very entertaining.  Buy the first novel at your local store and then order the rest here: The Lodge of the Lynx ;  The Templar Treasure ;  Dagger Magic ;  Death of an Adept

Ursula Le Guin

1)  The Earthsea Trilogy:  Classic fantasy.  The story is now somewhat commonplace, but was very original when written.  The writing style can best be compared to Tolkien in how she weaves the various tales together without resorting to sheer battle imagry.  If you like Tolkien, you will probably like Le Guin.  This is a very good fantasy series.   A Wizard of Earthsea ;  The Tombs of Atuan ;  The Farthest Shore ;  Tehanu: The Last Book Of Earthsea

C.S. Lewis

1) The Complete Chronicles of Narnia :  I read these books when I was a kid and loved them.  They are known more as kids books than adult books, and I can't think of a better way to introduce a child to the joys of reading than giving him or her these wonderful books.  There is a reason these have stayed popular for more than 50 years.  The link above is for a hardcover version that contains all seven books for only $35.  You can get each individual book for about the same total through the general link.  Or you can splurge and get the deluxe boxed set .

George Lucas

1)  The Star Wars Trilogy  You loved the movies.  You bought the paraphernalia.  You pasted the posters all over your walls.  You stood in line for hours to see the latest edition.  Why not read the books?  All three movies in one book.  This isn't great literature, but it's kind of fun to see how the movies translate to book form.  Then to really get your Star Wars fix, try some of these books (I haven't read most of these, so you'll have to read the amazon.com descriptions for recommendations):   Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace ;  The Star Wars Encyclopedia ;  Star Wars : The Essential Guide to Planets and Moons ;  Star Wars: The Visual Dictionary ;  Star Wars: Incredible Cross-Sections ;  Star Wars Scrapbook : The Essential Collection ;  Star Wars Chronicles ;  Star Wars : The Magic of Myth ;  Star Wars : From Concept to Screen to Collectible ;  Star Wars : The Annotated Screenplays ;  Anakin Skywalker: The Story of Darth Vader ;  Star Wars Episode I : Visual Dictionary ;  Star Wars: The Making of Episode I ;  Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace The Illustrated Screenplay ; Star Wars Episode I : Incredible Cross-Sections
