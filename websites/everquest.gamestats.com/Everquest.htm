    November 21, 1999

    Yet another update of the rapidly expanding fashion table.  A couple rules of thumb you should follow when sending in pictures:  To give everyone a chance to model, I am only taking one armor type per character;  take a full screen shot with no windows;  please don't send shots of the same character under different illusions, especially ones of a race that can't normally wear the armor;  make the load size of the image as small as possible while keeping a good quality shot.  Remember to put in the subject line the race, gender and armor type.  It makes it easier to sort through the pictures.

    I also updated the following sections:   Head Slot ;  Feet Slot ;  Wrist Slot ;  Arm Slot ;  Rathe Mountains Quests ;  Blacksmith Recipes


    November 20, 1999

    Addendum - I have updated the fashion section again with many new images that have been sent in.  Remember to take your pictures in the full screen mode with no tables open.  Feel free to pose, cast a spell or whatever and when you do the conversion try to make your image as low a resolution as possible to save space but still look good.

    As I've started to put together the armor and robe images, I realized it needed to be made for each race and gender in order to cover all of the various looks and styles.  I have put together the initial section with the images that were sent in last night.  I called it the Everquest Fashion Tables, and it is coming along nicely but still has a long ways to go to cover every armor set and robe.  If you have a full set of armor not covered in the table and you wish to submit a screenshot, please use the F10 key to get a pure screenshot (without any other windows) and use an image program to convert the image from the bmp file to a jpg or gif file.  I'm also slowly changing over the title fonts, since I discovered the font that I was using was an outdated one that most people don't have loaded on their machine.  Since this really isn't a big deal, I will probably just do it as I update each page over the next few weeks.

    November 17, 1999

    This update comes late and is brief because I spent the last several hours reading through all of the e-mail I recieved.  I must say that I am humbled by all the support that poured in.  I wish I could have answered each e-mail, but then I would never get around to updating the site. I realize that I was indeed catering to the vocal minority and ignoring my instincts.  I know from experience that vast majority of the people who come here and play the game are great people.  Where else can you log onto a game, shout for a group, link up with 5 strangers and invariably have a good time.  It's one of the things I love about the game.  At the urging of many people, I am going to put the popup ad back up.  However, I promise this.  It will only be in the banner frame, so it should only show up when you first log onto the site and will not reappear unless you refresh the whole site.  Also, I believe that the ad presently leads to a bunch of high quality sites and offers, but if that content deteriorates, I will take it down immediately.  I will also try to be better at forewarning everyone about major changes in the site in the future.

    The following sections have new or updated information:   Head Slot ;  Back Slot ; Bard Instruments ;  Hands Slot ;  Shields


    November 16, 1999

    After I removed the popup ad yesterday, I have to say I felt little desire to work on the site for a while.  I didn't start this site to make money, but I enjoy making a buck as much as the next person.  And after spending several hours a day working on this site since March to try to create and maintain the best EQ resource I can, I was offered a lot of money (to put the offer in perspective, I graduated with an advanced degree 11 years ago and the offer was more money than I was making for my job only a few years ago - it is also more than I make on all the other ads, etc. on the site combined) to put up an ad that pops up when you first enter the site with a yes-no question.  If you click no, it disappears and if you click yes, it opens a new window into a site with links to various legitimate site and software offers such as Delphi Forums, Priceline.com, The Wallstreet Journal, USA.net and even Sony.  I got bombarded with negative e-mails.  Some were just nasty.  Quite frankly if keeping the ad up would have lost those visitors, I would have put more up.  Others were more well reasoned.  Every time I make a change, I get complaints, so that doesn't shock me (heck some people wrote to complain about the new graphics).  I figure that shows how much people care about the site.  It was the volume and anger of the responses that caused me to decide to take the ad down.  I personally can't see how taking an extra second to click on a mouse should cause such angst, but while this is indeed "my" website, I try to make it something everyone who comes here will enjoy, and this was obviously something that bothered a lot of people.  I will say that people are spoiled by the internet.  Such a great amount of information is available for free at just the click of a button that people seem to lose perspective on the amount of work actual people put in to put that information up.  There is a reason that 99% of the websites out there are out of date and never updated.  People sign up with xoom or geocities or whatever and get their free site and think how cool it will be to whip together a website.   They put together a format, write up some copy, try to make it look nice, put it up and tell all their friends to check it out. Then after the initial thrill wears off they start to realize that they have only done the easy part, and it sinks in how much work it will be to keep it up and expand it.  At that point most people just stop, and another skeleton of a dead website joins the graveyard of the web.  For those who continue, the prospect of some extra revenue is often what decides whether a person will spend an hour or two working on the site or will instead spend that time watching TV, playing a game or doing some other activity. Although I haven't completely ruled it out, I doubt that I will put the pop-up ad back.  If it just drove away the idiots, I wouldn't hesitate to bring it back.  From my experience in the game, that's really a small percentage of the Everquest community, and I could care less about catering to that type anyways.  Too many people whose viewpoint I respected wrote to complain as well.  Nonetheless, I will not rule out other forms of revenue should they come my way, and maybe I will also take the time to step back a bit and choose to read a book, watch tv or play the game more often.
