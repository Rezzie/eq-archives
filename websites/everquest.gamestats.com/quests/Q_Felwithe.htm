Felwithe

Bandit Sisters:  This starts in Felwithe with the High Elf Paladin Guildmaster.  He will ask you  if you are "ready to serve the Clerics of Tunare"  Say "I am ready to serve the Clerics of Tunare" and he will ask if you are new to this or experienced (something to that degree).  If you say you are inexperienced,  he tells you about a fallen member of his order in Kelethin.   (If you say you are experienced, he gives you the jojongua's pelt quest).   You then need to talk to the drunk named Tandan Nybright in Kelethin and listen to his tale.  His sad story centers around his four daughters in Lesser Faydark who have joined the dervish cutthrouat and are giving his family a bad name.  Being a good father and paladin, he asks you to murder each of his daughters, retrieve their pearl necklaces and return them to him all at once.  Beware.  These bandits are pretty tough and will not willingly part with their jewelry (approximately level 10 to 13).  You will get experience, faction and an item when you return all four necklaces.  (Submitted by Awen, Talison & Zanshin)

Bat Wings:   The Magician's guildmaster, Niola Impholder, is researching a spell and needs some bat wings, 4 actually.  Get them for her and you will recieve faction, experience and a spell scroll.  (Submitted by Aqulistor)

Black Wolf Skin:   The Wizard guildmaster Tarker Blazetoss needs a black wolf skin for a spell.  Return one to him and gain faction, experience and a minor item, sometimes a spell.  Faction will change as follows:  increase for Keepers of the Art, King Thearisthex, Faydarks Champions, and decrease for The Dead.  (Submitted by Soren)

Bone Chips:  The cleric guildmaster, Yeolarn Bronzeleaf, will reward you for killing skeletons and returning with bonechips. Return to him four bonechips (stacked will work) for faction, money and an item, normally a first level cleric spell.  (Submitted by Turlo Lomon)

Enchanted Longbow:  Talk to Kinool Goldsinger in the Enchanter Guild  and ask him "Where can I get an Enchanted Trueshot Bow?"  Go throught the dialog with him and you find that you need several items.  1) Faerie Dust.  Although this is in the order you actually get the details, you may want to do all of the Antonica elements first and then do the Faydwer parts to save a couple of boat rides.  You need to talk to princess Jolena of the fairies about this.  Asking her about it reveals that one of her wings was stolen by a bald person who smelled of rust and refuse. She asks you to bring her wing back and she'll give you the Fairy Dust. In Ak'Anon by the Necro Guild there is a Gnome named Sanfyrd Featherhead. If you ask him where Princess Joleena's wing is, he will tell you that you can't have it because he read that it will make his hair grow back. He also mentions that he has no [friends].   Patronize him by saying "I'm your friend"  at which point he excalims that you are "just saying that to get my feather, if you are really my friend you would help me get some hair tonic" (or something to that effect).  Converse with him further and he will give you a 10 slot crate to fill with hair tonics.  You then have to jump the pond to west freeport and visit a gnome merchant in a tent outside the west gate by the name of Ping Fuzzlecutter.  Ping sells the tonics you need, so just buy ten and combine them.  Take the new box  (no longer a container) back to Sanfyrd and he will ask one more favor before forking over the wing.  He will give you his tattered toupee and ask that you get it fixed by the same man who sold him the tonic (this is the meat and potatoes of the quest).   So once again jump over the pond to WFP to talk to good old ping.  NOTE: The wording here is important.  You need to ask ping "can you repair a toupee?"  I tried the word fix and it didnt work.  Ping will go into the history of his wig, "The Mane Attraction" and tell you that he can still fix it because it is under warranty.  But in order to fix it you need to fetch him two clumps of hair from a hulking gorilla (the magillas in LTR of Cazic-Thule) and a lock of the golden tresses from a "certain mermaid".  This certain mermaid is a golden haired mermaid (thats the exact name of the npc) that resides in Kedge.  You will absolutely need at least two well balanced 40+ groups to safely do this (kedge is no joke).  Once you retrieve the mermaid hair, u go back to WFP and give Ping the 2 clumps of Gorilla hair, the golden locke, and the wig and you will get the mane attraction (ac 4 +10 cha).  Go back to Ak'Anon and give the wig to Sanfyrd to get the glimmering fairy wing.  Take the wing to Princess Joleen and she rewards you with the dust.  I am told she will give you the dust even if she cons dubious. 2) A Treant Heart. Wooden Hearts are dropped by Treants, but a ranger shouldn't attack a Treant.  Instead, you can get this by giving lanterns to Jyle Wyndshot in the hogcallers inn in Freeport (which you did for the trueshot bow quest) and about every fourth lantern or so he gives you a heart instead of just treant wood].  3) Your TS Bow, and 4) 3000gp.  The bow is called the Rain Caller. (bow pic submitted by Jarron)

The Falchion:  This quest is given after you complete the Orc Runner Quest.  The Paladin Lord, Lord Jyleel, in the basement of the Paladin's guild wants you to [prove yourself a faithful paladin of the court].  He asks you to present him  3 things to prove this, and you shall wield "The falchion", a paladin only  weapon.  Bring him the heart of Dvinn, find the supreme caster of the orcs, and also find the supply crate at the supply drop point from the orcs to the Tier'dal.   Killing D'Vinn in Crushbone gets you his heart and killing the orc prophet in Crushbone gets you a blue orc head.   The crate is dropped by Brarm in the Ocean of Tears.  He is about level 22 and spawns once per game day at 5am with 2 dark elf dragoons (approximately level 18 to 22) on the rocks with the Sirens just as you enter the Ocean of Tears from Faydwer.  Bring the items back to Lord Jyleel and he will give you a Falchion of Koada'Vie.

Going Postal:  This is a massive quest involving most of the cities in Norrath.  See the full quest description in AK'Anon.

Illegible Scroll: Lord Jyleel will ask you to bring him an illegible scroll,  which can be found, among other places, on the Crushbone Orc Oracles.  Your reward ranges from a minor item to a low-mid level scroll. (Submitted by Doriath)

Jojongua's Pelt:  The Felwithe Paladin guildmaster, Tynkale, will ask you if you are "ready to serve the Clerics of Tunare" if you hail him. Say "I am ready to serve the Clerics of Tunare" and he will ask if you are new to this or experienced (something to that degree).  If you say you are inexperienced, you get the Tandan Nybright quest.  However, if you say you have been with them for awhile (or whatever  [xxxx] line equates to that) he will give you a quest to go to Innothule Swamp and slay Jojoojojgogogoguna, the long-named huge Kobold lord in the aptly named Jojongua's Tower. He is a rare spawn and drops a pelt called a Very Large Hide.  Take his pelt to Tynkale for xp, faction and an item.  The following factions go up:  Clerics of Tunare, King Teristhex and antimage.  He will then ask you to meet up with a man named Tolon Nurbyte.  He is found at a local inn, and will spawn if you ask the innkeeper who is Tolon Nurbyte.  Likely this part is just a lead in to the Princess Lenya quest in Highkeep.

Orc Runner:  Talk to the Paladin Lord, General Jyleel, in the basement of the Paladin guild in Felwithe.  Occasionally an Orc Runner can be seen running from the entrance to Greater Faydark along the path towards the crossroads.  He is relatively tough (be at least level 8 if you want to solo him), but usually will yield a sealed letter and a pouch upon his death.  Take the letter to the Paladin Lord.  Give it to him to receive a faction bonus and the quest for the falchion.  (Submitted by Tshober)
