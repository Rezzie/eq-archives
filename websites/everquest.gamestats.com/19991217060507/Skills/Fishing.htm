Fishing

If you have additional information on this skill or corrections on the information that is here, please e-mail me at  Allakhazam@gamestats.com

    Fishing can be a very useful and lucrative skill to have, especially at lower levels.  Just be aware that, much like real life, you will spend a lot of time in one spot constantly throwing your line in and out of the water hoping to get a bite.  This can be pretty boring, but if you don't mind being bored, you can make some extra cash.  This is probably the easiest of the secondary skills to learn and train.  All you need is a fishing pole and some bait and you are ready to head to the nearest body of water and start fishing.  Some would advocate putting a few skill points here to avoid the risk of breaking your pole.  Personally, I would rather lose a few gold pieces than some skill points.  You can eventually recover the lost gold.  As far as I can tell, you can catch one of four things:  a generic fish (worth about 2 silver pieces), a fish scale (worth about 4 copper pieces), a rusty dagger (worth about 1 ½ silver pieces) or a tattered shoe (worth about 1 silver piece) .  While you're fishing you can talk to the people around you or practice your spells and other skills.  Remember to sell your fish as soon as possible before the temptation overwhelms you and you start to eat them.  This skill is especially useful for races such as Erudites who live on an island and spend a lot of time either waiting for a boat or riding it to another town.  Between the time on the dock and the time on the boat, I estimate that the trip to Qeynos garners me about an extra gold piece each time. Note that the fish scales are used in the enchanter water breathing spell, so alot of times you can get good money for them from an enchanter of adaquete level to use them.
