Selections Recommended by Site Visitors

If you have a science fiction or fantasy book or series that you wish to recommend, make sure (1) it isn't already listed on my main list or this list and (2) it is in print and available from amazon.com [no need to torture people with books they will never find].  Then e-mail me the author's name and the book titles (with the proper order if it is a series) with a brief description/review and the name you wish to use for the recommendation (if I get multiple recommendations, I can't guarantee that everyone will get mentioned).  I will try to put it up as soon as possible, but understand that it takes a while to get the links up.

Alfred Bester

1)   The Stars My Destination Recommended by Zhagadksa as "easily one of the best novels ever written".  Several others have also agreed.  Other Novels by this author that have been recommneded by various people include: The Demolished Man ;  Virtual Unrealities : The Short Fiction of Alfred Bester ;  Psychoshop
 

Wayne Douglas Barlowe

1)  Expedition : Being an Account in Words and Artwork of the 2358 A.D. Voyage to Darwin IV   Recommended by Qeyy, who writes:  "It has a very scientific and analytical aura about it, and ensnared me instantly when I chanced upon it."

Philip K. Dick

1)  Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep :  The book that inspired the movie Blade Runner.  Krabkey say that "as in most cases, the book is much better and focuses on other issues."

2)  Now Wait for Last Year :  Krabkey says that  "this book deals with the concepts of warfare going awry and alternate time lines" and is "very interesting reading."

Gordon R. Dickson

1) The Dorsai Series:  Nenand says he "can't decide if this is the best SF saga or best military SF.  It's definitely SF and not fantasy, but with good elements of both genres."  The books in order are:  Tactics of Mistake ;  Soldier, Ask Not ;  Dorsai! ;  Necromancer ;  The Final Encyclopedia (Sf Series , Vol 1) ;  The Final Encyclopedia (Sf Series , Vol 2)   He says these are additions to the main story linke also worth reading:   Young Bleys ;  Other ;  Lost Dorsai : The New Dorsai Companion ;  The Spirit of Dorsai

2)   Way of the Pilgrim :  Described as a "thought provoking" pure SF novel which "outshines the Dorsai series."

Kate Elliott

1)  Crown of Stars:  A series still in progress which was recommended by Thermopyle who states that "they are books that you must read, whether it be now or later" when the series is finished.  There are three out so far.   King's Dragon ;  Prince of Dogs ;  The Burning Stone (hardback)

Leo Frankowski

1) Adventures of Conrad Stargard:  Jon writes "If you are any type of engineer or even just a good thinker who can actually see in your minds eye what the book is drawing out for you, then you will enjoy the adventures of Conrad Stargard as this 20th century Polish engineer who accidentally gets shipped back to 1231 Poland."  The books in order are:   The Cross-Time Engineer ;  The High Tech Knight ;  The Radiant Warrior ;  The Flying Warlord ;  Lord Conrad's Lady ;  Conrad's Quest for Rubber

Neil Gaiman

1)  Neverwhere :  This is described as "a nicely done book with sickly, comical villains."

2)  Good Omens :  Written with Terry Pratchett, this is a "highly recommended ... comedy about the apocolypse."

Peter F. Hamilton

1)  The Disfunctional Universe Series:  Recommended by Brynie.  He writes:  "What I like about this series is that it seems to start out as a fairly standard Space Opera. By the end of the fourth book, it is clear that what is going on is defining mans real role in the universe, the meaning of death and the concept of punishment in a broad sense. I eagerly await the final book in the series "The Naked God".   The Reality Dysfunction : Emergence ;  The Reality Dysfunction : Expansion ;  The Neutronium Alchemist : Consolidation ;  The Neutronium Alchemist : Conflict

Guy Gavriel Kay

1)  The Finovar Tapestry:  Recommended by Sugarman, who says this is "some truly incredible stuff" amd Sani who says it is "incredible."  Others have also mentioned these books, so they must be good.  I have a feeling that this will soon transfer to the other list of books I have read.  The Summer Tree ;  The Wandering Fire ;  The Darkest Road

2)  Tigana :  Siegfried says this "probably has the best concept of any of his books, and is arguably his best book."

3)  The Lions of Al-Rassan :  Siegfried also recommends this book, claiming "it's a great book, and brought me closer to crying at one point than any other book."

Stephen King

1)  The Dark Tower Series:  Yes the horror writer apparantly can write dark fantasy.  Several people have recommended this series.  Ozgott says that this is "By far one of the best book series that exists.  Don't underestimate the ability of Steven King when it comes to Dark fantasy."  The Dark Tower : The Gunslinger, the Drawing of the Three, the Waste Lands ;  The Drawing of the 3 : The Dark Tower II ;  The Waste Lands : The Dark Tower III ;  Wizard and Glass: Dark Tower IV

George R.R. Martin

1)   A Game of Thrones :  This get the cool icon because it has been recommended by a lot of different readers.  Descriptions range from "one of the best fantasy books ever written" to "great fantasy, with swords, kings, evil magic users and kick ass descriptions of castles and keeps across a rich landscape" to simply "a great series".  I have to say that no other book has gotten as many individual recommendations.  Equal raves have been sent about the second book in the series, which is out in hardback, called  A Clash of Kings .  If you aren't picky about starting an unfinished series, I have a feeling that this is a good one to try.

L.E. Modesitt

1)   The Forever Hero :  Newly release compilation of three 1980's novels:  Dawn for a Distant Earth, The Silent Warrior, and In Endless Twilight.  This is recommended as "better than Recluse."

Elizabeth Moon

1)   The Deed of Paksenarrion :  Recommended by Artthgar, who says it is "the best book ever written about a xxxxxx (can't tell ya, would ruin the story)"  Others have also mentioned this series as a good one.

Patrick O'Leary

1)  Door Number Three :  Siegfried states that this "is a science fiction novel about time travel, aliens, World War III, and dreams.  Sort of.  It's almost impossible to explain or classify, but it's incredible.  I can guarantee you'll never read anything else like it."

2)  The Gift :  Siegfried also recommends this book, stating "it's a fantasy, mostly, and its style is probably most like "The Eyes of the Dragon" by Steven King.  It's much, much deeper than that, though.  It's probably the single best book I've ever read."

Terry Pratchett

1) Discworld:  Several people have written me about these novels.  I have actually read a few of these books, and agree that they are well worth reading.  Very funny.  There does not seem to be any particular order you have to read them.   Small Gods ;  Soul Music ;  Lords and Ladies ;  Men at Arms ;  Feet of Clay ;  Interesting Times ;  Soul Music ;  Jingo ;  Hogfather ;  Maskerade ;  The Last Continent (Hardback) ;  Mort (Special Order) ;  Colour of Magic ;  Light Fantastic ;  Sourcery

Melanie Rawn

1)  The Dragon Prince Trilogy:  Recommended by Seayn, who thought they were "terrific."  Rushman and Mad Mike agreed with her, saying "this series was a masterful show of Fantasy and Magic intertwined with great politics and war."   Dragon Prince ;  The Star Scroll ;  Sun-Runner's Fire

2) The Exiles:  The third book is not yet out, but this is recommended as being at least as good as her other series.   Exiles : Ruins of Ambrai ;  The Mageborn Traitor

Christopher Rowley

1)  Basil Broketail Series:  This was recommended by several people as a good series of fantasy war novels.  I'm not sure if I have the correct order here:  Bazil Broketail ;  A Sword for a Dragon  ;  Dragons of War ;  Battledragon ;  A Dragon at World's End ;  The Dragons of Argonath ;  Dragon Ultimate

John Steakley

1)  Armor :  Vashtari recommends this book which he says "has some similarities to Starship Troopers, but a far superior story.  Definitely my favorite Sci-Fi of all time."

A.E. Van Vogt

1)   Slan :  This was described as "my favorite all time science fiction book" by Tarzt.

Gene Wolfe

1)  Book of the New Sun:  Another series recommended by several people.  It is commented that "Wolfe is  a magnificantly beautiful writer."  These books have just been re-released as a pair of two-volume sets.   Shadow & Claw : The First Half of the Book of the New Sun : The Shadow of the Torturer/the Claw of the Conciliator ;  Sword & Citadel : The Second Half of the Book of the New Sun : The Sword of the Lictor and the Citadel of the Autarch
