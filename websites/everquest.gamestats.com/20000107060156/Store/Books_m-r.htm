Book List - M through R

Julian May

1) The Pliocene Exile Saga:  Is it science fiction?  Is it fantasy?  Well, it's a little of both, and is very well done at that.  There are some times when it bogs down a little, but this is a really interesting concept of time travel and the mysteries of life on earth.  Highly recommended.  The Many-Colored Land ;  The Golden Torc ;  The Nonborn King ;  The Adversary

Anne McCaffrey

1) Dragonriders of Pern:  This trilogy is considered one of the classics of science fiction, and is clearly loved by a lot of people.  The novels are as much romance as science fiction, and have a more broad appeal than many SF works.  The story is very unique, and well told, but it may not be for everyone.  This version combines the original trilogy of Dragonflight, Dragonquest, and The White Dragon into one novel.   There were quite a few sequels to the trilogy, which were set in the same world, but explored different times and events.  I'm not sure if I have the right order on these:  The Renegades of Pern ;  Moreta : Dragonlady of Pern ;  Nerilka's Story ;   Dragonsdawn ;  The Dolphins of Pern ;  All the Weyrs of Pern ;  Dragonseye ;  The Masterharper of Pern

2) The Harper Hall Trilogy:  This was the first sequel series after the original Dragonrider series, and is almost as entertaining.  If you liked that weries, you will like this one as well.  While it is not necessary to have read them to enjoy these, I would recommend it.  Dragonsong ;  Dragonsinger ;  Dragondrums

Dennis McKiernan

1)  Iron Tower Trilogy:  A very fine fantasy trilogy.  The race of the warrows is a nice addition to the fantasy world.  This is a good version of the classic story line of the little guys who get involved in a war against evil and become heros.  It's a shame that the duology which follows these novels is now out of print.  You can't go wrong with these.  The Dark Tide ;  Shadows of Doom ;  The Darkest Day

2)  Other Mithgar Novels:  These are all stand alone novels that take place in Mithgar, the world where the Iron Tower Trilogy is set.  They take place at different times and have different characters, so they are not a series, but simply related novels.  Each one is well worth reading.  The world he has created is very real and fascinating, and his characters are always interesting.  Read the descriptions of the novels, and pick the one you find most interesting.  Once you start, you will probably want to read the whole set.  Voyage of the Fox Rider ;  The Eye of the Hunter ;  The Dragonstone

3)  Caverns of Socrates :  A must read for fantasy role players.  Engrossing story of a group of role players who step up into the next step of computer gaming.  It starts off a little slow, but hooks you part way in and leaves you waiting for the sequel, which from the ending, may or may not happen.

4) The Hels Crucible Duology:  More Mithgar Novels, taking place before the ban.  If you enjoyed the other Mithgar novels, you will also like these.   Into the Forge ;  Into the Fire

Patricia A. McKillip

1) Riddle-Master : The Complete Trilogy :  A re-release of the whole trilogy in one book, including the novels The Riddle-Master of Hed, Heir of Sea and Fire, and Harpist in the Wind.  These are based upon Celtic mythology and are a different type of fantasy than the standard novels written today.  Kind of a mystic tale, and worth reading.

L. E. Modesitt

1) The Recluce Saga:  I really like the world the author has created and enjoy reading his books.  My biggest complaint is that he skips around from book to book, with characters introduced in one book not reappearing until several books later, so that plot lines hinted at in each book can wait a long time to get resolved, if ever.  This wouldn't be so bad if the books weren't so good.  Even though they are listed in a certain order, there is no real order to this saga, since the books cover different characters and even different time periods.  Still, some later ones refer back to older books, so it is best to read them in order.  The Magic of Recluce ;  The Towers of the Sunset ;  The Magic Engineer ;  The Order War ;  The Death of Chaos ;  Fall of Angels ;  The Chaos Balance ;  The White Order

Michael Moorcock

1) The Elrick Saga:  Except perhaps for the Thomas Covenant series, you will not find a more unlikable hero than Elrick.  This is part of the charm of these books.  Very original brooding dark fantasy.  One of the classic fantasy series that spawned the genre.  Elric of Melnibone ;  The Sailor on the Seas of Fate ;  The Weird of the White Wolf ;  The Vanishing Tower ;  Title The Bane of the Black Sword ;  Stormbringer

Larry Niven

One of the classic Science Fiction writers, with a large library of excellent books.  Unfortunately most of them are out of print.  I would urge every fantasy gamer to go out to a used bookstore somewhere and find Dream Park, which describes the ultimate RPG.

1)  Lucifer's Hammer :  Several movies have come out over the past few years about a comet crashing into the earth, and I always felt they were ripping off this book without giving it its due credit.  A very believable rendition of the doomsday scenario.

2)  The Mote in God's Eye :  (Written with Jerry Pournelle) Excellent novel about first contact with aliens.  The alien culture is very well thought out and detailed, and the problems encountered are fascinating.

3) Ringworld :  A science fiction classic.  A completely unique idea for a world that is amazing in its conception.  The book is not perfect, but is well worth reading.  There were two sequels which, while not as good as the original, were still very entertaining, especially the first one.   Ringworld Engineers ;  The Ringworld Throne

Mike Resnick

His novels of the Inner Frontier are some of the most unique and fun science fiction novels ever written.  Santiago ranks as one of my favorite books ever.  He has basically transferred the western to outer space and has created characters that are just amazing. Once you start reading them, you will just tear through then and wish he would go out and write more.  There are other ones besides these, but unfortunately, they are out of print.

1) Santiago : A Myth of the Far Future All of his books are worth reading, but you should definitely get Santiago.  The book lures you in and overwhelms you with the fantastic characters who make up the myths of this far future.  You will tear through this book and wish it was 200 pages longer.  I must have read this book 5 times, and enjoyed it just as much each time.

2)  The Widowmaker Trilogy:  Also set in the inner frontier, this is almost as good as Santiago.  I just love the whole concept of these books.   The Widowmaker ;  The Widowmaker Reborn ;  The Widowmaker Unleashed
