ROUTE FROM WEST FREEPORT TO QEYNOS

If you are under level 5 then I would not take this trip yet because if the amount of risk involved.

If you are under level 10 then it is a good idea to drop any important items into the bank BEFORE you make this trip, it will be long and hard and don't expect not to die the first time if you try this on your own. My advice would be to get another player who is of a higher level or has experience in this trip to guide you. Also make sure that you have a lot of Water and food, I would recommend getting about 10 of each as the journey is long, however this isn't essential because you can purchase some on your way.

You could also get someone to cast a spell called Spirit of Wolf (SOW) on you which will make you run faster and will reduce the time this trip takes (normally a few hours earth time) and will maybe save your life. However SOW does not last forever and you may need a re-cast to complete the journey. Please note that Bards cannot cast SOW no matter what level as well as many other classes. Classes that can are (depending on their level) Druids (at level 14) and Shamen (at level 9).

Also reading both the Good and Evil Races routes is a good idea because there maybe tips in one which I have missed in the other.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GOOD Races Route (and some Evil Races)

    West Freeport.

This is a newbie zone so you should not have a problem with this zone.  Go out of the Gates of Freeport and walk directly away (gates behind you) from the gates towards the zone to East Commonlands.

    East Commonlands.

This zone gets a little bit harder. The route is once you have zoned from Freeport is to follow the right hand wall of the zone.  Follow the right wall of the zone after a little while you will see an opening in the wall (Plus 1200, Plus 900) this is the zone entrance to the Dark Elf Area called Nektulos Forrest. This place should be avoided at all costs unless you are of an evil race or a high level (20+) good race.  Continue to follow the right wall keeping as high up the wall as possible. You will then pass a large orc camp on your left. As you pass the orc camp be careful of one of the orcs that is sometimes slightly up the wall, however if you are high enough up the wall he will ignore you. Then you will pass an Inn and then another smaller Orc Camp both slightly on your left.  After you have passed this last Orc Camp the zone (Plus 1200, Plus 5000) isn't far so keep going until you zone into West Commonlands

    West Commonlands.

This Zone is hard for people even if your level is in the Teens. You MUST be careful of all monsters here, as a lot of them will Kill On Site (KOS) and "seem" to roam randomly around the zone.

As you are already on the right of the zone the best route to take is to follow the right hand wall as high as you can like you did in East Commonlands. You will pass a building that looks like a tower with some skeletons (and maybe zombies) however they will not touch you if you are high up the right wall. Then you will pass a very big Dervish Camp.  The next camp you will encounter will be a very big Orc Camp. You will not be able to go up the wall for this camp so you will need to go into the open and walk around it. You must make sure that there are no kodaks and roaming orcs, in fact any monsters walking around the area (Make yourself invisible if you can). Then walk around the Orc camp and get back on the right hand wall as soon as you have passed the Orc Camp. Follow the right hand wall and you will eventually reach an opening in the far corner of the zone (Plus 900, Plus 4000) that will take you to Kiticor Forest.

(Alternative Route)

If you want to you can follow the left side of the zone in the Desert. All you do is go as high up the left side of the wall as you can and keep going. Watch out for any Hill Giants (Approx. level 34) which sometimes roam the area.  This is why I personally prefer the right side of the zone. However if you take this side there is a zone to Befallen which you can use if you get in trouble (Minus 900, Plus 500).

    Kiticor Forest.
 

You now have two choices on which route to take. Rivervale way or Highhold pass. They both eventually meet up in the same place.  Highhold is harder (in some circumstances) than the Rivervale route. However the Rivervale route is a bit longer and can be dangerous in the Gorge of King Xorbb or Runnyeye.  This is an on going argument on which way is the best way to go. One of the deciding factors is if you can cast invisible or camouflage then its probably best to try Highold pass as the monsters will not see you if you are invisible. However if you cannot then it may be safer to go the Rivervale route.

    Once you have made your choice then...

In Kithicor Forest To Highhold.

Follow the path, do not deviate from it as there are some high level monsters here, athough they are very few and in between and you are not likely to see them. Take the following Turnings along the path - Right, Right, Right, Left. You will come to the zone (Plus 500, Plus 2000) of Highhold pass
 

In Kithicor Forest To Rivervale.

Follow the path, again do not deviate from it as there are some high level monsters here although they are very few and in between and you are not likely to see them. Take the following turnings along the path - Right, Right, Right, Right. You will come to an entrance cut into the rock face which is the zone (Plus 2000, Plus 3800) entrance to Rivervale.

    Highhold Pass Route.

As you zone into highhold, Orcs will spawn just around the corner. These Orcs are about level 18+. Normally people are camping the spawn of Orcs so they normally busy however you may not be so lucky. If there are some Orcs you will see them just a few steps into the zone. It is probably a good idea to ask someone to give you directions to Highhold Keep as Highhold is like a maze and can get very confusing on your own and for the first time (I still take wrong turnings sometimes).  The route goes through a tunnel.  Then a left turn to where you see a bridge and a guard.  Then a right turn (do not cross the bridge) which follows a path to a fork where you turn right to the entrance to Highpass Keep.
 

Once you get to Highold Keep you will then need to ask the person or maybe another if they can show you the way to the zone of East Karanas. This is because going out of Highhold is also a problem as this side there are Gnolls and again they are high level (Level 15+).
 

If however you decide to go it alone when follow these instructions. NB: I originally wrote this when my Bard was at level 16 and the Orcs were conning red so be careful now at level 30 they ignore me. :)
 

Once you have zoned into highhold follow the path straight under an arch turn left and go up towards a Guard and cross over the wooden bridge. Follow the road down to the left and you will come to another wooden bridge that runs over a river on your left, you will also see Tigers Roar on your left that you can normally rest or buy provisions there if you want to.
 

When you are ready follow the other path from Tigers Roar straight up the hill which will veer you to the left at the start then turn right at the next junction. You will go down a narrow bank that at the bottom you should turn left. You should pass buy a NPC then turn left at the next junction and go over a couple fallen logs on the floor. At this point there may be high level Gnolls patrolling, however the zone isn't far and must make a run for it. One word of warning though some of the Gnolls are mages and they are capable of rooting and hit hard however if you run really quickly they normally don't get time to cast before you zone. You will go under an arch and quickly after zone to East Karanas.
 
 
 

East Karanas (from Highhold Pass Route).
 

Once you have zoned into East Karanas follow the valley down to the right. You will eventually come to a tower and a crossing to the other side of the pass. Cross over to the left and follow the left wall all the way down.
 

Try to avoid looking over the edge of this cliff as you can attract Griffons (Level 34+) or Griffawns (Level 18+) or you can plunge to your death if you go a little too far. Also keep your eye out for roaming monsters on the way down which might attack you.
 

Once you get to the bottom of the pass follow the windy path avoiding all Lions and Wolves etc. as these KOS and are about level 13+. Also if you do get in trouble follow the path and you will get to a junction, turn left and run towards the buildings where the guards "should" help you.
 

Following the path you will come to a junction in the road which you must take a right turning. Follow the path until another junction. There will also be a milestone showing the direction to Highhold pass on the right of the path. Turn left towards another milestone at this junction and follow the road to a Large Bridge. When you cross the bridge it will Zone (Minus 10, Plus 1000) you to North Karanas.
 
 
 

Rivervale Route.
 

As you zone into Rivervale follow the path past the guards (and cleric house on your right) you will see an exit on your left go towards this and turn right. Follow the path until you see a brown building called fools gold. Go around the right of this building towards some merchants. You should then see the Priest of Discord on your left. The Priest is facing the way you need to go which is the zone to Misty Thicket you will also see some guards at the zone exit on the left. Behind and to the right of the Priest is a tower built into the rock face, in there you will find a bank if you need to use it and a Merchant. When ready Zone to Misty Thicket.
 
 
 
 
 

Misty Thicket (from Rivervale Route).
 

Follow the path at the first junction take a right and then follow the path to a wall with some guards. The Rivervale side of the wall has very low level monsters however going through the arch you will be going into the higher level part of the zone (max of level 14).
 

As you go through the arch the path will end. This was where I got a bit lost the first time I went this way. However stand in the arch facing away from Rivervale with the wall to your left and right. Then think of a clock as being straight ahead is 12hrs and behind is 6hrs then go in the direction of approximately 10hrs or ask a nice halfling to help you, there are normally some at the arch resting.
 

You need to avoid all of the goblin camps and the occasional Orc, Young Kodak and Goblin Warrior roaming the place as these will all KOS. When you get to the other side of the zone there will be an entrance into the wall normally guarded by two goblin warriors (Minus 820, Plus 1640). If you are not very high a level then you need to either ask someone to kill them for you or make a run for it as the zone is not far and you should be able to make it unless you are stunned by one of them.
 
 
 

Runnyeye Castle (from Rivervale Route).
 

This is a dungeon and you need to be careful as there are a few roaming goblins here. My advice is to get someone to escort you through this place, as it isn't very far and however if you take a wrong turning you can easily die by the odd Evil Eye or Dazed Goblin Guard.
 

If you cannot find anyone then the route you should take should be pretty easy. Effectively you turn right at every turning. Walk along the corridor and turn right you will see a small river in the floor go around the right of this and turn the next right. Then you will see a Head on a Stake turn right at this point and then zone to the Gorge of King Xorbb.
 
 
 

Gorge of King Xorbb (Rivervale Route)(Another name is Beholders Maze).
 

Once you have zoned from Runnyeye you will need to go through the Gorge of King Xorbb. There are ALOT of high level monsters (level 15+) here and you really need to be careful. The easiest way is to go up the left-hand wall as high as you can and follow the wall along the left valley. When you see some monsters they should not attack you if you are up the hill (high up) follow the left wall.
 

One word of warning, when you follow the left wall you will need to watch out for two possible occurrences.
 

1. Taking some corners too quick. Some of the corners are sharp and you must not take them too quick or you will fall to the ground. If you fall you can take about 50hp of damage so be careful if that's all you have.
 

2. You will be taken to the ground once along the wall. This will be obvious if you take the journey slowly. Make sure that no monsters are down the bottom and quickly walk down. The drop is a gradual one however if you run with Spirit of Wolf (SOW) or with a Bard's Accelerando playing you will probably take damage if you don't slow down for this drop. As soon as you can, go back up the left wall. DO NOT try walking down the bottom for any more time than necessary.
 
 
 

Then follow the left wall until you get to a junction. This will be at the point where you cannot follow the left wall anymore. So check that no monsters are around and walk down to the floor. You must take the left junction keeping close to the left wall trying to avoid any mudities or goblins that might be roaming. The zone (Minus 1210, Minus 475) to East Karanas is down this valley.
 
 
 

East Karanas (from Rivervale Route).
 

Once you have zoned follow the valley until the area expands into grass you will see some huts on your left which are merchant huts and you can rest there and buy water and muffins if required and is relatively safe.
 

When ready go up the right hand wall as high as you can and follow the wall to the right avoiding the Bandit camp with Tallus Holton. You will then come to a river in front of you just after the Druids stones (teleport site) that was on your left. You must turn left and follow the shoreline of the river however you must watch the Cyclops in the water called Broon (Level 28ish) as he will attack you if you get too near to him, just walk a bit inland around him.
 

Follow the coast to a wooden bridge and cross the bridge to Zone (Minus 10, Plus 1000) to North Karanas.
 
 
 

North Karanas (both Routes have met).
 

Come off the bridge and follow the coast to your left (have the water to your left). This is a big zone and you need to follow the coast all the way. DO NOT veer off onto the plains, as you will probably die, however the coast is normally VERY safe so don't worry (but keep your eye out). You must however watch for a group of human, half elf and Barbarian looking raiders and are camped at the edge of the shoreline (Approx. Minus 4200, Minus 2000). Go a bit inland and walk around them, not too far inland though. Follow the coast PAST a stone bridge (Do not cross the bridge), which is the bridge to South Karanas, and then continue along the coast until you zone you West Karanas.
 
 
 

West Karanas.
 

You will need to follow the shoreline all the way in this zone. There shouldn't be any monsters however avoid any if you see them as they will probably attack on site but don't worry. You will also see the occasional Farmer at the water edge however unless you are evil they will be harmless. Once you have followed the coast until it ends turn right and follow this wall. You will eventually find an opening into the wall (Minus 10, Minus 1000) that takes you into Qeynos Hills.
 
 
 

Qeynos Hills.
 

This zone is reasonably safe, however you need to keep your eye out for Gnolls, Wolves and Grizzly Bears which regularly roam the plains if your a very low level.

Follow the path straight past a Guard Tower on your left and to a T-Junction. If you want to go to Surefall glades (Druids/Rangers) then turn right here and follow the path until the end. If you want to go to Qeynos City then turn left and follow the path until you zone.
 
 
 

Qeynos City.
 

This Zone around the gates is for newbies.

Once you have zoned follow the path straight. You will then get to Qeynos.

EVIL Races Route (Good Could go this way)
 
 
 

So your an evil Race? Well your journey will be hard unless you can make yourself invisible. Follow the Good Race Guide going the Highhold way if you can make yourself invivible however read below for important information regarding Guards and other NPCs which might attack you.
 

N.B. I am inexperienced with travelling as a Evil Race therefore there may be some mistakes however I will do my best and would take any suggestions to improve it.
 
 
 
 
 

West Freeport.
 

This is a newbie zone so you should not have a problem with this zone.

Go out of the Gates of Freeport (If you can even enter freeport) and walk directly away (gates behind you) from the gates towards the zone to East Commonlands.
 
 
 

East Commonlands
 

You will need to follow the left wall of the zone. Follow the wall as high up as you can. You will be walking in a desert. Follow the wall until you see an opening (Minus 1100, Plus 900) go into this cave and follow the path. When it starts to open turn right and follow the path you will then zone to North Ro.
 

If you are a Dark Elf you will be probably coming from Nektulous Forrest. When you have zoned into East Commonlands follow the path until you get into the open. Then go Exactly straight forward and maybe slightly to your right however not much (don't follow the path after the area has opened up). Also watch for any Griffins and any other wildlife. You will eventually cross a path however you must watch out for a roaming a guard that sometimes walks down the path. Keep going into the desert and you should see an opening to a cave on your right (Minus 1100, Plus 900). Go into this cave and follow the path when it starts to open up into an larger area turn right and follow the path you will then zone to Northern Desert of Ro.
 
 
 

Northern Desert of Ro.
 

Once you have zoned follow the path until you come out into the open. You will see a tall rock in frount of you. Go around the rock to its left side and go straight ahead into the desert. Keep going in the same general direction until you eventually get to the Ocean. When you are travelling through the desert be carefull of roaming Madmen and Orc Raiders or any other monsters which lurk in the dunes.
 

Once you have reached the waters coast follow the coast to your right (have the water to your left). Keep walking along the coast until you zone to Oasis of Marr.
 
 
 

Oasis of Marr
 

The oasis is a level 20ish zone and can be very hazardous. I have travelled here many times with my level 8 Troll Shadow Knight on my own so it can easily be passed without any problems. Before you go you must be aware that Sand Giants (Level 34+) and Spectres (Level 37+) sometimes go to the coast however this isn't that common also the crocodiles should not attack you unless you attack them. Follow the coast again (water on your left) you will pass a dock and then you will see a small tall rock in the sea.
 

Keep following the coast until you start to see the end of the zone. This is the difficult part of this zone where you must go into the desert for a little while because of an Orc camp. At Approximatly Minus 1000, Minus 1300 go out into the desert at a (approx.) 45-degree angle. Keep going avoiding any nasty monsters that do roam this area. I would advice trying to get Sprit of Wolf (SOW) from one of the players you will see at the coast, as this can be a lifesaver. Also the zone is quite big and is between two rocks. The zone will at Plus 10, Minus 1900 and you will zone to Southern Desert of Ro.
 
 
 

Southern Desert of Ro.
 

This zone is very difficult to explain where to go. Effectively as you zone in you want to go towards the right of the Zone. However and a big However. On the far right of the zone there are Dervish and Orc camps which you MUST avoid. So run to the right of the zone keeping away from any camps which you see. This is probably the most risky part of the trip so take your time. You will also find that you will be initially walking in the desert and then eventually get to the grassy part of the zone.
 

You are aiming for the Innothule Swamp zone which is at Minus 3192, Plus 1139 once you get there zone.
 
 
 

Innothule Swamp.
 

This is a very dark place however you shouldn't have any problems in seeing because you should have infravision. As you zone in follow the path until you meet the waters edge. If you want to go to Grobb (Troll City) or GUK then take a left here and follow the edge of the zone. The first entrance you come to which is guarded by who Frogloks is the GUK entrance. If you continue past this following the edge of the zone you will see about three troll NPCs this is the zone to Grobb. If you do not intend on going to Grobb or Guk then turn right and follow the edge of the zone (have water to your left). You will be looking for an opening in the side of the zone which will be the first opening you come to...you will then zone to Ferrott.
 
 
 

Ferrott
 

This is inhabited by Lizards and should be relatively safe if you dont stray. Follow the path taking the first right that you come to. Continue to follow the path and you will come to another junction which you should take a left. You will then walk though the zone for a little while and eventually see another fork in the road this time you should take a left. You will pass some ogres on your left and then come to a T-Junction. If you want to go to Oggok turn right here otherwise turn left. You will then cross a bridge over a river. Keep following the path taking a right at the next turning. You will then eventually get to Rathe Mountains zone. NB: If you are a good race then you have to be invisible in this zone because of Ogre Bouncers. They will attack ALL good races and will probably kill you in one hit you have been warned.
 

As a side note...If you have intentions on going to Cazic Thule then when you zone in from Innothule swamp. Follow the path until you get into the open turn left and follow the left wall (mind the bard there not sure if he attacks Evil people) until you come to a river. Swim directly across the river to the other side. Then follow the edge of the building until you see a large ramp in front of you. Go around to your right and then go up the ramp. In the middle of this large ramp you will find a small opening which is the zone to Cazic Thule. A word of warning Cazic Thule is a level 20+ zone DO NOT go in there if you are any lower because you will almost defiantly die if you do.
 
 
 

Rathe Mountains
 

Not many people know their way though this zone however it is very easy to navigate. As you zone in from Ferrott the zone will start to open up. You will be presented with three choices in directions to go. DO NOT turn left or Right or even explore down there unless you are of a very high level (level 40ish). Go straight on and follow the path, you will eventually see a left hand turning which you need to ignore and instead veer to your right. You will then come to an area where I am unsure how the monsters here react to Evil Races. There will be some Giant Scarab named monsters here. Make sure that you con them before you attempt to go past in case they attack you, however I know that that they con indifferently to my Wood Elf Bard and will not attack him. Follow the path to the right and you will then be taken into an open area of the zone. There will be some Inns on the left side of the zone. You need to follow the left side of the zone (same side as the Inns). However keep away from these Inns because the merchants can be hostile even too good races. Follow the left side of the zone until you find an opening on the left into another area. Then follow the right side of this new area. Keep following the right side until you see an opening on your right and then follow down there to zone to Lake Rathe.
 
 
 

Lake Rathe
 

I hope your swimming is good or it will get better. As you zone in you will walk the edge of the water and you should see a pier. Also watch out for a named Froglok which sometimes roams this area. He is very high level and will defiantly attack you if your an evil race. Turn right and follow the edge of the water you may need to swim at parts so be prepared. Follow the right side of the zone. You will then see a camp on the top of a hill that is a bandit camp, keep away from this and follow the right edge of the water (water to your left). Now take the journey slowly at this point because there is an Orc camp on the edge of the water which can be nasty. When you see the Orc camp swim into the lake around the Orc camp and then get back on the edge of the zone when you can. Give yourself a nice wide berth around the camp as they will see you in the water if you get too close. Follow the edge of the lake again passing another place with a empty house and then you will come to an area with another pier. Be careful here because after 6pm Norraith time (use /time command) Dry Bone Skeletons and Zombies spawn and are lethal unless your level is in the 20s. Also watch the Gnolls during the day which are of a lower level but will attack you on site. You need to turn right here and I advise going up the banks around the edge of the camp which will mean you will pass without any problems. You will then zone to South Karanas (Plus 4352, Plus 1147) after following the path.
 
 
 

South Karanas.
 

There are lots of ways to travel though this zone. However to make it simple follow the edge of the zone although it is a little longer. The quickest way is to go straight ahead into the zone and never turn and head due North, however this can take you past some nasty monsters which might kill you. Once you have zoned from Lake Rathe follow the path until the area opens up. Then take a left and follow the edge of the Zone. Keep following the edge if the zone you will be force to turn right do this and keep following the left side of the zone. After a long time following the edge of the zone you eventually meet some water. Turn right and follow the edge of the river (water on your left). You will eventually come to a stone bridge crossing the water which you need to cross to North Karanas (Plus 2889, Plus 907).
 
 
 

North Karanas.
 

IMPORTANT: You will need to jump off the left side bridge before the tower because there are guards there and they will attack you. Swim away from the tower to the edge of the water. Then Follow the coast to your left (have the water to your left) continue along the coast until you zone you West Karanas. If the guards see you then zone back to South Karanas, which can be done in the water if needed.
 
 
 

West Karanas.
 

You will need to follow the shoreline all the way in this zone. There shouldn't be any monsters however avoid any if you see them as they will probably attack on site but don't worry. You will also see the occasional Farmer and Barbarian Camp at the water edge which you will need to avoid because they will probably attack you. Once you have followed the coast until it ends turn right and follow this wall. You will eventually find an opening into the wall (Minus 10, Minus 1000) that takes you into Qeynos Hills.
 
 
 

Qeynos Hills.
 

Once you have zoned in walk along the path until it opens. Be aware that a guard walks along this path regularly and will probably attack you if he sees you. When the zone opens up turn left and follow the edge of the zone until you zone to Qeynos City.
 

Qeynos City.
 

This Zone around the gates is for newbies.

Once you have zoned you will need to be careful of any Guards. You can get bound at the zone point if you need to. Also if you follow the left side of the wall you will eventually see part of the wall look like Vines on it. This is the secret entrance to the Qeynos sewers that also has a Necromancer guild.
