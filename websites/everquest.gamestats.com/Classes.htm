Everquest Classes

        The choice of what class to play is really one of personal preference.  They all have advantages and disadvantages.  One major factor in your choice of class is whether you plan on grouping or playing alone.  There are some classes that just aren't suitable to play solo.  Read the descriptions carefully before picking a solo class.  Of course, every class is fun to play in a group, and grouping is really encouraged in the game design.  Therefore,  I would suggest you choose your class based upon how you would like to play in a group.  This needs to fit your personality and how you like to play within the game.  I see the classes in three categories (with some crossover of categories):
  Fighter Types - or tanks as they are referred to in the game - including Warriors, Paladins, Rangers, Shadow Knights,  Monks, and maybe Rogues.  Clerics, Shamen, Druids and Bards can fill this role in a pinch, as can the pets of Necromancers and Magicians, but not very efficiently.  These classes see the most action.  They are always at the forefront of every battle, and they rarely find themselves out of the action.  If you like wading into every battle, shouting bonzai, and hacking off heads and limbs, this is for you.  Because every party can use multiple fighters, you will never be at a loss for a group.  However, these classes need less strategy to play (and may seem less fun to some) and lack the means to travel and to extricate themselves from tough battles that the magic classes have.  The first time you have to run back to town to buy stuff, you will really envy that magic user's gate spell. It is also very expensive to buy equipment for most of these classes, and heaven help you if you die somewhere where you can't retrieve your corpse, forcing you to repurchase all of your armor all over again.

   Magic Support, including Clerics, Enchanters, Shamen, Bards, and Druids, and to some extent Magicians and Necromancers (though they really fit the third category better).  These classes, while possessing some offensive skills (especially the Shaman, Bard and Druid), are far more valuable in how they enhance the skills of other party members.  Most of them are very difficult to solo (except the magician, druid and necromancer).  Their primary role is to buff the fighters with enhancements and defenses and debuff the monsters to make them easier to hit and kill, then stand back and let the fighters do the dirty work.  They are also very useful for traveling, healing, sneaking around and other non combat related stuff.  You may enjoy these classes better if you are the type who likes to role play more than just kill and level.  The healers are also supposed to keep track of the hit points of each party member and keep them from dying.  If you chose one of these classes, you should be happy with the fact that while you may not be the hero that demolished the monster, you were the one who made it possible for that hero to do his job.  Every party needs one or two of these classes, especially the healers.

   Magic Attack, including Wizards, Necromancers and Magicians, and to some extent Druids.  These classes have the ability through firepower and summoning to quickly kill monsters when the fighters start to get low, and most importantly, kill off the monsters who break off and run for help before they can summon their big brothers to whoop your butt.  This is especially true of the wizard, who has no crossover and really only fits into this category, yet does it best of them all.  These can be soloed, but only with a perfect strategy and a lot of patience.  If you play one of these classes, you have to be prepared to sit back and patiently bide your time while the fighters bash at the monsters, conserving your mana to strike where it is most effective and, for necros and magicians, directing your pet to the most useful area.  You should also be prepared to spend a lot of the game staring at the inside of your spellbook, meditating to get back spent mana.

The Bard

Bards are almost always welcome in a group.  However, be warned that they do not make a great solo class.  While they are not as good at any particular thing as any of the specialized classes, they do a little of everything.  They also can cast forever since they don't use up mana when they sing.  Such ablilties as heal, find corpse, accelerated travel and group invisibility make a bard invaluable group members.  While the bard only does one thing at a time, he does it real well.  If you are a social person who likes meeting new people, the bard may be for you.  If you like to go off on your own a lot or are shy about travelling with strangers, you may want to think twice about playing a bard.  I once saw a pretty well reasoned argument on a message board that the most powerful group you could put together would be 6 bards.  One suggestion.  It costs you nothing but a little time to locate a corpse for someone.  Yet that can get you a friend who may someday save your life.  Don't be stingy with your locate song.

The Cleric

This is the most popular class in Everquest.  Not for people trying to play them, but for people trying to group with them.  If you play a Cleric you will have lots of friends, since a Cleric is an absolute must for a well balanced party.  AD&D has ruined the reputation of Clerics.  They are such wimps in that game that nobody wants to play them.  This isn't true in Everquest.  Sure, Clerics are most important for their healing and curing abilities, but Clerics are pretty decent fighters when given the chance, and you don't want to go after undead without one around.  Clerics are able to wear full plate armor, giving them a level of protection unmatched by other spell casters.  Coupled with your heal spells, you should be able to keep from dying a lot.  One thing you should know is that Clerics are constantly in need of money, because they have to buy both spells and heavy armor.  This is a class for the person who likes to socialize and wants to be welcomed wherever he goes.

The Druid

There are three things that druids do better than others - travel (camouflage, spirit of wolf, and solo or group teleports), damage shields (great for long combats), and playing with animals (sense, charm, buff them, terrorize).  The other great thing about a druid is that, while they are not the master of any one thing, they are the best class to fill in the gaps that a party has.  Need a healer?  Druids are the second best (tied with Shaman) next to Clerics!  Need extra firepower?  You got it -perhaps only the wizard outshines her here!  No Shaman or Enchanter?  Druids have decent buffs as well.  Druids also have the tracking skills of a Ranger.  Given time to meditate, they can buff you before combat, destroy creatures during it, and heal you afterwards.  No one else can do that for just one cut of the experience.  Because they have such a wide variety of skills, Druids are one of the better solo classes out there.  However, many of their best spells don't work unless they are outdoors, making them difficult to play in a dungeon, where many of the best items can be found.   Druids are also weaker at each given area than the equivalent specialty class. This is a good class for a person who likes to do a little of everything, without being a master of any.  (Thanks to Starkinder for the input on Druids)

The Enchanter

The enchanter is not a good solo class.  It has the weakness of a spell caster without the attack powers many of the other classes have.  However, Enchanters are very valuable group members.  They are sort of an all around magic class, with a little of every type of spell.  They can buff you up to make you worth about another level and then buff down the monsters to make them easier to attack.  They have some limited attack spells, and have spells such as root and lull which can make life easier for the whole party in battle.  With an enchanter in the party, the group can take on much higher level monsters and get a correspondingly higher experience and gold return.  In addition, they are also supposed to get spells which can create magical items, although many seem to not work yet.  The enchanter's main work gets done before and after the battle, and although once the battle starts they often tend to hang back and let the others do the dirty work, they have enough damage spells to pitch in when needed.  If you like to group, and don't mind being the one giving support to the others, this is a good class to play.

The Magician

This class is sort of a cross between a fighter and a wizard, because they can fight with their pet elemental and stand back and cast like a wizard.  Thus, while not as strong as a wizard or fighter in their specialty field, magicians are able to to do more than either one alone.  They are also popular with groups because they can summon food, water and basic weapons, which always seem in short supply, and get the invisibility spell fairly early on for easy travel and scouting abilities.  In some ways the magician's most valuable contribution to a group is as a provisioner.  Because a magician basically comes with a built in tank, it is one of the best classes to solo.  The biggest problem with this is that you spend a lot of your time standing back and watching your pet elemental do all the dirty work.  If you plan on playing on your own a lot, and don't mind playing a support role when in a group, this is a good class for you.

The Monk

The monk is one of the best fighting classes in the game, but it does have its drawbacks.  Because he can't wear armor, he takes more damage when hit, and the limitation to hand to hand combat stops him from using most of the cool weapons in the game.  But the monk's natural quickness allows him to avoid more hits than other fighters and hit the enemy more often.  The biggest advantage of a monk that I can see is that if you die you don't really have to go find your corpse since you don't need much stuff.  You also will not be constantly poor like most of the other classes.  Monks dish out some incredible damage since they get a lot more attacks per round, and have some really cool looking moves built into the graphics.   They also get feign death, which is a great skill for avoiding sure death, and can be used to help a group survive a tough battle.

The Necromancer

This is one of the best classes to solo.  Of course, since most NPC's hate the site of you, you may find it necessary to solo.  Like the magician, the Necromancer has the combined ability to summon a skeleton to work as a tank and stand back and blast away like a wizard, but the necromances has more powerful combat spells than a mage.  While not as strong as a fighter or a wizard in either specialty, the combination of skills can be pretty deadly.  The biggest drawback of a necromancer is that you are evil.  You can't even get a shopkeeper to sell you food.  There has been many a time where I've seen necromancers standing near a shop begging other players to go in and buy some stuff for them.  Necromancers are also useful in a group since they give the group both a tank and a battle mage in one package.  This is a difficult class to play, but is a good one if you want to spend most of your time soloing.

The Paladin

The paladin is a combination fighter and cleric.  You get the attack abilities of a fighter and the spell casting of a cleric.  Paladins spend a lot less time sitting on their butt than regular fighters.  The Lay on hands skill is like getting a get out of jail free card.  Once per day you can avoid almost sure death by completely healing yourself.  In a game where you spend a lot of time looking for your corpse this means a lot.  While not quite as powerful as a warrior, you are still pretty good at dishing out punishment and can take a pretty good licking.  Also, if the group doesn't have a cleric, the Paladin is the next best thing.  Like all of the fighter classes, Paladins can be soloed, but are better playing in a group.

The Ranger

Tracking makes this class really shine.  This skill alone makes Rangers welcome in almost every group.  Foraging is also a nice skill given to Rangers.  If you have a Ranger you can find the monsters that give you the best experience or gold without a lot of mindless wandering, and you can keep a look out for the ones that will kill you on sight.  Rangers are also pretty good fighters and can readily play the tank.  You can't dish out or take as much damage as a warrior, and you are somewhat limited in your weapon and armor selection, but you can definitely hold your own.  Because of his druid and tracking abilities, the Ranger is one of the better solo classes out there.  Tracking lets you pick your fights and only engage the monsters you can beat, and warns you when you are getting near something that can take you out in a hurry.  Besides the solo abilities, Rangers are also one of the most popular classes to have in a group.  This is one of the few classes for which I have seen people actively shouting to find for a group.  One thing about a Ranger is that you have to be careful what you kill.  You are supposed to be a lover and protector of nature, and killing too many wild animals may get you in trouble with your guild.

The Rogue

Show me the money.  All of the rogues I have played with have disdained looting monster corpses since they make so much money pick pocketing, and, since they are limited in their armor selection and have no magic, they don't need to spend a lot on armor and spells.  Never ignore the benefits of wealth.  Also, back stab is a great skill to have when grouping.  Let the fighter start in on the creature, get behind it and stab it hard and fast.  Oddly enough, considering that rogues are generally not the most popular types with the public (many legitimate vendors refuse to even deal with you), rogues are best when fighting in a group.  They can be soloed, but only with a lot of difficulty.   While they can fight fairly well, they don't fight as well as any of the fighting classes, and they get no magic.  It is their unique skills that make them stand out.  The backstab skill, poisoning, and the ability to pick locks and remove traps make the rogue very valuable in a dungeon.  There are also apparantly places in the game only a rogue can get to.  This is a tricky class to play since it doesn't fit easily into any mold.

The Shadow Knight

This is a very difficult class to play.  While it is basically a combination of a necromancer and a warrior, it is not very strong in either class.  From what I can see from the skills you get, this class really won't come into its own until quite a high level.  But then it will start to kick some serious butt.  If you have the patience to milk it through the lower levels and are willing to be hated by just about everything in Norrath, you may enjoy playing this class.

The Shaman

This seems to be a pretty good cross between a fighter and a cleric, with a little wizard thrown in, and in a pinch can fill either role.  This is a class that can be soloed, albeit with some difficulty.  You have some decent buffs, which along with your heal skill makes you a pretty valuable party member.  Plus you have the added advantage of being able to pitch in with a weapon and do more than token damage.  A Shaman is always welcome in a group because of his combination of abilities.  Since this is the only true spell casting class for the trolls or ogres, it is a pretty good choice if you are set on playing one of those races.

The Warrior

While they get no magic, the warrior dishes out the most damage over time, and can take the worst beating and still survive.  This is the classic hack and slash class.  Your high strength lets you arm yourself to the teeth and still carry around a lot of loot when necessary.   Warriors get the highest physical attack ability of any class.  There are supposed to be a number of weapons and armor that only a warrior can use.  As a tank, the warrior is the best class out there.  A group will feel much safer knowing it has a warrior up front to take the damage while it gangs up on the monster.  The biggest problem with the warrior is the complete lack of magic.  You will really miss having many of the magic abilities of some of the other classes.  If you don't group with a healer, you will also spend a lot of time sitting down and waiting to heal back your lost hit points.  You have to decide if pure straight up killing is your thing.  If so, this class is probably a good choice.

The Wizard

Once you get attacked you are in trouble.  But, if you have some nice tank taking the damage up front you can really kick some serious butt.  Nothing beats the wizard in sheer attack abilities.  The pyrotechnics are awesome.  That is until the wizard runs out of mana.  Then anything can beat the wizard.  Every party wants to have a wizard.  They may just hang around and leach for a few battles, but when the going gets tough, they can whack a monster to the ground in seconds.  Also, they are good at stopping monsters who are running away to get help.  The wizard is not an easy class to play.  Because he is all attack and no defense, he must approach a battle warily.  But it is really impressive when you take down that monster in one shot that the fighters were battling for five minutes.  Wizards can be soloed, but only if played perfectly.  If you play a wizard, be prepared to spend a lot of time staring at the inside of your spell book.  Also wizards, like all spell casters, are constantly short on money.  Those spells are expensive.
