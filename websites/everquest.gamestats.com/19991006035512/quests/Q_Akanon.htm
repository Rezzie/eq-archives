Ak'Anon

27 C Blackbox:After you complete the Series C Quest, Mantik gives you a 27C Blackbox and 10 copper to take to Jogl Doobraugh who is in one of the windmills.  He then gives you a note to take to the merchant Drekon Vebnebber in the warriors guild.  These factions will change when you give the black box to Jogl:  King Akanon,  Eldritch Collective and Gem Choppers standing gets better;  Dark Reflection and The Dead standing gets worse.  These factions will change when you take the report to Drekon:  King Akanon, Merchants of Akanon and Gem Choppers standing gets better; Dark Reflection standing gets worse.  The quest FORKS at this point. There is MORE THAN ONE report you can get from Jogl.  The quest you get from returning the report depends on which one Jogl gives you.  You will either get the Red 5 quest or the Duster Models quest.  (Submitted by Wael and Sluglug)

Air Tight Box:  Speak to Larkon Theardor in Ak'Anon, who is is standing with a Gnome Mechanic on the right side of the Library Mechanimagica.  He should be next to a mechanic.  Hail him and say you want the clean job.  He will tell you to go and look for a gnome located in the observatory in Steamfont to the left of the tunnel to Ak'Anon as you are coming out of the tunnel.  Hail him and say "<so and so> sent me." Where <so and so> is the name of the Gnome you spoke to in Ak'Anon.  He will give you an "Air Tight Box."  Put 10 infected rat livers inside and hit combine, then deliver them back to Larkon Theardor

Black Box Fragments:  Sanfyrd is the mechanic across the river from the entrance to the mines of Mishap. He will reward you if you turn in 4 blackbox fragments and the following faction changes:  King Akanon, Merchants of Akanon and Gem Choppers standing gets better; Dark Reflection standing gets worse.   You get the fragments from killing Obsolete clockworks.

Bugglegup:  The Magician Guildmaster, Tergon Brenclog, in Ak'Anon will ask you to kill Bugglegup, a troll who wanders the Steamfont Mountains.  Bring back his head for a reward.  (Submitted by Doriath)

Cargo Clockwork: This may be broken.  Jarah Reskar in the Gem Choppers Hall will tell you that the Cargo Clockwork goes from Ak'Anon to the windmills every five days at 8 am.  A group of 3 highwaymen (it is rumored that one is Bugglegup) have been attacking it.  A reward is offered for the bandits' heads.  Zondo Hyzill in Steamfont is standing next to the Cargo Clockwork.  He does make his trip every 5 days from where he normally is to the windmills.  He gets a little fix there from one of the gnomes.  Then, he starts on his way back to his normal sitting spot.  He gets about 1/4 of the way back, he does a few circles, then disappears.  When you go to look for him, he is back at his starting post.  Not sure what to do after that.  (Submitted by Doriath)

Duster Models:  This is sometimes given after you complete the 27 C Blackbox quest.  If Jogl doesn't give you the Red V report, he will probably give you the Duster Model report.  If you give this to Drekon Vebnebber in the Gemchoppers Hall, he will tell you that two of the obsolete Duster Models have escaped and must be destroyed.  You must give the proof to Sanfyrd Montop.  He's the guy that's on the other side of the river from the Mines of Malfuntion.  If you kill a Duster Model, you will get a piece of scrap metal and sometimes a gizmo/servo type item. You have to give two pieces of scrap metal (but not the other stuff) to Sanfyrd to complete the quest.  I got a gavel the one time I did the quest. I don't know if it's random or if you always get one. (Stats on the gavel: non-magical 1h blunt, 4 dam, 38 delay, weight 6.5, usable by warriors, clerics paladins, rangers, shadow knight, druid, monk, bard, rogue, shaman and any race.)  The Duster Model quest is tough because the Dusters appear infrequently and they move around a lot.  To make things worse, once you start fighting one nearby guides, etc will often 'help' you by killing it.  Of course it won't leave a corpse then.  Duster V can sometimes be seen walking down the path through "the wall" just inside the city entrance and around the priest of discord area.  At a certain point in his usual path he goes from walk speed to run speed.  I have no idea why.  He will eventually run into the palace and vanish if not attacked.   Duster X can sometimes be found near the paddle wheel on the area above the Mines of Malfunction.  He also walks around on the bridges that go to the small islands in the lake, but he doesn't appear very often.  (submitted by Sluglug)

Gearheart:  Baxok Curhunter will ask you if you will serve the crown, and once you are about 5th level he will give you a quest to hunt down a stolen clockwork named Gearheart who has been taken by the fae in Lesser Faydark.  Gearheart is found at  the faerie city.   He is in  his mid teens (probably around  level 15). When you kill gearheart, you get a faction loss (faerie), some change and a 'blackbox XIVD' which, when given to Baxok Curhunter, gives you some change, a couple of faction changes, and an item, possibly a round shield.  The quest is worth a platinum or two.  (Submitted by Wael and Toraque)

The Gnome Take:  Tell Jeet in Kaladim that you will run to the gnome city (after doing the boog mudtoe quest I believe) and say you will collect the gnome take.  He will then give you a key. You have to find a clockwork scrubber in Ak'Anon which responds to 628 and use the key on him to get the gnome take. The scrubber was down in the zoo area when I found him and responded by saying something like wiz... tik... 628... You give him the key and your faction with clockwork goes up and you get the gnome take. Then you take the gnome take to Jeet.  You get a couple of silver and a minor item for completing this quest, plus faction and experience.  (Submitted by Quegos)

Going Postal:  This quest may cover every town in Norrath, as well as some non-town areas.  Listed below are all the towns and contacts I know of.  The text to get the letters or bags is a little confusing. They say something like [deliver] mail to [Kelethin]. If you say 'I will deliver mail' or 'I will go to Kelethin' it won't work, only the form of ' I will deliver mail to Kelethin' seems to work to get the item to deliver. Kelethin, Freeport, Highhold, and Qeynos are international mail hubs. Delivering mail between hubs will always result in carrying a mailbag. All other mail origination sites are satellite postal offices and feed into the nearest major hub.  The reward varies (at whim) between 1gp and 12gp, with values in the middle more usual. With a couple of deliveries, low-level characters could get a few platinum easy.  Bards with Selo's Accelerando are really in good shape, as some of these routes can be done in only a few minutes.  The quest is sponsored by the League of Antonican Bards. Faction hits are League of Antonican Bards, Knights of Truth, and Guards of Qeynos get better, RingofScale and Mayong Mistmoore get worse. So remember, if you see a wood elf bard slouching in the shadows in your home town (and it's not Kelethin), it's just the local postal worker.

Iron Pellet:  Tergon Brenclog, located in the library, wants you to retrieve an iron pellet for him.  It can be found on Bugglegupp in Steamfont.  Return it to him for xp, faction and an item (usually a low level scroll).  (Submitted by Aadais Bard)

Larkon's Errands:  Larkon Theardon, a gnome standing outside the Library Mechanimagica, has tasks for young gnomes.  He would like you to bring him minotaur horns and basilisk tongues.  He will also tell you to go see Fodin (the gnome in the observatory next to the Cargo Clockwork) and tell him that Larkon sent you.  Fodin will give you a task if you are below level 16.

Micro Servos:  Give four Micro servos (found on rogue clockworks) to Sanfyrd at a time (not stacked) for some experience, money and the following faction changes:  King Akanon, Merchants of Akanon and Gem Choppers standing gets better; Dark Reflection standing gets worse.  (Submitted by Sluglug)

Red 5 Clockwork:   This is sometimes given after the 27 C Blackbox quest.  If you turn in the correct report to Drekon you will be told that the "rumors of Red V must be true".  You need to get Red V's blackbox and give it to Manik.  Red V is usually about level 4-6 and spawns in the back part of the Mines of Malfuntion between midnight and 6am game time. The corridors leading to and the room in which Red 5 spawns are short, so only small people(gnomes, dwarves and halflings) can actually fight or cast in there. Since you have to be standing to cast or attack. The spawning point for Red 5 alternates between "obsolete model" and "Red 5". Kill all of them, only red 5 gives the box.  Red 5 is killable by solo lvl 8 or bunch of lvl 5's.  If you kill him, his corpse will have an incomplete blackbox.  To fix the blackbox you have to put 4 blackbox fragments in the incomplete blackbox and hit combine.  You can get the blackbox fragments from the obsolete models that also inhabit the mines.  You will usually only be able to kill Red V once per game day.  (And sometimes not at all if you miss him.)  The incomplete blackbox is a quest item anyway, so you can only carry one.  When you combine the 4 blackbox fragments inside the incomplete blackbox you will get a rusted blackbox.  The rusted blackbox looks just like the ones you get from the rogue clockworks in Steamfont, but it's actually different.  (The other rusted blackboxes are for a different quest.)  If you give Red V's rusted blackbox to Manik you'll get experience, faction changes, a little money and a random item.  The item is often a piece of cloth armor (hardly worth the trouble), but sometimes you'll get a potion.  If your faction with then gnomes is high enough or you repeat this quest enough once you complete the quest you get the Bullsmasher a very nice weapon for any small class.  (Submitted by Wael and Sluglug)

Rusted Black Boxes: Manik Compolten in the gemchoppers hall wants you to retrieve rusted blackboxes for him.  These are found on rogue clockworks.  Bring them back to him and get experience, faction and some money.  You must give them to him one at a time, but you will only get rewarded for every other one.  (Submitted by Wael)

Scrap Steel:  Give scrap steel found on rogue clockworks to Sanfyrd one at a time for some experience, money and the following faction changes:  King Akanon, Merchants of Akanon and Gem Choppers standing gets better; Dark Reflection standing gets worse.  (Submitted by Sluglug)

Series C Black Boxes:  Manik Compolten in the gemchoppers hall will also ask that new warriors take keycards and go retrieve blackboxes from series C clockworks.  He asks that all four boxes be returned simultaneously, meaning that you must return after getting each one to get another keycard.  This quest is performed by wandering around Ak'anon and giving the keycards to Clockworks with a C in their name.  the locations of these clockworks are:  XIIC)  On an island in the middle of the lake. (You'll have to swim to get to it.)  XVIIC)  Just outside the Mines of Malfuntion.  (He's the newbie area 'guard'.)  XXVIC)   In the palace near King Akanon's room.  XXVIIC)  Near the tunnel that leads to the Mines of Malfunction. (In the paddle wheel room.), or at the top on the Akanon Wall.  When you give the series C card the following factions change: King Akanon, Eldritch Collective and Gem Choppers standing gets better;  Dark Reflection and Meldrath standing gets worse.  Return all four C blackboxes for experience and the following faction changes:  King Akanon, Merchants of Akanon and Gem Choppers standing gets better; Dark Reflection standing gets worse.  Plus, you get the 27C Blackbox Quest.  As an aside I have been told to be sure to ask Manik about the Asylum of the Mad and about Meldrath.  (Submitted by Wael and Sluglug)

Telescope Lenses:   Tobon Starpyre, the Wizard Guildmaster, wants you to collect four spare telescope lenses from four observatories around the Steamfonts. He gives you a rolled up note with the names of the four Royal Observers, three of which are located in the Steamfont Mountains while the last is located in Lesser Faydark.  These are supposedly the four lense locations  1) The gnome royal observer just outside zone line in Lesser Faydark will give  you one if you ask.  2) The gnome royal observer in one of the observatories in Steamfont will also give you one if you ask  3) The gnome royal observer in another observatory tells you it was stolen by a Dark Elf female, Zenita DRin.  She is in Freeport in the secret tunnels beneath an inn by the docks. You can get the lense two ways.  Kill her - she is approximatley level 15 and hard to kill.  Or you can play cards with her (if you have an OK faction with her) by continuously trading her a bottle of something you can purchase upstairs for a draw of a card until you manage to draw a King.  (This one is confirmed)  4) The final royal observer in Steamfont tells you his brother, Bidl Frugrin, went to Kelethin to do some observations with the spare telescope lens.  He says he has likely found the local tavern and has gotten drunk, wandering the forest by day, and hanging out in the tavern by night.  At night, he can be found in the tavern next to Trueshot Bows in Kelethin and happily gives you the telescope lens. Take the lenses to Tobon Starpyre and give all four to him at once.  He will give you a level 8 spell (Fire bolt being one) and approx 1 pp.  Faction changes include +Eldritch Collective, -DarkReflection, -TheDead, +GemChoppers, +KingAkanon.  (Submitted by Myrmidian and Madix Grizzlestrik)

Tergon's Spellbook:  Tergon Brenclog, located in the library, has lost his spellbook and wants it back.  It can be found in Crushbone on one of the high level orcs, or sometimes on D'Vinn.  Returninig the spellbook gives the following faction adjustments: +Eldritch Collective, - Dark Reflection, - The Dead, + Gem Choppers, + King AkAnon.  You also get experience and an item, usually a scroll (Elementalkin Earth).  You may then get the Toko's spellbook quest.  (Submitted by Ungrin)

Toko's Spellbook:  After returning the spellbook to Tergon, he tells you about another Quest:  "Now I have news of a larger matter. It has to do with a [defector].  It seems a gnome magician by the name of Toko Binlittle has gone and left the guild. He joined forces with the [Pirates of Gunthak]. Find him. He must be destroyed.  Our secrets cannot be known. Return his spellbook to me and I shall give you the [Elemental Grimoire]."  From what I know the Pirates of Gunthak are from the Southern Gulf of Gunthak. It seems that they have been operating within the Ocean of Tears, leagues from their own territory.  The Elemental Grimoire contains the knowledge which will  advance your techniques in research.  With it you shall learn to research spells and scribe them for your own spellbook.
