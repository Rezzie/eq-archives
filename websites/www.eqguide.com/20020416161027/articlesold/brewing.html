Brewing with Ligge

"Give me a bowl
of wine -
In this I bury
all unkindness" - W. Shakespeare, Earth

 

Brewers brew because they want to. There is no profit to speak of in brewing so if that is why this is a trade skill you are considering get that notion out of your head right now. Of course, there are a few exceptions to this rule.

Bog juice was profitable for a very short time before everyone realized that in Verant's infinite wisdom they created a component for a high level quest that even a beginning brewer could make easily on their own. The Heady Kiola's are needed for some of the cured silk items that enchanter's and tailor's can collaborate on. Tumpy's famed tonic does require decent skill (135 skill) and is used by many to "buy" experience so this can be sold if you really feel the need. Ol' Tujim's (135 skill) can be sold to rogues for their quest. Others may crop up and if you count your coppers closely you can probably make a little on some of the others by selling to merchants if you are the high charisma sort, but don't plan on getting rich. It is still possible to buy most of the beginning brews cheaper at merchants than you can buy the supplies to make them. That's just silly, but nevertheless the way it works.

We do it because we prefer the taste of a homemade brew after a victorious hunt rather than the watered down swill you find in local taverns. We love it for the exotic drinks Gregor promised he would help us make, that is before we found out he is a liar. Most of all we love it because we drink! Almost anyone that has met me in some of the more solitary camping spots - Mitty, Permafrost, etc - knows that I bring drinks to pass out. It helps break the ice and avoids the "Hey dude I am camping here!" attitude most start with when you walk in a room. Hand the poor camper a Bleeding Brain when you walk in a room and they know you are there as friend and comrade, not foe.

Most items are available at vendors. If you are a forager then you you even have more drinks available to you easily involving vegetables, fruit, and berries. And who knows maybe even morning dew. <laughs> If you are not a forager and want to make some of these drinks just ask around for some. In our core group we have myself and a ranger. The enchanter and cleric have no problem getting supplies for drinks and baking from us and it works out even better for me as I have had Cailette making vodka from my foraging to increase her skill and returning the vodka to me to make Bleeding Brain to increase my skill.
