Enchanted Jewelry

 

 

You see them auctioned all the time. Earrings, rings, necklaces... trades of a jeweler's craft. Attribute and mana enhancing items to aide in your abilities and therefore your survival. I was always unsure as to what items were in my best benefit as a druid... until today.

The Casters Realm site has answered my questions and some actually surprised me. Let's take the actual question I was really faced with and see. One thing of note... there is some restriction on mana earned based on level vs. items worn. This has been eluded to by the ever vague and untrustworthy Abashi so who knows what is true anymore, but to the relative adjustments should still be of value.

Your season/level and wisdom scores have everything to do with this so for results will vary and I encourage everyone to go check it out for themselves. I chose the cheapest route possible and in actuality I would have saved some platinum also. Prices vary and I am not going to get into that - take it up with the seller.

I took 2 comparisons at different levels, starting with Ligge in his 10th season.

Example A: Buys 2 silver jade rings (+5 mana, +5 hps, 1 AC for each). At that point in my career I would have a loss of the 2 Cutthroat Insignia Rings I wore, a loss of +4 Dexterity and 2 AC.

Example B:  Buying 2 jasper rings (+2 wisdom). Buying 2 wisdom rings instead of the jade may not be the way to go just yet.

Now lets try 10 seasons later and a different set of choices, based on what you can afford you should be buying better jewelry now anyway.

Example C: Sticking with jade again, as it is the most common auctioned, we now have bracelets of electrum (+10 mana, +10 hps, 2 AC) which means you will lose the AC you had on your wrists and get 4 back, traded for 20 hps.

Example D: With Jasper and wisdom pluses we move to earrings so you get to keep your jade rings you already bought and you don't have to give up your wrist AC just yet. Plus earrings are typically cheaper than the bracelets. Due to your level those 4 points added to wisdom actually count for some mana now. Ok so polished hoops might be more mana, but only for a very short time as your level increases and this way you probably save a little probably AND support a local enchanter.

If the AC and the hps are not huge issues for you the wisdom rings now give you more mana (16 now vs. jade at 10) than the jade rings due to your level. If your desire for mana outweighs the AC and hps need it may be time to trade into those now.

Now if you have enough cash or have saved after buying the earrings go buy those electrum bracelets.

Example E: My best selection covers your fingers (silver jasper wisdom rings  +4 wisdom total - 16 mana), wrists (jade electrum bracelets - 20 mana, +4 AC, 20 hps), and ears (jasper electrum wisdom earring +4 wisdom total - 16 mana) and garnishes: 52 mana points, +8 wisdom, 4 AC. and 20 hps.

As well as purchasing a total of 8 items from a local enchanter to help them get more supplies to learn even greater skills and jewel craft abilities. You also still have those jade rings to sell or trade back to your enchanter if they will buy back at a lower price to resell them. Now some will argue the jade rings are a better deal than the wisdom rings at this level. Well... maybe, but I tend to find its not hps and AC that saves me; its casting ability and speed. I normally won't die if I have mana left.

Of course we all have our favorite pieces and in some cases you will not be willing to give up a spot for one of my suggestions, that's the way things work. One thing to keep in mind is you don't need to buy in pairs. The least expensive +2 wisdom ring at level 20 is 8 mana. That is better than an aged jade ring and in my eyes competes well against a new jade ring at that level, for pocket change.

Now there are way to many variables for me to honestly say "go buy this!", but I can say go compare for yourself and your level and make a more educated decision based on all the information available. The EQ Enchanters site has done a great service with this calculator.
