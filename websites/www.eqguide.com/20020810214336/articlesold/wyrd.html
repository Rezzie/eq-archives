A Ranger's Dying Words

 

You are startled from your meditation by a shrouded figure. At first you take it to be just another wanderer, roaming the lands of Norrath, but you sense something cold... and sad from the figure. From under a hood a soft voice speaks. Her words carry a power, and a strange accent, as if she is not used to speaking.

"Ligge, thou art named here. I bring thee a...story."

She hands you a bloodied, soiled scroll, and slowly walks away, fading into the distance.

You open the scroll and begin to read the words written within.

The ranger struggled to stand. Many battles had he seen, and many wounds had he received. Blood welled from his lips, and he staggered to his knees. His weary legs unable to hold him he fell. Mumbling a few brief words to Tunare, he asked for her healing power, but this time it did not come. Tunare had forsaken him. Not far away, the enraged Drolvarg fought the roots entangling it's feet. Soon it would be free. When that happened the ranger knew he was doomed. Off in the distance a small group of mighty adventurers watched, and they grinned. They knew that the ranger hurt the drolvarg badly, but he was no match for the beast. They saw the ranger fall, and readied their weapons. Soon he would be dead, and they could finish off the drolvarg, getting an easy kill. Wyrd saw the adventures. With his half-elven hearing he heard their plans. Rage filled him! Many times he had stood on the line for those who needed help! Risking his own life for the safety of others. Many a night He had stood outside of Rivervale and, by himself, guarded the town from the army of undead that lurked outside it's walls. Yet the craven cowards stood their and waited for him to die. Agony filled his face as he stood up. His legs screamed and he felt his ribs shift as bone ground upon bone. Leaning upon his bow he steadied himself, and drew his swords. Looking up he saw the drolvarg break free of the roots, and charge towards him.

Wyrd lay on the ground, his shattered body was still. All but his hand, that fought to grab the hilt of his broadsword. In his last moments his thoughts went out to those he knew and loved. Ligge, Splinner, Cailette... The faces of friends lost to time crossed before his dimming eyes, Morghan, Nitewolf, Arwen and Zeilik.

"TUNARE!!!" He screamed, "Thou hast forsaken me! I have fought for you, strived to preserve your works! Yet the world remains unchanged! The same evil walks as it always has, and now at the last you desert me! I reject you!!! I reject this world! Let the beggars and "l33t d3wds" have it! But this you owe me, This one small act! Bring my story to my friends, so that they may know that I died in vain. Aye in vain, for this world can not change, and all that I have done was for nought!!!"

The light fades from his eyes, and a barely perceptible whisper passes his lips. "good bye my friends..."

A light summer breeze blows across the plains. In the distance a group of mighty adventures gleefully hack to death a badly wounded Drolvarg. A hooded figures walks in from the distance to lay a hand on the rangers forehead. She is too late, this one has lost his soul, and can not be returned to life. She touches his lips with a forefinger, and a scroll appears. She looks at it and slowly nods. She knows what she must do, obey a rangers dying words.
