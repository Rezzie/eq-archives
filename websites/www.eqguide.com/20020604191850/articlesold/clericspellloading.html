Spliner's Spells

 

I've recently re-written this article. I wrote it back when I was level 39, and the spell loads have changed since then as I have learned many things. Life at level 50 is a bit different than it was back then, and I wish I knew some of the things I know now, back then. So here you go, enjoy!

Having spent well over 100 of my real life days playing EverQuest, I'd like to say I always am prepared. That's not exactly true, but I do my best to keep the right supplies ready for myself as well as my teammates, that includes spell loads for different situations. Currently I am level 50 (about two bubbles shy of level 51), and I spend my days traveling with a druid, enchanter, and a ranger, as well as pickup clerics and other tanks, druids, and bards. Luckily the team I adventure with are all well seasoned, and I have complete trust in their abilities. Having said that, below are situations and spell loads I use to live in Norrath safely, and I hope other clerics old and new will find this information useful.

Traveling!

Ok, I'm level 50, so lets face it, outside of Kurnak and the planes; I worry about few dangers from traveling afoot in Norrath. However, with the new world come new dangers, and many that could make me into a meal in very short order. We have eight spell slots, and although I find that's usually enough, we always wish we had more available. Making good use of them in traveling situations makes sense IMHO.

I use the following configuration for traveling long distances on foot in the dangerous lands of Kurnak:

1-Greater Healing
2-Invoke Fear
3-Panic the Dead
4-Root
5-Force
6-Banish Undead
7-Retribution
8-Divine Aura

Now, I'll explain something. In Kurnak I don't travel without SoW. If I can't get a SoW by offering a nice druid some of my buffs in return, then I'll use a potion. If the day comes when I can't buy potions, and when I can't get a SoW from a druid or shaman or ranger, then I'll go spend my time making a shaman character to make my own SoW potions. It's that simple. Without SoW if I run into something nasty, my chances of out-running it are pretty slim. I always carry a couple of invis potions, water breath potions, and SoW potions with me. Below are why I choose the spells I choose:

Greater Healing - It casts fast, heals me almost two bubbles of health, which is enough to run at full speed with SoW. If I run into something I want to kill and not avoid, I can always root and swap it to superior software.

Invoke Fear - This should be obvious, if I can't kill it, or don't want to, I'll fear it and leave.

Panic the Dead - Used in the same manner as Invoke Fear, however it lasts two times as long on undead =)

Root - Yes, level 9 root. It's the best root spell in the game, and lasts just as long as immobilize and enstill. It casts faster than enstill and immobilize, I can get it off in a fight every time, and it allows you to do damage to the mob with less of a chance of breaking, unlike enstill and immobilize which will break almost immediately if you do damage.

Force - 5-sec stun @ 90 damage. If it's a fast hitter and I need it out of my face, I can usually get this stun off, even if it's just long enough to root the critter and run.

Banish Undead - 585 damage undead. Low mana requirements, long cast time. Unless I want to kill the undead critter, I won't use it just for traveling, but I keep this and Retro up just in case.

Retribution - Used in the same manner as Banish, it's here if I need it or want it. 516 DD spell, VERY slow casting.

Diving Aura - Yep, about 5 seconds of invulnerability with a cast time of 1.0! If I need it, it's there, it won't last long but it gives me time to put some distance between the mob and myself for a quick heal.

Fighting in a Group (Mix of Undead and Non-Undead)

1- Greater heal
2- Superior Heal
3- Word of Health
4- Force or Complete Heal (If I am tank heavy in the group I’ll swap force for CHeal)
5- Sound of Force
6- Banish Undead
7- Retribution
8- Divine Barrier or Root (If I have someone snaring or mezzing I don't use root)

Now, these spell loads I have found efficient for fighting blue critters at level 45-50. Some are level 44 and 49 spells, so if you're lower than 44 or 49, adapt each spell with your best version and give it a try. Everyone has a different playstyle. Mine is not necessarily best for you, but it does work for my group and me. Here are a few tips:

1) Always buff your party. You can add a fair amount of HP and AC to your party without reagents. If you don't, you're just going to spend more time healing and be out of manna at a crucial moment. If you keep your group buffed, and they continue to pull when you have no manna, you might want to think about finding another group.

2) Cleric buffs are going to eat all your manna, and possibly part of another bar of manna if you're buffing a full group. Make sure you let the party know they won't be getting heals during this time, at least then if you're with a bunch of greedy idiots who think because they are full health they can take on the world, you won't feel bad about having to resurrect them later =)

3) Always buff yourself first! That way if your buffs are wearing off, they'll be wearing off on the party next. You'll know when to rebuff instead of having to be told.

Spell loads are one thing, cleric tactics are another. I'll try not to get too much into cleric tactics in this guide, but I wanted to add those few tips. Enjoy!
