What is Changing Tide?

Well I guess it is time to answer a few questions I have been getting...

Its a new guild formed by Cailette and I. The idea behind our doing it was pretty simple.

1) We wanted to provide a chat channel to make some of our runs easier.
2) We have friends in every guild on the server and choosing one over the other was just not going to happen.
3) We wanted to create a place where folks who believe that if you accept the challenges of adventuring in Norrath, beating the odds and the baddies is the reward. Folks who want to build a group of tight teams that KNOW the others have their back. Folks who you know you are willing to die for. Folks who want to explore all the nooks and cranies of this land of ours. Those things being done, its a bonus if a nice trinket happens to be found while doing that exploring.

If you have ever asked "can we pull the rares to zone and pick them off" you probably don't belong with us. If you were in a group with two mages and a + int item dropped that they didn't have and could use and you suggested the warrior and druid roll for it, you probably don't belong with us.

I don't begin to assume I speak for Cailette or anyone else in our new family, but I do know we all think quite a bit alike. Personally I am tired of a lot of the "turf war" type mentality. I am also tired of the "my Dad is bigger than your Dad" attitude. I don't want to bicker or squabble or fight. I want to have some fun without stomping on the fun of others. I want to treat others the way I would like to be treated.... and have them treat me the same way. Sheer numbers do not make you powerful and money does not make you rich. Lewt most assuredly does not make you a respected person. Friendship however, covers all 3 of those bases quite well. Thats the most valuable thing we have in any life.

Well those are a few of the things that drove me to this point and this decision. We have no interest in recruiting from current guilds. We have friends in every guild and we want to keep those friends and build solid relationships with their guilds as well. It is a scary road we have chosen to traverse and we hope that these friends will help make the trip a little brighter.

This will also make calendar events easier as I can post as our guild rather than Ligge etc etc. Hopefully we have both earned the respect and friendship of enough people that it will continue and trickle down to the rest that unite with us.

We often have wondered how we have pulled off some of the feats we have in the past. Friendship, trust, and loyalty have been the cause. We knew the other person wasn't going to get greedy or careless due to an item drop. And we knew no one was ever running and leaving party members behind. Sticking through some tough times we have found a mix that makes it a joy to be in Norrath. So now it is time we put this to the test on a larger scale.

Thanks!

Ligge and Cailette

Code of Conduct

Always remember the golden rule. Odds are good that if you wonldn't like someone doing/saying that to you, you shouldn't even be thinking about saying or doing it. Plus, you don't know who that person really is at the keyboard. I can't stress this enough. I have made some good friends as my youngins that have no idea they were talking to me. The reverse is also true.

Guild chat should be rated PG 13. What is said in group is at groups discretion. There are people of all ages that will be part of our family and making someone feel uncomfortable due to language is just not a good thing.

Please no guild bashing on any boards, /ooc /shout /say or even in /group. If you need to, use our private areas. Everyone needs a place to rant. Send me a tell and whine all you want. However, remember one bad incident shouldn't focus blame on a whole guild. Again, keep in mind you have no idea who that person is on the other side of the keyboard and what other life/lives they may lead in Norrath.

Guild invites are, by design, reserved for Officers. Applicants need to be sponsored by at least one full member. Then they are discussed by all of us in the private forums. Officers report the vote of their team members. Final voting takes place in the Officer forum after all things have been considered.

Please no power looting or no ninja looting. Everyone in your group deserves a fair share. Turn on split, take turns, whatever works best for everyone.

Group loot should be awarded to or rolled by the appropriate classes and people present that will use them first. Senior Officer or group leader decides, guild tag has no bearing on the loot distribution. The people in the group putting their life on the line get 100% recognition for their efforts.

Odds are strong that there will be no "guild-only" events. We have too many friends that we will want to include for that to happen. However, there may be a time when we all go somewhere after a meeting as a group. This would be a guild only event in some aspects, but others are always welcome to join at that time. We have and always will maintain friends in many guilds and these people should be treated as family as you would anyone wearing our tag.

Disputes need to be taken up with your Officer if you can not handle it amongst yourself. If you don't feel comfortable speaking with your Officer then take it up with another or one of the Guild leaders.

Over all we oppose power leveling. Touchy subject and open to discretion to get over a frustrating bubble or two. However, a caster who doesn't know the range and depth of their spells or a meleer who doesn't have important skills maxed tend to be more of a danger to their team than an aid. It would be a disservice to your friends, groups and guild to be one of these types. Advice on hunting grounds and different dungeons will be always be given to help those levels and skills fly by.

You should always give everyone the benefit of a doubt once. Screw me once shame on you, screw me twice shame on me. If you suspect you have been mistreated or you have seen foul play always try to see the other side of the issue first.

You are 100% accountable for anything your character does. Anything they do is a reflection on us as a whole. This includes shared accounts or the sharing of accounts. It happens and that's fine, but please remember that if they mess up and give you a bad name and therefore the guild, you are the one that faces those consequences.
