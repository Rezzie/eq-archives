Journal - 25-JUL-1999, 24-JUL-1999

 

 

25-JUL-1999
After breakfast in Dagnor’s Cauldron, I listlessly strolled into the Estate of Unrest. Surveying the courtyard, I realized that I was practically alone. With that realization in mind, I decided to take my leave of Unrest, and headed back out to Dagnor’s Cauldron. The run was swift though, since I still had the Spirit of the Wolf. Once I reached the Butcherblock Mountains, I purchased more food supplies and sold what little loot I had acquired from the night before. Minuets later, I arrived at the docks, where I found a wood elf rogue waiting and a dwarf fisherman. Upon closer inspection, I recognized the wood elf to be Dulark (Level 19, Wood Elf, Rogue, <Borrowers of Norrath>). Many moons ago, when I was hunting bandits in West Karanas, Dulark would occasionally be in the same party as myself. He did not seem to recognize me though; this was fine by me, since I was in no mood for conversation. To pass the time, I put away my sword and shield, and took out my fishing pole. I was barely able to cast the rod five times, before I spied the ship in the distance. The ship docked and the two of us boarded. I noticed on the bow, the inscription "SirensBane". The ship had only one passenger; a half elf ranger named Naoto (Level 20, Half Elf, Ranger). He did not seem to care that the ship had docked and remained aboard when we set sail.

Once the coast faded from view, the whether changed drastically. Blue skies and white clouds gave way to dark skies and gray clouds. Then the rain began to fall with lightning following. The seas began to undulate in a sickening motion. I began to feel queasy, so I sat down and stared only at the deck. Hours later, the storm passed. I looked up and saw several large, scraggly rocks to my right. The nearest one appeared to have two sea creatures basking in the moonlight. I was not sure, but I took them to be sirens. Further to the right, I thought I saw an aqua goblin. As we harmlessly passed the rocks, I looked back and was surprised to see a silhouette standing on a rock far to the left of the larger ones. I squinted my eyes, but was unable to resolve the image in the darkness.

About an hour later, the ship docked at an island with an Inn not far from the dock. Due to the lateness of our voyage, Zachariah Reigh was not there to greet us. I imagined that even if he was, he would have frowned, since there were only three passengers. With a gust of wind in the sails we left island and continued toward our destination. With that, I went below deck and tried to catch up on some much-needed sleep.

When I awoke, the ship was already docked in East Freeport. I gathered my belongings and made my way to the West section. At the West gate, I found a wizard, Xcalaber (Level 15, Human, Wizard, <The Circle of Eternity>), to bind my soul. I paid him the usually courtesy, 2pp. It was still dark, and being fully rested, I was in need of something to do.

So, I veered left from the gate and headed towards the West Commonlands. My journey was uneventful; I continued along the edge of a mountainside into the East Commonlands. While clearing the top of a tall sand dune, I came to a spot where eerie stone pillars jutted from the sand at the base of the dune. A strange red glyph adorned the center pillar. Having been here before, I knew that undead conjugated at these ruins. I surveyed the ruins quickly and noticed nothing stirred. Satisfied that they were unoccupied, I continued along my way. After some time of traversing the sand dunes, I finally came upon my destination. An opening in the side of a sand covered mountain. This would be only my second visit to Befallen.

I walked through the entrance and into a medium sized hall. To the left, a caved in hallway. Rusty spikes lined both sides of the hall, with rotting heads impaled on them. Straight again and down a small flight of stairs, four other hallways, two on each side, adjoined the hall and each doored. In the center, a large rock blocked the view of the rear, where part of the wall had eroded away to reveal several skulls embedded in the rock. These skulls glowed in an eerie incandescent green light. As I stood there, absorbing my surroundings, a dwarf in bronze armor came running by with a ghoul in hot pursuit. I asked the dwarf Laudrac (Level 16, Dwarf, Warrior, <Clan of Light>) if he was in need of assistance. He grunted in the affirmative. With that, I began to cast Ward Undead upon the ghoul. I cast the spell three times and the decadent creature dropped dead. I used my remaining mana to heal the dwarf. I asked him if he would like to join forces, he said he only worked alone and scuttled off. I watched him open the second door on the left with a strange key. While the door was still ajar, I slipped in.

I cautiously explored the adjoined rooms. So far, they were only cluttered with rocks, bones and giant rats. Down at the end of the hall, the floor gave way to the level below. I peered over and noticed rusty spikes protruding from the floor, and broken stone stairs to the left.

Just then three combatants ran into the room below from a hall hidden from my view. It appeared to be a troll fighting an acolyte and his pet skeleton. The dwarf I had healed earlier came running up from behind me to see what all of the ruckus was about. The troll seemed to be losing, the dwarf Laudrac asked if we should help. Without waiting for an answer, he entered the room to my left. I followed, and noticed that the wall to the right of the door had crumbled and the stone stairs could be seen beyond them. The dwarf was already descending the stairs and nearing the battle. I too followed. The dwarf and the troll were engaging the acolyte, so I began to attack his skeletal pet. With the help of a few Ward Undead spells, I was able to finish off the pet. By then, the dwarf and the troll had finished the acolyte too.

Not knowing my way around, I ascended the stairs and returned to the level above. The dwarf followed and asked for another heal. I used my remaining mana to heal him and began to meditate. While meditating, I was attacked by a Dark Elf Skeleton. The foe was more than a match for me, in my weakened condition. I had to use Lay of Hands on myself and the skeleton still proceeded to gravely injure me, so I called for help. The dwarf, Laudrac was not far away, and quickly came to my aid. Once the skeleton was killed, I asked Laudrac to unlock the door for me, so that I might find a safe place to rest and meditate. He cordially obliged, and I made my way back to the main hall near the entrance.

While meditating, a cleric named Thuunder (Level 17, Human, Cleric, <Dark Circle>) happened by, in search of a companion. I told him that I was very much in need of a companion as well, so we joined forces. Luckily, Thuundar had been to Befallen many times before and was very familiar with its layout. In addition, he had two keys for two of the doors. The first order of business, he said, was to get me a set of keys as well. So, I followed him as he lead the way to a room where he said an ogre shadow knight stayed with the keys. Along the way we had to fight many greater skeletons, lurking mummies, ghouls and other undead.

Finally, we came upon the shadow knight and a furious battle ensued. Thuunder took on the surrounding skeletons while I engaged the shadow knight. The cowardly shadow knight began to flee when I was about to smite him. So I chased off after him down unfamiliar corridors and rooms. Finally, I delivered one last fatal blow and downed him. I was happy to find two keys, a large rawhide mask and barbed armplates on him. Thuunder informed me of the magical properties of the barbed armplates, so I replaced my banded armplates with them.

With the newly acquired key we gained entrance to a new section of Befallen. There, a dark elf skeleton greeted us. After we dispatched of him, a lurking mummy attacked us forthright. After the mummy, we were badly injured and in need of meditation. While meditating another dark elf skeleton attacked us. The skeleton focused his attention to me and I began to weaken. Thuunder tried his best to save me, but alas the skeleton was too much in my weakened condition and I feel unconscious.

I awoke, unarmored and equipmentless in West Freeport where the wizard had bound me the day before. I sat and began to re-memorize some necessary spells. Once finished, I summoned a Hammer of Wrath for protection, and cast Courage and Holy Armor upon myself. From there, I began to make my way back towards Befallen.

Upon re-entering Befallen, my companion Thuunder greeted me. Since my equipment and gear was locked behind a door on the third level, we would have to find someone with a key or find another shadow knight. We made our way back to the locked door to see if any other adventures would be there with a key. We found no such luck, so we headed back to find another shadow knight. While fighting skeletons near the shadow knight room, we ran into a fellow guild member of mine, a bard name Khoals (Level 18, Wood Elf, Bard, <Shepherds of the Wolf>). He eagerly joined us as we waited for a shadow knight. The time passed as we waited and waited. The room had two large rocks in the center, which meet the ceiling. Khoals, being bored, sat atop one of them. Thuunder mentioned that he knew a monk name Svancara who had the third key and would leave to find him. We agreed and stay behind as he left. Hours later, I lost track of Khoals, but meet up with the monk. We made our way back to the locked door and were attacked in front of it. He opened the door for me and also engaged the skeleton. I entered and found another dark elf skeleton waiting for me. He grinned maniacally as the door swung shut, and locked, behind me. Being armorless, the skeleton began to rip me to shreds. I tried futilely to fend him off with my Hammer of Wrath. Knowing full well that I would die again, I began to yell for help. Svancara unlocked and open the door, then I ran in. The skeleton became disinterested in me and attacked another adventurer who was standing near the door. After the skeleton was killed, I retrieved my equipment and went up the stairs to heal and meditate. While meditating I was attacked by a ghoul, luckily, Thuunder had just returned and helped dispatch it. Once we were fully healed we headed back to the shadow knight room where we found Khoals firmly wedged between the two rocks. He was yelling that he could not move and was stuck. Apparently, he had fallen asleep on top of one of the rocks and slide down in-between them while turning over. Try as we did, we could not free him. Finally, in desperation, Khoals suggested that we find our guild leader Graywolf, and have him teleport us out of Befallen. Thuunder being the only one in our group with the keys to the first two doors went back to the entrance to meet with Graywolf. About an hour later, Graywolf and Thuunder returned just as three skeletons were trying to attack Khoals. I killed one, and the others finished the remaining. After the brief, but chaotic skirmish, Graywolf gathered us all around the rocks and cast his group teleport spell. Moments later, we found ourselves at the druid ruins in the East Commonlands. There were also many other people near the ruins, mainly dealing with the vendors there. Graywolf, being a very busy person these days, had to leave to tend to matters elsewhere. Thuunder, decided to return to Befallen, so we bid our farewells. Khoals had business in Halas, and I wanted to venture to Permafrost, so we journeyed together. Khoals played a lively tune which accelerated our travel and with that we were off.

We made haste to Kithicor Forest, then through Highpass Hold, East Karanas, North Karanas, West Karanas, Qeynos Hills, Blackburrow, Everfrost Peaks and finally to Halas. All the while chatting about various sundry things. In East Karanas, a hound of some kind attacked me, then while running though West Karanas, a bandit made the misfortune of attacking us. Other than that, the journey was skirmishless. Once we reached Halas, I paid a female barbarian shaman 2pp to bind our souls there. Khoals worked on making and selling pottery while I made time for lunch.

Half an hour later, Khoals and I meet at the entrance of Halas and headed for Permafrost. We wandered the tundractic plains of Everfrost for hours in the quite darkness. Twice lone orc mountaineers ambushed us. While en route, we aided a barbarian being attacked by a polar bear by a tower. Afterwards, I fully healed him.

From there, we finally found the entrance to Permafrost, an hour later. The entrance resembled a giant keep with a large, unobstructed opening with torches on either side. Once long ago, I had been here before, so I know full well about the ice giants that guarded the entrance. Khoals sneaked up through the opening, slightly entering the courtyard, and peeked to the left and right. Two massive ice giants stood on both sides, waiting to stomp any passerby’s. Finally we decided to make a mad dash for it, using one of Khoals’s bard songs. With the song playing I lead the way, bolting through the courtyard with Khoals following close behind. As we cleared the courtyard, a hallway emerged to our left and a cubbyhole to our right with an ice giant waiting. We kept running and the ice giant was only able to smack Khoals once before we entered Permafrost.

Inside, I noticed that the walls were made of greenish-blue ice and they seemed tall enough for a giant to comfortably walk about. The hallway where we stood, ended before for us and turned right. We almost didn’t notice the ice goblin standing there; he seemed to almost blend into his surroundings. He attacked us and later fled when it was apparent that we were too great a foe for him. So we cautiously continue down the gargantuan hall to our right. Two more ice goblins blocked our path. I mumbled my concerns as we were forced into combat again. The goblin furthest away was my target. I sprinted up to meet him and noticed that the great hall made a turn to the left. I peer to the left, half concentrating on my foe, and saw that the hall opened into a room of sorts with a ladder going up and five more ice goblins. All five ice goblins turned at once and with a screech, and tore off in my direction. I started to back away as I saw an ice goblin diviner start casting nasty spells. The odds were not in our favor, I thought as I was running back towards my comrade. "Run!", I shouted. Just as I was about to exit Permafrost I was stunned by the onslaught of ice goblins surrounding me. Luckily, I was able to leave with half of my health. Khoals had also lost half of his health as the target of the ice goblin diviner’s spells.

Once outside, we knew we’d have to deal with the ice giants again. With a quick prayer to Karana and Khoals’s bard song playing we began our mad dash once again, this time with Khoals in the lead. Just as we cleared the courtyard and gate, I felt the cold presence of an ice giant behind me. I shouted to Khoals, "Run!". Just as I began see the outlines of a nearby stone temple, I no longer heard the bard’s song and began to decelerate. With that, I felt a crushing blow come from behind and fell lifelessly to the cold snow.

I awoke to the sounds of a flickering campfire; I was in Halas. Once again, I began to re-memorize my needed spells. Summoning the hammer and holy armor, once finished.

Hours later, Khoals returned to Halas. Then with the help of another bard song, we went in search of my equipment. Once I was re-equipped we decided to save Peramfrost for another day when we’re more powerful.

From there we hunted many wooly mammoth calves. Finally after we had slain our sixth calf or so, we were having a hard time finding more in the darkness. So, I decided to camp and Khoals said he would return to Halas.

 
24-JUL-1999
I joined a small group of adventures in the Estate of Unrest, Lucian (Level 18, Wood Elf, Ranger, <Azure Guards>), Mintin (Level 17, Gnome, Warrior, <Phoenix Trading Company>) and Bodhi (Level 15 Wood Elf Ranger).  Having fought along side Mintin the eve before, I asked if he had the 10pp he owed me for the small bronze collar I gave him.  He assured me he hadn't forgotten and would pay me later.  Soon thereafter, we began to engage the many vile undead creatures lurking around the unholy grounds.  We battled many a ghoul and skeleton.

While in the courtyard, an injured Paladin, named Hihi (Level 25, Half Elf, Paladin, <The Praetorian Guard>), ran by with many fowl undead creatures in pursuit.  As she feel from exhaustion and near death, I healed her completely with Lay of Hands.  She fled to safety and thanked me later.  Shortly afterwards, a wizard named Winthrope (Level 19, Erudite, Wizard, <Lords of Chaos>) joined us.  Many battles later, Lucian had to leave and a bard, named Naladen (Level 15, Wood Elf, Bard) joined as well.  A few battles later, Bohdi and Naladen made a trip to Greater Faydark to sell their loot and buy supplies.  Sometime in between, a dwarven rogue, named Vergadian (Level 15, Dwarf, Rogue) joined us.

Upon my urgings, we entered the derelict mansion in search of more undead to release.  Many other adventurers were also inside the mansion.  Finally, we found an unoccupied room, with rusty spikes in the floor and ghastly hooks hanging from the ceiling.  I shuddered at the sight of the hooks; contemplating their purpose.  While in the room we fought many ghouls, dark boned skeletons and even a lurking mummy.  Just as I was beginning to feel comfortable in the room, I heard the telltale screech of a werebat.  Moments later, the dreadful beast came jumping down the nearby stair and headed straight for me.  Before I could react, the werebat slashed out at me with many evil skeletons in toe.  I yelled for my companions to flee as I headed out myself.  Luckily, all of our party members made it to safety.  Sometime later, the Wizard Winthrope was smited by a mob of undead.  Upon his attempt to retrieve his gear, another mob of undead, fell him again.  With that, he took his leave and promised to return another day.

Soon after Bohdi had returned, I had to go to Kaladim to sell loot and buy supplies.  Mintin asked to return with food and drink for him.  With great speed, I ran through the bleak and dreary Dagnor's Cauldron and returned to conifer surroundings of Butcherblock Mountains.  I found my favorite High Elf vendor in a small house near the entrance of Kaladim and sold my loot and restocked my ration and milk supply.  With post haste, I began my way back towards Unrest.  Finally, upon returning to Unrest, I found my remaining companions, Mintin and Vergadian near death with two ghouls hot on their tails.  Mintin being the most seriously injured and closest to death, received my attention first.  A day had past since I last used Lay of Hands, so I fully healed Mintin and went off to find Vergadian again.  After a few Light Heal spells were cast on Vergadian, I felt my mana was completely drained and went back over to Mintin to dispatch his adversary.  Mintin was once again badly injured, however, his ghoul was easily disposed of.  Upon running back to Vergadian, I noticed he was on the verge of death.  Luckily, I was able to smite his ghoul before he met his maker.  Later, he recuperated in the relative safety of Dagnor's Cauldron where I gave Mintin twenty units of rations and twenty units of milk, free.

While meditating, I noticed that Winthrope had returned, so we eagerly accepted him back into our party.  This reunion was short-lived, though.  After running from many overwhelming groups of undead, Winthrope had to leave.  Soon thereafter, I informed my remaining comrades, Mintin and Vergadian, that I too had to leave, to find breakfast and rest.

Later in the day, I returned to Unrest in search of brave new souls to adventure with.  At first there were none, so I braved the grounds alone, challenging a few select tormented undead and animated jack o'lanterns, with the occasional death beetle in tow.  While meditating after a sweat less skirmish, a druid new to Unrest, named Everett (Level 16, Half Elf, Druid, <Lost Order of Akalabeth>) happened upon me.  So we joined forces.  As we waited for undead to release, I schooled Everett in the peculiarities of Unrest.  We found few undead to fight, so we entered the mansion though the rearmost door and occupied the room therein.  Although, we did not stay long for the surrounding chaos was too great for a small party of only two.

Returning to the empty grounds of the courtyard, we ran into a halfling warrior name Nean (Level 15, Halfling, Warrior) who eagerly joined our ranks.  The courtyard grounds seemed unnaturally sparse.  Just then I remembered that sometimes, the mindless tormented dead fall into the small mote around the hill with a waterfall.  So I dove into the chilly water and found a prospect about 20 feet underwater.  Once he was lured to the surface, my group easily dispatched of him.  Soon afterward, Everett wandered off, never to be seen again.  While Nean was engaging death beetles, I enlisted the services of another adventurer to replace Everett.  Avital (Level 16, Wood Elf, Druid), a wood elf druid joined us.  Shortly thereafter, a wood elf bard, Penaden (Level 17, Wood Elf, Bard), also joined us.  We fought many tormented dead and jack o'lanturns.  Whilst engaged in combat, an angry group of deadly undead forced us to retreat to the confides of Dagnor's Cauldron.  While healing and meditating,  we were separated from Penaden.  With everyone fully healed and meditated, we re-entered the Estate of Unrest; returning to our duties of bashing the undead.  

During one of our rest and meditation sessions, an old comrade, Bodhi (Level 15 Wood Elf Ranger), happened by and joined.  Bodhi, being unexplainably fearless, ventured into the mansion alone to find more prey for us to battle.  Some time later, a friend of Avital, Dias (Level 15, Halfling, Cleric) joined us just is time as we were being badly injured by a group of death beetles, a greater skeleton and a jack o'lantern.  Around ten battles later I felt myself become more seasoned and powerful.  I took this cue to take leave of my group to find some lunch and relaxation.  At the same time, another Paladin, Tanaman (Level 16, Half Elf, Paladin, <Aradani>) expressed an interest in joining our mighty group.  I told my group to take him as my replacement and was off.
A scant hour later, I returned to where I had left my party, to find them all still there.  They eagerly accepted me back into their ranks.  With that, we entered the mansion with high expectations.  Bodhi wanted to return to the room adorned with spikes and hooks, however, it was already occupied.  So we made our way to the rearmost room.

After about five battles or so, Tanaman asked that we invite a former companion of his named Jarred (Level 16, Dark Elf, Shadow Knight).  I felt somewhat hesitant about allowing the dark elf shadow knight into our ranks, however, the rest of the members felt he could be a useful addition.  Reluctantly, I succumbed to their wishes; though I would keep a sharp eye on him.

Into our first battle with Jarred, against a ghoul, I noticed him cast a spell on the near dead ghoul, which at first glance appeared to me to be Spook the Dead.  Once the ghoul was dead, I chastised the dark elf, telling him not to cast Fear on the undead.  It is a dangerous thing to do here, which could lead to our deaths.  And I for one, would not go chasing any undead with Fear cast on them in here.  He assured me that the spell he cast was not Spook the Dead, but another one that causes the victim to freeze in fear, not run in fear.  I accepted his explanation and readied myself for the next battle.

There we stayed and battled many ghouls, lurking mummies and the occasional dark boned skeletons and even a few dry boned skeletons.  Several hours and battles later, my group was wandering off and I noticed a brave bard, slightly struggling with a nasty ghoul.  I quickly cast the spells of Courage and Holy Armor upon him and healed him with my remaining mana.  Once I was through casting, I noticed the bard appeared familiar.  Lo and behold, it was Penaden, my lost comrade of earlier.  Once he dispatched of the ghoul he was eager to rejoin me, however, my current party was already full.  Since I was getting tired I advised my group to take Penaden as my replacement and bid them all farewell.
