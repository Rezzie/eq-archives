Continental Drift will accept new members from lvl 10 upwards. All potential members must read and agree to the following rules.

GUILD RULES

Recruitment

While we accept members from lvl 10 upwards we are in the main a mid lvl guild. Our parent guild Old Continent has many more high lvl players for the large 'epic' events. We generally advise our members to move to Old Continent at lvl 45, however this is in no way enforced members may stay in Continental Drift until lvl 60 if they so wish.

All potential members must fill in the members application procedure (at the bottom of this page).

This will then be put on our board for member approval where we will discuss your application for a few days. Existing members are given the chance to vote on your application, when you receive 4 votes your membership will be approved.

The way most people are invited into Continental Drift is if you know us or our parent guild Old Continent. Friends want to play with friends. So if you know some of us the approval and the trial period are mostly a formality.

As a new member you are obligated to register an account on EZBoard for communication. All guild information will be published on the Message boards. You are also obligated to participate on the boards. You must add your name to the Current Members section. Failure to do so will result in you being removed from the guild.

Rules of Conduct

This section could just be a reference to the everquest site. In short 

* Don’t train people on purpose, 
* Don’t KS, 
* Don’t give yourself and Continental Drift a bad name with rude behaviour in trades OOC or Shouts.
* Don’t bicker about camp sites, but state your right.
* Don’t let your first action be to /petition and call a GM if any of the Rules of Conduct are broken by others causing harassment to you. Seek diplomacy, call in friends or guild officers, then as a last resort /petition. 
* Many, many problems can be solved by the /ignore command.

Guild Chat

The guild chat is our common line of communication when online. Here is how we can use it:

* SOCIAL: Chat and joke, any normal socialising.

* HELP: Request help for ports, price checks. We are here to help each other. However do NOT see this as a service which you at any time can request and expect delivered.

* IGNORE: You are of course allowed to turn off guild chat if you are in a place where you do not need disturbance. Expect people to do this on raids.

* RAIDS: The guild say may be "borrowed" by a majority of people on a raid where fast communication is needed. If so, please try and keep socialising and jokes to a minimum.

* SILENCE: The guild leader or any officer can call "Guild say SILENCE". This basically means that information necessary for us all to hear are being relayed and just for a short period we need not to be interrupted.

*MOTD: The guild MOTD is displayed each time you log onto a character that is a CD member. It will often contain important information that the whole guild needs to know so please read this.

The Boards

You are obliged to read the boards. This is our primary means of communication! To do this, you must make an EZBoard account. For access to member protected areas, post your name on the General Boards and state that you want in. These boards are password protected and our officers will inform you of the password on guild say.

On the boards, use normal rules of conduct. Use them to debate, arrange raids, chit chat or suggest improvement if there is something you don’t like. Proper categories have been made for you to use them! 

Do not use the boards as a place for personal vendetta or flames. This doesn’t mean that every sentence needs be polished and politically correct, get to the point and say what you mean, but please be constructive in all cases!

Grouping

Grouping is the thing in Everquest. Always try and group with the guild ! Always ! 

If you are moving into a new place, see if other guildies want to go with you. For example post on the boards asking if anyone wants to camp any area you wish to go to.

Loot has been the reason for most disputes on Norrath. In Continental Drift we do not want to be known as ninjalooters, lootwhores or similar. Be fair and have a general rule that those that CAN and WILL use, will roll on an item. 

However be responsive to the group you are with, there is a thriving economy in Everquest and many items can fetch a good price on auction. In many groups all members are allowed to roll for any item. 

If you enter a group where the object is to gain wealth, be sure you agree upon the rules before entering the group. This will prevent any disagreement later on.

Raiding 

Anyone in Continental Drift can set up a raid involving a lot of people. In fact we encourage people to do this. There are however some rules you most apply to.

* Be sure that everyone joining knows the objective for the raid. This might sound formal, but it need not be. Having fun is a good enough reason for going!

* Never let down a guild member. This can be about joining the raid, help with corpse retrieval or similar promises. You do not leave the raid or let down a guild member for anything. That being said RL is more powerful than Lord Nagafen and will always win, but please make sure everyone is aware that you are leaving.

* You are now the warlord! This takes planning. Planning is best done on the boards. If you are looking to recruit many people for your raid you have best give at least a weeks notice. The officers will always help you plan and organise events, just ask us.

* There are many things to learn about multigroup raids which you will learn as a CD member. The most important one is 'THE WARLORD IS GOD!' all members on the raid must follow his instructions to the letter.


Raid Loot Rules

When raiding, loot rules are simple: We are not loot whores.

* Check the drops and announce them.

* Everyone who CAN and WILL use an item will roll.

* Rotation means, that once you have won a roll on a type of equipment you loose your right to roll on that type until all who CAN and WILL use that type of equipment have won such an item. There are four types of equipment: normal armor, breastplate, robe and weapons. For example you win a piece of armor and next a weapon drops. In this case you are allowed to roll on the weapon drop again with a chance to win it.

* Honour cannot be explained, it is just a common trait for Continental Drift members. There are no rules for this it simply has to be lived, honour makes people pass on rolls for one or the other personal reasons.

 

The main objective of the guild is to have fun, these rules are here to try to make that easier for all guild members and the people they group with. Above all else we are here to help one another in the land of Norrath.

How To Apply

1) Follow this link and create an ezboard account. http://www.ezboard.com/create_join.html

2) Once your account is created follow the link below to our message board. create a post with your character name as the title. In the body of the post give the following information.

    Your character name

    Class and Level

    How you found out about the guild, or the name of the member who recommended you.

    Some information about yourself

 

Once this has been done try to find members to group with so that they can get to know you. we operate a voting system which means that 4 of our existing members need to give you a yes vote before you are allowed to become a member. So by getting to know us you will get the votes faster.

Once you have 4 votes an officer will contact you to arrange an invite to the guild. We operate a 1 month trial period during which time you are not allowed to take items from the guildbank or vote for new members. Apart from that there is no other difference between you and the other members. you are welcome to attend all raids and will be included in all loot rolls as per the rules above.

We look forward to hearing from you.
