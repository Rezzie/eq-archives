Information

 

History

Old Continent was formed by Bjoran as a European only guild, this to be able to set up well organised raids with people playing in the same timezone.

Guildchat
The guildchat is our common line of communication when online. Here is how we can use it:

* SOCIAL: Chat and joke, any normal socialing.

* HELP: Request help for ports, pricechecks or resses for that matter. We are here to help each other. However do NOT see this as a service which you at any time can request and expect delivered.

* IGNORE: You are of course allowed to turn off guildchat if you are in a place where you do not need disturbance. Expect people to do this on raids.

* RAIDS: The guildsay may be "burrowed" by a majority of people on a raid where fast communication is needed. If so, please try and keep socialising and jokes low.

* SILENCE: The guildleader or any officer can call "Guildsay SILENCE". This basically means that information neccesary for us all to hear are being relayed and just for a short period we need not to be interrupted.


The Boards
You are oblidged to read the boards. This is our primary means of communication! To do this, you must make an EZBoard account. For access to member protected areas, post your name on the General Boards and state that you want in. These boards are not password protected and our EZBoard administrators will let you in.

On the boards, use normal rules of conduct. Use the debate to arrange raids, chit chat or suggest improvement if there is something you don’t like. Proper categories have been made for you to use them!

When posting use a consciece subject, bad examples are "Read this", "Very important", etc. Do not use the boards as a place for personal vendetta or flames. This doesn’t mean that every sentence need be polished and politically correct: get to the point and say what you mean, but please be constructive at any level !


Grouping
Grouping is the thing in Everquest. Always try and group with the guild ! Always !

If you are moving into a new place, check with guildies that some move with you. For example post on the boards asking if anyone want to camp Guk, Sol B, Kanor Castle, etc.

When in a group, always try and foresee when people are leaving and ask in guildsay, that in 15 min. you have an opening for a healer, tank, nuker, etc. in Lower Guk, Frenzy.

Loot may have been the reason for most disputes on Norath. In Old Continent we do not want to be known as ninjalooters, lootwhores or similar. Be fair and have a general rule that those that CAN and WILL use will roll on an item.

If you enter a group where the object is to gain wealth, be sure you agree upon the rules before entering the group or having new members on board. Meaning if you enter a group at the Archmagi in lower guk and you want to roll on the Shining Metallic Robe, even though you are a warrior: say so before!

In OC a guildcamp might have had the purpose to get Ipren out of his newbie robe, please notice the difference.


Raiding
Everyone in Old Continent can set up a raid involving a lot of people. In fact we encourage people to do this. There are however some rules you most apply to.

* Be sure that everyone joining know the object of the raid. This might sound formal, but it need not be. Having fun is a very good objective, getting as deep into Chardok might, kill Lord Nagafen, get an item from a mob in the Hole you name it!

* Never let down a guildmember. This can be about joining the raid, help with corpse retrieval or similar promises. This does not mean that you cannot host a 2 group raid on Chardok and that you would have to bring everyone. You do not leave or let down a guildmember for anything!

* You are now the warlord! This takes planning. Planning is best done on the boards. If you are looking to recruit 20 people for a hate raid you have best give a week notice.

* Some great guidelines are on our homepage. Best suggestion is to use them, or suggest that they’d be improved.

* Notify other guilds that you are going, post on EQVault.


Raid loot rules
When raiding loot rules are simple: We are not loot whores.

* Check the drops and announce them.

* Everyone who CAN and WILL use an item will roll.

* Rotation means, that once you have won a roll on a type of equipment you loose your right to roll on that type untill all who CAN and WILL use that type of equipment have won such an item. There are four types of equipment: normal armor, breastplate, robe and weapons. For example you win a piece of armor and next a weapon drops. In this case you are allowed to roll on the weapon drop again with a chance to win it.

* Honour – cannot be explained, it is just a common trait for Old Continent members. There are no rules for this it simple have to be lived… honour makes people pass on rolls for one our the other personal reason.


Raid Guests
Every now and then, actually very often, we invite friends to join us on raids. If we do so, we apply all internal OC rules to them. Meaning be sure they know the loot rules and our general Rules of Conduct. They will, when joining as guests, have the same rights as any OC member.


Rules of Conduct
This section could just be a referrence to the everquest site. In short

* Don’t train people on purpose,
* Don’t KS,
* Don’t give yourself and Old Continent a bad name with rude behaviour in trades OOC or Shouts.
* Don’t bicker about camp sites, but state your right.
* Don’t let your first action be to /petition and call a GM if any of the Rules of Conduct are broken by others causing harashment to you. Seek diplomacy, call in friends and guild … and as a last resort /petition.
* Many, many problems can be solved by the /ignore command.


Guild Expenses
All guild expenses are shared on a per-raid basis. Meaning the warlord or raidleader will announce if you are to bring peridot, coffins or coin when joining.


Recruitment
 To join Old Continent you apply on our homepage or tell us in game. And some simple rules apply.

To become member of the Guild you must have a Godfather. Each Member of the guild may sponsor as many apprentices but will be responsible for them.

Your Character must reside on the Xev server.

The recruitment will start at level 35.

WHY NOT BEFORE ?
We dont want to ignore low level characters but the policy of the guild is to form group able to work on high level zone and as quick as possible to organize planes raids. If you are below level 35 you are welcome to join our sister guild <Continental Drift> which will accept application below the 35 limit.

That will put you on our board for member submission where we will discuss your application for a few days. This usually means we ask around if we know you for anything bad failing to comply with our Rules of Conduct. Most people would never fail in this, but some do and we want to play with friendly people and keep up our good reputation.

After a few days or maximum 7 days an officer will contact you for an invite or tell you if your application has been rejected and why.

The way most people are invited into Old Continent are if you know us. Friends want to play with friends. So if you some of us, joining, trial period is mostly a formality.

 The trial period is 3 weeks (this period may be longer depending on the time played).

At the end of the period if no VETO is registered from other guildmembers the character becomes a full member allowing him to sponsor new members.

Weekly Camp
The weekly camp grounds of the guild will allow us to fight together for a common goal! In good time, the weekly camp grounds of the guild will be announced. This rule apply from Monday to Friday evening, thus not the weekend which is open for raids or whatever you want to do.

Meaning if you group, you group foremost with the guild... lay aside personal quests and play and have fun with the guild. That is - hopefully - why you joined... this will allow us to focus harder on a specific area, quests, rare drop or rare spawns and will ensure that when you play you always have a very good chance of finding a group no matter your class and no matter which time of day you play.

The groups on weekly guild camp is in no way restricted to "guild-only" ... group as much as possible with non-guildies and build good relations!

The weekly camp spots will not always be "optimal" for xp, but the spot is chosed taking level into concern. The weekly camp spot might be several zones close together, but is often only one.

These announcements of the weekly camp spot are decided by the guild officers from the ongoing discussions on the forums. These discussions are supplemented with polls, so the entire guild has a say, but officers will see to it that a fair rotation is enforced and that all requests are fairly enqueued.

The weekly campgroup will be divided into 3 groups:
* Level 30-40
* Level 40-50
* Level 46+

Exceptions to the rule - you are of course allowed to go on personal quests or plain simple solo from time to time. Such action are liable to free guildchat taunting... General "associal" behavior, will be questioned and you should as a member consider if Old Continent is the right guild for you.
