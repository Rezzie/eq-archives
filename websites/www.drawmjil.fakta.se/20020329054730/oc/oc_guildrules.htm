GUILD RULES of <OLD CONTINENT>

 

These are the rules for the EverQuest guild <Old Continent> residing on the Xev Server Realm.

 

<Old Continent> (OC) is mainly a European guild, but have members from Asia, North and South America as well.

 

Since OC is a high level guild 45+ we have a sister guild called <Continental Drift> which takes members at any level.

§1 Application for Membership

As an applicant to <Old Continent> you are expected to familiarize yourself with all our guild rules.

 

To become a member follow the steps below:

  1. Your Character must reside on the Xev server and be level 45+.
  2. To become a member of Old Continent you must post an application on our application board yourself, stating the reasons why you would like to join.
  3. When you apply, you MUST NOT be a member of another guild. Clear up your background truthfully.
  4. Eight full members must approve your application. This they can do so, by stating clearly that they give you their vote.
  5. Applying to another guild either when you are an Old Continent member or during the time that you have an active application for Old Continent will void your membership or application to Old Continent. We only want dedicated and loyal players.

After you have posted your application on the application board do not expect to get many votes right away unless you have really good friends in the guild who are full members. Gathering votes is a lengthy process at best where the initiative completely lies with the applicant. So you are going to have to put some work in if you really want to join our guild.

The worst way you can go about it, is to ask people to vote for you. The best way you can go about this, is to seek us out so you can group and raid with us. As far as we are concerned actions speak much louder than words. If you want to join a raid, you can read the raid board and see if we have any plans. Please leave a message there if want to join a certain raid.

 

Two things you should know about <Old Continent> before joining a raid, those are the general raid rules and the raid loot rules. Another thing you must be aware of is our stance on account sharing

Please keep in mind that <Old Continent> is primarily a European guild. Most of our raids are planned around 19.00-20.00 CET on weekdays and 14.00 CET in weekends. If that does not fit your playing hours we are probably not the right guild for you.

 

If some parts of this guide are not clear to you or you disagree with some parts, please feel free to contact an officer in-game, we are nearly always available to discuss matters with you.

§1.1 All characters must be in the guild

Because we only want dedicated and loyal players in our family, it is not allowed to have alts in Old Continent while your main character resides in another guild nor has an active application for another guild.

§1.2 Level limit

Characters in <Old Continent> must be level 46 or above. An exception to that rule is alt characters or the guild bank characters.

§1.3 Automatic voidance of application or membership

Applying to another guild either when you are an Old Continent member or during the time that you have an active application for Old Continent will void your membership or application to Old Continent. We only want dedicated and loyal players.

§1.4 Joining from <Continental Drift>

There are two ways players from <Continental Drift> can join <Old Continent>.
1) By recommendation of three officers from <Continental Drift>.
2) By following the regular application procedure for all applicants.

 

The first method is preferred; if this fails however the CD member can opt for the 2nd method.

§1.5 Veto to an application

Any full member can veto an application. They must however supply a reason for doing so - a veto should be given on the private members board. An officer will then inform the applicant that the application has been voided.

§1.6 Initiate Member

After you are invited to Old Continent you become an Initiate Member. An initiate is with minor exceptions not different from a member except, that an Initiate member is not allowed to vote for or veto applications.

§2 Initiate Member


An initiate is in almost all aspects equal to a full member of Old Continent. An Initiate does not get to vote for or veto applicants. An initiate will be removed from the guild more rapidly than a full member in case of inappropriate behavior. Also an initiate can not expect any guild help on his/her epic. It is only natural that you help the guild first for a while before the guild helps you.

 

If some parts of this guide are not clear to you or you disagree with some parts, please feel free to contact an officer in-game, we are nearly always available to discuss matters with you.

§2.1 Basic Rules

 

There are a few very basic rules:

·          Do not train people on purpose.

·          Do not steal kills.

·          Do not ninja loot.

·          Do not give yourself and Old Continent a bad name with rude behavior in trades, OOC or Shouts.

·          Make sure you know the loot rules in pickup group and follow those rules.

·          Do not bicker about camp sites, but state your right.

·          Do not let your first action be to /petition and call a GM if any of the Rules of Conduct are broken by others causing harassment to you. Seek diplomacy, call in friends and guild  and as a last resort /petition.

·          Remember that many problems can be solved by the /ignore command.

 

§2.2 The Boards

You are obliged to read the boards. This is our primary means of communication! To do this, you must make an EZBoard account. For access to member protected areas, post your name on the General Boards and state that you want in. These boards are not password protected and our EZBoard administrators will let you in.

Use normal rules of conduct. Use the debate to arrange raids, chit chat or suggest improvement if there is something you dont like. Proper categories have been made for you to use them!

When posting use a conscience subject, bad examples are "Read this", "Very important", etc. Do not use the boards as a place for personal vendetta or flames. This doesnt mean that every sentence need be polished and politically correct: get to the point and say what you mean, but please be constructive !

§2.3 The Raid Board

The raid board is where we announce our raids.

Anyone in Old Continent can organize a raid. This does not mean you have to warlord the raid you are organizing. You can ask someone else to be the warlord. However before you announce the raid you have to have a warlord. And that is just one of the many things you need. You also need a bare minimum of required classes.

For example you want to make a Plane of Hate raid you will need enchanters, clerics and wizards and maybe a monk puller at the very least. The recruiting can be done in the member chat forum but it is much better to send tells in-game to the people you think you need. If you have never organized a raid before it might also be a smart idea to talk it through with an officer first.

After you have gathered enough support for the raid you are ready to make a post on the raid board. You should do this preferably a week in advance but at the very least four days in advance.

The topic must contain: Place - date - time
The body must contain: Gathering place, warlord(s), goal of the raid and optionally raid specific loot rules, whether the raid is open or closed and links to maps and tactics.

If you want to raid some place but do want to organize it you can always discuss it with an officer and we will see what we can do for you.

§2.4 Open raids and closed raids

There are two types of raids:

1.       Open raids means friends outside the guild can be invited and all loot is equally rolled on by those who can and will use.

2.       Closed raids, raids with OC members ONLY. If outsiders still want to join for the experience they will be CLEARLY INFORMED IN ADVANCE that they are joining a closed raid. First OC members get to roll on loot, but if no one needs (i.e. an item will rot) the outsiders can roll amongst themselves.

The person that decides if a certain raid is closed or open is the raid leader. The raid leader is responsible for accepting any invites and making it clear to them whether it is an open or closed raid and what that means.

§2.5 Guild Loot

 

Guild loot is items which you received from being in a guild group, on a guild raid, received from a guild member or from the guild bank. We always use "need before greed" loot rules.

 

Guild items have to stay within the guild. If you dont use the item anymore you are expected to hand it down to a guildie. Selling an item that the guild got for you is considered a major offence against the spirit of the guild.

 

Note 1: We do not keep exact track of guild items and we never will. It is a players own responsibility to play within the spirit of the guild and if they cannot or will not, then it is best they rethink their membership.

 

Note 2: The guild bank is a private member forum on our boards so you at anytime can see what equipment and which spells are in store or in use.

§2.6 Private Loot


This encompasses equipment which you have received during your travels of Norrath and during hunts for experience, quests you did or maybe by attending raids of other guilds.

 

This is your property and you can do with it whatever you please. There will never be any rules in this guild forcing you one way or another concerning your property.

 

You are welcome to donate equipment to the guild bank. You can sell equipment to members, but all in good spirit a discount is welcome.

§2.7 Spells


Spells are instrumental to high level casters; everyone is requested to make all spells especially level 55+ ones available to the guild.

§2.8 Guild chat channel

 

Guild chat is the lifeblood of our guild. It is our common line of communication when we are online. How you use guild chat is of the utmost importance. EverQuest is a "team sport" you will never get far and do exciting things alone.

Do not pull too much attention to yourself and your wishes in guild chat.

 

If you want something to happen you better start working towards it by sending tells to the people you think can help you.

 

Repeating your wishes in guild chat will not amount to anything except that you will annoy people.

Other things you should know about guild chat:

  • SOCIAL: Chat and joke, any normal socializing. Guild chat is not for whining, swearing, complaining or personal attacks.
  • HELP: Request help for ports, price checks or resurrections for that matter. We are here to help each other. However do NOT see this as a service which you at any time can request and expect delivered. So do not keep repeating your request. After two times everyone online probably heard it. Try to solve your problem then by sending tells instead.
  • RAIDS: The guild chat may be "borrowed" by a majority of people on a raid where fast communication is needed. If so, please try and keep socializing and jokes low.
  • SILENCE: The guild leader or any officer can call "GUILD SILENCE". This basically means that information necessary for us all to hear are being relayed and just for a short period we need not to be interrupted.

§2.9 Grouping

Always try and group with the guild! Always! If no group is available, try to make one.


If you are moving into a new place, check with guildies that some move with you.

When in a group, always try and foresee when people are leaving and ask in guild chat, that in 15 min. you have an opening for a healer, tank or caster and e.g. Sebilis Disco.

Loot may have been the reason for most disputes on Norath. In Old Continent we do not want to be known as ninja looters, loot whores or similar. Be fair and have a general rule that those that CAN and WILL use will roll on an item.

If you enter a group where the object is to gain wealth, be sure you agree upon the rules before entering the group or having new members on board. Meaning if you enter a group at Disco in lower guk and you want to roll on the Froglok Bonecaster Robe, even though you are a warrior: say so before!

In OC, a guild camp might have had the purpose to get Catapult out of his newbie robe, please notice the difference.

§2.10 Going from Initiate Member to Member

An Initiate member can post a request for promotion on our private board after he or she feels at home in Old Continent. Only officers decide about the promotion and one of the more important factors considered is raid attendance, but also things like general involvement in the guild.

§3 Member

A member is expected to have read and understood all of the parts of the chapters Application for Membership and Initiate Member since several of these rules of course applies to full members as well.

A member has the power to vote and veto applicants as described in §1 and §1.5. One veto is enough to keep an applicant from becoming an initiate. However a veto without a very good reason will not be accepted. The right to veto should never be used lightly and is really only there to keep confirmed badmouthing, ninja looting and kill stealers out of our guild.

Just as the right to veto the right to vote should never be used lightly either. Voting for someone means at least three things:

  • You consider this person a friend.
  • You are convinced that this person has made a big effort to join our guild.
  • You think this person will be an asset to the guild.

 

If the guild runs into trouble with the person(s) you have voted for this will reflect back on you. In practice this could mean for example that when an initiate you have voted for does not play within the spirit of the guild, you will be asked to have a talk with that person.

If some parts of this guide are not clear to you or you disagree with some parts, please feel free to contact an officer in-game, we are nearly always available to discuss matters with you.

§3.1 Epics and high end Quests

Crucial parts in epics or other high end quests require that you obtain help from the guild. Use the following guidelines when doing so:

  • Research your epic or the quest!
  • See to it that you are 55+ at least. Apart from the fact that it would annoy everyone immensely to see level 50 players running around with epic stuff, they wouldn't be able to do much themselves.
  • Be prepared that you'll have to do a lot of work over a prolonged period of time. Some people starting their epics around L50 will most likely have gotten to L60 before completing them.
  • Split the epic into smaller segments. Decide where you will need the help of the guild. I.e., you can't do it with just a small group of friends.
  • Start with one segment and plan a raid around it. Decide what classes you need and how much people should be the absolute minimum. Read the boards and talk to people having done the quests to gather information.
  • Set a date make a post on the board about what is about to be attempted and get people to dedicate themselves to help out.

 

Now, a few hints:
Don't split your epic into 3 parts and try and have the guild doing these three parts three days in a row! Plan one raid, execute it and learn from it before going on to planning the next one. Most members will be joining because they want to have a good time, not because they have a driving ambition to see you flaunt your epic-gun. It is your responsibility to see to it that the participants have a good time and an exciting raid.

Don't expect that one word in guild chat, asking for participants and expect people to enlist, or even come if enlisted. It won't work that way! Make a post on the raid board where you describe in detail what is about to be attempted and what place and time is set - the latter preferably in several time zones. That is not enough by a long shot. Then you should make personal tells in game where you get people to book that day, and promise to come, to ensure that you'll have enough to do what you intend. If you can't raise enough people you will have to set another time.

You should have spoken at length with people having key-roles in the fight/raid. Do the thinking for the instrumental classes beforehand and go through their role with them ahead - confusion is the source of all failures!

Supply participants with as much information as possible. Not only in the post on the board, but at the start of the raid, and during. Several times if possible! Ambitious epic-questers might even want to supply Catapult with material for a guide regarding their quest. Having links to other message boards where the subject is discussed and sites dealing with it, on the informative post on the raid board is a good idea at any rate.

As always, officers will try and be helpful and assist with the raids, however they need to be informed and asked just like everyone else.

Do not make the mistake of pushing people to join your epic-campaign while totally ignoring others needing the same help!

§3.2 Account sharing

Some people have access to others accounts and this is beneficial to the guild in many situations, but please be aware that WHOEVER uses your account, it will be treated in the same way as if it was you.

 

 i.e. If someone else commits a breach of our guild rules while using your account it will be dealt with in the same way as if it was you playing - they are still wearing an Old Continent guildtag (or trying to get one) and as such must behave accordingly. At higher levels in EverQuest, reputation means a great deal.

So please make sure that anyone who has access to your account knows guild rules and sticks to them. It would be very sad to guild remove or veto anyone due to this but it will be done if it is felt necessary.

 

§4 Raid Rules

During a raid the Warlords word is law. His/her decisions are not open for discussion or other comments. If you have a problem with a decision you write it down and it can be discussed after the raid.

 

  • All the Warlords orders are to be followed promptly and precisely.

  • Be there when the raid starts. Do not login at the Sebilis bubble only half an hour in advance and expect to be on time for a raid in Permafrost for example. It is better to be 30 minutes early than 15 minutes late.

  • A raid might require extensive use of guild chat. In that case all members not on the raid are requested to keep their use of guild chat to an absolute minimum.

  • During a raid the shout channel is reserved for the Warlord(s), Puller(s) and Scout(s).

  • Limit your use of the other public channels, use group chat and tells as much as possible.

  • Do not send tells to the Warlord(s), Puller(s) and Scout(s) unless absolutely necessary.

  • Do not request for a certain mob to be pulled.

  • Try to announce at least 45 minutes in advance when you have to leave a raid early.

  • No raid is over until all Corpse Recovery is done.

  • When a raid is over, do not run for the nearest exit or plainly gate. We all leave together in an organized way.

§4.1 Raid loot rules  Conduct!

Loot is a very physical result of a raid! It is also the subject which has caused the most trouble! It has split up guilds and made lethal enemies.

 

Under NO CIRCUMSTANCES will we allow whining or flames to occur!

 

At any moment - have a relaxed attitude and be sure that OC is not the place where someone is trying to lure loot into a closed-circle group of people.

 

Have the courage to enjoy a friend winning a nice item and give him support and room to be happy about it! Nothing is more discouraging than receiving a tell after you won an item, that you thought that another person deserved it more.

§4.2 Raid loot rules  During the raid

When something drops during the raid:

  • Check the drops and announce them. Often there is a person handling loot  be sure he is the FIRST to know what dropped.
  • Everyone who CAN and WILL use an item will roll within the rotation system.
  • The rotation system: Once you have won a roll on a type of equipment you loose your right to roll on that type until all who CAN and WILL use that type of equipment have won such an item. There are three types of equipment: normal armor, chest pieces, and weapons. For example you win a piece of armor and next a weapon drops. In this case you are allowed to roll on the weapon drop, with a chance to win it.
  • Honor - Honor is letting someone else loot an item if it is a major upgrade for them while it would only be a minor upgrade for you. Honor is allowing someone to complete a set of armor. Honor is thinking twice before rolling against a guild-elder. If you are below level 55 please remember that the higher level players often make a raid possible or at least much easier. The guild is best served if the best drops go to the higher level players who need them.

§4.3 Raid loot rules  Mains before alts

Mains will loot before alts, so alts will not get any loot on raids unless no mains can use the loot or if the Warlord specifically asked you to play your alt. The Warlord has to announce this on a public channel. You then have the choice to loot as your alt or as your main if possible to move the main along. Places like Plane of Air might complicate this.

 

People who play two computers on a raid are a great help to the guild and the raid, but the main will loot before alt rule also goes for them as well.

 

§4.4 Raid loot rules  Special items

Some equipment is crucial for the entire guild to be used by the right people.

Epic pieces, breastplates/robes, money items, gems and spells will go to <Old Continent> so people invited to our raids (friends, applicants and Continental Drift) do not get to roll on these five types of items (unless no one in Old Continent can use). They MUST be informed of this before the raid starts - if they are not informed - they do get to roll as an OC member.

Note: Dragon haste items, mini boss drops are not reserved by these rules.

Epic Pieces dropping on raids

If epic pieces drop on a raid inexpertly - if the raid was not made specifically on the raid board to get that epic piece for that specific person, the piece will go to the person that is the highest level, has the highest raid attendance, is the longest with the guild and is the furthest with the epic quest. If it is not clear who this is or the initial choice is contested by multiple persons then all who can and will use that piece for his or her epic will roll. Meaning we do not "register" progress of epic pieces, but trust that you will relay truthfully how far you are.

Scars of Velius Armor - Clothe Drops

Robe will be rolled for first by Necromancers. The effect of the necromancer robe is very useful for the guild (It can cast the spell 500 mana cost New Summon Corpse using 80p small stackable coffins). If no necromancer needs it, then the robe is subject to the old loot rules. However it is very strongly suggested that those people who already have a similar planar robe pass. All other clothe drops will be rolled for by those who can and will use within the rotation system.

Scars of Velius Armor - Leather Drops

Leather Breastplate will go first to druids. It has the very nice spell effect of Regrowth of the Cove on it. Monks there are alternatives like Phase Spider Carapace or Ice Burrower Skin. The rest of the drops will be rolled for within the rotation system.

Scars of Velius Armor - Chain drops

I think the guild should not have a preference here. All the classes: rogue, ranger and shaman would benefit about equally. However again if you have a Plane of Growth breastplate, please pass roll to those who do not have such a marvelous piece of equipment.

Scars of Velius Armor - Plate drops

On ALL drops first ALL our level 60 warriors will roll. If they pass or do not need then all 58+ Warriors, 58+ Shadowknights and 58+ Paladins will roll. If they pass or do not need, then all who can and will use the item will roll within the rotation system.

Spread the wealth, so if you already have pretty good Plane of Growth equipment please pass on rolls for your guildmates who do not have such gear yet.

Note: If you have Kunark legs or Kunark Breastplate you are expected to offer them for roll if you upgrade to Scars of Velius armor.

§4.5 Raid loot rules  Warlord can call special rules before the raid

When organizing a raid, a Warlord can set different rules as long as they are announced to all participants beforehand. Use the discussion boards for this.

 

A raid can be organized to get a certain item for a certain person. But it is also possible for the Warlord of a Plane of Hate raid to announce beforehand that the first Breastplate that drops goes to a certain tanker for example.

§4.6 Raid loot rules  Money items

All gems and money items that drop goes default to the guild bank. All spells that drop that no-one attending can use go default to the guild bank. This includes spells that are too high to use. So if a level 60 magician spell would drop and there is no level 60 magician present, the spell goes to the guild bank. Even if there is a level 59 Magician present. Any officers present on the raid can represent the guild bank.

§4.8 Raid loot rules  Passing on items

 
If you win an item on a guild raid and it replaces the old item you had, you are expected to pass the old item down to a fellow guild-member  especially if the item you already use was won within the guild (and isnt no-drop). Spread the wealth!
