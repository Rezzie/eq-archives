1) Group roles:


Group leaders
Group leaders are the only group members who may talk in guild chat during the raid they do so giving mana reports, LD reports and relaying other group information. During the battle the group leader should know most of the time what his group members are doing so he can control the battle performance of his group to some degree. This includes telling the healers to heal when he/she thinks that is taking too long or telling the casters to help the healer or chanter out. A group leader should, during a battle, take a few seconds every now and then to assess the situation and make sure everyone is performing his/her duties to the best of their abilities. After a battle a group leader may evaluate the battle with the group and point out things that went well or things that went bad.

Group melee classes
Your main job is to trust your party members. You have to trust your puller to be on the right target you have to trust your healers to heal you in time, you have to trust your enchanter to mezz the mobs you are not fighting and you have to trust your casters to kill a mob once you have agro-ed it enough. You always assist the raid puller and never select a target of your own even when assist is taking a while to work. You never ever switch targets unless your puller does (hit assist once in every while to make sure you are still on the same target) or your group leader or the raid warlord says to switch targets to <insert mob name here>.

Group main healer
Their main job is to med uuuuh heal I mean. A group main healer should do nothing except heal and even med through most fights if no healing in the battle is required. Never heal too early except in emergencies because this will agro the mob(s) on you and throw the battle into disarray. Never heal too late either for obvious reasons. Preferably heal between fights or depending the situation start healing when someone goes below 40% health or when you see someone's health drop dramatically, you better watch those health bars till you have tears running down your cheeks. A main healer should only cast buffs when he/she is full mana or at least very high mana, depending the situation and the importance of the buff.

Group secondary healer
This is often a druid or a shaman and this role leaves a lot of room for the player to decide what course of action to take. They should buff their party members before a battle and they can melee and cast offensive spells in the beginning of a battle. Their main job is to leave at least 50% of their mana to heal party members when the main healer goes OOM. Furthermore its their job to keep the group main healer and enchanter alive by healing them or aggro-ing/killing the mob's) on them, depending the situation. This is the only valid reason for a secondary healer to agro a mob. In all other situations they should have their hands free to cast heals. If you agro the mob you were assisting on YOU ARE DOING SOMETHING WRONG.

Group casters
These are wizards, necromancer and magician types. Magicians and necro's can send their pets in the moment they are sure they have the right target (by assisting the puller of course). Furthermore it is best to look at yourself as a finisher as soon as the mob drops below (approx) two third of its health you kill with some big damage. Of course this doesn't work in every situation and good judgment is needed to know when to cast what. Furthermore your job is to protect the group healer and enchanter. If the group main healer is under attack but the chanter still has his/her hands free leave it to the chanter and keep assisting the puller. It is often hard to know when to help the enchanter, first look at his/her health bar if it is getting low fast you better start wasting mana big time to pull the mobs off the chanter. Only when helping the group main healer and enchanter do these caster classes have a valid reason to agro a mob. If you agro the mob you were assisting on YOU ARE DOING SOMETHING WRONG.

2) Class roles:

Bard
Bards are a bit hard to class but are still a melee class in my book. They truly excel in reducing a group downtime. They are hard to play because their group buffs tend to agro mobs a lot. So depending the situation a bard should probably decide to melee or play group buff songs and not do both.

Cleric
If present he/she becomes the group main healer because that is what they do best. If mana permits they also have some of the best defense buffs going, but they should never be used at the expense of healing. This guy is literally a life saver, be nice to them.

Druid
Can pose as main healer but their preferred role is secondary healer. They can also melee and have excellent snares to keep the mobs where you want them.

Enchanter
Well I am sure whole books can be written about enchanters but basically they do crowd control. They are one of the reasons why it is very important everybody uses assist. 

Enchanter should only speed buff people when they are full mana or at least very high mana. Breeze and Clarity should be cast and maintained on all group casters and primary and secondary healer and if mana permits on the hybrid melee classes. Preferably an enchanter casts mezz if multiple mobs were pulled then meds, casts mezz again, meds, mezz ad infinitum.

Magician
Caster class, with a pet. this pet can be a great asset to the group BUT it can also kill you if you don't watch it very careful. the first thing you should do is make 4 fast keys for  /pet back off,  /pet kill, /pet as you where and last but not least /assist, the assist key is actually something that everyone should have, this key should also be edited on every raid so that you have one /assist <Name of the main puller> and one /assist without a name to use on any other that you need to assist.

And as a magician and Necromancer you should sent in the pet in an early stage and always keep an eye on him, if you are in a dungeon also always have Invisible memmed so that you can kill the pet if it runs of and gets out of your range. The pet can also be used to help you escape if everything goes really bad, Just let the pet taunt the Mob and make sure everyone else is on their way out of there, then you just gate out and let the pet hold the mob of you, remember the pet is expendable.

And now a little thing about pets.
Fire: Weak pet with good fire dmg shield, best used in a big group where the pet take none to just a little damage.
Water: a good all-round pet, to use when working together with a Necro or anyone else that can slow down and fear a mob. also best used when the pet isn't the main tank. (yes you can use pets as main tanks.)
Earth: The best Solo pet but also good in group where you don't have anyone else that can slow down or root the mob, Don't use this pet when kiting a mob together with a Necro or druid the pet root will destroy the fear and make it harder for you to kill the mob. a very good pet to use when pet is one of the main tank.
Air: the best indoor pet when working your way through a dungeon cause to its ability to invis and not draw attention to itself, also a good pet when fighting casters because it will stun the caster.

Monk
Melee class with the Feign Death ability, preferred puller.  They deal an excellent amount of damage so should be in the front line to bring the mobs down fast.

Necromancer
These guys are masters of death, they are very powerful if played properly. They have strong pets and a range of spells that can help any group. They can use direct damage, dots and can even give the healers or casters extra mana if required. Same rules for the pets apply here as with the magicians, except there is no choice of pets. 

Paladin
Defensive tank. this basically means that they will be in the frontline dealing damage. They do not do as much damage as the warriors but have some magic abilities to make up for it.

The Paladin should be constantly taunting the enemy, not only with taunt but also with stuns and flash of light (care should be taken with FOL at low levels as it can cause the mob to run). in between battles they should be 'top-up' healing his party to let the healers regain as much mana as possible.

The paladin should also be ready to use his Lay On Hands ability to completely heal one of his party if things go really badly. Experience will tell you who to save, but its different in each situation. only use in emergencies.

Ranger
The ranger is a mix of druid and warrior. They are the masters of archery and can cause a good deal of damage before the mob even get to you, they are often used to pull the mobs because of this. like the paladin they can melee very well although not as well as the warriors. Again the ranger should me in the front line causing pain.

Rogue
Rogues are capable of dealing extraordinary amounts of damage with their backstab. However the whole group needs to understand how to fight with them. The Rogue should always be behind the mob and often needs to run about to be there. The melee guys should make sure that they are positioned in a way that makes it easy for the rogue to get behind the enemy.  The melees should constantly taunt to keep the mob off the rogue so that he may do his work.

Rogue can also apply poisons to the mobs which can be a great help during the battle.

Since they have the ability to hide/sneak around they are often called on to scout an area before entering or to perform corpse retrievals when things go wrong.

Shadowknight
The anti paladin. they can deal excellent damage and also have some of the necromancers spells to make the mobs short life even more unpleasant. they should work in a similar way to paladins in attacking the mob and using every trick in the book to keep the mob hitting them. 

They also have the ability to summon a pet to help them, however its rare that you find a Shadowknight that can keep his pet under control AND melee effectively, its just too much to do at once. Not so much of a problem in open areas but in tight dungeons a uncontrolled pet can be deadly.

Shaman
Can pose as main healer but their preferred role is secondary healer. They also slow the mobs down, buff the melees and de-buff the enemy. they have some powerful DOT spells at higher levels, and can give increases to every player stat there is. in short a damn useful person to be in the group.

Warrior
The warriors job is simple. Hit the mobs and hit them hard. They should constantly taunt the mobs to keep their attention. they can give out and take massive amounts of damage. 

Wizard
No-one does higher magic based damage than these guys. they should cast damage shields on the melee and nuke the mobs WHEN THE TIME IS RIGHT!. only experience will tell you the right time, but a good guide is when the mob is just over half its health points. If the wizard or any other caster agro the mob too early its a hard job for the melee guys to get it off of you.

3) Group composition:

Preferably a group should have at least two melee classes and one (main) healer. The remaining three spots
can be filled by:
one more melee class.
one enchanter
one secondary healer
one or two from wizard, necromancer, magician.

These are not hard and fast. clever players will learn each others abilities and create strategies to dealt with mobs no matter which classes are in the group. The main thing to remember is that every class has something to offer some combinations are very easy, others take a lot more work, but never be afraid to experiment with new combinations and different ways of tackling the enemy.
