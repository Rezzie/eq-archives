Section 1: General Message Board Guidelines & Questions



  1. What is Registration?
    The administrator has probably required you to register before you can post replies or new threads on the board. Registration also allows you to edit your posts, specify a signature, receive email notification of replies to your threads, and much more. Registration is free, and there is no need to enter personal details. Your email address can be optionally hidden, ensuring complete privacy. The administrator may have chosen to verify all email addresses during registration, so make certain that you have entered a correct email address.

    If you are a child under the age of 13, the administrator may require you to provide parental consent before you can begin posting on the board. More information about this is available during the registration process.


    2.
  2. Can I use HTML Code in posts?
    You may be able to use HTML code in your posts, depending on the administrator's choice. Details of whether you are allowed to or not are available in the Forum Rules, found on most pages within the Forum. If you are allowed to use HTML code, make sure that you close all the tags that you have opened, and don't close any tags that you have not opened - doing so will make sure that the design of the pages is not disrupted.

    As an alternative to HTML code, the administrator may have enabled vB code. This provides a sub set of the functionality of HTML code, while maintaining security from malicious scripting and layout damage. More information about vB code can be found here.


    3.
  3. What Are Moderators?
    Moderators control individual forums. They have the power to edit, delete, or move any posts in their forums. If you are unhappy about a particular forum, you should contact the moderator with the problem.


    4.
  4. How can I change my profile information?
    It is up to you to maintain an up to date profile. You should make sure, in particular, that your email address is up to date. You can change any fields in your profile, except for your username. Once you have a username, it is yours for life. Information about the various options that you have in your profile are available on this page.


    5.
  5. Are cookies used?
    vBulletin uses cookies to store your unique user id number and your password if you are a registered member. If you have chosen in your profile, this will automatically fill in your username and password in any place where you need them. Cookies are used to track when you are online - if you have not chosen to be in privacy mode in your profile, your username will appear in the logged in users list. Lastly, cookies are used to track which posts are new since your last visit. It is recommend that you use these forums with a cookie-enabled browser. If you do not, you will have to type in your username and password every time you want to post, and every time you visit a private forum.

    You may delete all cookies set in this forum by clicking on the log out link on the homepage.


    6.
  6. Can I edit my own posts?
    Only registered users can edit and delete their posts. The administrator can turn off this priviledge is he desires. You may also be able to edit your post for a certain length of time after you have posted it, depending on how the administrator have set up the board. To edit or delete your posts, just click the edit/delete icon by your post. If your post was the first in the thread, then deleting your post will result in the removal of the whole thread.

    When you make modifications to your posts, a note will appear at the bottom of the post telling other users that you have edited your post. Moderators and administrators can also edit your messages but this note will not appear.


    7.
  7. What is the signature for?
    Signatures are only available to registered users. If this board's administrator has enabled signatures, then you can specify in your profile a signature to use at the bottom of your posts. When you post, there is an option to use your signature. If you want to turn the signature off in the future, you can come back and edit your post. If you have specified a signature in your profile, the box will be automatically ticked for you.

    In the upper left hand corner of the message board, there is an icon marked 'Profile'. Click there. You will be asked for your user name and password. Upon entering these, click 'Submit Now' and you will be taken to the profile update page. There is a box about halfway down the page where you can input your signature. When liking graphics, please keep other peoples' connection speeds in mind, and limit the size of your sig graphic to 250x100 pixels and less thank 25K in size.


    8.
  8. Why does my sig show up with so many blank lines (in front of it)?
    Perhaps it is because you used HTML, and included extra white space for readability. Because of the way the board processes in messages for readability, the any carriage returns are converted into the BR tag. This will create extra blank lines in your sig. Due to the way that tables are interpreted by various browsers, these converted BR tags may be placed before the table... This is why blank lines would appear before your sig.


    9.
  9. How do I Search for Posts?
    You can search for posts based on username, word(s) in the post or just in the subject, by date, and only in particular forums. To access the search feature, click on the "search" link at the top of most pages. You can search any forum that you have permission to search - you will not be allowed to search through private forums unless the administrator has given your the necessary security rights to do so.


    10.
  10. What are announcements?
    Announcements are made by the administrator, and are meant to be a simple one-way communication with the users. If you wish to discuss announcements, you will have to create a new thread in the forum, since you cannot reply to announcements.


    11.
  11. Are there any censor features? Why do my posts have words converted to asterisks (*)?
    If the administrator has chosen censor your posts based on certain words, these words will be blanked out with stars (*) or a similar character in your posts. This censoring is done by a computer simply searching for words, and is by no stretch of the imagination 'intelligent'.


    12.
  12. What do I do if I have lost my username and/or password?
    If you happen to forget your password, click on the 'Lost Password' link available everywhere you are required to fill in you password. This will give you a form where you can fill in your registered email address, and your password will be emailed to you straight away.


    13.
  13. What is the email notification options?
    If you make a post in a thread, you can choose to receive email notifications when new replies are made. This option is set when you make a post and can be turned off by by editing your post. Registered users only are applicable to receive email notification and they can set in their profile whether they want email notification on or off by default.


    14.
  14. What are messsage icons?
    If the administrator has enabled message icons, you can specify a graphical icon to accompany your post. This can be used to convey emotion or the idea of the post. If you do not see the option to specify an icon when posting, the administrator has probably disabled this option.


    15.
  15. I have a question. Does it matter which forum I post in?
    If you have a question, you are strongly encouraged to use the search function first. Your question might already have an answer just waiting for you to discover. Also, please make all postings in the most appropriate forums. For example, postings about out of game stuff belong in the "Real Life" Forum.


    16.
  16. Is proper spelling and grammer required?
    Proper spelling and grammer are important, but not required. Keep in mind that if people cannot understand you, they probably will not reply. Try not to use "dood speak," especially when when asking questions. This includes things like: plz, pls, u, r, etc. If you can't take the time to type out a question, then why should we take the time to type out an answer. Do not, under any circumstance, use CAPS in the title of your thread. This makes it hard for people to read what you have to say and you are less likely to get a response.


    17.
  17. I want to post, but I'm intimidated by all these outgoing personalities! What do I do?
    Don't be afraid to post. We won't eat you.


    18.
  18. Rangers Suck! I'm gonna switch classes, what should I play?
    We do NOT suck. If something is causing you difficulty, perhaps you might want to start a thread on the topic of your problem. Or, perhaps the ranger class just isn't for you. You should try several different race/class combos, and eventually you'll settle on something you like. Also, if you are coming here to say that you think that the ranger class sucks, do not expect to receive a favorable response or to be liked.


    19.
  19. I am not a Ranger. Can I post?
    All classes are welcome to post here. Keep in mind though that this is a ranger-related message board.


    20.
  20. Can I use pictures in my posting?
    Usages of pictures embedded is acceptable, but in the interest of those with slow connections, you might want to post a link to your picture instead. Instructions for linking to other images or web pages can be found here: http://forums.interealms.com/ranger/index.php3?action=bbcode.


    21.
  21. Someone is spamming the boards with rude comments and trying to incite an e-riot! What do I do?
    Don't feed the trolls. Leave 'em alone, and they'll go away...eventually. Additionally when posting, make sure you try to have some content the majority of the time. Never assume you don't have any ideas to add. Lurking may be common, but it's not something which helps out the community as a whole. One good comment a month is better than twenty useless ones a day.


    22.
  22. Am I allowed to swear on the boards?
    While strong language is sometimes useful in illustrating a point or communicating an emotion, please attempt to keep it to a minimum. Your fellow gladesmen will thank you. Respect your fellow rangers. Heated arguments are ok, but do not use swearing or name calling. Reasonable /rude Verant comments are acceptable.


    23.
  23. What are all these 'titles' everyone's going on about?
    Look in the upper left corner of your next post, right below your member ID. You will have a title (Lurker, Commoner, Veteran, Teacher, etc). Certain people have, through one thing or another, come to the Glademaster's attention. They have been given special titles appropriate to reputations.


    24.
  24. How do I earn a title?
    Be outstanding. Be fun. Be memorable. DON'T ASK FOR IT. Be helpful. Be nice to people. You may or may not get a title, but people sure will like you better. Do not ask for a special title. Most likely Sunmoon will not give you the one you want. If he does give you a special title when you ask for it, most likely you will not like the title that you receive. If you are deemed worthy of a special title, one will be given to you at the discretion of Sunmoon.


    25.
  25. What smileys can I use in my postings?
    For a list of the currently supported smileys, check the following URL: http://forums.interealms.com/ranger/index.php3?action=showsmilies


    26.
  26. What does "bump" mean?
    "Bump" is generally used in the phrase "bump the thread". It is a practice to keep a thread on the top of the forum. Its side effect is to increase your thread count and power level on the Glade website by posting for posting's sake. It is not encouraged but not forbidden either. Use it wisely and sparingly.


    27.
  27. I tried to post a message in the "Humor" forum and get a "Sorry! This forum is not open for new posts." message. How can I post to this forum?
    This is a read-only archive forum. Most of the message here originated in one of the other forums. Just make the posting in whichever of the other forums you feel is the most appropriate.

  28. VI periodically Nerf's classes in EQ, is this true on the messageboards also?
    Yes, Fortunately there are astute rangers that patrol the boards, who keep Sunmoon in line and the other rangers aware of any possible Nerf's to the Rangers Glade.

    3.
  3. Why are the epic weapons sometimes refered as being FAs?
    "FA" is an Acronym for Fiery Avenger. It was the first epic style quest and the reward is a paladin-only sword with a particle effect. When the epic quests [for all classes] were being added, Verant stated that they were giving all classes Fiery Avenger style weapons meaning that all classes would have weapons with particle effects. Since the names of the weapons were unknown originally, they were called FAs for a lack of a better name.

  6. Where can I obtain the Abandoned Orc Shovel?
    The Orc Shovel is lying on the ground in a tent at the Abandoned Orc Spawn in Lake Rathe. You can just go pick it up from the ground. Its spawn rate is not clear at this point. Its place holder is "a Dirty Hoe." Since this is a very fast 2-Handed Blunt Weapon, it is a good weapon to use to build and maintain the skill at early levels.

  12. How do I roleplay my Ranger?
    You can roleplay your Ranger however you want based on your understanding of what a Ranger is. There are a lot of model Rangers in literature and history. However, our advise is to first of all be a Ranger at heart and be proud of your Ranger. Secondly, there are certain items, places, and characters in the game that have been vastly used as symbols of the Ranger class. These include: The Surefall Glade Ranger Guild, The Goddess of Tunare, Elven Wine from Kelethin, quivers, bows, Master Kithicor, Master Sureshot, Morin Shadowbane, etc. Thirdly, a Ranger generally has the following traits: master of archery, child of the woods, protector of the nature, and an agile fighter. One of the purpose of this website, is help you understand your Ranger and play your Ranger well. Please use the many forums here to learn all about your Ranger.

  14. What is consider and what is it used for?
    Consider [or con for short] is a way to determine how you are liked by an opponent and what is your power in relation to his/hers. There are the following 2 sets of results of "con":
    Relativity of 'toughness':

    • red: the target is much higher level than you and you will die if attempt to fight it.
    • yellow: the target is 1 or two levels higher than you and you will die if you attempt to fight it unless you are a Warrior and you are level 1.
    • white (or black in window mode): the target is the same as you. You may still die if you attempt to fight it unless you are a level 50- druid.
    • blue: the opponent is lower level than you. Hmm well you might win.
    • green: the target is much lower level than you. You will win. But it's still hard to say if you are lvl 50+ and fighting more than 2 of them.

      How well-liked are you:

      • warmly: you are very well-liked

        •
      • kindly: you are quite well-liked

        •
      • amiably: you are well-liked

        •
      • indifferently: you are not liked nor disliked

        •
      • apprehensively: you are not liked

        •
      • dubiously: you are very disliked but not enough to be hated yet

        •
      • threateningly: you are hated and therefore kill on sight ["KOS"]

The second number represents the x axis, i.e., East - West: the bigger the number, the more west (SURPRISE!) it is. The third number represents z axis or the depth or the height of the position. Using loc to navigate is a must-have ability, especially for Rangers. It does take a lot of practices though.
