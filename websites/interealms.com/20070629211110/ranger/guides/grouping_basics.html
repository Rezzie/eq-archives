Grouping Basics

Night falls in Surefall Glade. You walk into the cabin on the lake, seeking some sage advice. A lone half-elven ranger rests at a table, drinking some ale. He nods and waves you over. "Greetings, I am Baratol Alaryn," he says. "So you want to know the basics of grouping, eh?" He stares over at the wall a moment, then looks back at you. "Well, I've done quite a bit of travelling so far. Parties are the only way to get by in this hostile world. But, it's a long speech, and my mug is dry." He grins slyly.

Before you can learn how to be effective in a group, you must know enough about yourself. As a ranger, you have the potential to cause a fair amount of damage to an opponent with your weapons alone. Not quite as much as a warrior, but close. With dual wield, you can surpass paladins and shadow knights as well. However, melee combat is not your only specialty, as you also have the ability to cast minor spells. These grow in power as you progress, but of course, never coming close to the prowess of a true spellcaster. Nonetheless, spells are a useful tool to have. Early on, you match the other fighter-types in armor, but eventually, they will pass by you in that aspect, for we are restricted in our choices of armor. With the skills of Dodge and Parry, we can still avoid damage, but just not quite as well.

Of course, all this knowledge is important for being in parties. First, you will have to meld your techniques into the dynamics of grouping effectively. By knowing your strengths and weaknesses, you can do so quite efficiently. First, you will be expected to stand on the front lines and dish out pain with your blades. That is to be expected. Also, you will be expected to soak up the return blows from your enemies. If you are the only melee-oriented member of your party, trust the healer (hopefully there is one) and stay there unless told to back off. However, if there is another melee-type, such as a warrior, your best bet is to be a trouble-shooter. Rangers are rather effective taunters, especially when you gain the ability to wield two weapons at once. Engage in melee combat as normal, however, keep aware. As soon as you see that a spellslinger is being attacked, go to the rescue. With two swords flashing, and a stream of colorful invective(Taunt), it should be easy to enrage the offending opponent into attacking you rather than the finger-waggler. Who, hopefully, should start casting spells and killing the opponent you now face. Otherwise, you may be placed in the unenviable position of being attacked from both front and back.

Sometimes, you may find yourself in a group consisting of all melee combatants. In this case, you will be one of the healers, assuming you have reached a sufficient rank in your training. Your spell may not be very potent, but it is better than nothing, after all. When you see a fellow party member is taking damage in large quantities, back off for a moment and cast a healing spell or two. Also remember to cast your Skin Like ---- spell on your allies whenever they need it. It will greatly enhance the fighting abilities of your group. Also, if you are the one taking the heat, back away and hope your opponent does not follow. Cast some healing spells, and back in the fray, if you can. If you are unable to heal yourself sufficiently, feel free to contribute a bit of damage with your direct damage spells. If you lack mana, use your bow. It may be slow, and not exactly the most powerful of weapons, but it will contribute some damage to the battle. You may even critically injure an opponent with a well-placed arrow.

"These are merely basic concepts to grouping, but nonetheless, are important. Keep them in mind, and you should do well." The wiry half-elf stands up and stretches. "Ahh.Well, best of luck. And if ye see any gnolls, give them a taste of steel, would ye?" He smiles ferally, then gathers his equipment and quietly exits the cabin.
