But Toorac noticed a strange look pass on the first guards face, he recognized the sideeffect of the spell, "De'ja vu", the thought that one had been here or done this before. Toorac grinned allowing the guards to think it was because he had won the discussion when in fact he was pleased the spells held so solid.
Once inside the house Toorac drew on a map that he had commited to memory before leaving erudin, and remembered he found it almost childs play to memorize next to the complicated spells stored in that same mind. Toorac stood in the greeting hall, once again the family crest resounded thruout the room on banners, shields and even carved into the marble walls itself. The ceiling vaulted at least 40 feet into citiadellike arches, and the floor was covered in a fine wooven carpet depicting larger than life the master of the house enganged in a battle with a griffion. Before him was a great oaken fireplace and upon its majestic mantle sat a shiny brass shield with the family crest emblazed upon it. Hanging on the wall just above the shield was a jewel encrusted bastard sword like the one in the wooven picture. To his right two sets of stairs lead to a small balcony overlooking the hall, this Toorac knew was where the lady of the house would sit and watch over the master of the house as he greeted his guests and discussed his buisness,		   a tradition that had gained favor among the low humans over the last few years. Below that balcony sat twin oaken doors braced by rune covered iron strips. Remebering the map he knew this would lead to the house proper, the living quarters of the master and lady of the house. But they where not why he was here, to the left of the fireplace sat a undecorated wooden door. Toorac smiled, the entrance to the house major where all others residing within these walls lived.
Toorac moved at a swift but nonchalant pace toward the wooden door, this was the most dangerous part of his mission if he passed someone who had seen the old wizard pass moments before they may cast enough doubt to see through his illusion spell. His sources did tell him that after a evening visiting his guild the hired house wizard would normally head straight to his labratory to work on spells he had learnt that night. He passed through the door and entered a long narrow hallway decorated with the usual family portraits, each depicted a member alone or with others. Toorac stopped at one such portrait, upon it in vivid colors, obviously painted by a master, was a fairhaired girl in a flowing gown and at her feet a young boy dressed in fine black silk.
