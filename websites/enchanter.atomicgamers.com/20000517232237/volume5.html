And the race is on!

This is a short look into one of the races that may study the grand art of Enchantments.

today's race High Elf:
The High Elf city of Felwithe is possible the most organized city in the game once you have been through it once its hard to get lost. This city holds only a few of the guilds of Norrath mainly because of the lack of classes that the high elf has access to.

High elves share their newbie area with the Wood elves making their newbie zone one of the most crowded, but it seems that these elves have adapted well and the amount of infighting is relatively low. High elves also start with infravision which can be most beneficial in the night.

Combat Strategies

If you have a tested tactic or even a suggested tactic pleas send them in by filling out the Tactics submission form.

character name: Draconal
Server: Cazic-Thule
Level tactic good for: 12th to 15th

Spells used: Mesmerize, Tashan, languid pace, root, chaotic feedback, choke

Tactic Description: I used this little spell routine to get myself from 12th to 15th in Oassis in a matter of days soloing. First cast Mesmerize (the mob will now not attack unless it is hit with a damage spell meaning up until the first cast of chaotic feedback any resists can be recast) then cast tashans to lower the mobs resistance then languid pace (just in case root doesn't hold) then root, now once the mob is fixed in place cast chaotic feedback (mainly because its regen time is slow then cast Choke then chaotic feedback should be back up cast it once more then recast root and redo your damage spells. Just a note also if you are low on mana and the mob is high on Hps still wait until your choke dot runs out and cast mesmerize on the mob and walk away (note if your dot is still active it will break the mesmerize and send the mob after you again.) This kind of routine can be used into the higher levels just find your favorite debuffs cast mesmerize before the debuffs and then root and shoot.

Trade Skills and their shortfalls

First of all before I give my opinion on the shortfalls of the trades I would like to say that Verant has done an awesome job on adding more to and responding to the tradesmiths.
The most important shortfall that needs attention is the lack of degradation to items in everquest. This has spurred a problem with item overpopulation on old servers . Example is if one BBC (Bone Bladed Claymore) is looted every 6 hours then in one month the server sees 120 of these appear now in a normal world this would be fine as many of those would dull or break reducing the amount available, unfortunately EQ is missing this function so over 8 months there is now 960 of these swords sitting on the server they are no longer special as verant wished them to be and are very easy to come by for cheap. Why I use this example is if, due to overpopulation of BBCs, a tradesmith makes forged two handed swords and attempts to sell them for 30 plat with the stats of 12/45(i don't know the actual stats at the moment) non magical and a person is selling the BBC 17/50(or so) magical for 40 to 50 plat the tradesmith will lose out. This is also becoming an issue with magic jewelry there is an unbelievable amount of Silver and Electrum enchanted jewelry on each server. People are now able to use jewelry that was created and bought in the early days (after jewelry patch) and either hand it down to a new character, so we lose a customer, or sell it at a super competitive price, again losing a customer, and the problem is that that piece of jewelry is just as good after 5 months as it was the day it was made. Now in real life I would love to buy a pair of pants have them last for 10 years and then sell them to my friend for the same amount as I bought them but it doesn't happen. When you wear something it gets worn out, even if taken care of.
Now I have talked with other Tradesmiths about this and no player enjoys the idea of having their equipment degrade but sooner or later the servers are going to be way to overpopulated with magic items (I laughed cause its akin to my first D and D game where my draconal had 20 +1 short swords and 10 wands of magic missiles and alot more due to ease of collection).
The solution is simple to say but I fear alot harder for them to code (and as a hobby programmer I understand how hard it is to add code if you didn't think of it in the core coding) but items must begin to degrade. To make others happy in the game you could make it so that there is money sinks in the form of NPC smiths that can repair degrading items, I believe this would solve many problems.
First it would solve tweaking because noone would waste a expensive to repair sword on a newbie char and if they do you can make the degradation based on calculations of the offensive skill (I mean if you know how to use a weapon you will do less damage to it and you will be able to take better care of it). Now without knowing the code limitations (such as if each item is a separate entity in the game or just a reference to a database line of stats) I think items such as weapons should degrade in speed and damage and items such as armor and bonus armor should have a wear and tear on them degrading them in either AC or their bonuses. As a idea that may be useable you could make such things as sharpening stones and maybe patch kits for armor time used on a base like food and water.
Second it would revive the dying Tradesmith community. At the moment I am seeing more and more jewelers on my own server and they are driving the prices to a point where, with failures, I often lose money on a sale (even on very trivial items with a 100+ wisdom) also I have seen the amount of blacksmiths selling their wares drop to maybe one or two at the bazaar because they cannot get past the overpopulation of bronze and pre made banded armor. It has made it so the camping and selling looted items is not only more profitable but less likely to drain your money than making items by trades. Now competition is healthy but I cant see it as good if most all of us are suffering not because of competition against each other but instead against or own wares made two months ago real time (that's a long time in game hehe).
Third it would bring another level of challenge to the day to day survival of a character in the world of norrath if you have to care for you armor and weapons as well as you Character himself.

Oh and a minor idea also, how about replacing sense magic with recharge item so we can gain charges back on a item. Make this spell very slow casting and require a component also make it a mana hog. This is so a Stein of mogguk can not be used as a replacement to a healer. Just a thought :).

Draconal, Guardian of the Library

Prelude part 3

Toorac continued down the hall until he came to a T, he knew to the left would be the kitchen and servants quarters, but with the time on his illusion drawing thin he could afford no more delays. Toorac quickly cast a glance over his shoulder to verify that the old man was not within sight, he was not. He chuckled to himself even if the old man had been in the hall Toorac doubted if he would be able to see far enough to tell which direction he had gone. Toorac turned his attention to the right hall, this hall would take him to the house secondary, the living quarters of the Nanny and the Lord and Lady's children.
He increased his pace less interested in the paintings on the wall and more interested in fighting the waves of tension griping him as he approached closer to the apex of his mission. Toorac came to a stop midway down the hall before him was three doors, two on the right and one on the left. He cursed under his breath as he read the words on each door, not only did he despise reading common but his contact had been wrong about the placement of the rooms. His contact had told him that both children where bedded within the right rooms and the nanny's room was on the left. This was incorrect, Toorac made a mental note to pay a visit to his informant and discuss this infraction with him. With the nanny's room being beside the targets room it increased his danger, he would have to act fast. hurriedly he checked the door, a small smile graced the wrinkled face, they had not expected visitors and so have not locked the children's doors. With a lithe move Toorac twisted the latch and slipped into the dark room closing the door behind him. His eyes slowly adjusted as best they could from the torch light to the darkness within the room. In the shadows he made out the shapes of a dresser, a wardrobe, and reflecting the meager amount of moonlight intruding through the window was a full length mirror. Finally in the farthest corner of the room Toorac spotted the canopied bed and could barely make out the lumps that would form a sleeping child. Now that Toorac had his target in sights his heart raced and his head began to ache a bit, but fighting back this sensation he calmed himself and began to speak the words of his next spell. Once again he whispered the words that seemed barely audible yet seemed to scream through ones brain, also his hands once again traced streams of dull light into the air before him forming the mystic runes of his spell.
"Wh... wh.. who is there" a small fearful voice chimed "Hello is someone there"
Toorac opened his eyes and before him sitting up on the bed was the Lord of the house's daughter, her face illuminated by the reflecting mirror, . Toorac nearly choked as he saw the innocent face, almost missing his final words of the enchantment. With his recovery and the final word spoken the runes floating before him came to life and raced the room toward the child. Before she could even gasp the runes raced around her head and sank inwards until they only seemed to exist swirling within her blank eyes upon an emotionless face. Toorac moved closer to the bed, the tension of the mission now drown in the sorrow of his next act. Reaching within his robes he pulled out a dagger raising it to the light he studied the symbol upon the hilt, the symbol of the Wizards of Qeynos guild. Nodding to himself sure that all was as planned he steeled himself for his final act. Toorac placed the dagger above the girl's heart "Forgive me but your soul is needed elsewhere and your death will stop those fools that seek to release dread upon our world, you will not feel it I swear" and he thrust the dagger into the child's chest piercing the heart. As he watched the life drained from her eyes Toorac was forced to fight back a tear. "Forgive me child, but your death brings new hope to many" He spoke silently to the child as if she would answer. Toorac regained his senses slowly realizing he was not yet out of danger. Standing he spoke the enchanted words again and carved the runes before him. Once complete the runes swelled and swirled about him and within a second collapsed in on him, his form vanished, darkness and silence once again claimed the room.
