The Guide to Practice Runes

 

 

Note : For information of where to obtain The Concordance of Research and the practice spell books see Equipment.

 

 

The making of Runes is a delicate matter. It is not just one of your researching games. While at first you might doubt the might of my arts, I tell you that a rune must have two separate parts. With apologies to the Master Cat Bard.

 

Practice Runes, not to be confused with Wizard Research Runes, are used by Magi who wish to gain skill in research without the pressure of practicing on real research components.

 

There are eight types of practice runes, and two runes of each type - Azia and Beza. Azia and Beza runes can be constructed by combining specific components in a Concordance of Research. There is no skill required for this - the Magic of the Concordance is responsible for the transmutation of the components into Azia and Beza Runes.

 

Once the Azia and Beza Runes have been acquired, the Magi may practice his/her research by combining these Runes in his/her appropriate research book - a Lexicon for a Wizard, and Book of Dark Bindings for a Necromancer, etc.

 

However once Magicians have complete this research the resulting Runes are useless to us. You can either sell them to the Merchants to recover some of the cost of Research or pass them on to a Wizard.

 

All Runes require a Quill and a piece of Parchment, as well as other specified components.

Practice Rune Beza : Turquoise, Fire Beetle Eye, Any Rusty Slashing Weapon
