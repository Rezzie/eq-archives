Week Ending 23rd April

 

 

Date :    24/04/2000

 

Topic :   Arrival at Kunark

 

Firiona Vie, Sionachie, Thubr Axebringer, Sir Jevik Isqual, Al`Kabor, Dreezil Pocketdip, Dagda Icefury, Ognit Eznertob, Dabner Drednever, and Lyirae Oakwynd arrived at the tiny Elven stronghold in the new lands. Tired from their journey, they stepped off of the Maiden's Voyage and began to rest for a few moments. Galeth Veredeth came to greet them as they were resting.

Galeth Veredeth shouts "Lieutenant Thorn! Please do me the honor of taking our friends on a tour of the surrounding area"

The Lieutenant approached the group and began to lead them out of town. As they were walking along, the Lieutenant made an effort to point out the creatures and structures to Firiona and her companions. He informed them that the Iksar are an ancient race. The ruins that lie all about the land show that the Iksar were a great empire whose influence seemed to stretch the length of the continent. 1900 years ago, the Iksar attempted to attack and take control of Faydwer but the navy was wiped out by a terrible storm and they were not successful. Something terrible happened to cause the once great empire to crumble. None of the local inhabitants of the area seem very friendly with the exception being the Drixies.

As the party began to walk back, Danak Dhorentath, an Iksar shaman shouted "Visitors! Watch your backs, the Drolvarg are attacking!" At that moment, four Drolvarg appeared and ambushed the group. Danak and an Iksar Initiate joined the group in fighting the Drolvarg. After the Drolvarg were dead, Danak asked if everyone was alright.

While Firiona was explaining to Danak that they were seeking out the Ring of Scale, he seemed generally concerned for their safety. He told them that their journey would be incredibly long and dangerous. Danak offered a quicker means for them to get there. He offered the use of his special portal. Within a few moments, with serpentine speed, Danak Dhorentath grabbed Firiona Vie by the arm. Taking her by surprise he pulled her into the gate, which disappeared with a hiss leaving nothing more than the smell of ozone hanging in the air.

"I must return and tell King Thex the terrible news. Bind the vile Iksar at once! Dagda, Thubr, Jevik and I will escort the prisoner back to the mainland!" Galeth Veredeth shouted.

They escorted the Initiate to the Maiden's Voyage and traveled to Faydwer.

April 24th, 6:00am

***Press Release***

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

LEAGUE OF ANTONICAN BARDS ANNOUNCE NEW MAIL ROUTE

FIRIONA VIE, Kunark. February 1, 3178--

Today, the League of Antonican Bards, responsible for maintaining communication across Norrath announced that they have established a mail route to the newly discovered continent of Kunark, located across the ocean, south and east of Antonica. The new route will carry mail from Port Faydwer to the Elven outpost of Firiona Vie, which will serve as main port-of-entry and battle staging grounds for adventurers arriving in the new lands.

The outpost of Firiona Vie was established soon after word reached King Tearis Thex, King of Felwithe and leader of the Koada`Dal, that his daughter of the same name had been kidnapped by lizard creatures now known to be named the Iksar. The King commissioned Galeth Veredeth, once-Lord and High Paladin of Tunare, to establish the outpost on Kunark and return his daughter to him, lest Veredeth's life be forfeited for failure. "I cannot allow those vile creatures to keep my daughter and only heir to the Koada`Dal throne from fulfilling her destiny," says King Tearis Thex. "Galeth Veredeth had best not fail me."

A high level source in Neriak confirmed today that the Dark races will not be outdone in the endeavor to acquire new territory and power. The source said, "We believe that this nonsense about a kidnapped princess is just that. If the lesser `Dals and their human and stunted playthings think that they can wander the new lands unchecked, they are quite mistaken."

Many adventures are already beginning the trip to Kunark, a journey that is not without its perils. A human and halfling party was apparently slaughtered upon their arrival after mistakenly boarding a Kunark-destined ship in the Oasis of Marr. They evidently were not aware that the ship would arrive at a new port, firmly controlled by Neriak and their brutish muscle from Oggok and Grobb.

Druidic scouts have reported that there are some strange phenomena present throughout the new lands. "The land appears to have been ravaged by some viscous magical storms at one time," said one follower of Karana. "Some magics that we have come to count on just do not work." This phenomenon was apparently noticed to an extreme in the vicinity of a mountain peak rumored to be inhabited by followers of Veeshan. "Only through divine intervention did I escape that place with my life."

The League of Antonican Bards' representative in Kunark met with Galeth Veredeth, Governor of the Outpost Firiona Via. "It is my sincerest hope that many adventures loyal to the King will assist us on our Quest to return the Princess to her home," says Veredeth. "Though the dangers in these new lands are formidable, I have no doubt that many will come, if for nothing else than to reap the spoils."

*Kunark Goes Live*

In case the announcement from the League of Antonican Bards wasn't to your liking, we are very pleased to announce that the EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark expansion has been implemented on the live servers. Adventurers who have installed the expansion and registered their account key may now visit the new lands, exceed level 50, create Iksar characters, and access Kunark-related content in general.

Here is a short list of some of the new features available to those who have upgraded:

- Players may now reach level 60!
- Over two dozen new zones to explore!
- Tons of new quests, items, and spells to complete, acquire, and learn!
- All-new creatures and weapons, with new art too!

There are two minor issue that is known and that we are still in the process of rectifying: Action-based sounds for the Iksar and some other Kunark creatures are still being worked on. In other words, you might hear a giant mosquito "walk" by you, and your Iksar might sound curiously like a human when he is hit with a sword. In addition, there is some additional work required with a couple of the starting Iksar quests. This may cause you to reach a point where you cannot proceed further in a quest. This will not however cause you to turn in items and be denied your reward, so feel free to explore these new quests. We will be finishing them over the next week.

We would like to offer special thanks everyone who has been patient over the past couple of weeks while we've readied EverQuest for this awesome event. It has been quite a challenge for our team to get everything ready for launch, and your support has been instrumental in this process. We'd also like to thank the beta test team, who in the face of all of the new content managed to keep their thoughts between themselves and the Dev Team, while working to make the game better for everyone.

If you ordered Kunark on or before 4/19/2000 and do not receive your Kunark CD by today, we will be posting instructions later today on how to claim the shipping refund as well as the free month of EverQuest promised last week.

Thank you,

The EverQuest Team

Topic :   The Story Leading to the Discovery of Kunark Heats up

 

As the voices boom from the city of Kelethin in a glorious cheer of celebration, Lorisyn Oakwynd shouts "Brothers and Sisters of the Forest! Come, join the rest of your nation on this day. Today, the battle rests, and the Festival of Faydark's Children shall be held!" Elves begin to emerge from their huts atop the Tree City of Kelethin, and from the small valley leading into the Butcherblock Mountains, a small band of dwarves marches into the forest. A mule pulling a wagon carrying three large
barrels, each the height of an elf and the width of a dwarf, follows closely behind the dwarves.

King Tearis Thex, the high Elven king, welcomes everyone to the celebration.

"During this past year the Mother has shown much generosity and to our people. The restoration of my daughter and your princess, Firiona Vie, has been only the crown of the Mother's gifts to us this year. To all of you, I give my best wishes and though I am your King, it is not necessarily I who has brought you here today. It is your hearts, your spirits. the very
things that make you of the Fier`Dal and Koada`Dal nations, that have brought you here. To you, I raise my glass. May your blades prove strong, your bows aim true, your hearts remain pure and The Mother, may she bless you with every year being twice as good as the past. To old friends."

Firiona Vie, Thubr Axebringer, and Sionachie reunite with their friend from long ago, Lorisyn Oakwynd. They also receive the wonderful news of the existence of Lorisyn's twin sister, Lyirae Oakwynd. They all take part in the celebration of the Faydark.

The moment the King retired back to Felwithe, a band of villains disrupted the celebration. Ghargin Bumblok, an ogre shaman, and a troll warrior by the name of Rogkasth were led by Vahlai Ka`Izal from the Teir`Dal. These villains came to kill Lorisyn Oakwynd. Once Lorisyn was dead, they warned Firiona Vie that this was not the last they will see of them. Lyirae, vowed herself to take her brother's place by traveling with Firiona Vie and the others.

Firona Vie, Thubr Axebringer, and Sionachie journey by heading to Freeport to seek out the Erudite wizard and Human paladin.
