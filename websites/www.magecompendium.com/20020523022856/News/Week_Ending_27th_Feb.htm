Topic :   Fennin Ro Split

PLEASE READ THIS MESSAGE IN ITS ENTIRETY

We are in the process of finalizing plans to implement a split of the Fennin Ro server as announced a few weeks ago. At the time the split occurs, players from Fennin Ro will be moved to the new server on a voluntary basis. Current Fennin Ro players that wish to have all their characters moved from Fennin Ro to the Split Server will need to register for the move.

The registration log will be opened on Monday during prime-time hours. A server-wide Broadcast will be made on Fennin Ro to announce that the Move Registration Log is open. Furthermore, a patch message will be placed at that time to give the information to incoming players. PLEASE DO NOT ASK THE GM/GUIDE STAFF THE TIME THAT THE REGISTRATION LOG WILL OPEN. EXECUTING THE REGISTRATION COMMAND PRIOR TO THE TIME THAT REGISTRATION IS OFFICIALLY OPENED WILL HAVE NO EFFECT AND MAY IMPAIR YOUR ABILITY TO REGISTER AT THE PROPER TIME.

Once the registration log is open, and assuming you are a current FENNIN RO player, you may register for the move to the Split Server by logging into a character on Fennin Ro, and executing the command '/movelog yes' (without the quotes). YOU MAY NOT CANCEL THIS REGISTRATION AFTER IT HAS BEEN ISSUED.

PLEASE NOTE:

The physical move should occur on the following day (Tuesday). We will be moving people on a first-come-fist-serve basis, based upon the order that you executed the /movelog command. The number of people that will be moved is unknown at this time.

Moves will be made without regard to guild or group, meaning that should you choose to execute the /movelog command, you may be separated from your friends. In addition, your guild affiliations (guild-tags) will not carry over to the new server. You will need to establish new guilds when you arrive.

Some additional important notes:

  1. If you are selected for the move, all of your characters currently on Fennin Ro will be moved to the new server. Those characters will retain all of their equipment. We recommend that all characters settle any outstanding debts over the next few days.
  2. You will not receive any confirmation or feedback in regards to whether or not you have been moved. If you log on and find your characters missing from Fennin Ro, you will find them intact on the new server.
  3. CORPSES WILL NOT BE MOVED. If you have a corpse in the world, and you are moved, your corpse will not follow. Any equipment that was on the corpse will be lost and will not be subject to a reimbursement of any kind.
  4. As mentioned above, once you register by executing the /movelog command, it may not be revoked.
  5. The move is one-way. If you are selected for a move, you will not be moved back.
  6. No special consideration will be made to keep groups or guilds together. It is recommended that you do not execute the /movelog command unless you are willing to risk separation from your friends.
  7. The GM/Guide Staff will not be able to tell you if you have or have not been selected, but should be able to clarify general information in regards to the move.

-The EverQuest Team

Topic :   "Packet Storms" Explained

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Hey folks :


Simply put, there aren't "packet storms" produced by EverQuest. BUT, certain network conditions can perhaps look like that if you run the game under a sniffer. We use UDP, but we have a reliable protocol built on top of it. The reason for that is it's much harder to control the retransmit rate in TCP/IP. You will find almost all network games are moving to UDP.

 

So, how do I explain the scenario that Tony showed? It's pretty easy.. let me explain :

 

When the client zones or does something that needs guaranteed data (as in the case of zoning or many other critical cases) and the network conditions get extremely bad, the server keeps attempting to send the data at the normal retransmit rate. Basically sometimes (and this happens much more often on Modems) things can back up. I'm sure you've seen the PL meter rise, fall and then all of a sudden your data rate (right hand side number) goes up for a bit. Thats exactly the symptom here, except that TonySanders had that sort of symptom under a microscope. Those of you that think there's a better way of handling this situation will have to trust that we've looked very closely at this. The bottom line is that when you HAVE to send something reliably, then it either needs to get there or error out. In this case we have a certain amount of time that it attempts to retransmit and then it gives up (that's when you get the "You have been disconnected" message).

 

As for Firewalls seeing strange things - Games like EQ that use UDP will cause Firewalls to be alarmed because Firewalls aren't really geared for this sort of thing.

 

I hope this answers the questions - btw.. sorry I didn't answer it before, I just didn't notice the Thread.

 

John Smedley
President and CEO
Verant Interactive, Inc.

 

Oops.. I forgot to mention an important part in my last message.

When the server OR the client are attempting to retransmit packets, both sides resend at a rate that is MUCH lower than what you see in Tony's log. What Tony's log is measuring is when the data goes across his network, not when it's sent. So from the server end, it could retransmit the same packet 2 times a second, but on his end if it backs up for 20 seconds all of a sudden there could be a ton of packets that hit him all at once.

 

Again, this only happens when network conditions become poor (mostly you will see this happen on Modems, but it happens on Cable modems as well when PL starts to rise, or when there are temporary latency spikes).

 

- John Smedley
