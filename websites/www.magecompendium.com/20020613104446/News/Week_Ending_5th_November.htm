Date :   02/11/2000

 

Topic :  Halloween

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

I've noticed that there are a bunch of threads on the events last night. Some people enjoyed themselves, others didn't. I think that this is pretty much going to be standard for virtually everything dynamic event that happens in EverQuest.

We cannot have a situation where we provide ample warning for everything that is supposed to be a surprise in game. That surprise is a big portion of what makes these events exciting. If we were to start giving explicit warnings before things happen, the target zones would immediatly be innundated with people ready to take part when an event is launched, and the objective of the event would be overcome in very short order.

Yes, characters die in these types of events. They'll even lose experience if they are killed by an NPC. It's part of the game, and part of what makes EverQuest exciting.

As mentioned by Bill Coyle, one of our game designers, the characters who die in some of these events are the victims who need to be saved. Whether you are a victim, or one heros who overcome the challenge, you are still part of the story.

- Gordon

Date :   31/10/2000

 

Topic :  Patch Information

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

We decided to hold off on the patch so that we would have more time to test it.

 

I'm sorry about the confusion on this issue and I'm sorry for the delay.

 

I also want to remind you that when you install DirectX 7, it is a very good idea to install the latest drivers for both your video and sound card. It is important to install them in the proper order, DirectX first, and then the video and sound drivers. A lot of the problems that we've been seeing on the Test Server have been fixed this way.

 

Unfortunately there is no way to patch parts of the data (such as the epic quest information) separately due to changes in the data structure.

 

It also might be a good idea to play a bit on test after installing DirectX (and then installing or reinstalling the newest versions of your video and sound drivers).

 

Note: When creating a character on test, do not create one with the same name as one of your existing characters. Because of the changes to the client (the larger number of hot boxes), playing with a character on test with the same name as one on a regular play server will cause some problems with your hot box and socials.

Just don't do it.

- Absor
