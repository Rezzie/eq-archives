Date :   26/07/2001

 

Topic :  Spell Gems

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Switching spell slots to buff does indeed almost always occur during downtime. Switching the spells, however, does not create downtime, because you are medding during swaps anyway. The entire point that you missed, is that this is incredibly tedious to do, particularly for buffing classes. I play a chanter twink, i experience it every day. These solutions are being suggested to reduce the tediism(sp?) involved, NOT to reduce downtime.

Recently you have added several features that reduce tedious things like buying stacks of food, etc. There is a solution here that will reduce the tediousness of switching out buffs without decreasing downtime, or giving any other gameplay advantage. I really like the idea of extra slots, say 4, reserved for buffs only. Maybe this is the solution, i haven't thought of anything wrong with it, but perhaps there is a better idea.

Well, increasing the number of spell gems certainly does affect the balance of the game and all that above and beyond reducing tedium.

The idea of some sort of less tedious method for swapping spells might be feasibly set up so that it would only reduce the tediousness of the activity without affecting much else. I'm certainly not against that idea at all. Though I can imagine that it might be a lot of programming work.

The idea of adding extra slots that only accept buffs… that would be a change in the effectiveness of casters and their groups, making them that much more versatile.

In any case, I've sent both ideas to the team. Something like this isn't likely to see much internal discussion right now, what with all the other stuff going on here.

Here is the problem that I think is not being recognized. I have 8 spell slots to use -- but hardly any of them are free to be used. As a Shaman I ALWAYS have the following spells memd and do not swap them out: gate, heal and root. 3 down at all times. When I am in the field I have more spells that I do not swap out: malise and slow. Leaving me with 3 slots, just 3 slots that can be swapped. Since sitting down to swap spells out during combat can get you killed I am stuck in a 'mode' of operation regardless of what happens in the fight. There is also the spell cannibalize which I can only swap out for a minute or so. Down to just 2 slots that can be used reliably to get 'strategic' with. 2 slots are not enough and there are spells I have never even gotten a chance to use because of that restriction. I am certain it is much the same with other casters but from my point of view Shaman have it the worst.

But you've made my point.

"Since sitting down to swap spells out during combat can get you killed…"

Exactly. A lot of the strategy playing your shaman is picking what spells you'll need before the fight. If you have access to all the spells that you can even vaguely imagine that you'll need, there's no strategy until the fight starts.

And, well, you're the one that decides which spells you will always have memorized. Personally, I've played several casters that only memorize gate when I need it to go someplace. I rarely use it for escape, simply because I don't usually like to escape without my party. But that's how I do it. You do it differently. You 'set' spells vary from mine, and probably from most other players. That, too, is part of the strategy.

If you want to cast the spells that you've never 'gotten the chance' to cast, then memorize them. Break from your set spells and use them. Those chances to cast spells are always yours to determine. You might just find some new combination of spells that works well for you.

- Alan

 

Date :   24/07/2001

 

Topic :  Patch

 

July 24th, 3:00 am 

Item Changes

- Silver Chitin Hand Wraps' haste effect has been repaired.
- The Smite scroll no longer lists Enchanters as a class that can use it.
- The Augment Death scroll no longer lists Shadowknights as a class that can use it.
- Many pelts have been made stackable. Those that are not stackable now are quest items that will not be made stackable.

Spell Changes

- Focus of Spirit will now stack with Voice of the Berserker.
- Barrier of Force will now stack with Protection of the Glades.
- Manaskin and Protection of the Glades will now stack.

Trade Skill Changes

- Repaired some class/deity combinations on two of the deity specific pottery Idols.

Changes to Sullon Zek

- There are no faction hits for killing half-elves in any city.
- If an Erudite of Paineel is killed in Paineel the killer takes a faction hit (existing rule).
- If an Erudite of Erudin is killed in Erudin the killer takes a faction hit (new rule).
- Bards can't cast harmful spells on boats.
- If a character kills a higher level character, the higher level character will always lose experience. This is in addition to the fact that any player killed within the + or - 5 level range will lose experience.
- Goods and Neutrals will now be able to turn in Greater Insignias for a Magician only summon corpse potion usable only at levels over 45th.
- Characters under level 6 will no longer be immune to PvP in many adventure zones. Players will be notified when they enter such zones.

Misc. Changes

- /target playername will now work on invisible targets as long as the character doing the targeting has the ability to see invisible.
