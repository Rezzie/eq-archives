Topic :   More on Fennin Ro

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

1. Put a cap on Fennin, no new accounts.

 

We are absolutely opposed to capping server population, as we feel that it does more harm than good. New people join the game and want to play with their friends, and they can't do that if we've capped the server.

 

Secondly, as people leave a new server, people have to be allowed to move in and take their place. A healthy server economy needs newbies, mid-levels, and 50s. Yes, I'm basically saying that the economy isn't healthy on a brand new server until we see the proverbial bell curve. Luckily the "Racers" move in and help a new server obtain that balance within a couple of months.

 

2. This is for all servers and game in general cap the servers at a set number of accounts and only allow 2-3 servers per account.

 

Though its an interesting idea, I don't understand what positive effect this would have. Its not the number of people with accounts on a server that causes a problem, its the number of people actively playing on the server that does. Since people can't play more than one character at a time, I don't see a correlation between the number of servers that a customer is allowed access to.

 

Overcrowding doesn't just hurt one class or one ability, it hurts everyone's playability.

 

Agreed.

 

- Gordon

 

 

Date :    03/03/2000

 

Topic :   Gordon on Casual vs Powergaming

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

The two real issues for players with limited time (I'd call it casual gaming but apparently we can't decide on what that means) have are very simple.

 

1. Getting items. We do not want items to become more common. We don't want to diminish the sense of satisfaction one has upon earning an item. We do not feel that we should get items as readily as people who spend more time online...that would be silly. We do want to be able to make progress towards getting them. Camping is the only approach that works in getting high level items (which is sad and not role playing at all...but obviously that's a separate thread). As a player with limited time I have about a two hour window to get online. Therefore camping isn't an option because 1)most good camping areas are taken round the clock. and 2)If the spot isn't taken, the camp times are so long that there is a slim to none chance the mob will spawn during this time. Add in the time to get a group who is willing to camp there, as well as the coordination time and it's virtually impossible. I would love to be able to sink 30 hours into getting a level equivalent item for my class, but the game does not allow this to happen in intervals. It's either a ten hour camp...or nothing. You've even implemented changes to quests such as Jboots making things No Rent, which accomplishes little except making the item impossible for a person with limited time to acquire. There isn't an easy solution to this problem, but it's a problem that Verant should acknowledge and work toward addressing. But please READ what I'm saying, not easier, just POSSIBLE. Allow us to earn bits and pieces while we are on line.

 

2) Gain XP at higher levels. Hopefully the expansion will go a long way at curing this, because the problem is really overcrowding. Simply put again I have a two hour window. I log on at Mistmoore, try for 30 minutes to get a group and fail. As a cleric there is nothing I can do on my own to gain xp so I'm faced with a choice. Sit on my butt and hope a group opens up, or cross the continent to Guk or an equivalent dungeon and try to find a group. This is not an "enjoyable" use of my two hours, so usually I simply log off, and sadly its gotten to the point where I don't really log back on anymore. I don't want to argue the whole soloing issue again, although I firmly believe that every class should be able to gain some XP while waiting to group. However, logging on for an hour without accomplishing anything simply due to overcrowding and an inability to solo is a problem. Again, you need to address the problem, but LISTEN...we don't want an easier game with, we want it to be easier to accomplish something in a limited time frame. Reread that, there is a distinction. It can still be challenging while at least semi profitable in a limited time frame.

 

To be honest I cancelled my account about 2 weeks ago and I have no idea why I'm still posting to this board. The only thing I can figure is that I honestly loved this game for the 8 or 9 months that I've played. These issues are important, real issues. Please don't trivialize them or strawman the argument. Most of us "casual gamers" or people with limited time don't want an "easier" game, we want a game where we can accomplish the same thing everyone else can, even if it takes us a lot longer.

 

You've made some very valid points, not the least of which is your statement about overcrowding being the root to most of the "casual gamer" complaints. I think that before we can make a lot of progress with a bunch of "what if" arguments, we really need to get a handle on the crowding issue and get the expansion out.

 

The expansion will add tons of new areas which were created by people who play the game actively and understand what the players expect as well as what they want. These zones are in many ways much better than the zones of EQ Proper, and can hold a larger number of people in each zone without them stepping on each others toes. In addition to all of this, many of the new areas in the expansion are geared towards the higher level people who've hit that ceiling and run out of things to do from their perspective. Imagine how nice the world will be when 90% of the people above 45 run off to the new land to do something else besides Lower Guk and Solb :)

 

- Gordon

Topic :   Server Split Message

 

March 2nd, 6:30pm

 

***Additional Server Migrations***

 

Tonight, during primetime hours, we will again be opening the registration log on Fennin Ro to allow another batch of people to request an account-move to the new server, Druzzil Ro. In addition, we will also be opening the logs on Solusek Ro and Xegony, allowing some of the people on those two servers to move to Druzzil Ro as well.

 

All of the information contained in the patch message from February 25th (below) still applies to Fennin Ro and also applies to Solusek Ro and Xegony.

 

The opening of the registration logs will be announced via an in-game broadcast tonight.

 

Clarification on a few items :

 

1. If you are selected for the move, all of your characters on your account currently on the affected server will be moved. You do not need to log in each character and execute the command like many people did for the first character migration.

 

2. Please keep in mind that there will be no special consideration given in the move process in reference to groups, friends, or guilds. There is a good change that you will be separated from your friends, and it is recommended that you DO NOT REGISTER for the move unless this is not a problem for you. This is also true for people who have characters on multiple accounts.

 

3. Since people are being moved from multiple servers, if you are moved there is a possibility that your character's name may be changed in order to resolve any duplicates. If a letter "X" is appended to your character's name on Druzzil, you may contact a GM in-game to change your name to one of your preference.

 

4. REMEMBER! As stated below, CORPSES WILL NOT BE TRANSFERRED. If you are moved and your character leaves a corpse behind, the items on the corpse will be lost and will not be subject to reimbursable.

 

- The EverQuest Team

Topic :   Common Player Concerns

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

How good is that word of mouth going to be in a few more weeks, or months, as more and more people hear from these 40 ?

 

Oshlarian, people have been complaining about EQ and how it's doomed and how as soon as another MMORPG comes out everyone will leave and how they're going to tell everyone that EQ sucks, etc. for MONTHS.

 

40 of you ? There are probably hundreds of people who feel the way you do. EQ isn't for everyone. Some people don't like EQ right away, some people don't like it after they play 8 hours a day for months, earn a 50th level character, and then email me about how Verant sucks and the game design behind EQ is horrible, and then there's the vast majority of players who just keep on playing and having fun.

 

It's pretty normal. When you have 189,000 players you're going to have all types. Even the type that seems to hang around this message board the most... the players who just like to complain, yet keep on playing. It's not unique to EQ by any means, though. They are there on UO boards, they were around when I used to MUD a lot (heck, I was part of that crowd for a while on the MUD I played big time on).

 

Now I understand that this is your game, but a game in not really any good without players, and your company's policies and attitudes are alienating those very players.

 

Like I said, I'm sure we are alienating some players with our decisions. It's inevitable. You can't please everyone all the time. But one of our biggest problems right now is overcrowding, which is the result of waaaay more people playing EQ than we ever thought would play, especially almost a year after its launch. Were our policies and decisions negatively affecting even a small but significant portion of our player base, overcrowding would not be an issue.

 

The arbitrary and high handed actions of some of the people working at Verant has caused quite a backlash of anger among many of us here, because there seems to be little concern for the customer after purchase.

 

It "Seems" that once you get your 50 bucks for the game, you consider the $10 a month gravy, and could care less if you get it or not.

 

Now this may not be your attitude, but it dose seem to be the attitude of some of the people working for you.

 

I assure you it is neither my attitude nor the people working for me. With all due respect, the notion that we only care about the revenue from the initial box sale and not about the monthly subscription costs is ludicrous at best. If we cannot keep people subscribing to EverQuest the game will go away. Personally, I want to keep making these games (MMORPGs) for quite a long time so it's certainly deeply in my interest that EQ is and remains profitable. And, like I mentioned earlier, if we have a problem at all, it's that too many people are buying the game and staying subscribed to it (the overcrowding issue)

 

We have asked simple questions and been told.. in effect "You don't Need to Know.."  This is not acceptable behavior for a customer support group.  At least give us something to go on here. Please ?

 

I must disagree. There are many questions that we should not answer, just like when playing AD&D there are many questions and details the DM will not reveal.

 

What Basic (no formulas required) effects do stats have on melee combat ?

 

I believe this has been answered to an extent by Abashi in the past. We are not going to reveal anything more than he already has because we don't want to spoil the game. I encourage you to experiment yourself with different item configurations and to draw your own conclusions. You see, experimenting with different item configurations is PART of EQ's gameplay as per design. If we reveal the details it ruins an aspect of gameplay, just as spoiler sites that post the solutions to quests also ruin aspects of EQ's gameplay.

 

Why can Mob's attack through solid walls, and we can't ?

 

I have answered this in the past, and am more than happy to do so again.

 

When designing and implementing the engine behind EQ we had to make many sacrifices. Computer game creation is often confronted with these choices, because one is always limited by the performance available in terms of hardware and software.

So here's your answer: In order to create large outdoor zones in a 3D world and have our servers run at an acceptable level of performance we had to turn off server side collision detection for many outdoor objects.

 

What are you going to do about the over crowding/camping issues ?

 

Well, according to you and the other 40 people this shouldn't be a problem, no? People should be leaving this horrible game in droves.

 

But seriously, here's what we have planned :

 

More servers (the next server will be the Fennin Ro split -- yes, it's late -- we are still working on the scripts and infrastructure necessary to make this transition).

 

We will be launching the Expansion April 24th, which will increase the size of Norrath by about 33%.

 

We are working on revamping existing dungeons such that the spread of players is more evenly distributed amongst the zones we do have. Basically, we need to get players into the several underpopulated dungeons (Najena, Permafrost, Kerra Ridge, Mistmoore, etc).

 

And please don't take that as a threat, it is meant as a bit of advice from someone that really loves this game.

 

I appreciate that and understand it as well. In fact, that's why I'm here addressing these issues and trying to answer your questions.

 

I know that most of the people who complain here really do love EQ, or at least loved it for quite some time. As I mentioned earlier, I used to complain a lot in a MUD I used to play. I put about 60 days of playing time into my level 50 Ranger there. After a while I'd question any little change the people who ran that game made, especially if it affected me or my friends in any way. And though I'd play the game for hours every night, I'd spend some of that time complaining and whining.

 

I think perhaps this is inevitable in this genre, or at least while this genre is fairly new. People aren't used to playing a game for MONTHS, investing huge amounts of time into it. Most single player games you buy, take home, and either you discover you don't care for it right away and shelf it/return it, or you enjoy it and play it quite a bit for a few weeks until you complete it. With a MMORPG you end up investing an order of magnitude more time into it. Couple that with the fact that these games change and evolve and can be patched and that you have the opportunity to be heard by the creators of that game, and you end up with a small, vocal group who is constantly complaining and trying to bring attention to themselves.

 

I understand completely the personal investment our player base has made and the passion and attachment they have. I also understand completely that 'familiarity breeds contempt' and that the more you play a game like this, the more the little, minor problems that you ignored at first begin to bother you more and more. I also understand that nobody wants their character's class nerfed, or the item they worked for hours to obtain changed negatively, even if the change is for the overall good and balance of the game.

 

But I really submit that some of this phenomenon is inescapable due to the nature of this genre. I'm just hoping that a lot of it is because most people aren't used to the genre. Of the 189,000 people who play EQ, how many have played a game like this before? Maybe half of them played UO? Maybe. And before that? Probably very few (overall, MUDs have never been very popular, probably due to their text based nature). MMORPGs are sooo different than single player games (and so much better, IMHO I think they'll take a bit to get used to.

 

That, and future MMORPGs will be that much better too! Remember, EQ is part of the first generation of these games. In my opinion, we've only just scratched the surface here. EQ, UO, and AC are only the tip of the iceberg. Personally, I'm very excited to be part of this new and emerging industry, because we've only just begun.

 

(I didn't realize that until someone here pointed out to me, that we complain the most about things we care about. Many here have since denied it, but I think "They doth protest too much" to paraphrase "THE BARD")

 

Agreed.

 

Thanks very much for your comments.

 

- Brad McQuaid

Topic :   More on Pet Weapons

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

I haven't heard much from the EverQuest folk about pets and dual wield since the "quick fix" went into effect several weeks ago to allow mage pets to dual wield. I have begun to wonder if the problem ever will be addressed further.

I for one am not satisfied with the current state of things -- my pets can in fact dual wield, but only after a considerable time investment for each pet. Summoning a pair of daggers for my pet increases the time cost for summoning a pet by at least a factor of 2. Not only is it an annoyance from a player's standpoint, but it still is inconsistent with the game world, elementals should not need weapons -- ever -- to improve their fighting effectiveness. It also greatly reduces the effectiveness of one of my favorite soloing tactics -- the chain cast. When a pet is near dead, and I summon another to replace it, I don't have the time to hand off a pair of weapons before sending it into battle.

Don't get me wrong, I am glad that the good people at Verant realized their mistake with npc's and dual wielding and took quick steps to patch it. What bothers me is that what I once thought was intended as a "quick fix" now appears to be thought of as a permanent one. I want the development team to know that we mages and necromancers are not satisfied with this fix as permanent, that it is effecting our gameplay adversely, and that it is an issue that we strongly believe needs to be dealt with.

If I am wrong in assuming that the issue has been closed by Verant, please let me know. If not, I hope to hear that it will be reopened to set things right in the realms of Norrath.

 

- Nemuneir Noldaron

"This was a code problem from day one. It resulted in Verant beefing up all the mobs to account for the fact that dual wield wasn;t working (and they couldn't figure it out for themselves). For them to fix it now would require that they either beef up all characters or otherwise reduce the power of all mobs. In either case, it's more work than they want to do."

Yes, you have to hand your pet weapons should you want him to dual wield, and it is a bit at odds with the game world in general, but after much thought, it was the option that was the least suseptable to additional problems.
