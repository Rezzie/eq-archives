Area Effect Spells

 

 

Magicians are one of the many classes that have Area Effect rain spells, and they do not work properly in the game at present. There is a post on the EverQuest gameplay forum that covers this, but I will repost here for your information. This is something that would benefit many classes, even those without rain spells like Enchanters and Necromancers, because of the way they interact with pets.

 

Druids, Ranger, Shamans, Magicians, Wizards, and even Warriors are all affected by this spell line, and there seems to be a bug with it.

 

The spell line causes an area effect damage of various types (Cold, Fire, Magic) on up to 4 targets in a closely packed area. The damage is initially low, but is in three separate waves of action, and unless the creatures stay in place they will not suffer all three saves.

 

However, the spells presently do not work properly. The targeting code is in error, as it does not find 4 targets and hit them with up to 3 waves of lesser damage. Instead it will hit 4 times (Treating each packet of damage as a separate target, when it reaches the maximum it stops).

 

Thus, the effect is like this :

 

2 targets in place, rain spell cast, it hits both targets with the first wave of damage. The second wave of damage hits the two targets... then it stops, having found 4 targets. 2 targets, 2 waves, total of 4 hits.

 

The way it should work is like this :

 

2 targets in place, rain spell cast, it hits both targets with the first wave of damage. The second wave of damage hits the two targets, assuming they do not move, then the third wave hits the targets, again assuming they do not move. Two targets, three waves, total of 6 hits.

 

Abashi correctly stated some time ago that the rain AE spells are very powerful. He even gave a hierarchy of damage by mana for a single target with AE spells that was correct (It breaks down with more targets) :

 

RainRadiusLarge RadiusPB

However, for this to be true on more than one target, the spell needs to be fixed so it hits properly.

 

Second, and this is rather pressing, the AE rain spells (Possibly all AE spells) treat friendly pets as valid targets, reducing the total maximum damage by even more, since the pet robs the waves of hits.
