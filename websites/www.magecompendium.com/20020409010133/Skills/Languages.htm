Languages

 

 

Also see On Language, Research and Magic.

 

 

I recommend if you are serious about doing research by translating the spell books then learn these four languages first Old Erudian, Dragon, Combine and Elder Dragon, they are essential to mage research.

 

You may have acquired the basics in a few languages either from your guild or by grouping with someone who accidentally or purposely spoke few lines of them, so that another may learn. I have acquired languages in both ways but only one or two skill points at a time.

 

You need not spend your precious training points in the guild on languages, because they can be learnt quite easily.

 

Learning Languages by Teramenor Earthgate, Nameless Server

So you want to be a polyglot? Well, we can help you. This guide will explain the easiest and quickest way to learn and train languages in EverQuest.

Some Basic Facts About Languages

The maximum skill you can have in a language is 100, or Excellent.

Everyone speaks Common Tongue when they start.

Most races speak at least one additional language to start. Elves all speak Elvish, Dwarves speak Dwarvish, Dark Elves speak Teir'dal (Dark Elvish), etc.

You don't need to train a point (at your guildmaster) in a language to learn it. All you have to do is find someone who speaks it. (In fact, *ever* using training points in a language is a complete and utter waste, unless you are utterly incapable of finding someone to teach you.)

Someone can only teach you a language up to 1 point higher than they themselves speak the language (to a maximum of 100, of course). This means that if Rokinon the Dwarf knows the Lizardman language at skill level 73, he can only teach it to you until you hit level 74. (So why not just immediately teach him up to 75, then have him teach you to 76, etc. Well, you'll see. Read on!)

When you get better at a language, it doesn't tell you what level you're at, just that you've improved.

How to Learn Languages

The easiest way to learn a language is to find another player who speaks it, and is willing to teach it to you. Here is the quickest way to teach another player a language.

First, reprogram one of your social hotkeys to contain the following five commands:

/g This is a test language message 1.
/g This is a test language message 2.
/g This is a test language message 3.
/g This is a test language message 4.
/g This is a test language message 5.

You'll use this button every time you want to teach someone a language. (It doesn't really matter what the text actually is, as long as it starts with the /g command, so that you'll be saying it to your group.)

Next, drag the social button into a hotkey slot. Then, in windowed mode, right-click on your text box to get to the language box. At the top is shown the current language you are speaking in. Left-click on it to cycle through all the languages you know. For example, if you want to teach someone Dwarvish, left-click until the language changes to Dwarvish. Then, right-click again to get back to the main text box.

(You can also use the /lang command to switch languages. /lang by itself will show you what language you are currently speaking, and what number language it is; if you use /lang N, where N is the number of a language, it will switch to that language. However there is no good way to get a list of all the languages and their numbers, because which number corresponds to which language is dependent on what languages you know. But if you do know the numbers, the /lang command is faster than right-clicking on the chat box.)

Now you are ready to teach. The person you want to teach the language to has to be in your group, so group up with them. (I don't actually know if you have to be in the same zone, but I do believe so.) Then, go somewhere safe like a city or near some NPC guards. Then click on your hotkey (or use the keyboard, whatever you prefer) rapidly and repeatedly. Text will scroll by in the text box far faster than you can read, and your groupmate will see all of the messages you are sending. However, instead of seeing:

Teramenor says, 'This is a test language message 1.'

Your groupmate will see:

Teramenor says in a language you do not understand, 'Tkal nr a ntet ndjguare mrwkjwe 1.'

This is because he doesn't speak the language, so it appears garbled.

As you continue pounding on the hotkey, every so often (depending on his Intelligence) your groupmate will see a message scroll by:

Your language skills have improved!

Unlike other skills, where you might see something like 'You have gotten better at 2H Bashing (14)!', the language improvement message not only doesn't tell you what level your language skill is at, it doesn't even tell you what language you're learning.

Because your groupmate's text box is constantly being filled with your chatter, it may be difficult for him to see the 'Your language skills have improved!' message, as it will rapidly scroll by. He may want to turn off group messages (in the Options > Chat Filter menu), and even if he does, he will still learn the language.

Once he notices that no more 'Your language skills have improved!' messages have appeared in a while, that means that you have probably taught him the language as best as you can. But if he goes to his skills list, the language will NOT appear there yet, and he will still not be able to understand the language any better! Before this can happen, he has to cross a zone border. Once he does, the language skills are updated, and he can see how good he is. If he hasn't yet reached Excellent (or whatever skill level you are able to teach him to), then repeat the process until he does reach Excellent.

Once someone knows a language at Bad (around 30) or better, even though it will still come through a little garbled, that person will begin to see messages that say things like:

Teramenor says, in Dwarvish, 'Thib is a tgst lhnuuage mesaage 1.'

Notice how a couple of the words are still a little garbled, but generally it comes through pretty clear. (Numbers are never garbled.)

How to Learn a Language From Scratch

So what do you do if you want to learn some obscure language like Elder Dragon, and you only speak it at skill level 1? Finding someone who is fluent in Elder Dragon can be difficult (they are exceedingly rare), but it is possible to make YOURSELF fluent in a language, even if you only start at skill level 1!

Like we said before, you can teach someone a language up to 1 skill level higher than you yourself speak it. So this means that if you speak a language at level 1, you can teach someone who doesn't know the language up to level 2. Once they zone, they will now speak it at level 2, and can teach you up to level 3. Once you zone, you now speak it at level 3, and can teach them up to level 4, and so on and so forth, until you hit 100. This can take quite a long time, but in the absence of a fluent speaker, it's really the only way. This can be especially effective for learning several obscure languages; for example, my friend Caerollil Wildwood and I once taught ourselves Orcish, Faerie, Combine Tongue, Old Erudian, Gnoll, and Lizardman all at once, from merely Awful beginnings. Here's how.

We stood at the border of the Desert of Northern Ro and East Freeport. With one hand on my language hotkey, and the other on my mouse, I did the following:

1. Right-clicked on the text box (in windowed mode).

2. Left-clicked on the language name, to cycle to the next language.

3. Right-clicked on the language box to get back to the text box.

4. Pounded on the language hotkey for 3 seconds. (I was able to press the hotkey roughly 20 times, which meant 100 lines of text went to Caerollil, more than enough to guarantee that he'd go up the 2 points of language that I was able to teach him at the moment).

5. Repeat until I went through all the languages that Caerollil wasn't fluent in.

Since we were teaching each other 6 languages, it took about 20 seconds to do this. As soon as I finished, I ran across the zone border, and Caerollil, seeing me do this, followed. Once we hit the other side, he did the same five steps to me, and when he was done, went back. We did this for about 10 minutes, and each went up 20 skill points in all six languages. Another hour's worth of work, and we got all six languages from skill 1 to skill 100! While this is obviously not the fastest way to learn languages, like I said before, it is the only way when you don't have a fluent speaker to teach you. (I don't consider using skill points at your guildmaster to be an option; skill points are much better spent on other things. Using skill points to improve languages is, I'll say it again, a complete and utter waste. The only time you ever want to do this is if you are FORCED to learn a language from scratch; so you have to go train it at a guild trainer to begin with.)

So why does it work this way? Well, if all you had to do to improve a language was sit there and listen to it, then here's what would happen:

1. Person 1 would spam Person 2 until Person 2 hit their max (1 higher than what person 1 is at).

2. Person 2 can now immediately spam Person 1, raising Person 1 to two points higher than what Person 1 was at.

3. Repeat until done.

This is essentially the same as how it works now, EXCEPT that since Persons 1 and 2 don't have to cross a zone border, they could max out their language in almost no time at all. They'd each just keep pounding on their language hotkey for 10 minutes, and it would require absolutely no effort. Apparently Verant didn't want to make it quite that easy, which is understandable.

It can be tedious doing it the "right" way, but keep in mind that only ONE person per server actually has to do this. After that, they can group with five people and spam them all to Excellent, and each of those five people can group with five other people and spam THEM all to excellent, and so on until everyone and their mom speaks the language.

The Known Language List

Languages are in alphabetical order by their skill list name. Some languages have different names in the skill list and the language box. For example, Dark Elvish is listed as "Teir'dal" in the skill list, but as "Dark Elvish" in the language box. Names in brackets [like this] indicate the language box name, where it is different from the skill list name. Names in parentheses (like this) are merely something to take note of. The information after the dash is where or who the language can be learned from (if we know -- if you know, and we don't, tell us!).
