Topic :  /loc

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

/loc is a very artificial, out-of-character, and therefore undesirable method of direction-finding. It was supposed to be taken out when EverQuest first went live, but as I understand it it was left in due to public opinion. We really wouldn't want to expand on its use in this manner, as it would cause it to become commonplace.

 

- Gordon

 

 

Date :   20/05/2000

 

Topic :  In Development

 

This came of the official EverQuest development page.

 

In Development

The world of EverQuest is constantly evolving, and the ability to patch and update the game is one of the greatest strengths a massively multiplayer game has. The following is a description of issues and functionality that will be changed in the future — including planned new features and/or changes to existing features. .

Please note that the following information takes precedence over information that may be included in the EverQuest manual, strategy guide, and other official documents previously released.

Please also note that changes or additions are targeted to be implemented short term unless otherwise indicated, however there is no commitment to a specific date.

Future Development

Zones

Two new areas tentatively titled The Hole, and The Warrens will be accessible via Paineel and Erudin on Odus. The zones will be free of charge and available via an optional patch. The Hole is a high level dungeon which represents the ruins of Old Paineel, destroyed long ago in a war with between the Erudites and the Heretics. The Warrens is a dungeon for lower level players, and is the original home of the Kobolds upon Norrath. More information regarding these new zones will be made available as their release date nears.

General Issues

Though a majority of the EverQuest development team's time has been dedicated to creating The Ruins of Kunark expansion, we are now turning our attention to the enhancement of current game play related features, such as dungeon tuning and balancing, and quest implementation. Following are the features we plan to focus on:

- The Make Poison trade skill for Rogues will be updated.

- The retuning of Permafrost into a higher level zone.

- The retuning of Mistmoore and Gukbottom.

- Implementation of class-related weapon quests such as the Fiery Avenger quest.

Topic :  Exp

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Hello all,

Based upon some of the posts here, we've reviewed the experience-point (XP) rewards in the Kunark dungeons and some of the outdoor areas and found them to be deficient. Dungeons on Kunark were in general supposed to be giving more experience than equivalent level dungeons on the other three continents, where in-fact they were giving slightly less.

Over the next couple of days, we'll be updating the experience rewards for many of the Kunark zones, making them more profitable in terms of experience, in light of the increased difficulty and danger associated with them.

We wish to extend thanks to the players who brought this issue to our attention.

- Gordon

Date :   16/05/2000

 

Topic :  Patch Message

 

May 16th, 10:00am

General Changes

- Sounds have been updated for NPCs in Kunark. Your Iksar should no longer sound like humans.


- Dark Elven worshipers of Solusek Ro and Bristlebane will no longer be natively KOS to the outpost in the Overthere. Take care that if you have taken actions allowing you to be judged sufficiently by your deeds, you may still find yourself KOS.


- Increased the number of frightfingers in the Plane of Fear. Previously only 3 frightfingers would pop per respawn. 


- Several problems with the guards that were buffed up last week have been addressed. Some guards that should not have been buffed have been fixed. In addition, due to the level of the new guards, they all gained the ability to innately see-invisible. The level of those guards has been reduced so that they no longer automatically get this ability, making it a bit easier to sneak by them.


- Many quests have been updated and/or fixed, both in terms of ease of completion and reward.

Item Updates

- Corrected a typo that had the Canyoneer's Pike marked as NO RENT, causing it to disappear on logout.


- Corrected the model for the Axe of the Iron Back. It was showing as a 2H sword when equipped.


- The Halcyon Broadsword was mistakenly marked as an artifact. This has been corrected.


- Corrected item icons for various pieces of Burynai Hide Armor. It was displaying improperly in the inventory slots.


- The Skull Mask of Innervation has been changed so that it must be worn to activate its effect.


- Corrected a problem with the Scourgetail Whip whereby the damage and delay figures were transposed yielding a 30 damage, 5 delay weapon. 


- The "Illusion : Iksar" spell scroll is now droppable/tradable.


- The Goblin Eye Poker now looks like a dagger instead of a small sack.


- The Cone of Mystics, which incidentally also looked like a small sack, now looks as intended.


- Corrected a typo that had the Goblin Skull Crusher's effect set to the "Lull" spell, which is not appropriate for a melee weapon. The "Lull" effect has been replaced with an appropriate effect.


- The Pouch of Mistletoe Powder will now impart its effect on the targeted character.


- Mosquito Wings are available in game once again.


- Many items had incorrect usage restrictions, and have been repaired.

As always, we strive to avoid making negative changes to items that already exist in game. However, there are cases where a particularly unbalancing error has been made, such as with the Scourgetail Whip mentioned above, where the item in question must be fixed in interest of maintaining game balance. 

We will continue to repair problems, add content, and enhance gameplay in both Kunark and EQ proper. We appreciate everyone's support.

Thank you,

- The EverQuest Team

Date :   16/05/2000

 

Topic :  Corpse Decay Time

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

I feel for those who have been unable to retrieve their corpses, however the risk is designed to be part of the game. We want people to have to go back and get their corpses after they die, and if the risk of corpse loss did not exist, a good part of the incentive to not get in over your head would be gone.

Yes, corpse loss is not fun. But it is a possible outcome of playing that provides risk and excitement to going into a particular area.

- Gordon
