Pet AC Test

 

Well I finally took the time to do a systematic test on this.

I did as follows:

I went to dwarfs in The Ocean of Tears, the level 24 Elona Tunbrin and the level 25 Hugan Tunbrin. I summoned a spell level 29 fire pet. I gave it daggers, and kept Burnout 2 on them at all times.

The reason that I chose a fire pet is because:

1. They have a less varied damage output, since their damage shield is responsible for a lot of the dam they do.

2. They have low armor, and thus can benefit easily from such.

The pet I summoned was a 26 hitter and could solo all the combats without any assistance.

Please note that it was the very same pet that conducted all of the combats.

I conducted 3 series of experiments. First 5/5 without armor, then 5/5 with 2 summoned rings, and 3 coldstones (perhaps AC 13 added). Why 3 coldstones? In the case that coldstoned are placed on NPC as on PC, the first will place itself in the ammo slot... the second would go in the neck, and the 3rd is just irrational wannabesurethatIdidn'tloosethechancetogivemypetacoldstone-ity. Lastly with a self-made small patchwork tunic (ac 6) and patchwork pants (ac 4). Giving it an (assumed) additional ac bonus of 10, 23 in total.

Please note, that I gave the pet both small and medium sized armor. I did this under the assumption that the pet can use both sizes... no really good arguments for that.

After each combat I recorded the remaining health.

The result:

On the second run, the result was inconclusive. Perhaps some effect was obtained, but it was in no way certain.

On the 3rd run though, I believe that a very clear effect was visible. An improvement of 10% against the level 24 mob, and 20% against the level 25 mob was the result, so YES, ARMOUR HELPS PETS!! *cheers, dances, bows, nods, salutes, etc*

An observation on this would be that the same amount of armor helps more against a tougher mob...

Another is, that I noticed that the combats became longer. Meaning, I believe, that more hits was missing, leading damage away from the 'grey' damage of the fire shield, and over to pet-damage, which is important if you are soloing and want to out damage the pet...

I believe that the pet had about 925 hits (the small heal gave it between 9 and 10 percent health)

I attempted to keep the same angle to the Dwarfs in all the combats, to minimize the effect of changing direction, so that the mob would get approximately the same number of hits in the back...

Please note that there is a small improvement against Hugan (4%) but a negative effect against Tunbrin (-2%). I believe that these variations are to small to be decisive for anything, and due to statistical variance. For a conclusive result, this part of the experiment should be repeated, with a lot more than 5/5 combats. A optimistic, speculative and un-careful guess on the result could be that the effect on Tunbrin, the lower level mob, is smaller, and therefore swallowed in natural variance, while the effect mentioned that armor has more effect on tougher mobs could imply that the larger positive variance actually means that rings and coldstone has an effect... Although I believe this is the case, it is not substantiated in the material above.
