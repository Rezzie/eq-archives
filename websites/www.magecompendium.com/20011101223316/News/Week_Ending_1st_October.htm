Date :   28/09/2000

 

Topic :  Patch

 

September 28th, 7:00am

Patch Day

The following changes have been implemented on the production servers as part of today's patch :

General Changes

- NPC corpses level 55 or over will now last 30 minutes before decaying. This should help those on large multi-group encounters who need some prep-time before looting items.


- The item "Woe" has been repaired. If you receive the "Item out of charges" message when your "Woe" attempts to fire its spell, please contact a GM to trade out the item.


- The spell "Mark of Karn" has been changed. The recast-time has been reduced significantly and the Hitpoint-return is now scaled inversely based upon the delay of the weapon. In addition, a hitpoint return will only take place when the target is hit with a primary melee weapon, or the primary hand if you are unarmed.


- Some more tradeskill component vendors have been placed within the world to make certain tradeskills easier to use in your home town.


- "AFK" and "LD" status will now be reported above player's heads.

Fleeing NPCs

Last week's patch caused NPCs to flee more than before due to a bug that was fixed. After evaluating the situation, we decided to reduce the chance to flee, but not to the extent that it was reduced before. NPCs were previously much more "brave" than they should have been.

As part of the same change, charmed NPCs also began fleeing when low on health. We've determined that this is not a good situation for the players. As such, we have stopped charmed creatures from fleeing per player request.

Level 1 Character Purge

The Level 1 character purge has been completed. All level 1 characters that have not been played within the past 30 days have been deleted.

New Server Active

Tholuxe Paells, a new server open to new characters only, is now active and ready for play. Enjoy! As a reminder, /movelog registration will take place on Mithaniel Marr and Tunare Friday evening from 6PM to Midnight (PDT: GMT-7). Registered characters will be moved to a second new server, Drinal, early next week.

User Interface Changes

Many UI enhancements were made in today's patch:

Moving and Resizing Windows

The data windows in the full screen "HUD" mode may now be moved by clicking in the upper left-hand corner, moving them to where you want, then clicking again. The "spell effect" and "chat" windows can be resized by clicking on the lower right-hand corner, dragging to the appropriate size, and then clicking again. Coordinates for window locations in each resolution are stored in the EQCLIENT.INI file.

Bugs with Several Commands Fixed

We fixed a problem with /book, /sit, and /doability that allowed them to be executed at inappropriate times, such as while charmed or feared. They should now only function when the native button would have functioned if pressed.

Miscellaneous Additions

Added the following /-commands :

- /camp: Causes your character to camp out. You must be sitting to use /camp.


- /disband: Will disband you from your current group, or turn down an invitation to join another group.


- /invite: Will invite the target into a group, or accept an invitation if one is pending.


- /attack: Will toggle the state of "Auto-attack". You may also add a parameter of "on" or "off" to explicitly set the state.

In addition :

- CTRL-1 through CTRL-8 will cast the spell in the specified spell-slot.
- ":" (colon: Short for /emote) and "'" (apostrophe: Short for /say) will now bring up the chat line in the same fashion as the /-key and ENTER.


- The "R" key (by default) takes you into reply-mode as always, bringing up a reply to the last person sending you a /tell. However, you can now TAB and SHIFT-TAB to cycle through the last 10 people that have sent you /tells.

Pauses in Socials

You may now enter pauses in social batches with the /pause command. /pause takes an argument from 1 to 600, with the value being equal to the number of tenths of seconds (i.e. /pause 100 will yield a 10 second pause).

For example, a social consisting of :

/sit off
/cast 1
/pause 100
/sit on

...would cause you to stand up, cast the spell in slot 1, wait 10 seconds after you've STARTED casting the spell, and sit down.

Furthermore, you can also use the /pause command on the same line as another social by separating the commands with a comma. Even though the pause command is specified first, it is executed AFTER the command on the same line.

For example:

/sit off
/pause 100,/cast 1
/sit on

...would have the same effect as the example above. It is important to note that the comma can only be used to separate a /pause command from another, and MUST be specified first on the line.

Emote Codes

The @ symbol has been replaced with a series of %-codes that expand into various pieces of information, and now works in ALL chat channels (emote, say, shout, ooc, auction, group, guild).

The new %-codes are :

%T - Returns the current target.
%S - Returns the subjective gender-specific pronoun for the target (He, She, It).
%O - Returns the objective gender-specific pronoun for the target (Him, Her, It).
%P - Returns the posessive gender-specific pronoun for the target (His, Her, Its).
%R - Returns the race of the target (only works on Player characters).

For example, if you have a target of "a gnoll", the following emote:

/point say points at %T indicating that he is ready to kill %O.

...Your character will execute the "point" animation with a text output of "CharacterName points at a gnoll indicating that he is ready to kill it."

- The EverQuest Team

Topic :  EverQuest Accounts

 

I'd like to remind everyone that giving out account info, despite being prohibited by the EULA, often results in it being banned due to the actions of people logged on to the account. On 3 occasions in the past week, people banned for activities on this board later contacted us and said that it was some ex-friends, guildmates, or others "borrowing" the account.

While I'm on it, if someone contacts you via message boards, the usenet, email, or instant messaging, and offers to "trade" accounts for a while just to see what your character class is like and let you try theirs, they intend to steal your account.

Also, if someone offers to buy some items off of you, says that they need the items on the current character, and offers to log in their "alternate" to give you the money, they intend to steal your stuff.

Not to mention, if someone throws up a webpage with some 3rd party utility that allows you to "cheat" in EverQuest without getting caught, it just might contain a backdoor virus to allow them to get your account name and password. There are reputable 3rd party programs out there that do not affect gameplay that are as such not illegal and are distributed by reputable sources such as mainstream news sites that might be safe, but we make no warranties of any type nor do we endorse those programs. Just be sure of your source.

Finally, if someone with a level 1 character has a name very much like someone in your guild, and asks you to help twink them up with items, the person is most likely not your friend from the guild and just wants free stuff.

That should about cover it

- Gordon

Topic :  U.I. Changes

Hello all,

By now you've noticed some UI changes that were put in yesterday in preparation for some more robust changes that are planned. In light of the positive comments and the number of people anticipating some further enhancements, we'll have some new things for next week's patch.

In HUD mode, all boxes (target, group, chat, hotboxes, spell icons, etc) will become movable. You will be able to place them anywhere on the screen that you wish and their position will be recorded in the EQCLIENT.INI file. I know that many people felt the font size changed in this patch, but really only the spacing between lines was reduced at higher resolutions. The resizable chat box will let you set it how you please.

Date :   26/09/2000

 

Topic :  Events and Quests

 

There seems to be a misunderstanding between Events and Quests.

Events change Character (Player) and Server history, Quests change Norrath's history.

Events are run with GM's and Guides being GM's and Guides, Quests are run with GM-NPC's that can be anything from Gnolls to Dragons.

GM's that are involved in a quest usually are designed to be slain by players. GM's that are involved with an Event are never designed to be slain.

The only difficult point is the creation of true NPC's which both can do to make the Event or Quest better.

99 percent of Events take place in the Arena. I have never seen a Quest run in the Arena.

Events are almost always pre-announced, with rules, times, and dates. Quests are almost never pre-announced, and usually have very limited rules for players.

Hopefully this will clear up the misunderstandings between what the "Event Staff" and "Quest Team" are.

Lady Daegarmo
Online Events Coordinator
Verant Interactive

One clarification to this statement :

========

GM's that are involved in a quest usually are designed to be slain by players. GM's that are involved with an Event are never designed to be slain.

=======

Though in most cases the actors in a Quest "can" be attacked, meaning that it is physically possible to attack them, it does not mean that they are always "designed" to be attacked and slain. In most cases, unless an NPC being portrayed by an actor says that it's going to attack you or you see the NPC attacking others, you actually may want to try having a conversation with them first. Trying to kill the NPC may not be the best idea and you may wind up missing out or ruining your chance to participate in something unique and fun :)

Date :   26/09/2000

 

Topic :  Epic Quests

Hello all,

I've noticed a number of questions popping up in regards to the class epic quests implemented in the last patch, with concerns of them being broken in one way or another.

I just wanted to stop by and let everyone know that we've had a team of testers working on the quests, along with the game designers who implemented them, for the past two weeks non-stop. All quests have not only been confirmed as working, but have been confirmed with every race/class/deity combination available. We wanted to be absolutely sure that there were no problems in implementation and that players were not left wondering what is going on.

Given this, the GMs and Guides in game will not answer any questions in regards to these quests or components of these quests, other than to say that the quest is working. Furthermore, the specifics regarding every quest are known only by the designers and the Test team. The GMs and Guides have no way to look up any information that might be requested to address them in a more in-depth manner. It is essential that the Designers and Test team maintain absolute secrecy in regards to these quests in order to preserve the mystery for those who endeavor to discover and solve these quests on their own.

I will tell you this: These quests are different from any other quest implemented in EverQuest thus far. The designer of each quest spent in excess of a month on each design, several weeks on implementation, with a final few weeks of testing.

I wish you all well on your quests.

- Gordon
