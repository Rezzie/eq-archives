Date :   15/06/2001

 

Topic :  Spell Issues

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Lawrence sent me this update to pass on to all of you.

Hey guys, as I get ready to head out for the Fan Faire I wanted to sit down and write up this post I've been putting off all week. A couple quick notes.

The "problem" with rain spell pulses always being resisted if they will be the killing blow isn't actually a problem....It's an intentional design feature. The problem was much like the old DOT corpse poof buf. If the Rain killed the NPC and the server was unsure where the damage came from the corpse would poof, so these spells were coded in this manner to prevent that from happening. I'm working with the coders to come up with a better solution. We're all pretty busy right now so unfortunately I can't give you a time frame on when a better solution will be implemented.

Kunark Evac spells: these will now work anywhere in the world. You won't have to be in a Kunark dungeon to use them. This change will apply to both Wizards and Druids evac spells.

Druid Spell Egress: this spell will now work indoors. It didn't make sense for the group version to work indoors (a mistake on our part) and not have the self version work indoors.

Shroud of Death: I added Illusion Skeleton to shroud of death on test server..... I removed it the next day I just didn't get to update spells on test. I forgot that in skeleton form trolls and ogres would lose slam, sorry for the scare. I'll see if I can think up some other way to give SKs skeleton form.

Stacking issues: I continue to address these as time permits. We're working on some changes that will allow me to fix them much easier. More stacking issues should be resolved in the coming months. High level players should be pleased to know that I'm 99.99999% certain that Call of Earth and Speed of the Shissar will no longer conflict after the next patch.

New Translocates: as many of you know there are new translocate spells up on test. I'm not going to comment on these other than to say you can expect that cool cat Al'Kabor to be out and about researching again.

Now on to the "Burning issue". I know the Wizard community has been *patiently* awaiting the enhancement we have been working on The coding for the new spell effect has been finished and it is going through extensive internal testing. It has also been on Test Server for a week, but I doubt you would have seen it in a parser – cause I'm sneaky like that. Before I go into details on the new spells there's a few things I'd like to say. Honestly I feel that wizards are balanced fine from a power standpoint, to that end you will not be receiving an upgrade to your damage output. Where I do feel wizards need help is group utility.

As I'm sure you can understand coming up with a highly desirable utility type spell for an EverQuest wizard is not an easy task. I've spent countless hours pondering a solution, I've read countless message board forums and emails looking at suggestions from you guys. Finally a solution was found! I'm not naive enough to believe that everyone will be happy about the new enhancement but this is an idea I've seen tossed around on a number of forums and I think not only does it fit in well with wizards being the masters of arcane transportation, I think it will make them very desirable in the 50 to 60 levels where they seem to be struggling for usefulness.

The new spells will give wizards the ability to prevent NPCs from gating. There will be 3 versions of the spells starting at level 51. They will each have a different resistance type. One of the problems with duration based effects is stacking and negating the need for multiple characters of the same class. I am designing these spells with that in mind. If you want to 100% ensure an NPC does not gate you will need more than one wizard. I understand this may upset some people but the need for multiple wizards to "Anchor" an NPC won't really happen until you fight the more epic mobs with higher hit point numbers. For single group encounters one wizard with these spells should, in most situations, be enough to get the job done. My plan is to get these spells implemented and ready to go before the next patch.

Lastly I would like to send a huge thank you to all the players who have reported stacking exploits to me through Email, IRC, and ICQ over the past couple of months. The feedback is greatly appreciated.

Take care all!
/Lakers secret handshake

- Lawrence Poe

Topic :  Mana Bars

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

I've made a few posts about the possible future of all the Iksar class quests and if they will be brought to logical completion, but have never recieved any kind of response. I thought this would fall under the category of new topics, but i guess not. If this subject has already been addressed I was wondering if you could repost your comments about it. I haven't been able to find any info on the matter.

All of the Iksar quests are technically finished. All the ones that exist can be completed. Only the Greenmist quest even implies that there is a chance that the quest might be extended.

That being said, there are plans to extend some of these quests at some time in the future. I don't know when that will be.

Just one little thing I have given some thought to in the past. In a way its modeled after the change that came to the xp bar. The smaller line down the middle of the xp bar that represented the percentage of a yellow bubble.

I have wondered for some time if it would be possible to do something similar on the group list. However in this case the line drawn down the middle of each players health status bar would represent how much mana that person currently has. Now I know this would add to the amount of information be sent out to the clients, but I always thought it would be an interesting addition.

We don't have any plans to implement something like that. There really is no way that your character would know that status of your friends' magical energy. You can look at them and get an idea as to how wounded they are, but knowing their mana isn't something that we see as part of the game. I know there have been a lot of requests for this feature and others this it (one that asked for a %M that would report your percentage mana for macros). But we just don't see that as part of the game.

- Alan
