Topic :  Magician Item Discussion

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

About these lists:

First and foremost, understand that this is only a list of concerns to be brought to the Live Team. This is not a list of promises, and I'm certainly not telling you that any or all of these items will be changed or implemented.

Also, there are three types of items that I don't have listed here. Epic Quests, Magic Resistances in Velious and Mid-level quests. Those three items were mentioned by enough classes that I've pulled them out for separate examination.

All items are currently marked with NONE for status. Obviously we've already started working on some items, you've seen the lists from the patch messages on the Test Server. I will be updating the status for items early next week to reflect those potential changes and with any responses from the team. I just wanted to get these lists out to you fresh.

I want these lists to be final as you see them now, but I know there will need to be changes made. So this list is not final. But if you want something added to or removed from the list, please be convincing about the change. At this point I don't want to tack on too many more items, since the team is already reviewing the original list.

Thanks for the input so far.

- Alan

Magicians:

ITEM: No Rent Items.

DESCRIPTION: Recently the way players log back into the servers after disconnect was changed, and it affected magicians (not to mention people holding no rent quest items). It would be nice to fix this by making No Rent items disappear on log-in instead of log-out, and only if the last log in time was more than 30 minutes prior. Whatever the solution, Magicians are greatly inconvenienced by this change.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Rain Spells.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that removing the pets from the equation would be the best way to make rain spells useful without making them too useful. And it might also just eliminate the bug where some pets attack other PCs when they are caught in a rain or AoE spell. It might also be a good idea to look at the resist rates on these spells, which seem to be higher than with most spells. Some people would also like there to be one or two waves to these spell types.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Pathing.

DESCRIPTION: Any continuing improvement on pathing would be a great benefit to magicians. There are several places where the pathing, rather than the actual pet or the zone design intent, is bad enough to exclude the use of a pet.

STATUS: None

Notes: Plane of Hate, Chardok, Nurga, the Hole, Old Sebilis, Trakanon’s Lair were the zones suggested.

---

ITEM: Pets and AoE fear.

DESCRIPTION: Pets are unwanted against any enemy that can cast an area of effect fear spell (and those are some of the more important encounters, at least emotionally), because the pet will always flee, and will often run directly into other monsters that the party does not want to engage. It might be nice to give magicians the ability to make their pets immune to fear for short periods of time. It could be debated about letting them make other pets immune to fear. And it will most certainly be expected by the players that immunity to fear would make sense for necromancer pets than mage pets.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Voceration: Fire and Greater Voceration: Fire.

DESCRIPTION: The claim is that these two pets do less damage than the level 29 fire pet. Are they really less useful than the 29th level pet?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Spell Drops.

DESCRIPTION: Players believe that the 54th level Water Pet Spell (heck, all the 54th level pets) is dropping off the wrong level monsters. There is a rumor that the water pet is dropping off of lower level mobs in Skyfire. Is that really where it should drop? Also, the Muzzle of Mardu is supposedly way too rare.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Pet Names.

DESCRIPTION: How about letting players pick a pet name (just one per character) and have all of their pets named by that name. It would be no more likely to be abused than the naming of characters, and it would make people with pets much happier. Not to mention it would help diversify the landscape a bit. Or, perhaps, have pets show up as <Character>’s Pet.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Pet Graphics.

How about a new graphic for high level magician and enchanter pets, something to match the spectre for the necros. At least can we look into getting an interesting look for the epic magician pet?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Rage of Zomm.

DESCRIPTION: How about making it look like a big fat Eye of Zomm

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: DoTs.

DESCRIPTION: Why give them DoT's with Velious? It does seem a bit out of line.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Dyzil's Deafening Decoy.

DESCRIPTION: The claim is that it doesn't actually decoy very well. The ability for this pet to taunt could be increased. There seems to be no reason that it can’t be a super taunter. With its low hit points, letting it taunt the heck out of things wouldn’t seem to overpower it. Though, if it’s going to be attacked so much perhaps more hit points would not be out of line. Also, perhaps making it more of an escape spell would be a good idea. Give it a very short casting time (2 seconds), so that magicians could summon it in bad situations real quick and then run like heck. (Summary of requests for this spell: A lot more taunt, more hit points, 2 second cast time).

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Higher level pet healing spell.

DESCRIPTION: By giving them a better pet healing spell (a standard upgrade) at higher levels, they can decide if they want to heal or summon a new pet in a tight spot. The last one they get is at 20th level, I think. It’s a good spell line that really should be continued.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Improved Invisibility.

DESCRIPTION: Why don’t magicians get this spell when all other Int casters do?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Phantom Armor.

DESCRIPTION: Two concerns here. The first is that it uses 4 components! That’s an awful lot of components to carry around. It would be nice to make the component a single item that costs 4 (or even more) times as much. The second concern is that it is overwritten by Shield of Words. It seems logical that the 52nd level personal magician spell should hold over the 49th level cleric spell. Though the cleric spell seems to have more AC, the mage spell also gives additional regen…

ITEM: Robe of Conjuration.

ITEM: Pet Health Bars.

ITEM: Summoning text:

Date :   09/02/2001

 

Topic :  Mage Concerns

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

It's very hard to find anything wrong with the overall balance of this class. They can solo for their entire career fairly well. Because of their pets, they are welcome enough in any group, and at higher levels they get the Malo spell line that makes them even more welcome. There is some concern about the use of pets in higher level groups. Pathing in places can make them more of a danger than a help. And any creature with an AoE fear spell will exclude the use of magician pets. I'm not sure that these issues constitute any sort of imbalance, though. Fixing them, however, would most certainly eliminate any concern that magicians might have about having something to do in raids against the boss mob, or against the high hit point Velious monsters. Having powerful pets gives them the ability to contribute to the combat, regardless of the magic resistance of the monster, or the magicians available mana. So, most of what is listed below are essentially minor. The issue of a change to the way rain spells work crosses several classes, though, and is the only real sweeping concern here. The change to the way link death and logging back in works hit magicians pretty hard. The tedium and expense of summoning new items when you go link dead is harsh.

No Rent Items: We all know what happened when we changed the way players log back into the servers after disconnect, and how that affected magicians (not to mention people holding no rent quest items). The most viable solution for this seems to be making No Rent items disappear on log in instead of log out, and only if the last log in time was more than 30 minutes prior. This would be a perfect solution (and one that I’ve heard the players suggest a few times).

Rain Spells: Can we remove the pets from the equation? Just this would probably make them useful without making them too useful. I've looked at the numbers, and adding more waves would probably overpower these spells by a large margin. We might want to look at the resistance on these spells, though. They may be too high.

New Summoned Proc’ing Weapons: With the recent changes to the status of pets (making them Pets instead of NPCs) and the fix to the way their weapons proc (and the ‘nerf’ of the Sword of Runes) we also mentioned that we would be adding new proc’ing summoned weapons for them. Perhaps something like this could be a good way to introduce a few situational weapons.

Pathing: Any continuing improvement on pathing would be a great benefit to magicians. There are several places where the pathing, rather than the actual pet or the zone design intent, is bad enough to exclude the use of a pet.

Notes: Get specific zone examples where pathing is at its worst.

Pets and AoE fear: Pets are unwanted against any enemy that can cast an area of effect fear spell (and those are some of the more important encounters, at least emotionally), because the pet will always flee, and will often run directly into other monsters that the party does not want to engage. It might be nice to give magicians the ability to make their pets immune to fear for short periods of time. It could be debated about letting them make other pets immune to fear. And it will most certainly be expected by the players that immunity to fear would make more sense for necromancer pets than mage pets. But, as always, gameplay has to come before appearances.

Voceration: Fire and Greater Voceration: Fire: The claim is that these two pets do less damage than the level 29 fire pet. Are they really less useful than the 29th level pet? Can someone check? If it is, should it be? Perhaps another careful examination of these pets is in order…

Spell Drops: Could someone look into the 54th level Water Pet Spell (heck, all the 54th level pets) and Blizzard, just to see if they are dropping off the right level monsters? There is a rumor that the water pet is dropping off of lower level mobs in Skyfire. Is that really where it should drop?

Pet Names: How about letting players pick a pet name (just one per character) and have all of their pets named by that name. It would be no more likely to be abused than the naming of characters, and it would make people with pets much happier. Not to mention it would help diversify the landscape a bit. Or perhaps some other way to allow for more interesting names than the list they are randomly chosen from now.

Pet Graphics: How about a new graphic for high level magician and enchanter pets, something to match the spectre for the necros. At least can we look into getting an interesting look for the epic pet, something like the original appearance (this thing looked pretty cool the first time out, then something bad happened and it lost its cool looks)?

Rage of Zomm: How about making it look like a big fat Eye of Zomm?

DoTs: Why did we give magicians DoT's with Velious? It does seem a bit odd.

Dyzil's Deafening Decoy: The claim is that it doesn't actually seem to decoy very well. We should up its ability to taunt. I don’t see a reason that it can’t be a super taunter. With its low hit points, letting it taunt the heck out of things wouldn’t seem to overpower it. Though, if it’s going to be attacked so much maybe we should give it a few more hit points. Also (or maybe instead), perhaps making it more of an escape spell would be a good idea. Give it a very short casting time (2 seconds), so that magicians could summon it in bad situations real quick and then run like heck. (Summary of requests for this spell: A lot more taunt, more hit points, 2 second cast time).

Higher level pet healing spell: By giving them a better healing pet healing spell (a standard upgrade) at higher levels, we can force them to decide if they want to heal or summon a new one in a tight spot. The last one they get is at 20th level, I think. It’s a good spell line that seems worthy of continuation.

Improved Invisibility: Why don’t magicians get this spell?

Phantom Armor: Two concerns here. The first is that it uses 4 components! That’s an awful lot of components to carry around. It would be nice to make the component a single item that costs 4 (or even more) times as much. The second concern is that it is overwritten by Shield of Words. It seems to me that the 52nd level personal magician spell should hold over the 49th level cleric spell. Though the cleric spell seems to have more AC, the mage spell also give additional regen…

Aegis of Ro: The players think that this spell is more than rare, that it is not dropping at all. I've also been told by a very few players (one that I can think of) that it does indeed drop off a dragon in Dreadlands. Perhaps a slight increase in the drop rate on this spell would be good. (Note: Obviously we've fixed this one since I wrote this. This spell never was dropping, but is now.}

Pet Fear Immunity spell: One idea that came in during the necromancer thread spurred me to suggest this. Area of affect fear spells make pets unwanted in some places. How about we give magicians a spell that would allow them to make a pet immune to fear for a short while? (another note: See, the dev team DOES read these threads…)

Robe of Conjuration: Both necromancer and magician pantaloons (legs) for caster classes give them mana free level 50 pet buffs (Burnout III, Augment Death), but the necromancer robe has the level 57 Conjure corpse, while the Robe of Conjuration gives the level 49 damage shield. How about changing the magician leggings to the level 56 damage shield?

Pet Health Bars: It would be nice if the magician could see his pet's health bar at all times. Even better if every party member could see the health bars of all party pets.

Ok, keep in mind that this list was written a while ago. Obviously some of these items have already been added. And some of them really are not class balance issues (I wrote this up before the idea of starting the class balance threads even existed). So, please, let's only talk about these ideas (if you don't like them or think I've written them incorrectly) and new ones. But please keep in mind, if you know you've stated it before and it is not on this list, that's probably because I purposefully did not add it to the list…

- Alan
