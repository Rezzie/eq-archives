Mage Creation Guide

 

 

Introduction

 

I have split the guide up into two parts. The first one, if you are interested in role playing, explains a bit about the different races and their attitudes. The second simply discusses the statistics required by a Mage.

 

1. Role play

 

Wow, someone is really reading this. Role play means that you simply play/act out the role/character of the race/class you have chosen. So, you have to decide if you really want to play that short Gnome/Dwarf or a High Elf etc.

 

With this in mind you should activate the /roleplay command in game to indicate your wishes to other players. Overall, it is up to you which race you want to be.

 

You should just imagine how this person looks and what would be expected of their character in the game, you also want to consider what would be a fun and interesting role/character to play.

 

If you can not Imagine how you should play a special race, I wrote down some examples and suggestions about the behavior of the different races :

 

Alright, please imagine that all the different races in Norrath are really different. They all differ in their look, speech, politics, government.

 

So it is up to you whether you play a short, always old looking but highly intelligent Gnome Magician Technician or a proud, exalted, self-confident and of course highly intelligent High Elf.

 

Another possibility is a evil-aligned, untrustworthy, egoistic, selfish, extremely proud and of course highly intelligent Dark Elf.

 

Now, there are two races left. The Human races.

 

First we have the Erudite or another expression for them would be High-Men. They should behave in a similar fashion to the High Elf.

 

Finally we have the Humans, I guess that they need no explanation because everyone knows how humankind behaves. They simply have everything out of all.

 

We also have to consider what religion your character will want to follow, personally I would recommend being Agnostic as this will allow you access to most if not all the major Town/Cities on Norrath. However you will have to bear in mind that there are some (very few) quests out there that require you to be a follow of a particular deity before you can reap the rewards.

 

2. Statistics

 

Each Character's physical abilities are represented by the following Attributes :

Dexterity

How quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows.

Strength

Determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills.

Stamina

Affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath; also how long you can run/jump for.

Intelligence

Directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights.

Wisdom

Affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than Intelligence; also resistance to mind based spells.

Charisma

Affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (Charms in particular).

Agility

Affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit.

Note : The actual effect of Agility is disputed by some to be negligible.

Racial Hatred

 

Below is a racial hatred chart for the races inhabiting Norrath, the factions represented here are your starting factions and can be altered through quests and killing various NPCs.

 

To see how another race will react to you, choose your race on the left and column and follow the row across until you reach the column with the desired opposing race if the box is red I suggest avoiding them until some serious work can be done to alter your faction with that race.

 

Any boxes marked in red mean that if you encounter an NPC of this race you will likely be Killed On Sight.

 

Any boxes marked with Evil mean that the races concerned will view the other race as evil, and will treat you accordingly. However they may not attack you on sight.

 

Choose your race wisely.
