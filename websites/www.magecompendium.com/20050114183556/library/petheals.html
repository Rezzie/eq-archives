Healing and Balance

Or: Why Magicians Need Better Heals for Balance Sake

By: Sage Xalmat Lunaire
Arch Convoker of Brell Serilis
Lead Librarian of The Mage Compendium

First, I'm not a druid, shaman, beastlord. I'm a Magician and part Cleric. I'm not going to argue about healing balance for shamans, druids. I'm going to argue about healing balance for magicians, beastlords, and clerics. Druids and Shamans can fight their fight, while I fight mine.

Before Magician heals (the primary subject of this page) can be discussed, we must first understand the balance between the game's best healers: priests.

Cleric, Druid, and Shaman Heals

The common logic between these three classes is that Clerics have the most potent heals, and get them earlier, while druids and shaman get them later. Furthermore, Clerics have a 5% bonus for all spells except their Complete Heal.

For sake of argument, Complete Heal-type spells will be completely ignored, as if they don't exist. Its already been said by devs they are overpowered, and the dev team wishes they were never invented.

Enter: The Beastlord

Unlike the other classes, Beastlords were introduced with the Shadows of Luclin expansion (coincidentally, at the same time as the Vah Shir race. The race/class combo of Vah Shir/Beastlord is by far the most popular combo for this class). They were meant to be a monk/shaman hybrid, but were given gimped melee abilities as a penalty for being a hybrid. Their specialty: Warders, similar in strength to Magician and Necro pets. However, Beastlords also have VERY POWERFUL pet heals, as we will see shortly.

For sake of argument, we shall ignore Beastlord Hybrid healing. Hybrid healing of other players falls significantly behind priests, and is much closer to other hybrid classes. There is no problem with Hybrid healing.

Where the problem lies is their potent pet heals. Remember the argument for shamans: "Because Shamans have slow, they have the weakest heals. So, one would argue that slow is so powerful, less heals are needed." What we see in beastlords is the opposite effect. Beastlord pet heals rival very closely Cleric heals, and quickly exceed their parent-class Shaman, and in addition, because of their ability to slow mobs, their heals are much more potent.

While Priests follow the priest spell circle (1, 5, 9, 14, 19, 24, 29, 34, 39, 44, 49) Beastlords follow the hybrid spell circles (1, 9, 15, 22, 30, 39, 49). As such, we will ignore spell circles where Beastlords do not get new spells.

In the above situations, the pure priest classes will have no problem chaining their quick heals to keep pets alive. Even the Beastlord is able to chain heal and keep his pet alive. The Magician, on the other hand, cannot heal anything unless the mob is first pre-slowed. Additionally, slow gives Beastlords a permanent advantage over Magicians in that they are never fighting a mob at full capacity, in a group or while soloing. Magicians, on the other hand, must rely on other classes to accomplish this.

The Bottom Line

Magician Pet Midigation does NOT justify weak heals. Beastlord heals are too powerful given that they have near-magician strength pets, AND slow.

Not only do Beastlords have close to equal pets to Magicians, but they have more potent heals than Clerics for most of their career, AND the ability to slow monsters substantially. Additionally, there is no valid reason to give magicians a healing penalty when no other class has one

To make matters worse:

Because Beastlords have near-monk midigation, Beastlords are able to tank for their pet, and off-tank monsters themselves. Magicians have no such ability; all tanking must be directed towards only the pet. Beastlords have the capability to jump and tank for their pet at almost every level, midigating pet damage even further. Magicians can't, unless VERY highly geared, and even then for a very short time. Beastlords also have the ability to use their pet as a form of root, should they want to tank themselves; Fight monsters toe-to-toe, and when their health gets low, back off and heal themselves once the pet gains sufficient aggro. Again, mages can't do this.

The Solution

A TOTAL revamp of the healing lineup for Magicians, starting from the ground up.

Magician pet heals should be much, much more potent, equal to or better than Beastlord pet heals, and similar in strength to Cleric and Druid heals. Additionally, the long cast time and recast time must go.

I do not believe it appropriate for Magicians to be more potent healers than Clerics; however, Magicians should at least match the healing rate and healing efficiency that Beastlords can do.

Next, Magicians need ALL pet heals to go to Conjuration skill. Priests do not have the penalty of heals being one type, then spontaneously switching at higher levels, and neither should mages.

Third, Magicians must have healing AAs. Priests, Hybrids (including Beastlords) and even Necromancers get them. Why don't Magicians?

The ones that are present are very expensive to acquire, if they are indeed able to be acquired. More low-level items with Improved Healing need to be Mage Usable, and easily attainable.
