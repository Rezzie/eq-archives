Date :   20/09/2001

 

Topic :  Trivial Loot Code

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Hello all,

I wanted to update you all on our current thoughts regarding the Trivial Loot Code as it relates to Shadows of Luclin zones.

As per my last couple of 'Ask Brads', we've made both an effort to explain what the Trivial Loot Code does and why we were considering it for all Luclin zones. I'd also mentioned, however, that if most players appeared to be against its inclusion we'd not use it.

After talking with players, reading emails and feedback, and then very recently running a poll, it is indeed apparent that the majority of our playerbase does not feel that the Loot Code's advantages outweigh its restrictions.

In fact, after polling over 200,000 players, most want the looting system left alone, almost as many want us to explore a different solution for bottom feeding and rampant farming, and a minority were in favor of the Trivial Loot Code as it exists today in the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains (the remainder either didn't have an opinion or were unfamiliar with TLC).

Well, so be it.

We will head back to our drawing boards and keep brainstorming on this issue, but at this current time we do not intend on implementing TLC for the zones in our upcoming expansion, the Shadows of Luclin.

I do want to thank everyone for all the feedback and discussion regarding this issue -- there was a lot of debate surrounding this issue, and we've done our best to pay attention.

Brad McQuaid
Chief Creative Officer & Vice President
Sony Online Entertainment/Verant Interactive

Date :   17/09/2001

 

Topic :  EverQuest Trilogy Announced

 

- Epic Adventures Await in First EverQuest® Anthology -

 

SAN DIEGO, CA - September 17, 2001 - Sony Online Entertainment announced today that EverQuest: Trilogy, an anthology that includes the original game and two expansion packs, is now available in stores everywhere for the SRP of $39.99. EverQuest: Trilogy offers players five enormous continents to explore, 13 races, and more than 400 supporting characters from snow orcs to giant scorpions for one low price. With over 400,000 subscribers, EverQuest is one of the world's most successful massively multiplayer online role-playing games.

 

"We're pleased to offer prospective EverQuest fans the opportunity to experience Norrath on a grand scale," said Don Vercelli, Vice President of Sales and Marketing, Sony Online Entertainment. "EverQuest: Trilogy is a great way for new players to experience all aspects of EverQuest and prepare for this December's launch of the highly anticipated EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin expansion pack."

 

EverQuest: Trilogy features :

 

EverQuest® Classic game

EverQuest®: The Ruins of Kunark Expansion Pack

EverQuest®: The Scars of Velious Expansion Pack

Collectible map of Norrath

Two collectible EverQuest figurines *Collector's Edition Only

Strategy Guide for new players

 

EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark expansion was released April 24, 2000. Since then, it has become a part of the basic EverQuest package, giving players more than twenty new zones and a new playable race of lizard men called the "Iksar." In addition, the maximum player character level was raised from fifty to sixty. The most recent expansion is EverQuest: The Scars of Velious, was released on December 5, 2000. It provides nineteen more zones of content designed for level 30 characters and above. Due to launch this December, the EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin expansion pack gives players a massively enhanced 3D graphics engine to explore Norrath and its moon, Luclin. In addition to new adventure zones, items, creatures and spells, and a new playable character class, players will be able to traverse the lands of EverQuest on horseback for the first time.

 

Continuing to grow with top-selling expansions The Ruins of Kunark and The Scars of Velious, EverQuest is one of the largest fantasy worlds ever created online. During peak periods, more than 98,000 adventurers and dragon slayers have explored the world of Norrath simultaneously. The active global EverQuest subscriber base is comprised of players from various countries throughout the "real" world, including the U.S., England, Canada, Japan, Taiwan, Korea, Saudi Arabia, France, Italy and Australia.

The Station® (www.station.com) is the official provider of EverQuest, with software updates and news about the game, message boards and other community features, as well as an easy-to-use billing system for EverQuest's monthly subscription fee of $9.89.

 

Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), the online gaming division of Sony Pictures Digital Entertainment, is a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming. Headquartered in San Diego, California, SOE develops, publishes and distributes entertainment for the PC and online markets. With more than 10 million registered users, SOE's award-winning website, The Station® (www.station.com) hosts a variety of entertaining games and player communities spanning all genres. In addition to blockbuster hits EverQuest® and Jeopardy! Online, SOE has an array of cutting-edge online games in development such as PlanetSide, Star Wars® Galaxies and Sovereign.
