This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Before going into the Ask Brads I received, I wanted to first post this email I received about the trivial loot code and Luclin:

It completely destroys the role-playing feel of the game. It is also not farming to go get a specific item that you wish to wear from a lower level zone and then leave. Since I have played pretty much since the beginning, I was one of those people who killed for everything I wear and readily recognize that wearable equipment choices are very hard to come by. Any lower level item that is farmed now is due to a mistake in its placement or a mistake in not having another choice of similar equipment, farming is a very, very rare problem for anyone and can always be handled by your play nice policy anyway. I am also a very average player . A 34 year old truck driver that works full time and have my characters at high level and in a good guild through time played and being a good group member. I really think you should ask what everyone thinks, if an average player thinks the trivial code is awful you might just have made a bad mistake.

Good points. What we're trying to do is twofold:

1. Stop a small minority of high level players from storming into low and mid level zones at launch and then publishing all the treasure to spoiler sites. Yes, we know that will happen anyway, the information coming from level appropriate players, but it will happen more slowly. Right now, when we revamp or launch a new zone, it's filled with lookey-loo high level players who just blast everything. This inhibits the appeal of exploration and discovery for the players for whom the zone was designed. For example, when we re-vamped Runnyeye, this is exactly what happened. And while there were still some players who went into the Warrens and Stonebrunt and blasted everything, they didn't get any significant loot and therefore couldn't talk much about it.

2. The loot code should hopefully allow us to put some decent items in low and mid level zones. Now, an important point is this: the items will still be measurably less powerful than those found in tougher, high level zones. Also, the items will probably not hold much appeal for mid level players on very old servers due to the fact that better items are available because of the trickle down nature of the economy. But, we are trying to make a better, more interesting EverQuest, and we think the loot code will contribute to this, especially on newer, younger servers.

One of your points is that heavy bottom-feeding farming only occurs if the item is in a low or mid level dungeon and is still attractive to the high level player because there is no better item in a higher level zone. This is an excellent point, and when these instances occur, we should correct them (by either moving the item if it's too powerful, or by putting a better item in a higher level zone that is equivalent in nature).

Our plan with Luclin is to make sure there are more attractive items for high level players so that they won't feel they 'need' to adventure in zones that are too low level for them. If we can accomplish that, then, in my opinion, there would be no legitimate reason for a higher level player to be blasting mobs in a low or mid level zone, and therefore the loot code will interfere with very few players' enjoyment of the game. (By the way, I've seen the argument that the high level player has the 'right' to enjoy all content in the game and therefore has equal rights to be in the new zone as another player for whom the zone was designed and populated -- I must respectfully disagree -- the character who is in a zone designed for his level range should have the right of way).

We are aware this is a controversial issue, but honestly, I think most of the controversy comes from the fact that we've inadequately explained how the system will work, why we're doing it, and who it would really affect. I've received many emails and read many posts regarding the subject, and there are players who are both for and against the system. Going forward, Absor and I will attempt to better explain the system.

I will say that I'm generally against making any sweeping changes to the game because disrupting the status quo of a game more than two years old is not usually a good idea. When we do make big changes (for example, removing the class based experience penalties) it's typically after a lot of thought and consideration and after concluding that, overall, the change would make a better EverQuest long term. If we are unable to communicate the benefit of this new code in Luclin to most of our players, we will reconsider it.

Excellent email, thank you. And you are certainly not alone – there are a lot of players who enjoy the low and mid level game more than the high.

I probably wasn’t as clear as I should have been in my earlier Ask Brad regarding this issue. Our intent is to, over time, revamp the older low and mid level dungeons that just aren’t very popular. In general, our priority would be to keep the level range pretty close to what it was. But in some cases, for a variety of reasons (location, layout, etc.), just making the zone more interesting or putting new treasure in it isn’t enough. Those dungeons we will probably upgrade to higher level zones. For example, off the top of my head, Paw and Droga/Nurga. For their level range, they’re just too remote and/or the layout is too difficult.

This certainly doesn’t mean, however, that we’re abandoning the low and mid level player. You have our commitment that we’ll continue to provide hopefully interesting content for you. I think, in fact, that the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains as well as the recently revamped Runnyeye are a pretty good indicator of that commitment.

We do plan to launch EverQuest: Trilogy shortly, which will include the Old World, Kunark, and Velious. Luclin will, however, remain a separate product, requiring at least the purchase of the Old World first. We do want accommodate different combinations as much as possible, but we can’t have too many products on the shelf, lest it potentially confuse customers.

Anyway, my real question was, since SoL is going to introduce revamped character models, will it also introduce revamped spell graphics? Things like area-effect spells actually having the area ignite in flame or whatever, or buffs illuminating the character in an aura rather than just having sparkly things fly around them. We’re not sure yet, but we are going to try. We’ve wanted to upgrade our particle effects engine for some time, but whether we’ll get it done by Luclin or not remains to be seen. While I think we do have some excellent spell effects and such, I do agree it could even be more spectacular.

Will the new Umbra technology in Luclin cut down on zone loading time at all?

No, Umbra doesn’t really have anything to do with the size or amount of content in a zone, which is what determines its file size and therefore its loading time. To be honest, the Luclin zones will probably take more time to load than older zones because we’re putting so much more detail into them. The textures are richer and higher resolution, the number of polygons and the general detail level much greater, and the indoor zones are larger than they’ve ever been.

The best thing you can do to improve zone loading times is to increase your system’s RAM and/or the speed of your system’s hard drive – both will generally help dramatically.

Thanks once again for all of your emails and I’ll talk to you all again soon!

- Brad McQuaid

Topic :  Trivial Loot Code

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

There is a new, fairly involved quest, in the Stonebrunt Mountains. It appears to be a fairly interesting quest, and I'm hoping that the end result is nice, although its still too early to say at this point. There are, however, some problems that I'm seeing with it.

The problem revolves around the trivial loot code, unfortunately. The quest requires players to retrieve items from the Tower of Frozen Shadows, a zone in Vellious that is appropriate for players from roughly level 30 to level 40. The quest also requires players to loot items from the spiritlings in the Stonebrunt Mountains. Unfortunately, the level on these creatures is low enough that your typical player who can actually visit the Tower of Frozen Shadow and retrieve one of the needed items is probably not going to be able to get what he or she needs from the spiritlings due to the trivial loot code.

There is also an additional item worth mentioning, although I'm not yet certain if its a problem or not. Further along on the quest an 'apparition' will spawn. Apparently there are at least two types of apparition that spawn. One automatically attacks the player that spawned it, while the purpose of the other one is not yet understood. Both are apparently fairly easy to dispatch, and both drop trivial loot items. The apparition that aggros is apparently meant to be killed, so the fact that it has an item that is impossible to loot by anyone that can complete the quest is very strange. The other (non-KOS) apparition apparently has an item that falls under the trivial loot code as well, although not enough is known about the quest, yet, to know if it is meant to die in battle with a player that is unable to loot it.

At any rate, it is my personal suspicion that this quest needs to be looked at, and possibly have the tags on some of the items changed so that they can be looted by those players who can reasonably be expected to complete the quest.

There is one assumption that you're making here that I want to correct first.

Quests are not always designed to be completed all at one time. Many quests are designed for the character to grow into, so that a character might begin a quest at a certain level that he can't complete until he reaches a higher level. A lot of people see quests as something to be done all at one time, and that's just not the case.

So it might indeed be true that some quests in areas that use the Trivial Loot Code can't be completed by folks that didn't discover the beginnings of the quest in that zone when they were of a level to do so.

I understand that this may seem unfair to some of you. I've received a few comments about "punishing people for leveling" in regards to the Trivial Loot Code. Let me address that issue while I'm here.

The Trivial Loot Code is indeed a restriction based on the level of the character. But it isn't a punishment, as if gaining levels was a bad thing. It is simply a way of keeping content available to those characters of the level that the content was designed for. It's really no different than the fact that low level characters are restricted from accessing high level content, or that enchanters are restricted from accessing certain quests designed for paladins, or that worshippers of a certain deity are restricted from using items designed for worshippers of another deity.

All of that content is available to the player. If a player wants to wield a Fiery Defender, he'll need to be playing a paladin. If a player wants to have a character that can visit the Plane of Hate, he'll have to level him up. And if a player wants to do a certain level restricted quest (and, by the way, this isn't the only kind of level restricted quest in the game…), he'll have to be playing a character of the appropriate level.

Back to the question, though…. In this case there is indeed a problem with this quest. We'll take a look at it and see if we can get it fixed for the next patch.

Alan returned to the thread later to answer some more questions:

The difference with a low level character, however, is that they will eventually be able to increase in levels and obtain the higher level items/complete the higher level quests. A high level character doesn't have the same option.

I love doing quests - even if the reward is not an uber item-of-greatness. The Tunare Initiate quests for example - they came out after the quest mobs were already green (i.e., trivial), however, the RP potential and general fun of the quests has prompted me to work on them. If the TLC were in place in that instance, I would be blocked from the fun of completing the quest.

I'm not suggesting the TLC be changed - just that you give some consideration to the above specifically when designing quests.

And yes, I noticed that you said the quest at hand would be looked at, just wanted to make the point for any future quests, etc.

You're correct that a high level character doesn't have the same option. But what we're really talking about here, for the most part, is what the player wants not what the character wants.

A player with no high level characters that wants to experience high level content just needs to gain experience on one of those characters and he can see that content.

A player with no mid level characters that wants to experience a level-restricted quest just needs to gain experience on a low level character and he can see that content.

Yes, there are most certainly going to be some cases where it is the character's desire to complete a quest that he can't access due to his greater experience. But that is very different than a player that claims that they are being unfairly restricted from that content.

The inclusion of Trivial Loot Code for Shadows of Luclin does indeed sacrifice the desires of a few high level characters. In exchange we gain a greater ability for appropriately leveled characters to access that same content. But in no case is one player given less of a chance to access any content than any other player.

It's not that we are unaware of the desires of the players. We are very aware of those desires. But there are a lot of players, with characters of all levels. And all of those players need to be taken into account as much as possible.

All quests implemented for Shadows of Luclin will be designed with the Trivial Loot Code in mind.

- Alan

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

I read the recent announcement that viewing the number of players online on the various servers may in the near-future be removed. It seems to be the case on the server status page of this website already, though I assume this will apply to the server selection screen soon as well.

As you noted, we are still a business, and we need to take care of the business stuff with just as much energy as we take care of the game stuff.

- Alan

Topic :  Player Counts Removed From Test Server

 

This came off Lum The Mad's message boards.

Well, rather than let speculation run rampant, I thought I'd get very specific.

Due to the increasing amount of competition in the online gaming space we feel that it's not in our best interests as a company to provide our existing and future competitors with valuable information on our userbase. We have always had a very open stance on the issue of what our numbers are specifically, but as we grow in other markets around the world this information is simply not something our competition needs to know. We recognize that changing our stance on something like this makes many people wonder why, so there's your answer.

It's as simple as that.

- John Smedly

Another person then threw this situation at Smedly:

So it can be safely said that while Asheron's Call, WW2O and Anarchy Online were not considered significant competition, Dark Age of Camelot is?

That's interesting, because it means that the perception that DAoC is going to adversely affect playerbase numbers was judged to be less important than the reality of those numbers.

Honestly, it's got more to do with some upcoming moves we're making to expand EQ and our desire to capitolize on this expansion than any one particular product. I'm absolutely not saying we don't consider any of the products competition. We obviously do, and we take them seriously... but if you try something in business, and it works.. no point letting the competition know how well it worked (and yes, I realize we're operating on the assumption that our ideas will hopefully work out).

Anyone who is in business and fails to look over their shoulder often is usually not going to be at the front of the pack.

- John Smedly

Topic :  Press Release

SAN DIEGO, CA - August 29, 2001 - Sony Online Entertainment, a worldwide leader in multiplayer online gaming announced today that a free zone is being added to the world of EverQuest®. The addition of the Stonebrunt Mountains will bring a new set of landmarks, quests, and challenges to EverQuest subscribers at no additional cost.

Located on the small continent of Odus, the Stonebrunt Mountains are rich with vegetation and distinctive hills that give way to the towering Mount Klaw; the largest mountain in Odus and home to the Kerran village of Kejeka. Topic :  Test of Tactics 3

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Spectator Guidelines

Spectators will not be allowed to attend this event at any time. Only two current competing teams and judges will be allowed in the Arena Zone, all other character will be removed to Lake Rathetear and given an alternate bind location if bound in Arena.

Sign-up Guidelines

Sign up for the Test of Tactics III will take place on the Arena Forum. Each server will have a limit of 8 teams to compete. The sign up on the Arena Forum will be first come first serve and will take place September 17th - October 8th. During the period of September 17th - October 1st only 1 team per guild per server will be allowed to sign up. Starting October 2nd - October 8th a guild may sign up for additional teams if all spaces in the bracket are not filled on a server. All teams must meet the team level total to sign up (see Example of Team total for more details). Sign up will close at 5:00 PM PST on October 8th and no late sign ups will be allowed.

Contest Rules

The entry fee for this event will be 25,000 pp to be paid on final roll call on event day to events staff. A team may elect to not pay entry fee, but will then be ineligible for any prizes awarded for winning.

This event will consist of teams of 18 members per team. Each team will have no more then 3 members of one class. The teams will be formed by adding up the levels of each of its members to form a team total. This total number must be 1060 or more levels.

Example of Team Total

5 level 58, 9 level 59, and 4 level 60 = 5*58+9*59+4*60 = 290+531+240 = 1061

The object of each team will be to eliminate as many of the opposing team members with the fewest members lost of their own team in a 20-minute battle.

More rules will be following shorty as I work with the Events Staff on the finished rules. We wanted to get sign up information out as quickly as possible.

- Lady Daegarmo
