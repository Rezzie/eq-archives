A Guide to Kunark Expansion Spells

 

 

Note : Some of the information may be a little dated in this guide, but a lot of hard work went into it.

 

Well mages, you may have wondered what the world of Norrath holds for you now that the new world has opened. Well I don't have much of the new lands explored, but I can tell you all about you new spells. Almost all of them are right on the money on this site so I'll just hut up the highlights.

Pets

Pet may disappoint you at first glance but I feel that they have done something truly remarkable with our magician pets. True Variation. The days are now gone when we summon a water pet because we are in sol b and LDC are immune to fire spells. Here is what I've found our pets to be:

Earth Pets

Basically the same as other earth pets. Hard to tell but I believe more hp, and more strength. I thing that the difference between Earth and the other pets is much larger than it was before. Also the new spell Earth Elemental Strike is root with a longer range and Level of pet + 10 damage.

Fire Pets

What the hell is wrong with fire pets? May be the first thing you think upon summoning your first Voceration: Fire. Well nothing really, they no longer cast FireElementalAura, it's been replace by the pathetic O'Keil's Radiation. They also don't cast the FireElementalAttack spell anymore, they are little mini wizards, so far I've seen them cast: Lightning, Shock of Force, some stun spell, and both a hot and a cold spell. I believe that fire pets cast either 34 or 39 spells but that is going to take more investigation. The biggest problem with fire pets is they are no longer tanks at all, without the old elemental aura for Level of pet + 1 damage, and the long cast wizard spells, you really don't want to summon this pet too much solo.

Air Pets

Not much new about air pets as far as I've been able to tell. Their stun now does damage (Level + 10) and seems to last a little longer, and I think the base delay of air pets may have been reduced. I'm not much of a number cruncher so I can't say for sure.

Water Pets

Water pets haven't changed all that much. Their water strike spell now does (level+10)X 2. but the nicest thing about water pets is seeing them backstab. I've seen water pets backstab for up to 200 damage, but that may not be the cap, who knows.

Dyzil's Deafening Decoy-

And what the hell is this you may ask. Well a summoned decoy is a truly unique idea for pets. The Decoy looks like a big fire pet, and focus items don't seem to work on it. It has a much lower damage potential than other pets, but seems to have even more hp than earth pets. The decoy now casts the old FireElementalAura spell, but the neatest thing about the Decoy is it's super taunt AoE spell. It didn't seem to be 100% functional last time I played with it, but this is supposed to get creatures REALLY mad at your pet to let you nuke, run or what have you. I suspect this one will be VERY popular with enchanters in places like Sol B, Guk, and all the fun new train dungeons that Kunark will have for us. Careful, this spell has a very long Recycle Time.

Rage Of Zomm

Summons a Holy Hand Grenade = ). Basically just a guided eye that blows things up. Well I assume at least, at first the spell summoned pets named !Kibaer that ran all the way across the zone and bugged you so you couldn't summon another pet, now pets just die as soon as you summoned them in a little explosion that doesn't seem to really hurt anymore.

Item Summons

Well some of these are pretty boring, other not so much. I'll leave it to you to decide.

Gift of Xev

Well you get a 10 slot backpack = ). Also 20 food/water/bandages. Not too bad, I'll cast a lot of it I assume.

Bristlebane's Bundle

Summons a Firecracer, a bunch of Beer, Some Cake, and umm.... err I forget the 4th item *blush*

Quiver of Marr

I believe these arrows have a little higher damage than normal arrows, also have 75 range instead of 50. Summons 3 stacks of arrows and a quiver.

Bandoleer of Lucin

I'll have to look and see what this spell summons, I now forget *blush*

Pouch Of Quellios

Get ready to make lots of Monkish and Roguish friends. With this spell you get 60 7/23 Shruriken with 100 range. They also have +3 to Agi and I think Dex. They are rogue and monk only however.

Muzzle of Mardu

Summons a haste mask. Gonna have to give it to brego or dravith to test = ). Pretty useless for an item summon at level 56 since every tank I know has a haste item. Last I checked there was no AC on the mask and no stat modifiers. So unless it's SLTW speed I doubt anyone but newbies will be asking for these.

Offensive And Defensive Spells

Well here is everything else that doesn't fit into the above categories.

Scintilation

An AOE shock of swords. Kind of fun for clearing green trash. Good way to die still IMHO.

Malosi

We got a taste of it, now we ge it back. YAY!

Char

Its going to be everything I can do not to yel MANDER! every time I cast spell (pokemon joke, yes I'm ashamed of myself as well). Decent damage ratio, upgrade in our shock of flame line.

Phantom Armor

More AC, More Regen, More Cat's eye. Did you excpect something more?

Annul Magic

Dispell Magic upgrade.

Boon of Immolation

Dammit you mean I gotta wait until I'm 53 to get my first group damage shield? Well it costs as much as Lavashielding 3 tanks, I imagine I'll probably use it in groups with 2 or more tanks (because I'm lazy). Doesn't hit pets unfortunatly.

Shield of the Magi

More AC, More MR, More HP. Arch Shielding upgrade.

Scars of Sigil

My favorite offensive spell. I've been training myself with bolt targeting just so this spell would be mine. At 150 mana, 450 damage and 4.0 casting time (2.0 recast), this spell is a machine gun. If you are outdoors you can try turning your back on your target, the bolt will take a longer path and you'll be able to get a few more into the air before the first hits.

Call of The Hero

YOU WILL NOT EVADE ME RILLO! Costs 1 pearl and 500 mana, is a slow cast and has a slow recycle time, but it will summon a group member to you from anywhere in the zone. I haven't tried it between zones yet, but I can't imagine it working as there is no good way to target it. I hope (although I doubt) this spell works in PoA, save us a fortune in coffin fees.

Sirocco

Huge damage rain spell, but rains are bugged and your pets are counted as targets hit. None the less a kind of fun spell outdoors vs tall targets (forest giants and such)

Eye Of Tallon

YES! Eye of Zomm Upgraded at last! It actually turns you invisible and makes and invisible eye, but I think this probably works to make mobs not see your eye for some odd reason. Also gives you see invis and some telescope effect. Interesting scouting spell, and good fun in Plane of Air. (A spiroc lord says, 'TIRRANNA')

Cadeau of Flame

Bigger Flameshield...what did you expect?

Exile Summoned

More damage than banish summoned. Save this one for when hole opens.

Shock of Steel

Damage on this spell could be higher, but I love this line of spells and I imagine I'll be using this spell quite a bit. I think that at level 60 my offensive spell list will pretty much always be Shock of Steel and Scars of Sigil.

Malosini

Upgrade to Malosi. Faster cast time, lower mana, same debuff I believe.

Velocity

SoW for your pet...you just have to wait for level 58...Grr.

Manastorm

Interesting idea, it's aoe damage and mana tapping in a rain form. but still a rain spell so is subject to high resists and only a few waves. Quag is gonna see this spell, I owe him a few.

Seeking Flame of Seukor

Big Badda Boom! Make things blow up. Bolt upgrade, nice for a show I guess.

Aegis of Ro

Group Damage shield. Same damage as the last singe target damage shield for 3 times the mana cost. but you can't shake a stick at 33 damage now can you?

Banishment

For the mere cost of a star ruby you can send one summoned creature back to where it came from...oh it takes it's corpse and exp with it...

Mala

Listed some places as Malo, actually read Mala in my spell book. The debuffs on this spell are pretty small, but it can't be saved against. Also last time I tried Malosini overrode Malo. So for 550 mana you can make your Malosini harder to resist. If you plan on doing this I recommend letting a friend cast one spell or the other to lower the mana costs.
