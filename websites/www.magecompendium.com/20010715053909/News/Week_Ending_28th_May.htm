Date :   25/05/2000

 

Topic :  Patch Message

 

May 25th, 8:00am

Patch Day

Greetings all! The following changes have been implemented on the Live servers:

Spells/Songs

- Voltaic Draught, a high level wizard direct damage spell was improperly saving versus resistance to cold. It now appropriately saves versus magic.


- The higher level bard song by Brusco was improperly healing the target instead of damaging it. This has been corrected.

Items

The Shadowknight class-specific quest armor, Darkforge armor, has had its weight decreased substantially.

The Runed Fighter's Staff, previously useable by Monks and Warriors, has been restricted to Monks only. The statistics on this staff are much more powerful than normally allowed for warriors. This change is retroactive and will be reflected on items currently in inventory. Though we strive to avoid negatively adjusting items after they have been earned, this item presented a balance issue, making the change necessary. We apologize to warriors that currently own this weapon.

A data error with the Symphonic Saber, causing it to report "Item out of charges", has been corrected. Due to the dynamics of this particular issue, current owners will need to contact a GM to trade this item out for a new, properly working version of the item.

The Burning Rapier (rogue weapon) has been enhanced. In addition to an increase in statistics, it received a stronger spell effect. The lightsource effect on the weapon has been removed due to an aesthetic rogue desire to be "sneaky" :)

The effect on the Hangman's Noose will now work properly.

General Changes

- Some NPCs in Dalnir were casting spells much too powerful for their level. They have been adjusted.


- Pathing information has been updated in Highkeep. NPCs and pets should have a much easier time of navigating the zone.


- Some Quest-NPCs were not spawning properly in Rathe Mountains. These issues have been corrected.


- A handful of problems reported with research practice via the Concordance of Research have been corrected. All practice-rune recipes should now work correctly.


- Gorilla's in Burning Wood now have a new texture set that properly reflects their undead status.


- Fishing should now work properly in Kunark zones.


- /yell now reports the proper direction to people hearing the yell.


- Corrected a bug that gave some races the impression that they were allowed to start their character in Cabalis. They were ever actually allowed to create the character there, however the indication that they could has been fixed.


- Corrected sound issues that were causing some crashes to the desktop in certain cases.

We are in the process of patching music files (*.XMI) for zones in which music does not currently work. There are 55 small files (around 2K each) that will be patched over the next couple of days to enable music in all of the affected zones. 

The zone of Timorous Deep has been tuned and itemized. Certain aspects of the changes have made certain areas of the zone more dangerous, but the reward should be well worth the risk. Feel free to visit this zone and search out its treasures at your leisure. Note: These changes went in on Monday.

Upon investigation, we discovered a deficiency with the amount of experience being rewarded in many Kunark zones (mostly dungeons). The affected zones have been updated, and adventurers should notice the greater experience reward in return for the increased risk and challenge associated with these zones.

- The EverQuest Team

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

This is one of the long-standing myths in EQ, that what kills you has some bearing on how much experience you lose from the death.

Experience loss on death is based solely on your level and the amount of experience that you have (total). It may appear that you lose more in certain cases, but this is due to the resolution of the experience bar and also where you are in the particular level.

Topic :  Farming

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Abashi why aren't popular items like the Shiny Metallic Robe and Runed Cowl Lore ?

The LORE and NODROP tags on items have different effects on the player economy and the farming issue.

For instance, NODROP, though created for the purpose of making people complete their own quests, is *very good* at controlling the farming problem. However it's very bad for the player economy, and actually promotes camping, or gives people the feeling that they are required to camp. It also makes things such as looting a persons corpse for them when it's in a bad place impossible.

LORE, though not as good as NODROP at stopping farming still does does something, making it a bit cumbersome for one character to hold a bunch of items on their own. It's easier on the player economy than NODROP, but still presents some game play problems. It also gives people the feeling that they are required to camp because people are less likely to want to trade LORE items since they they have to go back and get another one for themselves.

Though the absence of both of these tags does present some farming issues, it is by far the best for the player economy, general game play, and gives people another reasonable option to acquire something besides camping it themselves. With the implementation of the play-nice policies, as well as the addition of all of the new zones with the expansion, the farming issues are being handled through other means.

So, to answer your question, we don't want to place the LORE and NODROP tags on items as a matter of course, and would prefer to give each item and situation some serious thought due to the negative consequences of these tags.

- Gordon

Topic :  Scam Confirmed

 

These things are becoming such a pain. In addition to this one, I've seen 3 others in the past week. One of them was a program that advertised that it gives characters GM powers. It of course just put up an official-looking login box asking for station names and passwords and transported that information to some account hackers.


Please, do not use any software that you aren't absolutely sure about. Even if they pass virus-checks, they can very well be programs that record keystrokes, or try to trick you into giving out information about your account. It is each players responsibility to protect themselves from these types of attacks.

There are no programs that will give you GM powers, Zone faster, or let you do anything to positively affect the status of your character in-game.
