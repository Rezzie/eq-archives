This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

We just received word of yet another scam directed at EQ customers attempting to get them to send their passwords and login information to parties impersonating Verant personnel. Please do not be fooled by these scams.

Topic :   The Ruins of Kunark Strategy Guide

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

Verant, why should players buy the new strategy guide being released for sale with Kunark ? The first was completely wrong, and a total waste of money. Will you be involved in the writing of this one to get it right ?

We are involved with the strategy guide for Kunark, yes. Definitely more so than with the original guide.

That said, we don't have total control. We are the developer, not the publisher (Sony), nor the company in charge of writing/creating the guide.

There is also another problem with strategy guides (and even manuals) when it comes to MMORPGs : these games change. The manual/strategy guide can't.

My view of strategy guides for games like these would be that they really be strategy guides, that they are full of valuable information that is more timeless. For example, grouping tactics and such.

But let me tell you, the strategy guide makers want to put in list after list of spells, items, skills, and other stats. Which is all find and dandy, I guess, for single player games.

But it makes for problems in games that change and evolve. I'm also against 'spoilers' in general, but that's another topic.

Another thread mentioned that the original guide said Humans have an experience/leveling bonus or some such. The reality is that they are one of the easier races to level in that some other races have experience penalties.

But they didn't word it that way, for whatever reason (Miscommunication ? who knows).

Anyway, to make a long story short, we'll do our best to work with the outside companies that write manuals and strategy guides, and we seem to be having more influence in the Kunark guide than in the original. That said, things are going to change, miscommunication will probably occur again (Let's just hope to a lesser degree), and things will be wrong.

*shrug*

- Brad McQuaid

Topic :   Spell Casting

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

Feature : Allow the ability to cast spells directly from spell book, with certain circumstances, and at a large casting time delay penalty (A penalty similar to the meditation time of the spell, or more, would be appropriate).

The spell-system of EQ is built around memorization, and would not easily adopt changes to the core architecture such as this. Also, the effort required to implement something along these lines wouldn't really return any advantage substantial enough to justify it, since any net-gain in time or delay would have to be zero in order to maintain balance. In other words, even if we did it, the penalty associated with casting directly from the spell-book would have to be equal to the time it would take to forget a spell, mem/cast/forget a new spell, and re-mem the first one.

- Gordon
