Topic :  Command Syntax

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

You can only put two commands on one line when one of the commands is "/pause". The syntax to do so is:

/pause #, /command

The pause command must come first followed by a comma to separate it from the next command. The pause is, however, executed after the command. It's a little kludgy, but it works.

 

- Gordon

Topic :  DataRate.txt

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Please can I have the number into this file explained to me ?

Someone stated that it would help with dumps to Servers Selection Screen with cable modems.

I have the same problem.

It's a way for you to tell our server how fast you can accept data. The default setting of 5 is roughly where it should be for a 28.8K modem.


If you are running something faster than than, feel free to bump it up. If you start having system problems, getting knocked off, or anything of the sort, bump it down a notch.

I ran 9 on a 512K DSK connection without a problem. Your mileage may vary. Don't set it above 9 or below 3.

The main benefit of bumping it up is faster zoning, and less of a chance of getting kicked off in high-bandwidth encounters (such as dragon raids).

- Gordon

Topic :  Online Events

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

There seems to be some very heated discussion on this board about the rules, the decisions, and the handling of the events.

Events are an ongoing process where we run events, make mistakes, learn from the mistakes, and make changes so that the next events we run have less mistakes.

Are we ever going to run a perfect event that pleases every customer in every aspect?

I doubt it.

We will strive for it, but with some 300,000 players, we cannot please everyone.

With the discussion of level divisions for events, the one currently in question is levels 29 to 38. Could I simply change the rules to state that the event is now levels 34 to 38?

Sure I could.

What did I just do? I just told the levels 29 to 34 they could not participate and took away a chance for enjoyment for those players. Does a level 29 have a chance against a level 38?

Probably not.

Why do I let the Level 29 enter?

We have scheduled the time of an Event Master, a Game Master, and Guides to running this event. If a level 29 wishes to participate, he knows that his chances of winning are slim if he faces a Level 38.

I feel that giving the level 29 an option to participate in this event is better then having him sit on the sidelines dreaming of the day that he can also enter an event.

Cutting the levels allowed into an event does not give us the ability to double the amount of events we run.

Twinking is part of EverQuest. Norrath is a have and have not society just like the real world. It is also a "Grey" topic, not a black and white. If a warrior comes into the event in a full set of Crafted Armor, when everyone else is wearing chainmail, I advise the player that he cannot enter in crafted. The customer may not have another set of armor that he can simply wear. What did I just do?

Told another customer that he cannot enter this event.

My dedication is to allowing as many players to enjoy events as possible. This will not be hacked to pieces regardless of how many posts are made, emails are sent, or people stomping their feet.

Perhaps in the future, we will offer some variations of the rules to have both non-specific equipment events, and specific equipment events.

If you do not like the rules, or the way that VI is handling the events, I ENCOURAGE you to run your own events.

Design your event, place your own limitations on levels, equipment, spells, classes, and other factors. Set up a date and time. Set up your own entry fee, go out and get your own prizes to offer. Post your message here, the same that I do, and run your own event.

We have, and are still making mistakes, as you will find yourself doing when running events. We are not "Gods" who do everything perfect, we do make errors.

There have been some mistakes made by myself, and the events staff in the Test of Tactics - Best of the Best. We are going to finish this event, compile all data received, look for feedback on this board, and make revisions for the next Test of Tactics - Best of the Best.

Perhaps the next Test of Tactics will allow full range of spells, including all enchanter spells, all activatable items, and almost no rules for those involved.

Perhaps we will start another series of events that will be run with very limited selection of items and gear.

We shall see, the Events Staff will review all data and feedback after the events, as we have always done when the events are completed.

Good luck, and see you on the battlefield

Lady Daegarmo
Online Events Coordinator
Verant Interactive
