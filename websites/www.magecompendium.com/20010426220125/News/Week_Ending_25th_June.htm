Topic :  Patch

 

June 22nd, 9:00am

The Hole

We are pleased to announce the opening of The Hole, a high-level dungeon on the continent of Odus. The Hole is a complementary addition to EverQuest, available to all subscribers regardless of their Kunark status. The files necessary to enter The Hole are available via the "Optional Patch" button at the bottom of the patch program interface. The files are quite large (about 9MB total), so if the download is going slowly, you may want to postpone downloading the files until later.

As mentioned above, The Hole is a high-level dungeon, designed to support characters starting at about level 40. It should be regarded as extremely dangerous. As a note, jumping in The Hole from Paineel is NO LONGER A SHORTCUT TO TOXXULIA FOREST. The GMs will not recover corpses for people who fall, or jump, or attempt to levitate into The Hole.

Vallon Zek is now Coin-Only

As we announced earlier this week, due to the number of people specifying interest in "coin-only" loot on Vallon Zek, versus those wishing to retain item- loot, we have implemented "coin-only" looting rules for PvP combat on Vallon Zek.

Resurrection

Previously, players had to be resurrected within two hours after death in order to receive any experience back as part of the resurrection. Due to the increased size and difficulty associated with Kunark zones, we've raised this limit from two hours to three. This should assist players and make it easier for them to obtain an experience-returning resurrection within the time limit.

Warping

We put in some code that should help decrease the "ghosting" (NPCs appearing to be some place they aren't) being reported in some of the larger Kunark zones. Please let us know how this goes.

Locked Doors

We noticed few weeks ago that it is common practice to use NPCs to open locked doors rather than by using the services of someone that is able to pick locks. In order to protect and increase the value of the abilities of the lock-picking classes, NPCs have gained the ability to walk through locked doors without opening them.

Vox and Nagafen

Over the past couple of weeks, we've received more and more reports of these dragons being bested by smaller and smaller groups of higher-level (high 50s) adventurers. As we've always felt that Dragons should be special encounters that require a large number of people, we were left with some choices.

One choice available to us was to increase the power of the dragons to a level where the level 60 folks would find them challenging. This would have the disadvantage of forever placing them out-of-reach for those who did not buy Kunark, and place them further out-of-reach for those who did, but are just now approaching the levels where they could go on Dragon raids. Another possible solution was to make the dragons flee (depop) when engaged by a more powerful group of people. This would have the undesired effect of allowing some people to deny others the experience of fighting a dragon.

Instead of raising the bar as mentioned above, we've instead decided that dragons, or at least these two dragons, should have the magical ability to pick their own fights. Players of level 53 or above will now encounter some difficulty when attempting to assist in combat with these dragons. This should help 40s and low 50-level characters with their chances to encounter a dragon without worry of getting forced out by much higher level players. In addition, it also has the added effect of creating a natural progression from the younger dragons of Antonica to the elder and more formidable dragons of Kunark.

- The EverQuest Team

Topic :  Monsters Walking Through Objects

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

This happens due to the way collision with objects works server-side versus the prediction engine of the client. Basically, the server doesn't acknowledge the object (a tree for instance) so has the NPC walk through it. The client on the other hand sees the NPC hit the tree, and figures that he NPC will get stuck walking up against it.

In a few seconds, the server re-synchronizes the client and everything is updated. It's one of the minor drawbacks to the system that we adopted to minimize the effects of internet latency and reduce bandwidth usage.

 

- Gordon

Topic :  Test Server Player Restoration

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Hello all,

After a good deal of discussion on the Test server status, we've decided that having the right or the ability to do something doesn't necessarily mean that we should. Our thoughts were that everyone was aware of the risks of playing on the Test Server, and always did so with the understanding that something such as a character-wipe was always a possibility. It's apparent at this point, given feedback from players on Test, that many didn't understand the risk.

That said, we do need to restore the integrity of the testing environment in order to insure that we are able to catch and address issues prior to them going to the live servers. Test has served well in this capacity since release, however item loot issues and other factors have reduced Test's ability to do this. Given these issues, we still feel that a complete character-wipe represents the best possible solution for continued functionality of the Test server. However, this is a case where, in the interest of keeping our current testers and players satisfied, that we need to compromise on the solution.

After our discussions today, we've decided that an item-wipe, without deleting characters, will accomplish the majority of our goal to restore the Test server as a functional testing platform. Given that, the EQLive and Operations teams are currently at work restoring all characters that were wiped yesterday, with the exception of characters known to be artificially buffed. This process should be completed later today.

Furthermore, in order to allow those that did not understand the purpose or risks associated with the Test server a chance to avoid future problems of this nature, we will be allowing built-up (not buffed) characters a one-time chance to move when the next split-server comes up, sometime in the near future. As Brad mentioned in his message earlier, we do recommend that those who are on Test more for playing, and less for Testing, seriously consider moving when we open registration. The possibility of a future wipe, whether it is a player or item wipe, is not necessarily unlikely in the future.

We hope that the players on Test will find this compromise acceptable and continue to participate in the testing process. Furthermore, we apologize for the insensitivity of our previous decision to completely wipe the server.

On behalf of the EverQuest team,

Gordon Wrinn
Internet Relations Manager
Verant Interactive, Inc.

Topic :  Test Server Patch Message

 

June 20th, 1:00pm

Character Wipe on Test

Over the past 15 months, the Test Server has been a fantastic platform for testing new and proposed changes to the live EverQuest servers. However, in the past couple of months, with the release of several new zones, plus the entire Kunark expansion, the characters on the test server, and the equipment they carry, have been further deviating from the norm that is expected on the Live servers. This has made testing certain aspects of the game more and more difficult as time goes on.

With great sympathy to those that have built their characters on Test and made the server their home, it is now necessary that we "clean the slate" on the Test server and return it to a state that will allow successful testing to take place. To that end, as of today, all characters have been deleted from the Test server. Though no reimbursement of characters, equipment, or levels is planned, strict guidelines have been established to govern future artificial buffing and equipment allocations on the test server in order to insure that the environment remains a clean testbed for enhancements proposed for the Live servers.

We would like to thank all of the Testers that have been helping on the server since release, and we hope that you will continue to do so. An email address (testserver@verant.com) has been set up to receive your comments on this issue. We will make sure that your comments are forwarded to the appropriate people.

Thank you for your understanding,

- The EverQuest Team

Keyboard Layout

Last week, the testeqgame.exe file was updated to allow users to modify their keyboard layout in order to account for some international keyboards. When the executable launches, it will create a file in your EverQuest directory named "KEYBOARD.TXT". You may modify this file using an ASCII text editor to substitute keys where appropriate.

The beginning of the file contains un-shifted keys, and the end contains the character that will be printed when the SHIFT key is depressed. Feel free to edit this file as you wish, but take care to leave any unprintable characters or spaces intact. If you make a mistake editing this file and have problems, merely delete the file. A new default version will be created the next time that testeqgame.exe is launched.

If you use a standardized keyboard layout other than QWERTY, such as the French AZERTY layout, we would be interested in seeing your version of the file. After you've modified and tested your version of the KEYBOARD.TXT file, please email it along with a description of what standard it is for to jbutler@verant.com.

- The EverQuest Team

Topic :  AE Rain Spells

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

I've read the original author's post, and from what I see it's pretty accurate. Rain spells consider all targets in their field, whether or not those targets can actually be damaged. This is the way that the rain spells work. Now if this makes them completely "useless" in all cases, perhaps we need to look at them again, but at the moment they are working as designed.

 

- Gordon

 

See Reader Articles section for an article on how AE Rain Spells work.

Topic :  Spell Book

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

The spell book obscuring the view is part of the "penalty" that lower-level casters pay in exchange for the power that they will receive later in life. From an RP perspective, the caster was so focused on meditating that they perceived little about what was happening in the world around them. Back in early beta we didn't even allow casters to speak while medding.

I think most are happy that we changed this stance in the interest of game play.

 

- Gordon

Topic :  Experience

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Experience given by a creature is based upon its level, and the zone you are in. It is not based upon its level in relation to you, other than at the point that you stop receiving experience from killing a particular level creature.

In other words, if you are level 50, a level 49 creature will give more experience than a level 48 creature, even though they are both blue.

- Gordon
