Patch Update
- Aurum @ 7:55 PM PDT
Updates were posted on the Patch Server after the servers were brought down again:

    April 17th, 5:00 pm
    ------------------------------

    ** Emergency Patch Update **

    We're sorry that we've had to patch again so quickly. We found a problem with the change we made to the Red Scabbard. This patch is intended primarily to fix that issue.


    ** Bug Fixes **

    - Buying a duplicate lore item will no longer cause you to crash.


    ** Clarification **

    To clear up some confusion, we wanted to let everyone know that we have changed the timing of the spawn of the Venril Sathir remains. It no longer appears directly after the death of Venril Sathir. And we also urge you to read the patch message below about the changes to this creature.


    Also since we're patching to fix bugs, we decided to throw in a new feature.

    - %M used in a macro will now place the name of your pet (if you have one) in the text of your macro.

    - Bag Positioning: Now bags will stay where you put them. Simply open a bag and move it to where you want it. The bag will remember it's location and open up there again next time. This will be persistent and will be remembered when you log out.

    There are two things to know about this new feature. The first is that the default position of every bag is going to be the same. So if you open one bag and then another, their windows will stack on top of each other. Don't let this alarm you, the first bag window didn't close. Just move the top bag to the desired location and you'll see the bottom one.

    The other thing to know is a bit more complicated.

    The saved locations for opening bags are linked to the bank or inventory slot that the bag is in, not to the bag itself. So if you open a bag that is sitting in your first inventory slot and move the bag window to a new location, it is the inventory slot that remembers the position and any bag opened from that inventory slot will open in the saved position. Take that bag from the first slot and move it to the second inventory slot and it will open in the location set for the second inventory slot. It will not remember where it was opened before.

    Experiment with it, it will make more sense once you try it.



    April 17th, 1:00 pm
    ------------------------------

    ** Cosmic Rift Enters Beta Testing **

    Sony Online Entertainment's latest massively multiplayer game Cosmic Rift enters open beta testing today, April 17. Cosmic rift is a multiplayer space action game where you take command of a star fighter and wage war with hundreds of other players. To take part in the beta testing, visit the Cosmic Rift web page (at http://cosmicrift.station.sony.com/) to get started.



    *** Patch Message Addendum ***

    Just a few notes to clear up some questions and some errors in the patch message.

    ** Food Changes **

    Along with the other changes mentioned in the patch message, some player-made food items will now have an effect on your character beyond just keeping him from being hungry. We'll leave it up to you to discover the rest.

    We've also added the ability to right-click food to 'force' your character to eat it. This will cause the food to be eaten immediately (though your character can only eat so much food at one time). Food eaten this way will only last half as long as food eaten naturally by the character.

    ** Run the Velious Patch **

    If you want to see the new Monk textures you will need to make the changes mentioned in the patch message. But you will also need to run the Velious Patch in order to get those textures to show up.

    ** Skewer Change **

    We didn't actually change the weight of Skewers, we changed the weight of Spits. Sorry for the confusion.


More Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:53 PM PDT
Patch Day proved, as usual, to be a very active day for Alan on the Dev Corner:

    Patch Message
    I wanted to post the patch message here and make a few comments about it.

    First of all, this might be the longest patch message I've ever seen. And there's no padding in it. :)

    Mostly I wanted to thank all of you that have sent in suggestions and comments. Many of the things that went into this patch were the direct result of your input. The items that are addressed with this patch encourages me. I'm pretty sure that this new system works well. The team has direct access to your comments and they are able to make changes based on your suggestions without having to weed through a lot of miscellaneous data.
    --

    The Dieties
    [Quote: I loved seeing this section updated but have one question.Where is Tholuxe Paels.All i know of this one is it is the god of lust (least that was what the message said in the early days of the server)nothing more is known as far as i know of this lesser deity.
    Teudar 55 enchanter TP
    Carle 46 warrior BB ]

    I was going to make mention of the new deities page. I'm glad you brought it up.

    Yes, there are a few gods missing from the list (and Drinal, who isn't a god at all.) We'll update them when we get a more in-depth description from the designers. But for now, the list is a lot more complete. I'll try to let you know when we update it in the future.

    Forging Issues
    Lybernia wrote:

    [Quote: I'm not convinced that this is working as intended. I was naked after a death and left without food and water of course. I was recieving the message that I was out of food and drink. I foraged and got food, I foraged 3 more times and got food as well. At this point I was only recieving the message about drink. I never sucesfully foraged water before getting back to my corpse and my belongings. ]

    Ok, I'm getting a lot of e-mails like this. I guess I wasn't clear enough when I explained this, so I'll try again.

    [Quote: If, and only if, you are receiving "You are hungry" messages (not if you are out of food, only if you are receiving this message) AND you successfully forage, you will automatically forage food.

    If, and only if, you are receiving "You are thirsty" messages (not if you are out of water, only if you are receiving this message) AND you successfully forage, you will automatically forage water. ]

    Being out of food or water is not going to guarantee that you will forage food or water.

    And you are not guaranteed to succeed at your foraging if you are receiving the 'hungry' or 'thirsty' messages. Your chance to succeed does not increase at all.

    For most people in most situations, the run to retrieve your corpse will not be long enough for you to start getting the hungry and thirsty messages.

    Topic of Religon
    Yegolev wrote:

    [Quote: I guess this is a general discussion of religion now, so my comment is this:

    I play on The Nameless server. I'm interested in creating a PC that worships The Nameless. I realize that the modern-day races probably have no inkling of The Nameless as an entity, but I want to run around chanting "Down with Veeshan", "Karana is a Poser" and "Tunare Go Home".

    No chance? ]

    Yup, no chance. :)

    You're correct in your evaluation (as far as I know) that most residents of Norrath probably wouldn't know who the Nameless was.

    But you could, therefore, play an agnostic character who claims to worship this god that nobody has heard of. You would be considered an agnostic by people simply because they think you worship a figment of your imagination or a myth.

    So there is a chance after all…

    Muktuk wrote:

    [Quote: As for Veeshan-worshipping bards and threatening faction: I've always thought worshipping dragonkind must be something like worshipping Cthulu--you've been devoted and faithful, sure, but you're still getting eaten eventually... ]

    /em laughs.

    [Quote: (quote)
    Here's another idea, one that kinda ties in to changing dieties, but dosen't have to. There are imbued items, that are diety specific; why not spells (not just the imbue spells) that are diety specific? Maybe wizards worshipping Solusek Ro would get better fire DD spells, for example.

    Astroshak
    (/quote)

    I saw the above in the Agnostic Iksar thread, and it sorta jived with something I randomly thought of this morning on the way to work. Instead of new spells, would it be possible to adjust the power of existing spell types based on religion? For example, a wizard who worships Solusek Ro (using the previous example) could have a bonus to fire based spells. Perhaps a wizard who worships Karana could have a bonus to rain spells. Prexus wizards could have bonuses to Ice spells, and I'm certain you could perhaps tie another deity to magic/lightning based spells, and maybe another to AoE spells. Note of course that I'm not certain of what deities Wizards can worship, so the examples might not be entirely valid.

    And you wouldn't necessarily have to make it across all levels, either, you could have the bonus only kick in after a certain level (as a Wizard grows in his/her spirituality or something) and even have a quest that might activate the power. If you can't tie the ability directly to just religion, you could just make deity specific items that boost each element or spell type's power, much like the focus items for necros and magicians, and make them questable, something to prove the wizard's devotion to their god. And by making the quests tough enough, and use combinable containers, you could perhaps prevent the quest from being completed before the wizard hits a certain level (approximately, at least).

    Such a focus type ability and/or item could help wizards' complaints about mana efficiency, and also help bring to the foreground the nature of their deity and the roleplaying aspects of such. It could be a great way to inject more story and more personality to some deities.

    Tenbil En`Haldar ]

    Sure, that's possible. But, actually, something like that would probably be harder to balance, as it affects a large portion of the characters abilities rather than just making new spells that can more easily be balanced.

    And when we're talking about adding spells or abilities for casters that are deity specific (and I noted that you broadened it to int casters, when my first thought was just wis casters), you have to think of Rogues that worship Brell, and Warriors that worship Cazic and any and all other combinations. Sure, after looking at all the combinations you might decide to restrict to just Wis casters at the end, but you have to go through all those other possibilities.

    I rather like the idea, personally. But the amount of work that would have to be done and the potential for making some sort of error would be huge and would certainly make me hesitant to do it.

    Continued Discussion on Population/Server Issues
    Crim wrote:

    [Quote: Absor, I've been following your responses on the topic of item decay and want to point out one aspect about it.

    You point out, correctly in my opinion, that item decay in EQ as it currently stands would be a disaster because of the tedium involved. But, the major tedium factor for item decay is due to EQ's overemphasis on making items rare. I have played games where item decay worked and worked quite well.

    Simply put, item decay works in an environment that places less emphasis on the rarity of items. I'm not talking about adjusting the risk/reward of encounters. Instead, I'm talking about increasing the availability of items such that it isn't tedious to obtain new items as your old ones decay.

    Not only would you essentially destroy the vast ebay market for items but you would encourage player transactions because of the increase in items and need for items. You would also discourage the twinking problem because of the need for items at appropriate levels.

    For example, imagine the ghoul lord dropped an SSB and a ykesha every time he spawned (and he spawned every time). But, ykeshas and SSBs decayed after a certain amount of time (or dropped to a lower effectiveness etc.). Basically then not only would the dungeon crawling experience be better (i.e. placeholders are a hack way of limiting item availability and ruin a good dungeon crawl) but there would be an active market for those classes who would use ykeshas.

    Anyway, I've rambles on long enough but I hope I have gotten my point across. If you take away the tedium in replacing decayed/destroyed items, then you can make a workable (and in fact better) system involving items. ]

    I understand your point. But I think maybe you're missing something.

    If ykeshas, for example, were to decay (becoming less effective) and at the same time more available to compensate, this wouldn't be as good of a thing as it might seem.

    You would spend as much time, if not more, camping to get your ykesha. You would be heading back to the same place to get a new one at levels beyond the point where getting the item is a challenge. Some people do that now, but everyone would have to do it if their ykesha was wearing out and they needed a new one. You'd be seeing more high level people working lower level dungeons. A lot of people don't want to spend their time re-acquiring and item in a place that they've already seen enough of when they were at the right level to be challenged by it.

    [Quote: Chadrack's ideas would have profound(not necesarily bad) effects on item-loot pvp.

    -Goladus ]

    Very good point. I'll have to think about the repercussions on PvP. But it's obvious that the ability to turn things No-Drop would have a tremendous effect on PvP where looting items is possible.

    [Quote: Alan,
    Thank you for your concern about this issue.
    - Doesn't the idea of monsters being "camped" around the clock offend you? This is a game of questing, and I can see making people have to do long, involved quests, involving travel, fighting, and almost anything else. But the idea of just sitting, waiting for a quest monster to appear, bothers me. ]

    Offend me? No. Bother me? Yes. But, and I know you've heard me say this before, nobody is forced to sit around and wait for an NPC to spawn. You may have to do that if you want the item that everyone tells you is important, but no item is required for any character. If you don't want to sit and wait for an NPC, go do something else.

    Yes, the fact that people sit and wait around for NPCs to spawn does bother me. But it doesn't offend me. I don't do it, so how could it offend me? :)

    [Quote: - I think that making all items NODROP is, as you said, not feasable. Making a lot more, not all, items NODROP/LORE may be a good thing. The player I am thinking of here is one like myself, up and coming, untwinked, looking to discover the world you have created. Making that person contend with an upper-level power player or guild who is camping what, to me, is a unique, special quest item, is troublesome.]

    Making more items No-Drop would not necessarily reduce the desire for those items. It just means that people that are now 'farming' them for their alternate characters would have to be more creative to get the items. And they are such an artificial thing, so contrary to the reality of Norrath (why is it that I can't give away this Kobold Molar? It's just a tooth.) that I hesitate to ever suggest making more of them. Personally, I'd love to be able to remove them. I'd love to think that everyone would go get their own when the time came, rather than farming items for people that aren't high enough level to earn the item. But that's not a reality.

    [Quote: - I believe your loot rules in the Warrens are trying to address these issues, and thank you for that. Have you considered simply placing level floors and ceilings on items? ]

    Sure, we've considered it. But that's no more 'realistic' than No-Drop items, and has the same drawbacks. People would still 'farm' the items, they would just hold them until their characters were high enough level to use them.

    [Quote: - How about limiting members to one character per server? ]

    That's something that we've talked about on a server with new rules. We're certainly not going to force people to delete characters off of existing servers. But there are all kinds of arguments for and against this idea. I love to play a lot of characters, and I have one for every mood. I'd be restricted to just one mood on each server. :)

    [Quote: Thank you,
    Manus
    Vallon Zek ]

    Thank you.

Patch Message: 04-17-01
- Aurum @ 12:31 PM PDT
A large new message was added to the Patch Server this morning:

    April 17th, 3:00 am
    ------------------------------

    ** Food Changes **

    - Food items now have a 'duration'. This means that some food items will not sustain a character as long as others, and some items will sustain your character longer than they did before.


    ** Bug Fixes **

    - We THINK we've fixed the problem with the Dusty Werebat. This fellow has been steadfastly refusing to appear where and when we ask him to. We've worked out a deal with his agent, and we think he'll be back to work with this patch.

    - Corrected an anomaly with hit point bars that occurred when a character was healed or damaged by spells. This could be seen, for example, when a character was healed and the hit point bar would go up and then quickly go back down a bit right afterwards. There was no error in the assignment of healing or damage previously, just in the way it was being displayed.

    - Fixed the visual bracer bug where other people would see a different bracer graphic than you would. Now everyone (including the wearer) will always see the same bracer texture. If you have two bracers that have a different look (one is leather and one is steel, for example), make sure that the one you want to show up graphically is on your right wrist inventory slot, because this is the one that the game will choose to show for you (unless you take off the left one and put it back on, which will show the left one, but that is only temporary, and it will go back to showing the right one after you zone or reenter the game).

    - Fixed zoning problem in Nurga to Droga that caused characters to be facing the wrong way when they zoned, often causing confusion that lead to accidentally zoning back the way they had come. You should be facing the right way when you make that zone transition now.


    ** Zone and Quest Changes **

    - The marauding Tar Goos will no longer be assisted by the Geonids. This should make at least one quest more available.

    - Grobb should now have a couple of ovens. Hopefully this will allow Troll bakers to keep their fellows full of carbs and ready to fight.

    - Fixed a bug that occurred when selling stacks of items. Previously, if you had 4 bat wings and sold them one by one to the merchant, the merchant would have 4 bat wings for you to buy, but if you sold all four at once to the merchant he would only have one for you to buy. Now he will have 4 in either case.

    - We have increased the spawn rate of Venril Sathir and his skeletal remains. Please note that only Rangers and Druids will be able to trigger the spawn for the skeletal remains, and those remains will only be dropping items for those epic quests. Hopefully these changes will dramatically reduce the competition and resentment over this spawn.

    - We have reduced resistances to some spell types on some NPCs in Sky Shrine and the Tower of Frozen Shadow. This is the beginning of our overview of the resistances of NPCs in Velious.


    ** Improved and New Features **

    - When you /consent someone it will give them the ability to drag all of your corpses.

    - You can now buy stacks of combinable items (such as rations, water, etc...) from merchants. It works the same way as sell in that you can hold down shift to buy a full stack and control to buy just one item.

    - Added the ability to change the color of all windows at one time with /wincolor (all windows that can be changed, that is). You can now use /wincolor ALL 0 0 100 2, for example, instead of having to set each window separately.

    - Right-clicking items while looting will 'auto-equip' them. This will work just as if you had picked the items up and dropped them onto the 'auto-equip' area of your character profile. So if there is already a piece of equipment on your character in the spot where the looted item would normally go, it will drop into your inventory, if you have room for it.

    - The tab key will no longer toggle between you and your last target when the chat bar is up. When the chat bar is open the tab key will take you through the list of your most recent /tell names or /reply names. When the chat bar is closed the tab key will switch you between yourself and your last target.

    - /assist has an added parameter. /assist on and /assist off will determine whether or not your character auto-attacks when you use /assist. So if you type /assist off, from that time forward using /assist will NOT engage your character's auto-attack. This change will be persistent, so it will remain as you left it when you log out. /assist also now has a range of 200 feet.

    - The destroy confirmation requestor now shows the name of the item you are trying to destroy, or it just says money if you are destroying coins. This should greatly reduce the chance of accidentally deleting the wrong item. PLEASE read the text in the destroy confirmation box to be certain that you are destroying the right item before choosing to destroy it.

    - Hailing Player Character will now say 'Hail Playername', rather than just 'hail'.

    - Added /charinfo command. Currently this command only reports the zone where the current character is bound. We may add further information as things progress.

    - You will now see how much damage your character has taken when he is hit by a spell that causes instant damage (DOT damage is not reported).


    ** Item Changes **

    - We've made a change to the Red Scabbard that should relieve most of the problems warriors were having with their epic weapon occasionally disappearing during conversion between the two-handed weapon and the two one-handed weapons. What will happen now is rather simple. The very next time you use the container to combine the weapons (or break them apart), the old scabbard will disappear and a new one will replace it.

    THE NEW SCABBARD AND THE SWORDS WILL APPEAR ON YOUR CURSOR, rather than in your inventory. So once you have combined them, make sure to place them in your inventory. And don't panic, the items didn't disappear. :) But also MAKE CERTAIN THAT YOU HAVE ROOM IN YOUR INVENTORY FOR ALL THREE ITEMS (the two swords and the scabbard, which is a container and needs to be able to fit into your main inventory). If you end up with a No-Drop container on your cursor with no place to put it in your inventory, you will have a problem...

    Also, right now the new scabbard is a little bugged. Right now you won't be able to open the new scabbard until you camp out once after it is created. We're working on fixing that bug. On Using /Report
- Aurum @ 7:54 PM PDT
Alan spoke on the subject of /report on the Dev Corner today. Here is his complete post:

    I wanted to give you all a brief explanation about how to use /report.

    When you have a problem in game where there is text describing the problem in your chat window, /report allows you to send that information to us. This information comes directly off the server and is stored to a database here, so there is no concern about anyone using /report to cheat. This is the most reliable way to get your information to us.

    The most common example for using this feature is when someone is harassing you. Use /report to send us the offensive text and then send in a /petition with full details of the incident (include the fact that you've used /report as well, so the GMs know that it's there).

    /report will only send us the text that is currently visible in your chat window. So make certain that the text you want us to see is visible when you use /report. Scroll up until you have what you need in the chat window.

    Also, the syntax for /report is /report . The is the name of the person you are reporting about. If someone is harassing you, use their name. But if you are reporting something that happened to you, such as a bug that made you fall for 10,000 points of damage, then put your character's name after /report.

    Nothing else is needed with /report. Please don't type /report and then a lot of text explaining your concern. Use /petition to explain the concern.

    Correct use of the /report feature can make it much easier for us to get things straightened out when there is a problem.

    Alan
