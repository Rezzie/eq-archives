Handing In Quest Items, Part 2
- Aurum @ 7:31 PM PST
Yesterday's thread started by Alan on handing in quest items has been split. Here's the latest response from Alan on the subject:

    [Quote from original post: Disco. Your argument for multi-questing really is just in reaction to a problem with the quest itself. The newbie shackle should be given out to any monk who asks the right NPC. Problem solved.]

    And there would undoubtedly be a few cases like this that would have to be fixed up. But they should be few enough and easily fixed. And, well, perhaps we should make this change anyway. I'll mention it to the team.

    Ludovic wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I meet a new NPC! Never spoken to me, because I hadn't done an earlier part of the quest.. I hand him a rusty dagger...
    I'm SORRY this is NOT what I asked for!
    *DING* I just discovered that this NPC is a Quest NPC]

    Then the NPCs should only react that way to the specific items that they are looking for. If you hand them at least one of the items they seek, but not all of them, then they return them. They should still accept any other sort of gifts.

    Good point.

    Brewhaus wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I don't get it Absor, basically you guys want to get rid of multi-questing, but since that will seem to be a nerf your willing to throw the users this bone. Why don't you just say that you want to nerf multi-questing? Why can't you leave multi-questing and add this. Really, it's not that hard. If your programmers can't figure out how to do it, all they gotta do is ask someone. I could think of many ways to go about this, for example, A pop up box. "You have turned in the wrong or incomplete items, would you like them back?".]

    Right now we don't know if it is our intent to change multi-questing. That would be the whole point of this thread.

    Despite your misdirected anger, you might have a good idea. I'll mention it to the team.

    Ludovic wrote:

    [Quote from original post: If noone in the group can use the item, and since multiquesting is out... They will shout to zone, and some newbie...*not a twinked secondary* just got half of what they need for an uber item... qll they need do now is weight around the other drops for the same and bingo they just skipped 30...50 levels worth of upgrading to the next best thing...]

    How is that different than it is now? Is it somehow less acceptable that some random person gets to be twinked than some high level person's twink? Is it less acceptable that the low level character get the item than the high level character that can't be bothered to show up and get the item himself?

    And the chances of someone getting a quest done in its entirety this way at a level way too low is extremely slim. And removing multi-questing will not have much of an effect on this anyway. Those high level people wouldn't be hunting that NPC unless one of them wants the item anyway.

    Hasuko wrote:

    [Quote from original post: -This is a multiplayer game that is supposed to encourage grouping and personal interraction. Multi-questing is a part of that.]

    Not really. It's not part of 'grouping' at all. If I'm grouped with you, I can loot the item in question and there is no multi-questing needed. Though I can see that it does promote interaction in some ways between people that might not have met otherwise. But those cases are rare. For the most part, muti-questing is done by people that know each other already.

    [Quote from original post: -Loss of items is regrettable, but I don't think they occur with all that much frequency that would warrant changing this aspect of the game.]

    If it didn't happen with some frequency, we wouldn't be talking about it. It certainly happens enough to make it worth talking about.

    [Quote from original post: -The real problem lies with higher level people camping hard to get items (i.e. The Ring of the Ancients) and then auctioning them in East Commons or GFay at the expense of other players working hard to get the items. That cheapens some quests.]

    Agreed. Obviously my personal opinion (again, personal, meaning just me) is that multi-questing is one of those things that, in general, cheapens the game. It allows people to basically split quests up so that the guy getting the reward isn't even doing most of the questing. But that's just my personal opinion.

    [Quote from original post: -However, if two or more friends work together to complete a quest and turn in the items so one person gets the reward, then that is well within the spirit of player interraction.]

    Well, those friends are not working together if they are multi-questing. They are working separately. They are not interacting at all. If they were together doing each part of the quest, they wouldn't need to multi-quest.

    [Quote from original post: -Another example: Guildmember-01 is in a raid in the castle in Mistmoore, and they get the werewolf claws. Guildmember-02 is a warrior that needs crafted armor. Guildmember-02 buys the rubies, then Guildmember-01 turns in the werewolf claws and Guildmember-02 turns in the rubies and gets the crafted BP is a perfectly legitimate example of teamwork and the spirit of multi-player cooperation.]

    Yes, that is an example of teamwork. And I'm not saying that it's a bad thing. But it is basically circumventing the quest (again MY opinion, not that of the team).

    The concern that someone mentioned, the link-dead group member that just can't loot the item that he did work hard to achieve, that's a real concern for me. That person would not be able to get the item (or have someone get it for him), and would lose the chance. In this case, I'm in favor of multi-questing.

    Gestaltmind wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Why are you even bothering asking us when you have almost certainly already made up your mind?]

    Then why bother replying if you are so convinced that we've already made up our minds?

    You'll note that we want to talk about this. When we are set to change something, we'll just do it. Some decisions can't be made by a committee of 300,000. We'll make those decisions and move on. But in cases where we want input, we ask for it. The fact that you assume we don't want to hear your opinion is both silly and false.

    AuntieShaynta wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Third things third: eliminating multi-questing is wrong. There are at least 2 quests I'm aware of that virtually require it. The "Orc Illegible Cantrip" quest in West Freeport that requires 2 of a Lore Item to be handed in, and a necro quest which I believe runs from Paineel that requires good faction with Halas (don't know details, just that a friend has asked me to help him with this).]

    I would argue that those quests are broken if they _require_ multi-questing to complete, and should be changed anyway, regardless of what happens here. By themselves, such quests are not really a good reason not to remove multi-questing. But, again, just my opinion (I'm gonna say that a lot, since I'm only representing myself, and no decision has been made by the team on this yet).

    Mr_Vandemar wrote:

    [Quote from original post: This will make at least one quest impossible to complete. The Necromancer "Mantle of Souls" quest from Paineel. It requires that you get a potion from a Barbarian in Halas.]

Handing In Quest Items
- Aurum @ 6:30 PM PST
This is news to note for those attempting to hand in a quest item:

    I wanted to talk to all of you about the way turning in quest items works. We are thinking about making a change here, and I'd like to talk about it.

    Let me set up a fictional example. Let's say that there is a quest NPC that gives out the final item for the quest in exchange for three other items. Currently, if you give the NPC two of the correct items and a third item that you think is the correct item but isn't, the NPC will take all of the items and give you nothing. This seems like a rude thing for the NPC to do.

    Also, if someone has come along and given the NPC one of the three items before you arrive, and you give him the three correct items all at once he'll give you the quest result, but the person that gave the one item previously will get nothing (the NPC is, basically, letting you complete the transaction that he started).

    Both of these situations cause someone to lose out. That's not something that we like.

    We've been thinking about making a change to the way this whole turning in quest items thing works. The most reasonable and eloquent solution to this problem would be to simply have the NPC give the items back if they are not enough to complete the quest. This would mean that if you give him one item, he would give it back to you and tell you that he needs more than just that item to give you what you want. If you give him two correct items and an incorrect item, he would give the quest items back to you and tell you that you made a mistake (he might or might not give you back the mistaken item, depending on the technical ability to get him to do that).

    This would fix a bunch of problems with questing. But, as you all have probably guessed, it would eliminate the ability to 'multi-quest'. We know that the whole concept of multi-questing is a touchy one.

Producer Letter: Customer Service

    Communication will be the key and, naturally, it’s a two way street. Your concerns have been voiced to us, and we are responding.
