Kunark

From EQVault Wiki

The continent of Kunark was introduced in The Ruins of Kunark expansion in March 2000.

Burning Woods
On the continent of Kunark, this once verdant forest is now riddled with the aftermath of a huge comet or meteor. This place is home to a myriad of creatures, including a huge nest of hornets, named and unnamed wurms, huge apes and gorillas, sarnaks, and undead.

Over the meteror crater, Giants built a fort, which was a very popular place to hunt in its day.

There is a very old joke concerning BW, which concerns where it is located at any given time, which stemmed from a teasing player telling a newbie that it Burning Woods moved to a different location at different times.

Cabilis is the city of the Iksar.
Cabilis East
Cabilis West

Cabilis, or New Sebilis, as it is often called, is the new heart of the Iksar Empire. The Iksar recaptured the city long ago and have since made it their new home. Theirs is an ongoing effort to tame the surrounding areas to ensure the safety of the city. The traveler can find all the castes of Iksar here: crusaders, hierophants, kloks, vessels, keepers, nihilists, troopers, and monk trainers. One can even find the occasional froglok slave. The area around Cabilis is a swamp, with giant mosquitoes, scorpions, and leeches.

The Iksar have, for centuries now, focused internally on rebuilding their culture and society, to be stronger and to be able to face the outside world again. That time has come, and the Iksar are now pushing back into the lands where they once ruled supreme.

There are four areas immediately surrounding the city that are of intense interest to the Iksar. First is the Field of Bone, a barren area that contains the ancient remains of Kurn's Tower and Kaesora. The Iksar are focusing many of their broodlings in this area in order to tame it.

The Swamp of No Hope is an area that the Iksar currently are letting run wild, despite maintaining a small outpost to protect their walls.

Warsliks Wood is similar, as the Iksar have met much resistance from both the goblins and the forest giants within during their attempts to control the area. There is also a strong outcast presense there.

The Lake of Ill Omen, however, is another area that the Iksar are very focused on regaining control over. A new enemy, the sarnak, have established a fortress within this region and are expanding their range slowly, coming into conflict with the residents of Cabilis and also the goblins of the region.

Charasis (Howling Stones or City of Kunzar )
This ancient city can be found in the canyons of the Overthere. The entrance is guarded by scopicus. A froglok slave, Drugoz, escaped, taking keys to the tomb with him, giving access to intrepid adventurers. Drusella Sathir is buried here, but can still be found in this haunted city, surrounded by her undead companions. In the city you will find golems, devourers, helot undead, sepulcher undead, skeletons, embalming fluid, undead oblations, crypt workers, undead oblations, swirling goo, a Golem master, and Drusella Sathir.

From a tome looted from the Dark Elf Spy in Northern Ro:

Nestled deep in the plains of the Overthere rests the cliff city of Charasis. This city of grandeur was home to the mighest of all Iksar tribes, the Kunzar.

Charasis is also recorded as the birthplace of the great Emperor Venril Sathir the uniter. Although Emperor Sathir ruled from the Kunzar city of Sebilis, he was rumored to have stored his most valuable possesions within the library of Charasis.

Emperor Sathir's beloved, Drusella, rests within a grand crypt in Charasis' depths. After her funeral and burial, Emperor Sathir cast all citizens from the cliff city. Only the royal guard and servants where allowed to stay, forever.

These Iskar were never again to feel the warmth of the sun or bask in the moonlight. It is written that Emperor Sathir crafted a spell to hide the entrance of Charasis from raiders and those who dared to steal his knowledge and defile his beloved Drusella's crypt. Emperor Sathir would visit the cliff city frequently. What he would do inside is not known.

Upon the death of one of Emperor Sathir's royal guard was revealed the secret to gaining entry into Charasis. Upon a tattered scroll did the guard record the secret. The scroll is now lost to the world. Taken as pure babble by the mortician, it was cast aside and lost to time.

After Emperor Sathir's death his son and new emperor, Rile is the last to hold the secret of entry. The ancient cult of Emperor Sathir known as the Arisen are rumored to have acquired the lost scroll of the royal guard. They are said to care for the crypt of the late Emperor Sathir.

It was during the time known as the End Days that the spirits of Tunare, Underfoot and the Rainkeeper destroy most of the great cities of the ancients. Charasis and its guardians and treasures are lost forever. May those entombed within guard her secrets from invaders.

So let it be known that Darkmask and Pizo have found a secret treasure, hidden in the Overthere, a land of many dangers, but apparently it yields quite a reward. To the library we must recover these lost items. (History of Norrath. SOE)

A locked Kunark dungeon (requires a key obtained via a quest involving two mobs around level 40), filled mainly with various undeads.

One of the specificity of Charasis is the multiple keys necessary to progress in the dungeon (unless you have a rogue friend that can pick the doors): there are 4 wings (North, South, East, West).

The North and East wings are the only ones that can be opened without a key, but the East Wing's stairs are trapped.

To progress in Charasis, one has to get West Wing's key from the North Wing's boss, then South Wing's key from the West Wing's boss, and from South Wing take a secret passage to East Wing where Drusella Sathir (the zone's boss, involved in many Kunark quests including the Veeshan's Peak key and the Iksar Shadow Knight's epic) is.

On top of two wings being locked, every wing's boss (but Drusella) is behind an additional locked door which requires a key found somewhere on the floor of the zone. Charasis' wings keys are all NO RENT, meaning that they're non-permanent items.

In 2004, a permanent Master Key (the Hand of Glory, able to open every door of Charasis) was added, allowing non-rogues players to bypass the regular and fairly annoying progression through the dungeon. Creating this key requires 5 items (one being a container) found in the various wings of Charasis.

Chardok is the site of the ancient sarnak military fortress that has existed for centuries since the time of Emperor Atrebe, and is considered by the sarnaks to be their home. The Sarnak Collective and the Brood of Di’Zok have their headquarters here. Many Di’Zok can be found inside, along with various Iksar and Ratmen slaves, Chokidai, and the various castes of the Sarnak culture. This place is home to the Overking Bathezid, Prince Selrach Di’Zok, Queen Velazul Di’zok, and the socerer of Di’Zok.

Chardok, Halls of Betrayal (Chardok B) is originally one of several "pocket zones" (zones which were appended to existing dungeons on a temporary, but as yet continuing, basis) Chardok B is populated by many undead Sarnaks as well as living Sarnaks loyal to Korocust, the hardest monster in the zone. The zone follows the theme of a haunted mine.

City of Mist (Torsis) is a haunted city for mid-level players. Found deep inside the Emerald Jungle in Kunark, the City of Mist is a lost Iksaar city Torsis. A great city with many levels, it was the pride of the Iskar Empire. Now it is home to many types of golems and undead, slimy goos, and other creatures. Adventurers can also find the lich Lhranc, and a number of reaver lords.

Crypt of Dalnir located in Warslik's Woods is the hidden burial tomb of Dalnir, the ancient Haggle Baron of the City of Cabilis. The crypt is controlled by Kly, originally a slave of Dag the Blasphemer Iksar, who made the magical dagger with the forge of Dalnir. Kly and a band of crusaders disappeared with the dagger, never to be seen again. They have taken over the tomb and expanded it for their own, currently unknown, purposes. The coerced of every race who have fallen under the Kly’s sway protect the upper floors while the Kly continue their experiments in the tunnels below. One can also find goo, undead, and ravenous nibblers patrolling the halls. (EqAtlas)

Dreadlands is the area of mountains and plains surrounding the ancient Karnor's Castle, the home of Venril Sathir and his drolvarg army, n the middle of the zone. Scattered with ruins, the area includes an area of forest inhabited by the Drachnids, as well as the edge of a massive glacier extending down from the mountains, where one can find Mountain giants and tundra Yeti. Buried within a secluded valley is an ancient outpost of the Combine Empire. One may find the ferocious Drovlags, or ycanthropes here, as well as undead and cockatrices. (EqAtlas)

Twin spires serve as the teleportation foci for wizards and druids, but are now nearly obsolete.

Emerald Jungle A vast, beautiful, and dangerous jungle. It is separated from the rest of the continent by a large cliff where it is sunken into the earth. A grad staircase allows travelers to descend to the jungle floor. An ancient city, the City of Mist, lies hidden in the twisted trees. A dangerous place, one must be on the lookout for tigers, huge apes, soulsippers (giant mosquitoes), iksars, undead, Errolosi bloodthorns and mantraps, which are flesh earting plants, and trakanosuars. (EqAtlas)

Field of Bone is the starting zone for the Iksars. It is home to Kurn's Tower, a newbie hunting ground for undead and living creatures. The Field of Bone is the original site of a vast reservoir for the city of Cabilis and the nearby structures. Long ago, however, the fatal battle between Galat the Ikasar and Jaled Dal the dragon was held in the skies above and the magical energies unleashed there forever charred the surface of the earth. The skeleton of Galat's steed, a hybrid of froglok and dragon, can still be seen there. Buryai, Iksar outcasts, wolf hunters, undead, scorpions, scarabs, spiders, and Iksar guards can be found here.


Firiona Vie is known also as FV, is a zone bordering on several other Kunark zones. The city of Firiona Vie was once a High Elven outpost, and the destination of the Elven boats from Timorous Deep. Following the war with the drolvargs, however, the elves fled to the east side of the zone. The zone itself is in fact much larger than the city which occupies only a fraction of it.

The Land of Firiona Vie was established by the High Elves under the direction of King Thex to the lands of the Iskar. Dominating the outpost is a statue of Firiona Vie, avatar of Tunare. Beyond the outpost, a forest stretches. Ancient Iksar statues and structures dot the landscape. The beach area near the outpost teems with evil drachnids. Pilgrims, drixies, pirates, forest giants, leeches, and goblins roam the woods. Sometimes an ancient Jarsath skeleton walks the forests here. Deep in the forest is a giant outpost, and beyond that, a drolvarg village in an ancient temple. The ruins are infested with frogloks.

The guards dislike wolf form of any kind, so watch out!

Frontier Mountains are the highest and roughest set of mountains located on the continent. These harsh mountains cut across the western half of the continent, and are inhabited by a variety of powerful, large creatures, for whom the peaks are mere hills. Many of these creatures operate vast mining operations in the mountains for the minerals that lie beneath them, and the adventurerer may find the Mines of Nurga and the Temple of Droga in these mountains. The unwary may be attacked by goblins, buryai miners, mountain giants, sarnaks, or gorging brutes. The mountain giants have established a huge fort in the middle of the area. (Eq Atlas)

Kaesora is the ruins of an old underground city led by a Sarnak named Xalgoz who holds loot required to build the key to Charasis. Deep within this zone is a ruined library now guarded by ghosts, spirits and feasters.

Karnor's Castle is the home of Venril Sathir, a boss mob which features prominently in a number of epic encounters, as well as many undead creatures. Karnor's is famous for having a large number of trains that continually disrupt adventuring in the area. Expect to hear shouts of "Train Left!" or "Train Right!" and be ready to move. (A train, in EQ terms, is large group of monsters, from 3 or 4 to as many as 20 or more, chasing a player who is trying to "zone", or leave the area.)

Kurn's Tower

Lake of Ill Omen is the ancient site of the city of Varnek, which was cursed ages ago and has since sank beneath the waters, giving the local area its name. The reptilian sarnak have moved into the area and have secured an ancient fortress to the southwest. The goblins have established a mine in the hills in north of the lake. The Brood of Kotiz has taken it upon themselves to eradicate the sarnak from the area, and have directed their trainees to do so.

Mines of Nurga was revamped from a low level dungeon to a 55+ level dungeon. Houses mostly goblins.

Old Sebilis ("Seb") is the home of Trakanon, a raid-encounter dragon for various epic quests and good loot. Sebilis has many levels to it, housing live and undead frogloks on the upper floors, live frogloks and myconids (mushrooms) in the sewers below, and golems and live froglocks along the path toward Trakanon. A key is required in order to enter Sebilis, obtainable from a quest completed outside in Trakenon's Teeth.

Skyfire Mountains is a rocky, fiery outdoor zone with dragons and wurms galore. Home of the red dragon Talendor. Items for the Cleric Epic 1.0 drop here, so you will often see solo clerics and groups searching for "pearlescent shards."

Swamp of No Hope is also a starting zone for Iksars, although not as popular as the Field of Bone.

Temple of Droga was revamped from a low level dungeon to a 55+ level dungeon. It is adjacent to the Mines of Nurga.

The Overthere contains an outpost for the evil races to use as a base in their exploration of Kunark. It is a large zone populated with beasts and living plants, as well as wandering Sarnak. Charasis, and Chardok are both located in the Overthere, and it is a direct route to the Skyfire Mountains and the Frontier Mountains.

Timorous Deep is the ocean zone of Kunark. A raid encounter dragon lives here, and various islands occupy hunting and quest opportunities for players.

Trakanon's Teeth is commonly remembered as the place where Sebilis is found. Trakanon's Teeth is a Kunark jungle, much resembling the Emerald Jungle, and houses various ruins, rivers and lakes. The answers to finding the key that allows access to Sebilis may be found within.

Veeshan's Peak When The Ruins of Kunark was released, Veeshan's Peak was the hardest zone of the game. It is the fortress of the Ring of Scale faction of dragons and is inhabited by six dragons: Druushk, Hoshkar, Nexona, Phara Dar, Silverwing and Xygoz. To access the zone, you need to do a long and difficult quest to obtain a key. Players are then able to pass through the portal located in Skyfire Mountains. Once inside, players have to keep moving constantly as it full of Racnar and Lava Drake which are quickly (2 minute respawn timer) replaced once they are killed. When the zone was new, teleportation was not possible inside the zone, so players had to clear the zone to the end to get out of it. This restriction was lifted several years later, and players can now exit the zone by any of the usual methods. Recently the zone had been revamped and a new, though no less difficult, a quest has been added for obtaining the key.

Veksar was rumored to have been originally an all underwater dungeon similar to Kedge Keep in the original Kunark expansion, but limitations in underwater AI caused the zone to be dropped from release. It was later added sometime around the Planes of Power expansion.
