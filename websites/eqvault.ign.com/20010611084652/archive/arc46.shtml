POVAR NEWBIE EVENT - 20 September 2000, 7PM EST

I awoke today to find my older sister missing from her bunk. I have searched the area, and now suspect foul play. This just cracks me up:

On September 18th, the year of our Lord 2000 at exactly 11:50:32 PM PST
Dwynn Stormhammer of Arcane Legacy performed an outstanding feat beyond
comparison: The Snipage of Post 10000 on Rodcet Nife's notorious Rant
thread. Dwynn's own words best describe the event:

"Our goal has been reached

It has been a long time since Orb firs posted his Rant, which grew and
flourished into the monstrosity known as Rant. Friendships have been
formed, and many laughs have been had.

I would like to thank my brother, Cadmus, and my friend Baglo. Without
these two guys, life would be bleak and no fun at all.

I can't leave out props to Wislem and Gegtik, some of the funniest Gnomes

snipe. I only regret that you couldn't be here.

Rant will finally die.

Newebie Event on Xegony
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 8:10 PM PDT
I received the following from Dirge:

Cresting the last of several gentle hills, the exiled
Teir’dal woman narrowed her eyes, focusing on the
stoic grandeur of the walls of her second home…
Qeynos. Amorsaylnn Frost smiled to herself. The
humans had come to accept her, though many still
harbored some doubts about her intentions. Her musing
was cut short by a cry of pain. "Ahhhgggg…" It
emerged from a young man, leaning against the
unforgiving stones of the wall.

Amor turned, fumbling in her pack for spare bandages,
but his wounds were quite serious, beyond her ability
to mend. He tried to speak. "Those mangy dogs put up
a tough fight. If I don’t make it back to the Clan
House soon, it’ll all be over for me." There was a
brave, determined look in his eyes. Amor had seen
death many times in her short life, but most often
people crumpled, whimpering for forgiveness and
safety. This man was different, strong and would
accept death if she chose to embrace him.

"What dogs?" Amor asked, trying to support him.

He answered between gasps for breath. "It’s those
Darkpaws… They’ve beat me pretty badly… but they’ll
get theirs soon enough!"

Amorsaylnn was not very interested in politics. Truth
be told, she was very much a mercenary, having been
hired by another Teir’dal known as Shivrehel Despana
and his House, Winterborne. She had fled Neriak in
the night, sent by her father, Makaz, to find this
Teir’dal and follow his path. As it turned out, he
had strange connections with Lady Vox, the mythical
dragoness of the North. Now she found herself
traveling frequently with his consort, a barbarian
witch by the name of Shar Hel Despana… and they made a
good team.

But being near Shiver meant hearing a lot of rumor and
suggestion. He was a politician, at heart. The last
bit of news she had heard before heading out to the
southern reaches of the Karanas was about a man named
Tomer Instogle, a member of the Silent Fist Clan. He
was missing since he helped try and defend the Qeynos
Gates against the gnoll forces.

Amor knew there had been no formal alliance between
the monks of the Silent Fist and House Winterborne,
but they were, in theory, given to a similar purpose.
They were sworn to upholding the strength of Qeynos.
She whispered to the wounded man. "I will take you to
your Clan House, Tomer." He asked that she carry his
backpack for him. It was the least they could do. In
moments, she had escorted the monk to his House and
handed his backpack to his brothers that came to
assist.

Master Seta Bakindo was greatly relieved. "We thought
that was the last we’d seen of him. Thank you!"

The Teir’dal nodded, smiling slightly. "It was the
least I could do. House Winterborne is quite
interested in assisting the other noble Houses of
Qeynos. These gnolls have caused much harm. We would
be your allies, if you are interested."

Tomer was overcome with joy. "Oh, you have the
makings of a true hero.. The Silent Fist Clan is proud
to have you as ally. May your soul guide and protect
you through these chaotic times!"

Gordon Infobits
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:56 PM PDT
More information was posted by Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board this evening. Here's the scoop:

  • Patch Hints
    [Quote from original post: Since the patch was delayed, can ya give us any clues as to what we can expect? From what Ive read from you so far it sound exciting.]

    A slew of enhancements and a handful of nerfs that we couldn't avoid any longer. I'm hoping that everyone will keep the posting rules in mind tomorrow.

  • BoB Tournament Rewards
    [Quote from original post: Does this ring fair to Nanak who won the BoB warrior tournament? He got a namechange, the level 60 one, and nothing more than that. In all fairness he should be given a very powerful special weapon that nobody else in the game can have as a reward for winning that

    Please Verant I think you owe it to him. ]

    Why? The reward for the competition is winning the competition itself. It's an event put on for the higher level folk who wish to offer themselves as the best of the best. Why should an additional reward be necessary?

  • Keeping Your Cool
    I'd really appreciate it if people didn't respond to those posts from people who think that they are "going out in style" by coming in here and spamming racial slurs and profantiy. There's nothing "stylish" about it, and replying to their posts only encourage them.

  • FBSS
    [Quote from original post: Please make the FBSS no drop.]

    We really don't like the idea of going around and making every desired item NODROP. Brad made a post a couple of days ago to that effect.

    In any case, I doubt that it will be as valuable after tomorrow. We found a bug with the way "haste" items stack with "haste" spells. Basically, even the worst haste item combined with the worst haste spell would put the wearer at the haste cap. This basically invalidated the better haste items and spells, and explains why people have been saying that the newer haste items aren't any better than the FBSS.

    This is being fixed in tomorrow's patch.

  • Disable Destroy Button During Looting
    [Quote from original post: In most situations we are forced to speed loot to avoid futher deaths. I have destroyed several items in these situations, items that where difficult to acquire or expensive to purchase. I realize that there is a Button to confirm item destruction, but thats just another button to have to click during loots. Most of the time in dungeons the game lags bad enough without having to deal with more twitching.]

    I'd have a hard time convincing the guys that yet another level of security is needed here. Sure, I've accidently destroyed items while looting, and when that happened, I turned fast item destroy off. I'd hate to not have the ability to destroy and item while looting, since that's where the majority of my destroys take place.

  • Mages and mana Regen Spells
    [Quote from original post: Will Magicians ever get Mana Regen spells? it really seems to me that all other pure casters have this. It would greatly help mage Magicians a more robust class.]

    Beyond meditation, no. Mages are pretty robust as it is, and the complaints that I've heard don't appear to be tied to mana.

  • Real Issue on Posting
    [Quote from original post: Many players post with more than one idea/issue/rant/etc on mind. The post is usually mixed with emotion and feelings and facts all jumbled up. Next step is Gordon (or whomever) reads the post and answers the post. Now the postee may have had 8 points in the post but all revolving around a central issue. Gordon (or whomever) replys to 1 or 2 or more of the points but may miss the main point.

    I suggest that we number our issues 1 by 1, making it hard for Gordon (or whomever) to "accidently" miss answering a point. Make your posts clear and concise. What Im suggesting is an unofficial format.]

    It's a good idea. Clear and concise points are more likely to be addressed. This of course assumes that it is a point that I want to address. In all fairness, those 8 point posts that you mention often only have 1 or 2 points that it would be beneficial to address.

  • Harvest: Make It Like Harm Touch/Lay of Hands
    [Quote from original post: There is one thing that REALLY bothers me about Harvest though. It permanently uses up a spell slot. Now, you might say necros need to use a spell slot for lich, but once they cast it then they can unmem it until they need it again. Same goes for clarity.

    Wizards can not afford to EVER unmem harvest ]

    It's a tradeoff. Wizards decide whether or not they want to have the spell available always, and "pay" for that decision with a spell slot should they choose that they do.

    It's a good enough spell that it is worth a slot, at least for those who keep it memmed all of the time.

  • Why is it NO-Drop?
    [Quote from original post: Verant-Abashi: Why is this Item NO DROP???
    Item: Sarnak Earring of Station ]

    I don't know, perhaps it's used for a quest?

    Anyway, this really doesn't seem like that big of a deal. There are over 10K items in game now, and as we get more and more it gets harder to insert them into the same mesh as others. Some are bound to have stats and settings that have no great plan behind them, or have one that isn't apparent at the time.

  • Playing Your Class
    [Quote from original post: yes, the only answer we have got, was something like: learn to play your class right and show others what you can do. i found this very insulting. someone who has no clue about hybrids (not a flame gordon, noone knows everything about every class..... )past 50 tells a lot of good SKs to learn more.......]

    Thankfully I have someone playing every class pretty close at hand :) Yes, that includes the SK. One of the most avid EQ players in house here has a high level SK built from scratch. He's also one of the decision-makers, so it works out fairly well. Suffice it to say that he thinks that SKs are fantastic, and I'll agree with him on that point, despite the issues that you brought up.

Conclusion to Discussion of the Week
[13 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:23 PM PDT
The latest Discussion of the Week has come to an end. Here's the final thoughts from Bill Coyle, one of Verant's Game Designers:

    First off I’d like to thank everyone who participated in this discussion. As I read through all the posts I was very pleased to see that most of the ideas, requests, and suggestions you all have here mirror my own ideas as a game designer. I thought I’d take this opportunity to address some of the key topics and concerns brought up and give you all my perspective on them.

    On Announcing Quests to the Public

    While we’d ideally like to have everyone involved in our dynamic quests, the reality of limitations such as internet latency, zone overcrowding, and sheer lack of manpower make this difficult to do. If you announce that a huge quest is going to happen in Qeynos Hills for example, chances are that everyone who can get there will. With a limit on the number of GMs and Guides who can facilitate the quest, this can make it almost impossible to run. And although we’ve increased our functionality to facilitate these quests by a lot, we’re still not at the point where we can have a large scale quest involving an entire player base of 2000+ people at once. Until that time dynamic quests are always going to have the luck factor of being in the right place at the right time.

    On Rewarding Role-playing

    I am a firm believer in the principle that for most dynamic quests the rewards to be reaped should go to those who role-played the quest best and made the biggest mark in the story line of it. The biggest problem with rewarding role-playing is that it is a completely subjective thing. What one person views as good role-playing another sees as acting like a fool. The other inherent problem with this is that we can’t watch everyone at once. You may be doing the best job in rallying the troops to defend Rivervale against the invading goblin army, but if no one facilitating the quest sees you do it they can’t reward you for it. We have a limited number of people who run these quests, and while we often do have a bunch of invisible people running around, we don’t see everything. At the end of such quests all the guides and gm’s who participated will usually talk about all the people they thought were doing a good job and vote on who to give the treasure to. If you can think of a fairer way to accomplish these I’d love to hear the suggestions.

    On Being the “Hero”

    Let’s face it, everyone in any fantasy role-playing game wants to be the hero, central figure in which the world revolves around. This is perhaps the greatest challenge for a massively multiplayer game to meet. Rather than just saying this is impossible I choose to look at it from a different perspective. It is my belief that everyone can be a hero in his or her own mind and in the minds of those around them. The 12th level warrior who makes a stand to hold off the gnolls while his party escapes is indeed a hero to that particular group. The 50th level cleric who travels the world resurrecting and healing those in need could be considered a hero on the server in which he does this. The point I’m getting at is that you, the player base, decide who your heroes are based on the way they act and the things they do. We provide the content, the background, and the stage. But you all are the real dynamic actors, the heroes of our world.

    On GM Slaughter Sprees

    We’ve all heard the tales and some of us have even been a victim. “I was sitting there camping something when all of the sudden a Red mob came running at me and killed me before I had a chance to think. Then I found out it was part of some GM quest…I have become a victim of a vicious GM out to get players.” This is a common tale told on message boards and in taverns and I’m not going to say it doesn’t happen that way. What I do want to know is what is the difference between that and a rare spawn popping in the zone and killing you? I remember one of the first times I was hunting in Oasis with my level 15 cleric and got a visit from Lockjaw. He was bigger, meaner, and scarier than anything I expected to run into there…and I didn’t stand a chance. There are encounters like this throughout all of Norrath; it’s that extra rare danger that makes things a little more dynamic and exciting. When a GM or Guide assumes the role of an actor in a quest they have a responsibility to portray that character properly. And if that character happens to be a bloodthirsty monster or a vicious dark elf, then you would expect them to kill people. Most quests have a certain level range in mind that goes along with the back-story of a quest. One of the biggest complaints I’ve seen is that quests often use npc’s that are a much higher level range than the players who typically frequent the zone the quest takes place in. The biggest reason that this happens is that it fits in best with the story. Dark Elves wishing to invade Qeynos for example would probably send some pretty tough troops in to throw at the city, and the fact that many newbies hang out around cities is none of their concern, invading Qeynos is. My advice to those of you that suddenly find yourself in a situation like this is to hide behind something and call for reinforcements. Whether you like it or not you are now part of a dynamic quest, you’re the victim of the story that people need to save =).

    On Dynamic Quest Etiquette

    Another big concern of most people, myself included, are the antisocial players who just try to ruin a quest for others. These are the people that run up to the dynamic Npc’s and shout, “Stop messing around you stupid GM” or “Gimme ur Ph@t L3wtz!” It should be noted that these people would promptly be ignored or worse by most dynamic quest npcs. In the type of quests requiring more interaction and role-playing, these people aren’t going to get anywhere and if they become too disruptive we are committed to removing them from the quest entirely. Recent additions to our functionality now give us new powers to more strongly control situations like this. One of the best new features we’ve added recently is the ability to set a maximum player level range that can still hurt the mob. This will allow us to create a lot more lower level quests without the concern of higher level players “stealing the quest” from those it was designed for. Just remember, if you want to participate in a dynamic quest portray your character and leave the ooc comments out of it, you’ll find that the npcs will interact with you a lot more and you’ll learn more of the story going on. And please, if you don’t want to participate in a role-playing type of quest, just leave the zone and let those who do have it. But whether you’re role-playing or not, don’t be surprised or angry at your swift death if you attack a npc for no reason, they will retaliate just as you would in their position.

    In conclusion I’m happy to see that many of things I saw suggested in this discussion are actually in the works right now. We have a dedicated team of Quest Masters who are working day and night on just this sort of thing. As our functionality increases, so will the frequency and quality of our quests. There will always be many varying kinds, from pure combat ones like a rabid wolf in Qeynos Hills to pure role-playing ones involving only npc interaction and figuring out some kind of puzzle. We are committed to conveying more of the history and story of Norrath to you all, so you can better understand the world in which you’re character lives. I think everyone will be pleased with some of the new dynamic content coming out, and even more importantly nearly everyone will get a chance to participate.


    Bill Coyle
    Game Designer, EQLive
Here's a follow-up:
    I think the biggest complaint I have seen about GM events is that single players, playing in a particular zone getting killed, then the high level players swarming the zone to kill the MOBs(GM controlled) and the high levels getting rezes and rewards and the bystanders that got killed get nothing."

    This has been a longstanding problem in dynamic quests that we recently got the solution for. Now that level 50 players can no longer do hurt a level 30 npc in a dynamic quest designed for level 30's this shouldn't continue to be a problem. It should also be noted that with the exception of pure combat quests, the reward for the quest is not necessarily given to the people who kill the end monster, but rather the people who were involved in the quest the most. This kind of system should also solve the problem of people stepping in at the end of a quest and getting the reward.
