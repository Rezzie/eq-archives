    I'll pass this on to... someone.

  • Faction Problem
    Call it a stealth fix, to be fair...

    Again, we just forgot to mention this in the patch message last time. It will be in this weeks patch message.

  • On Closing Threads
    SpineDoc wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "You closed my thread because you could not hack the flame, ok I can understand that, especially in this environment."]

    Actually, I closed it because it was your second (third?) post on the same topic AND because you were flaming.

    I understand your desire to see these issues fixed. We want them fixed too.

    Unfortunately, it's going to have to wait a while longer. Keep the post on the QA board current (and flame free) and when we can, we'll look into it. Right now our testers are very, very busy.

  • On the Reason for Nerfing
    An interesting thread. Perhaps a bit over my head, intellectually…

    I think it's wrong to imply that you can only do one or the other, either increase the power of the monsters OR decrease the power of player characters, if your goal is to make the game harder (and that's IF…).

    The real world answer is that you do both as the situation requires. In the case of one bit of the picture being off, say an item or a spell that makes one class or character type or tactic too powerful, relatively, you obviously reduce the power of that item. If you want to make the world as a whole more difficult for the heroes in it, then you can do either, really. Obviously the preferable method is to increase the AI for the monsters, or find other clever ways to make them more powerful.

    That being said, don't go overboard. If we do something that reduces the power of an item or spell or somesuch, it's done in consideration of the whole. I like to picture class balance as a sort of sea (ok, I've never pictured it that way until just now…). Waves are expected, even desirable. But when you see a tsunami, what do you do? Do you raise the ocean or lower the wave? Keep in mind that if you raise the ocean you'll drown the world…

    I love bad analogies. :)

  • Downtime Affecting Users Across World
    After having addressed many times I wonder if I should keep trying or give up, but I'll give it another short:

    EverQuest is played all over the world. ANY time that we take the servers down will be SOMEONE's primetime. We try to take them down when the fewest number of people will be affected, that will mean taking them down during off-peak time for the America's.

    It's a matter of math. How can we affect the fewest number of people possible? If *peak* was at 4am my time and the low point were at 8pm, we'd take them down at 8pm even though it was primetime here.

    I realize as an off-peak player you don't like this, any more than Americans who play during off-peak like it, but until peak time moves to a time other than the time it is, our scheduled patches will be in the general viscinity of when this one is taking place. I'm sorry, but there is really no other option that makes sense. I've seen people argue that we should stagger patch times so that it falls in someone elses peak every time, but that does not meet the goal of affecting the fewest number of people possible.

    Now, that said, no spawn is "exactly 7 days", nor is it exactly "three days". Any spawn over two hours has a +/- modifier that adjusts its spawn time. That means that the seven day spawn could very well pop up anywhere from six to eight days after it was killed.
    --
    It's plus or minus a percentage of the total spawn delay. That means that a two-hour spawn would have a variation of minutes, whereas a 7 day spawn could very well have a variation of days days.

Patch Message: 11-29-00
- Aurum @ 11:18 AM PST
A new message now appears on the EQ Patch Server:

    November 29th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Patch Day*

    Today's patch includes updates to the client to address some problems reported by people in regards to the DX7 update, as well as other gameplay issues:

    - Corrected an older bug that would cause people to stop gaining or losing faction with associates when the primary faction was at its maximum or minimum. Players will now be able to further modify associate factions in the appropriate direction.

    - Random-effects on weapons carried by pets will now behave as if the pet is a player character rather than an NPC. The change last patch reduced them to a rate below player characters. As part of this patch, target-specific random effects will only work if the target is the intended type. For instance, a weapon that processes "Dismiss Summoned" will only "go off" on a summoned NPC instead of everything.

    - Added some code that should address if not eliminate a problem reported by people whereas the client would "freeze" for a second or two every few minutes. For those who do not have IRQ sharing and/or driver problems, this should address the issue.

    - Made final positive modifications to several Epic Quest rewards. Included in these changes: the SK weapon will now allow you to bash while carrying it.

    - Fixed a problem that gave people problems when exiting the game and attempting to get back in.

    - Added some code to address reports from people who find exiting from the character-select screen back to the server-select screen rather slow when mip-mapping is enabled. Most of this problem is due to routines in DX required to release resources consumed by mip-mapping and may not be addressable until the next version of DirectX is adopted.

    - Changed customizable key mappings so that the ESC key will clear the "NOKEY" setting instead of the "Backspace". This means that you can now assign "Backspace" to a function in game.

    - Corrected a sound problem that would cause certain sounds to be played "rapid-fire" under some circumstances. There is still a potential for this to happen with some other sounds that will be addressed in a patch in the near future.

    - Corrected a problem in Kedge Keep that made the top encounter more difficult than intended after the server had been up for a while.

    - Higher level Magician, Necromancer, and Shadowknight pets now have the ability to dual-wield without being handed weapons. The calculation used to determine whether or not this ability exists takes several things into account, but for the most part 39th level and above summons (higher for the Shadowknight) will have this ability. It is possible that you could get a "good" 34th level pet that will, or a "bad" 39th level pet that will not have this ability.

    - Bards of the land, hark unto me: The notes have been scribed on tattered parchment, two score and nine, for you they are meant. Abandon your mates, though they may fume, and follow the call of the seafarer's tune.

    - The Monk's hand-to-hand skill cap has been raised to a level equal to their one-handed blunt skill.

    Remember! Only a few days left until the launch of EverQuest: The Scars of Velious. Reserve your copy at your local retailer today!

    - The EverQuest Team

Today as we went about our business in the fair city
of Firiona Vie, a shout was heard throughout the area
proclaiming that the defenders of the city come forth

  • The Vision
    [Quote from original post: . yet in the last patch this is what was changed, lowered spawn rates and overall less mobs in the game, less exp per kill on same mobs, more exp loss on death, less loot.]

    While it is possible that some isolated individual spawns might have had their rates (spawn or loot) adjusted in one direction or another:

    - No move was made to lower spawn rates across the board leading to less mobs in the game.

    - No change was made to reduce the amount of experience earned per kill on those mobs. It's always been based on the level of the NPC and the zone you are in.

    - No change was made to make people lose more experience on death. The only change to this that has occurred since release was when it was halved.

    - No change was made to reduce the amount of loot entering the world on a global scale.

    Now in regards to your suggestions regarding level based equipment, it was decided some time ago that EQ would not take that route. The FAQ has a pretty good explanation regarding this suggestion.

    [Quote from original post: BTW this was not a bash or flame, I am just concerend, I've been playing EQ for erm what 3 years now, and this latest move SCARES me.]

    Well two and a half years at most if you were in the earliest beta phase, but I don't believe that the "latest move" that you spoke of above occurred.

  • Necro & Magician Damge Rates Against Green Frog
    [Quote from original post: What is wrong with this picture:
    he never ran out of mana...
    he resisted unresistable spells...
    he never got hurt even with 2 50+ pets wailing on him due to lifetap...]

    Nothing really. He's not an NPC that we want to be killed. As for the "unresistable" spells, there are only a couple of truly unresistable spells in game, and they all belong to NPCs.

  • Patch Problems
    [Quote from original post: 1) Still can't activate spells with the control key, like you could before the patch ( + )]

    The default key was changed to ALT. You can check it back to CTRL if you like by going into Options/Keyboard.


    2. I've never heard of that one. It sounds like a problem with your video card or driver, perhaps the way it's handling mipmapping. Have you tried turning off mipmapping to see what happens?

    3. The spell line eats mana gradually. When you ran out of mana it didn't wear off. It was a bug fix. Should it have been reported? Certainly, but it's still a bug fix.

  • Witch Hunts
    I'm unaware of any case where someone found a ban to be "probably mistaken" yet did not reverse it. That just wouldn't be right. If you have a URL where I could direct customer service, that would be appreciated.

  • Questions for the "New Abashi"
    Well if you'll take answers from the "Old Abashi", I'll be happy to take a shot:

    [Quote from original post: 1. "Oh yeah, one more thing. Why doesnt EQ put the maps of the citys in there guide when you buy the game??"]

    There are indeed maps of the playable cities in the manual released with the Kunark full-retail box, which I believe is the only thing on the shelves these days.

    [Quote from original post: 2. "Its been 2 years and they still havn't made the game compatible with most sound cards (promissed June, '98 that there would be a new sound engine VERY Shortly, September '00 still no engine)" ]

    First, the game never had a problem with "most" sound cards as suggested. Some people running under certain configurations (with the SBLive for instance) had problems, where as others had no problems of that nature though they used the same card. The vast majority of people (including myself on my home system which included an SBLive) never had any problems.

    As to the new sound engine, it's in. It went in the same time as the DX7 update.

    [Quote from original post: 3. On the new expansion coming out "Still havnt fixed some bugs from beta.. "]

    It wouldn't be proper to comment without a specific list of "bugs from beta" to know what you are getting at. Most issues that exist and have existed for that period of time are not bugs, but rather a waived design issues that will never be addressed due to various technology limitations (download size being chief among them). They are part of the game now, and we'll have to live with them.

    [Quote from original post: 4. "I love EQ so don't get me wrong. I play AC also and, I have to admit, am wondering why AC makes upgrades and increases land/monsters/spells/items/etc etc, for FREE and EQ makes you pay for them? Just wondering..." ]

    They have their business plan and we have ours. We've added tons of new content (quests, items, spells, even zones) for "free" as well.

  • The Point of AFK
    Sinneed's response was pretty concise.

    Basically, there's no "pause" button in a persistant world, at least not ours. The only way we would implement a "safe AFK" would be if there was absolutely no in-game benefit to using it. That means that we'd have to make you blind and deaf as well as stopping any type of regeneration and skill-use countdowns.

    At that rate, the question indeed becomes, why not just log off?

  • Magical Damage
    This keeps coming up as an example, though I've addressed it before.

    What does "magical damage" mean to you? To you does that mean that any spell that does damage available to a wizard of a certain level should do more damage than any other spell available to any other class of a particular level?

    If so, that's not the definition we use. Our definition is damage over a time period. A DOT that does more damage overall than ICE comet does not do it in the short period of time like Ice Comet. Of those two spells you mentioned, one wizard and one mage, the wizard spell is faster and more mana efficient.

    Wizard spells across the board have usually have either better casting times, better mana ratios, do more damage, or a combination of the three than those of any other class (harmtouch excluded) ***over a period of time***. That's how it works, and wizards fit that definition.

  • More Druid Forms Needed?
    "Require" is a pretty strong word. While I understand that you *want* more forms, I don't think that they are required. As such, it's certainly good content for the wish-list, but it's not something that I can say we will or will not do at some point in the future.

  • NPC Named "!"

Screen Freezing
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:10 PM PST
Abashi stopped by the Gameplay Board today to make the following announcement:

    Some people have been reporting that their screen freezes every so often while in game, causing all sorts of mischief. While we are looking into that problem, and do have some changes planned for the next patch that should reduce its occurance, there are some things that you can do first to see if it improves:

    1. Virus scan software: These problems usually run in a systray (down by the clock). By default, they typically are set to scan files every time they are accessed. This could be causing some of those problems, as EverQuest accesses several local files at every character save. Try disabling it to see if your situation improves.

    2. IRQs. PCI devices are designed so that they can share IRQs. That works well in principle, but can cause problems when several high bus-bandwidth devices are on the same IRQ. Typically we've seen this when sound and video cards are on the same IRQ. You can try disabling your sound by placing a file called "NOSOUND.TXT" (file contents do not matter) in your EverQuest directory. If your freezing goes away, there is a good chance it's a shared IRQ problem. The method by which to reassign IRQs varies from system to system, but can be done by either moving the sound card to a different slot, manually assigning IRQs in the BIOS, or manually assigning them in the Device Manager. You probably shouldn't attempt any of these without the assistance of your local computer guru, unless of course you are the local guru.

    As mentioned above, we are putting in some changes in next week's patch that should help the situation for those that aren't affected by #1 or #2, but you'll want to make sure that the problems above are not the cause of your problems before counting on the patch to fix it.

    -Gordon
