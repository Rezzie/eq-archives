Customer Call to Zomax
[19 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:28 PM PDT
Lordstyle sent me some interesting information about his call to Zomax this morning. Here's his letter:

Well, I just got off the phone with Zomax this morning, at 8:35am PST, and I found out
something interesting about the trouble that was being caused with the shipping.

First, I would like to say that when I called, I was helped by a very friendly lady, who not only
found my order but even told me that it had been shipped and was due to arrive today.

The problem that Zomax had, which seems to be confirmed by more then one person at Zomax,
is that they ended up with corrupt files and had to fill each persons order by hand, since the
computer that normally handled it had corrupt files. Thus, the long long delay in people getting
their game. As she explained it, having to fill out the order papers manually slowed the shipping
down a great deal. So I would tell all users out there to please hold on, they should be getting
them in the next day or two.
Thanks!

Verant Confirms Virus
[55 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:10 PM PDT
After contacting Verant Interactive regarding the virus that was leaked to the EverQuest Patch Server this evening I received a response from Gordon Wrinn. Here's the letter:

    It appears that we did patch two MS-Word '97 format Doc files that were infected with a relatively benign macro virus.

    These viruses can only infect other files (and then only other Doc files) if they are opened in your word processor, so people who didn't open them prior to right now need not be alarmed. We at Verant proactively work to eliminate the possibility that things such as this happen, however some safeguards were unintentionally bypassed as party of the Kunark opening. We've closed this minor loophole and customers should not have to worry about anything such as this happening in the future.

    We would like to extend our apologies to everyone on this matter.


    Gordon Wrinn
    Internet Relations Manager
    Verant Interactive, Inc.
Update: Gordon just send me the following update:
    It just occurred to me that I forgot to mention that the files on the Kunark Patch have been cleaned. They will be replaced the next time everyone runs the "Kunark Patch" option on the patch server, and they should do so prior to attempting to open either of those *.DOC files.

    On another note, the PDF versions of the printed Ruins of Kunark manual are now available on the EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark website at http://www.station.sony.com/everquest/kunark/index1.jhtml. The manual supplement that is distributed via the Kunark Patch is also available for download at the same location.

    -Gordon
Thanks Gordon!

Suspected Virus in Manual
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:36 PM PDT
I'm being emailed left and right by people from across the net reporting that a virus has been discovered in eqrokmanualsupplement.doc and everquest_manual.doc. The virus in question is a W97M.Class Virus, but thankfully isn't the most destructive of viruses. Here's a clip from a letter Val sent me:

    W97M.Class

    Common Polymorphic Word97 macro virus. Some variants display insulting messages like telling user "is a jerk", etc. Some variants quietly do their infections on opening and closing documents.
I just scanned my directory with McAfee and didn't detect a thing but the reports are so numerous I decided to report it just to make people aware. I have contacted Verant and will hopefully receive a reply shortly.

For those who have it, the virus should be easily removed by upgrading your virus scanner's dat files and scanning your harddrive.

On Mage Pet Apperances
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:12 PM PDT
Gordon talked a bit about the appearances of some expansion pet spells on the Gameplay Board. Here's what he said:

    I like the new abilites, but I am extreemly disapointed with the appearnce. You gave necros, Specter-ish pets, which look cool btw, why couldn't you have upgraded ours?

    As we've mentioned before, pet models are limited to what can go in a global file. Spectre pets required one model be put in the Global. It was an undead critter that we figured had a high "coolness" factor. Mages on the other hand have 4 pets, which would require a lot more space to do some kind of visual upgrade to them. Furthermore, as mages summon elementals, there really aren't any other elemental models floating around game, and the djinn in the Plane of Sky is a bit of a stretch.

    -Gordon

A Nitepicker's Guide to EQ: The Map
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:59 PM PDT
As I was going through the pages of the Gameplay Board, I stumbled upon the following thread:

    As sad as it is, this thought actually kept me awake for a bit last night.
    I got my copy of the Kunark expansion on Friday. With said package, I receieved a small cloth map that shows all of Norrath.. so I decided to sit down and see if I had missed anything in the 9 months I've played the game. And yes, I have - a migrane.
    Let's first start naming spots on the map that have not been added to the game (excluiding the Kunark continent, since none of us know about it yet.. or aren't bound by a legal issue).

    Continent of Odus:
    The Hole
    The Barren Coast
    The Vastly Deep
    The Gulf of Uzun
    The Irrand Plateau
    The Stoneburn Mountains
    Continent of Faydwer
    Hills of Shade
    Rantblock's Ridge
    The Loping Plains
    Wayunder's Lake
    Elizerain Lake
    Dragonscale Hills
    Continent of Antonica
    Rojarkan Hills
    Broken Skull Rock
    Gulf of Ganthrak
    The Buried Sea
    The Dead Hills
    Unkempt Woods
    The Serpent Spine Mountains
    Lake Neriuss
    The Northlands
    The Hatchland
    Everfrost Peaks *or* The Fridgid Plains

    23 zones there that haven't been added to the game. Bad, bad Verant.
    And now, for the major nitpicks. Let's see how all these zones are joined together, shall we?
    Odus:
    - Most of this contintent is ok. If they ever activate the deadzones there, might be a little confusing but otherwise ok.
    Faydwer:
    - Lesser Faydark. As it reads on the map, a traveler to here would first run into the LFay before they can enter the GFay.
    - Elizerain Lake. You'd almost have to hit this non-active zone before you reach Steamfront.
    Antonica: (ohhhh.. you're going to hate me)
    - Deserts of Ro. And a river runs through it. Um, at least, it should. This same river should also pass through the Specter Island (which it doesn't) and then empty out into the OOT.
    - Innothule Swamp. According to the map, it should be a horizontal (east to west) zone, but instead, it's a vertical one in the game itself.
    - Lake Rathetear/Rathe Mountains. Ok folks, which comes frist. You're going through the Karanas to get to the lower zones. You hit South Karana, then zone into Lake Rathe. But check your map: according to it, the Mountains come first THEN the lake.
    - Everfrost Peaks. Now, we have two options here, we can either say this is the zone we all call Everfrost or we can say that the Fridgid Plain is. The problem is, if you consider the Peaks the zone we call everfrost, then why is Halas offset to the *right* of the zone, when it's in actually at the *left*. Or, you can say that the Plains is the zones we call Everfrost but then you have to wonder how would one get that far East from near the Jaggedpine Forest.
    - Everfrost again. Take a look.. looks like there should be a way to cross the frozen waste of this icy flatland into the less flat but just as wastish and certaintly much hotter Lavastorm Mountains. This would certaintly make alot of Barbies hapier if they had a shortcut back to their homeland from the rest of Norrath.
    And that's everything I can think of so far. Ya, I'm crazy. But you gotta admit, you love it.

    There certainly are. When the background for Norrath was being written, future expansions, zones, sequels, etc., were taken into account. In order to develop a solid foundation, you have set some boundaries for the majority of what will ever be in order to insure that your story is cohesive. This is a lot like the fantasy books that typically have a map printed within the forward. Most of the places on the map aren't visited in every book, but they are there for future volumes.


    Another thing to keep in mind is that just because a name is on the map doesn't mean that every name on the map is a zone name. For instance, the map refers to the "Plains of Karana", while there are actually 4 zones that comprise those planes. The map also refers to Jagged Pine forest, which is really a *part* of Surefall Glade, which doesn't happen to be listed.


    As for your comments on orientation, I'll buy it. It's not an exact replica, but it is very similar to the types of maps produced in our history when people were learning cartography.


    -Gordon

[43 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:42 AM PDT
The talented, daring and dashing Crispin of Fennin Ro has a tale to share with us:

Good day citizens of Norrath!

A few minutes ago, the first band of adventurers traveled to the recently re-discovered isle of Kunark. The first people on Fennin Ro to set foot on Finonia Vie were Vegeetta, Minara, Azyzik, Cloudstorm and of course yours truly.

The remainder of my report will be hidden, meaning you must highlight the white area below with your cursor to view it. I daresay I would not want to spoil all the surprises that will await you in Kunark.

The journey began at the Faydwer Docks close to the Dwarven city of Kaladim. Six small shuttles were docked neatly in a row. These shuttles could only carry a few people at time, but none the less they seemed capable of standing the voyage. Upon boarding them, we waited for additional reinforcements and prepared to get underway.

A few minutes later the shuttles were launched and headed to the unknown, departing what was then the known world.

Time seemed to freeze as we crossed the borders. When time returned to normal my sharp elven eyes tried to penetrate at the fog ahead. As we pushed forward it became clear that there was a massive object in front of us. The size of this object can only be described as unworldly at best. As we drew nearer it became apparent what it was.

Having sailed on all the known boats that exist on Norrath, my breath was taken away as I stared bleakly at the most impressive warship my eyes had ever beheld. The Elven armies had spared no expense for this mighty ship of war. The shuttles slowly came about and circled the ship halfway giving us a spectacular view of the massive ship.

The shuttles slowly docked one at time, allowing us to board the ship. When everything was secured the sails were unfolded and we got underway. The voyage wasn't short, but thanks to the vessel's speed we crossed the distance of the ocean quickly.

At last we arrived at the newly opened elven outpost of Firiona Via. We quickly disembarked and crossed the long length of the dock to set foot on the isle outpost.

A kind enchanter named Azyzik volunteered to bind the entire group to the outpost.

Feeling glad to have set foot on solid land once again, I gazed round about me to get a better feeling of my new surroundings. Despite the announcement issued by the League of Bards, this outpost seemed to be under construction still. Buildings were still being built, and the gates hardly seemed able to keep out invaders. As I wandered out to the perimeter, other people were stocking up on supplies at the nearby vendors and some were even engaging in new quests. As I scouted my surroundings, I quickly discovered that leaving the gates of the outpost would be foolish. As a 13th season Ranger, I would have no chance of surviving any kind of encounter with the gruesome creatures lay ahead. I was marooned.

Crouching down to the earth, I contemplated what my next move would be on this dangerous, but exciting new frontier.

Crispin
Ranger of Tunare

their faces drawn and somber. Dagda, Ognit and Dabner walk over toward
Firiona Vie and her companions. As Dagda gets closer to Firiona, the fair

pleads to them "The task set before us is difficult and the road we must

his twin sister, Lyirae Oakwynd. Dagda, Ognit and Dabner agree to journey
with them.

Fighting against the raging blizzard, their faces drawn and determined,
