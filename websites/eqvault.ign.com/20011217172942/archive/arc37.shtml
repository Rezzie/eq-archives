Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:01 PM PST
The dedicated "talk" threads dedicated to individual classes continues to be the dominate issues tackled by Alan on the Gameplay Board:

  • Bard Talk
    Ok, I suspect you've been preparing for this, so I'll warn Akriss to be ready to split this thing…

    So let's have it. Tell me what needs balancing for bards. You may as well give me bugs and broken stuff too. But my focus is going to be on things that are needed to move bards closer to 'balanced' in the game. This may include a nerf or two, I want to warn you of that. I am just as willing to suggest a negative change that helps to achieve balance as a positive one.

    Also, I understand that a bard is a complicated class to play. And I have no problem with that. This complicated nature will always mean that there are fewer bards in the game than other classes. I'm fine with that too.

    Please, be detailed where you need to. Try to avoid abbreviations, my simian mind can't decipher many of them.

  • Monk Talk
    I'm starting this thread today because I'll be leaving for Baltimore earlier than I had expected. I will have little access to the Internet while I'm there, and I don't want to start a thread like this right before I leave. Also, for you Rogues reading, I'm moving you back to Tuesday next week, I won't be in the office on Monday.

    Monks are one of those interesting types of warrior that, as a class, we have tried to balance a rather large volume of damage dealing with a lesser type of armor. It's not an easy task to build a fighting class that is 'balanced' with a warrior but doesn't wear armor…

    So tell me what you think needs to be changed about monks. Keep in mind that I am primarily looking for balance issues, but give me whatever you've got. I am also not looking for suggestions that would dramatically change the class, or that would require an army of people to get into the game. I'm mostly looking for tweaks that would help to balance the class.

  • Warrior Talk
    What is there to say about warriors?

    The idea of this thread is to talk about how to balance the warrior class in the game of EverQuest. I must admit that I don't really expect to see too many suggestions. The warrior seems to be a well balanced class. And we have to keep in mind that the warrior is a sort of staple class, upon which the relative balance of several other classes hinges directly.

    So this is the forum to talk about what is right and what is wrong with warriors. Go ahead and tell me about bugs you've seen too. I'm not focusing on them, I'm concentrating on class balance here. But I will also take note of non-balance concerns as well.

    Have at it, muscle-bound-types.

  • Enchanter Talk, Part 2
    Here’s what I have as a sort of summary so far. Keep in mind that I wrote this yesterday evening (during Survivor…), so it does not take into account posts made after that. I am reading them today, though.

    The first thing to think about is how enchanters work against the high-hit point creatures of Velious. Do they have enough to contribute over the course of a fight with such monsters?

    I’m not sure that enchanters are in need of help in this area. Perhaps the addition of something like a DoT-pet would help.

    Really, where the majority of higher level enchanters are expressing their concern is that there are too many creatures that are immune to mesmerization-type (and secondarily charm) spells. As I noted earlier, I’m not real sure that we can increase the functionality of those spells because they would simply over-balance the enchanter in those encounters (encounters where one or two very strong enemies are intended to challenge large groups, and one charm or mesmerization spell would eliminate all or half of the challenge).

    I’m not sure that I’ve seen an answer to this situation that will work.

    Rampage: This line does not stack with other strength buffs. We should evaluate this, and decide if this is how it should be.

    DoT Pet: Something not unlike the clerical hammer spell, to add more to the ability of the enchanter to help during fights with extremely high hit point enemies.

    Illusions: Mostly, you want more illusions. There are also a few requests to add effects to some illusions.

    Illusion, Wolf: There has been a call to have this spell grant Spirit of Wolf. If it gets no Spirit of Wolf component, then perhaps it could work indoors. I’ll have it on the list. I am very much against such homogenization of the classes. In fact, so much so that I don’t really think that enchanters should even be allowed to transform into wolves at all.

    Illusion, Gender: Seems like a good request.

    Illusion, Group Elemental (or race): A spell that grants the elemental illusions (or race) to the group.

    Illusion, Change Size: Some sort of illusion that makes the caster bigger or smaller. This is something that I am against, assize change is really the area of the shaman.

    Illusion Monster (and any copycat illusion): a spell that lets the enchanter copy the appearance of either a random monster in the zone, or a targeted creature. Faction issues will most certainly disallow this, but it’s listed.

    Major Illusion: If possible, a high level spell (even 60th if needed) that has the functionality of the original minor illusion spells. One that brings back the enormous illusions that were possible with that spell.

    Illusion face: Would give the enchanter a random face.

    Illusion Races Bug: It seems that some illusions don’t resize the character properly to people that enter the zone after the illusion is cast. We should take a look for the giant gnomes and see if we can fix them.

    Undead Illusions: I, frankly, feel that enchanters shouldn’t have these spells. I will ask that we look at them, and consider that they are the territory of the necromancer.
    Phantom armor: It would be awesome to create an illusion spell that allowed the enchanter to look like he is wearing plate. Perhaps a new spell similar to the magician phantom line (perhaps just like it) that has this illusionary effect (and we should also think of adding this to the magician spells...)

    Enchanting Items: I will add this to the list as a reminder.
    Lull spells: These spells seem to be resisted too easily, more often used to pull than to break up a group. There should be at least a fair chance that the creature resisting this spell will not immediately aggro.

    Bard Lute: We should at least look at this loot. Even if we don’t remove it (it is a very rare item), we should keep it in mind when making new items. It gives a key enchanter ability to another class.

    Voice Graft: We should evaluate the idea of giving this spell to all pet classes, including enchanters.

    Whirl til you hurl and Dyn's Dizzying Draught: Players feel that these spells are too easily resisted and too easily broken.

    Mesmerization spells: There are some targets that are unaffected by these spells, either due to level or race. Our anti-undead spell lines give the character back a message when he tries to cast on an invalid target, and the spells don’t even cast (costing the caster no mana). We should consider this approach for mez spells as well.

    Mesmerization spells and overwriting: Are single target mez spells supposed to overwrite area of effect mez spells? It seems that perhaps they should. Minimally, higher level mez spells should overwrite lower level mez spells.

    Mesmerization (Charm) Indicator: I, personally, don’t really like this idea. But there is a call for a change of the color of a monster’s name when it is under the effects of mez and charm so that people know by looking.

    Low level Evocation practice spell: Enchanters don’t seem to have a spell like Flare with which they can practice their evocation skills early on.

    Tiny Daggers: These items are unavailable in Qeynos. This needs to be fixed

    Pet Positioning: Because enchanters can’t tell there pets where to go, they often get in the way. There is a tendency with all pets to stand right in front of their master. This is a bit of a pain when the caster is trying to target something, or just trying to see something. Can we have pets (all pets) default to standing behind their master?

    'Charm wears off': players report that this message is sometimes sent long after the charm has worn off, after the creature is dead sometimes.

    Reoccurring Amnesia: The reports are pretty overwhelming, this spell does not reoccur.

    Boon of the Clear Mind: This is a group clarity spell, and should probably overwrite Clarity, and it does not. Either spell should probably overwrite the other, basically just increasing the duration (and the same should apply to the clarity 2 spells). It also seems that this spell has a very short useful life span. Lowering the level of this spell would allow its use for longer before they get Clarity 2.

    Failure Messages: Some spells (Lull series and stuns against giants are primary examples) give the same message on failure that they do on success. If it fails it probably should say so.

    Mask Faith: Players have been asking for the ability to mask their religion for a while. I think this is a bad idea. But I have a logical argument in favor of it, so I’m presenting it. I like the idea of characters having to deal with their religious choice, even enchanters. But, well, they aren't anymore because by now every single new enchanter is being told by their mentors to restart their characters as agnostic. That's something that I don't like at all. The solution to the problem here (the problem being that there are now very few enchanters that are not agnostic) might be moving their spells so they don’t have to enter dangerous cities (which they can’t get into if they have a religion), or making religion more valuable.

    Cantana (a bard song) and Clarity: I was under the impression that clarity spells would stack with bard mana regeneration songs. There might be something wrong with the stacking of these two spells. It seems that the bard song will stack over the enchanter spell, but the song must be stopped or the enchanter spell won’t take.

    Blanket of Forgetfulness: Is this spell supposed to be more effective than it is?

    Collaboration: This spell does not seem to have any affect in Skyfire, but it does in Kael Drakkal. Is this a bug?

    Illusions and Link Death: Can we make it so that Illusions remain up during a link death (not during zoning, though).

    The Essential Enchanter: We should look into why an enchanter is considered ‘essential’ in a Kunark group, and perhaps change that.

    Resplendent Robe: The effect is Rune IV, but it requires a component? That seems wrong.

    Rune IV: The cost of the gem used to cast this spell might be too high.

    Boon of the Garou: We should probably up the duration by thirty seconds or something, so that the spell can be kept up on two targets without it dropping (the duration is currently exactly twice the recast time).

    Tash Line: The thought from the players is that this line causes too much aggro. I don’t know if it’s that way for balance or if it is perhaps an exaggeration. We should look at this.

    Recast Bug on fast spells: After casting a spell, sometimes attempts to cast fast casting spells (Tash line, for example) will not cast right away, and will give a "this spell is not ready message".

  • Donal's Breastplate to be Nerfed
    We've changed this item before because we thought it was overpowered. Unfortunately, it is still overpowered. Let me explain why.

    This item is a breastplate that is only usable by clerics. If you right click this item, it will cast a Complete Heal on your target with a casting time of 30 seconds. It uses no mana to produce this effect.

    A group of two characters, one cleric wearing this breastplate and one warrior, can defeat any opponent, as long as that opponent can not kill the warrior in under 30 seconds.

    Let me restate that.

    One cleric and one warrior, using this one magic item, can defeat ANY opponent or any number of opponents that are not capable of killing the warrior in under 30 seconds.

    I hope that the impact that this game dynamic has on the game is obvious to everyone. If we allow this item to remain in the game as it is, we will have to change the game so that monsters are capable of killing a warrior in 30 seconds. 15 seconds if the group consists of two clerics with this breastplate and one warrior, and it gets worse from there...

    Of course, if we make the world so hard that this breastplate is not overwhelmingly powerful, we would have to, essentially, rebuild the game, rebalance the classes… No single item should be so powerful that the balance of the game revolves around it. Adjusting the game to fit this item is not a choice. We need to change the item to fit the game.

    We have had a fair amount of internal debate over this item. It really comes down to two choices. Eliminate the item entirely, or change the way it works. We would rather change the way it works. After much debate and some conversations with players that have characters that use this item, we decided on the following change.

    The item will cast a new spell (to be named later). The effect of this spell will be to completely heal the target. The spell will also leave a buff on the recipient that lasts somewhere between five and ten minutes (we are leaning toward a compromise of 7.5 minutes). This buff will have some small positive effect (perhaps a 1 point regenerative effect), but as long as this buff is on a character, he can not receive the effect of this spell again. This buff will not be removable until its duration runs out.

    In practice, what this will mean is that the cleric with a Donal's Breastplate will be able to heal anyone he wants every 30 seconds. But he will not be able to heal the same person twice unless 7.5 minutes has passed between healings. The breastplate will still almost completely eliminate downtime due to hit point loss, and it will no longer destroy the game dynamic of EverQuest.

    There is one concern that we have as to this implementation, and I'd like your feedback on it.

    There are some cases where a character would have all of his buff slots full and be unable to gain a new buff. This new Donal's effect would be, essentially, a buff. We can either have it overwrite a current buff, or have the spell fail if the character has a full load of buffs on him. I can see arguments for either implementation, and I want to hear your thoughts on it.

    Yes, we know this is a 'nerf'. And, frankly, we're willing to nerf this item to save the game as a whole. We are sorry about ever creating an item that is so easily unbalancing to the game, and we're sorry that we have to change it again. For now, I can't tell you when this change will take place. It won't be until after we've finished the work that has us on hold for updating the game. When I have a better timeframe, I'll certainly let you all know.

  • On Shadow Kights
    [Quote from original post: From shadowknights being labelled a 'defensive' class when we see ourselves as 'offensive', to this latest blunder with the spells and what levels we gain them…]

    Well, actually, that was not a 'blunder'. It was a random pair of numbers that I made up by way of example. Not the use of the word 'if' in the original statement.

    As for the perception that shadow knights are offensive or defensive… well, I think I can make a far better case for them being considered a defensive class than on offensive one, based solely on their skill caps. They are a defensive warrior with offensive spell ability. A paladin is a defensive warrior with defensive spell ability.

    And, yes, it may very well be the case that the double penalty of later and more expensive spells is wrong. That's why it's on my list of things to evaluate...

Editorial: 2cp For a SoW? Anyone?
- Aurum @ 6:08 PM PST
Neverly's latest editorial entitled 2cp For a SoW? Anyone? has been published to our Editorial section. Here's a clip:

    Now, unbeknownst to me, there had been a commentary running for some minutes before my entrance. It concerned, predictably enough, the stupidity of “newbies”, and was voiced by two 25th level characters: one druid and one warrior. The druid had been approached by a young halfling in the same situation as my own – he needed a SoW (Spirit of the Wolf) to retrieve his corpse. His request, though quite common, was answered with a string of insults, digs, and shouts of “LOL!!!” that filled many a passerby’s chat window to overflowing. Walking in at the end of this, I just caught the druid’s concluding statements as he voiced interest in having all newbies systematically destroyed by a wayward “bug”. Being largely unaware of the situation at that point (I was filled in later by a compliant wood elf), I ignored the harsh words and within moments had sent a tell politely requesting an SoW from the druid in question. Needless to say, the response I received was less than flattering. However, after a bit of verbal dueling and some liberal use of my stress ball, I managed to wrestle a response that wasn’t awash in abbreviated giggling, which I now quote:

    “SoW is my main source of income, so, if you can’t pay, don’t ask.”

    His main source of income, he says. I read that line with eyebrows raised, unbelieving. Could it be true? Could this man have such a long line of impatient customers that to spare a bit manna and some time for a 7th level cleric severely crimped his financial plans? Of course not, he was simply being nasty. Realizing this, I complemented him on his dedication to character, remarking that there has to be some greedy scumbags in the world. I then left, sans a SoW, and made my way towards Halas once again, tottering along and finding my mind occupied by the entire idea of charging for spells.*

CNet News.com Auction Article
- Aurum @ 2:21 PM PST
An article about the recent eBay/Yahoo auction controversy is now appearing on CNet. The highlight of this article is the comments obtained from an eBay rep:

    Both eBay and Yahoo cited their policy on canceling auctions that violate intellectual property rights.

    Kevin Pursglove, spokesman for eBay, said that Everquest joined the companies VeRO program, which stands for Verified Rights Owners program. VeRO members list items that they own the property rights to. Should they find an auction selling items that a member believes violate their property rights, eBay will shut down the auction.

    "The key is that they are declaring under penalty of perjury that the items they want removed are infringing on their intellectual property rights," Pursglove said.

    Pursglove added that an auctioneer risks suspension from selling on eBay should they continue to violate the ban.
