Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:17 PM PST
Alan had a few parting words to share with us before leaving for Baltimore:

  • Before Absor Leaves For Baltimore
    I probably will have limited access to the Internet while I'm out of town. So I won't be posting much, if at all. And I won't be doing too much reading of the class threads either, sorry.

    When I get back I'll be starting three threads (I think I'll be back on Monday…), Rogue, Paladin and Ranger Talk. I'm moving Rangers up because I want them all done by the end of the week. I will also be starting Necromancer and Magician talk threads later in the week.

    Some time the week after I'll be posting my lists for each class.

    I wanted to, once again, stress that the whole idea with all of this is to balance the classes, or get as close as we can. This might very well mean an increase in the strength of a class. But it might also mean a reduction in the strength of a class. We will consider 'nerfs' if they seem necessary. I am not suggesting that we are looking to nerf anything, but I also want you to be aware that we may do so.

    So I'll see who I see in Baltimore. Have a good weekend.

  • Bard Talk, Part 3
    Draelon wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I'm not going to argue that this change is necessary, but it is one of convenience. I don't see how summoning 5 or 10 or 20 at a time would imbalance anything. It's not like food and drink have any effect on how fast you kill a mob. This creates extra down time, when it should reduce down time by making our trips into town fewer and further between.]

    And that makes perfect sense. I guess this is just one of those things that I sorta shrugged at, and I probably shouldn't have. I'll add it to the list.

    [Quote from original post: I don't mean to be confrontational, but I think the error is on your part. We don't want our haste to bring us in line with warrior damage. Look at it this way... ]

    Ok, then it was the way it was written that was an error. The quote pretty much says that he (or she) wanted the haste to bring him in line with a warrior for combat ability.

    [Quote from original post: The stackable haste songs require an instrument to be played, eliminating the bard's ability to deal melee damage. In order for the Eravj songs to be useful in any capacity, they must recoup the loss of the bard's melee ability and then some, otherwise, it's more efficient for the bard to keep on using his weapons.
    As it stands, adding an additional 5% haste to the entire party doesn't even come close to the damage that a bard can deal out if he continues to use his weapons, that's why we feel the percentage is too low.]

    All of this I understand. Though it could easily be argued that a 5% haste, in the right party, could provide more damage than the bard's blades. But the point is valid.

    [Quote from original post: Our levle 39 charm song requires mana each time it pulses. This was implemented to limit the amount of charming a bard could do. Using Dirge drastically impacts our ability to charm. Furthermore, is 300 hp of damage so powerful that it warrants only being useable every 40-80 minutes?]

    No, it's probably no that powerful. I'm just leary of the suggestions that bards gain their mana back a lot faster, as that affects the charm song as well. Perhaps the better route is to look at the damage ratio or mana cost of the dirge. For safety sake, I'll add both to the list.

    [Quote from original post: Lastly, you mentioned that you would be looking at the Lute of the Howler. Can you explain what about the lute makes it imbalancing? Bards (along with rogues) already recieve some illusions via items (mask of deception, mask of obtenbration, iksar hide mask) how do these differ from the lute's illusion?]

    Actually, it isn't that different, not at all. Though I can see a greater objection to the werewolf form, for two reasons. It's not a player race, and it's the newest item to blurr that whole class distinction line. But you need to understand that my personal feeling that this item (heck all of them really) is a bit out of place does not mean that the team will agree with me. And even if they do, there's not a real high chance that anything would be done to the item.

    NadiraAndante wrote:

    [Quote from original post: My personal issues about bards sortof parallel their previous work. Let me tell you, it IS annoying sitting in a group with an enchanter/shaman, and really having to wrack my brains to find effects from my repetoir that will enhance group effectiveness. Part of the problem is bard song stacking among other songs, but haste and slow stacking with similar spell effects would really help our groupability, and bards are absolutely supposed to be a group oriented class.

    I don't see how helping our group reach the haste cap faster is unbalanceing. The game was played for a very long time at the haste cap and was still hard. Not all people have super haste items. Making them stack would increase our effectiveness while in a group, while not decreasing the effectiveness of the shaman/enchanter. Same with slows.]

    Well, it seems that the original design did consider this a balance issue.

    I'll certainly add the whole haste and slow stacking this (I hope I mentioned this before, because it's been on the list…). I just don't know that you should expect a change.

    [Quote from original post: Which brings me to my little tiff about our snare. We have to constantly recast this thing to make it work, its range isn't the greatest, and its resistance rate is pretty high...why does it also have no reliable effect time?? I know bards have to pay for no-mana effects, and believe me we DO pay for it our entire lives, but do we have to have every single disadvantage rolled up into one song?? When do the disads outweigh the ads?? Probably not in snare for a while, since it is the only effect of its kind we get from 23-51 so its
    low range, high resistance, short effect time, AND unreliable effect time (best case is what 12 seconds?? Level 1 druid snare lasts how many times that??)]

    Well, it may have a much shorter duration than a druid snare, but you can also re-snare it, what, every 6 seconds? But I guess I can see that there may be too many restrictions on it. But keep in mind that this is not really intended to be a song that you can lock down something forever with. There has to be a pretty serious limitation on songs ('free' is always a dangerous thing when you are talking balance).

    And, rather than quote more text, I understand your request for a raise in your secondary-type skills.

    [Quote from original post: So, to balance bards you have to increase our level-effect caps on our songs, taking into account the raise in monster power between 50-60 when WE become 50-60 (since our level=song effect level augmented by instruments when possible)

    And I would like to see our low-capped jack of all trades skills brought up to a level where they work at a fair level, like 2/3 or 1/2 the time of that skill's master expects success.

    Plus significantly raiseing our defensive skills so that we're still below SK/Pally/ranger by a recognizeable ammount, just not a very large ammount.]

    Good, I like summaries. Makes my job a little easier. :)

    [Quote from original post: PSALMS of purity and vitality (29/37)...they're supposed to cure poison and disease eventually right?? They are STILL broken. I have never been able to cure someone with either of these songs, regardless of how many times myself or that other see the 'you've been cured' text. Try it in hate on the kirakui disease. I left the cure song on for about 10 minutes straight to no avail according to the afflicted paladin. Thats a lot of pulses yes?? And she DID say that she saw the cure message a great many times. It's been broken since release, they say it was fixed, but apparently that is not the case. Check it please.]

    Sure.

    [Quote from original post: And many of our songs have caster effects now, and its the new ones. Namely, the chants line 30/38/46, Nivs level 47, Shield of Song 49, Selos song of travel 51, and I believe our cantana at 55...casting effects with a lute or drum, can you pleeeeeease let me play my instrument like the bard I am instead of throwing it over my head and stretching!?!]

    I can look into that. As always, I have to give you the 'that's gonna be a low priority item' warning on these sorts of things.

Alan again hit the class threads hard today on the Gameplay Board:

  • Monk Talk, Part 3
    NyogTha wrote:

    [Quote from original post: This post was meant to show you that it is possible to twink and cheat this quest which I KNOW was not meant to be done via the Verant design team.]

    Make all sashes/headbands NO DROP
    Take the robe off of Brother Zephyl/Brother Qwinn

Same damage level, making it only really useful for it's special power. A way to give a monk a special skill without linking it to a higher level skill (making that skill too powerful), and giving it the limitation of much reduced damage.

    This was only a thought, as I am not really convinced that monks really need any new skills. They have a pretty solid selection as it is.

    STUN on T-Kick is a bad idea. We already get a discipline at 60 that allows us to stun called "Silent Fist". Please do not add this to T-Kick.]

Once I have these lists of ideas, though, I am going to give them all a reality check. I suspect that the stun on Thunderkick idea would have been tossed out when I got up to speed and discovered that it was an ability that monks already got at 60th level. Obviously it's possible to give a stun earlier, but probably not warranted. Thanks for the heads up.

    We will. I think these might be the best two idea for raising monk damage a bit. But, again, let me clarify that I am not convinced this is needed. We'll have to decide if the class needs more damage post 50 before we even look at these. But I don't think either of these ideas is easier (linked special attacks and unlinking foot/hand attacks) than the other from a programming standpoint. I don't know for sure, but they sound like they might actually both be fairly easy to do (I can hear the programmers screaming now, I can see them lighting torches…)

    [Quote from original post: But giving us the ability to just "push back" the caster with our specials is EXACTLY what we need.]

    Fair enough. And maybe not with all of the attacks. Perhaps all past a certain level, or all kicks… something like that. A good idea, though, and easier to suggest than a stun.

    Neou_the_Cyber_Monk wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I think Absor leaves today to Baltimore so no more hearing from him anymore. ]

    Tomorrow morning, actually. So this will be the last you hear from me for a while.

  • Warrior Talk, Part 3
    First, a few of my observations about warriors (you need to know this so that you can correct me where I'm wrong). As the 'base' class that other combat classes are built around, it should be very hard to find anything that is in need of change for the warrior. I'm reading things like more hit points… no. If we change warrior hit points, then we have to change all the other classes hit points, since the basis for hit points, at least on tanks, is the warrior…

    I really don't expect to find too many things that need to be changed about warriors.

    Also, I actually hope to see as many suggestions for 'nerfs' as for improvements. Keep in mind that the warrior is pretty much the base class, so it is very unlikely that we will be improving something that has any major effect unless it addresses some deficiency. If not, then this might actually be one of those cases where we need to do a sort of give and take. I guess I'm saying that by their very nature, warriors just about have to be balanced, and an improvement to them might necessarily require a change to all other fighting classes to match them. And that's not really what I'm looking for.

    And, well, just like every other class, I want to warn you that a close examination like this brings the possibility that we will see something that needs to be nerfed. For example, I really don't like the idea that warriors have so many spells available to them through items, especially things like invisibility. I intend to ask that these things be looked at. My suspicion is that they won't be removed, we don't like to do that. But as a matter of class balance, I think it should be looked at.

    My most powerful D&D character, by far, is a warrior. He's not powerful because he fights really well, though that's part of it. He's powerful because he has a lot of items that allow him to act like a caster. He isn't that high level, but he is more wanted in groups than my higher level characters. He's obnoxious, too, so it's not because of his personality…

    Anyway, don't be too surprised if I don't pick a lot of suggestions from your lists. There might not be too much that needs changing.

    [Quote from original post: Another nice addition would be the addition of an "AE Taunt."     Ok, that's not such a bad idea. It has it's own drawbacks built right in. :) I'm even willing to suggest a version of this that might allow the warrior to attack all creatures in the area (one swing). But it would have to be very high level and very limited in re-use.

    [Quote from original post: Another area where Warriors seem to be a little lacking is in the AC and HP department. I am not suggesting that a huge move is needed in either of these areas, but in the high level game, there is precious little difference between a warriors AC and HP versus the hybrids and melee "wizards" (Rogues and Monks) ]

    That's exactly the opposite of what the hybrids, monks and rogues say… A lot of them think that there is too wide of a gap between their hit points and/or armor class and that of a warrior, and they want more.

    [Quote from original post: This is further compounded by the relative difficulty of the warrior epic as compared to the others. Why the double whammy?]

    I don't know… I really don't think that the warrior epic is relatively harder than most. It seems to be pretty middle of the road, to me. But I'll add it to the list (along with the request that we give it haste).

    Aanya said:

    [Quote from original post: Other classes getting to close to warriors
    I believe that warriors should be able to tank better than Paladins and Shadow Knights. I see that you "noted" those classes wishes for higher hp, better defensive skills, higher offansive skills etc etc. What they asked for, was for you to make their tanking ability close to the warriors. Our tanking ability vs most normal mobs is fine (except for taunt), but if you increase the tanking ability of the SK and Paladin, keep in mind that there would be no real reason to play a warrior at all. Why play a warrior without spells when you can play a warrior with spells.]

    We will keep that in mind. Remember, the warrior is the basis from which their skills and such are balanced.

    evilsofa said:

    [Quote from original post: I've been watching the SK talk, and part of the problem there is that an improvement of the SK's defense (and, I'm sure, the Paladin's, when their talk begins) would be an intrusion on the Warrior's role as the ultimate defense. One way to give the SK and Paladin some wiggle room to improve their defense would be to improve the Warrior's defense in tandem.]

    Actually, even if knights had exactly the same armor class and defensive skills as a warrior, they would be worse tanks, since they have noticeably fewer hit points. As I said earlier, improving warriors would almost by default mean an improvement in those other classes, so that the gap would not close. If we choose to close the gap, then warriors will have to remain where they are. I'm certainly not going to advocate an increase in warrior power _just_ so we can give more power to the hybrids. That seems sorta backward to me.

    ErikTorvald wrote:

    [Quote from original post: BIND WOUNDS....
    Please allow the percentage healed to increase with level. Start at 50 and by 20 let the tank be at 70% then at 30, 80% then 40 at 90% then at 50 at 100%. Do you really think it will effect balance? Heck no.. I will still be screaming for a clearic. The bind wound is nothing more for the times when you have no cleric, druid, shaman, or necro handy.]

    Well, I'm not one to advocate something that is specifically intended to improve the solo ability of any class. But I'll add it.

    JazyaVechette wrote:

    [Quote from original post: 2. (More of a general thing) 2h weapons not only do less damage overall, but taunt is laughable.
    Solution: Warrior hits from 2h Weapons generate 3x the 'taunt' factor of similar 1h damage.]

    That does seem odd. So, Riposte prevents the attack from hitting AND does a return attack… Ok, this is on the list.

    I'm not really looking for this kind of thing, but I'll add it to the list.

    [Quote from original post: I love the warrior class. The only thing i would like to see is the piercing cap to be raised from 240 to 250.]

    a_burly_gnoll00 wrote:

    [Quote from original post: 1. The level 60 warrior discipline (aggressive) is bugged. I am just as willing to suggest a negative change that helps to achieve balance as a positive one.

Also, for you Rogues reading, I'm moving you back to Tuesday next week, I won't be in the office on Monday.

      So tell me what you think needs to be changed about monks. Keep in mind that I am primarily looking for balance issues, but give me whatever you've got.       So this is the forum to talk about what is right and what is wrong with warriors. Go ahead and tell me about bugs you've seen too. I'm not focusing on them, I'm concentrating on class balance here. But I will also take note of non-balance concerns as well.

      The first thing to think about is how enchanters work against the high-hit point creatures of Velious. Do they have enough to contribute over the course of a fight with such monsters?

      I’m not sure that enchanters are in need of help in this area. Perhaps the addition of something like a DoT-pet would help.

      Really, where the majority of higher level enchanters are expressing their concern is that there are too many creatures that are immune to mesmerization-type (and secondarily charm) spells. As I noted earlier, I’m not real sure that we can increase the functionality of those spells because they would simply over-balance the enchanter in those encounters (encounters where one or two very strong enemies are intended to challenge large groups, and one charm or mesmerization spell would eliminate all or half of the challenge).

      I’m not sure that I’ve seen an answer to this situation that will work.

      Rampage: This line does not stack with other strength buffs. We should evaluate this, and decide if this is how it should be.

      DoT Pet: Something not unlike the clerical hammer spell, to add more to the ability of the enchanter to help during fights with extremely high hit point enemies.

      Illusions: Mostly, you want more illusions. There are also a few requests to add effects to some illusions.

      Illusion, Wolf: There has been a call to have this spell grant Spirit of Wolf. If it gets no Spirit of Wolf component, then perhaps it could work indoors. I’ll have it on the list. I am very much against such homogenization of the classes. In fact, so much so that I don’t really think that enchanters should even be allowed to transform into wolves at all.

      Illusion, Gender: Seems like a good request.

      Illusion, Group Elemental (or race): A spell that grants the elemental illusions (or race) to the group.

      Illusion, Change Size: Some sort of illusion that makes the caster bigger or smaller. This is something that I am against, assize change is really the area of the shaman.

      Illusion Monster (and any copycat illusion): a spell that lets the enchanter copy the appearance of either a random monster in the zone, or a targeted creature. Faction issues will most certainly disallow this, but it’s listed.

      Undead Illusions: I, frankly, feel that enchanters shouldn’t have these spells. I will ask that we look at them, and consider that they are the territory of the necromancer.
      Phantom armor: It would be awesome to create an illusion spell that allowed the enchanter to look like he is wearing plate.     • Donal's Breastplate to be Nerfed
      We've changed this item before because we thought it was overpowered. Unfortunately, it is still overpowered. Let me explain why.

      This item is a breastplate that is only usable by clerics. If you right click this item, it will cast a Complete Heal on your target with a casting time of 30 seconds. It uses no mana to produce this effect.

      A group of two characters, one cleric wearing this breastplate and one warrior, can defeat any opponent, as long as that opponent can not kill the warrior in under 30 seconds.

      Let me restate that.

      One cleric and one warrior, using this one magic item, can defeat ANY opponent or any number of opponents that are not capable of killing the warrior in under 30 seconds.

      I hope that the impact that this game dynamic has on the game is obvious to everyone. If we allow this item to remain in the game as it is, we will have to change the game so that monsters are capable of killing a warrior in 30 seconds. 15 seconds if the group consists of two clerics with this breastplate and one warrior, and it gets worse from there...

      Of course, if we make the world so hard that this breastplate is not overwhelmingly powerful, we would have to, essentially, rebuild the game, rebalance the classes… No single item should be so powerful that the balance of the game revolves around it. Adjusting the game to fit this item is not a choice. We need to change the item to fit the game.

      We have had a fair amount of internal debate over this item. It really comes down to two choices. Eliminate the item entirely, or change the way it works. We would rather change the way it works. After much debate and some conversations with players that have characters that use this item, we decided on the following change.

      The item will cast a new spell (to be named later). The effect of this spell will be to completely heal the target. The spell will also leave a buff on the recipient that lasts somewhere between five and ten minutes (we are leaning toward a compromise of 7.5 minutes). This buff will have some small positive effect (perhaps a 1 point regenerative effect), but as long as this buff is on a character, he can not receive the effect of this spell again. This buff will not be removable until its duration runs out.

      In practice, what this will mean is that the cleric with a Donal's Breastplate will be able to heal anyone he wants every 30 seconds. But he will not be able to heal the same person twice unless 7.5 minutes has passed between healings. The breastplate will still almost completely eliminate downtime due to hit point loss, and it will no longer destroy the game dynamic of EverQuest.

      There is one concern that we have as to this implementation, and I'd like your feedback on it.

      There are some cases where a character would have all of his buff slots full and be unable to gain a new buff. This new Donal's effect would be, essentially, a buff. We can either have it overwrite a current buff, or have the spell fail if the character has a full load of buffs on him. I can see arguments for either implementation, and I want to hear your thoughts on it.

      Yes, we know this is a 'nerf'. And, frankly, we're willing to nerf this item to save the game as a whole. We are sorry about ever creating an item that is so easily unbalancing to the game, and we're sorry that we have to change it again. For now, I can't tell you when this change will take place. It won't be until after we've finished the work that has us on hold for updating the game. When I have a better timeframe, I'll certainly let you all know.
