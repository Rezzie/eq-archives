Brad McQuaid Q&A: Part 1

Massive Postings from the Official Gameplay Board

Is it REALLY true that 2/3 of the quests in the game are yet unfound, and there are 90 caster robes yet undiscovered?

**No. There are quite a few, but not that many.

Do you have plans to allow a command so pet classes can be told the level of their pets?

**Probably not, no - can't you roughly determine it's level anyway?


Will there ever be a message so we know if a rogue is trying to pickpocket when he claims he is not?

**Interesting idea - we'll toss it around.

Why haven't you answered questions based on the many stat tests players were told to perform by Verant? Specifically, when these test results were posted why didn't you make an appearance?

**I believe Abashi has addressed the stat issue several times.

Do you have an estimate on when or if the main storyline of EQ will have an even stronger affect on the players as a whole in the future? (GM Events, new quests introduced etc.)

**I think this will happen over time as we do more GM Events and get more static quests in the game (which often reveal the history of the world).

Humans in EverQuest. What is the Vision(tm) on them and what is their place in Norrath.

**The intent was that they would be neither great nor poor at any one thing. They have the most flexibility when it comes to class selection. Their stats are fairly middle-of-the-road.

Apart from RP, What do the players get from selecting Humans as their play choice (and getting mediocre stats and being night blind)?

**More class selection and one of races on the lower end of the experience needed table.

Abashi hinted that there were class equivalent or near equivalent weapons to the Fiery Avenger in EverQuest. Are there indeed weapons which we haven't found yet (hard to believe) which involve quests other than PoS alone (which can't be done until after the expansion).

**There are a few very powerful unfound items, yes. Fiery Avenger status items, particle effects and all, are still coming for the other classes and will take us some time.

I know you don't want to answer questions about the future, but how about some hints on these new melee skills? I'm getting quite jealous looking through the Kunark spell list in Castersrealm.

**Can't say yet, sorry.

What is this 'Vision' I keep hearing of. I have never heard from your words exactly what the 'Vision' was intended to be. Not a flame here, just a question.

**It would probably take me pages to answer this properly, but in a nutshell here goes: to recapture and recreate the fun people had grouping together and going out on quests and adventure playing paper & pencil games in an online virtual world; to create a successful, fun, and popular graphical MUD.

Can there be more Dynamic GM quests?

**Indeed, and we hope to have more in the future.

Why are JBOOTS, such an incredible item that it vastly imbalance the game in outdoor areas, still in the game? Shouldn't this item be made more available to everyone or just dropped?

**We felt that after addressing the kiting issue some months ago, making it not as abusable, that Jboots no longer 'vastly imbalanced' the game.

In all seriousness.. I don't know how specific this question is. but are dark elf clerics ever going to get any race and class specific quests?

**I will ask about that.

Why can't we bash with the Unicorn Shield? Any fix in the near future?

**I believe the shield is more of a weapon - 'fixing' it implies it is broken in the first place, which I don't believe is accurate.

Can someone please explain why the tests on stats are incorrect? Our tests show a negligible impact on melee ability with their use. Can you please post an example to demonstrate where we are going wrong in our testing?

**Some stats certainly have more of a direct impact on the game than others. This is by design.

Would it be possible to attach a server designation to each .opt file on our end so that when we play characters on different servers, it doesn't use the same global file for hotkey assignments? Whenever I play on another server, I have to redo my hotkeys every time because each char name has only one .opt file irregardless of the server.

**It would be possible but would require a bit of programming and I'd probably prioritize it fairly low, but we'll see what we can do.

In the expansion, will there be any new classes available for any of the existing races? i.e. like having Halfling bards or rangers? (its a long shot I know but I had to try hehe)

**No, just the new race.

Server crashes, item and exp loss from hardware failure at Verant -- why is the burden on the players and not on Verant when this happens?

**Loaded question. Both players and Verant are burdened when there is data loss and neither of them are happy about such occurrences.

Are more relaxed item and exp reimbursement policies planned?

**Not in general, no.

Are monetary compensations like free 1 month playtime for affected players feasible?

**No, data loss, albeit rare, is unavoidable at times. Compensation as you describe would indeed not e feasible.

Why would a mob that is obviously far, far tougher than the con system have you believe actually be low blue. i.e.: why is that guard that just kicked my ass not yellow or even?

**The higher level you get, the more we expect you to be grouping. The 'con' system assumes, over time, that you are grouping more and more - it does not necessarily assume you are alone. Also, the color is an indication of the experience you will receive from killing the NPC, while the text is supposed to indicate difficulty.

Why are there very few mobs in outdoor zones for people 40+ (aside from the nasty sorts like sea furies, etc...) is this to slow people down once they get to level 40+?

**While we'd like people to group more and more and to enter dungeons and such at higher levels, we do agree that higher level outdoor areas are needed and you will see several in Kunark, including a 40+ outdoor zone.

Will shaman ever be able to shrink in outdoor areas? i.e. to fit in the bank in Kelethin

**Unlikely. There are supposed to be size penalties in the game, depending on the race you've chosen for your character. Over time, we've implemented more and more ways to overcome the size restriction and I think we've gone far enough.

What will it take for you to admit there may be need to change the Vision(tm)?

**Whenever something doesn't turn out the way we wanted, or whenever it did but the desired effect failed to occur, we adjust the VisionTM (if it's possible to do so). The VisionTM is both pure in its idealistic form and flexible when it comes to what really works and doesn't work.

EQ tuning is not by mob rule, I agree; but 99% of your customer base are up in arms over the melee class bind issues (or lack thereof). What will it take to break such an inflexible vision(tm).

**I definitely think your 99% number is greatly exaggerated. Changing binding would change the pace of the game for both melee and caster classes. It would also lessen the death penalty. We've no intention of changing the binding rules.

When is the HOLE going to open?

**The Hole will open when we're done with it, which should be sometime in May, we hope.

I think one of the craziest ideas I have ever had was to develop some kind of in-zone game you can play that involves a ball or spell aura that you pass around. Sort of a PvP event but I'm just talking crazy here..... would be great for guild wars. People need something like that to break from the norm, guess you could call it a mini-game.

**There are no plans currently to implement those sorts of mini-games.

What new skills will warriors get in the expansion?

**Can't say yet, sorry.

Will the skill caps be raised with the expansion?

**Some will, yes.

Why do the gnome guards in Steamfont punch me for 106hp but when armed they would only be hitting in the 60's?

**Guards are often very nasty, highly trained individuals that excel in combat far above and beyond the average create in Norrath.

I have a question of the vision, well sort of, to encourage more player interaction, why did you allow 8 slots? Why not limit it to one slot per server? I think that would have helped a lot of the issues we hear about now if that had been from the get go.

**When designing EQ we had NO idea if we'd even have close to 8 servers. The fact that we're at 28 now is mind-blowing. So with 12 races and 14 classes, we wanted to make sure players would have a chance to experience at least most of the race/class combos, so we made it such that a player can have 8 characters per server.

Also, can you expand a bit on why you don't like archery or throwing weapons to be a viable class weapon?

**EQ was designed to be a melee & magic based game. There is a reason in real life that humanity switched over to range weapons when the technology was developed to make them a reality: they're better. It's almost always better to shoot at somebody from the distance than it is to get up close and dirty. Knowing this, and knowing we wanted melee combat to be integral to EQ, we designed archery and other forms of ranged combat to be secondary in terms of how effective it is.

What are your 5 favorite musical groups?

**Fates Warning, Dream Theater, Psychotic Waltz, Enchant, and Yngwie Malmsteen.

What do melee stats do?

**Affect the outcome of melee to varying degrees.

IF poison is spell code why is the overhaul taking so long. i.e. just up the dmg on them and give the vials charges, poison fixed.

**We don't want a simple fix, we really want to do it right.

I heard a rumor some time ago about an EQ movie, some company that was working with final fantasy the movie I think.... but any truth to this?

**I hear the same rumors you do, but am unable to confirm or deny at this point. It's not something that Verant is involved in.

The simplest solution is to nerf but is that the right solution?

**Sometimes it is, yes. More complicated solutions, like enhancing every other class and then upping the difficulty level of the game to compensate, are very time consuming and prone to error.

Why is it in almost all cases of gameplay tuning that you "nerf" class / race abilities and spells?

**Loaded question (and we don't).

The average player impression is that there are way more nerfs happening than there are class/race enhancements. So much so that we get very paranoid on very patch and lose all sense humor during April 1.

**I don't think that's true, and with all due respect I don't think you speak for the 'average player'.

When you created EQ, were you able to include the Entire Vision, and is this expansion and all *possible* future expansions an expansion of the vision? Or were you only able to program a very small portion of the realm you envisioned?

**We accomplished a great deal of our Vision for both gameplay and world creation, but yeah, there's a TON of stuff we'd like to do in the future. I think the long, long term goals of MMORPGs is to create holodeck technology, and, well, we have a long way to go J

What is the Vision as regards removing/replacing/rotating items in EQ?

**We need to do it more often. One of the reasons, I think, that people get so upset when we alter the status quo is that they're used to things staying the same. If the treasure in dungeons was removed, rotated, and replaced more often not only would the game be better in terms of discovery and exploration but also people wouldn't be so upset when something did change.

Could you give us an example of a bug fix/feature that was implemented via suggestions and logs sent in through /feedback and /bug?

**Feature: Messages letting the party know a member went link dead.
**Bug Fix: Randomly twirling around when using the /follow command.

Is there any place we can go to find out what quests are broken?

**Not really, no. The problem there is that it would require listing the quests, which we don't want to do because we don't want to post spoilers.

Where you as pissed as I was when you found out you weren't selected for beta testing Diablo II?

**Who said I wasn't already in the Diablo II beta? J

Player disruption seems to be the major factor in GM quests not working well. Are there any plans to address this issue? If so, could elaborate on what those plans are I would appreciate it.

**Yes, and there are a couple of ways to make it better. First, involve less people and announce events less. Second, create a storyline in which players who try to disrupt aren't rewarded (perhaps even penalized). Third, flat out make it against the rules to try to screw up and disrupt a GM event and discipline accordingly (this is obviously the most controversial of the plans, and we're still tossing the idea around).

Can we look forward to more higher level class and race specific quests (sort of like the Paineel ones) being added to existing cities relatively soon?

**Yes, depending on your definition of 'relatively soon'.

Will the Expansion CD contain a few fixes to things considered to be too big to download, say like the barbarian shield issue?

**As a matter of fact, yes. The barbarian shield problem should be fixed via the Expansion, and other similar problems can hopefully be addressed in the future thanks to some new coding we've worked on that should allow us to fix some of those problems sans a huge download.

Is the wizard class the way you envisioned it in group usefulness and effectiveness not in regards to paper stats but in in-game real-time situations of level 50 players? If the answer is no, will it ever be?

**Roughly, yes. They're not perfect, but then neither is any other class, nor the game for that matter.

I have read (not sure if it is real) that you requested GZ to do some tests on the class for in-game time Vs Damage compared to other classes is this true?

**Yes, he's agreed to run some tests after he's finished working on new spells for the Expansion.

Stats in EQ and why the stonewall from Verant? Why are stats details (like how they affect/enhance the character you create) veiled in such secrecy?

**Because we feel revealing the inner working of stats and other core game mechanics spoil much of the game.

Is it that they do not work in any measurable degree and are broken?

**No, they are not broken. Yes, some stats have a more measurable and direct influence on the game than others. This is by design.

Is the game manual misleading in its description of char stats?

**Unfortunately, it is inaccurate in some aspects, yes.

Do you believe druids to be currently balanced as a class, or are they overpowered in your estimation? If not, do you have any future nerfs for this class under consideration?

**We feel they are fairly balanced at this point.

What does the 'MAGIC ITEM' tag really mean? For boots and gloves and shoulder pieces it means you can kick, punch, and slam magically, and rogues cant pickpocket it. I always thought it meant the item had stats, but a few things like the molten cloak have stats but aren't magic.

**Generically, it means that it's special treasure and not mundane. Beyond that, it can also mean being able to hit 'magic' creatures, as you described.

Why doesn't Dead Man Floating (level 44 necro spell) work in Kedge Keep? This is the perfect use for it but you can't cast it inside.

**I'm pretty sure that's a bug and on our bug list.

Why does a person's health go down when they go link dead? It seems like it drops to their unbuffed hps and then comes back up when they come back. Always thought this was strange and just wondered.

**When you become link dead the server takes control of your character and you are in many ways an NPC and treated as such.

Why can't you automatically meditate? Why do you have to push the button every time for it to work? Is there any chance it could be changed to default on? Because I don't know anyone who wouldn't want to be medding if they had the skill.

**Good question - we'll think on it.

Why isn't run on by default? I don't know anyone who walks through EverQuest.

**Another good question - we'll think on it.

Can you fix pronouns in emotes? For example if you type /drool without anyone targeted it says ' drools -- something must have $#%&^%$ excited!'

**I'd like to, yes, but it would be fairly low priority.

Can you get faction without getting a faction message

**I don't believe so, no.

Why can't I remove one item from a bag in the bank, instead of having to remove the entire bag?

**That's simply a feature we never coded - I'm certainly not against such functionality, but would probably prioritize it fairly low.

Why is it that we (Paladin) are only given slow (but good, not complaining about QUALITY) weapons? (e.g., why make FA 34/44, but not 17/22?)

**I don't know if that's true across the board, but it's probably true that our vision of a Paladin is a mighty knight, clad in plate armor, wielding a really, really big sword.

If faeries are a good race, why is it that paladin's killing them seems to be appropriate to Verant (e.g., Ro armor quest, FA quest, Princess Lenya quest)?

**Many Paladin quests are set up to create a moral dilemma for the Paladin who RP's.

How would you rate, on a scale of 1 to 10, the variation in new graphics for the Expansion? You know what I mean, will there be a multitude of new robes for casters, armor for fighters, etc. or are you following a similar routine as with the class specific quests: i.e., will it look like plate mail but basically just have a different color tint to it.

**Most of our effort went into creating new zones to explore, not creating a ton of new item graphics. We are planning on adding some though.

One more question off topic Brad -- have you and Hook raced your cars yet? Who would/did win?

**He won't go to a real track with me, and I sure as heck wouldn't drag race him.

Why are basic fundamentals of character creation, such as stats and exp tables, not given clearer information on?

**To preserve a sense of mystery and exploration in the game.

Why was poison nerfed so HORRIBLY with a 7 second apply time "in anticipation of an upgrade" almost a month ago, and yet poison is not slated to get work until after expansion?

**We'd hoped to have the Poison upgrade complete by now, but were delayed by the Expansion.

Why has GZ been allowed to discuss at length what stats do for casters, but no such information has been given to melee classes?

**In hindsight GZ would probably agree with me that he's revealed too much over time.

What level is you ranger at, Brad?

**In his mid 20s - I just haven't been able to play as much, and when I do it's usually using test characters on Kunark L

Do you think having ONLY static encounters with static mobs as the method of loot distribution may have been a mistake? i.e. 'we should have had more loot dispersal methods to begin with"

**Probably, yes, to a point. We are experimenting with more randomization of loot in the Expansion and I hope it works out. That said, I remain against total randomization of loot in that one loses the ability to provide context for an item (e.g. the Iron Crown of the Fire Giant Warlord should probably be found on the corpse of the Fire Giant Warlord).

Would it be possible to allow Rangers to target track? i.e. type /track hill giant and they track nearest HG.

**That would be a neat enhancement. We do want to enhance tracking in the future, but have no short term plans to do so.

What was the original vision for the rogue, and what is the vision for the rogue now?

**The original version included their ability to detect and disarm traps, but it turned out we couldn't get traps working correctly with our engine such that they were truly a challenge and hindrance. Also, upon reflection, we should have had more locked doors and therefore more interdependence between rogues and other classes when dungeon adventuring. We hope to address the latter issue in the Expansion, but traps just aren't really doable. So, in a nutshell, our vision for Rogues has changed somewhat, but we feel they will continue to improve in the future.

I don't understand Verant's Concept of good and evil in EQ, and am often baffled by certain quests. What races are good, and what races are evil? (I mean orc, fairies, fish, etc..not elfs, humans....)

**Morality is supposed to be more relative than absolute in the world of Norrath, hence the faction system as opposed to a more simplistic alignment system.

Do you think EQ has evolved into more of a straight hack-n-slash than an RPG? I personally know many players who feel the game mechanics themselves actively discourage role-playing.

**No. EQ was always supposed to be a more adventure oriented RPG. Role-playing, in our opinion, is up to the player, and we feel we've provided many tools to help the player role-play.

Are there any plans to add quest-able Armour sets for the monk and druid classes?

**Rough, general plans, but nothing specific that I could announce.

EverQuest gameplay and the need for secrecy. How do we know if it's a bug and not a feature?

**You can't always, but usually it's fairly clear. Typically, anything that gives a player a significant and unbalancing advantage in terms of advancement is a bug and not a feature.

Any changes at all to Alchemy planned?

**We're pretty happy with Alchemy as of this last upgrade and have no short term plans to upgrade that particular trade skill.

I would like to hear what the original "vision" of character stats was, and in what ways the current implementation varies from the original vision, and *what* the current implementation is?

**I think the original vision was that the stats that currently affect the game less would do so more. But it didn't turn out that way as we balanced and as the game evolved during beta, and it would require very unbalancing and major changes to make them 'matter more'. The current implementation is that some stats affect the game significantly moreso than others. This is by design.

-Brad

Brad McQuaid Q&A: Part 2

Massive Postings from the Official Gameplay Board

**NOTE: going to be serveral hours before I can get to the third bunch -- time to rest and take a few tylenol :)
---

What is the high-level view of the current state of the player run economy: specifically trade skills and their usefulness, and the affects of twinking on the economy?

**Trade skills, by their nature, need to be updated periodically in a deflationary player run economy (and it is our intent to do those updates). Twinking is also part of the economy and it accelerates the deflation, but can still be controlled by adding more to the game (better weapons, harder monsters, etc.). It can also be controlled to a point with no-drop items.

What are the current efforts to deal with bug reporting? I think a bug tracking system with a web front end would go a long way to easing some tension in the player community. Flaming on the message boards is out of hand because it is perceived that this is the best conduit for feedback. I would suggest a bug tracking system that deals with technical *and* gameplay bugs. (for an example of a similar system see:
http://bugreport.apple.com)

**The combination of the /bug feature as well as in-game player feedback and feedback from message boards has proved to be very effective in terms of us finding out about bugs. It's certainly more of a challenge to isolate and get them all fixed than it is often to identify them.

If you have been involved in the rogue tuning meetings, can you comment on poison being added as a one time proc per poison vial (or whatever form it is going to take) in DD or DoT form, or something completely different?

**I cannot yet announce the inner-workings of our poison upgrade because we're not done with it.

Why do paladins get a quality non planar 1hs weapon (ghoulbane) mid to low level, and sk's do not? Not enough people playing sk's to waste the time creating the item? I have heard that a 1hs is going to be put in for sk's that is of similar quality to gb any eta on that?

**I'm not going to address that specifically, but rather just say that all sorts of class specific weapons are on their way into the game, especially via the Expansion.

About EverQuest the game. If you had to do it all over again and start with a clean sheet of paper knowing what you know now, what would you do differently?

**Wow. No way I could answer that in just a few sentences, sorry. I'll say this - the game is certainly not without its flaws and we plan on making EQ better and better in the future, whether it be in the form of expansions or sequels.

I've heard a rumor about a dynamic quest team being implemented to only run dynamic quests.
Any truth and if so what type of time frame do you see them starting?

**We already have a team similar to what you describe, and we plan on adding more people to that team. We definitely want to see more dynamic quests and events and know our players do to.

Why are player concerns on Wizards dismissed out of hand? (too long to go into detail but there are posts 300+ messages long)

**They're not - we've posted several times in regards to those issues, and even announced that GZ would run some tests after he was done with the Expansion spell implementation and testing.

How do you expect Rogues to be a viable class in EQ when they come with such a horrible group unfriendly skills like PP?

**Loaded question. They are already a viable class in our opinion. As for PP, Rogues are Rogues... they're thieves, they're assassins... earning trust from other players as a Rogue is supposed to be more challenging.

Is a "second loot pool" feature for rogue's PP a viable idea in the future?

**No, no plans to do that.

Is it a bug or a feature that hybrids get less mana per wis/int from wis/int items than they do from their base wis/int?

**Don't know - I'll ask Abashi to look into it.

Also, why is it that hybrids get less mana than stated from mana items? ( i.e., 0-10 mana from a +25 mana item)

**See Above.

What do you think of the idea of allowing Shaman to forage for the herbs used in alchemy instead of purchasing them from a vendor? Would be a more realistic idea from a role-play standpoint as I don't think any self respecting Shaman ever bought their herbs at the local quickie mart.......

**Kind of a neat idea - we'll toss it around.

Are there any plans to make more "crafted" items from the trade skills needed to complete quests?
Much like banded is already required for totemic armor, could there be a quest out there that needs a forged weapon?

**Yes, but no short term plans.

Druid quests... can we get more? We kind of get tired of doing class armor quests for the other classes (two edged question there sorry)

**I think you'll see more and more class specific quests in general in the future.

Will hate and fear planes ever be changed to something more along the lines of air plane or killing the fire giants to allow high level groups to progress a certain amount based on the time they have available or will new areas in the expansion be structured in a similar fashion? examples: safe zone in points, removing roamers from hate so everything is on the same spawn to allow an even progression through the zone. After all, almost no-one ever really gets to see or enjoy the zone except for pullers or scouts.

**The planes are supposed to have a different feel and difficulty level relative to most zones. They are also supposed to often require more of a time investment from the players.

Why is it the stats of the guards throughout Norrath have not changed to either
a. more like Paineel guards to stop the High level farming of guard exp.
b. guards are made to be casters like clerics and shamans. I would think twice before I would attack something that could heal and cast blindness and/or a dot...

**We've slowly but surely been upgrading guards for the very reasons you state.

Why is that a lose of equipment due to a hardware malfunction... i.e. a Crash of the server cause people to wait for weeks to hope to get the item back and be required to /petition at least 3 times a week...

**It often takes time to verify item loss and to search the logs that we do have. That said, it shouldn't take as long as you describe and I hope we can increase the responsiveness of our customer service team in the future by adding more people.

Why is it after spending 1 week in hate (7 days) one piece of enchanter gear was dropped yet several pieces for the other classes, yes in the teens. And, how is this fair to even the loot being dropped per class.

**I'm pretty sure we've made some adjustments to Hate recently and that players on the Test server are reporting things are much better.

I know that every person wants "enhancements" for their primary class, and this is not regarding any particular class 8o), but why is it that certain spells do not work in the planes for certain classes?

**Planes are special places, and supposed to offer special challenges as well as some unique tactics to conquer. We reserve the right to have all sorts of spells either not work or do strange things in the alternate planes.

Have we all driven you mad yet with our inquiries?

**I'm getting there, thanks J

Can you speak on the possibility of Rallos Zek adopting the Vallon Zek or Tallon Zek PvP loot rules?

**No plans at this point to do that, no.

Why do hybrids get an xp penalty if they are balanced without it?

**Hybrids are generally more flexible classes, able to melee as well as cast spells. In order to maintain balance, we decided to compensate for their extra flexibility and often power by requiring them more experience points in order to level.

What are the general pros and cons you have seen with the implemented EverQuest class system?

**I have been and continue to be a huge believer in class systems for MMORPGs. They encourage interdependence, which leads to a more social environment - people are heavily encouraged to work together and often forge friendships and alliances over time. The only con that comes to mind is that some players appear to be eternally envious of the fact that they can do some things with their class, but not everything.

It seems like the good majority of complaints fed to Verant are related to player's beliefs of perceived class imbalances. Has it ever made you consider a different way to handle how characters are defined? Perhaps a more complex method that gives the players more control over their character while still maintaining game balance?

**Perhaps. More flexibility in general is a good thing, but you'll never play an EverQuest that doesn't have some sort of class based system.

Why are there so many broken quests in a game called EverQuest?

**Loaded question. I don't think there are that many. There are some, and we try to fix them. But we are also constantly adding new content and quests. With human error, that means we will inevitably add more broken quests as the game evolves.

Why not run 4 dynamic quests all at the same time in 4 remote locations. They would go a lot smoother =P

**Great idea. It just requires more manpower (here's hoping that, over time, given such a successful game, we'll get more).

Good Lord! -- Why are you working on a Sunday?!?

**Heh, good question. I think that even though I know that the vast majority of players love EQ and that the number of people who bitterly post on these boards is very small, that it still bugs me to hear false claims and allegations about Verant and our game. Some people say we don't care, or that we don't listen, or that we don't respect our customers. I've always been a person that is bothered by people believing things that aren't true about me (or my company, whom I represent). I also know that for every poster there are probably 10 or even 100 'lurkers' who just read boards, and I know that they appreciate when we post. Heck, whenever I do I see new names either post or email me, thanking me for the extra info. So I do what I can, and today it occurred to me to try to answer as many questions about the game as I could.

One of the great draws of a graphical mud is to see your character evolve and develop graphically as well as statistically. Why is it that most classes have a huge range of robes/armors that change their look, while Monks and Druids must look like "newbies" for a long, long time?

**There is a limitation of texture memory (which used to be much more restrictive, btw, but recently 3D cards have been shipping with a LOT of memory, thank God), so we were limited as to how many texture sets we could put in the game for player characters. We were able to get around this limitation to a point by using 'tinting' which allowed us to tint a single texture set multiple colors without affecting texture memory. The more uniform the texture, the less 'bad' it looked when tinted. Monks, having lots of skin showing, are the least tintable without things looking bad. Anyway, in the future I hope we have the resources and time to add more texture sets - heck , you can never have enough variation in a game like this, I agree.
