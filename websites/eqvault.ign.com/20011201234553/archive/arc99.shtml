Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:29 PM PDT
Gordon responded to a few more board posts late this evening on the Gameplay Board:

  • Veeshan's Peak
    Just for a bit of clarification, back when the planes were brought online, John Smedley commented that people just don't die in planes, they die hard. Veeshan's Peak makes the planes look easy. There exists a non-negligible chance that not only will you die there, but you might not be able to raise the necessary forces to allow recovery of your corpse. It is *very* dangerous.

    In order to avoid any appearance of impropriety in the zone, as sometimes follows when we've assisted in corpse recoveries in other zones due to claimed bug-deaths, we're not going to risk that for Veeshan's Peak. Veeshan's Peak is incredibly dangerous, and in order to guarantee that everyone is operating under the same set of conditions, we've elected to make the zone "enter at your own risk".

    That said, there are no known bugs in the zone that would cause the death of a character, and it was tested extensively in the Ruins of Kunark beta. We will certainly investigate and eliminate any bugs that are reported, but we won't be reimbursing items or experience, nor will we be moving corpses or people within the zone.

  • Sharkjaw Baby
    [Quote from original post: When I enable my Sharkjaw in my 2nd hand (warrior barb) it shrinks. It looks about 75% size. I looked at a friend and his ykesha was also shrunk in his 2nd hand ]

    Thanks for the note, I'll pass the info on.

  • More on Server Splits
    We've postponed the server split for a bit as we didn't want to go taking down the servers for a while. When the split is being done, both the source and destination servers need to be offline. We'll probably do it by the end of this week.


Gordon Infobits
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:56 PM PDT
Gordon has visited the Gameplay Board numerous times over the last 24 hours. Here's the scoop on all of his latest:

  • Bard Issues
    [Quote from original post: There have been some excellent bard issue posts recently that (surprisingly to me) haven't included this issue. Still, this issue involves direct danger to bards, (unlike the issue of bards lacking a lvl 49 song), so perhaps it is better separated from such Abashi-ignorable discussion.]

    It's looking at this time that there might not be a level 49 song. Geoff poured a bunch of good ideas into the 51+ songs. We would however be willing to entertain a discussion on this though.

  • Keeping Track of the Posts
    [Quote from original post: One thing I think would help is a FAQ of your respones to the various game mechanics and class balance questions/rants. Recently I read your explanation of taunt on Castersrealm, if I hadn't of checked that site, I never would have seen it. I still don't know which thread you posted that response in.]

    There's a couple of websites out there that do a pretty good job in cataloging our posts and maintaining a history. EQVault now provides a link to the original post with every quote, which makes it easier for people to come and commment. Some others are keeping ad hoc FAQs as well.

    The reason that we don't want to do something official along these lines is that I'd hate to get into a situation where I'm constantly referring people to the FAQ. What I think would be helpful is if more people here would provide the information that they have knowledge of. I'd really like to see more of a community develop here. EQ Stratics has a good message board of this type.

  • Too Many Busy Signals on the Customer Support Lines
    I agree, unfortunately phone lines and personnel are finite. There are just a bunch of people trying to get through to both places. This should clear up by the end of this week. We appreciate your patience.

  • Split Servers
    The split process is a bit more complicated. Not only do we have to estimate the number of people on at peak, but we have to come up with a figure for how many people executing the /movelog command are actually peak players. That's why we open registration during peak hours, but it never stops a non-negligable percentage from off-peak from logging in to run the command.

    It also doesn't take into account the number of people who have more than one account who'll run it for all of them. I know a few people who have upwards of 4 accounts, who only play one at a time. Of course I know two people who have 4 accounts and play all four at once too :)

    There's a bit of trial and error involved. When we did the first split we took less people than we are taking with these. We now figure that we can safely move more people without risk of overcrowding the destination servers. We'll use data obtained in this process to further refine it for any future splits.

  • Alchemy Prices
    [Quote from original post: I am only 30 at alchemy, but have looked thorugh many of the potions. The only thing that I can make to sell so far is a SOW potion. No one wants to buy those stat potions that raise 2 stats 5 and drop one 5 for 10 to 12 plat. I noticed the regen potion will cost about 50pp to make. Don't think too many people will give me 75pp for 1 regen. Seems that alchemy could use a little revamping.]

    We'll be able to start taking a look at these costs after things calm down a bit from the expansion rush. It sounds like some of the stuff is still a bit too expensive.

  • Questions about Iksar
    [Quote from original post: Please let me know if Iskars not being able to wear plate armor is intended? What is the point of making an Iksar Warrior if I cant wear plate. Maybe they have an inate defense for AC, but most all the stat based armor that would benefit a warrior is plate based. Please shed some light on this.]

    Please keep in mind that thousands of items have been put in along with the expansion. You shouldn't have a problem coming up with eqivalent armor sets for your Iksar.

    [Quote from original post: Is there a place to read up on Iksar... what they can and cannot wear and why?]

    You can get some information out of the History of Norrath section included in the manual that is available for download on the Kunark website at http://www.everquest.com/kunark.

    As for why they can't wear plate, it chafes their scales :)

  • Complement
    [Quote from original post: Wow. Hehe First of a hearty congratulations to Verant Inc for this incredible new expansion.]

    Thanks. We're pleased with the comments that we've been hearing. It's nice to see people enjoying the new areas.

  • Iksar Sounds and Quality Issues
    [Quote from original post: Is it against Verant policy to ship a finished product?]

    EQ is not a single player game where development stops once it ships. It is continually changing and evolving, long after it ships. Since the focus of our company is on massively multiplayer games, they will all follow this philosophy.

    As for sounds, we didn't feel that the issue merited postponing release for the odd sound or two. We'll have them in in the next week or two.

  • Monk Attacks - Iksar and Human Differences
    The tail attack, called Tail Rake, replaces the Dragon Punch skill for Iksar Monks.

  • A Suggestion
    [Quote from original post: Well why not an afk thing like this takes 30 seconds and then afk. Well thats not all it does once afk you cant move or do anything (cast, stand sit,) While Afk all Mobs will ignore you no matter what and you will be safe. Heres the point where someone (probably Abashi says) Someones gonna abuse it! But as stated above its just as though u camped out just u dont have to go through the log out log in process. This would make afk much safer and useful. Just an Idea )]

    This wouldn't work well, as there is definite value to being able to see what is going on around you. This would essentially be a "personal pause" for the game, which doesn't go well with the idea of a massively multiplayer game. Now perhaps if we completely blanked the screen, disallowed chatting, and muted the speakers, and stopped healing and mana regen, it might be an option, but at that point we'd rather the person be out-of-game so that the servers don't have to track their status any longer.

  • Corpse Decay Information
    [Quote from original post: The .txt .doc and .html versions of the manual do not have the correct information about decay time on corpses.

    Quoting from http://everquest.station.sony.com/s_guidefaq.html

    Characters Level 6 and above: Corpse will disappear within 24 hours if the player is online (being at the Character Select screen STILL counts as online). Corpses will disappear within 1 week if the player is offline. Note: the amount of time you spend online, while a corpse is decaying, will be proportionally subtracted from the amount of offline time available. For example, if you are online for 12 of the 24 hours, your corpse will have 3 days and 12 hours of offline time (half of one week) available to it.]

    Thanks, I'll double-check this and get whichever is wrong updated.

  • When Will the Store Return?
    We'll be putting the store back up soon, we just want to make sure we get the current issues resolved before throwing any more orders into the system. It will probably be back up by the end of the week.

  • Hintpoint Modifiers
    [Quote from original post: Abashi stated that Hybrids get an enchanced Hitpoint modifier. Well Rangers DONT]

    I didn't say that you get it AT 51. Please read the post that I made today where I discussed how hitpoint modifiers work.

  • The K-Patch
    The optional patch is used to patch new content that everyone will have access to. The "Kunark patch" button is used to patch content that only kunark-authorized players can access. The button that you press will depend on where the new zone is. For instance, when we launch The Hole, it will be patched via the optional patch.

    You should K-patch regularly to insure you have the most up-to-date Kunark files. We'll also be requesting that you k-patch within the patch message from time to time.

  • Why is Verant Anti-Kiting?
    We aren't anti-kite per se. We are however against situations where characters can reap rewards substantially in excess of the risk associated with them.

  • Kunark and EQ Keys
    The upgrade key can only be used to upgrade. The retail key can be used to either create a new account OR upgrade an existing one.

  • Top 10 Ways to Know it's 5AM
    This thread is great! Here's a contribution from back when I was a guide:

    271) You receive a petition: "Bob fell asleep 3 hours ago and we're getting kind of tired of protecting him. Can you kick him off or something?"
    272) You receive a petition: "I fell asleep a while ago while bound in Cazic-Thule, and my group left. I lost over 15 levels. Can I get them back?"

  • Gordon on Wizard/Rogue Issues
    I feel a bit bad for posting nearly the same response here as in a ranger and rogue thread today, but before we close this thread I did want to comment.

    I've addressed a ton of wizard threads over the past several weeks, and it appears that we are at the point that we'll have to agree to disagree. We firmly belive that wizards are useful to and desired by the party, and as such are essentially well-balanced. There is no need for us to "fix" them as we do not feel that they are broken, even given the posts here on the board claiming such.

    Like I said in the other threads, I'm sorry that this issue can't be resolved to everyone's satisfaction.

  • Bard Charm Bug
    Yes, it appears that we introduced a bug last week that capped a bards attempts to charm at their level. This unintentionally affected the lower level charm song as well as the upper level. We'll be fixing this when we patch spells next. No other charm spells were affected.

  • Addressing Ranger Threads
    Brad and I have addressed at least a dozen ranger threads over the past 3 weeks, if not more. We've said all that we can on the subject, that being that they are essentially well-balanced and as such we have no short term plans to "fix" them.

    I understand that not everyone will agree with this and apologize that everyone can't be pleased in this matter.

Dark Zion is a player-driven, player-controlled persistent online world. Players will have an unprecedented level of control over their environment; beyond building a character, a player will have the opportunity to build a business, a town, an army, even an entire society. There will be no arbitrary quests or super-imposed events; the story is the success or failure of the world at large.
Other features at Dark Zion Vault will include game information, news, concept art, screenshots, humor, reader and community participation features, humor and much more. New features and content will begin to appear in the very near future.

We invite all members of the community who are interested in the future of online gaming to stop in and check out the coming excitement from Wombat Games and best-selling author Tad Williams at our brand new Dark Zion Vault. Enjoy!

Couple Kunark Issues
[17 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:36 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn just posted the following post on the Gameplay Board:

    There's a couple of issues that have several threads running behind them. It might be easier for me to address them at one time:
    Spirit of Scale:

    We'll be making this spell group-only (e.g. must be grouped with target) shortly. This should alleviate people's concerns about it being used offensivly to harm other players.

    Backstabbing Pets:

    Up until the level 50+ pet spells, all pets were based off of warriors. We now have the ability to base pets off of other classes, and that's what's been done here. There are now rogue pets, wizard pets, and monk pets, which represent additional abilities for the pet-users above 50th level.

    Rogues need not worry that a backstabbing pet somehow reduces the desirability of their class. First, the pet does not have the backstab skill that a rogue of that level would have, so they won't hit as often nor will they do as much damage. Furthermore, they don't have all of the stat bonuses that increase the rogues effectiveness. Certainly they can be buffed up, but they'll never come close to having a real rogue. Finally, rogue pets will never have the ability to double-backstab as rogues do.

    Rogue pets won't be picking locks either, and there are quite a few places to do that in Kunark.

    -Gordon

  • Questions about Disciplines

The customer service staff (GMs and Guides) will not assist players in any way in regards to this zone. This includes help for issues such as unrecoverable corpses (see your local Necromancer), or characters finding themselves stuck (see your local Magician), bugs, etc. The only assistance that will be offered by customer service reps in this zone are those covered by the Play Nice Policies.

  • Warrior HP at level 51
    Number of hitpoints gained per level is assigned on the basis of race, class, and a multiplier based upon level. The multiplier increases in steps every X (where X is a value based upon race and class) levels. When someone hits a level where the value of X is increased above that of the previous level, the character will experience a HP "pop" over that that they usually receive. This in essence "makes up" for all the extra hitpoints they would have received if the multiplier was that way all along.

    The Palys, SKs, and Bards all hit a step of that sort at 51, causing some of them to get upwards of 100 HP for the level. This doesn't mean that they are getting 100HP per level, just that for that level the number of hitpoints received "made up" for the hitpoints they would have gotten if the multiplier had always been at that level.

    Warriors operate on a different schedule than SKs and Palys, but the curve will always favor them. For instance, warriors should see a fairly substantial hitpoint pop when they hit 54.

    It's important to remember that 51 to 60 is not considered a "new" game. What I mean by this is that algorithms that take level into account and improve something every Y levels don't start over at 50+, they continue from where they left off. For instance, if someone has an attribute that improves in effectiveness every 4 levels, and his last improvement was at level 48, he won't see the next one until 52. This might make it appear that someone else who gets an improvement every 10 levels, and got their last improvement at 41, and gets their next one at 51 somehow gets something before the first guy. It's not the case, it's just the way that the math works.

    I hope I haven't confused the issue further :)

    At 60th level, the Palys, rangers, and SKs, will all *melee* better than a 50th level warrior. You of course won't melee as well as a 60th level warrior, but that remains as designed.

  • Taunt Skill Issues
    The skill level associated with taunt interacts with your level to determine how likely the taunt is to be successful. It does not control the level of success. A successful taunt will *always* make the creature shift to you, assuming that it can be taunted (Yes, some creatures are untauntable).

    Taunt makes a creature hate you just a little bit more than it hates the next guy on the list. In other words, multiple successful taunts, or taunting a creature that is already attacking you doesn't do anything extra.

    I hope that clears up how Taunt works.

  • Skill Cap Question
    [Quote from original post: All of the new skill caps are still under what is acheivable by a level 50 character. So my question is do you need to buy Kunark in order to have higher skill caps?]
