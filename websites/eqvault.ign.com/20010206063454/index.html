Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 5:24 PM PST
Alan roared back onto the Gameplay Board with a vengence today. Here's the scoop:

  • Ranger Talk
    Hi all.

    You may already have seen a list of some of the things that we are already testing as changes for the ranger class. Essentially, we are adjusting the level at which some of their spells are attained to make them more in line with their power.

    Here is a list that I, once again, stole from Everlore because I'm lazy and because it seems accurate:

    a) Add Endure Cold at 22
    b) Add Skin Like Nature at 59
    c) Add FireFist at 22
    d) Move Bramblecoat from 49 to 39
    e) Add Spikecoat at 49
    f) Add resist cold at 55
    g) Add Panic Animal at 22
    i) Offense and weapons skill made identical to warriors

    Let's talk about these changes and others that might be needed to 'balance' rangers.

    Keep in mind that I primarily want to talk about 'balance' here. I'll certainly list any bugs that you would like to see addressed, but the focus should be balance. And please be concise when responding. These threads will get very long, and it helps me to be able to read all the responses if they are to the point.

    Let's hear what you have to say.

  • Nerf Absor!
    I would gladly except a nerf, especially if it was a nerf to my work schedule. Perhaps I would be unable to 'function' on Fridays. This would make me far less powerful...

  • Paladin Talk
    By now you may already have seen some of the things that we plan to do with the paladin class in the hopes of adjusting their balance. Those changes came about, mostly, from our folks reading the threads about the other hybrids and reading those about paladins that were started in preparation for this thread. Feel free to discuss those changes as well, though I think they are all very much in line with what is needed for the paladin.

    Here's a list that I stole from Everlore… I was too lazy to actually look them up (but this list seems accurate):

    a) Add Endure Magic spell at level 30
    b) Add Resist Magic at 55
    c) Move Guard spell from 49 to 39
    d) Move Armor of Faith from 53 to 49
    e) New level 53 paladin spell, Divine Glory, clone of divine strength but 100 hp instead of 200
    f) New level 59 spell, Celestial Cleansing 110 hp healdot per tick, for 4 ticks. 1.5 second casting time. Recast time 30 secs.
    g) Defense skill cap made exactly same as warriors

    So let's start the discussion. Please be detailed when explaining things. But at the same time, please be precise. I really don't need your background as a player, or detailed examples of gameplay (unless they are needed to explain a game mechanic). These threads get long, and anything that you folks can do to cut down the amount of text or increase the readability of the posts is appreciated.

    And remember, we're primarily discussing 'balance' here. Bugs and such are fine to point out, but they are not the main reason we are here.

    So let's hear what you have to say.

  • Rogue Talk
    Hello again.

    This will be the thread for discussion about the rogue class. The idea is to look at the things that need to be changed to make the class 'balanced'. With that as the primary goal, I am still interested in hearing about bugs and other such things. But, primarily, I want to talk about balance.

    My take on the rogue is pretty similar to that of the warrior. They are pretty well balanced. Obviously with EverQuest as a primarily player vs. environment game, some of the things that thieves traditionally do are not really part of the package. That's why, in EverQuest, they are titled rogues.

    Again, I have had little time (actually, no time at all) to do research on rogue concerns before starting this thread. So please be as detailed as you need to be to get your point across. But, at the same time, please remember that this thread will get long. So being concise and readable is important too.

    I do want to report one thing that we have already discovered internally about rogues that is broken and will be fixed when we make these changes. Yes, it is a nerf, but not in the true sense of the word.

    In the old days, NPCs had a limited size to their hate-list (basically the way that they track who they are angry at). In those days, evade would just drop you to the bottom of that list. Recently we increased the size of that list dramatically. This allows us to do a lot more with NPCs. But evade was never changed to reflect this, so it still drops you to the bottom of the hate-list. Essentially, this allows a rogue to do significant damage to an opponent, and then with the use of evade, become less 'hated' than someone that has done no damage to the NPC at all, but just has a somewhat negative faction with that NPC and is standing someplace on the other side of the zone. The rogue can then beat the NPC up with impunity. Basically, evade is far too useful for avoiding aggro from an NPC, and we're going to change it to be more in line with its original function.

    I wanted to let you know that at the outset. Please don't make it the focus of this thread. It’s certainly something we should talk about. But there are certainly other things that are worth discussion as well.

    So let's hear what you think.

  • More on Fan Faire
    Akriss-Ziller wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I don't know that either.....I will mention it to Abigale, maybe we can space the panels out when everyone comes to San Diego for the next one]

    Dude!

    There just is no way that one panel of two or three Verant people could answer questions from 1,200 people at once. We had to split them up and hold them all at the same time so that they would be of a manageable size. Though I was hoping for a more vocal and boisterous crowd in our panel, I heard that the rest were very lively.

  • Verant and Clerics
    I'm really going to be busy today, so I'm sorry for only skimming this thread before responding.

    Clerics will always, let me repeat, always be far superior healers than any other class. Keep a few things in mind here. Clerics get any shared healing spell many levels earlier than any other class. Clerics have a larger variety of healing spells to choose from, making them much more flexible in their healing powers. Clerics are also innately better healers than any other class. They will heal more hit points with the same spell than any other class (they have a built in bonus on all their healing).

    I had the pleasure of talking to several clerics at the Faire. After talking with them in depth about this change, each of them was willing to admit that it seemed like a fair enough thing to do. Admittedly, they had their reservations, but they accepted the changes as probably a good thing for the game.

    I wish some of you that disagree would have been able to come to the Faire and we could have talked about it in person.

    But I am convinced that this is the right thing to do. And, from my skimming this post, I really see no arguments that validly suggest otherwise.

  • Verant Banning People Expressing Their Opinions?
    ShockaZuulu wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I would like to know from a blue name if posting my thoughts strongly but not obscenely is now bannable.]

    Nope. Be as strong as you want. Just remain polite.

    And, yes, we can delete individual posts. But, unlike the edited ones, there is no way that you would be able to tell they had been removed, since they are gone…

    [Quote from original post: If the offending posts were deleted then say so. Please don't let us think that you are ogres.]

    To be perfectly honest, it's not worth our time to explain every post deletion or editing that we do. We're busy enough without doing that, and it's kind of like having the press televise car chases. If you live anywhere near Los Angeles, you've seen what that does. We actually had people that would start chases just to be on television. I'm not about to start any sort of hall of infamy on these boards. And our time is better spent doing other things.

    reckless_fist wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Hmm Absor was pretty rude in some of his responses to the bard talk thread. I hope he's getting banned or at least suspended.]

    Really? I didn't think I had (and I'm pretty good about noticing when I'm being rude).

    Yes, we are strict on these boards. We will not allow people to be rude to others. If that seems rude or controlling, then so be it. But all we want is to allow these boards to be a place where we can talk to you about the game, where we can work together to make the game better. Those that insist on being rude or offensive are not contributing to those goals.

  • Hybrid Changes on Test Server
    -Dr wrote:

    [Quote from original post: the new D&D rules are totally lame. Mages in plate armour? bah just plane silly]

    And, oddly enough, it was the D&D rules that first made us all think that spell casters should not be able to wear armor…

    The changes you see on test are not in any way going to make warriors obsolete. They will still have the best defense and offensive power (when compared to the hybrids). They will still have a significant hit point advantage. They will still have the best armor and weapons, and the greatest versatility in equipment.

    Also, the changes you see are not likely to be the last. They were generated from the talks we've been having, and they were the most obvious changes to address the hybrid classes. We are not done evaluating things, as should be obvious by the fact that I've only just opened the last to hybrid talks today.

    Slackjaw wrote:

    [Quote from original post: The thing that _does_ bother me, in reflection, is that aside from fixing a bug with 2 disciplines, it appears that the other warrior concerns were deemed inappropriate.]

    My comments above apply to all classes. These are just the immediate and obvious changes. We have not delved into the entirety of class balance, and we haven't even finished all the talks.

    Let's not jump the gun here.

  • Fan Faire Review
    Thanks. I had a blast, I think we all did.

    I kinds wish our forum had the kind of action that the others did. I was kind of looking forward to some heated debate... :)

Vox Slain by the Karana Council of Guilds
- Aurum @ 12:36 PM PST
Shoji Tsunami informed me of the following:

Name_Of_Submitter: Shoji Tsunami
Event_name: Vox Slain by the Karana Council of Guilds
Email: xphlyboy@yahoo.com
Guild_Sponsoring_Event: Sacred Band Alliance
Date: 02/02/2001
Time: 07:30 PM CST
Event_Description: Last night, February 2nd, the combined forces of the Karana Council of Guilds under the leadership of Haemish MacClellan and the Sacred Band Alliance, invaded Permafrost Keep to slay the fair Lady Vox. There were a number of interesting things that took place that made this raid so unique.

- The last server split took most if not all of our talented raid leaders and strategists, so Haemish, a Barbarian Warrior, whom at the tender age of 39 Seasons, took up the mantle of leadership by organizing and leading this raid.

- The final raiding parties consisted of 8 groups ranging in level from 52 at the high end all the way to our lone bard at 37(who was critical in keeping the forward group alive long enough for the nukers to kill Vox). All in all, the average level was about 46.

- The servers went down halfway through the raid – but we regrouped and cleared the castle again for a second push and then launched our attack.

- We did this raid with only 2, yep count em, 2 !!!! clerics (a 51 Vicar and a 40 Cleric) and 1 Bard.

- The raiding parties consisted of players from a collection of over 15+ small to medium sized guilds that all belong to the Karana Council of Guilds.


Lady Vox was very generous on this raid and dropped the following (sadly no FA books for the Paladins):

(2) McVaxius’ Horns of War – One defaulted to Merithment 37 of Illuminatus, and the other was won by Edina Elvandar of Haven of Light

Crystalline Spear – Tanniss Riverwinds - 51 Champion of Forever Knights

Staff of Forbidden Rites – Drusila (DRU) of Kindred of the Moon

Warhammer of the Divine Grace – Azuriem Angelgrace – 40 Cleric and Leader of guild Illuminatus

Tobrin’s Mystic Eyepatch – Morgail D’unistar - 51 Wizard of Fell Omen

King room loot – Vox Bracers to Salani (47 Warrior) and Crystalline Sword to Cretar (51 Ranger). With all the talk of Magic and Cold resists – you can imagine how hot and heavy the rolling was for the Vox Bracer .

We also managed to retrieve 2 Prickly Pears for the Shadowknight Lifeleech spell quest won by Silver Daggers and Fell Omen to be passed down to their respective ranking Shadowknights AND, 2 Blocks of Permafrost – marking the beginning of the epic quests for 2 more of Karana’s Warriors.


The KCoG would like to publicly thank Jzonn and Halagar on Ayonae Ro, Steelheart of Avenging Crusaders, Solonel, and Jannelle Alcedonia of Ashborne, and Pippi Langstrump of Silver Daggers all of whom provided the new management team with assistance in planning, strategy, and execution of the raid. Your advice and guidance in advance of this raid was the lynchpin of our success.

The Karana Council of Guilds is a loose community of dozens of small to medium size guilds. The KCoG sponsors a variety of player driven events ranging from player run “Battle-Chess” type chess tournaments in Butcher Block, to low level Tournaments all the way on up to Planes and Dragon raids. For guilds that are not planes or dragon capable, the KCoG provides a fantastic opportunity for players of sufficient levels in those guilds to experience these large target encounters. For more information on the KCoG please visit us on the web at http://go.to/karanacouncil.

Donal's Breastplate - Additional Clarifications
- Aurum @ 6:53 PM PST
Brad McQuaid shed some more light on the changes coming to Donal's Breastplate in this thread he created on the Gameplay Board:

    I just read through Absor's post and the replies to it.
    I wanted to clarify some things.

    First, it didn't take 6 months and 300,000 people to find the problem. The problem was only made apparent recently, with Velious and the removal of the HP cap. The breastplate didn't appear as a problem until encounters started lasting much longer than they had previously. We made one attempt to address the issue, found out it wasn't nearly enough, and now are faced with having to do this change.

    Removal of the HP cap added more variety to the game -- it changed strategies and tactics. No longer were we forced to simply make mobs kill in a very short period of time. Overall, this is a very good thing for the game -- variety and encounters that require differing tactics is good.

    That said, whenever we do change or add behavior or other significant aspects of the game, other unforeseen problems often surface. It's the nature of the genre -- these game's change and evolve, and every change, small or large, can have unexpected ripple effects.

    A second clarification: less than 5 or so mobs in Velious were created assuming the breastplate would be used, and this was mostly likely in error (i.e. we shouldn't have made that assumption in those cases). We will be addressing those mobs and tuning them down accordingly.

    In summary, I'm sorry we goofed up -- it does happen. It will happen again. It's the nature of the beast, but we will always do our best to minimize these occurrences. And we will also do our best to react as fairly as possible when we are forced to act, attempting to do the right thing for both the individual player as well as the game as a whole.

    It is unfortunate that the item was created in the first place, given its potential for problems, and it's also unfortunate that we didn't realize how much more magnified that problem would become when the HP cap was lifted.

    That said, again, we're not perfect, and we will continue to make mistakes as long as this game continues to grow and evolve. And, no matter how dedicated our testing staff is, or how carefully we watch for problems on test, we will miss things that 300,000+ players will find. I don't see this changing, and I certainly don't think this is something unique to EQ, but rather something inherent to the genre -- no other type of game changes as often and as significantly, nor involves such a huge community of players who both work with and compete with each other.

    I know that what I've said probably won't stop the flaming, and I know this is disappointing to both players who have the item as well as those who were planning on obtaining it one day -- I don't expect to post and have happiness follow -- but I did want to at least try to explain the situation in more detail, and to clarify it. And I also think it's important for us to try to manage expectations as they relate to the future.

    -Brad
