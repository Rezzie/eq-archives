Patch Message: 12-01-00
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 3:15 PM PST
Last night a new message went up on the EQ Patch Server:

    December 1st, 7:00pm
    ------------------------------

    *SBLive Soundcards and Hardware Acceleration*

    Due to some past problems with SBLive cards, users of those cards who called into tech support were asked to turn "Hardware Acceleration" down one notch in DXDIAG. The last patch corrected those problems and most users should again increase hardware acceleration to maximum.

    To do this:

    1. Click "Start"
    2. Click "Run"
    3. Type "DXDIAG.EXE" and press enter.
    4. Click on the "Sound" tab.
    5. Increase the "Hardware Acceleration" slider to maximum.

    Doing this should correct issues such as not hearing certain sounds or sound volume disparities.

    The only users who may not want to do this are Windows 2000 users. Creative Labs is currently working on new drivers for Win2K, so you may wish to wait until these drivers are completed prior to increasing hardware acceleration.

    Finally, all SBLive users should get LiveWare 3.0 from http://www.soundblaster.com/liveware/. After installing LiveWare, you will then want to REINSTALL the latest drivers for your sound card. The order of installation is important.

    - The EverQuest Team


Nerfs, Scrapped Features & More
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 12:00 AM PST
Brad McQuaid added his comments to yesterday's thread focusing on a variety of EQ issues including nerfs:

    I do recall replying to AuntieShaynta publicly quite some time ago, stating that it was still our intent to implement Enchanters enchanting items, but that such plans were still very much in the planning stages and considered long term.

    And I stand by it -- I still think we'll see that one day (and given our desire to pretty much freeze class balancing, it will probably be one of the last significant class enchancements). But hey, never say never :) Consider traps :)

    Also, if I recall correctly, item enchantment was also talked about in earlier pre-release (probably pre-beta, but I'm not certain) FAQs, but I later had it removed (along with guildhouses, lycanthropy, vampirism, etc.) It simply was one of the few features we'd planned on but didn't have time to deliver on.

    It does trouble me a little that even though I try to be clear about whether something is short term, or long, or something we're only thinking about, etc. that it often turns into a 'promise' over time... but I guess that's unavoidable. It's a form of the 'telephone game' quite often, where one person hears that we said one thing, and then they tell somebody else, and the message sort of evolves from there :)

    Just to let you all know, there are still a TON of things we want to add to EQ, and we feel EQ has a very long life ahead of it. For example, although it's too early to say much, I will say that we've got some VERY big plans post-Velious.

    And Tera, wow... not sure what to say :( I certainly do my best to live up to my word. Again, though, some times things I talk about as something planned evolve somehow into 'promises'... And then some things I hopefully characterized as 'long term' are still just that: long term plans that haven't happened yet.

    All things considered I'm very proud of our track record and just how much of the game we talked about pre-release game into fruition. And I'm also very proud of where the live and expansion teams have taken the game post-release. IMHO, EQ is almost everything we'd planned it to be and a whole lot more.

    -Brad

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:55 PM PST
This is today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • "We're locking threads..."
    Posts that are monopolizing the front page with a particular issue are being locked. That's clearly defined as against the rules in the board FAQ. Chances are that if an issue is that important it's already been addressed a number of times. The mage pets issue that's currently hot falls under this definition. We've announced exactly what changes were made, and no changes other than what we've told you have been made. We've continued "looking into" the issue over the past several days and can find absolutly nothing to suggest that any other changes have occurred. If something changes we'll let you know, but until then the issue is closed.

    Posts that are rude or antagonistic are being locked. See the FAQ.

    Posts asking why threads are being locked are being locked. Posting threads of this nature is essentially spamming the boards, so please don't do it.

    Thank you for your cooperation,

    -Gordon

  • Pets
    I did some testing, specifically with 49th level necro and mage pets, and compared them to my results from early this year. Both pets were naked, no buffs, and both had similar results to what occurred earlier this year with the same tests.

    I'm not sure what I can say to put everyone's concerns to rest.

  • Verant Ignoring Reasonable Requests?
    This issue has come up a number of times and I've explained our position. We do not do ad hoc moves between servers.

    The sheer number of requests along this line that would come in would keep a staff three times our size busy 24 hours per day. Remember that there are over 300K people playing EverQuest, and number of characters are well into the millions.

    Now it has been suggested that we should charge for the service, but we feel that server communities are important, and that characters should have to live with their reputations.

    The short of it is that it is not economically feasible to do ad hoc moves, and even if we were to set it up so that it was, we'd still be dealing with the issue of the importance of server communities.

  • Endless Threads on Same Subject
    Endless new threads on the same topic: Why they hurt everyone...

    It's simple. People go looking for "official" posts on an issue, and instead of having to look at two or three, they have to filter through dozens. The ones with the official replies are missed and end up down on page 10, while post after post gets bumped to the front page asking "Why are you ignoring this". Please, do everyone a favor and use the search tool to look and see if there's a post on your issue that may have been answered already. I've personally answered 3 of these pet posts today, and read at least half a dozen threads asking why we haven't responded to the issue since then.

  • More Q&A
    [Quote from original post: Did you say that DEX was much lower for pets now ? ]

    No. The Dex of pets is not lower than it ever was. What is lower is their proc percentage chance based off of that dex. It used to roll of the NPC table and now rolls of a PC table.

    [Quote from original post: Does DEX affect the fact that an Attack lands, and also affects damage ? ]

    No. It does not.

    [Quote from original post: Did you also lower AGI on pets now ? ]

    We have not lowered any pet stats.

    [Quote from original post: Now... I have the same example in about 4 or 5 threads of a battle with a blue croc in other posts. My pet was getting hit for more damage, and was hitting less often for less damage. Coincidence ? How can 2 hours of trying the same thing be coinsidence ? I played in Oasis for a few hours prior patch and it wasn't that way. ]

    You aren't dealing with constants when fighting things in Oasis. NPCs have level ranges. Pets have level ranges. Hit and miss percentages have ranges. Damage dealt and damage taken have ranges. A combination of good or bad rolls on one side or another can make it appear that things have changed in one way or another, but they haven't.

    I've been back and forth with several programmers over the past two weeks about this, in addition to running my own tests and having them run theirs. The only determination that we've made is that aside from the procing weapon issue, which I think people understand as a bug fix at this point, is that they have not changed.

    Perhaps the best way I can "prove" to everyone that they haven't changed is by pointing to the lack of people with concrete figures here. When we *really* change something, the board is flooded with a certain type of player making complaints. These are the people who log and parse everything, and can find changes with the combat engine down to single percentage points. Those people are not here. What we have are people who "get the feeling that" something is different.

    Trust me, if there was something different with the basic way pets were in combat, there would be players here with exact figures explaning exactly what changed.

  • When will SoV go live?
    Providing that retailers don't sell it too soon, it will be live on the servers by the time you get home from the store with your box.

  • Patches and Bugs
    We correct outstanding issues with nearly every patch. As much as I'm sure everyone would like to see every outstanding issue addressed in one patch, it's not realistic for us to do that. Just because we don't fix all of them in every patch, or don't reply every time they are reposted on the bug board, doesn't mean that we aren't listening.

    By the way, could you explain #2 a little better? I can't make sense of it.

  • Polls and Statistics
    My first instinct was to pass this post up because it uses the "pull numbers of out thin air and call them facts" method of debate. The only way to "address" a post of this nature is to spend a day researching all of the correct numbers for the purpose of showing what really is fact. That would take too much time and only validate this method as a way to get a response, which is why we pass these posts up.

    That said, there is one claim I would like more information on:

    What server is this that you play on that you've noticed a 200-300 player drop at peak over the past year?

  • Female Rest Animation
    We don't like the way that the stance looks. Perhaps we'll go through and revisit it in the future, but no promises at present.

    AuntieShaynta:

    Well, I stand by my 'victims' statement. I'm not going to argue it with you, except to suggest that perhaps you didn't read it as I intended to write it.     I've been following this game as long as anyone, longer than most. I've seen what you saw too, though not in exactly the same way.

    At first the idea of enchanting weapons was just that, an idea. It became a 'something we'd really like to do' thing before beta. During beta it became a 'we really want to do it, but it doesn't look like we can' thing. At release it was the same way, something that they wanted to do. I don't remember any promises, from Brad or anyone else. But I might have missed that.

    I can't say I disagree with you about the apparent ability that another class might or might not be getting to do something that seems very similar to enchanting an item. Enchanters are unique in their way, as you say. They get pets at all levels. But you'll note that they also have very weak pets, in comparison. That is by design.

    I think the problem here is perspective. Mine is that enchanters are secondary pet classes, and therefore they shouldn't have pets that get to dual wield without a weapon. Yours is that you are a pet class and therefore your pets deserve that ability. I don't know what I can say to change your perspective. And I don't know what you can do to change mine. I only wish you could understand why we made that choice. It's really not a matter of 'compensation' for anything done in the past. It's a matter of how it should be. We can't sacrifice the balance of the game just to compensate for things we might have done in the past.

Clarification on Pet Changes
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:06 PM PST
Pets have been a hot issue lately on EverQuest boards across the net. In the following thread Alan summarizes things up:

    Let me summarize, for convenience sake, the changes that we have made to pets over the course of the last few patches.

    Pets wielding weapons will now proc with those weapons just as frequently as a player character with that weapon and the same stats. This has reduced the frequency of weapon procs from pets. To make this change we simply removed an artificial multiplier to pet proc chances, no stats or attributes were changed.

    Pets wielding the Sword of Runes will proc with that sword as often as they do with any other procing weapon, but it will only proc against its intended target, summoned creatures.

    Magician and necromancer pets (and because they use the same spells, shadowknight pets as well) now have the ability to duel wield without a weapon, if they are powerful enough. This will happen with some regularity with pets cast from 39th level (52nd for shadowknights) spells and above.

    No other changes to the duel wielding ability of any other pets was changed. This ability was not given to any other pet because magicians and necromancers are primary pet classes, the others are not.

    Alan

    - Added some code to address reports from people who find exiting from the character-select screen back to the server-select screen rather slow when mip-mapping is enabled. Most of this problem is due to routines in DX required to release resources consumed by mip-mapping and may not be addressable until the next version of DirectX is adopted.

    - Changed customizable key mappings so that the ESC key will clear the "NOKEY" setting instead of the "Backspace". This means that you can now assign "Backspace" to a function in game.

    - Corrected a sound problem that would cause certain sounds to be played "rapid-fire" under some circumstances. There is still a potential for this to happen with some other sounds that will be addressed in a patch in the near future.

    - Corrected a problem in Kedge Keep that made the top encounter more difficult than intended after the server had been up for a while.

    - Higher level Magician, Necromancer, and Shadowknight pets now have the ability to dual-wield without being handed weapons. The calculation used to determine whether or not this ability exists takes several things into account, but for the most part 39th level and above summons (higher for the Shadowknight) will have this ability. It is possible that you could get a "good" 34th level pet that will, or a "bad" 39th level pet that will not have this ability.

    - Bards of the land, hark unto me: The notes have been scribed on tattered parchment, two score and nine, for you they are meant. Abandon your mates, though they may fume, and follow the call of the seafarer's tune.

    - The Monk's hand-to-hand skill cap has been raised to a level equal to their one-handed blunt skill.

Event Report - Bertoxxulous
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:53 PM PST
A detailed report of a recent event on the Bertoxxulous server was sent to me today by Iuveniel:

Today as we went about our business in the fair city
of Firiona Vie, a shout was heard throughout the area
proclaiming that the defenders of the city come forth
and speak with a man by the name of Danak Dhorentath.
Upon arriving at the stone bridge we glimpsed an Iksar
figure in the distance, away from the guards. Curious
we went forth to his side and asked what business he
had demanding that we come to his call. Unworried
about being surrounded by the Defenders of Firiona
Vie, he explained that he had cast a ritual that would
shield him from harm and that we would do best to
entertain his speech.

This Iksar, Danak Dhorentath, apparently is
responsible for the kidnapping of Firiona Vie and has
held her these past six months torturing her for the
information only she herself can give up. Danak
talked of the staff that she carried, a blessed weapon
of Tunare, and how his necromancer friend Vahlai had
discovered a ritual to steal away the Elven Princess'
powers. These powers would be given instead to
Vahlai, Danak, and the evil Iksar Venril Sathir. He
spoke of power over all of Norrath and how one person
in attendance had the power to stop this ritual,
though he never alluded to who it may be except in
vague hints and cryptic words.

This person would be presented a chance at some point
to stop the ritual or else claim the power with Danak
and rule over Norrath. The evil being also spoke of
Galeth and the Companions returning to the lands of
Kunark. Only time shall tell what the future holds
for our world and if Firiona Vie shall be released!

/ooc This event was wonderfully played out. The group
of people gathered was only 10-15 strong at first, but
then began to grow as word spread, although it never
exceeded 25. The GM did a wonderful job of keeping
the story VERY interesting and entertaining and I for
one am happy that I was able to participate. One
person did attempt to strike down Danak but quickly
met his demise. VERY quickly! I applaud everyone at
the event for doing such a wonderful job roleplaying
it out and hope to see you all on the next segment of
the tale!
