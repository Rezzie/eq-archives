Adventuring: Key Guide

Author: Saoshen (N/A)   Organization: N/A

Overview/background

Over the course of the last 7 months, I have been shown, and have shown other players how to make more efficient use of the EQ user interface. Specifically, how you can setup the keyboard and hotkeys tailored for your particular class/playing style. I play a 20's monk on Cazic-Thule, so most of my experience comes from the melee style of play. I have enjoyed both great excitement, and frustration with the world of Norrath. I have been to many areas, but by no means have I been to them all. I recently filled up my test server screen with new chars, to check out the other classes, as Saoshen has been the first and only character I have played up until lately. So far I have a 6 lvl bard, rogue, and warrior, a 4th druid and, an enchanter and shaman still to get started on. Hopefully this guide will help not only new players, but will teach some 'old dogs new tricks', so to speak. : )

Keyboard mapping

After playing Half-Life for several months, I came to like the keyboard arrangement, and have since rearranged any game I play to slightly emulate the HL configuration, with a slight adjustment. These are the movement and and bindable key settings I use for EverQuest. I have an MS Natural Keyboard Pro, so most keys are bound to the left side.

KEY Action Explaination
Q Auto-Attack This is probably the *MOST* important key to change. By default the attack key is 'A', and many people have died when they accidentally tried to talk to an NPC without first pressing the ENTER key. If you change nothing else, change this key , or you will wish you had.
W /con (Consider) Placing this next to the attack key should help you to remember to always con, before you attack. : )
E Forward Move forward.
R /reply Inserts a /tell to the last person to messaged you.
T /tell Inserts a /tell at the log window, makes it easier to send a msg to someone. (Sure wish EQ had nick completion, like mIRC)
I Inventory Brings up the inventory screen where you may access your equipment, loot, and stats. I keep this here because I was to lazy to change to anything closer, and I was running out of keys on the left side. ; P
A Nearest NPC Selects the nearest NPC, This will help select an NPC in the middle of a large group, it is good for both casters and meleer's. WARNING, you do NOT want to use this when GOOD NPC's are near, as you might accidentally attack a guard or other, and end up dead, or with a bad faction hit. : (
S Turn left Turns you to the left.
D Backup Moves backwards. NOTE, backwards is slower than running forwards, so don't try to retreat like this unless you got the HP to spare.
F Turn right Turns you to the right.
G Hail The default greeting.
Z Nearest PC Selects the Nearest PC, which may or not be a member of your group.
X, C toggle screen modes, view Set these to your preference of toggling the full screen modes, and views (3rd person). When traveling, 3rd person mode is usually better to use, as you get to see a much greater area around you. Also good for us non caster types, to check out the surrounding area while sitting/healing.
V Crouch Crouches down so that you may fit through smaller doors, etc.
SPACE BAR Jump Jump up, this uses up your stamina pretty quick. (In my experience)
CAPS LOCK Auto-run There is no visual identification in options, but you can set the caps lock to the auto run.
TAB   The tab key toggles between YOU and the last selected object. good for a quick heal, then return to combat.
F1-F6   Selects the members of your party, starting with you.

 

Hotkey Initial Setup

When you first create a character, typically the first things you will want to do is get your hotkeys in order. You may also want to set your auto split option on too, as this is off on a new char. You will need to select the combat screen and right click on a slot number on the right side of the screen and associate an attack to the button. Do this for each button, NOTE the melee attack, and range attack cannot be changed, and you may not have enough, or too many skills to apply.

Do the same for the abilities screen. Some attacks will also appear on the abilities screen, thus giving you the option to quickly change a button to an attack on the fly later on. (explained later)

The Social screen may seem to be the least useful one, but you can reassign the buttons to do or say many things, and up to 5 at a time per button. To change a key, right click on it, then click the fields to change. The field will clear when you select it, so you cannot edit the existing text. : (

Some useful buttons to define...

KEY Explaination
Start /log on (turns on logging)

/played (a reminder on how much time you have, uh hmm, spent on this character)

/ all friend (lists all your friends)

/time (displays game time, and local time on your computer)
Corpse /corpse (this will summon your corpse, within a short distance. also good for checking to see if you have any in zone)

/loc (will display your coordinates within the zone, good if you checked before you died)

/corpse (i repeat so that it is easier to drag it while running...)

/loc (repeat)

/corpse (repeat)
Loc /loc (I also have a separate button for /loc, when you don't necessarily need the /corpse part, optionally you can put corpse on all 5 lines)
Follow /follow (auto follows the party member you have selected, heed the warning you get when you use it)
Assist /assist (selects the monster that is attacking the person that was selected, and starts attacking)
Yell /yell (a newer command, when you need help, people in close proximity can hear which direction you need help in)
Speak (put any commonly used text you may want to say /say, for normal, /gsay for group only, etc)
Sell or Buy /auction ( put any items you may have for sale, or want to buy)
other if you like to use the emotes a lot, put your favorites on their own button so you don't have to type all the time [ ;
   

Once all your buttons are defined on the right side, you can now click and hold until the button stays on your pointer, then drop on to the bank and slot position that you want. If you want to cancel, just drop into main window. Sometimes it doesn't drop in easy, just keep trying till it gets in there. If you want to change a slot, just drop the new button onto the old one, they will switch on the mouse. You can then put it in a new slot or drop into main window to get rid of it. If you want to completely remove a button from a slot, drag it to the main window.

If you are a caster, you may already know, that you can do the same thing with spells. Click and hold the spell from the memorized spell slot, drop on the bank and slot you want. You can also select the SPELLS button in the same way, drop it into a slot so you don't have to switch to window mode to med. Be careful not to right click on a spell unless you want to forget it, but you can right click and HOLD and the stats of the spell will be displayed. 

 

Recommended button placement

Upon logging in, you always start with the default screen mode, windowed, set to walk, and with the hotkey bank on 1. So I put all my startup buttons there.

Bank 1 - Startup tasks

KEY Explanation
1 SPELLS button (for bringing up spell book), Mend (if your a monk), any other heal type skill
2 Corpse key that was previously defined on the socials screen
3 Sense heading (so you know which direction you are faced) if you have a corspe loc spell or song this is also a good place to put it.
4 Sneak, hide, etc, or conversely, SOW or speed spell (to get to your corpse and drag it to a safer place to loot)
5 The START button previously defined in the Socials screen (to start log, find friends, etc)
6 RUN/WALK button, man I really wished EQ would preserve some of these settings when you log : (
   

 

Bank 2 - Melee tasks (up close stuff)

KEY Explanation
1 melee attack skills (backstab, kick, punch, slam, tiger, etc)
2 melee attack skills (backstab, kick, punch, slam, tiger, etc)
3 Hide, sneak, sense heading (so you know which direction you are faced)
4 secondary skills that can be slipped in before, during, and after attack (beg, pickpocket, instill doubt)
5 Range attack (bow and arrows, or throwing knives, shurikens, etc)
6 Sit/stand, or the SPELLS book, to rest/med
   

 

Bank 3 - Casting tasks (further away stuff)

KEY Explanation
1 range attack/spell or melee attack skills (kick, punch, slam, tiger, etc)
2 closer spells or melee attack skills (kick, punch, slam, tiger, etc)
3 primary use spell (depends on class ie, nuker, heal, snare, etc) Sense heading (so you know which direction you are faced)
4 secondary skills that can be slipped in before, during, and after attack (beg, pickpocket, instill doubt)
5 Range attack, or secondary buffs/heals
6 Sit/stand, or the SPELLS book, to rest/med
   

 

Bank 4 - Non combat tasks

KEY Explanation
1 Fishing
2 sneak/hide
3 Sense heading (so you know which direction you are faced)
4 sneak/hide
5 beg
6 Sit/stand, or the SPELLS book, to rest/med
   

 

Bank 5 - Speaking/Social tasks

KEY Explanation
1 invite
2 disband
3 yell
4 buying
5 selling
6 speaking
   

 

Bank 6 - Camping/healing tasks

KEY Explanation
1 bind wound
2 camp
3 sense heading
4 mend/heal
5 feign/hide/sneak
6 sit
   

 

Other info

The screenshots here are mostly taken from shots I've taken as I explore the world, a few I made special for this guide, so they will tend to evolve as characters grow and figure out which are most important. Now that you got your keys organized, your raking in the XP, you got a new skill.... now what? Well, you can right click on any button with a skill or attack and switch to any other skill or attack available. This will change ALL the same buttons on any bank, so don't go switch off mend, feign death, or any other important thing on one bank, thinking you can switch to an alternate bank and it will still be there...

I am always trying to use any skills whenever they are available, like sense heading, sneak, hide, etc. Casual use when not busy will definitely help keep your skills honed to the maximum for your level.

 

Summary

The hotkey system available in EQ, may not necessarily be the best system, but it does allow a fair amount of customization, especially since there are so many available race/class combinations. A little organization and standardization will help you live and fight with a minimum of fuss. I hope I have made at least a few things clearer with this guide, and I am always looking for any new or more effective techniques. Find a system that works with you, instead of getting in your way, and you will be one step closer to playing with the god(s), whichever you worship. : ) Good luck and see you around Norrath!
