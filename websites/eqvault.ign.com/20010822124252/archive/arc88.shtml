Gordon Infobits: Sunday Posts
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:49 PM PDT
Gordon made a few posts today on the Gameplay Board. Here's a wrap-up of them:

  • Why was my account banned?
    This is not the type of issue that we discuss in public. Peoples account status is only discussed directly with them. If the owner of the account would like to contact the head GM at lcarrick@verant.com, his questions will be answered.

  • Questions for Gordon
    [Quote from original post: 1) If the Firey Avenger is the only 2hs that will allow a player to bash while it is equipped, why can a stupid little skeleton with a rusty 2hs bash?]

    NPCs are not bound by the same rules as player characters. They can bash/backstab without weapons, they don't take damage from falling, they don't require air, and their spells don't use components.

    [Quote from original post: 2) If all of us players would be upset when all of a sudden a dual-wield capable Mobs did dual-wield, why were all the weapons removed from the dual-wielding Mobs?]

    I'm not quite sure that I understand this question. Many NPCs carry weapons.

    [Quote from original post: 3) If a Ranger is a hybrid of a Warrior and a Druid, why does the Ranger track at level 1 and the Druid at 20?]

    Class perk.

    [Quote from original post: 4) Why is the Ranger the only hybrid that retains dual-wield and kick?]

    Another class perk. Rangers have melee skills nearly equaling the warrior, and in return give up heavy armor and hit points.

    [Quote from original post: 5) Back to number 1, how can a dual-wielding Ranger NPC bash?]

    See number 1 :)

  • Canadian @Home Problems
    I've received a few emails from a Videon customer in Canada who's getting timed out by a router in New York every minute or so for about 30 seconds, I assume that this is the same problem. At this point, my understanding is that the responsbile parties haven't acknowledged responsibility for the issue yet.

    Web users wouldn't really notice this timeout which is why I suspect that there haven't been too many complaints. Real-time games are another matter. I would suggest that Canadian customers having this problem be very firm with their ISPs on the issue.

Brad on the Gameplay Board
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:32 PM PDT
Brad McQuaid responded to a thread asking a few questions about what's next for EverQuest as a whole. Here's the original post and Brad's response from the Gameplay Board:

    I'm going to take a moment here to eat some crow and commend Verant on Kunark.
    Kunark is split into two apects: Levels past 50, and the Kunark zones. Both have been extremely well done, and both exhibit an intellegence and creativity that your past work didn't even hint at. The new kunark zones and the amazing capabilities of characters past level 50 make the rest of the game pale in comparison.

    Now we've gotten to the subject of my post: What about the rest of the game? Kunark is stunning but there are still zones in the "old game" that aren't even near fully utilized.

    Will work on the "old game" be abandoned in favor of doting on the new and expensive-to-create Kunark?

    Will we ever see graphical enhancements to the "old game" to bring it at least a little more in-line with the current majesty of Kunark? Will "The Hole" ever open!? Will Odus ever be worth going to? Will NPC's which previously just stood around (and there are a lot of them - the majority in fact) saying nothing when you hailed them ever start saying things?

    Will the "Old Game" ever be more polished than it is now, or will you neglect it in favor of your pretty, new Kunark?


    Faxor,

    First, thanks for the compliments -- Kunark was an opportunity to learn from our mistakes and do better. And in the future, we'll top Kunark

    Now, to your questions.

    1. No, we will not be updating the graphics on the old zones. This is primarily for two reasons: a. Resources are limited and I really think most of our customers would rather see new content as opposed to re-doing older content, and b., as was mentioned in this thread, it's not really appropriate to change the system requirements on the existing lands, which upgrading the graphics significantly would. That said, we have at least one minor way we're going to be upgrading the original world soon.

    2. The 'old game' will definitely NOT be abandoned. The EQ Live team will continue to address crowding issues by revamping the lesser used dungeons and other zones. In addition, many of the zone mechanics that are being tried in Kunark, should they prove successful, will be used in the older zones when they are revamped as well.

    3. The Hole is still on target for May, probably late May. After the Hole comes the Warrens, which is also on Odus. And we will continue to add free zones after that.

    4. Yes, I think slowly but surely existing NPCs will be made more interesting, possibly involved in quests and storylines.

    Hope that helps,

    -Brad

Dungeon Crawl - Luclin
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:19 PM PDT
Mince sent me the following event announcement:

Mince's Dungeon Crawls (Luclin server): Runnyeye, May 3rd

Greetings adventurers, I am Mince, a proud Gnome Gemchopper and Nomad Lore guild officer. I am of the opinion that many areas of Norrath, especially dungeons, are underutilized. In order to promote these areas as well as the art of the Dungeon Crawl, I intend to periodically lead expeditions to these locales. The last expedition to Befallen was a great success with over 40 participants combing the dungeon. I am looking for hearty adventurers to join me in these expeditions. Be forewarned that dungeons are dangerous and have their own unique challenges (i.e. locked doors, traps, large groups of monsters, etc.). Also, it will be a Dungeon Crawl (meaning we will be moving from room to room, not camping in the same spot). Therefore, power levelers, campers, and people that run at the first sign of danger may not find this experience to there liking. However, for those who enjoy joining a band of adventurers and conquering a dungeon while focusing on fun, companionship, and defeating foul beasts, please come out and join me.

Target: Runnyeye Citadel, a goblin stronghold located between Misty Thicket and the Gorge. It is a huge dungeon that I know very little about. It contains hordes of goblins as well as some other exotic creatures like Evil Eyes and Gelatinous Cubes.
Level range: 15-30th. Groups will be formed the day of the event. Rough group ranges will be 15-20, 21-25, and 26-30.
Rallying point/time: The Wall in Misty Thicket at 8 pm Pacific time (Wednesday, May 3rd). Please be geared up and ready to go. It is encouraged that you bind yourself in Rivervale beforehand. The dungeon is roughly 1 minute travel time from the rallying point.

Any questions? Contact mince@luclin.com or Mince in-game. And be on the lookout for future Mince's Dungeon Crawls in the weeks and months to come.
Thank you!

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:00 PM PDT
Here's the Late Night Edition of the Gordon Infobits for your reading pleasure:

  • Posion Update..sorta
    In cases where we have a firm date, we don't have any problem giving them. Unfortunately this is not one of those times, and I would just be making things up if I gave you one. I can say that the chances of getting it done before the end of the month of May is slim but possible. The month of June is looking better.

  • Player Events
    [Quote from original post: I've been thinking, about creating a tounament, a fighting tournament. Once a month earth time, with 6 divisions, one for each level group and class. Only natives to that city may participate. With great prizes for the winners. Held in the city's arena.(Pvp area) Would this be a good idea? Would many people come? What do you think?]

    Well we've appoited someone to coordinate events. Soon we'll have an email address that you can send requests to for assistance. It should be pretty interesting.

  • Kunark Sounds Update
    [Quote from original post: I heard that the Kunark sounds will be added in a later patch. As of now, Iksar make no noise when they get hit, and everything else sounds like a human (goblins, skeletons...). Any ETA?]

    Aiming for a week, perhaps two.

  • RoK Refund Confirmations
    You will indeed receive a confirmation when your request is processed. We ask that everyone who's getting one hang tight, much of it has to be done manually since the request is filed through email.

  • Binding in Kunark
    [Quote from original post: So what's the story with not being able to bind in the zone? Casters can bind in every zone except for one why? Magic barrier? Hole in the ozone layer? What?]

    Actually, with Kunark, we implemented many zones that do not allow binding, dungeons espicially. As for not being allowed to bind in this zone, I think that even some of our bigger critics will agree that allowing binding in this zone created a bit of a balance issue.

  • NPC Binding
    But on to your question, we've discussed the issue of NPCs binding recently. It's been a bit of a recurring topic, and we've firmly decided against allowing it. There are a number of reasons, only one of which is the economy of lower level casters. For example, we want to encourage people to meet each other, and giving everyone a reason to come to the cities is a good way to do that.

  • Server Status
    [Quote from original post: just curios because the new servers don't show up on the eqvault or everlore evermon pages. it also shows veeshan and the other splitters went back online at the same time as the others]

    Yep. The other web sites have to get their developers in to update their pages before the new servers will show. Saryrn and The Seventh Hammer are up.

    Note from Aurum: We're working to see what the problem here is. Whenever new servers are added our Server Scanner adds them automatically.

  • No Prexus Split
    The message on the scheduled downtime page that mentioned Prexus and a split was incorrect. It was supposed to say Bertox. As for whether or not Prexus will ever be split, it's too early to say for certian.

  • Ape Question
    [Quote from original post: Why are all the Apes in burning woods Undead?? Shouldn't they be animals? They sure look very much alive.]

    Just checked into this. They are indeed meant to be undead. The other live apes in the land are a little too evolved to be considered animals in EQ.

  • More on Veeshan's Peak
    [Quote from original post: AT NO TIME SHOULD A PLAYER RUN THE RISK OF LOSEING EVERYTHING THEY OWN PERIOD. I can understand a risk of getting killed and loseing Exps if they want to do something make deaths in these zones no res so its a permant exp loss and they have to earn it back or put a delay in the cropse recovery time so that after 24 real hours your corpse will pop back to the zone line or to a safe room.]

    A little bit of fear is a good thing. It keeps things exciting. If the thought of losing what's on your corpse is not palatable, the simplest suggestion would be to not put yourself in a situation where that could happen. This isn't a case where you risk everything you own, just everything that you choose to carry with you.

    [Quote from original post: There is NO FUN in loseing a corpse ]

    Perhaps not, however there is great fun being in a situation where you know that you could lose your corpse, and prevail.

  • Ninja Looting
    [Quote from original post: Ok I want ask you folks a question and I'd like you tell me what you think. Ninja looting is now forbidden. What constitutes "Ninjalooting"?

    Situation 1.)Lets say your in Oasis, your prowling around looking for DW crocs to kill. On the ground next to the lake is spectre corpse. There is noone around. You wait for a minute and still see noone. Or at least noone is medding nearby, no pc's are hovering nearby.

    Is it ok to loot this corpse or should you let it rot?

    Situation 2.)Your in freeport and run accross a dead guard. Again nooone is around. Is it ok to loot the corpse?

    Situation 3.) your in a group killing orcs in crushbone. Noone moves to loot the fallen orcs.

    How long do you wait until you loot them?

    And do you folks leave groups with people who don't adhere to loot orders?

    I don't mean to sound like some greedy SOB but this game requires money to play. My mage has expensive spells to buy, priest has spells AND money to buy, etc.But I want to be fair as well. Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition

  • A 'No Nerf' Server
    [Quote from original post: Why can't we have a server that does not have anything to with balance of power. This server will just add fun content. I mean if some class comes up with a good idea then they just add it. No more hearing that it will make things unbalance.]

    One of the foundations of massively multiplayer gaming, and gaming in general for that matter, is that there must be challenge in order for the game to be fun. In this game, game balance is required for there to be a challenge. Implementing suggestions without concern for game balance would lead to there being less "fun" in our opinion.

    Most of the people, even those who feel that there class is horrendously underpowered, would agree that if every suggestion, even every "good" suggestion, was implemented, that the game would be trivialized. In the end, we'd be constantly tuning things up, trying to reach a point where each class felt that they were powerful enough, yet when each individual class was enhanced, other classes would still feel that they get the short end of the stick.

    Though it's somewhat taking it to extremes, the end result would be a game where every NPC had 1 hitpoint. At that point, every class would finally be immensely powerful, yet the game would be awfully boring.

  • Spell Casting & Animation
    [Quote from original post: My character casts all his spells the same way (the same animation for casting). Since we have all these new emotes around, would it be possible to integrate that into the spells?]

    Funny that you should mention that. Keep an eye on the gestures made by some of the higher level casters :)

  • NPCs and Taunting
    Some NPCs aren't tauntable. However, given the fact that you play on Morell-Thule, I'm pretty certain that you haven't reached many of those yet.

    Taunt only makes the target hate you slightly more than it hates the next character on the list. It might be advantageous for the person you are trying to taunt the critter off of to back off a bit and let you get a bit of a head start before jumping back in.

Brad on Swaying Trees & Preformance Issues
[23 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:44 PM PDT
EQ Executive Producer Brad McQuaid produced the following post on the Gameplay Board regarding some RoK speed issues:

    Just to clear up this rumor:

    The trees swaying have NOTHING to do with framerate.

    Two things affect framerate: number of polygons drawn, and fill rate (the number of objects in front of other objects in front of other objects, all of which have to be drawn).

    Framerate goes down in a forest because of the additional polygons needed to respresent all the trees, as well as the trees behind the trees behind the trees having to be drawn.

    Anyway, there are three general ways to increase your framerate (starting from easiest to hardest):

    1. Adjust your clipping plane down under the display options section.

    2. Buy a faster 3D card.

    3. Upgrade your CPU/Computer

    The level of 3D sound can also affect overall framerate, depending on your system's configuration (which sound card, etc.) so you might want to experiment with those settings as well.

    Bottom line, Kunark pushes the EQ engine a lot harder, hence the better visuals. Even though this was perhaps a non-issue for you with the rest of Norrath, when you're on Kunark you might now need to adjust your display settings.

    -Brad

    ps. just to be very clear, the animating trees do not measurably affect your framerate.

    pps. framerate is how fast your screen is updated and has little to do with 'lag', which is generally another word for 'latency' which has to do with internet performance (timing issues, sending data between your client and our servers). hope that helps :)


Gordon on Kunark's Preformance
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 5:36 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn had the following to say about the preformance of Kunark hardware-size on the Gameplay Board:

    As Brad mentioned in another thread, the swaying trees really have nothing to do with the framerates that people are getting. What is probably more responsible is the fact that the zones are very large and that we allow you to push your clipping plane out quite far.
    The beautifully crafted landscapes of Kunark push the abilities of your graphics card and CPU further than the lands shipped with EQ proper. The outdoor zones are also densily populated with NPCs.

    Bringing your clipping plane further in should help the framerate issues. We are also looking into seeing if we can make any further performance tweaks, but the issue is primarily caused by there being so much more to see on Kunark.

    -Gordon
