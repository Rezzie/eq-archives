  • The Vision
    [Quote from original post: . yet in the last patch this is what was changed, lowered spawn rates and overall less mobs in the game, less exp per kill on same mobs, more exp loss on death, less loot.]

    While it is possible that some isolated individual spawns might have had their rates (spawn or loot) adjusted in one direction or another:

    - No move was made to lower spawn rates across the board leading to less mobs in the game.

    - No change was made to reduce the amount of experience earned per kill on those mobs. It's always been based on the level of the NPC and the zone you are in.

    - No change was made to make people lose more experience on death. The only change to this that has occurred since release was when it was halved.

    - No change was made to reduce the amount of loot entering the world on a global scale.

    Now in regards to your suggestions regarding level based equipment, it was decided some time ago that EQ would not take that route. The FAQ has a pretty good explanation regarding this suggestion.

    [Quote from original post: BTW this was not a bash or flame, I am just concerend, I've been playing EQ for erm what 3 years now, and this latest move SCARES me.]

    Well two and a half years at most if you were in the earliest beta phase, but I don't believe that the "latest move" that you spoke of above occurred.

  • Necro & Magician Damge Rates Against Green Frog
    [Quote from original post: What is wrong with this picture:
    he never ran out of mana...
    he resisted unresistable spells...
    he never got hurt even with 2 50+ pets wailing on him due to lifetap...]

    Nothing really. He's not an NPC that we want to be killed. As for the "unresistable" spells, there are only a couple of truly unresistable spells in game, and they all belong to NPCs.

  • Patch Problems
    [Quote from original post: 1) Still can't activate spells with the control key, like you could before the patch ( + )]

    The default key was changed to ALT. You can check it back to CTRL if you like by going into Options/Keyboard.


    2. I've never heard of that one. It sounds like a problem with your video card or driver, perhaps the way it's handling mipmapping. Have you tried turning off mipmapping to see what happens?

    3. The spell line eats mana gradually. When you ran out of mana it didn't wear off. It was a bug fix. Should it have been reported? Certainly, but it's still a bug fix.

  • Witch Hunts
    I'm unaware of any case where someone found a ban to be "probably mistaken" yet did not reverse it. That just wouldn't be right. If you have a URL where I could direct customer service, that would be appreciated.

  • Questions for the "New Abashi"
    Well if you'll take answers from the "Old Abashi", I'll be happy to take a shot:

    [Quote from original post: 1. "Oh yeah, one more thing. Why doesnt EQ put the maps of the citys in there guide when you buy the game??"]

    There are indeed maps of the playable cities in the manual released with the Kunark full-retail box, which I believe is the only thing on the shelves these days.

    [Quote from original post: 2. "Its been 2 years and they still havn't made the game compatible with most sound cards (promissed June, '98 that there would be a new sound engine VERY Shortly, September '00 still no engine)" ]

    First, the game never had a problem with "most" sound cards as suggested. Some people running under certain configurations (with the SBLive for instance) had problems, where as others had no problems of that nature though they used the same card. The vast majority of people (including myself on my home system which included an SBLive) never had any problems.

    As to the new sound engine, it's in. It went in the same time as the DX7 update.

    [Quote from original post: 3. On the new expansion coming out "Still havnt fixed some bugs from beta.. "]

    It wouldn't be proper to comment without a specific list of "bugs from beta" to know what you are getting at. Most issues that exist and have existed for that period of time are not bugs, but rather a waived design issues that will never be addressed due to various technology limitations (download size being chief among them). They are part of the game now, and we'll have to live with them.

    [Quote from original post: 4. "I love EQ so don't get me wrong. I play AC also and, I have to admit, am wondering why AC makes upgrades and increases land/monsters/spells/items/etc etc, for FREE and EQ makes you pay for them? Just wondering..." ]

    They have their business plan and we have ours. We've added tons of new content (quests, items, spells, even zones) for "free" as well.

  • The Point of AFK
    Sinneed's response was pretty concise.

    Basically, there's no "pause" button in a persistant world, at least not ours. The only way we would implement a "safe AFK" would be if there was absolutely no in-game benefit to using it. That means that we'd have to make you blind and deaf as well as stopping any type of regeneration and skill-use countdowns.

    At that rate, the question indeed becomes, why not just log off?

  • Magical Damage
    This keeps coming up as an example, though I've addressed it before.

    What does "magical damage" mean to you? To you does that mean that any spell that does damage available to a wizard of a certain level should do more damage than any other spell available to any other class of a particular level?

    If so, that's not the definition we use. Our definition is damage over a time period. A DOT that does more damage overall than ICE comet does not do it in the short period of time like Ice Comet. Of those two spells you mentioned, one wizard and one mage, the wizard spell is faster and more mana efficient.

    Wizard spells across the board have usually have either better casting times, better mana ratios, do more damage, or a combination of the three than those of any other class (harmtouch excluded) ***over a period of time***. That's how it works, and wizards fit that definition.

  • More Druid Forms Needed?
    "Require" is a pretty strong word. While I understand that you *want* more forms, I don't think that they are required. As such, it's certainly good content for the wish-list, but it's not something that I can say we will or will not do at some point in the future.

  • NPC Named "!"
    The exclaimation point is indicative of an NPC that has been "permakilled", which usually happens when a problem is found after the live servers come up that is severe enough to justify removing the NPC from the game until they can be patched.

    There are a few other problems which can cause them to pop up unintentionally, requiring a patch to fix them. Often, if it is a critical NPC, we'll patch the zone without taking the servers down to clear up the problem.

Brian Hook Leaves Verant
[27 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:38 AM PST
Brian Hook, one of the most recognizable people in the gaming industry, resigned from his post at Verant Interactive yesterday. The guys at Voodoo Extreme caught up with Brian today to get the full story behind his depature. Here's a snippet:

    VoodooExtreme: Why did you leave Verant?

    Brian Hook: First, let me state up front that this was a completely amicable departure. Verant was very good to me. Smed [John Smedley, Verant/SOE Executive VP] and I have a good rapport, and he’s a genuinely good guy that I like a lot.

    But to answer your question, I was just getting uncomfortable with the very corporate environment there. You have to understand my background I left 3Dfx when it had less than 50 employees; when I worked for Silicon Graphics I was just a contractor, so I never had to deal with “big company” issues; and at id there were about a dozen employees. So I come from a very small company mentality. When I went to Verant it felt absolutely huge I think there were about 70 or so employees, but I don’t quite remember but after the acquisition by Sony it got even huger. All of a sudden I was working for this gigantic company based out of Japan instead of a relatively small game developer, and that just wasn’t as enjoyable for me. This isn’t a slam on Sony, it’s just that I’m a lot more comfortable working with a small group of people without a lot of corporate “stuff” to deal with.
Thanks Steph!

Verant Vs. Players
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:27 PM PST
A lengthy post was made in response by Gordon to a thread entitled "Verant vs. Players" found on the Gameplay Board.

    This is the retail version of EQ, the classes and races should have been balanced before you released it.

    That's easy to say, and for the most part it was true. However we *have* been adding content to the game with every patch, including tons of new zones that over doubled the worlds size since release. Every time we add something, we run the risk of creating an imbalance that needs to be addressed, or else we have a broken game that people will not play. Make no mistake: People will not play a broken game.

    All the patches should only be adding content and bug fixes, not plugging up ingenious strategies which the players found and which you didn't think of.

    It goes without saying that 300K people can discover strategies that weren't found by the 40K or so that were in all phases of the beta, and the 2000 people that were in for all but the last month of beta. Additionally, many of these "ingenious strategies" that you speak of were created by us attempting to add more content by way of items, quests, abilites, etc., to the game.

    We players are not here to find crack's in the design of EQ for you.

    No, you are here to have fun. However that aside, you will still find cracks in the design of EQ for us throughout that process. I'm thankful we have a dev team working to address those cracks as they are discovered, or again we'd have a broken game.

    If you truely don't like how we players play this game, or the strategies we come up with, then keep them in mind for when your designing EQ2, but dont punish us here and now.

    Again you seem to be saying that it would be better to leave gross imbalances in game that cause people to leave rather than correct them. I have to respectfully disagree, and say with some authority that this will not be the case with any game we make. These games are designed to last in the marketplace for a number of years, and need to last that long to cover development costs, both past and present. If they do not draw enough subscribers or last long enough, the game fails and future development is hurt by it. We won't sit around and watch a game fail when we can do something about it. Now EQ was never in jeopardy of that because we've always addressed these problems as they came up, but if we left every problem, or tried to address them all in a "positive" manner by increasing the power of "A" through "Y" because Z is overpowered, it would not be long before everything in game was elavated to broken proportions.

    Maybe you should realize that this game you created has taken on a life of its own when boxed it and sold it.

    Actually it took on a life of its own over a year before release when the community was formed by people interested in the game. As with anything alive though, things are in a constant state of flux. Things change, and we have to change with them to be sure that everything stays in line.

    thats exactly my point. Don't do anything. So what if warriors found a way to solo blues up to 60. So what if every other warrior is doing it 3 weeks later. How does it hurt anyone?

    So what if all classes found a way to do it? Should we leave it then? Suddenly you have everyone in the game running around without a need to group. Classes become meaningless as one class can do pretty much anything that the next class can. Class A is no longer dependant on class B for good or services in order to advance. That alone would reduce the social impact of the game by making it unnecessary for people to meet each other. Suddenly we'd have 300K people playing a single person game on our servers, with nothing really to hold them together. Aside from the hardcore players, the game would be dead within 6 months.

    Always ADD never TAKE away because when you take away (nerf) you punish.

    And *never* make a policy absolute, with exception to the policy of *never* making a policy absolute. Once you completely block out a particular course of action you limit yourself for no good reason other than it being "policy".

    As mentioned above, the policy of buffing up A-Y every time Z is overpowered is not at all suited to this environment. We would be constantly increasing every classes abilities, or the power of their spells or items, because of "inequities" cited by one class or another.

    Can we agree that as long as we have a class-based system, where classes have complimentary strengths and weaknesses, that there will always be people who believe that the strengths are not strong enough and that the weaknesses are too severe?

    If Verant really does really not like this new strategy, then they should tip their hat to the player who discovered it, and remember to make that strategy impossible in some future EQ game.

    That's certainly the policy advocated by the ever-quoted "mudwimping" article, an article that I personally disagree with. As I mentioned above, absolutes are dangerous. Completely ruling out one reasonable course of action and exclusively increasing power or abilities of the game as a whole is a BAD thing to do. It's as bad as exclusively decreasing power or abilities. Neither is an appropriate course of action. That article gets a lot of mention because it suggests a course of action that is popular with socieity: more, more, more, never less.

    There's a quote that suits this situation quite well, so I'll paraphrase: What is right is not always popular, and what is popular is not always right. It basically speaks to the fact that people do not always make the choices that are best for them. I'm sure that we could adopt the suggestions in the mudwimping article and please a lot of people in the short run, but as soon as something came up that completely unbalanced the game, invalidating a class or two, and we decided to leave things as is or enter the "enhancement death spiral", there would be no peace.

    Let me string a series of analogies together. I'm sure they all have their flaws, but I think that the point is sound. Imagine if:

    - banks have a policy of *always* taking the customers word for it in a disputed account.
    - paying taxes was voluntary (some argue that it is, but still)
    - we released all of the "innocent" people from jail.
    - *everyone* has a college degree
    - *everyone* has at least a million dollars

    These are situations that are pleasing and popular to those who are to be affected, but what happens when they are applied to everyone across the boards? Would any of this be right? Yes, if *I* have a problem at the bank I'd like to know that they'd just take *my* word for it. Yes, *I'd* like paying taxes to be voluntary for *me*. Yes, if *I* was in jail, I know *I'd* be innocent of any crime that would put *me* there so *I'd* like to be released. Yes, *I'd* like to have a college degree. Yes, *I* would like a million bucks.

    But everyone?! NO. *I* can vouch for what is good for *me*, but I know that it's not good for global policy. No, I wouldn't like the idea of banks taking everyone's word for it, as I know that fraud would increase to the point of critical failure and that the entire economy would collapse. No, I wouldn't want paying taxes to be voluntary, because then very few would, government programs would fail, we'd have no standing army, and the government would collapse. No, I wouldn't like everyone who say's that they are innocent to be released from jail. That's why we have a court system. No, I wouldn't want *everyone* to have a college degree, because then a college degree would be meaningless. No, I wouldn't want everyone to have a million bucks, because inflation would run out of control, milk and bread would cost thousands of dollars per unit, and the money would all be in the hands of the top 1% within a month anyway, putting them that much further out of *my* reach.

    One final analogy that fits better than the rest. Imagine if:

    - we increased the power of a class/item/ability, etc., every time "enough" (whatever that means) people complained.

    So to answer your question, Verant should only add content, or leave things as they are.
    Koen

    Well, while I appreciate your ability to state your opinion in a manner that is appropriate for this forum, I must respectfully disagree with the points that you've made.

    -Gordon

New Password/Credit Card Scam
[21 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:10 PM PST
'Tis the season for password scams. Mr. MooKow was good enough to send us a copy of the latest scam to alert the community. This latest ploy is a little different than your average email scam. It asks you to visit a page linked to via html in the email, the email will even read that it's from a Sony email account. Don't be fooled though, following the links will take you to the freebie hosting site known as AngelFire and ask you to input your password and credit card information in web form.

I will post an exact copy of the email to let you know what to watch out for. I cannot stress enough that the following text is a scam and it's directions should not be followed:


******Warrning: Scam Email*****

Subj: EverQuest Technical Request (KMM153748C074L)
Date: 11/27/00 5:09:18 PM Eastern Standard Time
From: EQTech@station.sony.com

Hello,

This message is from The Station Customer Service department regarding
corrupt account information you have entered into our databases for your
everquest account login name. It seems while we were updating our server's
your login name was found with no home information, unless we recieve you

Screen Freezing
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:10 PM PST
Abashi stopped by the Gameplay Board today to make the following announcement:

    Some people have been reporting that their screen freezes every so often while in game, causing all sorts of mischief. While we are looking into that problem, and do have some changes planned for the next patch that should reduce its occurance, there are some things that you can do first to see if it improves:

    1. Virus scan software: These problems usually run in a systray (down by the clock). By default, they typically are set to scan files every time they are accessed. This could be causing some of those problems, as EverQuest accesses several local files at every character save. Try disabling it to see if your situation improves.

    2. IRQs. PCI devices are designed so that they can share IRQs. That works well in principle, but can cause problems when several high bus-bandwidth devices are on the same IRQ. Typically we've seen this when sound and video cards are on the same IRQ. You can try disabling your sound by placing a file called "NOSOUND.TXT" (file contents do not matter) in your EverQuest directory. If your freezing goes away, there is a good chance it's a shared IRQ problem. The method by which to reassign IRQs varies from system to system, but can be done by either moving the sound card to a different slot, manually assigning IRQs in the BIOS, or manually assigning them in the Device Manager. You probably shouldn't attempt any of these without the assistance of your local computer guru, unless of course you are the local guru.

    As mentioned above, we are putting in some changes in next week's patch that should help the situation for those that aren't affected by #1 or #2, but you'll want to make sure that the problems above are not the cause of your problems before counting on the patch to fix it.

    -Gordon

    Reports of GM misconduct are forwarded to the customer service management team. In cases where the true story cannot be determined with any certainty, a note is made for the GMs file. Misconduct, when it occurs, is hardly isolated to a single instance, and if a GM shows a pattern it can them be necessary that we go further.

    The same is true for players. It's very easy for someone to be falsely accused once, being falsely accused of the same thing in a half a dozen different instances is another thing entirely.

    Anyway, this board is not the proper place to voice complaints about GMs. It tends to reduce the credability of the complaint as it's being made in a public forum for the purpose of garnering public support. If you wish to report something to the CS team concerning GM misconduct, feel free to email gscotto@station.sony.com directly.

  • Contiually Making EQ Harder
    [Quote from original post: Are we too good? IE: why must the game be constantly made more difficult!]

    Well first, you are good. Players have consistantly discovered ways to do things that amaze us to this day. It's therefore essential that we keep raising the bar to provide challenge to those that have figured out how to overcome previous challenges. Obviously, we don't intend to make unbeatable encounters, though we could rarely do that when we tried, but we still need to provide a challenge.

    There's actually two issues here. One, making new content more difficult, which I think should be expected by everyone, as what would be the point if things did not get more difficult as you progressed.

    The other issue is making old content more difficult by reducing the effectivness of tactics that allow a challenge to be overcome without risk. This is a different issue entirely, as some people apparently don't expect this. Here's the short of our intentions: You play the game to have fun. You have fun by overcoming challenges. A challenge is something that has the prospect of failure. Failure must have consequences to be meaningful. Overcoming the challenge without failure yields a reward, whether tangible or ideological. Basically, discovering a way to do something without risk (or with less risk than intended) negates the challenge and creates a situation that we must address in order for the game to be fun.

    [Quote from original post: Absor your comments please on any of the above." ]

    Well, here are my thoughts on this:

    [Quote from original post: 1) It might be a false entry in the sp.dat file, however, Necro's get to become Spectres with a new SoV spell at level 60. Were we could not add more things to the global memory before, we have more room now. You were not lied to. I'm not going to comment on any Scars of Velious spells, real or imagined.

    [Quote from original post: 2) WHY would you add a "attack power" stat buff to any of our Self Illusions.     Here we can have an interesting discussion about what an 'illusion' is. If it were me making the spells, there would be no effect to an illusion beyond the visual effect. After all, an illusion is just a change of perceptions, not a manifestation of anything real. So, in that way, you're right, I don't think there should be a stat buff on any of the illusions at all.

    But, with the EverQuest definition of Illusion, the spell can incorporate the other powers of the Enchanter, as long as it follows the look and feel of the illusion. There must be some link between the power of the enchanter and the form of the illusion. Enchanters don't have the power to increase foot speed, so there is no way they could move as fast as a wolf with that spell. Besides, keep in mind that we don't want enchanters to cast Spirit of the Wolf. So a strength buff makes some sense, considering the form involved.

    Should there be post 44 illusions? Maybe. I can't see why not. And I'll keep it in mind to ask someone about it.

  • Barbarian Bards?
    There are a lot of class/race combinations that would make sense in EverQuest. But at some point we had to draw a line, so that there would be some race and class diversity.

    Sure, Barbarian bards make sense. So do Dwarven bards (Dwarves are an old race, very interested in their own lore), or Gnomish shadowknights… this could go on until all races had every class. There just has to be a line somewhere…
