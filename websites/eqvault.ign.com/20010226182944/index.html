Druzzil Ro Alliance
- Aurum @ 9:48 AM PST
Banegrivm announced the following:

Greetings,

I wanted to take this time out to make everyone aware of the Druzzil
Ro Alliance. What is the Druzzil Ro Alliance? The Druzzil Ro Alliance is a
collection of Guilds on the Druzzil Ro Server that have formed together for
the purpose of doing high level dungeons, planes, and dragons, without having
the necessity of being in a high level guild. With such an alliance all the
participating guilds remain in their present guild and are never forced to
lose their guild tag. This way they are not forced into giving up their home
or their friendships with those they have grown to know and love, and still
get to play the game...on their own terms.

Their is now an option for those that want to be involved in the high
level end of the game. No longer will people be forced to be in a guild with
people they do not care to associate with on a daily basis. Now people will
have the option of remaining within the confines of their guild and doing
said raids and staying in their home without ever losing their guild tag.
When raids are conducted no guild will ever demand any guild member from the
alliance ever to disband from their guild for the purpose of the raid. All
those in the raid remain tagged into their guild for the conductivity of the
raid. No one ever loses their guild tag under any conditions where it
corresponds to the Druzzil Ro Alliance and its operations with regards to
raiding.

The Druzzil Ro Alliance has also come up with some base policies such
as looting and simply working together as a team. The later will force guild
leaders to be guild leaders and help to keep relations friendly between
guilds. Not everyone will see eye to eye one hundred percent of the time, but
that does not mean we can not be civil and respectable and try our best to
bring about an honest and honorable resolve to whatever the situation may be.
In short the Druzzil Ro Alliance is in it for fun. We pay monthly fees a
month to have fun, so fun we shall have, and what better way to do it then
with our friends.

If you are a respective guild leader and interested in joining the
Druzzil Ro Alliance please visit the forums at www.druzzilroalliance.cjb.net
and read the looting rules under the General Discussion forums. If you agree
with these rules then please leave a post in the General Discussion forums
requesting to join and you shall be prompty added to the Druzzil Ro Alliance.
Please make sure you leave your website URL if applicable in the post so we
may add it as a link. This way anyone interested in being in a guild apart of
the alliance will have an easier time in chosing a guild in which they can
call their home.

If you have a greivance with an alliance member and wish to enter your
guild into the alliance please leave said greivance at the door upon
entering. No one will hold anything upon any other alliance members. The past
is simply the past and this is a new beginning for us all. As well, no one
will be refused admittance into the alliance as well...so long as they agree
to the loot rules. We shall endeavor in all things to enjoy our company, and
our fellowship, and the game we have come to know as EverQuest. Here it is an
even playing field, and for us involved it shall be our fun and our enjoyment
of the game. To those of you who may be joining you are welcomed with open
arms. May the gods and godesses bless you in the days ahead, and may the
hunting begin.


Sincerely Yours,
Banegrivm Shadowblight
Sovereign Lord of the 1st Fist of Light
Member of the Druzzil Ro Alliance - http://www.druzzilroalliance.cjb.net
Banegrivm@aol.com
Thanks.

Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:15 PM PST
Straight off of the Gameplay Board, here is today's edition of the Absor Infobits:

  • Tracking Problem: Bug or Working as Intended?
    Dun wrote:

    [Quote from original post: This post is a misrepresentation of the facts. The Druid cap was raised on TEST SERVER. No changes to Druid tracking have been made to live servers. Please stop posting this kind of inaccurate nonsense.]

    True, this is only on Test right now. And you folks all need to be aware that stuff on Test will not always go live.

    But I can explain why Druid tracking might be getting an increase. It's really the same reason that it was always lower than a Bard's skill before. Before all the cool changes to tracking it was actually better to have a lower skill number than a higher one, unless you had a really high skill. Basically, because there was no scrolling, Bards with their slightly higher skill were actually worse at tracking than druids because the higher skill would create a lot of clutter. This was done at the request of the Druid community.

    So the plan is to raise the Druid cap so that they are still better at tracking than Bards. That's the plan anyway.

  • Whiniest Class in Norrath Poll
    Sorry folks, but I'm closing this one. I can't even imagine how this could be a productive discussion...

  • Clerics Post-Patch
    Rolluk wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I haven't played my cleric since the patch. I have no desire to. My idea of fun is not "One heal and die". As a matter of fact, after being disgusted with the changes in the patch message, I've logged in for a total of about 25 minutes.]

    And therein lies the problem.

    Far too many people are not trying the new changes out. I hate to do this, but let's look at the Evade. Some Rogues were convinced that the changes we made to Evade were going to make them worthless, long before they had a chance to play with it. 'One backstab and die'. It turns out that they are, if not excited about the change, accepting of it. But only now that they've actually played with it.

    Please, play the game. See what we've done. We want feedback from people that have tried it out. You do yourself and your fellow players a disservice with posts like this.

    Thanks to those of you that have played with the changes. As you continue to play with them, and as your tactics evolve (and we know that you'll find good ones), let's talk about the effects of this change.

  • NPCs and Inventory
    Lieah wrote:

    [Quote from original post: No where does any NPC state that a general inventory lot was to be opened. Are they encouraging us to read the spoilers then? How psychic did I have to be to know this? I find it appauling that they would be so inconsiderate to a customer like that. Its just a game, let me have my fun and give my item back, this is pathetic.]

    I'm sorry that you lost your item. But in the case where the NPCs is going give you a container you must have an open inventory slot for that container. This is something that most people understand by the time they've played long enough to be trying to complete an epic quest. We've posted about this, and I'm pretty sure that this was also placed on a patch message.

    I wanted to take this opportunity to stress this again. If you are completing a quest, any quest, make certain that you have an inventory slot open to receive any items that are given to you by the NPC. It is recommended that you keep a few open, just in case.

Alan on CH Hate Changes
- Aurum @ 6:55 PM PST
Here's the last response Alan posted so far today on the Gameplay Board:

    Lets say I have a level 25 Warrior fully buffed. They pull 4 mobs and make sure they hit them all. Then the cleric casts CH and the mobs all aggro onto the cleric. If someone puts a dam shield on the cleric the mobs will all die and the warrior will get credit.
    It also means that although we can't kill the top mobs anymore, we have now given Clerics the BEST area taunt in the game. If the mobs can't summon, the cleric can do a CH and as long as the cleric is faster than the MOB [snares?] stay out of range. The other party memebers attack the mobs. When the aggro off the cleric the cleric does CH again.

    I haven't had time to think this out yet, but I bet Verant didn't even give it a thought. We have now helped the power levelers in a big way.

    Actually, the agro rules are based on the level of the target, not the level of the caster, and the cap for a pre-51 target is much lower than a 51-post target.

    Alan
