    Roleplaying or just playing?
    nowimnothing22 wrote:

    [Quote: Well, as a player who actually tries to take the game as more then a endless struggle to level, roleplay is quite possible and can be worked into the character without sacrificing the ability to advance said character. For example, I play a level 30 ranger on the Druzzil Ro server, who worships Tunare...So obviousally, I never got into groups to hunt Treants, but I didn't take it upon myself to hang out near them and harass players that decided to hunt them. I think this is a example of how to intergrate roleplay into a system that dosn't require it.

    It's all about attitude. For instance, my ranger is going to raise a few eyebrows at the dark elf shadowknight that comes up to him and quotes:

    "Sow, plz!"

    This is where my chracter might /glare and tell the dark elf that he has no clue what he wants, and to please take his presense elsewhere...Ironically, one of my characters closest friends and groupmates is a Dark Elf Shadowknight, and my ranger has no problem fighting for this Shadowknight, and doing most anything for him. OOCly, it's becuase the player behind said shadowknight is a friend. In Character, my ranger trusts this character, and beleives him to be exception to the "Evil" shadowknight...

    I think the trick to roleplaying on a non PVP server is to realize that it's not always possible. I myself made a few rules for it that made everything really simple.

    /say and /emote and /shout are in character things. My characters will not /say "I'm a level xxx of this class" or "Give me sow and i'll start pulling yellows and we'll rake in some nice exp"

    /group, /guild, /tell, /ooc, /auction -- there are all out of chracter things, I don't really roleplay in these forms unless someone else is and it's the logical reason to roleplay back, for instance, if a friend sends me a tell that is obviosally in chararacter, i'm going to respond in the same way.

    It's all about balence, and knowing when roleplay is called for, and when you can't do it. Basically, try as hard as you can to make your chraracter have a personality, and roleplay should come quite naturally. ]

    You've pretty much nailed it. When playing tabletop roleplaying games I'm usually very careful to keep my in character comments in character. But that doesn't mean that I don't throw things at people or comment on someone's particularly bad B.O. out of character. Though when sitting around the table we can more easily rely on visual clues to in and out of character conversations.

    In the end, roleplaying isn't a chore. And a character with a personality is very easy to play.


    [Quote: Hi Alan,

    I've been reading the above-titled discussion with great interest. I've always had an affinity for roleplaying, and despite the fact that I don't roleplay much myself, I'm very receptive to those who do, and quickly jump into it when confronted with somebody who is roleplaying. I've also noticed that it appears to be the roleplayers who are most likely to be "chosen" for active participation in the "big events" that are part of the historical quests. (One example: Banegrivm on Druzzil Ro was "chosen" to lead the Light armies in battle against Lanys T'Vyl and the Tier'Dal in Bloody Kithicor.)

    This ties in very closely with another topic earlier regarding "What is the Story of EverQuest" or something along those lines, where people were proposing such things as the Qeynos Guard burning down Fish's Ale and such.

    I think you missed the point of the person who took issue with your statement, "Pacifists don't make good adventurers."

    What he was trying to say, I think, and I don't know if I can be any more clear about it, is that "adventuring" does not mean killing or helping people kill. There are many, many things that can happen in a roleplaying game that can be considered "adventuring" that don't involve killing at all. Riddles, goal-oriented quests, information gathering, etcetera.

    I definitely agree that a pacifist doesn't make a good adventurer *if* you define "adventuring" as "go kill this beast and bring me its head" and so on. However, I think that is a very narrow definition of "adventuring".

    An example: there is a small quest for Wizards in Qeynos, in which the quester must bring a note to a wizard NPC (I've forgotten his name) who tends to loiter around the guard tower in Qeynos Hills. The reward for this quest is a spell and some experience. Now this might not seem like much of a quest, and it's not, but making that trip from Qeynos to that tower at first or second level does have some element of risk to it, yet does not require the slaying of any NPC. In my book, this is still "adventuring".

    So I think a more appropriate statement would be that "pacifists don't make good killers". And, well, that's a no-brainer.

    Cheers
    Darkas Dusk
    Tier'Dal Warrior

    (agnostic, and very difficult to roleplay!) ]

    That's reasonable enough. Pacifists make poor killers. :)

    And, in essence, you are correct. We have defined 'adventurer' in the more aggressive sense for the most part. But you can most certainly adventure in EverQuest as a pacifist, as long as you are adventuring as such. So you can see the world, a lot of it anyway. You can talk to the people. You can do several quests. All that is adventuring.

    But EverQuest was designed around the concept of an adventurer in the sense of Conan, or even Robin Hood. A design choice that I think was a good one. And, yes, in that world an 'adventurer' that is a pacifist won't be as good of an adventurer as the brutal warrior.

    Questing for XP, Not Loot
    [Quote:
    --------------------------------
    quote:

    Regardless, you can travel and see the world without having a quest. I know a lot of folks that simply make up their own reasons to visit >other places (such as their High Elf wanting to see the library at Erudin). Developing your >character is not entirely our responsibility, most of that falls onto the player.
    --------------------------------

    My first comment may be more suited to the role playing thread, but it's in response to something here, so that's that. Once, when my ranger was but a young, level 6 or 7 lad, he went mad. He babbled about "steam" and how steam would change all of Norrath, and vowed to go visit the gnomes to learn about steam. So, at a young level, I found a good role play reason to journey all the way to Ak'Anon for the first time, RPing the whole merry way. (This was before the steam ship to Velious, too)

    That leads me to a possible questing idea for you to toss to the quest makers. Some have complained that more quests must be made that offer greater experience rewards, or that involve your character more. How about "curse quests," or whatever you'd like to all them?

    Simply put, they would work by putting a burden and/or penalty on a character for the time the quest is active, only being removed until the quest is finished. Because of the downside for players - they could be stuck with the penalty for a while if they don't get right to questing - the exp rewards for these quests could be bumped up while still remaining fair. Of course, some way to ensure players know they are about to undertake a quest of this type must be in place. Easy enough. Make the bracketed text, or the quest text, in red (or some other BOLD color). Here are some simple ideas that can be easily modified to fit a variety of quests:

    Cindy Perlam of Qeynos has a problem. Her infant son is terribly sick and needs medical attention, and only the kind a high elf doctor can give. She wants you to take her son to Felwithe for treatment. The problem is, the lad takes up a full inventory slot and leaves a character with a permanent encumbered and/or snared effect until the trip to Felwithe and back is done. If there is a way to have the infant NO RENT to ports (a little boy can't survive a port, can he?) all the better.

    Sir Lenderslat in Freeport has had some nasty rumors about him passed around, and because you were set up at the local tavern (maybe prompted by another quest), he thinks you did it! You have to clear your name, and his, by shuffling a series of notes and keywords around Freeport. The problem is, you have gone completely KOS to every guard in the city. You must clear your own name by restoring Sir Lenderslat's good name, at which time the 10,000 point faction hit you took will be counteracted by another 10,000 point hit in the opposite direction.

    Lugga the Ogre just doesn't like being fat anymore ... so if she has to be fat, you all have to be! When you agree to help her get the diet food she needs to slim down, she fattens you up first to punish you in case you failed. Your AGI and CHA plummet, and worse yet, you begin eating at three times the normal rate. The effects remain until you can get Lugga the low fat grub she needs, which must be gathered from several locations and cooked up for her by a master baker.

    What about a quest that ruins a character's ability to speak common, leaving only, say, halfing as a means to communicate? Or one that has every banker in Norrath thinking you're a bank robber and refusing to serve you but one, the one in Runnyeye, who is also the only one who can clear your name? Or a troll who thinks you're so ugly, he casts a semi-permanent invis on you until you can finish his quest?

    And so on. Will players take on quests that stick them with penalties if it means better role playing or more exp in the end? I don't know. But if possible to incorporate quests like these, I think they would make questing more interesting, dynamic and fun. Just an idea,

    Talmer [Winter Raven]
    Ranger, Bristlebane ]

    I love some of your ideas. I'm not sure how many people would be entertained by the idea of having an Ogre stuff muffins down you until she's comfortable in your presence… but I would!

    Thanks for the amusing ideas. I'll make sure the team sees them.

    *** Patch Message ***


    ** Zone Changes **

    - Runnyeye has been changed. Something is up in Goblin land...


    ** Spell and Song Changes and Bug Fixes **

    - We've been fixing holes in the spell stacking code. For the most
    part the changes fix bugs that allowed tricks to get spells to
    stack that were never intended to stack.

    - Increased duration on Bard Charm songs to 18 seconds from 12 seconds.

    - Fixed the Spell Scroll for Imbue Amber so that Erudite Clerics of
    Cazic Thule can scribe it.

    - The casting messages for Necromancer pet summoning spells have been
    changed to be more appropriate.



    ** Features and Interface Changes **

    - The variable %t will now work with /assist. So you can use /assist %t
    if you wish. Though it should have exactly the same effect as /assist
    without the %t.

    - %r returns "corpse" for corpses and "NPC" for targeted NPCs.

    - Sense traps failure message text color has been changed to
    user color 15 (same as other skill messages). The success message
    was already set to user color 15.

    - Ignore Lists now work the same way that the Friends List does.
    It has been expanded to 30 names per character per server. The new
    ignore list is stored in the same ini file as the friends list
    (_<#>.ini). The first time a character is created or
    loaded after the patch their new ignore list will be a copy of the old
    ignore list, but will from then on be unique to the character.

    - The Alt key now works in several new situations. It will now show
    item names when in the trade window. Because the trade items of the
    local player and the trading partner are so close together, the item
    names of the trading partner will always show up unless the player
    positions their mouse pointer over the local player part of the trade
    window, which will cause the game to only show the local player's trade
    item names.

    - The Alt key will show item names for items in your bank.

    - The Alt key will also pop up names for merchant items and corpse
    items when you hold it down in merchant mode or loot mode.

    - The Alt key will even pop up names for items in open containers when
    held down and the mouse cursor is over the open container window (they
    don't show up otherwise).

    - The client will now automatically reject a /duel request from any
    player that is on your /ignore list. Some players were using this
    to annoy players that had ignored them.

    - You also will no longer see /consent messages from people that are
    on your /ignore list (this was also something people were doing to
    annoy people that had ignored them).

    - /who GUILD (and the GUILD must be in caps) will now show you the
    members of your guild in your zone. You can also specify a guild name,
    such as /who "Irontoe Brigade".

    - /safelock command added. /safelock will lock the EverQuest interface
    (mostly just mouse clicks, keyboard commands/movement, and most slash
    commands) with the password that you supply to the command. You must use
    this command a second time, supplying the same password, to unlock the
    interface. Passwords are NOT case-sensitive. So to lock your interface,
    you would type /safelock password. You would do the same to unlock it.
    REMEMBER: If you forget what password you typed, you will need to
    reboot your machine.


    ** Item Changes **

    - All the shields in the game that have the Ribcage graphic should now
    also have the ribcage icon when they are in your inventory.

    - Tinkered Watchman helm should show up now.

    - Ring of Pureblood is now equipable only on your fingers instead of
    your waist.

    - Grand Master's Leg Wraps and Fists of the Grand Master are now No-Drop.
    They were intended to be No-Drop when they were created.

    - Peacekeeper Staff and the Bug Collection box are no longer equipable.
    There are problems that can arise when containers can be equipped, so
    we had to disable that for these items.

    - Sewing Kits: No existing sewing kits are being changed with this
    patch. But from now on, kits sold on vendors will be somewhat different.
    Large Sewing Kits will have 8 slots. Deluxe Sewing Kits will have 10
    slots and weight slightly more than the Large Sewing Kit.

    - Changed the color of the Kael SK quest armor from a very pretty
    blue-green to a more appropriate reddish-gray.


    ** Other Stuff **

    - Changed the spelling of Troubador to Troubadour for the 55th level
    bard title. :)

    - King Tormax's spawn rate has been adjusted to be more in line with
    that of his counterparts in Velious. This is not the sort of thing
    that we will usually announce, as spawn times and things of that
    nature are part of the dynamic content of EverQuest. We're
    announcing this, however, to help avoid some confusion.


    - The EverQuest Team

Editorial Analysis of Virtual Economies
- Aurum @ 7:48 PM PDT
Mandos has written a new editorial that provides an Analysis Virtual Economies. It's a very interesting read on a subject that affects everyone who plays the game. Here's a clip:

    To understand the problem of deflation and inflation in VE’s EQ is a good place to start. Inflation, as Milton Friedman pointed out in a Nobel Prize winning body of work, is when too many dollars chase too few goods. Deflation is the opposite; too few dollars in pursuit of too many goods. In the 1970’s in America for example, as the money supply (the amount of money in circulation) grew more quickly then the amount of goods and services available in the economy we had runaway inflation; a can of tuna fish might have the price tag raised on it three times before it left the shelf.

    Forever Questing For Exp
    [Quote: Alan, I see where you are going with your thoughts, but I really dont think you truely understand where we as players are coming from. Your comments of Gnoll teeth or Lizzard Tails in Ferrott, yes it is a nice little add on for stuff that you are going to kill anyway, but that is not the type of quest that we are talking about. Faiery boots from Gfay, Sandshark meat from Erudin, a Lizzrd doll from Ferrott, A rare leaf from the Overthere, A mammoth whisker from Everfrost. All No Drop, Lore. Give us lower levels, lower level epic quests, not necessarily for items, but for exp. Just make it worth our while to be "(For)Ever Questing".

    Munpoppy
    22 Ogre ShadowKnight
    Bertox Server ]

    Well, I've received about an equal number of responses from people saying that they hate quests that make them travel as those that say that travel is one of their favorite parts of quests.

    Regardless, you can travel and see the world without having a quest. I know a lot of folks that simply make up their own reasons to visit other places (such as their High Elf wanting to see the library at Erudin). Developing your character is not entirely our responsibility, most of that falls onto the player.

    But I do agree that there will always be more things that we can do to help out in that department.

    -Fix- wrote:

    Greetings !

    [Quote: Just an idea here, but maybe some NPC's (like hometown NPC's) could only give quests to those who have a very good (or very bad) faction with particular mobs.

    For example, let's imagine a sailor in a freeport bar, talking about his trips around Norrath. During his adventures, a dragon attacked his ship, and slew all his comrades.

    Maybe this sailor could only give his revenge quest to those who have a BAD faction with dragons, and not just a good faction with him.

    This way a sergeant in Kelethin could give a quest to a foreigner who slew many orcs in Everfrost, because he would then have a reputation of orc slayer.

    Very interesting idea. I'm not sure how practical it is, but it's certainly interesting. Thanks.

    More Server Populations and Economies
    [Quote: I have been watching this thread for some time and I like many of the suggestions that were made. It has gotten me to thinking of some possibilities that may be feasible. The idea of item decay and maintenance is a good one, if only implemented correctly. Now I realize that these are just suggestions and may not make it into the current game but, maybe they will see light in some future incarnation of a MMORPG.

    The idea is that items have a certain quality or power value. As a characters xp and level increase then the character's equipped items values decrease in an equal amount. This can be justified as use of the items. The items don't decrease just because you have them but rather as the result of quantifiable use. The value can be viewed by right clicking the object and looking at it's bar. As the bar decreases so does it's usefulness. Damage ratios go down, beneficial effects go down, AC goes down, etc.

    Now in order to return the item back to it's original usefulness you must find a PC tradesman of appropriate skill for the item. The person with the item and the tradesman must be grouped and at the towns forge, spinning wheel, etc. and both must enter items for the repair of the item in a trade like window. For example:

    Lany the bard's lambent breastplate is down to half power it's AC 10 of 20. He takes it to Sampson the blacksmith. Sampson and Lany group at the forge and Sampson adds a specially crafted hammer (this hammer would have taken a great deal of plat, ore, molds and such to make), metal bits and water. Lany adds a lambent stone, a gem and 100pp. They click the forge button and Lany gets back the breastplate plus the power goes up based on the value of the items in the forge. Sampson gets back his hammer and the rest is consumed. They both also get some xp.

    You could adjust what is needed based on the item and how much power needs to go up. Some things will be always required such as the hammer, lambent stone and gem. The plat could be adjusted for how much increase is desired. Like a heal, 100pp - minor healing +20 power, 200pp - greater healing +50 power. This way players can decide when they want to repair their items and at what cost. If an item is left to go to zero power there could be a chance it breaks.

    Better items would have higher power and therefore longer lifespans and inferior items shorter lifespans but cheaper to repair. The reason that I suggested xp gain for repair is so the tradesman could reasonable pursue that as a profession. This also has the advantage of limiting abuse as it consequently lowers the power rating of equipped items thus necessitating additional repairs.

    Here is a quick rundown of the advantages and disadvantages:

    ADVANTAGES
    1. Creates a viable alternative to hack and slash
    2. Helps make trade skills more useful
    3. Creates a money sink
    4. Creates an item sink

    DISADVANTAGES
    1. Coding all items with power ratings
    2. Creating new interface for repair (trade window mechanics are already in game however)
    3. Tracking power ratings when items are traded (items with charges are already in game)
    4. It's something new, players may not like it (item decay and repair is in many popular games)

    These are just my suggestions hopefully your programmers and glean something viable out of them.

    Faejon Dorunrun
    Captain / Webmaster
    Battle Cry of Terris-Thule ]

    That's a nice way to do it. But it is still item decay, and there is one key item you left off of your evaluation, a disadvantage. Tedium. Nobody is really going to want to go to battle with each of their armor pieces down a few AC points. People will want to maintain them at full value, and doing so will be tedious. That is the nature of item decay, and that's why I'm not a big fan of it.
