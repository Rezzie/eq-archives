Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:55 PM PDT
As usual, Gordon was once again on the Gameplay Board answering people's questions and responding to comments. Here's the latest posts:

  • Rare Spawn Complaints
    Most of the complaints I see in regards to these rare spawns for quest components is related to the general feeling that there is an "entitlement" to these items. There isn't. No one is entitled to any particular item.

    You can't fix a situation where everyone is camping by making what they are camping for more plentiful. That just encourages camping, as the camp duration gets shorter, and more people feeel that the camp time is worth the effort. As it is now, maybe people will get the idea that the camp is too long, and just choose not to camp. Then perhaps someone happening by can get lucky and find the spawn in question. If people are still choosing to camp it, perhaps we need to look into making it even rarer, closing the quest, or look into creating other items that represent reasonable alternatives to the item that people are choosing to camp.

  • EverQuest & FPS Games
    I completely agree that Doom and other First-person shooter type games are very fun, despite the lack of a penalty for death. However, those games are not about building and molding a character to your desires. The worlds aren't persistent. There's is nothing that can be achieved by playing for a year that can't be achieved in the first few minutes of each life.

    The games are completely different and cannot be measured against each other. The concept of "Challenge" is implemented in different ways. EverQuest implements challenge by providing risks. In order to provide risks, there must be some downside that will be realised should a task not be completed. That downside can't be enjoyable, or it wouldn't be a risk.

    I do wish that I could explain this to everyone's satisfaction. If anyone can offer any suggestions to this end, I'd certainly appreciate it.

  • Gaining Experience
    Experience given by a creature is based upon its level, and the zone you are in. It is not based upon its level in relation to you, other than at the point that you stop receiving experience from killing a particular level creature.

    In other words, if you are level 50, a level 49 creature will give more experience than a level 48 creature, even though they are both blue.

  • Online Store Status
    The status is that the online store in it's former incarnation will probably not be up in the foreseeable future. We are however working on a web-based store that we expect to open in a few weeks.

  • Spell Drops
    [Quote from original post: I have yet to see a difference in the spell drop rate. Hours of hunting skyfire, chardok, etc. still yield nothing]

    Most people who cite problems with spell drops cite hours of hunting in Skyfire and Chardok. Consider for a moment that though spells drop there, those might not be the best places to find spells.

  • The Bammbo Bo Stick
    [Quote from original post: We were told by you Abashi that the Bammbo Bo stick was a mistake and was being lowered in weight.]

    Actually I never said that. I said that some of the monk-only weapons in Kunark were too heavy, and I never meantioned what specific weapons. Anyway, the live team went through the weapons and lowered the weight on some of them. Some of them are remaining rather heavy, and are that way on purpose.

    I'll ask the live team to look it over again, but I wouldn't expect a ton of dramatic changes. The fact that a particular character has the ability to use a particular weapon doesn't necessarily mean that they *should* use it, nor that it's the *best* thing that they could be using.

    Wait until you see some of the cursed items that will begin turning up soon.

  • Account Hacking
    [Quote from original post: Gordon this must be fixed(account hackers) Now this is an ongoing problem and need to be addressed instantly!]

    I would love to be able to find out how this stuff happens. In most cases, we can usually track it back to passwords being given by the account owners to guild members or real-life "friends". In most other cases, it turns out to be some auction or account sales related fraud.

    Players have to be responsible for their own account information and never provide it to anyone. They also need to refrain from buying characters online as the original owner can get the account back at nearly any time. We'll of course ban the account in the case of the latter, but we don't at all get involved with any lost funds.

    [Quote from original post: Set up direct link command just like bug or feedback and have it go directly to all gms guides and customer support peeps online so that the peeps hacking are caught in the act!]

    Very few people could get caught hacking in the act as most people don't have multiple accounts to log in and see it for themselves. That said, some type of more direct system-wide petition would probably end up being used for things other than hacking.

    All of that said, I would like to hear from folks who've had their accounts hacked, and honestly never gave the password to anyone and never bought or sold the account. I do have some questions that I'd like to ask. Please email me at gwrinn@verant.com.

  • Why can't Paladins use piercing weapons?
    It sounds like it might be possible that some of our apprentice game designers might have been unaware that paladins couldn't use piercers. We probably won't give them that ability, but we should address the fact that they cannot use these weapons.

    We're doing a weapons search to see what weapons out there are actually usable by Paladins so that we can correct any discrepancies.

  • I can attack 72 times in one second, what do you think of that?
    The test department took a look at this just to see what would happen. We were concerned about the possibility of a potential loop.

    No one takes any damage when this happens, but it doesn't loop.

  • Robe of Living Fungus - Nerf or Bug?
    I've asked around here, spoken to everyone with the ability to make or modify items, and can't find any indication that the robe ever had that effect. The closest thing I can guess is that we might have had a bad item export at some time that went live.

  • Rogues Picking off Spells
    [Quote from original post: Rogues pickpocketing spells off of Kunark mobs Please fix this so it's not possible to pickpocket spells]

    As infrequently as pickpocket will get an item, and as infrequently as the creatures drop spells, and considering that there's no way to tell when killing it that it has a spell, I really don't see a need to eliminate this.

  • Spell Droppings
    [Quote from original post: Please try that again where you find a place you know spells SHOULD be dropping and kill 250mobs to see how many scrolls you got just like you did before..... Please! I beg you....]

    I did last night, and saw a good number more drops. I probably got some good rolls, but still received several more spells even after accounting for that.

  • Enstill Won't Override Root?
    This is the same issue that causes lower level attack speed debuffs to overwrite the higher level ones. It's a problem with the spell stacking code and on our list.

  • After a 7 Month Vacation..
    Wow, I'm really glad that I went looking on page 5 and came across this post. It's nice to see someone spend this much time on a positive post. We really appreciate the opinions. It's a nice outlook.

    [Quote from original post: What are your thoughts on no_drop? Am I missing an important reason why no_drop should still be in game for all but the most uber/critical items?]

    NODROP was originally created with the intent of requiring that someone be at a specific event in order to reap the benefits of it. Quest items are a good example of what NODROP was intended for. We also expanded upon this initial design as an anti-farming measure.

    Of course, with all of the new lands, items, areas, creatures, etc., that Kunark brought to the table, and with the introduction of contextual random loot, the need for anti-farming measures is decreasing. Most of the items in Kunark are not NODROP unless part of some quest.

    We probably won't, however, go about removing the NODROP tags from existing items.

Gordon Infobits, Part 2
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:32 PM PDT
As promised, here is the second part of today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Explain ATK and AC x/1000 Caps
    Numbers over 1000 are indeed calculated.

    Back in beta, for a time, AC and ATK were displayed with two numbers each, showing the different components of the factor. For instance, AC was displayed with both a damage avoidance and a damage mitigation factor. This was highly confusing to a majority of the players, so both numbers were combined for the purpose of display.

    Basically, the two numbers were added, then divided by a theoretical max that one could attain at the time, yielding a percentage. It's this percentage that is displayed.

    Now, when we brought new items into the game that raised the true theoretical max (as opposed to the calculated theoretical max), and espicially when we brought out Kunark, we could have raised the calculated theoretical max so that the percentage would always be below 1000, but that would have resulted in most people seeing their AC drop, making them think that we had somehow nerfed it. So we left the calculated max the same, yeilding numbers greater than 1000.

    For actual combat calculations, the values you see are still reduced into their specific componenets which aren't capped with exception of lower level twinking-impact caps that we've referenced before.

  • Speed Advantage
    Well with any internet-based game, a better connection never hurts. However EQ is designed to mask some of the typical problems experienced by slower connections. For instance, some games won't allow you to move unless packets are flowing. EQ does, and sync's up with the server regularly to see that you aren't doing something illegal.

    The biggest difference experienced is usually the time it takes to zone.

  • Server Population Cap
    As I've mentioned before, capping server population is not something that we consider to be a valid option, and certainly wouldn't help retain players. People would get upset and leave if they suddenly found themselves unable to play that character that they have months of playtime invested in. It would be an all-too-regular occurence if server population were capped.

    Also, people new to the game often have friends that already play. We'd be shooting ourselves in the foot if we just told all of these new players that they couldn't play with their friends.

    As for your assertions on what would be fixed:

    [Quote from original post: Camping would be lessened]

    No, it wouldn't. People still camp on the test server which only has about 500 people on it at peak. They camp because its an easier and preferred method to get experience and loot. What there *would* be is less people waiting to join a particular camp. We're always working on this issue by trying to get people to spread out, moving loot around, randomizing drops, etc.

    [Quote from original post: Class envy would be less because competition would be less.]

    Class-envy will exist as long as some class can do something that another class can't. It's not related to competition for most.

    [Quote from original post: Finding groups would be easier]

    Actually we've found that smaller populations have more problems finding groups than larger ones. With more people on the server, there are more groups, and thus more groups that aren't full, leading to more groups that need people.

    Servers with smaller populations (PvP servers, Test) often have people who cite problems finding groups, as well as problems getting services such as binding or resurrections during off-peak times.

    [Quote from original post: Economy would not be as screwed up]

    The economy being "screwed up" is subjective. Trading among people happens very frequently on the servers. Signs of a screwed up economy in a game of this nature are indicated by a lack of demand, due to an excess of supply, or vice versa. Either the items are too valuable to trade, or they are too worthless to be traded.

    Since people are constantly trading items, the economy isn't "screwed up".

    [Quote from original post: People would be able to play and enjoy themselves and when the can actually play, they will tend to dwell less on the things they *cant* do, and the things they *cant* get.]

    That's based upon the personal psyche and choice of each player. Many "play and enjoy themselves", others obsess on items, levels, and what they can or cannot do. It's different for each individual.

  • Nerfing Items
    Casting times were applied to the cloak and the guise due to the "bounce" of levitation. Since the low-Z of the bounce would be reset to the Current-Z on each cast of the cloak, or on each cast of the Guize while levitating, it allowed people to get to places in the game that weren't designed to have PC's flying about (tops of buildings, trees, some ledges, etc.)

    The staff was done to the spam on the target.

    Jboots, however, don't have this problem since it is self-only.

  • Alchemy Prices

    We bring up new servers when the population on existing servers gets to the point that a new server is needed. All three of the PvP servers are currently underutilized, leaving no reason to bring up another at this time.

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[24 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:35 AM PDT
Here's the morning edition of the Gordon Infobits for your reading pleasure:

  • EverQuest Issues
    I had a few comments on some of these:

    [Quote from original post: 1) GM's will almost always take the side of the higher lvl player during a dispute. The high lvl players are usually friends with the GM group. They have been around the longest and have probably have even grouped with GMs.]

    False. Someone can be "high level" in a matter of calendar days. The GMs don't run around seeking out high level people to be their friends. The only people that the GMs really get to know are the ones who petition more than others. 90% of GM and Guide time is spent servicing 10% of the people on the server.

    [Quote from original post: 2) Abashi will only fight the fights that he can win. He will not respond to "real" problem issues.]

    I prefer to spend more time on issues where forward progress can be made rather than the ones that are entirely subjective in nature I'm really not here to win.

    [Quote from original post: 3) Twinking will not be stopped.]

    True. People twink. Most people don't have a problem with it. We've tried to design the game in a fashion that the effect of twinking in minimized. The main issue left is envy, which I'm not sure if we can solve. Someone will always be able to get more things faster than someone else.

    [Quote from original post: 5) There is a 'experience range' around the monster when it is slain. The closer you are to it when it dies, the more exp you get.]

    Though there is a range to get experience, the amount of experience you get is not scaled by distance from the creature, damage that's been done to it, or how many spells have been cast during the fight (some of the more popular myths).

    [Quote from original post: 6) Almost all of Verants statements have a hidden agenda.]

    Sometimes a cigar is just a cigar... - Freud

    [Quote from original post: 7) Following the path is not any less dangerous, and will not get you to your destination any sooner than running a straight line.]

    Maybe they are just trying to see where it goes? I usually follow the path in Nektulos. I get turned around in that zone so easily.

    [Quote from original post: 9) Kiting was nerfed, but not how you was told. Way back when, Verant decided to nerf kiting (dot and run). They told us that if the monster is moving, it will not take damage by the dot at all. Only if it is stopped, then it will be harmed. This is not true. What they did, is decrease the time the dot is present.]

    Huh? Kiting was addressed, but not in the way that you mention here. Duration is a constant. The variable is damage per tick. If the critter is moving and chasing (but not fleeing) at the time that the damage is applied, it will take 2/3rds the damage that it normally would. At the time this change was made, all DOTs had their duration upped slightly.

    We originally considered not applying any damage in the event that the creature was moving, but scrapped that idea due to public opinion.

  • Kunark Loot
    [Quote from original post: You have reduced coin-loot to lower than typical levels on most mobs in Kunark as part of your effort to prop up the value of plat?]

    The idea was to redefine, worldwide, the definition of good coin loot. The problem is that certain static costs in game remain just that, static.

    We're going to discuss the coin drop issues, and other things related to the economy (trade skill costs, component costs, etc.) in the tuning meeting next Monday.

  • Level Limits
    We really don't intend to close off areas of the game based on people being too high in level. There are a number of reasons for this, not the least of which is that some high-level areas require passage through lower level areas.

    Lower Guk, for example, requires passage through upper Guk. If you are in Qeynos Hills, EverFrost and Permafrost require passage through Blackburrow. There's also the fact that we'd have a few problems coming up with an RP reason to restrict higher level people from entrance into lower level areas.

    We only put the level restriction on the Planes because we wanted the items there to be a badge of honor for the person wearing them. When the plane of fear was first opened, people were taking level 1 characters up there to loot things that people in the group could not otherwise use.

    This limit also let us bend some of the previous game mechanics that were required in order to insure that twinking does not have a huge impact on the game.

  • Class vs. Skill Based Systems
    Actually I think that most people would agree that the class-based system is one of EQ's greatest strengths, even if they don't agree with the particular way we've implemented it in regards to a class or two.

    Skill-based systems, in my opinion, inevitably lead to all of the characters migrating slowly to whatever combination of skills is deemed "most powerful" at the time. The only safeguard against that would be a skill-based system that doesn't let you switch to the skill-lineup du jour, but of course, then it would really be a class-based system.

  • Are Hybrid Hogging Experience?
    Experience allocation hasn't changed, and is how it always has been, so nothing has changed. The specifics surrounding how experience is passed out within the group isn't something that we plan on releasing.

  • Desktop Bug Still Around?
    [Quote from original post: Why is it that every time I either log in or zone into Southern Karana, West Commons, or The Lost Temple of Cazic Thule I get booted back to the desktop?]

    I'd try deleting the XMI files for those zones. We created and distributed a bunch of them and it caused a few problems. We're working on them now.
