- Aurum @ 7:26 PM PDT
With another day comes yet another round of updates from Alan on the Developer's Corner:

    EverQuest: The Story
    [Quote: I started playing this game about a month after it was released. I read up on the manuals before I started playing, and in game I talked to each and every NPC, looking for quests, and the history of the world to immerse myself into it.

    The one thing I never found, after 2 years of searching, is the story. I know the history of Norrath, but I just can't find the story of Everquest.

    Maybe it's me, so maybe someone can help me find the story of Everquest? In particular, what's the plot of Everquest and who/what is/are the protaganist(s) and antagonist(s).

    Afterall, every roleplaying game has a story, or there wouldn't be any roles to play.

    HELP!

    Aloran Al`Aan - The Lost One
    Tunare Server ]

    The history of EverQuest is our job to convey. As the world builders, we set the setting and prop up the props.

    But as for the story of the game as it exists today, that's mostly up to you all to write. What happens on Norrath in the current days is motivated primarily by the actions of player characters. The story of EverQuest is what happened to your group last night in Unrest. The tales of EverQuest are told around the water cooler in your office the next day as you relive the battles and travels of the weekend with your friends. The epics of Norrath are written in long drawn out e-mail conversations with your guild members.

    Obviously there are restrictions to your ability as a player to tell your own tale, limitations based on the technology that restricts us and the game world. You can't burn the tavern down in a moment of drunken panic. You can't permanently remove Emperor Crush and replace him yourself (after all, you're far more handsome).

    But, barring these limitations, the story of EverQuest is written by the players.

    Bring life to your characters, and you bring life to Norrath.

    I know, I sound a bit like a commercial there, but that's what we have in mind. We set up the world and you all people it.

    [Quote: I'm not sure which thread this would go under better, so I'll... ahem... put it under both :)

    I was reading the Agnostic Iksar thread, I was reminded of an old MUD I used to play (it's still around actually). One of the things you could do in that game was donate valuables and cash to your guild (equivalent to class in EQ). At regular intervals, the game totalled up the donations for that interval, and whichever guild had the highest donations would receive a small boon to their abilities (though the exact quantities of the bonus were not revealed).

    Anyway, my idea is to allow donations of some sort to your deity. Donations could include cash or items; items would be valued at their nominal merchant-sold price, i.e., the lowest price for which a merchant would sell the item to a player character. After some interval - weekly, perhaps - the donations would be tallied and divided by some normalization factor (so the very few worshippers of Veeshan would have the opportunity to compete with the fifteen gazillion worshippers of Brell), and the followers of the leading deity would gain a small bonus of some sort for that period. Wizard Faire Update - Fennin Ro
- Aurum @ 6:48 PM PDT
Dynamite brought us this event update:

Hail good people of Norrath!

Again, I come before you today with news of Fennin Ro's Wizard
Faire! This Faire shall be the first of its kind, where wizards of all
levels come together to have fun. We shall have games, contests, duels,
prizes, and on top of it all, our monthly Rune Trade!

For those who do not know, in past months I have hosted a wizard
Rune Trade on Fennin Ro. Wizards of all levels came together with their
runes and helped each other: if there was a rune you needed that someone
had, you would ask and it would likely be given to you. Subsequently, if
you had a rune another wizard needed that you didn't, it was expected
that you would offer it to your fellow wizard in order to further his or
her own advancement. Several higher level wizards also offered their
time and skills in research to the cause, ensuring their lower level
comrades were able to get their spells.

This month, the trade is still on. However, on top of that, we will
be having several contests and games just for fun, some of which will
have prizes for the winners! As this is an effort put forth by the
community, as a member of the community you are welcome, and even
expected, to lend your assistance. If you have ideas for games (we
ALWAYS need these), prizes or money for prizes you would like to donate
to the faire, or any questions, please feel free to e-mail me at

fenninrunes@hotmail.com

or contact me in-game.

If you are making a donation, I would ask that you please hold on to
it until the Faire, but please let me know that you intend to donate it.
This way I can schedule the games around the prizes, making sure the
appropriate prizes are awarded for the appropriate games and contests.
Also, if you are not able to attend, but have runes or prizes you would
like to be at the Faire, then please give them to a wizard friend who
intends to attend. On that note, be sure to tell all your friends! The
Faire schedule is currently:

Fennin Ro Server
Toxxullia Spires
Saturday 28th April
Begins: 2PM PST (5PM EST)
Ends: when it's over! =)

The trade itself shall begin when attendance has peaked, so the more
people you bring with you, the faster we can begin trading. Also, I am
only one brewer, and in past months, I have had JUST enough of my fare
to please the attendants of the trade. If you are a tradesperson and
would like to bring some of your stock to the Faire, no matter what it
is (baked goods, alcohol, anything!) then you are more than welcome...
just remember to save yourself for the drinking contest!

Thank you my fellow wizards of Fennin Ro, for being such a wonderful
community and making this possible. When we stand together like this, we
truly shine in every way. It is you that makes the Faire, and the Faire
is magnificent. I look forward to seeing you all there!

Dynamite Shortfuze
Fennin Ro
Thanks.

    I've heard that sort of thing before. You're not alone in thinking that you don't know how to roleplay and that you would feel silly doing it.

    Most people already know how to roleplay. A lot of us roleplay at work (we act like we enjoy our jobs…) or when meeting the girlfriends parents (we act like a responsible person…). It's not a hard thing to do, and everyone can do it. There is no requirement to be clever, or funny, or silly, or any other such thing. All you have to do is try to think like your character.

    I've always told people that if you want to start roleplaying, build a character that would think and act a lot like you already think and act. If you are a jovial person that tries to see the good in things, then make that the personality of your character. The only 'trick' to roleplaying is to allow yourself to think as if you were really a citizen of Norrath. That citizen can have all of the same principles and values that you do. But he doesn't know about your favorite baseball team. He does know about the way that people treat him in Neriak or in Qeynos, and that matters to him.

    As for looking silly, don't worry about it. You said yourself that you think it seems like fun. You see others doing it and to you they don't look silly. And, well, it isn't silly. It's fun. Personally, I think people look silly when they dance…

    Ne Patch Feature Comments
    [Quote: Alan,

    Had a chance to test most of the new patch features (as much as a 28 enchanter can)and I am imensly impressed, thanks to you and your team! =)

    I have 2 comments reguarding new features however:

    1: /charinfo; can this be extended to include the number of kills and deaths attributed to the PC?

    2: Container Placement; As only 2 containers may be open at any one time, it would be nice to just save the location for [open container 1] and [open container 2].

    Finally, one bug I found related to new container changes: (told the QA team about it too, just passing it on as a warning to others):

    If you do NOT use Screen Same Resolution (makes your non-full screen view smaller) then if you open bags in full screen and place them near the edge, then switch screens to non-full screen your bags will be locked in the void outside the interface and you'll have to ESC out of your inventory and start over.

    Well, thanks! But I don't do the work, so I'll pass on your hug to the team (though not literally…).

    I really don't think we would want to track the number of kills a character made. First of all (and to me most importantly), this isn't a first person shooter. We're not emphasizing any sort of scoring system here. Secondly (and probably most importantly for our programmers), there tracking the number of kills you get is another bunch of data that we would have to track along with your character, and that's not something we want to do unless it's important to the game. And as you can see by my first point, it really isn't.

    And the answer to your second point is similar. As it is now, we only have to track the location that each inventory slot opens to. That's about 16 pieces of information per character (17 if we get around to world containers…). If we had to track this for each bag, we'd have to store it on the bags. So each bag would have to know where each person that holds it wants it to open…

    And as it is now it's just as useful as having the information attached to the bag.

    Special Food Items & Eating Food
    I need to make a correction to something I've said about eating food by right-clicking and about the way that special food items work.

    Right-clicking a food item only sends out the message that you've eaten something. It does not confer any of the special properties that the food may have. And the food will only last half as long as it would if eaten naturally.

    The special foods will give you their benefit as long as they are the food that you are currently eating, meaning as long as it is the first food in your inventory.

    I was mistaken in how I thought this worked, and I have been giving you the wrong information about it. I'm sorry.
