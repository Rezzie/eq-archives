Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:03 PM PDT
Hot off the Gameplay Board, here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • NPC Bards
    [Quote from original post: Why don't NPC Bards use their songs?]

    There are targeting problem. NPC bards singing an offensive AoE song would also affect any other NPC in the area. In many cases, it would cause those other NPCs to turn on the NPC bard.

  • EQMail Bug Reports
    The request for bug reports to EQMail and the award program only applies to Dupe Bugs. I don't expect it to be a very large volume at all. In fact, if the past two days are any indication, hardly any volume.

    It won't take time away from helping with CS issues.

  • Follow-up to Discussion of the Week
    I think that if anything, the thoughts presented supported my original post where I spoke about the diversity of EverQuests players, and the fact that many people see different things as fun. Some spoke, for instance, about trade skills being fun, while some others said specifically that they weren't fun.

    The task before us is to provide more. More options. More content. More things to do. We need to provide as many avenues to fun as possible, without infringing on other players' idea of fun. For instance, there are those who find it fun to cause other players grief. We do not wish to support that playstyle.

    EverQuest already has many avenues to fun available to the players, leaving it up to you to decide which avenues to take. We can always provide more, as long as those avenues do not cause other avenues to break down as mentioned above.

  • Water Races
    [Quote from original post: There is already such a race in EQ: The Kedge.

    Of course, They went extinct centuries ago.]

    Well not completely, as Phinegal somehow keeps rising from the dead. But they are huge anyway :)

  • Balancing the Classes
    [Quote from original post: You missed the point completely ! There is a huge amount of work that still needs to be done in balancing the classes.]

    That's certainly a valid opinion, but it remains an opinion. As I've mentioned before, regardless the status of the classes, and no matter what changes we make, there will always be people who have opinions regarding the relative power of the classes. The only way to avoid this would be to make all classes the same, at which point we wouldn't have a class-based system and thus the game wouldn't be EverQuest.

    As you mentioned in your post, nothing is exactly black and white. The concept of balance fits this perfectly. Balance is not a point that we aim for, but a range. Some classes might be at the bottom of that range, other classes might be more towards the top. It could be said that we should keep making adjustments until everyone is in the middle, but it should be obvious to anyone looking at these boards that we're not going to get universal agreement regardless.

    Right now our biggest concerns at higher levels is can you get a group. No class is so disadvantaged that they cannot get a group, providing that they've cultivated the friendships necessary to do so. Does this mean that a character that has soloed from 1 to a high level might not be able to get a group? Certainly, but that is OK. That is one of the prices of choosing to do nothing but solo.

    I haven't yet heard from anyone who will say that they have a bunch of friends in game, yet all of them say "sorry dude, I can't group with [your class] any more." People choose their groups not only based upon a specific need, but based upon who is "cool" and whose company they enjoy.

  • Monk Speed
    We'd decided long ago that a faster running speed wouldn't be a part of a class-specific skill, but would be handled via spells and such.

  • Age Matters?
    [Quote from original post: I am not bashing younger people with this comment. It is mostly the young that come up with innovative ideas. But without a certain degree of guidence from more experienced folks...innovation becomes idiocy.

    I have said it before....I saw the clips from the EQ gathering...and from the look there is not one Verant VIP over 25.]

    Age isn't important. It's ability. That said, I'm not sure where you got the "25" figure, but a good number of people are over that age.

  • No Response Bias
    [Quote from original post: Some people on this board are using the posts of others to show how bad one thing or the other is. How many of us that read these boards and are somewhat happy with what Verant has released. It is time for our voices to be heard. Verant has released a good product. I am not saying that it is perfect because everything has room for improvement. I may just be feeling this way because I am in this profession as well and realize the amount of work that has to go into even some of the littlest of tasks. I just want to encourage everyone who is happy with what is happening with Everquest to start to post. You may start getting more if the programmers feel like the work is appreciated.]

    Thanks for the note doc, and you are correct in that the programmers enjoy feeling appreciated. I and everyone else wish that players would go on and on about things that please them, but understand that it is not exactly human nature to do so.

    We're going to try again this Tuesday, with the implementation of another stunning UI enhancement, the biggest yet, care of Rowyl. I don't want to talk much about it yet until I'm sure that it will make that patch package, but it should be very well received.

  • Corpse Location
    [Quote from original post: 1. get someone and pay that person alot of money to help me find my body.
    2. Lose my body (fat chance)
    3. Attempt to locate a gm and ask for their assistance to locate and perhaps retrieve my body.]

    Don't forget bards, necros, and shadowknights. All three have the ability to locate your corpse, and necros can summon it if you can come up with the cash.

    /loc is an extreme out-of-character tool that was left in game under some objections upon lauch. It wouldn't be appropriate to expand its use in this manner.

  • Memorized Spell Limit
    [Quote from original post: So, the question is why have only 8?]

    To inject the use of strategy into the spell memorization decision.

    It also has the effect of creating risk since you could have the wrong spell lineup ready for an unexpected encounter. Better get someone to watch your back while you remem :)

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:20 PM PDT
Making a few late-night posts, Gordon Wrinn had the following to say in them:

  • Bard Thread
    I've noticed that we have about 15 different bard threads on the front page upset about today's conclusion of the lullaby issue. I am indeed sorry that everyone is upset, however I'll have to ask that you try to avoid making tons of posts on the same subject monopolizing the front page and try to restrict it to a thread or two. Feel free to use this one.

    Secondly, I'll have to ask that everyone keep the message board rules in mind when posting, specifically those prohibiting profanity, personal attacks, and flames. There is no idea worth stating that requires the use of any of the above.

  • A Player-made Proposal: Gordon's Response
    At some point we are going to have to stop making balance adjustments to classes and focus solely on content development. The problem is that many true problems have been solved over the past 9 months, and this has given the impression that we're out to enhance any class that can form a solid enough backing on the boards.

    However, there remains the problem that no matter what we do or what enhancements we make, we'll never be able to get to the point that everyone is happy and doesn't come to the boards here to complain.

    At some point, we'd cross a line where half of the people were complaining that the classes were too weak, while the other half would be complaining that they are too strong, and we'd end up upsetting someone any time a change was made.

    That point has already been reached. Most of the complaints these days are along the lines of direct class-envy, where one class perceives that they are lesser to another. The problem is that that other class is perfectly happy with that perception. Fixing the first classes issue upsets the second class.

    We saw that with warriors and monks in the narrowing of the damage gap from 50 to 60. Monks were happy outdamaging warriors by such a large margin once we put in the hand-to-hand speed bonuses. Warriors were quite upset about that. When we narrowed the gap from 50 to 60, warriors were satiated, and now monks are upset again. We could keep adjusting this gap until both classes are hitting for ice-comet damage every round, and we'd still never get it "quite right" in everyone's eyes.

    Regarding your specific topic, I imagine that we could get more creative with the stats on items and create a set of armor. Of course, we have to prioritize between what is needed, and what is wanted. Druids for instance still don't have mid-level quest armor because it isn't needed. The same issue exists with monks and Kunark now.

    We are doing one thing though: We're increasing the weight cap on monks in two steps: level 55 and 60. Initially we're testing 4wt at each step.

  • EverQuest Glitch or Worse?
    About the only explanation I can think of for what you are describing is that you probably have a virus on your system. I'd run some virus-check software. Failing that, you should probably give tech support a ring again.

  • Monks
    [Quote from original post: Cmon and reply Abashi]

    Andy's post already covered it. Gloves can't have combat stats. Items with combat stats can't use the hand-to-hand skill. Yes, in retrospect it would have been nice to build this in from the begining, but it's not something that we can go back and change.

  • Surname Question
    [Quote from original post: Once you make lvl 20, can you legally take the surname of an npc in the game? I know you can't take both first and last name, but say for RPing reasons you wanted your character to be related to an npc? Is that legal according to the rules?]

    Unfortunately it's not as simple as a yes or no answer, and is more the subject of a judgement call on the part of the GM and the Senior Guides. For instance, I doubt that the surname "Irontoe" would be preferable, since the Irontoe brigade is a major part of EQ lore. Other names however may be more acceptable.

    Contact your server GM should you have any concerns.

  • UI Improvement Idea
    [Quote from original post: I play an enchanter and one of the greatest difficulties I have is targetting the right MOB to mez. Would it be possible to make F8 (or some other way) cycle through all close-by Mobs if I press F8 more than once? Similar to F1-F1 to target your pet. F2-F2 should target the first party member's pet, but that's another request. Comments?]

    It's highly unlikely that we could implement this in a reasonable amount of time. It would require indexing of all creatures that are close enough, or on the current view screen, loading them into an array, and cycling the position of the array each time the button is pressed. The difficulty is that the array would have to be updated on each press to account for creatures that had entered or exited the range, making keeping a logical position in the array difficult. We could try going alphabetically based upon the true name of the NPC (a_moss_snake24) but it would still require loading NPCs into the "in range" list upon each press.

  • SKs and Summon Corpse
    [Quote from original post: Abashi, how come Shadowknights never get Summon Corpse?]

    Simply, because we decided that it would be a necro only spell. The fact that paladins get a version of resurrection is another matter entirely.

  • Changes to Sony.com
    I'm not sure. www.sony.com is controlled by Sony corporate. We're quite far removed from that division.


  • Upset Customers
    [Quote from original post: I've been lurking here a while now and I have to admit that recently there seems to be a huge increase in the average level of player frustration with the game and Verant in general.]

    The number of daily posts total has been pretty much constant for 9 months now. My impression is that there's been a greater number of positive posts here on the board as of late.

    [Quote from original post: So I am asking you, what do you think of all this? I can't imagine this recent rise in complaints has gone unnoticed. Is there any particular reason why so many decisions have been made lately which run contrary to popular opinion?]

    Just because so many decisions are being made. Any decision has the potential of being in accord with or contrary to public opinion. The Live team has been hard at work making a lot of changes to the game, so it's natural that a greater number of decisions would be contrary, just as a greater number of decisions are in accord with public opinion.

    Please keep in mind how these boards work. People tend to voice dissatisfaction where they feel they will be heard. They are heard on this board. If you go look at Stratics or EQ Vaults forums, you'll see a far greater percentage of positive posts.

    Also, people are far more likely to get worked up by something that annoys them than something they are pleased with. It's pure human nature. That doesn't make the complaints any more or less valid, but it does speak to the percentage of posts on the board.

    I'd say about 75% of the threads started, perhaps more, are to voice dissatisfaction with some element of gameplay. That does not mean that 75% of players are unhappy with the game as a whole, nor does it mean that 75% of people have the same issues. It just means that of the people motovated to post in our forum where they have a good chance of being heard, 75% choose to use the forum to get a particular issue of theirs addressed.

    [Quote from original post: Customer satisfaction has been steadily falling off for quite a few months now.]

    I disagree. Evidence points to it going up quite well. How I measure customer satisfaction is by those who go out of their way to praise the game, the team, and the changes that are made. Emails and positive posts here on the boards are up dramatically.


  • A Small Favor
    [Quote from original post: Could you give EsterTheTester a small ring and let her know that resistance rates are based on player level NOT spell level?]

    Actually there are components of both depending on the spell.

    Some spells are inherently more or less resistable than others. An example of this is the "Fear" series. The higher levels of the fear series are inherently less resistable.

    Concerning caster level, it's not so much the casters level as it is the casters level versus the level of the target. Resistance values receive a modifier based on this difference.

    [Quote from original post: If you could let her know this it would really help with her testing of Lullabye for Bards.]

    The testing is complete, the game designers and programmers have been consulted, and results are in. I'm afraid that the bards will not like those results. We'll announce more later today.


  • More on Guide Bannings: Right or Wrong?
    [Quote from original post: What *I* want is for Verant to do the decent thing and tell *her* why her player account was banned.]

    And we will, but that is not subject for discussion on the boards here. How we interact with the guide program is between us and the members of that program.

Test Server Smithing Q&A
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:03 PM PDT
Yakatizma made a very informative post on the Coalition of Tradesfolk Board regarding some smithing issues. Here's the complete post:

    There are a lot of threads being posted on the topic of the cultural forges, racial armors, and magical armors that are currently on the test server. The new blacksmithing content is still being added to and altered on a daily basis, thus certain aspects of it will not be commented on until it goes live. I will however address some of the concerns that have been posted.

    I. General Blacksmithing
    Many new tools and materials are being introduced that will be used within the blacksmithing trade.
    Many of the new general blacksmithing products are materials that will be in demand for other trade skills such as arrowheads for fletching, tools for tinkers, etc.

    II. Chain Classes and Smithing Upgrades
    Chainmail wearing classes are receiving upgrades as well as the plate wearing classes, including cultural chainmails and magical chainmails. Banded Armor is the standard blacksmithed chain class armor. The materials for banded are readily available and it has a broad market since it can be produced cheaply by all races and in sizes for all races.

    III. Plate Classes and Smithing Upgrades
    Plate wearing classes are finally receiving craftable plate, which can be produced in a variety of colors. In addition there will be craftable cultural plate armors. Fine Plate is the standard blacksmithed plate class armor. The materials are readily available, requiring only a little traveling and currency, and it has a broad market since any smith can produce it in a variety of colors and sizes for all races.

    IV. Weapons and Smithing Upgrades
    A wider variety of weapons will be capable of being smithed and cultural weapons will be introduced. Forged Weapons are the standard blacksmithed weapons. The materials are readily available and the weapons have a broad market since they can be produced cheaply by any smith for any race.

    V. Shields and Smithing Upgrades
    Forged Shields will be capable of being produced by blacksmiths. Cultural Shields will also be introduced. Forged Shields are the standard blacksmithed shields. The materials are readily available and the shields have a broad market since they can be produced cheaply by any smith in sizes for any race.

    VI. Cultural Armors, Weapons, and Shields!
    Rumors of these items have been causing quite a stir here on the Coalition of Tradesfolk board. The Cultural items are not profit making items for a smith. The materials are rare and expensive and the chance of failure to successfully craft a cultural item is high. In addition, the market for these items is very small being restricted to only a few races per culture. Obtaining your class specific quest armor is a cakewalk in comparison to obtaining smithed cultural armor. The primary purpose of the cultural smithing is to provide more cultural distinction to Everquest and enhance the role-playing environment.

    Q: Are all races capable of wearing at least one of the cultural blacksmithed armors?
    A: Yes

    Are all races capable of forging at least one cultural blacksmithed armor?
    A: No

    Q: Why Not?
    A: Several reasons, most of which have been brought up by players on the Coalition of Tradesfolk board. The main reason is why I call it Cultural Smithing instead of Racial Smithing. I'll use dwarves as an example. Kaladim is a mining city and the best dwarven blacksmiths are revered as heroes. They are heroes because of their "unique" blacksmithing techniques and secrets that allows them to forge armor that no one else can, like the Bloodforge Armor that the dwarven npc's produce. Sharing those smithing secrets and techniques would greatly diminish their importance. Dwarven players who have a love of blacksmithing are simply now given the means to reach for and achieve that status "within" their culture, but crafting that ultimate suit of magical imbued dwarven brellium platemail is harder than any other quest that dwarven player can attempt.

    Q: What races have their own Cultural Forges and who can wear the Armor they produce?
    A: Dwarves can craft Brellium armor that can also be worn by Gnomes and Halflings.
    High Elves can craft Mithril armor that can also be worn by Wood Elves and Half Elves.
    Dark Elves can craft Adamantite armor that can also be worn by Half Elves.
    Ogres can craft Adamantite armor that can also be worn by Trolls.
    Humans can craft High Quality Ore armors that can also be worn by Barbarians, Erudites, and Half Elves.
    Iksar can craft Skyiron armor that can only be worn by Iksars.
    Barbarians culturally do not prefer plate armors but do have a chain class armor in addition to their line of weapons.
    Gnomes have a forge that will be used in conjunction with tinkering to produce some gnome only cultural suits of armor.

    Q: Will other races have cultural products produced by trade skills?
    A: Yes. Erudite craftsmen are known for producing some of the finest robes and silks. Halflings are travelers and collectors; thus have a cultural demand for comfortable leathers and lightweight bags. Wood elves admire intricate leatherworking and Kelethin is home to Norraths finest fletchers. Iksars have an abundance of rich clay at their disposal and have a long history of crafting fine pottery, plus their claws are great for intricate etching. Trolls...well, trolls eat a lot.

    Q: What about Half Elves?
    A: Half Elves are a unique case. They have no culture of their own. The Half Elf advantage is that they can use more of the cultural items than any other race. Their disadvantage is that they have no cultural items of their own.

Brad on Dynamic World
[13 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:01 AM PDT
Brad responded to a post written by a player seeking a truely dynamic world. Here's the thread from the Gameplay Board:

    Anyone see one?
    Lower Guk is still the same (you will see it all again in 30 min or so...

    A truly and realistically dynamic world is one of the MMORPG 'holy grails.'

    I expect we'll 'get there' some day, but it will be a while. On the road there, however, you'll see incremental advancements. Second generation MMORPGs will be more dynamic than first, etc.

    Personally, I'd like to see a huge, detailed world that changed according to what players and GMs wanted/did. BUT, the changes need to make sense, they need to retain context.

    One short term way to achieve a 'dynamic world' is to randomly generate and scramble content, whether it be quests, world geometry, or whatever. I think we'll see more experiments into this realm in the future because it is a much easier route to a 'dynamic world.' You don't need hordes of game designers and world builders... you don't need incredibly sophisticated world and NPC AI that reacts and changes based on events... rather, you just need lots of variables and a good random number generator.

    This approach, however, at least for a player and designer such as myself, leaves me wanting. One day I'd like to see a huge, detailed world with every alleyway having a story behind it, a world so vast that it might take several years for a player to have explored everywhere (if it were even possible for one player to do so!). A world that changes but makes sense... perhaps a war ravages several villages, or perhaps a volcano erupts creating new mountain or island (and you SAW it erupt!)... perhaps a group of players went on a daring quest and, barely surviving, triumphed -- and the nobles of the land talked about your accomplishment and treated you differently for some time...

    Anyways, we have a long road ahead of us in terms of MMORPG development and I'm glad to be part of a company who is committed to reaching those goals :)
