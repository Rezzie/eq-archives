Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 5:45 PM PST
This is today's installment of the Absor Infobits:

  • Magicians and Summoning
    I know this is gonna be an interesting thread. And I’ll start it with just one question:

    What new kinds of things would Magicians like to be able to summon?

    And I’ll end it with a disclaimer:

    This does NOT mean that I am promising that you will see new summoned items in the game! We are just fishing for ideas here, not promising any sort of new stuff. In fact, maybe you should just assume you’ll never see any of this stuff, just to make it easier on everyone’s already over-stressed hearts. :)

  • "Talk" Threads and Deadlines

    Well, one a week would mean that we get done in... 10 weeks or so.

    Why?

    And, yes, that deadline is due, at least in part, to internal 'things'. It's just a good time to have things ready. I don't really see a reason to wait until the end of March to have these things looked at.

    And it's not as though I stop reading about or thinking about one class just because we are talking about another at the moment. The thinking and talking is always going on around here.

  • Planetside the EQ-Killer?
    Planetside is gonna rock. I plan to play the heck out of it.

    But I'll still play EverQuest.

    I suspect there are a lot of people out there just like me. Well, not too much like me, that would be just nasty... :)

  • Attention on Class Issues
    I have given no more nor less thought or attention to those other threads as I have to the necromancer thread.

  • Quad Kiting
    First, I feel the need to make comments about this whole quad kiting thing.

    ALL I was saying was that I, as a player and a person, don’t particularly like it as a tactic. As a Verant employee, my intention is to bring that up in a tuning meeting (as MY opinion, not as something that the players want changed). So, please, can we stop all the threads about how bad my idea is? I’ve got the message already, I know that many of you don’t want anything done to reduce quad kiting. I’ve received several e-mails explaining how it is a valid and balanced (from a risk/reward perspective) tactic. I will bring that with me to the meeting when I mention quad kiting, have no fear. And that meeting isn’t going to be next week, so don’t panic…

    I also wanted to talk about a trend that I have been seeing lately. It has probably always been there and I’ve just been slow to see it. But I have been reading more and more posts wherein people are attacking other people. And it bothers me.

    I have absolutely no problem with people engaging in spirited and even aggressive arguments over ideas or gameplay or any of that. I encourage it.

    But I do not want to see one customer attacking another. I have the responsibility to try to keep this place a forum for talking about EverQuest. For Verant this is one of our main sources for discovering new ideas, areas for improvement, and the general sense of things from our customers. When discussions break down into personal attacks, all that functionality is lost, and bad feelings are generated. Those bad feelings ultimately harm EverQuest and its community.

    I would really like to see a dramatic reduction in personal attacks. I hate having to be the bad guy here, but I will if it becomes necessary.

    Calling other customers ‘idiots’ is not acceptable here (or any other form of personal attack). And, if I start seeing an excess of personal attacks on Monday or over the weekend, we will be issuing warnings.

    I don’t want anyone to feel that we are trying to control topics, or control what you say. But we are willing to control the way you say it. Basically, BE CIVIL TO EACH OTHER.

    Or else… :)

    As a final note, I want to get through all of the classes with a ‘talk’ thread for each before February 9th (or thereabouts). This will be… well, a mess. I will be trying to start a new one every other day, starting on Monday (looking at a calendar, I may have to do more than three a week…).

    This will take up a lot of my posting time, I suspect. I think it’s a good thing for the game, though, so I’m going to do it.

    Don’t be expecting instant results on these issues, though. There are always tons of things going on here. And we really want to look at all of these issues as a whole, so I want to get them all done before we dig deeply into them. You might see some of the easy issues resolved right away, and others might not be addressed for a while (and by ‘addressed’ I mean either changed or checked off as ‘ok as is’).

    I’ll do what I can to keep you updated on all that.

    Let's not start acting like I've called in the four horsemen or something. You're starting to make me wonder if I should ever voice any of my personal thoughts again...

Absor Infobits
[36 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:30 PM PST
These are the latest quotes and excerpts written today by Alan on the Gameplay Board:

  • New Binding Spots
    They didn’t go in with this patch, so don’t look too hard.

    Hopefully they will make the next patch. There’s still some work to be done on them.

  • Druid Changes Affecting Wizards?
    It would be my intent to look into it for any class that can do it, and determine what to do from there.

    I'm certainly not telling you that tomorrow morning quad kitting won't work. I'm just telling you that my personal feelings on it are that it's something that needs looking into to determine if it's overboard or not.

  • Verant And The Use Of The Word "Fag"
    At first I was just going to close this thread and hope it this all would go away. I don’t really like commenting in these threads because it just brings more attention to this sort of thing. The last thing I need is someone quoting me on my stance about the word ‘fag’…

    But, here I am.

    And the rules for word usage here are pretty darn simple. It’s all about context.

    If you say that you have had a great day, and you feel gay, I will smile and read on.

    If you say that you are angry at something gay that someone did, I will think about issuing you a warning.

    If you want to talk about your cigar collection, fine.

    I am far more concerned about the impression people are giving with their words than the words themselves. But there are some words that used in most context will always be unacceptable. ‘Fag’ and ‘gay’ are among those types of word.

    So, hence forth, I’m going to ask Akriss to start sending out warnings on this sort of thing. If you consider this the wrong decision, or if you think it is wrong of me to try to direct your morality or freedom of speech, I accept that. But I’m still not going to allow it.

    And, well, I don’t really want to see any more threads started to talk about this subject either, as most of the time they are just thinly veiled attempts to use ‘forbidden’ words without being punished. Talk about how totalitarian I am if you want. But I’ll be unhappy if I have to issue warnings for things said in those threads that violate the ideas I’ve mentioned above.

    Like it or not, it is my job to keep things pleasant in here. If I have to do that by sending warnings for childish behavior, I can and will. But I’d rather be reading threads of import to the community than writing rebuttals about the poor word choice of some people.

  • Censorship
    Santerre wrote:

    [Quote from original post: I start a thread stating that Verant, and Abashi in particular, should apologize for shutting down threads on topics like melee binding and xp adjustment.]

    And I’ll answer that. I don’t think any sort of apology is needed. So, I suppose, I could offer you one, but it wouldn’t be sincere, and I wouldn’t want to do that.

    Shutting down threads on topics that are repeated over and over again is still the policy on these boards. This is now done at my insistence, and it’s not likely to change. So I suppose if you have a problem with that policy, then you have a problem with me as well as Gordon. And since your post is all about that policy, I’ll talk with you about it.

    [Quote from original post: Kill the thread. MORE censorship.]

    Sure, if you want to see it that way. I explained why I was closing that thread, I don’t feel a need to justify it again. I certainly reserve the right (probably more accurately described as ‘duty’) to close any thread here that I find does not meet the intent of this board, breaks the board rules, or is generally disruptive to the community here.

    [Quote from original post: But my original post was CERTAINLY in the realm of normal business communications. It had both kudos and concerns, stated intelligently, without the use of invective or name calling.]

    It was relatively civil in tone, but not in intent.

    Look, it’s actually pretty simple. At the time those posts were made, we had no intent of changing the experience system. Continued posting insisting that it be done was disruptive to the intent of these boards. The fact that things have changed now does nothing to affect the need to close posts in the past. At the time, those posts were disruptive, so they were removed.

    [Quote from original post: And that would be ANOTHER apology your firm owes to it's customers.]

    Actually, now you are asking for a personal apology, since I seem to have offended you, not all of our customers as a whole, by closing that thread.

    If my closing that thread has upset you, I am sorry.

    Would I close the exact same thread tomorrow? Most certainly.

  • New Mage Spell?
    Ok, technically Aegis of Ro IS a new spell. After all, just because you could see it in the dat file does NOT mean it's actually a spell in the game...
    :)

  • Druid Talk, Part 2
    First I want to ask you all to keep within the generally existing mechanics. We’re looking for balance, but I’m not going to bring a dozen new spell ideas to the team as a balance tool… And, realistically, we have to keep the ideas to things that won’t require a team of artists and programmers to create.

    Here’s what I’ve gleaned so far, filtered through my own ideas.

    Healing:

    Druids (and Shaman) want to be better at healing. Not only would this give them a more active roll at the end of the big raids, while at the same time reducing perceived ‘need’ for clerics at such times. But we also have to keep in mind that healing is the clerics role, and we do not want to infringe on that role.

    So the proposal is this:

    Move Superior Healing to 49th level.

    Add a new healing spell at 55th level. My suggestion so far is Remedy - a fast casting, low healing spell, and a solid upgrade from Superior Healing that won’t really infringe on the clerics power, which lies in big healing spells.

    Maybe add another healing spell at 60th level - perhaps Divine Light (though I’d rename it…)

    Reduce the fizzle rate on druid healing spells at the 50+ level.

    More Quests: There have been repeated requests for more mid-level quests for druids. Though this isn’t really a class balance thing, I’ll keep it on this list.
    Charm Animals: For a long time I’ve liked the idea of a druid being able to charm pets without making them angry. But this also has a ton of gameplay issues attached to it. Keep in mind that the main drawback to charm is that aggro factor. Taking it out would require some other serious drawbacks… But I’ve also got that on my list. It should make for some interesting discussions. :) The idea of making them act like enchanter pets would certainly help. ” How about a High level charm animal spell that only works on animals that con kindly or amiable. If the spell works, it says "The wooly mammoth decides to join you", if it fails it says "The wooly mammoth declines your offer".” Right, something like that… :)

    New Druid Rings: I’m not likely to advocate placing new rings, those require redoing whole zones to get graphics in for them. But some of these ideas might be feasible (I’m not saying that they are good ideas, just feasible…):

    If we assume that trees themselves can work as foci for druid travel magics:

    Surefall Glade
    Warslik Woods
    Greater Fay OR Lesser Fay

    Form of the mouse! (I’m only slightly kidding, I like the idea).

    [Quote from original post: Simple: Damage shield damage gets credited to the caster if more than 8 levels above the one being casted upon- same with mages.”]

    Actually, I’ve always wondered why this damage wasn’t always credited to the caster… I’ll ask.

    [Quote from original post: Any chance of treant form?]

    Hmmm… just a higher level version of tree form, that would come after wolf form, that is mobile… Interesting.

    Other new forms aren’t gonna fly (heh, good pun). As I’ve said before, I’d much rather advocate the use of any free space for giving enchanters more forms.

    As for Spirit of Wolf - part II the revenge of the doggie: I’ll suggest it, but I don’t anticipate it happening, and I’m not one that would vote for it.

    Soloing: And, as a note, I’m not inclined to suggest improvements to the druids ability to solo. Keep in mind that we are encouraging grouping…

    New looks: this isn’t a balance issue. But I’ll bring it up. I wouldn’t expect any new armor looks to come out as a result. Those kinds of things take a lot of resources to make.

    Class specific armor is too heavy (Vermiculated): This is also something I can ask about. Heavier than warrior armor?

    Elder Spiritist Boots: no component should be needed.

    Faydedar: I’m pretty sure someone is looking into this beasty and his lifestyle. I’ll follow up on it.

    Quad Kiting: My personal opinion is that the ability to do this should probably be reduced. I will probably suggest changes that have that effect. Though the only one I’ve seen (that wouldn’t also affect the rest of the gameplay for the class) that might work is to reduce the range on AoE spells…

    NO LURES! Not gonna happen, I tells ya.

    No resurrections either. I don’t see a need for it at all.

    I admit that after about seven pages I started scanning instead of reading. But most of what I was seeing were repeats of the same requests. I consider that a good sign, really. But if I missed something that you don’t see above, let me know.

  • New Code Bugged Experience and Damage?
    LadyMbc wrote:

    [Quote from original post: My lvl 35 mnk is now doing worse in groups than soloing... The amount of xp he gets from soloing seems to be more like 10 times the amount he gets when killing the same mobs in a group of 6. Even when i'm the highest in the group.]

This report was brought to us by Cadsuane Meladhrin:

While on a forray into the filthy lair of the Splitpaw gnolls to obtain spells for our necromancers today, we encountered an Iksar monk by the name of Master Vyyth Swiftscale deep in the bowels of the dungeon. He beseeched us to aid him in locating the paladin Brother Hayle so he could be 'freed'.

While some were weary about aiding a follower of Cazic Thule on such a benevolent mission, we agreed anyway, if for no other reason that to find out the Iksar's true motives. Upon arriving at the entrance to the jail holding the Qeynos paladin, he made his those intentions clear: He was to destroy Brother Hayle. Being an open-minded group of adventurers we decided to listen to his motives before making the decision on whether or not to aid him. He sat down and told us a story of a pact of Gods and betrayal (the log of which I will have linked up shortly). Being Gods with similar motives, he explained a secret pact made between Innoruuk and Cazic, overseen by the Tribunal, to spread the glory of the deities across the face of Norrath. This began with the opening of terror in the Kithicor Woods and was to continue with the razing of Qeynos. To this end, Cazic Thule sent his son as an avatar to give him the city. This son was Brother Hayle. Overcome by fear, Brother Hayle decided to turn his back on his father, and sided with the Paladins of Qeynos, becoming one of them. Enraged by this turn of events, Cazic Thule banished his son to the festering cave of Infected Paw. However times are changing, and Brother Hayle's death has become necessary to fulfill the pact made by Cazic Thule and Innoruuk.

After hearing his story we were shocked. Everyone's reaction was different. The paladins stalwartly insisted that no harm come to Brother Hayle. The Iksar and Tier'Dal viewed his death as a holy mission, and the key to unleashing the glory of their deities onto the face of Norrath. Worshippers of Fizzlethorpe could not see past the irony of the situation, thinking it all a great joke. The godless among us scoffed at the entire situation, disdaining meddling in the affairs of the supposed 'Gods'.

In the confusion that ensued, Master Vyyth ran for the jail. The paladins and others attempted to block his entry into the cell, bodily, but the slippery lizard managed to dart past them. It was then that Brother Hayle was confronted and defeated by Master Vyyth, along with some present, as others watched in horror, knowing they were powerless to save the paladin.

Master Vyyth left with cryptic words of returning to the Plane of Fear to report to his Master. One can only speculate at what, if anything, may arise from these developments. Only time will tell.

Server Population Issues
[14 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:34 PM PST
Brad McQuaid, who from the looks of it likes working on 3 day weekends ;), fired off a post on the Gameplay Board concerning server population issues:

    Hi all,

    I wanted to bring up this issue officially and also talk about some aspects of it, and how we are looking to solve some of the issues.

    First of all, we are working to come up with a new target server population at peak hours number. Our inclination at this point is a number somewhere between 2300 and 2500 players during peak hours. We feel we need to adjust this number because of how much Velious has added to EverQuest's size and quantity of content.

    We also feel that undercrowding is probably more detrimental to the game than overcrowding, in that while crowded areas are frustrating in that there is greater competition involved, there are some advantages to it (help from others when an encounter goes bad, help with CRs, etc.). (No, I'm not saying Overcrowding is good overall -- so please don't misunderstand me). Undercrowding, however, can mean a player can't find anyone to play with, and thus leaves. In other words, that frustration can be worse.

    There have also been some problems with packet loss and link death, specifically on E'Ci, when populations are growing high.

    So, what are we going to do about all of this?
    Well, before launching new servers we're going continue to analyze where players are at, what zones are crowded, and what zones are underused. Significant experience bonuses are going in next patch for underused zones (mostly dungeons).

    With E'Ci specifically we found out that after a recent hardware change, the network interface was incorrectly configured -- last night this was fixed, so please tell us if anything has improved.

    Also, when there are network problems out there with the Internet and a large group of players go link dead, this can cause zones to go down. We are working on this issue as well.

    So, there you have it, both gameplay and hardware solutions we hope will have significant effect. And please understand that any hesitation to launch new servers is due to undercrowding fears only -- if after these adjustments and attempts to better utilize all the zones we have it turns out target populations are lower than we believed, we will launch new servers, both split and new.

    thanks, and we look forward to your comments and feedback.

    -Brad

*HUGE* Changes Coming to EQ
[35 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:17 PM PST
Brad McQuaid and Gordon Wrinn announced monumental changes to the world of EverQuest this evening on the Gameplay Board. The post weighs over 5 pages and it is highly recommended that you read it all, as it will affect everyone in some way. Because of it's length, and at the request of Gordon, I will only be posting the intro and summary in this post. For the complete read please click on this link.

Here's the intro:

    Hi all,

    The following is the new Producer's Letter, which will be posted to everquest.com on Tuesday. I wanted to get it up on the message boards sooner so you all can get the word out, and because you guys are where we get most of our feedback and deserve a little advanced info now and then :)

    These are BIG changes, and are a summary of reactions to months of thought and a ton of feedback. I don't think we've made changes this fundamental since beta, so cross your fingers and read on:

    ---------------

    January 14, 2001


    Hello all,

    Since the launch of EverQuest: The Scars of Velious, the EverQuest Live team has been working on a number of things, from addressing issues in Velious to adding additional content to other portions of the world. At this same time, we've been working towards a point where we can stop tweaking class-based abilities, and declare with confidence that they are as "balanced" as we can make them.

    It is a foregone conclusion that the diversity of EverQuest's population will never let us reach a point where everyone will be perfectly satisfied with their class's abilities, as nearly every change we make tends to bother some people while pleasing others. Still, we are confident that we can reach a point where problems in our current game systems can be reduced to the point that those left are either negligible in their impact, or offset inversely by a similar problem affecting gameplay in an opposite direction. We're nearly there, but following heated debate and the review of material and opinions sent in by the players, we've decided to make some exciting and unprecedented changes to a game system that has remained intact since release: the experience system itself.

    While the EverQuest Message-board FAQ states that the experience system is not subject to post-production changes, it also explains that as developers of an ongoing MMORPG in a dynamic world, there are times that we need to be flexible and address issues as necessary. Though it has always been our goal to avoid changes to core systems whenever possible, our additional goal of finalizing class balance takes precedence in this case.

    We're going to depart from our usual method of releasing information by giving everyone some specific information in regards to how these systems currently work, and how they will work after the patch. It is very important to us that everyone understands our goals, and the fact that these changes are helpful to all of the players. We can only do that by giving you the details necessary for you to draw your own conclusions.

As I stated, this is a gigantic read. I recommend that you read it, but for those who don't have time or just want the details quick here's the summary Gordon provided:

    Summary

    The changes that we are making in the next patch should have a profound impact upon the game:


    • Classes no longer have a true class-based experience penalty, making it easier for people to play the class that they want to play, rather than the class that they feel compelled to play due to faster advancement.

    • Race based penalties are no longer shared with the group, and in truth are not severe enough to greatly impact the advancement speed of any particular race. Even the race with the worst penalty will level no slower than a human monk does now, and in truth will level faster due to the other changes that went in.

    • The grouping bonus increase, in addition to the other changes, makes grouping much more efficient, without reducing the current efficiency of soloing for those who prefer that route.

    • Experience modifiers turned up in the other zones should encourage people to spread out, have more fun, and allow those staying behind in other areas to have more fun themselves since the area is less crowded.

    • All players, assuming that they have an experience penalty, or ever group with anyone who does, will level faster.


    Many of these changes are quite involved in regards to their implementation, and as such we want to be sure that everyone interested understands them. Alan "Absor" VanCouvering will be happy to answer any questions that you may have on the EverQuest Message boards. Until then, we sincerely hope that these changes will help all players, whether "uber" or "causal", experience and enjoy EverQuest to their greatest potential.

    For Brad McQuaid, Jeff Butler, and every dedicated member of the EverQuest Team,


    Gordon Wrinn
    Associate Producer, EQLive

The Event Staff may use any sort of decision on who stays and who leaves to ensure the event is a success. This should not be implemented until a minimum of 150 players in the zone has been reached.
