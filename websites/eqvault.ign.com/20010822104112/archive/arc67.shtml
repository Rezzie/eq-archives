Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:30 PM PDT
Gordon made quite a few posts this afternoon on the Gameplay Board. Here's the full scoop:

  • More Rallos Zek Issues
    [Quote from original post: There is NO WAY AT ALL that this can be implemented! The gankers just die, then come back again and again. They get an item then either bag or bank it. There's no way at all to "outlaw" the practice.]

    You can start. Refuse to take them on plane runs. Spread their name across the server. Don't socialize with them. Refuse to trade with them. Turn them into social Pariahs.

    [Quote from original post: Secondly, you mention that the POD can only control one flag and that flag is already set for all Rallos players. Then PLEASE tell me why the POD is even there? He serves no purpose whatsoever.]

    You are correct, he serves no purpose on Rallos Zek. NPC spawns are not configured on a per-server basis. It wouldn't make much sense for us to maintain 34 different sets of spawn data to cover each server.

    [Quote from original post: You yourself know that programmers can do anything and everything with the code. So, the POD can be changed to control other flags.It's that simple.]

    It's not that simple. Sure, a programmer can do anything. However doing it in a reasonable amount of time without affecting anything else isn't always possible, nor in the best interest of the game. In this case, there is no "other flags". Creating "other flags" would require a pwipe. Creating "other flags" without a pwipe would require more time than is feasible.

  • How much do un-nerfed Pumice Stones go for?
    I doubt that the change has been in long enough for a stable market to form. It's pretty likely that when we missed a few vendors the first time that we were trying to implement the change that people rushed out and bought tons of the things. In any case, I'm pretty sure that the value will be severely limited due to the fact that it is still a 5 charge item.

  • What's the scoop on today's Tuning Meeting?
    Hasn't happened yet :)


  • Karana Still Overpopulated
    [Quote from original post: Any chance of getting another split to TT off Karana?]

    At this point that wouldn't be fruitful. We've already split them off a second time, leaving /movelog registration open for 24 hours, and only 169 accounts chose to move. It appears that everyone, or most everyone, on Karana at this point wants to be there.

    If we were going to take another group, it would probably be from one of the other servers, but nothing along those lines is planned at present.

  • Monk Posts on Gameplay Board
    [Quote from original post: Good lord 17 monk posts on page 1?]

    That's interesting. For the past several months, monk posts haven't been out of the ordinary in number. It sounds as if this "drive" is being organized from an external source. I guess I'll take this opportunity to mention again that attempting to monopolize the front page with a specific issue will just lead to the posts being closed.

    I haven't read any of the posts yet, but my guess is that they make note of specific issues relative to monks. Things such as "Thunderous Kick is too weak", "Flying kick should have some minimum damage", "Shiverback armor is low in AC", "Our Kunark armor isn't very good", etc.

    While those might be good and accurate observations about some specific issues, stepping back and taking the entire class, even with its faults (which every class must have), leads to the inevitable conclusion that monks still rock despite their faults.

  • Monks Lose Power Post 50
    [Quote from original post: The gap, apparently, closes GREATLY.]

    Yes, the gap closes. Monks go up. Warriors and Rogues go up faster. No one "loses" relative power.


    [Quote from original post: How can you keep calling monks the best toe to toe fighters? Perhaps pre 51 this was true, as we had a huge bonus in skill over other classes. But now that you've made Warriors and Rogues *nearly* equal in skill to Monks AND given them triple attack and double backstab ... there is NO way monks are still the best toe to toe melee damage.]

    Backstab is not a "toe-to-toe" situation. Yes, monks still do more damage in a toe-to-toe situation over a given timeframe than the other melee classes. Not as much as they used to, but they still do more than the others given appropriate equipment.

  • Invis Bug?
    [Quote from original post: I was invis in CB throne room looking to see how full room was so that I would not start train. Since when can Orc Lego's see invis? I was standing there looking at them when one somehow saw me and attacked. I still had the "invis" icon on my screen and had just casted at steps to castle. Please explain ]

    There's at least one thing that could have happened making it possible for a creature in there to see invis. Some creatures also have a chance, however miniscule, to see through invis regardless.

    In any case, NPCs gaining or losing abilities that they had or didn't have in previous weeks is one of the things that can change in the game.

  • Wrongfully Suspended?
    Though I can't speak to your specific issue, it's not at all unusual for suspensions or bannings to affect every account registered to a person or on the same credit card. This is particularly true if the suspended person logs on to the other account to contest the action.

    For instance, if I have 4 accounts in my family, get suspended in one, then procede to log on to each of the others and contact a GM, they will all end up suspended.

    [Quote from original post: I just have to laugh. In several years of running BBSes, every time someone tried to hack something or create multiple accounts or some stupid thing they got caught at, it was always "my little brother did it"]

    Hehe, you'd be surprised how unruly those "little brothers" are.

Morell-Thule: Plane of Hate Raid
[14 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:46 PM PDT
I was sent the following report by Aaeandar:

The masses gathered in the confines of the Wizard Portal in the Greater Faydark, the Land of Tunare's beloved. They gathered together to unify and take our the minions of Innoruuk, and if possible, Innoruuk himself. Members from Hamunaptra, Pax Tharax, In Virtue, Sixteenth Notes, Iron Legions, Coveit Emptor, and Circle of the Unseen, and set out as the sunset in greater faydark.

The masses stealthfully moved into position and prepared for the long insuing battle. For what seemed like days the warriors slowly but surely began to eliminate Innoruuk's minions, building by dreaded building. Only suffering minor casualties in the process. Then it came time to pull the dark lord's Maestro of Rancor from his dark pulpit at the heart of his pipe organ.

The Evil best tried its hardest, but even it knew that it had met its match. The victory was ours, and again, we had only suffered minor casualties in the process. We cleared the path to the Rancor's chamber and set up the last camp until we would attempt the Dark Lord Innoruuk himself. But, would he even show his face after the beating we had issued to his lower minors, and beloved Maestro? Only time would tell.

As the lower extremities of Innoruuk's plane of hate were gutted and destroyed, we began to to destroy the Ashenbone Drakes. The Drakes would fall in quick order, and then it came time to see if the Dark Lord was there to face the onslaught of warrior bent on his very destruction.

As we moved our way up to his chamber we destroyed the last of his minions only to find that the coward of a god did not even both to defend himself and his own home. We rejoiced at the victory, all had been slain at our feet. Rare items distributed, and crusted blood on our weapons. Soon, we will return for the Dark Lord himself, and then it will be his day of reckoning.... soon.

OOC -

Once again I'd like to thank the following people for organizing this raid, Oppression did a fine job in keeping things organized. Diggle and his brave band of monks did more than sacrifice themselves to pull the mobs safely and with excellent precision. Although we did suffer some casualties and 2 near full group deaths, we pulled of the corpse recoveries without a hitch and didnt lose even a step in getting back on track. We destroyed the entire static spawn with 2 hours to spare until the respawn, and (to say it quite appropriately) we OWNED the Maestro. The raid itself took roughly 10 and a half hours to complete, and for a Plane Raid was an outstanding success considering it was the server's first raid on the Plane of Hate. Admittedly we were a bit disappointed that Innoruuk wasnt up, but nonetheless it was a very enjoyable experience.
I would also like to ammend my previous post about the Nagafen Raid we had a few weeks back, and add that Iron Legions was also present for the raid. My apologies to Ekelhaft who also did a fine job as the main and secondary assist in this raid.
All and all, I'd like to thank those that came, and I'd like to thank those who invited me to come along. This was incredibly fun, and very well organized.

- Aaeandar "Bard of Doom" Vie (Pax Tharax) 46th Level Bard

Gordon Infobits: Saturday Edition
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:10 PM PDT
Here's the latest quotes from Gordon Wrinn that he posted on the Gameplay Board:

  • Rallos Zek
    [Quote from original post: Why wont you answer rallos?]

    I have, many times. It's just not the answer that people want to hear. We've no plans to change loot rules on Rallos at this time, nor do we plan on allowing any moves in regards to Rallos. I'm sorry, but there's really only so many ways that I can address this, and each time has lead to a flame-o-thon on the boards. I'd prefer to stay away from the threads for the time being.

    [Quote from original post: You all put up alternate servers and are doing splits like crazy.]

    I've seen people recommend that we should put up another server with Rallos rules, sans item loot, and do a split, however the number of people playing on Rallos wouldn't make that an economically feasible course of action.

    [Quote from original post: It was supposed to be the most real roleplay server. Now it has turned into an ebay-athon, and you allow the two new pvp servers to go coin only and stop the insane lack of roleplay which comes from a broken pvp system where a melee class is outclassed by ANY caster at ANY level over 12.]

    If it has turned into "naked item gank" server as so many say, does that mean that a majority of people are engaging in that behavior? If not, why is it that people who are against this don't socially isolate the people who are acting like this? I see posts here every day from people who want it one way or the other. I saw people on Vallon complaining as loudly for coin-only loot as people on Rallos. We made the change, and then people from Vallon complained just as loudly for item-loot.

    As of now, we've switched two servers to coin-only loot, which is the majority of the PvP servers out there. While we do want to please the majority and believe that they should have some say, we need to keep in mind the wishes of the minority. The minority here is significant.

    The 4 level limit on Rallos makes item-loot much more viable on that server than it was on the team servers with their 8 level limit. I recommend that if the majority on Rallos truly detests "naked ganking", that you come together as a community and outlaw the practice through social action.

  • More on Bows
    [Quote from original post: This is a problem, but what about the rest of the post, and the blocking of arrows by PC's? Or is this not an option / concern at this point? Any clairification is appreciated.]

    Projectiles are designed to be blocked by obstacles. The same issue occurs with "bolt" type spells. So to answer your question, it is not something that we consider a problem, but rather the way it was designed.

  • Why aren't there custom text colors?
    It's a suggestion that's been made frequently, and is something that we'd love to do at some point, however the "problem" at hand keeps getting more and more complex. When EQ was first launched there were only a few different types of "messages" that were displayed in the Chat window. Since then we've added a lot of different things due to player request, such as loot messages, taunt messages, we're about to add item drop messages, NPCs are now doing different things, and the list keeps growing.

    Our ability to implement color selections for all of those different types of messages in the client is wanning. It is however something that we will definitely keep in mind.

  • Melee Classes Getting Pets?!
    As the melee classes as a whole are balanced, certainly in terms of damage output, a pet wouldn't be appropriate in this case.

  • /Consent Changes

Truth be told, given the short term indications following the change, the immense load off of the GMs due to corpse scaming, we probably don't want to go down the road again.

    Even with seperate commands, many of the corpse scamming issues surrounded people being scamed in to giving consent in the first place. Those issues would still remain if we opened back up corpse looting. Though I do understand that it might be a difficult change for some to accept so soon after it's been made, experience has shown that the number of times players want others to loot their corpse rather than drag it is *very* small. So small in fact that it's not worth the risk to reenable it.

Gordon Infobits
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:36 PM PDT
Gordon addressed a few posts on the Gameplay Board today. For your convience I have compiled them all in the following text:

  • Roleplaying Server Thread
    My understanding from reading the first post, and please let me know if I'm off base, is that you'd like us to set up a server, designated as "Roleplay", provide guidelines for playing on the server, with more GM/Guide support for dynamic content. Furthermore, though the guidelines would exist, there would be no enforcement of it.

    Assuming that summation is close to what you are proposing, I'm pretty sure that your goals wouldn't be met in the long run. I think immediately people would go to the server with ideas about always staying in character. However, there are also people who play the game that would go there just to annoy the roleplayers. Then there would also be people who would go there due to having more GM/Guide support for dynamic content. In the end, without any enforcement, which most will agree cannot be applied consistantly, I think they would end up less in-character role-playing than other servers in the game.

    Experience has shown that without enforcement in an environment such as this, the desired condition cannot be met. Take this board as an example. Flaming, insulting, swearing, threatening, and being a general anti-social troublemaker is not allowed on these boards per the Station Terms of Service. Attempts to ask those people who post in that fashion to keep it respectful and courteous further drives the overall environment towards the undesirable. Why? Because it's not enforced. We are going to enforce those rules in the near future, and it will either work, or the boards will be shut down. I think that an unenforced roleplay server would have the same problem.

    But we can come up with consistant rules to enforce the boards, where we can't with a subjective concept such as roleplaying. I really like Har's definition of roleplaying, but can we expect any 10 people to enforce that definition in the same way?

  • "Reconsider Yellow Spell Damage Text"
    There's obviously people here who both like and dislike the yellow color. Given that we introduced the yellow color, I don't think that we'll make everyone happy by changing it back, so we'll probably leave it as is. Perhaps if we had left it white/black it wouldn't be an issue, but the cats out of the bag :)

  • "Why was Invisibility Nerfed, again?"
    It seems that after nearly every patch someone feels that invisibility has been nerfed in duration. I know that I've had to make posts such as this after well over a dozen patches since I've been at Verant.

    Just to be clear, invisibility was touched in this patch, aside from making it so that pets are no longer a valid target. In order to try to head off future claims of this nature, I *promise* that I will let you know if we ever touch invisibility's duration.

  • "As a Master of Magical Damage, why is a Ranger's channeling skill higher than mine?"
    [Quote from original post: Rangers cap at 215, Wizards at 200. What is the vision behind this?]

    As it turns out, we increased the channeling cap for pure casters in the last patch.

  • The Vision
    I've answered dozens of questions entitled "what is the vision". Why is it that these keep coming up with people assuming that it is some tangible thing that can be put up on a website for all to view? Have you ever said to anyone, "That wasn't really what I had in mind..."? What you are saying is that it doesn't agree with your vision.

    There is not some all-encompassing document that I can post somewhere for everyone to read, because it's an ever-growing and ever-changing concept. When something new comes up, we sit down and ask, "Is this what we had in mind?" A discussion follows at which time we decide whether or not it is. The vision is every email exchanged by every member of the dev team. It's the original design document for EverQuest (which will not be posted). It's the understanding that comes out of formal meetings in the conference room and the informal ones around the watercooler. It's every hope that everyone here had and has for EverQuest.

    Do you want to know the vision? Read our web site, read our posts, play the game, and ask yourself honestly what we had in mind, or, ask me. I answer questions every day here that consist basically of, "Why did you do XXX?" The answer to that question is the vision. But please, don't expect me to be able to lay out anything and everything that everyone ever had in mind about everything to do with the game. It's not that simple.

    [Quote from original post: I dislike playing a game where 95% of the important information is hidden from the consumer's eyes. ]

    That 95% of that information is meant to be discovered by you while you play the game. If you choose to go to spoiler sites to find out as much as you can, that is your choice, but we won't be divulging the mystery of the game. We feel that mystery is important in the game and that no one should know with certainty everything that there is to know. That's our vision.

    [Quote from original post: The only thing I know is what the VISION isn't. It isn't kiting, it isn't FD pulling, it isn't having good stuff, it isn't a lot of things. ]
