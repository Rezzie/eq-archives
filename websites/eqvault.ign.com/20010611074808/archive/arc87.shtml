King of Ak'Anon Slain - Tarew Marr
[25 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:23 PM PDT
Zanth sent me the following:

Zanth log:
Tuesday, April 11, 2000

There had been rumors of a new evil coming from Ak'anon, centerof the Gnome Kingdom. The rumors from weeks ago about the darkness near Erudin had been true. But there haven't been any more reports of strange happenings from Toxxulia Forest since we killed the Overlord in the Paineel. It seemed strange to hear of a 'new' evil from the heart of the Gnome Kingdom. The necromancers have always existed in Ak'anon and they are still no threat to Norrath...

This is what I can recall from the day the Gnome King fell.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

..Outside of town we prepared for our journey into Ak'anon. I glancedat the night sky and took a deep breath. Full moon, figures. Dear Karana,let us die well this night, and quickly.

Only five of us, not enough, not enough! The four others with me arepraised by some, unknown to most. They are good and I have great faith.The night passes quicky, we must go, time is of the essence.

..Inside we have problems early. The troll Uglar does not know when to sheath his blade and when to lay low. Perhaps it is the magic of the bloodfirethat drives the madness into him. Looking at his eyes you can see... small firesburning. Blade and whip swinging furiously, Uglar takes out bot after bot.He is not alone tho, next to him the barbarian Carressa shreds bots into scrapmetal. The first battle is over quickly; the two warriors had been hungry for some killing.

Quickly we moved towards the Palace. The whole way I had to listen to theranting of the drunken cleric Bannek. "Whars the ALE!" he says... "HAR HAR!" hecackles gleefully. If we didn't need him, I might have slit his throat. Damndrunk dwarves.

..There! The palace is in front of us. With the troll masked through druidmagic so he is not seen, the guards do not pay attention to him nor the others.It is common for barbarians, halflings, and dwarves to visit the Ak'anon.Through the front doors we walk in, smiling to the receptionist. "Are youhere to see the King?" she asks. I respond, "Why yes, we are," as I barelyhold back an evil grin.

Down, down, down we go, deep into the Ak'anon Palace. My heart is pounding now,glancing around you can not help notice the platemail wearing guards lining every corridor. _gulp_ ...Finally! We are before the throne room.

..Still hidden by the druid magic, the troll scouts about the throne room entrance.He's back and grinning. Trolls have really bad breath. "What are you so happy for?"I ask. Uglar says, "I found a torturer, here is proof of the evil!" "Lets kill it"We enter the torture chamber and lo and behold, one gnome torturer and two gnomeguards. Out come the swords and whips and the two warriors proceed to teach thegnome a little on the proper way of torturing. I'd hoped to get some informationfrom the gnome about the evil engulfing Ak'anon. Unforunately in the heat of battleCarressa put a six inch cut into his neck. Damn, the cut is too deep for the shamanwith us to heal the gnome. We decide to speak directly to the King.

..Hail King Ak'anon. King Ak'anon says, "Its druids like you who have ruined yourown lands! You will not ruin mine!" ACK!!!!!

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The battle that pursues is intense. The exact events I can not recall. When all was done, only 5 adventurers stood in the throne room, alone. At their feet liesthe corpse of King Ak'anon. With the evil King defeated the 5 friends looked at eachother, shrugged, and headed out after...

The Mayor of Rivervale (The TRUE Evil!)

Gordon Infobits, Part 2
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:15 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn has made even more posts on the Gameplay Board today. Here's round two of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Quivers and the Ammo Slot
    We agree that quivers *should* be equipable in the ranged ammo slot. Unfortunately, due to the way that we store data on the character, an insurmountable techical issue made this impossible given our core architecture. With the way that we store data on the character, a full p-wipe would be required to change this, and as such the prospects of this happening have been ruled out.

  • Level 50 Experience
    [Quote from original post: I was just wondering what will happen to people that have a full experience bar in 50, will they automatically become 51 when they launch RoK or will the exp bar be reset?]

    We won't be subtracting any experience points from anyone. The only *possible* thing that could happen is that we make an adjustement to the experience curve for level 50 prior to the release of Kunark which might make it appear that you have less experience. It is very unlikely at this point that we will do that.

    We expect that when Kunark goes live that there will be many people who are but a kill or two away from level 51.

  • Will we ever see full screen interface windows for high resolutions?
    Though I'll avoid saying that this is outright impossible, it is highly unlikely at this time. The design of EQ's interface uses custom bitmaps that have to be produced for each supported resolution. We don't want to do something cheesy like stretching these images out, as it will not only look bad but cause some technical problems.

  • Problem with Graphics: Missing Shields
    We've had a few reports of this come in from other sources now, and our testing team is taking a look.

    It appears that no shields are showing up. I'll get back with everyone when I have more information.
    --
    Update:

    Turns out it's just the Shiny Brass Shield that isn't showing up. Trying to find out why now.

  • Future Server Splits
    The server splits will take place prior to the servers first coming up, so people playing on them won't get surprised by a few hundred level 50s showing up. We'll announce more information when we have the servers ready to go.

  • What's up with Kerra Isle?
    Yes, something indeed went wrong with the patch. Our operations personnel are currently in the process of patching the correct files. This can be fixed without bringing down all of the servers, as it only affects that zone.

  • How much hard drive space will Kunark take?
    I have 622MB in my EQ directory. I'd say about 650 (for the data) to be safe.

  • More on loud footsteps
    The problem with the loud footsteps was indeed something that we implemented today. It has been fixed on test, and will require a client download on the live servers for the problem to be fixed there. Unfortunately repatching the client will probably not occur until early next week. Our apologies to those who have to endure the loud footsteps in the meantime.

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:32 PM PDT
Gordon has been posting up a storm today on the Gameplay Board. Here's excerpts and quotes from his latest round of posts:

  • When will the Hate drops be fixed?
    In the data patch tomorrow. (Note: this was posted yesterday)

  • Future Servers
    Yes, 4 new servers are planned in the very near future. Our current plan is for two of these to be split-servers (like Druzzil) and the other two to be clean-slate.

  • Camping Big Spawns
    I really don't think that this is possible any longer. Some time ago we put in code that added a random component to the spawn timer for the longer spawns. Though I can't specifically announce the size of the random component, I can say that the window for a dragon-type spawn is in excess of 24 hours. That means that even if they had it timed before we did this, they'd have to sit around a LONG time.

  • Patches and the Expansion CD
    The expansion CD will include all of the patches made through about the middle of last month when the master was burned.

  • Adding New Classes
    Adding new classes is a very complicated process. It raises all sorts of balance issues that have already been dealt with concerning the original 14.

    Except for the art, new races are comparatively easy.

    In any case, we have no short-term plans to add new classes at this time.

  • Will there ever be Agnostic-only weapons?
    No plans for this. We really don't see it making sense in the grand scheme of things.

  • Will we ever be able to change religions?
    No plans for this either. It's really no different than someone being unable to wield/wear an item due to their selection of race or class.

  • Removing Rare Spawns from Tracking
    It has long been our stance that the ability to track down rare spawns is a benefit of having tracking. Removing rare spawns from the tracking list as you request would negate that benefit.

  • Logging out in the PoH
    [Quote from original post: My question is this: when i log in today, should i expect to instantly die to a horde of new roamers you put in there? that would, to say the least, suck...]

    Well I of course can't comment on any specifics, but I would recommend that you arrange for a suitable army to meet you upon login.

  • Qeynos Hills to Western Karana Zone Bug
    It hasn't been overlooked, it's just difficult to fix without requiring a 4+ MB download for everyone. It's on the list for repair.

  • Patch Message Inaccurate?
    [Quote from original post: Also, for enchanters, DDD did NOT have its casting time reduced. The RECAST time was reduced. I'll post up a message about getting this changed in a minute unless Gordon reads this one.]

    Doh! It's right on the list that GZ gave me, I apparently wrote it down wrong. I'll see about getting it updated after we wait a little while to see if I missed anything :)

  • I cancelled my account, are my characters still there?
    They are probably still there. To my knowledge we haven't yet had to clean up old accounts. That's not to say that we won't do it some day (I believe that we are only required to hold them for 90 days), but drive space hasn't become an issue at this point.

  • Changing the Terror Forged Mask
    The same day that the terror mask was first found, we announced that the spell effect on it was a mistake and that it would be changed back. In addition, the NPC that hands it out had his quest dialogue changed to tell people acquiring it that the mask would soon lose its current spell effect and gain the power of fear.

    Though it is true that we try to keep existing items as is, there are cases where an item is so severely overpowered that it must be changed in order to maintain the integrity of the game. This was such an example.

    All of that said, I'm sorry that you are upset. It is next to impossible for us to insure that every player is made aware in advance for every issue in the game. We rely heavily on the news sites to help spread the word when something interesting happens.

  • Patch Issues
    I'm chatting with the team about these issues now. Of the people that have noticed a problem with footsteps getting louder, what sound card(s)? Also, is it your footsteps, or others footsteps?

    As for "No change to Kerra Ridge", it was patched incorrectly and is being redone now. We're still looking into the beggar.

  • Why does root and/or snare not have a "message" when it breaks like the DOTs and charm?
    As they are offensive time-based spells, they should be issuing a message when they break. I've asked the programmer to look into it.

  • Blackburrow's History
    Hahah, I remember when Blackburrow was the only dungeon. Dang, that was nearly 2 years ago.

  • Gating Spells
    Good thought. We just gained this ability (a cast-on-others "go home" spell) recently. I've also raised the idea of a "Group Gate to the Wizard's Bind Point". I'm not winning that argument though, so please don't hold me to it :)

  • Deity Restrictions
    By choosing "agnostic" a person makes a choice to avoid both the positive and negative aspects associated with choosing a deity, in the same fashion that his selection of a race or class allows him to avoid the positive and negative aspects of another class. By the same logic that we do not allow people to change their race or class selection based upon new items or spells in the game, we likewise won't be allowing the change of deities based upon the same.

    EverQuest is a game that is subject to change, and when a person makes a choice that choice not only affects them in the present, but for the future as well.

I received a report of a GM event from Malfane:

I wanted to take a moment to thank the GM's on the Luclin server for the
outstanding job they have done thus far.

A fine example of this was the small scale event they held last Friday night
in Qeynos Hills, Blackburrow, and Everfrost. Three merchants, the Goodbuys,
were walking through the Qeynos Hills area shouting that they needed an
escort to Halas so that they could deliver supplies to the Wolves of the

their way to the mouth of Blackburrow. There the impromptu party set forth,

find Halas. Finally we met a few kind Barbarian souls who guided us in to
Halas. Throughout it all, we protected the merchants and had a lot of fun.
The only sad point came when we suffered a single death due to someone who
was KOS approaching the guards too closely.

In appreciation for our efforts to guide and guard them, the merchants
handed out Black Pearls as a reward to many of their protectors.

This was a fine example of a well run and quite humorous GM event, with the
only death due to the GUARDS!

Thank you GM's of Luclin, keep up the good work!

Patch Day Message
[42 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:03 PM PDT
A new message was just added to the EverQuest Patch Server:

    April 10th, 5:30am
    -------------------

    ***Patch Day***

    The following is a list of changes that were implemented on the Live
    EverQuest servers today:

    *Spells*

    - You will now receive a message when attempting to mesmerize a creature
    that is immune to that type of spell.
    - You can now right-click-and-hold on bard songs to determine what
    instrument skill is used by the spell.
    - Dyn's Dizzying Draught has had its casting time lowered significantly.

    - Corrected a bug in the Druid root+damage spells (Enveloping roots
    series) that caused the spell's damage to have a chance of breaking the
    spell. They work much better now.
    - The Magicians Phantom Armor series now grants HP regeneration.
    - The Druid spell, dance of the fireflies, has been changed from
    "Outdoor only" to "Night only". It may now be cast in dungeons.
    - Corrected a typographical error that allowed high level magicians to
    inscribe the Malosi spell. It was not intended for magicians under
    level 50.


    *General Changes/Fixes*

    - Items have gained the ability to be restricted by Deity. If a deity
    restriction is placed upon an item, it will show when you
    right-click-and-hold on the item.
    - The AC on the mid-level cleric quest armor has been increased.
    - The Terror Mask will now cast "Fear" instead of "Call of Bones" as
    previously announced. The "Call of Bones" effect was placed on this
    item by mistake.
    - The Mempo of Likato now grants AC.
    - The Quest for the Short Sword of Morin has been fixed.
    - Some spawn-time problems in Mistmoore have been repaired.
    - Five spawn-time problems were identified in regards to the Armor of Ro
    (Paladin) quests. They have been fixed and Paladins should have a much
    easier time of obtaining their armor.
    - Pets should now attack Willowisps on command.
    - Many spell-gems and icons have been changed to reflect the appropriate
    action of the spell. Be sure to reacquaint yourself with your spell
    line-up.
    - Fixed a bug that caused targetable bard songs to ignore the instrument
    in use.


    *Kerra Ridge*

    Kerra Ridge has been completely revamped. The target level-range for
    the zone has been slightly increased. In addition, many new quests have
    been added to the zone. The faction for Kerrans has been changed so
    that those who killed them indiscriminately before today's update can
    take part. Be warned that the Kerrans still do not like everyone.

    *Paw*

    Paw has received another minor difficulty adjustment. In addition, item
    drop percentages have been increased.

    *The Plane of Hate*

    The Plane of Hate has received an item-drop/spawn-time revamp. Take
    care in your exploration of this zone as many things have changed.


    - The EverQuest Team
Thanks Zykstar!

Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:35 PM PDT
Verant I-Net Relations Manager Gordon Wrinn spent some time on the Gameplay board this weekend. Here's this weekend's round of infobits:

  • Any plans to implement a quest to change starting stats?
    We have no plans for this. It was part of the core design of EverQuest that base stats would be fixed.

  • The Consider
    The /con system is an indication of level. It makes no note of special skills, abilities, or attacks. For example, some encounters of a specific level might be more difficult than others.

    As people level up, they get stronger. However NPCs get stronger on a steeper scale. For this reason, the /con system goes from being representative of solo abilities to being representative of group abilities. The rate at which this happens varies for all of the classes.

    NPCs of a higher level MUST be stronger than a solo player, or groups will tear through them with no problems and no risk. For this reason, the /con system is not broken. At lower levels it fairly well represents the abilities of a solo player. At upper levels, it very closely represents the risk to a full balanced group.

  • On releasing Kunark early for those who ordered on net
    Actually I think it was Brad that mentioned that. We're still aiming for it, but it's too soon to guarantee it, so the official date is still 4/24.

  • Can you tell me how to get Kunark?
    There will be two ways to get to Kunark. Teleports and ships. If you haven't purchased Kunark, the teleports just won't work for you, and if you are on the boat, you'll be transported back to the dock when the boat zones to a Kunark zone.

  • Why Even Have Zones?
    Massively Multiplayer RPGs have a long development cycle compared to other games, and the graphics engine is the first thing that is designed. Back when development started on EQ, the development team had several goal for operation of the game. For example, they wanted to minimize the effect of internet latency. They also wanted to have each "area" have its own look-and-feel, with its own set of appropriatly placed NPCs.

    At the time, the "Zone" technology was the best technology available to meet the goals of the development team. Of course since then, new technologies have been developed, and though it makes sense that we would take advantage of them in future products, EQ will always have zones. You just can't change graphics engines on the fly post-production.

  • Blacksmithing Bug?
    The "Block of Ore" listed in the blacksmithing manual is just for the purpose of scaling measurements. For instance, the book says "3 large bricks = 1 block", therefore when a recipe calls for "a block of ore", you can substitue "3 large bricks".

    You can't combine 3 large bricks to make 1 block. 3 bricks is the equivalent of 1 block.

  • Are Ogre's "unstunablitiy" by design?
    Yes, this is by design. It is one of the advantages of their large size that offsets the disadvantages. I think that it could be arguable that they should be stunnable while shrunk, but it might be too late to see that if it isn't there already.

  • Summon Corpse Nefed?
    I'm not sure where the rumor came from, but the spell hasn't been changed to self-only. As a precaution to insure that something inadvertent didn't happen, one of our testers checked it out. Nothing has changed with the spell.

War of Flowers - Vallon Zek

I received the following from Mlaria:

I wish to stage Norrath's first "War of Flowers." This is a name taken
from a ritualized war that the Aztecs would have with the tribes that
lived in the surrounding area. They were wars that were conducted in
times of peace so that warriors could "prove" themselves on the field of
battle, as well as to capture human sacrifices to offer up to the sun.
(This is a simplistic explaination of what the "War of Flowers" or
"Flowery Wars" where. It's some damn intersting reading and I suggest if
you have any interst in this sort of stuff to do a search on the web
about it.)

This war will take place in 3 zones:
West Commons.
East Commons.
Desert of North Ro.

Object and Rules:
1. Each team will pick 4 players that are their Standard Bearers
(Standard as in a flag)
2. The Standard Bearers will be of levels 9th-11th, 16th-18th, 23rd-25th
and 37th-39th.
3. Each Standard Bearer will be given a jewel (the standard) that must
stay in a lootable slot.
4. Each Standard Bearer MUST stay in the 3 war zones with his anonymous
and roleplay turned off so his/her location can be verified at all
times.
5. The war is won when 1 side has looted all the opposing teams
standards. (jewels)
6. Teams will be Good vs Evil.
7. Out of Range healing is acceptable.
8. CR will not be respected (except as stated in rule 11)
9. If the Standard Bearer leaves Norrath for any reason, he is in
essance dead. Wether LD, RL or whatever.
10. If a Standard Bearer leaves the 3 zones, wether getting killed and
returned to his bind point by a MOB, accidentally zoning, gets knocked
out of a war zone by something like a wiz spell or any other reason,
they are dead and must surrender their standard.
11. If a Standard Bearer is killed and his Standard (jewel) is not
looted, then the Standard Bearer will be afforded standard CR
privilages. If the Standard Bearer does not return to his corpse within
15 minutes then he is dead and must surrender his Standard (jewel).

Discussion on rules/team leaders/etc will continue on the War of Flowers
