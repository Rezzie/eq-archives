Alan on Class Talk
- Aurum @ 7:06 PM PDT
Alan spoke for a while on the status of the Class Talk Threads in a recent update on the Dev Corner. Here's the entire post:

    We have been receiving a fair amount of mail regarding the status of changes to classes based on the items brought up in the class talk threads that I started a while ago. Right now I have little new information for you, but let me spend a moment talking about this.

    The team has read and reviewed the lists that I put together. We've had several informal discussions about them, and a few formal discussions about specific items on those lists.

    So far you've seen a lot of changes based on your input. They have been going into the game as they get finished and tested. Things that didn't require a lot of in-depth investigation on our part, such as changing the Bread Pants for Bards, increasing the drop rates for many Kunark spells, fixing the Track Corpse spell and many others.

    And even some of the more complicated issues have been looked into. Things like giving Druids and Shaman the ability to act as secondary healers, upgrading Mark of Karn (a very complicated spell to balance), the aggro created by debuff spells (and, yes, the aggro created by healing spells), and others that have required a lot of consideration have been added or changed.

    We've also started on some of the much more complicated issues, such as Wizards and their ability to do damage against certain NPCs with the Bane spells.

    To finish the lists we'll have to make the time to have some long and complicated meetings over issues that can't be changed without rather a lot of effect on the game world. We have not had those meeting yet. Recently we decided that we would put PvP issues to the forefront, considering that lack of attention they have had over the life of EverQuest.

    I just wanted to let you know that we haven't forgotten the issues that you brought to our attention.

    Alan


Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:01 PM PDT
More updates just in from Alan from off of the Developer's Cornder:

    Mmm...Cake
    [Quote: Personally, I prefer my cakes made with High Elves baked inside. If not High Elves, then Barbarians will do in a pinch.

    Sinfully Yours,
    Paradise Perdu

    Well, hopefully we can reduce the need for you to go to such gruesome extremes. We will be improving the drop rate for brownie parts with the next patch. Other than that we'll probably be waiting for the other food related changes to work their magic before making other changes.

    More Graphic Issues
    [Quote: Just a note about this. Not sure if it has been noted in other threads (but it should be in this thread). If you are using a Glide capable card (voodoo 4 & 5), by default EQ sets your video driver to the Glide driver. Got back to the first screen of EQ (Where the "Connect" button is), and select options. That will bring up the video driver options you have available for EQ. Make sure that "Primary Display Driver" is selected and not the "Glide" driver.

    Things should work fine after that as long as your video card is capable of the 32 bit mode.

    Regards,

    CJ ]

    Thanks for the tip. Yes, the 32bit option will not work if you're using glide drivers. CJs suggestion is a good one.

    Panic Animal
    Dmiedema wrote:

    [Quote: Not sure which board this is supposed to be posted in, so I posted in both. If it is intentional, I guess it goes here, if not it goes in the bug board.

    The panic animal spell gives no message when it lands. As it works now, the only way to tell if it lands is if it doesn't give a resist message.

    The undead fear spells give a message, the regular fear spells give a message, and every other spell that I have ever used gives a message when it lands, so I'm guessing that this is an oversight and should be corrected.

    This spell has become a very vital soloing tool for rangers since it has been put in, and I can't believe no one else has mentioned it.

    If you guys can't think of anything creative to put in as a message, I would suggest just re-using the regular fear message.

    Oh, and if it is supposed to be this way, an explanation why this spell having no message is the exception to every other spell out there would be nice.]

    Well, I have to admit that my first thought was, 'if the animal runs away, then you know the spell worked…'.

    But for consistency's sake we've added success messages for those spells. You should see them after the next patch.

    Pathing Problems?
    Normally we would want you to send your pathing problems to the Quality Assurance – Testing form, but I wanted to bring special attention to this for a while, and to respond to a comment that we received. First, the comment:

    [Quote: Subject: Pathing at the Ry'gorr camp in Eastern Wastes

    This has been something of a problem since I've hunted there. It can be avoided, but sometime it can be fatal or abused. Let me explain:

    The pathing inside the main house at the Ry'Gorr camp in the Eastern Wastes is horrible! It's almost non-existant. For instance, if you pull anything from inside the house, you have to be careful not to outrun it and keep it in a straight line and lead it out the doorways. The orcs are programmed to run in a straight line towards you, no matter what.

    If you do not lead them out yourself and they get caught in a wall or doorway, they continue to run in place trying to get to you. After about 15 seconds or so, they will "magically" drop from the sky and attack you. Most of the time, they get in a few free hits. I have found that it doesn't matter how much distance you put between you and the orc, whether you are running or standing still, or any other variant makes a difference. They WILL catch you. And these free hits can be fatal if you are a caster, especially if the Chief is along. Even if you survive, these orcs run at Jboot speed and are now in the correct distance to keep smacking you til you die.

    Another problem area is the balcony in the chief/oracle room. If you stand anywhere close to that doorway but don't enter the room, you pretty much can assure yourself that you have aggroed the Chief and his Elites. Again, with the fact that they run in a straight line towards you and you don't see the message that they have aggroed on you, it's a rude awakening to have all 3 of them drop from the sky on you.

    This also works for any orc that happens to spawn on the walls outside the house if you sit in the house. They aggro on you and drop from the ceiling and begin combat. Sometimes they land on the roof and continue to hit you.

    There is only one time where these orcs follow any kind of pathing, and that is when you fear them. Then will run from whatever direction they come from, counter-clockwise around the bonfire room, and out the doorway. They then run outside the walls of the camp and continue up the hill and into the snow.

    Another problem I had was I was fleeing from the Chief. I ran completely outside the camp and up a nearby hill. Being a level 51 necro, I always have DMF cast on me and I proceeded to run off the hill and float over the wall and back into the camp, making a break for the Crystal Caverns orb. Now when I made the wall, the Chief was close behind me but had not caught me yet. I get to the gate and try and open it. Now, this is the only gate in the game that I found hitting the "U" key does not open. In fact, you miss half the time trying to left click that gate to get it open. Can you also check into why you can't hit "U" and open it? Anyway, I finally get the gate open, just barely get inside when the Chief appears and slays me instantly. He managed to get by a wall and a second room wall to get to me in a matter of seconds.

    Now the place where this can be abused is the fact that you can turn the orc tactics against them. This tactic is mostly used by wizards and druids. They run into the house invisible and target themselves an orc. Then they run outside the house and get as far as possible and snare the targetted orc. They can still cast at the orc even though they are outside and the orc is inside. This usually gives them many free hits before the orc appears and hits them.

    So my question is this: Is there a way to fix the pathing in this camp? Possibly give these orcs a set way to run out of the camp and avoid getting snagged running in place? I know it's part of an outdoor zone so the "line of sight" doesn't apply it seems... but perhaps at least giving the orcs a way to stop dropping from the sky would be great. They already run very fast so I don't see where the extra time would hurt.

    Thanks for looking into this if you can! I hope to avoid another 5-death day like I had a few weeks ago due to this pathing.

    --Cy ]

    First of all, I wanted to thank you for the detailed description. It helps us a lot in these situations when you give us all the details. The only item that you didn't send us that might have been useful would be a few /locs for key problem points. These make it a lot easier for us to find specific areas that need attention.

    The bad news is that there isn't much that we can do to fix this problem. The code that allows the NPC to pop directly to you is needed in the zone to prevent exploits. And it seems that you know how to avoid the problem, it just requires that you be very careful when pulling not to get too far ahead of them.

    Now, on to my request.

    What I would like to ask all of you is this. Reply to this post with places that you've found pathing problems. We can't fix them all, but we want to know about them and fix the ones that we can fix. Like I said, normally we would want you to send such things to the Quality Assurance folks. And after a short while I'll probably just lock this thread and ask you to again send your pathing issues to Quality Assurance. But for now I'm hoping to generate a few more responses on this issue so that we can focus a little effort on it.

    Please be specific about the location and NPC names involved. But at the same time please try not to include a lot of extraneous information. We don't really need to know about the whole adventure that you had to get to the pathing problem, just the problem itself. One of our talented designers is going to be reading your messages, and we'd like to use his time as efficiently as possible.

    Keep in mind that some of these problems just can't be fixed due to limitations in the game engine. But we'll fix what we can.

    Thanks.

    Player-baked Food Stat Bonuses
    [Quote: I read this rumor on the Prexus General Messages boards. Raises interesting questions, if it's not a hoax. The essence of the rumor is that foods made with the Baking skill (didn't see if it was all foods or just some recipes) can now confer stat bonuses. They can also be right-clicked to be eaten immediately, but last half the duration of other foods (i.e. you get hungry again sooner).

    1) Will you eat the food automatically if it's at the top of your inventory? If so, who would ever right-click on it if you could get full-duration effects by always eating baker-made food?

    1a) If you do automatically eat the baker-made food, does that simply mean that consumption rate is doubled? Kind of a poor trade-off for first having to either raise your tradeskill or buy the food (at what will be sure to be inflated prices) from skilled bakers, then have it only last half the time a regular stack of food would?

    1b) Does the food's effect last until you next consume food, or does it wear off sooner? Or does it possibly decline slowly over that period (decreasing the effect as you get "hungrier")?

    2) Do multiple foodstuffs have stacking effects? For instance, could I click on three different foods to eat them all, and get three stacking stat bonuses, or would the second overwrite the first, third overwrite the second, etc.?

    3) Any foods planned to affect not only stat bonuses, but saves as well? Something like Polar Bear Pie (just made that up) which gives +5 SV Cold? Edible Goo gives +2 SV Poison?

    4) When will Brewers be able to make drinks that do something besides get you drunk? I'd like to be able to brew a drink that confers (albeit small) beneficial effects with no negative effects... even it meant that the drinks lasted half as long like baked foods. Brew some Green Tea and get a stamina boost, or Hot Chocolate to get SV Cold... You get the idea.

    Thanks,

    Feydakin ]

    There is a patch message on Test server that explains most of this. Let me copy the pertinent part of the patch message:

    quote:

    Baking and Food Changes:
    Now when you right click on a food or drink, it will describe how sustaining that food or drink is. *Note* The message and food values are subject to change over the next week or two as they are being balanced.

    Now you can right click on a food or drink item to consume it just like alcohol. Currently consuming food this way only provides half of the sustaining value of the food. There is also a limit as to how much food and drink you can consume at once.

    Some player prepared foods and drinks now have statistic modifications. These are once again most likely to change during the next week as they are being balanced.

    So let me answer your questions to the best of my ability. Keep in mind that all of this is still being tested. It is entirely possible that none of this will go live, or that it will end up working differently when we're done testing.

    1) Yes, you will still consume the topmost food and drink (excluding alcohol) in your inventory. You might prefer to choose to eat a food for a few reasons. Your character might be a glutton. Or, more likely, you might want to gain the benefits that can be gained from eating a certain food type right away rather than waiting for it to be eaten naturally.

    1a) I'm not sure I understand the question, so I'll shotgun some answers. Each food item will have a duration that it feed you. Automatically eating them allows them to last that given duration. Forcing them down your throat will only allow them to last half as long as they would normally. The sustenance duration of player made foods will be set at whatever value seems appropriate. I doubt that all of them will last half as long as store bought foods. In fact, I would imagine that they will in some cases last longer. Remember, not all player made food will have some special bonus to it.

    1b) I don't know how bonuses from food will be treated. I'll know more once we've fiddled with it a bit.

    2) Much like my answer to 1b), I don't really know. We'll let you know more when we've finalized more.

    3) Actually, the only food item that I've heard about so far specifically is the Scorpion Sandwich that was the first one created to test with. It gave (and might still give) a +1 to poison saves.

    4) I don't know the specific plans for brewing. Again, I'll have more details once we've more testing time.

    I'm sorry that I don't have more details on this. It's still early in testing and I don't have a lot of details for you. But I think you'll like the results. I'll get back to you with more information when we're closer to finalizing it all.

Alan Comments on Fan Faire
- Aurum @ 8:38 PM PDT
Alan kicked up a few of his thoughts and impressions taken from the recent Fan Faire event in San Diego recently. Here's the post directly from the Developer's Corner:

    As a follow up, I wanted to thank everyone that attended the Faire, both players and staff. It seems that everyone had a good time. It's always nice to meet people with similar interests. I went home with a few impressions:

    Players on PvP servers are not mean in person. :) I don't know how it is that I gained, someplace in the back of my mind, the idea that players on a Zek server would be sort of dangerous in person.     Even the old jokes are kinda funny if you're in a room with a lot of other people that can groan at them with you. For a few years now we've been hearing the same camping, spawning and SoW jokes. They actually seemed a little fresher when in a room full of 1,200 people that understood them.

    It does rain in San Diego. I know it's not my fault, but I'm sorry that it decided to rain on us all at the Faire. It doesn't seem fair…

    I just wanted to let you all know that the Live team had a lot of fun. We talked a bit about it in the meeting today, and it seems that we all had a good time. I wanted to express their thanks to you all for making it so.

    And it was nice to see people cheering for their GMs and praising their Guides. Those folks work hard to help people out, and I was glad to see that you folks were so kind to them, they deserve it.

    But Fan Faire is about the fans. So I was wondering what you all thought about the event. If you have any non-dirty tales to relate, let me know.

    Alan

Few More Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:49 PM PDT
Here's the round-up of the few updates Alan made to the Developer's Corner today:

    Plane of Mischief Items To Become No-Drop
    There is a rather extensive list of items from this plane that will become No Drop with the next patch. I'm not going to list them all, as it's probable that some of these items are unknown to many at this point, and I'm not going to spoil any surprises.

    So if you have any items that originated in the Plane of Mischief that you were thinking about trading, do so before the next patch.

    Sit On & Off
    [Quote: Subject: A Few changes

    I have only two suggestions. First, since there is a /sit command, why not a /stand command? Currently I have to type /book and then hit "i" to stand up that way.
    The second was more of a question than a suggestion. I heard rumors a while back about letting the players buy in bulk. That is to say, buy more than one of an item at a time, like full stacks if we wanted to. I don't see why this would be a problem. If it is the fact that maybe we don't have the money for it, then just let it buy as much as we have money for. Am I making sense? Anyway, I would appreciate an e-mail response if it is possible. Thank you.

    Emoth Lasundi of The Guardians of Innoruuk
    Karan Server ]

    Well, programmers are an unusual lot. When setting up the /sit function they treated it like any other function that just toggles between two states (in this case, sitting and not sitting)

    The Cat Who Walked Through Walls
    [Quote: Forgive the Heinlein quote, I couldn't resist :)

    I am a new player, so this question may be long since answered but I could not find it anywhere so thought I would ask it anyhow. Creatures can see and walk through walls. This appears to be common knowledge, something I found out the hard way.

    Quoting Heinlein titles is fine with me. A good bard needs to be aware of the great bards, after all.

    Yes, that question has been asked several times. But I'm more than willing to answer it again.

    Creatures walk through certain walls on purpose. The huts and inns you see out in the plains of Norrath, such as those out in the Commons near Freeport, don't prevent the passage of NPCs because if they did they would be easily abused. For example, Hill Giants would not be able to attack a player character through the roof of the inn, but you might be able to stab at his feet. And creatures used to get stuck against the geometry of those buildings all the time. So, while we certainly understand that it seems strange to have a bear wander through the walls of a hut, it's something that needed to be done.

For example, I've gotten the impression over the last two years that folks playing Dwarves tend to be more likely to roleplay than others. I don't know why, or if it's even true, but I've played at least a little as every race in the game, and I think it's true. Or I've noticed that it is a lot easier to be in-character when playing a good cleric than an evil cleric. As a 'good' cleric, it's easy enough to heal people when they ask. But for an 'evil' cleric this just might not be in-character (though it can be).

    It is always easiest to abandon roleplaying when it comes to the 'high stakes' stuff. The temptation is great. And most of the time it's in-character to do such things as dealing with a necro to help get your corpse, even if your character might normally never do such a thing. High stakes for the player is usually high stakes for the character as well (your character doesn't want to lose his stuff either). But there are cases where you should sacrifice what you want for what your character wants. When the two desires are not the same, roleplaying means putting the character's desires first.

    Pacifists DO make poor adventurers. There's nothing false about that statement.

    What I think you disagree with is that adventuring is a poor focus for roleplaying. I disagree.

    While the focus of EverQuest is adventuring (much like most pen and paper games), it certainly doesn't exclude the roles that you've mentioned. Explorers certainly have a place in Norrath. Explorers in a dangerous world are basically adventurers anyway. Historians, cartographers, merchants, sages… all of these are not roles on their own. If you are playing a cartographer that likes to sit around the house and read maps, or a historian that likes to read history but doesn't like to talk to others about it, then you don't really need a multiplayer game. Hermit is so much not a roleplaying idea (sure, the GM could use it, but as a player character a hermit would never, ever, be in a game… Evangelists would be something that would be hard to do in EverQuest.

EverQuest does not have any sort of automated map making system by design. But then if it did, who would need a cartographer. As for drawing your own maps in game, that is more of a technology issue than a design one (or, if you want, it was a design decision not to develop the technology to allow such a thing).

In most cases where they are limited the limitation comes from technology or time restrictions, not a desire to hinder roleplaying. The best example would be your evangelist. Despite the fact that you can't actually change the little word(s) on the character description that the player sees, you most certainly could 'convert' people. You could convince them to work for the betterment of your chosen deity. You could convince them to do things that would bring their faction in line with the factions best suited to your deity. You could convert someone to your religion if the player of that character was convinced that his character would be converted.

That an Iksar monk should be equally able to get that item if his player is roleplaying or if he's not. I don't agree.
