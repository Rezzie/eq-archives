    Design Balance
    Kione wrote:

    [Quote: I've actually found the whole idea of a Cleric/Druid/Shaman thing to be fairly interesting, and it actually shows quite a bit more depth than one might otherwise think. To preface, let me outline what races can be which classes:

    Cleric: Dark Elf, Dwarf, Erudite, Gnome, Halfling, High Elf, Human
    Druid: Half-Elf, Halfling, Human, Wood Elf
    Shaman: Barbarian, Iksar, Ogre, Troll

    So anyway, there are a few things we can infer from this, I think. We can note the following:

    1) All races have at least one sort of Wisdom caster. Some races don't have Int casters (Barbarians, Dwarves, Wood Elves, Halflings, Ogres, Trolls, Half-Elves,) but religion is esential.
    2) The Shaman class is exclusive of both Clerics and Druids (ie, no race can be both a Shaman and a Cleric or Druid.)
    3) The Druid class is compatable with the Clerics only in two races: Humans and Halflings. All the other races are one or the other.

    The way I see it, there are three religious approaches, and these approaches are not terribly compatable. We have a sort of a stylized and organized worship of a god, that's the way of the Cleric (note the sophistication of just about all the temples.) On the other side, we have a rough, primal embracing of the god and his beleifs, the Shaman. For a Druid, I think that the god is secondary to the worship of Nature itself, thus being limited to the two gods who typify Nature: the one who gives birth, or the one who nurtures life, Tunare and Karana.

    So clearly, Clerics and Shamans require a wholey different way of thinking, and such a thing will never appear in the same race. The closest we get are Humans, Erudites, and Barbarians, who are all the same species, but are very different "breeds." Should the Iksar race manage to regain its heights, then probably the Shamanistic way of thought would dissappear from them completely. Or perhaps some sect of the Iksar would cling to the old ways, at the cost of not embracing the newer way of living (and hence be a different "race," much like Humans and Barbarians or Wood Elves and High Elves.)

    On a related note, I might add that Druidic and Clerical thought are also radically different, and as such are only accessable by the most open minded and flexable of races (humans and their diminutive mirror.) ]

    That's a fair analysis. Heck, sounds like you might have pulled that from some old EverQuest design document. :)

    Shark Meat
    Breno wrote:

    [Quote: yea just want to know if VI is going to allow the selling of shark mea back into the game i have a bard that all he does is skill skill skill master baker potter tayler and the other day i found a recipie for smoked shark but when i arrived in hurugan in velious and went to morgans meat where i was told sold the suff to my suprise it had been taken out do to druids and ppl exploiting some ques that they ended up nerfing anyway so why take the shark meat away when he quest was nerfed. some ppl need the stuff for making good food for parties

    just wanted to know

    thanks ]

    The short answer is, no.

    Here's the series of events that lead up to that decision.

    Some time in the far distant past, someone created a quest that required shark meat. At that time the only source of shark meat on Norrath was living sharks with sharp teeth. The quest rewards were balanced based on the facts of the day.

    Recently we added a bunch of new recipes for bakers. Shark meat was used in one (or some) of those recipes. So shark meat was added on a vendor or two to facilitate the use of that recipe.

    This made the original quest extremely easy for people that could transport themselves. People used this fact to gain rewards beyond the intended difficulty of the quest.

    Sure, we should have caught it. But with the huge number of quests and the large increase in baking recipes and items, we missed it. To fix this we had, really, two choices. Remove the quest or remove the meat from vendors.

    We chose to remove the meat. Bakers don't suffer in their ability to make skill, there are other recipes that will get them through those skill ranges. All this does is make shark dishes a rarity, and that's actually something that I think is a good thing. If someone gives you food with shark meat in it, you know that they've gone to some trouble to make that food.


    Pet Questions
    pyrian wrote:

    [Quote: Greetings,

    I wasn't sure if this was ever brought up, but I just wanted to address it anyway in case it wasn't. I've just recently started a necro character a couple of months ago who is already level 32. As of now, my character has reached the level in which a pet is essential for survival. This means that if I wish to survive an encounter, my pet has to be able to survive the fight. Likewise, to have my pet to survive, it must be able to effectively deal with any mob that it encounters. And to do that, the pet usually has to be armed.

    So my question is this, are there any plans to have necro pets return the weapons it was given when you no longer need the pet? Also, are there any plans to let necro retrieve the weapons given to their pets if their pets die? I think that it would be a great addition, espeically since the necro's primary mode of defense and offense are their pets. Please let us know what you think. Thanks. ]

    Arming your pet is part of the bargain for using them. Losing that weapon is part of the cost. In a way, this is another one of the slight and perhaps subtle advantages that magicians have over other pet classes, as they can summon weapons for their pets.

    No, we have no intention of allowing pets to return weapons.

    Silfox wrote:

    [Quote: JanePetOwner casts Invisibility on herself, and Jobober panics and goes poof. Shouldn't it be possible for the caster to cast See Invisible on the pet, then invis herself without the pet suiciding? ]

    The pet goes away when the owner disappears for game balance reasons as much as for any roleplaying reason. Unfortunately an invisible pet owner could, basically, send an endless chain of pets at an NPC and never be at risk themselves. I mentioned this to Kendrick and he remembered a time in beta when a 15th level character was able to use this trick to easily take on a 28th level NPC with no risk.

    So, yes, it would make sense that if the pet is freaking out and disappearing when the master disappears, then casting See Invisible on the pet would prevent that. But we can't allow pets to be attached to invisible masters for reasons having nothing to do with 'realism' and everything to do with gameplay.

    Hello everyone. I've got some news for you that I'm sure you'll like.

    We plan to have the new PvP Deity Teams server up and running some time tomorrow. I also have the name of the new server and the basic rule set for you below.

    What we'll be doing is putting the new server up as a Test server for a short while, a few weeks or it may only be a few days, depending on how things go.     Please use /bug to tell us about any bugs you find with the new rules and PvP code.

    Remember, this is a Test server, and there is no promise of customer service.

    Don't let any of that intimidate you if you want to test out the new rules once we get the server up. Just run testeverquest.exe and start up a character.

    Anyway, on to the rules. This isn't necessarily a final list, we are still thinking of adding and modifying some rules. But this is a pretty good summary.

    ----

    Welcome to Sullon Zek, the first Deity Team PvP server.

    Important Notice:

    This server will only be featured on the Test EQ Patch for some time for testing purposes. You will need to run testeverquest.exe to access this server. While this server is featured on the Test Patch, all the conditions that apply to the Test Server also apply to this server. There may be cases where the server comes down multiple times a day for updates. There is also a small chance of corpse or item loss. There will be very little to no initial customer service support. When testing of this server is complete and the server is ready to be introduced to the Live Servers, all characters will be wiped. Below are the new rules specific to this server. But also be aware that these rules may be changed as we feel the need to adjust them.


    The Sullon Zek server is a new server with a new rule set. The rules for this server are very different than those on any other EverQuest server. In general the server is based on the concept of a PvP teams server, like Tallon and Vallon Zek, but with the teams chosen based on which god the character worships rather than the race of the character.

    This document includes the PvP rules for Sullon Zek, and a list of the teams for the server. The 'teams' will be referred to as 'alliances' in this document, as they represent an alliance of the gods, rather than some sort of sports team…


    Sullon Zek rules:

    1. Player v. Player (PvP) combat can occur between any characters that are not on the same team, regardless of level. A level 50 character can attack and kill a level 6 character if that character is not a member of his alliance. But rules 2 and 3 apply.

    2. Characters will be immune to PvP combat until they have gained enough strength to survive the struggle. Characters below 6th level are not able to participate in PvP combat. The first time a character zones after gaining 6th level he or she will then become PvP enabled.

    3. Characters will lose experience when killed in PvP combat if the character that killed them is within 5 levels. The amount of experience lost will be the same amount that is lost if that character is killed by an NPC. Your corpse can be resurrected by a cleric to partially restore lost experience.

    4. Characters will only be able to loot coins from other characters, and then only if the victim was within 5 levels of the killer. Only the character getting the killing blow will be able to loot the corpse.

    5. Players may only have one character at a time on this server. This means that you will have to delete the existing character in order to create a new one.     10. /consider returns only three kinds of results when used against player characters. 'Green' means that the character is below your range. 'White' or 'black' means that the character is within your range. 'Red' means that they are above your range. 'In range' means +/- 5 levels.

    11. /consider will also display a message indicating what team the target is on. Members of your own team will be displayed as an 'ally', members of another team will be shown as 'threateningly'.

Paineel and Erudin are both starting cities for erudites, and killing one in either city (regardless of their place of origin) will result in a faction loss with that city. Other cities are not considered home towns, despite being run and controlled by a certain race. For example, HighHold is not a human home town. Thurgadin is not a dwarven home town.

Causing experience loss by 'training' NPCs on other characters is not an offense that would warrant a warning on PvP servers. HOWEVER: All rules regarding general decency still apply. Cursing, threats and other such things will be dealt with sternly, and are still unacceptable behavior on PvP servers.

    14. Beneficial effects can not be used on members of another team.

    15. No character will be able to bind in dungeon zones.

    16. Beneficial spells will not affect characters more than 20 levels lower than the spell level. Existing spell restrictions on spells over 50th level will supercede this rule.

    17. There will be 'insignia' dropped on the corpses of characters over 20th level that are killed by another character. We plan to allow those to be turned in for a reward of some sort. What that reward will be is undetermined at this time, but we are leaning towards a team reward rather than a personal reward.
