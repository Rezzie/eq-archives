Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:42 PM PDT
Gordon kept busy this Sunday by making the following posts on the Gameplay Board:

  • Coffee and Milo
    [Quote from original post: What coffee do you drink that hops you up so late at night]

    An espresso roast that I order from Seattle. I'm crazy about my coffee, and I've got a pretty nifty setup to make it up :)

    [Quote from original post: Just find it interesting that you are up again another weekend making posts]

    I actually find it a lot easier to be more personable at night away from the office. I guess it's the difference that comes with doing it on my own time by choice rather than as part of my job, even though I'm still doing the same thing. Am I making sense?

    [Quote from original post: Any comments on Milo :/ Really curious, love his column as many others do]

    We had to come to grips with the fact that anyone who is known to be working here represents us in public, especially when writing for an EverQuest-related site read by EverQuest customers. Though I am very aware that many people enjoyed the column, there was indeed content that wasn't appropriate from a representative of the company.


  • Gone Campin'
    [Quote from original post: Too Crowded? I came from half way across the whole game planet just to get to a zone w 7(SEVEN) people in the zone, including me! SO i call ou 46 shaman looking for el groupo!]

    If the zone had seven people in it including you, chances are the other 6 were in a group together already. I know that you said later in your post that the other group had 5 members, leaving one solo person. In Lower Guk?

    [Quote from original post: You tell me why anyone should leave a single spawn camp to just get slaughtered by greens waiting to get picked up in a group?]

    Because it's more fun than camping a single spawn?

    At 46 I doubt that there is much green to you, and I don't think that there's anything 15 levels below you. I could be wrong about that.

    [Quote from original post: while i stand invised at the enterence, what happens, someone shouts Train to zone look out, so i run down and to the left out of the way of the train. and stand by till things get cleared up, but no, a wanderer comes by drags the whole freeking train on me, and i loose 46 in less than 6 seconds]

    Why did you run from the entrance, deeper into the zone, when someone yelled "train"? You said there were only 7 people in the zone, which is going to make Lower Guk a pretty dangerous place.

    [Quote from original post: Is 2 hours, or more at higher lvls, of time wasted due to this meaningless crap acceptable "fun" for you? It sure the hell is not for me!]

    Come on Robert. You obviously made some poor decisions here. Why do you come to the boards and blame it on us? The game is supposed to punish you for making mistakes, most games do. You ran solo into a pretty rough dungeon when there were barely any people in there and died. The game worked like it was supposed to.

    [Quote from original post: The game you have created at higher lvls is more about NOT DYING, than it is about playing the game and adventuring!]

    That's a decision that you as a gamer make. Why does it have to be about not dying? Why is advancement in level through the boring process of camping a single spawn for experience preferrable to having fun, and not advancing as fast?

    [Quote from original post: You say alot that if there is no risk then it is not exciting, but my point is, when everything involves huge risk of time lost]

    This is the second time you've mentioned "lost time" or "wasted time" in the post. The entire concept of "lost time" suggests that there's an end to the game, achieved by advancement. I'm sure that's true for some players. Some feel that they have to reach level 50, or 60 and then they can "finally go out and have fun". Any thing that slows them down is costing them "time", or "lost time" in this case.

    I'm not sure what to tell gamers of this type. I'm not sure that we could ever make advancement fast enough to their liking, but I am sure that we don't want to speed advancement. If all you play for is to advance in level as fast as possible then deaths will equate directly to wasted or lost time. Perhaps if leveling is more important to you than anything else, you should take the fastest and safest route to advancement, which will be camping a single spawn someplace.

    However, if leveling is more important to you than anything else, and you choose to do whatever you have to to level as fast as possible, is it our fault that this is boring? Should not the quickest and safest route to leveling be boring?

    I've said before that risk was necessary in order for there to be a challenge, and a challenge necessary in order for there to be fun. Excitement is one form of fun, and most would agree that boredom is roughly the opposite of excitement. If a gamer makes a choice to remove risk from an encounter, should they not be responsible from removing the fun from that encounter as well?


  • EULA
    [Quote from original post: Just to let you know, you shoulda researched when you did that, it is starting to violate Federal Interstate Wire Fruad laws.]

    The EULA doesn't violate any laws, it just defines that you are allowed to do with our software and your access to the service.

    I certainly agree that if player A sells the account to player B, and later steals it back, then player A has committed a crime, but that is between player A and player B, and really has nothing to do with us.

    The extent that we get involved is to ban the account if player B calls us up and proves that he bought it from player A. We do not however compensate player B in any way, since he acquired access to the account through a violation of the user agreement by player A. It is then player B's option to file legal charges if they wish, but that doesn't involve us.

    See, this is why we don't allow account transers, because we can't be responsible for all the fraud that goes on. Someone might look reputable on those auction sites, but takes only a couple of days and costs a couple of dollars to work up a convincing profile. There is more fraud involved in account and item sales than you can possibly imagine. I wish I could tell you how many calls we get every day about this.


  • Sharing Accounts
    There's no problem if that person is your child, however if it's anyone else, it's not permitted by the EULA.


  • A Bug?
    [Quote from original post: I play on the tribunal and informed a guide about the presence of this bug and was told they are investigating the problem. The bug is as follows, Normally i as a cleric could cast invis vs undead on myself and them have REGULAr invis cast upon and they would stack just fine. Since the last patch the msg "You are allready invisible" is given when attempting this. Could you please give an update so we know if this bug has even made it too the right people and when it might be completed. Thank You Much]

    It's not a bug. No class (except for rogues) can be invisible to both living and undead regardless of spells or abilities. So even though the spells would appear to stack, the recipient of the spell would only be under the affect of one spell.

    A few weeks ago, I asked the coder to make the spells unstackable so that they didn't give the impression that being invisible to both is possible.


  • Tinnkering
    [Quote from original post: Ther have been countless upon countless threads started to get some idea of when Tinkering will gets its upgrade. We have been nice, cordial, and patient. We have entire websites that are for the explicit purpose of furthering our skills, ( http://www.eqportal.com/tinkering/ ), and yet we are ignored on a continual, repetative basis. PLEASE have mercy and give us an idea of when our skill will be addressed! =( =( =( =(]

    All I can tell you is the same thing that I've told all of the other trade-skill types, that being that several of them are being worked on currently. I wish I could solidify ETA's and project lists, but doing so wouldn't be appropriate given the sometimes volatile nature of the designer's workload.

    We will be reopening the tradeskill board next week. The designer responsible for them will be a regular reader/poster there.


  • One-Way Dungeon Doors
    Considering that the classes that can gate are probably grouped with those who can't, I'd have to argue that the one-way dungeons affect everyone. Who's going to group with a gating class if they bail out at the first sign of trouble leaving their non-gating brethren to take the fall? Also, I can't say that I agree that a dungeon design of that nature constitutes a penalty.

    There are of course dungeons which don't allow people to gate out already, but there's really no point in expanding that to dungeons that already allow it by design. That would be a penalty.

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:02 PM PDT
Gordon made a few posts today on the Gameplay Board. For your information here they are:

  • AoE Spells
    [Quote from original post: Long promised, never delivered, non-class issue. Well Gordon? When are pre-50 AoE spells going to be fixed?]

    What promise? I don't recall anyone promising anything in regards to these spells.

    Furthermore, how are these spells "broken" in your opinion? I've seen a number of posts complaining about damage ratios, or regarding the number of creatures that they hit, or the types of creatures that are affected. It's all working as intended as far as I can tell.

    Can you elaborate on "broken" and include what spells you are speaking of? Please be as brief as possible while explaining the situation.


  • Summaries of Weekly Discussion
    [Quote from original post: A (the) conclusion(s) that Verant has come to in regards to the topic based on the discussion?]

    If whatever we are discussing has a solid conclusion, sure, but I think that most of what we'll discuss will be a little more intellectual and a little less tangible in nature.

    As far as camping goes, about all we determined is that people differ widely in opinion on the issue. One of the most common suggestions is that we work actively to promote more rewards for playing the game in the preferred manner, and leave rewards as they currently exist for camping.

    Unfortunately, doing that would require making playing the game in the preferred manner easier, safer, faster, or more profitable, which would in our opinion decrease the fun to be had from it. In our opinion, there is more fun to be had from playing the game in the preferred manner than by camping, and that fun should be the sufficient additional reward to get people to do it.

    However it's obviously not, so we're left with reducing the reward for camping as an option. Of course, many made it clear in the threads they they prefer camping over doing anything else in the game. Hence our quandry.

    I think that at this point we can continue trying new ideas going forward, but try to avoid making sweeping changes to the way existing things work. We'll of course have individual exceptions to the philosophy in both directions, but on an as-needed basis. That said, there were some very good ideas included and good insight offered within the thread. Even if those ideas aren't something that would work in EQ, they are still something that we can keep in mind for future products.


  • "Lets make that 10 slot bag 6 slots and dump the 4 items in the deleted slots!"
    I'm not at all familiar with this change; I'll look into it Monday. That said, if the item was a combinable item used for a Quest, people are not supposed to store items in combinable containers.
    --
    Just a follow-up with this... One of the guys is still in the office, and tells me that our records indicate that the last time this item was modified was in February. When is the change supposed to have taken place?


  • New Dragons?
    Dragons and deities are kind of a rite of passage in EverQuest. They are a goal that people aim for when playing the game. We wouldn't want to cheapen the experience by giving every level range their "own" dragon.

  • An Explanation
    [Quote from original post: So agian I was just wondering what is the different between the song and this.]

    The difference is that the NPC is moving. It is carrying out its commands and making progress towards that goal. Movement introduces a variable that injects some risk into the situation. Now where these particular people were fear-kiting, perhaps there is a pathing issue that reduces the risk, but that's something associated with a specific point in the world, not an ability that can be taken anywhere.

    The "Fear" spell tells the NPC to flee. It's the same "flee" that they do when they run low on health and try to get away. They can't really *get* anywhere, so the only reason they flee is to inject additional risk.

    An NPC that is fleeing, and is then rooted, will turn around and fight. An NPC that is fleeing, and then snared, will not turn around and fight, even if their movement rate is reduced to zero (an effective root). "Effective Roots" don't have the drawbacks of a root (e.g. fighting NPC, unpredictable breaking of the spell).

    Our choices in this case were: Make it so all NPCs will fight back if their movement speed is reduced to a certain point, or, limit the ability of snare spells to reduce their movement rate to zero. We did the latter. I'm sure that some could make a case for the former based upon situation.

  • Spell Slots
    [Quote from original post: There are several Ideas that I have seen that EVERYONE wants, but you all refuse to do it...WHY? Such as more than 8 spell slots, everyone wants more, 10 would be good, But you all refuse to do it? WHY??]

    I can only address specific issues rather than something as broad as what "everyone wants". In regards to this, the 8 spell limit is one of balance. Spells are organized in many cases to provide an excess of spells that people would want memmed at all times. For that reason, they must decide what spells to mem given the limited pool. This provides tradeoffs and presents unique challenges in game.

    On the broader issue of what "everyone wants", sometimes what people want is in conflict with other things that those same people want. People want to be entertained, and some suggestions, though being pleasing in the short term, would harm the "want" of long term entertainment.

    To present a real-world analogy, I'm sure that everyone would like the government to give them a million dollars. However, were the government to do such a thing, rampant inflation would consume country leading to a lesser standard of living than what we have now.

New EQClient.ini Tip
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 11:21 AM PDT
Another tip for the EQClient.ini file was sent in by Ungwe:

Greetings,

After several inquiries on the ability to customize our colors for actions, chat, ect... I decided to do a little experimentation with the following discovery. If you will open your Paint program, which most everyone or their children have played with in Windows, click on the Colors button in the tool bar. Than click on Edit Colors; a small arrangement of colored squares appear with another tab called Define Custom Colors. Click on that and you now have the color squares and a color pallet. Under the color pallet is six areas with numeric values in them. Notice the right column of numbers. These are marked as; Red, Green, & Blue. If you click on the colored squares the numeric values in the red, green & blue boxes change; ranging from 0 to 255.

Thus the discovery of using the numbers the Paint program gives you to change the values in your eqclient.ini file to your liking. You can of course further the customization of the color by moving the target around in the pallet or the arrow up & down on the slide bar to the right of the pallet.

Sure hope this helps someone.

Ungwe Lonewalker - Eci'
Styyx Myythos - Rodcet Nife
