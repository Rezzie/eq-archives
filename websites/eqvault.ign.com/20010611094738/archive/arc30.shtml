Screen Freezing
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:10 PM PST
Abashi stopped by the Gameplay Board today to make the following announcement:

    Some people have been reporting that their screen freezes every so often while in game, causing all sorts of mischief. While we are looking into that problem, and do have some changes planned for the next patch that should reduce its occurance, there are some things that you can do first to see if it improves:

    1. Virus scan software: These problems usually run in a systray (down by the clock). By default, they typically are set to scan files every time they are accessed. This could be causing some of those problems, as EverQuest accesses several local files at every character save. Try disabling it to see if your situation improves.

    2. IRQs. PCI devices are designed so that they can share IRQs. That works well in principle, but can cause problems when several high bus-bandwidth devices are on the same IRQ. Typically we've seen this when sound and video cards are on the same IRQ. You can try disabling your sound by placing a file called "NOSOUND.TXT" (file contents do not matter) in your EverQuest directory. If your freezing goes away, there is a good chance it's a shared IRQ problem. The method by which to reassign IRQs varies from system to system, but can be done by either moving the sound card to a different slot, manually assigning IRQs in the BIOS, or manually assigning them in the Device Manager. You probably shouldn't attempt any of these without the assistance of your local computer guru, unless of course you are the local guru.

    As mentioned above, we are putting in some changes in next week's patch that should help the situation for those that aren't affected by #1 or #2, but you'll want to make sure that the problems above are not the cause of your problems before counting on the patch to fix it.

    -Gordon

    -Gordon

    A good deal of reengineering was done prior to Kunark's launch to create the 59th necro summon that dual-wields without weapons. Additional code was put in as part of the last patch to further differentiate between pets and other NPCs for the proc changes.

    Given these changes I figured that it would be a good time to address a minor annoyance.

    -Gordon


More on Pets
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:41 PM PST
Pets continue to be a dominate issue on the boards. Here's the first post in a brand new thread created by Gordon:

    I would have posted this in the other thread but figured that it was different enough to deserve its own thread for discussion:

    I was just sitting here thinking over the latest issue with pet procing, and the sword of runes, and I noticed something.

    A number of the complaints in this thread cite changing something that's been that way since release, or saying that if we are going to nerf something, why don't we fix something before we nerf something else.

    In truth, we did. Mages only got the ability to hand weapons to their pets 10 months ago (January 28th to be precise). The problem with the procs going off on everything and going off so often only became an issue then. Sure, there was the odd necro who had a magician friend who would summon up weapons, but it certainly wasn't wide-spread enough to make a global difference.

    Your pets are more powerful now than they were at release, even given the latest change. They dual wield now. Sure, that was a bug workaround, but the pets were bumped up in power several times before the dual wield workaround because they weren't beating necro pets. In many ways, they became overpowered when they suddenly began dual wielding, an imbalance that wasn't addressed until now.

    So, as you see, we did address a problem that resulted in increasing the power of the pet prior to "nerfing" a small portion of it. Now I do wish that there had been less time between cause and effect, but it's too late for that at this point. The point is that this is not an ability that has been around forever. While I agree that many people might have come to take it for granted, mage pets are still stronger than they were for nearly a year after release.

    -Gordon

    In all truth, if it *really were* that clear-cut, it is not likely that the GM would have made the wrong decision.

    In any case, responding to these issues in cases where the true happenings is anything less than black or white isn't fruitful. We only have one side of a three-sided story posted in most cases, and one of those sides is not likely to come to the board and post theirs. So there's really no point in arguing it.

    Reports of GM misconduct are forwarded to the customer service management team. In cases where the true story cannot be determined with any certainty, a note is made for the GMs file. Misconduct, when it occurs, is hardly isolated to a single instance, and if a GM shows a pattern it can them be necessary that we go further.

    The same is true for players. It's very easy for someone to be falsely accused once, being falsely accused of the same thing in a half a dozen different instances is another thing entirely.

    Anyway, this board is not the proper place to voice complaints about GMs. It tends to reduce the credability of the complaint as it's being made in a public forum for the purpose of garnering public support. If you wish to report something to the CS team concerning GM misconduct, feel free to email gscotto@station.sony.com directly.

  • Contiually Making EQ Harder
    [Quote from original post: Are we too good? IE: why must the game be constantly made more difficult!]

    Well first, you are good. Players have consistantly discovered ways to do things that amaze us to this day. It's therefore essential that we keep raising the bar to provide challenge to those that have figured out how to overcome previous challenges. Obviously, we don't intend to make unbeatable encounters, though we could rarely do that when we tried, but we still need to provide a challenge.

    There's actually two issues here. One, making new content more difficult, which I think should be expected by everyone, as what would be the point if things did not get more difficult as you progressed.

    The other issue is making old content more difficult by reducing the effectivness of tactics that allow a challenge to be overcome without risk. This is a different issue entirely, as some people apparently don't expect this. Here's the short of our intentions: You play the game to have fun. You have fun by overcoming challenges. A challenge is something that has the prospect of failure. Failure must have consequences to be meaningful. Overcoming the challenge without failure yields a reward, whether tangible or ideological. Basically, discovering a way to do something without risk (or with less risk than intended) negates the challenge and creates a situation that we must address in order for the game to be fun.
