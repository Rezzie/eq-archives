  • Experience Ressurections
    There's really no way to determine with any certainty that something of this nature occurred. More Pet Changes
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:45 PM PST
Speaking of pets, Alan added this announcement to the Gameplay Board tonight:

    A while back magician pets were given the ability to wield weapons. This was a workaround for the fact that these pets were unable to duel-wield properly.

    After the next patch, pets summoned using necromancer or magician spells of 39th level or higher should duel wield, even without weapons. This will also include the shadownight pets that are the same as necromancer pets of 39th level or better. So these pets will be duel wielding properly henceforth.

    Alan
Here's a follow-up from Gordon
    Just one clarification, the "auto dual wield" ability is based upon a calculation of both the dual wield skill and the level of the pet. So, it is possible that you could get good summon at 34th that auto dual wields without weapons, or a bad summon at 39th that doesn't.

    As a note, no change is being made to their likelihood to dual wield. The change is only going to remove the requirement of handing your pet weapons to get them to dual wield at the higher levels.

    -Gordon
One last post from Gordon:
    We were told this was impossible on Jan 28. We were told that this would be impossible to code around.

    Well, it was impossible on January 28th, as the game engine didn't distinguish between pets and other NPCs.

    Please explain what changed.

    A good deal of reengineering was done prior to Kunark's launch to create the 59th necro summon that dual-wields without weapons. Additional code was put in as part of the last patch to further differentiate between pets and other NPCs for the proc changes.

    Given these changes I figured that it would be a good time to address a minor annoyance.

    -Gordon


More on Pets
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:41 PM PST
Pets continue to be a dominate issue on the boards. Here's the first post in a brand new thread created by Gordon:

    I would have posted this in the other thread but figured that it was different enough to deserve its own thread for discussion:

    I was just sitting here thinking over the latest issue with pet procing, and the sword of runes, and I noticed something.

    A number of the complaints in this thread cite changing something that's been that way since release, or saying that if we are going to nerf something, why don't we fix something before we nerf something else.

    In truth, we did. Mages only got the ability to hand weapons to their pets 10 months ago (January 28th to be precise). The problem with the procs going off on everything and going off so often only became an issue then. Sure, there was the odd necro who had a magician friend who would summon up weapons, but it certainly wasn't wide-spread enough to make a global difference.

    Your pets are more powerful now than they were at release, even given the latest change. They dual wield now. Sure, that was a bug workaround, but the pets were bumped up in power several times before the dual wield workaround because they weren't beating necro pets. In many ways, they became overpowered when they suddenly began dual wielding, an imbalance that wasn't addressed until now.

    So, as you see, we did address a problem that resulted in increasing the power of the pet prior to "nerfing" a small portion of it. Now I do wish that there had been less time between cause and effect, but it's too late for that at this point. The point is that this is not an ability that has been around forever. While I agree that many people might have come to take it for granted, mage pets are still stronger than they were for nearly a year after release.

    -Gordon


Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:39 PM PST
Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • More Pet Changes?
    [Quote from original post: OK - Am I the first to notice that pets are only doing about 2/3 their regular damage in a duel? There is no reason whatsoever for my necro 49 pet to be doing a low 30s in combat with anybody, let alone a PC.]

    This change was put in around 15 months ago.

  • Venril Sathir
    First, there are approximately 52 weeks in a year.

    Regarding the number of guilds capable of getting the item: From item drop calculations, the number of guilds isn't part of the equation. It's the number of people that counts.

    Finally, no one is entitled to the items. We don't expect that everyone will get them. The specific intent is that they not be acquirable by everyone. Items that are meant to be gotten by everyone are far more common in game. The rarity is purposeful.

  • Nerfs
    Very well-explained lordiezu, I probably couldn't do a better job of explaining it if I tried, so I'll leave this thread with your response.

  • Stop Changing Things!
    [Quote from original post: Please Verant, stop changing things that have been that way for a long time!]

    I've actually seen a number of requests along these lines, but at the same time, a number of complaints about issues that have "been that way for a long time".

    Is there a middle ground? I don't know. Obviously many would prefer that we only address the complaints and leave the things that no one complains about alone, but at the same time, that couldn't be done while maintaining balance. It's a bit of a catch-22.
    --
    Oh, as for the rest of the post, I really don't see a correlation between your ability to roleplay your class and the latest changes.

  • Damage Increasing Items
    Right. Clarity, mod rods, manaconversion spells or items, and other things along that line serve that purpose.

    Haste sashes for melee give an indirect increase to damage. You swing more, and as such you hit more per period of time and thus do more damage. What the original author suggests is something that directly raises the amount of damage done, but that would be more comparable to new weapons for melee, or new spells for casters.

  • Mage Epic Weapon Quest
    I somehow doubt it will remain "unsolved" because of these two items. I believe both have been in game for over a year at this point, and it's reasonable to assume that at some point a mage has gotten both items.

    Right now the bottleneck appears to be the other two items.

  • GM Abuse Stories
    [Quote from original post: Why do Absor and Abishi never respond to these GM abuse stories?]

    Well "never" is a bit of an exaggeration, however, hardly anything is ever as "black and white" as it's made to seem in the "GM Abuse" threads. Most of them consist of some player interaction issue where the GM was summoned mid-incident and required to make a decision. The person who the decision went against usually ends up on the board and posts a very clear-cut story explaining why the GM was wrong.

    In all truth, if it *really were* that clear-cut, it is not likely that the GM would have made the wrong decision.

    In any case, responding to these issues in cases where the true happenings is anything less than black or white isn't fruitful. We only have one side of a three-sided story posted in most cases, and one of those sides is not likely to come to the board and post theirs. So there's really no point in arguing it.

    Reports of GM misconduct are forwarded to the customer service management team. In cases where the true story cannot be determined with any certainty, a note is made for the GMs file. Misconduct, when it occurs, is hardly isolated to a single instance, and if a GM shows a pattern it can them be necessary that we go further.

    The same is true for players. It's very easy for someone to be falsely accused once, being falsely accused of the same thing in a half a dozen different instances is another thing entirely.

    Anyway, this board is not the proper place to voice complaints about GMs. It tends to reduce the credability of the complaint as it's being made in a public forum for the purpose of garnering public support. If you wish to report something to the CS team concerning GM misconduct, feel free to email gscotto@station.sony.com directly.

  • Contiually Making EQ Harder
    [Quote from original post: Are we too good? IE: why must the game be constantly made more difficult!]

    Well first, you are good. Players have consistantly discovered ways to do things that amaze us to this day. It's therefore essential that we keep raising the bar to provide challenge to those that have figured out how to overcome previous challenges. Obviously, we don't intend to make unbeatable encounters, though we could rarely do that when we tried, but we still need to provide a challenge.

    There's actually two issues here. One, making new content more difficult, which I think should be expected by everyone, as what would be the point if things did not get more difficult as you progressed.

    The other issue is making old content more difficult by reducing the effectivness of tactics that allow a challenge to be overcome without risk. This is a different issue entirely, as some people apparently don't expect this. Here's the short of our intentions: You play the game to have fun. You have fun by overcoming challenges. A challenge is something that has the prospect of failure. Failure must have consequences to be meaningful. Overcoming the challenge without failure yields a reward, whether tangible or ideological. Basically, discovering a way to do something without risk (or with less risk than intended) negates the challenge and creates a situation that we must address in order for the game to be fun.

  • Enchanters and Illusions
    [Quote from original post: Absor your comments please on any of the above." ]

    Well, here are my thoughts on this:

    [Quote from original post: 1) It might be a false entry in the sp.dat file, however, Necro's get to become Spectres with a new SoV spell at level 60. Regardless of Level, Enchanter's asked for this spell and we're LIED to be Abashi. Were we could not add more things to the global memory before, we have more room now. You were not lied to. I'm not going to comment on any Scars of Velious spells, real or imagined.

    [Quote from original post: 2) WHY would you add a "attack power" stat buff to any of our Self Illusions. Abashi STATED numerous times that Enchanter's are not meant to melee and yet they give us Illusion Spirt Wolf (which should have a SoW effect) but instead only gives us Attack Power? Same thing with Illusion Werewolf. " ]

After all, an illusion is just a change of perceptions, not a manifestation of anything real. So, in that way, you're right, I don't think there should be a stat buff on any of the illusions at all.

    But, with the EverQuest definition of Illusion, the spell can incorporate the other powers of the Enchanter, as long as it follows the look and feel of the illusion. There must be some link between the power of the enchanter and the form of the illusion. Enchanters don't have the power to increase foot speed, so there is no way they could move as fast as a wolf with that spell. Besides, keep in mind that we don't want enchanters to cast Spirit of the Wolf. So a strength buff makes some sense, considering the form involved.

    Should there be post 44 illusions? Maybe. I can't see why not. And I'll keep it in mind to ask someone about it.

  • Barbarian Bards?
    There are a lot of class/race combinations that would make sense in EverQuest. But at some point we had to draw a line, so that there would be some race and class diversity.

    Sure, Barbarian bards make sense. So do Dwarven bards (Dwarves are an old race, very interested in their own lore), or Gnomish shadowknights… this could go on until all races had every class. There just has to be a line somewhere…

Recent Pet Changes
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:33 PM PST
Alan made an important announcement regarding pets tonight on the Gameplay Board. Here's the complete post:

    Alright everyone, here's what happened.

    As all weapon-wielding pet users probably knew, up until recently pets would proc weapon effects much more frequently than player characters. We recently changed this so that they now proc effects with just about the same frequency as player characters with the same dexterity. The exception is targeted (meant for only one type of enemy, such as summoned creatures) effects, which will now proc about half as often as they do for player characters, but will still proc on anything (this, as you know, is a bug).

    Shortly we will be fixing this bug so that pets with targeted proc items will only proc on the appropriate target. At that time, we will bump up the proc rate to match those of any other weapon proc for the pet.

    This only effects weapon procs, not innate procs.

    No pet attributes were changed.

    All pets currently have the same dexterity. It is possible in the future that we will modify the dexterity of pets based on level and type to make them more flexible for their masters.

    I'm sorry that we didn't mention this change earlier.

    Alan

    Because the proc rate on PCs is appropriate given that PCs can seek to buff up their own dexterity. Pets all have pretty low dexterity, which we'll probably scale up in the near future given this change.

    A pet with maxed out dexterity will proc nearly as much as it used to.

    Didnt Abashi PROMISE he would post ANY and ALL nerfs in the server patch message????

    Indeed, of course mistakes do happen and things fall out of the loop. Given the difference in old procs versus new ones, it would be unreasonable to assume that we were trying to slip this one in, don't you think? In any case, the namecalling you engaged in in the latter part of your message wasn't called for. Please don't engage in it again.

    -Gordon

    Of course, who's to say that there won't be uber-uber items at some point. This was weapons. Who's to say that we can't have uber helms, uber arms, etc?

    [Quote from original post: It's amazing what's possible if you don't reduce everything to phat lewt. ]

    Of course that's not to say that Velious doesn't have phat lewt. I also think people will be amazed at what is available to them in regards to customizing their appearance.

  • Magician Issues
    It looks like half the threads on the first page are about mage issues of one kind or another. Let me see if I can clear up a few of the rumors going around, and please try to keep the number of threads down.

    Though it might seem that this many threads are necessary to properly express the feelings that some are having, all that will happen is that we'll have to start closing threads.

    Call of the Hero: No intentional change was made to the way that this works. However, there were some changes made to the way intra-zone teleports are handled (to actually make them work better), so it is possible that it might have had an unintended effect on Call of the Hero.

    Pets: No changes were made to pet stats. The only change that was made in regard to pets is that they now proc all-target procs as if they were a player character. They proc special-target procs half as often as if they were a player. The next patch will make it so that special-target procs are proced just as often as the others, but will only work on the intended target type.

    This change does NOT affect what we call a "contact innate" (e.g. A spell that procs directly off of an NPC such as the necro lifetap procs on 44th and 49th) or "spell innate" (casted spells such as that done by mage pets).

    We will MOST LIKELY be scaling the dexterity of pets with their level so that the higher level ones will proc carried weapons more often, nearly as much so as before. We will also look into creating new summoned special-target proc weapons for mages.

    All of the other things that we should have fixed instead: Every time we make a change considered negative to a particular class, the boards are filled with complaints that we should have fixed something else instead of spending time on this. While I appreciate everyone's thoughts, we're not going to exclusively stick to making changes that everyone will like at the expense of the overall detrement to the game. To use an analogy (which you all know I like to use), complaining that the officer should be out catching real criminals instead of writing you up for speeding is not going to make him say, "You're right", and stop writing your ticket. There are things that need to be done. Things that upset the balance of the game (typically by making it too easy thus trivializing aspects of it by removing the challenge) will get priority over other things that don't have this type of impact. It's the way it works and the way we need to do things to keep the game interesting.

  • Patch problems
    [Quote from original post: Why, by the way, has this problem suddenly erupted with the patch? I was running DirectX 7.0 in combination with EQ fine before the patch.]

    It's not only the version of DX running on the client machine, but the version that the client's code was compiled under. Previously, the EQ client was compiled under something in the 5.x range. This earlier version of DX was far less system-friendly than the current version, and didn't like to give up much processing time to other applications. The new version of directx allows the operating system to handle the majority of that.

    As for you still having a problem, if you could post your DXDIAG information, I'll be happy to have the tech guys take a look.

  • Faction Hit For No Reason
    They don't happen for "no reason". What happens is that somehow you ended up on the hatelist of the NPC before it died. This can happen by aiding someone on the hate list or by ending up on the hatelist of another NPC within range of the NPC in question. For instance, if an NPC casts an AoE spell that catches you just because you happen to be standing there, you are then on the hatelist of the NPC who cast the spell.

  • Logic Problem
    I read it, and I was going to reply, but I'll do it here.

    With a game like EverQuest, where every item that you see needs to be an actual 3D model (unlike other games where items are essentially flat sprites), we have to keep the number of models as small as possible. Each time you enter a zone the game loads up these models into your video memory. There is only so much memory there for us to use, especially since we have to stick to the system requirements for the game as it was released (at least for basic EverQuest…). It was decided early on that some items (cloaks, shoulder pads, etc.) just couldn't be displayed. Cloth items, it was decided, wouldn't be displayed either, also for reasons of these very same restrictions.

    We don't like these restrictions any more than you do. But we just can't display everything.

    Fortunately, we have pushed the technology a bit, and with Velious we've included more textures. Hopefully in the future we can push your machine even further…

Absor Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:54 PM PST
Alan VanCouvering, Verant's Community Relations Manager, stopped by the Gameplay Board and posted the following there:

  • Outstanding Issues After Last Patch
    MipMapping: As another poster suggested, you may need to fiddle with your settings. Now that things are working better, some settings (especially the gamma slider) probably need to be adjusted. And anyone seeing black sky or other odd things should update their video drivers.

    Video Modes: Nope. The 'fix' for that is something set in the far future. We'd love to fix it, but redesigning the entire interface takes time.

    Bulk Purchases: Another thing we want to do, but it's not high on the priority list.

    Paladin 2H piercing: Velious.

    On Line Store: Dunno, still trying to get answers on that one.

    JBoots: They're fine as is.

    Bank: Another thing we want to do that has a low priority.

    Banners: I can't say that I like then either, but they seem to be here to stay.

    Music: *shrug* I don't know what to say…

    Mouse Wheel Support: I'll put it on my list, but there aren't a whole lot of places where the wheel would be useful…

    Bug/Feedback: Good idea, I'll mention it to Roy. But, again, low priority item.

    Graphics: That sort of things will get upgraded as much as it can. But we have to keep the game compatible with the original minimum system requirements.

    Epic Weapons: *sigh* "They do not cause video lag like swaying trees." I'll say it again, swaying trees have only a very tiny effect on framerate (the animation for all the trees loads ONCE). It's the volume of trees that is the issue, not the swaying. As for the epic weapons… I think they're fine as they are, but I'll bring it up (and yes, they would cause MORE of a framerate issue than the fact that trees sway, since they have a particle effect).

    Grimstaff: I agree, when blind you should still see text. I'll add it to my list, but again it's not going to make it to the high priority list.

  • Driver Problems
    If you are still having problems after updating your drivers, send us the results of a DXDIAG and we'll take a look at it. Make sure to include a description of the problem and your system configuration. Send it all to eqtech@verant.com

  • Unfair Attack Thread
    I'm closing this one.

    It's unfair to attack Rowyl here, since he's not going to be able to go into details here (and neither am I). I'll say it again, we won't discuss the details of anyone's account in a public forum.

  • Mapable Key Problem
    We're working on some problems with the mapable keys. I'm pretty sure, however, that the backspace key is not going to be user mapable. You'll need to find another key, I think… You also won't be able to map ESC or /. But you will be able to map the Caps Lock key.

    All to be fixed, soon. :)

  • Game Exploits
    That's right. In any case where you think someone is exploiting the game, do this:

    Make sure any useful text is in the visible chat window and do /report [exploiters name].

    Then send a /petition with complete details of the event.

    Then move on. Go have fun and let the Guides and GMs worry about the problem. Don't let these things ruin your fun.
