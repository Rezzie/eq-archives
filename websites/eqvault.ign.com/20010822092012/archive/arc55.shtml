Gordon Infobits, Part II
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:39 PM PDT
Here's a compiled list of the many board posts Gordon Wrinn has made this evening on th e Gameplay Board:

  • New Server Question
    [Quote from original post: PS Probably a little out of the subject, but do you still know if there is going to be any newbie servers?]

    Of the two servers on order, we're not sure yet whether only one or both of those will be a split server.

  • Board Issues
    [Quote from original post: When you decided to make the boards avaliable to subscribers only I knew what you were trying to do. You actually believed that the "whining" would go away because you created a great game and only those people who quit the game were disgruntled and kept posting here to stir up the "silent minority".]

    No, we never believed complaints would go away. We did however believe the constant baseless flames would go away, because that's what we were going to commit to make happen. We also hoped to see more people who formerly only lurked for fear of being flamed come onto the boards and let everyone know their thoughts as well. We've made great strides towards both of those goals.

    [Quote from original post: The reason that it did not go away is because, although you created a very immersive game, you have made some bad decisions in the design, testing, alteration, and expansion phases of the game.]

    The concept of "bad decisions" is entirely subjective. What one person thinks is a bad decision, another person thinks is a great idea. In any case, we'll be the first to admit that the game isn't perfect, but it is inline with most of our goals and as such is responsible for the number of people that choose to play it.

    No game can be perfect, and at some point continuing to tweak it to get it closer to "perfection" in *our* eyes will sooner or later take it further away from that goal. Perfection is subjective as well. I've yet to see any subject that 280K could agree upon. Even the latest UI changes which hurt NO ONE still had poeple who disagreed with the changes because they felt we should be working on something else that was important to them.

    [Quote from original post: It is not our fault that this is so. How could it be the players fault that we are losing interest in a game that we love and feel so passionately about.]

    Who says that it has to be anyone's "fault"? Growing disinterested in a game over time is part of the natural course. I just wish that instead of people saying, "I've played a year and a half, wasted 150 days played 4 level 50 characters, and you've finally upset me enough that I'm quiting", they'd say "OMG! I've played for a year and a half and put 150 days played into my 4 characters! I've never had something keep me this interested for this long. It's time to move on, but I just wanted to extend my thanks for the entertainment. It was well worth the money."

    Frankly, if someone plays for a year or a year and a half before quitting, we feel that we've done our job well, regardless the reason stated for quitting. A computer game keeping someone interested for that long is truly amazing, and we're happy that our game was one that could do it. Over 70% of everyone who has ever purchased EverQuest is still subscribing today, and that is a fantasitc testement to what we're doing.

    [Quote from original post: Dont talk to me about burn-out either. There is a difference between baing burnt-out on a game and getting tired of the negative changes imposed on that game.]

    Oops, too late, I spoke about burnout already. Anyway, I've found that people who are ready to quit are looking for a *reason* to do so, rather than quitting just because they've had enough. These changes are excellent fuel for that. I sincerly doubt that anyone goes from completely happy to "quitting" over one minor change such as the stats on one item or the location of a drop changing.

    You talk about "negative changes" like "negative" is a factual statement when in fact it is an opinion. Take this Veeshan loot issue as an example. People want risk and reward to make sense. We're going to make it make sense. That is a positive change if we are implementing what people want, right? A great many people have posted how some loot in other areas belongs on dragons. This was truly a case where it wasn't a case of items in VP being underpowered as much as it was a case of items elsewhere being overpowered. We can't keep raising the power of items found at the higher levels every time an item shows up in the mid levels a little more powerful than expected.

  • Necro and AoE Spells
    [Quote from original post: All other classes get them. Clerics get group heals, shamans get group buffs, druids get group wolf form ETC. Shouldn't we get group DMF or a AoE fear, lifetap or something??? As well as we dont get lev till 44. Why can't we get dead man bobbing at a lower level? The only AoE spells we get are the ones for close combat and do no real damage so are of NO use. Any input would be nice..]

    Necros pay the price for their superior single-target offensive power by getting little in the way of group-benefit spells, and all of them are single-target in nature. You do get a line of point-blank AOEs however.

  • /Con Biased?
    Some classes excell at soloing, others at grouping, some can do either just as well. Strangely enough, the two top soloing casters and the top soloing meleer (for the most part) aren't on the top of everyone's "must have for the group" list.

    It's worked out quite well in that regard.

    [Quote from original post: This is the main prob I have with this, as I understand it the higher the mob cons to you the more xp you get if you kill it putting classes such as warriors who have to group at a xp disadvantage.]

    I disagree. I've illustrated before on the boards how a group can get more experience than a soloer in most cases. Though members split the experience, they are killing things so much faster and with less downtime that it's more efficient for most applications.

    Of course, if all the group is going to do is sit in one place camping a single spawn killing no faster than a solo caster of the same range could, the group will of course advance slower.

  • Clerics
    [Quote from original post: Proposed Solution would be a group variant (and lvl 58 is way to late in the class life to get this ability, how many cleric do yuo think will ever be 58?). I suggest at 52 a group version of Pinzarn be available ]

    How many clerics will ever be 52? More than reach 58, sure, but that's not really an argument for changing it. The 58th level spell is certainly a nice benefit for reaching that level. Until you do, perhaps those you are buffing could share in the cost of the components.

    [Quote from original post: Invisibility - We get group and single invis undead, no invis live. I think we are now the last class with no ability or item (without charges) that can't invis.]

    There are other classes that can't do something that other classes can. Class interdependancy is based on this. For instance, it could be aruged that necros should be able to case their invis upon others since every other class that can cast invis can do this. This isn't, however, how it was intended. Clerics can do so much for a group in other areas, it makes sense that they would have to rely on the group for some things as well.

    [Quote from original post: AC Lines.. SoW cost so mouch to cast.. Why should we burn 300+ mana for an AC buff, this seems like a awfully large jump in mana cost from AoF. We should see this buff reduced to the 175 mana range.]

    It's a tradeoff. If the spell costs too much to cast, and is not worth that cost to you in that case, it's your option to choose not to cast it. If the group on the other hand wants every single stackable buff possible, it's going to cost more in the way of mana, components, and time.

  • Non Agressive Dragons
    [Quote from original post: I haven't seen the one in Emrald Jungle, but I have seen the one in Dreadlands a few times. The thought occured to me that wouldn't it be great if these beasts would give a quest of some kind?]

    At some point there probably will be, but not just yet. Those dragons operate on a timer while the dragon is idle until it depops. Any time they respond to quest text, that timer is suspended. We had NPCs in the game like this at one point, but found that people were able to get them to stay around indefinitely by constanly /hailing them.

    At some point we might change the way those timers work, so ruin your faction with those dragons at your own risk :)

  • Kunark Upgrades Postponed?
    I believe that Brad said we were running a couple of weeks behind. I certainly do expect to see the Kunark upgrade available once again prior to the release of Velious. We just want to be absolutely sure that there are no repeats of the fulfillment problems with Kunark.

  • Displeased with Kunark
    [Quote from original post: From reading this board, I used to think Kunark sucked...I rarely went. Receintly, my guildmate told me to go to FM. I am hooked now. The exp is great. I belive that since the MOBs are harder, the experience for them is also boosted. I suggest killing lower things than you are used to, or play a class more known for it's solo ability.]

    Thanks for the note, and you are indeed correct that with the harder NPCs comes more experience. That's how it should be :)

  • VP Loot
    [Quote from original post: Can't you add a few high-powered items to the dragon drops instead of completely nerfing Sebilis?]

    We're not going to "completely nerf" Sebilis or any other zone, just balance item allocation out in a manner consistant with risk. There are some items from Sebilis that will certainly move to Veeshan's peak as common loot on the dragons there. Yes, the dragons in VP have common, rare, and ultrarare drops.

  • Bothersome Buffs
    I know that this issue was brought up back in beta, and it was determined at that time that it shouldn't be too much of an issue. That said, anyone using the spell in an offensive manner would be in violation of the rules of conduct.

    If players do find this to be a big issue, I could look into seeing if these types of spells could be limited to group only.

  • Magical Question
    [Quote from original post: do got a question: its 35 right? not 32 as ya thought (med without a book)]

    It's been a while since I was looking forward to the magic level. I'll go whip up a character tomorrow and get it straight in my head.

  • Morell-Thule Problem
    [Quote from original post: Our server will have to ignore both Nagafen and Vox and favor a large amount of time sitting on our collective duffs, praying to whatever EQ god that the boats mess up and Faydedar is forced to repop.]

    I don't understand your complaint. Are you saying that you won't be able to kill Vox and Nagafen but will be able to kill Faydedar?

    Or are you saying that people powerleveled past 52 before there were enough people capable of taking on Vox or Nagafen?

  • 10 Questions
    [Quote from original post: 1) With the update of parry and dodge for rangers being in 'fine tuning' when can we reasonably expect for it to go live?]

    Friday. We postponed the patch for this and a couple of the other issues that we wanted to get in.

    [Quote from original post: 2) With the lowering of cast time on jolt to go down, when can we reasonably expect it to go live? Also will it still maintain its high resist rate?]

    Should be this Friday as well.

    [Quote from original post: 3) How will the implementation of the upgrade to parry and dodge be introduced? I.E. will it just be allowed to progress from its current level or will it be maxed out to your current level? i.e. im level 55 and say dodge is increased to 225 would i expect it to be maxed at 220 for my level or would it begin to work up from its current level. ]

    The increase in the cap will give you the opportunity to build it naturally. No skills will be auto-accelerated to the cap.

    [Quote from original post: 4) The UI tweaks. Will we have to wait till velious to see them go live?]

    Some yes, others no. It turns out that one of the stunning changes that I alluded to earlier this week is one on the Velious plate, so it won't be going live tomorrow.

    [Quote from original post: 7) Regarding trade skills, would it be possible to actually have a new title at skill 200 such as grandmaster? (seems this would be pretty easy and quick but not of high importance, but im quite sure many of us would appreciate having a title other than master just for self gratification) ]

    Only if it fits into Rowyl's new UI issues. This is more related to art than "vision".

    [Quote from original post: 8) Can we get all the gms together and make a rock solid rule regarding some pvp issues that are affecting Rallos Zek server such as what defines corpse camping, bind camping, and when it is allowable to engage a person after killing them. ]

    If the GMs are going to intervene in these matters at all, I doubt that any rule could be "rock solid". It's all highly subjective in nature.

    I'll check into the questions I didn't answer.

  • Spell Slots
    [Quote from original post: But abashi is very firm on this topic, he wants to keep it like it is. Its not going to happen, I wish it would though. It does force you to use strategy.]

    Indeed. 8 spell-slots is absolutely firm, like melee-binding, the game being group-oriented, and the experience system. It's a core foundation of the game that isn't subject to change.

    Though in all fairness it's not solely my decision, but one made by the design team.

  • A Bard's Role
    [Quote from original post: All I know is how I perceive my role in EverQuest. Let me explain how I see it in the hopes someone from Verant can explain where I differ. For me, a bard is the ultimate group member. We are designed to operate most effectively in the company of others. We are often called a Jack of All Trades, but a master of none. We have so many abilities that are spread among several other classes. Need someone to buff your attack speed? A Bard can do that and more at higher levels. Need to slowdown a mobs attack speed? A Bard can do that and more at higher levels. Need a boost to your resistances? A Bard is one of the best at boosting resistances. Need to regenerate health or mana faster? Again, a bard can do this. Need to charm a creature for a short while and make him your ally? Bards. Need someone who can wear armor and take a FEW hits ok? Bards. Need to get from one location to another fast and teleports or gates are not feasible? Bards.]

    While I'll leave in-depth determination of "role" to those playing it, I think that you've answered your own question here. Bards are good at so many things, you're surely here to do some of those things. I feel that Bards excell at making a good group better. That's my opinion. What's more important though is, what is a bard's role to you? If you look at it as a walking-talking mana battery, there's not really much I can do to make you feel better about it. It's the same issues with the clerics who often complain that they are a walking-talking hitpoint-battery. You are a support class. You support the group, and you do it very well. Whether or not you are happy with that role is up to you.

Veeshan's Peak Loot Follow-up
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:03 PM PDT
Brad wrote a follow-up to his Veeshan's Peak loot post. Here's the entire message:

    The following was my third post regarding the subject prior to the thread corrupting (*sigh*):

    I don't mean this in a condescending way at all, but I honestly wish some of you would at least try to put yourselves in our shoes.

    What options do we have in these situations?

    First, let's establish a few facts:

    1. This is a dynamic game. It may not be as dynamic as some would like with massive invasions and huge volcanic eruptions and entire NPC governments taking action that affect players' lives, but it DOES change. We add to the game, we change the game, and some times we take away. (Actually, it makes me worry for when the technology is such that we can do all of that stuff -- how will people respond... 'That volcano created a mountain where my favorite hunting grounds were and blocked the entrance to my favorite dungeon and now I have to walk farther to the new entrance at the top of the crater! You nerfed me!').

    We cannot have stagnant games, yet when the status quo changes, SOMEBODY always get's upset, especially if the change is not universally positive (and what change is?).

    2. We're not perfect! We will make mistakes. We will continue to make mistakes as long as we continue to add content, and things will be out of balance in the future.

    3. We learn and get better. For example, lots of universal class non-specific loot like Rubicite is bad.

    So, what options do we have:

    1. We could just leave mistakes and aspects of the game that didn't turn out as planned. I think that leads to stagnation. I also think it leaves areas of the game untouched... empty zones, ignored quests, etc.

    2. We could upgrade the VP items that are weaker to what they should be. This doesn't address the other items that are too powerful relative to their difficulty to obtain, but it would address one side of the problem. The problem is that they'd be comparable then most likely to the other broken items. Many players would then simply try to obtain the broken items since they are as good as the upgraded VP items but easier to get. And we'd still have broken items.

    3. We could upgrade the VP items and make them better than the non-VP broken items (and not adjust encounter difficulty). Here's where the MUDflation comes in. Now, what have we done to all the other items in the game... they're all indirectly weaker now. And, we've set a new (lower) standard of risk vs. reward. The game becomes less challenging, etc.

    4. We could upgrade the VP items and make them better than the non-VP broken items (and then re-adjust NPC encounter difficulty throughout the game as well as other treasure tables everywhere, raising the entire risk/reward ratio but keeping it balanced). This, by the way, is what the infamous mud wimping article would have us do -- either that, or nothing. Basically, it's a ton of work and would probably introduce a whole slew of new problems and potential for mistakes. Bluntly, we'd rather allocate those resources elsewhere.

    So, our solution is to identify the non-VP items that are out there that are too powerful and probably move them to VP. And then take some underpowered VP items and move them elsewhere AND/OR make some new items to replace the over-powered ones.

    I also want to emphasize that there really aren't that many items. Also, just to let you know, MORE of this is going to occur when EQ Live starts revamping old world zones (Permafrost, Najena, etc.) We will be adding new content AND moving existing content around.

    I know this isn't compatible with some players' feelings that how and where things are in the game the day they started playing MUST also be the same 3, 6 or 12 months later. But, with all due respect, that's simply NOT how MMORPGs work. As mentioned earlier, if anything, MMORPGs will become MORE dynamic and less predictable/dependable in the future.

    In closing, I'll quote from one of the EQ Live game designers emails to me regarding some of the items that very well might change -- I want to emphasize again that there aren't that many we're talking about:

    "Broken Weapons include: Blade of the Black Dragon Eye, Warding Star, Runed Fighters Staff (Somewhat easy to acquire, godly proc ratio), Moss Covered Twig (we all know this one), etc.

    Those are a few examples of broken items which have been acquired by players and severely hamper our attempts to create new and interesting loot. The players should be made happy soon however. We just went through and nerfed the drops of those broken weapons and "added new content" -- replacement new items. Near copies of some of the broken weapons are being placed into VP." -EQ Live Game Designer

    -Brad

    p.s. I know this might encourage people to camp more short-term, but I wanted to be up front and detailed with all of you nonetheless. Heh, everything's a trade-off.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 2:58 PM PDT
Gordon's made quite a few posts on the boards this afternoon. Here's the latest from the Gameplay Board:

  • Twinked Corpses
    Interestingly enough, the GMs get /petitions every night from people who lost a twinked up corpse like this. Needless to say that it isn't something that we can intervene in.

  • Resist Bug
    I'm aware of this happening at resists over 255, however this is the first I've heard of it being over 200. Are you certain that this is the case?

  • Abusive Users
    If she's being threatened in that manner, she'll want to /report the text then /petition to let the GMs know that a /report has been filed. Providing that the /report shows the threats, he'll certainly be removed from game. I'm not really sure why she got the response that you've indicated, so if more information can be provided I'll be happy to look into it.

    Secondly, if she has a restraining order against him, chances are that it prohibits any contact between them. The courts would probably not be too happy about learning that he's contacted her on the internet.

  • Putting Stuff in the Bank
    [Quote from original post: Would it not be easier to be able to put thing in a bag that is already in the bank?]

    This is indeed one of the UI enhancements on Rowyl's list. I'm not sure yet if it's one that will make the final cut due to the number of good suggestions, however we'll give it a shot.

  • Dupe Bug Question
    Good suggestion Wyld, and to answer Sabaack's question, we'd certainly take intent into account.

  • Class Changes
    We have nothing in particular planned right now. We do plan on retesting all of the classes to ensure that they fall where we feel that they should prior to freezing the classes. Most of these tests have already been completed for most of the melee classes, with only a few more steps necessary to finalize those.

  • More on the Story Elements in EQ
    On one hand we have players asking for more of a story line to be overtly implemented into the game. On the other, there's also those who prefer an open "floorplan" if you will. In either case, we want to provide for both players.

    "Story" requires that some limits be placed on the breadth of things that players can do. There's really no way around that. Whether the story is done in short bursts, such as quests, or in large multi-zone epics, there is some limit on what you can do to take part in the story.

  • Determining Balance of the Classes
