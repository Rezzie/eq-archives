NPC Q-S

From EQVault Wiki

Queen Christianosthex is the Evil Queen of Neriak

Rakothian Kingdom is the first ogreian kingdom in the nearby Rakthokian ridge mountain range, named after the first ogre leader "Rakthok the Warlord”.

Ralem Cristoff is a ranger serving as the Field Marshall for Antonius Bayle's roving patrol.

Rile is the Iskar son of Venril Sathir. He assumed the throne of Sebilis after his father became a lich. He kept his power using two warlords: Kurn Machata, Karatukus, and the vampire Chosooth. The Dragon lords saw the threat from Rile, and convened the Ring of Scale and discussed how to proceed. Trakenon advocated swift intervention; but he was voted down and the Ring of Scale elected to intervene in the affairs of Sebilis only indirectly. Trakenon took matters into his own hands, and conjured a squall at sea, drowning King Rile. This action triggered a war amongst the dragons.

Ring of Scale
The organization of dragons on Norrath. The Ring of Scale has been interfering in the affiars of the other races since the beginning of Norrathian history. Lady Vox and Lord Nagafen were shunned from the Ring of Scale after their forbidden mating. Trakenon is also looked on with disfavor by the Ring of Scale because of his impetuous nature.

Riptide Goblinsare Aqua Goblins of Norrath

Rogues of the White Rose are the Halas Rogue Guild. The Rogue Guildmaster is Dun McDowell leads the Guild in quiet neutrality, not supporting or hindering any other factions. Of interesting note is that the Banker in Halas is a supporter of the Rogue’s Guild. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Rogkasth Vr'l A cold and cunning killer, Rogkasth is the troll Shadowknight in the service of the Dark Elven wizard Valhai Ka'Izal. Though Rogkasth abhors her, and often entertains images of the wizard's severed head hanging from the tip of his blade, he is inextricably bound to Valhai as a consequence of the horrific experiment that left him with a heightened intelligence, above and beyond that of his trollish kin. Rogkasth holds respect for his own people, but has inherited Valhai's intolerances. All other races are inferior, and Ogres, in particular, are a lowly and pitiable lot to be used in whatever manner best serves his twisted purposes.(History of Norrath, Verant International)

Root of Innuruuk Shaman's Guild (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Rygorr Clan Snow Orcs A group of orcs found in Kunark.

SabertoothClan Black Burrow Gnolls (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Sarnak Collective The Sarnaks, a race which was created by the meddling of the Iksar Necromancers are in an ongoing state of war with the Legion of Cabilis (the Trooper Guards). They have a fortress at the Lake of Ill Omen, as well as their ancestral home - the city of Chardok - which is within the burning woods. Sarnaks are highly intelligent, and have a strong presense of magic users within their ranks. Their warrior classes also tend to have a high magical resistance rate. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Scaled Mystics

Scorpiki are ancient protectors of the hidden temples in Kunark. Found in the OverThere.

Scouts of Tunare can be found in the Treetop city of Kelethin. This rogues guild under guildmaster Tylfon remain neutral with no declared allies or enemies.

Shadow Men are invisible except for their weapons and sheilds, Shadowmen can be found all over Norrath.

ShadowKnights of Nightkeep
Troll Shadow Knight guild in Grobb called Shadow Knights of Nightkeep. Led by Hukulk these knights like Da Bashers and hate the Broken Skull Clan. They hate the Ogre Shadow Knights of Oggok called the Greenblood Knights even more. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Shaman of Justice
Led by Margyn McCann, the Shaman of Justice follow The Tribunal with a strong sense of judgement. For this reason, they support the Merchants of Halas, and are opposed to the Rogues of the Circle of Unseen Hands in Qeynos, Coalition of Tradefolk III in Freeport, and the Ebon Mask in Neriak. Despite this hate of Rogues, they do not actually hate the Rogues of the White Rose. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Shamen of War are Ogre Shamen from Ogguk.

Shissar
A race of snake men who enslaved the Iskar. Their capital was Chelsith, now the Overthere in Kunark.

Green Mist, Green Death
The Shissar's disloyalty and blasphemous pride would bring the wrath of the gods upon them. No power could stop the force to come. A green cloud formed in the skies above Kunark. The cloud began to descend upon the land. From its green core, the face of death began to form. It was of no god they knew of. It opened its giant maw and spoke in tongues unknown. From the mouth of death rushed forth a green mist. The Shissar shaman began to cast spells in hopes of shielding their city and the necromancers began to assault the mist with great weapons. All were futile. The mist rushed forth. The first snakes to be swallowed by the mist had their scales and flesh rot and fall from their bones. Horrid screams of suffering could be heard from within the mist. A river of flesh and blood flowed. The Iksar stood and gazed upon the gristle that was once the Shissar. With the Green Death came the liberation of the Iksar. (History of Norrath, Verant International)

Could this be Trakenon?

Shralock Orcs' are an Orc tribe that lives in Kithicor Forest.

Grigog, Bgug, Sagg, and Glunn are a trio of orcs of the Shralok clan. Tyrana the Darl Elf often used these three as escorts for her journeys between Neriak and Highkeep. Though she always paid the orcs well for their services, they were always willing to help protect to "bootifal" Tyrana Slil and probably would have done the job free of charge.

Grigog was the leader of the mercenaries, slightly bigger then the others and a rather powerful shaman to boot. Bgug was the most demented of the Orcs and delighted in the "torture" of his already dead opponents. He would usually cut small openings into different parts of the body trying to guess the identity of certain anatomical parts. He would do this until Grigog kicked him in the back of the head, breaking his morbid trance.

Sagg was easily one of the biggest and dumbest orcs on the lands. He was utterly useless when given orders more complicated then a word or two but when someone would point at something and say "kill it" it is assured that it will stand no more, be it human or inanimate object. When not fighting he enjoyed staring into empty space and drooling. Glunn was another matter altogether. He was as happy-go-lucky as an orc could be. He was celebrating his 12th birthday but because he could not remember exactly when he was born, he had been celebrating his birthday for several days and planned to continue to celebrate it for a few more, just to be safe. (History of Norrath, Verant International)

Silent Fist Clan is a Monk's Guild.

Sionachie Heartsingeris personal Bard to the Princess Firiona Vie.

Skytalons are the fae drakes of Faydark

Snowfang Gnollsare a clan of gnolls in the Icelclad Ocean that survive on the skins and meat of walrus and otter. Therefore, they are disliked by the Othmir. They are the main prey of Lodzial, the sea turtle.

Soldiers of Tunare
The Kelethin Druids guild is called the Soldiers of Tunare. Their guildmaster is named Heartwood Master. While dedicated to The Mother of All, Tunare, they remain loyal to the Elven King Tearis Thex and support the Emerald Warriors. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Solusek Mining Co
The company of Gnomes living and working in Solusek's Eye form this group. They are connected to the main gnomes in Ak'Anon. They are opposed to their neighbors the Kobolds. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Songweavers
The Songweavers can be found in the treetop city of Kelethin in Greater Faydark. Under the leadership of Sylia Windlehands, many young bards receive their training. Sharing their guildhall are the League of Antonican Bards Mail bards. Here, the local mail is collected and then carried by mailpouch to Freeport and beyond. The Songweavers, unlike the League Bards, remain neutral in the affairs of faction. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Spirocs of Timorous are a colorful bird race inhabiting a city similar to the Aviak city in South Karana. They closely resemble the aviaks in the Ocean of Tears.

Splitpaw Clan
The clan of gnolls which formerly lived in Splitpaw Lair before being run out by the stronger gnolls which now inhabit the lair, the Nisch Mal. The Splitpaw Gnolls are aligned with the Sabertooth Clan of Blackburrow. Both the Guards of Qeynos and the Residents of Karana take a dim view of these gnolls. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm) Splitpaw is the site where in gnoll legend, their god set his foot upon the earth, and the surface markings are his footprint. Nobody knows if this is true, but hunters in Southern Karana know the three talons rising from the ground that mark the entrance to the lair. (Eq Atlas) From the Gnoll Scrolls: Fear not my brethren! We have taken this home and we shall make it our own! Use what resources we have to establish ourselves here. Care not about the Theth, for they are our inferiors. Use their goods, use their services, and use their skins if need be. In time, we shall feel our former glory! Ishk kal hobra!

Volume 1 'History of the Verishe Mal' We were forged from the Earth by the hands of our lord, Brell Serillis. He molded us into an image that suited him, and we were glad. Such is our roll to learn and expand, and be one with the underworld. We were born to know many things, and we shall. Our lord told us that we suffered not the curse of the others, and for this, we are grateful.

Volume 2: History of the Verishe Mal
Our forefathers gave us many tasks to better us. We learned the way of the sword, and the way of magiks. Each test we proved victorious, and for a time, quite prosperous. In time, we grew to be a large race, riddled with a rutheless empire that spanned many a mile. Our worth was broken down into a caste system, and many were made slaves.

Volume 3: 'History of the Verishe Mal'
The Verishe Mal were then ruled by the Ishva Mal, a hard and stern ruler. In the old tongue, Ishva meant Supreme and Mal meant ruler. Beneath the Ishva, there were the Rosch, and the Tesch, the caster and warrior caste. Above the Rosch and the Tesch were the Nisch, the spiritual leaders of the Verishe Mal. Shortly after the abolishion of the old ways and the Ishva, the Nisch Val formed a council of nine, which still rule us to this day. (History of Norrath, Verant International)

The Spurned
In the Neriak Commons can be found the Guildhall of The Spurned. Here Dark Elf casters practice the lesser magical arts. Being spurned by Dark Elf Society, they actively oppose The Dead whose Necromancers and Shadow Knights enjoy the favor of the Dark Elven Queen. Carnia V'Retta trains the Enchanters, Jayna D'Bious the Magicians and Gath N'Mare the Wizards. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)


Steel Warriors
The Steel Warriors have two guildhalls, one in South Qeynos and one in West Freeport. Both are situated around PvP areas, so care should be taken when visiting them. In Freeport, Cain Darkmoore heads the guildhall called The Bunker. In Qeynos Ebon Strongbear leads the warrior guildhall there. The Steel warriors support The Knights of Truth in Freeport and the Guards of Qeynos in their fights against the corruption of the Freeport Militia and the Corrupt Qeynos Guards. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

'Stone Hive Bixies are a type of bixie.

Stormguard
In North Kaladim can be found the Warrior Guildhall of Stormguard. Guildmaster Furtog Ogrebane leads the guild and can often be found overlooking the small arena in front of the guild. The Stormguard warriors support the Dwarven King Kazon Stormhammer, the Merchants of Kaladim and the Paladin's guild called Miners Guild 249. They have a specific hatred for the Craknek Warriors of Oggok. With a name like Ogrebane, somehow this is not surprising. (Allakhazam’s Magical Realm)

Stormreapers are a guild of druids

Swifttails are a guild of Iskar monks.
From the Iksar Lore Books in Cabilis: The Swifttail Caste, Fists and Tails of Cabilis

It was in dawning of the Sebilisian Empire that the art of fist and tail was forged. In the beginning, the pugilists of the great tribes were nothing more than warriors without weapons. Soon we would be forged into weapons.

Grandmaster Tynn was the father of our disciplines. He came to the empire from an unknown tribe. Some say he was a ronin who wandered the worlds of Norrath where he formed the basis of the city of Sebilis with no rebirth. He entered to form a caste which did not exist. He was quickly challenged by the warlords. He spoke his words and they did not listen. He knew the only way to make them understand was to challenge their finest in The Gauntlet. He called for four of the finest warlords and allowed them arms. The warlords agreed, thinking his challenge as easy task. Grandmaster Tynn quickly dispatched of the warlords with nothing more than fist and tail. He fought in a way never displayed. His victory earned him much respect and Emperor Sathir soon commanded him to form the Court of Pain and raise an army of monks. Tynn formed the Swifttail Caste. Soon the way of fist and tail had hundreds in its ranks, climbing the rungs. Tynn showed them they were not warriors, but monks.

They would don shackles as testament to the Age of Snake when the Iksar were a race enslaved. Inner strength and harmony were taught. The Swifttail were born.

The Swifttail were disbanded during the End Days. It was not till the rise of the new empire under Emperor Vekin did the caste reform. Grandmaster Glox, a former disciple of Tynn, came to New Sebilis to rebuild the Court of Pain. In honor of Tynn, all masters now drape themselves in a robe like he wore.
