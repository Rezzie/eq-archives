Vault Network Enlightens Dark Zion
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 12:11 PM PDT
As the online persistent world category continues to grow and evolve, one of the most exciting aspects to watch is the way each new project reveals different design ideas and influences. For this reason, it can be pretty difficult knowing that a potentially exciting project is in progress, but still waiting patiently until its developer is ready to unveil it. A bit earlier today, Wombat Games formally announced Dark Zion, a game we’ve been waiting to learn about for some time, and one that seems certain to attract considerable interest from the online gaming community.

At a time when game makers with experience creating online worlds are at a premium, Wombat’s co-founders include Rick Delaschmit, former Lead Programmer of Ultima Online, and Jason Spangler who held the same position on the Ultima Online: The Second Age expansion. In addition, the team’s Creative Consultant is the internationally acclaimed New York Times best-selling author Tad Williams.

To bring the online gaming community the best possible coverage of this exciting new title, the IGN Vault Network announces the opening of our latest dedicated site, Dark Zion Vault. And as the highlight of our launch, we are pleased to present the first-ever exclusive and very extensive interview with the game’s principals at Wombat Games. Here’s a brief summary of the game to pique your interest:

Dark Zion is a player-driven, player-controlled persistent online world. Players will have an unprecedented level of control over their environment; beyond building a character, a player will have the opportunity to build a business, a town, an army, even an entire society. There will be no arbitrary quests or super-imposed events; the story is the success or failure of the world at large.
Other features at Dark Zion Vault will include game information, news, concept art, screenshots, humor, reader and community participation features, humor and much more. New features and content will begin to appear in the very near future.

We invite all members of the community who are interested in the future of online gaming to stop in and check out the coming excitement from Wombat Games and best-selling author Tad Williams at our brand new Dark Zion Vault. Enjoy!

Ancient Teleporters Found to All Cities!
[113 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:59 AM PDT
Mandos of the Lore Seekers sent a very intriguing report detailing information about mysterious teleporters that will deliever a player to one of Norrath's major cities. Here is the report, you'll need to highlight it with your mouse cursor to view it as some might consider this a spoiler:

Honorable Guildmaster Sorontar:

As a Wizard, I am professionally interested in all things having to do
with ethereal transport. As such, I was drawn to rumors concerning
firepots throughout the lands of Norrath. These rumors spoke of an
ancient transport system "for those of evil race", which could be
accessed through having the appropriate "key", and inserting it (or
using it) with the appropriate "firepot" in a Norrathian city, to be
instantly transported to another city of Norrath, without the need of
spell, Wizard or Druid (shudder!).

Knowing that most ancient magic was not related to the evils that plague
Norrath, I have long discounted the likelihood that such devices, if
they existed, were for evil races' exclusive use. Evil races are prone
to destruction, rarely creativity of any sort! However, it has come to
my attention, that such teleportation devices do, in fact exist, and can
be utilized by all races. However, their utility is, at this stage of
my investigation, limited to travel from one hub point to twelve (12) of
the cities of Norrath.

This hub point is deep under the Timorous Deep, located at -12260,
+4365, -277. It came to my attention, that the top of a classic Combine
Era Wizard Spire, with its familiar four tines, was observable above the
waves in this depth of Ocean. If you swim down directly between the
four tines, you will find a square opening on the ocean floor.
Descending, you will find yourself suddenly in air, in a square room,
with three firepots along each wall. In the center burns a magic fire
of blue, such as can be seen at certain ancient Elvish and Combine
towers (e.g. Oasis). Note that the descent from the surface is one way,
and I was unable to find any way to leave the room by normal means. No
monster nor person disturbed my researches that followed.

Binding myself to the locale, I commenced to mediate on the twelve
firepots. It was immediately apparent that the style of each was
different, and each was familiar to a particular culture of Norrath. I
proceeded to test my hypothesis, and touching the rust-colored low
firepot, similar to many in the city of my youth, found myself, being
dusted off by a very surprised guard (my second cousin, twice removed),
in Northern Felwithe. Without answering my startled cousin's queries
(he knows I tend to gate around hastily...), I immediately gated back.
I tested all twelve pots, and here is the key of their wisdom:

North Left:(fire on floor)Grobb (entrance)
North Center:(6 side gray)Ogguk (entrance)
North Right:(6 side tall)Erudin (near dock)

East Left:(6 side gray)Neriak Commons (entrance)
East Center:(4 side wood)Greater Faydark (under Kelethin)
East Right:(6 side rust)Northern Felwithe (entrance)

South Left:(Brown box)Halas(near dock)
South Center:(Dark with blue)East Freeport (on dock)
South Right:(tall gold vase)Cabilis West (entrance)

West Left:(White marble)South Kaladim (entrance)
West Center:(Lava Lamp)Akannon (entrance)
West Right:(torch stand)Rivervale (entrance)

It is to be noted that "the Black Hole", Qeynos, and Highpass, are not
represented here.

An intriguing question, beyond the origin of these firepots and their
creator's identity, is: "Are there other pots out there that bring you
to The Timorous Deep?"

Respectfully submitted,

Mandos Turambar, Wizard Peregrinator of the 48th Degree
Treasurer of the First Chapterhouse of the Seekers of Lore (Tarew Marr)
This is a very interesting addition to the world of Norrath. Feel free to post your comments.

Couple Kunark Issues
[17 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:36 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn just posted the following post on the Gameplay Board:

    There's a couple of issues that have several threads running behind them. It might be easier for me to address them at one time:
    Spirit of Scale:

    We'll be making this spell group-only (e.g. must be grouped with target) shortly. This should alleviate people's concerns about it being used offensivly to harm other players.

    Backstabbing Pets:

    Up until the level 50+ pet spells, all pets were based off of warriors. We now have the ability to base pets off of other classes, and that's what's been done here. There are now rogue pets, wizard pets, and monk pets, which represent additional abilities for the pet-users above 50th level.

    Rogues need not worry that a backstabbing pet somehow reduces the desirability of their class. First, the pet does not have the backstab skill that a rogue of that level would have, so they won't hit as often nor will they do as much damage. Furthermore, they don't have all of the stat bonuses that increase the rogues effectiveness. Certainly they can be buffed up, but they'll never come close to having a real rogue. Finally, rogue pets will never have the ability to double-backstab as rogues do.

    Rogue pets won't be picking locks either, and there are quite a few places to do that in Kunark.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits
[19 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:52 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn was posting up a storm on the Gameplay Board. Here's quotes and excerpts from his latest posts:

  • Questions about Disciplines
    [Quote from original post: 1) why are most of the durations 9-18 seconds? ]

    Disciplines without a downside have a short duration for balance reasons. Some of the warrior disciplines have upsides and downsides, and thus have a longer duration

    Most of the short duration disciplins allow you to do something dramatic that drastically increases your effectiveness. The short period of time is necessary for balance.

    [Quote from original post: 2) since the durations are so short why are the refresh times so long? ]

    Balance again. Some of them are so powerful that we don't want them available constantly.

    [Quote from original post: 3) why are all the disciplines on the same refresh timer? ]

    See balance :) Bascially /discipline is one ability, though there are different types of disciplines available. We basically didn't want a discipline to *always* be available.

    [Quote from original post: what range is the reduction in refresh time as a player advances in levels? 1%/level 5%/level 10%/level? ]

    Generally 1 minute per level.

    [Quote from original post: I'm just looking for some general answers. Originally I thought disciplines would be extremely powerful...but if a monk who uses Ashenhand can't use another discipline for 72 minutes...or a warrior who uses Mighty Blow can't use another for 60 minutes...UGH! Just would like to know why. ]

    Though they aren't equivalent to LoH or Harm Touch, these are two very powerful disciplines that can be used for one encounter. Please remember that warriors, rogues, and monks are considered essentially well-balanced without these disciplines, and as such they are something extra. We have to be careful when giving extra abilities to insure that they don't "overpower" the class.

  • Why are are Enchanters 'Forced' to Solo to Level 29?
    [Quote from original post: why am i forced to solo to 29? yes you put in breeze but it is still useless]

    Your argument appears to be based upon the assertion that Clarity is the only thing that makes an enchanter a worthwhile group member. In our opinion, this is certainly not the case.

    [Quote from original post: so i cant get the spell. unless i want to pay 60p for a level 16 spell? ]

    The spell just became obtainable yesterday. I'm sure the price will drop once supply catches up with demand.

  • Veeshan's Peak
    Considering Veeshan's Peak, I'd like to explain something about this zone: It is designed to be the most difficult zone in the game, even more so than the Planes. As such, its rewards are much higher than other zones, but so are the risks.

    We recommend that only the most advanced and organized players in the game even attempt to enter this zone. The customer service staff (GMs and Guides) will not assist players in any way in regards to this zone. This includes help for issues such as unrecoverable corpses (see your local Necromancer), or characters finding themselves stuck (see your local Magician), bugs, etc. The only assistance that will be offered by customer service reps in this zone are those covered by the Play Nice Policies.

  • Warrior HP at level 51
    Number of hitpoints gained per level is assigned on the basis of race, class, and a multiplier based upon level. The multiplier increases in steps every X (where X is a value based upon race and class) levels. When someone hits a level where the value of X is increased above that of the previous level, the character will experience a HP "pop" over that that they usually receive. This in essence "makes up" for all the extra hitpoints they would have received if the multiplier was that way all along.

    The Palys, SKs, and Bards all hit a step of that sort at 51, causing some of them to get upwards of 100 HP for the level. This doesn't mean that they are getting 100HP per level, just that for that level the number of hitpoints received "made up" for the hitpoints they would have gotten if the multiplier had always been at that level.

    Warriors operate on a different schedule than SKs and Palys, but the curve will always favor them. For instance, warriors should see a fairly substantial hitpoint pop when they hit 54.

    It's important to remember that 51 to 60 is not considered a "new" game. What I mean by this is that algorithms that take level into account and improve something every Y levels don't start over at 50+, they continue from where they left off. For instance, if someone has an attribute that improves in effectiveness every 4 levels, and his last improvement was at level 48, he won't see the next one until 52. This might make it appear that someone else who gets an improvement every 10 levels, and got their last improvement at 41, and gets their next one at 51 somehow gets something before the first guy. It's not the case, it's just the way that the math works.

    I hope I haven't confused the issue further :)

  • Hybrids in the Expansion
    [Quote from original post: Hybrids are what 60 percent warrior 40 percent druid,cleric, or necro? Yet all we get in expansion is spells that have been in the game for a while???]

    You also get enhanced damage tables, higher skill caps, higher hitpoint multipliers, etc. But it is true that the only spells you get will be those of your parent class, and this has been a pillar of the class since the game was release with exception of the 49th level quest spells.

    At 60th level, the Palys, rangers, and SKs, will all *melee* better than a 50th level warrior. You of course won't melee as well as a 60th level warrior, but that remains as designed.

  • Taunt Skill Issues
    The skill level associated with taunt interacts with your level to determine how likely the taunt is to be successful. It does not control the level of success. A successful taunt will *always* make the creature shift to you, assuming that it can be taunted (Yes, some creatures are untauntable).

    Taunt makes a creature hate you just a little bit more than it hates the next guy on the list. In other words, multiple successful taunts, or taunting a creature that is already attacking you doesn't do anything extra.

    I hope that clears up how Taunt works.

  • Skill Cap Question
    [Quote from original post: All of the new skill caps are still under what is acheivable by a level 50 character. So my question is do you need to buy Kunark in order to have higher skill caps?]

    Skill cap changes are associated with level, and only affect the 50+ game. In other words, a warrior who maxes a skill out at 40 won't have a chance to raise it more until level 51. So, yes, you need to buy Kunark (and level above 50) to get an increase in your skill caps.

  • Wolf Form is KOS to Druids in Fironia Vie
    The city governments have been enacting leash laws lately to try and curb the number of strays running wild through the cities. Wolves that aren't on leashes (e.g. a pet) will be attacked and killed on site by many of the guards.

    That’s an RP reason anyway and I thought some might get a kick out of it :)

    OOC:

    Most NPCs were viewing a druid in wolf-form as a BetaNeutral NPC, instead of as a wolf. This was fixed a few weeks ago in many of the cities, and should have been in the patch message.

  • Pronouncing Kunark
    [Quote from original post: I-K-S-A-R (ick-sar) ]

    On another note, just so everyone knows, Iksar is both singular and plural. For example:

    The troll slaughtered a human.
    The troll slaughtered 5 humans.
    The troll was slaughtered by an Iksar.
    The troll was slaughtered by 5 Iksar, but they really only needed one :)

  • In Response to Recent Shipping Comment (see below)
    [Quote from original post: How far would that be Abashi?? Sent to Zomax? Already packaged and waiting on the Zomax shipping docks?]

    "Too far" is "In Zomax’s queue for shipping today" as I understand it. Sorry for the confusion.

  • Range Attack Bug
    [Quote from original post: With the creation of the new buttons on the Combat menu Splitting up Melee Attack into Melee 1 and Melee 2, and moving Range Attack to the next row, my Range Attack has disappeared.]

    This is an art bug that was apparently introduced in the past day or so under the combat-skills menu. The buttons still function appropriately, its just that the names are being displayed improperly. The fix for this shouldn't take long.

  • The Scale Spells
    [Quote from original post: From this day forth, anyone asking me for a SOW that I am not grouped with shall receive Tunare’s blessing of Scale Of Wolf. I strongly encourage all Druids and Shamans to follow my example until Tunare (a.k.a GZ) decides to fix this spell.

    -Wolfen / 50 Druid - Druzzil Ro]

    I think that this might be a good time to mention that the "Scale" spells are not upgrades to spirit of wolf. They are a spell of a different line that happens to have the same effect. The Journey to Kunark - Fennin Ro
[43 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:42 AM PDT
The talented, daring and dashing Crispin of Fennin Ro has a tale to share with us:

Good day citizens of Norrath!

A few minutes ago, the first band of adventurers traveled to the recently re-discovered isle of Kunark. The first people on Fennin Ro to set foot on Finonia Vie were Vegeetta, Minara, Azyzik, Cloudstorm and of course yours truly.

The remainder of my report will be hidden, meaning you must highlight the white area below with your cursor to view it. I daresay I would not want to spoil all the surprises that will await you in Kunark.

The journey began at the Faydwer Docks close to the Dwarven city of Kaladim. Six small shuttles were docked neatly in a row. These shuttles could only carry a few people at time, but none the less they seemed capable of standing the voyage. Upon boarding them, we waited for additional reinforcements and prepared to get underway.

A few minutes later the shuttles were launched and headed to the unknown, departing what was then the known world.

Time seemed to freeze as we crossed the borders. When time returned to normal my sharp elven eyes tried to penetrate at the fog ahead. As we pushed forward it became clear that there was a massive object in front of us. The size of this object can only be described as unworldly at best. As we drew nearer it became apparent what it was.

Having sailed on all the known boats that exist on Norrath, my breath was taken away as I stared bleakly at the most impressive warship my eyes had ever beheld. The Elven armies had spared no expense for this mighty ship of war. The shuttles slowly came about and circled the ship halfway giving us a spectacular view of the massive ship.

The shuttles slowly docked one at time, allowing us to board the ship. When everything was secured the sails were unfolded and we got underway. The voyage wasn't short, but thanks to the vessel's speed we crossed the distance of the ocean quickly.

At last we arrived at the newly opened elven outpost of Firiona Via. We quickly disembarked and crossed the long length of the dock to set foot on the isle outpost.

A kind enchanter named Azyzik volunteered to bind the entire group to the outpost.

Feeling glad to have set foot on solid land once again, I gazed round about me to get a better feeling of my new surroundings. Despite the announcement issued by the League of Bards, this outpost seemed to be under construction still. Buildings were still being built, and the gates hardly seemed able to keep out invaders. As I wandered out to the perimeter, other people were stocking up on supplies at the nearby vendors and some were even engaging in new quests. As I scouted my surroundings, I quickly discovered that leaving the gates of the outpost would be foolish. As a 13th season Ranger, I would have no chance of surviving any kind of encounter with the gruesome creatures lay ahead. I was marooned.

Crouching down to the earth, I contemplated what my next move would be on this dangerous, but exciting new frontier.

Crispin
Ranger of Tunare

Kunark Live! - HUGE New Patch Message
[69 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:50 AM PDT
I just spotted the following Patch Message annoucning several things Kunark-related. This is a must-read for all players:

    April 24th, 6:00am
    -------------------

    ***Press Release***

    FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

    LEAGUE OF ANTONICAN BARDS ANNOUNCE NEW MAIL ROUTE

    FIRIONA VIE, Kunark. February 1, 3178--

    Today, the League of Antonican Bards, responsible for maintaining communication across Norrath announced that they have established a mail route to the newly discovered continent of Kunark, located across the ocean, south and east of Antonica. The new route will carry mail from Port Faydwer to the Elven outpost of Firiona Vie, which will serve as main port-of-entry and battle staging grounds for adventurers arriving in the new lands.

    The outpost of Firiona Vie was established soon after word reached King Tearis Thex, King of Felwithe and leader of the Koada`Dal, that his daughter of the same name had been kidnapped by lizard creatures now known to be named the Iksar. The King commissioned Galeth Veredeth, once-Lord and High Paladin of Tunare, to establish the outpost on Kunark and return his daughter to him, lest Veredeth's life be forfeited for failure. "I cannot allow those vile creatures to keep my daughter and only heir to the Koada`Dal throne from fulfilling her destiny," says King Tearis Thex. "Galeth Veredeth had best not fail me."

    A high level source in Neriak confirmed today that the Dark races will not be outdone in the endeavor to acquire new territory and power. The source said, "We believe that this nonsense about a kidnapped princess is just that. If the lesser `Dals and their human and stunted playthings think that they can wander the new lands unchecked, they are quite mistaken."

    Many adventures are already beginning the trip to Kunark, a journey that is not without its perils. A human and halfling party was apparently slaughtered upon their arrival after mistakenly boarding a Kunark-destined ship in the Oasis of Marr. They evidently were not aware that the ship would arrive at a new port, firmly controlled by Neriak and their brutish muscle from Oggok and Grobb.

    Druidic scouts have reported that there are some strange phenomena present throughout the new lands. "The land appears to have been ravaged by some viscous magical storms at one time," said one follower of Karana. "Some magics that we have come to count on just do not work." This phenomenon was apparently noticed to an extreme in the vicinity of a mountain peak rumored to be inhabited by followers of Veeshan. "Only through divine intervention did I escape that place with my life."

    The League of Antonican Bards' representative in Kunark met with Galeth Veredeth, Governor of the Outpost Firiona Via. "It is my sincerest hope that many adventures loyal to the King will assist us on our Quest to return the Princess to her home," says Veredeth. "Though the dangers in these new lands are formidable, I have no doubt that many will come, if for nothing else than to reap the spoils."

The Quest for Kunark, Part IV
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:26 AM PDT
Firiona Vie, Sionachie, Thubr Axebringer, Sir Jevik Isqual, Al`Kabor,
Dreezil Pocketdip, Dagda Icefury, Ognit Eznertob, Dabner Drednever, and
Lyirae Oakwynd arrived at the tiny Elven stronghold in the new lands. Tired
from their journey, they stepped off of the Maiden's Voyage and began to
rest for a few moments. Galeth Veredeth came to greet them as they were
resting.

Galeth Veredeth shouts "Lieutenant Thorn! Please do me the honor of taking
our friends on a tour of the surrounding area"

The Lieutenant approached the group and began to lead them out of town. As
they were walking along, the Lieutenant made an effort to point out the
creatures and structures to Firiona and her companions. He informed them
that the Iksar are an ancient race. The ruins that lie all about the land
show that the Iksar were a great empire whose influence seemed to stretch
the length of the continent. 1900 years ago, the Iksar attempted to attack
and take control of Faydwer but the navy was wiped out by a terrible storm
and they were not successful. Something terrible happened to cause the once
great empire to crumble. None of the local inhabitants of the area seem
very friendly with the exception being the Drixies.

As the party began to walk back, Danak Dhorentath, an Iksar shaman shouted
"Visitors! Watch your backs, the Drolvarg are attacking!" At that moment,
four Drolvarg appeared and ambushed the group. Danak and an Iksar Initiate
joined the group in fighting the Drolvarg. After the Drolvarg were dead,
Danak asked if everyone was alright.

While Firiona was explaining to Danak that they were seeking out the Ring
of Scale, he seemed generally concerned for their safety. He told them
that their journey would be incredibly long and dangerous. Danak offered a
quicker means for them to get there. He offered the use of his special
portal. Within a few moments, with serpentine speed, Danak Dhorentath
grabbed Firiona Vie by the arm. Taking her by surprise he pulled her into
the gate, which disappeared with a hiss leaving nothing more than the smell
of ozone hanging in the air.

"I must return and tell King Thex the terrible news. Bind the vile Iksar
at once! Dagda, Thubr, Jevik and I will escort the prisoner back to the
mainland!" Galeth Veredeth shouted.

They escorted the Initiate to the Maiden's Voyage and traveled to Faydwer.
