Infobit: What is going on in Kael
- ChrisLTD @ 7:19 PM PDT
Absor on the Developer's Corner:

Any chance there will some explanation of why giants in Kael can no longer be charmed? Seems odd that the zone would come down out of the blue and giants no longer charmable. Crowd control? I personally think it was a bad move since giants cannot be charmed... and I have an idea it was due to Avatar of War being killed, but I'm sure if a comment was mad it would clear things up.. Thanks!

I'll do my best to explain the situation.

Most of you are aware that there is still a bit of a bug with the way pets work in the aggro code. In Kael there are a lot of NPCs that can be charmed that are significantly more powerful than any pet. Some folks were using this combination of bug and situation to make encounters much more trivial than they were intended to be.

Fixing the bug will require a full patch, and a largish one at that. We weren't willing to make a large emergency patch, taking down all the servers and requiring all that downtime for our customers to fix a bug being abused in one zone.

This left us with pretty much three options.

1) Allow folks to continue to use the bug to their advantage.
2) Begin punishing people for using the bug until it could be patched.
3) Remove the ability to use the bug in the situation where it was most abusable, Kael, and then fix the bug with the next patch and remove the temporary fix.

We chose the third option.

We don't want people using bugs to their advantage. We feel that it harms the game integrity and the gameplay of those that don't use bugs to their advantage. We couldn't take option one.

We could choose option two, but policing such things requires resources, and banning people isn't something we like to do. This is a new bug, and perhaps people were unsure that it is indeed a bug. And in most circumstances this bug only changes the play style of some people. Only in a few circumstances does this bug become abusable. So fixing the specific circumstance is better than giving warnings to players.

I know the next question, and let me answer it right now. This is a new bug, and it could be argued with some conviction that players using it to their advantage were unaware that it wasn't purposeful code change. In the case of the abuse of safe zones, it is well known that using such locations is against our rules. It can't be reasonably argued that any player with a character over 50th level doesn't understand that.

In any case, we plan to fix the bug with the next patch. Until then, the NPCs in Kael will probably remain uncharmable. We understand that this adversely affects single and double group raids in that zone, and for that we are sorry. But things should be back to normal soon. Assuming anyone knows what normal is anyway…

Code Red Virus II
- ChrisLTD @ 7:37 PM PDT
The EverQuest Operations Team would like you to have some Code Red Virus II Information:

We have become aware that the Code Red Virus is hitting the @home network particularly hard right now. Although this has nothing specifically to do with EverQuest, @home users may experience some potential network problems that could cause some degradation of their network performance. Once the Code Red virus infects a machine, it attempts to infect other machines. Users that have firewall logs may see random IP addresses attempting to access port 80 (which is the HTTP Port). This is the Code Red virus attempting to spread itself. We thought it would be a good idea to let people know about this.

*** UPDATE ***
It appears as though this virus is a variant of the Code Red virus called Code Red II.

*** UPDATE #2 ***
It looks as if this virus is all over the place now, not just on @home. Many reports are coming in about it hitting Time Warner (RoadRunner) networks as well as many, many others.

Check out the Network Status archives on the Official Network Status page.

Wizard Faire on Fennin Ro
- Aurum @ 7:34 PM PDT
I received the following from Dynamite Shortfuze:

Hail, Wizards of Fennin Ro!

I come before you once again, as I have in the past, with news of
this month's Wizard Faire! I have been collecting runes earnestly in
anticipation of this month's faire, and I hope all of you have been
doing the same!

Firstly, I would like to apologize to one and all for my lack of
presence last month. I was there for the first half hour or so, but then
my ISP went down for the rest of that night and I was unable to attend
properly. I received several tells and emails, however, thanking me for
organising the event, and with a couple requests pertaining to the time.
These requests shall be honored, but first I would like to share with
you some exciting news.

Taedirk Fyreslinger, fellow wizard of Fennin Ro, has been wanting to
organize a language faire, and we have decided that we could hold our
faires together for all wizards to benefit. To this end, we would like
to organise the language training area near a zone line. This must be
easily accessible by most, and must be in a neutral area where none
shall worry of being KOS. As the rune trade is normally held at the
Toxxulia wizard spires, Taedirk suggested that we do this at the Kerra
Ridge zoneline, which I feel is a good idea. If anybody has any
objections, or feels that there is a better place to do this, then we
will welcome all suggestions.

At the present time, the details of the faire are as follows:

--

Where: Toxxulia Wizard spires / Kerra Ridge
Starts: 4 PM PST ( 7 PM EST ) Saturday 4th August
Finishes: When we're done! =)

--

For newcomers to the faire, the concept is simple: bring all your
runes and research materials to the faire. If you are in need of a rune,
then ask for it, and if anyone has one they are not in need of, then you
will get it. Consequentially, if you have a rune that you have no need
of, and someone else asks for it, then it is expected that you will give
that rune to your fellow wizard. The language trading part, I will leave
to Taedirk to decide the rules at the faire, but I imagine people will
be split into groups of five students and one teacher and then go about
learning.

This faire is held for the community, by the community, and we are
all here to help each other. Even if you haven't any research to do, I
strongly encourage you to come out and meet all your fellow wizards.We
can only grow stronger as a community, and we can only grow better as we
gain friends.

Even though this faire is primarily geared towards wizards, I invite
everyone to attend. We have had mages, enchanters, even warriors and
monks visit us in the past, and we continue to welcome any who wish to
join us. The community is not restricted to any one class, and friends
do not come in any specific race or class.

As I have in the past, I will put forth an effort to brew sufficient
refreshments to appease the masses, but I am only one person. If you
practice a trade skill, then I encourage you wholeheartedly to bring
with you the product of your trade. Taedirk, I know, makes some of the
finest lizard on a stick I've had yet, and I hope he will bring some of
his own work to share with us, as I hope that you will bring some of
your own.

If you have any questions, comments, concerns, or ideas pertaining
to the faire, please contact me at fenninrunes@hotmail.com. I will get
back to you as soon as I possibly can, and I hope to see you, one and
all, at the faire.

Dynamite Shortfuze
Fennin Ro
Thanks!

Absor Infobits: The Afternoon Edition
- Aurum @ 4:30 PM PDT
A few updates were made this afternoon by Alan VanCouvering on the Developer's Corner:

    Roleplaying Server
    Majelix wrote:

    [Quote: I realize you are busy with SoL, and the "RP server" hasn't gotten much more than beyond the discussion table at Verant, but I'd like to ask, will the race/class restrictions be removed for the rp server?

    IE, it would be fun to play a halfling paladin or a drunken ogre bard. Of course, this presents the problem of the odd combinations becoming common, which is exactly why you disallow them on normal servers.

    Rather than race/class restrictions, perhaps you could have diety/class restrictions? IE, since iksars -have- worship CT, they can not be paladins. ]

    It seems unlikely that we'll change any of the class/race combinations for this server. There is no roleplaying need to do so, and there are no balance or gameplay issues that would be created by this server ruleset (unlike Sullon Zek) that would require such changes.

    No doubt someone could come up with a reason for a halfling to be a paladin, or an ogre to be a bard. But the race/class combinations are set up as they are to represent the way things are in Norrath.

    And if there is some concern that there would be a disproportionately large number of halfling paladins in Norrath on a regular server (though I've never heard anyone voice a concern over it…), then the same would apply on a roleplaying server.

    Sullon Zek Point System
    [Quote: Absor,

    When you say you will lose 5 points per level if the victim is 6 or more levels lower, is that on top of the base points per kill? IE, a level 30 kills a level 24... will they get 95 points, or -5 points?

    Sugarplum ]

    A level 30 killing a level 24 (for the first time in over an hour) would get 100 points, less 5 points for each level lower because the victim was more than 5 levels lower than the killer.


    So it would be 100 – [5*(30-24)] = 70 points.

    Whereas if the victim were one level higher, 25th, the killer would get 100 – [2*(30-25)] = 90 points.

    The idea here is to reduce the value of killing folks that don’t /con as even to you.

    [Quote: QUOTE:

    "Look for a News section on the Sullon board for more info in the near future.

    Hobart"

    Where is this 'Sullen Board' could we get a link? ]

    I think I listed the link earlier in the thread, but here it is again.

    http://everquest.station.sony.com/stats/sz_summary.jsp

    There isn't a news section up there yet, though. That's coming… soon. :)

    Fear_SZ wrote:

    [Quote: You need to update the points then on the link from the stats page, it still says 2 points per level, not 5.
    Thanks. ]

    Well, your e-mail arrived on Sunday at about 8 am. It seems that someone has already updated the rules. They're on the ball, that's for sure.

    Grumples wrote:

    [Quote: I think there may be an error in the SZ point calculation.

    According to the updated points rules, we still get 100+2x(level difference) for even con kills.

    Yesturday I killed two even cons and did not die. I got 225 points.

    For me to get 112.5 points per kill, they would have had to have been:
    100+2Z = 112.5
    Z = 6.25 levels over me. ]

    Let's look at that again.

    You didn't give your level, and it might matter, but killing someone 5 levels higher than you would produce 110 points. Two of them would produce 220 points. This leaves 5 points unexplained until you remember that insignia also give you points. I'm not sure how you would get exactly 5 points from them in this fashion. The point system might not be perfect, but it's pretty close.

    [Quote: They weren't red though, so I know that's not true.

    It appears you are using 100+5x(level difference) for all kills now? ]

    If it was, the numbers would be a lot more off in the scenario you described.

    [Quote: This is fine by me, I think it's better -- level difference should count for more going up as well as down, I'm just not sure it's Working As Intended.

    Also -- does this mean if a level 60 kills a level 20, he gets 100+5*(-40) = -100 points? Or is there a minimum cap?

    I'm somewhat worried about getting negative points for kills, because if you're level 60, a level 54 will con as green as a level 20. You won't realize he's so low until you blast him dead in one shot and lose 100 points. ]

    Yes, a level 60 killing a level 20 will lose 100 points.

    Right now we don’t plan to add minimum cap. If you are a level 60 character that blasts everyone you see without conning them, then you'll lose points. If you're a level 60 hanging out in a level 20 dungeon killing folks, you'll probably loose points.

    Slowmotionz_SZ wrote:

    [Quote: Under this change a level 60 defending themselfs from a level 20 would lose 100 points just for defending themselfs. Kinda screwed up if you ask me. I assume your going to make it so 0 is the minimum you can have from this penalty (with -25 per kill after that beinga ble to cause negative points)]

    This level 60 'defending' himself from a level 20 would know that this was a person that would cause him point loss. A player with a 60th level character will be able to tell that the person that ambushed him is casting spells or doing damage that does not match with a character over 30th level or so. He will have the choice to make a dignified retreat, or to teach the person a lesson and blow them to bits.

    But there's really no chance that the situation you described will cause the 60th level character to be losing those points without at least understanding that they will lose points for killing their opponent.

    The penalty exists to reduce the… bottom feeding. In any case, 0 would never be the lower limit. A penalty is required to reduce the desire for folks to blow up those that are just way low level. Right now, a 60th level character could toast a 40th level character with no penalty. That seems like a pretty large range. And, well, the effective limit is –100 points. :)

    [Quote: Can someone please explain how points are given for PvP before level 20? I /petition'ed last night and was informed that, yes, PvP before level 20 does yield points.
