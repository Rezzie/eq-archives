EQ Producer Brad McQuaid talked about the downtime the Nameless server experienced this weekend. Here's the first post:

    Actually, I think this has happened 3 times in the history of EQ... maybe 4.

    It does suck. Thank God it happens very rarely thanks to the RAID setup on our machines. Basically, 2 drives have to go bad around the same time for this to happen.

New Patch Message
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:21 PM PST
The following Patch Message has been added to the Patch Server:

    Emergency Patch April 1, 2000
    -------------------------------------

    The EQ servers were quickly brought down this morningfor an emergency patch update. We'd apologize once againfor taking the servers down, but what would be the point?

    The following changes were made:

    1.) Wizards - We've finally given Wizards a few quick"Insta-Death" spells. Our hope is that this will makeall the wizards shut up for a little while, until theystart to complain about lack of plate armor for their class.

    2.) Druids - Because of the over-abundance of Druids in Norrath, the next timeyou login one out of every three Druids will be randomly reassigned a newclass.

    3.) Warriors and Monks - These melee classes have been given magical abilities at last.They now can bind, gate, heal and summon food.

    4.) Magicians and Enchanters - New pet spell added: Nagafen. We've given these two classes a fine new pet to helpthem in their everyday struggles in Norrath.

    5.) Rogues - We feel this class is our crowning achievement. We believe it has been perfectly balanced, and as suchwill not receive any modifications for the rest of EverQuest's life.

    6.) Hybrids (Rangers, Paladins, Shadow Knights) - After careful data analysis we've come to the conclusion that simply giving these classes a few of their spellcaster counterparts spells is not enough. So thus we've given them all the spells they would normally receive had they been a Druid, Cleric or Necromancer.

    In addition to these changes, the Necromancer and Cleric classes will be overhauled in the near future! Unfortunately if you already have a character that is one of these two classes you will loose all your items and be randomly renamed.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:02 PM PST
Good grief has Gordon been busy today. In the past 24 hours Mr. Wrinn has made over 60 posts on the Gameplay board alone. Here's a rundown of some of his latest:

    Quest Structure
    We're not at all opposed to quests giving out real (e.g. tangible) experience, however that's really not going to be possible given the current architecture. The problem is that we need to have the ability to store completion-info as part of the character. This would keep the same character from doing the same quest over and over again, which is what keeps us from doing things like giving out half-a-level for a tough quest. Tracking this unfortunately isn't possible without doing a player-wipe. The Quest engine could be improved in many other ways, but they all require the ability to know exactly what a character has or has not done.

    Hybrid Debate (Click here for original post)
    Wow! These are probably some of the best arguments I've seen. I am impressed...however...

    Hybrids don't get teleports, not even Gate. Only two classes get teleports, and even though rangers are a hybrid of one of those classes, there's really no compelling reason to make an exception.

    Money and the PoS
    If you are paying 3Kpp per person to go to the Plane of Sky, there's only one reason: You want to. The zone need not cost a cent after the first trip in. Deaths can be handled with resurrect. Heck, you can even get by a few of the key requirements using death, resurrect, and summon-corpse (this one costs money though).

    Now of course if you want to bring more people than there are keys, or skip by the first island, it's going to cost you. We want the Plane of Sky to remain challenging regardless of how many warm bodies you can throw at it.

    Skill Caps and Individuality
    [Quote from original post: What about giving characters one generic skillcap point per level that they could assign to any skill? Or… implement some quests that can raise skillcaps for different skills. A 50th level character could have assigned 50 skillcap points to one skill raising its effectiveness by 10 levels. If this is too much there could be a skillcap addition cap for certain skills.]

    First, just because you stop gaining in a particular skill doesn't mean that your skill level does not go up. For instance, a level 50 person with a 200 skill in 1HS does not hit like a level 40 person, they hit like a level 50 person because their is more to calculating it than just the skill.

    Secondly, please keep in mind that all of this stuff is balanced based upon the caps and how they work now. We just can't arbitrarily decide to start letting people increase their skill-caps on a whim. Sure, it would be neat, but since things are currently balanced, the only way that something like this could go in is if we substantially nerfed the skills so that any net gain would be zero.

    Finally, enabling what you ask for here would not diverse characters. Skill-based systems never do. Everyone gravitates towards having whatever skills are perceived to be ideal. This has been persistent flaw in every skill-based MMORPG architecture that has been developed. That’s why we chose to go with a class-based system.

    Drops in PoH
    Just trying to verify your logic: The drop percentages in Hate are low so people with armor in Hate will play longer? I suppose that means that since the drops in Fear are higher, we must want everyone with armor there to quit.

    Please... We have Hate updated on the Test server and hope to patch it live the next time we do data. Probably next week.

    Bonus Skill Points
    Well I can say that we don't have any intention of including bonus points. It's certainly possible for people to work up any skills that they need, the inclusion of more points would just serve to save them some time, and go a long way towards making point meaningless in the first place. Save them up!

    Kunark and International Orders
    Our plan is to have the Expansion in everyone's hands prior to the official launch, even the overseas people. That's our plan, and it is looking good so far. However we really won't be able to nail it down further for a bit yet.

    (Click here for original post)
    Unfortunately, this is the way resurrection works, and it's not considered a bug. If the resurrection doesn't go through on the first time for whatever reason (e.g. Zone, cancel, LD, etc.), the experience cannot be restored.
    [Quote from original post: I disagree with the behavior not being considered a bug. A res should give the exp, period. No, two reses should NOT give any more exp than one res.]

    I understand your opinion, however when making coding decisions, the programmers have to decide how many contingencies they are willing to trap. There are functions that you can perform in the game that account for all different kinds of things that can go wrong, such as everything that can go wrong in a trade.

    Resurrection is one of those things where not everything possible is trapped. It makes resurrection fairly black-and-white, but has the side-effect of missing them under certain circumstances.

    Resurrection Bug
    [Quote from original post: I too recently lost the exp from missing the first res attempt. What happened was, I was waiting a while on the cleric to med and res, and going through inventory. The window popped up as I clicked on an inventory item - and clicking on the item made the res window disappear!!]

    Yeah, I'll buy that that is a bug with the confirmation-box code. You would think that a confirmation box wouldn't disappear unless you actually click on something. In *most* cases, it's safe to assume the answer is "NO", which is how the item-destroy confirmation works, but I can see this being a problem in reference to a resurrect. I'm not sure if this can be worked around, but I'll bring it to the Team.

    Fear and Charm in PvP
    Fear and Charm spells have been disabled in the PvP environment for some time due to abuses that surround them. They made it possible for all kinds of bad things to happen, including intentionally causing experience loss and they allowed people to bypass the PvP level limits.

    When this was done, AE Fear spells continued to work due to how those spells interact with the players. This latest patch merely fixed that issue, causing PCs to be immune to them as well as the single target spells from before.

    On Reported Bard Bug
    GZ has vowed to take another look at it, though no progress has been made. He's fairly tied up with trying to get the expansion spells in order. Due to the complexity of the spell system, we prefer to have only one person with the ability to modify spells.

    Terms and Phrases
    [Quote from original post: Just time for another "pop" word I think...."Uber-guild" is dead....both the word and the definition.]

    Hmmm...

    Overguild
    Superguild
    Surguild
    Pas Sous Guild
    Powerguild
    Capo-guild
    Bossguild
    |3W|_guild
    Getsmolewtguild

    Hmmm... Maybe we should just stick with Uberguild

    Pro Asheron's Call Posts on the Official Boards
    We really don't mind seeing the posts on the board proclaiming one game better than another. Everyone is entitled to their opinion, and that opinion means so much more when it is tested. It's one thing for us to talk about the positive aspects of EQ when people haven't tried another game. It's another thing when they've tried the other games and then affirmatively choose EQ.

    Thank you for continuing to choose EQ.

    Magician Spells
    Breaking a moment from not speaking about 50+ spells, I'd like to say that magicians will keep Malosi, it just won't be as a level 49 spell.

    DoT Spells
    There's a difference between having one message that's sent to one person, and having one message that's broadcast to several. For things like Taunt and Spell damage, it would result in an increase of network traffic that would be problematic :(

    Do Celebirties Play EQ?

Don't tell me that you don't want them to be pushovers, just possible. They are possible, and take the same amount of work based on experienced no matter what the size of the guild going after it.

    There are things that take more than one group to kill. People would be pretty well up-the-creek on creatures like that if corpses didn't automatically unlock after a given time. It also ads a nice aspect to player interaction. Following a group around and looting their leavings can be a good way to make money.

    Vendor Locations
    Not every city has everything. That's just one of the design aspects of the game, and is there to encourage people to travel around to get the goods that they need. It's that way to add some distinction to the cities and give people reason to visit them.
