Absor on Naming Policy
[22 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:11 PM PST
Alan addressed the latest controversy to grace the pages of the Gameplay Board last night:

    Last night on Morell Thule, the guild Tolkien's Revenge was disbanded after being around for almost half a year. The reason submitted for being disbanded, copyright violation. Give me a break, almost everywhere you look, you will see character names and guilds borrowing from popular fantasy literature. Is Verant gonna disband every guild they see for copyright violation? Are you gonna disband Fires of Heaven for being a copyright violation of a book in Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time series? I doubt it... I really do. Of all the lame excuses for disbanding a guild, this has to be right up there. If you are interested in any screenpics of a conversation with a guide about the disbanding of Tolkiens Revenge, go to http://www.wyrmfire.net/deleted/deleted.htm

    Let me wade in here with my opinion.

    First off, keep in mind that any naming convention must be ruled over by people, and judgement calls must be made.

    Tolkien's Revenge, in my opinion, as a guild name clearly violates the naming rules. Tolkien is a famous figure in fantasy literature. Arguably the founder of the whole genre (I've read that it was because of The Hobbit that book stores created fantasy/sci-fi sections in their stores). Using his name in a guild name is similar to using our president's name (even if we don't know who he is for sure).

    There is no question in my mind that the guild name violated our policies, in fact and in spirit.

    Alan

Patch Message: 12-07-00/12-08-00
[62 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:06 AM PST
Two new patch messages have been posted very recently on the Patch Server:

    December 8th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Velious Subscribers*

    Please run the "Velious Patch" off of the patch program.

    *New Patch*

    We appreciate everyone's continued patience over the past couple of days, and are happy to see that the problems that have been experienced are relatively minor compared to those of the past. Tonight's patch is expected to be the last one we'll have to do for a while, so we hope to have you enjoying Velious over the weekend non-stop.

    Today's patch corrects the following issues:

    - The user interface problems that occurred as of last night have been addressed, such as window positioning and transparency. Also addressed were problems with targeting, enabling and disabling windows, and switching to and from resolutions above 1280x1024.
    - Corrected a problem that would cause some Voodoo card users to lock up when killing giants.
    - Added messages for some new skills that some NPCs have (e.g. Rampage, and Flurry).
    - Fixed items and item drops in a number of zones.

    Enjoy your weekend!

    - The EverQuest Team


    December 7th, 4:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Patch Day*

    Today's patch addresses a few issues brought up by players over the last several days:

    - Fixed an issue with /split that would cause people to go linkdead in the event that they used the command in quick succession.
    - Fixed a bug where you could not edit socials or skills (via the hotbutton window right click) in fullview mode if you had hidden your "MainMenu" window.
    - Fixed a bug where you would not see hotbuttons in "normal" mode if you had hidden that window in fullview mode.
    - Fixed it so you can't use hotbuttons when looting, trading, banking, or using a merchant in fullview mode (which is how it is in normal mode, but with the inventory working in fullview mode now it wasn't blocked properly for that).
    - Fixed the message for the /dynamiclights command to report properly.
    - Fixed a problem where INI setting were not being read properly if you had camped or went LD to server select then reentered the game. This was causing various UI problems with window positions and colors being wrong.
    - Fixed a problem with Sirens Grotto - Some people entering the zone were getting stuck.
    - Fixed a problem with passage from Skyshrine to Cobalt Scar. Travel in that direction was supposed to require a key.
    - We believe we fixed a problem where NPCs sometimes would hit a pet-owner no matter how far away the owner was from the pet and the NPC.
    - Corrected loot drops in Thurgadin

    We again appreciate patience while we stabilize following Velious launch.

    - The EverQuest Team

    Well, we're doing our best to minimize the impact of these patches. But they are necessary, or we wouldn't do it. I'm sorry that these things are inconveniencing our customers, that's never our intent.

    Honestly, this release has been much smoother than Ruins of Kunark. The problems that we are encountering have rarely been devastating. And considering the shorter production time on this product and the ambitious nature of the product, I think we've done a fair job.

  • Nerfed SoV Items
    This was, by no stretch of the imagination an injustice to anyone.

    We changed the weapon into something that was appropriate for the creature that it dropped from. The item that was dropping there shouldn't have been dropping.

    We corrected our mistake.

    Leaving it as it was would have been the greater injustice. It would have promoted the idea that getting the expansion first entitled a player to items that they gained due to an error in our data.

    I can't comment on the reasons for past actions about matters like this. I personally feel that this is the proper response to this situation.

    I am sorry that we had to change that item. It wasn't done to anger anyone. It was done because it should have been done, to keep things in balance. There was no intent to 'bait and switch' anyone here. Yes, we missed this problem, but we fixed it as fast as we could. I'm sorry if any of you had your items changed.

    In cases like this, we should always choose to correct the mistake, not just ignore it by leaving the broken items in the game. The Griffon Sword was intended as a top item off a top mob. Leaving it in would have made getting it in the appropriate way pretty worthless.

Teleporter Changes
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:03 PM PST
The Skyshrine teleporter will be changing soon. Here are the details from Alan:

    The teleporter that takes you from Skyshrine to the Cobalt Scar is being changed. This was intended to be a keyed teleporter (a key that you need to look for).

    Unfortunately, it wasn't set properly, and characters were able to use the teleporter without the key. It was intended that characters acquire this key in order to make their way into the Scar.
