Absor Infobits
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:57 PM PST
Alan roared back on the Gameplay Board with a vengeance with several posts on a very broad range of topics:

  • The Death of Necros
    Demrack wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "Ok, so if this is to be our only role in large raids where is the necro mana version of the cleric complete heal?"]

    Not at all. There are other things that a necromancer can do. You all eschew the use of pets on raids, but they work just fine against any creature that doesn't use fear. And there is Dead Man Floating, summoning corpses…

    I'm not going to pretend to know everything about how a necromancer works. My concern here is that some of you are calling necromancers useless in any big raid, when it's not true. You may not be able to do a ton of damage to the boss monster, but that is by no means all there is to doing a raid. If you see massive damage dealing as the only way that your necromancer is useful, then that is where we disagree.

    Musicicus said:

    [Quote from original post: "First off clerics are used in Raids more favorably because not only can they heal, but if the raid goes bad, they can return the exp of the bad raid."]

    And a necromancer can return the corpses so that the cleric can work… No, necromancers have as much to do post and pre raid as any other class, and more than some. And I don't hear most of you concerned with that. The major concern here seems to be that at the 'big' fight you are relegated to a support role, and you don't like that (and that you're not exceedingly efficient at it). In other parts of a raid, clearing or recovering, a necromancer is as useful as any other class (yes, even clerics). I hear that druids are in a similar position. On a raid all they do is a bit of healing and other support stuff…

    Oh, and you mentioned DoTs making corpses poof.

    They don't. As far as we can tell, it hasn't happened in months. We haven't been able to make it happen. At least not on anything like a raid-level mob. If anyone has seen a high level NPC corpse poof from a DoT in the last three months, please let me know and tell me exactly how and where and when and what. High level mobs shouldn't poof anymore. We've coded it so that it just shouldn't happen. It will happen on rare occasions for lower level stuff, and that's probably not going to change (though we are looking at this whole thing again right now). But the high-level-raid-boss-monster-type-NPC corpses shouldn't poof at all due to a fix we made months ago. Don't let that stop you from using DoTs.

    LordZemus said:

    [Quote from original post: "But let's face it. Yes there is a drawback to our soloing power, and you can't complain of it. First off, having a necromancer or two on a raid is a good safety net. Do you know anyone else who can fd and res? And yes, your guildmates will be willing to let you sacrifice some secondaries once they notice you're the only one left at wipeouts. Cleric or wizard used too much mana? Pump them. Enchanter getting pummeled by a mob? ST, turn your pet's taunt back on (you do leave it off for raids, don't you?) and send it, or cast anything out of the Heart Flutter line, guaranteed to make the mob want to eat you. Throw a shadowbound out to save that tank, because Bond of Death and Deflux WILL hit every mob except the last one, despite these asinine complaints. Oh, and your DoTs do hit. Try using the right ones."]

    Some folks don't agree with the idea that a class that is exceptionally powerful at all other portions of the game might have to take a power-hit in this one part of the game. And it's not such a big hit, as there are still several useful things that make a necromancer useful on a raid. Thanks for pointing some of them out.

    GeistTallon wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "LordZemus:

    I'm suspicious of you even being a necro. Half the stuff you said doesn't work at all, or isn't a viable option 90% of the time."]

    I'd love to read your counter arguments to those points of his that you don't agree with.

    Aerik N'Krosis wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "I wish they'd make out pets immune to fear at least (like a mindless undead should be!). Maybe use the owner's resistances for the pet? Or else lower the ubermob resistances now that they can have more than 32k HPs. This way we necros could at least DoT them and make a contribution to the fight that is in keeping with the original PURPOSE of the necromancer."]

    It seems pretty unlikely that things that are currently immune to spells will be made less immune. But I kinda like the idea of making powerful undead pets immune to fear… In any case, rest assured that I'm passing this thread to the team to read, so it's not like I'm final arbiter on anything you all suggest.

    Mongo wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "1. Ignite/Chill Bones: There is no functional purpose for the skeleton effect. The coolness factor fades very quickly, and the effect has the potential for making an epic encounter harder.
    Solution - remove the illusion. These spells do land on some epic encounters, but they're forbidden because of the effect. With the illusion gone, Necromancers would have the ability to cast DD spells during these encounters. With no functional purpose to the illusion, is there an actual reason to keep it?"]

    This is something that I want to look into further.

    [Quote from original post: "2. DOT spells: Very simply, corpses still do poof at times when using these spells. This effects not only epic encounters, but really any encounter that may have a desired drop.
    Solution - With as hard as this has been apparently to recreate on a consistent basis, I don't know what kind of programming solution may be available. If the problem itself cannot be reliably fixed, is there anything that could be established so that (for example) any loot on that monster would remain in play on the ground? That may not be possible, but this needs to be addressed, directly or indirectly."]

    They shouldn't be poofing any more. Not on NPCs over 55th level, which would include anything considered a raid-level encounter.

    [Quote from original post: "3. Mana-dumping: Necromancers make very inefficient manastone's on these raids for the simple reason that they cannot draw mana from the mob. If this is what Necromancer's are left with on epic encounters, it should at least be somewhat efficient.
    Solution - if making Lifetaps usable is not an option, could the mana-transfer spells be looked at so they are viable in these situations? Certainly, most would prefer that taps be usable, but if Verant more or less officially sees this as the role of Necromancers on these type of raids, at least make it a viable, efficient one."]

    Changing the resistance to Lifetap is probably not an option. And making mana transfers more efficient would be a balancing nightmare…

    Let me suggest to all of you that there is no reason that your role in this one part of the game can't be different than it is in all other parts of the game. For example, enchanters don't do 'crowd control' on, say Trakonon. Their role there is different than what they do in, say, Guk or wherever. I, personally, don't see a changed role in one part of the post 50 game as a bad thing, not at all. Whether that role is useful enough, now that might be something worth talking about. But a complaint based solely on the fact that your role may be different at one point of the game than it is in others isn't something that I can fully support. I'm not saying that it's not a valid complaint, I just think that it's more of a personal opinion than a threat to the value of the class. And where one personal opinion varies from another… there may be as many necromancers that like being able to support their group on big raids as a change from just beating things up. I don't know, and it's hard for me to know. It's important to keep that in mind during this discussion.

    It would be more useful to explain all that to me in response to the thread that you are responding to. Making a new thread sort of takes everything out of context...

  • Bug Fixed: NPCs Hitting Caster Through Pets
    A recent patch fixed a bug that was allowing NPCs to hit pet owners no matter how far away they were. The problem occurred when three NPCs were already attacking a pet. Any additional NPCs would swing at and hit the owner of the pet, no matter how far away he was.

    This was fixed, but we forgot to announce it. I thought you'd like to know.

  • Bard Son Bugs?
    Fair enough. I'll pass that on to the team.

    SSoT (the 51 song) no message.

  • "Suspended For Too Long"
    I don't know the specifics of your situation. But I want to clear something up about suspensions.

    I have been told that they generally last 7 to 10 business days. This would usually not include weekends and holidays, so it's possible that you may have a few more days to go.

  • Have there been pet level changes?
    The only change that we've made to pet levels at all recently was the change that made it so that pets summoned with the 39th level spells would automatically raise themselves to 36th level if they were lower level than that. This only affects that particular spell. It was not any sort of global summoning change, and had no effect on the summoning spell that you are talking about.

    It's possible that you've just been having a run of bad luck.

  • Archery Damage Problem
    Yes, bow damage was unintentionally capped recently. We'll be changing it back when we patch next.

    The reasoning behind it this mistake is pretty complicated. Basically, there was a damage cap already in place, but it wasn't being used. When we were setting up stuff for the new discipline we activated that cap (that's how it was intended to work when first designed, I guess).

    Basically, we'll be putting it back as it was soon. I'm sorry about the mistake.

  • "DOTS Should Stack"
    ShockaZuulu wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "This is no different than 2 wizards landing 2 ice comets. The only difference is your limitations in programming."]

    Sure there is. Damage over time spells are more efficient than direct damage spells. Spell effect stacking is the trade off for more damage at a lower mana cost.

    I do agree, though, that the whole thing with corpses disappearing needs to be resolved.

  • Welcome To Negative Respect (Two Posts From Alan, One From Brad)
    I'm going to respond only to the original post. It would take me hours to respond to the whole thing in any detail at all… :)

    AuntieShaynta:

    Well, I stand by my 'victims' statement. I'm not going to argue it with you, except to suggest that perhaps you didn't read it as I intended to write it. The fault there would lie with me, I understand. I could spend another ten posts discussing it, but that would be redundant. I'm sorry if you don't agree with me. But I see no reason for that to make you respect me less. That's something I'll have to think about.

    [Quote from original post: "Instead, what you (Verant) did was to purposefully and knowingly give an ability to other classes and purposefully and knowingly did not give enchanters this ability -- nor any form of compensation." ]

    Yes, we did. And we didn't give it to the other… minor pet classes either. We gave it to the classes that we consider primary pet classes. I honestly see nothing wrong with this. And I see no logic behind the idea that one class should be 'compensated' for things done to other classes. Should we, then, reduce the power of your class any time we reduce the power of another? I think you're failing to see the larger picture. We didn't make that choice to spite anyone, be they enchanter or shaman or any other class (in a way, rogues could use your argument and want to know why they were not also compensated for something that the these other classes were given).

    [Quote from original post: "1) I was told by Verant that I would be able to enchant weapons, including the ability to enchant weapons to cast spells. I have seen this degraded several times despite a direct promise from Brad that enchanters enchanting weapons WOULD be implemented "in the long run". Now I see several spells that appear to be the "enchant weapon" spells -- being given to other classes. I'm reserving judgement, frankly against my better judgement."]

    I've been following this game as long as anyone, longer than most. I've seen what you saw too, though not in exactly the same way.

    At first the idea of enchanting weapons was just that, an idea. It became a 'something we'd really like to do' thing before beta. During beta it became a 'we really want to do it, but it doesn't look like we can' thing. At release it was the same way, something that they wanted to do. I don't remember any promises, from Brad or anyone else. But I might have missed that.

    I can't say I disagree with you about the apparent ability that another class might or might not be getting to do something that seems very similar to enchanting an item. Personally, I plan to push for that ability, if it exists, to be given to enchanters in some way. But there may be technical issues that neither you nor I are aware of. So let's both wait and see. Though I kinda fail to see how any of this make me less respectable… Remember, you were talking about Absor, not Verant…

    [Quote from original post: "2) I saw the by-any-definition poorly-handled "mage pets aren't dual wielding" fiasco solved in what had to be the second poorest way possible -- requiring giving pets weapons but removing the delay-reducing benefit of doing so. A blatant nerf to the two classes that could already have a dual wielding pet by giving them weapons. One of those classes got an admittedly small compensations. The other was told "You're not a pet class" -- despite the fact we get pets at every spell level pre-50. I took this in stride, but it was noted.

    3) Now I see what should have been the solution to the mage-pet-dual-wielding fiasco being put in much later -- and once again enchanter pets are purposefully left out. Without compensation. In fact, with a mild attendent nerf (I can't attest to this myself, as I haven't even seen a SoR yet ingame) of our pets not proc'ing as much as they used to." ]

    And I guess your disagreement is with the idea that enchanters are not a pet class. They are a secondary pet class. There are two primary pet classes, magicians and necromancers. All others are secondary pet classes. *shrug* I can see how you might not like that definition, but that is the definition (we not only 'get' to set those definitions, we 'must' set those definitions or the classes wouldn't be balanced at all). Enchanters are unique in their way, as you say. They get pets at all levels. But you'll note that they also have very weak pets, in comparison. That is by design.

    I think the problem here is perspective. Mine is that enchanters are secondary pet classes, and therefore they shouldn't have pets that get to dual wield without a weapon. Yours is that you are a pet class and therefore your pets deserve that ability. I don't know what I can say to change your perspective. And I don't know what you can do to change mine. I only wish you could understand why we made that choice. It's really not a matter of 'compensation' for anything done in the past. It's a matter of how it should be. We can't sacrifice the balance of the game just to compensate for things we might have done in the past.

    [Quote from original post: " What's more, I know you (Verant) aren't going to correct any of the "core" problems with EQ. Everything is subject to change, it seems, except the things that most need changing." ]

    What are the core problems? I'm curious about your take on that. Certainly the fact that enchanters don't make magic items isn't a core problem. It might be the core of your personal dissatisfaction with your favorite class, one that I can't deny. I wish it was in there too. But enchanters work, they work well. They are unique, they fit into the game, they… they work. Do they work as they were initially intended? Maybe, but not entirely. Do they work the way you want them to? It seems not. That's unfortunate. But enchanters are still a rockin' class, and they have some of the coolest abilities of any class in game (and I'm not talking about Clarity, I'm talking about illusions).

Father Pleads Guilty in Death of Son
[92 Comments] - Aurum @ 12:23 PM PST
We always tend to get a good laugh, or roll our eyes at unfortunate individuals who end up spending their lives in EverQuest and then get interviewed in some article or go on some talk show because they goofed up in real-life. These people give the MMORPG genre a bad name and help popular culture associate the people who these games as stereotypical losers with no lives. As the webmaster of this site I've come across and met such people who unfortunately have little self-control...

...and until this day I thought I'd seen it all as far as real-life incidents caused by the result of someone overplaying, causing some sort of disaster, and then subsequently blaming it on EQ. (Remeber, it's always the game's fault. Never the individual.)

The following excerpt, from an article published today on the St. Petersburg Times (a Tampa Bay newspaper), is just sickening:

    TAMPA -- As his 9-month-old son cried last July, Tony Lamont Bragg Sr.'s mind was on something else: a computer game called Everquest.

    Authorities believe Bragg squeezed Tony Jr. to keep him quiet, then left him unattended in a utility closet in their Temple Terrace apartment for more than 24 hours. The boy died.

    On Tuesday, Bragg, 24, pleaded guilty to aggravated manslaughter and was sentenced to 15 years in prison.

    Bragg had been playing the online fantasy role-playing computer game for hours and appeared to get annoyed by his son's crying, said prosecutor Suzanne Rossomondo.
*shakes his head and goes back to work*

  • The Simple Art of Online Role Playing: A Practical Guide
    That’s right, the simple “art” of role-playing. Maybe some of you reading that headline scoffed, but the fact can’t be denied: there is no science to role-playing, no method that can be easily applied to ensure success. That said, “art” is the only remaining classification broad enough to cover the myriad of elements that can come into play when “role playing” a character. It may sound a bit high-minded, but it’s true all the same, and the sooner all us online RPer’s accept it, the sooner we’ll realize that there’s more involved in successfully role playing a character than simply choosing an unpronounceable moniker and chopping up monsters. Yet, realization and implementation are vastly different things, so, to make the transition easier for you gaming masses, I’ve decided to write this article and provide some simple guidelines. So read on brave warrior/cleric/thief/druid/paladin/ranger/wizard/magician/enchanter…sheesh…

  • The Name Police Strike Again
    Every time I play EverQuest I'm confronted by characters named after rock
    stars, real world companies, other famous personages or names that just
    don't fit into the game. It bugs me and it detracts from the game
    atmosphere. Fortunately EverQuest has a naming policy that, as vague as it
    is, does not permit characters to have such names. Unfortunately all of
    the characters I'm talking about are computer controlled NPCs. Apparently
    Verant Interactive doesn't see fit to abide by its own policies. No big
    deal, right? After all, it is their game and they can do with it as they
    wish. I think the issue goes a bit deeper than that.

    -By Rolli Polli

    There comes a time when a tale of an adventurer’s life must be told. Perhaps now it’s time for mine.

    My name is Connavar Demonblade, a Wolf of the North and Guild Officer of the Swords of Rhigante. A burden lies across my soul and only through sharing it with all of you can it be lifted. Have you ever asked yourself the question “Why am I here?” or “What is my purpose?” Until recently, I did not understand the importance of those questions, meaningless babble from those educated, well-mannered nobles.


    It was one of those nobles, that drove me across Norrath on a quest that would change my life and that of those with me. Are you settled? Then let me begin.
