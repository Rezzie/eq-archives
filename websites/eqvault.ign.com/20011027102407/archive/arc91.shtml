Deciding What's Best
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:42 PM PDT
I spotted the following thread on the Gameplay Board which contains post Gordon Wrinn created:

    Who are you to decide whats best for the game?

    I think the people should decide whats best for EQ.
    If melee want to bind themselves in the city. Let them.

    If people feel that classes are unbalanced ask them why and make those changes.

    It is the people that make the game. Isn't it?

    Or you may not see people sign-up for Star Wars or EQ2 later.

    Frankly Brian, as the designers of the game, we are the ones with personal and financial stakes in its success. Someone (or some entity) has to be the final arbiter of what is good or not good for the game, and it this case it is those people on the EQ tuning team. The people, those who choose to voice their opinion, whether on the boards, through email or feedback, or with their monthly subscription and the people they refer, do have say in this process, and we have implemented TONS of features and changes based upon opinions coming directly from the players.

    This doesn't mean however that we can implement every suggestion we receive, or even every suggestion that the people at-large feel is a good idea. We are in the business of keeping our customers, all 217K+ of them, happy, given their multinational orgin, separate desires and expectations, even when those desires, expectations, and needs are in direct conflict with those of another customer. This "direct conflict" is best illustrated by the class-envy posts, emails, and feedbacks we regularly receive.

    Keep in mind that for every class that is absolutely sure that they deserve some new upgrade, enhancement, or feature, there is another class that will feel that they are equally deserving of an upgrade, enhancement, or feature if the first class gets their way. People who have been on this board for a while will be able to attest to this, given that "nerfs" have historically caused less problems than enhancements. This is because when we "nerf" something, only the users of the "nerfed" aspect become upset, whereas when we enhance something, we often start receiving complaints from multiple other sources. A good example of this is when the hitpoints were upped on Paladins and SKs. This action spawned complaints from warriors, rogues, monks, rangers, and bards.

    In the end, we provide a service offering, and if people like that offering they choose to buy our product. If they continue to like our offering from month to month, they choose to continue buying our product through the monthly subscription fees. We'll always strive to encourage you to make this choice, and will continue to listen to the opinions and suggestions expressed in this and other forums to do everything we can that is good for the game as a whole.

    By "good for the game as a whole", I speak of customer retention. Since customer retention is directly tied to customer satisfaction, that is the focus of every decision we make. We believe firmly that people want a game that is challenging and provides some sort of reward for playing, which is why we are so firmly against suggestions that make the game too easy, or reduce the rewards such as by making powerful items extremely commonplace. This is a delicate balance, and though I am sure that we could make a number of people happy in the short term if we made the game easier, made advancement quicker, made classes more powerful, or made items more plentiful, the end result would be that people would become bored much quicker, and leave the game at a greater pace.

    As for our customer retention, an excess of 70% after 14 months is unheard of in the industry, and therefore we are very proud of it. Certainly it could be better and we will continue to aim towards doing more things in game that helps keep people interested in the game. Given though that our customer have very different needs and desires as mentioned above, its very possible that some of the things we do along these lines will upset some customers while encouraging others to stay with us longer. For instance, a good number of people wanted zones that were much more difficult, being far more challenging than those in old EQ. A good number of the Kunark dungeons were built with this in mind. Of course, by doing what one group of customer wanted in this case most certainly has made another group unhappy. Only time will tell what the right decision was in this case, but we continue to believe that challenging and intriguing content is the best strategy to follow.

    -Gordon

Player View Update: Verant Responds
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 11:33 AM PDT
Gordon Wrinn just responded to the Discussion Thread dedicated to the Player View that was posted earlier today. In the message Gordon clears up some misconceptions about the post he made last night:

    In regards to the news posting made today on the front page, of course I
    meant GM and Guide *characters* are the ones that are excluded from being in
    player guilds, and vice versa, not the person behind the keyboard in
    general. Once in a play character (which is on a different server from that
    which they are a GM or Guide on), the GM or Guide is NO LONGER a GM or
    Guide, but a player, and subject to all of the same rules and policies that
    apply to players.

    The authority of a GM or Guide solely follows the character that is flagged
    as such. When in a play character, GMs and Guides are not permitted to
    acknowledge that they hold that position on another server, in order to
    avoid event he appearance of impropriety.

    I do wish that we could get away from the idea that "someone must be abusing
    their authority", unfortunately the only way to do this would be to bar the
    GM and Guide staff from playing the game. Considering that the Guides are
    volunteer players, and GMs mostly come from the player populace, and
    considering that everyone generally wants the GMs to play the game so that
    they can stay in touch with problems that a player faces, this is something
    that we'll probably always have to deal with.

    I realize that there are some who believe that someone who is a GM or Guide
    shouldn't be allowed to be in a player guild with their player-character
    either, but we do have to draw some line as to what is reasonable. What is
    a guild really? It's basically a group of characters who've decided to hang
    out together in game. There's nothing really "special" that changes when
    someone accepts an invite into a guild, so there's no reason to bar GMs or
    Guides from joining guilds with their play characters. As I mentioned above,
    the only thing we could do would be to bar them from playing altogether, and
    that just wouldn't be reasonable.

    -Gordon
Thanks Gordon!

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:22 PM PDT
Gordon was back on the boards answering several posts and even creating a thread himself. Here's the scoop:

  • Item Weight
    I'm having some of the weights on Monk stuff looked at today. In other news, Darkforge armor (SK) is having its weight lowered, and should go live the next time we patch items.

  • Ranger Issues
    The whole ranger-defense thing is coming from the logic that: At 50 they are balanced. At 60 everyone else gets bonuses to defense, and rangers don't, therefore they aren't balanced.
    The problem with this logic is that we don't balance classes on a skill-by-skill basis. We balance them as a complete offering. Everything that a class has is part of the equation. The fact that skill-point allocations, ratios, and percentage change as you go level to level is not in and of itself indicative of an imbalance.

  • Wind Saber
    Bow's themselves cannot proc (randomly cast a spell on a successful hit). This item was probably supposed to be setup as a charged item. I've asked the Live team to check it out.

  • Frontier Mountains Nerfed?
    We haven't retuned the zone. One thing to keep in mind is that most spawns have a level range that they can spawn in. For instance, hill giants can be anywhere from level 33 to 37 in most zones that they are found.
    My advice would be to kill off all the blue ones, which will again give the higher level ones a chance to spawn again.

  • Buying Stackable Items in Bulk
    This subject has come up several times in the past few weeks. As I mentioned before, this is something that we would like to do at some point in the future, but it's not something that we can assign a high priority to right now. It's certainly a good idea and a possibility for the future.

  • Do enchanters have any hope of getting illusions past level 44?
    No new illusions are planned for enchanters at this time.

  • EQ on a 56K Modem
    The majority of the data that EQ transmits is well within the capabilities of even a 28.8K modem. The notable exceptions to this are zoning, which will take longer with a slower connection, and very large battles (ones with a bunch of people involved in the same relative area, such as dragon runs), which may at times cause some data queueing.

  • Caster Items in Kunark
    More caster items should turn up as people get around to the deeper areas of Kunark. Charasis, Veeshan's Peak, most of Chardok and Sebilis still haven't been explored yet. The other dungeons as well.
    As people's comfort level grows, I think we'll start seeing the items turn up as peopel head deeper into the dungeons.

  • Items 'Morphing' Into Something Else?
    We did have a morph bug about six months ago that would occasionally cause something to change into another item. In all cases I've seen of this, the item would change to something not at all related to the original item, since the process through which it happened was more or less random. For instance, I had a friend who's glowing torch turned into a pair of cloth pants.
    Though it certainly may have been possible for something to morph from a silver ruby veil to a silver star ruby veil, my first guess would be that it may be possible that you bought the wrong veil and only noticed it now. It's very easy to miss "Star" in the description.

  • Hasten/Zephyl
    We've figured out the problem with these two NPCs not popping/depopping. It's being updated on Test now, and will go to production when next we patch zone data, which will probably happen in a day or two, as a complete server outage isn't required.

  • Quest Armor Camp Time Excessive?
    A 72 minute spawn seems ok. Quests for items aren't supposed to give instant gratification. I think that one of the problems we are running into is that when people want an item, they want the item NOW, and any time that it takes to get the item is considered a camp.
    Things need to take time, or the prestige that comes with having is gone.

  • More on Spawning Time
    [Quote from original post: Now in a recent Post (http://boards.station.sony.com/everquest/Forum4/HTML/040476.html) You claim the spawns are 72 mins? Get real.]

    I said that a 72 minute spawn on a particular NPC that the person talking about didn't seem out of line. In any case, I think a common misconception that people have is that everyone should be able to get every item. This isn't the way it works. There are things that drop in game once every few weeks, and are truly not meant to be common equipment for everyone. Certianly there is an element of luck involved (right place at right time).
    Does this mean that people who play more have the opportunity to get more things? Yes. Is that something that should be changed? No. Everyone can't get every item. If that were the case, there'd be nothing special about getting a very nice item, and at that point we might as well sell everything on vendors. Players need to decide what items are important to them, and put in the time that they feel that that item is worth. No one forces anyone to spend 8 hours camping an item. People spend 8 hours camping an item because they feel that it is worth it.
    The addition of some random loot tables in some areas of Kunark helps the "casual" player that you speak of even more. No longer do they have to track down one particular NPC, but only be fighting in that dungeon or area to have as good a chance of getting a particular item as anyone else fighting in the area.
    Back to my point, there is no magic shot to make everything "fair" for the casual gamer, because you can always find someone who's more "casual" than the last guy. There's no way to make things equal in every respect whether people play an hour per week or 20 hours per week, and we probably wouldn't want to pursue it even if there was, as it would probably make the game boring.
    The best advice I could offer to people in your prediciment would be to play the game first, and see what items you get, rather than deciding what items you want, and then playing the game to that end. Discover the items through your normal travels rather than scheduling your travels around items you want. You might find this path a bit more rewarding.

  • The Hole
    We're probably looking at sometime late June for a release of the Hole. There have been a few setbacks in its development, including the zone designer having a couple weeks of jury duty, followed by some meetings that the people working on it were involved in.
    The zone geometry is done, and now it is being textured. After that we have objects, lighting, sounds, NPC pathing info, population, and itemization.

Gordon Infobits
[12 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:03 PM PDT
Hot off the pages of the Gameplay Board, here is today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Pet Regen Rates
    Pet regen rates haven't changed. I imagine that you might have gotten a new or higher level pet. Though newer and higher level pets do get more hitpoints, their regen rate is always the same number of hitpoints per tick.

  • Hangman's Noose (haste) From Trakanon Seems to be Broken
    I've forwarded this to the test team so that they can have a look. I'm not sure off the top of my head what level haste this item is supposed to have, but assuming that your times are correct it does appear to be broken.

  • Clarifying Previous Post
    [Quote from original post: A few weeks ago 2HS was fixed by changing the minimum damage that it would do. Was this change for 2HS only, or does it apply to weapons of all types?]

    It applied to all 2-Handed weapon types.

    [Quote from original post: There are many relatively slow 1H weapons (delays of 35-40) that did not perform as well as their lower-delay cousins. Do high delay 1H weapons now perform as well as low delay 1H weapons of the same ratio?]

    Lower delay weapons still have an advantage over their high delay counterparts of the same ratio due to the way the damage bonuses are applied.

    For instance, assume that a 8-point bonus will be applied on a weapon. In 4 seconds, a 20 delay weapon would apply that bonus twice, whereas a 40 delay weapon would apply it only once. However, if the 40 delay weapon were 2-handed, it would apply a larger bonus.

  • More on Corpses Decaying
    [Quote from original post: Reason Why Corpses Should NOT Disappear. Though I have not personally ran into the problem of having a corpse disappear, I am sure I will. But is this realistic? No! Though the flesh and bone of a corpse may rt all it wants, the fact is: ARMOR DOES NOT ROT AS QUICKLY AS FLESH!So why not have the corpses turn into skeletons after a while? The armor would still be on it, and we can all live in peace.]

    Corpses decay in EQ due to the need to maintain a playable framerate for people in the game. As many people have mentioned in some other threads, the number of corpses in the vicinity directly impact the framerate that people experience. Each corpse must be drawn on the screen. Packing tons corpses in close proximity basically has the same effect on framerate as packing a ton of live characters in the same area.

  • AE Mez
    [Quote from original post: This is one I cannot fathom for the life of me. in another post, it was mentioned that a pet is "part of the person" by a GM when explaining why pets could not be charmed. Ok...doesnt make total sense to me...but ok.

  • Tranquil Staff Proc is Still Broken
    Ack, I knew I forgot to announce something. The staff is fixed, but you'll need to get ahold of a GM to trade it out for a working one. The current one you have thinks that it is out of charges.

  • A Big Problem with Gyrating Goos
    [Quote from original post: Gyrating Goos, a level 28ish mob [in dalnir] now proc a poison which does 800-900 points of damage!]

    Ouch. That certainly doesn't sound correct. I've got the live team checking it out now. We'll do a special zone-patch for that once it is corrected.

  • Rotting Corpses
    [Quote from original post: Blah, its NOT fun and exiting to have your corpse rot!!! Dont even try to tell me that! You cant say, i know its exciting for you]

    Very true, in the same fashion that getting severely injured or killed in extreme sports isn't fun either. However, the prospect of that, however remote, does lend a good amount of excitement to the endeavor. If we start retrieving corpses in order to eliminate corpse loss, then the challenge and discretion imparted to its prospect is also null and void.

    Given that corpses must disappear for multiple technical reasons including framerate, and the fact that we cannot build a server to track an infinite number of corpses, we've engineered the possibility of a lost corpse and items as part of the game in the same fashion as done by other games in the genre.

    As always, I would recommend that if a specific aspect of the game provides more risk that is palatable for you, you might not want to engage in that aspect. In other words, if a dungeon is basically unexplored and not populated with people that can help you, you might want to visit places that are safer.

  • Login Server Inaccurate
    There's really no way for the client to determine whether it is down, or just busy and not responding in a given amount of time. Either case provides the same information back to the client, that being that the login server is unreachable.

    Perhaps we should change the message so that it points people to the network status page in the event that the outage is planned.
