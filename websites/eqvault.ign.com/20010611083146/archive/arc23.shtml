Editorial & Fan Fiction Updates
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:41 PM PST
As a New Years present I've published a few editorials and fan fiction updates. This should complete the big update to those sections, if I've missed anyone's submission (or if you have any EQ-related content to submit) please email it to me at aurum@vaultnetwork.com. Content updates should occur normally after I finish catching up this week. Here's today's updates:

  • The Simple Art of Online Role Playing: A Practical Guide
    That’s right, the simple “art” of role-playing. Maybe some of you reading that headline scoffed, but the fact can’t be denied: there is no science to role-playing, no method that can be easily applied to ensure success. That said, “art” is the only remaining classification broad enough to cover the myriad of elements that can come into play when “role playing” a character. It may sound a bit high-minded, but it’s true all the same, and the sooner all us online RPer’s accept it, the sooner we’ll realize that there’s more involved in successfully role playing a character than simply choosing an unpronounceable moniker and chopping up monsters. Yet, realization and implementation are vastly different things, so, to make the transition easier for you gaming masses, I’ve decided to write this article and provide some simple guidelines. So read on brave warrior/cleric/thief/druid/paladin/ranger/wizard/magician/enchanter…sheesh…

    -By Neverly

  • The Name Police Strike Again
    Every time I play EverQuest I'm confronted by characters named after rock
    stars, real world companies, other famous personages or names that just
    don't fit into the game. It bugs me and it detracts from the game
    atmosphere. Fortunately EverQuest has a naming policy that, as vague as it
    is, does not permit characters to have such names. Unfortunately all of
    the characters I'm talking about are computer controlled NPCs. Apparently
    Verant Interactive doesn't see fit to abide by its own policies. No big
    deal, right? After all, it is their game and they can do with it as they
    wish. I think the issue goes a bit deeper than that.

    -By Rolli Polli

  • Ascension & Fortunes of War
    There comes a time when a tale of an adventurer’s life must be told. Perhaps now it’s time for mine.

    My name is Connavar Demonblade, a Wolf of the North and Guild Officer of the Swords of Rhigante. A burden lies across my soul and only through sharing it with all of you can it be lifted. Have you ever asked yourself the question “Why am I here?” or “What is my purpose?” Until recently, I did not understand the importance of those questions, meaningless babble from those educated, well-mannered nobles.


    It was one of those nobles, that drove me across Norrath on a quest that would change my life and that of those with me. Are you settled? Then let me begin.

It’s also possible that we’ll go the other way and make them more accessible for the lower level crowd... Right now we don’t know. We might add more locks, traps, quests, etc. But we’ll pay close attention to the availability of dungeons for all level groups.

    I have a love of those underused zones as well. I often like to hang out in those places just to avoid crowding.

Brad on Experience Loss
[45 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:35 PM PST
Brad responded to a thread on the Gameplay Board dealing with experience loss. Here is a copy of the original post followed by Brad's post:

    Seriously, i was pondering the matters at hand and i couldn't figure out how you can lose exp.

    If i kill 5000 mobs and i die have i not the exp from killing 5000 mobs? Even if i die i still killed 5000 mobs and i have gained that exp.

    I am a better fighter for what i have done. I should not be told that i am not as good as i once was just because i was trained by 15 mobs.

    Bring on the flames -- I am only half serious and i know you have to pay a penalty for dying but is losing exp the answer?

    The fundamental advancement mechanism in MMORPGs is Time Invested; therefore, Time Lost needs to be the fundamental death penalty if the principle of risk vs. reward is to be preserved.

    The time lost can be in the form of experience loss, an experience debt (a cool idea, btw), items lost (providing it takes time to acquire items), corpse retrievals, etc.

    As a corollary, being efficient in terms of time spent is the quickest way to advance (assuming advancing quickly is your goal, of course). Downtime, which is likewise often debated and criticized, is also a penalty based on the Time Invested/Time Lost principle. Minimizing or even eliminating downtime using intelligent tactics in a well balanced group is an efficient time investment. Failing to do so incurs a Time Lost penalty, though probably not as severe as dying.

    -Brad
