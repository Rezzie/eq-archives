Gordon Infobits
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:29 PM PDT
Gordon was posting up a storm on the Gameplay Board as usual. Here's the scoop:

  • Iksar Can Wear Plate?
    Keep in mind that "Plate texture" does not necessarily equal "Plate item". Rangers, for example, cannot wear plate, yet have a host of items out there that use the plate texture. This is primarily because the plate texture looks better when tinted.

    Rubicite is a good example of a plate-looking item that is really chain.

  • No Level 49 Spell
    There isn't a 49th level song at this time, and it looks like there might not be one. We've used the rest of the cool ideas that people had on the 51+ songs, where bards get two per level. I’m certainly willing to listen to suggestions though and talk them over with GZ. As it is now, we could throw a song together just for the sake of putting one in, but it wouldn’t be cool.

  • Wolfform Issue
    Cros and Holly shouldn't be jumping on Druids in wolfform. I've asked the faction designer to take a look.

    Cros and Holly are Protecters of Pine, correct?

  • PnP Addition Idea
    [Quote from original post: Can you add in that if a caster and a warrior is in an argument over a warrior weapon camp, that the warrior has priority over the caster? Chances are the caster has already farmed it a few times and saw how easy it is to sell so he keeps coming back, where the warrior is just trying to get one on his own. Comments?]

    I don't think that we want to get into situations that we are policing camps based upon what drops. Camps are for whomever is willing to spend the time at them.

  • More Memorization Slots
    [Quote from original post: I'd like to be able to have more spell slots so I don't have to memorize spells everytime my buffs run out.]

    No plans to do this. The limitation to the choice of spells available to you at any given time promotes strategy. Some classes have a harder time with this than others. Wizards, for instance, usually don't have a hard time making their choices, where enchanters (and necromancers from personal experience) will always have an ongoing debate as to what the best spell lineup is.

  • Why do other classes have a level 55 spell and Paladins don't?
    Just checked my list. There's a spell on there. If there's not really one in game at that level, there's probably a typo. I'll check with GZ next time I see him.

  • Drops in Kunark
    The updates that we made to item drop percentages and locations were not directly based upon complaints from people about loot being too comon, but rather economic reports that we run to determine the health of the economy and the rates at which items enter the world.

    Basically, once we got 100K people over in Kunark, we'd have found the problems regardless, so it isn't the "fault" of the players who brought the issue to public light.

  • Compass Training
    Well we don't have any way that we could easily implement skill-increase potential based on what is in your inventory on a per-skill basis. I suggest you train the skill up a bit. The way skill increases work is that they require a successful skill check before they'll roll for a skill increase.

  • Server Situation
    There's really no situation that could cause everything on the server (including NPCs) to "hang" for an amount of time. If there was a network connection issue, PCs could lag, however NPCs would go about their normal business. If it were a server problem, it wouldn't affect everyone on the server, just everyone in a particular zone.

    If the server were to have a problem causing the zone to hang for a bit, spells ticks wouldn't expire either. I imagine what happened in your case was a network problem.

  • More Communication Ideas
    The Guild MOTD sounds nifty. I'll pass it around.

  • Hotkey/Mouse Problem
    [Quote from original post: I'm just curious about the fact that pressing the keyboard shortcut for a hotkey doesn't work while medding. Is this a bug? You can press the hotkey button with the mouse and that works fine. Is there any chance this could be fixed? My carpal tunnel syndrome is getting worse because of this 8-).]

    Since we allow the buttons to be pressed with the mouse, I'll check with the client programmer to see if we can also allow the key to be pressed as well.

  • Faction Information
    [Quote from original post: I am a DE Agnostic Enchanter. I'm not KoS in Freeport, Qeynos, Erudin, and several other non-evil cities. Yet I'm KoS in FV. Why is this? It seems completely based on race, and not at all on religion. I figured that by being agnostic and not being able to do religious specific quests would at least allow me to not be KoS in both good and evil cities on Kunark. This seems unbalanced...]

    Firiona Vie is an outpost built by the High Elves and Wood elves, who do not like dark elves (regardless of religion). I've seen some DE people who managed to get their faction with Felwithe and Kelethin up to the point that they could go in, but FV is a different faction, so you start over there.

    Factions are based on four components:

    1. Your race
    2. Your religion
    3. Your class
    4. Things that you've done.

    Any of those is sufficient to make an NPC dislike you. Just being a dark elf is enough in this case.

  • Quest Ideas
    [Quote from original post: Many items are NO DROP that, once something better is found, sit in the bank and rot. Example, Razing Sword of Skarlon, great sword, collecting dust. (this is just an idea for warrior ATM) For ALL races, make a 45+ quest starting with your guildmaster that will USE all these no drop items. So, you turn in the Skarlon Sword, Fleshripper, an item from sky, AND a sword from the gods, and you get THE BEST warrior weapon, period. None of this AWESOME l33t sword some newbie can win in a lotto in some zone, I am talking about a DIFFICULT quest to finish, that takes a LOT of time, and a lot of effort, and with THE BEST reward one can get. The same could be done for ALL melee classes. Every melee class deserves somethig for their efforts, the paladins got FA (which could have been a better sword btw, nothing in Kunark should be better, UNLESS FA becomes a quest component.) It would give a lot of classes something to drive for beyond the next level.]

    We like this idea, but it does have a downside, that being that it causes people to want to go camp something outside their level-range. For instance, there is a quest for Rangers that requires the EBW as a component, and this was a frequent complaint when this quest went in.

  • Ideas for Communication
    [Quote from original post: Would it be possible, or difficult, to implement a way for members of a class to talk to each other over a channel (like guildtalk). I think this could be useful for players communicating with others in their class regarding quests, strategy, exchange of research components, etc. e.g. /class Anyone have any information on the spell call of bones?]

    Blanket communication channels tend to reduce the sense of community in game, rather than enhance it. Instead of dealing with a specific person, and forming a relationship with them, it ends up pretty cold. This is somewhat apparent with zone-wide communications such as /shout and /ooc, however it's not as bad as a server-wide mechanism would be. In addition to this, there are some technical issues surrounding this idea (e.g. the number of additional packets to broadcast) which make the idea unattractive.

  • Splitting the Loot
    [Quote from original post: Every day, I get into groups with greedy master looters. When I master, I split after every battle and sometimes short myself just so the group gets an even split (i.e. if I loot 5 pp 4 gp in a group of 6 I /split 6). I just think it's fair.]

    We did the loot items due to the number of complaints about the ninjalooting of rare and expensive items. Coin really doesn't fit this description, so the best suggestion that I can offer is that you only master-loot with people that you trust.

    [Quote from original post: And yes, /autosplit is terrible. If you have 6 people, and the MOB drops 5 plat, 5 gold, 5 silver, and 5 copper, guess what happens? The looter gets 5 plat, 5 gold, 5 silver, and 5 copper, the other 5 players get NADA (because 5 coins of each type can't be evenly distributed among 6 members). ]

    This was actually done based upon numerous requests from people playing the game in beta. They got tired of ending up with tons of heavy coin due to the magical splitting of it. If you are masterlooting, autosplit can work very well providing the person doing the final /split is aware that coins are not automatically reduced to even the split. If you are not masterlooting, an extra coin here, one coin less there, isn't a big deal and evens out in the long run.

  • Does Grouping Give You an EXP Bonus?
    There is both an XP bonus assigned per kill based upon the number of people in your party, and it is also expected that you can take more critters in the same amount of time in excess of what you could do solo.

    For instance, if a solo person can kill 1 blue mob every 2 minutes, then a group of two might be able to do 3 in that same amount of time, yielding 50% (plus the bonus) more experience per person per time unit.

  • Stats Clarification
    [Quote from original post: Before the release of ROK there was a post by someone on the Verant staff stating that basically EQ does not have the best stats system, and that the effects of stats have been purposefully minimized to reduce the the effects of twinking.]

    I don't recall anyone saying that. What I do recall saying is that the reason we don't have any +skill items out there was to reduce the impact of twinking. Stats really don't affect twinking as their effect is scaled based upon the level of the recipient. For instance, a level 1 wizards with 200 INT has roughly 1 mana point more mana than one with 100 INT.

    One other thing, I've seen a rumor going around that the effects of stats were drastically reduced at some point in late beta. Nothing like that happened.

  • Pronunciation
    We're actually thinking about doing a pronunciation key. Here's a few. If anyone has any others that they'd luck to ask about, feel free:

    Qeynos = `Key-nos
    Kunark = `Coo-nark
    Najena = `Nah-jen-uh
    Guk = Guck
    Ykesha = Yuh-`kesh-uh

  • A Compelling Reason
    [Quote from original post: This is NOT a class envy post. I just want to thank you, Gordon, for supplying us with the answer to the basic question of "what makes a class ballanced?" I, for one, think that you and Verant are using entirely the wrong yardstick to measure ballance, but at least I understand where you are coming from when you say class x, y, or z is in fact ballanced in spite of my opinions and observations otherwise.]

    I respect your opinion, and thank you for voicing it, however it is very important that everyone is aware that balance does not equal equality in all things. The pinnacle of a good class-based system is interdependence, and homogenization of the classes doesn't promote that. [Quote from original post: 1. Is there any available information as to how many characters of each class exist in Norrath?

    2. Is there comparitive information as to the number of class-hours each class has since the game began (I know, this is a bit much, but I want to know if the 8.6% of the existing characters that are of class X are actually played or just hold twink items, etc.)]

    We have access to the information, but it's not something that we'd generally release in a tabulated form. One thing I should mention is that we had no expectation that the number of characters in game would be spread across the classes evenly. This can easily be illustrated on the group level. For instance, there is no point of diminishing returns when adding warriors to a group. In other words, the benefit to the group that one warrior adds can always be doubled by adding another. Some other classes can't do that. For instance, adding a second bard to your group will not do as much as adding the first one did.

  • Monk Questions
    [Quote from original post: Abashi - Is the monk robe quest still on the 'fix list'?]

    It's on the re-evaluation list still.

    [Quote from original post: 2) The 'combine' attempt when all the pieces are assembled has a chance of failing. Since the quest is so expansive, perhaps the final combine can be made to not fail.]

    As long as it takes to acquire all of the items for it, this sounds a little severe.

  • PK'd in the Bank
    I've read this over, and chatted with the customer service folk just to make sure rules haven't changed since I guided on a PvP server, but there doesn't appear to be anything "illegal" here.

    There's no rule baring someone from attacking you in town. The only thing that is illegal in regards to anything of this sort would be exploiting guard range issues, such as attacking someone in guard range while you are out of that range. If you get into a situation where the guards like your attacker more than they like you, it's feasible that you could be killed in town.

    Here is sounds like they just sniped you while you were coming out of the bank.

  • More Hot Key Ideas
    [Quote from original post: As any seasoned healer will tell you, the F1-F8 keys are a neccessity. Targeting your players with these keys are a must. Since we can heal pets i was wondering if it were possible for clerics to hit the Fkeys twice to target a mage's(or any pet class)pet. I also play a magician and i am able to target myself with F1 and then press F1 again and it targets my pet. I dont think this is to unreasonable a request. It would just make it alot easier to monitor the health of a pet in a group situation. =)]

    It's a good idea, and I'll mention it, but I imagine that this might be a bit difficult to implement at this time. What the F-keys do is client-side, and the client really doesn't know who's pet is who's.

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:03 AM PDT
Just from patrolling the Gameplay Board this morning, I can tell that Verant's Gordon Wrinn didn't catch much sleep last night. Not only did he post more, but he posted as early as 2:00AM in the morning. Here's the Morning Edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • The Gorillas
    [Quote from original post: The Gorillas in the Burning Woods are not animals.... Is this as intended? If so, why? There is absolutely no point for furthering the animal-line in the Druid's spell list if there are no animals on Kunark. To tell the truth, I have yet to find one creature that is an animal...]

    Actually none of the Gorillas are animals as I recall, and I believe that these particular ones are undead.

  • Hide & Hate
    [Quote from original post: Why doesn't hide wipe the hate list?]

    Because we didn't implement it as an escape spell. It used to do this in beta, but it trivialized combat and provided too big of an advantage to those who could invis.

  • Timorous Deep Not Itemized?
    [Quote from original post: just wanted too point this out since i've not even been able to get a coin from a bandit]

    No coin? That certainly doesn't sound right. I've sent it to the EQLive team for investigation. Much thanks.

  • Refund Problems
    [Quote from original post: This might sound a bit harsh but I feel pretty strongly about people charging my creditcard for something they find out they cant deliver and then not refunding my money IMMEDIATELY!]

    We apologize for any delay. We are processing refunds as fast as possible in the order that the requests were received.

  • Tech Support Number
    [Quote from original post: Is Verant going to add "a" second phone number to call now that the masses of subscribers have gotten way out of control for 7 technicians to handle? Trying to talk to a live Verant employee is like trying to talk to a live KROQ employee.]

    It's one phone number, but it certainly is more than one line. The line rolls over to the next available technician.

    The majority of EverQuest technical support occurs at installation time. Once people can get in game, only a very small percentage ever need tech support again. Certainly there are cases where that number and percentage increase, such as in the case of ROK, however the number of people that we have maning the phones do a very good job of keeping up with the load.

  • Ressurection Line Nerfed?
    I've heard a couple reports of this (thanks to those who emailed me). No intentional change was made to this line, but I've forwarded to the test team to check it out.

  • Clipping Plane & Kunark Preformance Issues
    [Quote from original post: In a older game I used to play, (Wing Commander 3) it had a setting that made the game detect if the framerate dropped below a certain level. If this happened, the game lowered the grafics lvl automaticly to improve preformance.

    I suggest that you implement something like this in EQ. Instead of lowering grafics, you could tell the game to stop drawing the last 1/3'd of the tree branches if the frame rate gets to low. As I understand, its the tree branches that cause the low FPS. And if you have 30 trees in the front 2/3's of your screen, then the only thing you'll see in the back 1/3'd of your screen is possibly the trunks anyways. So just have the game draw the trunks if the framerate drops to low.

    I'd really appriciate some feedback on this idea. ]

    Though we could probably have it so that the clipping plane automatically came in or the particle engine turned off, I suspect people would find this more annoying than anything. Our engine doesn't have the capability to automatically turn off features such as this.

  • Haste Items Question
    [Quote from original post: Since learning that +mana items are also subject to the level restriction and have a reduced effect at lower levels]

    Actually that's not entirely accurate. You can only raise your mana with +mana items based upon the total base mana that you have. For instance, if someone has only 50 mana, and they don a +50 mana item, they do not have 100 mana. This isn't a function of the level, but of total mana. We won't let someone double their mana supply with +mana items.

    [Quote from original post: Im wondering if permenant haste items fall under this rule as well. I know that the exe axe doesnt proc haste til level 30 so Im suggesting that permenant haste items like the FBSS and Mith 2H dont work until a certain level or have a reduced effect ]

    Permanent-haste items do not share the level restrictions of a proc on a weapon.

    The skills that NPCs have are based on level and class, not on the model that they use. For instance, that puma that you are fighting might really be monk. I suppose that it might have been nice if we had a way to suppress the use of certain skills based upon body type.

  • Mana Regen Nerfed?
    There has been no intentional change, however due to the number of people reporting it I'm not ready to dismiss it just yet. However, I highly suspect that the observations are due to a number of things, not related to the actual rate. For instance:

    1. Perception: Mana above level 50 continues to increase at a rate much like it was before, however the mana regen rate does not increase on the same curve, giving the perception that it takes longer to med, even though you are still getting the same amount of mana back per tick. This is something that people also notice and claim as they level up or gain +INT/WIS/MANA items. They increase the amount of mana that they have without increasing the regen rate in a linear manner.

    2. Watched Pot: The addition of Kunark has caused people to spread out more. The net result of this is that the ratio of people to targets has improved. Where a group used to be at full mana the next time it was their "turn" to kill a particular spawn, they are now staring at their mana bars while a surplus of critters walk by that they could be killing.

    3. Placebo Effect: The "Placebo effect" also causes the "Watched Pot". Someone, perhaps due to one of the reasons mentioned above, perceives that the mana regen rate has changed, and comments on it. This causes other people to become sensitive to the issue, causing them to pay close attention to their mana bars, giving the impression that it is taking longer to regen.

    4. Of course there is always the good old fashioned "bug", which shouldn't take too long for us to figure out as soon as I can corner the programmers :) I'm looking into it this afternoon.

  • Loot Tables Changed: Who Gets Hurt?
    [Quote from original post: Loot tables changed - Who gets hurt... Those that had delayed shipments. They should have stayed the same so everyone had the same opportunity... What about the people still waiting for thier CD's?]

    If "fairness" is the main concern here, there's really no way to make these changes "fair". Certainly we could have waited until everyone (within reason) received their CD's, but the same subject of fairness would come up for those who have had their CDs and just now decided to make the trip to Kunark.

There were cases where loot was dropping in the wrong place, and it has in most cases been moved to a more appropriate location. We even had a few dragon/god items dropping on level 40 creatures (though at very small percentages), and the people who were there first certainly lucked out. Unfortunately at some time we have to fix these errors, and making it fair in everyone's eyes probably isn't possible.

    Please, lets not look at this as people who got there later getting hurt, but perhaps as people who got there sooner getting lucky.

  • Who Ya Gonna Call?
    [Quote from original post: When we can prove the troubles are on Verant's side...can we do the same and expect a response?]

    Of course, but the nice (and bad thing) about running a destination service where 25-60K people are connecting at once is that when we have a problem, we know it because we lose a good number of people at one time. We have alarms set up internally that polls the number of people connected and lets us know if a number of people drop all at one time. Aside from the zone server, where we could lose only a small number of people, any other error that we have control over would affect thousands of people.

    Source services (such as ISPs) don't have that advantage (and disadvantage). ISP XYZ might only have a few people connecting to the EverQuest servers, so when they have a problem with the route to us, they only have that small number of people who call in.

  • What is balance?
    Balance is about there being a compelling reason to choose one class over another aside from the desire to roleplay a particular type of character. It really isn't about relative power compared to another class, nor would we claim that that relative power is meant to be fixed from levels 1 to 60.

    Offensive power is only one equation in determining "balance". Other factors are usefulness to a group, and special skills or abilities. For instance, though a wizard's teleporting capability does not at all affect their offensive efficiency, it does contribute to other factors of "balance". A bard gives up much offensive prowess in exchange for being a very desirable member of a group.

    By "compelling reason", we mean that one class does not invalidate another. For instance, the monk can do more offensive damage at a give level than any warrior. Now, if they had the hitpoints of a warrior, could wear their armor, and carry as much weight, then there would be no compelling reason to play a warrior other than roleplay. The monks weakness opens the door for another class to be viable.

    Now any time I speak about "compelling reasons", posts on the board follow along the lines of "Just what is the 'compelling reason' to play a [insert class here]." All I can say in all of these cases is that "compelling" is in the eyes of the beholder, and I would encourage everyone to find that out on their own. Our opinions might differ from yours, but we believe that there are compelling reasons to play every class in the game, which is why we consider them balanced.

Brad on the Gameplay Board
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:32 PM PDT
Brad McQuaid responded to a thread asking a few questions about what's next for EverQuest as a whole. Here's the original post and Brad's response from the Gameplay Board:

    I'm going to take a moment here to eat some crow and commend Verant on Kunark.
    Kunark is split into two apects: Levels past 50, and the Kunark zones. Both have been extremely well done, and both exhibit an intellegence and creativity that your past work didn't even hint at. The new kunark zones and the amazing capabilities of characters past level 50 make the rest of the game pale in comparison.

    Now we've gotten to the subject of my post: What about the rest of the game? Kunark is stunning but there are still zones in the "old game" that aren't even near fully utilized.

    Will work on the "old game" be abandoned in favor of doting on the new and expensive-to-create Kunark?

    Will we ever see graphical enhancements to the "old game" to bring it at least a little more in-line with the current majesty of Kunark? Will "The Hole" ever open!? Will Odus ever be worth going to? Will NPC's which previously just stood around (and there are a lot of them - the majority in fact) saying nothing when you hailed them ever start saying things?

    Will the "Old Game" ever be more polished than it is now, or will you neglect it in favor of your pretty, new Kunark?


    Faxor,

    First, thanks for the compliments -- Kunark was an opportunity to learn from our mistakes and do better. And in the future, we'll top Kunark

    Now, to your questions.

    1. No, we will not be updating the graphics on the old zones. This is primarily for two reasons: a. Resources are limited and I really think most of our customers would rather see new content as opposed to re-doing older content, and b., as was mentioned in this thread, it's not really appropriate to change the system requirements on the existing lands, which upgrading the graphics significantly would. That said, we have at least one minor way we're going to be upgrading the original world soon.

    2. The 'old game' will definitely NOT be abandoned. The EQ Live team will continue to address crowding issues by revamping the lesser used dungeons and other zones. In addition, many of the zone mechanics that are being tried in Kunark, should they prove successful, will be used in the older zones when they are revamped as well.

    3. The Hole is still on target for May, probably late May. After the Hole comes the Warrens, which is also on Odus. And we will continue to add free zones after that.

    4. Yes, I think slowly but surely existing NPCs will be made more interesting, possibly involved in quests and storylines.

    Hope that helps,

    -Brad
