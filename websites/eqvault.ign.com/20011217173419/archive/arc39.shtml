Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:02 PM PST
Alan was around on the Gameplay Board lot today and discussed many topics:

  • The Word "Gay" Acceptable?
    Isladar_PK wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Everyone I have heard in the last few years since high school have always said 'damn that book was gay' or 'my teacher gave me some gay ass homework'. That is just how people talk now!]

    Right. And that is certainly how the word is used now. But look at the meaning you are giving it.

    In your examples, ‘gay’ = ‘bad’.

    But there is a portion of our society that also use ‘gay’ to mean homosexual.

    And, therefore, it can be very offensive to some people to simply use ‘gay’ to mean ‘bad’.

    So that common usage that you might see every day (I have a good friend that is a schoolteacher, I like to check with him on what’s hip these days…), it’s not polite. Using that word that way you are essentially calling all homosexuals bad.

    I’m all for bringing back the use of the word gay to mean happy, myself.

  • Druid Talk
    Eventually I’d like to do one of these for every class. I know, your first question is going to be, how did I decide to pick druids to be next? Well, honestly, it just came up in conversation today with a co-worker. There’s no trick to it. I would certainly address the ones most in need first, but until I’ve actually done all these posts, I won’t know which is most in need… (let’s not make that the topic of this post, please)

    So the question is, what is it that the druid community thinks they need to become a ‘balanced’ class? What is lacking? What is over-powered? - yes, that’s a valid question for any class –

    This time I’m starting this post with very little information to lead with, so I feel like I’m at a bit of a disadvantage. I do know that, like many other classes, some druids feel that they don’t have enough to do while at the final encounter of the big raid. One of the suggestions to change that falls along the same lines as one proposed by the shaman community, changing/improving their healing abilities post 50.

    I want you to understand that I think druids are pretty well balanced all around, and I really see little that needs to be changed (and that’s partially why I picked them to be the topic today…). So you’ll have to be convincing, logical and articulate to convince me. :)

  • An Apology?
    Closing this post for two reasons. Firstly, well, the original post is pretty nasty and doesn’t warrant comment anyhow.

    But I’m also closing it because I’m not going to allow threads to become personal attacks on other posters. Those of you that have issues with each other, please take them elsewhere. This board is for the discussion of issues about EverQuest. Complain about Verant or the game, or praise us if you want. But I don’t want to see any more attacks on people. Consider this a warning shot from an over-caffeinated Absor. But the next step is polite warnings, and then real warnings.

    I understand that some people just don’t get along, that’s human nature. But try to keep from assaulting each other.

  • Blackouts
    Not only have they not happened here, but we have a huge generator attached to the buiding like a leech...

    So if the power goes out, my CD player will at least keep going.

  • More Patch Information
    Ok, things get a little confusing in here around patch day. I wanted to clear up one item that was put into the patch message that wasn’t completely accurate.

    [Quote from original post: - Last week's patch attempted to fix Celestial Elixir, Celestial Healing, and Torpor, so that they would stack another similar spell, unintentionally causing it to stop stacking with standard regeneration spells. This has been fixed. The downside to this fix is that these spells will now overwrite the "Call of Bones" series. As the three former spells are cast to save a character's life, this solution appears to be the most beneficial for all of those involved.]

    We’re looking at this one again. Due to the fact that at least some of us thought this was going to work one way and other another, we’ll have to go over it again…

    But, as it is in game now, Celestial Elixir, Celestial Healing and Torpor will stack with most regenerative-type spells. The will NOT overwrite Call of Bones, Lich or the DoT lifetap spells. The heal over time spells will not take hold on someone with those spell lines up. That’s how it works at the moment. As I said, we’re giving the whole thing one more review.

  • Link Dead
    OscuroCorazon wrote:

    [Quote from original post: As you all know you can still be hit when LD. Well you might or might not know, you cannot be healed, buffed, or saved in anyway while LD. This is extremely unfair. We should not be able to be killed when we have no control over our char. It doesn't seem right that we can be pounded but not healed. Half the zone wend LD the other night, and many of us died. Guide would not rez. (i understand the policies he has to follow) He might not even have been bothered if the cleric in my group could have healed me.]

    You have to look at the whole thing here, not just the bad thing that happened to you (though it is a bummer).

    If people were untouchable when link dead, then people would go link dead a lot more than they do now. When fighting a dangerous opponent and about to lose, players would suddenly go link dead. They would become immune and would be safe from any risk of death.

    But rather than just leaving you in game to suffer as a helpless player character when you go link dead, we’ve set it up so that you become an non-player character when you go link dead (which is why you can’t be healed). So you will at least gain the benefit of the non-player character AI and your character will defend itself when the player can’t do it.

    The current setup seems to be the best solution.

    lagacy_lovemaster wrote:

    [Quote from original post: you can bandage a LD player .... if that helps]

    You can? That’s odd… are you sure about that? Because, from what I know, you should not be able to bandage a link dead player character.

    Which of them is the smartest player?

    Which of them makes me laugh the hardest?

    Answer those questions, and I'll pick my group...

  • Zone Modifers
    carmageddon wrote:

    [Quote from original post: ”We all know what zones are gonna be upped exp wise ( it still isnt gonna make anyone go to kerra isle or eruds crossing though) but are any zones gonna get xp modifier decreased? Much of what you add does have a downside that always isnt announced. Like yep the hybrids will lose more exp on death then before. Please tell us ahead of time yes or no, whether popular spots like lake of ill omen might get modifier downgrades too. Thank you.”]

    No, we are not reducing the zone experience modifier in any zones. And, well, we did announce that hybrids will be losing more experience on death… (of course now I need to explain that they will also be gaining experience at a faster rate that offsets that increase in death penalty, so that it won’t be taken out of context).

  • On Suggestion for Full Screen Mode
    You can make the chat window background any color and nearly any opacity that you wish using the /chatwindow command. So /chatwindow 1 1 1 4 should make your chat box solid black. But I may have the commands confused…

    The ability to do what you need is already there. You may need to check your supplemental manual in your EverQuest directory for the exact command.

  • Servers Affected by California Power Crisis?
    We have a massive generator attached to the building, it's right outside my window (well, the window near my cubicle anyhow, I can't really claim it as my own).

    So, yes, our servers would remain up if we were hit by one of these proposed rolling blackouts. Though I can’t promise you that all of the service providers between you and our servers would remain up, but I don’t imagine that would be much of a problem, since the Internet is a pretty flexible beast.

    So far, so good. No blackouts here yet.

Patch Message: 01-16-01
- Aurum @ 5:51 AM PST
Today's new patch message is up on the Patch Server:

    January 17th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Baltimore Fan Faire - Last Chance*

    Monday is the registration deadline for the Baltimore Fan Faire! We are almost sold out so hurry and visit http://everquest.station.sony.com/fanfaire/fanfaire.html before you miss your chance to attend this event!

    *Patch Day*

    The primary focus of today's patch is to implement changes noted in the new Producer's Letter which can be found at http://everquest.station.sony.com/news/letter.shtml

    It is HIGHLY RECOMMENDED that you read the Producer's Letter before playing EverQuest. It contains some unprecedented and exciting enhancements to gameplay.

    In addition to these changes, the following changes have been implemented:

    *Bug Fixes*

    - Fixed a bug that caused "root" and "snare" type spells to function incorrectly when stacked. They should now stack properly.
    - Fixed a bug that negated the "instrument modifier" for bards using Selo's, causing them to run slower than they otherwise would.
    - Fixed a bug that allowed two or more spells that grant levitation to stack. Levitation spells should not stack.
    - The "Track Corpse" spell now works.
    - Tracked down and eliminated several bugs that were causing a very small percentage of people to crash when zoning or upon entering the game.
    - Fixed a problem with some Ulthork armor that caused it to disappear on death if the wearer was of extremely low level.
    - Tserrina's Whip has been fixed. If you received one prior to today's patch, you will need to trade out your whip with a GM in order to get one that works correctly.
    - Last week's patch attempted to fix Celestial Elixir, Celestial Healing, and Torpor, so that they would stack another similar spell, unintentionally causing it to stop stacking with standard regeneration spells. This has been fixed. The downside to this fix is that these spells will now overwrite the "Call of Bones" series. As the three former spells are cast to save a character's life, this solution appears to be the most beneficial for all of those involved.
    - The stronger /lootnodrop options will no longer prompt you for confirmation when looting your own corpse. It is highly suggested that everyone reset their option to the strongest security available: /lootnodrop ALWAYS (always ask for confirmation).
    - Added some code to address an issue reported by a few players, that being that an NPC named "Ragefire" would die and not leave a corpse. Though we were unable to reproduce this problem in testing, the code we added should function as a catch-all for this problem. Please be sure to let us know how this goes.


    *Changes/Enhancements*

    - While not mentioned in the Producer's Letter, the Zone Experience Modifier has also been increased in Guk (guktop), by roughly 12%.
    - "Lay on Hands" has been enhanced again to heal more hitpoints.
    - Overlooked in the last patch message, new spells were made available to Rangers and Paladins in the upper levels.
    - "Voice of the Berserker", a beneficial spell, no longer drains mana over the duration of the spell.
    - The maximum duration of "Strength of Nature" has been increased.
    - A new spell for magicians is now available in game.
    - The "Shield of the Eighth" spell will now last a number of minutes equal to your level.
    - You can now use the arrow and enter keys to select characters in the character select screen. In addition, the last character you chose will be automatically hilighted when entering that screen. This value is stored in the EQCLIENT.INI file as "LastCharSel=0-7"
    - The ranger's "Trueshot" discipline has had its damage increased after analyzing data from its fix last week. Prior to the last patch, all bows, while under the discipline, were hitting as if they were 45dmg bows regardless of the delay. Last week's patch fixed it so that damage was based on the damage of the bow. This weeks patch increases damage bonuses in relation to delay (longer delays yield better damage bonuses).
    - You can now reverse the left and right channels on your speakers using the /reversesound command. This should be very useful for those who have had to place their speakers backwards due to cable lengths.
    - Made some improvements to Paw, Mistmoore, and The Hole, to address some concerns brought forth by the players. Both zones had some problems that would cause them to be exceedingly difficult in some cases.

    - The EverQuest Team

  • Corpse Poofing and Ragefire
    I just wanted to update you all on our research into this whole Ragefire and other corpse poofing thing.

    We’ve been trying to replicate the poofing of Ragefire, both on Test and on live servers. A lot of tests, using methods provided by players, and we can’t replicate it.

    However, we are adding some additional catch-all type code that should prevent Ragefire from poofing. That will go in with the patch tonight.

    So I’d be grateful if you could let me know if you continue to see this happening after the patch.

    In our campaign to make some areas more appealing, we have increased the experience modifier in Guk Top as well as all the others previously mentioned. Experience in that zone will be increased by about 12%.

  • XP Based on Levels in Groups
    Steevin wrote:

    [Quote from original post: For example, I'm a level 45 Bard. I'm grouped with a level 43 Cleric and a level 47 Warrior. We kill a mob worth 10,000XP. How is that divided between the three of us, and why?]

    Well, the nice thing about the changes happening tonight is that it’s now very easy to tell what portion of the experience you will gain from a group. I’ll use your example:

    Total levels = 135
    Total experience given = 10,000

    The 45th level character (it does not matter what class he is) will get 45/135*10,000 experience, that’s 3,333 (DO NOT ask me to tell you how the experience system rounds off, I’m not going to even ask about it…)

    The 43rd level character will get 43/135*10,000 = 3,185

    And the 47th level character will get 47/135*10,000 = 3,481

    Let’s check the math: 3,333+3,185+3.481= 9,999… ok, I lost a point someplace…

    But, basically, the only thing you will need to take into consideration if you are worrying about experience distribution when choosing group members is their level.

We want to reach that point sooner rather than later. But we’ll let you know when we feel we are there.

Server Population Issues

Brad McQuaid, who from the looks of it likes working on 3 day weekends ;), fired off a post on the Gameplay Board concerning server population issues:

    Hi all,

    I wanted to bring up this issue officially and also talk about some aspects of it, and how we are looking to solve some of the issues.

    First of all, we are working to come up with a new target server population at peak hours number. Our inclination at this point is a number somewhere between 2300 and 2500 players during peak hours. We feel we need to adjust this number because of how much Velious has added to EverQuest's size and quantity of content.

    We also feel that undercrowding is probably more detrimental to the game than overcrowding, in that while crowded areas are frustrating in that there is greater competition involved, there are some advantages to it (help from others when an encounter goes bad, help with CRs, etc.). (No, I'm not saying Overcrowding is good overall -- so please don't misunderstand me). Undercrowding, however, can mean a player can't find anyone to play with, and thus leaves. In other words, that frustration can be worse.

    There have also been some problems with packet loss and link death, specifically on E'Ci, when populations are growing high.

    So, what are we going to do about all of this?
    Well, before launching new servers we're going continue to analyze where players are at, what zones are crowded, and what zones are underused. Significant experience bonuses are going in next patch for underused zones (mostly dungeons).

    With E'Ci specifically we found out that after a recent hardware change, the network interface was incorrectly configured -- last night this was fixed, so please tell us if anything has improved.

    Also, when there are network problems out there with the Internet and a large group of players go link dead, this can cause zones to go down. We are working on this issue as well.

    So, there you have it, both gameplay and hardware solutions we hope will have significant effect. And please understand that any hesitation to launch new servers is due to undercrowding fears only -- if after these adjustments and attempts to better utilize all the zones we have it turns out target populations are lower than we believed, we will launch new servers, both split and new.

    thanks, and we look forward to your comments and feedback.

    -Brad
