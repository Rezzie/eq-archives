PvP Teams Revisited: Thoughts on Crossteaming
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:54 PM PDT
A new thread was started by Verant's Gordon Wrinn this Sunday centering on the issue of PvP Teams. Here's the entire post from the Gameplay Board:

    Hello,

    I posted this in another thread but it seems to warrant its own.

    The biggest difficulty in regards to PvP in EverQuest is that EverQuest is, and will remain, a primarily PvE (Player versus Environment) game. The addition of PvP to the game should not change the basic fundemental mechanics of EverQuest. PvP in EverQuest has to result in "EverQuest with PvP" rather than "PvP EverQuest", if you get my point.

    One of the largest issues on the PvP Teams servers is the subject of cross-teaming. Players of different teams get together for battles. Due to the PvE focus of the game, this has to be allowed for sake of game balance. Though some have suggested that cross-teaming, either in groups or guilds, not be allowed, it's something that will need to remain for the PvE game.

    The problem comes in when cross-teaming is applied to PvP. Cross-teaming creates a situation that is often referred to as the "invulnarable healer" scenario. Basically, one group of PCs (Team A) attack a group of people on Team B, while supported by a healer from Team B. In this fashion, you have a situation where a member of one team is taking an active role in the deaths of members of their own team.

    There is another "invulnarable healer" scenario that is more common and often just as bad as the cross-teaming scenario. This one revolves around a group of players from Team A attacking a group from Team B, while supported by a healer/buffer who is far superior in level to anyone from Team A or Team B. For example, a group of level ~20 trolls go on a killing spree in Greater Faydark, while supported by a level 50 healer/buffer. The problem in this case is that someone out-of-range is directly contributing to the deaths of players below their range.

    On Friday, I spoke with one of the programmers about possible solutions for the PvP Teams servers, assuming that people in general believe these issues to be a problem. Suffice it to say that there is no easy solution. Each one has its disadvantages and "loopholes". For an example of some of the solutions:

    1. Disallow cross-team buffing/healing
    2. Disallow out-of-level (+/- 8) buffing/healing
    3. Combination of 1 & 2.
    4. Redefine "Teams" servers to GvE (good versus evil) servers and implement idea #1 on that basis, rather than on cross-teaming.

    The problem with all of these solutions is that they have an undeniable impact upon the PvE aspect of the game.

    The final and best idea that we were able to come up with allows things to continue as they are now in regards to PvE, and only places restrictions on the basis of PvP. In this idea, any player who has taken damage from another player would not be able to be healed/buffed/receive any beneficial spells from anyone who wasn't on their own team or in the +/- 8 level range for a given amount of time (a few minutes), or until they zoned.

    In the cross-teaming "invulnarable healer" scenario, the person from Team B would be able to support people from Team A for PvE engagements, however once a member of Team A took damage from another team, they would not be able to receive support from anyone on Team B for a few minutes. In a real game scenario, the maurading band of trolls in Greater Faydark couldn't be healed/buffed by a high-elf assuming that they were taking damage in their attacks.

    In the out-of-level scenario, that same band of level ~20 trolls wouldn't be able to receive support from a 50th level cleric.

    There are some disadvantages to this, such as in the following:

    4 Trolls are grouped with a high-elf fighting PvE encoutners in a dungeon. Humans come in and "slap" the trolls every so often thus keeping the high-elf from assisting them. Perhaps this should just be a disadvantage for cross-teaming in general. Perhaps this is just a risk that people take from grouping with the enemy.

    What are your thoughts on these ideas? What other issues do you see causing problems in regard to this idea? Do the benefits (e.g. reaffirming the servers as PvP Teams) outweigh the potential disadvantages?

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits
- ChrisLTD @ 1:01 PM PDT
Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Official Boards:

  • Gordon or Abashi Please Answer This As Best You Can
    [Quote from original post: I believe EQ players currently have access to 3 Planes. I believe all 3 are malevolent. Are there any plans in the works for a benevolent plane to be made accessable to players. Just seems like an even trade off. Just thinking maybe some of the characters that are paticpating in the slaying of Innoruck and Cazic would probably feel more comfortable encountering their gods adversaries.

    In regards to your question however, though I can't really release any information, we're definitely aware of the players' desire to whup up on the minions of some of Norrath's kinder deities.

  • Need definite rule re: Invulnerable Healers on PvP team servers
    [Quote from original post: I am running through a zone when I observe a PvP fight. A dwarven palladin is laying the smack down on a poor dark elf. I check the health on the DE and seeing he is about to die, I heal and SoW him. This allows him to run away. Both combatants were in their low teens, whereas I am level 27. Am I going to be warned?]

    No.

    [Quote from original post: My guild runs to Nek forest and starts laying waste. We have 6 members, 5 of whom are level 20, and the sixth is a level 40 cleric. The cleric is only there to heal the other 5 and no one in the zone on the opposing team is high enough to attack him. Is my guild in trouble?]

    No.

    [Quote from original post: A group of Evils is attacking a group of Elves. Everyone in the fight is of equal level. However, the darks have brought with them a cross-teaming Elf, who heals the Evils, and can't be attacked by the other Elves since he is on the same team as them. Is the cross teaming Elf going to be banned?]

    No, there are currently no rules againt the "invulnarable healer", whether its due to an "out of range" healer or a "cross-teaming" healer.


    [Quote from original post: No one should be invulerable in combat and you shouldn't have to call an enemy to kills guys of your own race. A design flaw to be sure and this should at least be commented on or addressed by Verant. ]

    Brega, you can say that the system is flawed and should be fixed, but none of the solutions that have been presented have been endorsed by plurality, much less a majority of the players. There are certainly some pretty rash and obscure things that we could do to try and "fix" this to your liking, but what of everyone else?

    [Quote from original post: I've never seen this addressed by Abashi either. Most players just want Racewars to be coded as Racewars. Invulerable healing and buffing to help "Racewar enemy teams" defeat your own team should be coded out as impossible.]

    Sure, that seems simple, but what is the reasoning behind, for instance, a human paladin and a high-elf paladin to "refuse" (cf. be restricted by code) to heal or buff each other? Why *must* there be animosity between them?

    [Quote from original post: 1) Make the healer attackable by his own team for say the next 2 minutes everytime he heals (this happens in guild wars, so it should be possible); ]

    Can't do this with the way that attack rights are handle. In EQ, if I can attack you, you can also attack me. There's no way around that mechanism. The exploit that pops out is that all I'd have to do to be able to go kill my own team would be to cast a beneficial spell on someone of another team. EQ also can't handle a persistant agressor flag. We know who is casting the spell and who the recipient is at the time the spell is cast, but not after. The same is true with melee attacks.

    These are the options available to us:

    1. Do nothing.
    2. Disallow beneficial spells across teams. (handles cross-teaming problem)
    3. Disallow beneficial spells out-of-level (handles, for the most part, the out-of-level situation)
    4. A combination of 2&3.

    The difficulty is that while we want to support PvP and the Teams servers, we cannot in that process harm the PvE aspects of the game. It's still going to be EverQuest, but will be EverQuest with PvP, rather than PvP EverQuest.

    There's another possible solution (if indeed people feel that there is a problem) that one of the programmers and I were just brainstorming about. Please let me know if you feel that there are any unacceptable problems with this....

    How about... If a player takes damage from another PC, they can only receive beneficial spells from someone in their own team for X-minutes or until they zone.

    Let's apply this to the 3 scenarios that the original poster mentions:

    Case #1: Dwarf is attacking DE1. DE2 is over 8 levels above DE1. Since DE2 is out-of-level with DE1, DE1 cannot take his beneficial spells since he's being attacked by the dwarf. DE2's only option would be to attack the dwarf if he was in range, but in this case, he's not.

    Case #2: The level 40 DE cleric would not be able to support he level 20 marauders since he is out of range.

    Case #3: Evils are attacking elves. Assuming that the elves have done damage to the evils, evils could not be healed/buffed by the cross-teaming elf.

    Other scenarios:

    Human is grouped with 3 elves, they can buff each other all day long. However, a troll attacks the human. The human can now only be healed by other people on the human team.

    Human is grouped with 3 elves, and can buff, etc... The troll now attacks one of the elves. That elf cannot receive direct support from the human, but can from the other elves. The human could, of course, attack the troll if he was in range, just couldnt' heal the elf.

    Any problems with this idea that you can think of?

  • Abashi: UI Idea: Compass/Radar (mildly plagiaristic)
    [Quote from original post: In essence it is a compass (in that it shows which direction you are facing/heading) that has "blips" on the screen (a la Radar).     For the past several weeks, the complaints by monks here on the board (most of the complaints anyway) have been that they no longer outdamage warriors. However, when people now say, "We can't take hits like a warrior", I agree with that.
    Dealing out more damage than the warrior doesn't mean that you'll beat the warrior in a duel, nor that the ratio of damage dealing to damage taking capability is constant. The benefit of extra damage has more weight than the disadvantage of being defensively inferior.

    I'm sure that some would say, "If a warrior can solo creature X in two minutes and be almost dead at the end, then a monk should be able to do the same in a minute and end up almost dead". This would be an example of constant ratio between offense and defense and the two classes. This however isn't the case. If the ratio were constant as suggested, there would, again, be no reason to have the warrior, because the monk's other skills would make them preferable in all cases. Not to mention, there's an added benefit any time a creature is killed faster than normal, a benefit that can't be measured directly against the power of the class, but more against its usefulness to the group. Would a group, for instance, like to kill things in 2 minutes and end up OOM and near death at the end? Or would the rather do it in 1 minute and end up the same way? They'd want it done faster in all cases. That's what monks and rogues add as melee classes.

  • Probably been addressed but here goes... Changing your religion
    [Quote from original post: Ok is it possible to change your diety?
    If it isnt.. WHY NOT?]

    No.

    Deity selections are designed to have positive and negative effects upon what a character can do in game and how the NPCs react to them. We want that change to be permanent, as we don't want to see a situation where people switch deities based upon what is convenient at the time. For instance, if a really cool deity-specific quest or item comes in game, we don't want to see people flocking to a new religion.

    As it was established that a change in religion would not be allowed, the game architecture was built in a way that now makes such a change impossible. Your faction (which comes from your race, class, and deity) is determined when you first start your character and can't be changed on the fly.
