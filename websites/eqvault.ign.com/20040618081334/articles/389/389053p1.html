How to Raise Your Child - EQ Style
April 04, 2000 - It has come to my attention that many children have been neglected by their parents since the dawn of the EQ era, so I have taken it upon myself to replace some of the conventional wisdom that may have been causing so many tender minds to be snubbed in the past. Below are a few suggestions that I have chosen to share with those of you who have children, but find that when you are busy camping for your Ykesha or beating down the doors of Nagafen, you just can't find time to aid the child in coming to grasps with the world around them. I have detailed in this document the secrets to discarding the world, and replacing it with the things most important in all our lives... Problem: Child is curious why the sky is blue. Solution: Inform the child that it isn't actually blue. It is made up of a number of different hues that you have been able to maintain in the sky because of your great video card and you have it set for double layer so that it looks like clouds move independently. Problem: Grandmother dies and child is bereaved. Solution: Remind child that Grandma will be back. She just returned to her bind point, and is likely to be back to loot her body any minute. Make sure that you ask child if he would like to help Grandma get her exp back before bed time as well; that generally makes the child feel wanted. Problem: Child accidentally drops his gerbil off third floor of your house after hearing that they were related to the feline family and watches with dismay as the animal splatters thirty feet below. Solution: Congratulate the child and remind it what incredibly good experience gerbils are at that level. Recommend moving up to another mob soon though, because gerbils will be turning green soon. Problem: Child is horrified by water and he refuses to go to swimming lessons you have arranged for him. Solution: Comfort the child and remind him that many of his best items are underwater. Assure him that there is nothing to be afraid of since he can get a fishbone earring with a little patience, and he will certainly need the swimming speed he can develop with his trainer before he can put that earring to any real use. Problem: Child has acted out in class and has been put into detention for his actions. Solution: Be assuring to the child and suggest that he practice his little used skills while in detention. 2 hours should be more than enough time to bring those troublesome trade skills into line. Be sure to provide the child with some money to purchase any additional equipment that he may need to pick up while practicing. Problem: Child has difficulty in math and appears to be unlikely to pass the class. Solution: Start using mob base levels in the child's teachings. When multiplying 2 x 50, refer to it as Fire Beetle x Zazamoukh and when the child replies Excellent as the answer to the question, you have reached your goal. The child has become better at Math (100)! Problem: The child is going to a career day and is curious what path would be best to get information on. Solution: Suggest that the child avoid any careers that currently pay well. Programmers, lawyers, doctors, network administrators, and politicians are likely to get their salaries nerfed when the next patch comes out, so instead encourage such engagements as brick layer, bus boy, musician, and artist. All of these should be boosted considerably in the future for balance. Problem: Child is busted for fighting on school property and you are contacted to pick the child up. Solution: Reprimand the child strongly. Remind the child that if mobs are aloud to escape they will come back and cause trains. Instead suggest that next time the child should Snare the mob, kill it, and try to avoid letting it seek help. With those types of tactics the child should be far more likely to level quickly. Problem: You discover child has been stealing from you. Solution: Do not get angry. Try to push the child into a different center of focus. Rogues are a weak class, but you can't tell a rogue to quit. Have the child try killing a few things. Buff it a few times to see if it likes the augmentation role. After a few short rounds of trying any other class, the child is sure change its behavior immediately. Problem: Child has killed its substitute teacher with a pencil. Solution: Hire excellent lawyer to defend child from Juve under the argument that it was a rare spawn and should at least be killed once to see what it carries. Problem: Child is found drinking with friends after school in your back yard. Solution: Purchase more alcohol and join them. You never know when a high alcohol tolerance is going to be useful. Encourage the child and his friends to drink liberally and quickly so as to see their skills rise quicker. If your child has brought home an attractive teenage girl in his batch of friends, make sure to encourage her to drink more than the rest and begin a private tell conversation as soon as you have aided her in getting to her skill cap. Problem: Child has disappeared and you can't find its equipment. Solution: Make a new child that looks just like it. Look back with fond remembrance, but keep in mind, it was just one child and doesn't really matter in the whole scheme of things since you can have up to 8 on your account. Make sure the next one you make is a different class. *** DISCLAIMER *** I am not a parent currently, but I am however friends with a few parents who have found this advice sound. If you choose to use any of the above methods of child rearing, be wary that it does tend to cause detachment, painful urination, law suits, back aches, sterility, and heavy blood loss in some specimens. Check with your psychiatrist before taking any action described above. If you do however find that these work for you, be sure to tell two friends!
-- Carnuss Dire
