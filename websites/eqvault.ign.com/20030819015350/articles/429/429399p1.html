July 18, 2003 - James Parker, the Producer of Lords of EverQuest, was at it again on the official website. This week's Producer's Letter covers several issues:
Producer's Letter
Hello again,

Well, as most of you know, we have been hard at work doing internal testing for the beta; and we will have about another 2 or 3 weeks to test out the load. Hopefully, next week I will be able to give an exact date on the start of Beta. I'm of the frame of mind that you release a game when it's ready and not when people tell you to. So, we are taking a little extra time to polish up some of the loose ends.

In other news: very soon we'll have some brand new units and info coming your way, and we'll announce the next faction. I know that you all have been waiting, so I will give you a hint: Can someone tell me what faction casts that endless intellect thing-a-ma-jig? I hope that helps whet your appetite.

Now on to my favorite part of the week: questions and answers. (I just get downright giddy when we get to this part.)

What happened to the forums?
Before anyone gets out the rope and tries to wrangle me to a chair, I would like to apologize about the forums. It seems that they developed a little bit of a "cold" over the weekend; but, we should have them back up and running in no time. I mean, I told you that there were ninjas after me. Vehemence came in daggers-a-blazing and chased me up and into the boards; kicking over PC's, breaking monitors, destroying everything in sight. But, even though we got rid of them pesky ninjas for a little while, some damage was caused to the forums that we can hopefully fix. The good people in ops are looking at it, though. Vehemence will be just fine as soon as it takes a few days off and relaxes with some of Freeport's finest.

Will I be able to download the Beta?
There will be a downloadable version of the Beta to allow people to get in and play at their leisure without worrying about the cost of shipping or CD manufacturing.

Will people from foreign nations be able to participate in the Beta?
Well, as a matter of fact, if you go look at the Beta sign-up, I think that you will see a few players from foreign nations included within the registration process. Sorry for the delay on getting back to everyone with answers on this question. It took a little bit of time looking into legal issues and such. All I can say is: don't shoot the messenger.

Why are you making a Warcraft 3 clone?
This is my favorite question. For some reason, it always shows up as one of the top five questions of the week. For the record, I will say thank you very much for the compliment. Warcraft 3 is a great game. The experience I am having playing the expansion is amazing. There are a lot of industry rules that can be learned from the creators of this title. Lords of EverQuest will stand on its own merit upon release; then everyone can pass judgment.

Where do babies come from?
Oops! I think this is supposed to be for Dear Abby.

Well, once again I take my leave. As always, if you have any questions, feel free to send them to lordsofeverquest@soe.sony.com. I read through every one of them and post the most frequently asked questions to my producer's letter each week. If you have something on your chest, feel free to ask. See you all next week.

James S. Parker
Producer

P.S. People have sent me questions about VNV. I can't comment on it at this time.

P.S.S. What producer letter would be complete without a P.S.? Well, maybe one of these times, I will remember everything I was supposed to write within the body of the letter.
Thank you James!
-- Lepidus
