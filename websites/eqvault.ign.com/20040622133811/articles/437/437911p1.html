Fan Fiction: The Initiate, Chapter One
The first chapter in this exciting tale builds off the Prologue we previously released.
September 16, 2003 - More Fan Fiction is always wanted. So please, do send it in to Robert Swanigan, the man good enough to take care of this section.

Also, remember to read the Prologue to this tale before jumping into chapter one!


The Initiate, Chapter One
By: Phoebie Cupcake


Dust swirled around the horse's feet as Gremio pulled the reins tightly and screeched to a halt. Hoisting Rowena over his shoulder, he traipsed up the path to the modest hut and shoved his way through the door.

"MOTHER - M O T H E R come QUICKLY, HURRY...."

A small short-haired blond woman emerged from a back room wearing an apron, with soot smeared generously across her left cheek.

"For the love of Tunare Gremio, what do you mean bursting in here without....Oh God...." She stopped her scolding mid-sentence when she saw the wilting form of her only daughter in the arms of her son, who was pale and bleeding from several scratches and welts on his neck and face.

"Poison mother...she has been stabbed and poisoned." Gremio stifled a sob and dropped his chin to his chest trying vainly to stop the hot flow of tears he felt pressing at the corners of his eyes.

"What...how....poison? Gremio...how on earth....put her down on the bed, let me grab my herbs."

Gremio was always amazed at his mother's ability to stay calm amidst tragedies. No matter how frightening or mortal a wound, Felena Del'Arean approached every situation with calculated science and calm gentleness. Even now, as her only and youngest child lay unconscious from an unknown poison, she mixed and pressed ingredients quickly but skillfully into her pestle. Unwrapping the wound Gremio had so hastily bandaged, her eyes grew wide as she saw the 2 inch wide gash that stretched from Rowena's collarbone all the way over to the rotating cuff of her left shoulder. She reached for a kettle that had just begun to steam on the stove, and pouring scalding water on a clean rag, she pressed firmly against the wound and dabbed the area around the arm, pushing against the swollen patches and coaxing fluids out of the wound and into the rag. The metallic warm smell of blood filled the room as the rag soaked up what was left of the poison from the outer crevices of the cut. Rowena stirred slightly, her eyes rolling back into her head, her forehead clammy and wet with feverish perspiration. Felena pressed the salve into the wound, spreading the herbs and oils evenly over the cut until it was completely covered, and wrapped it tightly in rags soaked in boiling rosemary water. Rowena fell back into her semi-coma, breathing steadily but shallowly.

"That should pull what is left of the poison up to the surface. She has taken much of it into her bloodstream, and all we can do now is just wait, and pray."

Gremio saw his mother's ageless face tense up into a look of concern and fear, and he placed his arm across her shoulders, feeling her strength ebb as she relaxed into him and allowed herself the tears she had held at bay up until then. She wept almost silently, her small but sturdy frame convulsing quietly against his broad chest.

"Sssh, Marma...she will be okay, you have done well, as usual. Rowena is too strong and stubborn to fall to the weak poisons of some rogue orc. It will be alright..."

His mother straightened up resolutely, wiping tears from her cheeks, running her hand through her spiky blond hair. She busied herself with breakfast at the stove, glancing back frequently to eye her sleeping daughter. After a few minutes, she sat down with her son over scrambled eggs and fresh coffee, and Gremio told her what he knew about what had happened.

"I swear, Marma, Rowena really has a knack for finding trouble. It is almost as if she is marked by Bristlebane to be a target for mischief and controversy. Remember, even when she was a child, she was always in some sort of trouble at school or at prayer circle. I guess we all have our paths in life, and I fear hers will always be littered with the unexpected and troublesome."

His mother nodded silently over her coffee cup, and though she was not a woman of many words, her honesty straightforwardness always managed to hit those listening square in the face.

"It is time for her to grow up. Rowena can no longer wander aimlessly, daydreaming, beating up orcs, flirting with soldiers. She needs to have a purpose. When she is well, it is time for her to begin her training in earnest. She has what it will take to be a fine druid, but someone must force her to be more disciplined. I suppose, as her mother, that duty falls to me."

Gremio sighed. He knew eventually his mother would force Rowena towards a trade or a study that he knew would squelch the flighty and unpredictable nature of his younger half sister. Gremio had to admit; he would rather see Rowena safely studying and training somewhere as opposed to wandering around Norrath with him pursuing one dead-end adventure after another. He looked over at his sister, her face beautiful despite the drained color and cracked and dry lips. He loved her. He wanted nothing but the best for her, even if it meant taming her wild spirit and forcing her to embrace structure and learning.

Gremio reached across the table and squeezed his mother's hand warmly, and with a long sigh he resolved to support her decision about Rowena. He would do whatever he could to protect these two women, even if it meant being unpopular.
-- Phoebie Cupcake
