Kunark Zones

From EQVault Wiki

The continent of Kunark was introduced in "The Ruins of Kunark" expansion in March 2000.

Kunark has been the home of the dragons since ancient times. A race of intelligent snake men, the Shissar, once ruled the land, until a great plague wiped them out and left their slaves, the Iskar, to hold sway over the land. The Iksar race of ages past thrived and flourished, creating an empire whose only equal in all of Norrath was the mighty Combine Empire of old. The entire continent belonged to the Iksar, and their cities, vast monuments to their power, were scattered across its surface. They built vast waterworks to supply their cities with the water that they enjoyed, as well as religious temples and villages all over Kunark. It was impossible to walk for a few hours without seeing some stonework that the Iksar had created to mark their homes or their triumphs. All who entered the lands knew it belonged to this lizard race the first time they saw an immense statue of an Iksar appearing out of the swampy mists.

The Iksar formed five tribal states: Kylong, Nathsar, Obulus, Jarsath, and Kunzar. Kunzar was led by Venril Sathir, a young and powerful warrior-mage, who raised an army of undead with the assistance of the dark magics learned from the Shissar. Venril Sathir ruled until he died and became a lich. His son Rile took up the throne and continued to expand the Iksar empire, engaging the dragons in battle. He enslaved the giants and froloks. Rile died when the draghon Trakenon sunk his ship. Rile's son Atrebe succeeded him, and during his reign created the Sarnaks and a kind of flying creature from a dragon and a froglok. Atrebe was suceeded by his son Ganak, who fought a fiery battle against Jaled Dar and was killed along with Jaled over the Field of Bone. The dragons retreated, but before they left they destroyed the imperial palace and all Ganak's decendents in it.

Instead of a new king, the Iksar nobles created the Emerald Circle, who worked to rebuild the empire. Strife followed, and the empire was again divided into five tribal states. The Ring of Scale was also trying to destroy the empire from within, using their forces to create strife amongst the tribal leaders.

The mighty legion of evil forces, led by the great dragon Trakanon, assaulted the Iksar Empire. The slaves in Sebilis revolted, and goblin lords from Faydwer invaded. The giants mustered their own army to attack Iskar cities. Over a short number of years, the Iksar lost battle after battle until finally they were driven from their great capital city of Sebilis by Trakenon himself, who lives there to this day.

Following that, the tribes of Iksar were forced to survive in the wilds of Kunark for many years while they gathered their strengths. During this period, the basis of the modern Iksar society was formed. The necromancers gained in power over their evil forces, and the legions of warriors of the Greenmist were formed to help protect the tribes. Following their unity, they recaptured their walled city of Cabilis.

Even there, they suffered for centuries, trying to return their society to the basics, by which it had accomplished so much in the ages prior. Cooperation was fostered among all the sects in this small city, and gradually posts were obtained in the surrounding areas. Iksar who fought against this concept were cast out to survive in the lands beyond Cabilis' walls.

Now the Iksar civilization are at what many hope to be a turning point. Their society thinks as one, and is beginning to take on their ancient enemies in the surrounding lands, fighting the goblins, sarnak, and frogloks to reclaim the swamp over which they had ruled. The struggle is not an easy one, however, for as the good races of the continent of Antonica have become more powerful in the ways of exploration and magic, they have discovered ancient, unused gates into the lands of Kunark and have started to arrive in small groups. Shortly thereafter, small boats began ferrying adventurers to a small beachhead on the continent, the only stronghold of the vast lands beyond on this continent. The Iksar are waiting...(EQ Atlas)

[edit]

Burning Woods

On the continent of Kunark, this once verdant forest is now riddled with the aftermath of a huge comet or meteor. This place is home to a myriad of creatures, including a huge nest of hornets, named and unnamed wurms, huge apes and gorillas, sarnaks, and undead.

Over the meteror crater, Giants built a fort.

There is a very old joke concerning BW, which concerns where it is located at any given time, which stemmed from a teasing player telling a newbie that it Burning Woods moved to a different location at different times.

[edit]

Cabilis

is the city of the Iksar and is broken into Cabilis East and Cabilis West. Cabilis, or New Sebilis, as it is often called, is the new heart of the Iksar Empire. The Iksar recaptured the city long ago and have since made it their new home. Theirs is an ongoing effort to tame the surrounding areas to ensure the safety of the city. The traveler can find all the castes of Iksar here: crusaders, hierophants, kloks, vessels, keepers, nihilists, troopers, and monk trainers. One can even find the occasional froglok slave. The area around Cabilis is a swamp, with giant mosquitoes, scorpions, and leeches.

The Iksar have, for centuries now, focused internally on rebuilding their culture and society, to be stronger and to be able to face the outside world again. That time has come, and the Iksar are now pushing back into the lands where they once ruled supreme.

There are four areas immediately surrounding the city that are of intense interest to the Iksar. First is the Field of Bone, a barren area that contains the ancient remains of Kurn's Tower and Kaesora. The Iksar are focusing many of their broodlings in this area in order to tame it.

[edit]

The Swamp of No Hope

is an area that the Iksar currently are letting run wild, despite maintaining a small outpost to protect their walls.
[edit]

Warsliks Wood

is similar, as the Iksar have met much resistance from both the goblins and the forest giants within during their attempts to control the area. There is also a strong outcast presense there.

===The Lake of Ill Omen===, however, is another area that the Iksar are very focused on regaining control over. A new enemy, the sarnak, have established a fortress within this region and are expanding their range slowly, coming into conflict with the residents of Cabilis and also the goblins of the region.

[edit]

Charasis (Howling Stones or City of Kunzar)

Howling Stones was built by the vain Venril Sathir as his own personal retreat, where he studied and perfected the arts of necromancy. The zone is now filled with the remaining, eternal minions he creates for himself and the lingering spirit of his ancient companion, Drusella Sathir. (Eq Atlas)

This ancient city can be found in the canyons of the Overthere. The entrance is guarded by scopicus. A froglok slave, Drugoz, escaped, taking keys to the tomb with him, giving access to intrepid adventurers. Drusella Sathir is buried here, but can still be found in this haunted city, surrounded by her undead companions. In the city you will find golems, devourers, helot undead, sepulcher undead, skeletons, embalming fluid, undead oblations, crypt workers, undead oblations, swirling goo, a Golem master, and Drusella Sathir.

From a tome looted from the Dark Elf Spy in Northern Ro:

Nestled deep in the plains of the Overthere rests the cliff city of Charasis. This city of grandeur was home to the mighest of all Iksar tribes, the Kunzar.

Charasis is also recorded as the birthplace of the great Emperor Venril Sathir the uniter. Although Emperor Sathir ruled from the Kunzar city of Sebilis, he was rumored to have stored his most valuable possesions within the library of Charasis.

Emperor Sathir's beloved, Drusella, rests within a grand crypt in Charasis' depths. After her funeral and burial, Emperor Sathir cast all citizens from the cliff city. Only the royal guard and servants where allowed to stay, forever.

These Iskar were never again to feel the warmth of the sun or bask in the moonlight. It is written that Emperor Sathir crafted a spell to hide the entrance of Charasis from raiders and those who dared to steal his knowledge and defile his beloved Drusella's crypt. Emperor Sathir would visit the cliff city frequently. What he would do inside is not known.

Upon the death of one of Emperor Sathir's royal guard was revealed the secret to gaining entry into Charasis. Upon a tattered scroll did the guard record the secret. The scroll is now lost to the world. Taken as pure babble by the mortician, it was cast aside and lost to time.

After Emperor Sathir's death his son and new emperor, Rile is the last to hold the secret of entry. The ancient cult of Emperor Sathir known as the Arisen are rumored to have acquired the lost scroll of the royal guard. They are said to care for the crypt of the late Emperor Sathir.

It was during the time known as the End Days that the spirits of Tunare, Underfoot and the Rainkeeper destroy most of the great cities of the ancients. Charasis and its guardians and treasures are lost forever. May those entombed within guard her secrets from invaders.

So let it be known that Darkmask and Pizo have found a secret treasure, hidden in the Overthere, a land of many dangers, but apparently it yields quite a reward. To the library we must recover these lost items. (History of Norrath. SOE)

A locked Kunark dungeon (requires a key obtained via a quest involving two mobs around level 40), filled mainly with various undeads.

One of the specificity of Charasis is the multiple keys necessary to progress in the dungeon (unless you have a rogue friend that can pick the doors): there are 4 wings (North, South, East, West).

The North and East wings are the only ones that can be opened without a key, but the East Wing's stairs are trapped.

To progress in Charasis, one has to get West Wing's key from the North Wing's boss, then South Wing's key from the West Wing's boss, and from South Wing take a secret passage to East Wing where Drusella Sathir (the zone's boss, involved in many Kunark quests including the Veeshan's Peak key and the Iksar Shadow Knight's epic) is.

On top of two wings being locked, every wing's boss (but Drusella) is behind an additional locked door which requires a key found somewhere on the floor of the zone. Charasis' wings keys are all NO RENT, meaning that they're non-permanent items.

In 2004, a permanent Master Key (the Hand of Glory, able to open every door of Charasis) was added, allowing non-rogues players to bypass the regular and fairly annoying progression through the dungeon. Creating this key requires 5 items (one being a container) found in the various wings of Charasis.

Chardok is the site of the ancient sarnak military fortress that has existed for centuries since the time of Emperor Atrebe, and is considered by the sarnaks to be their home. The Sarnak Collective and the Brood of Di’Zok have their headquarters here. Many Di’Zok can be found inside, along with various Iksar and Ratmen slaves, Chokidai, and the various castes of the Sarnak culture. This place is home to the Overking Bathezid, Prince Selrach Di’Zok, Queen Velazul Di’zok, and the socerer of Di’Zok.

[edit]

Chardok, Halls of Betrayal (Chardok B)

is originally one of several "pocket zones" (zones which were appended to existing dungeons on a temporary, but as yet continuing, basis) Chardok B is populated by many undead Sarnaks as well as living Sarnaks loyal to Korocust, the hardest monster in the zone. The zone follows the theme of a haunted mine.
[edit]

City of Mist (Torsis)

is a haunted city for mid-level players. Found deep inside the Emerald Jungle in Kunark, the City of Mist is a lost Iksaar city Torsis. A great city with many levels, it was the pride of the Iskar Empire. Now it is home to many types of golems and undead, slimy goos, and other creatures. Adventurers can also find the lich Lhranc, and a number of reaver lords.
[edit]

The Crypt of Dalnir

located in Warslik's Woods is the hidden burial tomb of Dalnir, the ancient Haggle Baron of the City of Cabilis. The crypt is controlled by Kly, originally a slave of Dag the Blasphemer Iksar, who made the magical dagger with the forge of Dalnir. Kly and a band of crusaders disappeared with the dagger, never to be seen again. They have taken over the tomb and expanded it for their own, currently unknown, purposes. The coerced of every race who have fallen under the Kly’s sway protect the upper floors while the Kly continue their experiments in the tunnels below. One can also find goo, undead, and ravenous nibblers patrolling the halls. (EqAtlas)
[edit]

The Dreadlands

is the area of mountains and plains surrounding the ancient Karnor's Castle, the home of Venril Sathir and his drolvarg army, n the middle of the zone. Scattered with ruins, the area includes an area of forest inhabited by the Drachnids, as well as the edge of a massive glacier extending down from the mountains, where one can find Mountain giants and tundra Yeti. Buried within a secluded valley is an ancient outpost of the Combine Empire. One may find the ferocious Drovlags, or ycanthropes here, as well as undead and cockatrices. (EqAtlas)

Twin spires serve as the teleportation foci for wizards and druids, but are now nearly obsolete.

[edit]

The Emerald Jungle

is a vast, beautiful, and dangerous jungle. It is separated from the rest of the continent by a large cliff where it is sunken into the earth. A grad staircase allows travelers to descend to the jungle floor. An ancient city, the City of Mist, lies hidden in the twisted trees. A dangerous place, one must be on the lookout for tigers, huge apes, soulsippers (giant mosquitoes), iksars, undead, Errolosi bloodthorns and mantraps, which are flesh earting plants, and trakanosuars. (EQAtlas)
[edit]

The Field of Bone

is the starting zone for the Iksars. It is home to Kurn's Tower, a newbie hunting ground for undead and living creatures. The Field of Bone is the original site of a vast reservoir for the city of Cabilis and the nearby structures. Long ago, however, the fatal battle between Galat the Ikasar and Jaled Dal the dragon was held in the skies above and the magical energies unleashed there forever charred the surface of the earth. The skeleton of Galat's steed, a hybrid of froglok and dragon, can still be seen there. Buryai, Iksar outcasts, wolf hunters, undead, scorpions, scarabs, spiders, and Iksar guards can be found here.
[edit]

Firiona Vie=== also termed FV, is a zone bordering on several other Kunark zones. The ===city of Firiona Vie

was once a High Elven outpost, and the destination of the Elven boats from Timorous Deep. Following the war with the drolvargs, however, the elves fled to the east side of the zone. The zone itself is in fact much larger than the city which occupies only a fraction of it.

The Land of Firiona Vie was established by the High Elves under the direction of King Thex to the lands of the Iskar. Dominating the outpost is a statue of Firiona Vie, avatar of Tunare. Beyond the outpost, a forest stretches. Ancient Iksar statues and structures dot the landscape. The beach area near the outpost teems with evil drachnids. Pilgrims, drixies, pirates, forest giants, leeches, and goblins roam the woods. Sometimes an ancient Jarsath skeleton walks the forests here. Deep in the forest is a giant outpost, and beyond that, a drolvarg village in an ancient temple. The ruins are infested with frogloks.

The guards dislike wolf form of any kind, so watch out!

[edit]

The Frontier Mountains

are the highest and roughest set of mountains located on the continent. These harsh mountains cut across the western half of the continent, and are inhabited by a variety of powerful, large creatures, for whom the peaks are mere hills. Many of these creatures operate vast mining operations in the mountains for the minerals that lie beneath them, and the adventurerer may find the Mines of Nurga and the Temple of Droga in these mountains. The unwary may be attacked by goblins, buryai miners, mountain giants, sarnaks, or gorging brutes. The mountain giants have established a huge fort in the middle of the area. (EQ Atlas)
[edit]

Kaesora

is the ruins of an old underground city led by a Sarnak named Xalgoz who holds loot required to build the key to Charasis. Deep within this zone is a ruined library now guarded by ghosts, spirits and feasters.
[edit]

Karnor's Castle

is the home of Venril Sathir, a boss mob who features prominently in a number of epic encounters, as well as many undead creatures. Karnor's is famous for having a large number of trains that continually disrupt adventuring in the area. Expect to hear shouts of "Train Left!" or "Train Right!" and be ready to move. (A train, in EQ terms, is large group of monsters, from 3 or 4 to as many as 20 or more, chasing a player who is trying to "zone", or leave the area.)
[edit]

Kurn's Tower

Kurn’s Tower The history of Kurn's Tower is a convoluted one. The tower is named after Kurn Machta, an evil warlord of the Sebilisian Empire during King Rile's reign. Rile's son, Atrebe, performed many experiments on various creatures within Kurn's Tower, eventually resulting in the creation of the Sarnak race. Now, however, the remains of the temple are haunted by the creatures destroyed in those experiments, and the burynai are using their necromantic powers to slowly reclaim the Tower. (EQ Atlas)

[edit]

The Lake of Ill Omen

is the ancient site of the city of Varnek, which was cursed ages ago and has since sank beneath the waters, giving the local area its name. The reptilian sarnak have moved into the area and have secured an ancient fortress to the southwest. The goblins have established a mine in the hills in north of the lake. The Brood of Kotiz has taken it upon themselves to eradicate the sarnak from the area, and have directed their trainees to do so. Bloodgill goblins, sabretooth cats, Iksar brigands, barracudas, and undead can be found in and around the lake’s shores.

In old school EQ, this used to be referred to as the "Lake of Ill Manners" because of all rude /ooc and shouting that went on there.

[edit]

The Mines of Nurga

was revamped from a low level dungeon to a 55+ level dungeon. The Mines of Nurga are part of the large underground home of the goblins. Long ago the mines were a vast network of busy tunnels. Now all that remains are the ruins of the functioning mine and slaves busy doing the work by hand.
[edit]

Old Sebilis ("Seb")

Sebilis is the vast, ancient capital of the Iksar Empire, founded long ago by the Emperor Venril Sathir. From here, the Iksar Empire ruled for many years. Then the wurms, led by Trakanon, came and destroyed the Empire and their capital city, claiming it as their own. Since then, it has fallen into ruins. It is now inhabited by frogloks, myconids, scarabs, and undead, all still loyal to the undead Emperor Sathir.

Sebilis has many levels to it, housing live and undead frogloks on the upper floors, live frogloks and myconids (mushrooms) in the sewers below, and golems and live froglocks along the path toward Trakanon. A key is required in order to enter Sebilis, obtainable from a quest completed outside in Trakenon's Teeth.

===The Outpost===is the name of the evil city established by the dark races on Kunark. The city is allied with Venril Sathir. If you hunt drolvargs in Karnors Castle's, be wary when venturing into the Outpost, as they don't take kindly to people who hunt Venril Sathir's allies.

[edit]

Overthere

The Overthere is a vast area of plains that gradually evolves to reach the northern shore of the continent of Kunark. There is a vast area of ravines and chasms that have existed for centuries, infested with the evil Scorpiki, ancient protectors of Charsis. The evil races of Norrath have also claimed the shore of The Overthere and have established their beachhead here. One can find all kinds of creatures here: cockatrices, Sarnaks, moving succulents, and much more. (EQAtlas)

[edit]

Skyfire Mountains

is a rocky, fiery outdoor zone with dragons and wurms galore. Home of the red dragon Talendor. Items for the Cleric Epic 1.0 drop here, so you will often see solo clerics and groups searching for "pearlescent shards."
[edit]

The Swamp of No Hope

is also a starting zone for Iksars, although not as popular as the Field of Bone. It is a vast marsh, extending from the southeast corner of Cabilis far to the east. After the fall of the Iksar Empire, the frogloks, ancient enemies of the Iksar, retook the swamp and made it their home, built a massive town deep within the swamp and near to the spring that feeds it all. For many years they retained control over the swamp, despite frequent conflicts with the Iksar of Cabilis, who occasionally take them as slaves. Unfortunatly, they lost their ancient home and now reside in a refugee camp in the Rathe Mountains.
[edit]

The Temple of Droga

was revamped from a low level dungeon to a 55+ level dungeon. It is adjacent to the Mines of Nurga.
[edit]

The Overthere

contains an outpost for the evil races to use as a base in their exploration of Kunark. It is a large zone populated with beasts and living plants, as well as wandering Sarnak. Charasis, and Chardok are both located in the Overthere, and it is a direct route to the Skyfire Mountains and the Frontier Mountains.
[edit]

Timorous Deep

is the ocean that lies between Kunark and the other continents of Norrath. The Deep is dotted with islands that stretch across it, and these islands contain a mixture of the modern powers on Norrath, those that have fallen out of their societies, natural predators, and ancient civilizations.

A raid encounter dragon lives here, and various islands occupy hunting and quest opportunities for players. The Deep is separated into many separate islands, some of which are much more dangerous than others, with the most dangerous being the Raptor Islands. The raptors that live on these are very powerful and very aggressive, and it seems that if you can even see one it will attack you once you step foot on the island. Apparently, their clawed feet are also webbed, and they can swim faster than you as well.

The other aggressive creatures are the powerful golras on the iceberg, and occasionally a much more powerful golra named Halara appears here. All the golras are aggressive to everybody. There is also a powerful ogre that appears in the ogre encampment named the Great Oowomp.

There is also a dragon that frequents the southwestern corner of the Deep around the Combine Teleportation Room. This dragon is aggressive and found in the water, and as powerful as Nagafen. The other creatures in the Deep are also aggressive, like the bandits, the undead, and the ogres and elves, and all these creatures will group together. The Spirocs will also group together and fight, and are as powerful as the raptors, although less aggressive. (EQAtlas)

[edit]

Trakanon's Teeth

is commonly remembered as the place where Sebilis is found. Trakanon's Teeth is a Kunark jungle, much resembling the Emerald Jungle, and houses various ruins, rivers and lakes. The answers to finding the key that allows access to Sebilis may be found within.
[edit]

Veeshan's Peak

At the time the Ruins of Kunark expansion was released, Veeshan's Peak was the hardest zone of the game. It is the fortress of the Ring of Scale faction of dragons and is inhabited by six dragons: Druushk, Hoshkar, Nexona, Phara Dar, Silverwing and Xygoz. To access the zone, you need to do a long and difficult quest to obtain a key. Players are then able to pass through the portal located in Skyfire Mountains. Once inside, players have to keep moving constantly as it full of Racnar and Lava Drake which are quickly (2 minute respawn timer) replaced once they are killed. When the zone was new, teleportation was not possible inside the zone, so players had to clear the zone to the end to get out of it. This restriction was lifted several years later, and players can now exit the zone by any of the usual methods. Recently the zone had been revamped and a new, though no less difficult, a quest has been added for obtaining the key.

[edit]

Veksar

was rumored to have been originally an all underwater dungeon similar to Kedge Keep in the original Kunark expansion, but limitations in underwater AI caused the zone to be dropped from release. It was later added sometime around the Planes of Power expansion.
[edit]

Warsliks Woods

is a massive, rough forest that spreads from Cabilis to the ocean to the north. In between lies the most untamed area near to Cabilis. This area is under the rule of the forest giants, who have made this area their home for generations, and the goblins, who long ago took over the ruins of an ancient trading post overlooking the gorge that runs through the area. (EQAtlas)
