Tuesday, June 13, 2000


Gordon Infobits
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:31 PM PDT
Gordon has been busy on the Gameplay Board lately. Here's all the latest posts:

  • Iksar Faction Issues
    It's not a bug. Iksar have some serious faction issues.

  • "Gordon Caught in a Lie" Thread Deleted
    I deleted it.

    The message "quoted" two things that I never said, neither in letter nor in spirit. I figure if someone is going to have the nerve to call someone a liar, they should at least get their facts straight. Besides, I really have enough work clarifying the things that I did say, let alone the things that I don't.

    Now if someone wants to ask if I said "XXXX" or request clarification when I said "XXXX", feel free. But don't come on the boards declaring as fact that I said something that I didn't, calling me a liar for it.
    -------

    [Quote from original post: We're still considering these issues. Though we don't want to see level 60 folk bottom feeding on dragons, we don't necessarily want to buff them all up either. We'll come up with something, but everyone will have to wait for the release of the expansion to see what that is]

    Actually that quote was specifically about Dragons. We have something in mind for that, and wanted to get it in at launch, but had to put it off for a while. I wouldn't be surprised if you see something happen along those lines in the near future.

    As for the Planes, I believe that I said that we are sensitive to everyone's feelings concerning the planes and that we had no plans at that time to upgrade their difficulty.

    That's still accurate.

    [Quote from original post: But just like the rest of your post, you leave room for error on your part]

    Error, changed minds, reevaluation, etc., sure. Nothing is absolute in this business except for the fact that nothing is absolute. Things change, ideas are refined, and arguments are analyzed. "should", "might", "probably", and "may" do not mean "will" and shouldn't be taken as such.

    We have to be flexible to remain responsive.

  • Balance and Equality
    This thread appears to be going along quite well without me, but I did want to comment on this quote.

    [Quote from original post: If a wizard and a warrior are balanced, they are both about equal in power level.]

    Actually, offensive power is only one component in the equation. Usefulness to a group is another, which leads to versitility. Versitility is a big one, meaning that classes that are very versitile may not be as offensively powerful or useful to the group as another class in a specific role.

  • Faction Messages
    I'm not sure who you may have been speaking to or what they meant, but the information isn't correct. Faction messages are all printed, though it is certainly possible for someone to be unaware of the particular faction that an NPC is on.

  • More Fun Spells
    [Quote from original post: I have been thinking lately that there should be more "fun" spells in the game.. Spells that dont' neccisarily (misspelled) have a purpose in the game other than to play around with.]

    I do like the idea of fun spells, and would like to have more of them around. We have several in now, including a line of magician summons that are there for fun. Of course, one of the problems with fun spells is that people have an expectation that all spells should be "useful", with "useful" being defined as something that helps advancement in one form or the other.

    It would be interesting to hear how many would like to see more fun spells of this nature in the game.

  • Favorite Class Duo
    Though every situation is different, I've personally been fond of the Necro/Rogue combo. The necro ability to both debuff movement rates and fear lets the rogue do a heck of a lot of damage.

  • Reforming Guildes
    As far as getting the guild reformed, that will only happen if the guild leader comes on and does it himself.

    Once a guild is created, the person who created it has rights to the name, and can create or disband it on other servers as he sees fit. Ownership of guilds is system-wide, not server-based.

  • Lost a Level
    I suppose that it might be possible that whatever problem disabled the use of spells also affected the experience attached to the corpses of anyone who died at the time. The GM Staff, however, are the ones who would determine this and decide what reimbursements if any would be awarded.

    You'll really need to contact them per the Guide's advice.

  • Moving Characters Between Accounts
    We've spoken about it. Moving characters between accounts just opens up too many loopholes where the ability could be used for something other than intended. Though I do indeed sympathize with those who are in this boat, there are too many ways around any verification that we could do to insure that the transfers aren't being used for illegal sales of characters.

    I do apologize to those who we cannot service in this matter.

  • Death Penalty Comments
    [Quote from original post: I can see a fair amount, but let's face it the death penalty in this game stinks!]

    Drootes, if the death penalty did not hurt, it wouldn't be a penalty. If everyone was happy with it, then it wouldn't be serving it's function.

    [Quote from original post: The death penalty is completely wrong and needs to be more in line with reality.]

    Now *that* would be extreme :)

  • Couple of Questions Answered
    [Quote from original post: GM's can do an Exp res when there is a game play problem right? Well then why do you have exp loss (you still don't get all your exp, but most), you still get res effects, and you lose all your mana and most of your HP. If a GM has determined that you deserve a GM res, why not have it a full, no strings attached res?]

    The process that attaches the "Resurrection Effects" is on the back end of the resurrection code. At the time that the effects are applied, all it knows is that you have been resurrected, not by what spell, which is why it's attached to all resurrections. I agree that this is a bit of a pain, and I'll see if we might have another way to determine what kind of Rez has been cast.

    [Quote from original post: 2.) What is with all the random faction hits in the Lake of Ill Omen?]

    They aren't precisely random. They occur when you are on the hatelist of a creature killed by another group. Basically, it's part of the Hatelist propagation issue described here a few days ago.

    For instance, you attack NPC A, who yells for help to NPC B, who yells for help to NPC C. NPCs A and B are close enough to you to chase after you, however NPC C is not. NPC C wanders around the zone and yells for help to NPCs D, E, F, and G, who are all too far away to come after you. However, since they all have a copy of the hatelist, whenever they are killed you will take a faction hit.

  • Massive Spell Glitch?
    I've heard about the serverwide-spell failures, it affected servers on one of our ISPs. Players having a problem should camp out and log back on, or zone to restore their spell casting abilities.

    Please be cautious in play until the issue is resolved. Our Ops team is working on it as we speak.

    Players who were killed may petition for a resurrection.

  • PVP Comments
    [Quote from original post: I'm sorry....by taking away a benefit to non "gankers" who can bind isn't going to be a fair solution. Part of what makes having a cleric/necro who can bind in a area close to where you are doing a dungeon raid to get a rez is to lower down time. If on PvP, you in sense want to increase down time because a few "exploiters" want to ruin the game, then you are causing more problems than you are fixing.]

    The challenge has always been to add PvP as a viable option without adversely affecting or trivializing the PvE (Player versus Environment) aspects of the game. That said, PvP needs to have risk associated with it. Since item loot creates a caster/melee risk disparity, and since coin-loot is only a negligible risk, and since experience loss isn't going to happen, restricting binding might might be the best way to go.

  • PvP Suggestion Response
    Being very honest with you, any type of experience loss for PvP deaths is not at all likely to be put in EQ. The GM/Guide staff is already inundated with requests for customer service based upon harassment even though there is substantially little risk. The core engine of EQ cannot support the changes that would be necessary in order to implement experience-loss on PvP in an equitable manner.

    That said, I do recognize the need for risk in PvP. One idea that I'm partial to is making it so that binding is *only* allowed in cities, regardless of class. That would encourage people to win battles, punish them if they lose, and be fair for both melee and casters from a PvP perspective.

    Thoughts?

  • Robe of the Lost Circle
    Scaevola certainly has my sympathies for the whole situation.

    Here's what happened for those interested:

    1. Scaevola fails to make the Robe of the Lost Circle. Of course, at this time, there is a chance of failure. The fact that I posted that we're removing this skill check the next time we patch items has no bearing on what is in game now. The failure isn't a bug, but rather a poor decision on the part of the quest designer. These happen from time to time.
    2. A GM gives him a robe. This was a mistake on the part of the GM who thought that failure was a bug.
    3. The GM later finds out it wasn't a bug and takes the robe back. This was probably also a mistake, as the GM should probably have let him keep the robe then deal with future requests for serivce along these lines by admitting that he'd made a mistake. Of course, what he wanted to avoid was a bunch of peopel deciding to go and make their robe under the mistaken impression that they'd get reimbursed if it failed.

    All of that said, giving the robe back to Scaevola at this point would be a further mistake, as things are now the way they were back before the GM got involved.

    I certainly apologize to Scaevola for the whole series of events.

  • When can I buy the expansion upgrade again?
    [Quote from original post: Am I correct in that since the release of Kunark, there has been no way for someone who already owns Everquest to buy a copy without re-purchasing the entire game?]

    Yes, that's the case. The "Online Store" was taken down shortly after Kunark launched due to some distribution, billing, and shipping issues. We're still working on these.

    [Quote from original post: Please tell me that's not the case and where I can buy the expansion.]

    If you need it right away, the only option currently available is purchase of the retail box, who's key you can use to upgrade your existing account. I've heard that a few retailers are offering some pretty sizable rebates to existing EQ subscribers, so you should be able to get it near the expansion-upgrade price.

  • Feign Death
    [Quote from original post: Today i used one of my spells that is supposed to save my life at least once in a while. I was in Karnor's catle and 4 drolvgar were pulled when i was not in a group yet. 2 died and i went oom and very low health so i feigned and checked to see if it worked. It worked both were indifferent to me.]

    Every experience I've had with feign death indicates that it either works, or it doesn't. This is the first I've heard of it working, the person having the item to /con each creature that was on them, then it breaking for no reason.

    Possible reasons it might have happened:

    1. Another mob came into range and wasn't fooled.
    2. Perhaps hit by an AE spell? (haven't never been hit by one while feighed before, I'm not sure if this would do it.)
    3. It's possible that the critters might get several chances to see through it if they are just standing around. I'll check into this tomorrow.
    4. Some bug?
    1-3 would not be bugs if it is one of those that was happening.

    Please remember that creatures do not get a direct save versus feign. If you get a "fallen to the ground" message, none of them are fooled. If you do not get that message, you have then fooled them subject to a low percentage chance of the creature making it's intelligence check.

    You do, however, get an indication if a creature isn't fooled, it keeps beating on you.

  • Spawn Rate Question
    If that's indeed what has to be done, it does sound a bit out-of-whack. I've forwarded this thread to the Live team for them to check it out.

  • TZ/VZ Question
    [Quote from original post: My question is this - say 15ppl from TZ ask to move to VZ yet 600ppl ask to move from VZ to TZ. Are you still going to allow that switch to take place?]

    That's a good question, and we're still tabulating numbers here. If the move shows a complete imbalance in those wanting to move from one to the other, we may reevaluate the decision.

    [Quote from original post: and then he continues "and I don't like the idea of moving, but I"m so tired of the prophets,"]

    I would be interested in hearing how many people registered to move from VZ to TZ in order to get away from a guild on VZ.

  • Are GM Ressurections constrained by 49 cleric spell?
    [Quote from original post: GM ressurections constrained by 49 cleric spell...]

    No, they are not. They return 100% of the experience lost. They used to be constrained, not by the 49th cleric rez, but by the XP modifier for the zone. All rez's were constrained by this.

    It was corrected a few months ago.

    [Quote from original post: 2- The 49th level cleric spell has a 2 hour time limit on xp return.]

    All rez's have this two hour limit, not just the 49th cleric rez. We're currently looking into the possibility of having the GM Rez excluded from this time limit, but it's difficult, so I don't have an ETA.

    [Quote from original post: Also, when you make a petition and log out, that petition is cleared.]

    No, it's not. Petitions are held for several hours before they expire.

Gordon Infobits
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:00 PM PDT
Here are more excerpts and quotes written by Gordon Wrinn and found on the Gameplay Board:

  • TestEverQuest.exe
    [Quote from original post: What gives? Was this a mistake during the recent client upgrade I think I heard about? Ive never had access to the test server before, and I dont think I do now, so where in heck did the shortcut (and corresponding executable) come from?]

    "Testeverquest.exe" is the patch program and loader that will give you access to the test server. The shortcut has probably been on your desktop all along, or, perhaps someone else on your system put it up there for you.

    As for logging in, the entire interface is the same except for the server-selection screen, which will only list the Test server as a valid server. I would imagine that if you had problems logging in, though I'm not sure at what point you had those problems, it may be because the Test server goes up and down frequently to put in the newest changes.

  • Mother of All Whines
    [Quote from original post: heh, verants been aware of the alchemy problem since last year and they STILL havent touched it]

    Er, after we (finally) relized that there was a problem training the skill, alchemy as a whole was revamped. In addition, prices are being lowered. The changes that didn't go in in the last item patch will go in in the next.

    All of this was "touched" since last year.

  • Procs on RoK Stuff
    All those who can wield the sword are melee types. The addition of the mace allowed some other classes, not melee in nature, to obtain and use an item with a similar effect. The SSoY proc's sooner because it is only for classes that deal their damage primarily with weapons.

    There is also the matter of the higher ratio as someone else here mentioned.

  • Rogue's Hide/Sneak Messed Up?
    [Quote from original post: I play a rogue and a necro why is it that when my rogue hides and camp he is visible when i log back on but this doesnt happen to my necro invis spell? when my rogue zones he is not hidden but by necro is invis?]

    Much like illusions, "sneak" will not cross a zone border. Camping is equivalent to a zone border from a funcationality standpoint, which is why the same behavior is observed. It's not a bug, but rather the way it works.

    [Quote from original post: when I hide next to a Bard in my group his song brakes my hide... but the caster siting next to him is still invis?...]

    For this issue, we're looking into changing hot it works. This also goes for the similar issues with caster invis.

  • Bugs on the Board
    Hello all,

    Every once in a while someone will post all of the reproduction steps necessary for our test department to investigate an exploitable bug, and "bump" the post in hopes of it getting acknowledged.

    Though I do appreciate the fact that you want to bring the issue to our attention, and I do forward the issues to the test team, I generally won't reply to a report of that nature here on the boards as I don't want to call the attention to it, thus spreading the information across the net.

    I would ask that if you have something that is exploitable, meaning that it can be used to harm other players or allow unreasonable gains for the user, please email it to eqtesting@verant.com, rather than posting it here on the boards.

  • Concordance Still Having Trouble?
    [Quote from original post: Although the leggings did combine once to make a rune, 3 attempts failed to combine to make anything. Losing your quill is a bummer not say the trouble of having to go find a burned out lightstone.]

    Hmmm, I haven't heard of any intermittent problems in this regard. If a recipe works once, and does not check a skill, it should always work. The caveat here is that it is assumed that there isn't another item, with the same name, in game, that isn't accounted for in our tables.

    [Quote from original post: I can't help but think it is some perverse design built into the game that when I wanted a GLS, all I ever saw were burned out ones. Now when I need a burned out lightstone all I ever get are GLS's.]

    We have Murphy on retainer as a consultant.

    [Quote from original post: I have noticed that the ring-mail components for making Study runes (beza) are more expensive then those required to make Expert runes (beza). Was this intentional? You can buy a ringmail braclet from a merchant for 5pp. I try to aquire my own components when possible.
