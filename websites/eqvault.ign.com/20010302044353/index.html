Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:16 PM PST
Alan discussed and talk about a lot of issues today on the gameplay Board:

  • On Druid Tracking Issue
    Kenross is right on the button.

    And I kind of doubt that lowering the ranger tracking cap to something like 50 would have been seen as an improvement by many rangers. It might have been an improvement because of the way the skill worked, but reducing their cap would probably have upset people.

    All that really matters now, though, is that it's pretty much the way we want it, and it's working great.

  • On Magical Death Item
    Never heard of it.

    And, well, maybe they were pulling your leg.

  • New Producer's Letter
    Ok, it wasn't actually written by the producer… but it's up anyhow.

    Take a look by clicking here.

  • Wizards and the Vision
    Calvarro wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Yes, we are making some positive changes to wizards.]

    Yes, we are making some positive changes to wizards.

    Alan

    I'm not being flippant. It's true. But you'll just have to be a little patient.

  • Bashable Doors
    Wooden doors would have to bash on their ripostes...

    I'll mention the idea to the team.

  • Q&A To The Extreme
    drizzut_1 wrote:

    [Quote from original post: 1. Are there plans to split any servers in the next month?
    2. Are there plans to add any new servers in the next month?
    3. Are there plans to split any servers in the next 3 months?
    4. Are there plans to add any new servers in the next 3 months?]

    Not that I know of. But that doesn't mean that there won't be new servers some time in the near future, just that there aren't any plans that I know of for one. As I've said several times before, as soon as we make such plans, we let you know.

    [Quote from original post: 5. Are there plans to increase lvl 55+ spell drops in the next 2 months?
    6. Are there plans to sell lvl 55+ spells (that are currently dropped) from vendors in the next 2 months?
    6a. Are there plans to make lvl 55+ spells (that are currently dropped) questable in the next 2 months?]

    We're still looking at all that stuff from the class talk threads. Again, as soon as we have solid plans about these things, we'll let you know. I'm not prepared to predict the unknown…

    [Quote from original post: 7. Will there be an adjustment made to the Cleric/Hate issue on the next patch?]

    Seems likely. There are changes on Test now. They might go live, they might not. Until we're done testing them we don't know. But it seems likely that at least some of the changes on Test currently will go live.

    [Quote from original post: 8. When the servers drop as they did today can we PLEASE get something posted on the system status/scheduled downtime page to inform us of what is going on?]

    That often depends on why they went down. Often the servers seem to go down because of ISP problems at one end or the other. We do our best to get a notice up. But for very short-term outages it's not always practical or useful to put up a notice.

    [Quote from original post: 9. Will changes to the epic quests be made in the next month?]

    I don't know. We're working on some changes. Because these changes are complicated and important, we're not going to make them until we're ready.

    [Quote from original post: 10. Are there plans to make the Pegasus cloak tradeable?]

    Not that I know of.

    [Quote from original post: 11. Are there plans to change the faydedar spawn so that at least a handful of druids can get their epic?
    12. Are there plans to move ranger/druid drops off VS or at least split them up so you don't have 2 classes competing for the same drops?]

    See answer to #9.

    [Quote from original post: 13. Will more boats be added within the next 2 months?]

    Not that I know of.

    [Quote from original post: 14. Are there plans to change the SLeeper's tomb key to soulbound item?]

    Not that I am aware of.

    [Quote from original post: 15. Are there plans to fix the Monk Mend issue in the next 2 months?]

    We're working on locating the problem.

    [Quote from original post: 16. Are there plans to release the zone THE WARRENS in the next 2 months?]

    That is planned. We'll announce it's release when we nail it down.

    [Quote from original post: 17. Is POM Itemized at this time?]

    As far as I know it is.

    [Quote from original post: 18. Has anyone from a LIVE server gotten any items out of POM other than the cards we are hearing about?]

    I don't know.

    [Quote from original post: 19. Are there plans within the next 6 months to release diety specific quests (such as POG)?]

    Deity specific? I don't understand the question.

    [Quote from original post: 20. Are there plans to reduce the UBER MOBS HP or offensive power in the next 2 months?]

    We are looking at some of the creatures of Velious for potential change.

    [Quote from original post: 21. Would you consider setting up special chat room sessions with people who post on this board to discuss customer communication issues in a small group format?]

    We have developer chats just about every month, hosted by fan sites. We are considering hosting some ourselves. But it doesn't seem likely that we would restrict attendance to any sub-group of players.

    [Quote from original post: 22. Are there plans to upgrade ranger defense in the next 2 months?]

    Again, we're looking at all the class balance stuff. We'll certainly let you all know when we've made decisions.

    [Quote from original post: 23. Are there plans to implement better written polls on the log in board (the last one on customer service gave many of us no choice to really voice our opinion on the quality of customer service)?]

    No. We plan to start writing polls in thieves cant so that nobody can understand them… :)

    I find it amusing that you would ask a question that leaves me no way to answer it under your rules (yes or no) that is so skewed that just by answering I must also agree with your assumption that the poll in question was poorly written. Perhaps you should reword that and ask again.

    [Quote from original post: 24. Do you play on a live server?]

    Sure do.

    [Quote from original post: 25. If the answer to 24 is Yes is your character above lvl 50?]

    I don't talk about my characters. I will not reveal level, class, race… none of that. Doing so, unless I could claim to have played all classes and all races to 60th level, would do nothing to improve my credibility. It would only serve to focus people on the facts of my class preferences and be a hindrance to conversation on nearly any topic.

    [Quote from original post: 26. Are there plans to give the clerics a JOLT type spell in the next 2 months?]

    Not that I know of.

    [Quote from original post: 27. Are there plans to implement more portal type spells in the next 2 months?]

    Not that I know of.

    [Quote from original post: 28. Are there plans to revamp POA in the next 2 months?]

    I don't know. I haven't heard any such rumors.

    [Quote from original post: 29. Are there plans to implement more bind points in the next 2 months?]

    We've already announced such plans. The exact date of implementation is not set. Again, we'll announce them when we know them.

    [Quote from original post: 30. Are there plans to change the Succor type spells so they will become more effective in cuttong down on travel time (ie DL and WK place group near a current portal rather than the opposite end of zone which would be MUCH more helpful in cutting down travel time)?]

    I have no idea. It seems unlikely to me, though.

    [Quote from original post: Are there plans to reduce the Spellbook med from lvl 35 to lvl 20 within the next 3 months?]

    Not that I know of.

    Sorry, I don't follow restrictions like only yes or no. :) They certainly don't make your questions easier to answer. Some of your questions just can't be answered with only a yes or no. My general answer is that we'll announce changes as we decide them.

  • The Term "Broken"
    [Quote from original post: Stop using the term and come up with something that is more true to the situation.]

    Actually, I only use that term when it's accurate. When something was fixed because it was broken, that's what I say. When something was changed to affect the way the game works, I say that.

    *shrug*

    I try to be very accurate with my terminology. In the long run it makes everything much more coherent.

  • Recasting And Armor
    I'll pass your message to the programmers.

    And you can always e-mail me ideas if for some reason you don't want to post them here.

    avancouvering@verant.com

    But I'm not the one that makes the job offers around here. :)

  • Temple of Veeshan Experiences
    Thread closed due to testosterone contamination.

  • Of Druids and Taking Things Out of Context
    [Quote from original post: In response to the word the tracking cap on druids had been increased Absor said:
    "This was done at the request of the Druid community." ]

Brad On Community Boards & Feedback
- Aurum @ 6:18 PM PST
This is the original post and Brad McQuaid's response from a thread devoted to various community issues:

    They should just change things as they see appropriate. They should have invested the time and energy they put into these boards to have more people test the game actually IN-GAME.

    It is THEIR game, they can make the rules however they want. Allowing the player base a forum to vent and suggest changes has actually decreased customer satisfaction. It looks good on paper, and is a tremendous effort to keep communication open, but it has been a failure. Yeah, they probably gleaned a little useful info off of these boards. But has it been worth the headaches? Have they gotten the return on the investment? NOW the customers perceive it is their RIGHT to mandate changes to VERANT's product. Of course we can just stop playing. If they never would have opened up this can of worms, players would just....PLAY. Of course we would get angry over issues or changes, but an open forum such as this amplifies dissatisfaction.
    I know it is not quite right to compare other traditional games to EQ, but have you ever heard of a game or product manufacturer that goes through so much effort to solicit customer input? And 99% of these posts are negativem it seems.

    I appreciate the effort, and I can imagine the meetings when they decided these boards were a good idea.

    "Yes Alan, we will have a whole site dedicated to hearing our customer, and responding to their needs on an ongoing basis! Johnson, you work with Bradley and design it. That is the key to a long lasting MMPRPG people, real time customer feedback."
    Maybe it has helped EQ to last longer. EQ has lasted a long time, and I bet will last a few years more. But they also have learned some very important lessons.

    First, the majority of people in the world are selfish morons who are unable to see past the tip of their nose.

    Second, soliciting input from this majority is a BIG DRAG and largely pointless. They end up spending days and hours and hours sifting through the unending load of crap and looking for a little gold.
    And third, message board detail is NOT a fun assignment, but great for shuffling some poor schmuck into the limelight for 15 minutes. Wonder if just prior to reassigment here the next 'victim board moderator' goes home to his/her spouse all excited about their 'big break'? "Honey, this is big time, I am on the front line between our cutomers and the company. Man, I cant wait until Monday...this will really look good on a resume...I am really gonna change things there too"
    Like I said the effort is appreciated, but the resource committment is misplaced.


    "Honey, nothing is WRONG, I just hate that place. I have been listening to people whine and cry all day on that @#$@ board, I dont need you whining now when I get home..."

    Thanks for the feedback so far (excluding the x-files Aradune = Manglosh sillyness).

    We will continue to read this thread very carefully.

    I will say that if we do make a major change, we will still absolutely have a very real means by which our players can give us feedback. This board has been a huge resource for us (and hopefully many of you) -- many, many changes have been made because of ideas and feedback from this board, and so any change we might make cannot risk losing that feedback.

    -Brad

Producer Letter: Customer Service
- Aurum @ 6:00 PM PST
George M. Scotto wrote the latest Producer Letter found on the official EQ site. Customer Service and several policy updates were the main focus of this letter. The big and most important points of the letter can be found in this excerpt:

    Communication will be the key and, naturally, it’s a two way street. Your concerns have been voiced to us, and we are responding. As you read this, several policies are in the process of being refined. Within the coming weeks, we will be posting several new policy FAQ’s at http://www.everquest.com/support/index.jsp. Our goal is to make it perfectly clear not only what we expect from you as player-citizens of Norrath, but also what you can count on from us here at Verant/SOE.

    Our first step in response to players’ concerns was posted recently. Our new and updated Naming Policy (http://www.everquest.com/support/naming.jsp) should hopefully be clearer, more consistent, and bring us even closer to a healthy role-playing environment. Subsequently, we will be conducting a thorough review of all current character names on all servers, including surnames and guild names. Names not necessarily in line with the updated Naming Policy will be evaluated and handled accordingly.
Thanks Alan.

My name is Lyle Harris and I'm a reporter with the Atlanta
Constitution. I'm doing a story about people who play massive, multi-player
online role playing games and was interested in talking to guilds or clans
based in the metro Atlanta area. The story will be published in the paper
on Wednesday, March 7, and I need to interview several people as soon as

Required Reading For Official Boards
- Aurum @ 7:25 PM PST
Auric Flamewalker wrote up an extensive post on the rules and policies enforced on the official boards:

    Hail and well met all.

    I have seen recently many questions regarding why a post was closed or deleted. The main reason we close or delete a thread is when it is an attack on one person or a group of people. Below are the guidelines for posting on this forum. Please read through this carefully if you post frequently on this board. Any posts that do not meet the criteria below will be deleted or closed. Any infraction of these rules can result in warnings issued to your Everquest accout, suspension or banning of your Everquest account, depending on severity of the offense.

    1.0 Official EverQuest Forums
    This section of the FAQ is primarily concerned with the Official EverQuest forums, and the conduct that is considered appropriate and inappropriate when posting to those forums.

    Q: What is the purpose of the Official EverQuest forums?
    The Official EverQuest Forums exist as a place for EverQuest players to gather and discuss issues related to EverQuest. Some forums (e.g. Customer Service, General Discussion, Quality Assurance) are visited frequently by official representatives regularly, and serve as a place for players to communicate with some of the various EverQuest teams (e.g. Development, QA, and Customer Service). These forums allow us, as developers, the opportunity to discuss issues that the players feel are important.

    Q: Who may post on the Official EverQuest Forums?
    In the initial implementation, "Customer Service / Technical Help" will be open to the public. While anyone may read all of the forums, the remaining forums will only allow posts from active EverQuest account holders.

    Q: Why is posting to the Official EverQuest Forums restricted?
    While we would like to keep posting open to everyone regardless of their account status, in the past, the official EverQuest forums have had some problems maintaining a suitable posting environment due to the relative anonymity of the posters. With little to no tangible reason to keep posting behavior in check, some posters took advantage of the anonymity provided by the forums to post often vulgar and otherwise inappropriate material on the boards.
    As EverQuest is a family game, with people of all ages participating in the gaming community, we as developers have a responsibility to maintain a suitable environment to the best of our ability. The only way to accomplish this goal in regards to the Official Forums is to reduce the relative anonymity of the forums, thus allowing us to eject posters who post material that is inappropriate for public consumption.

    Q: I'm concerned about security of my account. If I must log in with a valid Station Name to post, doesn't that give someone half of the key needed to hack my account?
    No. Though you will be logging in (through a secure connection) with your valid station name and password, the station name will not be displayed. Instead, entirely within our local network, the Station login server will connect to the registered account database and obtain the "Chat Handle". This is a unique identifier that you chose when creating your EverQuest account.
    All posts made to the board in restricted forums will display your "Chat Handle" rather than your "Station Name". Should you wish to change your Chat Handle, you can do so within the EverQuest chat server. Type #help within the chat server for a list of commands.

    Q: What are the rules for posting in these forums?
    As the Official EverQuest Forums are part of The Station, posting to these forums is bound by The Station's Terms of Service that can be found in their entirety at http://www.station.sony.com/services/tos.jhtml

    In addition to these terms of service, we have a few supplemental rules that apply to the EverQuest Forums.
    In general, you may post any material, written in a courteous and mature manner, providing that it is on-topic for the forum to which you are posting. This includes material that disagrees with the way that we, the developers, operate the game. We will not interfere with the communication of thoughts and ideas as long as the presentation is appropriate for all those capable of reading the forum.

    You may not, however, post any material that:
     Attacks or insults others on the board. Feel free to debate the idea, but do not turn your disagreement into an attack upon the poster.
     Engages in name-calling, harassment, or threats. See above.
     Contains obscenity, vulgarity, or profanity. Though we do have a filter in place to catch the unintentional obscenity from time to time, this is not a license to abuse it.
     Disparages any religion, race, nation, gender, or sexual orientation.
     Is considered inappropriate for children 13 or over as governed by general standards of decency in the United States.
     Infringes on anyone's privacy rights.

    Furthermore, you may not:
     Make multiple posts on the same subject for the purpose of getting attention paid to your issue.
     Post off-topic comments in threads for the purpose of drawing attention to another thread on a different subject.
     Monopolize the front page of any forum by "bumping" threads of the same or similar content to the front. We may intervene by closing threads any time that this occurs.
     Use the official forums as a staging-ground for creating general unrest within the game (e.g. Sit-ins).
     Use the official forums as a place to advertise the sale of EverQuest characters or items in exchange for real-world money.
     Advertise web-sites with content that violates these rules, the EverQuest EULA, or the Station Terms of Service.
     Advertise products or websites that are not related to EverQuest or other Verant/Sony Online Entertainment products.

    Q: What happens if these rules are broken?
    Action that we might take in regards to a post in violation of these rules on the Official EverQuest Forums will vary depending upon the transgression. It could include any number of the following:
     Editing of the post.
     Deletion of the post/thread.
     Closing of the thread.
     Suspension (results in user being unable to play EverQuest for the duration of the suspension).
     Banishment (results in the user's station account being canceled. User will no longer be able to access EverQuest, or any Official EverQuest Forum).
     Legal Action (I.e. Contacting law enforcement personnel in regard to a threat.)
    People who violate the rules of the boards may or may not receive any warning before any action is taken. In the event that a warning is given, the warning will be recorded upon the game account and may result in further action should the player show a pattern of engaging in prohibited conduct. Philosophically and administratively, there is no difference between conduct in game, and conduct in the Official EverQuest Forums.
    Q: Ok, we know what we shouldn't do, what should we do?
    Give your posts some thought, and try to be courteous and respectful. Disagreeing with someone does not mean that you have to be anything less than courteous in your dealings. Read over what you plan on posting before hitting the "Submit" button, and imagine how you would feel if the post was aimed at you. Would you be personally offended? If so, you should consider rephrasing the post or canceling it altogether.

    Thank you for your time and thoroughness.
    Auric.
