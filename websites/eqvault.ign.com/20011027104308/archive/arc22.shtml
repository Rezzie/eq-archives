In Development Updated
- Aurum @ 7:22 PM PDT
A rather large update was posted on the In Development section today:

    04/12/2001

    We're finally starting to get back on a regular biweekly patch schedule. Here are the things we are currently working on that you can expect to see over the next few months:

    Loot rules in the Warrens: We plan to continue modifying the special loot rules in the Warrens. We will be eliminating several ways around the current "Trivial Combat Code" as well as looking at the problem of "spell twinking" in regards to the loot rules. We'll have to see how it goes, and see what people think.


    Stonebrunt Mountains: The next "free" zone will be the Stonebrunt Mountains, which will be located off the Warrens. It will support a level range roughly equivalent to that in Lake of Ill Omen. We're expecting to release the zone sometime this summer.


    Dungeon Touchups/Revamps: We have plans for some updates and/or revamps for some of the dungeons in the world. Stay tuned for more specific information as it is released.


    Big Changes Planned for PvP: We've had a number of meetings lately to plan changes to the PvP environments in EverQuest. One of the first things planned for this is to bring up a brand new PvP server with a new rules set on it. This will allow us to test our changes for everybody without throwing any current rules sets out the window. The name of this new server and when it will actually be going up are still to be announced.


    Continued UI Enhancements: A number of things are planned, from allowing people the ability to buy stacks of items at one time, to improving the way things look on the screen.


    3D Engine Enhancements: One of the first things up is the enhancement of the engine to support 32bit color with a 24bit z-buffer. This will get rid of the "moving stair step" visual artifact that you see in distant places where surfaces meet at odd angles. For the best explanation of this, look at the blockiness of the river in the Great Divide or the lava river in Skyfire, and finally look at the emblem at the entrance to Veeshan's Peak while moving off at a distance. For those with video cards that support a 24bit z-buffer, appearance will be greatly improved. Update: This is actually now implemented however is not yet bug- free. You can take a look at the new capabilities by setting VideoModeBitsPerPixel=32 in your EQClient.ini. Note: If your card doesn't support 32 bits per pixel, you will probably crash. Also, your Deity Specific Quests: We're going to continue expanding on some deity-specific quests that went into game several months ago. We like the idea of these and have more in the works.


    The History Unfolds: Keep an eye on the Harpy's Head Tavern site. As dynamic events take place across all of the servers, more information about the history and the future of the world will be made known.


    Dragons, Ice Dwarves, and Giants: We're going to continue adding more quests and flavor to these three major Velious factions. Our goal is to continue balancing out the three paths so that the risk vs. reward is balanced for each one. Be on the lookout for new mobs and dialogues popping up in each of these races' territories.


    New NPC Return Policy: Who hasn't accidentally handed a semi valuable item to a mob and gotten the "Thank You!" message? We are currently working on a way for mobs to return any non quest items back to the player who accidentally gave them to you. More on this will be announced as it develops.


    Class Balance Discussions: Some recent changes have been introduced as a result of Absor's now infamous Class Discussions. We're continuing to evaluate each class on an individual basis and will be making changes based on player feedback as we see fit.


    Velious Magic Resistance: In an effort to further balance the SoV expansion, we're taking another look at the unnaturally high magic resistance of Velious mobs. We'll evaluate this on a zone by zone basis and make changes to better balance out the risk vs. reward in the zones for both melee and caster player characters.
    We'll fire off more updates as we have information available for release. Until then, enjoy EQ!

    - The EverQuest Team

    Inspection
    I still have yet to understand why the /inspect on/off was added to the game. This was supposed to keep people from accidently inspecting you when they target you.

    I would of preferred to see the /inspect on/off make it so others cannot inspect me.

    Bolerro
    18th Season Druid

    Let me go over the philosophy of 'inspection'. The first thing to keep in mind is that nobody is actually inspecting you, at least not in the way that the word would indicate. They are looking at you closely, not turning your clothing inside out to see the labels.

    Inspection does not show your britches.

    What it does show are the things that the game engine doesn't show. If your character is wearing a cloak the only way to know it is to 'inspect' them. The game engine isn't showing what would otherwise be obvious. Inspection only shows the name of an item and it's type (and only that by the icon that shows in inventory). Inspection does not reveal what is in a bag, stats on any item, or what color underwear your character has on. It only shows enough information to allow other players to know what your character would actually look like if the game engine could show every item you are wearing.

    There is nothing invasive about inspecting someone.

    Now if the messages that you receive from the game about someone 'inspecting' you are bothersome, you can turn those off. That would prevent people from using inspection to annoy you.

    I don't disagree with the idea that it is polite to ask someone before you inspect them. But, like many courtesies, it certainly is not necessary. You have the tools you need at your disposal to remove the factor that irritates you about it, should you choose to use them.

    [Quote: About 4 patches ago, "bind sight" was changed in some zones to require line of sight from the caster to the target. There was no announcement of this change, and it considerably reduced the utility of the spell.

    Our 'spell guy', Lawrence Poe, had this to say in response to your question:

    [Quote: This was an unforeseen side effect to a change we made recently.

    The change was made to all spells. Basically we made it so if you are casting a spell be it harmful or beneficial and your target runs out of the spells range, or the casters line of site the spell will not go off and no mana will be used. This was a great enhancement however it had the unforeseen side effect of limiting bind site. There are currently no plans to revert Bind Sight to its former functionality. ]

    Not much more for me to add. The functionality of the spell has most certainly been reduced. But you can still stay bound to the target for the duration of the spell, even after it leaves your line of site. The check is only made at the time of casting. You just can't use the spell to attach yourself to another NPC from the first to chain yourself out to infinity.

Alan on Class Talk
- Aurum @ 7:06 PM PDT
Alan spoke for a while on the status of the Class Talk Threads in a recent update on the Dev Corner. Here's the entire post:

    We have been receiving a fair amount of mail regarding the status of changes to classes based on the items brought up in the class talk threads that I started a while ago. Right now I have little new information for you, but let me spend a moment talking about this.

    The team has read and reviewed the lists that I put together. We've had several informal discussions about them, and a few formal discussions about specific items on those lists.

    So far you've seen a lot of changes based on your input. They have been going into the game as they get finished and tested. Things that didn't require a lot of in-depth investigation on our part, such as changing the Bread Pants for Bards, increasing the drop rates for many Kunark spells, fixing the Track Corpse spell and many others.

    And even some of the more complicated issues have been looked into. Things like giving Druids and Shaman the ability to act as secondary healers, upgrading Mark of Karn (a very complicated spell to balance), the aggro created by debuff spells (and, yes, the aggro created by healing spells), and others that have required a lot of consideration have been added or changed.

    We've also started on some of the much more complicated issues, such as Wizards and their ability to do damage against certain NPCs with the Bane spells.

    To finish the lists we'll have to make the time to have some long and complicated meetings over issues that can't be changed without rather a lot of effect on the game world. We have not had those meeting yet. Recently we decided that we would put PvP issues to the forefront, considering that lack of attention they have had over the life of EverQuest.

    I just wanted to let you know that we haven't forgotten the issues that you brought to our attention.

    Alan

    Pacifists DO make poor adventurers. There's nothing false about that statement.

    What I think you disagree with is that adventuring is a poor focus for roleplaying. I disagree.

    While the focus of EverQuest is adventuring (much like most pen and paper games), it certainly doesn't exclude the roles that you've mentioned. Explorers certainly have a place in Norrath. Explorers in a dangerous world are basically adventurers anyway. Historians, cartographers, merchants, sages… all of these are not roles on their own. If you are playing a cartographer that likes to sit around the house and read maps, or a historian that likes to read history but doesn't like to talk to others about it, then you don't really need a multiplayer game. Hermit is so much not a roleplaying idea (sure, the GM could use it, but as a player character a hermit would never, ever, be in a game… Evangelists would be something that would be hard to do in EverQuest.

    Yes, you can play a cartographer in EverQuest, and play one well. But I won't argue that such a character is somewhat limited by the mechanics and content of the game. Most certainly you can't make and sell (or give away) maps in game. That is partially a design limitation, but primarily a technological one. EverQuest does not have any sort of automated map making system by design. But then if it did, who would need a cartographer. As for drawing your own maps in game, that is more of a technology issue than a design one (or, if you want, it was a design decision not to develop the technology to allow such a thing).

    But none of the careers you mentioned are directly prohibited by the game itself. In most cases where they are limited the limitation comes from technology or time restrictions, not a desire to hinder roleplaying. The best example would be your evangelist. Despite the fact that you can't actually change the little word(s) on the character description that the player sees, you most certainly could 'convert' people. You could convince them to work for the betterment of your chosen deity. You could convince them to do things that would bring their faction in line with the factions best suited to your deity. You could convert someone to your religion if the player of that character was convinced that his character would be converted.

    Oh, and it is possible to be a 30th level tailor and still not be able to fight your way out of a paper bag. It would be really hard, but it could be done.
