Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 6:38 PM PDT
Mr. Poe assited Alan several times today by helping to explain certain spell questions on the Developer's Corner:

    Scale of Wolf
    MagisteRex wrote:

    [Quote: I posting to learn if the way Scale of Wolf works currently - drops the way in invis does if you cast or attack or are attacked - is a bug or as intended?

    If as intended, please reconsider this. Druids get an upgrade to sow in wolf form at the very least and I believe they get one other. Bards call all outrun sow. Why cannont Shaman have an upgrade that is functional? It is worthless even as a long distance traveling spell since it drops if you are attacked. I have ended up in bad situations due to casters casting on me and even due to a greenie skellie getting in a lucky hit as I passed it.

    This is absurd.

    If the way it currently acts is a bug, please fix it! ]

    I don’t want this to end in a lecture… so I'll do that now.

    This was a perfectly nice comment. You made your points, you had your reason, and you were making sense. And then you decided to add the "This is absurd." line. This line changes the tone of your post from upset, but rational to upset and verging on irrational. It does not make your point stronger and it will not somehow make us more likely to agree with you.

    I just needed to point that out.

    Mr. Poe has an answer to your concern:

    [Quote: This effect was changed several patches ago. Scale of Wolf will only drop if the spell expires, the enchantment is dispelled, the person with the buff turns on auto attack, or the person with the buff casts a spell. The spell will not drop if a spell is cast on the person with the buff, or the person with the buff is attacked. ]

    So, while it was not a bug, it was something that we decided to change.

    [Quote: I think you should stop and take a look at the fact that all of the translocate spells are being pointed towards light-race held areas. Not one of these teleports is geared towards Dark Elves, Trolls, Ogres. Arguably Iksar too. Throw in a Nektulos or a South Ro please. ]

    Today seems to be teleportation day here on the boards… :)

    More from Mr. Lawrence Poe:

    [Quote: These spells are being pointed towards "four post" wizard spires, as they are the strongest conduits arcane teleportation on Norrath. ]

    Thanks for the information, Mr. Poe.

    Ro's Fiery Sundering
    [Quote: As far as I know the only way to get this spell is to kill Sifaye which give you bad Tunare faction, or get incredibly lucky and nail Garou who is defintely on Norrath's Most Wanted list.

    Is it intentional that Verant put one of our most useful and sought after spells on mobs that would ruin our PoG faction if kill them? Quite a few of us rather like keeping our faction. So that means we have to pay 10k plat to buy them from necros.

    And having such a good spell be hard to get isn't contrary to our intent. Good stuff should be hard to get. We're adjusting the frequency of its appearance a little. But it's never going to be easy to find.

    Spell Stacking Bug?
    Nicol wrote:

    [Quote: Topic: Spell stacking bug

    The ranger spell call of earth overwrites/blocks the enchanter epic quest effect speed of shissar.

    The latter is almost always the preferred buff.
    Their effects are unrelated, far as I know. ]

    Actually, they both add armor class. I believe that the ranger spell adds greater armor class than the Speed of Shissar, and that would explain why it is the spell to take precedence.

    This is not a bug. There will be cases when you must choose one spell over the other. Not all spells will stack, nor are they intended to. Making decisions about which spells to have cast on you is the same sort of decision that you have to make about which weapon to use or which armor to wear.

    Voltaic Draught / Draught of Jiva
    [Quote: I hope this is the correct forum for this question:

    I acquired a spell the other day Draught of Jiva and found out I already had it but it was under another name, Voltaic Draught! These spells are not merely similar they are identicle in terms of resist, mana, casting time, spell recovery and damage per level. (the only difference it seems is the level of the spell: one is 54 the other is 55) Since I find this type of thing no where else in the EQ spell lists I have to ask whether there has been some mistake in the creation of this spell.

    Validata Tempestas
    Wizardess Of Saryrn ]

    They may appear to be the same, but they are not. Draught of Jiva has a slight stun attached to it.

    Critical Hits With Bolt Spells
    [Quote: Hello,

    I have recently been hearing about "Bolt" type spells doing critical hits (doing about double their normal damage). I was wondering if this is true or merely a rumour.
    Any information would be much appreciated :)

    Feynan Escafon'ea
    45th Wizard
    Karana Server ]

    It's a phenomenon, not any sort of new special skill… here's what Lawrence Poe (spell guy) had to say about it:

    [Quote: We have not added critical hits to bolt spells. I've read a couple emails about this phenomenon. I spoke with Steve Clover about this, what appears to be happening is that the mage will cast a bolt spell and due to lag or the zone server not updating it will miss and fly off. The caster of the bolt will cast another bolt spell as the 2nd bolt spell is hitting the first one will correct itself and also strike the target, giving the appearance of a "Double attack" or "critical hit". ]

    I asked, 'cause it seemed strange to me, how a bolt could wander away like that. It seems that it's not something that strange. Bolts aren't that different than NPCs. If one can go astray, the other can.

    So if you've seen this happen, check the circumstances. If these double hits have occurred after a previous bolt seemed to have missed or you thought it just failed to report damage (and might have, indeed, missed), then that would explain it.

    Questions about Cannibalize 2, 3 ,4
    RazcoForte wrote:

    [Quote: what is the significant changes ?
    ex. HP to Mana exchange? ]

    Lawrence Poe, spell guru, has a short answer for your question:

    [Quote: The Ratios of Cannibalize II and III were made slightly better. The hit point cost remained the same, but the mana return was slightly increased. ]

    I also wanted to address something that I've been hearing a bit of lately. The changes to these original spells and the addition of the new one don't dramatically increase the power of the spell line. What they do is address an issue. Shamans everywhere were using Cannibalize I in preference to Cannibalize II because it was the more efficient. The improved spells address that problem. That's why they are only slightly better. The higher level spells are now preferable to those of lower level.

    Teleport Spell to Bind Spot
    [Quote: I dont know if anyone has mentioned this before but I think it would be a much welcomed addition to the teleport line of spells! Teleporting a group to the casters binding spot would speed up guild raids, trips to town. There have been many times I got a ride to a druid ring and then got left to walk as they gate to town. Toss the idea around and see what you think please.

    thanks,

    Radrez of Lanys T'Vyl ]

    A spell like this will probably never be created in Norrath. There are far too many ways that such a spell could be abused. We're certainly not set against the convenience that this spell might add. But there are far too many ways to use a spell like that to trivialize zone content and the challenges that are presented in the game world.

    Sadly, this isn't much different than watching many episodes of Star Trek where the writer has to create silly reasons why transporters can't work in a given area or why they can't use their replicators to do, well, just about anything. I mean, if you can transport people by taking them apart and putting them back together again, then you would never need a doctor. Nobody would need to die…

    But I digress. The idea here is that we don't want to introduce things into the game world that, by their very nature, make many of the zone designs and concepts meaningless. So, this spell will probably never be seen in Norrath.

It is true that sometimes these "safe spots" are not always safe, but we try to fix them when we find them. In the case of extraplaner zones however, there really are no "safe spots"]

    The Plane of Mischief
    Manglodin wrote:

    [Quote: Was just wondering, since most items are kind of vague in figuring out what combos make what in PoM. There will be items that, upon first getting, will go to the wrong class. Are you aware of how the quests/items work there and does this seem correct to you?]

    Yup, we're very aware of how the quests work. I announced those changes the second I heard them so that you would have as much warning as possible.

    Since then we've looked into other, more interesting ways of fixing things that we deemed wrong with this area (I'm not going into details, I love the fact that this zone is complicated, and I'm not spoiling things). I'm not sure how, exactly, we're going to make these changes yet. And it might very well include making many of these items No-Drop, as I mentioned. But I think that you'll see that it works out well in the end, and making those items No-Drop shouldn't actually be a problem for those that are playing in the zone. We're adding levels of convolution to the zone. :)

    I can't explain it further, but if the items do become No-Drop, we are aware of the concerns that folks have brought up, and we know that it's nearly impossible to tell what's what up there. We're not going to make it so that those that do the experimentation will get the least reward. As to how that will be accomplished, you'll have to trust us.

    In-Game Story and Plots
    Machineghost wrote:

    [Quote:
    Still, you have to admit that there is a running story to the world. It is the story of Firiona Vie and her companions, a story of GM events, and zone changes. Bloody Kithicor, the whole Bertox thing in Qeynos, the dragons who are outcasts, this is the story of EverQuest. Granted, there are lots of sub-stories, and other stories in the world, but I think most people would agree that the real story revolves around the stuff with epic characters and worl-changing consequences. ]


    Ok, I'll admit that. :)

    But I disagree a bit. While those quests do lead to world altering events, they aren't really the 'story' of the game. While some folks do get involved in those events, and they become part of the story of their character's life on Norrath, for some these events are tales not reality. To me, at least, the real story involves my characters.

    [Quote:
    Apostrophe problems in the Subject again =)...just for note.

    One thing that I've always wondered about concerning the "dynamic" nature of EverQuest, the ever evolving storyline, and the variations between Servers, etc.

    Now, I understand that there are some storylines that come up dynamically that cross all the servers (like the Dark Elven war that changed the face of Kithicor). My Cleric has sniffed out another brewing in the Karanas (tremendously well done, BTW :), and smells the work of the foul Bertoloxxus behind it. Those seem to also coincide with big product releases, and that's a very cool thing as well.

    But, beyond those, it does not seem that the world of Norrath is particularly dynamic in nature (at least not to any lasting extent), nor does it seem that servers have their own specific individual "character" by and large.

    Here's what I mean, for the most part, no matter how monumental the efforts of a group of players, no permanent effect can ever be had upon the face of Norrath. That's expected, due to the "Everlasting" nature of its inhabitants. Still, you run into a roadblock "as a character" trying to write the story when you realize the static nature of the environs.

    What I do wonder about is the "power" that a lead GM for a particular server might have in suggesting a change to his/her world. In other words, if a GM decided one day that he wanted pirates to have swarmed over the Ogre/Aviak island in Timorous Deep, and have "taken over" the Ogre outpost there. What power would they have to (even for a short time) have someone change the spawns there so that Pirates spawned there instead? I understand that there could be severe technical limitations to this...but it would open the possibilities of characters being able to effect a "change" upon their world.

    For example, let's say that a Guild of Followers of Marr declared a crusade (much like the Pirates above). Let's say they staged a fantastically successful raid, and kept the island cleared of Ogres for a full week (real time). Let's say that in the course of this, the GM's ran an event where the Ogres tried to take the island back, and failed. The GM would then have the option of effecting a "permanent change" on the island to change it around. The world would be dynamic, and a change would have been made on that individual server.

    I know the kind of nightmarish changes this would cause programatically, and I understand how difficult the patch process might be to try and keep up with it. What I might suggest, however, would be the possibility of localizing this to a special server (Perhaps even a RolePlaying server? Hmmmm? Sorry, had to get that in :))

    Call it a "Dynamic Storyline" server if you like, and give the GM's license to do anything they wanted to effect the storyline. Restrict them to rules that they have to coincide with the "global" storylines, and give them a specific list of things they can do to the environment at different levels. Use the current event structure as a guideline, but take the kid gloves off. Let the GM's play Gods, smite down characters for no reason, create general havoc in their world, even without the "world" being affected. Let them do anything necessary (even destroying character's items, changing religions, heck, change a character's SEX if it takes that). Let them submit changes to the world based on story, and schedule some programmer time to do them. Keep the patching scheme on a different line than the main program (much like the Test Server is done today).

    Obviously, it's a pretty bold step, but it's something to consider strongly for EverQuest II (The Second Story), or any other MMORPG that you guys might design. It might even be a way to spur on interest when your "next big thing" starts to take away from the EQ player-base.

    All just suggestions, as usual, take them as ye will...

    -Zekestrom of the Many Names ]

    Heads will role… er… roll!

    I've no idea why those apostrophes are such a nightmare. I'll hassle someone about it.

    Unfortunately, maintaining completely different data for each server would be as much of a nightmare as you imply.

    I certainly would love to play in a world such as you describe. But many people wouldn't. There are a lot of EverQuest players that do not like the idea of allowing a GM to 'smite down' their characters, from on high or from below, with or without reason.

    And even if this were to happen on a single server it would require a ton of resources. One GM can only entertain so many people at one time, after all. Writing up, testing and coding changes to a zone (even the ones that might seem simple enough) is time consuming. The awesome thing going on now regarding the plague has taken several people a lot of time to conceive, code and build. They deserve a ton of credit for it, it's a great event. But it's a time consuming process.

Smed On Server Crashes
- Aurum @ 5:21 PM PDT
I just received moments ago this note from John Smedley concerning the server problems that have troubled EverQuest this week:

    Hello All,

    We apologize for the problems we've been having this week. Basically it was
    a nasty server side bug that we now believe we have tracked down and
    eliminated (there's always the chance that this wasn't the problem). The
    approach we are taking now is to load the new code onto the servers one by
    one so as not to cause downtime to the entire game like we have the last 3-4
    days. Everything should be back to normal soon.

    John Smedley
    Chief Operating Officer
    Sony Online Entertainment
Thanks John!


Raid on E'ci
- Aurum @ 5:13 PM PDT
This was sent in by Ted:

Public lowbie Raid on Frogtown, Wed April 25

Why are those frogloks living in the corner of the Swamp of No Hope?
What are they grinding in their windmills? Whose spit was used to make
the houses and platforms of Frogtown?

Burnt Stump intends to find out.

Public lowbie raid on the Frog city, Wednesday April 25. Levels 15-25
invited.

We will meet in the Swamp of No Hope at the base of the waterfall from
Trakanon's Teeth at 7:30 pm est to form groups. We hope to charge the
gate at 8:00 and to fight until 11:00pm est. At 7:00pm SHARP a high
level druid will be leading a convoy of folks from the landing in
Firiona Vie to the waterfall in the Swamp. If you do not know the route
or are unsure of the way then this is the best way to travel.

We will have a level 29 cleric on hand to revive people back to corpses,
although the cleric is on the same account as the main tank so don't
expect fast revives. We have a caster bound in the Field of Bone
outside of Kaesora who can gate back and bind people on request.
Otherwise we intend to run the raid without using high level
assistance. There are no babysitters.

Raid leaders are Caradoc and Arianera. Please send email to
arianera@yahoo.com or Willaena@yahoo.com if you have questions or want
to reserve a spot.
Thanks!
