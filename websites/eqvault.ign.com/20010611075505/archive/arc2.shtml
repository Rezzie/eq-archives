- Aurum @ 8:14 PM PDT
Check out the latest tidbits from Alan directly from the Developer's Corner:

    Charm Change
    [Quote: perhaps the bard above did not phrase it properly. Yes, it removes charm kiting as a solo method for bards, but that is because it practically removes charm as an effective bard too (I have played a bard, and I will vouch for this).

    This means that bards have real trouble charming while soloing OR in groups. Actually, its a little easier soloing because the bard can simply keep swapping charmed targets. This is not viable in groups.

    I understand the need to fix exploits, but please take a close look at this soon. Bard charm was a great part of crowd control when an enchanter was not available (hey, I can't be EVERYWHERE at once), and I would hate to see them lose this.

    Thanks,
    Leirra ]

    Oh, don't misunderstand me. We certainly understand how this change affects group play to. My only point was that the fact that the change makes soloing harder isn't a very persuasive argument. :)

    Imbue Caution
    [Quote: I caution you to be careful if you're going to allow bards to imbue gems. If you are going to let one religion of bards do it, you must let them all do it.

    I play a bard that worships Karana, which only confers role-playing benefits to me; there are no in-game mechanics benefits for a bard worshipping Karana. And as such, I would feel offended if bards of one religion could imbue their own gems, and I could not. Agnostic bards, who already have advantages over non-agnostic bards with faction, would not be hurt by this since the advantage gained by their faction is greater than that gained by imbuing gems.

    --Daveon Silverstring
    --50th level bard, Brell Serilis ]

    Well, I'm not a subscriber to slippery slope arguments. Yes, we have to consider how people will feel if they don't get to do something others can do. But there's no 'must let them do it' involved here.

    Look at it this way. The reason for letting Veeshan worshipping bards imbue gems is because no worshipper or Veeshan can imbue gems, so worshippers of Veeshan are left out. Therefore we 'must' let them do it? Maybe…

    You bard can get imbued armor, a Veeshan bard can't. That's really the concern to be addressed. The fact that other bards can't imbue gems isn't going to be an issue of game play. It might be an issue about game universe consistency and player concern, but we won't need to add imbue ability to other bards for game balance or gameplay reasons.

    No final decision has been made here about this, and we'll talk over the consequences. But to me having the armor available (so as not to be a restriction for Veeshan worshipping bards) is more important than making the imbue spell available to all bards (not so much a restriction on other bards, as a lack of an advantage…).

    Questing
    [Quote: Just to respond to the comments on camping a fixed spawn being boring...

    Boredom is an emotion, not a pre-existant state. If I want action or companionship in EQ but spend my time solo, killing the same 6 minute spawn over and over, then clearly I am going to be bored and frustrated.

    If, on the other hand, I am listening to a good play on the radio or reading an exciting novel, then the control and predictability and minimal need for attention at a fixed spawn is ideal.

    Add to that an existential appreciation for the easthetic of repetition (should be familiar to all devotes of Philip Glass or The Velvet Underground) and solo single spawn camping becomes less about boredom and more about art.

    The thing is, lots of people enjoy moaning, me included. But if they didn't at some level have "fun" camping static spawns, they wouldn't do it.

    Anyway, you see a lot less of it than did before Velious came out. It's going out of fashion, but I still enjoy a bit of retro-boredom chic once in a while.]

    :)

    And, actually, you do make a very good point. If people do it, it must be what they want to do.

New FanStory.com Interview
- Aurum @ 4:28 PM PDT
From Lava:

Sing Lung is an editor in "real life" and the editor-in-chief of our new online magazine fanMAG. In the interview she offers advice on how writers can improve their work. She also talks about her motivation for her book Path of the Dragon and her love of massive multiplayer gaming.

An Excerpt:--

---------------

FanStory.com: How would you suggest a writer improve his writing?

SingLung: Learn to read critically, and learn to take criticism. Read and review as many stories as you can. Pay close attention to how the author writes -- what pulls you in to the story, and what knocks you out of it. If you don't like a story, figure out why. When you get a critical review, give the reviewer's suggestions some serious thought (as long as they are constructive). Try them out. You might be surprised if you do -- or you might find a better solution. When a critique puts me on the defensive or makes me feel compelled to explain my story, that tells me I should pay attention to it. It doesn't mean the reviewer is necessarily right, it just means I might not be getting my point across as well as I thought.

------------

The Interview
