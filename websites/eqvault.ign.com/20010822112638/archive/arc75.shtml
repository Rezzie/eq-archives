Tuning Meeting Highlights
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:59 PM PDT
As promised, Gordon Wrinn posted a summary of the tuning meeting held today at Sony HQ in San Diego, CA. Also of note, the Rogue class may be getting some attention. Here's the post:

    Hello all,

    I thought I'd bring a quick list of some things decided in today's tuning meeting. There's not much information here besides what I'll list, but please feel free to comment:

    * Concerning Nagafen and Vox: We're looking into a way to keep Nagafen and Vox as reasonable encounters for the high-30s-50s characters without having the high 50s+ killing them constantly with little opposition.
    * We'll be extending the "Rez-for-XP" window from 2 hours to 3 hours in light of size and difficulty of some of the new zones.
    * VZ/TZ move: See other thread.
    * We're looking at a way to stop players from bypassing the need for a rogue by using an NPC to open pickable doors. In other words, make friends with your local rogue :)
    * Poison update is expected on Test next week.
    * "Jolt" will have its casting time lowered.
    * The Hole is expected to go live this week.

    -Gordon


PvP-Teams (VZ/TZ) Status
[75 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:57 PM PDT
Gordon shed some light as to what is the current status of the big Vallon Zek/Tallon Zek transfer option. Here's his original post in it's entirety:

    Hello all,

    Due to the results of the /movelog command executed by players on the PvP-Teams servers the weekend before last, we are reconsidering our decision to do the move. Out of nearly 1800 accounts across TZ and VZ that requested move, only 278 asked to be moved from TZ (Coin only) to VZ (Coin and Item loot), while 1513 requested a move in the other direction.

    The result of completing this move would most likely result in crowding on the Tallon Zek server while leaving Vallon Zek nearly empty. While we have considered allowing a smaller number of people to move from Vallon to Tallon, we've concluded that that would most likely be more destructive to server morale than our current plan.

    As of now, and for the reasons stated above, our plan is to change the Vallon Zek server to Coin-only loot making it identical to Tallon Zek in that respect.

    We'll wait a few days to view comments here prior to finalizing the decision. Please followup here with your thoughts.

    -Gordon


Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:55 PM PDT
Gordon was back answering questions and posting to the Gameplay Board today. Here is all the latest:

  • Guild Housing (Officially Canned)
    [Quote from original post: It would be nice to have a place called home not just the city where you started. You can rest there without being attacked, store food and supplies. Why not other games have it?]

    Though we considered the idea in EQ, we ended up waiving it in favor of spending more time on the adventure aspects of the game. Overall I think it was a good choice given our goals and timing constraints.

  • eBay Question
    [Quote from original post: I am kinda getting bored with my character. Is it against the rules to trade accounts with another player? Now before you say no as fast as you can, think about it. You are only trading an in game commodity for another in game commodity, that being characters. Almost like trading all of your gear for all of someone else's gear, and trading all of your 'class A' exp for all of their 'class B' exp.

    Yes, this is also against the rules. Though I appreciate your arguments, trading a sword for another sword in game (perfectly acceptable) is not the same as trading characters or accounts. Transfer of accounts has always been prohibited.

    It's not just the philosophical ideals concerning gameplay, but the amount of fraud that results in such transactions. Even with our policies as they are, completely forbidding character sales and account transfers, we still receive a number of emails daily citing fraud in relation to account transfers.]

  • Cleric Armor
    [Quote from original post: I'd like to know why the cleric arms from the Ostiary in the library in Sebilis were nerfed, without any warning. These arms had the effect of Valor, which could be cast every 6 seconds. After the patch last week, they now take twice as long to cast, which in essence has rendered them useless, since Remedy and Superior Heal can be cast much faster with great hp return, even tho they cost mana and the arms do not. These were one of the nicest cleric pieces in the game, and like everything else that proves useful to a class, wound up getting nerfed and made useless without warning or explanation.]

    First, I apologize for the fact that this wasn't listed in the patch message.

    As for why they were changed, as you mentioned, use of the arms for the purpose of healing rather than for the "buff" that it gave was the primary motivation. The fact that that whole series increases current hitpoints in addition to max hitpoints was based on it being balanced by mana usage. Since the arms do not use mana, the casting-time becomes the entire cost. A cost of six seconds was too low for what it granted.

  • /Friends Increasement Status
    [Quote from original post: 20 friends just isn't enough for a happy-go-lucky flesh-pressing baby-kissing ogre warrior like myself. What is the status on increasing the size of the /friend list?]

    We've had to back-burner (postpone indefinitely) any plans to due this due to some technical issues. If we take it up actively again in the future I'll be sure to let everyone know.

  • Spell Drops
    We've tested and found the spelldrops appropriate at this time. They did increase, but as I said they weren't being increased to the point that people would find them plentiful by any means.

  • Tuning Meeting
    [Quote from original post: Gordon, It's Monday. How did that tuning meeting go?]

    The tuning meeting went well. I think I'll post a summary a bit later.

  • Linux Port & Development Question
    [Quote from original post: I know this is cluching at straws, but any chance of having EQ run on Linux?]

    We've no plans at this time to develop or release additional platform ports of the EverQuest client.

    [Quote from original post: Also, what system was EQ developed on? If you can reply to that one without revealing too much...]

    We use different tools for many different aspects of EQ. Outdoor geometry, indoor geometry, object building, character building, texture art, etc., all take place in different software packages. As for actual programming, it's done primarily in a combination of C and C++.

    As for what particular hardware platform is used for each thing, it varies between WinTel stuff, various Unix platforms, and the like.

  • The Simple Truth
    [Quote from original post: The more time spent regaining levels and leveling itself, the more months you spend on the game, and the more money Verant gets.]

    Actually we prefer to look at it from the standpoint that the longer we can keep you entertained, challenged, and intrigued, the longer you'll play the game. We believe that things earned are cherished more than things given, which is why some work is necessary in order to advance.

    [Quote from original post: Now for some, the fact that Verant MAKES you spend more months and months to "win"(achieving level 60) in this game and getting more money from its accounts may bother alot of people.]

    There is no way to "win" in EverQuest. Though some might hold "level 60" as the main goal they wish to reach, research shows that people don't "reach 60 and quit", or previously, "reach 50 and quit" like posts of this nature purport. We don't "make" you spend time to do anything. You choose a goal that you want to acheive and make the decision to do whatever is necessary to achieve it.

    [Quote from original post: The simple truth is, exp loss will not change...]

    Very true, but as mentioned above, it's not some devious thing to "make" you play longer. It's a risk that is present in order to provide challenge, making it difficult to obtain goals, which a big part of the reason that people play the game.

  • Caster Loot
    I guess that one of the situations is that things that are considered "good" caster loot aren't very diverse. Int casters want int, and that's it. Wis casters want wis, and that's it. Now, since the int/wis softcaps weren't changed in regards to Kunark, a lot of people are running around right up against 200 points. There's only so much more that can be done with them.

    Melee characters on the other hand need weapons, AC, and every other stat they can get. As a caster, I really don't care about AC items that I can get, since if I get hit I'm dead anyway.

    Basically, one of the cool things about being a melee class is that more options are open to you in regards to equipment. I think that casters will always be at a disadvantage in this area.

  • The Hole
    [Quote from original post: Has anyone taken a look at the items that will be coming from the hole to prevent more post-release item nerfs? You know what I'm talking about. Robe of Living Fungus, Wind Saber, Rod of Mourning, Vambraces of Mourning, Manastone, Mistwalker, etc..]

    Sure, we've looked at the items, but as always I cannot guarantee that post-release adjustments won't be necessary. As always, 250k+ people can find more issues than any test team could. I certainly wish this wasn't the case, but the only way we could eliminate the need for any post-production changes with certainty would be to stop any post-production enhancements to the game. I doubt anyone would like that option either.

  • Unreliable Binds
    I've heard reports about this from time to time. To my knowledge, our testing team hasn't been able to find a reliable way to reproduce this. It would be nice to hear if anyone has a way to help us track this issue down.

  • Disappearing Boats
    We're working on the problem with the disappearing boats. In the meantime, feel free to petition a GM should they appear inoperational. I do apologize for the delay in addressing this issue.

  • Trolls and Centaurs
    Centaurs are ornery and irrational creatures. It seems that they are prone to fits of rage for little to no reason, and have been known at times to attack nearly anyone. We think that it has something to do with tension in the area created by the number of people encroaching on their hunting grounds.

    As for your LD and where your corpse went, LD's will happen from time to time in internet based games for a number of reasons. As we don't have control over the majority of individual LDs, we don't do resurrections for most of them. Corpses have also been known to end up strange places when dying while LD. If it ends up someplace inaccessible (like on top of a mountain that a player cannot climb), the GMs will be happy to move it.

  • Shackle of Ro Not Fixed?
    [Quote from original post: Not to be a pain in the tuckas, but still no finger bones to complete the quest. If it does work and we are just looking in the completely wrong place tell us...You don't need to tell us where, just confirm it is working...]

    Can anyone attest to finding fingerbones? In the meantime, I'll ask the designer of this quest to take a look.

  • Crashing
    I'd be interested to know if your crashing is sound-related. Do you still have the problem if you turn off all sound support in the Windows control panel? Is your crashing zone-specific?

    I'd really like to help solve the problem, however as we don't have a whole lot of people having crashing problems, it suggests a driver or hardware-specific issue. We are continuing to work on the sound-related issues, and will probably have a new patch up in that regard in the next couple of weeks.

  • Track Corpse for Necros
    [Quote from original post: Just wanted to remind you/inform you that this spell has been broken since the release of it. It does nothing when cast. This has been verified on all servers as far as I heard.]

    Yes, we're aware of the problem. It's in our bug database. If I get an ETA on it I'll be sure to let everyone know.

  • Lack of Paladin Weapons
    I had the guys run off a report of weapons that Paladins can wield. I've a list of well over 300 1HS and 2HS weapons in game for them, and over 150 1HB and 2HB weapons. It's hard to say how many of them are class-specific.

    That said, just looking at some of the other things that have been released, it appears that there might be (stress *might*) some disparity between SK and Paly weapons in Kunark. Of course, that doesn't necessarily mean that there is a problem. If you recall, SKs were quite vocal about the lack of 1H weapons in game for them prior to Kunark.

    I'll mention this thread to the guys, but just to be clear, I'm not promising anything, and usually won't give periodic updates on item-related issues.

  • Rain Spells
    I've read the original author's post, and from what I see it's pretty accurate. Rain spells consider all targets in their field, whether or not those targets can actually be damaged. This is the way that the rain spells work. Now if this makes them completely "useless" in all cases, perhaps we need to look at them again, but at the moment they are working as designed.

  • Spellbook Follow-up
    The spellbook obscuring the view is part of the "penalty" that lower-level casters pay in exchange for the power that they will receive later in life. From an RP perspective, the caster was so focused on meditating that they perceived little about what was happening in the world around them. Back in early beta we didn't even allow casters to speak while medding.

    I think most are happy that we changed this stance in the interest of gameplay.

    Hello all,

While I cannot comment on the authenticity or lack thereof in regards to the posted information, it is not appropriate for us to allow true or manufactured information of that type to be passed on to the public as "fact".

    That said, the original poster of that message, "Habenera", does not speak for and is not affiliated with Verant Interactive or the EverQuest Guide program. I do indeed apologize to anyone subjected to the rantings of this individual.

    -Gordon
Upon further investigation I discovered this was linked with a post Gordon Wrinn made last week asking a former guide named "rpgadvocate" to personally email him. Rpgadvocate made his response public and you can read it by clicking here. I have come to the conclusion that the reason Gordon asked him to email him was to deal with his views of the GM/Guide system in private.

A few minutes later someone, presumably from the guide program, posted rpgadvocate's personal file, which was the force behind the post Gordon made. Yes, we know that post was been published on other sites and boards but I will not be posting it here. (so please don't email me asking for a link to it)

This is entirely too scary. Let me explain:

• Those records are accurate. I was ejected from the program the first week in February. One thing he has wrong however is that I was indeed asked to resign, but I refused on principle.

• So does that mean my Guiding was impeccable? OF COURSE NOT!! One night I acted like a complete ass, as I had had a bad day. Guess what I spent the first 20 minutes of my shift doing the next day? Editorial: A Word About Guides and GMs

    Any Questions?

    First, thanks for the screenshots -- it helped us understand part of this issue.
    Jeff Butler and I looked into this personally today and came to some conclusions.

    But first, some info into how clipping planes work.

    1. When an outdoor zone is exported from the tools we use to create it, the artist chooses a maximum clip plane. This setting affects how far out visibility is calculated, and can affect file size and frame rate.

    2. Most old world zones were exported with a much lower number than most Kunark zones.

    3. Each zone has a minimum and maximum clip plane number associated with it server side. These are the numbers you move between when you adjust the clipping plane slider in-game.

    4. The slider bar is supposed to work as a percentage. In other words, in one zone if the min is 1 and the max 1000, were the slider in the center, you'd be at 500. But if the min was 1 and the max 100, say for another zone, you'd be at 50 with the slider in the middle.

    Ok, so, after looking into this and checking out the screenshots referred to in this thread we were able to determine the following:

    1. For some reason (we'll ask the programmer tomorrow), when rain stops, the clipping plane moves out beyond the max briefly and then settles back at the max. For example, I went to Overthere and verified what the screenshots showed: the Overthere's max clip is 1800 feet. It looked like the clip went to 2000 or so briefly when the sky was still gray and then returned to 1800 when the sky was on again.

    2. Another bug (though not terribly consequential) is that depending on the max clip, the clip slider bar appears to do nothing the last 25% or so. I was in the Overthere using the clip slider and watching the clipping plane distance in feet (something that doesn't show on the normal client) and it got to the max, 1800, at around 75% of the slider bar.

    Ok, so what does this all mean? And could people see farther in the past?

    Possibly, yes -- we've changed the max clipping plane in zones several times. The fact that the slider bar appears to max out early, though, isn't stopping you from setting your clipping plane to the max setting server side. Neither is the rain.

    A good question, though, is 'if the Overthere displays out to 2000 briefly, why isn't the max clip for the Overthere 2000?'

    I agree. What we'll do is go through the zones again and make sure the max server side is really the max the zone was exported at.

    Do keep this in mind, though: we cannot move the max clip beyond the clipping distance the zone was originally exported at -- the visibility data is just not there and you'll see nothing beyond that hard limit. Unfortunately, the vast majority of the old world zones had max clips between 600 and 1500 feet. In other words, East Commons will never have a clip of 1800 or 2000 like the Overthere could.

    I'll also ask that the bug associated with the rain that has the cilp pull back past the max clip be fixed, although honestly, it's not going to be a high priority bug in that it doesn't hurt gameplay.

    Anyway, I hope this clears things up. I'll watch this thread tonight to see if there are any additional questions.

    -Brad

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:55 PM PDT
As usual, Gordon was once again on the Gameplay Board answering people's questions and responding to comments. Here's the latest posts:

  • Rare Spawn Complaints
    Most of the complaints I see in regards to these rare spawns for quest components is related to the general feeling that there is an "entitlement" to these items. There isn't. No one is entitled to any particular item.

    You can't fix a situation where everyone is camping by making what they are camping for more plentiful. That just encourages camping, as the camp duration gets shorter, and more people feeel that the camp time is worth the effort. As it is now, maybe people will get the idea that the camp is too long, and just choose not to camp. Then perhaps someone happening by can get lucky and find the spawn in question. If people are still choosing to camp it, perhaps we need to look into making it even rarer, closing the quest, or look into creating other items that represent reasonable alternatives to the item that people are choosing to camp.

  • EverQuest & FPS Games
    I completely agree that Doom and other First-person shooter type games are very fun, despite the lack of a penalty for death. However, those games are not about building and molding a character to your desires. The worlds aren't persistent. There's is nothing that can be achieved by playing for a year that can't be achieved in the first few minutes of each life.

    The games are completely different and cannot be measured against each other. The concept of "Challenge" is implemented in different ways. EverQuest implements challenge by providing risks. In order to provide risks, there must be some downside that will be realised should a task not be completed. That downside can't be enjoyable, or it wouldn't be a risk.

    I do wish that I could explain this to everyone's satisfaction. If anyone can offer any suggestions to this end, I'd certainly appreciate it.

  • Gaining Experience
    Experience given by a creature is based upon its level, and the zone you are in. It is not based upon its level in relation to you, other than at the point that you stop receiving experience from killing a particular level creature.

    In other words, if you are level 50, a level 49 creature will give more experience than a level 48 creature, even though they are both blue.

  • Online Store Status
    The status is that the online store in it's former incarnation will probably not be up in the foreseeable future. We are however working on a web-based store that we expect to open in a few weeks.

  • Spell Drops
    [Quote from original post: I have yet to see a difference in the spell drop rate. Hours of hunting skyfire, chardok, etc. still yield nothing]

    Most people who cite problems with spell drops cite hours of hunting in Skyfire and Chardok. Consider for a moment that though spells drop there, those might not be the best places to find spells.

  • The Bammbo Bo Stick
    [Quote from original post: We were told by you Abashi that the Bammbo Bo stick was a mistake and was being lowered in weight.]

    Actually I never said that. I said that some of the monk-only weapons in Kunark were too heavy, and I never meantioned what specific weapons. Anyway, the live team went through the weapons and lowered the weight on some of them. Some of them are remaining rather heavy, and are that way on purpose.

    I'll ask the live team to look it over again, but I wouldn't expect a ton of dramatic changes. The fact that a particular character has the ability to use a particular weapon doesn't necessarily mean that they *should* use it, nor that it's the *best* thing that they could be using.

    Wait until you see some of the cursed items that will begin turning up soon.

  • Account Hacking
    [Quote from original post: Gordon this must be fixed(account hackers) Now this is an ongoing problem and need to be addressed instantly!]

    I would love to be able to find out how this stuff happens. In most cases, we can usually track it back to passwords being given by the account owners to guild members or real-life "friends". In most other cases, it turns out to be some auction or account sales related fraud.

    Players have to be responsible for their own account information and never provide it to anyone. They also need to refrain from buying characters online as the original owner can get the account back at nearly any time. We'll of course ban the account in the case of the latter, but we don't at all get involved with any lost funds.

    [Quote from original post: Set up direct link command just like bug or feedback and have it go directly to all gms guides and customer support peeps online so that the peeps hacking are caught in the act!]

    Very few people could get caught hacking in the act as most people don't have multiple accounts to log in and see it for themselves. That said, some type of more direct system-wide petition would probably end up being used for things other than hacking.

    All of that said, I would like to hear from folks who've had their accounts hacked, and honestly never gave the password to anyone and never bought or sold the account. I do have some questions that I'd like to ask. Please email me at gwrinn@verant.com.

  • Why can't Paladins use piercing weapons?
    It sounds like it might be possible that some of our apprentice game designers might have been unaware that paladins couldn't use piercers. We probably won't give them that ability, but we should address the fact that they cannot use these weapons.

    We're doing a weapons search to see what weapons out there are actually usable by Paladins so that we can correct any discrepancies.

  • I can attack 72 times in one second, what do you think of that?
    The test department took a look at this just to see what would happen. We were concerned about the possibility of a potential loop.

    No one takes any damage when this happens, but it doesn't loop.

  • Robe of Living Fungus - Nerf or Bug?
    I've asked around here, spoken to everyone with the ability to make or modify items, and can't find any indication that the robe ever had that effect. The closest thing I can guess is that we might have had a bad item export at some time that went live.

  • Rogues Picking off Spells
    [Quote from original post: Rogues pickpocketing spells off of Kunark mobs Please fix this so it's not possible to pickpocket spells]

    As infrequently as pickpocket will get an item, and as infrequently as the creatures drop spells, and considering that there's no way to tell when killing it that it has a spell, I really don't see a need to eliminate this.

  • Spell Droppings
    [Quote from original post: Please try that again where you find a place you know spells SHOULD be dropping and kill 250mobs to see how many scrolls you got just like you did before..... Please! I beg you....]

    I did last night, and saw a good number more drops. I probably got some good rolls, but still received several more spells even after accounting for that.

  • Enstill Won't Override Root?
    This is the same issue that causes lower level attack speed debuffs to overwrite the higher level ones. It's a problem with the spell stacking code and on our list.

  • After a 7 Month Vacation..
    Wow, I'm really glad that I went looking on page 5 and came across this post. It's nice to see someone spend this much time on a positive post. We really appreciate the opinions. It's a nice outlook.

    [Quote from original post: What are your thoughts on no_drop? Am I missing an important reason why no_drop should still be in game for all but the most uber/critical items?]

    NODROP was originally created with the intent of requiring that someone be at a specific event in order to reap the benefits of it. Quest items are a good example of what NODROP was intended for. We also expanded upon this initial design as an anti-farming measure.

    Of course, with all of the new lands, items, areas, creatures, etc., that Kunark brought to the table, and with the introduction of contextual random loot, the need for anti-farming measures is decreasing. Most of the items in Kunark are not NODROP unless part of some quest.

    We probably won't, however, go about removing the NODROP tags from existing items.
