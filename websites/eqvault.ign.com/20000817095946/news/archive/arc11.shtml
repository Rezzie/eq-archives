Gordon on Vallon Zek
[21 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:50 PM PDT
Gordon responded to a lengthy post about the Vallon Zek server on the Gameplay Board. Here is an excerpt from the original post followed by Gordon's response:

    There are two purposes to this note: 1) to make a logical argument for keeping Vallon an item-loot server, and 2) to make an emotional one that Vallon is one of the best and most fun servers on EQ. I love my server and I hope that passion comes through.

    There are a number of things that make Vallon special. Many of these will be lost if Vallon becomes a coin-only server. Here they are, in no particular order:

    1) ROLEPLAYING on Vallon is not an optional frill. It arises organically out of the rules. Everyone on the server has definite ideas about cross-teaming and when it's appropriate to Player-Kill (PK). There are guilds that are all-team, all-Light, single-Team, single-Race. There are people who kill anyone not on their team, anyone Dark, anyone Light, anyone Elf. There are solo-PKers whose biggest thrill in life is killing green /cons and use every mean trick in the book, noble PKers who refuse to PK naked or to kill people at low health, and group-PKs who love camping the teleport circles and the banks. And everyone feels so passionately about these issues that they don't even call it role-playing, which naturally makes it the best kind of role-playing there is.

    I'm a Gnome warrior, and I only kill PKers. I spent a few days in Blackburrow the other week, collecting Gnoll teeth to improve my faction with Steel Warriors. When I first got there the Barbarians and Humans there were really rude, attacking me occasionally, inspecting me without asking, shouting for everyone to watch out...a Gnome was in the zone. But while I was there I killed an average of two Dark Elf PKs a day, and before long I'd say /ooc "I can't help with the Shorts, but if you get any Dark PKs, just send me a /tell." And people would shout "All right! Caper's in the zone!", or I'd say /ooc "I have a Senior Apprentice robe I just looted of a PK wizard. I'll give it to the first caster who /pokes me", and they would shout "WTG Caper!" "Woo woo Caper!" It was more fun than I've had in a month of levelling and fighting AI mobs.

    On a Blue server, what can you do that's a truly despicable act? Start a really bad train? And what can anyone do to stop you? Petition a GM? That's noble. Good and Evil become just an affectation.

    2) TWINKING on Vallon is risky. If you're like me, you think twinking is cheating. It wrecks the economy, makes a mockery out of role-playing, and destroys competitiveness. I know I'll never convince many of you about this, but look: there are 2 dozen servers where you can twink to your heart's content. There are only 2 where it's in any way risky. Why can't those of us who hate twinking have our servers, too?

    Once I saw a very low-level halfling warrior in Misty wearing a bronze breastplate. I thought, "That guy's going to be stripped naked inside a half hour." But that BP blinked on and off everytime he heard a noise. Blink orange, blink naked hobbit chest, blink orange. He was spending so much energy keeping the thing that I couldn't resent him for it. And that's the way twinking should be.

    3) PvP TACTICS are difficult to learn and fun. Re-arranging the default keyboard layout and learning to move with the mouse are not affectations, they're necessities. Melees spend as much time collecting PvP gear as PvE gear, and that makes for tremendously exciting stories. "Hiding" on Vallon isn't a push-button skill, it's an art that works regardless of whether or not the other guy has his See Invisible spell up. For instance, a sitting Gnome will just fit inside the belly of an Ogre corpse, and no one can see her, and she can see out at everyone. Train a Young Kodiak to the Nektulous zone and kill it there, and you have a hiding spot that will let you eavesdrop on the glory of the Dark nation as they parade by, and no one will know.

    The most fun I've ever had in EQ was running around LGuk trying to get some NO DROP Shin armors and the Basalt Carapace. I was level 15, chased by Dark Twinks and Shin Ghoul knights. I followed a high-level Dark group through the Bedroom, into the Heart room, to the Gargoyle room, shaking the whole time. I spent 10 days down there and never got a single experience point. But the stories I can tell!

    4) The RISK is so much greater on Vallon, and that makes the game much more exciting. There's so much at stake. The first time you're murdered in cold blood and
    your best item is looted, you're tramautized. You're shaking and you might feel sick at the stomach. You're probably filled with a futile hatred that some *%&$ wizard 8 levels higher than you are just killed you in two nukes and there's no way you will ever get back at her. Hatred on Vallon isn't artificial, it's real.

    But so is gratitude...normally Elves hate Gnomes like me, so I'll never forget the Wood Elf Druid, a stranger, who saved me from a Ranger PK one day. Once you get a good reputation, those suprise acts of kindness become increasingly common. You make instant friends. And you begin to figure out the PvP tricks...how to hot-key inventory slots for quick bagging, how to move around in combat so you're not caught by surprise.

    I'm a level 18 warrior with 15 days of /played, and with luck and skill I haven't lost an item since level 8. But I know I will again, and I'm ready for that. I'm proud that I'm levelling on the most difficult server there is, and you would be, too.

    5) But the greater CHALLENGE of playing on Vallon varies greatly depending on class. Melees are the hardest, being the most dependent on equipment, and you never want to log on and level for a few hours when you're sleepy. On the other hand, pet classes can level to 50 stark naked, essentially without risk.

    So when I'm in the mood to be challenged, when I'm ready for an adrenaline ride at any moment, then I play my warrior. And when I'm not, I play my mage. My warrior gets 85% of my playing time.

    And Vallon is a team server, so there's always someplace SAFE. When I pull into Rivervale I breathe a sigh of relief. I can go fishing on the docks there in full armor without risk. I don't have to be paranoid all the time.

    6) My favorite thing about Vallon is that it's so much less PREDICTABLE than the Blue servers. People are much more surprising than idiot computer AI MoBs. The game never reduces itself to a stopwatch and a calculator. You never get the feeling that you're just floating around a visually-appealing chatroom. You have so many more STORIES to tell.

    I'm a regular reader on the main Warrior board, at Stratics, and in a thread on pulling recently an argument was made that a good puller is essentially just someone with a good memory for spawns and pathing and a knowledge of MoB's aggro radii. This poster was very denigrating about the skills of a puller, and even the best and most prideful pullers on the board agreed that he was essentially right.

    I felt such pity. On Vallon when a warrior is in her best armor, in dangerous territory, a long way from her group, the spawn times of computer MoBs is strictly a secondary consideration. Even when you have a zone memorized, you're still always on the lookout, sharp and alert...at any time, an adventure can find you.

    7) Vallon has a HISTORY of nations and a politics. Unlike Blue servers, racial hatreds aren't a static lookup on a database table, they evolve. It's fascinating to read about the fates of the four nations on our server as they won and lost influence, as zone controls shifted and attitudes changed. And you can aspire to something besides personal aggrandizement...you can imagine changing history.

    Elf-Gnome relations have been particularly volatile since I came on the server in December. When I first got on, a low-level Gnome could run to Crushbone and level. But a series of Shorty-only PK guilds who hunt contstantly in GFay has changed all that. GFay has become the most savage zone in the game. Corpses everywhere. On the other hand, if an Elf and a Gnome meet out in Qeynos, they're almost instant friends. That's deep human territory. I see a High Elf walking toward me, my gut clenches up and I stop, and so does he, but before long we're bowing and swapping food and buffs and promising to help each other if someone tries to PK us.

    8) I love the POLITICS involved in cross-teaming. I've been betrayed by group members, I've made good Dark friends, I've been forced to group with Darkies who hated my guts but who needed me at the time to get a job done. It's fascinating.

    9) Vallon has BORDERS. This makes for many interesting consequences. For instance, some items are MUCH harder to get than on a Blue server, and when you get them they're so much rarer, and so naturally you're much prouder of them. Crude Steins, for instance, might sell for 20 pp on a Blue server, but they'll go for 10 times that in Freeport on Vallon. Small Bronze is very hard for a Dark Elf to get, since it drops only in Unrest, a light zone. The Gatorsmash Maul requires kills in Crushbone and Ogguk, the Wee Harvester requires adventuring in Rivervale, the Karanas, and Ferrot. You can make a killing by SMUGGLING these things, but that has its own risks...if you want to sell a Langseaxe in Nektulous, both of you at some point are going to have a huge quantity of cash on you and be vulnerable to a backstab.

    And you don't find jarring combinations in newbie zones. Dark Elves don't run over to Misty Thicket to level, or if you see an Erudite levelling in GFay, that's a once-in-a-lifetime event. Some zones are war zones...you'll find 70 people in Nektulous daily, but maybe 10 in East Commons, and only a few of those are there to level.

    10) On Vallon the ECONOMY works much better than blue servers. Items retain their values pretty consistantly. For instance, Bronze sells for about 2pp per AC, Banded for 1pp. Trade skills are actually worth something...if you're a master smith or a master tailor, you have an endless supply of decent armor.

    If Vallon goes coin-only, then many of these things will disappear. Role-playing becomes an affectation. Without the ability to commit truly despicable acts, it also becomes impossible to commit noble ones. No good, no evil...everyone becomes sort of neutral unless they want to pretend to be something different for a little while. The server becomes less risky, more predictable, less exciting. It's no more challenging to level than on any Blue server, so a lot of the pride goes away. There's no reason to develop PvP tactics except as a hobby. Anyone can twink, the borders become soft, the economy gets as bad as on the Blue servers.

    I don't want to see that happen. There's little enough server diversity in EQ. I'd hate to see a unique server become just another Care Bear server. I think Vallon is the best server in the game. I don't want to lose it.

    Caper the Periodically Poignant Gemchopper
    Level 18 Warrior Vallon Zek.

    You're using Roleplaying as your argument here. However, Roleplaying doesn't require that items be looted in order to play your role as an anti-PK police guild. The "crime" of killing someone and taking their coin doesn't have to be any less important than the "crime" of killing them and taking an item. This is espicially true when the chances of getting an item back from someone, unless they are the honorable sort who always wears their gear, is very slim.

    As for the rest, I have read it, and though I appreciate your opinion, we do not plan on changing Vallon back. The arguments here are roughly the same ones as on Tallon when the coin-only change was made there. Your arguments aren't any less important than those of the people who wanted coin-only loot, however the item-loot people was a much smaller population.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:44 PM PDT
A few posts were made today on the Gameplay Board by Sony's Gordon Wrinn:

  • eBay
    [Quote from original post: sorry phelan..but that is not good enough. Before everyone goes and flames this "musk" you need to know it is him. ANYONE at all with a FA coold sell their account and try to MAKE it seem like it was this guy...hmmmmm would maybe casue people to try and bid more.]

    Very true, and it's the same issue that we face daily. We've already had several cases of people putting up auctions, making it appear to be someone on their server who they don't like, in an attempt to get them banned.

  • Does EQ Support Dual Processors?
    Though EQ will run on a multiprocessor machine, any performance increase will be negligible. The application, though multithreaded, wasn't built specifically to take advantage of multiple processors.

  • Jumping Into the Hole
    [Quote from original post: No one said not to levitate in... Read the patch message again it didnt say.]

    The patch message included:

    "As a note, jumping in The Hole from Paineel is NO LONGER A SHORTCUT TO TOXXULIA FOREST. The GMs will not recover corpses for people who fall, or jump, or attempt to levitate into The Hole."

    I felt that this was as clear as I could make it without completely spelling it out. The risk of exploring new zones is quite high, and it should be. It really wouldn't be appropriate for us to describe in detail everything that can kill you.

  • Rubricite & Manastones
    [Quote from original post: I was wondering why Verant doesn't put rubicite back in the game? It is so rare now that the price is inflated too high for its worth.]

    Prices never inflate too high for its "worth". The economy doesn't allow that to happen, as no one will pay more than it is "worth" to them. As for putting it back in, I suppose that it's possible that it might make an appearance or two.

    The manastone, however, will not go back in. The manastone stopped dropping due to balance issues associated with it. Rubicite stopped dropping due to rampant camping.

  • If I do "this" will I get in trouble?
    It basically falls under fraud or harassment. Someone is bugging you for a port, you agree to port them, you port them someplace else. You do something other than what you agreed to do. Next thing you know the guy is dead, and lost experience due to something that another player did intentionally. It's basically the same as purposely training someone.

    Now if someone is harassing you with non-stop agressive begging, and refuses to leave you alone when you tell them to, please /report and /petition a GM.

  • Experience Loss & Death
    Experience loss on death isn't calculated as a number of bubbles, but rather a numeric value. That value, is however, capped at the higher levels. In other words, a level 60 person will appear to lose less experience (comparing "bubbles") than a level 55 person.

  • Manastones and The Hole
    The Hole shouldn't be marked as a "Kunark" zone, so the manastone should work fine at the moment.

    Though I'd certainly like to see more critters out there marked as "animals" to allow druids to charm them, we don't plan on giving druids a way to permanently charm an animal, as they are not a pet class. The ability to have a pet, for any period of time, certianly makes them stronger for that period, so I hope you consider it a benefit.

    As a note, we have introduced an animal-only resistance debuff which should help with some of those charm times. However I suspect that you might need to check out some Charisma gear to extend the duration of your charms.

  • Locked Doors: Another Options
    [Quote from original post: A better solution would be to not allow NPCs to walk through locked doors at all. How come that rat in Befallen can open a door (locked or not) anyway?]

    The problem is that spell effects don't collide with objects or world geometry. In other words, as was done at one point in beta, people could cast rain spells and such and have it affect NPCs on the other side of the door, with no risk to the caster.

  • Dragon Complaints
    [Quote from original post: Why won't Gordon/Abashi comment on dragon complaints?]

    I've commented on several other threads as well. You might want to check out the "search" funtion, it can help you find the active discussions which we are participating in.

    It's not unusual for situations like this to result in dozens of threads where everyone is asking similar questions or making similar arguments. In these cases, it makes the most sense for me to only concentrate on a couple of them.

  • Lockpicking Negatively Affects Players?
    [Quote from original post: Well, I don't know about your servers, but on mine there are probably 5-6 level 50+ rogues.]

    With the lockpicking change, 3 of the 4 level 50+ dungeons in the game now require a rogue to access all/part of them. Now the 2 to 3 50+ rogues that are online at any given time have absolute control over where the other 100's of level 50+ players can go.

    Players have always shown a remarkable ability to adapt to changing conditions. I doubt it will be long before we see a surge in rogue population in response to this change.

    I think everything will work out well :)

  • Nagafen/Vox
    [Quote from original post: You never once said we'd be punished for levelling beyond 50th]

    This is a special case. As I stated in the patch message, our goal was to avoid having to raise the bar for existing players. Had we been able to make no changes, and still have the result be that the 40s and low 50s were killing the dragons, we would have done so. However every week I've gotten more and more reports of high-50s folk coming in with a small number of people and besting the dragons.

    We still think that the lower level folks deserve a chance.

    No, he doesn't. I looked at the items that they got off of Trakanon, and they are certainly different than anything that has been found in game.

  • Shrink Anywhere Potions
    I'll see if I can walk this issue through. I apologize that it's taking so long to get this change made.

  • Bug While Following Mobs
    This happens due to the way collision with objects works server-side versus the prediction engine of the client. Basically, the server doesn't acknowledge the object (a tree for instance) so has the NPC walk through it. The client on the other hand sees the NPC hit the tree, and figures that he NPC will get stuck walking up against it.

    In a few seconds, the server resync's the client and everything is updated. It's one of the minor drawbacks to the system that we adopted to minimize the effects of internet latency and reduce bandwidth usage.

Patch Message: 6-22-00
[86 Comments] - Aurum @ 11:38 AM PDT
Today's big Patch Message is up on the Patch Server now. It reads as follows:

    June 22nd, 9:00am
    -----------------

    ***Today's Updates***

    *The Hole*

    We are pleased to announce the opening of The Hole, a high-level dungeon on the continent of Odus. The Hole is a complementary addition to EverQuest, available to all subscribers regardless of their Kunark status. The files necessary to enter The Hole are available via the "Optional Patch" button at the bottom of the patch program interface. The files are quite large (about 9MB total), so if the download is going slowly, you may want to postpone downloading the files until later.

    As mentioned above, The Hole is a high-level dungeon, designed to support characters starting at about level 40. It should be regarded as extremely dangerous. As a note, jumping in The Hole from Paineel is NO LONGER A SHORTCUT TO TOXXULIA FOREST. The GMs will not recover corpses for people who fall, or jump, or attempt to levitate into The Hole.


    *Vallon Zek is now Coin-Only*

    As we announced earlier this week, due to the number of people specifying interest in "coin-only" loot on Vallon Zek, versus those wishing to retain item- loot, we have implemented "coin-only" looting rules for PvP combat on Vallon Zek.


    *Resurrection*

    Previously, players had to be resurrected within two hours after death in order to receive any experience back as part of the resurrection. Due to the increased size and difficulty associated with Kunark zones, we've raised this limit from two hours to three. This should assist players and make it easier for them to obtain an experience-returning resurrection within the time limit.


    *Warping*

    We put in some code that should help decrease the "ghosting" (NPCs appearing to be some place they aren't) being reported in some of the larger Kunark zones. Please let us know how this goes.


    *Locked Doors*

    We noticed few weeks ago that it is common practice to use NPCs to open locked doors rather than by using the services of someone that is able to pick locks. In order to protect and increase the value of the abilities of the lock-picking classes, NPCs have gained the ability to walk through locked doors without opening them.


    *Vox and Nagafen*

    Over the past couple of weeks, we've received more and more reports of these dragons being bested by smaller and smaller groups of higher-level (high 50s) adventurers. As we've always felt that Dragons should be special encounters that require a large number of people, we were left with some choices.

    One choice available to us was to increase the power of the dragons to a level where the level 60 folks would find them challenging. This would have the disadvantage of forever placing them out-of-reach for those who did not buy Kunark, and place them further out-of-reach for those who did, but are just now approaching the levels where they could go on Dragon raids. Another possible solution was to make the dragons flee (depop) when engaged by a more powerful group of people. This would have the undesired effect of allowing some people to deny others the experience of fighting a dragon.

    Instead of raising the bar as mentioned above, we've instead decided that dragons, or at least these two dragons, should have the magical ability to pick their own fights. Players of level 53 or above will now encounter some difficulty when attempting to assist in combat with these dragons. This should help 40s and low 50-level characters with their chances to encounter a dragon without worry of getting forced out by much higher level players. In addition, it also has the added effect of creating a natural progression from the younger dragons of Antonica to the elder and more formidable dragons of Kunark.

    -The EverQuest Team

      Hello all,

      I don't think that I'll ever get everyone to agree, but my point has been, if a situation is too risky for your taste, then either don't put yourself in the situation, or take steps to mitigate the risk if fulfilled.
