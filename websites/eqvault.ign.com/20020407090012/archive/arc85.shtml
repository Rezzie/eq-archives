Event on Tribunal
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 8:31 PM PDT
I received the following from Ignitz:

Greetings fellow Norrathians of the Tribunal Server. My name is Ignitz
Gemcaller, and I am the guildmaster of the Heroes of Valor. It is my distinct
pleasure to announce to you today the start of a month long events sweep.
That's right, once a week for the entire month of August the HoV will be
holding an event, and there promises to be something for all walks of the
Norrathian Life to enjoy.

The first event, I am proud to say, is the first of it's kind (as far as I
know). Once I read of an event where a guild held a gnome race across the
world. Well, today I announce a dwarf race of the same nature, except that it
is across all the known zones of Faydwer with the notable exception of Kedge
Keep. That's right, all participants will create newbie dwarves and traverse
Faydwer, to include Unrest, Butcherblock, and Mistmoore Castle. As well, we
are raffling off a full set of Crafted Armor for some lucky adventurer to
win. This is just the beginning, and it promises to be a fun filled month. So
without further ado, I will proceed to lay out the details of our first event.

What: Crafted Raffle
Where: Greater Faydark at the Wizard Spires
When: After the Naked Newbie Dwarf Race (see below)
Rules: As of now you may log on to the Tribunal Server and begin purchasing
raffle tickets for a full set of Crafted Armor for warriors. The price is 5pp
a ticket and you may purchase as many as you want. Any HoV member will take
your order so long as you go to them and pay for the tickets. They will take
your name down and list how many tickets you bought, then relay that
information back to me. The next day you may ask me (Ignitz) what numbers you
have if you wish to know. The winner will be chosen at random using the
ingame random number generator. In the event that the winner is not present
to receive his/her prize then the set will be held until the winner can come
collect. Proceeds from the raffle will go to support more raffles like this.
Good luck to all who wish to participate.

What: Naked Newbie Dwarf Race across Faydwer
Where: Kaladim near the Paladin's guild
When: Friday, August 4th at 7pm CST sharp!
Rules: Registration will begin at 5pm CST near the entrace to Butcherblock
Mtns in Kaladim. All participants must register as the character they wish to
receive the prizes should they win. When creating the newbie dwarf you must
use Hov as the first three letters and the name of the character who
registered as the rest. (IE. Ignitz = Hovignitz).
To make things more interesting and to add an element of strategy to he race
the following rules are made:
1. Warriors, Rogues, and Paladins will start at 7pm on the dot with no
penalty to their start time.
2. Clerics will start at 7:01. This is to illustrate that they have a
little bit better chance of surviving the race without much difficulty due to
the spells that they start with.
3. Clerics may choose to accept the spell Divine Aura in order to give them
an even better chance of survival, but doing so will cause you to start at
7:02.
4. Warriors, Rogues, and Paladins may choose to begin the race with the
spell Skin Like Diamond cast upon them. Doing so however means they will
begin the race at 7:04.
5. Clerics may also begin with Skin Like Diamond with a start time of 7:05.
6. Warriors, Rogues, and Paladins may choose to begin the race with Spirit
of Wolf cast upon them with a start time of 7:09.
7. Clerics can start with Spirit of Wolf at a start time of 7:10.

The race course is laid out as follow:
1. Kaladim near the Paladin's Guild to Butcherblock Mtns.
2. BB Mtns to Dagnor's Cauldren.
3. Dagnor's Cauldren to Unrest.
4. Once in Unrest you must make your way to the back of the zone, near the
backdoor of the mansion, and /poke the HoV member that will be stationed
there. Once that is done you may make your way back out.
5. Unrest to Greater Faydark.
6. Greater Faydark to Crushbone.
7. Once in Crushbone you must make your way to the area behind the castle
and find the HoV member that is there and /poke them. You may then make your
way back out.
8. Crushbone to Felwithe.
9. Once in Felwithe, you must find the HoV member hidden within the city.
Once you have found them, /poke them then you may make your way back out.
10. Felwithe to Lesser Faydark.
11. Lesser Faydark to Mistmoore Castle.
12. Once you are in Mistmoore, make your way to the other side of the lake
at the bottom of the entrance ramp and /poke the HoV members that will be
waiting there. You know the drill :)
13. Mistmoore Castle to Steamfont Mtns.
14. Steamfont Mtns to Ak'Anon.
15. Once in Ak'Anon you must find me (Ignitz). The first four people to
/poke me are the winners of the race.
There will be HoV members in all the zones mentioned and upon entering each
zone for the first time you must /shout "Naked Newbie Dwarf Race!". This is
to prevent people from shortcutting. The winners will be not only the first
four people to /poke me but will also be the first four people to have
/shout'ed in all the zones and /poke me. If you forget to /shout the phrase
but manage to be one of first four participants to /poke me, I am afraid that
I will not be able to reward you on the grounds of not knowing if you
actually went through all the zones (non-negotionable).

Prizes: 1st place: 500pp and 10 tickets to the Crafted Raffle
2nd place: 250pp and 5 tickets
3rd place: 150pp and 3 tickets
4th place: 100pp and 1 ticket

The prizes will be given to the character who registered the winning dwarf at
the Wizard Spires in Greater Faydark, where we will also be holding the
Crafted Raffle to take place immediately after the last participant finishes
the race.

Good luck to all participants in both events. Questions, Comments, and
Concerns can be addresses by emailing me at moontayle@hotmail.com
'May Valor be your Virtue'
Thanks!


Gordon Infobits, Part 1
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 8:07 PM PDT
Here's part one of today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Fixing the Shaman Pet
    Guys, the wolf is meant to be more of a companion than a pet. It's certainly not meant to be a major factor of how the class stacks up from a balance perspective. Shaman pets will never be near mage or necro pets in power.

    I'll look into the mana requirement, but pet strength sounds proper.

  • "The Trend"
    [Quote from original post: What's the deal? I grew up in a household where "No" from an authority figure was pretty much the end of the discussion. Am I the only one left who comprehends the meaning of "No"? Why are you guys wasting your energy on these dead issues? Wouldn't you be better off focusing your efforts on things that haven't already been shot down by Verant?]

    Part of the problem might be that we don't actually say "No." as it's construed to be impolite. We'd much rather say that "we have no plans at present..." or that "we'd like to do it at some point but..." The fact is, the answer itself is very *rarely* a solid "No." It's more often a soft-maybe with strong tendencies towards No. Perhaps we should use "No" more often :)

  • Moving Characters to Morell-Thule?
    Well we had moved all of those Test people there before someone realized that MT was still in the "race" (the period before the server matures where being 'first' at various things is enjoyed by the players). We moved them off, so my first indication would be that the guy came from Test and we missed him when moving them back out.

    So no, to answer your question, Morrell is not a split server and we aren't performing character-moves as a matter of course. I'll be happy to look into the issue; Do you have the character's name?

  • Miller's Spawn Rate
    [Quote from original post: Abashi said the militia guards were changed from 6 minute spawns to 15 minute spawns in order to allow dungeon grouping to be a viable alternative, so shouldn't the same apply to the Millers?]

    They were originally set this low since they are quest NPCs. Of course, since I'm in a "replying to nerf requests" mood today, I'll talk to the guys about either making the Millers a lot tougher, or increasing their spawn time.

  • Alchemy
    Alchemy is a skill that we want to see financed by dealing with players, not NPCs, which is why NPCs have little use for the fruits of the effort. It's kind of the same situation as with fine-steel, which no one buys off of merchants since they charge so much for it.

    It's one of those things in game to encourage people to deal with each other.

PvP Rulesets
[38 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:42 PM PDT
Gordon made a lengthy post on PvP rules on the Gameplay Board a short time ago. He also asks for thoughts and comments so don't hesitate to speak. Here's the complete post

    Before I get into the subject of this post, let me define the rulesets in play on each of the PvP servers:

    Rallos: Complete PvP. Players can attack any other player within a 4 level limit. Looting is coin plus one item, providing that the item isn't bagged, NODROP, or carried in a melee slot. There are no faction penalties for engaging in PvP combat.

    Tallon/Vallon: PvP Teams. Players can attack any other player within an 8 level limit, assuming that the target is on one of the 3 opposing teams. Looting is coin-only. There *are* faction penalties for engaging in PvP combat.

    As many here have noticed, there have been drives for each of the PvP servers, most recently Rallos, to get the server switched to coin-only loot. However, though the same drive once existed for Vallon, after the change was made, there was likewise a drive to switch it back to item-loot. This suggests that while there are people who enjoy coin-only loot, there are also people who enjoy item-loot. The difficulty that we are faced with is trying to make everyone happy. Simply implementing majority will on each server doesn't accomplish that objective, as it addresses the needs of the pro-coin crowd without taking care of the pro-loot crowd. As much as both groups come here insisting that "everyone" wants what they want, I don't think that anyone can truly deny that both groups exist.

    Right now, we have two servers where coin-loot is in operation, and one where item-loot is the rule. That layout is roughly equivalent to the percentages that enjoy coin-loot versus item loot. The third who enjoy item loot, though technically the minority, are still a significant portion of the gaming populace, and as such should have their concerns addressed as well. Rather than just changing Rallos to coin-loot, it's been suggested that we allow people a one-time chance to move between the PvP servers. Though we are not yet prepared to commit to a plan of that nature, I do want to get everyone's opinion as to whether or not they would find such a plan acceptable.

    If we were to allow moves, a two-step process would be used. First, Rallos people would be given the option to move to Vallon or Tallon (by choice), then Vallon and Tallon people would be given the opportunity to move to Rallos. If moved, players would suddenly find themselves in a new ruleset, subject to every facet of that ruleset.

    Former Rallos players would find themselves on teams, in an environment that discourages socialization outside of your team. They would suddenly be subject to attack in an 8 level limit, and would take race-based faction hits for any PvP kills made. Players with strange deity selections (e.g. Half elf worshipping Innoruuk) would suddenly find themselves homeless for all intents and purposes.

    Former team-server players moving to Rallos would have their ability to engage in combat restricted to a 4 level limit, and be subject to attack and able to attack anyone in that range. Any race-based faction penalties assigned while on the Team servers would follow to Rallos, and would not be reset.

    Players on all PvP servers would encounter an influx of characters of all levels, who wouldn't be completely familiar with any server-specific player-originated rules of etiquette that may be in place. It is possible that aspects once understood as a "given" within the server community might break down and be no more.

    It may sound like I'm trying to discourage acquiescence to this change, however I just want to be sure that all aspects of the proposal are understood prior to anyone giving an opinion on the matter. If done, the change would likely be the biggest to be made to any server community in EverQuest, and we want to be sure that everyone is well-informed.

    Thoughts?

    -Gordon


The New Item-Reimbursement Policy
- Aurum @ 3:38 PM PDT
Gordon posted the following on the Gameplay Board about the new item reimbursement policy:

    Hello all,

    I'm sure that most everyone is aware of the new item-reimbursement policy for loss due to bugs. For those that aren't, here's a quick recap:

    Our intention is to handle all claims in a timely manner. Upon receiving a claim of item loss due to a bug, the GM will begin the investigation process (checking logs, backups, etc). This usually takes roughly 30 to 60 minutes to complete. The GM will then immediately take action and reimburse the item(s) based upon the outcome of the investigation. Our customers diserve courteous and prompt service, and we'll do everything in our power to insure that they receive it.

    One thing that we should have made clear in the announcement is that unfortunately, this policy is not retroactive, meaning that those who experienced loss prior to the time that the policy was announced (7/18/2000) would not be covered by the new policy.

    As mentioned above, we do need to perform due diligence when investigating the claims, and though we will make reimbursements any time that we lack proof of any misconduct, we still need to have that information (backups, logs, etc.) present to perform the search.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits: Saturday Edition
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:10 PM PDT
Here's the latest quotes from Gordon Wrinn that he posted on the Gameplay Board:

  • Rallos Zek
    [Quote from original post: Why wont you answer rallos?]

    I have, many times. It's just not the answer that people want to hear. We've no plans to change loot rules on Rallos at this time, nor do we plan on allowing any moves in regards to Rallos. I'm sorry, but there's really only so many ways that I can address this, and each time has lead to a flame-o-thon on the boards. I'd prefer to stay away from the threads for the time being.

    [Quote from original post: You all put up alternate servers and are doing splits like crazy.]

    I've seen people recommend that we should put up another server with Rallos rules, sans item loot, and do a split, however the number of people playing on Rallos wouldn't make that an economically feasible course of action.

    [Quote from original post: It was supposed to be the most real roleplay server. Now it has turned into an ebay-athon, and you allow the two new pvp servers to go coin only and stop the insane lack of roleplay which comes from a broken pvp system where a melee class is outclassed by ANY caster at ANY level over 12.]

    If it has turned into "naked item gank" server as so many say, does that mean that a majority of people are engaging in that behavior? If not, why is it that people who are against this don't socially isolate the people who are acting like this? I see posts here every day from people who want it one way or the other. I saw people on Vallon complaining as loudly for coin-only loot as people on Rallos. We made the change, and then people from Vallon complained just as loudly for item-loot.

    As of now, we've switched two servers to coin-only loot, which is the majority of the PvP servers out there. While we do want to please the majority and believe that they should have some say, we need to keep in mind the wishes of the minority. The minority here is significant.

    The 4 level limit on Rallos makes item-loot much more viable on that server than it was on the team servers with their 8 level limit. I recommend that if the majority on Rallos truly detests "naked ganking", that you come together as a community and outlaw the practice through social action.

  • More on Bows
    [Quote from original post: This is a problem, but what about the rest of the post, and the blocking of arrows by PC's? This I believe is the largest concern, as I said the other was but a minor suggestion. Are there any reforms to deal with this problem being discussed? Or is this not an option / concern at this point? Any clairification is appreciated.]

    Projectiles are designed to be blocked by obstacles. The same issue occurs with "bolt" type spells. So to answer your question, it is not something that we consider a problem, but rather the way it was designed.

  • Question about the Item Dropbox
    While the commands that we finally implement won't be as verbose as you request, we have looked at the requests of people and will probably make them more verbose than was originally announced. I'll refrain from commenting further on it until we've nailed down the technical issues and are ready to implement it.

  • Why aren't there custom text colors?
    It's a suggestion that's been made frequently, and is something that we'd love to do at some point, however the "problem" at hand keeps getting more and more complex. When EQ was first launched there were only a few different types of "messages" that were displayed in the Chat window. Since then we've added a lot of different things due to player request, such as loot messages, taunt messages, we're about to add item drop messages, NPCs are now doing different things, and the list keeps growing.

    Our ability to implement color selections for all of those different types of messages in the client is wanning. It is however something that we will definitely keep in mind.

  • Melee Classes Getting Pets?!
    As the melee classes as a whole are balanced, certainly in terms of damage output, a pet wouldn't be appropriate in this case.

  • /Consent Changes
    As I mentioned in a few other messages, there were technical and other considerations involved in the decision to get rid of looting. Truth be told, given the short term indications following the change, the immense load off of the GMs due to corpse scaming, we probably don't want to go down the road again.

    Even with seperate commands, many of the corpse scamming issues surrounded people being scamed in to giving consent in the first place. Those issues would still remain if we opened back up corpse looting. Though I do understand that it might be a difficult change for some to accept so soon after it's been made, experience has shown that the number of times players want others to loot their corpse rather than drag it is *very* small. So small in fact that it's not worth the risk to reenable it.

  • "Play a Bard and see how bad they are!"
    [Quote from original post: I am sick and tired of seeing that every gm in a zone is a DE necro, or a SK or somesort. Obviously they know that bards have a disadvantage and are not "good enough" to play]

    There are quite a few GMs who have bards as their GM character, but it really doesn't matter since the GMs don't play their characters.

    That said, bards certainly aren't "bad". They are loved by groups which is right where their area of expertise is supposed to be. I have received word of a few songs that might be having problems right now, not working as intended, but those shortcomings are only ancillary to the entire scope of the bard.

  • PVP Thread
    With the proliferation of posts like this, I thought I'd stop by and recommend that you rephrase your post in a new thread, sans insults, and I might take a stab at it.

  • The VAK'ers
    [Quote from original post: Abashi only answers VAKs]

    Hmmm, several months ago the posts read, "Abashi only answers flames." I must be making progress.

    It does disturb me to see that anyone who posts something positive here is labled a VAK, or even worse, accused of being "planted". Seriously, it doesn't even have to be a "positive" post, just one that's written in a courteous manner and they get flamed. It's so bad that I've received hundreds of emails that say something along the lines of, "I don't want to post this on gameplay and get flamed....but..."

    But back to the subject, if you want to know what type of posts I look for, it's the courteous, well-written ones on subjects that I haven't addressed in a while. I also gravitate towards those *posters* who have always written in a courteous and respectful manner, even if they've been in disagreement. And of course, by the same token, I typically avoid posts by people who generally flame and only get courteous when they have an issue that is important to *them* that they need addressed.

  • Mage Utility Improvement Suggestion
    [Quote from original post: Proposal: summoned bags turn into permanent, non-weight reducing, NODROP bags upon camp/LD.]

    That's an interesting suggestion. I know that we'd have some technical issues with it, but they might not be insurmountable. I think that we'd just have to be certain that there is nothing to gain by going linkdead.

    I really don't like the idea of injecting risk via out-of-game random chance either. I'll talk to the team about it.

  • Problem with Bows
    [Quote from original post: One minor problem, and it is minor, but a slight decrease in delay times for bows globally would help to increase the use of bows as well, and add a whole new facet to gameplay without, in my opinion, unbalancing it.]

    We wouldn't be able to do an across-the-board lowering of bow delays due to the amount that it would increase damage output over a large time model. However, we are looking into negating the delay in the event that you are too close or too far away when you activate the bow. This would allow you to correct the situation and fire again immediatly.

  • Belt of River Nerfed?
    [Quote from original post: I was just finishing off a Spectre in Feerott and out of nowhere Roror started attacking me. I'm a Halfling cleric who has never hunted in Cazic Thule so I couldn't have messed up any factions. This Lizard can kill a well armored and fully buffed lvl 42 cleric in about 5 seconds. I thought about this and am wondering if he hates everyone after the patch how in the world are you supposed to do the quest? He should at least not be Kos to Haflings!]

    The lizardmen outside Cazic Thule were all attached to the wrong faction (Faction "LizardMan"). They were supposed to be aligned with the "good" (relatively) lizards inside the temple. Due to the potential for unexpected death on the part of the players, warnings of this change was put in the patch message. Word has it that there's a way to get in the good graces of those who now attack.

Announcing EverQuests: The Comic
[15 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:32 PM PDT
I am very proud to announce the unveiling of EverQuests, a new exciting addition to the EQ community. EverQuests is the first professionally penned adventure comic that takes place in Norrath and is available to view by the general public.

The Story
The EverQuest's "Official Player's Guide" contains the gruesome story of Miragul. This tale relates how he traps himself inside a phylactery before transferring his soul inside his lich. At the end the lich wanders aimlessly while over the centuries Miragul rages, a captive inside his device.

Our story starts with a small band of dissidents on the Frigid Plain. They plan to help Miragul by freeing his soul so he can possess the lich and continue with his "endless exploration and discovery". They achieve this.

But, unknown to them, Miragul has gone insane and now wants to only have revenge on all of Norrath...

The head of the Wizard's Council sends an Erudite apprentice wizard to gather a small group of brave souls and one innocent cleric to undo the damage. The cleric carries a new phylactery to house the soul of Miragul, should our heros manage to capture him.

And our story begins...
The characters in this comic are famous NPCs that players can recognize and see regardless of the server they are playing on. We start out with Jys D'Bious, a Shadow Knight seller of spells, Eonis Mournunder, head Nacro gnome, and a troll basher named Oggrik. They are the dissidents who let Miragul loose on the world.

EverQuests was created by Barry Blair and is drawn by Blair Grafix. We here at the EQVault are very excited to be able to exclusively host the comic online.

Page one will be published this Monday and plans are to publish page two on Tuesday. Until then, check out the promo cover available now! We have also created a Message Board to discuss the comic in-depth.

Be sure to check this one out!

Gordon Infobits
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:36 PM PDT
Gordon addressed a few posts on the Gameplay Board today. For your convience I have compiled them all in the following text:

  • Roleplaying Server Thread
    My understanding from reading the first post, and please let me know if I'm off base, is that you'd like us to set up a server, designated as "Roleplay", provide guidelines for playing on the server, with more GM/Guide support for dynamic content. Furthermore, though the guidelines would exist, there would be no enforcement of it.

    Assuming that summation is close to what you are proposing, I'm pretty sure that your goals wouldn't be met in the long run. I think immediately people would go to the server with ideas about always staying in character. However, there are also people who play the game that would go there just to annoy the roleplayers. Then there would also be people who would go there due to having more GM/Guide support for dynamic content. In the end, without any enforcement, which most will agree cannot be applied consistantly, I think they would end up less in-character role-playing than other servers in the game.

    Experience has shown that without enforcement in an environment such as this, the desired condition cannot be met. Take this board as an example. Flaming, insulting, swearing, threatening, and being a general anti-social troublemaker is not allowed on these boards per the Station Terms of Service. Attempts to ask those people who post in that fashion to keep it respectful and courteous further drives the overall environment towards the undesirable. Why? Because it's not enforced. We are going to enforce those rules in the near future, and it will either work, or the boards will be shut down. I think that an unenforced roleplay server would have the same problem.

    But we can come up with consistant rules to enforce the boards, where we can't with a subjective concept such as roleplaying. I really like Har's definition of roleplaying, but can we expect any 10 people to enforce that definition in the same way?

  • "Reconsider Yellow Spell Damage Text"
    There's obviously people here who both like and dislike the yellow color. Given that we introduced the yellow color, I don't think that we'll make everyone happy by changing it back, so we'll probably leave it as is. Perhaps if we had left it white/black it wouldn't be an issue, but the cats out of the bag :)

  • "Why was Invisibility Nerfed, again?"
    It seems that after nearly every patch someone feels that invisibility has been nerfed in duration. I know that I've had to make posts such as this after well over a dozen patches since I've been at Verant.

    Just to be clear, invisibility was touched in this patch, aside from making it so that pets are no longer a valid target. In order to try to head off future claims of this nature, I *promise* that I will let you know if we ever touch invisibility's duration.

  • "As a Master of Magical Damage, why is a Ranger's channeling skill higher than mine?"
    [Quote from original post: Rangers cap at 215, Wizards at 200. What is the vision behind this?]

    As it turns out, we increased the channeling cap for pure casters in the last patch.

  • The Vision
    I've answered dozens of questions entitled "what is the vision". Why is it that these keep coming up with people assuming that it is some tangible thing that can be put up on a website for all to view? Have you ever said to anyone, "That wasn't really what I had in mind..."? What you are saying is that it doesn't agree with your vision.

    There is not some all-encompassing document that I can post somewhere for everyone to read, because it's an ever-growing and ever-changing concept. When something new comes up, we sit down and ask, "Is this what we had in mind?" A discussion follows at which time we decide whether or not it is. The vision is every email exchanged by every member of the dev team. It's the original design document for EverQuest (which will not be posted). It's the understanding that comes out of formal meetings in the conference room and the informal ones around the watercooler. It's every hope that everyone here had and has for EverQuest.

    Do you want to know the vision? Read our web site, read our posts, play the game, and ask yourself honestly what we had in mind, or, ask me. I answer questions every day here that consist basically of, "Why did you do XXX?" The answer to that question is the vision. But please, don't expect me to be able to lay out anything and everything that everyone ever had in mind about everything to do with the game. It's not that simple.

    [Quote from original post: I dislike playing a game where 95% of the important information is hidden from the consumer's eyes. ]

    That 95% of that information is meant to be discovered by you while you play the game. If you choose to go to spoiler sites to find out as much as you can, that is your choice, but we won't be divulging the mystery of the game. We feel that mystery is important in the game and that no one should know with certainty everything that there is to know. That's our vision.

    [Quote from original post: The only thing I know is what the VISION isn't. It isn't kiting, it isn't FD pulling, it isn't having good stuff, it isn't a lot of things. ]
