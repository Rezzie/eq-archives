    Mana Costs for Hybrids
    [Quote: Concerning hybrids not being as efficient at using mana, and the spells of parents. The argument was put forth that no one would play the parent if the hybrids could perform as well as the parent class. The problem is that you penalize the hybrids MULTIPLE times. (I think that hybrids are over-penalized there as well, but I wanted to focus on the efficiency issue.) Just my opinion.

    Larsk Thunderfoot of Prexus ]

    Well, we disagree. There were three differences between hybrids and their caster parent classes; Mana pool, casting cost, and spell selection.

    Recently we removed the mana pool difference and significantly reduced the casting cost difference. We also gave the hybrid the advantage of unique spells and a significantly reduced penalty for casting while fighting (weapon delays continue to count off while spells are being cast – only for hybirds). In combination, all of those changes make for a very nice balance of spell casting power and melee power for the hybrids.

    Yes, we'd have to remove the spell availability restriction before there would be any chance that people would prefer the hybrid over the parent caster class. But just that restriction alone isn't quite enough to make the balance that we want. We're very happy with the current balance, and I don't see any need to change it.

    [Quote: Absor said "If hybrids could cast spells as well as their parent class, then nobody would really want to play that parent class."

    If your ranger had the same spell line up as a druid with no penalties (cast as well as their parent class), then there would be very few druids.

    Yes, I really think that. You do to. I'm not trying to be tricky or cover up the fact that there are indeed two restrictions on hybrids for their spells (see post above). There's no need to read things into my comments. And if I wasn't clear (though I thought I was) then I apologize for the confusion.

    Wizards and Druids Spells: Transportation
    [Quote: My question is, are we going to see something like this for the druid class? I think it's a great idea, and would love to be able to cast these myself.

    Thanks,

    Aeslyn Stormwolf
    Druid of the 29th session
    Terris-Thule ]

    The short answer is, probably not.

    Regardless of past perceptions or texts or class descriptions, Wizards are becoming masters of teleportation. They will probably never get foot speed enhancement spells, that sort of travel will always be the real of the Druid, Bard and Shaman.

    The reasoning behind this is pretty simple, really.

    Druids have few weaknesses as a class. In fact, with the recent addition to their high level healing abilities, Druids are a very well-rounded and balanced class (though this is not the result of any final balance decision about the class, and things might be changed). Their ability to transport people is pretty darn good as it is. Not only can a Druid pop you across the ocean, he can speed up your running and get you to the final destination pretty quickly.

    Wizards right now have two specialties, damage and teleportation. They should be the masters of arcane magic and ripping holes in the fabric of space. Making them the masters of teleportation not only makes a lot of sense, it also adds a dimension to the class.

    Yes, we are moving toward making Wizards the master of arcane transportation. This seems like the right direction to take the class.

    EverQuest: Plot & Story
    [Quote: Absor emitted: "But for the new player, who has never seen the old content, the fact that Qeynos is 'new' won't add much to the value of the game for them. Whereas new content is new for everyone."

    In one sense, you're very right. I was partying with some folks the other day who started playing about a year ago, and I mentioned something about "since Kithicor was changed". They were completely confused, especially when I told them that Kith used to be a n00bie zone. To newer players, it's almost as if Bloody Kithicor never happened. (That's a sad fact, but is beside the point of what I'm getting at here.)

    In another sense, this isn't the whole truth. For example, even after two years of being involved in EverQuest, my highest-level character is at 21. I'm certainly not high enough level to experience a large portion of the game, and that includes much of the content that was released new since EQ's release in 1999: most of the Planes, Velious, and much of Kunark.

    You can go on releasing new high-level content, and you can tweak and change the existing high-level content as much as you like, but it'll be new to me once I finally get there. The question is really whether new players will be more likely to stick with EQ and derive more enjoyment from their low-level play if the low-level content is updated and redesigned as well.

    Another way to look at it is, if you change Qeynos Hills or Misty Thicket or Butcherblock Mountains, you're updating content accessible by nearly the entire player base, and what's more, you're updating around a pre-existing structure, taking care of most of the work of content development. When you do another add-on or change some underused zone in the depths of Velious, you're excluding a lot of casual gamers from experiencing our ever-changing Norrath.

    The plague of Bertoxxulous in the Karanas is a great example of the sorts of things that should be going on all the time in EverQuest. Huge expansions of high-level content may be more exciting from a press-release standpoint, but as game designers, it's just as important that the entire game be kept exciting. To be honest, I'm quite surprised that Freeport has yet to be taken over by Dark Elves (or the same for Neriak).... :)

    Server Split Questions
    I'm going to respond within your post. With so many questions, it's easier to read if the answers appear directly after the questions.

    [Quote: I have been playing EQ for about 6 months now, and I have found out that the server I play on was initially a split server. Recently, a new split server (Zebuxaruk) was opened, splitting off E'Ci and The Rathe, as I recall.

    My questions revolve around split server economics, and are many. Though you might be able to answer several or all of them with a simple publicized policy statement regarding split servers. ]

    Well, there isn't a lot of mystery about it, but I'll answer your questions as best as I can.

    [Quote: 1. What metrics do you use to determine when or if a split server is created, and what servers it draws from? ]

    We create new servers based on all sorts of factors. Primarily we put one up when their seems to be a need because of overcrowding on existing servers. There's no science to deciding whether a new server will be a 'split' or a new server. We judge that based on the perceived need as much as anything. And when it is a split server, we will split people from servers that are the most crowded. Keep in mind that some servers don't have their busiest times when others do. Recently, for example, we split Karana when it wasn't one of the top two busiest servers during the usual prime time. It was, however, much more crowded during its own prime time than the others were in theirs.

    [Quote: 2. Why are all the split servers delineated from exactly two "older" servers? ]

    Actually, I think one was split from three servers. Splitting from two servers usually gives both the older servers the relief they need, while not flooding the new server with too many people. More than two could easily fill the new server (when you include players that start fresh on that server) so much that the new server would be needing a split… And that sort of defeats the purpose.

    [Quote: 3. Why are there not more opportunities to "split" from one server to another? ]

    Undercrowding is just as much of a problem as overcrowding. We won't split a server based only on a slight or possibly a temporary increase in population. Splitting a server and ending up with an underpopulated server is something we need to avoid. We always approach adding servers with caution, nobody really knows how a server will react to a split, how many people will move and how many will stay.

    [Quote: 4. Once a new server is designated the "split to server", what is the likelihood of it becoming the "split from server" and in what time frame?]

    It's really the other way around. We'll usually identify that we need to split a server or two and then gear up to set up a server to split them to. The timeframe varies, as do all things, based on what's going on here or with our Ops department at the time. But we can move pretty fast.

    [Quote: 5. What happens in the case that you have characters on both of the servers being split from and you /movelog from both servers? ]

    If you have a total of less than eight characters, then they would all be available to you on the new server. If your total, however, is greater than eight, some of those characters will not be available to you. You'll have to delete the ones that you are willing to lose until you have reduced yourself to just eight.

    [Quote: 6. What is the procedure when characters from the two different splitting servers have the same name? Who keeps it, who changes? ]

    When two servers are split to one server, one of the characters with duplicate names will have an 'x' appended to their name at random. The player whose character has had the 'x' appended to its name will be able to have his or her name changed by a GM to a new name. But the character without the 'x' will be the one to keep the original name. If you get the appended 'x' you will have to go by a new name, you won't be getting the old one back.


    But, as was the case recently, if characters are moved to a server by a second movelog and there is an existing character with that name on the server, the existing character will keep the name and the new one will have the 'x' appended. The newly moved character can petition to have his name changed to something new. This is one of the risks of moving to a new server.

    [Quote: 7. What is the chance of a new server that would allow splits from ANY other server? ]

    None. For reasons I explained before, the new server would be hopelessly overcrowded.

    [Quote: 8. More specifically, what is the chance of Xev and Drinal splitting onto the same server in the future, and if they were to do so, how long into the future do you think that would be? ]

    I can only guess that you have friends on one of those servers, and you play on another… :)

    I can't possibly tell you when or if those two servers will be split. The odds of them being split to the same server would seem slim.

    [Quote: 9. Could you explain again publicly why you will not move characters between servers except under the split server scenario? ]

    There are a few reasons. People would want to switch servers just to avoid their reputations. We don't want people to be able to duck out on their reputations. And server economies vary. Pulling an account with a ton of items that are not as rare on one server to a new server where they are extremely rare provides an advantage to that player. And a lot of moves like that could affect the server as a whole. Moving characters also requires a fair amount of work on our side.
