Thoughts on SZ Teams
- Aurum @ 6:51 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn shared some thoughts on the configuration of the Sullon Zek teams on the Developer's Corner. Here's the post:

    Most Sullon players, I think, noticed the MOTD on Sullon last week hinting that the Nameless was thinking of levying large penalties against those in the Evil Alliance due to the outnumbering of others. Frankly, we're wondering if anything *really* needs to be done.

    Evils currently outnumber good and neutral combined and have a higher average level due to there being safety in numbers and the geographical isolation of the Iksar.

    So why did so many people join Evil? It's not one of power -- The class availability of the teams is in good balance. I imagine zone-control is a big part, but my guess is that it comes down mostly to "evil" seeming "cooler" than good. Do players *really* find this a problem? How many players joined Good expressly for the higher difficulty level and the "target rich environment"? How many joined evil due to a desire to be a part of what's imagined to be the "winning team"?

    Right now based on the point-system announced last week, evil and good are scoring a similar number of points despite there being 4x as many evils as good. Neutral on the other hand is laying waste to both other teams despite being outnumbered by evil?

    I suppose what it comes down to more than anything is: Are people having fun?

    We have been discussing potential "solutions" to the team lop-sidedness. Some have suggested that we tweak experience rewards so that Good and Neutral level faster in order to make those teams more attractive. Others have suggested that we make evil level slower. Differing experience penalties on death have also been discussed. Frankly I'm not a fan of either of these ideas for one main reason: Everyone caps out at level 60, and come level 60 differing experience requirements don't mean squat.

    There is another idea that we've been discussing. It's passed internal muster, which means it's time to bring it up and see what Sullon players think. As it's hard for us to query players of individual servers or rulesets on their thought, I'll ask everyone replying to open their reply with whether or not they play on Sullon currently.

    Our idea is this, in the words of EQLive's Assistant Producer Bill "Solist" Coyle, what's the one thing throughout fantasy literature that has made it possible for Good to stand their ground and beat back the overwhelming forces of Evil?

New "Ask Brad" on Developer's Corner
- Aurum @ 6:44 PM PDT
Vice President Brad McQuaid announced the creation of the second "Ask Brad" column on the Developer's Corner. The following is the complete announcement:

    Hello all,

    Some of you who read voodooextreme.com might have noticed the ‘Ask Brad’ column that was recently added. Well, I was thinking, if there’s an interest, of doing something similar here as well.

    How it works is this: people send in emails, asking me questions. I sift through them, pick out a few every week or so, and then answer them. I was thinking of doing both columns, in that different people read that site as well as this one (although I’m sure there’s overlap).

    I am more interested in answering certain questions than others, though. In other words, I have neither the time nor the inclination to compete with Absor I’m not looking for EverQuest current event questions, or why a certain item was changed or has certain stats, or whether a quest works or not, or why was ‘taunt’ changed recently, or to engage in a big debate over the precise rule set of Sullon Zek vs. Tallon Zek.

    That’s what this board in general is for, and we already have a working feedback loop in place.

    Rather, I’d be interested in answering more broad questions. Questions about game design in general, or why certain decisions were made in creating EverQuest. Or even questions about some of our other announced products, and how they might be similar or different to EverQuest. Or questions about our company in general, our philosophies, what we do every day, how games are developed... or even, 'Brad, who the heck are you anyway?'

    Now, I’m not at all certain how many people are interested in this sort of thing, but heck, let’s give it a try. Send your emails directly to me, but make sure they have ASK BRAD in the beginning of the subject, or they’ll slip through my email filter. Then every week or so I’ll reply to as many as I can with a public post in this forum (btw, when you send an email in, if you don’t want your name shown, please make sure you let me know).

    And please try to adhere to the themes and topics I’ve described. I’ll have to ignore everything else (and, of course, that includes flames and the like – please be nice to me :) ).

    My email: bmcquaid@station.sony.com

    -Brad McQuaid
    Executive Producer, EverQuest
    Vice President, Premium Games
    Sony Online Entertainment/Verant Interactive

    I think I can honestly say, Brad, that noone is buying what your selling here. Even with your reasons, as sound as you might percieve them, I doubt very many people you are doing it simply out of the goodness of your hearts. The fact that Verant is actually stating that giving players this option will STRENGTHEN the community is laughable.

    I also agree with a previous poster about the policy on not moving no drop items. While I think it's fine and dandy that you say "We are not allowing any items/coin to be moved, including no drop items," your logic in dissallowing it is, quite frankly, shoddy(sp.) I think you'd have just said "We are not moving any items" and left it at that.

    I did read all of the rules and such, and I' quite glad no moves are allowed to/from Test and Sullon Zek. Very good move there.

    Malakie
    50 Enchanter
    Test Server

    BTW, Brad... Test Server just passed it's 1 year anniversy of the item whipe. Just thought I'd throw in a friendly reminder.

    malakie: I'm sorry you feel that way. The reason we say we are doing it to strengthen community is because we want to bring friends or family that might have started on different servers together.

    What we don't want are extreme power gamers hopping from one server to another, collecting phat lewts, ever in search of servers where one can 'buy low', and then transfering to another to 'sell high'.

    Also, players know how many high-end items are in the game, including no-drop items. We don't want to disrupt that aspect of community either. Some players take pride in knowing they were one of the first on their server to obtain an item, and I think it might be a bummer to suddenly have someone else with that item transfer in. So that's why we're not allowing no-drop items to move either.

    I assure you that were we doing this just to make a buck, we'd lower the price and allow people to move with items -- the result would be a LOT more people interested, and therefore a LOT more revenue.

    But, and probably contrary to what some might believe, I don't believe in hurting the health of the game long term to make a buck short term.

    -Brad

McQuaid Talks About Character Transfers
- Aurum @ 7:27 PM PDT
In this post, Brad McQuaid attempts to explain Verant/SOE's decision to allow transfers, for a price, after years of saying it wasn't an option:

    There have been three major reasons we've not accomodated server moves before:

    1. We were concerned that it would upset the economies on the destination server

    2. We lacked the manpower to accomdate the moves.

    3. We were afraid one server might be perceived as 'better' or more 'popular' and that players would flock there.

    We've now addressed these three issues:

    1. You move without items, and the cost is somewhat prohibitive. In other words, players will hopefully move for the right reasons now, and being sans items shouldn't measurably disturb the destination server's economy.

    2. We've updated our billing system and automated much of the task, such that the manpower hit is minimal.

    3. We're pretty sure we have enough servers now (which wasn't the case in the past) such that this won't happen. That said, if it does happen (or if anything 'bad' happens because of this), I assure you it will be turned off ASAP.

    Therefore, I must respectfully disagree with Shadoe's assertion that we are 'selling out' in any way and/or abandoning our principles.

    -Brad

    Your faction will travel with your character.

    As for why we won't move items, here's the logic behind it.

    We decided it was in the best interest of the EverQuest community to only move characters without items, coins or the contents of their bank. If we permitted characters to move with their items, coins and the contents of their bank, it would affect the economy and item balance on their new server. Server economies vary and a move to a different server by a high level character or a large guild could completely change the economy on that server and could also drastically affect the gameplay and game balance.

    No drop items are a part of that economy. Just the appearance of a new high level character on a server can have a dramatic effect on the social and economic properties of that server. Allowing no-drop items to suddenly appear on a server where perhaps only a few, or none, have been gathered yet impacts those that were on that server already. No, that person couldn't sell these items for some unreasonably high price on the new server, since they are no drop. But those items affect the culture on a server. And many no-drop items are stepping stones for large quest rewards. It would be possible to transfer with quest items that would make gaining certain items, such as epic items, much easier to get on the new server if that server wasn't mature enough for the characters to tackle certain obstacles that were more easily tackled on another server.

    We also have to consider the reasons that people move. Folks moving to join friends on a different server will have people there to help them recover their items. People moving to escape a bad reputation won't.

    We understand that this service will not appeal to all of our customers. But it will appeal to many, and this is the form for that service that has the least negative impact on those that don't use it, which is the majority of our customers.

    Nope, we're not going to change them.

    I've been getting a LOT of requests for changes to this tinting or that armor appearance.

    Please stop.

    We're not going to revamp every piece of armor in the game to meet the desires of a few people just so we can upset other people that liked the way they looked already. In a few rare cases we've seen some changes that seem to make sense. But we are not going to go back and add this graphic to that sword or that color to your favorite piece of armor…

Emergency Patch: Three Fixes Made
- Aurum @ 7:35 PM PDT
The Developer's Corner was updated by Alan with some info concerning the emergency patch implemented today:

    We fixed the bug with feign death spells that was causing it to fail and sending a "Your spell has been interrupted" message.

    We fixed the way that NPCs were treating pets. They were accrediting more hate to pets than they should have.

    We also fixed a bug with factions that was making it impossible to raise some factions. There may still be factions that you can't raise because there are no methods for raising them, not because of a bug. If you get a faction message you will also get a faction gain (or hit).

    We also added a message that will let you know when you can no longer gain or lose faction with a certain group. It will say:

    Your faction standing with <faction name> could not possibly get any worse.

    or

    Your faction standing with <faction name> could not possibly get any better.

    That was the essence of the patch last night. Sorry about the problems.

    Alan
