Through rain, snow, sleet and rain (not to mention server problems) we present to you today's edition of the Absor Infobits:

    Spelling Errors
    First, I want to thank all of you that have been sending in spelling errors. I have a few comments to make about some of the types of comments we've been getting.

    A few people have pointed out that when you use the Hail feature, it creates a sentence like: "Hail Bob." Such a sentence should, we admit, have a comma after the word hail. It should read: "Hail, Bob."

    Unfortunately, changing that would be a real problem. NPCs are trained (through expensive drug therapy) to respond to the existing text, without the comma. Retraining them (which would probably mean intensive electric shock therapy) would require that we change every NPC that responds to that hail text. While perhaps a worthwhile endeavor, we have decided that the grammatical headaches that this causes some people doesn't warrant the time needed to make the change, and the likely bugs and broken quests that would occur. Sorry, but it's going to stay as it is.

    Also, we've had a lot of comments about things on the character creation screens. Those have to be changed by programmers, not designers. We've created an extensive list of changes needed, and the programmers will be fixing them when they get the odd spare moment. That's just going to take time to finish, since it seems more worthwhile to have the programmers working on other things most of the time.

    Otherwise, we are tackling the spelling errors as they come in. Many of the ones you've sent in are already fixed. Thanks for the help in this.

    [Quote: Absor said: "Yes, you can trade across teams. I don't see any real reason to prevent people from trading."

The RP reason is the same reason that you can't group with someone on another team and that is that your god would not permit you to render assistance to an enemy. Trading is the exact same thing. So if a neutral character sees a good character that has lost all of his stuff, he can help that person out a bit. I think that's the reason that I'm in favor of allowing trades across teams.

    Pets and Spells
    Halbarad wrote:

    [Quote: I'd like to point out that this doesn't make any sense within the gameworld mechanics that I am aware of.

    Invisibility has one effect, and one effect only: It prevents you from being added to a creature's hate list by moving close to it. (i.e. makes all creatures that don't see invisible indifferent to you). It does NOT prevent you from being added to the hate list for other reasons, nor does it remove you from the hate list if you are already on it.

    Having your pet attack a creature adds aggro towards the caster; if it didn't, a monster wouldn't come after you if you had your pet attack it and your pet died. Being invisible will in no way protect you from the monster that just killed your pet, because you are on it's hate list, and it will find you and attack you.

    So, insofar as I understand it, the above explanation doesn't hold water. Now, if you don't want someone to be able to travel invisibly with a pet, that's fine - just say so - but let's not have some confused explanation that makes it sound like aggro works differently than has always been demonstrated.

    Oh... and as an aside, if, for some bizarre reason, you do happen to allow invisible casters with pets, could you make it so that /pet get lost breaks charm? Thanks. :)]

    That is true, now. But back then invisibility, if cast before the pet attacked, would prevent you from getting onto the hate list of the NPC. So with the NPC targeted and the caster sitting out of sight, the caster could send pets in for as long as his or her mana held out. Both invisibility and the way pets work were changed because of exploits like that. It may be true that neither you nor I can think of any specific exploits that would come from reverting this. But I'm pretty convinced that some would crop up.

    It works fine as it is now. The only reason to change it would be to reduce the thought required for pet casters (Should I cast now, or wait until we're further along? Will casting now cause me too much trouble?). The ability to know when to cast a pet is important to the smart pet caster, and can distinguish a well played magician from a poorly played one (though obviously less so that the ability to successfully command you pet). With the ability to summon your pet, I don't really see any need to change the way this works, and certainly not at the risk of a potential exploit.

    Charm is a separate matter. Get lost doesn't break the charm because the pet can't choose to break the charm, you have to do that with the spells provided you.

    Shark Meat
    Breno wrote:

    [Quote: yea just want to know if VI is going to allow the selling of shark mea back into the game i have a bard that all he does is skill skill skill master baker potter tayler and the other day i found a recipie for smoked shark but when i arrived in hurugan in velious and went to morgans meat where i was told sold the suff to my suprise it had been taken out do to druids and ppl exploiting some ques that they ended up nerfing anyway so why take the shark meat away when he quest was nerfed. some ppl need the stuff for making good food for parties

    The short answer is, no.

    Here's the series of events that lead up to that decision.

    Some time in the far distant past, someone created a quest that required shark meat. At that time the only source of shark meat on Norrath was living sharks with sharp teeth. The quest rewards were balanced based on the facts of the day.

    Recently we added a bunch of new recipes for bakers. Shark meat was used in one (or some) of those recipes. So shark meat was added on a vendor or two to facilitate the use of that recipe.

    This made the original quest extremely easy for people that could transport themselves. People used this fact to gain rewards beyond the intended difficulty of the quest.

    Sure, we should have caught it. But with the huge number of quests and the large increase in baking recipes and items, we missed it. To fix this we had, really, two choices. Remove the quest or remove the meat from vendors.

    We chose to remove the meat. Bakers don't suffer in their ability to make skill, there are other recipes that will get them through those skill ranges. All this does is make shark dishes a rarity, and that's actually something that I think is a good thing. If someone gives you food with shark meat in it, you know that they've gone to some trouble to make that food.

    Sullon Zek rules:

    1. Player v. Player (PvP) combat can occur between any characters that are not on the same team, regardless of level. A level 50 character can attack and kill a level 6 character if that character is not a member of his alliance. But rules 2 and 3 apply.

    2. Characters will be immune to PvP combat until they have gained enough strength to survive the struggle. Characters below 6th level are not able to participate in PvP combat. The first time a character zones after gaining 6th level he or she will then become PvP enabled.

    3. Characters will lose experience when killed in PvP combat if the character that killed them is within 5 levels. The amount of experience lost will be the same amount that is lost if that character is killed by an NPC. Your corpse can be resurrected by a cleric to partially restore lost experience.

    4. Characters will only be able to loot coins from other characters, and then only if the victim was within 5 levels of the killer. Only the character getting the killing blow will be able to loot the corpse.

    5. Players may only have one character at a time on this server. This means that you will have to delete the existing character in order to create a new one. Because we are introducing a new class and a new race with the release of Shadows of Luclin we plan to make allowances at that time for those that wish to explore these new characters.

    6. Characters will not be able to group with anyone that is not on their Deity Team.

    7. Characters will not be able to invite someone to join their guild that is not on their Deity Team.

    8. Combat skills' effective value is capped at 2 levels above a players current level.

    9. Resistance debuff spells have a 50% increased effectiveness against player characters.

    10. /consider returns only three kinds of results when used against player characters. 'Green' means that the character is below your range. 'White' or 'black' means that the character is within your range. 'Red' means that they are above your range. 'In range' means +/- 5 levels.

    11. /consider will also display a message indicating what team the target is on. Members of your own team will be displayed as an 'ally', members of another team will be shown as 'threateningly'.

    12. Faction hits for PvP will be taken for killing characters within their racial home towns. Killing a human in any human starting city will cause a faction loss with the locals. Paineel and Erudin are both starting cities for erudites, and killing one in either city (regardless of their place of origin) will result in a faction loss with that city. Other cities are not considered home towns, despite being run and controlled by a certain race. For example, HighHold is not a human home town. Thurgadin is not a dwarven home town.

Cursing, threats and other such things will be dealt with sternly, and are still unacceptable behavior on PvP servers.

    15. No character will be able to bind in dungeon zones.

    16. Beneficial spells will not affect characters more than 20 levels lower than the spell level. Existing spell restrictions on spells over 50th level will supercede this rule.

    17. There will be 'insignia' dropped on the corpses of characters over 20th level that are killed by another character. We plan to allow those to be turned in for a reward of some sort. What that reward will be is undetermined at this time, but we are leaning towards a team reward rather than a personal reward.

In short, what they did was carefully orchestrate 100,000+ hps encounters so that, for 75% or more of the time that it took to kill the encounter, the monster got beaten on and was not able to fight back.
As you know, I dislike tackling GM abuse stories on the frontpage because most the time we can never seem to get to the truth. Keeping this in mind, I will not be covering this event any further unless something extremely important arises from this incident. For readers who wish to find out more, this report has provided both sides of the story with links to find out more information from each source.
