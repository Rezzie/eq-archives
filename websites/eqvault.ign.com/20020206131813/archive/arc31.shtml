New DX Initialization Code
- Aurum @ 7:41 PM PST
A few Direct X issues were addressed by Brad McQuaid and Rowyl and the Developers Corner recently. The following excerpt is a complete copy of Brad's post:

    Yes, we did make some DX intitialization code changes that should enable us in the future to take advantage of newer 3D cards and their feature sets.

    There are a few problems associated with this code that are affecting some players. Addressing these issues and getting a new client up is a priority and we appreciate everyone's patience in the matter.

    -Brad
Another important post in this thread was from Rowyl:
    RE: new features available as a result of the new DX initialization code.

    Please note, these features only work with D3D, so don't try them if you are using Glide.

    32 Bit Color Rendering
    If your video card supports 32 Bit color 3D rendering then you can turn this on. Currently, the way to turn this on is via editing your eqclient.ini file. Look for the line that reads VideoModeBitsPerPixel=16 and change the 16 to a 32. Not only does this use 32 bit color rendering, but it utilizes 24 or 32 bit Z-Buffering (depending on your card) which improves the visual quality of many zones. Cards that I know will work are ones based on the nVidia TNT or Geforce chipsets, ATI Rage 128 and Radeon cards, and 3Dfx Voodoo 4 or 5 cards. There are others, you can check your video card manual or box to see if it lists 32bit color rendering as a feature. Cards that we know do not support 32bit color rendering are the Voodoo 1, 2, or 3, and nVidia Riva 128 based cards (like the STB Velocity 128 and others).

    DirectX Texture Compression
    This feature will work with any system or card. When enabled it will compress the textures at load time greatly reducing the memory needed to run without swapping to the hard disk. However, it will increase your load time, primarily between server select and character select. If you have a system with more then 128 MB of ram then you probably don't need to turn this on and suffer the longer load times. However, if you are running with 64 MB or less then this could help with your in game performance at the cost of a little extra load time. You turn this one on by editing your eqclient.ini and changing the line that reads UseD3DTextureCompression=FALSE to be TRUE instead. Also, note your video card does NOT need to support DXTC for this feature to work, however if it does you will gain a little more performance from it.
Thanks Skip!

Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:41 PM PST
New messages were posted by Alan to the Developer's Corner today:

    Guild Message of the Day
    [Quote: New functionality: /guildmotd ("guild message of the day")

    When executed by guildmaster, it stores a text message server side in his guild's record in the guild database. Length of text same as maximum permitted for tells, etc.

    When members of the guild log on, the message is played back to them automatically. The message can also be replayed at any time by any guild member by executing /guildmotd

    This is a very high value-added feature to guild life. It's a powerful and simple way to get 50 people to go to the same place at the same time for an event at short notice. Many guilds use message boards to try to coordinate and plan events (e.g. raids) in advance, but there are ALWAYS people who show up late or not at all for guild events because they didn't read the board, they didn't remember the right time etc.

    It's been suggested many times and every time tons of your customers supported the idea as a great one, but it seems it just doesn't reach the right set of ears in VI.

    Datalore
    The Rathe ]

    Actually, it has already reached the right ears. I've been told that this is on the list of things that we want to do. It's not a real high priority item, though, and I don't have any sort of a timeframe for its implementation. But we've certainly heard the request, and we rather like the idea.

    Sign Your Comments
    When you send us your comments, please sign them at the bottom with your character name or real name, or whatever name you want us to use. It would be nice to have a name to put with the letters that we post.

    Board Issues
    [Quote: suggestion for boards

    Could you guys make it so there was some tracking information automatically put in the subject line of the Submit Comment form? So that the post we were responding to (if we were responding to a post on the boards) would be clearer. Just a though....I really like the new boards overall, they've become a place to visit again!

    Tali ]

    Actually, I think a lot of people misunderstand how the "Submit Comments" thing works. I should have explained it. Thanks for bringing it up.

    When you hit the "Submit Comment" button, you are opening a web form. That form does not know the title of the subject that you are replying to. So when you are sending in your comments, if you want to refer to a specific post, please list it in the subject. The form does not know which subject you are replying to, and so it can't tell us. We've received several messages from people that spend several paragraphs talking about a particular item. By the end of the message I still don't know what there are referring to because they didn't mention it specifically in the message, and it wasn't in the subject line.

    So please, make sure to be clear with your subject line when sending comments.

    In the mean time, I'll see if the web team can get your suggestion implemented. Thanks.

    Fearsome Shield

    We are not going to be transferring these no-drop shields. As a shield, it is one of the best (arguably THE best) in the game. Allowing it to be worn as a 40 armor class cloak was an error on our part, and for that we are sorry. But as a back item it was far too powerful.

    Colorblind Players
    [Quote: I believe I asked about this before but it got lost in the meriad of useless posts. As far as my research into configs for the game goes, there does not seem to be any way to change the color of the target's health bar. Currently it is a (or so I'm told) a dark red color. This is a serious problem for me when I am in areas with a somewhat dark background, including almost all interior dungeons. I can't distinguish the target's life bar at all. Would it be possible to put in a config to change this life bar to some RGB color code?

    thanks, and kudos on the new forums.

    --Gotte, Druzzil Ro ]

    Right now the target's health bar is indeed dark red. You should be able to make it much more visible if you change the color of the background for the target box to something solid and light. Use the /wincolor command to accomplish this. You might want to use something like /wincolor target 100 100 100 4. This will make the box a sort of solid light gray. You can increase the first three numbers and the color will appear more white. The basics for the /wincolor command are this: "/wincolor" is the command name. The first three numbers are RGB color numbers. Try different ones to get some idea how they work (like 0 0 100 is dark blue). And the last digit is the transparency value. 4 is solid, 1 is very clear. If you type /wincolor in game with no parameters it will give you more information.

    I hope that helps. I don't know that we'll be changing the color of the health bar itself, since this feature has the same effect.

Gameplay Board Closed!
- Aurum @ 7:01 PM PST
A big announcement was posted by Alan VanCouvering this evening. Sony Online Entertainment has shutdown the Gameplay Board and have created a different solution for submitting feedback. In this post Alan gives his reasons for the change:

    Hello all.

    We've made some changes to the message boards, as you can see. The letter below is from the team.

    Alan


    ------------

    Hello all,

    Upon EverQuest’s release, Sony Online Entertainment, then a separate company, came to Verant Interactive and proposed that they set up and host community message boards on the EverQuest site. The purpose of these boards was to provide a place for EverQuest players to hang out, get to know each other, and basically do what EverQuest players do in any of dozens of message forums hosted by EverQuest fan sites.

    As they grew more popular, a culture of dedicated and knowledgeable players formed, and the forums quickly evolved into a place where game design concepts were discussed freely by a relatively small group of people. As a developer always open to new ideas, the boards did not go unnoticed by the EverQuest design team, and we began commenting on issues there regularly.

    As the weeks went on, some EverQuest players heard that the “Official” EverQuest forums were the place to go if you wanted to speak with the EverQuest Development Team. As issues were addressed, it was apparent that the boards were a phenomenal tool for collecting player feedback, listening to issues, and occasionally to release information.

    In late 1999, we decided to even further our commitment to communicate with players about development issues – a philosophy that remains true to this day. And it seemed the boards were the best place to do this. To that end, a full-time Community Relations Manager was hired to not only disseminate information to the public via the boards, but also to bring the public’s issues to the development team such that valid player issues would be addressed and not overlooked – the latter part being key.

    Fast-forward to today: Unfortunately, the EverQuest message boards are often a hotbed of exaggerated negativity where only through one-upmanship, melodramatics, and dauntless persistence can one hope to create a big enough thread surrounding an issue as to have it addressed. Posters with the opportunity and time to engage in these tactics at least get to see an Official tell them “Yes” or “No”, while others with equally valid stated concerns and the same desire to make the game better for everyone see their posts fall off within minutes, only to be overlooked or missed in the end. That, or they simply don’t post at all, avoiding the boards and the negativity found therein (we’ve received a lot of player feedback via email and at Fan Faires attesting to this phenomenon).

    Well, this is simply not acceptable. Our original goal for the boards was to have them serve as a place where valid player issues would be addressed and not overlooked. We need and want to hear the feedback of anyone willing to send it. You deserve to know that your feedback is being read, that your issues are being considered by the design team, and that the chance of having your thoughts heard is not tied to your ability to be outspoken and persistent in a public forum. That is one thing all of our goals have in common, and one thing that we need to improve such that we are able to give players the input and feedback they deserve – the current “public” process.

    To that end, we’re making a number of changes, both to the message boards and the procedures for sending feedback. First, you’ll notice that the Harpy’s Head and the Official Message Boards have been shut down. This is a temporary measure to allow people to time to read up on the new processes and rules in place prior to posting on official EverQuest forums. Some boards will be reopened in one-to-two weeks time under the new rules.

    The Harpy’s Head boards will reopen under their existing rules (in-character), but will require an active EverQuest account to post. The Wishlist, the Newbie Zone, and the Customer Service/Technical Help forums will also reopen at the same time, but will have very strict guidelines concerning the type of content that is allowed in those forums. Other former forums, including the server and class forums, will not reopen.

    Two new forums, “Developer’s Corner” and “Quality Assurance”, have been added. These forums will take the place of the former “General Discussion” and “Testing” forums. The difference here is that attempting to post a message or reply will take you to a special form where you can “Submit Comments” to the Development and Quality Assurance teams. Representatives from each team will read all of the comments submitted and choose several daily to which to reply publicly. Those representatives will then quote your comments if chosen in individual posts under the appropriate read-only forum. These forums will also be used for special announcements, inquiries, and the release of important information as it is appropriate. And we also hope it will be much easier for players to keep track of what is going on in EverQuest.

    It is very important to us that we are able to address the needs of our customers. It’s not only important from the perspective of our goal of providing good service, but also for our primary goal: To be the number one online gaming company in the industry. Player feedback is so important to this process that we are taking these steps in order to gather feedback from more people more effectively. We are committed to responding not only to more issues, but also to more issues of concern and interest to a broader range of players. Finally, we are committed to giving our playerbase more input into what we do, and more insight into how and why we are doing it.

    These changes are a sincere attempt to make all of that more possible. Please join us in ushering in a new age of communication and collaboration and visit the EverQuest Message Boards to submit your comments today.

    - The EverQuest Team
Obviously this is big news and your free to comment on it on our General Board. I'll have a new poll up later this evening for additional feedback.
