Gordon Infobits
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:59 PM PDT
Gordon stated he had too many espressos last night as he went on an early morning binge posting on the Gameplay Board. As always, here's the scoop:

  • Gordon: What are you doing up this late?
    I made the mistake of indulging in one-too-many espressos when I got home this evening. It has me up a little later than normal :)

  • /Who Question
    No /who listing will ever give level if a character is /anon or /roleplay. You'll have to rely either on what they say or the return from /con.

  • "Why can I not see my fire ball when I cast "Fire Bolt"?"
    We moved missle creation server-side recently. That probably has something to do with the problem. I'll look into it Monday.

  • Rogues Killing Greenies: Playing Nice?
    [Quote from original post: Where do the rogues stand on the play nice issue of killing multiple green mobs to obtain poison components.

    I'm asking because a player complained they were going to report me for killing greens that would have granted them EXP. I didn't kill steal any mobs they attacked but i didn't really leave much for em to kill.]

    Unless there's something more to this to which I am not privy, there's generally no problem with you killing whatever you wish providing that you aren't stealing the kills.


  • Corpse Question
    [Quote from original post: Why in world is there a zone line that leads to instant death and an almost unrecoverable corpse?]

    The corpse is not unrecoverable. You can zone into the "preferred" entrance to the Hole and get a necro to summon your corpse.

    As for why it's like this, that is just how the Hole is designed, and your death is the price of the knowledge that there might be a better way to go about what you are trying. Many things in EverQuest have to be learned, and are likely to cause death until they are. You should enter new areas cautiously until you learn how general rules and physics apply there.

    Falling into an instant death situation is really no different than turning a corner and finding an NPC against which you have no chance given your current group makeup. Things of this nature need to be discovered and learned.

    When the Hole first opened, we did put a short, somewhat vague, message in the patch message, since jumping in the Hole previously teleported people out to Toxxulia. The message was "GMs will not recover corpses for people who jump, fall, or levitate into the Hole".

  • Bracer of Vajeen: Why can't Halflings wear it?
    I'm not sure about this particular item, however if halflings were accidently excluded, we'll see about getting them put back on. I'll have to check into it on Monday. It's possible that they may have been purposely excluded.

  • "Why GM Events Don't Work"
    [Quote from original post: GM events have always involved killing something.]

    Not true. There have been many events that have taken place as stories to be witnessed and participated in. There have also been events where "killing something" has only been a very minor part of the event, rather than the central focus. Of course, we've always had the problem of getting people to refrain from killing the story-tellers, but we've taken steps to prevent that. Read below.

    [Quote from original post: Who are the people in the game who can always kill something regardless of lvl? The lvl 50+s. So when you make a GM event and have most of the Mobs be around lvl 35, the people who are gonna kill them are 50+s.]

    The majority of events deal with actor npcs (npcs played by gms) rather than spawned NPCs (npcs that are still under the control of the servers). We've implemented the ability for the GMs to place a maximum level on who may attack them. That solves the majority of the problems that you cite.

    [Quote from original post: As for risk vs. Reward, these lvl 55s have NO risk killing lvl 30 mobs, and are getting all this ph4+ l3w+.]

    Finally, I should mention that actor npcs hardly ever carry "phat lewt". We abolished that practice some months ago in order to avoid having the reward go whomever could kill the actor first.

  • "Ack! Too many spell book pages!"
    Actually we're implementing a command that will let you flip to a specific page of your spell book.

  • Howling Stones & Dungeons
    When dungeons or new areas are designed, they have to be implemented as if they always existed. As such, it's possible that the quest would "require" some bottom feeding by people who have already out-classed the dungeon where the quest components are located. However, someone who is in the process of building their character could very well happen across the components during normal adventuring.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 7:41 PM PDT
Gordon kept the posts coming today on the Gameplay Board. Here's the coverage of today's posts:

  • "Bring Down the Server and Fix This!"
    [Quote from original post: I can't wait a week, as an enchanter I need to see when my Mez line of spells is resisted I got killed by wizards cuz someone in my group rooted some and I thought they were mezed, I can still mez with flux line of spells and remez but then it drains some cleric mana...]

    I'm sorry, but it wouldn't be appropriate of us to bring down the servers for a text-color issue. We'll get it fixed up next week, and I apologize for the difficulty that you'll have until then.

  • Blacksmithing Broken?
    Hmmm, interesting. I'll forward this to Testing and the designer who's working on this.

  • Risk Vs. Reward
    [Quote from original post: Now, I know Abashi will disagree with my opinion, but this suggests to me that Verant's calculation of risk vs reward is slightly out of whack.]

    How so? Some people prefer the game to be the same (cf. predictable) all of the time, others would get excited at the prospect of something different happening. I think that there are more people of the second type.

    If I find a quote saying:

    "Here's a general hint, when some "role-playing" sort of text scrolls across your screen from somewhere, and you're deep in the dungeon recuperating from a bad fight, heal up quick, because something fun, exciting, and different is about to happen."

    ...would that suggest that our calculation of risk vs reward was in line?

    People have differing ideas of what constitutes risk, and what a reasonable reward for that risk is. This guy had two deaths, so he's upset. Some other might have two deaths and be happy for the reward of even being able to participate, as most haven't had that opportunity.

  • Inconsistant Logic?
    [Quote from original post: Be consistant in your logic and put paladin and sk AC on Kunark armor where it belongs. Half way inbetween cleric and warrior. As it is on quest armor as it is on Plane armor.]

    Our logic is consistant. Warriors have the best armor. Everyone else's armor is lesser. Clerics are a plate wearing class. Their AC cannot be the same as warriors due to the fact that warriors have the best armor, therefore their's is as strong as hybrids.

    I think that your misconception is that "Paly/SK armor is as weak as cleric armor", where in truth, "Cleric armor is as strong as Paly/SK armor". There's a subtle difference in those two statements.

  • Roleplaying
    [Quote from original post: Why must Roleplayers be Anonymous?

    Personally, I think anonymity hinders roleplaying. It forces you to have OOC discussions about your class and level. I (and many others I have met) would use the /roleplay flag to indicate my preference for roleplaying if it did not hide my class and level. I'm not saying that all roleplayers need be non-anon, just let us use the /anon command to choose that separately.]

    Interesting point. I think that the idea comes from the standpoint that roleplayers feel that they should have to introduce themselves to others. I can see however how awkward it might bee for two people to get together and try to decide if they can hunt together without using OOC communication.

    [Quote from original post: /roleplay - turns your name purple, identifies you as a roleplayer
    /anon - hides your class, race and level
    /showguild ON/OFF - hides your guild affiliation]

    I like your suggestions, however were we to implement them, do you think that the request would be followed up with requests to further seperate the hiding of race vs. class vs. location vs. level?

  • Posting Downsides
    It's occurred to be over the past two weeks that there's a big downside to the posting restrictions here on the boards. You can see it when you read most of the threads here on the board, and espicially so when reading the discussion threads thrown up over the past day or two.

    It used to be very easy to find posts in need of a reply. It was easy to go through and toss out post after post on the basis of it being flame-ridden, insulting, or generally obnoxious. Now, it's much more difficult. There's too much worthy content here to comment on :)

    I'm glad to see that the board changes are working out so well, and apologize if your post goes unanswered amongst the plethora of good content here these days. I'd also like to extend thanks to everyone for helping clean these boards up :)


  • Clipping Plane
    [Quote from original post: Brad promised me it would get fixed after Kunark was released. Then later he said "we're working on it" and now months later its still not fixed!]

    Darkmatter,

    What Brad promised is that he understood what the claim was and that we'd look into it. At no point have we agreed that there is a problem. The screenshots that were posted only show that the when the rain turns off, the fog moves out past the max clipping plane for an instant, then back in. This isn't indicative of there being any problem with the way that the plane works. Many zones have clips that are determined by the area. For instance, Toxxulia Forrest has a max clipping plane that is pretty close because we wanted the zone to appear eerie.

    We're still looking into the issue, but it's not the highest of priorities at this time. The most likely "fix" will be for us to make it so that the plane doesn't extend beyond max when the rain turns off. The net result of this fix is basically nothing, which is why it's not a high priority at the moment. We have already confirmed that the min/max clipping plane boundaries are indeed set to the appropriate limits.

  • Item Enscriptions
    [Quote from original post: Is there a way we can inscribe lore items with sayings? like "Happy Birthday?" I mean it would be fun. I know this was brought up before and Gordon realized people would abuse it to use profanity. To prevent this it can force the player's name who scribed it to be at the bottom and a hidden "watermark" that only GMs can activate that tell that players station name and acct.number. This way GMs can issue a scribal warning to the player, 2nd offense means 24 hour suspension, 3rd offense 72 hours, and so on.]

    We like the idea of "markable" items, however EQ's item architecture does not support it. Items are stored in character files as a number that points back to a global entry in the database. The item itself is actually a reference to an object, rather than a copy of the object itself.

    It's part of the core architecture and not something that we can modify in the existing game going forward.

  • Guild Housing
    [Quote from original post: My Guild seems to have this notion that Guild Houses will be implimented in November-ish (possibly Velios?), and will cost about 50k pp.]

    We have no plans to implement any type of purchasable or rentable housing in EQ at this time.

  • Monk Nerf?
    It wasn't a monk nerf, but you are correct in that the fix to the attack speed debuff problem is what caused it. Sometimes fixes have both positive and negative consequences. Attack speed debuffs will always overwrite haste spells now.

  • "Fair is Fair"
    [Quote from original post: empty handed
    last time i looked erudites couldn't be monks. Now I'm swinging away with my enameled blackened mace and when i hit her hahahaha I hit her for 10 if I was lucky. Needless to say she killed me pretty good. My ac is above average for my level 450 aprox can't remember the exact number.]

    In EverQuest, with exception of one character model (spectre), any creature that has a visible weapon will drop that weapon when killed. It's actually loot upon the creature. Since we don't want to load weapons on every NPC capable of wielding them due to the effect on the economy, NPCs that are unarmed will still have a good damage capabilities regardless of their class.

    See the section of the FAQ that talks about NPCs having special abilities.

  • Boss Frog Camped to Death
    Well, given that item creation has to remain constant, the problem remains that people feel that *need* a particular item to be competative. Pre-Kunark, there were frequently warriors on this board claiming that they couldn't get into a group unless they were dual-wielding SSoY's. Given that they feel that they *need* a particular item, they feel that they are then *forced* to camp it until they get it.

    Your example in this case would extend "Camp till I get it" times, upsetting those who decide that they *need* the item and are going to do whatever they have to until they get it. It would, however, throw an "unexpected benefit", however rare, on other frogs in the area.

Debate Update: New Sub Topic
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:12 PM PDT
Gordon started a sub topic for the recent debate he began on the Gameplay Board. Here's the post he made:

    I've noticed that many of the responses to the thread "why camp" still suggest randomizing loot, beyond that which is present in Kunark. Certainly in Kunark, we've been trying contextual randomness (randomness that makes sense in the environment) on good loot, while leaving the best loot on standard "boss" NPCs. We've been fairly happy with the results, as it allows the casual player a good chance to pick up loot that will work for their class without "requiring" them to camp.

    Still, many continue to suggest that we further randomize loot. I'd like to map out an example that shows why this isn't always the best answer. I think that the biggest misconception that I see here in regards to random loot is that the items will be more available. This isn't the case, as we still need to control the rate at which items enter the world. Randomizing loot isn't going to cause this rate to increase.

    Let's say that CoolItem has a 20% chance to drop off of BossFrog in Lower Guk, who spawns 100% of the time on a 20 minute timer. This means that in two hours (six spawns, assuming he were killed instantly after spawning, which is close enough to the case), the frog would have just under a 74% chance of dropping CoolItem at least once. A group choosing to camp BossFrog would have a pretty good chance of getting 1 item in two hours.

    Now, lets say that it was random. We can't increase the overall rate that CoolItem enters the world. So lets say that we give 20 frogs (still on a 20 minute timer) a 1% chance of dropping the item. Anyone care to guess how many of *these* frogs you have to kill to have the same 74% chance of getting the item? 135. Assuming the dungeon was completely camped, and that you could only kill one frog every 20 minutes, it would take 45 hours to have a 74% chance of getting the item. Suppose the dungeon isn't that crowded and you can take a whole room with 4 frogs in it every 20 minutes, it would still take 135 frogs to get that 74% chance, taking over 11 hours. The single-spawn 20% drop frog would have a 67% chance of dropping at least 6 during that time.

    This same logic follows for suggestions that have the creature spawn in any number of random locations. If we have 20 places that BossFrog spawns, on a 1% spawn, and give him a 100% chance of dropping the item, we're still at 135 Frogs to get that 74% chance of him spawning.

    As you can see, the level of randomness that some people are suggesting would create more frustration than people currently cite.

    -Gordon

Camping Discussion
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:44 PM PDT
Gordon started a discussion on camping today's on the Gameplay Board. Here's what he said:

    I had a thought earlier and figured we could have a bit of a discussion about camping. Now before everyone jumps in and says, "because the game forces you to", let's agree that for whatever reason, people decide to camp. Perhaps so many people are camping that newcomers feel that they have to camp, but you still have to make that choice: Am I going to camp or am I going to adventure? Perhaps the game encourages it by rewarding camping. If that is the case, wouldn’t the logical solution be to remove those rewards? More on this later…

    So here's some questions:

    1. Why camp?

    Is it because you feel it is more profitable? Is it more profitable to sit in an overcrowded dungeon fighting over spawns and being on loot lists than moving out into the lesser-used areas of the world? I'm reminded of a recent Kunark review where the reviewer, an avid EverQuest player, indicated that there's more than enough room in Kunark to go off someplace with a group, but he couldn't get his group to do anything but sit in one area and camp a spawn.

    2. Why not spread out to some of the lesser-used areas?

    This question will inevitably bring about discussions of bindpoints, risk, reward, etc, or talk about how loot "sucks" in other areas. However, at the same time it begs the question, why do people choose to do something that they find boring and without challenge. Again I ask, is loot in any unused area really worse than what you'll get by camping a crowded dungeon, fighting over spawns, and being on a list? Certainly risk is less, but risk is essentially a small amount of experience in a well-balanced group. Do you feel that there is more net gain in the crowded dungeons? Can two groups taking turns killing 3 critters that spawn every 20 minutes in a room really be more profitable than going off someplace on your own, even given increased risk?

    3. Do you actually enjoy camping?

    There are people who do, and I'd like to get an impression of how many people honestly prefer camping. We've made several changes in the past few months to make camping less profitable, leading to a slew of complaints from this group, so I know that they are out there.

    4. What do you propose should be done?

    Some say that random loot tables are the only way to stop camping, but people don't just camp loot, they camp experience as well. We've all seen people move through the game staking out solitary 6 minute spawns for the purpose of camping experience.

    Keep in mind that anything you suggest has to scale to 2K people online per server, all trying to do the same quest.

    ---opinion---
    My feeling is that people camp because they feel it is more profitable, whatever "profitable" means to them. I personally consider "having fun" to be "profitable", to an extent greater than getting experience or loot. I won't go in certain dungeons because they are camped. I enjoy places where you zone in and you might be the only group there. Other people however often value loot and experience over fun, or believe that they can only have fun by acquiring loot or experience. Some people feel that they can only go have fun once they've acquired all of the loot and experience that they'll ever need.

    I really don't think that random loot is an option for loot-camping, as people will still pick a spawn to camp and kill there until they get the item. Certainly, it won't lead to everyone camping the same NPC, but will still result in all of the NPCs being camped. I feel that as long as there exists the relative assurance that "If I spend enough time in this one area, I will sooner or later get the item".

    As far as experience camping goes, I feel we can only get rid of it if we make it less profitable than other things people could do. Perhaps if we could programmatically detect XP camping, we could put something in to counter it. We've been adjusting spawn times that were set incorrectly on some NPCs in the world, causing them to spawn too rapidly, leading to it being a profitable camp, but what else do you think that we should do?
    ---opinion---

    My last question is:

    5. If we do whatever is necessary to get rid of camping, by making it less profitable, how many people will that *really* upset?

    People camp because it's easier, faster, safer, and more profitable to do so than to move through areas and do something more exciting. People dislike camping because it's boring (without challenge), yet this situation is created by it being easier, faster, safer, and more profitable. Completely randomizing loot will only make more NPCs worthy of camping, not reduce the amount of camping going on.

    Logic suggests that if people camp because it’s easier, faster, safer, and more profitable, the way to stop camping would be to make it harder, slower, more risky, and less profitable. Yet, any time we've done that with an aspect of the game, it creates a *large* number of upset people here on the boards. If we make something harder, we're trying to eliminate soloing. If we make it slower, we're trying to hurt the casual gamer. If we make it more risky, we're trying to make you die more, which is supposed to make you play longer and pay us more. If we make it less profitable, we're trying to force everyone to join uberguilds.

    In all honesty, any time we’ve made something harder, slower, more risky, and less profitable, we’ve been trying to motivate people to play the game in a manner that we would consider more fun. Should we stop trying to do that? If yes, should we stop trying to address camping?

    -Gordon
