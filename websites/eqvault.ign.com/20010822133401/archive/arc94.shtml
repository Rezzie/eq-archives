Gnome Race Report - Tarew Marr
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:42 PM PDT
The race is over and here are the results and report from Avalan:

All was calm in Lesser Faydark Saturday when a cry shattered the peaceful afternoon, "The Gnomes are coming, The Gnomes are coming!" as nearly 30 gnomes burst forth from the Steamfont zone, feet beating the ground as fast as their little legs could push them. Battles stalled, women screamed and Beasts of all kinds licked their chops as the small horde of Gnaked Gnome Racers streamed by. And so it went from zone to zone, continent to continent as the dwindling number of gnomes strove to reach the gates of Halas and prove that such a quest could be done. One by one and in groups the brave gnomes fell to beasts, raiders and orc, in the Commons, the Karanas and Everfrost as they struggled to reach their goal.

Just as race officials were beginning to think that none would survive the ordeal, shouts of "Run Gnome Run!" could be heard echoing through the mountains of Everfrost. Of the 28 racers that started the race, only 7 made it to the Everfrost zone. Exactly 55 minutes after the start of the Antonica leg of the race, the first weary gnome staggered up the ramp to the Halas zone and finished. 22 minutes later the last of the 5 finishers tagged the race official.

/ooc This event, sponsored by the Starrwarriors Guild of Tarew Marr had 3 parts. A Sprint from AK'Anon to the end of the docks in Butcher, The main race from ship docking in Freeport to the Halas ramp in Everfrost, and a Last Man Standing Brawl at the end of the race. Winners could select from cash awards and special items for their prizes

Winners of the Sprint:

3rd - GGRacer (Zenar)

Winners of the Race:

GGRevealer (Villad) and GGNefertiti (Pyroth) also finished the race and were rewarded for their efforts.

We do not have their names because a representative of their guild collected their prizes, but I would like to give a special tribute to the top three finishers of the race as all were members of the Alliance of Amicus. They have done their guildmates proud.

Congratulations all and thanks to all racers, the citizens of Norrath for cheering/cajoling these racers on and to my guildmates for helping make this, our first running of the Gnaked Gnomes, such a successful event.

Gordon Infobits, Part 2
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:17 PM PDT
More posts have been made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn today. Here's round two of today's Gordon Infobits:

  • Server Downtime
    We announced several weeks ago that 1 or 2 servers would be coming down for about 4 hours per day for the purpose of performing several upgrades needed for Kunark. Our apologies to those who find this unpalatable, but it has to be done.

  • Religion Matters
    [Quote from original post: My enchanter started out in the beginning as a worshipper of Erolissi Marr. Another person that I was traveling with at the time, a wizard, started out worshipping Solusek Ro. Around the same time (level 24 I believe), both of our religions were changed to Tunare. We petitioned then and the guide told us they'd log it as a bug, but that there was nothing they could do.]

    There was a bug a while back that would cause the deity name to change spontaneously, but this was just a display bug. It didn't change what deity you actually have selected, because that is based upon a faction that is created at character creation. As for the Deity that is displayed, the GMs were correct in that they do not have the ability to change that.

    In other words, should a deity-specific item be implemented, it will take into account your original deity, not the one that is displayed now were you affected by that bug.

  • Kerra Items & Erudites
    [Quote from original post: Ok Abashi, let me see if I've got this right. Kerra Isle has been re-done. GREAT!!! New Items were added that you had to quest for. GREAT!!! Some items are NODROP to prevent farming. GREAT!!

    All Erudites are hated upon entering. GRE.... HUH?!?!?! This makes absolutly no sense.]

    Sure it does. The Erudites are not known for their kindness, especially to the lesser races. With Kerrans being a tad "lesser" than most, it's not too big of a stretch to imagine that they really don't like the Erudites.

    [Quote from original post: Some of these items are really nice and useful, but being a Erudite means I can never have them.]

    Don't give up so soon. There are ways :)

  • Druid Issues
    [Quote from original post: Why have druids not gotten any class specific armor quests as of yet, and will there be in the future?]

    As to "why there isn't any", the answer, as simple as I can make it is the same reason that there aren't quests in other areas that there probably should be: We haven't put them in. Yes, it is entierly possible that we will put in druid class-specific armor at some time, in the same fashion that it is possible that we'll put in class-specific quests for gnome necros, or dark elf clerics.

    The point I am trying to make is that just because something isn't in at present doesn't mean that we have no intentions of putting it in, just that we haven't put it in *yet*. EQ is an evolving game. New quests go in in nearly every patch. I do imagine however that druid-specific armor hasn't received much priority at this point due to the fact that the of adventured-armor available for them at that level is probably superior to class-specific armor we might put in.

    Please trust that we have every interest in providing you with the content that will keep you interested and playing. We don't like hearing about people quitting because they feel that they've run out of things to do.

    [Quote from original post: Why cant player bought houses be implamented?]

    At a certain point in the development process the team has to start making decisions in regards to what features will be in the game. This lets them put some ideas to rest while allowing them to spend more time and devote more effort to things that are approved. Back in beta, it was decided that player-owned structures would not be a part of EverQuest, much like horses.

  • Annoying Footstep Sound Bug
    The footstep/sound issues should have been fixed in today's patch. Sorry for the problem.

  • Item Crash Bug
    Those item-based crash bugs that we implemented with the patch yesterday have been fixed in the patch today.

    In your particular case, it was the totem that was causing you to crash.

Special Player View: The Rights of Guides
[73 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:59 AM PDT
A very unusual situation took place on the Lanys T'Vyl server recently. Five guides were stripped of their rank and status and had all of their EQ accounts, guide and regular, banned. The first report I've decided to post was sent by an anonymous resident of Lanys V'Tyl:

Ozzadyen, Calais, Desmo, Valdul, and Tygira--five Guides--were apparently stripped of their authority and fully banned from Everquest for participating in PVP action with regular players at the Lake Rathetear Arena on Thursday night of Lany's Tvl.

This experience, prior to the banishment of the Guides, showed to us that Guides were players like us, that they cared about us, and that they like to have as much fun as us!

Whatever rule the Guides broke by battling players in the Arena does not justify the punishment handed down by Verant! What harm was done? Are Guides never to interact with players outside of either Customer Service Issues or Verant-sanctioned events? What about weddings? In many cased, GMs or Guides preside over or conduct the ceremonies. Will this now be cause for banishment?

All people on Lanys are sad to have lost 5 guides. We want them back!

The second is a letter that has been written to the public by former Senior Guide Ozzadyen. Here's a copy of it:
I was once known as Ozzadyen a Senior Guide of Povar for a long time and Lanys T`Vyl for a very short time. I devoted almost a year of my time to helping others and giving them some enjoyment. I was released from the program for Improper behavior for going to the Arena and having fun with players on Lanys T`Vyl.

Now that I am no longer a part of the Everquest Guide program I can freely speak and I have every intention of doing so. First off Verant was on a Witch hunt to get me removed from the program anyway, because I was very unhappy with what had happened during the Rending of the Veil Part 4 on Xegony and let them know this. It was brought to my attention that there were 2 Councils on Xegony Councils of Light and Councils of Dark. On each of these councils were 5 players for a total of ten players. The Heads of these two councils were guides. At the end of the Veil 4 Quest a member on the Council of Light Allora was awarded the Title and Veeshan Claw. I found this very disturbing and wrong they have 10 Elite players on Xegony now and 1 was awarded a Title and an Artifact know as the Chyrstalline Claw of Veeshan.

Now while for the most part the Guide program is not to blame I will miss everyone there I worked with and knew for the last year. Go easy on those Guides, Apprentices and Seniors they are only doing what Verant directs them if they Deviate at all Like I did last night this is what happens to them. I have absolutely no respect for anyone at all in Verants upper Echelon, they rule with an Iron fist and use those in the Guide program without ever thanking them. Should something go wrong they immediately blame this huge Volunteer force they have. These Volunteer Players provide 85% or better of Verants Customer Service on a daily ongoing basis. I was told I was a disgrace I found this very amusing coming from the Upper Echelon of Verant GM staff. There are some GM's that are genuinely great and care about the players, but they are Temporary staff so even they live under the threat of a Hatchet day in and day out if they do not do what they are told.

But we learn, and we go on, perhaps you will hear more from me. There were others with me this night who helped provide great entertainment for several players not just 10 Elite ones like on Xegony. To Calais the Gnome Kicker, Tygira is Stark Raving Mad, Valdul, and Desmo, all these Guides, Verant immediately removed from the program, and Suspended their accounts. To them I give my deepest apologizes. I was the Senior Guide, and had I not gone to the Arena none of the others would have followed because of me they lost there rights and access to there playing accounts I can in no way tell them how sorry I am and repay them for that. They were the best damn people you would have ever wanted to meet. Every last guide I had the pleasure of meeting and working with on Povar and Lanys T`Vyl in the last year were the greatest. The Senior staff and every last soul in the Guide program devote endless hours of devotion and time to help make EQ what it is today and they do this with Thanks from the players and not a single thank you from Verant. So ends the tail of Ozzadyen Servant to Lanys once Ozzadyen Servant to Povar, those always were my titles and I always lived up to them.

Now another thing that really gets to me is players are given warnings. As a guide in the program you are given no such Luxury not only was I immediately suspended but every account I had has been suspended, all of them, not just my guide account but every account I have. They devote hours of their time each week to do service to the community. Good ones are hard to come by, and harder to keep. Certainly a program as important and vital to EverQuest must have rules, but because of one insignificant and pointless incident good guides can be banned instantly and without a second thought?

Gordon Infobits, Part 2
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:15 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn has made even more posts on the Gameplay Board today. Here's round two of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Quivers and the Ammo Slot
    We agree that quivers *should* be equipable in the ranged ammo slot. Unfortunately, due to the way that we store data on the character, an insurmountable techical issue made this impossible given our core architecture. With the way that we store data on the character, a full p-wipe would be required to change this, and as such the prospects of this happening have been ruled out.

  • Level 50 Experience
    [Quote from original post: I was just wondering what will happen to people that have a full experience bar in 50, will they automatically become 51 when they launch RoK or will the exp bar be reset?]

    We won't be subtracting any experience points from anyone. The only *possible* thing that could happen is that we make an adjustement to the experience curve for level 50 prior to the release of Kunark which might make it appear that you have less experience. It is very unlikely at this point that we will do that.

    We expect that when Kunark goes live that there will be many people who are but a kill or two away from level 51.

  • Will we ever see full screen interface windows for high resolutions?
    Though I'll avoid saying that this is outright impossible, it is highly unlikely at this time. The design of EQ's interface uses custom bitmaps that have to be produced for each supported resolution. We don't want to do something cheesy like stretching these images out, as it will not only look bad but cause some technical problems.

  • Problem with Graphics: Missing Shields
    We've had a few reports of this come in from other sources now, and our testing team is taking a look.

    Doh! It's right on the list that GZ gave me, I apparently wrote it down wrong. I'll see about getting it updated after we wait a little while to see if I missed anything :)

  • I cancelled my account, are my characters still there?
    They are probably still there. To my knowledge we haven't yet had to clean up old accounts. That's not to say that we won't do it some day (I believe that we are only required to hold them for 90 days), but drive space hasn't become an issue at this point.

  • Changing the Terror Forged Mask
    The same day that the terror mask was first found, we announced that the spell effect on it was a mistake and that it would be changed back. In addition, the NPC that hands it out had his quest dialogue changed to tell people acquiring it that the mask would soon lose its current spell effect and gain the power of fear.

    Though it is true that we try to keep existing items as is, there are cases where an item is so severely overpowered that it must be changed in order to maintain the integrity of the game. This was such an example.

    All of that said, I'm sorry that you are upset. It is next to impossible for us to insure that every player is made aware in advance for every issue in the game. We rely heavily on the news sites to help spread the word when something interesting happens.

    I'm chatting with the team about these issues now. Of the people that have noticed a problem with footsteps getting louder, what sound card(s)? Also, is it your footsteps, or others footsteps?

    The spell hierarchy compares all of the effects of the spell to determine the cases where one spell overwrites another. In this particular case, it sounds like the duration of Skin Like Steel is causing it to overwrite Bravery.

    That said, it's not unusual to have to educate other classes that you group with on the capabilities of your class. My character, a necromancer, is sure to remind druids to avoid snare and other casters to refrain from root. About Screenshot Submissions
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 1:39 PM PDT
Just a reminder to everyone, we do not accept screenshots that are in BMP format. This is because of the huge amount of time it takes to download them and convert them. The BMP must be converted into either a GIF or JPEG. There are several shareware/freware image converters on the net, one such example is LView Pro.

Also, I know this will come as a big surprise to everyone that's tried to submit a screenshot in the last 4 months, but eqshots@vaultnetwork.com is officially back online. :) From now on please direct all screenshot of the day submissions there. Event screenshots that are accompanying a news report should continue to be sent to eqnews@vaultnetwork.com.

Thanks!


Funeral on Veeshan
[38 Comments] - Aurum @ 10:49 AM PDT
Brimstone sent me the following:

Tibbit of LOA reported the following event from Veeshan. I thought that theplayers of our community might also like to hear ofthis:
Normally, I don't get too emotional over stuff in Norrath,but this was an exception.

I was minding my own business late last night soloing some birdies in LakeRathe when I came upon a small guild holding a funeral for one of theirguildmates. (It was located on the island with the bonfire.) I wish I couldremember the guild and the name of the fallen.

From what I could discern (I dared not interrupt), the guildmate was killed inRL in a horrible car/plane accident. Since it would have been supremely hardfor them to all attend a RL memorial, they held it in Norrath. His corpse wasthere (Human paladin, full plate), and the guild (45-50 people) was assembledin a circle. Prayers were read, memories were reflected on, and in general itwas really touching.

But what I thought was the most emotional was a silent 21 firebolt salute from7 of the guilds wizards. they all stood atop a rocky crag, and simultaneouslylet loose with three 7-bolt volleys across the zone of Lake Rathe. They wereall sent in the same direction, and all 21 bolts were visible for about 5seconds.

A guide/gm was then summoned, who placed the corpse in the fire and thenremoved the character. I guess it was about as close to cremation or a funeralpyre as norrath can get.

It was quite a sight to see, and I'd be lying if I said I didn't feel a pangof emotion throughout the whole event.

=Tibbit=

Submitted by Brimstone of Akalabeth
