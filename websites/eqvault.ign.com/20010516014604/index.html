Tournament/Trade Fair on Rodcet Nife
- Aurum @ 5:03 PM PDT
Draco Mortis sent word of a new event on the Rodcet Nife server::

Noradvo is hosting a open Guild Event and Tournament/Trade Fair
Rodcet Nife server Friday May 18th, 8pm to 11pm est. in The Arena (entrance
in Lake Rathe). Anyone that would like to join us for some friendly PVP fun
is welcome to attend.

Events will include:
* PVP grudge matches - Who would you like to take a few shots at ?.
* Team PVP - we will split all participants into equal teams. (2, 3, 4
teams)
* Falling ladder tournament - We start with the two highest level
Adventurers dueling and the winner stays in the ring. The next contestant
immediately challenges until last man is standing.
* Free-for-all - mayhem at its finest
* Tactics battles - Tanks vs casters, Lots of Lowbies versus single
Uber char, etc.
* Anything else we can dream up

Yes you will DIE, perhaps several times over. Your friends will kill you,
repeatedly. And they will enjoy it. They will laugh and jeer (all in good
fun) but there is a bright side. You will get to slaughter them in return.
Everyone can bind in the Arena itself outside of the PVP area.

For those that get there early we will also be having a Trade Fair those
with equipment sitting in the bank that they don't need. Or for those trade
skill artisans that have unique wares to sell. Bakers and Brewers are
encouraged to come and sell some delicacies to our tournament contestants
and spectators. So get there early to get some good deals.

Sorry but the plans for the official "I went to the Arena and all I got was
this lousy Bronze T-shirt" were shot down.

Noradvo is the official guild for the Norrath Adventurers Order. We consist
of a group of friendly adventurous players that are after some fun and
camaraderie. We consist of a large number of twinks and semi-retired players
wanting to enjoy some time away form the pressures of Uber Guilds, camping
and over-grinding. If you are interested in a low-pressure fun-seeking guild
you are welcome to apply.

Our web page is at www.noradvo.com or you can send an email to
Draco@Noradvo.com.

-=Draco Mortis=-
52 Channeler
Guild Leader - Noradvo
Thanks!

Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 9:07 PM PDT
Here are the latest information given by Alan from off of the Developer's Corner:

    Flowing Thoughts
    Let me start here with an apology. Someone pointed out that I was ranting a bit with my last post, and I was. I shouldn't be doing that. I apologize.

    Aarreran-CrimRen wrote:

    [Quote: Even though I agree with you that Flowing Thought isn't a bad thing, I though I'd point out that you were dead wrong when you said...

    "It only appears on items found in the Plane of Hate and are no drop. So you won't find anyone under 46th level with an item like this."

    This is 100% incorrect. An item was found with Flowing Thought I on a Giant in Great Divide who was apart of the ring quest. Not only was an item with Flowing Thought I found on him, but an item with Flowing Thought II was found as well.]

    Yup, it seems to be the case that there are a few of the super high powered Flowing Thought II items available as well…

    Flowing Thought II is, well, a little better than Flowing Thought. It is still far less useful than Clarity (though it might rival Breeze…), and it still stacks with all of those spells, so it's still not much of an issue. I would agree that an item better than that might be worthy of some concern, even if it does stack with Clarity.

    And, as one reader has pointed out, there are in fact a few Clarity items in the game.

    There are perhaps three items in the game that have the ability to cast Clarity II on their wielder (and only on the wielder). When I say three, I mean three total, across all the servers. These are, in fact, artifacts given out at two of the gatherings. As far as I know, no more of these items will ever come into the game (though I suppose they might, it's not part of any current plan, that's for sure).

    Were these items dropping off any NPC in the game, no matter how rare, I would be concerned. But having three of them on all the servers should have absolutely no effect on the desire for enchanters or bards.

    This one is kinda long, so I'm going to respond within the post. My comments are not bolded.

    [Quote: Greetings Absor,

    I was reading over the post you made regarding wizards and I had a couple comments. Forgive me but I dont know the codes for this board.

    Absor said: "Wizards can't sustain their damage output in fights that last longer than those that existed before Velious. This is the issue you're heading at. And, sure, giving Wizards a larger mana pool or a form of massive mana regeneration would fix it. But it also makes them more efficient in all other forms of casting. "

    Mostly true. The wizard community feels it doesn't do enough damage, which is our primary focus. You mention that giving us 'massive' mana regeneration would too greatly benefit our other casting abilities and you would have to compensate.

    Raising mana cost of non-dmg spells seems reasonable given what a wizard is, dmg caster, but mana regeneration may not entirely be the solution. At least in my opinion it isn't because of other problems wizards experience: ie agro. I dont have a useable skill\spell to lower my agro so typical raid fights entail casting and waiting for melees to rebuild up agro for me to cast again. THIS is why mana regeneration isnt entirely the solution for wizards. It may help, but in raid scenarios wont solve our problems.

    To put it simply, wizards have 2 problems:
    1) Low dmg
    2) High agro doing that dmg. ]

    Ok, I don't agree that wizards have problem #1. Low damage needs to be qualified with a 'compared to', in other words 'Low damage when compared with what a rogue can do over the course of ten minutes of continuous fighting' or 'Low damage when compared to what any other class can do when dumping mana to finish something off'. I'd say yes to one and no to the other. The problem is that when you make comparisons like that it's all in the wording.

    As for #2, well, no more aggro than anyone else that does the same damage in the same time frame.

    [Quote: Mana regen solves 1, but makes 2 worse. ]

    If wizards could suddenly regenerate mana at ten times the rate they do now, they could control their aggro, and in fact aggro control would be the only limit on their damage potential.

    [Quote: Absor said: "Not true. Casters are just as AC and HP dependent as anyone else. It's just that their hit points come in the form of melee classes and pets, in much the same way that melee characters draw their hit points from healers and their speed from casters."

    You should stress this more as it is the crux for grouping in EQ. Unfortunately, if any two of those become too strong the third isn't necessary. Velious fights last minutes and in that duration melee is the most effective damage source. In that context, healer-melee is the only dynamic that matters. Casters just are added to fill in the gaps if necessary. ]

    Agreed. And this is the place where the imbalance exists. But people too easily forget that when you are comparing melee damage to caster damage, you have to keep in mind that there are two people doing that melee damage, the melee and the healer. Take away the healer and your melee isn't going to live long enough to outdamage the caster in question.

    But it is this situation where the imbalance exists, and it is these situations that we are aiming to fix.

    [Quote: Absor said: "Heck, wizards have a great damage over time ratio and this isn't even something that we're looking at improving." ]

    This, to me, doesn't make much sense. You mentioned above it was something being considered as a result of Wizard Talk. So how can we have 'great damage over time ratio'? Maybe explaining your qualifications for this would make it clear because in nearly all raiding fights melees do more than me.

    Perhaps we're thinking of different uses of the term 'damage over time'. A lot of the responses sent in were telling me that a melee character does a lot more damage over an hour than a wizard. That's probably true at the upper levels. But what about over the course of one minute? If an NPC is fleeing, who finishes it off? I was thinking of damage over time as it relates to most fights, not the rare long fight one can encounter in Velious, since I've covered that separately.

    [Quote: Absor said RE dmg/mana: "Bane spells. Other than that, I don't know."

    I am not sure if you know this Absor, maybe ask Poe about it for further detail, but banes have a very very select group of creatures it can hit. About 10 in game roughly I would guess can be hit by both banes. Being effective on about 10 mobs doesn't make us more attractive. Just means you CoH in the wizards when you are getting ready for the final boss NPC. You also have to be level 60 to use them. ]

    I know exactly how the bane spells work. That 'select group of creatures' are exactly the creatures that take a long time to kill. They are the creatures where the wizard has the greatest problems, and is the exact group of combats that we wanted to target.

    [Quote: Absor said: "Isn't now, hasn't been in the past, probably won't be in the future. Just because you are the 'best' at travel does not mean that travel is what you do best."

    Cool.

    :)

    -Alch ]

    Ok, I've received a lot of comments that were much less… polite that Alch's comment. I'm not going to repost them here. But I'll address their concerns.

    Too frequently folks get caught up in their current concern and don't step back to look at the big picture (I hate the phrase, someone get me a new one…). If we do something that allows Wizards to crank out the damage on high hit point NPCs in any way other than bane spells, then the life expectancy of old world NPCs would be nil. Improve the ratios of damage for Wizard spells to make them effective in the places where they feel weak now, and they destroy the old world with but a thought.

    And I've noted that every (and I mean EVERY) person that has sent in some numbers showing how their wizard can't do the damage of a melee character sends in data comparing them to a rogue that has Ragebringer and is constantly backstabbing? Is that a good comparison? Keep in mind that the rogue is a three person damage dealer. He needs a tank and someone to heal that tank to do his damage. It might be fairer to add the damage dealt by all three of those people in a fight and divide by three, then compare…

    The problem is that we're comparing melee and spell damage, and that's not a good comparison. The two are very different.

    Group desirability is the other thing that is most mentioned about Wizards. They say they aren't. I argue that if there were no complaints about your ability to deal damage, then there would be no problem getting groups… But we also have plans that will dramatically reduce the problem with getting groups at higher levels. I can't tell you about it until we get it working and until we're done with the balancing stuff, but it will make a wizard desirable to groups.

    I hate to say this, but it might be possible that part of the reason that a wizard might not be getting invited to groups is that you've spent so much time telling everyone how you aren't useful… It's hard to tell the sequence of events here, it's a chicken and the egg thing. But I often worry that this might be a self-fulfilling prophecy. Tell enough people that you're not useful and they will believe you.

    This may not be true, but I worry about it nonetheless.

    I received and e-mail from a friend this morning, saying many of the things that many of you have said. He said something though, that struck a cord with me. He mentioned something about Wizards in literature and how they are something to be feared.

    They sure are. But, well, Gandalf isn't a balanced character. He can't exist in Norrath because then there would be a majority of these literature-based character class and few of the others. Tolkien never needed to balance gameplay with Mithrandir in mind. We do.

    I know that what I've been saying sounds like I don't understand your point of view. But my concern here is that you don't see mine. There is more to balancing a class than just turning up the damage volume until everyone that plays that class is happy. It's not that easy.

    Keep sending in your comments, we'll read them all. But for now I'm not going to make more comments on balance issues until we're done with our run through of all the classes.

    Special Food Items
    Nynfolyn wrote:

    [Quote: I may be mistaken, but so far I have been unable to eat any food by right-clicking on it if the food is in a bag. If its in my main inventory there is no problem. Is this intentional, a bug, or my mistake? ]

    You can't right-click anything that is in a bag. The idea is that something needs to be handy to use it, and you it isn't on hand if it's in the bag.

    Luclin Content
    [Quote: When will you be adding content to the Luclin site? And when you do, will you post a message here? ]

    Yes and yes.

    Right now we're gearing up for E3. After that we hope to have an update for the site, but exactly when I'm not sure. But I'll definitely let you guys know when we do.

    Faction Hits
    Alavigne wrote:

    [Quote: I have a small question RE: faction hits.

    If I'm a high elf & kill a Dark elf in his home hunting grounds I get bad faction with Dark Elfs.

    If I am a high elf & kill a dark elf in my home hunting grounds do I get any positive faction hits? ]

    Nope. The only faction hits for PvP on those servers will be killing a race in one of its home towns. No positive faction will be gained from PvP combat.

    Unfortunately, giving anything positive at all for PvP combat tends to be abusable, since someone could simply have a friend create a character to kill to gain whatever benefit is given.

    Travelers Boots
    Saeul wrote:

    [Quote: Alan:

    You need to acknowledge the level of desparation that people feel regarding the Journeyman Boots and the Traveler's Boots. People have been frustrated beyond words at the additional barriers and frustration put in their way. You need to acknowledge it and explain WHY it's being done, not just say 'that is the way it is'.

    It is NOT the way it is, it is the way Verant has DESIGNED it to be. Explain why. An explanation could go like this:
    *beginning of one suggested dialog*
    Folks I wanted to take a moment to have a short dialog with you on the Journeyman Boots/Traveler's Boots situation. We recognize that this one quest has continued to be a sore spot and we are taking an action that we hope will improve the situation.

    On the one hand, it isn't fair to everyone who spent time and effort getting Journeyman Boots have their accomplishment trivialized. On the other hand, just money isn't, by itself, a sufficient 'effort' to replace the JBoots quest. So we made two decisions that you now see:

    1. You must have faction to buy the boots. This affects those who hunted for Melatite, Charred Guardian Shields, etc. If you perceive your faction is SO BAD that you'll never qualify to buy the Traveler's Boots, then you are no worse off than you were--you can still do the Journeyman Boots quest.

    2. You do not get as good of an item. The Traveler's Boots are somewhat inferior to the Journeyman Boots. This was intentional and is done to avoid the problem of trivializing other people's efforts.

    In closing, I hope you see that we do care about our playerbase and we are trying to make the game more accessible without making it 'easy'. That is a difficult trade off and we're just people making tough decisions about how those trade offs work. Please allow us the latitude to come up with these ideas and try them out.

    Trust us that we are trying really hard to keep the big picture in sight.

    *end of suggested dialog*

    Clearly this kind of discussion COULD cause more problems than it solves, but with your control of the message board, I doubt you'll actually find that negative of a response. ]

    What he said…

    Actually, that's pretty close to the truth.

    Traveler's Boots are, however more than somewhat inferior to Journeyman's Boots. Because of the casting time they can not be used while moving. Because of that they won't be very useful as tools for kiting, and they will, primarily, be useful for travelling.

    But most of what you say is true. I guess the problem here is that I thought those points were rather obvious (as they seem to be to you), so I didn't specify them very clearly.

    And I don't think I ever said that they are as they are just because they are that way. I thought I was pretty clear that we put them there on purpose, not that they fell there by random chance. I'm not sure how you got the impression that I was even suggesting that they were placed in game by fate or some other such thing. No, we put them there and we did it on purpose. We thought out the ramifications and are content with them.

    I'm sorry if I sounded equivocal about that, because we certainly are not.

    [Quote: Two more points:

    3. Traveler's Boots aren't as desirable as Journeyman's Boots, since they have a casting time

    4. For the price of Traveler's Boots, I could buy a *lot* of SoW potions. Suddenly the price of a SoW potion seems pretty reasonable to me...

    -Azlina Crafte [Ebony Order]

    The Nameless ]

    Thanks.

    We understand the concern that some people have over the possibility of harm to the salability of Blood of Wolf potions. We don’t think that this will be a problem, we think that the sales of these potions might drop a little, but that they will still be valuable and salable. The truth is that nobody knows what will happen until it happens. So let's see how it goes.


Important News Concerning Next Patch
- Aurum @ 8:49 PM PDT
Alan announced some very important news about the upcoming patch on the Developer's Corner today:

    Our next patch is planned for May 30th, probably some time in the wee morning hours here on the West Coast.
    The important part of the message is not that, though. It's this:

    Due to some of the things we are going to be doing, there is a small chance that some corpses might disappear during the patch. GMs will have the ability to verify any corpses that are lost. That means that they can help you get them back if you do indeed lose one. It also means that I should remind everyone that attempting to scam a GM about a lost corpse could mean banning.

    So please make certain that you have as many of your corpses cleaned up as possible before the next patch. We're not expecting a lot of corpses to disappear, but the fewer lying around, the less chance of them disappearing.

    Thanks.

    - The EverQuest Team

Roleplaying Event on Xegony
- Aurum @ 9:11 PM PDT
Sarmikgar sent me this announcement:

As you stroll steadily, approaching the Shady Swashbuckler, a large colorful
scroll hanging on the far wall in front of you catches your eye.
Disregarding the appropriately named "Shady" merchant, you continue forward,

GM Event Report - Ayonae Ro
- Aurum @ 5:46 PM PDT
Darksome and Kryonis informed us of these events:

Last night in the castle of Mistmoore, 55 Half-Elf Ranger Darksome
Greyshadow and 55 Human Monk Kryonis Sougai were about to head out after a
brief argument with the cooks in the kitchen, but were met by a beautiful
Dark Elf lady named Khasra Vei`Ras. My trusty companion Kryonis greeted
her, and in difference to most other NPCs in this area, she actually spoke
back. We were quite surprised and a conversation started.

She welcomed us and informed us that she was the "keeper" of the castle
while her "master" was out. And that she meant us no harm, for if she did
we would be dead by now. Her reason for seeking us out, she said, was that
she was wondering if we knew anything about the chosen one (Firiona) and
why she was spotted in Lesser Faydark, and if these stories was indeed
true. It came around to one of us mentioning a unicorn and the story
progressed further.. After we were done talking she left and ordered a
messanger to be sent to the "master". We went out to look for clues and the
messenger but sadly could not find anything. Eventually we ended up in
Greater Faydark where she walking along the path close to Felwhite. We
watched her for a minute before she spotted one of us and another
conversation started. To sum it up, appearently she was once a high elf and
known as a "moon beast". The high priest ordered her to be slain due to her
being a moon beast. Her mother, scared for the Khasra's life took Khasra to
Lord Mayong Mistmoore to be "saved". However, she was not really saved, but
only imprisoned in death for eternity. She hates her foolish mother and
seems to want to be a high elf again. She also respects the high elves even
though they would kill her if her whereabouts be known. She feels she is
obligated to Lord Mistmoore and does what he asks. At the end of this
conversation she made us promise not to tell anyone (of course this was all
ingame). Darksome later received a "tell" from her stating that she was
once again safe within her lord's domains.

The important part of this event must be the messenger sent to her master,
to inform him of Firiona Vie's return and the possibility of the portal
opening for Tunare.
