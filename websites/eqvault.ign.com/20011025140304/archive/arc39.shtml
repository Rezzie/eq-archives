Sumarization of Current Lure Situation
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 7:19 PM PST
Alan summed up the situation with Lure spells in the following message he posted on the Gameplay Board:

    I just wanted to summarize what is happening and what has happened with Lure spells (hopefully I understand it).

    Three weeks ago, the resist rates for NPCs in regards to Lure was fixed. This means, basically, that they will resist Lures that they are supposed to resists (a fire creature might now resist a fire-based Lure that it did not resist before). This change really shouldn't have affected most wizards, once they realized the change and adjusted tactics accordingly. It is my fault for not informing you of the change. For that I'm sorry.

    However, all the talk about changes to this line of spells brought about a more in-depth look at how they work, leading to this post by one of our programmers (given in full here for your enjoyment):

    "After looking closely at the way saves are done when the target is higher level than the caster, it appears that Lures would get saving throws in situations where they should not. I'm fixing it now, it should go on test shortly.

    So to make everyone happy, the target of a lure spell will NOT get a saving throw addition if it's higher level than the caster. I thought this already was the case, it was not.

    Currently on live some NPCs are getting saves versus lures that they should not be getting."

    This change, however, will not take effect until we patch next. With the holidays on us, it's hard to tell when that will be.

    So, over all, Lure should actually become a more powerful line of spells, for those that are fully aware of their proper use…

    Alan

Patch Message: 12-19-00
[24 Comments] - Aurum @ 11:20 AM PST
Along with Patch Day comes a new Patch Message and today is no exception:

    December 19th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Patch Day*

    Today's patch primarily consists of tuning of Velious zones as well as content and other additions. In addition, the following changes have been implemented on the Live servers:

    - High-end items that have a activatable (right-click) healing effect have in several cases had their casting times increased.
    - Factions for some of the higher end encounters in Velious have received some adjustments. Basically, "bad acts" receive more weight than "good acts". For instance, if "A" hates "B" and "B" hates "A", killing "A" will hurt your faction with "A" more than it will help your faction with "B".
    - The stamina reduction with "Resurrection Effects" has been replaced by a slight attack-speed debuff.
    - The Ranger's Trueshot discipline now works correctly. It was previously only working versus other player characters.
    - The Shadow Knight's "Unholy Aura" discipline has received an enhancement. When in use, harmtouch resistances will be checked versus disease instead of magic. In addition, harmtouch will resist in the same fashion as lifetap spells, meaning that unless the creature is immune to disease, you should get full damage most of the time.
    - The spell attached to the "Fiery Defender" can now be partially resisted instead of being "all or nothing". The net result is that the average damage will be higher.
    - The Paladin "Holy Forge" discipline will now give paladins triple the normal chance of scoring a critical hit. Each critical hit has a percent chance to be scored as a critical blow.
    - Added parameters to the /camp command (the command that will cause you to camp out if you are already sitting). "/camp server" will take you back to the server selection screen. "/camp desktop" will take you out to the desktop following a successful camp. Despite popular request, "/camp spawn" will not be implemented.
    - Walruses now indeed sound like they should.
    - Added the /chatfontsize [0 to 5] (default is 1) command. This command will allow players to adjust the size of the font in the chat window.

    - The EverQuest Team

Backdoor Trojan Warning
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:12 PM PST
A site, which mimics the look and content of the official EQ site, was recently discovered to contain an ActiveX backdoor Trojan Horse. For your reference I will post a copy of this url in plain text (with a few X's placed for caution) and not hyperlinked, DO NOT visit it:

    xxsephiroth.xoasis.comxx

Here is a little more information from EQ Live's Associate Producer Gordon Wrinn:
    Now, if you've curiously gone to the address anyway, you should be able to
    easily deinfect yourself by deleing EA.HTA from your system (just do a
    search, it can be found in your C:\windows\start menu\programs\startup
    directory) as long as you do so BEFORE YOU REBOOT.

    If you've done gone and rebooted anyway, you'll need to go the long route to
    remove it (see below). In any case, please feel free to send out the alarm.
Gordon also included some important information to tell if you have it, and how to remove it:
    **HOW TO TELL IF YOU ARE INFECTED**
    Go to your start button, press run. Type in msconfig and hit return. Click
    on the startup tab. Look in the list presented for an instance of ;
    run= onz.exe
    If you have this entry, you will need to manually remove it. To remove this
    back door;
    1) Uncheck the check mark next to the run= onz.exe line and then click the
    apply button at the bottom of the window.
    2) Click on the general tab then click on advanced on the bottom of the
    window. Check the "enable startup menu" check box, click ok. Click ok again,
    and allow system to reboot.
    3) DO NOT let the system boot normally. You MUST select "Safe command
    prompt" from the boot menu.
    4) At the DOS prompt, type in "cd windows" (or the path to your window
    directory), press the enter key.
    5) Type in "del onz.exe" and then press the enter key.
    6) Reboot your system again.
    7) Select "normal" from the boot menu.
    8) If you get a window stating that the system can NOT find onz.exe, click
    ok.
    a. Click the Windows Start Button and then click Run.
    b. Type in "system.ini".
    c. You will now see a line that's looks like this; shell=Explorer.exe
    onz.exe.
    d. Remove the instance of onz.exe so it looks like this; shell=Explorer.exe.

    e. Save the changes to the file and then reboot once more.
    You have now removed the trojan from your system.
As long as you haven't stumbled onto this site in the past, or visit it now of course, you should be fine.

Thanks Gordon!

    TEST OF TEAMS
    GM Ozzmar – GM Lithgar

    Following the wall the stocky dwarf slows his pace, he sees a mass of people along the huge the blood caked pillar. The stout half sized warrior draws his sword and keeps a deadened gaze at the onlookers, as eyes meet in hatred. Locked, intent on foreseeing each other’s moves, the dwarf cautiously passes the crowd. One heckler shouts at him “Dorf! Joo com ere, mez stik yous now, mez saves us da trubl ltr har har!” A troll of unordinary size hearing the ogres’ humor stands up “ yessh wwwee kud see some foooood before the battle!” The troll uncovers an axe, the blade nearly half its size and bounds towards the dwarf. A scaled warrior seeing the ensuing battle springs into action and follows stride along the husky Troll. Upon seeing the danger, the dwarf throws his packs down and braces his stance as he grips both hands on the hilt of his weapon. His veins begin to bulge on his muscles as he raises the sword up into attack position. Unwavering he scowls at the two hideous forms of intelligence charging at him. But suddenly out of no where, a fire of from the heavens crashes between the Troll and the dwarf. An unfortunate Iksar, now just a slab of charred lizard, finds himself making a second attempt in reaching the arena in time. The dwarf nearly falls over in laughter at the turn of events, remembering clearly the warnings of the coordinator, “No PvP combat until the event starts.” The dwarf understood that this would be a troublesome rule especially in the arena, but in light of what could have just happened, he was glad the rule has been set. Picking up his packs and giving a gesture all too familiar to the Troll he bolts off further down the wall, deep wheezing laughter could be heard fading away from the evil, dark mob.

    The next mob of people was more his size and nature, the dwarf sheaths his sword back into his packs. Gnomes were chattering to each out other in their fast paced tongue. Halflings were casually resting along the wall, so calm and collected they were he noticed. Some halflings were causally combing their foot hair like they didn’t’ have a care in the world, as if they impending battle was to never take place. Grimacing, the dwarf never did like the happy folk but in battle there is something to be said about being able to look a comrade in the eyes. Soon though he did find his Kaladim brothers religiously drinking some ale and with an evil grin that would put Bertoxxulus to shame, he joins his brothers in celebration. “Aye good brothers, pass me some of that fine spirit!” Wiping his mouth he glances up at his dwarven brothers, “The purse? How much?” A dwarf with a holy symbol on his chestplate lifts his head with a smile, “5,000 shinies brother, bettered with my coin and yours.” He pauses for just a second before saying “ and I fully intent on getting my coin back and then some.” All the dwarves and even a few halflings drink to that.
    A green named coordinator walks up the short team drawing their attention with her words. “The rules are this!” as she continues with her speech another cooridinator begins to chant a binding spell on every team member, while taking note of the name of the bounded person and wishing them good luck. The other guide continues “ you will be bound to this location outside of the inner circle, if you fall to misfortune inside the arena you will find yourself where you are now, You are to stay here until the end of the match. If ye decide to break this rule and make way into the inner circle you will be promptly killed and pulled back to your bind point, most importantly though…” the coordinator pauses and makes sure everyone is paying attention. She continues, “…Noted. This note will be used against your team and will result in 250pp being withdrawn from your winning purse and one less person left standing in the end will not count as standing. Beware gladiators this match could easily end early even if some of your team still stands if in fact this rule is broken.” The short warriors and casters nod in agreement all peering at each other in hopes that none would break this costly rule. The coordinator continues, “Once word is giving we will make way into the inner circle and you will all follow me to another pillar much like the one you are now taking shade under. At which point you will gather and patiently discuss your strategies for the battle. “ She pauses. “YOU ARE NOT to engage anyone before the battle is to begin. If so you will be removed from the event.” The coordinator then answers a few questions from some not so bright halflings and in time finally beckons the mob to follow her into the deadly inner circle of the arena.

    The short team was not the first to enter the arena, at the far wall one could see a mass of gleaming weapons and all around them magic being casted in astonishing displays of color. The dwarf was not unnerved by this comical show the elves were displaying. He was quite certain that the dwarving clerics would take very good care of him before the match began. The dwarf did respect the numbers the elven brotherhood held though, when he was finally settled in the location the guide had brought them, he stared intently on the elves counting their numbers. He counted 3 or 4 scores of the pointed eared kind from his vantage point. Comparing the force of his army to theirs he knew the elves had a few more blades. This prompted another deep chuckle from the dwarf for he also knew without a doubt that elves were terrible in battle. Feeling confident the dwarf opens his flask of ale and throws back a gulp, undoing his weapon from his pack along with a sharpening stone. The dwarf relaxes in savoring the sound of the stone against the metal and little sparks that it creates. “Warrior magic…” he mumbles letting his thoughts drift over the prizes the purse will buy him.

    The short team and the elf team already stationed inside the arena, next comes in the human team. Their chins held high, they all walk into a chosen spot between the two teams already preparing for battle. Not much is heard from this team as they talk amongst themselves. If looked closely you could see the inner struggles some of the human clans were facing about the two other teams inside the arena. Fortunately, greed has its way of temporally setting aside friendships and the human team starts to enchant each other with spells to improve prowess in battle. The Eurdites form a circle and meditate in unison, while the barbarians much like the dwarfs sit quietly sharpening their weapons carefully inspecting the opposition with wry smiles on their battle worn faces.

    The final team entering the arena creates a terrible display of vulgarity, bringing out the worst in the three other teams. The hated participants make their way to their chosen starting location. All eyes are on them and they know it. Taunting back, the evil team composed of the Trolls, Iksars, Ogres and Dark Elves, answer a barrage of insults with hideous laughter. What was most astonishing about the evil team was the size of the army they brought together. 5 or more scores of the wretched kind were eagerly awaiting a chance to inflict pain and suffering on the other teams. This brought much pride to the evil brethren but on the same token much fear. They all knew that the other teams would likely stop at nothing in bringing them down. The general air of the arena among the other 3 teams seemed to silently reinforce this disheartening thought.

    A cooridinator raises his voice to the point that everyone in the arena could hear. “All teams are in place, no other participants will be allowed, and no spectators will see this event. The only way to now leave this inner circle is through Victory or Death!!” this prompts battle screams and mottoes from all 4 of the teams. When the cries calm down the coordinator continues, “The purse is counted at 7400 platinum pieces to be divided up amongst the winning team. We will now give you 10 minutes to make final preparations for battle. No PvP will be tolerated before the announcement is to begin.!!” The coordinator heads back to his chosen team and watches for infractions of the rules he stated.

    For the next ten minutes, the atmosphere of the arena is of a calm before the storm. Casters are hurriedly finishing off final buffings to their teammates. Leaders are planning strategies and organizing teammates into groups. Coordinators are taking their places at both entrances readying their weapons for infractions of the rules. A two minutes warning is giving to the zone to prepare for battle.

    A noble High Elf woman makes her way to the very center of the teams. Equipped with a flaming sword, Lady Daegarmo asks for a moment of silence in the seconds before the battle. Welcome to the Test of Teams citizens of Norrath! I wish you all the best of luck! On my mark…” She pauses for the remaining seconds of the 10 minute warning.

    At the top of her lungs Lady Daegarmo screams with the power of the Gods, “FIGHT!!!!!”
