The Epic Tale of the Darkflame Knights

By Mimsy
Toxxulia Forest - far o'er the sea,
Wherein lie the skeletons cackling with glee,
Is a place I once wandered with lute in my hand
peering out through the dark as I travelled the land.

The kobolds did scatter - at my sight they did flee,
Leaping high, as they do, to the tops of the trees.
I stood brave. I stood tall. I sang with strong might,
of the deeds of the Darkflames - a famed group of Knights.

I sang loudly of Trog, emperor on most high,
who for being a troll, is one sweet, big, green guy.
Of Aearen I sang, Ranger... Hugger of Trees,
And his brave battle cry, "Trog, buff me up, please?"

I sang praises to Biggis, short rogue - half a pint,
and told tales of his battles in Freeport one night.
Othar's song did huk huk, for the cleric so furry
Whose brave, fuzzy toes never care, never worry.

Of Davaren I sang, of his sleek perfect hair,
and his helmet for opening ales without care.
Genasi, lady fair, who never seems tire,
lugging 'Flames back and forth from each druid spire.

I ran through the forest, singing loudly and plain,
Of Jolith the brave, and his new Ghoulbane.
Menelaos the sage, who I oft want to tickle,
to see if he might crack a smile - perhaps giggle.

I told of Strumm, DarkFlames' highest of bards,
My lute rang out true as I strummed out more chords.
Aaeldevenya the dark, in her robe blue and pale,
with her tongue twister name she just answers to Aael.

Of Nujai the fair, and the long lost Beldrew,
who in Oasis one night, greatly speeded my youth.
I sang loudly of Beltaine, our leader most recent,
and his deeds as a wolf, always honored and decent.

I ran o'er a bridge- sang of Aegeis and Soulfire,
some sword caught aflame - a monster's funeral pyre.
Of Ceasar dressed in steel, all shiny and bright,
A walking lightstone in the mid' of the night.

I sang tales of the Darkflames, of young and of old,
Trivian, Faludir, Darin, Cal' and Quatto,
of others whose names are known through the land,
as the bravesest of Knights, as a great merry band.

As I rounded the island and my song did well end,
with suprise I did see this collection of friends,
Standing proud, true, and tall near the gates and the guards,
with merely a chuckle for the foibles of bards.

With swords at the ready, they did make quite a sight,
As I slipped in the ranks and we made off tot he night.
Kobolds did scamper, skeletons fled in fright,
At the sight of the famous and great Darkflame Knights.
