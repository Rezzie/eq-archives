A New Day
Fiction by Sariah

This installment was written by a reader, not by the original authors of this series.

The sun rises in the east, the crow cakles, and all begins anew. The bards fresh to ignore the wrong notes of last nights concert, the rangers a new chance to hunt game, without thought of the kodiak that escaped their grasp the day before. All may start fresh, except for one. The paladin Banegrivm, lost and returned. One whose victories were without number, but with a defeat still so fresh in his mind. All the visions return, the triumphant battle against Murlune, the sickening blackness that soon folowed, the stinging blade of the vile drow Vinenae, and the face of the cleric he never saw, yet haunted him, never fully comming into detail. His mind awash with emotions, he knew little more than to head off to the tavern, completely forgeting to report to his master. A seasoned drinker, it took him little time to fall into the drunken stupor that is on the verge of utter drunkenness. Something in the corner of his eye drew his attention. He swivled on his chair, to see the cleric, this time in full view, with a quizzical look on her face. Then with a twist of her fingers, he blacked out, succumbing to the alchohol that he made his life.

***

Out of the blackness, images arrise that cannot be penned with the complete, vivid nature that comes from a mind that is not fully awake, yet not asleep by a long shot. These visions flashed before him, then faded to aqua as he burbled in the ocean, then was yanked out.

"What are you doing getting yourself drunk like that?" The cleric loomed behind him.

"Was the last battle nothing more than a chapter torn out and burnt like a log? If that is the case, then Marr's care was misplaced."

He turned around, unsteadly as he had never been confronted on this topic.

"I survived didn't I, that means that the chapter is done, now let me drink in peace!"

Annother cold dunk awakened him fully.

"But the final word have not been writen, Vinenae is still alive, therefore the battle is not finished. She will come for you, and I cannot protect you from yourself, only awaken you to the truth."

She let go of his tunic, and let him fall to the docks.

"Remeber this feeling? The feeling of falling onto the same planks just a night before? This time there is no blade to sting you, but next time, the blade will not be denyed."

A cold wind made the paladin turn around, to find that she had vanished.

***

"What does Mithaniel have in mind for this boy," Sariah wondered aloud, atop the same balcony she had disapeared to before. "What could he accomplish."
