The difference is in the details. Developer-unique items might have unique meshes/skins. They may also have interesting histories and very special abilities. By making special abilities part of a random generator, you've got to come up with them in the first place. If the special abilities are just baked-in spells, then yeah, you could generate them uniquely, but you'd only be talking about a handful of unique items. What's really the difference between Google's Mace of rooting versus Ziggy's Flail of rooting?

My main point is that if you want to produce lots of unique items with a random generator, they will look randomly generated (and they will be too common). It's the lore about the item as well as it's abilities & appearance. Plus, certain items should have some kind of notoriety, so that people will know you have them even if you don't reveal it directly.

The problem I have with randomly generated items is that they will have no interesting history that is incorporated into the world's history. If I want random items, I'll play Diablo. If EQ is just randomness, there's no point in playing. I can write my own random item generator and let it run and watch the names that come up, but so what? It's the idea that you're discovering something _real_ that makes the game. That's why the most basic custom built RPG module beats a randomly generated game hands down.
