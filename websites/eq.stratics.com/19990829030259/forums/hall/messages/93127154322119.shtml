Yes, I know that you apparently had some very bad incident happen to a friend of yours in EQ, that rightly and justly pissed you off. But compared to any decently run paper and pencil RPG campaign, ANY online roleplaying game will be without substance. UO and AC will have certain advantages over EQ is what all you can do with one character. But both games will have their drawbacks that counterbalance these, and any online RPG will still pale in story and plot vs. a real RPG. Because you simply don't have a GM there, at a table, running an adventure for a group of people.

However, even paper and pencil games have their drawbacks. Some (few) GMs can be good, but many I have spent all too much time with were fair at best. And it takes an incredible amount of effort to run a good RPG campaign. I should know, I ran a fine one (one of my players told me that most of them would rather dip their eyeballs in paint thinner than miss one of my games, true story) for many years. But those were years I could have been doing quite a few other things, and all in all I'd rather have them back.

So any RPG will have it's limitations. EQ has quite a few, but for many folks it accomplishes it's goals well. I know that myself and 2 of my real-life friends are pretty happy with it, and we spend entirely too much time playing. Again, there goes my Real Life. No, I'm not arguing that they need to make major changes to the trade skills. That they need to rework how the "kill-ownership" works, at the very least making group damage cumulative (thus removing the lone kill stealing wizard/magician/whatever in one move), they need to start implementing some guild houses, they need to add some zones to cities where you can either buy or build houses...residential districts. They need to enlarge certain dungeons and make them multizone. They really need to include a "quest flag" in your character file, so that if you've done a one-shot quest once, then you can't do it again. Then give much improved exp. and rewards for quests. It'd be nice if they'd hire enough bloody GMs, and give Guides enough reason to guide, so that they could get some real dynamic quests going.

Yeah, they need to do all these things. And I doubt that they will do all these things. But AC won't do them either, nor does UO. Quests? I never saw any quests in UO period.

Well, anyway, I know that you have a legitimate beef with EQ. But it's not as bad as you paint it, many are happy with it.
