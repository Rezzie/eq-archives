EQ Restricts MY Access To My Own Computer!!!! (long)

Posted by Dark Councilor on July 01, 1999 at 12:35:51 PM CDT:

(63.192.246.197)

In short we, as users really need to be able to run this game in a window! The inability to multitask while playing presents some pretty signicant inconveniences, at least for me, and I grow more and more tired of them as time goes on.

There are several apps that I really would love to be able to use in conjunction with EQ.

ICQ

I want to know when I receive ICQ Messages. Currently, if a friend wants to know if I’m online playing so that he can join, he or she must log on and use the /who command or call me on the phone (I have a cable modem). It would be a lot more cool for them to be able to send me a quick ICQ:

“Hey, you online playing EQ?”
“Yep, go ahead and connect to my Battlecom session and join up! The IP is. . .”

Battlecom

Which leads me to the next app I want to use – Battlecom. For those of you who don’t know, this is an app that lets you actually use your voice to talk to each other while you game, rather than using the clunky typing interface of the game. Not only is this a lot more convenient, it can save your life in a tight situation where you know that if you take the time to type something, it may mean your life.

Yes, currently you can use Battlecome with EQ the way it is today, but in the case above, if I’m already online and someone wants us to start a Battlecom session, I have to log out, start the session and log back in. This probably means a good 10 minutes of downtime for me, especially since I’ll have to re-summon all my food when I log back in!

And , the way it is now, if someone logs on to EQ, sees that I’m on, and wants to join my Battlecom session, THEY have to quit, join, and relog too.

The Internet

Finally, being a caster, I spend a lot of time meditating. While I’m meditating, wouldn’t it be nice if I could be doing game research at my favorite EQ site instead of sitting on my hands or just watching while irrelevant OOCs and Shouts scroll up my screen? I know that there are a lot of “purists” out there who are completely against using sites like EQ’lizer, but for me, doing the research on the internet is half the fun of the game. If I want to do this, or just check my email while I wait, I should be able to!

Right now I have to do all of this research when I’m not playing. I’d much rather take advantage of the down time I have in-game. That way, when I get bored of the place I am and yearn for a new item to quest after, I can just ALT TAB to a browser and see what interests me in the next zone.

I realize that the full screen only format is one of Verant’s ways of preventing something like EQExtreme from getting a foothold, but I think that restricting how I can use my computer to accomplish that is just plain being inconsiderate to users. I feel like I’ve been given a MacIntosh – something dumbed down and locked down with no room for flexibility. As far as I’m concerned, this should be restricted by monitoring users in-game and investigating complaints from users. Restricting my access to my own computer is not the answer.

Besides, I think that all of the above are legitimate, valid uses for multitasking that only enhance the game. I know there are technical issues with video to work around, but I think that the obstacles are surmountable, and that windowed play could, and should be an option!

-Dark Councilor

PS – Flame away all you who say “we don’t need any of that stuff so you should’t either!” I want it, and just because you don’t doesn’t me the rest of us shouldn’t have it.
