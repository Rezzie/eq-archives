EQ should have a character corpse backup. They could preserve what your previous corpse had as equipment so that if your current session corpse gets toasted a GM could see aproximately what you should have and at least compensate you approporiately. The way it works now they can't do that at all because a GM has no frame of reference to judge a players belongings.

Hindsight is relative of course. In the long run when you play oneline with persistant characters they are going to take hits. It's going to bug everyone, but since it's really the very nature of online gaming I really recommend not playing games like these if you are overly concerned with fairness and absolutely consistant characters. You are destined to lose some stuff at some time.

I just hate to think of a lvl 30 warrior losing his full plate and magic weapons in the same manner this player did above. I have to admit that could very much kill a character's usefullness in such a case. verant has shown some effort in making amends in cases like that. They may not be adequate, but they are at least trying to set up a fair policy.
