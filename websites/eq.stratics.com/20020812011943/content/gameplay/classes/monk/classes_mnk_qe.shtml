Playing a Human Monk: Starting out in Qeynos

 Qeynos 1-4   Qeynos 5-8   Qeynos 9-12   Qeynos 13-16 

1-4 Qeynos
First thing; you will start in south Qeynos, near the bank. Take this time to get to know the city. Then, skip on over to North Qeynos, and drop off your guild note, get a guild tunic, and be off. North Qeynos hunting grounds are very good for newbies, with gnolls, rats, bats, snakes, decaying skeletons, fire beetles, and the occasional normal skeleton, zombie, queen Klacknak, or named gnoll. First off, beat up on things near the guards, plenty of rats in this area. You want to stay near the guards, so that you have the option of running, screaming like a little girl, to the guards. When you do venture away from the guards, keep your eyes open for gnoll pups, not only for the scalps, but also because they carry the occasional backpack, pouch, sweet sweet liquor, and some pocket change. At level 2, you should start killin Klacknaks and Fire Beetles. They give good experience, as well as fire beetle eyes, which are a low-level light source, and good money. You should direct the cash you have left after you buy equipment (covered below) towards bandages, as bind wound is a pretty useful skill later on. Once around level 4, you can head into Qeynos hills. This zone has quite a few mid level baddies, as well as a couple high level NPC Tree-Huggers that will kill you if they catch you killing animals. The main targets here are fire beetles, giant rats, gray wolves, and gnoll scouts. Careful not to wander too far into the zone, because of bears and rabid animals.


1-4 Qeynos (Equipment)
Pretty much the only equipment you need here is cloth armor, you should have enough money from fire beetles and whatnot to afford quite a bit of it. Remember not to go for leather or patchwork armor, because that crap is too heavy for a monk. You should get in the habit of not exceeding 14 pounds. It may not make a difference now, but trust me, at higher levels; it puts a huge dent in your AC.



1-4 Qeynos (Quests)
Your first quest will be for the white headband. For this, you will need 4 gnoll pup scalps off... (Drumroll please) GNOLL PUPS! These should be pretty easy to get in the outer area of north Qeynos, and the near area of Qeynos hills. Your other quest for these levels will be to find Tomer Instogle. Just walk into the guild house, and ask seta about him. I am not gonna tell you how to do the quest, you figure it out, I need a drink!


Qeynos 5-8
You should be sticking around the beginning of Qeynos hills, for levels 5 and 6, then you can move out farther and kill giant bats, giant snakes, bears (but watch out for Holly Windstalker [referred to lovingly by the locals as "The Bitch"]) and the gnolls that loiter around the entrance to Blackburrow are a good target too. At level 6 and 7 there are 3 good places to go. The Ruins: where you can find regular and putrid skeletons, which are both good experience. Also, the putrids drop putrid rib bones, which are needed for a quest. But you need to watch out for dread bone skeletons, which, at this level, will not hesitate to slap you around like a red-headed stepchild. Next, comes the Qeynos Sewers: some good things to kill down here, there are little alligator things, as well as smugglers, skeletons, frogloks, and occasionally, Drosco and Cubert. Drosco and Cubert are both part of quests, which I will cover below. (But no spoilers, deal with it). 3rd, BlackBurrow: here you can while away your time killing scrawny gnolls, normal gnolls, and even the occasional patrolling gnoll (Tell them what they've won Johnny!). This low level dungeon can provide experience and loot from here all the way up to level 20! (But if you spend levels 6-20 completely in Blackburrow, I am gonna have to kill you.) But be careful of trains down here, because they are capable of killing you even faster than I am.


Qeynos 5-8 (Equipment)
Down in those Qeynos sewers, there is some good equipment to be had. Remember those frogloks I mentioned (If you don't, read it again or I will have to kidnap your dog!) well, they can drop some nice netted armor. Netted is the magic equivalent of cloth. Same AC, and it weighs less. In addition, the netted gloves can hit magic things (Ooohh Ahhhh!) which will come in real handy later in life, when you are fighting wisps, and ghouls and whatnot.


Qeynos 5-8 (Quests)
First, there is the yellow headband quest. For this you will need 3 putrid rib bones, which you get from putrid skeletons over at those ruins I mentioned earlier. Another quest is the one involving Drosco, which I also mentioned earlier (No spoilers, just kill Drosco and you should be able to figure it out from there). Also, there is a guard somewhere in North Qeynos (I forget where, you figure it out) that has lost his daughter, and is offering a reward for any evidence of her. Also, in south Qeynos, there is a guy near the entrance to the sewers talking about a girl that got lost down there, he also offers a reward for evidence of her. Last one; go ask Phin (at the guild) about Konem.


Qeynos 9-12
BlackBurrow, like I mentioned before, you can stay here until level 20 (But there will be dire reprocutions!) At level 9 you can start to kill some burly gnolls, carefully. You can also Group to kill gnoll guardsmen. At level 12 and 13 you can start to solo those guardsmen, and group to kill elite gnoll guards, and maybe the occasional Shaman and Commander.


Qeynos 9-12 (equipment)
Around now you may want to look into woven or raw silk, if you can afford it. Woven is the magic equivalent of patchwork, with the same AC, but a lot less weight. Raw silk is the Player made equivalent of Woven, with the same AC, and (I think) less weight. I think the only place woven comes from is, inside, and in the immediate vicinity of Guk, which is a long way off, so probably pretty hard to get around here. Raw silk is (as I mentioned earlier) player made, so probably easier to get. At the top of this level range you may be making your way into Mesh, and Cured silk armor. Mesh is the magical equivalent of leather, with the same AC, and the same weight as netted and woven (which is a lot lower than leather). Cured silk is the player made equivalent of mesh. It also has the same AC as leather, but only weighs .1 pounds, every piece only weighs .1! Another thing to add about Cured silk, it looks great. Look at the stuff that the trainers are wearing, that is cured silk. The big tan pants, the see through tunic, the cloth strips wrapped around their wrists, that is exactly what cured silk looks like. Mesh and Cured silk will cost you a pretty penny. Mesh (like woven) comes from Guk, so it will be extra expensive.


Qeynos 9-12 (quests)
Not a whole lot of quests to speak of in this level range.


Qeynos 13-16
These levels give quite a few options in hunting areas. Deep in the Qeynos Catacombs, there are some options. A ways down there, there are smugglers and thugs. These are some pretty good targets. They drop raw-hide, and sometimes, bronze weapons. Also inside the Catacombs, near the temple of the BloodSabres, there are some abnormally powerful skeletons. These skeletons are in 4 little nooks at the entrance of the temple. These skeletons should range from white at level 13, and some will still be a low blue at level 16. Another good place for these levels, are the Western Plains of Karana. Out here, there are lots of wandering bears, lions and beetles. North Karana is more plentiful in beetles, but they are more powerful, so this is a good place for grouping. Another viable option in North Karana for groups, is griffawns. These may con white or blue to you in this level range, but trust me, you won't be able to solo them, thus, these are a very good option for groups. Back in West Karana, you can kill Bandits at the bandit camp. These are of similar level, and drop similar loot to the thugs and smugglers of the Qeynos Catacombs. Yet another option for these levels, is "Wisp Island" in Erud's Crossing. This Island is swarming with wisps (hence the name). There is also a sunken ship off the coast of the island, if you swim over by this ship, you will find lots of zombie sailors. Both of these are very good for this level range. To kill wisps, you will need magical gloves (I'll cover it in the equipment section). The wisps will give you serious cash. As well as dropping reasonable amounts of cash, wisps also drop lightstones and greater lightstones. Lightstones can sell for about 1pp to a PC, and about 9gold to an NPC. Greater lightstones can sell from 5-7pp to PCs, and most shopkeepers will pay about 4pp for them. They are also part of a very lucrative quest that I will cover in the Quests section below.


Qeynos 13-16 (Equipment)
As I mentioned above, you will need magical gloves to hit wisps. At this level, you basically have 3-4 options for magical gloves. Netted, Woven and Mesh will all hit magical creatures. You can get netted gloves from the frogloks in the Qeynos Catacombs, but it is going to take awhile. Your 4th option (If you have the cash) is, Wu's Fighting Gauntlets. These gloves have the AC of leather (4) the look of cured silk (look like cloth strips wrapped around your hands) a weight of only .1, and to top it all off, they act as a lightsource equivalent to a Greater Lightstone! I will warn you however; these gloves cost a pretty penny. They can run from 40pp up to about 75pp, but the standard price is around 50pp. As your other equipment goes, you should be working on getting as much cured silk and mesh as possible. The armor you have now is going to be with you for a long time. A few considerations for tunics are: snakeskin jerkins (Not sure if these still drop. They came from paw, but since they updated it, I don't know if you can get them there anymore) These Jerkins have the same weight as leather (2.5) 9AC (one more than leather) as well as some poison resistance (+8). Your other main option for tunics is the Lockjaw Hide Vest. These Vests are very nice for this level. They have 12AC, and adds 5 stamina. However, they come from the oasis of Marr (Near Freeport). This is a long way off, so they are going to be hard to get around Qeynos, and they will probably be pretty expensive. The going rate for Lockjaw Hide Vests around Freeport is 30pp, but in Qeynos, I wouldn't be surprised to pay 40pp for one. With the money you have gathered, there are some good pieces of equipment from blackburrow that you may want to consider acquiring. First is the Onyx earring. This is a nice earring that comes off Elite Gnoll Guards, Gnoll Shamen, and Gnoll Commanders. This earring adds 2 agility (An important stat for monks). The other pieces of equipment are for the neck. The two options are the spiked collar, and studded collar. Both come from the same enemies as the Onyx earring. The spiked collar gives 3 AC and 3 strength. The studded collar gives the same AC, 1 strength and 1 stamina.


Qeynos 13-16 (Quests)
As I mentioned above, there is a very lucrative quest that you can take place in at these levels. It involves Greater Lightstones. As I mentioned above, Greater Lightstones can be sold to players for 5-7pp and vendors for 4pp. There is however, a Gypsy in North Karana that gives you 8pp and experience for them. Simply go to the Gypsy can in North Karana, and hail all the female Gypsies. One of them will say that she is the keeper of the concordance of research, or something of the like. This is the one you want. If you inquire deeper, she says she will reward you for giving her Greater Lightstones. When you give them to her, you will gain some experience, and she will give you a Concordance of Research. Sell this back to her, and she will pay over 8pp for it!
