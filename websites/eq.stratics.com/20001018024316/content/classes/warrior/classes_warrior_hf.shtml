Playing a Halfling Warrior

 Warriors   Halfling   Halflings As Warrior   Stats   Using Training Points   Choosing Your Weapon   Starting Location   Closing 

Warriors
Warriors are made for combat, period. They are made to be able to wade into the thick of battle and withstand more punishment than any other class. As far as physical offense or defense they have no equal. As far as solo hunting they have the easiest time until around 20th level. 20th level is when things start getting very hard for the soloing warrior. The creatures you fight have a lot more health and are in much more dangerous areas (usually dungeons). The fights take longer. Dungeons can become a deathtrap very fast for the older soloing warrior who takes too long in a fight or has to run down a fleeing Mob whos running for backup. Healing up between fights take a very long time. Sure you have a ton of hit points; you need a ton of time to get them back too.

Mages do much more instant damage at those higher levels. They can finish creatures faster thus equaling shorter fights. Problem with mages is they usually come out of a battle with full health or none at all. Older mobs will tear a mage to shreds if it gets in close enough for melee. So what do we know? Mages have better offense later in life whereas warriors have better defense and can actually survive after 60 seconds of melee combat. Everquest is definitely group oriented. Mages need tanks to keep the mobs off them so they can destroy the mob, while warriors need mages to keep the fights quick and to finish off fleeing mobs. This means that a warriors strongpoint later in life (in a group) is defense. Whats important to defense? AC, agility, stamina, and defensive skills. We will get more into these later.

Halflings
Halflings are about half the size of a human thus the name. Many people wont play halflings because they dont look as "imposing" or "cool" as many other races. Also, a lot of people have a short complex. There are a lot of advantages going with a halfling. They are extremely agile and dexterous. They naturally have the ability to hide, which can keep you out of a lot of hurting. Hide is excellent when you need to sit down and heal up or are in a dangerous area and need a minute to get your wits about you. Halflings also get the innate ability to sneak. Basically being able to move while hidden without becoming visible. Another excellent skill for getting out of potentially bad situations. Lets say you are deep in a zone and get cut off from your party. You are too weak or injured to solo in this area but you can sneak your way out of the zone or to where you think your party is. Another plus to halflings is that they have infravision. Infravision helps you immensely at night. From being safer running around to finding stuff to hunt, it always helps if you can see whats in front of you. Halflings wear small armor, which is cheaper and lighter. Finally, the last really good thing is their starting city, Rivervale. Ill cover Rivervale later.

Halflings As WarriorsWhere are they? You dont see many about. You see a lot of barbarian warriors. Ive read about five different warrior guides and have never seen they words "halfling warrior" mentioned once (at least not in a positive light) I see ogre, troll, and barbarian mentioned in almost every guide. I guess because they have the highest strength, and stamina. Plus they look real tough and powerful.

In my OPINION halflings make the best warriors, and Ill tell you why. The reason is defense. Higher defense means getting hit less and when you do, taking less damage. That means, if you have a healer in your group they dont need to spend as much mana on you as they do on the clumsy barbarians, ogres, or trolls who seem like they have a big bullseye on their chest. More mana usually equates to more buff spells for the party, more mana to heal other members, and more mana for offensive spells. All that equals less chance of someone or a whole party ending up dead. Survival or Victory. Whatever you want to call it. Usually around now someones screaming, "yeah but I have a lot more hit points then a halfling". Yep sure do. When you get older and mobs start whacking you for over 100 points a shot every other swing because you cant get out of the way, and they have a ton of health, and the party cant take the mob down as fast because they need to heal your damage receiving arse, who cares if you have more hit points. Id rather not get hit in the first place, or when I do not for much. If the tank dies the party usually follows if you are fighting formidable foes. The next argument I hear is yeah but I hit for more damage than halflings at older levels". Yep sure do. And the mages do a lot more damage than you do. You will not be able to take mobs down as fast as a mage at older levels. If you want to deal out the most damage in the group than be a mage. Why try to be the offensive hero when you cant compare to the mage standing behind you? The main reason why halflings have such great defense is agility. If you maximize your agility it will be 125. Another nice thing about having a high agility is finishing ability. You notice that when you are down to your last bar of health your agility starts dropping like a rock? I notice I usually lose around 30 points of agility before Im actually out of hit points. So it drops to about 95. 95 agility isnt too bad. I cant imagine what its like to have a low agility to start with and then lose 30 points on top of that. Doesnt give you much hope of survival. Also when Im almost dead I still run at a pretty decent speed. Taking this into account I can usually stick it out in a fight a little longer before Im forced to run or back out, and if I do run I usually have a better chance of escape.

Stats
The most important stats for warriors are strength, stamina, agility, and under rated intelligence. Strength for being able to hit hard and carry loot and armor. Stamina for hit points. Agility for defense. Intelligence for speed of learning and skill. I say stick everything towards agility for the reasons I covered in the above paragraphs. Your strength can be buffed pretty easy by group members and is high enough as it is to comfortably wear plate mail. Stamina is a waste if you have the mindset of not being hit in the first place and you will have plenty of hit points with your starting stamina as it is. Warriors often overlook intelligence. You know how your skills rise as you use them? They rise faster if your intelligence is higher. You will probably cap out on most of your skills at the lower levels but skills get harder to push at later stages in the game. (On a side rant, this is the main advantage of the also underestimated gnome and dark elf warriors who are pretty agile and extremely smart (for a warrior). This is also the main downfall of ogre and troll warriors. Ogres and trolls have such a low starting intelligence they are really hurt because later in the game they are not as skilled in offense, defense, and other warrior skills like other warrior races. Even with their low starting Intelligence I bet most ogres and trolls still boost their strength, stamina, and agility stats, leaving their Intelligence in a pitiful state. So you want to play a Troll or Ogre and want to hit hard, you can still dump those points into Strength, but at least put the rest into Int. Or better yet put them all in Int. Youll find out later in life you are more effective in combat than other ogres and trolls because your skills will be higher.) Anyway, Ill get into how to keep your skills maxed out even with a lower Intelligence when I discuss training points.

Using Training Points
I cringe every time I read a guide that tells you at level 1 to go put training points in skills that go up on their own. If you spend any points on a weapon skill you use before level 10 then you are throwing away those points. Usually even before 20 is a waste. In my OPINION there are 3 ways to use training points. First way is to develop a trade skill, which would be expensive to develop otherwise. Second way is to learn the basics of a skill (by spending 1 point ONLY on it) you do not have such as a language your trainer offers you or a skill you obtain once you reach a certain level (such as dodge at 6). Third way is to keep your skills maxed once they start having a problem maxing out every level. When using this third way later in life, it is best to attempt to spend points on basic skills right before you make your next level. Once you hit 10 start visiting the trainer when you have less than a quarter of a bubble of experience to go to make your next level. Try to train your important skills higher. This way if a skill hasnt maxed out by the end of your level then youll know its having a hard time keeping up and you can force it to rise from the trainer since your own intelligence isnt making it rise fast enough. Once again, if you spend training points on skills that would max out anyway for your level then you are wasting points and possible silver.
 top


Choosing Your Weapon
You probably want to decide at an early age if you want to use two handers, two weapons at once, or a weapon and a shield. Most warriors will go the two-handed or two-weapon route for the obvious offensive advantage. Personally Im sticking with a shield for the ac. If you look at a level 40 halfling warrior with a 125 agility, good taunting skill, platemail, and a shield and compare him to a level 40 barbarian warrior without a shield, lower ac, and lower agility, in a group setting (which is probably where a 40th warrior will be) the halfling would be a much higher asset to the group for the reasons I talked about above. If you dont want to go the shield route thats fine (maybe youll have a halfling warrior with a shield in the group who can tank for you so you wont get hit), youll need to decide between using a two hander or dual wield. Personally I prefer dual wield because when combined with double strike itll hit 4 times a round rather than 2 with the two-hander, and also lets you break out a shield when you need it without having to train in two different weapon types. Now dont get me wrong, I love having two handers in the group with me. I try to tank and let them dish out the damage when mages arent around.
 top
Starting Location
Halflings start out in Rivervale, which has one of the best hunting areas in the world for levels 1-10. Rivervale is a one zone, easy to get around in town. Rivervale is probably the most central location in the world that you could start in. If you want to go hunt in runnyeye, beholder, befallen, or paw they are all close. Rivervale also has one of the best lower level quests for warriors, the squad ring quest. Im not going to spoil it so youll have to figure it out. Its definitely worth doing. Thats all Im going to say about Rivervale. There are good city guides out there if you want more info.

Closing
One thing I wanted to mention but didnt want to do a whole section on was etiquette. If someone helps you out, try to give him or her a tip. Most people wont take tips but its the thought that counts. You want a faction increase between you and your fellow players? Thats one way to earn it. I didnt go into group strategy, hunting areas, or a lot of other things that warrior guides usually cover because there are plenty of resources out there for that stuff. There was a gap in the warrior guides concerning Halflings (gnomes too). The average person thinking about playing a warrior who reads one of them guides may think I need to decide between a barbarian, troll, or ogre. I wanted to make sure halflings get their fare shake. The people who designed this game arent stupid. Many come from a long time mudding background. They know what works and what doesnt. They understand balance. They didnt make any bad race/class combos. I think they did a great job (especially with balance and the economy).

If anyone wants to look me up I play on the Tarew Marr server and Im in the Talisman guild. If you ever need help feel free to ask or drop me a line from the Talisman guild website at www.norrath.org. I hope you got something out of this guide even if you dont play a halfling warrior. Good hunting!
