Armor Statistics

 Arms   Back   Chest   Face   Feet   Hands   Head   Legs   Neck   Shields   Shoulders   Waist   Wrists 
Armor is sorted alphabetically by area covered (and by protection offered within each particular category). AC, or Armor Class, protects your character from damage received during combat, and counts against his or her Weight Allowance. Only one piece of armor may be worn over each body area (wrists count as two body areas). The maximum possible combined AC that a player character can attain using standard armor types is 120, including shield. This is an addition to any natural or magical defenses that the character has as a result of skill, class, or spells.

All armor types will be one of three sizes: Small, Medium, and Large, and weight varies accordingly. Dwarves, Halflings, and Gnomes are restricted to Small armor; Dark Elves, High Elves, and Wood Elves may don either Small or Medium armor; Erudites, Half Elves, and Humans wear Medium Armor only; and Barbarians, Ogres, and Trolls are considered to be Large.

Prices were determined using a character with a 75 Charisma. Many prices have been rounded back to their two highest coin values. In order to determine the approximate cost of a full suit of each of the standard armor types, use the following table:


Cloth Leather Chain+Iron Plate
43gp, 7sp 26pp, 5gp 517pp, 5gp 5,425pp, 6gp

pp=platinum, gp=gold, sp=silver, cp=copper (Currency Conversion Table)

Tattered Cloth and Leather Armor: These well-worn and disintegrating pieces of Leather and Cloth offer protection of up to 1 to 3 points worse than their newer counterparts and are significantly less expensive.
