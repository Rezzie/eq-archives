Cleric Spells Analysis

 Healing Powers   Versus the Undead 

Section I - Healing Powers of the Cleric
As a Cleric, your power lies in two main areas. The first area is in your ability to heal and cure the ailments of others. The second area is your ability to combat the forces of what we know as the undead. Let us begin our discussion with the Cleric's healing powers.
Rank I
Minor Healing - One of the first spells an aspiring Cleric learns. Minor Healing, like all of the spells in the healing group, is based on the Cleric's expertise in the arcane skill of Alteration. This spells is a relatively low power spell, and as such doesn't require much in the way of mana expenditure. As an Apprentice Cleric (Rank I), Minor Healing will be your only healing spell. At higher levels however, you will more choices available to you. These more potent healing spells require a large expenditure of mana however, and the Cleric should always remember that he or she can fall back upon the basic Minor Healing if needed.

Cure Poison - A basic spell, but one that should never be forgotten. Not much can be said about this useful spell except, do not forget you have it. If you or another should chance to be poisoned, the use of this spell as a cure is much preferred over the option of letting the poison run its course. Most poisons occurring naturally in Norrath are slow acting. Thus, even if you don't currently have this spell memorized, you should have time to do so before the poison can cause too much damage to the infected soul.

Rank II

Light Healing - The Cleric's spell of Light Healing is a natural progression of skill from the less effective Minor Healing. While Light Healing uses more of the caster's mana to perform its task, it is also more effective. Speaking strictly mana -vs- effect, Light Healing is worth the extra expenditure. In addition, when you have attained the experience of higher ranks, and thus more efficient healing spells, never forget that you can fall back on Light Healing or Minor Healing in emergency situations when mana is critical.

As an addendum - Experience has found Light Healing to be the most efficient spell to use to heal party members amid battle situations. Using more experienced spells can consume too much mana, leaving the Cleric without the ability to aid others.

Cure Blindness - This spell is one that most Cleric's do not keep memorized, but always remember its there. It is highly unlikely that you will need to do a battlefield cure of this obscure disease, but you may find the need to help one has recently been stricken.

Cure Disease - Most diseases will be caused by disease carrying animals. However, since these diseases can very quickly ravage the body of the victim, it is best to have this spell pre- memorized and ready for use whenever you feel the chances of a disease being caught are high.

Rank IV

Healing - The pinnacle of a Cleric's training into the ability to heal others. This spell, while absorbing a tremondous amount of mana, can nearly completely heal most injured souls. However, as a battlefield healing spell it should be used with caution. The use of such a spell will often leave the cleric drained of important mana that could be used more importantly elsewhere.

Section II - The Cleric vs. Undead
Next, let us deal with the Cleric's ability to combat the undead creatures of Norrath.
Rank I
Spook the Undead - The first spell in this category that the Cleric learns doesn't really help to combat the undead directly, but can still be extremely valuable in this fight if properly applied. When a Cleric is amid large numbers of these creatures doing battle, he or she can quickly become overwhelmed. Proper application of this spell can help the Cleric to scare away extra targets so he or she can concentrate on just one or two.

Rank II

Ward Undead - The Cleric's first step into the ability to deliver great damage to the undead. Ward Undead, even as a lower level spell, should not be underestimated. In addition, when mana is low, or your target is nearly dead, it is good to be able to fall back onto this spell and not waste the larger amounts of mana required for the more devastating spell Expulse Undead.

Rank III

Sense the Dead - A basic spell for a basic purpose, Sense the Dead makes is easier for the Cleric on such a quest to locate his or her potential targets.

Rank IV

Expulse Undead - The mainstay of a Cleric's fight, Expulse Undead can do devastating amounts of damage to these creatures. It should not, however, be over used. With such a large expenditure of mana required, one should always consider whether or not the extra firepower is required. Never be ashamed to fall back on Ward Undead.

Invisibility to Undead - This spell can allow the Cleric to wander untouched through a sea of undead creatures. This spell is best used to scout out your potential targets. Then through careful use of attacks and Spook the Undead you can control the pace of the battle.
