Here, from my experiences, are the various faction standing levels, from best to worst, and the effects they give..

..looks upon you warmly: The highest faction possible, the final goal of some quests is to attain this faction to get your reward.

...kindly considers you: The forgotten faction level, between warmly and amiably, besides that nothing special.

...judges you amiably: Another "nice" rank, but besides that nothing special.

...regards you indifferently: They don't really like you as much, but will still sell to you.

...looks at you apprehensively: Not so happy with you, they charge extra money to sell to/buy from them, but still will do business.

...glowers at you dubiously: Won't do business with you, but won't attack. Also usually won't talk aside from insults.

...glares at you threateningly: Won't do business and might attack, though the latter isn't definite if you move by quickly.

...scowls at you, ready to attack: D'uh.

Now, while I've definitely noticed a price hit for apprehensive shopkeeps, I have never ever seen a price benefit from having high faction.. with my old dwarf rogue character I made it all the way to warmly with the warrior's guild, and was still indifferent to the merchants, and the warrior's guild merchant (who looked at me warmly) offered items for the same price as all the other indifferent merchants. However, your race/class does have a small effect on prices no matter the faction standing, you'll always get the best prices for selling or buying within your own race, even when illusion magic is employed to deal with other races. At least, that's been my experience. If there's anyone out there who can correct me on any of this (yeah right, like I'm ever wrong) please do so. :)
