Introduction

So you've decided to play a rogue. Backstabs are effective, and sneak and hide can be a blast, and it's generally a fun class to play. Personally, I find playing a rogue more fun than any other class. I have started several casting characters, and become bored by level 10. So melee it is, with a twist.

My rogue is currently level 11, so this guide cannot be totally accurate past that level. Also, this is the way *I* chose to play, and is only a general guideline for this class/race combination. So, without further ado:

Your Job and Equipment

As a rogue, you are able to wear most non-plate armor. You can use slashing and piercing weapons, but if you choose to single wield, use a piercing weapon. With Dual Wield, always keep a piercing weapon in your primary (left) weapon slot. You cannot backstab without piercing, and it works off of your primary slot. Feel free to use any kind of weapon you want in the off hand, just keep your piercing skill high.

Your job as a Rogue is to be a damage dealer. Due to the lack of strong armor, you are not really a tank. You do not have Taunt either. You may be asked to tank, but if possible, leave it to the Warriors, Paladins, and Shadow Knights. Concentrate on your offense, but try to keep your AC up too.

Important Stats

Strength - How much you can carry, also how hard you hit. Especially affects your backstabs.

Agility - How often you get hit, how often enemies miss. However, given a choice between agility and AC, go with the AC, you'll take less damage.

Dexterity - The most important Rogue stat. It controls how fast you learn all of your rogue specific skills. It also determines how often you hit, and how often magical weapon effects go off.

Stamina is not too important, because you are not a tank. Intelligence and wisdom aren't important either, and charisma may not be important because you can always get an indifferent /con from an NPC with sneak (see below).

Stats seem to go in quarters, meaning you'll see your ATK rating go up more if you raise your strength from 74 to 75, than from 79 to 80. So aim to get your stats to the 75, 100, and 125 milestones. Remember, at later levels, items will make everyone have near the same stats.

Race choices

  • Halfling:
    • Pros - Traditionally, the race with roguish tendencies, best starting agility and dexterity. Good starting zone. Twinkers' favorite.

    • Cons - Everyone and their brother plays a halfling rogue, it's the most popular race. Low starting strength.

    • Recommendations - Put all the bonus points you can into strength.

  • Barbarian:
    • Pros - Best starting strength, good agility, looks cool.

    • Cons - Being big can sometimes be a problem. Low starting dexterity. No night vision. This is the second most popular race for Rogues.

    • Recommendations - Put all starting points you can into dexterity, leftover into strength.

  • Dark Elf:
    • Pros - Looks very cool. Fits, if you are a role player. Good agility and dexterity. You get racial Night Vision.

    • Cons - Tied for lowest strength with Wood Elves, and Gnomes. Evil race. You will have a bad starting zone, with an inconvenient location. Kill on sight many places.

    • Recommendations - Beef up the strength.

  • Wood Elf:
    • Pros - Very good dexterity and agility. You are short and cute. You get racial Night Vision. You have a popular starting zone, and the racial Forage ability.

    • Cons - Start with low strength. Starting zone has the most public chat usage in the entire game. You will get hit on if you play a female.

    • Recommendations - Raise the strength, go play in Butcher Block.

  • Dwarf:
    • Pros - Great strength, stamina, and dexterity. Start with racial Infravision, Sense Heading in one of the best starting zones.

    • Cons - You have low agility. The women have beards.

    • Recommendations - Lay on the agility.

  • Half Elf:
    • Pros - No stat is incredibly low, agility and dexterity are very good.

    • Cons - Start with somewhat low strength.

    • Recommendations - I think you know what I'm going to say. Go with the Strength.

  • Human:
    • Pros - Very average

    • Cons - Very average. No night vision.

    • Recommendations - Split points equally between strength, agility, and dexterity.

  • Gnome:
    • Pros - Very good agility and dexterity. With a helmet, looks like a little ninja. This is a very rare class/race combo. Racial Infravision, and Tinkering is a gnome only trade skill.

    • Cons - Lowest starting strength. Starting zone is awful.

    • Recommendations - Put all the points you can into strength, and go to play in Faydark or Butcher

I decided to play Gnome, because the combination was so unique, and you really can play any race to 50 relatively easily.

Skills

  • Sneak: You get this at level 3. It lowers the agro of NPCs and mobs you stand behind. Your speed slows to a crawl when doing this, but you can move. This can be used in conjunction with Hide, so you can move while invisible. With this, you can move in cites where you are kill on sight, and buy and sell to NPCs, as they will now /con indifferent to you. Put one training point into it, and then practice a lot.

  • Hide: You get this at level 4. It makes you invisible to living *and* undead. Does not work on a few high level NPCs. Looting a corpse, attacking, and getting a spell cast on you (including Bard songs) will make you visible. Only works while standing still, unless used in conjunction with Sneak. You can use the /corpse command to pull your corpse to a safe location while staying invisible. Put one training point into it, and practice a lot.

  • Evade: A new skill rogues were given to balance the fact that the lockpicking and trap skills are near useless. It is available once you get hide. Evade is not a trainable skill per se. As your sneak/hide skills get better, it becomes more effective. To use it, turn off your attack, and press hide. If it works, you are lowered on the NPC's "hate list", and if there is someone else around, the monster will go attack him or her. This is very useful if you do too much damage, and the tank can't taunt the monster off of you.

  • Pickpocket: This skill becomes available at level 7. This is a very cool skill, for it allows you to steal money from the your enemies. It is accomplished by pressing the "pickpocket" button when not attacking. This can be practiced on all mobs, but will only give money from the vaguely humanoid ones, such as orcs and gnolls (i.e. those that might have pockets on them). Using this skill will make the NPC attack you, which is why it is unwise to use it on townspeople and guards. Make sure you can beat whatever mob you are pick pocketing from, unless of course you are stealing from someone else's kill. If that is the case, watch out for NPCs that can speak, like guards other people are fighting, and dervishes, because the will alert that you are trying to steal. This skill does take from the pool of possible loot the enemy drops, so it is considered bad form to loot while in a group. However, if someone steals your kill, follow him or her and pick the monster clean. Put one training point into it, and practice it.

  • Backstab: This is your defining skill, achieved at level 10. You may want to save up the majority of your practice points up till now, and then blow them all here. It is worth it, for your backstab is typically five or six times your weapon damage. Backstab is a piercing only attack that can only be done from behind the enemy. You will spend most of your group life opposite the group's main tank. In groups, try to make all the tanks stand on one side, so you do not have to keep turning. The three things that most affect how much you backstab for are you skill in it, your strength, and your weapon damage. As you achieve higher levels, you will find yourself looking for strength items more than anything, and higher damage weapons. A level 50 rogue with 200 or so strength, and a 13-damage weapon can hit for over 300 per backstab. It should be noted that monsters hate to be backstabbed, even if you miss. It is one of the most effective taunts in the game, even if you don't want it to be.

  • Safe Fall: Level 12. This skill can help you survive those nasty accidental falls (Dagnor's Cauldron...). While the skill cap is lower than the one on monks, this is still a useful skill. The best way to train it is to find one of those steep hills, that you can fall down and repeatedly get hit for 1 HP of damage five times on the way down. Just keep falling down the hill, and the skill should go up fairly quickly.

  • Dual Wield: Level 13. This allows you to wield a second weapon in place of a shield. This will not, however, double your damage output. You hit with both weapons more often as your skill goes up. You can use any weapon you would like in the second slot, it doesn't have to be piercing. Many higher-level rogues use a procing (effect) weapon in this slot. Put only one point into this skill, and it will go up on its own.

  • Double Attack: Level 16. This skill gives you the chance of hitting a second time each round, per weapon. This leads to a possible four attack round. This skill will rise by itself, after the starting point is placed into the skill.

  • Throwing: This is an under-utilized skill. While other classes get throwing, and Warriors can get critical throws, only a rogue can get a Deadly Strike. A Deadly Strike has a chance of occurring when you throw something at a monster's back. It does much more damage than a critical hit. Critical and DS's have a better chance of happening with a high dexterity and throwing skill.

  • Pick Locks/Disarm Traps/Sense Traps: I do not know much of these skills, as they are generally broken. Verant decided they couldn't implement traps way back before they released the game, and pre-expansion, and there are less than ten lock-pickable doors in the entire game. One of the only zones where this skill is useful is Befallen, which has three locked doors. You need a lockpicking kit from a vendor to accomplish the skill. It will go up with practice.

  • Instill Doubt: I do not know much about this skill, except that it works like a Fear spell.

The Mask of Deception

I thought I'd put something about this item in this guide. This mask is a Bard and Rogue only No Drop item. It is the common drop off the Froglok Assassin in Lower Guk, which also drops the Serrated Bone Dirk (one of the best rogue weapons). The mask adds 13 to your charisma, and, starting at level 15, lets you cast Illusion: Dark Elf. This will make you Gnomes and Halflings taller, or Barbarians more manageable. Of course, you should always /con guards and NPC's, because the illusion changes your faction with good and bad cities. Most of the dark elf rogues you see around are that in name only. The reason that I put this in, is that while Lower Guk is a high level (40-50) dungeon, this mask can be obtained as low as level 4. If you have never killed a froglok, this is very simple. Put all your equipment and money in the bank, and run down to Innothule. Go into Upper Guk, and you should be able to run around with impunity (assuming you haven't killed a froglok). You should always /con, just to be safe.

Once in Upper Guk, find the live side entrance to Lower Guk, and zone in. Nothing on the live side except gargoyles will attack you, but watch for wandering undead frogs, which will. Find the exit to the dead side, and go into sneak/hide mode. Ask directions to the supply room, where the Assassin spawns. This being Lower Guk, he is perpetually camped. Since the mask is No Drop, and bard and rogue only, the groups usually destroy it. Ask if you can loot it if it drops, and sit down and wait. I got my mask at level 10, and the Assassin spawned within five minutes of me getting there. This was pure luck; you may have to wait hours. Backtrack to leave, once you have you mask. I'd recommend you wait until a peak time to try this.

With sneak/hide, and the mask, a Rogue can go near anywhere.

Ravenscale Armor

Rogue class-specific mid-level armor is not made of quest items, unlike most classes. It drops off named spawns in the Cazic-Thule dungeon. Most are very hard, especially the Chest Guard, which is dropped off the Avatar of Fear himself. The zone itself should not be attempted by anyone below 20, preferably 25.

Places to Hunt (Recommendations)

  • Levels 1-6:
    Don't leave your starting zone, unless you are going to another one. Kill low-level enemies, and loot everything that is stackable. At your level, 20 of anything will be decent money. Solo, you really don't get much from a group at this level.

  • Levels 7-14:
    Find your local low-level dungeon. These are usually Blackburrow, Befallen, and Crushbone. You could also hunt wisps at around level ten, but you need a magic weapon to hit them. Starting at level ten, you really need to be in a group, especially since you get backstab.

  • Levels 14-17:
    You can do the castle in Crushbone, or most anywhere in Oasis. I know Oasis is overcrowded, but it's a huge zone. The "Orc Highway" alone can easily support 3 groups.

  • Levels 18-20:
    You have many choices. You can stretch the Crushbone throne room for a few more levels, as it's good money, or you can go to Lavastorm. You can hunt crocs and caimans in Oasis. You can try the yard in Unrest. My personal favorite was the orc spawn in Highpass. It is usually camped by two groups, but if you can get in at an off-peak time, there is only one. It is one of the fastest spawns in the game, and usually spawns at least two orcs at a time. The loot is primarily money, up to and including some platinum, bronze weapons, and ring armor. I made 3 levels in as many days there.

  • Levels 20+:
    From now on, you can hunt most zones in the game. Go wherever you feel like. I like Unrest, at low twenties with my main character, but go explore some other zones.

Well, there's my guide on playing a rogue. I'd like to thank all the high level rogues, and rogue message boards which I've pestered for knowledge. I hope this guide is useful to you, and you have fun with the class. Remember that a warrior, cleric and rogue group is a very effective three-man group, and don't let anyone know that you are pick pocketing. Remember that with sneak/hide you can do corpse retrievals for other people, and that you are not a tank.

Have fun.
