How many hours have they been playing their characters? That will give a much closer idea of whether the rates of advance are off or not, rather than the amount of time since release. It's quite possible if it's all you do for you to play the game 100+ hours a week, which means some of these folks could have put in several hundred hours on their characters. That's the only way I can see this is possible.

As for it being faster now than in beta, it simply is for many of us who did beta test the game. My bard is now approaching ninth level after a mere 36 hours or so of play spread over just a week, whereas it took me about three weeks of play to get her that high in beta. I knew where to go and what to do to get her there this time, which sped things up tremendously. I expect the rate of advance to go down radically from here on out, since I won't have done it all once (or more than once--I've also switched servers once since commercial release) before.

If they are abusing the quest features to get there, though, you're right that they need to do something about it. I feel a little funny myself doing the Songweavers' spider silk quest over and over again, but it doesn't pay that well (a little less than 4 gp per completion); if there's a big ticket item quest that can be easily completed over and over again, they need to fix it.
