I agree with you on most points. I do not agree you should be in a heavy concentrated area just casting away heals and expecting compensation. Most of the ones being healed are too busy to properly thank anyone or know from which way the heals are coming from. I've been on both sides.

On the receiving end, when I have been healed in a heated battle, by the time I recgonize what has happened I can't locate my benefactor to thank. Others don't believe they needed the aid to take the monster out. I always compensate a healer if there is time, but thats me.

On the giving end, if I'm in a group I do not pass out heals or anything when near a dangerous location. I've been burned by this, someone runs up near death fighting something he should not be fighting, I feel sorry for him and heal him up, he thanks me and runs off. Then right after that we get attacked and we lose a member because I am short on mana. Now, I simply make it a policy not to aid outsiders when I am a member of a group. If it is safe to do so, they will definately pay for the services. If I am alone, I only respond to polite request. I roleplay a somewhat godlike healer who carries the live or die potential in his hand. Therefore I feel anyone receiving heals or cures from me are less than deserving and if they are rude I berate them as they die in front of me for not showing more respect. They do not pester me now and if the need arises and they are quite polite. (Or the hand of God turns away)
