 Ak'Anon

Ak'Anon is located beneath the Steamfont Mountains of Faydwer and is home to the mysterious Gnomes and their clockwork minions. Coated in both metal and steam, the caverns of Ak'Anon are filled with mechanical marvels that few men have laid eyes upon. There exist two exits from these winding caverns; one leading to the dungeon underneath the city, the other back to the surface.

 Felwithe

Nestled in the hills of the Greater Faydark, Felwithe is the home of the High Elves. Lakes and trees surround this city, and vines and blossoming flowers bedeck nearly every wall of its Northern half. Gazing over its lower portions from an elevated vantage point is Southern Felwithe, comprised primarily of guildhalls joined together by teleportation platforms. The city's single entrance is marked by a large stone bridge and a towering gate of marble and gold.

Researcher: Malketh, Cartographer: Malketh, Author: Den

 Freeport

East
 The most densely-populated human settlement of Norrath, Freeport stands as a bleak testament to mans' will against nature. Placed on the edge of the desolate Commonlands of Antonica, this port city is home to not only the humans that created it, but to a variety of other displaced races as well. Folk of all sorts have been seen passing through its streets and harbors, and the bustling crowds are just a facet of this huge city's facade. Four quarters--each correlating with a navigable direction--comprise Freeport, and exits from the city lead to both the scrublands outside its walls as well as the seaward path to the continent Faydwer.

 Grobb

Within the fetid swamps of lower Antonica exists the hovel that the Trollkin call "hum". The cries of the near-deceased echo throught this wooden and mud city of death, and glowing red eyes peer from every shadow. The only chance of safety comes in the form of a Troll, if it be your reflection. Grobb's single route of escape leads only deeper into Innothule Swamp.

Researcher: Bohica, Cartographer: Bohica, Author: Den
 top


 Halas

Within the snowy wastes of Everfrost lies the Barbarian settlement, Halas. Access to the city is limited to a single rickety raft that floats over an enclosed lake of freezing water, which in turn is reached through a cave etched into a mountain of stone. If one survives the cold of the path and the imposing entrance to Halas, they will find this city of snow to be every bit as stony and rough as the lands surrounding it.

Researcher: EQ-Online and Den, Cartographer: Den, Author: Den
 top


 Kaladim

As much a part of the mountains of Butcherblock as the rock itself, the home of the Dwarves lies betwixt the land of the surface-dwellers and of the domain of the earth itself. Guarded by a huge sentinel of stone, the entrance of Kaladim is impenetrable to all but those who are openly welcome by the Stout-kind. Kaladim is the bastion of order and solidarity on the continent of Faydwer, and joins together both the roads and peoples of that landmass.

Researcher: Jektal, Cartographer: Jektal, Author: Den

Kelethin
Haven of the Wood Elves and their halfbreed kin, Kelethin is mounted atop the ancient trees of the Greater Faydark and provides only a glimmer of light within its shadowed depths. Maps of Kelethin

 Neriak

3rd Gate
 Hidden beneath the north-easternmost corner of the continent Antonica, Neriak is the sprawling network of caverns that the Dark Elven call home. The Teir'Dal and their ghoul minions bring to the underground complex an aura of pure evil and darkness, while the moisture rising from the underground streams that wind through the city bring further discomfort to non-indigenous travelers. Glyphs of fire mark the walls and even the very air that comprise Neriak's stony depths.

Qeynos
