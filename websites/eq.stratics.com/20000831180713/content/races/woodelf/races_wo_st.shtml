Starting as a Wood Elf

Why Play a Wood Elf?
This really is a good question, and before you start making a character, you really should answer it. If you want to design a good character concept, this should really be the first thing that you ask yourself, and the first thing that you answer. Well why would you want to play a Wood Elf?

There are a number of reasons, especially if you choose to play a Ranger or Druid. Both of those classes spend 95% or more of their time outdoors. In fact, most stories will portray both of these classes as characters that dont like cities. This makes the Wood Elf homeland perfect for them, because it isnt so much a city, as a collection of Tree houses that suddenly appear out of the forest. There are other reasons to play Wood Elves depending on the type of class that you want to play, but part of the fun creating a character is designing a good concept, so I will leave that to you.

Getting Around Kelethin
Watch that first step! Kelethin has got to be the most dangerous city to walk around in as a newbie. This became very evident to me last night, the opening night for the Final Release of EverQuest. I had just finished making my character, got all of my quick keys set, and headed out to meet with a few of my friends from the Beta, when I see three people fall out of the trees and make very liquid splashes on the forest floor. (Ok, so there isnt THAT much realism in EQ, but that would make corpse retrievals hard anyway). So if this is your very first visit to the land of Norrath be very careful, walk everywhere in the City of Trees. For that matter, if this is your first visit to Kelethin, watch your step, that land comes up really fast.

As far as where to go in Kelethin, there is a really nice map in the back of the book that comes with the game, and some fine cartographers have put in the time during the beta to map out the city. Kelethin has several taverns (getting drunk will make it that much easier to fall out of the trees, just warning you), a nice bank, and several different guildhalls. There are three different safe exits out of the city, and they are each nicely made cargo elevators. It might sound strange to call it that, but really once you see one, that is what comes to your mind, you could easily cram 15-20 people on one of them.

Once you step off of the lift, be warned you just entered into Greater Faydark, the most crowded zone in the game currently.

Look for the Moss
Greater Faydark is definitely one of the most crowded areas in the game, and especially now that everyone has to start over. This particular zone seems to be the largest to me, but that might just be because it is pretty easy to get lost in Faydark. The trees pretty much all look the same, there is a constant fog, and to top it all off, of the three major land marks, being near one will probably get you killed at lower levels.

All connected to this zone is the entrance into Felwithe, the entrance to Crushbone, and Kelethin is in the treetops. The best way to make sure that you dont get lost is to spend some time and figure out where Kelethin is in the map compared to the direction that you head off in. Use the skill Sense Heading until you get a response and set off in the opposite direction. Keep using that skill whenever you can, and try to watch out for those Orcs. I spent probably 1/4 of my time in the Beta around Faydark, and I still get lost a little in it, so I really cant stress the importance of trying to find some unique way to figure out how to get around that works for you. Just remember that moss doesnt seem to grow on the fine trees of the Greater Faydark.

Them Bones, Them Bones, Them Crushbones
This will be a great place for you to test your skills once you start to get above 5th level. I wouldnt suggest actually going into Crushbone by yourself, and probably not without a good group for a little while. However, there is a nice Orc camp just outside of Crushbone, and if you are careful you can start to rid your homelands from the worse enemy there could be. If you arent the adventuresome type or if you just feel that a good Wood Elf would protect his homelands, you could probably get all the way up into the high teens without ever really leaving the area. Now I am in no way suggesting this, in fact I only played an elf for about one month in the beta, the rest of the time I was leaving MY hometown to go play in Faydark. This game is called EverQuest, and the only way it will be an Everlasting Quest for you, is if you go out and Quest.

I can remember the many times that I have seen high level Wood Elves running around with armor on and killing all of the high level creatures in the area. They really do look very nice in armor, males and females, and they even take a piece of their forest with them on their armor.

Views on Other Races
Really this is just what I think that Wood Elves would feel against other races, and has no substance other then my own thoughts.
  • Dark Elves: Come on, how can any surface Elf get along with a Dark Elf? You might not feel that they deserve to be killed on sight, but you would definitely spend no time around them.
  • High Elves: They are your cousins, unfortunately, they are just a bit misguided in their view. Tunare made this part of the world beautiful naturally, why build a city where nature could grow?
  • Ogres and Trolls: Ugly foul creatures. While they dont hate them to the extent that they would Dark Elves, there is definitely no love lost between these races.
  • Barbarians, Humans, and Erudites: This would really be on an individual basis, just like how you judge people in real life.
  • Gnomes, Halflings, and Dwarves: Again this would be on an individual basis, but given the proximity to the Gnomes and Dwarves, you would probably be friendly with them. On the same side, they have some very different habits then Wood Elves. Gnomes have all of their crazy inventions they use, and for some reason those Crazy Dwarves like spending time underground!
That just about sums up my view on the Wood Elves. While I am sure others have a different outlook on them, I think that this portrays a pretty good overall on the race, and how they react to their surroundings. I look forward to adventuring with you all in the game.

"Cousin elf, why is it that you live locked behind cold Grey walls instead of in the openness of the forests? Is it perhaps because in your pursuit to sharpen your mind, you have let your spirit grow weak?" -Galeiwyn on High Elves
