Thursday, June 7
Sullon Zek Updates
GeneralNews [ 7:52 PM EDT ] - Witt
Absor has responded on the Developer's Corner to some of the comments he received after yesterdays announcement about the new Deity PvP server Sullon Zek. His original comments can be found here.

On intra-team communication :
Will intra-team communication be impeded in any way? (removal of Common tongue?)

No. We considered it but decided we didn't want to do too many things at once. Not to mention, there's a real question among the team as to how this would add to gameplay. We aren't sure it will. We'd be happy to entertain arguments on the subject for the RP server which is coming up next.
On the lack of a level limit for PvP :
My only complaint? That ANYONE can kill a level 6 player.

Grief players are going to flock to this server, level up at light speed, and spend their nights in lowbie/newbie zones AOE killing low level players... sorry, but I seriously disagree with this one.

That may very well happen. But we also anticipate that if it does, people will jump at the chance to defend their teammates. Picture it like this. Someone Innoruuk follower decides to wipe out the newbie humans in the Commons. He blasts ten or twenty of them only to find that he's being attacked by two humans that are hurting him. He ends up getting killed by them, and losing experience.

Will he come back to pester the newbies again alone, or will he find friends and make a war on the guys that killed him?
On the possibility of a movelog :
Will there be a move log for this server?

Nope.
and finally :
- The environment WILL cause experience loss. If you jump off a cliff and die, you will lose experience.

- Naked corpses WILL disappear when looted by a player character. If you are naked and killed by a player character, your corpse will disappear when it is looted.
Have your own say on this topic here.

New PvP server open for testing
GeneralNews [ 3:15 AM EDT ] - Witt
The full rules and details about the new Team PvP server Sullon Zek have been announced over at Developer's Corner. The full story on this can be found here.
We plan to have the new PvP Deity Teams server up and running some time tomorrow. I also have the name of the new server and the basic rule set for you below.

What we'll be doing is putting the new server up as a Test server for a short while, a few weeks or it may only be a few days, depending on how things go. In order to play on the server during the testing period you'll have to log in using testeverquest.exe. At the end of the test period we will be wiping all characters from the new server in order to restart it as a Live server.

The server will be open to anyone that wants to help us test it. You'll have to understand that the standard Test Server disclaimers will apply (see the note below). And our Development Team and Testing Teams will be running around on the server. Please cooperate with them and don't bother them with requests.
He also gave some details on the rule set for the new server.
Sullon Zek rules:

1. Player v. Player (PvP) combat can occur between any characters that are not on the same team, regardless of level. A level 50 character can attack and kill a level 6 character if that character is not a member of his alliance. But rules 2 and 3 apply.

2. Characters will be immune to PvP combat until they have gained enough strength to survive the struggle. Characters below 6th level are not able to participate in PvP combat. The first time a character zones after gaining 6th level he or she will then become PvP enabled.

3. Characters will lose experience when killed in PvP combat if the character that killed them is within 5 levels. The amount of experience lost will be the same amount that is lost if that character is killed by an NPC. Your corpse can be resurrected by a cleric to partially restore lost experience.

4. Characters will only be able to loot coins from other characters, and then only if the victim was within 5 levels of the killer. Only the character getting the killing blow will be able to loot the corpse.

5. Players may only have one character at a time on this server. This means that you will have to delete the existing character in order to create a new one. Because we are introducing a new class and a new race with the release of Shadows of Luclin we plan to make allowances at that time for those that wish to explore these new characters.

6. Characters will not be able to group with anyone that is not on their Deity Team.

7. Characters will not be able to invite someone to join their guild that is not on their Deity Team.

8. Combat skills' effective value is capped at 2 levels above a players current level.

9. Resistance debuff spells have a 50% increased effectiveness against player characters.

10. /consider returns only three kinds of results when used against player characters. 'Green' means that the character is below your range. 'White' or 'black' means that the character is within your range. 'Red' means that they are above your range. 'In range' means +/- 5 levels.

11. /consider will also display a message indicating what team the target is on. Members of your own team will be displayed as an 'ally', members of another team will be shown as 'threateningly'.

12. Faction hits for PvP will be taken for killing characters within their racial home towns. Killing a human in any human starting city will cause a faction loss with the locals. Freeport, Qeynos and Surefall Glade are considered starting cities for humans. All of Greater Faydark and Felwithe are considered the starting city for all elves (high elves, wood elves and half elves), except dark elves. Paineel and Erudin are both starting cities for erudites, and killing one in either city (regardless of their place of origin) will result in a faction loss with that city. Other cities are not considered home towns, despite being run and controlled by a certain race. For example, HighHold is not a human home town. Thurgadin is not a dwarven home town.

13. Rules such as the Play Nice Policy do not apply to this server. Characters have the ability on these servers to deal with their conflicts through combat. Causing experience loss by 'training' NPCs on other characters is not an offense that would warrant a warning on PvP servers. HOWEVER: All rules regarding general decency still apply. Cursing, threats and other such things will be dealt with sternly, and are still unacceptable behavior on PvP servers.

14. Beneficial effects can not be used on members of another team.

15. No character will be able to bind in dungeon zones.

16. Beneficial spells will not affect characters more than 20 levels lower than the spell level. Existing spell restrictions on spells over 50th level will supercede this rule.

17. There will be 'insignia' dropped on the corpses of characters over 20th level that are killed by another character. We plan to allow those to be turned in for a reward of some sort. What that reward will be is undetermined at this time, but we are leaning towards a team reward rather than a personal reward.
You can discuss your thoughts on this new server on our forums.

Kevdawg_the_Rapper had some concerns about the recent 1.5x resist debuff change in the recent patch.
The 1.5 resist debuff was a bad idea. as a melee i use resist to help protect my self from the casters. its bad enough for meele casses with all the unresistable spells casters have and the eggshaped prumice nerf , but now Root ,snare, and blind spells can can stick now with 150+ resist. anyways befor you ruin pvp anymore how about telling or asking us what you going to change befor you put it live

Well, it's a fine line there. As it is now a melee character can make him or herself nearly immune to all enchanter spells with high resists (and high resists are probably much easier to get than they really should be).

If I were to take a poll on this change, I promise you I would get a yes from every caster and a no from every melee (as a rough generalization). I suspect I could simply count the number of each type of character on the server over level 40 or so and that would fairly approximate the results of a poll on the matter.

We've examined the numbers and looked at it carefully, and for now we're sticking with this change. It may indeed turn out to be too much of a change. It also may turn out to be too little of a change (as most enchanters would probably tell you).

Shadow Knight and Necro Spell Issues
GeneralNews [ 2:10 PM EDT ] - Witt
Lawrence Poe responded to some minor concerns about some Shadow Knight and Necromancer spells over in the Developer's Corner today. The original article can be found here.
Since spell issues are all the rage, I have a couple small nits I'd love to see fixed.

1) Feign Death doesn't give any indication to the caster that it's worked. In heavy combat it can be hard to tell if it when off or not. The caster is told if the spell didn't work "Soandso has fallen to the ground." and if it does work *others* see "Soandso dies.". If would be nice if the caster got the "dies" message. A spell icon would be even better, but I can see where that might be a problem if you already had 15 effects on you.

2) Tap type spells wear off twice. Spells like Siphon Strength and Shroud of Hate have their own message when they wear of. But you also get the stock yellow "Your xyzzy spell has worn off.". For example:

Your Shroud of Hate spell has worn off.
The hatred departs.

It might make sense to get the yellow message if the MOB you cast the spell on was still alive, but you get it when they are long dead as well. However if you zone before the spell wears off you get only the spell's message and not the yellow one

3) Dead Man Floating doesn't stack with Deadeye (self only see invs/infravision). Both have see invs, which might explain it, but the See Invisible spell does stack with Deadeye and I'm pretty sure that Infravision stacks with DMF. It all seems pretty odd. It is true that DMF should not overwrite Deadeye as infravision would be lost.

Thanks for looking at these!

Mynegon
Lawrence responded with:
1) Having played a high level Shadow Knight, I agree there are times when I will not notice my spell going off. However when I get into situations where I think I'm going to need to FD/DP I just pop into 3rd person mode so I can see my character.

2) I'm sure you understand why you get two messages. The effect is on you, and the NPC. When it wears off it wears off from both of you which is why the double message. That being said I doubt we will get this changed any time soon.

3)These spells should stack with each other. I'll modify Deadeye and throw it up on test and see if any other stacking issues arise from the change.

- Lawrence Poe
If you have any comments on any of these issues you can submit them here.
