The Iksar Monk

Levels 1-3
Levels 4-6
Levels 7-10

Skills
Tips


Level 1-3
You start out right in front of the monk guild so its no big deal to find your first quest(give note to guildmaster) and train with him. (i'd train mend because it heals 25% of your total hps and at low levels of mend you can hurt yourself with it(25% of you current hps in damage). Train mend for all these levels. Make sure you put 1 point into dodge, it wont go up unless you do.At level 3 you might wanna put 1 practice into safefall but its no big deal). Go to the merchant across the way from the monk guild....ask him the right questions and he'll give you a backpack to fill with curscale hides. Go to the field of bone and follow the road unntil it drops down innnto a desert like area. Kill pups and decaying skeletons(there is a guard at entrance to field that will give you faction,exp and a small bit of money for 4 bone chips) at level 2 you can start killing scorpions and beetle types. When you are full of hides combine them and take the new bag back to the merchant he'll give you a piece of nice light armor and you can do the quest again for a different piece(the piece is random so you can get the same type more than once) By the time you are level 4 you should have 4-5 pieces of armor)

 top

Level 4-6
Go to the Lake of Ill Omen((zone is in west cabalis) go just past the main buildings area and kill goblin whelps/scouts and sabertooth kittens. At level 5 on the right side of the area past the buildings kill skeletons/sabertooth cubs and goblin watchers((careful though the watchers tend to call other goblins to help them). You can also at this point do your monk quest. Get a large scorpion pincer from the field of bones(ask someone nicely for one), a skippinng stonne from the swamp(again ask for one....i looked for a hour or so and couldnt find the mob with one :)), a goblin scout beads(from lake of ill omen) and a sabertooth cub canine(also from the lake of ill omen) and you can get your 2nd shackle.

Train mend at level 4 with all pracs and 1 round kick and rest mend at level 5.(at level 5 i started to use mend a bit to raise it up some. it healed me like 4 times and hurt me 2 times and didnt work the rest of the time(like 10 times))

 top

Level 7-10
Just past the first area bit in the Lake of Ill Omen(where you kill kittens and scouts) around loc plus 5300 neg 4300 is a building....all around it are skeletons(level 4-6) iskar footpads(level 5-7 or 8) and sharnak broodlings ((the only one i saw conned blacck/white to me at levell 6) also an occasional goblin hunter(conned yellow at level 5). All these should be good enough to get you to level 9. I had some money saved up so i spent my pracs on mend again at level 6 though it does start costing copper and silver after skill level 20 so its purely optional to use it...you can use mend once every 5 min and if you constantly use it its fairly easy to raise.

I plan to put 1 pracc into sneak at level 8 and just use it to raise it up Make sure to get Tiger Strike at level 10 its another combat skill(like round kick) it does a bit less damage((10 max to round kicks 13) but you can get in 2 strikes to 1 roundkick.

 top

Level 11-?
At this point i am in The Field of Bone and either fighting solo in the pit area or fiighting with a group in Kurn's Tower.
At level 14 or so you can either stay in the The Field of Bone area or you can go the Overthere and catch a boat to the Oasis which is a good place to solo or group into the low 20's.(the only problem with this option is that you'lll have to go back to Cebilis to train as you will be KOS to every(or nearly) NPC off of kunark) After that you have several good options.....back to kunark and kill in the Sarnak Fortress area(lake of ill omen) or you could go to South Karana and kill aviaks or treants.You should be experinced enough at this point that you dont need a lot of help with what to do. Practice Bind Wound as it'll help you(and your groups) spend less time waiting around to heal. At level 12 make sure to learn Block but only spend 1 practice point in it(like dodge it'll go up automaticly. After you get bind wound up to 20 you'll need to decide what to do with your practice points because there is no other combat skill the monk get that you want to put more than 1 prac into. I'd suggest getting into tailoring the supplies for it are fairly light(except the pelts or animals) but if you can use them right away.With spider silks you can make silk armor which is a fairly light armor(0.3-0.5 weight per piece) which monks can wear.

Out of all the classes the monk is the class (except for warrrior) that'll have the most practice points to spend at level 60 :)

 top

SKILLS
Hand-to-Hand or 1 Hand Blunt?
This all depends(for me anyway) on how much money you got....if you have 250-500pp to spend on weapons, i'd go with 1 Hand Blunt. Get a couple of Cold Iron Morning Stars or Enameled Black Maces and the monk is hard to beat. You can occasionally solo yellow(at least at low levels) especially if mend works :)

Hand-to-Hand starts out at a measly 4 damge(at level 1) and slowing raises(1 per 5 levels starting at level 5) and the delay for hands(36) doesnt starting getting better until level 29 or so and does so slowly(even more slowly for iksar monks) So take your CIMS(cold iron morning star) whicch is 9 damage and 35 delay. you wont equal that as a monk until level 25 and the EB mace is 8 damage and 28 delay which makes it more attractive that a CIMS because it swings so much faster.

Kick, Round Kick, Tiger Strike, Eagle Strike, Flying Kick and Dragon Punch
Always get the latest one when you reach the level for it. it'll generally do more damage or be faster than the last one.

Dodge, Block and Riposte
Must haves, these skills are automaticly used in combat get them as soon as you reach the level needed(you start with dodge, block at level 12 and riposte at level 35) riposte does what you expect block the monsters hit and allows you a free hit on them.

Feign Death
Easily the best skill in the game....soloing or in groups....soloing something and its about to kill you? Feign Death will make it go away. Wanting to pull for your group? Feign Death allows you to pull whatever monster you want(if you do it right) without bringing any ffriends with it.

Mend
2nd best skill in the game it'll heal you(and only you) of 25% of your max hps. At mid levels this is the best healing skill/spell in the game

Bind Wound
Another good skill to have monks are the only(i think) class that can raise this skill to 200. It heals 1/4 of the skill level in hps to anyone you use it on(they must be under 50% hps) only problem with this skill is that bandages are expensive for a low level character unless you find a friendly magician to summon some for you and magicians bandages dissapear after you log out(you can give them to another player though)

Safe Fall and Sneak
Not nessecary to have but can be interesting.

Imtimidation (instill doubt)
It causes targeted monster to flee away from you(if used right you can follow it along and get free shots oon it while it flees) the problem with this skill is that the monster could come back with friends.

 top

TIPS
Try to keep your total weight 14 or under. Monks gain AC bonus if they have low weight(14 or less for full bonus, you get partial bonuses up till around weight 25)

Solo unless you are a overly social person or you have a lot of down time after killing monsters
Having a trade skill is good. Tailoring is the probally the best trade skill for a monk because you can keep a fairly low weight and still do it. If you know a enchanter and have some cash to spend Jewel Craft could be nice too. Blacksmithing i wouldnt bother with, everything weighs alot. The rest of the trade skills are pretty worthless.

 top
