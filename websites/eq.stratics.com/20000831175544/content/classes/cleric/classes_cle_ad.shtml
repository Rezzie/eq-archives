Teffo's Guide to Trancendental Cleric Power
This document is designed for players who are proficient with theinterface of EverQuest. If you are not, please read theeqmanual.txt and additionally please read Intermediate Group Combat andAdvanced Group Tactics if you plan on ever joining a group with yourCleric. Additionally, please read EQSS's Playing a Magic-User for moregeneral information on casters vs. NPCs.

This is advice for intermediate/advanced players who may or may notknow some of the more effective tactics for Clerics. It startsslowly, with some concepts to keep in mind as a Clerics, and movesinto detailed group combat tactics and spell analysis.

This guide does not walk you through the early levels! 'Guides'(using the term loosely) that walk you through levels teach you nothing.

Read. Learn.

Introduction
Clerics are healing incarnate. While a Cleric is around (and hasmana) your chances of dying are greatly decreased. This makesClerics one of the most 'in demand' classes in all of Norrath(especially by fighters of all kinds). Many groups will not evenattempt certain enemies or areas without a Cleric, and for goodreason. Clerics save lives. It's just that simple.

Healing, while the main job of a Cleric, isn't all that a Cleric cando. In fact, this class is extraordinarily versatile. Buffs, heavyarmor, and a bit of melee make Clerics all around useful. About theonly thing they can't do is do it all on their own. Clerics aredesigned to work in a group. But even in a group there are thingsto learn: Healing is a good start. You are a mediocre cleric ifyou can heal well (including the right people at the right times). Atruly great Cleric knows how to use all of a cleric's abilities inconcert to achieve truly astonishing results.

You just haven't felt alive in EQ until you've taken 3-4 white andyellow enemies, with the entire party ending at about half a bubbleto one bubble. Only a great cleric can drag a group through thatsort of hellish furnace without panicking and losing a few partymembers. And when you do have that experience... you'll never lookat a Cleric the same way again.

Cleric Theory
1) Healing makes a body good.

Healing is such a key ability. It is the primary determinant in anyfight, both survival and recovery. If you can heal during a fightand your opponent cannot, you should almost always be victorious. Healing also increases the effective level of party by almost 1-2full levels. A group of level 10 characters with a good Cleric caneasily function as a group of level 12 characters with respect tothe individual difficulty and group difficulty of enemies. Thisincrease in 'effective level' tremendously increases the amount ofexperience and loot that a group can aquire.

2) Undead? You should have stayed dead.

Clerics break Undead like twigs. Almost all of their primaryoffensive armament is designed to wipe undead opponents across arough surface at very high velocities. Ward Undead, Expulse Undead,and later versions are at least as efficient as Wizard burn withrespect to mana/damage ratio against the walking ick. When fightingUndead, which would you rather have? Frail Wizard? Or a partialtank that can also save your life while reducing Undead to char?Right. So bring a Cleric or two with if you expect to find somecorpses walking around.

3) Soloing? *cough cough* Not.

In general, Clerics have huge problems dealing damage in combat. Atearly levels, they can solo rather effectively. Middle levels itbegins to get rough, because Clerics just don't have the health totake the damage that blue or white enemies will dish out. Atmiddle-high to high levels, you are almost impotent solo. You justcan't do enough melee damage without double attack or dual wield tokeep up with enemies that are striking you for 40-50 damage twice around. Even with healing.

While fighting undead, your damage output increases rathertremendously, however you basically become a Undead-only Wizard (infull Plate). You burn your enemy down and then have to meditate forlong periods. And may your God help you if more than one enemysimultaneously decides you're lunch. At least you can goinvulnerable and zone before they finish the meal.

3) Groups LOVE you.

So, Clerics can't solo. Wow can they group! Even if the world werepopulated with 25% clerics, there would still be an effectiveshortage. Clerics are that amazing in a group. Even multipleclerics do not get in each other's way - they simply keep adding tothe group.

What makes a Cleric so useful to a group?

a) Damage output.

While not able to put the hurting on an enemy that a normal fightertype can, they still have a melee attack which can add up the damagein a long fight. The real damage output of a cleric is theirspells. Start to think of Healing as indirect burn. The AI thinksthis is true, and so should you! If you keep a fighter fighting,they're going to do MUCH more damage than you would with those 80points of mana used as burn.

A Cleric's offensive magicks are not as powerful as any pure caster,but Clerics have some unique advantages when casting Burn. When aWizard or Magician casts burn (especially area affect burn), it hadbest be from maximum range or you can expect a very bloody mage bodyto hit the floor in the near future. Clerics, on the other hand canburn with near impunity because of the tremendous protection theirarmor affords them.

b) Defensive buffs.

These are as much or even more important than healing. Clerics canlayer spells on friendly party members like bandages on a mummy, andshould. If you do not buff your party, they take more damage. Ifthey take more damage, they are in more danger, if they are in moredanger, you must expend much more mana trying to heal them. This isa vicious cycle. Your defensive buffs make your healing spellsabout 20-50% more efficient. Use them on almost everyone who willget into combat (Pure casters like buffs, but don't really need themif they play well).

c) Tanking.

Clerics can tank for a group, but only for a limited amount of time. When thegroup is engaged in multiple enemy combat, a Cleric can safely takesome heat off of the primary tanks while they finish the firstenemy. When the first enemy is slain they can pull the next onefrom the cleric (who can survive for quite a while) and finish thatone with much less danger for the tank. Try not to get two enemieson your primary tanks. Spread the damage around, it's moreeffective, and less dangerous to any individual.

d) Defensive utility spells.

Clerics have a bevy of utility spells that can make a combatsituation (and non-combat situation) a heck of a lot less complex.We'll cover them later in detail, but remember: Lull before combat,Fear at the start of combat, Root near the end of combat. Basically, these spells allow you to divide and conquer enemiessimilar to the Enchanter. These are subtle effects that may notseem that impressive, but make a huge difference.

Character Generation
Race:

Possible choices: Dark Elf, Dwarf, Erudite, Gnome, High Elf,Halfling, Human, Wood Elf.

Almost every race has its healers. The race choice should really beone you feel comfortable playing. But remember, that your racechoice has some effect of the 'type' of cleric you play. Youroverall effectiveness will be dependent on your ability to maximizestrengths and minimize weaknesses of your race to your advantage.

Clerics come in basically two types: Support caster and Combatcaster, though a good Cleric can adapt to fit either role as demandrequires.

Support casters are usually Dark Elf, High Elf, or Erudite. Theseraces have lower physical statistics (Str, Agil, Stam) and generallycan achieve slightly higher wisdom (and thus more mana). Most oftenSupport casters provide combat overview, act as coordinators, whilecastings spells to maximize combat effectiveness.

Combat casters are much more of a 'get in, get dirty' player. Theystand in the middle of a fight directing things, dealing damage,healing, and tanking for the group. This is a true test for aCleric. The ability to perform all the duties required while inmelee range with an enemy or two can stress test even the mostbattle hardened player. The natural Combat casters are halflingsand dwarves, because they have tremendous physical attributes (Str,Agil, Stam).

Other races have more balanced statistics and thus have parts of theCombat Caster and the Support Caster in them. The Humans are bestat this, considering their balance. The Gnomes and Wood Elves bothdo a little of both, but are somewhat lacking in Strength andStamina. This reduces access to bigger armor and drops combatsurvivability. Note: Small armor weighs less. Thus,races that can wear small armor (Dark Elf, High Elf, Dwarf,Halfling, Gnome) have a large advantage in Strength because fullPlate will weigh 5-10 pounds less.

Statistics:

Prime Statistics: Stamina, Strength, Wisdom

Strength: On the obvious side this is the statistic that determinesyour damage cap and adds a little to your chance to hit. Moreimportant is that Strength determines your maximum carryingcapacity. As a cleric, you are going to run short every time. Youcan wear plate at 75 Strength but you would pretty much have topass on looting and summon your food and water in small amounts. Allof a Cleric's equipment is heavy. Their armor is heavy, theirshields are heavy, and their weapons are very heavy. You can neverreally go wrong with extra points in Strength, even if you don't planon doing much melee. The carrying capacity is key.

Stamina: This determines your hit points as well as the rate atwhich you tire. Some warn against putting points into Stamina, but with heavy plate armor, each point of health gained fromStamina is much more effective because of the additional protectionthe armor provides. Other classes do not have this advantage. Itis also important because of the weight of the weapons that clericsare usually swinging around - they will tire you quickly if youneglect Stamina.

Wisdom: This one is a bit obvious. Wisdom determines mana gainedper level. It also provides better resistance to spells and ischecked on a failed skill roll. This is the last statistic youshould put points into, and only after you decide what type ofcleric you are going to play. Having a large pool of mana is notthe end all be all of effectiveness. It really only helps inemergencies and to some degree at high levels. If you have 25 pointsput into Wisdom, and you're playing well enough that you do not dropbelow one bubble, you may have made the wrong choice on statistics.I (a Combat Caster) personally put only as much as I feel isabsolutely necessary because I feel that the points better serve mein combat survivability rather than providing me with extra manathat I rarely use. Support casters should probably go maximumwisdom.

Recommendations:

If you're planning on playing a Support caster, recommendations arefor high Wisdom (more mana), and a bit into strength, so you can getsome decent armor layered on. Armor is more important than agilitynow. A Cleric in Chain with 95 Agility will not last as long as aCleric in Plate with 85 Agility. If you can, go for the Strength towear Plate. We calculate that you'll need about 90 Strength forfull Plate. However, you can go partial Plate and Chain forprobably 75-80 Strength. Keep it in mind.

If you're playing a Combat caster (with a Dwarf, Halfing, orsomething else), we recommend that you diversify point allocation. Combat Clerics need strength for Plate, good Stamina for combatviability, and Wisdom for spell-casting. Dwarven Clerics mightconsider adding a bit to Agility, but that's not a requirement,because of their enhanced Stamina.

The most important statistic for a Combat Cleric isDexterity. This is the statistic that determines your ability tojoin the melee without ending up with dead party members. Dexterityaffects your ability at Channeling. With a high dexterity,Channeling allows you to cast faster and occasionally throughtattacks by an enemy that is actively attacking the Cleric. Do notignore this statistic.

Additionally, Stamina is now a very important statistic. You willsee a return on investments into this statistic that are comparableto Agility. This depends also on your choice of Cleric 'type'.

Level 4: Channeling
Level 8: Meditate
Level 15: Dodge

There are only a few notable Cleric skills. Having to wait untillevel 8 for Meditate is painful. Levels 6 and 7 tend to drag on. Group for those two levels, then go back to soloing. It gets badagain around 12-14 because Healing is no longer effective againstthe amount of damage you're getting hit with. At level 15, a clericgets dodge. It helps a bit, but Cleric Dodge caps out at Good, andthus it functions more as an augmentation to Channeling. It increasesyour chance of getting spells off, but won't increasing yourcombat abilities that much.

Specialization doesn't work as of yet. Down waste any trains init. It is completely non-functional and the official word is they"forgot" to turn it off in the guildmasters. Should it becomeenabled, it might be worth investing in Alteration or Abjuration abit so your heals are more effective and faster, and yourbuffs are a tad stronger.

As a side note, all Cleric skills are skills that only need a single point each. They will all improve from zero with use. This leaves alarge number of points to devote to training weapon, language, ortrade skills at some point. Don't be surprised to find yourselfsitting on hundreds of unused points when you get to higher levels.

Spells
Healing:

Healing is good. Efficient and powerful, it allows everyone else todo their job during combat. It is basically the Cleric version ofindirect burn, and has the efficiency corresponding to Wizard burn(in fact it's almost exactly the same damage/mana ratio at eachcorresponding spell rank, if not slightly better). It keeps yourtanks in the battle longer allowing them to deal more damage andhave a greatly increased chance of finishing the enemy(ies). Moreobviously it keeps people alive. People like that.

Level 1:

Minor Healing

  • Mana: 10
  • Health: 10
  • Eff: 100.00%

    10 points of healing for 10 points of mana. It is useless when youreach level 5 because you (coincidentally) receive the next healingspell at that point. It's not a bad idea to keep this spellmemorized for a while, since it may save you in a situation whereyou are caught with critically low mana and need just a tad bit morehealth. You really don't have a whole lot of spells to choose fromat that point anyway.

    Level 4:

    Light Healing

  • Mana: 20
  • Health: 26
  • Eff: 130.00%

    It is a little bit more efficient heal/mana and much more efficientbased on heal/time. It's a big help at low-intermediate levels, butreally starts to go downhill at level 11. At this point the enemieshit for much more in a round than you can heal for. Clerics healthe 'weakest' in this level range compared to their usual ability. This clears up as you enter level 14. However, the period from 11to 14 is rather rough.

    Level 14:

    Healing

  • Mana: 60
  • Health: 88+1/level above 14
  • Eff: 146.67%

    This spell is the beginning of your more mana efficient big effectheals. This one has very little problem getting you to the nextlevel of heal. At Level 23 you will be fighting creatures that can do 90in a round but their damage will average closer to 40. It will seema little weak near the time you are about to get greater heal butnothing terribly crippling like light healing at level 13.

    Level 24+:

    Greater Healing (~300 for 120)
    Superior Healing (~600 for 250)
    Complete Healing (L39)
    Word of Health (Party area affect heal)
    Word of Healing (Better version of the previous)

    Your healing gets exponentially better as you go up. The completehealing spell is amazing, but generally will not be as manaefficient for you as superior healing except on the truly obscenelyhealthy tanks (1200+ hps). The Word of Health spells aren't amazingin combat (unless you're fighting something that has area affectdamage as it's prime mode of attack) but are good for speedingrecovery in an efficient fashion.

    Cures:

    Cure Poison
    Cure Blindness
    Cure Disease

    These spells cure you of the corresponding affliction. These firstlevel versions of this series may require multiple casts to removethe adverse effect. Poison and Disease are the most commonafflictions.

    Buffs:

    Clerics have the best defensive buffs around. Resistances,Defensive armor, and even Statistic assistance all fall here.

    Defensive Buffs have two effects: They increase health, and theydecrease enemy attack effectiveness through AC bonuses. This allowsBuffed characters to stay in combat longer and thus have a betterchance of finishing the mob they are attacking. Buffs also ease yourjob of healing during combat. Always buff your group before afight - it's well worth it.

    Level 1:

    Courage

  • Mana: 10

    This is the first in a series of spells that add hit points and asmall amount of ac. Even this first buff on a level 15 characterwill make a difference.

    Divine aura

  • Mana: 10

    This spell and it's later versions are not so much buffs as they are a panic button. They render you and at the high levels your group invulnerable for a short period of time. While invulnerable you cannot attack or cast spells. It does allow you a better chance to make zone or for your group to finish the creaturethat is attacking you while you are protected. It is good, as well,when you are in the middle of combat and are just short on manafor a heal. In this case you can go invulnerable and meditate inthe middle of the fray for a short time to get enough mana for thatlife saving heal. It is also good for when your health drops belowthe 40% mark causing enemy frenzy. When your health drops below 20%,you can also go invulvernable giving your group about four roundsto finish the creature on you (if they won't be able to do that,attempt to zone.)

    Flash of Light

  • Mana: 10

    Blinds a target for about 10-20 seconds. Prevents spellcasting aswell as being a general pain in the ass. Primarily a PvP spell, buthas some specific applications during normal hunting.

    Reckless strength

  • Mana: 10

    This provides a target with a longer, though decreasing, burst ofstrength. This first one will provide the target with a 20 pointincrease that will decay down to -10. This spell is great for whenyou are fighting alone with a tank around level 10 to 14. They willbe able to finish the mob quicker because of the increased damage. In general though this spell is not terribly useful because of itsshort duration and the fact that it goes negative. It is useful forsoloing as well as around 10-14, since the increased damage extendsyour limited solo ability a few levels further. This spell also hashigher level versions of it.

    Yaulp

  • Mana: 5

    This is a personal ac and strength buff that lasts for roughly 18sec. It is extremely useful early on when you are soling but isworthless after level 12 or so. It provides a nice burst of addedability but after level 10 you really are not providing enoughdamage in melee to warrant the mana expenditure and the exposuretime. You're already casting spells (healing), and I doubt yourtank is going to be very amused if you are out of mana when he needsa heal because you Yaluped through combat. This is the first in aseries of spells. The upper level ones rarely get used either.

    Level 5:

    Holy Armor

  • Mana: 20

    This is another first in a line of buffs for you as a cleric. Thesespells increase AC as well as reduce the amount of damage the targettakes.

    Level 9:

    Center

  • Mana: 40

    This is the upgraded version of courage. This one adds 44 + 1 perlevel above 9 and a small amount of AC. It also reduces the damagetaken from each attack similar to Holy Armor but to a lesser extent.

    Endure series:

    Level 5:

    Endure Fire

  • Mana: 20

    Gives a bonus to resistance of fire.

    Level 9:

    Endure Poison

  • Mana: 20

    Gives a bonus to resistance of poison.

    Endure Cold

  • Mana: 20

    Gives a bonus to resistance of cold.

    Level 14:

    Endure Disease

  • Mana: 20

    Gives a bonus to resistance of disease.

    Level 19

    Endure Magic

  • Mana: 20

    Gives a bonus to resistance of magic.

    These spells give you a bonus to the corresponding saving throw. They are also part of a series that is improved in higher circles ofspells.

    Level 14:

    Symbol of Transal

    7hps per level added.
    This spell begins your third series of buffs. The symbol spellsstack with the armor and courage spells, providing more health toburn, but require a material component (about a 2.5gp gem). Thisfirst spell usually makes people angry because it seems useless. It can be a huge bonus for the tanks, adding an effective 2-3 levelsof health. This, combined with decent healing (Duh, you have aCleric) makes this spell a big help for dungeon hunting. Your tanksjust have that 50 extra damage that can easily sneak up on you in adungeon. It is circumstantial, but can be funded easily with therusty weapons your group would otherwise toss away (medium levels).

    Level19:

    Daring

    This is the third in your line of courage spells. Bigger hp bonus,bigger protection.

    Spirit armor

    Holy armor v 2.0. More protection, more AC, more damage absorption.

    Burn

    There is a dark side to healing, and that is the taking of health. Clerics have some reasonably priced 1:1 mana/damage burn. It's nicefor finishing runners. It isalso good for when something needs to be finished immediatly. Clerics deliver damage in decent amounts but the mana cost is toohigh to use regularly. This is of course with the exception of theundead damage spells. These spells are on par with spells a wizardof the same level would have.

    Level 1:

    Strike

  • Mana: 12
  • Damage: 8
  • Eff: 66.67%

    8 damage for 12 mana. Think of it as a way to draw creatures toyou. It is never really any better than that. By the time you canuse it well (ie have meditate) it is too weak to be worth casting. It is the beginning of a series of spells. They are not terriblymana efficient but they have good damage and are not often resisted.

    Level 5:

    Ward Undead

  • Mana: 30
  • Damage: 41
  • Eff: 146.67%

    This is the beginning of your undead burn spells. They are verymana efficient and extremely helpful in the undead dungeons, as wellas for those occasional targets of opportunity.

    Stun

  • Mana: 30

    This is not so much burn as a debuff. This spell will stun acreature for 2 - 3 rounds. Excellent for fighting something that isdoing a lot of damage with a group. It is with a series of spells.Later versions of this will have a bit of damage in addition, as wellas longer stuns. Note that with any debuff, resistance is much morelikely.

    Furor

  • Mana: 20
  • Damage: 19 max
  • Eff: 95.00%

    This is an upgrade of strike. While it is not the most efficientspell it is still extremely useful. It allows you to quickly applya bit of damage without committing too much mana.

    Level 9:

    Word of Pain

  • Mana: 47
  • Damage: 29
  • Eff: 61.70%+

    This is the start of another series of spells. These are pointblank area effect spells, meaning they hit a lot of baddies in thenear vicinity (4-6 character widths away). Note that because thisis an area affect spell, it can actually become very mana efficientif you hit 3-5 enemies with it.

    Level 14:

    Expulse Undead

  • Mana: 60
  • Damage: 88 initial
  • Eff: 146.67%

    This spell is Ward Undead v2.0. Very nice. [Equivalent to Shock ofLightning L12 Wizard]

    Smite

  • Mana: 70
  • Damage: 74 initial
  • Eff: 105.71%

    Strike v 3.0. This spell is good damage, bad efficiency. Still it's good to have in your arsenal, though most time you will beholding the mana back for heals.

    Ward Summoned

  • Mana: 30
  • Damage: 41 Max
  • Eff: 136.67%

    This is again, first in the series. It is a version of the wardundead spells that only works on summoned creatures.

    Level 19:

    Word of Shadow

  • Mana: 95
  • Damage: 52
  • Eff: 54.73%+

    Basically an upgraded version of word of pain. The mana investmentfor this spell is significant, so Word of Pain might be the superiorchoice for a while (1-3 levels).

    Holy Might

  • Mana: 60?
  • Damage: 29
  • Eff: 48.33%

    Upgraded version of Stun. It provides a touch of damage as well asstunning the target for 2-3 combat rounds. Really makes a mess ofenemy spellcasters. Badly.

    Summonings:

    Summon Food
    Summon Water

    Summons one food or drink that will disappear when you log out (orif you go offline in any fashion aside from momentary LDness). Withthe time it takes to summon up to 5 of each it quickly becomes alarge annoyance. It is still worth the effort for two reasons. First you have little else to raise your conjuration to anacceptable level. Second it saves you a bit of money over time. Since clerics are the most expensive class to play any bit of changeyou can keep around will help.

    Hammer of Wrath

    The Summon Hammer spell series starts here. This spell summons verynice magic hammer (damage 6, delay 32). It is fairly light and willnot drain your Stamina nearly as much as other standard Clericweapons. The best part about this weapon is that is strikescreatures immune to normal weapons. This saves you a tremendousamount of money around L10, as you are not required (as most classesare) to buy a Combine weapon. Clerics are not required to do muchdamage to their opponent (they're in a group with a fighterusually), so this weapon is more than sufficient until you find anice weapon, or get to the next Hammer spell (of Striking).

    Other summoning spells:

    Abundant Food (4 food at once, merely a time saver)
    Abundant Drink (4 drink at once, time saver)
    Hammer of Striking
    Hammer of Smiting
    Hammer of Requital

    Utility spells

    Most people don't know that Clerics are part enchanter (I think thatis because most people haven't ever played an Enchanter how they aresupposed to be played). Clerics have 3 series of spells designed tocontrol the numbers before and during battle.

    Lull

    This is the first of a series that allows a cleric to pullindividuals or small groups of mobs from an area that containsnumbers of creatures that would overrun their group easily. It alsolowers frenzy on a mob so it allows someone to back out of combatwhen they are lower than 40%. This makes it an absolutely vitalspell for attacking camps, especially in dungeons. (See strategysection for more details.)

    Lull is not any where near as effective for this purpose as latervariants of this spell.

    This first spell is not terribly useful though, for itsdreadfully low success rate. It is resisted, by even bluecreatures, nearly 50% of the time and evenwhen not resisted only about 60% time is it going to holdthem in their position if another is attacked near them. Lull isbetter than nothing, but Soothe is exponentially better. As a note,this spell does not work on the undead. Only Necromancers get theLull the Dead type spells.

    Spook the dead and Fear

    These are your fear line of spells. This is your second line ofdefense against overwhelming odds. When cast these spells cause thetargeted creature to run away from you until the spell effect ends. What this does for you is turn, for example, a 3 on 3 fight into a 2on 3 fight for a limited amount of time. Putting your group at anadvantage for this time should allow you to finish one of thecreatures who has stayed without the added damage and interruptionsthat the third mob would have caused. Fear spells should neverbe used in dungeons except, under controlled situations. Thefleeing mob will make anything it runs by immediately go aggressiveon the caster of the fear. This quickly turns a bad situation intoa frenzied run to zone, leaving dead bodies in its wake.

    Solo Combat Tactics
    If you want a good shot of how boring EQ can get, soloing a clericis your gig. Clerics are a group class almost exclusively. Justlike every other class they can solo to level 13 without too muchproblem but after that point, you begin to see exactly how littledamage you are doing to the creature. As enemy health and damageincrease, fight duration increases as does recovery time. Basicallyit gets to the point where you have two choices. You can eithermelee the creature for a long period of time and heal during andafter the fight. This allows a somewhat shorter recovery but fightstake sooooo long that I am often AFK while fighting from boredom. The second option is using your burn with melee. This finishes themob somewhat faster and is my preferred method but makes for longrecovery times because of the mana inefficiency of the damage. Onceyou get past level 12 these methods become so time inefficient thatall but the most antisocial will realize that Clerics are a groupcharacter.

    You may suggest that Clerics should solo undead after this point. This would be a great idea, if it worked. You can solo undead thatwill give you decent experience, but you will be running back foryour corpse often. 95% of all the undead at your level will be indungeons (or outdoor micro-dungeons and ruins). This invariablymeans you are going to be fighting multiple enemies which is alosing situation, regardless of whether you win, or die. Even if youwin, the recovery time will make this method of gaining experienceinefficient. And dying... well, that's never good. Usually, if youmanage to find a location with a single undead opponent of thecorrect level, you still have to deal with little green annoyingstuff (we call it chafe or trash) that will attack you whilemeditating. Once you hit that meditate button, you will have everypiece of green chafe within two rooms all over you. You don't getmuch recovery done when you have to fight a rat down to half andthen chase it down as it runs at full speed around corners andthrough doors that you can't burn through. No fun.

    Group Combat Tactics
    The group is your home. You are a group character and thus youimprove a group exponentially by your addition. When you are there,your group fights more effectively and safely. Your duty: to keepeveryone alive.

    This is where the fun of the cleric lies. Your spells, yourweapons, your armor, and even your very HP are the tools that youhave to use to keep everyone alive to fight another day.

    Clerics must keep a cool head under the most extreme situations. Acleric who panics gets people killed. You, as the cleric, have tobe willing to ride your health down to under a bubble to keep thetanks in and damaging. You must also have the trust of your groupso that they will not get spooked and try to bug out when they seethe entire party at half life and none of the opponents dead (but afew are close). When things get ugly and the odds are not in yourfavor, everyone has to have trust in your ability and the group as awhole.

    The first thing you must learn is when to heal and when not to heal. If a Cleric runs out of mana, combat becomes 2-3 timesas dangerous as it was. Clerics must conserve mana to keep youroptions open as combat progresses.

    Healing most often is a 60% health twitch response. However, a 60%twitch heal may be premature. The enemy can be naturally drawn offit's initial target by a more dangerous foe (Monks draw enemies offfighters almost 100% of the time after 5-10 rounds). If you twitchheal the primary target, then you're wasting that mana on a targetthat is no longer in danger unless they can manage to draw the enemyback onto themselves.

    This sort of healing intuition depends to a great degree on yourparty composition, and as a Cleric becomes comfortable in a party, heknows how combat is likely to progress and who will be in the mostdanger as the fight goes on. Healing in most situations is ajudgement call, and panicking and responding to somewhat low health will only be more likely to hurt the party than help it.

    Additionally, a good Cleric becomes amazing if the group knows howto distribute damage by 'taunting' with different effects (Burn,special attacks, skills, etc). Please read Grouping 101 and 201 formore information.

    Sometimes, a good group in a tough fight will often let people ridedown to about 40-50%. This may seem risky and dangerous, but iteffectively makes Healing a dual effect. Cleric healing can be usedas a taunt to pull an almost frenzied monster back off a Tank (whichis not attacking now) and onto the Cleric. Then another tank stepsin and takes the enemy off the Cleric. Remember, if you're going todo this, everyone had better know this is going on, otherwise yourgroup may panic and bug out.

    Someone who is somewhat familiar with Clerics might say that onlybad Clerics use Direct Damage. They should instead be saving manafor healing. This is oversimplifying the role of a Cleric, and is adiscredit to the class.

    Simple fact: Burn can be as much of a lifesaver as your healing. If an enemy is going to dish out more damage in a slightly extendedcombat sequence than you can easily or safely heal, it's time forthe group to 'light up the enemy'. Basically, all the casters gointo nuke mode, chain casting burn at the enemy trying to reduce itto slag before it does the same to a party member. In manysituations, this will include the Cleric! If this is a part of amultiple enemy engagement, the Cleric must be a bit more careful asthere still will be a need for Healing later in the sequence. Lighting up an enemy can become very addictive forgroups. This is a very inefficient method of combat, as it incurs asevere recovery time, and thus should only be used when the party isin extreme danger.

    The most common usage of Cleric burn is as a taunt. Lightapplication of burn can turn enemies off of one player and on toyou. This is a form of healing in itself, as you prevent furtherdamage to a party member. This works the best on intelligentopponents (greater undead, and most demi-humans) as they are mostlikely to notice the cause of the 'ouchie'. Burn should be usedsparingly but it is not without its place.

    Aside from their very effective anti-undead arsenal, Clerics haveamazing point blank area affect damage in their Word of Pain series. These PBAOE spells don't do a lot of damage (about 3 damage perlevel of caster on average) they have one very important effect. Area affect spells draw enemies onto the caster like flies to rawmeat (think about it, it's very apt). For a normal caster, that'sdeadly, but as mentioned above Clerics can take the added heatbecause of their armor.

    When the group is rushed by a few targets, the enemies will all lockonto the first one that strikes the group. If this is a tank, therest of the party has a little while to get their act in gear andpull a few of the enemies off. If this is a pure caster, the partyhas only a few rounds to get the enemies off before they frenzy andkill the character. In either situation, the Cleric has theadvantage in being able to launch WoP (or later versions), whichwill pull any enemy not already being attacked onto the Cleric. This can be a lifesaver. No one character can survive beingattacked by many many opponents simultaneously for very long, butthose in heavy armor will obviously last the longest.

    At this point, the Cleric has the option of just tanking theenemy(ies) until the party has dealt with the previously engagedenemies, or having another tank pull the enemy off of the cleric. The latter is the safest, as it gives the Cleric free reign to castother spells (like healing!).

    Clerics are more than just health reserves. Their enchanter spellsmake them able to control the pace and difficulty of combat. Theseare the lull and fear spells that clerics have available. Both oftheses series of spells even the odds for the your party in a fight. The soothe type spells are for situations when there are more mobsin an area than you care to try at once. By soothing some you caneffectively divide and conquer your enemies. The fear and spook thedead series are the cleric's second line of defense againstoverwhelming odds. Fear spells will send one creature running forroughly 30 to 45 seconds for the first versions. This will saveyour group that monster's damage for the time that he is running. It effectively neutralizes them for several rounds of combat. Thisis a life saver, though a somewhat subtle one compared to clericsother abilities

    Soothe - Cast it on a few of a group of enemies, then target adifferent enemy and attack. This should have the effect of leavingthe Soothed creatures behind. They will not assist. But pull theaggressive ones out of range quickly, because Soothe doesn't lastforever.

    Fear - Cast it on an extra enemy (usually a big one) during amultiple enemy engagement. It will run off and you can finish theremaining monsters more easily. Be wary for the Feared enemiesreturn as Fear does not have a set duration.

    The Cleric as a Canary? Ok, bad old reference to the birds used by miners to detect gaspockets (and thus death). The Cleric is likely the best judge ofhow well a combat situation is going (or not going). Good clericsare likely to know very early how much longer the party can sustainthe abuse that it is currently receiving. If your Cleric callsRUN!, your party had best be heading towards the nearest zone borderin a damn hurry.

    When you have two Clerics in a party (assuming they are bothcompetent), each must be careful to take into consideration theother Cleric when analyzing the situation. Mana reports duringcombat become vital to maintain awareness of 'heal status'.

    It's better to call RUN early and have everyone live, than later andhave some of the party limping to zone (or walking back from theirbind point).

    Miscellaneous
    Armor

    Clerics are one of the few classes (others being Bards, Fighters,and Paladins) that can wear Plate armor. This armor is just trulyamazing, providing immense AC as well as the best damage absorptionin the game. Upgrade your armor to the best you can find, steal, orpurchase as often as possible. It is more important than some ofyour spells. Even if you are playing in a Support Cleric, the factthat you can stand up to damage if an enemy turns on you isextremely important. Your group needs you in combat and healing,regardless of the number of enemies on you. You are not a Magician,Wizard, or Enchanter. They need serious protection during combat. A Cleric that begs like a caster for protection is going to get hisgroup killed. A Cleric that refuses to cast while engaged in meleeis even worse.

    Money

    If you're a Cleric, you're broke, that's life. The combined costsof armor, weapons, and spells can seem overwhelming.

    Don't expect to ever really have more than you can spend hand tomouth until very high levels. Many times you will find yourselfborrowing money for armor and spells. You can reduce the amount youspend somewhat in a supporting role by skimping a little on armorbut still you will feel the pinch when you need to buy that nextcircle of spells. The only way you can really keep up in the lowerlevels, if you do not have a group to support you, is to sell yourservices. There are always warriors willing to shell out littlemoney for some healing or others willing to pay for their disease tobe gone. It won't get you fortune or glory but most of the timeit's a lot less boring than fishing for 3.5 hours for one low circlespell.

    Conclusion
    Clerics are all but a necessity in every party. It is possible toplay without one but I would not want to try it. Even using anotherhealer (Druid or Shaman) will not be enough as you go up in levels. Their healing spells are very helpful and a welcome addition, butlack the potence at medium to high levels to keep a group alive(especially against foes like Hill Giants).

    Clerics are not for everyone, and in fact, may not be for mostpeople. They don't solo well and they pretty much don't do damage. They do, though, allow a party to fight well beyond their normallimitations. They provide pulse racing fights and epic talesinstead of runs to zone and much more forgettable demises.

    If you're into the adrenaline rush you feel when rushing headlonginto an impossible fight, you've found your calling: Cleric.

    Good luck, and good hunting!
