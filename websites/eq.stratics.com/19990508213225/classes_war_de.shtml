Playing a Dark Elven Warrior

 Introduction   Starting Out   Nektelos Forest   Moving On 

Introduction
So, you've decided to join the Neriak Indigo Guards. This is probably one of the more unique combinations of race and class in Norrath. When most think about warriors, they think one of the larger races like Barbarians, Trolls, or Ogres.

Truth is, almost anybody can be a warrior except the High Elves and Erudites. Dark Elves make just as good of a warrior. True, physical stamina and strength play a large role in being a successful warrior, but the natural elven racial attributes of agility and dexterity will definitely help your swordplay. If they can't hit you and you never miss, who needs lots of hit points and biceps the size of a tree trunk? Plus, your race has ultravision which means you never have to worry about being in a cave or forest at night. Although there are stores that sell torches in Neriak city, you don't have to buy them. Also, your race can hide. Basically you can turn invisible, but you cannot move while you do it. This is nice when it comes time to sit down and heal; however, it doesn't always work and there is no message to tell you if it worked or not. If your with others, you can ask them if your visible or not. Also, the Teir' Dal have the next most classes to choose from after the humans. This means you will be able to group with a wide variety of classes to form well balanced parties; however, as a general rule, I observed that 50% of the Dark Elves were Necromancers and another 25% were shadow knights. There are wizards, magicians, clerics, rogues, warriors and a few enchanters to make up the rest of the population, but expect those two classes as the norm. However, this is to your advantage. Your skills and weaponry will be needed by the magic users. Since most of the Dark Elves tend to be non-melee fighters, your in demand. It's always nice to be needed.

Starting Out
Before rolling up a Teir' Dal warrior (Teir' Dal is the word in their own language for the the Dark Elf race), you should consider a few things. Although strength and stamina are important, I still believe that you shouldn't be deficient in other attributes. I did put most of my attribute points into strength and stamina. I also upped all the other attributes to at least 75 which is the average attribute of humans. Humans, of course, are the measuring stick of all other races. I try not to be inferior in any way.

Another consideration is religion. I chose to be agnostic. One of the things NPC people will judge you by is your race, your class, and your religion. If you choose agnostic (meaning you don't worship any god), you stand a better chance of being tolerated in foreign cities. Notice that I said tolerated. Often you will be hard pressed to find a merchant to deal with, but at least you walked by the city gaurds unharmed. I was able to travel to both Qeynos, Freeport, and the halfling city of Rivervale unharmed; however, traveling to Fadwyr turned out to be the final demise of my character. The dwarven guards protecting the docks attacked me on sight. A NPC wood elf attacked my character during a rest stop while voyaging the sea, but luckily fell into the ocean before she could severely injure me. Be wary. The easiest way to tell if they will attack you is to do a /consider on them. The /consider command will naturally tell you if you can defeat them in combat or not, but the first half of the message will tell you the targets body language. For example: "The leatherfoot deputy looks at you dubiously, what would you like your tombstone to say?" If they are dubious, then they will not deal with you, but they won't attack you either. If you can find somebody who is indifferent to you, remember where you found him. You can come back and sell loot to them and maybe buy food and water.

Ok, now your ready and standing in Neriak city.

The first thing you must do is get to know the city. Neriak is divided into three zones, and they can be very confusing to the new player. All have twists and turns in them, and you cannot see very much of the city in one glance. Mostly you can only see the alleyway in front of you. One thing I can tell you is both the Neriak Commons and 3rd Gate zones are each split in half by a wall and the main gates enter into a chamber with doors leading out the sides. So if you cannot seem to find the exit, but you walk through an empty chamber, stop and turn. You will probably see the gate. The foreign quarter zone is also divided into two parts. The human section and on the south side there is a ramp that leads into another chamber full of Trolls and Ogres.

Once you can reasonably find your way around you should immediately look for the Warrior's guild. It is downstairs under the arena. This is common for warrior guilds to be in the form of a gladiator pit in Norrath. Neriak's pit is easy to find. It is in Neriak Commons and has a pair of large stone pillars outside of it, one blue and one pink. Find the Guild leader Seloxia Punox and give her (yes, the Dark Elf leader of the Indigo Guards is a woman) your guild note. You should then go to the trainers in the guild and train up in the weapon skills you plan to master. I only recommend you put one point in each area you want, since they will go up in combat by themselves. I also recommend you pick a trade skill. It is going to be awhile till your good in a trade skill and putting training points towards one will help boost it up without wasting lots of money on attempts to use the skill.

Now it's off to the forest to attempt to prove your mettle in combat.

Kick and taunt as much as possible in combat to get these skills up. You can stop taunting enemies if your alone when your skill reaches your level's limit. That limit is, level multiplied by 5 with an additional 5 added to it. Thus at level 1, the most you can increase you skill is to 10. Kicking is a very useful ability that warriors start with. A few other classes get kicking later on in their careers, but you have it from day one. It does not do as much damage as your sword, but more damage to the enemy is always a good thing. It also comes in handy later on if you get killed. Once, I was on the way back to my corpse when I was attacked by a bear. Without my weapons and with no ability in unarmed combat, I was still able to defeat it by just kicking it to death, though I should say that the bear was considered an easy fight at my level. This is the time when Warriors shine. The other Dark Elf tanks are generally shadow knights and Necromancer pets, and until level-9, when they start learning spells, the shadow knights are not as good at combat as warriors and no pet will ever match a warrior. Even after that, warriors are still excellent in combat using just swords. One of the advantages over the shadow knights is that they must purchase spells, while we can save our copper coins and buy armor and weapons. We also can use our training points when we level up on trade skills to make our own armor and sharpen weapons, while shadow knights usually increase their spell casting skills.

Nektelos Forest
This zone is your newbie zone for the Dark Elves. It has both some pros and cons. One of the pros is the size of the zone. Qeynos and Freeport both have monsters outside the city gate, but still inside that same zone for their players to fight. This means they have a distinct over-crowding problem as players rush around trying to fight a creature before another player does. We don't have this problem since our newbie area is an entire zone itself. Plus, our guards generally kill-on-sight the rest of the races of Norrath. This means you will not have to compete with anyone but your fellow Dark Elves and maybe an lone Ogre or Troll. This means you can encroach onto human/half-elf territory and fight monsters there, but they cannot do the same. Also, the Lavastorm mountains are attached to Nektelos forest, but has only one entrance. Although Lavastorm is not recommended until level8, it will be empty of competition when you are level 8 or more. That, plus it has the entrances to THREE dungeons in it as well. Only the evil players have access to these dungeons unless the rest of the good races turn evil or can figure out a way to sneak past the guards. The bad things about Nektelos is the zone has no merchants inside it to sell loot to and only the skeletons have rusty weapons occasionally. As a warrior, your first weapons will mostly be rusty ones you acquire off defeated enemies. Actual living, breathing races offer the best warrior loot in the form of armor pieces and weapons, but you have to leave Nektelos to find Orcs and such to fight.

Moving On
The Eastern Commonlands are right next door. It is also a nice place to adventure around it, though almost all of the monsters here will kill you on sight and are significantly tougher than the ones in Nektelos Forest. The Deathfist Orcs in this zone are terrific sources of loot and rusty weapons. They even have cloth armor you can use too. Plus, there is a warrior guild quest to bring back four orc picks (a crappy weapon the orcs carry that isn't worth much). The trainer will reward you with a rusty sword or wine worth more than the picks. The decaying skeletons in this zone also seem to have more weapons too. On top of that, there is a merchant in a tunnel that will buy from you so you no longer have to change zones to sell off your loot. In this zone you will most likely see your first live humans and half elves. You will probably have seen a few dead ones on the roads of Nektelos Forest after the guards find them poking around.

In summary, the Dark Elves have a niche that needs filling, swordsmen. If you are up to it and want to be something other than a human, but want to group with people other than Shamans, then the Dark Elves await you.

Plus, we dark elves have got to have the coolest looking plate helmets in all of Norrath.

From here on out I will let you discover the secrets of Norrath on your own.
