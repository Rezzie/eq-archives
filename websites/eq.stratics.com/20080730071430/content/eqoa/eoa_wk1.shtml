EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers

 

By Alex von Minden, Community Relations Manager, EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers

Welcome to the first article in a four part series detailing the exciting new Class Mastery system included in EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers. Frontiers is the 2nd edition of EverQuest Online Adventures (EQOA). The original version came out in February of this year, bringing the first Massively Multiplayer Online Roleplaying Game (MMORPG) to the PlayStation 2 computer entertainment system in North America. Frontiers, scheduled for release mid-November, will add a host of new features to the game, including:

New Playable Race, the Ogre - These massive beings are brutal warriors and wield powerful magic
New Playable Class, the Alchemist - Become a master of powerful spells and abilities
New Areas - One continent, three cities, hidden planes, newly discovered zones and forbidden areas
Updated Graphics - Redesigned and highly-detailed player character models, armor and "pets"
New Items - Hundreds of new items to acquire, barter, trade and sell
New Non-player-Characters (NPCs) - Dozens of new and fierce non-playable characters to battle
Level 60 Cap - Increased player character cap (60) for greater character development
New Quests - Challenging new quests to discover and conquer

Along with all that, you also have the inclusion of a Class Mastery system that adds all new depth to character customization as they advance. Starting at level 6 you can choose to divert a portion or all of your experience toward gaining points that you can spend in the Class Mastery system. At any one time you can have up to 25 times your level in mastery points, with a total possible of 1500 points at level 60. The cost per point scales up, with the first points costing 250,000 exp, with the last point costing, well, a serious amount of time for a level 60 character. So, mastery points are not that easy to come buy, but you can gain some pretty cool and powerful abilities.

Once you have points to spend, you can place them into five different categories:

General: Masteries that everyone has access too.
Race: Each race has different, unique masteries available to them.
Archetype: In EQOA there are four Archetypes of classes: Casters, Priests, Tanks and Melee. Each class archetype has their own specific masteries available to them.
Class: Every class has their own masteries.
Master Class: These are the very top end masteries that add unique, powerful new abilities to your character.

Each of the above categories can be considered its own progression tree. The more powerful masteries in any given tree could have level requirements, statistic requirements like a particular minimum strength or stamina score to purchase it, for example, or lower masteries that you must first obtain to gain the higher ones.

Looking at the names, you can probably get a good idea of what different masteries are about in that particular class. General – stuff everyone can use, like increasing base statistics or resistances, being able to hold your breath longer, increasing health or power regeneration rates, etc. Racial masteries add some extra “flavor” or “color” to each class, pulling from different aspects of that particular Race: Haflings can make some nice Pies, Trolls can regenerate faster, Gnomes can tinker up a clockwork companion, things like that. Archetype masteries enable further statistic improvement in stats that are more important to those classes over what you can gain through General masteries. Class specific masteries generally enhance or expand upon defining traits of particular classes: Clerics could improve their ability to combat Undead creatures, Warriors could improve how well they take blows or gain combat abilities, Monks might spend points on “stances” that improve their offensive capabilities, etc. Master Class abilities are just that – very powerful and hard to obtain abilities that well, are pretty cool.

Keep in mind that, even with a possible 1500 mastery points possible to obtain, any one character could only gain roughly half of all available masteries. So, in how you spend your points and what upper end abilities you will unlock will make your character different from others of the same class and race combination. You can, if you find you have spent points incorrectly, delete points to allow you to reconfigure how you have spent them. The system allows characters to become more specialists in different aspects of their roles, adding further diversity to the already expansive and immersive world of Norrath.

In our next installment we will dig further into the specifics of the General and Class specific Masteries to see how you can enhance existing aspects of your character. The following article will focus on the Race and Archetype abilities to see ways that you can gain new abilities for characters, along with giving an exclusive glimpse at the Master Class abilities. Our final installment in this series we will give some specific examples to see the life path of a couple possible choices of the many, many different courses characters can take in Frontiers.

See you next week!
