PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Change Pending...
[ 7:12 PM PDT ] - Dhark
A message from Gordon on the Official EverQuest Forums :


Hello all,
Today we've been brainstorming about what has become known as the PermaMez bug present on the PvP-teams servers. Without going into specifics on how the bug is used, basically, a player can use the interaction between illusions and the mesmerize spell to cause an opponent to be permanently mesmerized. If you play on the PvP Teams servers, you are probably somewhat familiar with this issue.

The issue that makes the bug possible is deeply woven into the fabric of how illusions work, making it something that cannot be addressed directly. Initial ideas (ruled unacceptable) on how to fix this issue included:

1. Turning off illusions entirely on the PvP Teams servers.
2. Making stun and mez spells invalid in PvP combat.
3. Making an illusion cause a team-switch. In other words, if a dark elf cast "Illusion: Human", they would become a human in all senses, able to attack and be attacked by people not on the human team.
4. Making an illusion cause a team-switch, but only for the purpose of Mez and Stun spells. This idea would lead to situations where one person could attack someone who couldn't attack them in return.

The final solution that we've arrived at is the best method that we've thought of to address the issue, and the one that will most likely go live unless someone here can bring something to our attention that we've overlooked. The solution is:

On PvP Teams servers, casting a *non-beneficial spell* upon another *player-character* will result in any active illusion upon the caster, even illusions that have other effects, being canceled.

"Illusion" in this case is defined as any spell effect that makes you look like something different than you actually are. This would include all of the enchanter illusions, as well as spells such as treeform, wolf form, the Call of Bones series, and Item-activated illusions such as the Guise of the Deceiver, etc.

If anyone has any questions or comments on this change, or can think of any issues that make the solution worse than the problem it solves, please post follow-ups in this thread.

Thanks,

-Gordon

Gordon Responds..

>>>Who in the blazes could possibly write code so hopelessly, helplessly interrelated that an ILLUSION has any effect whatsoever on a MESMERIZE?

The teams-server ruleset, devised after release, is actually responsible for the problem, not the illusion and mesmerize itself. This is why the change made will only affect those on the teams servers, and not the servers as a whole, and neither will it affect the standard PvP server RZ.

The core problem is a mismatch in team assignment understood by the client, and the zone-server that the client is connected to at the time.

>>>What I mean is, say someone is using illusion: Ogre to get around Ferrott, and then they start a fight with an Ogre PC... Their Ogre illusion drops and the next thing you know, a wandering Ogre Bouncer is pounding them into the ground.

If the person in Illusion:Ogre were to cast a non-beneficial spell, it would drop the illusion and could possibly, based upon whatever other issues are present, make the enchanter subject to attack by NPCs. However, since the illusion would not drop based upon BEING attacked by the other PC, the person being attacked could certainly try to exit the zone under the cloak of the illusion.

>>>Curious: is cancel magic a beneficial or harmful spell?

Cancel-magic would be considered a non-beneficial spell in this case.

-Gordon

>>>Errr. Maybe i read this wrong or you explained it wrong. when does the illusion drop? If I am the Enchanter with an illusion on and I am casting the *non-beneficial* spell on another player, does it drop then? OR if I am the Enchanter with an illusion on and someone else casts on ME it drops? Which are you refering to?

The former. If player A has an illusion up, and player A casts a non-beneficial spell on player B, player A's illusion will drop. However, it will not drop if player A is minding his own business and player B comes along and casts on player A, nor will it drop if player A casts on an NPC.

-Gordon

Gordon Answers Bard Twisting Questions
[ 2:24 PM PDT ] - Dhark
Gordon responds to some posts from a thread on the Official EverQuest Forums :


>>>Is it possible for the one song to generate two non-conflicting effects? the invis and the speed? Then the invis could be broken as normally but the speed wouldn't be?

No. The core spell engine only allows for an entire spell to be negated in the event that a single component of the spell is. That's why spells that have several effects may not stack with spells that do specific singular effects, since the single effect is often stronger than the effects done by spells that do several.

>>>Are the people who want invis removed from the song saying this because invis isn't very useful in Kunark?


Not sure. They are obviously running into something that causes invis to break, but I'm not sure where. GZ's been getting more mail about this issue than I.

Found on This Post

Make greens give experience.

Soloability in its current state is as intended. The classes who were designed to solo can do so efficiently. Some other classes have developed strategies that allow them to solo under certain circumstances, but not as efficiently. Finally, most if not all of the classes have the ability to kill an occasional experience-giving
critter, but not to the extent that they could really say that they are "soloing".

My point here is that since soloability isn't broken, giving every class the ability to go "soloing" (Meaning efficiently killing creatures for the purpose of advancement) really wouldn't fix anything.

Finally, we really don't want to encourage people to bottomfeed (killing things much lower level than you, thus denying the people who should *really* be killing those things the experience of doing so). Bottomfeeding is a fairly present problem these days, to the extent that we've actually considered making it so that things that don't give experience won't give loot either.

-Gordon

New Patch Info
Patch_Server_Announcement [ 9:03 AM PDT ] - Baladur


*Patch Day*

Greetings all! The following changes have been implemented on the Live servers:

*Spells/Songs*

- Voltaic Draught, a high level wizard direct damage spell was improperly saving versus resistance to cold. It now appropriately saves versus magic.
- The higher level bard song by Brusco was improperly healing the target instead of damaging it. This has been corrected.

*Items*

The Shadowknight class-specific quest armor, Darkforge armor, has had its weight decreased substantially.

The Runed Fighter's Staff, previously useable by Monks and Warriors, has been restricted to Monks only. The statistics on this staff are much more powerful than normally allowed for warriors. This change is retroactive and will be reflected on items currently in inventory. Though we strive to avoid negatively adjusting items after they have been earned, this item presented a balance issue, making the change necessary. We apologize to warriors that currently own this weapon.

A data error with the Symphonic Saber, causing it to report "Item out of charges", has been corrected. Due to the dynamics of this particular issue, current owners will need to contact a GM to trade this item out for a new, properly working version of the item.

The Burning Rapier (rogue weapon) has been enhanced. In addition to an increase in statistics, it received a stronger spell effect. The lightsource effect on the weapon has been removed due to an aesthetic rogue desire to be "sneaky" :)

The effect on the Hangman's Noose will now work properly.

*General Changes*

- Some NPCs in Dalnir were casting spells much too powerful for their level. They have been adjusted.
- Pathing information has been updated in Highkeep. NPCs and pets should have a much easier time of navigating the zone.
- Some Quest-NPCs were not spawning properly in Rathe Mountains. These issues have been corrected.
- A handful of problems reported with research practice via the Concordance of Research have been corrected. All practice-rune recipes should now work correctly.
- Gorilla's in Burning Wood now have a new texture set that properly reflects their undead status.
- Fishing should now work properly in Kunark zones.
- /yell now reports the proper direction to people hearing the yell.
- Corrected a bug that gave some races the impression that they were allowed to start their character in Cabalis. They were never actually allowed to create the character there, however the indication that they could has been fixed.
- Corrected sound issues that were causing some crashes to the desktop in certain cases.

We are in the process of patching music files (*.XMI) for zones in which music does not currently work. There are 55 small files (around 2K each) that will be patched over the next couple of days to enable music in all of the affected zones.

The zone of Timorous Deep has been tuned and itemized. Certain aspects of the changes have made certain areas of the zone more dangerous, but the reward should be well worth the risk. Feel free to visit this zone and search out its treasures at your leisure. Note: These changes went in on Monday.

Upon investigation, we discovered a deficiency with the amount of experience being rewarded in many Kunark zones (mostly dungeons). The affected zones have been updated, and adventurers should notice the greater experience reward in return for the increased risk and challenge associated with these zones.

- The EverQuest Team

Ignoring PvP?


We're not ignoring PvP, we're concentrating on issues that affect the players as a whole, regardless which server they play on.
We're still clearing up issues with Kunark and I think we'll be able to pick up where we left off in early June. That said, there are a couple of specific issues here that I'd like to address:

1 - Exp loss from DoT spells

For months, players killed by a DoT spell would lose experience. Very unfair to those without DoT spells of course. Such an obvious problem surely had to have been tested/known before opening PvP servers.

This issue was addressed on the PvP servers shortly after TZ and VZ launched. It still exists on the normal servers for PvP people and people who engage in duels. Due to the way DOTs work, changing this aspect beyond its current state is not feasible.

2 - DoT casters can't loot victims

Even to this day, when soemone dies due to DoT, the caster cannot loot that victim. Very unfair to those with DoT as their main form of damage.

This issue is the same problem that causes/caused XP loss due to DOTs. As with the issue above, changing it beyond its current state is not feasible.

3 - Exp loss from pet death

For months, people who were killed by someone's pet would lose exp. Very unfair to those without pets, and to those casters with pets who hated to make that happen but were left with little choice. Surely Verant knew about that problem.

I don't know off the top of my head if this still happens in PvP situations (/duel, guildwar, etc.) on non-PvP servers, but it has been correct on the PvP servers. The exception to this is cases where owner of the pet is LD or under the effect of Fear (cast by an NPC since PCs can fear each other any longer). As with the issues above, addressing these exceptions is not feasible, and we'll consider any cases of intentional abuse a customer-service issue.

5 - Charm/Fear taken out of game

Due to problems that would occur to people who got charmed/feared (such as being treated as an npc and bearing full bore spell damage instead of the nerfed rate, etc etc.), verant simply too charm and fear out of the game. For enchanters, clerics, necros, sk's and others, that was a major blow to their offense. Instead of fixing the game code problems, Verant simply trashed a major component to entire classes.

The nature of these spells made them abusable beyond all repair. People would fear people in dungeons, where they wouldn't break out until in deep an alone causing XP loss. People would charm people and have them attack nasty critters and die, causing XP loss. People would charm people and sit them down next to NPCs that are aggro to the native race of the target, but not to the charmer. When charm broke, the NPC would attack the person who was charmed causing XP loss. People would also keep other people charmed for extended periods of time thus denying their ability to play the game. The only solution that would have allowed these spells to remain in-game would be to eliminate XP loss entirely, which would too drastically change the PvE apsects of the game.

6 - Melee v. Caster imbalance

It didn't take long for people to realize that in general, casters are far superior pvp players to melee players. In a fast-fix attempt to balance, Verant simply reduced all spell caster damage to do only .63% of full damage when used in PvP. Of course, this was very unfair to wizards who only have spell damage for offense, whereas other casters were still left with their other forms of damage, pets, useful spells, etc.

We still believe that this was the best option. Spells were designed to be effective against NPCs, which have many more hitpoints at a given level than PCs do. The change made sense. You'll notice that we also reduced pet damage in PvP at the same time as well.

Verant's response? Let's just change one of the race war to coin only and see what happens. No poll, no option to move. Anarchy. Eventually, especially at higher levels, people just wear no-drop items, or trash so it ends up that the loot system basically punishes those who choose not to fight like a cheeseball.

We did a poll. It suggested that the majority of people who were taking part in the discussion supported coin-only loot. However we didn't know whether that population was representative of the whole, which is why we changed one server. I think that the numbers are showing now that more people prefer coin-only loot. However just to be sure, we are still considering allowing people the option to move in the near future.

No drop and lore blues

Abashi why aren't popular items like the SMR and Runed Cowl Lore?

The LORE and NODROP tags on items have different effects on the player economy and the farminmg issue.

For instance, NODROP, though created for the purpose of making people complete their own quests, is *very good* at controlling the farming problem. However it's very bad for the player economy, and actually promotes camping, or gives people the feeling that they are required to camp. It also makes things such as looting a persons corpse for them when it's in a bad place impossible.

LORE, though not as good as NODROP at stopping farming still does does something, making it a bit cumbersome for one character to hold a bunch of items on their own. It's easier on the player economy than NODROP, but still presents some gameplay problems. It also gives people the feeling that they are required to camp because people are less likely to want to trade LORE items since they they have to go back and get another one for themselves.

Though the absense of both of these tags does present some farming issues, it is by far the best for the player economy, general gameplay, and gives people another reasonable option to acquire something besides camping it themselves. With the implementation of the play-nice policies, as well as the addition of all of the new zones with the expansion, the farming issues are being handled through other means.

So, to answer your question, we don't want to place the LORE and NODROP tags on items as a matter of course, and would prefer to give each item and situation some serious thought due to the negative consequences of these tags.

PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Change Pending...
[ 7:12 PM PDT ] - Dhark
A message from Gordon on the Official EverQuest Forums :


Hello all,
Today we've been brainstorming about what has become known as the PermaMez bug present on the PvP-teams servers. Without going into specifics on how the bug is used, basically, a player can use the interaction between illusions and the mesmerize spell to cause an opponent to be permanently mesmerized. If you play on the PvP Teams servers, you are probably somewhat familiar with this issue.

The issue that makes the bug possible is deeply woven into the fabric of how illusions work, making it something that cannot be addressed directly. Initial ideas (ruled unacceptable) on how to fix this issue included:

1. Turning off illusions entirely on the PvP Teams servers.
2. Making stun and mez spells invalid in PvP combat.
3. Making an illusion cause a team-switch. In other words, if a dark elf cast "Illusion: Human", they would become a human in all senses, able to attack and be attacked by people not on the human team.
4. Making an illusion cause a team-switch, but only for the purpose of Mez and Stun spells. This idea would lead to situations where one person could attack someone who couldn't attack them in return.

The final solution that we've arrived at is the best method that we've thought of to address the issue, and the one that will most likely go live unless someone here can bring something to our attention that we've overlooked. The solution is:

On PvP Teams servers, casting a *non-beneficial spell* upon another *player-character* will result in any active illusion upon the caster, even illusions that have other effects, being canceled.

"Illusion" in this case is defined as any spell effect that makes you look like something different than you actually are. This would include all of the enchanter illusions, as well as spells such as treeform, wolf form, the Call of Bones series, and Item-activated illusions such as the Guise of the Deceiver, etc.

If anyone has any questions or comments on this change, or can think of any issues that make the solution worse than the problem it solves, please post follow-ups in this thread.

Thanks,

-Gordon

Ignoring PvP?


We're not ignoring PvP, we're concentrating on issues that affect the players as a whole, regardless which server they play on.
We're still clearing up issues with Kunark and I think we'll be able to pick up where we left off in early June. That said, there are a couple of specific issues here that I'd like to address:

1 - Exp loss from DoT spells

For months, players killed by a DoT spell would lose experience. Very unfair to those without DoT spells of course. Such an obvious problem surely had to have been tested/known before opening PvP servers.

This issue was addressed on the PvP servers shortly after TZ and VZ launched. It still exists on the normal servers for PvP people and people who engage in duels. Due to the way DOTs work, changing this aspect beyond its current state is not feasible.

2 - DoT casters can't loot victims

Even to this day, when soemone dies due to DoT, the caster cannot loot that victim. Very unfair to those with DoT as their main form of damage.

This issue is the same problem that causes/caused XP loss due to DOTs. As with the issue above, changing it beyond its current state is not feasible.

3 - Exp loss from pet death

For months, people who were killed by someone's pet would lose exp. Very unfair to those without pets, and to those casters with pets who hated to make that happen but were left with little choice. Surely Verant knew about that problem.

I don't know off the top of my head if this still happens in PvP situations (/duel, guildwar, etc.) on non-PvP servers, but it has been correct on the PvP servers. The exception to this is cases where owner of the pet is LD or under the effect of Fear (cast by an NPC since PCs can fear each other any longer). As with the issues above, addressing these exceptions is not feasible, and we'll consider any cases of intentional abuse a customer-service issue.

5 - Charm/Fear taken out of game

Due to problems that would occur to people who got charmed/feared (such as being treated as an npc and bearing full bore spell damage instead of the nerfed rate, etc etc.), verant simply too charm and fear out of the game. For enchanters, clerics, necros, sk's and others, that was a major blow to their offense. Instead of fixing the game code problems, Verant simply trashed a major component to entire classes.

The nature of these spells made them abusable beyond all repair. People would fear people in dungeons, where they wouldn't break out until in deep an alone causing XP loss. People would charm people and have them attack nasty critters and die, causing XP loss. People would charm people and sit them down next to NPCs that are aggro to the native race of the target, but not to the charmer. When charm broke, the NPC would attack the person who was charmed causing XP loss. People would also keep other people charmed for extended periods of time thus denying their ability to play the game. The only solution that would have allowed these spells to remain in-game would be to eliminate XP loss entirely, which would too drastically change the PvE apsects of the game.

6 - Melee v. Caster imbalance

It didn't take long for people to realize that in general, casters are far superior pvp players to melee players. In a fast-fix attempt to balance, Verant simply reduced all spell caster damage to do only .63% of full damage when used in PvP. Of course, this was very unfair to wizards who only have spell damage for offense, whereas other casters were still left with their other forms of damage, pets, useful spells, etc.

We still believe that this was the best option. Spells were designed to be effective against NPCs, which have many more hitpoints at a given level than PCs do. The change made sense. You'll notice that we also reduced pet damage in PvP at the same time as well.

Verant's response? Let's just change one of the race war to coin only and see what happens. No poll, no option to move. Anarchy. Eventually, especially at higher levels, people just wear no-drop items, or trash so it ends up that the loot system basically punishes those who choose not to fight like a cheeseball.

We did a poll. It suggested that the majority of people who were taking part in the discussion supported coin-only loot. However we didn't know whether that population was representative of the whole, which is why we changed one server. I think that the numbers are showing now that more people prefer coin-only loot. However just to be sure, we are still considering allowing people the option to move in the near future.

- Gordon

No drop and lore blues

Abashi why aren't popular items like the SMR and Runed Cowl Lore?

The LORE and NODROP tags on items have different effects on the player economy and the farminmg issue.

For instance, NODROP, though created for the purpose of making people complete their own quests, is *very good* at controlling the farming problem. However it's very bad for the player economy, and actually promotes camping, or gives people the feeling that they are required to camp. It also makes things such as looting a persons corpse for them when it's in a bad place impossible.

LORE, though not as good as NODROP at stopping farming still does does something, making it a bit cumbersome for one character to hold a bunch of items on their own. It's easier on the player economy than NODROP, but still presents some gameplay problems. It also gives people the feeling that they are required to camp because people are less likely to want to trade LORE items since they they have to go back and get another one for themselves.

Though the absense of both of these tags does present some farming issues, it is by far the best for the player economy, general gameplay, and gives people another reasonable option to acquire something besides camping it themselves. With the implementation of the play-nice policies, as well as the addition of all of the new zones with the expansion, the farming issues are being handled through other means.

So, to answer your question, we don't want to place the LORE and NODROP tags on items as a matter of course, and would prefer to give each item and situation some serious thought due to the negative consequences of these tags.

Gordon On Linking Groups
Official_EverQuest_Forums [ 6:44 PM PDT ] - Takon
Abashi Responded to many questions pertaining to why groups cannot link to share experience in Everquest. His post was made on the Everquest Gameplay Forum

There seem to be a couple of issues here in the thread, the first being linking groups for shared experience, and the second being linking groups for enhanced communication. In both of these cases, we've weighed the various pros and cons of each situation and decided against them.
Linking groups for shared experience isn't feasible from a technical standpoint. The code that determines how experience is split up, and the group tracking mechanisms in game are part of the central core of EverQuest, something that we would rather leave intact and instead focus our time on adding new content to the game.

Shared communication has been ruled out both for that reason (though communication would probably be easier), and to increase the challenge of encounters that require multiple groups. One thing that is difficult about these encounters is that communication is difficult, where it should be. You have 30 people in a room all going after the same critter, and it takes real skill to keep everything sorted out, even with everything filtered out via the chat filters.

-Gordon

Iksar Monk Vs. Human Monk
[ 4:36 PM PDT ] - Spiritus Mundi
Posted by Abashi himself.

Previously, with only one class capable of becoming a monk, balancing the monk class on the basis of race was not difficult. However, the inclusion of the Iksar, with many different racial attributes, has made this a bit harder.
Infravision and regen make life much easier for the young Iksar, which leaves us with a need to validate humans as a monk class. In addition, monks are the only race that get an AC bonus based upon their race. This leaves them already getting one just by virtue of being a monk, and now another because they are also an Iksar.

In this case, we decided that in addition to including the expected XP and faction issues, we've also made it so that Iksar monks will not be as "good" at *being* monks as humans. It's not by a large extreme, but human monks will always be better at doing what monks do: dealing damage.

-Gordon

Gordon Knows Balance



Balance is about there being a compelling reason to choose one class over another aside from the desire to roleplay a particular type of character. It really isn't about relative power compared to another class, nor would we claim that that relative power is meant to be fixed from levels 1 to 60.
Offensive power is only one equation in determining "balance". Other factors are usefulness to a group, and special skills or abilities. For instance, though a wizard's teleporting capability does not at all affect their offensive efficiency, it does contribute to other factors of "balance". A bard gives up much offensive prowess in exchange for being a very desirable member of a group.

By "compelling reason", we mean that one class does not invalidate another. For instance, the monk can do more offensive damage at a give level than any warrior. Now, if they had the hitpoints of a warrior, could wear their armor, and carry as much weight, then there would be no compelling reason to play a warrior other than roleplay. The monks weakness opens the door for another class to be viable.

Now any time I speak about "compelling reasons", posts on the board follow along the lines of "Just what is the 'compelling reason' to play a [insert class here]." All I can say in all of these cases is that "compelling" is in the eyes of the beholder, and I would encourage everyone to find that out on their own. Our opinions might differ from yours, but we believe that there are compelling reasons to play every class in the game, which is why we consider them balanced.
