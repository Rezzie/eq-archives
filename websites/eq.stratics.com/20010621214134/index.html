PvP Changes Clarified
GeneralNews [ 1:00 AM EDT ] - Witt
Gordon Wrinn has made an official response to the rumours from the Minneapolis fan faire about possible upcoming changes to the existing PvP servers. He made these comments over at the Developer's Corner.
Hello all,
It’s apparent that as a result of the Fan Faire this weekend in Minneapolis, inaccurate information regarding our PvP plans for the future were released and/or misinterpreted.

That has always been one of the difficulties of having a real-time interactive discussion where one doesn’t have the time to think about the precise wording of their answers. However, what we gain through the interactive forum makes it worth the effort, even when things appear to come out wrong like they did in the PvP panel this weekend.

For the most part, regarding the information that has since shown up on web sites, it appears that when it was mentioned that we “discussed” an issue, it was interpreted as “we are doing this”. The fact is, we discuss a TON of things when we’re driven to improve one area of the game. If you name it, we’ve probably discussed it at one time or another. That doesn’t necessarily mean that what we discussed has either been decided or shot down, just that we’ve given it some measure of consideration in the past.

So, with that in mind, here’s the quote-by-quote clarification of the story on Castersrealm (eq.castersrealm.com) and RallosZek.net. (www.ralloszek.net).

Quote from Article: In approximately one week, PvP 'rules' will be as on Sullon Zek. In other words, there are no rules with respect to PvP. Standard blue server rules apply and sexual/racial harassment will not be tolerated. Everything else (training/bind point camping/exp killing, etc) will be completely legal. No PvP related petitions will be handled by the CS staff. The standard blue server type problems such as lost corpses, bugs, lost items, etc will still be handled.

Clarification: Sure, we discussed it, much like we discuss anything. As we were putting together the ruleset for Sullon Zek, someone suggested that we role it out across Sullon first and see how that goes and role it across the other servers if it works out. Will it go across the other servers? We don’t know. We’ll have to see how it goes on Sullon first.

Quote From Article: ShowEQ is actively being pursued to be 'fixed'

Clarification: As actively as “bang for the buck” allows. We really need to do something revolutionary and complex in our next attempt to address the problem. It’s just a matter of having enough time to implement and test that. Timeframe? Mid-term, probably prior to Luclin’s launch.

Quote from Article: Classes are being re-examined for viability in a PvP environment. Lady Daegarmo specifically mentioned rangers as being one of the classes that is woeful in PvP.

Clarification: …with nothing specific planned and nothing beyond minor tweaks, certainly nothing class-redefining planned. Regarding rangers there was talk about improving archery, but that was spoken of with plans to put something out across all servers rather than just as a PvP-related improvement. The truth is, the classes are not balanced for one-on-one PvP, no more than they are balanced for one-on-one PvE (Player versus Environment). If you have to fight as part of a group to be successful in PvE, you’ll most likely have to do so with PvP. Yes, this does mean that a solo melee player will be at a disadvantage when meeting casters, especially those configured for solo play, and yes, that is part of the plan.

Quote from the Article: Lady Daegarmo is pushing for the return of egg shaped pumice on vendors for approx 200P each

Clarification: Oh it’s been pushed, but has yet not been taken up by the dev team for consideration. We’re kind of like the supreme court that way

Quote from Article: It was noted that Harm Touch and Lay Hands are on the same spell function with opposite effects so there's no way to give Lay Hands back to paladins on death while restricting the same recycle on HT

Clarification: Not entirely accurate. The reuse time is not tied to the spell effect, not to mention that each ability is treated as a skill by the game and uses its own reuse timer. The reason it’s not being done is not a matter of technical unfeasibility, but rather a specific design decision not to. Both are easy to exploit. The level to which they can be exploited is acceptable on the PvE servers and not acceptable on the PvP servers.

Quote from Article: Cross teaming is coming to an end. Teams (see later notes) will be locked down. There will be no ability to crossteam at all. No grouping, guilding, buffing, etc with non-team members. This lockdown is probably 60 days out. (I stated that the Development team would make an official date on the lock down in which they would give a minimal of 30 day notice. I also stated that I doubted you would see these changes within the next 2 months.)

Clarification: Yep, we’re planning on addressing cross-teaming, probably through locking down the teams. However, the specifics on how this will be done, along with what if any beneficial abilities will be allowed to cross any lock down is still being discussed. Opinions are welcome.

Quote from Article: The lockdown will entail a guildwipe. All guilds will be deleted and must be reformed. There will be extra staff for the first few days after the wipe to assist in this endeavour

Clarification: If we decided to do the most severe lockdown (no cross-guilding), then this would probably be the case. However something like this won’t be decided until we decide what kind of lockdown we’ll do.

Quote from Article: It is highly likely that common (the language) will be removed from the racewar servers for general communication use (i.e., public channels). Training in non-team languages will also receive very low caps.

Clarification: No. While this was one of the many thing suggested as part of a panel discussion, it has only been discussed as one of the many possible options for an “RP Server”, for lack of a better term, by the development team. The “RP Server”, by the way, will not be PvP. Those looking for PvP rules similar to an “RP Server” should look towards Sullon Zek.

Quote from Article: Strong possibility of adding a long (40 or so hours) quest to change your team. Certain classes/races would not be allowed to join certain teams (such as a Paladin joining the evil team).

Clarification: No. It was one of the suggestions brought up by players in the panel discussion, and has not been discussed by the development team. However, I can say with some certainty that this probably won’t happen.

Quote from Article: TZ likely to be changed to a 2 team server (Good vs. Evil). VZ may be changed to a 3 team server

Clarification: These were ‘off the cuff’ examples of how a lock down could possibly be accomplished, rather than an indication of the direction planned. To clarify further, we previously decided not to implement a 2-team system, and are considering a 3 team system, by race, due to the percentage breakdown of players on each team currently.

Quote from Article: Rallos will always remain coin + 1 item loot. Code for rain spells is currently being changed to not effect your pet or your immediate group

Clarification: Yep and Yep, but no ETA.

Quote from Article: Sullon Zek will be live within 3 weeks

Clarification: Probably within a week actually baring major problems coming up.

Quote from Article: There's discussion about adding a new Guildwar server with no 'teams' but lockdowns based on your guild.

Clarification: This was previously discussed and ruled out by the development team, as it would be insufficiently differentiated from the current servers. In other words, most of everything that would be doable on the “guildwar” server is currently doable on Rallos, or one of the existing teams for that matter.

Quote from Article: On Sullon, the new summon corpse mechanism for good races will be obtained via a quest which likely involves PvP/crests of fallen opponents.

Clarification: This is accurate. In fact I’ll tell you now that Mages are in line to gain the ability on Sullon. And no, we won’t roll it out that way on other servers

I hope this gives everyone a better idea of what we have planned for the near and mid-terms. We’ll work to be a little more explicit about what is currently planned or not planned to avoid misunderstandings in the future.

Thanks,

Gordon Wrinn
Associate Producer, EQLive/Luclin
Sony Online Entertainment

Upcoming Enhancements (?) to Everquest...
CommunityNews [ 2:28 PM EDT ] - Ogamok
Thanks to Kilyin for submitting this!

Greetings Norrathians!

We have received many letters and emails asking what exactly exploitation
is in light of the recent banning of the guild formerly known as Conquest.
We have compiled a list of known exploits at www.everquest.com, but for your own convenience, here are a few simple rules to go by:

1. Using the landscape to assist you in any way with the killing of a mob is an exploit.
2. Using spells that do not stack with a mob?s spell line thus rendering the mob incapable of hitting you with his spell(s) is an exploit.
3. Using CoH to clear agro is an exploit.
4. Doing anything without the intended number of people for that encounter (see the list of expected participants in certain encounters at www.everquest.com) is considered an exploit.
5. Agro management is an exploit.
6. Battle rezzing is an exploit.
7. Waiting until X amount of mob life is left before nuking is an exploit.
8. Having casters stand further away than melees is an exploit.
9. Binding and then nuking after death is an exploit.


If you are considering doing anything and are not sure if it is an exploit, please petition a GM. If the GM determines that what you are considering is an exploit, you will be suspended/banned without warning.

This latest patch also contains several large changes that we hope will make your gaming experience better.

Cazic Thule and Innoruuk have been upgraded once more. They will now randomly summon and death touch any person in the zone every five minutes. Cazic Thule has been given a 1000 hp damage shield, and Innoruuk can now Lay on Hands three times in an EQ day. Both now wield a non-resistible area of effect disease/fear DOT that will kill those inflicted in under a minute. God levels have now been increased and melee damage upped appropriately. Upon initial agro of the god, all mobs will be respawned and instantly teleport to defend their god. Because we wish to maintain the current influx rate of planes gear and items, these respawned mobs will not have loot.

The Sleeper will now do a zone wide death touch every five minutes, regardless of whether he is spawned. There are eight warders instead of four, and the warder levels and skills have been upped accordingly.

All giants in Kael now see invis.

All mobs in Skyshrine now see invis.

It has come to our attention that there are Iksar who have actually managed to up their faction enough to be non-kos in cities other than Cabilis. These factions have been reset. Experience loss from death loops due to binds next to kos guards will not be reimbursed. Reversely, all Cabilis factions have been reset for non-iksar.

In order to reduce camping in Lower Guk and Sebilis, all loot from these zones has been removed and may randomly drop off of any spawn in Norrath.

All epic quests now require a drop off of either Innoruuk, or Cazic Thule.

In order to keep variety among the classes, All beneficial spells are now self ONLY. Group spells such as pack regen have not been affected.

In response to popular demand for rangers to have a once a day ability, rangers can now cast ?revive? on their corpses once an EQ day. We believe this ability follows our Vision for the ranger class. *note: because of this ability?s potential for being overpowered, clerics will no longer be able to resurrect rangers. Other classes abilities to be rezzed are not affected by this change.

Call of the Hero has been changed. The magician and his target must be able to see each other for the spell to work. Agro wipe will no longer occur when successfully cast.

The effect on the Singing Steel Gauntlets has been changed from Enduring Breath to Summon Wisp.

There is now a confirmation box for all Evac spells. The evac will not occur for anyone unless all party members click yes.

Your group members will now receive a message any time you automatically eat, or drink. A message will also be sent when you open or close a bag.

If your avatar is standing in one place for more than a minute, any PC you are facing will receive the message ?Soandso is STARING at you?. If you are looking at their back, the message will read ?Soandso is STARING at your BUTT.?

Spirit of the Wolf is now available to all classes to cast, but because of the power of this spell, a ruby is now required for each cast. This applies to all classes.

The agro on the snare line has been increased.

The agro on the Tashani line has been increased.

The agro on all weapon procs has been decreased, unless you are a ranger.
If you are a ranger, the agro on weapon procs has been tripled.

Root is no longer available to rangers. This spell line has been replaced with Summon Wisp.

Evade now has a 10 second ?casting time?.

Mobs will no longer move away when a PC feigns death. They will stay next to the PC until either the PC gets up or the mobs are killed.

Necromancers can no longer Feign Death. This spell has been replaced with Summon Wisp.

Fear and Snare/Darkness no longer stack.

Guild size is now limited to no more than 30 total accounts. If your guild has more than 30 accounts, it will be deleted in the next guild wipe.

Our next guild wipe is tommorrow.

Inspect messages are now more detailed. In addition to ?Soandso is inspecting your equipment?, a message will be sent for every item looked at. IE. ?Soandso is examining your Wurmslayer, Soandso is examining your Fine Plate Greaves.?

The Clarity spell line has had its mana regen reduced.

Mez will now break if any spell is cast on the mob.

Lodizal can now spawn in any zone with a large body of water.

Wandering Brownies have been added to Kithicor, Wakening Lands, Toxxulia Forest, Swamp of No hope, Trakanon?s Teeth, Emerald Jungle, Burning Woods, and Misty Thicket.

Brownies now see invisible.

Griffons of all kinds now see invisible.

Shadow Guardians now see invisible.

Wyverns and Drakes now see invisible.

The Kromrif Death Squad now has a chance of spawning in Great Divide, Cobalt Scar, East Commons, and Thurgadin.

Wuoshi will now spawn randomly at any wizard portal. In addition to Velious zones, this includes Kunark as well as Antonica. Wuoshi's faction has also been changed to Guardians of Veeshan.

The 96% Reviviscence spell has been replaced with Summon Wisp.

The effect on the Water Sprinkler of Nem Ankh (cleric epic) has been changed to Abundant Food.

Because of item envy, all weapons are now variations of rocks and sticks. In order to keep the epic value of epic weapons, epic rocks and sticks will glow slightly. There are rumors of lava boulders and sharpened stakes in Dragon Necropolis and Veeshan?s Peak.

-- The Everquest Team.

Magic Changes
GeneralNews [ 10:06 AM EDT ] - Ogamok
I found this here

Hey guys, as I get ready to head out for the Fan Faire I wanted to sit down and write up this post I've been putting off all week. A couple quick notes.

The "problem" with rain spell pulses always being resisted if they will be the killing blow isn't actually a problem....It's an intentional design feature. The problem was much like the old DOT corpse poof buf. If the Rain killed the NPC and the server was unsure where the damage came from the corpse would poof, so these spells were coded in this manner to prevent that from happening. I'm working with the coders to come up with a better solution. We're all pretty busy right now so unfortunately I can't give you a time frame on when a better solution will be implemented.

Kunark Evac spells: these will now work anywhere in the world. You won't have to be in a Kunark dungeon to use them. This change will apply to both Wizards and Druids evac spells.

Druid Spell Egress: this spell will now work indoors. It didn't make sense for the group version to work indoors (a mistake on our part) and not have the self version work indoors.

Shroud of Death: I added Illusion Skeleton to shroud of death on test server..... I removed it the next day I just didn't get to update spells on test. I forgot that in skeleton form trolls and ogres would lose slam, sorry for the scare. I'll see if I can think up some other way to give SKs skeleton form.

Stacking issues: I continue to address these as time permits. We're working on some changes that will allow me to fix them much easier. More stacking issues should be resolved in the coming months. High level players should be pleased to know that I'm 99.99999% certain that Call of Earth and Speed of the Shissar will no longer conflict after the next patch.

New Translocates: as many of you know there are new translocate spells up on test. I'm not going to comment on these other than to say you can expect that cool cat Al'Kabor to be out and about researching again.

Now on to the "Burning issue". I know the Wizard community has been *patiently* awaiting the enhancement we have been working on The coding for the new spell effect has been finished and it is going through extensive internal testing. It has also been on Test Server for a week, but I doubt you would have seen it in a parser – cause I'm sneaky like that. Before I go into details on the new spells there's a few things I'd like to say. Honestly I feel that wizards are balanced fine from a power standpoint, to that end you will not be receiving an upgrade to your damage output. Where I do feel wizards need help is group utility.

As I'm sure you can understand coming up with a highly desirable utility type spell for an EverQuest wizard is not an easy task. I've spent countless hours pondering a solution, I've read countless message board forums and emails looking at suggestions from you guys. Finally a solution was found! I'm not naive enough to believe that everyone will be happy about the new enhancement but this is an idea I've seen tossed around on a number of forums and I think not only does it fit in well with wizards being the masters of arcane transportation, I think it will make them very desirable in the 50 to 60 levels where they seem to be struggling for usefulness.

The new spells will give wizards the ability to prevent NPCs from gating. There will be 3 versions of the spells starting at level 51. They will each have a different resistance type. One of the problems with duration based effects is stacking and negating the need for multiple characters of the same class. I am designing these spells with that in mind. If you want to 100% ensure an NPC does not gate you will need more than one wizard. I understand this may upset some people but the need for multiple wizards to "Anchor" an NPC won't really happen until you fight the more epic mobs with higher hit point numbers. For single group encounters one wizard with these spells should, in most situations, be enough to get the job done. My plan is to get these spells implemented and ready to go before the next patch.

Lastly I would like to send a huge thank you to all the players who have reported stacking exploits to me through Email, IRC, and ICQ over the past couple of months. The feedback is greatly appreciated.

Take care all!
/Lakers secret handshake

- Lawrence Poe

Absor said: "Yes, you can trade across teams. I don't see any real reason to prevent people from trading."

There are roleplaying reasons and gameplay reasons. The RP reason is the same reason that you can't group with someone on another team and that is that your god would not permit you to render assistance to an enemy. Trading is the exact same thing. Is not selling someone a SoW potion the same thing as paying someone to cast sow on you? Also, I think you underestimate the number of people with multiple accounts. Heck, I know that if *I* built up a character on another team to the 50's and wanted to start a newbie, I'd certainly give it stuff, just because I can.

From a gameplay standpoint, I think you're missing a wonderful dynamic that preventing inter-team trades would create. If there is a certain item that is considered "great" (such as E-boots), a team can literally prevent other teams from getting them. The dynamic is jealousy. With enough cash, a player could ply an item off of an opponent. But without inter-team trading, that person (and others like him) would be jealous of that race. That creates even more conflict.

And on the other hand, there are some interesting possibilities that not allowing trading would restrict. In fact, in your example, imagine the wealth that can be gained by a team that is able to corner the market on something and then sell it. It also allows players to set up some of their own events, like convincing the evil guild leader to give up some item to a neutral guild to prevent them from aiding a good guild in some action.

I can see both sides here, and I can't see one as clearly better, except in one way. Sometimes it's just plain better to be able to help someone out. So if a neutral character sees a good character that has lost all of his stuff, he can help that person out a bit. I think that's the reason that I'm in favor of allowing trades across teams.

Alan

Conquest Guild Statement Update
GeneralNews [ 3:02 PM EDT ] - Witt
Aradune has posted some more information regarding in response to some comments made by a player over the Sleepers Tomb and Conquest guild incident in the Developer's Corner.
Brad,
I play a Abru, a 60 druid, on the Nameless in the guild Legacy of Steel, and I've been following the disciplinary and exploit mechanics discussions on the net, and most recently your investigations.

While I still haven't even been to Sleepers Tomb, I know, from experience killing a lot of tough mobs, that many of the top mobs require a whole lot of tactics and tinkering with game mechanics to beat, and it can be a fine line between that and an "exploit".

I'm sure you must be aware of this, so why not make it Verant policy to just fix bugs (aka exploits) when they are found, rather than punishing people for being creative?

Abru

Well, first, they're not mutually exclusive. We DO fix bugs AND we go after people who exploit. Now why don't we just do the former? Because in the time between a player discovering a bug and us fixing it, in some cases, a LOT of damage to the game can be done. An extreme case would be duping -- a player exploiting a dupe bug could irreparably harm the server's economy before we had a chance to fix the bug.

In this particular case, while not as extreme as a duping bug, the guild did appear to be conquering one of the hardest zones in the game with far fewer people that what would have been necessary had they not been exploiting. This hurts the game -- more specifically, it cheapens the experience for those groups of people who might have been one of the first groups through that zone NOT exploiting.

No man is an island in a massively multiplayer game. What one player or group of players does can OFTEN affect other players, and so we have to protect the players as a whole by cracking down on those few who would make the game less enjoyable for others via exploiting bugs. And, hopefully, the discipline in these cases will also act as a deterrent, reducing the number of future exploits.
