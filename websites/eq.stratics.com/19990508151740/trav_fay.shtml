A Traveler's Guide to Faydwer

 Introduction   Greater Faydark   Butcherblock Mountains   Steamfont Mountains   Lesser Faydark 

Introduction
Faydwer is a wonderful continent. It houses 4 races and a host of critters. Diversity exists from the hills of the Dwarven Butcherblock mountains to the majestic forests of the Greater Faydark.
 top


Greater Faydark
The forested lands of the Great Fay are home to the Wood Elves of Kelethin and are right outside the doorstep of the noble High Elves. This land is rich with smaller creatures to build your strength upon, yet it also houses some middle level critters that a weak traveler may wish to avoid.

Kelethin can be a Traveler's favorite. It is truly a city of novelty. No where else in all of Norrath can you dance from platform to platform so high up in the trees. The ingenious elevator system easily transports you from the beautiful forest floor to the wonderful city of the sky; however, traveler be warned. This city is hard to learn and harder to stay up in (Partially because of the delicious beverages you may find in the many taverns here).

Felwithe is certainly one of the easiest cities to navigate. Shopping is made easy with the near circular design of the city, whether you need Armor, Weapons, or just some good ol' fashioned gear, you can certainly find it here. Many a quest may be found inside this city, mostly to help rid the land of the dreaded Orcs which permanently endanger Kelethin and Felwithe.

Aside from the cities here there is a healthy population of bats, decaying skeletons, spiderlings, and Orcs. A lucky traveler will catch a glimpse of the beautiful Fay Drake Hatchling or possibly see one of the elusive Brownies. There is also a bandit camp not too far from Kelethin, but it is recommended that you not kill these as they are higher races, and it will lower your faction with either the High Elves or Wood Elves, depending on the bandit.

There is a constant local population of Orcs in the Faydark, ranging from mere Pawns to dangerous (level 5-9) Centurions. They often hide out along the roads around Felwithe, but more so near their own Camp and in Crushbone. The Orcs are aggressive, so watch your step if you leave the main roads to search out Crushbone or the Orc Camp.

Crushbone is an Orc dungeon ruled by Emperor Crush and his Ambassador. This area is located along a steep ridge (Zone wall) and is marked by pyres of flaming logs. Only an experienced traveler (level 7+) should enter, and even then, it is very wise to have a companion.

Regardless of where you enter this land from, you should be able to get to Felwithe with just a bit of aid. From the gates of Felwithe, a road will stretch out in front of you. It will fork; to the left lies the Butcherblock mountains and the Lesser Fay, to the right; Kelethin and eventually Crushbone (Crushbone is not accessible directly by the path, you may with to find a guide in game for help finding it).

Butcherblock Mountains
The Butcherblocks are where you will end up if you have just come from overseas. This land is the connection with the outside world from Faydwer. Those crafty Dwarves have set up camp here also, Kaladim is their city. Weaker creatures do live here; however, for the most part, they live near the city. A low level traveler should certainly watch his step when traversing this wilderness. Luckily however, the Dwarves have set up guard outposts along their paths, so one may always just run a little ways forward or back if you come into trouble.

Kaladim is a wonderful city, though it can be a bit hard to navigate at first. No self respecting sightseer should go to Faydwer and not see the statue of the Dwarf that resides above the entrance to the city! The city itself is underground, and the initial reaction is of the beauty and quaintness of the town; however, the second reaction is most likely "Gee, I have made a lot of money in my journeys, if only I could locate the Town Vault." If the bank is what you seek, head down a level, look for a white building, and I believe there are some Rogues standing outside of it.

The Butcher blocks are home to a host of critters, some friendly, some not so friendly. There are Dwarven Skeletons, Lowland Basilisks, various Orcs, bats, lots of Goblins, snakes, and Aviaks among other things.

 top


Steamfont Mountains
The Steamfont Mountains are reached from the Lesser Fay which connects to the Great Fay, which in turn touches the Butcherblocks. This area is home to some Kobolds and some higher level nasties. My advice to the traveler is to stay on the path unless you are sure you know what you are doing!

Ak'Anon is the city of the Gnomes. Deep in the cavernous city they sit, fiddling with clockwork contraptions, and basically just ignoring the outside world. There is a zoo inside this curious city, and I heartily encourage any sightseer to check it out.

There is some downright unpleasant stuff in various places of the Steamfont, including Gnomish Skeletons (Tougher than they sound!) and even Earth Elementals and Minotars! I'd recommend caution unless you are sure you can handle these or are in a group.

 top


Lesser Faydark
The Lesser Fay is filled with lots of fairly unpleasant things. There is a Brownie village here; however, they don't appear to be too friendly to outsiders. If you see a Brownie, /con it from a distance as some can be QUITE powerful. There is a picturesque tower here where High Level spells are sold, careful with that ladder though.

This is the land of the fabled "Horse." While I have never laid eyes upon it, some claim they have, and from paintings a curious Gnome sent me, they are an impressive beast. This is basically an area for mid levels to fight a bit, but its most critical importance is linking the Greater Fay to the Steamfont Mountains.
