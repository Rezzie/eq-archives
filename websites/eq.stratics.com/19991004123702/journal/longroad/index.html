The Long Road


Journal Visions
 
Steamfont
 
Kaladim
 
Swimming under the Docks
 
Freeport
 
A Hill Giant in the Commons
 
Runnyeye Citadel
 
Qeynos
 
Erudin
 
Hail friend. Pull up a chair at mine table, rest yourself. Eh? You've traveled a long ways you say? Come here to Qeynos from Rivervale, have you? Aye, 'tis a long a long walk, but I have traveled further before. Let me introduce myself, my name is Kryndall, and I am a ranger from Surefall Glade, just north of here. My disposition is that of a wandering man, and I have covered this world from one end to another. My travels led me to the far city of Ak'Anon, the home of the gnomes you may have heard tales of. Having been away for some time, homesickness caused me to begin a journey across the world, from the farthest reaches of Faydwer to the shores of Odus. Let me tell you the tale as we rest here by the fire.

I began in the city of Ak'Anon itself, an ingenious place covered with machinery and robots. Upon deciding to return to mine home, I bought the necessary provisions, and proceeded to the gate to the outside, when I noticed that mine favorite Minotaur Axe was missing! An unfavorable beginning to my journey, but I equipped my trusty combine sword, and proceeded with my task, undaunted. I waved goodbye to the gnomes as I left the city, and proceeded into the Steamfont Mountains.

The mountains are amazing, great geysers of steam rise out of the cracks around them. And many are the wonders that can be seen here as well, including the great windmills the gnomes have constructed. There also lies here a huge sprocket, sunken into the earth, its purpose forgotten. I have wondered what the gnomes had begun to construct with it, but I have yet to find an answer. My path here took a short branch, the first of many on this journey. I found myself in the pass to Castle Mistmoore, where a party of hardy adventurers was doing battle with a stone guardian. I lent my sword to their struggle, perhaps hindering more than helping. Their quarry escaped, and led back reinforcements, whereupon we were forced to flee. Seeing that this was no place for one of my ability, I quickly left and entered the pass that would take me to the Lesser Faydark, a dark forest with many wonders.

In the forest, I chanced upon two sights that I had never before seen. The first was a tower rising up out of the forest, whereupon lived some brownie merchants that sold many a powerful spell. My magic abilities are not of the level for which their spells were intended, so I left them soon after. I then chanced upon a small village of fairies, their tiny houses built out of the tops of mushrooms. 'Twas a wonder to behold. Seeing them well protected by fairy guards, I proceeded on my way to the Greater Faydark.

Ahh.. what to say of this place. Here I have known many a happy hour, doing battle with the Crushbone Orcs, traveling in the city of the High Elves, Felwithe, and walking amongst the wood elves in the treetop city of Kelethin. I first traveled to the infamous entry to Crushbone itself, dispatching some orc centurions that wandered about. Entering the dungeon, I fought centurions and legionnaires, dispatching a slaver as well. Eventually sheer numbers forced me to flee, and I left Crushbone behind me, back into the Greater Faydark. I then proceeded on mine journey, and found the pass to the Butcherblock Mountains.

The Butcherblock is familiar country to me, here I stopped to slay the lowland basilisk and a few goblins on my way to the city of the dwarves, Kaladim. Having restocked earlier, I had no need to enter the city proper, but took the left-hand path around the outer wall, and followed it to the docks where the boat that travels across the ocean arrives. Alas, I had missed the boat by only moments, and here I sat upon the dock for some time till the next boat arrived. I passed the time in conversation with a young dwarf who was fishing there, trying to save up enough money for plate armor. When the boat finally arrived, I took leave of the young dwarven paladin, and set my fortunes out upon the Ocean of Tears.

What to say of this crossing. Very serene, but deceptively so, for sharks infest the waters, and should one fall overboard, death almost surely awaits, unless one is strong enough to swim to one of the many islands that dot the path across the ocean. Upon such small islands one can find the dreadful aqua goblins, as well as alluring sirens who attempt to lure the unsuspecting to their deaths. Also, a wondrous sight may be spied from the safety of the ship, that of a hill giant of some form, standing up to his waist in the water! The ship stops upon one major island, where the Sisterhood of Erollisi makes its home. Here one can stay for some time, although I chose not to. I have heard rumors of pirates on this island, and that they had stolen something of value from the Sisterhood, though I know not of what came of it. Eventually, the great ship made its way into Eastern Freeport, where I set foot upon the Antonican continent once again.

Ahh, Freeport is a city to mine liking. So much that, in my haste, I stepped off of the ship a tad too early, falling into the water! Undaunted, I swam to the docks. Great adventure is ready at hand through the gates to either the northern desert of Ro or to the East Commonlands. The city itself is a winding maze, yet there are signs that help point the way to those who have no knowledge of the city proper. In Freeport lies the Arena, the great battleground so many tales revolve around. I was in a hurry to return home though, and traveled into Western Freeport in order to make my way out the western gate to the East Commonlands.

The Commonlands are a great area for one of my skills, though there is no nearby guild where I might train. The orcs here are not easily defeated by myself, yet I learn much of fighting by doing battle with them, and their belongings are easily sold at one of the inns that lie upon the path here. Following along the path, I crossed over into the Western Commonlands, an area much more dangerous. Here I became involved in a fight with 3 pumas at once, striking them all down. I rested briefly, taking great care to avoid the Hill Giants that make this area home. I find that they do not seem to like to walk upon the hillside, so there I traveled silently along, till I found the entrance to the dungeon of Befallen, where I chanced a brief entry. I killed a skeleton that was besieging a wizard, and then studied the ground inside for tracks, whereupon I decided to leave, seeing traces of mummies, ghouls, and many other undead. I returned to the Western Commonlands, and traveled to the Kithicor Forest, avoiding the route to the dark forest of Nektulos, inhabited by the Dark Elves, where my appearance would result in an untimely demise for sure.

The Kithicor forest is swiftly traveled through, one passes by the burned out homes of settlers. Here one can enter the land of Rivervale. Rivervale is a winding, twisting city of burrows and shops, filled with good-natured halflings. The city and area is protected by their masked guards, and oftentimes one sees the members of the Vale Patrol hereabouts as well. Having traveled through before, I quickly found my way to the exit to the land of Misty Thicket.

Here I traveled quickly, for there is nothing in this area for those who are as experienced as I. However, once past the great guard wall, I proceeded to the entrance to the home of the goblins, Runnyeye Citadel. I slew some of the goblin warriors that guarded the entrance, but avoided the main part of the citadel, as I headed for the entrance to King Xorbb's Gorge, commonly known as the Beholder Maze.

This place can be very dangerous, for it is inhabited by Minotaurs and evil Eyes that will attempt to slay all who pass. I proceeded through, joining battle with a lesser Minotaur whom I promptly defeated. The maze itself is quite confusing, but a trick I learned as a child led me through, that of keeping one hand on a wall, and following it all the way around the outer edge of the maze brought me through it well. I believe I used my left hand, since my blade is borne in my right, and I wanted it free for immediate use. Leaving the Maze, I entered into the Eastern Plains of Karana.

The Plains of Karana are a dangerous place, and one had best be aware of what lives therein before travelling through. Bandits, Hill Giants and Griffons inhabit the land, and they do not take well to travelers passing through. I kept the mountain I emerged from on my right as I traveled, tracking ever so often to prepare for the beasts. Reaching a large body of water, I continued with it on my right as I traveled to the large wooden bridge that crossed into the Northern Plains of Karana.

This I deem the most dangerous part of the journey, for I have seen 3 hill giants and numerous griffons, griffawns, and griffennes at one time here. I swiftly and silently traveled through, stopping only briefly at the gypsy camp where I had once purchased the combine sword now borne in mine hand. The path led me on, with stone markers now pointing the way to the Western Plains of Karana, a place I knew well. Deciding to abandon the path I knew to be watched by bandits, I followed the edge of the mountains around, keeping an eye out for the few hill giants I knew to live here. Eventually, keeping the mountains on my right side, I made my way to the pass to Qeynos Hills, the place where I learned what it meant to be a Ranger.

Qeynos Hills holds nothing that I could not deal with, even the vile Tovax Vmar does not bother me any more, so I sped through my homeland on the way to the cave to my home, Surefall Glade. On the way, I passed by the place where so many have done battle, the home of the Gnolls, BlackBurrow. I entered Surefall Glade and spoke with my guild master, for I was now home. And yet, I found myself strangely restless, as though my task was not yet complete. My journey took me from the end of the world to this place, so why not finish it? Buying supplies, I traveled on to Qeynos, healing some new warriors who were curbing the rat and snake population outside the city. One in particular was doing battle with a gnoll scout, severely outmatched, but my healing was enough to help her overcome the beast. Upon entering the city, I walked to South Qeynos, and the docks by the sea, where the great boat would come to take me across the sea to the isle of Odus. Here I found a man who sold me a new Minotaur Axe to replace the one I had lost in Ak'Anon, and my spirit lifted to once again hold my favorite weapon.

Once the boat arrived, I stepped aboard, and we soon entered the sea known as Erud's Crossing. This trip was much shorter than my previous voyage, and I was soon brought to the land of the Erudites, and their city of magic, Erudin. Here, I found the end of my journey. I had traveled from one end of the world to the other, and I was still alive. My wandering ways will always be with me, but I will never forget that trip, and perhaps my relating it to you will save some trouble if you perchance decide to travel some part of it. There are many ways to get from this land to the next, and my path is not the only one. I have heard tales of Highold Pass, and I will soon seek it out as well as the southlands, where the trolls live. I have even heard tales of lands that lie beyond the three continents, and you may be assured if they are true, I shall one day be found walking there. Until then, friend, remember the traveler's blessing, "May the road rise to meet you, may the wind be ever at your back, may the rains fall gently upon your head, and until we meet again, may Karana hold you in the cup of his hand."
