The Power of Guilds and Allegiances

The Power of Guilds and Allegiances
Written by Anthony "Brimstone" Pearson

I. WHAT IS A GUILD, REALLY?
II. VIRTUAL AND NON-VIRTUAL PRESENCE
III. UNITY AND COMRADERIE
IV. FUTURE OUTLOOK
I. WHAT IS A GUILD, REALLY?

In the beginning, I didn't entire see the big picture. I thought, "Cool, we will have a unified presence in a game - making life in that game easier and more enjoyable. Common cause, common theme. The months turned to years, and we were actually doing a damn good job with running a guild, and attracting people who fit our mold. As with many guilds, we did a lot of growing and had our share of heartaches, successes, fame, etc. Through it all, one thing became quite evident: Our members really, really cared about each other - like a true family. We offered real world advice, a shoulder to lean on (and sometimes, cry upon) - and many other things common with 'REAL WORLD' friendships. All of a sudden, our "Guild" became a family of friends who will ALWAYS support each other in good times and bad.

So, what is a guild, really? A guild is a place to hang your hat, a group that makes you smile and knows when you are doing well, or if you are not. A unity that is so great, that trust and respect are a given in most cases, and reciprocated often. A guild not only has the potential to do great things in the "Virtual" sense, it also has GREAT opportunity to do things in the "Real World" sense as well. As guilds grow, they become a melting pot of talent, creativity, professionalism, diversity, experience, and more. You will find that if you solicit information from your members, that you are no different than a large company, organization, 'club' or 'family'. If 'member x' loses his job from the Engineering Firm, odds are, 'member y' knows of someone hiring for that position. If 'member z' is now going through a divorce, odds are, 'member b' has been there and can offer advice or support. Additionally, we all feel pride for the accomplishments of others - at least our guild does. If 'member c' has been hired by a new company, or perhaps promoted to a senior support role in a game, or 'whatever', we all feel equally excited about it. It is not uncommon for our members to communicate such things openly, and receive praise from others. It is this unity and camaraderie that keeps us growing and keeps us interested in the guild.

Really, a guild is a family, and if managed properly, it can be a family of friends for the rest of your life. Why not? Some of you are shaking your head, saying, "I think you are taking this just a little bit too far." I of course would beg to differ. It is this 'family' and 'community' vision that has kept us growing and introduced us to many, many wonderful people, as well as opened the doors for many opportunities - both in online gaming and in the real world. If I were a prospective recruit and had the choice of joining a 'guild' that was a loose bunch of people that didn't really 'care' about unity, real life friendship, etc., or a guild that had a vision of things GREATER THAN simply playing games - I would choose the latter.

Consider the following: I can go just about anywhere in the world and always be within 100 miles of a guildmate. This includes such places as: Ireland, Australia, Norway, Germany, Panama, Canada and ALL over the United States. I have already met several dozens of members, and never been disappointed with them. They are just as mature and friendly as their online persona. Now, keep in mind that I would NEVER meet someone from the guild, alone, in a non-public place. Trust is a good thing, but it is always better to take little steps and meet people in the safety of a public place, preferably with other friends/guildmates.

II. VIRTUAL AND NON-VIRTUAL PRESENCE

No matter what game you are playing, you should try to get your members into the mindset of, "Teamwork, Unity, Brotherhood". Events are so much better and impressive when you are all together. Three people adventuring/hunting is NOTHING compared to a host of adventurers ready for battle! Imagine the surprise of those people on the Veeshan server (EverQuest) when the LOA hosted a night that consisted of "Running around the world as a 'pack of wolves'". Thirty people in wolf-form, running - flying - attacking. What do you think gets the most attention and respect? Keep this in mind, and always, ALWAYS push the limits of your imagination. Just make sure you push those limits as a TEAM, and support each other. Additionally, our AC faction now hosts "Akalabethian Tales" night in a guildhall. Imagine walking into a dimly lit chamber, greeted by 30 to 40 people, then enjoying a night of story-telling and bragging about past adventures and mysteries. It simply wouldn't be the same if two or three people did the same thing. Again, the presence you build as a guild is KEY. Use your numbers wisely, and PUSH events that require team involvement.

As for non-virtual presence, this simply means that at least once a year, you should TRY to host a REAL LIFE gathering of your members. Pick a place that is centrally located (if possible), or pick a place that is near the person initiating this gathering. If your guild is large enough, odds are, many will make a huge effort to attend. To date, we have hosted about 10 gatherings. Some large-scale, others were considered "mini-gatherings" that had 5 to 15 people in attendance. Either way, this is SUCH a great way to boost guild moral, friendship and the unity. When everyone returns home, gets online and enters the game or chat room - it is never the same again, but better.

About five months ago, the LOA decided to donate money to a charity (Salvation Army) as a guild. We collected about $500+ dollars and sent the payment to the charity in the name of our guild. Needless to say, we felt SO INCREDIBLE and so proud, it was immeasurable. Other guilds learned of this, game developers, other players, etc. - and everyone commented about it. The greatest part is that we affected the lives of many, many children who received Christmas presents due to our donation - something that we hope to do again and again.

III. UNITY AND COMRADERIE

Up until this point, I have mentioned this aspect a few times already. I wanted to again stress the importance of this topic. The fact remains that as a large group of friends, or accomplices, we will fight. It happens. People disagree, people argue, people threaten to quit (or really DO quit), etc. We need to realize that this is normal, and we should use our arguments and differing opinions to foster a better guild environment. Quitting never helps anyone, it only leaves a problem unresolved. I would instead challenge guild members to stick around and TRY TO MAKE A DIFFERENCE if you feel that your ideas are in the best interest of the "GUILD MAJORITY". Quitting lacks so much effort, it lacks involvement, it screams out, "I can't handle being in a guild environment, so instead, I will quit and take the path of least resistance." Well guess what? As soon as you find a NEW guild, you will again face those challenges again and again. It is LIFE. It is NORMAL. It is unavoidable, unless you choose to be a solo player...

So, as they say for married couples, "never go to bed angry with each other". Try to keep your arguments respectful and mindful of the other persons feelings. "TALK" to each other, don't scream or become abusive. Keep your mind open to new opinions and try to list the GOOD and BAD points of BOTH sides of the coin. Player X and Player Y may have different opinions, but perhaps a nice blend of both will EQUAL a superb guild enhancement.

IV. FUTURE OUTLOOK

The future for guilds looks promising. Here is my prediction, and you heard this here first, ladies and gentlemen:

Guilds will become so sought after and so powerful (their presence) that GAME COMPANIES will seek them for advice, ideas, and "MASS BETA SUPPORT". How nice would it be to get a group of players to test my game, knowing that they get along and would work collectively to address the real issues affecting the game design, balance, player support, etc. A 'group of friends' would make a better attempt at supporting a game in beta, knowing that their efforts were unified and appreciated by the company who owned the game. I think that oftentimes, beta-testers play the beta and expect to have FUN FUN FUN, without the WORK that is behind it all. I remember people in beta CURSING because of design changes, big patches, their characters being wiped, etc. Why? Beta testing is mostly about reporting issues about the game and HOPEFULLY having some fun while at it. Guilds (in my opinion) would do this MUCH better than your 'average' solo player/tester. (I expect people to disagree with me on this point)

Guilds will also launch their own games, programs, guides, etc. - such as the F*ckheads (colorful name) did with their INCREDIBLE UO Map utility for Ultima Online. This utility earned them MUCH fame, as did their name... ;)

Guilds will work together to publish BOOKS, MAGAZINES and many other things, making MONEY FROM IT AS WELL! I know that the LOA has well over 300+ original stories, if we put them into a book format and got them published, it would no doubt be a success. There is NOTHING to it but to DO IT!

Guilds will form their own companies - and figure out a way to be profitable!

Guilds will share resources to buy land, property, etc. in the REAL world. Such properties could be used for camping, gatherings, paintball arenas, or just about anything. A $50,000 plot of land is hard for one person to buy, but when you throw 50 people at that amount, it isn't so hard to imagine, is it? We are considering this very thing!

Guilds will pursue patents for conceptual ideas that are SO GREAT that companies will PAY to use their patents in their own games, web sites, etc. If you have a CONCEPT that is good, people WILL PAY to use it. Just make sure you get the patent first, THEN go blabbing about it.

I realize this topic is corny, some of you are laughing or shaking your head. If that is you, you are in DENIAL! ;) But seriously, while some of these things SEEM far-fetched, they really aren't. Many of the ideas I covered here have been discussed wholly or in part by the members of the Lost Order of Akalabeth. We realize that some things may never happen with OUR guild, but that doesn't mean that they aren't feasible. Somewhere, someday, somehow, someway... guilds WILL do the things I mentioned. I belong to a few organizations, such as the Veterans of Foreign Wars, American Legion, Disabled American Veterans, Freemasons, National Adobe Photoshop Professionals, etc. I consider my guild to be no different than any of those other 'clubs'. They own property. They publish magazines. They have a large membership all over the world. See my point? Do you see my vision now?

Well, I hope you enjoyed this article. I would have no problem at all with you copying this and sending it to your guilds, friends or publishing this on your own web sites. Feel free to comment on it, add to it in your own words, etc. I want this to get around, and I am VERY CURIOUS to see what others think. My email address is: brimstone@lostorder.com. I can handle good and BAD criticism, I have been there before and will not have my feelings hurt. So say what you will, so long as I hear about it.

Brimstone,
Co-Founder,
Lost Order of Akalabeth
