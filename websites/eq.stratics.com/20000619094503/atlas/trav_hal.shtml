Traveler's Guide to Halas

 Overview   Prominent Places   Legends of the Land   Neighboring Zones 

Overview
The home of the Barbarians is a stark, cold town made up of mostly wooden and stone thatched constructions and is the Northernmost town in Norrath. The townsfolk are pseudo-Celtic in origin with last names commonly starting with "Mac". Most of the male residents wear kilts and proudly display intricate blue body tattoos. Although Halas may seem stark and uncivilized to many outsiders, the indiginous people are fiercely proud of their home and do not take criticism well. Due to the prevailing girth and height of the citizenry, the size of all wares sold in this town is large. The closest place to purchase small or medium wares is the Human port city of Qeynos. The warrior, shaman, and rogue are found in abundance here. Unlike the more cosmopolitan cities, Halas rarely sees any but the strong and brave due to the confusing maze of glaciers and the fact that visitors must brave Blacksburrow in order to enter.

Prominent Places

Bank of Halas - A two-storied building with an ornate door and sign. Located just inside Halas, it houses the both the banker and a provisions trader.

Cave Mouth Entrance - From Everfrost, the entrance to Halas is a large cave mouth bracketed by two large fires and manned by two barbarian guards. As you enter the cave mouth, there are a couple lean-to tents on the left where you can buy provisions without having to enter Halas proper. The only way to enter Halas proper is to swim or take the fairy across the freezing waters that separate Halas from Everfrost.

McDaniels Smokes* & Spirits - Halas' largest tavern. A favorite of the men and women of Halas, it's customers are often on the shady side of the law.

Rogue's Guild - Ironically, this den of mischief makers is directly behind the Bank of Halas.

Shaman's Guild - Hidden in a side passageway, this sunken area gives shelter to the Shaman of Halas and their spell vendors.

The Pit of Doom - The large arena area dedicated to the training of warriors. This area is just beyond the well of the marketplace and can be glimpsed from Mac's Kilts.

*Author's Note: Although there are several vendors that profess to sell "cigars" and "smokes," I have seen no evidence of actual smokeable materials in Norrath.

Legends of the Land

Dargon McPherson - Lead trainer of the Wolves of the North. He is much concerned with the recruitment and well being of the defenders of Halas.

Karg Icebear - A lone Barbarian tailor who roams the Everfrost plains searching for polar bears.

Margyn McCann and Shamus Felligan - Two shaman who train the shaman of Halas. They are "judge and jury" for those who stray from the way of justice in Halas.

Tinish, Tartain and Tasvan - Little is known about this dark trio who haunt an ancient ruin in Everfrost, other than they smell of death magiks.

Tundra Jack and Iceberg - A Barbarian miner fond of the Everfrost plains. He is accompanied by Iceberg, his giant pet polar bear.

Neighboring Zones

Blacksburrow - The evil home of the Sabretooth Gnolls that plague the Qeynos Hills and Everfrost. To travel between the Qeynos Hills and Everfrost one must cross through Blacksburrow. This is an extremely hazardous trip and should not be attempted by the weak. Even the stalwart warrior attempts Blacksburrow with hesitation as the gnolls tend to attack in large groups (5-30) and can overwhelm even large groups of adventurers. This dungeon is also known for some of its potentially lethal traps, cliffs, and pitfalls.

Everfrost - A frozen maze of glacial crevasses and miles of snowy tundra. Barbarians, Polar bears, Gnolls, Ice Goblins, Ice Giants (more deadly than even the dreaded Hill Giant), Snow Leopards, Wooly Mammoths, Wooly Spiders, Mountain Orcs, and other cold loving beings make their home here.

Permafrost - The home of Ice Goblins, Ice Giants, and much darker beings. Only the most hearty and brave of adventurers should attempt this frozen place of twisting halls and secret passageways.
