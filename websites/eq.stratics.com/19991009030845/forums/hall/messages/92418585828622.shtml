Ok, It seems there are several people playing the game who do not have a basic understanding of economics. I you do know, great, but forthose that seem to have trouble with this subject, here is a small lesson.

The price that a PC auctions an item for is NOT fixed. The price (and hense the value) of an item is what people are willing to pay for it. The price range of an object will usally fall between what an NPC buys it for and what they sell it for, but not always. There are several items (namely special items) that will sell for a lot higher then an NPC would sell it for. I am stating this (which seems pretty obvious to me) because I keep getting the following situation happening.

For this example, I will use dervish rings. These rings give you a +2 dex and +1 ac. The are found on cutthroats in North Desert of RO and have become very common (which is why I am using them as an example). These rings can be sold to NPC's for 2gp. However, NPC's sell them for about 2.5 platinum. Because they are so common place in freeport, people sell them for about 5gp. So, being the merchant, I travel across the waters and sell them to the dwarves and elves. My normal asking price is 1 plat. Why? People over there pay that much. Now, here is what gets me. I auction them for 1 plat. Then someone will reply an auction of "1 PLAT.. thats a rip off. I bought mine for only 5 gold."

Hello... Folks. This is the wrong answer. If you see something that you have being sold (and bought) for more then you payed, you don't yell out that it is overpriced. It isn't. This is the price I sell them for, and people buy them for, so it is the price (or value) of the rings I am selling. What you should be thinking is "wow, I got a good deal on mine." or "hey, if he is selling them for 1 plat, then I can sell mine for 1 plat each and make some profit. Then I will go buy more at 5 gold."

Don't sell yourself or others short. Be a merchant. If someone sold you something underprice, be thankful that you got such a deal. If you can get more at that price, do it and make yourself a profit. But, don't yell out that a price is too high. There are no set prices in this game. That is the whole idea behind a player driven economy. We set the price by bargaining. If you think a price is too high for an item, make a lower offer. I lot of people are willing to drop there price a little. I for one will try for the best price possible, but I am usally willing to lower it.

Think smart and make some money. Get this economy going. Use prices to your advantage. And don't ruin it for those of us who want to play the merchant and not just go hack and slash mobs all day.
