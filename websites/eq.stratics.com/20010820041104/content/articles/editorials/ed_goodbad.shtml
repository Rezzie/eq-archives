The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly - An Editorial
 Introduction   History   The Good   The Bad   The Ugly   Overall Impressions 

Introduction
This article's purpose and mission is to open the pathways of communication between the developers and players of EverQuest. Its intention is not to attack EverQuest or bicker over its development; rather, it is intended to improve EverQuest beyond its expectations. If you have any comments, suggestions, or questions about the information contained within, please email Cold Rain. Note that the issues discussed below will change as EverQuest changes.
 top


History
I would first like to tell you a little bit about the author of this document, Cold Rain. I began hearing about EverQuest when I was playing Ultima Online. My interest began to peak when I attended my first EverQuest Developer Chat - better known as the Games.Net chat - held in April of 1998. If you were there, you remember the excitement and craziness that EverQuest was causing in the online gaming community and the new world which captivated our interest. Following the Games.Net chat, I took a greater role in the EverQuest community by joining the Stratics Network. As time went on, I watch the development of EverQuest with envy for those selected for phase .5 following E3. As development continued, I waited as phase 1 and phase 2 passed. More and more information was released, and my excitement began to grow. With the start of Phase 3, the Stratics Network was given "press accounts" to begin discussing the development of EverQuest and sharing it with fans. After my brief exposure to EverQuest, I have come to the below conclusions. This information does not come solely from myself. I would like to take this opportunity to thank everyone who participates in the chatroom #Everquest on the Stratics IRC. Particularly, I would like to thank Ffil, Tenarrus, DarkSoul, GreenDragon, and all the others who have helped develop these points which I will layout for you and the EverQuest Development team.
 top


The Good

Graphics

  The screenshots do little justice to the truly awe-inspiring majesty of EverQuest. EverQuest allows the user to fully immerse themselves into a realistic environment. Rain or sunshine, the EverQuest world looks much like our own. The graphics are superb, far surpassing the expectations of myself and others.

Class Variety

  The variety of classes in EverQuest makes it truly unique. The differences are distinct, and each one has importance in the world of EverQuest. While some are better at combat, others provide a necessary role in EverQuest, anything from healing the injured to protecting the weak. Your class will play a valuable role in your group, community and world.

Race Variety

  Race variety allows the element of role-playing to be taken to the next level. The variety of races in EverQuest adds an element which other online games never could achieve, individuality. The races allows the player to choose how they will play EverQuest. If players are willing to immerse themselves in the role of their race, EverQuest will gain an element of true fantasy.

EverQuest Development Staff

  For the most part, the EverQuest Development Staff has been actively involved with players in the ongoing development of EverQuest. They seem to welcome opinions and views which may not match their own, and seem eager to help when they can. I often see many of them in the EverQuest chatrooms on their beta server asking and taking questions. Hats off to all the EverQuest staff.

Gaining Levels

  I believe (although not positive) that the EverQuest Development Team has done an excellent job on restricting how fast you gain levels in EverQuest. The graduated system of leveling makes every player work harder for their next level. This difficult and challenging task of gaining levels continues the excitement of EverQuest for each player.

Item Variety

  From eyeballs to magical swords, the diversity of items to be collected in EverQuest is truly amazing. EverQuest always keeps you wondering, "what might be found the next time I defeat this creature or slay this beast."

Monster Variety

  With few exceptions, monster variety in the lands of Norrath is sufficient. From snakes to griffins, danger lurks everywhere in many different forms. You should not have to worry about fighting the same creatures day in and day out. If you search hard enough, you can find the challenge you seek.

Monster Intelligence

  With a recent patch to EverQuest 3.0, monster AI has dramatically increased. Monsters will takeoff running if you hit them hard enough, and they do not just run one direction. They can twist and turn until you loose track of them. In other words, never under estimate the intelligence of your opponent, may it be rat or dragon.

Substance

  Since the beginning of Phase 4, EverQuest has increased its addictiveness by adding alternate means of entertainment. Players can now find a variety of quests all over the world to challenge them. Trade skills have also been implemented to give EverQuest more depth.

PK Switch

  The player killer switch has taken online role-playing to new levels. Those who do not want engage in combat with "Powergamers" or "Random PKers" can now be safe. The PK switch seems to be effective and reliable, although there are far fewer players who want to play as a player killer than those who do not.

The Bad

Name Server

  As many of you are aware, EverQuest will require that your character name fit the rules of "high fantasy". The name server seems to be, for the most part, stable; however, I have seen many players with names that do not seem very "high fantasy". Probably the fault of the players in EverQuest more than a bad name server.

Monster Trains

  It seems like some players are beginning to find a way to player kill people in EverQuest. The method is known as a "train". Essentially, a strong player will go around attacking very difficult monsters and build up a "train" of them behind him. He/she will then run into a "newbie" or beginner area with this train of monsters. The monsters really do not care who they attack, so sometimes they will just attack the closest opponent. So "JoeNewbie" is fighting his giant rat when "BobPowerful" comes running through with an Orc Raider. The orc may attack "JoeNewbie" as he passes. A possible solution is to have the monster lock on to the attacker until the monster is attacked by another player, or discipline those who use this method of player killing. One point that should be made is that if the orc did kill "JoeNewbie", "BobPowerful" could not loot the corpse.

Lag

  With the start of Phase 4 of beta testing, server lag has increased slightly. Highly populated or large zones seem to cause moderate lag on less powered systems. "Lagging out" occurs more frequently than in the past, but still is not a serious threat.

Giving of Goods

  An inherent problem with any online game is the giving of goods from powerful players to weaker ones. Since many of the items are class specific, a level 30 monk has no use for a magical sword, and might just give it to a friend. This kind of abuse is not rampant in EverQuest at the moment, but can and will happen. A possible solution to this problem is put level restrictions on items. For example, a certain magical sword could not be held by just anyone. Such as a level 5 warrior could not even touch a sword designed for a level 25 player; however, this maybe a bit extreme and could cause more problems than do good.

Server Stability

  The EverQuest server is down from time to time for various reasons. Downtimes can be as short a few minutes to as long as several hours. Early evenings seem to be a prime time for down periods while servers are patched.

NPC Interaction

  One of EverQuest's major promises was an interactive NPC environment which has yet to show itself. While I understand this is only phase 3.5 of beta, NPC interaction is limited. You cannot just walk up and start talking with the barkeep as if you were talking with a real person. In fact, most of the time, the barkeep will saying nothing at all. A possible solution is to program some of the NPCs to carry a conversation with players while others merely say "Leave me alone, you vagrant."

The Ugly

Interface

  The EverQuest interface screen screams for optimization. An overall unfriendly and unforgiving system of clicks. The organization itself takes much time to get used to due to the fact that the location of options are unusually placed. For instance, if you want to view your stats, you would click a button on the upper-left hand side of the screen; however, once you are finished, you must click a button in the lower-right hand side of the screen. Secondly, many times you must scroll through a list of items (e.g. skills, chat messages, etc.) by clicking on tiny little scroll arrows unlike traditional scroll arrows. The buttons themselves are very picky on how and where you click. Third, combat is a nightmare when trying to engage a moving target. One must master the ability to use the keyboard and mouse simultaneously. Those who maybe missing an arm or fingers will find EverQuest impossible to play effectively. Please keep in mind that this observation is the impression of just a few testers, not all. A possible solution for these problems is to completely redesign the interface system. Perhaps a system of windows to make the game more universal. I can understand wanting to make something unique, but let's keep this game as simple to operate as possible.

Chat System

  The chat window for EverQuest is lacks much to be desired. When running in 640x480 mode while in combat with monsters, chatting with your group, using other forms of chat (e.g. shouts, tells, says, out of characters), and guild chat, the chat window is not large enough to display all the incoming information adequately. Further, when in combat with monsters, you will find that correcting you typos is somewhat challenging. The chat system seems to ignore keystrokes when in intense combat. There are two possible solutions to this problem: 1) Make the chat window's size adjustable or 2) Redesign the chat system into a series of windows (i.e. shout window, tell/say window, group window, combat window). With the current system, be aware that you might miss the words "Dragon coming at you".

Overall Impressions
My overall impression of EverQuest is very admirable. I still believe that EverQuest holds for many people what other games failed to give. As March 16 roles near, we, as testers, need to be diligent in expressing our views, opinions, and experiences. 989 Studios wants to make a great game, and we want to play a great game. Together, we can make it happen.
