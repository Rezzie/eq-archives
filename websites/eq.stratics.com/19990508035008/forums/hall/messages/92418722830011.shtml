One point worth mentioning. Alot of people, myself included, are new to this game. As such, we have no frame of reference for what something is worth. We don't know what the supply and demand for particular items is yet. People who stop merchanges from taking advantage of these people are providing a service to them. Now you could argue, "You don't need a frame of reference, just decide what the item is worth to you." But that argument doesn't hold up. In the real world, water for example, is extremely valuable to me. I need it to live. However, I wouldn't pay for it since I can get it for free. Similarly, I may not pay 1plat for a ring if I know I can get it for 5gp next time I'm in Freeport.

Just my two cents,
