Leveling Efficiently with a Magiciancentric Group

Introduction

The magician is a strong solo class. Many magicians solo their characters to post level 50 without feeling any real need to group. While there are advantages to soloing (e.g., no experience sharing, no need to look for group, etc.), going solo is not always the most efficient way to advance after you hit level twenty or so. Around level twenty the nature of the game shifts so you can no longer easily solo difficult mobs. The shift in the nature of the game is subtle at first but becomes very apparent by the mid-twenties and impacts your solo leveling efficiency:

  • The quality of mob improves significantly. The mobs post level twenty tend to hit very hard, double attack, dual wield, cast stronger spells and have great tolerance to your melee attacks. The mobs are more social and will usually come to the aid of one of their kind. The mobs get smarter as well. While they are impressed with your pet, they know who is pulling the strings and will tend to prefer you to the pet if you get too close. The result is that white and yellow mobs are very difficult to solo now and you may be limited to killing one or two blues (if even that) safely at one time with little safety margin left at the end of the fight.

  • The game shifts you from the outdoors more to dungeons. Post twenty there are still many outdoor areas to hunt. There are, however, fewer mobs in these outdoor areas at your level and a lot of the really good loot is below ground in the dungeons. The dungeons tend to be far more crowded with social / smart mobs and have fewer safe places to pull. There is a much greater tendency to pull multiple mobs or be caught by several patrolling mobs while you are trying to take on the one mob you intended to engage. The result is more multi-mob engagements and the resulting deaths if you cannot manage the inflow of mobs or quickly escape. Deaths at this level are a real issue since you now lose significant experience when you die. The partial bubble you lose at these levels can take hours to make back up (not to mention the lost time weaving your way through the dungeon to try to recover your corpse).

  • The magician's melee skills and hit points have not kept pace with the mob's. Your melee skills increase slowly and by the mid-twenties you have a very hard time even hitting a blue. Even when you can hit them, you have so few hit points that you cannot afford to wade into melee for too long. The mobs on the other hand have superior weapon skills and appropriate melee health levels. The result is that your pet must do most of the melee and you must cast damage spells or heal your pet to win the fight. In both cases you will use a significant amount of mana and can spend a long time down between fights while you heal and recover your mana.

Overall, then, starting in the twenties you see a decrease in your solo leveling efficiency due to:

  • Less experience per mob (result of taking on blues vs. whites and yellows)

  • More frequent deaths and higher experience loss per death (higher probability of death due to multiple mob situations and harder escape in those situations)

  • Longer downtime between engagements (increased mana usage to bring down a single mob)

The impact on your soloing level efficiency does not mean you cannot solo, far from it. It means that there is a value in the safety and efficiency of a group and our mid-twenty magician should consider what groups and partners make the most sense for him or her.

The Warriorcentric Group vs. Magiciancentric Group

The traditional answer to what group makes sense is the warriorcentric group. We have all seen these balanced groups: A warrior or two to tank and absorb damage, a cleric (or other healer) to heal the tank, an enchanter to control the flow of mobs to the tank, a wizard to provide air cover if a the tank gets in trouble (or another caster shows up), a shaman to buff up the tank, and a tracker (a ranger or druid) to pull the right mobs to the tank. In this group the warriors are at the center and everyone supports them. This is a melee group and very efficient. The magician can play several roles in this group (though not as well as the primary class choice). In many cases the availability of the right classes will limit the group leader's preferred choices and open up a spot for a magician in the group. These groups are a lot of fun and completely address the magician's efficiency issues.

The magician's rub with the traditional well-balanced group is that he really does not need all those group members (nor do they really need you). They are there to support the tank. In reality you can almost solo a small stream of the mobs if you just had a little help. There is no need to spend the time and effort to round up a full group if you can form a small group that supports you. So the issue becomes can we find a good partner class or two to form a small group supporting the magician that can efficiently allow the magician to take on several higher-level mobs with a high probability of survival.

The group we are looking to form would have the magician at the center of the group with other classes supporting the magician's weakness (a magiciancentric group). The group should be small (you can almost solo anyway) to minimize sharing the experience (your pet takes minimal experience in a group regardless of whether you do more than 50% of the damage). The group / partner should allow us to handle multiple blue mobs, a white / yellow mob (possibly two), or a shallow red mob. We should be able to handle these mobs safely with minimal downtime. The goal here is not to duplicate the full warriorcentric group's ability to handle a steady stream of high level mobs, but to allow an extension of our soloing ability from the pre-level twenty era and increase our leveling efficiency. We want to understand what classes the magician should lean towards when there are only a couple of people in an area to pick from to form a magiciancentric group.

Magician Weaknesses and Strengths

To understand how we best form a strong magiciancentric group, we should start by understanding what magician weaknesses we have to bolster and what magician strengths we can build around. Magicians have several weaknesses:

  •  No ability to control a crowd. This is the primary weakness. When the young magician solos in the wide-open space of the East Commons it is very easy to find a good spot, pull to the spot and take down one mob at a time. In the post level twenty world it is not that easy. Mobs are very social and in dungeons tend to quickly group on a solo player. A magician and an earth pet can usually handle two blues with some difficulty providing that both mobs will stick on the earth pet (the pet's root spell helps). If one starts chasing the magician, it becomes difficult to assist the pet and take the pet's mob down (which can lead to a dead magician or wasted pet). In the post twenty world you cannot effectively melee a mob for an extended period of time and help out the pet. The pet needs to be the magician's barrier so he can cast and support the pet and multiple mob situations make this difficult.

  •  No real escape ability. Magicians have very few escape options. Early on our gate spell works fine. You bind just outside the zone and gate when things get bad. In the "one mob at a time" world that escape strategy works fine. When the magician and pet are battling several mobs or a high level mob, things can get out of hand quickly. The gate spell takes a long time to cast so if you do not start casting the moment you get into trouble you will not likely make it out. The magician has no SOW to go fast or snare / root to slow down the mob. The solo magician, then, must either carefully select his battles or fight near a zone.

  •  Significant downtime after a battle (even with blue mobs). In fighting two blue mobs there is not much safety margin left at the end of the fight. Most mana is gone and sometimes health is down too. The magician's pet is usually low on health as well. Now the magician must sit and med for an extended period of time to recover and move on. There are several reasons for this:

    •  Magicians have very low hit points and need to burn more at this level. While you can go toe-to-toe with a blue mob for a little while, you do not do a lot of damage with your staff and mostly provide your pet the ability to get the bonus from hitting the mob from behind or extending the pet's life a few extra rounds. The magician will need to burn more to kill a mob than he needed to in the past. The burn will quickly deplete the mana, especially if a couple of big spells only do partial damage.

    •  Magicians have only two real buff spells to aid a pet. The burnout spell and damage shield are a great help to the pet. The magician, however, has no other way to bump up the pet's statistics, increase the pet's effectiveness, or significantly reduce the mobs ability to fight (the malaise line is a help but still gets resisted a lot). Without these kinds of buffs to increase the pet's melee efforts, the magician has to spend more mana to bring down the mob.

    •  Magicians have only bind wound to heal themselves. With no heal spell, a magician that is taking hits while a mob is chasing him around has a significant downtime to heal. It is not unusual to have to hold off one mob while your pet is finishing off another. With hard-hitting mobs, a magician can quickly lose his health and need to spend downtime recovering health.

The magician has one real strength: he can deal significant damage to one or two mobs very quickly. This is what make the magician so effective solo. This damage has four parts:

  •  Direct damage spells. The magician has the second best direct damage spells (only the wizard is better). This allows the magician to quickly bring down a mob. The necro and druid have DOTs that work well, but they must hold the mob off long enough to let the DOTs take effect.

  •  Damage shield. The magician has one of the best, if not the best, damage shield. It cannot be resisted and starts to hit for significant damage in the mid twenties. Shielding an earth pet can soften a mob very quickly.

  •  Pet damage. The magician's pet is an effective melee combatant. Unlike the magician, the pet can hit hard (including double attack and duel wield) and frequently on blue mobs. It has a harder time with white and yellow mobs but still hits. Melee is efficient and also not resistible. You investment in mana to cast a pet is very well spent.

  •  Pet damage absorption. The magician is not effective while he is being hit. The pet absorbs damage and draws the mob while the magician cast spells from a safe distance. Having the pet take the damage gives the magician the time to cast his direct damage spells and bring down the mob.

In searching for a small group, the magician needs to look for partners that can control crowds, provide escape, reduce downtime, and benefit from strong damage capabilities. There are a variety of ways for partners to accomplish these goals and we will look next at a comparison of potential partners.

Group Selection Framework

Each class has something to offer in support of a magician. Some, however, offer more than others do. We need to develop a framework to evaluate our potential partners and guide us in-group selection. The framework should allow us to evaluate each class based on their ability to correct the magician's specific weakness.

The criteria we are working against, then, are control of crowds, provide escape, and reduce downtime. Each of these criteria can be broken down one more level in to groupings that allow us to evaluate each class's spells, skills, and abilities to bolster the area:

  • Control of Crowds. This area breaks into two additional groupings. The first is control prior to engagement and the second is control during engagement. In the prior to engagement segment are the lull spells, harmony spells etc. They allow us to calm the mobs ahead of time and then pull the one we want. A bad pull here, however, can cause a problem so we would put slightly more value on a partner that can control crowds during the engagement. The "during engagement" segment includes the mesmerize spells, fear spells, charm spells, memory blur spells, taunt skills, etc. They allow us to control the mob once we start the engagement and allow us to continue working one mob at a time during the engagement.

  • Provide Escape. This area also breaks into two categories. The first is mobility adjustment, which includes snare spells, spirit of the wolf spells, root spells, darkness spells, etc. These spells slow a mob down or speed you up so you gain a mobility advantage and can escape. Quick escape is the other category. This category includes wizard and druid evacuation spells, group gate spells, and group random teleport spells. These spells allow the caster to remove the group from a dangerous situation through teleportation.

  • Reduce Downtime. This area breaks into several categories that either increase your mana efficiency or allow you to use less mana during or after the fight.

    • Increase mana efficiency is the first category. Recovering mana is usually the largest cause of downtime for a magician. The breeze / clarity line directly increases your mana regeneration and falls into this segment. Partners that can cast strong direct damage (or DOT) spells are also in this segment, as they will allow you to share the casting of damage spells and conserve your mana. You essentially spread the mana use out across you and your partner so each of you has more mana left at the end of the fight. Also a partner that can deal significant melee damage will require you to cast less damage spells and increase the efficiency of your mana usage. Since mana loss is such a large cause of downtime this segment receives significantly higher weighting.

    • Heal / hp regeneration spells fall in the second grouping. While the magician and his pet are not usually hp limited for downtime, saving your mana by sharing the pet healing duties is helpful.

    • Increase in direct damage spell efficiency is the third grouping. The enchanter's Tashani spell allows your direct damage spells to hit more often and be resisted less. You, therefore, save the mana from having to recast on resisted damage spells. This spell also allows your pet to hit more often on his damage spells and brings the mob down faster thus reducing the number of direct damage spells you must cast.

    • Increase pet effectiveness is the last grouping. This grouping includes buffs for pet strength, agility or other statistics as well as debuffs cast on the mob to allow the pet to kill the mob more quickly. You then need to spend less mana to help your pet bring the mob down.

We will rate each class's ability to affect the above areas (1 is bad / 10 is excellent) and weight the ratings based on their importance to supporting the magician. The total result is a weighted average between one to ten. The results are tabulated in Figure 1 below. With these ratings in hand we can determine which classes make the best sense as partners and then discuss specific strategies on how engage mobs to take advantage of the group's strengths.

The Enchanter as a Partner

The enchanter makes a strong partner for a magician. The enchanter's mesmerize and charm line of spells are the strongest crowd control capabilities in the game. The Tashani spell in not resistible and when combined with the magician's malise line significantly reduces a mob's resistances. With resistance down you can again take on yellow mobs and use significantly less mana, as your spells are not resisted as much. With breeze and clarity your mana regeneration allows for little downtime between engagements and the enchanter has a wide array of buffs and debuffs to help you pet quickly cut through mobs. The only area that the enchanter lacks in is providing escape. His ability to control crowds and root, however, still makes escape a high probability during most engagements. The enchanter's spells work both indoors and outdoors so he is a partner that can help in a dungeon and in the wide-open areas. A magician with one enchanter partner can take on a wide variety of situations with little to no outside help. There are several aspects of enchanter / magician group to consider:

  • Pulling. The goal of the pull is to leave the enchanter open to do crowd control if needed. If you are pulling at a camp or in a dungeon it is possible that more than one mob comes in the pull. Ideally you would like to work on a single mob and pull by having your pet initiate the attack with a pet attack command. This method focuses the mob on the pet and leaves both magician and enchanter free. More than likely, however, you will need to choose a safe spot and send the magician out to pull. The magician should us a low level damage spell (or a summoned throwing knife) to pull and move the mob back onto the pet. The pet then will take over and taunt or damage the mob back onto itself. If more than one mob comes the enchanter is free to mesmerize or charm the extra mobs as needed.

  • Pet Selection. There are really two pet choices for this group. In dungeons you are best to use an earth pet. The earth pet will root and keep the mob from running and also has the highest hit points so makes a good shield. Outdoors a water pet works well. It still has the high hit points but also has a strong ice spell and in the open spaces you may be less concerned with a runner. Enchanter's pets are optional. While the magician's pet is not easy to handle, the enchanter has no control on his pet. Outdoors two pets are usually helpful; in a dungeon they can be a problem.

  • Enchanter Role. The enchanter is the primary debuffer and crowd controller. Prior to the pull the enchanter should buff the magician's pet. As the mob is pulled back to the pet the enchanter should cast tashani to lower the mob's resistance. After the magician hits the mob with malise, the enchanter should apply any debuffs. The enchanter should then hold his mana and watch for additional mobs. As new mobs appear it is the enchanter's job to hold them with mesmerize or charm them and turn them on the first mob. When the pet is done with the first mob it can move onto the next. The enchanter should control the flow of which mob to go to next. If things go bad, the enchanter can use his burn spells to help bring down a mob (this may be needed against a yellow or light red mob that is chewing up a pet). If things are getting out of had and the flow is too high, then the enchanter should control as much of the crowd as possible and call for the group to run with the pet staying behind to hold as many mobs as possible. Overall, the enchanter should buff and be alert for new mobs entering the engagement.

  • Magician's Role. The magician is the primary damage dealer and pet healer. One of the big differences between grouping and solo is the experience that the pet takes. Solo, you lose half you experience to the pet if you do not out damage the pet. In a group you lose a negligible part of the experience to the pet regardless of how much damage you do. This opens up new tactics that involve healing the pet. The magician should damage shield / burnout the pet, malise the mob, then allow the pet to engage the mob. In a single mob situation, the magician should let the mob do most of the work and save mana in case things turn for the worse. As the pet gets down to 50% or so, the magician should heal the pet. Another note is that healing a pet does not aggro the mob so this method keeps the mob focused on the pet. If the pet is losing ground fast the magician should heal once then burn the mob with direct damage. After two nukes, he should evaluate the situation and if things are still going bad the enchanter should do the next burn. They key is to keep the mob on the pet as long as possible then shift him to the magician so that the enchanter can keep looking for new mobs and have mana for crowd control. With breeze or clarity, the use of mana does not create as much downtime so you can afford to let the magician burn more than his share. The magician should also nuke the runners if you are not using an earth pet (earth pets can root to stop the mob).

    In multiple mob situations the magician may want to nuke sooner to bring down the pet's mob. With more than one mob to deal with, the enchanter should have control of the mobs other than the one the pet is attacking. The magician should use the assist command and help the pet dispense with the first mob. With the tashain and malise spell on the mob, the magician's direct damage spells should routinely hit for full effect. Let the pet do some initial damage to prevent the mob from immediately aggroing on the magician. But then hit the mob with several direct damage spells to get the mob down. After the first mob is down, heal the pet and have it attack the next mob. The magician and enchanter should coordinate how many mobs they can handle this way, but if either group member is down below two bubbles of mana, it might be wise to back off and regroup before moving on to the next mob.

  • Mob selection. The number and type of mobs to pull and engage is a function of the risk you are willing to take. With escape options limited, you are relying on the enchanter's ability to control the inflow to allow you time to escape. In general you should try to pull one mob at a time and then manage the stray mob that shows up. It may be hard to do this in many situations. I generally do not like to try to deal with more than three blue mobs at once (a typical mob camp). Once white and blue is also fairly safe. If you pull a yellow mob you generally can park any blue mobs that show up and decide if you have the resources to engage them after the fight with the yellow. The same is true for a red mob. But if you are pulling a red mob, you should be prepared to run sooner if anything other than a blue shows up. Fighting near a zone or guards helps and lets you stay in the fight longer, but you should not attempt to overreach your capabilities, experience loss at these levels is fairly time consuming to regain. Escape sooner rather than later if there is any question.

Overall, then, the enchanter / magician group is a strong pair. The magician can deal damage and the enchanter can control the mob flow. While the pair is somewhat limited in their escape options, the enchanter's ability to control flow makes escape still a very high probability.

The Druid / Ranger as a partner

The druid / ranger classes also make a strong partner for the magician. Their ability to use the harmony spell allows crowd control prior to engagement and since harmony cannot be resisted ensures that the initial pull should go well. Their spirit of the wolf / snare combination allows you to have significantly greater mobility than most mobs and facilitates a high probability of escape (this combination can also help to control crowds after the pull as well). The druid succor line of spells can also allow your quick escape by completely removing you from a bad situation. Their DOTs and direct damage spells also allow you to bring a mob down fast if needed and spread out the mana usage to reduce downtime. The ranger gets his spells later in life, but has good offensive melee damage that he can add significant damage prior to getting some of the more useful spells. The only drawback to this pair is that some of their spells are outdoor only. Even with this drawback they still are a good pair indoors as long as you realize the limitations and are cautious about your pulls.

  •  Pulling. Given the druid / ranger's tracking skills and harmony spell they are clearly the choice for pulling. Outdoors finding a mob camp of blue to yellows is an ideal situation. They can harmonize the camp and choose one mob to pull back to a safe spot. In this situation the group should be located in a safe space well out of the aggro range of the remaining mobs. As the druid / ranger pulls they should snare the mob and bring them back to the pet / magician team. In a more open area the druid / ranger should track specific mobs in the general area of the group and pull them quickly back to the engagement location to avoid dragging other mobs with them. As they get near the engagement location the magician should have the pet buffed and then use one of a variety of strategies to use to engage the mob. Indoors a similar strategy works, but you must be more careful in the pull since the harmony spell does not work indoors. The druid / ranger needs to pull single mobs from close by and if the pull goes bad they need to root or snare the extra mobs to give the group time to escape.

  •  Pet Selection. If the pet is going to tank then the water pet is a good alternative. Indoors, if you do not need the earth pet's root spell, then the water pet is the clear choice. Outdoors if you plan to kite then the water pet or fire pet should be used to allow you to take advantage of their damage spells. Outdoors the water pet also allows you to use the pet as a tank and provides the group the most versatility.

  •  Druid / Ranger Role. The role of the druid / ranger depends on the engagement strategy you are going to use. There are two basic strategies to use:

    •  Kiting the mob. In this situation, the druid / ranger is the bait. Their role is to pull the mob and get it back to the engagement area. They should SOW the group (along with the pet) and buff the pet prior to pulling. As they bring the mob into the ambush, the magician should send the pet in to run behind the mob and hit it. Turn the pet taunt skill off (/pet no taunt) and the druid / ranger then moves in a square around the magician. The pet will hit and burn the mob as the mob follows the druid / ranger. Once the mob turns on the pet the magician can take over and hit the mob with a direct damage spell to bring the mob to the magician. The druid / ranger then takes on the role in the center of the square and snares any new mobs that come into the area. The druid / ranger basically has the role of crowd control and evacuation if things get out of hand.

    •  Pet tanking. In this situation the pet will take most of the damage. As the druid / ranger pulls back to the ambush spot, the magician should command the pet to attack the mob. The pet should begin taunting and hitting the mob so that it quickly comes off the druid / ranger onto the pet. Prior to the pull, the druid / ranger should buff the pet and the magician should damage shield the pet. Once the pet is on the mob the druid / ranger should DOT the mob and then assume an over watch role to snare / root any new mobs that enter the fight. The druid / ranger can also heal the pet without aggroing the mob and can share in the burning if things get bad.

Pet tanking works in all cases whereas kiting is generally only useful outdoors where you have lots of space.

  •  Magician Role. The magician is still the damage dealer and pet cleric regardless of the strategy used. The tactics, however, vary somewhat based on how you choose to engage the mob:

    •  Kiting the mob. The magician's job during kiting is the keep the mob focused on him to allow the pet to hit from behind. As the pet engages the mob when the druid / ranger returns from pulling, you should attempt to malise the mob. The mob is focused on the puller and malise will not usually pull the mob onto you. As you take over the role of kiting the mob, you need to do enough damage so that the mob will focus on you. With just one mob you can run ahead with your SOW and turn and watch the mob. If he is following, stand and wait for him to approach you. As he gets near you, run of 90 degrees from your last path so that you end up working a square with the druid / ranger in the middle. If the mob turns on the pet hit it with a bolt spell to pull it to you.

      When another mob shows up, the druid / ranger should snare it and move it near the first mob. When the two mobs are near each other you can use your column line of spells (or a rain spell) to hit both of the mobs at once and turn them on you. The column line works well since it hits in one wave over a very narrow area. Casting the spell will not likely pull other mobs in the area. Keep using the area spell to keep the mobs after you. The druid / ranger can keep adding mobs to the kiting track as they show up. The druid / ranger may need to take over kiting duties to allow you to meditate if needed.

    •  Pet tanking. In the pet tanking case you can focus on healing your pet or burning the mob down if things are not going well. The druid / ranger should be available to help with the healing or burning if needed. The pet's damage shield and boosted statistics will help in wearing the mob down. The malise spell also helps reduce the mob's resistance so you waste less mana on spells cast on the mob. This strategy is the best one to use indoors and can reduce your downtime by sharing some of the healing and burning with the druid / ranger.

  •  Mob selection. With the druid / ranger partner you need to be more selective in your choice of mobs. It is much more difficult to control the crowds (especially indoors where harmony will not work) so ensuring a good pull on a single mob is very important. Several blue mobs outdoors should not be an issue. More than two mobs indoors can be a problem. You should be able to take on a single white or yellow mob (and potentially a blue if it shows up during the engagement). A single light red should be possible, but your pet can get in trouble fast and it is much harder to get your damage spells to hit. Dual healing the pet may be your only choice.

The druid / ranger makes a good selection as a partner. Their abilities, however, truly shine outdoors. They can provide the magician a way to pull selected mobs at camps and then provide the group enough of a mobility advantage to ensure an escape. Indoors they also are good partners, but many of their advantages are decreased.

Ideally the best-rounded magiciancentric group is a magician with a druid and an enchanter. In this group all the bases are covered. The druid can pull from camps with harmony outdoors. Indoors he can still pull, but then the enchanter can provide crowd control if the pull goes bad. The enchanter's superior crowd control allows you to take on multiple mobs and the druid's ability to increase the group's mobility can ensure escape. With several options for healers, burners, and melee (with a ranger vs. a druid) you can reduce the group's downtime significantly by sharing the mana drain across the party members. This group can handle multiple mobs up to several whites or yellows and safely engage a light red mob with high probability of success or escape. When possible this threesome should be the magician's choice for group support.

Other Partners

The necromancer, bard, and cleric are second tier partners. Each of these classes has some benefits as they play the roles of the enchanter or druid / ranger above. The cleric provides a slightly different role as a partner.

  • The Cleric Partner. The cleric / magician group can provide a different strategy for playing either indoors or outdoors. The cleric provides very strong buffs for a warrior type pet. Using an earth or water pet buffed by a cleric creates a very formidable defensive shield. Buffed with a magician's damage shield, the pet can inflict a fair amount of non-resistible damage on the mob. The cleric can also use his lull or soothe spells to try and pull a single mob from a group (with varying success). The cleric can also use his root spell to slow down the mob's ability to get to the group while you focus on a single mob. With a heavily buffed and damage shielded pet, the magician and cleric can sit back and heal the pet without causing the mob to aggro. If another mob shows up the cleric can root it while the magician burns the first mob down and turns the pet onto the second mob. There is little margin for escape, so this group works best near a zone or with a safe pulling area where you can be sure that you won't have to deal with more than two mobs.

  • The Necromancer Partner. The necromancer can play some of the enchanter / druid role in a group. The necromancer can mesmerize a mob and park it while you work on one mob at a time. With clinging darkness series and fear you can kite with two pets vs. one. The necromancer has strong DOTs if you chose to let the pet tank. The necromancer, however, doesn't really need you that much and may prefer to solo versus group with you. He can benefit from the added safety of a partner but is very strong by himself.

  • The Bard Partner. The bard can provide many benefits though his songs. The problem is he can only twist two or three of them a once. He can mesmerize, heal, adjust mobility, and provide short escape teleports. He is, however, not fire and forget and he needs to be playing the songs to get the benefit (i.e. the druid can SOW and move to heal the bard needs to keep playing the song to get the benefit). He can fill the roles in kiting or tanking situations and makes a good secondary partner.

Conclusions
Overall the warriorcentric group provides solutions to most of the magician's issues. It is not always possible or desirable, however, to join this type of group. With an enchanter and druid group, the magician forms a team that supports his weaknesses and provides ample benefits back. This magiciancentric group can bring down multiple mobs of higher levels with a high probability of survival and restore much of the efficiency in leveling with a group.
