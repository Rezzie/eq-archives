Starting as a Barbarian

Barbarians
What does a Barbarian look like? Barbarians are huge, buffed, stupid, and very cool... so am I. So maybe that is why I identify with them so much! If you've got any Scottish blood or have a girlfriend who is red-headed, plays women's professional baseball and surfs every day (and her name is Alicia) your going to love staring at the Barbarian Ladies too (as I do!). Heck, I even found a Male Barbarian that looks like me, so I'm sold! Shirtless and with a kilt on, I can guess the ladies wouldn't mind taking a gander at us either, and you know what a good Scot wears under his kilt, don't ya? His boots! As for in-game playability and role-playing, part of the coolness of their looks is that they really tower over the smaller "good" races. Even compared to a regular human, they are massive.

Selection of a Barbarian is generally based on a few things. Their natural strength, stamina, and associated "brute force" capabilities can be, in some classes, augmented by great dexterity and agility. The result is a fighting machine! Barbarian Shaman's have some interesting qualities (I hear), but for the most part, if you want to be a crushing blow sort of warrior, Barbarians are your key. There are down sides, of course. Barbarians have naturally low intelligence which slows down your ability to learn skills. It will take you some time to develop that ability to tailor or learn a not so easily practiced skill. Your charisma is somewhat low, so I would expect your interactions with NPC's might be hampered (although I have yet to leave the Halas area). I would expect that as those mega hit points accumulate you will, no doubt, be the "bait" in any group-based monster traps of the future. But having played a human rogue for a long time and then starting a Barbarian rogue, I can crush low level monsters with ease now! I'm looking forward to that backstab ability that multiplies damage times 3 or 4 as well.

Halas is a nice place if you're fond of frozen wastelands. The shopping abilities in the neighborhood are somewhat reduced, but everything you need can be found readily. The forest of EverFrost is the immediately surrounding hunting zone, and I have been extremely impressed with the level of loot available in the low difficulty monsters (and that's all I've seen thus far). You'll find yourself hip deep in little critters that /con at "even fight" by your second or third level, and without armor you'll be able to beat their brains in if your smart about your tactics. Halas, so far, doesn't seem to have all the intrigue and secrets that us rogues dream of, so I don't know how long it will be before I leave "home" for ventures beyond. As a starting place, I found it easy to get to 6th level in about 6 hours though, so its not a bad home to have!

When starting a Barbarian, your immediate prey is goblin whelps and gnoll pups. These are relatively easy for a 300 pound kilt wearing stomping machine such as I. I teamed up with one of those massive red hared beauties and stomped the crap out of several hordes of them. She was a Shaman, and I noticed that she was having a little difficulty solo. I'd suggest teaming up a healer and a warrior type (low level rogues are basically warriors). Let the warrior take lead, and let the healer keep you happy. As a shaman, she had a pretty good ability with her club even so. The numbers of animals and monsters right at the gates of Halas is enough to keep you busy, and you will soon appreciate the fact that the monsters attack YOU rather than you having to chase them down individually. Given the high loot factor in the zone, your need to run back to town is reduced, and you get lots of money piled up relatively easy. On many occasions the loot from Goblin Whelps would included backpacks and bags that made loot carrying much easier. Normally these are a bit expensive and require a run to the store every ten to fifteen kills. Further, there are vendors in the local hunting area who can convert your loot into readily useable cash quickly and easily.

Down sides include the relative lack of interest in the Barbarian race. This means that fewer people are in the area and very few are of other races. Further, as Barbarians are fighting machines, they are generally out fighting instead of learning to sew patchwork armor. The result is that very few auctioned items are for sale, and this makes early level armor a bit harder to come by. This isn't too big a deal because you can pound monsters to your hearts content, but you'll have to kill hundreds of itty bitties to get from level 5 to 6. While a human and living in Freeport, I saw a Barbarian babe covered from head to foot in chain mail; however so, I think your best bet is to hold out and buy the good stuff. She looked like a killing machine!

The Barbarian lands are pretty simple. Barbarian characters have a nice home to start out in. They have lots of loot and lots of power to get it. You'll have very little problem finding your way around to begin with. You won't be a raging success with wizards and necromancers (in fact, you can't be one), but if slamming your fist into a gnoll's head and feeling his bones crunch are your gig. You'll love it. I wouldn't recommend it if your interested in being a spell caster and, as a Barbarian, I think one of my first missions will be to find a nice Erudite spell caster to be my friends and cover my arse as I wade through the hordes of monsters I'll be killing in the near future.

Good Luck!
