Druid Overview and Half-elf Background

Many people considering becoming a druid never even think about the Half-elf. In many ways, Half-elves have lived with the stigma of being neither Human, nor Wood-elf. Most druids look down on the Half-elf druids as well, but for different reasons. However, the Half-elf druid can be just as powerful as any other druid, but in diverse areas.

Druids are masters of nature and the enemies of all summoned creatures. With this, most animals will perceive the druid to be their ally, and kindly allow them in their territory. However, summoned creatures and some extremely territorial, aggressive animals will attack druids without provocation. In order to combat this, druids can charm many types of animals and deal substantial amounts of damage to summoned creatures, even higher than most wizards! The Half-elf druid is best suited for charming animals to fight for him, instead of against him.

Druids have many limitations for armor, weaponry, and melee skill. Druids are only able to use natural weapons, made of wood or other materials. The exception is the scimitar, whose speed and precision can be the best tool a druid has in his fighting arsenal. Druids can also only wear natural or magically enhanced armors. This includes the lower protection armors; Cloth, leather, mesh, reinforced, and raw-hide. The druid has a few choices for specialized armors once he has gained many seasons and has become a master druid: Vermiculated, Elder Spirits, and Withered. These give many different kinds of enhancements, such as spell casting without using the druids natural energy - mana, statistic increases - such as stamina, strength, and wisdom, and higher protection in melee battle.

The other side of a druid is his spells. A druid has many types of spells and is considered a 'priest'. A druid can attack with the various forces of nature, mainly fire but also lighting, rain, hail, wind, and earth. Druids can summon other forces from nature to assist in combat, like roots or vines to slow or even stop the movement of an opponent. They can help themselves and their allies by increasing their strength, offense/defense, movement rate, or ability to withstand magical attacks. In addition, the druid can heal and cure many infirmities.

In essence, a druid can do a little of everything, but is a master of his environment. Many young druids, with awe of the magical powers available to them, focus on this ability alone. A Half-elf druid has no chance to have the ability to cast the larger amounts of spells the Halflings and Wood-elves can without resting, but he has his own advantages.

Exiled by the Humans and Wood-elves, Half-elf druids reside in Surefall Glade, outside the Human city of Qeynos, in the hills. Here, druids train along side of rangers, their nature-loving cousins. There are caves and a waterfall in the glade, near the hollowed-out tree of the druid guild. The Qeynos hills, Blackburrow and West Karana areas allow for exploration of what your druid can and cannot do, as well as learning many tactics that apply to large groups, small groups, and solo hunting.

Beginning Statistics

Half-elf druids have some of the lowest starting characteristics of the druids. Charisma and Intelligence a tied with all but the Halfling druids, wisdom is the lowest, strength, stamina, agility and dexterity are all tied for second highest. These stats allow the Half-elf druid to be the most versatile type of druid in the game, as they all relate to melee offense and defense.

At creation, most younger inexperienced druids try to increase their wisdom, as it relates directly to the amount of mana they have and the likelihood that a spell, once begun, will result in the desired effect. This urge comes from a certain attitude and inferiority complex. Many measure the worth of a 'priest' by the size of his mana pool. This can be confusing and frustrating.

Half-elf druids will never be the best druid caster in the game, and thus should look for alternatives to assist them in their travels. A druid cannot inflict the massively damaging blows of a warrior, or attack from a distance with thrown or launched weapons like arrows or throwing daggers. With the right balance of dexterity, strength, and agility however, a druid can become a decent assistant tank with the quick scimitar in hand and a shield to soak some damage. Half-elves are best suited for this, as they have more strength, agility and dexterity collectively than any other race of druid. The dexterity increases the chances to hit, the strength increases the likelihood of hitting for the maximum damage, and the agility decreases the amount of damage and how often the druid gets hit.

Strength also allows the Half-elf druid to carry more items. The Half-elf druid could be the pack-mule of the group, carrying the arrows and components for the other classes with increased strength. Conversely, the Half-elf druid could go into a dungeon empty of all but backpacks, bags and boxes and fill up to carry the loot out - leaving the tanks unencumbered. Druids gain a strength buff that magically increases the Half-elf druids strength, further enhancing this ability.

Agility greatly affects the ability of the Half-elf druid to dodge those damaging blows. This can become essential once a pack of angry monsters attacks you, seeing you hit 4 of them for large amounts of damage at once with the druid nature area-attack spells. It also decreases the slowing effect of being encumbered with too much weight.

Dexterity does two things in melee; it increases the chance you hit the monster, and it increases the chance a special spell effect on a magic weapon takes effect or processes. Druids at times have to play a solitary role, as another class may come along that can heal better, cast direct damage spells better (nuke), buff better, or melee better. A Half-elf druid has less mana than other druids, so one way to make up for this is to have a greater ability to give melee damage in a fight. One the mana runs out, you better be able to hit the monster or run.

Stamina is necessary for all druids. Half-elf druids need a little more. When you give or take damage, your stamina allows you to continue moving. Every time you swing a weapon, cast a spell, or get hit a little bit of stamina goes away. It will eventually regenerate once you rest. Stamina also gives you a few extra hit points for those really close fights. Long-distance running also takes stamina away. Half-elf druids need all of these, as they melee more, run more, and generally get hit more. Stamina also increases armor class, but not by much.

Types of Half-Elf Druids

Many people see druids as one-dimensional, having only one way to play. Nothing could be further from the truth for the Half-elf. The Half-elf druid can be all of the following types of druids and can switch between them quickly when needed. There are also different types of armor and jewelry that assist the druid in his various roles.

Caster/Nuker Druids

Half-elf druids have the least amount of mana to begin with. The Caster/Nuker druid uses a wide range of techniques to achieve his goal: killing the monster he's attacking with spells without being hit. Half-elf druids normally can take on only lower-level monsters with this tactic as they run out of mana too quickly. However, this is still very possible when the Half-elf has lots of wisdom enhancing jewelry.

Healer Druids

Druids are the second best class when it comes to healing. Healing is not normally as mana-intensive as nuking, as it can be timed and allows for meditation between casting. Most Half-elf druids can tell better than any other druid how much mana they have left, and how much mana a healing spell will take out of them. Because of this, they can get their timing down better. As healing is more of a group tactic, the Half-elf druid Healer tends to seek out groups rather than go solo. Some kinder, gentler Healer druids may even heal people not in their group, as they run by.

Buffer Druids

Half-elf druids often enhance their melee abilities with things called buffs. A buff is anything that increases the base statistic through a magical spell. Half-elf druid normally use this ability so much, that they quickly reach the maximum skill allowed at each level. A druid can buff many of their own stats and can also provide special defenses like thorns that spring from their skin to harm any creature that attacks them. Some of these buffs can also be applied to others, specifically strength, thorns, speed, and hardening of the skin.

Tank Druids

Druids can take a lot of damage when they have items and spells that increase the amount of hit points they have. The spell line that hardens skin is most effective in helping here. As Half-elf druids have more agility and stamina, they can stand toe-to-toe longer than the other races of druid. Their higher strength and dexterity also allows them to give some of that damage back. At higher levels, however, the ability of the druid to be a tank decreases significantly as the monsters hit harder and faster. Still, there are the occasional monsters that Half-elf druids can tank in a group in the lack of a warrior/fighter class. However, it goes without saying that a warrior is the best choice for a tank.

Puller Druids

Druids can run faster and dodge better than many classes in the game. The animal-specific line of spells also lets the druid be picky as to what, how many, and how often a group of monsters attacks him. The picking of one or more monsters from a group and bringing back to the group to kill is called pulling, and is an essential skill to learn. A Half-elf druid can do this better than any other druid by using every statistic at once. Casting, meleeing, dodging, and running all help the Half-elf druid become an efficient puller. I've heard many people comment that druids make the best pullers in the game and I believe this is due to the Half-elf druids.

Travel Druids

At higher levels, druids increase their ability to travel and to bring others with them on their travels. If you have problems with abbreviations, get really familiar with this one: SOW (spirit of the wolf). Oh, and here's another: TP (teleport). One last abbreviation is EVAC (evacuation - also known as succor). Druids can run faster, longer and can magically transport themselves to stone rings that were set up by their ancestors. The more senior the druid becomes through his adventures, the more easily he can travel. Many friends and even strangers would like a druid to help them travel. Unfortunately, most brash adventurers EXPECT you to help them and can become quite rude. Try to be polite and understanding, as the better you relate to people, the more this ability can work positively for you. For example, a donation from an elder adventurer can be sizeable when you help them in their time of need. Conversely, you should try to help those less experienced than you when they cannot offer anything in return. Half-elf druids can travel just about equally to other druids, but their charisma, agility and stamina are all higher collectively that other races, and allow them to be more successful in getting to their destination unharmed.

Special Abilities

Half-elf druids have only one more ability over humans, the least endowed with special ability druid. All druids get the ranger ability to forage up food and water without using mana, health or stamina. Half-elf druids also get infra-vision which increases their ability to see better at night. A fire-beetle eye in the main inventory slot or in the hand will enhance this at close distances. Wood-elf druids get hide, and Halfling druids get sneak and hide as well as a slight movement speed enhancement. In the special abilities department, there's not much special about Half-elves.

Conclusion

Half-elf druids can do a little of everything better than all other races of druid, but are not the best at anything. This can force the Half-elf druid to embrace this, but can also send the druid into denial. The more the Half-elf druid can adapt to his situation, the more appreciated that druid will be by others. In the end, if one is content with one's circumstances, those around them will accept and appreciate them. - Motto of the Half-elf druid.
