Wizard Guide Levels 1 to 20


 ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 This guide is a continuing project. I will update this with every new expansion/patch
 ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Selecting a Server

You can do what I did and pick the name of a server you like. Or you post on our Server Boards and find some people you like. Then play on their server. Its all up to you.
 
 - Creation

 Pick your race, Erudite, Human, High Elf, Gnome, or Dark Elf. All have their advantages and their disadvantages. For a
 start, Erudites have the highest starting INT, making achieving the maximum of 255 INT quite easily attainable, but
 they are also night blind and have one of the worst newbie zones in the entire game, Toxxulia Forest is very dark and
 Can be confusing to navigate.

 Humans are an all around race, having pretty average statistics. They are also night blind. But have decent newbie zones
 in both of their starting cities.

 High Elves have a higher than average starting INT, quite close to the Dark Elf. They have innate Infravision(Night Vision).
 But. the High Elf newbie zone is shared with the Wood Elves. and there can be Kill Stealers at times.
 Gnomes have a very nice starting INT, newbie zone, but their city can be very confusing. They also have Infravision. and
 They're really short and quite aggressive to taller races, very fun to role-play.

 Dark Elves have a very nice starting INT, newbie zone, great looking city, fun to role-play, and have Ultravision (best night
 vision in the game). but they are considered evil and are not welcome in many cities other than their home(Neriak).
 
 
 - Level 1 to 4

 -Erudite:First of all, hand in your newbie note for a robe. Now scribe your spells 'Shock of Frost' and 'Minor Shielding'.
 Now memorize the two spells and cast 'Minor Shielding'(The manual that came in your box will describe how to do this). Now,
 you MUST take the boat to Qeynos, as much as this city displeases me and my fellow wizards. It's is a lot better for leveling
 than Erudin's newbie zone is. You can also get a 'Port' or 'TL'(Translocate) from a high level Wizard to a place called 'West
 Karana', then ask them to 'Evac'(Evacuate) you to the 'Qeynos Hills' zone. I would then ask for an Invisibility, so that you
 can get to Qeynos unmolested, Zone into 'Qeynos Hills', you will find yourself in a small valley, exit this valley. You will
 now see a path. Follow it until you reach the crossroad and then turn to your left. Follow the path to the Qeynos zone, once in
 Qeynos, follow the path to the 'Gate', then shout "Can someone please bind me at the gate" a mid level Wizard (12 or higher) will
 be able to help you here. If they are busy, respect them. You wont want to be annoyed by people wanting binds all the time when
 that level. Once you have your bind, go out and kill Rats, they will sometimes drop a "Plague rat tail", this sells for 1 platinum
 or more. Once you have some money(about 5 gold) ask someone to lead you to the caster guild, there. find the merchant that sells
 your level 1 spells. You can buy whatever spell you like, but I'd save my money for my level 4 spells. Once you make level 4 from
 killing rats. Go buy your level 4 spells and give yourself a pat on the back. most of my wizard characters didn't make it that far.
 I gave up at level 3 from frustration a lot, but take my advice. don't the Wizard is a very fun class later on.
 
 -Human:First of all, hand in your newbie note for a robe. Now scribe your spells 'Shock of Frost' and 'Minor Shielding'.
 Now memorize the two spells and cast 'Minor Shielding'(The manual that came in your box will describe how to do this).
 Go out and kill Rats, they will sometimes drop a "Plague rat tail", this sells for 1 platinum or more. Once you have some
 money(about 5 gold) ask someone to lead you to the caster guild, there. find the merchant that sells
 your level 1 spells. You can buy whatever spell you like, but I'd save my money for my level 4 spells. Once you make level 4 from
 killing rats. Go buy your level 4 spells and give yourself a pat on the back.
 
 -High Elf: First of all, hand in your newbie note for a robe. Now scribe your spells 'Shock of Frost' and 'Minor Shielding'.
 Now memorize the two spells and cast 'Minor Shielding'(The manual that came in your box will describe how to do this). You as a
 High Elf have two choices, get a port to 'West Commonlands' or 'North Ro' and run to Freeport, get a bind among other things. Or you can stay in
 'Greater Faydark' and level. its up to you, but I prefer to go to Freeport, can be frustrating at level 1 but the newbie zone there
 is a lot better. read hunting part of human guide if you go to Freeport. If you stay in Greater Faydark. Just kill everything that
 cons blue/white/black.
 
 -Gnome: First of all, hand in your newbie note for a robe. Now scribe your spells 'Shock of Frost' and 'Minor Shielding'.
  Now memorize the two spells and cast 'Minor Shielding'(The manual that came in your box will describe how to do this). Now go out to
  Steamfont. if you have trouble. find a brown metal gnome looking thing called 'Guide <roman numeral>' Hail it and say 'where exit'. then
  follow the metal gnome to the exit. simple, once out in Steamfont. kill anything that cons blue/white/black. In particular rats. for their
  tails.
 
 -Dark Elf: I can't really put anything here. I will create a Dark Elf wizard in my spare time to make you a good guide worthy of the Dark Elves
 
 - Level 4 to 8

Woot, you are now level 4 and have the spell 'Root', this spell will completely stop a monster in its tracks. so you can cast on it till it is
dead or Root breaks, monsters have a chance to break root every 6 seconds. And every time you nuke. The place I really recommend is North Ro,
on the grass there are tons of blue things to kill. this will work until about 7. At 7 go to the 'Platform' in the desert. There is usually
someone here that will help if you get in trouble. kill Jackal's and Orc raiders until 8. Then do anything blue conning. East Commonlands is
Great exp too, just find some blue cons on the sand near the tunnel.
 
 - Level 8 to 12

Now we have some useful nukes at our disposal. :)
At level 8 you can group at the orcs until about 11. Then the exp slows down, hand all the belts you get into "Captain Hazran" for great exp
and some coin. At 11 I suggest Dervish camps in North Ro. until 12, or 13. As long as you can bear it.
 
 - Level 12 to 16

Now we have a couple nice choices. 1 is High Hold pass, 2 is Paludal Caverns. Paludal is getting rather crowded now, and is getting hard to find
group. but it is very good exp. High Hold pass is VERY fast exp at 12, and the exp here will do till 20. just remember to bind close, wherever you
choose to go
 
 - Level 16 to 20

Woot, More spells. More choices, at 16 you can go group in LOIO, but this place is crowded and you have to turn off shout /ooc before you enter the zone.  Many people will have bad manners. I recommend staying in Paludal, or High Hold pass.
 
Well, that's it. the 20-40 guide is coming soon! Watch for the spell usage guide too!
 
 -Fsck, EQ Stratics News Reporter, Wizard class portal manager.
