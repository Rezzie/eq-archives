Friday October 24 2003
EQOA: Frontiers Exclusive!
GeneralNews - 8:34 AM PDT

EverQuest Stratics is proud to host a four part series of articles written by Alex von Minden, Community Relations Manager, EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers! These articles will provide great new information about the new Class Mastery system included in EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers. Frontiers is the 2nd edition of EverQuest Online Adventures (EQOA). You may read the first article here. Or here:

    By Alex von Minden, Community Relations Manager, EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers

    Welcome to the first article in a four part series detailing the exciting new Class Mastery system included in EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers. Frontiers is the 2nd edition of EverQuest Online Adventures (EQOA). The original version came out in February of this year, bringing the first Massively Multiplayer Online Roleplaying Game (MMORPG) to the PlayStation 2 computer entertainment system in North America. Frontiers, scheduled for release mid-November, will add a host of new features to the game, including:


    New Playable Race, the Ogre - These massive beings are brutal warriors and wield powerful magic

    New Playable Class, the Alchemist - Become a master of powerful spells and abilities

    New Areas - One continent, three cities, hidden planes, newly discovered zones and forbidden areas

    Updated Graphics - Redesigned and highly-detailed player character models, armor and "pets"

    New Items - Hundreds of new items to acquire, barter, trade and sell

    New Non-player-Characters (NPCs) - Dozens of new and fierce non-playable characters to battle

    Level 60 Cap - Increased player character cap (60) for greater character development

    New Quests - Challenging new quests to discover and conquer

    Along with all that, you also have the inclusion of a Class Mastery system that adds all new depth to character customization as they advance. Starting at level 6 you can choose to divert a portion or all of your experience toward gaining points that you can spend in the Class Mastery system. At any one time you can have up to 25 times your level in mastery points, with a total possible of 1500 points at level 60. The cost per point scales up, with the first points costing 250,000 exp, with the last point costing, well, a serious amount of time for a level 60 character. So, mastery points are not that easy to come buy, but you can gain some pretty cool and powerful abilities.

    Once you have points to spend, you can place them into five different categories:



    General: Masteries that everyone has access too.

    Race: Each race has different, unique masteries available to them.

    Archetype: In EQOA there are four Archetypes of classes: Casters, Priests, Tanks and Melee. Each class archetype has their own specific masteries available to them.

    Class: Every class has their own masteries.

    Master Class: These are the very top end masteries that add unique, powerful new abilities to your character.



    Each of the above categories can be considered its own progression tree. The more powerful masteries in any given tree could have level requirements, statistic requirements like a particular minimum strength or stamina score to purchase it, for example, or lower masteries that you must first obtain to gain the higher ones.



    Looking at the names, you can probably get a good idea of what different masteries are about in that particular class. General – stuff everyone can use, like increasing base statistics or resistances, being able to hold your breath longer, increasing health or power regeneration rates, etc. Racial masteries add some extra “flavor” or “color” to each class, pulling from different aspects of that particular Race: Haflings can make some nice Pies, Trolls can regenerate faster, Gnomes can tinker up a clockwork companion, things like that. Archetype masteries enable further statistic improvement in stats that are more important to those classes over what you can gain through General masteries. Class specific masteries generally enhance or expand upon defining traits of particular classes: Clerics could improve their ability to combat Undead creatures, Warriors could improve how well they take blows or gain combat abilities, Monks might spend points on “stances” that improve their offensive capabilities, etc. Master Class abilities are just that – very powerful and hard to obtain abilities that well, are pretty cool.



    Keep in mind that, even with a possible 1500 mastery points possible to obtain, any one character could only gain roughly half of all available masteries. So, in how you spend your points and what upper end abilities you will unlock will make your character different from others of the same class and race combination. You can, if you find you have spent points incorrectly, delete points to allow you to reconfigure how you have spent them. The system allows characters to become more specialists in different aspects of their roles, adding further diversity to the already expansive and immersive world of Norrath.



    In our next installment we will dig further into the specifics of the General and Class specific Masteries to see how you can enhance existing aspects of your character. The following article will focus on the Race and Archetype abilities to see ways that you can gain new abilities for characters, along with giving an exclusive glimpse at the Master Class abilities. Our final installment in this series we will give some specific examples to see the life path of a couple possible choices of the many, many different courses characters can take in Frontiers.


    See you next week!



You will find exclusive screenshots in the article along with a desktop background image available in four different sizes (800, 1024, 1280 and 1600)! Grab your copy here!

Each week for the next three weeks we will be releasing more information about EverQuest Online Adventures: Frontiers so be sure to check back for more great information!

Monday October 20 2003
Upcoming November Updates
GeneralNews - 9:46 AM PDT
Here's a list of upcoming updates for the November Update from Absor:

Here are some things we're working on for the November update: (Please note: my producer is making me tell you that in addition to this list, we'll still be bug fixing and maintaining the live game and that, while we hope to get each of these features in, nothing is certain. But this is what we're working on at the present time.)

New LDoN raids will go Live - We be moving the two new raids that were introduced on Stormhammer to the Live servers. These raids will prove an interesting challenge for the best of guilds.

Another new LDoN raid - We will also be bringing one more new LDoN raid to Stormhammer with our next update. This raid will be created with Elemental capable characters in mind.

Font changes - Since EverQuest was launched, there has only been one font available. With the next update we plan to introduce the option for players to choose the font for their interface.

Targeting - Sometimes it can be difficult to tell which NPC you have targeted. With the next update we hope to add a targeting indicator of some sort (something like the ring that surrounds your target in EQOA). The exact form hasn't been decided, but the goal is to make seeing what you currently have targeted much easier.

Consider - Along the same lines as the proposed improvement to targeting, we want to add a way to distinguish the /con color of your target. We plan an indicator that will be easily distinguished once you have a target (probably someplace in the Target window), that should remove the need to use the /con command.

Other interface changes - We are also working on some other changes that will help you to organize your information. We plan to add a new text color option for damage shield reports that will allow you to pick what color those messages use. We also want to split the "My Pet Attacks" filter into two separate filters, one for hits and one for misses.

/tell windows - It is very easy to lose /tells in the confusion of combat. We want to add an option that would work much like an instant messenger. Each time you get a "tell" from a new character, a new chat window would open. That window will only display "tells" from the particular character, and any message sent from the chat window would automatically be sent as a "tell" to that character. Obviously this would be an optional feature.

Help for new players - New characters will start with a bag. This will give them a little more storage space and the opportunity to learn how storage containers work earlier rather than later. We are also working on removing one of the more tedious "features" of the game, the need to practice Sense Heading. Most folks have that skill mapped to trigger along with one of their movement keys. Not an exciting skill to practice. So we plan to have all characters start with a 200 skill in Sense Heading. This will allow new players to navigate more easily, and will reduce a bit of tedium over the course of gameplay.
Enjoy!

The Story of the Dawn Brotherhood
As the largest consortium on Norrath, the Dawn Brotherhood holds sway over everything from the glittering shores of Erud's Crossing to the barren wastes of The Frigid Plain. Given the Brotherhood's humble origins, few could have guessed how powerful their union would one day become.

In the days before the pact of Halas Humans, Barbarians, and Dwarves lived primarily in small, scattered communities and bowed to three different kings. Though forced by a scarcity of natural resource to be neighbors upon the northern tundra, they were neither allies nor enemies, preferring to keep company with their own kind. But news from the outside world was becoming increasingly dire. To the east, the Tier'Dal warred upon the crumbling remnants of the Elddar Empire, an act unthinkable in centuries past. Word also rushed from village to village that dark armies had been seen on the march, possibly angling for an attack on the newly constructed barbarian city on the shores of Loch Lir. As the evidence began to mount
that the isolation of the northland people might well spell their doom, the three kings agreed to discuss an alliance of their peoples. It was a meeting that almost happened too late.

On the eve of the great conference as representatives from all the northland bands gathered in Halas, an Iksar army invaded the city. Under the cover of night, Barbarians, Humans, and Dwarves fought side-by-side in a desperate, pitched battle against an enemy they frequently could not see and definitely had never encountered. Realizing only at the break of dawn that they had repelled the enemy attack the three kings vowed that their people must always stand at each other's sides. And thus was the Dawn Brotherhood born.

Determined to secure the liberty of all their kind, the Brotherhood blazed south on a mission of liberation. In the northern plains of Karana they freed the Erudites, a magical offshoot of humanity who had been plagued for decades by savage Blackpaw Gnolls. Upon the Plains they also encountered the swift, mounted warriors known as the Kerrans, and quickly won the cat-like people over to their cause. In only a matter of months, they claimed all the western lands north of the Plainscutter River, a feat unrivalled even by the
former Elven empire to the east. The second great consortium had staked its claim upon the lands of Tunaria.

Friday October 10 2003
Augmentation changes for Update - PLEASE READ
GeneralNews - 4:50 AM PDT
An important message about these augments:

As of our update on October 9th, the Solid Coal of Prowess, Blook Ruby of Understanding, and Ooze-Covered Pebble of Perfection will no longer be valid augmentations. If you had these augmentations bonded to an item, you will find them on your cursor. You can take those items to Dible Hedledrap in South Ro and he will restore your adventure points.

IMPORTANT NOTE: You MUST have some open inventory space to hold these items! Also, if you zone with those items on your cursor, they will disappear. So when you log in before the update, make sure you have some free space in your inventory. And when you log in after the update, make certain to get those items off your cursor and into your inventory.

This change is happening due to an issue with some of the new LDoN items. These three augmentations will be replaced with three similar augmentations that fit into slot 7 or 8. Along with that, pre-LDoN items that were given slot type 1 will now have slot type 7.

Essentially, this will mean no change for those with these augmentations in their pre-LDoN items. It will, however, restrict the use of multiples of these augmentations on LDoN items. We apologize for the changes, but these items needed to be more restricted to help maintain game balance.

Alan
Enjoy!
