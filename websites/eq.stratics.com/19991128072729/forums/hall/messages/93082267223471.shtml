Well I agree that there should not be that much experience for a quest. His example of a merchant doesn't need exp. Why would a merchant need to be above level 3. Trade skills are not limited by level I think, so the merchant player could become rich if he has a good means of making money until his skills are up. He can finance his skill with investors who get a return on his sales later. But like UO, this is not a great character to be on PvP. The Trader player would have to be tougher due to traveling risks.

Quests that involve combat should not give exp, the combat is the exp.

The loot: if it is easier to get loot, then everbody will have more money and there will be more items in the world. This will not effect prices unless they limit items in the world. Players will have more money to learn blacksmithing which could lead to more banded armor and lower prices. Then nobody would want to invest the time in Blacksmithing because it does not pay.

I would like for NPCs to approach a player (of a certain lv, race, and faction lv) for a quest. I mean if they can attack you, then can't they come up and talk to you? I hate seeing NPCs sitting around with their thumb up their butt if they are not merchants. I don't care if there is exp in it, I think it would be nice to be approached that way. It's kind of a recognition thing. Also, the quest would be for the appropriate lv that way. This would not be spamming, because the NPC would only ask say once every 15 min.
