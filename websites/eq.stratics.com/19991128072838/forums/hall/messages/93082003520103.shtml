I think he made a valid point that leveling needs to be primarily through combat, not questing. Just for the skills increases. The majority of the time though, your skills will max out long before you level. Spellcasting and Use skills (i.e. forage) are easy to increase just by hitting a key or clicking a icon. Swordmanships, offense, defense, kick, bash, dodge, parrying etc all require combat to increase. I usually max out on all of these at about 2-3 bars of exp. The rest could (in theory) could be gained by questing and it wouldn't hurt the character a bit.

I think quest should be much more complex, but at the same time more rewarding than they currently are. Take the trueshot bow quest. That's a good example of a fairly complex quest, yet the reward isn't even noticable at the level you can complete it. You can do it as many times as you like though. After about 20 times you might see a pixel on your exp bar and have items (all totaled) around 2 plat in value.

At the same time, it makes sense that we're doing a quest to help someone besides ourselves. Like in real life, we often help people without any notion of a reward. Since NPCs are just computer generated and AI controled, it might make it seem less like your helping someone and more like your playing a game.
