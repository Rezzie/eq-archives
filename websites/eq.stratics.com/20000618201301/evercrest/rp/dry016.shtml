Downward to Neriak
Fiction by Drysart

The journey to Neriak was quite dangerous on its own. At several points through the Nektulos Forest, Megaera, Barishi, and Drysart were forced to change their path to avoid a guard barely visible in the forest ahead. In fact, its a wonder they weren't discovered as its well known that dark elves can see much better in the dark than high elves and halflings. Of the three, the halfling Barishi seemed most comfortable in the dark forest, and he led the way around the guards and in the general direction Megaera had pointed that the entrance to Neriak lay.

Megaera was right, Drysart would never have found the entrance on his own. Even as they approached the cave in the mountainside and stood within feet of it, Drysart didn't notice it until Megaera stepped up to it and pointed it out specifically. It was only a small crack in the side of the mountain, barely enough to squeeze through.

The cave inside had much more space than the tight entrance. In the dark, it could be seen that the cave continued back and down into the mountain, presumably into Neriak itself. Before they continued on, they sat and prepared for what might be waiting for them deeper in the caves.

Drysart and Barishi each had only the stated goals, to recover the boy and to bring back the body of Vinalrii that it might find a place to rest in honor. Megaera wished for those things, as well that she might somehow help her sister Sithiya.

With little conversation, they began their slow descent into the mountain.

******

Inside Neriak proper, the scene was quite different. The dark elves were quite active, more than usual. They were, in fact, preparing for a ceremony. Their spirits were high and they were totally unaware of the party that was beginning to breach their city.

Lord Garish watched the elves scurry around with a muted sense of amazement. Quite easily the brightest thing in Neriak, Lord Garish carried a torch since orcs can't see in the dark nearly as well as dark elves can. He knew what matters were afoot in Neriak that day and he actually quite bothered with them. He never told any of the elves about his hesitance.

Instead, he found a quiet corner and pulled a scrap of parchment from his vest. A page. A page that his loyal goblins had stolen from the wizard Drysart many months past. Drysart never did find out what was written on the page in the strange language, but Garish knew. He was hired by Rubicant to recover the page, but Rubicant's extreme desire for it had piqued Garish's curiousity about the page, and he didn't turn it over. In fact, Rubicant still thought Drysart had it.

If Drysart had learned what was on the page, it could most certainly have led to the total extermination of the dark elves from Norrath. Garish realized the power of what he had, and he kept it with him at all times. Because with that same power, the dark elves could easily overtake all of Norrath.

He folded the page back up and placed it in his vest pocket. The ceremony was about to begin, and he was quite interested to see it. He headed down the now quiet streets in the direction of the Hall.

******

By the time Megaera, Barishi and Drysart made their way to the city itself, they were quite surprised to see how deserted it was. Even the guards who routinely patrolled the streets were conspicuously absent. Slightly set aback by the quiet city, they nevertheless used extreme caution in moving about. Perhaps, they figured, the dark elves knew they were coming and prepared a trap.

Their caution in moving about the streets was actually quite unnecessary, since it was no trap at all. Every dark elf in Neriak was in attendance at the ceremony taking place in the large red-bricked building that the party approached.

The building was immense, larger than anything in any of the cities on the surface of Norrath. It stood out in the city as not only the largest structure, but because of its color. It seemed to almost glow red.

Still being quite cautious, instead of entering through a door, the three managed to scale the building to a ledge and peer in through a window to the ceremony taking place within. Rubicant was standing and addressing the crowd. Behind him was a closed coffin. Drysart could only assume what was inside it.

"What are they saying?" Drysart whispered. The ceremony was in dark speech, of which Drysart only had rudimentary knowledge. Fortunately, Megaera was much more fluent in the language and she began a running translation of what was happening below.

******

"Brothers and sisters of Innoruuk," Rubicant began, "I've brought you all here for a matter of great importance. Allow me to introduce myself to the younger among us as I have been away from the city for quite a length of time. I am Rubicant N'Ariqi, crown prince of Neriak, and High Priest of Innoruuk."

Slight cheers washed over the crowed but quickly dissipated as Rubicant continued to speak.

"Behind me is the only daughter of our King Erist and the late Queen Poranni (long live the Queen), Vinalrii. She was slain by the treacherous high elves, namely one called Drysart Pennoble."

At this point it might be a good time to remind the reader that Neriak is ruled by Matriarchy. The Queen is the primary sovereign of her people, and the heir to the throne is the Queen's eldest daughter. It is for this reason, and the reason that Vinalrii was the only daughter of the Queen, that a worried murmur began to spread through the crowd.

Drysart was quite stunned to hear it as Megaera translated. Vinalrii had never mentioned anything of the sort to him.

"And obviously attempting to make a point, the dastardly high elf mutilated her body after slaying her. Our best clerics were unable to bring life back to her body as a result of its mangled condition."

The murmur in the crowd began to sound angry.

"However," Rubicant paused and smiled, "as she is the only heir to the throne, we did what we could. Our clerics were able to almost totally repair her body. It was at that point that we turned to our best Necromancers for help."

The crowd fell silent. Drysart's jaw fell open. Barishi's eyes grew wider and Megaera's voice cracked.

Rubicant turned and opened the coffin. Inside it was Vinalrii laying peacefully. She was dressed in a magnificant gown. The clerics had done quite a job and she looked almost alive.

Another dark elf walked onto the stage. Drysart vaguely recognized him, but could not place a name. He was carrying a cup formed from a half-skull. He slowly approached the coffin, chanted quietly and poured the dust from the skull onto Vinalrii.

Rubicant watched, then turned back to address the crowd. "My brothers and sisters. Witness the rebirth of Vinalrii, the undead Queen of Neriak."

As if on queue, Vinalrii slowly sat up in her coffin. She brushed the dust away from her eyes and opened them. Her eyes had changed. They were no longer the beautiful purple eyes Drysart had known. Instead they were entirely red save for a small black pupil in the center of each.

The crowd erupted into cheers. Rubicant went on speaking and Megaera began to translate, but Drysart had been worked into a fury and leaped from the ledge to the ground and headed toward a door to the Hall. Startled, Barishi and Megaera both leaped down and tried to stop Drysart.

The fury of what they had done.... nothing was going to stop him.

Nothing that is, except for the fact he had ran right into the orc Lord Garish who was storming from the Hall with the same fury. It was the first time the two had seen each other since Garish resurrected Drysart on the Ocean of Tears months before.

Both seemed slightly startled to see the other. Megaera and Barishi stopped in their tracks, neither knowing what to expect.

"Come with me, Drysart... I have something that may interest you."

Garish took Drysart's hand and led him away from the Hall. Megaera and Barishi followed. Neither Drysart, nor Megaera or Barishi quite knew what was to happen next.
