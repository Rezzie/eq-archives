ISSUE # 166

 March 18th - March 24th, 2001
The House at Clue Corner - Issue 166

Welcome back to another edition of The House At Clue Corner where we provide only the finest in hints for the confused, directions for the lost, and cluecards for the utterly misled. Our clues contain no additives or artificial ingredients and as always, Free Advice costs only one silver piece. Free cup of coffee with every clue. No Refunds.

This week Clue Corner is going to re-prioritize what we'd like to see in new online games. We've got a bit of a reputation here for babbling on and on about roleplay and immersion, but priorities change. It's amazing what a night spent trolling the message boards with a few beverages can do for your outlook on things.

Sometimes when you ask people why they play online games, what keeps them coming back, they will tell you it's The People. They make friends, form guilds, and even after the game becomes stale to them, they'll stick around because of The People. Let's have a look at some of The People that keep us all hanging around playing the game.


The Lost Boys -

These guys are the stray dogs of the gaming world. They find a game in beta and start following it around, yipping at the heels of the developers and rolling over on their backs for a belly rub at the least sign of attention. They set up camp on the developer's porch, ready to defend the new master from any threats of criticism, be it constructive or not. Because this game will be The One To Make All Their Dreams Come True, and the developers will love them forever and ever, unlike those other guys who turned on them and refused to speak to them each personally and implement all their suggestions and fix all their complaints. This will be the place they can finally call Home.

In time, of course, the blush will wear off and they'll move on again looking for a new home, but not without chewing up a few shoes and soiling the carpet.


Brad, you can't EVER do anything to this game to make it 'right' to me. I've tried and tried and tried to communicate to you people from the get-go. I started out positive and wound up negative. Can't take the time to personally respond to a customer? Fine. My money goes elsewhere. Think it's all a big joke? Fine---hide and watch when we all go to the new companies that really do care about us, our issues and our money. - Alcarian

Alcarian will now take his money and go find a game developer willing to personally chat with each and every one of their hundreds of thousands of customers and give them each what they want. He and all the other Lost Boys will keep searching until they find someone that Really Really Cares.

Then there are The Sheep:

The reason our household holds such a NEGATIVE attitude to the company that is VERANT INTERACTIVE is the TOTAL lack of HUMANITY and general laco of respect that VERANT presents to the public. I say household because we hold 6 EQ accounts. Yes it is the opinion of our household that Verant customer service is aloof and at times extremely VINDICTIVE to the player base. WHY?! Is it these boards. No not all. We rarely frequent here. What gave us this perception then? I could give examples but I think we all know what Im talking about here. All one has to do is listen to one's friends in game, other message boards, bannings for ridiculous reasons like Mystere, etc...
- grungetoy

Oh, the humanity of it all. Grungetoy is Very Upset with Verant. Nothing actually happened to him or to anyone else in his massively EQ household to make him upset, but he has heard that other people have become upset. In a fit of empathy, Grungetoy has tied his shorts into so many knots over it that he is one small fart away from becoming airborne.

Baaaa humbug.


There is one subset of players that live on a completely different planet:

The vast majority of industries have representatives that actually TALK TO and LISTEN TO their clients. The MMOG industry's version of that is the message boards. - hiddekel


I'm not happy with my Sony TV set. There's a little door on the front that hides some buttons I never use. Who needs them? I have a remote. The little door flopped down one day and now it won't close. Let me just call up the president of Sony and tell them to get rid of those little buttons…and that damn door…because OTHER industries TALK to and RESPOND to their CUSTOMERS. They CARE…and the CUSTOMER is always RIGHT and I AM the customer and if they don't listen to me I will GO BUY A TOSHIBA, because those guys will love me and care for me and TAKE THE DAMN BUTTONS OFF THE FRONT OF MY TV, and TALK TO ME…personally..whenever I want.

Ahem. Where were we? That's right, we were changing our mind about what's important to us in games.

It's the People, stupid.

The next game to come along that manages to attract a playerbase of mature, calm, intelligent, witty, entertaining players with a good deal of perspective is going to be our holy grail here at Clue Corner. We won't care what the features are, whether it's PvP, or how kickass the graphics might be. The first priority of the game must be to attract the kind of people that any reasonable person can spend a bit of time with without going insane.

Someone needs to start a gaming site that doesn't bother reviewing the games, but analyzes the customers instead.

Those are all the clues we have for you this week from the House At Clue Corner. Hope you enjoyed the coffee.
