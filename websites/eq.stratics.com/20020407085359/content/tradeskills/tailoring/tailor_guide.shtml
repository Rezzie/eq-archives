Vaalyn's Guide to Tailoring
Introduction Beginning Tailoring The Advancing Tailor Continuing Advancement Conclusion
Introduction
Tailoring is a relatively simple, and remarkably inexpensive, trade to have some fun with.  As a starting character you can, without a single moment spent practicing in your guildhall, garb yourself in fine hide armor.  Later, you can make your magic-using friends and monk allies happy with gifts of raw silk, and later make your monk companions even happier with cured silk armor and magical fighting gauntlets.  Add to this the profitability of sturdy backpacks, archery-enhancing quivers and thick bullwhips, and you have a well-rounded craft to be proud of!

So now that Ive touted the benefits of tailoring, lets find out how to use this wonderful skill.  Keep in mind that others may tell you to do things differently--this is just my experience on the matter.
 top
Beginning Tailoring
You have a few choices for yourself here. 

Many choose the schooled route, which is attending your masters in your guildhall and absorbing their wondrous knowledge by clicking on the tailoring button, and then training madly until you are thrown out for harassment.  This will get you to about 20 skill points in your craft.  Others prefer the more hands-on approach of killing animals for their hides or purchasing those hides from vendors, acquiring various patterns, and crafting patchwork armor for those first 20 points.  Yet others hunt spiders for their silk, and combine the silk into swatches for the first 18 points, prior to switching to patchwork.

Of course, regardless of the method, you will need advance from skill of 18 to 26 by making patchwork armor.  For this stage, you will need a sewing kit, large or small, patterns, and patience.  Here are the correct combinations you will need to learn.
  • Silk Swatch (advances you to 18 skill points)  place two non-stacked spider silks in your sewing kit and click the combine button.  Note that while the silk is not stackable, the swatches are!  Save them up for later.  Spiderling silks will not work for this.
  • Patchwork Armor (advances you to 26 skill points)  a ruined pelt (wolf, cat or bear) and an armor piece pattern (nearly any will do).  Note that if you are successful, you will make a profit by selling the armor to the merchants.  The skin is free (or one to one and a half silver if bought from a merchant) and the patterns are 5-6 silver.  You can sell the patchwork pieces back for over a gold!  Also, if you carry a stack of 20 patterns, you can make light armor to replace those heavy skins as you hunt.  Patchwork armor requires just one skin and one pattern placed in the sewing kit; click combine, and if youre successful youll be holding a piece of patchwork.
Remember, wolf, cat and bear pelts make small, medium and large armor respectively!
 top

The Advancing Tailor
Once your skill reaches 26, your choices are more limited.  But one thing is for certain; you will need silk, and lots of it!  Get yourself hunting any big spiders and collecting the silk.  Make swatches like mad.  Then you are ready to make some raw silk!  This will advance you to 37 skill points.  Here are the combinations you will need to learn.
  • Raw silk robe and pants  two swatches (non-stacked) and a tunic or legging pattern.  I think you have got the idea that youll need to click combine right?
  • All other raw silk pieces  one swatch and one pattern.
This step is also great, because you can sell the armor to casters and monks for around a platinum piece per point of armor class.  If you are in a pinch, you can sell it to the merchants for some quick return on your investment.
 top
Continued Advancement
Now you get into the harder stuff (and to the point where my experience does not give me precise skill levels).  Youll need friends that are smiths, brewers, and enchanters to do much of the items I will tell you about.  I would love to tell you the advancement degrees, but in truth, I am not one hundred percent certain when these items become second nature to you.  I do know, however, that I am listing the armors in their correct practice order.  You will have to experiment to see what the trivial levels are.

Studded Armor
Studded armor is wonderful stuff!  It offers some wonderful protection at cheap prices for lower-level druids.  You will need metal studs, and lots of them, so make friends with a blacksmith or become one yourself.  All armor pieces require a medium quality pelt of the appropriate size, a pattern, and one or more studs.  Oh, and remember, never ever yell Need to buy studs! or you will not like the response.
  • One stud pieces  Mask
  • Two stud pieces  Gorget, Belt and Bracers
  • Three stud pieces  Cap, Mantles and Sleeves
  • Four stud pieces  Cloak, Gloves, Pants and Boots
  • Five stud pieces  Tunic
Cured Silk Armor
Make your monk friends excited!  Give them cured silk!  But first tell them to learn brewing so they can make you the darned heady kiolas you will need.  Each armor piece will require one or more of the noxious drinks and one or more silk swatch.  Ill list them each with swatch/kiola numbers.
  • Gi (tunic)  3/2
  • Sandals, Gorget, Wristbands and Sash  1/2
  • Pants  2/3
  • Mask  1/1
  • Gloves, Sleeves, Mantle and Cape  2/2
  • Headband  1/3
Remember, it is all very light.  This is a nice moneymaker if some needy monks are around, and can sell for up to 2 platinum per armor class.  Unless you are really successful, I wouldnt suggest trying to make money selling it to merchants, though.

Reinforced Armor
Oh, joy!  Reinforced leather armor!  Better than leather and a wonderful little bit of protection for druids who havent got the super druid armors yet.  But youd better be good friends with a blacksmith, as you will need lots and lots and lots of steel boning.  All the pieces require a high quality pelt of the appropriate size and one or more steel bonings.  Oh, and remember, just as you didnt want to yell for studs, never ever yell Looking to buy boning!
  • One bone pieces  Mask, Gorget, Belt and Bracers
  • Two bone pieces  Cap, Mantle, Sleeves, Cloak and Gloves
  • Three bone pieces  Pants and Boots
  • Four bone pieces  Tunic
This sells better than the studded armor by far, and if you want to sell it, Id suggest you travel to Rivervale or the Faydark.  Otherwise you will face a big cash deficit at this stage.

Other Odds and Ends to Help Advancement
These are not the only armors you can make and practice at by any means.  You can also make Shadow Silk, Large Shadow Silk, and Inky Shadow Silk.  These items are not trivial at a skill level of 45, but are a little pricey and the components can be hard to get if you arent a necromancer!
  • Shadow Silk  One silk swatch, one shadow wolf pelt, one scroll of Gather Shadows (available at your local necromancer 7-11)
  • Large Shadow Silk  One silk swatch, two shadow wolf pelts, one scroll of Gather Shadows
  • Inky Shadow Silk  One silk swatch, one shadow wolf pelt, two scrolls of Gather Shadows
Great, now you have this stuff, what do you do with it?  Why, you make a necromancer happy and make him some Shadowbound Armor!  Here are the pieces you can make!
  • Boots  shadow silk, scepter of tears (Mistmoore), eye of shadow (Shadowed Men) and a skeletal toe (from Rathe Mountains)
  • Gloves  inky silk, scepter of shadow (Lower Guk), hand of shadow (Shadowed Men) and a skeletal finger (from Rathe Mountains)
  • Robe  large shadow silk, werebat wing (Unrest), mask of shadow (Shadowed Men) and globe of shadow (Nokta shaman in Guk)
Once you collect these items, bring them to the temple of Solusek Ro and give them to the appropriate person (you can hail the temple denizens to find out what they offer).  Oh, and the robe is pretty spiffy:  AC 9, Intelligence/Agility/Save vs Fire and Cold +4. Other Wonderful Salable

Tailored Stuff
Wus Fighting Gauntlets

A monks must!  They light the way as their fists pound monsters into submission!  You will need an enchanter to make you some vials of viscous mana for these.  Heres your combination:
  • One glove pattern
  • One silk swatch
  • One greater lightstone
  • One vial of viscous mana
  • Four heady kiolas
Sell them for whatever some desperate monk will pay!  Just dont gouge them.  Ever see a cheated monk get revenge?  It usually involves a herd of angry monsters coming at you and the monk feigning death.  Not a pretty sight.  You may even want to give them away knowing this.

Backpacks
Ten slots!  Get yourself the pattern and a high quality bear pelt and go to town--these lovely toys sell from 50-150 platinum coins, so save those bearskins!

Fleeting Quivers
Six slots!  Some folks will say you have to use a high quality bear pelt, but they are wrongyou can use high quality cat pelts for this.  Much easier to find and makes the failure rate much easier to handle.  Now, I know you will think that these arent in demand (and boy are you right!), but that is only because people dont know what they can do!  First off, from experimentation from a ranger friend of mine, the fleeting part of the name cannot be ignored.  He noticed a decent speed increase on his archery, to the tune of five or so percent!  Second, the quiver will automatically draw arrows from your inventory to keep itself filled, so you wont have to shift those 300 arrows about so much.  Still, dont expect to sell tons of these.

Whips
Oh, lovely whips!  They are actually decent one-handed slashing weapons at 7 damage/33 delay, great for lower level characters or those who want a hard hitting whip without camping for one.  The original recipe called for the dreaded medium quality wolf pelt, but you can throw that theory aside!  No more irking the rangers of Surefall to make your whips!  Go kill yourself some lions and pumas and get some medium quality cat pelts.  Put a pelt, the pattern and four heady kiolas in the tailoring kit and voìla!  You have a whip!
 top

Conclusion
So there you have it, all the current ins and outs of the tailoring craft gathered from the far reaches of many discussion groups and through experience.  So go out in the wilderness and remember each and every time that bear, wolf or lion snuck up on you in your younger years and robbed you of time and experience and repay them by killing them, skinning them and selling your new clothes for a profit!
 top
