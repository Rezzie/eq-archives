Patch Message 11/28/01
GeneralNews [ 1:33 AM EST ] - Sparrowhawk
The Patch message has been updated:

Patch Message 11/28/01

** Server Outage for Shadows of Luclin Launch **

In order to update our server for the launch of EverQuest: Shadows of Luclin, all servers will be taken down starting at noon on Monday, December 3rd. They will be brought back up again on Tuesday, December 4th at 8 am (Pacific Time - which is -8:00 GMT).

We regret the down time that setting up the new expansion requires. Shadows of Luclin offers some exciting new content as well as a fantastic new graphics engine. We hope that you'll agree with us that Shadows of Luclin is well worth the extended patch time.

Thanks for your patience.

** DirectX 8.0 or higher Required for EverQuest **

When we update the servers on December 3rd for Shadows of Luclin, we will also be updating a lot of the code for the existing game. This code is written specifically for use with DirectX 8.0 and higher, and should provide better performance to most of our customers. The improvement will be most noticeable for people using newer hardware.

This will require that all EverQuest players update their DirectX to version 8.0 or better to play EverQuest. We will be providing an instructional page to help people with this upgrade in the very near future at www.everquestlive.com. If you want to test your DirectX 8 configuration and drivers sooner rather than later, you can do so by running "TestEverQuest.exe" in your EverQuest directory and logging on to the Test Server. Please view the patch message there for information should you have any problems logging in.

- The EverQuest Team

Directx 8.. Its Coming
GeneralNews [ 2:35 AM EST ] - Sparrowhawk

The new dll file is increase the fps / performance by alot with my geforce3. It's the same for several friends as well.

SG_01 wrote:

When I try to start the game with DX8 enabled, it gets as far as the launcher, then it quits, also, I think I'm missing the DLL file I need to do this, is there anywhere I can download the file? it doesn't download with the launcher, as well as the test launcher!
Well, I just deleted my eqgfx_dx8.dll file and the patcher downloaded it for me just fine. So I'm not sure what your specific problem is. But let me post and review the message from the Test server.


November 23rd, 8:00 pm
------------------------------

*Your help is requested*

As we announced previously, we are beginning testing of the new DirectX 8 graphics engine that will become a central component of EverQuest. This new graphics engine will go live mid-next week, and will require that all EverQuest players have DirectX 8 or better installed, along with suitable drivers from your video and sound card manufacturers.

The new engine will include many features including a new visibility solution which will dramatically increase framerate in some areas of the game, and in addition will eventually support cards featuring Hardware Transform and Lighting (GeForce/Radeon or better cards).

We would like your assistance in testing this engine with the multitude of system specifications employed by EverQuest users before it goes live. To do this, please launch TestEverquest.exe from your EverQuest directory, and edit the line in your EQClient.ini in your EQ directory from "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx7.DLL" to "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx8.DLL".

Please email any newly experienced problems (cf. problems that you do not have using the EQGfx_Dx7.dll) to luclinbugs@station.sony.com. Be sure to include a description of the problem, the dbg.txt file from your EverQuest directory, as well as your DXDIAG.TXT, which can be obtained by running dxdiag.exe and saving information out to a file.

You will be able to run on normal EverQuest servers using the DX8 DLL once you get it as well. Just remember that if you have any problems you need only set the GraphicsDLL line in your EQClient.ini back to EQGfx_Dx7.dll.

Please take the opportunity to log on to Test and let us know about any problems in order to minimize the chance of problems when the DX8 engine goes live.

Thank you for your help.
Let me start by restating that we appreciate any help we can get with this. Thanks.

There are a couple of important things to be sure of when you try this out.

1) You need to have DirectX 8.0 or better on your system.
2) You need to make sure you have drivers for you sound and video cards that support DX8
3) You then just have to edit your eqclient.ini file, changing "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx7.DLL" to "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx8.DLL". All you really need to do is change the 7 to an 8. :)

If you experience problems, the first thing to do is make sure you've got steps one, two and three right. After that, send us your information to the email listed above. And if you can't get it working and you just want to play, all you have to do is go back and change that 8 back to a 7 and you'll be using the old dll file.

Again, thanks for your help.

Alan

Luclin Requirements, Take Two
GeneralNews [ 1:37 AM EST ] - Sparrowhawk
Absor stopped by the EQ Developer's Corner to update us on the requirements for Luclin:

conna_eq wrote:

Greetings,

I've seen mentioned several times where people can "turn on" the new graphics. Can you give a bit more detail on this? Will the new graphics option be something that's permanent once selected? Something that's set on/off per account?
Something that's set on/off per computer?

The reason I'm asking is because one of the computers I play on is a laptop. I know you don't "officially" support laptops, but it works fine with all the current expansions. I'm fairly sure that if I have the option of turning the new graphics off when I'm on the laptop then turning them back on when I'm at my desktop, things will remain as playble as they are now.

Thanks
You will be able to turn the graphics on or off separately for each of your computers.

You will also be able to change your choices. You won't be locked into any graphics restrictions you make now because of your current system. So if, for example, you turn off a few of the new race models because your system doesn't have enough RAM to run them all, you can turn them back on later. This will all be available through .ini file settings as well as through a setup option. But you'll only be able to make these changes out of game.


JackDRipper wrote:

Horses. What about horses? Will we be able to see them if we have to use the old models with SoL?
In order to see your character riding a horse, you will have to have both horses and your new character models turned on. If you decide to not display some of the new models you will not see any player characters of that race type on horseback. The old models just can't ride horses.

Len_Decairn wrote:

Hardware T&L:

It is not completely clear if this is a requried feature of Scars of Luclin to run *at all*. Please clarify as the video card upgrade costs more than the software expansion.
I have a quote from Gordon for you on this one:


We're considering a T&L card a required feature for anyone who doesn't have a P3 or Athlon processor (or better). If you have a 16mb non-T&L card, you can get away with it as long as you have a P3 or Athlon.

That said, we're listing T&L as a requirement for framerate concerns more than anything else. Luclin features some pretty beefy poly-counts and the more processing you can offload to the card the better. However there's nothing that will kick you out of the game and say "You must have a T&L card to play EverQuest".

I hope this answers your question.

-Gordon
Thanks Gordon.

I am not quite clear on the new specs. Ok, i have 128, when i install SoL, when i be able to see, lets say, the new dwarf, or will i not even have access to see them without 251?

-folok solot
-cavalier of Brell
-Drinal
It's basically as Gordon described. You'll have to turn off models until your system runs at an acceptable framerate (and load time). What acceptable is will really be up to you. But with only 128 megs of RAM, I can tell you that you most likely will not find your performance to be at all acceptable with all the models turned on. Though, as always, all systems are different and personal preferences vary. So you can turn the models on regardless of your RAM, but without the minimum requirements we really don't think you'll find the performance acceptable.


Throkmorten-Veeshan wrote:

I was curious. Since you are not supporting glide anymore are the 3dfx voodoo cards going to be able to work with Luclin at all? I have found that my card runs better in primary mode rather than glide mode anyway. I'm assuming the primary mode is the DirectX mode anyway. Could you please give me further details on this?
If you get the latest drivers for you card, the card should be able to run Shadows of Luclin. However, with some of the older Voodoo cards you will probably find the performance less than satisfactory. Again, that's really up to the preferences of the user.

Beldukil wrote:

Does this mean that win 95 users must upgrade their entire operating system?
Pretty much, yes.

We won't officially be supporting Windows 95 anymore. Heck, Microsoft isn't even supporting it anymore

The main reason for this is because Windows 95 won't support our efforts to localize the game. Once we complete our localization work, people using Windows 95 won't have the code needed to translate the text. This will probably lead to all sorts of badness, not the least of which would be that Win 95 users might not even be able to see any chat at all.

This is another case where it might indeed work, but we can't promise it will.

While I am not to concerned about the amount or RAM (I have 384 meg), I am concerned that the recommended is 512 meg. Microsoft does not recommend that Windows 95/98/ME run with 512 meg of ram. While a few out there have had minimal problems running 512 meg, I had serious graphic issues when I did. I went back to 384 and eliminated the problem.

So to say 512 meg is recommended may be a little misleading unless you add Windows 2000/XP to the recommendation.

Laknor
