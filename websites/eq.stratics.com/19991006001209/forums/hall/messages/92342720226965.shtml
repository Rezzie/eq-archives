Even the Rough Hickory Recurve is better than a Longbow for our use. Does 10 damage compared to 8 for the longbow. Add in the arrows damage and it's usually in the 12-13 range. Move up to Elm and it jumps to 14-15 for damage with wooden arrows.
I'm fletching bone shafted arrows for skill raises now, and base damage is 2 on these. That bumps my damage up to 15-16 per arrow. I can do 17 at best with my Mino axe. The Axe is faster tho and I can kick in melee combat.
The real power comes once you can afford and make a compound bow. The Rough Oak 1 Cam does 20 damage plus the arrow. With Steel shafted, Bladed, Shield Fletch arrow that could be 27-28 with the lowest of the Compound bows.
I'm a ways from trying this tho. Fletching skill is only 68.

Whoever made the point about very slow skill raise for archery is dead on. I've seen mine go up from combat only 2 times. It's now 25 and I hope the increase picks up, cause I'll soon not be able to hit the things that can give me xp in a fight. One tactic to use if you have a magic-user in the party is to root a single monster and turn it into a pincushion while it is stuck. Be prepared to spend lots on arrows with this technique.
