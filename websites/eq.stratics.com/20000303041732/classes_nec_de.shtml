Dark Elf Necromancer

 Introduction   Starting Off   The Guild   Levels 1-3   Levels 4-7   Levels 8-11   Levels 12+   Conclusion 

Introduction
The Teir'Dal Necromancer is one of the most difficult race/class combinations in EverQuest. Dark Elves are typically hated, and so are Necromancers. You end up with a nasty social deficiency; however, as far as Necromancers go, I would say Dark Elves are the best option. The other races are despised in their own home towns for being Necromancers. Dark Elves are well accepted, and this makes life much easier.
 top


Starting Off
The first step, of course, is to roll your character. You will want to put the majority of your bonus points into intelligence. I put 21 in just so it would be a nice round number (130). The other 'important' stat for Necromancers is dexterity. Dexterity increases your chance of recovering from being hit and casting your spell without being interrupted. This is kind of confusing to me since Necromancers are less likely to be hit than most other spell casters because of our pets. I put 4 into this, and my remaining 5 points into stamina.
 top


The Guild
Good luck finding it. I'm not going to give it away here. All I'll tell you is its through the gate in 3rd gate...somewhere. Oh, I guess I can tell you that its red in color, too. Once you find the guild leader, give him the note and train. I put my points into piercing, as your magic is pathetically weak at this level. You probably want to put your spells into your spell book now. Open your book, select the first scroll, then place it in a blank slot in your book. Repeat with the other spell. Then memorize them by clicking them and placing them in one of the 8 slots on the left side of your screen. Now you're ready to adventure.
 top


Levels 1-3
After giving your note to your leader and getting your tunic, start heading up to Nektulos forest. Stratics has maps which will be very useful for finding your way up. Use your minor shielding spell. It helps significantly at this level. Use lifetap as much as possible as well, just to get your alteration up. At level 1, kill moss snakes. Don't group with anyone, it'll only slow you down. Snakes will give you a fairly large amount of experience, and you should make level 2 quickly. At level 2, feel free to try fire beetles. These are EASILY outrun if you get in trouble if you run before the point at which your low health slows you down. The nice thing about beetles is their eyes sell for 2.2 silver. Try to collect about 5 eyes and sell them, then go back to the guild and buy cavorting bones. This is your first summon spell. It summons an incredibly weak pet, but it works well for now. Go back to Nektulos (I know, it's a pain going through all those zones) and kill some decaying skeletons for bone chips. A regular skeleton will destroy you in a few swings, so don't try them. Once you have bone chips, cast your summon spell. Now continue killing beetles and such until you make 3rd. At 3rd, spiderlings are good, and keep killing decaying skeletons for the chips.
 top


Levels 4-7
This is a turning point. You finally get meditate and a MUCH better pet. Get Leering Corpse ASAP. This will give you the ability to kill some regular skeletons if you're careful. Group if you'd like. I never did. Only train 1 point into meditate, it goes up FAST on its own. Level 7 is tough. I hunted the skeleton rune (by the zone to east commons) through most of levels 6 and 7. The halfling camp on the Neriak side of the bridge is also a possibility, too, but watch out for the deputy. I would suggest having help anyway. Watch out for the zombies and lesser mummies. They're not an option right now.
 top


Levels 8-11
Level 8 is a MAJOR turning point. Two word: Bone walk. This pet is awesome compared to what you've had. He can take major abuse and dish it out nicely too. You also get Ward Undead. This will do about 40 damage for 30 mana, and it seems to go up 1 damage every level (it has so far for me, at least). It's about time to move out. Hunt the East Commons or Befallen. Personally, I love Befallen. There's a mob in there, Skeleton Lrodd, that gives INCREDIBLE experience. He ranges from level 11 to level 13 and hits for 20's damage. I would group with another person until about level 10th or so, which is when you can solo him fairly safely. I've even solo'd him when he conned red, but it's a close one. I have to trade blows with my pet to keep it alive. Also, be VERY careful not to frenzy this guy. You WILL die. And don't think that just because you aren't doing massive damage to him you won't frenzy him. His doing damage to you pisses him off as well. Low hitpoints makes for a mad monster, and he WILL stick to you once he gets you down to about 2 bubbles of life left. However there's another mob, Gynok Moltor, that is INCREDBILY strong. And he DOES come up to the top floor of the dungeon, so you're not safe there.
 top


Levels 12+
Here is where we REALLY start to shine. Noticing a pattern here? You get extremely better every spell level. A level 6, a necromancer stands a chance against a level 7 necromancer. A level 8 necromancer would annihilate a level 7 necromancer. A level 12, a necromancer would crush a level 11 necromancer. This applies for all of the spell casters. I can't describe in words the difference between level 11 and level 12. I would get spells in this order: Convoke shadow, heat blood, life draw, engulfing darkness, bind affinity. Your pet becomes MUCH better.
 top


Conclusion
Dark Elf Necromancers are definitely great for a shady player that likes the idea of delving into death. They have healing spells like no other, not to mention their pets, and poison spells. In my experience, they solo rather well, and you won't die much if you're careful. You always have that sacrificial lamb to guard you when you get in trouble. I hope this guide helps you get started as a dark elf Necromancer. I've personally done rather well playing solo. I rarely group. These first 12 levels are the toughest. Things get much easier at 12th, believe me.
 top
