Journey to Infected Paw

Journal Visions
 
Splitpaw Gnolls Close Up
 
Last View of the Pillars
 
Elephant: Big n' Gray
 
Pachyderm Posterior
 
About a week after his arrival in Qeynos, my freshly level 11 Wood Elf Ranger Seldom was becoming a little uninterested with the local dungeon, Black Burrow. Its not so much that I don't enjoy venturing into the depths to crush members of the gnoll society, more so that I wanted to get out, see the world, and maybe find some magic items. Sarvas the level 11 Half Elf Ranger was also getting edgy, and he wanted to travel as well. So with our hopes high, we decided to head to Infected Paw, the next closest dungeon, home of the Splitpaw Gnolls.

The first problem we encountered was that neither of us has any idea of how to get there, so we inquired in the Qeynos Hills using the /ooc chat. A helpful level 13 Human Cleric named Bishamon told us he had recently come from there, and gave us simple directions on how to get there. Sarvas and I didn't feel overly comfortable going alone, so we enlisted 2 other members for our party; a level 11 Half-Elf paladin named Instar, and a level 16 barbarian Warrior named Warriortanarra. We headed into the narrow mountain pass that would lead us to the Plains of Karana, with high hopes and a sense of adventure.

We had entered the Northern plains of Karana, and Infected Paw was locating in the Southern's. We began to head Southward, with Sarvas at the lead, checking his tracking for any impending dangers, as the Karanas are quite the nasty place. Following the coastline we easily made it to West Karana, where we took a brief respite to rest, regain stamina, reinitiate enchantments and such. After awhile of continuing along the coast, Sarvas warned us of a raider camp that lay in our path, so we briefly broke from the coast to avoid it. We continued on, and passed a large white marble bridge, truly phenomenal, until we came upon our goal (or so we thought), a large wooden suspension bridge. We all ran over it into the next zone, thinking we were near our goal.

***Welcome to East Karana***. The group began to head out, and I thought to myself that something was amiss. I remembered Bishamon telling us South Karana. So, being the only person who could remember his name, I used the game's /tell command to confirm that we had gone the wrong way. He assured me we had, and we crossed back into the Western Plains. Up until now, we had been reasonably lucky, encountering practically no danger for such a treacherous place, but no sooner than us re-entering the West plains, did we see a level 32 Halfling Druid running for her dear life from a hill giant... directly towards us. She ran over the zone border, as did we, but not before the lumbering beast could take 1/2 my life in 3 hits. We took a break to heal, then cautiously returned to the Western Plains once more. The giant had gone, but night had fallen now, and we were tracking werewolves. We held fast until they moved clear of us, and set out for the white bridge we had seen earlier. Unfortunately, there was a rather large griffin blocking our path, and we carefully followed it from a distance until it strayed and we had a clear shot.

We made it to the bridge, and entered the correct zone, South Karana. Foolhardy and excited as we were, we ran right over a hill in our path... into a skeleton encampment. Luckily, we were hardy enough to fight them back, and regrouped at the bridge. Once there we saw Bishamon, who had decided to come to Infected Paw again for himself. He joined our party and we were ready to enter the dungeon, all we had to do now was find it. We chanced upon a traveler who knew of its location, and headed in the direction, this time carefully avoiding the camp. Along the way we killed a few lions and lionesses, and some Splitpaw Gnolls, which are similar to the Gnolls of Black Burrow except they are gray and furry, not blue and hard-skinned. Sarvas and I detected an elephant on track, an opportunity we couldn't pass up. We approached the mighty beast, and dispatched it with relative ease, Warriortanarra taking the hits for the group. It was my turn to loot, so I reaped the bounty of its tusks, I felt like a poacher :). We pressed on, and in the distance saw the mark of Infected Paw, a set of inward curved stone pillars, dripping blood. As we approached we saw the entrance, a tunnel carved into a mound of dirt. Without a thought we entered.

Upon entering, I did a /who to see how many people were in the zone. It was our party and one adventurer, who was on her way out. An entire dungeon to ourselves, this seemed so strange at peak hours. Bishamon knew his way best here, so he took the lead ad we pressed in. We found a similar hierarchy of Gnolls here as in BB, but I must say these are much more interesting to look at. We unfortunately lost Instar, and I believe he headed back to Qeynos since I saw him there later that night. The monster detail in the game is truly amazing, there is something so characteristic about these Gnolls beady red eyes. We found Splitpaw Gnolls, Splitpaw Gnoll Shaman, Splitpaw Gnoll Guards, and Splitpaw Gnoll Nobles (more or less in order of difficulty). All of these creatures were lower level than me, but they traveled in groups, which would have made it impossible alone. We slowly carved our way through the caverns, and I must say its the most beautiful place I have seen thus far in EQ. My screenshots really don't do it justice at ALL. We eventually came upon some shaman and nobles together on a rock outcropping in a large chamber, and killed them without any trouble. ***Ssra BROADCASTS: World coming down, everyone log out now.***

This was one of the most dreadful things I had seen yet in EQ... it would be difficult to log in at the same time again, and I certainly couldn't get back home by myself, let alone out of the dungeon. So, with a heavy heart I logged out. Time passed. Time continued to pass. Time stopped passing. I logged back in, and one of my party members was online, Sarvas. Unfortunately, he had jumped the gun and was now lost, so I sat tight hoping he would stumble back onto me. It was then that Warriortanarra's hulking figure appeared before me, and seconds after Bishamon's leather clad body appeared as well. We grouped together and set out to find Sarvas. As we began to move towards the entrance, Sarvas told me he had found his way out, so we would rendezvous outside the dungeon. We surfaced from the bleak tunnels near the entrance, and night was just falling again. We found another elephant, and dispatched it like the first, with slightly more trouble because this was harder one. At this point though, Bishamon needed to go, so he cast a gate spell to return himself to his home point; Qeynos.

It was Sarvas, Warriortanarra and I now, and we decided we would head back. I stuck with Warriortanarra and we sort of lost track of Sarvas, then his name disappeared from the party, so I assumed he zoned. We headed to the bridge and entered West Karana for the final time. Tracking was clear, so I followed Warriortanarra back to North Karana, when I received a /tell from Sarvas that he had been disconnected and was alone in the Southern Plains. I gave him some directions on how to return, and luckily he was able to use them to get to the West Plains. Warriortanarra and I pressed on until we saw a fishing village, a sign that my hometown of Qeynos was near. As we entered the pass that would lead us back to the foothills, Warriortanarra and I bid our farewells, and I returned home.

I ran up the crest of a hill to watch the sunrise, I just sat there watching it until day fully broke, and I felt truly safe and content. No one really got much loot or experience from this journey, the creatures were too easy for Warriortanarra and we were too far apart from his level to get experience in a group with him, but we all still enjoyed it. I think that is possibly one of the most phenomenal aspects of the game, its not about being better than everyone or having chain armor or whatever, its about getting together, making friends, and having fun.
