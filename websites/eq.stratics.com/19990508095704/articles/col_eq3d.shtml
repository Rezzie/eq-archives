EQ3D, by Ffil

Choosing a 3D Card - 1.30.99
EverQuest. One of the new breed of CRPGs that takes what seems like a powerhouse computer to run. You may be afraid you'll have to spend hundreds of dollars on upgrades to have a chance to participate in a game like this, but luckily, that is a misconception. This column will discuss ways to get your computer up to speed to play EQ, and to get the most for your upgrade money. This first article will discuss choosing a 3D card.

Choosing a 3d card is not a very difficult thing to do. You just have to decide how much you can spend, and which games you want to play. There are 4 major contenders in the market right now, the Voodoo 2, Voodoo Banshee, Riva TNT, and ATI Rage 128. Each card is good for a certain group, which I will go over right now.

Voodoo 2 has been out for about 8 months now, and it is still a contender in the market. That says a lot about the card. You can pick these cards up for under $100, and if you have a 2d card you like, it's a great choice. It has 2 TMUs (Texture Memory Units), like all cards in this article except the Banshee. This makes it up to 30% faster in multi-textured games, and most games being released now are multi-textured. The card supports resolutions of 640x480 or 800x600, or you can hook two cards together in Scan Line Interleave mode (SLI) to increase the max resolution to 1024x768. This becomes costly and requires 2 free PCI slots, but the power is unmatched by any other graphics card (keep in mind that for the most part the two cards must be identical). It supports the three major APIs (application programming interface): Glide, OpenGL, and Direct3D. The support of Glide is exclusive to 3dfx products, and if you want to play a Glide only game (there are a few), then a Voodoo 2 or a Banshee is the clear choice. These cards come in 8mb and 12mb flavors, and for the few extra bucks, I have to say spring for the 12mb unless you really can't pull it.

To be blunt, the Voodoo Banshee is not my favorite card. I think for near every situation there is a better card, but for certain needs, this is the perfect card. Voodoo Banshee was released recently, and runs at about $100. It's very much like a Voodoo 2, except it includes a 2d graphics core, and it only has one TMU. The card can support the higher 1024x768 resolution, but the frame rate drops so low I doubt you'd ever use it. The single TMU is a pretty large problem, 30% is a huge drop in frame rate to take on some current games. In non-multi-textured games, the Banshee edges out the Voodoo 2 slightly in fps (frames per second). The strength of this card lies in its versatility. If you want support for Glide, and need a new 2d card, get a Banshee. If you need new 2D and don't want Glide, however, the card for you is probably the TNT.

The Riva TNT is, in my opinion, the best card around. It doesn't support Glide, but it does everything else so well it doesn't matter. For about $100, you get the best 2d around, combined with the best 3d short of a Voodoo 2 SLI setup. It supports resolutions from 640x480 to 1600x1200 (in games that support such high resolution), and Brad has announced that in p4 higher resolutions in D3D will be supported in EQ (previously a major fear). The other major strength of the TNT is the image quality. The image is much crisper than a Voodoo 2 or Banshee, and the colors are much richer. If you don't want Glide and you want the best 2d and 3d around for a great price, do yourself a favor and buy a TNT.

The ATI Rage 128 was released recently. I don't know too much about this card, but from what I understand, it isn't the best choice for the EQ gamer. They run reasonably expensive compared to the other cards ($150 - $175), and fall slightly behind a TNT in terms of fps. What I do know, however, is that this card is great for Super7 users. From what I understand, it's one of the few cards that has very few conflicts with that chipset, so you AMD owners should look into this card if you've been having troubles.

As for cards on the horizon, they aren't really worth waiting for. Voodoo 3 is a powerhouse card, but still has image quality problems, and won't be out for awhile, and the specs for TNT 2 aren't even released yet. If you want to play EQ, you're better off just getting one of these cards now.

Picking a card for EQ really isn't as hard as it seems. You just have to set your priorities and make a choice. If you don't need 2d and you want Glide support, get a Voodoo 2; if you do want 2d, get a Banshee. If you don't care for Glide, TNT is the way to go, and if you are a Super 7 user, the Rage 128 may be the card for you. I recommend www.pricewatch.com for getting the best prices on these cards, or if you live in Canada, you may end up with better prices at www.pc97.com or www.oxycom.com.

Feel free to swing by #everquest on irc.stratics.com, or give me a shout at ffil@stratics.com to ask questions or argue with me. :)

Addendum (02-02-99): Two things I would like to add to this first edition of EQ3D:

  1. EQ is a multi-textured game, making the Banshee take the performance hit
  2. The image quality of the TNT is very important, because EQ isn't about high frame-rates from a V2 SLI, but appreciating the beautiful locales and scenery
I'm sorry for omitting those two points, and I hope this clears things up.

- Ffil
