Sunday, May 26
The Future of EverQuest
GeneralNews [ 6:03 PM PDT ] - Lord_Of_Dreams
Those who attended the E3 conference and expo this year were treated with the vision of what the world of Norrath is to turn into. We were shown the first glimpses of EverQuest 2 in action, one of the first in-depth looks at EverQuest: Adventures, and then plans for the future of the classic EverQuest 1. All I can say about all three is that they are amazing in their own right. EverQuest is being taken to new heights and both EverQuest 2 and EverQuest: Adventures are making ground breaking entrances into the gaming world of MMORPGs.

Ill concentrate with the two games that Stratics covers in this article however, and treat you to what was learned about the games and my personal impressions of them. (EverQuest and EverQuest 2)

While I dont have much experience in the plane raid aspect of EverQuest, I do know that the Planes of Power expansion is doing some really awesome things to that aspect of EverQuest. The new planes look spectacular, and the epic battles involved with it look like they are going to be tremendous. There were also some very interesting facts we learned about how the expansion is going to be set up. Unlike many of the previous expansions, there is going to be a good deal of content added for existing lower level characters so that they may fully enjoy the experiences of the expansion. There are also plans to ship this new expansion with copies of Everquest Classic so that new players joining the game will be able to delve right into the new content without purchasing a separate box for it. I think this is a great idea, it allows people to hop right into the expansion action as soon as they receive the game. Im personally looking forward very much to being at the level where I can go and test out some of these new mega-mobs.

One extra tidbit that we learned about the latest EverQuest expansion is that high level advancement is going to be beefed up big time. We asked if they would be raising the level limit, and while they wouldnt give us a yes or no it sounded like that is the most obvious choice to me. But, that is only me guessing and nothing is definite yet at all from SOE regarding what they will actually put into the game regarding new high level advancement opportunities.

After the Planes of Power expansion demo we made our way over to the EverQuest 2 station all I can say is WOW. EQ2 took the cake in the graphics department for any MMOG at the show in my opinion. The level of detail that is etched on armor and walls while also being spread throughout entire towns is truly amazing. We saw a few monsters in action whose movements and graphics were incredible. There was also this one frog that they were showing off to viewers, who in all honestly looked more real than a real frog! Well, maybe it looked a lot like one of those tropical frogs but it was still beyond belief in what they are managing to pull off with this game.

Unfortunately they were not able to release much game info at this time, but from what they were able to show us the game is going to be great. (If they are able to keep along with the same level of work that weve seen so far from them)

The one thing that we were able to solidly gather from the demonstration was that EQ2 was going to be a whole new game from the original EverQuest. Features like decay of items, no XP loss upon death, and a whole new story line set it apart from its predecessor.

The story takes place during a new era of Norrathian history a good number of years after the original EQ. We were told that there will be enough time between the two to dramatically change the landscape of the world of Norrath, but still a short enough period of time so that we will see some familiar NPCs scattered throughout the world.

All in all I was very impressed by both of the games, and think they will add whole new dimensions to the world of EverQuest; both as we know it today and in the future. I cant wait to see what comes next in this line of award winning games from SOE.

Physical Enhancements
GeneralNews [ 3:51 PM PDT ] - Abombpk
One poster asked why certain races got Physical Enhancements, and others did not. Absor answers with:
""This ability adds an additional 2% to the Natural Durability, Combat Agility and Combat Stability archetypes.
There is no pre-requisite that these archetypes be trained in, however the greatest effectiveness is achieved from doing so. It is believed that you need at least 1 point in an archetype to get the 2% increase in it (i.e. you would get no benefit in Natural Durability if you only had points in Combat Agility and Stability).""

Most every melee type has this skill under Archtype, Paladin, Ranger, Shadowknight, even Beastlord. But seems Warrior, Rogue and Monk are missing it, or were left out specifically. What was the reasoning behind this?

Renaan Hellfire
Tarew Marr

This ability came out of beta testing for Luclin.

Originally there were just the basic archetype abilities.

But there was some discussion among the testers about the usefulness of those abilities for hybrids. The logic went something like this.

Lets assume that a theoretical hybrid was 75% as effective as a warrior in each of those melee aspects (the warrior being the base at 100%).

If we boost the warriors ability by 5% (of the 100) he gains 5%.
If we boost the hypothetical hybrid by 5% (of the 75) he gains (gets out a calculator) 3.75%.

So the warrior with one level of the skill is 105% and the hybrid is 78.75%.

So rather than the hybrid being 25% less than the warrior, hes 26.25% less

As you add the levels of these bonuses up, you end up increasing the gap between the hypothetical hybrids melee skills and those of the warrior.

Now there was indeed much discussion about this, because if you look at it the other way, the hybrid is still 75% of the warrior (105 x .75 = 78.75). But in the end we decided to allow the hybrids to purchase a little more effectiveness.

Thats why its only available to the hybrids.

And, no, I dont really want to debate the numbers. My head already hurts.

Alan
Thanks, Alan!

Message To Legends Subscribers
Stormhammer [ 11:24 AM PDT ] - Abombpk
Here it is, From Verant to all you Stormhammer players out there:
New Target Reservation System Announced

Dear Legends Subscribers,

Through the course of Stormhammer's history, we have come to better understand the situation of guild competition for encounters. While such competition is an undeniable part of the "end game" of EverQuest, it can lead to considerable ill will between competing members, which in turn can lead to a negativity of the game in general. The large numbers of high level characters on Stormhammer exasperates this situation, leading to many spawn disputes that overall detract from the level of service we can offer to all of our customers. To address this and help build a positive community, we are offering a Calendar Reservation System for select encounters.

While we understand the feelings against a reservation system, we think what we have devised allows room for both friendly competition as well as the much desired ability to conduct raids at a leisurely pace without competitors at your heels. Obviously, this system will require your support, involvement and feedback to make it a success.

We have set up a new e-mail address for feedback and correspondence for the Reservation System, LegendsRS@soe.sony.com. I encourage you to visit the Developer Corner area where the Instructions, FAQ and Targets are listed and write us with your thoughts. Once we have the monsters patched into the Arena to be able to start the Trial of Heroes we will set up as many guild trials as possible to get everyone interested assigned into Tiers. This should happen in the early part of this coming week. We will let you know when we are ready to schedule Trials.

We thank everyone for their continued patronage of Legends that allows us to offer such a system.

Respectfully,


Alex von Minden

Lead Game Master Bordael
Legends Service





05/18/2002 8:00 pm Lead GM Bordael - Calendar Reservation System FAQ

EverQuest Legends Target Reservation System

FAQ

What is the EverQuest Legends Target Reservation System?

It's a service provided with your Legends subscription to allow fair play and planning for higher end content of EverQuest.


What does a guild need to do to be part of the reservation system?

The ability to be part of the reservation system will involve passing the appropriate Trial of Heroes for the Tier your guild wishes to be part of and sending in the required reservations requests and weekly reports.


What days can you reserve a target on the system?

The reservation system will begin on Wednesday when the server comes live (or 12 Noon Pacific, whichever is applicable) and will end on Saturday evening at 11 PM Pacific.


What happens on days not recognized by the reservation system?

From Saturday after 11 PM Pacific until Wednesday at 12 Noon Pacific, all listed targets will be viewed as "free for all". Any spawn disputes during these times will be handled in accordance with the Play Nice Policies as normal.


What will happen if a guild ignores the reservation and kills something my guild has reserved?

The Legends staff will strictly enforce violations of the reservation system. In the unfortunate event someone chooses to ignore the system it will be treated like any other breech of Terms of Service..


What happens if we reserve a target and it does not spawn during the allotted time?

A reservation of an encounter does not guarantee it will be up for engagement. However the target is yours until your guild has slain said target during the reservation days or the reservation time is over. Scheduled maintenance and emergency downtime will advance the rotation should the target come up, be slain and come back up again in the days of enforcement.


Once my guild has been accepted to the reservation system how do we make a reservation for the four-day block?

All reservations will be made via email to LegendsRS@soe.sony.com beginning Saturday at 2 PM Pacific Time and closing Monday at 10 PM Pacific Time. Only the guild leader or a designated officer may submit the guild's requests. Each guild will be restricted to having one person in charge of sending this email.


How many encounters can be reserved?

A guild can request reservation of any of the Targets from the Tier they are registered for and down. A guild that is registered for Tier 8 may request reservation for any of the targets in the system.


What is to keep a guild from recruiting and making it to a high tier standing and then falling apart but still taking spawns in the rotation?

Any guild that fails to slay any of their reserved targets in two consecutive weeks, for whatever reason, will be moved down a Tier.


What is to keep a guild from "temp" guilding members in order to pass a Trial in order to be able to reserve additional targets?

EverQuest servers log guild movement activities. Any significant guild movement can lead to an investigation into whether a guild is engaging in such activity. Based on the results of our investigation and our sole discretion to uphold the intent and spirit of the Target Reservation System, such activity could lead to disciplinary action on the involved accounts.


What will happen if two guilds request the same target during a week?

Rotation of targets over as many weeks as there are applicants will be set in the Bazaar via /random by a GM. Once the first guild has been set randomly, it will proceed through the other guilds that requested the target in alphabetical order until all guilds have had one reservation for the target. Then, the target will come open again for reservation.


What about encounters not listed on the target list?

Any target not listed is deemed "free for all" and can be engaged by any group during the calendar week, as things were prior to this system. Please be advised that the list of targets for the Reservation System is subject to change.


What if a guild asks to reserve a target they cannot kill?

Target reservation will be done through a tier system. Guilds can only reserve targets that are in their tier or below. Guilds must pass a Trial of Heroes to be admitted into a tier.


Can you still do joint raids?

Guilds can join to take down larger opponents. However, parties wishing to run a joint raid on a target above their own tier level must pass a trial for the tier with the guild or guilds who will be assisting them. Trials may be restricted in the number of people who may participate.

For example: Guild A and Guild B cannot take down Tunare by themselves. Guild A and Guild B schedule a trial and together pass the trial for the tier Tunare is on. Now Guild A and B can ask for reservation of Tunare but the two guilds would share one slot on that tier's rotation.


What about the Planes, Lord Nagafen or Lady Vox? Why are they not on the rotation list?

The planes of Hate, Fear, Air and Growth are not on enforced rotation. We will list them as well as the old world dragons for informational purposes only. Any group who wishes to list their intent to adventure in these areas or go after Lord Nagafen or Lady Vox may make their intention known on the weekly Reservation System Post. However, this will not insure that the group making the listing will have exclusive rights to these areas or targets. The listing is to help facilitate others in planning their activities and foster communication between groups who wish to do the same thing at the same time.

Viruses Run Amuck!
Site_News [ 2:08 PM PDT ] - Abombpk
Hello Folks,

Recently we have been accused of being the senders of virus infected emails to visitors of our site. Viruses seem to have been sent from various addresses in the stratics.com domain. Let me assure you that we are not sending out virus infected emails at all.

Closer inspection shows that although our address shows in the 'From' line of the email, the email was not sent from here. In all cases the evidence pointed out that an internet worm 'Klez' was responsible for this. Here is how it works: Suppose that John Doe is an online gamer who once emailed us a comment, and has some of our web pages copied to his local disk. When a Klez worm somehow has infected John's machine, it will scan the local disk for email addresses and send itself to a random found email address and stick another random found email address in the 'From' line of the email. John's friend could be receiving such an infected email from John's PC with as sender forumhelp@stratics.com or any other Stratics email address that was found on John's PC.

In fact, we are receiving more and more of those emails ourselves, which tells us that the Klez worm is still spreading. Needless to say, we'd like this to stop. Not only is this causing an annoyance for us and for the people that think we are sending them a virus, there is also a potential danger of files being erased or damaged on infected machines. On the 6th of every odd numbered month (except January or July), the worm attempts to overwrite with zeroes files that have the extensions .txt, .htm, .html, .wab, .doc, .xls, .jpg, .cpp, .c, .pas, .mpg, .mpeg, .bak, or .mp3. If the month is January or July, this payload attempts to overwrite all files with zeroes, not just those with the aforementioned extensions. Also, Klez tries to disable several virus scanners, leaving your PC wide open for other virus attacks. You can read more about what Klez can do here.

So, whatever you are going to do today, please check your PC for the presence of this Klez worm. There is a free tool available that will detect and remove the Klez worm at the Symantec web site here. Even if you think you are not infected, do yourself and others a favor by making sure your system has not been infected with Klez.

Firiona Vie and Sullon Zek Makeovers?
GeneralNews [ 1:24 PM PDT ] - Abombpk
Absor posted the changes made to Firiona Vie and Sullon Zek on the EQ Developers Corner today. Here's what he lists as the changes made:
Firiona Vie was designed in an effort to create a roleplaying ruleset on an EverQuest server. It was a valiant experiment, but it seems to have fallen short of expectations. Unfortunately the restrictions to both grouping and communication on this server have prevented the population from reaching the mass required to be a self-sustaining environment. With the tremendous size of the EverQuest game world, too small of a population on server is just as devastating to the health of that server as too large of a population.
So we've decided to change some of the rules on Firiona Vie in an attempt to bring more players to the server. The focus for the server is still roleplaying, that won't change. But we're going to remove some of the rules that make playing on the server inconvenient and restrict roleplaying. We will, however, be keeping the following rules in tact:

* /ooc will remain unavailable to remind people that acting in character is strongly recommended.

* The Common language will remain out, to help maintain the feeling of race-based community and to allow people to continue to roleplay someone with a native tongue.

* The Trivial Loot Code and all of the special rules for no-drop items will remain. This has worked out nicely for the sense of community on the server.


However, we will be removing the following restrictions:

* Anonymous/roleplay flags will no longer be required. They will remain available, but forcing anonymity on characters is causing undue problems finding groups and other such things that are essential to enjoyable gameplay.

* The language learning limit/speed restrictions will be removed. While the idea of limited languages seemed like a good idea, this is another item that was just making it too hard to actually play the game. Roleplayers that dont want their characters to be overly literate always have the option to play their characters that way.

* /shout and /auction will return to unrestricted use to allow easier grouping and selling. People are welcome to filter those channels if they prefer not to see them, but the benefits of having them enabled is just too great. /ooc will remain disabled on this server. Obviously, acting in character on any channel is strongly recommended.

* Alignment restrictions will be removed. Forced restrictions for grouping make playing the game too much of a chore. Characters, however, may always decide who they wish to group with based on any factors the character perceives. If your Dark Elf won't group with a paladin, he doesn't have to (and shouldn't). If your cleric of Quellious wants to heal a necromancer because healing people is the right thing to do for him, then you should be able to do so. But not allowing these things is a restriction on your ability to play that character 'in-character', and a real hindrance to general gameplay.

* We will be raising the number of characters available to each account to three. We've added a new race, class and a few race/class combinations since Firiona Vie was launched and it only seems fair to give folks on that server a chance to play them.


Again, it's important to understand that this is still a roleplaying preferred server. The rules that we are changing were often as much of a restriction to one's ability to roleplay as they were a help. And the artificial barriers to grouping, especially the trouble created in just finding a group, need to be removed to allow people to actually play the game.

We will also be increasing the number of characters that can be created on Sullon Zek to three per account to allow players on that server to create new characters of the new class and races made available since the server was introduced.

All of these changes will occur with the next patch on Wednesday, May 8th, 2002.

Alan
