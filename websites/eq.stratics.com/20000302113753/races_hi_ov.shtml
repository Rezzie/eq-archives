Overview of the High Elf

 To Be or Not to Be   Those Pesky Guilds!   Creature Combat   Role-playing with the High Elf 

To Be or Not to Be a High Elf?
The answer to that question is easy, to be. You start in the most fascinating town, covered with vines, Felwithe. Surrounded by lakes and blossoms of flowers, the main entrance is made of huge marble blocks and gold plating. Of all the races I have played, no other race is as fun if you have a big ego. They are charming, rude, handsome, and beautiful. EverQuest used a High Elf female as their mascot for the splash. Your fighting area is known as Greater Faydark. Swarming with smelly orcs and huge beasts with wings the size of a drake tail! With their pointy ears and "I am better than you!" attitude, these pale faced white-haired lanky intelligent specimens make the best mage user. If you have a shining to a magic class and don't know what race, High Elves are your thing.

Those Pesky Guilds!
Do you hate those huge towns with impossible guild halls to find? Do you hate wondering an hour and a half to find a single person when you could have been level 3 by now! Felwithe is your answer. There are five guild halls. Three of them are located right next to each other. The Paladin and Cleric guild halls are located in the main town, and the Magician, Wizard, and Enchanter guild halls are located in Southern Felwithe. This small vine-covered town is the easiest place to navigate in ALL Norrath, even easier than Oggock or Grobb made by those peanut sized Trolls and Ogres.
 top


Bats, Hornets, and Spiders oh MY!
When you finished your whole guild thing, you shall fight in Greater Faydark. Filled with humongeous hornets, beastly bats, and scary spiders, Greater Faydark can be gruesome to a low level fighter. This immense forest is also the home of the tree huggin' hippies and Wood Elves. Don't worry, these weak nobodies are hardly a threat to you. Once you reach those high levels (5 or 6 maybe 7), you can go to the Orc camp, east of the city gates. Once there, one simple motto is your answer: "If ye bad guy is red or yella, stay off that fella, if ye bad guy is blue or black, jump him and attack!" I made that up just now, so I don't care what you say about it. It simply means "/con" it. Once at the orc camp, you might want to group because those centurions and shamans are deadly to anyone!

Roleplaying the High Elf
I know what your saying,"It's easy to role-play a high elf, just act all high and mighty!". Yes, this is true, but there are other aspects to being a High Elf. Here are some tips:

  • Always complain about your tan, and how your pale complexion is being messed by the god-awful sun.
  • If another race says something (unless Erudite) say , "Who gave you permission to talk?"
  • Again, complain about your hair.
  • Talk with high vocab such as, "Damn you vile beast, ever since the day I spotted you, you have time and time again thwarted my schematic attempts to off you." or, "Have at thee fowl feline."
  • Be polite.
  • Make fun of the tree huggers. (wood elves) ALWAYS!
With the growing threat of Orcs in Greater Faydark, you should slay them once spotted. Raise your faction all the way to its highest spot (faction--how much you are liked by the public, raised by killing monsters hated by all races and such). With daily quests adventuring to the orc camps and kidnapping or killing an orcs, Felwithe has its fair share of quests. I cannot reveal this or where they are located, but trust me they are there.
