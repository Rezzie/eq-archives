Clarification on Pets!
GeneralNews [ 9:46 AM EST ] - Sandara
Absor took a moment to post a clarification to the recent changes to pets over on the Official Everquest Forums.

Let me summarize, for convenience sake, the changes that we have made to pets over the course of the last few patches.

Pets wielding weapons will now proc with those weapons just as frequently as a player character with that weapon and the same stats. This has reduced the frequency of weapon procs from pets. To make this change we simply removed an artificial multiplier to pet proc chances, no stats or attributes were changed.

Pets wielding the Sword of Runes will proc with that sword as often as they do with any other procing weapon, but it will only proc against its intended target, summoned creatures.

Magician and necromancer pets (and because they use the same spells, shadowknight pets as well) now have the ability to duel wield without a weapon, if they are powerful enough. This will happen with some regularity with pets cast from 39th level (52nd for shadowknights) spells and above.

No other changes to the duel wielding ability of any other pets was changed. This ability was not given to any other pet because magicians and necromancers are primary pet classes, the others are not.

-Alan

Patch Message for November 29, 2000!
GeneralNews [ 8:15 AM EST ] - Sandara
Here is the patch message for November 29, 2000!

November 29th, 3:00am
------------------------------

*Patch Day*

Today's patch includes updates to the client to address some problems reported by people in regards to the DX7 update, as well as other gameplay issues:

- Corrected an older bug that would cause people to stop gaining or losing faction with associates when the primary faction was at its maximum or minimum. Players will now be able to further modify associate factions in the appropriate direction.

- Random-effects on weapons carried by pets will now behave as if the pet is a player character rather than an NPC. The change last patch reduced them to a rate below player characters. As part of this patch, target-specific random effects will only work if the target is the intended type. For instance, a weapon that processes "Dismiss Summoned" will only "go off" on a summoned NPC instead of everything.

- Added some code that should address if not eliminate a problem reported by people whereas the client would "freeze" for a second or two every few minutes. For those who do not have IRQ sharing and/or driver problems, this should address the issue.

- Made final positive modifications to several Epic Quest rewards. Included in these changes: the SK weapon will now allow you to bash while carrying it.

- Fixed a problem that gave people problems when exiting the game and attempting to get back in.

- Added some code to address reports from people who find exiting from the character-select screen back to the server-select screen rather slow when mip-mapping is enabled. Most of this problem is due to routines in DX required to release resources consumed by mip-mapping and may not be addressable until the next version of DirectX is adopted.

- Changed customizable key mappings so that the ESC key will clear the "NOKEY" setting instead of the "Backspace". This means that you can now assign "Backspace" to a function in game.

- Corrected a sound problem that would cause certain sounds to be played "rapid-fire" under some circumstances. There is still a potential for this to happen with some other sounds that will be addressed in a patch in the near future.

- Corrected a problem in Kedge Keep that made the top encounter more difficult than intended after the server had been up for a while.

- Higher level Magician, Necromancer, and Shadowknight pets now have the ability to dual-wield without being handed weapons. The calculation used to determine whether or not this ability exists takes several things into account, but for the most part 39th level and above summons (higher for the Shadowknight) will have this ability. It is possible that you could get a "good" 34th level pet that will, or a "bad" 39th level pet that will not have this ability.

- Bards of the land, hark unto me: The notes have been scribed on tattered parchment, two score and nine, for you they are meant. Abandon your mates, though they may fume, and follow the call of the seafarer's tune.

- The Monk's hand-to-hand skill cap has been raised to a level equal to their one-handed blunt skill.

Remember! Only a few days left until the launch of EverQuest: The Scars of Velious. Reserve your copy at your local retailer today!


- The EverQuest Team

Nerf or Enchance? Which is Better.
GeneralNews [ 8:05 AM EST ] - Sandara
Absor addressed the issue of balance yet again in this rather lengthy thread over on the Official Everquest Fourms

I found a very lengthy post from Gordon addressing issues about the change of content. Gordons response is the standard fare from the VI line, I think he makes good points, but I think there is another principal that he does not discuss: the tradition of the game patch. Now I dont know if this is because many EQ players who do not like the constant changes are new gamers, or came from the FPS and RTS genre, but historically, strategy games often update AI's, "nerf" powers, and make other changes in a game in order to provide a challange for players. And those are not even persistant, changing games, they are meant to be static.

An interesting thread. Perhaps a bit over my head, intellectually

I think it's wrong to imply that you can only do one or the other, either increase the power of the monsters OR decrease the power of player characters, if your goal is to make the game harder (and that's IF...).

The real world answer is that you do both as the situation requires. In the case of one bit of the picture being off, say an item or a spell that makes one class or character type or tactic too powerful, relatively, you obviously reduce the power of that item. If you want to make the world as a whole more difficult for the heroes in it, then you can do either, really. Obviously the preferable method is to increase the AI for the monsters, or find other clever ways to make them more powerful.

That being said, don't go overboard. If we do something that reduces the power of an item or spell or somesuch,it's done in consideration of the whole. I like to picture class balance as a sort of sea (ok, I've never pictured it that way until just now...). Waves are expected, even desirable. But when you see a tsunami, what do you do? Do you raise the ocean or lower the wave? Keep in mind that if you raise the ocean you'll drown the world...

I love bad analogies.

Has the Direction of EQ Since Release Made Sense?

Instructions to help you Install Drivers
GeneralNews [ 3:03 PM EST ] - Ogamok
I found this here on the Official Everquest Forums

Installation of drivers:
First find out which video card and sound card you have.

Here are two ways to determine what sound and video cards, and drivers you have.

Click on start, and go up to run. In the Run window, type DXDIAG than hit ok. This will launch the DirectX diagnostics tool. Click on the tab that says Display to see what video card you are using as well as the latest driver you are using. . Click on the tab that says Sound to see what sound card you are using as well as the latest driver for the sound card.
(For people with two display tabs, one is a 2d card and one is a 3d card. You will most likely want to update both.)

Another way to check for your cards is to right click on My Computer at your desktop. Scroll down to properties, click on properties and go up to device manager. In the device manager, if you click on Sound, video, and game controllers, you can determine what audio device you are using, and If you click on Display adapter you can see what video device you are using. If you click on either device and then click on the driver tab you can determine which version you are using.
(An accelerator card will sometimes be located in the Sound, Video, and Game Controllers section.)

After you find out what video card or sound card you have, please visit our main Web site (http://www.everquest.com), visit the support section and examine the sound and video FAQ’s. This will usually have locations for the latest drivers for your cards from the manufacturers’ web sites. Please download the latest driver for your card unless it has been recommended to download an older set of drivers. Sometimes, an older set of drivers is more compatible with your system and you will have to shuffle around until you find out the correct driver.

When you download the driver it will ask you to either save to a disk or open file from its current location. You should check "save this file to a disk", and download to your Documents section. Open up your Documents section, and find the drivers you downloaded. Open up the download, which should be in zipped form.

Normally, you would download the driver or drivers to your desktop in zip form. Your computer should have an unzip program already installed. If you do not have an unzipping program please download the trial version from www.winzip.com . You should unzip the download into your Windows temp folder. Make sure you know the exact location of the folder. It should be extracted into the windows directory as: C:\WINDOWS\TEMP\DRIVER NAME AND VERSION NUMBER.

Please read the Readme.txt that is in the download that you have done. Follow the directions carefully, as they are almost always the best directions to correctly install the drivers for your card. It is highly advisable that your print these instructions out, if at all possible, before the installation.

Sometimes old drivers still present in your system will cause problems. Usually these drivers will not display themselves in regular Windows so you have to go to Safe Mode to remove them.

If you have Windows 95, reboot your computer. You can enter safe mode by hitting F8 when the message “Starting Windows95” appears.

With Windows 98 Reboot and hold down the left control (ctrl) key after you reboot your computer and you should, after a short period of time, see a menu which has an option for Safe Mode. Please choose the Safe Mode option.

Once you get into Safe Mode right click on My Computer, left click on Properties, go to Device Manager, and left click the plus button next to Display Adapters. If there are any listings for cards you no longer have or multiple entries for your current video card then remove them.


Good Luck

XP Bug?
GeneralNews [ 9:18 AM EST ] - NecroGod
I found this here on the Official EverQuest Forums:

I hit 55 last night. I killed a few mobs after that so i had just a spec of exp on 55. Well i went on a guild raid and died shortly after. I was very scared to look at my exp, but i did anyway. When i looked i saw that i not only lost that spec i had on 55 but 3/4 of a bubble on 54 TOO!

That may sound trivial or normal, but what's funny is i got a rez and that brought me back to just needing a sliver to lvl again. I died again very shortly afterwards without gaining any exp and i thought for sure i would be a bubble down on exp, but Nope i had was only down to the 1/2 bub from lvling.

It's not a bug. Experience loss on death is a numeric value calculated as a percentage of the numeric value required by a human warrior to level in the previous level. In other words, if you die in level 55, the experience you lose is based on the experiece a human warrior would have required to level from 54 to 55. It's not calculated as a percentage of experience required for the current level, so the number of "bubbles" or fraction thereof may fluctuate.

There is a cap of the maximum amount of experience (as a numeric value) that you can lose from a death, which classes hit in the mid-50s.

I used to play AC, got bored of it. I came to EQ because I wanted more in depth play and quests. Everyone said EQ had great Graphics hands down. Why is all the Armor SKIN Tight??? I mean Plate Armor shoudn't look like Leotards when you are wearing it...Sorry Folks....

EQ uses a mesh based character model system. In other words, with the exception of helms, the character geometry does not change to show new armor, instead new textures are mapped over existing geometry. This has the nice effect of ensuring that character geometry is contigious, meaning that your arm, for instance, is always attached to your body.
Helms are currently achieved by changing the geometry of the head, but results in all helm geometry being the same. We are currently experimenting with equipment-based geometry that attaches to the current character geometry, which will let us do some more exciting things in the future. The new helms to be included in Velious will use this technology.

    Does Dex Really Affect Weapon Procs?
    GeneralNews [ 9:35 AM EST ] - NecroGod
    I found this here on the Official EverQuest Forums:

    >>>Why is it that dex does not affect how a weapon procs???

    It does. The example in your post is not a large enough model to see the difference. You could certainly proc 5 times in a row even with minimum dex, but the chance is far less.

    Remember that previous successes or failures have nothing to do with the chance of future successes or failures. The fact that you just proc'd does not make the chance of your next proc any closer or further away, probabiltywise.

    Regarding the random number generator, people think that because streaks happen that there's some problems. Random does not mean equal and regular distribution over the course of the sample. In any 10 flips of a coin you still have a .1% chance of never having it come up "heads". The fact that you may sit down and flip a coin 10 times and not get "heads" doesn't mean that your coin flip isn't random.
