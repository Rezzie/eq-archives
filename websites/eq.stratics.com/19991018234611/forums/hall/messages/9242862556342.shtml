I think this would be a good idea IF the higher-level monsters weren't so aggressive. A good example is Everfrost (I can think of countless bad examples). Mammoths provide plenty of opportunity for higher-level characters (they are everywhere), but newbies and mid-level characters don't have to worry QUITE as much about being instantly killed because a monster appeared out of nowhere (due to lag or spawning).

Of course, there are still plenty of dangerous, aggressive foes for low-to-mid level characters, but most of these aren't of the "instant death" variety - you at least have a chance of making a run for the nearest guards, if you have a good idea of your direction.

One of the lousiest implementations of higher-level creatures is in Erudin, with Rugnupp the troll shaman. He /cons as green, isn't worth experience points at mid-level, even though you don't stand a chance in hell of winning a fight with him. The higher level characters ignore him, which means he goes around slaughtering the lower-level characters. The guards ignore him, which means he'll keep slaughtering characters as they respawn. This doesn't make for a fun experience.
