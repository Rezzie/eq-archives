Heres my opinions on this, no flames...

As far as roleplaying good with evil goes, if your on a non-pvp server I dont think you should be able to penalize another character in any way, buffing/healing mobs, killing them, etc, without consent of the other player. Most of are on a non-pvp server because we dont want people to have the ability to abuse us in a way that ruins our good time.

So what do you do ? Well, you can roleplay it out with emotes and speech. You can challenge to a duel. Thats about it. If you really are a roleplayer, you should be able to do just fine. If the other person doesnt want to roleplay with you, that should be their option, and you should leave them alone. Ignore them. Roleplaying is supposed to enhance everyones gameplay, not just one persons at the expense of anothers enjoyment.

If you want to be able to penalize people by killing them, buffing monsters, looting their gear, etc, you should go to the pvp server where everyone has already consented to this behavior by the very act of being on that server.

The reason you see limits to looting even on the pvp server is because of abuse. The sorry truth is that too many people are willing to abuse the system. It was proven in UO. Open looting would get so abused it would be impossible to roleplay any kind of character on the pvp server, except a naked mage :) Your honorable swordsman, and your wifes druid goddess, would get jumped by a pack of non-roleplaying naked mages from the guild "masters of disaster", killed, and looted clean so many times that you would probably quit in disgust. At least I would.

It does suck that it has to be this way. I wish we could kill people who offend us or deserve it. I wish I could pry the sword used to attack my from the dead hand of the person assaulted me. But, I know it will be abused, so im willing to live without it.

Ninecattiess
