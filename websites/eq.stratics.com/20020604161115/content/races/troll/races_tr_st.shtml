Starting as a Troll

 Introduction   Starting Out   Middle Levels 

Thugthug's Guide ta Barshin'
by Thugthug, Troll Warrior (translated, transcribed, edited and commented by Mardigan, High Elf Wizard)
Oy! So yas wanna be a barsher like Thugthug? Dat gud. Us trolls make gud barshers. Weez got regenerema... um, weez heal fast. Weez can see in da dark. Dat gud 'cause da swamp is dark and youze gotta see dem froggies afore ya can barsh em gud. We also very tuff an strong, doh not so much as our barshin' bruvahs, da ogres. But, when we take a break from da barshin', Thugthug is ready ta barsh much sooner dan his ogre barshin' bruvahs. Also, we got da ability ta slam, jus' like marm usta do when I wuz a little 'un.

Ed note: Trolls are foul looking, boisterous and somewhat dimwitted. In short, they make perfect warriors. They have the innate ability to regenerate damage (4 HPs roughly every 7 seconds), in addition to infravision (which can be a tremendous aid in the dark swamp, rest assured.) Trolls are second only to Ogres in starting stamina and strength. What makes the troll and ideal choice as opposed to the ogre is in the lengthy period needed to recover health between battles. Trolls also have the racial ability to "Slam", a nearly-unimproving skill which will do minor damage to and stun an unlucky victim (once the warrior class gains the bash skill at level 9, the slam skill seems to begin improving along with bash).

Starting out with a Troll Warrior - It isn't Easy beein' Green
Roit! All da new trolls start in Grobb. Grobb pretty. Grobb good fer barshin'. Grobb got lotsa barshin' stuffs fer trolls, doh it all very 'spensive. Youze gotta barsh lots to buy da good stuffs. Da big troll barshas are right close to da entrance, all da new troll warriors should go see 'em fast. Dey teach Thugthug more 'bout usin' da sword. Da spellin' barshers is far inta da city, roit near da bank, an da Shadowy Knights home is thru a wood tunnel near da entrance to Grobb. Dem teach all gud stuffs to trolls.

Ed note: Grobb is obviously a troll city. Unlike my beloved Felwithe, it's dirty, simple and lacking in any grace. That does, however make it easy to get around, if one can avoid the discarded body parts of those foolish enough to intrude uninvited into the city. One can find all manner of large armor and most weapon types for sale in and around the same building (Trolls would rather bash than build, so the city layout is rather utilitarian.) Since it tends to be useful to actually strike an opponent in battle, and since trolls are so slow that they take a long time to learn from experience, new trolls might be advised to spend their initial practices on improving their preferred weapon skills. Once one acheives a modest familiarity with their weapon, improves do come steadily on their own through judicious "barshin'."

So, once yas leave da city, da whole world full o fun stuffs ta barsh is roit outside. Da swarmp is a gud place fer barshin', but not as a new barsher. Dem froggies have lotsa friends and youze can find yerself surrounded by dem in less time den it takes ta pick a halfing outta yer teef. If yas go to da Feerrott first, youze'll find lotsa bats an little itty bitty jungle spidahs, and frogloc tads, an even some lizardman bros. Dem pretty easy ta kill and afore too long youze'll be ready ta go back to Grobb and get some more learnin'

Ed note: The Inothule Swamp is dark, confusing, dank and depressing. The kindly froglocs who call it home are in a constant state of war with the Trolls and Ogres. The Froglocs range in toughness from tads (quite easy) thru froglocs, to frogloc fishers, to frogloc foragers (the toughest). They have a tendency to be able to call on each other when in need, so it is quite easy to find yourself outnumbered and surrounded in an unfamiliar part of the swamp, with no knowledge of which way the guards or the zone might lay. Developing ones innate ability to Sense Direction is vital to navigating and surviving the Swamp. One should also be aware that froglocs are not the only hostile denizen of the swamp. Giant fungus-men and kobolds often wander about in close proximity to the froglocs. The amount of damage a single kobold hunter running amok in a group of youthful Troll warriors can inflict is impressive to say the least. Most new trolls find it more expedient to confine their early hunting activites to The Feerrott, which is just a short trek from the Grobb entrance. There are many good low level monsters for the promising Troll Warrior (or Shadow Knight or Shaman, for that matter) to practice "barshin'." Thugthug himself stayed in this are until he was 3rd level, at which time he moved to the swamp.

Me likes barshin' in da swamp. Dem froglocs give gud loot. Dey tricky, and dey run into da water and swim away when dey been barshed gud, so youze gotta learn ta swim gud too. Once Thugthug get to 3rd level, me go try ta barsh some froglocs. Me werk on da direction sense so dat I know where ta run ifen I get in trouble. Dem guards make gud barshers and dey save me life many times. But me stay in da swamps until 7th level. By den Thug be makin' gud money and fightin' lots of froglocs at da same time. 'Course me buy leather arma and a real sword fer gud barshin'. Also, me save all practices fer useful skills dat I learn when I get tuffer like Dodge, Parry, Dual Wield and Double Attack.

Ed note: Once Thug hit 3rd level, he had sufficient skills and HPs to be able to stand toe to toe with a frogloc or 2 before having to flee. Moving to the swamp proved to be a boon as froglocs provide nice loot and experience while not requiring a very lengthy travel from safety to find them. With the money garnered, Thug was able to buy some striking large leather armor, which, when combined with the various magical pieces of "netted" armor that he was able to retrieve from various froglocs, made him even tougher on the frogloc population. His first large purchase however, was to trade in the starting sword that he had in favor of a shiny new Bastard Sword. Thugthug explains his view on 1-handed vs 2-handed below.

Thugthug like 1 handed swerds. Thugthug friends like 2 handed swerds an sticks. Thugthug can hit fer 17 points o damage fairly fast wit his 1 handed swerd, but his friends hit fer 30 wit dere 2 handed and dey are only a little bit slower. But Thugthug is lookin' ahead to da future. When Thugthug reach 15th levels, he get Dual Wield an Double Attack. So dat mean dat wit 2 minataur axes, Thug can hit fer 17 4 times each set of attacks, plus he can kick fer some damage too, and his attacks come fasta dan wit da 2 handed weapon. But Thug realize dat at lower levels, da 2 handed weapon do MUCH betta barshin' an it not a bad weapon ta use. Asides, Thug tink that it look very cool to have 2 axes in hands.

Ed note: Thug has been saving most of his practices until he reaches 15th level, when he should be able to invest roughly 20 practices each in Dual Wield and Double Attack the moment he gets them. He did invest some practice in Dodge and in Parry when he got each of them, and they have proven most invaluable to him, warding off potentially hefty blows from Lizardman Warriors, Shamans, Orc Warriors, and the odd Aquatic Goblin. As to weapon preference, the Gods have done an excellent job of balancing out weapon skills, and it really is a matter of taste, but as trolls begin with a 1- handed edged weapon, it made sense to Thugthug to develop with an eye toward the future.

The Middle Levels - Lizardmen Anyone?
When Thugthug make 8th level, he realize dat the froggies just ain't payin' off enuf in shineys or in exp ta keep swimmin' round da swamp barshin' em. So, Thug make some new friends and go off ta barsh lizardmens wit' dem. Thug find dat his friends Kirak (Troll Shadow Knight), Kilaka (Troll Warrior), an Tyrg (Ogre Warrior) make a great barshin' bruvahs. We go to Cazic Thule, home o da lizardmens and we barsh lots of dem. With 2 er 3 of us, we barsh em fast and make lots o gud money and exp. Lizardmen dere have a trick of runnin' off and hiding in the entrance to da dungeon of Cazic Thule where yas can't hit 'em, den dey come back with a bunch o friends to barsh on yas. Dem very smert lizards. It gud dat we better barshers. Some of dem tuff lizards drop bronze weapons, and dem werth a coupla platnium, which is gud, 'cause Thug is savin' fer chainmail. Lizards be gud barshin' until about 12th er 13th level when dey get a bit borin.'

Ed note: Progress through the lower levels can be done solo and fairly rapidly with a Troll. That regenerate means you only need to sit for a minute or two before you can charge back into the action. However, once you get to the high single digits, teamwork becomes essential. While it is possible to solo the lizardmen surrounding Cazic Thule, more often then not, they are able to flee to safety before you can deliver the killing blow. And the amount of time that it takes to kill one is quite a good deal longer than if you have even a single partner. Essentially it seems that Thugthug made more progress by spliiting loot and experience but killing more and tougher monsters than he could have done alone. He confided to me that a Shaman would have been very nice to have around, as would a caster of any sort (Shadow Knights gain access to 1st level necromantic spells at 9th level, which is a minor help), but it appears that Ogres and Trolls are not well suited for intellectual pursuits (surprise, surprise.)

Thugthug now nearly 14th level, and him a gud barsher. But lizardmen is startin' ta be too easy and dem not always give gud loot, so him look fer udder stuffs ta kill. Barsh-bruvah Tyrg an' his friends show Thug and company da wonders of Aqua Goblins. Dem give gud exp and great loots. All that an' Thug get to meet humies, elfs, halflings and udders. It turn out dat them helpful (but ugly!) So Thug vow ta not eat 'em any more. Da place we huntin' is full o big crocs and orcs and giants. It like a barshin' playground. Most of dis stuff too tuff fer Thug ta fight alone, so Thug puttin' on his best manners ta make new friends fer when his old ones ain't around. Thug even brush his toof.

Thug startin' ta think 'bout da future. It a great big world out here, and lots of stuff fer Thug ta barsh. Stuff me not ever hear of or see. Trolls like ta see new stuff and den barsh it! Thug excitied about da future. Thug hear dat dere are some yummy Orcs called da Deathfist near da hummie city o Freeport, meybe Thug go barsh dem and try ta get a gud name fer himself.

Thug will tell ya all 'bout his new friends and learnin' once he gets 'em. -Thugthug, 'Prentice Barsher

Ed note: I persist in following this oafish clod, deciphering his pedantic, childish prattle for the good of future warriors everywhere. We will see you around, safe travels, safe journey and remember, never turn your back on a Sand Giant. -Mardigan
