Playing a Dark Elven Warrior

 Introduction   Starting Out   Nektelos Forest   Moving On 

Introduction
So, you've decided to join the Neriak Indigo Guards. This is probablyone of the more unique combinations of race and class in Norrath. Whenmost think about warriors, they think one of the larger races likeBarbarians, Trolls, or Ogres.

Truth is, almost anybody can be a warrior except the High Elves andErudites. Dark Elves make just as good of a warrior. True,physical stamina and strength play a large role in being a successfulwarrior, but the natural elven racial attributes of agility anddexterity will definitely help your swordplay. If they can't hit you andyou never miss, who needs lots of hit points and biceps the size of atree trunk? Plus, your race has ultravision which means you never haveto worry about being in a cave or forest at night. Although there are stores that sell torches in Neriak city, youdon't have to buy them. Also, your race can hide. Basically you can turninvisible, but you cannot move while you do it. This is nice when itcomes time to sit down and heal; however, it doesn't always work andthere is no message to tell you if it worked or not. If your withothers, you can ask them if your visible or not. Also, the Teir' Dalhave the next most classes to choose from after the humans. This meansyou will be able to group with a wide variety of classes to form wellbalanced parties; however, as a general rule, I observed that 50% of theDark Elves were Necromancers and another 25% were shadow knights. Thereare wizards, magicians, clerics, rogues, warriors and a few enchantersto make up the rest of the population, but expect those two classes asthe norm. However, this is to your advantage. Your skills and weaponrywill be needed by the magic users. Since most of the Dark Elves tend tobe non-melee fighters, your in demand. It's always nice to be needed.

Starting Out
Before rolling up a Teir' Dal warrior (Teir' Dal is the word in theirown language for the the Dark Elf race), you should consider a fewthings. Although strength and stamina are important, I still believethat you shouldn't be deficient in other attributes. I did put most ofmy attribute points into strength and stamina. I also upped all the other attributes to at least 75 which is the average attribute of humans. Humans, of course, are the measuring stick of all other races. I try not to be inferior in any way.

Another consideration is religion. I chose to be agnostic. One of thethings NPC people will judge you by is your race, your class, and your religion. If you choose agnostic (meaning you don't worship any god), you stand a better chance of being tolerated in foreign cities. Notice that I said tolerated. Often you will be hard pressed to find amerchant to deal with, but at least you walked by the city gaurdsunharmed. I was able to travel to both Qeynos, Freeport, and thehalfling city of Rivervale unharmed; however, traveling to Fadwyrturned out to be the final demise of my character. The dwarven guards protecting the docks attacked me on sight. A NPC wood elf attacked my character during a rest stop while voyaging the sea, but luckily fell into the ocean before she could severely injure me. Be wary. The easiest way to tell if they will attack you is to do a /consider on them. The /consider command will naturally tell you if you can defeatthem in combat or not, but the first half of the message will tell youthe targets body language. For example: "The leatherfoot deputy looksat you dubiously, what would you like your tombstone to say?" If theyare dubious, then they will not deal with you, but they won't attack you either. If you can find somebody who is indifferent to you, remember where you found him. You can come back and sell loot to them and maybe buy food and water.

Ok, now your ready and standing in Neriak city.

The first thing you must do is get to know the city. Neriak is dividedinto three zones, and they can be very confusing to the new player. All have twists and turns in them, and you cannot see very much of the city in one glance. Mostly you can only see the alleyway in front of you.One thing I can tell you is both the Neriak Commons and 3rd Gate zonesare each split in half by a wall and the main gates enter into a chamberwith doors leading out the sides. So if you cannot seemto find the exit, but you walk through an empty chamber, stop andturn. You will probably see the gate. The foreign quarter zone is alsodivided into two parts. The human section and on the south side thereis a ramp that leads into another chamber full of Trolls and Ogres.

Once you can reasonably find your way around you should immediately look for the Warrior's guild. It is downstairs under the arena. This is common for warrior guilds to be in the form of a gladiator pit inNorrath. Neriak's pit is easy to find. It is in Neriak Commons and hasa pair of large stone pillars outside of it, one blue and one pink. Findthe Guild leader Seloxia Punox and give her (yes, the Dark Elf leader ofthe Indigo Guards is a woman) your guild note. You should then go tothe trainers in the guild and train up in the weapon skills you plan tomaster. I only recommend you put one point in each area you want, sincethey will go up in combat by themselves. I also recommend you pick atrade skill. It is going to be awhile till your good in a trade skilland putting training points towards one will help boost it up withoutwasting lots of money on attempts to use the skill.

Now it's off to the forest to attempt to prove your mettle in combat.

Kick and taunt as much as possible in combat to get these skills up.You can stop taunting enemies if your alone when your skill reaches yourlevel's limit. That limit is, level multiplied by 5 with anadditional 5 added to it. Thus at level 1, the most you can increase youskill is to 10. Kicking is a very useful ability that warriors startwith. A few other classes get kicking later on in their careers, butyou have it from day one. It does not do as much damage as your sword,but more damage to the enemy is always a good thing. It also comes inhandy later on if you get killed. Once, I was on the way back to mycorpse when I was attacked by a bear. Without my weapons and with noability in unarmed combat, I was still able to defeat it by just kickingit to death, though I should say that the bear was considered an easyfight at my level. This is the time when Warriors shine. The otherDark Elf tanks are generally shadow knights and Necromancer pets, anduntil level-9, when they start learning spells, the shadow knights arenot as good at combat as warriors and no pet will ever match awarrior. Even after that, warriors are still excellent in combat usingjust swords. One of the advantages over the shadow knights is that theymust purchase spells, while we can save our copper coins and buy armorand weapons. We also can use our training points when we level up ontrade skills to make our own armor and sharpen weapons, while shadowknights usually increase their spell casting skills.

Nektelos Forest
This zone is your newbie zone for the Dark Elves. It has both some prosand cons. One of the pros is the size of the zone. Qeynos andFreeport both have monsters outside the city gate, but still inside thatsame zone for their players to fight. This means they have a distinctover-crowding problem as players rush around trying to fight a creaturebefore another player does. We don't have this problem since our newbiearea is an entire zone itself. Plus, our guards generally kill-on-sightthe rest of the races of Norrath. This means you will not have tocompete with anyone but your fellow Dark Elves and maybe an lone Ogre orTroll. This means you can encroach onto human/half-elf territory andfight monsters there, but they cannot do the same. Also, the Lavastormmountains are attached to Nektelos forest, but has only one entrance.Although Lavastorm is not recommended until level8, it will be empty of competition when you are level 8 or more. That, plus it has the entrancesto THREE dungeons in it as well. Only the evil players have access tothese dungeons unless the rest of the good races turn evil or can figureout a way to sneak past the guards. The bad things about Nektelos isthe zone has no merchants inside it to sell loot to and only theskeletons have rusty weapons occasionally. As awarrior, your first weapons will mostly be rusty ones you acquire offdefeated enemies. Actual living, breathing races offer the best warriorloot in the form of armor pieces and weapons, but you have to leaveNektelos to find Orcs and such to fight.

Moving On
The Eastern Commonlands are right next door. It is also anice place to adventure around it, though almost all of the monstershere will kill you on sight and are significantly tougher than the onesin Nektelos Forest. The Deathfist Orcs in this zone are terrificsources of loot and rusty weapons. They even have cloth armor you canuse too. Plus, there is a warrior guild quest to bring back four orcpicks (a crappy weapon the orcs carry that isn't worth much). The trainer will reward you with a rusty sword or wineworth more than the picks. The decaying skeletons in this zone alsoseem to have more weapons too. On top of that, there is a merchant in atunnel that will buy from you so you no longer have to change zones tosell off your loot. In this zone you will most likely see your firstlive humans and half elves. You will probably have seen a few dead oneson the roads of Nektelos Forest after the guards find them pokingaround.

In summary, the Dark Elves have a niche that needs filling, swordsmen.If you are up to it and want to be something other than a human, but wantto group with people other than Shamans, then the Dark Elves await you.

Plus, we dark elves have got to have the coolest looking plate helmetsin all of Norrath.

From here on out I will let you discover the secrets of Norrath on yourown.
