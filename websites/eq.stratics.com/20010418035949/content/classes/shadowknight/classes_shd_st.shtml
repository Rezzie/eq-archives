Playing a Shadow Knight

 Introduction   Getting Started   Combat Skills   Trade Skills   Spells 

Introduction
Shadow Knights are probably the most frustrating class anyone could choose. They level slowly. They are fairly weak fighters for the first 10 levels. You will die often. You will be killed on sight in many regions regardless of race. Stop reading now. Stop. Stop. You will die very often. Stop... You're still here? Congratulations, I think you might have what it takes to become a Shadow Knight: Patience. If you can hang in there until level 10, you will be rewarded for your time and effort!
 top


Getting Started
You have four choices of race for Shadow Knight: Human, Troll, Ogre, or Dark Elf. Race is the most critical decision to the future of the Shadow Knight. First off, Dark Elves, Trolls and Ogres are hated races. They are killed on sight in most regions of the game while the Human is usually left alone. The Dark Elf is the best spellcaster of the bunch, but I think to make a successful Shadow Knight it is better to focus on combat first, magic ability second. The Ogre and Troll stand on pretty even footing as far as melee is concerned though they are a little weak in the spellcasting department. The Troll is probably the best choice though because of its natural healing ability. You will be able to jump back into combat much quicker. If you choose the Troll or Ogre, put all of your character creation points into Intelligence to pump up your mana. I made the mistake of putting my Troll Shadow Knight's points into Strength. Though I was able to scribe the 'Leering Corpse' spell at level 9, I was unable to cast it until level 11 due to insufficient mana. If you choose the Dark Elf, put all of your character creation points into combat attributes. Humans are on mostly average footing when it comes to all attrributes, so distribute your points equally amongst your primary attributes.
 top


Combat Skills
First, find your guild master. Do not use all of your practice points to train combat skills! They raise very quickly on their own. Just spend one practice in the desired combat skill to get it started. Save the rest of your points so at later levels you can get your important skills like Bash, Dodge and Double Attack up to par quickly. In early career, you need to decide if you want to pursue One-Handed Slashing or Two-Handed Slashing. Two-Handed Slashing is a better choice for the Shadow Knight because you get the Double Attack skill at a later level and not Dual Wield. I suggest you invest one point into Two-Handed Slashing and another towards One-Handed Slashing. Since the Shadow Knight is slow to level after first few levels, I find that you quickly max out on raising combat skills. Each level allows you to increase each combat skill about 5-7 points. So try and keep a One-Handed Slashing weapon and a Two-Handed Slashing weapon on your person at all times. Once you find that you have maxed one skill for the level, switch weapons and max out the other skill. I would also suggest spending one point on Hand-to-Hand, which will immediately increase your punch damage from 1-2 to 3-4, which definitely helps in case you lose your weapons through death or other means. I would not suggest archery since Fletching is not a viable option for hated races (see below).
 top


Trade Skills
Forget about Fletching and archery! There is nowhere in Oogok, Neriak or Grobb to buy the components to make arrows [note: I have feedbacked and bugged this deficiency over ten times, but Verant has still not fixed it. If you like this guide, please send a /feedback demanding that arrow parts be accessible to hated races!]. There is one place to get bowery parts in the Feerott, but it is too expensive to make bows and increase your Fletching skill. If you are really gung-ho about archery either be a Dark Elf or Human (they can get into to "good" cities to buy the supplies), or have a friend or another non-evil character travel into Kelethin and buy the necessary supplies for you. Tailoring, Blacksmithing and Baking are better trade skills for most Shadow Knights because the components necessary for these skills are available in all cities. In my experience, trade skills just aren't worth the time or effort at lower levels. Save your early to intermediate practice points for combat skills, which you'll need in order to be a successful Shadow Knight. Being a hybrid class already spreads you a bit thin, distributing practices to trade skills merely magnifies this.
 top


Spells
When you finally make it to level 9, you get those extremely valuable Necromancer spells. They aren't great but they will make your life a lot easier. Finally, you are not as weak a fighter anymore. Your best spell for you at this stage is Leering Corpse. so buy it immediately. It will create a level 3-5 pet that will act as your ally during combat. It's best to use this pet as an extra attack rather than let it take all the blows due to the fact that it is very weak and dies quickly. At level 9 most Shadow Knights will be taking on things that will eat aLeering Corpse for lunch--the people I know use them for the often critical first attack (such as taking the Harm Touch or first spell from an NPC SK or spellcaster).

From this point on, things will be much easier for you, both solo and in groups. Good luck!
