Problem is this, and it's really quite simple;

Orc Trainer in Crushbone, ya know the one. Drops that item. Trainer is a level 13 mob. Crusbone is level 5 to level 21ish.

There you sit, you and two friends at level 14 or so. Waiting for the Trainer to get that item. You've "camped" him through three comings but no luck. You've been trading off with 2 others seeking to get the item also. All these people look to work for an item that THEY will personally use on the character that is there getting it.

In comes Wizbang, level 34 Wizard. He's there, 20 levels above the trainer, to get the item for his newbie who is 14 levels below the ability to get the item him/her self. Wizbang takes every kill in 2 spells no matter how hard the five there try.

Above scenario is why twinking is frowned upon, is why camping is basically frowned up, and really is a massive waste of time for the 5 people working to get something for themselves.

That's the jist of it all. Camping becomes less of a problem, for me so far, at level 40 and up from what I see. Especially since me and mine tend to camp in the center of a zone rather at the zone boundry. Don't get much traffic. Why else is it not a problem? Why aren't the "twinkers" here getting items for their newbie? Cause it's to damn hard and the item gained is better for them, that character there getting it, and their friends won't usually be there to help them get another item for a newbie.

11 people in two groups camping a couple of unique spawns to get an item 8 of them want. Those people usually do that till 6 of them have gotten the item then they get bored or want to see something new.

*shrug* Some thoughts on it I guess

As for the other scenario's, ebay, Smithing for cash for Full STEEL plate, all those are fine. It's that first scenario, Wizbang scenario, that's the sour spot.
