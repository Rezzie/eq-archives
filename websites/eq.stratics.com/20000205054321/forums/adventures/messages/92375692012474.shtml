The Dark Path http://www.geocities.com/Area51/Realm/6796/

Fire was the key. Entire villages were set ablaze with madness, as families scurried for their lives. Searching through the red and orange kaos, young mothers would see our faces and their impendng doom. The raging fires around them were nothing, compared to the molten fire that coursed through their veins as we fed.

The Great city of Tos was last to fall to our feeding frenzy. Not a drop spilled, but every life withered to Tremere hunger. The flowing land of Meridian, and empty shell, a dried up bank, a parched desert of death. Our hunger grew, starving we flew endlessly, searching, needing, wanting more. Britania was our promised land.

The land of mana, Britania. How we killed, how we gorged ourselves. There was never a love so perfect. The nights were spent with peseant cat an mouse games, tropheys of carved carcasses and decapitated heads. Castles, towers of magic, islands of evil bore witness to our heritage. So ripe were the friuts of this new land! Yet we demanded MORE! Innourak took notice of our aragance and smiled.

His voice, the voice of a thousand screaming souls, invaded my thoughts...
"In times of trouble, and times of fate,
there are many things to which we can relate.
Darkness in a blinding tunnel,
fire, on a wind swept field,
nightmares, on never ending dreams.
Through the Chaos and Disorder you must learn,
to quench the fires that eternally burn.
For in the darkness, pursued by the fire,
the nightmares are gaining, followed by desire.
Run as you must, hide as you might,
the beasts are never far from sight.
Until the day that you comically learn,
that the darkness is light,
and the fires don’t burn.
The Nightmares are mares that wishfully play,
the darkness is night, and the light is the day."

Endlessly I waited. Days to hours, hours to Seconds, seconds to what seemed an eternity. Even to a Vampire as old as myself, the days grew long and life had little meaning. As I watched the world slowly die I woundered who was Innourak? The Imported murial on the wall showing Vesper in its hight of decadence seemed to be my only retreat. For years I searched for the answers. The portal to Neriak almost went unnoticed. Upon entering a voice softly spoke the words "Welcome to the city of the damned De'Minion."
