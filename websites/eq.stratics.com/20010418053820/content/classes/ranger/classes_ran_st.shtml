Playing a Ranger

 Intro   General   Race   Stats   Starting Cities   Skills   Magic   Ranger's Role in a Group   Going Solo   Cons   Hints 

Introduction
Rangers are the protectors of the forest and the defenders of nature.And as Rangers, we can forage for food, track our prey and hide from ourenemies and sneak through their defenses. We can learn magic and survivefor long periods of time without ever going to a city to re-supply. Mostimportantly however, we are combatants! And as such, we can hold our ownin melee combat. We are the tanks that protect our party and we arecapable of beefing up our defenses via magic. At higher levels, we caneven heal ourselves when we are wounded. A very self-sufficient classthat can hold it's own.
 top


General
Rangers are one of the most well rounded character classes in the landof Norrath. With a wide variety of weapons and armor to choose from,spell casting ability, a wide variety of skills and the unique skill oftracking. Rangers are one of the few classes that can do it all. Maybenot all, but they have the skills that several of the other classesspecialize in. They aren't the masters that they are, but they areeffective none-the-less.

Race
Rangers can be one of three races: Humans, Half Elves and Wood Elves.Each race has it's own advantages and disadvantages, so it's up to youon what you want to play. Half Elves and Wood Elves, get Infravision,Humans don't. However I would advise choosing your race on your ownstyle and personal preference over stat limitations or things likeinfravision.
 top


Stats
I would advise buying up your Strength first. As one of the fightertypes you will need strength to dish out the damage and wear all thatheavy armor while still having enough space for things such as weaponsand equipment. Stamina gives you your HP, but I would advise gettingyour Agility up second. Agility is important because it adjusts yourarmor class and prevents you from taking hits. Your Wisdom determinesyour mana when you get magic at level 9. So if you want more mana later,you can increase this. (I didn't and I have sufficient mana for myneeds).

Starting Cities
Wood Elves start in Kelethin and Humans & Half Elves start in SurefallGlade (near Qeynos). I'm not sure why half Elves cannot start in eitherlocation, but that is the way it is right now.
Surefall Glade

Located near Qeynos, it is a beautiful forest with vast caves toexplore. The Rangers and Druid's of the land have set up theirsettlement away from the big city here in the forest. Note that all theanimals are under the protection of the NPC's. I would advise againsthunting any animals you find there. However it is open season on anypoachers you may encounter. It shouldn't take you very long to find theRanger's Guild. Hint: It's that cabin on the lake.

Kelethin

Kelethin lies deep within the Greater Faydark Forest. Kelethin does nothave a path or maker to guide visitors. (Er, that is until Beta 3.5 cameout. There is now a path from Felwithe to Kelethin). The Wood Elf Cityof Kelethin stands proudly in the treetops, keeping it's inhabitantssafe from the creatures that lurk below. To enter the city you will needto use one of the three "Lifts" they have constructed around the city.You will want to watch your step once in Kelethin, many adventurers havefallen to their deaths from the heights. The Ranger's Guild is difficultto find so I will provide you with directions.

You will need to find the city's main lift. You can't miss it, it's theonly lift with guards and a fire burning by it. (That and the path nowleads you straight to it). Once there (and you are in the city), youwill want to run straight across the first platform to a suspensionbridge. On the next platform you will see a sturdy wood ramp on yourleft, go up that ramp. This platform has the bank (remember that, youwill want to know where it is later), Run across the platform, you willsee another wooden ramp going up. Once you have gone up that platform,you won't see any other platforms...don't look down if you are afraid ofheights, you are at the top of the city, (falling from here will killmost characters). On your right side you will see a small fire burning.This marks where the ramp going down is. CAREFULLY find the edge of theramp and go down, I stress that because I've seen MANY foolishadventurers fall, and it's not pretty. On this platform you will see alarge building in front of you with the sign "Faydark's Champions." Youhave arrived. Now don't fall on your way back to the lift.

Skills
You will start with several skills you will want to hotkey right away.Tracking, Sense Direction, Kick, Taunt (and if you are a Wood Elf youget Hide and Forage at lvl 1). Here is a list of some of the skills youwill learn. So unless I say otherwise you will want to only spend ONEpractice point to learn the skill. Save up all the points you can forlater levels. You will never know when you will find a need for a skillyou haven't practiced.
Tracking - The trademark of the Ranger class. This skill is unique to the Ranger and Druid Classes (Druids get it a much higher levels). It lets you find specific creatures around you. You can track everything.From other players, guards and NPC's to all the creatures in yourgeneral location. This is an invaluable skill, which you should quicklymaster with practice. Think of it as your own personal Radar.

Kick - An extra attack available at the fifth level.

Taunting - An invaluable skill of the warrior. This allows you to getcreatures to attack you. When a comrade is low on health or needs tocast a spell, use this skill to get whatever is hitting them to hit youinstead.

Sense Direction - Keep this maxed out. You will want to know whichdirection you are running while guiding a group around, looking forsomebody, simple navigation or running for your life.

Forage - (lvl 3) Once you have this skill, practice it as much aspossible. You should never have to buy food again once you get good atit.

Dodge - (lvl 8) Once you spend one practice point on this skill, combatwill do the rest.

Magic - (lvl 9) You must learn all five magic skills: Abjure, Alter,Conjure, Divinate & Evoke to cast spells.

Sneak - (lvl 10) This skill allows you to walk right past guards andother creatures without them noticing you. Practice this as much aspossible.

Meditate - (lvl 15) You can memorize spells and recover mana faster.

Bind Wound - (lvl 15) If you have bandages, you can heal yourself andothers.

Dual Wield - (lvl 17) Put away that shield, you can carry two weapons atonce now.

Parry - (lvl 18) Same as dodge, works automatically when in combat.

Double Attack - (lvl 20) I'll let you figure this one out.

Hide - (lvl 25) Makes you "disappear" where you are standing, but youbecome visible once you move. This is invaluable when you are low onhealth and need to rest. Creatures tend to attack a helpless sittingtarget. However, they can't attack you if they can't see you. Doesn'talways work, so keep practicing it.

You will get more skills at higher levels, so just ask your guildtrainer what they are.

Magic
Rangers finally get to learn magic at level 9 and it's important tospend your 5 practice points on the 5 new spell skills you can now learn(see the skill section). If you don't, you might find it difficult touse your newly acquired ability. As for your spells, you don't getnearly as many as your Druid counterpart. You do get some very effectivecombat oriented spells along with some nice backup spells you will notwant to be without, such as Skin Like Wood. When you reach level 15 youwill be able to learn the second level of spells. You will receive yourfirst offensive spell "flame lick" and one you will have been waiting along time to get "minor healing."

At level 9 you will memorize all 5 of your spells, but at 15 you willhave to start making choices since you can have only 8 memorized. Isuggest you do the following: Replace "snare" with "root," They doeffectively the same thing, but Root is better. You shouldn't need "truenorth" because your sense direction skill should be good now. Memorizethe other new spells and you should be good to go.

Ranger's Role in a Group
Rangers get many useful skills that make them not only versatile, but avalue to any group. From personal experience, I have found manyadventurers feel better having a Ranger in their group. The reason beingRangers make not only a great tank, but are great at finding opponentsto fight. Some of the roles a Ranger plays in the group are:The Scout - While the group rests and meditates, the ranger can wanderoff and use his tracking skill to find out what is in the area. Also theranger can go out farther than most PC's and still make it back to thegroup without getting lost.
Tracker - You will be assigned the duty of finding creatures to bringback to the rest of the party to kill. There really is no escaping thisfate. But that is why I love this class, because everyone follows yourlead. I have seen more groups than I can possibility count following aRanger tracking down something for them.

Lookout - Some might argue me calling tracking, "radar", but if you haveever played a Ranger this describes exactly what Tracking is. There aretimes in Norrath when you will find yourself in a zone where there arecreatures lurking about that can single handedly kill all the Players(and sometimes NPC Guards) that are around. You will learn who thosecreatures are quickly (trust me), and you can then keep tracking to seeif they are around. Of course a good Ranger would then WARN other PC'sof his findings.

Tank - Rangers are front line combatants, there is no question about it.You are the one keeping the mobs off of the cleric and mages. Be sure tokeep your taunt skill up so you can keep the entire melee directed atyou instead of the spellcasters and your wounded companions.If you want an active role in any group, a Ranger is a good choice. Youwill find yourself leading groups all over Norrath. You will be soughtout by PC's to Find their lost pets, find certain NPC's (for Quests),locate a certain monster. This is not an antisocial class, much like thebard, you are a great support character with many aspects to add to agroup.

Going Solo
As one of the most versatile classes in the game, you will slowly findit easier to go solo in this dangerous world. Although youreffectiveness increases dramatically with a group to back you up.Rangers are lucky in that they can "pick" their targets when soloing.You can track down what you can handle and then kill it off while othersare forced to sit and wait for something to walk by them or respawn. Youwill find you are always moving as you solo, so be careful not to getlost! The down side of soloing is that you will get beat up a lot andyou WILL find yourself sitting and waiting for your HP to slowly heal.Then again, on a positive note: at level 15 you get the spell "minorhealing," this will cut your sit time in half maybe more. Something tolook foreword to!

Cons
Of course we aren't as effective as a Warrior or Monk when it comes tomelee, but I would rank Rangers as a close third. As for magic, we haveto wait for level 9 to learn how to use it, and level 15 to get ourfirst attack and healing spells. We also lack a large reserve of mana,so we will have to know when to use what we have, or run out in a badsituation. Also we get few spells. However, I feel they are sufficientfor a warrior. Other cons are that since we are the protectors ofnature, we lose status with our guild if we go and hunt many of thenatural creatures of the land... be careful about what you kill.
 top


Hints
Memorize the zones you are in. As a Ranger you are "expected" to knowwhere everything is and you can make many friends by being a guide. Thisis a typical stereotype, one that you will have to get used to.Practice your Tracking skill all the time! Keep it maxed out at eachlevel. Also keep your "Sense Heading" skill at maximum. These are twoextremely useful skills for getting around the lands of Norrath.Always know where you are! There is nothing more embarrassing thangetting lost, especially for the Ranger class. Use your tracking andsense direction to get "un-lost".

Be careful about killing animals. Some animals you kill will lower yourstanding with your guild. And trust me, you don't want your Guild Masterto refuse to train you. I've seen it happen.

Learn to use your spells effectively. Many of the magic using classesget so many spells they don't have time to learn all the subtle uses oftheir powers. They tend to just memorize a different one.Lastly

Read the Warrior and Druid player guides. Since Rangers are a hybridof the two, they cover many good points that I did not cover here.

By order of the Gods! Leave the bears of Surefall Glade undisturbed!
