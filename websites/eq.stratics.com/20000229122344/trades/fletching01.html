Fletching Starting Information

Well, here is what I have learned about Fletching and Bowyering as a Level 10 Ranger with a skill of 55 in the craft.

  1. I suggest you invest very few points in Fletching to get it started. I would recommend between 4 and 8. I personally used 5 pts at level 5 as a ranger to get going. The skill improves rather rapidly.
  2. Be warned that fletching is NOT fun. It is very tedious. You have to put 1 of each of the 4 arrow components in to the kit 1 at a time. If you put in a stack you will get back 1 arrow if successful. So, before you start get comfortable and be ready to excercize your wrist often.
  3. Begin by buying up 20 of each component, and get started. Buy the cheapest of each of the 4 components, so each attempt costs you 4 copper. You will use these arrows to archer with as they are the basic arrow, providing a range of 50 and damage of 1. You usually rise 2-4 pts per grouping of 20 attempts. So depending how many training points you invest, you will reach the cap of triviality on the basic Wood Arrow in 3 or 4 batches of 20 arrows. Not too bad.
  4. The next type of arrow is the Class 1 Wood Arrow. This requires the 3 cp notch, which brings your cost per attempt to 6 cp. You will fail a lot at this point in making arrows. Failures can increase your skill, however, and the rate of increase is comparable to the previous arrows. I would use these arrows as well to archer with, as they provide a range of 75 and damage of 1. The cap of triviality for these is a rating of 36. Making a rough guess at this point you have probably spent 1 to 1.3 platinum on increasing your skill to 35. You also have a nice number of arrows in your inventory. I would now go and get a bow....
  5. DO NOT BUY A BOW! The best part of being a fletcher is that you get to make bows. The store bought bows are very expensive and not good. With a skill of 35 you can easily make a bow that is better than the store bought ones. I would recommend buying the bowyering book, a Hickory staff, and some Linen string. This is rather cheap, and your success rate should be very high at 35 skill. This lets you make a Rough Hickory Recurve Bow with stats of Damage: 9 Delay: 46 and Range:50.
  6. Now go out and increase your archery skill. The best way by far to do this is beat on animals that tend to run from you. When they run do not chase. Instead you pull out the bow and you get easy target practice. All the players I've seen w/ high Archery skill do this. The second way to get in free shots is to group w/ a warrior or other melee type and fire at the monster's back while they are engaged.
  7. Once you cap out at a skill of 36 I'd make basic arrows to practice archery with. Otherwise your expense may increase. At some point however you wanna go above 36 to make better bows. Making Class 2 Wood Arrows will do this. They are composed of the better notch, and the better fletch. The cost per attempt is now 1 sp 1 cp. At this point things move up much more slowly. I only went up 1-2 pts per batch of 20 attempted. The skill cap for this type of arrow is a fletching skill of 46
  8. To go beyond 46 I suggest moving to the basic Bone Arrow, which includes a Bone Shaft and all the other basic 1 cp components. The cost per attempt now is almost 2 sp. I haven't reached the skill cap for this item yet. I sell back the made arrows, because in my opinion they are way to expensive to use. I take a loss on the sale, but I think it's the right move.
  9. At this point you can make an Elm Bow, which provides an important range bonus. Using a linen string and elm staff you can make a Rough Elm Recurve Bow, with stats of Damage: 12 Delay: 47 and Range: 75. Getting better. I now use Class 1 arrows as my basic archery arrows as they both have ranges of 75 and I can now hit for 13 pts per shot. The cost of the components for this bow are around 2.5 Platinum.
