Introduction:

First off, let me say I write this from experience. I am a level 54 Erudite wizard on the Rathe.  At times, the game was painful to play, as a wizard at times can be a difficult class to level.  There seems to be a series of levels where you just seem to cant make any ground.  At least that was my experience.  Maybe this was my tendency to solo a lot and get brave.  I liked to solo, and if you do too, a wizard is one of the best classes to solo.  Of course if I had to do it all over, it would be a little easier.

Wizards are a very straightforward class pretty much, dont let anyone tell you different.  Its when wizards start to believe that they are something they are not that gets them in trouble.  You are a caster. You deal damage with spellpower.   Let the melee types do the meleeing.   This does not mean you should not mix it up. Let me explain:

Up to the first 20-22 levels, some wizards think (once they get a staff that does damage) that they can melee mobs (and indeed they can inflict some damage at these lower levels).  In fact when starting out it is necessary to use your melee to finish mobs.  This is temporary.  The higher you get, the harder mobs get and their hps go up exponentially.  Soloing a level 45 Tatterback Gorilla (he has over 6000 hps - yes you too WILL be able to solo this) in Kunark will not be possible with meleeing.  In fact, the key to survival is to not let him hit you at all. One hit of double damage can be 137 hps per shot.   Why did I go over this? Because I want you to think about what you spend your character creation points on and your skill points when you level.

 top

Character Creation/Important Attributes:

This is a roleplaying game and as such, will probably determine what you select for race. You have several options of course.  High Elf, Erudite, Human, Dark Elf, Gnome. 

The most important attribute is intelligence of course. Everyone knows this for casters.  Intelligence determines mana, especially at the higher levels.  More mana means more usefulness to your group and yourself.  But alas, what is the second most important?  Hands down, I say Agility. Why? Because Agility makes you run faster. The more you have, the faster you can run.  And speed is the key to surviving as a squishy wizard. You must be able to make the zone point when hauling ass from a pack of mobs.  In theory AGILITY also helps you take less hits and it seems to work this way.  Who has the best agility starting out? Gnomes and Dark Elves.   Selecting a class of wizard with their starting attributes is of course the most important decision you must make. High INT? Use Erudite.  But dont think you have any special advantage with a beginning high intelligence wizard. It is so EASY to get intelligence enhancing items as you advance through the game, you can reach the 200 level cap easily in your 30s.  If your twinked, wellmuch earlier.  Agility enhancing items are a little less plentiful for a wizard so I recommend dumping a LOT of creation points into AGILITY when starting out.   In fact, go ahead and pick your class and dump as many points into AGILITY as you can and the rest into INTELLIGENCE.  You will not regret this when you make 50. Trust me. I made an Erudite wizard with starting 142 Intelligence, and dumped the rest into STAMINA because the game defaults this as important to a wizard.  Trust me, whatever race you play, your starting STAMINA will be perfectly fine to keep as is for your entire life. It is just not that important for a wizard (contrary what the guidebooks and game steers you toward).  

So I started with a low 70 Agility and 142 Intelligence. I was slow. I was easy to beat on. I had problems til I got Journeyman Boots and AGI enhancing items.  My intelligence is now 212 and my AGILITY 135 with stat enhancing items.  Not overly impressive although my other stats are well rounded out: 70 STR, 115 DEX, 103 WIS, 80 CHA. I always believed I should be a well-rounded wizard since I started myself at an AGILITY disadvantage.  My AGI will never go much higher because I can hardly raise it anymore with items. I already am loaded up.  But if you start your character wisely, imagine your stats at 54?  I can: INT: 200-205, AGI: 180+++???.  Who cares so much about the rest? They will take care of themselves in time.

Remember about starting a Gnome. You will be always looking up to people (I never liked this).  Human? Your stats are average across the board. Dark Elf? You will be hated in a lot of places.  But this only matters if you want to see every city.  I personally have ruined most factions nowadays and it dont much matter to me. Just personal preference. They like me in North Freeport. I can still get all the supplies I need there.

 top

Getting Started:

Ok so you got your character. Now go fight. Solo at first for many levels. Blast your direct damage spells. Root em.  Bash em. If you are an Erudite, take the boat and get bound in Qeynos immediately.  Tox forest sucks to hunt in.  Its pretty simple living the wizards life early on.

 top

Guilds:

Join a guild.  You will have a much easier time leveling (especially when soloing gets tough).  It is a social game.  You will make friends for life so to speak.  They may give you free stuff. Do it, do it before you reach level 12 or at least before 20.

 top

Information Gathering:

Once I hit level 12 or so, I went to West Karana and its rich hunting grounds.  I frequently started to group here with others taking on bandits.  I dont see W Kar as crowded and popular as it used to be. Many peeps like the Ro Deserts and Oasis of Marr and other places.  Its up to you and your starting Characters race.  You will find a place and partners in time.  I cannot possibly tell you all the combinations.  Read websites about mobs and such. Download maps.  Get an edge before you go into a place. Do your homework. After all, that is what you are doing by reading this huh?  You will save yourself much pain if you gather information first.  Too many peeps dont take advantage of the wealth of info out there and maps.  USE THEM!!!  Start now.  I still see so many people getting lost even at level 50 plus and dying because they DONT BELIEVE IN USING MAPS! Hogwash I say! Who wants to die? This game is tough enough as it is. EQAtlas is a very nice place for general zone/mob info and has very nice maps. Of course Allakhazams is one of my favorites.  They have links to all the nice maps and other websites. Also learn how to use the location command.  Do it, learn it now. It will make your life easier when someone gives you their LOC and you are too stupid to not know how to find them.  You are a wizard.  If anything, you will be one of the best at getting around and travelling.  You SHOULD be an expert on this.

 top

Early Mid-Life Problems:

Levels 20 -24 can be tough on your own. This is where you definitely want to be grouped. WHY? Because you will find yourself still using a level 16 direct damage spell and your level 12!.  Soloing these levels were tough for me, and I almost quit the game.  Died many times and you start to find out that maybe you are not the most powerful class out there.  So group up.

 top

Early Mid-Life Rewards:

Let me tell you where to hunt once you hit level 26: In Beholder.  Solo the named evil eyes at the temple in the canyon.  You may die a few times in this level, but mostly you will prevail and make exp fast and get rich.  After 26, I dont remember dying here except for maybe once.  I made well over 5000pp soloing eyes from levels 26 all the way to 31.  This is still BIG coin for your level.  This is one of your first camps also. You get Polished Bone Bracelets (PBBs) which go for about 100pp each. Also a lot of gems and such drops. It all added up. I was able to buy myself my first real gear. Int items and stuff.   When I was there, the camps were not very crowded and I usually quickly got one of the 2 spots.  Why? Because many peeps try this camp too early. Watch them get charmed and die.  They quickly give it up. Eyes are too dangerous for many, but perfect wiz food.  Low hps and such.  Blast the hell outta of them. Root, shoot again. Dead Eye. Do not be afraid. Face your fears. EQ gets much scarier later on.  Learn to deal with eyes at this stage in life, and you will be prepared mentally to face much tougher opponents later on. They say wisdom helps you from getting charmed and it seems to be true.  If you have problems, try buying a few wisdom items to boost this resistance. I rarely got charmed, but then again, I started with higher wisdom than most wizards being an Erudite.

 top

 

Early Mid-Life Item Buys:

Remember I said AGILITY is the most important secondary stat? Well this is true but now lets talk Dexterity.  When I came through the ranks, I always read that high DEX helps you recover from a hit while casting.  This is still debated by many and never been totally clarified.  Actually I had but ignored AGI until level 40 or so when I realized that I had made a mistake investing so much into DEX early on.  But did I make a mistake then? I had boosted my DEX at one point to 142 with items, and I still believe it helps you recover from interrupts and continue casting. My experience tells me it did and still does. I wasnt kiting mobs at this point yet. Still mostly root and shoot..  When doing eyes, and buying items with all that coin I earned, I bought DEX items mainly (remember I had high starting int).  I think it helped me with my root, shoot solo experiences early on in my late 20s and 30s and beyond.  So dont discount this stat. There are many relatively cheap items (serpentine bracer that not only boosts AGI but DEX as well. And I remember the day I bought platinum Star Ruby Vail and Golden Star Ruby rings.  I played with these items on me for many levels.  Actually when soloing in these level ranges, it may be MORE important than AGI, because lets face it, without Journeyman Boots and until you get Bonds of Force at 29, you cant kite mobs.  Ill let you decide. If you started with high AGI in your character, INT and DEX items are probably the way to go for now.

One more important point: Be frugal with your money. It is such a crock that lower levels think plat is unimportant at higher levels. Baloney! Actually, it is more important. Peridot costs 11pp a pop (used for many high level shield spells and such).  You will want/require better equipment and more diversified equipment.  So do RESEARCH on an item before you let some dupe talk you into spending your hard-earned plat. Once again, many websites such as MaximumEQ have nice pricing guides. Do the research, know what you want to buy, and go get it with knowledge as your bargaining chip!

 top

Early Mid-Life Grouping:

Peeps will think youre the man (or woman). Your power is considerable versus mobs youll be fighting.  I didnt use Tishans Clash much if at all in the mid to late 20s (because it has 2 saving rolls versus the one roll of a direct damage and gets resisted often.)  But then again, I didnt group much in these levels as you can see.

 top

 

Skill Points:

So you are leveling. What do you dump skills into? I dumped into research. Dumb me.  After level 50, research is not a gaming factor.  Post 50 spells are dropped from mobs after this point.  So you can make spells? Who cares.  Dont waste your points.  You will find many wizards who will make/give you spells nowadays.  There are many Rune events on most servers with wizards gathering and trading runes/spells.  So dump your points into DEFENSE first if you can.  DUMP some in a skill you would like to learn. Dump it into anything you like besides research.  If youre on the Rathe, ILL make your spells if ya like.

 top

Practicing:

I used to practice my spell casting while fishing to max it out for my level that I was at.  Also practice swimming. These long sessions can be boring, but you may find that your casting skills start to fall behind when you level up.  Take some time to practice casting while fishing on a dock.  Remember every little edge helps. Also when you gain Specialize skill at 20, make SURE you specialize in Evocation.  This skill will go up to 200 or so over time.  The other 3 specialization will stop at 50.  Evocation is your bread and butter direct damage spells use this skill).

 top

Mid-Life Soloing

Of course it seems I am talking a lot of solo experiences in my life.  This is just my preference.  I certainly have had my share of groups and enjoyed most of them.  But as a wizard, you have the ability to solo well at higher levels that many classes do not.  I got through levels 37-40 by soloing Vhalen Nostrolo in South Karana. I just rooted and shooted! Once again, at first it was scary but if done properly, will propel you through these levels. Of course you will take unrecoverable faction loss with League of Bards. Oh well, such is life. 

 top

Mid-Life Grouping:

It used to be that once you hit 35 or so, you went to lower Guk or SolB.  I never did Guk much, and had preferred SolB.  Of course you have many options now with Kunark out.  But I tell you SolB group fighting propelled me through the 40s in no time at all.  Just dont die a lot. Guk has more loot stuff but dying is probably easier there.  But then again a high level wizard is the master of Lower Guk.

In SolB and Guk a common mistake is to go there too early.  At level 35 in a group there you are actually hurting yourself because peeps will be hogging all the experience usually.  Dont go until 40 or so. I always got in the King group (just get on waiting list they always need a good nuker) from 41 on and pretty much stayed there til 47!  And that didnt take long.   I also did groups in South Karana doing gnolls at the spires a lot in the early 30s.  Whatever your preference, just keep safety in mind. Dying is not a way to level. Thats why at these levels I did SolB and such, because of easy escape routes.  Remember, in the mid to late 30s and 40s you must adjust your former tendencies to overburn mobs. Now you must learn more patience. Now is when you use Tishans Clash more and just STUN the mobs until an appropriate time to burn em down.  However, I found that in the frantic action of the King group in SolB, I was a nuking powerhouse.  If in this group, dont be afraid to throttle it up as being TOO conservative is worse than overnuke.  I got hit a lot there and you know what, my DEFENSE got maxxed out there (145 I think for wizards). Higher defense means a little more AC.  You should get your defense maxxed out in King room.

One wizard fallacy I want to dispel: At all costs conserve mana. This is not necessarily true.  What good is having a full tank of mana when youre all dead or running?  In a group, full mana = wasted mana.   Yes.  Think about it.  If I sit at max mana for 5 minutes that is at least 400 (or more) damage I could have had on a mob.  Max mana only helps in dire situations but by then it is usually out of control anyway.  On the other hand, dont always be too low.  They need your finishing touches if things get out of hand.   Strike a balance for the situation. If the group is steady solo pulling, you can afford to burn mana a little more than if your in a dangerous camp with multiple spawns.

You will know when to adjust your style, as a good wizard will know this.   Sometimes it is best to use a Stun, sit, Stun approach in a more controlled pull/multiple mob camp situation that is well in hand.  On the other hand some camps require quick deaths so that mobs dont spawn on top of each other. Adjust accordingly to your group and camp.  Never let anyone tell you that you MUST play one style or another. The SITUATION will always dictate.  You will learn.

 top

Late Life Camping:          

It is fun making 49. You get Ice Comet.  You can easily buy this spell. You dont have to do the staff quest to get it.  With the power of Ice Comet, you have the option of camping mobs for loot to sell for plat. My specialty was learning the spawn patterns in sola of the Kindle and his precious impskin gloves.  Always seemed to get lucky with him and sold them for 2000-3000pp at the time.  I also did ancient croc in Upper Guk, did the hero bracer camp/quest, and a few others. It is up to you. By now you should know your capabilities. Just look at an item you want and go camp it. Utilize websites and once again do the research first. It makes life easier.  You can also go and exact revenge on those mobs that killed you early in life. I particularly liked killing Chief Goonda and his cronies in West Karana!  If you need the cash, consider camping an item.

 top

Do the Solusek Wizard Quests:

Just what I said.  When I got around to doing quests like the Acumen mask, I was easily soling the mobs that dropped the items.  I still wear the Acumen mask and frequently use my cloak still.  One other thing, get the Staff of Temperate Flux. Every little bit helps. It lowers cold and heat resists by 6 each.  I have the old version and can cast while on the run instantly. I can spam mobs if they resist til it sticks (it actually casts the lower element spell). I understand this has changed for anyone getting the staff nowadays.

 top

The Kite Master: Jouryneyman Boots and Bonds of Force:

I got lucky. I zoned into South Ro one time and immediately locked on the ancient cyclops. My heart raced. Surely it will be engaged before my slow self gets there! But alas, I ran and ran and I was so nervous that I almost blew it. My heart raced. I was firing upon the ancient! My dream, Journeyman Boots!  Well, I did it rather easily, you see I was 50 at the time.  I did the rest of the quest and soon had Journeyman Boots. My wizard life changed a bit after that.  No longer did I have to beg for sow (though I never did, isnt my style - I always preferred to be self-reliant). Let me tell you this: Try and get Journeyman Boots.  I had been trying since level 40 or so. I always popped into South Ro and looked for the ancient. I got lucky one time. He is not hard to kill in the 40s as a soloist.  You can camp the one in Ocean of Tears with a friend (hard to solo Ocean of Tears ancient at these levels, S ro ancient much easier).  I always kept an ear out while in South Karana too.  You MUST get them.  Life is sweet when you do.

After Journeyman Boots I had discovered a little spell I hadnt used much: Bonds of Force.  A wiz snare with a good definite set length of time.  My kiting career took off from here. I soloed anything and everything that conned blue. Simple. Snare, run, blast, run a little, blast more, dead.  You may find you want to employ Bonds of Force much earlier than I did, but then again, I was in solb in the 40s and did fine with root and shoot from 37-41(Vhalen remember?). 

I now kite regularly in Kunark whenever I feel like soloing.  Level 45 plus mobs, no problem. In fact I mostly use the more mana efficient post 50 direct damage spells. Many quick blasts, dead mob. I have never died yet from kiting mobs here. Their aggro range is small and I can outrun them if I get in trouble. Once in a while I have to use root if Bonds of Force fails, but it mostly doesnt.  My best mob to date was soloing a Mature wurm in Skyfire. I had clarity and used Harvest once during a lengthy tactical battle.  I wound up doing over 9K of damage to take him down.  And he dropped a post 50 wiz spell I needed!

One other point about kiting. Learn to use the 3rd person view.  When I kite I approach a mob, stop hit F9 3 times til Im in LAST view mode and pan around by holding down right mouse button. Go to last view mode because if you quickly need to change back into first person view, you just need to hit F9 one more time.  I then cast by left-clicking spell icons.  I run using the arrow pads on my keyboard. It takes a little practice, but soon it will be second nature. Some prefer using hotkeys to cast but Im a mouse user except when running in this mode.  The third person view allows you to see not only where you are headed, but how far the chasing mob is behind you if you learn how to pan quickly.  I highly recommend this method.

 top

Area Effect Spells:

Ah yes, those stinky area effect spells. Didnt use em pre-50, cause they suck.  Unlesswell if you can find a group of 4 or 5 wizards, you can have imagination and blast a lot of supernovas. I have heard of 5 wizards clearing out King room in Lower Guk doing this. 

The post 50 spells are not bad at all. I am still learning them. However, most peeps dont want you to even THINK about using AE spells in their presence.  Most prefer the mezmerize, one at a time approach to killing mobs.  Dont think this will ever change cause Kunark mobs have tremendous hit points.  I just got a new spell, Pillar of Frost.  I snared 3 Kunark mobs in Burning Woods and took em all out at once for experience on all 3!  Its a little tricky to run and get them all lined up, but it worked with great effect. 

 top

 

What to buy/Stats:

At higher levels you will have the money hopefully to buy items that will alter your statistics any way you want.  I keep multiple items on hand for different situations.  For instance if youre gonna do Lord Nagafen, equip up with fire resist items or else you may die before you get off more than 3 Ice Comets.  When you do Gods and such, you need to equip yourself accordingly.  Those seemingly worthless buff items become more important than ever. Ask any professional god killing meleer.  They have lockers full of gear according to the situation.

You can pretty much buy items to alter your stats any way you like it.  That is why the starting attribute stats are so important. You cannot change these.  I have stressed Agility because I found that that is the second most useful attribute besides Intelligence.  It is easy to enhance your intelligence more so than agility. Of course, get your intelligence to 200, dont ever neglect that. You can also opt to go the mana enhancing item route. I.E. - Black Sapphire Plat Necklace gives you 55 mana, 35 hps, a nice item indeed that is very useful when in large planes groups.  Adjust your wearing of items according to what the situation calls for. I wear AGILITY for speed when soloing. I wear mana items when in Plane of Fear. I put on CHA items when selling back gems to NPCs and such.  You can never have enough equipment it seems. Dont think you are stuck with your stats. If you dont like them, do research on what you would like to have and make a plan and get them! I could have over 260 intelligence easily. In fact, someday when I get enough money again, Ill experiment with this setup.  I just believe in maintaining a well-balanced character and I can usually go into most situations with what I have on just fine.

Just dont get too hung up about it as your coming through the ranks.  You probably will not have enough money to buy all the equipment you want anyway.  You will do fine even if you cannot afford backup items.

 top

Spell Book Organization

Simple enough but keep it organized.  This is how I do it: I put all my self buffs, invis, snare, root spells etc. on page 1 -2 and my most frequently used damage spells on pages 3 - 4. My gate is left top of page 5 and rest of 5 -6 is gate spells. 7 - 8  are teleport spells, and 9-10 my evac spells.  I stuck all my defunct obselete spells on like pages 23 on up and arranged them there by type. It just makes it easier. Oh by the way, you wont be evaccing in Kunark hardly ever. The distances involved in getting back are just not worth it. Of course make a LOC hotkey, a CORPSE hotkey, sit, camp, assist, etc. hotkey. These are basics for all classes. I also have a guild all hotkey, etc.  Its your preference but take advantage of the games offerings to make your life easier from the start.

Well I hoped you enjoyed my ramblings as an EQ wizard. Look for me on the Rathe. Daneric, Knights of Phoenix.  Ill let you know what life is like as a level 60 Sorcerer hopefully by summers end.  Post 50 levels truly are hellish.

 top
