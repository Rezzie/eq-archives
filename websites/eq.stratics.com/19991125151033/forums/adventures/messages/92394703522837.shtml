I'm not exactly sure what you mean by "run" faster.If by running faster you mean less laggy , yes EQ runs faster but it's just like anything else , it depends on when ya get on and play. During peak hours ( usually primetime and weekends)it can get quite a bit laggy.
as for your other question about which is easier for a newbie to get started in I would have to say depends on the person.
You have to look at it like this.......In UO your character can spend his or her whole life never going out and questing or adventuring.Your more self sufficient in that game.And that was the way it was designed to be.More like a world simulation rather than just a world to adventure in.
EQ you can't do that.EQ was designed so that at some point you must rely on others to survive.It promotes a more you scratrch my back and I'll scratch yours approach.And in EQ it really doesnt pay to be a tradesman even though it is helpful to yourself and the party you travel with.
Each promotes such a different style of gameplay its hard to say which would be easier to start with.
Whew! i know that was long winded.But I hope I helped.
