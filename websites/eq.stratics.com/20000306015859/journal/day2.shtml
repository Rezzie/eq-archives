4:17 AM
There have been some questions concerning the horrible ping times displayed in the Journal's screenshots (the little green bar and the numbers above it represent the quality of a player's connection to the server)... I'd like to clear this up right now: the ISP that I'm using to connect to EverQuest is horrible. High ping times are normal for most servers when using this ISP, so the bad ping times displayed in my screen captures are strictly a result of the connection that I'm using. Even with this bad connection, I've been able to enjoy a smooth playing experience while in EQ. Although there are some laggy periods in crowded areas when the server is particularly full, I experience no lock-ups and am still able to play normally. The game progresses with a smooth frame rate during these periods, and the only really noticeable side effect is a major delay between typing and seeing your text displayed. On average, I see a ping time of anywhere from 450 to 1000 ms and am still able to enjoy myself.

8:24 AM
Man, it looks like I'm not going to have the stamina (EQ terms sneaking into real life, oh no) to stay up until 11:00 AM like I did yesterday... I've been working on updates for the EQSS since my last entry, and my mind is starting to become fuzzy with thoughts of sleep. I have to admit that I did sneak in an hour or so of playing EQ for fun during this period. :) I meant only to research items, I swear! But those critters were calling to me, "kill me... gain experience..."--you know how it goes.

So, I ventured out to the fields outside of Qeynos and began wracking up those items. In the process, I noticed some rather nifty monster AI... Until tonight, I'd encountered for the most part only solitary creatures who, up until the end, seemed to prefer loneliness over the company of their peers. However, this morning I noticed that Gnoll pups were beginning to run away from me in the middle of combat (that's just how tough Talonmarc is :). That's not the cool part, though. I soon discovered that the pups were attempting to run towards other members of their race in order to shift the advantage of battle by ganging up on me. At one point I pursued a Gnoll pup across the field only to run smack into two more of them, at which point all 3 teamed up against me. (Then I killed 'em. :)

I'm now halfway form level 2 to level 3, and am accruing quite a nice little pile of silver pieces... too bad everything seems to cost gold. :( I also found a belt pouch on one of the Gnoll pups that I slew, which made me pretty happy. EverQuest uses a system for holding items in your inventory similar to Diablo, only a tad more complex (tiles, each of which hold one item, or more, if stackable). After finding a small box a little later and combining its storage space with my belt pouch (which, oddly enough, couldn't be equipped on my body), Talonmarc's storage capacity has been increased dramatically. This means fewer trips back to town, and more time fer killin' them thar monsters.

All in all, it wasn't a bad hour of play. My only disappointment was as a result of waiting until nighttime for the Skeletons to come out, only to find that none of them had been carrying what I was looking for: a rusty scimitar. I wanted to evaluate the weapon's stats for verification purposes, but wound up having to rely on other rusty weapons that I had found in NPC shops instead. I did get a couple of rusty daggers from the bony corpses, but they only fetched me a few silvers apiece. Oh well, after reaching level 3, I'll just go out and hunt down a Wisp--I've heard those give good loot (ahh, I love those psychotropic delusions of grandeur).

Until I wake up this afternoon...

7:29 AM
I'm severely bummed. I spent eleven hours straight playing EQ (and boy is my ass sore) over yesterday evening and this morning, taking countless screenshots. To my chagrin, however, I just discovered that my screenshot program had glitched up while I was playing and not auto-saved my screenshots. That meant that every new screenshot taken overwrote the last, so I lost all but one of the 100+ screenshots I'd taken. Eleven hours worth of screens, from the entire western half of the main continent of Norrath, all down the drain. sigh Elephants, Minotaurs, Cyclops, Sharks, NPC Necromancers, sewers, rolling hills, stormy valleys, stone idols, hermits, mountains, keeps, and so on, and so on, and so on--all lost to evil, twisted fate.

On the bright side of things, I've officially decided that EQ is going to be one of my all-time faves after final release. Hopefully they'll fill in all of the missing features that I'd like to see by then... for example, one of my smaller disappointments is the fact that not all of the armor and clothes worn over your non-removable "newbie skin" shows on your 3D model (I want other players to see that leather mask, dammit :). All of this and more should be added to the game by final, and that fact makes me drool. The game as it is takes a little time to get into, but once you discover how vast this thing is, you start to appreciate how mind-blowing EQ will be once everything is finished and polished. Regardless, this game is gradually starting to knock my socks off in a major way even during Beta (and I'm sure it would have immediately if I'd had a Voodoo enhanced video card, which, by the way, this game has inspired me to buy).

I enjoyed countless adventures during this last playing session, and joined up with multiple and varied parties. Talonmarc's now on his way to level 4, and has some new equipment and gold under his belt. Alas, I'm too depressed to describe my eleven consecutive hours of playing EverQuest right now, so I'll post the entire tale when I wake up.
