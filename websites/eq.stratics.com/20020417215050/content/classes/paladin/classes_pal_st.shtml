Playing a Paladin

 Introduction   Pros and Cons   Starting Out   Spellcasting   In a Group   Conclusion 

Introduction
When we think of Paladins, we think of Holy Warriors, of mighty fighters, sworn to use their whole lives to serve the sacred ideal of their God. We think of these blessed heroes who fight to protect the weak and the innocent, these fearless soldiers of Light. In EverQuest however, not every Paladin has chosen to live only for these Sacred Ideals. But no matter if you choose to be the Holier of the Holiest or if you decided to role-play your character in a more conservative manner, this paladin overview will let you understand better the way of the sacred knights. Understanding your strengths and weaknesses is an important step in mastering your character and knowing how to play it optimally, and playing your character optimally is a small part of the greater objective you seek to achieve: having fun while fighting and role-playing your way through the lands of Norrath.

The Benefits and Disadvantages
Please sit down friends, grab ale, and listen. The noble path of the paladins is indeed a rewarding one. There are some hardships to be met along the way, but the rewards are great. First, let me explain to you what our strengths and weaknesses are. First of all, the paladin is a fighter before all, and his sword is often his best bet to overcome his enemies. But after level 9, he is also endowed with clerical magic, which can often be a life saviour for him. Like if it wasn't enough, the paladin is blessed with the ability to heal by laying hands once an EverQuest day (90 minutes in real time), and we get this power in the beginning of the game, at level one. This ability is a great one for sure. It allows the paladin to heal an enormous amount of hit points and can be used either on himself or on a friend in need. It can often save one's life, especially when the healers are low on mana and when there is no other way to refill your health bar. But of course, it should be used carefully since you must wait quite some time before using this precious skill again. Our ability to heal and use white magic makes us a lot more versatile and self-sufficient than regular fighters, not only when soloing, but also in groups, where we can save the day when the 'regular' healers are low on mana. And finally, we also have a last big advantage that can make us mightier than most warriors even without using our magic: our artefacts. Paladins can use some mighty magical weapons that are restricted to the paladin class only: the Holy Swords. These are undoubtedly the most powerful weapons in Norrath. The mighty Fiery Avenger is an example of these weapons. However do not expect to find of these easily, as only the mightiest and the purest of the Holy Knights manage to get one, someday. They are one of our long term advantages.

But of course, there is also downsides to our class. We get a small but noticeable experience penalty, which makes it about 20% longer for us to gain levels. Human Paladins can partly compensate this with the human ability to gain levels faster, but the other races may notice that paladins are harder to level than other non-hybrid classes. And of course, we do not possess all the abilities of warriors and clerics. Let me explain: in pure melee, which doesn't involve any magic, we aren't as good as warriors on the battlefield. They have about 20-25% more hit points (don't get me wrong, we still have a lot) and they get double attack sooner than we do (level 15 instead of level 20). They also have dual wield and kick; two battle skills that we do not have access to. Dual Wield allows warriors to use 2 weapons simultaneously, and kick gives them the opportunity to kick their enemies to inflict additional damage. Although very useful, these abilities aren't vital, and we can still fare very well without them. And finally, we do not have access to the archery skill, while others do. But don't get discouraged yet, we are still very powerful in close combat, and except the warriors and the monks, very few can match our melee skill. We still have others very important advantages. We can use all weapons (except bows) and all armours available in Norrath. Also, as soon as we reach level 9, we gain several clerical spells that can be life saviour more than once. And since we have access to our special ability to lay hands at the very beginning, we don't have to worry about staying alive as much as a warrior does. And, as I said before, we have the ability to use the Holy Swords. Their superior power gives to the few paladins that get them the possibility to inflict much more damage to their opponents than a warrior of the same level would do, even if he is using his dual wield ability. Not only that, but these swords are enhanced with great abilities like randomly casting expulse undead when using them against undead opponents. It's of course a simple example and any relation to the actual powers of an existing weapon is a pure coincidence or is it? :)

I have heard too often that Paladins were warriors wanna-be or weak fighters with ridiculously weak magic. For some time, I even believed these foolish rumours. But one day I understood the truth: we are generally stronger than warriors are. Why? Because of the complementarily of our magic. The powers our God grants us focus in three domains. Healing, buffing and anti-undead. The first category allows us to remain alive in battles were warriors would often die or have to wait a long time to regenerate their life after the battle. This gives us one advantage: versatility. When warriors need a Cleric to be efficient, we can solo pretty well without any danger of dying from disease or poison. Our healing skills reduce greatly our 'downtimes' as we can heal ourselves. Not only during battle to survive, but also after battle to be ready to fight again immediately, when a twenty minute break is often needed for the soloing high level warrior. In parties, we can also act as emergency healers when the healers are out of mana, this is one of the big advantages of our class. Our second category, the buffing spells are the ones that destroy this 'weak warriors with weak magic' myth. They allow paladins to increase their strength, armour class and hit points on the battlefield. They make a critical difference on the battlefield, as they allow you to take on significantly harder monsters more safely. A paladin using a spell such as 'center' can easily be more powerful in melee than a warrior of the same level who doesn't. And finally, the 'undead' category allows us to take on undead monsters of levels that would make a regular warrior of the same level to flee in terror, and make us very useful in undead dungeons. So here is the point: the magic of a paladin is the perfect complement of its fighting abilities. So its not true to say that paladins are weaker later on because they can't specialise or that their spells are useless. They make a big difference in battle and increase the paladin's power greatly. At higher levels, the paladins gets stronger comparatively to the other classes, and the rumours saying the opposite are false. In higher levels, we get powerful magic, which, although weaker than the magic of a Cleric of the same level, make us a lot more efficient, especially if we consider our always useful lay hands skill. Of course, we shouldn't forget that in these high levels, they could get these Holy Swords I've been talking about

Starting as a Paladin
Now that you know what makes us strong and efficient, let's see what await us in our travels. When creating your paladin, you'll have to choose between many interesting races. They are well balanced and all the possibilities allow you to create a good paladin. I advise to choose your race according to your role-playing interests instead of their statistics, but there are still things that are important to consider. First, night vision is very useful, even if it's not essential. Small races get an AC bonus that can be very useful. Once you'll have decided what you prefer, you'll have 20 points to assign to your various statistics. Strength, Stamina, Wisdom and Charisma are your primary statistics. Strength and Wisdom, in my opinion are the most important ones. Stamina also plays an important role, since you need high hit points to be able to melee effectively. However, always remember that other statistics also play a role. Intelligence makes you learn skills faster. Agility and dexterity can affect significantly your efficiency in battle. Once you'll have distributed your points in the way that you see fit; your adventure will begin. You start with the regular newbie equipment. As always, find your guildhall and give your guildnote to your master, train your first melee weapon and you are ready to begin. At this point your struggle for the 9th level begins, the level where you leave your relative weakness behind and finally fully access the great status of paladinhood.

I'm not here to tell you what to do step by step in your journeys, but I'll give you a few pointers to help you to advance safely and quickly. The golden rule is to take your time, as patience is a virtue. When travelling, you must be careful to do not attack stronger monsters. Using the /con command will tell you who you can fight. You must be careful, especially when you are alone, as monsters are strong, fast, and they can gang up on you easily. The smarter monsters will often group quickly, try to surround you or even call for reinforcements. Always remember that bravery isn't always the best choice: running back to the guards when overwhelmed can often save your life. So, should you ever be outmatched or in a bad posture, try to run to the nearest town or guard tower to get help from the mighty soldiers. Another important thing is to make reliable friends. While in Norrath, be a nice guy, since a jerks have a hard time making any friends, and friends are the most precious weapon and armor at the same time. When you begin in Norrath, try to behave like a real paladin. Begging for weaponry and gold isn't a good idea. Others found their precious gear by themselves, and you should try to do so as well. And you probably know there is nothing more annoying than a newbie broadcasting that he needs help, and it's certainly not the best way to build your reputation. If you really need help, asking in a role-playing manner is almost always the best choice to obtain a quick and helpful answer. After all everyone prefers an 'Excuse me, kind sir, I am lost in this magnificent city and I would appreciate if you could tell me where I can find to Guild of the Paladins, as I have to go there imperatively' than a 'Hey, man, I'm a newbeez and I want you to tell em where I can find my !@#$%?&*! Guild Hall!' And after all, EverQuest is a role playing game. Also, when you are soloing, don't steal kills, help whoever you can, and let be assured that it'll pay back someday. And when you group with other people, don't hesitate to take risks to help them when needed. You are a part of the team, and it's your duty to protect your companions at all costs, as they should protect you. Remember who is reliable and who isn't, and the next time you'll know who you can trust and who you can't.

Speaking of grouping, around level 3 or 4, you should really think of grouping to increase your experience faster. Soloing is possible, but grouping is so much more effective, especially before you get your healing powers. In general, remember to take your time and you'll advance little by little. If you are in trouble, remember that you can use lay hands, it'll surely save your life more than once. Setting it as a macro for critical situations is also a good idea. Should you die, do not panic, as long as you can get back to your corpse, it'll be okay. But be sure to not die in places where you cannot retrieve it, or you could lose a lot, and I mean it. Should you die in an enemy camp or another very dangerous place, make sure to gather a few friends quickly to retrieve your belongings before they decay and get lost forever. And remember that if you die before level 5, it's not very important since you don't lose any experience and restart with supplies. After that point, however, you incur more important penalties. Finally, always be careful when planning your budget, money is very important in Norrath. Especially for a paladin as your money situation doesn't shine a lot, since you have to afford weapons, armor and also spells. Even if they doesn't cost much initially, you could have a few problems with more advanced ones if you don't plan ahead to get some money in advance. If you need money, here are a few tips. Never waste a single copper, bank every penny that you can spare. Instead of paying for items, try to find them while adventuring. Sometimes it's not possible, but I already paid quite a lot for a chainmail and found an enchanted one that was much better only a few minutes after, so plan your important expenses in advance too. If you really need money in early levels, fishing may help you a lot. You can get some fish to sell for quite a nice amount of money, and if you are lucky, you may even find some rusty weapons or such in the water. Fishing is easy to build up and after all, a skill point gained anywhere is gained forever. Well, if you follow these small pieces of advice, I guarantee that you will progress smoothly and that soon you'll be reach the 9th level and receive your first spells. For a young paladin, patience is everything.

A New Beginning
Now, you did it, you have your magic, and a new life begins for you. You are now a real paladin. Since it's not trained, your magic will indeed be weak at first, but don't worry, spare a few points in early levels to train your magic and then use it a lot, soon you'll find that your powers are much more useful than you could have hoped for. And now the world lies before you. But don't get excited too fast, young one, as a little magic doesn't make you invincible. And remember to watch your mana gauge. Always save at least 30-40% of it in case of a very important emergency. At level 15 and 22 you will receive new spells and they will help you a lot more. By then you will slowly acquire enough money to buy chainmail armor. And for you powergamers out there, don't get discouraged at level 15 to see your warriors friends with dual wield and double attack, you'll get double attack yourself at level 20 and you will laugh at their double attack once you'll hold a Holy Sword in your hands. And these are only a few of the great prospects that lie before you. By the time you reach level 22 and gain access to the 3rd circle of spells, you'll have developed your magical skills and you'll be a great fighter who'll know how to dodge, parry, riposte, taunt, bash and double attack. These are the battle skills that you gain access to, and don't worry, you can use them quite efficiently. To be able to use double attack effectively, however, I recommend that you save about 20 to 30 practices and throw them on that skill as you gain it at level 20. At that point, teamwork becomes essential. You really cannot advance in high levels in EverQuest all by yourself. And even at level 3 or 4, teamwork often pays much more than soloing. So here are a few pointers about the role of a paladin in a group. Before I begin, note that teamwork is generally considered as the most important aspect of EverQuest, no matter what.

The Paladin in a Group
In a group, the Paladin plays mainly the role of a warrior. He is a tank that must protect the weaker spellcasters by taunting the monsters and taking the majority of the blows and inflicting damage upon them. But, of course, you are more than a warrior is. First of all, never forget to use your magic to boost yourself before an important battle, spells like courage and center makes a big difference. If the healers of your party are low on mana or if there isn't any healer with you, use your powers sparingly, as your mana reserve isn't unlimited. Heal only when it is very necessary, and remember those handy Lay Hands if you got nothing else to protect your friends and yourself. Often, you will be the difference between life and death for your party. But knowing how to fight isn't all you have to do, remember that there is other people fighting with you. Know how they operate and work with them. Taunting is also very important; it's used to increase the level of hate of your enemies toward you. The point is to prevent the enemies from attacking the weaker spellcasters too much and instead concentrating their power on you. You'll learn quickly that the best way to win a battle is to protect the casters efficiently. After all, if there is a healer keeping the tanks alive and a wizard destroying the monsters that try to flee or cause you too much trouble, how could you lose? As you spent time exploring Norrath with your friends, you'll undoubtedly get to create your own battle strategies, the only thing that is important is to remember to know how your friends operate and support them as much as possible. Finally, remember that you aren't in direct competition with the other members of your party to do the biggest amount of damage to the enemy; you all receive the same share of experience no matter who does the more damage to the enemy (Well as long as you are of the same level or a close level). Following the directives of the group leader is always the best way to optimise the gain and the fun your party can have. And if you are the leader then be sure to lead well, know where you are going and be fair with everyone. That is how you'll forge a successful group to destroy the forces of evil that plague the lands. Grouping efficiently is an important skill to master, and it's one of the most important ones in EverQuest.

Conclusion
Now that you have a better understanding of the paladin class (well, at least, I hope so), I'd like to remind you that fighting isn't everything in a role-playing game. There is a reason why EverQuest is group oriented: it is to allow you to have fun and share and explore the lands with your friends. Having a good role-playing experience can be as much fun for you and your friends as slaying the strongest monsters of the land. In fact it can be much more fun, even if you don't consider yourself as a role-player, you should give it a try someday, and you may get to like it. As you noticed, this overview was mainly based on technical details, since it's purpose was to explain who are the paladins, what they can do and what they can't do. My advice may help you, but remember that you alone will never be able to slay a dragon. Making reliable friends is, I will never say it too much, more important than any sword you could ever find. Now have fun my friends, and may my blessings go with you on your journeys.

May the Holy Light protects you!
