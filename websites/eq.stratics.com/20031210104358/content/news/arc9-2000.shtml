Lady Daegarmo on Cheaters in the Test of Tactics
GeneralNews - 1:19 PM PST
I found this here on the Official Everquest Forums

Lady Daegarmo entered The Arena, to be greeted by hundreds of smiling faces, all of them anxious, yet nervous to see what glory and honor the day would bring them. Leaders of great armies approached and introduced themselves with an incredible energy that words simply could not explain.

These leaders talked amongst each other, making an incredible social event where discussions were filled of great feats, of dragons slain, of immortals killed, of dungeons plundered, and even of the best brews that were made in the lands. It was a festive moment that you could not help but to feel apart of.

Morale was its highest, as everyone strutted around, happy simply making it to the finals, platemail boots barely leaving footprints in the soft sand.

Lady Daegarmo's smile could not be mistaken as she knew that all of her teams effort to make this tournament a story of legends was unfolding. As she turned a corner, she suddenly stopped smiling, as she saw a team in distress.

She ran up to the group, and smiles were not seen on the faces of these champions. Lady Daegarmo asked "What is wrong? Do you not have your gear? Is your team missing members? What has caused you to not enjoy the festivities that are in front of you?"

With heads hung low, and spirits crushed, the party answered. "We beg your forgiveness, for we have failed you. You see, our team cheated to win our regional event, to earn our right to be here with the true champions, and we are so heartbroken by our actions that we are unsure if we wish to compete with those who earned their right to compete."

"Our homeland has found out of our dishonorable deeds, and does not bless our presence here. We do not have the hopes and dreams of our people behind us. We do not have their prayers and their wishes to give us strength to defeat our foes. We ask for forgiveness and wonder if we should still enter the event."

This was not a request to enter the event, without question it was a plea to dismiss the group so that they would not need to make the hardest decision they would ever have to make, the decision they knew in their hearts was right.

Lady Daegarmo paused and thought for a moment. The team was defeated, a lost cause before they entered the match. With as high of spirits and hopes that the other teams had, no amount of skill or courage could make up for the loss of morale by this team. As a Knight herself, the Lady pondered what punishment she would give those involved.

Disqualifying the team was an option, but by those who truly understood justice, knew that such a decision would be considered merciful, for it ended the pain and suffering of having to
bring themselves to resignation.

Lady Daegarmo spoke "You have admitted to me freely the crimes you have done against your homeland. Send this message, that I give you the right to enter this event. Spread the word so that all of your countrymen know that you can freely make this decision to enter this event, or resign if your hearts so tell you that is the path you wish to follow!"

There was no relief on the eyes of this team, for the burden was not lifted from them. The weight so strong that they could not breathe without effort.

Daegarmo left and attended her duties as the primary judge of the event, knowing the decision was already made. Many great teams came forward, showing outstanding skill and spirit, refusing to let themselves be broken. This team quietly watched the matches, without the enthusiasm of the others. The bouts held not the fascination to them as it did the others, for they knew their turn would never arrive.

Lady Daegarmo instructed Lord Akkirus to take the next match, knowing what lied ahead. A team full of energy and spirit ran to its side, and immediately formed the requested starting positions and formations. Their faces glowed, their armor shined, and yet Daegarmo did not move. The shattered team then approached, and faced Akkirus. Words were said that Daegarmo could not hear, but she could quote them if asked.

Akkirus was unsure of what to do, and turned around to face the elder of the Knights.

"My Lady, the second team wishes to resign, they state you know the reasons behind the decision, what should we do? All lands must be championed!"

Lady Daegarmo sighed, knowing the ultimate justice had just been administered. "They may retire in honor, and I accept their resignations. The battle they just engaged in was far worse then any team here could assault upon them. They have been redeemed in my eyes, for they have done what is right. State that they have resigned, do not give a reason why to the other teams, simply let them leave in peace."

Akkirus nodded and delivered the message as intended.

Later that night, several hours past the famed event, the Ruler of the Lands of the only team that resigned that day approached an exhausted Lady Daegarmo. He knocked and entered to seek advice on how to further handle the situation.

"Should I take their titles? Their prizes? Their status in our lands? What shall you have us do in the name of justice?"

Lady Daegarmo remembered to a time long ago when she did wrong, and had to learn from a mistake she made. She remembered the rule she broke, as vividly as the day it happened.

She could never forget the distrust that was placed against her, and the path to redemption of her honor. The armor she wore during that time didn't shine, her sword felt unjustified for the longest time regardless of the great deeds she accomplished with it. Her shield was not worn with the same pride until her redemption was completed.

"The greatest of titles, the most glorious of prizes, the highest symbols of status cannot hide that which we know is truth, and can not erase the pain that we do to others."

"The titles and rewards hold not the value to those involved that they do to those who truely earned them, and I assure you that many will hide them, or destroy them because these things will remind them of the victories they could of accomplished."

"My request is to leave them in place, for each member of that team will redeem themselves someday, and hold those items not of the deeds that they did to accomplish them, but as reminders of the day those things lost their value in the hearts of those that wield them"

Lady Daegarmo fell asleep before her guest departed, too tired from the days events to stay awake. The elder of most elves knew in her heart, she already forgave them, and that the punishment the group would face in their own lands was worse then any should could ever of administered.

Lady Daegarmo

Correct use of /pause
GeneralNews - 6:04 AM PDT
I found this here on the Official Everquest Forums:

I've seen this somewhere before, but how do I put more that one command on a single line when I'm editing a social key??

Thanks

You can only put two commands on one line when one of the commands is "/pause". The syntax to do so is:
/pause #, /command

The pause command must come first followed by a comma to separate it from the next command. The pause is, however, executed after the command. It's a little kludgy, but it works.

Absor On The Guild Maintainence
GeneralNews - 1:38 PM PDT
I found this here on the Official Everquest Forums

Consider this section from the FAQ:
10.11 Will there be a limit to the number of official guilds on the game?

There is no practical limit, no.

Yikes.

Unfortunately, the FAQ is wrong. I'll see what I can do about changing it. But understand that the 'practical' limit is pretty sizable. And that limit is on a server by server level, not global.

I had always assumed that the guild database was filling up on some servers, and was making the guild structure unstable, or obsolete.

The problem is not that the database is filling up or becoming unstable. The problem here lies in the fact that guild data, like lots of other data, needs to be sent to the client at certain times, accessed by the server software, searched, written to... it's data that needs to be used. The more data, the more processing. It's not dissimilar to bank slots and other data storage. 'Stuff' slows things down. This is some of the stuff that we need to trim.

I hope 'stuff' is the proper technical term.

Let me take a shot at explaining why we restrict guilds to ten people. If you understand that too many guilds is a bad thing, then we can proceed from there.

Six people, using the /groupsay and friends list features will have all the functionality of a guild without a guild tag over their heads. The guild tag is not enough reason on its own to allow such a low number of members as six or less. Besides, we want guilds to mean something more than just a name over a players head.

Eight is better, but it's not really that much bigger than six. Skip nine for the same reason we skipped seven, even numbers look better . Ten is a good number, nice and round. But twelve is twice the size of a group. That's just as good as ten. Fourteen is too many. Someone flip a coin, heads its ten, tails its twelve...

There is no intent to favor 'uber' guilds. An 'uber' guild has a lot more than ten people. Raising the number to twenty or thirty could be considered favoring large guilds. Ten is the product of necessity and practicality.

For now, the current set-up will have to remain as it is. However, we are looking at alternatives. We most certainly will not be making any further changes to the guild registration process until after Velious, it's just too hectic in here for that. I'm sorry if this causes a problem for guilds with membership numbers close to or just under the limit, but for now we need to do it this way.

Alan

    Two New Patch Messages
    GeneralNews - 11:52 PM PDT
    October 13th, 7:01pm
    ------------------------------

    *Important System Configuration Announcement*. As many of you know, over the past couple of months we've made numerous enhancements to the EverQuest client. In addition to things such as new /-commands and other changes of that nature, we've enhanced the UI itself to allow moveable and resizable windows, among other things. At the same time we've been working on a brand new sound engine, and have been further concentrating on optimizing and expanding the capabilities of the EverQuest client.

    For most applications, the feature set of a version of DirectX is limited to the lowest common denominator between the version that the software is compiled under, and the version running on the client. This means that though you may be running DirectX 7.0a already, and are receiving advantages associated with the more advanced code, the feature-set of DirectX used when in EverQuest is limited to version 5.2.

    This limits the type and breadth of enhancements that can be made to the client. Given that we feel that EverQuest players would enjoy continuing to see new enhancements for the client, we will soon begin compiling the client under DirectX 7.0a, and implementing the feature-set available within that version. When we do this, everyone will need DirectX 7.0a (or higher) to play EverQuest.

    Here are some of the possible benefits from DirectX 7.0a:

    - Better performance (Graphics and Sound)

    - Gamma Correction working in D3D
    - Proper Mip-Mapping in D3D
    - Fixes fogging on some cards. (Some cards have no fogging at all and look pretty bad)
    - Allows us to use newer interfaces to support such features as full-screen anti-aliasing, and alternate Z-buffering methods, which tend to look better.

    Changing to DX7 does have a potential downside. Those using older or discontinued video or sound cards that do not have DX7 compatible drivers may need to upgrade to continue playing. However, we feel that the benefits that will come from upgrading to this newer version of DirectX outweigh the potential problems.

    We plan to begin requiring DirectX7.0a when the EverQuest client is next patched, in two or three weeks. We wanted to be sure to give everyone enough time to check their version of DirectX and install the newest version. You can run DXDIAG.EXE in your Windows\System directory to check your DirectX version and get the newest version of DirectX from here if it is needed.

    Please keep an eye on the patch message for further information regarding the planned implementation of DX7.

    - The EverQuest Team

    GeneralNews - 6:20 PM PDT

    I've seen the outcry regarding the banning of Mystere. While we typically do not discuss this, I would like to elaborate on several things surrounding this in order to address legitimate concerns raised by our playerbase.

    It is very important for everyone concerned to understand that EverQuest's reputation is a very important thing. In this day and age, video games are subjected to an ENORMOUS amount of scrutiny by watchdog groups and the federal and state governments.

    In this case, a parent complained to a lot of anti-child porn watchdog sites and several mainstream media outlets regarding the extremely graphic detail of the story and the fact that it apparently contained violence directed at a child.

    For us, this poses an extremely serious problem. EverQuest has a reputation as being a family oriented game. Yes it has a mature rating, and yes it does contain violence. That in and of itself gives pause to many people out there (especially parents). But, you would be amazed at how many parents play this game with their kids mostly because we do control the language and they feel like EQ is a family friendly environment despite the dark overtones of some of the stories and quests.

    You may say "a person has the right to say whatever they want"... and of course that's true. But, when that action is linked to a company's Intellectual Property (which is just a fancy way of saying people associate it with EQ) it becomes our business because it threatens our business by making us a huge target for all the folks out there that think we all are all a bunch of violent video game players. This just gives them fuel for the fire.

    For us, this story represented a serious problem because it used EverQuest as a backdrop. It's already been pointed out by our General Counsel in more legal terms, but the simple fact is that it's easy for folks to point at that story, point at EQ and link the two.

    Whether or not any of us likes that or not isn't really the question. The fact is that EverQuest is the very heart of our business and we have to take that very seriously.

    Now - Will we be policing the Internet looking for these kinds of stories?

    No. We won't. In fact, none of us was even aware of this until it was brought to our attention. That doesn't mean if someone crosses the line again and it's pointed out we won't do the same thing.

    The big question is - where's the line? And what right do we have to draw it outside the game?

    The answer is complicated and extremely subjective, so I'll just have to be honest and say we'll know it when it's over the line. We're going to discuss it in the upcoming few weeks and see if we can make it more clear, but I can't honestly say if we'll get anywhere because none of us wants to stop people from writing awesome fan-fiction about EQ.

    But we aren't going to be looking at every fan site and becoming the Thought Police. We have neither the time, nor the inclination to do that. However we need to protect EverQuest's good image as best we can.

    Did we handle this as well as we could have?

    No - We didn't. And for that, I apologize.

    In the future, we're going to handle this in a different way.

    Regards,

    John Smedley
    President and CEO
    Verant Interactive, Inc.

    EverQuest to upgrade to DirectX 7.0a
    GeneralNews - 6:12 AM PDT
    I found this here on the Official Everquest Forums

    Hello all,
    As many of you know, over the past couple of months we’ve made numerous enhancements to the EverQuest client. In addition to things such as new /-commands and other changes of that nature, we’ve enhanced the UI itself to allow moveable and resizable windows, among other things. At the same time we’ve been working on a brand new sound engine, and have been further concentrating on optimizing and expanding the capabilities of the EverQuest client.

    For most applications, the feature set of a version of DirectX is limited to the lowest common denominator between the version that the software is compiled under, and the version running on the client. This means that though you may be running DirectX 7.0a already, and are receiving advantages associated with the more advanced code, the feature-set of DirectX used when in EverQuest is limited to version 5.2.

    This limits the type and breadth of enhancements that can be made to the client. Given that we feel that EverQuest players would enjoy continuing to see new enhancements for the client, we will soon begin compiling the client under DirectX 7.0a, and implementing the feature-set available within that version. When we do this, everyone will need DirectX 7.0a (or higher) to play EverQuest.

    Here are some of the possible benefits from DirectX 7.0a:

    - Better performance (Graphics and Sound)
    - Gamma Correction working in D3D
    - Proper Mip-Mapping in D3D
    - Fixes fogging on some cards. (Some cards have no fogging at all and look pretty bad)
    - Allows us to use newer interfaces to support such features as full-screen anti-aliasing, and alternate Z-buffering methods, which tend to look better.

    Changing to DX7 does have a potential downside. Those using older or discontinued video or sound cards that do not have DX7 compatible drivers may need to upgrade to continue playing. However, we feel that the benefits that will come from upgrading to this newer version of DirectX outweigh the potential problems.

    We plan to begin requiring DirectX7.0a when the EverQuest client is next patched, in two or three weeks. We wanted to be sure to give everyone enough time to check their version of DirectX and install the newest version. You can run DXDIAG.EXE in your Windows\System directory to check your DirectX version and get the newest version of DirectX from http://www.microsoft.com/directx if it is needed.

    Please keep an eye on the patch message for further information regarding the planned implementation of DX7.

    ...which I've just found out is incorrect. The fact that players were allowed to use their bow directly after casting a spell was a bug that was fixed in that patch. The programmers tell me that it was intended for bows to become unusable for their full delay following the casting of a spell. In case anyone is wondering, the post-casting delay has always applied to the primary and secondary melee weapons. In other words, if someone using a 50-delay weapon casted, they wouldn't again try to hit with their melee weapon until 5 seconds after the casting of the spell. It turned out that this delay wasn't being correctly applied to the ranged slot as well.

    Now there is a problem regarding the way that the delay is handled. For instance, if you are wielding a 40-delay bow, and you were to try to use it 3 seconds after casting a spell, you'd have to wait an additional 4 seconds before you could use it again. This we will correct in a future patch by making the ranged button gray out during the initial delay.

    There is an additional problem regarding excessive misses in archery that went hand in hand with the skill being moved to the server side back when the change above went in. This is on the list to be fixed as well.
