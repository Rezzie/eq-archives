You're completely missing the point. Answer these questions:
1) How does it negatively affect your in-game experience of playing EQ if there are some people out there who know their way around because they explored the world and some who know their way around because they looked at maps?
2) Can you even tell the difference between these two types of players?
3) You're travelling with someone looking for a particular place and they say, "I know how to get there." Maybe they know because they travelled there before. Maybe they know because they looked at a map. What difference does it make to you how they gained that knowledge?

You have no right to complain about people's actions unless they negatively affect others, such as yourself. When you hit /who in a zone, or look at a group of adventurers fighting monsters together, or try to explore a new zone for the first time with a friend, you there is no way to know who among those people looked at maps. Your playing experience would be the same either way. It seems like you're more worried about others getting what you perceive to be an unfair advantage, than about really thinking about how this affects you.
