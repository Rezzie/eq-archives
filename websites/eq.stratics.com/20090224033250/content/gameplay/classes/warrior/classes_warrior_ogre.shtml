Ogre Overview
 

The Warrior is the backbone of any roll playing game. Warriors are strong, we have the most hit points, we can wear the best armor, we can double attack/dual wield for some serious damage, and we can disarm our opponents. No group is complete without a tank and the best tanks are warriors. We have no spells, we have no pets, and we don't need them. We need a good healer and we are good to go with no downtime between battles. If you like to run to the battle, take all the hits, and smash your opponent until he runs like a baby then the warrior is the class for you.

What race should you choose?

There are several races to pick from, but I chose the Ogre race. They are the strongest, they do the most weapon damage, and they have the most hit points by virtue of having the highest Stamina. They can Slam, which is the same as Bash but it does require a shield. They can see in the dark and they are fun to role-play. Role-playing an ogre is easy because they have the lowest intelligence in the game.

Which attributes should you increase when you create a warrior?

Some people spread their points around according to the items they intend to  wear, but I dumped all of my points into strength. It was an easy choice and I
don't regret it. Later in life, with items, I have over 250 strength and over 100 in all other stats except wisdom and charisma. A strong warrior is a real
powerhouse! The extra strength also helped me to quickly dispatch foes at early levels.

What hotkeys do I make?

The first hotkey I created was for sense heading. I put left turn and right turn on this hotkey so I would hit sense heading every time I made a turn. With the low intelligence of an ogre you need to hit sense heading quite often in order to gain skill points here. I also set up an early hotkey for taunt, kick, and Incoming. You will need Taunt in later life but you need to build your skill points in it as quick as possible. Spam it as often as it becomes available in early levels. Taunting is one of your primary skills as a tank in a group. You need this skill to get the bad guys off of your weak little caster friends. When a scrawny little wizard casts a big magic spell, it enrages the beast. The beast goes after him with a vengence. Thus, you have to run over and taunt the beast so that it will hopefully attack you rather than killing your weak little pajama wearing wizard buddy. If you have enough skill in Taunt it will get mad at you instead of him and leave the wizard alone. If you don't have enough skill points in Taunt, then you will probably be looking for someone to resurrect your little friend.

It is your job as the warrior to take the hits and allow your group to stay alive. This is what you were made for and it's what you do better than anyone else.

Smart players who group with you will create an "Assist" hotkey. This means they will be hitting the same creature that you are fighting and taunting. This helps keep all the creatures focused and aggroed on you. Hopefully this will prevent creatures from breaking away to attack the guys in your group who wear the house coats and the bath robes.

What do I do from Level 1-6?

Your first task is to read the note in your inventory (right-click on the note to read it) and follow the instructions. You will then receive some experience and your guild tunic, which will serve as your armor until you get better items from corpses. I put 1 point in Taunt, 1 point in kick, and the other 3 in 1hs so I could have better use of my weapon and level quicker. I only put one skill point in each of the other skills as they came available up to level 15. At level 15, I dumped all of my spare skill points into double attack.

The early years of an Ogre warrior's life are spent in the newbie area in the Feerrott. After getting your tunic, find and kill whatever creatures con white or dark blue.

At these levels, I saved all of my bone chips and silk to sell to other players for cash. Everything else at these levels I just sold to the vendors. You will need all the plat you can get to buy weapons and armor.

At level 6 you get two new skills, bash and dodge. You need to go to a warrior trainer and put a skill point in each of these in order for the skill to improve.

What do I do from lvl 6 to 15?

At level 6 you need to continue to hunt Feerot close to the guards, venturing out a little further as you gain courage. At level 8 you should go across the
bridge and turn right. Follow the river to the zone and turn left. You will find a set of three stone structures. Around each of the first two structures you see there will be 3 skeletons each, and sometimes a zombie. They will be tough at first, but keep hacking away at them. If they gang up on you and you die, just run back and start all over. Eventually, you will get the hang of pulling one at a time and you can level here. When you no longer get experience from killing the skellies and zombies, you can head to the lizard men in Feerott or if you have pop, you can easily go to crushbone and level up to level 13 killing orcs or you can go to East and West Commons to kill young kodiaks and other blue mobs.

You need to visit a warrior trainer at levels 10, 13, and 15. At level 10 you get the parry skill which helps you avoid some damage. At level 13 you get dual
wield, and at level 15 you get double attack. When I got double attack, as I said earlier, I dumped all of my spare skill points into it. This was when I finally felt like I was dealing some damage to the bad guys out there.

What do I do from level 15 to 25?

At level 15 I went I went to Paludal Caverns and joined a bandit group. I went from level 15 to level 25 in one day fighting here. This place gives great experience.

At level 25 you need to visit a warrior trainer and put a skill point in Riposte.

What do I do from level 25-30?

You can go to Castle Mistmoore, Lake of Ill Omen, or Highpass Keep. You will be fighting mainly in groups (for me there was no other way) and your group skills will mostly develop with practice.

What do I do from Level 30-35?

You can easily level in Frontier Mountains, the Tenebrous Mountains on Luclin, or Splitpaw. You can fight the burynai, goblins, brutes, and giants in Frontier
Mountains. You can fight the vampires in the Tenebrous Mountains. Again, I strongly suggest that you stick to a group.

At level 35 you need to find a warrior trainer and put all of your spare skill points in disarm.

What do I do at level 35-40?

I went to City of Mist. You will need to be in a group if you expect to get past the entrance. It's vicious, but it's lots of fun and fast experience.

What do do at level 40-50?

You can try Permafrost and Timorous Deep. Somewhere along in here I soloed some Ice Giants outside the entrance to Permafrost and got good cash from them.

What do I do at level 50-60?

At this stage of the game, I go wherever I want to go and I do whatever I want to do.
