I agree that most stats have a HUGE effect on gameplay. Some of the popular stats that you were a little cool on, I will add some more info on.

STRENGTH:
Strength is important for a couple of reasons. First, it allows use of heavier weapons without getting fatigued. Even early (as soon as you stop using rusty/tarnished weapons), there are a lot of heavy weapons out there that will quickly sap stamina.

It also helps you carry more. This is critical since any fighter/cleric type will have most of the strength points used up just for wearing armor. If you plan to carry any loot, this is critical. (I am always bumping into my strength limit)

It has a direct effect on attack value. Get a shaman to cast a +STR spell, and watch how much more efficient you are at hitting creatures. I believe this goes up with level.

I do not believe that strength affects max damage of a weapon. I don't believe it has a major factor in determining where in damage range a weapon does. I think DEX is the primary attribute in play there.

DEX:

I think DEX affects how much damage a weapon does within its possible range. I don't see an increase in attack when my DEX is increased, but I do seem to notice that I hit for more damage. I am not sure if attack speed is affected or not.

It is also rumored to affect how often a weapon special ability is triggered.

My conclusion: This is a very useful stat for combat. I notice a large subjective change when I have a high dexterity.

WISDOM:

I believe that Wisdom has a major effect on skills working correctly. It will certainly affect the chance/duration of enemy spells that affect the mind (anyone else been charmed?).

I have heard a rumor that WIS can give the possibility of a 'second roll' when a skill check is failed. Given my subjective experience, this might be true.

Still, a stat that is mostly of use for clerical casters.
