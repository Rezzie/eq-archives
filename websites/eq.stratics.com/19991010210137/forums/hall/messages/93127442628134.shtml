I've always found it ironic that the kings & queens of Beta always tell us how great something is, then all of us commoners flood the system, and then it all takes a nose dive. "How terrible EQ is now... look at all that is wrong with it. But AC will be better..."

When commoners like us make the world a real place (not some fairy Beta land), disenchantment sets in and all the ugly cracks are shown, people are beasts, and what a cruel cruel world it is. If you don't like it, then play Betas forever... yes, the joke is that this is Phase 5 of the EQ Beta, but now the world is real - full of real people, not a chorus of sycophants who praise a title to the sky.

Somehow, it was all guns and butter in Beta... but come release, the guns and butter extremes of powergaming and roleplaying suddenly reared their heads. "And this and that wasn't fixed, even though I told Mr.Developer about it while we were schmoozing in Beta."

It's also ironic that the beacon in the darkness is published by Microsoft, a company known for watching out for the little guy... and Sierra, publishing Middle Earth Online, hasn't been known for stellar, bug-free games as of late.

Back to Asheron's Call... when player-driven factions in that game are dominated by the Internet gangbangers and K3w1D00Dz, Middle Earth Online should be ready for your Beta pleasures.

Maybe us lunchpail-joe, commoner, low-level, newbiesque EQers are all a bunch of f--- ups who don't know a game from the hole in our bottoms, but that's from your perspective on high. Just because you are Gods & you are the Beta, does not mean you know how games should be.

People drive the MMORPG. Not the rules, not the graphics, not the broken blah blahs, not the class system, not the SOWs & FBRs & PGTs. Yes, EQ is a mixed bag of players. But it's a real thing now, full of real people and all their beautiful flaws. If you aren't brave enough to carve out your niche, then you won't live long in the real thing.

I don't think you'll ever be satisfied by a commercial release of a multiplayer online game if you judge all of us in this fashion. Us commoners don't live in wonderful fairy Beta land.

As for Oggog leaving... people come and go, for whatever reason. Some of us are insignificant, so we will not announce our grand departure from the stage. When new ones come, they will be ignored and perhaps ridiculed for a time until their presence is tolerated.

And when EQ becomes old and grey, we will leave Norrath for greener pastures... nothing stays beautiful forever.

If you're not expecting a bit of disagreement, then rain on someone else's parade.
