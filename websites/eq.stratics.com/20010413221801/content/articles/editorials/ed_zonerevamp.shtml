Editorial: How I would revamp <insert zone here>

 Introduction   The Dread Realm   Conclusion 

Introduction

Well, do you remember that story of St. George and the Dragon? How about when he sat there for hours waiting for the darn mob to spawn and how he was only there for all the awesome loot the overgrown lizard carried (Being the adept camper he is, George valued items more than women. For the most part, the damsel is still there, probably starved, thirsty and in some form of delerium).

How bout that time a group of gallant addventures wandered into a dungeon and sat at the entrance "pulling" all the big nasties because they were nothing but bedwetting cowards. These same adventures walked seven paces into the dungeon, went into a room and stayed there to kill the marshmallow monster (who's loot consisted of 1000 tumpy tonics) for hours... hours I tell you. In fact, they're probably still there.

Moral of this sad story is: Don't camp. It stunts your growth, makes you look like you dont have a life and, above all, is cheap, dull and boring. What will Verant do about it? Not a darned thing. What can you do about it? Complain like theres no tommorow.

Gordon speaks of "Doable" dungeons. This is the false conception I had of Everquest when I first purchased it: that you would actually adventure instead of sitting down on cold wet cobblestones, waiting for the Creature Spawner 2000® to clone another Shin Lord. Well, since no one else has actually gone through a detailed explanation of how this goal of "doable" dungeons should be attained (which is why Verant hasn't done anything. They like detail people, simply saying faster spawn times, more quests, more monsters won't cut it), I present to you for your reading pleasure:

The Dread Realm of the Marshmallow People People People People
 It looks like a gigantic gingerbread house with fun little gummy bears and candy canes strewn about, but once you get inside the "mallow" hits the fan so to speak.

Zone info:
  • Levels: 20+
  • Monsters: all kinds and lots of em
  • Items: All kinds and lots of em, including Gingivits Bane: The Fabled Sword of Cavity Slaying®.
Actually that was going to be a zone, but if you had read that Terms of Service thingy, you would see that your not allowed to make dungeons called "Dread Realm of the Marshmallow People People People People". That being the case, we'll just use good old Crushbone as an example.

1.  First and foremost, we nuke the orc pawns and make the Centurions tougher by 1 level. This way we eliminate a lot of worthless creatures that just take up space.
3.  We create 3 new patrols: One group consisting of 3 Orc Centurions who patrol the entrance, another patrol containing 3 Centurions and a Legionarre will patrol the back and the last patrol of 3 Legionaires and an Emmisary would be in the castle area. This way if anyone did want to camp they wouldnt stay put for too long as one of the patrols would get them sooner or later.
2.  I know #2 comes after #3, but this is my article and so i'll put it where I darn well please. =) Make more spawns. As you all know a dungeon is supposed to have monsters.
4.  Not only do we make the Trainer spawn more often but we make the Shiney Brass Shield a symbol of orc nobility and slap it on the Orc Lords and Emperor Crush. We could also make this a quest in Kaladim where one must collect 6 Crushbone Belts, 6 Legionaire Shoulderpads and 6 Legionaire Bracers. Or maybe rescue a bunch of slaves or some fun stuff like that.
5.  We make the dungeon somewhat progressive, as you get towards the tower, the zone becomes devilishly hard as only legionaires and the like would spawn anywhere near the tower. You could do a bunch of other things but ill leave that up to you.

 top

Conclusion
 To some it up all Dungeon Zones need a major overhaul which means
  • More monsters.
  • Randomized spawns for more items (I'm not talking about making the Major artifacts randomized, but all the other lesser objects should be randomized. This would make the "do the dungeon" concept, well, "doable".)
  • Increased difficulty (Now I'm not talking about a Paw job, but dungeons should be made more difficult).
  • Patrols, Patrols, Patrols, this will put the squatters on edge.
  • Progressive Difficulty.
  • Somewhat faster and more random spawn times, that way everyone doesn't book it for x when its 12:00. I mean come on Verant. In Norrath, we have no concept of PST.
  • Better spawn tables
