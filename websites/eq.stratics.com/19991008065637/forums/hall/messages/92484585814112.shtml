I have seen SOOO many posts either asking which class is best, what race has th best stats, which class is best at whatever levels, and I must say, people are really missing the point of a Role Playing Game when they ask these questions.

Now I am not against powergamers, not at all, I want my chosen class to progress and make higher levels and greater skills, but my first choice was "What do I want to be in Norrath?" not "What is best to be in Norrath", see the difference?

I started out with an Elvin Ranger, and it didn't take long before I realized this was not the race that I was enjoying, then I moved to a Dwarven Warrior (large leap there) and found the race I enjoied, but not the class, then I created a Dwarven Cleric, and let me tell you, for me, THIS is the race class I want. I suits me, I ENJOY this combination, I look forward to growing with this character.

The WHOLE point is that I don't care if it is the 'BEST' of eitehr race or class, it is a personal choice, and if I am to play and spend time and money to play, I am going to do so with something I WANT to, not HAVE to. (which is the mistake so many make, they choose a race class combo that is awesome in there field, but have no real connection with the character)

I have been playing Role Playing games all my life, before there were 'official' rules, we all have at some point, we used our imaginations, followed our hearts, and let the scenes play at as they will, no thought of ending, or winning, just playing for the sheer fun of it!

Many have complained that some things are imbalanced in EverQuest, and perhaps there are, but I ask you, does it REALLY matter? Life is full of imbalances, and we deal and in many ways accept those, why should an online game, with a virtual world we are invited to see and explore, be any different?

Enjoy what there is, accept what there 'isn't and have fun.

A wise person once told me;

"If you're not having fun, then quit"

Very wise words, you play to have fun, no other reason matters.

QM
