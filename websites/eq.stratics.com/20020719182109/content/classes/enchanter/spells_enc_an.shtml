Enchanter Spells Analysis

The true power of an Enchanter lies in control. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise. An experienced Enchanter controls and shapes the very powers around us and delivers them in multiple ways. Although it is rumored that it is possible for the Enchanter to mold these powers in such a way as to enchant various weapons and armor, I personally have yet to meet one who can so I can offer no information on the subject. What I do know about, and what I will discuss with you here, is the Enchanter's ability to alter the very behavior, strengths, and weaknesses of those around him or her. The experienced Enchanter can not only help to boost the effectiveness of his allies, but can also hinder the efforts of his foes. So, without further discussion, let us delve into the Enchanter's realm of power and see where best his or her spells are used.
Rank I
Strengthen - This low level spell actually forms the backbone of the Enchanter's ability to shore up his allies' strength. The spell uses very little mana and is in fact the only such spell at your disposal until rank VI where you will learn Berserker Strength. Considering the amount of mana that higher level spell requires, you will find Strengthen is more often used.

Weaken - Again, don't underestimate the effectiveness of this spell just because it is low level. By lowering your opponents strength, he won't be able to deliver as much damage. As with most lower level spells, don't forget that you can fall back on this one in cases of low mana.

Minor Shielding - The first spell most Enchanters are taught can only be used to aid thyself. However, with its low mana requirement you should never be without its effects. Once you have learned the spell of Lesser Shielding at Rank III you will want to maintain that spell. However, if mana is low, or required elsewhere, feel free to forego the use of Lesser Shielding and use Minor Shielding instead.

Lull - This spell will be very useful up to about Rank III or IV where you will want to start using more effective means. Until then, knowing how to use this spell is absolutely essential. As the Enchanter in a party you will be comparable to a God in that proper use of this spell and its later versions can allow you to completely control the pace of the battle. When you are your party find yourselves in situations with too many foes to handle all at once, Lull will allow you to "suggest" to your foes that they want to leave you alone. This in turn will allow you, and not your enemy, to decide who will fight and who will not.

Rank II

Invisibility - The ultimate form of stealth, this spell allows you or anyone you choose to enchant, the ability to scout deep into enemy territory without being seen. In addition don't forget this spells usefulness when you or your party simply need to pass through areas unmolested. A very important note to remember when using this spell however, is that you will remain invisible only so long as you don't perform any actions which would give away your position. This means you can't use any special skills, attack, or cast any spells if you wish to remain unseen. In addition, once a foe has located you, you won't be able to evade him or her, so don't waste the mana casting this spell. You will have to evade them the traditional way.

Fear - This spell, much more powerful that Lull or even the higher level Soothe, allows you to complete frighten your foe into running. However, use this spell with caution, as the mana required is great. Often, careful use of Lull or Soothe at the proper times will be adequate and use less mana.

Enfeeblement - A much more powerful version of Weaken, this spell should be used as the first line of defense against your foes. Once you had hindered them with this, they won't be able to deliver near as much damage. Also remember that this spell will require larger mana expenditure than Weaken, so don't get too crazy with it.

Haze - This is one enchantment that you should make sure to keep active on all members of your party at all times. Party members enchanted with Haze will be much more difficult for foes to hit.

Rank III

Root - This spell will allow you to hold a foe in place from afar. This can be extremely useful for your allies for two reason. The first is it prevents the foe from running to safety or reinforcements when badly injured. The second reason is it allows your allies to strike out of range or strike and then step back, keeping them out of danger.

Lesser Shielding - Once you have learned this spell, which can only be used on yourself, you should have its effects on you at all times. If, however, you are in a mana critical situation, don't hesitate to fall back on your Minor Shielding instead.

Soothe - A more effective spell than Lull, Soothe allows you even greater control over the combat situation. Take heed, however, that you don't overdo its use. The mana expenditure for this spell is three fold that of Lull, and in some situations Lull will suffice to do the job. Learn to recognize those situations.

Rank IV

Enduring Breath - This is a spell that you won't use all that often, but when you do its effects will be much appreciated. There are rumors of hidden cities and temples far under the oceans, and this will allow you and your entire party to reach them. When using this spell with party members however, take heed to pay attention to them. Be sure you can recast it on anyone who indicates its effects have faded. It would be most devastating to the reputation of the Enchanter class as a whole should you inadvertently allow a party member to drown because you were not paying attention.

Serpent Sight - The Human members of your party will greatly benefit from your use of this spell. Its effects will allow them to see perfectly well in the darker areas of Norrath.

Languid Pace - While the mana expenditure for this spell is extremely high, its effects are nothing short of astounding. The effects of this spell, when cast on your foe, will cause him or her to attack much slower, and thereby will reduce the amount of damage your allies receive nearly in half. For a true massacre, combine the use of this spell with the Rank V spell of Quickness. You will thereby increase the attack speed of your allies while reducing the attack speed of your foes.

Rank V

Quickness - This spell will allow your allies to attack at a much faster pace, and thereby cause more damage to your foes in less time. Combine this spell with the Rank IV spell of Languid Pace for a truly devastating melee.

Disempower - While in most cases you will want to use the spell of Enfeeblement to weaken your foes, this spell can be truly devastating to them. Not only will this cause them to be weakened, but their stamina will be lowered along with their overall Armor Class. Be forewarned, however, that the mana expenditure of this spell is very high, and should be used with caution.

Chase the Moon - This spell, while requiring large amounts of vital mana, is exceptional at forcing foes to flee should the Rank II spell of Fear fail.

Shielding - A stronger and more effective version of Lesser and Minor Shielding, this spell increases hit points as well as magic resistance. The mana required is high however, and as such this spell should be used only when mana is not in high demand.

Rank VI

Berserker Strength - When you cast this spell on a warrior, you turn him or her into a virtual killing machine. The effects of this spell not only make the warrior stronger, but also increase his or her agility in combat and increases maximum hit points. Considering the relatively low mana expenditure in comparison to its level, this spell is a very important one to remember.

Calm - For situations where Lull or Soothe simply prove ineffective, Calm can usually do the trick. However, it is suggested that you try to learn just what situations call for each, so as to not waste mana on higher level spells when lower levels would work.

Feckless Might - This higher level version of the Rank II spell of Enfeeblement is extremely effective at lowering your foes' strength. However, due to the large mana requirements, it should only be used in extreme situations or situations where mana levels are not a big concern.
