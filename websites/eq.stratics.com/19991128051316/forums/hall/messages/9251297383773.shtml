I don't think that players should assume it is safe to rest at a zone boundary, in fact they should expect quite the opposite. Because of the game design, it is very often that the only way to escape a creature (whether by accident or mistake or just plain dumb luck) is to attempt to zone. In my experience, guards cannot be relied upon to be where you expect or even to help when needed. It is also my experience that it is difficult to get help when you need it most - there simply isn't enough time to assert the need or the potential helpers cannot help quickly enough. I have been helped on occasion only to find myself dead anyhow because the creature will not change its focus (I think most players haven't a clue how to use taunt or haven't bothered to train it up). Hence, I try not to get into trouble, but when I do, the closest zone boundary is always my first choice of paths out of trouble. The bottom line is that its a tough world out there, and I take no responsibility for other players resting less than safe locations. Note that I would never intentionally train creatures to a zone boundary if I were not in serious trouble.
