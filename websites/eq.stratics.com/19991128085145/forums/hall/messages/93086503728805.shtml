As a high elf, I'd say... high elves, wood elves, and dark elves are the *real* 'real people', and dark elves are evil.

Then you have the sort of real people, the humans and half elves and gnomes and such. They're prone to weakness and failure, but they *try* to be honorable and to respect the rights of others, at least in a broad species sense. Individuals can be picked out and hunted down as animals, of course, like the bandits of the Karanas.

Then you have the clever beasts. You can tell they're beasts by their lack of civilization. Some foolish people are tempted to use intelligence as a determinant instead, but some dangerous animals are very, very intelligent. Evil eyes, for instance. Or Kerrans. So as you can see it's a misleading metric.

Any attempt to treat them like people is suicide, because they don't and can't share our values and priorities, and 'rights' have to be acknowledged by both parties, or they're worthless. There's no point arguing with an aviak about your right to life as they pluck out your eyes to feed to their chicks.

Now, it's true, they didn't actually attack me the first time I saw them, but I could tell that they wanted to, and were just waiting for the right time. After nearly wiping them out they've finally decided to show their true colors. }:)
