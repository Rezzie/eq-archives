Playing a Cleric - Intermediate Strategies

 Overview   Highs & Lows   Creation   Playing   Formative Years   Terrible Teens   Group Play   Hints 

Overview

So you want to be one of the healers of Norrath. Listen well and heed mywords faithful one, for the journey is long and hard - but infinitelyrewarding! Clerics are one of the most exciting and playable classes inthe land of Norrath, possessing healing and offensive spells that makethem a cornerstone of any well-balanced group, as well as being able towield blunt weapons and wear almost any armor they please. These uniqueabilities allow the Cleric to hit hard as well as take a beating, andlive on to fight another day. In addition, Clerics are granted abilitiesthat allow them to take on undead foes at levels that would have afighter at the same level fleeing for his life! Whether your style isgroup or solo play, the Cleric is equipped to deal with the perils ofNorrath like no other class.

Highs & Lows

To be honest, a Cleric is not as effective at solo play as the heavierhitting pure fighter classes. This is because round for round, a Clericis going to have a harder time dealing consistent damage to kill highlevel monsters, as well as having fewer hit points to survive thevicious counter-attacks. Clerics can dish out a beating with theirspells, but their most powerful damage spells take a while to cast -leaving them vulnerable to a well placed hit from their foe - and drainlarge amounts of mana. Take it from me, the one time you're guaranteedto need a quick heal is right after draining all of your mana to blastyour opponent down to an inch of his life! Additionally, while there areplenty of blunt weapons that do a fair amount of damage, only the mostpowerfully enchanted mace is going to keep up with a two-handed claymorein the hands of an experienced barbarian warrior. The one caveat to thisis the fact that Clerics are able to wear all kinds of armor, includingfull plate. Find yourself a powerful set of armor and wade into thefray, well protected from harm. This is an advantage none of the otherspellcasters can claim!

As with many other classes, Clerics come into their own in a group. Withjust one fighter to distract the monsters and keep your hands free forhealing and support, you'll find that your tank can live well 'nighforever battling against monsters far above your own levels. And youroffensive spells are potent enough to deal the decisive kill at the endof the battle.

One drawback to the Cleric is they have to deal with the worst of themoney situation of both fighters and spellcasters. On the one hand, youneed to save all the gold and platinum you can to afford the spells youwant in the next rank. On the other, if you don't get some protectioncovering those scrawny bones, your gonna be soup. So it's especiallyimportant to keep a tight fist on your money supply, and never waste asingle copper chip. Come level fourteen you're going to want to spend 50platinum to get all those great spells - and if you're not careful,you'll be broke as a milkmaid!

Creation

If you've played EverQuest before, you know that race plays a minor rolein your character's strengths and weaknesses. The beauty of EverQuest isthat any race/class combination, if played smartly and creatively, canbe successful. Blame this on 989, not the players - they have designed asystem that enhances character creation and allows you to play what youwant, instead of forcing you to pick the "best" class in order to keepup.

That said, the races that miss out on Clerics are the Barbarians,Half-Elves, Ogres, Trolls, and Wood Elves (Ogres and Trolls should checkout the Shaman class). Wisdom is the driving skill for Clerics, as it determines the amount ofmana you get. Strength is also important, especially if you are dreamingof your character crusading against evil with platemail, tower shield,and war hammer. You can't wear the armor if you can't carry it!Otherwise, adjust your attributes as you see fit and wade into the fray!

Playing
Playing your character is all about exploration and experience, mistakesand victory. Nothing's going to prepare you for adventuring and honingyour skills like Norrath proper, but the next section will help get youoff on the right foot.
 top


Formative Years
If you've played one character under level four, you've pretty muchplayed them all. Life for a rank newbie in Norrath is pretty much thesame no matter what class or race you're in. For a while your existencewill consist of killing everything you can find in the newbie gardens(usually snakes, bats, rats, and other unimpressive fare) and sellingwhatever loot you can. You will start out with Strike and Light Healingspells - scribe and memorize them immediately, and begin practicing yourmagical abilities. Use Strike to finish your opponents off, which willgive you the practice you need for timing your spells between youropponent's attacks. Cast light healing as necessary - you will find thatwith your healing abilities you will last longer on the battlefield thanyour non-Cleric friends! The five practice points you acquire forcompleting your guild quest will be best spent in one-handed blunt, asthat's what you'll be using to do the majority of your damage for quitea while.

When you can afford it purchase Courage. When cast, this will boost yourAC and HP, and at low levels is a tremendous boon. Unless you absolutelyMUST have one of the other Rank 1 spells, save your money until you getto level five, which has some more powerful spells.

Clerics gain new spell rankings slower than Magicians and Wizards. Thisis to balance the Clerics' ability to use blunt weapons and armor.Therefore levels one to four are more of the same stuff. For maximumexperience you'll want to stay away from parties until at least levelthree and depending on your personal inclination even until level five.At level five you'll want to purchase Light Healing, Holy Armor, Gate,and Ward Undead. Your prowess on the battlefield will have improvedquite a bit and these additional spells will help. But just because youhave Light Healing, don't put that Minor Healing away! Light Healingtakes significantly longer to cast as well as using up quite a bit moremana than Minor Healing, so you'll want to keep both of them memorizedand available.

Now that you have Ward Undead, try teaming up with a fighter and goundead hunting. Let the fighter take the damage while you crush theundead fiend with Ward. When you're comfortable, try fighting the weakerundead creatures alone. Look for parties to join and practice your groupskills. Buy Lull and start taking on goblin, orc, or gnoll camps onemonster at a time.

Terrible Teens
Life changes dramatically at level nine. At this level you gain accessto your first real damage spell - Word of Pain. WoP deals damage to allthe monsters in a small circle around you, and can clear a battlefieldin a hurry. WoP will draw quite a bit of mana at level nine so becareful, but you'll find it just the thing you're looking for to dealdamage while still being the party healer. Some other important spellslevel nine brings are Center, a powerful buff spell, Root, which willstop an enemy dead in its tracks, and Soothe, an improved version ofLull. Root is handy to stop a fleeing foe from rounding up his friendswhile you attack his back, as well as keeping a mummy in place while youpound it from a safe distance with Ward Undead. Soothe works the same asLull, just better, and is well worth the upgrade. And Center is Couragepart II - a much-improved version that no fighter in your party shouldbe without.From nine to fourteen you'll be much more powerful, and well equipped toexplore more challenging (and rewarding!) places. You and your party cantry out Crushbone proper rather than skulking about the camps at theentrance, and Black Burrow trains won't mean an instant death anymore.Soon you'll be to level fourteen, which is the shining level for aCleric. You can now cast Healing and watch that HP bar climb climbclimb, while Smite is a sorely needed replacement for the now useless(except for luring!) Strike. You'll also have access to Expulse Undead -and if you thought Ward Undead was incredible, you haven't seen anythingyet! Paired up with Expulse is Invisibility to Undead - the perfectcomplement to an adventure in the undead dungeons. Enjoy your new powersand abilities - you are well on your way to becoming a master Cleric!

Group Play
A Cleric can be one of the most rewarding classes to play in a group.When adventuring with your comrades, you are in charge of that mostprecious commodity - life. It is your sworn duty to keep yourcompatriots alive at any cost - lonely is the Cleric who can't masterthe ability of keeping a close eye on the health of his fellows whileclosely following the battle at hand. A well-played Cleric makes friendsanywhere she pleases, has no trouble finding a group, and is neverwithout a helping hand. The unsuccessful Cleric - well, he might as wellhang up his hat and swear off the holy path.

One of the key skills you'll need to master as a Cleric is the best useof your abilities to help the group. For the Cleric there are three keyroles for you to play:

Healing and Combat Support:
This is what being a Cleric is all about, and is the reason many choosenot to follow the healer's path. If you don't see the excitement, value,and fun in being the person your comrades depend on to keep themfighting, a Cleric's life is not for you. In addition to the obvioushealing, Cleric's have several powerful "buff" spells that increase hitpoints and armor class, provide resistance to certain types of attacks,and can cure ailments such as disease and blindness. Combat support alsoincludes using spells such as Lull or Soothe to pinpoint the exact foesyou will fight (or allow safe passage through a treacherous zone!), Rootto stop a fleeing monster dead in its tracks, and Invigor to restorewaning stamina when the battle drags on a bit too long. It's also theact of keeping on eye on your surroundings and watching for potentialtrouble during battle. Your name will go down in your partners littleblack books if you are the one to spot the King of the Minotaurssneaking up on your fighters during battle!

Spell Damage: Sometimes the combat is going particularly well, or your fighters aredodging and ducking blows like ballet dancers (maybe because of allthose buff spells you've cast on them!). This is the time to survey thesituation and see what you can do to help cause some damage. If you'refighting multiple enemies, a well placed Word of Pain might be theticket to evening the odds, or perhaps you'd like to flex a littlemuscle and take down that Mummy in a minute with Ward Undead. Remember,though, it's always wise to practice restraint with combat spells, orall too soon you'll know what it feels like to have used all your manato crush your foe, only to have nothing left to heal your bruised andweakened fighters when a wandering monster attacks.

Melee Damage: At low levels, the damage you can inflict on monsters with regularhand-to-hand combat is on par with the other fighter levels, andtherefore will make a difference in the battle. As you progress up theexperience ladder and begin facing tougher and more advanced battles,this isn't the case, so only step into the fray when there's absolutelynothing better for you to do. There's nothing wrong with sitting downand meditating in the middle of battle - your friends will appreciateyour forethought in recovering a few bars rather than lightly tapping aCrusader with your mace when his friends show up and start swinging!

Hints
Finally, here's a list of some helpful pointers, ideas, and conceptsthat will help you make your way in Norrath, and become a Cleric thatBards will sing tales of long after your passing!

If you find yourself alone in Norrath, the best things to fight areUndead. As a Cleric you have a natural ability to best these fiends fromthe netherworld, and there are several Cleric-only spells in yourarsenal such as Ward, Expulse, Spook, and Sense Undead, that you'll beable to use with great effect. Solo'ng undead is also a good way to earnmoney and XP - you'll be able to go face-to-face with undead at levels apure fighter type would shy away from.

Save your money! Your third rank spells will cost on average fiveplatinum each - a small fortune in Norrath's depressed economy. Untilyou get to around twentieth level, being able to afford both the spellsand the weapons and armor you want will be one of your greatestchallenges. After twenty, you can look forward to a little leeway, asyou'll be able to fight monsters that will give much more money andvaluable loot, while the price of your spells will not keep up inrelation to your money making ability.

As a Cleric you've got the easiest way to gain friends and allies inNorrath - use it! When you see a hapless soul stumbling away from amonster, help that person out! Heal him up and watch the tide of battleturn. Or offer your assistance to fellow travelers when the world isswarming with plague rats or other disease carrying vermin. When all issaid and done you'll have made a valuable friend and ally; in Norrath,you can never have too many friends.

In battle, your eyes should be checking your comrades HP more than yourown. If everything is going right, your fighters should be taking thedamage while you're on the sidelines playing support. In a long battle,remember to Meditate between casting heals - at Level 11 you'll recoverenough mana to cast a Light Heal every 2-3 rounds. And that makes a hugedifference.

Just like any other spellcaster, you'll have a hard time working yourcraft if an Orc is belting you. Also, some monsters take offense at yourhealing and turn quickly to attack you. Make sure your party membersrealize they need to keep you out of direct fire if they want to benefitfrom your healing - if they know it's a matter of their life and death,they'll be sure to Taunt more effectively!

In a party, keep your fighters in tip-top shape between battles as wellas during. When the fighting is over, make sure all your fighters are atfull or near full health before recovering your own mana. Also be readyto apply Center, Holy Armor, Courage, or whatever other buff spells youhave available as soon as your teammate signals readiness. It only takesone unexpected run in with a fierce monster to learn this lesson ofreadiness well.

Lull (or Soothe) and Strike is your friend! At a monster camp, controlthe pace and frenzy of the battle by selectively Soothing or Lullingcertain linked monsters, then Striking from a distance the remainingmonster(s) you want to tackle. Repeat as necessary until the camp isempty. This evens the odds and allows even a party of just two (you andyour fighter!) to handle dangerous areas.Divine Aura grants you 30 seconds of invulnerability when cast. You canmove while invulnerable, but you cannot cast spells or attack. 30seconds is a LONG time when you're being chased down by a ravaging hordeof ghouls or when you're providing a getaway for your compatriots. Learnit and use it!

Practice all of your spells you will be using in combat, and have a goodunderstanding of their casting times, duration, and effect. When you'retrying to get that decisive Expluse Undead in on the bashing, crushing,and slashing Greater Skeleton timing is everything!Be sure to practice both one-handed and two-handed blunt skillsregularly as you progress through the levels. Because you must hitcreatures that give you experience to raise a melee skill, it gets verydifficult to raise skills once you've neglected them for a few levels(try hitting that Level 20 Gnoll with a two-handed blunt skill of 3!).When you do find that Magical Dwarven two-handed Warhammer, you'll wantto be able to use it.
