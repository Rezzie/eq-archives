The Rogue

Equipment
Race Choices

So you've decided to play a rogue. Backstabs are cool, and so are sneak and hide, and it's generally a fun class to play. Personally, I don't find the casting classes fun. I've started several caster characters, and become board by level 10. So melee it is, and I already have a warrior.

My rogue is currently level 11, so this guide cannot be totally accurate past that level. Also, this is the way *I* chose to play, and is only a general guidline for this class/race combination. So, without further ado:

 top

Your Job and Equipment

As a rogue, you are able to wear wear most non-plate armor. You can use slashing and piercing weapons, but if single wielding, use a piercing weapon. With Dual Wield, always keep a piercing weaponin your primary (left) weapon slot. You cannot backstab without piercing, and it works off of your primary slot. Feel free to use any kind of weapon you want in the off hand, just keep your piercing skill high.

Your job as a Rogue is to be a damage dealer. You are not really a tank, as the lack of plate shows. You do not have Taunt either. You may be asked to tank, but if possible, leave it to the Warriors, Paladins, and Shadow Knights. Concentrate on your offence, but try to keep your AC up too. Important Stats

Stength - How much you can carry, also how hard you hit. Especially affects your backstabs.

Agility - How often you get hit, how often enemies miss. However, given a choice between agility and AC, go with the AC, you'll take less damage.

Dexterity - The most important Rogue stat. It controls how fast you learn all of your rogue specific skills. It also determines how often you hit, and how often magical weapon effects go off.

Stamina is not too important, because you are not a tank. Intelligence and wisdom aren't important either, and charisma may not be important because you can always get an indifferent /con from an NPC with sneak (see below)

Stats seem to go in quarters, meaning you'll see your ATK rating go up more if you raise your strength from 74 to 75, than from 79 to 80. So aim to get your stats to the 75, 100, and 125 milestones. Remember, at later levels, items will make everyone have near the same stats.

Race choices

Halfling:
Pros - Traditionally, the race with rogueish tendencies, best starting agility and dexterity. Good starting zone. Twinkers' favorite.
Cons - Everyone and their brother plays a halfling rogue, it's the most popular race. Low starting strength.
Reccomendations - Put all the bonus points you can into strength.

Barbarian:
Pros - Best starting strength, good agility, looks cool.
Cons - Being big can sometimes be a problem. Low starting dexterity. No night vision. Second most popular race for Rogues.
Reccomendations - Put all starting points you can into dexterity, leftover into strength.

Dark Elf:
Pros - Looks very cool. Fits, if you are a roleplayer. Good agility and dexterity. Racial Nightvision.
Cons - Tied for lowest strength with Wood Elves, and Gnomes. Evil race. Bad starting zone, with a convenient location. Kill on sight many places.
Reccomendations - Beef up the strength.

Wood Elf:
Pros - Very good dexterity and agility. Short 'n' Cute. Racial nightvision. Popular starting zone. Racial Forage ability.
Cons - Low strength. Starting zone has the most public chat usage in the entire game. Getting hit on if you play a female.
Reccomendations - Lay on the strength, go play in Butcherblock.

Dwarf:
Pros - Great stength, stamina, and dexterity. Racial Infravision, Sense Heading. One of the best starting zones.
Cons - Low agility. The women have beards.
Reccomendations - Lay on the agility.

Half Elf:
Pros - No stat is incredibly low, agility and dexterity are very good.
Cons - Somewhat low strength.
Reccomendations - I think you know what I'm going to say.

Human:
Pros - Very average
Cons - Very average
Reccomendations - Split points equally between strength, agility, and dexterity.

Gnome:
Pros - Very good agility and dexterity. With a helmet, looks like a little ninja. Very rare class/race combo. Racial Infravision, and Tinkering is a gnome only trade skill.
Cons - Lowest starting strength. Starting zone is awful.
Reccomendations - Put all the points you can into strength, and go to play in Faydark or Butcher

I decided to play Gnome, because the combination was so unique, and you really can play any race to 50 relatively easily.

Skills

Sneak: You get this at level 3. What it does is lower the agro of NPCs and mobs you stand behind. Your speed slows to a crawl when doing this, but you can move. This can be used in conjunction with Hide, so you can move while invisible. With this, you can move in cites where you are kill on sight, and by and sell to NPCs because they will now /con indifferent to you. Put one training point into it, and then practice alot.

Hide: You get this at level 4. Makes you invisible to living *and* undead. Does not work on a few high level NPCs. Looting a corpse, attacking, and getting a spell cast on you (including Bard songs) will make you visible. Only works while standing still, unless used in conjunction with Sneak. You can use the /corpse command to pull your corpse to a safe location while staying invisible. Put one training point into it, and practice alot.

Evade: A new skill rogues were given to balance the fact that the lockpicking and trap skills are near useless. It is available once you get hide. Evade is not a trainable skill per se. As your sneak/hide skills get better, it becomes more effective. The way to use it is to turn off your attack, and press hide. If it works, you are lowered on the NPC's "hate list", and if there is someone else around, the monster will go attack them. This is very usefull if you do too much damage, and the tank can't taunt the monster off of you.

Pickpocket: This skill becomes available at level 7. This is a very cool skill, it allows you to steal money from the your enemies. It is accomplished by pressing the pickpocket button when not attacking. This can be practiced on all mobs, but will only give money from the vaguely humanoid ones, such as orcs and gnolls (i.e. those that might have pockets on them). Using this skill will make the NPC attack you, which is why it is unwise to use it on townspeople and guards. Make sure you can beat whatever mob you are PPing from, unless of course you are stealing from someone elses kill. If that is the case, watch out for NPCs that can speak, like guards other people are fighting, and dervishes, because the will alert that you are trying to steal. This skill does take from the pool of possible loot the enemy, drops, so it is considered bad form to loot while in a group. However, if someone steals your kill, follow them and pick the monster clean. Put one training point into it, and practice it.

Backstab: This is your defining skill, achieved at level 10. You may want to save up the majority of your practice points up till now, and then blow them all here. It is worth it, for your backstab is typically five or six times your weapon damage. Backstab is a piercing only attack that can only be done from behind the enemy. You will spend most of your group life opposite the group's main tank/taunter. In groups, try to make all the tanks stand on one side, so you do not have to keep turning. The three things that most affect how much you backstab for are you skill in it, your strength, and your weapon damage. As you achieve higher levels, you will find your self looking for strength items more than anything, and higher damage weapons. A level 50 rogue with 200 or so strength, and a 13 damage weapon can hit for over 300 per backstab. It should be noted that monsters hate to be backstabbed, even if you miss. It is one of the most effective taunts in the game, even if you don't want it to be.

Safe Fall: Level 12. This skill can help you survive those nasty accidental falls (Dagnor's Cauldron...). While the skill cap is lower than the one on monks, this is still a usefull skill. The best way to train it is to find one of those steep hills, that you can fall down and repeatedly get hit for 1 damage five times on the way down. Just keep falling down the hill, and the skill should go up fairly quickly.

Dual Wield: Level 13. This allows you to wield a second weapon in place of a shield. This will not quite double you damage output. You hit more as your skill goes up. You can one hand weapon you want in the second slot, it doesn't have to be piercing. Many higher level rogues use a procing (effect) weapon in this slot. Put only in one point to start this skill, and it will go up on its own.

Double Attack: Level 16. This skill gives you the chance of hitting a second time each round, per weapon. This leads to a possible (but rare) four attack round. This skill will rise on its own, after the starting point.

Throwing: This is an under-utilized skill. While other classes get throwing, and they all can get critical throws, only a rogue can get a Deadly Strike. A Deadly Strike has a chance of occuring when you throw something at a monsters back. It does much more damage than a critical hit. Criticals and DS's have more chance of happening with a high dexterity and throwing skill.

Pick Locks/Disarm Traps/Sense Traps: I do not know much of these skills, as they are generally broken. Verant decided they couldn't implement traps way back before they released the game, and pre-expansion, there are less than ten pickable doors in the entire game. One of the only zones where this skill is usefull is Befallen, which has three locked doors. You need a lockpicking kit from a vendor to accomplish the skill. It will go up with practice.

Instill Doubt: I do not know much about this skill, except that it works like a Fear spell.

The Mask of Deception

I thought I'd put something about this item in this guide. This mask is a Bard and Rogue only No Drop item. It is the common dropp off the Froglok Assassin in Lower Guk, which also drops the Serrated Bone Dirk (one of the best rogue weapons). The mask adds 13 to your charisma, and, starting at level 15, lets you cast Illusion: Dark Elf. This will make you Gnomes and Halflings taller, you Barbarians more manageable, and lets everyone vacation is Neriak. Or any evil city. Of course, you should always /con guards and NPC's, because the illusion changes your faction with good and bad cities. Most of the dark elf rogues you see around are that in name only. The reason that I put this in, is that while Lower Guk is a high level (40-50) dungeon, this mask can be achieves as low as level 4. If you have never killed a froglok, this is very simple. Put all your equipment and money in the bank, and run down to Innothule. Go into Upper Guk, and you should be able to run around with impunity (assuming you haven't killed a froglok). Always /con, to be safe.

Once in Upper Guk, find the live side entrance to Lower Guk, and zone in. Nothing on the live side except gargoyles will attack you, but watch for wandering undead frogs, which will. find the exit to the dead side, and go into sneak/hide mode. Ask directions to the supply room, where the Assassin spawns. This being Lower Guk, he is perpetually camped. Since the mask is No Drop, and bard and rogue only, the groups usually destroy it. Ask if you can loot it if it drops, and sit down and wait. I got my mask at level 10, and the Assassin spawned within five minutes of me getting there. This was pure luck; you may have to wait hours. Backtrack to leave, once you have you mask. I'd reccomend you wait untill a peak time to try this.

With sneak/hide, and the mask, a Rogue can go near anywhere.

Ravenscale Armor

Rogue class-specific mid-level armor is not made of quest items, like for most classes. It drops off named spawns in the Cazic-Thule dungeon. Most are very hard, especially the Chest Guard, which is dropped off the Avatar of Fear himself. The zone itself should not be attemped by anyone below 20, preferably 25.

Places to Hunt (Recomendations)

Levels 1-6:

Don't leave your starting zone, unless you are going to another one. Kill low level enemies, and loot everything that is stackable. At your level, 20 of anything is decent money. Solo, you really don't get much from a group at this level.

Levels 7-14:

Find you local low level dungeon. These are usually Blackburrow, Befallen, and Crushbone. You could also hunt wisps at around level ten, but you need a magic weapon to hit them. Starting at level ten, you really need to be in a group, especially since you get backstab.

Levels 14-17:

You can do the castle in Crushbone, or most anywhere in Oasis. I know Oasis is overcrowded, but it's a huge zone. The "orc highway" alone can easily support 3 groups.

Levels 18-20:

You have many choices. You can stretch the Crushbone throne room for a few more levels, as it's good money. You can go to Lavastorm. You can hunt crocs and caimans in Oasis. You can try the yard in Unrest. My personal favorite was the orc spawn in Highpass. It is usually camped by two groups, but if you can get in at an off-peak time, there is only one. It is one of the fastest spawns in the game, and usually spawns at least two orcs at a time. Loot is money up to and including some platinum, bronze weapons, and ring armor. I made 3 levels in as many days there.

Levels 20- :

From now on, you can hunt most zones in the game. Go where ever you feel like. I like Unrest, at low twenties with my main character, but go explore some other zones.

Well, there's my meager guide on playing a rogue. I'd like to thank all the high level rogues, and rogue message boards which I've pestered for knowledge. I hope this guide is usefull to you, and you have fun with the class. Remember that a warrior, cleric and rogue group is a very effective three-man group, and don't let anyone know that you are pickpocketing. Remember that with sneak/hide you can do corpse retrievals for other people, and that you are not a tank.

Have fun.

Wakizashi
