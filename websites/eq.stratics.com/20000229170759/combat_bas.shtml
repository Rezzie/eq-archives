Battle Etiquette
  1. Don’t take someone else’s kill. If someone is already fighting something don’t jump in unless invited or if they are in trouble. If it looks like they are in trouble, ask them if they need help. If you actually do wind up jumping and saving their butt, the simplest way to do it is to target them and use the /assist command. This command allows you to attack whatever they are attacking.

  2. Don’t block someone’s escape route. This is a sensitive subject to anyone who has tried to flee the terrors of the dark deeps and found themselves blocked by….YOU. This can put you on the "bad" list of someone for weeks on end. Although EQ is non pvp oriented, the monsters can be so hard that having a friend around just when you were least expecting them can save your bacon. People who I thought were strangers have saved me several times…just because I was nice to them at one time or another.

  3. Don’t be greedy. For example: there are 3 female barbarians standing around in the snow shivering in their mini kilts and halter tops and one of them has 3 bear cubs attacking her. One of the other wild women tries to attack one of the bears and gets yelled at by the one wearing the "monster magnet" perfume for stealing one of "her" kills. This is just not right. Just because the monsters like the taste of your blood doesn’t mean that you need to hog all the action. See 1.2 about the "what goes around comes around" principle.

Manual Dexterity
  1. I don’t know about you, but after a long day of stomping monsters my wrists need some ice. One of the hazards of EQ addiction is what is called Carpal Tunnel Syndrom. This occurs when the tendons in the wrist become enflamed from over use or in this case misuse. The way to overcome this challenge (other than VR gear – when, oh when will my people be free of the shackles of mouse and keyboard) is to keep your hand and forearm in the same plane. In other words, don’t bend your wrist.

  2. In EQ, this is nearly impossible with the default settings for the different combat modes. The Z key (auto primary attack) must be struck every time you start meleeing. Also the number keys (1-6) are in use almost constantly during the game in one mode or another. If you look at your keyboard (yes, do it now) you will see that the Z key and the number keys are not close to each other at all. Since combat takes up more the 50% of you time in this game, this constant movement and bending of the wrist will almost surely lead to enflamed tendons (it happened to me and I’m no old fogey).

  3. The solution: move your "auto primary attack" key to the key pad 0 key. Other defaults that should be moved there for prime manual dexterity and general ease of use are the "NPC targeting key" (defaults to the N key), and any other key that you use often (for magic using classes, and monks there are several special keys that I would recommend moving to the keypad vicinity).

  4. How I did it: I assigned the "auto primary attack" role to the keypad 0 key, the "NPC targeting" key to the ". /Del" key, and the other number based keys I put on the key pad. With this method I can keep my wrist straight and get things done on average about 50% faster than other players.
