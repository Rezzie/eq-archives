Trust me.

Immersion is the feeling that you are living a dream; not the feeling that you are trapped in some slightly different mundane reality.

UO tried harder to be realistic than any other MUD, but even it learned this lesson. Take its much vaunted "realistic" ecology and economy, for example. They threw it all out the window in beta testing. The realistic ecology meant the players drove every monster species to extinction without hours of rebooting the servers. The realistic economy meant that no one could get a shopkeeper to buy their trade goods--ever.

If reality was so great, people wouldn't even play EQ to escape it. Games are nothing more than an absurd collection of lies. That's why we love them.
