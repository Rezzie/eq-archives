Important Notes



Playing the enchanter is by far no easy task, but reaps the greatest benefits, and definitely earns the title of being the 'best' pure caster.

NOTE: I won't be including any tactics that may be used for enchanters that are 51+, simply because some enchanters don't have RoK :(

***Raising/creating your enchanter, and knowing what button does what.***

Let's begin by raising your enchanter. Enchanters work good as any race, unlike other pure casters that simply go well with higher INT. I myself played a Dark Elf, with no reason in particular, besides I like playing an evil guy, with an INT of 124 and a CHA of 85. Two things should be held in the highest regard when creating your enchanter:

Commitment to a diety - The enchanter illusion system works not only off of race and gained/lost faction, but also your religion! If you want to be able to tread Norrath freely, then I highly suggest that your enchanter is Agnostic!

CHA vs INT - The enchanter's abilities are based off of two important stats: Charisma and Inteligence. Like any other robed caster, intelligence mainly determines the enchanter's amount of mana. Charisma determines mainly the success of certain spells, and how well they work, which is why I highly suggest you have your CHA pumped up at all times.

Skill importance:

Channeling and meditation are a given for any caster, but some casters don't use all of their magic skills. Enchanters on the other hand may make use of every magic skill.

Abjuration (Important) - For any caster, Abjuration fuels the self shielding line. For enchanters, it also fuels their AC buffs, Rune series, and Tashan line. I highly suggest you work on this line, mainly for Tashan and self buffs. Tashan will be used every battle by a good enchanter. Just keep using Tashan or higher at least once a battle and this will raise in due time.

Alteration (Important) - Enchanters will most likely use this skill more than any other magic skill. Most buffs, weakening spells, Root spells, and other effect spells will run off of this spell skill. I also suggest that you SPECIALIZE in this skill when you hit level 20, because you will most likely use the most mana in Alteration spells. Raise this skill by casting the low level strength buff

Evocation (Fairly useful) - Only 5 spells run from this spell skill, your direct damage line. Even though these spells should collect dust most of the time, I suggest you raise this skill. You never know when a need of self defense will be needed, or an extra spell hit may mean the difference between victory and defeat. The only way to raise this is nuke. Be it you or someone else.

Conjuration (Important)- Although this skill only works for two lines of spells, it's very important. Conjuration will determine your ability to summon an animation pet, and will work for your Mesmerise spell line. Both of these spells will most likely be used by your enchanter at many times. Raise this skill by Mesmeriseing yourself.

Divination (Important) - Invisibilities, illusions, and the Color series depend on this skill. Most likely, you'll use invisibility tons in dungeon situations, illusions while exploring towns, and the Color line during battle. It would be in your best interest to raise this and keep it maxed. Use Invisibility or a low level illusion to raise this skill.

Important combat spells:

Mesmerise, Enthrall, Mesmerization, Entrance etc... - These spells keep your enemy stunned for an extended period of time, unless they are damaged in some way. This is mostly used to keep other enemies out of a fight, while your party works on another creature. Just Mesmerise any creature that currently isn't being attacked by your party, but happens to be hitting a group member. These spells can also wipe the enemy's hate list if you do not do anything to provoke it once it is mesmerised. There are also Area Effect editions of this spell. Your first one is researched at level 16.

Charm, Beguile, etc.. - Will most likely not be used in excess, but will come in handy. This spell gives you temporary control of any being, making it your 'pet'. Any command can be used with it except the Suicide command. Careful when making pets sit down, since they will tend to walk away (it's a bug). Careful when charming a creature. Only do it when your CHA is high and the target has been Tashaned. Once the creature is out of the charm, it will attack you. It is not possible to Charm something that is out of your spell range cap, however you can still Charm beings that may be red to you (I charmed by first griffin at 31). A good charm with the lowest charm spell may last up to about 5 minutes, and the duration increases with a better charm spell.

Color Flux, Shift, Dizzying line etc. - This Area Effect spell will stun any enemies that are very close to you. This spell casts extremely fast and is good to use when you need to get a spell off quickly while your enemies are stunned. The first spell in the series will last approximatey 3 seconds. The second one will last about 6 seconds. Good to use along with the Whirl series (Stun target).

Root, Enstill - Use this to simply keep your opponent in place for a good duration of time.

Memory Blur - Great spell. Similar to the Cleric's 'Atone' spell. Use this on any target to wipe it's hate list. However, if it is KOS to you, you may want to use something else to get away from the target. (Will work on any level target)

Haste spells - Quickness, Alacrity etc... These spells make you attack a bit faster than before. Extremely useful when used on a pet or a good tank. This spell lasts shorter than mosts buffs, but will hold out for a battle or two. A more long-term Haste buff is Augmentation, which also increases AGI.

Clarity - Increases mana regeneration. Never leave home without it. There is also a new level 16 spell called 'Breeze', that is a little less effective than the level 29 Clarity. This spell is purchased in Kunark only, but should be usable by anyone that doesn't have the expansion (should someone bring it to you)

Tashan - Use this spell every battle, no questions asked. This reduces the target's magic resistance. May be used on any level target. Extremely important because all enchanter spells are magic based, and mobs have high magic resist.

Chaotic Feedback, Sanity Warp, etc... - Any Direct damage spell is important in some way. Your first DD spell will be at level 8, doing up to about 50 damage, which is comparable to the wizard's DD at that level. However, the damage ratio becomes a bit poorer, resulting in a 528 damage spell (maxed) at level 44. The biggest drawbacks are the slow recovery time/casting time, and they're an extreme taunt. Another important note is that these spells have no partial resist... either you do full damage, or no damage.

Rune - Makes the target invulnerable till a certain amount of damage is incurred. A great life saver.

***How to play the enchanter***

Unlike some classes, there is definitely a wrong way to play your enchanter. Enchaters are meant to be a support class, which means that they shouldn't be doing damage, or taking any. What enchanters do is to help others do their job.

Group combat

Enchanters greatly evolve through the levels. At low levels, you'll find yourself doing more conventional combat, such as damage spells, and even cracking at the enemy with your weapon. That scenario changes by the time you're level 20. You'll find yourself leaving the DD spells off your spell list, and will be using those Head icons a whole lot more :)

What to do in group situations:

Before any battle, buff where you see fit. Don't go overboard, because you'll need to conserve mana. Mainly, you'll buff tanks with AC buffs, STR buffs, and Haste buffs. However, you may have someone in your group who can provide better AC and STR. In that case, don't bother with either of those buffs. You'll also be using the Clarity line often. Also, if you are lacking in group tanks, you may want to make an animation. Once your buffs are placed, memorise the spells that will be used in combat. It's important to make use of every spell slot, because most of the time, you'll need to use a spell that isn't memorized, so choose wisely.

There's three things to do as an enchanter. First, be active in the group. This will include Tashaning, Color Fluxing/Spinning (more on this tactic later), mesmerising excess MoB's, Rooting, and if you have a pet, let yourself take a hit so he'll join in on the combat. If you don't have a pet, Charm an excess MoB if your party needs help in tanking (Note: Try to keep Charm and Memory Blur memorized at the same time). Second is don't nuke, this tactic is just damned foolish if no nuking really needs to be done. It only angers the MoB to attack you, and wastes mana, and will most likely get you tossed out of the group. Once your mana wears down (let's say below 2 bubbles), take a seat and wait for something bad to happen. One of the enchanter's trademarks is the ability to save a group's collective butt in emergencies. Your basic practice of saving your group is to Mesmerise the MoBs, or Rune party members. This is why it's important to make sure you don't go OOM. During downtime, ask your group every so often if your buffs are still in place.

Soloing as an enchanter:

Contrary to popular belief, the enchanter can solo. Although it may be more wise to find a group, you can solo somewhere if you can't find somewhere to group.

Also, in any case, use the following buffs on yourself: Self shielding, an AC buff, Clarity, CHA buff, INT buff, and Troll, Fire Elemental Illusion if your current position isn't in faction danger. I always keep Gate memmed incase things get hairy and I have a safe bind point. I also keep a Mesmerise spell memmed incase I bite off more than I can chew (more than 1 mob)

First thing is to make a pet. I highly suggest against using Charm to solo. Although Charming may get more monsters killed, it's quite risky, and you never know when that faithful Mesmerise spell may fizzle :D

Now that your pet has been made, give him some raw buffs, such as STR, AC, and Haste. Also give him a weapon or two. From here, there are a few main tactics you can try out (but there are many more). First is nuke, nuke, nuke. Second is to nuke and stun. Third is to let the pet do all the work. Here is how they work.

1. Nuke, nuke, nuke - (Note: I don't recommend this tactic, since it's relatively a bad habit for enchanters to get into, and it's not very reliable. HOWEVER, it kills the opponent fast). Make sure you have the following spells memorized: Your best DD spell, your 2nd best DD spell, a Root spell, a Mesmerise spell, a Tashan spell, a Color, and a Gate spell. The other slot is up to you. Make sure your pet has been buffed. Now Tashan the opponent. If possible, Root the opponent while it's running toward you. Once the opponent is immobilized, let loose with your strongest nuke. Now, while your nuke is recovering, use your 2nd best nuke. Now use your strongest nuke, etc. Chances. are that the opponent will break Root and hit you. This is when your pet will go in and attempt to tank. To get the opponent Rooted again, Stun him using a Color spell, and then Root while he is stunned. Now your pet will be on the opponent, and before too long, he will probably die if you don't help him. So, keep on nuking as before. If the Root breaks, stun and root again until the opponent is dead. After battle, you'll likely find yourself and your pet low on HP. This is mostly like a mage tactic.

2. Nuke and stun - (I use this one more myself). Make sure you have the following spells ready. Your best DD, a Color spell, a Spinning spell, a Mesmerise spell, a Gate spell, a Root spell and a Tashan spell. The other slot is up to you. Make extra sure your pet has buffs. First, let loose with your best Tashan spell. When the enemy hits you, your pet will rush in and start attacking. The trick here is to let your pet have some fun in the battle. However, enchanter pets have poor AC and HP, so you'll need to aleviate some of the damage being done. To do this, stun the opponent with a Color spell. Now while the Color is recharging and the effect has worn off, use a Spin spell. Once that effect has worn off, use Color again, and so on. Don't hesitate to Root if the MoB is VERY aggroed on you. By doing this, the enemy will not be hititng your pet so much, while your pet is whacking at the enemy. Now here's the other part - make sure you do more damage than your pet does, so you'll have to throw a periodic nuke at the enemy when you see fit. Some would call this tactic silly because it involves nuking, but the truth is that nuking is necessary sometimes in solo combat. This tactic also uses up little mana for the time it takes to kill the enemy, and is relatively easy on your pets and your HP.

3. Let your pet do all the work - (Recommended) Make sure these spells are on your list. Your highest Direct Damage spell, a Color spell, a Spinning spell, a Mesmerise spell, a Gate spell, a Root spell, a Tashan spell, and Memory Blur. Make sure you and your pet are fully buffed. Now the trick here is to let your pet do almost all of the work. This is similar to the Nuke and Stun tactic, except you won't nuke till the end of the battle. First Tashan the opponent, and make sure he gets a hit on you. Now Root, stun with color, and then stun with Spinning during recovery time and when Color has worn off. Keep switching back and forth while keeping the enemy Rooted. (During combat, I've seen people use a Weaken line spell for this tactic. However, I feel these spells are useless. I've also seen Rune used on the pet before and during fights, which can work if you bring lots of reagents). Now once the mob gets low on HP, Memory Blur it. This will make the mob forget any damage incurred by the pet. Now that you've done that, nuke the mob till it dies (try to do the job in one hit). This will give you all of the experience. The only problem with this tactic is that it's VERY slow, and I've had a couple of occasions where my DD was resisted and my pet ended up outdamaging me after blurring the creature. However, it's completely safe and makes you look like a good enchanter :)

There are many other tactics you can use. That is the point of the enchanter - an endless amount of possibilities come from playing these guys :)

***Important Notes***

Some of these may have all ready been mentioned, but let me index them.

Direct Damage spells - Highly resistable, long recovery time, all or no damage, act as a mega-taunt.

Pets - Relatively weak at tanking. Try to think of them as a DOT. All pet spells level 8 and above are puchased in High Keep.

Illusions - Twist around faction, however, religion and previous faction work still play a role. ILLUSIONS WEAR OFF IF YOU ZONE! Illusions also give the enchanter the advantages/disadvantages of the race. Here is a list

Humans, Erudites - Blind at night
Dwarves, Gnomes, Halflings - Make you smaller for better dungeon navigation, and grant invravision (As a Dwarf, you can sumersault if you jump forward)
Half Elves, Wood Elves, High Elves - Infravision
Dark Elves - Ultravision
Minor Illusion, Tree - Makes you 'no race'. Faction is based on religion and previous faction work. Blind at night.
Barbarian - Blind at night, may Slam
Ogre - Infravision, may Slam
Troll - Infravision, HP regeneration, may Slam
Spirit Wolf - Adds Haste, grants 'no race' faction
Skeleton - Hides nametag, KOS to guards. Good dungeon faction (does not help normal undead faction *shrugs*)
Drybone - Skeleton form with 25 SV Fire
Earth Elemental - KOS to guards, good dungeon faction, 5 to STR
Air Elemental - Levitation, KOS to guards, good dungeon faction
Water Elemental - Waterbreathing, KOS to gaurds, good dungeon faction
Fire Elemental - Damage shield (starts at 6), KOS to gaurds, good dungeon faction
Werewolf - Adds 80 ATK, good dungeon faction, KOS to guards.
Also note that some of the higher end illusion (and Halfling illusion) are located in Erudin's enchanter guild.

Memory Blur - Will not only remove hate list, but also forgets any damage incurred by a person.. Good tactic for KSing or powerleveling (I don't condone either).

Stat debuffs (ie weaken) - Relatively useless unless you are battling another player.

The Mesmerise line cannot be used on large creatures.

In closing...

The enchanter may be looked upon as weak, as they are in certain areas. Their damage output is very low, and their pets are weak. But proper manipulation of their spells will cause the enchanter to outperform any other class, it's just a matter of using the correct spell :)

Now go wear that guild robe with pride ;)

-Ruri Kagato
