Playing a Human Shadow Knight

 Starting Out   Gaining Levels   Later in Life   Conclusion 

Starting Out
Depending on your Deity, you start in Qeynos or Freeport. Both are huge cities, with places in which you can walk freely, and other places in which you should just keep your mouth shut. By all means do not go anywhere near the Cleric or Paladin guild houses. In North Freeport, Paladins walk the streets instead of guards... Once again, do not speak to them, unless you want to practice retrieving your corpse.

Now that you have been warned, look at that note in your inventory and quickly find your guild hall. Since you're a disliked class your guild hall is not easy to find. Once you find it, get your Guild Tunic and invest all of your skill points into 1-Handed Slashing, since Humans have no innate combat advantages to speak of. Now you're ready to brave the outside wilds of Rats and Snakes. Stick to these until you reach level 2, then mix in Decaying Skeletons and eventually Fire Beetles at 3. Remember to use Harm Touch when in a bind, which can deliver a blow which will oft times save your life. Use this ability sparingly, however, since it may only be activated once per Norrathian day.

Gaining Levels
Once you hit level 4, it's time to head out beyond your home zones. If you started in Freeport you can head to the East Commons for plenty of creatures to kill, or head to Northern Ro and hunt anything not in the desert area. If you started in Qeynos, you can now safely head into Qeynos Hills.

Once you make it to level 6 you can start messing with larger things. Do not bother in the East Commons, stick to Northern Ro and hunt the Orc Raiders that are found within. They should be a little difficult if they have weapons, but you have that Harm Touch saved in case. The Raiders are great for experience at this stage of your life, and they are great for cash. They carry on average 2 silver and occasionally weapons (these are generally Rusty Bastard Swords and Maces). You can even get lucky and find one toting a Wooden Shield along with some sort of sword. You will need all the money that you can get in order to obtain higher priced armor, so slay indiscriminately. As for Qeynos Hills, now would be a good time to start hanging around BlackBurrow and start smashing intermediate Gnolls.

 top


Later in Life
If you have been Raider-killing all along, you should have plenty of coin saved once you hit level 9. At this time, if you haven't done so already, you should be able to complete a full set of Leather Armor. 15 platinum should be enough to hit this mark. Look to get Cutthroat Insignia Rings, whether you win them yourself or buy them for 5 gold apiece. Magic is something that will seem really great at first, but it won't be a tremendous life saver at this time. You need to reach level 10 before you'll have 40 mana, which allows you to cast Leering Corpse (summons an undead pet) and, most importantly, Invisible vs. Undead ("IVU"). IVU will let you safely travel through the undead dungeon of Befallen. If someone shouts "train", you can 'cloak' and sit it out safely.

Also look for anyone who is selling Snakeskin Armor, as it's better than Leather and has added bonuses. (That is unless someone is selling Banded and you have he money to buy it, although this is highly unlikely at this point). The best way to make money at these intermediate to higher levels is to have developed a trade skill or by killing Orcs, Gnolls, and Skeletons, which are all great sources of loot since they carry weapons and money.

 top


Conclusion
Shadow Knights are quite possibly the hardest class to play because you have so many obstacles: plenty of NPC's who view Shadow Knights as KoS, difficult quests, slow level-gain, not being as strong as a Ranger or Warrior, yet not being as powerful as a Necromancer with his full array of evil spells. This may sound discouraging, but understand that, as a Shadow Knight, you're the Dark half of the Paladin. You gain power equal to them, if not greater. You do not have to kill a level 50 Red Dragon for your most powerful weapon. You can have a pet, which allows you to easily adventure solo. You also gain some very useful spells as your levels increase. In the end, any level 50 Shadow Knight with their best equipment can easily take any level 50 Paladin with their best equipment. So, if you have the will and the energy to invest into the unholiest of warriors, the Human Shadow Knight is definitely for you.

Good hunting. The life of a Shadow Knight is a hard one, but the rewards are so great that it proves that it pays to play evil.
