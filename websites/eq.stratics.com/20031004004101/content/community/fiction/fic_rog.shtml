Fiction: The Rogue - by Ed "Darter Elfgard" Haas

 Part I   Part II   Part III   Part IV   Part V   Part VI   Part VII 

Part I
As you walk into the Fool's Gold Tavern, you are amused at the sight of a seedy-looking halfling standing on a bar stool drinking his ale. You hear a melody in the background which, in another world, would be recognized as a famous song created by the The Stones That Roll...

The halfling turns to you, raises his honey dew ale in salute, and says:

Please allow me to interduce meself, I be a halfling o' stealth, and chased. I been all 'round dis continent, Takin' what suits me taste.

Pleezameecha, me name is Hiller Dain, Pleezameecha, thievin' is me game.

Hiller gives you a broad wink and finishes his ale in one gulp. "Set yer mind at ease, friend, fer in da world o' Norrath I kin nae steal from ye, lessen we both be open ta bein' kilt by others o' our kind. And, as ye can readily see, I gots no intentions o' bein' kilt in such a manner. Bristlebane forbid! There's enough murderin' monsters and such as it is, widout takin' such unnecessary chances... heh."

He reaches up and claps you on the elbow. "So, rest easy, and have a seat. Yer givin' me a crick in me neck from havin' ta look up at ya. Bartender! Bring dis smelly troll a drink!"

The halfling watches you with a twinkle in his eyes and chuckles. "So, tell me 'bout dis team yer formin' up, we goin' after gems, gold, er what? Erm...we don't havta cross no oceans, do we?"

Out of Character: What Hiller Dain said is true: a rogue cannot steal from a -pvp character--no way, no how, not gonna happen. Doesn't matter if he is +pvp or -pvp. Can't steal from a -pvp character. Also, a -pvp rogue can't even steal from a +pvp character. The only way a rogue can steal from another player character is if they are both +pvp. Here's an exerpt from the Everquest FAQ:

11.4 Can I steal or pickpocket with a -pvp character? Wouldn't that make it impossible for people to retaliate?

You may not (underlining is mine) steal from another character if you are -pvp. You can, however, steal from NPCs. If you are +pvp, you may steal from both +pvp players and NPCs.



Part II
The troll and the halfling passed the evening in the Fool's Gold Tavern, discussing plans for the upcoming quest. As the sun set behind the mountain, the bartender walked around lighting candles.

"Um, ya sed ye no kin steal frum me, right?" asked the troll shaman.

"Aye, 'tis true. Nor can any rogue."

"Why dat?"

"Why?" Bleary eyed and a bit woozy, the halfling rogue tried to focus on the troll. He shrugged his shoulders and said, "Some say that Bristlebane once tried to pick Erolissi Marr's pocket and Tarew Marr caught 'im at it. Tarew brought 'im before the council of gods ta answer fer his crime. As punishment, Bristlebane and all of his followers lost the ability to pick the pockets of anyone wi' a soul who chose not to do battle wi' others of their kind. But we can still pick the pockets o' those wi' no soul."

"Dose wid no soul?" the troll asked.

"Aye," the halfling replied. Like our friend the bartender." He waggled his thumb in the bartender's direction.

The troll eyed the bartender. "Sumpin wrong wid dat guy."

Hiller nodded. "Haven't ye got any like him in yer village?"

The troll furrowed his brow in concentration. "Um... yup." He suddenly grabbed his head with both hands. "Ow, dat hurt!

The halfling's eyes twinkled, but he managed to keep a straight face. "Anyway, I don't really know why we can only pick certain pockets. Heh, ye might as well ask me why we keep comin' back from da dead, or why the sun sets over that mountain and not the one over yonder. Mayhap a great mage could tell ye." The halfling rogue turned his gaze to the candle and stared into the flame...

Out of Character: I've read a lot of discussion lately about how to justify some the aspects of online roleplaying games so that they fit in a "pure" roleplaying context, i.e., how do we justify resurrection?

Maybe it's time we broke the "pure" role playing mold, or at least re-shaped it a little. Instead of trying to justify our resurrection and other abilities we are given in an orpg, maybe we should tailor our roleplaying to fit the framework of the world as it is...

Remember what the gods of Norrath have told us: "You're in Our World Now".



Part III
Back in his room at the tavern that evening, the halfling rogue sat on the edge of his bed. He stared at the candle flame on his night stand, deep in thought. Hunched over the table, he dipped his quill into an inkwell and and began to write.

Dear Mayor,

I have been able to follow the trail of the raiding party as far as the Fool's Gold Tavern. According to the bartender, a group of ruffians fitting the kidnappers' descriptions arrived here day before yesterday and spent the night. They left early this morning, heading north.

I have dispatched a post to Darter Elfgard in hopes that he may join in our efforts to find your daughter. He is an excellent tracker, and his talents are sorely needed.

Also, I have made contact with a troll shaman who is traveling north in search of a magickal artifact. We depart at dawn for the Halas region.

Rest assured that I will not falter in my efforts to recover your lovely and gracious Morningdew.

Your ever faithful servant,

Sir Hillsworth Tangletoes
Knight Errant Order of the Golden Oak
aka Hiller Dain

Hiller blew gently on the parchment, then rolled it up and slipped it in a tube and capped it. He rose and walked to the open window. He drew a small wooden cylinder from his pocket and brought it to his lips. A high, piercing whistle was heard. A moment later, a giant bat glided up and perched on the sill. The halfling tied the tube onto the bat's leg and sent it on its way. "Fly straight and true, leatherwings, and fly safe above all." Hiller closed his window, walked back to his bed, turned down the covers and snuffed out the candle.

Out of Character: What valid roleplaying reasons can we give for our chosen races to be out of their usual stomping grounds?

  1. You are looking for a missing person.
  2. You are a spy.
  3. You are a diplomatic courier.
  4. You're searching for riches beyond your wildest dreams.
  5. You are trying to enlist aid to stave off an invasion of your clan/homeland.
  6. You are a bounty hunter.
You may even combine one or more of these to include a hidden agenda, i.e., your party thinks you're out for riches, but you're really searching for a kidnap victim.


Part IV
The halfling and the troll set out at sunrise the next morning, turning north and trekking toward the frigid plain. As they approached a high mountain pass, the troll stopped and turned completely around, sniffing the air. He began to rumble deep in his chest.

"Me smell elf," he said.

"All right, ," said the halfling rogue."Don't go berserker on me. If it's who I think it is, we're gonna need him."

"Me no like elf," said the troll. "Me HATE elf!"

Hiller climbed up on a boulder and stared Trukk in the eye. "Lissen. Just how bad da ye want dat Eye of Uggluk, anyway? All ye know is some legend about dis Erudite mage named Miragul who came and conned yer people out of some artifact...and maybe, maybe went into the northlands wid it. If ye want any chance at all of finding da eye, we are gonna need a ranger. And dis particular elf is one o' da best!"

Trukk's eyes blazed as he looked at Hiller. He sighed deeply, then shrugged his hairy shoulders. "Ya. Me need eye more den blood of elf. But if not da one we want, me squash like fire beetle."

Hiller looked Trukk in the eye once more, then said, "Stay here, I'll see who it is. I'll come back fer ye when I know fer sure. Erm, which way?"

The troll pointed off the trail into the pine forest.

"Remember what I said. We need him. I'll be back as soon as I know." Hiller jumped off the boulder and headed into the forest. As he hiked deeper into the forest, Hiller wondered how in the world he was going to keep these two from killing each other, half hoping that it wasn't his friend that the troll had smelled. He came upon a small glen and peered through the bushes.

He felt a feathery touch on his ear and spun around. There stood his friend Darter Elfgard, twirling an arrow in his hand and grinning impishly.

"Looking for someone?" Darter asked.

"Darter!" The halfling gave him a bear hug and clapped him on the back. "Good to see ya, old friend!"

"And you, Hilly! Hows the missus?"

"Fine, fine. Still makin' da best tarts in Rivervale." Hiller smiled, then grew somber. "I am glad ye got my post. I was hopin' ye wouldn't be too busy ta help."

"I could nae deny ye, my friend. After all, I owe ye my life. Have ye got any more clues on the whereabouts of the missing girl?"

"Only that da raiding party was spotted heading north. Erm, I joined up wid someone else who happens to be going that way..." Hiller puffed up his cheeks and shuffled his feet.

"Aye, and?" asked Darter.

Hiller eyed Darter carefully. "Aye, and...well, erm, he is really a great shaman and quite strong, and...where we're headed, we need that kind of back up..."

"Aye, so?" Darter shrugged his shoulders. "I have naught against barbarians."

Hiller blinked. "Well, that's just it...heh...he nae a barbarian. He, well, he is a tr.."

Darter exploded. "Troll! He is carrion!" Darter frenziedly started checking Hiller's trail. "Where is he? He dies now!"

The halfling tackled the elf and held him down. "No, Dart, no! I told ya, we need his skills to find da girl and get out safely!"

"We'll find some other way," Darter shouted. "We nae need any stinking troll wi' us!"

"Yes we do! We can't do this by ourselves! Remember, Dart, ya do owe me!"

Darter went still. "Aye, I owe ye. I'll do this. But then my debt is paid. And I'll nae be seein' ye again. Our friendship ends wi' the payment of the debt. Understood?"

"Aye, understood," Hiller sadly replied.

The wood elf pushed the halfling off of him and stalked down the backtrail. Hiller hurried after him.

Out of Character: How do you justify seemingly incompatable races joining together? Obviously, you have to have very compelling reasons:

  1. They make a truce in order to accomplish a quest
  2. They temporarily unite to fight a common enemy
  3. They unite because they have no other choice
I wonder how the grouping of characters of different alignments will affect their alignments in Norrath. Would a troll become "more good" and lose the respect of his clan? Would an elf become "more evil" and find he is unwelcome in "good" society? Hmm...


Part V
Gulping for air, Hilly finally caught up with Darter on the way to the main trail. "Lissen, Dart, ye kin take point. Then ye nae be needin' ta rub elbows wi' da troll. And I promise, soon as da kidnappers' trail splits wi' da direction da troll is headin', we part company wi' him."

Darter stared at the mountains for awhile, then nodded his head in agreement. He stepped off the trail and disappeared into the woods. Hilly looked after his friend, sighed, and headed back to the troll. As Hilly came upon the main trail, he saw Trukk fashioning a club from an oak branch. "It was me friend, and he has joined up wi' us," Hilly said.

The troll glanced at the rogue and said, "I know dat." He pointed his club north. "Him dat way, not far." He finished his club and swung it a few times, testing its heft and balance. He went down on one knee and held the club out with both hands. "Tunare, bless me club, may it strength be great an it aim be true." He touched the ground with his club then brought it to his forehead. Then he rose and said, "We go."

As they made their way farther into the mountains, the trees began to thin out. Miniature waterfalls of frozen streams could be seen here and there, colored green and red and purple from the minerals in the water. Walking around a bend in the trail, they noticed a lone figure leaning on a sword.

The troll hefted his club and roared. "I be Trukk, shaman uv Grobb! Move or do battul!" The barbarian warrior threw back her head and laughed. "I am Huntra, warrior of Halas, and you're soiling my road with your presence!"

Hilly sidled to the left, trying to flank the warrior. "One more step and it may be your last, shorty," the barbarian grinned. The rogue froze.

Trukk roared again and stomped his foot, knocking the warrior on her rear. Hilly managed to keep his feet.

"Ah, so that is the way of it, then?" Huntra said as she stood back up. "Well, so be it. We fight!"

"Yup," said Trukk. "Ta deat' or ta yeilding?"

She looked at the two scruffy travelers. "You don't look much like you have anything worth dying for, so we fight to yielding or not at all, agreed?"

Trukk charged her and said, "Yup." He took a mighty swing at her head, and made contact with...nothing. The warrior had somersaulted forward as he ran past.

She slashed savagely behind her as she came up on one knee, gouging Trukk's thigh deeply. Trukk screamed and whirled around. He swung downward, trying to pound her into the ground with his club.

Huntra twisted to the right and held her sword point up, jabbing the troll in the wrist. Trukk moaned and gripped the club one handed. He feinted a jab to her belly and twirled the club up and over, smashing the warrior in the forehead. She instantly dropped her weapon, dazed, and fell to the ground. "I yield," she panted. " I cannot fight further. Take my sword, it's yours by right of victory."

Trukk cast heal on himself and the warrior as he loudly proclaimed his victory. "Yuh fight gud," he told the woman. "Yuh gib me honor in battul." He reached for her two-handed sword.

Just then another warrior stepped from behind a rock. "Not so fast, ye smelly pile of dragon dung! Now ye got to deal with me! I am Volgar, warrior of Halas, and clan-mate to Huntra! We fight!" He twirled his battle axe over his head.

"I think not," said Darter as he stood beside a lone pine, sighting along his arrow at Volgar's heart. Suddenly his eyes widened.

"Drop!" He yelled and loosed his arrow.

The warrior dropped as a spear just missed his head, slicing his ear. The air of Darter's passing arrow fanned his hair as it flew on and buried itself in the chest of the snow orc.

"To arms!" he cried. "Orcs all around! Trukk to the center to heal! Fight for yer lives!" Darter sprinted to the center of the trail, shouting and firing, while the others formed a circle around the troll.

Slashing and firing, the ring of fighters took on the oncoming snow orcs. Between healing casts Trukk managed to smash any heads that broke through the circle. As guickly as they had come, the orcs departed, leaving three dead behind them.

Hilly quickly searched the dead and distributed the spoils. "Dart, keep an eye out fer me while I disarm and pick the lock on this chest I found?"

"Aye," said Darter.

The rogue went off a ways and carefully examined the chest. He discovered a poison trap, which he deftly disarmed. He then took out his set of lockpicks and went to work. Presently a soft *click* was heard. Grinning broadly, Hilly opened the chest.

"Whoa! Lookit this!" he exclaimed. The others gathered around. Inside was a deep red ruby the size of Hiller's fist. "This what ye been lookin' fer, Trukk?"

The troll stared at the ruby and shook his head. "Nup, but it could be its liddle brudder."

Hilly's jaw fell. "Bigger then this, eh? Somethin' like that has got ta have some serious protection around it..."

The barbarian warriors glanced at each other, impressed. They nodded to each other and smiled grimly. Huntra spoke up. "Say, if you are out for more like this, you may need some fighters...mind if we tag along?"

The rogue looked questioningly from Trukk to Darter.

"Yuh fight gud. Ok wid me."

"Aye," said Darter. "Anything to dilute the smell of troll." He strode off toward the north.

The rogue stared after the elf's retreating back, then turned to the barbarians. "I am Hiller Dain, rogue of Rivervale, and that ill tempered drake whelp is Darter Elfgard, ranger of Kelethin. Erm, ye wouldn't be knowing anything of a so-called 'Eye of Uggluk', would ye? 'Tis a magickal artifact that was taken from Trukk's clan."

The barbarians looked at each other and shrugged their shoulders. Huntra said, "We haven't heard of it by name, but we have heard legends of a cache of magickal items that is supposed to be hidden somewhere in the frigid plain. Would that be what yer looking for?" "Mebbe," said the troll. "One way tuh find out. Lead da way."

The warriors turned and headed north, with the rogue and the troll bringing up the rear.

Out of Character: What if we could challenge each other to a duel in EQ, -pvp and +pvp alike, without change of status?

  • What if we could duel anywhere in Norrath?
  • What if we could choose to either "auto yield" after losing 2/3 of our health points or to fight to the death?
  • What if, when we choose "auto-yield", the fight would end as soon as one of the duelists lost 2/3 of their health points, and that was all the health they would lose?
  • What if the loser of an "auto-yield" duel would drop the most valuable item they have, and it could only be picked up by the winner as loot?
  • Finally, what if npcs carried booby-trapped and locked chests which only rogues could disarm any unlock?
Why, then:
  • Paladins could duel with Necromancers.
  • Highwaymen could challenge travelers.
  • Anyone could battle anyone.
Without fear of dying and loss of all items. And, finally, rogues could be more valued members of EQ. Imagine the possibilities...


Part VI
With Darter and the barbarians in the lead, the party trekked northward until early afternoon. They were deep in snow country now, and the air was still and cold. They deciced to stop early to hunt for food and to be fresh for the next day's journey. They found a suitable campsite and prepared to spend the night. Darter and Hilly went in search of firewood while the troll and the barbarbarians went hunting for game.

"So, Dart, what has yer tracking told ye so far?" asked Hiller.

"The party we seek continues to the north and the east. From what the barbarians say, that is the very direction of the legendary cache." Darter grimaced. "Seems we will be stuck with the troll for some time to come."

"Aye, seems that way." replied the rogue. "But ya gotta admit, ye'd be dead now if he hadn't healed ye once er twice during that orc attack."

"True, but he is still a troll."

Hilly shrugged his shoulders as they headed back with thier armloads of wood.

The hunting party returned as they were preparing the fire. Trukk had a mammoth calf draped over his shoulders. They quickly cleaned the beast and cut strips to smoke over the fire, and cooked the haunches for that evening's meal.

After the meal everyone settled around the fire, lost in their own thoughts. Darter unslung his lute from over his shoulder, tuned it, and began to play softly. In a tenor as clear and pure as a temple bell on a crisp autumn morning, he began to sing:

The lady with the beautiful eyes
She took his heart by surprise
And now he loves that lady
The lady with the beautiful eyes

It was just a moment's flirtation
He thought it was just a whim
But 'twas Erolissi's greatest creation
The rebirth of love within him

The lady with the beautiful eyes She took his heart by surprise
And now he loves that lady
The lady with the beautiful eyes

And though a rocky road may be waiting Mayhap filled with many pitfalls
His love will never be fading
He'll be there whenever she calls

The lady with the beautiful eyes She took his heart by surprise
And now he loves that lady...
that lovely lady The lady...
with the beautiful...eyes

"Ah, Darter, me friend, ye gots da heart o' a bard." sighed Hilly.

The ranger nodded. "I have played in the bard guild's performance hall a time or two. 'Tis an enjoyable pastime. The bards are a lively bunch, and they love to sing a song and tell a story now and then. Why, I have seen many a rogue, cleric and warrior ask to be invited into the hall to listen in on the performances, and even join in on occasion." He slung his lute back over his shoulder and stared into the fire.

Hiller looked shrewdly at Darter. "She musta been some lady, ta inspire ye ta write such a song."

"She was, indeed." Darter looked sad, and somehow deeply pained.

The rogue decided to change the subject in an effort to divert his friend's thoughts. "Erm, Dart, I kin understand yer clan's dislike fer trolls, but Trukk here..."

Darter turned and looked at his friend with haunted eyes. "Ye wonder why I feel the way I do? Well, I suppose tis only right I tell ye...

'Twas during the mere threshold of spring, that time of year when trees shed small branches and twigs like winter fur, and robins joust for territorial rights. The day dawned unusually warm and clear, showing the promise of summer to come. My beautiful Gethsemane and I decided to take our son Merlock on a picnic 'neath the sacred evergreen. My brother and his wife joined us.

Merlock was no more than a toddler, and Flitter and I were teaching him some basics of forest lore while my lady and Morgana were laying out the food.

Suddenly, Flitter started as though pricked by a thorn. He climbed up the great evergreen. Looking to the north, his visage paled and he scrambled back down to the ground.

"They come," he said. "Trolls on the rampage. Prepare for defense of our sacred tree."

Gethsemane grabbed up Merlock and hid him in a hole in the bole of the tree, then drew her sword and took a place beside me.

We had not long to wait.

They came upon us with the fury and force of a late winter storm, thundering into the clearing, their lightning fires burning the brush all around us. Gethsemane took a spear in the first wave. Later, Morgana fell under an axe meant for Flitter. We fought on for what seemed like hours.

Darter stopped his tale and stared into the glowing embers of the fire, swallowing hard. A solitary silent tear slid down the side of his cheek. "Sometimes I think that the only thing that kept us going was our rage and our burning desire to have vengeance on those who had murdered our loved ones...

And Flitter! Flitter was an inspiration to me. He was everywhere and nowhere at once. He fought like a firedrake! To this day his sword is known as Eyothewer, for it did carve an island in the sea of battle.

Finally, we began to weaken. I was down with a troll ready to deal me a death blow, and there was Flitter, slicing his head off with one swing of his singing sword, using his last ounce of strength.

There was but one troll left, and neither of us had the strength to raise hand 'gainst it. We looked each other in the eyes as the troll raised his club for a crushing blow. Suddenly, the troll blinked, shuddered, and fell dead.

Young Merlock, barely a toddler, had lept from his hiding place and pierced the troll's spinal cord with his whittling knife.

Darter let out a deep, shuddering sigh. "So. Now ye know."

"Two sides ta dat story. Me brudder..." the troll muttered.

The ranger glared at Trukk. "What ye say, troll?"

"Me can no talk like yuh can, elf, but me can show yuh..." The troll began a low rumbling chant, staring into the fire and slapping his palms on the ground in a steady, throbbing beat. "Tun Ah reh, Tun Ah re, Tun Ah reh," he chanted over and over. Slowly, gradually, the fire seemed to grow brighter and brighter, until it became their whole world. The flames seemed to take on shape and substance, until they became figures, and the figures clarified into the forms of trolls. Trolls marching, no...not quite a march...more like a procession. There was somehow an air of majesty and mystery about them as the shuffled on their path. They mumbled deep in their throats as they moved along. The mumbles grew louder and stronger, until they were clearly heard. "We come..ta honor..da dead." Over and over they chanted as they marched toward their holy ground.

The figures grew smaller as more of the landscape became visible, and was recognized as a swampland. But something was wrong with the swamp, it was not quite a swamp. It was dying, drying up. Many spots that once were pools of stagnant water were no more than dried up depressions in the ground, filled with dead fish and cracked plates of dusty dirt.

The procession moved on until it reached its destination. The trolls looked around in dismay. Their holy mud pits were gone, turned into sun baked bricks, deviod of water or life.

One of the trolls roared in rage and began to search the area. He followed the path of the dried up streambed and discovered a dam. A dam guarded by elves. He made his way back to his comrades. He yelled, "Da elves destroy our holy ground! We destroy dere holy tree!" Screaming in rage and hatred, they charged off .

The fire flared up, and the vision was no more. The flames died down and once again became glowing embers.

Darter blushed as he looked at the shaman. "We didn't know..."

Trukk nodded. "Yuh didn't know. Enemies neber talk. Only fight."

Volgar cleared his throat. "Say, Trukk, your clan honors the dead, but our dead come back. Don't your people?" The troll nodded. "Dey come back. One time. Two time. More dan two time. But sometime, dey don't come back" Trukk looked at Darter. "Me brudder die in dat battul. He don't come back. Yur Ges'me, she come back?"

Darter frowned and shook his head no.

"So we honor dose dat don't come back. Now our holy pits gone. Do dey come back?" Trukk rose and strode away from the fire. The others moved off and prepared to bed down for the night.

Out of Character: I will always be a bard at heart, and I know from experience that bards like to congregate and share their songs and stories, even if they are not "professional" bards, i.e. they may be a different class. The best example of this I have found had a place where bards could go in their guild hall and perform. Others classes could enter on an invitation only basis. They would ring a chime and shout a request to enter. Then any bard of a certain level (above level 5 ?) could go and escort them in. It was a fun experience, and gave me a haven from the constant battle for survival that is inherent in the leveling process. Something else on the role playing side: I like the idea of having role players as guest gms for role playing quests. From my understanding of pen and paper rpg's (never played one, by the way), this may be the closest an online rpg can come to that style of play. If these rpg guest gms control the direction and content of the questing party, they can filter out the "cool doodz" and allow those who truly try to role play to participate in role playing quests within the game. For instance, role playing quests are posted on special role playing bulletin boards throughout the cities of Norrath, with dates, times, party assembly points and rp gms' names. All interested participants be there. Maybe you could have people sign up up in advance so you could plan how many gm's you need in a certain area. Those details could be worked out. Guest gms have power to boot those who are detrimental to the rp spirit of the quest. Some guest gms would be party members (say, one per party), others could be adversaries. Guest gms would be given the powers they need to carry out their roles within the quest, and would revert to their regular character levels, plus whatever exp and loot they gained during the quest at its completion. Just a thought...



Part VII
The party of adventurers rose early the next morning. The air grew colder and colder as they continued north and east. Darter began to slow down as the weather took its toll on his endurance. Noticing his plight, Trukk cast endure cold on him. Darter nodded silently and plodded onward.

Their journey was not easy, nor without mishap. They often slipped and fell on the icy terrain. They encountered giant white spiders, snow orcs, mammoths and even a polar bear. They defeated them all and shared the loot equally. Once they saw what they thought was a moving iceberg. It was a frost giant. They hid as it passed them by. Finally they came to a range of rocky outcrops. Among them they found a crevice. In the crevice they discovered an entrance.

Stamping their feet and rubbing their hands, they gathered around to discuss their options. Hilly asked Volgar if this resembled the secret cache of their legends. Volgar nodded. "Aye, as near as I can tell. Only one way to find out. Go in."

The rogue turned to the ranger. "What do ye think?"

Darter raised an eyebrow and nodded in agreement.

"Trukk?" asked Hilly.

"We go," the troll replied.

Cautiously, they entered the cave. They worked their way deeper and deeper into the cave, with Darter in the lead, then Volgar, Trukk, Huntra and finally Hiller in the rear. Trukk cast night vision on Huntra and Volgar as he noticed them stumbling in the dark. Suddenly, Darter came to a halt. He signaled the others to gather round and pointed beyond. There stood an ice giant, five or six times as tall as the ranger. Darter signaled to the left, and the party snuck around the unsuspecting giant.

Deeper and deeper they went, hiding from an occasional snow goblin, working their way down into the caves. Finally, they became aware of a low moaning, almost like wind in a small crack. As they traveled further, the moaning grew louder. Searching the area, they found a small opening almost completely covered by a fallen boulder. The moaning seemed to be coming from the opening.

"Do we go on, or find the source of the sound?" asked Darter.

"It could be someone in need of help," replied Hilly. He looked meaningfully at his friend. "I think we should find out."

Trukk shrugged his shoulders and nodded. With a grunt he began to push at the boulder blocking the way. His muscles bulged until they looked like they would split his hide. The boulder finally gave way as he rolled it to one side. Darter got down on his stomach and tried to crawl in to the opening. Small as he was, he couldn't fit . "Let me try," whispered Hilly. It was a tight fit, but he managed to wriggle in.

The further Hilly crawled in the tight passage, the louder the moaning got. The air became warmer and the passage grew lighter. He crawled around a bend and suddenly the passage opened up into a large chamber. Torches lit the chamber in a blood red glow. He wiggled out of the crevice and stood up.

The walls of the chamber were stacked with chest after chest, some of them opened and revealing more gems and coins than Hiller had ever seen. It would take an army of trolls a week to clear the chamber of all the riches he saw before him. His heart beat wildly as he looked around him. He giggled uncontrollably, grinning like an idiot. Once again he heard the moan. He turned in the direction of the sound and saw the halfling maiden tied spreadeagled to a blood stained altar.

He hurried over to her. "Morningdew, its me, Hilly! Are you all right?"

"Oh, I am perfectly well, Sir Hillsworth Tangletoes, I just thought I would lay here and get some beauty rest!" She glared at her dubious savior. "Now would you be so kind as to untie me and get me out of this demon's abode?" Hilly blushed and cut her bonds with his dagger.

The maiden jumped up, grabbed a short sword from one of the chests, and ran to what looked like a blank wall. She pushed an oddly shaped brick and the wall slid open to reveal a doorway. She yelped and jumped back on guard as Trukk and the others rushed into the chamber.

"Not to worry, m'lady, these are my companions." She looked from Trukk to Hiller and said. "Some companions! Is this the best you could do for the mayor's daughter?"

Hiller blushed and nodded. He entertained thoughts of tying her back on the altar.

Suddenly, Trukk gasped and rushed over to an open golden box. Cradled in blue velvet was the largest Ruby any of them had ever seen. "Da eye uv Ugglukk! I have it back!" While he reached out for the stone, the others ran around grabbing as much loot as they could stuff in their packs and pockets.

From out of nowhere a blood curdling scream was heard. "Who dares to defile my sacred chamber! Your souls will shrivel and burn in the lava pits!" The party members blanched and looked deeper into the chamber. The smell of brimstone filled their nostrils as a column of swirling smoke formed against the far wall and solidified into the shape of a...

"Gahhhhhh!" Darter screamed. "Liiiiccchhhhh! Runnnn!" The entire party shrieked and bolted for the entrance.

Hearts pounding and blood pumping, the adventurers ran for their lives through the underground passageways. Snow goblins took up the alarm and chased after them, with the lich also in hot pursuit. Trukk cast slow on the lead goblin. The goblin ran slower and slower, blocking the way of those behind him. The party rushed onward toward the cave entrance and safety. They burst into the entrance chamber.

Huntra screamed in rage in frustration as she ran right into the leg of the ice giant and fell back. The ice giant roared and took a swing at her. She rolled to the right, dodging his blow. Volgar shouted his battle cry and sliced the giant's arm. Darter unslung his bow and began firing at the pursuing orcs. "Get out of here, Hilly, ye got what ye came for!" "Nay, my friend, I'll not leave ye!" Hiller replied.

Darter grabbed him by the collar and looked him in the eye. "Listen, get this through your thick skull. Morningdew might not make it back without your help. You have to go, and now, while there is still time." Hilly stubbornly shook his head and threw a dagger at the oncoming orcs.

"Trukk!" yelled Darter, "Take this hardheaded runt and his charge and get out while ye still can! Go now!" Trukk looked Darter in the eye and nodded. "Yur a brave fighter, Darter Elfgard."

Trukk grabbed Hilly and Morningdew. Hilly struggled out of his grasp and clutched his old friend. "Darter, promise me...promise me ye'll come back." Darter looked haunted. "Promise me now!"

"We shall see, my friend, we shall see." Darter turned and continued to fire.

Trukk cast strength on the warriors and the ranger, grabbed Hilly and literally flew out between the ice giant's legs. Hilly looked over at Morningdew, then turned and watched behind in horror as the lich entered the fray, casting magicks he had never before seen. They flew onward as the clash of weapons and the screams of battle grew weaker and weaker. Finally, they could be heard no more.

The End
