Playing a Barbarian Warrior

 Starting Out   Advancing   Big n' Strong   Conclusion 

Ok kiddies I've played a few classes since p4 started and I must say that I've come to like this race/class about the best. Must be my Scottish blood and the kilts. There has to be a piper hidden somewhere in Halas.

Starting Out

Well once you allocate you starting stats, get into the game and make a beeline for the 'Pit of Doom', that's where you'll find the warrior guildmaster. Give him your note and make sure you have your starting weapon equipped. Train all of your 5 points into one handed slashing.

Head out of Halas and once you get past the tents at the entrance to Everfrost Peaks, you're ready to start laying some whupass on the local population of gnoll pups and ice goblin whelps. Which by the way are the best newbie creatures I've found in the game. Why? I'll tell you: THEY HAVE MONEY! And sometimes they have bags and boxes on them, which are expensive when starting out. Just down from the camp, you don't need to wander very far to find these creatures. Start out taking them on one at a time, never getting too far from the guard zone at the entrance to Halas. Those little buggers can pop outta nowhere, and next thing you know you're fighting three of them at once.

 top


Advancing Levels
Once you get to about level 2 or 3 start looking for Decaying Skeletons. If you see one with a weapon, NAIL HIM! I didn't buy a weapon till 10th level because the skels were such a great source of them.

At 3rd level you can also start looking for polarbear cubs, when you kill these, there is a chance you'll get a ruined bear pelt. Pelts are important as that's what large size patchwork armour is made from. Either get someone to make you some armour from the pelts or buy a tailoring kit to make for yourself.

Keep this up to about level 5. By that time you're ready to start taking on the plains. Head out there and find nice group. 4 to 5 players of 5th level can take out a polarbear and they're great for exp.

 top


Getting Big and Strong
Once you hit about 7th level you're ready for BlackBurrow. BB is a dungeon that connects west Qeynos and Everfrost. BB is also a train station. Players are constantly starting trains of 6 gnolls 2 gnoll guardsmen, and such. If someone yells "TRAIN!" run for the exits, before you get run over. In BB you are best of to start trying to kill scrawny gnolls, and work your way up from there. Once you've mastered BB, you'll be in the 12th level to 15th level range and the world is your oyster.
 top


Conclusion
This class kicks ass for my style of 'get in there and slug it out' play. I don't know about higher levels, but Barbarian warriors are fairly easy to play, which means you can spend some time and roleplay. It's also not so hard to solo with (as long as you can find somewhere safe to sit and heal up.)

Have fun I hope it helps and remember, if this guide helped you at all, next time you see a Barbarian warrior named Munro or Mithras on Ferrin Ro, say "Hi".

 top
