Brad on the "Old Game"
[ 2:55 PM PDT ] - Yendi
Found on this thread of the Everquest Forums:

Kunark is split into two apects: Levels past 50, and the Kunark zones. Both have been extremely well done, and both exhibit an intellegence and creativity that your past work didn't even hint at. The new kunark zones and the amazing capabilities of characters past level 50 make the rest of the game pale in comparison.

Now we've gotten to the subject of my post: What about the rest of the game? Kunark is stunning but there are still zones in the "old game" that aren't even near fully utilized.

Will work on the "old game" be abandoned in favor of doting on the new and expensive-to-create Kunark?

Will we ever see graphical enhancements to the "old game" to bring it at least a little more in-line with the current majesty of Kunark? Will "The Hole" ever open!? Will Odus ever be worth going to? Will NPC's which previously just stood around (and there are a lot of them - the majority in fact) saying nothing when you hailed them ever start saying things?

First, thanks for the compliments -- Kunark was an opportunity to learn from our mistakes and do better. And in the future, we'll top Kunark.

Now, to your questions.

1. No, we will not be updating the graphics on the old zones. This is primarily for two reasons: a. Resources are limited and I really think most of our customers would rather see new content as opposed to re-doing older content, and b., as was mentioned in this thread, it's not really appropriate to change the system requirements on the existing lands, which upgrading the graphics significantly would. That said, we have at least one minor way we're going to be upgrading the original world soon.

2. The 'old game' will definitely NOT be abandoned. The EQ Live team will continue to address crowding issues by revamping the lesser used dungeons and other zones. In addition, many of the zone mechanics that are being tried in Kunark, should they prove successful, will be used in the older zones when they are revamped as well.

3. The Hole is still on target for May, probably late May. After the Hole comes the Warrens, which is also on Odus. And we will continue to add free zones after that.

4. Yes, I think slowly but surely existing NPCs will be made more interesting, possibly involved in quests and storylines.

Hope that helps,

-Brad

>>>Seems that the skill cap increases for our primary skills (HTH , 1HB, Offense, Defense, etc)have run into a hard cap of 252. I know this must be a programming issue, but it has had the effect of weakening us relative to our melee brethren (ie, Warrior offense increase from 200 to 240 - Monk offense increase from 240 to 252.)

We're beginning to dislike the "King of..." and "Master of.." titles that have been floating around. The only real important thing here is that some classes must be better and worse at some things than other classes. One class need not be the master of something. Maybe instead they are second-best at a few things.

In any case, monks will still do more melee damage in a toe-to-toe fight than the others.

Midnight Gordon
Official_EverQuest_Forums [ 6:07 AM PDT ] - Baladur
Gordon replied to this message from the Official EverQuest Forums:


>>>Why doesn't Verant put together a team of experienced Gamers (GMs, Roleplayers, Beta Testers as a balancing team for EQ. They could come to a consesus on various issues relating to Character skills, abilities and spells. They wouldn't have direct control over the design/code of EQ. Instead, they would submit their findings to the design/code team. Together aggree upon what would be best for EQ overall. Instead of just the whims of a few, EQ could draw upon a vast pool of RPG knowledge/experience.

Your assumption seems to be that a "team of experienced gamers" and the "design/code team" are two different groups of people. Everyone on the design/coding team *is* an experienced gamer, and *is* experienced with EQ from the perspective of a player as well. In addition to this, we also rely on the opinions of those on the Test Server, *and* the opinions of EQ players who make suggestions in game and on boards such as this.

"Consensus" however is not an easy thing to find in a large group. Certainly people can agree on broad concepts such as "My class is too weak", but the method for addressing the concept varies greatly.

>>>For example, most think a Ranger should be the most powerfull Archer in the game having only to rely on his blade for close encounters

I'd have to disagree that "most" think this. I would agree that "most" think that archery should be more powerful, but I don't think that people believe that rangers should be archers first, and melee second. Human nature is to want to have all of the advantages, and none of the disadvantages, or in other words, to have your cake and eat it to. Given that rangers are balanced, perhaps a better question would be how many rangers would be willing to lose (meaning that they'd no longer be able to do things that they can now) melee abilities in exchange for stronger archery?

Being honest up front, though the question is more fair than just asking "how many want stronger archery", it's not something that would be done for EQ at this point. We honestly are not interested in drastically redefining a class at this point, and a redefine is what would be necessary to make an "Archer" class.

-Gordon

Kunark Framerates
EverQuest_Newsgroups [ 9:26 AM PDT ] - Baladur
Almost missed this info from Brad posted last night on the Official EverQuest Forums


Just to clear up this rumor:
The trees swaying have NOTHING to do with framerate.

Two things affect framerate: number of polygons drawn, and fill rate (the number of objects in front of other objects in front of other objects, all of which have to be drawn).

Framerate goes down in a forest because of the additional polygons needed to respresent all the trees, as well as the trees behind the trees behind the trees having to be drawn.

Anyway, there are three general ways to increase your framerate (starting from easiest to hardest):

1. Adjust your clipping plane down under the display options section.

2. Buy a faster 3D card.

3. Upgrade your CPU/Computer

The level of 3D sound can also affect overall framerate, depending on your system's configuration (which sound card, etc.) so you might want to experiment with those settings as well.

Bottom line, Kunark pushes the EQ engine a lot harder, hence the better visuals. Even though this was perhaps a non-issue for you with the rest of Norrath, when you're on Kunark you might now need to adjust your display settings.

-Brad

ps. just to be very clear, the animating trees do not measurably affect your framerate.

pps. framerate is how fast your screen is updated and has little to do with 'lag', which is generally another word for 'latency' which has to do with internet performance (timing issues, sending data between your client and our servers).
hope that helps

    Iksar Books of Lore
    [ 10:56 PM PDT ] - Alethal

    While searching through the new Iksar town of Cabilis, I, Iskar necromancer Valus, along with my brother, high shaman Sebrn, discovered a series of books relating to the rich history of Kunark. The books outline everything from the history of Iksar enslavement to the rise of the Iksar monk caste along with much more! Quite a bit of the Iksar lore is found within. Below is a complete copy of the six volumes which were found upon the Court Chronicler in the Court of Pain.

    1. Genesis of Fist and Tail

    The Swifttail Caste, Fists and Tails of Cabilis
    It was in dawning of the Sebilisian Empire that the art of fist and tail was forged. In the beginning, the pugilists of the great tribes were nothing more than warriors without weapons. Soon we would be forged into weapons.
    Grandmaster Tynn was the father of our disciplines. He came to the empire from an unknown tribe. Some say he was a ronin who wandered the worlds of Norrath where he formed the basis of the city of Sebilis with no rebirth. He entered to form a caste which did not exist. He was quickly challenged by the warlords. He spoke his words and they did not listen. He knew the only way to make them understand was to challenge their finest in The Gauntlet. He called for four of the finest warlords and allowed them arms. The warlords agreed, thinking his challenge as easy task.
    Grandmaster Tynn quickly dispatched of the warlords with nothing more than fist and tail. He fought in a way never displayed. His victory earned him much respect and Emperor Sathir soon commanded him to form the Court of Pain and raise an army of monks. Tynn formed the Swifttail Caste.
    Soon the way of fist and tail had hundreds in it's ranks, climbing the rungs. Tynn showed them they were not warriors, but monks.
    They would don shackles as testament to the Age of Snake when the Iksar were a race enslaved. Inner strength and harmony were taught. The Swifttail were born.
    The Swifttail were disbanded during the End Days. It was not till the rise of the new empire under Emperor Vekin did the caste reform. Grandmaster Glox, a former disciple of Tynn, came to New Sebilis to rebuild the Court of Pain. In honor of Tynn, all masters now drape themselves in a robe like he wore.

    2. Before Green
    This is known as the Age of the Snake. The snake race known as Shissar, slithered upon Kunark. The Iksar had yet to unite. Our people had yet to learn the Black Arts and the ways of Fist and Tail. Divided, the Iksar were enslaved by the snakes. In the centuries before the Green, the Iksar were slaves of the Shissar. They ruled from the ancient city of Chelsith which rest deep within the land now known as The Overthere. From here would sprout much evil. The Iksar were tortured like Frogs and suffered at the hands of the Shissar. The Iksar who showed genius were to help the snakes in their dark rites. These few were the seeds of rebellion. The Iksar began to plot a revolt, but a greater force would strike first.
    The Shissar were an arrogant race. The power bestowed by the gods was not enough. They made pacts with other gods and demigods. They found the rifts of time and space and used them to gather magic items once held by the guardians of the planes. The Shissar's disloyalty and blasphemous pride would bring the wrath of the gods upon them. No power could stop the force to come. A green cloud formed in the skies above Kunark. The could began to descend upon the land. From it's green core, the face of death began to form. It was of no god they knew of. It opened it's giant maw and spoke in tongues unknown. From the mouth of death rushed forth a green mist. The Shissar shaman began to cast spells in hopes of shielding their city and the necromancers began to assault the mist with great weapons. All were futile. The mist rushed forth. The first snakes to be swallowed by the mist had their scales and flesh rot and fall from their bones. Horrid screams of suffering could be heard from within the mist. A river of flesh and blood flowed. The Iksar stood and gazed upon the gristle that was once the Shissar. With the Green Death came the liberation of the Iksar.
    The season is now 0 A.G, After Green.

    3. Talon Southpaw
    Talon Southpaw was the fastest pugilist ever to walk within the Court of Pain. His speed rivaled that of the great Grandmaster Tynn. He could often be found within the local taverns demonstrating the awesome speed of his fist.
    Talon Southpaw came to rebirth within this caste. Although quick with fist and tail, the speed of his left arm was not to come to blossom for a couple decades past his rebirth. It was to follow his captivity by the accursed Samak.
    Soon after fulfilling the Rung of Tynn, Talon went on a wandering retreat into the dangerous outlands of Kunark. He was not seen from for a total of 5 seasons. Upon his return to Cabilis he told of his captivity within an ancient city of Iksar now claimed by the Samak. He told of intense torture. He survived the ordeal. He escaped from the claws of the Samak with numerous scars which proved of his painful ordeal. Most of the worst scars were never to be seen. Talon worn a robe which he never removed. The scars underneath were for no lizards eyes. his great perseverance gave him an inner strength no other monk could lay claim to.
    He now possessed incredible, almost blinding speed with his left arm. He could tap a foes arm with his left and strike with as if having a third arm! He was indeed a paragon of the Swifttails. All the empire under respected or feared him. None would spar him outside of the taverns. with grog comes stupidity.
    Upon his death in Kurn, Grandmaster Tynn removed his hands in a private ceremony. Only Tynn was allowed to gaze upon Talon's body. The fists were then sealed in a canopic which would stay within the Tower of Kurn. His body was dusted. This is his tale as told by Rinmark.

    4. Venril The Chief
    Venril Sathir of the Kunzar
    The greatest lizard to walk upon the land was Venril Sathir of the Kunzar tribe. All that is known of his youth was that he was a member of the ancient Kunzar Tribe. This was during the time of division among the tribes of the Iksar. He was said to have never been a mere broodling. He was forever a master of combat.
    Venril would soon slay his father and take his rightful place as chief of the Kunzar Tribe. He led the Kunzar on many expeditions into the ruins and wilds of Kunark. He sought knowledge and weapons.
    In his late youth, he find the hidden library of the necromancer, Kotiz. He would come to open a large book which opened into a small room. Climbing into the book he finds a floating book. The Unholy Writ of War is it's title. Made of Iksar skins and containing great knowledge of combat. Venril becomes the books master.
    Venril soon shows signs of dark knowledge. He begins to wield the forces of the dark circle and his knowledge of combat tactics becomes supreme. He trains his army new tactics and even raises the bones of the fallen to fight yet again for their master. His army grows and marches to all corners of Kunark.
    Venril Sathir's army is now unstoppable. He shall soon plot the union of the entire Iksar nation. He no longer wishes to be just Chief, but rather, Emperor. Under his direction the four remaining tribe states of Iksar shall unite and a new age shall dawn.
    313 A.G.

    5. Captain of the Sea
    Captain Vlzk Irontail, Master of the Sea
    370 A.G.: Vlzk, student of the 5th rung, becomes the youngest Iksar monk to attain the title of Tynn Master. He takes his place among the masters of the Court of Pain.
    371 A.G.: Master Vlzk becomes the only Iksar monk to be promoted to the rank of Captain within the Iksar Navy. He is promoted by the great Emperor Rile. This act solidifies the alliance between the monks and the Legion and ushers in a new age of disciplines.
    372 A.G.: During a coastal patrol being conducted by Captain Vlsk, a fierce and sudden water spout hits and splinters his ship. The Navy searches for survivors and none are found.
    406 A.G.: During a Deep Range Patrol, a group of Legionnaires is greeted by Captain Vlsk. He has survived in the Outlands with no aid. His once proud tail has bee ripped from his body of a Trakanasaurus. He becomes a hero. The blacksmiths are ordered to create a tail make of iron. He is now Captain Irontail.
    Captain Irontail is promoted to Captain of the Iron Guard, a roving coastal armada. He is also rewarded with great items of magic. In his honor, the Baron and the Arch Duke create an awesome weapon called the Storm Sabre. They also create a lesser blade for all who serve in the Ironguard--The Tynn Foil.
    406 A.G.: During the Infamous Battle of Timorous Deep, Captain Vlsk Irontail is defeated as well as most of the Sebilisian Navy. A horde of dragons descended upon them. The navy was vastly outnumbered. So was the end of the great Captain Irontail. Long live his spirit.

    6. The Crusades of 1312 A.G.
    This is the time of the scattered and defeated Iksar empire. The great dragon, Trakanon and his allies of scale have left the empire in rubble and our people are divided yet again. the once grand city of Sebilis is claimed by the one who descimated it.
    The remaining Iksar are scattered among the outlands of Kunark. Their places of refuge are now the dwellings of dark denizens. In the wilds, a few castes come to unite. One of these are the remnants of the Crusaders of Greenmist. They are lead by a powerful shadowknight named Nok Nilok.
    The army of shadowknights number in the hundreds. Within their ranks are a few mystics and legionnaires. The tribe names itself after their leader, they are to be named, The Crusaders of Rok Nilok and they shall purge the lands of Kunark.
    The Crusaders burn across the Dreadlands and death comes quickly to those who dare stand in their way. They slay many giants and many goblins. There shall be no prisoners of slaves. The empire shall be rebuilt by the hands of the Iksar. All other shall perish.
    Kamors Castle is the first major objective of the Crusaders. It has now been taken over by goblin clans. The Goblins soon find themselves under siege. for twenty days they hold ground until the Crusaders choose to assault as a band of goblin deserters flee. A swift battle ensues and the castle is reclaimed.
    The Crusaders now set their sights on the city of Cabilis. While the march is designed, Rok Nilok discovers something very evil. Nothing is known of his discoveries. No record exists of it's evil. All that is known is that Rok Nilok made a pact and soon shall suffer for it.
    During the battle for Cabilis, a strange rain falls which shrinks them to the size of dolls and their heads are set aflame. Nilok's head burns red. And the goblins squash the would be emperor and his minions.

    Difference between Slam and bash
    [ 11:43 AM PDT ] - Zargon
    Found at the Everquest Gameplay Boards,

    Keeping it short, non magic shields cannot bash magic even if you have magic shoulders. Since magic shoulders allow large races to bash magic with a non-magic weapon in their offhand, shouldn't magic shoulders allow non-magic shields to bash magic as well? I consider this a bug, the 'magic shoulders' rule should apply to all items in the offhand, including nonmagical shields.

    This isn't a bug. The Bash skill requires a magic shield. The Slam skill requires magic shoulders. Hitting a magic creature with the weapon in your off-hand requires a magic weapon. Bash essentially turns your shield into a weapon, which is why it requires a magic shield. You are hitting them with your shield.

    Slam, though it looks like you are slapping them with your fist is more akin to checking them, hockey style. When you have a shield equipped, the slam is considered a bash. It's one of the tradeoffs that exists in the game. You cannot have all of the advantages of having a shield and all of the advantages of not having a shield at the same time, unless the shield is magic.

    - Gordon

    [ 3:28 PM PDT ] - Zargon
    Found at the Everquest Gameplay Boards,

    I am a 14th level enchanter and just seriously started using my charm spell instead of a pet. It was really fun to make a Griffawn attack another one but it takes time to set up. You have to buff and med and then get a SoW (because you don't want to be at normal speed when charm breaks!) and then find the intended mobs.

    None of this is a complaint but intended to show that it's not just a walk in the park. The problem is that when charm breaks, the ex-pet is almost always in mid-battle and then both come after you like a bat out of hell. Sometimes the mob your pet was fighting is really low in health and you can destroy it with a quick DD or DoT before you run. Unfortunately for some reason you don't get experience. My guess would be that Verant has it so that once the charm breaks your ex-pet has all the credit for damage done. I think this is extremely ridiculous and unfair to the enchanter who spent his time setting all this up. You also don't get xp if you kill the pet instead. So basically you have to kill both mobs after charm breaks just to get xp for one! If you're having any trouble understanding this dilemma, imagine that you are in a group and someone drops out right at the end of a battle and then gets the xp instead of the group! With how bad enchanters pets are (and believe me my pet sucks!) it would be really nice if you could look into making charm work like you were responsible for the damage....since you were.

    This isn't a bug. When a creature dies, the system checks to see what entity gets credit for the kill. If that entity is a pet, the credit for the kill goes to the pets owner. Since charm broke, and the entity that did the most damage is no longer a pet, you don't get the experience. Your choice in this case is either to kill the former pet first, or recharm it so that it is your pet when the first critter dies.

    Gordon Speaks about Changing Faiths
    Community News [ 3:40 AM PDT ] - Zep
    Found this thread on the Official EverQuest Forums:
    The decision to disallow a "Change of Faith" was made during the early design process for EQ. At that time it was decided that we didn't want people changing faith as a matter of convenience, to get whatever benefits that a particular religion might grant at that specific time.

    With that in mind, several other design decisions were made for EQ that now makes changing faith impossible even if we decided to allow it through a complicated quest or what have you. It's burried too deep into the core faction code for all of our work since then to be unraveled.

    Your initial faction is set through your choice of race, class, and deity. Other factions that you affect as you build that character are affected based upon your own faction. The entire faction system would basically have to be redesigned to support it, and with thousands of other NPCs and factions to consider, the work involved and the potential for problems makes it prohibitive.

    I still maintain that this deity-specific items issue is no different than if we added new items restricted by race or class. Sure, I've seen people say that if they ever had the idea that weapons or items would be restricted by deity, then they would have chosen one. However, knowing all the potential aspects of the game prior to playing it is not required in order to play the game. I'm sure that many people started the game with a limited or non-existent understanding of game mechanics.

    Though I understand the viewpoints that have been presented, we are firm on this design aspect of the game.

    -Gordon

    Brad Posts Stat Information
    [ 2:36 PM PDT ] - Yendi
    Found on this thread of the Official Everquest Forums:

    In working with the people doing the strategy guide for Kunark we decided to provide them with general information as to what the stats do.

    I'm going to post them here.

    This is a change in the sense that we were not going to discuss them or release more info. We changed our minds.

    That said, we're not posting any formulas, and it's absolutely true that some stats have considerably more direct impact on gameplay than others.

    We're posting this for clarification, and because previous explanations have not been accurate (manual, etc). We will not, however, engage in debate and our stance on how much some stats affect game play is that it's by design to inhibit some aspects of twinking. Are there better attribute systems out there? Yeah, but we're not going to change something as fundamental as a stat system in EQ after the game's been out for more than a year.

    Anyway, here you go:

    DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows

    STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

    STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

    INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights

    WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

    CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

    AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

    Why Zones in EQ?
    [ 12:46 PM PDT ] - Kang

    Massively Multiplayer RPGs have a long development cycle compared to other games, and the graphics engine is the first thing that is designed. Back when development started on EQ, the development team had several goal for operation of the game. For example, they wanted to minimize the effect of internet latency. They also wanted to have each "area" have its own look-and-feel, with its own set of appropriatly placed NPCs. At the time, the "Zone" technology was the best technology available to meet the goals of the development team. Of course since then, new technologies have been developed, and though it makes sense that we would take advantage of them in future products, EQ will always have zones. You just can't change graphics engines on the fly post-production.

    /Con System
    Community News [ 7:59 AM PDT ] - Kang
    This Thread was found on the Offical EverQuest Forums:
    The /con system is an indication of level. It makes no note of special skills, abilities, or attacks. For example, some encounters of a specific level might be more difficult than others. As people level up, they get stronger. However NPCs get stronger on a steeper scale. For this reason, the /con system goes from being representative of solo abilities to being representative of group abilities. The rate at which this happens varies for all of the classes. NPCs of a higher level MUST be stronger than a solo player, or groups will tear through them with no problems and no risk. For this reason, the /con system is not broken. At lower levels it fairly well represents the abilities of a solo player. At upper levels, it very closely represents the risk to a full balanced group.

    Community News [ 10:16 AM PDT ] - Zargon
    Found at Everquest Gameplay Boards. Even more questions answered there aswell
    Why not run 4 dynamic quests all at the same time in 4 remote locations. They would go a lot smoother =P

    Great idea. It just requires more manpower (here's hoping that, over time, given such a successful game, we'll get more).

    Good Lord! -- Why are you working on a Sunday?!?

    Heh, good question. I think that even though I know that the vast majority of players love EQ and that the number of people who bitterly post on these boards is very small, that it still bugs me to hear false claims and allegations about Verant and our game. Some people say we don't care, or that we don't listen, or that we don't respect our customers. I've always been a person that is bothered by people believing things that aren't true about me (or my company, whom I represent). I also know that for every poster there are probably 10 or even 100 'lurkers' who just read boards, and I know that they appreciate when we post. Heck, whenever I do I see new names either post or email me, thanking me for the extra info. So I do what I can, and today it occurred to me to try to answer as many questions about the game as I could.

    Whenever your inventory is full, and you have a stackable item in there, and you go to loot another stackable item of the same type, it says your inventory is full. So you have to manually pick it up and place it there. This is really quite annoying. Any chance of getting it fixed?

    No short term plans, no, but I'd love to see that addressed one day.

    Why does it take 2 months to patch data? Why is the time interval in-between data patches (whatever that means) take over a month. Why is it when a response like "next patch it will be done" really means "next DATA patch" and not clearly stated "which will happen in over a month"

    It doesn't always, but it certainly does take some time. Patching data can often be the most dangerous type of patch, because it's so easy to miss bugs, exploits, and other similar problems. So whenever we add a bunch of data to the game, we try to test it for a longer period of time. Also, moving data over from our test environment to the live servers takes more time than just moving code. Sometimes, when we've had a decent amount of downtime, for whatever reason, we are hesitant to move data over and have delayed the process in the past.

    Can you give wood elves the /lapdance emotes (yes with animation)?

    Hehe, no comment.

    A Rogue cannot evade when the only other thing attacking the creature is a pet. Is this intentional? Why isn't the creature considering the pet a valid target to attack if a Rogue evades?

    I'm not positive, but I think the skill was intended to work in a group situation, and that we assume there are other characters attacking the target.

    Why aren't most items that have charged effects rechargeable?

    Because having items with charged effects allow us to place items into the game that would be too powerful were the charges infinite and/or rechargeable. That said, it does indeed make sense to have some charged effects items rechargeable and I think you'll see more in the future.

    Are you ever going to reimplement fear and charm back into PVP? If not, why?

    I sincerely doubt it - the problems both of those spells caused became such a customer service nightmare that we took them out. And when we thought long and hard as to how we could re-implement them without creating additional problems, we couldn't think of a way.

    When will we expect to see new improvements to Tinkering? Right now there are only a handful of marginally useful items to be made with it.

    Tinkering has a planned upgrade after we get the Poison upgrade in.

    Will the new zones that you are creating post-ROK look like the new and improved graphics or will they have to be compatible with the old graphics engine.

    They will have the new and improved graphics.

    Will the old areas ever be redrawn to take advantage of better graphics cards.

    No, we won't be doing this. We'd have to recreate the old zones from scratch in order to do this effectively, and we'd rather allocate those resources (game designers/world builders) to creating new content.

    What is being done to stop showeq users and does Verant/Sony consider using it a violation of copyright laws or any other laws?

    We are occasionally introducing problems for showeq, breaking it with patches. This typically slows down the distribution of the program, but we've yet to devise a way to eliminate the program. Overall, it's not really that detrimental of an advantage to run the program.

    Religion... currently all its good for is getting oneself killed. Are there any plans to ever give people a benefit to having a religion? Say, quests limited to people only of a certain religion, for instance.

    Not quests, but we're thinking about making it such that certain items may only be wielded/used by characters who follow certain god(s).

    Does EQ Dev team feel that Magician class is fairly well balanced in levels 50 - 60? What's magicians role in EQ?

    Yes, we feel the Magician is in pretty good shape. For a good description of the class, please see www.everquest.com

    What is the underlying policy behind a nerf or enhancement to a class? Is it the "power" of the class? The desirability of the class (e.g. As expressed by player population)? The dependencies of the class?

    We nerf when we feel it's the best way to address something that isn't balanced and/or that's not working out the way we'd planned. We don't like doing nerfs, and realize it is upsetting to some of our players.

    My main concern now is with dependencies. Is it Verant's intention to balance classes with respect to dependencies? It's good to have classes that each need each other, but if there are classes that need others that don't need them, would that be cause for tuning?

    In a sense, yes. Interdependence is key to EverQuest and has always been. Classes should need each other, and some classes should need other classes more than others (i.e. some race/class combos are better for soloing than others). This is all by design.

    What use does minor illusion have on a non-pvp server where most mobs see right through it? (don't get me started on how sneaky and useful it is on a pvp server)

    This is probably something GZ should answer.

    Why doesn't the tree illusion give the same regen effect that tree form gives to druids?

    Another GZ question.

    Why is it that monks wearing magic gloves can hit magical creates but a spell caster wearing magical gloves gets the INVULNERABLE message(this may apply to all other classes...just don't know)?

    Monks, being reliant on their fists, are able to use the magic in the gloves to their advantage.

    Why can't stealthy classes with high enough sneak ability, sneak anywhere? Don't we remember that historical Halfling that burgled a cup from a dragon?

    Actually, you could get pretty much anywhere in beta with sneaking/hiding/invis. We changed it because it was simply unbalanced - players could map out dangerous areas without risk. I also think the One Ring that allowed Bilbo to recon Smaug's Lair was probably an artifact level item.

    Is there a possibility of Battle-Mage class or Ogre Mages(there is an ogre paladin in East Freeport)?

    No plans for additional classes, no.

    Any chance that ALL ae spells can be given the NON-PARTY effect like bard songs?

    Doubt it, but that's probably one for GZ.

    Is Verant trying to find a way to adjust the clipping plane for spells that are suppose to increase sight range? In their current state these spells are rarely used.

    Turns out we'd rather just let everyone see farther if their machines can handle it.

    Is Verant considering making Wizards more group friendly. When I am making a group level 20 and above Wizards are my last pick.

    Loaded question. I think Wizards are group friendly.

    Will Enchanters get an Illusion Iksar? I hope so.

    Can't say, sorry.

    I know you are sick of this one. Any idea how the Iksar will fit in on Tallon or Vallon yet?

    On the Troll, Ogre, Dark Elf team, those evil bastards!

    In your vision, what are Bards supposed to be the masters of?

    Song and dance.

    Will reported broken quests be repaired? (some have been broken for months and have been /bug, petition and email reported)

    I certainly hope so, eventually. We fix them all the time.

    To whom do I report game bugs (broken programming)?

    Please use the /bug command.

    Will reported game bugs be repaired? (some have been broken since beta and have been /bug, /petition and email reported)

    I certainly hope so, eventually. We fix them all the time.

    What will you do to help Melee classes in the expansion? Items are nice but with all the caster classes getting 20 spells or so do you think Maybe they will give some new Combat skills or abilities more than maybe just the 2 that the rumor is.

    I can't answer specifically yet, sorry, but we've got some pretty cool things planned (and in testing now).

    How much do you plan to have pathing improved for EQ? Will we ever see a situation where mobs take the most direct route between point a and point b in an open area rather than zigzagging? If not, will we see drastically improved pathing in EQ 2.0?

    We continue to enhance pathing here and there (we've hopefully fixed one of the bugs related to disappearing mobs recently). That said, the way pathing works in general isn't like to change in this incarnation of EQ.

    Is it possible to make a command like /bag head arm chest ect. for pvp servers, this would make it harder of naked pk's to make a profit pk'ing when people can hotekey all their armor.

    We've heard this idea several times, and like it in general. It would, however, require quite a bit of work to implement and we don't plan on it short term.

    Are there "legendary item" (aka fiery avenger) yet to be found via quest?

    The Fiery Avenger has been found. There are currently no other FA-type items in the game for the other classes, but it is our intent to add them over time.

    Are some quests only initiated if you hail or talk to a NPC in his native or non-common languages?

    I don't think so, no, but I certainly think that would be cool.

    Are there plans to put in Player controlled NPC's for quests/storyline enhancement? Perhaps they could be attained through a "guide-like" application system and follow a ongoing script written by Verant's quest/ history developers. Not guides as they are too busy with customer complaints.

    No plans, sorry.

    Is anything in the works to explain better the history of Norrath other than picking up bits and piece's in quests, both on the God level and within the day-to-day goings-on of Norrath. UO's town criers and GM controlled in game news boards come to mind.

    Those are great ideas, and we hope to do something like that in the future, time and resources permitting.

    Noticing a lot of people are complaining of lag and Zone disconnects, is it possible this is due to the security elements you have put in place since you are changing things around often to keep programs like ShowEQ from working. Seems a lot of these complaints peaked after you started thwarting programs like ShowEQ and other hack programs.

    No, no relationship at all. We wouldn't do anything to stop showeq that would jeopardize our playerbase.

    When is the next new non-split server going to come up?

    We hope before the Expansion launches - that's the plan, anyway.

    Kunark: What are you and the team generally shooting for when you're thinking of moving characters from being able to level from 50 to 60? I.e. comparative power to famous mobs? Would this be considered a "crowning" level with a very powerful new ability for each, or just an upgrade of 10 levels?

    Probably somewhere in-between. A level 60 player is certainly more powerful than a level 50 player, so much so that we feel it will be very attractive to keep playing beyond 50. That said, level 60 players are still going to get beat on pretty hard in the depths of higher level dungeons.

    You're going to revamp existing unused dungeons (Runneyeye, Kerra ridge) so people will go there - what is the basic scheme in which you plan to go about this?

    Primarily more and better loot, some of which may come from existing dungeons, and some of which might be new.

    I'm concerned that by playing on an older server I'm going to eventually reach a level where there's nothing I can fight because everything is camped solid. What is being done about this?

    We're launching the Expansion which will increase the size of the world by over 33%. We are revamping existing dungeons to make them more attractive. And we intend to keep expanding the world after the Expansion.

    How's Star Wars Online shaping up? (I suspect we'll be unable to play Jedi or own/use ships. Any comment?)

    Can't say much other than it's going to be an incredible, ground-breaking game.

    Will tracking ever be made more useful, and will rangers ever be able to track better than druids/bards (they currently cannot)

    Yes, but I don't have a time frame.

    Will rangers ever become the best at archery, because currently warriors are better (not including strange new ranger only bows, but trueshot and raincaller dmg ration is outdone by DW)

    I'm not sure this is accurate, but if it is, I think we'd rather that Rangers be better at archery than other classes, yes.

    Why do so many quests involve CAMPING for a specific item? Wouldn't it be easier to make larger quests involving the gathering of more items, from a wider area with slightly easier drops. That is in the one to two hour range. Not everyone can play in minimum 8 hour blocks.

    Totally agreed, and that is something we've come to learn over time. Our plan is to still have rare items in the future, but have obtaining those items require smaller and more blocks of time.

    Why can someone do the same quest over 100 times if they want and still get the item or XP.

    Because it slows down the absolute consumption of content, which is very important for MMORPGs where people plan on playing them for months, even years, as opposed to just a few weeks.

    Why are city guards allowed to be killed, session after session?

    Because they are NPCs, like any other. We have upgraded them recently, and will continue to do so if necessary, as a deterrent to people farming them and interfering with newbies.

    Why are there many people outside of PAW and so few inside PAW?

    I think Paw needs more work, and we will return to it after we revamp more zones.

    What is your stance on TWINKING?

    It's both good and bad, sort of a necessary evil. It upsets some players, yet it increases the replayability of the game significantly. This is one of those topics, btw, that I could write an essay on - I don't think I can accurately explain with just a few sentences, sorry.

    Why do casters upon death have access to their SPELLBOOK and yet a melee does not even get a rusty weapon?

    So they can help themselves and other players, both casters and melees, with corpse retrievals.

    One year after release why are rogue skills STILL so lacking?

    Loaded question. It is our opinion that after we revamp poison that Rogues will be in pretty good shape. We made numerous enhancements to Rogues over the last year.

    - Brad

    Brad on Kunark Strategy Guide
    Community News [ 1:34 PM PST ] - Yendi
    Brad posted to this thread on the Official Everquest Forums:

    Obviously buying the original guide was a mistake, just wondering if buying this one will be as well. Any information you have regarding this guide would be greatly appreciated...

    We are involved with the strategy guide for Kunark, yes. Definitely moreso than with the original guide.

    That said, we don't have total control. We are the developer, not the publisher (Sony), nor the company in charge of writing/creating the guide.

    There is also another problem with strategy guides (and even manuals) when it comes to MMORPGs: these games change. The manual/strategy guide can't.

    My view of strategy guides for games like these would be that they really be STRATEGY guides, that they are full of valuable information that is more timeless. For example, grouping tactics and such.

    But let me tell you, the strategy guide makers want to put in list after list of spells, items, skills, and other stats. Which is all find and dandy, I guess, for single player games.

    But it makes for problems in games that change and evolve. I'm also against 'spoilers' in general, but that's another topic.

    Another thread mentioned that the original guide said Humans have an experience/leveling bonus or some such. The reality is that they are one of the easier races to level in that some other races have experience penalties.

    But they didn't word it that way, for whatever reason (miscommunication? who knows).

    Anyway, to make a long story short, we'll do our best to work with the outside companies that write manuals and strategy guides, and we seem to be having more influence in the Kunark guide than in the original. That said, things are going to change, miscommuncation will probably occur again (let's just hope to a lesser degree), and things will be wrong.

    *shrug*

    Gordon on Quest Experience
    Community News [ 9:47 AM PST ] - Yendi
    Abashi posted to this thread on the Official Everquest Forums:
    The game is called EverQUEST. Verant themselves have made that point to justify increasing the importance of quests. I agree, and would like to suggest Verant go farther by having quests give the best commodity: experience. Not the near-zero amount they do now for any high levels, but real amounts for all levels.

    We're not at all opposed to quests giving out real (e.g. tangible) experience, however that's really not going to be possible given the current architecture. The problem is that we need to have the ability to store completion-info as part of the character. This would keep the same character from doing the same quest over and over again, which is what keeps us from doing things like giving out half-a-level for a tough quest. Tracking this unfortunately isn't possible without doing a player-wipe. The Quest engine could be improved in many other ways, but they all require the ability to know exactly what a character has or has not done.

    -Gordon
