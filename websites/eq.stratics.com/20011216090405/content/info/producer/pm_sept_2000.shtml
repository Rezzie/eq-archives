The Evil Overlord
Greebo's Rabbit Hole - September, 2000
Introduction Where We've Come From What We've Been Doing Where We're Going Next

EverQuest Stratics Project Manager's Letter

Its been two and a half months now since I became the EverQuest Stratics Project Manager, and I thought it was about time that I started writing these PM Letters to let you, our faithful visitors, know just what's been going on here at Stratics.

To be completely honest, its well past time, but the sad fact of the matter is that the EQSS Project Manager role is a part time position, and I have a real-world job which has to put food on the table. Since there are only so many hours in the day, there have been plenty of other priorities for me to focus on with what time I can pull together for EQSS without putting together a place for me to shoot my mouth off. Still, you are our customers, and you of all people deserve to know what's been going on and what's yet to come.

For those of you who haven't talked to me before, I started off at EQSS as a Forum regular on the Westwood Tavern. After a couple of months of shooting my mouth off there, I suggested to Jhariden (one of the Stratics Big Boss Types) that we create a forum where people could ask for and get directions for navigating through Norrath. Well, one thing led to another and I ended up as the Stratics Interactive Coordinator, or in simpler terms, the head moderator for all EQSS Forums. A few months ago Jhariden approached me about taking over as EQSS Project Manager, and on June 15 of this year, I signed on.

First off, let me bring you all up to speed on what's been going on and where we've come from. Brace yourself, since we've been at it for nearly three months without a report, this is a long one...

 top

Where We've Come From

There's no really kind way to say this - the site was in disarray. About the only thing that was operating smoothly were the forums, and even those were plagued by serious slowdowns affecting not just the EQSS site. The News coverage was sporadic at best, the Editorial staff was nonexistent, and we'd been staring at the same dark elf on the Qeynos Docks for weeks on our Screenshot of the Day and seen the same poll for just as long if not longer.

On top of the staff issues, the web site itself was suffering in terms of organization and plain old HTML layout. The pages looked OK on the surface, but when you started actually looking at the tag structure, well - it was a real mess.

 top

What We've Been Doing

Forum Issues

One of the first projects I got involved in as the new Project Manager was addressing the Forum Speed issue. This was an ongoing project that took several months, but the end result now is that our Forums are running on one dedicated web server and one dedicated database server, and each major Stratics site has its own cache for their forums. This was a joint effort and only a small amount of the credit belongs to me. Jhariden and Frostbyghte really deserve the Kudos, as well as Auz, on the Stratics technical staff.

Message Boards

Since the Forums were running smoothly (with regard to the moderators anyway, at first), selecting someone to carry them on was easy. Thundolfe stepped right up to bat and has been keeping them going strong. Thundolfe is funny, entertaining, and knows how to have a fun conversation, but also knows when to reign in a thread that's gotten out of control or when to let someone know that they've gone a bit too far. Don't give her any grief, either - she's in a league of her own. (Volunteer positions for Forum Moderators are open and inquiries can be made by emailing Thundolfe.)

News Department

That was the easy part. The rest of the departments were not doing nearly so well. The next step was selecting Ogamok as the News Lead. What can I say about Ogamok - he's practically carried the News department on his shoulders while we've been rebuilding the staff. We've cleared out a lot of dead weight in the news staff and when the dust settled, there were only two names left from the old department who were doing much of anything. I'm sure you have all noticed the dramatic improvement in our News Coverage. To be honest, I'm getting scared to post any news on my own because he's usually right behind me making it fit the correct format, putting in cross-references, etc. (Oh yes, and we're still accepting applications for News People too so if you want to help out, email Ogamok today!)

Editorial Department

The next major department here at EQSS is the Editorial department. When I came on board, there wasn't one. Onnie had just been brought on as Lead Editor by the previous management and the two of us were left to try and figure out just exactly what we were going to do to build a brand new Editorial staff. Thanks to Onnie's help, we've now got four great editors helping out: Delgren, Gibra, Serani and Serevalt. We've also come a long way towards working out an efficient article editing system. We've run a few articles through the process now, and I am pretty sure we're ready to really crank up the juice. More on this in "Where We're Going Next". Sadly, due to health challenges in her family, Onnie has had to step down as Lead Editor, but we do deeply hope that when her family is well again (We're praying for you, Onnie), that she will rejoin us. (And yes, we are looking for more editors - especially those with either extensive game knowledge and/or HTML skills. Email me to get more information.)

Web Site Revamp

Along the way, we had to address the HTML issues, and since we were at it, we decided to revamp the Look and Feel of EverQuest Stratics at the same time. Thanks to Jhariden's hard work and excellent graphic design skills, we were able to go over every page on EQSS and apply a new, consistent format to each one, and reorganize the content along the way into a more meaningful structure in about a week. (It was a week of some very, very, late nights.)

Miscellaneous

In addition to all of the above, we've:

  • Started running our weekly poll on a Weekly basis once again
  • Fixed the Screenshot of the Day (Daily Vision) (Thanks to Reijas for managing that job!)
  • Developed and updated our EverQuest Client Color Editor several times
  • Added a Staff Biography section with a new bio going up each week
  • Committed to attending the EQ Festival 2000 in November (Hope to see you there!)
  • Started the monthly Project Manager's Letter

     top

Where We're Going Next

When I started this role as Project Manager, my main focus was on getting the staffing levels up to where they should be. This is a challenge because unlike many professional sites, EQSS is almost purely a volunteer organization. Finding hard working, dedicated people to work on a project is hard enough - add to it the fact that its a labor of love, and you're really facing a challenge.

Thankfully we have been very fortunate in that regard, so the next big focus for us is content revision and improvement. There are three main areas to this task:

  • Existing content review: Every current page at EQSS needs to be looked at by the Editorial Staff and if the article is still worthy of being on our site, revised for accuracy and revisions to the game, and basically gone over with a fine-toothed comb.

  • New Submissions: Like existing content, these need to be gone over, but with greater speed in processing. Most of the holdups in the new machine have been due to the number of other tasks that have required my attention. Also, its come to my attention that a number of people have submitted articles in the past and haven't heard anything further about them. You fall into one of two categories:

    1. You submitted your article before June 15, 2000: Please re-send your article to me. I have no idea what happened to your article before this date because I wasn't the Project Manager.

    2. You submitted your article after June 15, 2000: Please email me asking for the status of your article. Include the title and the approximate date of the submission. Its probably in our Queue awaiting processing, but I'll let you know either way.

  • Cross Referencing: In addition to content, we want to have every article on our site properly link to relevant other pages on our site. This is more of an indexing job than anything else, and will likely occur in tandem with other projects over time, unless we get a volunteer to do the entire task at once.

Other areas that I want us to start working on include adding an EverQuest Zone Guide section with strategy guides and maps for the various places to hunt in Norrath, and a Fiction section for short-stories about adventuring in Norrath and other prose. There's more, but those are the big ones.

One final note - I still get a lot of inquiries about the old Items Database - you know, the one where you could search for items by stat, race, class, et cetera. The best answer I can give you right now on what happened to it is, "I don't know." That database stopped being a part of the EQSS pages before I took the job. I have the data files and I have the scripts, but I have not been able to make them work yet. I am looking into it, but to be frank, it is not my highest priority at this time. I know many of you are going to be disappointed to hear that, and I'm sorry, but there are only so many hours in the day that I have to spend on EQSS.

That about wraps it up for this month - thank you for bearing with me, I know its been a long read. As always, I invite your feedback by emailing me at greebo@stratics.com, or you can comment on our progress by replying to this Feedback Thread I've created in the Westwood Tavern.

Thank you for being a part of EverQuest Stratics,

Greebo
EQSS Project Manager

 top
