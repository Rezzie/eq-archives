This is my opinion about stamina vs. agility for you magic types (not cleric types).

Stamina is more important than agility.

Agility is great for adding onto defense, but seems to make a difference for armor pieces. In other words, a magic user can only wear crappy armor so agility won't make that much of a difference. Plus, no matter what a magic user can't really stand up in a toe to toe fight (pet or no pet). Magic users can't tank period. At lower levels, some magic types switch off tanking with their pet. When you get to higher levels, this is NOT an option because the mobs do so much damage.

So why dump the points into stamina? I've noticed that as you go up levels, there tends to be more magic user vs magic user battles from a distance. In that case, with even level/skill magic types, the higher stamina player/mob will win. Also, if you happen to attract the attention of the mob (you weren't waiting long enough or standing far enough away), the higher stamina means that you have a little longer for you incompetent tanks to rescue you.

hope that helps.

BTW, as a tank, let me say this to you magic types. If you have a competent tank and a mob switches to you, unless you are planning on fleeing the zone and even if you are not, as a general rule, do not run away from the tank if you are getting attacked. It is impossible for me to catch up and get that silly mob off you. run towards me or stand still. If you can't maintain that upfront personal composure, think about how us tanks feel when you magic types (clerics or otherwise) start fizzling as we go 4 bubbles 3 bubbles 2 bubbles ACK
