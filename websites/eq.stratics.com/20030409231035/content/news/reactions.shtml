EverQuest Balance Reactions

Below are just some of the reactions found on the web regarding the "nerfs" that were announced October 8th.

Manaburn

For

Naldain from Westwood Tavern:
I think the reason they changed MB is not so much because it trivializes encounters currently available. If you look at the other changes they made, modrod and CH, they talk about making encounters more interesting and faster, which means lower hp mobs. They can't very well try to add difficult encounters with lower hp mobs if you could just bring along a few MB wizards and kill it in 10 seconds. IMO, thats why they are fixing MB now, before that content is actually added.


XarekdaWizzie from Graffe's:
I am FOR all the nerfs, simply for the fact that the game must change. There is no way in hell I would play PoP if it was another luclin, with 1.5 hour killing marathons for 1 named, and mind numbing clearings (ok, well it might still be mind numbing clearings, but at least ill be semi-at the keyboard during the named take down!)
Against
jrapp from Westwood Tavern:
Give every Wizard who ever put any points into Mental Clarity the option of getting all of the MC points back. Almost none would have taken this skill when they did, but for MB.


flevius KoT from Graffe's:
Manaburn is the class-defining AA skill for wizards. It is not as useful as dire charm or Archery mastery/endless quiver, but it's what we were given. Changing it leaves wizards without a real class-defining AA skill, and is not acceptable.

Mod Rod

For
usrbinperl from Graffe's:
By Nerfing rods, they can now make fights were casters do run out of mana, thus tactics become more important than just a well timed CH rotation. By denying Wizards endless mana (and the ability for each Wiz on the raid to MB the boss mob once) , it significantly lowers Wizard sustained damage, so it means that if you don't kill a regen'ing mob quickly, it will out regen your damage (unless you have metric assloads of melees lying around). This again leads to a reliance on tactics rather than mindless nuking/roding. Now, throw in the possibility that some mobs might have uber damage mitigation, or even weapon immunity like the last mob in the BST epic, and you really have to plan and execute well to kill big mobs.

Against
flevius KoT from Graffe's:
While mod rods make a complete heal rotation easier to maintain, they offer a variety of other utility that will be broken by the proposed change. One example is manaburn; without being able to mod back up, the new manaburn will be effectively useless. Dragonsbane and Giantsbane will no longer be nearly as effective if we're unable to quickly regain the mana spent. A group with an effective magician can stray from the traditional warrior/cleric/enchanter format with t-rods.


Complete Heal

For
Sylvene from Westwood Tavern:
We knew a long time ago that getting that at 39 with a level cap of 50 was one thing. Then the cap was pushed to 60 and with PoP, it will be 65. I think we saw that nerf coming too. Reducing it to 7500HP down from the original cap of 10K means that... the truly PHAT tanks are going to break that 10K cap in PoP.

Against
Any good arguments?

Monk Defense

For
Ididar from Westwood Tavern:
I think non-end-game monks might [hate it] but the end-game folks have been waiting for this kind of thing for quite some time.
Teluete from EQ Cleric Forum:
Verant messed up majorly with Monks and their tanking abilities... Monks were never intended to tank nearly as well as a Warr as well as having massive their damage output... overall Monks at high levels are overpowered and as they get more high level gear this becomes so blantantly obvious it's a wonder it took Verant so long to retune this. This is going to *directly* impact the Monks in my guild.. and while I sympathize with them the honest one will admit that they were over-powered.
Against
flevius KoT from Graffe's:
Monks have become the premier pullers in the game at all levels of play. They already die more than any other class on a raid when pulling. This change will make them die even more, and limit their ability to regain the experience in non-raid settings.

Vendarus from Monkly Business:
Now, if they want to nerf our AC to take us more out of the role of being able to take some hits and have a lot of endurance to that of a frail guy able to deal out a lot of punches but not take them, thats fine. But we better get some offensive increases.

Overall

For
Asurman from Westwood Tavern:
I like it. If encounters are quicker it would promote individual skill and decision making, and i'm a fan of that. But i'm also really worried. To do this properly will require a complete rebalancing of every endgame mob, and many classes. Honestly, i'm not sure they can pull it off. It sounds more like something they should be doing with EQ2 from the beginning, theres just so much to break with EQ as it stands. I'm predicting that endgame EQ is going to be an absolute mess for a while. Its a big risk for Verant to do this imho.

Against
Thuriniel from Westwood Tavern:
I would imagine that more than a few folks will give up after this. Especially after all the promises that Complete Heal and Manaburn were "working as intended".

flevius KoT from Graffe's:
The classes that are being adjusted here are not unbalanced, they're just bad at public relations. I could easily point out AA skills, spells, and abilities of other classes that are far more beneficial than the ones being changed. I'm not interested in focusing your ire on other unsuspecting players who believe in good faith that the abilities and skills they've invested so much time to gain will be left unchanged, however. I do ask you to ask yourselves honestly though, how much of the desire for change comes from whining and class envy, and how much comes from actual need for change?
