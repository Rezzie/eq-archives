On Bards

Greetings to all.

Here are a few things I have learned about playing a bard in my lifetime in Norrath. Before I begin, let me stress that hotkeys are utterly important to a bard. You use them for your songs, and to make switching from weapons to instruments faster, for warnings, and at higher levels, to give fast commands to charmed creatures. You should have hot keys set up for all eight song slots, your melee weapons, your melee attack, at least three inventory slots, and for any other skill you use frequently such as sense heading or forage.

First, on twisting- Hotkeys are indispensable to twisting songs. Choose one page of hotkeys, and place therein the six songs you use most frequently in and after battle. You should always include in this page Selo's Accelerando and Hymn of Restoration, with the other four songs being variable to the situation. I generally have Brusco's Boastful Bellow, Largo's Melodic Binding, Selo's Consonant Chain, and Anthem de Arms in the remaining four. Twisting is a skill that requires practice, but as you become adept you can balance three songs in battle with ease, even as you busily slice or crush the mob (or mobs) into pulp.

To twist, hit the hotkey for the first song you want. For me, this is usually Bellow, to get the mob's attention. When you see in your chat box the message that your song has taken effect, quickly switch to your next song, wait for it to take effect, then on to the third. As soon as the third song has taken effect, go back to the first and repeat the process. In this way, you can enhance your party's attacks while slowing/hurting the mob you are attacking. Second, after the battle, you should always start the song Hymn of Restoration. Do not attempt to use this song during a battle to save a mate, as it's effects are too slow in comparison with the damage most mobs do. If a mate is in danger of dying, switch on Selo's and get them the heck out of there.

After the battle, while your party is resting and medding for the next attack is time to bring out your lute, which will considerably enhance the effects of Hymn. Your party will heal much faster, and be ready to face the next creature in hardly any time at all, and clerics, druids, and shamans will love you for saving them mana that they would otherwise have had to use in healing the party.

Some special notes on twisting- Some songs you should not/cannot twist. Hymn of Restoration you should not try to twist with other songs, as it does it's healing only when it pulses, and does not carry over between pulses. Other songs, like Shauri's Sonorous Clouding you cannot twist, as starting a new song will immediately cancel it's effect.

On kiting, and hunting solo- Bards are perhaps uniquely suited to hunting alone, and I have found that we can "kite" creatures several levels above us. This, like twisting, requires practice, but once you have mastered it, you will be amazed at the mobs you can kill on your own.

To kite a mob, first bring out your drum. You won't need weapons, as melee does not occur in kiting. Start your Accelerando, and go looking for a mob to annoy. When you find one, target it, and bellow once you are within range. Immediately go back to Selo's, and start running around in a circle. With care, you can stay far enough ahead of the mob that it cannot hit you, while still keeping it within range of your bellow.

When your Selo's has pulsed again (you see the message "Your feet move faster") bellow at the creature again. Switch between these two songs while circling until the beast drops dead behind you.

A warning- Always check what other creatures are in the area when you are kiting, and keep an eye out for others to come running to the aid of the one you are kiting. In some areas, it is a good idea to find a place where mobs are few and far between to take a creature to kite it. A perfect example of what I mean is North Karana. Depending on your level, griffawns and griffennes are perfect to kite, but you should take them either far to the north or to the south to kite them, as any others who see one chasing you will join in on the fun. And then there's GrimFeather.......

Kiting a creature can take some time, but seems to me a useful skill to have. There will be times when you cannot find a group to join, and bards are not a strong melee class when solo.

On melee- A bard's strength is not in melee, but they can add to the melee skills of a group. Anthem de Arms is a good song for battle, as it boosts the strength of all members of your party, making them hit a bit harder. Another valuable song to sing in battle is Largo's Melodic Binding, which slows a mob's attack and lowers their armor class. Be careful though, as this is an area effect song, and does tend to get a creature's attention. For some reason, mobs don't seem to like it when something happens to make them easier to hit..... Be sure the tanks of your group are up to the task of taunting an irate mob off of you.

Other songs, and abilities- There are several songs bards have which are often overlooked, or discounted. We have the ability to shadow step our entire group, a song which can be dangerous, but can also save most or all of your group in certain situations. We can make our party invisible, in order to sneak past a particularly dangerous mob, or let the casters in the party med uninterrupted. We can charm a creature, at least for a time. We can allow our party to breathe underwater, or levitate them above it. We can sing Clarity, to allow casters to regenerate their mana more quickly. Explore these other songs, and abilities, and use them to your advantage and to the advantage of your group.

Bards are the jack of all trades in Norrath, combining traits from almost all the other classes, and as such, are versatile and valuable players. Go forth, and make music wherever you go!

Felini Starsinger, Bard on E'Ci The Keepers of the Blood
