Guide to Blacksmithing

Introduction
Ok so your interested in Smithing? Want to make studs or other armor supplements? Sharpen your rusty weapons? All the basics will be covered here. It's a long read, but if your interested it'll save you valuable time and money.
 top


Things Needed for Blacksmithing
Like many of the trade skills in EverQuest, blacksmithing requirs that you purchase the Basic Blacksmithing book before you work. In addition to the supplies and materials you may need (i.e. ore, water, molds, etc), your going to need some skill. I recommend not starting until you have at least 10 skill points but you can start earlier if you wish (you'll just waste more stuff). Most cities have armor/weapons shops in which one vendor will also sell ore. For now, you'll just be working with and buying the small pieces of ore. The book can be bought just about anywhere that sells metal armor. Now for some races (Ogres and Trolls), Blacksmithing is a pain because only Oggok has a forge and only Grobb has a guy that sells the ore. Neither have anyone who sells molds that a low level smith will need. So keep that in mind. Most other cities have everything you need.
 top


Lesson One: Sharpening Rusty Weapons
To sharpen a rusty weapon you'll need: a sharpening stone (found at the vendors that sell ore), a rusty weapon (off skeletons or found fishing [my favorite]) and a forge. Left click on forge causing it to open much like a container would. Place the weapon and the stone inside then hit the COMBINE button. If you fail, both the weapon and the stone are destroyed. If you succeed, the stone is use up but you'll now have a Tarnished weapon! Failures happen often and can be quite expensive losses(I'll talk more on success rates later). A Tarnished weapon doesn't do more damage than a rusty one but has less delay time. An example, Rusty longsword: delay 35... after sharpening: delay 33. Not much of a change as far as I'm concerned but sharpening is good for your skill and heck, every little bit helps.
 top


Lesson Two: Real Smithing
Before you can make the metal items you desire, you must first smelt the ore into workable metal. To do this go to a forge and left click on it. We're going to start off with Metal Bits---the basic building block for most things in Smithing. To make metal bits, you'll need to place two small pieces of ore in the forge and one flask of water. NOTE: the pieces of ore must be separate, NOT STACKED! if they are stacked, it won't work. Hit combine(and cross your fingers) and hopefully you'll have succeeded in making metal bits! Make a bunch of them, you'll use'm up quickly.

The one item I get the most requests for is studs. You shouldn't try for studs until mid-twenties and even then, it'll cost you (more on that later). The first thing you'll need to make studs is a file. You must make this of course. To do so is easy, using the Basic Blacksmithing cookbook under file you'll find you need: metal bits, file mold, and water. Finding the vendors that sell the different molds you'll need is a pain but keep track of them as you explore your starting town. Buy a few file mold and head back to the forge. Place one metal bit, file mold and flask of water in the forge and combine. Files are relatively easy to make so you should have no problem. To make studs you simply put a file, flask of water and three SEPERATE metal bits into a forge and combine. That's how Blacksmithing works... just follow the directions of the book and you'll do fine.

Success Rates at Different Skill Levels
This is a early chart I've started keeping track of my successes and failures:

Skill Level: 10 20
Metal Bits 60% 80%
File 50% 75%
Sharpening 20% 33%
Studs 0% 11%

At skill 21 you'll begin to get a message when making metal bits and files saying "This item is trivial for someone of your skill level to make". Even so, my success rate was between 75-80% for these items. At skill 20 I attempted to make studs 9 times and was successful only once (11%). Not sure if this was just bad luck but I've heard similar figures from others. Figuring the cost of materials that's goes into an attempt at studs (over 500cp), that one set of studs cost me over 4500! Hopefully I'll be able to add the next column (30) with more items soon.

Of course the goldmine for Blacksmiths will be other players, but I think you'll find some of the data I gathered interesting. Advice for New Characters and Smithing
I guess if your interested in Smithing the way I would suggest you do it would be to invest 10 skill points in at the earliest possible point. Next, sharpen all the junky rusty weapons you find (I use rusty daggers I get fishing) and make as many metal bits as you can afford. This alone will advance you into the upper teens. Even though it's possible to make files at this point, I wouldn't yet because unfortunately they don't "stack" and tend to take up space in you bags and bank. Also any attempts at studs in the lower twenties is just throwing money away right now... I'm not even sure how much better it really makes the leather armor. Does it justify 5000cp in the attempt for one set? Not sure.

That's it. All my knowledge on the subject. Hope it was helpful. If you have any corrections or additional data feel free to submit it. Good luck Smiths!
