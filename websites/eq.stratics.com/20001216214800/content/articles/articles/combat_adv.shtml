Advanced Playing


 Preface   Groups   Fighting 
Preface
Here goes. Playing about lvl 35 and above can be very different from playing from 1 to 34. From 35 on even the druids (the king of soloing) find that groups can be a good thing. At this point I would hope you have a very good idea of how to play your character. In this I hope I can give you some pointers on 1. Finding a good group (very important in dungeons) and 2. Keep you from getting your entire group killed.
 top


Groups
When you reach your mid to high 30's you start to find your selection of places to hunt getting more and more limited. You also find that to survive in these dark and dreary places where everything wants a piece of your hide, you need a strong well put together group. Now what I am about to suggest is not necessarily the only combination that makes a good group, just one I myself have found that works well. A group consists of 6 players, one of the truly Needed class in the dungeons is a cleric (or if one is not available), healer type (druid, shaman). Personally when you reach the 40's you will find that most druids and shaman are not there as healers, since their healing spells are not as efficient as a clerics. Next needed in a group is the punching bags or Tanks (Warriors, Paladins, ShadowKnights, Rangers, Bards and Rogues) all fit in that categeory. Now I would suggest having only rogues as your tanks simply because they do not have enough HP's to be truly effective, but as a second tank to say a warrior they can rock with their backstabs. A good team will have at least 2 tanks sometimes 3, anymore and the cleric may have a problem keeping up with healing in a tough situation. Next would be a nuker or caster type (Wizard, Mage, Enchanter, Necromancers), some of the healer types could also be considered casters as well like druids and shamans if they are not in the group as the primary healer. There you have the three main groups. I myself play a lot with guildmates and I have found 3 Tanks, 1 cleric, 1 Wizard/Mage, and an Enchanter makes for a very well rounded group.

Now saying that, when you step into a dungeon and start advertising yourself off to find a group. Make sure you ask some very important questions before you accept a group. It will be in your best interest. 1. Is there a cleric? That should be a given, but don't be too sure, I have seen players think they can do without. I also usually find their bodies on the floor not long after. 2. Find out the makeup of the group, casters I truly believe jump for joy when they find out an enchanter or bard is in their group. All casters but you in a group tends you make you feel like a ping pong ball running from one caster to another as they burn and get attacked by the mob. Once you find that perfect group you will find it goes smoothly and fast.

Fighting
Once you are in your group and ready to kill. Then its broken down into your class as to what you do. I will talk mostly from the tank viewpoint simply because that is what I play. First if you are camping a room, and you are new to the group find out the timing of the room spawn. Very important so that you or whoever is the puller does not do a pull as the room spawns. That can be very deadly especially in rooms like the Lord and King rooms in Lower Guk. Once you have that basic info the fun begins.

I cannot say this enough, Enchanters ROCK when it comes to high level dungeons where you are very apt to pull multiple mobs. Their Mezzing and charming capabilities have saved this poor Paladin's hide so many times. And an enchanter is a given for breaking into big rooms, for that same reason. With my guild, if we don't have an enchanter available we don't even think of trying a room like the King in Lower Guk. It would be suicide. (some people would not agree, but myself I feel a lot safer when there is one). Now saying that, there is a very strigent set of rules you have to follow when playing with an enchanter. First is everyone must, MUST assist the puller. If every tank and caster is on a separate mob, you might as well not have an enchanter there. After you get the first mob down, the enchanter will usually point out the next to take down. This is important, after selecting the next mob do NOT attack right away, the tanks that have Taunt anyway should taunt the mob at least 2 to 3 times before engaging the mob. This has a very real effective of keeping the mob off of the enchanter. I know some people don't agree, but I have seen it with my own eyes. Try it sometime (with your enchanters permission of course), try taunting first then attacking and it has a much higher rate of not immediately pouncing on the enchanter, and try attacking without taunting first. You will see a difference and you will make your enchanter happy when he isnt constantly attacked. I know I have been sort of long on this subject, but I have seen this little trick not used and the enchanter dying many times.

Well, I am not going to get into the caster areas, for a couple reasons. One I don't play one so I am not going to try and describe how they should play with no knowledge of my own. Two as long as the casters know not to attack right off and wait a few rounds of melee, there is not much more to be said. One last little tidbit, at all costs keep the mobs OFF your Cleric.

With that I will bid you good hunting, and safe Travels.

Written by Saraheart, Xegony
