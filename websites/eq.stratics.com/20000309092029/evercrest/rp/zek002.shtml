Tarne's Revenge
Fiction by Zekk

"Yes, my lord, they have yet to kill one of our member. ", Tarne humbly spoke to his commanding officer, J'larda, of the Dark Lighters.

"Aaah, that is what I like to hear, and what of the one you killed Tarne, will he be missed?", replied J'larda.

"Perhaps only by the one he was accompanied by. The warrior I told you about, the one with the sheath made of dark elven skin.. He appeared to be of the warriors guild of Freeport. Even though, he is highly skilled and apparently one of our race had already fallen to his blade."

"Yes...Yes.., well, I shall dispatch one of my assassins to kill this unworthy opponent. You should have killed him, and for your insolence, you shall be placed under my discretion for one month's time. You shall no longer partake in any of our rituals, and you shall be allowed one meal a day. This meal will be bread and water. This will show others what we do to people who don't do their jobs properly. You fled from battle, a most dishonorable thing. Get out of my sight.", replied J'larda, dismissing Tarne before he could speak.

The guards grabbed Tarne before he could react, and dragged him to his cell in the Commons. Thrown into one of his own race's cells, Tarne fumed silently. He swore he would get that elf someday, so help him Innourouk. Tarne glances at his sorroundings, a bleak rock built cell with a door of steel bars. His hand went instinctively to his blade, but did not find it. Well, kudo's to those guards for being able to disarm him so quickly. Of course, they weren't of the First Rank of the Inner Circle of his masters guards. His hand found his dagger in a cut in his cape. The daggers hilt, made of bone, felt warm in his hand as he thought of how his revenge should work.

Tarne stood up, listening for the guards walking the halls of the jail building. Walking over to the door of steel, he shifted the dagger in his hand.

"Hmm", thought Tarne silently, "Let's see, a simple lock, what idiots."

Tarne drove his dagger into the hole of the lock, twisting it around, trying to find the place he wanted to. One thing troubled him as he attempted to pick the lock, the fact that he was going to try to escape from this prison. He would forever be an outcast to Neriak, and would probably never be able to set foot in the forest ever again, for it was a dark elven land and he would not be allowed in for penalty of death. His heart raced as he heard the almost inaudible click of the lock being picked. Tarne slid the door open, wincing at the loud "creak"ing sound. Creeping out of his cell, he glanced down the hallway quickly.

Weilding his dagger, still wearing his cape, Tarne crept down the hall warily. Coming to the end of the hallway, Tarne glanced around the corner, praying to his god that there was nobody there. Confrontation was not something he needed at this point. Pleased that there was noone wandering the halls, he fled towards his destination, thinking only of his revenge to this ignorant elf. Escaping out the door of the prison up and then over a hill of stone he sat considering his options.

5 seconds of consideration brought him to a conclusion and he headed toward's his own residence prepared to exact his revenge from the elf's hide. . The only problem Tarne had now was getting inside J'Larda's residence without disturbing the guards tower that stood right next to it. Entering his home, Tarne ran over to his chest sitting near the edge of his bed. Tarne opened his chest, pulling his longbow from it along with a quiver of arrows, which he hung over his shoulder. Out the door Tarne went, again heading towards J'Larda's residence.

PHHT! An arrow sped through the air heading towards it's target with superb accuracy. Tarne watched as the look-out guard was felled by his arrow, falling of the guard tower and hitting the ground with a thump. Tarne walked slowly to the fallen guard's now quite dead body and pulled a few platinum pieces out of the man's money pouch for provisions. Tarne stood up, gazing at J'larda's residence. He walked slowly, once more considering what this decision would bring. Tarne slipped through an open window in the house, gazing at his sorroundings. This was apparently the sitting room, where J'larda entertained company. There was a fire lit, sitting in a circle of stones.

Tarne heard movement upstairs, like someone searching rapidly for something. He made his way silently up the wooden stairs, slowly removing his dagger from his cape yet again. Upon entering the room he noticed J'larda bent over a chest searching through it madly. Gripping his dagger with renewed fury just from seeing this elf, he tapped on J'larda's shoulder lightly whispering , "Now you die Sir.". The last thing J'larda saw was Tarnes grinning face starting back at him. J'larda, now permenant wide eyes started back at Tarne in such horror that he grimaced for a moment.

Tarne turned his head towards the chest which now lay there open with things thrown all over the room just as he heard the guards yelling not 50 ft away from where he was standing. Resting against the upstairs window was Tarne's sword, which he quickly sheathed knowing if he had to use it it would be the end of him. Glancing out the window, he quickly scanned what was happening. The guards were doing a full search of every house in the area because of the dead guard. Pulling himself out the window, Tarne used the momentum to leap onto the next house's roof. Tarne hit the roof at a dead run, pulling strength from every part of himself he knew of and even part's he did not.

Escaping Neriak would not be easy, he knew, but he vowed to make it. One day he would return, and lay waste to these people that would condemn him from now on, knowing he killed J'larda. Exiting the forest with grim satisfaction he made camp not far from his homeland but far enough away knowing that the guards would not reach him.
