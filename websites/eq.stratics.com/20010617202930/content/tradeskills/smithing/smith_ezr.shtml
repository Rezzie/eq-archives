Beginning Smithing

 

Smithing is an expensive venture but hopefully worth while in the end. I've spent lots of time and vast amounts of money to improve my skill (now 27). My goal here is mainly to provide you with a guideline of cost along with some idea of what you can succeed at making at the lower levels and how one can start learning this trade

I started out pumping a few training points into it until he was no longer willing to train me in it. That took me to high teens. Then I sharpened every rusty weapon I could get my hands on. The cheapest blacksmithing skill. Stones cost 3-4 cp (copper) I'm a high elf with decent charisma. I found stones at the second windmill near Ak'Anon where they charge me 4cp and Freeport where they charge me 3cp. Keep in mind they may be more costly to lower charisma charachters. I would also smelt ore into metal bits when I could afford the ore (58 cp in freeport and about 67cp at windmill). At skill level 21 I started making files ( I buy my file molds in front of Kaladim for 13cp - one of the cheapest molds) Someone also told me that scaler molds produce the same files but I have not verified that. By the way, a file is needed to make studs. I was pretty successful making a file about 75% of the time at skill level 22. I decided to try a stud (I will tell you the cost of this later) At level 22 I failed once and decided to wait since it was so costly. Finally at level 24 I decided to try again since I had a nice pile of bits by now. I tried 6 times and succeeded twice. I also tried once at lev 25 and failed. At level 26 I again went to make studs. The first attempt was a success and I leveled to 27. Two more attempts also succeeded - I'm sure I had a bit of luck. It does not yet say that the items is trivial for me. Of course, I had to make the bits before I started studs and although it is trivial I failed 4 out of 15 times - 492 cp down the drain. I also tried two files and failed once. At this level files are trivial for me so I won't be making any more of them. especially since I found out that you keep the file now whether you succeed or fail at making the studs. Even if you did need to get a file it is cheaper to buy one then make one. you save 37 cp - more if you fail. Of course if making files is not yet trivial for you - file away, it's still the cheapest item to make. If it is trivial save your metal bits for bigger ventures.

studs require:

3 bits = 2 ore +1 water or 128cp per/bit (58 ea. ore and 12 for water flask) X 3 = 384cp
{incidently I can't use summoned "globe" of water}
1 file = 1 bits + 1 mold + 1water or 153cp (128 + 13 + 12) or buy one for 116cp
1 water = 12cp

Total Cost (I won't include the cost of the file since you keep it now) 396cp (this will of course vary depending on what you can get the materials for - the lower your charisma, the more expensive)

Keep in mind this is the cost per attempt. When one fails they are left with only a file.

So how much did two studs cost me at skill level 24? Remember I failed 4 out of 6 times so 6 x 396.

A whopping 2376 cp. That averages to 1p1g7s3cp to make one stud at level 24. Want a kicker? I'm not including failed ore smelts to make bits in this calculation since I had been storing bits from lower level smithing.

I started from scratch at level 26-27 to make studs, so we'll see how failing at making the bits factor in. I bought my ore and began making metal bits. Although, it is a trivial task I only succeeded 11 out of 15 tries. The failures cost me 492 cp (4 x 128) So I spent 1900 cp for 11 metal bits or 172.72cp per bit as opposed to the 128cp it should cost at 100% success. Then I attempted 3 studs and, by Tunare, I got all 3! So I can simply multiply that 172.72cp by 3 (3 bits per stud) and add the 12cp for the water and I made out with 530.16cp per stud made (not too much higher then the 396cp cost assuming 100% success. Much better then that 1173cp per stud at level 24 which didn't even include failed metal bits in the calculation. Of course, I could fail the next 3 times at making studs which would effectively double my cost per stud made [knock on wood].

That's all my research for now. I hope I wasn't too technical, I was attempting to be very accurate with my data. I'll try to keep and submit more detailed data when I get to a higher level. I hope I didn't scare you off of blacksmithing, it actually brings me great joy when I see that skill level go up, since it involves so much work. It's a great feeling of accomplishment. I can't wait until I make my first plate piece of armour.
