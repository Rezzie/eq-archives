As Ogg said, you could run a sniffer and either save the data, or (better) send it to a laptop you've got hooked up, and get results as good as or better than alt-tabbing to a sniffer in the background. A hack program could be run in the background and used by trapping keystrokes.

The reason no one has a hack out is because the EQ client isn't trusted. It sends everything to the server, which actually performs the action and sends results back to the client. That's why you get "stuck" halfway through any inventory action when your connection gets lagspiked. You clicked to put the item in your inventory, but the server didn't confirm, so it doesn't "drop" into the slot.

The only things the client can do "untrusted" are trivial little things like sense heading. Someone supposedly had a hack out to the client that would always succeed on a sense heading, because it doesn't bother to wait on the server.
