Playing a Wizard - Beginning

 Introduction   Creating a Character   Level 1-3, The Infancy 

Introduction
This guide is intended to be a reference for a budding wizard. It is divided into sections based on level and is wrought from my personal experiences.
 top


Creating a Character
Picking a race is a matter of preference for wizards. If you choose to be evil, then become a Dark Elf. Otherwise, I recommend Erudite, since they have the highest intelligence of all the races in Norrath, as well as an ideal city for a beginning wizard. As for assigning skill points, I recommend assigning 2/3 of them to intelligence to increase your mana, and put the other 1/3 in an area where your character is especially weak. For Erudites, I recommend pumping up your charisma to 75, so that the prices in shops aren't too expensive. Religion is a matter of preference, but keep in mind faction status when picking which God you shall worship. Once you have select it, prepare to enter Norrath for the first time.
 top


Level 1-3, The Infancy
Once you have entered Norrath, the first step is to find your guild and receive your robe, as well as some training. In your inventory there should be 2 letters, as well as 2 spell scrolls. One of the letters is a PK note, which lets you be pvp+ if you wish. The other is the guild invitation letter. Read this letter and follow the directions to your guild leader. Hand him the note. Equip the robe you receive, and then talk to the guild master again to receive your first practice points. You have 5 points to spend, and I recommend you put all of it in channeling, as it will make spell casting easier, as well as making it easier for the skill to be raised through practice (don't ask me why, but it works, and at level 3 my channeling was 17, without any further training!!).

After you have trained, scribe and memorize your spells, then leave the town, as it is now time to open a can of whoop-ass on some monsters. At level one you can't handle much, so you have to figure out what you can handle without getting killed. From my experience, I could take on snakes and bats, but it might be different depending on your location. To find out what you can handle, target a monster and hit C to consider it. If it is an even fight, then you can handle it, but don't attack anything above that level. When fighting, use spells as often as you can, but don't neglect melee combat since your spells won't be powerful enough to kill anything on their own yet. It should take you approximately 20 kills to reach level 2, and during that time, you should amass a fair amount of money from your kills.

Once you have hit level 2, don't go inside and train, instead stay outside and build up to level 3. Advancing to level 3 can be tricky, but very easy if you attack the right monsters at the right time. To do this, consider the monsters and attack the ones that are again an even match. But this time, make sure you are at full health and mana, as one of these monsters will likely leave you in poor condition once it has been killed. DO NOT attack these monsters when low on health or mana, as they WILL kill you.

If you follow these steps, you should be level three in quick order, at which point go back to town, sell any objects you have gathered, and then go and train at your guild. You should have 10 points to spend, and I recommend putting most of them in skills which haven't risen quickly. These skills vary from person to person, which is why I recommended you wait until level 3 to spend your points, so that you would have a better assessment as to which skills needed training . Once you have trained, buy the cheapest spells that are offered at the guild, and scribe and memorize them. These spells cost about 1 gold apiece.

Congratulations, you are level 3 and are well on your way to becoming a worthy wizard.
