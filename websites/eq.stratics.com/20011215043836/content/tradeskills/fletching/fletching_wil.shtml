Advanced Fletching and Bowyering

So, you are seeking wealth and fame young fletcher? I would advise you leave now. Fletching is about the art of making arrows, and when accomplained by its brother Bowying, is quite remarkable. Be warned though. If the temptations of the loot smiths can make is what you want, go talk to a smith.

I will pass the basic ideas of making an arrow and a bow, excpet I wish to impart this bit of knowledge before I go on with higher level fletching information.

  1. You will not make money
  2. This tends to be only for your own benefit, unless you are willing to work hard to make some of the more complicated bows. Then others may buy your bows.
  3. In my experiecnce as a Master Fletcher, the main source of damage comes from your Bow, so just use the cheapest arrow that has the range needed for your bow.
  4. Max damage down by a bow shot is determined as followed.
    Damage of Bow + Damage of Arrow= Max potential damage
  5. range is determined by the shortest range of the bow or the arrow.
  6. The difficulty of the item, except in Dual Cam bows, is determined by the most expensive item involved, not by the sum of the parts. True for Arrows and Bows

Now that we have those important things addressed, on to more information.

Here is a chart I have to get your skill up on arrows.

  • Use all basic components, raises you to 16
  • Use of a medium grove, raises you to 16, raises range though...
  • Use of a round cut, raises you to 36
  • Use of a parabolic cut, raises you to 46
  • Use of a Small groove, raises you to 56
  • Use of a bone shaft, raises you to 68
  • Use of a small cut, raises you to 82
  • Use of a hooked arrow head, raises you to around 100... I forgot exactly.

Some interesting side notes on arrows.

If you use a field point, woodshaft, small cut, and a large groove, you get a damage 2 arrow, that is only limited by the range of your bow. I thought that was interesting.

On bows.

Three string types, as noted, Hemp, linen and silk.
Wood types, Hickory, Elm, Ashwood, Oak, and darkwood.
Tools, whitling knife and a planar
Odds and ends: Cams

Here is what I have learned about bow making.

  1. As you increase Damage, you increase Delay. If you decrease Delay, you decrease damage. This is true with all bows, except with Cams.
  2. By using the different strings, you can tune a bow to the user. With an elm bow, you can make the following:
    Damage 13 Delay 51 Uses Hemp, no tool
    Damage 12 Delay 47 Uses Linen, no tool
    Damage 11 Delay 43 Uses Silk, no tool
    Damage 10 Delay 39 Uses Silk, whitling Knife. Called a Carved Elm Recurve bow

    This type of trend is common in bows. I have not made a Elm with the other two strings and a knife, so I do not have the stats for you.

  3. I do not know the points at which to try a fair amount of the bow things, since I only did that when I had a skill in the 80s, but here is what I have been told:
    • Hickory bows - skill 35
    • Rough Elm bows - Skill varies with string of course, but around 75 or 80 to be trival
    • Use of the Whitling knife becomes trival around 90
    • According to others:Rough Ashwood, around 100.

Other than that, I have little to impart upon you. I wish you luck with your Fletching young one. Be steadfast and true with your shots, and you will raise in power and fame. May the Goddess watch over you all.
