Thick Blood
Fiction by Murlune

This installment was written by a reader, not by the original authors of this series.

Murlune held a pile of bones from slain skeletons. He crossed the femur making a circle in the sand of the Northern Desert of Ro. The bones shimmered in mid-air and animated into a blood stained skeleton, leering off in the distance as it awaited it's orders. Murlune, silently handed the undead spawn of Rallos Zek a club. The skeleton cackled with glee, and held it in it's right hand, ready to do battle.

"Patience my pet, for I sense one of us has been slain by Light. He will soon know the thick blood between Ogres and Trolls, especially us Shadow Knights. He will soon learn what this thick blood bond means." The skeleton, obviously confused, just stared at it's master. It hissed questioningly, waiting for orders. Murlune just indicated with a hand gesture for it to follow him.

Murlune found Banegrivm standing stoically near the docks in the Oasis of Marr. Banegrivm didn't flinch as Murlune walked up and sniffed him. Murlune, speaking fluent common, which was very surprising for a Troll, said, "Banegrivm, I challenge you to a duel. No intereferance from outsiders, nothing tricky, just a match of force and wit."

Banegrivm accepted, and winced at a slap in the cheek. The skeleton knew it's cue, and attacked. Banegrivm easily slew the skeleton, to turn around and find nothing. Suddenly chanting from under the docks flooded around him, and dark green bubbled flew in all directions. Banegrivm dropped to his knees, struck to the ground by the force of Darkness.

A sword burst through the wooden docks, slicing Banegrivm's left hand. Blood spurted, but was quickly mended with some alien, Light magic. Murlune emerged from the mutilated docks, and touch Banegrivm with a withered, charred index finger. Banegrivm's screams ripped through the deserts, and, once again, a white glow circled him, as he was filled with vigor. Banegrivm's sword ripped through Murlune, slicing him from the groin to the neck.

Murlune dropped to the ground, dying slowly, as an evil smile spread across his bloody face. His last breath was a cryptic enchant, spoken in the tongue of Old Erudian. He had been slain, but the battle was not over. Murlune suddenly gasped in, then, as he let out his final breath of life, instead of exhaling the clean air he had inhaled, a green gas spewed from his inner core, and Banegrivm collapsed to the ground, blacking out. If Murlune couldn't kill him, one of his brothers would soon.
