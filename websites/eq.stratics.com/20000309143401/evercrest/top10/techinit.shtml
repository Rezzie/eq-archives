Top 10 New High Tech Initiatives Caused by EverQuest

(Company names are variously trademarks or registered trademarks of their respective owners, as real brand names that are spoofed here.)

10. Microsoft Corporation announces support for new ActiveQuest standard "We don't know what it means, but it's definately Active"

9. Apple's hardware reasearch teams are now shipping "QuestMac" -- not the cheapest or fastest way to EverQuest but it does actually work and it's available in your choice of designer colors.

8. Oracle Systems announces "Oracle E-Quest Solution" -- bringing our high price -- err -- quality -- directly to the massively multiplayer game community.

7. Autodesk Inc. releases "Auto-Quest" software featuring great EQ mapping services -- hmmm it looks like a stripped down version of AutoCAD with some macros? Big surprise.

6. Corel Corporation of Canada has released a new and excellent PerfectQuest EQ competitor but in a brilliant profit maximizing strategy they cut deployment and moved directly to sue the Canadian government for ignoring them as usual.

5. Sun Microsystems software division launches "JavaQuest" supporting roleplaying and hunting in a safe, sandboxed environment... Lack of a 'walk' feature supports their "run everywhere" philosophy.

4. Sources report the Netscape branch of AOL is set to release Questigator via the open software method. There are only a few bugs left to work out...

3. Inprise/Borland announced a upgrade to their beta of TurboQuest -- an incredibly fast, incredibly inexpensive, product from which they plan to lay off the entire development team shortly.

2. In anticipation of a soon to come corporate breakup, and because they just more money than they know what to do with, Microsoft Corpration is also working on a DirectQuest technology which does pretty much the same thing as ActiveQuest only more Directly and less Actively. Naturally they are not compatible.

And the number one high tech initiative caused by EverQuest...

1. Internet giant America Online will produce a new, user friendly and easily installed EQ competitor, with disks to be distributed free at every toilet in the civilized world. The catch phrase for AOL's opus will be "You got exp".
