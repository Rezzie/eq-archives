A Guide to the Dark Elf Magician


This guide details the path I took to get from level 1 to 40. There have been some changes in the game since, but most all of this information should still be viable.

Creation: Pick dark elf, because it you don't this guide becomes useless for the first 8 to 16 levels. If you have your heart set on being another race, do so and come back to this guide in a few levels. Put all points into intelligence that you can. This leaves you very smart and with 5 points left to spend. I see arguments for putting those into strength, agility or charisma. Strength would let you carry more, but starting at level 8, you can summon weightless bags, so putting points here is a waste. Agility is a good choice; it increases armor class which is always a good thing. And lastly, Charisma. Dark Elves have a base 60 racial charisma, which means bad prices. I put my points here because I don't like being poor, and your pet should be taking the hits, not you. At level 40, I've had no regrets about where I put my points.

Religion: Unless you want to roleplay so badly you'll shoot yourself in the foot for it, be agnostic. If you want to roleplay, be agnostic and pretend to worship Innoruuk. Worshiping the god of hate does really nasty things to your faction in a lot of places.
1-8: This level range is the same for almost all Dark Elf characters. You wander around killing things in Nektulos Forest. To save money, I recommend that you only buy one pet per spell level for most levels 20 and under. The differences aren't that big except for earth and water. I used water from level 4 to level 29, when there is no water pet for sale. Water is nice for its double healing rate. Earth is nice for it's root spell. I wouldn't waste money on the other pets in the early levels. Also, the newbie armor quests have been put in game since I was this level. I went back and did some of them though, because they were better than some pieces I was wearing at 35. You should try to complete these while you are in the levels to get XP for them.

Spell note: You need to buy at least some of your summoned weapons for use by your pet. I bought them all and they are still fun to hand out to newbies. If you are pinching every copper, just buy summon dagger. If you can afford it, the summon focus item spells from Lublin are also very nice armor for your level. You need to get the dimensional pocket spell to hold all that heavy treasure. Just remember they go away when you log, so don't leave anything valuable in them. And lastly, make sure you buy and use your damage shield. And lastly again, renew elements. It's hard to cast cause you haven't really practiced alteration much, but it can be useful.

8-16: In these levels, I hunted in three places: orc camps in EC, WC and Misty, darkweed snakes in EC, and elementals and drakes and such in Lavastorm. The orc camps are slow sometimes, and the money isn't wonderful, but it tends to be safe in a decent group. Orcs can take you up to level 12ish safely. Darkweed snakes have a very nasty poison. You MUST use a water pet to hunt these. Water pets are almost totally immune to poison. Darkweed snakes give decent xP into the teens. If you get hit and get poisoned, sit so your healing rate goes up to fight the poison. If you drop below half, get up and start bandaging yourself. If you've been practicing bind wounds (and with free summoned bandages, you should be) you should be able to keep up with the poison and not die. Lastly, when you get to level 12, you get two nice spells for hunting in Lavastorm. The first is ward summoned. A nice spell to blast summoned monsters with. Second is cancel magic. When fighting fire elementals, pull with cancel magic to dispel their damage shield. Without that damage shield, fire elementals are very weak.

Spell note: Spells of note, burnout and summon throwing dagger. You MUST get burnout. Keep it up on your pet. It is a haste and attack buff.. A very good spell line. Summoned throwing dagger isn't so nice and many people don't bother, but you can help keep your offense skill up by using them yourself. And it's a cheap no mana pull if you don't feel like blasting to get a pull in to your pet.

Also, at level 12 you get bind. I recommend that you bind in the firepot room in Timorous deep. As a dark elf, this lets you gate back to Neriak, Oggok and Grobb easily, and as an agnostic dark elf, you should not be KoS in Freeport, or Erudin either. If you want to use the other pots for a quick transport, make certain that you use invisibility before you go in. And lastly, remember that the Oggok and Kaladim pots look very similar. If you are going to Oggok and aren't absolutely certain you have the right pot, use invis. I died to the hands of those grimy dwarves because of that. If you want to fix your faction so you don't have to worry, go to misty thicket and slaughter large numbers of runnyeye goblins. This will make you non KoS to dwarves and halflings.

16-22: for this level range, I went to the Overthere. I killed Rhinos, succulents and cockatrices along the north portion of the zone. A good spot to hunt is near the north east portion of the zone where there is a large mud puddle and an iksar merchant who is not KoS to anyone. His prices are terrible, but if you have to dump something, you can sell it there. If you plan to hunt here like I did, you may want to hold off on binding in the firepot room. There is no convenient way to get to the Overthere from the firepot room. Bind in the overthere outpost instead. An interesting feature that I do not think has been changed. In the Overthere, all the creatures con glaring ready to attack, but do not. You can be sitting, medding and have a rhino walk over you without attacking you. The only things that will attack you are the Sarnaks that wander around. Also, nothing but sarnaks will assist each other in a fight. Speaking of sarnaks, there is a small fort of them near the mud puddle. Don't go near it. They are much higher level than anything else nearby. The money here is good. Creatures drop up to a couple platinum each and they all drop parts that sell for gold and platinum. Here's a tip. Bank everything saleable that you get. When you bank is full, make the trip back to Neriak to sell. The prices in the Overthere are hideous. You will get at least 30% more cash by selling back home. I came out of level 22 with several hundred platinum more than the cost of my level 24 spells.

20-26: For these levels I recommend the Paludal Caverns bandit camps. I believe that the cavern counts as a dungeon and gives an xP bonus. I can think of no other way to explain the ridiculous amounts of xP that I got there. I camped the bandits in a group from 22 to 26 when they begin to green out. I also leveled a druid here starting at 19, so you could start as early as that possibly. They drop some kind of chain armor that sells for some decent plat. The cash wasn't as nice as the Overthere, but the speed of the xP was well worth it. And your weightless bags mean you can hold a lot more of that armor than the others in your group, so you might manage to get more than a fair share, but be nice and don't grab it all..

Spell note: level 20 must get spells. Renew summoning. This spell is the strongest and last healing spell for your pet that you get, unless they changed something when I wasn't looking. It increases as you level fairly well, so that even at level 40, it's still useful. Summon Jade Bracelet. If you didn't get any other summoned caster focus items, get this one. An extra 10% damage to all DD spells is very useful. 24; Malise is a wonderful debuff that will help your DD's and pet attacks stick. If something is clearly gonna be a bit of a pain, I pull with malise. Not so needed at this level, but around 29 to 34 you will be glad you have it.

26-40: I spent most of my levels in this range in the Feerott. I tried other places, but the Feerott was the best. Go to the island in front of the lost temple of Cazic Thule. There you will find a series of temples guarded by lizardmen. Kill them. They range from about level 19 to 35. There are warriors and clerics. The warrior in increasing level are warder, defender, sentinel, protector, fanatic, zealot. The cleric types begin at Disciple, probationer (those two may be backwards) page, herald, justicar, judicator. The Justicars and Judicators have a 200ish point nuke that is a real pain. I have a Magic resist of 54 at level 40 and they still hit for about 150 to 200 most hits. You are warned. There is a diviner that spawns. He is an enchanter, so be careful. And occasionally a crusader spawns. I'm not sure if they are SK's or Necros. They don't have pets, and I don't think I've seen one harm touch, but they do fear and do other necro things. Be careful of them. The crusaders also drop the decayed armor pieces for the dark forge armor quests for SKs. At the top of the ramp into Cazic Thule, there is a pair of gorillas. They can be pulled without pulling the lizards nearby. Sometimes a silver flanked guardian spawns there. That gorilla will sometimes drop ravenscale pieces. I've gotten the head and chest pieces from him. So keep you eyes open.

How to kill lizards: to start, there are 4 lizards near the bridge that leads to the island. If you pull them one at a time from the south east side, along the rivers edge, you will be able to pull them without any adds. The first temple from the bridge is easy too. Each rear corner can be pulled without pulling anything else. The two at the front will always come together. The top can be pulled out from the side without agroing the lizards at the corners. After that there is a temple to the left and right before the ramp to Cazic. The left temple has three spawns on the top, two at the back corners and two at the front. The two back corners can be pulled singly. The two at the front will come together, but will not pull the top. The top three will all come together. The temple on the right has two on the top, two on a middle row on the front corners, and two right in front. There are two on the back corners. The two in the back can be pulled singly. The two in the very front can be pulled together. The two on the front corners look like they can be pulled alone, but they will pull at least the closer of the ones on the top, which will likely pull the other on top and maybe the other on the corner. Do not attempt to break this temple without a good group, a druid to harmony, or until you are about level 36 to 39, depending on what is spawned on top. The ramp to Cazic itself has a lizard spawn at each bottom corner. They tend to be higher level and can be pulled single. At the top of the temple ramp there is a pair of gorillas that will assist lizards and be assisted by lizards. They can be pulled down the center of the ramp without adds if you are careful. They will very rarely drop jewelry. Sometimes Silver flanked guardians will spawn. These will drop ravenscale sometimes.

The lizards drop fine steel and good money. I have made a few thousand plat here easily. Remember to sell your fine steel back in Neriak for the good prices.

Also, while you are in this area, kill every alligator you see. Don't bother with the swamp gators, but the plain alligators will drop torn words of mastery for the mage epic and the torch stein broom and shovel for the pet focus item quests.

These lizards took me to 40 pretty easy and still give xP. They will probably begin to green out in about 2 levels. From there, you are on your own unless I get there first and write an addendum.
