Evil races is general are discriminated against from lack of attention or inefficency of game design. Evil races are supposed to be HARDER not LESS INTERESTING. The arrow problem you pointed out is just the tip. We can't easy go to half the places or have access to most quests. The current limitations on evil races does NOT make the game harder. I can level just as fast as any other player (except for those silly druids and their possible game abusing SOW/SWARM). The problem is that because of the type of limitations placed on evil races I just need to do the same mobs over and over again instead of getting more variety. That equals less fun not more harder.

My personal opinion is that it is from a lack of attention paid to evil races. We see that in the form of lack of quests for evil races. Any goofy who quotes the difficulty rating obviously never really played an evil race. Like I said, I can level as fast if not faster than equivalent characters. The only difference is my hunting grounds are more limited and my choices of activities are more limited.

Finally, if terms of in game history, this whole evil vs. good system doesn't make sense. Evil races are harder to play and even worse more limiting in activities. Yet, there is no corresponding benefit to playing evil characters. For example, if XYZ evil race warrior at 50th level is more powerful than XYZ good warrior at 50th level then it would make sense. That does not seem to be the case. So, how in the hell are there any evil races left in the game? The good races, with all their benefits, would have long since wiped out the evil races. In reality, they should really get out of this good vs evil mentality. There should be more variety in factions. In other fantasy literature, dwarves and elves never really knew or liked each other.
