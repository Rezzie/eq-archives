Part I. A Good Day's Work
Something was wrong here, Simeron knew it. He could feel it in his bones and beard. The darkened street was totally empty of life. No beggars or urchins, no travellers, not even a rat was out...except him. He didn't like it at all but, he had a job to do and by Brell, he was going to get it done. So, with a heavy sigh, he hefted his hammer and shield then walked out of his small house, locking the door behind him. He wished to be with his fellows this night. Cragghorst and Mekkun were both off to the north hunting down a band of ogres lead by Filthy McNasty of the Skorchers. Both were skilled spellweavers, being a cleric and druid. and they complemented each other well. Together, they were nearly unstoppable but, they had to hunt Filthy down first and despite Mekkun's tracking skills, so far they had not caught up to him. They had found and killed several of his ogres and trolls along the way though, greatly reducing the number of their raids. Castro was off at a wizard's cauldron and Lodossx was busy helping to slay an invasion of undead. Suddenly, the glint of moonlight off an assassin's blade came streaking toward him. Just in time, he raised his shield and deflected the blow. The blade of the sword glanced off the shield with a loud clang and imbedded into the door with a thud. Quickly, Simeron swung his mighty warhammer, DwarGlum, at his assailant. The blow landed with a crunch of metal on bone and chips flew in all directions. He slammed his shield into the skeleton before him with all his strength further opening the wound. Undaunted, the skeleton tried to bash him down, to finish him. Simeron again parried with his shield and called upon Brell's power to drive this undead thing from the lands of Norrath. A blaze of blue-white light exploded from the skeleton's head and it fell in peices to the ground. Simeron scanned the area and saw a dark skinned female elf wearing Necromancer robes running off into the darkness. Sighing heavily, Simeron extracted the sword and gathered what few items of loot the skeleton had and proceeded to the clan hall for the weekly meeting of the Council.

Light from inside the hall shown into the street from the open door and he could hear the voices of his friends even now. Grimalkin was the loudest of course, with a chest like a barrel and the belly of a drum. Dergan could be heard calling for more dwarven ale to be brought and the meeting to be called to order. Each warband's leader, or Rik Drungi, was in attendence he knew well for Dergan's message had said there was to be important news to be discussed tonight. But, there were other voices too. Unfamiliar ones but, dwarven to be sure. So, with a grunt and a stomp he entered the hall.

"Hail and Well Met me kin! I be here fer our weekly meeting." he bellowed over the noise within the hall.

"Hail Simeron!" came the reply from his fellows.

Simeron looked about the hall and saw several new faces among his kin. Dergan, seeing his stare, introduced the newcomers.

"I want you to meet our two newest allies Simeron. This is Thorgrim, leader of the Grudge Bearers Clan and this is Grimm, commander of Grimm's Heavy Foot."

"A pleasure ta be sure M'Lords" Simeron said as he bowed to them. "What may we of Odin's Hammer Clan do for you?"

"You can vote to join the Mithril Alliance and help return the Mithril Age to the land and our peoples. Everyone else has already committed to this. You alone are the last to vote." replied Thorgrim.

Simeron looked on the two mighty dwarven warriors. Grimm or Thorgrim could easily be his equal in battle but, what was this 'Mithril Alliance' they spoke of?

"Well, truly I tell you, I know not of this alliance and until I do, I cannot vote one way or the other in good conscience. So, who would tell me about it?"

Dergan volunteered, with help from the others on different points, and soon Simeron agreed to the alliance idea. He also decided to jump into it in his normal fashion, both feet first and full speed ahead. He tossed the sword onto the council table and told of the continued assassination attempts against him.

"I say we go out and kill the whole lot of 'em. Damn Necromancers." Grimalkin said. "We're dwarves by the gods."

"Aye, that we should." echod Thorgrim raising his axe.

"I agree with you both but, WHERE do we find them? Personally, I'm more worried about me fellows than meself." Simeron answered.

"Well, now that we have formed the Mithril Alliance, maybe they will get the message that the dwarves have returned to Faydwer and are a force to be feared and respected!!!" Dergan said with more than a little pride.

"AYE!!!" erupted the unified shout of every dwarf in the hall.

"We have a saying that is most fitting Dergan. Barak Khazad!!! Khazad A-mienu!!! Axes of the Dwarves, the Dwarves are upon you." said Thorgrim.

"A good saying to be sure Thorgrim. We shall have to discuss adding that to our flag and banners so that all will know of our Mithril Alliance." Simeron replied to the nodding heads of his fellows.

"Aye, a good days work has been done here this night." Dergan said to the host of dwarves. "Let us drain the kegs of fine dwarven ale to the dregs in celebration of the Mithril Alliance!!!"

Outside a window, shrouded in darkness and invisibility, stood a lone figure. Her eyes were cold blue and her heart filled with evil. She had followed her target to the hall in hopes of getting a second chance to finish the job but, had instead watched and listened to the developments in the hall with great concern. A uniting of the dwarven clan would indeed be a terrible thing for her employers and all the dark minions of Norrath. She would need to tell BloodWyrm about this and hope that he would send word to the leaders of the other evil guilds to counter this threat. Moving away from the window and finding a dark alley, she cast Gate and returned to her safe spot. As she faded away, she muttered to herself,

"This Mithril Alliance will be countered and destroyed quickly. So have your celebration little ones while you can. Soon, the bodies of your dead will far outnumber those of the living. And that is the way is should be."

With a flash and a smile at her last thought, she was gone.



Part II. The Skorchers
Xiltara truly hated coming to Oggok, period. The ogres always looked like they were sizing her up for that night's side dish or worse, the next attempt at crossbreeding. But such was the problems of being a shapely dark elven female in a land where the combined intelligence of the entire city wasn't half of hers. It wouldn't be so bad but, on top of that was the awful smells of urine, blood, bile, excrement and who knows what else. Burning flesh mixed with it all as the ogres burned whatever they had caught for food. She had to say burned because ogres never really cooked anything. In fact, most of the time the victim was just eaten raw where found and killing the victim first was not a requirement for eating. But, even with all this unpleasantness, she had to make her report to Bloodwyrm, Warlord of the Skorchers.

How she had come into that ones employ was a bit comical. Ogres could be rather effective Shadow Knights, although no match for her own people's. And it was from one of these she learned that Bloodwyrm had gathered a group of ogres and trolls together for the expressed purpose of hunting in the commonlands for loot and food. Now, food was easy to find. If it wasn't an ogre, troll or able to kill you, it was food but, loot was a bit harder to come by. It seemed the humans in the commonlands would see the Skorchers coming and quickly take the majority of the women and children, the tenderest morsels, and the best loot they owned and send them packing all the way to Freeport if necessary to spoil the Skorchers fun. So, one day, they decided to explore a new pass they found that lead to her home. They attacked the lone guard at the pass, quickly overwhelming him. Then, they began to move into Neriak proper. Unfortunately for them, the first bridge had more then one guard. In fact, it had several highly trained guards and spellcasters at most times. She happened to be there when the Skorcher patrol showed up. Now, while ogres and trolls aren't known for their brains, they are not completely stupid. And the leader quickly determined that this fight was going to be more then they had bargained for so, he grabbed one of the shadow knights with him and sent her forward to speak with them. She knew exactly how she felt, for the same thing happened to her. In the ensuing conversation, not only did the Skorchers get to leave but, she became an "adviser" to the Warlord, Bloodwyrm. Her job had started out mainly as getting the people to flee toward the Skorcher patrols by attacking the opposite side of the town with undead and summons from the magic users of her race. Soon, she had grown enough in power to cloak herself in the darkness and thus, she began to scout the towns. Now, Bloodwyrm had heard from a dwarven traitor that several clans were joining together to return all of Norrath to their idea of perfection.

On a throne made from the bones of his enemies sat Bloodwyrm, Warlord of the Skorchers. Before him, a troll carver was preparing to carve up a frog-man for his dinner. How he wished it was a dwarf instead of that croaking thing. Dwarves were much more fun to prepare because they lived longer and therefore screamed more. He raised a small keg of beer to his mouth and drank deeply. He really hated dwarves. Always getting underfoot and trying to spoil his fun. Now, a dwarf had been brought before him saying he was an ally and had news to prove it. It seemed some of the dwarven clans had banded together and formed some sort of alliance. A Mithril Alliance he had said. Bloodwyrm was actually almost sorry he had decided he didn't want a dwarf for an ally but, that dwarf was fun to prepare. His attention was moved from the croaks of pain from his dinner to the shapely dark skinned female that entered his hall. Unlike most of the leers of his troops, Bloodwyrm's stare was not one of physical lust or hunger, but of a lust and hunger for the knowledge he knew she brought him.

"I take it you completed your mission Xiltara." he spoke clearly.

"For the most part Bloodwyrm." came the reply.

"For the most part. That means you DIDN'T complete it then. Just what am I bothering to pay you for if you can't get things done?" the ogre scowled.

"You pay me to stay alive long enough to bring you important news." Xiltara scowled back.

Bloodwyrm was actually impressed with her arrogance. He could respect it, but not tolerate it.

"If you wish to REMAIN alive necromancess you best learn you are no longer in your homeland but mine."

Xiltara understood the threat. She had indirectly challenged Bloodwyrm in his own hall. Not the smartest of things to do. But, the fact that she still breathed was proof enough that he was not overly angry...this time at least.

"My pardon Bloodwyrm. I did forget, you know how we females of Neriak are. I was not able to eliminate the problem you wanted. He was too quick and strong. But, I know he is not with his warband and I know why not." she said.

"So Simeron Steelhammer still lives. That is good I think. His death will be made sweeter when done by my own hand anyway. If he is in Kaladim, where is his warband and why is he not with them?" he replied.

"His warband was not in the city, not a single member. Where they are, I could not find out but, he was there because of his position as the leader of the Warhammers. There was a meeting of the leaders of the Odin's Hammer Clan's warbands and chieftain along with the leaders of the clans Grudge Bearers and Heavy Foot. They have formed an alliance to return all of Norrath to the "Mithril Age". I thought you would like to know this rather then having me kill Simeron and die for it."

Bloodwyrm did not like the sound of that. The Warhammers had been getting in the way recently of his troop's raids and now, he found the leader was not with them, but in Kaladim. Furthermore, it seemed his old enemies the Grudge Bearers and Heavy Foot had joined with this new threat, Odin's Hammer, to form one large dwarven problem named the Mithril Alliance. Blast that Thorgrim and Grimm. How good it will be to feast on those two dwarves. This was not good, not good at all. But, something would have to be done and he knew he better be the one to do it or the Skorcher might wind up wiped out as fodder for the dark elves.

"You were right this time to bring me the information. Tell your people this and that I wish to speak with them. I will send word to all the hordes of this danger. Soon, the Mithril Alliance will be no more then a bad dream. We will bind together against the common threat and crush them, with the Skorchers in the lead!!!"

Bloodwyrm waved Xiltara away and turned back to the near dead frog-man, still being slowly cut and torn to pieces. Its weak cries of pain and dispair made him hungry. And his thoughts of fresh dwarf being prepared for him soon forced even his grim face to hold a smile. Maybe this Mithril Alliance wasn't so bad after all.



Part III. A Rumbling of Thunder
Simeron sat on the dock fishing and thinking of rejoining his warband somewhere in the heart of the west commonlands. Castro had joined him and was practicing some new spells he had learned while at the cauldron. The recent attack by a dark elf necromancer had worried him. Not that he was worried for himself but, she had struck at him in the heart of Kaladim, the dwarven nation's capital. That fact worried him because if an assassin's blade could find him there, how much easier for it to hunt his fellows in the commonlands? He remembered how his band had grown, only to be cut down once again in battle. Darlentan, Kringe and Galendor were all gone now. Killed in a pitched battle with a Skorcher patrol. He himself bore a still tender scar on his cheek from the sword of a troll shadowknight. But, he took solice that they had won the fight, killing all but one of the six ogres and trolls and losing only three of thier six. Still, half the group dead was not acceptable to him. As he pulled in another fish and rebaited his hook, he could see the sail of the boat that would take Castro and him to Freeport where his Lok Trommi Cragghorst waited. They would join the rest of the warband in the west commonlands and then proceed to finish off the remnants of Filthy's band. He pulled in a last fish and put away his pole, retrieving his warhammer and shield while Castro finished meditating. As they stepped onto the deck of the ship, Simeron could not but feel something strange and terrible was brewing. Deep inside, he heard Brell's call and warning to prepare for war.

Cragghorst made his way through the streets of Freeport toward the docks. Simeron and Castro were due to arrive soon and he was supposed to join them and lead them to the rest of the warband in the commonlands. Freeport was everything he had come to expect from a human town founded on greed and corruption. Smugglers, Paladins, Necromancers, Thieves, Rangers and just about every other class of person could be found here. In fact, about the only races not found were the ogres, trolls and dark elves. And with a little coin, even these could get some unsavory mage to cloak them from view so they could slip unobserved through the city and onto the boats. The city streets had the normal beggars and travellers about, a millita man was having a heated arguement with a knight over who was the rightful law in Freeport and the odd thief was about too he knew. He worked his way down to the dock in time to see the boat pulling up to the dock and his friends disembarking.

"Hola Sim." he said smiling.

"Hail and Well Met Cragg. I have much news for the warband. Castro has got some new tricks to help us too." Simeron said as he clasped Cragg's arm.

"News? Good news I hope. We've enough bad news to last a good while."

"Aye, that we have but the news is good. Odin's Hammer has joined with the Grudge Bearers and Heavy Foot to form the Mithril Alliance. We are all pure dwarven clans and are going to work together to return Norrath to the Mithril Age."

"That is no small undertaking." said Cragghorst looking about him. "Especially from what I see here..." he sighed.

"Ah well...that never stopped us before did it?" Simeron smiled. "Let's be off to the warband now and tell them."

The high elf and two dwarves headed through the city and out the west gate toward the commonlands. Unknown to them, a human Shadowknight had followed them from the port and out into the commonlands. When they came to the warband, he noted the location and immediately reported to Xiltara and the Skorcher patrol. Xiltara decided to launch an attack at night with undead pets first followed by the two orges and two trolls that the patrol contained. As night fell, the pets were summoned and the whole group got into position to attack. The dwarves and elf were still in the same area and had killed a couple of trolls and an orge. The last of Filthy's band save Filthy himself. The pets were sent into the fight first followed by the foot soldiers. Xiltara stayed back and struck from afar.

Simeron hear the bones clattering before he saw the skeletons attack as did the others. He summoned the power of Brell and hit the lead one with a ward and then closed on it. He saw several others wards hit two others and knew the others were joining the fight. As he got closer, he knew they were in trouble though and yelled to his warband that these were no skeletons, but pets of some necromancers and to be wary of ambush. Three pets clawed and bashed the troop while getting beat down to pieces themselves. Castro let loose a bolt of fire that dropped one of them on the spot and a few more wards left the other two piles of bone on the ground. It was then that the ogres and trolls charged into the them howling with rage. Xiltara and Castro began having a wizards duel, each firing off spells at the other. Simeron was hit with two harm hands and was saved from death only by his own healing touch and that of Sandman. Cragghorst and Lodossx were healing, smiting and bashing the trolls while the Simeron, Haven and Sandman fought the two ogres. Xiltara knew she was losing the duel with Castro and why, she had no clerics to heal her and her life drain was not enough to compensate for their healing magic. One orge bellowed and fell with a loud thud as the other turned and began to run. Seeing this, the trolls broke also leaving her with no choice but to join them in flight and hope she could live. Haven let out a yell and started to give chase but was called back by Cragghorst. They were all hurt badly and each one was too close to death to go charging off into the night. Simeron had to agree. The healing magic of the clerics had turned the tide this night but, that was gone as was Brell's gift to his two paladins, thier healing touch. Castro was low on mana and all were dangerously low on life. Just then, a flash of lightning and clap of thunder broke the silence as rain began to fall as if to confirm the groups feelings of dread. Simeron thought to himself that the rumbling in the clouds was not the only rumbling done this night. He knew in his heart that the rumble of war would soon overtake this place and others. So, looting the dead ogre, the band returned to Freeport while the Skorcher patrol and Xiltara returned to Oggok telling of the pitched battle to Bloodwyrm.



Part IV. The Stormclouds Gather
Within days, the Skorchers had received word that many others were concerned over the Alliance and would rally to the call to destroy it. This had been what Bloodwyrm had been waiting for. The dwarves had made many enemies over these long years. The Crushbone Orc Clan, the goblins of Dagnor's Cauldron and the Butcherblok Mountains themselves, the Deadfist Orcs, the Dark Elves of Nerriak and so many more. Even some gnomes, necromancers all, had spoken to him of joining with the Skorchers in killing off all these dwarves. Yes, this was shaping up to be a very nice battle.

"The time has come to deal with this dwarven alliance once and for all. Today, I, Bloodwyrm, Warlord of the Skorchers have declared war on the Mithril Alliance. We will take special care to kill anyone that even speaks that name with anything but hatred." he told to his troops. "We will wipe out the very memory of this Mithril Alliance !!!"

In a hall far away, Durgan, Thergrim and Grimme sat discussing how the Alliance should go about setting up and doing its business when a high elf paladin knocked on the door and walked in.

"Well met dwarves of the MA. I am Palantir, friend of Simeron. I bring word that his warband was ambushed in the commons by a Skorcher patrol. All survived and even managed to fell one of the foul beasts. He said to tell you that they have received word that the Skorchers and their allies have declared war on you. We of the Blackwatch would like to help if you are willing."

"As are the Tal Kor." came a voice from behind him. A wood elf stepped into the hall.

"I am Celestak, also a friend of Simeron's. Well met to you all."

"It seems Simeron has been busy these long days in Norrath." smile Durgan to his fellow dwarves. "Too bad he seems to prefer elves to dwarves though".

"Aye," grinned Thergrim and Grimme. "One has to wonder if he has tree sap in his veins." Thergrim laughed. "But, you are both welcome. We will discuss how you can help us. And please tell us what our favorite troublemaker has gotten us into now...."

Simeron and the Warhammers boarded the ship bound for Kaladim. Along the way, while the others fished, watched sharks swim by or looked at the odd creature on the various islands, all he did was sit and dwell on the coming war. War brought so much carnage and waste of resources and lives. But, it had always been this way. Ogres and trolls were a dwarf's natural enemy as much as orcs and goblins were. Just as they had been part of the campaign against the Crushbone Clan Orcs and the goblinkin of the Butcherblok Mountains, so they must again set about building strong teams for the many skirmishes and open battles that would come in the near future. The good news was, there had been a dramatic increase in the number of members in the Mithril Alliance as well as several other guilds and orders wanting to help. The bad news was that most were untested in battle and had only the barest of training. Add to that the rumors of the Skorchers getting other evil groups to side with them and it spelled many deaths in the coming war. In all the warband's fights, it was the teamwork and trust of each to do what their job was that carried them through the hardest fights. The clerics healed, the rouges backstabbed and scouted, the warriors and paladins carried the brunt of blows and taunted their enemies to keep them off the rouges and healers. He had added friendly elves to increase the firepower of the groups and found that while elven mages couldn't take a hit very well, given a chance, they could inflict horrendous damage in a short amount of time and kill fleeing opponents without a lengthy chase that might bring other enemies to bear on the band when they were spread out. If the rare bard, ranger and/or druid could be joined, it opened up many possibilities. Many a time in his travels did the lively tune of a bard make the trip shorter or he find his spirit filled with lupine auras that did the same. And the tracking of rangers and druids was invaluable when hunting a specific target. As he watched Casto take a pot shot at a hill giant on a distant island, thus getting it to wade dangerously close to the boat, he had to shake his head. Yes, having elven friends was a good thing.

Xiltara had finally returned to her homeland. It was good to see it again. As she hurried to the guards and soldiers of her race though, she did not take time to enjoy it. A group of dwarves had attacked the guards at the pass and slew them. She had come upon the seen as the foul little pests were looting the fallen dark elves. They proceeded to head into Nerriak and she had to warn her people of this. It seemed that the war had hardly been declared and already the dwarves were infesting her homeland. Not infesting she reminded herself, not yet. One small band is not an army. But, it did have to be dealt with.

"Captain!" she yelled as she approached the first bridge. "Why have you allowed those accursed bearded gnomes into our homeland?"

"What bearded gnomes are you talking about miss?" came his reply.

"The ones that just killed your two men at the pass dolt! Now take your men and hunt them down and deal with this problem NOW."

The captain, who was not used to this kind of tone when someone addressed him, was incensed.

"And who are YOU to give ME orders might I inquire?" he hissed as he lowered his hand to his sword.

"Finish that move Captain and you shall serve as my slave in death." Xiltara hissed back. "I am Xiltara Necross. Of House Necross, first house of Nerriak and an initiate of the third circle in the Hall of Death. Do you wish to challenge me to a duel or should I just kill you all right here and now?"

The guard's eyes widened and the captain gulped, quickly moved his hand away from his sword and was visibly shaken. House Necross boasted the deadliest necromancers and shadowknights in all of Neriak.

"Forgive me please M'Lady, I did not recognize you." blurted the captain.

Xiltara's eye flared and her face grew stern. She wasn't powerful enough to even take the captain, let alone the entire group but, they didn't know that and many of her family could, though probably wouldn't. This would work to build her name among the soldiers of her race.

"Be thankful I don't have time to deal with you properly now. Go do as I told you and get replacements for your men here to guard this bridge. And if a single one of those THINGS gets away, I will do things to you that will make you welcome death gladly. Understand?" she said smiling evilly.

"Y-y-yes M'Lady. Right away." stuttered the captain as he ran off to get more troops.

As she entered Nerriak, she immediately headed to the Hall of Death. Once she had told the guildmaster of the events of the last few days, he brought it to the attentions of all the guilds and leaders in Nerriak. Over the next few days, it was decided that while Nerriak in general would officially stay out of the armed conflict, it would send ambassadors to the various allies of the Skorchers and allow any dark elf group or person to join with them upon Bloodwyrm's approval. As Xiltara once again began to cast her Gate spell to return to Bloodwyrm's land, she held not only this message in her hands but, she though, the final nail in the coffin of many a bearded gnome.



Part V. The War Begins
Bloodwyrm liked what he saw before him. The Orcish Horde and several other groups had come to stand with the Skorchers against the Mithril Alliance. Xiltara had brought word that the dark elves officially would not get involved but, that any who wanted to had to go through him to do so. Also, the black elves had sent Ambassador DVinn to Emperor Crush of the Crushbone Orcs to add thier wieght to the already large mass set against the Alliance. Yes, things were looking most nice for the Skorchers. Soon, many stew pots would have dwarf meat in them.

Turning away from papers on hit stone table, he headed to his throne for some more beer and prehaps a halfling or two for a snack. Just as he sat down and grabbed a small cask of beer, Darrig entered the hall very upset.

"The Alliance has gained more allies to stand against us!!!" puffed Darrig.

"So? We smash dem too. Dey all little tings to us big ogres and trolls." Brassdog replied before stuffing some dwarf pickles into his gaping maw.

"And who exactly are these allies Darrig?" Bloodwyrm queried before taking a long pull of the amber liquid within the cask.

"I have heard rumors that the Blackwatch and several other guilds are joining with them. This does not help matters at all." Darrig replied.

No, this was not a good thing indeed Bloodwyrm thought to himself. The Blackwatch had many members. He wondered how many would show up to aid those accused dwarves once the war came to its full fury.

"Just the Blackwatch? What other guilds are answering the dwarven call to arms?" Bloodwyrm said to Darrig.

"I have heard Tal Kor mentioned and several smaller ones. This will give them much magic to support thier fighters. We need to find more help and soon."

"We have enough now but, will have more coming. The dwarves are already fighting the orcs and goblins of Faydwer and will not be able to send their entire force against us. Also, we have the Horde and other allies that will add to our already strong legions. No, we dont need more strength. We need to attack. And I have just the plan for that." Bloodwyrm said with an evil gleam in his eye.

***

Across the Ocean of Tears, in the heart of Kaladim, Simeron sat and listened to an endless parade of speakers discussing the terms and rules of the Alliance. Some dwarves simply got up and left, stating that they were a member of thier clan but not of the Alliance. Other still were concerned that the clans would be absorbed into a single one under the Alliance.

"So much we've to do to prepare for the war and here we sit listening to idle prattle and worries of things that will never come to pass." he said to Cragghorst. "We'll never be ready at this pace."

Over the next few weeks, he, Cragghorst, Dorgin and the others set about to form a group dedicated to the Light and all it protects. Naming the new order after his beloved Warhammers, Simeron drew up a charter for all to see.

It is this group, the Order of the Mithril Warhammers, that have taken it upon themselves to be the guardians of those that are under the protection of the Light. To defend those that can not defend themselves against the darkness that still dwells in this world. To carry the tale of the War of the Gods that was the last age. The descending of the gods marked the end of one era and the beginning of another. So, things had changed after all. They had been tested and found strong. Both sides had proven they could resist the other and that in the eternal war of the gods, mortals could tip the scales. But, the lessons learn would be told to the following generations and kept safe my the Warhammers for all future generations. Thus, at the dawning of this new era of Norrath, it is only fitting that the old pass away and from it come the rebirth of the new.

I have penned this tale of the War of Ro for all future generations of Norrath to read and know what happened in those days when the gods tread upon our soil. When honor and bravery stood against vile treachery and betrayal. When good people laid down their lives so that you may breath free air and have a safe home. Know that since those days, my Mithril Warhammers have remained faithful and true to our cause and will never stop until all that is evil has been purged from our world. So rest well good people of Norrath where ever you are and know that the first line of defense is manned by my friends and comrades. May your gods bless you with your hearts desires enough to make you happy and may your life be as full as mine own.

Lord Simeron Steelhammer

Rik Drungi of the Order of the Mithril Warhammers
