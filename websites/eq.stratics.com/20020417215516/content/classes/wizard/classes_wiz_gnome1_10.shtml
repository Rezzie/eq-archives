Playing a Wizard - Gnome, Levels 1-10

 Introduction   Creation   Beginning   Preparing to Hunt   Basic Combat   Early Years   Conclusion 

Introduction
So you want to be a wizard? Where should I start? First off, let me state that being a wizard can be very frustrating at first; however, in the long run, all your hard work will pay off. There is a fair amount I should cover, so let me start with the basics.
 top

Creation
I'm not going into heavy detail on this except to say you should try and max out your intelligence, and if you have any left over points, spend them on stamina for hit points. I'm a gnome and I do really well, but Race is your choice and you should play what you want.
 top

Beginning
When you first log in, you have some food, water, a dagger, a guild note and a PK note. I suggest the first thing you do is go to the bank, deposit your PK note there (if you don't plan to use it), and then find some directions to your guild.(NOTE: The EverQuest interface is really tricky at first, and you can easily go from speaking with your guildmaster to auto-attacking them, so I also suggest you put the auto-attack button in a spot you know you won't accidentally slip) Once you've gotten your guild tunic, you can now go out to hunt. But wait! There is something you must do first!
 top

Preparing to Hunt
Before you set out to start hunting, make sure you are prepared properly. You must have your spells scribed and memorized. To scribe, you single left click one of your spell scrolls, click on spells, and click it into a spot in your spellbook. Once it is scribed, you then left click that once, and drag it to one of the 8 empty slots to your left. These are your memorized spells. Spells must be memorized in order to be used. You can't just cast them out of the book. (NOTE: I strongly suggest making your spells into hot keys. To do this, left click on it and hold down till the button pops out, and drag it to one of the 6 slots at the low left hand corner of your screen.)
 top

Basic Combat
As a wizard, you have your strengths and weaknesses, like everyone else.
The Good

You can "nuke" monsters as they run away. You blast away a lot of damage at monsters in a short period of time. You can cast lots of tranportation spells. You can usually get out tricky situations when the going gets tough because you are so far away.

The Bad

You have a low amount of hit points. As a level 8 wizard, I currently only have 136 hit points with spells up, while warriors my age have about 240 without spells. If you do get hit by something, you are almost surely going to die. If a skeleton gets a good stun on you and sends you reeling, for instance. It only takes a few hits to send you into critical hit point range, rendering you a slug while trying to run. On top of this, some monsters love to go for the person with the least hit points, which is usually you, the wizard, or some other spell caster in the group. Aside from the low hitpoints, you're really in good shape if you get into a decent group.

 top

Early Years
The early years of wizard (1-5) are fairly easy. If you are in Ak'Anon, which I was, I suggest you kill rock snakes. If you choose any other race, I'm sure there are rats nearby when you can wallop. At level 4, you get a chance to cast 2nd rank spells. I suggest buying only Gate and Shock of Fire. If you can find a group, I recommend trying out regular Kobolds. They will give all of you some decent experience for the time being. Getting to 5 is fairly easy. From there on is a different story. You can't learn from kobolds, yet you are too weak to take on many other things. So, you have to group with some older people, and just rough it through until you're around 8-10. At this point, you get another new circle of spells. I suggest first buying Shock of Ice which hits for around 40 damage at level 8. Now you can group and fight harpys, young drakes, orcs, and such. Now at first you can't use your new spells a lot because you have limited mana, but at around level 10, you're now a force to be reckoned with. There, that wasn't SO bad was it? From level 10, I can't give you any more advice (since I'm not there yet) except to say have fun, enjoy yourself, and find out what works for you. (NOTE: Jumping while running is a lifesaver. It gives you that little edge in speed over the monster to build up a fair amount of distance until you can get to the nearest guard. I know it has saved my neck a number of times.)
 top

Conclusion
Wizards will always be my favorite of any class; however, you may find your tastes are different. I've tried many classes before settling down on the wizard, and I suggest you do the same. We're not tanks. We're sort of the air support. Out of danger, and yet at the same time in it. I personally think all the spells and the mystical power make it the most enjoyable. So look around, find what's right for you, and if you decide to like spell casters, I strongly recommend the wizard.

Happy hunting, and safe travels.

 top
