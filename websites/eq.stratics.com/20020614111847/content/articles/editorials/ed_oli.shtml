Outside Looking In - A Guest Editorial

The Life of a Wannabe EverQuest Beta Tester
I was standing around the forge in a city known as Britain, otherwise known as the player killer paradise, when it happened. A blacksmith named 'Butter Nuts' was working on making the third set of armor for me that day already, since I had lost my previous two sets at the hands of 'Arrogant God' and 'Easy'. (Woe to the player killer who has a non-fantasy name.) As I was waiting to shell out a large amount of gold, in which I made fighting perilous battles against the lag beast, another fellow asked, 'Anyone here going to be in the Phase 4 beta?' Now, when I heard the word 'beta', I must admit I was stricken with a paralyzing fear of large amounts of lag and server downtimes that were as long as Yoda's life span. But, being the curious (and quite bored) person that I was, I ventured the question. 'What beta would that be for?' I asked. After 47 seconds of lag, my words danced across the screen in an almost readable font. He then replied 42 seconds later (The lag must have been down that day) 'EverQuest'. After some banter about what this game was, I surmised this person was clearly delusional. A game where you can turn off player killing? Yeah, right! And President Clinton doesn't use cigars as toys. But the promises kept spewing from his mouth, and there was a glimmer of hope! Minimal lag! True 3D environment! In-depth history! That was when I threw down my resurrection robe of oppression, and quickly logged off. (Well, actually it took me awhile to find a log-off safe place, and then there was a thief, Picky, I had to ditch, but I eventually logged off.) I started up my trusty browser, (always call Internet Explorer trusty, or Microsoft's spies will send you a virus to make it even buggier then it already is.) and set off for www.EverQuest.com. There a FAQ that promised pure online gaming goodness dazzled me. Much better than that other world I was residing in; dang what was that game name? No matter! I now had EverQuest. Ah, looks like the Phase 4 ships soon. I'll just sign up for the beta and---argh! No signing up anymore?! What foul trick is this? Well, no problem, I'm sure it's coming out soon-March 16th?! That's how many days? I can't even count on my fingers and toes that high! Ok, ok. Don't panic. Maybe this game isn't that great anyway. I'll see what people have to say about it on IRC.

'This game rocks!'

'I can't wait 'til Phase 4!'
'I feel sorry for all the losers that aren't going to be in it.'

Bastards! Now they mock me. I must have a CD, I must! And so my futile attempts begin. I withstand mockery and endless emotes of getting smacked by a large trout, all the while begging for someone with an extra Beta CD to sell it to me. But alas, I find no compassion, only endless taunts. The closest I get is a person telling me he has an extra CD, but he needs it for a coaster. So I turn to the EverQuest newsgroup. Again I put forth my plea: 'Need Beta CD or will burst into lag induced flame. Willing to pay gold, cash, or fake monopoly money.' Again, all I receive is endless torment. Actually, everyone ignored me, but that's not the point. Then I realized my mistake: I wasn't going to the source. I will put myself at the mercy of the 989 gods. (Yes, everyone at 989 Studio is a god. Especially a 989 employee that is reading this and can hook me up with a CD.) I send an email to them begging them, telling them how my pet hamster 'Boo' just died and I need an online gaming fix. But alas, there is no respite for my tortured gaming soul. As visions of playing a mighty Ogre start to vanish, I weep. I don't just cry for myself, but for all those gaming geeks like me, who will be running away from 'Froggie Da Killah' instead of enjoying the splendors of Norrath.

P.S. Anyone have an extra Phase 4 Beta copy of EverQuest? :)
