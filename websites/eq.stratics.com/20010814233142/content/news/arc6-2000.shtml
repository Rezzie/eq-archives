Morell-Thule Event, August 6, 3pm EST
MorellThule [ 6:02 PM EDT ] - Greebo
The Elves of Surefall, a guild on the Morell-Thule server, are hosting a festival in Surefall Glade this coming Sunday, August 6. The event will begin at 3:00 p.m. Eastern Standard Time and will run as long as there are people in attendance. If you aren't a newbie, there will still be much of interest, and we hope M-T players young and old will come and roleplay with us.

The New Item-Reimbursement Policy
GeneralNews [ 8:16 PM EDT ] - Ogamok
I picked this up here on the Official Everquest Forums.

Hello all,

I'm sure that most everyone is aware of the new item-reimbursement policy for loss due to bugs. For those that aren't, here's a quick recap:

Our intention is to handle all claims in a timely manner. Upon receiving a claim of item loss due to a bug, the GM will begin the investigation process (checking logs, backups, etc). This usually takes roughly 30 to 60 minutes to complete. The GM will then immediately take action and reimburse the item(s) based upon the outcome of the investigation. Our customers diserve courteous and prompt service, and we'll do everything in our power to insure that they receive it.

One thing that we should have made clear in the announcement is that unfortunately, this policy is not retroactive, meaning that those who experienced loss prior to the time that the policy was announced (7/18/2000) would not be covered by the new policy.

As mentioned above, we do need to perform due diligence when investigating the claims, and though we will make reimbursements any time that we lack proof of any misconduct, we still need to have that information (backups, logs, etc.) present to perform the search.

Before I get into the subject of this post, let me define the rulesets in play on each of the PvP servers:

Rallos: Complete PvP. Players can attack any other player within a 4 level limit. Looting is coin plus one item, providing that the item isn't bagged, NODROP, or carried in a melee slot.

There are no faction penalties for engaging in PvP combat.

Tallon/Vallon: PvP Teams. Players can attack any other player within an 8 level limit, assuming that the target is on one of the 3 opposing teams. Looting is coin-only. There *are* faction penalties for engaging in PvP combat.

As many here have noticed, there have been drives for each of the PvP servers, most recently Rallos, to get the server switched to coin-only loot. However, though the same drive once existed for Vallon, after the change was made, there was likewise a drive to switch it back to item-loot. This suggests that while there are people who enjoy coin-only loot, there are also people who enjoy item-loot.

The difficulty that we are faced with is trying to make everyone happy. Simply implementing majority will on each server doesn't accomplish that objective, as it addresses the needs of the pro-coin crowd without taking care of the pro-loot crowd. As much as both groups come here insisting that "everyone" wants what they want, I don't think that anyone can truly deny that both groups exist.

Right now, we have two servers where coin-loot is in operation, and one where item-loot is the rule. That layout is roughly equivalent to the percentages that enjoy coin-loot versus item loot. The third who enjoy item loot, though technically the minority, are still a significant portion of the gaming populace, and as such should have their concerns addressed as well.

Rather than just changing Rallos to coin-loot, it's been suggested that we allow people a one-time chance to move between the PvP servers. Though we are not yet prepared to commit to a plan of that nature, I do want to get everyone's opinion as to whether or not they would find such a plan acceptable.

If we were to allow moves, a two-step process would be used. First, Rallos people would be given the option to move to Vallon or Tallon (by choice), then Vallon and Tallon people would be given the opportunity to move to Rallos. If moved, players would suddenly find
themselves in a new ruleset, subject to every facet of that ruleset.

Former Rallos players would find themselves on teams, in an environment that discourages socialization outside of your team. They would suddenly be subject to attack in an 8 level limit, and would take race-based faction hits for any PvP kills made. Players with strange deity selections (e.g. Half elf worshipping Innoruuk) would suddenly find themselves homeless for all intents and purposes.

Former team-server players moving to Rallos would have their ability to engage in combat restricted to a 4 level limit, and be subject to attack and able to attack anyone in that range. Any race-based faction penalties assigned while on the Team servers would follow to Rallos, and would not be reset.

Players on all PvP servers would encounter an influx of characters of all levels, who wouldn't be completely familiar with any server-specific player-originated rules of etiquette that may be in place. It is possible that aspects once understood as a "given" within the server community might break down and be no more.

It may sound like I'm trying to discourage acquiescence to this change, however I just want to be sure that all aspects of the proposal are understood prior to anyone giving an opinion on the matter. If done, the change would likely be the biggest to be made to any server community in EverQuest, and we want to be sure that everyone is well-informed.

Thoughts?

More on Hell Levels
GeneralNews [ 12:25 PM EDT ] - Alethal


Given my time here at Verant, I think I can finally articulate this in a more accurate manner. I think "bug" is probably not the correct term to describe why hell levels are there. "Unintended Mathematical Consequence" would probably be a better description. Trying to put it in easy terms:

Basically, everyone knows that the experience needed at every level is a curve. Well, it's not so much a curve, as it is a series of straight lines. As you level, the "curve" gets steeper. However, due to the formula used, in the first level after the curve gets steeper, you must "make up" all of the experience as if the curve had been that steep all along. As such, a "Hell Level" is the first level where a new experience curve took affect. Ideally, it would have been nice if the formula spread the additional experience required over all the levels of the next range, rather than requiring it all in one level, but the outcome is the same.

As for 50+, it's probably safe to say that experience required in those levels won't be on the same curve as 45-50, and as such, any time the curve steepens, the "Hell Level Phenomenon" will be present for the first level of the new curve.

Basically, this is just the way it is, and we won't be readjusting the experience curves. They haven't been changed since release, and no one ever said that progression would be linear.

How many are we going to lose? That's hard to say. We all know that EQ has a finite life. Newer games will come along that will grab peoples attention, just like we've grabbed the attention of the current players. This is how the gaming industry works. You grab their attention and keep it as long as you can.

I don't think that this qualifies as a "rake it in while we can" policy, at least not in a negative sense. We provide a service offering and people decide whether or not they want to be our subscribers. This service offering is flexible, but only to the point that it remains the same offering. Given infinite resources, sure, we have tons of things that we could do for EQ, but will only do those things that make the game "EQ". We won't risk completely changing gameplay to the point that we'd lose the magic that is responsible for EQ's success.

If you recall, EQ was supposed to "Kill" the top MMORPG of the time (UO), and was supposed to be "Killed" by the next (AC). Niether of those things happened, as we all continue to co-exist peacefully. We each provide a niche that a varying and increasing number of subscribers enjoy.
