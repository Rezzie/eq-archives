Whats the Point?

That's one of the interesting things about MMORPGs: They are what you make of them. If all you choose to see or partake in is "camping items and exp", then that's all there is here for you. On the other hand, if you choose to believe and pursue something more, you can get as much out of the game as you allow yourself to.

Is there a feeling at Verant that there is much room for impovement or is it true that you guys are satisfied with the current state of the game as Brad likes to hint at?

It would be entirely arrogant of us to try and say that "EQ is perfect". EQ is however, extremely successful, and fulfills the general goals that was laid out for it. EQ generally adheres to the vision of the perfect game, but there is and always will be better ways to do things than what we chose for this incarnation.

Will you try to do things different on future games or keep the same attitudes and practices towards players?

I think it makes sense that any future products that we create in the MMORPG genre will try to capitalize on EQ's strengths and leave behind its weaknesses, much as competitor’s products will try to do the same. As an example, we would try to retain the things about EQ that makes it a very compelling game to play, and might try to rid future products of the aspects of EQ that people tend to be unhappy with (e.g. downtime). Though downtime is integral to EQ at this point, and will remain a facet of EQ, future products might focus on limiting its impact.

This morning's update consisted primarily of server-side changes to improve performance of the game engine in general. However, there are several changes to note:

*Spell Changes*

- Skin Like Nature has had its damage shield removed and has regained its regeneration component. We're certain this time :)
- Many Shaman buff spells have had their recast delay removed. You can now cast them on multiple people without having to wait between each cast.
- The Wizard spell "Rend" was improperly saving versus cold. It now saves versus Magic.

*Bug Fixes and General Enhancements*

The spawning problem in Solusek's Eye (SoldungA), related to the spawn of the Clockwork gnomes, will be fixed later today in a zone patch.

The +/= key will no longer take screenshots.

You can now adjust your clipping plane by using the SHIFT/- and SHIFT/+ key combinations.

You can now view the Message of the Day by typing /motd.

Corrected a bug in the /follow process that would cause your character to loop around to the right at times when following someone.

Corrected a bug that would cause item-loss associated with trading a container marked LORE.

Corrected some grouping bugs that had the potential to cause the client to crash.

*Feign Death and Pets*

The Feign Death spell will no longer wear off. This change had been made due to an issue that was discovered that allowed Necromancers to solo creatures while feigned and AFK. The change had some unintended effects, as we discovered that there are a few cases where someone could be legitimately feigned for a longer period. Instead of having the spell wear off, any summoned pet will automatically suicide 2 minutes after its owner feigns. This is the solution requested by most of the players who voiced an opinion, and should serve the same goal as the original change.

- The EverQuest Team

Gordon Comments on the Wizard's Role
Community News [ 4:17 PM PST ] - Ruri Kagato
This was found on the Official EverQuest Forums:
WIZARDS DO NOT WANT TO NE THE 'MOST POWERFUL' CASTER, THEY JUST WANT TO DO MORE DAMAGE LIKE VERANT SAID THEY WOULD
This weekend, I asked how the author defined "damage", meaning that damage is not a standalone concept. Damage could be defined as damage per spell or damage per point of mana, in which case Shamans or Necros would seem to take the cake. It could be defined as damage per "load" of mana, in which case Necros would seem to edge out there as well. It could also be defined as complete damage potential, in which case one of the pet classes (necros/magicians) would seem to have an edge. It could also be argued that Enchanters have the largest damage potential due to charm, and their ability to make a pet that hits in the 90s. I've actually seen an enchanter solo 5 spectres at once using charm and mez.

"Damage", from our point of view, is defined as damage over the shortest period of time that the character can inflict that damage. The statement that wizards have no rivals when it comes to inflicting magical damage is true under this definition. So, as to the quoted "Point #2" above, wizards do indeed do more magical damage than the other classes.
WHAT IS THE MOST REASONABLE INTERPRETATION OF VERANT'S WIZARD DESCRIPTION?

A. This means that Wizards can out-damage every other spell caster. In exchange for this power, wizards will be one-dimensional, lacking the variety of spells that the other classes have. Wizards will have no pet, no real buffs, no group buffs, no group friendly spells, no healing spells, no DoT spells, no Bolt spells, no shield spells, no disease or poison spells, etc.

or

B. This means wizards have no rival at chain-casting the 'direct damage' variety of spells. Thus it is possible that in a short period of time, a wizard might possibly out-damage another spell caster, but if you are talking about a 90 second fight, instead of a 30 second fight, Wizards will do much less damage than many other casters. And when you reach level 50, you will have to have an enchanter and/or shaman with you in order to be effective.
First, addressing option "A". Though I did say that wizards are rather straight forward, they are far from "one-dimensional" and they aren't exactly "lacking the variety of spells that the other classes have". In addition to direct-damage spells of 3 out of the 5 types (which no other class has), they also have roots, stuns, movement slowing (e.g. Bonds of Force), and travel spells, as well as a reasonable series of self-buffs. This is not a one-dimensional class. Now people can feel free to insist that all of those other spells "suck" and therefore refuse to count them in their estimation, but they are counted, and do not suck, in ours.

Option B, though essentially correct, is an entirely minimalist point of view that could be adopted by any class in order to emphasize their weakness. As for the suggestion that wizards have to count on a shaman (or other resistance debuffer) to be fully effective, we have no problem with agreeing with that. In the same fashion that a melee character needs to count on heals and buffs to be fully effective, it is reasonable (to us) for wizards to count on a resistance debuffer in the same way.

Now I'm fully aware that there are people out there who now dislike their class or don't feel it is what they had envisioned, but it is what we envisioned. I'll agree that there is not much I could do to help these people come to like their class, short of handing out enhancements just for the sake of enhancing them. Wizards are essentially well-balanced, and we won't be making any massive changes to them aside from the general refinements we make to all classes as time goes on. Much in the same fashion that rogues need to insist upon their value to the group and overcome the stigma of months and months of posts complaining about how ineffective they are, wizards now need to do the same.

I know I get flamed every time I say this, but if you dislike you class that much, I'll suggest you play another. That's why we provide 14 of them for you to choose from, since not everyone is suited to play every class.

Thanks for listening,

-Gordon

This is a dead horse that I don't think I need to go into again. Just to attempt to put it to bed, widespread binding for melee characters isn't in our plans for the game.
Make the melee stats STR/DEX/AGIL actually mean something. WIS/INTEL are noticable improvements to casters when they get stat increasing items. You have explained to the casters what these do for them but seem to hesitate when explaining STR/DEX/AGIL to the classes that use these stats most.

RAISE SKILL CAPS...or better yet change them for melee classes
Skill-caps are completely arbitrary. We could have made most of them cap at 100 instead of 200, but 200 was the number that was chosen. The skill caps alone are not the sole indicator of how well you hit, but only a part of it. Other things like your level versus the level of the critter you are trying to hit are also taken into account.

Raising the skill caps for the 1-50 game is not planned because the game was balanced with the caps being as they are set now. The end result would be that even if we did raise the caps, we'd have to decrease the impact of level and other issues in order to maintain balance.
Stop saying that ranged attacks by melee classes are too powerful. Fix this. Archery/throwing should cause just as much damage/hit as does melee. A well aimed arrow is no different than a well placed thrust or slash.
I've also discussed this in other threads. Melee is a primary form of combat, as is spellcasting. Throwing/archery is not, and never will be a primary form of combat. We don't wish to have people standing back in this game and plugging away at critters, doing as much damage as melee, yet without significant risk, and where the only balancing factor is cash. Spell-casters have mana to mitigate these issues, there's no similar meter by which to control archery or throwing. Though many good suggestions have been submitted, we consider ranged combat done with respect to the mechanics of it.
give crit hits for standard melee to monks, warriors, and rogues. Why they dont have them is beyond me. They all are masters at melee.
Warriors will continue to be the only class that gets crits from normal combat. With things as they are now, we consider melee to be essentially well-balanced.

Sure, all of these suggestions are reasonable from the standpoint of what could be improved, but we need to look at what is necessary, not what is reasonable. We receive MANY resonable suggestions every day (several thousand), however most of them aren't *needed*. We feel that the melee/caster disparity is pretty much covered at present. Melee users continue to be desired in groups, at all levels, which was our intent. They are considered “fun-to-play” by those who enjoy playing in well-balanced groups. In short, melee classes are not broken. They are looking good.

30 35 40 45, you lose a quarter bub.
31 36 41 46 you lose a full bub.
1 through 29, 32 33 34 37 38 39 42 43 44 47 48 49 you lose a half bub.

------
It looks like you lose more experience in Hell+1 because you have more experience total, and therefore lose a bigger percentage of the bar. For example, suppose level 30 takes 100M experience, and level 31 takes 30M experience to get through. When you die in 30, you'll lose a percentage of your total experience (NOT the experience required to level, but the TOTAL experience). For example, suppose you lose 5M experience. That will appear to be a quarter bar.

Ok, now, suppose you are in level 31. You have more experience total, so when you die you lose 6M experience. 6M is 1/5 of the experience required for 31, so you lose a full bar.

As I mentioned above, the amount of experience you lose (in points) is the SAME regardless what level they are. So, if experience loss were 10% (it's not, this is just an example), and a level 36 Human has 100M experience, he loses 10M. A troll SK might only be level 32 at the time when he has 100M experience. --------

    Gordon on Roleplay Server
    Community News [ 6:44 AM PST ] - Wurlaben
    The following was found on the official EQ Gameplay forums:
    Some time ago, on the wishlist forum, there was a thread where lots of RP gamers stated their desire for a RP server. I know the Wishlist forum is not the most popular one, and there is lots of garbage, so here it is again: We, the players who get odd looks every time we do a "Ho there wanderer", want a RP server. How do you (Verant) reply? Yes or no will be fine. And how does everyone else feel? Far as i can tell, there is not a single drop of RP in this RPG.

    We consider all servers to be RP servers since this is a role-playing game. Role-playing is a personal decision that each player makes for themselves. The joy for role-playing should be role-playing itself, not any tangible benefit handed out by the game. That's why when people say "There's nothing to encourage role-playing", I get very confused, as there is everything to encourage it, except perhaps ones own propensity to become self-conscious.
