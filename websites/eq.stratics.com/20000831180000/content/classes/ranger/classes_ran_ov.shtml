Overview of the Ranger

Rangers are a very interesting class. The pairing of utility spells/skills and above average melee make thema force to be reckoned with. Of all the hybrids, rangers are the most combat oriented, having all the warrior skillsexcept bash. This comes at a price however, as you are limited from all but a few magical plates (forget regular plate).This tradeoff is worth it. With your array of spells and skills, you make a great traveler, good partymember, and open a wealth of role-playing possibilities.

Being a derivative of druids, rangers use wisdom as their primary casting attribute. Their spells cover generalutility from armor buffs to healing, invisibility, and running really fast ;). They also share some of the wardsummoned spells of their druidic kin. Their skills are no slouch either, track and forage for example, help the rangerto be self-sufficient and always have a bearing. This makes them valuable in a travelling or hunting party, as theycan keep the group on track and in supplies, and hold their own in combat.

Rangers are limited to three races: Human, Half-Elf, and Wood Elf. Wood Elves may start in Kelethin, Humans inSurefall Glade (a small druid and ranger community outside Qeynos), and Half Elves in either. All are good choices forrangers, despite the currently dominant numbers of Wood Elven ones. All three offer their advantages and role-playingpossibilities, and the choice should not be made on stats alone.

The first 9 levels of being a ranger are hard and somewhat unexciting. You are a warrior with lower hps thatdoesn't acquire kick until level 5, takes more experience to level and can track. At level 9, however, the ranger comes intoits own. With the acquisition of a healing spell, you can reduce your downtime quite a bit, which is nice when there isnot a healer around. You certainly cannot be the combat healer in a group, as your spell is quite weak, but the downtimereduction is significant. Most of the other spells will be used seldom, save skin like wood, which you will tend toalways keep on yourself (hey, its free hps an ac) and snare, which is great for stopping runners.

The next big change for a ranger occurs at level 17. With a new rank of spells, the skill meditate (level 14) andthe ability to wield two weapons, you further individualize yourself from the warrior. Your DD and root spells help youduring combat, after which you meditate and heal yourself to full, beginning to drastically shorten downtime compared to thatof a warrior. The extra damage in combat and stopping of runners is incredibly useful too. You also acquire an outdoor onlyinvisibility spell, which makes travelling oh so easy.

Things only get better from here with the ranger. Eventually you will get other cool toys, such as underwaterBreathing and levitation spells. There is lot of fun equipment to wear, and parties will always want you around. Allin all, the ranger is a solid class that has is a serious consideration for anyone who intends to play a melee or hybridclass.
