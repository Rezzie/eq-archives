This is my opinion so far that I know of from past experience:

1. Strength: Not nearly as important as one would think, especially for pre-level 30 players(95% of us). Strength has not added more than one or 2 bonus to damage no matter what the weapon. I can fight as an enchanter in a group along with a level 12 warrior/tank and if I am using my 2H blunt, do 12 damage with a strength of only 60 base (70 with "strength"). The warrior, most with mino axe are usually hitting for around 17. Does't matter what the strength is.

2. Agility: Very important in my opinion, especially for melee types. Directly related to armor bonus's. ie. a piece of armor is rated as AC 5, but your agility of 95 makes that armor equal to a bonus of AC 7 on your stats. If you are a fighter, with many armor pieces, it can add up and make a heck of a difference.

3. Stamina: Not as important as suggested by game. Each class seems to get a hp increase dependant on their class, plus a small bonus or penalty depending on their stamina. Even if the bonus gave you 200 extra hit points, at a higher level, those 200 hps are gone pretty fast.

4. Dexterity: I think this stat is much more important than we all believe and than the game suggests. I have played with 3 different types of rogues, barbarian having the least amount of dexterity (100 I think) I made a rogue and put his dexterity to 120 and noticed a definate difference in performance. Anything that was blue or white was barking, screaming, etc. nearly every time I attacked. I was amazed at how obvious it was.

Wisdom: I have not played a wisdom based character, obviously it determines the amount of mana for a wisdom based mage. Rumors of other benefits are out there but seem to have not been proven either way.

Intellect: Obviously determines the amount of mana for int. based casters. Also influences the rate at which a person can learn their kraft.

Charisma: If you are concerned with prices from vendors then you should bump it up. Other than that, I have heard few valid reasons to fart with this stat. As for vendor prices, there is not a real big difference, but there is a difference.

Hope some of this helps out the new character a bit. I'm still the same old enchanter wandering the land and stumbling into giants, griffons, dry bone skeletons, lockjaw, etc. Maybe I will roleplay "Noquid the Martyr".
