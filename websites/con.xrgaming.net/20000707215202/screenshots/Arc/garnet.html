It was a warm day near Ak'Anon and Arcy, a lovely impish Gnome Magi was conducting her daily sweep of those things that she felt needed fixing in the land of Steamfont.  By the waning hours of the afternoon she was laden with all sorts of bric-a-brac that she had taken from the spiderlings across the land.  The legs and silk were valued for alchemy, and her impish smile was proudly on her face.
Leaning heavily on her staff, she drew a deep breath and spied a small nook in a distant mountain that had previously passed her eye unnoticed. Curiosity got the better of her (as is common among Gnomes) and although she was tired from her travels, she felt overwhelmingly drawn to the landing. As she approached it, her fascination grew stronger and stronger. She thought to herself "It clearly is a landing big enough for something... but what? Gems perhaps? Could I be so fortunate?" Her imagination was cut short by the swift blade of evil.
Mydl Byrz, and his swift blades had ended Arcy's life before she should would have another moment of dreamy thought. She had traveled to far, and fell victim to his madness. As life was brought back to her crumpled body by the gentle Gods, Arcy was utterly stunned. Never before had any such thing happened to her, she simply didn't know what to do.
Her favorite staff was lost. As were the guild robes of which she had been so proud. Most importantly, her faith in Gnomkind would never be the same. She simply sat down and cried... Wailing about the criminal known as Mydl Byrz.  Another Gnome shouted out sympathy. As did another. Then came a new cry, that of a brazen fellow - Garnet bellowed out, that "Mydl was not worthy of the title as a criminal." Arcy was again in disbelief... and called out to Garnet, that if he were of such an opinion perhaps he could help to slay the evil Mydl Byrz.
Garnet was indeed up to the challenge and told me to join up with him at the windmills, for I would have to lead him to Mydl. He joined her as promptly as possible. There that sat and rested, and made plans for the attack on the criminal. Arcy was still skeptical of Garnet's boasting, and she suspected that he too was beginning to wonder if such talk of bravery was beyond his skills. Yet, they were prepared for battle.
The Gods shined on them and brought them a greater and deeper rest than they could've imagined.* Soon after they awoke, they began the journey from the mills to the hiding place of Mydl. Arcy's heart raced, and Garnet moved with the cautious steps of an experienced adventurer.  As they approached the platform, Arcy searched out everywhere for Mydl, fearing again he would leap from behind and end her life without forethought. But Mydl was no where to be found. Indeed, it seemed he had fled when it was clear that Garnet would join in the attack.* Her precious guild robes had been destroyed by his blade, yet all the other possessions were intact. And most gratifying, was the notion that Arcy's faith in fellow Gnomes was much restored. The new morn, was spent singing a song of praise for Garnet, as she skipped her way back to the city of Ak'Anon.  Her perception of life was forever changed, and yet Arcy knew it somehow had changed her for the better.
