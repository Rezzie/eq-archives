How to Fashion Patchwork Armor

To make an item of clothing made of patchwork leather, one must have a small, medium or large sewing kit, a ruined, or low-quality pelt appropriate to the size of clothing one wishes to make, and a pattern in accordance to what item one wishes to create.

Example: I wish to sew a medium size patchwork tunic. I take my sewing kit, and place a ruined cat pelt and a tunic pattern into it. I combine the two- if my skill is high enough, I have produced a Patchwork Tunic.

 

How to Fashion Studded Armor

Studded armor is a hardier leather than the simple patchwork, and is covered with silver knobs which give added protection. To create one, one must have a medium-quality pelt of the appropriate size, a pattern for the type of armor one wishes to make, and lastly, studs. To create them, one simply places the items into a medium or large sewing kit, combines them all, and if the skill is high enough, a piece of studded armor will be produced.

The number of studs required for each armor piece is listed below:

1. Masks require one stud.

2. Gorgets, Belts and Bracers require two studs.

3. Caps, Shoulderpads and Sleeves require three studs.

4. Cloaks, Gloves, Pants and Boots require four studs.

5. Tunics require five studs.

Note: Studs must be made by a blacksmith.

 

How to Fashion Reinforced Leather

Reinforced leather is the best armor that a tailor can make. The required components are a high-quality pelt, a pattern, a medium or large sewing kit, and steel bonings. The process for creating reinforced leather is similar to creating studded leather, with the exception of using steel bonings instead of studs. The number of bonings required for each piece of armor is listed below.

1. Masks, Gorgets and Bracers require one piece.

2. Caps, Shoulder pads, Cloaks, Sleeves and Gloves require two pieces.

3. Boots and Pants require three pieces.

4. Tunics require four pieces.

Note: Steel Bonings must be made by skilled blacksmiths.
