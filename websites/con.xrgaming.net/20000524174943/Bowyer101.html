Bowyering

 

To create one’s own bow is perhaps one of the most fulfilling acts of an archer’s career. There is nothing like the swell of pride which ensues when an arrow strikes dead-on target, due partially to the fine crafting of the bow. The following words will give the archer- or simply the tradesman- basic instruction on how to create a simple bow.

For a Rough Hickory Recurve Bow, the simplest (and most affordable) bow one can make, three components are needed:

A Fletching Kit

A Hickory Bow Staff

Hemp Twine

These are the statistics of this particular bow:

Damage

10

Delay

50

Weight

40

To construct this bow, one must place the staff and twine inside of the fletching kit, and combine them. If the skill be high enough, a bow will be created! This bow is the simplest- and as such, even a bowyer with a relatively low skill can attempt it and succeed.

Instructions for a slightly more difficult cousin to the Rough Hickory Bow uses silk string as opposed to the rough Hemp Twine. This bow provides a bit less damage, but flies much further than the standard bow.
