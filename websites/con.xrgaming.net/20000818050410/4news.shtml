Sneak Peak of Next Expansion
Sony has released a Sneak Peak at the next expansion pack. Here is some of what they had to say:
Before recorded time, Veeshan the great Crystalline Dragon marked Norrath by striking the continent of Velious with her foreclaw, and deposited her brood there to dwell. The legends of that time also tell that Veeshan blasted Velious with her breath, encasing it in eternal ice and creating a barrier of deadly ice flows and stormy weather. This marked the beginning of the Age of Scale.

The Age of Scale lasted untold millennia. From the tidbits of information passed down, it is known that a group of dragons mastered magic and spellcasting during this era. Additionally, dragons of power and influence among their kind gathered together and formed a hereditary ruling council known as the Claws of Veeshan, who rule the dragons of Velious to this day. Few of Norrath’s inhabitants know more of this Age, and none that do would dare to speak.

n the Elder Age, the Ogres, Giants and Goblins conquered much of Norrath and began an assault on the Plane of Earth, precipitating retribution from The Rathe in the form of a great curse against all of Rallos Zek’s creations. The fertile homelands of the Giants (now known as Everfrost) were frozen, and those giants caught in the epicenter of the curse were transformed wholly into immortal creatures of living ice. Those nearby were struck to the core of their beings, becoming lesser creatures in mind and body than they had been. Those few who escaped the effects fled Tunaria for other lands, coming to the attention of Prexus the Oceanlord, who sent a great storm to destroy them. The storm drove many ships back to Tunaria and to Kunark, where the curse of The Rathe took hold to a lesser degree than in Everfrost. Many of the most powerful giants managed to escape the storm thanks to their great powers, and passed through the Frigid Barrier to land upon the Icy Fingers in Velious. The Kromrif established themselves in the Eastern Wastes and lived as nomads, eventually coming into conflict with the Coldain. The Kromzek were the most powerful of all Rallos Zek’s creations. Not content with a nomadic lifestyle, they established a mighty fortress city named Kael Drakkal in the Great Scar of Veeshan. The Claws of Veeshan took exception to the intrusion of the Kromzek upon their sacred lands, and thus began the long conflict between the two mightiest races on Norrath, in this most inhospitable of all places. Ages passed, leaving the eastern regions of Velious largely in the control of the Giants. It was during this time that the ships of the Coldain, or Ice Dwarves, were wrecked upon the ice of the Frigid Barrier. Those few who survived to reach the shore escaped the notice of the Claws of Veeshan, but were devastated in a conflict with the fearsome Kromrif. Fleeing the giants, the Coldain established Thurgadin, a hidden underground fortress in the mountains. Since these times, none of the other races of Norrath have dared to approach the Frigid Barrier, and thus Velious remained undiscovered.

In the present day, the Gnomes of Ak’anon have revealed an ocean vessel with the capability of navigating the roughest seas and breaking through ice flows. Upon it’s maiden voyage, the Gnomish Icebreaker successfully penetrated the Frigid Barrier and discovered the islands known as the Icy Fingers. Upon the largest of the islands, the crew discovered an ancient and massive bridge, which arced away into the mists and revealed the tip of a massive glacial continent – Velious! For a price, these Gnomes and their allies will offer the brave adventurers of Norrath passage to these frozen, uncharted lands.

You may be one of the first to explore this deadly and forbidding icy land. Your feet may be the first tread its deadly caverns and ancient shrines. If you are among the stout of heart, you may dare to enter The Scars of Velious. You are in our world now!

No Changing Classes
A player asked on the EQ Message Forum about a quest to change his current character class to another. While the idea was interesting, it would not be acceptable in game. Here's the scoop:
>>>I don't see why im not allowed to do a quest to change my class/race since in RP terms this would be perfectly acceptable

I really don't think that this would be acceptable in "RP terms". Your character has spent his whole life learning the way of the wizard, there's no reason that he should suddenly forget everything that he knew about being a wizard, and become another class, say a rogue, and be nearly as advanced. In the real world, it would be roughly equivalent to a master electrician suddenly becoming a doctor.

-Gordon

Changes On Test Server
Abashi, VI's PR man, posted about the recent changes on the EQ test server, and what changes they hope to bring live. Here's the news:
Hello all,

We're currently testing some new changes on the **Test Sever** that players should enjoy. I thought I'd come give everyone a sneak peak at what we are working on:

- Text messages that wrap will be indented on subsequent lines. This means that if a person or NPC says something that is more than one line long, all subsequent lines will be indented a few spaces. This should make it a lot easier to pick out separate messages.

- /trackplayers [ON|OFF] command. This will allow rangers (and only rangers) to remove or include other players on their tracking display.

- New file, EQCLIENT.INI in your EQ directory will allow you to set some interesting options: 1) Color of all text messages (by setting RGB values for each defined text type: text types listed below), 2) Default "RUN", 3) Default "Overlay" view mode.

The EQCLIENT.INI setup info follows:

EQCLIENT.INI documentation

If the file does not exist it will be generated by eqgame.exe with default values. If an entry in the file is missing it will be added by eqgame.exe with default values.

I Med Too Slow!
A complaint by some higher level casters is that med skill values stopped going up around level 50. Consequently when you get to level 60 you have alot more mana and spells that chew up mana faster yet you still meditate at the same rate you did 10 levels ago. Doesnt seem right to some players. So they asked Gordon lately. Gordon's answer is:
The "solution" for what you are mentioning is grouping with classes that have the ability to increase your regen rate. Bards and enchanters can do this for you, and can certainly increase it at a rate beyond what it was at level 50.

Putting in other ways to increase mana regeneration would unbalance the game. The methods available to increase the rate of regeneration do so to the greatest extent that we've designed for the game.

Spawning Copies of Dungeons
A player proposed an idea to the EQ Discussion Forum, involving multiple copies of dungeons so each player (or group) could find their own experience within. Abashi later commented on the idea:
We actually want to encourage people to spread out in the world and experience new things. Spawing multiple copies of dungeons would work against this goal. Another issue would be that people might have a hard time getting into the same "copy" of the dungeon when they wanted to. Finally, we would lose control over the rate at which items enter the game. I can see a situation where large guilds would purposely create multiple copies for the purposes of camping the same thing in those multiple copies.

It's not a good solution for a MMOG.

-Gordon

Necro, Master of Death
A player asked for an answer to why Necro's couldn't cure poison, but could cure disease. The player's reasoning was based on necros receiving both disease and poison spells. Here was Abashi's answer:
Actually I believe that Shaman actually have better poison and disease-based spells. Anyway, necros get cure disease because rotting corpses and disease tend to go hand-in-hand. Their ability to cast poison-based spells doesn't mean that they should be able to cure them.

In any case, I do think that necromancers have more than enough abilities as it is, so it really wouldn't be prudent to expand them into this area.

-Gordon
