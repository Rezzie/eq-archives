Just an idea which came to me today. If it works it makes buying ingredients from 'busy' merchants a whole lot easier.

*Digression to explain merchant delving begins*
First a quick explanation for those who don't know what merchant delving is. Each merchant has available for sale a certain 'standard stock', of which they have an unlimited amount available, plus whatever they have been sold by PCs since the last server reboot. Of this extra stock, only the first 10* items are shown when you interact with the merchant.

For example, Innkeep Harold in EC has food water, bandages, and other standard gear as his 'standard stock', and typically tons of rusty weapons, spider legs, orc pawn picks, etc etc that he has been sold.

Now, those of us in search of spiderling silks, or pelts of Quality (guess my trade ), or any other specific item, know this technique for finding out if a merchant has what we want. Simply buy out the merchant's stock of one of the 'extra' items (choose something cheap ) and one of his item slots will now be empty. Then sell the merchant ONE of whatever we are looking for (eg a spiderling silk).

If that item immediately shows up, then that is his only one, and we can buy it back and move on. If however the item we sell him disappears, it means it has joined all the others of that type in his back stock. So we click DONE, and right-click again, and if we are lucky the item we are interested in is now listed, so we can buy them all.

A way of making this easier is to have a container with a COMBINE button in our own first inventory slot, so as we are buying out his trash, we can periodically combine it to nothing to save ourselves space and weight. This is why it's best to choose a cheap item to buy out

*Digression to explain merchant delving ends*

OK so this is my idea. It concerns what actually happens when two players both interact with a merchant at the same time.

Let's call the PCs A and B, and the merchant M:

A clicks on M and sees standard stock plus first 10* items of extra stock.

While A is still in merchant screen, B clicks on M and sees standard stock and... what? Does B see the same 10* items that A does? I don't think this is possible for this reason: If A and B both see same items, and A proceeds to buy out one of the items, that item would have to disappear from the list B is seeing. I don't think this can happen while B is in the merchant screen.

If that's the case, then either: B sees the NEXT 10* items of extra stock, or A is prevented from buying out an item while B has the screen open. I don't think this second thing can happen either, what would the message be?

So that leaves us with the good possibility that B is shown the standard stock plus items 11-20* from the merchant's extra stock.

This means that with sufficient allies, it's possible to look through a merchant's ENTIRE STOCK, without having to buy out anything!

If this works it would be great.

But I don't know if it does. So anyone who's on before me, try it out. Get a friend to open up a merchant screen (choose a busy merchant), and then open up the same merchant yourself. Compare notes on what extra stock you are offered.

If I'm wrong, and B is offered the same extra stock as A, then there's got to be some fairly odd mechanism in place for when A and B both try and buy out the merchant on the same item. At some point, an item's got to disappear from the list one of them is offered. As I said earlier, I don't think this can happen.

I'll be conducting this experiment myself as soon as possible. Will report back what happens.

Nichola of Tunare
