Congrats Gordon for your successful guidance of the 300,000 head of cats that are the Everquest community herd. You probably now know EVEN more about your own personal strengths and weaknesses than you ever imagined possible.

We know that you care deeply about the community and have tried in every post to keep the positives in front of us while proactively responding to the negatives. We know that sometimes your only hope of guiding us through some of the darker tunnels was to remind us that there was a light at the end and it wasn't a train.

At the end of this tunnel has been an explosion of good. Starting with the Shaman improvements, Verant visibly demonstrated the realization that EQ is a living organism and feeding it is causing it to grow.

No standing game company that I am aware of has released patches that signficantly and substantially changed a product 18 months after original release. EQ did that with the introduction of Rowyl's changes.

No standing game company that I can recall introduced major new game features for 'free' (only have to be a current subscriber) to its original customer base without packaging and selling an expansion or new release. Verant did that with the introduction of Paineel and the Hole.

Of course, I'm certain that someone can cite exceptions to some of this, but I would argue that if it happened in the past it wasn't at this level of scope for the game as a whole.

I can't personally say that Gordon did all this any more than we can blame Gordon for all the nerfs that took place during his tenure. But I sense that he was passionate about everything he did for the players. I can also state with a high degree of confidence that we all saw Gordon grow personally and professionally right in front of our eyes.

I can't ever state with certainty that any of my posts directly affected the outcome of some decision that Verant made, but with Gordon in place I have this nagging suspicion that we were listened to in ways that simply weren't possible before he came on board.

Thanks for serving us Gordon, and welcome Alan to the wildest ride of your life!!!
