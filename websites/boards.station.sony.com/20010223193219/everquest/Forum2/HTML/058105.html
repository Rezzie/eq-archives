Test conditions do not always reflect actual game conditions. DD vs Line of Sight may not be equated under many conditions, landscape, number of players or mobs fighting a single target at a given time, etc. Pet damage is neutral damage, though the mage receives the hate. 100% of the damage done by a wizard is attributed to the wizard, but mages receive no credit for the pet dealing 100% of the damage unless no other player deals damage.
Additionally, the two bigger factors here are type of damage and and damage over time. Cold damage in the majority of zones is superior to fire because of innate resistances that players and npcs have. Less resistance = greater chance to do full damage. Additionally, the mage is limited to magic and fire based magic. Wizards have more choices and the lure line is designed specifically to have less chances to be resisted. The other important issue here is that wizards, who claim full credit for damage dealt, are able to deal this damage at a faster rate. Timing is everything in the game, and wizards are the most efficient mana and time-wise for killing something faster (and receiving credit for the kill).

Another factor you don't account for is that specialization makes up for this difference. A mage may also choose evocation specialty, but this choice limits many other important, mana-costly aspects of game play -- especially chain pet casting.
