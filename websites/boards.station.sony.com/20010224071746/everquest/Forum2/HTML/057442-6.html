I've read through most of this thread and had some comments I wanted to make.

I play a 50+ warrior (about to ding 56), I have no alternates and outside of a level 10 cleric I haven't played since about May of 1999 I have no direct experience with the casting classes.

Some people still say well caster_class_01 can still do this in a raid or that. Well that's true, every class still has some small thing they can contribute to a raid. But what gets them (I apologize for assuming but knowing how I felt as a pre-upgrade/RoK warrior at release I think I can relate somewhat) is they they kinda say to themselves well right now I'm about 10% effective, if I was a warrior of equal level I'd be at 100% effectiveness. After awhile that gets to you and many casters have switched to a melee class because of this.

In order to stop this from happening you gotta try to balance the classes to some extent for any situation. The side effects of not doing this and having the player base gradually shift to one side are staggering. Look at the markup on warrior equipment these days for example (not to mention it's general rarity to begin with). Us pure warriors who aren't decked out are on the market for warrior items but we can't afford them cuz there so many warriors clammoring for them the prices go through the roof. And no fault to the uberguilds but when extra warrior items drop in a raid respectable caster classes with up and coming warrior alts get them over the 'hardcore' warrior who's been around the block. I'm _NOT_ saying this is wrong - I know casters who play their warriors just as well as I do or better and so are deserving of this equipment - just stating what is becoming of the economy and such.

As far as creating caster items that would directly alter cast times, mana costs and target resists I'm kinda undecided. It's a great idea for facing ubermobs, but what happens to the casual XP group when casters all over have these? It'd be pretty nuts, could you imagine AE groups in OS using lower mana, lower casting time AEs that the mobs had a -50% resistance too. If you could tailor the effects to specific spells (like fire bolt spells only, or healing spells only) then I guess you could balance it between raids and casual groups.

Another side effect of reducing any kind of casting times is it will produce additional text output for clients and already with all these low delay weapons it's getting kinda hard on modem users. But the reverse effect could be that casters become powerful enough you don't need as many people to raid something and 10% more text output from 1 person is welcome if 1 or 2 more people aren't needed on the raid.

I do like the idea of a select set of no drop spells, I'm not sure I like them only dropping on raid level mobs though, perhaps questable spells that would require 1 common drop from any uber mob to get 1 spell. Something more common than "you're prolly gonna have to clear VP and most of WW to get 1 of your no drop spells".

Another side effect of increasing caster effectiveness too much on raids is overcrowding. If you only need 24 people instead of 36 people to raid something that's 12 people looking for something else todo among the vast horde of people on some servers (Fennin Ro) already looking for something to do. But I'd say it's a much better trade off to make casters more raid friendly and adding new content (zones) than keeping the status quo.

So this is just a warrior giving a friendly bump to a good cause that needs addressing.
