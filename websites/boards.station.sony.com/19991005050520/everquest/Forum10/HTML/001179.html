CygnusStargazer
Station Member 		posted 07-31-99 01:30 PM           
At the very top of my wish list is this...

Change the way EQ determines "who gets the kill."

Right now, the kill goes to the individual who did the most damage. If that individual happens to be in a group, then the group splits the experience and anyone in the group can loot the corpse. If the individual is not in a group, then they get the exp/loot.

While this may seem like a viable method, consider an example (taken from last night).

My group attacked a rare spawn. We were the only group in the area. An invisible wizard showed up about 3 minutes before the spawn. He was anon, but was red to the highest member of our group, so he was obviously a bit above us.

When the bad guy appeared, everyone attacked.

I hit for my max of 93 damage.
A wizard in my group hit for 102.
Our two tanks hit for a total of 60.
Another mage in my group hit for 80.
The two pets in my group hit for a total of 39.
I was the only one to get a second shot in, and hit for 56.

That is a total of 430 points of damage done to the baddie by my group... 391 if you take away the pet damage (not sure if it counts).

The lone high level wizard got one spell off, and hit for 175 damage.

The wizard got the kill, we got nothing.

Please do not get me wrong, I do not think wizards are too powerful. I do not think that players should be forced to play the "socially acceptable" game (in other words, if you want to be a jerk and steal kills, go ahead, just be ready for the backlash from other players).

What I *DO* think is that it is just plain wrong for a player who did less than one third of the damage to the bad guy should get the credit. If he had done more than half, then sure, he should get the credit.

If a group of six players shows up and does more damage to a beast than a group of four players does, then the group of six should split the experience... it should NOT depend on individual performance (unless of course, the individual is playing solo).

I am not asking for Verant to solve the social problems of the game, just give us the tools to do it. For example a magician is taking all the kills at a spawn area designed for charactrers of lower level. Right now, there is NOTHING these characters can do, because even if they group together, a caster 4 or 5 levels above them will ALWAYS be able to do more damage then any of them can do individually. If GROUP damage was used, these same lower level characters would be able to band together, combine their damage, and maybe be able to compete with the lone mage for the loot/experience. At least they would have a chance.

The designers have said several times that they want to encourage group play. Well, here's another way to do it.

A couple points.

One, I wasn't intending to bash wizards or imply that they are all kill stealin' bums.

Two, if a bunch of wizards grouped together, then they wouldn't be kill stealing, they would be killing the damn monsters... which is what they should be doing. I am sure that a group of 6 wiz's would do more than 50% of the damage to a creature in a contested spawn, and they would rightfully claim the kill. I have no beef with that from a game design standpoint. (As a player it would certainly upset me if they hogged the spawn, but that is a social issue, not a game design issue)

My post was not about "hogging the spawn" (which is one thing a group of 6 wiz's would be damn good at). If six wizards band together and take over a spawn point, then fine, at least they will have to do the work themselves, which is all I ask. My problem lies in the fact that right now they don't have to do the majority of the damage to get the kill, and that just plain sucks.

Some of you seem to think that if party damage was considered, then a group of 6 wiz's would be able to claim any beast they wanted. You are right. But, they would have to actually kill the damn monster themselves, instead of counting on another group to do 3/4 of the work, and then take advantage of the fact that they can do more damage individually then the opposing group did.

I don't have a problem with a group taking over a spawn point... I have a problem with an individual taking advantage of other people's work. For instance, a party of 6 does 100 points each to a monster, and a kill stealing rogue comes in at the last minute and backstabs the beast for 101. The rogue gets the kill. It should not work that way.
