All right, due to the impression that you all have given me, it seems that I need to cover wizards next…

I’ve already made several notes to myself about wizards, and I may as well start out this discussion with those notes. Keep in mind that these ‘talks’ are geared toward making sure that the classes are ‘balanced’. Please work toward that goal when posting (yes, even if that means you suggest a ‘nerf’). But I’m also interested in hearing about bugs and such too, I’m just not going to focus on them.

Here are the notes that I already have. Keep in mind that these are only my notes, and just because I’ve listed something does NOT mean that I like the idea or that the designers are working on making changes based on these notes. This is just what it is, a list of ideas. I’ve also added my notes to you all under the header of Note:

AoE spells: Mentioned elsewhere… (earlier on my list, probably under mages: basically the idea here is that maybe pets should be removed from the equation).

Gaze/Glimpse: Due to the clipping plane, these spells don’t really allow further seeing, just zooming in (which makes your resolution worse anyhow).

Note: this may not be ‘fixable’ at all due to the way the game works. It’s not like we can force your PC into a higher resolution when you use this spell…

Fade/Shadowstep/Yonder: There seems to be a problem with these spells. Since they will not teleport you up, they don’t work too well in hilly terrain; because the spell only sends you a few feet (stopping when it reaches a hill or building).

Note: Again, this may also not be something we can change, but it’s on the list.

Bind Sight: Seems to cause viewer to see the target as running into walls or spinning in circles if target goes up an incline.

Enstill: It seems that this spell is not a more useful or more powerful root, it’s just more expensive.

Cazic Gate: At level spell is attained, using it will most often cause death because the caster appears amidst many aggressive monsters that /con white to them.

Note: We’ve actually discussed this before. If I remember correctly, the general feeling was that having a dangerous gate like this wasn’t necessarily a bad thing. But it is also still on my list.

Tishan’s Relocation: This spell evacuates you to a very dangerous location, a monster crossroads in the Skyfire mountains. Could the location be moved, or was it intended that this be a very dangerous evacuation spell?

Note: See note about Cazic Gate.

Concussion: A level 39 spell dropped by a creature in a location that requires a party of level 50s to handle. Should it be in such a location?

Icestrike effects: Graphical effect looks like fire.

Lightning Bolt effects: looks exactly like Fire Bolt.

Other lightning and cold effects: It seems that there might be other spells that have the look of fire when they should not.

Having just re-read this list, I see nothing at all related to actual balancing stuff. This is all nit-picky stuff about minor effects and bugs and such (with the possible exception of Area of Effect spells, changing those would certainly have an effect on class balances). So, please, try to focus on balance issues…

Alan

Obviously, it wouldn't be a spell that one could use to kill-steal, and it would clearly need a target level limit to avoid powerleveling.

Those are just my two "weird ideas"--I'll leave further detailed discussion on numbers, mana-efficiency, and so forth to the more experienced.

That's a fairly effective way of determining the class's desire post-50.

It's been mentioned before by GZ that giving us O'Keil's Radiation (the 2dmg fire shield) at level 4 was a mistake left over from beta. Why then cement the mistake at level 30-something by giving us an equally useless spell, O'Keil's Flickering Flame, in Velious? A 5dmg fire shield at level 30-whatever is pretty amusing. I'm not sure why it was given to us. Some sort of more useful, personal buff instead would be much more in-line with the rest of our spells. Was this a mistake? A joke? It's a good one, if the latter. I can always get a good chuckle out of groups by casting it.

Atol's Spectral Shackles. Great spell... when it goes off. I'm not sure if it's intended or not, but at level 55 (it's a 51 spell) with a maxed Alteration skill, the spell still has the propensity to fizzle 4-5 times before going off. The problem is, it tends to "chain fizzle." Waiting 10 seconds after a fizzle is almost guaranteed to work, whereas trying to cast immediately after a fizzle is almost guaranteed to have it fizzle 5-6 times. Is this intended? Is this spell *supposed* to fizzle so incredibly often? I know many many wizards who refuse to use the spell, instead relying on the level 29 Bonds of Force, instead, just for this reason.

Harvest. It's a great spell. Very class-defining. I love it, other classes are envious. Well... until the high levels. Harvest from when you get it (level 34 I believe) up to around level 48-50 is great. It's scaled to give slightly more mana each level so you get the same "percent" of mana back (roughly 3/4's to a full bubble of mana) regardless of your level. However, it apparently was never scaled past that point when Kunark came out. After 50 it gives less and less. At 55, my Harvest gives me a quarter bubble of mana, if that. At 10 minutes a cast, that's less effective than the 1-mana-point-a-tick Manaskin. I've had relatively the same gear since 34, so my mana pool hasn't artificially inflated at all. I know of many wizards who bid a fair farewell to this spell in their lineup at level 45 or so.

To wrap up, an overall class complaint. Pre-Kunark, wizards were very well balanced. Post-Kunark, as far as Kunark material was concerned (levels 50-60, the higher mobs like Trak, etc), our effectiveness was reduced relative to other classes quite a bit. Melee types were given better gear. They could last in fights longer. Many classes were given special armor (Cobalt, Donals, Singing Steel, etc). Wizzies got, well, basically nothing. There were a lot of class-specific stuff added, but nothing class-specific for wizzies. All of our items were in the broader, "caster" class. This isn't a wiz only complaint -- many caster classes felt left out as well.

In Velious, the problem isn't the phat lewt. The problem is, the hitpoint cap on uber-mobs has gone through the roof. When you have a class who's sole purpose for existence is dealing a HUGE amount of damage, fast, to kill something before it annihilates the out-of-mana wizzie, this is a problem. A full tank of mana, our only source of damage, doesn't even NICK a Velious uber mob. Once our mana is gone - that's it. We have no pets, no melee ability, we can't heal, buff, etc. We can nuke for a sliver of damage, then we're through. Out of commision. Meanwhile the tanks keep on tanking, the rogues keep on ripping through the baddies with their Ragebringers, (incidentally dealing nearly as much damage a second as a wizzie can deal chain-nuking)...

Wizzies, on these raids, end up doing the least amount of total damage to the mob than any other damage-dealing class there. The *anti-thesis* of what our class supposedly should be about.

And last, but not least, our epic item. A count was done on Graffe's Wizard Forum (the most popular wiz class site on the web), pooling everyone's knowledge about how many wiz uber weapons are inexistence across the servers. We arrived at the number "13." Across every server. The ONLY reason for this insanely low number is ONE item - Cazic Skin.

Where other classes' uber weapons require huge packs of friends, planning, skill, and epic battles, ours revolved around one item which requires nothing but mind numbing tedium. To place an item on a 1-week spawn mob, which is ALREADY camped and killed within hours of his spawning even *without* the epic weapon taken into consideration, is silly. To make the item drop chance on him so low that only 13 have dropped in all of EQ is ridiculous. (Nevermind the question, why does Cazic only have his skin 1% of the time anyway?) I can think of few other uber weapon decisions so poor, except for perhaps the Mage's reliance on Quillmane in SK.

This isn't a question of "skill" or "time invested." It's a question of luck and frustration invested. That's simply not fun. I know not everyone can have an uber item. I would gladly give up the chance of ever personally getting one to have this fixed. But please, please, please... please have this epic weapon rely on something other than luck, months of camping, and strong-arming other Fear guilds out of the way. It does nothing but create grief for everyone who ever wants to hunt in Fear. And an item which does nothing but introduce grief and hatred into the game isn't worth it. Introducing an item into the game which does nothing but piss off thousands of people (most of whom aren't wizzies), with almost no benefit to the server in general, is pointless. Ragebringers, Chanter sticks, Water Sprinklers - they're all wonderful ways to invest your time, for the individual as well as the guild helping. The reward is great, and it's a way of encouraging cooperation and teamwork. The wizzie weapon requires strong-arming hundreds of players out of Fear, and upsetting a large number of people. It doesn't encourage teamwork. It doesn't require any skill. It doesn't encourage cooperation. In its current state, it just encourages emnity and hatred.
