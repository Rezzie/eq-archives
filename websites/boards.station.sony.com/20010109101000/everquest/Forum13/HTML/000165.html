It seems that there are still a number of people who are dissillusioned by useless stats such as dexterity and agility, and to a lesser degree, strength. I am going to attempt to put forth a guide for all of you who still contend that to be an effective warrior, your strength needs to be at least 200 and your agi in the 150s.

I will address firstly the role of warriors. Warriors prime role in life is to be a hitpoint sponge. We do this in several ways; long long pulls, holding hard hitting or multiple mobs at bay, or general longetivity. A good warrior trusts his healers and doesn't get nervous when he hits one bubble of health (though, this sort stoicism is not recommended with and healer other than a cleric.)

-Dexterity-
By Verants description, dexterity increases the rate at which weapons proc, how often a warrior crit/crips, and there is a bit of debate on wether it affects dodge. Regardless, it is a stat based upon EQ's random number generator. It is not based in hard math, therefor far too random to be depended on.

Lets take a ykesha, even now one of the most favored weapons (though I never knew why.) Based on an 8 hour camp at Sea Fury Isle in Ocean of Tears, and a perl based battle damage parser written by a friend, a 75 point DD only yielded 3 percent of the total battle damage. Hardly worth the 9 pounds of combined weight of the ykeshas or the wasted stats putting Dex up to 144.

As for crits, crips and dodges, these are all random and can not be relied upon in any certainty. Great, so you get a 170 point crit with your mith two hander, your next 3 crits are all kick based, then your next is a 68 point crit. Big woop.

-Agility-
Agility affects your ability to parry and riposte, as well as your armor class. For every 5 points of agility, you get 1 measely point of AC. You do the math, it is not worth it to dump tons of points into agility when you will yield only 4 or so AC points. 4 ac is not a critical amount of armor.

As for parry and riposte, these again are skills based upon random numbers, ultimatly unreliable, and certainly not important enough to contribute points to.

One caveat, if your agility is -under- 75, you will receive big penalties. There are few races with this problem, so don't worry too much.

-Strength-
Strength is not the defining stat of a warrior, it is not the be all end all deciding factor on how much damage is done, it serves two purposes: bragging rights and the ability to carry more items.

Again, based upon two 8 hour battles with Sea Furies, I tested the effectiveness of strength to damage. Here is how I did it: For the first 8 hour battle, I removed all extra equipment that gave me strength, dropping me to 100 (103 really, but you only get bonuses every 5 points). I fought for 8 hours, logged the results and saved the data.
The next day, I again fought the Sea Furies for 8 hours, but this time, with the help of a shaman, I kept my strength at about 200. Again, the battle was parsed and data was compared to the fight from the day before.
What I found is for every 100 points of strength, you do approximatly 3.5% more damage over an 8 hour period using Ykeshas at level 50 with maxed weapon skill. Very unimpressive.

-Stamina and AC-
Stamina and AC are king. With stamina, your warrior will have tons of hitpoints, allowing him to soak up more damage, giving him more time in the ring, and allowing the cleric that extra smidgen of time to throw you a complete heal. AC allows you to absorb more damage more consitantly, again, letting you stay in the fight longer.

At level 50, 5 stamina gives you 24.5 hipoints. while it doesnt sound like a ton, this does add up, and it is not terribly difficult to build up, as there are many stamina based items with great AC.

Also, if your stamina is greater than 100 points, you will not suffer stat penalties when your yellow bar runs down to zero because you have been whacking away for 10 minutes straight. You will however slow down a smidge, but its not horrendous.

Armor Class is complex. It is based upon two numbers, Defense AC and Item AC. In Beta 3, there were two numbers in your stat window for armor class, but Verant later obfuscated this, consolidating the numbers into one. Regardless, there is no reason any warrior should have less than 880 AC at level 50 with non kunark items, and at least 920 with kunark items.

Don't be afraid to sacrifice stength for armor, it will make you a better warrior in the long run. Eventually your high level, high AC armor will have strength bonusses anyhow, so if you are that concerned with the stat, patience will get you what you need while also letting you do what your class does best: tank.

In conclusion, AC and stamina are good, strength is a second thought, agility and dexterity aren't worth a damn. If you create your character around this this system, you will have a heartier, more robust, more -effective- warrior.

One final note, if you don't use the mouse to move in game, you are at a sore disadvantage.

For Car.. whatever his name is, benefit

Strength: Strength determines how much you can carry. It influences maximum and average damage and how quickly you learn many offensive skills. For a Warrior, strength is the most fundamental statistic. It increases the consistency of damage output, as well as helping the warrior to improve their skills. You should assign the majority of your bonus statistics here.

Agility: Agility affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit. The true effect of Agility is often debated. Whilst adding points to your Agility will increase your overall AC, it will not be a great deal unless you assigned the majority of your bonus points to this statistic. This would mean a lower overall damage output since you have reduced your base strength, but would mean you had a chance of lasting slightly longer in combat. It is certainly true that a high AC is something to be aimed for, but we recommend that the extra Agility is not worth the loss in Strength. Agility is still a statistic very worthy of considering as for allocating your remaining bonus points.

Dexterity: Dexterity affects how quickly you learn weapon skills, and how often weapons will do their special attack. This is another very important stat for Warriors. Most warrior classes already have an innately high dexterity. Most high level weapons have special abilities which help to deal more damage during combat. Dexterity also affects how often Warriors deliver critical or crippling blows. As a result of this a high dex is something which is always handy. There are also a number of items and buffs in the game which help to increase your overall dexterity. Putting bonus points into dexterity is a very viable choice.

Wisdom: Wisdom affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers and paladins. Since most warrior races have higher Wisdom than Intelligence they should look to buff this statistic when doing trade skills. But it is not a statistic to invest your bonus points in..

Intelligence: Intelligence directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills. It affects the amount of mana for bards and all pure casters, as well as shadow knights. Like wisdom, intelligence is only useful for skill raising for warriors. Dark Elves, Gnomes and Half Elves should consider buffing this stat if they are doing trade skills. It is not a statistic to increase with your bonus points however.

This was taken from everquest casters realm web page. It mirrors my many warriors experience in game and what was stated by Gordon regarding warrior skills. It should be noted that when wis and int are indicated as helpful for raising skills here they do not refer to any combat skill.
