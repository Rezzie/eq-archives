The best way to get money is to figure out the quest and how to use them.

For example: in Mistythicket, clerics will be popping in from time to time looking for honeycombs. By the time they are on this quest they will have the pocket change to buy them (they'll get no exp for Bixies and nuking a newbie zone is considered bad manners.) A newbie halfling who stocks up on these will find that clerics will GLADLY give them 2-3plat EACH for them.

For little money, you'll find the tailor skill is your best low-level money maker. Get your tailor skills to 20 and make silk swatches for Monks that pass through. Save the spiderling silk to make bandages for the tanks (and you thought they were useless didn't you, hehe.)

Eventually, you'll find the trade skills will net you more money than fighting. Later, you can get 100plat together and get Blacksmithing to a point where its VERY lucrative.

Use your character skills to your advantage. If you're an enchanter, selling enchanted bars is very lucrative at any level (most players haven't figured out you can enchant metal with beetle eyes yet.)

Use your imagination and the plat will flow, kill-loot-sell and you'll find yourself poor well into the 30s.

***Word of Warning: Charging for ports will come back to haunt you. Do what you feel is right, but remember that other characters keep lists. Later, you may find yourself paying 1000plat for the REZ, 5plat for a breeze, ect....***

Ruined Pelts = GOLD!!!

Find a critter that has a pelt that you can reliably kill and kill em.

You can auction off your High Quality (HQ) pelts for big dough, sometimes 10-30 pp for a HQ bear skin. You can either auction or sell your medium and low quality pelts also for gold, sometimes plat.

Take your ruined pelts and combine the suckers with a pattern in a sewing kit and you will soon be making patchwork armor to cover your hide. If you cant wear the stuff you make, sell it to a vendor and you will get 1-2 gold usually. The components are only 2-3 silver (ruined pelt = 8cp pattern = 1-2 silver).

Like thousands of others I was able to cover my massive Barbarian Shaman (with a pitiful intelligence of 60 or so) with PW armor by the time I was level 6. All the ruined hides I got after that I was turning into gold.
