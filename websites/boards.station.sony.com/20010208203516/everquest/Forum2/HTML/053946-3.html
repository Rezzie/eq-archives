While I feel for you and your wife, Elementalist, you have to realize that both first and last names were in violation of the naming policy. Both names are anachronistic and combinations of RL words. While this is certainly true of other names that have not been changed, your wife got caught for some reason (probably a petition from another player) and was forced to change it. It's suspiciously arbitrary how they enforce the policy, but the policy is still a good goal, IMO.

I agree in principle with the fact that there has to be some control on names in EQ as it is, nominally, a role playing game in a fantasy setting. That being said, VI's handling of naming issues is half assed and capricious, which leads to hard feelings such as your wife experienced. They've made the naming policy into a useful tool for the malicious to exact revenge on people. Most clever people can come up with some reason that any name violates the policy and most GMs seem incapable of offering any coherent implementation of that policy.

My suggestion for VI would be that if they are really serious about names, have all names be submitted for review at the time of character creation. At that time your name has a probationary status and is only finalized after a GM signs off on it. Then your name is officially sanctioned and immune from being changed. That would remove a lot of these hard feelings.
