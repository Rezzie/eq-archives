quote:
so the ones who DONT get the bonus find themselves falling farther and farther behind. i find this ludicrous

It wasn't that way to begin with. Originally, members of a group received disproportionate amounts of experience SPECIFICALLY so that every member leveled at the same rate. So if a Troll Shadowknight (with an extreme penalty) grouped with a Halfling Warrior (with a decent bonus), the Troll would actually receive a larger share of the experience. That would keep them leveling at the same rate.

Unfortunately, this meant that if you had the choice of inviting a warrior or a shadowknight/paladin, the choice was clear to take the warrior -- he would take less of your experience.

I think the original expectation was that a group of regulars would level up together, so an experience penalty wouldn't be a problem. But with pickup groups rejecting hybrids as experience leeches the sharing of experience was a hardship. After the first round of "melee balancing" SOE decided that there was no reason for an exp penalty for classes anymore. Bonuses were retained, but penalties were removed -- I don't recall the date.
