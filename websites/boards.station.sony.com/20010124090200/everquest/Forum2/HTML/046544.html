I'm afraid you are mistaken about the impact this change has. Others have explained it and you've ignored it, but I will attempt to clear things up.

The current technique works thusly:

1. Root your victim.
2. Dot the mob.
3. Meditate.
4. Reapply Root when it breaks.
5. Reapply dot.
6. Mob dies, start back at 1 with new mob.

What you are saying is that we will now do this:

1. Snare your victim.
2. Root your victim.
3. Apply dot.
4. Meditate.
5. Reapply root when it breaks.
6 Reapply dot.
7. Mob dies, start back at 1.

Now, how did our game change? Well, you added an extra step that's really not all that beneficial. You forget that we already meditate 20 yards away from the mob. Since we have sow at our disposal, the mob will not catch us, snared or not. This is the same for a shaman, as well, since they can also sit 20 yards away and they possess sow. No competent player who uses the root/dot method need ever get hit unless the mob they are fighting moves faster than sow. That is the only time a druid will have an advantage, since a druid can snare.

The REAL benefit of this fix, in my opinion, is actually that it minimizes the damage done by idiots who root snared mobs in dungeons. Invariably, there will be someone who does not realize the benefits of snare and thus roots the mob I've already snared. When root breaks, the mob gets away and it brings back 23904 of its closest friends. This is usually followed by the idiot who rooted demanding to know why the heck I didn't snare. :P Then I have to choke him and that's unfortunate. As a grouping druid, I am thrilled about this change.

Root works better than Tishan's for this, because it casts faster, and has a much smaller recycle time. Every soloing Wizard past level 15 has learned to root a mob while it is beating on him.
