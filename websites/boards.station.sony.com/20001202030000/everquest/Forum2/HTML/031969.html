MadWand
Station Member 		posted 11-17-2000 02:05 PM           
The pause is probably caused by a slight delay as your HD spools up again because you are accessing it. It will depend on your bios settings for power saving etc, you RAM etc..

For example, if you have enough memory to cache an entire zone, then your HD shouldn't have to spool up that often while you are in that zone, unless you are doing something were the client has to read or write to your HD (Like /log, /note, changing the .opt file etc.)

Psychotic,

128 Meg is no where near enough to cache all the zones in this game, especially the Kunark ones. You can test this out easy enough.... after you enter a new zone, run around for a while. Bind at one end of zone, then run to the far end of the zone and gate back to your bind point. If you cached the zone you will go "pop" and teleport instantly, and your pet (if you have any) will survive and come looking for you. If you couldn't cache the whole zone, you will go through the whole long delay loading, just like when zoning, and you will loose your pet.

I currently have a system with 256 Meg ram, and I can cache ALL non-Kunark zones, but still many of the Kunark zones will not cache completely. (In fact I lost a pet just yesterday gating back to the overthere outpost from the Skyfire zoneline.. pissed me right off)

To stop the pauses from HD accessing, you can change your bios settings to always have the drives spinning or make it a high number like 30 minutes. Unless you are just medding, you should have to access your drive at least once in that period of time.
