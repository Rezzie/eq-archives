First of all, I love the recent Ranger changes and I think the proposed spell changes are also in the right direction. However, all of these offensive changes are turning Rangers into the supreme suicide machines. Rangers dish damage and have self-damage shields, but don't take hits well.

First, I accept that Rangers are not meant to take hits. They have fewer hitpoints and lower AC than other melee classes. However, a Ranger's strength should be AVOIDING being hit. When a Ranger and a Warrior go up against a creature of their level, the Ranger should be hit less, but hit harder when hit. Having lower defensive skills and avoidance skills is a double (triple, quadruple?) whammy.

Rangers should have better PARRY, better DODGE, and better DEFENSE than they do now. In fact, Rangers should be the best or right up there at skills that avoid damage. Rangers should be poorer tanks based only upon their lower hitpoints and lower AC (tied to equipment not as good as warrior).

Secondary, but still important, there should be some consideration of a self-bind or self-port/evac of some sort. This ties into the belief and I think necessity, that Rangers should be better than others at avoiding damage, whether in battle, or roaming the world (this is NOT true now). Rangers right now have to tie themselves to cities. As a level 1 Ranger, I was bound in Qeynos Hills and was a guardian of the forest and it to me. I cannot bind there anymore, as I am now dependant on cities.

I noticed several people talking about trading jolt for evade. Having a rogue I understand the evade ability and I honestly don't have experience with jolt BUT:

1. Casters should cast spells. Pure melees should get unique skills. Jolt fits better as a spell for Rangers from a game world perspective than evade (rogues are dark and rangers generally are light, as one point of discussion.)

2. Jolt, evade, and feign death are all hate management abilities/spells. Mem blur is NOT ENOUGH for a ranger since it wipes some or all of the WHOLE hate list and the real problem is rate of hate accumulation. Jolt and evade properly move the ranger/rogue down the hate list. Evade is an arcade spell and requires the rogue to:

a. stop attacking--if you're moving it fails
b. 'hide' (hit the hide skill button)--the button must be white
c. succeed at the roll--based on 'hide' skill and probably succeeds 60% or so when skill maxed at level
d. have others on the hate list nearby to be attacked (or an aggro monster just turns back on you)

Jolt has a series of resists and apparently increases hate if the monster resists (yes...resisting spells STILL seems to increase hate more than actually missing a spell...talk about back***wards.) Jolt:

a. Takes time to cast (1.5 seconds according to castersrealm) while evade is mostly instantaneous
b. Takes mana (60 is pretty large, though it IS a high level spell)
c. Occurs late compared to when the AC and defense discrepancies start at level 39
d. Is most valuable if a good tank is available (SK/Warrior/Paladin) and in melee range same as evade

3. Ranger ability to manage combat as a "secondary tank" or "light tank" is strongly affected by poor defense/AC. It is even more strongly affected by the poor effect of stats on gameplay (particularly agi) and the lack of tradeoff for defensive skills when heavy armor is used. One suggestion was to put a weight limit on Rangers, but I think it fundamentally makes more sense to cut down the effectiveness of the defense skills if heavy armor is used. The trade off SHOULD be heavy armor but hit more often v. light armor but hit less often. (Verant has FAILED to implement THAT game, and the one that is implemented requires more hate list management instead.)

I fundamentally believe that giving evade to Rangers is a mistake from a class differentiation perspective, but Rogues would probably survive that change. I believe it would be better to lower Jolt to when it is needed (level 39) and make it a faster cast (0.5 sec) and less mana (20 at most) AND make it lure-like or lifetap like from a resist perspective. The latter is particularly important as the monster spell resistance goes up and may be the single most important reason to CONSIDER giving evade to rangers (since it does NOT get a spell resist roll).

On the other hand, Rangers not being useful at high level is symptomatic with a complete breakdown in the game design process at high level. IF monsters have 50,000 hit points then it only seems fair that the typical party should have about half of that damage come from melees and half from magic users. I believe Verant would be hard pressed to demonstrate that even 5% of the damage comes from magic users in the high level game. The hybrids suffer through the same comparison precisely because the spells which should make a tremendous difference in combat can at best contribute to the 5% while their combat abilities seem to contribute less than half of a warrior, rogue, or monk.

The game is fundamentally broken from a gameplay perspective and the best description of the problem is mudflation. Verant MUST rebalance melee v. magic or throw up their hands and admit that their vision sucks at high levels since magic users and hybrids really can't affect fights all that much.

Alan: just admit it out loud and deal with it. Trust me that the players know the problems and we're trying hard to overlook Verant's tendency in the past to ignore problems. You asked us to deal with you honestly, but you're absolutely ignoring some of the problems that are being raised in these threads. Admit them all and then tell us which ones the company actually plans to deal with and give us time frames. We're patient or we would have left already...but unless you want us to leave for the next game, deal with us honestly and forthrightly.

Most of the points that I would like to mention have already been covered, but there is an issue of sense that I would like to bring up. Many posters before me have tried to define the Ranger as a class, I've heard "sons/daughters of Druids", "woodland Rogues", "Monk-like" and so on, but the fact remains that the key to balancing the Ranger as a class lies in it's historical precedent. If you read any classic fantasy literature (Tolkein's LOTR is an excellent place to start), you will find that Rangers are depicted as woodland WARRIORS, using SKILL to face their daily toils. In that vein, they should have Warrior-like abilities. They are master's of the bow, highly skilled trackers, cunning, alert and deft. While they do dabble in magic, it is in a very limited fashion (if at all).

This leads me to the conclusion that the keys to balancing Rangers are as follows:

-fix Track and Archery. We should be the unquestioned masters of these skills, and they should be a significant presence on the battlefield and off (longbowmen rendered the English efforts in medieval France virtually invulnerable).
-give them better "passive" defensive capabilities (the deftness/evasion ideas from before really hit a chord)
-DO NOT go slap-happy heaping wacky, unrelated spells on the class, they actually serve to dilute it's core ability.

My 2cp worth. Hope it helps.

Ok, here is my personal bias as to what would balance the ranger class:

1) Give jolt lure level resists. It is pretty clear that at the high level, rangers are not tanks, but damage dealers. Rangers should have a fairly reliable way to reduce aggro, even on 100percent magic resistant mobs. A ranger should be able to nuke, snare, duel wield in combat, and be able to avoid most of the attention, most of the time, with jolt. Many "good" rangers forego the use of spells at lvl 50+, just because of the excessive aggro this causes. This should not be the case. A ranger should not be "afraid" to use his nukes, given judicious use of jolt.

2) If rangers are to "step up" to the damage dealing plate, they will need to use spells. Ranger melee damage is comparable to warrior damage. There must be some benefit to choosing a ranger with low hps and ac over a warrior, spells must be it. Currently, we have 3 damage dealing spells, all fire based: Call of Flames(lvl50), Firestrike(lvl52), and Calefaction(lvl59), doing 200, 300, and 450dmg respectively. Now, I have no problem with these spells except the recast time. Call of Flames has an 18second recast time, Calefaction has a 45 second recast time. Firestrike has a low recast time, but at 4.6 second cast time, it is questionable as to whether this spell can be cast in combat situations. Considering the total mob hp's at high level, and the built in penalty for chain casting (ie, aggro = dead ranger), is the recast time a bit excessive?

3) Fizzles. There have been numerous complaints of excessive fizzles on our high level spells. This is similar
to caster complaints, except most rangers dont carry nearly as much wisdom gear as druids. (Higher wis = lower fizzle). Please look into this.

4) Tracking. Rangers should be the premier trackers. This is slated to be addressed in a future patch, but I would just like to stress how important this is. Rangers are the 3rd best damage dealers and the 7th best tanks. Rangers need a "selling point" to get into a group. In my honest opinion, the current tracking bards and druids get is 90percent sufficient to meet most groups tracking needs. I would examine the correlation between tracking skill and range very closely. Currently, a very low tracking skill will allow you to track things withing "normal pulling range". There should be a real benefit to having a 200skill, as opposed to a skill of 50.
