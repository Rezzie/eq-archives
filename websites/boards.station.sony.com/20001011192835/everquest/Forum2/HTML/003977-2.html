Why an RP server?

I spent six years playing Neverwinter Nights (on AOL) and enjoyed my stay there immensely. I wasn't entirely satisfied with the RP then, but in retrospect... it was amazing. Since mid-1997 I've tried Diablo (Battlenet), the Realm, Ultima Online, Asheron's Call, and EverQuest... none have had more than, perhaps, a 1% in-character rate. There tend to be small enclaves of RPers. The best place for RP now is UO Emulators (Mytharria.org comes to mind) and MUDs.

The high-level MOBs, "dynamic" world changes, and scripted GM quests aren't adding to the EQ experience... at least not much. What's missing is the community, the RP atmosphere... an RP-tagged server would provide a *chance* for that. That's what appeals to RPers about online games. Without it, you have a computer game with levels, treasure and some "risk" (if you call sitting in front of a screen risky). Ultimately, that's very dull, regardless of the advances in graphics and sound.

So why won't Verant tag a server for RP?

Simply put, Verant won't place an RP tag on a server because they think the complaints would be too numerous to handle. Role-playing isn't as precise as player-versus-player combat, and what that entails. They don't believe they can "enforce" even the perception of a role-playing server... and that would lead to a lot of disatisfied players.

But RPers are already unhappy with the game.

That said, I think two simple steps could be taken to make an RP server. 1) Tag the next new server "RP" and 2) impose some sort of global experience point penalty (25 to 50%).

Will that assure RP? Of course not, but it will encourage it while discouraging power-gaming--or rather power-gaming without regard for an in-character atmosphere.

Staff shouldn't concern themselves about what is RP and what isn't. I suspect many players would be willing to create characters (and move guilds) to an RP-tagged server. They may not give up their 47th level shadow knight, but I think their are enough RPers to provide a more RP-intensive environment. How different would the game be if even 10% of the characters were RPed? 25%? Imagine the change if half the characters you've encountered were role-played!

Verant doesn't need to say "you can't be a smart troll" and decide what is RPing and what isn't. They need to provide some tools and let the thousands of dedicated players build an RP community. Unofficial RP servers don't work. We need a few tools... a tag, and perhaps a method to discourage power-gamers/farmers/etc. XP penalties are what I feel would be most effective, but at this point... I suspect those that consider themselves RPers would be willing to compromise.

A tag is enough. If it fails, the players aren't going to be any more upset about the game than they are now.

Well, here's one more post. Perhaps, if nothing else, it might serve to let Abashi know that we're not all raving, deaf lunatics.

I hear you, Abashi: there is no ISO standard for roleplaying. It's subjective and unenforceable. Well, it's subjective. As one poster noted, it's enforceable (a la the subjective whim of the AD&D DM). *Equitable* enforcement is more problematic, and nearly impossible. I can understand Verant/Sony/et al. not wanting to go down that road. All-in-all, I wouldn't want someone imposing their version of roleplay on me. So thanks for not going there.

But I do like to roleplay. And that seems to be an anomaly.

To my fellow roleplayers, I caution you not to request or rely on polls. I think the evidence that we're asking for an RP server -- and I do say "we" because I support the request -- tells both us and Verant that the majority of players are *not* roleplayers. If they were, the servers already would be as we are dreaming: a place where non-roleplayers are ostracized by the majority. In fact, several posters have reported the reverse: being ridiculed/ostracized for roleplaying.

Abashi, please listen when I say that I hear you again: an "RP" tag-only server will not be respected by 100% of the player base. I'm sure that you hear the response among roleplayers: "that's okay." I think you hear the rest: that we roleplayers want a place to gather with like-minded folks. There are other means to do this; perhaps you advocate one or more: looking for other players with /roleplay on; independantly organzing ourselves to play on one, specific server. The roleplayers' response is that these methods are overly-cumbersome. They also place "virgin" players -- players new to the game as a whole and/or those who don't read the message boards *anywhere* -- at a big disadvantage. We're asking for a little help in meeting folks.

I haven't read every post. Many of them are long, and I'm guilty of being longwinded here, too. But I haven't seen many posters say that they understand Verant's point of view on this next part. I think one of of Verant's concerns with tagging a server as "RP" without some distinguishing "RP"-type rules is that these same virgin players will come to the RP server with expectations that, if Verant "endorses" this server as roleplay, then they have a right to complain if someone isn't roleplaying. All of *us* may sign a blood pact that we won't /report non-RPers, but the tag still sets an expectation in the public mind. I think Verant fears -- and with some justification -- that the Guides and GMs on such a server will spend a proportionately larger amount of time addressing RP issues, even if just to say, "Roleplaying is subjective and we take no stand on what is 'acceptable' roleplaying." You can well-imagine the irritated and highly emotional player's response: "Then *WHY* is the blankity-blank server tagged *ROLEPLAY* if you [insert expletive string here] people aren't going to *enforce* it?!?!" Angry customers, stressed GMs...not a good situation as Verant envisions it. And I suspect that they're not far off the mark. And how about those "disruptive influences?" I can just picture them petitioning as "harrassment" those of us RPers who take steps to ostracize them. Again, the Guides and GMs spend time answering misperceptions created by the RP tag. In Verant's mind, it's wiser to avoid the misperception in the first place.

So is this the end of the line? Well, as many of you have said in one way or another, it is, sort of. Depending on how you want to look at it, either Verant or the majority of the player base holds all the cards. We roleplayers are neither of these (see above). We *are* a minority. The decision is really out of our hands.

If Verant were so inclined, I think both they and we would be better off to alter the requested tag slightly to "Roleplay preferred," "Roleplay requested," or "Roleplay encouraged." I have no emperical evidence, but I believe this would go a long way towards mitigating player misperception. As the roleplayers have already said, it would also go a long way toward putting us together with like-minded players, i.e., roleplayers.

In conclusion, it's a compromise. The roleplayers don't get everything we want. There will still be disruptive influences. And Verant will not be totally spared RP-based petitions. I'll say it again: I don't think it's that much to ask.
