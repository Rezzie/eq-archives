The naming policy is one where GMs should err on the side of leniency in my opinion. The name a player chooses for their character becomes that character's identity. To change it effectively wipes that character's indentity and severs the identity from the character history. It only alienates the player and the antagonism such a change generates should be avoided.

If the name is a well known fictional name like Gandalf, Darth Vader, or Drizzt Do' Urden then yeah, change it. If it's an obvious slur or insult then yeah, change it. If it's something that just doesn't fit like Laserbeam or Goodyeartires then change it. If it's something that breaks the rules AND is uncreative like Everquester, Bigtatas, Haywudja Blome then change it.

If it breaks the naming policy BUT it's creative such as Story Thyme..LEAVE IT ALONE! If it's something stupid like calling your Wizard "Wizaro" hey, that's their choice, LEAVE IT ALONE! If they want to call their character something like Gambit, ok questionable but not offensive and doesn't stand out(unless you've read x-men comics), LEAVE IT ALONE!

Just because someone petitions a name does NOT mean it has to be changed. This is a common revenge tactic used to cause grief to another player.

If a player's name is to be changed they need to be given adequate time to come up with a new one. At least 24 hours. The character in question should be given a temporary name immediately and they should be given 1-2 days to think of a new one. When the deadline is reached they can either email or petition to have it changed depending on how Verant wants to handle it. It would probably be a good idea to require the player to also come up with an alternate name should their first choice be rejected. In all instances of name changes the player should always be given a choice as to their new name. The players should also have some recourse if they are unhappy with how their name change was handled.

Is this more work? Yep
Does it require the GMs to think a bit more and exercise good judgement? Yep
Does it make the customers happier? Yep
