Hi all,

I wanted to bring up this issue officially and also talk about some aspects of it, and how we are looking to solve some of the issues.

First of all, we are working to come up with a new target server population at peak hours number. Our inclination at this point is a number somewhere between 2300 and 2500 players during peak hours. We feel we need to adjust this number because of how much Velious has added to EverQuest's size and quantity of content.

We also feel that undercrowding is probably more detrimental to the game than overcrowding, in that while crowded areas are frustrating in that there is greater competition involved, there are some advantages to it (help from others when an encounter goes bad, help with CRs, etc.). (No, I'm not saying Overcrowding is good overall -- so please don't misunderstand me). Undercrowding, however, can mean a player can't find anyone to play with, and thus leaves. In other words, that frustration can be worse.

There have also been some problems with packet loss and link death, specifically on E'Ci, when populations are growing high.

So, what are we going to do about all of this?
Well, before launching new servers we're going continue to analyze where players are at, what zones are crowded, and what zones are underused. Significant experience bonuses are going in next patch for underused zones (mostly dungeons).

With E'Ci specifically we found out that after a recent hardware change, the network interface was incorrectly configured -- last night this was fixed, so please tell us if anything has improved.

Also, when there are network problems out there with the Internet and a large group of players go link dead, this can cause zones to go down. We are working on this issue as well.

So, there you have it, both gameplay and hardware solutions we hope will have significant effect. And please understand that any hesitation to launch new servers is due to undercrowding fears only -- if after these adjustments and attempts to better utilize all the zones we have it turns out target populations are lower than we believed, we will launch new servers, both split and new.

thanks, and we look forward to your comments and feedback.

-Brad

First of all, I agree with the person who said that more people need to be drawn to the West side of Antonica.

What if there was a boat that went from Paineel to Ak'Anon? Or something similar, to round out the world so that we don't have these far corners that no one wants to get stuck in.

Another thing I've noticed is a problem across the world, but no zone brings it home like South Karana. Here you have a zone with all sorts of wandering 20's mobs: centaurs, elephants, etc. It's a 20-30 paradise. Plus you've got the Aviaks and the spires, not to mention Splitpaw.

So the Spires are *always* camped, and I've never seen as much KSing as there is at KFC. There will be 18 people, in three groups of 6, camping the 6-10 spawn points at the spires, and 20-25 people trying to make a bird mad enough to follow them back to their group. While this is going on, and people are getting frustrated with the downtime waiting for pops at one place and the KSing at another... the centaurs and elephants walk about, unmolested.

Why? Because *we can't wander and stay together.*

This is, in large part, a technology limitation. Lag and prediction make it difficult to follow either automatically or manually. Autofollow has that huge warning on it because it is quite falliable, and it makes it clear that it's simply not viable in many situations. manually following can be a pain... prediction can make it look like someone's running off in one direction, when in fact they turned around during a lag spike. In just a few seconds of packet-loss, you can end up out of sight of your group.

This is a tough thing to solve, but if it could be, it might make it feasible to do dungeon-crawls, wander large zones, and such, which would greatly alleviate crowding.

Some suggestions:

- Use some of the NPC pathing that is now in place to guide autofollow. This would be very useful in dungeons, to make it possible to use it without getting stuck in corners and such.

- Maybe introduce a 'radius' boundary around the group leader. Allow the group leader to set a radius (say, 20 units) and the members could agree to it and then they wouldn't be able to go farther than that distance from the leader. Either side could turn it off at any time, and it would be broken automatically by zoning, including gating. If packet loss put you outside the radius, you'd teleport into it.

- Give us another command, like /y, that gives a directional cue. Let's call it /holler. If you /holler group, your group will hear

<soandso> hollers to you from behind and to the right.

/holler all would be the same as /yell, such that all people within a radius would hear you, and just /holler would only show for the person you have targeted. Maybe even let it give a direction, so that if you /holler group and some of your group is in another part of the zone, they can come find you. i.e. <soandso> hollers to you from the NorthEast.


These suggestions would reduce some of the risk of wandering and traveling, but given the current population distribution, I would argue that the risk vs. reward ratio is too high. People are not willing to try to wander, at least not with people they don't know very well. Heck, I've had terrible experiences just trying to get a group to change camp spots by 100 feet.

As an out-of-game temporary solution, I suggested this in another post, but it died quickly.
For the most crowded servers, perhaps a moratorium on new accounts could be established until such time as the green servers population hits a certain threshhold and it's time for a new server. More specifically, if an account already has a character on that server, they could create additional characters. But if an account does not have any characters on that server, they would have to select another server (preferably a green one). The main disadvantage to this would be cases where RL friends take up the game; you wouldn't be able to twink them up with your level 55 whatever. But nothing would stop you from creating a new character on the server they select, and adventuring with them.

At any rate, I hope it all gets worked out before my server gets too crowded. The Nameless feels just about right right now.

Grundall-BT:

No no, don't worry, this has NOTHING to do with cost savings or ROI or anything of the sort.

It's simply the fact that with increasing the size of the world with Velious, we need to reestablish a target population maximum at peak hours, and that undercrowding is a concern.

As we increase the target number, we are monitoring and watching our servers and network infrastructure and will make adjustments and modifications as necessary.

For example, in addition to the misconfiguration that was plaguing E'Ci, I just talked to operations and it appears some machines on the Rathe need more RAM to support the additional people, and that that might be the cause of the problems you and others are describing.

All in all, the EverQuest server infrastructure really scales well, and we're confident we can continue to grow and expand it as the world itself grows and expands. Unfortunately, there will be some growing pains, but we are committed to addressing problems as they arrise.

Bottom line: real ROI (return on investment) comes from a great game, and part of making a great game means always trying to find that target population between undercrowding and overcrowding, that makes a fun environment for players during peak hours AND off-peak hours.

-Brad
