I agree with you on the fact that melee classes have little to look foward to in the higher levels. If you are a rogue, the skills stop at 30. Not to say it is that much different of not getting skills after 35. Just wanted to add to it.

I really don't understand the balancing factors that Verant relies on.

Caster - can bind and be bound anywhere and therfore closer to their belongings/corpse.

Melee - have quite a distance to travel when trying to return to the higher leveled zones.

IMO, if any class should be able to be closer to their body, it should be the melee class. The reason is, everything that keeps that melee player alive is on that corpse. Yet, a caster basically can memorize spells and be almost 100% as effective as pre-death. Some of you might say, "Well in the higher levels you can just get a cleric to revive you." What about when you can't get one to revive you? There is not always a cleric around when you need them and not always are they willing to go out of their way to revive you.

caster - have the ability to solo post 30

melee - 18 and up is near impossible for them to solo, for a legitimate melee player anyhow. Some exceptions might be for warriors, but as I recall some warriors have this problem as well.

(There was a recent post by either Gordon or Brad in which they said, "The melee classes were not meant to be able to solo.") Then how does this make us balanced?

Also, there is an arguable case that pets are capable of winning a fight with a melee player. Well, this melee player can vouch for the pet winning. Had an occurence with a pet when the pet's owner went LD. I tried to pickpocket it just for fun and it began kicking my ****. If it hadn't finally disappeared, I would have died. I did have a healer as back up if it would have come to it, but the pet was definitely going to win. Everyone got a kick out of it, even the mage when he came back and heard the story. I know also, the mage was 2 levels below me. So, I assume that the pet was also lower than I.

caster - not necessarily dependent on any other class. Well, maybe a wizard.(Non-group dependent)

melee - very dependent on other classes. Healing, transportation, revives, and buffs. All in all, survival.(group dependent)


caster - ability to charge for buffs, teleports, and revives. I know they all don't charge. I have a level 29 wizard as well and I don't charge.

melee - hmmmmm...let's see, don't think they can charge for any service. Trade skills, yeah, but anyone can do that.
