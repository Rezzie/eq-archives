As a starting character this would be my list of things to do with my character:

1.) Get AGI to 100
2.) Get STR to 100
3.) Get STA to 100
4.) Get AC as high as possible

Basically I wouldn't bother intentionally raising stats beyond 100 until I had enough items to get one of the stats to 125. Once you get 100 in everything focus on AC. Then some of your equipment will have stat bonuses in addition to good AC. If you see a stat aproaching a break point (I think those are 125/150/175) then try to push it over the edge if possible. Otherwise ignore stat items.

This basically holds true for all melee classes except that STR is useful to keep as high as possible so you can carry more (monks don't have this issue).

If I were to start a monk today I would go with 93 AGI, 85 STR, 88 STA and 85 DEX. Then I would give him one of my extra Gold Blackpearl necklaces (wgt. 0.1 +7Dex +7AGI), a lockjaw hide vest (wgt. 1.5 AC 12, +5STA), 2 Jagged Bands (wgt. 0.2 10 AC +6STR total), An executioners Hood (wgt. 0.2 5AC +4STR +9DEX), a Bracer of Erollisi (wgt. 1 5AC +5STR), a Silver Bloodstone earring (wgt. 0.1 +2STA), and a Black Iron Girdle (wgt. 2 AC 6 +5STA)

This would bring my stats up to:
100AGI 100STR 100STA 101DEX
AC 38
Wgt 5.1

Now obviously I am capable of twinking myself with all this stuff. But if you look at the prices on these items anyone could get these things at a reasonable price except maybe the blackpearl necklace. By now serpentine bracers (AC1 +9AGI +3DEX) are a dime a dozen on most servers and those would fill in nicely for the necklace with some changes to your starting stats. Especially since you don't NEED to get dex above 100. Since almost all of these things add a decent amount of AC and none of them weigh very much except the tunic and the girdle you still have plenty of room for raw silk, cured silk, woven, or mesh armor in the remaining locations.

I imagine the total price of outfitting yourself this way would be about 200-300 plat. If you have a character level 25+ you can go get more or less all of these items yourself if you wanted to. If you are worried about the executioners hood, don't be. People practically throw those away these days. Usually people camping the axe argue over who gets stuck with the hood!

As for the lizard men. Same rules apply. Find out what the starting stats are. If AGI and DEX are going to be very high (95 each for instance) then put all your points into STR and STA with a tendency towards STA because quite frankly STA items are much hard to get than STR items.

Again,

The only person here who REALLy knows what they are talking about is the 50th monk who posted earlier, and Mathilda.

If you are a monk, put 15 pts into STR. Put the other 5 pts wherever you want, but I'd probably say AGI just for the hell of it.

At 50th level, all AC starts to even out due to the "law of diminishing returns" that EQ adheres to.

Adding 15 to sta would give you about 130 more HP at level 50 if you were a warrior, but would only give you about 50 more if you were a monk. Not worth it.

Face it people, monks either pull or attack/damage. They dont tank. Defense for a monk comes second to doing the most damage the quickest. If you are in the planes pulling, and if FD doesn't work, no amount of HP or AC in the world is gonna save your ass, short of a miracle.

Go 15 STR. This will mean 1 to 2 pts of more damage PER HIT at level 50. And, if you saw how fast a level 50 monk with a haste item (CoF, sash, or Rakusha) coupled with a Swift like the Wind hits, you'd appreciate the extra 300 pts of damage.
