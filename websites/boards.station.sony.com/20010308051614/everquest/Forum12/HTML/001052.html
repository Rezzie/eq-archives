Recently, Absor responded to the following:

>”Whatever happened to the suggestions for self-only ports to a few select locations...

Absor:

"Teleportation is not part of the Necromancer package..."

I submit to Absor and the whole necromancer community that the father of necromancy Miragul did extensive research on personal teleportation and successfully managed to do it. Check out the following excerpt from EverQuest: The Official Players Guide (c) 1998, page 18:

"Necromancy, more than any other art, became Miragul's obsession. Eventually he discovered a means by which to creat portals within his own plane and made them to travel great distances in mere seconds..."

This is clear cut evidence that teleports *are* part of historical necromancy. It can no longer be shrugged off or denied.

Also, necromancers are given the following spells that demonstrate a capability to teleport:

Shadow Step - lvl 8 - mini random self teleport

Levant - lvl 55 - self only teleport to safe point in zone

So folks, there is a basis for giving more teleport cabability to necromancers. I also believe that this inherent capability which is historically supported could be used to make us more group friendly at higher levels. I suggested in both the official "talk" thread and the "list" thread the following 2 new spells:

Portal of Tears - lvl 53 - evacs party to spectre island in OOT, dangerous in the fact that party will probably be attacked by spectres upon arrival, also this zone is considered remote, no way to zone out without gate or boat travel

Portal of Sand - lvl 60 - evacs party to spectre tower in Oasis, dangerous as well because of spectres on island.

The addition of these 2 new spells would not increase the power of a necromancer to gain experience rather would help make us group friendly at a time in a necromancers career when pumping mana is all we seem to be good for.
