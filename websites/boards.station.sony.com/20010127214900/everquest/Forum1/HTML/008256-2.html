Nnova:

Try setting the PnP Os Installed (located in your PnP and PCI section of your bios: Same area as your IRQ settings.) Your Os should do the rest of the work from there.


On a side note I found out where my error was occuring. Processor was running to hot, had to slow it down by 100mhz. It just goes to show that similar errors can happen from completely different problems.


Some possible fixes for those with crashing errors are: Increase your virtual memory, reduce hardware exceleration, replace wsock32.dll with a newer version(run a virus scan in your entire systems directory in fact,) reset your bios to default, disable external cache(L2) if you are a K6 user, or if none of this works do a bios flash(instructions, flash utility, and bios drivers can be found at your motherboards official web site.)

Kiliz first make sure you have recent drivers for your video card. If you do also make sure you have the software tools for your card installed.

Personally I would just use direct3d to play eq and not glide. Load up everquest.exe and at the screen where you normally select connect select options and choose direct3d instead of glide. If direct3d is not listed there should be an option to use primary display driver. That might put you in direct3d mode. If this doesnt work right click on your desktop and select properties >>> then go to settings >>> then select advanced. As long as you installed the software tools for your video card you should be able to force it to use direct3d.

I know next to nothing about video cards so I can't tell you if your card is even a glide card(STB merged with 3dfx so it might be.) If it is not then perhaps you have the glide dll from a previous card.
