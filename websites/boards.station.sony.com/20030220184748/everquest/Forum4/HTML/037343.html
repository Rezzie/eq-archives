It depends.

It's not EQPC, that's for sure. I've heard it described as a cross between EQPC and Gauntlet, and it's not too far off. There's a definite arcadey feel to the game. So far from what I've played, it's more action and less strategy than EQ.

But most classes solo well. Food/Drink speed up down time (you can eat food for health regen, and drink for mana regen). So hopping on, soloing for an hour or two, and logging off is a great way to play. It beats LFG in EQ for an hour or two.

I prefer EQ though. But my wife likes EQOA. They're both good games, but are different.

Crimson,

I was in EQOA Beta for all stages and enjoyed the game. It is NOT PCEQ. It is its own game.

It is set in Norrath, but about 500 years before the EQ we know and love. There is only one continent and one race of Elves (no wood elves, half elves, or high elves), there are Dark Elves too, but I consider them a separate species. There are no Vah Shir, no Iksar and no Ogres (who are still in their self-imposed exile). The Erudites have not yet sailed, the dwarven homeland is close to Halas (which has a bridge over the lake), and there are a few other things like that.

Some things are very different, like the use of food and drink to regain health and power. Power is used by all classes, melee use it for their skills just as casters do. There are no corpse runs. There is a transportation system (stables) that lets you quickly go from city to city. Group size is set to be optimum at 4.

I love the spell effects and personally I like the artwork. No, it doesn't look like EQ, it looks like EQOA. Some of the mobs are tremendous, I especially like the birds, awesome graphics.

As Aandaie said, it is wonderful for people with less than optimum computers. All changes are made server side so there are no real downloads. To set up a PS2 for EQOA does cost some money. You probably already have a memory card but might need to buy a fresh one if it is largely full. You do need to buy a network adapater. You don't need to buy a keyboard, but I really recommend getting one.
