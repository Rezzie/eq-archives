Shadowknight lifetaps just aren't meant to be used as a primary form of inflicting damage. If you try to use lifetaps as nukes, you cripple your damage output. Rangers are the hybrids who can nuke; My 47th level Ranger can nuke for 99 damage, which is more damage than she could inflict in melee in that amount of time. (She dual wields two very fast weapons, so the weapon delay resetting isn't really an issue.) At 50 she'll get Call of Flames, 205dd. If you're thinking of lifetaps as nukes, you're thinking the wrong way.

What, then, are lifetaps supposed to do? They do give Shadowknights some versatility, if you use them outside of the straight-tanking setting. A soloing SK can fear a mob and lifetap it to heal; an SK can lifetap mobs that another group is fighting to heal; if the SK is wounded and there's another tank in the group, the SK can back out and lifetap, to maintain some damage output as well as heal; etc.

Do lifetaps "suck"? Yes and no. You obviously can't inflict pure damage with the spell like a Ranger could with his or her nuke. You obviously can't heal yourself with it as well as a Paladin could with his or her heals. So Shadowknights are kinda stuck with this bastard child of a spell. Sure, it would be nice if it were more powerful, but SK's can already do a good deal of magical damage by stacking their DoT's. I think that if they also had the ability to go "full burn" with their lifetaps and come out ahead of the game, they'd probably be overly powerful. <cough>Necros<cough>

This thread's gone way off the topic of shields, but I thought I'd post my thoughts on the lifetap issue anyway. =]
