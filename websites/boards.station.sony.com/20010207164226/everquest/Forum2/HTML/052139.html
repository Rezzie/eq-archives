Aeogolism (60th level Cleric spell): Adds 1,100 to max hit points and 54 to AC for a single target. Lasts 2.5 hours. Costs 750 mana.

Spells:

- Make charm and mezz spells act the same as DD vs Summoned / Undead. That is, don't let the spell even be cast if it cant affect the mob. Just return the message "This spell will not work on this mob" and not waste the mana, and incur the frenzy.

- Examine the Lull line of spells (especially Wake of Tranquility). Currently they are resisted in the extreme, making them useless (read: useless, not situational. useless)

- Debuffs (Cripple, Weakness, etc) have no discernable affect on NPC's. Cast any one of these on a PC and the affects are devastating. Please make modification so that the affects of these spells are noticeable on mobs, similar to how they affect PC's. A suggestion would be to change these spells to reduce mob stats by percentages instead of fixed amounts. Mobs seemingly have endless amounts of strength and other stats, so reducing them by a fixed amount has no affect.

- Sentinel: Remove it

- Illusions. Opinions vary on this topic, but some higher level enchanters view illusions as tools instead of toys. They allow us to venture into hostile territory, so adding new illusions is not a priority unless they serve a purpose.

- Enchanter animations are balanced. The only change I would suggest is making the last animations (level 55) duel wield automatically when summoned. This is our last animation, this addition would be a welcome change.

- Remove recast times on speed buffs (VoG, etc). This element only serves to be annoying, in a game designed to be enjoyable.

- Reduce the component cost of Rune IV, it is unbalanced for the benefits it returns.

- Make Reoccurring Amnesia act like a dot. Cast this spell upon yourself to get an idea of what it is supposed to do, but it doesn't work as it is now. No reoccurring at all.

- The current implementation of Boon of the Garou is unsatisfactory. It currently lasts 6 minutes for 100 mana with a 3 minute recast time. Any change would be acceptable, just keep in mind that buffing is not fun. A suggestion would be make the spell last 30 mins for 500 mana with a 15 minute recast time (5 times the length at 5 times the cost).

- Re-adjust the casting time of the Shielding line of spells. Shield of the Magi has a 12 second casting time. Is this necessary? It detracts from the "fun" of EQ.

- Umbra appears to be broken. It doesn't follow the standard AC upgrade of that line of spells. Currently is it no better than the level 49 Shadow spell.

- Memory Flux (level 55) is a single target memory blur that costs 350 mana. This spell is extremely unbalanced in both it's effectiveness and it's mana cost. Currently it does not work as well as much lower level memory blur spells (to be blunt, it's only real purpose as it currently stands is to waste large amounts of mana, with no upgrade to it's memory blur affect).

- Reduce the enormous agro of the Tash line of spells. It is completely unnecessary to have this spell agro the way it does. A compromise can be reached, adjust the agro somewhere between it's current form and that of the level 60 Magician spell Mala.

- A heavy price is already being paid in the form of extreme mana cost when using Glamour of Kintaz and Rapture. Please make them “lure like” in resists to offset the high mana cost and extremely short duration.


Equipment:

- It is clear that little is known about how the enchanter class operates. We resemble other INT caster classes in appearance only, but in all other forms we are poor tanks wearing cloth armor. Acknowledge the role we play, and create equipment that compensates for the frenzy we incur. Flayed Skin armor is a good lower level template for enchanter gear. We need a choice of high AC / HP / Resist gear that is designed solely for the enchanters needs.

- Examine the effects placed on Velious enchanter gear. Clarity on the leggings? How is this useful, considering the level you will get these leggings? Clarity 2 would be a much wiser choice. The effect Rune IV on the robe is a novelty. The horrendous casting time, and the fact that it eats a peridot makes it less than desirable. Consider changing the affect to Rune V, or change the affect to Bedlam and lower the casting time to 12 seconds. Also, re-evaluate the difficulty of the quests. Currently the difficulty of the quests are much higher than zoning into PoG and looting the armor. Balance out the difficulty and make the choice of “quest vs. kill” be a choice made on which one is more enjoyable, not which one is easier in the extreme.

General

- Take time to re-evaluate the decision to make "clicky effects" have long casting times. The explanation given by Verant for this was that it is "too hard" or "too embedded in the code to change". Snicker. Rewrite the "clicky effects" code to eliminate the need for horrendous casting times, and introduce recast times. 20 and 30 second casting times are unrealistic and are not useful. Recast times would be much more balanced.

- Examine the role of casters in high level dungeons and encounters in Velious. The size of the mana pool has not changed, so having a caster over an additional meleer is a huge hindrance. Melee can do non stop damage as long as they draw breath. Caster can dish out an embarrassingly small amount of damage, might as well change them out for a useful class in these encounters.

- Consider removing the extreme resistance to Magic on uber mobs. This was completely necessary on mobs like Cazic Thule or Nagafen, since then were limited to 32k hit points and a slow or a snare would be devastating. This is no longer the case when the sky is the limit on mob hit points. All it does now is alienate classes that have no other means of damage or debuffing.

- Higher level Velious dungeons are “boring” at best for enchanters due to the extreme Magic resistance placed on these mobs. Lower resistances to Magic in many Velious zones to stop the alienation of enchanters.

Epic quest

- What a beautiful quest. Extremely entertaining and very difficult. Up until the point where you must kill Cazic Thule to get a Shissar Spirit to spawn. Please re-evaluate the decision to require a 7 day spawn npc be slain. There are huge numbers of enchanters who will be forever stuck on this part, with every other piece of the quest completed (and rotting in the bank) after months of effort. We are fully aware that you wish to limit the number of these in the world, but it is not necessary. Having a server full of enchanter epics is not the same, as say a server full of warrior epics. The only thing the enchanter epic does it allow us to have more fun in the game instead of non-stop medding. If we don’t cast Speed of Shissar, then we cast VoG. Same difference, only that one allows us a pittance of entertainment, while the other dooms us to forever sit and med. Yawn. What we ask is that you make the distribution of these items based on FAIRENESS, not locale. Many of us are cursed to never see a rare spawn alive due to our time zone. Please consider changing this final step to a triggered spawn (such as Ixiblat Fer).

Hi Absor,

I'm delighted to be able to give you my feedback on balancing Enchanters. I'm a long time Enchanter player, with two enchanters in the 50s.

First, enchanters are a popular class, regarded as almost essential in high level single group play. In looking at balancing the enchanter, their popularity suggests that they do not require signifigant work. Tweaking here and there could make the enchanter a more enjoyable balanced character at various levels however. Let me give you a breakdown of how I see things by level:


Levels 1-15 (Infancy)

These are difficult levels for enchanters compared to most characters, but no tweaking is required. Enchanters accept this in part as a rite of passage. Our mana pool is too low to mes effectively at these levels. Our damage output is low. Our buffs and debuffs are not very useful. I repeat, I think we enchanters like this the way it is.


Levels 16-28 (Childhood)

These are somewhat difficult, but not overly so, and no tweaking is required. The addition of breeze makes finding groups easier. We can use mes with some effectiveness now due to a larger mana pool. Level 24 is a big level, as we get a new bolt and a useful charm.


Level 29-43 (Youth)

Augmentation and clarity at 29 allow us to easily secure our place in groups. Anarchy at 34 is an excellent (if high taunt) bolt. Our expanding mana pool makes messing a useful and regular part of our repetoir. One small negative thing happens here, mobs start to 'resist' memory blur, but that's not a big deal. Certainly no positive adjustment to enchanters is needed here, we are in our prime. Nerfing would not be unreasonable. A "good" nerf would be to make even+ conning mobs resist mesmerize and enthrall more, our two most mana efficient messes.

Level 44-50 (Adulthood)

More powerful buffs and debuffs, and an expanding mana pool, makes the enchanter a truly powerful force. We can mes with virtual impunity. Our level 44 bolt while high taunt is useful. Swift like the wind and shiftless deeds are powerful. Our charm at 49 is very useful. Certainly no positive adjustment in this period is necessary. Nerfing would not be unreasonable, and could be best expressed by making even+ mobs resist mes more.


Level 51-60 (Dungeon Crawling)

The power of mobs in the higher level dungeons where we hunt makes them more of a challenge for us to deal with. This challenge is exciting, though we think wi****lly of lower level dungeons where we were (quite frankly) overpowered. Life is a little more boring as we need to be cautious given the power of mobs. Our bolts become almost useless but that isn't that big a deal really. No tweaking is necessary.


Level 51-60 (Raids)

Our time is spent mainly buffing, and we provide valuable service in doing this. We mes mobs on occasion, but that is a rarity and not the norm. When facing powerful mobs, after we throw a tashania up, we're usually not that useful. Casting Rune on tanks can be valuable on occasion, and in encounters where we fulfill this role we do feel useful. For many encounters this isn't useful or necessary, and we are unable to affects the mobs due to high magic resistance. We could use something useful to do during combat in these situations.

Enchanters do not stack well in raids. While having several enchanters is useful for "backup", most raids really only need one enchanter. It would be nice if when hunting in large groups, we were able to scale more effectively. Distributing buffing is really not that fulfilling, and doesn't make you feel valuable.


Ideas worth exploring

- We need more to do at high levels against highly magic resistant mobs. Here are some comments:
- This is difficult to do without unbalancing us for single group situations.
- Suggestion 1: A pet similar to the cleric hammer pet. This would be a non magic based dot. Make it expensive so it's clear this is for situations where we can't otherwise use our mana effectively. Make its damage in line with pet classes at that level, because it's expensive, temporary, and we're not doing anything else. Don't make it look like our sword pet, that's confusing. Suggestion: make it look like the caster, that would be cool! Or, make it look like the mob, but with smaller size / different texture, so there isn't confusion.
- Suggestion 2: Our rampage line does not stack with other strength spells. It should. Most tanks want strength buffs all the time.
- Suggestion 3: Examine the resistance rates of mobs to mes at level 55+, especially in velious.


- Enchanters need to be more useful in large raid situations where there are several enchanters:
- It is difficult to come up with useful ideas that would not overpower enchanters in the "single group" situation.
- The above pet idea would work nicely here, since extra enchanters would have something useful to do with their mana.
- Please keep this issue in mind when reviewing other's suggestions.

Small issues

- Illusions are not truly a balance issue, but for the happiness of enchanters, we'd appreciate some more variance here. They don't unbalance us either!
- Some kind of visual feedback that a mob is messed would be nice

Thanks for your time in reviewing my suggestions!

When I think of balancing classes in EQ, I think Verant's goals should be the following:

1. Make every class contribute something valuable to groups
2. Make every class an active contributor to their groups success
3. Achieve goals 1 and 2 across all level ranges

When I think about my enchanter, I believe that for the most part, items 1 and 2 are achieved. The only problem areas are at very low levels and at very high levels in high level encounters / zones in Velious. Quite simply, Verant has chosen to make the majority of 50+ mobs in Velious some combination of unmezzable, unstunnable, uncharmable, magic resistant, or magic immune. When I look at the options left to me as an enchanter in Velious and in most boss mob encounters, I do 4 things: group resist magic, speed, c 2, and occasionally gift (although the value of doing this is questionable). Every 20 minutes I can do something useful.

Am I still valuable to the group?

Yes.

Am I an active contributor to the success of my group?

No. I can play a twink tank or cleric on my other account, put my chanter on auto-follow, and speed click, C 2, and grm every 20 minutes. What is worse, I can do this for a raid of 36 with one enchanter with an epic.

The most critical problem with Chanters, and a few other classes post 50, is that they are no longer fun. The biggest cause of this problem is that with Velious and high level mobs have insane MR and anti-crowd control flags basically invalidate core class abilities. Enchanters are not alone in this, but they are the most profoundly affected because EVERY enchanter offensive spell is magic based.

What do enchanters need to be balanced?

Below 50, I am not sure there is a huge justification for anything to balance a chanter. Perhaps in the early days before level 16 and Breeze, something should be done to make it a little easier to survive, level, and get groups. Getting to 29th level as an enchanter (pre-Breeze) was so difficult I almost gave up twice. Level 7 and 13 took me two weeks apiece and I lost 8th level more times than I wish to count. Granted, I did not know my class, but I suspect I would still find those levels difficult because of the lack of power in the class at those levels. Perhaps some level limited Color Shift spells at 8th (level 20 limit) and 24th (level 39 limit)? Perma stun ( chain casting ae stuns to keep mobs from doing anything ) is a technique that only becomes viable at 44. Adding this series would drop it down to where it became somewhat viable at 8 and again more viable at 20th. Lower mana pools, lower skills, and lower stun times would still make this risky in the extreme for a low level chanter.

After 50 and in the large raid world, Enchanters need something that allows them to play an active role in the battle. They need a compelling reason to be at the keyboard, paying attention to the action in hopes of turning the tide, saving someone's arse, etc. with a crucially timed cast or action.

What could be done to give chanters a role?

1. Sharply reduce or eliminate aggro on Tashan line and increase magic debuff. This would allow chanters to debuff sufficiently to allow magic spells to stick more frequently on all but totally magic immune creatures. Right now, tash = death. A common question before the main attack is "Which chanter gets to die from tashing this time?" It would be a start, because it would open up more possiblities for familiar tactics to be used, but that nullifies the point of the challenges created for Velious. Fine, you don't want me to screw with the mobs abilities, let me mess with my group's abilities. Personally, I'm not sure I like this one. I do not mind not being able to mez mobs if I have some active role I can play in the battle.

2. Give chanters a series of short duration, long recast time spells that have great opportunity to affect the course of the battle. A couple existing spells could be tweaked to help with this or some other spells could be created. Some possibilities:
a. Make Memory Flux work much more often. I could save tanks, clerics, etc. in my group if Flux actually made a mob forget the players they decided to hate more than 10% of the time.
b. Improve Boon of the Garou so that the stacking issues are resolved ( it does not stack with several other spells, most notably, I believe the new ranger buff) and remove the motion effects - it gives some people motion sickness when it is cast on them.
c. Give me a targetable Jolt/Blur that allows me to save a member of my group (group only spell) from taking a severe beating.
d. Give me a spell that resets the target's discipline timer to 15 to 30 seconds. Give it a long recast time and/or expensive / infrequently dropped reagent.
e. Give me a spell that allows me to increase another casters mana supply in infrequent, high impact bursts. Harvest for others.
f. Give me a pet only magic resistance buff that allows all pet classes to use their pets in a fight against fearing uber mobs. ( should be far superior to the player versions to account for lower pet levels. )
g. Make Lull line of spells work well enough that casting them actually helps seperate mobs in a pull.
h. Give me a rune upgrade that absorbs hits rather than damage. Mobs that hit for 850 laugh at rune V.
i. Give me a targetable pet, that allows my Warrior to get a stupid enchanter pet and have it aggro when HE gets hit. (Limit one per customer, as always) Call it Gift of Kintaz. Have the pet suicide after a fixed duration of time.
j. Give me a targetable Illusion: Air Elemental that causes the target to proc Color Flux. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
k. Give me a targetable Illusion: Earth Elemental that causes the target to proc Root and gives the target a large strength boost. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
l. Give me a targetable Illusion: Water Elemental that causes the target to proc Memory Flux, Tashania, or Evade. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
m. Give me a targetable Illusion: Fire Elemental that causes the target to gain a damage shield and proc Feedback. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
n. Give me a targetable Illusion: Skeleton that causes the target to proc a short duration Fear. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
o. Give me a targetable Buff that causes a PC to proc Theft of Thought, a baby version of theft of thought, or a Mana sieve. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
p. Give me a targetable buff that causes a PC to proc Cripple. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.
q. Give me a targetable buff that causes a PC to proc Bedlam. Duration and recast times same as Boon of the Garou.

One note: j, k, m, n, and p are unlikely to stick the same way that chanter magic based spells are unlikely to stick. The difference is that when the resisted spell aggro's something, it aggro's on a PC capable of taking the hits. In effect, I am grafting a spell onto someone instead of casting it myself. If a spell resists 3 of 4 procs like they would if I cast em, I don;t die in 3 hits to a mob that quads for 850. (Can you say Velketor?) It will be a nice bonus when it does stick. Still, I prefer other suggestions more.

How is that balanced?

Simple. A long recast time means the use of another different, unmemmed spell while a spell is rechargining is impossible. In the end game, traditional spell choices go out the window because they are ineffective. What are they replaced by now? Nothing. That is the whole problem. If you are going to fine tune the end game, the capabilities have to be high impact, infrequently available tactics with sufficient duration to be viable in a high end encounter. For casters, that means spells with long recast times, for tanks that means disciplines, for hybrids that means some balance of spells and disciplines.

Let me chuck up most of an e-mail I sent you with some of the issues...

>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

First, my basic views on enchanters:
Enchanters are one of the most powerful classes in the game. Flat out. The more people you group us with, the more powerful we are. Give us a party of 6 and unless they are all necro's, we'll add significantly to the party. Mez itself doesn't make this so. Nor does slow. Just clarity and haste make it so. Haste will increase the power of a melee class by 25 to 50%. Clarity will do the same for casting classes. Inspite of that, we have our drawbacks and merged together, this has formed a class that is balanced and due to our downtime reducing properties basically become viewed as a must have by most dungeon groups. Downtime isn't fun and the best way to reduce downtime is the enchanter. Any changes therefore, need to take into account that the enchanter is already an extremely powerful class.

My view on the types of enchanters:
I generally see 4 types of enchanters.

1. Control. These are the guys that can stop a 10 mob pull single handedly (well, with heals from the cleric, of course). They are the ones responsible for the enchanters godlike image. These people like to be active. To contribute.

2. Illusionists. More roleplay/fun loving types. These guys like to go to different forms. Typically faction buffs as well.

3. Enchanters. They were told they could make enchanted weapons and they liked this idea. The tradeskill people.

4. Debuffers. Looking at the enchanter spell line up, it would appear that the enchanter has the power to debuff a mob greatly. Most of these have either quit playing or shifted to control types.

Now let's take a look at SoV, from the perspective of each of these groups:

Control: High MR to 100% MR on mobs have reduced the enchanters to just buffing in many zones. Buffing is a passive activity. To these people, this represents a massive shift in the focus of the class. From active to the most passive of all. Your comment on necro mana feeding and clerics basically deals with this. Enchanters still contribute for the party. However, like necro's, what was once an active class with visible, noticable impact on battles is now a passive class that relies upon others to do the work. It doesn't come close to a clerics, who has to juggle the casting time of the heal, the mana cost, and amount healed while trying to keep everyone in the party alive. Just buffing is boring.

The illusionists. Other classes get illusions. Enchanters don't. They aren't very happy about this.

The enchanters. Still can't enchant, although boon of garou at least lets them add procs to people. Nice spell.

Debuffers. There really aren't many of these left. Stat debuffs don't make a difference, aside from acting as a quasi snare on some mobs. The high level mobs with high MR are too risky for an enchanter to slow. We don't have the HP or AC to survive a resist. And forlorn deeds has too long of a casting time to cast through getting pounded.

Now, the challenge is this... how to fix the class without upsetting the power balance. Yes, the class is broken. From a power balance perspective, the class is balanced. But from a fun standpoint, it isn't. High MR zones in SoV broke the class.

So... how to fix the class...

From the control view:

The class needs something active to contribute. A token fire spell might work, but too many mobs are resistant to that. A lure type magic spell might help, but that's a wizard thing and doesn't help at all against 100% MR things. Lowering the resists in zones where it is all out of whack. A great solution, but one that would require a lot of work, in short, not likely to happen. None of these are optimal in my view. What I would recommend instead is the addition of 2 spells:

Glamour of <some anagram here>: this spell would have the duration and casting cost of glamour of kintaz. It would only work on critters level 55 and below. But it works on giant flag critters. This would enable enchanters to crowd control somewhat against giant type mobs, but at a high mana cost.

Absor's Animation: (okay, they'll scramble the name, but that's how all our pets are named). This would be a level 55 spell. It would cost as much as our 55 pet and take two tiny daggers to cast. It works like the clerical/mage pets. It summons a dual wielding pet (preferably with a haste spell already on it) that attacks the current target until that target dies. This pet will have 2x the hp of the 55 pet spell. This will allow it to survive a hit or two from the big nasty boss mobs. This was basically the original intent of how enchanter pets were supposed to work. Against 100% MR mobs, enchanters could contribute damage without the hassles of getting hit first. Another alternative for this pet is one that dies when the target does, very little offense but very high hp and mega taunt... you cast it on a mob and it keeps it occupied for a while. The only big drawback is that against zones where everything is MR, that's a lot of casting and tiny daggers to go through. Perhaps only one tiny dagger per casting or a chance of taking one tiny dagger, but you must have one?

What this accomplishes:
Expands crowd control to a few more zones in SoV and gives enchanters something somewhat active to do against MR mobs. I don't think it's enough, but it's something.

From the Illusionist point of view:

I don't think new textures should be added to the global file for enchanter illusions. This space could be better spent to add a cloth texture set or 3, in my opinion, something to let us wear something besides dresses or plain leather. So that leaves us with just the current textures.

Bear and Lich form are out. That's fine, unique to those classes, I can live with that. However, we need some turnabout there. Werewolf is our class defining form. We have it, nobody else should, unless we cast it on them. There is a bard only lute with illusion werewolf, Lute of the Howler. It needs to no longer drop. Let those that already exist continue to do so. But it needs to be gone from the drop tables. There is also an item that supposedly drops in MistMoore, the werewolf skin. It is a 30 ac, bard/warrior bp that can cast werewolf. I believe it is a hoax, but if it exists, it too needs to be removed from the drop tables.

In addition, a few fixes need to be made. Illusion spirit wolf used to be castable inside. This was nerfed to outside only because wolf elementals were on beta-nuetral faction and this made corpse recovery on dragon raids too easy. Wolf elementals are no longer on beta-nuetral faction. As such, the reason for the nerf is gone and the nerf should be undone. Second, and far more important, lichform and bear form both need their factions adjusted. Right now, they can be cast anywhere to get non-KoS status. The enchanter equivs (or druid/ranger wolfform) result in being KoS in those same areas. An evil shaman casting bear form in PoG is no longer KoS. This is a bug and needs fixing.

What does that leave us left with for new illusions? A few textures that aren't used like Eye of Zomm. But it's kind of hard to think of anything cool and useful to go with that. Illusion gender would rock and I believe you'll add it when you get it working right. But it shouldn't be a 44+ illusion. Illusion target or random would also be nice, but that has code issues and, being a random critter, wouldn't be good for much more than a look. Again, not really worth of being a 50+ illusion. What I would recommend is the elemental line. It has 4 spells that could all be useful but balanced group spells. Factions aren't an issue as about anywhere elementals gain you a benefit, skeleton form or werewolf gets you the same benefit. These are available via the amulet of necropotence and boon of the garou.

51: Group Earth Elemental: adds +10 str. A minor benefit. Any large races lose slam.

54: Group Water Elemental: Gives undering breath. It's a utility spell. Saves a fish scale. Downside is it doesn't cross zone-lines and doesn't last through link-death. Cool looking form though.

57: Group Air Elemental: gives levitate. Again, a utility spell. It saves a few batwings, very useful for travelling or in areas with slippery ice covered surfaces.

60: Group Fire Elemental: this one is the only one with balance issues. Fire elemental starts at 9 points at 34 but increases to 15 by 59. It stacks with other damage shields. For balance reasons, I would set the damage equal to -51 + players level. So a 51 player gets a 1 point shield and a 60 gets a 9 point shield. This is assuming it works like the single target fire elemental and stacks. If it doesn't stack, then the amount could be set to 11 (same as the enchanter level 29 feedback spell). Personally, I like the stacking version.

What this accomplishes:
Restores the validity of the unique argument by ensuring that enchanters have their unique look. Adds 4 illusions post 50 adding validity to the enchanter illusionist title. Fixes a few bugs to ensure that from a faction standpoint, the enchanter illusions are the best or at least equal. Undoes one of the many incidental nerfs enchanters have suffered over the years as the reason it was done no longer exists (and also provides a nice argument to counter the give it SoW requests=) ).

From the Enchanters point of view:

Personally one I don't really care too much about. I tinker not enchant. However, I think cultural smithing provides much of the answer here. Simply put, the addition of religion based weapon mixtures for smithing. These recipes would require an imbued gem (stops enchanter monopoly), mana vial of some sort (use for these spells and enchanters are enchanting) and enchanted metal (more enchanter stuff). In addition, the recipes would require a drop from somewhere, like a short sword of ykesha (aka yak, an 8/24 1h slash with a 75 point DD proc). This accomplishes two things... it limits the number of items entering the world and provides a way to give the weapons some real power. An item that takes a yak for the prime healer, for instance, could result in an 8/24 weapon with a procs that is an 80 point heal on the weilder. Depending on how cruel you wish to be, you could make the failure of an attempt eat the weapon required in the making or return it. This will leave the agnostics feeling a bit short, so a few religion-free race recipes could also be introduced. However, I like the idea of items that proc heal for the prime healer, ones that proc disease dot's for Bertox, a weapon that only procs indoors for Brell, etc. Adds some nice flavor to things. Most of these changes are long term, of course.

What this accomplishes:
Gives Yak much more work in other areas when he should be focusing on tinkering. But seriously, a way that enchanters are involved in the making of enchanted weapons without giving them such a foot hold that they dominate it like jewelry. Enchanters really are the best class to do just about any tradeskill with so precautions must be taken in this regard. (Try and find another class that can boost str by 80, int/wis by 20, has the cha and faction of enchanters, and can boost dex by 100).

From the Debuffers point of view:

Not really much to be done here. Stats don't mean anything and that's the way it'll stay. If they ever do start meaning something, then this will mean something. However, against anything MR, nothing to be done here. Slow is the only debuff that matters and against the tougher mobs (read non-xp group areas), only shamans have the hp's, ac, and casting times to slow them reliably. And we're fine with that. An unresistable minor slow could be added, but slow has become the class defining ability of the shaman. An unresistable str/agil debuff could be added to enchanters, but, well, that means nothing. One interesting idea here would be sort of an offensive cleanse we could cast on mobs. Cleanse is a proc for a cleric/paladin weapon that removes all negative effects on the weilder. A dispel that removes all positive magic effects on the target but leaves the negative (like slows and dot's) would be cool and fit the enchanter as master of enchantments. However, such a thing might upset clerics as the main thing we want gone is damage shields and mark of karn does that. This would pretty much be an unneeded bonus, but would be a cool spell regardless. Clerics and paladins could be given a cleanse type spell to cast on people at the same time maybe. *shrug*

Now let's talk about Gear:

Afraid I'm going to get a bit passionate here. I've always been sorely disappointed in Verant having no clue as to what casters are looking for in gear.

Who designs this crap? We get jack and most of it isn't any better than what we've been wearing since hate opened. The only piece of the new enchanter only armor that is clearly better than the planar is the robe. The sleeves, legs, and gloves are close to planar. The bracers just provide more int we don't want or need. The head slot and boots are worse. And that's saying something, as hate plane enchanter boots suck, especially compared to loam encrusted. Enchanters have a 20 point int buff. They have a 50 point cha buff. Int gear is out there everywhere. And many items that give us other things we want also give us int. Enough with the int. We don't want it. Bad enough the Staff of the Serpent got nerfed and had 5 points of stamina moved into int. Additionally, the AC... we'd like some please. Kunark upped how hard mobs hit by 20/30 points in the upper levels. SoV added another 20/30 to that. After 2 expansions, we've gotten a new armor set. For the most part, the ac on a piece is 1 higher, say about 7 points total. Tanks, in the same time frame, have gotten that much of an increase on a single piece of armor. Added to that, int casters get AC on character at a rate of 5:6. A piece of gear with 5 AC gives us 6 when worn. Any non-int caster gets it at a rate of 5:8. This means that from a gear perspective, we've gained the total benefit from replacing a whole set (which we won't do because the stats on most of the pieces are not an improvement) as any other class gets from replacing a single piece of gear. Int casters are supposed to be more fragile. But if you crank up how hard the mobs hit and up how often we have to cast on the mobs to have the same effect (higher hp, higher resists), we need a way to survive it. And that means AC and HP gear. Take the SoV robes for instance. 12 ac. Up from 11 on the insidious robe. 1 point. With the 5:6 ratio, this just doesn't cut it. There's no way we can compete with tanks, there is no way we can become tank mages. We don't have the defensive skills to do so. That robe should have an AC of 14 or 15. As the set is right now, we're better off with going mostly insidious (except for the robe) and then using a slot like belt for a high ac belt. We'll get more AC and better stats. Each piece should have it's AC upped by 1 and the robe and pants by 2. It's not enough, but it's something.

The effects? Clerics get their level 59 heal spell and CH as well as group heals, spells they'll use. Necro's get summon corpse that uses the stackable corpses. Great stuff. Useful stuff. So what do enchanters get?

Reclaim energy on the bracers. We aren't a pet class, you've told us that time and time again. Most enchanters just use invis to kill their pet. We really aren't worried about mana reclaimed from the pet. We rarely use our pet, and when we do, mobs get rid of it for us. This is no good. It needs replacing with something useful. For instance, enthrall (or entrance, but duration on entrance would require a longer casting time), as long as the casting time was around 7-8 seconds. People will complain that they can get that much mana back just medding and about how it would be useless due to mez stacking issues (enchanters use dazzle or entrance, mostly and different mez spells don't override each other), but I think it would be pretty useful. Another pretty useful spell would be resist magic. This would be helpful mainly for raids, as you'll use group resist magic on your own group. If it had a 4 or so second casting time, it would be quite useful on raids. A spell from outside our class, preferably a fire based DD would be nice, even if it was for 120 or so damage. It would allow us to get around our MR problems somewhat. The level 34 mana theft DoT would have been an interesting choice as well. But reclaim energy shows that someone with no clue about the enchanter class designed the armor.

Invis on the boots. No problem with this. Quite useful, especially for releasing charmed pets. This one is well done. Everything else about the boots suck. But the spell is nice.

Rune IV. Not Rune III with it's jasper. Not Rune V with it's 300 poins more skin. No, Rune IV with a need of a peridot and a 28 second casting time. If it was Rune III with about a 15 second casting time, that would be fine. That's about a 300 point heal every 15 seconds. Not bad. Doesn't compare to celestial elixir or CH, but usuable somewhat in combat. Rune V would have been nice. This is a spell we use. It's 700 points of skin. Rune IV just isn't. When we need rune, we need 700 points of it. When we NEED we need the best. When we don't, we aren't going to waste the money on this... especially when we get mana the way we do. It really should be bumped up to Rune V or else replaced with Bedlam at a 15 second cast.

I think that having people design for classes they don't play has its merits... but it is very vital to get input from those that do play the classes. At the very least, check out Castersrealm's player lists and see what the high end enchanters on it are wearing. That will show what we value and provide some insight in what we need and what we'll use.

Lastly, robe looks. Mainly, Ishva, Robe of the Elements, and the new one from SoV that looks like the blighted robe. Retint all 3 to a different color. Planar robes are a status symbol. Giving the exact look to non-planar robes is an insult.

Conclusion:

Myself, I'm a crowd controller. I need crowds to control. There are camps I don't go to simply because they are boring to me. Guardian in sebilis, for instance. The pulls aren't big enough to provide me any excitement and this pretty much leaves me with just buffing, no need to go jumping over people to be able to get the right mob targetted, no quick stuns to buy the cleric healing time, no rush, no fun. I'm an enchanter and a damn good one. And yet in much of SoV, I play my warrior. Simply because all you need is 1 enchanter to buff everyone. And I pity that guy, because he's bored as hell. Why the warrior? Because if you get an additional mob, you have a warrior tank it. Yup, that's right, warriors are now crowd control. I can accept zones where I can't mez. I think it's nice to mix things up. But I can't accept lots of zones where I can't mez or cast any of my offensive spells (although, even when I can, like Kael, I don't bother. Our DoT's don't stack with superior necro ones or another enchanters, our DD's are too high taunt, too long recast, too low damage, too resisted... all we're really good for is buffs and crowd control). I can't accept zone after zone where I can't crowd control, where it is more effective to just tank the additional mob. Zones where all I can do is buff. Don't go comparing it to clerics. They matter. The spells they cast mid-fight and the choices they make of what spell to cast can decide victory or loss. They can watch their mana bar drop and the red health bars rise. Buffing every 24 minutes (haste for tanks) and 32 minutes (clarity for casters) doesn't compare. It's boring and something needs to be done. We need something active, something fun and useful mid fight. You've got some of the best quitting after 60 levels... you've got some of the best that won't play their enchanters in much of SoV. Something is wrong.

I honestly don't know what the answers are that'll fix everything and make it all okay. All I know is that SoV broke what was one of the most fun and well designed classes in EQ.

(1) Illusion: Male and Illusion: Female. It's one of those spells (like the Necro spell that lets them talk through their pet) that doesn't really have any specific "game" purpose (ie. it doesn't make you more "powerful"), but it would just be fun. I think we need a few more "fun" spells for every class, within reason of course.

(2) Illusion: Monster I, Illusion: Monster II, etc. The new Velious Mage pet spells (which are a great idea, by the way!) gave me this idea. If there's no room in the global file, why not do for Enchanters what you did for Mages, only with illusions instead of pets. When the Enchanter casts the illusion spell, they would appear to turn into a creature of the appropriate level range from within the current zone.

Second, many creatures have no faction, so such an illusion would grant no real protection. Third, if I turn into, say, an Orc in Oasis, that might allow me travel through the Orc camps safely, but it wouldn't protect me from anything else.

(3) Allow "group" illusions at higher levels. Level 49 - Group Illusion: Human, Group Illusion: Half-Elf

Level 57 - Group Illusion: Troll, Group Illusion: Ogre, Group Illusion: Iksar

[C] Group Alliance. At higher levels, perhaps there could be some way that an Enchanter could temporarily raise the faction of a group.

In conclusion, I would like to see each class have more abilities that are unique to that class, especially ones that give them more to offer in a grouping situation.

-Pets-
Like most other enchanters I've come to realize my pet is more like a dot that occasionally bashes to interrupt casting

Give us a few pet commands, not all of them just a few
/pet guard here - prevents pets from wandering off on the bad pathing in dungeons
/pet report health - saves us time and having to switch targets

Add these to pets past level 39 and you'd make a lot of enchanters happy, I know we're not a primary pet class, but neither are shaman, yet they have all the commands.. most of us just want a few

-Summoning Spells-
Almost every other castign class in the game has some sort of Summon item spell, we don't. Give us something like Summon Globe of Light. We need some way to summon something so our corpse doesn't decay in 30 seconds if we get killed before we can be ressed. even Wizards have soemthing like this, so no way it could be unbalancing

-Rune spells-

Otherwise I like the way these work, regaents are damn expensive, but not to the point it's as much of a burden on us as it is on clerics

Jacinths needed to enchant Steel, and Brellium used in jewelry making, hard to get ahold of for smiths as it makes some nice MR gear. Smiths get the short end of the stick most often.

Blue Diamonds, not too hard to obtain.. but I think I heard soemthign about them being used in jewelry now.. thats a BAD idea imho because now we yet again have the same problems we do with jacinths.

Change the regaents please, will make everyone happier

One very larger concern of mine is the high magic resistance rate of high level creatures in the expansions. One possible solution could be another magic resistance spell or an upgrade to the one we have. It should be a very large upgrade, maybe double the current one.

Another idea is 'potions'. Clarity is similar to SoW when viewed at how much its wanted, there are sow potions in the game so I see no reason for there to not be clarity items. I definitly do not want clarity potions but jewelry would be nice. It should cost about 10pp per charge to make, maybe take two metals and a vial of mana. For example maybe a ench gold bar + ench silver bar + vial of <somekind> mana = 1 charge clarity ring. Maybe a ench plat bar + ench gold bar + 2 vials <somehigherkind> mana = 10 charge clarity ring/necklace/braclet whatever. Some formula for a alacrity (24th lvl 40% haste) would be nice too.

These would be a great benifit durring high level raids where enchanters can't mez because of the resistances and spend all their time sitting and waiting until everyone needs clarity refreshed and the tanks need haste refreshed, it would allow for the mana to be spent earlier so we could nuke etc.

More illusions are always nice. While you don't see a reason that enchanters should get undead illusions or animal illusions, I do. Enchanters are the masters of illusion, we should be able to make others think we look like any one or anything. We shouldn't be limiited because an illusion is an animal or undead, cmon I can be a torch since level 4, I think I can pretend to be a bear later on.

On a side note of illusions, they don't show up in /w all. If I am a dark elf and use a high elf illusion, a /w mahkahl will show my race as high elf, a /w mahkahl all will show my race as a dark elf. Not to important on blue servers, and I guess not too important on red servers because of anon etc., but it would be very nice for this to work. It would also be nice if the races tree, skeleton, wolf, elemental, spectre and bear were added to the races that showed instead of <unknown>.

For the most part I think my class is really good, I have no trouble getting into groups most of the time and its fun to play.
