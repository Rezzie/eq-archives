hm, i forget. there was a time when a mob had to be over a certain level to be able to summon (51+ maybe?), but that's changed now, and i think any mob can be flagged to summon.

obviously, running away from a summoning mob can be quite hard ;P

you know about the hate list each mob has, yes? he wants to hit the person who's highest on his hate list first (preferably a tank, that's why paladins/shadowknights are good tanks, they can create a lot of hate points with spells). if that person is out of reach, a summoning mob will not run to that person, but instead it will say
"You will not evade me, XXX"
and the person will be summoned right up to the mob.

how do you handle this? by having someone who can take the hits stand at the mob.

mobs that summon are not soloable by those classes who use distance to be safe from mobs (wizards, druids, necros, rangers etc.). putting summoning mobs into areas has been sony's way of berfing quadding wizards and bards alot.

it's important to know that a mob will only start summoning if he has lost at least some health (1%? 5%? unsure), so your puller who shoots an arrow at a mob and damages it for 50 points can still pull the mob without being summoned.

if there are summoning mobs around, putting a damage shield onto your puller can be risky. if he takes too many hits, the mobs who were hitting them drop too much health, and start summoning - the puller might not make it back into camp.

pet classes can do summoning mobs by having their pets tank them. even tho the pet's master is nr1 on the hate list, as long as the pet is alive, the mob will not summon the master.

a mob can only summon so often in a certain period of time, but i forget how often. once per 30 seconds? once per 18 seconds? /shrug

Just to add a bit of intrigue...

Those spells that grant invulnerability (Divine Aura, Harmshield, etc), will NOT stop you from being summoned. The mob may not be able to hurt you, but that doesn't mean it can't keep you from getting away until the spell wears off.
