This is advice from the "official" strategy guide for EQ:

Bards - "Don't be a packrat. One kit (or bag even) full of stuff is plenty." So bards are supposed to get less loot than everybody else?

Mend - "It can't be used more than once a day or so at lower levels." Really? Man, my monk must be really special - she can mend every 6 minutes of real-time.

"The only real drawback to Mend is that using it unsuccessfully causes damage instead of healing it." When unsuccessful, there's a chance that it will cause damage, more often than not it doesn't.

Monks - "Don't even try to learn a weapon." Ummmm...Verant has basically forced Monks to start working 1hb and 2hb, with all the awesome weapons coming out of Kunark.

"A monk can easily outpace his enemies." Last I heard, Monks run no faster than all other classes, which means at the same speed as most mobs.

Monks - "Regular attacks (hand attacks) don't become magical in and of themselves no matter how high the Monk's level..." Every 30+ Monk out there can refute that one.

"Iksar Monks immediately start off with Dual Wield ability, so once you can come up with a second weapon you're ready to rock." And Human Monks don't start with Dual Wield? And what's this about a 2nd weapon? Didn't it just say (about 4 paragraphs ago) not to try to learn a weapon?

Monks - "And never forget the ever-present tail attack." Tail Rake is a skill learned at lvl 25 in place of Dragon Punch.

Monks and Strength - "Furthermore, it affects how much you can carry, which is of major concern for a Monk." The 14 lb limit is NOT affected by Strength.

"Older Erudite Necromancers will often give you a teleport of off Odus, if you can find any." Good luck finding a Necro that'll teleport you (and by the way, that typo "of off" is in the book, not my typo).

Rangers - "Go for max damage. Given the choice between 2-Handed Slash and 1-Handed Slash, remember that 2-Handed does more damage. 1-Handed is great with a shield, but then, shields are heavy, and there's the encumbrance issue ... and Rangers don't get Bash, anyway. So, unless, there's a good reason not to, go for the 1-Handed Slash." Am I wrong, or was that whole paragraph saying that 2hs is better? And then it says to go with 1hs? Huh?

Shadowknight - "Vs. red NPCs. Best to party with strong characters and Clerics." Shouldn't that be in a general advice section? Or is it only for Shadowknights?

Wizard - "INT suggestion. Wizards should put all but 5 of their starting points into Intelligence..." "...The other five points? The Wizard should keep putting as much as she can into Intelligence, even raising it to 200, before worrying about just increasing mana. Increasing the attribute increases more than just mana." Huh? What about the other 5 points?

(This is probably my favorite. It speaks for itself.) "Only the Wizard can take people to the Planes of Sky and Hate, but she shouldn't."

Dwarf - "Warrior. Because of their size, Dwarves are harder to hit in combat." Size means nothing.

"As a Dwarven Warrior, you can wear a full suit of bronze armor - no other race can do so." Oh really?

"Short legs. For obvious reasons, Dwarves have a hard time running away from their opponents." Size means nothing.

Gnome - "Like a viper. Gnomes can strike fast in combat and move out of the way even faster." Uh, no.

"Half Elves can Track." Sure, if they're Rangers, Druids, or Bards.

Half Elf - "The only other race that can yield Bards is Humans" Wood Elves are REALLY good at imitating Bards then.

Halfling - "One drawback Halflings have is that they earn experience more slowly than most other races" And I could have sworn they gained exp the FASTEST.

Iksar - "they have Dual Wield from the outset." Umm, if you're a Monk, sure. Otherwise, nope.

"Iksar have regenerative powers. Like Ogres, they're hard to keep down." Shouldn't that be "Like Trolls"?

Iksar - "They've got an extra attack (the tail)" Iksar Monks get Tail Rake at 25 in place of Dragon Punch. No other classes get a tail attack.

Wood Elf - "Practice your 1H and 2H skills in equal increments..." I'll make sure to tell all the Wood Elven Druids and Bards this.

"Necromancers are the kings of kiting." A little later on..."Druids are the masters of kiting." Consistency please.

Wizard - "Level 20-24 are hell levels" Really? I'm sure that they mean those levels suck, but using the term hell level is really stupid.

Shaman "Keep...everyone moving quickly with Spirit of Wolf (haste) spells." Again, I know what they mean, but calling it a haste spell is really stupid. Use the terminology that the real players do. Haste spells are ones that increase your attack speed/decrease the delay on your weapons.
