The concept was that Alchemy would be a trade skill one sold to other players.

Well, the problem is nobody can carry enough potions for a decent session, 'cause they don't stack. And people don't wanna buy them in bulk, for the same reason.

This is unavoidable 'cause it appears they use up the 'size of stack' field in items that have effect for other data, so items with effects can't stack.

So, until potions can be compressed somehow, you won't have mass buying of SoW potions, and a player based sale system for practicing alchemy won't work.

The solution, as I see it, is top provide compression recipies that turn 6 sow potions into a 5 charge sow potion, and then 5 5 charge sow potions into a 20 charge sow potion, then 6 20 charge sow potions into a 100 charge sow potion. Add an expensive reagent at each stage, with increasing trivial levels, and give the potions back on failure(*), and you get:
A> More money sink.
B> A reason to use the lower charge number potions - they are much cheaper per charge
C> Better prices for sow potions.
D> More useful stuff for Shamans to make while increasing skill. The compacting would be harder than the actual creation - after all, you are making a far more potent brew.

(*) is important, because otherwise the prices for the multi-charges would quickly spiral out of control, and their being trivial higher than standard potions would make them nearly useless for practicing up, which is half of the point. The punishment for failure of the expensive 'condensing' component is enough, I would think, and can be easily throttled. Or, you could simply only get 5 of the potions back on failure.

You could even require the 100 charge one to require a high-level mob drop, keeping supply under control, and giving yet another rare item for folks to lust after. If you wanna be evil, make the 100 charge one LORE, providing a secondary economy of low-charge 100 charge potions.
