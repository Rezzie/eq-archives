I hate to argue this point further, but there is indeed a bug in your rogue pickpocketing code.

You are very correct when you say that at least 1 coin of every type is remaining on the mob when it dies if the rogue pickpockets it. That is true.

However, where your bug is is in the looting of the mob.

Lets say that you have a group of 3, one rogue.

The rogue pickpockets the mob down to 1pp 1g 1s 1c.

Whoever in the group loots that mob will get all of the loot because it can't be divided by 3. The only way the group would get even money is if there were 3pp 3g 3s and 3c. This is a huge bug in your code.

If you're in a group of 6, if a mob doesn't carry 6pp, and you have one looter, the rest of the group will NEVER get a plat if the total plat on the mob is less than 5.

This is why, if you watch, groups all play with lootsplit OFF and take turns looting.

In my group of 3 the other day, with one designated looter used (because he could carry the heavy weapons looted) with lootsplit ON, he had gotten well over 34p more than the rest of the group due to your lootsplit coding bug.

So, to get back on topic, a rogue can FORCE the lootsplit to not split fairly and by looting the corpse beforce anyone else, get the major majority of the money off of the mob due to the logic of your lootsplit code.

The only way to fix this is to pool the money (IE 1p 1g 1s 0c becomes 1110c which is then divided by group members total and then converted into proper coinage to reduce weight.) Or, remove lootsplit all together, but I guarentee, playing a rogue, I can get WAY more money than the rest of my group and furthermore by playing a warrior and saying, "I'll loot everything because I can carry it" I can use the poor lootsplit bug to my advantage and make over 10 times more pp than the rest of my group in a given day.

I don't care for the common loot pool either. I don't think any Rogue does, at least those of us who've been around awhile. They changed it around 10-12 months ago, I believe around the same time they gave us a higher Resist Poison stat. No one noticed at first - we didn't have Abashi writing the Patch messages back then - but once we caught on....

At first, of course, they denied it. Well, Abashi denied it; I'll give him the benefit of the doubt and assume he was lied to as well... he was still rather new to the position, and their attitude towards the customers was already becoming pretty negative. We complained for months about it to no avail - and believe it or not the Rogues were the most vocal. We tried, they didn't listen. So don't blame the Rogues... it's a Skill that actually works (don't get us started), and it's what we do. And since it's still an active Skill I can only assume it's 'as designed', so no one can complain and no one can be warned/banned for doing it.

All I'd like to know - the question that was never reasonably answered back then - is what was so wrong with the skill that it had to be changed in this way? There were other options - arguably better options - so I think we finally realized that this was just the simplest to program; don't know if that was ever acknowledged by Abashi. Apparently the skill was checked against the MOBs 'loot table' every time Pick Pockets was clicked, rather than just once; there was never a seperate 'loot pool'. Assumptions, yes, but reasonable ones given the evidence; thus, we were getting tons of cash off low level critters.

Why couldn't they program in a seperate loot pool for Pick Pockets? Maybe it was too much work for the benefit returned. Or maybe they were overly concerned about the economy and didn't like the extra cash flowing in. Either way, I doubt it will be changed again - it doesn't have to be. However, if they're willing to consider changing it back (Dare I say it... Fixing the Skill?), we can give them all sorts of reasonable options.

The most reasonable option I've seen - and arguably better than the current setup due to 'thievery' issues - is to increase the reset delay on it to something similar to Forage; significantly reduces the chance of multiple hits during a single combat, so a seperate 'loot table' check wouldn't be near as big an issue. Then they could return it to the previous arrangement, and the 'economy' would not be seriously affected; all the new 'money sinks' considered do compensate, and many people past the mid-20s destroy silver and copper anyway. Also, since we cannot pick 'tagged' items (Magic and No Drop, not sure about Lore), if possible the skill check could remove those types of items from the loot table.

I doubt Abashi will check this thread again... not the type of issue he generally comes back to. So, if I don't see a response by Monday I might post a seperate thread. Feel free to bring up the issue and quote me (or any other good ideas) if you want to start a thread of your own on this issue.

Thanks for listening.

In summary:

1. Rogues can pickpocket. It is a skill unique to them.

2. Rogues make some money pickpocketing. Most do some PPing to keep the skill maxed. Others do it to build up a collection of gold.

3. No rogue can pickpocket a monster clean. If you loot a monster and get no coins, it is either because your party member beat you to it (and had split off), or because the monster had no coins.

4. If you consistently get 1 coint of each type when looting, and there is a rogue nearby, you might have a heart-to-heart talk with him or her.

5. The only valuable items that Rogues can pickpocket are things like high-level spells and gems. They can NEVER pickpocket an item with the 'MAGIC' tag turned on.

6. Unlike other classes, Rogue 'quest' armor is dropped, not quested. My feeling is that Verant intentionally did this to help 'free up' left over currency being held by those with quick hands.

7. Most Rogues are played by human beings. If you suspect one of 'over' practicing PP, then gently, but firmly, remind them to only take their fair share. If they continue, talk to your party leader and decide whether or not to keep them in the party.

The rogue class is very handy in sticky situations, so I dare say no one should dismiss it out of hand. If you find yourself not trusting the rogue in your party, it is time for communication. Painting all rogues with the same brush requires an enormously large brush.
