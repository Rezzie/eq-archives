Yes it works. The delay is minimal, and has very little effect on the game. And the increased bandwidth more than makes up for the minimal ping delay (averages 600ms, about twice a normal modem ping).

The problem is that satellite modem companies really don't like sustained traffic. They have mechanisms in place to "freeze" your connection after some unknown period of time if they see a sustained level of traffic, even if it's not too high.

They expect small, perhaps rapid, bursts of traffic, typical when browsing the web or downloading e-mail. But they hate it when you use a steady chunk of bandwidth like EQ uses. So what happens is that anywhere from 10 mins to 4 hours, you will be playing and then you will suddenly go LD for no reason. You will exit out of the game, your satellite connection will seem fine, but no traffic will flow. You can either wait like 10 mins for the freeze to be lifted, or just disconnect and reconnect.

Not so good for playing the game. I now use my sattelite modem for downloading EQ patches, Windows patches, and e-mail. Then I switch to my dial-up connection for playing EQ. Lame.
