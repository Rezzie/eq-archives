Ok - so if you make it have a component with a 10 hour camp..err i mean "spawn" time to get, it makes the item enter the world at a rate of 1 every 10 hours.

So obviously only one person can complete the quest every 10 hours, provided that spawn is always camped.

but if you break it up into 5 separate pieces of 2 hours each, 5 separate people can be doing the quest at once, providing a "richer" experience for more people, without 5 people standing in a spot pulling PnP on each other.

Which is more beneficial? 5 people travelling the world and getting the item in 10 hours, or 5 people sitting in a circle in south karana petitioning the GM's and getting frustrated. Verant's policy is obviously the latter on the nice items, such as the Aegis of life and Jboots.

There is another option though...

Make the items drop once in 10 hours off a type of npc in a zone, or make the npc not spawn in a static spot...IE, have grimrot pop once every 5-6 hours in one of the karana's in a random spot, and de-pop after say 10 mins. Sure its a lot harder to program, and would require more spawn tables, but it would eliminate camping.

The downside is that the D3wd druids would have a distinct advantage...but just make the faction requirements such that you'd have to be pretty persistant to get it, and make the resultant object no-drop.

Make questable items as good as the droppable ones, and you'll see campquest deteriorate into something nearer "the vision" We don't want to sit for 20 hours in a little room looking for goblin_421 to pop, we'd rather be out exploring the world and doing quests...but the risk/reward points eq players to sit and camp. why do a quest when you can get better items and exp by sitting on yer butt and fighting once in 5 minutes?

The only way to end camping is a) no longer require it or b) random loot tables c) make it craftable

a) Involves keeping the static spawn system but go with a Monty-haul loot system. Where the monsters always have the goodies everyone is after. How rare something actually is remains a unknown until a lot of the same item goes into circulation. Supply and demand will determine the trade price.

c) The only reason to gain items is to make the character better in some way.
