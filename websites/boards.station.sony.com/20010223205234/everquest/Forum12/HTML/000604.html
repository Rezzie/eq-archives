Enchanters are over-powered because they have four of the most important needs of a group - mana regen, slow spells, haste spells, and crowd control.

The clarity and haste spells are too obvious to be looked at. Everyone knows why they're good, how good they are, and how that makes enchanters over-powered and overly desired. Let's get to the meat of the issue, though. What is that? The absolute NEED for enchanters due to crowd control and slows. Why are these two things NEEDED at 50+? That's the issue at hand. So here we go.

Back in the old days of level 20 enchanters sucked. They were nearly useless because mezzing mobs was a waste of time. You just got the tanks in there tanking everything while the casters nuked one or two down as fast as possible to lower the overall damage done by the mobs. Drop a few really fast, and you can survive. What happens today at 50+? Well, in Sebilis if you have 3 mobs come attack and you don't have a crowd control you run or evac. The fact is that no tank/cleric combo could stand up to 3-4 mobs without any form of slows or crowd control. Why? What happened?

The key --->> TANKS CAN'T TANK ANYMORE

Tanks simply can't take damage anymore. Remember back when warriors could solo nearly anything that was blue? Not anymore. If it doesn't con green, they aren't messing with it. Even then they are very, very wary. But how does this affect enchanters? It makes them necessary. If an enchanter mezzes two Sebilis mobs they are preventing an enormous amount of damage that would be devastating to the tanks and the cleric's mana (which means it would be devastating to the group in general since cleric mana is the heart of the group). The same holds true for slows. Without slows in some good groups it would take 1/3 of the cleric's mana to kill a single blue mob. At level 20 in Mistmoore, the enchanter is preventing unwanted, but fully tolerable damage. Yes, it's helpful and warrants the enchanter's place in the group, but you know what? The group would live on if the enchanter left. Everyone knows what happens when an enchanter leaves a Sebilis group - you leave.

The point -->> Enchanter power is directly (and probably linearly) related to the power of the mobs in relatioin to the tanks. (i.e. the less the tanks can tank, the more powerful the enchanters get).

There is also a linear relation between the normal speed at which casters med and the necessity of enchanters due to their clarity spells, but we won't discuss that further.

What other effects does this have on the game and its players? Obviously it has any and all effects you can imagine due to every group after Karnor's basically REQUIRING an enchanter to do anything. If you do anything in Sebilis except exit or guardian without an enchanter, you'd better have a bunch of 60's in the group (specifically the puller). But what other effects are there? Let's take a peek at the casters (mages and wizards more specifically).

What was the roll of mages and wizards back in mistmoore at level 20? When you get 3-4 mobs, killed 1-2 of them right away to get the fight under control and managable. They were darn good at it too, I would say. What is the roll now? Nothing, really. Mages do damage efficiently with that monster pet, and wizards evac. Nukes have no place anymore. Nukes don't bring the fight to a controllable level by eliminating mobs quickly. Crowd control is how you survive a hard fight. Simply turn the mobs off until you're ready to deal with them, because you simply can't tank them long enough for the now-less-effective nukers to bring a few down.
