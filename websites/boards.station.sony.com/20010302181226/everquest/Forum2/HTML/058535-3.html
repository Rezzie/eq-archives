You are a caster, thus the spellbook until level 35. VI has given a decent reason as to why it is that level. If you don't wish to stare at the spellbook, play a Monk, Rogue, Warrior. Simple as that.

1) How did you get to the place where you are fighting?

Players have plenty of time to see the world as they make their way through a zone to where they wish to fight.

As for the argument that it gives a sense of missing something...How? You aren't missing out on a fight. You see the "Inc....one per". You get up, look around, see where it is coming from, cast, and if needed, sit back down to med. If you really don't want to miss out on the action, then med only between fights. Put up with the time required for you to get back to full. This way, you can stand up the entire fight so as not to miss out on anything.

A spellbook is not a hindrance to a caster. If you solo, even without a spellbook, you will more than likely find a safe area to do it in so as not to have a fight on your hands. If you are in a group, staring at a spellbook, you have players to make sure nothing attacks you.

During a fight? They really don't last all that long now do they? What are you missing? A bad pull? You should know that before they even get back to your group (with a good puller telling you anyway).
