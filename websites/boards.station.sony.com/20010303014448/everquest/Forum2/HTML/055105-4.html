After reviewing the entirety of the posts again... hey, it only took a few hours! I've noticed that the bards are falling out into roughly two groups (setting aside the exceptional individuals who have some of the more bizarre points of view):

1) the "more-melee" group, and the
2) better songs group

I respectfully submit that BOTH groups should be satisfied, since bards are a class that can be played in different fashions, with little noticeable benefit of one path over the other, and that should continue.

Where they agree:

Both sides seem to agree on a reduction of conflicts between songs and spells. Overwrite issues, and stacking issues need to be looked at carefully. Buffing and debuffing effects should also be carefully reconsidered, since they are currently both incompatible with spells, and lower in effect. Considering the fact that a long-lasting spell is preferable to 99% of the players than a constantly maintained song, all non-damage combat-related songs should either stack seamlessly or be significantly better than the like spells.

Both sides also seem to agree that utility songs, which do NOTHING to affect experience potential need to have durations and ranges adjusted. Levitate, and invisibility especially need major adjustments (isn't it enough that one person deciding to cast a spell, loot, or anything else will get an entire invisible party killed, do we have to deal with a 10 foot effect radius, too?)
And would it be "unbalancing" for levitate to last about as long as water breathing?

These two points are almost universally recognized by bards as needing work, mostly to enhance a bard's usefulness to groups. Less bard-caused snafus during combat, and fewer bard-lag created deaths in travel.

Where they differ:

Melee effectiveness... some bards want more melee at all levels. However the most convincing argument is for melee abilities at the stratospheric levels, due to the facts that: parties are itemed and spelled to their buff caps already, so without melee damage or worthwhile song damage, bards are reduced to manasong. Further, many of the target creatures are virtually immune to magic, making even spells ineffective, and rendering a bard practically useless.

Now, I would personally rather have better damage songs, and maybe even some new "creative" songs that would affect combat, but the melee-bards have just as much right to play their way.

So... I would suggest, rather than simply increase melee abilities across the board for bards, add a serious version of the Whistling Warsong to the upper-level bard. Make it work while playing only, as a self-only song which allows no twisting with only minimal visual effects. It should enhance combat skills to a rewarding level for the 50+ bard, while making it impossible to actually use other songs while playing it. In effect, hit that song, and you're a weak to moderate melee class for a few moments.

This simple addition would allow the post-50 melee bards to be useful, without creating an unbalancing effect. Whereas simply increasing melee abilities might introduce complications with song effects.

As for the Song-based bards: scale up song effectiveness and damage to rise at a rate comparable to other classes. If a bard's damage potential at level 10, while grouped with another class is 45% (for example) of the group damage, then that same bard at level 55 grouped with that same class should STILL BE 45%. No class should lose effectiveness as they rise, but ours apparently does.

---------------------

I've tried to summarize and encapsulate the major issues without going into too many specifics. Mostly, because our problems are larger than a song or two, here or there. They are across the board and need to be approached as such.

Very few of us want to be the ultimate solo class, and none of the balancing changes should affect our current solo abilities (showy, effective, yet ultimately way too risky and/or slow to be a serious advancement technique). However, our group abilities should be exceptional, not overshadowed by every caster class and hybrid in the game.
