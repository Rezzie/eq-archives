A couple of thoughts.

Hate list changes... Clerics are a plate class, Shaman are a chain class, druids are a leather class. So one perspective is that clerics lost some of their stranglehold on efficient healing, but have better surviveability when they use that healing. Plus no other class has the short term invulnerability they do as a trump card. Show me a cleric that has never had a cause to use that?

Also, as many others have brought up, if you use a utility class (shaman or druid) as a "cleric-light", you are giving up all the other benefits they could provide the group due to mana usage. Doesn't that make you a bit squeamish thinking that your primary healer has to choose between the heal that keeps you alive or evac? Or that the shaman still has to decide between slow and heal for efficiency? Especially now that slow doesn't generate the amount of aggro that it used to.

In my opinion Verant has made some good adjustments. The most efficient group is STILL the current model of CLERIC/ENCHANTER/WARRIOR/(rogue or whatnot), it's just that some other combinations are now viable in a wider variety of settings, and that's good for everybody.

Yes clerics are boring to play. So are warriors. There are different classes to suit a wide variety of playstyles - if you find that the one you are playing does not suit yours maybe you should try a different class. If verant succeeds in balancing them and making no single class necessary, then you should have no reservations in choosing the one with the playstyle that best matches your preferences.

Now I do agree that druids fit the "monty haul" RPG mentality more than any other class. They have the most versatility, adequate offense to solo, and are probably responsible for about 75% of the item farming that goes on. So I can see where giving them a better heal is somewhat upsetting given all the apparent advantages they have - but remember that the heal upgrades are to encourage grouping, and not particularly relevant when soloing. Now those druids can be solicited to join a dungeon group that would have otherwise had to give up due to lack of healing, and that is good for everyone. Although (/sarcasm on) if Verant really wanted to get some cash out of the economy and limit the use of these adjusted healing spells, they could require a 1pp component to cast them. (/sarcasm off)
