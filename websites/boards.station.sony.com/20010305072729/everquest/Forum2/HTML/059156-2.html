I posted this on an earlier monk thread. Keep in mind this was all from me so may need 'balancing' as far as timers, percents, and penalties go. I feel this way about it...I've done the creative part, now why can't Verant do the number crunching? =) I would like to see something like this happen for each class and would gladly post ideas for them as well...if I am being taken seriously that is.

This is what my post will focus on, giving monks more choices over how they do damage and having more fun along the way. Let's start by seeing where the choices begin and where they end.

-Point A- here you are and there's the mob
-Point B- here you are (still alive) and there's the mob (quite dead)

We all want to get from point A to point B. At least I hope =) I don't want this to be greatly altered by huge damage increases or overbalancing skills. I would like to see How we get from point A to point B change. This is what I propose: making use of a popular martial arts idea of 'chi' or 'lifeforce' energy (make sure you read the entire post before drawing pre-mature conclusions). As with everything in EQ, there is a need for balance. I realize we cannot bestow monks with powers and not expect a downside. In hopes of having this considered for implentation, I have tried to make it balanced as possible.

I have created a set techniques that could be used from earlier levels and up to 60. I feel that what you learn should always be useful, no matter how early you gained that knowledge. Here is where we make use of the empty mana bar all monks carry around. The last thing I want is for monks to turn into hybrids and just carry around spells or have any association with having mana. But, since the mana bar is there we can use it to represent our 'chi' or 'lifeforce'. Each technique would cost a certain amount of 'lifeforce', but it wouldn't be a situation where you click a button and use X amount of your energy. For example, if you wanted to use the Open Wounds technique (this will be detailed later), you would charge it for X amount of time. The longer you charge it the better of an effect you will have and the more energy it will drain. As you drain your energy in preparation to perform the technique, you are more vunlerable to attacks and cannot be disturbed by damage or it will be ruined and energy will be lost for nothing. Basically, you are 'stunned' for the duration of the charging and will be free to move normally after executing the technique (barring the various side effects). Your energy will regain over time, but cannot be effected by clarity, bard songs, or other mana giving items or spells since a monk's energy is not mana. It's just that, a person's lifeforce. Your lifeforce could become stronger after gaining levels, but not by leaps and bounds.

These techniques would be usable only if both hands are free. They are meant for the true, bare-handed martial arts masters out there. I feel this would bring about an interesting set of strategic options. Do you choose to go bare-handed and use your techniques and pressure points (i posted about pressure points earlier)? or do you use your nice blunt weapons with good stats and procs? As I stated, your hands must be free to use a technique and there will be a time limit on when you can equip a weapon after using a technique..maybe as low as 20 mins or as much as an hour (starting from your last use of energy). Now let me explain a few of the techniques I've pondering over, inspired somewhat by the discipline concept and all those movies i've watched over the years:

-Shadow Fist Technique-
1. The upside:
A technique used to concentrate dozens of blows to be given within moments of one another. The damage of each blow would scale from 50 percent of your maximum fist damage to 75 percent of your maximum fist damage, depending on the amount of energy used to charge. The actual number of blows attempted would range from a few dozen up to several dozen, depending on the amount of energy charged. This would occur in a very short amount of time, about 2 or 3 seconds (the actual punching, not including the charge time).

2. The downside:
After usage of Shadow Fist, your spirit is tired and will not allow you to execute any technique for 5 minutes and will not allow you to use Shadow Fist again for an hour. For 5 minutes after using Shadow Fist, your movement speed will be reduced by 15 percent up to 35 percent depending on the amount of energy used. The delay on your fists would be increased by 20 percent up to 60 percent, depending on the amount of energy used. I feel this balances out the nice damage output you could give to any single mob in such a short amount of time.

-Flaming Palm Technique-
1. The upside:
A technique used to harness the fire of one's spirit and focus it into tangible flames. While charging this technique, perhaps flames could surround the fists (similar to when casters begin casting fire dd spells, the graphics are there so lets use them). Each successful blow would carry 75 percent up to 125 percent of non-melee fire damage based (based on charging time) on the total amount of damage given by that melee blow.
The bursts of fire that hit the mob after every few punches could carry a small fire dd graphic, it's there so lets use it =) This would last for 20 to 45 seconds, depending on charge time.

2. The downside:
After the technique is completed all resists except fire will be reduced by 50 percent up to 75 percent, depending on the amount of energy used. This will last for 15 minutes. Also during this time your fists will need time to recover from the extreme heat and will only do 65 percent up to 85 percent of their normal damage, depending on energy used. Your lifeforce will need time to recover as well and will not allow you to use any techniques for 5 minutes and the Flaming Palm technique for an hour. The extreme heat has also left your defenses hindered. Your Ac will be reduced by 10 percent up to 20 percent depending on energy used. Also, dodge, block, and riposte will be slightly less effective. I feel this adequately balances the fire-based damage monks could dish to mobs.

-Open Wounds Technique-
1. The upside:
A technique used to exploit the wounds previously given to mobs. This technique would only have effect on mobs that have lost at least 10 percent of their total life. Starting after the first successful blow, each following hit would do a minimum of 5 percent up to 15 percent extra damage than the previous blow, like a crescendo, depending on charge time. This would top out at a certain point, however, doing no more than 3 times the fists maximum damage on any given blow. This technique would not carry from mob to mob. It could be used on a single mob until it dies or the time expires on the technique. It would last 10 seconds up to 30 seconds depending on charge time.

2. The downside:
After using Opens Wounds technique, you cannot regain health for 1 minute via healing spells or natural regain. Your lifeforce will not allow you to use any technique for 5 minutes and Open Wounds for an hour. From all the mess caused by exploting wounds, your aim is greatly hindered. For 15 minutes after using Open Wounds your ATK is decreased by 15 percent up to 25 percent and you are more likely to blocked, dodged, parried, or riposted...the severity depending on the amount of energy used. I feel this balances the nasty amount of damage possible.

Now that I've explained 3 of my Techniques, I'll explain further on their general states. These 3 could be learned by monk guildmasters and their could be hidden monk master in the lands that would train you in other techniques. They could be available at low lvl's, maybe starting in the teens and range up to 60. The negative effects of techniques will stack.

I feel that this could increase the effectivness of monks, but due to the risks of the side effects, would not be overbalancing. This would give monks the opportunity to use strategy instead of targeting a mob and pushing auto-attack. And most of all...it would be fun.
