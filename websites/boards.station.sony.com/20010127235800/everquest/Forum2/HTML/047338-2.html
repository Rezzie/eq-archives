# Actually, it's EXACTLY like a cop arresting you on suspicion of a crime. #

No, it isn't.

In the real world, cops have to arrest people because if they didn't, the suspects could well be in hiding when it came time to have the trial. Or at the very least, they need to be taken in to the station house in order to be conclusively ID'ed (fingerprints, etc.) and questioned.

NONE of that applies to EQ.

Your character's identity is not in question. Nor is his location -- he's right there on the servers, and he can't escape or "go underground".

In a world where everyone's existence and identity was registered with the "authorities", and the government had the ability to summon anyone at will to the police station for questioning, or the courtroom for judgement, or the jailhouse for sentencing, there would be no NEED for arrest.

So since EQ is such a world, why is Verant "arresting" people for mere suspicion? Actually, being summoned to be asked some questions is more like being arrested. What Verant is doing is more analogous to throwing people in jail and refusing bail until they get around to having a trial.

Furthermore, the EULA gives Verant a blanket "wiretap and search warrant" for everyone in their world. They could get more results in less time by letting the suspects continue their activities, closely monitored to catch them in the act of committing another "crime", as opposed to suspending them and then trying to sweat a confession out of them.

So it raises the question of whether it's being the way it is in order to feed someone's need for a powertrip, since it appears that just catching and punishing the guilty could be done more effectively by other means.
