"However, they should make an option to be able to move characters on any server to the roleplaying one so that people can powergame all they want to the level of their choice and then once having obtained sufficient level can then roleplay on the other one. After all, it's far too hard to level while roleplaying and such an option just might prevent powergaming from emerging in the environment."

"2. /movelog ( for the reason that many people will be coming from all servers with all different char.'s, so it will be balanced )"

"/movelog baby Mass Xodus to the promisedland Baby!"


What I leared from RPing among the Dark Elves and Orcs on UO is this: the more impediments there are to powerlevelling in the RP community you are trying to foster, the higher the percentage of true RPers you play with.

Allowing /movelog will allow levelled characters to come to this theoretical new server. So solo type powerlevellers who want to escape overcrowding/guilds/bad KS reputations will come to the new server in droves. Remove /movelog and only people willing to level up (only a SECONDARY motivation for RPers) will come.

To take it further, I absolutely GUARANTEE you that the best way to create a true unenforced RP server is to DOUBLE the exp required to level.

Only the most extreme of powerlevellers will come to this server to reach 60 first - and they will be few, far between, and dramatically removed from the main population. Everyone else coming to the server will endure the doubled exp penalty only for one reason - for the RP. And they will find it, because the ONLY other people to come did so for the same reason. Those who get frustrated and leave (further reinforcing the popultion of the server) will be those for whom the levelling ended up being more important than the social interplay with others.
