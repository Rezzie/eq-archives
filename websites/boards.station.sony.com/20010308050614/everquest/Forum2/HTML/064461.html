I tend to disagree with some of the issues here.

I too am quite burned out with this game. I play a 50 Wizard, 35 Warrior, 21 Cleric, 18 monk.

The reason I can understand folks leaving is that if you do not belong to a large or uber guild, the end game is basically gone.

A good example is my 50 Wizard. What is the motivation for me to go higher? Better equipment? Spells? Adventure?

Well - I am a casual player. I hold a 9-5 day job and have a family. The time that I have invested into this game I feel has been all to nothing. Right now, I have never been to a plane, never killed a dragon. My guild is too small to tackle these things and after trying to get into a larger guild for 3 months with nothing to show for it, I basically only log on my Wizard to Tp folks around the zones. Is that fun? Heck no. But at this point - what else do I have to look forward to? Not a thing. Just log to say hi to friends, maybe gate and help some newbies out. That's it.

Since most of the high end areas to play are based upon large multiple groups to adventure, the time and coordination to pull something like this together is far to great for me unless I want to quit my job, get a divorce, and neglect every family member I have.

Verant has made it exceedingly clear that they are gearing the game to Uber and large guilds to provide content for them, and in the mean time feel that if the UBERs are happy, the challenge and adventure is there for my player demograhpic as well. They are trying to make the average player play the game how THEY want us to play it.

Unfortunately, this is not the case for most subscribers. The launch of Velious makes that point painstakingly clear. I should have never bought it in the first place. My Wizard is useless there and the rest of my chars and having a hard time even trying to tackle the easy potions of that continent.

So - as far as people leaving, please understand it may not be because they hate the game. It is just after a while, there is nothing left to do within their means. The gap is so wide between the UBERs and "normal" players, that eventually it is only the UBERs that will remain and new subscribers that see that game and want to try it.

For me - the only thing left to play for is fun. Start new chars with my friends and Plevel to 50 only to retire and start a new one again. End game is not a possibility for me. The only thing I can do is play to be happy and not worry about getting to 60 and be uber twinked with the best equipment and go to Veeshan's Peak. All of these scenarios are far from my grasp. Either that or quit, unsubscribe and decide that I do have a life.
