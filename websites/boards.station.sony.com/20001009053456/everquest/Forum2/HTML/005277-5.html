Besides which, preventing twinking would be one of the poorest ways to create a better RP environment. The first step would be to have a character's actions affect the environment, beyond the faction adjustments. If a character goes out and slays the dragon that has been terrorizing the land, the local citizens should know WHAT he or she did, not just that the character is viewed warmly now. That adventure should have some signifigance. The dragon should no longer exist in the character's environment. Sure, it would be visible, since other character's need to see it and be able to have the same adventure. But, the dragon should no longer be able to affect the dragon-slaying character, nor should the character be able to affect the dragon. MMRPG mechanics require some compromises. This adjustment would also require a change to the ridiculous camping of rare drops. Said dragon would have to drop the same loot for all characters involved. Then, we could have locked treasure chests for rogues to open... etc.

This is just one example of a better RP environment. Verant WANTS endless camping of the same NPC for rare drops. Keeps us playing and paying longer. Twinking keeps people playing and paying longer.

Why is it that my character can attack a townsperson, get slaughtered, then return to the same NPC and hear, "Welcome to my shop, would you like to see..."? Shouldn't my character have to do something for the NPC to make up for his stupidity?

You CAN play EQ as a RPG if you try. If you claim to be trying to roleplay, then say twinks are ruining your RP experience--well, you are simply very bad at roleplaying. Roleplaying means you play the role suited to the environment. If the environment is laser-blasting space cowboys with jetpacks, then strap on your pack and blast away. If the environment is crook-slaying, police-dodging urban vigilantes, then load your .357, **** your double-barrel, and rescue some theatre-goer. The EQ environment IS Ro-wearing, Silvery-weilding, buff-item-carrying newbies. It may not have started that way, but it has evolved to that. Worlds change, not always the way you want; MMORPG worlds should, too.

If you can't beat them: join them, leave them, or b*tch about them on forums.
