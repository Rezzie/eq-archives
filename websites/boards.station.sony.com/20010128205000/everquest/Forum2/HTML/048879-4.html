The best server in my opinion, after having played on Tallon Zek now for over a year, would be Good vs Evil such that evil races (to spite what god they worship) are evil as well as "good" races that worship evil gods. Then disallow any healing/buffing between the good and evil team. Positive effects would be:

No more crossteam issue, as they'd be all but disfunctional together, and certainly no help in PvP. This also makes sense from a roleplaying perspective as the opposing gods simply do not allow their followers to assist them magically.

The good team would be purely good, as they would loose all their necromancers and shadowknights. The loss of these two potent classes would help assist the dark team who is (and has always been) without tracking (which may make a huge impact on PvP if tracking players becomes more viable in the future). It would also allow for better roleplaying on the part of good characters, as they could attack one of their own if they were known to be evil, and a light race evil character would try to hide their nature. (For example, necromancers/SK's would hide their pets before entering town, having undead wandering around good cities would end)

Evil aligned characters from the good races wold be more rare. As it stands necromancers are very popular in PvP teams because of their inherent power, but their numbers are unrealistic from "good" races. An evil Human or Gnome on a GvsE server would be worthy of some respect for enduring what they no doubt did at low level, dealing with not only NPC faction issues but PC's as well. Their numbers would be fewer, but this seems more realistic, as the races they come from are generally good.

One thing that would have to be changed along with the designation of the team would be to remove racial faction hits. If a Human paladin kills his nemesis the shadow knight it is neither fair nor does it make sense that his guards, merchants, or even guild would shun him for it. If each character could give the faction hit for his particular faction (IE a PC Human SK from Qeynos would give negative Bloodsaber faction, but only that faction), that would be great; if not, just remove faction hits.

My vision of the utopic PvP (and roleplaying, by happenstance) server.

-Todd (Tallon Zek)

Roleplaying & PvP

First off, I'll admit that I play on PvP teams for the sole purpose of roleplaying; in general I find PvP irritating and rarely ever done in good spirit. This greatly effects why I'd want a GvsE server and not simply a "new PvP" server of some other sort. I can feel the flames now of the PKs screaming "blubie" in my ear; feel free, I don't care.

Everyone saying they want diety based PvP has the right idea, but I think what must be considered is that all PvP must be restricted to fights that are always in character to occur. Yes a dwarven paladin and human paladin may not always be on great terms, but in general they should get along, and very rarely fight outright (and if they really must, there's /duel). People may argue that it is up to the player to decide who they get along with, not code restrictions, but I would point out that "4 team PvP" has faultered in some cases on the current teams servers. Players will always make unwritten rules, and others will then break them, they must be coded into the ruleset to truly exhist. If the teams were:
Evil: All evil races and all worshippers of Bertoxx, Innoruuk, and Cazic.
Good: All others.
Any instance of PvP would be justified from a "good vs evil" stand point. Some strange "teams" would exhist (such as an evil Erudite and Trolls) but this makes more in game sense then to have said Erudite teamed with good characters that would despise him (at least in this case they'd both worship Cazic Thule, so there may be a good reason for such an alliance).

I hope item loot is not reinstated, it throws class balance out the window. If it is, though, I'd hope the PvP limit would be reduced to +/-4 levels. Even that may be enough to keep people away from such a server. To those that want more PvP and item loot, there is Rallos, correct?
