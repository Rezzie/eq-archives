It seems that in the distribution of anti-summoned spells, Verant tried establishing the same kind of relationship as they did with the Necro and Cleric:

The Necro starts off learning how to summon undead before they learn how to control or destroy them. It is a somewhat foolhardy approach, but it leads to quick power. Only later do they learn how to control and destroy the forces they have begun dealing with.

The Cleric is opposed to this manipulation of the dead and therefore learns early on how to counter this blasphemy, long before the Necro himself bothers.

The Mage does a similar thing, where they only later learn how to banish the forces they have learned how to summon.

The Druid sees these summonings as an offense against nature and therefore early on learns how to return the elementals to the element they were summoned from.

Not as much of a basis in fiction for this ability, just a way of mirroring the Cleric/Necro relationship.

As for why you don't get charm elements, etc:

Necro's have power over the dead. Therefore, they can animate the dead, charm the undead, etc.

Magician's have the power to summon. They can summon items and they can summon pets. They are not necessarily defined as the masters of elementals, they are just the masters of _summoning_ elementals.

They have learned a few things here and there to help buff the elementals they have summoned, and they also know enough to force most summoned things back to where they came from, but they don't know how to control an elemental creature that has already found it's way to the material plane without being summoned.

In other words, Magicians only know how to exert control over an elemental by summoning it from its plane of existence. The binding process is part of the summons.

Hope that helps.

You may not agree that things _should_ be this way, but I believe it does describe how things _are_.
