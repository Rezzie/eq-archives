I think the necessary things to keep in the bank for a CR would be a light source of some type (if you don't have night vision) and a Chipped Bone Rod. They aren't that expensive, and have 3 charges of locate corpse on them. If you're ever not sure of where you died, they could be invaluable.
When I was a young ranger, I lost my corpse several times, and it took forever to find it. By the time I was old enough to learn about the chipped bone rod, I'd learned to do a /loc right before I died. I also got pretty good at finding my way around.
Food and water aren't that necessary. Armor and weapons aren't that necessary *if* you're careful, or if you can cast camo.
Look into the chipped bone rod, though. It might come in handy.

For a good number of levels now Ive made sure that I had some CR kit availible in the bank. As you increase in levels this kit becomes a little less important when groupe due to Resses and Cummon Corpse.

However one of the most important lessons I learnt early on is to be very careful in the make up of the CR kit.

Lightweight items are GREAT.. There is nothing more annoying than doing CR only to be encumbered with items afterwards.

No-Drop Items are good as CR Items.. You cant sell them, trade them so rather than destroy them keep them for CR.

Dont go over the top. Look at what the MINIMUM you are going to need is.. There is nothing more annoying then looting corpse, putting your CR kit in a backpack and finding your out of space and have to destroy something.

Remember to put the CR kit back ASAP.. Doing CR the 2nd time, to get your main kit AND your CR kit is not fun....

It depends very much on your race, class and level.

The chipped bone rod is a good thing to have, but really the best possible thing you can have in the bank other than that is money. It takes up no space and you can get just what you need for the particular circumstances of your corpse run.

Apart from cash, the things I always have in my casters' bank vaults are some bat wings and some fish scales. There are too many zones where you aren't going to find these readily in the shops, and you can't rely on hunting or fishing for your own. I had a horrible underwater corpse recovery once where I just couldn't hold my breath long enogh to get it and it was so frustrating to have Enduring Breath memmed and not be able to cast it.

And try getting your body back from the bottom of Crystal Caverns without any batwings in your inventory.

A necro, of course, should always keep some bones in the bank - I know there are a lot of skeletons around, but there are some zones where they are still pretty darn rare. And you feel so much safer doing CR with a hefty darkbone skel watching your back.
