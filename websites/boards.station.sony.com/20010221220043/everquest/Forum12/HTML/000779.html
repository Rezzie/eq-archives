About these lists:

First and foremost, understand that this is only a list of concerns to be brought to the Live Team. This is not a list of promises, and I'm certainly not telling you that any or all of these items will be changed or implemented.

Also, there are three types of items that I don't have listed here. Epic Quests, Magic Resistances in Velious and Mid-level quests. Those three items were mentioned by enough classes that I've pulled them out for separate examination.

All items are currently marked with NONE for status. Obviously we've already started working on some items, you've seen the lists from the patch messages on the Test Server. I will be updating the status for items early next week to reflect those potential changes and with any responses from the team. I just wanted to get these lists out to you fresh.

I want these lists to be final as you see them now, but I know there will need to be changes made. So this list is not final. But if you want something added to or removed from the list, please be convincing about the change. At this point I don't want to tack on too many more items, since the team is already reviewing the original list.

Thanks for the input so far.

Alan


Necromancers:


ITEM: Ignite Bones.

DESCRIPTION: Despite the coolness factor, this spell can't really be used on the big raids because it suddenly pulls the enemy out of range, and by the time you relocate your range, one warrior could conceivably do more damage than the spell is going to do. The best solution is to make the skeleton match the target in body type and size. If the skeleton can’t be sized, then perhaps remove it and find some other cool way to make sure that the spell 'sticks' to the target to prevent chain casting and to make it more than just another DD. Or, give it a longer recast time instead of the ‘sticky’ thing. This would give value to multiple necromancers in a group.

STATUS: None

NOTE: Any change to this spell should also be made to Chill Bones (55).

---

ITEM: Lifetaps.

DESCRIPTION: The number one request is to allow Lifetaps to land on the big monsters. A necromancer uses these spells, at least in part, to fuel there mana transfer spells. Allowing them to land on the big encounters would make them much more useful for doing damage and for transferring mana, and would help them feel more useful in the final encounters on raids.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: DoTs and corpse poofing.

DESCRIPTION: This affects everyone. Hopefully this is now much improved, but I’m not taking it off the list until I’m convinced.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mana transfer.

DESCRIPTION: If the Lich series bug is fixed, then perhaps a 2:1 transfer rate might be a fair change. Reducing the effectiveness of the hit point regeneration for necromancers will also lower their ability to transfer mana. Necromancers would like to see this ratio raised in any case.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Infusion.

DESCRIPTION: Make infusion targetable. The necro might still see it as a 'worthless spell', but if another caster wants to sacrifice their experience for 1000 mana, then might actually cast this spell. Otherwise, those Essence Emerald’s will only be used for raising the dead.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Defoliation.

DESCRIPTION: There seem to be plenty of plants that this just doesn't work on. Certainly this spell needs to be limited in its use because it is overly efficient against one monster type. But it might be worthwhile to evaluate the things that are not considered plants that maybe should be.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Track Corpse.

DESCRIPTION: Broken spell. Is it going to stay that way?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Lich Series.

This series of spells is broken. It is more powerful than it should be. It takes the necromancer's hit points and turns them into mana. It was calculated at a certain ratio to be 'in balance'. Unfortunately, these spells were adversely affected by the reduction of damage to spells used by players on players. This also reduced the hit points taken from the necromancer by Lich spells, so that the ratio of mana gained for hit points lost is nearly 1 for 1 (instead of about 3 for 2). This is a bug that was left alone because it would be a 'nerf'. In conjunction with a serious look at the entire class and the balance of all the classes, this series needs to be looked into and a decision should be made.

Other ideas for this line would be to allow it more stacking (with Clarity and Heal over Time spells).

There is also some question as to why there are two Lich spells at level 60.

STATUS: None

NOTE: Series includes: Arch Lich (60), Demi Lich (60), Lich (49), Call of Bones (34), Allure of Death (20).

---

ITEM: Trucidation.

DESCRIPTION: This is the 1100 point life tap that requires an essence emerald and has a 10 minute recast. The next weakest lifetap does about 750 points. The suggestion here is that this spell be unresistable and do more damage. The high cost of the reagent and the long recast time, are very limiting factors for the spell, even if it were unresistable. It also might allow the necromancer a lot more leeway against the big opponents, doing some damage and also restocking their hit points for more mana distribution.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Pets and Fear Immunity.

DESCRIPTION: It makes some sense that undead pets would be immune to fear. Again, this would help the necromancer do more at the end of a raid. Perhaps only the highest level undead pets would be immune to fear.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Stacking Bug with Splurt.

DESCRIPTION: Heart Flutter, Asystole, or Cessation will stick if Splurt is cast first, but Splurt will not take hold if they are cast first. Seems like a bug.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Summon Companion.

DESCRIPTION: Giving this to magicians requires consideration about giving it to necromancers (and all other pet classes).

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Screaming Terror.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that Screaming Terror is not overwritten by enchanter mesmerization spells. It also seems to work on NPCs that are unaffected by those same enchanter spells, making it a bit more powerful than enchanter mez. Also, it appears that it was intended to be a fear spell that also wiped the hate list, but it wasn't technically possible to make it work that way when the spell was first introduced. If this is so, perhaps now it is possible to make it (or some new spell) work that way.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Sedulous Subversion.

DESCRIPTION: The ratio of mana transferred with this spell seems to be worse than the previous spell in the line, Covetous Subversion. That seems like an error.

STATUS: None

---

STATUS: Coffin Availability.

DESCRIPTION: There appear to be no regular coffins available in Necromancer/Shadow Knight guilds other than Neriak. They should probably be available elsewhere
STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Spell Availability.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that there are no 49th level necromancer spells available on vendors in Paineel.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Shadowbound.

DESCRIPTION: The previous spell in this line stacks with regen buffs and Skin Like Nature, but Shadowbound does not. This seems like a bug.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Component Usage for Summon Corpse.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that if you duck during a casting of this spell you can prevent the component from being used for a failed casting, otherwise it is used up. If the component is supposed to be used on a failed casting, then it should be used up, regardless of the character's movements. If not (and this seems to be the proper course of action), then the component should be used only upon successful casting.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Spell Icons.

DESCRIPTION: Most of the icons for necromancer spells are the same black skull on a red background. It sure would be nice if there were some new icons to help distinguish one spell gem from another.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: 59th Level Pet.

DESCRIPTION: Many necromancers think that this pet is not much stronger (if at all) than the 56th level pet, and the cost of use is higher, so most just use the 56th level pet.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Dead Man Walking.

DESCRIPTION: Necromancers would like a spell that has all the benefits of Dead Man Floating except the floating part so that they can use it in places where levitation does not work.
STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Rare Spells.

DESCRIPTION: Chill Bones, Devouring Darkness, and Quivering Veil of Sarn seem to be very rare, perhaps too rare.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Cannibalize and Mod Rods.

DESCRIPTION: These two items should probably be checked to make certain that they are not also affected by the PvP damage reduction change.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Group Dead Man Floating.

DESCRIPTION: This is a spell that seems to be universally wanted by necromancers.

STATUS: N/A – This is an idea for future use, no action is expected on this item.

---

ITEM: Temporary Animation.

DESCRIPTION: A new spell idea. It would return a character to life for two minutes, at which time they will take lethal damage and die (again).

STATUS: N/A – This is an idea for future use, no action is expected on this item.

---

ITEM: Possession.

DESCRIPTION: An idea for a new spell that would allow the necromancer to possess his pet and play him like a character, while his own body crumples to the ground and remains helpless.

STATUS: N/A – This is an idea for future use, no action is expected on this item.
