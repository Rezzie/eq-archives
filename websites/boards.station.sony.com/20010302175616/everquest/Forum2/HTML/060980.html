Hi. I'm Melara, and I'm a Gnome.

I want to bring up some problems dealing with Gnomes.

One problem as a Gnomish Necromancer I have found: There is no book of dark bindings in Ak'anon. As far as I know there is a "research" book for everyone that needs it, in every other guild around the world, in every city. I think this was just an accident. Please... PLEASE put the Book of Dark bindings in Ak'anon. The closest place you can get one is Freeport.

Next is Quests. Most of our low level quests are very, very, disappointing. In fact most people don't even know they exist they are so bad. Can we please have an improvement in this area?
We only really have one good quest: Red V. The quest *might* result in a Bull Smasher (very nice newbie weapon), however it is *very* hard to get because getting the reward is random.

Also there is not much story to the Gnomes. Why are the Kobold's in Steamfont? Why are the Mino's in the cave? Why is the king a clockwork? Inquiring minds want to know.

Why in the Gnomish Newbie zone is there a level 35ish spawn that is aggressive to all Gnomes? (The Mino Hero.) Hunting in the Mino caves is pretty useless because a Lord will spawn (in his late 20's I think), and so will a Mino Hero.

I realize that these are for quests, but why have such low level mino's there for lower level people to hunt, but have such high level Mobs spawn that wipe out entire parties in seconds?

I have a suggestion to fix a few problems:

Make the Mino caves it's own zone, and expand it. Have quests from Ak'anon involved the Mino Caves for the low and mid levels. Have the caves be aimed at levels 10-30. This would alleviate some of the over crowding Crushbone sees, as it is the only low level dungeon on Faydwer.

Just some suggestions/thoughts.

Just think, without gnomes there would be no gnome skin leggings, gloves, etc, and it would be much harder to produce gnomeskin footballs and other gnomish by products that we all enjoy. I hear it takes about ten gnomes to make a single pair of gloves, but we must ask ourselves reasonably: Is that such a high price to pay for a successful raid against the dragon Trakannon, or that we might have a better chance of resisting the next poison based spell cast on us? I would say not. If anything, I believe gnomes should become a tailoring ingredient, and if I see one scurrying around my hometown of of Felwithe I should have the option of clicking on him to pick him up, placing him in a tailoring kit, and combining him with a pattern to make gnome skin armor, with a failure rate of no more than 60 % and no less than 30 % with a skill of 100. Icons exist that would suit this need admirably, and the programming surely would not be that challenging. Whether or not the gnome was returned upon failure is trivial, as there are so many of the little things running around that it would be no large feat to just grab another.


But I want to make something clear: these ideas and thoughtful consideration of gnomekind are brought about by a very sincere respect and understanding of the gnomish race. I think, contrary to the title of this post, most people share my respect and understanding. The next time you see a monk in gnomeskin saying "Look, I'm a ninja!" or druid going incognito in black, just sit back as a gnome, relax and think: "Hey, I am special!"
