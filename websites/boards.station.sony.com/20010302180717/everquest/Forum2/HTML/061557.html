An excellent idea, jen_starling and a great example -Gonodil-. The 3 top crafting skills are very unbalanced in three dimensions -- internally, between other tradeskills and between mob drops.

1) Internal inconsistencies. There are inconsistencies in all crafts. Jewelry makes noting sellable in electrum -- the cost differences between electrum and gold make it unwise to purchase electrum items. Smithing makes nothing usable until you get over 100 skill. Tailoring has the 115 gap, where if you aren't a wood elf/halfling, you have no way to progress.

Solution -- fill in the skills for these with items that are sellable. Lower difficulty of weapons and make better non-magical weapons under 100. Change the Electrum jewelry forms to make these different from the Gold and Silver versions. Then some of these items could sell. (e.g., change Electrum Jasper from 2 Wis earrings to 2 Wis bracers). Add robes (from Puma, Mist Wolf, other unused skins), ruksacks (8 slot, 50% weight reduction), curiboli armor (plate look, but tan) and duffel bags (10 slot, 60%) crafting for all races.

2) Between other tradeskills. Using the example provided by -Gonodil-, level the component costs among all three crafts to be roughly equivilent at the same skill level. If an Electrum Jasper trivials at 92 and costs about 3.5 pp per attempt, give smithing and tailoring equivilent costs for items that trivial at 92.

3) Between Mob Drops. The really unfortunate thing about tradeskills is that they make items that are generally inferior to those dropped from mobs. The only craft that even has a chance is jewelry, and then only in specialized or very high ticket items. Even the specialized cultural armor is not good enough to be desirable.

Solution? Reduce the frequency of usable items dropped by mobs and increase the frequency of components dropped by mobs. Would it really hurt the game if no more Ghoulbanes dropped? Or Axes of the Iron back?

Just some random ideas.

YET MORE REASONS TO BALANCE TRADESKILLS

I think tradeskills need to be balanced and improved for a number of reasons. When I say balanced and improved, I mean, make the hassle equal to the reward. This has been partially achieved with tailoring. The high end tailored items are great now. And they should be! Tailoring is a major, major headache to master, much less grandmaster. I thank Tunare every day I log in that I have a WE druid, heaven forbid being a half elf! Jewelry is horribly expensive to master. The end products are nice; HOWEVER, they shouldn't be so nice when all that is required is platinum and a little luck for the really high end items...as opposed to smithing. As high end gems for cultural armor can only be obtained through planes raids and deep level high end dungeon raids, the stats conferred upon the items they make should be much nicer and should create a similar demand from players as does high end jewel-crafted items. In addition, jewelers are able to profit earlier in their careers and can therefore blow 2-5K for high end gems. Smiths cannot afford this.
For those craftsfolk who must search high and low for components, a thought on stats and bonuses.
Perhaps the mobs from which components drop should help determine the stats of an item. For mithril cultural, essence of moonlight is required. Most items from MM come with a plus 5 stat bonus - HBC, nightshade wreath, sac dagger, etc. Mithril also requires BDs. These are dropped in places where armor has multiple stats, great AC, etc.. So, in order to be competitive, the stats should be increased.
Let the hassle factor and risk and loss of time/money doing other things equal the reward. This has been applied elsewhere, surely it can be applied here.
NOW ...
Having defined improved...reasons they should be improved.
1. Slow down farming. Have you ever heard a reference to static spawns in a good sci-fi fantasy novel or other RPG? Would you have read The Hobbit if he continually killed Golum (sp?) over and over and over for the ring? The people who consistantly put out a product are trained and skilled in it; for this reason they can make it and sell it and the economy goes on. You can't kill the same cow over and over to make meat.
2. Slow the camping. See number 1. and add... I remember when EQ first went live. It was much funner to walk into a dungeon just to see how far you could go in without you and your party getting ganked. Now you don't "do" a dungeon, especially on a server like TM that's been around since day 1. You instead hear, "CAMP CHECK!" and hope there's a slot open somewhere. In fact, LG is so camped, it's pretty much safe to walk around on a weekend, it's so camped. To reiterate, the only place you should be able to consistantly buy a product is from a craftsman who has trained and practiced in that skill.
3. Curb the perfectly insane, INSANE, economy. About a year ago, I bought gator legs for my druid for 500 PP. A couple of days ago, I wanted to buy another pair. 2500 PP!! The farming and camping are ruining the game for people just starting. I wouldn't buy it on general principle even if I HAD the cash! The economy right now is such that only high-levels twinks can buy decent gear. They've pushed the demand up well beyond the supply and can afford to pay whatever they desire. Poor, poor noobs - really! Meanwhile, the poor noob wanting to camp a pair can't get to it because of the farmers. If player made items were improved to be commisurate with the level of difficulty and craftsfolk remained sensible about pricing, the economy might stabilize a bit, at least to the extent that newer players will continue to play.
4. Realism. See 1 and 2. (granted this is a completely fictional world, comprised of pixels..)
5. Player satisfaction and enjoyment. Some people like to hack n' slash. Some people like to create things. Some people like a little of both. Some days you don't have time to hit Seb or Chardock or even Unrest. It would be nice for these skills to be worthwhile on those kind of days. If EQ is to be a hack and slash game, why put in trade skills at all? Then people wouldn't have this love/hate relationship. Put it at face value. You hack and slash or you find another game, we just threw in some tradeskills as an afterthought so we could bulk up the manual. Sorry, I'm ranting aren't I? There have been improvements, I can't deny that. Cheers to the folks bringing them about!
Anyway, there's my 2 CP and my reasons for it. Oh, and it would be nice for people to at least have these things acknowledged. Especially when they are paying for a service.
