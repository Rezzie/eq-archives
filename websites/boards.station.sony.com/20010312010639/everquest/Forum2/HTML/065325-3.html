For me, the problem with gems is the immersion factor. I am "immersed" in the world of Norrath when I play. I don't see this as a game. There are no levels, no end bosses, no real linear storyline. I believe the world I am in, and that world has rules. And I think that sticking a downtime game that doesn't fit in this world is a mistake. Gems is a video game through and through. There is no way to hide that fact. Gems dropping from the top to the bottom to be collected in groups of three to disappear. I hate to use the word Tetris, cause we all know what it is, but it's the truth. There is nothing about it that can even remotely be considered "fantsay".
I understand that this may be a stepping stone in the evolution of mini-games in the world of Norrath. But why did this step in the process have to be exposed to the masses? Why not say, "It works, now how do we implement a game that is feasible, not distracting from the actual gameplay going on and makes sense?" Makes sense! When I heard that a game called /gems was going to be in the next patch, I had visions of glorious downtime game play. I actually thought of Arcomage from the M&M series. Now that was a minigame that fit in the context of a fantasy setting. That was a game that kept me interested, even in a game that had NO downtime to begin with!
Please save Norrath from being corrupted by this non-fantasy trash and dispose of it in the next patch. I will be here waiting for an actually genuine downtime supplement in the mean time. Since I doubt that the actual issue of downtime reduction will be simply discarded. Like this post probably will.
O.k., I'm ready to get back to NK. Cya in the world.
