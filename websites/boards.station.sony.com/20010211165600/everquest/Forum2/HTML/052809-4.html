Some ideas:

1) A warrior is supposed to be the master of weapons. Yet SK's, Rangers and Paladins all have the same weapons skills and caps as warriors. In my opinion if a warrior and an SK (for example) were wielding the same weapon, the advantage should be to the warrior, yet it is obviously not. I would suggest that every 10 levels (15) or so, a warrior receives either a plus to damage (+1) or a minus to delay (-2 I would suggest) of a weapon he is using. Thus, in the hands of a 15th level warrior, a 6/28 longsword would become 7/28 or 6/26. This change is not major but would differentiate the warrior from the other hybrids, make them slightly more desirable in combat, but preserve their place in the overall damage-dealing rankings. Monks would still outdamage the warrior overall, but warriors would still be the masters of armed combat.

2) Allow Berserk to be a selected ability much like Lay Hands or Harm Touch. I would put it on the same timer as these buttons. The warrior would get increased Str and Attack, decreased defense and the ability to land Crippling Blows for a limited amount of time. This would at least allow the warrior something to think about during combat. Every other class has a special ability that they must tactically use. The warrior has nothing.

3) Add some new skill between the ultimate dead zone of levels 15-25. Some ideas: (A) Staunch Wound -- an ability that allows the warrior to immediately heal the last blow landed (B) Iron Skin -- an ability that gives the warrior a minor defense buff (C) Aimed Blow (my favorite) -- allows the warrior to land a critical hit but lose all attacks for the next round (timing for warriors would become crucial and give them another tactical choice)

Basically, in my opinion, the warrior is fairly balanced class, but misses out on those abilities that make him or her stand out. All other classes must make tactical decisions, whereas the warrior, who should be the ultimate tactician, must make none. Crits and Crips are nice, but are completely random. Other classes get the ability to dual wield (rangers) and double attack (melee hybrids, monks). Give the warrior something to prove that he is the master of combat.

These really aren't balance issues. Being the benchmark and the best tank by default, I'd say balance really isn't a very big issue for us. However there are a few issues that should be looked at nevertheless.

>>TAUNT<<
Taunt needs a bit of tweaking. As it stands now with the new 50+ taunting rules taunt still does not work at ALL on yellow or red mobs and it's just a little too weak with high blues. Make taunt a little more reliable on blues and have at least some effect on yellows and reds

>>SMALL WARRIORS<<
Give small warriors a meaningful combat advantage besides just being small. Agility just doesn't cut it, as we've learned that agility is pretty much meaningless after you pass 75.

>>STAMINA ITEMS<<
Make more items with good ac and stamina or perhaps a piece of "cobalt-ish" warrior armor that casts a mild stamina buff. It is very easy to boost strength without sacrificing ac with the current items out there. On the other hand items with lots of stamina and good ac are somewhat rare.

>>EPIC QUEST<<
Perhaps revisit the epic difficulty vs reward. The difficulty should be toned down a bit or the epic itself improved.

>>DISCIPLINES<<
Some of the disciplines are very poor and should be examined. Charge, Evasive, Precise, and Aggresive all come to mind in particular. I believe some people have posted details.

>>TWO-HANDED<<
There is a need for better =>warrior only<= two-handed weapons. Why do I say this? The vast majority of the good two-handed weapons are shared by all the heavy plate classes (war/pal/sk). However, they are balanced in such a way that a paladin or shadowknight cannnot trivialize the advantage of dual wielding. This makes perfectly good sense. However, the side effect is that in the 50+ game, warriors have no reason to use two-handed weapons. By putting in warrior only two handed weapons that can compete with dual-wielding damage, 50+ warriors can have a little more freedom with their weapon choices.

>>IKSARS IN PLATE?<<
Iksar warriors using Plane of Growth warrior armor is very iffy. One of the tradeoffs iksars traditionally make is they lose plate armor but have a natural AC bonus. Now pre velious, the gap arguably put iksar warriors at a disadvantage, but Plane of Growth armor gives them a huge advantage over all other warrior races and violates their "no-plate" restriction. My reccomendation is that there should be better non-plate armor available for iksar warriors than exists currently, but they shouldn't be able to wear identical top-tier armor like the softskins do.

>>INDO, COBALT, and VELIOUS QUEST ARMOR<<
The Indo drop rate is rather poor overall. Yes I know it's all random and it could be merely a streak of bad luck, but after dozens of Hate raids, a pattern does emerge. The cobalt drop rate overall is a little low, specifically the bracers and helm are obscenely rare. Furthermore, the Velious quest armors are ridiculously difficult to get if you don't feel like trashing your faction in PoG. That just serves to punish good-race roleplayers and reward pure powergamers. Velious quest armor should be about on par with PoG armor, both in difficulty and quality.
