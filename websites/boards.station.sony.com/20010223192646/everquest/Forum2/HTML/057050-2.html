Chickenlegs.. you are mistaken regarding the new exp system.

______________________________________________

Monk kills a mob worth 100 exp when solo.. monk gets 120 exp for it.

Pure caster kills a mob worth 100 exp when solo.. caster gets 110 exp for it.

Druid kills a mob worth 100 exp when solo.. druid gets 100 exp for it.

Warrior kills a mob worth 100 exp when solo.. druid gets 100 exp for it.

>>> Only the warrior has an exp bonus.. the rest are getting exp mutliplied by their exp penalties and thus level at the same speed for the same amount of mobs
_________________________________________________


Previously a group would get a 2% bonues per person. Now the bonus is like 2%-6%-10%-14%-20%.


Previously group exp was split by the amount each person had.This meant that if you grouped with a person with a bigger race/class exp penalty.. their share per kill would be bigger than yours.

Now group exp is divided evenly per person.. the group bonus exp is applied and then the persons class based penalty is multiplied by the amount they receive. This has the effect of removing exp hogging by costly classes and making everyone in the group level more quickly.


Now a monk killing a mob worth 600 exp in a full group would get a share of 100.. they would then get 20% more for their group bonus and 20% more for their class requirement.

If a player dies they lose (in normal levels) 9% of their exp for the last level multiplied by their exp requirement (as those with exp requirements will have higher totals).

____________________________________________

The only person that really win are rogues and warriors as they get 100 exp points per 100 exp monste even though they need 90% of the base class exp per level for each of their levels.


______________________________________

Bottom line is:

A Monk needs 1.2x base class exp per level.. but gets 1.2 x the exp per kill (plus a bonus if grouped).

Frenzy, you're wrong about monk fists because you're not factoring in the primary hand level based damage bonus. There's more to damage output than weapon ratio alone - because there is a flat numeric bonus (based on your level) added to the damage of each swing (which does not vary with weapon delay or base damage or damage ratio), faster weapons apply this bonus damage more times. For instance, at level 50 the damage bonus is 8, so roughly I believe monk hands become 22/30, wu sticks 16/18 - an immense difference - even plain old fighting batons perform significantly better at 16/20.
