I know the topic of camping has come up time and time again by people wondering why is there just so much of it, so I thought I'd post this, maybe it will kind of illustrate why its so prevalent in the game.

Right now, there are two opposing forces in the game:

Force A) Players who want exp and items, and they want them NOW!

Force B) Verant who wants items and exp to enter the game at a certain rate.


To illistrate this, lets look at one item in particular that everyone is familiar with:

Jboots

I don't recall where, but someone familiar with The Vision stated that Jboots should only enter the game like once every 16 hours or so. I don't recall the exact number, but it was something like that.

Used to, this was accomplished by putting the boots on drelzna in Najena, but horror stories of the camping, kill stealing and constant petitions finally compelled Verant to move the Jboots to be a quest item.

Despite it being a quest item though, the original delay, of Jboots entering the game every 16 hours or so, was still the goal.

Now this is where the laziness factor comes in:

Instead of designing a quest that takes players to 8 or 9 different places running various errands, doing various tasks that would add up to 16 to 20 hours of game play time, Verant instead decides to put all of that time into the spawn of a single monster, namely, the Ancient Cyclops.

In other words, instead of making an elaborate quest to take up 16 - 20 hours, someone just said, "Yeah, put that guy in the desert and in OOT, and set his timer to be about 16 hours or so, and that'll take care of the time sink".

So anyways, its that kind of issue thats really at the heart of camping, and its not likely to go anywhere. THe best option is to either a) buy the items instead of camp them or b) just don't worry about camping and do other things.

Items that are given via quest enter the game whenever the quest giver is handed the required items.

To date the method used to limit supply was described by abashi above: The longest part of the quest determines the rate the items enters the game.

There is another way.

Say the Jboot quest was changed so that, on average, each of the components (other than the cash) could be gotten within one hour of "camping" the AC or shadow men. So how do you stop one pair from entering the game per hour?

Make the quest NPC rare. He's only around once every 16 hours.

So, you're thinking, now you have to camp the NPC rather than the items? What does that solve?

Modify that situation like this and you get a different scenario: What if the npc's appearance was predictable? What if Hasten told you that he'd appear each day at 12:00 noon?

So now you just all gather at 12 and give him the items right? Nope. He despawns after getting the items. One per day. No camping, the supply is limited.

People would be more understanding of this situation. They spent all of thier time gathering the components and now just have to check back at the right time every so often. They know that SOMEDAY they'll get the item, its just a matter of catching him first one of these days.

It is impossible to "do a dungeon" in EQ because the healers cannot regen mana fast enough while moving to keep the tanks healed. Even with clarity and manasong this is the case. No one regens anything wihtout sitting down, and healers have to med every possible chance to keep everyone alive.

That being the case there is only one tactic: camping. Pull a manageable number of mobs to the group to allow the healers to med as much as possible. This condition holds true for the solo classes such as necros as well.

Since this is the case, is makes sense to camp a spot with monetary returns as well as exp returns. Bingo, you are camping for items.

This is exactly how the game was designed to be played.
