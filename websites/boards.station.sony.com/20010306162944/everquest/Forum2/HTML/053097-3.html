I've actually retired from EQ for a month now, but this issue brought me back, long enough to make this post.

I've had a Donal's BP since well before the first nerf, and I have to admit it was overpowered. However, seeing as a nerf is inevitable, here's a thought on the way it should be nerfed.

Back in Fall 2000 when I was level 57, a level 57 monk and I duo'd the efreeti in Sol B. That's right, DUO. Certain mobs, like imps in Sol B and most things in Karnor's Castle, are designed to be very easily tauntable as long as people are standing up. Sit down, however, and they turn on you in a heartbeat.

What Donal's BP did for me when I was duo'ing in Sol B and Karnor's is remove the need for me to sit. I used no mana (and if I did use mana in Sol B I could manastone/click BP it back) and as a result the monk could tank 3 or 4 imps (or dogs/skele's in Karnor's) indefinitely.

Now, let me make one thing clear. The monk and I weren't huddled in the efreeti room camping the efreeti for boots and not pulling spawns (like many raids of 2 or 3 groups do when they go up there). We pulled ALL (read: every last one of) the imps. Imagine, 2 people getting duo exp for ~18 spawns every 27-30 minutes.

We did this whenever the efreeti was left uncamped for a period of 2 weeks. We yielded the efreeti spawn itself whenever another group wanted to camp it, and would simply kill all the other imp spawns in the area. Net result, 3 levels in 2 real weeks (and this includes not starting til 7-8PM each day due to work).

Was the BP overpowered then? Sure it was. In fact, as we were duoing the efreeti we had conversations with GM's and guides discussing the very problem at hand as they came to watch us.

Keep in mind however, the system we had worked would only work in a few zones. The imps in Sol B and spawns in Karnor's were easy enough to taunt that they could tank 3 or 4 and the mobs would not switch to me while I chain clicked the complete heal.

Attempting the same thing in Chardok, The Hole, and other dungeons resulted in the monk and I promptly getting getting our rear ends beaten into the ground as mobs would switch and interrupt the BP effect repeatedly.

Extending it to 30 seconds made things like duo'ing the efreeti risky, and in a way fixed the overpowering that Verant was afraid of. But, since the BP is usable from level 46 onwards, there are many situations at the lower end (and many big mobs at the higher end) where it needed to be addressed.

I'm sure we were not the only people using the BP to its full potential. We may not even have been the ones to first catch the eye of the GM's. I'm sure there had to be much more than 2 people duoing the efreeti for the nerf to be this drastic. The point is, the overpowering nature of the BP when seen first hand is obvious.

Now, Absor, as far as fixing the BP is concerned. The compromise, in my view, could go something like this.

The BP, before this nerf, was hands down the best item for a cleric (as far as we know, in any case). It drops off of Trakanon. Therefore, it has a right to hold such a place. So, in order to maintain as much as a game balance as possible, the replacement effect needs to keep the BP in the same place on the totem pole, so to speak. It's unfair to clerics who have Donal's BP who decided not to pursue the new Velious BP's to be suddenly penalized into not having a good BP.

Nerfing it to 7.5 minutes is unreasonably long. Even if you only use it as a downtime reducer between fights, many fights only last a signifigiantly shorter amount of time.

At the very least, the buff icon should fade in 5 minutes. You can debate the pro's and con's of 5 minutes versus 7.5 all you want, but the truth is until the change goes live you can't tell if 7.5 or 5 minutes is balanced or not. Therefore, why not give the players a break. If for some odd and unexplained reason 5 minutes is so short it is abused again (although I don't see how), pushing it back to 7.5 could be considered.

You're already ripping the guts out of this item, at least cut us a break in this aspect.

Secondly, the BP's effect should in some way be good in such a way that clerics will desire it even if it can only be cast on a person once every 5 minutes.

Already, the design of the nerf includes a little of this. The buff icon will benefit the castee in some way.

Let's be honest here, currently, that effect is a cantrip. For all intents and purposes, it's useless.

Now, as I asked myself if there really was a useful buff that was in line with a cleric's abilities. If it could be made so that the added buff effect worked in harmony with the existing BP effect of complete heal, so much the better.

The result of my pondering is this idea: Have the BP buff icon overwrite res effects. Yes, I know res effects are there for a reason. There's a reason warriors cannot cast invis as well. However, high end items are supposed to remove some of these encumberances. Cobalt legs give the warriors the ability to cast invis, why not give Cleric BP's the ability to remove res effects? At least with the Cleric BP the effect would be in line with the class.

What is the net result? The BP is used as a downtime reducer. The fact that it removes res effects in addition to this allows it to keep it's place as a Good(tm) item. Therefore, it makes the item good enough where it retains it can at least (arguably) retain its place on the totem pole without being overwhelmingly overpowered.

Even if that works you're leaving no margin for error. Interrupts, extra mobs, lag spikes and so forth. The bp heal is a like an emergency heal it is way too risky to rely on as a normal heal because it is so slow and due to the likelyhood of interrupt.

AS for uber mobs the overower claim is even more absurd because every uber mob I have ever faced has some sort of AE or flurry or rampage. All of these have great chances of interrupting a caster. Not to mention the mobs ability to summon which can further impede a 30 second cheal.

The only time this would be overly effective are fight which last longer than say 5 minutes i.e the brownie something or other in PoG which take 35-45 min to kill.

However this doesn't meant that there aren't rewards to using the bp in both epic and non-epic fights. They do help before during and after the fights. Their impact is vital but not pronounced.

I won't delve into specifics simply I don't want to divulge the guild tactics regarding spefic velious mobs but I will vehemtly underscore the fact that Bp's are not used as primary healers but rather auxiliary heals.
