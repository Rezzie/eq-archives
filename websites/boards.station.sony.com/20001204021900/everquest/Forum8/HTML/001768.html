Yep, HQ wolf drops a bit rarely.

Minor issues: these recipies actually look usable for training. Before, Ruined pelts trivial'd out at 26, you are making them trivial out at 50 (in large groups). So, it wouldn't stop the flood of leather backings - it would help it, 'cause newbies would practice using their ruined pelts, get more LQ pelts (even at a low failure rate), and skill up faster. Not that I think that is a bad idea. I would love there to be a flood of backings!

The asymmetry of excluding a MQ to HQ Bear pelt recipie also worries me. The MQ to HQ recipies in wolf and cat _exclude_ the addition of a highly useful (on the level of HQ Bear) recipie for the other pelt times. Asymmetries are often signs of flawed designes.

Here is an example of the flaw:

Lets say Yaka wanted to add a new recipie, Halfling Cultural Boots of the Wolf from HQ wolf that gives a 5% speed up proc with unlimited charges, requiring a 2 SoW potions as a curing agent.

See what I mean? The above recipie would make HQ wolf pelts as much in demand as HQ bear, well, on the same order of demand. So, in order so save the Boot market, would we remove the MQ -> HQ recipie on Wolf?

I would think a better choice, if you wanted Reinforced to be easier to make, is to bump the pelt quality required for the existing armors down by one. So, Studded would require LQ, and Reinforced MQ, leaving the HQ pelts for neato stuff. And getting rid of the LQ 'gap' in armor manufacturing.

Really, Reinforced armor does not deserve the rarity that the game currently gives it.

The above might require (ik) trivial level munging, or not - after all, while Reinforced is _currently_ pretty much the top of the tailoring heap, when recipies go to 200 it will become as low on the totem pole as banded gorgets are currently.

And, unlike your suggestion, it doesn't ruin the rarity of HQ pelts of the other two types!

It does make gaining skill in Tailoring easier (MQ to 108), but if it was introduced with a bunch of other improvements, I'm sure the existing GrandMasters would be to busy being excited over the new stuff to be too disappointed at it being easier to skill up now (which won't be the case, due to increased pelt demand that would be caused by any upgrade).
