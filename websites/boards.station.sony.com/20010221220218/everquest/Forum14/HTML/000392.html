About these lists:

First and foremost, understand that this is only a list of concerns to be brought to the Live Team. This is not a list of promises, and I'm certainly not telling you that any or all of these items will be changed or implemented.

Also, there are three types of items that I don't have listed here. Epic Quests, Magic Resistances in Velious and Mid-level quests. Those three items were mentioned by enough classes that I've pulled them out for separate examination.

All items are currently marked with NONE for status. Obviously we've already started working on some items, you've seen the lists from the patch messages on the Test Server. I will be updating the status for items early next week to reflect those potential changes and with any responses from the team. I just wanted to get these lists out to you fresh.

I want these lists to be final as you see them now, but I know there will need to be changes made. So this list is not final. But if you want something added to or removed from the list, please be convincing about the change. At this point I don't want to tack on too many more items, since the team is already reviewing the original list.

Thanks for the input so far.

Alan

Clerics:

ITEM: Standing Meditation.

DESCRIPTION: Either a spell or a 'discipline' that would allow clerics to gain sitting meditation rates while standing. This would allow a cleric to wander around and perhaps fight (see other items on Defense Skills and Bash) rather than meditating. One way to do this would be to make is a spell or ability that they can use that would be interuptable and would turn off when they sit down. The drawback is that if clerics have this 'blessing from god' ability a lot of other classes will want it too.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Defense skills.

DESCRIPTION: Higher dodge and defense skills. This, in conjunction with giving the cleric bash, might allow them to get into the fight more often. If so, it seems more appropriate to raise parry than dodge.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Bash.

DESCRIPTION: Combined with somewhat higher defensive skills, this might allow a cleric to jump into the fight more often.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Unswerving Hammer of Faith.

DESCRIPTION: Perhaps this spell should be re-evaluated as if it was a DoT. But, as with the Wizard flaming sword pet, perhaps this can be made more interesting and useful DoT by making the hammer immune to AoE spells and riposte. This way it will only die if it gets up on the hate list. Used this way, it would allow the cleric to 'do something' other than healing…

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Stuns.

DESCRIPTION: Stun spells, like charms and mesmerization spells, become useless on certain powerful creatures. Perhaps stun spells could be made at least somewhat effective where they are currently useless, giving clerics (and a few other classes) something more to do in those battles. The 58th level spell, Enforced Reverence, might be a good candidate for a ‘lure’ level of resistance on a stun spell. Changes to the general Magic Resistance in Velious item might remove the need for this item.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Bind Wounds.

DESCRIPTION: As the primary healer, it seems that they have a less than maximum cap on their Bind Wounds skill. It seems logical that, if anyone, Clerics would have the maximum cap.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Death Pact / Divine Intervention.

DESCRIPTION: These spells are expensive to cast (5 or more plat). These spells work too infrequently and don't have a large enough effect when they do work (they should heal more hit points). In general the agreement seems to be that a percentage heal from these spells (25/50), and a much higher chance of it actually going off is a good idea. These spells should at least be re-evaluate, since it seems that they are very rarely used, and that's a shame for such an interesting spell set.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Healing Over Time.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that the healing over time spells do not stack with poisons or diseases (one player also noted that Celestial Healing does not stack with Regrowth). This would seem to be the kind of things that these spells are designed to work against. Is this intentional? Also, if the shadow knight epic weapon proc (Soul Consumption) is on a target, the healing over time spells won’t stick. But if the healing over time spells are on, Soul Consumption will stick. This seems like a bug.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Aegolism.

DESCRIPTION: This spell might be too expensive (10pp) to cast. It basically seems to do what it was intended to do, save time and spell slots. But the players think the mana cost is too high.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Symbol of Naltron.

DESCRIPTION: Again, the feeling is that it is too expensive. Perhaps moving the reagent for this spell to something that costs about half as much (5pp), or increasing it's duration would ease the monetary burden. We should also look at the fizzle rate on this spell, it seems too high.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Shield of Words.

DESCRIPTION: The complaint here seems to be that there is a double mana cost for a 6 point armor class increase from the previous spell. This exactly follows the pattern of the spell line, though, and may not need to be changed. Though perhaps it would be more appropriate to offer a larger armor class increase considering that the level increase, though the same, represents a much harder level range.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mark of Karn.

DESCRIPTION: Perhaps a slight increase in effectiveness is in order.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Divine Light.

DESCRIPTION: One of those spells that fizzles too much. All spells that fizzle too much should be looked at.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Heroism/Heroic Bond.

DESCRIPTION: It appears that Heroic Bond, the group version of Heroism, won't overwrite itself or Heroism. It probably should. As it is, it creates problems when people join a group late, or go LD. The cleric can't just renew the whole group at that time with the group spell. This might not be as much of an issue if changes are made to allow players to click off beneficial spells.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Fortitude.

DESCRIPTION: This spell gives lesser buffs than its predecessor, in exchange for a longer duration. Shouldn't it at least give the same or greater buffs as well as the longer duration?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Hammer of Striking and Hammer of Requital.

DESCRIPTION: More spells that never see use. Players would like to see these improved somewhat, stat-wise. They have suggested giving them some sort of proc, perhaps a proc versus undead isn't too bad of an idea. But the concern is that just the weapons themselves are pretty useless. At the level the cleric gets these spells, he is never going to fight on a corpse run, he's just not good enough even to take out greens. So these spells see no use.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Abolish Disease.

DESCRIPTION: They want the 49th level shaman spell. The question here is, is the curing of disease the exclusive area of the Shaman?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Invisibility (personal only).

DESCRIPTION: A cleric finds himself running to do resurrections all over the place. This would be a nice addition that would make that easier.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Self-only Gate to Temple.

DESCRIPTION: It might be too late for such a big idea, but here's the concept. Set one temple up as the temple for each god. Make a gate spell that takes the cleric to the temple of his god (might be one spell, might be a spell for each temple that you can only memorize or pick up if you worship the god of that temple). It's a time honored idea from D&D. And it adds a little mobility to the class (reducing downtime). These could even be the object of another quest.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Lull Spells.

DESCRIPTION: Players feel that these are resisted too much (or, alternately, cause too much aggro). Since these spells are used to break spawns and deal with things that will usually cause characters to run screaming to the zone, a high resist rate is considered problematic, so the spells are generally not used, and other targets are sought by the players.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Holy Armor / Spirit Armor / Guard / Armor of Faith / Shield of Words / Bulwark of Faith line of armor spells

DESCRIPTION: This actually came up as part of the Magician Talks. But it seems that this spell can not be clicked off by the recipient. This causes trouble for the magician because it also overwrites his armor spells (with lower AC but they include a regeneration component). Perhaps it would be a good idea to allow any character to turn off any beneficial spell. I understand that there is a roleplaying reason for not allowing it (it isn't your spell, after all, and how would a warrior know how to turn off a clerical spell anyhow). But for gameplay it might be too restrictive. Or, allow the magician armor spells to overwrite them a lower level clerical armor spell.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Banishment of Shadows.

DESCRIPTION: This level 60 spell does not work on targets over level 45. It destroys the target, leaving no corpse and giving no experience. It also costs a Star Ruby to cast. If this spell was intended to help the cleric deal with annoying green, then perhaps the component cost is too high.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Group Resist Poison.

DESCRIPTION: Since clerics are all about stopping poison, how about a group version of a resist poison spell. A nice time saver for clerics.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Root or Mez undead.

DESCRIPTION: This might be a good idea for new spell lines for the cleric.

STATUS: N/A – This is an idea for future use, no action is expected on this item.

---

ITEM: Gate to Temple (personal).

DESCRIPTION: A spell for each worshipable deity in the game that would take them to the temple to that god. So, for example, Innoruuk followers would be have a spell to gate themselves to the temple in Neriak.

STATUS: N/A – This is an idea for future use, no action is expected on this item.

---

ITEM: Deity specific spells.

DESCRIPTION: Similar to the Temple Gate idea. Create a unique line of spells for each god that can only be cast by clerics that worship that god. They would have to be balance with each other and still be unique and representative of the individual god, so they might be very hard to balance.

STATUS: N/A – This is an idea for future use, no action is expected on this item.

I've just posted this over at EQCleric, but here I'll expand on it

Initial Impressions:

Things that were shot down
--------------------------

* Gate to Temple
OK, bad idea, never been a big fan of it myself.

* Diety Specific Spells
Sigh. The Cookie Cutter Cleric syndrome continues. I'm a Human Cleric of Mithaniel Marr. Why Should I be gameplay-wise exactly the same as a Dark Elf Cleric of Innoruk ? Perhaps just actually finish putting in the diety specific symbols into the game ?

Things that were ignored in the original thread
-----------------------------------------------
* Group Armour Class buffs
CLERICS WANT THESE. I cannot stress how important this is. Make it a group version of our 39th Armour spell, at tripple the mana cost. Here is a major clue : BUFFING IS NOT FUN.

* Offensive Melee skill increases, such as 1 hand blunt
Saying that Higher defense and dodge skills will " let us get into the fight more often" betrays a major lack of knowledge about Cleric Melee.

Clue : Clerics. Cant. Melee.
Hitting for 1's and 5's on a mob with 15,000 hitpoints is NOT meleeing. It's not even amusing to watch

Things that have been worded so as to be unlikely to happen
-----------------------------------------------------------
* Standing Meditation
Yes, our power is given to us from our god, many of whom now walk Norrath. So why should we have to sit and med like the other classes that use their internal energies to cast spells ?

* Aegolism changes
The spell is too expensive manawise. Again - BUFFING IS NOT FUN Make it entire group for 2000 mana, which at least we can med back with bardsong, necro infusions, modrods. Rather then Buff / Med / Buff / Med / Buff / Med

* Shield of Words AC vs Mana
The Armour class spells badly need a group component to get it all out of the way in one hit. Notice a trend about buffing yet ?

* Abolish Disease (49th Shaman Spell)
Clerics and druids should get this spell, because we have always gotten the other anti disease spells one level later. You have already blurred the lines by giving Druids and Shamans Remedy and Divine Light, it's a bit late to get nervous about the minor issue of Diseases.

Shamans get cure disease at 1st, clerics and druids get cure disease at 5th
Shamans get Counteract at 24th, clerics and druids get Counteract at 29th

* Invisibility (Absor is VERY against this in past comments)
I agree with a previous poster, lets get rid of Pacify from the Bracer or Tremor from the boots and give us invisibility. Warriors can cast from their armour, why not us ?

Things that have a chance of actually being brought up at the meeting

* Parry and Bash
Bash could actually be useful. After all, we have 6 stun spells, all of which have been made useless on your Magic Resistant Complete Healing mobs, so something else that we can fail with every time would fit in just perfectly.

* Unswerving Hammer of Faith Immune to AOE and Riposte
For this spell to EVER be useful, it has to be able to do at least 1000 damage. Why ? Because at 54th level, we get a 240 mana 575 direct damage that gets +10 damage a level. The hammer is almost 3 times this mana cost 600 mana, so it's effectiveness should be at a minimum equal damage.

* Possible 58th level low resist stun
Why not remove the double resist check from all our stuns, and revisit some of the higher ones ? I still use my level 5 stun, because it has no damage component, so it is twice as likely to actually land.

* Bind Wounds
My bind wounds skill is around 5, at 59th level. Because, since I have to sit and meditate to get my mana back, it is more efficient for the group for me to sit down and do nothing. Every tick that I'm spending giving a tank thirty hitpoints from a bandage is another tick I'm not medding.
This is the core issue with Cleric's - Because our mana is a major factor in group effectiveness, every tick we are not trying to get our maximum mana back is another tick we fail to do our job.

This means, to do our job best, we have to sit down and do nothing except watch our mana bar creep upwards, while everyone else gets to do things. You seem to have missed this point the first 300 times it was brought up.

* Death Pact/Invervention
It costs money. It costs mana. It costs a buff slot. It should work 100% of the time, not some arcane formula based on the casters charisma.

* Symbol of Naltron 1/2 price or increased duration
Increased duration is not the problem. The cost of the component is. An increased duration won't help us against dispelling mobs, or for that monk that dies a dozen times an hour on any average SoV raid.

* Heroism and Heroic Bond stacking
If all else fails, why not make it so Heroism is 1 hp or 1 ac less then Heroic Bond ? It's a simple change, and Heroic Bond will always overwrite it then

* Fizzle rate of Divine Light
And Mark of Karn, which is far worse then Divine Light. I cast Mark of Karn and Celestial Elixier all the time on raids, and Mark (a spell I got 3 levels ago) fizzles far more often then Celestial Elixier does, a spell I got at 59th.

* Fortitude
Again, make it a group AC buff

* Summoned Hammer spells
About the only way to actually make these useful would make them droppable and not lore. Of course, then we are cutting in on the Mage class summons, and we know how much you hate merging spells between different classes.

* Lull spells
You mean that these arn't used to pull mobs ? I've been using Lull and Pacify as a low agro pulling method for when I'm the highest level person in a group. To fix these, make it so that if a person is more then half the normal agro distance away, the mob won't agro.

* Allow clicking off of beneficial spells
PLEASE. Nothing worse then a Tank playing the get-dispelled and rebuffed because the best buffs are on him first, or he's run into the 15 buff limit

* Banishment of Shadows component cost.
Why do rogues' assasinate( which they can do with their normal equipment) still let them get XP and the corpse, but our spell which has an expensive component remove the corpse and cost everyone XP ? The wizards would like to talk to you about Disintergrate also, I'm sure.

* Group Resist poison
More group spells are good. Remember, BUFFING IS NOT FUN

Understatement of the Year
--------------------------

"Mark of Karn - Perhaps a slight increase in effectiveness is in order"

NO. SIMPLY CHANGE IT BACK THE WAY IT WAS.
If you continue to insist on the strawman argument that Mark of Karn is unbalanced because of the single wield / Dual wield complaints, here is my explanation again.

Complete Heal benefits Paladins, Shadowknights and Warriors, classes that use a lot of 2 handed weapons. Paladins and Shadowknights Cannot dual wield at all.
So they receive minimum benefit from Mark of Karn, and maximum benefit from Complete Heal

Complete heal CANNOT be used safely on Rangers, Monks or Rogues, three classes that use a lot of dual wield weapons.
So they receive minimum benefit from Complete Heal, and maximum benefit from Mark of Karn

Whoever designed this spell originally knew what they were doing, and was aware of the Ranger / Monk damage sponge comments, and introduced this spell specifically to counter that.

Don't destroy his brilliant idea, put Mark of Karn
back the way it was.

Jimette
59th Human Cleric
