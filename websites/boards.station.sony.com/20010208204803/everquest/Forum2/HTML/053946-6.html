BigJP24, it's funny you should mention the "Rule of Thumb."

That phrase is pretty old. It came from Merrye Olde Englande (everything back then had a silent e at the ende). Back in those days, there was only one ruler, and he was NOT made of wood. That ruler was the King.

You see, one day, the Kinge ofe Englande decided that a standard of weights and measures would keep people honest. Up until then, if someone sold you 50 "feet" of rope, the merchant would actually walk off the length with his own feet. This meant that the merchants with the smallest feet made more money.

Since Kings hate whenever anybody else has a better scam than they do, the Kinge decided that he would make a standard of weights and measures so that everywhere in the Realm, an inch was an inch, and a pint is a pound the world 'round.

The measurement for the "yard" became the distance from the end of the Kinge's nose to the tip of his outstretched middle finger (no jokes now), the "foot" was his own foot's length (I think he was wearing shoes that day though), and the "inch" became the length off the Kinge's thumb from the second joint.

Thus we get the story of the "Rule of Thum." It is actually a rigidly-measured standard length based upon the physical person who represented the entire Realm. If anything was publically-known and published, it was the exact standards by which to measure everything.

No judgement calls. Absolute printed certainty. That is what the "Rule of Thumb" was REALLY all about. When people refer to the "Rule of Thumb" as if it means "an obvious choice," they are ignoring the origin of the concept. What the real meaning of that phrase is SUPPOSED to be is that standards are arbitrarily-decided upon, but then set in stone.

Nothing could be further from this situation. This is all judgement call, and I don't have any faith in the judgement of a person who would change Managirl's name in the name of the Naming Policy, when that name does not violate the Naming Policy as written in any way.

If Verant wants to have a naming policy that is vague, they need to have a true system of arbitration so that people like Managirl have some recourse of appeal when they are /petitioned. A jury of her peers would not be a bad idea. Why doesn't Verant set up a system of Ombudsmen, who can be elected in each server, to arbitrate behavioral and PnP issues? Why set up guides and GMs to have to be Judge Dredd with the entire rulebook?
