Axem: I think that hybrids and warriors are reaching a pretty ideal balance around now... why? Because more and more, groups are able to take a "tank" into the group, who fulfills the "tank" role. It's not about who gets picked over who; it's about who can fulfill a function.

The different classes will perform differently. Random examples: if our tank is a paladin, I'll need to heal a lot less. If our tank is a shadowknight, we'll have feigned pulls on our side. In either case, I'm probably going to have to use some mana on DoTs. If our tank is a ranger, I'm going to have to religiously slow everything and not skimp on hit point/AC buffs, but I won't feel any great compulsion to cast any damage spells. If our tank is a warrior, s/he has a billion hit points, and I'll have the luxury of letting regrowth do practically all my healing, because emergency heals will be less necessary.

What's my point? All those classes can fulfill a role in my group--they mean different things to what my group will be able to do and how it will work, but they're all roughly equivalent in terms of "all the jobs of this group are being handled." I think that's balance.
