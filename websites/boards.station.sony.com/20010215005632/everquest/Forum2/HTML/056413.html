Unfortunately, as will probably be the case from here out, I just don't have time to do any research before starting this post. I'll have to rely on you folks to tell me all I need to know about enchanters…
Again, the usual reminder. I am primarily looking for balance concerns here. But I am certainly willing to put bugs on the list. Also, though I really do understand the fact that you want to emphasize and back up statements by others, please, please try to keep the repeat information to a minimum. I'm finding that by page three or four on these threads I'm rereading the same suggestions by people that start their posts with "I didn't read this thread, so I don't know if this had been said…" I don't want to stifle any creativity. But I have to admit that I tend to get bleary eyed reading the same suggestions over and over again, and I know I'm missing good new ideas late in threads when they are buried among things that others have covered several times before.

So, please, let's hear what you think is unbalanced or broken about enchanters. Here are the few things that I had already jotted down:

Gift of Brilliance: When compared to similar buffs by Clerics and Shamans this spell doesn’t seem good enough. Sure, it will see use, but it could be a better spell. Quick comparison:

Aeogolism (60th level Cleric spell): Adds 1,100 to max hit points and 54 to AC for a single target. Lasts 2.5 hours. Costs 750 mana.
Focus of the Spirit (60th level Shaman spell): Adds 405 hit points, 67 strength and 60 dexterity for a single target. Lasts 1.5 hours. Costs 500 mana.
Gift of Brilliance (50th level Enchanter spell): Adds 150 mana and grants a mana regeneration rate increase of 2 points per tick for a single target. Lasts 1.5 hours. Costs 450 mana.

The Shaman and Cleric spells essentially replace other buffs. Gift of Brilliance stacks with Clarity II. It might be nice to have the mana regeneration be 2 points greater than Clarity II and NOT stack with it so that enchanters could use it to save a slot. And perhaps rather than increasing the mana bonus, give it an intelligence bonus. But perhaps at this level enchanters just don't need any more intelligence boosts…

Illusions drop when zoning: Is there a gameplay reason behind the fact that illusions drop when you zone? If not, can we make it so they do not?

More Illusions: I think it’s a good idea to dig through the list of current global characters and see if there are a few more illusions that we can give to enchanters.

I know there are more issues…

Shakaama has good points (as do all the other lengthy posts), but, again, I do have some disagreements.

An enchanter alters our virtual reality. An enchanter's gift is to change the situation to help ensure a favorable outcome (to the enchanter). This means that things like buffing allies and debuffing enemies is an important part of what an enchanter does.

Crowd control is senior. Without crowd control, all the buffing and debuffing (especially, since debuffing NPC's is of limited use) which we can muster is inadequate.

So, while there are many good points which Shakaama makes, I strongly disagree with the idea that enchanters shouldn't be good at the kinds of things they do buff. I agree that shamans should be good at it, too -- it would be fantastic if the shaman's buffs were of more use (i.e. stats mattered more) than they do now, but that's a shaman and general gameplay issue, and not an enchanter one.

The other point is an enchanter's damage spells. The very fact that Shakaama has mostly soloed his way to 50 contradicts his argument. Our damage spells are NOT what enchanters do. I picked an enchanter because I wanted a weak class which needed to use other skills to win the day -- not just blast, blast, blast. And, amazingly, this is exactly what I got.

Enchanters don't need more DD. Had Shakaama (and many others of us) had inadequate DD, then we'd never be able to solo past 15th level -- and yet we can. It takes awhile, sure -- we don't layeth down the smack like anyone else -- but we can solo, using the other tools we have available to us.

We do not, in general, want to be wizards. We want to be enchanters.

No enchanter would have a problem with Velious if the difficulty of Velious were that it required a change of tactics for us. Instead, Velious simply says, "No, you can't do that, have a nice day -- somewhere else."

Imagine for a moment that Verant turned the tables in Velious and had enchanter mobs which actually used the Mesmerize line, and mobs which knew how to coordinate their attacks on a single target. In other words, imagine that mobs did unto us what we enchanters have been doing unto them for so long.

THAT would be a challenge, and it wouldn't require millions of HP's. Every caster with Dispel Magic suddenly has a vitally needed spell, and mobs wouldn't need to cast AE Pillage Enchantment (oh what fun THAT was...), because we'd be stripping buffs and debuffs and mez's off ourselves!

Instead, all we have to look forward to are extra spells of questionable or minimal value, coupled with the reduction of our activities to dispensing two buffs every twenty to thirty minutes, and mobs whose AE dispel attacks make the value of the only thing we have left -- our buffs -- questionable, anyway!

Wouldn't you feel betrayed?

I do.
