The key to making everyone happy in the high level game is to give the classes specialized capabilities and then creat a variety of mobs with a variety of weaknesses. Right now Verant is just pumping out one kind of epic mob, fretting one players find that only one kind of strategy works best, and trying to blur the classes enough so that everyone can take part in the raids.

This is pissing everyone off because no one has a "class" anymore. Everyone is just a healer, melee, caster or buffer. What happened to paladins, rangers, bards, wizards? These used to be individual classes with specific specialties that shined in specific situations. Verant can make the high level game like this again if they just force a little more creativity into their designs. Start by getting away from melee dependant raiding parties. Here are some ideas:

Dragons fly, right? How about an outdoor fight with a flying dragon? Suddenly, the nukers are the heroes. Melees can still use bows and take the occasional swipe when the beast lands, but you need range to tackle an air-borne foe

How about a caster monster perched on a distant ledge? Melees can't reach him, so you need nukers to take him out.

What about the age old gelatenous (sp?) cube? Why would you let that thing take damage from swords and such? Makes more sense that only fire could hurt it, or maybe ice and then melee when the thing freezes?

What about heavily armored targets? Monsters wearing loads and loads of heavy metal or rock armor don't flinch at a sword, so you'd need nukers to light em up from the inside out. Maybe at a certain hp level they shed the armor and become vulnerable to melee.

Give paladins and clerics back their crowns as undead killers and let them shine versus the undead epic mobs. Give rangers and druids bonuses against those rogue animal epic mobs. Let the necros and shadowknights take out the holy mobs. Give the wizards and magicians some decent mana regen post 50 so they can contribute more frequently.
