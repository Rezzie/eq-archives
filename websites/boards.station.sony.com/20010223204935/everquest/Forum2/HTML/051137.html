In addition to the time (and money) spent creating the trade items, there is also the time spent selling your wares. This can be more that the actual creation time.

It'd be nice to be able to open up a shop that you could stock with goods and have other players come to. Sorry, Verant, but beating up something together is NOT the only form of social interaction. The interaction between the customers, players looking for equipment, and the tradesmen, leads to very ALIVE cities, bustling with something close to medieval city atmosphere.

In EQ tradeskills are useless because most of the items you can produce are useless. Why should somebody go out and smith a skewer when nobody in this game NEEDS a skewer? Not the players and not even the NPCs, which just buy it for an amount lower than what you paid for the mold and the metal. Why should somebody learn baking and spend lots of money on producing food items that are in no way superior to cheap muffins you can buy from a NPC? Yes, some people do it because they are bored and idle and rich.

As a newbie in UO you have the same problems as a newbie in EQ. No money or equipment to speak of and difficulties to kill even a small spider. So in UO you have the choice of either killing small things and making money from the loot, OR first following a tradeskill, earning some money with that, and buying a rusty sword from the profits. Pity the newbie who tries that in EQ.
