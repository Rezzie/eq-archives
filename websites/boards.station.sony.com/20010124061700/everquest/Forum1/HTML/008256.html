Greetings to all,
We will be proactively trying to help people to solve their problems that have not been solved by installing the new DirectX version AND then reinstalling or updating their video card drivers.

We will be asking for the dbg.txt files as well as DXDIAG files.

First delete the dbg.txt file. The dbg.txt file is located in the Everquest folder. To get to that file you need to left click on my computer, than left click on your hard drive with the Everquest game installed on it, than go to program files, than click on your everquest folder and find the dbg.txt file. Clicking on the details button can help you sort and search the Everquest folder. After deleting the Dbg.txt file play once and get the crash message.

To get the DXDIAG file you would go to start than go up to run. In the run window please type DXDIAG and hit ok. This will launch the Direct X diagnostic tool. At the bottom of the tool is a button that says save all information. Hit the button and save all your information. Than save the document to your document file.

We will most likely need your motherboard version as well if possible.

Customer Service/Tech Supports Number is
1-888-STATION (782-8466)
We are open Monday through Friday 8:30AM to 5:30 PM.

Here is two ways to determine what sound and video game drivers and cards you have.

Click on start, go up to run. In the Run window, type DXDIAG than hit ok. This will launch the DirectX diagnostics tool. Click on the tab that says Display to see what video card you are using and what is the latest driver you are using. . Click on the tab that says Sound to see what sound card you are using and what is the latest driver for the sound card.

Another way is at your desktop right click on My Computer. Scroll down to properties, click on properties and go up to device manager. Please click on device manager. In the device manager, if you click on sound, video, and game controllers, you can determine what audio device you are using. If you click on display adapter you can see what video device you are using. If you click on either device and then click on the driver tab you can determine which version you are using.

After you find out what video card or sound card you have, please visit our main Website and visit the support section and examine the sound and video FAQ’s. This will usually have locations for the latest drivers for your cards from the manufacturers’ websites.

An incompatibility or conflict with your system and the soundcard often causes crashes to desktop from the game. To check to see if your soundcard has a conflict, you should slowly disable the sound card. If the problem seems to get better as you disable the card you will be able to see that the problem is with the soundcard.
You would first go to Start, than go to Run, in the run window you would type in DXDIAG. This will bring up the direct diagnostic tool that comes with newer versions of Direct X.
You would go over to sound. You would lower your soundcard acceleration from full to standard. If you crash to desktop still, you go back and lower the acceleration from standard to basic. If you crash again, you would lower it from basic to none. If you crash again you would disable your sound card in device manager.
To get to your device manager, you would Right click on your [My computer] icon. You would scroll down to properties, and than go up to device manager. You would go into device manager and scroll down to sound video and game controllers. In sound, video, and game controllers, you would find the audio. You would open up the audio device and in the general section you would check the Disable in this profile box AND uncheck the Exist in all profiles box. It is very important that only one box is checked. This should disable your sound card. If you still get kicked to desktop, you would want to open up the case and unseed (pull out) the sound card from the motherboard.
If you pull out the card and you still crash to desktop from the game you should call tech support at 1-888-STATION Monday through Friday 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM Pacific Standard Time.
If you can not pull out your card from the motherboard because it is built in, you should read your motherboard instructions and disable the sound card in your BIOS. The Bios is the drivers or software that runs your motherboard and the motherboard setup. Some older motherboards have jumper settings that need to be turned off. Again, you should read your motherboard manual about disabling soundcard for built in soundcards.
Finding a NEWER or sometimes an OLDER version of the drivers for the soundcard can sometimes resolve the conflict or incompatibility. This depends on the Windows operating system you are currently using.

Please note that an IRQ conflict can interfere with the sound card operating correctly.
Right click on my computer> go to properties> go to device manager> click on computer and view resources for the IRQ’s. If your video card shares resources it might inhibit its performance. You might need to take other conflicting cards out and put the cards in other matching slots in your motherboard to free up the IRQ for the video card. Check your IRQ setting number to see if you have any numbers that match. Feel free to ignore PCI steering. Perhaps your Sound card and Video card are sharing resources. If they are you would need to pull one of the cards out and place it in another compatible slot.

Don't blame David for these problems. His advice to check drivers is sound advice and may seem redundant to those who keep coming back to the boards. You can not acurately diagnose a problem over a message board. Each system has its own personality, each and every device on it acts diferently than each and every device on any other system.

The simple truth is that 90 percent of the time it is a driver problem, yes the code for everquest isn't very dynamic, but alot of the time a newer driver will will be able to handle any exceptions thrown by the program.

Could they fix this problem by fixing some of their code? Yep. Would it be easy to find where the problem is? Nope. Would it be worth the effort and the time? Nope. With hundreds of thousand people playing a few hundred people with a specific bug are not going to warrent a patch.
Now if it was a bug that was seen on the majority of systems, for example the SB Live problems, then it is worth adressing.

The best course of action is not to flame David but to give him usefull information. Yes he will probably tell you to install the latest drivers even after you mention you have tried that; however, word will get around the office about the problem and it just might get enough attention to warrent a fix.

Finally, it is likely that a sollution to the crashes can be found for each and every system out there. You just have to keep fiddling till you find it. There is no black and white answer of "oh I know what the problem is. Here go to Start, select control panel...."

For those of you that come to a tech forum asking for assistance and then get angry when when the sollution is complicated you must realise that there are no simple sollutions to anything when it comes to personal computers.
