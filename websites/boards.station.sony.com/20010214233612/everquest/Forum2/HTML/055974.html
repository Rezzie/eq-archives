Several things that I believe would help clerics, all I believe have been stated before by others, I am only throwing my support to these ideas along with why I think they are good/needed.

Temple Port
This would port a cleric to the temple of their deity. It would be a self only port. This fits in nicely with the clerical type of role as well as giving the cleric a slight expansion of current ability. While I fully agree with the increase in healing ability for Shamans/Druids, clerics feel that ‘their’ domain has been taken from them with nothing in return. This simple port gives us something that is both very useful, yet doesn’t take anything from another class.

Prayer
Clerics have asked many times for some type of self-mana regen as we are one of the few (if not only) casting class that doesn’t have it. I think Prayer would be a great way of giving it to clerics while keeping a ‘clerical’ flavor. IMHO it should work along the lines of a spell. The duration should be about 5 minutes with a 15 minute recast time, self only. I think a recast time of greater than 30 minutes would render the spell useless. During this time a 10 mana regen per tick would be in effect. During this time the cleric can move around, sit stand, etc. However, the act of casting a spell, any spell, would dispel the Prayer. Note that the recast time would still be in effect. This could greatly effect downtime (a much despised aspect of any game) while not affecting balance of the game during combat.

Group AC Buffs
We have Heroic Bond, and later (I’m only 53rd level) a group Symbol. What happened to our group AC buff? Personally I think you would find very few clerics that would mind losing our incredibly useful ‘pet’ and having it replaced with a group AC buff. Again, this simplifies a clerics job by changing 6 casts to one, as well as saving a little mana.

Defense/Offense
Seems there are a fair number of clerics that fall on both sides of this. Myself personally, I started a cleric because I enjoyed healing others, helping them do things they couldn’t do themselves. As such I’ve always seen myself in terms of defense, not offense. Because of that I personally don’t see a good reason for increasing the offensive capabilities of the cleric. I’m sure others disagree with that though. However, I do think that the defensive capabilities of a cleric could use some improvement. Because I don’t see us as the nimble tank jumping around avoiding blows, I don’t think this means an increase in the dodge capability of a cleric, but in the Defense skill. I think it should be raised from 175 to 200. I think this can also be justified as our Offense skill is at 200. Doesn’t make sense to me that the Defense skill should be lower.

Deity Specific
I realize that you have stated that while this sounds like it would be wonderful for making the cleric class more interesting, it would be unlikely to be implemented at this late stage in the game. However, a suggestion on this. Currently the Dark Elves (I am one of them) have our 3 deity specific necklaces. Each one of these cast a different low level Necro type spell. While the spells themselves seldom have a usefulness in group situations, they stats of the necklaces are worthwhile. Beside, on more than one occasion the spell (Darkness) has come in useful. I always enjoy answering the question ‘How’d a cleric do that?” with ‘Well, I’m EVIL!” So while making true changes to clerics to reflect their deities might not be an option, please press the development team to complete the equivalent deity-specific type quests for the other clerics.

Well, these are some of the things that I’ve seen that I believe would be valuable to clerics. All of it is presented solely as my opinion though. Thanks for reading it. J

The backlash on the announced changes on test for Druids and Shamans mask the real problem here. Those two classes need better heals, and most clerics will freely admit it.

The problem is that at high levels, clerics are already a vanishing breed on many servers. This surely indicates a huge problem, does it not, given that most people agree that clerics are a "required" class? By "clerics", btw, I mean people who have played a cleric as their primary character since they started. They are fading away, and being replaced by "clerics" who are just powerlevelled alts in staggering numbers.

This doesn't seem to matter though anymore. Why? Because a cleric has become so one-dimenional in the high level game that anyone can play one. Stuns, a cleric's best friend during the middle of their career, become increasingly useless at high levels. Roots break on the first blast a majority of the time. The role of a cleric as an all-out mitigator of damage is narrowing and narrowing to just casting the most efficient heal and sitting back down.

Many people are saying that clerics should be happy they are so needed for multi-group events. The fact is, a large number of clerics *hate* multi-group events with a passion becasue they are *constantly* hounded to attend them, and this is why they are picking up alts and dropping their clerics. The high level game is broken, and clerics and chanters are driven to distraction by the peer pressure applied to them to anchor every single guild event that occurs. Say "raid" to a post-50 cleric and many hear this: "every night, no exp, sit down, spend cash, rez us all, make the game fun for us because it's your job".

How do you fix this? You have to add some variety for clerics, and you have to make single group activities much more prevalent and viable post-50th. The class balancing only addresses the former, though, so let's talk about that. These suggestions all go towards increasng the variety of the cleric's ability to protect their group.

1. Stun Command: Verant was smart on this one...expand on it. AE stuns are a perfect way to add variety to clerics without unbalancing anything. Why? Because if you are in a group AE stunning a pack of mobs and stop to heal, you will never get back in control long enough to start AE stunning again. It's an either-or kind of role. Add more AE stuns, with lower recast times and mana cost, at a wider range of levels, all somewhat less than the equivalent Enchanter line. Infringing on Enchanters? Barely; they have mez, clarity, haste buffs, slow, etc. Heck, I know many chanters who rarely mem AE stuns at all and have never participated in a group doing primarily AE spells.

2. Evac to your own God's Temple: create a single evac spell to one fixed location in the game, your chosen God's temple. Save your group, but extremely limited travel applications, minimizing impact on druids and wizards. Put the entire group under rez effects when this is used if you want to balance it further.

3. Examine what mobs are immune to stun at high levels. There are too many that are. This was a cheap way to make mobs tougher to beat without improving the AI, but in effect you narrowing the roles of the classes with stuns. Fix this.

4. Fear line...with Dread of Night clerics were reduced from having a standard fear to having an undead only fear. Further narrowing that could be fixed easily.

5. Fix the hammer pet and also replace the last summon hammer in the spell line with a reduced power pet. Make it a hammer and shield pet that lasts more than one fight (can still limit it some other way) and then tune it more for defense so clerics can us it to solo better at high levels where they need it (druids and shamans are having their grouping abilities increased to solve everyone's problems in getting groups, give clerics better soloability to solve their problem). Make it non-viable in a group somehow (damage dealt, hit points, and AC are divided by number of group members present perhaps?), and decrease the offensive output if needed overall.

6. Make Cure Blindness actually work ...sorry just had to throw that one in there, not a balance issue.

First of all, by all means improve the healing capabilities of other priest classes. I'm a cleric of the 35th circle and being the primary healer, I certainly want my backup healers to be able to heal me effectively. Any improvements to druid or shaman healing only benifits the cleric class, as I see it.

The whine about not being able to increase mana regen has some merit -- druids eventually get a skin buff that does it, shamans can cannibalize, clerics = nada.

So that made me realize that my suggestion really is a class balance issue. But I'm not advocating that clerics be given mana regen capability! Nothing of the sort. But my suggestion does allow the cleric to regen mana at meditation speed while still healing the party: the ability to bind wounds while sitting down!

This would greatly reduce downtime during the game which is something that everyone hates. It adds an extra ability to our class that is right in line with our class' purpose. And because of the restrictions on bind wounds, it in no way overbalances the class. It can only be done after level ~35 because that is when you can just sit down to meditate.

To remind people, bind wounds completely freezes the one doing the binding for several seconds during which you can do nothing. If the person you are binding moves, the bind wounds is cancelled, and in fact requires a sit-down/sit-up combination to clear the binding state. Finally, the amount it heals is severely restricted -- only 1/4 your skill is healed in hitpoints. For clerics, the current skill cap is 200, which means a maximum of 50 hps can be healed. Which further means that this will only be used after everyone is severely injured and the priests are out of mana.

There has been talk of raising the maximum percentage of hit points healed with bind wounds depending on class. I would add the following to that discussion:

Bind up to 50% -- All classes, bandages only.
Bind up to 70% -- Melee and priest classes, 2x bandages plus ointment of some kind.
Bind up to 90% -- Cleric class only, 3x bandages plus 2x ointment plus balm.

The ointment and balm requirements puts a price/carrying-capacity limitation on the ability. The ointment should be purchased in melee and priest guild halls. The balm should be purchasable only in cleric halls.

Shamans should be able to make the ointments and balms as a part of the alchemy skill.

Thanks again for your attention to these matters, Alan!
