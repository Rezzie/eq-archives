This is a tad conspiracy theory, I'll admit, but another reason Verant will never change the money system is: real-world money... If my character got money easier, I would play less, no because the game would be worse, but because I wouldn't have to work so damned hard for every red cent. I could log on for two hours and log off with better equipment. As it stands it takes alot more than three hours to get enough money to buy better equipment than what you are using to hunt the monsters at the moment. This makes you play more, and thus feel that you are getting mor eout of the game. Which you are, so you keep playing.

Three pieces of advice for 1st-5th lvl players who want cash.

1)Buy Bags and Backpack, at least a couple. Then you can stay out slaying monsters way longer and more effectively.

2)Unless the item is very valuble, only pick up stackables, like bat wings. This way you can get alot more before you have to go trade them in.

3)Go after skeletons as soon as you hit 2nd or 3rd, and humanoids too (ie gnoll pups, orc pawns). They have much better loot in the long term. 20 Bat wings will get a gold piece and change, but a couple of rusty longswords will get almost four GP. They may also have weapons better than the one you are using, or more handy (staves over dagger for instance, or rusty broadswords over normal shortswords). Just my 2 cp (it's all I can afford!
