There are good reasons why Verant NEEDS these boards, though.

1. It's safe to say that many of the people on these boards have WWWAAAAYYYY more /played time with their characters than anyone one the EQLive team does. It's a well known fact that there are basic limitations on what a designer (of anything) can know. Theory is one thing. Practice is another. A player who has played this game for 100+ days of REAL TIME knows a tremendous amount about how the game works IN PRACTICE. This player will understand certain things that even the best game designer never could. We all have seen many ideas in EQ that must have sounded just great on paper in the EQLive team meeting room, but just didn't work in practice.

2. It is also a simple numbers game. There is no way that 10 or 20 members (or however many there are) of the EQLive tuning team could POSSIBLY have as much collective Everquest knowledge as 300,000 players. No way.

If you shut down these boards, you shut down the ONLY good source of communication between designers and players. Sure, we players could still send you e-mail or something, but that just won't work because it won't be a DIALOGUE. The players need to hear direct responses to their concerns as much as possible.

Sometimes, you guys at Verant are right and we players are wrong. When that happens, we need direct feedback from you, so that we can know WHY we are wrong. Sometimes, we players are right and you guys at Verant are wrong. When that happens, we need to know what you are thinking so we can set you straight.

Also, these boards provide a means by which players can correct each other. Although we aren't always very polite in the process, it does get the job done. I have personally seen an incredible number of posts on these boards that say things that are just not true. The best way for us to find out when we are wrong is to get responses from other players.

Everquest is an incredibly complex game. It's one of the most complex RPG's ever made. The only way to have any hope at all of getting things right as a designer, is to have access to REAL DIALOGUE, where players can talk to each other and to designers, and get feedback on our comments.

Very interesting discussion.

I too have wondered about these boards (especially Whineplay) and the value they serve. It's true that only a minority of players even know of its existence, and a small minority thereof make up the majority of posts. Also there's a disturbing tendancy toward "fad" posts. Suddenly everybody is whining about hybrids being useless one day, warriors being obsolete the next, clerics and aggro after that. What gives?

But on the other hand, these hardcore posters know the game a lot better than anybody else. Say what you want about -Thogg-, and yes his priorities in life may be loopy, but he probably knows more about weapon ratios and hate lists than many people on the EQ Live team. Therefore his opinion may be in some ways more valuable than that of John Q. Customer who just limits his feedback to the online poll.

Also, regarding one of your first assertations, this kind of board is not really unknown in the gaming industry. The RTS Warlords Battlecry had an open and active discussion group with little of the acrimony we see here. The lead producer (Brad's equivilent) posted there daily. Also, another Sony game Infantry does the same thing, and its lead guy posts there regularly. (And I think Aradune himself posted regularly back in his Tanarus days, I could be wrong here.)

No, this isn't universal. I don't exactly see John Romero having a polite discussion of his project with his customers. And huge corporations are not known for their eagerness to sponsor criticism of their own products. But with an ongoing project like EQ, with its endless updates and patching, some sort of two-way discussion system MUST exist, or the viewpoint of the designers and coders will inevitably stray from that of their loyal fanbase. I don't care how great of a QA team Verant has, there's no way they can accurately represent the player base.

This board DOES serve a valid purpose, and there ARE compelling reasons to allow it to exist. Simply silencing the players, limiting their feedback to one-way only, or forcing them into an off-site board like Stratics would only serve to alienate Verant's most loyal customers.

We need..

1) A MODERATED board, where useless flames / misinformation / repeat posts of the same thing get deleted. It would not be out of the question for new topics (not replies) to be approved before posting. Yes it would be all too easy for Sony to just delete any and all criticism of its product but even I acknowledge some compromises need to be made.

2) One or more staff members to both read posts, and respond. This staff member has to be able to get off the high horse occasionally and see it from the player's viewpoint, or he or she will just make things worse (can we say Abashi). And this staff person must respond occasionally, especially to the most glaring concerns, if nothing else than to say "Hey, we hear you." I think Alan's been doing a pretty good job, considering the sheer volume.

3) A concerted effort on the part of the players to present valid concerns, and not just spin off into "YOU RUINED MY CLASS, YOU SUCK" flames. Maybe we need to realize that Verant isn't just "out to get" one class and its players. Sure they may be totally wrong when it comes to the Wizard class (for instance) but what motive would they have to just screw over part of their subscriber base?

"Hey Brad, some poor schmuck just made a wizard on Tarew Marr!"
"Bwahahaha, wait'll he learns that we hate people who play wizards! Hey, let's nerf that wizard-only dagger just for kicks!"

A fun theory admittedly but not the base of a reasoned discussion

Anyway my point is.. and yes I have a point to all this.. sure people whine here a lot, but a lot of times there's a reason why they're whining. A moderated board; a cool, non-arrogant moderator; and cool players who lead by example in posting reasoned topics can only help Verant make a better product.

As an example (and I don't want to go off on the substance of this, merely the way it was handled): On the recent cleric nerf, there was no communication or discussion in advance ... indeed, there was no testing, which appears to have been intended to avoid discussion. That looks bad, particularly when it concerns a major game change. Secondly, despite the existence of the cleric talk threads, which were supposedly to exchange ideas, this was not mentioned at all. Finally, about the only communication which ever occurred on that was to state that it "needed" to be done. Why? I know this board may or may not be representative of the total player base, but on that issue people in the game say precisely the same things. Anyway, that could have been handled a lot better. Do you really think that situation would have been better if they just dropped the change in with no opportunity to respond at all?

As for Absor, I don't want to make any one person an issue here. I understand his position, but I don't know what background or expertise he has in customer communications. Overall, I think he does a perfectly fine job, but there are times when he phrases things in a manner which tends to irk people. I did some time in customer relations myself when I entered the business world, and you always had to remember to restrain yourself, no matter how upset the customers were. The point was to solve the problems as best possible. You recognize, of course, that some problems cannot be solved and there will always be some customers who are irrevocably upset, but you can still simply be nice to them and treat their views with respect. Yes, people certainly sometimes push you to the limit, or maybe you've just had a bad day, or whatever, and you pop one off. But communications do need to be handled in a productive and effective manner as much as humanly possible ... and there is no harm in hiring someone who is specifically an expert in just that.

One thing which I think should be done is to put up forum sections for EACH individual class. As it is, people log in to Gameplay to see if there's anything interesting concerning them, and they tend to just pop into other threads there and drop comments which may or may not be productive. Let's make it a little more work to intermeddle in things which don't concern you and about which you may know little or nothing. In fact, practically every third party board in existence has sections for each class, and that does somewhat reduce the incidence of the cowboys patrolling the Indians', er Native American's, threads, and vice versa. It provides a much more controlled environment in which to exchange views pertinent to a limited group. As for Gameplay, leave it for issues involving the game as a whole.

And frankly, there are some people here who obviously just come to start arguments and flame others. There are people here - and many are well known - who simply disagree nastily with whatever others say. There are obviously some people here who are completely unhappy and/or frustrated with every aspect of the game, and their neverending crusade is either to kill the game or drive others away. I would not advocate anyone being suspended or banned from the board for popping off once in a while, but the habitual abusers should be warned, and if they don't straighten up, then suspended or banned from the board (NOT the game ... two separate things).

But if Verant wants people to be unhappy without them knowing why, then kill the board. If they want people to quit without them knowing why, then kill the board. If they want people to be VERY unhappy when major changes are introduced with no exchange of views ... well, you see where I am going. I can't see that approach. I just think more effort and expertise needs to be invested in doing this in a productive manner.

No company that ignores customer views, or refuses to hear them, is going to continue to succeed. In order to take customer views into account, you have to get them somehow. Certainly there should be more polling in the game, but even that has problems. E.g., most of the controversy in the game lately concerns post-50 play. But if you put relevant questions on in-game or other polls, you are not going to be able to sort out the responses of people who have actually played post-50 from the people who just think, for some reason, that they can predict what's it's going to be like. That really almost eliminates the usefulness of the poll. Certainly some matters could be covered in polls, but I think most of the "burning" issues really are not suitable for polls ... better to see what people are saying and gauge whether their statements are reasonable, well thought out, and fact-based.

Anyway, I ramble ...
