In alphabetical order:

Bards: odd coincidence that I start with bards, since they are the real jack-of-all-trades class in EQ. I didn't expect them to be the best at anything -- but they are truly unique with their full-group manaless buffs. I've heard that they are critical for successful raids against, for example, Vox, who can not only AoE fear and AoE ice dd, but can also AoE dispell the buffs you put up to protect yourself -- except for the bard buffs, which come back with the next pulse of the song. Some server recently split, losing a lot of their higher-level characters in the process, and I was absolutely shocked to hear that they had a successful Vox raid with just one level 40 bard. Other than that, they are the best at being versatile -- which makes them the most difficult class to play well.

Clerics: the best heals, the best HP/AC buffs, the best resurrections. Gotta love them. Even against undead, they do not actually have the best dots or dd; shaman and wizards still outgun them, just not by much.

Druids: the best multi-mob outdoor soloers because they have the best snare; arguably the best teleports. One of the most popular choices when people make characters on a new server to do the "race to 50", and for good reason, so I may as well call them the best levelers.

Enchanters: they're another complex class, up there with bards, so I'm not sure I understand them yet. They're the best at manipulating their faction, are the best at crowd control (mesmerization), are the best charmers, have the best attack speed buffs, the best mental stat buffs and debuffs, are the best at controlling hate lists, have the best damage-absorption spells, and are the only ones who can enchant metals for use in various trade skills. I don't know if they or bards are the best at increasing mana regen. I also feel like there's something else I'm missing, just because it is such a tricky class. Anyway, they have a lot of "bests".

Magicians: the best damage shields, and the best summoned items (including pets).

Mathematicians: the best at sucking the fun out of the game by over-analyzing it.

Monks: the best at splitting dungeon pulls (with feign death). I do not know how they compare to magician pets for constant melee damage from the front; all I know is that both outdamage warriors by a significant amount at the higher levels. (I suspect that monks do more.)

Necromancers: they are the best single-mob outdoor soloers, with fear/snare/dot/pet; they are also the best at summoning corpses, and the best at arcane transfers (they can move HP, mana, and even experience around). Along with bards and druids, they are one of the best at being versatile (read their spell list sometime, you might be surprised), which is part of what makes them such good soloers yet not the first class that comes to mind when putting together a dungeon group.

Paladins: the best damage-preventing tanks. (It's hard to explain...I wrote an essay on it once, just do a search on my chat handle if you're interested.)

Rangers: the best trackers, the best archers, the best outdoor pullers, and the best taunters. (That last deserves some explanation: as the only class with the taunt skill, fast dual wield, *and* spells to add hate, there is no better class in the game for pulling a mob off a caster ASAP.)

Rogues: the best scouts, the best constant melee damage (as long as the mob keeps its back to them), the best lock pickers, the best trap detectors/disarmers (are there actually any traps that can be disarmed?), the only pocket pickers. (Actually, I don't know. Maybe once necromancer pets get backstab they have the best constant melee damage in the game, as long as the mob keeps its back to them. I'm sure that if I'm wrong about rogues being on top, then someone will correct me.)

Shadowknights: the best soloing tanks (the only one with a pet), the best self-sustaining tanks (nothing beats a lifetap for increasing survivability while speeding up the battle at the same time).

Shaman: the best physical stat buffs; the best physical stat, attack speed, and resistance debuffs; and the best dots.

Warrior: the best gear available, the highest-capped combat skills, the best HP, and arguably the best AC (there is a rumor that nothing over 1120 makes a difference). A straight-up tank: nothing more, nothing less. I consider it the single most specialized class in the game, and along with rogues the least soloable (let's just say there's a reason why those two classes get a small xp bonus ).

Wizards: BOOM! Not only the biggest dd, but also the widest range of types. No one else can finish off a fleeing/gating mob the way a wizard can. Also the only ones who get you into certain planes of existence. Also generally considered to be the ones who actually kill <substitute boss mob, such as Vox, here> while everyone else just tries to keep <substitute boss mob, such as Vox, here>'s attention (or keep the person who has <substitute boss mob, such as Vox, here>'s attention alive) in the meantime. They can actually do quite a few other things; that's just what they're better at than everyone else.

Ok, I'm getting a little silly now since that post took so long to write. I hope it helps someone in choosing the right class for themselves, or in adjusting to their role (which isn't always obvious at the beginning).
