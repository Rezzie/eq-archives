Just a quick post of my 2cp...

- I think banning the sale of items, if it were possible - would improve gameplay.
- I don't think they can ever really stop it from happening, they will simply fragment it into smaller markets.
- VI can put whatever they want in their EULA, and ban any account for any reason, so complaining about that seems rather pointless.
- I am not a lawyer, but the thought has ocurred to me that PLAYERS CAN ONLY SELL THEIR TIME. What I mean by this is that the transfer of an item in game is not transfer of ownership or even really possession. VI still owns the data represented by those accounts and items. You can't sell what you don't own or have. You have to make a pretty large leap to define character possession as personal possession, since by all reasonable terms you can't do anything else with a game item but use it in the game. Even intellectual property has other applications - these items have NONE. Thus, I would add the argument that it is possible only player time has ever been sold by players. If anything they are guilty of misrepresenting their product.
- most are happy that verant is doing trying to stop this (I am one), but in reality the problem exists because of design flaws in both trading and the economics of the game. VI completely controls this experience. I don't think they anticipated this problem, or they would have designed certain things differently. So I just want to offer the perspective that VI has produced a product that is arguably psychoaddictive (ie. FUN to the point of personal neglect), and these people are merely operating within the system created by VI. Furthermore, VI still has the power to change drop types, drop freqencies, the way camping and the way trading is done in the game - remember that they completely control this experience. This 'external' solution is probably not going to work simply because the demand exists. You can't really stop economic laws folks.

So..just my 2cp. Not sure it contributed much to the discussion, but I hope so.
