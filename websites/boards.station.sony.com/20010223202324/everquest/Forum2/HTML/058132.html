Rain you're missing what's really wrong with wizards. It's not our selection of spells, or the purpose of them. Instead it's how we stack up to the rest of the classes in terms of contribution to a group.

Before Kunark it wasn't much of a problem as wizards were pretty well balanced (if not overpowered). However, Kunark weapons have made melee the kings of the 51+ world. Wizards, being the only class that totally relies on direct damage to contribute to a group, are hurt the most by this.

What we wizards really need is either an indirect method of contributing to a group (other than dd) or greater mana efficiency to keep up with melee output. Our snare is fine (seems like you need to learn how to use it), our AEs are fine (especially past 51), our armor spells are as good or better than other casting classes, and our Lures set us apart from the other nuking classes.

Verant's vision on what a wizard is supposed to be is fine - namely dish out magical damage. It's just that they didn't provide the wizard with the appropriate tools for the task after adding Kunark melee weapons to the game.

Let me spell out to you how AE works. Since you seem so skeptical of AE, I'll use actual numbers.

Karnor's Castle. Average mob has around 6k hps. Can pull as many as 20 mobs from the basement at once if you so desire. Since my personal best is only 14 at a time, let's use that.

Group composition was 2 wizards, 1 druid, 2 enchanters, plus a few clerics and tanks. In all we had 2 groups of 5.

When the tank brings 14 mobs, the trick is to have the enchanters AE stun till they are all bunched up together. Then wizards chain Jylls Static Pulse (2s cast, 285 mana, 495 damage) and Jylls Zephyr of Ice (2s cast, 313 mana, 594 damage). The damage is to ALL mobs in a radius around you. That's why it's important to bunch them all up.

Each cast of Static Pulse does up to 6930 damage for 285 mana or a 24 to 1 mana efficiency. Zephyr does up to 8316 damage and has a ratio of almost 27 to 1. Each wizard would have to cast about 7 AEs to kill every mob between the 2 of them and they would still have about 20% mana left.

The trick is to AE stun to keep the mobs off the wizards. Enchies have 3 of these at their disposal so they can easily keep 14 mobs permanently stunned. This particular train was dropped in less than 30 seconds with no casualties. In fact, the only one that took any damage was the puller.

Should try it sometime. Wizard AE is the best in the game by far. And no, it's not useless.

Rainlin, some basic concepts for you:

First, learn the power of the spells you have. Single mob, no pets, the rain spells will generally do as much if not more damage to that mob than your DD spell.

Second, AoE spells work very well, even when NOT in an AoE group, you just need to learn how and when to use them. I've been in many groups, start with a bonds of force, or Atol's Spectral Shackles, wait a little bit, then go in, and use your PB AoE spells, then run back out from the melees. Let the enchanter mezz the mobs again, duck back around, AoE, mezz, repeat. With this method, a group with even a single wizard will take down a set of 6 mobs without much of a problem. Just remember to use a powerful enough AoE to agro the mobs to you.

I've experimented a bit with all of this, and have used it in practice, it works well, and the tanks appreciate not being bored because only one mob is free at one time, without endangering the group.

From what you have said, it's clear you've never worked with a highly experienced group that knows how to work well together. It's not always easy to find, but a well balanced group can do amazing things. Groups that let the wizard use rains(up to 1800 damage from Tears of Druzzil for under 400 mana), and will set up pulls, but then pull the "active mob" away from the mezzed mobs to encourage this sort of activity will give a new appreciation for the wizard class. Groups that will allow a wizard to use AoE also tend to clear mobs VERY VERY VERY quickly. Those that limit themselves to DD spells, and medding, are the players that make people not want to group with them. I've had tanks amazed when they see wizards using our full abilities, just because so few use them.

The fact that you havn't learned how to control agro yet is also obvious. There are times when mezz breaks that it can be up to the wizard to save the group. Over 54 levels, I've made a LOT of friends, just by knowing that sometimes it's better to agro the mobs to yourself and then run, than to stay and have the group die. Paladins do it, there are a few skilled wizards that do it, warriors do it all the time. The trick is knowing when it's time to do massive damage, and then run. Worst case, you save your group by getting yourself killed. Best case is you've done so much damage with your AoE, that the group is able to kill the mobs before YOU get killed. Remember, snare is a form of crowd control too.
