I know it's out of my traditional mindset, but I'll try to think like an exp hog for this post.


Assume a mob gives 1800 EXP, even though you can't really tell how much they really give.


If a person killed it, he would recieve 1800 EXP.

If a person brought 5 random people together and they kill it, he would recieve 360 EXP, as would all people in the group.


Most exp hogs would see this as losing 1440 EXP (4 times the mob's EXP he got) to a bunch of people he doesn't even know. He doesn't think of it in terms of how fast the mob died, or the fact that everyone is gaining exp, or even if it would be quicker to kill 5 of these mobs (hence more than the usual 1800 for him) in the time it takes for him to kill one)

And some people are just anti-social, like Necromancers, keeping company with only the dead..


But everyone has their different opinions. Personally, I like to duo with my friends, knowing that the exp is going to a 'good cause', even if I get less exp overall.

Recently, one of my friends started a Beastlord because she said she liked the ability to solo. The Beastlord got to 15, then I noticed that I had a character at that level (also a beastlord).. I proposed a duo, and then the mobs which normally take me like 5 minutes to kill and rest from, were falling in a minute each (including nessicary downtime) and we were rampaging them.

So, personally, I feel that grouping with friends is the best way you can possibly exp in Everquest.

"assuming 6 people are roughly the same level, isn't it better to get a full group and get the 20 percent exp bonus for grouping than soloing?"

The camps you are talking about have static spawn points with a fixed spawn timer. If a single player can kill all the spawns at the camp, then he gets the full exp. If 6 players group to kill them, then the exp is divided amongst them. Since the spawn rate is fixed the soloist gets more exp than the groupmember, even with the exp bonus. If the grouping bonus is 20% with 6 members, then each gets 20% of the value of the MOB (120%/6 = 20%).

On the other hand, if a group of 6 can find a place where they can hunt continuously, then if they are efficient it is possible for them to earn more exp than a soloist. That just isn't the case at the Gnoll spires, nor in most of the game world.

Yes, maximum range to get experience is grouping with someone 50% higher than your level (with some odd rounding errors). At really low levels, you can always group with someone at least 5 levels higher.

The problem of waiting for spawns (or even just waiting for the puller) is exactly the crux of the problem. If your 1800-pt gnoll spawn every 6 minutes, assuming the soloist takes a couple minutes to kill each spawn, then the soloist gets about 7.5*1800 =13,500 exp pts an hour.

Now even assuming your 6-man group kills very fast, and gets 10 gnolls, they get 10*1800*120%/6 =3,600 exp pts an hour. Yuck!

Similar math shows that to beat a soloist in exp, you need to be chain pulling mobs and killing each one fast. Some well-known locations such as DL, LoIO, or PC offer this. Of course there are others, but I'm not going to give all my secrets away in one post...

The best solo spots are those that have a spawn available as soon as the soloist is done recovering from the last mob, the recovery time isn't excessive, and there is a low chance of death.

Of course, a good dungeon modifier helps either case (*cough* Paludal *cough*)
