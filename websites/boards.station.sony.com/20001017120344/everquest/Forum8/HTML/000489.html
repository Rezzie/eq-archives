I think folks are going way to the extreme on this cultural armor thing, and blowing it way out of proportion. It is no big thing, it will not effect your ability to make it as a smith, make money, and Yak is absolutely correct on this.

It is not that big a deal. Sure I would like the woodelves to have a cultural chain (we are rangers after all), but it is not something that the "culture" would be into. (I have a high level woodelf smith btw).

Cultural armor is based on the culture. Dwarves mine, wood elves live in trees. A wood elf could become a fine smith, but that would be an individual choice-not a cultural thing. The wood elf people live in trees and work with what they have (as a culture). They would use wood, stones, hides. They would employ the bow and arrow made from stones and wood-firing at enemies from the tree tops or ambushing them in the underbrush.

Dwarves live underground, they mine as a culture. They would fight their enemies on the ground, in the open, they wold need heavy metal armors. It only makes sense that they would have developed cultural, finely made armors.

Bottom line here is that it all makes perfect sense. If I want to keep my wood elf smithing, there is no problem. I can make the profitable armors all I want. But I am not born to smithing so I don't have that auto advantage, and my culture does not have in it a specific armor. I can buff my str if need be to make up for that, but I can't change my culture simply because I want to be different anymore then a German can change his culture because he likes spaghetti, or an Italian because he likes sushi.

Its just not in the culture.
