If I could have my wish list to balence SKs (without stepping on the warriors), I would do the following:

1) Add the ability for hybrids to bash / slam interupt unstunable mobs (in order to stop mobs like Trak from gating).

2) Give SKs the level 44 necro mana transfer (in order to allow us to contribute in a small way to downtime reduction).

3) Add 10 to our defense cap, and 10 to our offense cap (keeping us still below warriors, but higher than we are now). Alternately, I would take 12 - 15 to defense and no offense.

4) Reduce casting times of SK spells, sort of like is currently on test, but perhaps less extreme (IE, instead of an across the board reduction, pick out the combat spells that actually need it and reduce to cerca 3 sec, + or - 1, CT).

5) Fix Leachcurse to what it should have been in the first place, and currently is on test--hit every time for the 20 seconds, and get the healing.

I think that, together, these things would balence the SK class nicely.

Numbers 1) and 2) together would make us more needed on raids and on uber mobs, without trying (vainly) to compete with a warrior in the ability to tank said mobs (it's not like we are tissue paper atm anyway).

Number 3) would generally increase our tanking and offensive ability across the board (without stepping on any warrior toes, as our caps are still less than theirs).

Number 4) would address the problem that many of our spells are basicly useless in combat, due to long casting times and interupts taking more melee damage than the spells would do.

(A more limited aproach to CT reduction, however, would keep weird things from happening, like Hybrids being faster casting all spells than their parent classes--IMO, CT reduction should apply to battle spells only.)

Number 5) would make our level 60 disc something worth using, without nearly making it overpowerful.


Taken *together*, I think these would balence the SK. On the other hand, I firmly believe that something like Defensive or unresistable Lifetaps (while I would *love* to have them) would overpower the SK. There are some interesting changes proposed, but I think there are some other items to address:

1. Pets. These are still an issue. I must admit that once upon a time I liked the idea of having pets. But now I have joined the other camp. First, they can't be used in so many places. So you put a certain portion of our power into the fact we have one, but we can't use it everywhere. Could we trade the pet for something more universal?
Second, we are fighters, why would we summon a weak fighter for us? Maybe the pet should be a caster!
And finally, it doesn't fit many people's vision of the SK. Everyone has a slightly different take on it, vampire/knights, evil killing machines, etc. I tend to view the SK as a win-at-all-costs, no quarter type character. They will fight dirty, use poison, disease, tap life, fear, use darkness and anything else they can summon to win. And I think they would be loners, not tromping across the countryside with their faithful pet Jabober! So maybe remove pets and replace them with more poison and disease dots, or even maybe a few weak poison DD.

2. Pets, the alternative. Ok, if you insist we keep them, you can buff them, up their power a bit or do whatever you want. The one thing I personally still want to see, and I know a lot of others would as well would be to make them con AT OUR LEVEL. That would allow us to actually summon one in a dungeon without attracting hordes of green trash to us. It would help to make them more universal, and I might add, group-friendly (how many groups let you SKs make a pet in a dungeon, show of hands please?).
Other pet classes don't have this problem because they create pets near their level. Only SKs have this problem. So to solve it, when a mob cons our pet, let it see our level instead. Or if you feel that makes it too easy, at least make our pet con at the level we get the spell.

3. Archery. I don't know about others, but I really don't care about this skill. It is class defining for rangers, for Paladins it is good for pulling, but we have spells for pulling. What good does archery do me? And I really don't see a dark knight with a bow. Maybe you could remove this skill entirely and give us something more in line with our role, like improve disarm (make it useable), or one of the many shield-based skill proposals.

4. Skeletal form. Nice idea, but I still like the idea for SKs of a look more like a spectral. It would be a new look, so not infringing on any other class's look. Call it Phantom Form or something like that. With effects similar to the enchanter werewolf form (ie. VE as part of the form). It would be nice to still look like yourself, but in a semi-transparent, spectral sort of way.

5. Casting times. I am glad we are finally getting some faster casting, but it comes very late and seems too little. Could we maybe have a skill/discipline to make us fast casters? Since we have a small selection of lower powered spells to choose from, is it unreasonable to assume that we may learn to cast them quickly since we use them all the time? I cast spells necros haven't used for a long time. Maybe my long use of them makes me better at casting them? I can see this is the line of reasoning you are getting at with the change, I would just introduce it earlier (maybe starting at 39) and ramping it up to maybe 50% at 60 instead of 30% (making our longest spell a 3 second cast).

6. Spell upgrades. Things like VE at early levels don't last long. For most classes, self buffs are usually upgraded every so often with longer lasting versions. Could we either have an "upgrade" to some lower spells like siphon strength, VE, etc for longer duration, and maybe greater effect, or at least have their duration scaled with our level (eg. VE lasts 2 minutes at the level it is acquired, but lasts 20 minutes at level 60). It's just annoying to have to mem spell, cast, unmem all the time.

7. Self buffs. This one I find a little annoying. Don't get me wrong, "draining" AC, ATK, etc is nice. Makes me stonger, and makes the mob weaker for everyone attacking it. However in practice I don't have the mana and open spell slots to cast all these spells all the time. So they end up getting used as a SELF-BUFF. So, I shadow vortex, siphon strength, etc on the first mob, and don't bother with subsequent ones, saving my mana for heart flutter, heat blood, etc. It might help if these are made proc effects (like VE), or just a buff, or the mana cost decreased.

8. Summon Corpse. Why shouldn't we have this spell? We can locate corpses, we are supposed to have special knowledge about the dead. If I can find your corpse why can't I summon it? I don't see this as overpowering or anything, and the cost and weight of components means we won't be running around offering summon services.
The comparison to the Paladin situation is correct. Giving them res didn't infringe on Cleric ground. And they even get an upgrade, we don't get the improved spell (though that would be nice).

9. Piercing. Yes, raise it with the other skills. I don't understand why this is lower, and in fact I do use daggers from time to time.

10. Damage. I know I'm not the only one wanting to do more damage in melee. If that just isn't in the cards, then maybe our damage spell lines should be upped and we can switch to being casting tanks.

11. Harm Touch. I like the idea of this being a tap. It would put it in line with our other spells and make it the supreme tap we get. It would also be useful as a last chance spell. If a fight is being lost, you let off HT, which taunts the mob onto you, hurts it a lot, and heals you enough to be able to tank it a bit longer while your party evacs or whatever.
I also think changing the HT discipline is in order. You could call it Death Touch and make it work like the other high level caster insta-kill spells. It would allow you to instantly and irresistably kill ANYTHING (ok, maybe almost everything, might be hard to justify that you can insta-kill Cazic Thule). But the corpse poofs and no experience is gained (and in PvP, none lost).

Absor, I do truly hope you read this far. Anyways, although this post with an SK-only concern, I have posted it here because it really only is a concern on PvP servers. I have been playing an SK from the moment EQ went into Beta 4, and on TZ since the day that server went live... just so as you read the following you can know its an informed opinion.

HARM TOUCH

First, I am the first to agree that the reduction in PvP damage via HT is a GOOD thing in general. HT had become too powerful, expecially at higher levels. Circumstances had flooded in of Lvl 60 SKs being able to "auto-kill" casters. Harm touch in essence became Death touch. The 12% decrease (from prior to the patch) in HT damage in PvP still allows HT to be as viable in PvP as it was before the damaged was upped, while allowing it to be more useful still in PvE.

However, the change I do feel needs to be looked at it the fact that HT will no longer respawn upon death. In my (almost) 2-years of playing EQ, and since TZ has opened, I perhaps have heard, let alone seen, of maybe only 2-3 incidents where SKs bind-rushing another and HTing them was a problem. However, I am willing to accept that some (albeit it a huge minority) may feel it is a problem. Below I will make some points as to why I feel it is LESS of a problem than it is made to be on PvP-servers, and finally, a simple compromise that could be integrated with no large coding changes (I am assuming here, but once you see the solution I think you can agree its not a major change).

1. A typical Lvl 50 SK HT will do approximately 500 pts damage (rough estimation) in PvP. If they use it, are killed in that battle, they return to their bind-point and must wait 72 mins (I belive this is the refresh time... never actually had the patience to check it in-game) before it comes back. However, a Lvl 50 wizard, killed in PvP returns to his bind-point with no mana. How long does it take for that same wizard to meditate enough to be able to cast a 500 pt DD? 5-10 mins? And that is being generous I would assume.

2. Seeing as how SKs, being melees, cannot currently bind anywhere that does not have a "city" flag (i.e. Neriak, CoM, GF), while casters can bind almost anywhere, doesn't this make caster bind-rushing still more viable currently than SK bind-rushing? This leads to point 3...

3. When an SK uses his HT in PvP, and then dies during that encounter, he now must wait upwards of 72 mins before he is at full fighting capability. HT IS a very important part of an SKs arsenal in PvP. A caster on the other hand is at full strength againonce all their mana is back... say, approximately 15 mins. So, for over an hour after a PvP death in which the SK used his HT, he is penealized for that encounter. In all subsequent PvP encounters during this time he is far from able to function at full capability. No other class, not even paladins, have this restriction placed on them

4. Previously an SK could know that in any PvP battle he could use his HT (provided it was up), even if the encounter was in doubt, because on death it would refresh, and therefore in any other PvP encounter following this death he would not be at a severe disadvantage, even though he had died in the first battle. Now, the situation is essentially that you MUST be sure your HT will kill your opponent, or change the outcome of the battle. Why? To use it and still die means you have essentially wasted your HT, since it will not refresh for 72 mins, even after dying. Therefore, in all subsequent PvP encounters during the period the SK is at a severe disadvantage.

SOLUTION:

Upon death, if the SK has previously used his HT, and it has not yet refreshed, instead of having HT refresh immediately (as prior to the latest patch), have a 10 or 15 min timer start, at the end of which the HT will refresh.

This means that no SK can use his HT for bind-rushing, since those who killed him have at least a 10-15 min repreve. This seems consistent with the fact that a caster who returns to his bind-point with no mana after a PvP death need only med for 10-15 mins to be at full functionality, and cause considerably more damage than an SKs HT.

This solution also means that an SK may use his HT in PvP without being concerned that if he were to die in that encounter, he would be at a disdvantage for quite some time even following retrieving his corpse.

Its a good compromise. Ask any questions you may have about the reasoning Absor, I know I kind of began to ramble.
