I would propose a change so that Rangers only had the opportunity to use archery as a viable ranged combat system. Vanidar_Monnwhisper,
While I agree with you Rangers need more HP (and arguably this is more important than AC at the level you are referring to), I think you are looking at one very specific example and focusing in to tightly on it. Velious mobs and raid mobs is really an entirely different subject. There is no question that HP is all that matters on these types of encounters (this is why low AC resist gear is so critical on Dragon Raids). However, day to day grouping tells us that 25-50 AC DOES make a significant difference in both taking less hits and getting hit less hard. Right now Rangers are below the "break line" of Armor Class for their level. Above the "break line" you take significantly fewer "max hits" than below that line (attack vs. AC). It is excruciatingly difficult for Rangers to get above this "break line", having to get the top of the line equipment at every available slot.

Basically, HP is needed, however so is Defense (and dodge/parry/riposte as far as I'm concerned). Not to be able to tank (we still wouldn't be able to do that), but rather to allow us to fill our role as a "damage dealer". We can't deal damage if we're forced out of melee when agroed.

Lowering jolt to a more reasonable level really doesn't solve the problem by itself either (yes it needs to be lowered), since one of a ranger's true value to a group is to be able to get a mob off a caster in short order (can really save a group). If a ranger pulls off a caster and then immediately has to jolt to keep from getting killed himself, the mob is right back on the caster. As it is now, if he continues to tank until the warrior/sk/paly can get it off, he's usually dead.

Only a combination of more Defense (AC), HP, AND lowering jolt will allow the ranger to fulfill his role as a true "damage dealer". The only other option would be more effective archery, but frankly I think rangers melee skills mixed with a substantive increase in archery would be out of balance.

Many great thoughts have been posted already and I'm very proud to be a part of the Ranger community. Here are a few other things that I'd like considered:

* Tuning of the Ranger (and others') Epic Quests. There seem to be two types of quests: ones that are bottle-necked by highly desired spawns (for other reasons) with long spawn intervals (Rangers, Druids, Wizards, etc.) and the others that are more based on triggered encounters (Clerics, Monks, Rogues, etc.).

There are other issues about the inequality of the epic quests such as difficulty (Rogue on the easy side, Mage on the hard) and usefulness of "intermediate" items that result from doing the quest (Rogues get Ravenscale Mask, etc. Shamen get some nice items), but those are Ok and I am happy for those classes that benefit.

What is most irksome is that for several quests the necessary components drop from highly desired, perma-camped-as-soon-as-the-patch-is-over spawns with ridiculously long spawn intervals. The two Ranger Epic gates are Venril Sathir and Innoruuk. Mix in the fact that Druids (THE most populous class in the game, with Rangers coming in a respectable third or fourth) SHARE Venril Sathir and a change is needed. Upping the drop of VS to two stones was a GREAT idea, but not enough.

Please consider replacing at least the VS, and possibly the Innoruuk, epic encounters with triggered encounters. The triggered encounters could be VS or Innoruuk, but with them ONLY dropping the needed quest component (and in the case of VS, only ONE of the stones, so that you don't have two stones for every quest completed to that point). That'd be great!

I envy my Monk, Rogue, and Cleric brothers and sisters, among others, who spend weeks planning a raid, gather up all their friends, show up at the ordained time, start the encounter, and have one HELLUVA fight! No stress on beating other guilds to the punch. Or taking off from work to be home when the servers come up. Just worry about the opponent and how to get the resources to beat it.

That is what the Epic quests are about. Not getting lucky and getting the resources and timing right to take down the opponent. But about drawing upon fifty-plus seasons of sweating and bleeding with your friends to bring them all together for one final series of battles. The struggle should not be about finding time off from work, or getting lucky on the patch, but on making friends, strengthening the community, and then being able to draw upon that to accomplish and epic task.

* Jolt lowered in terms of casting cost, resist rate, and level. Forty-nine to fifty-one seems to be a more appropriate season to acquire it, that is when the troubles really begin.

* Ranger-only Tracking changes go in. I hope they are still on the table!

* A watered down version of Charm Animal. One that allows us to Charm an animal and have it follow us, etc. but does NOT attack on our behalf nor defend. I know this will appear to some to be a "waste", but I ache for a Charm Animal that let's me befriend a bear or somesuch and have her as a companion while I am in the zone for roleplaying reasons. * Gate or self-ports.
* Group ports or evacs.
* Please don't remove Hide and/or Sneak from indoors use. They have their uses. You can have Disarm, though.
