I am sure there is many who will give you this advice.
1) Keep everyone off the walls - you will agro other rooms if you touch them.
2) Only let the main tank the puller and the enchanter(s) near the first arch in break in room.Everyone else stay right back and out of the way.
3) yes all invis on port up BUT it is not vital.You will not agro if you do not move around, but if someone does agro ( and some clown often will) then invis will help you.Just do not attack and stay invis untill you have full force and you can rez anyone who died on port up.
4) the first pull can often bring a few through the walls and so on but if you can hold this little rush first off you will be fine.

Try to find a puller who knows the zone.This helps a lot...even more than you might imagine.

When you look out the door of the break in room you will see a corner ahead of you where it turns left.Often a monk will pull to there and then the tank can tag off them.Safer than having the monk come back and maybe just maybe having agro from many pathing crazily around the zone coming to say hello.

Do not have people raise their height in any way..ie climbing onto chairs or each other...you have an upstairs that can and will agro you through the floor/ceiling.They may hit you but not be able to path to you....yet.Once you get into pathing distance they and all their friends will come to say hello.

Many more tips will be posted I am sure but I think the most important one is to STAY OFF THE WALLS.

And of course have fun.....

Aye, Maestro's AE is pretty much a non factor, just be sure you have a very solid tank to take his melee, and make sure it's one that can hold the aggro. Otherwise, make sure your healers are sharp and sledge him under, though a mostly sub-50 raid will probably fail him.

Innoruuk is much the same, few hints, though. His AE is a grav flux + knockback, which means you must fight him in his building, preferably at or very near his spawn point. Innoruuk is a wizard with a full spell book, and he will gate as well as cast spells such as Garrison's Sundering and that bloody 500-3k nuke. Make sure that your MT is capable of tanking a mob that hits mid 500s while casting those big blams unbuffed, because Innoruuk procs a HUGE dispel on his target. Pre-POP, a 3500 HP SK and a 3600ish HP Paladin were able to tank him pretty easily, switching back and forth due to aggro fluctuations from Grav Flux. Otherwise, Innoruuk is a pretty straight forwards Velious Era fight: Mediocre melee from the mob, and a quarter million HP to chew through.

Your force sounds like a young 50's to mid 50's group, so my advice for break would be:

Zone up one group at a time invis. Be sure to have someone invis the wizard (will detail how to do that below) The groups take a right turn out of the entrance house and run straight to the wall. Run MIDWAY up the wall. DO NOT get near the ceiling and DO NOT walk along the floor. Head north, go all the way around the wall until you're in the "safe spot" which is shortly before the southwest corner. Camp to an alt.

When all who made it are camped at the safe spot, log back in. At this point you need a rogue to go fetch the bodies of those who died along the way (there are some see invis mobs, and someone invariably aggros maestro) If you do not have a rogue to do this, the corpse run can be done by a monk, but it's much more tedious and requires a lot of /q or calling.

To invis a wizard for porting:

Have at least one other class who can cast invis in the group. Caster starts to cast invis, wizard starts to cast port, invis lands on wizard while they are casting (it won't break invis) caster then invises themselves while port is casting.

The other way to "break" hate involves porting up one to two strong groups to basically fight and pull at the entrance building while all the groups port up. This is VERY difficult for lower level raids as it's not unheard of to have 5-10 mobs at a time. For higher level raids, this break is much faster than the invis method. Just clear everything around zone in building, head to the east wall, go south, and follow around to the safe spot.

I would strongly suggest against a 55 ranger tanking in Plane of Hate unless you are VERY well equipped.

With a group of low to mid 50's who are not well versed in the plane expect to die, maybe even completely wipe. Expect a LONG raid, and potentially a LONG time sitting on your butts naked somewhere trying to figure out how to get your bodies back. I'm not saying you'll definitely wipe, but don't be surprised if you do.
