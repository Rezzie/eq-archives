Greetings, all!

Since sometime last week (no real fix on a day, so I can't plame a particular patch) I've noticed a peculiar thing with one of my mage spells when in an LDoN.

The spell is Cinder Bolt (although I had the same thing happen with an earlier version of the same spell) and it goes like this. With full mana, a clear target, and not being attacked or interrupted in any way, I cast the spell. The spell bar appears, the countdown runs through, I see the "You begin to cast a spell" in the dialog box, and I burn the mana--but absolutely nothing happens. No resist message, no "You have been interrupted" no partial damage listed, no target has moved out of range message--nothing. It's as though the spell ceased to exist somewhere between me and the target.

To make it worse, it doesn't happen every time--perhaps once in every five casts--but that spell is the big gun for my poor mage, it costs a (relatively) big slice of mana, and in critical situations when the target just absolutely has to die RIGHT THEN it can be the difference between life and death for everyone concerned.

Such being the case, you can imagine that it's frustrating to respawn naked at your bind point after having judged a situation correctly, targetted, and cast all in good time, only to have your spell vanish into the Twilight Zone.

And before anyone asks, yes I did /bug it more than once, when it became apparent that it wasn't a one time only thing.

When did druids get mage spells???

This is the well known LOS problem. A low pile of sand, a group mates elbow, just about anything can block a bolt. Yeah, its a pain.

Sometimes, you can hit if you can see the midpoint of the mob's body. Sometimes, you can't hit if the mobs feet are partially hidden from view. Bolt LOS is not equal to visual LOS. Yeah, its a big pain!

Also, if the mob is pushed up against the wall, bolt spells (and arrows) won't land even if LOS is absolutly crystal clear.

In LDON, I rarely bother with bolts. You are indoors, its crowded, melees tend to move into the way, mobs get pushed into the walls. As you point out, it is a lot of mana to spend and have a chance for a LOS to make it do zero damage, not to mention the wasted casting time when you could have been medding or casting something with a better chance to hit.

Admit it, the bolt at your level is spectacular because it does a lot of damage, but Blaze is very very close in damage/mana ratio, and has no LOS issue.

Also, use your best "Shock of" spell to interrupt casting mobs.

I don't think you have a good one yet, but rain spells can also be very mana efficient, but only on single pulls!
