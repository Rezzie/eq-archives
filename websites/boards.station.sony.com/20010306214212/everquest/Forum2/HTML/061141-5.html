And let's not forget Infocom games.

Ahhh, those were the days....

One of the few nice things about being 30ish is that I got to grow up with video games from their beginning, and I do mean their beginning. Stuff like the original atari home version of pong.

One thing that you will notice about the old video games is that, becuase of the graphics / cpu limitations at the time, they had to focus on ...*gasp*... gameplay.

Some of them were so well designed that they are still great games, even today. I have turned into a collector of sorts for the games I remember the most from the 80s, and I now own, in upright coin operated configuration, the following:

1) Defender (which i modified to play Stargate also)
2) Tempest
3) Joust
4) Asteroids Deluxe
5) Gravitar

One day, I will pickup a Galaga and a Lunar Lander, but I am quickly running out of space now. =)
