Station Member 		posted 01-31-2001 07:27 AM           
When doing a /report you need to scroll up until the text you wish to report is on your screen. Reguardless of the fact that it says "reporting the last 10 lines of text regarding playername", it will send only those 10 lines that are on your screen at the time. This includes emotes, spells, bard songs, shouts, auctions, tells etc. Anytime you have information you think your GM will need to see, it is important that you do this. It can be done once per zone.

Here's what it does (More explaination than Absor's response):

Syntax: /report playername

By doing so you report the contents of your visible chat buffer to a report under the title of the person you /reported.

The next and vital step is to /petition for the GM to actually go look at the report. Reports are stored on a separate computer and therefore the GM must actually go look at the report.. simply issuing the /report command is not enough. Once the GM has looked at it (And yes, specifically GM.. not Apprentice Guide/Guide/Senior Guide).

Once the GM has verified your /report, action can be taken.

If you must report multiple people or more info than what's visible in 1 chat buffer screen then zone and /report again... be sure to tell the GM you reported multiple times.
