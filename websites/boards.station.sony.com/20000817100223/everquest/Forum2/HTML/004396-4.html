Gordon:

It occurs to me you already have most of the code in place to provide an elegant solution to this problem: the factions code.
Each team has a related faction. Killing a member of another team raises my faction with my team. Killing a member of my team lowers my faction with my team. Healing/buffing somebody who kills a member of my team lowers my faction with my team.
Now if six people from Team A find five from Team B and one Team A healer, they can kill them secure in the knowledge that their team faction is okay because they gain a net 4 faction increases (5 increases and one decrease). However that healer can watch his team faction circle the drain when he gets six negative faction hits should his team be victorious.
Associate a proper penalty with not maintaining team faction (such as being KoS in your own team territory) and you've created a negative feedback mechanism for cross-teaming, at least when doing PvP killing.

Now, when suggestions of this sort float over the transom normally you (Verant) pleads it "can't be done." Probably more accurately, it costs more man hours than you want to spend. However if you plan to use the faction system any more in EQ2 than you do now, what better time to start expanding it?
