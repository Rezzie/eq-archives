In DAOC the whole point of restricting communication between realms is to heavily reinforce the view of other realm characters as the enemy - to make their presence so foreign as to incite an assured defense of your castles to any other-realm invasions. There won't be any of the EQ style dark-elves grouping with high elves. DAoC sounds interesting, but frankly there's no way I'm going to commit limited playing time to something that (a) requires pvp to really explore the game, and (b) requires such extensive involvement in an entire realm as a community.

I think the problem with the design process for the current MMORPG industry is that the games are basically being designed by devout, virtually fanatical gamers, who have very strong personal opinions about HOW these kind of games "should" be played. They bring a lot of conceptual baggage in from various other RPG formats, along with prejudices about how other people "ruin" the game, and are determined their game be played the way it's SUPPOSED to be played.

I think that this kind of virtually fanatical devotion to the genre, when one is in charge of conceptualizing and designing a MMORPG world and its rules, is innately problematical, because if the game truly is successful, then 90% of your client base isn't going to BE fanatical gamers devoted to the genre.

Their veiwpoint of what is enjoyable and fun, and what is important to the structure of the game, is going to be radically different than the designer's.

I appreciate the heads-up that EQ2, if there is such a thing, will continue the same PvP/PvE policy as EQ does. I've played EQ since it went live, and frankly I still haven't explored roughly 75% of it. Frankly, I'm pretty sure I'll never get beyond level 51 or 52, and will probably devote more time to sheer exploration in the future than anything else.

I do hope that Verant continues to expand EQ, and in those expansions create lots of content for the 40-60 crowd and quit worrying about creating Ubermobs for Uberguild consumption.

The only way to really improve EQ in GENERAL, IMO, is to work out the "mini" dungeon concept that creates alternate space dungeons or zones for each group that enters them, limiting the event to a single group that could progress linearly through the dungeon without interfering with, or getting interference from, anyone else.

IF the best content in the game were tied into SINGLE group successes in mini-zones (where you can't just throw more bodies at a creature or chain-rez armies of the dead), then I'd say EQ had the perfect game for just about every style of play and didn't arbitrarily code casual players out of the end-game.

Rigadoon,

I think it would be very easy for verant to go to a 100-level system .. .however, I think that the nature of levels should be changed after level 60.

I think that it would be nice if they could code it so that Players could elect from a variety of permanent stat increases (via quests or special trainers), or choose certain avenues of special skills only available after level 60, it would generate a lot of interest.

For example ... no new spells available, no automatic HP or Mana pool increases at each level. Instead:

At 60 perhaps each class could choose to learn a new skill from a set of options. At 70 that skill branched off to 2 exclusive variations of that skill. At 80 it again branched off to 2 further specialized, exculsive variations. At 90 it did so again. At 100 you would be the master of a very specialized version of that skill.

An example:

At 60 a Cleric can learn the skill "Prayer", among other exclusive choices (let's say then can also elect to learn advanced 1hb or 2hb combat skills, but must choose BETWEEN "prayer" and "blunt weapons"). This allows the cleric the ability to regen mana faster than normal meditation.

At 70 the cleric must choose between "Devotion" and "Clergy" forms of prayer. One a self-only furtherance of that skill, the other turns the prayer into a form of "group clarity".


Let's say that the cleric chose "Clergy". At 80 the cleric must then choose between "Ministry of Healing" and "Ministry of Protection". Ministry of Healing adds a constant group healing to the clarity effect, while slightly increasing the power of the "group clarity". So now the cleric is increasing mana regen and group HP regeneration at the same time.

The other route, Ministry of Protection, would create generate an group AC bonus as well as increasing the group clarity effect.

Let's say the choice is to train in Ministry of Protection. At 90 the cleric must choose Congregation of Purity, which adds across the board resistances to the Ministry of Protection effect, or Congregation of Defiance, which adds group HP buff to the Ministry of Protection effect.

Let's say the choice here is Congregation of Purity. At 100 you max out your CoP skill. When you press it, you group gets a mana free 30 minute mana regen boost, AC and across the board resistance buffs. The re-use time is, let's say, once an EQ day.

Remember, the Cleric COULD have elected to learn blunt combat skills INSTEAD, and that choice would have led to equal specializations (perhaps choice of procs on weapons, ability to double attack with a blunt weapon, etc.).

This would provide a real reason to advance into the 60+ levels without dramatically having to alter gameplay. Verant could simply make higher-level content ... but anyone moving into the 60-100 range SHOULD experience an "easier" game. They should just be relationally more powerful.

This way would allow for real, solid diversity in the game and a whole new reason to advance your character.
