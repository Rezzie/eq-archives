quote:

-I bet for every low/mid level dungeon you can name, I can name 2-3 50+ dungeons.
-I bet for every low/mid level dungeon you can name, I can name 2-3 low/mid level outdoor zones.

There are lots of zones in this game. There are several in very level range. On a comparative basis, the low/mid level range definitely has fewer dungeons than the upper level ranges.

Even the definition of "dungeon" is an iffy thing. Is Crushbone a dungeon? It's outdoors, isn't it? Is the Zone Exp Modifier in Crushbone any better than the ZEM in Gfay? Just cuz it may *seem* to be better doesn't mean it definitely *is* better.

Is Unrest a dungeon? It too is outdoors.

Are dungeons strictly "constructed" (e.g. Befallen), or can they be natural as well (e.g. Paludal Caverns)?

quote:
They definitely need to stop adding zones, and make the ones that are available more usable.

There is this common misconception that software development employees are just one big pool of workers. People seem to think they move from task to task like the average Joe worker at a fast food counter. This could not possibly be farther from the truth.

The "expansion pack" team is extremely unlikely to be re-assigned to the "maintenance" staff. If they aren't re-assigned, then what do you do with them when the expansion pack is released? You make them create another expansion pack! That's what you hired them to do, isn't it?

quote:
Seriously though, the problem I've seen in "everquest" for four years is that it ISN'T. Its a misnomer.

I've often referred to this game as "Neverquest". The current philosophy among the creative staff is that quests will be abused if the players are given better quest rewards. I groan everytime a level 40 mob drops a level 40 piece of equipment that is immediately usable. I groan when a level 40 mob drops a trade skill component that is used to make a trade skill item that a level 20 *might* use. I groan when a level 40 mob drops a quest item that is only 1 of 12 pieces needed to obtain an item a level 10 probably would never use.

Can quests be abused? Sure, but farming mobs for ready-made gear is an abuse that is even worse for the game.
