In Highkeep (at least on the Tribunal server), there is a "list" system. It's a system that's in use elsewhere, but for those unfamiliar with it, I'll explain.

There's three main "camps" in HK: The guard room, the war room, and the raider room. The group leader in each of those rooms is responsible for keeping a list. When you enter the zone, you can ask "Who's keeping the warrior room list?" and eventually you'll get an answer (as long as the war group isn't fighting at the time, of course). The leader of that group will put you on the list. First come, first serve. The person who's first on the list gets the first open slot in the group. So as one player leaves the group, the next one on the list fills the slot that's vacant.

That accomplishes several things:

#1, It keeps the hunting areas from being horribly overcrowded. It ensures that only one group at a time is competing for a particular room.

#2, It provides a method of ensuring a full group which is utterly necessary since those are difficult spawns.

#3, It provides a method for sharing, albiet in a line-up-in-the-queue sort of way.

HOWEVER, under the PnP rules, some yahoo could walk into the zone, go to the warrior room, and demand to have access to the war spawn right then and there, bypassing those who are courteous enough to get on the list and wait their turn. They could invoke a GM to come and force people who are voluntarily cooperating to yeild to someone who is NOT willing to cooperate with the rest of the players. In this sort of situation, the PnP actually promotes antisocial behavior rather than encouraging it.

The PnP policy fails over and over again to do what it was supposedly desgined to do. That's why I suggested an alternative to the PnP in one of my other posts. 99.9% of the players who want to see the PnP abolished do so because they want to see things made MORE fair, not less fair.

Just to put things in context, here is the Play Nice rule (#3) from http://everquest.station.sony.com/s_conduct.html

-----
There are cases where two or more groups wish to kill the same thing. In these cases, the groups are required to compromise.
-----

There is more, but this is the gist of it. Abashi has also observed that he is open to suggestions so long as they do not require additional GM staff. Clearly, the overall goal from Verant is to create an environment which is primarily self-governing. All they want is for us to be able to reach a compromise-- they are NOT saying that anyone can walk up to a spawn and take it (that is well covered by rule #1.)

There are many in-game compromises going on. Some of the popular ones are the group member rotation, waiting lists, spawn alternation, rolling for spawns, etc. If someone comes up and says you MUST do XXX because of the Play Nice rules, they are wrong. All you have to do is reach a compromise. Refer them back to the website or quote the rule verbatim while offering to reach a compromise.

Now, negotiation can be difficult, particularly if the other person has the advantage in level, firepower, friends, etc. Running to the GM is clearly a final option given the reqirement to compromise. (If I were the GM, I'd be seriously inclined to just boot both parties out of the zone for each dispute unless one party were clearly willing to compromise and one clearly was not.) Let the OTHER guy call the GM-- you can say "I was trying to compromise with him, and then you showed up." NOT calling the GM shows that you are trying to live up to the Play Nice rules. Be prepared to continue negotiations and show how you are willing to compromise.

If other people in the zone have spoken with you and already reached a compromise (e.g. the waiting list) encourage them to speak up and show how they already reached a compromise with you.

It's all dictated by the number of players at the spawn and the spawn rate. Seems to me that is the way the game was designed. Let me explain, as it's rather simple:

Rotation system:

There are 10 people at a spawn waiting to fight. There is an informal "list". The person who has been there the longest is next up when the spawn pops. The person who came last goes last. Therefore, by the time 10 creatures have spawned everyone has had a shot at one kill. If more or less people show up the list is adjusted. No one is told to go away. No one owns the camp. It's a dynamic, absolutely fair way to play the game. The last person to arrive is always last in line. Why do you powercampers not understand that? No one is stealing your camp. They're only getting their fair shot. It's just like queing up at a movie theater.

Instead, you have people who want to own a camp or create these exclusive lists. This is more like a nightclub. They'll let a certain amount of people in at a time, depending on whether or not someone leaves. If it's busy you'll have to wait a long time to get in, and you're at the whims of the other people playing. If no one leaves you can sit there all night waiting and you won't get in.

Which systems seems more fair to you??
