I have 3 Incoming macro buttons.

1) Incoming! %t
2) Please assist me on %t
3) Add %t

The idea, is that I like to be in control of pulling (not just getting jumped on), and I like to keep the group informed (communication is key).

I am scouting. I want to pull a mob, looks like 2 others will follow... target the expected "adds" and press my ADD button. Then target the target I think we should kill first (a good puller will know which mob should go down first based on the group dynamic and ability), and press the ASSTME button.

Group hears:

"ADD A Dar Ghoul Knight"
"ADD A Dar Ghoul Knight"
"Please assist me on A Reanimated Hand"

THEN after I press those 3 buttons (takes but a second to target and press the macro button) I pull the last targeted mob (the first to die... hand in the above example).

Every person in the group knows exactly what to expect (or at least what I expect).

Pulling is a very fun part of the game... there is much responsibility on the puller's shoulders. A bad pull can kill the entire party.

Good communication, knowledge of what is being pulled, and an idea of your groups strengths and weakenesses are essential in good pulling.

NOW, for the exp group pulling in DL or OT... any INC line will work... some just get VERY annoying when you hear it the every 50 seconds.

I always found that the easiest-to-read and most informative incoming message was something like this:

--== INCOMING: %t ==--

It's not a clever phrase, so it doesn't get old; and it's really easy to read because the mob name is easy to parse out visually. The --== and ==-- make it easy to pick out of all your other messages (especially if you have a chatty group), the INCOMING makes it clear what the message is about. I've been trying to come up with one that's even better than this.

I think it would make everyone's lives better if we all used some variation on this instead of cutesy messages that get old real quick. Unless you're clever enough to be changing your pull message every 5 minutes...

This was another one I thought of that is even easier to read.

>>> INC:::: %d :::: <<<

- Teramenor
