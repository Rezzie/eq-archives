Your contention that whomever gets there first should own the spawn until they leave, Egodonor, is simply stupid. Verant knows this: hence the Play Nice policy. Think about it.

1. EQ is played around the world. Should prime-time players in Japan have a harder time getting good spawns because the late-night US players are holding them all?

2. EQ is played by people with widely varying amounts of time available for EQ. The spawn policy needs to be fair for people who play for a couple hours a night as well as those who play for 16.

For a spawn management policy to be fair, it needs to meet at least two requirements:

1. It is not biased toward people who play at any particular time.

2. It is not biased toward people who play different amounts of time. Two characters with approximately the same /played time should have had approximately equal chances at things.

The "Play Nice" policy accomplishes this. The "I was here first, so f**k off" policy you advocate does not.

"Play Nice" is not perfect, but it is better than any policy that does not satisfy the two properties I list above. Verant could make it better by making all spawns random, so that your chances of getting a given rare drop off a known spawn depend *entirely* on the number of times you kill it...then "Play Nice" is fair to everyone.

KidF: <I>how list camping is any different from guild camping, aside from who gets to make the list</I>

They are fundamentally different. They are different in such an obvious way, I can't believe you have bothered to repeat this point, as if it was your primary argument against a list policy. A guild list is closed; i.e., not open to the public. The standard list policy is open. Everyone gains access through the same method. Then, when they get access, they have to <B>share</B> with five other people. BTW, to demonstrate that I am adequately qualified to use share in a sentence, and know what it means, I will cite Merriam-Webster: "to divide and distribute in shares; to partake of, use, experience, occupy, or enjoy with others; to have in common; to grant or give a share in; to have a share; to apportion and take shares of something".

If a guild continually puts new people into the group exclusively from their own guild, never letting Player X get access to some uber XP spawn, or uber loot dropper, that is wrong. We all agree to this, including Abashi and Verant.

An open invite policy onto a list, first come, first served, is the compromise worked out amongst the vast player community. Hell, I wish lists were used <B>more often</B>. It would be nice to see the ancient crocodile camped by an ever-changing group of 6, with them doing 'order of arrival' split on the gator armor drops. Alot more fair than letting someone sit there for an unspecific number of hours, although most people do have a sense of fiarness, and will acquiesce the spawn once they acquire their desired item if another person is clearly waiting.

A "list to get in" is how the democracy that is the player base has chosen to apportion spawns.
