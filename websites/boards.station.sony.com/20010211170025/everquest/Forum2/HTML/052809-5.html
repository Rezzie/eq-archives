Aha! What Edone wrote above reminded me of something I've been thinking about for a while, and totally forgot until now.

I remember when Crafted Armor was AWESOME. It was the first great class specific quested armor. It was a defining thing for warriors to be decked out in blue. But I always felt that the whole crafted thing was not finished. Where's the crafted mask? Where's the crafted cape, girdle, and collar? How about a crafted shield? (Ew!)

So, here's the idea: make a new set of crafted quests for the "missing pieces." It would be easy and simple to do, just have the dwarf smiths who do resizing also offer to make the newer pieces. Make the quests doable by people in their mid to high thirties, and make the pieces like the original crafted was, really nice stuff!

But wait! There has to be some balance issues here, and that's why I was reminded by the post above about this idea. Warriors are arguably the most equipment-dependant class in the game. There are currently very few "item sinks" in Everquest, and anybody who fails to see THAT as a problem is probably playing on a new server. Anybody remember when the Lanseax of the Wolves was a good two-hander? Every LoW that was ever gotten is still floating around in game somewhere, except when people have gotten tired of the game and deleted a character with all stuff on it.

So, here's the idea. Make the suit of crafted complete, so the class-defining armor is there in full. Then, have the dwarves offer an upgrade path. You have to do a quest quite a bit harder than the original quest, (but still doable by mid to high 40's) which entails a goodly bit of cash, a couple of items from reasonably zesty mobs, and two or more items to turn in, including the original piece of crafted.

For example, you want to upgrade a crafted bracer to the next level, you have to find the next smith, then turn in the crafted bracer, a thousand plat, an old Bone Bladed Claymore, a nice gem, and some mob drop, like say a tottering gorilla arm or something. You get back a nice bracer, call it a Forged Bracer or something. Give it like half again the AC and double the stats of Crafted, make it some cool color.

This helps Warriors do what we like, which is soak up the hits, and it helps the whole game by sopping up some items and cash. If VI decides they need to get rid of different items, they just change the quests so they require different items.

Just a random idea, that kinda sorta fits the thread!

I very often have situations where I'll taunt, mob will turn onto me for a nano second before turning back onto the player that's getting the beatdown. This only happens on 50+ level mobs. My suggestion would be to alter Taunt, say from 55 upwards, so that it adds enough pseudo damage to keep the mob on the warrior until taunt refreshes. This would get countered by the fact that taunt fails every so often. By all means set the raid encounters to be untauntable, but for day to day grouping, the warrior should be able to keep the mob on them for the majority of the battle.

2) Taunt again:
Give us a new discipline that allows us to AE Taunt. One minute duration, an hour refresh or something. Usable as a last ditch save the battle discipline. For that minute, each taunt acts as an AE taunt.

3) Rampage and Flurry:
Ok, this is a wishlist but I'd love to be able to have these disciplines, maybe Flurry at 58 and Rampage at 60. Make them work off the stamina bar in addition to duration/reuse timers. If the warrior is out of exhausted, they won't have the stamina to perform such an action. This would prevent them being used at the end of a long fight without stamina management (invigor line).

4) Epic:
The warrior epic is the only pure melee epic without Haste. Why is this? I can live with the dmg/dly ratio but not every warrior has dragon haste or better. I am two items away from my epic (Maestro's Hand which has eluded me 10 times now, and the Queen's Blade) yet I do not have dragon level Haste.

5) Hitpoints regen:
Would it be too much to double the warrior's hps regen if they are sitting? It takes a long time to heal up after bouncing along Dreadlands if you don't have a regen BP of some sort, and even then it takes a while.

6) Epic again:
Maestro's Hand is too rare. I have had the pleasure of seeing 10 Handless Maestro's in a row and have no reason to go back to Hate other than this Hand. The point of the warrior epic is to lead raids to kill various high level mobs, not to farm a 22 hr spawn that clearly has two hands each and every time he spawns.


I love my warrior, and those changes would simply be the icing on an already delicious cake.

In well over a year as a Warrior a few things have stuck in my mind. I'll just make a list for easier reading.

1) Taunt: I'm a warrior. I'm supposed to take the hits, it's what I am born to do. With a rogue or monk in group with decent weapons, it is hard to out-taunt them due to their low delay and high haste respectively. When the healer is spening more mana on a monk than on me something is very wrong. A monk is not a tank, a rogue is not a tank, a Warrior is a Tank. A more effective taunt would be greatly appreciated by all warriors, and many clerics.

2) Critical Hits/Crippling Blows: When I first saw that wonderful message that I had scored a critical hit, I was ecstatic. It is a great idea. The only problem I see is this, the average critical hit is no better than a decent normal attack. When I see a slew of critical hits no better than my normal attacks I want to throw down my weapon and walk away. This is supposed to be a well placed and crushing blow to the enemy, not a slap in the face to the Warrior. Perhaps adding a damage base equal to the max damage of a normal attack would be the way to go. The hit is not critical if it is no better than a randomly placed strike. Crippling blows follow this. What exactly is a crippling blow that is less than the average attack? It's a crippling blow to the Warrior who has to tolerate the snickers of the hybrids, monks, and rogues. "Hey nice crit/crip! Way to stick it to 'em"......"/growl".

3) AC: Warriors were meant to be the damage absorbers in the game. That's difficult when the average melee has AC equal to your own with similar equipment for the level. Perhaps increasing the base AC of the warrior would return them to that role. No need to change warrior armor, just the heart of the tank.

4) Defensive skills: Why is it that a Warrior with close to double the HP of most other melee dies just as fast? Our defensive skills are not up to par. Warriors get hit. This is our job, but we can't compete with the classes who have less hp, but get hit less often. Dodge/parry/riposte should have higher caps at lower levels, and should max at the highest possible. This is what we do. We stand in teh way. We can't backstab, we can't toss mana, we can't feign death. We stand in the way of a mob and hope we live longer than it does. We have nothing else.

5) Two-Handed weapons: Using a Lamentation at 9 damage, I can hit between 50-60 on a good hit. Using a Felhammer at 34 damage I can manage 160-170 on a good hit. Felhammer is 3.7x the damage of a Lamentation. The damage output is 2.8x of a Lamentation in raw damage per hit, without considering duel weild. This was looked into previously, and the damage was raised, but was it raised enough?

6) Large Race vs Small: The larger races have the ability to Slam, stunning the target, and interrupting spells. This is because of the size of the race, they are just huge. But also, because of their size, could it not be assumed they would also be hit more? I am not saying decrease the defensive skills of the large races, but I am saying an extra skill should be given to warriors of the particularly small races. A natural tendency to avoid hits, due to the diminutive stature. Naturally it would be more difficult to hit a gnome compared to ogre; it's like hitting the bulls-eye versus the wall the board is hung on.
