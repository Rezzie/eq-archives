Let's see if anyone in blue bothers to respond...

Have any of you noticed a major decline in the number of half-elves and humans? Not terribly surprising given their zones.

If I create a half-elf ranger, first, SG is useless so it's off to Qeynos Hills to kill snakes, rats and bats for 5 levels. If I'm an Erudite, well, forget it. That zone is even lamer. Kobolds snakes and skeles and then it's off to the boat.

At that point you pretty much have to go into Blackburrow and find a group because there is no soloing in BB at level 6 if you know what's good for you. After that can go into WK, or maybe Permafrost.

Quests? Well you can turn in gnoll teeth but they are not much for xp after about level 8 and the lightstones are worth even less. WHen you hit the teens you can go after bandits in WK, and that's good cash for the reward items you get. Of course if you get in trouble in WK, don't run to the guards because they won't help.

One problem: both are no-drop items.

Now compare that to Faydwer. I create a high elf enchanter, wood elf druid, wood elf ranger, dwarf paladin or high elf wiz. Thriving newbie zones, and stock up on those bone chips. Run over to Kaladim and turn in the stacks to Gunlock Jure. Ding Ding Ding!

Level 5, head to orc hill. Solo or group there to level 8 or so. Stock up on CB belts, buy em, beg for em, they are tradeable. Back to Kaladim. Ding Ding. Into CB, get some belts and pads, more dings. Then take a boat to Freeport, hit the orc camps, get the DF belts, or buy em. Turn them in to Capt Halloran. Ding Ding!

(now if I know VI and it's usual thought processes, they will make CB belts, DF belts and lego pads NODROP)

Get my point? The western region, Qeynos/BB/Erudin, are terrible zones with nothing for high single/low teen players, no decent turn-in-item quests and the ones they have are no-drop that can be sold or traded. The zones are weak and the xp/loot is anemic.

My point is, the way things are now, there is NO incentive for me to create a half-elf, Erudite or human (especially a human), unless I had friends who were gonna help me, and the first thing they did was get me the hell out of the Qeynos region and over to GFay.

Don't you think there's a reason GFay has 120+ people during prime time when Qeynos has 15?
