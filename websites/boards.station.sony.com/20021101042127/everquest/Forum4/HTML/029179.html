Yeah, satellite would be bad for gaming. Some quick numbers...The satellite is roughly 24,000 miles in space. Radio waves travel at roughly the speed of light, sounds fast, right? Well, from you, to the SAT, back to earth is a 48,000 mile round trip. Then the data has to come back that much again...so 96,000 mile round trip, not to mention all the latency to servers and the data routing thru the 'net. You're looking at roughly a half second of latency just for the data to go from you, to SAT, to earth, to SAT, back to you.

The big issue with satellite access and gaming is with latency.

It takes close to 200 ms for a packet of data to be sent to the satellite, and then back down to your house. This is added time above and beyond normal latency that you would get.

Your best bet is to ping one of the servers using a larger packet size (using the console command ping) and then add 200ms to that time. That is the speed by which you would be playing everquest.

You may also consider checking around locally to see if wireless broadband is available (specifically through the major phone companies). It is a wireless technology that does not use satelite transmission. Instead its mircowave based.

In either event, do not sign a contract with them. The access options for the internet are becoming more and more readily available. If you sign say a 2 year contract, and 6 months down the road they do offer wireless internet access in your area, then it will cost you even more money to switch over.
