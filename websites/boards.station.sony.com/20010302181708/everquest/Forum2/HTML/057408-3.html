My provisory summary would be :

- the mob pathing in the game in whatever form is a feature of the game

- strange forms of pathing (strange meaning that a player wonders why the mob takes this particular trajectory) MIGHT be unintended but become part of the game and therefore implicitely intended when they stay unchanged for an unreasonnably long period of time .

- From the RPG point of view it is perfectly admissible (e.g logical) that a feared mob may run in a wall .

- VI being responsible for the state the game is in , has no justification (legal , gameplay , contractual) to prevent players to use strategies that base on the state the pathing is in .
In other words GMs have no business warning players because of particular strategies using pathings .

Then there is a controversial point if those strategies signify "cheating" .
First cheat whom ?
Not VI because they get the money we pay .
Not the other players because they are not hurt by those strategies .
Not the spirit of the game because there is any amount of strange pathings - sometimes helping the players , sometimes hurting them .

So who is actually left to be "cheated" ?

Seems to me that this strategy is one of thousands where the players use the way the game works in order to have their fun without hurting anybody and therefore are perfectly admissible .
