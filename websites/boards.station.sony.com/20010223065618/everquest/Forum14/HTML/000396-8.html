I'd like to say I'm very pleased with changes so far and appreciate the effort in making this a great game for all.

Here's my feedback. Diving right in,

The need for Something Special: we need a class-defining, community-centered spell, something that others desire. Enchanters give clarity, clerics can res, druids and wizards can teleport, necros give DMF and can summon corpse, mages can Call-of-Hero. SOW fills this role for us at the early levels but then quickly becomes as common as Iron Rations on food vendors. Perhaps add Spirit Essence, a long-lasting SOW and lev combo (and maybe with a neat semi-transparant look). Perhaps alchemy (if fixed) can help fill this role.

Scale of Wolf: Make it faster. Why? Because right now the 15% difference between it and regular SOW is not enough to make up for the time and effort it takes to mem and cast, recast when some other buff (e.g. lev or invis) fades or if we stop to SOW others we meet along the way, and finally re-mem regular SOW and re-cast it at our destination. I'm not thinking bard-speed here, just an increase over regular SOW that's comparable to the increase Jboots give our non-SOWed friends.

Scale of Wolf: Fix it so it only drops when you try to cast or attack. Right now it also drops when a mob casts upon you, and I feel it should not (unless they're casting dispell)

Malaise line: (echoed many times in this thread) either make it much less resistable, or have it check something other than magic resist (disease is a good one). Why? To get around the "I need to land Malaise before I can land Malaise chicken-and-egg problem".

Pox, Bane of Nife, Tigir's: spell-drops too rare. Possibly look at damage:mana raition, currently only 25% better than chain-nuking.

Rage line: I like the way it's working now.

Healing: I love the changes so far! Chloroblast has been a lifesaver (ask the enchanter I group with I think SH would fit better at 49 or 44 given the healing-spells progression up to the 30's, but the reduced fizzle rate and chloroblast has solved the biggest problems. (Thank you!) Whatever you do, I feel that druids and shamen should be very similar in healing power, and wonder why druids get a big heal at 60 and shamen don't.

Pet: I like the changes that have been made, particularly the reduction in mana cost. However it seems to only hit as hard as the 49-pet now, and not sure if this is a bug, bad casting luck, or as intended. I'd like to see the pet be a little stronger. Perhaps make it so you have greater chance of summoning the top pet as you level up. Also, give pet SOW as an innate ability.

Stat buffs: make group versions have same effect as the top single version.

On stat buffs in general: There's been many a cry to make stats mean something. While I too would like to see the effects of buffing become greater, consider that they need to become better at the expense of *something*. The game has been set up for melee at around their current effectiveness. If each buff were to become 10% more effective, a fully buffed melee-type would be 40% more effective than they are now and that might lead to more balance problems than it solves. On the other hand, if you reduce the baseline 20% so the balance swings 20% in each direction, you'll have the melee classes screaming "NERF!". Perhaps the reason the buff question has not been addressed yet might be because it's a very complicated issue, and not that they dont care about us.

Anyway, that's my 2cp for now,

Qali - 59 Shaman of Test
