Tyronnax brings up a good point.

There is some well-earned frustration relating to 888-STATION. I hope that Verant is obtaining call volume information from telco and looking into a solution. It seems clear that:

- The current number of lines is entirely inadequate; busy signals are common.

- The PBX software or hardware is inadequate; calls are not queued, but instead you get a recording which says "we are currently experiencing heavy call volume" and then instructs us to use internet-based resources to resolve issues. Then it hangs up. This is particularly disturbing given that people are often told on the boards and in email that they can only resolve their issue by calling the 888 number.

- The staffing is probably also inadequate. Certainly expanded hours would better server *all* of your customers, not just those in other time zones. I live in the Pacific time zone, but if I needed to call 888-STATION, I would have to do so before work in the morning (and hope it took less than 45 minutes) or during my lunch break. But beyond that, increasing the phone lines and the queueing will not serve more people faster unless there are also more people to answer the calls.

The 888 number seems to be a bottleneck in the current CS system. While I'm sure that telco would love to sell, er, talk to you about solutions, it may be that those solutions have been researched and they are not cost-effective. However, if this is the case, better management of the current system is in order. A couple suggestions:

- Overhaul the Kana database, and add a tag to auto-generated responses that *says* they are auto-generated. It is obvious to those who know that the software exists, but for those that don't it looks like someone doing a half-a**ed job of reading.

- Create a simple "troubleshooter" on the tech support web form. Determine in general what kind of problem people are having, and then prompt them to create/upload the necessary files. Currently, if you fill out this web form, you get:
-- in 24 hours, an automated response, confirming that they recieved your message.
-- in about three days, a request for dxdiag, eqinfo, and dbg files.

If people started off by sending these files, the techs could initiate contact with proposed solutions or more specific requests.

- End the cycle of sending people back and forth between the website and the 888 number. Either change the referral recording to something like "www.everquest.com contains many helpful resources for new players and people experiencing technical problems; it makes a great first stop. Billing questions cannot be handled over the internet."

- Maybe implement a secure system where billing inquiries *can* be handled over the internet.

I can see how the mass negativity on the boards can be perceived as a problem that might be solved simply by getting rid of the boards. For example, I have never had a negative experience with a GM, but I get upset when I read about the arbitrary behavior of some gms. If there were no boards, I wouldnt hear about any of this.

On the other hand, there would still be eq boards not sponsored by verant so I would still be reading about such things and, to the extent that complaints on the board cause problems to be fixed, my minor upsettedness is a small price to pay.

I do think some of the problems could be averted if Verant adopted less of a shoot first ask questions later attitude to complaints and suggestions. I will give two examples.

The massive discussions of what changes each class needed were a good idea. However, verant dropped the ball by implimenting the changes before the discussion was complete. Instead of implimenting changes that way, they should have come back with a list of what changes they proposed to make to get a second round of feedback. Sure some people would have provided non-helpful feedback here, but others would have pointed out problems which could then have been avoided.

A second example is the imbued jewelry. This was poorly thought out and poorly executed. If Verant was planning to put imbued jewelry into the game, why not ask for advice on it? Again, lots of people would have complained about jewelry getting all the attention, but the ideas from other people could have been helpful. Certainly, there is no way that the player community could have come up with ideas for imbued jewelry that were as useless as most of it turned out to be.

In summary, Verant should continue the boards, but make the diologue more focussed on specific issues, respond to the players on those issues, and get the response before taking action rather than after.
