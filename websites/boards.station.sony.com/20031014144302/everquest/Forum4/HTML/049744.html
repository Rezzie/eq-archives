SK vs Warrior

Aggro - SK can out aggro a warrior without any special equipment. With good weapons that both proc and the right spread of AA abilities, a warrior can sometimes steal aggro from a SK if the SK doesn't taunt, cast, use a procking weapon, is AFK and in a different zone.

Offence - SKs have a higher weapon DPS then warriors thanks to the fix made on 2H slashing weapons. Additionally, SKs have a wonderful spread of spells to add to thier total DPS along with a burst DPS that borders on obscene at times.

Defence - Here the warrior has the advantage - with higher base AC, HP and defensive skills along with class based diciplines and AA skills, a warrior can definately take a beating better than a SK.

Solo skills - A warrior usually realises that he can't solo anymore between 20-30th, sometimes a bit higher if he's heavily twinked. A SK usually stops soloing around the low to mid-50s, not because they can't anymore, but simply because he ends up in so many XP groups after a raid finishes.

Group desirability - Warriors are in high demand for groups if they can make good coffee or if there aren't any SKs or paladins around. SKs are prefered for groups since they make excellent MAs, are the best pullers in the game and can also take up the duty of snaring and even debuffing mobs to a degree. Thier DPS is obscene, they hold aggro so well that casters can cast at will, bards can play whatever songs they want and rogues need not fear worse than the occassional wus slap from a mob while they are BSing it.

Raiding - Warriors are still needed for raids - lots of people need coffee, especially during long raids, plus, they make good MAs against boss mobs. Depending on the make up of the raid though, one can often times substitute a SK for the MA since the SK will hold aggro alot better than the warrior and can take the damage dished out by all but a few exceptional mobs.

This is correct - you'll see a SK doing 5k+ harmtouches against anything - not limited just to undead.

Additionally, what most paladins don't like to admit is that the bulk of thier damage dealing offensive spells are limited to undead only. Yes, they've got some really premo undead only spells - HOWEVER - the bulk of the SK offensive spells are usable against anything - SK nukes, dots, etc are not undead limited.

As a result, the SK will have a higher overall DPS due to his spells not being so limited to certain types of mobs - the SK spear line is a fantastic example of this - combine those with our lifetaps and a few dots and a SKs spell damage against any target can match that of a paladin's melee damage.

A paladin might be slightly more useful if you only ever intend to take it into the undead rich environment of LoDN, but if you intend to play in zones other than LoDN, you're ultimately better off playing a SK.
