This problem looks annoying. Hence, fun.

Standard exploits that have to be filled:
Camp and relog
Zone
Trade the item
Put it into a bag and take it out
Change slots
Put it in bank and take it out

Issues:
A player can, theoretically, have over 200 items with recast delays. A player can have no more than 8 spells with recast delays.

Hence, recast delays on objects is orders of magnatude worse than recast delays in general.

Random solutions:

Adding 'item gems', or reusing spell gems as item gems. So, you have to have the item 'ready' to be used, and when you do, you have to wait the recast delay before you can use it.
The item might be able to be used in the 'old' way without putting it in the item gem, but have the long casting time.
Reusing spell gems is unfair to natural casters - it makes warriors better users of magical spellcasting equipment than wizards, which is wierd.
It does keep the problem to a manageable level. All you gotta make sure is that:

When the item leaves the slots that can use it, its gem gets 'unmemed'. Flag the 'cursor' as a valid location in general, but a trade window as invalid.

Voila. Not an elegant solution, but...

If you really wanted to be fun, give item gems out in varying quantities:

4 for melees
6 for hybrids
8 for casters
10 for mages. They summon clickable effects, after all.

Alternatively, you could just restrict melees into using only 2 of their 'spell gems' as item gems, everyone else can use all 8. The advantage of using the item would be the effect would be mana-free.
