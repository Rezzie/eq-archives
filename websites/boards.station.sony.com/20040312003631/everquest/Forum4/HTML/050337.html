If you want close to Greater Faydark, then I would suggest:

-Steamfont up near the Minotaur Caves. Some elementals and minotaurs should still be dark blue to level 15. Lots of room to kite around once you get to know the area. Loot is so-so, but there are vendors right there (Ennixy Frennor) to take it off your hands.

-Dagnor's Cauldron. There are LOTS of wandering mobs for the level 15-20 crowd and *nobody* hunts here. The terrain is rough, so you will probably need levitate as well as your other buffs, but the density of the mobs is rather low so you should be able to avoid adds rather easily once you know the area.

- Unrest. The death beetles in the front courtyard should still be dark blue to a level 15. If not, the Tormented Dead surely will be. These days Unrest is probably seeing very little action, and even if there are a few people, you can still move safely around the back areas by hugging the walls. Outside the house should be lots of mobs to choose from. Kiting around back might be harder than planned, but should still be possible if you work at learning the rules in tight spaces.

-Lesser Faydark. There is a full orc camp right next to the ranger outpost. A druid with Harmony would find single pulling here to be quite easy. Few mobs are social and/or KOS in this zone so kiting should be a non-issue for a druid. The only real dangers are that dang unicorn and the occasional brownie. The unicorn can be avoided with SoW if you're not killed on the first tick, and the brownies can be avoided by staying out of the middle of the zone. The orc camp is near the south wall, so this should not be a problem. Sell at the ranger outpost, or maybe even take a bad pull there too.

-Butcherblock. The chessboard should still provide some good experience and there's lots of players there if you want a duo. I'm not sure if the goblin camps still provide experience, but that would be another possibility. Sorta between the Wayfarer's Camp and the Crossroads should be a bandit camp and an orc camp just north of the road. These spots should definitely be filled with dark blue mobs for ya.

There are some excellent suggestions here for sure. As a druid you need to know that you can root rot or just kite and nuke em down or dot em down or both. A druid could start from nearly no mana and no health and kill a yellow con mob if he wanted to. If you have enough mana for a snare on the mob just run ahead of it and then med while it chases you. Get enough to SoW yourself again to make sure that lasts, then run at right angles and med while it chases you. DOT the mob, reapply snare as needed. Med while it runs at you, heal yourself as mana permits. Keep the mob going in a nice little square somewhere quite and you will kill it.

I did this with yellow con brea giants in Frontier Mountains sometime along the high thirties. Did it in Merus Seru along about the mid 20's.

My favorite place to race through the teens before the PC racetrack opened. My thoughts on PC, as a long time noob... 3 years of playing, four characters in mid 30's. That's always when I tended to get bored and take breaks...

I think PC is a mixed bag. It is by far the best experience through 23 or 24. XP in LDoN is now great, plus the loot is a whole lot better so at 22-24, take your pick. Newbs don't know that the goal is to assist one tank, or that enchanters mez, and not to break mezzes, etc, etc... The easiest place to get into a group and begin to see how the classes interact is now in PC. There are established camps, no trains, and you will also learn at how the community reacts to camp stealing...

Granted, my first character through 25, running across the world, and leveling with the sirens, taking a lovely moonlight stroll through Kithicor for about 25 second, were the most memorable and most fun times. But the problem is it takes 3 months to get to 25 at that rate, and can be very discouraging.
