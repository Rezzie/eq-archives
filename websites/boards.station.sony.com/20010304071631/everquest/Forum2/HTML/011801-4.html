Bard: AE mezzes four MOBs. Oops. He CAN'T. OK, he single-target mezzes four MOBs. Oops, he CAN'T. He can only have THREE spell effects active. OK, He single-target mezzes THREE MOBs. Kenross got it exactly right.

I'm preparing a longer, quantified comparison of Enchanter abilities vs bard songs, to show how they balance at various resist rates. Here are a couple of tidbits:

1. A level 34 enchanter can keep 1 or 2 mobs mesmerized forever, and gain mana in the process, even with resist rates as high as 25%
2. A level 34 enchanter can keep 4 mobs mesmerized for up to 6 minutes before running out of mana, even with resist rates as high as 25%.

That's the baseline for comparison.

Now, suppose Pixie Strike were UNRESISTABLE. Could a bard keep one mob mezzed better than an enchanter? No. At best; he'd be just as good (since the enchanter is perfect). In reality, because the mez effect from Pixie Strike can break early, the mob would still get to attack at whatever its saving throw rate is -- 10% of the time? 5%?

Increase the resist rate to 20%, and now the mob is attacking 15% to 20% of the time, not including extra attacks from song fizzles, bashed bards, etc. So, with no secondary resists and no mana used, the song is STILL significantly weaker than the spell for a given resist rate.

What about 2 mobs? Here, the bard's effectiveness is more than cut in half, but the enchanter is still perfect. No attacks for the enchanter, 2 mobs each attacking 20%-25% of the time for the bard. No contest -- and yet Verant claims that this is overpowered for the bard, but not for the enchanter? (Don't believe my math? I'll post the details later. Oh, and please note -- a bard using pixie strike CANNOT MELEE, because of the way targeting works.)

What about 4 mobs? Enchanter gets hit a few times up front, then is perfect for about 5 minutes, by which time they're all dead or the party has decided to bug out safely. The bard has 2 choices -- Pixie Strike 2 and let the other 2 beat on him (i.e. suicide), or Lullaby.

At a 20% resist rate, Lullaby will result in 4 attacks every 5 rounds on average. Add in bashes and interrupts, and it's more like one attack per round -- equivalent to only mezzing 3 of the 4 mobs. Since no bard can take a beating for as long as 5 minutes from a blue mob, this is clearly significantly less powerful than the enchanter version, even at the same resist rate.

Conclusion: the claim, that a no-mana song would be "obviously" overpowered at the same resist rate as enchanter spells, is fatuous. If you actually do the math, you find that bards would be significantly less effective at crowd control even if (1) pixie strike were unresistable, and (2) Lullaby had the same resist rate as Entrance.

Care to refute this, Gordon?
