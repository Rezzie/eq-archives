quote:
This is kinda the beginning of the wind down for EQ....

I rather doubt it, EQ2 will be a very different game and one that is very much more group-oriented whereas EQ has become much more solo-friendly and there'll always be a viable commercial demand for it. An increasing number of players are sick of grouping and with more games to play and gradual boredom with MMORPGs creeping in they'll become more casual in their playstyle which in itself mitigates against grouping. I agree that expansions may slow down and that if individual server populations fall there will be some mergers but there's no sign of that despite the increased competition already this year.

EQ's biggest problem was always going to be the lack of non-cult zones for grouping. In other words you pretty much have to go to places like Paludal in order to get a group as 90% of the zones don't have more than a player or two in them. I was in Oasis of Marr yesterday, for example, and I was alone. Unthinkable a while back. However, that particular problem has been eased by LDoN although one side-effect of that is of course that the empty zones become even emptier.

Don't forget that EQ could lose half its present playerbase and still be left with twice the playerbase of most other MMORPGs - there's no business sense in running down a game of that level of popularity and SoE are keen to ensure that, with some allowance for crossover, EQ2 will largely appeal to a different audience so that both games can be kept viable.
