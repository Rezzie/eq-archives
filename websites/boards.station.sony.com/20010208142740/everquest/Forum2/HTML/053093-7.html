After reading many of the responses over the last day, there were a few points I wanted to throw my 2 cents in on...

1) Taunt: While an AE Taunt is an interesting concept, I really don't think this necessarily falls under the category of 'needed for balance'. As a few have pointed out, class composition of your group and player abilities do have a significant impact as well.

I do think an enhancement to taunt would be appropriate, however. This should be something a warrior shines in, and while other tank classes have alternate means to lay on the charm, a warrior must rely in great part on equipment. Either a boost to the warrior's taunt factor, or a new skill gained at high level that is inherently better would be a good addition.

2) Attributes: I love the idea that our strength/agility/etc. would actually mean something, and that each race would have their emphasis. For example, an Ogre warrior should always be stronger by far than a Gnome warrior, and this should be clear in battle. Conversely, that Gnome warrior should be faster and much harder to hit than the lumbering ogre counterpart. I don't really see this as a change Verant is likely to make at this time, but I hope they take the concept of diversity to heart for EQ II. A 'simple' suggestion would be to give significant damage bonuses, as well as defensive bonuses based on size and toughness to the bigguns, while adding an inherent (increasing) haste bonus and defensive bonuses based on quickness. I say 'simple' in terms of concept...putting that in and overall balance issues might not be so simple

3) Binding/Travel: I don't feel like warriors need the ability to bind. Additional bind points have already been brought up as a general issue, and I think that would be a good move to help make some areas more desirable to fight in. As Remf mentioned back in the original thread, I do have to wonder why the Locket of Escape was removed...I don't think it could cause any problems (and certainly was a rare item). I think this was a truly rewarding item for melee types.

General travel is an interesting 'problem'. Here's a different perspective, taking Dreadlands as the example. This is a large zone that sees constant traffic; the worst obstacle(s) are the many green mobs (to 50+) that wander throughout. The warrior is the only class that does not possess some natural means of traversing that area safely (or at least getting away with relative ease. Most casters can become invisible, and all will have some means to root/snare/mes/stun an annoying greenie who latches onto their tail. A ranger can invis/snare, a paladin can root/park, a shadowknight can invis/snare/feign, a monk can feign and a rogue can hide/sneak. The warrior must rely on one of those others to facilitate travel, or equipment (jboots being the best example) and they are the only class that falls in this situation.

As has been mentioned (Remf covered it specifically in terms of the greenie horde, and others mentioned related ideas), I would propose an inherent 'respect' effect. Green mobs (with perhaps specific exceptions due to location, etc) would not automatically agro on the passing warrior. I haven't thought this one all the way through, but I really cannot see anything truly unbalancing about this. There are probably other options for this as well (quests for items that would help, etc).

4) Equipment: I think most people agree that the cobalt bracer/helm drop rates are very low. To get more detailed, the Myconid drops in Sebilis (from whom the bracer is obtained) seem completely wacky...gems that drop off level 10-20 mobs seem fairly common, and fine steel weapons seem out of place as well. Please examine this.

It's been mentioned a few times, but I have to agree with the idea that the Cobalt gauntlet's effect is just useless. No one will use it over the boot's effect; if it at least stacked with Strength of the Kunzar, it might be considered 'usable'. Can this be looked at?

Back-slot wearable shields is a great idea, imho...perhaps this could be generally implemented only for specific classes, and would be one way to deal with pleas for more AC.

The Epic Weapon drops, specifically Maestro's hand, the Spiroc blade and the Queen's blade, seem to be the biggest frustration with the quest itself. I'd like to see these re-examined; seeing 9+ Maestro's fail to drop a single hand (as I've read on various boards) seems overly rare.

5) HP and AC: I think the biggest question remains whether or not there is a soft AC cap at 1100. I do think the nature of the warrior should involve the potential to have the most AC and significantly more HP than any other class. The best way to approach this (if Verant agrees), I'm not sure of. Perhaps back-slot wearable shields would be a good answer for AC (and could potentially address defensive issues for other classes, such as Paladin and SK). Perhaps inherent class bonuses. With regards to HP, I'd probably say simply, take a look at a maxed out warrior (equipment and level) and compare with similarly maxed out examples of other classes. If a warrior's HP should be significantly higher than the others, make any changes appropriate to make that so.

That's all for now...Mongo go bash da phone company peepuls now...grrrrrr....

I think a lot of the warriors posting should remember the purpose of this thread...that being to try to balance our class with others. That being said, here are some thoughts:

Kunark Armor
- No other class has basically the "same" effect on two pieces of armor (that being gloves and boots). Give the gloves a different effect which is distinctive like the other classes.
- Cobalt BP effect should have the same casting time as other classes (agan consistency!)
- Drop rates for bracers and helms are a bit too low. I dont know of ANY class which has a more difficult time in getting those pieces. Let's make it somewhat more equal.

Plane of Growth
- BP is the only piece with an effect. How about being consistent with other classes and making another piece with an effect. I like the idea of putting invis on the legs which someone else suggested before.

Warrior Epic
- 100% drop on the Queen in Chardok. Again, consistency with other classes epic items = balance
- Give the weapons inherent haste like the other melee epic weapons (again NOT consistent)
- Make the weapons class defining, give us AC on them or make it proc more with higher taunt value
- Make the red scabbard a useable item...perhaps a waist AC item with haste

Skills
- Raise the cap on Taunt. In most situations I can not out taunt a monk, ranger or rogue and I wield dual jade maces with PoA haste
- Make disarm useful. Currently the only good use is in PvP. An idea is to deequip the weapon vs. dropping it. This would make it useful for those mobs which drop "named" weapons (e.g. Lamentation)
- Raise bind wound cap at higher levels. 250 at level 60 is reasonable.

HP/AC
Contrary to what most people think, Paladins and SK's are very close to warriors in terms of AC and HP. Due to the rarity of cobalt, a lot of Paladins and SK's have equal or in some cases higher AC. A lot of monks also have almost the same or higher AC than I do
- HP and AC is what defines the warrior class. Increase HP by a moderate 5-10% (perhaps a bonus at levels 55 and 60)
- DO NOT increase AC of our gear but rather give warriors an AC bonus at level 55 and 60 which totals between 5-10%

Disciplines
- Disciplines which are shared amongst other classes (e.g. riposte) should have the same duration and reuse time (again consistency!)

Critical Hits/Crippling Blows
- Minimum damage for critical hits should be x2 maximum damage. Average damage currently is less than 1x maximum damage
- Minimum damage for crippling blows should also be x2 or perhaps x2.5 maximum damage
- Increase the rate of crits and crips. Currently these hits are a small blip in our current melee damage. A suggestion to increase this by 10-15% at least (more if the minimum damage will not be increased)

Something also to consider for all the non warriors is the fact that we pay a hefty price for the VERY few advantages we currently have. We can not solo, are EXTREMELY group dependant and have NO ability to escape if things go wrong. To top it off, without equipment, we are NOTHING and completely useless.

The only two problems I see with warriors are:

Taunt - Someone always has a bigger damage spell or a more aggro spell than the damage a warrior can do. End result is the warrior chasing a mob around trying to beat it off every group member one by one. Taunt just does not work in this case.

Bind - When you have to bind in a city and run three or four zones to get to a dungeon and then enter that dungeon for a raid. After the night is over people leave randomly through the night and low and behold you are stuck running back to the city. What a major waste of time.

Proposed Solutions:

Taunt ability - Once every 4hrs game time, have a button that refreshes with an area effect taunt that keeps the mobs aggro'd for 15 secs on the warrior that used the ability. Make this overwrite the hate list until expired.

Take Me Home ability - Once every two days game time, have a button that refreshes that allows a warrior to use that takes them back to thier bind point. You can make this a long delay cast so that it can not be called an evac and it should be self only and not usable while melee'ing.
