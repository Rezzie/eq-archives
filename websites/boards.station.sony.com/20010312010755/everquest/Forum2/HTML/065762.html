First, lets fave it, EQ is starting to lose its luster for a lot of customers. This happens with any game, and EQ has lasted, and will continue to last, far longer than most. Personally, i still believe that many improvements can be made to the overall design of the game to make it more interesting for us and to extend the life of this game even longer.

Both ideas i will discuss (one has been discussed a lot already, the other not much at all i think) involve making the towns a more interesting place to hang out. For many people, and for better or worse, this game has become a sort of glorified chat room. Many people now log on just to say hi to friends they have met in game, or even RL friends they play the game with.

With that in mind, I think EQ would be a much better game if we had more to do in the game with friends (or for that matter even total strangers) than get together, find an open camp spot in Karnors, and pull mobs all night long hoping for the occasional uber drop. And what better place to get together to do "something/anything" with other pc's than in the now deserted towns?!

OK, here are my thoughts on how to make the towns the real center of PC life that they should be:

1) Implement some kind of semi-automated PvP combat in the PvP arenas!

This can be done in hundreds of different ways, but roughly i'm thinking something along the lines of this:

*first, have some kind of npc vendor/ringmaster or whatever basically be "in charge" of the arena. Anyone who wants to fight in the arena had to communicate/sign up with the vendor during the alloted time for their level/class. Maybe it cost 100p a person to sign up, and the winner takes 95% of the total loot (this also could be a good money sink if implemented wisely). At any rate, you must go through this npc character who pretty much controls who is fighting and when. If you try to sign up when it is not your time, the vendor turns you down, saying you are not experienced enough etc. etc. to fight with this group of players.

*As i alluded to above, the PvP combat is segregated by different level ranges and classes, and a system can be set up whereby maybe all classes and levels get their time in the arenas every day, or maybe an arena in Qeynos is for levels 1 to 40, the arena in Freeport is levels 41 to 60, etc. Rangers and Monks get to fight Mondays this week, maybe Wednesdays next week. Obviously many different methods could be worked out to give all levels and classes their time in the Arenas each week.

*Also, times for group combat can be set up separately from individual combat. Who you fight can be determined at random by the vendor or manually by each pc when they communicate with the vendor.

*Most importantly, give "fans" a place to watch these battles! Personally, i know that if i knew the Arenas would always be open and people, both individuals and groups, would be duking it out for a nice Plat prize, i would love to sit in an arena and watch it all happen . . . and maybe place a few side bets with a friend or two! (with Platinum of course 8).


2) OK, here is where i drag out the dead horse and beat it to death (erm . . . something like that).

Please, please, pretty please, reconsider your long-time anti-gambling stance. If EQ actually had casinos (like the one in HH, but that actually work), that would probably prolong the shelf-life of this game by a year at least. It would be a ton of fun for the customers, and it would bring people to the towns again if they couldn't find that open camp spot in Karnor's or Seb. etc.

I would love to be able to log on, and play a few hands of Pokir with my RL bro, friend in the guild, or even total stranger, or, hell, even an npc. And, again, if implemented wisely this would be another great money sink for the game! (I'm of the opinion that EQ's economy right now is basically broken, and it's only going to get worse. the more money sinks the better)


Anyway, i had a little time on my hands and just thought i'd post a few ideas, as far out and impossible as they may sound. I'm sure there are a million issues that i'm not aware of that may make one or both of these issues impossible to implement (i dont' buy the arguement that gambling with plat could be considered illegal however . . . no way). But i think if you really wanted to do either one bad enough, i'm sure it could be programmed into the game . . . maybe as an add-on, maybe implemented over several weeks or months of patches. There are many benefits to doing something like the ideas i mentioned above; moneysinks, give people who can't find groups (and there are a ton of these) something to do, open up a lot of spots out in the dungeons etc. (if you attract a lot of people to the cities), plus some more that i can't really think of right now.

As for responses, please feel free to post why you think my ideas stink, why you like them, or please post any other ideas you might have to improve the game (from a general design standpoint please, not class balance related, loot, spell improvements etc.)

Absor/Abashi/Verant: at least let me know why the above ideas aren't going to work. 8)
