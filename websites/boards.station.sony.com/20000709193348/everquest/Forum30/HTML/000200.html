I think it shows a lot of ignorance on the part of Verant to "Ordain" a play nice policy when the very design of Everquest pits players against eachother. For one, I can't think of another role playing game that was more dependent on items, most character classes in this game are utterly defenseless against most mobs without their nice gear. And in order to get that nice gear you have to camp ONE mob for hours and even then he might not have what you were looking for. I think it's clear that the camping isn't going to stop, and it's understandable how frustrating it can be to want something that you can never get because it's always being camped, especially if you're a melee class competing with blasters who can do more damage in one cast than you can do the whole fight. In that respect which is a clear design decision by Verant you can see how animosity would build between two players, in many ways these design choices are pulling people apart more than bringing them together and it's damned irresponsible of verant to have let it go on his long. The only clear solution is to randomize the spawns and the drops somehow, so that everyone has an equal chance of finding that great item in their travels. This is an idea which actually encourages travel, instead of encouraging people to sit on their butts for hours on end which they're going to do no matter how long Verant nerfs the item spawns. I mean, wouldn't it be nice to be surprised by something instead of laboring for hours camping a spawn you know will eventually have what you were looking for? And if you should happen across a lore item you already have one of, then to someone else's utter delight, you could leave it on the body and auction it's location. Verant's answer to camping has been to remove items from a particular spawn and break up the camping in the form of a quest. J boots being an example, well if you've ever done the quest you know the camping part hasn't changed, it's become 3 times worse! Since now you have to compete with countless others for the Ring of the ancients and camp the cyclops, then you have to camp the Shadowed Rapier, then you have to Camp Hasten Bootstrutter who, due to a bug that Verant seems very reluctant to fix, may not spawn for days. And of course if you fail to see him and log off you have to camp that Rapier all over again. I know I speak for a lot of people when I say that I'm looking warily forward to the Kunark expansion, when Verant will show us how they've dealt with some of their fundamental flaws the second time around. I know that as this program stands, I certainly wouldn't invest in another Verant program. The subtle design errors and lack of foresite that I've mentioned here is pretty unforgiveable in light of what other software developers have achieved, and I expect just a little better for my money.

Verant's problem is that they aren't making enough money. With only $2 million per month coming in, they can't afford to spend $30,000 to add another 10 servers and reduce the overcrowding.

Yeah, right.

Figure it out, people. Verant is pulling in $24 million per year from monthly fees alone (this doesn't take into account royalties on CD sales with the initial program at all). Now, assume only 1,000 players per server, and assume a paid full-time staff of 4 "GMs" (aka "admins") per server, to give 24/7 coverage. Assume each GM is paid $60K/yr.

Staff expenses: $480,000/year (always double salary to find personnel expense due to benefits, taxes, etc.)
Hardware expenses: $10,000 will buy 3 reasonable servers (one primary, 2 backups). Let's assume $100,000/year just to be safe.
Network expenses: A full T-1 is between $1,500 and $3,000 per month. We'll assume $3,000, which is $36,000/year.

So, annual expense directly related to the server operation is $616,000. Player subscription fees (at a rate of 1,000 players per server) total $117,600/year. Obviously Verant isn't going to do that.

Now let's look at how it really seems to us players:

Verant loads the servers up to 2,000 players each; assigns one GM per 5 servers and has them on only 40 hours per week (and probably pays less than $40k/year), and spends probably $3,000 per year on hardware. The T-1 is still $36,000 annually.

That's $235,200 income per year per server versus expenses of $55,000 annually.

The turth is probably somewhere in between. However, it's obvioust that Verant's "vision" has nothing whatsoever to do with RPG, and everything to do with maximizing profits.
