Constitutional protections only apply to the exercise of governmental authority. So, while your state governor can't toss you in jail for writing a satirical story about him/her because of First Amendment protections, Sony/Verant can choose not to allow to use their product (EQ) for flaming them or their world.

The only limitation is that they cannot ban someone in a discriminatory fashion. That is, they can't ban based solely on race, ethnicity, physical disability, gender, etc. If they ban someone who has blue skin because he violates the EULA, they had better be certain that it was his violation of the EULA and not the blue skin that led to the banning.

So, in short, I can criticize my elelcted official, and he can't refuse to serve me because of it, but Sony/Verant can. Whether they should, well, that's what is up for discussion.
