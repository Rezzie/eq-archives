I just wanted to clarify a few things about the threads that I have been starting, the Class Talk threads.

The most important thing to remind everyone about is that I am just gathering ideas to bring to the team. Nothing suggested, either by myself or by you, is set in stone. Ideas that make sense to me will be brought to the team to evaluate. For the most part, it is unlikely that we will implement any majority of these ideas. Implementing any more than a few of them would most likely throw things even further off balance…

I also wanted to stress that the general idea of these threads is to look at the balance of the classes. As most of you know, we still intend to bring the game to a point where we are content with the balance of all of the classes so that we can stop expending our time worrying about the balance of classes and use that energy for providing new content and other cool things. My intent with these threads is, primarily, to be ready when that time comes, and to have a solid idea of your opinions on the classes.

However, there is no reason not to include bugs and other such things in these talks. So go ahead and give me those too.

Some have suggested that I should start these threads in the class specific boards. Well, perhaps I should have. But I don't want it to seem like I only want opinions from people that play the class being discussed. I actually want input from anyone that has ideas.

I am also getting a fair amount of e-mail with suggestions and ideas. While I don't really mind getting them, I would much rather that ideas be posted in the threads. I want everyone to have all the information that I have from the players, so it can be debated and discussed. And since I am now spending nearly every minute reading these threads, I find it harder to read long e-mails about those same topics…

I have created a hasty and very changeable schedule for these posts. I reserve the right to adjust this list at any time. And the order is pretty random, since I won't really be taking action on them until later.

Already started:

Shadow Knights (today)
Clerics
Wizards
Necromancers
Magicians

I know, some of these were not specifically Class Talk threads. But I do feel that I have a solid grasp on the concerns of these classes, as the threads about them (necromancers, for example) were the inspiration for this whole Class Talk idea.

Enchanters: Friday, Jan. 26th (I may very well be reading this one during the Superbowl…)

Monks: Wednesday, Jan. 31st

Paladins: Monday, Feb. 5th (or Tuesday the 6th, since I may very well be stuck in Baltimore…)
Rangers: Wednesday, Feb. 7th.

Once I have all of the ideas I can stand for all the classes, I will re-post the lists that I have gathered. I might very well do that on the class specific boards. The lists will always be open to change, so it's not like I'll be posting a 'final' version. But the lists, at that point, should be pretty solid.

At some future date I hope to update these lists with notes as to the status of those ideas. If implemented, I hope to give you some details about the form of that implementation (though I don't plan to give out formulas or such things), and if they are not going to be implemented, I hope to tell you why not.

Again, I want to stress that NONE of these lists or talks include any sort of promise that the ideas in them will be added to the game. I will simply be providing the team with the lists. And when I give you my opinion about an idea, it's just that, my opinion. I'm just one guy. I have the ability to make suggestions and I get to argue with the team about the value of those ideas. But we look at these things as a team, and we make these decisions as a team.

That's why the game is as cool as it is.

I also wanted to apologize for not posting as frequently during the next few weeks. I am pushing to get all these talks done as soon as I can. I think they are important. But reading all these posts is eating up all of my time. Formatting and rewriting those ideas is taking up time that I don't even have. So my ability to post for a while will be reduced significantly.

Alan

Groller - not necessarily. Effectiveness can translate to balance in most situations, assuming classes are equally effective in the grand scheme of things. True, there are situations that call for one class over another, but the true question is... are there an equal number of situations in which each individual class is preferred over the others for their unique and useful traits, and their effectiveness? If so, then balance is there. If not, then a problem has arisen. High level groups say they have to have an enchanter and cleric. Then there's something wrong. Never heard a high level group say they needed a rogue, or a ranger, or a shaman... does that mean they couldn't use them? Heck no. Just means that they'll take pretty much anyone, but they feel they NEED those two classes. So there's a balance issue. Rather than farm out cleric and enchanter abilities, why not think up new abilities for the other classes that can take the place of the earlier abilities... Why not a AoE instill doubt that the rogue can use that perhaps takes all the members of your group out of the fray for two seconds but almost always works and is a discipline that is once an hour or what have you? While that doesn't exactly = replacement for enchanter mez, it helps... or perhaps rogues could have a paralyzing fear ability, effectively rooting and terrifying a creature but also making it so the rogue himself couldn't move either, having to retain eye contact with the enemy...

While that was rogue stuff, well, was the first thought off top of my head. Fact of the matter is, when all the classes are equally effective in different situations, and no class is really NEEDED for the majority of situations, well...

Depends on your definition of balance. In my book, there you have it.
