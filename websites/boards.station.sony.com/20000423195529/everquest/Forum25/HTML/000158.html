Well I just logged out of EQ after another GM event turned into a joke. My guildmate Dwaveon (who lives in MM - heheh) said over guildchat that a Deathly Herald had appeared and announced Mayong Mistmoore was waking. Mayong appeared soon after together with his henchman (Lord Galendore or something like that) and a wood elven prisoner they had captured. The scene started to play out and thats when things got out of hand. Players trying to nuke the main NPCs, crowding Mayong, heckling the GMs, and generally spoiling things.

Verants fault? No. I sympathised with the GM playing Mayong Mistmoore - I must admit that I would have used the Death Touch far more often faced with so many players only interested in being disruptive (simply trying to slay major NPCs for loot, instead of seeing the scene pan out).

Some players excused their actions by calling it roleplaying, and indeed many players should be congratulated for continuing to roleplay despite the crowds of Kewl d00dz swarming Mayong. Maybe Mayong should have used more force so that he was treated with the proper respect and fear due to him - the death touch was used sparingly (Asian got it twice that I saw) and an AoE summon that moved players away from Mayong up the ramp was also used a few times. Mayong is easily god and dragon level, yet this very impressive vampire could barely move for the players around him making quirky comments and trying to nuke him every once in a while - hmmm.

Maybe Mayong should have appeared in his throne room inside MM and done the sequence by shout. Or maybe he should have had a killable bodyguard of L50 ancilles (that the GMs could keep respawning and throwing forwards to keep the bloodthirsty players busy).

I have heard that some servers have missed out on unique GM-given artefacts because players have nuked GM controlled NPCs before the quest could be given, and I feared this was due to happen again. I have been at past GM events, even though I am not high lev (L37 now), because I like to observe the history of Norrath unfolding, but some of the childish behaviour tonight ruined the sense of epic grandeur that Mayong's appearance should have provoked.

Solutions?
1) ooc, shout and auction channels should be disabled in the event zone. The GMs should forcibly use a silence command on disruptive individuals.
2) Using an apparition (such as a vision of a god, or a ghost) to give quests may be an idea - they wouldn't be targettable so couldn't be attacked.
3) If the scene is just to further the history of the world, like today's event, then the major NPCs should have death touch and not be afraid to use it to get the respect and awe due to them.
4) I agreed with some players that these events need more interraction, but if a sequence like tonights was just to advance the history then I don't see what Verant can do. Maybe finishing the sequence with a Mistmoore army in L Fay moving towards G Fay zone (and Felwithe) would have satisfied people.


And so to the event itself. From what Mayong said it seems Veeshan and her dragons are involved - he mentioned both a mystical veil (as in a barrier) which restricts their power, and the continent of Velious which is where Veeshan clawed Norrath (see opening movie). Mayong also mentioned an evil dwarf that his henchman should seek out to help him slay the dragons Nagafen and Vox. Many players are running around Norrath at this moment looking for this evil dwarf (probably to nuke him - sigh).


I won't speculate further than saying I'm sure this ties in with making the dragons stronger and higher lev with the expansion due soon (with possibly a new level 60 limit for players). I know Brad said in an interview that they wouldn't want players ever being powerful enough to kill Vox and Nagafen easily. Maybe it will also pave the way for Kunark dragons, the Velious continent, and for Veeshan to appear in Plane of Sky now its open.

I'm starting to ramble and must get some sleep - cya
