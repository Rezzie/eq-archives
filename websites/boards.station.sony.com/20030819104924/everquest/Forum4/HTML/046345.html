I was impressed with the way EQ looks after recent hardware upgrades, I had to write about it.

A few months ago I bought a new computer (including an NVIDIA Ti4600 graphics card), although I kept my old 15" LCD (1024x768, analogue input) monitor. Things got better (all the setting maxed out), but something was missing.

Yesterday I bought a new Philips 17" LCD monitor (1280x1024, digital input) and I can't believe the difference. It was late when I installed it and I have only seen the bazaar but it looks good.

The display is crystal clear, textures are better, shading is great, people look good and don't overlap each other (even in the most congested trading spots). All the UI windows are smaller and even thought I've made my chat windows bigger, there is so much spaced to move the mouse around without hitting a window. There was even no lag (ok a slightly noticeable lag but nothing serious).

I checked in on my trader this morning before heading off to work and was still impressed with the new quality of the graphics I'm seeing. Getting the new monitor was definitely worth it.

Only one annoyance (there has to be at least one). I was hoping that now I have a larger screen I could sit further away from it. Unfortunately, the font size in the chat window is now so small I need to move closer to the monitor. If anyone knows how to increase the size of fonts in the chat windows, please let me know.

Looking forward to exploring EQ and seeing the quality of other zones over the weekend.

I have just done exactly the opposite. After making my old system struggle at the edge of its capabilities for months I finally got fed up with having floating mouse pointers and frame rate lag just to see the detail in the graphics.

I turned off all Luclin models except horse/elemental and gnome (couldn't turn those off and still use my horse, sadly), put the textures back to 16 bit and the screen size to the second lowest option.

The immediate result was a totally playable game, even in the zones that used to be virtually unplayable for me.

The second, unexpected, result was I preferred the more cartoony, old-EQ look. Even if I had a state-of-the-art system I wouldn't turn Luclin models back on.
