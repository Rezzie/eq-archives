It's funny when I look back at EQ, the day I bought the game about one month after retail release.

"Hmmm, EverQuest? Wow...an online persistant Dynamic world where you get to build up a character and do quest after quest! Sounds great!"

Was happily amused for several months. Building up my toon. Exploring the different zones. Seeing the sights. It was quite fun and a worthwhile gaming experience, but my experience was far from what I expected.

It was EverQuest, because there was no ending. People quit when burned out or tired of the game. There was no epic struggle between good and evil. The world wasn't dynamic- items and abilities were dynamic and zones were added via expansion and the rare free zone addition.

Quests in EQ seemed to be lessons in frustration. Usually horrible risk v reward. Impossible to gain decent exp or level by questing past the initial quests. Quests could be done over and over, since there was no player tracking of quests.

So, in the end EQ was a worthwhile and overall good gaming experience, but not in any of the ways I was hoping for or expecting.
