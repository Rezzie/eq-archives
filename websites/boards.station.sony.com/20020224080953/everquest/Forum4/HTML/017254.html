while it wouldnt -hurt- anything to build up 1hb/2hb i wouldnt do it unless your bored or just want the satisfaction out of saying your a master of all forms of melee or something like that

9 times out of 10, you'll be duel wielding two 1hs weapons, reason for this is your main "goal" as a warrior is NOT damage output, its taunt, and the more times you smack a mob, the more you taunt it, especially aroune level 40+ you'll prolly almost -never- ( i said ALMOST heh) see a warrior use any 2-hand weapon, unless they are NOT the main tank.

1hs's = more taunt
more taunt = protect rest of party

Again though if your on a bigggg raid (40+ people) and your not the main tank, or even a backup, sure use a 2hs and dish out the damage, but otherwise stick the 1hs's for exp groups and such.

I am a level 53 warrior (maybe someday I might get 54... maybe). I have 1hb, 1hs, 2hs, and peircing all maxed. some people go with 2hb but our options as a warrior are limited there (rangers get insane 2hb weapons). Here are the benefits.

1hs - Your primary weapon. Lots of choices here, sort of a no-brainer, get it.
1hb - Especially in the later levels, there are some excellent weapons in this line. I have used atleast one 1hb for the last 15 levels (train of the tranquil, smoldering brand, some luclin mace or other).
peircing - same as one handed blunt, from 25-35, I used a blood stained fang to keep this up.
2hs - This is the debated one. I use it alot and always strive to keep it up. Before 50, you will find it easier to do damage with a good 2hs (which are also found in the dozens). After 50, Our dual weild daamge really starts to out power the 2 handers. Before that, 2 handers have an edge at times. Another reason for a 2hs is the mob might have a damage shield. same damage but less swings = less damage taken.

Aldur is right, our job is to hold a mobs attention. The main factors in taunt are damage caused, number of hits and procs. Any one handed weapon will cause a high number of hits. I prefer to go with slightly faster weapons with procs when I am trying to be the main taunt. If I am the secondary, I switch to either slightly slower but better damage weapons or 2 handers.

just me opinion lad, take it at face value

Also, there are a few things to consider:

1) Though it is a LONG way off, you always want to keep your epic in mind. For instance, while a lot of druids work up 1hb, their epic is a 1hs, same for shamans which is a 1hp. a warrior epic is somewhat odd, in that it is TWO 1hs which can become a 2hs and break apart back in to two 1hs. So you want to keep both those skills up there for when you get your epic, you can either dual wield the sucker, or have an uber 2hs depending on circumstances and your mood at the time

2) are you a big race and/or do you fight a lot of caster mobs? If you are a big race (ogre, barbarian or troll) then this is not of relevance but if you are NOT then you might want to keep a 1hs around so you can drop in a shield to bash casters and interrupt them. Nothing like having a mob cast Complete heal to ruin your day. With a 2hs you can't bash since you can't wear a shield

3) You got dual wield now, mix and match. Keep a 1hs in your main and a 1hb in your secondary, you'll raise both skills, abiet much SLOWER with your 1hb as you'll hit less often, but at lvl 15, head to NK and beat around some lions wolves and bears, you can solo them without much of a problem, and there's a guard tower nearby if you get in trouble. With the druid rings in the zone, a sow/heal is never very far if you are in need of one, and Greater Light Stones are still some good PP at your lvl

2a. Adendum, you might want to find a unicorn shield, it's a shield that also counts as a 1hp so you can get the ac bonus of a shield and still dual wield it (it's not REALLY a shield, think of it as a dagger with an ac bonus and a shield graphic, you can't bash with it)

so thats about it, I suggest something simple for a 2hs like a drakkel forged bastard sword, some nice 1hs such as icetooth claws and a babs, and a 1hp (my shammy carries a nice spear...ice bone or something) and 1hb (velium morningstar is fine for now). 2hb and 2hp are really not important, you can train them if you want, but I personally wouldnt bother.

I have a level 60 warrior. My 1hs and 2hs are maxed (epics), 1hb is around 240, piercing around 220, and 2hb just above 200.

It's easiest to get weapon skills up before you hit 45. Skill ups past 50 seem to take forever. I play a small race warrior and I haven't used a shield since I got dual wield. IMHO, the damage output on "real" weapons overrides the switching between shield and weapon just to get a bash in. And if bash skill is low, you WILL miss a lot of hits.

2hs still isn't worth using in my opinion, without a good haste you won't create enough taunt. Keep the skill up anyways, there's some destructive stuff available later on in the game, that is worth using if you're not the main tank.

When choosing/using dual wield weapons, remember this:
- lower delay weapon in mainhand. Damage bonus is added only on mainhand weapon so lower damage on it isn't such a big deal
- slower weapon in offhand, try to get one with high damage to compensate for the damage bonus.
- Try to get mainhand weapon with delay under 25, offhand delay can be upto 40 if damage is good. On your level, don't go for the uber stuff, get something that isn't crap and save up for armor and weapon upgrades later on.

If you want to pick up a new weapon skill, use the weapon with good skill in your mainhand, and put the lower skill weapon in offhand. Granted, you won't get skill ups as fast as if it would be in mainhand, but you will keep making damage and not missing. Try to get all weapon skills to 200 when you hit level 40 and you're set up for 50s.

Remember, warrior is defined by their gear. You don't get spells or special abilities, your job is to get the ac and hp to absorb damage and keep a mob busy while the others in the group do their stuff. When you advance little further, past 20, and start looking for stat upgrades, go for sta/ac/str.
