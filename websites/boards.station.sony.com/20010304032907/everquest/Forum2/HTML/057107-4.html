To all those that don't understand why someone that has spent 1, 2 or more years playing shouldn't be allowed to sell there account, here is the good comparison.

Join a Health Club, or a Golf Club, or any other association of those types. Those 'memberships' are 'non-transferable'. Same thing here.

To try to compare EQ to buying a car, driving around in it, fixing up the sound, getting a better paint job and then reselling it to recover some of the money put into the car is a flawed comparision. The only part of EQ you own is the CD's. And, once these are registered to you and you agree to the EULA, then you no longer have to right to sell those CD's to someone else, as those registration keys have already been used.

Now, I could see the arguement that if you deleted every character on every server that you had, you might be able to justify the transfer of ownership of the CD's and registration codes to another person.

As to the bit about selling in game items. Well, that part I understand where some people stand on the issue, as I too have had rare spawn/drops gacked out from under me by some KSer of a higher level. Probably for the purpose of Twinking another character, or maybe because they didn't want to mess around with it at lower levels.

But, as long as there are those out there that don't have the time/interest to spend on getting the items by them selves or through grouping, you will always have people willing to pay real life money. And, as long as there are people willing to pay RL$ there will always be people who would consider it idiotic not to sell to them.

What is the real affect on Farming?

I have monitored Ebay since I started playing in July of 2000, and, have seen the mass majority of the auctions go unpurchased. The most common purhcased items were PP in the multiple 1000's range, and items that would be considered roughly equal to their amount of PP. Not very uncommon to have seen 10000pp go for about 150.00 to 250.00. And, items that sold for roughly the same amount of pp have sold for about 1/2 to 2/3 their pp rating on that server.

So, what has been purchased the most would appear to be the massive amounts of Plat needed to purchase items IG. As an exchange rate goes, it is roughly 15.00 per 1000pp when sold in bulk and as much as 30.00 per 1000pp when purhcased in single 1000pp sales.

What causes this inflation in the price of items? Simply put, their difficulty level to be obtained.
Why are they, often times, so difficult to be had? Combination of factors:
First: Rareness of Spawn
Second: Rareness of Drop
Third: Percieved Value of the Item (for resale either in or out of game)
Fourth: Number of people that are camping the spawn for the drop

Simple solution: I wouldn't, as a DM/GM/ST, allow characters that are obviously out of level for the encounter (lvl 30 characters in D&D are not put up against lvl 10 encounters and when they do match up against these encounters, they get no XP and nothing of value is dropped off of them, DM's perogative). So, why doesn't Verant look into the Farming issue from this standpoint.

If you are a lvl50 Magician and you want to help a lvl20 Magician get that nifty item, bring that lvl20 Magician along with you. He gets to fight the encounter, you just provide support. When he/she gets the item, you bug out and let others take the spawn.

Ways to limit Farming, make more of these items Lore or Limited Lore Items. Makeing it impossible for one character to have more then, say for the Limited Lore Items, two of them at one time. GBS for example. You can only use two, so why not build in a limit of two on the character (not the account, you may have more then one character you want them for).

Further solutions, make the benifits of some items of the nature of weapon procs. The +Int Robe that you shouldn't be able to get your hands on till you are lvl18 or better, doesn't work until a few lvls before or right at 18.

Sure, these may seem like penalties, but, think about it in terms of a Roleplaying game. Do you give your lv1 characters Rods of Polymorph, or Bags of Holding? Do you run your lvl 125 (yes I have seen these lvl campaigns) Warrior against some lvl 5 Malitia Men. . . No. At least, not as something to get XP or items from.
Possibly there will be some at the very beginning of a campaign on the way to the, say, lvl 150 Witch, but you also weren't planning on trying to take that Witch out alone where you, you did bring friends right.

So, basically, make the items work only within certain lvls. Make the items not drop if someone above the appropriate lvl, by what ever math they decide to use, kills the creature. Make sure that players understand they are to be progressing along in their encounters, not farming items for sale in EC or Ebay.
