A suggestion for making Bards more useful in the 50+ game
--------------------------------------------------
I apologize if the following has already been covered. I've skimmed the thread and did not see it. I'm also a first time reader/poster, but a long time player with multiple high level chars. So...

THE IDEA:
My suggestion is a new song that would give bards something more useful to do both on big raids and in average grouping situations. Why not a song that adds a very small damage proc to the weapons of all group members? Something we'd get around lvl 50, that does around 15pts of damage (perhaps scaling up a bit with level) and stacks with any existing dmg procs built into the weapons.

THE REASON:
In the post 50 game, Bards still have some good defensive buffs, healing, mana regen and even mezz, but are severely lacking on the offensive side. As a class seemingly designed to be able to fill what ever niche is needed at the time, we need a boost on the offensive side. A dmg proc song gives us that. Here are two examples of my most common grouping situations and how this song gives me a nice niche to fill, making me a desirable addition to a party:

A)Typical Dungeon Group: I'm almost always grouped with a couple of melee classes and at least 2 of the following - Druid, Shaman, Cleric, Enchanter. As has been argued here over and over, those classes greatly reduce the benefits of our staple songs in the 50+ game. The proc song, coupled with dmg shield and a third buff of our choosing would allow us to fill the offense niche nicely, making it easier for us to get groups.

C)Big Raid Mobs: Without fail, I'm placed in a primary melee group with the sole purpose of singing a resist song. That's not so bad to me really, except that in the 50+ game a lot of the people I play with have EXCELLENT resists without a bard's help, diminishing my usefulness. But what if I could boost their resists AND add to their offense with a little extra proc'ing action? I think that would make me more attractive to the raid organizers, who always want more dmg over time.

So there ya go, one song that fills a niche we're lacking in and shouldn't be unbalancing (but still useful)if the dmg isn't set too high.

I've always enjoyed playing a bard, and I have trouble thinking of myself as anything else. However I must say that we have some fundamental problems that become more and more apparent at higher levels. I think the problem is that Bards just don't quite fit the molds of the other classes. I think that the confusion of what a bard should be is even a source of confusion for Verant themselves. At least this is a logical conclusion from the many strange ways they have designed our class. Are we a meleer with a little bit of magic? Are we a caster with a little bit of meleeing? If we are something totally different, are we being penalized for being perceived as being part of the other two choices? I want to look at bards from the different ideas of what a bard is and discuss what I see as wrong with us and show how we are "double penalized" in both melee and magic.

Bards as Hybrid
--------------------
We are hybrids of sorts. We have the ability to use many kinds of weapons (1 handed), and we can wear "plate". So, what's the problem? The problem is that we are heavily penalized melee wise more than any other hybrid. Pallies and SKs lose a lot of offense in exchange for almost having the defense of warriors, and spell abilities. Rangers get less defense in exchange for having spells and almost the offensive ability of a warrior. Bards, on the other hand, get the worse end both ways. From what I've seen, we are inferior to all hybrids on both sides of offense and defense. Bards deal much less melee damage than even the weaker hybrid offensive skill of Pallies and SKs. Bards, supposedly a tank class, are also fairly weak defensively as well. I have about half the hitpoints of some similar level warriors I know. I can wear plate, but many of our plate items get chain type AC. Our defense as a whole is also probably weaker than the Ranger. So, we are worse at offense and defense of all other hybrids, and therefore are double penalized. We are also the only melee class or hybrid that does not get double attack. Even if we could get this at level 50 it would at least make us feel like we were contributing more, even if the cap wasn't very high on it.

If Bards are hybrids or meleers at all, then I think something must be done to improve our melee abilities. I think that both offense and defense could reasonably be raised, however I think mainly defense. I think that bard should be inferior offensively to all other hybrids, but maybe by not such a great degree. However, I personally feel that defensively, any plate wearing class (such as a Bard) should have significantly better defense compared to a chain wearing class (Ranger).

Bards as Casters
--------------------
One of the Bard's strengths is that he has a good variety of magical abilities. However, this is not without some shortcomings. In early and mid level careers of a bard, these abilities make a huge contribution to the party. We have a great number of songs that can help almost any party. However, the higher up you get, the less useful you become for anything other than a mana battery (and those of you who know me KNOW how much I hate to admit that). Our songs are redundant and inferior to those in almost any party. Also we have the added problem of that many of our short lived and inferior spell effects overwrite many more powerful effects (particularly ones with multiple effects). At higher levels, we just can't provide anything to the average party other than mana and magic resist buffs, which is very depressing for a bard who likes to feel useful and to have a bit of variety.

I think some of our songs are great for parties, and are at just the level of power, however there are many that become useless after just a few levels (if not useless immediately). The problem with most of our songs is either the effect is too weak or it is resisted too often. The problem with Bard magic is very similar to our problem in melee; we are double penalized. Let me explain.

Our magic (with a couple exceptions) doesn't cost mana. It is true this is a great advantage. However, we pay for this advantage in many ways. First, almost all of our songs are short lived, lasting between 1 and 4 ticks. Fair enough. Second, because of the first limitation, this creates the indirect limitation of being only able to have only a 2 or 3 songs effects going at a time on yourself, party, or a target. Again, I can live with that… though one extra song would be wonderful. Third, many of them are very highly resistable due to the fact that some of these songs have multiple effects. However, I think the main problem bards have is that despite having all of these disadvantages, we are stuck with the biggest disadvantage, and that is that our songs effects are just so weak in many cases. The question is, for the sake of balance, are ALL of these penalties appropriate to offset the fact they are mana free casting? There are many items out there that people can buy that have much superior effects to any bard song and are also mana free. I think that if some of our songs were improved in effectiveness, this could go a great deal to us feeling more useful in parties. I'm going to assume that by balanced, you mean that there is a compelling reason to include a class in a group. Based on that assumption, what are the compelling reasons to invite a player into a group?

To survive most encounters a party needs the following things:
1. A damage doer
2. A damage take
3. Crowd control
4. Runner control
5. Damage mitigation (primarily healing)
To maximize gains and lower risks the following things are also nice:
1. Mana regen
2. Ressurection
3. Evac's
4. Buffs

Granted, the number one reason we all play this game is to have fun and so the most compelling reason to invite a player into a group is to have fun. However, for the majority of the people playing the game, ESPECIALY in the lvl 30+ crowd, fun is largely defined by gaining lvls and gaining loot.

Therefore, there is only a compelling reason to invite a class into a group if it falls into one of the above listed categories.

From lvl 1-20, bards are fine. In fact, the classes are all fairly well balanced and the differences between them remain minor.

From lvl 20-50, bards remain a compelling class.

However, what is the compelling reason to include a lvl 50+ bard into a group?
1. Damage doer: Bards can not match the output of a monk, rogue, wizard, warrior, SK, necro, druid, or mage. Probably not a shaman either. Against undead paladins will also outdamage us.
2. Damage taker: Uhhh don't think I need to detail this one :P
3. Crowd control: Our charm hard caps at lvl 51 mobs. Our aoe mezz is worthless and our single target mezz, while improved for the mid range bard, becomes essentialy a non viable solution at this lvl. Enchanters are NOT the only way to go, but they are the primary means of crowd control at higher lvls. Even necro's get a better crowd control than we do.
4. Runner control: The problems with our snare song are numerous and well documented. Besides, at lvl 51+ the real issue is interupting mob's trying to gate. That means stuns. That means chanters, clerics, wizzies, etc...
5. Damage Mitigation: We could potential be very good at this role. However, our slow songs do not stack w/ chanters and w/out a chanter you general don't have reliable crowd control. Therefore, most lvl 50+ groups have a chanter and so there is no compelling reason to have a bard. Our heal songs do stack w/ regen, however the song must be stopped while the regen is cast and the strength of our regen songs do not scale up vis a vis shaman/druid regens. In fact, starting around lvl 40 this song appeared less and less often in my lineup. Our rune songs are inadequate as a form of dmg mitigation since they absorb less that one hit from any given mob.

Note that a bard can not adequately do ANY of the 5 things needed by a party. This wouldn't be quite so bad if we could ASSIST others in doing these things, but we can't do that well either. Here's why:
1. Damage doers: We can use our dmg songs to hurt the mobs, but since a true dmg does does this better, why use a bard? We can haste the tanks, but shaman and chanters do it better and our songs don't stack with their haste. Since lvl 50+ groups will almost always have a chanter, we can't help here either. Our stat buff songs are nice, but at this point most tanks already have high stat scores, and are likely being buffed by another party member.
2. Damage takers: Well... given the high taunt nature of our songs, we can kind of act as a walking rune... you know, the tank takes no damage from the first 10 hits because the bard absorbs it all (then dies).
3. Crowd control: Our mezz overwrites chanter mezz and lasts a shorter duration, if it lands at all.
4. Runner control: See above
5. Damage mitigation: Same problem as with damage doing. Stacking issues mean that rarely can we be of much use here either.

So, not only can we not fullfill any of the needed jobs, we can't even act in tandem with another class to do the role.

Let's look at the secondary jobs:
1. Mana regen: Yes, we still rule the roost here :/ The problem is, bards play the game to have fun as well... and going afk while playing the mana song for hours at a time and asking the group to port you out when they're done is NOT fun. I exaggerate, but you get the point. What's worse is that while we are better at mana regen than a chanter is, chanters are a passable substitute in this area, while bards are NOT a passable substitute for chanters in the NEEDED roles.
2. Ressurection: Clerics. Yeah, paladins and necros too to a very very limited degree.
3. Evac's: well... we don't get them. In the mid levels, we do have some tools that substitute or at least enhance. AOE fear song is a great way to get a pack of goblins off your wizard in Droga. It's a great way to die at lvl 50+
4. Buffs: The problem with our post 50 resist buffs has been detailed extensively. Our haste buffs have also been detailed. Our part wide charisma buff is... interesting. Yeah, we can offer a party something here, but not much... not at lvl 50+

Ok, given that, let's form a party. We need a damage doer... get a monk, rogue, wizard, warrior, ranger, etc.. 1 group slot gone.
We need a damage taker... Warrior, Paladin, Sk. 2 group slots gone (and if your damage doer IS a warrior, it's still 2 slots gone because you're gonna need 2 of 'em to match the dmg output of the real dmg doing classes.) We need someone for crowd control... Enchanter. Maybe in a pinch, a necro. 3 slots gone. We need runner control... Lot's of options here, none of them include a bard, 4 slots gone. We need some form of dmg mitigation... Cleric. Sorry, very very few post 50 groups venture out w/out one. 5 slots gone.

Who are we going to put in slot 6? Well... we have mana regen covered w/ the chanter. If we don't have a wizard or druid yet in one of the other roles, they better take slot 6. For buffs, shaman rule the roost and they help w/ dmg mitigation a lot as well. Ressurection is covered by the cleric.

Where does that leave a bard? Out in the Eastern wastes...

Let's look at the same thing from a different perspective. Which role can each class play (ie.. what is their compelling reason)

Warrior: Damage taker, primarily, also some dmg doing.
Monk: Damage doer, some limited crowd control (fd pulling)
Rogue: Damage doer
Paladin: Damage taker, some dmg doing, more against undead
Shadow Knight: Damage taker, some dmg doing.
Ranger: Damage doer, runner control
Cleric: Damage mitigation, ressurection
Shaman: Buffer, damage mitigation, some dmg doing
Druid: Buffer, damage mitigation, some dmg doing, runner control, evac
Wizard: Damage doer, evac, some runner control
Mage: Damage doer, combination dmg taker/mitigation w/ use of pet
Enchanter: Crowd control, dmg mitigation (slows and runes), assists dmg doing (haste), mana regen
Necro: Damage doer, combination damage taker/mitigation w/ use of pet, some runner control, some crowd control
Bard: mana regen

Solutions:
There are many many possible solutions to this problem.
1. Beefed up crowd control abilities. Bards don't need to be as good as an enchanter at crowd control. They should be able to fill in as an acceptable substitute in MOST situations. Raise the lvl cap on bard charm (it's mana based w/ no mana regen and lasts a maximum of 9 seconds). Make lullaby require an instrument, capped at 4 mobs and take out the secondary resist. A chanter can lock down 4 mobs w/out breaking a sweat and while they are mezzed, cast other spells. Additionaly, spells cast by the chanter prior to the fight remain in effect. Giving bards the ability to lock down 4 mobs is not unbalancing because unlike the enchanter, bards would not be able to do anything BUT mezz until the end of the fight.
2. Increase EITHER the offensivie OR defensive abilites of bards. Give us the ability to dish out more dmg either through increased melee skills or scaled up songs, OR give us the ability to TAKE more dmg through higher AC and defensive skills.
3. Make our haste/slow songs stack w/shaman and chanter spells. Let shaman and chanters be better at haste/slow, but let us contribute when these classes are present (and they will be in almost any lvl 50+ group)

First off, let me say that I strongly oppose any introduction of autotwisting in the game. A great deal of the skill of a bard lies in constantly changing twists, double pulsing a vital song that gets resisted (eg snare), and generally paying constant attention to the changing battle scene. Any change that encourages bards to in essence play badly has got to be wrong.
However, pulse songs such as hymn of restoration, clarity amd the cantonas should certainly pulse every 3 seconds to allow us to take a break between fights.
Before writing this post I should say that I've put a great deal of thought in the last few days into what I think a bard is (and isn't). My view of a bard is as a buffer, rallying his allies with rousing songs; as a debuffer, freezing or scaring an enemy; as a limited fighter able to take a little damage; but mostly as a group supporter able to step in at the vital moment. In no way should a bard ever be a damage dealer and I feel that where bard damage at high levels is sorely lacking it would be more 'bardic' to compensate with group skills at this stage than to have any real change in direct damage output.
Now to issues of balancing. The buggest problem with bards currently must surely be the issue of haste/slow stacking, particularly at high levels. Having thought about it some I must agree with the design team that whilst it could be tweaked a little full stacking is just going to be unbalanced with the super hastes/slows of enchanters/shamans. Starting from that basis it seems sensible to gives bards enough good secondary songs to make them useful to a party even without hasting/slowing but to make it so that these are not optimal when an enchanter and shaman is not present.
Looking for a useful debuff my suggestion is a good AC debuff song. This song should be such that hasting a typical tank does more damage than lowering it's AC, but that the amount of additional damage done with tanks hasted by an enchanter is significant. A good time to get this song would be level 44 - it's about this stage that the haste issue becomes most thorny and the existing level 44 song is not really worth buying for most bards.
When looking for a good buffing line I focused on the level 49 song that is in effect a 20 point rune for both melee and magical damage. My suggestion is to split this song into a melee rune song and a separate magical rune song but make it so that both absorb more damage at higher levels. A low-powered but suitable formula would be (level minus 45) times 5. This gives rune values of 20hp at level 49 rising to 75hp at level 60 - not excessive given the amount that mobs hit/cast for but at least allowing the bard to add something tangible.
Another topic I have seen raised is that of bard hitpoints. I believe it is quite correct that bards pay heavily for their songs and should not generally make good complete heal targets. Having said that I think there is some scope here to raise them above the caster category. If an unbuffed level 50 human bard with base(75) stamina were to have about 1500hp that would seem about right. I'm not sure of the exact figures but I'm guessing about a 10% rise in base hitpoints would give that and still make bards by far the weakest hybrid class in this category.
On the fighting front, a small increase in skill caps for dodge, parry, riposte and piercing would seem in order. It's right that bards should not be experts like warriors but we should be able to take some damage.

As I described above I see bards songs as essentially affecting the emotions/minds of their listeners in a modest way. Whereas enchanters can use powerful magicks to wipe an entire mind a bard's mind affects should be short-lived and/or only have partial effect. Within this frameweork there are a few songs that would enable a bard to be of more use to a party both as a tank and as a crowd control stand-in without impinging on the specialised roles of true tanks or enchanters.
The first such song would be a pure taunt. This song would do no damage but add a temporary (9 second) boost to the bard's position on the hate-list. This of course is a song of limited uses, really only when the bard is acting as main tank at low levels, or as an emergency quick taunt off a caster. As it doesn't stack hate it's never going to be even close to a tank's taunt skill but it will at least make a party think before sending bards away saying they need a taunting tank only. Looking at the song list the level 24 song is now almost useless due to changes elsewhere so that would be a possible time to introduce it.
The second idea here is to have a selective memory blur song allowing a bard to save party members in limited situations but not as powerful as an enchanter's blur line. The idea of this song is to remove only the most hated foe from a creature's hate list. In this way it would take 6 pulses (18 seconds) for a bard to fully blur a creature. More practically it can erase a caster from the hate list when they are being beaten to a pulp. A nice side-effect of this is that if the bard taunts a mob wrongly this song would act as an antitaunt hopefully moving the mob to someone else. This seems like a mid level sort of song to me and as the level 35 mana drain song is rarely if ever used in non-PvP this is a good level for such an effect.
The third idea is really just an upgrade to what I thought bards should always be about - making creatures fall asleep in a radius about them. The level 15 bard song has this effect but becomes very highly resisted from 25 upwards. During the mid-levels a bard can generally cope with crowd control by a mixture of mezzing (Pixie Strike) and tanking. As mobs hit so much harder post-50 this becomes infeasible so a repirse of this role at this stage is in order. I would say that an area-effect mez song with about 3/4 the effectiveness of enchanter AE mez would sit well, forcing the healer to spend massive amounts of mana to keep the bard alive but at least making an encounter feasible with good teamwork. It also has the effect of allowing the bard to 'take a death' while the party fless in relative safety. I suggest level 50 for this.
All of these 3 songs have the common theme of giving the bard a way of helping group members when they need it most without adding significant power to the class. I believe this is the key in balancing them, to concentrate on giving them skills useful in certain situations but without increasing their power significantly across the board (as that is fine for the most part).
Lastly I can't finish without asking why the charm line finishes so abruptly and early. Bards get charm songs at level 27 and 39, so I don't think a song at level 51 would be out of order or give themn something that the vision of a bard suggests is wrong. However, as they already get 2 songs at level 51 perhaps waiting till level 52 is timely. Given the above ideas this would give high level bards the full range of crowd control abilities (target/area mez/stun, charm, blur) but just in a very limited way when compared to an enchanter.
One more thing - I wholeheartedly agree about the lute of howling. Such magic is too vulgar for a simple bard who's magic should generally be subtle IMHO. My suggestion here, rather than remove it, is to add a right-click effect that adds 10 STR and 10 ATK for 5 minutes - call it Howl to the Moon or some such.
