I am going to take some time here to pen down my thoughts not only on this current Enchanter issue, but on the Enchanter class generally.

I have been playing the Enchanter class since the game was released. In many ways I have watched as the class has developed from being seen as weak, almost to the point of being useless, to being recognised as one of the most powerful classes in the game.

I have also seen many of the eldest Enchanters question the Enchanters role in EQ as they have grown more experienced. This occurred most heavily when the level cap remained at 50. In the planes and on the two dragon raids available Enchanters were useless crack machines pumping out clarity. For a long time there was a despondency in the Enchanter class as we felt ignored and unlistened to as we explained how this was unfulfilling. With the increase in the level cap this has been alleviated to an extent. We can now mes effectively in the planes of the original release, and in some areas of Kunark, but in many ways enchanters still feel as if they have received the short end of the stick.

Before I go on I should explain where I am coming from. I see the Enchanter class as one which can perform miracles. They are the class which turns certain death into a victory. They are the class who are trusted to do their job even in the most trying of circumstances. Their true roles lie in crowd control, through the use of mes and charm, and in the area of buff and debuff. It is to these two areas I will discuss in just a moment.

I'd like to reflect for a moment on what the Enchanter was once able to do with their illusions. Once upon a time Enchanters were the best of scouts, able to vastly adjust their factions and reconnoitre. The design team decided that at high levels this was making dragon and plane raids too 'easy' to recover from and almost totally removed the ability. Nowadays there is a very limited use of collaboration at high levels to assist in scouting new areas, but by and large illusions have become pointless. Most of the statistic benefits from illusions are irrelevant to the class (unlike the shaman illusion), and generally they are just for looks. The only enchanter illusion really beneficial is more of a buff than an illusion in the guise of the new Boon of the Garou spell, but its long recast and short duration make the spell useless in all reality.

The design team fail to realise that an Enchanter only has 8 spell slots from a total of 211 possible spells. Whilst many of these are lower level, in most circumstances the Enchanter will have approximately 20 useful spells at any given time. Both the Wandering Mind spell, and Boon of Garou waste these slots horrendously. I will discuss this further in a moment.

Fundamentally the issue with illusions as I see it is this. There are two reasons why enchanters should have self-only long duration illusions at higher levels. Firstly, because they are illusionists. They are the one class who should be able to alter their form better than any other class. It is NOT an appropriate response to say "but you can already change to all the races". The issue at hand is that NO other class should be capable of turning themselves into something that an illusionist cannot if they are truly the master of illusions. If the illusions are with no statistical benefit then I do not see the problem. I understand the position that other classes deserve to have unique abilities to increase their enjoyment. And I do believe that this is a very valid point. The thing which grinds on the Enchanters nerves is that Enchanters have been told time and time again there is insufficient memory to add illusions. For a long time I argued for a goblin form illusion, a froglok form illusion or something which other classes do not have and was told there was no memory. Not a month after being told this a LARGE number of items received upgraded models. The point is that Verant's position on this issue changes in the wind as they come up with a new reason to deny requests to give the enchanters high level illusion spells. The claim that new illusions would provide faction to allow running anywhere they wanted is simply ignorant. The change to the faction of the spirit wolf illusion displays quite clearly that any faction (or none) can be associated with an illusion.

The issue of higher level illusions is no different to that of higher level summons for mages: the class feels incomplete without something to show their mastery of the craft. At this time Verant is failing to meet this perception from a large portion of the Enchanter community. It is this reason why their is so much unhappiness.

Leaving illusions aside for a moment I would like to reflect on other matters about the class at this time. Firstly Verant have to decide what the point of the Enchanter class is. We know for certain they are not considered a PvP class any longer since they removed the ability to charm or fear players on PvP servers, severely altered the mana sieve spell, and made high mr on players so easy to achieve that Enchanters in most situations may as well not bother casting at all. Verant have never made any substantial restitution to the destruction of the Enchanter PvP characters, and this commentary wouldn't be complete without mentioning this fact even if they are a smaller portion of the overall community of Enchanters.

Leaving PvP aside though there are still some common issues with the PvM Enchanter which we are most familiar with:

Firstly, the lack of a truly powerful MR debuff. Even though the Enchanter has an unresistable debuff of MR, the addition of unresistable debuffs to other classes which remove MORE than the enchanters was a blow. Especially considering that ALL enchanter spells are magic based. The level 57 spell Tashanian should reduce MR by 60, currently it is only slight better than the level 49 spell. This would be a great improvement in at least helping enchanters land spells on higher level monsters.

Secondly, the imposition of requiring only high level mes spells against high level mobs and players. The mana costs on these spells are obscene for the shortness of their duration. Whilst it is acknowledged that being able to take a monster of level 55 or higher out of action for a lengthy period is a powerful ability it also makes the Enchanter incredibly hampered. Most especially in Velious in zones where many mobs cannot even BE mesd. Now in this circumstance what is the Enchanters roles? They can cast clarity spells. hastes and Runes. There is no point casting slows as shamans quite deservedly excel in this area, and there is little point to their debuff as the unresistable high level shaman ones are also more powerful. Their group magic resistance is nice but easy to share around. So tell me - what is the role of the Enchanter in 55+ areas of Velious?

We have zero offensive capability in all truth, our charm spells don't work with any serious duration if at all, we cant mes. All we can do is every now and then right click on our epic weapon, and give the casters their mana drugs.

Our new Velious spells are pathetic. Three spells who have the sole purpose of increasing base mana with a pathetically low tag-on of mana regeneration is simply a joke. What enchanters needed were spells to make them viable in high level encounters and they received nothing in this regard.

Enchanter equipment is also way off the scale of practicality. Enchanters need only three things: Mana, HP, and AC. Whilst there rages a debate on the usefulness of charisma there have been numerous player tests to show that fundamentally charisma doesn't really make any difference once its over 150 (some say 120). Like the Agility cap of 75 Charisma isn't that important. Enchanters get hit. Our equipment should seriously help us to have a higher AC and overall Hitpoints than any other pure caster class, but it doesn't.

This COULD have been helped with our Velious spells but instead we received 3 spells which will never be cast unless the enchanter is bored with too much time on their hands (which probably will occur since for al intensive purposes they have zero combat role in many high end Velious zones), and an illusion which is pretty much a waste of a slot.

Lastly, and I bring you back to the issue of the long recast spells - if Enchanters are going to be reduced to buffers at high levels (because shamans totally destroy the enchanter when it comes to debuffing) then they need to be improved. It is all good and well to say but you can haste really well! But to be perfectly frank I would be very disappointed if the sole purpose of my class was to cast haste every 30 mins on the group. If that is what we are - high end buffers - if that is what the class is going to be reduced to, then our spells need to be modified at high levels to reflect this.

NO Enchanter spell (with the exception of the fixed duration charms) should have a recast time of more than 30 seconds. If they ARE going to be longer then these spells must be of such power that they last SIGNIFICANTLY longer, or provide SIGNIFICANTLY more benefit in the given buff or debuff. Enchanters need to be able to hot swap their buffs whilst maintaining their basic required spells of a mes, charm, stun, and tash (for what use it is).

Enchanters should receive more powerful spells besides simply haste buffs. They should receive the highest AC buffs (preferably in a group form), or alternatively receive a variety of group or targeted illusions which provide different benefits. A group innate cold, poison, or fire (etc) resistance illusions, or an illusion which provides group water breathing or levitation. This would provide enchanters with the ability to choose different illusion buffs of USEFUL consequence at higher levels. The memory to do it IS there and it IS available and it WOULD make the game more enjoyable not only for the class, but for players who would receive the benefit of a class which has a more significant role and purpose within the game.

The Enchanter class IS powerful. No one denies that. However the class is also going stale at higher levels because in many circumstances the class suddenly changes from an action packed crowd controlling crack dealing charming UNIQUE class, to a mana/haste/rune battery. There ARE alternatives to fix it, even if it means a need by the design team to reassess what the Enchanter class is really all about.

Baelish
