Norrathian Dictionary Reference for Cleric:

Main Entry: Cleric
Pronunciation: Cl'air'ik
Function: noun
Etymology: Middle Norrathian cleric, from Old Norrathian kler'ik, ultimately from Late Antonican presbyter -- more at PRESBYTER
: one authorized to perform the sacred rites of a religion especially as a mediatory agent between inhabitants of Norrath and their God(s); specifically : an Antonican, Dwarven, Elven, Gnomish or Erudite clergyman ranking below a bishop and above a deacon (note, we do not recognize as clerics, the practitioners of dark healing arts that are part of the fell races - kobolds, orcs, etc)
;healer, protector of the faithful
;dispenser of divine wrath

Ok, so my argument starts with the premise that Clerics are healers/buffers, and that their secondary function is dealing with infidels (as compared to their faith) by bringing down their God's divine wrath. By comparison, Paladins are more the justicars of their faith, generally roaming the countryside and using their God-given prowess as a fighter to deal with brigands and the like - they receive some divine powers to support them in their quests, but their primary ability lay in their martial skills. A paladin is expected to be more martial in bearing, and able to deal with more "wordly" issues, whereas Clerics first function in life is definately as a healer, but when circumstances warrant, they should be able to deal with more "other-worldly" adversaries (undead, arcane) with the powers their God has granted them.

Taking this to the game setting, we see that Clerics currently are the foremost healers in the world. Shaman and Druids are no where near as functional in that regard. On the other hand, Clerics have no reasonable expectation of being able to solo effectively - not that they should necessarily - but when you start talking about granting better healing powers to these other classes, you definately need to take this into account.

As it stands right now, the MAIN reason you take clerics on UBER raids is to ressurrect people. Healing is certainly nice, but in general the cleric is there to res. Reducing downtime CAN be accomplished through healing, but it can also be accomplished with enchanters mezzing, shamans slowing, using pets to tank, etc - and with Druids and Shaman you get healing as well. So if you look at it in that light, a cleric is already somewhat redundant in a party setting, again, mainly brought along to perform res. The res ability is more in demand for when you are in a zone that there is no easy way out of (Veeshan's Peak, etc), but also for going deep into zones that you don't want to have to fight your way back into. You will see on many raids Clerics are told to camp (Fear, Hate, etc) when things go bad, so they can come back after everything has settled to res people back in. Giving summon corpse to necros and SK has somewhat mitigated this dependency, as well as giving paladins the ability to perform res.

Based on all of this, I would like to suggest the following:

1. Clerics should be given some more effective damage-wise 1-shot nukes - ie they should have a nuke as powerful as, or more powerful than, ice comet but the recast time should only allow its use 1 in a game day. Probably the magic type could be different based on the God they follow (thus gving some reason to follow different Gods) so that some clerics might be cold-based, some fire, some poison, etc. This would be considered their "Divine Wrath" type spell that they could receive a version of at 51, (maybe another at 55) and 60 that would require a quest through their temple to receive. Also make it so I cannot use this nuke on creatures of my God.

2. On a similar thought to the first item, make it so I can not cast damage spells on creatures that worship my God.... hmm... not sure what the implementation of this one would cause, it is just a thought.

3. Invis - common request, maybe at 34th or 39th you can give Clerics self-only invis (and see invis) so they can get around to perform their res function a little easier. You already give necros DMF at 44th which as a spell, has little to do with their class (not that that is justification for this, just precedent) - it seems emminently reasonable to give Clerics invis at higher level, and probably not much of an imbalance issue.

3. Possibly make a temple quest spell available in the mid levels to give the cleric some form of short burst mana regen ability. Call it Divine Meditation - the Cleric is stunned for 30 seconds (they are meditating) and gets back maybe 30 percent of their mana from the spell, recast every 10 minutes.

4. Concerning Stuns - make the stun component less resistable, but allow the damage to be resisted independently - just don't stop the stun if the damage is resisted. I would just like my stuns to work, but the only one that does consistently is the level 5 stun... seems kind of strange in the grand scheme of things that my higher level stuns are less effective.... In any case, this is one of the Clerics secondary functions - seems funny to give me 4 levels of a stun spell only to make them almost useless post 50.

5. It seems Clerics should be MORE effective verus undead, but there is really nothing in the game that makes that true. All other classes that currently are better in martial and damage dealing abilities are better than Clerics at dealing with undead. Cleric's pre-50 undead spell costs a little less than Reckoning and does less damage - its main claim to fame being almost instant recast, but then we hardly get to use them because we are (pre-50) there to heal most of the time (assuming giving better heals to Shaman/Druids would allow Clerics more lattitude to nuke). Seems like our undead damage could be upped considerably, as there are few post 50 undead that we have to deal with on a regular basis. If Clerics are master undead nukers, they should receive an Ice Comet level undead nuke at 44th or 49th that is only usable on undead. Maybe as a reason to even have undead nukes, you make it so that nukes not specifically tailored to undead only do 75% of their normal damage. Again, that would make it more desirable to have a cleric in a group earlier on when dealing with undead, and you aren't totally wanting a cleric just to res. Give the undead nukes normal cast recycle times.

I think if you looked at implementing some, or all, of these ideas it would help give Clerics a more effective purpose to being in the game other than just primary res and healing/buffing. I think these ideas would also be good as offsets if you intend to give better heals to Shaman and Druids. At least if you give Shamans and Druids better heals, Clerics might actually be able to be a bit more offensive in groups than they currently are - I don't think anything I suggested here would be unbalancing, especially with the low number of undead encounters at UBER level - kinda nil at this point. Also, I think these changes would help with the boredom most clerics are seeing at higher levels and definately in post-50 play.

CONCERNING MATERIAL COMPONENTS:

1. Any spell that requires a material component to cost should have a very long duration. Lev = bat wing; End breath = fish scales; Symbols = cat's eye, jasper, peridot; etc. Currently the duration of most of these spells is VERY short compared to the fact you need a material component. Then at 44th level you give Necro's DMF that lasts forever and gives lev/breath/poison resist/see invis with NO material component. Even the mana cost isn't that significant. Any spell with a material component should last at least an hour most likely. Look at pets - they have some of the cheapest casting components around, yet they last forever unless they are killed or you zone - although the Cleric pet spell is just DUMB (see, enchanters don't have the worst pet in the game anymore).

-OR-

2. Material components should be CHEAP. Peridots should cost maybe 1pp to 2pp each at most and price others accordingly and then leave the spell durations alone.

BUFFS/CURES:

As it is for a Cleric, the AC buffs cost a large amount of mana for the benefit received, so the long duration is based on mana instead of a component. The HP/AC buffs are cheaper, and some have good duration, that is fine since the principle buff a Cleric can give is HP.

It does make some kind of sense that as a Cleric I would be able to abolish poison AND disease...

Regen seems best left to Druids and Shaman - they are the nature guys

PETS?:

Speaking of the Cleric pet spell - can we like, get rid of it or something? The idea was kinda cute, but I can't even parade it around as a parlor trick - it just pops. If you could give it a few more hps that would be great, you could make it material component based - I need a certain type of hammer to summon it, but then make it so it doesn't pop immediately, or, if I can retarget a bad guy within 10 seconds it can fight that creature....? I dunno, my first thought is just get rid of it

There are two big pictures to consider here.

The first is balancing group desirability and soloability.

We are the masters of group desirability. Even the healing changes to shamans and druids won't change this. It will mean that sometimes we will go LFG for longer because some of the groups in the zone already took a druid or shaman rather that wait for us to wander in. So be it.

We all know that we can't solo to save our lives. Killing 3 very light blues per hour, if we're lucky, running from them because the make a couple resists and we can't finish the fight when we're unlucky, is no way to solo for advancement in the game.

The issues we need to focus on, then, are issues that make us stronger when not grouped.

Of course, Verant has already stated that this is a grouping game and they will be reluctant to consider any enhancements to a classes solo viability, so we're probably SOL on asking for soloing enhancements, but that is where we need them

Some things that help us when not in groups:

Better spells vs. undead, particularly our undead DD line. Did you realize that at 49th level, wizards can do 2x as much damage to undead as clerics of the same level with fewer resists? Undead DoTs would help, too. And not that crappy SoV 39th spell that nobody can find and those who are lucky enough to find it, usually well into their mid-40s, don't use it because Banish Undead is far, far superior in damage output and mana efficiency.

This might help in groups in, say, Lguk. But even there, I am often busy enough healing that I won't be nuking the undead often enough to matter. Except when I am stuck lfg and decide to solo a few blues while waiting for a group.

Traveling spells will help us find groups, get to groups, and possibly help us solo. It would be nice, after burning a sand giant to the ground, to be able to throw a normal invis while I med. If nothing else, it will keep the stupid pumas from aggroing on me.

Ports to our home temple, or even better, to any temple (once we earn the faction and do the quest to obtain the spell) would be very nice. I don't expect to get it, primarily because I think there is some voice at Verant who has convincingly put forth the suggestion that having the classes most group-required be the least mobile is balancing. And in a weird kind of way, that is true. If everyone wants a cleric, but it will take an hour for the only ones LFG to get here, then we might as well ask a druid who can be here in 5 minutes. But it still would be nice to get a temple-port bone thrown our way.

Asking for mana regen is nice. Very nice. I do believe that since every other caster class has some way to regain some mana that it is time to add some balance to clerics and give is this ability too. And for the most part, this doesn't enhance our grouping game nearly as much as our soloing game. In a Lguk example, this might mean killing 15 ghouls instead of 14. No big deal. Soloing, it might mean going from 3 blues an hour to 5 or 6. A big improvement.

Whoever suggested a Peridot for it is sadly wrong. Burning a peridot for a Harvest-like spell is not soloable, it is only going to be used in groups. When I'm OOM and willing to burn 12 plat to get enough mana to save my tank, I'll use it. When I have finished burning down a mob that drops 2-6 plat, I won't burn 12 plat so I can kill one more of them a little sooner.

No, if we get a Clarity-clone, maybe a peridot is justified. But we won't. If we get a Harvest-clone, no reagent worth more than a plat or two is justified, and even that is unnecessarily detrimental.

Skill caps. Primarily Defense, Dodge, 1HB, 2HB, and Bash. All of these could be looked at and improved. Again, these skills will benefit us more in soloing than they will in grouping. Grouping will still be med/heal, but soling we take more of a beating more often and requires some defense skills more often. Soloing we are more likely to want to stun a caster with bash, or melee his last sliver of HP with our weapon skills. Adding a little Parry or Double Attack is a good idea, too.

Again, the reasoning for this is simple. We wear the best class of armor (plate-class), we carry shields our entire life (except most clerics switch to paws/books/homework/etc. because our shield is usually worthless), and we wield a weapon that, in the real history of Earth, was a favorite of knights and soldiers because it was so lethal to heavily armored opponents. All this points to being a factor in a battle. Maybe not a big factor, but it does indicate that we should be some kind of factor in a battle. If we were a robe-wearing, staff-carrying old bearded feeble priest, then I wouldn't expect to be a factor in a battle.

I would never suggest we should infringe on real tanks in battle. Just that we should be able to have a little impact (no blunt weapon pun intended).

The second big picture is low levels vs. high levels.

Clearly, in the high level game, we are extremely desired. In the low-mid level game, we are often second-class citizens. Particularly at levels 19-24 and at levels 29-34.

In lower levels, we compete with shamans and druids who have similar, and for 15 levels identical, healing abilities. Yet those two classes have all their other useful utility spells.

Most class-talk threads, ours included, have focused on the high-level game. But I think clerics, of all classes, have the hardest time before reaching the high-levels. We just don't offer enough to groups, until 39th level, to really be in demand yet.

The solution here is harder to reach. We can't ask for higher skill caps (though our Dodge cap is so awkwardly low that raising it would help us in the mid-levels). We can't ask for pets, or more healing spells, or better undead nukes (at these levels our undead nukes are just fine.). So how can we fix the low-mid levels?

Well, adding Bash and Double attack might be a bit of a start. Letting us join the tanks in the front line and make a difference would really improve our desirability in groups of this level.

Another big, big help would be to move our healing spells around so that there is no level, not one, where a druid or shaman is casting the same healing spell we are. Well, maybe all three of us get the same healing spell to start the game, but after that, we should always upgrade one grade of healing spell ahead of druids and shamans. That would make us more desirable for healing to offset being less desirable for utility.

Another thing might be to tweak our entire line of AC buffs, starting with Holy Armor and ending with Bulwark of Faith. Holy armor adds the same AC as a banded bracer. Big deal. It helps, sure, but not enough. I never heard anyone saying "hey, can I get that extra 6 ac now, I really need it." This line of spells needs to be reevaluated and enhanced to where other group members are asking for the spells. Especially in low levels. Perhaps a 50% jump to every spell in this line, maybe a bit less of a jump in the higher levels where tanks are asking for them. I don't even care if the mana cost is increased accordingly. The tanks aren't looking at mana cost, they are looking at how much their AC jumps after we cast it.

I don't have many suggestions for our low-level through mid-level game. Making us fight a little better, making our AC buff more desirable, and moving healing spells around a bit so our heal is preferable to any druid or shaman of the same level would probably be enough.

I welcome any other thoughts.

Oh, and please. We don't need better Bind Wounds. Sure, from a role-playing perspective, this skill belongs to us healer types. But I can sit and med, without clarity, then cast a healing spell, all in less time and for less money than I could bind wounds. So unless clerics receive some kind of 3x modifier to how effective the results of binding wounds are, as in one successful bind wounds heals 3x as much HP as other classes, then I would never use this skill. And neither would any other cleric, probably.

Also, Turn Undead? Are you kidding? I can see it now. Cleric in Frenzy room in Lguk. Puller brings too many ghoulies, so cleric saves the day by turning undead. Result? Entire groups are wiped out at Lord, Ass/Sup, Frenzy, etc. as the entire dungeon is trained by 30 or 40 enraged ghoulies. Even the group with the foolish cleric will get wiped out. No thanks.

If by Turn Undead you basically apply a fixed-duration root and a fixed-duration snare so the mob turns its back and stands there, not attacking, then maybe it's viable. Especially if it is short duration but attacking/nuking the turned undead doesn't break it. That's probably a bit much, though. Unless there is a very long reset timer on the ability, like there is on Lay on Hands.

Wow, this post is long enough. Sorry to ramble.

Morodullin
Rambling Priest o Brell
