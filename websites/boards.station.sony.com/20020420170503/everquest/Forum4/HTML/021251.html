The subject says it all.

I was in a group that camped orcs in Misty Thicket, and my share of the loot was 19 Deathfist Slashed Belts. Later that night, I headed to East Commonlands to unload them for some plat. After putting out the auction to sell them, I received two tells, one for plat and one for a trade. The first offered 65pp for all of them and the second offered a Glowing Stone Band.

First mistake - passing the plat in hopes of eventually unloading the GSB. I decide to trade.

Monday I go to Allakhazam's to check the price for it, and the average price is 405pp. I thought that was pretty good, and proceeded to read the posts below where I saw a Barbarian on my server just two weeks before buying one from "a fellow barbarian" for 400pp. Splendid!!!

Monday night I log and auction the band for 400pp

Silence......

Last night I log again and

/auction WTS Glowing Stone Band - 400pp OR WTT Glowing Stone Band for AC/HP ring(s)

I immediately receive a tell stating that "those won't even sell for 200pp, and nobody is going to trade for it"

Of course, I don't always believe everyone I receive a tell from, but I knew the band had a couple strikes against it:

1) Only had a single purpose - Unlimited Serpent Sight (infravision) while worn

2) Only beneficial to a limited number of races/classes

Not to mention 400pp is quite a bit to just have some better vision when it's dark.

"Common sense" took over and I decided to auction it for 150pp. I get another tell. Someone had no money, but was willing to trade spore covered armor for it. Don't ask what got into my tiny brain, but I was thinking FUNGI!!!!! LOL!!!!

I said, "SURE!!!"

I met the individual at the tunnel and proceeded to trade the band for the following:

Spore Covered Hood
Spore Covered Sleeves
Spore Covered Gloves

Of course, I knew fungi stuff got a pretty good price, but after realizing later that I had something else in mind when I accepted the terms of the trade, I decided to log and sleep before I do something else stupid!!! Not to mention, I now had NO idea what spore covered is worth.

Now I have to try and make heads or tails of the situation so I can at least come out with something useful for the DF Slashed that I unloaded.

I learned my lesson - I hope.......

Colga - lvl 14 hum/monk, Innoruk

Hmmm... I would have paid 400pp for that ring. I have a human warrior, and I've been trying to find stuff that gives infravision for quite a while.

Also, never believe people who send you tells saying that something's not worth what you're auctioning it for. I *always* get idiots telling me that I'm nuts for trying to sell something at the price I'm selling it for. I remember two instances in the recent past. One was for a polished steel ulak I was trying to sell for around 300 or 350pp, per the pricing on Alakhazam's at the time. I got a tell from someone telling me I was nuts, it was a player made item, not worth more than 100pp, etc. etc. The guy would not shut up, and I eventually put him on my ignore list. Within a day or two I sold it for my asking price.

Same thing with some Ry'Gorr legs. I was auctioning them for 650pp. Got a tell saying I was nuts, they were only worth 200pp. I said "OK, thanks for your advice" and kept auctioning them for 650pp. I got the full price within 5 minutes.

The law for selling is always supply and demand. Maybe that ring isn't "worth" 400pp, but at the right time and place, I would have bought it. There's plenty of times I'll spend plat on stuff I could get easily, simply because I don't want to spend the time to get it myself. In the case of my #$!@$ night-blind human warrior, infravision is quite a nice thing. Had my warrior been an elf, there's no way I'd buy it.

Sometimes I accept trades for things I don't know very well, but before accepting I check what the item stats is.

For instance, I had an amulet of insight for sale. I was told it was worth 1,5K but could not find any buyer. Suddenly a lady offers to trade it for a Kobold jester's crown. A strange item 15 AC +30 CHA -30 INT +5 DEX -5AGI Weight :0.0
The lady said it can be sold easily for 2K to bards and monks. It made sense to me (especially bards because CHA help for charms) so I accepted. The next day I sold it 2K to a monk (15 AC is quite good for them)

Another example : a guy tell in /bazaar that he is selling a velium swiftblade cheap for 4K. I ask him what the usual PC is and he answers 6K, but he needs money to buy the FBSS. I do the trade.
Later on I traded the Swiftblade + 500 pp for Bone Handled Scimitar (aslo value unknown to me) + Swarmcaller.
I just sold the swarmcaller 1,25 K and intend to sell Scimitar 5K or twink my druid until I can sell it good. Anyway 1,75K profit ...

Gotta be careful.

Often times when someone offers an item for trade worth more than what you are giving them, what means you are going to have a hell of a time trying to sell it. Either it's worthless, farmed into oblivion, or not many races/classes can use it.

I make this mistake still sometimes, but the best thing to remember is don't make any trade where you don't know for sure the true value of all the items involved.
