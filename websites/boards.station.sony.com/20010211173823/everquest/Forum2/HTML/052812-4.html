Absor....

My comments from my perspectives of my two monks (21 and 17 HUM/IKS respectively) and from the perspective of a post-50 cleric with a bunch of monk friends...

-------------------------------------------------

Comparisons of the three Melee classes:

We have rogue, warrior, and monk. I would like to say that I think rogue and warrior are well balanced. The rogue, in my opinion, is the supreme damage dealer in a group, not the warrior, or even the wizard. Properly equipped rogues outdamage every other melee, in my experience. On the other hand, warriors. Warriors do decent damage to mobs, even though that's not their true function. The explicit purpose of the warrior is to take all the damage from the mob when possible, and to that end they have extra HP's and incredible armor helping them out.

In my opinion, the current hierarchy is this:

Damage Done:
1) Rogue
2) Warrior(*)
3) Monk(*)
(A properly equipped monk will outdamage a warrior, but it is far more difficult to outfit a monk than a warrior in the weapons department. Also, see my next point)
Damage Taken:
1) Warrior
2) Rogue(*)
3) Monk(*)
I don't think monks are better at tanking than rogues. They have slightly better caps, but that is mitigated by the fact they take damage at all. Let me explain:

Rogues have Evade. Warriors have Warrior bonuses. These both mitigate Damage Taken. Monks have "Feign Death" except that that really doesn't help!

Warrior tanks, their job is simple. If a rogue gets agro'd, they will evade. A good rogue can stay almost entirely off the hatelist, and they will be swinging EVERY round.

A good monk will have to Feign (loosing a few rounds, possibly up to 1000 damage a fight (5 feigns)) while they wait for someone else's agro to build up. They can't be main tank, perhaps they are better than a rogue, but in a group situation they end up wasting cleric mana when they try to tank and there's a warrior present.

--------------------------------------------------

Conclusion/Comments:

I would like to see Monks get Parry, a fourth defense skill. This would help lessen the damage they take. I would also like to see the AC on a bunch of their equipment upgraded (Shiverback? hah!). If possible, give them third attack. And finally, and most importantly, give them a way to move up and down the hatelist WITHOUT halting their attacks. Discipline, Evade, whatever.

Their damage is comparible to a warrior, but their defense is not. Despite what Abashi said months ago, in my experience monks can and often are outdamaged by warriors (and this is post-50) and I believe that they deserve both an offensive and defensive increase, as well as an evade or discipline that will lower their agro.

A monk that is tanking when there is a warrior in the group is going to end up wasting the cleric's mana. A monk that can't stop tanking except by feigning is wasting combat rounds.

- Gaeb

Ok problems I see :

Using *only* their melee weapons and no special abilities (even reflexive ones), monks, rogues and warriors will do almost the same damage with the same weapons ( jade maces are a good example ).

Add in the special abilities and we get:
Monk
Flying kick - flat damage increase not tied to weapon.

Warrior
Critical hit - low percentile increase based on weapon.
Triple attack - low percentile increase based on weapon.
Kick - low percentile increase based on weapon.

Rogue
Backstab - high percentile increase based on weapon.
---

The problem is fairly obvious - flying kick is lagging drastically behind where it once was. While once providing an indisputable damage advantage over a warrior, it is now in many cases exceeded by the combined damage of critical hit, triple hit and kick/bash.

This means that for a monk to outdamage a warrior, they need better weapons, and the more powerful the weapon is to start with, the bigger the bonus for the monk weapon needs to be. This will continue to get worse the more new, improved, weapons are added to the game.

---

Miscellaneous points of imbalance :

Jade mace : ( 9/18 most powerful primary mid-high 1HB shared with monks, warriors, rogues, rangers )

Willsapper : ( 13/20 roughly equivalent to monk epic fists with powerful slow proc. )

Double backstab : completely bucking the trend of all velious disciplines, this ability has no cost or drawback, while providing a large increase in melee efficiency. In a token effort at "balancing" this ability, monks were given a potential 68 points of AC in the form of increased weight limits.

Assassinate vs Ashen Hand : Assassinate is another total freebie. It has no cost, it has no disadvantage, it is used 100% of the time.
Ashen hand has a 72 min recycle, prevents you from using any other discipline, requires a weak untrained attack with a low cap, has a substantial chance of failure, provides no experience, leaves no corpse.

Evade : the increased rogue damage would be impossible without it, monk damage signficantly exceeding warrior cannot happen without it.

Complete Heal : made free in the form of Donal's breastplates, it substantially lessens the relative tanking ability of every class except warrior. To add insult to injury, many uber encounters are "balanced" against raid groups having warriors tank with defensive and CH.

Discipline Defensive : uber mob encounters in SoV are balanced against this discipline. This means that any raid attempting to do without this discipline has almost no hope of success.
