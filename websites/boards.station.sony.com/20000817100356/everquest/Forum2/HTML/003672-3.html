Goatweasel, you make it sound as if rogues and wizards are the only ones who can reduce the amount of time a party spends killing a mob and therefore increase the total number of kills. You're not thinking outside the box.

Clerics increase the amount of kills by reducing tank downtime. This adds up to a boatload of extra kills in ANY party, and tank-heavy parties especially.

Rangers increase the number of kills by dealing a lot of damage and, especially important in dungeons, by snaring and preventing runners and therefore needless party deaths. They can also harmony and split spawns to help with crowd control and thereby increase efficiency.

Monks and warriors added to any party will increase productivity. I think this one's a no-brainer.

Enchanters have vastly increased party productivity in any group I've seen. Competent Enchanters, at least. But we're assuming sufficient competency in all these examples anyway.

I could go on and on, but there isn't a need (I hope). Sheer damage dealing power is not the only thing to consider when you're talking about increasing the number of mobs a party can handle. IN fact, I'd say that rogues are on the bottom of the list if you're talking about efficiency. They can only work on one mob at a time and they have to have a taunting tank to be effective. Classes like cleric, shaman, and enchanter can make a party mow down mobs two or three times as fast as the party could without them. As a tank, I've got a front-line perspective on this sort of thing.

While I DO agree that backstabbing is a really nice thing to add to a party, there are lots of other classes I'd take before I took a rogue, even *forgetting* about the pickpocket issue. Rogues are fine for what they do, don't get me wrong. It's just that I don't think you were fair to make it seem like none of the other classes could do what rogues and wizards can do.

As an aside... I played a wizard. I don't think they really increase the amount of mobs a party can handle more than any other class. (Please realize that I'm comparing efficiency here.) A ranger or paladin could probably dish the same amount of damage over the long haul as a wizard doze because wizards just have so much damned downtime. Adding an enchanter to a party would really help a wizard, but otherwise they're effectively hamstrung by mana considerations. That's why they ***** a lot.
