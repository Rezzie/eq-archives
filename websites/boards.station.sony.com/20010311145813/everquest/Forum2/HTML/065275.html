This is regarding the clerical Plane of Growth breastplates, the ones with the ability to cast Celestial Elixer (the Chestplate of Stability, Akkirus' Chestplate of the Risen, the Breastplate of Forbidden Rites and the Templar's Chestplate). For many of the same reasons that we made recent changes to the Donal's Breastplate, we are going to make some changes to these items with the next patch (which will be next week).
Existing instances of these Plane of Growth breastplates will have the casting time for Celestial Elixer increased to one minute.

No new instances of the Plane of Growth breastplates will occur (they will stop dropping).

In places where the Plane of Growth breastplates were being created (off of NPC corpses or as the result of quests) new breastplates will be created.

Basically, the existing breastplates are having their casting times increased and they won't be dropping anymore, new breastplates will take their place.

I'm sorry that these items are being changed so long after their introduction. But this is something that we feel needs to be done for the integrity of gameplay in EverQuest.

Alan

First of all, why does someone always have to say "First" in forums, seems stupid to me. Do you win something? Or is everyone else a rotten egg or something?

The reason why Verant has such disregard for higher levels and the "End Game" is simple economics. It works out better for their profit margins, and is much easier not to care, rather than rise to the challenge of creative coding and game design. To meet such a challenge takes considerable resources, and alot of talent. Things that Sony/VI might not be willing to apply.

Here are some reasons why:

BANDWIDTH:

Everquest's subscriber base is like an ISP's subscriber base. 90% of the resources are used by 5 to 10 percent of the subscriber base.

Bandwidth is the most obvious one. The hardcore players, the highlevel ones that keep getting screwed, spend the most time playing, use up alot of bandwidth, that Sony/VI have to pay for. These hardcore players are fully utilizing, and exceeding the $10 a month they are paying in subscriber fees. Basically they are losing money on the players that play all the time, just on bandwidth ( haven't even got to the other issues with these players yet ). Sony/VI want casual players who only play a couple hours here and there, they don't cost very much in bandwidth, this means profit for Sony/VI.

This is exactly the same thing that ISP's desire, a bunch of subscribers that only casually use their connections, but still pay the monthly fees. They make all their profit of these people, the 90% that only use 10% of the bandwidth. ISP's are recalcitrant to try and keep the hardcore users too happy, because if they leave they aren't losing any money, infact they are making more since the hardcore user isn't there to eat up more bandwidth then their subscription fees cover.

ATTENTION SPAN:

Sony/VI know that each subscriber has a certain amount of time that they will end up playing EverQuest. Players who are in the higher levels are nearing the end of the time they will be playing. Why spend money and effort on players who most likely in the end of their playing cycle, getting ready to move onto something else?

DEVELOPMENT EFFORT/ COST vs EXISTING CONTENT:

This one is a little more subtle, but has a huge profit motive.

Caring and supporting the higher levels REQUIRES new content such as items, monsters, lands, spells, abilities, and quests. This equals design time, development time, and testing time. This equals dollars that don't go into profit. Sony/VI already has a vast world that has already been designed, coded, and tested. This is the pre 50 lvl world that has been around for 2 years. It doesn't cost them any money ( other than normal maintenance ) to maintain this as is. In fact they have had tens of thousands of people PAY $10 a month to test it for them. This means that if they can just keep a low-level, casual, or new user in the pre 50 plus world, they don't have to spend additional money and effort to maintain their world. Supporting the higher levels doesn't allow this as any high level player can't find anything to do in the pre 50 world other than parlor tricks to impress low levels.

Sony/VI would love not to have to produce any additional content, and just sit back and reap the profits off of work already done. This is another example of the ISP metaphor, 90% of the resources going to 5% to 10% of the subscribers.

IMMEDIATE PLAYABILITY vs LONG TERM:

This breaks down rather simply. You have already paid the $40 for the EQ cd, and probably $30 for the Velious expansion. By this late in the game, the EQ package is fully paid for, hence pure profit, and Velious with its 4 page manual of outdated data and cd that came with it, is surely pure profit also. Sony/VI is pretty sure they can't soak you for much more that way. Other MMORPGs do monthly or similiarly frequenced content updates and such as part of the game, like a feature that comes with part of your subscription. Sony/VI chooses to release content updates in 9 month spaced updates and charge you $30 dollars for it. To ensure that the update is attractive to lower level players, they put usability enchancements into the upgrade. So instead of giving everyone the ability to manipulate inventory without switching resolutions, they make you buy the expansion pack. Velious is only viable for 30 + characters. The casual users I referred to above are who Sony/VI wants to entice into buying the expansion pack. Most of these players aren't at this level of play. So they force you to buy the expansion in order to reap the rewards of usabilty enchancements, which in my opinion should be part of paying for a service. The early/mid-level game is what is important inorder to sell new pure profit game cds.

This leads to halfhearted, "comprise" type additions to marginally appease the non-profitable highlevel customers, but still make the game a bit more attractive the casual player who may work their way up to mid levels or who may want usability enhancements only available by purchasing such addtions.

Given some high levels buy 2 or more addition cd's to have mule characters. However this is sure to be a small percentage, and for reasons mentioned above Sony/VI does not want these customers. Sure they make the extra money off of the game cd sale, but now they have a hardcore gamer using twice the resources they were before. Won't take long before that gamer eats up the profit of the additional game cd sale.

MANAGEMENT CONCERN OVER HOW HIGHLEVELS ARE AFFECTED BY BIAS:

The lack of concern over highlevel characters by the upper echelon, probably allows personal preferences to affect changes. Being that only the non-profitable hardcore customers are up in arms about changes being made, management isn't going to question whether program managers or developers are bending the game to their biases. Not necessarily their fault, management usually doesn't know what is actually going on in the ranks below them. They only know what their leads tell them, and what the spreadsheets say. Thus they are number, thus dollar driven.

Many people really don't like the fact that it took them 6 months to work there way up when the world was new and uncharted, and now players who come after them can do it in a matter of weeks because it has been done already. Some of those people may now work in positions that they use against the "newbies" they resent. Just like the DM who gets attached to his MOBs, some of the developers and testers might be attached to theirs. The creature that they put so much time and thought into can't be just be kicked around, so they surmise that they gave the players too much loot.

I think that it is probably less sinister than this, however I believe it is an angle worth attention.

INDICATIONS:

What does this mean?

1. Highlevel players as a small minority of the subscriber base, cost more to support as they tend to be the hardcore players that consume the resources. Furthermore, since they are a small minority, they contribute substantially less to the revenue stream.

2. Casual players who spend small amounts of time in comparision to the hardcore players are a majority, and thus consume less resources while generating a substantially larger revenue stream.

3. Highlevel players are likely to be nearing the end of their play time with EQ anyway. So even the highlevel players who aren't resource hogs, are perceived to be less likey to generate revenue for much longer, thus making retaining them unimportant.

4. Creating content for the highlevel characters is expensive requiring financial and talent resources to be allocated to EQ in order to design and implement such content.

5. Keeping the existing content that has been designed, coded, and tested, is sufficient for players less then about lvl 50. Thus the game requires no further investment in development resources, and is probably sustainable as such for some period of time.

6. Sony/VI have a vested interest in selling the cds as the profit motive is high since the overhead has been drastically reduced this late in the product lifecycle with so many copies sold and such an extended time in production.

7. The highlevel characters have already long since paid for the game cds, thus making the new player the important market since they will need to purchase game cds.

8. Management most likely isn't concerned about the costlier highlevel characters, thus allowing less oversight over the actions of developer's who personal biases may affect how design and implemtation decisions are made.

SUMMATION

Basically it comes down to this:

Sony/VI is a business, plain and simple. The marketing crap is just that, crap. They don't want to make a great game, the most immersive world online, or strive to be a great customer service company. They are here to make money. It is really that simple. Highlevel characters threatening to quit doesn't bother them, they are losing money on those players. As long as the short term game is attractive, the revenue will come in, making profit. This makes Sony/VI a good business and that's that.

I am pretty sure it is nothing personal against any class or race, it is what the company is here to do, succeed by being profitable.
