I did a couple of buff tests last night and here is what I found entertaining.
I am a 20 lvl shaman and when I buff myself one of the buffs is "Spirit of the Bear" As I have not gotten "Sprit of the Ox" yet.
First we all know from everything said so far that the only thing buffing stamina does is add hit points, that said here goes...
When I buff SoB it costs me 40 mana, it buffs my stamina by +15, and my HPs go up +15 that is 2.67 mana/hp.
If this trend keeps up SoOx will cost me 60 mana for +23 Sta and +23 HP which is 2.61 mana/hp.
Now lets look at a level 1 spell Inner Fire. IF costs 10 mana and adds 20 hp that calculates to a ratio of 0.5 mana/hp. hmm?
Now lets look at level 34. We get
1) Health - 80 mana for +29 Sta/hp 2.76 mana/hp (even more costly than SoOx)
2) Tal. of T'Narg - 125 mana for +134hp which is 0.933 mana/hp

Judging by these calculations the +STA spells are just very inefficient +hp spells that seem to get more ineffecient at higher levels. Just something to think about, and I am sure the same calculations could be done for the +AC and +AGI spells.
