6. Statbuffs need to be effective and worth casting.

It's been parsed and we understand what stats do. We also understand for the mana expended, they are JUNK compared to a haste buff. They equate to
nothing but a placebo for most occasions.

Strength:
Most melee classes want strength because it increases their maximum damage and they think "Gee golly this is great!". Well, to the Shaman who has to cast and keep STR buffs up on 15 melee characters it is a waste of time,
resources, and the raid is being slowed down to give out a placebo confidence boost. Here is why: Strength only increases the MAXIMUM damage,
it does not increase the "magic number". Every weapon has a "magic number". If you parse enough logs and total the number of times you hit a target, you will notice one damage point hits far more times then any other number. This is called the magic number. If strength were to effect the magic number in such a way that the AVERAGE hit was for more damage, it would be useful.
Since it does not, it is merely eye-candy (like crits) and a placebo effect for any melee that is not a flying kick or backstab type. Let me illustrate:

5 Damage weapon
Magic number: 2*5=10
The majority of hits by this hypothetical 5 base damage weapon will be for 10 points of damage (the magic number). The player may also do a few extra points of damage over and above the magic number, depending on str, level, skill among other things.

Let's say the maximum damage you can hit for with this hypothetical 5dmg weapon without being str buffed is 14 points. If we represent 14 points of
damage with asterisks it would look like this (one * per 1 point dmg):
[**************]

Occasionally, due to a strength buff the player MIGHT hit for an additional 1 or 2 points damage.
[***************]

All the strength buff adds is "*". SOMETIMES. Compare that to the mana required for a haste buff, which can increase attack speed 50, 60, even 70% and you can see how truly useless it is to cast strength when you can cast haste.

Strength of course also allows you to carry more weight, and it seems to have the effect that if your weapons skill is not maxxed it makes up for
that defficiency and allows you to hit almost or as hard as if your weapon skill was maxxed. For more info on strength buffs check this post out:
http://forums.eqforge.com/shaman/showthread.php3?threadid=3678

Agility:
Agility is an easy one to figure out. If you have less than 75 Agility, you will take a severe AC hit. Thus most players will want to keep at least 75 agility at all times. Past the 75 Agility "hump", there are only 2 benefits
given by an agility buff. You will gain 1AC per 5 points of Agility buff. Thus a 100 point Agility buff would give 20 points of AC which would
theoretically be a good thing if not for the massive stacking problems of Avatar!! Two, agility helps increase the speed at which defensive skills (like defense, dodge) are raised. Other then that agility has no real value.

Dexterity:
I lost the link to the thread but an old Shaman posted their results of testing Dexterity to be not really worth casting. They had found for every
10 points of dexterity buffed, .05% or .5% increased chance of proccing occured (again, going from memory here). So let's say you buffed someone's dexterity and they have an additional 5% chance to proc. It's not additive. That doesnt mean if you already have a 5% chance to proc this will increase that chance to 10%. It means you will gain 5% of 5% additional chance to
proc. This really doesn't seem worth buffing under most circumstances. The other effect is offensive melee skills are learned faster (1hb, 2hb, 1hs, 2hs, piercing, offense, etc).

Stamina:
This is probably the only stat I don't gripe about buffing or needing to be buffed. More stamina equals more hitpoints, and everyone needs extra hitpoints to stay alive. Stamina=good, nuff said.

With the effectiveness of these stats above known, the veil of ignorance concerning them has been lifted from most high level players who instead opt for Haste, AC, Hitpoints, and resists. Fully a 33% of Shamans spells are
stat buffs and largely considered useless or undesirable by a LOT of knowledgeable players. If they were given good reasons to have, this 33% of
spells the Shaman has would become useful. As a final closer on stat buffs I want to ask you if the effect of something is so small that logs have to be parsed to determine its usefulness, is it really all that useful?

1. Fix stat buffs.

Make Str, dex, agi, sta, and cha buffs have greater impact. Right now the benefit is marginal and they are primarily used to buffer the better buffs so that these get dispelled, not AC, HP, attack rating, defense rating, Mana Regeneration, Hit Point Regeneration, attack speed, and damage shields. It should be just as noticeable when a str buff (or any attribute buff) is lost as when a attach speed buff is lost. Its no where near that now.

My suggestion is to add a static effect to each buff. I used the 40% benefit of a haste spell as a model for these suggestions

STR: have a fixed number that this spell increases a wielded weapons damage by. This effect should be a percentage base of the damage rating of the weapon. This way a STR buff is more desirable to people who use weapons with a very high damage score. Say that strength (49th level str buff) gives a bonus of 40% to the damage rating of a weapon. A person with a weapon that did 10 damage would now act as if it had a 14 damage rating. A person with a damage rating of 40 would now be 56, gaining a larger benefit. Note that this matches the attack speed increase of 40%. It would be best if the str buff and attack speed buff could stack.

AGI: Agility should increase all defensive skills by a percentage. Say the level 44 spell agility increased defensive skills by 40%. This would increase your defense, dodge, parry... by 40%. If a persons skill is 100, its now 140. If its 150, well the agility buff will bump it up to 210.

DEX: Dex should increase the chance of a weapon procing. Once again scaling up to 40%. It should also decrease the chance of a caster fizzling by 40% and increase the channeling skill of casters by 40% for the 49th level dexterity buff.

STA: Would increase a person Stamina regeneration by 40%, and/or increase total HP by 40%.

CHA: Increase the rate at which mana is regained by 40%.

Keep in mind these changes are scaled up to 40% at the 50TH level spells. Post 50 I think the approach should be different.

Well.since we have one last chance to post our concerns...

First question? Who is more qualified to judge what the purpose/ primary function of a class is...The hundred (thousands?) of players who play the class day in and day out? Or the design team, who may play one from time to time, but clearly (based on their biases) do _NOT_ play the same game as the rest of us. The primary purpose of a shaman is DEBUFFING the opponent. This is emphatically true at the higher levels, but is also true at the lower levels...Ask any experienced shaman what the one spell is that is going to be cast before entering melee with any challenging mob. Answer? His slow spell. If he has to choose between skipping the slow or skipping ALL of his buffs, he would be better off to not buff and go with the slow, then to fully buff and not slow the mob. Other spells which fall into this category are the malosi and incapacitate spells, although with more specialized application.

BTW...A comparison was made between giving a mage malo and giving a shaman better healing as being one and the same...NOT TRUE! A shaman is by definition half healer, we spend a good part of our lives acting as backup healer. Magicians are NOT debuffers, and no one ever tries to get a magician to join the group, just so he can debuff (or at least not till they get malo). If you want to give magicians a debuff..Give them one that is specific to the spells they cast...Say a COLD and FIRE (maybe even ac) debuff. That would be in keeping with a pet and nuke class. Giving a mage a malo spell would be more like giving a shaman the ability to summon mod rods….Something I do not recommend and which I agree would be cutting into one of the mage’s primary functions.

More on Malo: The resistance rate of the lower level versions is obscene. If the spell sticks, it isn’t needed as any other spells I intend to cast would stick anyways. And many times, after having it be resisted a few times, if I try DoTs or Nukes, they stick for full damage every time. Seems like a spell designed to REDUCE resistance would be one of the least resisted spells (or else why have it) rather than one of the MOST resisted spells.

Alchemy: I understand that the goal is to have the potions be sold to players rather then to vendors …But that is never gonna happen when most of the potions have NO market. Not that I wanna see enchanters get nerfed (I also play on of them)…But why is it that when an enchanter/ jeweler makes an item (A MUCH larger percentage of which are useful and saleable), if he wants, he can resell it for a reasonable percentage of what it cost him to make (90ish%), but when we try to resell a potion, we are lucky to get a few percentage points of our investment back. Seems like the same approach should work for both. Alternately….Remove the nerfs on the potions (make them add a single stat, instead of adding two and subtracting one, for example) or lower the costs (for example regen or damage shield) to where they might actually be affordable for the benefit.

Stat Buffs: I realize that there is no way to make stats more significant, as that would create many more balance headaches than it would solve. With that in mind, I do like the idea of secondary effects being tagged onto the stat buffs (as a previous poster suggested). In an ideal world, these buffs would start with negligible effect (not unlike the buffs themselves), but might actually become significant post 50th. The easiest way to add these effects (IMHO, as a player, not a coder) is as a proc..as we already know the ability to add proc effects via a spell exists. The effects I would suggest (and I am sure this list will differ from reader to reader) is:
Str: A stun…Maybe 1 sec with the first str buff, increasing to around 6 secs with the highest level buff.
Agil: If it can be done…a dodge (don’t know if this can be given to a spell effect). If this is not possible, then a very brief damage shield (lasts for 1 tick, absorbs an increasing amount of damage….say 10 pts at first buff, increasing to 100 at the highest level). This may seem illogical, but it is really a way to kludge a damage avoidance proc due to increased agility.
Dex: A haste…Ranging from 5% to 25%..nothing incredible, but stacking with the spells we already have.
Stam: Regeneration…Ranging from 1pt/tick to 6 pts/tick or so.
Char: Not sure what would be useful here….maybe a faction benefit..but since charisma isn’t really a combat buff for most shaman, may not be relevant if it doesn’t have a proc.
Finally, one benefit of these is that you can make the high level group buffs useful by adding these effects, without having to also increase their stats (although why you chose to make them buff less than the single buffs I don’t know…For example..AE spells tend to do the same damage as the equivalents at the same level, and the AE is balanced by increased mana cost…Seems like the same logic should apply).
