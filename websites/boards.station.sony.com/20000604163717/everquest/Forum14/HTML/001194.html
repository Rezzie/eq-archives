I read rumors today that paladins and necros will be getting 90%+ experience res'es in the expansion. I have a concern this will greatly reduce a cleric's role in a group at higher levels. Let me explain:

First of all, druids, shamans and paladins will all be getting supperior heal in the expansion. This will be a great spell for them and help keep party members alive longer when no cleric is around. The clerics will still be much better healers, but at least now groups have the option to try zones without a cleric.

My concern arises from the fact that now paladins and necros may get an experince res which will restore most of the experience lost upon death. This means that dying, in general, will be far less bothersome since most of the experience lost can be restored by a res spell. This will greatly reduce the fear of death since the experience penalty will pretty much be a non-factor now. Of course healing will still be needed, but since the penalty of death is much lower now, a druid, shaman or paladin can fill that role nicely. The occasional death that results will simply need a res, which necros and paladins may be able to supply, and the group is back in business. Now being that a cleric's primary role, and the role they do best, is keeping people alive, will be less useful now. If you die, oh well, just get a res, get most of my exp back and back to action.

I think for the cleric to continue to be a viable class at later levels the "fear of death" (and the penalties it brings) needs to be firmly in place.

Just my opinion and welcome to hear others.

Relax please, hehe, we haven't even seen the spells yet. Much less played to 60 to compare.

Besides, here's an analogy for you. Clerics get minor healing at level 1, paladins at level 9. Do they compete? NO. Does a level 9 paladin affect a level 1 cleric's ability to find a party? NO. Why? Because of the level difference. For a newbie party to add a level 9 paladin as a REPLACEMENT for a cleric, just cause he's got the minor healing spell... that's dumb. They won't get any exp.

Same thing will happen with sup heal. We get it at 35. They get it at 50+. Will a group of level 35's ask a level 52 paladin to join them as a replacement for a cleric? I really doubt it. How about a level 40 party? Still no. 45? Maybe, but it's still better to find a level 45 cleric, as far as experience goes.

Level 50+? We get new healing spells at level 50+. They'll be faster and more efficient than sup heal (or at least that's the intent). They still won't be able to compete. A level 54 cleric will be way better AT HEALING than a 54 paladin or shaman with sup heal.

You can't compare the abilities of a level 50+ with the abilities of a level 34 and say that the 50+ will compete with the 34. They'll be in different zones, trust me.
