The current batch of MMORPGs are all going to bomb because they all include mandatory PvP. From the examples of EQ, UO and AC, we can see that 90% of the people who play these games will choose to opt out of PvP if they can.

The game that eventually kills EQ (and there will be one some day) will have to be a whole lot better than EQ. Not only will it have to win in the "Is it worth $50" and "I could spend any particular hour playing EQ or that" areas, but it also has to be good enough to make people forsake their characters.

What a game will have to have to clobber EQ:

1) Better graphics. EQ's graphics engine is very primitive. The character and monster models are amazing, but the graphics engine forces the world to be a chain of sandboxes.

2) A means for characters to have a lasting impact on the world - creation of cities and dungeons, political structures, unique items, and general content created by players.

3) Rich depth for many different play styles. The only play style that is really supported in EQ is the adventurer. There is really no game for craftsmen, merchants, explorers and socializers.

4) A clean well balanced game system. A game system that is based on a statistical model that both the developers and the players understand helps the developers balance the game and helps the players form intuition about their items and abilities. Game systems based on hokey mysterious code blocks that nobody really understands are prone to breaking and resulting in annoying and unintuitive results.
