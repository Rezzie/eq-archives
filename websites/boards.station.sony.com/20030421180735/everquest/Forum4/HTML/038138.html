I'd like to have sampled every race and class but then they'd all still be level 5. You have to draw the line somewhere.

At least I have seen major parts of Odus, Antonica, Faydwer and Luclin with this assortment.

As far as Toxx forest goes, it isn't any worse than Greater Faydark as far as visibility is concerned. However the Elves, unlike the Erudites, have the benefit of infravision. My wizzy has the ability to conjure a wearable light source, which solves that particular problem. Other classes will have to get themselves a fire beetle eye or a lightstone. (Fire beetle eyes are easily obtainable in Toxx.)

What makes Toxx undesirable as a newbie zone is that guards are scarce, vision-obscuring trees are plentiful, and there are some relatively nasty creatures that wander fairly close to the zone line (e.g. abandoned necromancer pets).

Get lost, get aggro, get dead.

Most Erudite newbies I knew quickly took the boat to Qeynos (this was pre-PoP) and leveled up there. Now of course you can take the book to just about any newbie zone.

I never felt that my Erudite was treated any differently than any of my other characters. If anything she was treated "better" (more politely) on account of being female in appearance, at least by rollplayers.

Although I usually don't volunteer that the player behind the character is male, if I am asked directly I always answer truthfully. Personally I don't care and don't ask.
