Absor,

Please consider my argument. In many ways, I agree that mage has good balance, both solo pre-50 (for xping; we can camp a variety of things solo post 50, of course) and outdoors. In groups, however, we have been cheapened immeasurably over the last two expansions. Classes independent of the mage may summon nearly every spell a mage can give to another, with the exception of modulation rods. With the inclusion of the cleric Epic weapon, even this item has fallen into disfavor, as clerics do not need the rods to facilitate revivisence.

Do not get me wrong, the utilitarian aspect of mage is nice and I'm sure every class appreciates the ability to summon arrows, thrown daggers, food and water, bandages, etc. Bags are not used, especially with the increasing connection problems causing the player to permanently lose everything it contains. Where swords of runes were once a valued commodity to other pet classes as well as to the mage, they are neither necessary nor desired with the proc fix and the ability of pets to duel wield without weapons. Before you write this off as a slam, it is not, and in fact, I appreciate the innovations Verant has created for each class. The problem is that we are losing our utilitarian value. We were not meant to be strictly a selfish class, and when our spells chiefly benefit our own ends and not those of the group, we have failed to offer unique and dependent group strength. As it has been firmly established that pets in the majority of indoor as well as some outdoor zones have bad pathing (and if you do not ascribe to this view, invite me onto test with my class/level equivalent and I'll demonstrate how difficult it is for a competent player to handle pets falling outside of normal parameters).

In short, what I'm asking you to do is add value to us as a class that benefits the group. Contributing to overall damage output is something every member of the group is capable of to a greater or lesser degree. We do not receive credit for the damage our pets do, maybe to temper the overall taunt a mage's spells and pet's damage would do. If our pet dies, the mob immediately goes after us, as some of the hate crosses over from pet to master (regardless of whether or not we cast on the mob), yet we receive no credit for the damage dealt by our pets. Doesn't that strike you as odd? The game mechanics may prevent this being changed, but now think again how this necessary evil weakens our ability to claim a kill or contribute to the overall damage of a single group.

Just to refute your earlier statement about advancing at all levels, with the equipment available to each class, no classes can be argued as unsoloable. Some more powerful than others, though the fact remains that we never are or will be close to the kind of soloer a class capable of kiting, fear-kiting or snare/root/darkness can manage. Necromancers, shadow knights, bards, some clerics, enchanters, rogues, and monks may fear-kite, to greater or lesser degrees. Druids can fear-kite animals. These are with innate abilities. Druids, bards, rangers, paladins, shadow knights, necromancers, enchanters, wizards, clerics, and shamans may root or snare/darkness or both, enabling kiting. With the addition of certain warrior weapons, Warriors are now capable of casting darkness as well. Mages cannot kite because they cannot slow their target (jboots and sow from a potion the exceptions, though these are available to all classes as well, not an exception per se), and they cannot root without engaging the mob. And of course, all of our dd spells, including the dots (unlike the dots of other classes), have a good chance to break root. Again, maybe I need to reiterate, I'm not attacking you or anyone here. I wish to point out facts. We are the only class which cannot independently root/snare, we receive no class-specific equipment to make up for this shortcoming, and given that on many raids as well as when traveling invisible, invisible to undead or invisible to animals, we lose our primary means of protection, a shortcoming that no other class has to deal with (we are the only pure caster who does not get invisibility to undead, curiously) we are weakened as a contributor to the group (or with invisibility, to self-preservation). I'm not pointing out that you should give us all these things just because the classes mentioned have them. Instead, I'm trying to refute your argument earlier that we have very little do adjust to maintain balance, and more importantly, that we may solo competently through all the levels.

And let us not forget that the other classes are losing their utilitarian dependence on the mage class. Items are replacing our function as conjurers. Unfortunately our balance is becoming increasingly threatened by a surplus of new item effects.

On the surface, I agree that there looks to be very little that needs to be altered, and I can understand your point of view as someone who does not play a mage. All classes have their weaknesses, and I think that we should approach this class realistically to provide rational solutions while maintaining balance.

** Though I cannot say my answers are the best, I have contributed a few ideas on Part 2 of the mage talk.

Thanks for your time.
