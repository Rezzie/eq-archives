Most new graphics cards come with a 'tv-out' s-video port on them. nVidia cards, for example, will copy anything up to 1024x768 to a tv-out using n-view.
if your card doesn't support that, then you can buy a hardware solution extremely cheap. Something like the AverKey monitor->Composite port will let you plug your computer into a VCR.
Once in a VCR you can then use a tv-grabber solution such as Pinnacle, Belkin, etc (also cheap) to import the recorded image.
Failing that, you can use software capturing means. Microsoft Netmeeting supports recording of sessions, but these tend to be a bit pants.

I've had great success using a second pc with a video capture card/sound card and the composite-out on my video card.

DISCLAIMER: you MUST make sure that both PC's are using outlets which are properly grounded! I have seen people do this with improperly grounded wiring---in a worst case scenario, it can smoke your graphics card or capture card. So, be warned.

The only advantages of using 2 pcs are improved image/sound quality and timesaving---you can split up the video file from the hard drive, instead of cueing up each shot on the VCR, capturing it, etc. I record demo videos of games that I'm writing, so the 1st generation video quality and audio is more important to me. Your mileage may vary.
