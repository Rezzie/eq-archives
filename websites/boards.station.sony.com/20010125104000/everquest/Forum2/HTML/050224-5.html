Hope your still reading.. But here goes.. I want to stay on topic so I will stick with just brainstorm Ideas for Summoned Items only (and will stay away from pets as well because I think that is outside of the scope of this topic).

First let me state my two cents on an ideal that Verant subscribes too that I think is “over”-emphasized. That ideal being that summoned items should not take the place of real world items in terms of desirability. While there are obvious reasons why this is true (and I doubt anyone especially mage’s would disagree in principal), the enforcement of this principal has for the most part gone to far and as a result yielded a basically useless summoned weapons line. The counter to this principal is that summoned items should not be so underpowered, least they be reduced to underutilized, page 50 in the spell book items. However that is exactly what has happened for a good percentage of our summoned items. The reasons ”I” think have to do with the “NO RENT” not being properly calculated in terms of inconvenience to player for balance concerns. Now this is where the principle becomes a little hazy.. “NO RENT” for the player who can produce said NO RENT item this inconvenience is completely mitigated. In terms of Inconvenience (which has an impact of item “Value”) NO RENT to Magicians is very small. But to other classes the Inconvenience is much, much greater and should be considered when balancing item Value. In other words what I am trying to get at here is that I don’t think it is unreasonable to have summoned items that are on par with items that one would find at a that particular level range. The fact that they are summoned does offset their power in a lot of cases. Just my two cents on this discussion, which I am sure that, will be addressed with the tuning team on this topic… Now (sorry for that long comment) for ideas for summoned items, I will try to list a summoned concept and then a particular concrete example of that concept:

Summoned Armors with very specific purposes. It would have a large mana expenditure for a bag of armor (all no rent of course) that has a very specific usage. Resistance gear can probably be that best example I can provide (I know others have mentioned it). For example summoning a suit of Fire Armor that (along with cool textures ) provides close to +100 Fire resistance when all pieces are equipped (by tank classes only) but with less than stellar AC and significant weight costs for balance.

Summoned pet only weapons are a great idea but I have a feeling that there is a coding problem here (I would bet that there is no Pet identifier for the item table). Maybe a small code change could make the weapon appear in the pet’s hand (would mean that we couldn’t give other pets these summoned weapons and might alleviate some balance concerns). An interesting concept here would be to make incredibly mana expensive summoned pet weapons that increased damage output of the pet by 10-15% possibly. Or maybe a 2H version that increased Damage output by 30-35% (no duel wield).

Summoned weapons that have a very specific in usage. A little less than decent stat wise weapons that we would hand out to party members that have Very specific circumstances for usage. Vs undead weapons, Vs plant weapons, Fire/Cold based procs, with very high proc damage (set to proc at appropriate levels to avoid twinking). A weapon that isn’t at all great in all situations but stellar in one or two particular circumstances. Again balance with “NO RENT” in mind. It would be nice if the summoned Items could “scale” with magician level but I imagine there are code concerns related to that.

Summoned items that enhance other class abilities. Personally inter-class enhancement is one area that I wish VI would have placed more emphasis on (you did a decent job don’t get me wrong). For instance items that reduce another classes mana cost/casting times for certain spells. I am sure there are coding issues with that, however a nice alternative would be items that could be handed to a specific class that cost us lets say double the mana cost (of the spell) but had one charge of a spell that that class uses very often with a greatly reduced casting time cost. For instance summoned healing rod that has one charge of CH which cost us 800mana (400 mana for Ch right?) that is cleric only and Lore that has a 20-50% reduction in casting time. Problems I see being that there might not be code that allows that effect to only be utilized by players of a given level (10th level clerics shouldn’t be allowed to CH just because they know a level 54 mage). I used an extreme (probably unbalancing) example but you get the picture.

How about an upgrade to Cornucopia and Everfont that summons food and water that is Not NO RENT.

I’d love to see a summoned item that has a few charges of the upgrade to eye of zorm. That way we can give that item to a tank that can us it an not have to worry about having a pet. In fact I’d love to see those spells changed to summoned items instead of their current almost useless form.

Possibly a summoned item that will proc a reverse taunt effect (or cast one). Items that manipulate hate lists can be unbalancing I know but I think an Item like this could be balanced.

Finally a summoned item that with one charge of a spell that only works on the casters pets. This spell would freeze the pet for about 5-10 seconds and continually wipe its hate list (or at the end of the freeze period wipes pets hate list). It would work even it the pet is feared. It would basically allow the pet or caster to get beaten upon without retaliation for a few seconds. Would make enchanters hate us much, much less. Maybe let us have the use of our pets on raids again.

Long but I hope it helps….

Some great ideas---some sorta goofy. Overall, we've given the LT a lot to think about. Generally, I think (and I haven't read every single post in the two threads) we want:

Pet Heal somewhere in lvl 30 range

We want AE spells either fixed or replaced.

The above are the ones I saw most often mentioned. If anyone can do a better summary, please give it a go. Just trying to condense the list for Absor

Now, for my idea about the Walls/Barriers. Only a couple of us came up with this idea. I'd like to explore the notion further. The greatest problem I see with trying to construct barrier spells is the particle effect. I'm not a programmer, btw, so I can't accurately describe the drawbacks---but I can make some guesses.

Ok, what I envision is a series of spells based on the elements that would conjure a barrier. IF cast in a dungeon with typical corridors, a panel of elemental force forms touching both walls, floor and ceiling in front of the mage. The barrier has hit points which an attacker must beat through before it can reach the caster. In addition, it would also have a damage shield effect so the attacker would take appropriate damage from each hit. The barrier would also have a duration.

If cast outdoors or in a large chamber inside, the barrier should form a circle around the mage with the same effects noted above. Anyone standing near the mage should be inside the circle. In either case, the barrier would also have a reasonable duration.

Pros: this spell is for emergency protection for the mage and the group. It can provide enough time for the mage or group to med, heal, or to prepare other magicks necessary for escape. It's unique, uses existing particle effects and effects already coded: ie, various spells used by Necros and Wizards that provide similar protection but for themselves only.

Balances: it takes time to call up a barrier--probably longer than a damage shield in casting; the duration should probably be similar to a damage shield; it works both ways: anyone attempting to pass through it from either side encounters the damage and hit points; it can be dispelled; it is unmoveable and cannot be seen through; it should probably take a reagent/gemstone of some kind to cast it; exceptionally large creatures might be able to physically attack over the barrier.

Cons: possible drawbacks associated with a continous particle effect in the case of fire, water or air walls (although I have a solution for this); would require additional coding to include the new graphics everywhere; would require extensive testing for balance; could be abused--used against fellow players outside the intent and purpose of the spell; could be used as an exploit if not carefully balanced between risk and benefit; could cause some serious class envy

A note on the particle effects. At first, I envisioned logical effects associated with the elemental barrier: fire would be a continuous erupting of flames from ground to ceiling, etc. But how about this: the texture used for our pets could instead be applied to the elemental barrier of the appropriate kind. In addition, in order to ensure the proper use of this spell, what if the mage must sacrifice his pet in order to erect the barrier? That is, the pet is transformed into the barrier. While the barrier lasts, the Mage can have no pet conjured---it, in effect, becomes the barrier.

Anyway, this is the best I can do for an explanation of what I meant by Elemental Barrier spells.

Please feel free to critique my idea. Point out my oversights. Tell me what you think about this idea.

thanks
Alc
