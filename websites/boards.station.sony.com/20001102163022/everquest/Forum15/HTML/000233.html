In melee heavy groups, how many of you actually consider medding during the easier battles? Just like a wizard should save his nukes for the more difficult encounters, it could be beneficial for hybrids to sit on their rears during the easier battles to have more mana to dump later.

For instance, while a ranger medding will be doing less damage overall, he'll be able to chain nuke for a little while when the damage is more important. Or a SK could mad DoT a mob. It might even be more efficient for a paladin to med for heals than participate in some fights.

How many people actually consider this, both when discussing class balance and in actual practice?

Because the ranger / paladin can melee too.

The idea is that while the hybrid should probably be meleeing most of the time, every now and then a fight comes along that's so easy that there's no reason to melee; for instance a lvl 40-50 group in the frenzy room and the puller brings back a vis or wan ghoul knight. Suppose the tanks are a warrior, monk, and ranger. The warrior and the monk will tear through the vis ghoul knight so fast that it doesn't really matter if the ranger melees too; the ranger would then opt to med through the fight, then nuke away a few frogs later when the dar ghoul or the bloodthirsty ghoul is pulled.
