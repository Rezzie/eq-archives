Dwilgar, are you Absor operating under an assumed name?

There have been numerous suggestions in the Shaman Talk threads for improving stat buffs that would not involve a massive revamp of code, nor imbalance the game.

They don't have to change what stats fundamentally do nor rewrite all the item codes. They just have to alter what the shaman buffs themselves do.

Examples:

Shaman str buff increases minimum damage per swing by a certain percentage. Does this mean that any item which adds str should do this? No! Only the buff.

Shaman agi buff reduces damage taken by a reasonable percentage. Does this mean that any item which increases agi should do this? No! Just the Shaman buff.

Shaman dex buff increases the chance to hit by a certain percentage. Does this mean that any item which increases dex should do this? No! Just the Shaman buff.

You get the idea, I hope. Again, there is no need to revamp massive amounts of code, nor every stat improving item in the game, nor change the way stats function. Only the buffs need to be fixed (i.e. made more useful/effective).

OK...my take on the literal meaning (not what Verant has made them mean in game) of the available buffs would be that STR means you can hit harder and carry more, DEX means you are more accurate with your weapons of choice, STA means you can fight longer, AGI means you can dodge better, HPs mean you live longer, AC means when you get hit it doesn't hurt as bad.

Why, Absor, can you guys not make the buffs mean these things?!?!

I mean...OK STR buff allows you to carry more...why not have it up the max damage possible for the buffed PC? Say a players STR is 150 and their max damage is 100. Now, with Shammy buff (I know these numbers aren't right but bear with me...stream of thought here his STR is 190. That's about a 27% increase in STR. So now the PC can carry 190 stone in equipment/loot. Why not allow this buff to increase max damage too? With this his new max damage is 127. That's not very unbalancing and which group wouldn't want a shammy for his buffs with that one.

Now DEX...DEX now only slightly increases your chances of procing a weapon (according to popular belief anyway) BUT, the literal meaning of DEX is to be more accurate with your hands. That would mean...to me anyway...that the PC's DEX should change the chances of hitting or missing. So, back to the numbers if a PCs DEX is 100 and it is buffed to 140....that's a 40% increase, so why not giving the PC a modified ability to hit with the buffed DEX taken into account? Or even better, how about increasing the chances of the PC striking for max DMG? Think about it, someone with high DEX will hit his opponent in a critical location more often than someone with lower DEX...

AGI...same story...give the PC a better chance of dodging/parrying the attackers blows. OK, maybe with higher AGI...to make the numbers work Verant had to play with the AC of the character to show a benefit of the attackers blows brushing off and therefore not hitting for the damage it could have hit for...BUT, that's still not agility in my mind....

STA...why this one adds HPs is beyond me...actually makes very little sense at all, but why can STA not increase the overall STA pool for the PC? Wouldn't this make more sense? I mean there are other spells in the game that increase the overall HP and mana pool...why not allow this?!?
