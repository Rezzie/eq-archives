I'm not sure how many of you have been to Harpy's Happy Tavern, but I just recently went there myself to read over a few things.
Well, I know that Absor and Abashi, among many others, have always said there is a story line behind Everquest and Norrath, just nobody have ever gone out there to try and find it.

So, I'd figured I would check it out myself. Sigh, I have to say, after reading the history of Norrath ( not even in it's entirety ), and I've totally lost interest.

There isn't any real 'fantasy' feel ( or any feel at that ) behind the story. Many things are left open, and never explained giving the idea it's 'Not important'.
The grammar gets to me, granted, I'm not the best in the English grammar, however; It's not proper to start off a sentence with the word 'And' or 'But', let alone a new paragraph. Many sentences looks more like a game of yahtzee. There isn't must as far as run on sentences, but they, allot of them makes just as much sense.

Writing style: Again, I'm not an ace in this field, but I have done a few stories and read a few to understand, what you all have here, isn't good. First off, lack of detail. You give story of things happening, but no detail as to who, what, why, when and, most importantly, where.
Example:

"But then came the last curse of the gods, as Solusek Ro, Lord of Flame, arched the spine of the serpent mountains, bringing heat from the burning sun to the ancient forest. The rivers ran dry, it rained less each year, and while the great elven druids fought long and hard, using their powerful magics to combat the change, they could only delay the inevitable. Slowly the forest gave way to desert, and eventually even great Takish-Hiz crumbled and the elves were forced to flee Tunaria, leaving much of their greatness behind. "

This is quite apparent that this 'story line' is about the old elf city ruins located within the Desert of Ro's, but anybody that doesn't know this, will have no clue to where this is happening. Great, the elves had to leave Tunaria, but again, when and where. Missing both of those.

Paragraph endings ... Now, I've done this by mistake myself, but promptly correct upon re-reading my stories. Just about *ever other* paragraph ends with "Thus began the Age of .... ". The way to keep interest in the story, is to change the why you word things around. Keep the user moving and wonder. End every paragraph with the exact same phrase gets old 'fast'! ( It's like, a blonde, like using 'like' all the like time .. like .. ahhh! ).

Important events: If something happens that's important, detail and information is the most important key to getting it across to the user. Using the example above. Again, and important event that later lead to the civilization of the elven races on Faydwer, but no detail was given as to: one, where they where from in the first place. two, there they where heading to.
Okay, two can be left out until later in the story, but alas, it's not even stated later on


Now .. Those are just the prominent problems I see with the story. There are many interesting twists w/in Everquest and on the lands of Norrath that truly interests me. I would like to know more about them, but find it very hard to learn when the 'opening story' is so vegue and poorly written.

I would love to learn how the Bards fit in the opposing faction of the Ring of Scale, and how and why they relate to Mayong Mistmoore. I would also love to figure out WHY Venril Sathir's faction is all over Kunark ( but yet his followers are KoS to him and most of his minons ).
I also want to know what's the deal with all these faction surrounding cities like Cabilis, Freeport and Qeynos, and how they play a role with another.

You do learn 'some' while playing the game, but never the underlining detail.
The opening story is so vegue and left open, there are literally *no* clues as to where to search for these answers. Shoot, there is nothing mentioning how the Bards of Antonica are in opposing faction with Mistmoore and Ring of Scale, even though MM and RoS as in opposition as well.

Sure, the clues are in the game, but please, give us out of game clues ( and most story ) for the in game clues.

Story's writing like Star Wars Episode I .. it's got something in there, but too much detail is left out to make anything out.

The reason the story doesn't interest me much isn't so much the writing style or it's technical qualities. For a MMORPG I personally feel that a story for the world needs to do a few things in order to capture, and hold my interest.

1) Provide clues that players can look into. It should help with solving quests and foreshadow possible conflicts (which IMO is where events should draw their inspiration/content from).

2) In the revelation of a history, there should also be the lure of adventure, and an idea as how participating in such an adventure has others look upon one (i.e. faction adjustments-must like Guice pointed out, why do the factions oppose each other).

3) Participation and adjustment/advancement of the storyline. The servers don't have any real distinctions other than what uberguild dominates what spawns. In order for each server to have it's own character, it has to be influenced in it's evolution by the people who play there.

Some things could be done to reflect this, i.e. the result of a epic scale quest, rather than resulting in some epic particile weapon, results in FV becoming a full fledged town rather than a outpost. Same could be done for The Overthere's Dark Races outpost. This is the sort of thing that gives long lasting senses of accomplisment rather than just toons and their toys.

unfortunately, that would require more effort than I think VI is willing to put forth, especially as it's not the GMs who do the coding but just handle bugs/petitions/reports ect. It's the strongest feature of the text based muds that sadly, never made it to the video rich ones.

On a side note, what's up with the Rogue epic quest? Yet you are supposed to make sure this cannot be tried again?? The WHOLE goal goes 100% contrary to the goal of all Dark Elves, and guess what? Dark Elves can be Rogues! Heck, you even KILL the important General, who calls upon Innoruuk when he dies, to complete the quest.

It has been said, in your opening note, that if you make attempts to go contrary to the will of Innoruuk, you will be unwelcome in Neriak.
