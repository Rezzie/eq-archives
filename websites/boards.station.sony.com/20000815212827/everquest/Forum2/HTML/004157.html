Abashi,

Your point is well taken and there are indeed serious drawbacks to adding this ability. But...

One of the reason that groups "camp" (sit in one place and pull mobs to them) rather than "adventure" (whole group moving from room to room exploring) is that it is an incredible pain to try to keep a group together in a dungeon. I cannot count the times that my group has said something like "let's move up to room x", we start to move out and people start to get lost. Part of the problem is the warping inherent in multiplayer games. One moment you are in front of me, next frame you are gone and I have no idea where. Next thing you know, the party is scattered all over, mobs are aggroing, trains are forming and people are dying.

I know the response can be "well, this gives an advantage to well-organized teams" or "this is realistic" or "people find ways around it". All of which are true. BUT... it has the practical effect of making camping and pulling a much, much safer and rewarding game-play method than adventuring. And since it is more rewarding, it is much more likely that playes will do it that way.

The general comments I have heard in the past from Verant were that you guys were always looking to find ways to turn the game from "EverCamp" to a more exploration-like dungeon crawl kind of game. I would love for that to happen. But I cannot imagine how that is going to happen if it is as difficult to keep a party together as it now is.

I hope you guys reconsider it and perhaps try it on test. I think that just the ability to see where your teammates are will help so much that it will make up for the loss of "realism".

Thanks.
