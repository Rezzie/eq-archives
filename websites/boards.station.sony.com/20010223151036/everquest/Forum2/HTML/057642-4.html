Hi Folks, here are a few more musings on things to help out the class.

Spell that convert the Wizards life into dd.
SoulBurn: lets say the conversion is 25% of the Wizard’s hp times Wizards level

Example: 225hp X 37 level wizard = 8325 dd

Now the numbers are just for illustration but the concept is sound, a 37th level Wizard throwing 8300 dds is a bit extreme (but its fine by me)! The tuning is for the coders not the players.

Have Soulburn 1, 2 and 3 that gradually increase the % conversion to the point where a Wizard can be effective against Uber Beasts in the high level game.

Big deal you say now it’s aggroed on me and I am lower on hps.
Give it a debilitating effect, have the wizard fall to the ground like a feign death,
A_Wizard_00 has lost consciousness and collapses! Make it last 5 seconds or so but have it reduce agro.
Hey! Look at this! Now A_Cleric_00 can heal me and I in effect get back manna in the form of hp! Reload the glass cannon for another shot Vicar!

This adds value to both the cleric and the wizard in the group situation. Can you solo with it? Not bloody likely unless you want to lie on the ground and get pounded.
Chain casting this is possible but it becomes less effective as your hit points go down. Will you toss this about a lot in xp groups, only in emergencies otherwise the healers will get mad at you.
This is the classic Wizard “Big Nuke” where the caster does huge damage but at great cost to himself.

The issue of manna regeneration is vital. A wizard needs to have the ability to cast some sort of dd at a sustainable cost in order to contribute in xp groups. If my 37th level Wizard could contribute a steady stream of 100-120 dds thru multiple pulls (again the numbers are for illustration) then I am a welcome addition who pulls his own weight and not a guy we took just to fill out the group (Dang there’s a druid looking for a group, if only we had waited a minute!). I like faster medding personally as it fits with the idea of the Masterful Wizardly Intellect.

There are many good concepts on this thread from many thoughtful individuals. Verant is fortunate to have such an engaged player base. Use the wizardly virtue that you learned while staring at your spell books. Be patient.

The first 2 suggestions are the most important because while making the Wizard more attractive to a group, they do not enhance soloing abilities.


1.) Wizards could be more mana efficient when grouped. By drawing on the energy of the Wizard's allies, he/she becomes more effective and focused.

2.) Wizards could be able to cast effective damage shields on other members of the group. Wizards need something to offer to other group members that is tangible. Damage shields seem to make sense for a Wizard.


The next 3 suggestions would benefit the Wizard while grouped as well as while soloing. To me, this makes them secondary to the first 2 suggestions, but they have the potential to reduce the frustration level experienced by Wizards by letting the Wizard feel that he/she is performing the specified function of Wizards: Doing damage to enemies through spells more efficiently than any other class.


3.) Removing/increasing the Damage cap on lower level spells would give a wizard another option in his/her spellcasting arsenal. At higher levels these spells would still do far less damage than the contemporary spells, but their low mana cost and low aggro value would make them useful in certain situations. This would add to the strategy needed for playing the class, and therefore (in my opinion) add to the fun of playing the class.

4.) Some sort of Critical Hit system could be put into effect for Wizard spells. The effect of the Critical Spell could be double damage, stun, lower aggro, fear, or any other effect that was deemed appropriate. This could be explained by the Wizard having a greater knowledge of the ways of Evocation than all others and during a particular cast the Wizard was attuned to the energies of the target.

5.) This is a general type of suggestion that seems to make sense to me. Because of the Wizard's prowess as a damage dealer, the resistance rate should be reduced compared to other classes.


There are 2 more suggestions that are very interesting to me, but would cause the need for a new spell line to be introduced for each. This doesn't seem to be the direction that the design team is headed, so these suggestions are at the bottom of the list.

6.) The Wizard could have a one-time (perhaps self-only) Damage Shield. An example would be "Electric Eel Skin". At Level 8 the Wizard could cast this spell on him/herself (or others possibly) and the effect would last until the Wizard is attacked, and it does 40 pts. of damage one time. It would cost 40-50 pts. of mana and take 4.5 seconds to cast.

This would be a spell with limited use, because it has low mana efficiency (but if casted before battle or while medding mana efficiency is less relevant) and long casting time (once again only an issue during battle). It would, however be something interesting and new to add to a Wizard's arsenal that could make strategy more important.

7.) The Wizard could have a line of spells that does high amounts of mana efficient/low aggro damage, while having an adverse effect on the Wizard such as a prolonged stun effect. The spell could also have a long casting time, or do a bit of damage to the Wizard, or blind the Wizard, or root the wizard, or some combination of negative effects that balances out the positives of the spell when used inappropriately.

I know this is a long post, and most of these ideas have been expressed previously, but Absor has stated that summaries are helpful to him. So, I compiled this summary of what seemed to be useful ideas from other posts as well as some of my own input. If I missed any interesting or useful ideas for this topic I'm sorry. I'm just trying to help. Also, I haven't credited anyone for the ideas that I put on the list, but I do not intend to take credit for them myself. 8 )
