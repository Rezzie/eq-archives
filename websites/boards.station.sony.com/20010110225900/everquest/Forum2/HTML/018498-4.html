---------------------------------
Not to detract from the importance of the loss of Llara, but I'd like to make a suggestion. As a member of the guild that got the first ever memorial for a RL lost one in UO, I know a little about the situation. In fact, we had two shrines, one public, and one private for our fallen comrad, such was the impact he made. Llara was not the only player to have died IRL, and become a great loss to those that knew him in game(someone said it was a guy right?).

Your petition would most likely recieve alot more acceptance, and have a better chance of success if the shrine was not dedicated to merely Llara, but to all the RL people that have passed on and caused a great loss to the game. In fact just a few days ago I read that another guide had also passed on. It would be a little selfish for the loss of one to be put before the loss of others. All people are important to those that know them, and there should be a place to remember that loss and the mark left by the their lives.

I fully support the placing of a Shrine of Rememberance. I will sign your petition, and urge you to work toward something that can be shared by all, for the good of all.
---------------------------

My suggestion on the matter is a simple statue or such, preferable non-racial, bearing a plaque that when clicked on reads something along the lines of "In Rememberance of Lost Friends" or something (Heros?)...

It would be one memorial for all who have passed, and preferable in a location that all can access easily, and perhaps with room for any future services. My own suggetion is in the Arena zone, outside of the PvP area. Mebbe soon as you exit the tunnel against the outside wall of the inner arena. A memorial to the fallen is fitting in a zone where so many fall in game. The zone is sparely populated most of the time, and would provide a quiet place to pay respects, as well as a large area to hold a cerimony. Not to mention, it's such a simply designed zone that it would be easy to modify.

But then that's just my idea...I hope VI takes this issue seriously, something of this nature goes a long way toward respect and appreciation from their customers.
