Hi Lucefer,

Well, DE's are considered Evil and therefore you can't just walk in in FP. You can raise your faction by killing Orcs (A LOT of orcs) but it needs time. Same for other "good" cities.

Starting up as a Mage is not as easy as for a warrior. You will have to "play a warrior" but without the skills. Stick with easy kills and stay near to the guards. Don't hesitate to run to them for help. Also, use your spells. Don't worry about the mana, you will get it back fast in the first levels. All you need now is patience for level 4 and your pet. :-)

If you found the lvl 8+ spells then you have found the Tower of the Spurned (the casters guild in Neriak). But did you find the stairs (going down - odd sort of tower) in the back corner? The guild leaders and low level spell vendors are down there.

The advice about sticking close to guards is sound advice - but sadly doesn't work in Nektulos. You should find your way to the newbie log and hunt round that for a while - concentrate on moss snakes, decayed skellies and black wolves till level 3. (DO NOT cross the road until you are about 5th level - and even then be careful) Save all the bone chips (sell to necros for 3 - 5pp per 20) and snake skins / teeth (superb quest for these which I wont spoil unless you ask).

Once you are feeling brave you can start on beetles and spiderlings - but as you now know these group ferociously in Nektulos (where they are abundant) so you must be cautious.

As a DE wizard (Innoruuk follower) I can walk into Freeport unmolested (even before I killed orcs) but am KoS to certain things (monks and some folks in the waterfront tavern - and I avoided North Freeport where the paladins act as guards). I couldn't trade there - and I still can't, and I am sick of killing orcs

The life of the Teir'Dal is slow and hard - but it makes us strong.

Ahhhh....the life of a Dark Elf Mage. A fun combination, in my opinion. I chose Agnostic when I created mine, so long ago. Of course, I had no idea what implications that would have at the time, but I knew that it would make it easier for me in the long run as far as travel goes. 55 Levels later, I still love playing the class/race combination.

As far as your spells go, sounds as if you are in the right building. Even close to the right room. If you have found the Guildmaster, and turned in your note for your Robe, you are close. In the hallway, as you head towards the room with the Guildmaster, there is a room to your right. That is where the 1 and 4 level spells are sold, and there is also a vendor there that sells the Malachite you will need.....for ever Lapiz Luzil will be used for a while, but get used to carrying Malachite. And you are correct, you get your pets at level 4. Buy them all if you can, and find out the differences and when and how to use them. I'll not go into that here, as many people have posted that are better writers than I.

Freeport faction can be gained various ways, mainly the Orcs in East Commons. There are also some Quests that you might do. However, if you have chosen Inny as your God, you will be VERY hard pressed to ever be trained in Freeport. It's really not all that bad. I never have gotten enough faction to train there myself, then got mad and started killing Freeport Militia for Paladin faction so I could Bank in peace

Some things to avoid. Don't kill any Orc that has a blue skin. These Orcs are aligned with Crushbone, and Crush has a treaty with the Dark Elves. Your faction will go down, and you can end up KOS in Neriak. This nearly happened to me, so be careful. Avoid letting your pet get loose when you get one. Practice with your pet, get good, and you will always find a group when you want one. And yes, soloing is an option.

Some people may have told you that Mages can't deal out the damage that a Wizard can, that we can't port, that we can't Kite, yada yada. I can promise you that if you play well as a Mage, you will love the class. I do still.

All this being said, play your Mage for a while and see if you like the class. And be sure to come back if you have questions
