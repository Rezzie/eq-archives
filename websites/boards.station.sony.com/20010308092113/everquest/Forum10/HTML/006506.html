Sounds like you want a Bard or a Ranger. Both get dual wield, are good for humans, and can get decent equipment.

Some people might argue that a Paladin is better over a ranger, but it really depends on what you want to do. If you want spells that you can cast that do damage to the monster, the Ranger is the way to go. Paladins get damage spells against Undead, but against other living creatures they don't get a direct damage spell until 49 (I believe). Paladins do get healing spells that are better than a Ranger's, but then the parent caster for a Paladin is a cleric, versus the Druid for Rangers.

As for looking good, keep in mind that Rangers cannot wear Plate armor, but Bards can. Rangers however will be a better damage dealing tank compared to a Bard. Bards have some great songs that people love to have in a group, but then Rangers get some helpful spells and skills like Snare and Tracking that groups find very helpful. Both the Bard and Ranger are good for groups, and really need groups to get better once past about level 15 or so.

Lastly, the downside to playing a Human is the night vision issue. You will be blind at night if you play a Human. You may want to consider a Half Elf. Plus if you play a Bard, the Helm for Humans looks rather odd, while the Helm for Half Elves looks pretty cool. But hopefully you won't pick a race because of the way they look in armor. Who knows.
