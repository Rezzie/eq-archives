The old threads were getting a bit nasty and were wandering. Let's not have that happen here.
Let me sum up my thoughts on this whole thing.

Necromancers are just fine, in all aspects of the game, with one possible exception.

On raids they can find a lot to do up to and after the encounter with the boss mob. But in that encounter some necromancers don't feel that they have enough to do, or feel that what they can do isn't what they want to be doing.

In this thread I would like to keep to one narrow focus. What things could be done to give necromancers more to do in that one encounter, without improving their power in other situations?

It's a very narrow question, and a very hard one to answer. Here are the ideas that I've seen that won't work, or won't happen:

DoT stacking: We're probably not going to allow more DoTs to stack than already do. And this would have an impact far beyond the scope here, it would increase the power of necromancers across the board.

Unresistable Lifetap: Probably never going to happen. I understand the whole concept of Lifetap to power mana transfer. I'm just telling you with a pretty firm tone, that it's really not likely to happen.

Mana Transfer: The rate will probably not be improved. As I've said before, currently necromancers can convert 1 hit point into 1 mana. Right now they can give that 1 mana to a cleric (as an example) for the cost of about 2.5 mana. The cleric can convert that mana into about 2 hit points. That's the right ratio, really. There shouldn't be a time when the amount that can be returned is higher than the amount that is given out.

Poofing: with the next patch, this should be pretty much fixed… let me copy one of my other posts:

"Ok, this whole thing was Alan getting confused.

There was special code put in several months ago that prevented nearly any possibility of a corpse poofing if the NPC is over 54th level. I was right there.

But recently, a few weeks ago, we added some code to deal with a unique NPC situation (that I'm not going to go into…) that might have been causing corpses to poof is the kill was assigned to a Link Dead PC over 54th level. This will be changed with the next patch.

I was confused and thought that this change was already in.

I'm sorry about the confusion.

Alan"

In any case, let's leave this one off the list until we see how things go post-patch. I have noticed, though, that the only complaint I've seen was about Ragefire… I wouldn't be worried about poofing due to DoTs anymore. At least try it out.


Issues that we can discuss:

Ignite Bones: I'm willing to suggest some change to remove the skeleton. I think to do that we need to have something that does the same sort of thing, but I don't know what that is. This spell will probably never be changed into a standard direct damage spell. I like some of the ideas (like changing the tinting on the target), but unfortunately most of them don't seem possible technically. I'll keep thinking up ideas. Post any you have here.

Group Feign Death: I haven't mentioned this idea to the team yet. I suppose that there might be a lot of reasons not to implement this idea…

Making pets immune to fear: This probably won't happen. Then we'd have to do the same for magicians (not an inherently bad thing, but it has broad repercussions), and there's PvP to consider. But I think I have some interesting ideas along these lines that might work out. I want to here other ideas along these lines.

Possessing the pet: that's a cute as heck idea (maybe 'cute' is the wrong word…). Another one that I'll mention to the team. But keep in mind that I'm not really looking for totally new stuff. I'm not advocating that we start making up new spells and whole new concepts. It would be hard to justify spending a ton of time making new concepts at this point. But it's certainly interesting…

Essence Emerald: I'm kind of in favor of using this device as a way of changing things. It's a very expensive casting cost… For example I kind of liked the idea of allowing the mana transfer spell to be usable on others, considering the cost.

Unrelated Issues (meaning that I'm mentioning them here so that you don't have to bring them up…) :

I'm aware that Track Corpse doesn't work. I'm going to ask about that.

The epic weapon. It's not really related to this anyhow, and I am already very aware how you all feel about it.

Defoliate: If someone wants to start a new thread about which things they think should be affected by this spell, go for it. But it's not part of the focus of this thread.

I may have missed some…

Keep in mind that I understand that many of you are not happy with a support role on raids. I'm fairly convinced that it is perfectly fine for a necromancer to act in that role during the final battle of the raid. But I'm open to ideas (barring the ones that I've already vetoed) that would make them more aggressive at that one final battle.

Alan

=== SUMMARY #2 ===

ABSOR,

I have read through the entire thread and have compiled a summary that you might find useful. I will try and add to it if something new is brought
up. Please note, the vast majority of the ideas below are NOT mine but rather those of the community at large. I am merely the editor and
compiler with one exception.

Changes fall within several categories

Lifetap
1. If a tap is cast on a mob that is less than six levels above the player, then the tap will check resistance as normal, but always return the full
amount of health of the spell to the necromancer even if resisted.

2. Really a corollary to the above in that it allows partial damage while still returning full health of the spell to the necromancer.

2b. Makes Taps irresistable, but nerfs ToN with an increase in recast time.

Fear
3. Provide a fear protection line that allows the necromancer to make the target immune from fear for a time.

4. Group fear protection--similar to above, perhaps an upgrade if a line is considered.

5. AoE fear removal spell which would encourage the necromancer to meditate near the fray and cast this spell somewhat regularly to keep all
players and pets on target. This spell would need both fast cast and recast times to be effective.

6. MOB fear elimination spell which, when cast on a critter would prevent it from casting AoE fear for the duration.

Pets
7. Make undead pets immune to AoE fear

8. Additional control commands
a) /no attack--pet will not defend itself
b) /sever--kills pet regardless of distance
c) /one target--prevents pet from changing targets unless countermanded

9. Raid-Pets: Max pet per summon, but have very slow movement rates, are immune to fear, and suicide after MOB is killed


General Grouping
10 Ignite Bones--Remove skeleton illusion in favor of a fiery particle effect

11. Provide a 42 second "splut-like" debuff overtime.

12. Group DMF

13. Group Feign Death. I think the general consensus from both necros and non was that this would be interesting to try out on test to see how
it worked. Some thought it would get more folks killed than it saved. Given the supposed ease to code, it might be worth a test. My personal
note is that the spell would probably need to be all-or-nothing based on the necromancer's save.

14. Mana-Link--a link similary to shifting site, but cancelable, that allows 80% of the necromancer's mana to immediately channel to the target
along with 80% of all mana gained during the duration of the spell. This spell should probably have a relatively long duration, 2-3 mins, but also a
very long recast time like 30 mins. Part of the compensation for this restriction should be a better ratio, perhaps 2-1.

15. Lower level requirement for Conjure Corpse from 57 to 55. Reasoning behind this from the poster is three-fold:
a) Summoning corpses is unarguably one of the few things necros do on raids.

b) Summon Corpse was gained 19 levels before, which is a very long time to wait for an upgrade

c) Shadow Knights are supposedly getting Summon Corpse at lvl 50 and are far better equiped to carry the heavy coffins than are necromancers.
Generally speaking, hybrids do not get a parent class spell until the parent class gets any available upgrades. At lvl 55, necromancers are still 5
lvls above the point where their child class gets the upgrade.

=== End Summary ===
