In a group you are usually the tank. What SKs do better than other tank types is taunt. You can be a snarer sometimes if there is no druid or ranger, but your dot's are not going to do significant damage. So use your mana to taunt and debuff the mobs. Disease cloud and siphon str are your bread and butter for a long time. Eventually heart flutter and shadow vortex work there way in. Keep casting these and the rogues, rangers, and casters will love that you are taking all the hits. Your other good spells are darkness and fear, but both are somewhat mana intensive and resisted alot.

This is my spell line up:

1. Dooming Darkness
2. Siphon Strength
3. Shadow Vortex
4. Shroud of Hate
5. Shroud of Pain
6. Disease Cloud
7. Life Leech or Siphon Life
8. Feign Death

Situations:
Puller: I don't worry about taunting as a puller. I usually just pull the mob in, and as soon as it's off me I got fetch another one. U will pull with Siphon Life, Shroud of Pain or any one of the other spells. I usually rotate so all the buffs are kept up.

When I'm FD pulling I pull with Disease Cloud. It really pisses the mob off and has a good range. That diseased mob usually hangs around my FD'd butt for a bit longer then the other mobs I agro and the tagger can smack it and pull it into the group.

Tank:
I am usually not the puller because the people I group with have the higher lvl Warriors pull so that I don't bring a green mobs by mistake.

When a mob is pulled in I run down my debuffs from Siphon Strength, Shadow Vortex, Shroud of Hate then Shroud of Pain. By this time the mob is pretty pissed at me and at about 80-85% health. Now I start chain casting Disease Cloud. After about 4 casts the mob is on me forever. Even if I stop attacking, taunting, casting and back off the Warriors will not be able to get the mob off. Warriors are sissies anyway and should leave the tanking to Troll SK's. They can sit in a corner and polish their |_|B3r armor. Wizards can IC at will and it will stick on me. Now the Warriors (lvl 55 and 56) are fully and totally pissed at me because they have hardly been hit. When the mob is at about 30% health if there is not a dR0oiD or |\|3[r0 in the group I cast Dooming Darkness. Bye bye mob, next.

Solo: outdoor zones only (BW or DL usually)
Spell line up changes a bit:
1. Dooming Darkness
2. Siphon Strength
3. Shadow Vortex
4. Shroud of Hate
5. Shroud of Pain
6. Invoke Fear
7. Life Leech or Siphon Life
8. Feign Death

I jazz up my Jboots or drink a SoW potion (pre-Jboots), fire up Vampiric Embrace and head out for a mob. I pull from a distance with Shroud of Pain and run back to where my nasty little pet Gobogonnerpecker is. As the mob approaches I cast Dooming Darkness. The mob starts whacking me now and my little pet starts to attack (god bless him). Now I cast Invoke Fear. As the mob runs away I cast Siphon Strength, Shadow Vortex and Shroud of Hate. I'm now usually down 2 bubs of mana. Most mobs take 2 Dooming Darknesses and 3 or 4 Invoke Fears. I usually get beat on a little bit while I recast, I could time it, but screw it. After a fight with a Typical Drovlarg Rager I have 4 bubs of health left and 1 bub of mana. I run back to my camp spot, med 4 min, the pull again. With a Clarity I acatually have much less down time then a dR0oiD or |\|3[r0.

Good luck,

I use a slightly different technique to pull, I ALWAYS pull with engulfing darkness. This gives me alot more control over the Mob coming back, I can stop and stack my DoT's and debuffs on it along the way back without getting hit. Especially without SoW, this gives you the ability to ensure agro and time for the group to be ready when you tell them "INC".

This also works great when intentionally pulling multiple MoB's. Darkness 1 and run, once they split apart because of the snare effect target and darkness the second, repeat as necessary. The advantage, 1st Mob in, everyone attacks, (No problems with assist becasue he's the only one there), MoB 2 arrives alone and Enchanter Mezzes him, debuffs him and waits for 3 - whatever. If your enchanter is good and you let him know up front this works great for those areas that it's almost impossible not to get 2.

Another benefit is it gives you a few more steps out in front if things go bad and you need to feign.

Thats what works for me, anyone who wants to enlighten me to any errors I'm making doing this, Thanks for the new Info. I always try to keep learning new tricks.
