If your goal is leveling, you should always try to get the best gear you can get. Having said that, full banded at L12 should give you enough AC to take little hits.

If you don't already have done that, get all the open slots filled first. Orc fang earrings are very inexpensive (10pp or less) and give good stats. Quite a bit more expensive, but useful for a very long time are Velium Fire Wedding Rings and a Goranga Idol.

You also should get and use Weapons of all types, 1HS, 1HB, Piercing, 2HB. You never know what might fall into your hands later, and training up weapon skills later is a pain to say the least.

Then, when you still have money and see something nice in the Bazaar, you can upgrade your armor piece by piece.

As a dwarf warrior, I'd save some money and buy all crustacean plate. Crustacean is cheap as heck in the bazaar and at the point where your stamina is at base (I'm guessing at least 95.. probably 100 or more) its going to add a bunch of hit points at level 12.

Banded is fine where you are at, but if you save some money and buy all crustacean (though a skull shaped barbute instead of the helm, for the raw 35 hp) and a pair of velium engagment rings (those ac 6 65 hp player made ones) you will massively increase your HP and AC from banded while really not expending huge amounts of cash (especially if you have guild jewelers who will sell the rings at around cost)

That would carry you to level 50, pretty much, as far as armor. I'd not waste money on more until you replace the crusty bp with a barbed ringmail one, 30 ac 50 hp etc, which is down to about 2200 - 3000 plat now. Crustacean is seriously NUTS as low level armor, its just really a GREAT BUY. Its very similar to dwarven cultural. Dwarven cultural is better (talking about the "low" dwarven cultural, not the high level BD stuff) but is going to cost TONS more.

That might sound like lots of money, but even as a low level character you can make quite a lot per night in the bazaar selling things like bonechips/spiderling silks/spider silks. Oh, another hint is save the "Orc Scalps" from CB. You can use them in a quest with the captain near the orcs in high hold OR sell them in the bazaar to other people who might want to do that quest. Its nice because it raises freeport miliita, as well as some other factions and gives you experience.

One nice thing is that many orc scalps are left on the bodies there, since NPC vendors will not buy them. So as you run around in cb fighting, you can often find abandoned corpses with orc scalps.

If you prefer to aquire gear on your own, rather than saving your plat and purchacing it, I'd wait for another 10 levels or so, and then begin doing the crafted armor quest. For a middle-range warrior, it's pretty decent, has nice stats and just looks darn cool on a dwarf. The only piece I didn't get was the helm, for which I found a skull-shaped barbute on sale from a vendor in FP for fairly cheap. My warrior used this armor all the way up until his retirement. There is better, of course. But I still see warriors in thier 50's with pieces of crafted armor on them.
