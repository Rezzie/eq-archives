While I was taking my Vah Shir Shaman through North Ro, I sat down above a Dervish cutthroat encampment to pay closer attention to guild chat. Suddenly, I hear in Erudite, “This is great, you take the right one and I ‘ll take the left.” Before I can even stand up and say “Huh?” A caster type comes screaming by with the whole camp of Dervishes on his tail. Needless to say he died. The caster plaintively asked on /t why I had not helped. I responded that I did not even realize he was there before he got himself killed.

I felt a little bad about what had happened, so I stuck around and buffed him well when he recovered his corpse. He promptly pulled another dervish, got in over his head and screamed for help again. This time I managed to run him down and kill the mob chasing him, after which he complained because he had gotten no XP for the 2% or so damage he had managed to do between running and screaming.

*sigh*

Since he left in a huff and logged before I could advise him of why things happened the way they did, I decided to post it here in the hope he would see it.

1. When you come across a cat sitting by himself on a sand dune out in the middle of nowhere apparently doing nothing at all, do not assume that he knows your obscure language or that he even notices your existence. If you want him to help you, establish communication before you pull down a multiple mobs. Introducing yourself to a new acquaintance by trying to scrape off a train on him does not get that relationship off to a good start.

Which leads to point 2: You are a caster, not a melee. You have no armor or hit points to speak of and if you wander around by yourself poking red con mobs with your little magic stick, the mobs are going to take it away from you and put it where the sun don’t shine.

Point 3: If you are silly enough to disregard points one and two, and pull multiple red cons anyway, (you did con them first, right?) When you get in trouble, HOLD STILL! If you run around like a blind hopper you have little chance of being targeted for a heal or for someone tougher to draw agro from you.

Finally, point 4. There is no XP given for running around screaming for help. If someone else does most of the damage, they get the XP for the kill.

The moral of this story for new caster type players:

When you wander about clueless, all you do is die a lot and make a hairy nuisance of yourself

Stay in the Newbie yard until you learn how to play your character class. That’s what it is there for. Ask someone who has played your class for a while for pointers. Most will be happy to help and flattered that you asked.

A lot of that is good advice for melees, too.

I would like to add to #1 that if you see a cat on a dune, then he/she is probably rolling around soaking up the sun and having fun playing in the dirt. You can expect this cat to not notice armageddon, should it choose that moment in time to spawn.

For #3: If you're in trouble and start to run away, but aren't getting OUT OF TROUBLE, then by running away from danger, you're also running away from help. If you are outrunning your opponent then fine, outrun him/her/it, but if not, then stand there and take it.
