There is a point where a game needs to seperate itself from the competition visually, and it's clear that DAoC has NOT done this. If you want to see similar systems that are different, look at the Wizardry series. Wiz 6, while ugly, puts things in a different place from Wiz 7. Wiz 8 looks to be VERY different, while keeping a very similar system to the previous games.

While it's too early to be sure, Horizons appears to be a VERY large step forward from EQ.

Clones are those that look the same, while having differences under the cover. It's clear that DAoC is trying to copy a LOT of the old generation of EQ. BUT, look at Kunark and Velious, notice the lack of brackets around the quest text? Notice that the new areas have a LOT of advanced elements not seen in Kunark or "Old Norrath"?

Sure, EQ needs a LOT of improvements, but don't compare a clone of EQ(pre Velious) to what EQ 2 might be. You can bet EQ 2 will have the graphics, sound, and effects to at least not make you cringe when you compare it to newer games. And the design flaws that prevent large-scale changes to the world will be missing in EQ2(if it ever comes out).

If you want to look forward to something, look to Horizons, not to a cheap clone.

Just a note on the DAoC system -- there IS an alternative to PvP combat. Someone asked about this during one of the chat sessions that the developers were holding. I don't know the exact wording, but the developers said it is entirely possible to avoid PvP combat entirely and still make it to the maximum level in game.

However, if you don't participate in inter-Realm combat, some quests and the drive for titles will be unavailable. You earn Realm points (and the attendant titles) based on how many enemies you've killed, relics you've stolen, etc. But you can still max the level of your character.

The reasons I am so looking forward to DAoC? The elimination (pretty much) of twinking -- high level gear does no more for a low-level character than the gear appropriate for their level -- and a WORKING ECONOMY. Gear decays and gets worn. Swords have to be sharpened. All gear eventually wears out and has to be replaced -- and player-made stuff is some of the best available.

We'll see if ANY of the next-gen MMORPGs can live up to it's promises, though... My money is on DAoC in the short-term, and Horizons in the long-term.
