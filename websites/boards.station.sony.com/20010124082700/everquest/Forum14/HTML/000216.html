There is a world of difference between the healing capacities of a level 11 or even 27 druid, and a level 60. Basically the higher level one gets as a druid, the worse one's healing abilities become relative to those of a cleric.

Pure capabilities aside, druids also tend to make less effective healers simply because they do it less. They have less practice watching HPs and judging when a heal is in order and when a heal should be delayed to avoid pissing off mobs. Further, they have to divert their attention from healing because they have to worry about snaring and damage shielding, at the very least, in addition to backup healing.

Druids also have to worry about whether their scarce mana should be diverted to healing or saved in case an evac turns out to be necessary. And if a druid does mess up and get a mob on her/him, s/he can take less of a beating because of not being a plate or even chain wearing class; further, it is especially important that the druid keep mobs off her/him because it will be his/her responsibility to evac.

While it's depressing that on a dragon or other uber-mob run, druids are relegated to the role of either third-rate healer or third-rate nuker ... it is also true that druids are welcome group members on other large scale excursions, for example planar/Hole/CoM/etc raids, where we are able to use all our strengths effectively.

Unfortunately the problem of post 50 spells fizzling seems to be common at least to all priest classes, if not all casters.

As for calling this forum "Healers," personally I believe it should be called "Priest Classes," since this is a more proper description of the commonalties between clerics, druids, and shamans (wis casters). The only reason I can think of for it not to have this name would be confusion among newbies as to what "Priest Classes" meant, as well as possible confusion as to why monks would be excluded from that definition.
