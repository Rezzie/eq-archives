Every Druid does it at least once... it just hurts the most at higher levels.

You set up your kite, be it DD, Root and Rot or some multi-kite. (Now, no feedback about kiting in general please, it takes some skill and is a viable tactic when you can't get a group.) Anyhoo, eventually you will mess up between tabbing on critters that you are lining up and yourself. Wham, you snare (bad and likely dead) or root (hope you are rooting and rotting if you pull this one off) yourself. If you are quad kiting the results can be truly stunning. You should see what four blue cons can do to a Druid with less than 700AC. The only good news is that only three get to beat on you at once. The bad news is that it only takes three of them one or two rounds to pulp you to death.

I have also certainly seen the perma-kiter syndrom. A high level Druid abondons his kiting ways to begin grouping. (Yes, this is a stereotype. Many, many Druids make excellent groupmates.) Having never really had to worry about aggro he/she doesn't know that slinging high-taunt spells from the word 'INC a %T' is a bad idea. Generally this one is figured out pretty soon. Still, the open with DoT, follow with ensnare (try three times to stick) and then finish by tossing in what heals you can strategy ends up with a dead Druid pretty quick. The lovely new SoV spells feature taunts that magnify this marvelously.
