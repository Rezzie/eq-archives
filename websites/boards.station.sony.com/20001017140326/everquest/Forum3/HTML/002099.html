It's a fairly rare occurrence for most casters, but it happens all the time to Bards. Here goes:

If a spell takes effect at about the same time as a previous casting of the same spell fades (within about a quarter second of the fading, my estimate) then the new effect is cancelled instantly. Most casters usually don't have this problem because their buffs need to be renewed only once in a while. The short duration and pulse nature of Bard songs, however, lend themselves naturally to this bug and it regularly strikes us.

An example of how it goes sometimes:

Selo's accelerrando started...
It takes effect.
Selo's stopped and a couple songs twisted...
Selo's Accelerrando started again, just before it fades.
Selo's Accelerrando fades.
Selo's Accelerrando takes effect and fades instantly.


Another Bard bug:

Whenever someone's buffs start to fade, they flash in and out to show they're fading. This doesn't happen with Bards; since songs flash on other people's screens and would be really annoying if they flashed on ours, they don't flash on ours at all.
