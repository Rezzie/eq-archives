Well, that makes sense. Same thing would happen with the Unreal Tournament engine. Anytime their NORMAL hud stuff was displayed (weapons, health, etc), it was fine because they were just throwing up a single item on the canvas for each picture (each graphic was 2 polygons. In a 3d engine, it takes less time to draw 2 triangles and put them together to make a square than it does to tell the renderer how to draw a single square). However, when you brought up their menuing system, the speed anything ran at if you had the option to show 3d world while menu's etc were being displayed, decreased dramatically. The reason was that there was no easy method to take all the text being displayed and put it on a single texture, THEN render it as 2 polygons, so every single character you saw on screen in every word was 2 triangles. The more text you got on screen (the built in UT IRC client is a good example), the worse the performance got. I've only seen it perform well on higher end systems than mine (600mhz+).

I suspect that the EQ engine suffers the same problems, only amplified due to the fact that it's a 3 year old 3d engine with virtually no enhancements if any for current hardware.
