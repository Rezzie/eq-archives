These threads on changes the shaman community are all well and good, but most of it's just gravy. They don't HAVE to be stackable. The durations don't HAVE to be longer. That would be nice, but it shouldn't be our greatest concern right now. In my opinion, here are the only aspects of alchemy that need immediate changes:

1. Null Potions. Right now the herb Mandrake affects disease resistance instead of magic resistance. This clearly seems to be a bug, since disease resistance is already covered by another herb.

2. Component Requirement. The potions for Scale Skin, Water Breathing and Levitate require the same components as the spell in order for the potion to work. Once again, this seems to be a bug and should be fixed.

3. Mana Consumption. It does seem odd that drinking potions requires mana. However, if it's difficult to fix this isn't an extreme concern. Most mana costs are negligible, and the potion will still work if you are out of mana.

4. Blistering Flesh. The upgrade has the same effects as the base level. My guess is the code for this spell effect doesn't exist yet--implementing that would be nice.


For now, that's it. Let's get these important fixes taken care of first and THEN worry about a "wish list".
