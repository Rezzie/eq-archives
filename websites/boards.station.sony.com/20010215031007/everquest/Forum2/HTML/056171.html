Point 1: Against a fast hitting melee, a damage shield is the single most mana efficient spell. Making the shield 'proc' based upon the level of the recipient would be nice.
My first thought was that due to code sharing between MOBs and characters, this would not work. Then I thought about the level range of MOBs. In one area, most MOBs are of a comparable level to each other. Those that damage shield others would not have those shields reduced by much. It could work.

Point 2: Any player cast spell which has a reduced chance to save against is overpowered in PvP. Code sharing stops this from really being fixable. Any reduction in saves versus the movement reduction spells will have a HUGE impact against the characters who use that tactic against MOBs. In example - in PvP, Verant could give snare a chance to break each time magical damage is done (like root). This would carry over to any snared MOB. Druids, Necros and Rangers immediately loss the most common way they fight. Any melee who uses a weapon which procs a snare suddenly becomes far more powerful (unless they are also using a DD procing weapon). Just increasing the resist rates would have a different effect - it would take longer to get a snare to stick, leading to more chances that someone is going to be trained over.

Point 3: Pretty much total agreement. If I am logged in as a caster, why do my spells take the 66% nerf but a virtually uninteruptable ability which has a chance to "one hit" a caster at high level does not. Worse yet, if the shadowknight is bound near the fight, he can return to harmtouch every 30 seconds or so. Paladin lay on hands and shadowknight harmtouch have a built in timer of one game day. All Verant needs do is set the timer to start counting before it can be used. In other words, paladin/shadowknight spawns at bind point with the ability greyed out. One game day later the ability is usable. The code already exists. Look at the wizard spell 'Harvest' -- once memorized, the gem remains greyed out for 10 minutes. Divine Aura and Spirit of Cheetah show this as well.

The evade nerf should not affect PvP at all. Evade should give the rogue a chance to cause the other character to lose targetting. Or better yet, cause the evaded character to target something else!
