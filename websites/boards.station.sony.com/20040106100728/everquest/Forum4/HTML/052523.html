I am afraid your 'reliable source' is a bit off.

Far as I know, Longhorn (Win 2005) will include certain security features, which will take advantage of particular hardware. If you have older hardware, these extra security features will be simply turned off.

Companies are not anxious to upgrade in the first place, if present hardware/software works well. My company still has Windows 2000 on all work PCs - cause switching to XP Pro would not yield any noticable advantage.
Microsoft will never shoot itself in the foot by releasing an OS that requires a full hardware upgrade as well.

I'd say if your PC can run DOOM 3/HalfLife 2 (both coming out REAL soon), you should be more than ready for EQ2.
I'd say...
Pentium4 3GHz+, 875P chipset mobo, 1GB DDR400 RAM, a high-speed SATA drive and a Radeon 9800Pro card should let you enjoy EQ2 will all bells and whistles and lots of headroom.
