Heh heh I remember Beta and the First night of Release. The biggest thing was seeing a decaying skeleton pop in GFay with a scythe or a scimitar. You were cool then because you didnt have a weapon that looked like the chrome strip off the side of a car door. heh heh

Patchwork was a big deal, and people showing up with a mino axe in each hand and chainmail were gods. Everyone would gather around them and oooh and ahhh. People fell to their deaths constantly in Kelethin, and there was a sound bug in the game where you could hear spells really loud, so youd be out in the dark forest(actually only 50 feet from the guards), and hear the sound of a summoning spell, and it gave the forest a really eerie feeling. The growls of orcs sent people running back to the guards, and orc centurians were the uber mobs. heh heh

Kill Stealing was unavoidable upon release as there were hundereds of Wood Elves and High Elves running through the newbie areas of GFay. So everyone was always running around from mob to mob trying to gain that little bit of exp. Mino axes sold for 10pp in Qeynos and Halas and people learned to make a fortune off them by carrying backpacks full to the far corners of Norrath. And since hardly anyone had TP spells then, actually walking from GFay to Qeynos or Halas was a big deal.

Heh heh During beta, test server would sometimes have 5 people on it total. Sometimes being the only Dark Elf in Nektulos had its advantages.

It was alot of fun. The feeling of immersion and newness was overwhelming and quite euphoric. But alas, the dewdz came to town and prices shot up on items and camping and uber guilds slowly came into play. And now you basically get what you have today.

Station Member 		posted 02-11-2001 08:44 PM           
Hah, I remember Aradune's speeches (or rather, apologies) in Chat in the entire first week of release. Most communication I've had with ANYONE related to the game ever since. Kinda sad.

The great Mino Axe trades were considered the best way in the entire game to make money, and the theory behind "Buying in LFay and selling in the Western half of Antonica" was a well-kept quasi-secret. I never bought one, as I played a High Elf wizard, but I witnessed a large amount of them be traded. Then there's one of my interesting memories: Someone was selling Dervish rings for 3pp each in GFay, at the bank. The line stretched out the bank, around the tree, and down the ramp. I was certain that ANY +stat items would help my High Elf Wizzie. Guess I was KINDA wrong.

I think the Screaming Mace was one of the most-camped items on the server. You'd see them going for insane (to a newbie) prices in GFaydark. Interestingly, just about no one hunted in Butcherblock, except in the immediate newbie area. I made a whole "ton" of money there, selling off the precious Skunk Glands and Spider Silks (which were then used for NOTHING). First learned about the effect Charisma had on selling prices there, due to SkunkSpray.

I even managed to somehow get an interesting bug happen to me: I dinged to level 7. I went adventuring in Steamfont, died to a Kobold, and went down a level. When I returned to my bind point, I was STILL level 7, and had a full exp bar. One kill, and level 8. A level I never had to go through.

Oh, and of course, in EXTREMELY early release, nothing in the game gave you faction hits for killing them. Well, actually they DID, but they didn't report it. You just killed something, and it died. No faction lists. /loc also used to give you 3 coordinates, but instead of the Y, X, and Z axes, they gave you X, Y and Heading, which basically made Sense Heading 100% pointless, once you learned the numbers.

My favorite thing as a newbie were the events held by guilds considered high level (at least they were then... they could afford to GIVE AWAY 50 platinum!!) There were a couple of events held by Clan Banzai on my server (The Rathe), and I managed to finally get a prize: a Thaumaturgist Robe. Ugly, and gave only +agi, but no one could afford to give away an FBR. I wore that robe for months.

Speaking of FBRs, I haven't seen many, if any, worn recently. I see an absurd number of Green Silk Drapes, SMRs, Robe of Enshroudments, Robe of the Elements, and Ishva Robe (I'm a proud Ishva owner, too!) and even more newbie robes worn than FBRs. I guess it went out of style at one of the points I quit playing the game for a month or so.

So yes, early release was quite interesting. Wizards were universally hated because they could KS anything (no Group Aggregate Damage), no one EVER got a teleport, Bind Affinity sellers (no one did it for free) were mobbed with people within seconds, and you wouldn't BELIEVE the amount of corpses under Kelethin.
