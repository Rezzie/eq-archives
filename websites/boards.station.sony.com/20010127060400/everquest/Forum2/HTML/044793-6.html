Ok Alan. You wanted suggestions that would not make necros more powerful, but would solve the problem. Here is my suggestion:

What about making uber mobs take one quarter damage from taps? This would take some coding, but since there is code already for partial resists I imagine it can be done. This way the mob would take 180 damage from our 720 tap, but still give the necro 720hp back. Then you can lower the resist rate to say 30%. To keep necros from abusing this by chain casting you can make the agro for the spell fairly high. This way a necro can only cast let's say 3 taps on the mob for the duration of the entire fight. Any more than this and the necro gets attacked. This would be a VERY bad thing for the necro as it would either take a LOT of mana from the cleric to save him (thus defeating the purpose of tapping in the first place) or get him killed (which not only defeats the purpose of tapping, but in addition takes a lot of mana from the cleric to try to save him AND takes time and mana to resurrect the necro). So restricting the necro to 3 casts means they would do on average a total of 378 damage to the mob (180x3)-([180x3]x30%) but receive a good amount more health to convert to mana and give away. Even if the necro decides to use up an essence emerald per uber mob casting a Trucidation he still only does 443 damage total ([275]-[275x30%])+([180x2]-[{180x2}x30%]). This is hardly a scratch to a 32khp mob. In order to kill a 32khp mob with pure necro damage output it would take 73 level 60 necros (32000/443) to do and even more with the new Velious mobs. This does not, however, take into account that for the 18 seconds (All 3 taps have 6 second casting times) the mob will be killing off the necros and regenrating hit points. For obvious reasons I just don't see this ever happening.


You also have to take into account that in all my examples the necros are 59th or 60th level and they all have the 59th and 60th level lifetap. They would be doing MUCH less damage with lower versions of a tap.

Okay, here's a good idea that will "fix" necros during raid time, but not give them any more power or be unbalancing.

First off I want to say that I agree with Absor on most suggested necro enchancements. Unlimited stacking dots, unresistable lifetaps, and so on are unbalancing, and increase our power not only during raid time, but during every other time as well. Necros are just fine during normal grouping conditions, and so dont need any enchancements.

However, we are less than useful during the actual boss encounter. A few mobs, we can land the occasional DoT, lifetap, and so on, but on the majority of mobs we are limited to dumping mana.

Mana pumping is just fine, in my opinion, but it is horribly inefficient. I can pump about 500-600 mana from FOM to OOM. That's using around 2k of my own mana. This translates into about one complete heal plus change. Liching, I can give 100 mana per minute. So during the 3 minute fight, I'll have given 800 mana total: two complete heals. Why bring a necro when you can bring anoher cleric, or another tank (a "complete heal")?

My solution is the following: make mana transfer efficient *only during raids*. How is this possible? Add a spell that transfers ALL of the necros mana to another class. Recast time on the spell: 30 minutes (or 1 hour, or whatever). The recast time makes this spell useless under normal conditions, as it will only add "free mana" (Absor's example of liching necro + cleric, ie infinite mana) once every hour,and it will take up a spell slot. It will only be memorized during the raid.

This spell benefits necros in that it lets a necro effectively become any casting class during a raid. A necro can make a cleric have twice the mana, or a wizard twice the mana, etc. It will still, however, be better to have the actual extra cleric there. Yes, a necro and cleric could have exactly the same mana as two clerics. However, two clerics can heal twice as fast. Two wizards can do damage twice as fast as a necro and a wizard. A wizard with a necro pumping him might get himself aggroed because he did too much individual damage, which wouldn't happen to two wizards. Basically, this new spell makes necromancers desirable on raids, and yet still lets other classes be more desireable.

You may be thinking that with lich on, we could start medding and pump even more mana after we "complete mana dumped". This is true, although I've found that I can only pump 100 mana per minute. This is almost trivial... During the course of a fight, I MIGHT get enough mana to the cleric for another complete heal. However, if you think this is "too good" with the necros new complete mana dump, make the new spell have this effect: decrease necros HP by 70%. Since HP = mana for a necro, this will effectively stop the necro from liching / pumping any more after he complete mana dumped.

Whew, I'm done I personally think this is a decent suggestion, which is completely realistic. I'd like to hear your comments about it, as well as Alans.

It seems that the majority of the problems here boil down to one simple fact. The necromancer class breaks down if they cannot regain health via taps and that MANY high level mobs resist taps completely.

I have a coupld of suggestions:

1) Change the resist type of Taps from Magic to whichever is found LEAST on mobs, Disease or Poison

2) Decrease the resist rates on taps but leave them magic based

3) Here is the really creative one and I'm going to use a bit of simplistic PseudoCode for when a Necromancer attacks a mob via the Tap Deflux which damages for about 310 and heals for about 310

If MOB_LVL >= (Player_Lvl + 6) Then 100% Resist ELSE Check Resist
If Check Resist=True Then MOB_Damage=0 and Necro_Heal=310 ELSE MOB_Damage=310 and Necro_Heal-310

In case my pseudocode doesn't make sense, here it is in english.

If a mob is 6 lvls or less above the necro, he will ALWAYS be healed based on the power of tap used. If the spell passes the resist test, then the mob will also have damage done to it.

Now..some of you might be thinking...what a coding nightmare, but I don't think so, because this is already how things work a necro Tap-Over-Time spell is active on the mob. For example: If Necro #1 casts Bond of Death on the Mob, the Mob is damaged about 800 over time and the Necro is Healed the same over that period of time. If Necro #2 casts Bond of Death on the Mob while Necro #1's Bond of Death is still active, then the Necro is healed, but the Mob is NOT DAMAGED.

Personally, I think this solution is quite clever as it does not add any significant power to the Necro Class, yet allows that class to perform its new support roles efficiently against high-level mobs because it can regain health lost to lich whilst giving mana to others.
