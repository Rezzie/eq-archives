I suppose I'll chime in on the pro-twinking side.

I twink, I don't hide it. Heck, I uber twink, and you know what? No one in game has ever said anything about it, it hasn't prevented me from getting groups or given me preference in groups either. The main difference for me is that I don't twink my characters til after level 10, usually around level 12. Why? Because I want to learn the class first. This doesn't entitle me to twink because I have "put in my time", it is just my own arbitrary point. I was also one of the people who suffered from equipment envy for a while, then I got over it, then I moved to a different server, started naked and got to a higher level. Once there, I decided to see what other classes/races were like.

[The above isn't quite true, a higher level SK was looking at me weird for being a level 15 SK with full darkforge, but I told him who my other character was (we had grouped several times) and his response was "okay, cool, nice to see you playing a shadow knight."]

I am amused by this whole string of threads on twinking and how it is morally wrong or wrong from a game perspective etc etc.

Why do I twink? Several reasons.
-The first is that I like doing quests, its fun. This is probably the main reason that I have a bard with more play time than a lot of the level 50's around and I am in my mid-30's.
-A lot of the time the quest item may rot on the corpse. I play mostly during off peak, so I am not depriving anyone of the quest items (for the most part, I'll come to this again later). This relates closely to the first reason for twinking, and for the one following.
-I like to try out the different classes. I have played every class except for the paladin. Once I get around to twinking, I can play and enjoy the class (more) because I don't have to do the naked guy scramble for equipment. So what if I make a sudden shift from a regular robe (or leather, or bronze) to decent equipment?
Twinking doesn't give me much of an advantage, but the advantage is there... I have slightly less downtime that I did before I twinked. I still die lots, and I still have to be careful. I attribute my success to being reasonably intelligent and learning the class a bit before I twink.
-My playing style is a preference for soloing. I solo'd, for the most part, on MUDs when I played those, and I solo in EQ a lot. And before anyone tells me that I can't solo without twinking, you are utterly wrong. I have solo'd 3 characters to 20 (at least) without twinking. It's around the mid 20's that I have to stop soloing (whether I have twinked equipment or not, it is a flaw in the game design as far as I am concerned), which is a shame. I don't have anything against grouping, and I do it for a change of pace, or if someone wants to share a spawn with me.
-I also twink just for the looks of the items. This last part is pretty shallow, but some of the stuff just looks far too cool.

The above are my personal reasons. As a character I always try to play nice with others. I share spawns if needed, and if I have camped/killed something needed for a quest when all I was doing in that area was looking for the critter for the quest and someone comes looking for it, I'll stick around and help them kill it when it pops up next if they want the help.

I think this particular thread was started by someone complaining that he lost a dragoon dirk when he dropped it. Guess what? They do that when you drop them, they did a year ago when I lost mine. I accidentally dropped the pack that I use to store my instruments/weapons for swapping. I had grabbed the pack rather than opening it(left rather than right click), and missed the edge of the hot box that I was using for that inventory slot, so it fell to the ground, I did this now and again with instruments or weapons when swapping too. I am terribly thankful to Verant for finally putting in the drop confirmation. When I picked the pack up less than 3 seconds after dropping it, the dirk was gone. I filed a bug report, then went a bought a combine longsword with the money I had in the bank. The point to this story?

Get over it, it's a game.

The same can be said to people on both sides of this arguement. Anti-twinkers are deluded in thinking either 'if I can't, no one should be able to', 'if I don't, no one should, that it somehow ruins balance or gives an extreme advantage and finally that people of a low level can't possibly have earned the items. Having played twinked and untwinked, I can assure you that it does not matter much either way, the balances that Verant puts in pretty much takes care of that. Also, for an enterprising individual, there are many ways to make money. Heck, I made a few hundred plat long ago selling language lessons. The pro twinkers are deluded in thinking that they have 'earned' the right to twink or that all the anti-twinkers are just jealous. I believe in a lot of cases that it isn't jealousy, it is a feeling that low levels with 'higher' gear somehow trivializes their accomplishments in getting to their higher level (or the gear that they have). It doesn't, you still have your accomplishments.

Your opinions are just that, opinions, Just as these are my opinions, and I have a feeling that I will probably get flamed for them. There is no right or wrong on this issue, it's pretty much all grey. Twink if you want, don't twink if you don't want, live and let live.

The problem and the cause is simple:

Items don't EVER wear out.

Yeah, that's right... I can use my Runed Blade from 1st level to 60th and it will still have the exact same stats. Same thing for my Crafted Armor or my rings. They will never wear out and need to be replaced.

What this means is that when I no longer need these items because I received better items, then I have the choice of either selling for plat (wasting my already scant time) or giving it away to someone that would LOVE me for it. I have yet to find anyone who didn't thank me profusely for the items that I have given them. They will send me tells for weeks about how much they love the item and how it makes them enjoy the game more.

You might wonder how this applies to my point about items not wearing out... well, there are probably somewhere around 4000-5000 people who play regularly on Solusek Ro every day (various times). The FBSS spawn comes around every 8 hours I believe, maybe more, maybe less. This means that around 2.5 enter the game every day. We'll say that it isn't camped for around 4 hours a day. That means that 17 FBSSs enter the game every week and 884 every year. The game has been out for nearly 2 years now... we'll say that there are nearly 1400 FBSSs in the game now. This means that because they never wear out, around 1/3 of the players on Solusek Ro have an FBSS. Since approximately 1/3 of the people who are on a server (even though they play many characters) are of the warrior subset (war, shd, pal, rng, mnk), this would mean that every warrior type playing could have one easily if people didn't twink.

Obviously if every main warrior had an FBSS, the cost of the item would plummet quickly, same for every single item. The items will continue to lose value as any server becomes more 'mature', as there are more of the items entering the game and nearly none of them being taken out of the game. Even if items simply wore down over time (lower haste %, lower damage, higher delay, weaker proc, etc...), then the value of the higher level (indeed even mid-level items) would maintain much better.

Twinking does not ruin the economy, if anything, it keeps it healthy as the items are removed from the general population and put on characters that are either rarely or nearly never played. What ruins the in-game economy is the fact that items never have to leave the game. Items only leave the game when someone gets either forgetful and lets their corpse decay, they destroy the item, or they quit the game and don't give away their items to in-game friends. All 3 of these events is very rare and probably happens less than twice per day on any server involving a character above level 10.

Imagine you bought a VCR and it never wore out. You would nearly never buy a new one and the market would drop out on them. In the real world, either fewer VCRs would be made, or the price would drop drastically. Both of these have happened with VCRs, even though they wear out after a time. Think about that boy with the Hummer, if it didn't wear out ever, then he could use it forever and the market would drop out on vehicles, one only being bought for aesthetic reasons or because the previous one being involved in a wreck which ruined it. This is how economy works. Things wear out and go bad in the real world and a decent amount of the economy is based upon replacement of these worn out pieces of equipment. This is where Verant made a mistake.
