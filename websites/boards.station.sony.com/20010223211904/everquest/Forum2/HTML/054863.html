Guys, today I'd like to forego the usual second part of my writings to talk about something I believe in. And it is not comedic in the least. Today on the boards I read a thread 636 posts long by Elementalist925. I encourage you to look it up if it can still be found.

Elementalist has played since beta, his wife just recently joined in April of 2000. She created a character named Managirl Beachbum because she loved oasis. I won't word it in the way Elementalist did since I want to keep to the facts here, but suffice to say she had fun, as all players do. One day she got "the call" and a GM popped in and told her she was in violation of the naming policy. Managirl asked why, not knowing what it was. 5 minutes later Managirl Beachbum became Named Matemp.

The real person behind Managirl started crying after that, and Elementalist wrote that post to say that he and his wife were leaving.

Those were the facts. And that's sad.

It's one thing to blame guides. Guides are just normal players whose hands are tied by Verant's policy. They don't get paid and they get upset and tired and cranky like anyone else. It's another issue when a GM, a Verant employee is involved.

Were something like this done by a guide and there were 600 posts on the board, Verant would likely change it immeadiatly. But since this was a GM, and Verant has to speak in a unified voice, it's unlikely things are going to change, and often they don't.

It all goes back to Verant's marketing hook-phrase:

"You're in our world now."

Which *is* a good line, but it's not true. It was never true, and it never will be. Verant doesn't own this world. But they like to flex by saying they own the "intellectual copyrights" to the software, which I suppose is okay if they use it correctly.

Like shutting down E-Bay, I can support that.

But removing the account of a writer on an unofficial board because of their fan fiction, that I can't support much at all.

And hurting the person-behind-the-person because of some stupid, arbitrary policy, that won't be supported by anyone.

These are all symptoms of "You're in our world now."

But we're not, because you own exactly what you say you do, intellectual copyrights. *We*, the players, own this world. It's always been our world, we just let you play around with it like the miniature Bristlebanes you are.

Just like the hackers trying to create a hacked copy of EQ, you seem to have somewhere missed the point.
