You will find only regret hunting in North Ro. The place is filled with wandering agressive monsters. My level 12 could mostly walk around without fear, but before then you just never knew when a madman would run up and start whacking you. Dangers of North Ro:
pumas, shriveled mummies, orc raiders, jackals, madmen, desert madmen, crypt mummy, named mummy (Rahotep), the rare sand giant

Derv's don't wander unless pulled. Dorn B'Dynn stays in his camp (it's the Derv camp closest to the ocean) unless pulled away, and he's a mean SOB. Desert Tarantulas and Giant Scarabs aren't aggressive unless attacked but be careful because they will swarm you under.

At 5th level, there's not a lot you can hunt in North Ro. Not sure what level the Jackals are but I think they lowest level of them would give you a serious run for your money.

I would advise against hunting there alone for a few more levels. But that's just me

North Ro, a zone I am very familiar with. Spent my youth there (lvl 8-16). North Ro is very dangerous for a youngling like you. Level 5, unless grouped with a few other higher levels, is extremely dangerouos. Here is why? Orcs, Jackals and Rattlesnake are fairly easy but there is always something else nearby that likes to join in. As a few of the posters stated Madmen can be quite nasty as well as the shrivelled mummies. Pumas are found in plentiful supply around the plat which is located close by the ec tunnel entrance. The nice thing about the plat is a lot of people fight there and more often than not will assist you when your in trouble. Don't depend on the help though because they are sometimes all low level and they don't like fighting Shrivelled mummies either.

I would probably not go any deeper into Nro than the plat if there is no one else there to either assist or group with so you best bet is in EC until you hit level 8-10. The pumas aren't going anywhere and when you hit that level you will have more than you can handle in Nro. A note about Dervs. They don't wander unless pulled by a group. Very often a derv group will get overwhelmed and there first inclination is to run to the plat for help. Sometimes you get a warning and sometimes not. Be prepared!! A dervish thug would make short work of you unfortunately.

There is the odd Sand giant the swings by Derv camp 3 (along the cliffs) and over to Derv 2 but that is rare.

Only general advice I give is if you chose to fight there try to get into a group but unfortunately if you goal is to fight pumas only for their pelts you might have difficulty finding others who only want to do that. Derv groups are very popular.

Cheers
