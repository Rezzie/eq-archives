My Ranger, Tolusknar, recently dinged 51, and as a break from experience grinding I decided to go on walkabout and visit Nedaria's Landing.

Boy, am I glad I did. Talk about your eye candy! But first, the journey.

I hadn't spent a lot of time in Jaggedpine before, so Nedaria's being accessed from there made this a great opportunity to do some scouting.

I was pleased to note that the Jaggedpine animals (bears, cats, and wolves) all were amiable, even in my wolf form. But that's to a Tunare-worshipping, human Ranger; your mileage may vary, of course.

The poachers were dubious, but still not KoS.

The griffawns and griffons were scowling, but that was no great surprise (I've yet to meet a friendly griffon). Although Jaggedpine is crawling (flying?) with them, it wasn't too difficult to avoid them. For whatever strange reason, they are considered "animals" for the purposes of casting Fear Animal, so when one did aggro, a little bit of fear-kiting made quick work of it.

And those anime-style dryads, well, they're just so cute you want to hug one of them and their tree.

The entrance to Nedaria's Landing is located deep in the cave inhabited by the Brell-worshipping gnolls.

It took me a while to locate a gnoll of Brell from a safe distance in order to get a /con and find out I wasn't KoS, even in wolf form, which made it safe to trot through into Nedaria's Landing. I stopped to chat with a few, but it seemed that all they were interested in was gifts of food. Dryad pate, indeed. None would tell me what [favor] awaited me, should I choose to sate their appetites.

A quick dash under the waterfall behind the Oracle, a short swim and I was there.

On zoning into Nedaria's, all I could say was, "Wow", probably about 20 times over. First thing I noticed was the awesome stone textures on the zone entrance/exit. The next thing that catches the eye are all the waterfalls-- there must be a dozen of varying sizes, including a nearly Niagra-sized one.

More surprises waiting down at the mouth of the river... surf! There is a sea with moving foam "waves", and a school of tropical fish! The water is very clear so that you can see the underwater rocks and the creatures swimming amongst them... reminded me very much of a Carribean beach scene. I was so overwhelmed by the beauty that I forgot to try fishing there to see what one can catch... Doh!

Watch out for the cove frogs and sea turtles, both of which are highly aggressive. The pesky frogs kept aggroing through the pier as I attempted to talk to all the NPCs standing about. You can do a couple of "quest" turn-ins of various frog and turtle parts, but the rewards aren't currently worth it, in my opinion. You are promised some "information" along with a monetary reward (I got about 7pp for turning in four frog eyes), but the "information" is (currently, at least) a bit of useless trivia about the quest NPC. Hardly exciting stuff.

Still, I got to speak with the famous Morden Rasp, Calliav, Tondal Di'Xevar and even Nedaria herself, who seemed rather down-to-earth despite having an entire zone named after her.

There isn't actually a boat under construction-- yet. But many of the NPCs hint that that could change very soon. Certainly they seem all ready and geared up to get started.

Can't wait to see what comes next!

Make yourself a new human Ranger or Druid and pick Surefall Glades as your home base, and you will nearly be there.

Bum a SoW off someone, take the teleporter stone in the bear caves to Jaggedpine, then run south along the west wall to the cave of the Brell Gnolls (having the LDoN maps helps a lot as the cave entrance is hard to see). The only dangerous part is that brief run through Jaggedpine, and a level 1 character has nothing to lose. If you get killed, you'll be back in Surefall and you can just try again until yo make it.
