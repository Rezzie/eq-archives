I'll try to keep this short.

Main: half elf ranger.
His career started in Kelethin when another half-elf ranger tutored him and passed along his equipment. The elder ranger was forced to retire due to a bizarre disease he received from the Bloodsabers which made him allergic to steel. Since the elder ranger was from Surefall Glade, the younger ranger grew to hate gnolls and orcs alike. As his knowledge of the outside world grew, he discovered that the dark elves were assisting the Crushbone orcs in the destruction of his hometown. Therefore, the ranger developed a hatred of dark elves as well, and refuses to help them except in neutral territory (e.g. the Nexus, Plane of Knowledge).

His desire to travel and learn new things led to his keen interest in languages. He has mastered several tongues and seeks to learn the few he has not yet heard yet. He is willing to share this skill with others who ask... provided they are not dark elves.

He had invested considerable time and money into fletching and other trade skills in the desire to make himself a Faydark Thunderbolt. When he discovered that "his people," as he had viewed them, would not let him construct the items due to his half-human heritage, he was devastated. He now spends much of his time wandering the wilds of Kunark, increasing his skills and plotting for the day he can venture into Neriak to "persuade" the dark elves to leave the Faydarks alone....

Alt 1: Iksar Necro
Unlike the cruel, dominating approach many of his kind have towards outsiders, this necromancer believes that his race evolved more from a practical standpoint, and the dominant attitude is a byproduct of their forced isolation. With the discoveries of Luclin and the Plane of Knowledge, he seeks to test this theory by learning as much about other races as possible. He is known for his tendency to take out a book and pen and ask strangers, "So, why do you hate my kind?"

His idea of a practical standpoint is often viewed as "evil" by others, but explaining this can be difficult for the young Iksar. For example, he sees the custom of burial to be wasteful, not only of good wood used for coffins, but also for a perfectly viable corpse, which could be animated to perform menial tasks and make life easier.

On a constant search for knowledge, he has found an abundant source of interviewees on the moons of Luclin - that and the Vah Shir don't want to kill him instantly. Lately, he has been visiting an area known as the Warrens, near Paineel. Hearing of the Erudites storehouse of knowledge, he hopes to get in their "good" graces so that knowledge can be exchanged.

Alt: Troll Beastlord
This troll embraces his cruel nature almost as much as he embraces his warder, whom he refers to as "Snappy." Generally, those around him are viewed as a food source, either to actively kill and consume, or to provide him with what little is left of nearby corpses so he doesn't consume them. In groups, being able to have what remains after all others get what they want is enough to keep him content.

His favorite food, actually, are pickles. A handful of these items, regardless of their source, is enough to keep him happy for hours.

After the invasion of the Frogloks into his home of Grobb, the troll beastlord has vowed revenge and trains to become skilled enough to kill the frog invaders (and make lots of froglok pickles). As a result, he will never help or group with a froglok, if he can help it. However, he was uncomfortable in Neriak, since he had to resist the compulsion to try and eat the guards. He has since moved to Shar Val, home of the Vah Shir. He doesn't have the same compulsion to eat these noble felines, since he gets terrible hairballs afterwards.

My three main characters are a magician (65), Druid (60), and Shaman (51)

Human Magician (Erollisi Marr):

She's a complex and wounded soul. She inadvertantly had a hand in her mother's murder (her mother was murdered by the Freeport militia as revenge for her own slaughtering of their forces) Her first marriage ended badly and her current husband is on trial for murder and failing to heed a call for help (he's a paladin) She's distant and difficult to get close to, some would call her cold. In reality she yearns for close friendships, she just doesn't know how to let people in. She has a sense of humor but it's often used only in sarcastic ways.


Halfling Druid (Karana):

Adopted younger sister of the Human Mage. She's been spoiled her whole life and has never grown up. First her adopted mother spoiled her, then her sister did the same (out of guilt) and now she's under the impression that the whole world loves her. She hides any intelligence she has behind a wall of childish speak and innocence. She's very generous of nature and willing to go to great lengths to help people. She giggles almost constantly and has died her armor a rainbow of colors in an attempt to be "prettiful" Her relationship with her sister is one full of arguments and bickering, but in reality her deepest love is for her sister. She finds males confusing and understands little of "adult" relationships, although she finds herself having a crush on a troll beastlord (this is my favorite storyline, two very childlike characters playing out a romance. It's too cute)

Vah Shir Shaman (no god):

She is the pet of the druid. Some aspects of her feline background have left her with a rather masochistic nature. She enjoys being injured, bleeding, cannibalizing herself. She often disturbs groups greatly with her seemingly obssessive desire to be injured. Non feline customs both confuse her and intrigue her. She loves males of any species, flirting in her own way with all of them, but would never consider "mating" with anyone but another cat. Monogamous relationships are foreign to her (she's a cat) and she's completely comfortable with the idea of taking many mates and her mates taking many mates.

Roleplaying is an aspect of this game that I found rather late (my magician was 55 when I joined a roleplaying guild) but it is an aspect that has kept me playing when I would have quit. I have no roleplay background at all (prior to EQ), but being in a roleplaying guild is like being a part of an ever evolving novel. The characters in this guild have long histories and backgrounds that interweave with each other in ways that make them seem as real as any other person. This group of characters, out of nothing, has created a living world of stories and history that is truly a wonder to behold. It always amuses me when someone challenges roleplaying in EQ. I'm surrounded by some of the best roleplayers in the game every day. There isn't a single thing we can't do while staying in character. Roleplaying isn't dead or impossible in Everquest, not by any stretch of the imagination.

Nightseer is an adventurer, a seeker of treasures and amusing times -- and a nice wardrobe. With a wink of an eye, the snap of her fingers, the quick draw of a dagger from her boot, she's ready for action -- be it a hearty bar brawl, a fat purse that begs to be lightened, a bit of mischief to take an arrogant merchant down a notch or two, or taking pity on a fine lad who's in need of a good tumble. (Aren't most fine lads truly in need of a good tumble?)
A queen among thieves, she is a true follower of Fizzlethorpe Bristlebane, earnestly seeking all things fun, clever, witty, charming and mischievous. Contrary to her Dark Elf heritage, she is never truly evil or wicked in her intent, but focused on fun and adventure. She eschews the ways of the followers of Innoruuk, though he is considered the father of the Teir'Dal.
