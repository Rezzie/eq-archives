I assumed that the problems they had were just problems with their drivers, like the Verant reps said would remedy these situations. In my opinion this is something that most people WILL NOT be able to do this without the help of someone who knows what they are doing.

When the patch for Dx7 finally arrived I of course checked to see whether I would need to upgrade and found that Dx7 was already in my machine, so I assumed that when I went to play everything should work just fine. I also saw all of the persons on the boards that were complaining, and again I assumed that these people would be the exceptions, and that most people wouldn’t have problems. Of course there are the people who won’t know what they are doing when it comes to drivers, and these would be the ones you’d expect to have problems, but I never thought this would apply to me.

When I got home from work, I first logged into game to see if I would indeed need to update my drivers. I downloaded the patch in about 4 minutes (cable modem) and then went into game to see how everything worked… I was stunned, no visible graphic problems, the colors seemed brighter and the clipping plane seemed to be greatly increased… Wow, I won’t need to mess with the graphics card was all I could think. I heard there were sound problems, but my first impressions were that the graphics seemed fine..

I could hear the ambient sounds a little louder than usually, and most of them I hadn’t noticed previously so I figured these were improvements too. I also thought that my sound card drivers would be fine, seeing as there were no real problems here. Then I started playing the game, and this is when I realized there might be a problem. Ok, so I have to look for new sound drivers.

So, I logged off for the day, and decided I would find the correct drivers at work the next day.

At work the next day, I saw all of the rants and flames on the board, and most of them were people who surely wouldn’t be able to fix their systems (on their own), or whose systems would need to be upgraded before they could get back into EQ. I also spent ten minutes finding the correct drivers for my sound card, but discovered that most manufacturers don’t warranty the new drivers. Also, while looking for the drivers I had to accept a disclaimer for the generic driver from the sound card company that would work for my sound card. The disclaimer stated that any system problems that occurred as a result of the drivers was not the driver mfg’s fault, and also that the hardware company was responsible for the drivers (even though mine didn’t have the drivers available).

Now, again I assumed that this wouldn’t be a problem, because I knew what I was doing. I took the drivers home on a floppy, and then when I got home I checked to see if these drivers were updated from the ones I already had. Windows responded that it would continue using the drivers I already had, so the drivers were not actually updated. This meant I would have to deal with the sound problems, and I figured it could be a problem with the patch and maybe it was something that Verant would remedy.

I went in and removed the extra sound cards, and now was not getting any sound at all… So I restarted again…. And it detected new hardware again, and kept installing it over and over, this time I got up to 5 installed before I did a manual shut down.…

This is where it gets really interesting…!! When the computer came back up from the manual restart it began installing the sound card again, and again…. It wouldn’t stop installing the sound card. I couldn’t even get to Windows this time, and it wouldn’t let me skip files or cancel out of the installations. So now I can’t even get in to Windows, and it has installed at least 10 sound cards, and the last time I actually got into windows there was NO sound…. Basically, I’m screwed…

Here is a general purpose tip: Invest $50 or so in a copy of PowerQuest Drive Image 4.0 or Norton Ghost 2001. Any time you do something major like adding new hardware or making serious driver updates, take an image of your Windows partition. Should a serious error occur, simply boot from floppy and restore the image. Voila! Your computer is as good as new...er, as good as old, I guess?

As a side note, these can also be used to keep a "clean" Windows install image, for those of you who like to reinstall every so often. A 5-minute restore is much faster than a 30+ minute reinstallation. Also, I usually make a new image at every step in a new install on a new machine as well in case something goes wrong.

Sadly, this is the problem with an operating system thats trying to be everything to everyone. A business machine to business', a games machine to gamers, a development machine to developers etc... This is nigh on impossible to do flawlessly.

Its other problem is the fact that there is very little control over accessory and peripheral hardware. Sure, hardware can get certified by MS, but its still upto the driver company to bring their drivers inline. With the plethera of graphics and sound card manufacturers out there, theres going to be discrepencies and differences in the way they perceive and implement the standards. And the fact that they can be running on 2 different platforms (AMD and Intel) with different companies chipsets (Intel, VIA, RTech etc...) just adds to the compatibility problems.

If we had 1 platform, 1 chipset, 1 soundcard and 1 videocard then compatibility problems would be scarce if not non-existant, but then again that would be come exactly like a console system and we'd lose the flexibility and upgradeability of the PC.

Basically we don't have much choice in the computer gaming arena, MACs have been getting better and even have reasonable gaming 3D cards now but are still far behind the PC in games company support, and I definitely wouldn't throw a computer newbie or illiterate at Linux (though I do like it myself) and it also suffers from lack of PC gaming support.

All-in-all Windows is still our only real choice for PC gaming (even with its problems). Theres usually someway to get around the incompatibility problems many people have but sadly it does usually require some computer knowledge in order to fix them (or the trusty ol reinstall, hehe).

For Everquest to survive it had to upgrade to the latest version sooner or later. Microsoft can only support older DX version for so long before it becomes to bulky and inefficient (hmmm, wonder if thats Windows problem? hehe) and if Verant was caught napping then everyone would have to choose between playing EQ or playing the newer games coming out.
