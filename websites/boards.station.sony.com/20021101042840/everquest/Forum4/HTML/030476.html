There is no message at all when you swipe something. In Muta's case, they probably both just knew or assumed the other was pickpocketting as well.

However, there are a couple ways to tell, depending on how successful the rogue is at pickpocketting. If the rogue is very successful, you'll probably start seeing a string of mobs that will only drop one of each coin they were holding. Pickpocketing coins will not reduce them below 1 (since it takes half of the coin type available, rounded down). If the rogue isn't doing so well, and you're fighting mobs that talk (usually only the ones that say "I'll teach you to interfere with me, Tank_01!" when they get hit with the Taunt ability), they'll say "Stop, thief! (Rogue_02)". Otherwise, there is no indication, unless the rogue says something.

If you're going to pickpocket in a group, either be VERY careful, or make sure to speak up and ask. Often rogues will Pickpocket AND be the group's master looter so it doesn't matter (he'll just split all the loot anyway at the end of the group). This is probably the best way, since it gets you the practice you need to do your epic, and doesn't piss anyone off in the process.

As for poisons, yes, they're a one shot deal, and they go off on the first piercing attack you make (not including backstab). Sort of like a one shot proc, and they can be resisted.
