FOLLOWUP ON MY SKILL SUGGESTION FOR RANGER DEFENSE

I suggested several posts earlier that we train an ability named deftness or avoidance at lvl 39-45. I figure that one way you can make this work is that have it roll first on any attack by a mob - if the roll is successful, place a multiplyer on the following skills Dodge then parry then riposte.

By doing this, you would be able to slowly lift the deftness skill to a reasonable level - rather than having to nerf it down.

here is a possible scenario

Attack by mob

1. AC roll check (unsuccessfull)
2. Deftness check (unsuccessfull)
3. Dodge check (unsuccessful)
4. Parry Check (unsuccessful)
5. Riposte check (unsuccessful)
Mob hits you for 110 damage

Attack by mob

1. AC roll check (unsuccessfull)
2. Deftness check (successfull) (all defense skills checked at 252 skill this attack)
3. Dodge check (successful)
Mob MISSES


Using something like this woould avoid too much riposting (if that is VI's concern since both Dodge and PArry would be checkd first at 252 skill)

Advantages:

1. A relatively low skill in deftness might make all the difference in the world, ie lvl + 2(or 3)times lvl. Also the current defense caps could be maintained. Testing would quickly see what skill of Deftness/avoidance would be balancing.

2. Would not change the low lvl balance that I feel exists

3. Riposting damage would only occur less than 1/6 of the time since parry and dodge would be the more likely outcome of a successfull deft/avoid roll

4. Finally for the roll to occur, ranger would have to be below a weight cap for the skill such as 130 stones ( lower or higher maybe appropriate, since I don't have the hi end armor I can't speak to a "good " weight at which to specify).

I have heard many people say that in the normal spell progression, Rangers should pick up SOW earlier in life. This is based upon the differences in levels that Druids and Rangers get their spells.

Getting SOW earlier in life, or even a Ranger unique inferior version of SOW, would help to balance Rangers against the other hybrids, Shadow Knights and Paladins.

Paladins and Shadow Knights both get, in addition to their Spells, abilities that are unique to their class. Paladins get Lay on Hands, and, conversely, Shadow Knights get Harm Touch. Rangers of course get Tracking, but we must share that ability with Druids and Bards at higher levels. Rangers can also Dual Wield, which goes toward balance.

I think Rangers getting SOW as early as 22 or 30 could help even this out. We are supposed to be "masters of the outdoors" and this would help to cement that image. Even an inferior ability could help this.

Also, the damage that a Ranger does with a Bow and Arrow needs not only to be modified for higher level characters, but also for lower level ones. Rangers are supposed to be Masters of the Bow and Arrow, and, apart from quested bows and bows that are Ranger only, many of which aren’t available until very high levels, Rangers really do only as much damage as anyone else with a bow. The Trueshot discipline was meant to address this, I believe, but it is not available to younger Rangers, for obvious reasons.

I think that many of the people that post on these discussions will have gripes about higher level Rangers, and I am sure that many of those gripes have real merit. My contention is that there also perhaps need to be changes made at lower levels. Certainly the changes on test, if implemented, will help this, but, of course, I want to be able to SOW.

I do not have the “fuzzy math” (can’t believe I got that phrase from George Dubya), and am also a fairly young Ranger, but I just wanted to express my feelings along with everyone else.

If as early as level 23 Rangers are excluded from groups looking for tanks simply for reason of being Rangers, as has happened to me, I believe some changes need to be made.
