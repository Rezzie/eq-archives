Excellent thread.

I agree that although the uber-guilds are a part of the problem they really aren't to blame unless they exploit or cheat. Otherwise they're just a bunch of players doing the best they can.

But Verant REALLY has to start working on ways to deal with the uber-guilds while not ruining the game for everyone else.

Here's my own personal story:
Well over a year ago I had a paladin. This was before anyone had a Soulfire and LONG before anyone had the Fiery Avenger. Anyway, several paladins started working on the Soulfire quest. It was complicated and broken in many spots but they did what they could. For example, many became master brewers in pursuit of the elusive bog juice (which turned out to be really low level when the recipe became known but no one knew that at the time). I never saw any indication that any were in uber-guilds. There was certainly no one flaunting an FoH tag at the end of their name like the FoH memebers like to do.

Anyway, the quest was solved and of course someone posted the solution on the web (inevitable I suppose although I wish they hadn't). Within a day or so uber-guilds like FoH were mass producing Soulfires. They did none of the work of figuring out the quest but reaped all of the rewards. This caused Verant to immediately turn off the quest by removing a key NPC. After awhile they turned it back on with GREATLY increased spawn times of key mobs and GREATLY increased difficulty of other mobs. It pretty much knocked me out of the running for a Soulfire.

I just about quit in disgust over that and other issues. The only reason I'm still here to post this is I started an Iksar monk when Kunark came out and, with reduced expectations, am managing to have fun until Horizons is released. I specifically chose the monk because they're the melee with the least dependence on equipment. I didn't want to go through that again.

If the Soulfire episode were an isolated event it would be one thing, but it appears to not even be close to isolated.

It's possible that some people at Verant may be interested in this topic. After all, 90% of their paying customers are not in uber-builds and soon those customers will have alternatives. If the people not in uber-guilds leave then EQ (probably known inside Verant as their cash cow) will die.

The fact that an FoH member was on this board recently complaining about Verant taking down Skyshrine just before they killed the dragon indicates that maybe uber-guilds aren't getting their own way all the time anymore. However, considering past history I simply cannot feel sorry for FoH.
