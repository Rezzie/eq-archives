I've seen two arguments for giving Paladins more class-specific weapons. One I agree with. The other gives me some concern.

The first is an economic argument. Paladins having to compete for generic weapons and the basic laws of suppl and demand mean that Paladins pay higher prices for good weapons. This argument is sensible and for this reason I agree with paladins.

The other argument, that Paladins need class-specific 1 hand weapons because the generic weapons are made for dual wielders and hence aren't "powerful enough" for Paladins gives me concern.

It seems to me that if Paladins want to do more damage than the current 1hs weapons provide, then they can use a 2hs weapon. "But then they can't wield a shield, you say, and will give up a lot of AC". Yes that's right. That's the tradeoff they make. After all, isn't that basically the same argument many Paladins made to Rangers? That if we wanted to avoid aggro problems with mobs post-50 that we should cripple our class purpose of damage output by wielding inferior 2hs weapons or even [shudder] equip shields (disregarding for a moment that the 2hs tactic doesn't work)? Well applying the same argument, if Paladins want to become more offense oriented then they should not expect to be able to maintain their class purpose of being a defensive hybrid. As many Paladins derisively told Rangers, "You can't have your cake and eat it too". So pick one: Defense with shield and 1hs weapon, or offense with 2hs weapon without the shield.

So giving Paladins class-specific 1 hand weapons that are not much better (if at all) than current generic 1-hand weapons would address the valid economic arguments made and I certainly would support that. However, giving Paladins class-specific 1 hand weapons whose damage approximates that of 2hs weapons would present real class-balance issues.

Bedukil: The only part of the damage/delay formula that I am unclear on is the difference between the damage bonus as applied to 1H vs. 2H weapons. I have heard several different formulas, and Verant has stated that they are not going to confirm any more statistical information about the game, so we'll never know for sure how it factors in. The most common theory is (LVL-25/3) for 1H and (LVL-25/2 for 2H). This means that in order to even out the damage bonus at level 50, a 1H weapon must do 9.3 more points of damage than a 2H does in the same period. This does, indeed create a deficit, as the ratios of the uber-2H weapons are often equal to or even slightly higher than the uber-1H ones.

There are other things to consider in this debate, however, than simple damage delt:

First is the point that at level 25+, even a fully-stacked warrior cannot come close to dishing it out like an uninterrupted Wizard can; at this point tanks have to resign themselves to being little more than bait to keep the casters clear. An extra couple dozen points of damage per hit is neglibible compared to the Ice Comet that got interrupted because taunt didn't work and your weapon didn't recycle fast enough to draw the focus back to you from the caster.

Second, if a weapon has a good proc, a faster cycle time can seriously upgrade the damage (Skyfire on a halfling warrior with lots of dex items can easily deal an extra 500+ damage per combat from procs alone!). If this is the case take after the warriors and carry DEX enhancing items, to improve the chance of a proc.

Third, using a shield allows you to bash without having to Ghetto Bash. This is a good way to raise your attack rate and chance for an interrupt and stun (without the chance for a mis-timing and losing an attack when ghetto bashing). There are also some really nice shields out there now (albeit not as many as there probably should be), and most players will let you loot them since not many classes are interested in them due to the obsession with 2H weapons, and warriors dual wielding.

If you want to be a tank, you have to get in there and hit as fast and often as possible to keep the casters from harm. I don't know of many good tanks who give a rat's behind about how much damage they do (there's always a caster that pipes in and shuts them up); what they care about is how fast they hit, and how long they can last face to face with a formidable foe. The glory of a kill belongs to the group, and tanks make it possible for the group to survive long enough to get the kill.
