Weeeell, that's a lotta question, but hopefully I can help you a little. I'm hoping Cyranth will show up and correct my bumbling weekend attempts to help.

Necros are a class designed with Soloing in mind. For the most part, they are self sufficient, relying on themselves and their pets. Many of their buffs are self only.

They also do have many faction problems and are KOS in many places. The exception to this is probably the Dark elf necro. They are welcomed in their home city as well as (I think) Grobb and Oggok. (Don't depend on me for that one.) I believe that Gnome, Human and Erudite necros have a much harder time. Yes, many merchants will not sell to necros and they must find friendly merchants or alternate ways of aquiring the basics.

There are lots of things that a Necro can bring to a group, but many groups don't realize this. They see the necro as the loner. Darn it, they CAN solo so why don't they? Some Necros themselves also don't realize what they can bring to a group and have little grouping experience. This is not always the case, mind you.

I don't know if I've helped shed any light or not, hopefully a vet will come along and expound upon these meagre basics. I know there are some good resources out there and you may want to check out some of the player guides at eq.castersrealm.com.

Yep, faction issues. Even where they aren't killed on sight by the guards, the merchants might not sell to them until they've done some faction work (killing monsters the merchants hate or doing quests for allies of the merchants). I recommend everquest.allakhazam.com for faction info (under the bestiary section).

Have you been reading GenDis again? Ignore the over-level-50 multi-group-raid issues for now. It will take a *very* long time for you to get there, and by the time you do, the game could have changed a lot (it sure has since I started playing in May!).

I've never seen a necro turned away from a group, even when the members didn't realize all they could do. Of course, everyone knows they have fear, snare, dots, and a pet. Did you know they get a ghetto heal, that transfers some of their hp to someone else? They're an exception to the rule of int casters being a good single partner for pure melees. Did you know they get a ghetto clarity, for transferring mana to someone else? And a self-only damage shield! I usually think of only magicians, druids, and rangers as having damage shields. And how about that single-target mez? Of course enchanters need to make a hotkey asking everyone to use /assist and attack a single target so that they can save damage to the group by mezzing adds, but I didn't know necros could do that until I was over level 30. Since I don't really have any interest in playing the class myself I haven't researched it at all, so I'll probably be learning new things about it as long as I play the game. Really, I think the main reason why necros rarely group at the lower levels is because they don't have to, and pickup groups can be a pain under level <fill-in-the-blank-with-the-nearest-multiple-of-five-under-whatever-their-highest-character-is> since everyone is still learning how to play their class properly.
