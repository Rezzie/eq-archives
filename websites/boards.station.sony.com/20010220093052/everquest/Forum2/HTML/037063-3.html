First of all, there's a big difference between American culture and the rest of the world, and it has a lot to do with both our Puritanical founding and our seperation of Church and State, which actually seems to foster Evangelism.

Europeans (and likely Aussies and NZers as well, but never having been there I can't say for sure) are bound to think this whole thread is ridiculous. Why would anyone get banned for this? And, indeed, why would it occur to anyone to do it at all?

But we Americans love our taboos. And while I will agree with the parent who has taught their 3-year-old to say "penis" instead of using silly baby-talk, there is a matter of context. A child saying "my penis is caught in my zipper" is communicating with their mother. A teacher telling the child "let me see your penis" is doing something quite illegal and probably very harmful.

And someone shouting (and I got the impression it was repeatedly... not sure why) the word to the zone completely out of context is clearly just doing it to bother people. There is no other reason.

For those who said that it was "bad parenting" to let kids even play EQ in response to the idea that the person who complained was probably a concerned parent whose child was online in the zone: it is in my opinion bad parenting to allow your child to get input in any form, whether movies, tv, or the internet, in an environment where the parent is not accessible for review and discussion. It is also bad parenting, in my opinion, to try to block your children from these sources of input to avoid having to review them. That which is forbidden becomes attractive.

Which brings me to a final comment on the morality debate regarding prostitution. The origins of prohibitions on prostitution are rooted deep in the American Puritanical history. Prostitution is not illegal because it is untaxable; it is untaxable only because it is illegal. Where it is legal, permits cost money and income is taxed just like always. Neither is it really because of disease; with an underground, there is no regulation, and disease is rampant, but properly regulated legal prostitution can drastically reduce the spread of disease. It is illegal now because sex is "wrong" and violence is much preferred as a method of expression (see our ridiculous gun "control" laws).

But there is an issue about legalizing prostitution. Say a woman is beautiful, but destitute. Let's paint a tragic picture of someone who was widowed early in life with two small children. Her husband had a good income and she didn't go to college or work, but now the life insurance won't pay off the house and she has no income and no skills... only two children to raise.

So she goes to the EDD and they tell her, "ah, but you're in luck... a woman with your beauty can easily find work in a brothel! You're healthy and pretty, you can make good money for many years in that trade." Is this right? As wrong as it is to tell people they can't or shouldn't have sex, is it right to tell them they must?

Generally I feel that vice laws are tools for keeping poor people poor and in prison, but it's hard for me to get over this little hump. The above scenario is little better than rape. Most of us know that few people love their job; should anyone *have* to go into this line of work?
