It would be nice if Wizards and Druids "the masters of travel" had the following:

1. The ability to have a secondary bind point. It would be nice to be able to teleport someone and return without losing your "home" bind point.

There are numerous times when someone needs a lift out but no porters will take them due to the fact that they will lose their bind point or bind in an unsafe location. You can find this occuring at almost any "event" but lets use PoF as an example. For some reason someone has to leave before a breakin is completed (maybe an angry mother forcing one off the computer, or maybe he has had 4 or 5 deaths too many) and needs a lift.

One of 3 things will happen:

a) A Porter will have to bind on the "unsafe" hill and chance LOADING back into trouble after a death - not really a nice option for someone that is trying to help out another player.
b) A Porter will gate the person out and have to chance another run through the portal - which can lead to trains and/or deaths - again not really a nice ending for a helpfull player.
c) The character is forced to camp in hopes that the area will be clear to zone into at a later date so that he may make his escape - which is not a valid option at long camps like Efreeti


2. The ability to teleport someone to your bind point. It would be nice to be able to gate out and grab a friend that just comes online and have him join you at a camp.

For Example - wandering around Kedge and a friend gets on that would like to join. He happens to be a Tank currently in NRo. He will need a port and then have a nice run to kedge - at which point we have to backtrack to zone to grab him, or leave him to feed the fishes.

If the first suggestion was implemented I would suggest that the second spell only allow you to port others to your "home" bind point and that the primary and secondary bind points not be allowed in the same zone. This will place the caster at risk to MDS (multiple death syndrome upon LOADING) which should help discourage and/or eliminate abuse.

If the time for the these spells is not now then maybe they can sneak themseleves into the expansion ...


I would like to see Wizards become - not more powerfull - but more "group friendly."

I ran a MUD for years, and "Mages" had the ability to establish multiple "portal points". The drawback to the portals was a certain failure rate -- which could sometimes be deadly. Here's how it worked. The Mage would cast a bindpoint on an object or character (PC or NPC) to mark that object as a destination. Obviously agressive mobs didn't like this. As far as objects, if a PC picked up the object, there's no telling where that portal would end up. Also, if the PC/NPC dies the bindpoint is gone.

The rough part of my implementation was that there was a certain percentage of failure. If the spell failed you could end up in the wrong zone, and the bindpoint would be erased. The spell should be given to wizards starting at the bind level (level 12 for wizards) to teach them early that it's not to be abused -- similar to Evac spells later.

If you happened to fail and were tossed in the middle of Unrest, for example, you'd certainly die if you were <30, as could the person you were toting along with you. Of course there was also the risk of leaving someone behind. Once that bind point was used, it is erased.

Just an idea from one fantasy game coder to another.

"Safe"?

The wizard class is already one of the most powerful classes, that's why I play them almost exclusively. I enjoy the freedom. There are drawbacks, of course. It's hard to solo, for example.

I think putting in a dangerous failure in such a powerful set of spells is a fair risk for that kind of mobility. I'm not talking about extreme failure rates. It's really up to Verant anyway -- this is all just wishing. But I'm pretty sure that Verant doesn't want some class that can go anywhere at any time without some sort of disadvantage to discourage the abuses that accompany this extra power.

You know how people play EQ. MOST do not adventure at all. MOST camp a place and stay there for 4 to 5 levels. MOST would use this spell to maintain a camp. You throw in a deadly risk -- they'll think twice after falling in front of a mob equal to or 1 to 2 levels above them. In other words, the failure rates I'm talking about merely discourage the frequent and repetative use of the spell set.

People want everything to be easy, and safe. Well, the fantasy world in EQ is already extremely "safe". I have managed to get a wizard to level 30 in 7 days (approx 150 hours). This, in spite of the resist caps, and the supposed "limited ability to travel".

I don't like how root or enstill work. None of the level 20 spells are useful, period. Now I could complain about this to Verant and cry about how "unfair" it is. But I know better. Wizards are NOT tanks, if they want to take on creatures that require a tank, they had better group. If they choose not to do that, the risk is death and XP loss.

The point of all that is to make clear that there is nothing "weak" about the class already. So having a fair failure rate -- with it's dangers -- is not unfair to the wizard class.

Glad everything is not easy as pie -- where's the fun without the risk?
