I think that any GvE teaming will have to be limited to being based on race. I dont think god or class can be taken into account. One example used in a lot of posts is a gnome necro, so let's stick with that one. True if it was a strict GvE then the necro should be on the evil team. However, without drastically changing the whole game structure the gnome can only be created in Akanon. This means that you have a character having to be in a place where the majority of people around him are on another team. As he gets known, he becomes a target a sort of "here is someone I can kill without having to go far to do it". Ultimately you will probably not have gnome necro even chosen as an option to play, and applying this to all the class/god combinations that would end up being KOS to most people in their home towns (necros, shadow knights from any "good race" for example) you will cut down on the diversity and choices people feel they can make in what to play.

GvE might be a good idea, but it needs to be GvE based on race only, no one should end up being killable by the people they have to share a home with, everyone needs some place in the world that they can feel at least a bit safe in. If you could change it so necros have a choice of evil cities to start in regardless of race that could work, same with diety. Unfortunately I cant see Verant making that type of change, so the idea of Neutral, or team based on god or team based on profession, would be more damaging than helpful.

There has the potential to be very exploitable. Many people are making this a balance of power issue. On Tallon Zek the teams have subsided into a Light vs. Dark environment. The players have come to this on their own because the balance of power for over 9 months laid with the dark races (team orange). The server is not simply about numbers but about equipment and levels as well. For a very long time team light has fought to overcome the force and are now starting to gain ground. Since this natural shift of power has happened some very few vocal members are consistently petitioning the GM's to try and change the gaming code to benefit team orange.

Tallon Zek is a very political server that has shown the ability to adapt to any number of situations. The community has worked very hard to prevent light vs. dark X-Teaming, as time goes on there are more people who wish for a 4 team environment. The community has a way of adapting and working through balances of power. Alliances are formed and broken on a weekly basis, this is what makes the game very interesting and extremely fun.

Out of range healing has been on the server since day 1. It very rarely becomes an issue of harrasment but when it does the GMs do a wonderful job of dealing with the situation. Healing lower levels on your team promotes team unity and should not be impacted. Would a knight let a squire be killed while he is standing there? Preventing +8/-8 healing and buffing would be a VERY BAD IDEA. We have learned to deal with difficult situations, when faced with an OOR healer we simply call in for reinforcements...it is very gratifying to overcome these odds and an important part of the game.

1) NO: *VERY* Bad idea which will take away the political aspect of the game which is a big reason why many people still play.

2) No: *VERY* Bad idea which will hurt the teamwork of a single team.

3) No: Code should not control the server environment, the population should.

4) G vs. E is an idea, but one that would need careful consideration as characters and classes were formed with the current server environment in place. Also the balance of power would greatly benefit the Evil and not the Good team. This arrangement should be about benefiting both sides...team orange has been in power for 9 months, it would be wrong to simply squash what team light has just begun to accomplish. Again putting code in to prevent helping others would be a bad idea - the server community has the ability to keep this under control. We have done it for over a year on TZ.

Comparing the same-team healer and out-of-range healer problems on TZ:

Out-of-Range Healer:
----------------------
1) Very common.
2) EXTEREMELY unbalancing when (as is usually the case) the healer is many levels higher than the combatants.
3) Can be fixed without negtively impacting non-exploitive healing, simply by preventing healing only in PvP situations.

Same-Team Healer
-----------------
1) Very rare on TZ.
2) Unless the healer is much higher level (which would be prevented by fixing the OOR healer exploit, so is irrelevant), the situation is much less unbalancing, since the healer will often not be able to heal beyond the capacity of the combatants to overcome.
3) Fixing this with Abashi's proposed solution will have a major negative impact on non-exploitive healing, eg a dwarf healing humans fighting a group of trolls.

For these reasons, out-of-range healers seem to be a much bigger priority in terms of code-changes.

My suggestion would be
A) to use Abashi's suggestion to prevent OOR healing in PvP, though perhaps making someone ineligible when attacking a PC, not when attacked.
B) Address same-team healers by imposing faction hits when a PvP healing leads to the death of the opponent.

However, the faction hit should apply ONLY if the person killed was attacked by the healers "friends", not in the reverse case. The reson I say this is that some members of single-race guilds on TZ have stated their intent to ruin the faction of their opponents.
