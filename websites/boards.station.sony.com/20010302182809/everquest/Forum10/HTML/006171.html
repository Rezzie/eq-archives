Except for my Testament of Vanear, a Polished Bone Bracelet and Hand Woven Leggings, the rest of my equipment is bought. And I don't have a problem with it, in Real Life (tm) I didn't tailor my trousers myself neither. Did you?

The real reason for that is mudflation, the supply of items is continually increasing, so prices drop. This corresponds to a deflation in Real Life (tm) economic terms.

Take for example my neckpiece. A Blackened Iron Medaillon. It drops in the lowest level of Runnyeye. While I could go there and get one myself, I couldn't do it alone, being a level 30 druid. It would take me a lot of effort to get a group of other level 30's together to go to Runnyeye, which is usually deserted due to the Evil Eyes. And if I got such a group together, we would have to camp a room in that dungeon for hours, before I would win a roll for a BIM. On the other hand, I can buy the BIM in East Commons for 40 PP. And I can make those 40 PP easily in less than one hour by teleporting.

Again, it is just like real world economics. It is less effort to earn enough money to buy a pullover, than it would take to shear a sheep, spin the wool and knit the pullover myself. If we wanted less items traded, we would have to make more items NO DROP. And the NO DROP items should be slightly better than the dropable ones, when coming of a mob of the same level.
