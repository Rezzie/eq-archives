Item recast delays were tried before. They failed, because players could simply drop the item on the ground and pick it back up again. Items in everquest are simply a number. There is no extra space for recording when the last time it was used.

However, no drop items solve the dropping problem. This brings up problem two: logging out will reset the item. This happens because there is no space in the character record for the item recast data, it can only be held in memory, so when a player camps, that data is lost.

For a short recast item, this works fine, i.e. the BP could have a 1 minute recast delay, because it takes that long to camp and log back in. However, for it to be well balanced it would need a longer delay.

So, here's the way to do recast delays, without any painful character record expansion:

When a recast item is used, change the item to another equivalent item, similar to the way charges are handled. Have the memory only data keep track of how long that item has been there. When it's been there 3 minutes (or whatever the recast is), change the item back to the original item, at which point it can be used again.

If a player camps, the timer is reset, meaning the player may camp at 2.5 minutes, then have another 3 minutes to wait when they log in, but that's better than being able to exploit the item by camping after each use. The flaw in the system then hurts the player, instead of helping him, so there is no incentive for the player to exploit this bug.

For items that have a very long recast, there could be several versions of it. For example, for an item with a 60 minute recast, use version 1 and it changes to version 10. Every 6 minutes it changes to the previous version, version 9, then version 8, then version 7, down to eventually version 1, when it can be used again. This way if someone goes LD at the 59 minute mark, they lose only the last 5 minutes, and not 59.

Abashi

>>>Items that were impossible to balance?

Yeah, we've pretty much agreed all the way around that high HP heal items are a bad idea. These items were in fact created by a guy who plays a high-50s cleric in an "uberguild" and even he didn't realise the impact.

In any case, while I personally agree that 1 minute more or less makes the effect useless, these items are such that there's not really a middle ground. Usually somewhere between "Overpowered" and "Useless" there's "Balanced". I don't think there is in this case given our current limitations.

>>>Why not make the recast time to 3 minutes but leave the cast time alone?

That might be a good option, but unfortunately we can't put recast times on items. It's one of the things that we want to do at some point, but it's not a small undertaking. There's so many things that have to be taken into account to enable recast times. To liken it to spells, having to wait the full recast time when memorizing a spell is one such feature that was necessary in order to get them working there. Getting them working right on items would require much the same type of constraints. We have to account for what happens if you remove the item then put it back on.

>>>Please call Absor, wake his ass up, and ask him to respond to this thread.

He's usually playing EQ about now In any case, in all fairness he wasn't involved with the decision up until the tail end, where his concern became just getting the word out rather than by everyone getting 'surprised' when they heard the reports from Test over the weekend.

Anyway, I'm not going to try defending the items or the fact that they were put in game in the first place. They were a bad idea. It unfortunately didn't become appparent just how bad until a significant number of people had them. As for just stopping the drops, versus swaping and nerfing the old ones, we didn't want a situation to arise that would make the clerics without those BPs "useless" as sometimes happens. Given the outcry over the announcement, and the fact that people are saying that clerics are useless because of the change to these items, I think our worry was a fair one.

Anyway, as far as the healing hate stuff on Test goes, here's what's going in:

1. The healing-hate cap is being reduced. (less hate max per spell)
2. The calculation of hate is moving a ratio of less than 1:1. In other words, currently if you heal 1000 (assuming the target is over 51), you gain 1000 hate. After the change you'll gain less than 1000 hate.
3. We're fixing a problem with the temporary hate from sitting. Sitting was causing an extraordinary amount of hate making it nigh on impossible to med when in combat.
4. Heal spells assign hate on what we call an "assisted" basis. In other words, any NPC that sees you cast a beneficial spell on their enemy (someone on their hate list) will add hate since they saw you doing that. They do this whether or not they are the primary target. This is why in multiple-attacker scenarios *everything* jumps the cleric as soon as he heals. This is also why everything that's mezed jumps the cleric as soon as mez breaks. We're fixing it so that critters that are mezed or stunned only add enough hate to prevent exploits of mez and stun, rather than the full load they do now when they see you cast a beneficial spell on their enemy.
5. Along the same lines, we're fixing that problem whereby hate lists would propagate across zones. Everyone has been sitting in the dread lands between combat when something ran from half way across the zone to slap your cleric, right? Basically the same thing.

That's basically it for what's on test at the moment.

-Gordon
