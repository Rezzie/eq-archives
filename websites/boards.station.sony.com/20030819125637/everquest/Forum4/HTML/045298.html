One of the problems with putting higher ZEM's in some of the older zones is the relative strength of the mobs.

The bandits in Paludal hit pretty hard for level 16-18 mobs, even harder if you're not in twink gear or at least newbie armor. Compared to the NRo derv's or orcs, they're extremely tough mobs. If they were to increase the ZEM, the mobs would be very unbalanced in terms of risk vs. reward.

One of the problems is that they have increased the power level of the average player over the years, but they haven't gone back to the older zones and done so with the mobs and loot. Ok, so I'm stating the obvious there. The second problem is that, rather than retune old content to be more in line with the player's power, they came out with Luclin.

Luclin did a couple of things that had notable impact on the way the game was played to that point. First, it added the bazaar, though that was a little late after release, which made all that droppable high end equipment accessible to everyone to a greater or lesser extent. Again, this is stating the obvious.

What was not so obvious at the time was, while Luclin did add content for all levels of play, the lower level content, up to about level 50, was pretty much spoon-fed content. Pre-luclin, we had zones with very wide levels of mobs, somewhat isolated content, and rare camps and spawns that were in competition from level 20 on up. Once Luclin arrived, all the low level content it introduced was conveniently located in a nice local, linear arrangement that everyone could find, with no rare high level wanderers to speak of. It eliminated the outside risks from hunting, and made camps very controlable with minimum effort. You no longer had to worry about a kodiak wandering across your group while you were pulling the orc camp, for example. Instead, the bandits just stood around and respawned in their nice predictable patterns, with almost no danger of outside interference. Once you broke the spawn, you simply wouldn't wipe unless you screwed up badly. The kodiak was never going to come by and break up your monotony.

Granted, the majority of players have always flocked to the most efficient hunting spots, but Luclin reduced leveling to a simple matter of putting in enough time in one place, then moving on to the next.

Hopefully the revamps continue, and the zones regain some of their balance and challenge. However, EQ has become a Monty Haul world. You old D&Ders know what I mean. The DM has let the power of the players far outstrip the basic rules of the game, and he is now finding it difficult to come up with new ways to challenge his players. This will only worsen if the power trend continues to increase, i.e. beter gear and higher levels, more powerful AA's, etc. To fix this, the power level needs to stay consistent, but the DM needs to retune the entire world to compensate, while finding new and innovative ways to challenge and keep the interest of the players.

Sorry for the length of this semi-rant, it's just been something I've been thinking about for a while.

the problem has nothing to do with not "merging."

The problem is that a lot of old game content is either too boring, too little experience, too few mobs or a combination of all of those things. Due to this, people no longer are eager to run to East commons to see if the orc camps are free. Instead they might prefer to go and group in Hollowshade where plenty of stuff is free.

Because of that, west karana will always be empty while Paldul Caverns will always be filled. If you combined 6 servers, West Karana would still always be empty and PC would always be UBER FULL with people yelling and scratching eyes out and throwing hissy fits.

Until enough old zones are rebuilt that all zones are equally attractive at a particular level, with similar mob density and xp reward, this will never change. It would not matter if you gave West Karana a 30 percent zone modifier, it would still bite the big one due to the mobs that are there and how small the density is. You just don't have very many camps/mobs for many people in ONE GIANT BUTT zone.

In a nutshell the problem is two fold:

1) It will always be more appealing to SOE to spend limited resources making NEW zones they can SELL in a new expansion. Call them an evil empire if you want, but it is really just simple good business.

2) It will, of course, always be more appealing for players to go to newer zones with higher mob density.

For some reason SOE seems stuck on zones they have already upgraded once, which bombed in a big way, such as Droga and Nurga. I don't think anything very different will happen in these zones if you change them again. The biggest problems they have is they are actually a tad bit difficult to find unless you have LOY currently and a clearly marked map. The only dungeon I could think of which was originally of a similar level, while even being more insanely hard to find, was Dalnir.

Why they continue to blow resources to try and save those lost areas instead of doing something amazing with an area as large as West karana, for a much wider level range, is beyond me. Hopefully after this one they will give it up.

The server doesn't need to be merged when 2,000 people are on it in the "best" zones during prime time, but your renegade happens to notice that East commons or West Karana is empty. It needs to be merged when only 1,000 people are on it and several other servers during prime time. Only SOE really knows when that is happening now.
