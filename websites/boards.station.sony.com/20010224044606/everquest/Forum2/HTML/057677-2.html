Powerleveling can have some side effects, like class skill left behind or not knowing how to group, but these can be improved fairly easily.

In my opinion, the most important aspect of powerleveling is finding out if you'll enjoy that class when the powerleveling is finished.
Take away all the nifty extra spells, teleports, and sows because when the work is done there's no second person around and you should enjoy the class you've chosen.
Otherwise you just like getting powerleveled all the time more than you like your class -- which is nice but either you have two computers and it's tiring, or someone else is helping you and it's tiring for them.

The difficult part is that you kind of don't know how a class will be like until after level 20 or so.

The following are my observations on what i've tried.
- A bard to 27 and it's a very fun class but I found out I don't like getting easily mauled and only being able to sing and run.
- A cleric to 31 but I got tons of begs, got tired of buffing others, always medding, hard to duel with, can't solo.
- A druid and got so many begs for powerleveling, sows, buffs, tp (and i was anonymous!). If you do actually superbuff any newbies, you'll never get rid of em cause they'll send tells when you log in. I never want to play a druid.
- An enchanter to 12. Interesting class, can't really solo enough for me. Could die fast if in melee. I made it my tradeskill character.
- A necromancer and can do some amazing things with those pets and kiting. Being too dependent on a strong pet makes it tough without one. Could die fast in melee. Spectre pet is overrated for how long it takes to get to level 59 (I just hang out in Qeynos-Bertox shrine to see some). Their value at raids is controversial.
- A warrior to 14. Dual wield nice. Tanking nice. Healing is unbearably slow. It was nice to powerlevel but after that "the magic's gone". (Started a Gnome Warrior for Clockwork armor, but after seeing it I don't want to be one anymore)
- Currently working on a Shadowknight. So far it's the only class i've gotten to a semi-decent level and haven't had regrets or been disappointed from expectations. In fact, their abilities seem better than expected with each new level. I think i'll continue to enjoy playing one even when I don't feel like leveling. (The tank-mage concept from Ultima Online has always been my favorite)

It's taken much trial and error to find all the things I don't want to be. Hopefully I will be happy with the class i've chosen. There can be only one.
