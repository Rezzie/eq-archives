I'll try and keep this short...

Iceclad Ocean: WAYYYYYY too empty.

Thurgadin: A breath of fresh air - I'd like to see the npc "animation" go further and in more zones. It adds dimension to "the storyline".

Icewell: We have to take two groups down here, and people rapidly stop wanting to go because balancing exp can be difficult. A reasonably nice zone tho. The bear is a novel challenge

Tower of Frozen Shadows: Whilst this was a terrific zone to explore, it seemed a bit unrewarding, although a plentiful supply of pet weapons is on hand. A bit like Najena, it felt very boss-mob focused rather than dungeony. Maybe we just didn't see the rare drops, and many of the mobs seem to be all or nothing droppers.

Great Divide: Not hunted here much, but it appears to have settled in to the usual "find a safe spot and camp from it". NPC pathing could use a review so that encounters of multiple mobs are rarest if you move around and make it the "hunting" zone it appears to have been intended to be.

Eastern Wastes: Superb zone. Ry'gorr armor seems to be fairly well placed, but the other armors, e.g. ulthork, seem to be twink material as obtaining it is generally just beyond the people who would probably most want it. Also the Ulthork stats are a little... Odd... Who would want to wear -35hp armor?

Kael: One tough dungeon. The "giant scalemail" armor is a bit of a mystery to me. You're lucky if merchants will pay 1pp for it. Getting the quest material is multi-group hunting stuff. Our two groups got in "comfortably deep" and were fighting lvl 55 and 56 mobs. Again, balancing exp was difficult because in a raid of 12 you only really have 6 'juggleable' slots (3 taken up in each group by cleric, chanter, evacer), and we didn't want to start telling people "you can't come you will unbalance things!" Seems to be a bit small, we got torn enchanted silk. Other than that we didn't really come back with much.

Captain Bvellos drops interesting stuff that has monk-like stats but is WAR only. Our monk cried when he saw the stats and the WAR only attribute =)

Velketor's Lair: Not hunted here, just explored. Boy o boy o boy, what a superbly designed zone. Great work. Why haven't I hunted there? All reports have been of atrocious loot and horrible resists.

Wakening Lands: An interesting, underused zone. Mainly underused because people have bad CoV faction. Also because of the faction-while-zoneing bug that is killing lots of people who try to get into the zone via portage. Loot? Is there any here?

Skyshrine: What an awesome zone! And although it is the biggest non-ocean zone in the world (people think the courtyard is deep; I have to laugh at that, they aren't even out of the basement, rotflac [lac = laughing and crying]). Unfortunately many of the quests are broken; the baker & cook quest (they give you the quest but not the items needed for it), the sentry emil quests (items aren't available), the mercenary assignments quest and giant toes quest (neither kcor or emil will respond to being given the items). Also, yelinak faction is a little *too* hard to come by given these broken quests.

Cobalt Scar: A nice zone, actually. Not sure what will happen when general populations finally reach there and learn more about it. I've seen no loot drop of worth or mention. The quests are a little "iffy", and it's a long haul to have rewards more random than the sentry kale ones. Only two real issues with this zone; the siren's undercon somewhat. They are rather on the tough side. Other issue; fighting bulthar's is a bit of a tight squeeze, especially with the shellfish collector that regularly wanders up and steals kills. We lost probably one in 3 kills to him, until we got a cleric to start rooting & atoning him, but even then we had problems.

Plane of Growth: Only explored this one. Looks very impressive. After a year of trying for Imbrued armor in hate, I'm about ready to blow tunare and go up there and hunt... Everyone is telling me great bard armor rots up there almost every hunt, and after over 305 logged (probably over 500 hours total) hours in hate and singing steel being more suited to warriors than bards...

Beyond? I don't think ordinary players like myself or my guild are intended to be able to pass the rather firm barrier that is Siren's Grotto. Most players seem to consider Velious the realm of the uberguild. Personally my feeling is that Siren's Grotto is the beginning of that.

I haven't tried to venture beyond Cobalt Scar, tho, primarily because the nearest cleric is usually nearer thurgadin, and I don't fancy paying 1kpp for a cleric to haul himself all that way for a rez...

This is the opinion of one Albrion Hammerfell. lvl 45 necro. Spent lvls 28-45 almost EXCLUSIVELY in velious. so here goes. As a former 50+ cleric since day one of retail EQ, I have played Kunark and Norrath prime for almost 2 years, so I can judge these zones based on those.

Here goes...

********ICECLAD OCEAN*********

**INTENDED LVLS: Good for lvl 30-40ish

**RISK vs REWARD: 6 (rating 1-10)
Sparse loot but decent, Cougar Claws too common now, and are saturating the market. Should likely be more rare given the tremendous amount of cougars around. As for XP, Some mobs are decent xp, while others are not, no huge variance of note though. Melees get real antsy about fighting here because the bind location is very far away and dangerous for one who cannot self invis, so caster classes are the only ones who tend to xp here with any frequency.

**FUN FACTOR: 4 (rating 1-10)
Gets old fast, not much to do here but pull to coast and fight there. Not a lot of people choose to fight here for very long because of this. The teleport isle is a deathtrap of aggro roamers, so noone hunts around there, kind of a waste of space. Might have been different if you could have your back to a wall or a lake, but no such luck so people tend to run to the bridge as fast as you can. The "ubermob" of the zone is the turtle, and some people have already pointed out the problems with that mob. There is an awful lot of wasted water space here, and an interesting far off island might have made this zone, and the ubermob more interesting, rather than a KS fest.
**OVERALL: Decent zone, nothing special.


********TOWER OF FROZEN SHADOW*********
**RISK/REWARD: 2 (rating 1-10)
This is another Kaesora, a waste of a great dungeon design because of the risk/reward. Most players of appropriate lvl have come here once, or never. Mostly because they come in to see blue conning mobs at lvl 40, yet these mobs hit hard and fast well beyond their con. Even greens hit pose a risk here. The dungeon layout is great for a dungeon romp, but the risk in this place is just to great for a 6 man party of appropriate lvls. Hence, people rarely come to this zone for xp, as it is VERY HIGH risk for the lvl of the mobs, yet XP seems no better than your average dungeon. Like Kaesora, this zone usually has several lvl 50s farming the good item drops at the top lvls. Thats all. Also, this is very dangerous for a Melee to die in, as the bind point is so far, and with a dungeon with keys, the general rule of thumb is... dont die. For players who "should" be coming here at he lvl intended, this place is : Major danger, little reward.
**FUN FACTOR: potential 9, but currently 2
This dungeon has the potential to be a really great zone. It has a story, it has different lvls, and a real sense of achievement. The problem is, it is a keyed dungeon, and that produces prohibitive risk for the lvl that the mobs were intended for. Most people who have done this dungeon have had to fight there way through greens to get the keys, and were suffieciently overconned that to them, there was little or no risk involved. Only then, was it worth the risk reward, which was.. no risk, high reward. For appropriate lvl parties, it is High risk, low reward.
**Overall: I came here first with a lvl 58 mage who was farming for key/items. The next four times I came I was 38, 39, 42, and 44. At none of those times was the risk/reward worth it. The mobs hit so hard compared to the mobs just outside that I find it hard to reccomend to my permagroup to come here. Sad really cuz its a really cool zone.


********EASTERN WASTES*********
**RISK/REWARD: 7
Very good zone for XP and grouping. This zone allows full well for both grouping and solo play. The orc camp is a great gathering place for people, and it is usually easy to find a group there. People pull to bridge area when hunting regular mobs, and the Oracle was a well known solo or duo xp permacamp for lvl 55+ players. The walrusses and Uthorks are good risk/reward in both loot and xp for thier lvls, and are fun to fight, but... like everywhere else, the common method is to pull to the waterline. This gives the obvious advantage of not having wanderer adds, at the very least, from only one side. Wandering mobs are all over this place so it makes the center areas devoid of players. The southern part of this zone has no players because it had no camps or varied mobs of note. The Giant fort here has the occaisional group, but mainly giants are done for faction or soloed by necros/druids. Giants give very poor xp for the time/risk involved in killing them. It used to be at least that Giants at least gave good treasure to compensate for having double or triple the hps of a similar conning mob. But without the rewards, people tend to do giants only for faction and quest pieces. At the lvl they con here, its just much easier to find other mobs to xp on. Again, this is too bad, since Giants are very fun to kill in a group. Increasing their lvls such that they are still blue to a lvl 53 will help things a lot here.
**FUN FACTOR: 7
This is a fun zone overall. The mobs are nice and varied, the xp is generally good. The risk is decent but not overly so. The Loot is decent but not too great. This zone has a lot going for it, but like most others, it could have been much better if the south part was more interesting. Adding a few more smallish Orc outposts would have really juiced up this zone a lot. A helluva lot. Also... where is the Ubermob of this zone? Usually, every zone like this has a really high con rare spawn Ubermob to add spice to the zone. There is no such mob here. This zone would have been much cooler if a named large griffon spawned here to add a bit of excitement. Again, not a fault, but could have been better.

********CRYSTAL CAVERNS*********
RISK/REWARD: 10
This zone is PERFECT. The risk reward here is great. Rygor Oracle cloaks are very rare booby prizes. XP is not great per mob, but the mobs are more frequent and are not overly difficult which gives the appearance of danger, and creates a lot of excitement. The random spawns of some of the named mobs makes this zone difficult for high lvl players to roam through and Farm. Beautifully done in that respect. Not a single item here is "overpowered" for the difficulty involved in getting them, but they are great for their intended lvl. The fact that there is a hidden dwarven city down below adds so much spice to this place. Too bad Melees cannot bind in this little city. Kind of strange that they cant.
**FUN:
If you were fortunate enough to be of low enough lvl to experience this place for xp, you know full well that this is likely the best and funnest dungeon crawl to date designed by Verant Interactive. Whoever it was who designed and tuned this place should get a raise. Beautifully done on all counts. The ubermob (spider queen) is the only mob constantly farmed by high lvl players, but that is hard to avoid. At the very least, they made it difficult enough for those high lvl farmers so that they usually cannot do it alone. I would have liked to see more quests obtainable in the dwarven city there, but because of faction issues for the lvls required, most vendors and NPCs were non-responsive.
**Overall: This zone puts a lot of faith into the idea that Verant can really get things right sometimes. Too bad there arent other zones like it in Velious. Velkinor's could have been like this. It could have, but its not, for many reasons. I bet the same guy who designed CCaverns designed Velkinor's but it was someone else who screwed up the tuning there. Anyway. Congrats on such a fine piece of work. Im just glad i got to experience this zone while it was blue. This zone in itself made me very happy that I returned to Everquest after 7 months away. Cheers.


********EASTERN WASTES*********
RISK/REWARD: 8
People XP here. They do it because Tizmaks are great XP and are fun. They do it because Kodiacs are great XP and fun. They do it because Wurms are AWEFUL XP but fun and (used to) drop good loot. Wurms suffer from the same problem that some Giant camps have. They take a long time to kill, and present a decent amount of risk, yet provide for Very Low group xp. Anyone in the group who is looking for XP will be sorely dissapointed. You can make 4 or 5 times as much XP on bears as you can on Wurms. Risk vs. Reward is kind of out of wack there. The Wurms also used to drop other items with much more frequency. It is common for a group to spend all day on worms and leave with nothing to show for it. That would be ok if the xp and pp was decent, but it is not. Not at all. People will still hunt them with frequency, as its the only place to get some of the dropped lower lvl spells, but it does not have to be laborious.
Oh. And one more thing. The rate at which ICETOOTH CLAWS come into the world is way too high. There is no way that an 11 29 1hslash weapon should be selling for 200pp now since the market is so flooded. This is the cause of some major low lvl mudflation.
The Tizmaks are good constant XP for their lvls, though it is very dissapointing that despite the world builder's effort in creating such a large and diverse cave system for them... they populated the whole caves with the same 4 or 5 tizmak mobs. It would have been much greater had they created a "benefit" to exploring within. Noone goes deep in outside of the one area with the named. Why go inside when you can just as easily pull to the outside? This is a waste, and if it were retuned to harder mobs as it gets deeper, players of higher lvl would camp and explore deeper inside. As of now, the inside caves remains a ghost town, and for obvious reasons.
**FUN
This zone is really kind of fun. The geography layout is interesting. There are plently of places to set up shop, and pull to as well. Loads of landmarks to keep you from getting too dissoriented. Many many caves which free the options to form new groups or join ones in progress. Very well thought out zone. Kind of surprised that there was no scary Ubermob randomly roaming around here. Remember the Griffon in West Commons? Remember the roaming Giants in East Commons? That one random Sand Giant in south part of North Ro? The Mino Hero in Steamfont? Most larger zones have a form of ubermobs to keep players in check and to raise anxiety and fun level. This one does not. If there was, lets say, an Abominable Giant, (a white colored Larger version of a Gaint) then people would have to keep a lookout for him and hence there would be a greater sense of danger and fun in every general camp.
**Overall:
This zone is a very well made zone that could have been a little better, but is very good nonetheless. No major issues.

********VELKINOR'S LAB*********
RISK/REWARD: 3
What a great dungeon design. As a lvl 45 necro, I only xped on the inside entrance area to this place. Everything was blue to me, but they were green to my lvl 53 SK friend. Only thing is... they might as well have been high blue, because they hit for well over 100 damage and to my surprise, had ZERO for loot and provided miniscule xp. I really tried on several occasions to get my group to come here to explore or XP, but when risk/reward was evaluated after an hour or so on several tries, the group just realized it was not worth the time or effort. Sad. Really sad. I explored much of the front area by myself, using feign and invis. Some random spiders seem to see invis, which is ok, but the problem is, the density of the mobs here makes it Train central. Also, DMF for some reason doesnt seem to work here. Kind of strange that my best buff is somehow negated in what seems like a normal zone. There are FAR too many spiders at the entrance. Less density of the mobs and higher lvl and xp would make this place a viable xp area and grind. But... the way it currently stands. NOONE is ever here unless its an uberguild raid. Seriously take a look at retuning this zone. The risk/reward for the intended lvls is NIL, so at any given time there is NOONE in the zone. Welcome to Chardok II. At least in Chardok, people get decent xp for the entrance mobs. But those mobs are lvl 50 and hit for the same damage as some of these lvl 40 mobs. A real mistake.
FUN:
In exploring this place as much as I could, it seems clear that this dungeon would KICK BUTT as far as dungeon romps and fun place is concerned. The layout is very interesting and there are plenty of places to pull to and explore. The only thing is... why? Why would anyone in their right mind fight hordes of underconning low xp no loot mobs? Well. The answer is... noone does.
OVERALL: A broken zone. Nice layout, great design. Poorly tuned. No risk/reward unless you multigroup raid to get to the ubermobs.

********KAEL*********
RISK/REWARD: 4
Did some grinding here. A lot of people enjoy killing giants as they also give great Thurgadin faction. The problem is, they have higher hps than other similarly conned mobs so therefor they should net much more xp as the kill per minute ratio is much lower for a full group. Well. They dont. XP does seem better inside here than it was on outside giants, but over the course of 4 hours full group grind, xp was less than to be expected, and loot was very low considering the risk involved.Giants should drop spells and gems a little more often and perhaps the random rich giant drops 21pp or so. Its just unrealiztic to look 8gold pieces off a lvl 42 frost giant, no matter what the economomy tells you. And as for XP, boost it up a bit. People WILL grind here more often and have a better time if they get decent XP on these mobs. They dont though when compared to other mobs elsewhere. There is nowhere to run here, its an indoor zone with giants all over the place, so risk is higher than outdoor mobs. But XP seems the same. People will notice this over time.
FUN: The simple fact is... people like long fights. They like Big adversaries. It is why we play the game. It is much more fun to kill a huge giant than it is to kill for instance a similarly lvled frog. People inherently like the battle. The challenge. The risk. The whole drama of it. But.. when its all said and done, people will learn to avoid places where risk/reward is to their disadvantage. That is the case here, though not nearly as drastic as the Wurms of Great Divide.
OVERALL: A strange hybrid of dungeon and town, I have a hard time thinking what to make of this zone. Very cool dungeon design, but there seem to be a select few areas to pull to when fighting, otherwise it becomes a deathtrap. IF Everquest allowed for 12 person groups, exploring might be more of an option here, but a six man group is relegated to pulling the same old spawns to a safe spot. Strange tuning here. It makes me wonder what they had in mind for this zone in the first place.


I havent spent as much time in Western Wastes and Skyshrine to give it a judgement, but ill say this.

********WESTERN WASTES*********
A good zone which allows for some serious raid possibilites. Loads of dragons and many new mobs to hunt. For me, at lvl 40-45 I grinded on some Sentient Armors, Tigerraptors, Pixie thingies, Panthers, and flying apes right out of wizard of oz.
The loot was good, and the xp was also quite good. Seems like a zone I will be spending quite a bit of time in. The density of the trees in the northern areas presents a very large risk since you have to pull to the wall if you want controlled fighting.

********SKYSHRINE*********
Severely nerfed drop rates on the armor have caused the grind rate here to slow considerably. The mobs here seem to hit hard, and xp seems very slow but given the previous drop rate of Sentient and Sentry items it was sometimes worth the risk. That seems to be no longer the case, and single group grinding has become much more difficult to accomplish at the intended lvl now.

.....

So there is a brief synopsis of my experiences so far in Velious. Overall I have to say I am extremely pleased with the expansion. I am even more surprised by the fact that there are no HUGE bugs and pathing errors. I have yet to see people exploiting pathing and quests with great frequency, and the speed at which verant developed this expansion is nothing short of remarkable.

PS: Plane of Growth is a joke, and is called by all the guilds PLANE OF GIMP as it seems to be designed to be farmed. This makes the whole high lvl risk/reward ratio very sad indeed, and diminishes the accomplishments of those longtime players who tried so hard to earn their Trakanon and Venril Sathir BP and Leggings. Also. The fact that there is no Break-In is really quite pathetic. This plane is Farm Central and will and has thrown the risk reward ratio into a spin.

I'll comment on the Velious zones I've hunted in, from the perspective of a level 36 - 39 enchanter (levels achieved while in Velious).

Iceclad Ocean:

The cougar hunting next to the gnome huts was very good. The occasional drops were great, not overpowering and even useful to some characters of the level that were hunting there. Decent solo, decent in groups. I did both charm soloing and full grouping.

The gnoll camp is great, I love how the gnolls freak out when the turtle comes by... one thing though... They let the turtle kill them one by one. If the whole camp rushed the turtle they could take it for sure, but they all stand their scared out of their boots until the turtle finally gets close enough to the "boss gnoll" that he aggros. The gnolls should really team up when the turtle comes by.

The boat is pretty slow, anyone with sow and levitate skips it because the boat doesn't need to zone.

Tower of Frozen Shadow

Great design! I love the portals... no huge trains to the entrance! Thus far I've been to level 4, grouping all the way. I wouldn't want to solo here.

The first floor is a bit frustrating because for any group that just wants to move to the next level it requires camping a load of greens for the keyholder to spawn. We spent over an hour on the first floor with no keyholder.

It also isn't a viable area to hunt in because if you're low enough to get exp off the skeletons (the majority of mobs in the 1st level are skeles) that means the rest of the mobs on the floor will wipe you out easily.

The second floor, library... As far as atmosphere goes you guys couldn't have done better! Sounds, mobs, voices, *shivers*.

Again my complaint would be the rarity of the keyholder, Angry Librarian. We camped this zone for 2 hours without seeing her once. She does drop some very useful items (ie. homework, I love it because it fits in range slot) If that's why she doesn't spawn very often then I'd suggest lowering the drop rate of her items and increasing her spawn.

3rd and 4th floors: Was in a full group for these... where they already had the keys so I can't comment on keyholders. Again the level 4th keyholder was a big yawn. Was there for over 2 hours and never saw it. Again if you're concerned about their items then make them rarer drops but increase spawn times.

East Wastes. Great both from a solo and group perspective. Some drops are useful, others are nice enough to pass on to newbies at least. (like the walrus tooth and uthgor armor)

That pretty much concludes my Velious adventures.
