Some points to be made.

In game prices are based on rarity. The farmer actually helps keep prices low. When everyone has gone home, the farmer comes in to kill spawns that would get left alone otherwise (farmers don't like wasting time in a crowded dungeon where they can't farm multiple items, it just isn't as profitable). So because of the farmer there are more of the given item in existance, hence more competition to sell that item and thus the price gets driven down. Think about it, which items will be offered cheaper, those with 3 farmers and one camper offering, or if the camper has the auction zone to himself?

If the farming drys up, supply will decrease I expect by a third (the 8 hours normal people sleep). Thus we can all expect prices to rise. Good for the camper who finally got his rare drop, bad maybe for the rest who want to buy stuff.

That said , I don't think farming will decrease in the near future, since people develop a play style, and habit will cause most farmers to continue doing what they are doing even if they have no reason to do it now.

As to the ebay auctions ,who can say? I think it is sad that, when we finally have proof positive that virtual communities have real worth and value to people (they'll pay money to make their character better), Verant is trying to snuff that out. I guess they feel threatened; imagine if your character was considered at the same level as a family pet - and you could sue Verant for the emotional trauma from an accidently deletion of it. Verant wants it to be "just a game" and not recognise or respect the community and attachment people have to their characters.
On the flip side I suppose a lot of people were getting ripped off and that needed to stop. People need to be protected from their own stupid decisions.

Yes I'm a farmer, so I can afford all the phat loot I'm too low level to get.
Yes I respect people's camps.
Yes I'll give you a deal on the item I'm auctioning (because I have 8 more in my backpack I need to move).
And yes I'll still be farming.
