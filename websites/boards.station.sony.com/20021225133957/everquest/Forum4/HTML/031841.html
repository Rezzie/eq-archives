You can pick up the Basic EQ game and 2 expansions all in one -- EverQuest Trilogy -- which contains the original EQ, Velious and Kunark for about $30US at any Best Buy, Circut City, Electronics Botique, etc.

This would probably be the best starter kit....

As for Luclin, I personally think it is a MUST have just for the Bazaar alone (Another $30US). The Bazaar has a great search menu that allows you to see ALL available items for sale and can sorted but Race, Class, Inventory Slot, price, etc.

You'll quickly find that buying new equipment and stuff is MUCH faster then trying to go out and hunt for it yourself, as well as most higher level weapons and armor is cheaper buying from other players then from buying from NPC Merchants.

Plus, if I'm not mistaken, it was Luclin that introduced the Transport Spires. These allow you to "warp" up to the moon (Luclin) and then back down to other areas in the world much faster then you could run to them.

These 2 factors make Luclin a --must have--.

As for PoP, the latest expansion, it makes world travel a sinch. Every starting town and some other areas in game now have an immediate port via a stone book pedistal. Simply click on the pedistal and you are warped up to the Planes of Knowledge (PoK) where you can then find the appropriate portal stone to just about anywhere in the world, or darn close to it. Travel in-game has never been so easy.

So in conclusion:

Start with EQ Trilogy
Add Luclin for the Bazaar and Spire Portals
Add PoP for even faster ports and more in-game content.

All told, about $90US to get started, but trust me, you'll thank yourself later for having everything up front rather then piece-mealing it later.

If you are a first time player, get SoL first. This, apart from the Bazaar, contains some of the best low to mid level experience zones in the game, which will make finding groups a lot easier.

Then get PoP. The travel times are well worth it.

Then get RoK. Granted, you can not do your epic without Kunark, but there is a minimum level to even start the epic quest, and to complete it you will need the full support of a medium sized guild anyway, so there is no rush there. It has some decent hunting too, but is largely outdated with the advent of later expansions. Nice to have, but not nearly as useful to a starting player as the first two.

Finally get SoV. With the new interface, the only selling point that expansion had for low level players has been lost, leaving only high the high level content it provides. You can do without it for quite some time, perhaps even skipping it completely if you just move on to PoP instead of ToV.
