About Alchemy being "cheap" relative to its payoff potential--

Do remember what a cash cow smithing used to be, until the twinkization filtered down and the availability of bronze killed the market. I payed 25pp for my set of banded sleeves, and didn't feel at all ripped off. Now its hard for me to sell my own homemade banded for anything more than 1 or 2pp above the "1pp per AC" that everybody wants.

Getting back to Alchemy, it will probably have similar competition problems. Once more people become Shamans to make money on Alchemy, the prices will tumble.

Its terribly unfortunate, but since the EQ economy is a little messed up (too much cash flow/equipment flow in, not enough going out), Alchemy's profitability is doomed to go the way of the other trade skills.

It is interesting to see all the varying results folks have had with trade skills. It is also a real lesson in economics to see how the old price/demand equation works even in Everquest!

My personal experience has been VERY good with trade skills. At levels 4-10 I was a tailor and made my own armor (patchwork) and also sold to other newbies ... not enough to make an real money but I suppled myself with good weapons (for a newbie) with the meager profits.

I recent months I have taken up fletching ... not to make money to to supply my ranger with ammo. Well, lo and behold ... once a decided to build my own Trueshot Longbow (so had to get a skill level around 120) I discovered that other rangers would pay a LOT for a Trueshot. Sure, it cost me about 700 pp to get to level 150 but I have made/sold about 25 bows since then. At between 200 and 300 pp each that adds up FAST!

I do regret the time I missed leveling up (it very time consuming gathering all the NO DROP materials) but I now have a nice bank account, very nice weapons (purchased) and a full set of Ivy Ethed Armor (almost all purchased). So, what I lost in leveling I made up for in equipment and now I hope that I can level without getting killed so much (hehe).

I guess that what you get out of trade skills is in direct proportion to what you put in (as well as the skill you choose and a little luck).
