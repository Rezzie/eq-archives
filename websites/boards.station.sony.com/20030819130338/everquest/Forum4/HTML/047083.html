The "Lich" series that people keep referring to are the spells that transfer HP-to-mana. Usually in a 3-for-2 ratio. This is why HP becomes important to the necro, more HP = more time you can safely keep the transfer spell going.

Additionally, the Iksar necro innate regen may only be 1 HP/tick extra to start out with, but even that little bit is very noticable. The experience penalty for the race notwithstanding, I'd give the race choice here serious consideration.

One of the most dangerous things you will ever see in this game is a level 60+ iksar necro buffed with virtue and any form of shaman/druid regen spell... while holding Zlandicar's Heart.

Max hit points only matter if you go from full to 0.

Max mana only matters if you go from full to empty.

The key is that in a HARD fight, you may med up to full power, heal to full health, then fight until you are OOM and very low health.

But how often will that happen?

If, for you, the answer is (almost never), then neither the size of your mana pool, nor the size of your HP pool, matter.

What really matters are your mana regen, and your HP regen. This determines what your sustained DPS is, which largely determines how much of the time you spend fighting, and how much resting.

For moi,
Fighting = fun.
Resting = boredom.
