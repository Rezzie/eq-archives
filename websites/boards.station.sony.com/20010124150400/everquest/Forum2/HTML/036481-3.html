I know we're way down on page 3 already, so probably a lot of people will already be in "skim mode", but here goes....

A lot of people here put their main complaint as "I'm level [51-60], but they changed my name!" Probably a lot of those people have names like "Star Wars" or "Peter Jennings" or "Every Onelovesme". Well, just from looking at the first name - "Star" or "Peter" or "Every" - there's no reason to suspect that any of those names need changed. And when you do a /who - yes, even when guides and GMs do a /who - all you get is the first name.

So you create your character, work your way up to 20th, with the name "Karma", for example. Nobody thinks anything is wrong with it, and nobody suspects anything. Ding, 20th level, and you immediately type "/surname Chameleon". Well, the guides and GMs don't (can't) hear about it until they see you running around somewhere or someone /petitions it.

Back in the day, only GMs and Senior Guides had the ability to grant surnames. Some of you old-timers (though I figure you're all playing the game instead of reading the boards) remember the waiting - one week, two weeks, perhaps longer - for your name to come up on the surname lists, which were often dozens of names long. Then they invented the /surname command, and put trust in the players (and gave them the benefit of the doubt) in order to eliminate one of the worst sources of customer dissatisfaction.

Name changes are done with care and discretion, and (except for the really obvious ones) often with the consultation of other guides and GMs who are online at the time. They're not taken lightly, because even though this is one of the rules intended to keep EQ from degenerating into UO or AC, it tends to greatly upset the player receiving a name change.

In fact, the broken hearts of the people whose names I had to change were one reason I retired from the Guide Program.

Take care, all,

From the Everquest License Agreement, part 6:

"...any player activity whatsoever which is, in our sole discretion, inappropriate and/or in violation of the spirit of the Game as set forth in the player rules of conduct, which are posted at www.everquest.com."

The Naming Policy is a part of the Rules of Conduct.

Now granted, perhaps the name policy should be a bit more prominent (and I like the idea of putting a line or two about it in the character generation screen.) But it's at least referred to in the EULA, and you have no one to blame but yourself if you didn't bother to read the EULA before you hit 'I Agree'.

And to those who say that the naming policy is stupid because EQ isn't a roleplaying game, I have to disagree. EQ is very much a roleplaying game. We can have a long discussion about what roleplaying is, but suffice it to say that it's up to the players to roleplay, not Verant. Verant can help to foster a roleplaying environment, and while I feel that they could do much more, the naming policies are one way they do try to foster it.

And one quick thing to those who feel that people who petition names are 'Nazis'. When I see a name that I feel is against the policy, I may or may not petition it, depending on my mood. *But* it's up to the server guides and GMs to change it. The policies are there, but the people enforcing them are human. That's why there are some inconsistencies.

Also, the length of time which a name has been in violation doesn't (and imho shouldn't) matter. Someone in the first post made a reference to stealing from the company, and was told that it didn't pertain. It certainly does. Another reference would be like telling the cop 'But I've been speeding on this stretch of road for 2 months!' as he's writing your ticket. If your name is in violation (as decided by the GMs) it's in violation, whether you've played for 2 weeks or 2 years.

Not all name changes are good. Not everyone will agree on every single name change. But the policy itself is necessary. Look over the thread and you'll see that we all have different opinions on how to enforce name changes, if at all. Verant has taken their stance with their policy.. the rules they have are their opinion on names.
