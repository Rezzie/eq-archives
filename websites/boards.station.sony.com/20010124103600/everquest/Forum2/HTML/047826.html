I know this problem has been around for a while, and I don't expect it to be addressed any time soon, but I figure I'd give Verant a heads up for EQ2 if they are not already aware.

The problem occurs when a spell/buff (let's use SoW for instance, doesn't matter though) that is already on you starts to go on the blink (ie wear off). If you try to recast SoW to rebuff yourself, depending on the timing, you may get the normal message "You feel the Spirit of the Wolf enter you" followed shortly by "The Spirit of the Wolf leaves you". The mana it takes to cast the buff is spent, but you must recast it to have it stick again.

I never considered it a big deal at all until I played a bard. My bad luck only occured once in a blue moon as a caster/priest, but as a bard, I am constantly refreshing songs that are about to wear off. When twisting, you usually have a cycle that you play in order to keep all the buffs up, so it can be annoying to see the message that your buff stuck followed by the message that it wore off, and don't want to let the other buffs die just to get the first one off again.

Anyway, I know its a minor problem, and I'm sure it has something to do with server/client lag. I think it's just a matter of the server not receiving the message that your buff is refreshed before it sends the message that your buff has worn off. So, just wondered if you guys at Verant can stick that on the long list of problems to fix. Thanks =)
