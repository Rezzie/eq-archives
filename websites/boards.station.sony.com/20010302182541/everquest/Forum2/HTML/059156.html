First, the one glaring problem with the class echoed by shaman after shaman was that stat buffs are, statistically insignificant in real world play, placebos at best, that a level 29 shaman haste buff (quickness) will increase the performance of a level 49+ tank more significantly than every level 50+ shaman stat buff combined. Dramatically more. This is a fundamental problem to a class that seems to be built around the buffing/debuffing role. Second, speaking of roles.. please set aside your pride and personal prejudice about the class for a moment and listen to what people who play the shaman class say without hesitation is their priamary role. You said we were buffers first, you were wrong, we were quick to point that out. Over and over. Shaman are preeminently debuffers. A shaman's job is centered around one thing. Turgur's insects. Add Malo on raids, but still, our focus in actual play is primarily on debuffing what our groups fight. Stat buffs are nice to keep up, even if they are mostly placebo, but they are a secondary focus, our dedication is to our debuffs/hexes/curses whatever you will call them.

I think your "list" indicates you neither read the replies to the original threads in detail, nor listened to what you read.
