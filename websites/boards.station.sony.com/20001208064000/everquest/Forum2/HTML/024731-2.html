As a rogue of 41 levels, being cast on while sneaking / scouting / running corpse retrieval is a constant danger.

Heals, buffs and group-active bard songs always break hide. This doesn't bother me anymore particularly, because as most high-level rogues have, I have the habit of saying "sneaking / hiding / scouting! Don't cast on me!" whenever it's imperative that hide doesn't fail. I even have a hotkey for it. It's an inconvenience at high levels though for group spells.

The only dangerous aspect - and unavoidable one - about that particular set of spells is the ability of it to break hide when I am NOT cast upon; for example if someone casts sow on me but I already have sow. The hide breaks even though the spell doesn't take hold. Or, if I am out of range, hide may drop all the same as though I am being cast upon.

There is another range of spells which poses far more threat: AE's. Enchanters AE stun breaks hide whether I am grouped or not. Bards and wizards AE active spells also break hide. Generally the message "so-and-so tries to cast a spell on you but you are protected!" is shown, but all the same you're suddenly very visible.

This happened to me doing CR through the bugs area for the members of an Efreeti raid the other day; as a fairly low level for that particular area, and myself being naked - my own corpse was in Efreeti also - to have my sneak broken in the Deathbeetle room by an enchanters AE stun was the cause of a near-death experience. Having an LDC chewing on my naked wood-elf rogue rear was highly unpleasant and I don't recommend it... especially seeing as those spiders are scary enough when clothed. They mis-con too, being "ready to attack" even to a hidden rogue though they do not see through hide.

Not a huge priority for me to have this fixed. The bard's ability to break ALL invises is one that needs more urgent attention... and as I have a bard I can assure you it disturbs me a lot to have people's possible deaths on my hands due to an unwanted affect of my already difficult-to-use abilities.
