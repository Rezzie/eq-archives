Well, my idea goes along with the need to implement the possibility to enchant weapons. I was brainstorming, and came up with this: Make it where a magic weapon that doesn't either already have an effect or proc able to be enchanted via Necromancer (for a proc enchanment say Disease Blade) by a Wizard (an enchantment that adds damage to the weapon's strike, say Flame Blade # of fire damage each time weapon hits, resistable based on heat) and an Enchanter (Increase damage by one, make magic, put a minor stat buff on an item, decrease delay slightly, ect). Magicians could come into this with the ability to summon up weapons, and an enchanter could make the summon permanent for the purposes of magic a magic weapon (expensive ingredient for permanent spell of course. Also another idea for permanent would have to be used while putting enchantments on item or it would have either limited charges or fade after awhile, or also be a target for a higher level dispel magic) Enchanters would have to put a spell on a weapon allowing it to be enchanted in the 1st place, would have to hold weapon in hand while casting (so no No Drop weapons) where 1 of the 3 spells could be placed on it, say a lvl 39 spell. At level 49 another slot could be placed on the weapon, allowing another type (no 2 procs or 2 buffs/damage boosters) to be placed on the weapon, would have to be made permanent for each individual addition or the new one will overwrite old one (With the expansion coming out, an above 50 spell could be placed where the weapon could have 3 enchantments). Well, can't think up anything more on this right now, just that in order to put spells on would take an immense casting time of over 1 minute even, and immense mana. If anyone has ideas/comments please keep adding to this!
