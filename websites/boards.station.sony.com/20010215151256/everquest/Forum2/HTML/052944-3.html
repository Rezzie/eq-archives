What is needed here is logic, not a quick reaction that may prove as unsatisfactory as the first one.

Logic:

Is the BP overpowered for it's ability to reduce downtime? No, a drop from Trakanon is rare enough, let anyone who can get it have a decent reduction in downtime. It's not even that decent because I can cast my own CH on the tank and a couple Sup Heals for any other characters that need it in less than 30 seconds, and with clarity/bard song I regen the mana quickly enough it's not really missed. Not a big deal.

So, the overpowering is the ability to chain cast in combat to keep a fighter alive, as Absor stated.

We don't need to weaken the BP, we simply need to prevent its use in chain casting.

Absor's proposed solution does this, but costs a buff. Suppose the BP overwrites Aegis (450 mana) or Bulwark (350 mana). The benefits of this spell are lost for 7.5 minutes. Without this spell, the warrior will take more damage than he would with this spell. That means that for 7.5 minutes, our tank takes extra damage and we have to cast our own CH more often to keep him alive.

Result: We burn one extra CH because of this extra damage. That means we might as well have just cast our own CH in the first place, then cast one less throughout the fight. In other words, it makes the BP useless. Worse than useless because now we have to recast Bulwark or Aegis (whichever Verant decided to overwrite), thus wasting 350 or 450 mana. CH costs 400 mana to cast, so if I know I have to recast a buff for about the same cost of a CH, I might as well cast CH in the first place and keep the buff. Otherwise we will have less mana because we use the BP. I guess I'll dust off my chest plate of constant and put it back on.

No, logically, this solution does not work.

Other solutions that might be viable:

1. Up the casting time to 45 seconds? How many cleric/warrior combos out there that are high enough level to get this BP are fighting blue (or higher) mobs that can't kill the warrior in 45 seconds? Answer: None, or very nearly none. We won't like the extra 15 seconds, but it's better than the mini-buff idea.

2. Make the BP buff equivalent to the buff it overwrites. If you overwrite Aegis with a tiny worthless buff, we won't use the BP. If you make the BP cast a 7.5-minute Aegis that overwrites the existing Aegis, it may be workable. This may be difficult, because some users of this BP are casting weaker buffs than others are.

3. Fix the silly code to implement recast times on items. I write software and I still can't figure out why you have an item that casts a real spell (available to spell casters) but does so in a different way that removes a standard feature of spell caster casting...

4. Simply increase the aggro effect of using the BP. If clerics learn that using the BP in a battle will aggro the mob onto the cleric, who will then subsequently die because the tank can't taunt it off fast enough, you will have regulated the BP back to a downtime reducer. I would be 10x happier with this solution than with the proposed 7.5-minute mini-buff.

5. Change the heal to a Sup Heal, make it cast in 10 seconds, and then up the stats/saves on it to justify the fact that it drops from Trakanon. This will also turn it into a downtime reducer.

I haven't had the time to read all 11 pages on this thread (it's not my job; I hope Absor or his assistants are able to read them all) so there may be more workable solutions out there.

Simply put, the proposed solution repairs the damage to the game at the cost of making this item worthless. If you're going to destroy the validity of the item, simply take it out of the game or remake it into something we can use (see point #5 above).

Please consider my suggestions, particularly number 4 or 5. Either of these suggestions will meet your needs in reducing the power of the item with a very simple code fix. Easy to program, easy to implement, and leaves us with a BP we will possibly use.

Please, I hope you read this far. I hope this logic appeals to you.

Thank you for your time.
