The second sentence is commonly stated by pro-evolutionists, but it is a misunderstanding of what that law means. It's trivial to gain order in one area at the expense of overall order (which is to say, on a universal scale.) Your air conditioning works in this way. So, too, does evolution.

Anyone, creationist or biologist, who studies evolution without studying computer scientist issues of neural networks, gradient descent, simulated annealing, etc. just won't have a proper grasp on what's going on. You wouldn't, but that's not how evolution works. It is NOT pure random combinations of molecules that produces life. The toughest part of life forming is the part about self-replication. After that, randomness plays little (see above re: simulated annealing) in continued evolution. But with trillions of molecules in a drop of water, and uncounted drops on uncounted planets, with hellishly long periods of time to wait, yes, it will happen eventually. Probably many, many times over, in fact. And there's good evidence that it may only NEED to happen once, with sizeable impacts capable of knocking intact molecules, or even one-celled organisms, off one world onto another while embedded inside cracks on rocks.
