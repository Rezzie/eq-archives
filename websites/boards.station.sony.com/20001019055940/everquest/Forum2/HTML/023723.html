While I've never heard of a 2 second pause being insufficient, I have seen this effect before and imagine that it has something to do with the way prediction is implemented. The client and server do not send position data constantly while you are running, but instead update position data either at a regular interval, or when something significant changes. Basically, the server thinks you are still moving even though from your point of view you are stopped.

The trick here is to be sure that the server receives the "I stopped" packet before it gets the "I started casting" packet. The best way around this I've found is to turn slightly upon stopping. I always had a much easier time of getting my spells off once I started doing that.

Station Member 		posted 10-14-2000 08:46 AM           
Hmmm.. for those suggesting the client can be "fixed" to "force" the packets through, think about it...

The client already does that.. it sends out the "STOP" packet when we hit the arrow to stop. The reason we have a short delay between when we stop and when we can cast is because our screens show us stopping instantly, but the server will take a split second to process that packet and change our "status" as no longer moving.

It's all like a sort of dance. Our client draws us moving smoothly and reacting instantly, so that in moments of packet loss and lag we do not "jerk" all over the place. To compensate for the difference in what our client is showing us, and what the servers actually have us doing, the server uses predictions. This is why you can move around freely for quite a while even after you go link dead. But when you log back in, your position is where the server "predicted" you would be. Not where you thought you were last, usually. Hehe, many a time I went link dead on an island then logged back in and found myself a half mile from shore

They can't allow the client to have control over "predictions" on casting, because someone could then easily make a hack that allowed for such cheats as casting while running, or running before a cast is done, or even never being interupted in a cast. All this has to be controlled by the server.

Your best bet is to use the trick Abashi mentioned, and to also make sure your channeling skill is always maxed, and try and boost your DEX stats, as that increases your chances of recovering an interrupted cast.

I believe "velocity" is stored client side, and the client only sends positions to the server. So even if the "start casting" packet is sent with a new position packet just before, if the previous position packet gave a different position, it would still think you were moving in between. What it would need to do is send 2 position packets, to show a velocity of 0. However this would not take into account properly if the character IS still moving when trying to cast.

The reason the turn, sit/stand, duck/unduck work are exactly for that reason. At each point in the turn, and when you sit, stand, duck and unduck, it sends a position packet, stating the change between the two points is 0, showing no movement and allowing you to cast.

The only thing we can do is the hack really, turn, sit/stand or duck/unduck.
