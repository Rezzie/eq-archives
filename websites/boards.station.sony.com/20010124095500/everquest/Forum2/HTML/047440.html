(I decided to repost my post from yesterday, as many good ideas were presented at the bottom of the thread)

The original indended function of the /roleplay command was so that roleplayers (a minority these days) may have an easier time finding one another.
Unfortunately the /roleplay command has a function that powergamers appreciate. This function allows them to be anonymous but still display their guildtag.

Hence, /roleplay has ceased to be ANY kind of indicator about wether someone is actually interested in roleplaying their character.

(This is especially prevalent on PVP servers where going anonymous is a precaution to avoid PKs, but you still want your guildtag to be visible)

I hope you will agree that this should be fixed.

There are a couple of ways to go about the change.

If you are commited to greatly enhancing the customization of /anonymous, then this would be the way to go:

Create a separate command for making each element of your character anonymous. None of these commands would change the color of your character's name.

/anon all - works as now.
/anon none - turns off all anon flags
/anon level
/anon class
/anon zone
/anon race
/anon guild - block or unblock each. Say you wanted to block everything but your guild. This would also hide your guildtag.

/anon all
/anon guild

Would turn off everything and then turn your guild back on.

/anon level class race
Would turn off those three things, but leave your zone and guild showing (wonderful for when guildmates are looking for someone to group with).

When you use /roleplay it would change the color of your name as well as add a <ROLEPLAYING> flag to your name in the /who list. Once roleplaying you could institute the other commands to decide how anonymous you wish to be.

(optional: add a /who all roleplaying *drool*
***

If you would only be interested in an easy fix, the very simplest method of restoring the proper function of /roleplay would be to implement a /guildtag toggle. /guildtag on /guildtag off. That way someone could have their guildtag while being anonymous. Eventually, the /roleplay command would be restored to its intended function.

Whatchya think?


Thanks,
Inkantra
