"When something is rooted, won't it attack the closest enemy? If this is the case, root said mob and have the casters back away....s this an option, or doesn't it work like this?" -- Syft

Yes, if you root a mob it will turn on the target closest to it. So sometimes rooting will have the desired result. The problem is that the root will break as soon as the mob is nuked (or 99.9% of the time it will), and it will proceed to summon and/or pound on whoever rooted it (i.e., root itself is a taunt). In addition, it's usually preferable to snare mobs rather than root them (to prevent the mob from running without worrying about a nuke breaking your root).

Obviously, people have come up with ways to deal with the fact that warrior taunt is pretty useless at high levels. But that's little comfort to the warrior who's standing there watching his enchanter be slaughtered because he can't taunt the mob off, etc etc. Groups have to be flexible, and taunt shouldn't be adjusted so much that it works all the time, but clearly an adjustment is needed to bring it back to some level of usefullness.
