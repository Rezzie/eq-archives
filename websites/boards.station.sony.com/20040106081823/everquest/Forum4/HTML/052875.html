Getting summoned is generally triggered by the same rules that can cause a mob to run to you and attack. But there is an exception or two to this.

Snaring a mob generates agro, but won't get you summoned as long as you don't do any damage. So, as long as you don't damage him, you can snare a mob that summons and he will run after you but not summon. I can't say if this is true for slow.

Sending in a pet and sitting too early can get you summoned. Non summoning mobs will agro on you because of this, but you might not notice because the pet usually will obtain agro before the creature gets to you, but a summon is instant and so more dangerous.

Snaring or debuffing and then sitting MIGHT get you summoned. I can't say for sure because I have never had a reason nor the time to sit down in this case
