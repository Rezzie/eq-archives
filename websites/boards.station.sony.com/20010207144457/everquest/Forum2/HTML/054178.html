Um, that's so totally against the EULA, I can't believed you asked...

You may want to check out UO if that's the kind of action you want.

But, if you're interested in making it a little easier, *without* violating the licensing agreement through use of third party software:

- find out the recycle time on the skill you want to practice.

- create a social of this format:
/pause <y>, /doability <x>
/pause <y>, /doability <x>
/pause <y>, /doability <x>
/pause <y>, /doability <x>
/doability <x>

Where <y> is the time, in 10ths of seconds, that it takes the ability to recycle (add 2 or 3 for good measure) and <x> is the number of the slot that the ability is in (Combat abilities are slots 7-10).

That will give you 5 attempts at a shot, with one keypress.

Huh? Don't quite understand...

You can call spell gems with
/cast #
where # is the position, 1-8, of the spell.

And abilities with
/doability #
Where # is the number of the ability.

You can also use any other commands available in the chat bar in these macros, such as /attack, /con, /sit off, /book, /book 15, etc.

What is it that you want to call?

Also, note that in using the pause command, you can put it on the same line as another command with a comma in between, but

- /pause must be the *first* command listed, and
- /pause will be executed *after* the other command.

That's why the macro I gave you doesn't have a pause on the last line; you don't need one at the end.

As for calling other socials, no, you can't. You still have to come back to the keyboard and hit the button every minute or two. Unattended macroing is strictly prohibited.

But you must have missed or not understood what I said about the combat buttons. You have 6 ability slots: 1-6. Then four combat ability slots: 7-10. /doability 10 activates the skill in the lower right-hand corner of the combat screen.

When you're not group puller, just practice feigning until the mob gets there. Practice while you're waiting for a group. Practice while you're talking to your guild. You'll get it up to max quickly, and then will get your max with each level just in normal use.

Malodium,

No, /doability refers to your list of abilities. Not to your hotkeys. You know, you have your main panel (sit/stand, camp, etc.) and your combat panel and your social panel and your abilities panel.

I have maxed my sense heading at 200. I put it on all my hotkeys.

Assist: line one is /sit off, line two is /assist, line three is message that I am assisting, line four is /loc, line five is /doability 1(sense heading)

Meditate: line one is /book 1 (after 35 became /sit on), line two is message that I am meditating, line three is /loc, line four is /doability 1(sense heading)

Heal: line one is /sit off, line two is /cast 1, line three is message that I am healing, line four is /loc, line five is /doability 1(sense heading)

Help: Assist: line one is /yell, line two is message that I am being attacked and need help, line three is /loc, line four is /doability 1(sense heading)

Nuke: Assist: line one is /sit off, line two is /cast 8, line three is message that I am nuking target, line four is /loc, line five is /doability 1(sense heading)

I have more, but you get the idea.

I include sense heading in all these buttons because it takes no time (I do it after I cast my spell so no extra delay; it senses heading while the cast meter is still on the screen).

I include /loc in all these buttons so that if I die, I can easily scroll up and find out where my corpse is. Or if I gate and need to return, I know where. Etc. (much more useful at lower levels where I was exploring new zoned more often).

It only took a few minutes to make up a handful of useful buttons like this.
