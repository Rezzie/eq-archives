Yep no one tells you the downside of the zone.

For levels 12 - 23 or so that zone rocks but there are difficulties involved.

From 12 - 16 or so you will need to get by the Recondites and Yelloweyes right near the shadowhaven zone, past all the Patog Fiends and get to one of the Bug and Mushroom halls. Will need a map of the zone and the knowledge of where these 12 - 16 critters hang out.

From 16 - 23 (for me at least) you will be doing the Plerg and Patog Fiends and possible the nice Outcast Shiknar in the northern halls. This requires a class that can cast cure disease as the Fiends have a very nasty disease that they freely transmute. But small groups do very well on these guys as long as one of them can cure disease.

Many switch to (or start with) the Recondites. Two camps of these, one right at the Shadowhaven zone and one further to the north of that same cavern area. Typically they are 18 - 25 or so groups trying to tackle these folks. There are always more groups looking to hunt them than there are Recondites so fights as squabbles abound.

The Recondites also hang out in and around buildings so it is very difficult to pull just one. So there are also loads of Trains between the Recondite camps and the Shadowhaven zone leading to even more fighting and squabbling.

It is a great zone but you have to be prepared for it and for my money stay away from the Recondites.

I have taken many characters through there and still get a chuckle out of merily killing the Fiends and other critters that abound in there while the other 40 characters in zone are fighting over the two Recondite Camps.

As a Necro at 24, I can reccomend either fear-kiting Aviaks in South Karana (fairly easy to kill even when yellow cons, reasonable XP, good loot) or killing Needlites in Echo Caverns (easy to kill, good XP, OK loot).

If you go for the Needlites, just cast Banshee Aura, melee and lifetap, you'll get your melee skills up and the Needlites will commit suicide on the damage shield; the only time you'll have problems at 24 is if the Queen spawns in the middle since you'll almost always pull both the yellow/red-con Queen and the nearby dark-blue/white/yellow-con Needlite at the same time.

Also, you'll need to nuke them when they run, or they'll drop their corpses under the wormspore and you'll probably get hit by them when you loot; Needlites don't drop often, but their legs and wings are worth about 3pp each to merchants and the Queen drops a back item and dagger which aren't bad for the level.

Another trick to get to the Shadeweavers side of Paludial is to find a Druid with Lesser Succor to help you. Lesser Succor ports the group from anywhere in Paludial to the Shadeweavers zone line. The only downside is that it sucks up 300mana from everyone in the group except the druid who loses 150. If you want to go the invisibility route, you can get an enchanter or mage to cast invisibility on you, superior camo also works. There are also invisibility potions that have the advantage of fixed invisibility duration if you have the plat to spend (around 15pp per charge). Note: that even with invisibility especially the player casted kind that can drop at any minute the trek to the easy side is still very dangerous if you don't know the way. Even with a map some of the turns and tunnel entrances are difficult to find and confusing.

That being said, once you get there, it is really fast leveling. Watch out for huge trains of fiends if you are on zone exit routes.
