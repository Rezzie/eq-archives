Me and my wife after 6 months of kicking around the idea finaly bought a second computer and EQ game. We promptly power leveled a cleric to level 12 up to our 2nd lowest level character who is a warrior so we can now play togather.

We chose this cleric/warrior combination purposely so we will always have the basis of a well rounded group. We can easily invite any other character of any class into our group as support.

I don't think I've has so much fun with my wife for years as we are having now, being able to play and work togather. I play warrior and she plays cleric. We have fun just playing alone but it's more fun to form groups which triply adds to the fun. The best part is that talking is soooo much faster than typing. I don't have to stop and type the word INCOMING! and get bashed and slashed 3 times. We don't lose each other because of lag and ghost figures running in circles because I can look over at her screen and see where she realy is and her me. Now we know when each other is going link dead or having a big lag spike bacause we can just say it. We all know we can't tell our game friends when this is happening because our text doesn't get thru till after the fact. If one of us goes linkdead, the other can warn the group before it shows on their screens. That has happend a couple times already.

We will later power level a couple other characters up to a couple of our other character levels and try other duel combinations. Just the power leveling is fun in itself. We may even start newbies togather on other servers. It's just we have our friends on the server we are on now or we would have started newbie characters on another server to begin with.

Intresting notes: The 1st letter of everything I type on EQ is capitalized automaticly. I don't capitalize the first word in anything I type on EQ.

When I am lagging, my character is still swinging and fighting even though I can't see it on my screen.

Sometimes I see what I typed before my wife and sometimes she sees what I type before I do.

Sometimes the buff spells are shown on her screen before mine and I cast the spell.

Sometimes creatures die 3 seconds before I see it and I have the 56k modem and she is only connected at 28.8.

Now I can see what others see. It's intresting.

Very cool, folks. I'm not posting this to brag, but to re-affirm that gaming in the same room just plain ROCKS!

Me and a group of friends(who are incidentally also members of my ADND gaming group) got together at one members house and networked 6 PC's together and we all played seperate characters in a group. It was a TOTAL BLAST! Shouting over my shoulder to the warrior on the PC behind me to MOVE YOUR BUTT AND TAUNT and teasing my companions verbally during and after combat was unbelievably fun. You also can also react much more quickly, and playing the cleric I was able to save my mana until it was truly needed since the warriors could just shout "Heal Plz!!" This also allowed me to know when I could use a bit of mana for a burn or a stun effectively.

If you ever have the opportunity to do something like this..as someone who has done it, I can tell you that all the setup time and troubleshooting are well worth it. Just be sure to have someone familiar with networks at hand and plan on an hour or two of prep time before actually getting into the game.

Might I suggest you save a phone line connection. Buy a Network in a box (Linksys sells one for $99) then after you have both computers up on a network buy a copy of SYGATE (3 user is about $40). Then only 1 PC is connected over 1 modem. The other PC has access to the Net via SYGATE thru the PC connected via the modem. If you can get Cable modem or DSL on that machine then all you PCs will have a fast connection to the Net. Have Fun
