4) Newbies may be saved from dying by their guards when they leave chords of dissonance going. If newbies aren't killed, their numbers will grow until food runs out, and they will starve. They must be killed to thin their numbers, for their own benefit. Stopping songs is inhumane!

etc.

Absor -

The stuck songs are only half the problem, though. It appears from posts that I've seen from the test server that you are attempting to add code that will cancel songs at arbitrary intervals. My assumption is that you are trying to do this to prevent Bards from turning on a song like Chorus of Clarity (aka Mana Song) and leaving the keyboard. Arbitrary song endings would mean that the Bard would have to remain at the keyboard to make sure that the song keeps going.

If this is your intent, you all had really better take a long deep breath and think about this very carefully. You will create a stink the likes of which you have never seen before from Bards if you do something like this.

Yes, there are a (much smaller than you think) number of times that Bards can exploit singing a song and leaving the keyboard. But for the most part, this is NOT an exploit. It's simple parity with all other classes.

Example:

Enchanter casts Clarity on all casters in his party, then goes AFK for 30 minutes to smoke a cigarette, or eat lunch, or go to the hardware store.

For a Bard to give his party the same advantage, he needs to turn on the Mana Song. Currently, he can then go AFK for 30 minutes, just like the Enchanter, and do whatever the Enchanter does when he's taking a break.

If you modify this code to add early song breaks, the Bard, and ONLY the Bard, will have to sit at his keyboard constantly without any break of any sort in order to maintain his utility in the party.

EVERY OTHER CLASS has naturally recurring times when they can take a break. The Bard, by design, MUST be doing SOMEthing pretty much all the time in order to be useful. That's the way the songs work. That does NOT mean that the human beings who PLAY Bards should be punished by chaining them to the keyboard for the entire duration of their playing session.

The US Department of Labor tells us that we should walk away from a computer for 5 minutes every half hour or risk any of a long list of varied health complications. Bards do this by turning on a buff song during downtime and taking a walk. If you add random song endings to the game, WE CAN'T DO THAT AND REMAIN EFFECTIVE. We will be the ONLY class that can't do that and remain effective.

This is not to mention the fact that early song breaks will render four more of our songs useless:

Invisibility. We want our entire party to become visible simulaneously in some dangerous area and all die? I don't think so.

Levitiation. We want our entire party to fall to their deaths? I don't think so.

51 travel song. We want our entire party to become visible AND fall to their deaths? I don't think so.

Underwater breathing. We have about 30 seconds of leeway here, but otherwise, we want our entire party to drown? I don't think so.

Yes, there are circumstances where you can exploit with a Bard to some limited degree. But you can do EXACTLY the same thing with any other buffing class. Log a Shaman on, buff, log him off. Same difference. To punish Bards and ONLY Bards would really infuriate me, and I'm sure I would not be alone.

Don't do this if that was what you were planning.
