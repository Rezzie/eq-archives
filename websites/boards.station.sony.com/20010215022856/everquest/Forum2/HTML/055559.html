The key to consistency is not in the policy itself, but in the application of the policy. The policy needs to be ambiguous to a degree for two reasons -

1) To allow some room for judgement calls. Ideally these calls are made by the same person, who takes time to ensure they are judging things consistently.

Well, as I have stated before (I think I have, at least obliquely), I don't think Tigergirl should have been a problem. I thought it was MADE a problem when the fellow was obstinate, particularly regarding his last name which could easily be considered offensive (or at least very inappropriate to the setting). This probably caused the GM/guide to interpret the rules far more strictly than would ordinarily be the case.

I wouldn't have any problem with someone reviewing this, deciding that Tigergirl is fine, and reinstating the name. This, of course, would also obviate any "real" complaint since the first name is the one which all of a player's friends are going to look for.

I've admitted that, being human, the VI official in this case overreacted. I don't think the person should be fired or even disciplined, because the frustration is understandable (counseling in this case *would* be appropriate).

I do have a problem with people trying to justify the last name (which is what I was piling in on in the first place), and nobody can convince me that Piddlemaker is appropriate or in keeping the naming policy.

Having one (more likely at least three people, considering that this is a 24/7 experience) or more people specifically responsible for checking names that are /petition'ed would be ideal, with the database of approved names as suggested. Of course, this would STILL not get rid of any problems with people getting their names changed at high levels, both because of the ability to level very quickly (heard of powerleveling?) and because it is possible the name will take that long to be noticed by someone willing to do a /petition. It *would* ameliorate the possibility of one GM saying yes and another saying no.

That being said, there are still a couple more issues. The first is this: How often is this happening? How often is a level 30+ character being forced to change their name due to a violation? My speculation would be that it isn't very often. Further, this is only a subset. How often are ANY characters being forced to change their names? This is the question that would have to be answered by VI in terms of deciding whether adding people to the staff just to handle this one issue. The second issue is WHO are these people who are violating the naming policy, and why should Verant be so solicitous to people who are going out of their way to break the rules?

Let's say I manufacture a VCR that people like to open up and modify/remove the Macrovision (copy protection) chip(s) from it so that they can copy videotapes easily. How much technical support/customer service am I going to give them when they come to me having broken something in the VCR, *despite* the fact that I informed them in the warranty that once they opened the case it was no longer covered? I would suggest that these people would be way down my list of customers that I am concerned about, and I would rather offer better support to my customers NOT trying to break the rules.

I think that, despite the fact these people get focal on the message boards, the people who are purposely trying to break/evade the naming policy are a small minority and should not be rewarded with extra attention from players in-game and Verant, which is probably what they are seeking in the first place. (Of course, I haven't been helping that, have I?) That's at least part of the reason that I simply /petition and move on when I see an offensive name, without engaging the d00d in conversation about why I don't like his/her name.
