It depends on the pet. Enchanter pets? Leave 'em uncast. Bitten Victims? A train waiting to happen. But Beasties, Necros, and all other direct-control pet classes NEED their pets in battle. Set up a hotkey:

/pause 5, /assist
/pet attack

Target the main puller and, when they return with the mob, hit that hotkey. The pet will now only attack the puller's target (which is how a good group should work). I've spent levels 20-32 in LDoN with my Vah Shir Beastie, and her pet has NEVER brought a train down upon us.

Useful info about pets: Once upon a time, your pet would wander away from you due to bad pathing. Your pet would have the same kind of aggro that you would, so when your pet wandered off if his bad pathing took him by a mob, that mob would get mad at your pet and by extention, you! This was a Bad Thing, and the main reasons pets get a bad rep in dungeons. HOWEVER! This is no longer the case. Pets do NOT aggro any mobs unless they are attacking a mob near another mob which is social with the first. Let's use blackburrow as an example... you and your pet are in the snake pit (room with 4 giant snakes, surrounded on 3 sides with ramps up and out of the room and on the 4th by a door that does nothing.) you are killing snakes, which are not social with gnolls...gnolls don't care if you kill off the snakes, snakes don't give a darn if you hit some gnolls. The snake you are on starts running up the ramp and past the 2 or 3 gnolls, and your pet chases. the gnolls will ignore snake and pet, but if YOU go up there they'd jump all over you.
Now, same place, but you have a gnoll you've pulled. That gnoll runs off and your pet chases after, past the same other gnolls...who now see your pet bashing their buddy and attack your pet. This can be an unfortunate situation, which you honestly could only have prevented by stopping the gnoll from running in the first place, because while the gnoll was running away he would be screaming at those other gnolls hey, that guy back there, he beat me up! help! and they'd go after you anyhow.

Same in the LDON dungeons--as long as the mobs don't run past other mobs they're social with, you haven't got a problem, pets or no pets.

Pets don't cause adds in dungeons. Ever. At least since the pets got a facelift like over a year ago. Mobs simply do not aggro at pets, they can walk all over them in plain sight, they will not aggro at any pet.

That is, unless pets aggro them first (by owner sending them to attack), or a mob aggroes because someone (like pet) is fighting a mob close to them, in which case they aggro because of that the mob, not pet.

Or in case where something that's being pulled is casting long range AE spells, pet (that is not on /pet hold) gets hit, starts running at the AEing mob, and is not stopped by /pet back off. But even if your pet owners are asleep, normal caster mobs rarely use AE spells on pullers, and only boss mobs have special AE attacks of sufficiently long range.

If anyone forbids you to use your pet in dungeons, feel free to call them stupid n00bs.

I have noticed that most of the MA's of late are not using the Assist me call. I have on a few occations had to ask the tank to set up his "Assist me NOW" key.

To clarify this for the newbs on the thread there are usually 2 calls for attack a pull call or INC

/gsay Incoming %t one per tell

This is from the puller letting the group know it's time to stand and get ready

then there is

/gsay Assist me on killing %t

This is from the MA. This is to let the group know that he has aggro and the rest of the group may now start attacking, nuking, dispelling, slowing, etc...
