I played a bit on FV when the server first came out, and enjoyed the old rules set. Unfortunately, they are long since a thing of the past. However, reading a few posts about the server recently brought up an interesting debate between my friends, though it might be a long dead horse here.

Everything on FV is no drop, with few exceptions. To combat farming, the trivial loot code is in effect in some form (been a while).

Wouldn't it have been more roleplaying-conducive to have all gear beyond storebought and crafted be no drop, but with no trivial loot code?

Granted, it is more "realistic" to have everything no drop and tradeable, and it promotes a thriving player economy, but when I think of a "roleplaying" environement, realism isn't always my first order of business.

If everything were no drop, people would actually have to work towards their goals, rather than purchase them, by doing quests and venturing into dungeons. To me, this translates to more people following storyline to get geared up, which creates more roleplaying opportunities.

I agree that the TLC would have to go if all non crafted/storebought gear were no drop.

I also agree that the game was obviously built with a player to player economy in mind.

That being said, I still think they got FV's basic loot system backwards. Of course, they would also need to redesign the quest system.

As a person who came to EQ, and later FV, as a roleplayer, the quest system is mindboggling, particularly when you factor in the TLC. Ok, hail an NPC, begin a quest. After many trials and tribulations, and not a few screw ups, you finally complete said quest, only you can't use the reward because it's for a different class/race. Add to that the fact that you may start a quest on FV and not know until you get there that a necessary part drops from a greened out mob, and you've ruined any chance that people might rely on exploration and roleplaying to carry them through, rather than spoiler sites.

Maybe to some truly hardcore RPers out there, the reward doesn't matter, and I'll agree that some quests are just plain fun. But the design is just plain awful.

I'm not sure why I'm rehashing old flaws. Maybe I'm just looking forward to some new games, and starting to think about what things would scare me off.

Greetings, all!

I've often thought that having a server with that ruleset--everything no drop, including storebought gear but excluding tradeskill items, with tradeskill-produced items having the same sort of coding as no-drop groundspawns, so that they could be made and passed to the customer but would then become no drop--would be interesting.

For those people who live for the bazaar, or who are accustomed to using it to produce plat to buy gear, such a server would be a nightmare; to those who, like myself, venture into the Bazaar only with fear and tremnbling, and who generally wear only what they quest for or make themselves, it would be most excellent. While I don't personally care what a person wears or how they acquired it (as long as they don't go shoving it up my nose at every opportunity), I think it would help the whole immersion factor if you knew that there wasn't the equivalent of a K-Mart in the Sky to service your every need.

To make it even more interesting, such a server might also have the one-avatar-only rule, and also have it so that only the person who gets the kill shot can loot the body (with the option to default to someone else in the group at the time, with the members of the group locked as per LDoN), and have the body rot without being lootable to casual passersby.

Having incoming transfers locked out permanently goes without saying.

The only real problem I can see here would be with people used to other rulesets. This would be such a drastic change that I doubt anyone used to the "normal" blue server rules, especially people who live for the high end, would like it other than as a temporary novelty, so the server population would suffer.

Who knows, though--it might be popular enough to be self-sustaining. I would certainly play there almost excluysively.
