Eranox and anyone else who cares, the actual information that Brad McQuaid and others have posted is that usage is lowest between about 4am and 6:30am Pacific time.

If you're going to use that information to determine the best time for a patch, then they should be starting their patch procedure at about 3:30am to 4:30am Pacific time. That would bring them back online sometime in the early afternoon for the East Coast and even earlier for Central, Mountain, and Pacific time zones.

Others here have overstated the case and been too rude about it, but it's probably pretty difficult to get the people required for their present patch procedure to come in at those hours.

Experienced sysadmins and server and network managers would recommend to John Smedley to take a thorough review of the patch procedure. Identify how to do two things. First, make the procedure more automated with scripts, disk images ready to be copied, swapping prepared hard drives into RAIDs, whatever. That way, a computer operator or two would be the only ones required on site in the wee hours to update the server. Second, institute more stringent version controlling. The kinds of mistakes that have been reported to us by Verant with past patch situations were clearly the result of lack of adequate version controlling. Provisions should be included to ensure all updates are adequately tested by QA staff, not just developers, also.

Most of the developers I've ever known do NOT enjoy having to be somewhere at 7:00 am to do an upgrade or install. I suspect that current Verant procedures usually end up requiring them there to even begin performing an install. Automating the install process, and having each new install appropriately tested beforehand, would let those key personnel get some sleep and better able to devote themselves to actual dev work. It would also let Verant move the install of a new patch to hours that better match the usage patterns of the servers.
