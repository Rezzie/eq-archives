Hi! wellcome to everquest

You got all the good advice above.

Only thing I can add is use your ranger tracking. Use the setting /track sort distance. This setting will always show you exactly what monsters are closest to you and where everyone is next to. By watching your track list you can avoid danger and never get lost.

Track sort distance is unique to rangers. Druids and Bards get tracking but it's nowhere, good as ranger track.

When you get your first spells at level 9 be sure to put one training point into each spell skill, so they will increase in skill. Use all your spells. Good luck and happy hunting.

All good advice (although there aren't too many gnome rangers ).

I would add to Lanys' fear kiting (which will be a few levels yet) that if you learn the feel for how long panic animal lasts, you can cast it again just as it is about to expire and the critter will *never* turn around to swat on you. This is an excellent way to improve lagging weapon skills.

Aha, another piece of advice for a new ranger--get at least four weapons--a 1HS, 1HB, 2HS, and a bow. Use them each in turn as you fight to increase the skill in all four (2HB if you want, too). I recommend maxing one of the weapon skills first, then using the other weapons to improve those skills. Then you have a *main* weapon that is "best" if you get in trouble.

Weapon skills will not rise when used against a critter that cons green (a few more levels for you yet).

You never know when, later in life, you'll run across an excellent weapon. You want to have the skill to use it.

Your weapon skills will max each level at (level + 1) * 5. So at level 1, your skill will max at 10; at level 2 it maxes at 15, etc.

quote:
Aha, another piece of advice for a new ranger--get at least four weapons--a 1HS, 1HB, 2HS, and a bow. Use them each in turn as you fight to increase the skill in all four (2HB if you want, too).

In my opinion this is a waste of time for a Ranger. Rangers get dual wield. A weapon in each hand means one-handed ("1H") skills. The number of superior 1HB (blunt) weapons available in the game is very small compared to 1HS. The Ranger Epics are 1HS. Concentrate on maxing your 1-hand slashing skill (1HS) at each level, and of course use a bow.

Typically you'll use the bow to "pull" your target. When you get "snare" and "fear animal" you can use your bow to plink your (animal) target while it slowly tries to run away from you. This is a great way to raise archery skill (as long as the animal cons above green). West Karana is a great place for this.

Fletch all your own arrows. Not only will this raise your fletching skill (which will allow you to make better bows than anything you can buy from most NPCs), it will also allow you to make higher-damage arrows than you can buy from most NPCs.

Don't worry about the Trueshot bow for a while. While you'll be able to gather the necessary materials in your 20's, you need a high fletching skill to succeed in the combine (unless you get very lucky). My ranger's skill is 102 and he failed about 20 times. What you can start doing now is raising your faction with the dwarves in Butcherblock (by killing goblins and Krag chicks there). There are a couple of quests you need to do there to do Trueshot and they won't give them to you if your faction is too low.

Welcome fellow Ranger!!

another piece of advice- setup a hotkey for forage and sense heading.
I'll have to repost when I find the instructions how to do it, but generally,
On page 1 of your Hot Keys, in the top 2 slots, you hot key Sense Heading, and Forage( when you get forage level 4 I think). You then map those two hot keys to your UP arrow key, so every time you press the UP arrow to go forward, you forage and do a sense heading at the same time.

If anyone else has done this and can give a step by step, please feel free, otherwise I'll repost a step by step when I find the instructions. I did this so long ago I forgot how to do it .

This will Skill up your sense heading and forage really quick. Forage is great for picking up spare food and water, but also for picking up lots of tradeskill ingredients. While hunting Spiders in East Karana, I'll press the UP arrow, and forage some Tea leaves - sell for 5 to 10 plat each in Bazaar.

My advice- 1HB is useful, I dual weild a Wurmslayer and a Centi Warhammer. Decent stats on the Warhammer.
