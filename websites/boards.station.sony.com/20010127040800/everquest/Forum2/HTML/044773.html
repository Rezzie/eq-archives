Im going to make this very very simple.

In the interest of the final ballancing of the paladin class, here is what Paladins ABSOLUTELY NEED to be balanced post 50.

1. A SERIOUSLY USEFUL discipline/spell for high level encounters that make them desireable on big raids.

Such as a 30 minute reuse lure resist stun that interrupts any mob; or SOMETHING to make us valuable on raids over the million warriors out there such as:

The chance to interrupt ANY mob with a successful bash for 30 seconds with a 30 minute reuse.


2. More weapons that are NOT ballanced for those who can wield two weapons. More 1h in the 19 24 range (like ebon mace) and more 2h in the 32 40 range that dont require ruining dragon faction.


Here is what paladins really SHOULD have but don't ABSOLYTELY need.

1. Increse the amount LOH heals by 125 hp per level post 50. An 1800 hp loh on a 4500 hp tank is just two bubbles and just not enough.

2. More buffs (castable on everyone) that stack with cleric spells like divine strength such as a stackable AC buff. (This does not include the rediculous idea to add resist disease to paladins as is on test now.)

3. Increase the level 60 Sanctification discipline to 20 seconds. As it is now its almost useless since its impossible to know when a mob will cast an ae breath spell.

4. Change Holyforge to auto crit/crip for 10 seconds vs undead. Even at 3x the normal crits for 2 minutes this barely does anything at all. Not to mention the total lack of undead mobs except in one zone in Velious.

Plain and simple. No BS

Im really wondering if the Ghoulbane wasn't just a big mistake... an over powered weapon that set too many false impressions about Paladins.

Back in the old days, pre Kunark, a 15/34 weapon with a 160pt proc vs undead really let a paladin stay respectable in melee because they could do damage close to the dual wielders. They were still doing the least damage of any melee or hybrid, but it was still respectable. Because ghoulbane was 1Handed, a shield let them stay close to warriors in the AC department also.

The thing about the Ghoulbane was that although it had an insane ratio for its level, paladins couldn't dual wield so it wasnt _really_ overpowered.

Rangers had a valid complaint because they gave up strong armour to dual wield and the Ghoulbane let Paladins get close to Ranger damage and still wear armour. This sounds good on paper but, until a LOT of paladins started getting Velorium and Kunark came out, it really didnt matter all that much because the AC on the Ranger chain armour was pretty dang close to the Pal/SK armour anyway.

ShadowKnights, especially the small ones, were pretty much out of luck because there was no common 1H weapon for them with such a nice ratio. If they wanted to bash it was most likely gonna be while wielding a weapon that other classes could dual wield. This meant that it was gonna be wielding a weapon with a more normal ratio.

In Kunark and there was no Ghoulbane upgrade. ShadowKnights got some nice 1H maces and whatever but it was a long long time coming. There were barely any Paladan only 1Hand weapons at all. A new level cap of 60 and a bunch of new multi-class 1H and 2H weapons meant that paladins couldn't really keep up anymore.

The lack of Paladin 1H weapons with ratios that are too good to be multi-class weapons has pushed paladins even farther down the melee damage list. They were already last. The margin is just larger now.

There have been a lot of complaints, even by Belduki I think. There has been a long time for this to be corrected if it was an accident.

But Verant has not added new 1H weapons with extrodinary ratios. From where I'm sitting, it seems like they are saying that Ghoulbane was a mistake and that paladin melee output was too high before.
