The entire point was missed. Over the head...747 at 35,000 feet...wooooosh! Even if we wanted to be some miserable second rate support class, we couldnt even do that. Let me break this down so those of you without a clue will understand.

Necromancers can control their hit points(their life), Necors have masterd the art of death, and in doing so have gained more understanding of life. We can make our little red lines go up and down whenever we want.

A Necro can make mana(the magical force behind all spell power) for themselves by depleting their own health with spells like Call of Bones, Lich, Arch Lich, so on.

Necros can in turn decide to use this mana to:
a) use it for an offensive or even defensive spell.
b) use this for a tap(a spell that takes hit points from a target and gives it to the Necro)
i) this in turn helps create more mana(see a pattern?)
c) give this mana to another caster who may need it. Such as a Cleric or Enchanter or Wizard.

Now...heres the trick. The Necromancer goes into hell zone X to fight boss mob Y with his guild buddies.

Boss mob Y is almost completely magic resistant(80 to 90% spells cast dont hurt it). The tanks are taking a beating, the cleric is running low on mana. The Necro can:

a) blow all his mana trying to land a tap on boss mob Y.
b) give all his mana to the Cleric and then sit there and do nothing.
c) Lich up anyway and pass off mana to the Cleric, in which case leaves the necro low on mana and low on hit points sitting there doing nothing.
