Here is a copy of a post elsewhere that brings up an interesting point:

I know it probably won't help, but here's soemthing the famous tester and mathmetician Kaearia posted:
"I've been thinking about the success rates GZ said Lullaby should have and comparing with the success rates I got in testing. I think the discrepancy may be due to a buggy message with the bard mesmerize songs.
Bard mez songs (Lullaby, Pixie Strike, Twilight) have two resists. The first is your ordinary resist that all songs/spells get. The second resist is something GZ threw in only for bard mez. When a bard tries to mez a mob, three things can happen:

1) The mob succeeds its first resist check and the song doesn't work. Message:

"Mob resisted your <song name>."

2) The mob fails its first resist check, but succeeds its second resist check and the song doesn't work. Message:

"Mob's head nods."
"Mob resists your attempt to mesmerize it."

3) The song is not resisted and works. Message:

"Mob's head nods."

Notice that although (2) is a song failure, it contains the same message as (3) which is a song success. Is it possible that when you were testing and playbalancing these songs, that you incorrectly counted the false "Mob's head nods" messages in case (2) as successes? When I apply this error to my test results, I get numbers that are surprisingly close to what GZ originally stated we should be seeing:

L1 mobs: GZ stated we should be getting a 25% resist rate, I got 35% true resist rate, but 27% "erroneous" resist rate.

L35-42 mobs: GZ stated we should be getting a 35% resist rate against L35 mobs, I got a 75% resist rate, but a 44% "errnoeous" resist rate.

This error could easily account for the ridiculous 80%-90% secondary resist rates high level bards are seeing against blue mobs using Song of Twilight, a level 53 song (NOT Lullaby, a L16 song). If these songs were playbalanced without knowing about this erroneous resist message, the designer could easily think these songs have just a 30%-50% resist rate when in reality they have a 80%-90% resist rate."
