The important thing with Donals Breastplate is to state what the original intent the breastplate was suppose to fulfill. If that intent is/was unbalancing, then don't go and try to concoct another method/type of (healing) spell to replace the effect. That is completely unnecessary and unwarranted.

CASE 1) If the effect is unbalancing because the original intent was unbalancing, then change what it does... period. Do go making up ways to curve the useage because it doesn't fit with how it was intended to work and players are taking advantage of the inherant weakness behind how it was created. Find another existing way to make the original intent work. Not a new spell/effect; that's definitely not needed.

The way I see the breastplate, it was intended to reduce downtime post combat (or even before combat). Downtime for a cleric has always been mana limitation (as with any casting class in certain circumstances) and clerics are the MOST self mana limited pure-casting class in the game. They are the only class without some form of minor mana recovery ability. It's not and never has been a HP down time, it's always been a mana downtime; it takes mana to heal (and is should always take mana to heal at the level of CH). Let me repeat, it's not a hit point down time, it's mana downtime as it takes mana to heal. And Donal's BP doesn't have that limitation, that's breaking the game. So perhaps the cleric is OoM at the end of combat and the tank(s) needs a heal, click the BP for a CH patch up so that mana downtime is reduced... wrong, that's not how it's working out and it won't ever be that way. The largest downtime for a cleric post 50 is not from healing, it's from buffing. Anyone that plays a cleric post 50 knows that after buffing a party, expect a long downtime to regain all the mana lost. My suggestion for Donal's BP, in its intent to reduce downtime, is to make the effect one of the frequently used high mana cost buffs; Shield of Words, Aegis, Bulwark of Faith, Aegolism, Symbol of Marzin. In the end, what this does is allow a cleric to recover a party(s) quicker after a catastrophic wipe-out. We have reviviscence on our epic (improves down time tremendously post wipeout) that saves a tremendous amount of CR downtime. Put a high mana cost buff on the breastplate, to facilitate recovery after wipeouts even further.

Make it Aegolism and make Aegolism not able to overwrite itself. Why Aegolish? It has a built in limitation factor, it uses a reagent. It is not as good as fully buffing with Heroism, Aegis and Symbol of Marzin. Don't allow Aegolism to overwrite itself, so that it can't be used as a heal. Aegolism is just right, it does a complete buff (but not a good as fully buffing with all stackable buffs), it uses a reagent (can even make it a more expensive reagent) and it can be made so that it doesn't cast over itself.

CASE 2) It could also be argued that the breastplate is intended to be a heal when things get rough and mana is low. There shouldn't be any item that casts heals at the level of CH in the game at all. Again, there shouldn't be ANY item that does a CH in the game at all. This goes all the way back to why there aren't any healing potions in the game as well. Items like that take away from the inherant initial class balacing made originally in beta and take away from the base usefulness found in the basic abilities that have been long defined for every class (in this case a cleric, and secondarily the other priest classes... druids and shamans).

Items that cast heals that are over 500 to 750 hps don't belong on click use items, items like that break game balancing that was already done. That's the problem with Donals, as it breaks the original balance mold established so long ago. After all, we don't have healing potions either, essentially the Donals BP is something like a healing potion... a heal with no consequence to control how often it can be used. It will always be a heal without a method of moderation, the 7.5 minute limitation is not a good moderator.

CASE 3) Another point, from another angle, the problem isn't how often CH is able to be cast from the Donal's Breastplate. That's how the problem is being addressed time and again... 20 second cast time, 30 second cast time, now an upcoming 7.5 minute recast time on someone. It's not the cast time that is the problem, it's the effect and the effect alone: Complete Heal.

In summary. Look at the original intent for how the BP was to operate (what it was to accomplish in the first place) and look at the meta-game. I believe the real intent is to reduce downtime. Downtime reduction can be accomplished in another way for clerics, besides allowing a click effect heal. A buff that takes a lot of mana, make it a good/decent buff that will be used, that can be cast from the breastplate is ideal for downtime reduction. It's commendable to stick to CH effect on the BP, but it is really the wrong way to go.

The BP itself is really a non-issue. In 30 seconds you can med up a brand new CH. The BP only becomes an issue against Velious mobs that have hundreds of thousands of HP where eventually your clerics will run out of mana no matter how efficient they are.

If Absor is convinced of the reasoning he puts forth to nerf the BP, the exact same reasoning must therefore apply to complete heal as a spell. C2+catana+liched necro feed = unlimited complete heals every 12.5 seconds. (You might not even need the necro.) This group is unkillable as long as the mob can't kill the warrior in 12.5 seconds. Add a second cleric, with reasonable timing, the mob has to kill the warrior in 7 seconds, or a third to cut it down to 5 seconds (rounding times up, with perfect timing the interval is even shorter). Enchanter can go home once he casts C2. This leaves us on the defensive end with a warrior, 3 clerics, a necro to feed, and a bard with cantana in a single group of six. The mob therefore cannot kill the players unless it is able to deal out over 5000hp every five seconds, or 1000hp/sec; and this is only a single group of six players. Add two more similar groups (still only 18 players) and the mob must now deal nearly 3000hp/sec (distributing damage via taunt is easy). Add regrowth to the warriors and it gets worse. Add defensive disciplines. Only up to 18 players right now. Add in a bunch of rogues to deal maximum damage.

This is why we have ubermobs that quad for 900, have hundreds of thousands of HP and regenerate incredibly fast. What's the use in this scenario for a druid, a shadowknight, a paladin, a ranger, a magician, or a wizard? None, considering the technical limitation of the number of players that can be in one place at one time without massive linkdeath.

Complete Heal must go.
