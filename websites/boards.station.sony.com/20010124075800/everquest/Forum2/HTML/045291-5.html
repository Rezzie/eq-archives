Here's some suggestions that's specific to the issue at hand.

1. Ignite/Chill Bones.

Remove the skeleton effect. The spell takes almost as long as Ice Comet to cast, is mana-inefficient, has a long recast delay and an all-or-nothing effect. Frankly, this nuke is worse than cleric's. There is no need to penalize it more. However, if you do insist, here are some ways to modify it.

(a) Remove skeleton effect. Give the spell a stun effect like wizard's Force Shock.
(b) Remove skeleton effect. Give the spell a fire/cold resist debuff like druid's Ice.
(c) Remove skeleton effect. Make it do 500 dmg initially, and then a 12-second 'burning/chilling' effect (that in reality is a 60 dmg per tick dot over 2 ticks). Change the dot damage as you wish.

I like (c) best. It not only gives necro ignite/chill bones a unique (but tolerable raid-wise) property, but also maintains the effect that the spell has in the past at disallowing multiple necros of varying levels to land this spell at the same time on a given mob.

2. Damage to Uber Resist-Everything Mobs

Necros across all servers want to have a chance at doing damage to boss mobs. That is the real issue at hand. Anything that you add/change that doesn't enable this will not satisfy the necro community. I have an interesting and viable idea that solves this issue almost perfectly.

Solution: Take away Infusion and Trudication. As a replacement, add a poison dot at level 54 and a disease dot at level 59. Both dots do 1000 damage to target in 1 minute, heals 1000 damage to caster, requires 1000 mana, almost unresistable (close to lure-like) and stacks with other necros. The only difference between the two spells being different resist checks (poison vs disease). Again, change the dmg/heal/cost/duration of the spells to suit your taste.

Here is why I think these two spells would solve all necro uber-mob fight complaints.

(a) As master of dots, it's only natural that necros deal a slow and painful death to mobs, which include uber mobs. Therefore, necros using dots rather than lifetaps to hurt these mobs are more in line with the vision for the class.

(b) It will not give necros more power in everyday groupings since Bond of Death (720 dmg/heal for 360 mana) and Vexing Mordinia (1098 dmg/heal for 495 mana) are already unresistable on normal mobs and are immensely more mana efficient than the two dots I suggested. Therefore, necros will only use these two spells on uber mobs that resist all other of their spells.

(c) It's in line with the necromancer design. Having a dot heal effect allows necros to lich in big mob fights, thus convert hp into mana, and then transfer mana or do more damage as necessary. Also, it's more necromancer-like, to see all necros turning liches when doing tough fights vs boss mobs.

(d) It makes the necro a poor man's wizard. A 60 Sorcerer with Lure of Ice can do approximately 9k damage to Trakanon, a poison dragon. Six level 54-58 necros will do zero damage to Trakanon since their level 54 spell is poison based. However, six 59-60 necros will be able to do 6k damage to Trakanon in 1 minute. If Trakanon lives past that, then another 6k damage in the 2nd minute. Then another 6k in the 3rd minute before being out of mana (even with liching). In comparison, six level 60 Sorcerers can do 9 x 6 = 54,000 damage in one minute, whereas six level 60 Warlocks can do 6 x 1 x 3 = 18,000 damage in *three* minutes. Doesn't sound too overpowering to me.

(e) If a mob is resistant to both poison and disease but is vulnerable to fire and cold, necros will be able to do zero damage. But this is acceptable, given the rare instances of the case. Also, it's fairly understandable that if the mob is particularly resistant to poison/disease spells (necro domain), necros wouldn't be able to hurt it while wizards can.

This in one swipe solves all necro complaints. Now, necros can do moderate damage to uber resist-everything mobs by inflicting a slow death through dots rather than using unresistable lifetaps. This damage is no where near what a wizard can do. Also, the necro design is kept intact through the heal effect property of the two dots suggested. Also, this two spells only give necros more to do in uber mob fights, not more power in every day groupings.

With this in place, you will see level 59-60 necros turning liches, rush to Trakanon, dot it to death, med for 1 minute (lich is sustained by the life force of Trakanon through the suggested disease spell), rise up after the minute is over, dot again. This makes necros good for long fights, and does exactly what every necro has been dreaming of since day one.

Thanks for your time.

=== Summary #1 ===

I have read through the entire thread and have compiled a summary that you might find useful. I will try and add to it if something new is brought up. Please note, the vast majority of the ideas below are NOT mine but rather those of the community at large. I am merely the editor and compiler with one exception.

Changes fall within several categories

Lifetap
1. If a tap is cast on a mob that is less than six levels above the player, then the tap will check resistance as normal, but always return the full amount of health of the spell to the necromancer even if resisted.

2. Really a corollary to the above in that it allows partial damage while still returning full health of the spell to the necromancer.

Fear
3. Provide a fear protection line that allows the necromancer to make the target immune from fear for a time.

4. Group fear protection--similar to above, perhaps an upgrade if a line is considered.

5. AoE fear removal spell which would encourage the necromancer to meditate near the fray and cast this spell somewhat regularly to keep all players and pets on target. This spell would need both fast cast and recast times to be effective.

6. MOB fear elimination spell which, when cast on a critter would prevent it from casting AoE fear for the duration.

Pets
7. Make undead pets immune to AoE fear

8. Additional control commands
a) /no attack--pet will not defend itself
b) /sever--kills pet regardless of distance
c) /one target--prevents pet from changing targets unless countermanded

9. Raid-Pets: Max pet per summon, but have very slow movement rates, are immune to fear, and suicide after MOB is killed


General Grouping
10 Ignite Bones--Remove skeleton illusion in favor of a fiery particle effect

11. Provide a 42 second "splut-like" debuff overtime.

12. Group DMF

13. Group Feign Death. I think the general consensus from both necros and non was that this would be interesting to try out on test to see how it worked. Some thought it would get more folks killed than it saved. Given the supposed ease to code, it might be worth a test. My personal note is that the spell would probably need to be all-or-nothing based on the necromancer's save.

14. Mana-Link--a link similary to shifting site, but cancelable, that allows 80% of the necromancer's mana to immediately channel to the target along with 80% of all mana gained during the duration of the spell. This spell should probably have a relatively long duration, 2-3 mins, but also a very long recast time like 30 mins. Part of the compensation for this restriction should be a better ratio, perhaps 2-1.

15. Lower level requirement for Conjure Corpse from 57 to 55. Reasoning behind this from the poster is three-fold:
a) Summoning corpses is unarguably one of the few things necros do on raids.

b) Summon Corpse was gained 19 levels before, which is a very long time to wait for an upgrade

c) Shadow Knights are supposedly getting Summon Corpse at lvl 50 and are far better equiped to carry the heavy coffins than are necromancers. Generally speaking, hybrids do not get a parent class spell until the parent class gets any available upgrades. At lvl 55, necromancers are still 5 lvls above the point where their child class gets the upgrade.

=== End Summary ===

=== SUMMARY #2 ===

ABSOR,

I have read through the entire thread and have compiled a summary that you might find useful. I will try and add to it if something new is brought up. Changes fall within several categories

Lifetap
1. If a tap is cast on a mob that is less than six levels above the player, then the tap will check resistance as normal, but always return the full amount of health of the spell to the necromancer even if resisted.

2. Really a corollary to the above in that it allows partial damage while still returning full health of the spell to the necromancer.

Fear
3. Provide a fear protection line that allows the necromancer to make the target immune from fear for a time.

4. Group fear protection--similar to above, perhaps an upgrade if a line is considered.

5. AoE fear removal spell which would encourage the necromancer to meditate near the fray and cast this spell somewhat regularly to keep all players and pets on target. This spell would need both fast cast and recast times to be effective.

6. MOB fear elimination spell which, when cast on a critter would prevent it from casting AoE fear for the duration.

Pets
7. Make undead pets immune to AoE fear

8. Additional control commands
a) /no attack--pet will not defend itself
b) /sever--kills pet regardless of distance
c) /one target--prevents pet from changing targets unless countermanded

9. Raid-Pets: Max pet per summon, but have very slow movement rates, are immune to fear, and suicide after MOB is killed


General Grouping
10 Ignite Bones--Remove skeleton illusion in favor of a fiery particle effect

11. Provide a 42 second "splut-like" debuff overtime.

12. Group DMF

13. Group Feign Death. I think the general consensus from both necros and non was that this would be interesting to try out on test to see how it worked. Some thought it would get more folks killed than it saved. Given the supposed ease to code, it might be worth a test. My personal note is that the spell would probably need to be all-or-nothing based on the necromancer's save.

14. Mana-Link--a link similary to shifting site, but cancelable, that allows 80% of the necromancer's mana to immediately channel to the target along with 80% of all mana gained during the duration of the spell. This spell should probably have a relatively long duration, 2-3 mins, but also a very long recast time like 30 mins. Part of the compensation for this restriction should be a better ratio, perhaps 2-1.

a) Summoning corpses is unarguably one of the few things necros do on raids.

b) Summon Corpse was gained 19 levels before, which is a very long time to wait for an upgrade

c) Shadow Knights are supposedly getting Summon Corpse at lvl 50 and are far better equiped to carry the heavy coffins than are necromancers. Generally speaking, hybrids do not get a parent class spell until the parent class gets any available upgrades. At lvl 55, necromancers are still 5 lvls above the point where their child class gets the upgrade.
