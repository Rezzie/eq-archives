Casters will suffer from the lag more than fighters will.

Mildaene was mostly right, so I'll just key in on a couple things.

Monks can solo up to 50+, but it's not as efficient as grouping. At level 17 you get a skill called feign death, and at level 18 you get one called instill doubt.

If you learn how to use these, you will be on your way to effective soloing. And your most important skill, feign death, will be key to surviving where most would not. I dare say that, as a monk myself, our class doesn't die much until later levels on large raids when we're pulling...but when we're pulling at later levels, we'll get a ressurection (raids don't happen without a cleric) if we die anyway, so it's of little consequence.

But to answer your question, Shamen solo better by far.

Monks are a fun class though, and there's much more to playing a monk than people think.

Binding is important, and can be a pain at times because casters aren't always willing to help out. Once you learn how to survive, you shouldn't die much as a monk (I've got streaks of 10 levels without dying once...with a non-powerleveled, non-twinked monk), but it will happen. So make sure that you do get bound (again, this doesn't apply to large raids).

Monks are easy to twink now. Before kunark, there weren't any uber-monk weapons that you could be twinked with. Now there are and it's depressing, but I'll live with it. The stuff that monks need is very expensive though. The weapons are the most pricey ones out there, but if you can't afford em, don't worry. I had to go 1-50 before there were even rumors about monk 1hb weapons coming out. It wasn't too hard.

As for my class recommendation? I love monks. I don't really play mine anymore because I've basically become bored with the game and have been trying to find something that I enjoy, but it's hard to do so anymore. My advice to you, is go with whoever makes you have the most fun.

I play a monk, and I love it.

Monks soloing ability is better than most other melee classes because of Feign Death, and Mend. If a high level warrior gets beaten up he'll have a good half hour downtime, a monks at most is about 10 minutes.

Casters can always solo better. Shamans have a large variety of DoTs, DDs,etc. These combined with Roots and Healing mean that they can take out way bigger monsters.

Lag... I'm a European player and it doesn't affect me very often. If you get a lot of packet loss and high ping then your life as a melee character will be hell. If the monster runs then zone, cos you just can't hit it if you've got a high ping.

Binding. From 1 - 17 this will be an issue but you won't be travelling too far so be patient. After you get feign death you become a lot more survivable, in fact you probably become the most survivable class in the game.

Monks are damage machines, but only at higher levels. You can solo blues until very high levels but through the lower levels you'll feel very inadequite. This gets better though. Remember that that twinked warrior won't get a new sword for a while, whereas your fists just keep getting better and better.

Monks equipment. This never used to be a big deal, and we are still very effective even when naked. However with Kunark we were introduced to very powerful monk weapons. At high levels these weapons are needed to remain competitive with other classes. However they are very expensive.
You can still do well without them. The rule for monks is that we don't need a lot of equipment but the stuff we do need isn't cheap.

Hmm... what else. You'll make friends with people in game. This means that SoWs and Binds are fairly easy to get once you know people. Also you'll usually find kind casters to bind you in most major cities. SoW is nice but you can live without it.

Shamans are a good class but monks ooze style. As for which class you should play, which one do you like the best?
