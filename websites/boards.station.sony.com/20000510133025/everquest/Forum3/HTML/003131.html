Heh Heh kill more rats...

Seriously, looking at things for sale from merchants can be misleading. VERY few people buy weapons from merchants, they are just too expensive. Most equipment purchases come from the player driven economy, and are a fraction of the merchant cost. But rat loot isn't going to make much of a dent there either. As soon as you get your pet spell from selling rat parts, and maybe even before, you need to move up to decayed or decaying skeletons. Especially the ones carrying visible weapons. Many of those weapons sell for over 1 gold, and if you get one swinging a staff with one hand (cracked staff) you just got yourself either a weapon upgrade, or a platinum. Slow going at first, but it eventually snowballs.

As others have mentioned, decaying skeletons often carry weapons that sell for a fair amount. One of the staffs goes to the merchants for almost a plat. Also, bone chips. A stack of 20 bone chips goes from 1 to 3 plat.

As soon as you can reasonably kill black bears, kill every black bear you can. They are good experience, and they occasionally drop a high quality bear skin. That will sell for 10 to 15 plat.

A lot of players don't seem to bother looting their kills, so check every abandoned bear you see. I've picked up HQ bear skins that way.

Fire beetlesgive fairly good loot for low levels. A fire beetle eye goes for about 2 silver. A nice thing about fire beetles is that they swarm, so as soon as you get high enough level to not be bothered by that, you can fight three or four at a time...that's over a gold and half of loot.

Fire beetles run slower than you do, by the way, so if you run by one, whack it on the run, and keep going, you'll pull ahead and won't get whacked in the back. Sweep back and forth across your newbie area a couple times this way, then stop and fight the crowd you've attracted. (Make sure you can handle the crowd before doing this, though).

--Tim Smith

Pretty good advice already given, just let me add a few things as this question comes up a lot.

Bat wings, bones (as you know), fish scales, and snake scales are spell ingredients. I've never seen anyone buy/sell/trade snake scales, but they are ingredients for a shaman spell. HQ Bear Skins (as was pointed out) are worth at least 10pp. Always check, HQ Bear Skins look like Bear Pelts.

I played a necro for a while (a poor one). I sold a stack of bones for 5pp (I was desperate), then, not 5 minutes later, someone offered 20pp for a stack of bones. You never know when a fellow necro will be very generous (they remember what it was like), or very desperate. I would carry a stack of bones with me to sell for the right price.

OK, this is how I made money at level 10. I repeated a number of quests (delivering mail, etc) and saved 8 plat. I raised my fishing skill and caught a lot of fish (on the order of 80 fish). I then baked fish fillets and sold them to vendors. Tedious, tedious, tedious; literally hours and hours. By level 14 I had earned 65 plat, but I haven't baked for profit since that time. I don't regret it now though; I can bake a lot of different foods, and create different beverages. Baking and Brewing are great RPing skills, and I find them enjoyable

Before you start, if you do, check some of the web pages so you know what you are getting into.

Wonderful thread...

With the new server(s) coming online, one tends to forget how to take advantage of resources until you are naked and copperless again.

First for those offering advice:
think Steamfont Mountain critters.
I don't remember seeing too many bears and wolves roaming.
Agreed - near Freeport is an ideal zone to grow-up.

There are quests and quests and even more quests. Some will even provide you a container that you eventually return, which can be a prize to many of us with not enough coin to afford even a small sack!

ROADKill - as noted are components for some spells as well as items used with Tradeskills.

Last night I heard an offer to sharpen rusty weapons for free! Tarnished weapons sell the same, but this enterprising blacksmith was gaining herself skill for merely having a gold coin's worth of stones in her pocket.

I was collecting snake skins to make bog juice (bottle+water+snake scale), just to get my brewing skill moving. I have already banked nearly 20 snake eggs gathered or bought from vendors.

Back to a gnomish childhood, my enchanter will share the fact he hates the lifts at Kelethin and it's a LONG run back to fetch things from Ak'Anon.
If you can find a bind at Felwithe do consider relocating. You may get a better return on critter loot from wasps and bats rather than rats and clockworks. Finding group support and older players willing to help or even buy your 'wings'.

Another quick note, do pickup a toolbox for tinkering. You can not gain skill until you are minimum level 16, but you CAN tinker easy stuff, it's a unique skill to the gnomes.

You can sell collabsable fishing poles and fireworks for a reasonable profit, which helps finance spells or other needed items.
