As a martial artist who has payed attention to physics, It is possible to break metals ONLY if the metal cannot bounce backwards. For every millisecond an impact is delivered, its force is decreased, which is why it is the sudden snap in a well placed kick or punch that breaks an object, not the power put behind it. If the Impact is allowed to be elastic (the armor, brick,or the whatever bounces off of the foot or hand) then twice as much force will be exerted on both objects, and I doubt the ball or heel of your foot could withstand the amount of force it would take to break the platemail and bounce off, unless the recoil speed was extremely fast, but I doubt that the (Human anyway) body could handle the speed required. However, chainmail would offer no protection at all, because it really only protects from slashing, not blunt (It distributes the pressure of a sharp weapon evenly over the surface of the body) However, against a mace or a foot, perhaps even a hand, It will provide no protection.

FOrce = mass X Acceleration

Acceleration = Velocity/time Measured in m/sxs
(metres and seconds)
mass = Kg not lbs (1 kg = 2.2 lbs)

higher the mass of the object...the harder the force... higher the acceleration of the object the harder the force(object = foot/hand).

just do the mathematics...

The equaton for Hookes Elastic Spring Law is:

Elastic Potential Energy = 1/2 k XxX, where X = Stretching of the material in question(metallic armour) and K is the coefficient of Elasticity.

Elastic Potential Force = kx (same units and symbols)

Just work with these 2 equations and you will see that it is ossible to break plate armour with a fist...but these equations are only true in an ideal world..in real life, with enough force the metallic armour will permanently deform, and in some cases even break...But always remember Newtons 3rd law: When an object applies a force on a second object, the second object applies an equal and opposite(in direction) force to the first object. IE. If u punch a wall, the wall punches you right back.

IN conclustion: If you Hit the armour hard enough(had enough mass and acceleration to back it) you can break the armour....but there is an extremely high chance that you will break your hand/foot in the process.
