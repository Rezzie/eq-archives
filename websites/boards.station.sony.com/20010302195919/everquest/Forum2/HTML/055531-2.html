First of all, I need to apologise. I haven't read every single post in these gargantuan threads. I have tried, but its so difficult to wade through pages and pages of statistics and whining and so forth. I pity you Absor.

Now, onto the rest. First of all, I play a Dark Elf Shadowknight, a hair's breath away from 41st level.

I love my class. Its very difficult to play a Shadowknight. Its much easier to play a Warrior, or a Druid, or a Necro or a Shaman. No, I don't say that to mean that those classes don't have their own weaknesses and flaws, more that those are the other things I have tried. And by far, I find the Shadowknight the most challenging.

But, I played a Shadowknight first - image is all important, and there is an undeniable cool factor to the class. It disheartens me to see that some people don't understand that, and can't look beyond the technicalities. But I don't want to start that whole debate either. I suppose what I am really trying to say is, that I love my Shadowknight.

The Shadowknight damage ratio in regards to that of a warrior is almost laughable. I've had a warrior four levels lower than me manage to out damage me time and time again. Now I pride myself on being an excellent representative of my class. No, I am not level 60, no I don't know anything about big raids. But I have worked DAMN hard to get where I am, so I believe that my, and every other Shadowknight's concerns out there warrant attention.

Issues I would like to see.

1) Weapon Specialisation.

Casters have Spell School specialisation. Why can there not be weapon specialisation ? And no, I don't just mean for Shadowknights, I mean for everyone it could be an idea to consider. You have the choice of choosing a specific weapon, and as you advance in your specialisation, there would be benefits. What kind of benefits ? My mind keeps drifting towards critical shots, but I cringe at the repurcussion and balance issues of that. Perhaps more that you would miss less often, and hit more often ?

This would of course level out the debate of "most shadowknights use this" and blah blah. I don't appreciate someone stating what most shadowknights use. I don't know who you are, I've never seen you, and you've certainly never seen me. Do a lot of shadowknights use 1hb and a shield ? Sure thing. And why is that ? Because of the stupidly high stats on certain weapons, such as the Ebon Mace (I think thats it, its late, forgive me). Me ? I prefer to use a sword, and a two handed sword at that. Its intimidating, its large, its powerful, and you certainly won't miss it going through your ribs. At any rate, my original point before I became side-tracked was that offering the opportunity for specialisation, would perhaps even enable the wielder of the weapon to go above "normal" skill caps for that weapon. Be it 1hs, 1hb, 2hs, or piercing. Whatever. I don't think it would be a GOOD idea for one weapon skill to be increased and another not to be. I've never used 1hb, and I never will.

2) Offense vs Defense.

In my opinion, I see a Shadowknight as "offensive" (no pun intended) and a Paladin defensive. The paladin is the healer, the defender of faith, the protector. The Shadowknight is the bringer of pain and darkness and destruction. And more than that, a Shadowknight is not designed to truly protect anyone other than themselves. The only 'other' cast spells we have are what, Dark Empathy, and the Endure/Resist Disease Cold line. I have no problem with the Defense skill increase, should it go through. But in my mind, I believe Shadowknights to be more offensively minded.

3) The Equipment.

This I think is an often made mistake. It seems that with every expansion there is more powerful armor and weapons. Yes, the foes are of a much greater level of expertise, and there are new and dangerous lands and so forth. Honestly though, I don't think rapidly improving or (shudder for saying this) nerfing equipment wholesale for class balance is a particularly good idea. I think equipment should enhance a class, not make it or break it. Does that make sense ?

4) Harm's Touch

I have noticed the resists seem to have improved, but the damage still makes me cringe in reality. Against a mob with thousands upon thousands of hit points, my harm's touch on one occasion wasn't even enough to taunt a Crawler from the wizard casting on it. 401 damage isn't that threatening. What do I think could be done to balance this ? Well, my main problem, is when I read the different formulas in regards to Harm's Touch and Lay on Hands. And it seems like we've been unfairly done by here. In no way do I think Lay on Hands should be downgraded. But it seems like a constant concern of all Shadowknights that Harm's Touch is often a token power. For a blessing from our God (despise us as he does), its not exactly earthshattering. Yes, it is handy, but I believe under-balanced in comparison to what the other classes have available. Warriors and critical blows, Wizards with endless nukes, Shamans with their DoT's, Rogues with their backstabs, and the list goes on. Is there some way that Harm's Touch could be given some dignity back perhaps. The idea of it being a damage to health converter is interesting, though even I have doubts that could be unbalancing.... I think it is honestly something to think upon. It is a constantly re-occuring issue.

5) Disciplines.

I think the Disciplines sound great as they are. And I will leave it at that. I haven't used them yet, as they aren't available.

6) Casting vs Hitting.

Mana costs, weaker spells, casting delay. Lions and tigers and bears, oh my. I like to use all of my abilities to my advantage, this includes casting of spells. Now keep in mind, at this moment, I'm not 100% sure of the exact changes on Test, and honestly, those are mostly irrelevant anyway. Its called Test Server for a reason. Just because its there doesn't mean it will be implemented. But, back to casting... I receive my spells later than my brother and sister Necromancers. Understandable, I have to devote equal time to my body and my mind. As such, I only have a small selection of the spells available to them, and it takes me much longer to learn them whilst I am trying to learn my sword-craft as well. But why should it cost me more mana ? Perhaps my spells might lack the strength of my brethren.. understandable once more, I do not spend the time they do upon casting,because I need skills for battle, for melee. So why wouldn't, in being less powerful, existing later, would my spells not take less time to cast ?

I don't know, I suppose I'm trying to tack on a RP motivated rationale there, but honestly, why would I essentially stab myself in the foot by using the lesser damage of a spell, when I can instead poke something in the chest twice with my sword in that amount of time ?


7) Miscellaneous.

Kick. I don't think its necessary. I wouldn't like to see an add on of a whole different plethora of skills, I would like to see an improvement upon the current lot of skills. Be it in their "cap" level, or their implementation (see Specialisation ).

Faction : Part of the joy of being a Shadowknight. Don't cry about the lack of faction. Who wants to enter those weak good cities anyway ? And its not impossible to raise faction, should you so desire. I did it accidentally in Highpass. The guards regard me warmly. Dark Elf who worships Innoruuk. And REALLY hates Orcs.

DarkForge: I agree. Cazic is a nasty place, and grouping and trying to even find all of the pieces of decaying is just absolutely horrible. At level 40, all I have managed to get is multiple sets of bracers. I have the melatite, I have my militia helms and my Qeynos shield. Its the decayed that keeps avoiding me. Is there even a chance of this being evaluated ?


Overall, I'm happy these discussions are ongoing. And whilst I love my class, I wouldn't complain if there were some little tweaks here and there.

After some thought, I think I know why shadowknights are considered a 'defensive' class. There are already too many 'offensive' classes in the game. I suppose it makes some sense being a plate wearing class, but anyway...

Someone earlier made an excellent point. If shadowknights are considered a defensive class, then why don't we have a defensive discipline?

After reading your (Alan's) post, it seems that you are reluctant to add new disciplines to the hybrids. If this is true, then that leaves us with modifying one of our other disciplines. A number of suggestions have been posted here, some good, some impractical. Perhaps if you were to list some limitations, the SK community could focus more on coming up with an acceptable alternative.

I myself am quite pleased with the majority of the changes being pursued on test. However, there are a few things.

Grim aura: At 22, 5 ATK is barely noticeable. Also, if I'm not mistaken, don't rangers get firefist at 22? Granted they are a more offensive class, but still. I'd rather see this spell replaced with scent of dusk/shadow. If paladins are meant to heal and buff, shouldn't SK's be meant to deal damage and debuff?

Lifetaps/Casting time: I think I have a hunch why the development team is against the 'lifetap-DoTs' and insists on giving us lifetaps instead. I think the dev team is planning on there being a fair number of interrupts, especially on the higher level ones, keeping us from chain casting. Before it was the mana multiplier to keep things balanced, now it's simply the casting time. Having a DoT, would allow us to only have to cast the spell once. To be honest, I don't really see how having lifetap-DoT's is unbalancing. I suppose the lifetaps are better in that there are no stacking issues, but the casting times are horrific. I assume that the dev team put them in as ways to lessen downtime, not as an alternative damage dealer.

Defensive cap increase: Excellent. Honestly though, I'd be willing to trade points here for increases in the other defense skills (parry, dodge, riposte). Increasing parry would be a great way to up our defensive abilities without raising our offensive power like increasing riposte would.

Bash: What happened to this one? Isn't it easier to defend with a shield? Why not increase the skill cap here?

Last but not least, uber raid encounters: If there's any topic that lights up the class forums anymore, this is the one. In fact, most of the changes I've proposed in this post have really been heading towards this issue. You said in the balance thread for pallies that you were reluctant to give any defensive disciplines to hybrids. Why? If that's the case, then what do YOU propose.

On a final note, I wanted to mention something that I don't think many people have before. Before Kunark, the big bosses were tuned (i.e. balanced) with the average level 50 class in mind. Also, there wasn't a terribly large difference between the classes in terms of power. However, with the addition of Kunark, and a max level of 60, the power of mobs scaled dramatically. For the most part so did the other classes.

The problem is that most of the skills (besides the disciplines) increased linearly. Against exponentially buffed mobs, the disparity is huge, especially for those without disciplines. The real unbalancing comes from the reduction of damage by percentages. i.e. 20% of 100 damage is a lot less than 20% of 500 (that's a reduction of 20 damage and 100 damage). You then compare this to, say, Leechcurse. Leechcurse is a completely linear discipline. It will always do X amount of damage and heal me X life. It doesn't SCALE with the mob like the defensive disciplines do. I hope I'm making myself clear, it's a somewhat abstract idea.

I'd often thought to suggest that when leechcurse is activated that we would hit 100% of the time. Well, after some thought, I don't really think that this is the solution. It still does not scale with the mob. Despite your reluctance, I'd prefer to see SK's/Pally's get one offensive and one defensive disciplines. Similar to Bards, Rangers, Rogues, Monks and Warriors.

Oh yes, before I forget. I noticed in the pally balance thread that you mentioned giving them full time crits against undead. While I'm all for this, you do realize that this then places shadowknights as one of the least offensive/damaging (in pure melee) classes in the game?

As for some random ideas of my own, I'd like to see SK's get triple attack and kick. Of course triple attack would need to be lower than a warriors as well as kick.

I don't see all of these changes going live but since this is a discussion board...
