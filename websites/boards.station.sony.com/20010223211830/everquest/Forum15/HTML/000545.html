Bards are probably the oddest, most unique, and reflective of the creativity and skill of the player classes in EverQuest. So... to answer the question, bards can either suck, or be awesome, it all depends on the person behind it.

As a level 30 bard, I can usually enable my group to fight creatures 2 or 3 levels above what they were fighting before I joined. Between magic resistance reduction, armor class reduction, slowing attacks, crowd control, and even charms I find ways to turn most situations to our advantage.

On the other hand, I watch other bards, from time to time... and most of them are useless. I see them double-wielding weapons and playing the regeneration song during combat, wearing full lambent at level 20... Sometimes I see them twisting songs... usually a combat enhancement, heal, and discord, while swinging weapons. Funny thing is, with the proper instruments, they could be doing as much or more damage... by playing binding or chains, they could be reducing party damage by 30% (which is a hell of a lot more than the non-instrument regeneration song will repair).

I have to assume that these are secondary or tertiary characters, who at best have gone to bard web sites and learned the "best" way to do things. Frankly, anyone who spends ALL their time barding, will never do the same thing all the time, because they realize that different situations require different approaches.

At this point, I play my bard 99% of the time... all my other characters have withered and petered out, because the sheer madcap nature of being a bard is too much fun for me to give up.

My only peeve is that some of these by-the-numbers bards with no clue build expectations with other players, who complain that I should be playing certain things while they fight because "so-and-so" does. Every once in a while, I even take the time to sit them down and run the actual numbers by them to let them know that what I'm doing is 3 or 4 times more effective than what they think I should be doing. Then I usually find a new group...

I believe the only way to understand a class, especially if they are very different than yours, is to play it past level 20. When I first started EQ, I thought enchanters and wizzies were worthless. Haha, now I know better! I now have a 38 enchanter and an 18 enchanter. I have a 45 druid, a 33 pally and a few younger accounts.

You know what? I feel MOST confident as my bard! I get bored playing any other class besides my bard.

Bards demand an attentive and adaptive player behind the keyboard. If you're the type that just wants to press Attack and maybe bash or cast a spell now and then, DO NOT be a bard. If you're the type that gives the game all your focus, and is constantly ready to do something beneficial, be a bard.

As for grouping with a bard, if you've ever grouped with a good bard, you would not even hesitate to welcome any bard in your group. Why? Number one reason: NO DOWNTIME! Do you hate having to sit and med between pulls? Do you hate having to heal up? Do you hate getting pulls that seem to slap your group all around? Then get a bard.

Bards are the fastest mana regenerators, if I twist my mana song with itself during downtime, the casters ALWAYS proclaim they can't believe how fast their mana comes back.

Every night I log in, and shout Bard LFG, I get tons of tells begging me to join their group. If I group with a bunch of new people I've never played with before, they all say over and over "This group rocks! We can take anything! I haven't been in a group this good before, there's no downtime, keep em coming!" I just smile, because I hear this every night....

So we can do a little of everything, but not do it the best, huh? Well, we do some things the best. We regen mana the quickest, boost MR the highest, charm the best, run the fastest, all without mana.

So please, next time you want to say a class sucks, try and group with a good character from that class, try and play one even past level 20.
