Without putting too much thought in it, I can imagine this thread will draw a lot of heat - mostly from the melee classes who seem to think I'm "whining", well maybe I am. I've played the game since release, leveled a Cleric up to 60 and seen every zone sans the Plane of Mischief(and Crushbone, shut up!). That includes clearing Veeshan's Peak and Sleepers Tomb. With that in mind I think I have the right to express my opinion on all issues caster related.

During the original release, before the onset of expansions, there were no real boundaries[spells/abilities] that made Class A more valuable than Class B. Obviously Warriors always had more hit points than Wizards and Clerics could heal more than Shaman could, but nothing earth shattering. Anytime I formed a group for exp it was 'first available', I didn’t pick and chose except to say, "no, I don't want 5 wizards."

Then came the first expansion, the Ruins of Kunark, and the obvious onset of class discrimination and favoritism. It may sound like a cheap shot, but I have to say this expansion wasn’t very well thought out before release. It's overloaded with items/abilities that make Class A exponentially more valuable than Class B, C and D. Who in their right mind wouldn’t take a 55 Warrior, with all those neat disciplines including Defensive, over a 55(or even 60) Ranger or a Rogue, with double-backstab, over a Paladin? Kunark also brought with it something we thought we would never see - items allowing melee classes to cast spells. Casters were flat out told that they would never see Clarity on an item, that would take away the need for Enchanters in a group, but now we see items that can cast Shrink and Invisibility being wielded by Warriors…

Scars of Velious was an even bigger slap in the face to casting classes. Verant chose to raise the bar on encounter difficulty without giving so much as a thought to how players would be affected. Well, I shouldn’t say they gave zero thought - I mean, they did consider that we might use the Donal's BP as a means of assisting us in these epic fights. They considered it so much that they admittedly designed certain encounters with it in mind, then they get offended when we *gasp* actually use the BP(/boggle). One major thing they absolutely did NOT consider was the impact of removing the hit point cap on casters. Up until this expansion Casters could contribute a noticeable amount of damage to a fight with spells or the combination of pets/spells - not anymore. What's 600 damage, or even 6K(assuming every Lure hits for max, ha!) to an NPC with 200K hit points(3%). Compare that 6K damage to the amount done by a Rogue(30K+) or Warrior(15K+) and your jaw will drop. No amount of proper spell selection will ever compensate for a MOB having more hit points than the combined total of everyone in the zone and quad-hitting for 700 a shot.

There are also NPCs in Velious that border on being 100% Magic Resistant, ummm… What the hell are they thinking. One of my best friends in the game, Azakar(a 60 Necro), tried to give me his stuff last night in Sleepers Tomb because he's feeling worthless. Why you might say, because he's fed up with NPCs resisting all of his spells. We comically refer to him as Azparse now, since he just sends in his pet and feeds some mana while standing under the MOB to let the real damage dealers know how they did that fight.

Why should any class be reduced to bean-counter status? Are we going to see Rogue only drinking fountains and a law stating that all Wizards have to sit in the back of the bus? I feel like I'm seeing the onset of racism(classism?) in a video game. We aren't making this up, some classes do literally ZERO damage to some NPCs, what's the point of them even going?

I would like to touch on the issue of items once again, having already mentioned the casting items given to melee classes in Kunark. Every caster I know, myself included is sick of the way items are designed in this game. Obvious favoritism is being shown to certain classes(namely melee) at every turn while others are left to wallow in the same-as-you-have-always-been abyss. There are items in the game that increase the effectiveness of melee classes on a percentage basis. If you get a Cloak of Flames you swing 36% more than someone without one. If you wield a Lamentation(9/19) you swing about 30% more than someone with a Ykesha. Percentage increases everywhere, and every zone is packed with melee items… Lets look at casters. Name one item that makes me 30% more useful than someone else my level, I'm waiting(someone please say Donals BP!)? I may have something that gives me a few more hit points or a bit more mana, no percentage increases for me. There is no casting haste, nothing that lets me cast a complete-heal in 9 seconds instead of 10, why? As stated earlier, I've been to and cleared Veeshan's Peak and Sleepers Tomb. Guess what spells drop in those zones? The exact same spells that drop outside those zones on exponentially easier MOBs. Where is the risk/reward? Why aren't there rare no drop spells just like there are rare no drop items? It's almost as if there are two distinct forms of government ruling EQ. The Democratic side rules the melee and the Communist side ruling over the casters. Why am I not allowed to be more powerful, via a unique spell or two, than someone not willing to take the risks I deal with daily?

Abashi, Absor, Aradune and all the other A's I'm missing should totally give up on the concept of ever balancing classes. It's too little too late in my opinion. What's the point of balancing classes when the items they achieve and the MOBs they face continually unbalance them? If you are truly interesting in balance then you should look at the game as a whole and not just Class A can do this and Class B can't, that’s not going to solve anything.
