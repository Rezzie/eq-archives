/em is suffering from her "take a troll post seriously" fetish today

Remove the druid class? Hmm...

Let me preface this monologue with a few pertinent facts.

1> My primary is a 35th enchanter; one of my 'lesser' characters is a 17th druid I'm leveling on the side so I can eventually teleport friends. I could have a higher-leveled primary if I so chose.

2> I'm not a "druid hater" strictly speaking. I have had a large number of negative experiences with players whose characters happened to be druids. It is my derived opinion that there is something about the druid class that draws players I would consider undesirables.

3> I nearly did not purchase EQ because of the class-intensive nature of it's character system. I felt then, and currently feel EQ has justified this opinion, that class-intensive systems are absurd for MMORPG environments in particular and would even go so far as to say they're impractical for RPG's period.

------------------------------------

The crux of what I'm saying here is this: there are too many classes in EQ and (at least partially therefore) not enough customization within the existing classes.

If you had fewer classes with more customization, I argue that you wouldn't have nearly the balancing problems that EQ's system suffers from. Such customizations would allow players to decide what abilities they're willing to sacrifice to have what bonuses.

For example, it might be argued that a druid gives up the best heal spells and the ability to rez in exchange for travel spells and better nukes. An oversimplification I admit, but I don't want to spend all day on this post.

Do you really need two classes to represent this? Wouldn't it make more sense to have a single class that, at some point in its growth (assumption: a true RPG will always have a growth-based system) can decide to persue one advantage (such as rez or travel spells) at the expense of the other? Or specialize in a "enemy" (clerics versus undead, druids versus summoned)?

In any class-based system (whether class-intensive or not), any (and I mean ***any***) change to one class affects the other classes. So doesn't it make a certain amount of sense that the few classes you have, the less overt balancing you have to do?

One of the biggest flaws I anticipated (and, unfortunately, have realized) in EQ was the fact that there aren't enough "nitches" for 14 classes. While I certainly don't advocate removing a class at this late a stage in the development (I've a proponent of "The only thing worse than nerfs are character wipes" line of thinking), perhaps future versions/competitors of EQ would do well to heed this.
