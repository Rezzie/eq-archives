About these lists:

First and foremost, understand that this is only a list of concerns to be brought to the Live Team. This is not a list of promises, and I'm certainly not telling you that any or all of these items will be changed or implemented.

Also, there are three types of items that I don't have listed here. Epic Quests, Magic Resistances in Velious and Mid-level quests. Those three items were mentioned by enough classes that I've pulled them out for separate examination.

All items are currently marked with NONE for status. Obviously we've already started working on some items, you've seen the lists from the patch messages on the Test Server. I will be updating the status for items early next week to reflect those potential changes and with any responses from the team. I just wanted to get these lists out to you fresh.

I want these lists to be final as you see them now, but I know there will need to be changes made. So this list is not final. But if you want something added to or removed from the list, please be convincing about the change. At this point I don't want to tack on too many more items, since the team is already reviewing the original list.

Thanks for the input so far.

Alan


Enchanters:


ITEM: DoT Pet.

DESCRIPTION: Something not unlike the clerical hammer spell, to add more to the ability of the enchanter to help during fights with extremely high hit point enemies. A short duration ‘pet’ that would work essentially like a physical DoT. To work, it would have to be immune to magic and riposte, and be for the most part like the clerical hammer in other ways.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Rampage.

DESCRIPTION: This line does not stack with other strength buffs (shaman buffs in particular). Is this as it is intended to be?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Illusion, Wolf.

DESCRIPTION: There has been a call to have this spell grant Spirit of Wolf. If it gets no Spirit of Wolf component, then perhaps it could work indoors (it is currently outdoors only). It should be noted that Enchanters have no foot-speed enhancement spells at this time.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Illusion Races Bug.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that some illusions don’t resize the character properly to people that enter the zone after the illusion is cast, causing some people to see giant gnomes or tiny trolls.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Undead Illusions.

DESCRIPTION: These transformations could be considered the territory of the necromancer, and maybe they should be replaced with something else, or remove them from the enchanter spell list.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Lull spells.

DESCRIPTION: These spells seem to be resisted too easily, more often used to pull than to break up a group. It seems that there should be at least a fair chance that the creature resisting this spell will not immediately aggro.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Whirl til you hurl and Dyn's Dizzying Draught.

DESCRIPTION: Players feel that these spells are too easily resisted and too easily broken. So much so that they rarely, or never, use it. And these spells make PCs spin, but not NPCs. Having it spin the NPCs would be a nice addition.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mesmerization spells.

DESCRIPTION: There are some targets that are unaffected by these spells, either due to level or race. The anti-undead spell lines give the character back a message when he tries to cast on an invalid target, and the spells don’t even cast (costing the caster no mana or aggro). Perhaps this approach could be used for mez spells as well.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mesmerization spells and overwriting.

DESCRIPTION: Are single target mez spells supposed to overwrite area of effect mez spells? It seems that perhaps they should. Minimally, higher level mez spells should overwrite mez spells that are of lower or equal level. Base this on the spell level, not the caster level, since the durations are not level dependent. Someone suggested that not having these spells overwrite was intentional (according to GZ) to make playing enchanters more of a challenge. If it is intentional, then leave it as it is.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mesmerization (Charm) Indicator.

DESCRIPTION: There is a call for a change of the color of a monster’s name when it is under the effects of mez and charm so that people know that monsters are under the affects of these spells just by looking. If it is determine that this would be too useful, making it to easy for the group, we might consider making it so that only the enchanter sees these color changes.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Low level Evocation practice spell.

DESCRIPTION: Enchanters don’t seem to have a spell like Flare with which they can practice their evocation skills early on. This may be intentional, though, as enchanters are not supposed to be experts at evocation.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Tiny Daggers:

DESCRIPTION: These items are unavailable in Qeynos.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Pet Positioning.

DESCRIPTION: Because enchanters can’t tell there pets where to go, they often get in the way. There is a tendency with all pets to stand right in front of their master. This is a bit of a pain when the caster is trying to target something, or just trying to see something. Can pets (all pets) default to standing behind their master?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: 'Charm wears off'.

DESCRIPTION: Players report that this message is sometimes sent long after the charm has worn off, after the creature is dead sometimes.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Reoccurring Amnesia.

DESCRIPTION: This spell does not reoccur.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Boon of the Clear Mind.

DESCRIPTION: This is a group clarity spell, but it does not overwrite Clarity. Either spell should probably overwrite the other, basically just increasing the duration (and the same should apply to the Clarity 2 spells). It also seems that this spell has a very short useful life span. Lowering the level of this spell would allow its use for longer before they get Clarity 2.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Failure Messages.

DESCRIPTION: Some spells (Lull series and stuns against giants are primary examples) give the same message on failure that they do on success. If it fails it probably should say so.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mask Faith.

DESCRIPTION: Players have been asking for the ability to mask their religion for a while. Part of the game is that characters have to deal with their religious choice, even enchanters. But, well, they aren't anymore because by now every single new enchanter is being told by their mentors to restart their characters as agnostic. That runs contrary to the basic concept of the world of Norrath.. The solution to the problem here (the problem being that there are now very few enchanters that are not agnostic) might be moving their spells so they don’t have to enter dangerous cities (which they can’t get into if they have a religion). The players are requesting a spell that masks their faith, making them appear agnostic to NPCs. Perhaps the best idea presented would be to create greater rewards for choosing the difficult path of being a pious enchanter.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Cantana (a bard song) and Clarity.

DESCRIPTION: There seems to be something wrong with the stacking of these two spells. It seems that the bard song will stack over the enchanter spell, but the song must be stopped or the enchanter spell won’t take.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Blanket of Forgetfulness/Memory Flux.

DESCRIPTION: Some players say that Blanket doesn’t work well enough, and some say that it does. But most think that Memory Flux is not effective enough.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Collaboration.

DESCRIPTION: This spell does not seem to have any affect in Skyfire, but it does in Kael Drakkal. Is this a bug?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Illusions and Link Death.

DESCRIPTION: Can illusions remain up during a link death and perhaps while zoning (thought would have to go into changing it when zoning. There are good and bad sides to that change, but if illusions zone, the players become responsible for how they handle those situations, rather than the software).

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: The Essential Enchanter.

DESCRIPTION: Some thought should be put into why an enchanter is considered ‘essential’ in a Kunark group, and perhaps change that. If this is determined to be a problem, then perhaps allowing some of the other classes to be better at the skills where they overlap with an enchanter (such as bards and crowd control...) would be a solution.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Resplendent Robe.

DESCRIPTION: The effect on this item is Rune IV, but it requires a component. Should items require components?

STATUS: None
