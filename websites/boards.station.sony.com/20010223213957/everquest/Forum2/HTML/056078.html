Ok, so I have pointed out "The Problem", so lets talk about some solutions.

Verants main fears:

1) If they do not make mobs Uber-Hard, high level people will just farm them.

Well, to stop farming, they just need to implement anti-farming measures. Do something like if the mob is green to the person who kills it, its chance of dropping its loot gets reduced 90%. Another solution would be to randomize the spawn points of mobs. If any rare could pop in the place of any spawn, well that would certainly cut down on someones ability to camp.

2) If the Uber players get bored and leave they will bad mouth the game and say its lame.

Verant has already addressed this problem with zones like Dragon Necropolis, Veeshans Peak and other places. There is no reason that entire continents need to be tuned for these folks, they will go play in the places that challenge them and drop the best loot for them. Sure most of us will never see these zones or the loot (which should all be no drop btw), but the zones we do hunt in will not require equipment of the likes that they have to succeed.

3) If the players in general are not challenged, they will get bored and leave.

Well, there is a difference between being challenged and having to enter a death trap. Death should be the penalty for doing something dumb, not the norm. With the huge agro in some zones, this is just crazy, there is nothing challenging about this, it about being lucky and memorizing the exact places you can and can not move and having and enchanter with you.

A perfect example is the right courtyard in Karnors, stand in the corner there and the whole frigging jail from the basement runs up to greet you. What is the sense in that? How would any mob that is in the dungeon below you even know that you are up there in the courtyard, do they have closed circut surveillience cams or something?

Make moving around in these places tricky, difficult even, but not stupid impossible with unreasonable things happening.

Other things that can enhance gameplay:

1) Do not make getting class gear impossible for the normal Joe.

As someone else mentioned in another post, Kunark BP's and Legs seem to be reserved for the uber (equiped) guilds with awesome tactics and limitless numbers of people. This class armour should be available to everyone, let the uber guilds have something better that ONLY drops in the uber places. Feel free to limit the number of drops, put level restrictions on wearing it, whatever, but at least let it be obtainable. After two years of playing this game I think I at least deserve a shot at it.

2) Quests (other than the Epic quests) should not require more than a group to obtain a item needed in the quest.

Velious armour quests require a minimum of 3 VERY well balanced groups to obtain. This is quest armour, not dropped armour. The very fact that it is a quest item should imply that you need to use your head, not just muscle to complete it.

3) Quests should never force you to spend endless hours slaughtering "greenies" and ticking off all the appropriate level people in the zone.

Quests should give experience. If the reward is a level 30 item, you should have to deal with level 30 areas to complete it, not level 12 areas. Is there some reason that if you are doing a quest you are not entitled to exp hunt at the same time?

4) Quests should appear and disappear and constantly be rotating.

A quest mob should not always give out a quest. However, new quests should be developed all the time and implemented, not huge quests, just little fun ones that have some minor (but worthwhile) reward. Maybe something like a stone of greater healing with one charge. By not always being available, or even given by the same mob or even in the same zone, you could control the "farming" of such a item.

5) Weapons of equal stats shuld be available in numerous different places.

Right now everyone wants a Jade Mace or Lamentation. How come there isn't 5-10 different blades/maces, all with different looks, that have the same stats? If nothing else, this would at least add some serious flavor to the game. There are some cool looking Kunark weapons (Kunzar Ku'juch) that I would love to carry if it had the stats.


Well I guess thats enough for now. I hope Verant reads this and finds some of this helpful.

I certainly agree with the title of this post. Even if classes are imbalanced, a skill set of a class is only about 50%-60% of what constitutes a character, the other 40%-50% of a classes abilities is determined by the equipment and weapons they are using.

It is pointless to even try to balance a class if the equipment a character is carrying is not appropriate for their level. A teens character using mid-40s or 50s level armour and weapons will have much more of a impact on how the game runs or plays out then any minor tweaking of said characters skill set or abilities.

Yes I'm talking about twinking, which for all intent and purposes really means cheating no matter how hard you try to counter-argue the point. Giving or allowing low level characters to obtain high level equipment makes what would otherwise be appropriately difficult encounters be a lot easier then they should be.

By no right should a low or mid level character be allowed to make use of items only obtainable by characters that are very high in level. Someone could argue that a weapon drop off a 45+ mob is of not a sufficient standard for a Lvl 45+ character, if that is the case then make the item/weapon drop off a appropriately leveled mob for the level of characters it is truly meant for.

From the first day Everquest was introduced, every single item in the game should have had a minimum skill level attributed to it. If for instance you have a 1HB weapon that drops off a Lvl 35+ monster, the weapon would require someone to have a minimum 1HB Weapon skill of 180 before they can even swing it, by simply just having a weapon not proc until a certain level just doesn't go far enough.

The more and more I do research on equipment and weapons for my character, the more I have come to realise that Everquest certainly is top heavy in regards to items a average player can look forward to. Nothing even remotely interesting is available from Lvl40- but from Lvl40+ everything starts opening up to a player. Throughout a characters history, they should be able to naturally progress from one good item to the next better one but all only obtainable from their own efforts. Sure you can buy that Lvl50+ mob dropped Sword or armour at Lvl13 but it is useless to you until you reach lvl 50.

As it stands, obtainablility of equipment is not evenly distributed through the entire ranges of levels, this grossly out balances the game and only rewards a player once they've spent a couple of months leveling hard to finally reach the level where they can earn good items for themselves.

I've already mentioned this exact same argument in another post, a player should feel rewarded for their efforts all throughout their adventuring career, not just at the last 20 to 15 levels they obtain.

If twinking was not possible, balancing of the whole of EQ would be a simple matter as everyone would be appropriately equiped for their level and you would not have the cases of players having characters more powerful then what they should be. Verant could easily determine what sort of weapons and equipment a certain level character would probably have and then the monsters would be balanced in difficulty accordingly. The problem at the moment is this is just not possible, the array of equipment available to any level character, regardless whether they should have it or not, is enourmous which is probably why all the creatures in Velious are balanced more towards the the uber equiped characters then your average joe.

So the only way balance Everquest and keep it balanced is to introduced minimum skill levels for every item in the game and makes sure they are can be obtain by the approriately leveled character that should be using them, not 20 levels higher then the person who truely should be using the item.

I would like to play devil's (Verant's) advocate here.

We want game balance. I agree.

But what can be done now?

First, balance classes would help. Hard to create new zones for the next expansion that all 14 classes can play in when people are only grouping with 4 or 5 classes (at the highest level).

So, ok, let's assume for a minute that within the next month or two enough class balancing will occur to make all classes playable and groupable to 60th level.

What then?

Maybe reduce the amount of experience required to level above 50 to help motivate us to keep leveling? Yes, it is ridiculous as it stands, but changes are also problematic. Suddenly all 57th level characters become 60th? Or, take away exp they have already earned to keep them 57th? This would be a tough change to implement, and no matter what change Verant makes, a significant portion of the player base will resent it.

Is the game still out of whack? If a druid, necro, wiz, mage, sk, ranger can go hunting anywhere a cleric, chanter, shaman, war, monk, rogue could, is it balanced?

Maybe, for the most part, it would be.

Still, plenty of zones that single groups can't hunt in. So many SoV zones are unplayable to the average casual player.

Is there a fix for zones that require multiple groups?

No, I don't think so. If one group can hunt for a great game experience, then two groups can massacre everything and farm the loot. Even making everything no-drop, the larger guilds will still need to get equipment on their main characters, or will still bring level 20 twinks along who will loot the no-drops themselves.

So there has to be zones that challenge the two or three (or more) group teams, and those zones must have loot that motivates people to find 20-30 friends to raid the zone. Believe it or not, some people do like to play this way.

But, these zones should be reasonable in number, and no single group should have to pass through these zones to play in single-group zones (read: Siren's Grotto).

What about time sinks? Camps like the Aegis of Life, or Testament of Vanear, Jboots, or Raster? Tons of other examples. All we do is camp greenies for no exp, no challenge, no fun, for hours on end. Can this be fixed?

Sure, by removing the quests entirely or by replacing with other quests. The first option is worse than having the quests - it leaves those of us willing to sink the time without an option. The second choice is better, but it would take tons of work, thousands of hours developing and programming and testing these changes.

Best gear being only available to people with 12 hours for raids, or with 30 friends all in the level range of 55-60?

Yep, this seems unfair to the 98% of us that don't have that advantage. We'll never get the good gear.

Is there a solution?

Probably not. Those players want to get better gear. Taking the uber gear out of the game is not fair to the players who have struggled hard and played hard to get there. Making it more available just makes it more available to twinks too. Any solution to stop twinking will also destroy the player economy, making it less likely for us casual gamers to get that gear than it already is.

Level limits on most items, and making most of them lore and many of them no-drop, would really reduce the uber-camping and the twinking. But at the cost of player economy again. Maybe that cost is worth it. I for one like the idea that when I find something better, I might make some plat selling the gear I am replacing. Or that I might give some of my gear to my 14th level paladin, just because I earned it, and I want to get more use out of it.

I could go on much longer than I already have.

My point is that there are tons of changes that could improve EQ.

Think Verant can make these changes now? Of course not. This would require massive rewrites of huge chunks of code. Rewrites of this nature are never called patches, or upgrades. They are called new versions. Going from 1.05 to 2.0. In other words, let's hope Verant plans this kind of stuff for EQ2, because very little, if any, will be implemented in EQ.

I wish EQ could be better. I fully support your desire to see improvements. I think we should continue posting these types of requests.

But, realistically, all that will change now is a few bones thrown our way to hold us over until we can start playing EQ2. Nothing else would be cost effective, especially since EQ2 is already in development. Hopefully threads like this one will make EQ2 become the game we all want to play.
