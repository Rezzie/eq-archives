What I mean is... most of the things you'll make with trade skills don't appeal to higher level players. They'd rather sit in guk and camp items.

Why can't tailors make magical leather armor? Why can smiths make magical plate? Why can't you use dyes to color the armor you make? Why can't a potter make ceramic arrows shafts?

Look at druids for instance. They have no class specific set of armor like other classes except what's on the plane of fear. What if you could tailor reinforced leather, add some viscous manna or whatever, and a bottle of green dye, and have it come out with green looking leather armor that has a couple stat bonuses? Maybe to make it you need the skill over like 150 or something..

What I'm getting at is it would be really nice if the items made with trade skills would appeal to high level players, besides tough arrows and some jewelry? Maybe if people could get similar items by making it themselves, camping for items in places might be less of a problem, and if people didn't feel they had to camp stuff they'd be more inclined to go out, explore and just have fun?

In fact, I just posted the following on KC's Blacksmithing Page not long ago:

----------

I was just thinking about how lame it is that smiths are seldomly needed after a person has banded. Not to mention that the market for banded armor is incredibly saturated on servers, and becomes more so as time goes by.

However, I just thought of an interesting idea that may help keep smiths useful throughout later levels. This could be achieved by implementing different types of armor that can be made from enchanted steel plates.

Like a jeweler gets his enchanted bars by having an Enchanter cast upon his various elemental ingredients, we smiths could have an Enchanter enchant our metal sheets. Depending on the enchantment (or possibly different type of metal used), the armor made would contain a different set of stat modifiers.

This idea is good for several reasons:

A.) Provides the smiths and Enchanters with something more to do.

B.) Introduces various forms of forged armor into the market place, each with pros and cons. Through this, the market is less likely to become saturated since different characters will want different pieces.

C.) Raises the demand in the economy for smiths. If there are forms of forged armor that have advantages over looted armor, then players of level 20+ would actually be interested in doing business with us.

So, what do you guys think? This is something that I would love to see. Also, if we had the ability to forge certain types of magical items, we would be greatly needed for people in need of that first magical weapon. I really think this would be great!

-----------
