quote:
What raid strategy could possibly rely on a paladin using Divine Aura?

It's called DA pulling. As Ganaiele said, mobs that hit insanely hard are the best candidates. A defensive warrior is not the best choice because they can't do it with their back to the mob.

Imagine being the person tasked with getting a 1200 point quad-hitting mob out of his spawn spot and into the ambush site. You are the only person slowly creeping into the lair of the dragon and you pray to your god for safety. The monster pounces and with that DA icon flashing you hope to make it back to your troops within the 18 seconds you have remaining in your life. Repeat after me... "adrenaline rush".

quote:
DA type spells do not protect against death touch.

This is partially correct. It is more accurate to say they don't protect against ALL death touches. Yeah, I know some people like to distinguish between "true death touches" and the simply "high damage HT-like" effect, but the fact is it still kills the non-protected person.

For those that don't understand what I'm referring to, there are actually 2 types of "death touch". One works just like Kargrog_Taeil says; you get hit, you die, nothing will protect you. The other is an extremely high damage effect that does something like 32,000 points of damage, thus effectively killing anyone not protected. DA will stop the latter, but not the former.
