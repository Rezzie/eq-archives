Oranaiche,

Your statement has a lot of truth in it. Bards and enchanters have a large taunting factor to them. Does that mean they are taunters? Not really. Taunters are the classes that you wish to be able to taunt mobs off another. In most cases, bards and enchanters do not wish to be the one responsible for taunting mobs off another. Therefore, they really are not taunters. They just have a high taunt factor due to the nature of the spells or songs that they are using.

Rangers, paladins, and shadowknights are taunters. They are classes whose role it is to taunt mobs off others. They do this in what ever manner that they can do. Because of their ability to use more then just a Taunt skill button, they are able to achieve this ability much easier then the pure melee taunt classes (warrior, monk).

In your particular case though, it is true you get the attention more often then others. The major reason for this is that many of the people who play taunter classes that you are grouping with still have not learned all the aspects of their class. They are still learning, just as you are, at the 20 levels. It is the case in higher level play, that those other players will be able to keep mobs off of you. Sure you will get hit. Sure you may die even there. But it will not happen as often as when you were at your current level. And when it does happen, you will notice that it is because much more is messed up then just an inability to get mobs off of you. Things do get a bit better for your class as you level. People will learn what they need to inorder to correctly utilize your beniefts.
