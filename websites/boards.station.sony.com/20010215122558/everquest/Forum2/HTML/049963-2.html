I am relatively new to the game, having only played for a couplr months. I am still trying out various race class combos to see which I really like, and want to commit to in advancing to level 60. Because of my real life experience in training with shaolin monks, I wanted to try out the monk character first.

Traditional monks are priests, plain and simple. Not only were they extremely proficient in unarmed combat, they were also skilled in weapons, and various techniques in penetrating armor, and shields. Although combat situations were avoided, they were also well versed in fighting strategy, and dealing with multiple opponents. To extend this to a fantasy environment, I would suggest some of these ideas to be considered:

1. Monks are priests, and should receive some spell like abilities, if not actual spells simialr to a paladin. Minor healing spells, calming spells, shield like spells, maybe personal combat hastes, food and water summoning, etc. I realize this would seriously upset the current balance and would probabally not be implemented, but when you look at the role of the traditional monk in history, and fantasy, you have to at least agree that they were priests, and should be given some of their powers.

2. Monks spend a considerable amount of time in meditation and should be granted an ability to simulate this. I would use meditation to increase HP regeneration, possibly as a method to obtain a low level saving throw versus any negative conditions they may have on them at the time. Also it could be used as a means to focus the mind in order to have a higher chance of success for a short while in trade skills, or other non combat situations. Perhaps a "tranquil" monk could pass by mobs that would normally attack them. Ie a tranquil iksar monk might be able to pass by guards in other cities that would normally consider them KOS.

3. Monks are capable of complex battle tactics and dealing with multiple opponents. Various techniques are used such as leading one opponents attack to hit another opponent, sweeping attacks, double kicks and strikes designed to hit opponents in opposite directions, etc. This is an area that is not really respresented and should be addresed. Dealing with multiple attackers is never as effective as dealing with a single one, so any area damage attacks would of course do lower damage. Also, there is a lack of stunning and knockback effects: monk attacks are precise, and usually have some kind of secondary effect. Strikes to the solar plexus, groin, throat, etc should at least stun the opponent or produce a small debuff (ie reduced mob effectiveness cause they just got a boot to the can, difficulty breathing because of getting a fighting baton in the solar plexus, etc.). While on the topic of combat effectiveness, the soft stlye of the art seems to be completely ignored, alhough it is the most effective style at high levels of mastery. Throws, pressure points, should all be utilized.

I would also like to see more martial artsy type items which enhance the hand to hand skill. Handwraps of differing materials (patchwork, studded, reinforced?) More variety in lightweight armor (although I recognize traditionally monks wore no armor), or a better natural AC.

I've babbled enough about this. In closing, I would like to suggest the following:

1. Monks get more special attacks which also produce some kind of special effect: stun, area attacks, chance to deal "critical blows".

2. Implement spell like abilities called meditations. Offensive meditation would the mind and body before combat allowing various personal buffs for a short time. Defensive meditations would increase HP regeneration, and grant mental focus (Int and Wis Buff?) allowing for greater success in non combat skill attempts. All meditations would have a high "casting" time, so would not really be useable in a meelee situation.

3. The ability to go tranquil, which would replace sneak (maybe just for the human monks since iksar aren't exactly tranquil) in the ability to go somewhere without being attacked by mobs that might normally attack you.

4. Allow more tailor made / smith made items useable only by a monk. Handwraps, martial arts weapons that use the hand to hand skill, better selection of armors, etc.

5. Monks were fairly self sufficient, and should be able to forage their own supplies. They sould never be as good as a ranger or druid in this regard, maybe give humans a cap of 50 so they can be in line with their iksar brothers.

Thank you for reading this post. Please consider some of these ideas.
