I am writing this assuming the current changes to the Hybrid classes on Test will go live. Furthermore, for brevity I refer to Paladins and Shadow Knights simply as “Knights”.

Some preliminary remarks. I feel that the changes on Test regarding skill caps for Hybrids are just and beneficial. I also feel that they in no way threaten Warriors, who will still maintain a necessary and desired role. Verant has been doing a fine job in their attempts, recently, to help Hybrids become more desirable.

The things I feel still require change regarding the Ranger class are as follows:

1) INCREASE RANGER DEFENSE SKILL TO 230 FROM 200.

REASON: Since Knights (and Bards) have had their Defense increased to 252 and Rangers have had their Offense increased to 252, and therefore Knights defend and Rangers offend almost as well as Warriors, I feel that a Ranger should defend as well as Knights offend. Therefore I think it is appropriate that a Ranger’s Defense skill (currently locked at the pre-RoK value of 200) should be increased to 230, which is the current Knight Offense skill cap. Knights will still maintain a significant AC advantage, both with regard to Avoidance and Mitigation, including approximately 300-350 more HP. I do not think that this position rationally can be argued down other than by an arbitrary prejudice. Rangers clearly need assistance in Defense, and balancing a Ranger’s Defense against a Knight’s Offense strikes parity between them. Rangers remain the “Offensive Hybrid” and Knights remain “Defensive Hybrids.” This fix (and it is a fix) just allows Rangers slightly better to defend themselves, about as well as Knights are offensively.

Defense pre-RoK: RNG/PAL/SHD/WAR = 200/200/200/210
Defense RoK: RNG/PAL/SHD/WAR = 200/252/252/252 (per Test)
Proposed Defense: RNG/PAL/SHD/WAR = 230/252/252/252

PAL/SHD RoK Offense = 230

The parity here is appropriate and not overpowering.

WAR = RNG = 252 Offense
WAR = PAL/SHD = 252 Defense
RNG Defense = PAL/SHA Offense = 230

2) INCREASE RANGER RIPOSTE SKILL TO 175 FROM 150.

REASON: When Kunark was release, Rangers received zero defensive increases. It was seen later on that this was a mistake. Our Dodge and Parry were increased, therefore, to the same proportions, as they existed with respect to Warriors pre-RoK. But Riposte was not increased, whereas the Knights’ were. Since both Warriors and Knights received 25 skill points in Riposte with RoK, it is felt that Rangers should also have received those additional points.

Riposte pre-RoK: RNG/PAL/SHD/WAR = 150/175/175/200
Riposte RoK: RNG/PAL/SHD/WAR = 150/200/200/225
Proposed Riposte: RNG/PAL/SHD/WAR = 175/200/200/225

3) ARCHERY ISSUES:

SUGGESTIONS:

A) Increase Archery skill cap to 252 from 240. There is no good reason that a Ranger should have the same cap as a Warrior.
B) Grant Archery a Damage Modifier similar to the Primary Weapon Damage Modifier. It is unfathomable to me how a 28 DMG bow with 8 DMG arrows can hit for 1 DMG.
C) Consider Double Attack with a Bow, or perhaps Double Attack with Discipline Trueshot.

4) HYBRID SPELL STACKING ISSUES.

SUGGESTION: It is unfathomable why Verant developed some very nice, unique and beneficial Hybrid-only spells, yet these spells do not stack with each other or with the most common Haste buffs in the Augment-line. Paladin Divine Strength does not stack with Ranger Strength of Nature, and Ranger Call of Earth does not stack with the Augment-line. Please reevaluate these Hybrid spells.

5) MODIFY/CHANGE JOLT RESIST RATE OR GIVE IT A SKILL CHECK.

SUGGESTION: Jolt’s resist rate or resistance check needs to be changed. It is currently highly resistible and utterly useless on Velious. On Kunark, however, Jolt works fine. Perhaps change Jolt to require a skill check as Evade does, and not a resist. In any case, Jolt needs to be looked at.

6) EPICS

SUGGESTION: Consider making the Ranger Epics usable in either hand, as the Warrior Epics are. The Ranger Epics are currently restricted.

7) UPGRADE TO FEET LIKE CAT.

SUGGESTION: Perhaps create a stackable AGI buff unique to Rangers, or at least an upgrade to Feet Like Cat.

My suggestions for the Ranger class:

Jolt at 39th--Improved Jolt at 55th

All melee weapons have a base max at 1x damage instead of 2x--starts to fix mudflation problems.

Smoother twink caps on weapon damage that fixes more than just the max damage on high damage 2h weapons. Again this is a mudflation fix that should help re-balance melees and casters and make the hybrid spells more meaningful.

More damage on hybrid damage spells--I would argue that a more limited range of very powerful spells is a LOT more useful than a broad range of relatively useless spells.

Smooth out the 'magic boundary' between too little AC (monster just pounds through for max) and too much AC (monster can barely affect combat). You can feel the difference when you switch from slightly blue to yellow monsters in all situations. It is the heart of the Ranger defense problems--unless you bring their defense VERY CLOSE to full tanks, it will still seem like you did nothing at all. The cliff (magic boundary) is just too steep.

The same works out for offense skills: unless your offense is sufficiently strong to overcome the monster ac, then it is like you aren't doing any damage at all (because you pretty much aren't.) Smooth the magic boundary.

Fix stats so they are meaningful--you make rangers spend a LOT of stats on agility when it makes an almost invisible impact on gameplay.

Fix defense and offense skills so there is a DECISION as to whether or not to trade off heavy armor v. light armor. E.g., if you wear plate, it makes no sense that you could dodge better than someone in chain or leather. Make dodge 3x as effective for leather as it is for plate...and 2x for chain as it is for plate (or something like that...you guys do the design, we just table talk.) Block/Riposte are arguably dex based, so use dex to determine outcome there. Make the curve smooth--if the floor is 75, then give a 1% chance to dodge (agi) or block/riposte (dex) and then increase it to double at 150 and triple at 225. If you had 3x3% then you're fully dodging 9% of attacks v. a low agility warrior's 1%--you start being able to FEEL the difference.

Note that these aren't specifically ranger fixes but instead address the game as a whole. The combat overwhelms every other part of the game and poor game design favors warriors and clerics over all other classes at very high level. Either fix the skill cap (uncap the 255) and make a real game, or consider some real fixes to the existing framework.

And...not to belabor the point but you don't seem to care about this: come up with a robust approach to PROVING your design works through modeling and testing. I will argue that you don't know what any change will do to the game because your modeling tools are either weak or non existent. This problem has existed on every online game and mud that I've played with the possible exception of GemStone 3 using the ICE Rolemaster rules. (In that case, the 'magic boundary' still existed, but it was ICE's fault, not Simunet's.)

The other advantage to fixing this is that larger ranges of parties can effectively group together because the level differences are visible but smaller. We really did enjoy grouping with a character 10-11 levels higher and it would have been even more fun if she wasn't SO MUCH MORE effective in combat. Meleers overwhelm the game, especially at high levels--time to fix it.
