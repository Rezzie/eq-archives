Baking Skill conclusions.

The following foods no matter what weight or cost will only last you for approximately 30.5 minutes according to Eric Plante who tested the following foods.

Muffins(0.5lb, 5cp)
Bread(0.5lb, 1.5sp)
Ration(1lb, 3sp)
Iron Ration(0.6lb, 1.5gp)
Meat Pie(0.6lb, 3sp)
Snake Egg(0.2lb, loot)
Wolf Sandwish(0.1lb, 1.5sp for bread)


The matter was petitioned to a GM (in case this was a bug) and he verified that ALL foods, including baked food, last about the same time!

Eric Plante arrived at these 2 conclusions
a) Muffins are the best food if we take weight, accessibility and cost into consideration.
b) No players should buy baked items from cooks at the price cooks should sell them.

I will add to this that at some times it is necessary to have a specific item made for role plying purposes to break the monotony eg. celebrations, guild meetings, etc So far this is the only reason I can see for purchasing baked goods.

The reason I'm writing this is because I am very new to this game and I chose to just play it without researching the game mechanics. Seeing as the makers of the game emphasise that the economy in their world is fair and balanced and encourages trading between player characters (with trading to NPC's being a secondary function), I made the following assumptions.

1. Heavier foods would fill you up more and thus would last longer between meals.

2. More expensive lighter foods would also last longer because of higher nutritional value and would not weigh you down as much.

3. Baked goods take a lot of skill, time, effort and resources to make there must be something special about them.

Silly Me :P

At level 3 and as a newbie to Everquest I decided to purchase iron rations instead of my usual loaf of bread thinking I could spend more time outside the West Freeport gates killing bats and collecting stacks of bat wings before I had to travel all the way back to the market to sell them and buy more food.

I can tell you now that I am truly sorry of my decision to buy Iron Rations as that money could have been better spent at such a low level. (Ouch!)

Ration vs Iron Ration

Ration(1lb, 3sp)
Iron Ration(0.6lb, 1.5gp)

Weight difference 0.4lb
Cost difference 1.2gp

Is 0.4lb extra weight off your back worth 1.2 gold?

As a newbie I have made many mistakes it seems. I "invested" in a portable spit thinking it could save me some money on food. I use the word investment loosely because it is probably just the opposite. Lets see what I can do now.
I can buy ten loafs of bread at 1.5sp each and go out hunting. If I kill a wolf and get it's meat i can combine a piece of meat and a loaf of bread to make a single sandwich(only one!).
What have I gained : 1 sandwich, duration 30 minutes
What have I lost : 1 loaf of bread, duration 30 minutes
1 piece of meat, 9cp
The cost of one spit.

The way I see things people say that baking aids in the roleplaying of the game but they eat a chocolate cookie and pretend it tastes better. How does this benefit their character. Do they get a sugar rush? Are they more energetic? Do they run/attack faster?

Benefits

Although I have many complaints it appears there are some benefits to the baking skill. It seems that if you combine 1 jug of sauces and 1 fresh fish you can make 2 fish fillets. I have heard that if you catch the fish yourself you can make a small profit. WOW! This just makes me want to throw all my skill practice points into baking right away! (note sarcasm)


As I write this I am currently a level 5 cleric with summon water spell. When I reach level 9 I will sell my spit and buy a summon food spell. This I believe would be a sound investment.

The way I see it unless you are at a very high level and just plain bored this is a futile skill to try to learn, especially so if you are a cleric.

Maybe fish rolls isn't the right thing. I know you guys talk about edible goo but fire beetle eyes and rats are hard to come by in Felwithe. As it is, you can never make baking skill a profitable skill. As 14 out of 15 fails, it means that I have to charge 15 times higher price for that one I sell compared to what I could if they all succeeded. Now, I don't really expect them all to succeed...but 4 out of 60 is WAY under what is reasonable.

Also, the fact that food last the same no matter the quailty etc of the food is just stupid. Someone has made a very lazy and silly programming short-cut here... they should try to make it so that differnet food had different abilities. One might cause you to heal faster, while one last longer and allow you to conserve your food intake.

The price difference etc indicated that... to hear that they are all the same is very sad for me. I didn't see a plat until I reached level 7 or 8, I could have spent those money more wisely if I had known.

They ought to fix the baking skill... It is plain dumb the way it works now.
