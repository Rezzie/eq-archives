So let's talk about necromancers.

As with all the other threads, I want to talk about class balance here, not about bugs or epic weapons. You may certainly present bugs and concerns about the epic weapons, but they will not be the main concern of this thread.

Unlike the other threads, I have a fairly good list of ideas already garnered from several other posts to start us out. It is not complete, I'm sure. But it is certainly a good place to start our discussion.

Also, we are not looking to make necromancers any more effective at soloing than they already are. And, well, the necromancer is a pretty powerful class as they are now. The primary issues that I see revolve around the battle with the boss monsters. But I certainly want to hear from necromancers with concerns about all levels of the game. And, once again, I have to warn you that since we will be looking closely at this class we may very well feel that something needs to have its power reduced to achieve balance.

Anyhow, here's what I have already.

Ignite Bones: Despite the coolness factor, this spell can't really be used on the big raids because it suddenly pulls the enemy out of range, and by the time you relocate your range, one warrior could conceivably have done more damage than the spell is going to do. The best solution is to make the skeleton match the monster, if we can find some way to do that. Fixing this one spell might solve a lot of concerns. If we can’t size the skeleton, then we probably should remove it and find some other cool way to make sure that the spell 'sticks' to the target to prevent chain casting and to make it more than just another DD. Note: Any change to this spell should also be made to Chill Bones (55).

Lifetaps: The number one request is to allow Lifetaps to land on the big monsters. If we are telling necromancers that they have a valid place at the final encounter because they can give mana to other casters, then they will need a way to regain mana. Lich and Lifetap are the way they do that now. I am not convinced that this is something that we should change. But this is the single most asked for thing by the necromancer community. Perhaps, if made in conjunction with the fixing the bug in Lich, then perhaps this could be balanced.

DoTs and corpse poofing: This affects everyone. Hopefully this is now much improved, but I’m not taking it off the list until I’m convinced it has been fixed.

Mana transfer: Again, if their ‘place’ against the boss mob is to transfer mana, then perhaps we should allow them to do it at a better rate. A 2:1 transfer rate might be enough. I'm not at all certain that this should be done in conjunction with any change to Lifetaps in those encounters, though, as a change in one makes the other far more effective and might just be too much of a change. But this could certainly be considered if we fix the bug with the Lich spells.

Infusion: Make infusion targetable. The necro might still see it as a 'worthless spell', but if another caster wants to sacrifice their experience for 1000 mana, they might actually cast this spell. Otherwise, those Essence Emerald’s will only be used for raising the dead.

Defoliation: There seem to be plenty of plants that this just doesn't work on. I fully understand how we need to limit the use of any spell that is overly efficient against any one monster type. But it might be worthwhile to evaluate the things that are not considered plants that maybe should be. I've asked the players for a list that they'd like reviewed, but I've received nothing yet.

Track Corpse: Broken spell. Is it going to stay that way?

Epic Weapon: The look and effect are not well loved in the community.

Lich Series: This series of spells is broken. It is more powerful than it should be. It takes the necromancer's hit points and turns them into mana. It was calculated at a certain ratio to be 'in balance'. Unfortunately, these spells were adversely affected by the reduction of damage to spells used by players on players. This change also reduced the hit points taken from the necromancer by Lich spells, so that the ratio of mana gained for hit points lost is nearly 1 for 1 (instead of about 3 for 2). This is a bug that we have chosen not to change in the past because it would be a 'nerf'. I think in conjunction with a serious look at the entire class and the balance of all the classes, we should also look at this series and decide if we should put it right. If it remains as it is, the necromancer is going to be limited in the improvements it can gain, as the class is already using a powerful and broken spell series. Note: Series includes: Arch Lich (60), Demi Lich (60), Lich (49), Call of Bones (34), Allure of Death (20).

Alan

Absor,

Thanks for revisiting the class.

First, I will only discuss the high levels since that is all I am accustomed to, post 60.

Before we begin, let us define the necromancer. What better way to do this, than to examine the spell lines available to us.

For almost our entire lives, we are responsible for dealing damage. We do not really do insane damage "in one shot" as that is the mark of the wizard. We deal slow death, through dots. Our Taps have a poor ratio, and as a result, are mainly used to suppliment our health.

As such, a necro is a damage dealer, in the same way that mages and wizards are, though more slowly.

1) The problem, at higher levels, is that its not how much you inflict, it's how fast you inflict it. In your average group, the creature dies in 30 seconds, and a 90 second damage over time spell does miniscule damage while still charging you the mana for a full dot.

Perhaps scaling the dots to do 45 seconds of damage, instead. (Similar to Envenomed bolt, and drifting death).

The dots would still rarely do full damage, but would at least do a good portion of it.

That being said, thats the only balance issue I can see at lower levels (and other necros can correct me if I'm wrong, as its been a while since I've been sub-50, and I'm sure things have changed).

2) The next issue, is Essence Emerald spells. Three spells require the use of this component: Trucidation (1100 point tap), Infusion (1000 point mana dump), and Convergence (Ressurection). Now I agree with the component cost for the third, but the first 2 need to be reconsidered.

Both Trucidation, and Infusion have a 10 minute recast time, which means that after memorizing it, you need to sit 10 minutes to use it. Would a wizard memorize a nuke that simply used mana but had a recast time of 10 minutes, instead of using their 700 damage spell with no recast time? No. In the same way, a necro will never memorize this spell, EVEN if it was free to cast (using just mana). As for trucidation, would you memorize a spell which gives you LESS than 1/3 of your full mana bar, if it took 10 minutes to repop up? Maybe, during those long velious battles, but never if it costs someone 4 hours of XP.

Solution? Change the component to peridots and star rubies for each (respectively), and cut down the cast time to 5 minutes. It'll still be cast rarely, but it will have situational uses.

3) Thirdly, we come to night time only spells. The main ones which we have issues with are:
a) Quivering veil of Xarn, and b) Levant.

Quivering veil of Xarn is an upgrade to our Harmshield spell which lasts 6 more seconds, turns us into a skeleton for the duration, has a minor damage shield, and a minor regen. It also, is a 10 minute cast. Unfortunately, if you have a regen spell on, it will NOT stack. Not only that, but it can ONLY be cast from 8pm to 6am norrath time. Would you really want to have a spell memorized that is meant to save your life if you can't rely on it due to the time of day?

Levant, is a self only spell like the druid spell Succor. It takes you to the safe spot in the zone. It is a high mana spell, and only useful for traveling one way.

Solution: Remove the nighttime only component and maybe increase the mana cost to compensate.

4) The epic weapon. I know, you said don't discuss it. The problem with it, isn't the look. I can live with it. The problem is the stats, and the effect.

The stats are slightly lower in comparison to the other epics, and in my opinion should have some mana removed, hit points added, and 5 more int.

The effect is our level 49 darkness spell with an additional 2 second casting time (9 seconds total). 1200 damage, over 96 seconds. The point of this spell is to snare our victim, meaning we need a fast cast. For the most part, necros use our 29th spell in order to accomplish this, and will not use the duck of doom as it takes far too long, and puts us at risk. It isn't even our best spell, as devouring darkness does 2000 damage, in the same time frame. And in case you didn't know, this spell is a HUGE agro, meaning, you never use it, unless you're soloing, or want to annoy your tanks.

Solution? Change the effect. Give it a held regen like most other classes have (why not, you give warriors fungal regrowth, and a small regen on Zlandicar's heart), give it effect slightly lower than regrowth, but better than chloro and call it "Revisit Death". This would make it a truely epic, and online with the effect of some of the others.

5) This is the big one. Uber Raids. Necros are not support classes, and shouldn't be one at 60th level when we weren't one all along. Currently we do nothing, or we dump mana, or summon corpses when its gone bad.

Solution: Make Deflux unresistable, its 320 damage for 198 mana, along with Vexing Mordinia. Touch of night being unresisted would be over powering, as would trucidation (as if we'd ever use it).

Also remove the illusion skeleton from ignite and chill bones series.

Remember, and this is VERY important: Not doing my first suggestion means we can not lich to dump mana or cast our heal spell as it hurts us. No income of hp means all our secondary fuctions shut down.

Also keep in mind that on average we have 1 dot which lands on a mob, which means 1 necro is useful, but what about the 2nd 3rd and 4th who want to come on the raid. So unless we can tap and ignite/chill bones, only 1 necro is worthwhile. Don't make us exlude our friends because of artificial limits.

Another topic to add here, is pets on these raids. Pets shouldn't be fearable, as they are mindless slaves to the necro/mage. Also, because of the inherantly low resists, pets die in 1 or 2 waves of a creatures AoE spell. Make them more than a 4 second npc on these encounters.

7) Finally, feign death. In Velious, mobs hit with such rapidity that casting feign death does nothing. Please make this spell useful in velious, or give us deathpeace.


Thats about it.

I love my class, and most of these things (besides the dot change) would not affect our power daily. Its simply making us viable where there are artificial restrictions placed on us.

Zrazzt WarDuck, Warlock of Tarew Marr.
