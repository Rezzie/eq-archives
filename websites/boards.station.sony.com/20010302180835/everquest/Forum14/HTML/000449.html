This post is not an attempt at bashing anyone. It's simply a chance to explain/discuss the nerfs clerics just got in the recent patch. If I'm wrong about them, please explain to me how I am wrong (notice, I said EXPLAIN...not flame with things like "you must be a n00b").

Nerf 1: Donal's bp -- this is pretty obvious; I don't think anyone's gonna disupute this one.

Nerf 2: Increased aggro from healing spells -- now healing spells (from anyone actually) will produce more hate than ever before and a cleric using a Cheal can be 99% sure that the mob (or mobs) will leave off the tanks and come directly for the offending cleric. If those tanks can't pull that mob off of the cleric, he or she is gonna die. I understand that VI says the aggro has been "broken" for some time. Exactly how long has it been broken? Since before Velious? Before Kunark? Even if it was broken sometime between Kunark and Velious, then all the mobs in Velious are based around that problem. VI "fixes" what is broken, but doesn't seem that interested in re-tuning the mobs created as a result of that bug.

Nerf 3: Reduced hate/taunt from tanks with weapons that proc -- ok, now you've used that Cheal on one of your tanks and the mob is on you. It's the job of that tank(s) to taunt that mob away from you. VI has lowered a tanks ability to taunt with this nerf, ensuring that barring some miracle taunt, the cleric is gonna die.

Nerf 4: Use of Cheal reduced: (call this one a "nerf by association") becaue of #'s 2 and 3 above, a cleric is going to be hesitant about using Cheal. Many clerics (notice "many", not all) will use Cheal less and less because, let's face it, no cleric wants to die after they cast one heal. The end result...Cheal is slowly phased out. It's no secret that many people think Cheal is overbalancing and want it removed, but to just flat out remove it from the game would be insane. The only way it could be done would be a gradual decline in use until the percentage of clerics who use it becomes negligible.

Again, I welcome any discussion about the things I've just pointed out. I want to *understand* why these changes have improved game play and made the game more fun for everyone. I know that I haven't experienced the post 50 game yet, and so my conclusions are drawn from my own experiences, the postings of rational, 50+ clerics, and discussions with 50+ clerics in the game.
