While I'm not a fan of memorizing things by the branching menus -- I can't figure out who it's practical for, honestly -- I love the spell sets. I've got them for every day use, XP groups, raids, special resistances, LDoN, etc.

One nice tip about them: If you demem ALL your spells, and just memorize a few spells for a special set, when you select that set, only those spell gems will be replaced.

That lets me, say, automatically memorize my chest spells in LDoN without having to redo the other ones (typically Harvest, my nukes, etc). My shaman has just SoW by itself this way, so when he gets a request, it takes one click to memorize it, and as it's a single level 9 spell, it takes a fraction of a second to do.

If you have one slot where you swap spells in and out duirng a fight, the best thing to do is memorize a seperate spell set with each of your most common swaps in that slot. Then when you change spell sets, you only have to mem the spells that are different from the ones already memmed.

Another thing is that if you leave any gems empty in your saved spell set, those gems will not be affected when you load that set. Any spells in them will stay there.

You don't have to sit and open your book to select the spell you want to swap in.

You can keep fighting as you right click on the book icon and select the set you want to load. You only sit once you select the set and stand right back up once the set is loaded automatically. Sit. Mem. Stand. All with one click.

For example I have one spell set named Gate with Gate in slot 8 and the rest empty. I also have another set named Pet with my current Pet spell memmed in slot 8 with the rest empty.

Regardless of what I have memmed in all the other gems. When I need to load Gate, I just right click on the book icon, select Load Spell Set -> Gate and click. I automatically sit, gem 8 clears, Gate is memmed into gem 8 and I stand. The other 7 gems are unaffected.

I spend less time sitting in places where sitting may be a bad idea.
