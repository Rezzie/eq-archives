That said, most of the experience changes are great. I wish though that experience were scaled _per mob_ in a zone based on more than on level, since some mobs are just tougher than other even at the same level and should reflect that in the experience given. Owell, hey.

Now for what I'm about to say I'm sure I'll get flamed by alot of people. I'm rather sad to see the experience penalty go for certain classes. This probably has alot to do with a strong enjoyment of D&D and games based on that, but the removal of this makes EverQuest seem less and less like an rpg to me, at least the ones I've played. The experience penalty was a convention in most of these games, because it offset the _better abilities_ of these hybrids based on a parent class or the fact they had more abilities. As Verant has now stated the hybrid has never had much of a benifit when compared to say the warrior so the penalty was deemed as a double whammy for classes that in several ways were inferior. Instead of removing the experience penalty, I feel it would have been better if they had looked at the hybrids and given them abilities that _justified_ those penalties. I'm not sure how others feel, but to me experience is the means to an end, getting better skills and better abilities as you level, not the end itself. So I guess really it's not that I'm sad to see the exp penalties go, but that a chance to differentiate and create more interesting classes was wasted.

On a similiar note though, I think it's a great idea that this extra experience is "created from thin air" and not exacted from the group. I always felt this was uncalled for. Penalties like that should never apply to the whole group, only the individual class.

In conclusion I applaud Verant for the ability to sensibly reevaluate these issues and make such great changes! I hope it continues

And by the way, if you want to fight "inflation" and still give players better money/loot in dungeons, just give them _something_ to use that newfound wealth for. Give shamans better utility potions, enchanters the ability to enchant weapons/items, and mages the ability to summon things the player would actually want and you will finally get that player economy rolling.
