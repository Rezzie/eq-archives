Okay so the title stinks but at least you were interested enough to click on it. Here Goes.

Since the days when a Multi-User Dungeon was around (for those young, Pre-EQ) there has been a method of having a Clan Hall/Guild Hall (whatever you want to call it). Of course it cost the guild their money to build, furnish and setup shop but in the long run a finished hall would provide Rooms for meetings, Message boards (well that is outdated), a merchant, trainer and banker.

Now we zoom into 2004 with yet another expansion on the horizon (some say it is the last, who knows). And EQ has nothing remotely like this. As a guild member I am forced to wade through the people to use the bank, merchant and to train. My thoughts are, why not add it into EQ where a guild is charged XXXX pp for initial building and another XX pp per item. Inside the guild can have a gathering pit where items no longer needed but still worthwhile for a newer person can be tossed and taken as necessary. A bank where the guild members can actually put money into their account or the guild account. A trainer for the skills (no spell merchants)

If in fact SoE decides to not do any more work on EQ, hopefully they will think about this concept for EQ2.

Please leave the flame-throwers to the Joke emails and consider this option EQOA has player houses built in where you can store your gear and other things.

This was and probably continues to be a common problem in DAoC where guilds can have shared vaults. It's a social problem, though, and not one I would expect the game producer (Mythic or SOE) to address beyond giving the players the tools to police themselves. Example: various levels of rights within the guild, a certain level required to access the shared bank. This was implemented rather well in DAoC, a Mythic first.

Housing, on the other hand, sucked rocks. For housing to work within a roleplay environment or even for social reasons, the houses must be placed throughout existing zones. A seperate zone (instanced or otherwise) eliminates any reason to have a house to begin with. Instead, I would hope they allocate "lots" throughout the various zones where housing would be appropriate, preferably grouped together as "towns", and allow players to erect homes on these lots. Vastly more social possibilities than shoving housing off into some distant zone by itself.

And we all know MMOs are little more than glorified chatrooms whose continued existence depends largely on the ease with which players can socialize. Look to AC2 as an example of how a producer can completely frell up (tax to Coyote is in the mail) an MMO by making socializing cumbersome...

I think this is a good idea - lets touch the points:
quote:
My thoughts are, why not add it into EQ where a guild is charged XXXX pp for initial building and another XX pp per item.

Good Idea. It would help to eliminate some pp from the game. The only coding (I would think) would be in creating the instanced zone. Once your guild paid the required money, that tag would get "Switched on".

Add a stone in PoK (the guild hall) where every guild that has the "tag" switched on can click on that to go to their instanced guild hall.

Depending upon what emnities your guild buys would determine which features would be available: Trainers, Bankers, Merchants, Gathering Pit, etc . . .

quote:
the guild can have a gathering pit where items no longer needed but still worthwhile for a newer person can be tossed and taken as necessary.

Excellent Idea. Make it work like this though. Anyone can toss an item in. Doing this puts an augment with the guild tag on it. This makes it
No Drop and slightly weaker (in that you cannot fill that slot with an augment). Only the guild leader AND (if possible) the original person that put the item into the pit can remove the augment (allowing it to return to its normal state).

If someone deguilds or is deguilded, than any tagged item they have returns to the pit (the augment checks against the tag). If someone deletes the character, than any tagged item they have returns to the pit (just have as part of the delete process a check to verify tagged items and return them to the pit). If someone's character is inactive for say 14 days, than any tagged item they have will return to the pit (Not sure if the accounts can be queried for this while the account is inactive or for while the player is playing an alt).

This will prevent the "rampant theft of items for personal gain" with the exception of the guild leader. Of course if you can't trust the guild leader . . .

It will also prevent the I died and couldn't log on for 8 days so everything poofed excuse or I hate this guild and game so I take everything and just retire that toon or cancel my account.

quote:
A bank where the guild members can actually put money into their account or the guild account. A trainer for the skills (no spell merchants)

Again, have these "Extras" be things you buy.

Buy a banker where your guildmembers can access thier personal account and access the guild treasury for deposit purposes only. Only the Guild Leader can make withdrawels from this treasury. Again if you can't trust the guild leader . . .

Buy a trainer. who is Either 1) Universal, can train all classes, but is not considered a guildmaster. or 2) have a sort of "Training stable" in which stands a trainer for each class. Either way would be only a single purchase (be nuts to have to buy each class a trainer seperate).

Buy a merchant. Merchant would have an inventory of nothing, but would by and resell items at normal merchant prices. Only special thing here is Guild Leader can "Purge" the merchant's inventory (individual items at a time or the entire stock all at once). This merchant is of course immune to server resets.

This would allow folks to dump items that have no augment spot (and therefore cannot go itno the pit), but might be useful to other people for trade skilling, question, regents, etc . . . (Bone chips, Pelts, etc . . .)

All in all, it would require a lot more thought, but I like the idea as a whole.
