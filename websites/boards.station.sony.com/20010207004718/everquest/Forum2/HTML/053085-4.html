Absor, I think that you have overlooked a few issues that the 50+ monk community has. Our place in the high level grouping game has been lost. The following section is not a plea or whine to increase monks abilities by comparing them to other classes, but instead a few thoughts to let you undertand where monks stand in the 50+ game. Suggestions on how to make monks fit into the game better will follow.

Warriors, paladins, and shadowknights all 'tank' much better than a monk. Their larger numbers of hps make complete heal so much more effective that even if they did have lower defensive skills it is more mana efficient for them to take damage. The monks ineffective tanking is only amplified when fighting "uber" mobs that hit for 600+ damage and miss rarely, in which the monk is dead before there is even a chance to heal him. This combined with the fact that the armor realeased in kunark and velious allowed the ac of the "tank" classes to exceed that of monks by a significant margin leaves monks undesired in the majority of situations where a meatshield is needed.

Monk damage is also questionable. Obviously rogues are meant to deal absurd amounts of damage now, that is their defining characteristic which makes them desireable and "cool." Rogue damage has been shown to nearly double that of a warrior, making them more desireable in certain situations. This gives rogues a "place" in the high level game, that of a damage dealer. Monk damage is considerable, but is on par with that of a warrior, not of a rogue. In fact, monks deal such a little amount of damage more than a warrior that the monks "place" and desireability in damage dealing is compromised by the warriors increased ability to absorb damage and be healed efficiently. Some of the new weapons in Velious available to warriors even exceed the damage dealing of monks.

The monks "old" role of pulling with assistance from the fiegn death skill is no longer effective in velious, and has been shown to be frowned upon by game designers.

Tell me then, what role do monks play? They cannot deal as much damage as a rogue. They cannot absorb damage as well as the tank classes. They seem to be headed towards a rogue/warrior hybrid that deals damage somewhere in between these two extremes and tanks somewhere in the middle of the spectrum as well. However, they are not outstanding in either aspect. They are left in the dust in both damage dealing relative to rogues and damage taking relative to the tanks. Even if a group did desire the flexibility of a member that can tank and deal damage, the current monk is simply too poor of a compromise.

What can be done to give monks a niche in the current 50+ game? Since they are a melee class it would probably be an enhancement of their melee abilities, ie. to recieve damage effeiciently and/or to deal more damage faster. How can this be done without stepping on rogues and tanks toes, so to speak? The truth is the designers have created a large problem in the game. Specialization is key in the game, as it creates more efficient groups. There are only 2 things for a melee class to specialize in, damage taking and damage dealing. These two area now have resident specialists, rogues and warriors, which leaves monks out in the cold. Note that doing the most "toe to toe melee damage" is hardly a specialty and I see it as an excuse to throw monks to the side when there is no other place to put them, similar to any claims that monks are valuable because they can fight well naked.

If monks recieved a bonus to their damage they would also require one of two things; an evasive ability, or increased damage taking ability. This results from the fact that when dealing more damage the monk will often be at the top of the aggro list and the focus of the attacking mobs attention. Simply recieving more defense moves monks into the territory of the warrior. Giving monks more ability to deal damage and an evasive ability simply makes monks a rogue clone.

I wish to be niether a warrior nor a rogue.

The only way I can see to make monks recieve a place in the 50+ game is to increase their damage without increasing their aggro generation. This also must be done in a flavorful, cool, and "monkly" way.

Option 1) Make npcs unable to dodge, riposte, block, or parry monk attacks. This goes in line with the claim that monks deal the most toe to toe damage in the game. A martial arts master should be able to anticipate these evasions just as they are better at performing them. Effectively this makes monks deal 10-30% more damage in an "invisible," monkly, and cool way. It generates no more aggro then we already have (which is arguably too much). Of course we can get this same damage bonus simply by standing behind the mob with the rogues, but this carries problems. When the monk does attract the mobs attention, the mob is now facing the rogues and severly hampers the damage dealing abilities of the rogue and finally the whole group. Allowing monks to deal similar damage behind and in front of a mob allows monks to get this bonus in a group friendly and "monkly" way. I prefer this route.

Option 2) Give monks a damage bonus to the offhand attacks. Not neccessarily the same damage bonus that all melees currently get for the main hand, as this could boost monk damage beyond rogues with certain fast weapons (moss covered twig and celestial fists). This suggestion also goes in line with the martial arts master theme, being masters of dual wield and coordination. Since there would be no more attacks then monks currently have, aggro would not be increased at all.

I have the overwhelming feeling that the "spirit" of the monk class has been lost to the use of weapons, rogues incredible damage, and warriors incredible ability to absorb damage.

I believe you have already seen the problems with a monks aggro and lack of evasion in other posts and are looking into fd's effectiveness in the higher level grouped game. If I am mistaken please adress these issues. Of course there are other issues, but I believe they have been covered already in other posts. It was only the fact that the above issues were being shoved out of the way, either to ignorant posts or poor articulation or any other reasons, that I wrote this, so here it is.

The major problems I see with the Monk class become evident mostly at higher levels (50+). The following observations and suggestions deal exclusively with that level range.

There are 2 things which I believe need to occur before the monk class is anywhere near balanced: 1) Persuasive reason for a monk to be 1 of 6 people grouping at higher levels, 2) Ability to stay alive in boss mob encounters for a reasonable amount of time.

To address 1:
Currently, Monks are invited into groups to provide melee damage, control the numbers of mobs pulled to the group, and in very rare situations absorb melee damage. As you have heard here, there are other classes which now can fill all those roles in equal, if not greater, capacity. Warriors can now come close to equaling the melee damage of a monk, Call of the Hero and the uselessness of feign pulling (as per Verant Interactive's stated objective) gives the warrior similiar-if not greater-abilities in pulling, and there is no question at all of the warrior's superiority in damage absorption. Add to that the warrior's ability to taunt and there is no question of his desireability over a monk in most situations is there? Of the 3 reasons I listed above for monks to be in a group, only 1 is greatly increased by having more than 1 person doing it: melee damage. In this category the monk is an equal or slightly higher choice over a second warrior yet still FAR, FAR below a rogue. Why then should a Monk be invited into a group?

The only 3 options really are: increase damage output, increase pulling abilities, or (and this is the one probably least desireable) increase defense abilities.


To address 2:
Observation-Many of you are familiar with Ixblat Fer. In a fight against him recently, I purposely stayed out of the fight for a period of time. This allowed the other melee characters to get ahead of me in damage dealt, yet when I began to actually fight, he turned quickly onto me. And when he did that, I died in less time than I could push a single button. Lag was an issue here but lag happens everywhere and it's downright pathetic to not even live long enough to twitch your finger over your defensive discipline button.
Problem-"Ranger Syndrome." Monks do their damage in such a way as to cause significant hate from your given NPC and can neither absorb that nor negate it in any way. Feign death does NOT work for this as it used to. The ONLY practical way to avoid this hate now is to turn attack off at some points and back away from the enemy. That, of course, makes monks inferior to most melee classes in terms of the amount of damage they can provide.

The options here are: reduce the amount of hate incurred by the monk without reducing damage output, provide a way for the monk to reduce his hate ("evade"), increase damage absorption abilities, or increase the damage such that it's worth the extra hate incurred.

I realize this is more vague than specific but I believe it paints the larger picture accurately. These are the problems; we would all like to see them fixed and there are many good ideas in this thread with which to do so.
