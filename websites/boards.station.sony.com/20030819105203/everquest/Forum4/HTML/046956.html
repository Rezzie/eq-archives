When in doubt, roleplay.

I know... it sounds simplistic... but developing your character's persona and history publicly can be very rewarding. You can always spend a couple pp to buy a couple stacks of cheap drinks and shouting for people to come hang out with you. Even in areas that are generally hustle-bustle, got-to-meet-imaginary-deadlines sort of places, you'll almost always find someone who is just as bored as you are... or someone who feels like roleplaying. Especially in places like the Plane of Knowledge.

My personal favorite place to chill there in POK is right on that little bridge between the bank and the nexus stone. I'll sit there for hours on end, emoting like there's no sun rising outside my window and getting other roleplayers to notice my efforts.

There are a lot of people on my server who now recognize my 52 rogue as "that poor bloke who's always sitting alone and admiring the view in POK"...

Sure, it doesn't help me level any faster, but it certainly gets rid of any feelings of tedium the exp grind can generate on EQ. *IF* you can find someone who appreciates your efforts, as I have. Many of them are now my regular group members. All to the good.

Quite honestly, if you are noticing the grind in the 20s, EQ might not be your ideal game.

There are ways to progress relatively fast, but the Catch22 is that the faster you level, the more repetetive the process of levelling is, and the faster you reach higher levels, the faster you hit the point where you can't level fast any more.

You might want to try playing a very solo-capable class, like a Necro, Druid or Beastlord. If you started one of those today. you could easily have it in the 30s by the time LDoN comes out, even untwinked.

On balance, though, if you find grinding for levels tedious and don't want to be powerlevelled, the only thing to do is to forget about levelling and just adventure. The levels wil come more slowly, but the fun will come much faster.

Really want a challenge? Roll up a new character (any class and race you want). Then have that character take the following oath:

"I will only use equipment that I crafted myself, or quested for myself, or looted off a creature that I killed."

I did this with a Halfling Ranger. He's 25th level. He has never been to the Bazaar. At least half his armor is still the newbie quest stuff. He made his own bow and arrows, and quested for, or looted everything else.

He has a story to tell about every piece of equipment he owns:

If you ask him about his Longsword (Gnoll Slayer), he will spin a tale of a daring rescue of a young boy, being held by Lord Elgnub in the bowels of Blackburrow. Or he might speak of the enchanted Tomahawk he and a friendly Bard took off of another powerfull gnoll in High Hold Pass. Or if you buy him an ale and ask him about his armor, he might tell of the horrible creatures that roam around the place know as Unrest....

Mind you, this is a very difficult path to choose. Certainly not for everyone. But I find it very rewarding.

I find that saddling up and going where you have never been livens things immensely.

I recently ran all over the world doing my Tashania quest. Basically, for any non-Enchanters, it requires you to go all over the world, to various good/evil/neutral cities, and collect coins. Combine them in a quest container. Take that coin to Neriak, Erudin, and then the Temple of Solusek Ro, turn it in, and get the spell (which is no-drop).

I went to places I had never been (Lavastorm, Gukta, etc.). I was too high to adventure in most with my main, but I have lots of new places to take alts now. Really broadened my horizons and refreshed my interest in the game.

I think your problem might be that you're confusing the "Grind" with playing the game. You can earn experience and loot without the grind, it just takes a little more work up front. Take decent people that you've met while leveling up and go someplace interesting. EQAtlas is a good resource for locating zones of the proper level (hint: dungeons are frequently more interesting than outdoors). It's not a grind if you're having fun!

Alternatively, if you simply feel you must go to the most tedious, populous places to level, take some time out now and again to hang out in lowbie zones offer buffs, try to help out newbies with tips, etc. Often a short change of pace will be enough to revitalize your energy for things. I've met a few friends this way, and it's fun besides.

Remember, it's a game, if you're not having fun, it's time to stop.
