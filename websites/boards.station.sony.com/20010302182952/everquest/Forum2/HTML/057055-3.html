I have tried to explain this before, with very poor results. Bards are not hybrids, they are pure casters, plate wearing casters like clerics.

Melee classes:

Warrior, Monk and Rogue: Dual Wield(2 hands) X Double Attack(2 attacks) = 4 hands worth of offense (note that monks and warriors can also get the 4 hands of offense by double attacking with a two-handed weapon)


Hybrids:

Paladin and Shadow Knight: Two-handed weapon X Double Attack = 4 hands worth of offense

Rangers: Dual Wield X Double attack = 4 hands worth of offense


Pure casters:

Cleric, Shaman, Druid, Necromancer, Wizard, Magician, and Enchancter: Two-handed weapons X one attack = 2 hands worth of offense

Bards: Dual Wield X one attack = 2 hands worth of offense

Now lets look at hit points. There is no doubt in anyones mind that a naked and unbuffed bard's hit points are much more caster like than melee/hybrid like.

Please stop refering to bards as hybrids, not even the other hybrids mention bards when they talk about hybrids. It is obvious that bards are "pure casters" as intended by the vision, and since that is obviously the intention, there is no need to make us lesser than our so-called enchanter parent.

As a side note, what would our melee parent be, we have such a diverse set of low level (some so low as to be useless or nearly useless) that it would be hard to definitively determine our melee parent. For those of you that think it is rogue, guess again. Rogues cannot track nor wear plate.

The plate was probably given to the bards to make them survivable. Due to the continuous singing (casting) we draw tremendous amounts of aggro. Having decent armor class keeps us alive.

Furthermore, as with most pure casters, late in life melee is meaningless anyway because bards do not have weapons out, they are using their instruments the way casters use their focus or int/wis/mana gear. Bards in combat do about the same melee damage as a druid or a cleric who is using a two handed weapon. It is only fair to compare all without shield, since the shield is an option that the character chooses whether or not to equip.

If you are trying to compare the damage that a bard does in melee favorably to a paladin or a shadow knight on the basis of we both get two attacks, you must be kidding. Because neither class gets dual wield, most will opt for a two hander that rocks (an option that bards don't have and even if they did have that option, they would get only one swing) or take a class specific one-handed weapon like an ebon mace or moss which has its stats based on the fact that the class can't dual wield. If the developers thought of bards as hybrids, we would also have similar class specific weapons that we could dual wield.

As far as armor goes, our armor selection is similar to that of clerics, but no one is accusing clerics of being hybrids. Allow me to summarize the things from this thread that I have either stated or learned. Absor, if you have gotten this far perhaps a this summary will be of use to you.

1) Bard players tend to really like variety. That is, after all, one of the greatest attractions of the class. Hence Bard players will tend to really really hate becoming 'Johnny One Note' at higher levels - even more than everyone else does (since a narrowing of role is an unfortunate result of the game design).

2) Bards have the potential roles of reducing downtime, buffing in combat (and at least the healing song will always stack in combat where it functions like a 1-200 point hit point buff), aiding in travel and corpse recovery, meleeing, using damage songs, snaring, and doing crowd control (arguably snaring is a form of CC. Oh .. and being a mana battery.

3) Due to the increasingly ineffective nature of the bards songs - buffs not stacking or being so much weaker than commonly available buffs, high resist rates and limited options on crowd control and snaring, lack of hit points or defensive skills to limit tanking, lack of damage dealing capability that scales with level (gosh i wish i'd get more than a wince when i play - i'd really like to konw i'm doing something) - bards tend to be shoved increasingly into the mana battery role as a matter of not only peer pressure (from the casters) but simple efficiency.

Just a thought for something new and interesting - but then ..i'm a bard .. i like choices.

well this is probably a done thing by now but here is a bit more to chew on and possibly spit out.

Sylok,

While our skills may be shared with different classes the hybrid term is used to refer to classes that have melee and casting ability. A shaman is NOT a druid/enchanter hybrid (healing and spirit of the wolf from the druid/haste and pet from the enchanter) but instead considered a pure caster.

Sylok said:
"we get things from pure casters"

Yes, we do, however, getting their melee skills and a caster's hit points is not what I would consider a great gift. Bard songs have such a short duration that they are entirely different from normal magic. For instance, an enchanter only cast clarity on a warrior by mistake, but bards sing clarity songs to warriors all the time. A shaman only hastes a mage in error, but mages are continuously recieving the benefits of bard celerity.

Our songs have such a short duration that we can not buff and inflict damage like most casters, we have a choice, one or the other. our other choice is to go half way, provide a little defense and a little offense, but all other casters can go on the offensive once the party is buffed.

Sylok said:
"fighting abilities from warriors"

Not exactly. Our fighting ability as I have noted in previous posts is most effective when not using an instrument, which is often the worst way for a bard to fight. But even when we are using weapons, our melee damage output is comparable to a caster, not a warrior. Once our hand to hand hits 100 it is capped.

Sylok said:
"rogueish abilities to pick locks, sense traps, etc"

Well, at my level there are not to many people asking me if I can break into Befallen for them. I would say that a bards so called rogueish skills are some of the most useless in the game, with skill caps so low, that other than corpse retrievals in newbie zones, I would not even notice if the class did not have them.

Sylok said:
"ranger abilities like track etc."

Or a druid ability like track and forage. There is skills overlap among the caster classes. However, that overlap does not make a caster a hybrid.


Bards have paid the penalty too long with marginal melee skills and nerfed songs since it would be unfair for them to be as powerful as casters. If the songs are going to stay crippled, at least give us the offense/defense/hit points of a hybrid. My preference would be to fix the songs to bring them into line with power level of a caster.
