Rather than starting with Quad Kiting, you should start with the basics.

Go to an outdoor zone with MOBs that are well spread out, with dark blue con MOBs (or greens if you want to practice a bit). Sow yourself, Choose a MOB, and do one of the following:

Kite/Blast Kite - snare the MOB, and run away from it, along a path that doesn't bring you close to anything dangerous to you. After 6 or 7 seconds, stop turn and cast a DoT spell on your opponent. Run for another 6-7 seconds, stop, turn and cast a Direct Damage spell. Repeat until dead, refreshing DoT/Snare spells as needed.

Root/DoT - locate an area in the zone that's away from the MOB path; this is your "safe spot". Snare the MOB, and pull it to your safe spot (or if you want, wait to cast Snare until close to your safe spot). Root it, cast your best DoT spell, then sit down and meditate. Refresh DoT, Snare, and Root as needed.

That's about it. I kited in South Karana at that level, then moved to the Drolvargs in Firiona Vie at level 37.

When you're ready to quad kite, practice that. Before trying this on any creature, test whether you have enough mana by blast kiting it using your AE Direct Damage spell. Also remember that those AE spells affect a very small radius, so you must get the MOBs right on top of each other. You can do this by running around in circles a few times after they are snared.

Good Luck!

Kiting is using your speed and the mobs lack of it to keep distance and allow you to hit it with spells without ever getting in melee distance.

1. SoW yourself
2. Snare the mob
3. Run just outside of spell casting range and start casting a nuke or dot.
4. After spell hits, repeat step 3 (if you are using dots just stay out of melee range until you get the message that your spell has worn off)
5. do this until the mob is dead.

Most often druids do this for quad-kiting which is what Tyrellan has described... Once they are tightly bunched, Lightning Strike can hit 4 mobs at once. But I would also recommend against quadding at level 34... quadding burns a LOT of mana.

My favorite technique is root and rot

1. SoW yourself
2. snare mob
3. Pull mob to a place with no wanderers if possible
4. root mob
5. apply DoT(s)
6. Med and wait for root to break or DoTs to expire and then reapply


This is actually a pretty relaxed pace and is fairly mana efficient. By far the preffered method for a while.

The first mobs I've ever quadkited were those Tesch Mas gnolls in SK. SK is a great place to learn because it's very wide open, flat and has very few mobs that will agro on you. I would say that Root/Rot is the best way to go at your level but it couldn't hurt for you to try to quadkite (unless you die, then it would hurt ).

The two spells you need to have memmed are Ensnare (not Snare) and Lightning Strike. Refresh your SoW and Ensnare a gnoll. Run ahead of it and hit it with Lightning Strike. Repeat this until you kill or you run out mana. If you run out of mana, you'll have to get better gear or more levels before you should try it again. If you managed to kill with enough mana left over for another Lightning Strike, you're good to go. Try it again with 2 gnolls making sure they are bunched up nice and tight. Work your way up to 4 gnolls when you get comfortable.

OK, some words from a 52 Druid who lived on Quads from 35-51.

** Most essential is that your WIS is --AT LEAST-- 170. If not, you're gonna have times when you are OOM and the mobs are still chasing you down..... (Ever try to med while mobs are hot on your tail? Not fun)

Persoanlly, like others said, I would wait till lvl36 before starting to Quad, you get a bit more manna and less "You spell fizzles" that way too.

Good places to Quad:

34-38 -- Crazed Goblins in Butcher Block. Only 3 spawns here, but ZERO wandering Mobs and they are on a 15 min. or so re-spawn rate so plenty of time to kill 3, sit, med to full, repeat. Plus they drop FS Weapons and around 2-4pp each. Bring lots of weight-reduce bags and sell-off at Huts just outside Kaladim.

38-44 -- Walrusses and Ulthrocks in Eastern Wastes. TONS of Walrusses on the SW side of zone, just don't grab any Ulthrock yet, cause they have ALOT more HP then the wallys. OR, Ulthrocks and Wallys on the Extreme North side of the zone. Ulthrocks on the North side have close to the same HP as the wallys do, so mix and match quads if ya want. Beware the Caldurin Camp as they --HATE-- you in Wolf form. As mentioned above, collect and keep the Ulthrock Tusks, which you can turn in in Cobalt Scar for nice jewlery (120-160pp to vendors) or maybe even a Blue Diamond (1k - 2k in /bazaar).

44-48 -- Back to Butcher Block to kill the Dwarf Women. Kinda hard to explain, check out Druid hunting guides, but basically there are 11+ Female Dwarfs scattered around BBM that can be quaded for great XP and NO FACTION damage.

48-51 -- Spirocs (aka Chickens) in TimDeep. Grab the raft out of Oasis to TimDeep and when you land head forwards and to the left till you find the Chiken Coop. Inivs yourself and have a look around to find where the static spawns are and practive your pull techniques on 1-2 ata time tell you get it down pat as these guys HIT HARD and can backstab too.

51-54 -- I've heard/read/seen Druids Quading Wyverns in Cobalt Scar, but you REALLY need to have good CoV faction or the Drakes up there will make life hell......

I'm 52 now and as of recent been more root/dot killing then Quading, but this path worked great for me.

Good luck, Happy hunting and GO PUPPY POWER to us Wolfies!
