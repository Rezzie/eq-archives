- Balance -

Please consider the cleric's perspective in this.

A cleric wants their mana use to be efficient. Obviously a complete heal on an warrior with tons of HPs is a very efficient use of the cleric's mana.

In a group of two warriors of roughly equivilent HPs the mob may be damaging the warriors evenly maybe not, but the cleric is getting the biggest bang for their mana reguardless of who receives the damage and at what rate. At a certain point the cleric casts a complete heal and restore 2500+ hps on the warrior.

Exchange a monk for the warrior. The monk has half the hps of the warrior. The monk does more damage and does fast damage to the mob keeping him high on the hate list, hence he receives most of the mobs attention. The cleric will need to heal the monk more frequently for 2 reasons, less hit points, and higher agro (monk receives more damage). Due to the low hit points of monks each heal cast on a monk is significantly less efficient than if it was on a warrior (like 1200hps to 2500hps). Consequently the Cleric needs to spend more then twice the mana with the addition of the monk over a warrior.

Whats in it for the cleric to invite the monk?

Slightly faster mob death (maybe 5% faster for a monk/warrior team over a warrior/warrior team) for a significant decrease in mana efficiency.

Rogues and Rangers have abilities and spells to move themselves down hate list, and warriors back on top. Paladins and Shadowknights can restore their own hps to provide additional mana efficiency (reduced mana use, from the cleric's perspective).

Monk's need a way to be equivilant to the other melee classes in the demands they place on the healing classes.

Armor Class

Monks do not need unreasonable AC upgrades on equipment. Due to our innate weight restriction, it's easy to reach a very respectable level of AC that rivals Paladins/SK's, and beats several plate classes like clerics, & bards.

This is because of the large number of items that are monk usable, relatively low weight, and high AC. (Tranix Crown, Azure sleeves, Symbol to Vox, etc)

The only piece of Shiverback I use are the gloves, and they will be replaced by the epic eventually. Increasing shiverback, or some other "special monk armor" set to a level that rivals the equipment we're already using would be nice. Requests for AC 18 legs, AC 15 arms, etc are out of line, and would give monks an unfairly high level of AC.

Damage output

My level of damage output is very good using a Jade Mace mainhand. Once I obtain the celestial fists, I will deal signficant damage. There are several very good 2H monk only weapons in the game. The Celestial Fists are our ultimate 1H weapons are are very good.
