Charms, slows, roots, stuns, fear, many nukes. That's a lot of effects, all of them pretty serious.

But it's one resistance.

For some reason, magic resistance covers something like 40 percent of the spell effects in the game. While that may be a little goofy from a game design standpoint, it works out well for the player who pumps up their magic resistance as high as possible, with gear, buffs and self-buffs. It's also a fairly easy resistance to buff up, with a vast amount of gear that helps and many classes with buffs and self-buffs to help with it.

Never fear an enchanter NPC, laugh as Gorenaire alternately tries to fear and blast you with cleric nukes, and handily win duels against players desperately trying to root or snare you.

Since embarking on this strategy about a year ago (and with ~223 MR self-buffed as a wizard with Shield of Maelin), I've been rooted ONCE. I now use my AoE snare on things five feet away and have been snared TWICE. Dragon fear, when it rarely hits me, lasts for under 10 seconds. I haven't been charmed in a year. And non-melee stuns? Forget about it.

Everyone can benefit from buffing up Magic Resistance, but the conventional wisdom seems to be that you should worry about those other resistances just as much. But why? One resist covers 40 percent of spell effects, and the other four cover about 15 percent each (well, disease and poison cover even less). Instead of having mediocre resistances against everything, knock yourself out on the one you'll face most often. And enough items have other resistances on them that you'll end up doing OK anyway.

The main thing is not how many spells are magic-based, but the effects that come with those spells. Vast majority of fire/cold/poison/disease spells are nothing but damage. There are some disease-based slows, and maybe a couple non-magic spells that have snare or root effect, maybe blindness - not sure.
Damage can be quickly healed. DoTs are mostly disease/poison based, and can be cured off.

Magic spells are of far more sinister nature. There are charms, roots, fears, snares, blindness, mezzes and stuns. Knockbacks too. If a player eats full ~2700 damage from Strike of Solusek, you can get it healed fairly quick. But that player fails to resist a charm, you suddenly has an extra fully buffed mob to deal with. You can try dispell, but it likely will take a few other important buffs with it. If your cleric chain gets a poison rain cast on them, you usually have an extra couple of clerics healing the chain. But if even half of the chain fails to resist an AE stun or fear, you are likely to end up with a dead main tank, and things going downhill very rapidly.

The rule of thumb is to have magic resists as high as possible at all times - all other resists are situational. For example, on our Trak kill this Saturday we had everyone's poison resistance to over 400. The dragon went down without a single death on our side.

I think it'd take a lot of special effort to avoid casters -- everyone's newbie zone contains them and they appear in every zone in the game that I'm aware of.

And they actually do make a big difference. If you can get over 100 MR, you're going to resist most magic-based spells by things around your level until at least level 50. Over 200, you'll resist spells cast by non-raid targets as well.

Raid targets cheat, though, as most of their effects are semi-lure or lure in nature, so you eventually need to crank things way, way up (over 300 for elemental planes, for instance).

Given how common resists are on gear, and LDoN enhancements added to that, I disagree that it's at all difficult to get your MR way up there quickly.

MR is certainly a good resistance to work on in general, but what it ultimately comes down to, is what type of mobs do you generally fight?

For example, I tend to deal with quite a few nuking mobs in general - as such, I focus on fire (doubly so seeing as how I start with a 5 save as a troll) and cold.

If I know that I'll be in a necro/shammy heavy area, then I switch my focus over towards disease/poison.

It's rather rare that you're going to be suddenly surprised as to what types of caster mobs you'll be facing - usually you've already made a general plan as to what area(s) you'll be hunting or raiding before you send your character out into the field, so then you can plan accordingly.

Also, don't forget that more than likely, you'll be able to pick up a few resist buffs from your group to help augment your situation.

So with a little bit of planning, you can ensure that you'll always have the best saves lined up ahead of time and make your plat go further as a result.

I also focused on my MR over all. Yes, when you start getting the higher end gear in VT and whatnot, you get pretty lucky in that there's resists on everything. But until then you make those hard choices between your mana pool and resists, or your HPs and resists, etc.

So basically to get the most "bang for my buck" with resist gear, I concentrated on MR first, then fire and cold to a degree, and poison and disease not as much. Those two have always been my pain in the behind to get to a decent level.

Without any resist buffs (self or otherwise) I sit at about 211MR and 200 CR, DIS and FIRE in the mid 100's and Poison sitting way down there at a measly 90 or something. I carry a couple of poison specific items to slap on to bump that up if I need.

Also don't forget about another resist *gear* you can have on you -- some tasty Holy Cakes and a smooth jug of Kaladim Constitutionals. The resist stat boosts off those two stat foods are nothing to sneeze at. When I'm trying to get my FR to 250+ before bard song, those cakes and drinks make ALL the difference.

But to further Whizbang's point: MR is the one that's going to save you from something that could DRASTICALLY cause something to go wrong. Poison and Disease tend to be dots- Okay you can still take care of business as your HPs tick away (we won't get into the nasty dots that you can only resist with 500). And there are always heals and cures. Even if you get tashed or slowed, cure Poison and cure Disease take care of em both. As does radiant cure, etc. Fire and Cold: DD or AE's. Pain is over quickly and you can get a heal.And these resists tend to be fairly common anyway. MR is the one that will prevent you from being feared, charmed, and all sorts of wacky things that can render you completely useless, render your party incapable of removing the effect until it wears off - or dead.
