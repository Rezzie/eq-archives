I managed to get EQ to run stably by implementing some of the suggestions here on the board, but there are still a few issues I'd like to work out. For one, I have no battle music in EQ. I don't know about city music, since I haven't had the opportunity to get to a city from where I am in Kunark. Another thing is that the music at the EQ splash screens sounds like MIDI music, whereas it didn't on my old AWE 64 card. I checked what the default setting was on the computer that AWE 64 resides in now and it was set to Creative Music Synth [220]. When using that card to play EQ the music worked beautifully, and sounded good, like wave music.

I have turned my hardware acceleration down to 75%, and updated to Liveware 3.0. I avoided problems by deleting the old drivers, shutting down, physically removing the card, booting, shutting down, putting the card back in and installing the downloaded 3.0 driver set. I also installed the newer drivers from 5/5/2000 (newest released by Creative). Any ideas?

As for those of you with one of Creative's Annihilator cards who are having problems, Creative released a new version of FastTrax on 10/6/2000 that uses the 634 reference drivers from NVidia. I installed them and stopped having problems with my GeForce2.

This is an older problem, so I'll tell you what you need to do:

under the creative launcher, goto the sound font manager.

on one of the tabs you'll find a slider that is the amount of ram to dedicate to loading sound fonts. set it to dynamic (if the option is listed) and about 20MB. Dont worry, it only uses the ram if it needs it.

save and reboot and you should hear music just fine. Remember though, not all zones have music, or will play battle music. If you get the login music, your fine.
