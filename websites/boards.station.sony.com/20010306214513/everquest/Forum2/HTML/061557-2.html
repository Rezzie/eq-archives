It is my guess that the game designers do not wish any trade skill to be "profitable" from a vendor. Why is this? Simple game design. Right now they have total control of the rate at which items and money flow into the economy of the servers. THEY can adjust drop rates, and spawn rates to limit anything that they wish. If ANY trade skill could make money, then that avenue of adding cash to the server economy would be PLAYER controlled, ie. anyone with the skill could make money at the rate they felt they needed. This would allow an uncontrolled flow of cash into the server. In the past whenever such a condition existed it was identified and instantly nerfed. To believe any trade skill will ever make a profit from vendor sales is silly.

The flip side of this is making items good enough so that a player would actually want them. This is EXACLTY the same. An item that people would want that can be created on demand by a player is an UNCONTROLLED method of adding items to the economy. Again this is not wanted, and if it ever happened it would be changed...

So now we see the problem. How can Verant make tradeskills GOOD and still maintain control of the rate at which things enter the world? Until this question is answered, no craftsman will have wares that rival drops, or that sell for a profit of any reasonable amount to vendors.
