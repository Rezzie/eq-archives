Ok, I've played a rogue for a year now, so I feel like I can throw in my 2 coppers here. Are rogues the kings of damage? No, at least not inherently. Like with all melee classes, it depends on the equipment. With a bloodclaw, for example, I could backstab for the upper 60s. However, with a pair of SBDs, I'm doing 100. Piercing goes from 12 to 20. So, with a pair of SBDs, I do much, much more damage. My wife plays a ranger - uses a short sword of morin and a thulian claw. We're about dead even in damage. The MOB is constantly turning from one to the other of us. So, are we damage kings? No more than well equiped rangers, pallies, or any other melee class. That said, however, I'd like to point out that there are things we can do that I have not seen other classes manage. An example would be in mistmoore near the castle entrance - that's a pretty good drop there. Easiest thing in the world for me to come up on a MOB, utterly unseen, BS it for about 80 or 90 (or more if I'm lucky), and then jump off the cliff (taking under 10 dmg, usually) and run like crazy. Meanwhile, the MOB is running down the ramp, taking the long way. Even without SOW, I'm back at my group ready to go long before the Ancil (or whatever) arrives. You see, it's all in how you play the class. Every class is like this, it has good and bad. The key is to learn what your class can do that nobody else can and work that...every day. Myself, I've got a bard, a mage, a monk, and a necro as well, but I keep comming back to the rogue.
