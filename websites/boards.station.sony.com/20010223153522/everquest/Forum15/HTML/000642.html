Yes paladins are a good class to have but truth be known there will be certains times in your paladins life you will come to the point where you truely think the classes suck, and other points where you think it owns as a class. you will probably struggle with your paladins till you get to about level 30 when you get your 300 hp heal i think its level 30 you get the 300 hp heal, oh well when ever you get your 300 hp heal things will turn around for you, but there might be a few bumps along the way to 60, but just stick it through and you should have an enjoyable time.

As for race, in my opinion doesn't make much difference, I for one play and erudite paladin and have no real complaints. But if you want a race thats considered the best for paladins I would say go with Human or Dwarf depending on your taste. Humans have all your stats right about where you need them with 20 points to put where you want, with dwarvs you have very nice starting str and will probably never have to worry about str in you gaming life and you have night vision, but your agi and cha are kinda low, most don't see a point in cha but it helps with your prices when buying and selling to npcs, and also affects your faction when you do certain actions and may also affect some of your spells and how well they work.

Now for starting points make sure your agi and sta are both at least 75 after that put the rest in str or wis.

I play a Dwarf Paladin, Sometimes its fun and sometimes it's Frustrating, Biggest joy for me is always hearing the healer in group say OOM then I Cast my LOH on the primary tank as he was about to die, giving him extra life and thus mostly saving the group. The frustrating parts is trying to find groups alot. Once you hit lvl 21 and up it becomes really dificult to solo blues, many a time I've Soloed a blue to end up with 1 bub of hp left when done then using all my mana to heal myself.
Unless its undead we rock against undead I myself can solo yellows with ease, and some reds. I never have a problem finding a group at unrest unless they are all full, make a note to yourself You cannot solo unrest unless your lvl 45 plus, too many wandering mobs that like to attack. Other zones sometimes have difficulty getting groups, unless the group is in bad need of a tank because no warrior is availble.
The best way to play a Paladin is to be unselfish, always heal after battles to take load off primary healers so they can Med, Never hold your LOH in researve just to save yourself, unless your the primary tank, your job is to keep the primary tank alive at all costs. Belive me people remember who they group with and will most likely want you in the group if your good. Most groups I get in is because of someone in the group, grouped with me before and told the others I would be a good fit. Then the others see it as well and tend to invite me in their future groups.

Good luck in your creation

Just remember.
