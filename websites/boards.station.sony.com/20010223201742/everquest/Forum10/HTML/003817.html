Spiderlings. Gather the silks, they'll sell for about 5gp per.
As soon as you can, get a sewing kit and combine them two silks into silk thread. Stackable, and the thread sells for about 1p each, or 20p a stack.
You might hold off selling these until your skill gets up into the high teens or 20's. At this point, you can start combining the thread with a LQ cat, wolf, or bear pelt, to make leather padding. Leather padding typically sells for 4p each, 80p for a stack.

All of this came about due to the addition of Fine Plate to the game. With the money earned from it, my 12th level Paladin is about as nicely equipped as my 19th level Warrior...the warrior actually had to play catchup recently, and was only able to do so by farming some silks and pelts!

Prices vary by server...I'm on Luclin.

Bat Wings in GF. I have a 4th level High Elf Enchanter with 35pp, untwinked, just from selling bat wings. The key is collect several stacks before you sell by "playing the market", but you have to have money to make money. Sell your first one or two stacks first - try to hold out for 3pp - you should find a buyer if you are patient. Then once you have that 6pp keep collecting bat wings, but also buy from everyone who is selling for 1 or 2pp. Buy a backpack and keep hunting and buying batwings until you can fill it (I think I collected 3 stacks myself and bought 5), then send out the following /auc:

/auc Selling Backpack of Bat Wings - 35pp, will deliver! That's seven fewer newbies you have to deal with!!!

I had 3 people try to buy it from me immediately and got a lot more laughs, plus I sold the stacks for ~ 4p 3g a stack!

I guarentee it will work every time, just be patient and don't sell your individual stacks!

Ironcast, 4th level Enchanter of Drinal

I'm going to second the leather padding idea with a little more detail. This is something you can do at fairly low levels with very little investment if you're willing to put in the time. All you need is a sewing kit (couple gp?) a skinning knife - (never had a problem finding a smith to make me one if I got the ingredients myself - less than 2 plat - maybe a lot less) and a few patterns (very cheap) The patterns are used to make patchwork from all the ruined pelts you find, which will make a set of armor initially for you but more importantly will get your tailoring skill up.

You can kill all the necessary ingredients yourself by the time you're level 5 - spiderlings and wolves - even the occasional black bear. I made and sold my first stack for 100 p before I was level 6 and 2 more before I was 7. That's a lot of money for a 7th level character. I've never had a problem finding buyers - all in all I've made and sold 9 stacks - 8 to the same buyer and I can't keep up with his demand. I've probably put 300 back into buying threads and pelts - the rest is profit, and of course I could have just kept getting all the ingredients myself and never invested anything in it at all.

Anyway - just my 2 cents - I'm a big proponent of padding as the most accessible way for a relative newbie to make relatively large amounts of cash.

Neflyte - I think 100 pp a stack is probably close to an 'average' price. - I've heard people say 80 on their servers - I sell for 100 but have sold one for 150, and have heard of people offering 200 when they're desperate.

Lastly - you can do all this with a small sewing kit - you don't need the large for paddings.

High level characters usually have tons of money, so they are the best source for money. Of course begging won't get you anywhere, running errands is much better.

- "ATM" means giving somebody platinum for his gold pieces at a favorable rate, for example 10 PP for 130 gold. Of course you need some starting capital for that.

- Once you have an idea of the geography of Norrath, you can look out for people who are far from the next point where they can buy/sell. A good deal is to offer to run to the vendor and sell the loot for the KOS player for 20% of the profits. My best profit was in OoT where I sold one stack of food and one stack of drink for the fantastic sum of 1256 gold (but that was with my level 20 druid).

- Another possibility once you know your way around (or don't mind occasional deaths) is to go to places where high level players kill mobs but don't bother to loot heavy stuff. Bronze Axes in Butcherblock for example or the things close to Permafrost in Everfrost. Be polite and ask whether they don't mind if you loot the stuff, but usually thats okay with them. After one afternoon of collecting Bronze Axes in Butcher my dwarven warrior had more than 50 PP!
