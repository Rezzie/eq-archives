Ok lets look at the numbers. I am using the spell stats from castersrealm for my calculations, if they are incorrect please post a URL stating otherwise.

I am using the equation

(mana cost of lifetap)/(mana given per tick of lich) = (number of ticks need to get mana) * (hitpoints lost per tick) = (total lost hitpoints)


(amount of hitpoints gained from lifetap) - (total lost hitpoints lost to lich) = Hitpoint efficiency

Call of Bones - takes 16 hps and gives 8 mana, costs 5 mana to cast (lvl 34 spell)
Drain Spirit - Does 226 dmg and gives 226 hps max costs 189 mana to cast. (lvl 39 spell)

Amount of hps lost to get to enough mana to cast Drain Spirit.
189/8 = 23.7 * 16 = 378hitpoints lost (not counting mana to cast since neglidgable)

Effect of casting one drain spirit after liching to get enough mana to cast drain spirit.
-378hps(lost) + 226(gained) = -152hps(lost)
and we are now back to 0mana.

So everytime we use call of bones/Lifetap combo we are actually loosing 152 hps, which we will eventually have to sit and med to get back up.

Ok now Lich

Lich - Takes 32hps and gives 20mana (mana cost 5 and lvl 49 spell)
Drain Soul - Does 338 dmg and gives 338hps (costs 225 mana lvl 49 spell)

Amount of hitpoints lost from liching to get enough mana to lifetap to get hitpoints back.
225/20 = 11.25 * 32 = 360

Effect of casting one Drain Soul to regain hps
-360hps + 338 hps = -22hps
and we are at 0 mana again

Demi Lich (Lvl 60)
Demi Lich - looses 48 hps and gain 31mana
Touch of Night - Does 720 dmg and gives 720 hps for 405 mana

Amount of hps lost to cast one Touch of Night
405/31 = 13.06 * 48 = 627.1hps

Effect of casting one touch of night
-627.1 + 720hps = 92hps

Arch lich
does roughly the same as lich at 49, only difference is 31hps taken instead of 32


So as you can see, Lich doesnt even equal itself out until Lvl 60, when we cant lifetap much of anything we will be using it on anyway. Clarity 2 gives ll mana for no hps with means you will always gain mana, and never have to worry about trying to get hitpoints back, and this is true for all of the clarity line. From these calculations, the lich line is actually less efficient than the clarity line.

A few things.

#1. Forget Castersrealm, forget whatever you parsed from the spdat.eff. Test the spells in game and see what they do to your health and mana. Those of you saying lich takes 32 hps per tick now...you are wrong.

#2. "Sorry but some of you necros need to learn math. Any necro that stops using lich post-nerf, is an idiot. It will still be far superior to C2 for most situations."

Okay, let's do the math for the new lich.

According to Absor, the new lich will have a 3:2 hp:mana ratio instead of 1:1. That means, where it takes 24 hps and gives 24 mana per tick now, it will take 36 hps and give 24 mana.

Again, sitting regen post-50 is 5 hp per tick. So we have 31 hps to replenish.

Bond of death, again, taps two hps for every point of mana. So, it will cost 15.5 mana per tick to replace the 31 hps lost.

Total mana regen = 24 - 15.5 = 8.5 mana per tick.

Clarity 2 is 12 mana per tick.

#3. "Free damage!" Maybe so, but it's not that much. Compare:

- bond of death, 720 damage over 9 ticks

- envenomed bolt, 1100 damage over 7 ticks

- splurt, 1600 damage over 15 ticks

Clearly, this is not a necro's primary source of damage.

Also, consider that dots need to land near the start of a fight to do full damage. However, bond of death will not achieve its full effectiveness unless you are down 1-1.5 bubbles when you cast it (depending on how much your hps are buffed), because it will tap much faster than lich will take hps away. The chance of you hitting this point just as a fight with a mob starts is somewhat slim; thus, you have to choose between:

a) cast bond of death early, and lose efficiency (but do full damage)

b) cast bond whenever you hit that point, even if a mob is low, sacrificing damage

c) let your hps run down until the next fight, which may or may not make you vulnerable (depending on what buffs you have, how quickly pulls are coming, etc)

d) use deflux instead of bond. You avoid the difficulties of a dot, as deflux is a straight tap. However, it taps 3 hps for every two mana spent, however, and as such is much less efficient as far as mana regen goes.

Finally, if you want to maintain efficiency with bond while grouped, you can't cast it every fight. You have to let your hps drain down first, and lich does so at a constant rate.

Bond of Death + Lich may allow a necro to do "free damage", but that damage is very limited in nature.

Aatudien, 54 necro, Lanys
