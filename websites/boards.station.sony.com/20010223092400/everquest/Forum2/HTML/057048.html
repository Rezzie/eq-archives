You may already have seen a list of some of the things that we are already testing as changes for the ranger class. Essentially, we are adjusting the level at which some of their spells are attained to make them more in line with their power.

Here is a list that I, once again, stole from Everlore because I'm lazy and because it seems accurate:

a) Add Endure Cold at 22
b) Add Skin Like Nature at 59
c) Add FireFist at 22
d) Move Bramblecoat from 49 to 39
e) Add Spikecoat at 49
f) Add resist cold at 55
g) Add Panic Animal at 22
i) Offense and weapons skill made identical to warriors

Ok, we're being asked for our feelings on balancing our class, yet get told that we aren't properly playing our class? Who knows better how to play rangers than those of us that have been doing it constantly for 50+ lvls now?

Many of the upgrades are easily within the scope of being "proper" for the class:

Self Bind:

Being the woodsmen of the game, we should be able to spend more time out in our beloved woods and not bound to some city. Create a self only "Call of the Woods" spell that binds us in outdoor zones only. We aren't made for dungeons, but this would help considerably.

Archery:

Ok, we invested all that time and effort developing our fletching skills. We've practiced long and hard on our archery and raised it to that 150-200 range. We now spend 7-10pp per 20 arrows to make those nice dmg6 - dmg8 arrows. Now, we proceed to miss...miss...miss..... dbl dmg hit for 2 dmg.... miss.... miss. Let's see the masters of the bow equiped with a viable archery skill to use in combat. incorporate a stunning blow, crippling blow, killing blow into our archery skill.

Offense:

We are mini-tanks, we step right in and dish it out with everyone else. Rangers do damage! Yeah, we're impressed with the warriors and monks, but we'll go toe-to-toe when we have to.

Defense:

We are a "lightly armored" class. We'll never have to pure armor ac we'd like to have. But being the nimble woodsman we are, we should be able to get out of the way of some of those haymakers L50+ mobs like to dish out. Improve our chances to dodge/evade some of that damage. Groups don't discriminate rangers cause we can't do damage, we get discriminated because we can't take the dmg or have the hp to support the dmg we do take.

Spells:

Another improvement for the dmg issue would be a chance at a regen spell. SOmething that might stack with other classes, and help facilitate grouping "Woodsman's Cure?" Yes, a class specific form would be a nice plus. Wouldn't solve the balance issue, but might quiet the dissension. Might be good to incorporate it with added defensive properties. Upgrade some of the spells for rangers, we're already limited on the amount of mana we use, becomes an issue of mana vs ac, but we definitely could use a better DOT, and some others. The added prot. vs cold spells are also a welcome addition.

Will a self bind ruin the EQ economy? I don't think so. Will it make it easier for us to group? Definitely. Will a class specific skill for rangers make us more play-able?... ie Archery, specialized Tracking.... Yes. Will added defensive abilities balance us for groups? Yes. The question now becomes:

Will these suggestions be taken to heart?

Will these things that truly seem to fit with what "being a ranger" is be implemented?

Will this post be read by anyone other than other rangers?

Only time will tell.

OK, let's admit upfront that a good portion of the threads in this six-page discussion are well above my weight as a newbie to EQ and the Ranger quest. But a couple things seem odd to me as a longtime (20+ years) RPGer regarding the ranger class.....
1. Archery. Why are the masters of the woodlands amazingly bad at archery? With a high-quality bow, good arrows and decent archery skill, I still miss low-level critters better than 50% of the time, and frequently hit for 2-4 points of damage. Seems a little odd.

2. Woodland creatures. It seems like we get neither of the two "traditional" ranger benefits -we are not master hunters; and we are not able to befriend or charm these animals in any way. How about an "animal companion", a charm animal spell for the second option, or an enhanced ability at getting useful items (better pelts, etc) out of the corpses of animals we hunt for the "master hunter" option?

3. Grouping. I keep hearing lots about how rangers can't match warriors, can't match paladins, can't match clerics, etc. Well, I came into the game expecting that rangers would be the typical aloof solitary woodsman type, perhaps occasionally assisting city-folk with guiding and tracking skills, but only associating regularly with other Rangers and Druids. Maybe something along those lines could be implemented? Rangers used as point-man/scout initially, then (with enhancements to Archery) used as missile-comabt support after battle is joined?

4. Woodlands vs. Cities. For being hearty outdoorsmen, it is awfully weird that we have to A)Get a third party to cast "Bind Affinity" on us and B) Have to have it cast on us in a city, of all places. A better solution would be the ability to do a self-bind in maybe 4-6 wilderness areas around the game world, so we are not doubly penalized by lack of self-reliant quick movement and our inability to bind ourselves to a new location.

Just the thoughts of a long-time DM, on ways to enhance the class. I, for one, love the game and will continue playing it whether these items are addressed or not. They just seem to be ways in which the playing experience could be improved!!
