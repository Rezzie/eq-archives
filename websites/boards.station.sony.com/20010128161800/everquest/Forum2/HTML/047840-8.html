I am all for the changes and am glad to see the game isn't set in stone and that drastic changes can still happen.

I do hope that these changes are not the "solution" to all of the problems and that you are still indeed looking into some problems which still exist in the areas of balancing risk vs reward.

Two problems:
1. Lesser used dungeons. Although a better reward for some dungeons sounds great in theory, I still hope you are going to revisit some of the dungeons in terms of loot/items and spawns.
-Examples, paw, befallen and najena.
Paw: I personally won't ever go back to paw due to the fact one enchanter gnoll killed my full party of 6 because of charm. Nobody even saw him coming due to the fact he was casting at extreme range (around the corner somewhere) and was an accidental pull. I have a real problem with this dungeon having monsters which can charm half a party hidden behind a wall. And for the most part this is what keeps all parties which gain exp in this dungeon restricted to areas before the double doors. If you have been there you know what I mean.
Befallen and Najena. Soul bound keys. Orc camps fulfill the need for a group but don't require hours of camping for keys. And dying behind a locked door, specially one a rogue can't unlock, is a huge penalty. And neither of these dungeons offers substantial reward to players in their apropriate range.

2. Class balance. I hope that you don't consider all matters regarding classes closed. There are definetely some issues regarding class which should still be examined. I am only refering to same class, different race combinations.

Examples:
Dark elf rogue vs any other type of rogue. This is due to the fact dark elves rogues have lower melee stats.
Iksar Warrior vs any other type of warrior. Lack of midlevel armor capable of keeping iksar AC comparable to other warriors. I understand highest level armor is available to them, that isn't the point.
Human druids vs other types.
Human casters vs other types.

Basically I am looking back to what you did with erudites and how you gave them "quests" for higher level items which made up for some drawback of the class. Lack of quests for all levels applies to all classes however.

My final comment is this. Please revisit stats with the same vigor you have shown the experience penalty. A huge aspect of the game, mainly stats being useful, is underutilized and I would love to see it worked out in more detail.

My example: Casters collect Int/Wis gear because it drastically changes the size of their mana pool. However strength doesn't drastically change the damage output of a melee class. I'm suggesting you be creative here. Here are a few suggestions.

Strength effects the minimum damage a character can do with any given hit (maximum remains). Things to remember, make it a sliding scale that takes into account strength/delay/damage.

Dexterity effects off hand damage, Proc rates and 2nd hit (from double attack).

Agility effects parry, dodge, riposte and additionally once a certain level is reached a player can decide to lower melee damage in favor of "deflecting" incoming damage. Thus allowing tanks to tank longer.

Stamina drastically effects hp gain. On the order of 10-15 hp per stamina point above 100 at level 60.

This is a fairly drastic change and would defintely take more thinking then what I have put down in this simple post. But my idea is that players can customize what they want their class to do. This would also make buffs by shaman or other casters much more important at higher levels (and lower). To the point where players may actually consider buying potions/buffs the same way they buy sow.

This game leaves a lot to be desired in terms of allowing a player to customize their character more.

Examples for trade skills.
1. Allow tailoring to change the "skin" of cloth (robes), leather. You collect a dye potion and change any robe (players can pick the robe with the stats they want) and change it to whatever skin they want. You would need to pick "common" skins (like solid colors), and "rare" skins so people aren't running around with the SMR look on their gossimer robe. This would allow guilds to colorize, players to choose their appearance and give you a whole sleu of additional items to place in the game. Remember though, this trade skill should have 0% chance of taking away the item (though you can lose the dye). Think more in terms of "player satisfaction" since this doesn't add any stats to the game.

Blacksmithing should be able to do the same. Why restrict dyes to fine steel plate when they could color any armor they want. And additional find high level mobs with dyes to make other skins.

The bottom line is don't stop working towards your vision.
