Nope.

It's a matter of a zone that was barely tested, was put into play and then restricted after release simply because it shares the "non-Kunark" flag with the rest of the original EQ zones (which were capped in spell level to prevent spells from hitting "Boss" mobs).

What you have here is the SAME normal encounter (non-boss) MOB that, based on level that you CANNOT determine if you are 55+, can be mezed if it's level is 51-52 but not if it's 53-54. This does not occur on any mob in any other zone other than the Hole. It's a matter of game mechanics.

These MOBs should be mezable, by nature of their "species" but aren't because of a hard level cap that was designed to protect "boss" MOBs. It's not the same as something not being able to be affected altogether, like Spite Golems in Hate, or ones that cannot be mezed or stunned, but can be charmed, like Giants of any name. It's not something that's intuitive based on an encounter with a creature where you determine... yeah, I can affect XXXX, or no I can't affect YYYY, so I won't cast on it. It's a matter of an arbitrary level cap put in to protect "boss" MOBs that inadvertantly affected normal MOBs in The Hole because the dungeon was partly designed for Kunark players (the specified level range was 40-55, and you can't be 51+ without having Kunark).

You show me a place where a wizard, necromancer, magician, cleric, shaman, druid, warrior, ranger, paladin, shadow knight, rogue or bard cannot affect a "blue MOB" of the same "species" with their core abilities/spells when the MOB is at or above a certain level, and they can when it's below that cap, then it's the same case. But, there is no place that this happens to any of them in this entire game. This is an arbitrary cap to keep enchanters from Charming Vox and storming over Permafrost, or Charming Nagafen and laying waste to Fire Giants, or doing the same with Phinigal and killing all his Swirlspine Guardians.

Before Kunark, boss MOBs were all 53+, making the arbitrary 53rd level cap viable. Kunark was released, followed the Hole that had normal encounter MOBs 53+, making the cap a game mechanics problem.
