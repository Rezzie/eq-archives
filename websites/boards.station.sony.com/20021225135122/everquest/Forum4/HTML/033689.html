You have a set point, called "bind point". For now, it is located just outside your home city. Wizard, Magician, Necromancer and Enchanter can change their own bind point to about anywhere thanks to a level 12 spell. Cleric, Shaman and Druid can do the same thanks to a level 14 spell.

Other classes can set their bind point to a different place with the assistance of one of the aforementionned casters, but they are more restricted in where they can bind. As a rule of thumb, they can only be bound in cities, while casters can bind themselves in the middle of nowhere.

You respawn where your character is 'bound'.

For a new character, it will usually be in the low level zone just outside your starting city.

To find out what zone you are bound in, use the /charinfo command.

You can change your bind point several ways:

1. Some classes (magic casters like clerics, wizards, etc.) have a spell called 'bind affinity'. This allows them to bind themselves almost anywhere, and bind others in cities or certain areas designated for that.

2. Many cities have soulbinders. These are usually in cities that are starting points for different races. If you say 'bind my soul' to a soulbinder, they will bind you where you are standing. There are some areas with soulbinders that aren't cities, like the Arena zone near Lake Rathe.

Finding out where you are bound -before- you deep into the wild woolies of someplace tough to reach is a good idea.

It is bad to be be bound in Kelethin and die in Befallen (at least before or sans PoP) because then you will have a long long corpse recovery (level 10 and above, of course). Or... worse (again sans PoP), die in Tower of Frozen Shadows and discover your bind point is Shar Vahl..... But, oh, no, I would never do something like that... *smirk*.

Generally the best place to be bound is either a central-ish location from which travel is easier (Nexus or Knowledge) or some place close to where you will be fighting. We never -intend- to die... but it has been known to happen from time to time.

The worst places to bind are those where you will have a hostile party within striking distance. If you bind in such a place, you can have a bind/death loop. You will respawn, get killed, respawn, get killed, in quick quick succession with little chance of getting away or getting rescued. It is bad bad bad to do that to yourself. Always bind somewhere safe.
