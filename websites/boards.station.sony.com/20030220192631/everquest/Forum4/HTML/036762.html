One of the biggest problems between the "play like a real (insert race/class here)" faction and the "play what you wanna play" faction probably has something to do with the nature of the RP game.

The nature of the unique, special, exception type character has a lot of basis in the tabletop RPGs like D&D, where you can get away with being the exception easier because there's only a handful of you. GM willing, you can make yourself a Drizzt if you want to.

However, in a larger scope, you're no longer the exception when everyone's the exception ... How many evil iksar/DE/troll/ogre turned good have you seen? When you've thousands of people all trying to buck the "trend" at the same time, what you actually end up with is just another trend ... "I'm unique, just like everyone else" sorta thing ...

I tend to fall on the side of "play what kind of character you want, give the players the freedom they need to do it", but often it starts to get out of hand. Drizzt wouldn't have been all that exciting if half the DE race was like him, after all. But then, there are more limitations to the Computer RPG that inhibit RPing in other ways (kinda hard to do the fallen paladin that has lost the favor of his/her god without completely rerolling and going to the effort of leveling again, where as in a tabletop rpg you could modify the character as necessary on the spot with significantly less effort, for example).

There's a balance between the freedom to choose, and believability/fitting into the setting. When the setting says DEs tend to be a certain way, it seems odd when three quarters of the DE player population are completely different. That sort of thing works better (imo, anyway) when you're the lone rogue DE of your race in a smaller setting, but not so well in a massive setting. Exceptions are exceptions because they're rare, after all. When 3/4 of a population acts a certain way, they're the rule, not the exception ...

The "exception" characters just seem to make more sense (to me) within a given story when they're rare changes of heart/mind/whatever, as opposed to mass migrations ...

Note, however, that none of this bothers me enough to get into anything more than a philosophical debate over it ... I don't berate people because they're yet another "exception" ..
