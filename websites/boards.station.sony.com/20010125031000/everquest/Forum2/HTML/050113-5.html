Okay, I finally read all the posts, and I like some of the stuff I'm seeing, but a lot is just unrealistic garbage.

One point I like is that us Mages dont really have an exclusive class defining ability, except "We can summon a lot of garbage." Other classes are slowly tapping into out summoning skills, why not let us tap a bit into theirs? Nothing earth shattering like free clarity sticks or something...

To start changing our summons, I'd remove nearly all of our current summoned weapons. We cant even use most of them, and no one else wants to either! Replace them with mage usable weapons at lower levels, and then weapons more geared for pets at later levels.
For procs on weapons, we need more diversity. With the fix of Sword of Runes, our arsenal was hurt, bit reasonably so. This is one of the spells we should definately keep, because it does have a use. But, we also need at least 1 weapon each with a magic based proc, and a fire based proc. Mages are all about elementals, fire, and magic - this wouldnt be a stretch at all.

For general purpose summonings, I'd like to see summon food/water work like summon bandages as others have mentioned. As an upgrade, let us summon different types of food every 10-15 levels. That black bread makes me nauseous.
Summon Ale would be a nice lower level skill, but I'm happy where it is. If it is added as a lower level summon, I'd also like to see other drinks like champagne and wine. Mages like to drink occasionally, but they're not too fond of ale. Especially us high elves.
An upgrade to Coldstones would be nice, as others have mentioned. Ultravision and See Invis together would be a nice upgrade.

Summoned armor would be very nice, but I wouldnt want a whole set of armor like others have suggested. My spellbook is cluttered enough! Give me summonable robes, with random color and style. I know the random color and style bit probably wouldnt get in, but I had to suggest it. Add duplicates to the Phantom line that created real wearable chest armor, with the hp regen effect built in. Make sure the effect doesnt stack with the Phantom line though.

Fun stuff! Bristlebanes Bundle is my favorite spell so far! I spent 30 minutes yesterday getting the whole north wall of Karnor's drunk and dizzy. More little games like the Spinning Bottle would be nice at lower levels. Dont make us wait until 52nd level to have fun!
A few people have mentioned things along the lines of 'pulling a rabbit out of a hat', and I think thats a great idea - but hard to implement. Best alternative I can think of would be the ability to summon a mob like the monster summoning lines do, but make it not be a pet. Have it create a totally independant mob, with 1 hp and 0 damage potential. Unless you can get rabbits in the global file, then I want to make little rabbits hop all over.

Stuff thats not really what you meant by summoning spells... Summon Mob would be great, but I'm not sure if I'd want it. As a mage at higher levels, the last thing you want is to be sitting right next to whatever you're fighting. Could also be abused. You want a Sand Giant at the docks in Oasis? Well, okay! ::bzzt:: I also wouldn't want to be spammed with all the pulling mages yelling "You will not evade me a_wurm_03!" I think a better spell would be Summon *to* Mob. This would teleport you to the targetted mob. This could be our 'SoW', as well as a pulling aide. There would have to be a maximum but generous range on the target, but it could work and be useful, provided the casting time and cost was relatively low.
Summon Pet would also be a great addition, and I cant really think of any bad sides of this one. It'd make pulling easier in cases where its easier to have your pet follow you into the field. Pet get stuck on some crazy mob? Summon him back, little bugger!
Summon Muck/Entanglement/something... Acts as snare on a mob, with different spell sparklies and emotes. Yes, this steals snaring from other classes, but that goes into the tapping into other classes ablities I wrote about way up at the top. Is it that much of a stretch to think a mage could summon a glob of mud to slow down a running critter? We wouldnt be swiping any other classes 'class defining ability', all sorts of classes can snare. Only problem would be that mages would now have a controlable way to slow down a mob, which we've never really had before. There'd be kiting mages everywhere, and I'm not sure I'd want that.

Okay, I have more ideas, but this post is too long, so I'll leave with 1 more......
Summon Staff of Elemental Mastery: Earth. Come on, this thing drops 2-3 times a year! At least the other items for our epic quest have a chance of dropping more than once a week. Not that I'll ever finish it, but if this was changed I'd have a tiny chance.
