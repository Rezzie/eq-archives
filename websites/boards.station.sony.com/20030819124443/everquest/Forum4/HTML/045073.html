Treeform - No, it does not act like Hide. You will attract the exact same attention you do while not in treeform.

Charm Animal - Yes, this can be effective if you have a decent charisma to help keep it from breaking early. Otherwise you are tossing out a large chunk of mana for little real benefit. Higher level charm spells, after you get nice Charisma boosting stats, can be extremely effective.

Kiting - Choose an area you know well. Learn the paths monsters travel in that area and play it accordingly. Most of the time I just run in a wide enough circle to avoid getting in melee range. A basic rule to avoiding adds is to select a monster that is not social (ie. other monsters of that type don't really care if their fellows are attacked or not).

Pulling - Pull with Snare (or Ensnare at 29). Snare only lasts about 4 minutes so kiting can be tricky. Ensnare lasts up to 14 minutes making kiting significantly easier. The major reason to pull with Snare, however, is to draw the monster back to your designated circling track and away from potential adds.

Barbcoat - The spells in this line are long duration and meant to add a fair chunk of protection to the druid. The DS portion is minor and not intended to act as a source of large damage.

Damage Shields - These are short duration, but can produce significant damage over their life, particular in trains and such. However, most druids avoid melee situations for the most part, so using it on themselves is generally pointless. Besides, the Barbcoat line and the Damage shield spells stack (ie. can be used on top of each other for additive effects).

Halfling Size - Jump up on the counters. Look those stretched out bags of flesh in the eye because staring at where your normal line of vision is natural to you is construed as a less-than-polite social blunder to them.

quote:
Quadding is pulling up to 4 mobs ...

Side note: The reason it is called 'quadding' is because of the 4 mobs. The reason 4 mobs are kited, rather than 5 or 6, is because most druid AoE spells will affect a maximum of 4 targets. It would be great to affect more, but the developers saw what that did in terms of druid soloing and figured the 4 target limitation would be enough to make the spells nice without making them too powerful.

Point Blank AoE spells will affect an unlimited number of targets, but in that case the druid is in melee range and must suffer any agro they generate.

Ok, some quick notes about charming and experience nerfs.

From the April 8th Patch

quote:
** Charm Changes **

- Healing a charmed pet now generates an appropriate amount of hate for the healer.
- Charmed pets now take up to one third of the experience for each NPC killed. This amount scales down based on the percentage of damage to the target that the pet does. Dire charm pets still take the same experience they always have.
- Charmed pets are no longer selected as a monster's preferred target if there are many players available for the monster to attack instead.


Basically, I take this to mean if the charmed pet does damage, it will take experience equal to the amount of damage it did to the opponent, up to a maximum of 1/3rd.

However, combine this with part of the patch message from June 11th regarding new group based experience awards:

quote:
The one down side to the new system is that there will be a small decrease in experience for those who do not group, and only for those who do so in Planes of Power zones.

And you see the major complaint regards extremely high level druids/enchanters/necromancers getting an experience decrease for dire charm soloing in Planes of Power zones.

The combo is seen as a one/two punch. Regular charming outside PoP zones got nerfed, and soloing experience inside PoP zones got nerfed. Soloing in PoP zones is significantly easier using Dire Charm.
