My major problem with the "it's a service so sell it" argument is this.

Only 4 classes have services to sell.

What services does a Warrior have to sell? Or a Paladin? Or a Shadowknight?

I've always been pro-melee and I still am. Because I know how much it sucks to play a class that can't do ANYTHING for themselves.

When there's a warrior seeking a bind, I go out of my way to do it, because I have options and HE DOES NOT.

Nobody should have to pay for basic necessities such as being bound. Or something stupid like having a LIGHT SOURCE when you die.

I think we casting classes as a whole are spoiled. I'm bound at the zone. Big whoop. I die, I go back and get my stuff.

When the warrior dies, he's bound in Freeport, so he's making the 6 zone run to LGuk.

No, it is not our fault that VI in their infinite wisdom decided that casting classes have the convenience of respawning anywhere they want, and the tanking classes must run 6 zones just to get their corpse.

If the issue was ONLY about XP I would understand.

If you're already IN the zone and the ONLY reason you want a rez is to make back the precious XP, I can understand charging for it.

But many times the issue IS NOT XP, the issue is PLAYABILITY.

If I spawn at the zone, yet you spawn 6 zones away, THAT IS A PLAYABILITY ISSUE.

No, it is not my *FAULT* that the melee classes were shafted in terms of playability.

But it is my Responsibility to equilize the boneheaded design decisions as much as I can by binding these people without a hassle, by porting them when I can, and by helping them out when they have trouble getting their corpse.

Because I am lucky enough to have these options, and they are not.

Someday VI will hit one of our classes with a nerf stick, and tell us that we can no longer bind ourselves. And then you'll see what sort of a FUBAR situation it is to make a 6 zone corpse run multiple times just to avoid losing your gear.
