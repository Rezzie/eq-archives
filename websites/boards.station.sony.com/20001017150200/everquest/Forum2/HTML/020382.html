Jochomo- On the spellcasting interupts (not stuns).

This really belongs in another thread, but I didn't see anyone else address it, so I will.

You didn't mention your class or level, so I have to assume from your post that you are relatively low level (obviously, I could be wrong).

I've played spellcasters from the beginning of beta, so I've had plenty of practice with spell interupts, and basically, what it comes down to is channelling skill (which means your level) vs. the hit/move you take.

Once you have a very high channelling skill, you can channel through some amazing things. My 52nd level Shaman has channelled through Fire Giant and Wurm hits that chewed *bubbles* of health off, but a newbie wizard, would be lucky to channel through the 1 point hit you describe.

Even with very high channeling, there is ALWAYS a chance for an interupt.

Basically, the game does a series of checks - when you get hit, the engine checks to see if your spell was interupted - usually a yes. If yes, then the game checks (or rolls if you will) against your channelling skill to see if your channelling allowed you to resume your concentration and continue casting.

No matter how high your channeling, there is always a base chance of failure (5 or 10% probably). Even my 52nd level guy gets interupted by a stupid 1st level rat hitting me for 1 point on rare occasions, but I can tell you that with very high channeling skill, you *will* notice that you are able to channel through stuff that simply shouldn't be possible.

I rooted myself while running full speed once.. that was a channelling roll from hell, and pretty annoying to boot :)
