Here are the thoughts that come to my mind on the rogue issue. I am not a rogue and have never played a rogue past level 2.

The first thing that comes to mind is that the change just isn't needed. Yes, that is pretty clear to me. There's no reason to change evade. Even if Verant can come up with 10,000 ideas they still won't be able to claim that the change is needed.

The second thing that comes to mind is that if Verant were to change the evade skill, what is their purpose behind it. Keep in mind, I have not read their justification. I only know how evade works from the stand point of friends. From what I understand of the skill it really only allows the rogue to move lower on the hate list of the mobs. This gives them a chance to live after getting the attention of the mobs by doing an enormous amount of damage via their backstab. Effectively, I believe this change will 1) not lower them as far, or 2) change the conditions underwhich they are lowered. To me, there's no good reason, as the rogues deserve just as much of a right to survive in the game as any other class. Right now, a rogue cannot operate as a tank and requires another class to help them backstab.

The third thing that comes to mind is that if Verant was to test this as effectively as they have tested the RANGER class for balance the rogue is in for a rough ride. Verant has done one of the worst jobs in all the gaming environments to balance some of the classes and I believe they know it. They have used hyperboil to cover up what they are doing by saying they have implemented a sliding scale of balance and some classes are on the high end and some on the low--just happens the ranger is on the low end.

This is very disheartening to hear Absor claim that they will extensively test the changes. The problem is that I doubt, and with good reason, that Verant has the right environment in which to test their game any longer. It is obvious that they have this vision of how the game is to be played yet, it is 100% obvious that 99% of the players in this game don't play it that way.

This means that no matter what THEY think their change is, no matter how good they think it is, it won't be done in a manner in which all the other players in the game use it.

It is very obvious that Verant doesn't know what balance is. What we hear from Verant is something called 'synergy'. Anyone with a modicum of experience in life knows that synergy does not equate to balance.

Because they have improperly defined the term it is inevitable their game will become more and more unstable. You can observe this by looking at Velious. The major part of this series of zones are unbalanced even against the classes played by players with a year of Kunark experience.

If this is any representative sample, out of all the other samples we have of their ability to properly test and balance, we know that the rogue evade will wind up as a change that is a very negative thing to the class and to the groups in which they play.

The question that comes to my mind is: Is Verant trying to reduce the rogue's survivability to a level which makes the ranger survivability (without any defense changes) look better? In essence I am of the mindset that they are actually nerfing the rogue to make the ranger look better. This is not just bad program design but bad politics. I believe this, being someone that has played a ranger since the game came out. I in no way resent the rogue class for their abilities. But it doesn't make sense to reduce their effectiveness just to make the ranger look better, when in reality they will be just the same.

Alan:

Listen carefully to the words you say and then compare it to OUR history with Verant. The fact is that there have been a NUMBER of changes that were poorly done and substantially broke chracters for weeks at a time. The most memorable (for me) recent one was the inability of instruments (drums) to affect Selo's Accelerando.

Add to that a NUMBER of /bug submissions I've made that have NEVER been addressed. One of my most recent was that NPC-cast engulfing darkness overwrote SoW even though snare doesn't.

You guys simply do not have the history to claim that 'we're testing it enough' and expect us to believe it. When we say we're nervous we have facts and history to point to that bolsters our claim. I think you would be hard pressed to point to a single completely successful new feature in EQ in the past 18 months. Even evade was tweaked within a couple of weeks of introduction and it is the closest I can think of to a complete success (it worked from the git go).

The change to the length of the hate list is another example of your designers simply not understanding the game well enough or the implementation team not being careful enough in understanding ramifications. EVERYTIME you change the hate list you need to consider all classes and all affects on it:

1. Warriors, SKs, Paladins, and Rangers taunt
2. Rangers jolt
3. Monk feign death
4. SK/Necromancer feign death
5. Necromancer Screaming Terror (is the mez gone yet?)
6. Bard mez and charm songs
7. Caster DD spells
8. Caster DOT spells
9. Healing spells
10. De-buffs against the monster
11. Buffs for the party
12. Caster Rain spells
13. Bonus damage and bonus reverse damage spells (damage shields, mark of karn, etc.)
14. Enchanter mez series.
15. Enchanter mem blur series
16. Enchanter charm series
17. And near the last but not least: Rogue evade.

If your design team does not have an intuitive understanding of how all of these abilities interplay to manage hate, then ANY thing you do to the hate list is more likely to break it than not. Start with my list, please, for your test planning. You need a test for each specific combination of abilities at LEAST at a general level.

You want us to trust Verant for a major change that allowed the class to be playable when introduced (YES...I had a rogue before evade was available and my ONLY hope of managing hate was to group with warriors AT LEAST 3 levels higher than I was.) We honestly believe that you don't know what the effect will be of the change, particularly in the high level game.

Your past proven abilities with regards to 'thorough testing' do not assuage our disbelief in any way. I'm not trying to be mean, this is just the way it is! I love the game and the rogue class MAY be overpowered at high levels, but that is a fault of doing too much damage with respect to other classes, not necessarily in how evade works.

There is a major balance issue that you are simply NOT discussing that is more powerful than evade: warriors are too good as tanks compared to other classes and melees (particularly pures) do WAY too much damage compared to all other classes. This area of imbalance is not even being touched by the evade changes unless you see rogues as being less able to do damage after the change. Until this imbalance is fixed, everything else is just a tweak. Unfortunately the 'evade tweak' is at the heart of giving the players tools to deal with the 'simple minded' AI and is CRUCIAL to the class.

You have yet to reflect that notion back to us: that YOU KNOW that evade is crucial and that breaking it breaks the game for Rogues. If you said that out loud, you'd go a MUCH LONGER WAY to fixing our concerns than you have so far. And claiming something is 'broken' when we know it works is a bit disingenous as well. It is NOT broken, it is too powerful. The game is not broken because of evade, it is imbalanced. The class is not broken because of evade, there is just not an appropriate point of check or point of balance to the ability.

Deal with it as a relative power topic, and you'll get a better hearing. Tell us it is 'broken' when we know it makes the game fun for us, and you won't get any traction. Understand your audience if you want to influence it.

Does evade need attention? In my opinion it is sufficiently unreliable as it is. Maybe the reliability of it becomes too high at higher levels, but right now (29th level) it is not something I can easily depend on and certainly not something I can take for granted.
