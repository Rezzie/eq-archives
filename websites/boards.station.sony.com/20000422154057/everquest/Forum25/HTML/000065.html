It was a fine day to be hunting in the Mistmore area. The sky was clear, the dark ones were not rushing us, and I was in good company. Old friends and close companions shared the alcove near the hill with my beloved fiance, Kegas, and I. Woodwind, my dear Druid brother, Trill, my sister of the sword, and Artel, a noble song Master, were making the pulls easy and simple. Yes it was a grand day.
Then, naturaly, it happened. There were the usual screams associated with Mistmore, screams od pain and terror, but the large mob that suddenly set upon us was not preceded by a shouted warning.
We had been fighting a pledge near the entrance to the hallway when a multitude of armored Dark Elves (ever notice they are all women?)fell on us. Our bad fortune also lent us the Negotiator.
Though there was no need, we screamed at one another to run for safety. But, alas, I would not join my companions in the safety of the forest.
One moment I was running for my life, the next I was hopelessly srvile to Her. My mind yelled to run but my body ignored it. She had me. The Negotiator. It felt like an eternity was passing thrugh me as I stood guard by her. A docile pet. She spelled and buffed the other hated creatures as they passed before she turned and lead me to the hallway leading to the castle. I knew what was happing and I was helpless to stop it. I would be taken to the castle and lost to the vampiric embrace of Lord Mayong Mistmore. I could not fight her, however, so I followed her to my doom.
Suddenly a familiar voice rang out around me.
"Keera is charmed!" cried Desiel, a good friend. Just as suddenly he was there with me and Marzokk, a would be tank mage, stood with him. My friends. But she still commanded me.
I fought with myself to the point of mentle exhastion but I had no choice but to obey. I crossed swords with Desiel and couldn't even scream. Out of the corner of my eye I saw Marzokk fall to an ancille and I wept from within.
Desiel was attacking my Mistress and I fell on him from behind. It seemed my Mistress admired his barbarian strength and courage for she began to weave a web of enthrallment over him, or tried.
Her concentration was so completely on the warrior that I found my mind free of her control. She lost Desiel with me so we bolted together to the safety of the forest. I fell as a red flash struck me downa the ramp to the exit. Everything went black and then a tunnel of light pulled my soul to safety.
In Greater Faydark I cryed softly for the sins committed against my dear friends. I looked up as Desiel jogged up to me in Spirit Form. He put a big hand on my shoulder and smiled gently.
"I wasn't goning to let them take you to the castle." Then he was gone, disappearing into Lesser Faydark.
Marzokk came up and I fell in his arms thankfully and wept. He smiled and smoothed my hair.
"I had to help he said," he said simply.
To this day these two men have my loyalty and affection, no matter how dangerous that may be
Dedicated to the fighters of Mistmore, especially Desiel and Marzokk.

Keera Mahal
Pheonix Knights

If you need elven brethren of strong wisdom to resist the charms of those blue skinned ladies (and I know how hard it is - lol) then feel free to call on me, or my noble ranger brother Dwaveon (he's been living in Mistmoore for the last ten levels - heheh). In fact I'm surprised Dwaveon wasn't with Artel, Woodwind and Trill - good friends all.

A few weeks ago when none of my friends were online (a rare occurrence) I wandered into Mistmoore and joined a nice controlled group holding the ancille pit - Dyermaker (enc), Aoeb (pal), Foxtrot (clr) and Kerien (war) were my compatriots that day. We held the pit for about an hour against all that Mistmoore could throw at us (I have a screenshot of about 15 ancille corpses - we barely had time to loot, the spawns were popping so fast - thank god for clarity). It was then we espied a Teir'Dal caster on the cliffs above and, seeking a greater challenge to test our mettle, we began the arduous climb up the steep path to the castle - to meet this sorcerous lady, and maybe to meet our doom. But something within the castle was calling to us and we could not resist (for me it was the call of a nightshade wreath - heheh).

We assaulted the heights and the death toll was impressive. With true steel, brave hearts and the wonders of group mez we managed to almost clear the hilltop. The sorceress fell, and we fought our way towards the gatehouse. Our combat must have caused more fiends to stir from deathlike sleep, for a vampirish horde rushed across the drawbridge and we knew we could not beat them, exhausted and trapped as we were. My companions were ready to sell their lives dearly, but crying out to Tunare to save them I channelled a wind that whisked us away to Butcherblock and safety.

It was then with horror we realised that brave Aoeb was not with us, and we feared him slain, fighting the ever growing tide of dark brethren to buy time for our escape. Fortunately he was still guarded by the ghost of a brave wolf I had called to watch over him and speed his steps (or SoW as the heathens call it - lol) and he escaped the wave of screaming terror at his heels.

I made a few plat and picked up some nice fine steel and bronze armour as well, which always makes for a happy ending

Truly epic, but I'm dying to get inside that castle (or is that dying once I get inside that castle - lol). I've heard a rumour that wolf form renders me non-aggro to the mobs in the castle, like against the undead in Unrest, but I haven't had a chance to test it yet. I'm sure one of you knows, maybe my old friend Blaade if you've finally tired of living with the frogloks

As much as I like Cazic Thule (spent a lot of time there), and even Sol B and Paw (just don't mention Perma - lol), something still pulls me back to MM from time to time. Maybe its just that ancille are prettier than greater kobolds . Or perhaps it is the mystery of the place, never knowing if Lord Mayong is home and watching you from a high tower window (maybe waiting on a pathing bug to call him out - lol).

Please call on me if you ever need another sword arm and the blessings of Tunare to hold back the vampirish tide. Any friend of Blaade, Artel, Trill or Woodwind is a friend of mine. Besides, giantslaying and troll-bashing is starting to get real old
