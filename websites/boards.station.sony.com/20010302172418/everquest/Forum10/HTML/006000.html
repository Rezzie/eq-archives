TheSpike
Station Member 		posted 02-23-2001 07:37 AM           
The game is designed to be played in a group - not solo. Therefore, the game scales in such a way that low level monsters are easily defeated (to get players going), then the monsters become more difficult to defeat alone. They have more hit points per level, hit harder and faster, have additional abilities, etc. Some classes have some abilities that can reliably keep creatures away from them while they deliver damage, but Wizards are not one of them. This is a sign that you should start grouping with your Wizard.

If you really want to play solo, the best classes are Druid, Necromancer, and Magician.

What everyone else said. Plus, as you get higher level, the range of blue gets bigger -- for a reason. It starts to get risky to try to kill something that was even to you last level; instead, when soloing, you may need to search for mobs that you somehow know are "low" blue, that will be green in just a level or two.

Indeed, there is a wide variance in difficulty for mobs of a particular level, and which way they vary depends on what class you play. Mobs that poison aren't any more difficult for magicians than other mobs, because (assuming they planned ahead and are using water pet) they can let the pet take all the hits, and water pet is immune to poison. They are much more difficult for wizards to solo than normal melee mobs, especially if you're still meleeing instead of doing root-and-nuke, since a single successful poison will take a huge chunk of your HP.

When you solo, I recommend that you look for mobs that don't have special abilites, and that have low HP and low spell resistance for their level, even if they're tougher in other ways (hitting really hard, hard to hit in melee combat, etc.). That way, you can at least be sure of taking them out fast with your spells. It will take experience (and its close cousin, death) to learn the strengths and weaknesses of different mobs.
