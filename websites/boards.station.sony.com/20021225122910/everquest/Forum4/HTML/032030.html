quote:
Fletching is maxed for my level

You say 'for your level'... there is no level limit on fletching a level 1 can go all the way up to 250 if they had enough plat.

quote:
I may also start baking/brewing sometime soon, my ranger is getting bored with Iron rations

Sounds like you haven't been working on foraging (i think by the time I reached 20 I hadn't needed to buy food in quite a while). An easy way to raise foraging and also sense heading is to set it up so that whenever you hit the left arrow you do sense heading, and right arrow you do forage. Using this method I keep both of those skills maxed... I don't remember exactly how to do this, but go to the options menu and there should be a way to set it up so that the forage hotbutton is pressed when you hit the left arrow. This really becomes a required skill for a ranger so unless later on you want to spend hours hitting the button to get your skill up, I would start now. A good foraging skill is also a help in increasing your baking/brewing skills.

Also, now that you are 22 get the spell panic animal if you haven't already...VERY handy spell for a ranger. Snare, Fear, Kick, Slash..., Kick, Slash..., Fear, etc... until dead. Fear lasts up to 18 seconds...kick refreshes every 6 seconds...so kick twice and re-fear before kick refreshes a 3rd time.

On the initial question - do not worry so much about your rate of increase in levels but rather your enjoyment of the time you have spent in Norrath. Levels will come but enjoyment is the reason we all play! Or at least why I do.

Now:

My first character was a Half Elf Ranger started in April of 1999 (missed the kick off month as I wanted to be sure this was not another Ultima Online with forced 100% PvP).

To him level 20 was truely 'uber' as you got to get a surname and he remembers looking at others running around with surnames and saying to himself "I will have one some day as well!"

I would have to say it probably took a good 10 - 15 days played (via the /played command) to get him to 20. LoL and he had only recently purchased a full set of Banded armor with money he had been saving for a Combine Long sword as his first Magic weapon

He is now level 52 and just completed one of his game long goals of participating in a Vox Raid this past weekend - two in fact but the first was a wipe out ) He has over 60 days played chalked up so far, but I always use him as my explorer of new territory when new lands are discovered and he is very active in his guild. I also run many other characters spread from this level 52 down to 17.

Your 5 days for level 20 is not bad at all. My best so far to that level has been my Dwarf Paladin on Tunare server who made 20 in 3 days 17 hours, and that is with tons of game knowledge that you do not poses as yet.

Now to you second question:

To inspect an item you right click on it and a subscreen comes up with the stats in it. If you right click and hold and then move the cursor off the item while still holding down the right mouse button, the subscreen will stay in place after you let go the mouse button.

Now start your message as normal.

When you want to put the item link in make sure there is a space between where your text is and where the curosor in the text field is and ctrl right clik on the icon of the item in the upper right corner of the item subscreen.

Voila the link will be placed into your text message line.

I have found that it is very important to have a space in front of and behind the link in order to be sure it will work when others click on it.

This can be used in any type of message - tell - say - auction - guild etc etc etc.
