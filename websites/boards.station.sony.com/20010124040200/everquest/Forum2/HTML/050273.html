Typhus
Station Member 		posted 01-20-2001 10:58 AM           
Before I propose this idea, I want to disclaim the fact that I understand how HARD this would be to change. At the same time, if Verant doesn't adopt some kind of change like this, I think it will have a difficult time competing against new games that come out in the future.

VERANT SHOULD REMOVE THE NECESSITY FOR SPELLCASTERS TO MEDITATE

Now, before you freak out, hear me out...

Unless I'm wrong, the concept of mana is there to ensure that spellcasters don't just go crazy casting zillions of spells and getting unreasonable power. The mana pool is what keeps the spellcasters balanced with the other classes.

Keeping the spellcasters balanced with the other classes is, of course, and absolute necessity for the game, but it think there could be an implementation which is more reasonable.

As all casters know, and I'm sure Verant is aware too, a tremendous part of being a spellcaster is staring at your spellbook. For the clerics, this is even more true.
Keep an eye on your mana bar
Keep an eye on your party members
Stand up to cast heals
Open up your spellbook again
Give Verant money
Repeat

A caster's life is generally spent doing nothing... waiting.

I believe this downtime is not only unnecessary, it is also a reason why some competing game company could have a tremendous advantage against Verant. Which would you choose? Everquest, or Everclone that gives you all the same abilities, but doesn't require you to wait for anything.

So, how do you keep game balance if you remove mana? Spell delays, casting times, refresh times - many of the tools already employed. For example, as a necromancer, my spell Harmshield takes 10 minutes before I can cast it again. My ability to cast it has nothing to do with my mana pool. It's all about the refresh delay.

I'll give you an example of how this could work with a cleric, for example. Complete heal, which is a very useful spell, would have a long refresh time - maybe 5 minutes. The cleric couldn't cast Complete Heal in that time. Instead, our healer would have to rely on other healing spells in the mean time. There could be a low level heal which has effectively no refresh time for the cleric, so they could cast it more regulary. Or maybe they have a heal spell that doesn't fizzle while you're moving and fighting, so they could be in combat at the same time. The cleric would join the combat with the rest of the tanks and use their mighty hammer to smite the enemies, being careful to time when they cast their spells, and on who.

Pullers would make sure the casters have got all their spells back before bringing something nasty back to the party.

Instead of INT/WIS giving you more mana, they would decrease the refresh times for lower level spells. As you became more powerful, and got more gear, you could cast the lower level spells more quickly - eventually almost non-stop. But the powerful spells you would have to manage carefully.

Breeze/clarity would decrease refresh times; necro Allure of Death, etc, would exchange life for decreased refresh times.

The bottom line is that that casters would be just as busy as the rest of the party. While they're waiting for the long refresh times to come back, they're casting other spells, or mixing it up in melee. They would have to be careful, and balance their use of spells, but essentially there would be no downtime: just fun.

I want to be clear, under this revised system, PROGRESSION OF ALL CLASSES WOULD BE IDENTICAL, AND COMPLETELY BALANCED.

I believe the concept of mana as a method of maintaining balance is only one of the many available to Verant.

As players, we give Verant money, and Verant gives us fun. In my opinion, Verant has done SO many things right. I love this game. But downtime sucks - and given a choice between Everquest and a game that has no downtime, that's no contest for me.

I know, I know, if I don't want downtime, I should go play a warrior. No, I want to play a spellcaster, and I want no downtime. Any game companies want my money?

Maybe I'm just a little grumpy; however, after having spent two complete evenings attempting to organize a group to go to Split Paw and then Cazik Thule.
Find who wants to go
Recruit new people
Bind the warriors in Arena
Wait while someone goes linkdead
One player has been waiting too long and needs to go to bed.
Recruit another member
Go back to Arena to bind them.
Enter Cazik
Warrior dies from a train as we're going in.
Wait until he makes the run back from Arena.
No one wants to play anymore.
Go to bed.

Tell me that was necessary, or fun, or part of the "Vision". Verant has 300,000 players who tolerate downtime, but only because there isn't anything better on the market... yet.
