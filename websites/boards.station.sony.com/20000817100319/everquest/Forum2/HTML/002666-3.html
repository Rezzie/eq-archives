PvP should be fixed with as simple of a solution as possible:

1) Make it where you can only cast buffs and heals on your own team.

2) DECREASE the number of teams from 4 to 2 (if you notice, most people play lightie vs darkie on the PvP servers anyways). This move to 2 teams will increased the "pool" of characters who can heal and buff you, thus minimizing any negative effects of my first suggestion.

3) ELIMINATE the options for guilds to house more than one team. This way, you actually create roleplay on PvP servers. I have no idea why a 4 team server allows a guild to house all 4 teams in it. Doesn't that defeat the purpose of roleplay?

4) Allow all players who don't like these changes to move to Rallos Zek where there are no teams.


That's it. Real simple, and brings Roleplay to the PvP servers.

<< If a player takes damage from another PC, they can only receive beneficial spells from someone in their own team for X-minutes or until they zone

A better solution would be to qualify the above change by applying it only in the case where the healer is of the same team as the charcter who did the damage.

On Tallon Zek, the sserver environement has evolved into a 2-team war, Good vs Evil. This is very much validated by Verant's design of the EQ background. Quests, geography and faction all combine to create the appearance that the different light races, whatever their differences, are natural allies against the "evil" races.

This would be a natural expectation of many newbies, anyway. Like the majority of fantasy novels, Lord of the Rings (which invented orcs <g> ) has the dwarves, elves, humans and halflings as allies.

Given the Good vs Evil orientation of Tallon Zek, restricting the ability of light races to heal each other would be pretty unpopular, I think. It is important to also cater to those who want to rebel against the server paradigm by attacking everyone not on their team - but not by allowing the desires of this very small minoriity to ruin the fun of the majority, by destroying their abiltiy to heal each other in combat.

Seems like applying the no healing rule ONLY in the case of an invulnerable same-team healer would be a reasonable compromise, rather than nerfing something wanted by the majority in order to please the minority.

<< the "crossteaming" is purely used as an exploit, it serves no other function in the game whatsoever

This is an pretty idiotic statement: the so-called "cross-teaming" serves many fuctions.

To name a few:
1) Making your side stronger through alliances, ie giving you more numerical strength;
2) Convenience: (a) when the only healer in a zone is from a different light team, you need that healer.
(b) Very often you will not find people of your own team to group with. It's hard enough as it easy, without making it 3 times as difficult.
3) Lights are spread out all over Norrath. It is totally impractical to try to create single team groups, given this fact.
4) Roleplaying and Fun: it is good RP for elves, human or dwarves to group. It also makes the game more practical.
5) Quests and geography: "good" races need to visit each other cities to do quests. They need to hunt in each other's areas in order to level. There are NO high level elf hunting grounds, for instance. Elves must go to Unrest (in dwarf territory, or else to human territory. Given this fact, is is impractical not to group with humans or dwarves.
6) On TZ, the "dark" team is as large as the other 3 teams together. Lights must group together.
