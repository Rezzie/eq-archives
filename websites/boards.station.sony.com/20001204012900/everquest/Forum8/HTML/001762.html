A very good and true post.

I have a lvl 44 Troll shamen, and to date i have spent over 5500K pp to raise his alchemy skill to 54, and the first 20 points were achived through training points. Of that money, almost nothing was recovered by selling my successes to players.

The biggest problem with the skill is that many of the recipes are still need to have thier costs halved. Verant wants to make "across the board" changes, and that worked the first time, but most of the higher trivial lvl potions are way to expensive for their usefulness. Someone needs to sit down and look at each potion, and weigh the "Cost vs Reward" which really is "Risk vs Reward" for the shamen.

Perhaps the costs of the potions could remain the same, if they would simply let them sell back to merchants at slightly below cost, as nearly every other trade skill does. It is not fair to spend 10 or 15pp trying to make a potion,(with an already high fail rate that no one will buy), just to raise your skill, and then have that potion sell back to a merchant for 4 gold.

I know verant views this and most other trade skills as "money sinks", but the size of the drain is simply too big. They just don't understand, because no one at verant has ever used their hard earned money on this skill.

Verant if you want proof of what i'm saying, try this:

Create a lvl 35 Shamen. Go out into the world and play and kill whatever you can, until you have saved up 1000pp. (note just how long this takes, and how much effort is put into it.)

Then go practice alchemy with that 1000pp, until it is all gone. (assume you put 20 training points into the skill at the start). See just how many trys you get, how many complete failures, and what potions you have, (in terms of usefulness or re-sale value to players). Then, as a last stab in the heart, take your worthless potions that cost 10pp to make, (that you can't sell to players), to a merchant and sell them for 4 gold.

I think you will find that you basicly wasted hours upon hours of gameplay earning that money that you basicly through away, on a skill you were excited about, and had faith in.

(Before you quote this and say it...NO it was not fun playing during that time and earning that money when, in the end, it is all wasted in just a faction of the time it took to earn it.) It is truely a waste of a person's time and efforts in its current state.

The skill has the *potential* to be useful and fun to the shamen doing it, but it needs adjustment.

Do the above, and you might get some taste of the heart-ache that the players feel, and why we are complaining and demanding change. It is a "Risk vs Reward" issue, and thats one of your favorite topics.
