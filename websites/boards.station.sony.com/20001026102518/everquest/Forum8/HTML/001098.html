In the past I have posted some ideas on trade skills, and have seem some of the ideas actually find their way to the game, so either I'm thinking along the same lines as Verant or maybe they got an idea or two from looking at these boards. So here are a few more thoughts:

Poison vials. When a rogue successfully poisons a weapon, it should return a Contaminated Poison Vial that is cleaned by putting it into a kiln with some alcohol, esencially boiling it out. This would return a Poison Vial. In other words, this would cut down the cost of using poisons slightly, by eliminating the need to at least buy poison vials.

Pelts. In a world that runs rampant with various fur-bearing creatures, and in a world that has a great deal of demand for such pelts, there are no trappers? There should be at least one fellow or lady in each zone with a lot of these creatures that is a trapper. This person could sell any given pelt that is in the area, and perhaps some tailoring supplies. They could charge for pelts by varying degrees... and this would GREATLY increase the availability of such pelts.

Consider this: ONLY Tailoring requires not only every piece of raw material to be hunted down (some of which are very rare, quest-level rare), but it is the ONLY trade skill that REQUIRES other trades to advance in, to its low maximum skill of 115. Honestly I really appreciate the smithing fixes and additions, but Smithing really was not the trade that needed the most attention.

Here I want to repeat a call for a tailoring idea. Presently you can take a skinning knife and pare down a pelt to a lesser state, MQ to LQ and so forth. This is very handy. However, a tailor could patch together pieces of lesser pelts to make something BETTER as well.

So here is again what I propose:

Combine 2 ruined pelts in a kit to make 1 Low Quality pelt of that type. (trivial at 20)
Combine 2 Low Quality pelts in a kit to make 1 Medium Quality pelt of that type. (trivial at 40)
combine 2 Medium Quality pelts in a kit to make 1 High Quality pelt of that type. (trivial at 60)

and I'd like to add this:

Combine 2 skins (lion skin, grizzly skin, etc) of no quality to make 1 Ruined Pelt of that type (cat, bear, etc).

I'm sure other people have ideas of this type too, such as recipes for bronze armor, fine steel weapons, etc.

People seem to have the idea that Easier == Better. Tailoring has the unique characteristic that every recipie requires a mob drop. This is a good thing. Unlike every other trade skill, I have never seen a Tailoring mule. It takes time to make a master Tailor.

Your 'solution' to this 'problem' would simply make Tailoring just another trade skill. A matter of a few hours and some plat. Way to invalidate every master tailors weeks of time they put into becoming master tailors.

There are Trappers in the game. I know many a PC who kills mobs and sells thier pelts. Oh, you are looking for NPC trappers? Picky picky.

The turning of named pelts into Ruined or LQ sounds reasonable. But, upgrading pelts is not. Same reason. If you patch together pieces of pelt, you get a patchwork pelt. And guess what? You can make patchwork armor out of it. The upgrades wouldn't be that bad if they where of bounded length (ie, the LQ pelts produced by the Ruined couldn't become MQ), _and_ if it was a much higher ratio (like 6 : 1), but as you planned it, it would make a HQ bear pelt a complete joke to get.

Do you know why BackPacks command such a high price? Because in order to make them, you have to spend weeks of time, and lots of plat, becoming a master tailor. Then and only then you can buy pelts from the Trappers (other PCs), and turn them into something useful.

If you haven't noticed, the blacksmithing upgrade has made BSing more like Tailoring, in that the new higher trivial level recipies all require mob drops (with the notable exception of Iksar non-magical cultural, which just confuses me).

I think this is a great thing, and I expect this pattern to continue. Your wish to turn Tailoring into yet another silly skill you can buy will probably never be fullfilled.

If you don't like tailoring, please go ahead and not work on it. I like tailoring. I hope that furture tailoring improvements follow the same pattern - recipies and recipie values are controlled not by the cost of purchased reagents, but by the difficulty of finding the ingredients. Hopefully with parallel, not as valueable, just as high trivial level, easier to get stuff to skill up with. ie, not much harder to do than say fine plate.

Here's what I find amusing. The only complaing people have with this idea isn't that its BAD or that it would ruin the trade, just that you don't like it because you either

A) wouldn't make as much money as before
B) it would be more like other trades

Now, since both of those are subjective answers, something you personally don't like, they are not real arguments for why Verant should not do it.

Real arguments for why Verant SHOULD do it:

1) Tailoring at present takes hours and hours of real time to produce anything useful and saleable, whereas every other trade skill in the game takes a matter of minutes.

2) Tailoring at present has no vendors, whereas every other trade skill does, which makes no sense from a Role Play or logic standpoint. There would BE vendors for furs in a world that had demand for them. There would and should BE trappers, not just adventurers, but actual people.

3) Tailoring relies more heavily on other trades than any other trade in the game, and has very, very little to offer them in return. In addition, things like metal boning not being stackable makes the trade prohibitively difficult to produce certain items. Thus, if anyone actually has even one piece of reinforced armor, it is a stunning oddity in the game. This kind of thing would actually make the production of the OTHER products in tailoring (than backpacks, that is) more feasible and useful.

4) It actually makes sense to sew together different pieces of pelts together to make better quality material. It's what people do right now.

5) Every time someone higher level kills off a bear, wolf, or cat, that is one thing that lower level people do not have to hunt. In many cases this actually is KSing. This is something Verant doesn't like, bottom feeding.

As an end result, the trade overall becomes a better skill, more profitable, and produces a wider variety of items, like Verant intended. Yes, you don't get to make as much on HQ Bear pelts, and aww I feel so bad for you.
