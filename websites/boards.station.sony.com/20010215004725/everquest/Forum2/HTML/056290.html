So let's talk about necromancers.
As with all the other threads, I want to talk about class balance here, not about bugs or epic weapons. You may certainly present bugs and concerns about the epic weapons, but they will not be the main concern of this thread.

Unlike the other threads, I have a fairly good list of ideas already garnered from several other posts to start us out. It is not complete, I'm sure. But it is certainly a good place to start our discussion.

Also, we are not looking to make necromancers any more effective at soloing than they already are. And, well, the necromancer is a pretty powerful class as they are now. The primary issues that I see revolve around the battle with the boss monsters. But I certainly want to hear from necromancers with concerns about all levels of the game. And, once again, I have to warn you that since we will be looking closely at this class we may very well feel that something needs to have its power reduced to achieve balance.

Anyhow, here's what I have already.

Ignite Bones: Despite the coolness factor, this spell can't really be used on the big raids because it suddenly pulls the enemy out of range, and by the time you relocate your range, one warrior could conceivably have done more damage than the spell is going to do. The best solution is to make the skeleton match the monster, if we can find some way to do that. Fixing this one spell might solve a lot of concerns. If we can’t size the skeleton, then we probably should remove it and find some other cool way to make sure that the spell 'sticks' to the target to prevent chain casting and to make it more than just another DD. Note: Any change to this spell should also be made to Chill Bones (55).

Lifetaps: The number one request is to allow Lifetaps to land on the big monsters. If we are telling necromancers that they have a valid place at the final encounter because they can give mana to other casters, then they will need a way to regain mana. Lich and Lifetap are the way they do that now. I am not convinced that this is something that we should change. But this is the single most asked for thing by the necromancer community. Perhaps, if made in conjunction with the fixing the bug in Lich, then perhaps this could be balanced.

DoTs and corpse poofing: This affects everyone. Hopefully this is now much improved, but I’m not taking it off the list until I’m convinced it has been fixed.

Mana transfer: Again, if their ‘place’ against the boss mob is to transfer mana, then perhaps we should allow them to do it at a better rate. A 2:1 transfer rate might be enough. I'm not at all certain that this should be done in conjunction with any change to Lifetaps in those encounters, though, as a change in one makes the other far more effective and might just be too much of a change. But this could certainly be considered if we fix the bug with the Lich spells.

Infusion: Make infusion targetable. The necro might still see it as a 'worthless spell', but if another caster wants to sacrifice their experience for 1000 mana, they might actually cast this spell. Otherwise, those Essence Emerald’s will only be used for raising the dead.

Defoliation: There seem to be plenty of plants that this just doesn't work on. I fully understand how we need to limit the use of any spell that is overly efficient against any one monster type. But it might be worthwhile to evaluate the things that are not considered plants that maybe should be. I've asked the players for a list that they'd like reviewed, but I've received nothing yet.

Track Corpse: Broken spell. Is it going to stay that way?

Epic Weapon: The look and effect are not well loved in the community.

Lich Series: This series of spells is broken. It is more powerful than it should be. It takes the necromancer's hit points and turns them into mana. It was calculated at a certain ratio to be 'in balance'. Unfortunately, these spells were adversely affected by the reduction of damage to spells used by players on players. This change also reduced the hit points taken from the necromancer by Lich spells, so that the ratio of mana gained for hit points lost is nearly 1 for 1 (instead of about 3 for 2). This is a bug that we have chosen not to change in the past because it would be a 'nerf'. I think in conjunction with a serious look at the entire class and the balance of all the classes, we should also look at this series and decide if we should put it right. If it remains as it is, the necromancer is going to be limited in the improvements it can gain, as the class is already using a powerful and broken spell series. Note: Series includes: Arch Lich (60), Demi Lich (60), Lich (49), Call of Bones (34), Allure of Death (20).

Alan

Variations for high-end balance:

Balance Infusion in comparison to Harvest, 34th level wizard spell, which at these levels gives ~200-300 mana every 10 minutes for no component. If Infusion requires an Essence Emerald for balance (an idea I like), it should not consume it.

Screaming Terror is powerful, but may be seen as impinging on the enchanter's area. Its original implementation was far more necromantic, but didn't work properly. With the program changes that have been made since then, would it be possible to go back to the original implementation - not a mesmerization, but an upgrade to Fear that totally cleared the hate list? If that's possible, then a later upgrade may be appropriate, such as a point-blank area effect version - but I can't see how an upgrade to the current version would be balancing.

Our undead-blasting spells, an area in which we should excel, become strangely useless in the mid-levels. Consider:
Shock of Poison (level 24): 100 mana, 160 damage, all targets, usually hits as it goes against typically low poison resistance.
Dismiss Undead (level 29): 90 mana, 162 damage, undead only, often resisted as it goes against magic resistance. The undead limitation and high resistance is hardly a fair trade for 10 mana.
Similarly:
Torbas Acid Blast (level 34): 130 mana, 220 damage, all targets.
Expel Undead (level 39): 130 mana, 273 damage, undead only. Slightly better, but resistance is again an issue, making it an inferior choice to a spell that can hit any target.

It seems that either we need these spells much earlier, or the spells themselves should be more effective given the undead-only restriction. This gets better with the higher level spells.

Less of a balance thing but more of a raid thing: Necromancers would also be good candidates for a kind of Death Pact. Maybe something like... cast on a party member before battle, when target's HP drop below 0, spell kicks in with 1000 point heal, illusion:skeleton, and a long-lasting DoT that does 100 points/tick. This effectively lets a necromancer "animate" a dead party member for a very short amount of time - enough to help out in a raid situation, but the target is still going to die. Pact of the Living Dead?

Upgrading all undead everywhere to justify our undead-only lines might make us more wanted (or hated for suggesting it), but I can't imagine it would bring "balance" in any kind of way. Darned amusing, though.

Obligatory balance opinions: Fix the Lich line, Lifetaps should always land, raidworthy 59 pet.

Lich line of spells - Don't change it. It's supposed to be a 3::2 ratio, and you say it's closer to 1::1? Ok, given that 3::2 translates into 1.5::1, how close to 1::1 are we talking, here? 1.4::1? 1.3::1? 1.2::1? 1.1::1? I really don't see this as a big deal, especially considering the fact that these spells can kill the user. A pretty helpless feeling to watch someone go LD while running one of these spells and die before they completely disco. Should we also mention the bug that makes you continue to lose hp even after cancelling the spell? Having this happen at the end of a bitter fight that you come out oom and almost hp can be fatal. BTW, this little bug can also pop up with our Words of Darkness item, although rarely.

Ignite/Chill Bones - Although the skeletal illusion is pretty cool, it's rather odd to see non-humanoid targets suddenly displaying a humanoid skeleton. A rather minor quibble compared to the targetting problem, however. An alternative effect might be, in the case of Ignite Bones, at least, to extend the duration of the flames effect, so the target appears to be awash in flames while the spell remains in effect. Something similar could be done with Chill Bones.

Lifetaps and Uber Mobs - Considering how much damage the melee classes can dish out in the same amount of time a necro can cast most taps, is it really that necessary to make these mobs 100% immune to lifetaps? Even giving the mobs only a 50% chance of completely resisting the lifetap would be an improvement over how it is now.

DoTs - Still cause corpses to poof. A guildmate of mine was in Fear over the weekend. Unfortunately for him, the raid leader included a number of newcomers to the planes, who insisted on DoTing stuff. He'd been hoping for some Carmine. Instead, he got to watch many Frightfinger corpses go poof.

Track Corpse - I'm happy that it works at all, frankly. A bit hazardous to use in zones like Lavastorm, though, and I'd hate trying to use it in a dungeon. Something similar to how normal tracking might work a little better.

Defoliation - I don't see this as being any more troublesome than undead-specific DD spells. Considering the lack of worthwhile targets when you get the spell, it's hardly worth having in your spellbook. If there are undead in the planes of Hate and Fear, why aren't there plants in the Plane of Growth? On a related note, whatever happened with the 29th level spell, Defoliate? Would be nice to actually make that spell available.

DMF - Perhaps reconsider some of the places where this cannot be cast? Group DMF would certainly be a cool idea for a spell, but I don't think it would address any balance issues.

Targettable Buffs - It would be nice if a few more of our spells could be cast on others. Deadeye, Gather Shadows, Shadow Sight, and Banshee Aura, perhaps. Shadow Sight (a spell which grants ultravision, to those not familiar with it), is useless to a dark elf necro. Making it castable on others would give it a place in my spellbook. Banshee Aura would be nice if it could be cast on a pet, even.

Debuffs - How about an upgrade to the 20th level Shadow Vortex spell? Making the Scent of X spells a little less resistable might help, too.

Screaming Terror - An upgrade to this might be nice, too. I agree with others in being puzzled why enchanters can't overwrite ST. Perhaps this should be looked into, as well.

Sacrifice & Essence Emeralds - The way it works now, the only people who can use these spells are the ones able to PL up sacrificial lambs for the purpose. I don't know anyone willing to undergo a rez-less death for my benefit. This leaves the rest of us out in the cold. Don't get me wrong, I love the concept, but in practice it leaves something to be desired. One suggestion might be that, in addition to being usable on a willing player, make Sacrifice usable on an unwilling mob. Retain the level restrictions, give it a damage component, and require that the spell actually kill the target, leaving no corpse. Maybe not even always result in an essence emerald, just a chance of it.

Epic Weapon - I think its nickname says it all.

Thank you for your consideration.
