The discussions on this are endless.

Perhaps rather than looking at starting over, we should take a look at your tactics with your Druid, because they are excellent soloists. Maybe we can "fix" your current character.

Here's a quick "Kiting 101, Druid Edition"


Find an outdoor zone, with MOBs well spread out. Use tracking to locate a dark blue /con opponent. Snare it, and pull it towards an area that is relatively safe from wanderers. Then:

1. Blast Kite - run around your "safe area" pausing periodically to cast Direct Damage (and DoT) spells on your opponent.

2. Root/DoT - root your opponent in a safe spot, DoT it, and sit down to meditate, refreshing spells as needed.

3. Fear Kite - with an animal, cast a fear spell, then melee/nuke/DoT it from behind as it tries to flee, refreshing fear as needed.


If you run low on mana, you can also do a small amount of Porcupining (melee with a damage shield spell on).

Now, how exactly are you having problems with your Kiting?

I always thought druids were a pretty straightforward class to play.

Snare, root, dot.

Or, snare, run, nuke (maybe run and dot too since they removed the penalty to dot's on mobs chasing you).

Or, snare animal, fear, dot or nuke, optional mellee while it's running.

Or, the ever popular quad kite. Snare four things, run in a circle around them to get them in a tight group, run and use area effect nuke to kill all four at once.
