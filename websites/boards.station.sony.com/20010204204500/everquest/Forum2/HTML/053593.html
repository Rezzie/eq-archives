I'm starting this thread today because I'll be leaving for Baltimore earlier than I had expected. I will have little access to the Internet while I'm there, and I don't want to start a thread like this right before I leave. Also, for you Rogues reading, I'm moving you back to Tuesday next week, I won't be in the office on Monday.
Monks are one of those interesting types of warrior that, as a class, we have tried to balance a rather large volume of damage dealing with a lesser type of armor. It's not an easy task to build a fighting class that is 'balanced' with a warrior but doesn't wear armor…

So tell me what you think needs to be changed about monks. Keep in mind that I am primarily looking for balance issues, but give me whatever you've got. I am also not looking for suggestions that would dramatically change the class, or that would require an army of people to get into the game. I'm mostly looking for tweaks that would help to balance the class.

Thanks.

Time to put a ripple in the pond...

What I would wish for in a monk:

I wish verant would take a look at the intrensic ac/regen/ultravision/better base stats coding from an iksar, and consider dumping a few of those attributes on a human.

Before I get bombed off the board, let me try to rationalize. A good deal of human monks were created LONG before the Iksar existed. There was no choice in the matter (nullifies the gnome warrior vs troll warrior argument, in that I was never given a choice). I cannot change my mind. I am 60, i have close to 200 days played, i have many many no drop items, and to scrap my human for an iksar would be an insult to my guild for rewarding me with these items. Simply leveling an iksar monk would be no big deal, but replacing the no drop items would be extremely difficult, as well as self indulgent. With that said, here's the wish(es):

I. Ultravision
First and foremost, as a human I would die for ultravision (naked ultravision, as i can get this through items / spells). Countless times on a total party wipeout, monks and necromancers basically 'race' to see who gets CR done first - i win, we save the guild coffin money/time, necro wins, generally when i die trying :/. Total party wipeout implies that im dead...and naked...and blind. How imbalancing would it be to make ultravision a class based attribute regarding monks? Ie: not all humans get ultra, but the monk class, with thier Zen ways, have achieved a skill, human or lizard, to see in the dark.

II. That ac thing
When im naked, my ac is near 800 (i think more like 780), while my iksar brother is closer to 900 (more like 860). I understand the scales and so forth. But when an iksar and human get all geared up in top line equipment, the iksar will always win, by about 30ac. Would it be too much to ask, to up the base ac of a human to that of an iksar? Can't be but 5-10%% difference. Cant be imbalancing, as its present in game.

III. Regen
Not a huge factor in the high level game. I'm content regenning less than my iksar counterparts.

IV. Stats
Again, no biggie, im content being a little less 'stat-isfied'. I can accept it.

V. Non iksar jealousy topics
1) FD - please consider letting FD wipe hate on huge mobs. It has nothing to do with splitting big mobs, it has to to with monk damage being handed over in small amounts with extreme rapid delivery. We are all over the hate list, and for some reason, FD wont take us off any more. Generally leads to me getting smooshed. Typical notion is to just let me die and get ressed, as is would cost far less mana, than to try to keep me alive.
2) Disciplines/damage output
I dont bother with many disc. Just stonestance,innerflame, and the occational riposte. Would be great to see stonestance duration extended a bit, and the refresh of innerf/whirl dropped a bit. Thunderkick is glamordamage. It doesnt interest me either way.
Im fairly content with damage output. I have to say flying kick for 3 is silly...something to consider.
3)Defensive Qualities
Would be nice to gain just a bit more ac (see above) and perhaps raise our defensive qualities a bit.
4) Inherent Resists
Naked, lvl 60 hum monk has 44 RF (iksar is deferent i think). (seems like i was born with 33 RF - so an 11 RF bonus through 60 levels). Would be extremely kind of you to move this 11 RF to RM.
5) Mend
after skill 100 mend is static. Would be neat to make mend a bit different after 201. For instance:
a) shrink the 6 minute cycle a bit (5-10%)
b) make it heal for a touch more (5-10%)
c) allow for us to target others and mend them (loh analogy)
6) Sneak
I see almost no use for sneak. Can help in scouting / pulling, but very limited. At the very least, id like to be able to run full speed with sneak, and maybe sneak in the water. I also wish it would work on at least 90% blue mobs, and not just mobs 7+ levels lower than me. Would love to have sneak function as invis, but dont want to trample into the rogues dept. :P
7) ID
If instill doubt is maxed. It should 'land every time', with a resist check for the fear component. As it stands, i can make 4-5 ID attempts (thus omitting 4-5 flying kicks, thus gambeling a kill by not handing the mob more damage) and have it work only once or twice. I'd be happier with a 'resist' than a 'nothing'.

I'd like to add, that im very happy with the monk class in gerneal: the monk role is great. The playability and 'fun-factor' is way up at the top. I'd just like a few minor enhancements that would bring us all in line with the evolution that has taken place with RoK / SoV.
That's all for now. Thanks for opening the thread.

I agree with what most are saying in the other threads, especially about mininum damage and such. I don't think a high-end special attack deserves such low-end damage with a huge time delay. But I am sure that will be worked out.

My ideas:

Probably been said already, but maybe not, and since I am using valuable work-time (hehe) I can't read all 250+ posts.

The vision statement for the monk class does indeed say toe-to-toe we should be the best melee damagers in the game. What do we lose for this glorious status? Well, hit points, AC, and we get the notorious weight limit. Do rogues or warriors get this? No. So we should be pumped up a bit to resume our high-end damage reputation. After all, we sacrifice more than rogues or warriors do to be allowed that ability, we should at least have the ability in return. From what I remember, pre-RoK was like that. Monks were, without question, the highest dmg dealers in the game. Were they the best melee class, unbalancing all? No. They did not have the warrior HP or AC, or the rogues evade.

Thus, while pre-RoK we could deal the best damage, we were balanced by our restrictions. Now we are not the best damage dealers, but still have these restrictions.

Solution: Either loosen up on restrictions, or enhance abilities. Either way I would be happy. If I can't deal the most damage, then give me some more HP and AC options in return. Or if I get boosted up to deal more damage, then the current restrictions will once again make sense.

A couple of feign death comments:

First, to the person who suggested you should be able to hit feign a second time to stand back up instead of having to hit the sit button, PLEASE don't do this!!!!! The way feign works right now, you can hit feign repeatedly without ever standing up to clear more agro (with the risk of course that you will botch a feign and everything will come running at you). Changing this will make it impossible for monks to clear agro on multiple mobs ever.

Second, something really should be done to eliminate the need for monks to /q when feigned. We already have the ability to totally wipe agro by /qing, it takes about 5 minutes to do but most high level monks do this often. Why not give them the ability to do it legitimately while staying inside the game? Here's what I would suggest. Create a new skill (or discipline even if it had a very short reuse time) that would cause the monk to feign and be effectively mezmerized for 3 minutes. During this time the monk can't get up, move or do anything, but if he's attacked the mez is broken and he can fight back or refeign. At the END of the 3 minutes, the monk is totally agro wiped and the monk can get up and proceed. This will only give the monk the ability to do what we already do anyways, but be able to stay in the game, not need to reform groups, etc.

And, of course, I agree with most of the other posters that a moderate increase in damage is needed for monks. If the typically equipped post 50 monk is really doing around the same damage as the typically equipped post 50 warrior (as most of us feel is the case), then we are relatively equal at offence but severely deficient in defense compared to warriors, and the balance needs to be adjusted.

My main character is a level 60 monk on the Xegony server. I'm very happy playing a monk, I think they're a very enjoyable and entertaining class. However I think there are several issues that need to be considered when attempting to balance them with other melee classes.

First of all, I think monks should be able to taunt. As a monk I have a lot of difficulty taunting a mob off a caster who has agro'd it, much more than any other tank class. Would this be unbalancing? No, and here is why--ALL other melee classes have taunt, with the exception of rogues (who are designed with anti-taunt in mind, fighting an enemy from behind while it is attacking someone else). I have good ac and hp's when properly buffed, and my defensive skills are very good as well. However groups I'm in usually have to rely on a "taunting tank" in order to feel secure that the casters wont get shredded the moment they agro a mob. Warriors, paladins, shadowknights, rangers all have the ability to taunt and get a mob's attention; monks can only beat on a mob and hope their melee damage causes agro to change at some point. Yes, melee damage does have a taunt factor, but it works nowhere near as fast as the taunt skill and agro caused by spellcasting. If a monk is expected to do all his taunting through melee damage then they need to have their damage output scaled up to be closer to a rogue's. Also there are now weapons available to other melee classes that allow them to approach, if not surpass, a monk's melee damage output so I don't think giving monks the ability to taunt would unbalance them. If monks were given the taunt skill they would be able to be balanced with other melee types as a group's tank and main damage-taker.

Another issue I have is with the near-total inability to interrupt casting mobs. As a monk I am at a severe disadvantage when fighting a mob who casts dangerous spells such as gate or complete heal. Monks need some type of stun attack to interrupt in these situations (yes we do get a discipline at level 59 that has a chance of stunning, but it only works every 10 minutes or so; basically this gives us bash with a 10 minute recycle, as opposed to other classes who have their stun/interrupt skills recycle every few seconds). My solution to this would be to give one of the monk's auxillary attacks (kick, round kick, eagle strike, tiger claw, dragon punch) a stun component. This way a monk would be able to choose between the greater damage of flying kick, or the ability to interrupt casting with the lower damage skills. If a warrior is able to kick with stunning potential, why would a monk not be able to do so with their much more powerful kicks and strikes?

Another issue I have is with monk enhancements at 55 and 60 compared with what other melees get. Warriors get stun kicks at 55 and triple attack at 60. Rogues get double backstab at 55 (making their damage output soar far above anyone else, period) and randomly lethal backstabs in certain situations at 60. Monks get....1 extra hitpoint per tick while feigned at 55, and another at 60. This is completely and totally insignificant, as no monk spends enough time feigned to notice any real effect from this. If a monk needs hp while feigned he simply waits for mend to recycle. My solution to this? Monks should be given the ability to double attack with auxillaries (flying kick, dragon punch etc) and should also get a higher minimum damage to flying kick (kicks for 3 are embarrassing when a rogue is backstabbing for hundreds); or they need to be given some type of stun with one of their special attacks, as mentioned above. Another possibility would be to make the monk special attacks independent of one another (allowing a monk to use both flying kick and dragon punch, for instance) instead the way it is now, where all specials are greyed until the one last used recycles.

Another issue has to do with shiverback armor from the plane of fear. Quite frankly it stinks. Monks are the ONLY class that does not look forward to getting their planar armor. Other classes drool from newbiehood at the thought of an apothic robe or indicolite breastplate etc, however monks typically dont even wear their shiverback, much less treasure it as their best pieces of equipment. I wore my robe of the lost circle (that I quested for at level 37) over a shiverback jerkin when I finally got it, simply because it is a superior piece of equipment. The same goes for nearly all of my inventory slots--I wear some random piece of all/all gear with good ac and/or stats simply because shiverback isnt competitive (I'm not talking about amazingly good or rare stuff here, I'm talking about stuff I've had for 30 levels like azure sleeves or gatorscale legs). I know this is a touchy issue because you dont want monks to have ac that outclasses warriors, but I think something should be done to at least make shiverback armor desirable (maybe give it some rightclick effects since monks got no rightclick kunark armor like other classes, or extra bonuses to resists perhaps?) to the monks who are just becoming planesworthy but not looking forward to improving their gear like other classes.

About the epic fists--I love them (although I do think the quest is too easy compared to the other epics, sorry guys but you know it's true, it's not even close). I think they're wonderful, they allow a monk to solo very easily, and have a very viable alternative to grouping. However when grouped the effect is hardly effective at all. Every haste buff overwrites it, so whenever I get aanya's quickening or speed of the shissar I have to sacrfice the 54 atk and 12ac granted by the fists effect in favor of greater haste. What should happen is the fists should be made to work like the robe of whistling fists did, namely that it should still grant the atk/ac bonuses while allowing the monk to get haste from a faster enchanter buff.

Another issue is with the level 60 discipline, Ashenhand. As many may not be familiar with it, what it does is make the next eagle strike landed do surprising damage (usually around 300, sometimes more/less), and in certain situations has a chance to outright kill a mob (this has NEVER happened to me or anyone else I know who has used this discipline btw). This discipline is much less effective than others; I can sometimes do around 2k damage in 10 seconds with innerflame, and it recycles more than twice as fast as ashenhand. I'm not sure what should be done to improve it, but I think it should be changed in some way to make it a desirable addition to the monks repertoire of disciplines, most of which are quite nice in my opinion.

I hope some of these issues are addressed, and I appreciate the effort that has gone into balancing the classes so that most people will be satisfied to a reasonable extent.
