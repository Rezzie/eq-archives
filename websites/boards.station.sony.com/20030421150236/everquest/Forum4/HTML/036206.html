"How can you get the stat benefit from different types of foods then? They cant all be in the next to be consumed spot?"

At any given time, you only get the benefit of one type of food. If you need a different set of stats, you switch places.

"Does the frequency at which you need food vary at all? By class? Depending on activity?"

The only differences I know of is that in some desert zones, drinks are consumed faster than normal. Also, if you are using a horse, then you consume food rapidly.

I usually keep a stack of iron rations and one or two stacks of water/milk on me. Then I forage as I play, force eating anything that won't fit into my 1st backpack. I haven't for a while, but I used to keep a Harvest Crystal and a Spring Crystal in my pack and just forget about them. Harvest summons food, Spring summons water (both No Rent), 10 charges to each. Buy them at your local alchemy shop (the guys who sell Gate potions and Chipped bone wands). I'd carry them forever, but when I'd finally get that "You are low on water" message in the middle of nowhere, it was nice to click myself a couple bites to eat or drink at a time until I could get back to town.
