I know that you said the list is likely closed, but I had an interesting thought the other night and I figured that I would spout out the idea since I think it adds some 'flavor' to the Ranger class.

A Ranger in an outdoor zone is at his or her best scouting the landscape for a safe spot to land their group, and tracking their quarry through the most daunting foliage. We are pullers, trackers, and often the battle scout for our groups. Well here's an interesting idea which lands on current skill sets:

In OUTDOOR zones only - allow Rangers of sufficient level a better tool for scouting those dangerous areas. We get sneak, we get hide, it would be an interesting twist for Rangers to almost become Rogue like while in the forest. Even capable of sneaking into that camp of Undead to retrieve a fallen comrade, or to report back for a well mounted offensive.

I do not believe that this makes Camo useless, as I see Camo to be more of a travel spell that can also be used on others. An outdoor Sneak/Hide ability would make Rangers even more complex in the outdoor world and further embed the idea that Rangers are most at home....outdoors.

Probably got this thought out too late, but I had to get it off my brain.
