Hmm, I find a lot of people who posted to this thread are claiming tracking by IP addresses isn't possible. Whether the IP is static or dynamic, it is possible to track it down, to a real life address, and eventually person at that address, as long as it is a personal computer. If you want to keep your anonymity (sp?) your best bet is to either post from a library computer, or a mall computer, something that can't be tracked back to you independantly. IP's are finger prints. It is very possible to trace them (though it does take a lot of time) to a individual system. even in a large LAN, which supplies differant IP's to each system, and then rotates them, it can be tracked. Tracking an IP to a realife address and then a real life name, is (although time-consuming) one of the easiest things for a cracker to do. It's, metaphorically, a piece of cake.

But you don't have to take my word for it. If you want to see how it's done, I suggest you to go to your local bookstore/computer store and purchase the following books:

Maximum Security, 1st edition and Maximum Security, 2nd edition. The 1st edition will show you from a hackers view how to do this very task, as well as many others, and the 2nd edition will show from a server administrators view how to prevent it. No administrator should ever be without these books in their personal library.

If you more information, go to any web search engine and put in the following key words, hack, crack, IP, anarchy, phreak. You'll get a load of addy's to see how it is possible to track people down via IP. I'm not saying there is a conspiracy, I'm also not paranoid, but I am well aware of the dangers that exist. and to say they don't is foolish. Internet and Anonymous, doesn't coexist.
