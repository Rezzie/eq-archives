Skalme,

Not sure about your "pool hall analogy", but I can only imagine that it was something like letting someone else take over the use of the balls when you leave without paying. This is not what is happening in account transfers. In account transfers, if the person "buying" the account purchases a new copy of EQ and picks up the monthly payments, then Verant has lost nothing. Perhaps there is some "perceived loss of possible revenue" due to the "perceived possible reduction in game interest" due to starting out at a higher level. The problem people seem to miss is the relatively high amount of overhead required to start up. The frustration of playing through the first few levels could turn some people off to the point where they cancel at the end of the free period, where they may have continued if allowed to take over from someone else.

As for the all-you-can-eat issue, what if you are a paid servant to the person at the buffet? Maybe you are his butler or something, and he pays you to prepare his meals. He sits there waiting for you to gather the stuff, then he benefits from your work. This sounds pretty much like paid account transfer to me

About the EULA comment I made, you missed the point. Verant permits the transfer of items in-game. They do not tell me who I must give my items to. If I like a person in the game, I am more likely to give my items to him or her. If one of those people is my real-life friend who may have given me a present or done a favor for me, then I may want to help him or her out in the game. This is where Verant's logic seems to fail (IMHO). When you put an item up for auction on eBay, you are not playing the game. Verant has no right to tell you what you can and cannot do OUTSIDE their game. When you go back INTO the game, then Verant can tell you what you can and cannot do. Do they allow the planning of raids outside the game? Yes. Do they allow the allocation of items (e.g. deciding who class-quest item will go to if it drops) outside the game? Yes. How is the transfer of money REALLY that much different? If the guild leader doesn't like you all that much IRL, then he's probably less likely to designate items for you. If you bribe him, then he probably will. That's just life.

All of this is a moot point though, because only this question matters:

Does Verant have the right to arbitrarily cancel your account for any reason? Yes.

The ONLY thing I think has a problem is when you prepay months in advance. When you subscribe for 6 months, you are paying for a service that Verant has agreed to provide. If they cancel your account, then they are not providing the service you have pre-paid for, and should refund the remainder of your money based on the monthly rate for the time you have used. Of course, I'm not a lawyer, but that's just my take on it. Again, I do believe Verant has the right to cancel your account, but I believe they should be REQUIRED to refund the money for which they provided no service.
