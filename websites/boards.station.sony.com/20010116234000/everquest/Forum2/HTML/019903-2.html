I have no problem with Verant deciding that GMs will no longer help people retrieve things dropped now that there's a drop confirmation box.

HOWEVER, I have run into two situations recently where the drop confirmation box is ineffective. In one sitatuation, I had 5k of platinum on my mouse cursor and tried to give it to someone in order to buy an item from them. They moved as I tried to click them and the platinum dropped on the ground, WITH NO DROP CONFIRMATION BOX (I do have it turned on). Thak god I was able to pick it up before anyone else in the crowded bank got it. Obviously in the future I will wait until someone puts the item in my window before I put up the platinum, but it's far too easy to make an innocent mistake with the current setup and drop money accidentally. And the policy is that the GMs won't help in this situation? This seems highly unfair.

In another situation, I had quested for a paw of opalla with a friend. For some reason he was carrying the paw on the mouse cursor with a full inventory. He put the paw up in the trade window and the recipient cancelled and said he needed to get money. The paw dropped on the ground _with no drop confirmation box_ and was picked up by someone else. After petitioning, my friend was informed that the GMs would no longer help with such a situation (because of the drop confirmation box, which failed to display). Admittedly carrying the paw with a full inventory was not a wise thing to do, but it still seems to me that the drop confirmation box should have either appeared when the trade was cancelled, or the paw should have returned to the mouse cursor, or it shouldn't allow a trade to be initiated with a full inventory, or _something_. If this is somehow programatically infeasible, I think the responsibility should fall back to the GMs to deal with these "software limitation" issues when they arise. As it is, I and my friend are out 25 or so hours of work getting the paw and the GMs say they can't do anything to help.

I saw a similar situation in nfp when someone purchased a wurmslayer and didn't have any inventory space (which is easy to not notice). The wurmslayer dropped on the ground and was picked up by someone else. An ooc argument went on for the next hour at least as some claimed the item was on the ground and thus ffa, others said a GM would help, others offered rewards for the item's return, etc. I don't know how the situation was resolved, but presumably the gms went with the official policy of not being able to do anything. I can see a similar problem occuring when you buy something from a merchant or turn in quest items for a container type reward. If the item is a container and you have no more slots your purchased or reward item drops to the ground and anyone can pick it up before you do. I had this happen buying a backpack a few times, though picked it up before anyone got it. Again, I think the npc should not allow you to complete the transaction if you don't have room. Merchants won't sell you non-container items when you don't have room, so why do they sell containers? All these sitatuations seem to be loopholes in an otherwise sensible system for keeping items from accidentally dropping to the ground. If they can't or won't be fixed, I say again that the GMs must pick up the slack and set things right when a loophole adversely affects a player.
