Well, I feel more than obligated to post on Badina's thread. I have held off because there really wasn't much I could offer.

Absor started a Ragefire thread.

But, I returned to this thread and I am seeing a negative turn and an uninformed turn seep into this thread. And Ragefire thread is lacking because it does not address the issue Badina has brought up in this thread.

There is a strong focus on the term "highly unlikely" and that is the basis of judgement by the GM in this case.

Allow me to illustrate why "highly unlikely" is an absurd connotation.

"Highly Unlikely" in terms of the overall population of EQ would be the probability of Ragefire poofing for the exact same person twice in succession given all of the players online and all of the encounters and all of the NPC's. It is an abstract number and thought and has no relevance.

The likelyhood of Regfire's corpse poofing is based solely on the number of participants attacking Ragefire that are linkdead against the number of players attacking Ragefire that are not linkdead. This does not count people that are sitting down and not attacking. If they went linkdead they would continue to sit down until they disappeared. If Ragefire attacked they would not defend themselves. They would just stand there and get hit.

It isn't possible to determine the probability of someone going linkdead. It happens. We all know it happens and we can all accept the fact that it does happen.

So we are now only concerned about the individuals that are engaging Ragefire. These are the only individuals that are going to contribute to Ragefire poofing.

Ragefire will poof only if the tiny miniscule of damage delivered at the end is the one that puts the damage hits over the top to declare Ragefire dead. Only that one tiny little hit at that one instance.

We are dealing with strictly one instance in time. In this case it is with at most 30 people attacking (assuming every single person is attacking and some aren't bothering to keep their party members alive). That is also absurd.

If just one person in this crowd goes linkdead, then the probability of Ragefire poofing is 1 person against 29 people. In attacks it is 1 attack out of 30.

2 people and it is 2 chances out of 30.

No way can the occurence of Ragefire poofing be considered highly unlikely. No way can any corpse poofing be considered highly unlikely if there is a linkdead member of the party attacking.

The problems of poofing are traced only to that one tiny last killing blow.

Let's stop the absurd declarations of "highly unlikely."

Customer Service will fall somewhere between "acceptable" and "assinine". I know which end of the spectrum it should be leaning towards. Justifications of "highly unlikely" definitely move it towards the opposite end.
