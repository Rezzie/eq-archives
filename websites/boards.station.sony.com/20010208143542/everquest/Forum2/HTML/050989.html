All right, due to the impression that you all have given me, it seems that I need to cover wizards next…
I’ve already made several notes to myself about wizards, and I may as well start out this discussion with those notes. Keep in mind that these ‘talks’ are geared toward making sure that the classes are ‘balanced’. Please work toward that goal when posting (yes, even if that means you suggest a ‘nerf’). But I’m also interested in hearing about bugs and such too, I’m just not going to focus on them.

Here are the notes that I already have. Keep in mind that these are only my notes, and just because I’ve listed something does NOT mean that I like the idea or that the designers are working on making changes based on these notes. This is just what it is, a list of ideas. I’ve also added my notes to you all under the header of Note:

AoE spells: Mentioned elsewhere… (earlier on my list, probably under mages: basically the idea here is that maybe pets should be removed from the equation).

Gaze/Glimpse: Due to the clipping plane, these spells don’t really allow further seeing, just zooming in (which makes your resolution worse anyhow).

Note: this may not be ‘fixable’ at all due to the way the game works. It’s not like we can force your PC into a higher resolution when you use this spell…

Fade/Shadowstep/Yonder: There seems to be a problem with these spells. Since they will not teleport you up, they don’t work too well in hilly terrain; because the spell only sends you a few feet (stopping when it reaches a hill or building).

Note: Again, this may also not be something we can change, but it’s on the list.

Bind Sight: Seems to cause viewer to see the target as running into walls or spinning in circles if target goes up an incline.

Enstill: It seems that this spell is not a more useful or more powerful root, it’s just more expensive.

Cazic Gate: At level spell is attained, using it will most often cause death because the caster appears amidst many aggressive monsters that /con white to them.

Note: We’ve actually discussed this before. If I remember correctly, the general feeling was that having a dangerous gate like this wasn’t necessarily a bad thing. But it is also still on my list.

Tishan’s Relocation: This spell evacuates you to a very dangerous location, a monster crossroads in the Skyfire mountains. Could the location be moved, or was it intended that this be a very dangerous evacuation spell?

Note: See note about Cazic Gate.

Concussion: A level 39 spell dropped by a creature in a location that requires a party of level 50s to handle. Should it be in such a location?

Icestrike effects: Graphical effect looks like fire.

Lightning Bolt effects: looks exactly like Fire Bolt.

Other lightning and cold effects: It seems that there might be other spells that have the look of fire when they should not.

Having just re-read this list, I see nothing at all related to actual balancing stuff. This is all nit-picky stuff about minor effects and bugs and such (with the possible exception of Area of Effect spells, changing those would certainly have an effect on class balances). So, please, try to focus on balance issues…

Alan

By the way, the AE RAIN SPELL problem again, in cause you all missed it, especially you Absor:

This spell line, like all AE spells, had a larger area, and had no maximum number of targets. All AE spells had their mana cost raised, damage lowered, and the areas and maximum targets reduced. AE Rain spells were FURTHER weakened by making them not hit with the correct number of waves.

Instead of hitting up to the maximum number of targets (4 now) with up to 3 waves each, it works like this, optimally:

1 target = 3 waves hit
2 targets = 2 waves hit each
3 targets = 1 wave hits each, with 1 extra wave on a single target
4 targets = 1 wave hits each target

Now, as you can see, unlike ALL other AE spells, the more targets you get, the more inefficient and more poor the spell becomes. As a result, the AE Rain spells are junk. In addition, the AE Rain spells actually hit with EVEN FEWER waves indoors than they did out doors.

In the Fall of 1999, Verant made an adjustment to the rain spells that made them hit more indoors, so they were the same as outdoors, but were still limited to 4 maximum waves, no matter how many targets.

In January, after repeated pleas, comments, and complaints, GZ Finally responded to Graffe, Baelish and I with an email that said, in effect, "wow, it does that? It's not supposed to, we'd like to fix that before Kunark comes out, AE Rain spells will feature heavily in that!"

In other words... they are broken. GZ was the guy in charge of spells then, the Archmage. Geoffrey Zatkins, works for Verant. Then after months of waiting we got no word, nothing was done, they STILL didn't work properly. So we brought that up AGAIN to GZ and he responded to Graffe with this (approximately) "We are going to get to those, but we can't right now, can't give you an ETA."

So we stand right now at that point. AE Rain spells have these three problems:
-Hit with too few waves
-Hit pets
-Resisted more than individual blasts
The last two are shared with all AE spells, but they are AE Rain spell problems

There is some confusion about AE Rain spells, how they would be 'overpowered' if they were working properly etc.

First off, There are two classes of AE spells for Wizards, who get the best ones in the game (damage and mana-wise). At level 4 and level 50+, the AE Rain spells, if all 3 waves hit, do about 150-180% of the damage a given blast that level does. The rest, between level 5 and 49, do about 110-120% of the damage a given blast that level does.

In other words, at level 4 and 50+, an AE Rain spell would do 15-18 damage when a blast that level would do 10.

At level 5-49, an AE rain spell would do 11-12 damage.

This assumes all three waves hit the target and are not resisted. Now, this seems exceptionally powerful IF you don't know how AE rain spells work. There are several balancing factors here that make them controlled with the power given:

-Three waves, each one is resisted separately, so that instead of the damage being added up, it has to be considered in three different packets.
-The damage is over 12 seconds (3 waves 6 seconds apart) so it has a similar effect as a DOT, it might not even take effect due to the creature dying in that time.
-The area is fixed at a given location, and the monster can physically LEAVE the area of effect, thus negating 1 or more waves entirely.
-Like all AE spells, they will affect the caster, and damage him.
In other words, looking at the damage as a single, total package is misleading.

ITS NOT JUST ABOUT PETS BEING HIT

Repost:
Well, it's been close to a year since I quit EQ to find a game where a wizard wasn't
gimpy.

There is one basic problem with the EQ wizard, which has been mentioned here over and
over again - a wizard runs out of mana very quickly, and once he is out of mana, he is
worthless. A wizard has no contribution besides his ability to turn mana into damage,
except for evacuations, which druids typically do a better job of anyway. For this, they
are the weakest of all classes defensively.

Yeah, there's other minor issues, concerning how pointless most of the wizard spells are,
or where their TP points are, or how they can't affect a red creature through most of
their lives. But when you get down to why wizards aren't desired much, its simply they
don't have enough damage output over time - even shaman and enchanters can do more
damage over an hour of playing than wizards.

So, if you can accept that as the main reason wizards are unpowered from other classes,
there are several ways to remedy that problem. The remedy should not step on other
classes specialties, such as buffs or damageshields, or mana regen (with 1 possible
exception). They are:

1) Create a skill, similar to that of backstabs, kicks and bashes, that allows a wizard to
do a DD or bolt, free of mana, at the touch of a button. This would obviously need to be
weaker than the wizards most powerful spell, perhaps as much as 1/10 weaker. Actually,
the best ratio would be to let the wizard have an attack between 1/4 and 1/2 of a
typical melee character, as the wizard won't be in combat, and shouldn't come close to
damage with the manaless attack as a warrior of that level.

So, a level 12 wizard could maybe do 5 to 8 points of a magical attack, say every 6 to
10 seconds or so, and a level 60 wizard 80 to 120 damage in that time (perhaps more or
less - its been a while). This lets the wizard do much less damage than a typical melee
type over that time. He can do this when out of mana, or can pace himself to use the
big, mana eating spells when needed.

This fits in perfectly with the wizard description, and again, is similar to other classes
who get rapid special attacks.

2) If this is too extreme, a very easy solution would be to dramatically increase the
damage cap levels for wizard spells. This means that instead of maxing out in spell
damage 4 levels after geting the spells, you can keep increasing the spell power for 12 to
20 levels. This lets a wizard chain cast a bunch of highly mana efficient weak spells (so a
level 40 wizard casting a level 20 spell would be doing 40 or 60 more points per cast than
when he first got it, instead of the 8 to 12 he currently gets), or bring out the big mana
hogs for power attacks.

This also fits in perfectly with the extreme focus of the wizard spell line on damage.

3) Give wizards a much higher cap per level on evocation skill, much like the monk gets
for dual weilding or hand to hand attacks (again, its been a while). Since the EQ system
for resolution is so opaque to the players, I cannot suggest what this would affect, but
hopefully it would dramatically lower resist rates or increase damage potential, or both.
I'm not positive that this would solve the problem as completely as the first two
suggestions.

4) Give the wizards a higher meditation rate. Now, this does not mean a clarity effect - it
would only be effective when the wizard was meditating, not while running around. This
would fit in well with the decription of the highly disciplined students of magic that
wizards are described by. I don't know that it would really fix the problem - the wizard
would still be sitting on his butt most of the fight, just less time than normal.

I think the order of effectiveness is the order given - the first is the most ideal because
it does not help the wizard in solo play at all - his damage output is actually less over
short periods, but is greatly increased over long periods. #1 also enables the wizard to
be a factor in combat without mana. The second would be easier to implement, since it
should just be a simple data change, and would acheive much of what #1 does - the
wizard could still run out of mana, but would be able to do more damage over time than
he currently does, since low level spells that now get moved to the back of the spell
book become much more mana efficient.

Good luck wizards!
