>>>>If SPEED WAS everything, and the PRIMARY taunting factor, then why don't all warriors DW FS Daggers at 3/19 until they can afford a jade mace?


Simply because the damage output would be sub-par.
A combination of taunt and damage are the factors that come into play when a warrior is choosing weapons.

For example:
If you are the lowest level in a melee heavy group, a Wurmslayer is an adequate choice for a mainhand weapon. It does decent damage and the low taunt factor leaves the mob to keep its aggro on the best taunter, thus allowing the cleric to concentrate on healing only one tank. Nothing makes a cleric OOM faster than having to heal 2-3 tanks.
However, if you are the only tank and/or you are grouped with a rogue, you would be better served wielding a low delay weapon like a jade mace, lamentation or any sub-20 delay weapon in your main hand.

A 'smart' warrior should know what weapons are appropriate for each situation.

Thogg (or is it Thog?), nobody will argue that the Wurmslayer has pretty decent damage output. However, it is NOT a 'good' weapon for a warrior in most situations, and thats what people are trying to tell you.
A well played, high level warrior will be able to control mob aggro like a puppet on a string. This requires the use of a high damage, low delay, high taunt weapon. The Wurmslayer does not fit the critera.
A level 60 warrior with a WS will always be a 'gimp' warrior.

I use a Wurmslayer myself (L49 Ranger), with a Dragon Horn Ykesha as my off-hand weapon.

WS has NO Taunt value whatsoever. It deals a metric buttload of damage, but I can't Taunt a mob off anyone to save their life. (Literally. Had a guild Shaman die in Fear last week because I couldn't pull an Amy off him.)

When I am in a situation where I will need to be able to Taunt (usually means the group needs me to tank, which is a bad sign in the first place), I move the DHY to the primary hand and off-hand a Short Sword of Ykesha. Anyone with Copeland's weapon analyzer can see that a L49 Ranger with DHY/SSoY (and 133 STR) will not outdamage a L49 Ranger with WS/DHY (and 138 STR), but I sure as hell will out-Taunt one. At this point, it usually means that no one can Taunt off of ME to save MY life. I see some skeptics here, but any Ranger can tell you that dual-wielding fast weapons will increase your Taunt value WELL over that of someone wielding a slow 2H weapon, even if the 2H user is outdamaging the Ranger by a considerable margin.

Recently it was asked on the Ranger's Glade if Wurmslayer was better than either Lamentation or a Revultant Whip. Both Copeland's and an analysis done by hand showed that WS is better than either until level FIFTY-EIGHT, at which point the Lammy and Rat Whip are tied.

As for damage bonuses, remember that slow 1H weapons recently had theirs increased, and Wurmslayer is slow enough to have qualified for the increase.

Wurmslayer does cause heavy fatigue, but if you have 101 STA that's not an issue. I don't, but thanks to the new hybrid casting rules I can throw an occasional Invigor on myself between swings with only minimal loss of damage output (basically I'm sacrificing a possible off-hand strike).

The worst thing I can say about it is that it looks stupid. That blade has no striking edge, and it's a two-handed model being used for a one-handed weapon.

It's not in my best interests to say this, but Wurmslayer is a broken weapon. It is far TOO good for its acquisition cost. If all you care about is damage output, there is no substitute for a Wurmslayer until VERY high level, far higher than the level one must be to acquire the weapon.
