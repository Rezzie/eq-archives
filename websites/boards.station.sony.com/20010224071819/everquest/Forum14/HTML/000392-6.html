Absor,

You've asked that we address balance issues, and not enhancements or 'toys' that might skew the existing balance. With that in mind, I'll try to relate my own thoughts.

The post-50 game is hosed right now. I think that the balance issues post-50 are the direct result of Verant breaking the scaling factor of the game in those levels. Balance in that game will have to wait until this latest patch issue is resolved in some way.

I think you'd hear much less *****ing from the clerics, however, if we didn't feel that we had to suffer so much in the pre-fifty game just to get to the point of usefulness.

Clerics have very little utility. While we do have a variety of spells to choose from, we don't have any strategic combinations that allow us to break from our 'heal and med' role. The shamans and druids have certain options that clerics don't. When a shaman/druid gets tired of grouping, he can effectively solo in the pre-fifty game, just for a change of pace.

Clerics, arguably, don't have the option of breaking from this role. We stare at that spellbook, and play out our single schtick. Until level 34 or so, many of us are passed up for groups because Shamans and Druids are effective enough healers as to make no difference in comparison, and the utility they add more than compensates for whatever healing bonus the cleric might have.

You can't balance this issue by removing some of the healing duty from us, because healing is the only thing we're efficient at. In order to balance the enjoyability of the classes, clerics need to be adjusted in such a way that they can solo effectively if they can't find a group, or if they just need a break from the routine.

Our undead line is probably the best way to balance the 'fun' factor and give the clerics a break from the traditional mold. Rangers have the ability to fear and snare animals, so why not add an undead snare to clerics?

Fix our broken spells (Summoned hammers, MoK, Hammer of Faith). Fix the stacking issues. Cleric AC/HP buffs don't stack with Druid group-resist buffs, and this really edges the cleric out somewhat, since the druid can stack all of his buffs together, and the combined effect is better than our few single buffs.

I apologize for the length. Thanks for your consistently professional demeanor, Absor, and I hope we can resolve the Hatelist issue as soon as possible.
