I first played EQ when Phase 4 of beta began. I played a Troll Warrior on Fennin Ro (still do matter of fact). Us trolls and ogres had an odd relationship back then as we would run around are little dark valley. Every so often a group of us would band together and try to break into Upper Guk or Cazic and be beaten back silly. One day as I was strolling out of Grobb I saw the most pecurliar sight.. a little darkskinned man fishing in the swamp! Whoa a Dark Elf! Around level level 8 or 9 a group of us travelled to South Ro and were amazed to be out of the gloomy forest.

"Look at the sky!"
"I see a desert in the distance past this orc army!"

After awhile we got curious and since I was the highest level in our group I volunteered to peak over the hill top into the desert and saw... A SANDGIANT!

"RUN!"

A few days after that we got up the courage to run across the desert and made it to Oasis of Marr. We camped the zone line there for awhile and killed a mummy and got a small wooden shield. We were so excited until we realised Trolls can't quite use it. So we travelled back to our swamp.

A ogre shaman friend of mine (Ningauble!) and myself later on decided we would try to run as fast as we could and see what we could fine beyond Oasis of Marr. We managed to avoid all the badguys and made it to North Ro. When we got to the grass area we had our first encounter with other player races, and them with us! It was pretty funny. I remember giving a trinket to some pretty wood elf woman who I still run into from time to time today (hey Anni *wave*)

That's just a little of the things I experienced the first few weeks playing, the biggest accomplishment we managed during beta was to take down Lockjaw. But outside of that those feelings of exploring the 'new' were outstanding and meeting new people it was incredible.

Many of those I met in beta I still adventure with. Some I haven't seen in a long long while but are fondly remembered (Banban, Neras).

Heh, gather 'round the fire boys and let me tell you some stories.

I'm nox. Personal friend to some guys who met the eq designers at E3 back in 1998. They put my name on a list of '10 folks we should add to beta', and that's how I got into early beta.

The game was pretty weird back then, and a lot of extremely odd things happened. Let me tell you about some of em.

Way back when, there were no robes for casters. Casters could use rapiers, and they could use bucklers. They could not use staves. All the casters in the game ran around with little shields and rapiers, it was very lame.

When they first added faction, sometimes strange things would happen. Once, all of the wisps became agro to humans. Qeynos hills was a nightmare of unkillable killer wisps chasing newbies around.

Once when they were adjusting factions, they adjusted some too far in qeynos...
They made the evil qeynos guards KOS to every good class/race including the good qeynos guards and the npcs.
When we logged in, there was a crowd of about 30 people watching the evil guards fighting the good guards. It was a huge riot, the guards running all over shouting, the evil guards casting spells, the other npc's joining in.
The mood was festive, people were drinking ale and laughing at the antics.. and then armageddon ensued. The evil npc's killed all the good npcs, and turned on the crowd in a vicious killing spree.
People were scrambling over one another to escape qeynos, there were bodies everywhere. ANyone bound inside qeynos died instantly as they logged in and the gates were a slaughtering house of people trying to run for the hills with a host of shadowknights slaughtering them in one hit.
As a necro, I was spared but the scene was one of mass carnage and insanity. There were at one point probably 200 player bodies near the pool and shops in north qeynos.

I started on release day. i remember quite a few things from those days...

--there was no music at first, and when they patched it, I heard beautiful music and scary sounds...it made the world truly come alive.

--there was no sun or moon in the sky. Then i saw the first sunset on the dock in ButcherBlock and was awed.

--i would boast about my Sense Heading skill and get laughed at because the third coordinate of /loc told you everything (i didn't figure that one out).

--i saw a gnome caster in BB as a young Paladin and he buffed me...i thought he was a god or something.

--i saw a GM standing in the zone near Kaladim and KNEW he was a god, completely decked out in white or silver armor, and I was awestruck.

--i saw the wizard spires in GFay and wondered about the mysteries they might hold...so did everyone else.

--i received a quest to acquire bone chips and made that my sworn duty as a Paladin to maintain peace in the lands...and i believed it.

--i joined in a battle against a giant skeleton on the Chessboard, and won. My party cheered for what seemed like hours, then we sat around and discussed the "meaning" of this chessboard and how it had come to be, and why...and we really wanted to know.

--I journeyed to the Ocean of Tears to deliver a card to some blacksmith to complete his deck...and was proud of my accomplishment.

--I never dreamed of Uber equipment or the "end-game", never sought to raid Planes or dragons...though taking down a dragon would have been a tale never forgotten. Instead i dreamed of wandering the lands as a master of blacksmithing, a story-teller in taverns, and a fisherman most of the time. I dreamed of walking everywhere I went, only so that it would take longer and i could enjoy things. I dreamed that one day i would only draw steel when someone was in danger and needed my assistance. I dreamed of a game where i could roleplay...where i could become a strong, powerful knight and paladin who defended the weak and protected the innocent. I was the innocent one at that time, but alas, there was no one to protect me from what this has become.
