I only have one account, myself. But on that account I have four 50+ chars (ranging to 57), a 39, a 30, and a 32. I leave the eighth slot free to help friends or to do temporary muling or whatever, a utility slot.

One of the chars is a cleric. I have seen the healing agro, from the perspective of the cleric and from the perspective of the one being healed and protecting the cleric. They obviously did overdo the healing agro, but they seem to have recognized this and are correcting it. I don't expect (or even want) the cleric class to be as risk-free as it once was; some challenge is fun.

But whenever there's such a bad implementation of a change, I have no problems going off and playing another char for a while. Heck, that's why I have so many that are as high in level as they are. And no powerleveling here; I don't want to be powerleveled. Learning classes properly and initimately requires that you play them the hard way, by fighting and grouping, not by having a druid hang around wasting his time healing you and casting damage shields.

And ya know, the game is STILL fun to me! I guess it's still fun because, for me, the fun is all in the journey, not the destination. Being 60th won't really be fun; I see that pretty clearly already. The journey TO 60 will be fun, with each char I choose to do that with. Ultimately, it'd be neat to have all seven of my chars 50+; that's one of my goals, actually, and I'm not really that far from it now. But it's the people I'm with, and the experiences we share, that's the fun for me, not the ubermobs or hitting level X or getting uberitem Y or acquiring zillions of plat to buy uberitem Z, or anything like that.

If you forget everything else except that the real pleasure is in the journey and not in the destination, then EQ has an immensely long 'fun span'. For those who are all tied up on one thing (killing ubermobs, achieving items, amassing wealth), well, yeah, your time of enjoyment will probably be far less in duration than my own. And that's okay. If it's ever not fun for me, I'm outta here too.

But I haven't run out of cool people to meet and hang with, and it doesn't really look like I will, so, for now at least, I'm still having a good time.
