There were some games around the late 1890s that had role playing elements. It is pretty certain that Gygax got the idea for making his medieval miniatures game Chainmail into a role playing game from a British game in the 1960s. But as for full fledge dedicated role playing games, D&D is as good a place to start as any (1974).

Bards Tale was probably the first 'role playing' computer game. Like all the ones to follow its RP elements were tenuous at best, you have to use a different definition for RPG in computers than you do in regular face to face games.

Probably the first real Computer Role Playing Game will be the new Neverwinter Nights, although Fallout does a really good job.

fridayjones
Station Member 		posted 02-02-2001 12:57 AM           
Definitely Wizardry on the Apple][!

That was a cool game. It had a couple of continents and some islands, and there were towns and dungeons scattered around the world. You could recruit men to follow you, and the merchants actually d|ckered over prices!

Once you had built up a party of men to adventure with, you could also recruit from the countryside. When you encountered monsters or bandits or something, the fight would cause casualties on both sides, so the superior force always won.

Dungeons were cool, it was like a different game than the countryside in many ways. Instead of looking at a map of the world with your party's position on it, the view would switch to a maze map view that built as you explored. The monsters in dungeons were pretty nasty!

If you wanted to travel by sea, you could get a ship and take your party on the high seas. Sea travel was, again, like another different game. Sea Monsters, Pirates, weather, and so forth. You could easily get blown far off course!

All in all, this was as good as you could get until that Oddessey Adventure came out. That one was mainly a single dungeon crawl, with 3/4 of the screen text and a 1/4 size box in the upper-left corner of the screen that had an actual vector graphic of the 3D hallways you were in. Monsters would just sort of appear in front of you. A good friend back in 1982 lost a job at Hewlett-Packard because his boss kept catching him playing it at work! :boggle: Now THAT'S the first case of RPG computer game addiction I've ever seen.
