Kiting is fine, and in a wide open zone it certainly has it's benefits. However, in a crowded area like the Hill Giants' Hill in Rathe Mountains kiting is what gives druids a bad name. I can only recall seeing one druid kiting in my 3 levels hunting there, the rest just pull and root.

There are lots of areas/zones where kiting simply isn't a safe option either for the druid because of adds or for other players who get continually trained by a kiting druid. I agree, tho', that kiting has its place in wide open and scarcely-populated zones.

Moreover, the real disadvantage of kiting versus rooting is that while you're running around your mana is going nowhere, whereas if you root the mob and sit down while the DoT works your mana is regenerating faster and so your downtime is less.

At the end of the day we all use different tactics according to our playing styles, our choice of equipment, and the way we have evolved through the levels, I wouldn't claim rooting to be better than kiting in all circumstances, most players will operate a combination of the two tactics depending on the circumstances. With hill giants I certainly find rooting and snaring more mana efficient and less troublesome to others than kiting with no danger of running into another hill giant, giant skeleton or cyclops, but to each his own.
