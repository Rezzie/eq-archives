I know the issue has been raised before... but I have never seen the issue addressed by Verant. My hope, foolish though it may be, is that the Live team will FINALLY address this issue that remains one of the more odd and silly bugs in the game.

Coin loot is one of three methods of rewarding players for successfully battling a mob. The other two are, of course, experience/training and item loot. Item loot is relatively balanced (yes, we all know that there are plenty of useless items dropped from high level mobs) as the powerful gear is found from the powerful mobs. Experience and training are balanced as both are better from more powerful mobs. However, coin is just plain whacked.

Case in point... as it stands now, Hill Giants and Sand Giants remain the best (BY FAR) cash loot foes in the game. Not even the powerful dragons Vox and Nagafen (or the Wurms in Skyfire) can rival the loot from the giants of the Rathe Mountains or Ro. While I have seen 30-40 pp and TONS of smaller coin dropped by giants, your average 45+ mob even in Kunark won't drop more than a few pp, if any. How ridiculous is it to loot Magi Rokyl in Nagafen's lair, and get 7sp, 9cp as your cash loot.

The fix for this is incredibly simple... and merely requires that they do it. Higher level mobs should drop more cash... simple as that. If you feel a mob shouldn't drop cash, that's fine... then make it drop none... not 7sp, 9cp. However, if a mob is "coin appropriate", it should be dropping cash at an appropriate level.

I will comment that the method that is in place currently to "balance" this is the drop of valuable words, gems, and the like from higher level mobs. When you fight Seafury Cyclopses, you occasionally get nice gems (sapphires, emeralds, fire opals, etc) as well as jewelry worth as much as 90pp each item. Though this does make Seafury Cyclopses equivalent for cash to giants, it still looks very odd. Though I suppose I can ALMOST get my brain around these cyclopses "spending their cash on gems", that concept falls apart entirely when you look at Nagafen, Vox, and the like.

Please fix this, guys... it isn't Rocket Science.
