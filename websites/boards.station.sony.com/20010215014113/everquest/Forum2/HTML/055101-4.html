This is not even considered in this game.

Body - the monk was able to take damage while in combat that would drop another man. the monk was able to avoid all manner of attack while in combat with little or no effort. the monk was able to take combat injury and store it for succumbing to it after combat, not so unlike berzerkers (mind over matter realy). Our multitude of special attacks are currently all linked together. When one is used, the others cannot be used until the timer expires. I felt a little cheated when levelling because each skill I'd worked so hard to master was once again useless and I had to start over with the new skill from scratch.

These attacks don't do that much damage. I don't have any hard numbers for you but the average in this list is what I remember. I haven't used any but Flying Kick since 30th, the majority of my career.

Kick, Level 1, Cap 250, Avg Damage 10
Round Kick, Level 5, Cap 225 Avg Damage 15
Tiger Claw, Level 10, Cap 225 Avg Damage 25
Eagle Strike, Level 20, Cap 225, Avg Damage 35
Dragon Punch, Level 25, Cap 225, Avg Damage 45
Flying Kick, Level 30, Cap 225, Avg Damage 50

All of these have a delay that is 30-50. Tiger Claw is a tad faster, I think Flying Kick is the slowest. Iksar get Tail Rake in place of Tiger Claw but I think it is the same ability just with a different name.

My suggestion is that you unlink the attacks and let use them individually. This means that I have 6 buttons to click for each fighting round, or one to script them with, but even so just having the use of those skills that nobody uses anymore would be a lot of fun and I wouldn't feel like 5 of my class skills were wasted and useless.

Someone suggested linking up combo hits but that sounds like a coding nightmare. I suspect it would be far easier to just unlink them. Instill Doubt is somehow tangled up in them as well in a way that I think nobody really understands.

It will give us a little more damage output, probably about the same increase we would get if you put a minimum cap on flying kick, but now we would attain it gradually and it would be tied into skils that we'd have to work to master.

Every stance would have a different quest, and they would have different effects on your fighting style. I agree with the notion that Monks need to see an increase in skill/damage to maintain their role in the world. I also like the idea that a Monk's fists should be his/her ultimate weapon. The question is, how do we boost fists without going overboard on monks with weapons?

Triple attack would be nice, however weapons would still out damage fists, so we would still use them.

Unlinking the "strikes" from the "kicks" in our special attacks would be nice (being able to Flying Kick follwed by a Dragon Punch). Again, this doesn't change the weapon to fist debate.

A raw increase in the dam/delay of fists may be required. Non-monks would complain that we wouldn't need to worry about acquiring weapons like warrior, but isn't that part of the reason we give up on AC/HP?

One idea that I like would be fist enhancing weapons, for example Brass Knuckles. These "weapons" would use the h2h skil for dam/delay but would add the ability to proc an effect. For example, the "Knuckles of Frost" would proc a cold based DD spell.

There could be a few lines of these weapons that are questable, much like the sashes/headbands. Place a Master of Frost up in Perma and have him give out quests of varying skill. The first quest yields knuckles that have a 20 point cold based DD that starts at level 25 (or whatever). The second quest results in knuckles that do a 40 point DD, etc... By creating a fire, cold, and stun based line of knuckles, Monks would gain in strength based on the H2H table, but could choose a proc based on situation. Against a caster? take out the "Knuckles of Dazing" (stun proc). Going against fire-weak creatures? take out the "Ton Po's Flaming Knuckles".

And since the knuckles only add in a proc at a certain level and do nothing for base damage, they are worthless as a twink item (although I would still make them No Drop)

Heya Folks, lots of good ideas floating around here for sling some of our issues. But I'd like to toss out a sugestion...

As many have pointed out (and I will not reiterate the reasons why) our damage in the post 50 world is no longer of the same comparative rank as it was previously.

To deal with that we have seen a lot of suggestions for increasing our damage output. But here's a different though on the "how".

Instead of changing our hand to hand damage delays (and again, the monks realy want hand to hand, ot staves, as was also pointed out too many times to mention) use gloves and boots.

The epic wepons shows that coding does exist to tie a change in hand to hand stats to a pair of worn gloves. This is our godsend.

Take a look at the various monk gloves that exist in game from wu's gauntlets to crescent, to shiverback to the many flavors of Velious armor. Each of these could have a similar effect on our stats.

Picture a world where monks work to increase their powers in hand to hand combat by attaining bigger better gloves. It would keep monks with a focus on going to dungeons and doing things and yet not bind us to StaffQuest.

A similar effect could also be reached for boots. The boots could enable our kicks to increae in damage and abilities so that the top notch ones yielded faster kicks, stronger kicks and with stun.

ALong with these quipment changes, please also look at the issue of our armor not having effects. It's a slight to us that of teh melee classes, we alone have entire sets of armor that simply have no effects (even small ones) where othesr get robust, entertaining and helpful effects. Would giving monks summon water, food, shirukens and bandages on our high level armor actually unbalance anything? Think about it. Is our going to town half as often a detriment?

Thanks for listening. Good luck everyone.
