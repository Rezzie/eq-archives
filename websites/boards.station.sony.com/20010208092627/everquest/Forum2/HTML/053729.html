The argument rages: Make shamans and druids better healers. Shamans and druids demand it. Clerics reject it.

It's been said before, but I'll try again:

Clerics are one-dimensional. They give hp. They do this 3 ways, through healing, through buffing, and through rezzing.

No matter how you look at it, the three main things clerics do is giving hp to people who are hurt, are about to be hurt, or who are dead.

This is very useful, but is fairly one-dimensional.

The two other healing classes can do other things quite well. They also give HP, but they can give speed, cause damage, give invisibility, do damge over time, reduce mobs' speed, reduce mobs' damage, etc.

Clerics do none of those things.

People like druids and shamans. People invite them into groups. Primarily due to their versatility.

People also like clerics. People invite them into groups, too. Primarily because they give HP.

If druids and shamans are significantly enhanced to give HP better, then this will tip some scales.

Shamans and druids will become even more versatile and even more desirable in groups. Great. Good for them. They certainly deserve it.

Clerics won't become less versatile as they are already fairly one-dimensional. However, there will be fewer groups looking for clerics because many of them will be making due with shamans and druids acting as primary healers.

Now, when those versatile shamans and druids have trouble finding groups, their versatility enables them to solo. Thus they can still play the game, still progress by gaining exp and loot by soloing.

When one-dimensional clerics can't find a group, they lack the versatility to solo and thus must sit around, waiting for a group to need them. They cannot still play the game, still progress by gaining exp and loot by soloing.

What happens if druids and shamans are given the ablity to heal as well, or nearly as well, as a cleric?

The number of groups playing won't increase. The number of primary group healer positions won't increase. The number of characters capable of fulfilling that role of primary group healer will triple. This means that there is only 1/3 the number of primary healer positions for clerics to fulfil.

Increase the viability of shamans and druids to join groups, thus taking a spot that a cleric could have filled, and that means that the next cleric in that zone to want a group won't find a spot; won't be able to play. Won't be able to travel as quickly or as safely to another zone to look for a group as a shaman or druid, either.

Do shamans and druids need high-level upgrades to healing to be playable in post-50 groups. Yes.

But never at the risk of overtaking those coveted group healing spots from clerics. Clerics cannot play this game without groups. Cannot even play this game at all without groups.

Do clerics need dots and sow and snare and all that other stuff. No. Shame on those clerics who are asking for it. That is not the answer. That is simply trolling and provoking hostility.

Clerics could stand a slight improvement on some of our weaker healing spells. They should see some improvement in their soloing skills, to make them viable to play without a group.

And shamans and druids should see some improvement to their healing ability, so long as it reamains, at all levels, weaker than the healing ability of a cleric of the same level, or even of a cleric a few (few, not a lot) levels lower than the druid/shaman.

No, most clerics are not paranoid. But that point above about having only 1/3 the healer positions open is a valid one.

Sure, you may say that if there is a cleric and a druid at the zone, and you need a primary healer, you would pick the cleric.

But how often does this happen?

Most often, when a healer leaves a group, that group takes the nearest healer available. Quite often none are available. Sometimes only one is available.

If no cleric is sitting at the zoneline, a druid or shaman will get the job. Once that druid or shaman is in the group, the cleric that zones in or logs in a couple minutes later is out of a job. Unable to play at all. Possibly for a long time before another group has a healer opening.

You say that is happening to shamans and druids now.

Point taken. But only at the upper levels. And I have already conceded that shamans and druids need healing upgrades at the upper levels.

I hope this sheds some light on the issue. I hope this puts those whining clerics in their place when they claim clerics need all that versatility the other two priest classes enjoy. Clerics do not need this. They do need their function, their only function, that of giving HP, protected so that their utility in a group is not overshadowed by instantly tripling the number of characters able to provide this function.

Excellent point. I agree, and admit I hadn't seen it that way.

I hope I had not implied that soloing ability should be balanced against grouping ability; that was not my intent.

My intent is to suggest, and support, that shamans and druids need better healing ability at higher levels. My intent is to demonstrate that this might and probably will meet greater competition for clerics to find primary healer spots in groups. It is expected that a cleric will likely win the competition against a druid or shaman when all are asking to join a group at the same time. However, many groups will form with a druid or shaman primary healer because no clerics are available at that time, only to have a cleric enter zone and now be unable to join those fully formed groups.

And when this happens to shamans and druids, they have the alternate play mode available. They can go solo. Not just the occasional, rare undead camp, but anything in their level range. 4 at a time, sometimes. When this happens to clerics, they have no alternative. They just don't play Everquest until they can find a group.

Soloability is not related to groupability, and balancing issues should not be made, or at least should give only light consideration, on the basis of this. However, talks about balancing any class relative to any other class must always consider the consequences of the balance.

Balancing the healing of shamans and druids will result in those classes getting invited to more groups. That is the whole reason, after all, for these classes requesting the upgrade. At least, I haven't yet heard a druid saying he wants better healing so after he's done quad-kiting, he can more efficiently heal himself and conserve mana for the next quad-kiting assault.

Naturally, since the number of groups won't increase, and therefore the number of primary group healer positions won't increase, but healing-enhanced druids and shamans are getting invited into these groups, some clerics are going to be excluded from them. And the natural question at this point is, "What can they do if they can't find a group?"

We already ask that about druids and shamans not finding a group. The answer is, "They can solo. They may not like it. It may be boring. But they can do it, and can get exp/plat/loot while continuing to seek a group."

Enhance the healing of druids and shamans too much and when we ask that question of clerics, the answer will be, "Not much. They will have to sit around, looking for groups, doing nothing. They won't get exp/plat/loot while continuing to seek a group. They can't play the game while they wait."

So while soloing and grouping are apples and oranges, they are still both in the same fruit basket, and one cannot be considered without weighing its impact on the other.

"Soloability is not related to groupability, and balancing issues should not be made, or at least should give only light consideration, on the basis of this. However, talks about balancing any class relative to any other class must always consider the consequences of the balance."

Point taken. The consequences must be looked at a larger scale too, in part, how it affects the desirability of each class, and also how it affects the gameplay experience for everyone else - which certainly cannot be seperated with the great deal of interdependence built into the game.

"Naturally, since the number of groups won't increase, and therefore the number of primary group healer positions won't increase, but healing-enhanced druids and shamans are getting invited into these groups, some clerics are going to be excluded from them. And the natural question at this point is, "What can they do if they can't find a group?""

I must disagree with the premise of your point. Before 50th level, a druid or shaman could simply grab a few people so long as there was some semblance of balance and start a group. I myself used to start groups all the time in the old days as a druid, just grabbing whoever was available and we'd make things work. That was lots of fun. We didn't disallow clerics to join us. In fact they almost always had their own groups. Thus the fact that I could act as the only group healer did indeed increase the number of active groups and really had very little impact on clerics at all.

Now granted, these days with experience loss being so bad I wouldn't do something like that again lightly. A druid acting as group healer takes terrible risks with the lack of clerical buffs, the divine series, and paper-class armor. Personally I wouldn't want to go into any of the higher level dungeons without a cleric to ressurect me even if they had no healing power whatsoever (not that I'm advocating that!)

However, on the other hand, one thing that happens very often is my guild will field a typical group in a dungeon complete with cleric. Given enough healing power to keep a group alive, I could coordinate with the cleric in the first group, gather any of our people looking for a group and start a "sister" group nearby. Once again we've increased the number of groups, made druids and shamans more group friendly, and opened more groups so classes that aren't typical first-tier choices will not "go hungry".

With pickup groups after 50th level. I can't imagine clericless groups being an overly common practice. Deaths without experience rezzes are unthinkable, corpse summoning is a gigantic hassle, dragging corpses manually to a cleric using invis, ivu, or even a rogue is almost impossible, so I know personally in a straight out pickup group I would never want to go anywhere without a cleric unless I was camping right at the zone line, or I was camping right next to a group with a cleric willing to uproot the group to come rez us.

So while groups could exist without clerics, I think the risks and special conditions it would take to mitigate those risks, combined with the simple fact that it would indeed create more groups, and thus have a very small impact on the number of groups available for clerics.

Regarding your question, "What can they do if they can't find a group?" This is a valid issue. I submit however, and I'm sure you'd agree, that there are more than a few classes who are even more group-dependent than clerics and are in the same boat.

My answer is this - in the big picture, class balance needs to be looked at in such a way to make it easier to make good groups that are reasonably safe and moderately efficient with whatever classes you have at hand as long as those classes cover the basic needs. The bonus for having specialists is that they should make a group more efficient, however not to the point where Verant feels they must adjust the encounters with specialists in mind to prevent trivializing any encounters. At that point the specialist goes from being the advantage - the cutting edge - to being the bare necessity and then we start this class balance nightmare all over again.
