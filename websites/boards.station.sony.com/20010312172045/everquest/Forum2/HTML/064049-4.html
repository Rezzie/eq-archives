EuroEQ,
I like and admire your idea of an advisory board for game development. I think it would enhance Verant's public image and quality of the game development (and community) if they could pull it off.

However, keep in mind where the online game industry comes from. When they say "You're in our world now" they *don't* mean "your-and-my-world", they mean "Us, Verant, Game Designers who Rule!"

The driving force behind the creation of EQ was the gamers' previous experience of MUDs and other online more-or-less graphical multiplayer games. They feel a sense of ownership, of knowing what's best for Norrath, and while they listen to users suggestions and while they take the temperature of the community, are not be motivated to relinquish control of the product. EverQuest, as a product, is less like a house that an architect is building for you, and more like an artist's creation. You don't like the shade of blue in the sky? Well, that's just your taste.

Personally, I think that having an advisory board, similar to a hospital (for example,) would be a stroke of customer-centered genius in a high-production-value, community-centered, persistent-world game like EQ. Pulling it off would be a coup. A game designer, somewhere, who is interested in evolving worlds, would want to do it.

---Opal
ex-customer service for a now-defunct online multi-user role-playing game... for a company now owned by an 800-pound-gorilla game company.
