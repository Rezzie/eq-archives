On why some players choose to solo rather than group:

The point of soloing is to try gain experience at a faster rate so you can level faster and gain better spells earlier than your peers so you would be more helpful to them. If you're useless to your friends in the end why do you still play this class and endure 60 levels of boredom (assuming you are really stupid enough to solo all the way)?

Now, with the two expansions, the world is designed around the concept of grouping. Group and dungeon experience bonus, group/raid friendly spell additions, hybrid experience penalty removal, Skyfire as the only post-50 soloing zone, outdoor soloable spawn time change and numerous other changes have made it much, much better to group than solo. No one in their sane mind will solo all the way from 50 to 60.

Now, the power of a class is measured by their ability to contribute in a grouping situation. Not everyone can see through this, but to someone (I don't) who has both a 60 enchanter and 60 necromancer, he can easily see just how weak and pathetic the necro class has become. So tell me Absor, about the immense power that the necromancer wields. Show me where such power lies in a 6 player group and a 36 player raid. Explain to me the logic of placing artificial restrictions on necromancers spells so only *one* necro is needed in any group AND raid when Brad goes around telling everyone to group.

In my humble opinion, the necro class at present is far too weak.

What are Sedulous Subversion, Touch of Night and Emissary of Thule compared to:

1. Greater Voceration, Scars of Sigil and Aegis of Ro
2. Gift of Pure Thought, Wonderous Rapidity and Rapture
3. Lure of Ice, Sunstrike and Ice Spear of the Solist
4. Focus of Spirit, Malo and Turgur's Insects
5. Protection of the Glades, Wildfire, Shield of Blades
6. Aegolism, Complete Healing and Celestial Elixir

There you have it, arguably 3 most useful spells to a group from each of the 7 classes and you can see other than the necromancer, the other 6 classes are GROUP-FRIENDLY.

Sedulous Subversion is NOT group friendly.
Touch of Night is resisted.
Emissary of Thule dies in a few seconds of AoE or ripostes.

End result: Necros? Oh they can summon corpse! And that's it.

If the intention of Verant is to make necromancers group friendly (and I hope it is), there are 10 things you should change.

(1) Make 50+ lifetaps unresistable with the level cap. Easy way to do this is to make them save versus disease and therefore this change will not affect shadowknights whose best lifetap is Drain Soul, the 49 necro lifetap.

(2) Maintain current ratio of mana transfer to keep it as a downtime reduction method during mass buffing rather than something strategic during boss encounters. Read my earlier post on page 2 in Part 2 which details how changing this ratio affects delicate game balance.

(3) Maintain current dots and make Ignite/Chill Bones a normal DD with an upgraded damage to mana ratio without the recast, skeleton illusion and unstackability OR give new dots that are the reverse of cleric's Celestial Healing/Elixir (ie. GROUP FRIENDLY dots) and maintain current recast time of Ignite/Chill Bones.

Note: Chill Bones is 510 damage for 210 mana (bad ratio), 6 seconds casting time (long), 12 seconds recast time (very long), all or nothing (high resistability) and skeleton illusion. But necros use this spell extensively in normal every day grouping for want of a more group friendly damage spell.

(4) Make 50+ pets a linear upgrade from 44 and 49 pets with immunity to normal fear, good magic/poison/disease resists and dagger speed buff. This makes it our primary source of damage. The 59 pet should be able to outdamage a 59 warrior but not out-tank him. Remove lifetap proc if you wish but lifetaps are there to break root so it already has its disadvantage. Also, this justifies the higher cost of our pet buff compared to mages.

(5) Make Convergence return 96-100% experience. Make Infusion a similar spell like Harvest (with the lower mana boost) that requires but doesn't consume an Essence Emerald OR make it require another reagent and remove its recast time. Make Trucidation require the same recast time with no reagent OR remove its recast time and make it require a peridot or a special gem bought from Neriak and Cabilis.

(6) Remove night-time only requirement on Levant, Skin like the Shadow and Quivering Veil of Xarn and give Death Peace at 60. If stacking issues of Quivering Veil of Xarn is too hard to fix simply remove the spell and give Death Peace at 58. Also, remove the 115% magnification on Skin like the Shadow. If you feel the need to balance out the spells for removing the night-time only requirement, here's what you can do. Make Levant suck up caster mana like wizard's Evacuate. Lower duration of Skin like the Shadow as a fixed invisibility spell. Remove some benefits of Quivering Veil.

(7) Make Defoliation work on all plants. Give us 29 Defoliate. It currently does not exist.

(8) Lower aggro and resistability on Scent line of spells. Make the spell debuff more and covers all Fire, Cold, Poison and Disease (not magic). Give an upgrade to Scent at 60 that is unresistable like Tash, Malo and Mala.

(9) Move Demi Lich to 55 and keep Arch Lich at 60. Lich is currently 20 hp to 20 mana, so therefore make Demi Lich 26 hp to 26 mana and Arch Lich 32 hp to 32 mana with spectre illusion. As for the Lich bug, test the benefits of using Lich, Demi Lich and Arch Lich against using Clarity 2 in various grouping situations. If Lich is overpowered, then fix it. If it is not, then maintain current 3:2 ratio on spell and leave the bug as it is, or change the ratio to 1:1 and fix the bug.

(10) Change current Root spell order to the proper order of Root, Enstill, Immobilize and Paralyzing Earth.

These 10 points covered all my concerns for the post-50 game and goes a long way to make necromancers group friendly and wanted in raids in the grouping EverQuest of today.

If the intention of Verant is to make the necromancer a solo class with hardly anything decent to offer to a group, then sadly this game only has 13 classes. It is easy to see why the necromancer class requires the most upgrades since it's caught like an odd child that is designed to solo at the beginning while the game has changed significantly into a grouping game half way through, with the release of Kunark and Velious.
