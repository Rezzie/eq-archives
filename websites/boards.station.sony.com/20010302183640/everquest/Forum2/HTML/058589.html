All of the games coming out are RPGs much like EQ that WILL require hours upon hours of time investment (you will not see many people playing more than one).

Plain and simple, you ONLY have a maximum of a few million people who will dedicate this time to a game. Right there the best any given company can hope for is maybe 100k subscribers per month.

EQ presently runs on about a thousand boxes (which probably cost somewhere around 5 million total to buy). Maintenance on said machines is roughly 100k a month. Server bandwith is at least 500k a month. support, live team, etc. is probably close to 1-2 million a month at least.

At costs somewhere in the neighborhood of 2-3 million dollars a month, and revenue of ~3 million dollars a month, a company can NOT compete without a product that does well from go.

With 3 (the ones that are actually going to hit the shelves, unlike say dawn or other garbage) BRAND NEW GAMES coming out, and an actual STANDARD to measure up to, how many do you think will be successful?

My guess is UO2 (already a big name game) will do well. Anarchy online should have a fair showing.

This is hardly anything of note. So maybe EQ's player base will fall to around 200k or so over the next year. BFD. For a game this old, that is still phenominal. EQ2 will be out the door soon enough, and then I can guarantee who will be #1 again.


Being able to connect to the internet means jack.

I can connect to the i-net with a friggin' ipaq, but I certainly can't run a modern game.

The other day I bought Alice. Minimum sys req. on that is 400mhz processor and 64mb ram.

That eliminates over 1/2 the playerbase right there.

UO2, horizons, etc. Will likely need 800mhz or faster chips, 32mb video cards, etc. as a bare minimum.

Regular folks (those who only use their computers for stuff like web browsing) will NEVER be a significant target for online games. (except perhaps on consoles)

People seem to think that right now graphics are approaching the state where they are as good as they will ever get.

Even some of the new X-Box and PC games in development are a long shot from being as good looking as CG movies, and those CG movies are a long shot from looking like real life.

What does this mean?
