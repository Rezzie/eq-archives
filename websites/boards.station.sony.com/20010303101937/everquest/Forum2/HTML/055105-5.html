Wow. What an excellent argument.

"Adding a bard to a group of 5 should increase their power MORE than any other class, not LESS."

We are, like the enchanter and the shaman, not a damage output/absorption class, but a group support class. Enchanters are better at damage output and crowd control, as well as increasing mana recovery. Shamans are better at damage output, healing, and buffing. Both shaman and enchanter buffs, once cast, have no limitations beyond time. A shaman can buff up all warriors of the party and not have to worry about it again for 20 mins to an hour. Which means that he can pile on buffs, and THEN blast and debuff the enemy, and heal the party. Enchanters buff some, blast VERY well, and have excellent crowd control.
While Bard songs do not use mana (mostly) the other limitations they suffer from are much greater. They must be twisted to be effective, which adds physical strain to playing a bard, as well as a susceptibility to increasingly common lag. It also means a bard can either run 3 buffs, or 2 buffs and a crowd control or 1 buff, a crowd control, and a Damage Shield. A real problem. Further, bard buffs are very limited in range. A SOW or levitate as an enchantment is great for the whole party. The bard songs that do the same require constant attention to avoid losing party members. Literally in the case of the levitate song.
Whether haste is made to stack or not, bard songs must be at least 2/3 the power of caster spells of the same level. The range limitations of the buffs, and the 3 song twist limitation put the lie to the idea that because they dont cost mana bard songs must be kept weak.

Either bard damage must be brought up to near parity with other classes, or their buffs/crowd control must be brought to near parity. The defensive increases suggest that there is some confused idea that bards will take on more of a melee role, but even if defense is increased, bards are still hopelessly deficient in 1)Damage dealing 2)Good AC items 3)HPs when compared with other classes.
