I don't post very often at all to this board but after the past few days I feel that in so doing I might get a bit off my chest and relieve a bit of tension. Not to imply this is going to be a flame or a rant, but simply to share my thoughts and feelings on the recent changes *cough* imposed not only on clerics but on the game itself.
Firstly, I would like to thank PlantingTZ for his/her support and for NOT being one of the Druids who live on the boards to constantly belittle and vilify the cleric class (ie. Reiryc).I was one of the clerics in SUPPORT of druids and shamans getting a remedy-like heal. Too many times I've watched our druids struggle to keep our casters alive, because you see, THIS was the druid role in our guild raids. Druids always had a purpose and always had a place. They were never there simply for SoW, evacs and ports. I felt, and I'm sure the casters they were healing did too, that they played a very important part in these raids. I was a better healer, yes. But that is what I did. That is ALL I did. As a cleric, I have little (really no) soloability, my dd's are laughable when so many are FAR more proficient at it than I, I can't tank, I can't invis, I can't dot, I can't evac....but I COULD heal. I chose that. I never found it to be boring. Any more than any other class can complain that standing and tanking is boring. When I made my cleric a year and a half ago I knew I was choosing to always rely on a group.
When I saw the spells put on the test server, and saw the heal that druids get at level 60, I thought that was pushing a bit much. In essence druids have forfeited absolutely nothing to have the ability to solo to 55 and beyond. If any class other than clerics should have gotten that level 60 spell, it should have arguably been the shaman. I really don't see Veran't reasoning behind druids getting the heal over shamans other than the vast sea of druids on each server. Maybe it's the same reason that druids get SoW2 before shamans when initially shaman's got SoW sooner. I've never played a shaman but I can see how they would be upset at these oversights by Verant. I have nothing against druids. I know a lot of very nice people who play them and there are some that I would love to always have healing me as I heal others.
That seems to be no longer the case though. With these changes and my new inability to use CH effectively, I have been basically turned into a level 60 druid BUT with a lower hp buff, no sow, no evac, no regen, no snare, no ports, no harmony, no invis, no mana regen...need I go on?
IF I choose to continue to play my cleric I know I will always have a place in a group, simply because I have friends who like me as a person, and also because I HAVE spent a year and a half learning how to be a HEALER. She will be great for PL-ing, and pharming (something she's never done, but I guess my younger char's may be needing better stuff). I just have to wonder how many druids and shamans REALLY want to be flat out healers. Most I've ever talked to have said "I never wanted to be a cleric."
The most astonishing thing I have seen on these boards since the patch was Abashi's complete indifference and casual disregard, to clerics specifically and to the high level game in general. His very words implied that he and as a representative of the company, Verant is completely ambivalent to clerics. The high end game in itself is difficult and experience is no longer something to disregard. When a player is excited to have gotten ONE blue bubble after hours of game play, the thought of repetitious dying, by the clerics and all of the other group members that will be falling after him/her is not to me, remotely a challenge, nor is the prospect fun.
THANK YOU ABASHI for addressing our concerns
