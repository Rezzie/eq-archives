There is a problem with V5 latest drivers and EQ that will cause stuttering or hesitations. You notice it most indoors when you are using the keyboard to make a turn. You also see these when you are running through the halls and turn left or right.

I'm pretty sure it's the V5 latest drivers that is the issue. I just upgraded my computer to a 1.2g AMB Athlon Tbird, 256 meg, SB live, Win ME (dx8) system and notice the stuttering in runeye. When I went back to my old system (PIII 500, V5, Mx300, Win 98fe, DX7a) I was not seeing the stutter in glide (there was a little in d3d).

I did an Fdisk on my hard drive and reinstalled win 98 first edition (how do I upgrade that to SE) and did the critical updates and installed DX7a. I then unloaded the V5 1.04.01 beta and loaded an older version (I think it is 1.01.00) that goes with dx7a and the stutter was gone. Running very smooth.

So my guess is that the 1.04.xx series has an issue with EQ. My other games seem to work fine with it.

For WIN2K Users:

To get a functional control panel control for DirectX, the easiest way is to install the DirectX SDK. Unless you have a fast connection, I recommend using the web install, as the full download is ~130MBs.

Install the retail, not the debug version of the DirectX drivers when prompted. After rebooting, you will have access to the fine tune control for each and every aspect of DirectX, including setting your debug levels. Hope that helps. Have fun. =)
