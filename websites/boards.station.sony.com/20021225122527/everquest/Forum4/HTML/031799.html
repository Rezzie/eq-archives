I bought it and like it.

Let's face it, it's meant for people who have already played the game for some time, and are looking for a reference, some background info, and a handy way to go "I just came in here, so I wanna go NE to get to there". It's also handy to write your own notes in. Mark spots as you come to them and write the locs/name at the bottom of the page. I think it's worth the price just to have a nice condensed map book to mark stuff in.

A lot easier than printing out 400 or so maps, and a lot tidier too. Hella easier than trying to sift through 400 pages of white bond paper to find something, and still even easier than having to log off to go look at a map.

However, for those of you who are fans of EQAtlas and Muse's map, she made a GUI for EQ that allows you to download the maps from her site and open them in a window IN EQ. Very handy indeed, and available from EQAtlas.com.
