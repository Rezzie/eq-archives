There is a thread here that has a misleading subject line. You should read the thread titled "What is the progression of items needed to raise tinkering" (or similar) - its NOT a question to the tinkering community about working up the skill. It is a summation of the items needed to work up tinkering skill to the close to 200 point, and why this is a problem where the Watchman Armor is concerned.

In case that thread gets lost, here's my own summation of the situation:

Once Powered Gloves and then Catapults become trivial (each about 20-30pp per attempt) the next non-trivial item is Aqualungs at roughly 210pp per attempt. From my own estimations (currently at this step myself) it will take me about 7k pp to 8kk pp to trivial Aqualungs (that's just money spent on making Aqualungs, not on all the stuff that went previously). When those become trivial, the only things left that can raise skill are Rebreathers and Watchman Armor. Rebreathers cost roughly 1k pp per attempt - 5 times as much as the Aqualungs, and if the other trades are any indication, skill increase slows considerably the higher the skill gets. 100k pp to 150k pp is not an unreasonable estimation of what it will take to get from the Aqualung trivial point to the Rebreather trivial point, which is estimated in the low to mid 180's (no one has done it yet, so the exact trivial point is *still* unknown).

Now, from success rates on Rebreathers and Watchman Armor (actually, *complete* lack of success on Watchman Armor) it can be surmised that Watchman Armor trivials somewhere past 200 skill. This being the case, odds are good maybe one or two people out of the entire EQ populace will ever devote enough resources to raising tinkering skill high enough to actually make this stuff. This makes the Enchanted Imbued Brellium Plate look like Banded in comparison.

I'm sincerely hoping that Tinkering will someday get the revamp it desperately needs - this wasn't it, unfortunately.
