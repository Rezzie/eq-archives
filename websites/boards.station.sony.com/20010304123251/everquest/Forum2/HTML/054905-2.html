My view of warriors and hybrids:

All this talk seems to center on a warrior and a Palangerknight (I love making up new words) competing for a spot in a group. I look at it differently. In most high level groups you really need two melee classes. I'm a warrior, my hunting partner is a cleric and when I group for experience the group is not planned by class, it evolves with what friends are online, what friends are busy, etc. There are a few exceptions for certain zones, such as Howling Stones where an evaccer and a chanter is required, or a rogue to open doors, but for the most part we have fun with whoever we end up with.

This makes for some interesting groups and adds challenge to otherwise boring xp hunts. Ever stopped a Chardok train of 7 with only necro mezzes and Paly parking for crowd control? Man, that was fun. My group last night was warrior, cleric, monk, shaman, shaman, shaman. It just happened. We changed some tactics, talked it out, and the hunt went very very well. I'm never going to tell a Palangerknight not to join us because we were hoping for a Warrior. Well, maybe if I didn't like the guy and wanted to piss him off, but that's not the point What I'm getting at is simple. The funnest xp groups I've been in have not been traditionally balanced groups. Had a group of low 40's once kill the Ishva: Warrior, wizard, necro, necro, necro, necro. It was hysterical, but we did the job. If you're always looking out for the 'perfectly balanced' group then you're wasting time you could be using to have fun. And balanced groups are so boring! Pull, kill, pull, kill, rinse repeat. 99.999999999 percent of most peoples' combat time is spent in xp groups. Why get into a grind? Not only do unbalanced groups make for fun hunts, but they make you think. They make you learn new tactics, and more importantly they force you to discuss and learn more about other classes. Or your own classes, even. One shaman in a group knows his role. Add a second shaman and both of them get to learn new tactics and how to work together to help the group. In the hunt I mentioned above, the shamans were parking attendants, and damn good ones. I could pull four sarnaks, and have no worries about our ability to win the fight. With no mezzes or charms. Everybody had fun.

Palangerknights have a very undefined, yet resourceful role in the groups I'm used to, and it really comes into play when we have multiple mobs.

Paladins make the best parking attendants I've ever seen. Why? Because they can take some hits while doing so. They can take their time, park the mob right where it needs to be, and come back to the main fight. They also rock hardcore interrupting mob Gates. Could have used their stuns last night... They can patch heal a bit too, especially the cleric if she's under attack.

Shadowknights get a lot of respect from me. I can't outtaunt them, really. In my mind, they shine best when the group gets ambushed. I stay on the main target, as the main tank, and the SK is quick to pull the new mob off the cleric or caster, and solo tank it while we kill the first mob. Yes, he tanks very very well. This is especially seen in PoG, where many mobs are unmezzable. On our raids there, I'm main assist and our favorite SK is main split. When we get two unmezzable mobs, me and the whole guild work on one, while the sk singlehandedly holds the other until we've killed the first. (Ok, often times a dern rogue backstabs the hell out of his mob while he's tanking it, silly ksing rogues!) This system works very very well. In PoG, the extra mobs always make a beeline for the clerics. A warrior just can't taunt them off as fast as a shadowknight can. In regular xp groups, the sk does a similar job. Melees with me (and yes, he does good dmg) and taunts extras off casters. We could have used some of his darkness snares last night too...

Rangers: Ok, Rangers need work, I'll admit. In xp groups, they're very well suited, acting similar to the shadowknights with their snares and taunts. But I have even more trouble taunting off them then I do the sk. Has a lot to do with warrior vs ranger epic weapons, but that's a different thread altogether. I love having a ranger pull, I think that is their strong point. Sure, I have more hps and AC, I can take more hits on the way in, but what's the point when a ranger can snare pull and not take a hit at all? This does cause a taunting problem, but it's a matter of talking the ranger into not attacking the mob in camp until I'll pulled it off him. Tracking is huge in quest related groups, also, I don't think anyone can deny that. But Rangers do have issues on large raids, I'm not sure what the solution is, really.

The more I think about the test changes, the less they bother me. I don't understand the bard's defense though, but I'm not going to go there right now. As long as there is a clear distinction between who is the main tank, and who is a secondary tank plus utility, then I'm happy. This test upgrade closes the gap some, but not enough to significantly change the status. And if you're grouped with Palangerknights, it benefits you as well. I'm just hoping some of the warrior concerns get addressed as well.

Wow, this post got long. Sorry about that.
