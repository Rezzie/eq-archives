----------------------------
1. High level damange scaling

Bard damage doesn't seem to increase between levels 50-60 as much as melee or caster damage. Here's what Thott (guildleader of Afterlife) had to say on it:

For the curious, chant [of frost and flame] damage increases less than 30% from 50 to 60.

Rogue melee damage, by comparison, increases from 30 damage/sec to about 100 damage/sec in the same period, going by parser data from both levels. 30 damage/sec is with the best weapons available when we were capped at 50, 100 damage/sec is with the best weapons available now that we're capped at 60.

In general, melee damage triples from 50 to 60 and spell damage increases 50%. Bard "spell" damage is really a form of melee damage, since the damage is slow yet takes no mana. Yet for some reason it follows a lesser curve than regular spell damage. I have no idea why.

Regular spell damage actually increases by more than 50%. 50% is the improvement in the spells themselves from 50 to 60. In practice the boost is greater, because total mana and mana regen increases from 50 to 60 as well. This isn't true for bards.

--Thott
Guildleader
<Afterlife>

Thott has done some nice graphs that show the problem pretty clearly:

www.afterlifeguild.org/Th...urves.html

Most bard songs don't get upgrades. They're designed to get better with level. If you take our Chant series as an example (a targeted DoT), from level 40 to 50, the song increases from 39 damage for 3 ticks to 48 damage for 3 ticks (+23%). From levels 50 to 60 it'll go from 48x3 to 57x3 (+19%). You designed the level 50+ spells to represent a larger increase in power than just 10 levels. Unfortunately, the power of our songs increases based on a level-dependent formula, and so going from 50 to 60 is actually a smaller boost for us than going from 40 to 50. What was impressive at level 40 becomes so-so at level 50, and is approaching negligible at level 60.

Suggested fixes that I like:

- Introduce new high-level instruments with bigger song effect multipliers. (And no, requiring wood elf bards to kill creatures in Tunare's plane does not count. Considering there are no evil-race bards, if you're going to put bard instruments in a good plane as drops, at least make them questable too.)

- Introduce weapons that can also double as an instrument. Kinda like the bard epic weapon, but only specific to a single instrument.

- Tweak the formulae used for song effects in the 50-60 range, to accurately reflect the large increase in damage/effectiveness other classes get in the 50-60 range.

2. Mana drain

Many high-level encounters are against mobs that heal or gate, and thus draining the mob's mana becomes an important strategy. Two classes in the game get mana drains - enchanters and bards. But for all practical purposes, only enchanters can drain mana.

Enchanters get one spell at level 34 which drains 214-370 mana (e.g. at level 57 it'll drain 352, has a mana cost of 150, and a cast+recycle time of 11 seconds. At level 55+ with Clarity 2, 150 mana can be medded back in 30 seconds. At level 57 these correspond to drain rates of 32 mana/sec considering only cast time, and 9 mana/sec if you factor in meditate time. As a bonus, it can't be resisted.

Bards get two mana drain songs, both of which are resistable. Both songs are instrument based, but an instrument does not increase the amount of mana drained.

The level 35 song (Denon's Dissension) is AE pulse based and drains 24 mana/pulse (i.e. you lose mana every time I sing the song). If I chain-sing this song (restart it every 3 seconds), I need 4x longer to drain as much mana as a like-level enchanter. It works out to about the same amount of time as the enchanter if you factor in med time, but you have to understand that an enchanter can haste and clarity his party before the fight, and mez extra mobs while he's draining mana. If I chain-sing this song, I can do *nothing* else except melee to contribute to the fight. Also, from level 37 to 57 this song only increases 9% in the amount of mana drained. The enchanter mana drain goes from 229 to 352 in the same level range (+54%). The way this song scales with level needs to be improved *badly*.

The level 57 song (Cassindra's Insipid Ditty) is single target and drains a laughable 1.5 mana/sec at level 57. The drain rate is so low that most bards mistakenly thought the song didn't drain mana at all. It is 21x slower than enchanter mana drain. I would need 4 minutes to accomplish what the enchanter can do every 11 seconds. If you factor in meditate time, in the 4 minutes takes me to drain 350 mana with this song, an enchanter can drain 2100 mana, sit down, and med back all the mana he used. If we haven't killed the mob by then. Consider removing or lowering the resist check as well so we can use it on level 63 mobs. Ditty has a wis/int debuff component so it will not stack with itself, and only a single bard at a raid can be using it on a target.

3. The decline in the importance of bard resists

In the 55+ large target encounters, resists seem to be less important. Back when I was 45-50, having bards to boost everyone's resists could make or break a Nagafen/Vox raid. Raising resists was so important most bards at these raids chose to use a drum instead of weapons, foregoing doing any damage.

Against Gorenaire, Talendor, Trakanon, etc, having my resists at 255 doesn't seem to make much if any difference. I get feared and take full AE damage anyway. Giving mobs spells that are so difficult to resist diminishes the role of a bard in these raids. I currently only play resists because a lot of my friends enjoy seeing their resists in the 200+ range. Personally I don't think it makes that much difference from the 150-200 resist most players are achieving with equipment and spell buffs. I hear there's a "soft cap" at 175 after which resists make much less of a difference.

Solution: Remove the "soft cap" if it exists. Improve the player resist rate against these high-end targets with maximum resist, so that there's a significant benefit to getting up near 255. Double-check to make sure there's no roll-over bug when resists exceed 255 (or whatever the max is in your code).

4. Instruments don't increase haste/slows

This is really a bug, but a pretty major one. Berserker's Crescendo (42), Rousing Rondo (57), Melody of Ervaj (50), Composition of Ervaj (60), Assonant Strane (54), and Anstlich's Assonance (60) all have hastes/slows that are sub-par for their level (i.e. lower level songs are better). All of these are instrument songs (the better hastes/slows are singing-only). It seems like you guys designed these songs assuming an instrument would enhance the haste/slow. Problem is instruments don't do that.

20% haste - Celerity (level 36) singing
18% haste - Crescendo (42) brass
30% haste - Verses (50) singing
45% haste - Celerity2 (54) singing
22% haste - Rondo (57) brass

5% haste - Melody of Ervaj (50) brass required
10% haste - Composition of Ervaj (60) brass required

Comparing Rondo (57) to our level 54 song is a joke. If you're singing Rondo, a mob hitting your party on average 1.5 times every 3 sec will take 4 dam/sec from the damage shield. For the damage shield to offset the 23% loss in haste, your party must be doing less than 12 dam/sec melee damage for Rondo to be better than Celerity2. A level 50 bard can almost do that much dam/sec solo. If the bard is using a horn, the party has to be doing less than 36 dam/sec - 20 dam/sec for bard melee = 16 dam/sec melee for Rondo to be better. This comparison is pitiful. There simply is no survivable situation in which it's advantageous to use our level 57 haste instead of our 54.

The same reasoning applies to our slow songs. Currently our first slow (level 20) is still one of our best (level 23 and 51 songs equal it). From level 20 to 60, a bard's slows scale from a 15% to 35% reduction in damage (a 2.3x improvement). In the same level range, an enchanter's slows go from a ~20% to 70% reduction in damage (a 3.5x improvement). A shaman's slows go from ~25% to 75% reduction in damage (a 3.0x improvement). Given that our slows are already the weakest to begin with, is it that unreasonable for our slows to at least improve with level to the same degree as enchanter/shaman slows? The level 54 slow if augmented by an instrument would reach about 50%, and the level 60 slow would reach 60%.

Thott has done a nice write-up on the history of hastes and the problems that led to the misunderstanding that instruments augmented them.

www.afterlifeguild.org/Th...cking.html

Please either fix hastes/slows so an instrument increases them, or change these songs to have double their current haste/slow (optionally making them instrument-only).

5. The effects on our Kunark and Velious armor show a misunderstanding of the bard class

Singing Steel armor effects are still worthless (literally). Using a right-click effect locks us out of our songs for the duration of the cast.

- The BP is a self-only 100 point heal with a 12 second cast time. In 12 seconds, a level 50 bard using a Plane of Hate lute can heal 80 hp for each party member. I don't even know what the point is making the BP's heal self-only.

- The vambraces have an effect of Invigorate - a targeted stamina replenishment. Our level 3 song replenishes stamina for the entire party. Our level 34 song does it while healing and giving mana.

- The boots summon 1 water at a time with a 5 second cast time. PLEASE change this to summon 10 or 20 at a time so it's at least useful. As it is, I just buy my water in bulk instead of taking 2 minutes to summon a stack of water that'll all disappear every time I go linkdead.

- The greaves summon 1 food at a time. See boots above.

For the other classes that get it, Kunark armor effects give them something they didn't have before. For melee classes it's access to spells. For casting classes, it's mana-less spells. For bards... well we already had song-spells that were mana-less. In order for the armor effects to be advantageous to us, they need to do something we couldn't normally do (which is why the BP and vambraces are silly). Bards don't get long-duration buffs. That's what Singing Steel should do. The bracer (damage shield buff) and gloves (enduring breath) are perfect examples of this. Summon food, summon water, summon eye would also marginally fall under this category. Please change the BP and vambraces to do something that we normally can't do on our own.

I suspect the same thing is happening with the Velious bard armor effects. Currently the Melodic BP right-clicks Song of Travel. Song of Travel has 12 second duration and an invis component so it drops every time you right-click to re-activate it. Since you have to re-activate it every 12 seconds, it's dropping every 12 seconds. It's pretty silly to put this on an item as an effect (actually, it's pretty silly to make right-click effects out of any song that bards usually leave playing, since you have to refresh it every 12 seconds).

6. Bard movement slowdowns are the only random duration movement slowdowns in the game

Selo's Constant Chain (level 23 song) was originally coded to have the largest movement slowdown in the game. You guys didn't seem to have a problem with the movement slowdown, but an interaction between it and fear caused a situation you felt was exploitable. So you capped movement slowdowns at the best necro darkness spell. This nerf affects us even when we aren't fearing, and you didn't compensate us for it in any way.

Chain, Largo's Absonant Binding (51), and Assonant Strane (L54 AE movement slowdown) are all random in duration. They could last the full 12 seconds, they could wear off immediately. No other movent slowdown spells in the game have a random duration, even though the spell versions last 10-50 times longer than Chain. It can't be because Chain has a slow component attached because no other slow spells in the game have a random duration either. I hear all the movement slowdowns except the level 1 snare used to be random duration, and were gradually changed to have a fixed guaranteed duration - except the bard ones. In light of the above one-sided nerf, perhaps you could give these songs a guaranteed 12 second duration?

There's also an infrequent bug that affects all the movement debuff spells. If you try to chain/snare/darkness a mob that's already begun fleeing, sometimes the spell/song will land but will have no effect (if this happens, repeated attempts to chain/snare/darkness it will have no effect). Due to the random duration of Chain, bards are the ones who get bit by this one the most.

7. The Ervaj songs don't provide enough haste.

Melody of Ervaj and Composition of Ervaj (50, 60) were designed to stack with all other hastes. There's been much debate within the bard community about these songs, but Thott wrote something that made everything click into place for me: "Generally what I found is that power for all classes rises proportional to level squared, except for bards. For bards power rises proportional to level."

That in a nutshell explains all the problems with bard haste, as well as paladin and SK melee. Bards get only dual wield. Paladins and SKs only get double attack. All other melee classes get both dual wield and double attack. Both DW and DA increase by level, so melee damage for bards, paladins, and SKs rises proportionally to level. Other classes get both DW and DA so their melee damage rises proportionally to level squared. That is why paladins and SKs are so weak at melee at high level. Bards aren't quite so bad off because we get hastes (with upgraded songs coming every few levels and so our self-hasted damage goes up as level squared). But when we're grouped with an enchanter or shaman doing hastes, that benefit completely disappears and we're in the same boat as paladins and SKs.

The Ervaj songs were a mistake. By giving us haste songs that stacked with our *own* hastes, you put yourself between a rock and a hard place. The stackable haste had to be large enough to preserve the boost bard melee needed from haste to stay balanced, yet small enough to prevent a bard from single-handedly out-hasting an enchanter.

At high levels, that margin simply doesn't exist. The 10% haste on the level 60 song doesn't come anywhere close to giving us the 45% haste advantage that's needed to keep us in line with the other melee classes. CoE will only be used when there's a better haste spell available than what the bard can provide, so figure 50% haste (shaman Celerity). Worst case all the melee folks will have no haste items. The bard has to use an instrument so you lose 20 dam/sec right there. For CoE to offset that loss, the other tanks in the party have to be doing more than 300 dam/sec hasted. [300/(1+0.5)]*(1+0.5+0.1) - 20 = 300. The only way to achieve this dam/sec rating is to have 2-3 rogues in the group. It gets even worse if you consider near-best case. Quickening (64% haste) + FBSS (21% haste) = 85% haste. For the extra 10% haste from CoE to offset the loss of bard melee, the other tanks in the party have to be doing more than 370 dam/sec. Basically, you've designed a song that'll only realistically be used in a group composed of a cleric, bard, and 4 rogues.

Get rid of the Ervaj songs. Take the Ervaj haste code and put it into our normal haste songs, to make them all stack with haste spells. That eliminates any chance of bards single-handedly out-hasting an enchanter, while still preserving the haste advantage bards rely on for their melee damage to keep pace with other classes. The game survived fine for a year and a half with the haste bug putting everyone at max haste. It'll survive groups with enchanter+bard or shaman+bard attaining max haste more easily.

9. Bard pick locks skill is useless.

Only two classes in the game get pick locks - bards and rogues. Rogues get it at level 6, it caps at 200 at level 40, and 210(?) at level 60.

Bards get pick locks at level 40. It's capped at 100. The only locks in the game we can pick are in Befallen, some of the locks in Najena, the entrance to the Hole in Paineel, and the Tower of Frozen Shadow. Except for the Tower, all of these areas are green to us by the time we get the skill.

My guess is that you don't want the high-level locks to be pickable by level 24 rogue mules (whose pick locks also caps at 100). My suggestion then is to raise the bard pick locks cap to 201. Make some of the high-level locks require 201 skill to pick. Make the rest of the high-level locks require 202+ skill so they're still rogue-only. No rogue mule problem, bards can pick some of the high-level locks, and rogues still have a monopoly on the rest of the locks.

OTOH, if you just don't want bards picking any high level locks, leave the cap as is, but give us the skill at about level 10. That way it's at least usable by low level bards in Befallen and Najena. I'm not sure why you'd want to do this though, seeing as rogues already have several ability monopolies (backstab, pick pocket, invis+invis to undead, poisons).

10. Lack of mid-level instruments.

The game is sorely lacking in mid-level lutes. Yes there are two "mid-level" lutes that come from Mistmoore. Unfortunately, they only have a +5% modifier. The only commonly used string song until level 47 is Hymn. Hymn does not heal 20 hp/tick until level 54, at which point the lutes give the bard +1hp/tick. Since the game rounds down all fractions, the Mistmoore lutes operate the same as a store-bought lute until a bard reaches level 54. So a mid-level lute effectively doesn't exist. Please increase the modifier on the Gypsy Lute and/or Lute of the Gypsy Princess to +10%.

Horns (brass) are lacking. There's one off Vox, and one which is a random drop off sirens in Cobalt Scars. The one in CS would qualify as mid-level, except you have to get a shot at one you have to go through Kael, Wakening Lands and Wuoshi, Skyshrine (need a key), and past the huge AE cobalt drakes in CS.

Percussion instruments seem to be fairly well done. There are a wide variety available with different bonuses. One is even questable, which every bard loves.

Mid-level wind instruments are easy to get. The problem with them is that the wind songs don't really benefit from an instrument so there's not much you can do here.

As a general view of balancing the bard, I think that the removal of the exp penalty helps a great deal.

But I found that penalty to be extremely unjustified for bards primarily because they are a VERY difficult class to play.

First of all, the way songs work is different from anything else in the game. You can play a melee AND a spellcaster into the 40's and you will still need to learn some game mechanics to play a bard. (I know this because the other night I was tutoring a guildmate who plays a 48 war and 42 dru in twisting.)

Second of all, we are the "jack of all trades". Necros need to learn effective use of fear. Enchanters must learn manipulation of charm and mez. Druids learn the careful balance of footspeed and snare. Bards must learn ALL of these, and more.

Finally, there is the "master of none" clause on that above quote. We can do just about everything... but only briefly. This adds another level of complexity, on top of that which is mentioned before.
