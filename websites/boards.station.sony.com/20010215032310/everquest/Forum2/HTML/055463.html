My point of view is that there were only minor changes that needed to be implemented prior to level 45 so I will concentrate on what I believe Rangers need for levels 46+ IN GROUP SITUATIONS.

THE PROBLEMS:
-------------
#1) Caster-like defense. Ranger's spend 90%+ of their time in melee range yet have armor which is equal or lower IN AC than that of shammies and clerics.

#2) Poor hit avoidance. Rangers are restricted to chain armor which I assume is because they are supposed to be light agile fighters, who can avoid hits better than warriors, but take considerably more damage when hit. However, Rangers have lower dodge caps than most of the other melee'ers and they don't have either the block or evade hit avoidance skills that the other armor restricted melee'ers, Monks and Rogues have.

#3) Poor offensive spells. With the progression of higher dmg/dly weapons, casting during melee will typically result in less overall damage than purely melee. Casting a 4.5 second spell will result in the loss of 3-4 full rounds of melee(taking into account fizzles/resists/aggros). Cloak of Flames(long quest) and Calefaction(lvl 59) being the only exception to the above rule.

#4 Poor overall offense. For being the "offensive" hybrid, Rangers still have lower damage than Rogues, Monks and Warriors.

#5 Poor stat buff/resist spells. LEVEL 50 Ranger stat buffs and resists are matched or exceeded by most LEVEL 19 casters. SOW and Str of Nature being the only oft useable group buffs.


POSSIBLE SOLUTIONS:
-------------------
#1) Caster-like defense. Raise defensive skill to 252 (even at the expense of the offensive increases that were proposed on test.) IMO opinion, even if offensive caps were maxed we would be hard pressed to out damage Rogues, Monks or Warriors.

#2) Poor hit avoidance. Raise the avoidance skills ABOVE those of plate classes. Either that or implement a block/evade type skill. This seems logical for lighter, agile fighters.

#3) Poor offensive spells. Model more spells after higher level druid spells with the DRAWBACK of recast times that are 5-10x longer than druids. This would give us spells that are useable, but balanced, while maintaining the claim that Rangers are 25% druid.

#4 Poor overall offense. I don't think that we will be out damaging rogues, monks or warriors any time soon. I would just as soon see defensive improvements.

#5 Poor stat buff/resist spells. Save for SOW, I would toss all the stat buff and resist spells for improvements in defense.


SUMMARY:
--------
It seems that VI wants to make Rangers the "offensive" hybrid. Unless our offense moves up past 4th in offensive melee power, this will not improve our grouping situation. However, increases in defense will have a dramatic effect on the defense and perception of Rangers. IMO I would like to see a more balanced Ranger with all the improvements being in the defensive area.

As far as the HP bump, it was implemented to make Knights better tanks during the Ivy Etched period when there was little difference in AC between Ranger armor and Knight armor.

That AC disparity hasn't existed since. Knight planar armor, Knuark armor, and Velious armor all have significantly increased AC over Ranger armor. Therefor the huge HP gap is not justified 50+.

During my 38 levels without SoW I made it a point to *not* go around begging or /auc'ing for SoW every 35 minutes. I did, waaaaay back in Part One, advocate a running speed buff for Rangers as low as L9, but with the no-combat/cast limit. I'll stand on that. Better travel speed, yes; better combat mobility, no.

Leave SoW where it is.

(And that's from a dedicated lone Ranger.)

Okay, so the theory is Rangers are the Warrior who gave up his armor for Tracking and Druid magic, but kept his offense.

One could also argue the Ranger is a Monk like class who gave up Feign Death and some offense for Tracking and Druid magic. Or, a Ranger is a Roguish class who gave up Backstab and other Roguish abilities for these things.

Warriors want to get hit. Monks can Feign Death when aggro'd. Rogues Evade. If Rangers are intended to be at Warrior level offense, they need to be able to inflict their damage for the whole fight, like a Warrior can. There is a difference between allowing Rangers to actively tank (not what anyone wants) and allowing them to attack without being so fearful of death that they need to reduce damage output in order to control aggro. Paladins and SK sacrifice offense for spells, but the spells compensate by providing the taunt lost with the offense, allowing them to tank (assuming they can take the damage). Rangers do not get as many spells or abilities that allow them to inflict damage despite the taunt that the damage creates as Paladins and SK get that allow THEM to take damage despite the low taunt their lack of damage provides.

If Rangers could chew away at mobs as second tank in place of a Warrior (maybe a bit less well, like the Knights are lesser tanks to the Warrior) then Rangers would be happy I think. But a Warrior as second tank doesn't mind getting hit, and the Ranger will get hit just as often and will not be able to continue the assault if he fears getting hit.

I am only level 25 as a Ranger and my highest is only 35, so maybe I don't know the real situation fully, but this is how it seems to me. Rangers should be able to be a solid damage inflicting meleer, a bit behind Warriors in that role perhaps, who cannot tank, just as Knights should be solid tanks, but a bit behind Warriors, who cannot do second melee very well. Rangers should not be dying faster than Monks or Rogues if they are doing less damage.

Let's start off quick to save you time Absor .

Half-elves: They are a mix of wood elves and humans. Now all their stats are in between the two extremes (which makes sense) - except for Wisdom. As this only affects rangers (only wisdom class half-elves can be) it seems appropriate to mention it here. I was just wondering if our parents ate GM food so that we ended up with a mutated Wisdom . Seriously, from a realistic role-playing perspective it doesn't make sense.

Spells: As mentionned on other hybrid boards, we are penalized three ways. Lower efficiency spells, more mana cost for the same spell, and lower Wisdom as we devote our equipment to melee as well as casting. I'd like to have the mana cost the same - easier to grasp than an ephemereal equivalency formula that decreases the casting cost as you increase in levels. From a roleplaying stand-point it makes sense as well - a spell is a spell is a spell. Regardless who casts it, the effect should be in proportion to the cost.

Melee (excludes special abilities - see below): If a ranger is to be comparable to a rogue, instead of a warrior, then we should be given skills in consequence. It makes for a difficult balancing act - both for Verant and us players - if we are comparing apples with oranges. Let's take for granted that we are a mix of druids and rogues. Also take for granted that rogues are balanced. Then our chain mail limitation and HP makes sense (matches the rogues). But, in that case our combat skills should also match those of rogues - offense, defense, parry, dual wield, etc. We are now balanced with a rogue and hence a warrior, etc as well. How does this balance out the spells we receive? See below.

Skills: Rangers receive several non-combat skills as do rogues. Tracking, foraging, stealth, hide, disarm. A rogue gets stealth, hide, pick locks, detect traps, backstab, evade, disarm, create poisons. Rangers rarely, if ever, use hide, sneak or disarm. We do not have the skill points to make it useful. To balance the class remove these skills. Doing so makes the rogue skills (including post 50+ disciplines) as a balance for the ranger spells.

Rogues vs. Rangers.
HP: Same
Armor: Same
Weapons: Same
Melee: Should be the same
Non-melee skills.
Pick locks, Disarm, Evade, Disciplines, Backstab, Hide, Sneak, Remove traps, Poisons vs. Spells, Tracking, Foraging.

Hope this gives you some ideas.
