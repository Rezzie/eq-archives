If you go to Plane of Knowledge (click on the book in Innothule), you can then go to any other starting city such as Freeport, but pretty much anywhere is going to be about the same time to level at this point.

quote:
4) How do you bind yourself to another location? I started venturing out with my froglok and got killed only to be taken back to Innothule swamp.

Casters get the Bind Affinity spell at 12 or 14. You can bind yourself anywhere that isn't bind-restricted. Other players can only bind you in city zones or areas otherwise marked for binding. Alternatively, if you seek out the Soulbinder in each major city, say 'bind my soul' to the soulbinder and you'll be bound to that spot.

Welcome back to Norrath and to the newbie zone.

(1) I really don't think server selection is a big deal. I certainly wouldn't change servers if you are reasonably happy where you are and have no real life friends who play on different servers.

(2) Very few people group much under 20 anymore. Some do. On my server the best places to find low level groups would be in the Greater Faydark/Crushbone area, and the East Commons for under 15. After 15 it is mostly Paludal.

(3) Leveling speed is largely a matter of game knowledge. Since you don't know all the tricks and secrets, it will take you longer than others. My first life took a lot of hours (lol days/weeks) to get to 10. Now I can get to 10 in a couple of hours if I don't hunt for my newbie gear.

(4) Some classes (basically all casters but no hybrids or melee) get the spell "Bind". Both of your characters are hybrids so don't get the spell. You can do an /ooc for a bind (and you can only be bound in cities or a few special zones). All starting cities and a few other locations also have Soul Binder NPCs you can hail to be bound. You might consider binding in Plane of Knowledge since it is only a zone or two away of wherever you may be fighting.

(5) Many items do show on frogloks, however, looted cloth type armors show as "naked". Some pieces (back, shoulder, rings among others) don't show at all for anyone.

(6) If you are enjoying your characters, keep with them. If not, don't delete, just make another until you find one that clicks.

Stromm being used doesn't mean they'll make another newbie server.

Other servers would have to be overcrowded, including Stromm. Part of the problem is that an overcrowded server doesn't suddenly become not crowded because they make a new server, unless they let that overcrowded one split. Often with newbie servers people leave for that first 3 weeks or so of "newbness" on the new server, then return home.

Yeah, my low level fast leveling secrets are:

1) forget the newb quest stuff, it is junk. The stats on it mean nothing and the quest drops are horrible. Even on stromm I ignored all but my cleric's weapon and instead bought banded by level 5, when the server was brand new.

Everyone else sold bone chips, snake eggs and beetle eyes to NPC vendors. I then used that to level fast and make lots of money on a few quests.
It took until level 14 until I bought out the last fire beetle eye on an NPC innkeeper in east freeport. Yeah, there are places to buy unlimited ones, but they are not as close to the quest. The xp return on the quest gets small around 14 anyway, so it was time to quit.

If it is an established server with a good economy, you can buy stuff better than the newbie armor with less effort than it takes to quest the newbie armor, even starting at level one and not knowing a sould on the server.

2) quests, like bone chips/beetle eyes in the east freeport sewer, are the way to go. I got to level 14 pretty fast.

3) generally see what other people are doing, like Paludral, then do something else which requires more imagination and is less crowded. Fighting crowds is frustrating. The FIRST people to Paludral leveled fast, the rest struggled and yelled about KSING, etc.
