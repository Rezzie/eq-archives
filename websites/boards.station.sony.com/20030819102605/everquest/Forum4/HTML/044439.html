This does not happen. What is very different on Stormhammer is the tier system. I can try and explain quickly.

Guilds progress through tiers starting at Tier 1 and going to Tier 8. Tier flagging for most tiers occurs at a guild event when a GM spawns a mob for a guild to kill. If the guild kills said mob in the arena they progress up the tier system. Different mobs are reserved from Wed. to Sat. and assigned to specific guilds.

For example a Klandicar is a tier 3 mob. He is reserved this week for the guild Legacy of Ages. Legacy of Ages must kill this mob within the reservation period (WED to Sat) to remain tier 3.

So yes GM's do spawn mobs, but only for tier trials, and they contain no loot. Check out
http://legends.everquest.station.sony.com/eventview.jsp?o=57055
for more information. How I didnt make it more blurry....

Things about legends:

1) The "legends" zones...legends USED to have afew zones exclusive to legends only, they don't exist anymore. What legends does have is they have been getting new zones before any other servers (the revamped hate went live on legends first, then after that the new underwater dungeon). Clicking to download them does nothing, even if you do download zone files specifically for legends you wont be able to use them, just as if you had the zone files for kunark but didn't have the expansion installed, though you have the files, you cant access the zone.


2) No, GMs DO NOT RESPAWN NPCs under regular circumstances. The way legends works is that many NPCs are on a tier system. Tier days are wednesday through saturday. If your guild has a specific NPC reserved, no other guild may touch it during those days. The "tiers" relate to the difficulty. In two instances (sleepers tomb and vex thal) the entire ZONE is reserved, on the theory that the entire zone is a raid area (other instances only the NPC is reserved...you can have tunare reserved but that doesn't stop a guild from hunting in PoG, however sleepers tomb and vex thal are reserved in full).

The only time a GM MAY respawn an NPC is if a guild killed an NPC that they did not have it reserved for. In that instance the GM may "make it right" by respawning that NPC for the guild that DID have it reserved.
