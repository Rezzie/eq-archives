A big part of this issue is the server you pick to play on. Think about it for a moment, on the "new" servers, there are ZERO uber-guilds, or they are just as low a level as the majority of the players early on. On The Nameless, sure, there are uberguilds, but they don't "own" the game. And many of the people who make it into the 50+ range get the chance to raid the planes, no matter what guild they are in, or not.

A big part of it is who you know, your own ability to work in a group and with others, and your skill. Want to go on a raid, just group with people in Karnor's, or in OS. Go places that the other non-uber guilds use for daily hunting when not on a guild event. Guess what, you end up with friends. Over time, if they need you, or have a use for you, you may get an invite to help kill Vox or Naggy. You show up, and if you did your part well, and with skill, and don't complain about the almost guaranteed death you will find, you will get invited again. Over time, you may find yourself being invited to more raids.

Obviously, my experience has been colored by playing on Nameless, where there are pick-up raids for just about anything. My own guild isn't uber just yet, but we are getting up there in power and skill. This allows us to hold guild-only raids, but in many cases, we WILL invite friends from other guilds when we do stuff. That's just how it is. How many people who are NOT in Uberguilds have discussed possibly gathering together to do a dragon raid, or to go into Fear or Hate? In many cases, you can probably even get it schedualed. Ask someone from an Uberguild how they schedual it with other Uberguilds to see who's going to the planes, they will probably tell you.

That's all I can suggest. A LARGE part of what makes Uberguilds work so well is they don't wait for someone else to do the work, they show leadership, and they don't complain because something has never been done before. If there's a closed door that only a select few go through, then stick your foot in that door before it closes. You want to raid a plane, then get your guild, and your friends together, and DO it. Stop waiting for others. And if you die 20 times while learning how to break into Fear or Hate, well, that's what EVERYONE goes through when learning how to run a raid.

Don't wait for others, do it yourself, and you will be able to handle all but the most difficult of mobs. As long as you avoid certain Uber mobs while you are forming and organizing your friends and others from their various guilds, you will be fine. Just a few words of advice:

Naggy is generally a piece of cake with a powerful enough force. Vox is more of a pain. If you plan to try Fear, make sure you get on the calender, and camp THE NIGHT BEFORE, don't try to break in. Also, don't do it without guidance if the God is up. It's hard enough without the god being up when learning.

It's clear from your post that you are a LOT more rational than many who complain on the issue, so you should have no problem gathering 24 or more people from various guilds to help you. Try using diplomacy when dealing with the uber crowd, it goes a LONG way.
