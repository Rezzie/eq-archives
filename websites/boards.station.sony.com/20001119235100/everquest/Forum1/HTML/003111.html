One comment on the "too much ram" post.
With older motherboards you could install more ram than could be cached by the onboard L2 cache. This would actually cause the computer to be slower than before the ram install.
If your board has 512k of onboard (or on-die if you have a coppermine, duron, or athlon thunderboard) cache then 256meg is not too much.
I dont know the exact numbers, but www.tomshardware.com had a good article about it a while back.

Now about the Win ME thing
I have found that some programs that would work on windows 98 will cause no end of problems with windows ME.
One good example is Symantec's Norton Antivirus 2000. It causes frequent slowdowns and lockups. I went to their web page (www.symantec.com) to find a fix and their article said that the entire symantec 2000 series was not supported for windows ME and never would be
They said buy the 2001 series. (Norton antivirus, Norton Utilities, Norton System works, etc.)
When I called them they said "read the box. Clearly states for windows 95 and 98. We dont have to support new operating systems for that product"
Anyway, my point is, make sure all the software you run is ME compatable or you will be in for alot of headaches.
