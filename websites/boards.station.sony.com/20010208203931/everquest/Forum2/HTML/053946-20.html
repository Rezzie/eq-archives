This is what happens when an element of a subculture turns mainstream. RPGs were invented by geeks, meaning people with nothing better to do than bring their parents furniture into the basement and play a game with people who were just as pathetic as they were. These were people who would give anything to escape not just from their lives, but from themselves.

Everquest lives in a moment of transition. It was supposed to be a Role Playing Game, that was the intent, no matter how ravaged it has become, but it had pretty graphics and existed on a platform appealing to and within reach of a large number of people. You have an ideal, where people jointly work to become someone else, and you have a practice, where a simple bored housewife sees a chance to take her vicarious bored flirting to the next level, at approximately the time the symbol " " just becomes too lackluster for her tastes. That is EQ.

To me, "Managirl" is no different than "SexyMistress119", no different than "28/f/Denver", no different than the most preposterous and undignified means of self-expression currently available. Not because of the superficial similarities, but because of the similar purpose.

You could argue the basic concept of "escapism" is still mostly intact, and that Everquest has "evolved" into a multifaceted platform of escape and how there should exist no prejudices and biases between the individual factions, and you'd be wrong. So there.


Now attack my opinion, dammit.
