The L61 pet is a beast, though - L58 base, hits for 60, enrages, shields - the only downside is he only has 2000 HPs, but again he's not meant to be a tank. There's a reason that Shamans get chain armour, a defense cap of 200 and 200 skill caps in weapons, and that's because we're meant to be tanks, not the pet - at least, that's the way I see it. Yes, it means Jabober won't be tanking golems in PoV any time soon, not unless he has Quiscence on him anyway - but if you've maxed Defense and got a decent AC you'll do a better job of mitigating melee damage anyway.

In short, pets are fine - the L61 pet is stunning in comparison to the others.

quote:

Also consider our slow damage output and resists on our disease/poison based kind of spells? You have to admit we got the slowest damage output than any other class out there right?

Yup, shamans are known for having low damage output. But mobs that are highly resistant to poison/disease (eg undead) are usually susceptible to our cold nukes (which hit the 1100s with an improved damage item at L64). Then there's the curse based DoTs from LDoN, which check against MR. I'd say our damage is fine really - you want big damage, get a wizard or rogue!

quote:

4. Alchemy. Well today I see alchemy personally as a big black hole. If you want to get rid of your money why go to "casinos" in bazaar? You could as well start Alchemy.

Agreed that alchemy is a huge money sink. But it's the way that Shamans can do almost anything - porting, damage shields, IvU and fixed duration invis (even if they do require a foraged component, gurr). There are even Breeze and Intellectual Superiority type potions, although the cost on those is extortionate.

5% mana reduction focus potions are also out there, not to mention illusions, as well as shaman buffs in a bottle, regen, SoW, levitation, disease and poison cures, STA zingers - alchemy is an incredibly useful tradeskill, but the cost is wallet draining.

Overall, though, I think shamans are pretty balanced - there are always those who want more, but as it stands we're a very powerful and versatile class.
