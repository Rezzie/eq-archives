Thread Subject: Balancing Bards

Funny: (Skip) If their heads werent so big they would be balanced just fine.

Issues: Group usefulness

Definition: If you are going to balance a class you should do so based on their effectivness in a group setting. Soloing, while viable could not be balanced between all classes. Each class should bring some form of intrinsic value to a group setting and that value should be equal to that of any other class.

Example: Cleric/Paladin, both classes have value in a group. A Cleric can out heal, out reserect and out buff a Paladin in a heart beat. A Paladin can out tank, and out damage a cleric in a heart beat. A cleric in a group with a paladin negates the need for the Paladin to buff the group. It does not, however negate the need for the paladin to heal or potentially reserect. Essentially healing is something that can be dont universily without any caps except the number of HPS and the amount of mana expended.

Example: Paladin/Warrior, both clases have value in a group. A warrior can out tank and out damage/taunt a paladin in a heart beat. A paladin can out buff, heal and reserect a warrior in a heart beat. Both classes can utilize their tanking capacity and melee damage out put, with the Paladin being slightly less useful but effective none the less.

I gave the above example to define what I understood balance to be. I believe it is the users understanding that a Bard should be a group based class. A class that offers allot of versatility to a class and enhances the groups over all effectiveness. Ill hit on the issue brought up in these posts:

Haste: Haste has a game cap. Bard spells do not stack with other haste casters. Haste is not like HP, mana or melee damage in that once the cap has been reached it does not degrade during combat but does so based on a given time limit.

Suggestion: To my fellow bards, I suggest we drop this one except for at lower levels where caps are often not met(unless twinks are involved...). We would be more effective if our spells effected certain abilities such as attack and AC. This would improve the melee out put of all our group members and I dont think there are any caps to them and no specific class is focused on these two attributes.

Damage Output (Spells): Our songs do not compare to the damage out put of a rangers, Paladins or SKs (Hybrid-Warrior/Casters). Even with the issue of mana consumption from these other classes, we can not come close to the speed and effectivness they have in this area.

Suggestion: When compared to the lowest spell damage dealing classes (Hybrids) we are far out of range. We do very little damage although we can eventually wear down something by kiting them with our dots. How does this help a group? It doesnt. Should we get some sort of enhancement to our spell damage? Even a 100pt manaless dd would be a significant enhancement compared to our 20pt'er. It would still not compare to a Rangers/SK's DDs, but being manaless it would have be balanced in effect.


Healing Output: Our songs are not as effective as the Paladin and Ranger classes. Our songs are more based on significant down time, and the issue with this is that the puller is often the person hurt the most in battle. So in oder to have a regen effect, we need to follow the puller out. Which is obviously unacceptable.

Suggestion: An SK can heal its self better than we could, and a Paladin can heal the group quicker than we can. The whole concept behind mid to low level groups is fast up time. Nobody has time for a slow acting limited area effect healing song. Regen is ok, but it stays on you especially if you are pulling. Healing is often twisted in especially if two bards are in a group (rare at low to mid levels, and not very effective) and has a limited effect in any given battle. The healing song also has a good effect if there are many tanks tanking damage, in my experience, low to mid levels usually have one main tank and rarely a secondary. The puller is usually beat up the most with the secondary getting healed the instant an INC is messaged. So if healing is something Bards should be doing at later levels perhaps this spell should be looked at in being effective in a group setting.

Damage Output (Melee): There are allot of sites that have done the math on melee out put based on double attack and dual wield. My own experience is that unless twinked, at levels 20 + Bard melee power is less than any of the hybrid classes.

Suggestion: Have Plate shall tank is not something that applies to Bards or high level clerics. So tanking is something we arent suppose to do... On to the subject at hand... Our melee out put is limited by a few factors, instrument requirements being one and the fact that the chance to get a double hit is very low until higher levels are reached. The damage out put even if a dual wack is made, is very low compared to the higher chance of a double attack kicking in on a high damage high delay weapon. If we Bards are not meant to be a melee class, perhaps someone should really look at why we are allowed to have armor and weapons? To fix this issue to meet what I would like, I would give Bards a low double attack skill (100).

Bard Role: We do not match any class or come close to any class in their various abilities. We have a wide range of songs, which are essentially the same as a 20+ level lower character. I dont know if anyone in the planes every wants the assistance of a 24th level class, but I would guess they wouldnt. Our charm songs are exceptionally powerful, in an out door invironment, and given that the casters dont mind missing out on the mana song. Our charm songs are just as powerful as the enchanters/druids/necros/shaman spells, with the limitation of duration. Yet all of the other things that you look for in group effectivness: Hasting/Healing/Damage Dealing/Tanking we are exceptionally weak in. I left one thing out, buffing and I did so on purpose. In the planes and in very unique instances the resist songs play a significant role from what I am hearing from high level players. At the low level and mid levels up to 35, they do not play any significant role in my Bards career. So even our buffs do not compare to those given out by other classes at least up to 35.

Twisting, auto-twisting, macros, etc...

Couple of observations here:

First off, it is patently obvious that bard twisting was an early oversight, exploited early early in beta, then determined to be a "feature" and taken into consideration in subsequent changes, to a certain degree. What this means, is that the basic framework of the game, i.e. interface, client software, etc., was not designed to support it. Songs appear to use the same routines as spells, with certain zero'd costs, like mana, and incredibly short effect lengths. In other words, the mechanics of the game itself were designed to allow spell routines to be used to simulate a song, but the overlap required to ensure that the song would stay in effect allowed "twisting".

Now... to time travel back, once the decision was made that bards could have the effects of more than one song active on their party through "twisting", further decisions about song effects and such were colored by the fact that bards now had 2 or more songs active, when they were originally conceived to have only one.

This means that all "balancing" from that point on, assumed that every bard would have multiple songs going all the time... They couldn't assume less, because you have to "balance" from the MOST effective (numerically) way to play a character, because someone is going to manage it.

I point out these patently obvious facts to explain why twisting is absolutely necessary, and no longer optional, at least not if you wish to play your class at the degree of effectiveness which the game is designed for.

So... if twisting is a requirement.. then let's look at the mechanics of it.

The pride some people seem to have for their twisting "ability" really does crack me up. I mean.... seriously... that's like bragging to the world that you can handle the fourth turn on a Simon game... ("...and then I pressed red, red, blue, yellow! It was soooooo hard!)

Anyone can "twist". It's not an amazing skill. Learning the songs that don't stack with one another is more difficult... but not much more.

Twisting is not "hard", it's tedious... it's dull... and it prevents things like... oh... talking. Ever try to explain to someone who is insistently "telling" you during the middle of an iffy "kite" combat? I use macros with prepared speeches about what I'm doing. Unfortunately reply doesn't macro, it seems.

Using macros to twist is fine... but has flexibility problems. I have a couple but rarely use them, since I constantly juggle different songs depending on what is needed. To illustrate, I commonly use a horn and play chain (initially for the slow component), discord, and anthem... unless casters are having trouble, then I'll switch up anthem with Fulfils to get a little MR reduction... or if no one is hitting it, Pixie Strike. Sometimes I'll even switch to a lute and play heal, chains, and anthem, to give a few extra hits to a faltering group. This doesn't even look at how I need to change up for unexpected adds to a combat... Or how I will learn different songs for different locations... or how I mentally rank every song in every combination and will retry a failed one if I consider it more crucial, dropping out a round of one of the others...

All in all, macroing every combination is way too troublesome for my style of play... I can just see getting lost trying to recall whether the combo I need is in song-macro-hotkeypad 5 or 6.

On the other hand, I wouldn't mind having the ability to talk to people during combat... or even, *gasp*, while levitate/running with a party.

All that said... I don't think it's possible for an auto-twist to be implemented into the game. Besides changes to the client interface to handle such a thing, changes to the entire spell-casting system which might cause new glitches for other classes, there are the difficulties of recreating the problems we currently face (dropped songs on restarts, sequential resists as we cycle through, etc etc).

In the fantastic world of wish-fullfilment, I'd personally love to see a new interface for bards-only, where each "song" was considered a verse... the bard would chose "verses" to form a song, and start singing/playing. The higher the level, the more verses you could have in your "song".

As it stands... I'm not sure the issue can even really be addressed, and maybe we shouldn't cloud the "balance" issues with it?

So, there will almost always be some damage ability lost to a defensive ability... something that no other class really has to deal with. ("Gee, I can't cast a damage shield on myself, because I want to use 3 offensive spells during the next combat.")

Balance: The issue of Odd Songs

Something just struck me, and I wanted to share it. Part of the attraction to the bard class for most of us, was the sense of being "different" than everyone else. We like the fact that a lot of people have no clue as to what we can or cannot do... especially during the first half of our careers.

This "strange" aspect to barding is aided early on, by some of the visibly odd effects of our songs: random teleports, water breathing, invisibility (but still glowing blue balls of light), and others. Later on, however, it seems we start getting a constant stream of nearly identical songs to previous songs... with little or no silliness thrown in.

Perhaps it would be good to add a song or two later on, that doesn't really afford a huge advantage to the class, but offers the opportunity for downtime fun... and just plain showing off.

The Tavern Teleport idea is one such... although, if that seems like it is stepping on some other classes' toes, how about making it random? Imagine a Pub Hopping song... where the bard and his group play the song, and appear in a random inn or tavern around the world... This way it wouldn't compete with the greed-meisters who fiercely defend their class rights to teleports so they can charge travel fees... but it would be semi-useful, and just plain FUN!

If random can't work, then how about a standard route through a series of bars, so that bard groups would have to hop along from stop to stop?

If a teleport is out of the question... then how about an AE memory erase song, which causes the creatures affected to spin around in circles? It could be the Dance of Madness, or something...

Players can use shrink or enlarge spells, why not an AE shrink or enlarge song... it doesn't even have to have any significant statistical effect to be fun. Although, if it doesn't have an effect, it would be most amusing if it was not considered an aggressive song. (Imagine giant guards after a bard passes through town... )

-----------------------

One last thing... consider putting a really useful different song into the post-50 game. Right now the general clamor is for more melee at those levels since nothing else seems to be of value. If adding more melee is anti-Vision, then how about something totally NEW.

Something like a song of Hate, single-target, that makes the creature inimical to every other creature that can see it, as long as the song is sung. This sort of thing could even function as a rough crowd control, used to occupy a camp so that creatures can be pulled by the rest of the party.

------------

These are just some rough ideas... but I'm thinking that there might be some merit in investigating the possibility of handling some of the balance issues through a few new songs, in a creative way...

I believe that the key question facing Absor and Verant in reference to bard balance is this:

Does Verant want Bards to fulfill the jack-of-all-trades role?
It's good for Roleplaying (OMG! Did I just say roleplaying in an Everquest context!?) and true to the classic concept of Bards in most fantasy roleplaying realms.

Or would Verant like to keep the game fair, for all customers and character classes and balance the Bard class to the others?

It would be good customer service to balance Bards, but the jack-of-all-trades concept that is the current role of Bards implicits denies the ability to balance them with pure and other hybrid classes.
Because Bards are basically hybrids of enc/dru/war/rog, there is little that Verant can do to make a fair balancing act.

The only way that I see Bards being balanced in a manner that would be acceptable to Verant, existing Bard players, and the rest of the Everquest playerbase would be to excise the classic concept of Bards being jacks-of-all-trades and give them a role that is unavailable or unfeasible for other classes to fill.

As it stands, logically, Bards are just fine the way they are. They are on a more powerful platform at low levels because of their diversity of skills/roles. At higher levels, the specialists (read: pure classes) are going to leave Bards far behind in their respective roles.

As it stands, Bards are written for a RPG. Everquest is, unfortunately, not a roleplaying-intense game, therefore the logic keeping Bards as the jack-of-all-trades role is illogical.

It is my humble opinion that Bards ought to be changed to have their niche, their specialty, their _thing_ that nobody else can touch. What that should be, mana drain monsters, faction masters, etc. is beyond my scope to envision.
