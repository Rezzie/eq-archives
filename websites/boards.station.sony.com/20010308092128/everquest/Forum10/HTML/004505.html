Whooboy...there are a lot of choices for magician...check out the creation guides at eq.castersrealm.com. Erudites have the highest int but they're nightblind, gnomes look cool with an elemental bigger than themselves following them around and they can tinker, dark elves have the best night vision and can sneak and hide but have bad faction, iksar have regen and natural ac and can forage but have the worst faction in the game...ummm...high elves worshipping Tunare get some rocking but incredibly expensive chain armor (you'll probably have to make it yourself, unless there's a fantastically high-skilled high elf smith already on your server) that helps them look a little less dorky...hmmm...humans are night-blind and...ummm...dang, I'm sure there's a reason to play a human, I just can't think of one since I'm so used to being one in RL.

Magicians are an interesting class to play. They get a custom version of common buffs (infravision, levitate, etc.) in the form of either a summoned item that provides the effect constantly, or a summoned item that has multiple charges of the spell. They have great pets to do extra damage and keep the mob off them while they cast, but I saw Thornweave explained the pets in another thread. They have a handy damage shield, which does very mana-efficient damage (a set amount of irresistible damage each time the damage-shielded player or pet gets hit...it really adds up fast), and is very useful both solo and in groups. They have good nukes, both magic- and fire-based (including AoE's of both types).

They have very little control over monsters, except in the sense that quickly killing the enemy with sheer brute force is control. They cannot even root an enemy without earth pet. However, they are good soloers (just under necro and druid) and group-friendly (nukes, damage shield, utility summons like bandages, and a tank, all in one tidy little package).

Two caveats: pets do not do well in dungeons (their pathing is controlled by the server, and can be erratic in cramped spaces), so along with necros, druids, and rangers, they could be considered an "outdoor" class. Also, they rely heavily on no-rent summons (as Thornweave pointed out). Really, unless you go LD regularly it's not a problem. However, anyone with an iffy connection (or who logs on for numerous short sessions instead of playing for several hours straight) should probably skip the magician class due to the serious pain in the arse of resummoning everything.

Mages are an interesting class...their power comes from summoning magic items and pets, and if played right a magician can be a very mana efficient character. Spend a few minutes at the start of your session summoning up weapons and "loaded" items like waterstones and rings of levitation, and then use these spell effects later on with no mana usage...it's very much a "mana up front" kind of class. Pay now, use later. That gives you more mana to use for offense during battle.

Race selection depends on (1) where you want to start your career, (2) whether you want to roleplay, (3) how bothered you are by vision problems.

Starting a Gnome or High Elf magician will start you off on Faydwer, which is a nice little continent for your first several levels (up to about 20, or higher if you play the dungeons there).

Starting off a Human or Dark Elf will put you in Antonica, the largest and most varied continent.

Erudites start on Odus, which is apparantly a "continent in progress." It's a nice continent for your first 4 or so levels; you can get up to level 7 without too much trouble. Getting up to level 8 is a chore, since the monsters in Tox have a bit of a level gap here. You can return for levels 12-18 fighting the tiny floating things if you can find them.

For roleplay reasons, you can choose to be evil (dark elves), or aloof (high elves, erudites). The other races are pretty much neutral.

Night vision should become less of a concern with the level 4 Summon Wisp spell, and starting with level 16, night vision is a non consideration. You will have spells or summoned items to give you the best vision in the game.

Deity is a personal roleplay consideration for all classes in the game. If you enjoy roleplaying, you might want to tie your primary pet with your religion. Followers of Brell might go with earth pets; water for Prexus; fire for Solusek Ro. Choice of religion will somewhat alter your faction standings and quest potentials, but you can almost always overcome these limitations by seeking out the right monsters to kill.

My advice: pick a race and deity that you have not explored with other characters. There is a huge world out there to explore, and the best way to find out its secrets is to look at everything from a fresh perspective.
