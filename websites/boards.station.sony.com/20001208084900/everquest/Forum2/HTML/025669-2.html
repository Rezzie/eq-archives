Bah, in the immortal words of GrollerDaBard:

"It's over. Let it go." ;-)

About the Draconian reference, it is indeed referring to an ancient Greek lawmaker in Athens named Draco. His laws were ruthless, and punishments severe. If any of you are familiar with the Code of Hammurabi, the Draconian Code is just as cruel, if not more so. I can't give examples from the Draconian Code, as I don't remember, but as an example from the similar Code of Hammurabi: If you are a builder, and a building you have built falls and kills a man, you shall be put to death. If it kills the man's son, so shall your son be put to death. If you are caught stealing, your hands will be cut off.

Thus, when you hear someone mention Draconian policies, it means rules that are overly cruel and unbalanced. As you can clearly see, calling the naming policy "Draconian" is pretty extreme, and not appropriate. You break the rules, you pay the penalties. It's about time people learned that actions have consequences.
