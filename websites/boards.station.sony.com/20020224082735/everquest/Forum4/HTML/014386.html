1) Instill Doubt takes a LONG time to climb. It is a good use of your training points to give it some help (but not past a skill of 45 or so).

You get into melee range, target the creature, and press your hotkey. You will cycle through your moves; at then end it does a skill check - if successful, you launch a kick (using your Instill Doubt skill - not your Kick skill). The skill check automatically fails if your special attack is greyed out, so only use it when your special attack is available.

2) 2-Handed Blunt is VERY important for secondary tank classes, because hate generation is heavily influenced by quantity of attacks (and therefore speed). Using a slower, high damage 2-handed weapon will help you to dish steady damage without drawing aggro - assuming your group's primary tank Dueal wields a pair of fast 1-handers.

3) My Human became a wimp at level 36, but he had no weapon skills and lacked the Iksar advantages. Once I developed his weapon skills, his single Fighting Baton carried him to about level 45.

4) Mend skill hasn't failed me or backfired since I reached skill 101+.

5) Monks can bandage to 70% health upon attaining a Bind Wounds skill of 201 - which can be reached at level 51 at the earliest (the pre-51 skill cap is 200). Note that the new alternate advancement system has a skill which will let you bind higher (up to 100%), if you take it.

There is a synchronization problem with the Mend skill which causes that message you receive (I've seen it off by more than 2 million seconds before). Verant has been working on the problem for at least 10 months, but they have not been able to fix it. They did make changes to the recycle timer, changing it from a constant 6 minutes to a variable time between 6 and 6.5 minutes, to decrease the chance of the problem occurring - but it still happens.

My only advice is not to use it as soon as it resets unless you really need to.
