When in a pick up group, is it ever appropriate to advertise your availability to other groups that might possibly be earning more xp/hr than you?

Last night I was in a pickup group in Paludal Caverns. Working on PL'ing an alt cleric. Teamed up with a buddy who's doing the same, only a monk. We had a low 30's shaman babysitting us and making sure we don't get in too much trouble.

We added two new players to the group who had done an /OOC LFG and after about 45 minutes (during which I earned lvls 14 and 15) I noticed that one of them was sending /OOC messages letting people know that he was available.

Now, mind you, during this 45 minutes I never once saw him swing a weapon or cast a buff, spell, nuke or anything other than run around, chase the puller out of camp, etc. and chat on and on about how he bought his drogmar at lvl 1, blah, blah, blah.

I asked if he was dissatisfied with our group and he told me that we were levelling too slow for his taste and that he could go from lvl 1-20 in an hour.

I said "go do it, then" and dropped him from the group. He then proceeded to call me some vulgar names before running off to try to find another group to leech from.

After I saw his next /OOC LFG message, I did an "/ooc beware of {name} being in your group, he does nothing but run in circles, bad mouth the others and complain about how he knows how to earn xp so much faster. Oh, and ask him about his drogmar he bought at lvl 1". Now, I freely admit that my actions were less than perfect, but boy did it feel good...

That's it, I guess. Real question was at the top...
