The MS Strategic Commander IS NOT a joystick. It cannot even be programmed to work as one. Basically, the MS-SC is a keyboard "emulator" (so to speak). It's pretty slick, but it takes a LOT of getting used to.

I have one (along with the MS Game Voice). To use the "puck" to move your character around, you have to program the movement keys into the MS-SC, so moving the "puck" around sends the appropriate keyboard command to the system. For example, you'd program the forward movement to the "W" key, and the forward+left and forward+right movements to "W+A" and "W+D" keystrokes (along with the walk backwards keys). Once it's programmed, it's a REALLY slick piece of hardware. The trick is that you have to program it correctly for EQ.

If your having problems pressing the -SHIFT- key on your keyboard, you may have some trouble with the MS-SC as well. The six buttons on the top of the unit have three corresponding shift buttons on the right side of the controller (that you use with your thumb).

I personally like the MS GameVoice for EQ more than the MS Strategic Commander. It's a little less money too. Either one will work for you, but if your having trouble with the keyboard (pressing keys), I'd look into the GameVoice. It doesn't need fancy voice recognition software installed to work, and it works with practically every program I tried it on. Again, the only limiting factor is that you have to program the MS GameVoice to act upon whatever commands you want (almost like the Strategic Commander, except you assign the commands to voice commands instead).
