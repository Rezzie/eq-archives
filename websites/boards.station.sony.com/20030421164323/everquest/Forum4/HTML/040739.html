quote:
Since I have you here, is there a way to ever figure out which creatures have the big xp (ie, a specific number) or do you just have to eyeball the given xp via the leveling bar (or whatever it is called)?

You can determine in a very general sense how much xp you'll get from a creature by 'considering' it. Target a creature and type /con or hit the "C" key. There will be a blurb of text describing your chances of killing the creature. The colour of that text is important and is based on a comparison between your level and the level of the creature. In order:
Green. The creature is much lower level than you. You will not gain any xp for killing it.
Light blue. The creature is significantly lower level than you. The xp you receive for killing the creature is significantly reduced.
Dark blue. The creature is lower level than yourself, but is relatively close to your level. Generally speaking, these are the creatures you should concentrate on fighting, since you'll have a chance of succeeding and will get full xp for the kill.
White. The creature is the same level as your character. Full xp is awarded for a kill.
Yellow. The creature is one or two levels above you. Full xp is awarded for a kill.
Red. The creature is 3 or more levels higher than you. If (somehow) you manage to kill it, you will get full xp. There is, however, an xp 'cap' per kill so you might not get the full xp for killing the creature (but you'll still get lots).

The amount of xp you get for killing a creature is entirely dependant on 2 factors: 1. The level of the creature and 2. The zone you are in. Dungeons tend to have better xp multipliers than outside zones, and newer expansions zones tend to be better xp than older world zones.

Welcome!

I'm a returning player...took a year off and coming back after flirting with DAoC, AO, Neverwinter Nights and even Ultima Online....EQ blows them all away and leaves them in the dust.

Luclin, by the way, is pretty important:

1. It provides you with the Bazaar. Great way to purchase and sell items without needing to haggle.

2. It provides you with the ability to purchase a horse. This is really only important for higher level casters, as the horses provide some advantages in combat regarding medding and aggro from monsters. But they are usable for faster transport and as a status symbol by all classes. Note that LoY allows you to buy a leezard (drogmor) to ride.

3. Many Luclin zones have a very good XP bonus/flow. So much that folk who are concentrating on levelling before anything else pretty much spend most of their playtime on the moon.

My advice to you, above all things, is to just sit back and enjoy the ride. Don't feel like you need to rush through levels like a ratrace. Rather, relax a bit and explore. Work a tradeskill or 2 every so often. Jump at the chance to crawl in a dungeon. Get drunk in game and build up your character's alcohol tolerance to insane levels.

By the way, what class are you playing now? There are many fine sites/resources available for advice for specific classes.
