People always say "go out, explore." Exploring gets you dead with a 4 hour corpse recovery.

Last time I tried to explore I died near King Room and it took me 3 hours and 4 deaths just to get my corpse.

No thanks.

Give me one good reason to go to a zone like Kedge or Paw or the Hole.

I don't care if the xp is better. I don't care if the loot is better.

The one overriding concern is convenience. If there's a good chance that I might have to run more than 2 zones to get my corpse, I'm not going, it's just not worth it.

The chances of me going to some out of the way zone or dungeon, and getting a group, and coming home 3 hours later with something interesting in my bag is just about nil.

The chances of me going to some out of the way zone or dungeon, not finding a group or dying and then spending 4 hours trying to get my corpse back are excellent. I've done it many times, it's not a surprise anymore. Every single time I've decided to explore, I've regretted it because all my time is eaten up just by getting my corpse.

The way that things currently are would make good sense *IF* the majority of the population were RL friends and always travelled and hunted together. Then it would all make good sense. The wizzy would port everyone around, the cleric would rez everyone, everyone would do their job and everybody would have fun.

But that's not the EQ reality for most of us. Most of the people I meet are through pickup groups. The wizzy is there for as long as he feels like playing. If I die and he feels like logging, I'm SOL. This is not my friend, this is not my buddy who's going to lift a finger to help me.

Ports cost money, rezzes cost money, plat is finite, and there is not always someone around willing to do it. I have spent up to about an hour just looking for a bind in FREEPORT. Even when you yell out that you'll pay for it, it's not like anybody's starving for cash. Why should they stop and bind someone when they could be out making exp?

The basic premise behind the game design relies upon people cooperating and forming tight bonds of friendship, but that's not the human race as I know it.

Yeah it would make sense that people would get together and help each other out, but it doesn't happen in real life and it's not going to happen in an unforgiving video game.

You think if I need a port somewhere after grouping with a bunch of people that the group is gonna stop what it's doing just so the wizzy can port me?

Fat chance. They're there to get experience and loot. The wizzy's not my friend, he's not my "companion." This is not a union of friendship, it's a business relationship. We all do what we can so we can all make the best exp and loot we can. That's it.

I've had groups in guk where half the group died, the cleric and wizzy ported and then the cleric got called for the Frenzy group. She's not gonna leave Frenzy group just to rez us. And when I finally make that 4 zone run from Freeport, I'm left dragging my own corpse and paying for my own rez.

Class dependency assumes that people are going to cooperate and take care of each other. But that's not the game we're playing. The cleric is taking care of one person, herself. The druid is taking care of one person, himself.

And that's very understandable. The game is unforgiving. When you're trying to make up all that xp you lost yesterday, you're not really apt to think about wasting 2 hours to help out some tank that died 3 times trying to get his corpse.

And that's why the zones that are packed will stay packed, and the zones that are empty will stay empty. Because there is no incentive to go there. I don't care what drops there.

I don't care how much xp the mobs give. If it's going to mean a 4 hour recovery, people just aren't going to go.

But it won't change until there's a playable MMORPG on the market. They have no reason to change EQ, there is no competition, nowhere else for people to play.

The only time VI will ever consider changing something as fundamental as class "interdependency" (I'm trying to say that without cringing) and travel is when people start leaving.

And frankly that's not going to happen until a polished product comes out from a competitor.

Salian.

There's a line. There's a line between making a game 'video-gamey' where distance is mostly meaningless, and what we have now, which is extreme frustration.

It was a laudable idea, for the game designers to make the world 'vast'. But it's ultimately not working. All it does is make the world boring, and dull.

Changing locations in EQ is hard, especially as a tank-class. Very hard. You have to hoof it through zone after zone, dodging mobs, to get to a bind site, then sit around waiting for someone playing an INT/WIS caster to actually decide to take thirty seconds from their life to give you the ability to go get killed safely.

Player teleports ... I wouldn't mind this system as much if I could, during primetime hours, offer a reasonable sum of cash (20-30pp should be enough at any given moment, honestly) and get a port within a few minutes. I would be okay with that.

Player teleports in reality, in the game we all play, are much, much longer than that. It can take hours, if at all, to find a Wiz/Dru willing to take a few minutes to teleport. The comment about twinks was dead on; who's going to bother to port people around when the Dru/Wiz they're playing is their twink? No way, they don't need the cash being offered if they have a 50+ whatever that can collect money easily.

There should be enough bind points so there's *never* more than a two full zone run to a corpse (i.e., die and go back to bind point. Run to bindpoint zoneline and zone, run through the next zone and zone, and you should be in-zone to your corpse).

There should be a teleport system between *all* the cities in Norrath. Period. You should be able to buy a 'port key' from a vendor for 20-30pp and click it on a pad object to teleport to the city corresponding to the port key you're holding.

There shouldn't be any zones that are more than three zones away from a teleport location (i.e., teleport in and zone out of that zone, cross a second zone, and the third zone is your destination).

It might be 'realistic' to make people sit at their computer 'travelling' for an hour or two, but it's sure as heck not very fun. I play games to be stimulated and for enjoyment. Hitting autorun and navigating between mob agro radii isn't my idea of fun though. Guess I'm a spoilsport.
