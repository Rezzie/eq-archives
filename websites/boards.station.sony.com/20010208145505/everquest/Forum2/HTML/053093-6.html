I had planned on saving this for the ranger thread, but I guess it does bear posting here:

The issue of taunt being ineffective is, more often than not, the fault of dumb players. It is entirely possible to keep a mob off of a monk, rogue, wizard, etc as long as they are doing their best to avoid aggro. The times when I go nuts trying and failing to taunt are when the rogue doesn't evade, or the wizard empties all his mana chain nuking right off the bat, or other such foolish actions. If people played their classes properly, this wouldn't be an issue, and those too dumb to avoid over-aggroing probably deserve the deaths they get.

The one exception to this is rangers. Rangers are simply a suicidal class. They are the fastest hitting and highest damage dealing hybrid, which means they draw a large amount of aggro on themselves. Furthermore, their spells (dots, nukes, and especially snare) are huge taunts. This, coupled with the fact that they have very low AC and HP for a melee or hybrid and most importantly no way to shed aggro pre-jolt leads to some serious problems. The only way I can taunt off of a ranger effectively is if they use a slow 2 handed weapon and don't cast, removing well over half of their effectiveness.

For rangers to be able to make use of their full potential and actually provide a benefit to their group, there must be a better way to taunt off of them, pre-jolt. Increasing a warrior's taunt effectiveness would be one answer, but the best solution is probably adding a skill to allow rangers to shed aggro, something like evade.

I think I speak for many many warriors when I say that the best thing you can do to "fix" warriors to make our class more enjoyable and effective would be to fix rangers. Nothing, not even the ability to gate, bind, and sow ourselves or something equally ridiculous would alleviate more frustration than making it possible for a warrior to keep a mob off of a ranger (or the ranger to keep the mob on the warrior, whichever).

Can we get an official reply to the rumor of an 1100 AC softcap? Does it exist or not?

If it does not exist, then great.

If it does exist, then we'll have some problems. All of us who have spent mass game time and plat building our AC up point by point thinking it meant something will have to unload this high end equipment at rock bottom prices since it's no longer in demand. Then we need to shift focus to +stat items of questionable worth. And of course we do not know what soft caps are on stats, or even how certain stats affect our fighting. There is constant debate among the warrior community concerning stats and as far as I've seen, nothing conclusive has been determined.

So please release the following information:

Does an AC softcap exist?
If so, what is it?
What are the softcaps for other stats?
Exactly what does each stat do for us in battle?

Warriors' equipment selection is as important as a caster's spell choices. Yet we have no way to know if we're blindly running down the AC trail and not improving our characters at all. With high end, no-drop items in the game, it's vital that we know if the stats and AC on the item will even be used or if the item should be passed to someone else.

Another note on the 'softcap': If it does exist, and if it is 1100, then we have a balance issue with hybrids. Warriors are cleary intended to be the best tanks in the game, meaning we must have superior AC and hit points. At higher levels, a plate hybrid can easily hit 1100 or even 1200 AC and come within a couple hundred hitpoints of us. If there is a softcap of 1100, then the only difference between a warrior and a shadowknight or paladin, from a tanking perspective, is about 200 hitpoints. Add in their hybrid spells and skills and they become a class cleary superior to warriors. I think that is the warriors' fear right now. Any information about this soft cap or any other AC issue would be greatly appreciated by our class. That's all it would take to ease our fears.

We play warriors to tank. That is our number one goal in this game. If another player, even another warrior, is getting hit during a fight I get jealous. I taunt like hell and try to bring the focus back to me. If I can't pull him off another warrior, that's not so bad. I give the warrior professional respect and keep trying. But if it's a ranger, or a shadowknight or a paladin that the mob is hitting then I get irritated. If the mob ignores me completely and continues to dote on the hybrid I get quite pissed. I am a tank. I am a meatshield, and it's my job to take the hits for the team. Without that I am nothing but a low level DoT spell.

So as you evaluate the classes please keep this in mind. Spell effects on armor? Cute. Disciplines? Eye candy, with a couple exceptions. Crits and crips? More eye candy, just not as tasty. What do we want? We want to be considered the best tanks in the game with no argument whatsoever. We need to have a noticeably greater number of hitpoints and an AC that cannot be obtained in any way by a hybrid (if AC over 100 has any use, of course). That's it.

Warrior class defined in three words:

AC and Hps.

Everything else is just packaging.

To begin with I'd like to address the complaints made by non-warriors here and there in this thread.

Warriors are specialists. The only use anyone has for us is in combat, to suck up damage and grab a mobs attention. To be so specialized we've given up a great many things. We knew this going into it. No complaints.

The thing a great many people that don't play warriors, who have posted to this thread, seem to be overlooking, is how utterly useless we are outside of a group or without an extensive support network.

You complain about the spells on our armor and the fact that they don't fizzle. The spells are generally worthless, or extremely low level buffs, and go unused. I could get buffed by a 9th level druid and have nearly the same utility as that provided by most pieces of cobalt.

You argue that we are the supreme tanks and that we need no modification at all, yet monks, sks, rogues, rangers and pallies all have nearly the same ac and, in most cases, superior offensive and / or taunt abilities to us at high levels.

All a lot of us are asking for is more ac and hit points and the ability to taunt more effectively. We're not asking for a decrease of your effectiveness. We're just asking for a more clearly defined specilization in what we are supposedly the best at, damage absorption and effectiveness at taunting mobs. We're not even asking for an ability to travel or escape better, for the most part.

In summary, damage absorption and taunting are all we've got that define us as a class. We'd like to be just enough better at it than everyone else to keep that distinction. So, why fight us on it? It will only help the group, and believe me there's no one more group focussed than your average warrior. Without the rest of you we couldn't have gotten this far at all.

As far as what I'd like to see to "balance" us;

More AC, More Hps. (It what defines who we are as a class, and 50 ac and 300 hps seperating us from folks at high levels is so little difference as to be non-existant)

Better taunt. (Its what we do, and unless we're the highest level in the group and / or have the best gear its hard to do more often than not)

Loot drop improvement (Cobat helms, cobalt bracers, and the epic quest examined)

Nothing new there.
