I'm an enc who does sit around PoK quite a bit and pimp out KEI. Just a couple of things about donations:

1) Personally, a donation system for me is not only a good source of income, but also a way to organize the people who want KEI. When they trade with me, a tradewindow appears and they are auto-targetted. It makes it really easy for me to click Invite then accept the trade. It could be for 50pp, 20pp or even 1pp. The main design is to lock a place in the group. I would go insane and probably wouldn't be too tempted to give out KEI if I had to follow everyone asking for it in /say and /tell. Sure I could just /rtell and Invite, but even that gets annoying when 6 people all /tell you at the same time.

2) If someone requires a fee for KEI, and sets a minimum cost, don't encourage his actions by paying. KEI is a regentless spell. It's free except for the mana and time spent casting it. Whoever demands more than 1cp for KEI is just being greedy. If we're talking about a spell witha needed component, then setting a minimum value which will pay back the cost of the component is definately acceptable.

On a sidenote. A little story. There was this one time, I was selling KEI at the Nexus stone, and another enc flew in who had MGB and offered an MGB at bank. Plenty of people showed up, and about 30 seconds before the MGB was to be cast, he went LD. After it seemed that he wouldn't return. I just offered in /shout that due to technical difficulties, I would be giving out Free KEI (non MGB, I didn't have the AAs at the time). All I required was for the person to trade anything with me, even 1cp, or a water flask, anything, and I would under no circumstances accept ANY of it. I cancelled every trade and instead just used the tradewindow as a means of organizing the groups. Let me tell ya, not only were people appreciative, but I got to see some really funky stuff being thrown in the trade window. That's a nice way to make new friends.
