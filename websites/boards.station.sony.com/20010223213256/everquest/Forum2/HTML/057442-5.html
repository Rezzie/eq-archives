Nice post, Vendrix.

I think that Verant was horribly shortsighted from day 1. SoV only highlights the fact that Verant never planned for 2 expansions aimed almost exclusively at the high end game.

Mistake #1: 1 byte stats. The highest you can get in any given stat is 253. While this worked well pre 50 it's awful post 50. For instance, monks were put at the max on melee skills. As a post 50 reward warriors were placed there as well. Monks had nowhere to go so they basically watched another melee class play catch up.

This has hurt casters much worse, however. Casters max meditation at level 50. There are 10 more levels, however. This means the closer you go to 60, the longer it takes you to completely regen your mana pool. This can take, literally, upwards of 10 minutes. Verant's solution appears to be Clarity 2 and Clarity song 2. That's fine and good, provided you have a class to give those to you. Otherwise, grab a book or turn on the TV because you're not going to be doing anything for a while.

Mistake #2: Increasing the Magic Resistance of mobs. I have stun. It is literally useless in more than 50% of SoV either because the mob has Lure only MR or else the creature is unstunable (yet another smart move). This has, ironically, increased the need for hybrids while pushing all damage casters out of the picture. Shamen, druids, mages, necros and even enchanters are forced to rely upon whatever other skills they have. Enchanters find this easy. Shamen find it somewhat easy. The rest are, literally, useless.

Mistake #3: AE silence (Tunare, Ali the Elder). This is so unbelievably unfair to casters it's not even funny. A single spell that can render 7 classes useless?!?!? This needs to be removed from the game.

Quick rundown of the post 50 classes...

Warriors - disciplines were awesome and made warriors even more optimal tanks.

Monks - watched Warriors catch up to them in melee ability without anything new for them being added. Disciplines were nice but really didn't help. Some skills (like Thunderous Kick) were just plain insulting.

Rogues - became the new wizards of combat. Rogues outdamage everyone, period. Backstab for 500 every few seconds really adds up.

Paladins - really wasn't much reason to play one and so the post 50 paladin community is often very small. Moreover, they were billed as definitive 1h slash and yet all their defining weapons are 2h. They have improved as a class but are still lagging behind.

Shadow Knights - in the same boat as pallies for melee but also really diminished as second rate necros. Giving them Summon Corpse was a good move tho.

Rangers - They've come a long way. Tracking is being fixed at last. Arrows can now do lots of damage, making RANGErs a bit more viable there. But rangers have gotten little respect. Their taunt factor is HUGE but they lack the AC to tank. To put it simply, there is no Vision(tm) for a light, fast tank. The high end game requires people to stand next to mobs and beat on them and take whatever is dished out in return. Rangers can give better than just about anyone but they can't take it. Rangers dying has become something of a cliche joke. I've heard rangers going out to putt say jokingly "Well, I'm off to die" only to have it come true shortly thereafter. To have put a ranged weapon class into a game where there was no intent to make ranged weapons a viable mode of combat was a mistake. Go play PSO and roll up a ranger. They're not easy to play, but they can match melees in long range damage. Hopefully EQ2 will make bows and arrows a better form of combat to justify their absurdly low AC.

Druids - Druids are a jack of all trades. At low levels they can fill all 3 roles: tank, nuker, healer. As melees begin to break away druid tanking ceases to exist but the other 2 roles remain. Druids actually wind up pretty well at 60... except that they don't excel at anything. They can heal, but not well enough to keep a tank alive. They can nuke, but thanks to the new MR that's pretty much gone. So druids find themselves in the "pretty good" category while the game puts out ads for "Wanted: specialists".

Shamen: Verant is scared of shamen. A healing class that can covert HP to mana has potential for abuse. Hence why Verant has refused to upgrade their healing and why Absor has repeatedly stated that he doesn't like Shamen getting Superios Heal. Without the ability to land slow and magic debuffs, shamen have no real uses to a group and that's what the new MR has done. A shaman with slow can keep a group healthy by themselves in Kunark but not in SoV.

Clerics: The one dimensional healers with some buffs and res. Clerics have no other roles. Period. Nobody takes a cleric into a group to do anything but buff and heal. And given the nice exp losses Kunark and SoV have it makes begging for a res a near necessity or else throw away hours of exp work. Complete Heal is the only spell that has scaled with the game and as such, it has sparked much class envy. But clerics really don't get much else. Moreover, clerics are not respected by Verant. Nobody listened to the class over MoK and nobody listened to the class over the Donal's nerf. Other options weren't even discussed. As such, many clerics resign in disgust and to replace them many people powerlevel cleric twinks up. As such, finding a good cleric has become much harder. Moreover, Verant has been reluctant to expand the class as they consider them "balanced already".

Wizards - aka the "Fixed in RoK" class. Wizards pre 50 had limited uses. Ice Comet was the king of nukes but it really didn't give people much of an incentive to play one. Post RoK came Lures which made the wizards able to nuke practically anything. Wizards have it very good now. They stand as one of the few classes that can attack anything in the game successfully and now find themselves wanted on every raid.

Mages - the other nuking class. They have pets and as such they blew past wizards and were enjoyable to play. Post 50, however, they lack lures and as such are often reduced to casting Call of Heroes and summoning pets. Worse, pets are often more of a hinderance than anything else in many areas and pathing sometimes demands that mages be without pets in many areas.

Enchanters - Always wanted for their ability to enhance thought, speed melee and slow and even halt mobs, the enchanter has become much more useful in the post 50 game, to the point they are often as required as clerics. They have been hit hardest by the MR increase as they must try to mes or slow mobs while buffing casters and tanks. Unlike the ranger, however, enchanters have no AC and much less HP and, as such, are often the first to die. Their spells have such a high taunt value most healers often spend all their mana trying to keep them alive.

Necromancers - the hated class. Necros are notorious for soloing. Snare + fear + pet + DoT = reverse kiting. They have no real grouping spells per se. They can stack DoTs like crazy and lifetap but, even with the low resist lifetap, they just can't keep up past 50. Verant has told them that, in the end game, all they are is mana batteries and pet producers and that is the penalty for having the ability to solo so well.

Bards - Bards have it good. They can wear plate, unlike their enchanter parents and they can do the same things... speed melee, speed mana regen and mes creatures, all for 0 mana. Their main problem has been that Verant simply didn't want to acknowledge problems the class has had. Bards have always had a long list of broken or problematic songs.

I'm waiting for the final "class balance" stuff to be posted. Hopefully we'll get some things fixed...
