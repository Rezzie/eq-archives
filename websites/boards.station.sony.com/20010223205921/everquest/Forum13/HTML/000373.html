With regards to the "Balanced Monk" Model:

I have a Human Monk on The Nameless whose primary stats are 88-87-88-87, and regret to inform you from experience that he deals NO more noticable damage at 88 STR than my other Monks which both have 80 STR, and in fact- the "balanced stat" monk model gets hit WAY more often for MORE damage... even with the best gear a non-twink, non-planar monk could buy- his AGI is merely 116, he has only 13 more Hit Points than the one with a STA of 85.

The "Balanced Model" is anything BUT balanced- it is the hardest monk to equip and enjoy. Balanced Monks rely, perhaps, on the notion that they will (eventually) acquire enough "uber-gear" that they can make up for their revealed short-comings.

The "STR monks" (those who tossed most of their points into Strength at creation) realize all-too-late that they are one-dimensional DoT's that can thrive only in the safety of a group which can either buff their agility (Aug/Nimble/FLC), or cast mana-efficient heals (Manasponge Syndrome).

The "AGI monks" (those who spent most of their points in Agility at Creation) are the most enjoyable (from lowest to high levels), easiest to pick equipment for, and can solo much better (In my experience) than either of the other two models.

Simply the *possibility* of Dodging and/or Blocking a hit from Velketor (hits for 850 per POP!!) is well-worth giving up 13 Hit Points for, or the 2% chance *more* of proc'ing "yer-uber-weapon" methinks.
