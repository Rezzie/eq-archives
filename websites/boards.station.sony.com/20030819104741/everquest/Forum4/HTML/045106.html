They won't do this because possibly 90 percent (arbitrary figure pulled out of my bottom) of players who "quit" one day return, wanting their characters back.

An improvement would be that if SOE did this themselves, the account could be fully transferred to the new buyer through SOE. The problems are many though:

If SOE was doing this, they'd certainly not do it in a way such as the original owner could profit. They'd obviously only allow the account to be transferred, likely for a fee as you say (Paid by the person getting the account, I'd assume)

Problems would arise with people trying to make profit outside of SOE, such as contacting the purchaser themselves and also arranging an out of game fee between themselves. Then if player A is transferring the account to player B, through SOE, while player b agrees to pay player A 300 additional dollars via an email but then doesn't do it, you have player A upset at player B and likely would have SOE in the middle.

Player A would contact SOE again, saying that an agreed for amount was not transferred after the fact and he wants the account back. SOE would say, "That is not part of our transaction service, we only transfer an account you are abandoning to another player at the wish of both parties."

And you'd of course have all the boyfriend/girlfriend/spouse/brother/sister etc. "revenge" transfers with the original account owner calling up SOE and blaming them for that as well.

So as you can see, SOE would seem to be more responsible for any problems while the same old problems would still arise. So it is much easier for them, as well as their legal department, if they simply say transfers are not supported in any way, shape or form and never share account info.

The last reason, as Ashtaroth says, is that they want people buying the software. Retail sales success is a part of the way that their success is measured in a way that other companies accept because it is counted by third parties. This is why they were approached by Lucasarts to do Star Wars Galaxies, etc. It isn't the same if they just say "Oh, we have transferred many accounts! Believe us! We have 100,000 new players!"
