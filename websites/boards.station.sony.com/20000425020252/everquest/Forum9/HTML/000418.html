Many many eons ago the gods put up a patch message stating that AoE rains when used indoors would be less effective. The reasoning for this was that "Rain" indoors is kinda odd.(dont ask me, I didn't write the patch)
Also, rain spells have a maximum number of targets and possible "hits". Usually this is 3-4 targets, and 1-3 waves. Depending on the spell you will have a total max composed by some obscure formula using the number of targets under the cloud and the number of possible waves. Therefore, if you cast rain AoE on one mob, you will get 3 full waves of damage. If you cast it on 2 you will probably get 3 waves but the second creature might only take 2 hits while the first took 3. After two critters you start loosing waves and hits more and more. The spell still does the same MAX damage(assuming no resists on any of the hits), but since you have more targets to spread that out over you dont see as many waves as you would think there should be.
Anyways, thats the way I remember it was explained to me a long time ago.
The reason they limited them was because some intelligent players(yep, there are a couple!) were going into Blackburrow at levels 20-50 and having some low level with a sow train the entire zone up to them and WHAM slam them all with a AoE wave and watch em fry. Was a great way to make money and increase factions but they had to nerf it like everything else...
oh well
