I need to preface this by saying I have never played a ranger. However, I have grouped with them frequently as a warrior, and I feel their pain.

Rangers are one of the most versatile classes in the game, they can do almost everything, and do it well. They pay for this in low AC, HP, and defense skills. Fair enough.

However, these penalties, in many cases, make it impossible for a ranger to use all their wonderful abilities effectively without getting themselves killed.

For instance, a ranger is a wonderful puller, between tracking, harmony, snare, and archery. However, no sane group would ever let a ranger pull, because once the mob gets on the ranger, it's never coming off.

Another thing that makes rangers a valuable asset is their ability to snare. However, snare is one of the biggest taunt spells in the game, and using it will get the ranger killed.

Rangers are also fantastic damage dealers, with their ability to dual wield fast, hard-hitting weapons. However, dual wielding fast, hard-hitting weapons is another very large taunt which will, again, get the ranger killed.

Basically, making the most of their abilities (or in some cases using them at all) will get a ranger killed. No hybrid or melee, doing everything they can to taunt, can taunt better than a ranger who doesn't even try to. Furthermore, no hybrid or melee class is less able to deal with the degree of taunt that a ranger generates. Warriors, SK's and Pallies have AC, HP and skills, rogues have evade, monks have FD.

To be an effective and enjoyable class, rangers need a way to deal with the amount of taunt they generate, either through increased defenses (AC, HP, defense skills) or some way to shed aggro, something similar to evade for rogues. To me a method of decreasing aggro is more balanced and more in keeping with the class. I realize that jolt performs this task, but not until lvl 55, and this is a problem that plagues rangers from their 20's on.

Given the idea that rangers are more of a dexterity/agility based fighter than a brute strength warrior (this seems to be the way rangers were intended, am I right?) it would seem very reasonable and in keeping with the class to give them an evade skill. This would solve a very key problem with the class, one much more fundamental than trueshot disc's or exp penalties or any of the other improvements that have been made recently.

I've read most of the posts above (although I skipped a few I admit) ...archery seems to be a recurring theme that everyone wants to see 'fixed' - Line of Sight, lost arrows (for no obvious reason) and Z-Axis issues are the major concerns, but damage is also a contentious issue. Its almost as frustrating to see a damage 8 arrow, fired from a damage 40 bow hit for 1 point of damage as it is to see it miss completely.

Here's an idea to enhance archery damage a little under 'normal' (i.e. non-discipline) conditions. First a little discussion of how things stand currently:

The basic bow damage formula, excluding any adjustments for skill level and dexterity is something like:

max damage = bow damage + arrow damage

e.g. Trueshot / RainCaller & 3 damage arrows = 20 + 3

There is no level-adjusted minimum damage, so even with 220 skill, 255 dex and the heaviest damaging bows in the game it is still possible to hit for 1 point of damage... and we often do. Also the contribution of the arrow damage to the formula is relatively small, especially with high damage bows (and arrows)

Instead of the simple a+b formula I propose a slightly more complicated (but not too difficult to understand) formula that enhances the damage slightly through increasing the proportion of the damage contributed by the arrow.

I propose a formula where:

max damage = bow damage * (1 + (arrow damage/N))

where N = a value to be determined by investigation. Let's assume that N = a constant value, e.g. 10 for illustration.

So using a Trueshot / Raincaller and 3 dam. arrows

max = 20 * (1 + (3/10))
max = 20 * (1.3)
max = 26

...and with 8 damage arrows max will be 36 (instead of 28 currently)

Assuming a 'best case scenario' (i.e. a ranger with a bow actually contributing some real damage) the values with an Efreeti War Bow are :

max = 40 * (1+(3/10))
max = 40 * 1.3
max = 52

max = 40 * (1+ (8/10))
max = 40 * 1.8
max = 72

In this case N is a simple constant value, but this might be some variable derived from skill level, dex, even bow damage itself ...or a combination of these factors.

In addition the minimum damage of any bow / arrow combination should be the damage of the arrow used. i.e. a 3 damage arrow will always hit for a minimum of 3 points, up to the max damage.

So for our example above the base damage range is 3-26 or 8-36 instead of 1-23 or 1-28 with a Raincaller (3-52 or 8-72 with an Efreeti War Bow). This would make 8 damage arrows (~1pp per shot) a more worthwhile investment without scaling the damage unreasonably.

An alternative, more simple solution would be

max damage = bow damage + (2* arrow damage)

I accept that it will never be a primary combat skill - Verant have always been very firm on that point as non-melee damage is 'low risk' (although pulling Lvl 51+ mobs with arrows can change your perception of 'low risk') - the suggested damage model keeps the damage below that which we are capable of in melee in normal use.

In Trueshot mode I believe the damage should be more than we are capable of in melee for a short period of time, assuming equal skill levels and that we land a similar number of hits. FYI The Trueshot disc lasts 2 minutes from activation. This is usually longer than the fight (unless you're in Velious).

The benefit of scaling the damage according to a factor based on arrow damage is to increase the payback on the higher damage arrows really... just so you can feel that you're getting value for your plat.

There might also be some consideration given to target size vs. damage. It should be easier to hit a target like a giant or a dragon, however the damage modifier might be less. As a competent archer in RL I find it frustrating that I 'miss' a target the size of a barn door at a range of 20 yards, when IRL I can place a 12" group into a target at 50 yards in a crosswind - on a good day at least

Wow! I hope you get this far, and find my suggestions reasonalby well thought out and flame-free!

Overall I think Rangers are in pretty good shape, except with regard to the end game, and certain idiosyncrasies (Rogues tank better than rangers?!?!?!). The following are what I see as tweaks for Ranger balance. Bugs and balance tweaks both discussed. Short versions here, justification arguments below:

Archery: Fix arrow ‘poofing’ bug. Eliminate or widen the range for the “target is too” high or low issue. Let us do some sort of respectable damage with archery. 5 damage arrows should do 5 points of damage minimum, not 1. Why can’t Rangers get crippling blows with bows if warriors can? Boy oh boy is fletching expensive for very little gain in either combat or economics.

Melee: Triple whammy on defense is too harsh (low AC, low HP and low defense caps). Maybe the low AC and low HP can be left alone, but at least boost our defensive caps. We are light, agile, chain-wearing fighters, let us avoid some of the hits. Sometimes we HAVE to tank. Bump defense to 215 or 220, riposte to 180 or 190, and dodge to 140 - 150. We have a caster’s defensive capabilities and yet are a front-line class.

Casting: In general leave alone. But if it is intended that casting make up for our weak defense, then we need stronger spells earlier.

Tracking: The changes Rowyl outlined sound GREAT when can we have ‘em?

Archery:
Arrow's poofing - fix it, please. It’s VERY annoying to spend lots of plat on higher level arrows just to have them disappearing.

The "target too far above or below you" as implemented doesn't make sense. If a Ranger can cast line of sight spells on the monster, then he should be able to hit it with an arrow. Not that the altitude restriction has no place but it needs to be opened up -- a lot. That mammoth is on a hill shorter than a Ranger is tall and he’s too far above the Ranger? But the Ranger can snare him, DOT him, or DD him so the Ranger should be able to hit him with a bow as well. Archery should have the same line-of-sight capabilities as spell casting, does especially given the miniscule damage it does compared spells.

Speaking of damage, can Rangers get a little better bow damage please? Not a lot, just enough to make archery (one of Rangers ‘defining skills’) a bit more useful in the low and mid levels, before the Trueshot discipline arrives? Perhaps a damage bonus similar to melee weapons. I don’t see how applying such a damage bonus will unbalance the game. In contrast, we have wizards and druids quad kiting and blasting the daylights out of monsters from a distance. The relative slow speed of archery would not allow it to become replacement for melee. But it would make stepping back out of a group fight and peppering the monster with arrows a viable option to compensate for the Ranger’s current inability to stand in there and take it.

As it is, with archery not being a significant part of the Vision and not being a viable damage-dealing tool why is it so expensive? I spend A LOT of plat working on fletching and it seems the expense is not commensurate with the reward. However, I would much rather keep the expense and have archery made more useful.

How can 5 damage arrows hit for 1 point. By default if it hits I should get at least 5 if not 6.

Warriors get critical and crippling hits for archery and Rangers only get criticals. This appears hard to justify. Rangers shouldn’t begrudge the Warriors their criticals and crippling blows in archery, but Rangers should get both of them too. It’s that whole ‘defining skill’ thing; the bow and arrow is a Ranger’s logo after all. The Ranger’s archery cap should be higher than that of any of the other classes.

Melee:

I understand and can accept that Rangers are supposed to be a damage dealing class (offense-based as opposed to defense-based), but there are still some issues, even taking that into consideration. I think that it is important to understand that offense-based classes’ defense CAN be set too low. Whether it actually IS or not is debatable. But that fact that the possibility exists needs to be accepted. (The converse is true well: defensive classes’ offense CAN be set too low, but whether it IS or not is open to debate.)

Rangers get a QUADRUPLE whammy against them in defense: low defensive skill caps, low Armor Class (natural), lower Armor Class items, and low hit points. For convenience sake I’m grouping the two AC issues and just saying low AC. The combination of these three strikes against Ranger’s defensive abilities is just too much when combined. Any two of the three might be justified, but adding the third pushes us below where Rangers should be. I think a slight improvement in HP and AC, and a more pronounced improvement in defensive skills is warranted.

It is an issue of damage avoidance versus damage absorption. By avoidance, I mean how likely a given attack is to hit, and absorption is how much the damage from the hit is reduced. Rangers should NOT be able to absorb much more damage than they currently do. That is to say, they are not tanks, standing there and taking hits. But Rangers should be able to avoid damage a lot more than they currently do as lightly armored agile fighters. No, I don’t want to be a tank, but I don’t think we should be paper tigers like caster’s either. The Rangers 200 defense cap is the SAME as a druid’s! We should be able to avoid damage better, though not necessarily absorb damage better, than a druid does.

With regard to Armor Class I’m a bit stumped as to how much it applies to avoidance and how much it applies to absorption. If it could be changed such that the avoidance was increased, without affecting absorption, that would be great. In terms of Armor Class, Rangers should be well below Warriors, Shadow Knights and Paladins. Rangers should be above (or at the very least on par with) Rogues/Bards/Monks. (Not really sure where the monks fit in.) As it is Rogues, a back-stabbing class, have better toe to toe melee capabilities than Rangers. That just shouldn’t be. Rogues also get additional defense mitigating skills Evade and Instill Doubt. Rangers should be hands down above the casting classes in Armor Class with the POSSIBLE exception of a Cleric.

Hit Points for Rangers should probably be below Warriors, Shadow Knights, and Paladins. But we should be on par with the other melee types. And we should be well above casters being less fragile warrior types.

In the area of the defensive skills, Rangers should not trail the Warrior, Paladin, and Shadow Knight by as far they do. We have already taken heavy hits in terms of Armor Class and Hit Points. In this single area we should nearly match the Warrior, Paladin and Shadow Knight. The defensive skills fall into the avoidance category and I believe these caps should be raised accordingly (defense, dodge, parry, riposte). . Bump defense to 215 or 220, riposte to 180 or 190, and dodge to 140 - 150. In particular, riposte has a damage dealing aspect that being offense based we should tap inot. We have a caster’s defensive capabilities and yet are a front-line class. Change these.

Bottom line is Rangers need a bit of improvement in melee defense. Improvement in at least ONE of the three areas discussed (HP, AC, Defensive skill caps) would do the trick. Or possibly a SMALL improvement in each area would do the trick. I happen to believe that the defensive skill caps are the best place to make changes based on what a Ranger is all about. Improvements to either HP or AC may work to also help balance the Rangers as melee class, as long as they are minor.

If a Ranger can’t stay in the fight long enough to deal the damage he is capable of because of his poor defense what good is he to a group. Ranger’s lack of defense prevents their offense from being effective. Rangers can’t outdamage Warriors by much, if at all, even if they didn’t have to back out of the fight. And when the Ranger backs out he’s not doing damage, which is what Rangers do. All this is less of an issue with a full group, but becomes a huge issue with groups of 2 – 4. (Where they are likely the main tank, having formed their own small group of misfits, because other groups want a Rogue, Monk, or Warrior. Over-heard in a Vox raid: “All tanks report, Monks, Rogues, Pallys, SKs and Warriors” and later, “No not Rangers, tanks”.) It is laughable that Rogues are considered better tanks than Rangers. That should not be. A sneaky back-stabbing class tanks better than a Ranger?!?! And NO I am NOT saying nerf Rogues, more power to them. Rangers just want some improvement in defense so they can be competitive for spots in a group with the Rogue, Monks and Warriors.

Of course instead of making a relative minor change of increasing the defensive caps, some of the melee issues could be improved by increasing our offense. As the offensive hybrid, we should be near the top of the damage output lists. As it stands, we are near the bottom for BOTH offense and defense. As a rough example, if we can only stand and fight half as long as a Paladin, we should be able to do at least twice the damage for that period of time. I believe that Rangers offense is fine, we just need tweaks in the THREE defensive areas we are penalized in.

I do understand the argument that we have casting abilities that should offset the defensive penalties. But frankly they can’t make up for the triple hit we take with low AC, HP and defensive caps. For the most part the spells are under powered for our level and the level of monsters we are facing when we get the spells. Speaking of spells.

DarkAdept,
While I value your opinion I think you are looking at it from a very narrow point of view. That being that "YOU" as an SK don't seem to have trouble keeping a mob off a Ranger. Let me explain why this is a case.

Arguably, the only skill a ranger brings to a dungeon group is our ability to SNARE mobs so they don't run off and bring back friends, and if you have your epic, to SLOW mobs to 50%. With a SK in the group, with MUCH higher AC and HP, the ranger allows the SK to snare (darkness line) to keep the mobs attention squarely on the SK. If the Ranger were to be the "snarer" you might have a more difficult time keeping the mobs on you as snare and ensnare are mega taunts. Especially if the Ranger is using his "new" procing spells which are also big taunts.

Now lets look at that same group with a warrior as the main tank (this is the case 90% of the time BTW). The ranger is expected to snare, as he can take the hits SLIGHTLY better than the druid in the group, or will be the snarer if there is no druid in the group. In this case, the ranger snares, moving him to the very top of the hate list. Wait til later in the fight to snare you say? Well what if you get resisted or fizzle (both of which are not uncommon on 50+ mobs). It then runs off and it's time for an EVAC if your group has an evacer.

Why not cast Jolt immediately after you snare? Simple, at 51-54 we don't have it! During these levels a ranger really feels like a heel in dungeon encounters. If I snare, I get agroed, if I get agroed, I die or become a mana leach. The only option at this point is to turn off auto attack and back out of combat, and hope the warrior can taunt off, at which point we are not fulfilling our role as a "damage dealer".

Now imagine the Ranger IS 55+ and has jolt. He snares at 25-50% health (depending on how fast the group is killing the mob), it turns on him and he casts Jolt. It is resisted, causing extra taunt. (meanwhile cleric is chain healing) He casts again, this time it sticks, but guess where it goes, to the warrior taunting or the cleric chain healing?? You guessed it, the cleric. The ranger now has to taunt off the cleric to save the group, in so doing, he can't Jolt, lest he put the mob back on the cleric, so he has to stand there until the warrior can taunt off (which doesn't work very well on 55+ mobs I might add).

So once again, as a shadow knight with the ability to snare a mob, you shouldn't see this problem (if the ranger in your group is worth his salt he'll let you snare to keep the agro).

-Mirk
