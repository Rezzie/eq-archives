Dont forget your color flux line eather. Getting beat on by a mob that needs mezzing.. Use your longer duration Flux, then Mezz.

Also dont forget that your flux can save lives.. you may get into situations where mobs can not be mezzed. A) The Tanks can't peal them off the healer, B) Someone got sloppy with a DOT. When you see your healer start casting, start spamming flux.. and the healer will most likely get the heal off. This also works well to save the hide of a over zelous nuker, keeping the mob from getting in the death blow till the heal gets in or the tank peals the mob. Also you may run into healing mobs, if your tanks can't stop the healing with bash. Get in there with Flux. When the mob gets under 50% start watching for the heal and when it comes flux.

Before I quit I played a chanter to the high 40's, one of my best real life friends played a chanter to his mid 50's. Flux is your friend. Learn to use it for all its worth. To few chanters really do this well.

One of the keys to succeeding in an LDoN adventure is communication. Let the group know what is mezzed (as most chanters do). If pacifying, let the group know when it is safe to pull. If a melee, let the group know that you are, in fact, assisting the MA. If the puller, tell the group that there is an incoming. Tell the group when to move up. If a cleric, let the group know whom you are healing. Et cetera et cetera, et cetera.

Communicate.

To expand on the "don't forget your color line" post.

ALWAYS keep one up. (I'm very guilty of not doing this on several occassions) ESPECIALLY on assassination missions. A lot of mobs have CH, so that stun can save time. If the mob starts with the telltale blue casting, spam that color spell. Remember, these dungeons are timed....so the last thing you want to do is get a mob down to -20% only to see it's HP bar jump back to 100%. If the healer is a second mob, just mezz it.

Make use of the spell sets that were introduced. This is great for hot swapping buffs in the middle of the dungeon.

If you can't see into the room, Pacify one that you can see, Bind Sight on him, and swing the camera around to Pacify the rest. Works very well, and in the event of a resist, gives you a second or 2 more to yell "Run!" or start mezzing.

Feel free to Pacify all but the 2 closest to the door. Then let the Tank open the door. When 2 come, let the tank pick one, /assist him to find his target, and mezz the second. That way the next mob is waiting when the first is done, and you can usually move the fight into the next room for the next set of pulls. This moves you along a bit faster, but the tank needs to be able to take a few hits (usually not a problem with a warrior, SK, or pally).

Keep your best available Rune on yourself at all times, and if facing casters keep Magic Resistance buffs on everyone.

Haste the melee's and slow the mobs as you fight them.

Make sure your group knows what /assist is. Then make sure they are /assisting whoever is the main tank. This will cut down on inappropriate mezz breaks.

Try to keep a stun memmed at all times. Feel free to use it to interrupt casting if you have a caster start to heal itself (blue light coming out of his hands, usually).

Watch out for fighting in a room with a Pacified caster. It may not aggro, but will still heal the mob your are fighting if within range. In this case you either pull the fight back to the last room or mezz the caster and pull it next.....

Just a few tips....
