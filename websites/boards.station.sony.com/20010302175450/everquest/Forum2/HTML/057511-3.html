No offense, but not all shamans are content with buffing newbies and twinking melees (which is the only thing we are really good at, btw). When I signed up to be a shaman I wanted to be a feared witchdoctor, a bringer of plagues, diseases, and curses, as well as the one able to cure such ailments. I wanted to kick ass and take names, and that was the class I got. Do you remember the days of old? Everquest before the expansions? Oh, shaman was a great class! We were MASTERS of slows, resistance debuffs and DOTs, we had a pet that, while not exactly a monster, could perform decently given some support. We were extremely close to necromancers in soloing power, and we packed a punch in PvP combat like few others. Then along comes Kunark and tragedy strikes. My shaman is now a shell of her former self, a monument of great things past. Sure, you can play her, sure, she's not useless, but the greatness is gone. People used to cheer when a shaman showed up, used to be amazed at what we brought to a group, used to marvel at our power and versatility. Not any more. We aren't masters of anything now. We don't bring anything unique to a group. Every single one of our masteries have been handed out like candy to others, and our essential abilities have not been upgraded properly. Once, we were a real presence in a group - now, we are grease to make the real classes do their job a little better, a filler to bring along if there are no real classes to fill a spot. Shaman went from one of the best, if not THE best class in Everquest, all things considered, to a distinctly average class easily replaced by others and almost never perfect for any job. Before Kunark, I never even considered another class, that was how good shamans were. Now, not a day goes by without me wanting to play one of the real classes instead, instead of this mediocre relic.

Many, many of my shaman friends and colleagues on my server have either quit entirely or started over as more a more useful and powerful class. Why? The lost identity of the shaman class (due to the pimping away of shaman abilities to everyone and their little sister) and the lack of any incentive to keep playing one (the lameness of the spell list, the difficulty of acquiring the spells, the diminished attractiveness of our abilities, and - on our server - the weakness of the post-50 shaman in PvP combat).

I don't know if the shaman class can be fixed. Somehow I doubt it. It might be too late. Perhaps our destiny to decline into mediocricy was sealed the day Kunark was released. Or perhaps I'm just a pesimist given Verant's track record when it comes to shaman concerns. We'll see. I'm not holding my breath.
