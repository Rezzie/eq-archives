Not necessarily.

Since the game becomes swollen with platinum in three ways:

Pure cash drops
"merchant" items that are sold to NPCs for cash
Quests that result in one of the two

Items come in the game from the few ways.

The question becomes which is more "glutted"

As in, which does the economy fill up with faster, items or cash?

If there are more "items" than "cash" the economy rather becomes glutted with STUFF as upposed to cash. The question is then, is it easy or hard to sell things?

Id argue that there is far more STUFF then there is cash, as there's more things for sale in the bazaar I'd warrent then there is enough total cash in the game at any given time to buy.

In other words if you pooled everyones PP from everyone online I do NOT think that you'd have enough to buy EVERYTHING for sale.

The accumulation of ITEMS is what matters then, not the accumulation of CASH. Thus the reason prices drop is due to oversupply rather than cash gluts.

And the problem is I don't think either one of us can PROVE otherwise, we have OPINIONS on the functional economy of everquest that are different, I view things in terms of what STUFF is out there, and you view it as what amount of CASH is out there, we view the predominant force differently.
