The simple fact is, with the price that people playing EQ are forced to pay for failure (corpse runs, lost XP etc), they are always going to choose the option with the least possibility of failure. They are going to choose the classes who perform certain roles better than any other, they are not going to choose a defensive tank with useless spells and a laughable damage output. As has been stated earlier - Defense. Does. Not. Stack.

When given the choice between a class with a high damage output who can also take a lot of damage, and a class that can't take as much, and outputs vastly less, what normal person is going to take the latter? Don't try to tell me that our spells make up for the vast gap between the two classes, because the people here who actually play the class know that they do not.

That is our problem. We are not useful within the dynamics of a game where our two parent classes dominate.

No one wants to be useful only when there is no other option. No one want to be obselete, but this is where we find ourselves as high level paladins.
What is our role?

Are we defensive tanks?

The concept of the defensive tank, without a higher defensive skill than all others, is a vision that is doomed to failure. How can a class be defined by their defensive abilities, when those abilities are superceeded by classes who also have other advantages above and beyond defense? What are paladins the best at?

What is the thing that makes people say "hey, let's get that paladin for our group"?

When you hear someone shout "group looking for war/pal/shd" you will know that the classes are balanced.

Monk - 70%O 30%D
Ranger - 60%O 40%D (spells included)
Warrior - 50%O 50%D
Pally - 30%O 70%D (spells included)
SK - 40%O 60%D (spells included)
