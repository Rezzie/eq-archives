Lots of good comments.

Personally, I like to select components and have somebody else build.

http://www.tomshardware.com is a nice starting place for reviews and benchmarks

http://www.neutronet.com has decent prices and will assemble and test for $35. Their customer support is pretty good.

I am an ATI fan myself... ATI and GeForce will both serve you well but each also has its "defeatured" cards which will grief you to no end. Read the reviews and read EQ tech support to find which cards to avoid.

Finally, keep EQ2 specs in mind. Anybody who buys a PC to run EQ and EQ2 should have a minimum of:

1. 2.5 GHz CPU (Intel or AMD)
2. 1 GB ram (look for dual bank features and get two 512 MB sticks)
3. 350W or greater power supply (Beware, Dell went through a period of minimizing their power supplies)
4. Solid video card

"official" spec list for EQ2 here:
http://everquest2.station.sony.com/faq.jsp
