Sorshasu,

Travel benefits both high and low level characters. At lower level, death is more common.

Lets take an my Ogre. At level 4, I decided to make myself some patchwork armor. There aren't any bears around Oggok and Ogres need ruined bear skins. Considering my options, I decided I would travel to the newbie log in Neriak. Best option, considering I could arrive safely.

So I travelled.
From Oggok to Feerrott
From Feerrott to Innothule
From Innothule to South Ro
From South Ro to Oasis
From Oasis to North Ro
From North Ro to East Commons
From East Commons to Nektulos
From Nektulos to Neriak

Finally, I could get a bind without worrying about the dangers that might happen upon me.

Not good. I could have had an 8 zone corpse recovery run, with no guarantee I would make it the second time.

No travel, no armor.

I got my armor and headed back (to train). When I got to Oggok, there was no one in sight that would bind, and my bind point was 8 zones away. It took me an hour to get a bind and during that time, there were at least 10 people of over 40th level who could bind. Guess they had bad faction with the only places I could get bound within 4 zones any direction.

Or they were busy with Important things people do at 40th level.

I have made this journey many times at later levels, only to die a couple of times due to uncontrollable circumstances (like Sand Giant getting trained on me from behind). When that happened, I had the inconvenient 5 zone walk back to my corpse.

Fact: There aren't enough bind points.
Fact: People aren't always available or willing to bind at existing bind points.

I don't hunt in the Karanas because they are too far to run from Qeynos, Highpass, or the Arena if a griffon sneaks up on you in Combat. If I could bind closer, I would.

I rarely hunt lizards in Feerrott because its so difficult to get a bind in my hometown. If I could bind closer, I would.

Guk, Innothule, and South Ro are out as well. They look like fine places to hunt up to 20.

I hunted in steamfont with an Ogre for a bit, but closest bindpoint is Greater Feydark. Its not worth it.

I might hunt in Unrest, but I know the closest bind point is Greater Feydark. (Kaladim is further and KOS to evil races).

The current system is limiting. It could and should be improved.

1) The number of places that non casters can be bound should dramatically increase.
2) There needs to be another mechanism besides player dependency to bind in unfrequented places; otherwise, they will remain unfrequented.

You are free to decide not to change your bind place; however, I contend that if you were a caster, your decision would be considered foolish. You will die sometime in the future, and when you do, you will have inconvenienced yourself. Since you are not a caster, it is not a foolish decision, but a foolish circumstance that you must make that decision.

Finally, there must be a balance between needless death and being so careful as to never die. One who dies occasionally might actually gain in experience and wealth faster than one who does not die at all.

There are achievements to be had by those who take some manner of risk. For example, I have enjoyed participating in the following.

1) Hunting Orcs in Highpass at level 14.
2) Pulling from the first level of unrest at levels 16-19 efficiently. Camping the hand, ghost, etc. in the low 20's.
3) Camping Guardroom in Highhold at level 19.
4) Attaining Rune of Ivy from Castle Mistmoore as level 24 ranger. (No, I didn't buy all my Ivy Etched Armor).
5) Attaining Heart of Fire from Solusek A at level 26.

etc.

I enjoy the challenge. Without the challenge I won't enjoy the game.

You lose 10 debate points for trying to compare the "reality" and "sense" behind game rules to fantasy novels, then revoking that "sense" when it suits your purpose.

Overall, and not by you, the selective application of what is reality (in the fantasy worlds) is one of the worst things that games suffer from.

Designers decide that bows with unlimited arrows aren't realistic, for example, so you have to buy or make the arrows to use the bow. But they don't care that arrows which vanish into thin air, rather then sticking in the target or laying on the ground for the archer to pick up again, isn't realistic either. Again, selective implementation of subjective reality, and it doesn't work.

Various games have had their own idea of how to handle the bind point concept. Asheron's Call has bind portals which anyone can use. Meridian 59 sent everyone to an underworld upon death, then players had to try getting out through a doorway that ported to different places in their world via a random cycle of changing locations. EQ has bind points.

An argument that one way of setting a home point for reincarnation is more "realistic" then another is silly. All you can argue is which one you find to be more desirable, and face the fact that when others disagree with you, their perception is as "realistic" as your own.

quote:
.....in future RPGs to come, players will insist on playing every variation [of character], and insist that they all be viable, preferably close to equally so.
end quote

I prefer, in my "reality", the logical fact that casters are, by their very nature, more powerful then similar level non casters or partial casters.

King Arthur was the finest fighter, magical sword in hand, in the land. Merlin could turn him to dust in an instant. This is back to your fantasy reality concept. Games that attempt to balance classes by cutting back on the power of spells while at the same time having them eat up mana so that in the end both the fighter and caster do about the same amount of damage are far from consistent with the fantasy reality that you brought to the table.

It is entirely possible that they [casters] have

This is an evil we put up with due to their also unreal concept of the level gap experience penalty in groups. In other games, usually the older ones, casters were naturally much more powerful then fighters, but fighters leveled at two to three times the rate of casters.

Two characters both created at the same time, one fighter one caster, would come into balance by the fighter making level 12 while the caster, who had much more to learn, only made it to level 4 or 5.

That's a debate for another thread though.
