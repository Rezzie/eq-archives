Oasis has some advantages that draw players. Sorsisis mentioned one - there are lots and lots of mobs there. Tanbill mentioned another - the gators don't help each other, so unless a sand giant wanders by, or unless someone pulls the specters off the island, it's a fairly safe place to kill.

Other benefits of Oasis:

- There are convenient vendors at the gypsy camp and the Inn, so you can sell loot and buy food and drink.

- You can heal and/or meditate on the pillars pretty much without any chance of being attacked.

- There are mobs there for a large range of levels, so you hunt there almost as soon as you can leave home.

- You can kill as many crocs as you wish without doing any faction calculations. And you can hunt there if you are evil, because there are no wandering goody two-shoes NPCs ready to kill any trolls they see.

- There are usually so many player there that you can get help any time you need it. Generally, you won't unless you just goof up, but there are sometimes wandering ghouls, madmen, or mummies that can mess up your careful plans.

Anyway, so much for my sales pitch for Oasis. It sometimes can get frustrating, when you go after a mob and another player beats you to it. But I like Oasis. Of course, if the gators dropped real loot, you would have wall-to-wall players there all the time.

Frazgt: If that goblin summoned you, he would have to be at least level 51.

As for Oasis: It is one of the safer places to hunt for teens level players. Crocodiles and caimans are ideal targets because they are neither aggressive nor social. The rare sand giant is almost always taken down quickly. The biggest dangers for most are Cazel (a level 51 sand giant), the occasional bad spectre pull, and wandering undead.

South Ro is a bit more dangerous due to the large number of wandering undead. It's also a bit further from a place good-aligned characters can sell. You'll sometimes see trolls or ogres killing orcs in the southern part of the zone. That zone's main purpose is as a gathering spot for Planes raids. Except for the ancient cyclops, no MOB is really sought after there.

Why?

Because the penalty for death in EQ is quite harsh.

Which means, the #1 rule you learn is to avoid death.

If you break this rule, you can:
A> Go backwards in XP.

B> Have long boring corpse recoveries.

C> End up multi-deathing. During CR, your chance of death is alot higher, esp when doing CR in zones full of aggro stuff.

So, players learn that it is extremely important to avoid death.

And, the penality goes up as you level. With the exception of when you group with clerics, or are a cleric. Even at that, a TPW is often a bitch to recover from.

So, that is why oasis is popular. Then, we have the grouping-centric part of the game. This means you go where other people are, in order to find a group. This is why oasis is +over+ crowded.

Reasons to make friends with your neighborhood rogue:

1: CR isn't nearly as much a pain when you can be invisible to darn near everything ...

2: A friend is less likely to pickpocket your mobs ...

Honestly, while I did my fair share of leveling in the Oasis/LOIO/OT/DL route, dying in other zones is NOT that much of a problem. If you're halfway smart and careful you won't get killed in the first place, and if you DO get killed hopefully you learned something to keep you alive the next time. Heck, you don't even have to go to a dungeon if you're that scared of 'em. Like someone already said, SRo is right there, with similar level mobs, and who knows, you might get lucky with the AC ...

Death is really not that harsh a thing to recover from until MUCH later in the game. I took unrezzed deaths in my 40s with hardly any skin off my back, and recovered the xp in no time (except for one time I lost level 46 and took three hours to get the three blues back, but that was some odd circumstances). Dungeons and non-main-xp-route zones are NOT that hard if you just take it a little easy until you learn the ropes, and then once you're rolling, the xp AND loot beats out anything you can get in the standard cycle.

Unfortunately, you still have to bring your own group half the time, since social inertia will have people sitting in Oasis/LOIO/etc until they shut the servers off permanently ... But if people could realize the above points, life would be so much nicer.
