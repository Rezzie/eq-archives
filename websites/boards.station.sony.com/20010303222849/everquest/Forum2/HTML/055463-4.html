My brethren have proposed a few things to help rangers achieve parity with the other melee classes in desirability for raiding/grouping for xp. Let me expound on them a bit, and share some things in these 2 threads that have been overlooked.

THE LIST IN ORDER OF IMPORTANCE

Hitpoint disparity:
Yes, I am sure you hear from EVERY class that they want more hitpoints, but in the ranger's case it is very evident an adjustment needs to take place.
-------History of hp balance
When I first started EQ, rangers had the same unbuffed hp (with equivalent stamina/hp items) as paladins, and sks. Rogues and Monks both had VERY SLIGHTLY less. Warriors had the same as well. Warriors got fixed (they needed it badly...now warriors are the epitome of tank. /who warrior all on any server compared to /who all anyhybridnamehere. DO NOT NERF WARRIORS WE NEED THEM STRONG).
Paladins and shadowknights received a large hp increase before Kunark came out because ivy etched quests (ranger ruby replacement armor) were slightly easier to finish, and also had the same ac as rubicite...so did the rest of the class armors. As a result of this, coupled with the fact that rangers without ruby all banded together on the Glade, www.eqforge.com/ranger, to find out that ac was all that mattered with hp in absorbing damage. Rangers had just slightly less or equal ac to paladins/sk at MID LEVEL and thus could tank equally well with the "ivy twink" armor. Add to this that paladins/sks never had to fight for armor class..so they went with "prom jewelry" aka non ac stat increasing items instead of real rings/necklaces/bracers/earrings that added ac...so more rangers actually OUTTANKED pal/sks out of simple all/all item usage. However, rangers with full planar armor vs. a paladin/sk in full planar armor were still outtanked by a decent margin.
This imbalance needs to be equalled out now with the huge ac difference in armors in Kunark and Velious. Add 200 hp for this.
-------Equipment hp modifiers
Rangers are damage dealers, they are to be classified with Monks and rogues in the new Verant vision. However, even compared with hybrids, currently in the best hp/ac configuration fully dressed rangers will have substantially less UNBUFFED hp than any Paladin, Shadowknight, Monk, or Rogue. Ironically, buffed, even some bards have more hp, along with Shamen, Shadowknights, Paladins, Monks, and Rogues. Naked, rangers will have slightly more than monks and rogues. Why? Add 200 hp for this in stamina and HP on class armors?
-------conclusion, high level game insight
Hitpoints are what distinguishes a melee class in raids. Even though rangers are not meant to tank, and properly played in EXP groups they shouldn't get hit much, rangers on raids will always be succeptible to area effect weapons from ubermobs, and WILL tank versus anything immune to magic based spells. Anyone who has fought Hoshkar or Trakanon KNOWS that hitpoints matter the most in survivability versus them along with resistances...Even though rangers shouldn't tank in exp groups for long (we can't as well as just about any melee, or bard, or cleric even), we have a taunt button and use our aggro control to protect the few classes that have less ac hp than we do, or need to have the ability to cast uninterrupted. Rangers have had to evolve from a secondary tank to less of one than a rogue..a specialty class. Many have quit and leveled rogues or warriors already, many just play badly and give us a bad name, but those of us who ARE still playing to the best of our abilities want to have increased survivability..not necessarily ac or defense...but the means to be an asset..a damage dealer. We need to have more hitpoints...or remove taunt from our ability list.

Defense issue:
Rangers want parity in skill points. Our triple whammy of low hitpoints/level 40 defense cap/low AC armor is very difficult to overcome. When we typed in the name of our ranger and went to the home point screen, ALL of us knew we were going to have lesser ac ITEMS available to us, in exchange for dual wield. This is a staple of EQ, I do NOT want to see store bought plate on rangers..nor do I wish to see class specific armor sets with equivalent ac to paladins, shadowknights, bards, and clerics. I do not expect to surpass the armor class of a well equipped high level Monk either.
