I strongly disagree with the original poster. It is a two edged sword that allows a developer to make dramatic changes (besides bugs) to gameplay in a game after release carte blanch. If a developer expects to make these changes at will, contrary to normal software development practices, then the players should expect the developer to actively seek feedback from their customers related to these changes.

For example, Diablo2 got a tremendous amount of grief in its balance patch. Why? Because the players did not expect to have any influence in any future patches (they expected them to be only for bugs since they do not pay per month, unlike EQ), so they were justifiably angry that Blizzard made such dramatic gameplay (class) changes after release (whether they were appropriate or not).

In other words, if MMORPGs developers are planning to constantly change gameplay AFTER release (one side of the sword), then they better be willing to LISTEN to current customers before those changes are implemented (the other side of the sword). If they are unwilling to seek feedback, then they better test enough before release to 'lockdown' gameplay (like Diablo2, no sword at all), because unilateral changes (other than bugs) afterward will not be tolerated by the majority of the players, their customers.

The problem is not the board. The problem is also not Verant. The problem is the majority of the posters.

Like it or not, but the quality of a board is depending on the quality of the posts. It doesn't matter what board system you use and how many people are posting. Want some examples?

Compare the "General Discussion Board" with "The Newbie Zone". Same company behind it, same board system, but worlds apart.

This board is so heavily used that people like to see it as a platform to become "famous". And since it's so perfectly anonymous, one can vent as much as he want (within a few guidelines). Why are there "trolls"? Because they want to be. They don't act trollish by accident, they act trollish out of their own decisions. This is the first major problem.

Then we have the problem that everyone here is an expert. You hardly see posts like "I'm not quite sure, but I think xxx may be a useless spell in my view." Most posts are "XXX is useless... and I have PROOF" or similar. As soon as someone is showing a weakness, he get's bashed and told: "You obviously have no clue, so stop commenting".

Then we have the replies of the blue names. Why do they post so rarely? a) They are only human. b) They are not allowed a simple mistake. Remember when Abashi (I think) said, that you don't have to look into the book to med at level 32? He got laughed at, called names and so on. And then there were the equations: Abashi says medding with books stop at 32 = Abashi has no clue about casters = Abashi has no clue about the game = Verant has no clue about the game = Verant sucks. People tend to exxagerate (sp?).

Most of the posters here think they OWN a response, because THEIR issue is the most crucial one. If there is no response, then Verant has no clue. If the response is not a total agreement to the post, Verant has no clue. If someone else sees the fact in another view, he has no clue and is a troll.

It's sad but the more "official answers" you get, the more people think they can expect one. And if none comes, it's bad.

On "The Newbie Zone" the situation is totally different. In the last 12 months or so I have seen about 2 or 3 blue posts. And that is what keeps the board so great. You don't see posts like "VERANT, READ THIS" or "ABASHI, HERE IS THE PROOF". The people there don't expect an answer and therefore don't beg for one. But still they can discuss issues with each other in a mature way. And still they can express negative aspects and discuss it without getting into a flame war.

So, what is the solution? There is only one solution that really works. The posters have to change. Stop flaming others, treat everyone as a human being, express concerns and critism in a mature way. Accept the blue names as human beings. THIS would cure the board.

but what do I know... I don't have a char above level 50 and haven't killed vox so many times that I can call it "Yard Trash"...
