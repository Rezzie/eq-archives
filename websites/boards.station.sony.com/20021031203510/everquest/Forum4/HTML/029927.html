Not that it matters to the discussion, inoffensive_name, but the parser was wrong.

EQ caster is not showing you exactly what is in the spdat. EQ caster interprets the spell file. It's not simply a reader. Actually, EQ caster is a bit more subjective in its interpretations than Lucy, which leaves more room for error.

All the spdat is.. is a bunch of binary numbers, some of which represent numbers and some of which represent characters(A, B, C, etc...) The spdat does not have much, if any, information that is not directly useful to the game. The only characters you'll see are the "cast on you/cast on other" type messages and spell names.

So for the wizard spell "Ice Spear of Solist," the spell file will read something like(I don't have the spell file open atm so I'm sure those numbers aren't in the right order.)

.....Shock.of.Frost..1..00..00..00..6..8..00..2..00..00

What someone writing a spell parser does is play the game and cast the spell. He'll see that the damage done is 6, while the mana cost is 8 and the cast time is 2. The programmer writes the program to say:

Level: 1
Damage: 6
Mana: 8
Cast time: 2

But now he knows what order the numbers should go in. So, for a spell with a similar entry, he shouldn't really need to play the game. He can make an educated guess for the new entry. So say the new Velious expansion is coming out, and the wizards want to know what their new spell is. EQ caster looks in the spdat and finds:

.....Ice.Spear.of.Solist..60..00..00..00..1200..300..00..4..00

It says ok this looks like a nuke that requires level 60, does 1200 damage, costs 300 mana, and takes 4 seconds to cast. It's called "Ice Spear of Solist." It adds the spell to the list shown when you run the program.

Now, Jolum's Abatement had a totally new effect on it, when it first came out. This means that they couldn't just look at the numbers and know exactly what they meant. They could see the actual numbers.. they saw that it did up to 15% of something, but didn't know what. Someone told Vaslin that the earring was reducing aggro by that amount, but that person was mistaken. Since people like to see actual descriptions and not "unknown effect 15%" when they use EQ caster, he updated it anyway, trusting his source-- which happened to fail him in this case.

Adding to this problem is that fact that people often do not keep their version of EQ caster updated. I just opened up my copy of EQ caster, and I still have the version that is reading the current spell file as aggro reduction. So Vaslin may correct the mistake right away, but because everyone is still using a slightly older version of his parser they're all spreading around bad information.

The error was in the parser, the effect never changed. (Unless they changed the spell on test before it was ever live, which I doubt)
