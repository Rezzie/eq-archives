This is more for the older folks who can actually remember way back when:

Once, times were simpler. We didn't worry about whos ISP stinks, what got nerfed or this class is bettr than that class. In a time before high-end computers, Sega Saturn and Playstation version XXX.

That's right, a time when the upright, coin operated video game was king. If we wanted electronic entertainment, we had to have about $400 to blow on an Atari 2600, or the better alternative, our $5 a week allowance to go blow at the local Arcade on the newest wave of the future.

Let's hear about the favorites of those days. Now, I don't want to hear about Mortal Kombat IX or Killer Instinct whatever that you had to have a novel sized book and wrists of steel to actually progress in the game. I'm talking the truly CLASSIC video games that started the industry.

Here's my top 5:

5. Space Harrier (Ok, this one is a stretch for Classic, but it was darn fun)

4. Vanguard

3. Star Castle (They called it "color" because it had those color celophane overlays on the glass)

2. Rip Off (No color whatsoever)

1. Marble Madness (I ws never very good at it, but the atmosphere it created as totally cool.)

So let's hear the favs of that bygone era before lag, loot and Dr00ds.
