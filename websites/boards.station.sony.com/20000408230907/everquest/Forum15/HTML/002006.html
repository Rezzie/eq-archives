Author Topic:   A few changes to the ranger to think of
nytcrie
Station Member 		posted 03-03-2000 12:48 PM           
Let me begin by stating that I think the ranger is by far the greatest and most rounded class in the game. I have always had an affinity for the ranger in other games (AD&D, MERP, and others) and was pleased to find them in EQ. Rangers in EQ are by far the best of the hybrids, and in my opinion, are better than most other classes in the game. I would like to see some minor tweaks, but as it stands I am proud to play a ranger, and will likely only play other classes when I am truly bored, or just need a break from the higher level stresses.

As the various classes get tweaked and tuned, I have noticed rangers get little attention. As said before, I think rangers are the greatest class, being highly balanced, yet I see them lack a certain something that would define them from all other classes. They are due for a tweak or two, in my opinion. I propose some changes below, which may or may not be possible, but would be greatly appreciated I am sure by Rangers around Norrath, regardless of how minor a change.

First, I would like to address skills, both Combat and non-combat. Rangers are to be the great outdoorsmen, and masters of the bow. They get some pretty nice bows, and the fact of Criticals is nice. However, bows don't get much use given normal situations (usually one shot to a mob before it is too close). I propose they get something that makes them show their mastery of the bow: give them point-blank range with it, or perhaps a delay modifier, or maybe a higher Critical chance. I have seen Warriors also get Criticals with bows, so this really didn't set the ranger apart too much. A point blank range, being the ability to fire up close and personal, is not unheard of. AD&D allows it, albeit at a slow attack speed. A modified delay, say a percentage of reduction per level (a level 50 ranger would have a 50% reduction for example), would allow the ranger to land a few extra shots on the mob before it closes to melee range.

On a similar note, raising the cap for Fletching for rangers would be a nice touch, and would go far to encourage the ranger to actually work for that Trueshot rather than wait to buy it.

I think the armor restrictions on rangers are fine the way they are, although other than the Etched Ivy armor, there really isn't much out there tuned for a ranger's use that gives decent AC (this also is with exception to Planes Gear). Maybe a piece or two, nothing major, such as a mantle or mask would be nice. Perhaps instead of AC points, there could be some armor that enhances both AGI and DEX. Also on the armor note, but not necessarily related to rangers, I have read the Ask Milo column often, and seen many a complaint that too many pieces of armor look alike, as well as that varying the colors of a piece degrades the graphic quality some times. Perhaps instead of more colors, you should toss a few new skins in there to differentiate armor types.

Damage output could be a little higher, be it by better weapon selection (although what we have is great) or by upping our melee skills a bit, but not to that of rivaling a warrior. Rangers were always primarily melee/archery fighters in lore, so a little extra output would be beneficial. I don't quite understand why our magical skills cap out at 235, but our combat skills cap out at 200. This is a bit off to me, as rangers spend much more time tanking than they do playing healer or nuker. I would think it not unreasonable to either reverse these numbers, or perhaps even them out. Rangers have less HP and AC than SK’s or Paladins, so the ability to do a bit more damage would not be too unreasonable I think. Again, I think we have a good damage output as it is, just those numbers confuse me a bit. In all my years I never saw where a ranger had been more spell oriented than warrior (in most RPGs they are listed as a warrior subclass). I am grateful for Dual Wield AND Double Attack, as they excel us past the SK and Paladin. Perhaps to make this skill cap change more feasible, you could lower the caps on other weapons skills, thus restricting rangers while enhancing them. For example, lowering our caps in the Blunt, Piercing, and 2Handed skills by a great amount to give us say 20 extra points in 1H Slashing, Dual Wield, and Double Attack. Perhaps another way to go would be more DEX boosting weapons or gear.

Tracking needs some work. I hate to track a mob from across the zone only to find it being killed when I get to it. Perhaps they should be listed in order of proximity, and even a scrollable listing, which would allow for more mobs to be on the list. Also, the ability to track corpses (by following the path of the recently deceased)(even just your own corpse) or tracking whatever you have targeted would be a plus. If I walk into the woods, and I see a critter from a distance, I should be able to follow it. Tracking your target would be really nice since occasionally mobs can get away from you, and you really can lose them (often wandering into an even nastier mob). Another though would be to list mobs on tracking by level, albeit a vague range, but it would make things simpler. I wouldn’t recommend being able to con them from the tracking list as some have suggested, but a general idea wouldn’t be bad (well, more general than the conning system, another aspect that could use some work).

Rangers do fall short in that they have no spell or skill that is unique to the class. An interesting idea would be a “befriend animal” effect or skill. Not really a charm per se, but something that let’s the rangers have an animal that is near by aid them in combat, or protect them in some manner. As the skill increases the level of the animal, or perhaps the radius of effect, or some parameter, would increase. This would be an interesting skill to see in the game, and as there are many zones without the benefit of wildlife, let alone high-level wildlife, so it would not be easily abused.

My other concern is in the spells we are allotted. I have very little to argue with current spell selection, I just think perhaps a few ideas for what we’d like to see in the future. First off, I would lower SoW to 30, as it is really awful long to wait until 39 for it. I would never go lower than that, but I think at 30 we have suffered without it far long enough. Although I hear many cries for them, I would never give a ranger Gate or any other teleport-like spell. Maybe as a charged effect on a weapon or piece of armor, but never as a spell. Lowering the price of gate potions would be lovely too (in 39 levels I have never had more than 3000 pp, of course, I never really sell anything, and the Fletching Market was never very profitable).

. I also hear cries for the ability to bind, but again I think that is for the casters and not rangers. If anything, I would give them the ability to bind outdoors only (no cities or dungeons), but that could lend to abuse as well, and perhaps make them unbalanced. I’m sure that could be further restricted in that they could only bind themselves. Eyes like Cat s pretty useless, as is Dance of the Fireflies, to the elven/half-elven rangers (which comprise 2/3 of the ranger contingent on Norrath) as they are self-only. At least Eyes like Cat should be castable on others, making it a viable buff.

I think our healing is balanced, we are not paladins or clerics, after all. A Regeneration type of spell would not be far out of the question, however, and could be made self only for balance if need be. Perhaps one of the lesser forms of the spell. A Dex buff would be loved and adored for an eternity, as this does seem to affect our weapon procs and combat accuracy. On the buffing note, an upgrade on Feet like Cat (the Agi booster) wouldn’t be a bad thought either. I’m not saying replace FlC; I would merely give a new spell that has a greater effect (much akin to the Skin Like xxxx spell line).

My other thoughts aren’t really in any single category, so they will be expressed as organized as possible.

Quivers need a bit of fixing. The first quiver I ever bought I did so thinking you could put it in the ammo slot. When I saw you could not, it soon became pointless as more arrows could be carried in a pack than in a quiver. Were this enabled, it would make them actually useful. Perhaps make them a mob drop, or even a craftable item. Either way, they really aren’t cost/space effective.

In the Ivy armor quests, some of the spawn times are ridiculously long. I have known people to waste entire weeks just sitting in OoT waiting for Wiltin to spawn and the same with Maldyn in Rathe Mtn. Some people can't afford to spend that amount of time on a game, and it is terribly boring while waiting. I also think is was a bit rough to put the quests in the relatively quiet zone of Kithicor, then throw a slew of lvl 35+ mobs in there, that's just insane. Nothing worse than putting a training point into Disarm and getting jumped by an Undead Cleric, and you have no SoW to even think of getting away with.

My last point can apply just as well to druids as it can rangers. There are no higher level zones, other than Kithicor, located in the great outdoors. All the higher level dungeons are just that: underground dungeons. This removes some of the usefulness of the druid and ranger, and limits their spells. A few outdoor zones geared for the higher level characters would be great, and may actually help clear out some of the more congested zones. Cazic Thule is a great combination zone, with indoor and outdoor areas, and I have noticed harmony doesn’t work inside the buildings there (which does disappoint me some, but I can live with that). Kithicor is great other than the fact that it is all Undead, and a bit rough for the poor newbie halflings and level 20s on their way to HighKeep. Some nice, high level, outdoor areas would be really grand, and probably quite welcome to many players.

In closing, I do believe that rangers are the greatest, and I really can’t see myself playing any other class (at least not with any kind of regularity). I just simply propose some minor tweaks to refine and define us a little more. I’ll never quit EQ for lack of these changes, but I think they are probably the more reasonable changes to make.

Please respond with any comments to both the board and my email nytcrie@lenzlink.net.

--The NytCrie

I have to agree with the tracking being broken. Not much I can say here that hasn't been said, but on spells, I have a little bit to say. The problem with Rangers and Druids right now is there's so little differentiation. A Ranger is just Druid with the ability to wear a bit better armor and no weapon restrictions, decent melee skills, and weaker casting abilities. Or said in the opposite direction, a Druid is just a Ranger with weapon and armor restrictions, but better spells.

If a Ranger and a Druid group, the Ranger's spells basically become useless. The only unique spell a Ranger has is the feet like cat. It'd be nice if the 2 classes were differentiated a bit more in the spells. I'm not saying the Ranger should have completely different spells from those of the Druid, but that there should be a few here and there. Right now the 2 classes don't really compliment each other very well, and with the Druid's tracking being superior in most cases to the Rangers (due to a lower cap, thus they don't fill up their tracking list as easy and they find closer creatures) the Ranger doesn't offer as much as it could or should.

For the most part I think the Ranger class is in good shape, but the spell selection could definitely use a little work and with the Kunark and the higher level cap about to occur, it would be the perfect opportunity to make some changes in this vein...
