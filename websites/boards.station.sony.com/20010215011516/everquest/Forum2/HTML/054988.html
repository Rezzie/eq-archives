They have explained before that they cannot add any timestamp anywhere without making mods they don't want to do because they are too extensive.

Here is what they could do, however, that would reauire NO changes to any items.

1. Change it so NO RENT items do not go away on logout or LD, but instead are processed on log in.

2. When you logout or go LD, a timestamp is saved in a table in memory. This table holds the last 1000 logouts or LDs.

3. When you log in, that table is checked. If you are not there, or the timestamp is more than 20 minutes ago, all your NO RENT items are destoryed. Otherwise, they are preserved.

Note that this requires no changes to the item or player database, nor any storage on disk. It just requires a cache of the last 1000 logouts or LDs. This will easily fit in 40 or 50k of memory.

Note also that the changes in the server code should be localized to just a trivial change in the logout/LD code, and a slightly more complicated, but still easy, change in the login code.

This solves the LD problem at pretty much zero effort and risk for Verant.

This has already been suggested and shot down several times by Verant. The reasoning they gave was that to add a field to store a timestamp for an item would require a lot more work that it was worth, not to mention managing that timestamp would place an additional load on the servers. Neither of which they are going to do. There have been several threads regarding linkdeath and ways to handle it, and Absor has stated that he has that list and they are going to talk about it, but he didn't know when.
I play a mage, and while Linkdeath is annoying, I solved the problem by not summoning items for myself. I buy my food/water, summon bandages only as needed, never use summoned bags except to give large quantities of summoned items to other players. Now if I go LD the only thing I lose is my pet. It bites, its not fair, but until they accept one of our suggestions, or we can find one that will work within the current EQ system, we just have to suffer.

For what its worth, the best "general purpose" suggestion that has been put forth that I have seen is this:

The system knows when you go LD versus camp - ie it marks you LD, whereas if you camp, it logs you off normally. If you go LD, add a "spell" effect like a rez effect, only it doesn't do anything negative to you. A lot of spells have a fixed duration, so the system knows when the buff is "put" on you. If you log back in in a certain amount of time, the LD effect is still on you, and you keep any no rent items. If you log back in after the LD effect duration has expired, you lose all no rent items. If you camp, then you lose all no rent items just like you do now.

They haven't shot this one down yet, to my knowledge, and I think it would take a little creative effort on their part, but its doable I believe under the current system.
