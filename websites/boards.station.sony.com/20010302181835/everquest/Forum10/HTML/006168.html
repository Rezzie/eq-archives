Hi all,

ok, this is a post I have in mind for a long time. I'm not sure if it's good to post or not. On one hand, I would like to pass it to the younger generation. On the other hand I don't want to come over as a "know it all" soundish post. I'm not THE EverQuest Gamer. Hey, I'm not even 50 yet (to be honest, still sitting in 40). But I have ONE thing I'm proud of. I can watch.

I read Cy's post about his current problems. I read Icegerl's post about bringing back the magic. I read questions of new players and answers of old players. Beside the "technical questions" like where to buy food, where to hunt and so on, there are often questions that base on a simple problem: "How can I make the most out of the time I spend in EQ?"

EverQuest is an addictive game. It's so addictive that the "game" part can be lost very easily. So here I present, "Ellegon's 7 rules of playing EQ". It is mainly intended at the lower levels, but some points (especially point 5) might be interesting for higher levels as well. It might help to "bring back that magic". It might help to improve the gametime of some players. And in short: if it doesn't help, it doesn't hurt :-)

Take it as a suggestion, not as a law :-)

*************************************************

1. Thou shalt not be impolite

EverQuest is anonymous. You create a character and nobody knows you. Some people use this fact to be rude or even harrass others. They wouldn't do that in real life, as they would fear the consequences, but they think in EQ they can get away with it. Well, that's not true. First of all, your reputation will grow and be spread. The longer you play, the more important your reputation is. Around Level 35 it will be hard to find good groups if you have a bad reputation. Just remember: the cleric you curse at at level 5 might be the one who has to save your life at level 40. And he WILL remember. In addition, some actions (like harrasement) are warnable offenses and may lead to suspensions or even bannings.

2. Thou shalt learn your strengths and weaknesses

The success of a character in EverQuest is based on his knowledge. If you are a caster, know your spells. Ask others from the same class how they use it. Watch others fight. It is amazing how many druids don't know about the power of Harmony (as an example). Try new strategies, be creative. And learn. The basic concepts you learn at level 10 will help you survive at level 40. It doesn't matter if you kite a lion or a Sand Giant. The hunting strategy is very similar. Practice new things on easy MoB's, even if they don't give experience.

3. Thou shalt not expect services

Every class has some unique powers. Many of them are nice to have and make the life easier. Examples are Spirit of Wolf (SoW), Clarity/Breeze, Teleports, Buffs (resists, shields, symbols etc.), heals. Now no class/race has ALL the possibilities. But that doesn't entitle you to get them. You don't NEED a SoW to get to your corpse. You don't NEED a Clarity to hunt. Especially if you plan to hunt solo, don't beg for help. If you can take the MoB alone, do so. If you can't, group. Why should an Enchanter, who can't really solo, give Clarity to a Druid who chooses to solo? Wouldn't it be better if they team up? Sure, a little help is nice, but don't feel you are entitled to it just because others have the ability. The same goes for begging. Everyone started with the same. Even the level 3 warrior in full crafted. The only difference is, that he worked up another char to be in the position to put these items to his 2nd char. Others may have RL friends that equipped them. But one thing is commonly shared: People HATE beggars. See section 1 and remember that. Asking for help is great, begging is not.

4. Thou shalt help others if asked

There is a huge difference between "helping" and "twinking"/"powerleveling". Give help appropriate to the level. That can be advice, tips or even some bits of equipment. But remember: giving 200 platinum to a level 3 player is not helping. It even takes away from the fun. Giving a better weapon (like a Fine Steel one) and 5 plat is much better. It helps for the current needs (like backpacks, food, spells) without taking away the fun of "earning" things. It's a completely different feeling to finally be able to afford a "Shield of the Slain Unicorn" than to just get it from a "friendly" player. If you feel like giving away higher priced items, pack it into a quest. Ask for a few things the player can get at his level and let him do something for it. Friendships start like that. The key is: give help without spoiling, receive help without being greedy. Sure, the Level 40 druid has "Skin like Diamonds" as a spell, but when fighting Fire Beetles at level 3, "Skin like Steel" is much more than sufficient. But helping can be more. It can be a heal at the right time. It can be 5 food and water. It can be inviting someone into your group even when you don't need another person. It can be offering advice to a younger player dieing over and over. It can be giving away the loc for a NO DROP item you don't need at the moment. It can be helping breaking a camp. There are so many possibilities to help :-)

5. Thou shalt have fun

EverQuest is a game. EverQuest is entertainment. It should not end to be work. Bad feelings come from things like envy, greed and the false idea of need. Good feelings come from fun, friendship and spending a good time. Don't lose your focus on that. Treasure the time you have in EverQuest as much as you can. If you feel "bored" or "empty", go back to the newbie grounds and help them out. Send them on small quests and hand out prices. Examples: "First bard to find me and sing me a song which rhimes and has at least 8 lines get's a price", "Every warrior that tells me a story about a furious fight he won gets free buffs" and so on. Be creative. Ask for creativeness from players. Or help others. Ask your guildmembers or friends if they need help with a quest or a task. Helping others can be fullfilling. And it ensures that you will get help when YOU need it.

6. Thou shalt roleplay

EverQuest is a roleplaying Game. So DO roleplay if you can. Even if you don't see a "gain" in it right away. The result is often fun, friends and maybe even help. You are a low level cleric and see a high level warrior at low health? Go and heal him. Tell him "My powers are not strong yet, but may your suffering be eased a bit." Then walk away. You'd be surprised what reactions you get. You got your first SoW from a druid? Play it out. Isn't it amazing how fast your feet suddenly are? Tell him how great that feeling is. Run around in circles. Jump. Shout out your enjoyment. What would YOU do if you suddenly could move twice as fast? Use your imagination. But remember: Roleplaying is not an excuse to be rude. Even if you "play" an evil character. This does not mean you can steal, beg or be rude to others. Roleplaying should never be used as an excuse to ruin the fun of others. Therefore it's VERY difficult to roleplay an evil char without being rude.

7. Thou shalt respect the others

EverQuest is a social game. You will meet other players from around the world. Some may have different RL religions, some have different skin colors, some speak a different language. But we all share one thing: we are human beings. And human beings have feelings. You will meet people you like and you will meet people you dislike. Treasure the ones you like, ignore the ones you dislike. But NEVER disrespect another person. To insult, harrass or threaten is not hurting the pixels on the screen, but it's hurting the human being behind it. Don't do it. Don't go there. If one thing is bad in the Real World is that people hurt people. Take this as a chance to make a difference.


That's it. Very generic, but it's the things I try to keep in mind while playing. It helped me some times, so I hope it helps others as well :-))
