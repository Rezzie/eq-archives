Verant has unfortunately lost sight of a major part of the game. There are THREE combat healing classes, not one. By making Shaman and Druid post-50 healing have both high fizzle and L34 Cleric volume, the two other classes intended to be combat healers... aren't.

This deficiency is mitigated by other spells in Shaman and Druid inventories -- the Shaman Slow line and Druid Damage Shields. Shamanic Slow acts as a healing aplifier... it reduces damage taken and therefore reduces the amount of healing necessary, hopefully to the point the Shaman can keep up with damage taken. In the post-50 game (where the healing issue has been a problem), Turgur's is incredibly powerful... usually. (Especially when a Cleric IS in the group. Shamans amplify Cleric healing incredibly.) It's that usually that is the problem. Where high magic resistance applies, Shaman healing is effectively at a level of a L34 Cleric. Without Turgur's, a Shaman watches in vain as his tanks die.

Druidic damage shields directly act to reduce MOB HP at the cost of damage taken. It isn't as effective as Shaman Slow (DS shortens fights to reduce damage taken vs. Shamans reducing damage directly) for translating into reduced healing, but it is unresistable, at least. The real problem is that post-50 MOB damage rises on a different scale from pre-50, and the linear DS damage output becomes overwhelmed the further you get from L50. So, the DS simply becomes overwhelmed and does not act to reduce time-to-kill noticably enough to affect the amount of healing required. Thus, the Druid is a L34 Cleric, under any situation after L50.

So, that means there is, in fact, only one combat healing class, where pre-50 there were 3. This translates directly into a major group dynamic problem: every 6 person group must have a Cleric, not matter how risky the eventual destination. That now requires that 1/6 of all players play clerics, regardless of target difficulty.

Given that Clerics are already necessary in any post-50 situation where someone expects to die (for resurrection line), the addition of better healing to the other combat casters will not affect many groups: a Cleric is, and always will be, necessary in a risky dungeon raid. There is simply no change on that front, no matter how good Shaman and Druid healing becomes.

The question is: should they also be mandatory in every non-risky situation? Should a Cleric be required if you want to camp the Chardok exit/entrance or Iksar Betrayer hall? Sebilis entrance? Skyfire? Karnor's corners? I think the simple answer here is no, clerics should not be mandatory in every group in every situation. There is no class in the game that can claim that need (even enchanters can't say it), so how can Clerics expect that kind of special treatment?

All Verant is doing with these changes is allowing those classes that were combat healers pre-50 to regain that status, for limited situations. The Cleric will always be the favored healing class for any dangerous (and profitable) dungeon raid, for their irreplacable ability to mitigate experience loss. That form of healing is inviolate and alone ensures the Cleric's position.
