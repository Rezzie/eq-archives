Just a note for anyone else who finds themselves in a similar situation, to keep yourself from being scammed.

If someone offers to trade something you want for something you've never heard of, and you just happen to see it for sale in the bazaar at a lower price than you are willing to pay for the item you want, try something along these lines:

I'll use the aforementioned cloaka and stein as an example...

Offer to buy the cloak for say 45k, and explain that they item they want is for sale for 38k. They get the item they want AND 7k profit. If they aren't trying to scam, there is absolutely no reason they shouldn't accept. If they don't accept it, the only possible reason is that the other item is worthless, and they were trying to scam you.

You can TRY to report them, but it won't work. I was in EC back in the days before the bazaar, and noticed someone trying to scam someone by asking to "try out" the item to "see how it looked on them". I saw them ask that of several people in say (then I sent them a /tell advising that the person was probably trying to scam them). I petitioned to report the would be scammer, explaining the scammer's game, but the GM told me there was nothing they could do unless he was using some sort of exploit to scam people.

The bottom line is, you're on your own. Caveat Emptor.

Actually, from what i understand of it, GM's will also act against other scams, but ONLY if there was an EXPLICIT lie in it. See, there is a fine line between an implicit lie (implying one thing and doing another) and an explicit one (saying one thing and doing another). If the person asks you to hold an item for a screenshot, he IMPLIES he will give it back to you, but doesn't actually say it, which makes it a scam GM's can not act against. If he said 'i'll give the item back in 5 minutes', the GM's can keep him to his word there. Just make sure in that case to /report that line.

Not that i condone scamming, but i just liked to make it clear there is a fine line which can be used to use someone's gullability against them without actually telling a lie.
