Code the spell so it:

4) Disables regeneration of mana while the spell is in effect (yet again, same effect as before).
5) Lasts the same amount of time, with the ability to cancel the effect, just like before the "bug fix" (not actually a bug, just a design decision that the powers-that-be have decided they want to work differently now... let's be honest here.)

Very simple solution with no new game balance issues that would be generated by cooking up a new, different version of the spell.

Thank you for responding in a reasonable manner and not flippantly or summarily
dismissing us out of hand. I feel we actually have a chance to work with you to solve
this problem. That being said, no matter how you slice it this is a nerf. When I heard
about, my first response was....there goes Verant again and without notice.

After some reflection, I thought about the spell line some. I came to the conclusion
that at least in my experience, it was a spell I never really used much. Was good to
have in a pinch, but when I needed it, it was never memmed so what was the point.
This was considered by most to be a last ditch spell to either buy yourself some time
or to help you clear greens or finish off a low blue mob. It would be used when you
were out of mana, youre pet was dead and either you couldnt use sow to get away or
you didnt want to waste the time you spent "almost" killing the mob.

Now with that being said, I thought about your offer about how to turn this around to
an acceptable solution. What I came up with is my own opinion but takes the spell in a
slightly different direction but one which has historical precedent. Im more of a group
player, always have been, probably always will be. While in earlier levels soloing is
possible, I think all post 50 shaman would agree that its a group game for shaman
almost exclusively. So when I look at spells sets, I look at them in terms of what do
they bring to enhance the group efficency to make me more attractive to a group.

If you look at our level 5 spell Fleeting fury, this is mid battle buff with a short
duration. It is 18 seconds long. Its recast is 4 seconds. It increases str 15, increases
Dex 20, increases AC 6 and has stam regen and is castable on anyone.

At level 14 there is a upgrade to this spell it is called Burst of Strength. It Lasts 18
seconds. Its recast time is 12. It increases str 20, increases dex 20 and AC 8 with
stam regen. Now these two spells mirror each other and have logical progressions
right?

Here is where the spell line goes wierd. So you would expect there to be a next
temporary battle buff that you can cast on anyone just like before right? However, the
next spell line that is even close but has no prior spell is the shaman Rage series at
level 19. Now, it drains the remaining mana from the shaman and last for up to 25
minutes or when you cancel it. In the meantime you cant cast any spells. It gives
approximately, 28 str, AC 11 and regens health 11. From that point on the previous
mid battle buffs which are great from a shaman "grouping" point of view recieved no
further upgrades. Instead, we received rage series upgrades.

So I ask you, why did the original series stop? Why did the rage series suddenly start
at 19? Looking at the situation now, doesnt it seem more logical for a group based
class to continue to recieve the mid battle buff series than a shaman only rage series
that we hardly ever use...? Now I dont speak for all shaman. I know there will be those
hardcore meleeing shamans out there that wil say they used this spell all the time. But
I think I can say for certainty that most shaman didnt use the rage series regularly
and that in today's climate the mid-temporary battle buff would be much more
beneficial to us.

In fact, if you take a look at our spell line, this would provide a logical connection to
our Avatar spell. From level 1-14 we recieved two upgrades that are similar in form to
Avatar. Spells that were meant to be a short duration, high efficiency battle buff.
From 15 to 59 we recieve no upgrades, then suddenly we recieve Avatar which is the
only thing that even closely resembles anyting in our spell line besides Rage which is
now completely useless. I think this idea would be an acceptable solution for all sides.
Its a win, win situation really. Shaman lose a spell that was marginaly used and would
gain a spell that would be used more frequently. With any or all of the two various
spell set mixes that could develop over time. Increase str, dex, AC, stam regen, HP
regen and finally if incorporated as a series which logically lead to Avatar would have
ATK increases in a temporary mid battle buff.

So at this point, why not just have the rage spells mirror how Fleeting furry and Burst
of strength works. Of course, twink the spells hopefully with some shaman community
input with increased durations and increased recast time that eventually end up with
avatar. Now maybe im missing someting but doesnt this seem like the easiest, simplest
solution and one that has a precedent and is line with our starting spell series of this
sort?

Please give this some thought, it would be a HUGE shot in the arm for the shaman
community and what a HERO you would be...hehe! Thanks for listening
