1) Provocative Q & A
-------------------------------
Questions:
- Is AE damage the third main gameplay attribute of Wizards after DD and Teleport ?
- Is AE damage a reason to play a Wizard ?
- Can Wizards "shine" with AE damage ?
- Do Wizards have at least one good AE damage spell per spell rank ?
- Are Wizards "masters" of AE damage ?

Answer to all these questions:
- YES... AFTER LEVEL 50 !

Because AE damage spells got a much greater "post-50 versus pre-50" boost than DD, in all respects: mana-efficiency, swiftness, and size.
Pre-50, only a few scattered AE damage spells are good (the newbie spells, the Columns, and Thunderclap).

Is this an incentive to level up beyond 50 ?
Or is it the insulting confirmation that intermediate-level Wizards (formerly high-level) were fooled ?
What if I don't want to buy Ruins of Kunark: I never get the shining AE ? Aren't levels 1-50 supposed to be a complete game, and levels 51+ be an EXTENSION only ?

2) Claim for Al'Kabor
------------------------------
Gameplay peril: Al'Kabor spells are range spells, and their wide radius makes it too easy to kite 5 mobs at once (much easier than to kite 2-3 mobs with Columns).
Main inherent nerf negating the peril: low damage/mana per mob (the lowest of all AE spells), so that we run out of mana before the mobs are dead (even high-green mobs).
Additional minor inherent nerf: long cast time and recast time.
Additional minor inherent nerf: you can hurt yourself with the spell.
Additional minor inherent nerf (or design blunder): the size of the level 20, 29 and 39 Al'Kabor spells doesn't keep up with level progression.
Result from a player's point of view: the wide radius is extraordinarily over-priced (in mana and time consumed), and NOBODY PLAYS the Al'Kabor spells, although some insist on three phoney and overrated benefits: low aggro (the same is achieved with a more efficient lower-rank Column or PB spell), spell interruption (only a few Al'Kabor spells do that, and the same is achieved with a more efficient lower-rank PB spell), and wide-radius initial pull (please find a way to pull your mobs without crippling your mana bar, there are many).

Fix demands (I want the two of them, 2.a AND 2.b):

2.a) Resize the level 20, 29 and 39 Al'Kabor spells with a mana cost increment of 75 instead of 50, so that they cost 175, 250 and 325 mana respectively (instead of 150, 200 and 250), AND give all the pre-51 spells +10% damage for the mana (with corresponding aggro). This is the MINIMUM ensuring:
- that the level 20, 29 and 39 Al'Kabor spells do more damage per mob than the previous Column (currently they do less damage, it's ridiculous),
- that the pre-51 Al'Kabor spells don't make us LOSE mana-efficiency versus DD on 3 mobs (still, with this fix, they will give a +3-9% bonus only, whereas Columns give +75-90%; and they will still NEVER allow to successfully kite packs of high-green or low-blue mobs).
Namely:

Lvl 12 Frost Spiral: 46 starting damage (42x1.1) for 100 mana.
Lvl 20 Fire Spiral: 91 starting damage (71x175/150x1.1) for 175 mana.
Lvl 29 Shock Spiral: 154 starting damage (112x250/200x1.1) for 250 mana.
Lvl 39 Force Spiral: 237 starting damage (166x325/250x1.1) for 325 mana.
Lvl 49 Wrath of Al'Kabor: 493 starting damage (448x1.1) for 500 mana.

This demand (2.a) is only the first part, so that the intermediate-level spells aren't plain awful; it isn't enough to make Al'Kabor spells good (the wide radius will still be very much over-priced, and players will continue to fancy the Columns doing about the same damage per mob, whenever available). Making Al'Kabor spells good is the second part below (2.b).

2.b) Give all Al'KAbor spells (including post-50) a specific reward to be attractive in spite of the low damage/mana per mob, which can be EITHER extra-swiftness or debuff (the two options will make use of the wide radius nicely).

FIRST OPTION of 2.b: EXTRA-SWIFTNESS (should be easy to code)
Squandering mana for superior swiftness on mob-pack scale would be an interesting development of the Wizards' classic strong point.
Al'Kabor cast time:
Mana and time are two resources which are consumed to cast a spell. If Al'Kabor spells are costly in mana, make them cheap in time (cost trade-off). It doesn't matter if the cast time is shorter than DD (they also do less damage per mob than DD, don't they ?). Let Columns shine with their higher mana-efficiency, and let Al'Kabor spells shine with greater swiftness: higher "damage/cast time" on 1 mob than Columns (also post-50). Columns have an average "damage/cast time" on 1 mob equal to 56-67% of DD pre-50, and 78-87% of Draught post-50. Give Al'Kabor spells a "damage/cast time" on 1 mob equal to 70-75% of DD pre-50, and 82-92% of Draught post-50.This is achieved with the following casting times (taking into account the damage increases in 2.a above):

Lvl 12 Frost Spiral: 1.8 cast time (instead of 3.1).
Lvl 20 Fire Spiral: 2.5 cast time (instead of 3.6). Note: the reference DD for swiftness is inbetween Force Shock and Flame Shock.
Lvl 29 Shock Spiral: 3.2 cast time (instead of 4.0).
Lvl 39 Force Spiral: 4.1 cast time (instead of 5.0).
Lvl 49 Wrath of Al'Kabor: 4.8 cast time (instead of 6.3). Note: the reference DD for swiftness is inbetween Ice Comet and Rend.
Lvl 53 Inferno of Al'Kabor: 4.2 cast time (instead of 5.0).
Lvl 56 Retribution of Al'Kabor: 4.2 cast time (instead of 5.0).
Lvl 59 Vengeance of Al'KAbor: 4.2 cast time (instead of 5.0).

The impact of these changes on the maximum single-cast swiftness (damage output speed on 5 mobs versus DD speed) is large, of course: DD's swiftness is multiplied by about x3.5 pre-50 and x4-4.5 post-50 (instead of about x2 pre-50 and x3.5-4 presently). So what ? that's the point.

Al'Kabor recast time:
A pseudo-chain-casting of AE spells can be achieved by rotating certain combinations of two spells (for instance, I think a level 54 Wizard can effectively rotate Pillar of Frost and Pillar of Lightning, and get a huge damage output speed on 2-3 mobs). Give Al'Kabor spells the comfort of being ALMOST chain-castable without occupying a second memory slot:
recovery time 4 seconds instead of about 9 seconds presently (Columns have 6).
Quasi-chain-casting will allow Al'Kabor spells to shine in their own way (double or triple cast for emergency take-down and/or voluntary mass taunt).
The impact of this changes on the maximum chain-cast swiftness (damage output speed on 5 mobs versus DD speed, taking to account cast time AND recast time) is large, of course: DD's swiftness is multiplied by x2.2-2.7 pre-50 and x3.6-4.1 post-50 (instead of x1.1 pre-50 and x2.2-2.5 post-50 presently). But that's the point.
Such speed for Al'Kabor MAY be worth letting the mana run low fast.

SECOND OPTION of 2.b (instead of extra-swiftness): DEBUFF (may be difficult to code, but can be most appreciated)
Is debuff against the spirit of Wizardry ? Not necessarily. The Staff of Temperate Flux is a hint, and we could get AE debuff spells instead of single-mob debuff spells, in the same way we get AE snare spells instead of single-mob snare spells. Will AE debuff spells make us independent from Shamen, Magicians and Enchanters ? No, because Al'Kabor spells are so expensive that they won't be worth using on only 1 mob, and hardly on 2. Typically, the debuffing classes will help Wizards against lone mobs, but will have more urgent things to do than debuff the mobs one-by-one when facing a pack, so a single cast of an Al'Kabor debuff spell would fill the functional gap.
The debuff we want is the debuff directed against the energy type of same-rank DD.
Of course, the debuff should not be as large as Mag/Sha/Enc debuffs, and, especially, the debuffs against magic must be lower than those against heat and cold, because they will also favorize Root and stun, and will challenge directly the Enchanter debuffs (Enchanters get an AE debuff of 40 against magic at level 60).
If this fix, for some game engine reason, makes the effect of Al'Kabor spells all-or-nothing (such as Force Shock or Tishan's Clash), the resistance likelihood must be REAL low - I'm not spending hours writing all this to make the spells better, not worse.
In this fix option, the cast time of the level 20, 29 and 39 spells can be bluntly increased according to the resize (2.a): who cares to get higher "damage/cast time" than before, if the debuff is the prize.

Lvl 12 Frost Spiral: 6 debuff against magic, 3.1 cast time (unchanged).
Lvl 20 Fire Spiral: 9 debuff against magic, 4.2 cast time (3.6x175/150).
Lvl 29 Shock Spiral: 15 debuff against heat, 5.0 cast time (4.0x250/200).
Lvl 39 Force Spiral: 12 debuff against magic, 7.0 cast time (5.0x325/250).
Lvl 49 Wrath of Al'Kabor: 20 debuff against cold, 6.3 cast time (unchanged).
Lvl 53 Inferno of Al'Kabor: 25 debuff against heat, 5.0 cast time (unchanged).
Lvl 56 Retribution of Al'Kabor: 15 debuff against magic, 5.0 cast time (unchanged).
Lvl 59 Vengeance of Al'KAbor: 25 debuff against cold, 5.0 cast time (unchanged).

3) Claim for Wizard Rains
------------------------------------
Gameplay peril: the capacity of Rains to stack 3 hits on the same mob makes them "a bit too good" at replacing DD.
Main inherent nerf negating the peril: easy resistance making it hard to stack 3 successful hits on the same mob.
Additional major inherent nerf: cap of 4 generated hits, so that only one mob has a chance of being hit 3 times (but a cap of 5 hits would have ensured the same). Pre-50 Rains have the lowest potential of all AE spells to multiply DD damage.
Additional inherent nerf: 4 successful hits are needed to earn a consistent damage/mana bonus versus DD, and it's very hard because of the resistance (but 3 hits give a bonus post-50).
Additional minor inherent nerf: you can hurt yourself with the spell.
Additional minor inherent nerf: the longest recast time of all AE spells (12 seconds).
False nerf: the "pet bug". This is a problem for Magicians, not Wizards. I don't mind if Verant reprograms all the AE spells so that they ignore friendly pets; but I'm not asking for it.
Result from a player's point of view: as soon as you accept to view Rains as hybrids of AE and DD, rather than genuine AE (it took me some time), there is nothing wrong with the above, after all, provided that there is a prize to gain in the lottery, and the prize is SIZE. The valued Wizard Rains (level 4 Icestrike, and post-50 Tears) are able to markedly outdamage DD on 1 mob when stacking 2 hits. The despised Wizard Rains (level 12, 24, 29, 34 and 44) can't do that.

Fix demand (very simple one):
Resize the level 12-44 Wizard Rains (+30-50% to damage, mana and aggro, depending on the spells) so that they outdamage the same-rank DD on 1 mob when stacking 2 hits. They will still not perform as genuine AE (they would need to generate additional hits for that), and they will not become as popular as the post-50 Rains (because the pre-50 Rain damage/mana is mediocre), but they will become somewhat RESPECTABLE hybrids of AE and DD. The cast time can optionally be longer too (all the damage is not immediate anyway, and we don't use Rains if we are in a hurry; but, if the example of post-50 Rains is to be followed, the present cast times can be kept).

Lvl 12 Firestorm: 42 damage (28x1.5) for 93 mana (62x1.5) and optionally 3.7 cast time (2.5x1.5). Note: 2x42 outdamages DD by 8% at lvl 12, and 4% at lvl 15.
Lvl 24 Lightning Storm: 98 damage (75x1.3) for 178 mana (137x1.3) and optionally 4.7 cast time (3.6x1.3). Note: 2x98 outdamages DD by 16% at lvl 24, and 8% at lvl 28.
Lvl 29 Energy Storm: 134 damage (96x1.4) for 227 mana (162x1.4) and optionally 5.7 cast time (4.1x1.4). Note: 2x134 outdamages DD by 13% at lvl 29, and 8% at lvl 33.
Lvl 34 Lava Storm: 173 damage (128x1.35) for 270 mana (200x1.35) and optionally 6.1 cast time (4.5x1.35). Note: 2x173 outdamages DD by 13% at lvl 34, and 7% at lvl 38.
Lvl 44 Frost Storm: 333 damage (250x1.33) for 415 mana (312x1.33) and optionally 7.3 cast time (5.5x1.33). Note: 2x333 outdamages DD by 10% at lvl 44, and 7% at lvl 48.

And the spells (except PB spells) have a mob count: they can hit only that many mobs (or, in the case of Rains, generate only 4 hits on mobs). If a pet is "hit", it means one true mob won't be hit. In the case of Rains, the pet can eat up 2 of the 4 hits. But this is not a genuine "bug": only game engine mechanics conflicting with the mob count of spells. The problem here is getting them to recognize and admit that the Al'Kabor line isn't worth it. The mana cost and damage is terrible, and their idea of the balancing factor (extra range, area, and targets hit) just doesn't work out in any actual player's experience to be worth it.

First, take a look at Al'Kabor spell lines and compare them to damage of other AE spells (incidentally some of your numbers are off, Nicolbolas):

Lvl 12 Frost Spiral: 44 damage for 100 mana.
Lvl 8 AE Pillar of Fire: 31 damage for 39 mana.
Lvl 12 AE Firestorm: 3x28 (84 damage) for 62 mana.

Lvl 20 Fire Spiral: 76 damage for 175 mana.
Lvl 16 AE Pillar of Fire: 76 damage for 84 mana.

Lvl 29 Shock Spiral: 118 damage for 250 mana.
Lvl 24 AE Column of Lightning: 136 damage for 130 mana.

Lvl 39 Force Spiral: 172 damage for 325 mana.
Lvl 34 AE Circle of Force: 216 damage for 175 mana

Lvl 49 Wrath of Al'Kabor: 448 damage for 500 mana.
Lvl 44 AE Frost storm: 3x250 damage for 312 mana.

You can see there is a considerable discrepency in damage and mana cost between even spells a full circle lower than the Al'Kabor line.

Let's examine the benefits of Al'Kabor and why players don't see them as being worth the crappy mana/damage ratio:

-Increased Area: while this is nice, it is rarely even useful. So rarely, in fact, that no one keeps an Al'Kabor spell memorized. Since controlling the opposition is so critical in a game where blues can murder you and it takes hours to get your xps back, having scattered foes mad at you isn't a very clever idea. Useful only for taking out camps of deep greens.

-Increased Range: This is useful, the only really handy thing the spell has. However, take a look at Bolt spells and calculate how much more mana their 50% extra range costs?

-Increased maximum targets: Again, this is rarely useful, unless against lots of greens because angering a larger number of blues is exceptionally dangerous. The only time you care about large numbers of creatures is if they are very, very green (a third your level or less) or you are using Point Blank because you can control the location and stun them all with Enchanters.

-Pushback/stun effect: Al'Kabor spells all have a slight pushback effect like Shock of Lightning, which can interrupt casting. While this seems like a good thing, how many times do you have a situation where there are more than one caster at range from you? If you do, Circle of Force is a better choice, because it is significantly better in mana to damage and does the same thing.

The last problem with Al'Kabor spells is that they hurt YOU if you are in the area, and as such, are useless for anything but your opening salvo in almost every situation.

ALL of this adds up to make them crap. I know what happened when they were made, GZ and his programming staff (or whoever originally came up with the spells) sat down and wrote the spells up. They looked at AE and thought up this extra large shocking AE and decided it was worth more mana, and when AE nerf came in late beta lowering max targets and area of all of them (and damage), Al'Kabor looked like it was even more awesome. So they gave it a different cost based on whim or a complex formula.

The problem is, IN PLAY they end up being useless. Not nearly useless, not kind of annoying and limited... useless. The only time any Wizard uses these spells is out of ignorance or against very green things. That is it. This uniform, unanimous contempt for the spell line and as a result the "mightiest Wizard in Norrath's history" Al himself are truly stunning in light of the fact that we are told repeatedly that these spells are fine as is. They are NOT fine.
