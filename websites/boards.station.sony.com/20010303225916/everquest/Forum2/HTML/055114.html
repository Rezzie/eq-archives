Hello again.

This will be the thread for discussion about the rogue class. The idea is to look at the things that need to be changed to make the class 'balanced'. With that as the primary goal, I am still interested in hearing about bugs and other such things. But, primarily, I want to talk about balance.

My take on the rogue is pretty similar to that of the warrior. They are pretty well balanced. Obviously with EverQuest as a primarily player vs. environment game, some of the things that thieves traditionally do are not really part of the package. That's why, in EverQuest, they are titled rogues.

Again, I have had little time (actually, no time at all) to do research on rogue concerns before starting this thread. So please be as detailed as you need to be to get your point across. But, at the same time, please remember that this thread will get long. So being concise and readable is important too.

I do want to report one thing that we have already discovered internally about rogues that is broken and will be fixed when we make these changes. Yes, it is a nerf, but not in the true sense of the word.

In the old days, NPCs had a limited size to their hate-list (basically the way that they track who they are angry at). In those days, evade would just drop you to the bottom of that list. Recently we increased the size of that list dramatically. This allows us to do a lot more with NPCs. But evade was never changed to reflect this, so it still drops you to the bottom of the hate-list. Essentially, this allows a rogue to do significant damage to an opponent, and then with the use of evade, become less 'hated' than someone that has done no damage to the NPC at all, but just has a somewhat negative faction with that NPC and is standing someplace on the other side of the zone. The rogue can then beat the NPC up with impunity. Basically, evade is far too useful for avoiding aggro from an NPC, and we're going to change it to be more in line with its original function.

I wanted to let you know that at the outset. Please don't make it the focus of this thread. It’s certainly something we should talk about. But there are certainly other things that are worth discussion as well.

So let's hear what you think.

Alan

I will try to analyse rogue class in view of balancing its power with
regard to other existing class, particularly pure and hybrid melee.
Melee basis is the warrior class with following abilities: tanking, high
melee damage, critical melee damage, high skill in every weapon, vast
choice of weapon and combat oriented skills. Monks trade tanking
ability, combat skills, weapon choice for secondary abilities like
healing (mend), scouting (sneak, safe fall, traps, locks) and spell like
effects (feign death, instill doubt). Rogues trade tanking ability,
combat skills, weapon choice and critical hits for secondary abilities
like scouting (sneak, hide, safe fall, traps, locks, pickpocket), spell
effects (fear, poison), extra damage from behind and critical damage
with throwing weapons. I will try to be "honest" in my analysis,
stressing abilities that should be ameliorated along with those that
need nerf (that mean I will search balance in class rather than mere
additionnal power). My experience of playing rogue come from Felyndira
a 35 lvl Teir'Dal Redemned Agnostic rogue member of the Amazonian
Sisterhood on Bertox, I do not get experience on high level rogue
issues. Also... forgive my english, as it is not my mother tongue.

Scouting:
Scouting is with melee damage the most important ability of the rogue
class. Rogue got the ability to sneak and hide for invisibility effect
against both living and undeads with two counterparts: be obliged to
move very slowly and being limited to mobs that do not see invisible.
Issues on this topic are to avoid un-hiding after zoning (similarly to
invisibility spells). A new skill for the rogue could also be
implemented to help the rogue by-pass situation with mobs that see
invisible: ID could be splitted into 2 skills: ID during fight for a
fear effect, and intimidation in non combat situation for effect faction
improvement or aggro. This would put the rogue on an even state with the
enchanter with regard to scouting capacity (enchanter got illusions,
invisibility and cooperation). Another issue concerning Hide and
looting. Please make an option to have a YES/NO box with the message
"are you sure you want to loot this corpse and break your hide?" (this
will avoid me behing killed while scouting when I unvoluntary clic on a
corpse), but never ever allow that looting dont break hide (that would
be cautionning ninja-looting).

Ability to deal melee damage:
This ability is shared by pure and semi melee class (warrior, rogue,
monk, ranger, paladin, SK): make damage on close combat range. Weapon
choice, Special attacks (crits, backstab, flying kick), Offense, Melee
weapons skills, Dual wield, Double Strike, Spells, Spell-like abilities
(poison, harm touch) influence this ability. In a group situation, rogue
is the undisputed best melee damage dealer. With regard to backstab,
what about allowing backstab with 1HB weapon, for NO damage but a
push/stun effect in relation with the base damage of the weapon. That
would give the rogue a definitive chance of interupting casting, to the
expand of damage. That would also favorise the use of 1HB (every rogue I
know use piercing, a majority also use 1HS but most didnt bother to
raise Hand to Hand and 1HB skills). Also regarding backstab, give a
message when backstab fire (like warrior critical), I am frustrated when
groupmates congratulate warrior for his nice 95 crit when I just
backstabbed for 115 (not a major issue but it could help some players
reconsidering usefulness of a rogue in a group, ... it would also be
sweet for my ego). I also have an interrogation on high end weapons:
ragebringer got a ratio of 0.75 with a base damage of 15. Willsaper, the
second best rogue weapon is 0.65, damage 13. There is a huge gap betwen
these weapons, both in term of ratio and base damage. Was this done on
purpose to make a significant difference between weapons and THE epic
weapon or could the choice of weapon for a rogue be increased by
introducing weapons with a base damage of 14.

Ability to tank:
This is the ability to take/avoid damage during a melee fight. It depend
on several parameters like HP, AC (available armors), Defense, Dodge,
Parry, Riposte, Disciplines (post 50). Rogue got an "average" capacity
to tank, worst than warrior, paladin, shadow knight, about even with
bard, monk, and rangers and better than healers and casters. From my
experience, tanking ability of the rogue is at it should be.

Ability to evade:
I remember when I played Felyndira before evade skill was implemented
... Evade is central for both rogue defense and attack, please do not
nerf it.

Ranged weapons:
Ranged weapon choice, Critical hits, Ranged weapon skill influence this
ablitity. Rogue got a poor capacity to inflict damage from distance,
even considering critical and crippling blow on throwing weapons. For
pure melee, ranged weapons are merely used to pull, not to make damage,
therefore, range rather than damage is the important thing to consider
(at least from my point of view). I would like to see a new line of
ranged weapon made on the model of bow/arrow to be implemented for
throwing weapons. This could be Sling/bullets (using existing tailoring
skill for making sling and pottery and/or smithing for making stackable
bullets) or Propulsor/javelin (implementing a new skill similar to
fletchery). Throwing weapon with better range and damage need to be
implemented. Other ideas of "exotic" throwing weapon are a boomerang
that would return in ranged slot after use (negate the need to buy
throwing weapons), tailored net (a short range, heavy, unstackable
weapon that deal no damage but root the opponent), bolas (weapon with a
chance to proceed snare).

Ability to heal self:
Monk get the best pure melee self healing capacity (bandage and mend),
followed by warrior (bandage), and rogue (bandage capped low). Monks
get this ability balanced by having reduced scouting capacity compared
with the rogue (lacking hide, traps/locks skills capped low). Increasing
this ability would favorise soloing, which I guess is not something
wanted.

Ability to feign death (FD):
Rogue dont have an "escape way" (like a gate spell or Feign death). On
the other hand, rogue are master scouts, and therefore have the
potential to avoid situation where an escape way is needed (Felyndira
always hide while waiting a pull and never engage a fight if she
consider the group cannot handle the situation. Just make it clear to
your groupmates before fight to avoid later flaming when everybody but
you died ... also they will be happy that you survived for corpse
recovery). Moreover, the Feign death ability of the monk is balanced
with the ability to make/apply poison of the rogue, therefore giving FD
to the rogue would unbalance relative power of rogue with monk. Monk FD
is a skill resulting in a single spell effect (fear) and not restricted
in its use (fast re-use delay, no mana use, no component). Rogue
make/poison skill, on the other hand, require long/expensive preparation
(very restricted use) but offer more versatility in effects (16
different spells effects). For this reason, I dont think giving to the
rogue unrestricted use of FD under the form of a skill (evade out of
line of sight, FD skill) would be a good idea. Alternatively, FD
potion (all/all) made using make poison skill (rogue) and/or alchemy
skill (shaman) could be introduced. With regard to FD potion, similar
effect (escape way) already exist with "gate potions" (from merchant,
drop or shamans), with the drawback of being moved to bind point.

Stackable poisons:
Rogue is a spell caster with serious limitations. Poison is a spell. As
it stand, the limitations are SO serious that most rogues dont use
poison at all, or only use it to a small extand. Also a tiny number of
twinks overuse/abuse poison use because the limitation on poison use do
not apply to them (due to "artificial" unlimited cash). I want to be
clear, I dont wish a rogue could use poison every single fight he is
involved in. Poison is for "special occasions" only and this is fair. I
only wish rogue have a fair chance to use any poison, at a given moment
where/when he need it.

16 different type of poisons are available to the rogue. Range of effect
is interesting, including DD, Dot, STR debuff, trade off debuff, trade
off dot, stun, manadrain, blind, snare, slow, root. Only remark concerns
damage poisons, which are fairly weak (at high level) when compared
with true damage spells (my guess is that high level rogue (post 50)
dont use damage poison anymore but favor debuff poisons). Every type of
poison should be interesting from level 20 to 60.

A rogue must "search" every single spell he use (collecting/buying
ingredients, preparing poison). This is THE limitation against rogues
having piles of poison ... if a rogue gets tons of poison... he invested
a lot of time/money in them and merit them. Some poison ingredients can
only be bough, I would like that every poison component drop, some could
still be bough but at least a broke rogue would have a chance to use
make these poisons. I would also like to be able to produce suspension
using a trade skill (brewery for instance). Overall give the possibility
to have cheaper poisons if more time is invested in collecting/preparing
poison. Do not sweeten the difficulty to prepare poison as this is
central for balancing the skill, reduce ingredient drop rate, increase
price on sold ingredients (or even remove all sold poison ingredients
and make them drop) if necessary. I think that poison preparation is
already long/expensive enough to limit the use of poison (and therefore
balance the skill).

A rogue must memorize a given spell every time he cast it (apply
poison), to the difference of a caster who must only memorize it once.
This is THE limitation against chain casting, nerf it if you consider
that necessary (increase delay). Taking into consideration the
difficulty to obtain poison (provided you are not a twink with unlimited
cash), I dont consider chain casting poison to be unbalancing: during
two minuts you are going to rock, but this did require hours of
preparations...do not only consider the use of poison alone, always have
in mind the preparation process attached.

A rogue can only have one spell memorized at a time (only primary slot
can be poisoned). By comparison, a caster can memorize 8 different spell
at any time. I would like the rogue to be able to "memorize" 3 spell at
a time, which is still well behind a caster, by giving the possibility
to poison off hand slot and ranged slot. (keep the limitation to
piercing/throwing weapons).

A rogue do not have a spell book, he must cast every spell directly from
the "scroll", that also mean he need a "scroll" (poison) for every cast.
Keep this as it stand: do not allow a single apply to last for any more
than one cast... that would be equivalent to give caster several cast
for one time the mana cost and would remove the preparation burden on
poison use.

Rogue "scroll" do not stack. Change this, the only limitation this
imply, concern the array of spell a rogue can carry with him. Let me
devellop the point: In practice rogues dont carry already prepared
poisons of each type and run in bank to refill poison bags every time a
poison spell is used. Every poisoner rogue I know carry stacks of
ingredients and combine them on the field. Making poison on the field
require you to carry mortar and pestle (only 6 slot container, vs a
potentially 10 slot container), vials (generally 1 or 2 different type),
suspensions (also generally 1 or 2 type) and ingredients (generally 2
per poison type). This means a rogue who want to use poison to full
potency (be able to apply any poison type at a given instant on the
field), need about 36 inventory slots to be filled with poison stuffs
(and lose 4 slot due to 6 slot container). When you think that 80
inventory slots are available at maximum that mean a rogue must
sacrifice 50% of his inventory slot to be able to use efficiently
poison: No sensible player would do that. Due to this most rogue only
carry with them ingredients for a very limited number of poison type
(personnaly I carry 5 at max). Of course this situation is acceptable if
you plan to raid a place where you know which mob/situation you will
encounter. But in the case of a travelling rogue (what I mostly play),
you can be pretty sure that when the ultra rare mob spawn, you will not
have with you the specific poison that would be usefull against that mob
on you (Murphy law). Make poison stackable, that way a rogue could carry
every type of poison (that would still consume 16 inventory slot... but
it would be manageable) and would not need to run to bank every time he
cast a spell. Alternatively if stackable poison is still considered
unbalancing (and I think it is not), only poisons which require dropped
ingredients could be stacked (this would give a supplementary
restriction on stacked poisons... you MUST spent time to collect them),
restricting twink rogue with huge amount of money to have access to
unlimited supply of poison (buying all required ingredients).

Also I am very surprised that past the initial 20 points, unlike others
trade skills, make poison and apply poison can still be raised with
practice points with a guild master. Nerf this as it is fairly
easy/cheap to raise skill that way when compared to raising skill
making/applying poisons.

With this topic, I would like to stress the point that every rogue is
not a twink and that nerfing the skill to limit the possibility of a
twink to abuse it MAY be equivalent todenie the access to the skill to
any non twinked character. In my opinion, the key to balance this skill
and reduce/remove twink abuse is to make poison preparation hard enough
to limit the use of poison, along with limited restrictions on use. As
it stand poison making require more dedication in time/money than any
other skill (jewelry, alchemy and smithing are time consuming but all
ingredients can be bought) and therefore very effectively limit poison
use. As it stand, raising making/apply poison skill is too easy (using
practice points with guild master). At it stand, poison use, is
over-restricted: restriction in poison availablity, necessity to
reapply, unstackable poison, primary hand poisoning only. Whereas
restrictions on poison supply and re-apply should be maintained (maybe
even be reinforced), restrictions on unstackable poison and primary slot
poisoning only should be dropped.

Apply poison: I have read some post proposing to give apply/poison skill
to necro and SK, arguing that this would help making poison making
profitable. Both already are casters or semi caster and do not need it.
Make/Poison skill is an expensive skill on purpose, to balance the
spell casting ability given to a pure melee class. Moreover you can
already make cash out of it selling poisons to impatient lvl 18-19
rogues (tiny market) and making dyes for tainting fine plate (small
market).

Instill doubt: both monks and rogues share this ability. Whereas this
skill use should always be unreliable, the chance to proceed/resist
could be modified to have this skill work sometime on things other than
green con. Also, why cannot we backstab after a successful ID? this make
no sense and remove a big part of the usefullness of the skill.

Disarm: this skill need to be modified. Give a better chance to disarm,
allow to disarm off-hand weapon and allow to disarm magical weapons,
along with nerfing the skill so that disarmed weapon is returned to
target inventory. This will completely negate the ability to use this
skill for "ninja-looting" and will improve the usefulness and
effectiveness of disarm.

Pickpocket: this skill need to be modified because it make rogues
un-welcomed in groups even when you do not use it. Pickpocket is a
roguish skill I would not like to see dropped. What about having a
separate pocket for pickpocket? (that would not be detrimental to other
players, but another rogue). Or what about sharing pickpocketed cash
with group members, and sending message to groupmates when items are
stolen (similarly to regular looting).

Safe fall: good skill, but I would like that mobs also suffer falling
damage, that would give a tactical/offensive use to this skill. Also I
am of the opinion that falling damage should stun. Also nothing to do
with rogue balance but please fix the bug in Crystal Cavern that make
you receive 10000 damage if you fall in the hole from tunel near
entrance, and only 200-500 if you fall in the same hole from the stone
bridge.

Tracking: I would like to see this skill implemented on the rogue as it
would nicely complete the array of skill a rogue already possess while
fitting the "spirit" of the class. After all, a rogue is supposed to
have access to informations, spies who can help him find someone,
specially in cities and dungeons. In order to limit infringement on
ranger/druid domain, I would give a restricted use (city/dungeons only)
and possibly a lower cap in skill. Also I am not sure of this but if
bard got tracking, I see no reason why rogues should not have it.

Find/Remove traps: Thank for implementing (in the end) a use for these
skills with SoV. Didnt had the opportunity to use them yet.

Picklocks: Nothing of note on this skill. SoV and Kunark introduced
many doors to use this skill.

Trade skills: This subjet concern every class. As a general rule, trade
skills are difficult/long/expensive to raise, depending on the
usefulness of the skill. At low level, trade skill can be a good way for
a "newbie" to honestly make some money (making/selling patchwork armor,
metal studs...), and I am really in favor of this use of trade skills.
Under normal game condition a trade skill will increase regularly as the
character gain levels and got access to sufficient amount of cash to buy
the needed ingredients and practice. A problem arise with "twinks" who
master trade skills at level 1. I am in favor of capping every trade
skills with level after the initial training by Guild master (Lvl+1) x5
. Trivial skill should also be modified to suit the skill progression
(having silver jewelry become trivial at level when "enchant gold" spell
become available to enchanters, having a reasonable chance of making
poison vials with a skill of 105 in pottery (obtainable at level 20)
when make poison skill become available to a rogue.

Grouping: Hybrids were carrying the burden of being "experience eater"
and had difficulties grouping because of this, it has been modified so
they dont suffer this disandvantage anymore. Rogues have difficulty to
group because he possess several player-unfriendly skills (disarm,
pickpocket) with the potential to reduce loot amount for groupmate
and/or ninja-looting. Modify these skill so that they are not anymore
damaging to other players, or rogue will keep having difficulties to
find a group due to game mechanic, which is completely unfair
(specially when we consider the capacity of the rogue to solo). I am
concious that nerfing playing unfriendly skills is completely
anti-roleplaying and that stealing from silly groupmates/players is
probably the most enjoyeable part of playing a rogue, but it must be
done as it does not fit the "spirit" of the game as Verant design it
(play nice policy, play in group, interdependancy of players).
Roleplaying an evil rogue (Btw following the character presentation of
a Teir'Dal rogue worshipping Innoruuk or Bristlebane to the letter) will
get you banned. Everquest is not a roleplaying game specially if you
play an evil character, live with that or quit playing.

Overall the rogue class dont need tremendous changes in order to get
balanced, as most of the job was already done (I remember when I started
playing Felyndira in dec 1999). Should the suggestions I made be
implemented, I would be completely satisfied with the balance of the
rogue class.

PS: Most important, I wish that rogue get enhanced backstab damage (x10
instead of x5) and that backsatb work from the front also with off hand,
that double strike also apply to backstab (for duoble backstab), that
you introduce piercing weapons for rogue with base damage of 20 or more,
that rogues got HP equivalent to warrior, armor with equivalent AC to
warriors (with reduced weight), that evade completely clear aggro, that
poison once applied last till you zone and proceed for each strike.
