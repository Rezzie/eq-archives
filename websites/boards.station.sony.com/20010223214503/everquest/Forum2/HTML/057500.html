That's because the tracking interface is somewhat badly designed. That is, even if you have a higher tacking skill (50) than the bard(25?) you will have a harder time to find things in zones when you tracking list is full. Actually, anything above 25 is bad when you run around in a fairly small zone trying to find an already spawned spawn.

This is because the tracking list isn't sorted after range from the tracker, but rather after the order they spawned in, in the zone.

That is, the tracking list is the list of the most recent spawns within your tracking range(range defined by skill).

This is great when you are looking for a spawn in the zone and the spawn has not yet spawned. Rangers have a huge advantage here since they can usually "see" almost the whole zone, and when something spawns it will be on top of their tracking list.

The downside is, that if you track "too many" things(the tracking list gets full) you will only see the 20(don't remember the length of the list) or so latest spawns, not the 20 or so closest.

This is not a bug with druid tracking, this is a design flaw in tracking(in the client, because they could easily add sorting after range, the information is there anyway)

The reason why the bard could "see" the spawn you couldn't was probably because the bard's tracking list wasn't full(or at least not as full as yours with spawns that spawned before the spawn you were looking for) so it could show up among the 20 most recent spawns within her tracking range.
