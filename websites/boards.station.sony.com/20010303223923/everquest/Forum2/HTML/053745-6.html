Client side machine speed - dependent on many factors also;
Connection speed or lag thereof (sorry, couldn't resist ) on both the client and server side;
Physical ability/disability such as hand/eye coordination; carpal tunnel syndrome (pre-existing or bard-created - remember, not all players are under 30 or physically fit, or just plain genetically lucky); or even a large variety of physical illness/handicaps, which could include Parkinson's, Cerebral Palsy, physical injury or disfigurement, etc.

Two points:

First, the new level 20 "Breeze" song. If you think of this as an independent move, then it makes good sense. Bards from 20 to 25 are pathetic, and this would give them a lever to get into groups. I can't fault that (although I can certainly fault the decision to give them the one ability bards don't want to be known for).

Main point: the new song does nothing, zero, zip, nada, to address any of the points raised in this thread as most critical to balancing the class. If you want to balance the class, you need to make bards able to contribute to groups at all levels, in ways that they currently cannot. There are a very limited number of ways you could achieve this:
1) give them buffs that are worth using in a balanced group
2) give them melee skills (and anti-taunt) that would let them function as effective damage-dealers
3) give them hit points and defensive skills that would allow them to tank effectively.
4) give them crowd control/debuff abilities that don't conflict with other, better classes

Personally, I favor #1 & #4. Allow bard haste songs to stack with haste spells; reverse the stacking order of enthrall-type spells, so that bards can't overwrite long-duration mezzes but CAN help control mobs who resist the enchanter; give the stat buff songs effects that are comparable to what an enchanter, shaman, druid or cleric (or at least a ranger or paladin!) could do if they were spending 1/4 of their mana pool just to maintain buffs.

Nothing else that you do for bards will improve their basic situation -- except, perhaps, to recalibrate the usefulness of resistances, so that highest-level bards can be useful on raids again. You can tweak the class, fix annoying bugs, even give us our drum animation -- but that won't help high-level bards be useful in groups that already have an enchanter or shaman, except in their already-stultifying role as group battery charger.

On Weak Levels:

I do not think 20-25 are weak levels at all. Bards are premier healers; clerics don't get a *real* heal spell until 24, and druids/shamans until 29. Bards are also great pullers, and crowd control with lullaby is pretty effective too (its not an AE mez, but it does allow you to melee at the same time). You also have a nice haste at this point... a good AE slow, and the regeneration is monster big.

A low level mana song is nice... but these are not a weak area.

----

The weak area for bards is 45+. This is mainly caused by a couple things:

1) Caster spells become more efficient.

A level 20 shaman struggles to do anything if he is buffing. A level 50 shaman can drop a ton of buffs, heal, and debuff.


2) Caster spells get better non-linearly.

A level 39/40 buff is generally more than twice as good as a level 19/20 buff. Bard songs at best progress linearly. Relative effectiveness drops behind over time.

3) An emphasis on multi-group tactics

Lots of dungeons are designed in such a way as to require multi-groups; where bard buffs are group only. This causes bards to be less useful than other, more flexible, casters.


4) Post 50, other classes get better non-linearly

Lets face it, a level 55 warrior is more than 10% more effective than a level 50 warrior. 10 level 50's warriors are not the equivilant of 9 level 55 warriors.

On the other hand, 10 level 50 bards are probably *better* than 9 level 55 bards. That's one more mana song to begin with... buffs and debuffs are about the same, and its one more guy hitting on something.

For example, my feeling is most raids would be indifferent between a level 50 bard and a level 55. They would not be indifferent between two characters of any other class.

SOLUTIONS:
- Have all bard songs take step increases at certain levels (40, 45, 50, 51-60) in addition to the current increases. This takes care of the non-linearity of other casters and should be designed to keep bards in the same position relative to other casters.

- Have 'combo' songs that reflect the fact that caster spells get more efficient. This is already the case with many of the 50+ songs... they just need to be modified to keep the resistances the same as the two individual effects. This is the equivilant of giving bards the ability to twist more songs.

SPECIFIC GRUDGES:
Ervaj - make these singing so bards can use weapons at the same time. The (a) loss of damage from a bard and (b) the opportunity cost of a loss of a different song, is just to high right now to make these useful.

Highsun/Midnight - make these ususable all of the time. They are good, but not overpowered in any way relative to other caster abilties. Having them useless 50% of the time makes them so unreliable as to be worthless.

Meditation - Please increase this as bards go up in level (maybe make it available at level 1, and increase 2 pts per level.)

-----

These should not be major changes... they should be on par with adjusting paladin/SK hitpoints to be better, but not as good as warriors.

For example:
If a bard haste is currently 30% at 50, and a enchanter is 50%... maybe the bard one should be moved to 40%. They don't stack, so no issues. No balance issues (chanter ones are still better). Groups with a chanter are still going to use the chanter ones (they are still clearly better); but bards are still more useful to groups.
