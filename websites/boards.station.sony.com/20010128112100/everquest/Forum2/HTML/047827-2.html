Player distribution is based on risk vs reward, correct? The game contents a component which allows players to modify the risk factor - the cleric. The formula for risk then becomes one of availability of the modifier device, and in EverQuest a component of this formula is distance.

Ensuring a distribution and spread of players, then, is going to require /ensuring/ the distribution of clerics. A zone where high-level, full-exp rezzes are available has a negligible risk factor, regardless of the rewards; Example, Karnors. A zone where obtaining even a low-level rez can be difficult if you didn't bring a cleric has an much higher risk factor; Examples, PermaFrost, Split Paw.

I'm based in the UK, my "peak" playing time is when the daily camps and spawns are just being set out - it's a very good time for a newly formed group to set out and hunt, because stuff is generally uncamped.

The distribution of clerics in the afternoon is the KEY factor to what will be camped and where people will be in the evening.

I've seen rants by rangers, paladins, druids and bards about starting up groups. It is hard for ANY class to start up a group without being able to say "Yes we have a cleric". People aren't interested.

Aradune, there is one little issue that should be considered when dealing with overcrowding.

"Balanced" distribution of players in every zone must take into account the average level of the players. Players do not de-level(well, not most of the times :P) and thus everyone is getting higher and higher in levels. This causes a HUGE influx of players to the high level zones, whereas the lower level zones will become underutilized.

The ratio of high to low level players need to be considered. The fact of the matter is, many high level players are making twinks simply because there aren't enough places to fight at higher levels. Velious as an expansion greatly enhances EQ as a whole, but Velious has one HUGE weakness and that is the fact that it can't be done decently without HUGE raid groups, as well as the faction problem(it's a great idea, but it limits the amount of players in certain dungeons, and that needs to be taken into account while considering server population).

It seems to me nowadays that most people are either concentrated in outdoor zones or Sebilis/Karnor/Lower Guk/Solusek B. Chardok and Howling Stones are underused high level zones that nevertheless become "packed" ONLY because Sebilis and Karnor is full. Kael Drakkel, Necropolis, and Skyshrine are often raided by high level guilds(to my knowledge), but the Hole, Siren's Grotto, and Temple of Veeshan are empty most of the times. The lack of good exp zones in Velious greatly hampers the "it should expand the amount of players per server" theory, in addition to the "more players, more lag" problem that already exists.

There are definite problems to the zones that are under utilized, and I'm afraid the solution of making exp go faster in them is not going to be, in the long term, the "best" solution, especially with zones that are perceived as great, balanced zones(Chardok, CoM, HS), but have serious problems that hamper players. Chardok: Lack of named spawns, lack of variety(there are mostly only two models in this zone: golems and sarnaks)

City of Mist: Lack of named spawns before reavers(which require raids not normal groups), trains involving bad pathing from mob from the sky, bad placement in the world(Emerald Jungle is a very difficult place to get to)

Howling Stones: Takes forever to get NORENT keys(requiring a rogue to do 90% of an exp dungeon is NOT a good idea), unbelievably large population of mobs, many sks, requiring more than one typical group, bad placement in the world requiring invis and hard-to-get key, bad faction for evils

Nurga: Lack of named spawns, too small a dungeon, lack of loot or incentives(but I think the exp bonus might change this)

Droga: Arrgh... Lack of spread out named spawns, lack of variety, too many casters, goblin swarm heaven(too hard for one appropriate leveled group), too hard to navigate

Kerra: The quests suck, overpowered mobs, lack of good loot(comparable loot to Sol A/CT would give lots of players incentive to come)

Permafrost: Goblin swarm galore, healers out of nowhere, too small of a level range

Solusek A: Too many farmers :P

I think Verant knows what makes a good exp dungeon. Aradune,

I still think you're missing the point about underused dungeons. 10-20% exp modifier increases isn't suddenly going shift populations into under-used zones. The under-used zones are simply badly-designed from a gameplay point of view. The risk vs reward is simply not as good as the more popular areas, 10-20% exp increase isn't enough. You need to re-examine the design of these places.

Most of the listed places to get increased bonuses have a common trait of lots of fast-spawning mobs usually with a lot of casting mobs thrown in. This is a recipe for group-death and 10-20% exp bonus isn't going to off-set that. Neither is putting more items there really as they'll just get farmed by high level players. You just have to re-tune the dungeons and reduce the difficulty in a lot of them, especially places like the Hole which are way out of way and don't get pick-up groups.

I'm also very disappointed with the list of zones to be given additional bonuses. They are all low-mid level dungeons - why? Create yourself a lvl 52 char and spend a week trying to advance. You knew you had to give the increasingly-high level server population a fresh challenge and designed a lot of zones the are basically raid(guild)-only places. But at the same time you failed to realise that a significant portion of time for lvl 50+ players isn;t spent raiding, we need exp to get to 57+ where we can finally be useful in high level encounters. Only there is still basically only Karnor's to go so the increasingly large high-level population is crammed into the place.

They need to be balanced - that's not the same as making them easy.

There are several issues you need to consider if you are raising the population target on the servers:

- Item entry rates. Does it still make sense to have an item enter at the same rate as it does now, with 50%+ more people on the server? There's still only the same source of Journeyman's Boots (and a SoW potion with a casting time isn't the same thing). There's still only the same source of the FBSS (although you have added more haste items to the game, thank you).

- Big Game Hunt encounters. Does it still make sense to have these creatures spawn once a week when there are 50% (or in the case of some older servers, 100%) more people who want to hunt them?

- Server Demographics. A server target of 2200 people means a very different thing if 50% of the people logged in are over 45th level, than it does if only 25% of the people logged in are over 45th level. This relates to both item entry rates and big-game-hunt encounters.

- Encouraging social ties. While undercrowding may make it initially harder for your average person to get a group, it definitely encourages people to make friends and keep them.

I have the feeling you are basing a lot of your decisions on the recent poll asking "how do you usually play EQ". I answered, and I would suspect the plurality of people answered "In a group of people that I do not know." Please allow me to clarify that.

Most of the time I'm playing, I don't know most of my group. But I remember people I group with, and often times I am invited and/or invite someone to my group on the sole basis that I grouped with them before and had fun. So while several of the people in my group are "whoever happened to be there", I usually have friends in my groups. And if I don't start with friends in my group, by the time I leave, I've usually made at least one--isn't that why we are playing a social game?
