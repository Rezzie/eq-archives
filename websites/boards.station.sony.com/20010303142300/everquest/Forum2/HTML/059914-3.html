Absor,

It is important to immediately grasp that PvP in EQ is different, fundamentally so, depending on whether you are playing on the teams servers or RZ. The starting rules are different on each server, and so the problems are different. You will hear from the players on TZ and VZ much unhappiness about crossteaming while that on RZ will be mainly about item loss. Different rulesets lead to different priorities. I'll try to just hit the general comments.

PvP in EQ has several general shortfalls. The first is the melee/caster problem. Melee classes require close quarters to hit their opponents, casters do not. Consequently, casters have a much higher damage potential in a situation where latency is a factor. Consider that a small amount of lag combined with the predictive displaying of position built into EQ causes the melee player to be out of melee range with his opponent, while the caster is not so constrained. Consequently, even though in a duel between a caster and a melee the fight might be close, in an unplanned PvP situation, the melee class is almost always at a disadvantage.

The second general problem revolves around targetting. It is understood that Verant has chosen not to make LOS a necessity for casters in PvP, even though it does require it in PvE. This, unfortunately, leads to quite distressing problems for melee classes. Casting through walls, completely out of line of sight and range of any possible retribution, makes casters again supreme.

Gate, in and of itself, is a massively unbalancing PvP spell. The ability to remove yourself from danger is granted to some classes and not others. It also happens that those classes are the ones that are most able to deal damage in PvP most effectively and are least reliant on the equipment they could possibly lose.

The ever increasing power of casters in PvE does not scale properly in PvP. DD Spells which are able to do 600 HP of damage and pets that can out melee tank classes do not compare properly versus classes that are hitting for 60 points a hit. Pet classes are very unbalancing to pure classes that lack the ability to root/snare/fear. Channeling skills in the 200s means channelling gate spells through hasted melee classes with 20 delay weapons duel wielded. The extra 30 HP gained by a melee class does not compare well to the added power of the next line of spells.

The ability of almost all casters to selfbuff, either with a damage shield or a HP buffer/barrier of somesort, places a huge burden on melee classes to become dependent on dispelling items. As an example, attempting to fight a caster with the skin or rune line up requires spending multiple seconds repeatedly pumicing buffs, all the while absorbing damaging spells, before actually being able to damage the caster. When comparing the effectiveness of classes in PvP, do not compare fully buffed x vs fully buffed y. Episodes like that happen rarely. More often than not, you should compare fully buffed x vs unbuffed (pumice) or partially buffed (PvE setup) y, as one side is prepared for PvP, while the other is not.

Lack of clear, enforceable guidelines in PvP have led to multiple standards being enforced, ranging from no enforcement, to rigid enforcement, to enforcement of only some standards. A good example is "loot and scoot", an action that is not codified, is enforced rarely and only through direct GM intervention. As stated earlier, there can never be too many Guides/GMs on a server where the main problem will be interplayer, rather than mechanical.


Problems specific to RZ fall mainly in the risk vs. reward category. The risk to any class requiring gear to operate at any level of effectiveness is out of proportion to those classes requiring little to no gear. For example, most pet classes and classes that can dot/root require little in the way of items to allow them to garner xp. Melee classes, on the other hand, do not share such a luxury. Naked casters regularly farm geared melee classes who are attempting to get XP with no potential loss to themselves, and are still able to get XP with no potential losses, assuming they were attacked.

Personally, I think PvP mechanics broken beyond repair in EQ. The balances in the game are designed for PvE and PvP is an afterthought. So be it. Instead, the way to fix PvP is totally through personnel. Increase your guide/gm staff, get clear protocols and prosecute the people who regularly break your posted guidelines. This will remove the worst aspects of PvP (zone killing, root/training, corpse camps).

How to fix "delevelers":

I have been part of the management of a pk based MUD, there are easy ways to program fixes for delevelers.

Quite simply right now damage is based on your skill in that area, for example backstab is based on the skill of around 100 (if maxed) for a level 20 character. If that person delevels down to level 10, they still have a skill of 100 in backstab, therefore they are still having their damage output based on their skill of 100.

Fix option 1:
Make it so that when you lose a level you automatically go back to the max skill level for the new level. For example if I am level 15 with a 1hs skill of 75, I die, lose a level, drop my 1hs skill to 70. If clerics can't use their spells for level 15 if they are level 14, why should warriors be able to use their level 15 skills at level 14? If in the above example, my 1hs skill was 50, I drop to level 14, my 1hs skill would remain at 50.

Fix option 2: (preferred IMHO)
Have the damage calculation tables provide a simple "if" statement when determining damage output. IF skill>max_skill_at_level, then damage=max_skill_at_level * modifier, ELSE damage=skill * modifier. I do not believe this would be that difficult to implement.

At last, an official PvP thread.

Firstly, I'd like to say that I'm impressed with the quality of the discussion so far - much more civilised than on threads of this sort in the past.

Anyway, here are my thoughts from the perpective of a 50 necro on Rallos who has played there since release.

Loot rules:
As has been pointed out already, item loot causes all sorts of problems on Rallos. It's really just a symptom, however. If PvP wasn't so imbalanced, if there was actual risk involved for all parties involved in a typical PvP encounter, there wouldn't be so much of a problem. As it stands, however, it is far too easy to be caught in a bad spot and ganked. You lose your best item before you even know what has happened.

This is not fun, except for the gankers. The gankee has just had several hours (possibly days) of time stolen from him - the time it took to get the item in the first place, and the time it will take to replace.

Something needs to be done about item loot. Either change it in some way (either go coin-only and remove the main element of risk, or some other option which retains risk, but levels the playing field), or properly balance PvP so that with the current item loot rules everyone has a fair chance to survive a gank attempt.

On a related topic, the current way that loot rights are awarded is untenable. As it stands now, I can suffer 99% damage to PvE, but if a PK whacks me for the remaining 1% of my life he gets to loot. This is unfair, and should be fixed.

Perhaps if you die to PvP damage and they didn't take 51% or more of your life your corpse is unlootable?

Other comments on mechanics:

AE spells: useless on Rallos, as pets and party members are affected by them.

Solution: make party members and their pets immune to AE spells.

Pets: pets in PvP will defend themselves if attacked by their masters. They also cease to be pets, so they don't obey /pet commands anymore. This is not so on blue servers, why is it this way on PvP? This makes it suicide for any pet caster to use an AE spell near his or her pet, as it will cause the pet to rebel.

Portal camping/zone camping: even though this is expressly forbidden, it's also common practice. This is especially bad outside dungeons, as folks will zone out in full gear and low on health. Boom, one cast, thanks for the SMR.

Solution: make players immune to PvP for a set time after they zone or make a no-PvP zone around druid/wiz rings and zone lines so that you're safe there.

Perhaps implement a "in pvp mode" flag that will turn on if you attack/are attacked by another player which would not allow you to exploit these safe areas. This flag would reset when either you or the other player died, or after a certain time had elapsed.

Faction: there was a poorly conceived faction system implemented on RZ some time ago, and it was removed. The reasoning was good, the implementation wasn't. The problem is that there needs to be consequences for PKing. Faction would work well here, if implemented properly. The problem is that there is no way in the game to judge intent - the game doesn't know if I'm killing someone because I want that shiny new breastplate they're wearing, or because I'm defending my friend, or because I'm revenge killing them. If there was a way to help determine intent, faction hits would be great (or some other punishment). This is especially important for same-race PKs.

Duels: in a consentual, formal duel guards will attack the players if they like either of them (or both). Guards should not interfere with duels.

Newbie PKs: someone posted a little while back saying that newbies should not be PvP+ until level 5 or so. Hi Absor

There are two problems that concern the Tallon Zek community (as evidenced by frequent posts on TZ Times).

The first is the Invulnerable Healing exploit. Please note I am not talking about Out of Range Healing, since this is really an issue that can be resolved by bringing in others who are in range of the OOR healer and can then battle him.

Tallon Zek has historically been a Good vs Evil server. The Human, Elf, and the Short team has been battling the Dark team without many problems. Recently however, an uber-guild has formed on the server which accepts members of all four teams. This has, how shall I put it, basically screwed over all the other guilds on the server as they are now completely powerless to compete with this uber-guild. If for example, your guild, which consists of all Darks, tries to fight this uber-guild, they will be met with a force which includes Dark clerics. Your guild will be defeated because they will be unable to touch the Dark clerics, which will stand back with impunity and heal all their comrades. I have seen this guild in action, and let me tell you, they are truly unstoppable. The only, the ONLY answer to this is to cross-team to fight them, in essence forsaking the entire server history and the charters for many a guild.

Some have been proposing to hard code against buffing cross teams. This would however destroy almost all Light guilds, who have historically been united in their struggle against the Dark team. I support a hard coding, but only as it applies to the Good vs Evil concept. Please make it impossible for the Dark team to cast beneficial spells on any of the other teams and vice versa. This would restore the historical GvE style of play common to this server, and make skill, not the use of an exploit, as a measure of power.

The second issue is the dreaded Uber Guards. Some time ago, all the newbie guards were uber-buffed and given insane resistances in order to prevent high level players from farming these guards and thus leaving newbies defenceless. The motive behind this 'fix' was good, but the implementation has severely hurt PvP play. The Guards now make it completely impossible to perform any sort of city raid. They are also frequently trained at anyone on another team, with very disagreeable results. Any one of these guards is probably very capable of taking on Naggy or Vox all by his lonesome. I kid you not.
I propose that in order to alleviate this, the guards be returned to their former pre-uber status, with the following modification: make the guards like merchants - no loot and no exp. This will eliminate any reason to farm them, but will allow city raids and PvP play in zones which are populated by such guards.
