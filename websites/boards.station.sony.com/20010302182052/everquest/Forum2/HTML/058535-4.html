I already see what kind of thread this is - the poster makes a statement and some folks disagree, then the poster flames all who disagree. Nevertheless, I'll reply (even in a non-flaming manner).

"The character is meditating, not the player. When the character takes damage, the player is not hurt. When the character gets hungry, the player's stomach doesn't grumble. When the character is fighting, the player can even pan around and admire the scenery."

When the character gets hungry, the player has to buy more food; the character won't do it himself. If the character takes enough damage and dies, the player has to run back to the corpse/loot/mem spells. When the character is fighting, and the player isn't paying attention (instead, he's admiring the scenery), the character will most likely not be fighting to his full potential.

Like it or not, there is a correlation between the character and player.

-

There are also rewards for leveling. Long ago, as a Paladin, my reward for leveling to 9 was a new set of spells. Now, you could argue that it made NO sense for me to get these at level 9; instead I should get them at level 1. I am paying my $9.89 a month, after all! I was at a serious disadvantage prior to having Light Healing and a buff or two - I had to run to the zone or use Lay Hands on myself when I was low on health. This was extremely time consuming and hampered levels
1-8.

Like it or not, this is a game where the abilities of your character increase as you gain levels. The skills/features (or lack thereof) of a character are based on level and class. Why can't a druid Track until level 20? Why can't casters bind at level 5? Why can't my Paladin cast a spell to increase his speed (a la Spirit of Wolf)?

Instead of looking at it that way; try looking forward to level 35 as "the level where I don't have to look at the book anymore".
