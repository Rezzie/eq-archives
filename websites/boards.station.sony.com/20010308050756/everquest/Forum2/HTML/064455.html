That is a bass-ackwards policy that frankly does more to hurt us normal folks than deter the exploiters.

Let me tell you a story about a guy I used to work with, we'll call him Jeff. (his real name) Jeff was a guy with no morals. Jeff had put an old, outdated tape drive in the box of a brand new DAT and the clerk just couldn't believe that's what was in there. He called the store manager over, who couldn't believe it either. But Jeff kept insiting that this crummy little tape drive came in the shiny new box. Ya know what? He got his money back.

Even though the store clerk and the manage were *sure* that he was wrong, they gave it to him anyway. They didn't want to take the chance that he might be a customer with a legitimate complaint and not some sort of scam artist. So the store ate the $400 cost of the new tape drive to make him happy. That's customer service.

$400 is a drop in the bucket to a big national chain like Office Depot, but it still affects their bottom line. What would respawning the dragon have cost VI if it turned out that someone was trying to scam them? Not a damn thing. It's all just bits and bytes in a computer. Instead, they choose to tick off their customers on the off chance that one of them might be a cheater.

Verant, we are not the enemy.
