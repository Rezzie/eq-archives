First off, I'm impressed. I'm a 49th enchanter who likes to solo because I almost always get better experience this way.

If I kill 1 mob every 5 minutes (normal rate), that's 12 mobs an hour. A full group would have to kill 72 mobs minus 7 mobs (10% bonus), or 65 mobs an hour. That's 55 seconds per mob, sustained without pause. Good luck. Then factor in xp penalty classes in the game pulling an additional 5-10% of my xp per kill, my grouping bonus was just barely cancelling that out. So I got better xp solo or partnered, and grouped for entertainment or to aleve solo boredom.

Intelligence casters like myself will receive 10% xp tacked onto their xp/kill group award. Snooches.

Monks will receive 20% xp/kill tacked on in group. Boochies.

Hybrids will be receiving 40% xp/kill tacked on in group. Sweet.

NOTE to Hybrids - My 'chanting butt ate that xp penalty right along side you for those levels, so don't act like you're the only one who suffered.

Now... those on the 1.0 modifier (Wisdom casters if I recall) see no change to their xp/kill. No problem.

EVERYONE who ever grouped with an experience penalized class no longer suffers for it. Great.

Now for my questions I have: I would greatly like to see an Abashi/Absor reply to this as I hope to take advantage of full disclosure here. These are issues likely thought of, but I want to know what is and isn't being changed:

1. Warriors and Rogues have 10% xp bonus, because they require 10% less xp. Halflings require 5% less xp because of their races. Will they be receiving and xp "penalty" in group to keep them from shooting ahead of group members? This seems justifiable.

2. Races that have an xp penalty (5% for Barbarian, 15% for Ogre, 20% for Troll/Iksar) will fall behind group members. It's not a "small impact". 20% means that in 5 levels advanced with a non-penalty partner, they will have advanced 6 levels. Granted, being level 45 when they are 50 isn't a HUGE disparagement, as you will not be ungroupable. How is this being viewed?

3. Solo fighting hasn't been mentioned. While EQ is group-oriented, it's acknowledged that many solo do to various desires. Will this "xp out of the air" apply to solo as well? I'm under the impression the answer is no. That means that my dark elf necromancer will require 15% more xp than the halfling druid over there soloing. The halfling warrior will level MUCH faster than the troll shadowknight while soloing (very viable through level 20). That's a 15% bonus to xp table for the halfling warrior, and a 60% xp penalty for the troll shadowknight. So the question again, will the "free xp" be applied to ALL kills, or just grouped kills?

I understand the reasoning for just adding the "free xp". It stops the penalty dead in it's tracks and doesn't require recalculating levels. But if it's not going to apply to soloing, then hybrids are going to be FORCED to group to advance well.

4. Last question. As everyone is losing xp at death on a 1.0 scale now instead of 0.9 from warriors, and penalty classes will lose additional xp based on their race/class. Will halflings/warriors/rogues lose LESS xp? This seems like they should, even from a coding standpoint--it should likely be a blanket multiplier. (Level-1)(XP Factor) = XP lost.

The reason I mention this is the impact SOLOING will have. A dark elf necromancer is out soloing *gasp* and bites off more than he can chew, and feign death doesn't save him. He loses xp at a 1.1 factor based on the previous level. No problem--everyone is affected this way. Now he starts earning the xp back--SOLO, is he getting xp/kill on a 1.0 factor or 1.1? This is a GIANT penalty to traditionaly solo caster classes (necromancers especially).
