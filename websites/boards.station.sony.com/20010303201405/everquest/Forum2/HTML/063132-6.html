What "right" does the general public/customer base have to make any decision (or even give input) for any company? We are not talking democracy here folks, we are talking about a private company deciding what is best for them. If you want to get right down to it, Verant is a subsidiary of Sony, which is a Japanese company, a country where democracy is not the norm.

All companies make decisions on how to best service their customers, customers DO NOT DICTATE how a company will service them. To even feel in the slighest that you have that power, let alone that "right" is ludicrist. Companies offer support based on internal reasons alone, the bottom line is an internal reason by the way.

If Verant decides to only offer support and obtain feedback through the game interface/email/telephone, that is their "right" to do so. The only people who have any "right" what-so-ever to dictate how support is handled are the majority stockholders, of which I am guessing few if any players are.

The only way you as a customer have any sort of input to what Sony/Verant does it to vote with your pocketbook, that is what makes or breakls companies. If you are happy or gripe but keep paying them, they made the right decision. If you and many others cancel your account, they made a bad decision and may rethink it.

Feel free to gripe all you want, but until exercise the only power you have over what Sony/Verant does, they will feel they made the right decision. A customer is a customer happy or not, happy customers do bring extra benefits, but an unhappy one is still paying you.

That and how silly is it if someone asks you "Is EQ a good game?" and you answer "No, its all nerfed, messed up, etc..". Then the person asks "Are you are still playing?", and you answer "yes"... hehehe, kind of a mixed message wouldn't you say?
