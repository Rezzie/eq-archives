Abashi
Station Member 		posted 10-21-2000 05:28 PM           
It's a way for you to tell our server how fast you can accept data. The default setting of 5 is roughly where it should be for a 28.8K modem.

If you are running something faster than than, feel free to bump it up. If you start having system problems, getting knocked off, or anything of the sort, bump it down a notch.

I ran 9 on a 512K DSK connection without a problem. Your miliage may vary. Don't set it above 9 or below 3.

The main benefit of bumping it up is faster zoning, and less of a chance of getting kicked off in high-bandwidth encounters (such as dragon raids).

-Gordon

Don't do anything stupid.

In quake, there is a simmilar command. It's called "Rate". The normal rate is around 3000, this is for 56k users.

If you have a DSL line and set the rate to 10000, The server updates you more. You can acheive EXCELLENT ping rates because you can HANDLE more of them.

Now... say the 56k user does this...

The server will be sending TOO MUCH data. You can get INCREASED lag and INCREASED zone time because of this. At worst, it will increase the occurance of linkdeath and other nasty things.

So if you aren't on a "phat link", you really shouldn't try to overtax your line.

At the same token, setting the rate on a high speed line to 1000 is just stupid. USE the bandwidth, and bump that sucker up!

Something to note though... and this happened in some T1 quake servers as well... If too many people set their rates high, that increases server load. Server load gets too high, EVERYONE lags.

The solution quake had was to initiate rate caps... you can't go over 5k on some servers, for example.

I suspect this is why you can only go up to 9, and why I never get 30 ping in EQ like I do in quake

-Ayla
