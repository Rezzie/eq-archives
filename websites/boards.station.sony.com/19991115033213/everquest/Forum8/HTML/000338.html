Station Member 		posted 08-14-99 08:35 AM           
OK, a summary...

1.)
A forge looks like a smoking cylinder with glowing coals and a chimney, with bellows underneath. An Oven looks like a brick oven with a door on the front and an exhaust stack. I've never seen a kiln, as I haven't traveled much yet.
2.)
All you need to do sharpening is a rusty weapon and a sharpening stone. No water.
Any more advanced operations in the forge need one flask of storebought water (not foraged/summoned pod of water.) All items must be laid out singly, not stacked, i.e. 1 small chunck of ore, 1 small chunk of ore, one flask of water for Metal Bits. (not 2 chunks stacked, you'll just lose them.)
3.)
Unlike anything else I've seen, sharpening weapons has no failure message. If you succeed, you see "You've combined the items to produce something new!" If you fail, you see "You've combined the items to form an alternate product."
If you are sharpening weapons publicly to raise your skill, make sure you actually sharpen the item before handing it back. The sword will never break, just keep adding one more stone and combining until you get the "new!" message. At least in the Steamfonts, stones are cheap.
4.)
Because stones are cheap (4cp), they're the best way to raise your skill until about 20 (some have told me you can sharpen "large" weapons till 27 before it's trivial, not sure what constitutes a "large" weapon, maybe 2H.
I have, however, successfully made metal bits since practically the beginning, but since the materials cost more (1gp4sp for the 2 small chunks and 1sp2cp for the flask) they aren't as economical for raising your initial skill (your prices may vary on location, charisma and faction.)
5.)
Save your metal bits, you will use them for almost everything!
