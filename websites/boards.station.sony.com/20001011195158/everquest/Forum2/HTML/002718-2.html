The point is that certain classes are used for certain things. Necromancerss, Enchanters, and Bards are able to mez/charm indoor and outdoor (necro undead). Clerics, Paladins, and not to forget Druids, can do outdoor only. Thought not nearly as effective as the three mentioned before. FD is NOT intended to split spawns or be used in any pulling method. This is not what is was designed for, it was designed to SAVE the Monk period. Human adaptability allows us to figure out better ways of using FD, the controversial FD split pull, which I myself have employed alot. With the advent of certain patches this has become harder, such as making 35+ mobs virtualy impossible to forget you. Monks cling to FD pulling become of a slew of other issues they have, such as weapons which should not have been introduced so late. Hand to hand, one hand blunt, and 2 hand blunt should be viable options for a monk from 1 through level 60. Just as the same for Warrior in all his/her area of combat, cept hand to hand. This includes archery. Rangers, Paladins, Shadow Knights. Even Rogues, though admitidedly piercing is a must in primary hand. But viability is the key here. /shrug

There is historical precedent for the use of such a tactic, it was called a feint (same root, actually). By its use one could draw an enemy from a difficult position into an accessible one. Monks do the same thing, and it is not surprising that it has occured to players, since the concept is actually pretty well known. Divide and conquer; draw the enemy out; luring a rabbit from its hole; beating the brush to get the pheasant to take wing; etc etc...

Its attempting to maximize your position and minimize the enemies defenses. This is called tactics, and in Verant's eyes, this is wrong.

Some places are simply not doable without it. VI can blame themselves for creating dungeon encounters that resemble a killer-dm's wet dream.

I wonder what is more upsetting to Verant, that players are playing the game with more depth than smash and slash, or that the players have displayed more cleverness and intelligence than the designers have?
