With my chanter, I turned on /role just so I could avoid the across-the-zone tells for clarity. When I want to buff, I either set up a free buffing station and do /ooc announcements of the location or I announce that donations are entirely optional and amounts are up to the players. "Whatever you feel it's worth," is what I say when they ask how much they should give.

With all my toons, if I need a rez, port, etc., I either wait for a guildie who will be more willing to come to the rescue when he/she is free or I find someone in the zone who has NOT turned on /anon or /role and send them a tell. If I receive no response or a negative one, I give up or move on to another likely candidtate. If there are 3 or more of that class in the zone, I'll do an /ooc and offer to donate, which I do generously.

After my rogue got killed doing a quest in Neriak, I needed a high-level Dark Elf cleric to do rezzes. I did a /who dark cleric all and sent a few tells to people in PoK and Nexus only. As often as not, one of the first two people I sent tells to offered to come for the promise of a donation (or maybe they were just being nice). I would never have sent a tell to anyone in a fighting zone, and I always assume that people who are /role or /anon would prefer not to be bothered, even if I know they're the right class.

I try to help others, within the limits of my own play time, and I have found a moderate donation or useful buff will usually make it worthwhile for the average player to go a bit out of their way for me. Otherwise, I manage on my own.
