Illusions holding up after zone? Unless this is also being applied as a result of changes to echanter illusions, not a needed change. Range increase on thrown weapons? Excellent idea!

Poison on range items? Still wouldn't make me want to suffer the indignity of hours of greenie smashing, but would add something to poison usefulness.

Poison weapons for others? No. Enchanters should have first crack at anything like it. And we really don't need it. Besides it is not practical.

One idea I would like to put forth, is the idea of race/deity based skill branching. It may be impossible to implement in EQ, but I think it would be a great idea in a future VI project.

Race/Deity Skill Branching (RDBS) What is it? RDBS is a way to further define a charcter based on roleplaying choices made by the player at character generation. In this case, race/deity selection. This would allow for special skills/abilities/bonuses appropriate to the combo.


For example: We all know that the Scouts of Tunare are rogue woodelves or halfelves who worship Tunare. We also know that the the Black Hand are darkelf, rogue assassin-types. Two completely different ideas of what a rogue is. Why should their skills reflect it? Scouts in this case get a limited track and a slightly faster speed over land, while their dark cousins get a harm touch-esque (in function, not damage) deathblow skill. Just think of how much that would change the way you look at race/deity selection, more importantly it would help distinguish you from others of your class more. What about all those evil deity worshiping clerics too? Would be nice to see hard choices when picking a combo, and some benefit to those who go against the obvious picks.

Anywho, just a quick idea. I don't think you could really retro-fit something like that, but its a neat idea. Maybe there is a way to take that and turn into something that could function within EQ in a balanced and fair way.
