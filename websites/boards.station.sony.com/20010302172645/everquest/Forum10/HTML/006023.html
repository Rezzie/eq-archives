What do you mean by fast attack response time? Weapons each have a delay associated with the weapon, not with any particular class. Spells take time to cast, also dependent on the spell, not necessarily the class of the caster. If you mean able to do a large amount of damage very quickly, you may want a rogue or a monk, or a wizard. None of those really have the hit points to last long against 5 gnolls, though. If you want pure defensive capability (the ability to stand toe to toe with monsters and not flinch) you want a warrior. Warriors do a decent amount of damage, but not the most.

Sounds like you want a warrior or other melee class, but probably a warrior. One thing to keep in mind about those bandits and gnolls, they will be a challenge to you until about level 15 or 16 at the most. But those aren't a fair yardstick to use to judge a particular class.

Unlike a game like Diablo 2, where you can get fast attack equipment early in the game, Everquest doesn't really have an equivalent item at lower levels. What you are looking for are Haste items, and unless you are a twinked player, chances are extremely high that you won't see a haste item until those bandits and gnolls run away screaming from you rather than attack.

During the time you are fighting those monsters, you are learning how to play the character, and the character itself is learning how to use its weapons and skills. Rangers can cast snare to slow down the monster's running ability, but at the lower levels, the cool Slow spells aren't available, so you get to figure out how to use your strengths and weaknesses to your advantage.

I guess what I am saying is that I don't think any one class will give you all the things you want right away. Sure, you could get a mage or necro and send your pet in to fight multiple monsters at once, but when the pet dies, the monsters will come kill you rather quickly. As a warrior you can rely on your skills to fend off multiple attacks, but then again at about any level, its hard for any melee character to fend off multiple attackers at once.

Gee, I am getting long winded here. Sorry I can't seem to give better advice. If its any consolation, whichever class you pick will get better in time and soon enough you will go say hello to those bandits who will studiously ignore you, praying that you don't attack them. And when you do take a swing, they will scream like children and try to run away from you while their buddies look the other way hoping you don't notice them.

As a warrior, I'd have to say that you're definitely describing a monk. Maybe a necromancer or shadowknight. Warriors have excellent AC and HP, but lack escape abilities, and are not at all the fastest damage dealers. In situations where just about any other class would have some way of fleeing, or avoiding the aggro in the first place, I get crushed. (Except Unrest...as long as I remember to get shrunk so that I don't get stuck in doorways, there's no way I can die there since the zone line is so close, and my AC, HP, and resistances are so high.) Monks have Feign Death, which is the best defense of all against multiple aggro -- it makes the mobs think that their job is done, and they just wander off one by one. They also have sufficient AC for soloing, are one of the two best melee damage dealers in the game, and their bare hands are deadly weapons with a level-based haste (so they're always ready for battle, even when naked on a corpse run).

You ended up only listing two things. What were the other three you wanted? Perhaps we can find a class that actually has all five, or at least rough equivalents.
