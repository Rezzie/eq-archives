GH:

Harmony is discounted because you don't cast it in combat. Thus you don't really care what the casting time is, there's no pressure.

"Class defining ability" does not mean "something you share with no other class". If that were the case there'd be two classes with them, Monks (Mend and special attacks are not shared) and Shaman (Alchemy is not shared). Attempts to define yourself into victory are rarely successful.

A class-defining ability is something you excel at, some area in which no other class can hope to match you, and every class has such an area. Today, for Rangers, that ability is Tracking. If the Druid cap is increased to 125, that will no longer be true.

Your math falls into the Who Cares catagory. At a skill of 125 Druids will be able to track everything within the range that you are most likely to be able to reach in time, before another PC.

We are not happy for you because you are taking something away from Rangers, something we only just received after two long years of being inadequate at our own specialty. The recent Ranger improvements didn't affect Druids; it does not matter to a Druid if a Ranger casts SoW at 30 or 39. If they'd moved it to 22, however, I'll be Druids would have screamed bloody murder. (And for the record, I advocated leaving it at 39.)
