Pulled from another thread because I think it needs to be in this one too...

Monks are supposed to be loose cannons - high damage at a fast rate, low tanking skills. Monks used to be the combatant most likely to stop an enemy caster from casting with a click on the "Flying Kick" key. If the kick went well, there was a Good chance the caster would be interrupted. Warriors did NOT take this from us, they have a hard time stopping higher level casters from casting as well. Monks with their proper weapons (HANDS) now do approximately the same damage as a warrior of the same level with high end equipment. Unless the monk has a superweapon which 99% of monks will never see. The prior balance of the melee classes is Broken now. Groups will suffer for it because the monk does not have the extra offensive power to stop a casting mob from completing it's spell. Did you ever notice the shiverback armor defenses? lots of magic/heat/cold defenses - monks are melee casterkillers. Even a single lvl 50-60 offensive spell can make a HUGE difference in the outcome of a battle. Sure monks will still have their place in groups - it is a game after all and we have friends. Until some relatively good low-weight, more than once per month drop, high damage monk WEAPON (*snarl*) is implemented, we will not be able to do our JOB - stop the casters from casting. If lvl 50-60 enemy casters start dropping 1000+ point damage Dots and massive debuffs on the tanks, the tanks will eventually die, then the hapless party wil get smacked around by casting mobs that punch for 100+ damage.

I believe that in an even fight, a warrior Should be able to beat a monk about 50% of the time or more. Against a warrior type mob, the monk should get squished, a warrior of the same level and general equipment quality should do FAR better. Against a spellcaster type mob, the warrior should get fried - the mob will cast between attacks. The monk _should_ have a better showing here because he will be interrupting spells, but against anything in the 50-60 range this is not the case unless the monk has a ultrararesuperstick of some sort.

If there was balance before, then the relative strengths of the melee classes should remain the same. Monks aren't the only ones who are suffering - Rangers are hurting too - their defenses need a lot of help - they are a puller class, but can suffer horribly in many cases if they get a crit on an initial pull (now with 2x damage), it will be a while before the warrior/pally can taunt the critter off unless the ranger is using 1 damage arrows (Doh!). Both classes still have good core abilities - monks have FD, mend and warrior level damage ability, rangers have great bow skill, some spells, and tracking.

Forgive me if I am a bit suspicious, but I am _almost_ to the point of believing that both of these "nerfs by default" were intentional in order to make the game more "challenging" to groups. There are supposed to be unbreakable upper limits to skills (252). This means that mobs cannot have skills above a certain ability. Where am I driving with this? Again - back to enemy casting...

I'm thinking that if rangers were allowed to actually have defenses, they might be able to (with the new more powerful buff spells) stop casters from casting by plunking them with an 8 damage arrow out of a shaped darkwood compound bow, or perhaps even a better bow, and actually live through the brief furious assault likely to follow after doing a big chunk of damage. Monks would also be able to do the same thing - do enough damage to stop a caster from casting - they have a possible way to avoid being nuked in worst case scenario though - FD, so the easiest solution was to simply not give them the same relative offensive capability as other non-casters - if they can't hit hard enough to disrupt high level casters regularly, then the developers do not need to figure out a way to increase the skill levels of mobs beyond the current limits of the game in order to reduce the monk's chances if interrupting spells. In order to give the appearance of maintaining melee class balance, ultrarare new monk weapons were introduced. If I am correct, the new ultrarare monk weapons will go the way of the dodo once a few are established on each server, and monk offensive capability without weapons will remain at their current level.

However your methodology is flawed.

The damage potential of a PC is directly derived from the resources avalible to the PC, and this is part of the reason why things are so utterly hosed up in the game when it comes various classes capacities when compared to anothers.

In order to do an accurate test, the mob one beats on must be class-appropriate (something that X class would reasonably fight, that does reasonable damage to the PC), and the PC must fight until their resources (HP/MANA/STA) are exausted either by zeroing out & death, or some short bit just above death.

THAT provides an accurate test. Look at high level rangers as an example, the class does ok damage for a melee class (nothing to brag about), but has a very low offensive potential becasue they die like freakin flies the minute a high-end mob starts beating on them.
