Well, my point about soloing and groupability and the post 50 game is this: you don't balance classes 0 to 50, then stop, then rebalance 0 to 60. Game design and class balancing is a 0 to 60 issue. It's also true that everyone is better off by grouping post 50. But the spells hybrids add are equivalent to a huge boost in versatility, and versatility means power.

The ability to solo means that you can always gain exp. It meanst that, while you wait for a group, instead of having to sit waiting for a group, you can lessen the tedium of shouting for group. The ability to snare/root means you can solo in dungeons while waiting to group. And the idea that bandages equate to the hp recovery that hybrids have isn't a serious arguement.

I can give you a concrete example. It's pretty late right now. I have a lvl 49 warrior on Tholuxe Paellis, a relatively new server. Right now, as I type this, he was looking for a group in Sol b; there were like 4 people in the zone. 3 were a lower lvl window group, another a lvl 53 sk soloing bnb with his snare. If I were a hybrid I could at least do the solusek kobolds due to the snaring ability. As a warrior I simply gotta call it a night. Yes I coulda logged on earlier and played -- but sometimes I like to log on to eq for just a bit and play. Soloing capable classes have a big advantage there.

You could say: versatility is meaningless at high lvls when in-group power is all that counts, or even "raiding" power is all that counts. But that's neglecting the 0 to 50 road that *every* player travels, and which classes like clerics and warriors and rogues pay their dues, so to speak.
