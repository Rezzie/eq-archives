IDEA- Poison crafted with poison skill that instantly casts a spell that both kills the user and restores some of the experience lost for dying.

WHY IT WORKS- First, this would only be used when you KNOW things are going bad. How often do you know this? Not too often. Allow res to bring the rogue back to the area, but not restore experience. The cost should be about 3-4pp and aimed chiefly in the 35+ range, for those new-era dungeoncrawlers.

IDEA- As it is, there's a poison rogues can make that hastes and buffs a mob, but gives it a killer DoT. What I'm thinking is, keep with this idea and make a self-only poison that buffs the rogue nicely, but has a chance to kill him. Another last resort thing. Cleric supervision advised.

WHY IT WORKS- There's natural downsides built into it. Remember, though, who will risk death to win a battle unless the poison itself actually affects the outcome? Make it strong stuff.

IDEA- Quest: Turn in JBoots, get a pair of boots that cast indoor-only SoW. Of course, don't call it SoW.

WHY IT WORKS- No other class can SoW indoors. This is generally done to prevent kiting, right? Well, rogues can't kite, especially in a dungeon. This allows speedier pulling and CR, two of our nice roles. It helps push the idea that a rogue is a dungeon's man.

IDEA- In line with the above idea, a sprint discipline activated around 30-35. For a while, rogues have had no escape skill. We run, we die, we beg for a rez, etc. Monks can feign. Warriors can just suck it up. SKs, feign. Paladins, LoH. Clerics, Invul. Enchanters, mez and gate. Get the idea? We're dead in the water here. Add something that lets us run like hell for a minute. Refresh every hour or so.

WHY IT WORKS- Rogues have a chance to live now when the porter dies. Yay!

IDEA- Illusion Masks. Player races only.

WHY IT WORKS- I swear, I don't see why this would upset enchanters. Firstly, if we want even HALF the illusions they have at our disposal, that's a bagful of masks we had to camp, quest, and carry. That's precious inventory room for resist gear, ph4t l3wt, and supplies. As it is, I could barely justify carrying an Erudite, Iksar, and DE mask at once, but I would. Rogues are the masters of disguise, with the right tools. Most of us like to look half-decent, too.

IDEA- Try to keep our armor colors a dark tint. Ravenscale- Black. Woven Shadow(hate)- Dark Green. Mrylokar's(kunark)- Dark red. Velious armor- Bright Yellow. Heh, no thanks.

IDEA- Don't nerf evade. You have no idea how mad we make monsters, and how hard it is to evade. 30-50% success rate, tops. Because it's linked to hide, if we're being hit when we press the button, it usually fails to to "moving while hidden". Don't think of it as a magic portal out of danger, it's far from it. As soon as the tank's dead, it's coming after me, and evade just prolongs that.

DON'T- Listen to these people about the whole Feign Death, Total Hatewipe stuff. Some people have no idea what balance is. It's tough to walk sometimes.

REMEMBER- Monks and Rogues are an intricate balance. Both are damage monsters. Both have a few extra abilities. Now, there is a decent balance. DO NOT UPSET THE BALANCE. Buffing rogues and nerfing monks, or vice versa, could make a whole class useless in the eyes of players. Don't do that, please.


Thanks for reading.
