I was at first concerned by the idea that not pumping enough initial points into the primary stats hurts you more than buffing it later would help. People have said if you didn't pump enough points into your stat then scrap the character and start over.

Sure, at later levels when you can get your hands on stat buffers it can raise your skill up to what it should have been when you created the character. And if you had created the character with better stat in the first place, you would have that many more points to work with.

But I don't think it makes as much of a difference as it seems. Missing out on 10 points of wisdom for the life of a character doesn't make it useless by any means. 10 points of mana gives you 2 more points of mana per level. That's 100 mana at 50th level, which at that level isn't much.

Keep collecting stat buffers for more mana and you should be fine. Ultimately, everyone needs those stat buffers to be more successful at the higher levels. That is why they are there.

A wisdom if 162 is absolutely excellent! Wish I had that!
