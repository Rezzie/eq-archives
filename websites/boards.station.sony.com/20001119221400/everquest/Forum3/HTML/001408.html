I currently have a level 11 Iksar Warrior on Tallon Zek. Through doing the Iksar warrior quests, I have gotten to know the area surrounding cabilis fairly well, and I appreciate the fact that these quests give you an opportunity to pursue a goal while exploring your surroundings.
However, I must post on a few frustrations about the Iksar warrior quests. I am currently working on the (footman's?) pike quest, where you need the javelins and redwood, etc.. Here are my complaints regarding this quest:

1) The froglok bounder javelins are much too difficult to get for a (level 8?) quest IMHO. It is not the frogs themselves are that dangerous, but the fact that they:

a) flee, and
b) run into the water a LOT, where it is EXTREMELY difficult to pursue them.

I have found that all too often, the froglok will flee, I'll give chase, and after he enters the water I have to wait for him to come back. This can EASILY happen 4-5 times for one single frog. Giving chase is hard enough over a high-latency 56k connection, but even on a cable modem or LAN connection, I have yet to sucessfully pursue a fleeing, swimming mob of ANY kind regardless of the swiftness of my connection. I am also disgruntled about the frequency that they load the spear, as well as the frequency of the spawns of boundres, but the fleeing into the water is BY FAR the worst part of the collecting items part of this quest.

2) One of the componants that you QUEST for to get is expendable in the forge. Yes, that accursed geozite tool. I think this leads to more frustration and tedium than is appropriate for this level of a quest.

3) YOU have to SMITH your pike. I say again, **YOU** have to smith your pike. It is a nodrop lore item, so that means any person attempting this quest MUST have put practices and perhaps even money into their smithing skill. I do not think FORCING people to learn a trade skill to complete quests is a good idea. Pretty much all the intermediate ingredients for Iksar (not just warrior) quests that I have seen are nodrop, so you cannot hire someone to smith these for you. I was planning on becoming a smith anyway, but I was hoping to be able to make money off it. If all my other lizard brethren are smiths too, my hopes of scraping an income together from smithing are hosed as well. PLease, ENCOURAGE players to seek skilled (PC) tradesmen, instead of forcing people to take up skills to complete quests.

4) I have to agree with Nox, the clues and formulae that NPCs give regarding these quests are at best vague, and are sometimes innacurate or downright misleading. One of the NPCs says if you have the componants you can forge the pike "...if the plans are yours". What does this mean? Inventory? In the forge? In the bank? Where do the plans go? (I know they go in the forge only because I have read posts on other sites by people who have completed the quests) It seems like the NPC should give a formula that is accurate an unambiguous.
