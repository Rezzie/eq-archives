I just read thru several "Cleric Class is dead and I'm gonna quit my cleric now" threads. Also my class was just "NERFED" threads...

First, No one, with the exception of those on the test server, knows the effect that the latest changes will have on gameplay. Those that read the patch message are simply guessing at it's impact on gameplay. No one will know the true effects until they play the game with the new changes in effect.

Secondly, now is not the time to complain about changes that haven't been experienced in gameplay. If you have nothing to base your thoughts on besides your assumed effects, you have nothing. There are no numbers to justify the interpreted effects of the patch.

Now, let's assume that the changes are as they are presumed to be. Instead of debating with other classes about how the changes will have drastic effects on certain classes, why not discuss new tactics for the changes?

By title, this is a discussion board. Try to see the bigger picture instead of having "tunnel" vision. Figure out ways to counteract the new changes. The Everquest designers are finding new ways to allow classes to interact with each other and use the spells given to each class to compliment others.

An example: Assuming that every time a cleric casts C-heal, they hop to the top of the aggro and suddenly the MoB is attacking them; You'd need to have a stunning class in the group to allow the cleric to Atone. Or an enchanter to stun and mem blur. (Please note: I don't know that this is the solution, but I am offering suggestions as to how to solve the aggro.)

I'm just saying offer solutions to the issues assuming that this is the way it's going to be rather than changing what is to be.

I don't think that this can be done until after the patch is in and we have played the game and seen the changes effects first hand. And if there is no solution and the game play is poor, Verant will have to look at their changes further.

Thanks for reading. I hope I made sense =)

Enchanters have been dealing with high agro spells for a long time. Nearly every offensive spell an enchanter has, has a high taunt factor built in. Enchanters manage this while wearing cloth armor, lower defensive caps, and about 60% of the hitpoints that a cleric has.

How do they survive? Mesmerize spells, memory blur spells, and good working relationships with tanks.

Cleric's get plate armor for a reason. A cleric gets a memory blur spell called Atone at level 34, the same level that they get Superior Healing. They also get Divine Aura at level 1, an invulnerability spell that lasts about 18 seconds, which is plenty of time for a melee class to climb back on top of the hate list. They get Divine Barrior at 29 which is an upgrade to Divine Aura with a regeneration component.

I don't think that this is a coincidence. I think that Verant had always intended that healing have a high taunt value, and gave clerics the tools that needed to manage it. The problem is that clerics seldom learn to use these tools, because they really didn't have to. Now we know why. They weren't doing the agro that they were intended to.

Cheer up clerics! You complained that you were bored at higher levels, doing nothing but sit-heal-repeat. Now you are at risk, and have the excitement of taking your destiny in your own hands. Your survival is up to you! I'm serious, that isn't sarcasm. Fun comes from risk.
