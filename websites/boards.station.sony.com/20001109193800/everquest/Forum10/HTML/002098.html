You can make off-the-cuff emotes by either typing /em (text of your emote) or text of your emote). Remember when typing it that it should be in the present tense, third person, since the game just sticks your name in front. One of my current favorites:

:rolls her eyes at %T.

It makes it say "Ashara rolls her eyes at Sasandra", assuming that I have Sasandra targetted.

If you're going to be using an emote a lot, you can change one of your socials to the emote. Right click on one of the social buttons that you don't use, and then click on one of the lines and type in the text of the emote. Don't forget to put the : or /em in front. Then, whenever you click that social button, it will do the emote.

You can also use the canned (pre-programmed) emotes that have animations, but make them say other things. For example, /kneel makes your character appear to kneel briefly, and says "Ashara kneels before %T in humility" or something like that. But by typing /kneel say gets down on her knees and pleads with %T, I can make it look like I kneel, but instead it will say, "Ashara gets down on her knees and pleads with General D'Veers". Those can be amusing .

Good luck!

Another form of emote that I like is rephrasing the animated canned emotes...

a favorite one of my DE SK...
/cheer say revels in the carnage.

results in the cheer animation with the text:
Dalharuk revels in the carnage.
(although it reads different to the player doing the emote)

SAY is the keyword which changes the canned text to whatever comes after.

Another one I use often is uncanning /point...
/point say points in the direction of Qeynos.

If you are pulling, and want to make the Incoming, a <monster_name_here> key, make sure that you *don't* put the : or /em in the hotkey text. Right click on your social, then type:

/g Incoming, %T

Then when you hit the key, it'll tell your group the incoming message. If you put

: Incoming, %T

it will do an emote, which probably won't be heard by your group. Just to clarify .
