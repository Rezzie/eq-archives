You thought they were small and swift, didn't you ? He he, wrong:

1) Post-50 Wizards don't need the missing large DD spells, because a double cast of Draught does the job (so Draughts are "large" if cast twice...) but
2) Draughts are swift in single-cast, but "slow" in chain-cast, and this might be the hidden reason why some post-50 Wizards feel less effective than before. I suspect Draughts need a cast time of 2.5 instead of 3; the level 60 Sunstrike could also be given a shorter cast time (same as Ice Comet).

I know, DD isn't everything, but Gordon knows I have another post about AE damage, and he had better answer it some day :-(

All I had to say was said above; read the rest only if you want the details.

1) Post-50 Wizards don't need the missing large DD spells

A level 51 Wizard chain-casting two Draughts of Fire may generate:
1286 damage (643x2), for
430 mana (215x2), in the course of
7.2 seconds (3+2.2+3).

(Note: I was never able to measure precisely the standard recovery time, but I assume it is 2.2 seconds).

The double Draught of Fire looks very much like a classic upgrade to Ice Comet:
+15% damage (versus 1120)
+7.5% mana cost (versus 400)
+30% cast time (versus 6.3).

However:
- the "spell" is split in two halves (with added versatility, but a few situational drawbacks versus a "whole" spell, such as: more chances to break Root, risk of earlier aggro when chain-casting, etc).
- the "spell" has a poor swiftness: the double Draught has a -12% swiftness penalty (damage / cast time) versus the single Ice Comet.

If you are a 51+ Wizard complaining that you don't get "large" new DD spells giving you enough "damage output", I believe you're mistaken. Cast double Draughts; and don't seek Rains as a replacement, unless you want a chance to improve your damage/mana (that's another matter).

2) There IS something wrong with 51+ Wizard DD: downgraded swiftness !

Isn't swiftness supposed to be the prime strong point of Wizards ?

Draught of Fire may be felt as swift, because its single-cast swiftness (damage / cast time) versus Ice Comet is +21%: looks good and versatile, but... it's not that good.

As outlined above, Draughts need to be chain-cast, more than earlier DD spells, if large damage is to be dealt. Chain-cast swiftness (damage / 'cast + recast time', an indicator of non-stop chain-casting damage-dealing speed) is the best measure of how a Wizard is able to "kill the right mob at the right time" to earn his/her place in a group. Draught of Fire deals slower damage than Ice Comet when the time comes for the Wizard to step forward and chain-cast. A minor gain in chain-cast swiftness versus Ice Comet is achieved only at level 57, when Wizards gain Draught of Ice. Isn't it somewhat late ?

Table of how much CHAIN-CAST SWIFTNESS (damage / 'cast + recast time') Wizards gain with each new DD spell (excluding Bolts and all-or-nothing Shocks):

Level 4: +83% versus level 3
Level 8: +99% versus level 7
Level 12: +96% versus level 11
Level 16: +18% versus level 15
Level 20: not worth mentioning (Force Shock can't be chain-cast)
Level 24: +36% versus Flame Shock at level 23
Level 29: +20% versus level 28
Level 34: +11% versus level 33
Level 39: +8% versus level 38
Level 44: +39% versus level 43
Level 49: +62% with Ice Comet, versus level 48
Level 51: -6% with Draught of Fire, versus Ice Comet at level 50
(Voltaic Draught and Draught of Jiva have the same stats as Draught of Fire)
Level 57: +7% with Draught of Ice, versus earlier Draughts at level 56, and +4% versus Ice Comet
Level 60: +26% versus Draught of Ice at level 59, and +33% versus Ice Comet.

The "shining" gains are obtained at levels 4, 8, 12, 24, 44 and 49.
The poorest gains (or the penalties) are obtained at levels 20 and 51-59 (the improvement of level 57 comes too late).
The "so-and-so" gains are levels 16, 29, 34, 39 and 60 (the improvement of level 60 comes too late).

With a Draught cast time of 2.5 instead of 3:
At level 51, Draught of Fire would give a minimal +4% chain-cast swiftness bonus versus Ice Comet, instead of a penalty.
The double-cast Draught of Fire would also be 4.5% swifter than the single-cast Ice Comet, instead of being less swift.
At level 57, Draught of Ice would give a +15.5% chain-cast swiftness bonus versus Ice Comet.
At level 60, Sunstrike would still give a +13.5% chain-cast swiftness bonus versus Draught of Ice at level 59, but, if Verant was kind enough to give Sunstrike the cast time of Ice Comet (6.3 instead of 7), the chain-cast swiftness bonus versus Draught of Ice would be restored to a well-earned +23%.
All this wouldn't be tremendous, but at least the post-50 Wizards would have a chance to feel they are capable of a respectable damage output.

(Don't ask me about the cast times of Lures and Flames of Ro, there's a limit to what I can do to help).

It's a catastrophy for Wizards if they lose kill speed more than other classes do (as level improves), because speed is supposed to be the Wizards' most valuable asset.

The Draught line was created to give wizards the option of doing less damage at similar mana/damage ratio compared to ice comet, and also to give them similar spells across the three elements that the wizards control. The reasoning behind "less damage" was to reduce the amount that the wizard would aggro the target. Had we wanted to continue along the same lines as ice comet, we could have given similar damage spells with equal damage, mana, and casting time requirements.

In this case, the additional time required to stack two draughts versus one ice comet is what you "pay" for the reduced amount that you aggro the target.

Regarding the uninterrupted med time... actually medding between the two casts will probably give you more mana.

The reason is mana regens only at certain times (they call it tick). If you happen to be medding when mana regens, you get more mana. It doesn't matter if you had been medding since the last tick or just sit down. As long as you are medding when the tick counts, you get all the medding mana regen.

So, when you are casting your ice comet, you are going to lose all the medding mana regen during the cast. If you are casting twice and medding in between, you will get a chance to have a slightly better mana regen.
