At level 7, you should be saving your practice points for when you get magic. You'll need to put at least one point into every magic skill to be able to use them.
As for armor, at your level leather is most likely to be within your price range. To get money, you should be fighting in Crushbone. The Crushbone belts are turned into the Canloe Nusback of the warrior's guild in Kaladim. He'll give you exp, money and patchwork armor for each belt you turn in. You can make a couple gold off each piece of patchwork you sell (also, if you're a wood elf you can wear it if you need it). At level 7 you should also get decent exp just for fighting in CB, and you should be able to solo as long as you're careful.
Hope that helps. If you're on the Rathe, look me up, I'll be glad to help you out if I can.

What Baelrath said is just about right, although I would like to make one more suggestion about your magic skills that you will be receiving in a couple of levels. DO NOT place a practice point in the skill Conjuration. When you place the first skill point in a new skill, it is called "Priming the Skill". When you Prime a skill, the rating that you will have is equal to the level you are current at. That is to say when you place your first skill point in the magic skills at level 9, the skill rating will be 9. At level 9, there are no spells for a ranger that uses Conjuration. The first spell rangers receive that will use Conjuration is at level 15. So it is wise to wait until you reach level 15 to Prime this skill.

Glimpse uses Divination while Skin Like Wood is an Abjuction type spell. The spells that rangers receive that use Conjuration are Dance of the Fireflies (level 15) and Stinging Swarm (level 30). You may be confusing Conjuration skill with the Channeling skill. The Channeling skill can raise with the casting of any spell type. The Channeling skill is used to determine the likelihood of you to be interupted if hit while casting.
