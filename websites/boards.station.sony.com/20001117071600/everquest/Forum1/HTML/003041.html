This sounds like a frame rate issue due to the card and the drivers.

First thing you want to do was mentioned above: get the detonator drivers from the link here at the site. The Gateway drivers are notoriously out of date. And the techs there are 90% $7/hr people reading from a script. Don't waste the time on hold.

Second: the card you mention is not a true 128 bit video card. It's ony 64 bit, hence the title "Model 64". This is a typical Gateway/Dell tactic to save money; they ship a sub-par card with a "high end" computer. That card sells almost anywhere for $50. Be wary of these cards on E-bay or elsewhere. They can be spotted with "M64" in the title. They are poor 3D performers.

Even though it is TNT2 with 32mb, your framerate drops significantly when pumping D3D through the 64bit pipeline. It bottlenecks and is very EQ noticeable. I recommend anyone who wants more knowledge on the subject to research at www.sharkyextreme.com

Lastly, make sure you have the latest DirectX drivers. This can make a big difference in performance.

If I were you, I would pull the card and upgrade to a true 128 bit card. For $100 or less, you can get a Viper with the TNT2 chipset, or even a Voodoo3 with 16mb that will still outperform your current card.

Personally, I was VERY budget minded and just upgraded to a Viper V770 TNT2 with 32mb. This card is a year old in technology, but runs EQ amazingly well. And it only cost me $50 on Ebay.

Realize that EQ was designed when cards that were 16mb TNT were 'cutting-edge', so it doesn't take a super-expensive card to play the game. But of course, if you want top performance, spend a few extra dollars and get a good card.

Remember: A stereo is only as good as the speakers attached; a Ferrari is only as fast as the tires it runs on; and a computer game is only as good as the video card it passes through.
