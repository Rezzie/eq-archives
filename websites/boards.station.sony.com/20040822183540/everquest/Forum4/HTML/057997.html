I've been tossing ideas back and forth in my head as I see more and more portions of the game. I look at my 59th level Beastlord and when I saw swap to my alts I say, "I wish I could do this or I wish I didn't have to worry about that".

Admit it. It's a different game then when it was released in 1999 and there are some new players that don't have all the time in the world to play. These are the casual players that may eventually see the planes and such but may feel overwhelmed if they can't get parchments and things for their spells and may not be able to afford that 100k spell in the bazaar because of time constraints and not being able to go out and farm it.

So I have some interesting ideas and would like some thoughts:

Ethereal, Spectral and Runed Parchments: Make them purchaseable via adventure merchants. Of course varying costs - make them available at all camps to make it fair. This would ease the pain of someone who has time to do an LDoN but not necessarily commit to a 3-4 hour long game session in Plane of Valor. LDoN was awesome in the fact you can get some real nice gear for it without being in a raiding guild and raiding 6-10 hours a day.

Sparring Partner NPC's would be useful for raising neglected weapon skills. Make them set so you have a 50pp price for an hour long session. This will build weapon skills and defensive skills. The boon to this with raising defense skills is the NPC would DS itself at the beginning of the sessions. Power-leveling's going to happen, does happen and has been around for years. Let's give the people that do it some skills so the rest of us in groups don't have to suffer with neglected skills of all sorts. I know even myself when I started my Beastlord I neglected piercing because it was hard to keep up with everything and I didn't have the money at that point in time for a good piercing weapon. Once I finally got a piercing weapon that was worth while in my low 50's it would've been nice to have an NPC to bang away on just for weapon skill ups. My piercing was a measely 46. Now luckily I was grouped almost constantly at this point and through Hastes piercing maxed in a level but I still felt bad about making the group suffer a bit for lacking weapon skills.

Anyway, just some thoughts I've had that I'd like to see added potentially. They've been gearing the game more and more towards a more casual player and I think these two items would go a long way in helping.

Suneri Wolfmaster

I really like the sparring partner idea, Sun. I try to keep my cleric's defensive and offensive skills up, as well as her melee, by jumping up into the fight now and then in groups, and maybe taking a hit or two there. An alternate way to get those parchments would be great.

Spending 6 to 10 hours of my day being bored (as a lot of people I know complain raids are boring as heck) isn't my idea of fun, but doing adventures for that long and gaining points toward something worthwhile would be. So I really agree with your ideas here.

Or even getting as many as half your level. But when you're level 30 something and you get, what, 3 points on a success at normal? That hardly makes the adventure worth doing, especially if there are no nameds, or aug drops, or even good loot. There is definitel room for improvement on incentives for low levels to LDoN, and even high levels as well.
