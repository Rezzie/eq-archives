Absolutely the walrus drum was a steal. I'm still using one at 62.

As for modifications... look for Reed Stormwind's spreadsheets. I am sure eqdiva has them somewhere. Reed did an outstanding job of building a spreadsheet that will show the effects with different instruments. It also lists the multipliers for most instruments.

A Drum of the Beast is a 2.3 modifier I believe. I think that a Walrus Skin Drum is 2.1. I'd have to look it up myself.

The modifier is the multiple over singing that you will get using that instrument. If you heal 10 points of health using singing, a 2.2 modifier lute will heal 22 points.

I don't know about the lutes. I picked up a lute of the howler and haven't bought another since.

I was wrong

Drums of the Beast = 2.6
Walrus Skin Drum = 2.3
Combine Drum = 2.0
Lute of Gypsy Princess = 2.1
Orcish lute = 2.1
Lyendiln's Lute = 2.47
Combine Lute = 2.2

If you're getting started, go to Katta and go buy combine instruments from a vendor. They're like a few plat each and they are very competitive. Only blow money on drums if you are worried about instrument performance.

Course, Lute of the Howler gives Werewolf Illusion that helps with melee quite a bit, so make sure you look at effects/stats before making your purchases.

Maybe with crission's pixie strike that is true but with Song of Twilight I haven't seen much help either with or without. Same with Dreams of Thule.

If your group is relying on your healing, get a better healer.

The lute haste is only 40% and is self only. If you have no chanter then you should be doing group haste songs anyway. Personally, I use the werewolf illusion for the ATK buff.

In all reality, using instruments in battle has never been proved to me to be effective unless raiding or chant kiting. Meleeing in a typical 6 man group is just as effective as wringing out a little more STR from using brass. Unless you're in a raid scenario, the extra few points of DS isn't going to make or break you.

If you can get with the rogues and put up a fight, you're better off in my opinion meleeing. 40-60 DPS beats the heck out of an extra 10 or so damage per second from a DS. I have NEVER been cussed out for not using instruments.

If you're truly raiding, you can make the arguement that using a instrument in battle makes a difference. I don't do it often, but if needed I will go the extra mile to put on a insane DS for raid level mobs.

Course by the time you hit good primetime raiding levels (say mid 50s) you might be weilding epic, which makes this whole arguement moot.
