Hello all....

It occurred to me last night that I haven't seen a lot about Druids recently on the boards here and I thought I would put a few of my thoughts concerning what I have discovered about playing my Halfling Druid up to 37. Maybe it'll inspire a newbie.. or something

So... in no particular order, here they are.

1) You'll hear a lot of people Dislike/make fun of Druids. There are a lot of them in game and unfortunately because of that you are more likely to run into someone who had a bad experience with a Druid in the past. Don't let this get you down. Make it part of your gaming style to win your groups over. Druids have a unique personality and if that shines through in your gameplay you may just win a convert.

2) A timely foothair joke can help relax a stressed out group. Don't forget, chicks dig us.

3) Snare, when combined with SOW, is one of the best spells in the game. Throw this on anything you are even THINKING about attacking. If it's rooted, snare it anyway. Nothing is worse then having root break (and it will at the worst possible time) and dealing with a really PO'd FULL SPEED mob. Snare can help turn a wipeout into XP or at least into a graceful exit.

4) When soloing, your DoT spells are the key to your success. Here is how I solo:

Snare the mob and pull it to a safe location
Root it
Dot 1, Dot 2, Dot 3, Dot 4 (Yes, they all stack!)
Sit.
Med.
Med some more.
Nuke to finish off.
Rinse, repeat.

Your Damage over Time spells allow to to inflict damage while medding. With KEI this can be especially deadly. When you get good at traffic control you can handle two or three at a time this way. A lot of folks suggest kiting, but I actually like doing it this way better.

5) In a group, your task will always be to be flexible. A druid will almost always find him/herself handling multiple roles simultaneously.
You will almost always be on secondary healer duties. If the Primary healer runs low on mana, your mediocre healing ability may mean the difference between victory and a Total Party Wipeout so be aware.
You'll also likely be on buffing duty. Keep the tanks hopped up on Regeneration and Damage Shield. Cast it on yourself so you know when it wears off, or have them let you know when it gets low. It'll save mana on heals and you'll go through pulls faster.
Thirdly, you may be on snare and root duty. A snared mob doesn't go get help from it's buddies, and it doesnt lead the tank on a half mile chase to finish it off. Snare it and when its health gets low it'll stay put.

***Don't use root for this however. A rooted mob will fight until its last breath. A snared mob gives up and lets you beat it to death***

Root is used to control adds if you don't have a chanter handy to mezz them. If you get some unexpected company, lead it a little ways away from the group and root it there. It can be handled once whatever you are currently fighting is disposed of.

Last, you may be in charge of evacing everyone out if things get messy. This is only true in places where a total Party Wipeout is possible. Many times, if you are pulling near a zoneline or in a relatively safe place this won't be necessary. Ask the group leader ahead of time if he wants you to keep an evac spell memmed. If he does, make sure the party members know ahead of time you'll be using it if things get out of hand. If things DO get messy you won't have time to ask while it's happening.

A well played Druid can make a huge difference in a group, especially when things get out of hand. Because of this, be juducious with your mana. Sure, its great to land that final nuke, but if your tank brings back adds and the cleric is OOM, you'll be glad you saved it. So will your group.

6) It pays to port back to Misty Thicket to do your selling if you are a Halfling. Most of the folks there are Amiable to you and that means extra profit for you. Plus, invest in some Charisma gear. It'll make you money in the long run as well.

Um...

Thats all I can think of for now. I am sure there is more. Any other Druids care to add?
