Hey, and welcome to EQ!

As a Barbarian rogue, you cannot cast spells.
As far as hunting, you can stay in Everfrost until upwards of level 20, although you may want to check out the adjacent BlackBurrow (Lair of Gnolls).

As far as cash goes, you should not worry too much about that right now, just kill monsters and sell what you find on them. Later, you can hunt mammoths in Everfrost for tusks and way later Ice Giants. Around your late teens and early 20's you will start to get a little bit of cash, and if you are persistant, can make your first 1k platinum in your 20s.

Quests you can go on, but the Barbarian Newbie Armor Quests have not been implemented yet, on my last check. Check out Allakhazam's Magical Realm or other EQ web sites for other quest information.

Backstab is a rogue only skill, but you will not get it until level 10. ONce you spend training points in it then, you can use it. You must have a piercing weapon in your hand and be behind the mob for it to work.

I also believe hide and sneak are class abilities of rogues, but not sure of the levels.

OK, here ya go:

Walking mindlessly around killing small animals is pretty much what EQ is about . . . except that eventually you get to kill dragons and stuff.

You know those polar bear skins that you get when you loot (right-click) the cub you just killed? Turn them in to an NPC in Halas (your starting city), and you can get nice Patchwork armor. Hail an NPC (/hail) and they will respond, usually including some [text in brackets]. If you target them and say "What [text in brackets]?" they'll frequently respond by giving you a quest to do. Know where the bank is in Halas? If not, ask; then go upstairs and talk to the shady folk hanging out there. That's your guild, and they'll have some good quests for you.

Sorry, you're a Rogue - Rogues don't get spells. So don't worry about that now.

Money? Just keep killing stuff and selling what you loot off the creatures to merchants. It will add up, eventually.

Hide, Sneak, Backstab - For a Barbarian Rogue, you have to train these abilities at your guild. Back to the bank for you. Right-click on the NPCs up there until you see a window with a bunch of abilities listed. Left-click on Hide, and the NPC will say, "Interested in training in Hide, Elpida?" You click the Train button (I think that's it), and voila, you have the ability at a very low level. Same for Sneak. You don't get backstab until 10th level, but I think you get Pickpocket at 6 or 8.

To use these abilities, go to the bank of abilities, left-click on one of the buttons, and you should get a window with a list of everything you can do. Click on Hide, and the button will now say Hide. Stand still, click on this button, and you'll get a message telling you you've hidden yourself from view, if successful. Do the same for Sneak - if you sound like a Cat, you're successful, and can now move around hidden.

OK, sorry, getting tired of typing. Hope that helps, and welcome to the world. Ask around - people will be happy to help you in game.

Have you visited your guild's trainer? It is a person in the guild, either the person that you got your initial tunic & weapon from, or someone standing nearby.

You need to right-click on this person, and you will see a window pop open which shows you all the skills that are available to you, and how many points you have to spend. For skills where you need to be a certain level, this is also shown in the window.

A little clarification on what people have said.

First, Ruined Bear Skins, Low Quality Bear Skins, Medium Quality Bear Skins, High Quality Bear Skins, Polar Bear Skins, etc., are all completely different items. The specific item that you hand in for quests should be mentioned by the NPC that takes the item. The items are NOT interchangeable, so be absolutely positive that give them the correct one.

Hide/sneak are 2 of the best abilities that a rogue has. You will be using them throughout your rogue lifetime. Read up on the user interface to figure out the best method of using them quickly and to best effect.

Backstab is not available until level 10. You will need to return to your guild trainer with at least one training point in order for him to teach it to you. After that, regular usage during battle will result in skill increases.

Quests are best started by targetting and hailing any NPC you encounter. If they respond, there's a decent chance that particular NPC is involved in some sort of quest. The quest might not be intended for rogues to perform, but that may not stop you from doing it anyways.

As far as money goes, you won't see much cash at all until level 20-30. Certainly not as a new player. It takes a fair amount of time to understand the huge world that is EQ and how to make cash in the process. Trust me, play the game and you will have fun regardless of how poor you think you are. Bigger, better stuff can be obtained, but it takes a fair amount of hard work.

2) hide is essential to evade, so keep it up. when you start grouping, you will depend on evade for your life, because a rogue does a LOT of damage and the mob will turn on you no matter how good your main tank is. you must evade (a combination of hide and stepping back that loses aggro)
