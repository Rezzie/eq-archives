I have been reading a lot of these posts and it boils down to a lot of folks wanting there monks to do a lot of different things.

I want my monk to do one thing. Be a monk, just as it is described. The perineal eastern Martial Artist.

So what is the traditional martial artist like, read the "Bubashi" or "Karate-do nyoman" or "This is Karate". Simply put traditional Martial Arts or Bujutsu or Budo "depending on how you study" mostly revolves around unarmed combat. You start adding weapons and now you are studing warrior type skills.

Personally in the real Art forms I have studied I have learned to punch, kick and simple movements first (monk 1-20), more complex attacks and defenses with punchs,kicks and locks, blocks and throws(jujutsu or judo like not throwing something) being so much more powerful that a good martial artist can break a rock with bare hands (monk 20-40) to advanced learning. Looking into how to foil a opponent through their own movements, be it human or animal, striking and killing with one or a short series of blows, locking down a opponent so that they cannot move at all. Learning how fragile the body is and how easy it is to destroy it with the right movement (monk 41-60)

So how does this reflect in the game.

1. A critical strike with bare hands, learned at a high level 35 perhaps. Cannot be trained only raised through practice.

2. The ability to throw and lock opponents down, once again learned at a high level perhaps 30 this time.

With the addition of simple "Vision" type abilities you would see the monk transformed into the fighting machine that he is.

Remember that the Warrior is learning tactics to destroy over the large scale and learns to absorb the punishment to achieve that goal. In other words a warrior is more concerned with destroying a whole encampment of baddies.

The monk is concerned with learning to destroy one thing very quickly using that creatures abilities against itself.

For instance Mas Oyama hand chopped the horns off of 50+ bulls in his life, 20th century 50s to 80s. How did he do it, he used the bulls charge to place it in a position he could deal that particular blow.
