Verant as a company seems to make decisions that are simple difficult to understand. But the post-Kunark decision to focus high end content to a tiny elite is perhaps the most strange.

Let's just work from the numbers.

In Kunark there was Veeshan's Peak. A zone which most players will never see. To even get there requires a guild organisation beyond most *players* although it is feasible for those who work at everquest.

This wasn't *too* bad. If you just wanted to say "I've been there" there is a long term possibility of getting the key and entering the zone - of course getting out is another matter...

At the time this was 1 zone amongts 50+. Still a significant proportion of content but not unbearably so.

Then comes Velious. 15 or so new zones of which 2 are replicas (in terms of feasible access - PoM and Sleeper's Tomb) of Veeshan's Peak. Fully 13% of the content of an expansion that 7,500 people per server will be expected to buy and its tailored for a tiny minority of players.

How many players?

Well these zones are genuinely limited to a very few elite guilds - usually one per server maximum, which have a specific demographic - full time players. Judging by my knowledge of various servers and the sizes of these very few guilds, it appears that there are only 50 to 100 players per server who will *ever* see these zones.

That's between 0.5% and 1% of the entire player base!

Getting 13% of the expansion (and now 4% of the entire game content) not *even* geared specifically for them (zones such as Temple of Veeshan you can *enter* but don't even *think* about trying to play there) but can only be *entered* by a tiny minority of the player base.

Verant! What is the reason for this? There is *lots* of content that the average level 60 cannot encounter in the *other* zones. Without committing more time than most have the very top end encounters are already restricted. But you add to that entire zones which cannot be entered by most players?

And is there content for those who are beating the *rest* of these zones?

No!

There is *nothing* between the Shroom King level mobs and the Dain level mobs. A huge gap where the *majority* of 55+ players play and yet there is no content designed for them.

You go straight from the single group encounter to the 4 to 6 group raids. Nothing in the middle.

But there is a simple solution. End the over-delivery of content for this tiny elite and retune the high end game so that the majority of high end players can participate. Remove the reliance on 4 to 6 group raids and rebalance these encounters for the *average* Guild of 10 to 18 55+ players.

When the tiny elite leave (as they are threatening to do) they will not dent your income. When *we*, the majority, leave it *will*.

Regards,
Eduin
