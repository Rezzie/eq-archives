Technology, programming, and coding. Simply, the way EQ has zones setup, it's nearly impossible to setup housing for people. Also game design, unless they really re-write a lot of the codes, which they have talked about doing, I really don't see EQ having the capability to have housing. EQ2 might, all depends on the game design.

If you look at the games that provide housing, first of all, most of those games have big, dynamic zones. AC for an example, you can run hours without zoning. DAOC is more like EQ but it has newer technology and game design, so it's doable. Dynamic content and interactive NPC's is something we probably won't see much in EQ.

Like for an example in AC, you can go buy a couch and a tree from the store, and then place them in your house. When people walk by, they actually see the couch and tree you just put there. Most of the items can be picked up and dropped down on the ground, and you see the item's graphic when it's on the ground. You could also hang things up on the walls, like your swords, shields, paintings, etc..

But for EQ, you can't really pick things up and set them on the ground. When you drop something, it's a stupid bag. The environment isn't dynamic, you don't really change anything and can't really place anything in the game. Just older technology, and I doubt they'll go through to try to change it right now. Not to mention the way EQ loads the zones, if they add housing, you can expect 5 minute loading time everytime you zone.

Oh well, housing is cool and a good part of online games. I would like to see it happen with EQ but it just won't. I'm not sure how EQ2's engine is going to be like so we can only hope EQ2 will be able to offer us something similiar. Sony EQ is trying to learn from other games by utilizing the instanced zones for more dynamic content. Also the new AA skills and other functionalites in GoD expansion tells you the devs is trying to improve the game. But with programming limitations I don't think they'll be able to squeeze housing into EQ.

However, during my recent stint in SWG ( my head still hurts from that episode) players were allowed to purchase and place their houses where they wanted. This lead to entire landscapes being clogged with "player cities". I have no desire to see kaladim become cluttered with cities.

For it to work with the environment ( other than the fact that the majority of items appear as bags when dropped) player city zones would need to be created.
