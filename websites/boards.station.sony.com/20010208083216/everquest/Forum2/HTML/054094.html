Yeah, I hate crap like this.

We (a group that have been practicing together for a while now) were on Orc Hwy in Oasis. Now as the puller, I was generally smacking 3-4 little orcys and draggin' them back to the group (6 people total BTW) to kill.

This person (I remember the name, but won't bother mentioning it) watches us for a bit. Then I get a tell asking if I'm running the group. I reply that I am. I got a reply back asking to join. Obviously I replied with "Sorry, we're full, but thanks for asking. Good hunting to you."

Ok, this person tries soloing. "Gotta admire the tenacity", I thought, and continued about our business. Boy was I wrong. They'd pull one or two and start fighting. They'd get themselves in trouble and /yell for help. First couple of times, we went over and pounded the offenders to oblivion. I figured out their game and told the rest of the group not to help them anymore. All we were getting was a beating and no exp.

Now granted, we handled the critters pretty handily, except when they'd bring back on that conned red to us, then things got interesting. But this slowed down our progress. As the puller, I time my pulls based off our casters' mana. So, more healings cast...more nukes expended...for no gain. So, I called a halt to it.

This person restarted the same scenario. They died. I felt bad, but hey, they'd gotten free exp off us already. They sent me a /tell saying "thanks for letting me die."

I replied with "we just finished a fight and our casters were OOM". Ok, only a slight exaggeration. Our Cleric, however, was OOM.

So this person started training us. Now, I'm not the type of person to use /petition. In fact, I only used it once when someone was KSing the group I was in to gain FP faction, meanwhile robbing our exp, all because we wouldn't sell our DF belts for cheap.

Anyway, I stopped pulling, obviously. They kept training us, and we kept taking it. They'd train and watch. I told our casters to go on mana conservation, and basically this person was pulling for us.

I sent the person a /tell "Thanks for pulling for our group. We really appreciate it. How much longer do you think you can do this for us?"

I generally have /shout and /ooc off in Oasis, so I couldn't tell what, if anything, they said in response. But I got no further /tells and had to go back to pulling for ourselves.

Oh, and in the interest of full disclosure, this person was not a druid.

Station Member 		posted 02-01-2001 02:04 PM           
Being a warrior, I don't have any fancy ways to kill a half-dead creature and take the experience away. I've been on both sides of the situation, and when someone is taking a beating I will ask them if they need help. If they say "yes," then I will walk up, try first to taunt and then take a couple desultory hits if taunting isn't working. I have no problem taking some damage if someone is really in need.

On the other side of the equation, there are times when I am just having no luck finding a group (not in any guild on TP) so will attempt to find something in my range to kill solo. Unfortunately, especially over the past few days, I've had times when something even bigger pops and proceeds to whipping me badly. A couple of times there have been players in range who have just stood there watching even as I type /y to try and get a little assistance. At these times, I don't care about the experience, except that which I'm about to lose, so I'm all for anyone piling on and taking what they can get. However, it seems like people outside of the newbie zones are a little too jaded and think that anyone who needs help is someone who should "not be soloing and should find a group." Some of us are just having trouble finding one and don't want to sit on our butts waiting for something to open up. Others of us might have just been trying to get from point A to point C and got unlucky at point B. This negative effect on me, as the example, is magnified by the fact that I wasn't able to bind myself as soon as I walked in the zone so that I could save time in corpse retrieval.

My point is that it's wrong to assume that the person yelling for a little assistance is doing so because they were doing something that they shouldn't have. It's equally possible that they are an "innocent" victim like myself and really do need the help, not caring where the xps go.

As sort of a PS, also remember that there are newbies around every day, and not everyone has been playing 20-30 hours a week for six months.
