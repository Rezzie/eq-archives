"The Death of the Healing Classes"


"Behold, brethren, the mightest sweep of the dark scythe called 'Nerf'. See how it castrates clerics and leaves them dead, free to 'slash camp desktop' to leave this mortal coil and those who surround them and give them false praise and friendship in exchange for the almight power they hold against us. And see how we druids and shaman capitalize on the newly introduced weaknesses forced upon the priestly class by gaining acceptance by others. Now we shall have our day and rule without consequence as the whining masses of dread-filled clerics bound away with their tails between their legs, never again to dominate a deep-dungeon raiding party! Our wishes are here!! We are now the primary healers!!! Rejoice!!"

/vast applause from the remaining healers in Norrath, the druid and shaman community.

The ripples of delight spread through the landscape as the newly-appointed saviors of EQ get together with others and prepare their new healing spells for use in combat. Druids scout positions and shaman cast their buffing spells on the nearby group-members. Enchanters prepare for their cascade of powerful magiks and warriors sharpen their Lamentation blades in preparation of battle. The lithe monk heads out past the rounded corridor and throws a shuriken at the Froglok Armorer.

"Incoming, incoming!! I have captured this foul creature's attention!!! Kill it!!" growls the brave monk as he rounds the hallway and returns to the group with the enemy in tow.

The battle is joined. Swords and clubs pound the froglok as invasive energies penetrate the froglok's body and mind, weakening it and clouding it's mind. A hard-hitting backstab to the creature's upper chest gains notice and the Froglok turns and slams himself into the rogue, crushing bones and armor alike. Swirling blue lights surround the wounded rogue as the shaman pumps magical life into his ripped body. The Armorer turns and in two great leaps, lands in front of the shaman both fists flailing at the tall Norseman. The enchanter streams balls of colorful lights at the creature, hoping to stun it momentarily while the group regains control of the situation.

The druid uses his own healing energies on the shaman while the rogue and warrior taunt and mock the amphibian from behind, slashing at its thick green skin and opening up massive wounds. The froglok pays them no heed and continues to pummel the tall healer backwards into the wall. Moments from unconsciousness, the shaman manages to bolster his failing health with his own spells, further aggrovating the Armorer.

The froglok doubles his efforts and bashes the shaman one last time before its fists silence the medicine man forever. The rest of the group, enraged by the loss of their comrade, scream and wade deep into battle, using everything at their disposal to kill the foul creature in their midst. Finally, the creature falls smoking and in tatters as the group sits down exhausted, lamenting over their pyrrhic victory and the loss of their shaman. A silence spreads over the small battleground as the group acknowledges what was gained and more importantly... who was lost.

"Rallos' blood, I could NOT taunt that evil thing off of you, friend" chimed the warrior, saddened at the loss and his own recently changed abilities.

"Did you see the madness in that froglok's eyes? It was crazed from the moment my healing spell landed. This is something new, good fellows... something which smacks of evil magiks and wrong doing. The gods must be angered by something," spoke the shaman in solemn tones.

"Brother, where are you bound?" asks the monk as he picks through the pockets of the deceased froglok.

"Firiona Vie" replies the saddened Barbarian.

"How long will it take you to get back here, good sir?" asks the rogue. He fiddled with the small clammy coins he was able to pickpocket from the froglok during the fight.

"About fifteen minutes.. but that is not my main concern. I lost my level - - and I have no idea on how I am going to rejoin you gents," deadpans the shaman, knowing that his invisibility won't help him get more than a few hundred feet into the fetid dungeon before the other frogloks descend upon his naked form. "This is indeed a weighty problem."

The group now must decide to fight their way back to the entrance dragging his corpse the entire way OR hire some group to escort the naked barbarian back to their location. This may take them the better part of 45 minutes, which will allow the freshly respawned frogloks to entrench themselves once again within layers of nearly impenetrable guards and soldiers. The group may even lose its hunting rights in that area because others will take it over when they see that no one is there to claim it.


All of this because there isn't a resurrection to be found. Because all the clerics' broken bodies litter the floor of this area. Because their gods have reared up and lay seige to all that the holy men knew and accepted during their slow and painful climb up to their loft status. The latest onslaught of of the dark and mysterious 'Nerf' has left many clerics sprawled about the battlefield, broken... weary... and doubtful as to their future. And in their minds their spirits lie trembling and alone, wishing and hoping against hope for something to lead them into the light. Their prayers unanswered, their dreams quietly die a little bit at a time.


And for just a flash - just a split second - each member of the group looks long and hard at the remaining druid... and silently wishes he were a cleric.

It's a bad time to be playing EQ. A bad time.


(Note: Severe sarcasm permeates this post, but there are elements of truthfulness and "what remains to be seen" aspects of this latest patch. )
