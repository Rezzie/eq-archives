D&D clearly has rubbed off a bit into EQ - but it is a different style game..

EQ is really set up around combat - quests are cool, and there are thousands upon thousands of quest - some hard, some easy - but all have combat somewhere in them...

Melee classes are all about damage - rogues are the single best ouput of damage in the game - Rangers with certain abilities are clearly the 2nd best damage output in the game.... Paladins and Shadow Knights are the best 'quick tanks' - and warriors are the longterm tanks...

There are certain areas where rogues are quite necessary ... Picking locks for doors, etc.... - there's even a few quests where if you have a rogue - you can do it quicker and easier than you could without...

I have 3 favorite classes: ranger, beastlord and shadow knight... (as for race: wood elf, vah shir and dark elf - in that order to the above classes...)

Rogues are, I think, one of the toughest classes to play. Their best rep comes from their backstab ability which really doles out the damage.

For a very first character, I'd say a paladin or shadow knight are ideal. You melee to lvl 9 so you don't have to worry about spells for a long time. You have one special ability so you get a flavor of that. But mainly, you don't have to worry about spells and the like for awhile so you can consentrate on learning how to move, socialize, fight, etc. And, about the time you've got a pretty good grip on this, the spells come along and you have something new to master - casting.

However, if you've already had a cleric for awhile, I assume you have a pretty good idea what the game entails and how to do the basic thing. So the choice is pretty much one of personal taste, I'd say. Try a variety of classes that seem to appeal to you and delete those chars that you don't really take to.

Some advice from someone who has started over 30 characters as part of an effort to get a real feeling for the game. No order implied

*>Gnome Rogue. Fairly straightforward but not as one-sided as warriors, nice introduction to how the skill system works in the game. Good newbie zone (once you find it), quite possibly the rat-tail capital of Norrath (translation, you won't have the money problems some other race/class combos have).

*>Iksar Monk. If this is the first race/class combo you ever try, you WILL be disappointed in the downtime other race/class combos experience. It happened to two of my real-world friends. Good newbie zones, regeneration, mend, and tanking ability makes for a devastating combination at first. You may find that your interest trails off after the upper 20's to mid 30's, however, when the racial XP bonus really starts to kick you where it hurts.

*>High Elf Enchanter. The first race/class I ever tried, still my favorite. Not for everyone, though -- you will have trouble purchasing all your spells until well into the teens or twenties. At higher levels, every group wants an enchanter; however, you may at times have trouble finding a pickup group because almost no group wants 2 enchanters.

If you are looking for a race/class combo with a very good role-playing feel to it that also introduces you to EQ, I would highly recommend the Vah Shir Beastlord.

Shar Vahl has some of the best 'newbie' quests around. The first thing any young Vah Shir has to do is complete their citizenship quest which sends you all over town, and you even have to pledge your loyalty to the king. The town itself is interesting, with a consistent theme to it, and the quest itself will net you enough experience to get to level 2 without killing a single thing. It really gets you into role-playing a Vah Shir.

The beastlord class is a good one to learn the ropes of EQ with. You are a 'melee' type character so it's relatively easy to get the hang of it at the very low levels (e.g. point at nasty, hit autoattack) but once you progress to level 9 you will get spells. Having spells introduces a whole new aspect to the game (as well, one of those spells is a pet - which are always fun to have around). The beastlord isn't too dependent on good equipment to start out with (it's one of only 2 classes that can get by with just punching - no need for a weapon even) and once you hit level 9 you've got a pet to take some of the blows for you if you do find your armor lacking at that point.
