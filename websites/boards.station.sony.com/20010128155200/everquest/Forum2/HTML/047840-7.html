Amberwind,

I too am inclined to agree with you.

Even from a programming stand point, this should be a flat multiplier applied all xp gains/losses. In short, all xp you gain is now multiplied by your class modifier.

If you gain 100 xp for a kill (whether a solo gain or from a group "cut") it looks like it's just going to be multiplied by a number:

Warrior & Rogue: 0.9
Cleric, Druid & Shaman: 1.0
Enchanter, Necromancer, Magician & Wizard: 1.1
Monk: 1.2
Paladin, Shadow Knight, Ranger & Bard: 1.4

If you lose xp to death, same multiplier. I doubt it will matter if you're in a group or not, and if it does, I pray Verant will look at this thread still and see that THIS is the fix needed. If you want to call all classes balanced, then yes, 1.0 should be the class modifier for all classes. However this is the BEST way to implement the change, as it's the easiest to stick into the code. Otherwise a full re-working and re-calculation of the xp tables would be required, which sucks.

That also removes any concerns I have about the soloer suffering a larger penalty from death (losing a 1.1 factor and gaining a 1.0 factor for those who didn't read my earlier post), if the xp modifier applies to all xp gains.

It would be easier to just say, "All xp gains will be modified according to your class so everyone levels at the same rate, and suffers the same proportion of xp loss from death."

I have mixed feelings about race penalties still. Trolls will have a 20% penalty compared to a dark elf; grouped from 1 to 50, the dark elf will hit 50 when the troll hits 40. Is the regeneration and slam ability worth that?

Likewise, a pair of clerics, halfling and dark elf (what a duo) would equate to the halfling hitting 50th while the dark elf was halfway done with 47th. Much smaller impact, but still apparent.

If anyone has the wrong impression, I LOVE this new change... just voicing my concerns and what I HOPE this will equate to--a flat modifier to xp gain/loss based on class, thereby removing the xp penalty for class from the game.

Increased loss at death is unfortunate, but I will trust Verant if they say it's too much downtime to implement a new xp table without retroactive level changes. It seems the good will HEAVILY outweight the bad here.

Regarding the ZEM to places like the Hole, yes--that seems the best place to go obviously, but when I avoided Karnor's Castle due to overcrowding, The Hole wasn't a viable option because the reward wasn't worth the risk. If The Hole is overcrowded, I can go to Karnor's Castle, though.
