The main problem with Planes of Power does not have to do necessarily with the design of the expansion itself, but could have been prevented by some design changes, hence a flaw in the expansion.

A good deal of the tier two and beyond "boss" encounters (ie flag encounters) have a very long reset, even if you fail. That means GuildX can keep any other guild from ever doing that flag event by having people there to fail it every time it spawns (once you get it on a timer it's easy to do) This keeps other guilds from advancing and allows these farmer guilds to farm the upper tier planes without competition. That can't be what was intended by Sony.

In addition to this you have these same farmer guilds coming back with their, now, all level 65 guild in massive equipment and taking out the smaller targets as well. Since it takes little effort for them it's not unusual to see GuildX come down, take out Klandicar, Zlandicar, then pop into North Temple to take out the nameds. There's no reason for them to do it other than to keep other guilds from advancing. They don't need the loot, they don't need the key pieces, they just don't want competition anywhere else.

Not all "uber" guilds do this, but the ones that do prevent any guild from advancing beyond killing a few kunark dragons here and there.

The main problem, obviously, is the players. However, the game designers have a certain responsibility. The game becomes no fun and quite frustrating if you are in a guild capable of advancing but are unable to simply because other guilds choose to keep you from doing so. Your advancement should not be at the mercy of the selfish griefers.
