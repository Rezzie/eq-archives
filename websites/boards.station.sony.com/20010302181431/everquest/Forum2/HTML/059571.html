I can only assume that while the patch process is being administered to all of these servers... the Verant people are now looking at all these threads.

I am betting that they are SHOCKED at all the uproar being raised here.

This patch did so many good things to so many different classes. Why such an uproar?

....

It's simple.

Hidden among all these beneficial "adjustments" was what I believe to be the single biggest gamechanging "fix" ever to hit the world of EQ.

The hatelist. Particularly the hatelist as it regards healing. By doing such a "fix" this far into the game, you really must have but your testers on fast forward. How do you create a world and tune it over 2 years to certain "constants", only to decide to drastically change one of those constants so far after everything has been tuned?

Clerics will have to now re-learn their healing skills, which is to say thier class. They have all new aggro management to deal with now, and timing will be competely thrown off.

An awesome cleric yesterday may very well be a terrible cleric today. No matter how much sugar you pour on that, it still does not become a palatable change.

A nerf to clerics is a nerf to everyone. It seems that rather than make the cleric class more enticing to play, verant chose instead to spread all of the cleric's skills to the rest of the classes, thereby blurring the lines that seperate the cleric from his peers. Unfortunately, its the cleric who gets blurred, while the other classes get sharpened. Resses, Heals, buffs have all been slowly given to the other classes, and cleric items and abilities of interest have pretty much all been nerfed or in some cases multi-nerfed.

Clerics are currently coddled and pampered by the large guilds because they are becoming rarer and rarer at the real high lvls. Guildleaders CRINGE when they see this patch because they dread the aftereffects of losing yet some more veteran clerics.

Well... keep following this path and you might as well call clerics "dodo birds".
