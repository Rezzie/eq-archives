/shout WOO HOO!

Best news I've seen since the CS revamp.

That said, PLEASE do it right. What do I mean when I say "right"? Glad you asked. In descending order of importance...

1> Do it in such a way that it gives time-impaired and casual players an alternative to hyper-camps. With my playing time more than doubled now, I'm trying some of these for the first time and let me tell you they're NOT fun, by any viable definition.

2> Craftable items should come in two flavors:
a> Those that PLAYERS are going to want enough to pay more than cost for;
b> Those that the MERCHANTS are going to buy for *slightly* over cost (110% - 125% would be fine) to help recoup the cost of training the skill to the point the crafter can reliably create items from the first category.

3> Do it in such a way the skills can legitimatly be trained at all points along the growth chart without undue risk/expense. There are gaps in jewelry, for example, where the expense of moving to the "next step" is prohibitively expensive, but going to the next cost-efficient point is prohibitively risky. And I said UNDUE risk/expense, not free or easy. Sound Bite Version: Heck levels are bad enough, don't have Heck Points in trade skills.

4> Do it for ALL of the trade skills. Yes, I said ALL of them. Fishing, Tinkering, Begging, and even Research could have interesting revamps.

5> Do it in a timely fashion. Repeat your performance with Poison and Alchemy at your own peril.

Just a thought or three on how the skills could be revamped.

TAILORING: Introduce a caster-specific version of Cured Silk with has small/moderate amounts of mana per piece -- enough from a whole suit to cast an extra 12th level spell, on average. Also introduce a Druid-specific tailored leather armor (check with druids on what they'd want). The once-suggested and excellent delay-reducing quiver idea should also be implemented.

BLACKSMITHING: The main thrust initially should be weapons, as that's where the main "power gap" between the time-impaired and time-blessed appears to be. That's not to say improved armor isn't called for -- I'd recommend a single style wearable by multiple classes (not unlike Curscale).

POTTERY: Two words -- containers, symbols. Pottery already has a Clay Diety pattern; allow potters to make religious symbols using this single pattern but different ingredients based on faith. As for containers, let me name two very specific desires:
a> a 10-slot, small-items box with no or minimal weight reduction (and possibly the Lore limitation) to hold components, reagents, research items, and the like.
b> a 10-slot, large-items "urn" with a prohibitive weight (in the 20-pound range), also with no-to-minimal weight reduction, to serve to hold items in the bank (this item *definitely* should not be lore).
[By The By -- anybody found Urn Sketches yet?]

TINKERING: Tinkered items should come in three forms. To Wit --
a> Items that serve as superior replacements to existing in-game tools (retractable fishing poles shouldn't break, for example)
b> Items that provide constant spell effects (such as the Rebreather)
c> Very specific, narrow-use items that are as expensive to make as they are useful (a non-expendable throwing item, for example).

Thank you again, Gordon. If there's any way I can be of help implementing this, you folks have my email address.
