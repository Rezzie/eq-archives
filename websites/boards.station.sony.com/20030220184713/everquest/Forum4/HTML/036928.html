"i went out and started to hunt, i hit a bat, 14 damage! my pally was doing 14 damage at lvl 10!"

How hard you hit at low levels is a function of the Damage rating of the weapon. A 7 Damage weapon can hit as hard as 15.

When you gave the Level 1 Monk a weapon he didn't earn, you did something called "twinking". This has a huge impact on melee classes. This is why your Monk is doing as well as your Paladin; had you done the reverse (used your Monk to provide a nice weapon for your lower level Paladin), the same thing would have happened. Would you have called for a nerf of Paladins?

Also, you should really be aware that how hard you hit is not relevant in EQ. Damage output is about how much damage you deliver over a period of time, not in one blow. Since each weapon takes a unique amount of time to swing (its Delay), you should start analyzing damage output by looking at the ratio of Damage/Delay.


"whats with the armor class going up with your agil"

That's what AGI does - it makes you a little better at avoiding blows. Therefore, it raises the Damage Avoidance portion of your AC.

"his dual wield, dodge, kick, and hand to hand go up almost evry turn"

The probability formula for skill increase is exactly the same for Monks as every other class.
