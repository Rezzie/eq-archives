I do the same as Khel.

I make the armor, and hope like heck I get a skill increase in the process.

However, to raise the skill.

A friend of mine is grand master now. He made Gorgets to 182, then made masks to... errr... I think he said 192 (not really sure) then had to move to 2 sheet items.


When I do my skills,

WIS 103
STR 137
INT is 69 or so
DEX (if it matters) is in the 100 range.

Your skill increase/success rates are based on INT or WIS, whichever is higher, so I'd get one of those (whichever is closer) to over 100, THEN start worrying about STR.

Right before you start, Get yourself a REALLY good shaman buff. I've been pushed up to 203 STR by a 49th shaman. I normally average a skill increase about every 20 tries or so, but with my STR at 203, I got 7 increase in 25 tries, (banded cloaks from 168 to 175) and succeded on 24 out of those 25. It DOES make a difference.

I do see that you're a bard. I don't know if your STR song stacks with the shaman buff (druid str buff helps too). If it does, thats great, if not, *shrug* you have your song!

Good luck!

I have goal of stopping at a skill level of 199 in smithing with my Erudite mage (INT 204, STR 83) and am currently at a skill level of 195 in smithing. Though there is a hint of a cultural armor for Erudites, I would rather max out as a tailor and specialize as a tailor (go over 200 first in it). That said, I have not focused on skilling up. I just made sure that my INT was high while making fine plate pieces.

As for smithing, I've made nearly 500 pieces of fine plate now. Helms, collars and bracers are trivial now and I've made 40 pieces since hitting 195, but still no skill bump. My IRL brother and his jewelcrafting enchanter have seen S L O W increase in skill ups near 200. So, I'm not expecting to be at 200 soon.

Initially, I concentrated on selling fine plate armor and now I do primarily dyed fine plate. Demand seems to have waned for undyed armor and increased for color (or vanity, as I prefer to call it). This means that I stick to selling the visible pieces: bracer, boots, gauntlets, helm, vambrace, breastplate and leggings. I cannot remembere what of the non-visible pieces last wanted by a plater. So, I don't stock them. Otherwise, I keep 2 to 4 full sets of undyed armor in stock with a full visible set of base red, light blue, base yellow and base black, if I can. (The dye components and their extracts have been damned difficult to find -- especially, jack o'lantern fungus, charcoal and evergreen leaves. In most evenings in FayMart, I have gotten only about 2 responses with PC's willing to sell them.)

What to do about skill ups? I just focus on having it pay for itself and when skill ups come, they come. Keep the stats of inportance to skill improvement (INT or WIS) and I don't understand how STR (or if it even does) affects it. I might have had a different goal if cultural armor made sense. So, the message is if you watch the pot (in this case, skilling up), it will never boil (skill ups won't occur).
