Physts:

What was that rape comment for? It's amazing me how immature and insulting the "no nerfs!" crowd is being. Wait a minute, no it's not amazing me at all.

"Ask for the old stuff to get better. Never ask for a nerf. Please put some thought into your complaints next time and think about what really ruins the game."

I have yet to hear of a game that actually died because of overnerfing (not saying it couldn't happen, just saying I haven't heard of it). What I -have- heard that kills games far more often is Mudflation. Mudflation is exactly the scenario we're talking about. At some point, something gets put into a game that overshadows everything else (Wurmslayer is a perfect example). So, in order to keep loot balanced, your suggestion is taken and rather than fixing one item, everything else (which in EQ would be like 300 weapons worse than this one) is made better too. But wait, one of those weapons that was upgraded, or a new one that comes along, is now made out of whack too. SO, boom, upgrade everything else again, cause we can't just have a nerf, oh no, we need to rework 300 items again cause we all know just how easy balance is to achieve , everyone on these boards could have done a better job than Verant with their eyes closed. And then you upgrade again... and again... and soon you have games like Diablo 2 and Asheron's Call, that are easy as sinning, no challenge at all, gets very boring, people finish it (or see that it won't be any challenge to finish) in a couple of weeks and put it on the shelf.

It's happened to many MUD's out there, precisely because there's this vanguard of people in every one that can't look at a bigger picture and repeatedly intone "nerfs are bad, nerfs are bad". It usually takes them witnessing the destruction of a game in this way for them to realize, but by that time it's too late.

Frankly, I think Verant has been both light and wise with the nerf stick in general, and have managed to maintain the integrity of the game without going over the top.
