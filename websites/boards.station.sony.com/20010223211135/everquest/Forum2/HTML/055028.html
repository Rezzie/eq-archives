Thogg,

Your numbers are, um, incorrect.

The Mage epic pet hits for a maximum of 66.

Occasionally, on a death blow, the pet will hit for 150-170. This is a scripting error that can occur if the pet procs on the final hit (proc damage + melee damage are added and displayed on the screen). The pet is NOT doing 170 damage. It is a display error.

In an epic battle, the epic pet works something like this:

procs = resisted
damage shield = irrelevant (this is HUGE, as the damage shield, I guarantee you, did ~50% of the damage in the above tests)
melee damage = similar or less than that of the monks, rogues, and warriors

The epic pet misses ALOT. It happens to have a great number of hp and a massive damage shield ... which isn't particularly useful in groups. Pets don't tank, in case you haven't noticed.

Also, I would bet my life that the pet in the scenarios above was Burned Out (IV) and likely Muzzled. Not that it really matters ... the pet could not fight back and the melees would still lose. It's a matter of the following numbers:

Warrior hp
Warrior damage/blow
Pet hp
Pet Damage shield

None of these things have the vaguest applicability to PvE scenarios, where the damage shield (and the pet hp) are all but irrelevant.
