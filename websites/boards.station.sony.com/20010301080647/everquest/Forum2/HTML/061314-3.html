Seeing that Absor has graced this thread with his presence once, lets hope he continues to visit.

The Healing aggro change is _under_most_conditions_ (i.e., pre50th level) not as bad as people make it sound. Sure, I was playing around in the DL last night, and in 14 hours of play, saw only two deaths, both clerics. But both of them could have been avoided if the clerics were more used to dealing with being aggroed (in one case, the cleric emulated a newbie and ran off trailing the mob, heading for the zone, making it impossible to either root the mob or heal him..and didnt make it). Now, before you start flaming me about not understanding the higher game, let me get to my point.

There are three different issues regarding healing aggro:

1) Encounters involving one or two mobs that are below the 50th level cap.
The new aggro rules work fine here. This is undoubtably the situation that was being considered when the new rules were designed and 'tested' and they make sense here. Aggro is higher, but manageable amd the encounters are not super deadly.
Solution: None needed, although fixes applied to the following solutions will affect this situation as well.

2) Encounters involving many mobs that are below the 50th level cap. Note: there are many areas in the world where this situation is unavoidable. Single and double pulling is NOT possible in all areas.

The new aggro rules DO NOT work under these conditions. The problem is that a 1000 point heal generates MANY TIMES more aggro than does a 1000 point nuke or 1000 pts of damage. Scenario: Party beats on mob 1, while mobs 2-5 are mezzed, rooted or allowed to beat on tanks. After a while, someone needs a heal (could be the tank, could be the chanter,doesnt really matter). Cleric heals injured person. Mob 1 has cleric elevated to a higher aggro, but this is offset by previous damage. Mob1 _might_ aggro on cleric, but can be taunted/ damaged off. Mobs2-5 ALSO aggro on cleric, but have no offsetting hate list and thus cleric shoots to the top. If they are free to attack, they do so and the party has NO WAY to get them all off. And, even if they do (re-mez, for example)...As they beat on Mob2, a new heal is necessary. But not mobs3-5 have twice the heal aggro they did earlier...Etc. By the time you get to that last mob, it has the cleric on a hate list equivelent to ALL THE DAMAGE THAT THE PREVIOUS MOBS DID (because all that damage had to be healed at some point). This hate list cannot be offset by simple damage or taunting, and can only be offset by mem blur or Atone (and may require MANY atones, as the mobs dont always cooperate in their positioning). Thus, this situation CAN be managed with great difficulty...and if the cleric has to cast several atones, he isnt healing or medding and may not be able to get off the next heal when it is needed.
Essential problem: HEALS ADD TO THE HATE LISTS OF ALL MOBS HAVING RECIPIENT ON THEIR HATE LIST, BUT DAMAGE AND TAUNTS ONLY APPLIES TO THE ONE BEING HIT.
Proposed Solution: Heal adds to aggro, but at a 1:3 ratio. Under this rule, although each heal will add less aggro, as the cleric casts several heals, his aggro on the unengaged mobs will still be going up, resulting in a more dangerous situation for him (which was your goal, no?) while not inflating his aggro as currently occurs.

3) Post 50 Mobs.. The problem here is, as has been pointed out earlier, that the mobs to damage to the players many times faster than the players do damage to the mob. But the heals HAVE TO KEEP UP WITH THE MOBS DAMAGE RATE. And, even if you spread the damage out over several tanks, unless you have more clerics than tanks, the damage get re-concentrated BACK onto the clerics. Thus, to restate the same situation:
mob damage >>> player damage
cleric healing = mob damage
therefore: cleric healing >>> player damage

Even if you have twelve tanks and four clerics (for example)


So how do players successfully overcome heal taunt?
Solution: Add a higher level version of the DA line which has a hate reduction capable of offsetting the CHeal aggro. This seems like a logical spell and is completely in keeping with the existing spell lines and the changes being made to the hate lists. Obviously, some clerics will still die if they cant get that DA off within the three seconds they have (2 sec recovery from casting and 1 sec to cast, more or less). Sure, clerics will still die occaisionally, but at least they will have a fighting chance.
