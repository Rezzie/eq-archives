Hi everyone,
I've taken a bunch of topics out of various posts and cobbled together this monster. I ended up not getting the names of the original posters, I hope you can forgive me if I don't attribute your posts or your ideas. I guess I'm more interested in addressing the ideas than giving credit…

First, some news about a few things:

1) Despite what I previously wrote about our satisfaction with the 55th level pet spell, we are still looking at it. I'm not promising any changes. But the debate continues…

2) The Rage Line: All the spells in the line should be working along the same lines as Rage now. But we did find an error. Fury was (is) giving a dex debuff instead of a dex buff. That should be fixed with our next patch (though there is no date set for a patch yet). There were some stacking issues to consider. We've looked them over, and they are working the way they should work. So, yes, it does overwrite some stat buffs and not others. That's the way it's going to stay. Changing anything about the way they stack would undoubtedly cause other stacking issues, and nobody wants that.

Ok, on to the various post snips. Keep in mind that this is sort of a first run through. You're getting my ideas and comments here, not company policy. I'm trying to get my ducks in a row so that I can present cogent ideas to the team.

>"Icons for group disease and poison buff - when applied, the icon appears exactly the same as if you are poisoned or diseased, would it be possible to tint the icons blue so people know its a buff and not a dot. This also applies for the druid group resist fire and cold."

Actually, I think a lot of icons probably need to be redone. It's something that I really would like to see get a revamp. But, really, this isn't a Shaman thing, it's something that everyone would probably like. I'm not ignoring this idea, just moving it off this thread and into my want list.

>"Alchemy - 2 things

1) NPC merchants will only buy back potions for between 9 gold and 2 plat, regardless of the cost to make the potion. Could this be made more in line with jewlry, maybe were merchants can buy back potions for 80 - 90% of the cost. Currently, the cost of practicing is enormious with no chance of profit in the end. I spent 2500 pp to practice the other day, and went from a skill of 50 to 90, to practice above this level will cost between 15pp - 200pp per attempt."

I'll ask into it. But keep in mind that trade skills are designed to only be profitable when items are sold to players. But if the return isn't at least reasonable when selling to vendors, I'll see if I can find out why.

>"2) Currently only SOW potions have any demand at all, and occasionaly gate potions if people can afford them (cheaper to pay a druid or wizard). Could new gate potions be added to the new expansions zones?"

I'll look into it. But my first thought is that Velious is designed to be hard to get to, so that's unlikely. And, well, it might not be the intent of Alchemy to reduce the need to have a wizard or druid to zap you around the globe.

>"Torpor – Doesn’t stack with chloroplast/regrowth, spirit of wolf, Turgur’s Insects, Ignite Blood, Plague, Celestial Elixer. None of these cases make sense except for SoW. Well, stacking regens is always going to be very limited. And it makes some sense to me that you can't go into torpor while under a DoT. It's a lot of healing for little mana. It greatly reduces downtime. Personally, I think it's fine as is. But feel free to try to convince me otherwise.

This is not the main problem however, which is that for 325 mana, a 1 minute duration and a 3 minute recast, with quite questionable benefits (for example this is not anywhere near what 70% haste spells do), shamans do not use this spell at all."

Again, it seems like a great spell to me. Perhaps others can wade in here and explain to me why this isn't a good spell. Keep in mind that stacking is a very complicated issue.

>"Talisman of the Serpent – Most shamans consider this spell nothing less than an insult. Please just remove it. If you are feeling generous replace it with something a little more useful, a group Talisman of Kragg would be OK."

Not all spells will be 'A' list spells. There's no real reason to remove this spell. Heck, if you're playing with a lot of bards…

>"Stacking of Curse of Spirits, effect on epic weapon Spear of Fate- Splurt, Choke, Ignite Blood all overwrite it. It does not take on someone with Skin Like Nature series buff on. These are all obviously oversights on your part please correct."

I'll look into it and see if these are, in fact, oversights or if they are as intended. It's not so obvious to me…

1. Buffs far beyond any other class

Sounds right to me, for the most part. I have a disagreement with point #2, but I'll get to that in a second.

>"On the face, it sounds like a good plan. The realities of play at level 50 had some interesting effects:
1. Stats were never implemented in a manner to make stat buffs overly useful. The ratio of their effect to mana expendature make them iffy to use in a lot of circumstances. You must be comparing efficiently, I guess, to just blasting things. Keep in mind, though, that blasting things may not always be an option. Buffing the melee types in some cases might be more efficient.

But I'm willing to accept your comments as they are, even if I might not believe in them as strongly as you do.

Goodness, pets again!

But, essentially, pets for shaman work as intended, right? They add a bit of damage and tanking, and they look cool.

>"6. True, but largely irrelevant since at higher levels, necessity really dictates the higher efficiency of medding over melee in most situations, and we were never close in combat effectiveness anyway."

But not at all irrelevant to those in the level to use it. The ability for a shaman to melee pretty well makes the earlier levels a fairly easy go for them. Again, I think this works out well enough.

Absor I've been thinking about the problems with epic defense and tanking for melees. The issues with giving more skill points to hybrids or not to make them viable in their traditional roles and front line fighters.

What I think is that your developers are running out of dynamic range in the parameters that they are using to balance these classes, skills in defense, parry, dodge, block and riposte. These are all damage avoidance skills and are the only variables that really prove effective against uber 1000 damage per hit npcs.

Verant does have more room in dodge than the other skills and I think rangers should get much
higher dodge for example.

Now on to the point of this being a shaman issue. Two things that are being overlooked in the balancing act are the physical stats: strength, agility, stamina and dexterity. We all know that these are almost nonfacters currently for combat purposes. Being much stonger doesn't help you to hit or hit much harder for example.

I propose that Verant improve the effect of these stats on combat performance by 10 to 20% depending on the stat. Log parsing has shown that most stats offer little to no measurable improvement above the 3 to 5% error tolerance for the sample.

The main stat I think should be is AGILITY because it does improve damage avoidance and mitigation in some small measure. By enchancing agility's effects by 20% this empowers more melee classes to have more defense. Adding more room for balance and more interesting variables to achieve it by the players.

Increasing stat effects also returns much balance to the shaman class. It was a terrible blow for shaman (and jewelers) when the players learned how little stats (and hence race) matter in EQ. A poor choice by Verant, but one you really SHOULD reconsider at this point in game balancing.

Increasing stat effectiveness creates more room for balance, more room for diversity, more room for equipment choices. Verant doesn't have to spend $$$ on more content. Just making stats measurably effective and worthwhile instantly makes alot of existing equipment and spells valued and used. Instantly more content for the players.

I propose that these stats be improved:

Strength - 10% more effective
Stamina - 15% more effective
Agility - 25% more effective
Dexterity - 10% more effective

At the highest levels, hitpoints and defense need to scale up alot more to match the abilities of players versus npcs. Agility and stamina do it, but not enough. A wood elf warrior doesn't get missed enough and an ogre warrior doesn't have enough extra health.

Shaman's array of currently useless stat buffs would become valuable and desired. Instantly giving them dozens of "new" spells that are wanted and balanced.

Verant you have more variables to play with in balancing the game than you seem to realize. Quit beating your head against the equipment and skills wall. ENABLE your stats to work for you as well and your players will rejoice!

Ok, here is the second try on this post. First one got lost at the end of the last shaman talk 5. This is nothing new, more a refinement of ideas for adding side effects to stat buffs. First, as has been proposed before each stat buff would have an additional bonus effects. There is a minimum stat level for each bonus to take effect. For instance if the minimum for str is 150 and you have a 180 str. 30 points of str will count towards the bonus. The actual bonuses could be as follows:

Str - added percentage damage to *each* hit. We use percentage as opposed to a fixed value to avoid giving an advantage to smaller faster weapons.

Agi - added precent chance to dodge. This should be significant at higher levels.

Dex - added percent chance to hit or alternatively an increase in attack speed.

Sta - added percent to all resists.

These bonus values would only apply when a shaman buff is in effect. For instance a Ogre warrior with a natural str of 200 would not get the bonus. However an Ogre warrior with a natural str of 160 plus a 40 point shaman str buff would.

The minimum values for these stats could be set high enough so that they would only be effective when cast on melee classes, or the bonus effect could even be limited so that it *only* works on melee classes. This also gives a melee character the choice of specializing in offence or defense by choosing to concentrate on raising one stat. He would then know, once he gets a shaman buff to trigger the bonus, he would be really good in one area.

This would make stats more meaningful without unbalancing the game, since it woudn't change the way stats work under normal circumstances. As you can see this would be something that would get shaman into groups, without giving them more soloing power. Stat buffs after all are supposed to be our forte. This would really make them so.

Finally Verant would not have to add a single spell to make this work.
