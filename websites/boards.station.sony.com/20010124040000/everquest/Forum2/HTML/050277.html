I've been listening to all the post 50 class complaints and it seems to me that there is a core issue here. CHANGE

Please do not dismiss this factor as the basic human resistance to change - there is more to this than that.

Let's look at character "evolution". A new player buys this "way cool" game and after a little reading selects a character he / she thinks he'd like to play. By the time this person hit's level 20 they either "like" the character and how it's played or they re-roll a new one. By then they also have some idea as to how the other classes interact too and are more informed and better read on the class differences. To cut to the chase: by the time someone plays a character to level 50 or more, they presumably *know* this character and *like* this character.

It is to a large degree the addition of the 2 expansions that caused fundimental changes to some of the classes - I'll use Rangers and Enchanters as examples because I'm very familiar with the issues, NOT to slight other classes with the same type of "growing pains".

Rangers always lived under the shadow of the low defensive skill caps. However, when Kunark and 10 more levels came out, this problem that reared it's head at 45-50 became UNBEARABLE at levels 50+. The addition of Kunark and 10 extra levels coupled with a lack of corrosponding change to the Ranger class made for an unworkable situation.
Rangers were VERY unhappy. The way they played (or HAD to play) thier character changed TOO MUCH.

Now in Vellious, Enchanters find that their major group skill - crowd controll - is seriosly hampered by the fact that the MoB's won't Mez. The way they have to play there is CHANGED from the way they know and love thier character.

A major design consideration when adding content should be to *not* change the game for the people that love it. In both expansions, this hasn't been looked at well enough in advance and now the classes are (justifiably) crying about their new lot in life.

What I submit to Brad and Verant is *not* to look at it "class by class" first, but rather to say, "What did we do to the post 50 game to CHANGE the way people have to play their favorite class?" Secondly "How can we restore the old order and balance?"

I am *not* asserting here that a little variety and challenge, forcing people to adapt to new threats and challenges isn't good. I'm saying that when *as a general rule* people are forced to redefine their character and it's role is bad.

Class dilution with high level spells / abilities doesn't help this iether. Again, people want to feel "special" not easily replacable. Each class should bring a "special ability" to the table.

In summary - to force people to learn/play a whole new game post 50 isn't fair. Some character evolution is a good thing, forcing a massive change on classes isn't good and leads to complaints.
