The Warrens, though you might have to hunt some greens for bronze and molars, to save some plat.

Also, 10 is kind of difficult from an xp perspective in the warrens. Too many aggroing light blues, to few blues until about 12, when you can penetrate back to a higher concentration of blues.

Might want to look at buying a nonmagical broadsword from a weaponsmith. Its pretty poor, but beats the starting weapon, and costs 3-5 plat. The bronze and money from the molars quest in the Warrens will fairly quickly get you 50-100 plat, where you can get a much better weapon.

Also, there is a quest up in AkAnon for the bullsmasher... non magical weapon, but quite nice.

Bullsmasher

Damage: 6
Delay: 20
Ratio: 3.333 Good
Str: +2 Agi: +2 Dex: +2
Weight: 4.0
Weapon Skill: One Hand Blunt
Classes: Ranger Shaman Cleric Paladin Shadowknight Warrior
Races: Halfling Dwarf Gnome
Inventory Slot: Either Melee
Can be stored in Medium and larger containers

At eqatlas, look for the Red V quest for the bullsmasher. Can be quite tedious (like many quests) but the result is well worth - you can use this weapon to keep your one hand blunt up for a long, long time (use it at higher levels on mobs doing the walk of death - that slow fleeing walk when a mob is near death).

Hmm, also, in the Paineel newbie zone, you can accumulate few stacks of bone chips, and then sell them in Greater Fey for 4-5 plat per stack. And, there is a nice bone chip quest in Kaladim as well, that returns items that can be sold for a couple of gold per, plus faction which you will need later in your career.

You've got lots of options at this point. The warrens and Paineel are actually fairly close by, if you have Luclin.

The first thing to do is grab a rusty weapon from a goblin or decaying skeleton, buy a sharpening stone, and sharpen it. Sharpening stones can be purchased for 3-4 copper pieces, and sharpening is done in a forge using the smithing skill. The good thing about sharpening weapons is that a failure does not destroy the weapon. Sharpening a rusty weapon turns it into a tarnished weapon, which will have slightly lower delay.

Then go shopping for a Fine Steel or Velium weapon.

Go to Crushbone and kill orcs. They drop decent loot, are plentiful and nearby, and they also frequently drop Crushbone belts. These are a nice little quest item in your home town. You get experience, faction, and some money/items.

An often-overlooked item in Crushbone is the Dwarven Mace. It is an interim item in the Screaming Mace quest, but it's a nice weapon itself. You get it by giving a Dwarven Ringmail Tunic to the dwarf smith in Crushbone. The DRT is often free, since it's lore, and people camping the tower often can't loot them and will shout that they are available.

You can easily take the Nexus Express to The Warrens, which I consider one of the best lower-level zones in the game. The druid ring isn't far from the Nexus/wizard spires, and you can sell loot there. If you are like me, you will struggle out of The Warrens every 30-60 minutes, encumbered with loot.

You can also make a lot of money hunting bears in East and West Commonlands. The HQ pelts sell for 35 pp on my server; other servers apparently vary, but they still bring some nice money.

Shop around for a Velium weapon of some kind. These are magic, so they will hit a wisp, scarecrow, or ghould. Otherwise, they are equal to Fine Steel in damage and delay. (They have a little Velium Shards proc, but it didn't start firing on my druid's scimitar until level 27. Most characters will have a much better weapon before 27, so you can ignore the proc.) Once you have a magic weapon, you can lay waste to the West Commonlands wisps. Each greater lightstone can be finagled into about 9 pp in North Karana.

Good hunting!
