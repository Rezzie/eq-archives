Station Member 		posted 10-27-2000 08:46 AM           
Everything that is sold to a vendor does go into
his/her inventory, you just don't get to see
it when their display is all filled up.

So, you find something cheap and, preferably, stackable and buy it out. If you have the patience, sell it to another vendor (Unless this is the first vendor you are delving, in which case just blow it up in a large sewing kit).

Now, if you are looking for something specific, say, a spidering silk, once you have the empty slot in their inventory, sell them a spiderling silk. IF the silk jumps into that slot, they don't have any, move on. BUT if it doesn't, it has joined it's brothers and sisters buried in their backstock, just waiting for you to find it.

So, you escape out and right click again. All the inventory will have moved up and something new will be on the end. Rinse, repeat until you get what you want.

Of course, if you want tons of stuff, just keep digging. Look for my post on page two about backstock. That will give you an idea how much good stuff you can get (11 stacks of silk swatches, 39 stacks of backings, etc). Takes time and money, but in the long run it is worth it.

A merchant mining tip:

Buy up all of one visible item in the merchant's inventory, then SELL one of the item you are looking to buy from the merchant. If your item is shown in the recently emptied slot, the one you sold is the only one the merchant has. If the slot remains open, the merchant has more of that item hidden away. This will save a lot of time in buying merchants out just to see if they carry what you want.
