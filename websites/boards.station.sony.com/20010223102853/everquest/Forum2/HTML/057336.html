I believe that warriors are having a bit of an identity crisis. EQ is a role-playing game, and we are struggling to find our role.

The warrior class is one without flexibility. We have no spells, and no unique special abilities. (some disciplines, but they are mostly useless, and most do not come into play until the 50's)

What do we gain to offset this lack of flexibility? According to the EQ manual, we are the "masters of armed combat". But we are not, monks are. Instead, we are glorified punching bags. We stand and swing our weapons, not so much to inflict damage, but to distract the mob from hitting the monk, rogue, and casters. Is that all we are, glorified punching bags, begging to be bruised so that someone with martial or magical mastery can inflict enough damage to kill our enemy?

It is, and now that small ability to absorb damage, the only thing that we can do better than other classes, is threatened. If we are only slightly better tanks that hybrids, why play a warrior?

The root of the problem is that if warriors are noticeably better tanks than hybrids, then groups will almost always prefer a warrior to a hybrid. Conversely, if there is not a noticeable difference between the tanking abilities in warriors and hybrids, a group will almost always perfer the hybrid. Neither of these two situations is balanced.

It seems as though Verant has gone with the second scenario.

I express sympathy for hybrid characters, since inferior melee ability + inferior spellcasting ability = inferior character. However, I don't think that diminishing the melee superiority of warriors is the way to go.

This is what I suggest:

1. Fix taunt, so that warriors can compel mobs to engage them in melee combat

2. An area effect taunt that would work against multiple targets within a certain radius of the warrior. This would be a seperate button.

3. Give us better gear, at all levels of the game. Also it would be nice if we could actually get it.

4. Improve the hybrid classes without diminishing our relative melee prowess. The hybrids should get abilities that improve them that are not derived from their parent classes. A possibility would be reducing refresh time for ht and lh.

5. Increase the ammount of damage that warriors do. This falls partially under the gear issue, but I think it is worth mentioning seperately. Also, all melee damage should be increased. Does it make since for an unarmed mob 5 levels below me to be out-damaging me?

Now some issues that are not warrior-specific, but still affect warriors:

6. Melee classes should be able to bind themselves in a city. What would be wrong with a /bind command?

7. Why should teleporting be dependant on 2 classes? When many people create alternate characters primarily for this ability, doesn't this reek of imbalance? As Norrath expands, the issue of mobility will become more important. Does verant want the majority of the population to be wizards and druids? Even now, how long would it take to go from kunark to velious by boat? I don't like to spend time begging for ports and sows, or watching my character walk or ride a boat.

14 months ago after playing a few different classes to level 10, I decided that I'd rather focus on being a melee class, in particular the melee class charged with focusing the mobs attention on me. I chose to be a warrior. Why is the warrior the best at this? Well at the time it was supposed to be because of the fact that we had superior HP and AC. Why could we attract the attention of the mobs? Because we could do enough damage and taunt, so we could stay on the mobs hate list.

Why did I choose a Barbarian? To have more HP. What did I sacrifice and gain by being a Barbarian? I sacrificed decent night vision, I have a hard time moving around in certain places and I have an xp penalty. I gained more HP and the ability to slam, thus interrupting spells, without using a shield.

What did I sacrifice by being a warrior. I sacrificed every form of independence possible. I am completely dependent on a group, for both travel and combat. I have no spells. I have no skills other then taunt, disarm (when does a warrior use this other then duals?) and slam. I can't invis myself, heal myself, fall dead, snare or root a mob when I'm in trouble, sneak or hide, harmony, SoW, etc.

What were supposed to be the rewards for these sacrifices? To be THE damage taker.

What happened to me because of this? Until level 50 when I really began to shine I waited on lists, sometimes 4 hours to get xp. Hybrids, druids, necros, shamans, mages could solo at these levels, not efficiently, but I would have loved to have spent those hours sitting in the corner of the tiger room waiting, to be instead killing something every 15 minutes. I died frequently traveling because I had no get out of jail free card.

So now I'm level 56. When I go to a dungeon people want me in a group usually to take one spot out of the 6 available. Because I can take the damage and stand. This supremacy in the damage taking ability was supposed to be (according to what verants stance used to be) my reward for having no independance, no spells and no skills.

Disciplines, lets see... the resistance can is useful, but warriors are not unique in having it. Fearless is ok for dragon raids, but in order to use it, you have to give up using something more useful. Defensive disciplines? Most nerfs I can understand, and have a basis on SOME form of logic. Most boosts to various classes have some shred of reason embedded in them SOMEWHERE. I'm happy for the hybrids, don't get me wrong. I'm glad that they are getting some things they have been crying out for for a LONG, LONG time. I just wish Verant didn't make these changes at the expense of the warrior class. The druid and sham boosts do not seem to be encroaching on anywhere near the abilities of the clerics.
