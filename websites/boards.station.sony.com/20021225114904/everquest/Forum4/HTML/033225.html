Welcome Raven

I too have a woodelf ranger and really enjoy the class. I think you made a good choice for a first life.

My best advice to you is to remember to have fun. Don't worry too much about starting over if you are not or if something happens to your gear.

You will also want to check out Allakhazam's or EQTraders on your newbie gear quests and other quests in the Kelethin area. Some people enjoy questing, others do not.

If you think you do want to make your own Trueshot, start fletching as early as you can afford to. Go to http://www.eqtraders.com for how to fletch, also follow the link there on the link page for Aeonla's Guide to fletching (or close spelling to that...), btw, link to Allakhazam there too.

If you have the Luclin expansion, save your foraged goods and spider and spiderling silks to sell to other players in the bazaar. Or, store them up and sell them when people auction WTB (want to buy) in the zone. Don't sell them to vendors unless you want someone like me to come up and buy them for much cheaper than we are willing to pay.

Hi! wellcome to everquest

You got all the good advice above.

Only thing I can add is use your ranger tracking. Use the setting /track sort distance. This setting will always show you exactly what monsters are closest to you and where everyone is next to. By watching your track list you can avoid danger and never get lost.

Track sort distance is unique to rangers. Druids and Bards get tracking but it's nowhere, good as ranger track.

When you get your first spells at level 9 be sure to put one training point into each spell skill, so they will increase in skill. Use all your spells. Good luck and happy hunting.
