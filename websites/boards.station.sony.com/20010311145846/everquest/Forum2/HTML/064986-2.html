First of all, this is a great post. Very well worded, very calm, and no "you suck" in it at all.

>>Some great ideas here. Can't beat having a liason for each class.<<

This is probably the one thing I reallly disagree with. By having a *single* liason for *each* class, you can't get a broad view of the classes and how they work together in the CS team.

Take the Epics as an example. If you look at each one, the quests are all difficult, and most of the rewards are befitting. But if you hold them up side by side, you see great disparities in risk, effort and reward. This is a result of everyone being interested only in the quest they were designing and programming.

Better yet, have a team of six to ten liasons, each of which works with at least two *different* classes (Warriors and Enchanters; Rogues and Mages). Have them shift time to time; have them handle different projects for different classes. Give them a broad view of how the parts all work together so they'll look at the whole machine, and not just how shiny their part can be.

Sorta makes sense?
