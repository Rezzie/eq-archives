quote:
.... to ask a warrior to stop using a 2h weapon and use 1 handers?[/quote
In short answer - it is okay to request it.

Hitting a mob 4 times for 30 points each = more agro than hitting once for 120.

That said, with "good aggro management" the Main Tank can use 2H weapons fine.
[quote]cleric was getting mad because he had to heal multiple people.


If you were being healed by the cleric, then that is your fault as a monk. In a group (especially where mobs hit for 100+) as soon as the mob targets you, you should FD to drop immediate aggro and give tank aggro back.

If this means FDing 10 times in a fight, that is part of being the "non-tank". I have been the main puller in several PoN and PoD groups and I will go the hole group with only patch heals as I rarely fell below 75% health.

If a rogue gets aggro, they should stop backstabbing/attacking until tank regains agro.

If a hybrid gets agro - stop any attacks and any casting.

DD casters shouldn't cast any DD spell until mob is 60% (In traditional group settings). Blast too soon and get aggro real quick.

Aggro management is a group effort and the only person who is kind of exempt is the main healer, but if the group isn't pulling mobs too hard for the make-up of the group - then cleric shouldn't have to heal too quickly into the fight.

/em takes a breath.

Now that being said, how you mention it can make a big difference. Sis you tell him that agro can be easier held with 2 - 1H weapons? Or did you tell him to switch. Were people in the group yelling at him to taunt and keep aggro? That would frustrate a warrior pretty quickly. Were you FDing EVERY time you gained aggro? Was everyone else doing what they could to not gain aggro?

Not getting aggro in the first place is as much work as holding aggro, so I doubt the sole problem was the warrior.
