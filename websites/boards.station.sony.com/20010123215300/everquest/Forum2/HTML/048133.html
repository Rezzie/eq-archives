To graern :
"actually, humans are the baseline for the other races' bonuses/penalties. not to mention the fact that they already said they are not changing racial exp modifiers, only class modifiers."

Humans were originally not conceived to be the "baseline for the other races". You may have noticed that the various races all receive certain racial bonuses. Ranging from forage to hide to infravision to stun immunity to regeneration to incredible starting stats (for certain classses). Humans get none of these perks. The one "bonus" that humans were supposed to receive according to the little paper manual that came in the box a year and half ago was that they had an "experience bonus". I played a Troll SK for 25 levels and started over because I could already tell that the time investment required to play the game was going to be enormous. Other players who were much higher level only confirmed this when I spoke with them. So I made a human thinking it would be better. I suspect a number of other players made humans characters following the same logic. Let's face it there isn't much of a reason to play a human from the standpoint of game mechanics only role-playing. It is not that big of a deal, just a small thorn in the side of every human.

I was thinking that now would the time to address this issue because the experience system is receiving development attention. If this is unreasonable please tell me why.
