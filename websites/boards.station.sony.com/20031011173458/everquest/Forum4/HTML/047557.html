Trying to find good xp in EQ is like trying get stock market prices by disembowelling a chicken and examining the entrails.

I love watching xp in MMORPGs - it's like a little sub-game. In EQ xp seems to change constantly, depending on known factors like who comes and goes from the group, what mobs you kill, what zone you are in, what level you are and lots of more nebulous, impossible to nail down factors.

Personally, I never found the Tier 1 Planes to be good xp, not from when I first started there at 51 and definitely not now at 59. However, that was very much because I was almost always the highest level in the group (tanks tend to be). Other people in my guild were getting great xp, because the dark blues I was getting were yellow and red to them.

They were getting 1% - 2% a kill in PoN. I have never seen better than 0.5% a kill there and nowadays at 59 it is more like 0.25%, if that. I did, however, get about 0.75% a kill outside the castle in PoD at 59, where the mobs are 55 - 57 which suggests the key factor is mob level rather than ZEM.

I prefer to look at xp-per-hour as "good xp", not xp-per-kill and for that you want a steady stream of easy kills - a ZEM and group bonus helps a lot there too. What really kills good grinding per-hour xp is the time wasted by deaths and having to set up again.
