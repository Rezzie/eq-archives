Actually, from a role playing point of view, it kind of makes sense that your attention would not be focused on what is going on in the world of everquest.

When you are meditating, you should block out all external stimuli.

When I proposed this idea:

http://boards.station.sony.com/everquest/Forum2/HTML/056923.html

The idea was for the "game" to be more like power meditating.

Instead of it being just something to do during down time, it would provide a distraction from the world of Everquest, and would allow you to gain mana at a faster rate then just looking at a book, and watching the conversations and health of your group.

If your in a group, low on mana, and you are in a situation where you need to be focused on the encounter, then you SHOULDN'T be in /gems.

If your group just finished an encounter, and you want to get mana up fast, then go into /gems for a few minutes, to get your mana back faster. The better you do at the game, the faster you will get mana back, but if sheets hit the fan, and you are not ready, well, that's the price you pay for being able to get the extra mana.

If you are soloing, and you don't really need to pay attention to the group dynamics, then /gems just relieves the boredom of meditation.

I don't know if they implemented /gems to provide for faster mana generation then normal medding. But, it sure seemed to go a lot quicker to me.
