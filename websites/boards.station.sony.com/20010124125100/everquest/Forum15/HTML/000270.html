I wanted to re-visit this topic, since it's been awhile since the original post.

Hybrids used to be great, back when EverQuest first came out (that's when I started my Paladin). We had almost the same fighting skills as Warriors, wielded the same weapons (everyone wanted a ykesha, mithril 2-hander, or skarlon sword, it seemed), and wore the same armor (everyone dreamed of Rubicite).

Since then, Verant had given Warriors:
- berserker rage
- critical hits
- critical blows
- kicks that stun
- the best armor in the game
- the best weapons in the game
- massive resistance increases vs hybrids
- massive hit points increases vs hybrids
- greatly increased skill caps vs hybrids
- spells (in the form of disciplines)
- more spells (in the form of armor that casts spells they didn't already have)
- huge increases in skill caps vs hybrids at 51+

The justification for these major changes that resulted in a slow but dramatic shift in the viability of warriors was that the class was originally not good enough, and not enough people were playing them.

The problem is, the main price that hybrids paid for their advantage over warriors was their xp penalty. Well, the advantages over warriors have diminished into a distinct disadvantage against warriors (to most), yet the xp penalty remains.

The Verant FAQ implies that the real reason for the xp penalty is that hybrids are allowed to cast spells in addition to maintaining reasonably good fighting skills, and the xp penalty is the price for this. The problem is, at the very highest levels of play, the fighting skills of hybrids are dramatically inferior to the pure melee classes, and their spellcasting abilities are extremely limited. Warriors have been enhanced so much (and the dependency on pure casters at the highest levels is so great), that there is little room for hybrids left in a high level (level 50+) group.

So, even if you can find a group that doesn't mind you sponging up more xp than your fair share (based on your contribution to the group), chances are you won't be the best fit, because at the highest levels the specialization inherent in pure classes is much more desirable.

What is the solution? There is no easy one, unfortunately.

The easiest to implement would be an increase in skill caps. Perhaps add some better armor for hybrids that doesn't double-tax them (lower defense plus significantly lower AC from your armor is a very tough price to pay at high levels). And better swords too (same argument...lower offense + lower weapon efficiency makes it very tough for you to effectively contribute).

How about making the spells on the hybrid armor at least as useful as the spells on the warrior armor (i.e. don't give us spells we already have and no longer use, give us spells that add a new ability, as the warrior armor gives them)?

How about giving us a bigger mix of spells? It's a real disappointment to high-level (49+) Paladins that in group situations with a cleric present (and they are always in groups at this level, I assure you) the majority of their spells are obsolete. Yes, Valor at level 49 looks great on paper...but in-game, it's not terribly useful, because there are plenty of clerics around to give you Resolution or Heroism.

I'm open to constructive comments, intelligent feedback (especially from Verant), and differing opinions. I do not appreciate flames ("well, if you knew how to play your class, you wouldn't think it sucks..." Trust me, I know how to play a Paladin...been doing it since day 1 of EQ). I do not appreciate posts like "yeah, but my class is even more screwed, so shut up!" either. Give me a little credit, I have 140 days played, have taken my Paladin from patchwork armor to leather to banded to rubicite to valorium to deepwater, from rusty sword to combine to ghoulbane to mithril to Fiery Avenger, and have used all of the spells available to me extensively and it many, many different situations.

Thanks for your time.

Glaux, nice reply, I agree with some of your points. However, in my opinion the SK is in slightly better shape, balance-wise, than the Paladin (their weapons are better, and their spells are more useful in group situations because many of them deal damage or are self-buffs, so don't necessarily lose effectiveness when the parent-class is present in the group).

That being said, I disagree on berserker rage and critical hits/blows being "not terribly important." These add up fairly quickly, and also change the strategy of warriors a bit, which would be a very nice addition to the hybrid classes. I'm not saying Verant should make hybrids be as good in berserker rage mode or achieve critical hits and blows as massive as their warrior counterparts, but at least let us in on the action a little bit...it would make our classes a lot more interesting to play and wouldn't unbalance things at all, in my opinion.

As mentioned by someone else, warriors getting the ability to have kicks than stun is not comparable to our bash ability (they have bash too, of course). The problem is that they previously had to make the tradeoff (small race warriors, anyway) to either be able to stun (using bash) or dual wield. Now they no longer have that tradeoff, so it's quite an improvement to their class. Hybrids should also receive some kind of skill upgrade at level 55 or 60 (as warriors do)...why don't they? Wouldn't unbalance things I'm sure, and, again, would make our classes more interesting.

"Warriors with out gear don't have much to offer a group. However with high AC they can take hits.. that's what they are good at." This I really don't understand. One of the primary things that Paladins and SKs have to offer groups is their defense (they sacrifice offense for improved defense in RoK), so shouldn't this argument, that they are not as useful to their group without great armor, be even more true for us?

Massive HP increases "are not that extreme." I disagree...regardless of what equipment you put on a hybrid, they really don't even come close to a warrior, in terms of HP, at the higher levels. I'd say warriors have anywhere from 800 to 1500 more hit points than a hybrid, wearing similar class-specific armor, at level 60.

"discipliens...are over rated." Absolutely not! These provide key strategies for the warriors I know and enable them to do things (such as fight effectively against Venril Sathir) that hybrids cannot. I am encouraged that Verant has said they are looking into adding disciplines for hybrids (but highly discouraged that they believe they'll not be very useful).

As far as the skill cap increases go, why not maintain the ratios set forth by the original skill caps? You would think all the other changes made since the initial release of EQ would have been enough to balance warriors out...but along comes RoK and Verant dramatically changes balance again by giving them massive skill caps increases. To me, this was the nail in the coffin to the hybrid classes at lvl 50+.

---------

Again, just to make it clear, i don't think Hybrids should be as good as Warriors in every respect (or, even, any respect, melee-wise at least). But they should be closer than they are now (as they originally were, when EQ was first released, and many of us die-hard hybrid players created our characters).

Glaux, I'm sorry but I have to disagree with your argument:

"For example a paladin can park a mob, a great technique when no chanter or shamman is around."

Do not forget that Clerics, Druids, and Rangers can do this as well, if not better, than Paladins. So that leaves us 6th on the list of usefulness in this category.

"Paladins make good secondary healers. For example, not to long ago I was in a long involved battle in Karnors, the cleric in my group was oom as was the shamman in the group. A paladin in my group kept me alive long enough to finish the battle."

Yes, Paladins can serve as a good 3rd or 4th healer in a group (and that's not exactly a key position in a group, is it?), though due to lack of mana and advanced healing spells this ability diminishes rapidly as your approach level 60 (i.e. with all the hit points players have from the advanced high level buffs, not to mention the greater number of hit points they have from being 55+, Paladins can't exactly heal as much of a player's hit points, percentage-wise, as they can at lower levels).

The bottom line is, due to the extremely wide gap between hybrids and warriors at the highest level (55+), and the extremely wide gap in the effectiveness in our spells vs our parent classes at these levels, it is extremely difficult to argue that a hybrid is a worthwhile addition to a high-level group.

This is a situation that is started with the release of RoK, mainly because the increases in skill caps (not to mention disciplines and enhancements to the relative effectiveness of their armor) that warriors were given greatly diminished the relative melee strength of hybrids.

In other words, at the highest levels (55+) it is very difficult to make a good argument that a hybrid can be as beneficial to a well-balanced group as a pure caster or meleer, especially when you consider the additional xp tax they place on the rest of the group.
