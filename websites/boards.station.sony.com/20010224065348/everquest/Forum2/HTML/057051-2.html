Ok here is an idea I have for making necros more useful in groups without affecting our solo power.

Problem: dots are too slow for normal exp groups - creatures die in 20 or 30 seconds, not the 2 minutes it takes for the dot to run. And making dots faster would make necros more powerful solo.

Solution: dot run time is determined by group size. Specifically, dot duration = current duration / number in group. So soloing, they are identical to now. In 2 person groups, half the time. In 6 person groups, one sixth the time.

So, take your typical 2 minute necro dot. In a six person group, this dot will run through in 20 seconds, which is about right! Most kills in 6 person exp groups take 20 to 30 seconds.

It still doesn't solve dot stacking of course.

More on this: "DoTs are inherently efficient spells but are countered by the duration of the spell..."

This got me curious to look up the efficiency of DOTs compared to nukes. Let's take a 49th level player and compare the two.

A 49th necro has a few dots he might use. I don't know of any necros who cast the Cascading Darkness dot (we pretty much have to use the lvl 29 dooming dark), but let's compare everything handy. For the necro we have, in order of damage/mana/cast-time/time-to-do-damage:

Cascading darkness(49): 1150/300/7sec/96sec
Bond of Death(49): 720/360/7sec/54sec
Asystole(44): 690/210/6.1sec/60sec

For wizards we have:
Ice Comet(49): 1120/400/6.3/instant
Rend(49): 784/280/6.3/instant
Force Strike(44): 625/250/5.5/instant
Conflagration(44): 625/250/5.5/instant

The necro spells are more efficient in terms of damage/mana, but not radically so. The ratios on the necro spells are 3.83, 2.0, and 3.28. The ratios on the wiz spells are: 2.8, 3.14, and 2.5. And no necro that I've ever met uses the 49 darkness.

When you consider that in most group situations the necro spells do less than half their total damage, things start looking much worse indeed for the necro spells. Cut those efficencies in half or in third then (not counting resists, which every class gets).

On the other side of the coin, the necros pet (in situations where it is permitted, and lives more than 2 rounds) is essentially a super-efficient dot. Cast once for 500 mana, and he'll dish out damage until the cows come home, even when the necro is OOM.

But the point is that necro dots don't have some sort of super-efficiency compared to nukes. If they were coming in at 6:1 ratios, then sure. But they're not. In grouping situations, nukes are far (far!) superior. So superior that I generally don't even USE my dots except for the lifetap one so that I can offset lich and heal a little with pact. I just don't mem them in groups, except for situations where, for instance, I am on snare duty (and then I use the 29 version, not the 49 version).
