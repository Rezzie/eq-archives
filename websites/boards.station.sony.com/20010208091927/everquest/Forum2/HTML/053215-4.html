Fact of life that anyone who's ever whined about being a ranger will tell you:

Offense means nothing if you can't take hits. Ask most rangers,who can draw mob attention fast but then get chopped to bits faster than any other hybrid would.

The only thing upping our offensive skills(with the exception of bash,which is as much defense as offence) would do is up our taunting without giving us a method of living through it. And we don't live through it at 50+ as it is.

And they aren't going to give us both. What do you want-the ability to hit like a warrior,or the ability to survive hits like one?

Warriors hit things and use the taunt to get their attention. We should be able to:

1) Taunt
2) Effect-over-time spell them with multiple types to keep atop the hatelist
3) Bash/Slam accurately enough to stun lesser mobs and add in another dose of pain to the hatelist
4) Use our spells to further our survival in close and heavy combat AND have those spells increase our taunt capacity still further
5) Live owing to our defense skills despite being able to keep the monster on us literally the entire fight,assuming timely healing and support.

Giving rangers the "offensive" role fits them as hybrids-because they can compensate for it if archery is done right.

Giving paladins a "defensive" role fits them as well.

Giving us a "defensive" role fits here,too. We are the shield against the light as the paladin is against the dark gods. And that is what the class should be focused towards-holding a target on us and using that target as the method to survive being beaten on by that very same thing.
