Ok. here it goes. I'm not superman like absor have the time to read through most of these posts, so please forgive me if i repeat some things which others have said.

My main char is currently a 52 wizard on Vallon (also known as the home of Xteaming) and here's what i've come to know about PvP in my lvls in no particular order:

Problem 1: Cross teaming must stop. Granted Xteaming makes sense from an RP perspective, there will be some traitors among us, and I'm ok with that. The problem I have is that it isnt just a few players RP'ing their chars as traitors, its a bunch of players who are simply out to cause others grief through immortal healing.

Solution: Ideally, I would like to see a flag set on the person casting a beneficial spell on a member of another team. This flag would set that person to being on the team of the person they helped, allowing the target of the attack to kill both of the attackers.

Problem 2) Faction hits. I dont really care about normal faction hits myself, but i know others do. I play a DE, i'm never going to walk into Erudin or some place like that. But for enchanters, this is hard. If you PK someone when you're only protecting yourself, and take a faction hit, its not right. The hits i do care about are on duels. I dont think it's right if i want to hone my PvP skills voluntarily with someone of my own team, or in a guild event, that i should take a faction hit with my own people for it. Its just silly

Solution: get rid of faction hits all together i think is the only way to really deal with it.

Problem 3: Classes are not very balanced once you get to the higher levels. As a wizard, I can kill an even con caster fairly easily. 2-3 Nukes and they're dead. An even con melee. I really have no chance of killing, especially paladins. I have on several occasions, unloaded a full bar of mana into a melee and not killed him. Red cons are far worse. Enchanters have it far worse with resists. Sometimes they cant even kill a caster due to high MR. Some classes (druids/necros) also have unresistable/nearly unresistable DoT's. Yes as a wizard, i have nearly unresistable Lures. But one lure, or 3 for that matter, will NOT kill you, but ONE unresistable DoT will.

Solution: While probably not feasable due to the fact that it would take too long to code into the system, would be an alternate set of spell effects. One for PvE, one for PvP. That way, we could tone down certain spells and upgrade others without having any effect on PvE, but unfortunately, i dont think Verant is really up to that task.

Problem 4: Spell resists. As a wizzie i find that resists count in WAY too much on DD's in pvp. On Vallon, this isnt naked warriors running from caster gankers any more, this is uber-resist buffed warrios, running over casters. I said before, its tough enough to bring down an even con warrior on a full bar of mana, even without him hitting me, much less trying to take a red-con one. Levels factor far too much into PvP resists. I can kill an even con caster in 1 Ice Comet and 1 draught, usually. A red con, on numerous occasions has taken me up to 4 Ice comets. for a caster! From what i've seen, a chanter trying to land any DD on anyone post-50 is nearly impossible.

SOlution: reduce the amount that resists save in PvP.

Problem 5: Invulnerability. I see four basic problems with PvP.

1.) Invulnerable Healing. This is a direct result of cross-teaming, and is constantly used as a tactical advantage for zone control. The effect of this is that the player who is subject to this is left feeling helpless and frustrated, destroying the fun aspect of the game. The only way I see of stopping this is coding the teams to prevent cross-teaming. I understand the effect on the community, but the core gameplay is what is at issue here, and it is broken in this respect. I signed up for four teams PvP, this is the way the server should have been coded in the first place. A dark elf should never be able to use the power of his god to heal a wood elf whom he should by his very nature hate.

2.) Resist differences due to levels, not due to equipment or true resists. Why is it that a level 52 with the same resists as a level 60 takes so much more spell damage from PvP combat? I understand the need for there to be some difference between the way a spell affects a level 60 and a level 52, but I think the difference as it stands is far too extreme. An Ice Comet from a level 52 will scratch a level 60, an Ice Comet from a level 60 will devastate a level 52. The level 60 player should have a definite advantage, but not completely overwhelming insurmountable odds just due to the level factor.

3.) Class Imbalance. Druids unresistable DOT's such as Winged Death are overpowered, they should be at least somewhat resistable. High level Necro Lifetaps are overpowered. Clerics offensive ability is too underpowered, as well as their defensive abilities. Enchanters offensive spells are too easily resisted in PvP. Resists keep going up and up, rendering wizards less and less effective and limiting their PvP combat spells to lures. Sunstrike is slightly overpowered, but with resists going up I believe it's highly overrated. Lifetaps are nearly unresistable in PvP, and are much more effective than lures.

The class imbalance issues I believe are secondary to the level resists issue, and both are far behind the invulnerable healing and cross teaming problems plaguing PvP servers.
