Andervin, look at it from a hybrid's perspective. Prior to Kunark, hybrids were the casting melee classes. We had spells that were used by groups (invis, snare, darkness, fear, etc.) The spell casting was reserved for the hybrids. Kunark through this all out of wack by adding effects to armor pieces. We were no longer relied upon for our casting abilities, which really minimized our need in a group. That's where he's coming from.

Do I think they should remove something that's been given to them? No. I think they should just leave the armor as it is and move on. But I think the point he was trying to make was that warriors with Cobalt armor really infringed upon ground they should not have been privvy to.

As far as warriors are concerned, when Everquest was released, warriors didn't have much of a place in the game. They didn't have a significant HP bonus. The armor available to warriors wasn't any different than was available to the plate-class hybrids. Then Verant started making changes. They added hitpoint bonuses. They added warrior only armor with higher armor class than was available to paladins and shadowknights. They added critical hits. They added a lot of stuff to make them more attractive to the game. They balanced warriors. And they are now finally balanced. Like most of the people here have said, they can use a few tweaks, but overall they are a balanced class.

For whomever was saying that a rogue's armor class is the same as a warrior's armor class, you are dead wrong. You have to compare apples to apples here. A naked rogue might have similar armor class to a naked warrior. When equipped with similar genre of armor (ie, warrior in full cobalt compared to a rogue in full mrylokar, or a warrior in full indicolite compared to a rogue in full woven shadow) the number changes considerably. The rogue is and always will be a chain class in Everquest. They will always suffer from chain class restrictions and their armor will always have lower armor class than warrior armor of the same genre.

A warrior doesn't do as much damage as a rogue, monk, or even ranger in a particular party with those classes present. Why? A warrior is standing toe to toe with the mob. They are tanking. A percentage of their hits are being dodged, parried, or riposted by the mob. Meanwhile, a rogue and a good monk or ranger will be behind the mob removing the damage avoidance of the mob by removing dodge, parry and riposte. All the classes are doing their job. The warrior is eating damage and doing some good damage output. The rogue, monk and ranger are dishing out great damage.

This does leave the question of the purpose of the shadowknight and paladin though. The real benefit of having a warrior eating mob damage is that they have a significantly higher number of hitpoints. Paladins and shadowknights fall somewhere around 600 hitpoints behind a warrior at level 60. They can't take as much damage, therefore their categorization of being a "defensive" tank is somewhat questionable. The paladin can heal, DA, and LoH. The shadowknight can FD. I really see no defensive quality of a group member feigning death so they don't die. It makes little or no sense.

The question that's going to be raised over the next few weeks is, how much of a penalty do you impose upon the hybrid classes for their spell casting capabilities? If paladins and shadowknights are the defensive tanks, give them tanking abilities. That includes increasing their armor class to warrior levels, as well as increasing their hitpoints to warrior levels. The warriors will still have a vast edge on them in damage dealing. Would it even out? Heck if I know.

Lastly, I'll talk about rangers for a second. Rangers are deemed the damage dealing hybrid. Rangers fall 4th in line in damage dealing capabilities at level 60. Rogues, monks, and warriors all outdamage a ranger. For being the "offensive" hybrid, they are penalized with no defense skill updates. I play a level 56 ranger. I am pretty much uber equipped (almost full Tolan's armor (no bp yet), lozidal shell shield, lots of +AC items, epics, etc.) My unbuffed armor class is 926. That's at level 56 with nearly all of the best of the best equipment available to me. Does my being 4th in the damage hierarchy warrant my extremely low armor class? Fully buffed I can get to about 1050 AC. That's with every stacking AC buff there is. Fully buffed, the level 58 warrior I play with all the time (uber equipped) has 1342 ac. This makes sense. The level 60 rogue I play with, fully buffed and uber equipped, has 1260 ac.

These are all balance issues that are being brought out into the open by Verant in an attempt to help balance things. Everyone will always want their character to have 1500 ac and 7000 hitpoints. But that's not going to happen. Warriors will (and should) always have the highest armor class of any of the melee classes, as long as they are equipped properly for their level.

To whoever was talking about armor class on your warrior at level 51 being 890, er, you are now coming into your own with respect to your character. You have 1 piece of indicolite on. You have sleeves of the ancients, meaning you haven't been to the plane of hate enough times to get more pieces of indicolite. You are still building your character. When your character is built, you WILL have higher armor class than any other class out there. And every time you see that little "You have gained a level!" message, you will start tacking on a little bit more armor class. As you get more items, your character will grow. A similarly equipped paladin or shadowknight at your level (ie, one that hasn't gotten any planar gear and has a couple pieces of Deepwater armor) will have at least 30 ac less than you. A similarly equipped ranger at your level will have 100 ac less than you. The gap will widen as your character grows. When you start getting disciplines, things will begin to change for you. At level 56, if you get in trouble, you can just hit the riposte discipline and get a few seconds of auto riposte. This can guarantee you a cheal in time to save your life. An SK will have to hit FD and hope like heck it takes. A paladin will have to hit LoH then wait 90 minutes for it to recycle. A ranger just dies and gets a ressurection.

I play with warriors every day. About the only thing I can say a warrior needs is a bit more ability to taunt. When I play with the warrior in our guild that has epics, this is not a problem. Once he procs, I never get agroed on again. If I get agro before his proc, I simply jolt the mob off me and get it back on him. I would love for taunt to actually work 100% of the time for a warrior at level 60. It would ease a lot of the odd agro problems that arise. Other than that, I think warriors are pretty darned balanced in the end-game.

If you want to look at classes that need help at level 60 for end-game events, go look at the druid and necromancer classes. Both classes need some serious work to be group friendly when killing epic mobs.
