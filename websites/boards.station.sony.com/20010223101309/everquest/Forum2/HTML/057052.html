Eventually I’d like to do one of these for every class. I know, your first question is going to be, how did I decide to pick druids to be next? Well, honestly, it just came up in conversation today with a co-worker. There’s no trick to it. I would certainly address the ones most in need first, but until I’ve actually done all these posts, I won’t know which is most in need… (let’s not make that the topic of this post, please)
So the question is, what is it that the druid community thinks they need to become a ‘balanced’ class? What is lacking? What is over-powered? - yes, that’s a valid question for any class –

This time I’m starting this post with very little information to lead with, so I feel like I’m at a bit of a disadvantage. I do know that, like many other classes, some druids feel that they don’t have enough to do while at the final encounter of the big raid. One of the suggestions to change that falls along the same lines as one proposed by the shaman community, changing/improving their healing abilities post 50.

I want you to understand that I think druids are pretty well balanced all around, and I really see little that needs to be changed (and that’s partially why I picked them to be the topic today…). So you’ll have to be convincing, logical and articulate to convince me.

Alan

Alright, enough of this. You people seem to misunderstand Absor, he wants a list of suggestions to BALANCE druids (Read: Make them useful in groups post-50), not unbalance them by adding every wishlist item under the sun.

That said, there are only a few things wrong with druids, both fundamentally and level-wise.

Firstly, what is our role? What is our specialization? Most would say our specialization is being fairly good at almost everything. Unfortunately, that just doesn't stack up in the post-50 world. You see, if you need something done, post-50 it is far better to get a specialist to do it for you. Example? Let's use the cleric's. As was mentioned before, a level 39 cleric is a more efficient healer than a group of 56 druids (perhaps not post-patch). That is what we must seek to change.

Two druids should be able to team up, and heal decently post-50. The way it is now, before any group dare enter the high-level dungeon zones, they REQUIRE a cleric. A level 52 druid can go out of mana, simply by trying to heal an equal level tank up to full from 25%. This is not balance, it is a joke.

Moving Superior heal down was a good thing, but it's not down far enough. The level 51+ fizzle rates are rediculously high across the board. This spell will be so fundamentally needed, it MUST be put in the level 50 or less range. Remember, the biggest complaint about superior healing was that it fizzled far too often.

Secondly, while we are in the group down in Sebilis, what are we doing when we're not healing the cleric? We're either meditating, ensnaring mobs, or nuking, trying to make ourselves useful. Let's pick ensnare, since that an evac are the only reasons Druids can currently get a group in OS.

Elder spiritist's Gauntlets have the effect of our level 1 Snare. This snare lasts two minutes. Considering the effect on our vambraces is a level 34 DoT for 350dmg, would it not be possible to put the level 29 Ensnare spell on these gaunts? As it stands now, the long cast time for such a low level spell is absurd. These gauntlets are never used.

Secondly, while we're on snaring, wizards get AE snare before druids do, and when druids DO get AE snare, it's the level 1 snare, which lasts two minutes. Considering our primary role in any 50+ group is going to be not healing, but SNARING, this spell should be changed to the Ensnare type duration. Envision this picture: Tank overpulls, enchanter mezzes the group of four froggies. Druid walks up into the group and does an AE ensnare. Boom, every mob snared, and the druid can get back to his healing much faster.

Verant seems to pick the group-buffing role for druids post-50. I can tolerate that, but our buffs do not last even half as long as their single-target equivalents. Why should I pay such a high price just to do all my group members at once? If you want to try to balance things, make the group buffs last as long as the normal ones (Case in point, Group SoW last half as long as normal sow, perhaps less so. No one is going to USE this, the spell wears off far too soon.), but increase the mana cost.

Alright, that seems to be it. I'll refrain from the wishlist, but there is something I do want, that is currently done in every zone for enchanters, except with static objects:

Random animal form. To say there is no space in the global file, let me explain. The magician summon monster spells summon a pet which takes a random model from the current zone. Thus it is possible to have a pet sand giant while you are in oasis, but not while you are in NK. Enchanters at level 1 get Minor Illusion, a spell which takes the form of the nearest static object, such as a lamp post. Druids at level 9 get treeform, which takes the form of the nearest tree.

Making a form such as treeform, which is called animal form and takes the form of the nearest animal (just stick wolf-form faction on these), would be much appreciated. For masters of the outdoors, we sure don't have much in the way of nature spells, beyond renamed nukes and half-hearted attempts at charms.

Also, please look at the weight on Vermiculated, it is rediculous.

Thank you.

I have a few ideas and solutions to problems of druidic balance, I'm a 54 halfling druid of Karana, on the Fennin Ro server, in a non-uber guild.

1. Mana regen: People have proposed to increase it, but w/ current spells there's no way to do so without increasing druid's solo power as well, and druid solo power is good enough (probably too good) as is. Instead of a SINGLE TARGET or SELF-ONLY spell, make it a GROUP BUFF, like circle of winter/summer, but make it regen 1 mana/hp for EVERY EXTRA PERSON besides yourself in group. So, for example, if you had 6 people in your group, you cast the spell, and everybody will regen 5 mana/hp per tick, stacks w/ everything. 2 mana/hp w/ 2 people, 4 w/ 3, etc. This would go a long way in increasing the desirability of druids in groups, and would be totally useless solo. Make it lvl 44 w/ an upgrade at 55, times when druids start to have serious desirability problems in groups.

2. Teleports: frankly not really a balancing issue, saving 10 minutes to run from nk to halas isn't that big of a deal...no need to teleport to kelethin either, druids can teleport to nk and train right at the ring, and butcherblock's only 2 zones away.

3. Heals: changes on test are good enough, we're not meant to be clerics, just good enough to make an APPRECIABLE difference in a group so people will invite us

4. Charms: DO NOT in any case upgrade this line of spells, druids should not be necros/mages w/ reliable pets, if animal charms are upgraded, druid solo power would explode exponentially (imagine kiting a mob w/ a reliable pet buffed w/ savage spirit hitting it from behind...we'd be better soloers than necros)

Equipment: certainly a concern, but as someone who's still wearing a mithril runed tunic at lvl 54, I'm willing to accept it as a trade-off to my well-rounded abilities, although a mid-30s set of quest armor (similar to totemic stats perhaps...but green chain look) would go a long way to help druids.

If you take a good look at what it is that certain classes bring to high level encounters, it becomes apparent that the key skills and spells that they bring to the table are 'special'.

What I mean by this is that all of the desired classes have abilities and spells that are OPEN ENDED. They are not based purely on a set number of hitpoints (for example). I'll use Open Ended to describle any spell that has potentially limitelss use.

An obvious example of this is the Cleric's Complete Heal spell. Technically this spell could heal 5 Trillion hitpoints of damage... sure no one will ever have that many hitpoints, but the nature of the spell has no limits.

Other spells/abilities that are open eded (or at least I view them as): Shaman Slow line of spells, Enchanter's AQ speed spells, Enchater Mes and charm spells, Riposte Discipline, Feign Death, Tashani line of spells... etc...

All of these things approach conflicts with the approach of changing the nature of the conflict. Not merely doing X amount of damage, or healing X amount of hitpoints. These kinds of abilities are key to making certain classes highly valued. It is in this area that druids have a shortcoming.

True, Druids have several spells that fall under this category:

- Thorns
- Charm Animal
- Snare (yes, this is a powerful spell, but too widespread for druids to claim it as a class feature)
- The new line of Ro spells from Velious.
- Harmony

I feel that most debuff spells fall under the Open Ended category as well.
