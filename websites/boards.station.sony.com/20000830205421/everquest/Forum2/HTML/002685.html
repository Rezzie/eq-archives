<sigh> B, for the wrong reasons

The real issue here is that RP isnt as encouraged as it should be, and people dont necessarily respect other people's wishes to RP. Not only is RP not encouraged, but anti-RP activities are not DIScouraged.

I like to RP. When I "RP" I'm no longer ME, I am my character. I want those my character to be as different from me and unique as possible. Thats what makes it worth it.

For example: I'm a male who likes to play a female character. When you do this and actually make male friends in the game, the question always comes up.... "So, are you a female IRL?" This pisses me off. I want to say. "Gawd Dammit, dont you see my name is purple? DONT ASK ME QUESTIONS ABOUT ME IRL!!!!! "

Its a totally unfair question, because you either have to:

1. boldface lie
2. step out of character and answer truthfully about yourself
3. dodge the question basically indicating that youre really a guy.

These things can really ruin the RP experience for some people. Personally, I lie about it. They dont need to know. And after that I say that I'm in /roleplay and no more IRL questions.

Another example is when people try to use "roleplaying" as a mask for doing unethical things in the game. For example, the Rogue ks/ninjalooter/con artist, the pvp-switch avoiding necromancer (I'm RP'ing evil- really I am!) the Uber-warrior KS'ing newbies and single-handedly clearing dungeons in "defense of his homeland" etc.
