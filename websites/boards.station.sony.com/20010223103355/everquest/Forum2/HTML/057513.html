Unfortunately, as will probably be the case from here out, I just don't have time to do any research before starting this post. I'll have to rely on you folks to tell me all I need to know about enchanters…
Again, the usual reminder. I am primarily looking for balance concerns here. But I am certainly willing to put bugs on the list. Also, though I really do understand the fact that you want to emphasize and back up statements by others, please, please try to keep the repeat information to a minimum. I'm finding that by page three or four on these threads I'm rereading the same suggestions by people that start their posts with "I didn't read this thread, so I don't know if this had been said…" I don't want to stifle any creativity. But I have to admit that I tend to get bleary eyed reading the same suggestions over and over again, and I know I'm missing good new ideas late in threads when they are buried among things that others have covered several times before.

So, please, let's hear what you think is unbalanced or broken about enchanters. Here are the few things that I had already jotted down:

Gift of Brilliance: When compared to similar buffs by Clerics and Shamans this spell doesn’t seem good enough. Sure, it will see use, but it could be a better spell. Quick comparison:

Aeogolism (60th level Cleric spell): Adds 1,100 to max hit points and 54 to AC for a single target. Lasts 2.5 hours. Costs 750 mana.
Focus of the Spirit (60th level Shaman spell): Adds 405 hit points, 67 strength and 60 dexterity for a single target. Lasts 1.5 hours. Costs 500 mana.
Gift of Brilliance (50th level Enchanter spell): Adds 150 mana and grants a mana regeneration rate increase of 2 points per tick for a single target. Lasts 1.5 hours. Costs 450 mana.

The Shaman and Cleric spells essentially replace other buffs. Gift of Brilliance stacks with Clarity II. It might be nice to have the mana regeneration be 2 points greater than Clarity II and NOT stack with it so that enchanters could use it to save a slot. And perhaps rather than increasing the mana bonus, give it an intelligence bonus. But perhaps at this level enchanters just don't need any more intelligence boosts…

Illusions drop when zoning: Is there a gameplay reason behind the fact that illusions drop when you zone? If not, can we make it so they do not?

More Illusions: I think it’s a good idea to dig through the list of current global characters and see if there are a few more illusions that we can give to enchanters.

I know there are more issues…

Alan

Alright, Time to RECAP FOR OUR VI FRIENDS.
This is my try at a summary of the things that we wish to be fixed for our beloved class, and at last, things that we wish would be changed. I'll take a backseat conservative type of approach to this to appease as many as I can, while staying well within our bounds of power.
1) PROBLEM:
Getting Killed Frequently, Taunt, Defence, HP, and AC.

TREND:
I've read all the threads, and it seems that the root of all these problems are the insanely high taunt rates of our spells. If we did not have such high taunts, we would not be aggroed so much, and we would therefore be hit less. This would eliminate large changes to Equipment, Hp, Defence, or AC. It would also eliminate the complete need for escape spells, even though they would still be nice.

SOLUTION:
Lower taunts on all spells such as Mez, Charm, DD's, Tashan, and Stuns. This would stop the general trend of spell resisted == Instant MoBs on you. -- This would solve all of these balance issues, and would NOT require major changes to the position or use of the Enchanter as it is.

2) PROBLEM:
Illusions do not change religous factions, which disallow quite a few enchanters any way to get into areas that they should be able to go with Illusions.

TREND:
Did not know that religion would be a hinderance later in life when they picked it.

SOLUTION:
Give a quest that allows us to return to Agnostic. Not too difficult, so that those who do not have the time to play too much may accomplish it as well. Or compensate.

3) WTYH LINE PROBLEM:
Pets, tanks, and other melee types, consistantly break this spells hold, and it would otherwise be a wonderful addition to the arsenal.

TREND:
WTYH connects, holds, and affects the monster. A matter of seconds later, the MoB is able to break it for it was bashed or attacked alternately. Immediately comes running to the Enchanter infuriated.

SOLUTION:
1) Make MoBs that are WTYH'ed un-bashable, slammable, and other such attacks that would have a similar spell breaking affect.
2} Make it so that WTYH does not break from bashes and such skills.

4) DD and DoT PROBLEMS:
See taunt issue above.

SOLUTION:
At current, most seem to be agreeing that we are alright in this sector, we are not the major damage dealers, yet it would be nice.

5) TASHAN LINE PROBLEMS:
See taunt issue above. Restore Tashania is it?

SOLUTION:
To a more powerful - as it was previous or about that - state.

6) MEMORY BLUR PROBLEMS:
This is a great idea, and it would be absolutely wonderful, if it worked.

TREND:
No resist or inaffective message, no way of knowing what worked unless it stops attacking you, if it's not an aggro MoB. No way to call pet off. Too high resists. Too high casting time.

SOLUTION:
Decrease resist rate, mana, casting time, and recast of any form. This would allow us to use it as an escape spell.

POSSIBLE WISH LIST:

MEZMERIZE: Lower resists at higher levels.

CHARM:
Possibly make this spell more reliable for monsters under even or blue con, which would be reasonable. If this is seen as 'unbalancing' you may remove our pet spells, as they are useless in their current state anyway.

STUNS:
These seem to be less of an issue than almost anything else, yet still note-worthy.
-- DURATION:
Stuns seem to be very, very short compared to their cast/mana cost/recast time. -- Increase Stun Duration.
-- RANGE:
Stuns have such a small range that it puts the Enchanter in danger to cast these. -- Increase Range.
-- RECAST TIME:
Much longer than needed recast time on these spells than there should be. -- Lower recast time.

PETS/CHARM: I shall not say much about this, for we seem not to have come to any form of concensus on this issue. If we do not stand united we do not stand at all. There is one general consensus at the moment: Pets are too weak, and charm is not nearly as reliable as it should be. Also see Taunt Issue and Charm Issue.

MEM BLUR SHIELD:
People seem to agree that this is a nice idea, and that it would most definitely help enchanters to survive in battle more. Once hit, and hti again, and again, it shall decrease aggro based on level and skill. One of those one on one psychology sessions where they tell you to "Hit the pillow, and get your anger out." -- In this case, it's us.

RUNE IV/V:
Component costs seem too high. Hardly soaks up any damage to survive.

SNARE:
As suggested before, a spell that could possibly be like the suggested 'Stagger' would be great. Like an Snare type spell. It's within our range of powers. 'Stagger,' would affect the mind of the beholder, so that the target would be in a bit of a feverish dazed state, and have normal function, yet stagger around as if very wobbly on the legs.

DOPPLEGANGER TYPES: I, personally, think these are a good idea. Yet we should keep requests simple for now, and see what ground we can gain later.

Thx for your time,
