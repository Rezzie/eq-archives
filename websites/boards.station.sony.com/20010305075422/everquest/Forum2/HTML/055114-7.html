The problem with making evade lower aggro by a fixed amount, is that the effectiveness of evade will then be in direct relation to the melee output of a rogue. At 60, the amount of decreased aggro would not go up over time. However, with the constant evolution of the game, with bigger, better weapons coming out.. evade's usefullness must decline.
For example, let's say you have decent Kunark weapons. You do x amount of damage. Evade might keep you alive after a backstab. Now you upgrade to common Velious weapons.. you do more damage, but evade stays the same. Now, on high damage rounds it's getting harder to evade. Maybe you evade.. then land a bs and it's right back on you. We haven't even found all the good weapons in Velious yet, let alone another expansion. However, while weapons will constantly inflate, this skill won't.
I suggest then, that there be a base amount of negative aggro, but that it also be affected by the power of the weapon that the rogue is wielding, so that as more powerful weapons come out evade will keep pace. Such a calculation should take into account both the ratio of the weapon, and the pure average (for backstabs).
In my opinion, anything static will run into problems down the road.

Hey all. Just a couple of things about rogues...

1) Multiple poison charges

I won't say "stackable" because there are many ways to implement this. What needs to be done is that poisons need to have more then one use or be able to be combined at a moments notice. A couple of other ideas I've seen bandied about that I really like for poison usage include:

- high apply skill resulting in more "charges" per poison vial and less time to apply (it currently isn't feasible to use more then one charge of poison per encounter). As your apply skill goes up, it takes you less poison to poison the blade: effectively increasing the charges on the poison to those with high apply.

- Also, combining poisons with ranged weapons to produce an unstackable single shot poisoned ranged weapon. Perhaps make the requirement throwing weapons only (which will also reduce the usage of bows by rogues).

- Further, the idea of manufactured drinkable poisons with innocent names and no visible proc creates a number of interesting PvP implementations ("Hey Bob! I just dinged. Wanna drink??")

- Finally, suspensions can only be bought off of merchants. It would be nice to make this relatively self-sufficient for rogues to use perhaps by brewing a suspension, pottery-ing the vials and make-poison-ing the poisons together.

2) Hide / Sneak

It's been said before: allow this to remain active across zone boundaries.

3) Pickpockets

The utility of this skill is limited and usually negative. Perhaps it would be interesting to allow rogues to pick items from vendors? This would reduce the negative perception of rogues fishing through the pockets of a common mob while enhancing the utility of the skill. Can't afford that loaf of bread? Try picking the mob. Implementation ideas include a pick priority list wherein the items go from least valuable to most valuable with a percentage bonus based on the skill level. Basically, higher level rogues with higher level PP skill are able to pick more valuable items off of merchants. They already kill younger rogues instantly (and many older rogues as well) whenever a pick fails so why not let the reward be worth the risk? Besides, who else is it worthwhile to pick that won't betray your groupmates? As well, by implementing a pick priority list there is a very good chance that a vendor will keep "dropping" (the results of a good pick) one of their unlimited resources. Further, make it have a large initial skill requirement (like many of the locked doors) so that it doesn't become a viable money twink (unless you want to pick 200 loaves of bread to sell back at 1sp a piece).

4) Items

Rogues need more diverse throwing items. It's been said before and I'll say it again.

Besides all the other neat Rogue equipment ideas I thought I'd throw this one out:

- A rogue-only mask that allows you to see through hidden walls as if the illusion just didn't exist (so we see those pit traps as holes in the floor rather then peering intently at every inch of wall/floor to find the teltale inconsistencies).

Just about everything else about the class is quite good. There was a point in time when Rogues were the masters of useless skills but as you guys come out with more expansions, the utility of these skills increased to the point of viability. Thank you. As well, Rogues require relatively skilled players to master (since we have to BS, evade, PP, H/S and whatnot at the right times and places to avoid instant death) and are naturally rich in roleplaying opportunity.
It takes time and energy to level a rogue (without twinking them or P/L'ing them, of course) and the skills learned are unique.

Thanks for the time and the enjoyment.

Hello, Absor.

Welcome to the tidal wave of wants, needs, and insecurities that is rogues. I've only skimmed the messages so far -- in one way or another, I've read most of them before.

Sorry you haven't done your research on us -- as you can see, we are mighty defensive about our abilities, with much historical justification. As you probably have figured out by now, we were the class that needed the most fixing out of the box, since the Vision (tm) of rogues got jettisoned at the last moment, but not replaced with anything until a year or so later. Sense traps & disarm traps are part of our legacy as a result.

I think if there is an underlying theme of Rogue requests for "balance," it is this: We are, essentially, a skills-only class, like our brother warriors and monks. Spells are easy to add to the game, skills are not. Our skills are all we have. You can say "what about this weapon or armor" or whatever, but the world of items is too chaotic to really say we are balanced or not based on items available.

Yes, we are very good at melee damage. We excel in that perhaps because we have little else we excel in. We can backstab. We can evade. We can hide & sneak. We can pickpocket, but it is generally inadvisable. We can pick locks once in a blue moon. We can make poisons, which is fun, but expensive and not for everyone.

Technically, we can instill doubt, but with 3 checks to fail (skill check, to hit check, fear spell resist check), and fear being a generally hazardous tactic, using instill doubt is more like playing the lottery than having a skill. My rogue is 43rd level, has a skill of 192, and a 93 CHA, and I succeed fairly rarely. About half the time would be great.

And we can throw things for criticals. Have you tried using range 50-70 damage 5 throwing weapons? If I am that close to the monster, why am I not in melee if I am going to do perhaps 5 points of damage? If I am pulling, with my moderate AC and anemic hp, why am I not shooting with a bow at 5 times the range? I can't turn and run fast enough from the monster if I am in range of my thrown weapon - they are already pounding me.

So you see, compared to most other classes, we lack considerable diversity. This is what we do, this is what we've always done.

Most of the "we need this, we need that" arguments you will read are based around two points: (1) we are a niche group, so we want to be as good at our niche as possible, and (2) we'd like more diversity in what we do, please, especially if our niche role is going to be reduced.

I love being a rogue, but I wish something fresh and new would turn up from time to time in our skills. I understand that new spells are easy to add and that new skills aren't. That doesn't mean that we don't like to get new abilities and see fresh ideas added to our class. Please warmly consider any suggestions for rogue - hell, for melees - that add some more flavor to our classes. They don't have to be damage-based, just something interesting. Short duration see invis with sense traps - that sort of thing.

I can continue this line of argument - I really have a lot to say about it - but you are probably already battered with our posts. Absor, if you read this and feel like continuing the discussion, please post, I'll check back from time to time.

-- Auspice
