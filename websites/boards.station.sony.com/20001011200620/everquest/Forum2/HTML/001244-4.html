The ideas here were really great. I'm new to this board so it took a while to read through the 4 pages but well worth the time and effort I think. I think that the mage class has been overlooked. We should be the entertainers that is inherit in our name alone. I don't see how cantrips and parlor tricks should be saved for magicians of post 50 seasons. It makes no sense. The tricks should get better every spell level. One post suggested that we get some entertaining spell at every spell circle. That would be great!

I liked the ideas of creating clothing for those special events, creating flowers and cake at lower levels to celebrate a friend's birthday, and having random spell effects every week or so to keep the class fresh and exciting. After all, who knows what'll come outta that hat?

Also, I don't see how it would step on any other classes' toes to allow the magicians different pet summons at higher levels. The necros get spectres right? Well I know in the game there're other summoned creatures. For example, Shadowmen. I'm not saying that it would make a fabulous pet but at least it would be different! And being a magician, we should be able to control our pets to a greater degree than the pets of the imitating classes. We should be able to simulate having a conversation with our pet by using spells like graft voice etc. Even summoning a lost pet should be conceivable, right? All the ideas I've heard only round out our class, they do nothing to imbalance it. Why is it that necros can aspire to a new and cool looking pet and mages, the summoners, can dream of a larger, yet an exact physical replica of the level 8 summon. Doesn't seem right to me.

Lastly, if we are the elementalists that we would seem to be, has anyone ever wondered why the majority of our spells are fire based? That has always puzzled me. Especially since we can summon Globes of Water and eventually, beer. But that's another thread for another day. :P

I really hope someone is reading this and responds to it. A lot ofplayers have taken the time to comment seriously and constructively. Even to know that we are being heard...

Thanks.
