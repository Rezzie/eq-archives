There are lots of fine posts suggesting enhancements that the community agrees on, as well as many horrible suggestions. Please ignore the latter. Most of the former can be found in Bodi's compilation.

Let me stress again some of the broken aspects of monk life:

1) The effect of our epic, Celestial Tranquility, will not stack at all with pure haste spells and is blocked by the ranger's Call of Earth. See details above. What we'd like, as with our Robe of the Whistling Fists, is to be able to use the pure haste of enchanter spells while still being able to use the AC/ATK boost of the CT song. Failing that, is there any way to make the AC/ATK boost permanent while changing the song to haste only? The AC/ATK boost is a big part of the desirability of our epic weapon.

2) No graphic for our epic gloves, Celestial Fists. We'd like a unique look to them, perhaps using some new Velious texture that would match our high-end armor. But just about anything is better than having no graphic.

3) No minimum damage on flying kick. It is embarrassing how often I can still kick for less than 10 points.

4) Feign death problems. Many details on the bugs were given above. Additionally, perhaps a shorter reuse time at higher levels might help. After all, shadowknights get a new FD spell that has a much shorter recast time than our skill.

5) Weight too high on black pantherskin armor. The tunic is 2.5 stone, the legs are 3.0. To make this armor more attractive and worthy considering the time spent making it, weights should be lowered on these pieces especially.

6) Ineffective disciplines. Thunderkick suffers from the same damage problems as flying kick. Since the discipline has a duration, why not make all flying kicks that land during that time be thunderkicks? Also, the level 57 discipline of 100 Fists could have its duration increased. Fifteen seconds of haste is hardly a compelling reason to tie up all your disciplines for 30 minutes. Ashenhand has a chance to kill a target outright, yet unlike the Rogue's ability to assassinate, killing the target will make the corpse poof. Silentfist, another duration-based discipline, could also allow all dragon punches within that time period to stun.

7) Balance between human and Iksar monks. In exchange for an experience penalty and bad faction, Iksar monks get a significant AC gain, increased regen, and serpent sight. Regen alone compensates for the experience penalty, as the Iksar monk has so much less downtime. Faction hardly plays a roll, as that can be fixed through normal killing (in Blackburrow, for instance). There needs to be additional compelling reasons to play a human monk for true balance between the two races.

8) Epic quest is too easy. This may not be a popular opinion, but it is true. The Robe of the Lost Circle is obtainable by killing the quest NPC's (please change this). Headbands and sashes can be traded instead of quested for. The Robe of the Whistling Fists can be multi-quested. Once the last robe is obtained, three groups can do all four of the battles needed to get the fists, and none of the mobs are rare spawns. In theory, you only need to camp two components - the pipes for the RotWF - the rest can be purchased or killed for. Our epic weapon should mean more than that.

Bottom line: monks want to do tons of damage and have a reasonable chance of staying alive while doing so. That's ultimately all we ask.

Well, here's some of the stuff I think on the monk class, just off the top of my head.

1) Give us an actual set of Planes armor we would actually look forward to. Every other melee and hybrid class has or had a new set of armor in kunark (and now velious) to look forward to. Monks had zip-squat-nada in kunark, and the Velious armors, are rather underwhelming. Virtually every single item spot it is quite easy to find a better item that is usually much easier to obtain, often at much lower level.

I can give a list if desired, but when people are still wearing Azure Sleeves or Gatorscale pants or a Froglok crown over, say, quest armor which requires killing L60+ stuff in the testing halls of the Temple of Veeshan, then I think there's something out of whack.

2) Balance us better against the rest of the melee classes. Right now, we only barely outdamage warriors, and not even that by much given some of the new velious weapons. (The willsapper, at 13/20 with a slow proc has a better ratio than pretty much any monk 1h weapon I know of, not to mention a more useful proc than anything aside from the Adamantite club.) To pay for this slight extra damage, we have 2/3 the HP at best, 150 less AC, a truly crippling weight limit, and a lesser set of disciplines. Rogues will always outdamage us assuming someone can actually take the front of the mob, which is most of the time, and have at least as good HP and AC.

We need to further differentiate ourselves here. Heck, even with one of the top weapons in the game, the Bo Staff of Trorsmang, out of VP, my damage per second just barely outpaces a warrior using a willsapper + 1 epic weapon. As a result, there's become a situation where there is not much compelling reason to bring a monk along. All the new mobs have anti-feign pulling logic so we can't do that, and we often don't out-damage a warrior or rogue enough to justify including one of us over one of them.

3) To go with the request for added dammage, we also need some method of aggro control. Feign Death does not count as this, as it does not modify our position on the hate list once we come back from it. Rogues and Rangers both have aggro control methods, and we're in much the same boat as "can dish it out but can't take it" types. Can we please have Feign in combat work for us like Evade does for rogues, with aggro reduction/mem blur capabilities? If you want to make sure it can't thus be used for feign pulling easier, only give it that sort of effect if the mob is currently in melee combat.

As a side thing to this, PLEASE have someone look into the well-known among the monk community bug with feign. As it stands now, a mob I can feign off successfully 9/10 times solo will often NEVER believe feign if I am grouped at the time. This happens with no falling down message, and often being at half health plus. It is much worse if your health is very bad, which of course is when you most want it to work. This bug did not exist pre-kunark, only post-kunark.

4) Do something about our disciplines. The vast majority are really rather useless, and are almost never used. I'll go through 'em one by one:

Inner Flame: This is actually one of the oft-used ones. However, a duration of 12 seconds greatly limits it's utility. Also, this discipline is nowhere near as good as the Warrior discipline, Defensive, though it is similar in nature. Would be VERY nice to get the duration on it upped, probably at the expense of some re-use duration, but then again, warriors get 3 min of defensive every 10 minutes...

Thunderous Kick. Never ever ever used. Completely and totally useless. Not much is more disheartening to use up your discipline load for the next however many minutes only to see "SoAndSo lands a THUNDEROUS Kick (24)" which happens the vast majority of the time. Make this discipline a duration-based discipline, for say 60 seconds. Then, during that period of 60 seconds, every flying kick landed will be Thunderous (and thus a crit). It still wouldn't be that impressive, but at least it would have a purpose. You want to make this discipline REALLY useful, give thunderous kicks the chance to stun.

Whirlwind: Actually one of the used one. Kinda short duration, kinda long recycle time, but no major complaints about this one. Still, due to the massive re-use timer, I use stone stance over this 90% of the time.

Void Dance: Why is this higher level than Whirlwind? There are actually far fewer applications for this discipline. When you can't afford a mob summoning you, or when you are running away is about it. Every other time, whirlwind is so much better. Duration is pretty durn short too, and the re-use time is rather long.

Inner Flame: Very often used discipline. I am still not sure, however, why the main effect of the discipline seems to make most of the hits you land be simply (4x<base damage> )+(damage bonus). For example, with my 40 damage staff, most of my hits with inner flame are for 178. 4x40 + damage bonus, ie 160 + 18. Some hits indeed go much higher, this is true. If you read the text of the discipline itself though, it sounds like what SHOULD be happening is every attack that lands gets 160pts added to the total. Recycle time is a little annoying, and duration is short, but this is a good one.

100 Blows: Fundamentally useless. The haste effect it gives seems pretty negligable, overall. Also, what possible advantage is there to using this discipline over inner flame? Using inner flame generates much more damage. Maybe if you were trying to get as many hits in with a tranquil staff to hope to interrupt a gate or something, but that's kinda rare.

Dragon Punch: Haven't used this one much. Seems a bit silly though, that we have to use a L59 discipline with a medium-length recycle time to even get a chance at a stun, when every L55+ warrior can stun with a 1pt kick. Again though, like the last discipline, there seems to be no major compelling reason to use this over inner flame.

Ashen Hand: Again, haven't used this one much, but I can't see that I ever will. From reports, it makes eagle strike do somewhere in the 400 pt range. Why would I use a 72 minute discipline to do an extra 300 pts over a flying kick, when I can add 1-2k damage over 12sec per inner flame, with luck, and do nearly 3 inner flames over the same recycle time? Also, the insta-kill portion only works on mobs that are basically green anyway, works rarely, and am told often poofs the mob.

What we would REALLY like is unbinding each discipline's re-use timer from the others. That would give us some actual incentive to use the more obscure disciplines. Failing that, we need some revamping of the disciplines to actually give a compelling reason to use them, and bring them more in line with some other ones.

5) Moderate the weight that is being put on some of the newer items, plus the monk-weapons. 10lbs for staves is way too much in most cases, even for higher level monks to really be able to use. All, or almost all, of the high level 1hbs are 3lbs or heavier. New leg items we can use are 2lbs, new tunics 5lbs, etc. It makes it so that in many cases, we can't even use good items we find, cause of the extra weight. Oh, and there's the laughable items, like Imbrued Granite Spaulders. Why exactly is a 20 lb item monk useable? We really need to have the weight of some of this stuff re-examined.

6) Make it so there's a compelling reason to play a Human monk. (I am sure I'll catch some flak here.) Right now, there's not a great deal of game-mechanic reason to choose a human over an Iksar as a monk. They get regen, 50+ more ac, infravision, etc. True, faction is a problem, and they do end up with 2 higher delay on bare fists than humans. However, that last penalty is completely null-and-voided by the fact that no high level monk ever uses their bare hands anyway, unless using Celestial Fists, which sets human and iksar alike to 9dam/16delay. I'm not saying to reduce Iksar, I am just saying there needs to be SOMETHING for humans to compensate. Maybe an extra +1 or +2 base damage bonus that an iksar doesn't get. That'd make it so Damage Monks would be humans, Tanking Monks would be Iksar, and both would have their own specialty based on what their racial advantages suited them for.

Anyway, that's kind of my random ramblings on the topic, sorry if it's disjointed, etc., but it is 1:30 am here. Heh. I'd be happy to clarify, etc. any of this. Thanks.

I wanted to make a point I'm not sure you guys are thinking about at Verant.

You have to keep in mind balance when you're making weapons and armor. This is especially true for melee classes who rely on equipment for much of their ability. You could make all kinds of tweaks to the fundamental aspects of a class for balance, but if you make new weapons of high quality available to one class and not another, then you just shook that balance up again.

If you balance the classes now, you have to seriously consider the effects and balance of the weapons and armor you put in the game, and who can use them. I know you have an enormous database of items, but there has to be SOME way to keep track of it. Designers have to discuss what they're putting in the various zones. It simply doesn't say much for balance when one zone is semi-hard with tons of good stuff when another zone of equal or greater difficulty has so few. Look at Old Sebilis vs. Chardok for a good example.

Problems also arise when you add great items for one class but items that simply don't compare for another. Look at the breastplates off Trakanon. Without looking at the stats they give, compare the Blood Ember (shadowknight) with the Donal's. A shadowknight's efficiency improves very little with a 75 hp, 14 ac, 17 magic resist buff, but a cleric receives a vast improvement with a free complete heal (even with the changes being made).

In short, weapons and armor are as big a part of balance as fundamentals about certain classes. It would help if the designers both communicated more and also had a better knowledge of the current item database before they make new ones.


In this light, I'd like to add my support for a little more variety in effects on weapons for monks, and a little more power on Velious weapons. If there exist weapons for monks that the masses have not seen yet, fine.

As it is, however, warriors are doing just about as much damage as a monk equipped with the epic Celestial Fists (effectively 9/16 weapons) with a Willsapper (13/20) and nice offhand weapon (Priceless Velium Short Sword, for one). I'd suggest you do some tests of a warrior vs. monks vs. rogues with various weapon combinations available in Velious and see if they are still where they are supposed to be in relation to damage. Monk have seen no weapons in Velious which are more powerful than rogues' and warriors' weapons yet, which is balanced for what our class is supposed to be (high damage output in exchange for low defense and hit points).

Monk weapons also lack the variety of effects that rogue and warrior ones have. The 50% slow on Willsapper is a good example. They also have weapons which have lifetap effects (Scimitar of Lifestealing, Blood Point), another nice addition for them. Before it is brought up, the mend ability of monks does not balance this, as mend is a fundamental class ability and not related to the balance of weapons and armor. If monks were balanced (with mend) in relation to the other melee classes before those weapons were put in the game, adding them throws that balance off.

I don't think an overall 15% increase in damage is the way to go. Just make weapons available that allow us to do the amount of damage we should be doing in relation to our counterparts, and give us some of the good effects they have.
