This post is solely concerned with complaints regarding the random number generator. Please keep responses relevant.

Just a thought, but why does everyone assume that the chance for trade success or advancement is RANDOM? Does anyone else see how many variables could go into each sill advancement or success calculation?
Let's take Jewelry as an example.
The collective understanding seems to be that you max your relevant statistic(s), INT or WIS or whatever's popular rumor, and simply combine, and there should be a linear progression in skill with a predictable number of failures in a given situation, modified only by the random number generator.

I've compiled a short list of other possible variables that could have been added by those devious programmers. Personally, if I was in on it, I would have implemented as many of these as possible.

Metal Enchanted?, Difficulty of Metal, Difficulty of Gem, Class, Race, Religon, Current Lighting, Indoors or Out, City Zone or Dungeon, Time Since Last Attempt, Time Since Last Success, Time Since Last Advancement, Current Health, Current Mana (if applicable), Experience of Jeweler's Kit, Relative Solitude.

Some of these are irrelevant perhaps, but look at these situations.

--First, an able (INT, etc.) Erudite Enchanter with skill (X)
- full mana/hp
- alone in Erudin Palace in good lighting
- using a Jeweler's kit he's used all his career
- on his third attempt in the last five minutes, having not succeeded or advanced for his last five attempts
- and attempting a gem/stone combination that's one point away from trivial

--Second, an equally able (INT, etc.) Barbarian Warrior with same skill
- half beat from the last Ruins train breakup
- in the company of many noisy traders and duelers in EC tunnel, at night (no concentration)
- using a just bought Jeweler's kit (gave his old favorite to a guildie)
- hasn't worked on skill for a game month (rusty)
- last five attempts were successful with two monster only gems in there (i.e. lucky history)
-attempting the same gem/stone combo (limit of reasonable success)

Of these two, who should really expect a better chance at success or advancement?

Just an example of how many things could go into a check. It depends on the extent of reality going in to the algorithims. I have renewed faith in the programmers evil genius, as I have discovered a few neat obviously intentional quirks.

Just a thought. )
