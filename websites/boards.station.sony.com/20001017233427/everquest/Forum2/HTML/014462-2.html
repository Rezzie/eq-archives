ok boys and girls. This message is to all of you missing the point.

A class specific ability is one that is not shared with other classes. This means those of you posting those really, really annoying posts about how this is class envy and those other people who come here and show incredible ignorance by posting things about how they have spells too that should be abilities are obviously not reading the post.

Harvest (like Harm Touch and Lay Hands) is an ability that is not given to any class but Wizards. Gate, fear, healing, and feign death are all multi-class shared. That means that the concept of a spell slot being allocated to them makes sense. If Harvest were shared with another class, it'd make sense to be a spell. It is not and therefore makes more sense as an ability.

I don't expect to ever illicit a change in wizards anymore. Countless thoughtful posts are either ignored or are ruined by people who begin screaming class envy and bringing other annoying statements to the wizard thread to distract or troll.

Whine and moan about wizard threads all you want. There are glaring, obvious issues with the wizard class. Why does everyone feel so threated with simply talking about them?

There's a big difference between class envy and class improvement posts. There's also a big difference between whining and presenting a well thought out argument for change.

Let's step back for a moment and ask ourselves why wizards got harvest in the first place. Someone asked in a response what a wizard was before they got harvest and made reference to shrubberies...

Not a bad analogy! When a wizard runs low or out of mana, they are the most useless class in the game. They found themselves in the not-too-enviable position of being a wallflower for extended periods of time, even when managing their mana well. A good group outpaced a good wizard before the Kunark expansion.

Now, the argument that wizards were supposed to be masters of magical damage and aren't is as old as Everquest itself. We were told by Verant time and again that our payoff for being able to do damage fast was that we would be outdamaged by the pet classes, and there is nothing any wizard is going to do to change Verant's mind about that. Whether that is an inequity or not is up to you to decide.

However, it is clear that somewhere in Verant's think tank the wizard complaints were at some point listened to.

Because Harvest was added to the game. There was one compelling reason for this spell to be added. WIZARDS COULDN'T COMPETE. Wizards were subpar. It was done somewhat quietly, without anyone at Verant coming out and saying "Gee you were right, you WERE always out of mana."

Any way you slice it, HARVEST WAS PUT IN IN AN ATTEMPT TO BALANCE THE WIZARD CLASS.

To suggest that harvest is the only spell that a wizard needs to have up is a joke. People say "Well I wouldn't unmem my heals." Great. I wouldn't unmem my nukes. It is not like divine aura, or any other spell of that nature. It was put in the game to tip the scales closer to even.

Why then am I, as a wizard, paying a price to come up to par with other classes? Why should I sacrafice my versatility to be up to par?

I highly doubt now that Gordon has responded that he will even read this again, but dismissing this as a spell I can mem or unmem like any other is demeaning.
