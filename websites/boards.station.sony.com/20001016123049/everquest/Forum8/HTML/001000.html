I, like many others, wish to be a self-sufficent smith. Because of this I have gathered all my components thus far on my own. To help others here i will outlined a few things i have done that helped me gather these components.

Silk Threads.

I remember reading a post here that had someone stating that you can buy these from a gnome vendor. I searched and could not find this vendor. Therefore i made my own.

I logged on my 12th level bard and ran him to the trainer and got 20 points in tailoring. (probably didnt even need to do that). Then i picked up a sewing kit (small would be fine although i opted for large). Then i ran where i remember seeing the most spiderlings. Ferrot. When you enter that zone from the swamp, hug the right wall. Eventually you will come to an area thats got several stonehenge-type rock formations. Here is where many spiderlings (and a few spiders) hang out. After several hours of work here i had 8 stacks of silk threads.

I then transfer this to my primary smith character. I trained him 20 in tailoring as well.
It would be smart to go ahead and head to Freeport and get your brewing up sufficent to make Heady Kiolas. You will need some a bit later if you wish to increase your tailoring skill above 37. At 37 making leather backing is trivial but im not satisfied with my success rate (im a dwarf btw )

Go to East Karanas. This will be your home.

On your way through HH pass, stop and get some mask patterns from the tailor (next to the forge). Get 2-4 stack for starters. Other things you will need are a skinning knife (most important later), your sewing kit, some heady kiolas (probably 2 stacks minimum although they can wait a bit), at least 4 stacks of silk thread. Dont carry any extranious items. You will need all the free space you can get.

Start by killing crag spiders and the occassional carrion spiders if the crags run dry. You want to get your tailoring skill up ASAP. Combine the silks on the spot to make Silk Swatches. When you get a stack or 2. Sit down and make silk mask. Sell them to one of the nearby vendors as you create them. If you are doing okay inventory space-wise, go ahead and start killing any type of cat you see (note that i have gotten low quality pelts off war wolves also). Strange as it seems i have the best luck on medium/high quality pelts from regular lioness's. *shrugs*. Pelts take up room and take it up fast. You will want to convert them to the lovingly stackable leather backing as soon as you can. Down-grade your medium/high pelts to low quality, add a silk thread, and attempt your backing.

Before too long you should max your tailoring skill at 37. The bad news is that all tailored items after that and until cured silk need some quality of pelt to use! Since you want to save them for that leather backing you need to attempt cured silk (which caps at around 87 i believe). Cured silk requires the standard patten, a silk swatch, and a heady kiola (just one needed for mask). You will get a ton of failures with just a 37 skill. But since you wandering around looking for cats its really not that big a deal (to me).

Hope this little guide helps. As a side note i am a 32 level warrior (twinked) and have no problems slaying multiple crag spiders and sometimes will run around and get 4-5 on me at once. SoW is VERY nice to have here as hill giants (and the occassionl griffon) wander around from time to time. So get you some SoW potions if you can. Also i auction peridoically for pelts. I offer 3 plat for low/medium and 4 for HQ and get fairly decent response.

All times are PT (US)
