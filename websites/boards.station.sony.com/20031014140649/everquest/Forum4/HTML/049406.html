I understand your frustration but as someone who's tried to put such groups together, here's what often happens. I'm a cleric btw, which gives me a small advantage in forming grouips, but not always a significant one. Anyway here's what typically happens when everyone in guild is already occupied when I log on:

I first ask a slower to join, since they typically are what hold up group formation. Slower asks me if we are ready to go and I tell him he's the first one I've asked, so he says he'll join me if I can get a full group, but until then, he won't commit.

Fine, so I next try to get a tank, since that's second hardest to get, and Tank asks me if I have a slower and I tell him what slower says and typically tank will join. Then I get dps, usually with little problem, but by the time group is committed to joining, slower tells me he has another group. At this point, I start looking for a replacement and get one, but then find out that tank committed to another group because we had no slower, and now second slower quits because we have no tank (won't even wait a minute to find out if we can get one quickly). Now this might not seem so unreasonable but all of this can happen in the span of no more than a span of 10 minutes.

In fact, I've had one person quit the group to join another group 5 seconds before the person who committed to join gets the invite. I'm not disagreeing with your impatience at all, but I'm just saying that I've seen the opposite extreme far too often and other people's impatience has recently led to an hour or more delay in my attempts at constructing even a minimally workable group.

BTW I totally agree that it's rude to start looking for a group if you have other stuff you need to do first. However, typiecally, the worst problems are when someone invites a friend/guildmate who they fail to tell you is involved in some other activity at the time, or (true story -- multiple times) hasn't even logged on yet.
