It's like a big armored car on some tracks, transporting people and wares from town to town.

OR

It is when you go and try to increase your skills at your guild

OR

If you aggro more enemies than you can handle and run to a safe spot (guards, zone line), then you are pulling "a train" behind you. The danger here is that you bring enemies to a spot where it would not be expected. So for example, if someone is sitting at the zone line, he might get attacked by them. That's the reason why players shout "Train to Zone" if they flee. It's meant as a warning to others.

Good question! How do some people get off those witty saying shouting about a train heading to the zone while they are running around corners, opening doors, and frantically trying to get out with a bubble of health left before the train kills them?

Well, as you will find, many people have a hotkey set up that allows them to hit a quick button and the shout goes out to everyone. I have hot keys to tell people what is incoming when I pull monsters to my group (/g Ready thy weapons! Incoming %T) When I am in a zone like Mistmoor or where trains are a distinct probability (Blackburrow is another great spot), I have a Train key setup on my main list of hotkeys. (/shout Look at the shiny Paladin run away! Must be a TRAIN to zone!!!)

Hotkeys are great for getting out quick shouts or group tells when you are running and don't have time to stop and type out a long saying. It takes a few minutes to setup your hot key, but it is well worth it!
