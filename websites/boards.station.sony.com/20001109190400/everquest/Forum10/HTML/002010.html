VI never released the specifics on how giving pets weapons affect their performance, and as such the theories about it vary greatly. Some say that necro pets are only affected by delay, and consequently should be given fast weapons, while mage pets will not change delay, but will use the higher of their intrinsic damage or the damage they can do with a weapon. There is however consencus that, at least in the lower levels, giving your pet a weapon does help. At later levels it might not boost their performance, but it will enable the pet's ability to dual wield and consequently double their damage. As a necro, stick to giving your pet whatever rusty weapons you can get off orcs and skellies (be sure it's rusty! i recently grouped with a mage who accidentally gave a bronze weapon to his pet!). Alternatively, ask a mage who took the trouble of visiting the libraries to summon some weapons for your pet. This will allow your pet to hit magic.

It's important to note that pets will not start hitting magic creatures until level 12, and not all of those summoned will at that level. Jump to the levle 16 pet, and they will always hit magical creatures from then on. This is at least from a necro vantage. But you can give your level 16 pet a rusty weapon and it will still hig magical creatures.

What giving your pet a weapon does is help it hit for the max damage more often. If you give a low level pet a 2h weapon, it will use the weapon's damage over its own. But after a while, the pet will outdamage the weapon's damage score.

Also, once your pet gets dual wield (level 24 for necro pets I think...can't remember) you have to give it a weapon in order to get the advantages of the dual wield.

Weapon delay used to effect pet's speed, but they nerfed that a long time ago. A pet's speed is 25 I think.

Pets are NPCs. All NPCs choose whichever base damage is higher: their fists, or the weapon wielded, and use that as their base. Delay of the weapon used to affect them, but this was changed when they patched to allow magician pets to dual wield.

There's nothing mystical about what weapons do for pets, you can test it really easily. A level 4 summoned magician pet can be, at most, level 5. This level 5 pet can hit for a max of 10. If you give this pet a rusty two hander, with a base damage of 9, it will suddenly hit for maxes of 18 or 19 (i forget which, haven't done it in awhile). Lets move up a ways, the level 8 mage summons can at most be a level 9 pet. This level 9 pet will hit with its fists for a max of 14. Give this pet a halberd from a sister of erollisi (base damage 10) and the pet will choose the halberd's base over it's own. It will hit for 20 or 21. The delay does not change with any weapon given. Lets move further along, to level 16. A level 16 summons for a mage is at most a level 17 pet. This pet will hit for 20 max. Now, will you help this pet by giving him a rusty 2 hander? Nope, you just outleveled the useful range of giving pets single weapons. The only reason to give a pet a weapon later on is to force the pet to dual wield.

Giving rusty daggers/summoned daggers to pets at any level excepy possibly 1 does nothing for the pet's damage. A dagger's base is 3, which would give a level 5 mage pet a max hit of 6 or 7. That same pet's fists hit for 10. Guess which one he chooses?

That's how weapons work on pets.

Starrfall is right on. Just to clarify and answer our SK friends question directly.

ShadowKnights get necro pets, at roughly half their level. (4 pet at 9, 8 pet at 16, 12 pet at 22, 16 pet at 30, 20 pet at 40, 29 pet at 49, 39 pet at 53, 44 pet at 59)

So as you can see, not until 49 is the pet truly useful. Even then SK pets are really only good out doors, as most likly, at higher levels, you'll be the puller (Feign Death is a blessing/curse) <G>


Now as for the Necro/Mage pets:

Necro pets obtain duel wield at level 29.
Mage pets obtain duel wield at level 24.

Necro pets max 1 level lower than mage pets 20+
(Example the 49 Necro pet is at most, lvl 40, hits for 55. Mage pets are at most, lvl 41, hit for 56 (and it is REAL cool to see him hit for 56x4 and bash for 24 <G> )

Arming pets will in no way, effect their delay.

Pets attack inately at delay 30. Mages and Necros have pet only hast buffs, the best being a 60% haste, giving your pets roughly 17 delay at level 49. Mages get a summond haste mask at 56 (I think) That is 10-15% so delay 15 is possable at that level.

Mages can also Focus their pets, this DOES effect every pet from level 4 to 49. There are 4 focus items, obtainable as quests from the Temple of Solusk Ro. The 50+ pets can only be effected by a Mastery Staff, of which only 2 have ever been found, (Earth and Water)

Now, Enchanters and Shamen also get pets (shamen not till level 34 though)

The enchanter pets can duel wield, and obtain that ability at 29. They have VERY few hitpoints, and will not respond to /pet commands like Mage, Necro, ShadowKnight, and Shamen pets will.

Shamen pets cannot duel wield, and cannot be handed weapons. Nuff said.

I hope that all makes sense. Happy hunting!
