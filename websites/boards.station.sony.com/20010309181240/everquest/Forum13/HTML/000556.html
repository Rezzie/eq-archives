About these lists:
First and foremost, understand that this is only a list of concerns to be brought to the Live Team. This is not a list of promises, and I'm certainly not telling you that any or all of these items will be changed or implemented.

Also, there are three types of items that I don't have listed here. Epic Quests, Magic Resistances in Velious and Mid-level quests. Those three items were mentioned by enough classes that I've pulled them out for separate examination.

All items are currently marked with NONE for status. Obviously we've already started working on some items, you've seen the lists from the patch messages on the Test Server. I will be updating the status for items early next week to reflect those potential changes and with any responses from the team. I just wanted to get these lists out to you fresh.

I want these lists to be final as you see them now, but I know there will need to be changes made. So this list is not final. But if you want something added to or removed from the list, please be convincing about the change. At this point I don't want to tack on too many more items, since the team is already reviewing the original list.

Thanks for the input so far.

Alan


Monks:

ITEM: Unhooking Feet and Fists.

DESCRIPTION: In order to increase the monk’s combat ability, we could allow fist and kick attacks to be on separate timers, so that they could use kicks and punches independently.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Chain Attacks.

DESCRIPTION: If the monk successfully uses a Flying Kick, then allow that to have a chance to trigger a Dragon Punch (based on Double Attack skill). If the Dragon Punch lands then allow a chance, based on Triple Attack or something like it, that the monk will try an Eagle Strike. Make this a post 50 enhancement. If balanced properly, this could allow for monks to do more damage in a fun way.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Improved off-hand attacks.

DESCRIPTION: Another way to improve monk damage would be to make their off-hand as powerful as their primary hand. Of course, doing this would require a re-evaluation of a lot of weapons to make sure that they were not usable by monks if they would be too powerful (those that were made off-hand only so that they wouldn’t be too powerful in the primary hand).

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Monk Hand to Hand.

DESCRIPTION: It would be nice to make it so that monk fists were more likely to be their main weapon. Perhaps increasing the damage/delay ratio for monk fists to make them more competitive with weapons at every level, but especially past 50th level. With the better weapons available since the release of Kunark for other fighter types, it might be fair to make monks fist as good as 16/26 at 60th level.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Dodge, Riposte and Block.

DESCRIPTION: Currently these skills cap at about 230 for monks. We might want to think about increasing them a bit. Increasing Riposte might be one way of increasing their damage output as well.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Thunderkick and Flying Kick.

DESCRIPTION: There should be a minimum amount of damage done by Thunderkick and Flying Kick. Several ideas were suggested, but for Thunderkick, doing 24 points or even less with a 9 minute recycle combat discipline at 52nd level is pretty weak. The minimum damage should probably be close to the monk’s level or level*1.5. Perhaps level/2 for Flying Kick. In fact, it might be a good idea to look at a minimum damage for all monk specials.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Knock Back.

DESCRIPTION: Monks really don’t have a way to interrupt spell casters. Perhaps allowing kicks to do a slight bit of knockback just for the ability to stop casters, starting at the appropriate, level might be fair.

STATUS: None

---

TIME: Old abilities get new life,

DESCRIPTION: How about giving some of the old monk attacks new abilities once the monk reaches higher levels. For example, letting kick critical, or letting punches have a chance to stun. Basically, allowing the old skills to be used to achieve special results as needed at the higher levels (this idea should be abandoned if any sort of triple attack is given to monks, since this would probably make it too powerful). The thought is that at, say, 50th level, one of the skills you gain at 5th might get a new bonus, like stun or something. Same damage level, making it only useful for it's special power. A way to give a monk a special skill without linking it to a higher level skill (making that skill too powerful), and giving it the limitation of much reduced damage.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Mid-level weapons.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that there are not enough weapons for the mid level monk, especially of the two-handed blunt variety.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Appearances.

DESCRIPTION: Monks are one of those classes that tends to look the same throughout its career. It would be nice to give them new looks.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: 55th level discipline.

DESCRIPTION: There isn’t one. Perhaps we could add one with a stun component at that level.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Celestial Fist effect stacking.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that this effect doesn’t stack with much. Common buffs, like Call of the Earth, enchanter hastes, bards songs, and Speed of the Shissar will prevent the Celestial Fist effect.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: More balanced weight limit increase.

DESCRIPTION: How about spreading the weight limit increase more smoothly over the last ten levels. Perhaps 1 lb. for each level over 50 (though that would take it over the current 22 pound limit). Or start it at 52 and run it up one pound per level up to 59 to leave it at the same limit.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Combat Caps.

DESCRIPTION: Why do Flying Kick, Eagle Strike, and Dragon Punch all cap at 230 instead of higher?

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Feign Death.

DESCRIPTION: Does it make sense that Area of Effect spells or Damage over Time spells would break Feign? It might also be a fair idea to allow a successful feign death to lower the monk on the hate list, even if it’s only by a small fixed amount.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Feign Death Bugs.

DESCRIPTION: It seems that sometimes when a monk stands from a feign death he can only move forwards or backward, not side to side. The only way to fix this is to feign death again. Also, feign death does not work if the monk is in an area where they are forced to crouch, such as the area in Sol A near the lava elemental. There is also a claim that feign death is less effective if the monk is under two bubbles of health. That seems like a bug.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Treant Fists.

DESCRIPTION: Treant fists are harder to get than Gem Incrusted Gauntlets. It might be a good idea to consider making the Treant Fists a bit better. (more AC, perhaps Serpent Sight...)

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Dodge.

DESCRIPTION: Perhaps monks could be allowed to use their dodge ability when the attack comes from behind. This would represent their greater awareness, and might help to make them slightly better tanks in some situations.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Netted Armor in Kunark.

DESCRIPTOIN: There isn’t any. Perhaps something of similar nature for use by Iksar monks. The same goes for Woven and Mesh.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Shiverback Armor.

DESCRIPTION: They would like us to look at the quality of this equipment when compared to others of its type.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Sash and Belt quest.

DESCRIPTION: How about finishing the sash/headband quest up through the black belt level.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Natural Armor Class improvement:

DESCRIPTION: Allow monks to become less dependent on their armor by adding a large bonus for monks that had absolutely no armor on. Perhaps a chart like this:

Over 14 lbs: current penalty
10-14 lbs: no bonus, no penalty
8-10 lbs: 25 point AC bonus
5-7 lbs: 50 point AC bonus
0-5 lbs: 100 point AC bonus
or something similar.

STATUS: None

---

ITEM: Black Panther Armor.

DESCRIPTION: A full set of this armor weighs 14.1 lbs. This is over the weight limit for monks. I can accept that perhaps it was intended that they only wear parts of this armor, but it might also be heavier than intended.

STATUS: None

It seems you've forgotten something in your posts about class changes. Since most monks are trying to get more damage, I've decided to try to promote the idea of more defense.

I believe that monks should get an additional skill once they get to 51+. For now, I'll call it Sidestep. Similar to dodge, but at a much higher percentage. This will enable monks to avoid far more damage than ever before.

Here's the good:
Monks won't need to do anymore damage. As long as we are able to stay in the fight as long as warriors without an "evade" type skill, we will do more damage. Monk AC and HPs can stay the same, because we won't be getting hit nearly as much. Also, without the a taunt skill, monks can let the warriors tank, without disrupting the tactics. Feign Death can remain the same as well.

Now the bad:
Monks won't be viable tanks. Because it's based on a percentage, monks will have a "chance" to sidestep more attacks. The problem is that it is possible to get a bad roll, and get killed very quickly. Although the chances are slim, the possibility remains. Monks also cannot taunt. Monks have very low magic resistance (unless we buy/camp something) and low HPs. A NPC caster can still toast us in a hurry. Monks also have no way to stun a NPC (except with a disciple).

How to implement:
I think the best way to implement a chance like this is to give Monks this ability only at reaching 51. The cap for is ability should only be 100. Monks will get a cap on the ability every lvl. 51 will cap at 10, 52 will cap at 20, etc. At lvl 60, monks will be nearly impossible to hit in melee combat (nearly). This will also give monks a reason to reach lvl 60, because currently, there isn't much to urge someone playing a monk to get to 60.

To sum it up...
Increase damage evasion. That's it. Simple and Easy.
