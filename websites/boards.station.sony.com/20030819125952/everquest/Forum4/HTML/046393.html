The people that replied to your post are not jaded. They are simply pointing out that there is no way you can convince a large enough portion of the EQ player population to give up the few hours a day/week/month/whatever to come do something that offers no real benefit to them. To make matters worse, even if by some quirk of fate or chance you DID manage to get that many people online at one time with that sole purpose in mind, it's only temporary. They will all have to log out eventually and things will continue unchanged.

You spoke about taking an active role in the development of the game. It's too late for that. SOE won't allow it. They have total control over what happens in this game and they will not allow the players to make arbitrary changes like that. The simple fact of the matter is the game is too old for something like that to happen. The code can't take it. Implementing dynamic, player controlled cities on all the servers was never a consideration for EQ, nor will it ever be. Would it be cool? Hell yeah! Would it be possible? Not really... no. Unfortunately not.

Now, a great many of the newer games have features like this. In SWG a town's alliance was decided based almost entirely on it's inhabitants. Of course, players can build and run their own cities there too, but the game was designed with that sort of interactivity in mind. EQ was not. The basic game structure won't support it.

So, we're not being jaded by saying it can't/won't/shouldn't be done. We're simply being realistic about the game's limitations and the company's motivations.
