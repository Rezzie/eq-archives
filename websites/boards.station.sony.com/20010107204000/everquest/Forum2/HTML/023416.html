Throughout the past few days, we've received unprecedented response from Verant. A bunch of our concerns have been adressed, though many Bards are still not satisfied. Here's what I think are the most important issues of the Bard class (discounting bugged songs).

(Issues are sorted by # and letter. A is problem/suggestion, B is effect and reasoning)

1a. Haste stacking. Currently, Bard haste songs negate other Haste spells, even with their inferior effect. What many Bards want (and I haven't yet met any that disagree) is that Haste songs stack with Haste spells.
1b. This would allow Bards to group with Enchanters and Shamans more easily, not supplanting them but rather complementing them. Songs stack with every spell in the game (resists, damage shields, DoTs) except Haste. It wouldn't have a supreme effect on game balance--after all, there aren't enough Bards to make a significant difference either way.

2a. Re-examine Chains. It currently breaks too soon and doesn't even slow down some monsters.
2b. It used to be on a par with Snare. Granted, the higher-level song was probably too powerful, and it's possible that the lower level one was too. To fix it completely, just keep its effectiveness at 20% health monsters being stopped (like Snare) but give it a reliable resist rate and duration.

3a. Bards will NEVER stop complaining about Lullabye. It needs to be reexamined.
3b. Field test this song. Get yourself a real volunteer Bard (around level 15ish) and see how well the song works against appropriate monsters--not how much it resists, that is apparently working, but how useful it is with those resists. See how long the Bard can survive trying to mesmerize 3 monsters of equivalent level. If that doesn't convince you, nothing will.

Removing the secondary resist would not unbalance things. The specialist is still capable of more than the generalist, while the generalist is still useful.

6. Re-evaluation of buff songs that include a rune effect. The rune effect wears off with one hit, removing any other buffs with it. These songs seem poorly designed and need another look before you stop class balancing.

Chains and lullabye are most likely dead issues like melee binding imo and doubt they will ever be changed, but the other stuff I listed i'm a bit more hopeful about.

I'm happy to see a thread which hasn't yet degenerated into paranoid oversimplification.

I have yet to conduct my own tests on mezzing the snakes and rats, but I plan to do so. Until then, I have no comment on Lullaby, though I've found it very limited in practical usage.

Haste stacking, again, is something that we seem to be embroiled in conjecture on. Some say "it used to work this way, now I think it does this..." and others say "no, that's not it, it used to do this and now does this..." In the absence of scrupulous analysis, it's silly to debate it. It should stack, we think it doesn't, but that's where it ends.

The argument for "it should stack" is another matter... and saying that "all the other songs do" is erroneous, so should be avoided. Better to say "by and large, our buff songs stack with other buff spells of similar types. This allows us to be a help rather than a hindrance in a group." The former argument is simply brought down with Selo's/SoW. The latter opens debate, and offers a concrete reason for allowing the stack other than "we want it that way"... if our haste songs can overwrite or block haste spells, our casters are swearing at us for wasting their mana. Simpler for them to stack, also helps us feel the haste fix is not such a terrible slight to bards' abilities.

But I'm very heartened at the civility and rationality I see presented here. Congratulations to Talanic for having started this thread.
