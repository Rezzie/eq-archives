geejayess,

You are mistaken. Just because it's blue, and has about the same hp and requires the same mana, doesn't mean it's the same level.

Level is the only deciding factor in BASE mob exp. Zone modifers apply and make things different.

It doesn't matter if the mob casts spells, or has poison, or carries a weapon, or hits really hard, etc.

Yes, every mob in a zone of a certain level will give the same exp. Higher level mobs in that zone will give more exp.

Cross a zoneline and things change, because the zone experience modifier (ZEM) changes.

That's really all there is to it.

And yes, it's unfair that a spellcasting mob will do double the damage to you, have 5x the risk, but only give the same exp as any other non-casting mob of the same level in the same zone.

*****

So I think the original poster meant to ask:

Given the actual risk vs. reward, what mobs provide the best exp for the least risk and recovery downtime?

Yes, disregarding zone exp modifiers, creatures of the same level will yield the same amount of experience.

Having said that, however, creatures of the same level do not always do the same amount of damage, hit as many times, have the same resistances, or have the same hitpoints.

Bloodgills in Lake of Ill Omen are a good example. I think they pretty much have the lowest hitpoints of all the non-caster mobs of their level. So because you spend less hp/mana killing them, you have less downtime per kill, so you can kill more in a given period of time. Therefore, indirectly, the experience you get from them would probably be better than any other mob of that level.
