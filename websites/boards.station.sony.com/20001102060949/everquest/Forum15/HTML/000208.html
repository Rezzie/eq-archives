Well, until you hit 20 and get double attack, any level before that your hits won't be all that effective anyways. Once you get DA though, you should see the damage output start to climb. Also after level 20, you damage cap (can't hit for more than 30) is gone. Currently at level 48 I can hit for around 22 with a 5 damage weapon, and over 100 with a 25 damage weapon. As to what level your DW will be effective, any skill level is when it will be effective. You will just see it working more at higher levels is all.

As to limitations on the off hand weapons. There is only one limitation, some weapons don't equip in the off hand. There is no limitation as to the type you can use there. You have access to all 5 types of melee weapons (1&2HS, 1&2HB, piercing), and you can equip them all in the off hand.

You don't really need to worry too much about the off hand delay, the primary delay is much more important. The DW check is done after the primary hand weapon is used, then the off hand weapon's delay is checked. At lower skill levels, the DW check is infrequent enough to make up for any offhand weapon's delay.

At 17 I was dual wielding combine swords, medium delay, low damage weapons, also magic. After that I switched to a PGT and a combine, combine in off hand.


Hope some of this helps you out.

Keep your dual weild up, but don't ever neglect two hand slash. At higher levels, as a ranger dual weild is actually more a liability then a benefit. This is not to say that its useless, dual weilding fast ranger procing weapons (yak / flux axe / rat's tail) you can keep a casting mob stunned and prevent it from doing more damage to the group.
Best at your level is to first try to obtain a silvery war axe or a PGT (about 200pps give or take) and a dragoon Dirk (about 60pps give or take) Combine long sword is not worth it as it is.
And if you can, invest in a good 2 hand slash weapon.
