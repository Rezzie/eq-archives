Dranthor,

In one of your other post, you were wondering how a ranger could have more AC then you. From what I can see from your items, your AC would be very close to that of a ranger, but he may have a small edge on you. The main area that he might beat you on is as follows:

Chest: You use a Darkforge Breastplate which is good. If the ranger replace his Ivy Etched tunic for a Tree Weave tunic, then he would gain about 5 raw AC points here. At your level, Tree Weave should be easy to obtain. Its AC is 25 to the DF BP's 20.

Arms: You use the Darkforge Vambraces which is good and has some stat adjustment. A ranger may be using the Azure sleeves here which would give him 1 raw AC increase but no stat adjustment. He is sacrificing stats for AC, if he does.

Back: You are wearing the Cloak of the Ice Bear. This is a very good piece and with most ranger it would be equal to what they have. But if you by chance compair against a ranger that was lucky enough to obtain a Hooded Black Cloak (me ), he would have a 2 raw AC advantage.

Shoulders: This is one of the piece that you have that is kind of poor at your level. The magic resist is very good but the AC is very poor. A ranger could easily gain 6-7 raw AC points on you from this one item alone. He could be wearing the Crested Spaulders (6 raw AC) or the Tattered Mantle (7 raw AC). This is a big difference, one that may make up the advantages that you receive on some of your other Darkforge armor.

Earring: Sapphire Electrum Earrings are stat only earrings. I used to wear these all the time, myself. But when I decided to follow the prevelent AC moto of "AC is King", I switched them out. You can get many earrings that can give you 2-3 raw AC point each. That means 2-6 total raw AC points. Such earrings I am talking about are the Obsidian Bead Hoop, Diamondine Earring, Opalline Earring, and Gold Topaz Earring. All of these items are easily obtained at your level either by buying them (eg Gold Topaz Earring) or killing the green con mob (eg Obsidian Bead Hoop).

Neck: The banded collar is good for a ranger. IMO, it is not really good for a shadowknight. You can get better. Collar of Undead Protection will give you 1 raw AC point more and can easily be camped while you are trying to get a Rune Bone Fork for a range slot MR item. There are many other options, including the steel collar you mentioned.

Belt: Well the Thick Leather Apron is an excelent item. It works until you get either an TBB, Thick Straw belt (the Kunark giant drop item), or a haste belt. If you are compairing your AC against a ranger that has not obtained an FBSS, you will have not advantage over them. Almost every ranger that I know of that does not have an FBSS or greater, uses the Thick Leather Apron or better.

Do not get me wrong, I am not critizing your equipment choices here. I am only trying to give some examples of where a ranger could get more AC from you because of specific choices. I do this in responce to one of your post from another thread where you wondered how a ranger could have more AC ever if their class specific armor has less AC per piece. All of your listed (and for that matter, Nemesis's listed) equipment is very good things, IMO. We ranger tend to sacrifice stats for AC more. You two seem to be focusing on a lot of Dex and Str adjustment items with a backup in MR proctection.

Dranthor,

I figured as much seeing how much Darkforge stuff you have. Plus, I figured you would be replacing a lot of that with Umbry or Blood Ember soon. But there is a lot in the non-visable armor that can make a lot of difference. Even if you compair a ranger with full ivy to you, the Bloodstained Mantle vs. Crested Spaulders are a huge difference. The Crested Spaulders are the common drop of the Avenging Caitliffe in Mistmoore and can be easily soloed at level 35+. You may want to check that out if you are interested in AC. Personally, I traded that off for the Tattered Mantle which will give you 1 raw AC and 1 hit point more then the Crested Spaulders but is obtained from a much more difficult and higher level mob (Gullerback in BW, a level 48-51 mob).

It is good to hear that you want to get rid of the Sapphire earrings. Things like that and the Black Pearl Choker are highly over-rated, IMO. I used to have a BPC and watch it do absolutely nothing to my AC between wearing it and wearing nothing at all. The increase of agility above 75 makes a very small difference. Increasing it above level 105 makes absolute no difference in AC (atleast in the ranger of 105 to 148). I have seen this first hand.
