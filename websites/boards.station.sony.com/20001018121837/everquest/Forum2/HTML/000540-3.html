I think Verant is see this whole area as possibibly very unbalancing to the game. If you look at the Buff potions you can see that they tried to achieve balance by providing good effects with bad side effects. The other way was to set the prices up so high that not a lot of items would actually sell.

They saw that their first attempts with Alchemy were way too cautious and had to rething the balance. So besides fixing a few bugs, they lowered the component costs, but what did that really do? Well, for one it made it so more Shamans would try the skill and use the money skink, not much else changed. The original poster is right that increasing the vendor by-back to %90 would lower the practice costs and probably would also increase the number of Shamen attempting the skill.

However, we're back to my original observation, does Verant really want all that many "unbalanced" magical items in the world? I don't see these things as unbalancing due to their throw away nature, and the fact that none of the spell effects are different than what you might get from a regular caster's spell. Even if Verant didn't feels that Alchemy threatened the balance, they probably feel they can't allow the potion prices to fall so much that they would be commonly used because this would impact on the other spell casters.

One way around this problem is to implement entirely new effects that can only be found with potions. These effects would have to be desirable, and the cost vs benefit would have to be explored, but at least this way Alchemy would have it's own nich and not impact on other casters.
