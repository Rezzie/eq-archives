Drewbles wrote: "on a greater scale, shouldn't any trade that will leave items on the ground require a confirm box?"

Every trade requires a confirm box. The trade window *is* a confirm box.

There's a few things that need to be done to limit the possibility of these things happening:

1) Change autoinventory, such that it recurses into bag slots *before* going to the next main inventory slot. What I mean by this is if I have an empty backpack in slot 1, and nothing in slot 2, and I loot an item that can go in the backpack, it should go there instead of into the main inventory slot. If they changed this, it would be much easier to always keep slot 8 of the main inventory available for things like this. As it now stands, if you try to keep a main slot open, you are forever manually opening bags and putting things in them.

2) If a person is trying to receive traded items and any of them will drop to the ground, instead of dropping them, the trade should go back to red and a message should appear in the chat box. For the player receiving the items, it should say: "You do not have space for all of the items in the trade window. Clear inventory space or press cancel to end the transaction." For the player giving the items, it would say "The recipient of this trade does not have inventory space for the items. You may press cancel to close the trade window and restart the trade."

3) It would be VERY cool if items had a "size" tag. Granted, we've now got so many tags... magic, lore, nodrop, norent, slot, etc., but this would be really nice. I remember buying a wrist pouch for my enchanter and being mystified that tiny daggers didn't fit in it. They're "tiny", right? Tags might be Infinitesimal, Miniscule, Small, Medium, Large, Gigantic, Humongous or something. Then when you right click on a container, it will tell you the largest item size it can hold.
