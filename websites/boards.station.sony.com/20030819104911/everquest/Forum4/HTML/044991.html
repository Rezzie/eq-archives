I believe I answered your question back in your first thread - you sometimes need to hit reload button for replies to show, though.

Band damage adds to base damage when weapon is used against the right mob.

So, a 31/30 weapon with +4 bane:undead becomes a 35/30 if used against undead. You crit damage, including Slay Undead damage, is based off that number (35).

Then, there's elemental damage. That differs a bit. For example, a 10/18 weapon with Magic Dmg +2 checks mob's MR on every hit. If mob resists, it's as if he's hit by a DMG 10 weapon. If not, it's a DMG 12 hit.

Paladins who have Combat Fury archetype AA can score critical hits the way warriors do.

Paladins further have Slay Undead class ability, which has Combat Fury 3 prerequisite. This class ability, whenever you score a critical hit vs. undead, intensifies it into a massively damaging blow. The text message accompanying this is smth like:
A Valiant Spirit is cleansed by Tristane's holy blade(1200)

Paladins can do some sick damage vs undead with this AA. Yesterday I saw a pally with PHF doing 2400hp crits vs spirits in HoH.

Elemental damage modifiers like Magic, Fire etc can also be *partially* resisted. Thus a 16+5magic/29 weapon will sometimes act as 16/29, sometimes as 17/29, ..., sometimes as 21/29.

Easiest way to think about these is to regard the damage as including half the elemental damage; eg think of a Long Sword of Ethereal Energy (16+5magic/29) as 18.5/29; think of a Disemboweler (12+3magic/20) as 13.5/20. It all averages out in the long run

Of course, this simple approximation will fall down if you do something silly like use +poison weapons against spiders, etc etc.
