Split-server moves have a time window for the /movelog. You just missed one for moving to the new Morell-Thule server. Furthermore, only a lmiited number of players are admitted to the new server on a first come, first serve basis. And even then, as mentioned above, only one or two servers are usually allowed to move to the new server. Yours might not have even been in the listing.

And to make things more confusing, the /movelog announcements in game are broadcasted throughout all the servers, even the ones that aren't involved in the move. I suppose that helps people who play on multiple servers to camp out of one and log into the applicable server (if they have characters there) and intiate the /movelog.

Split-servers may also tend to have somestrange or shaky economies with a limited number of high level characters coming into what is essentially a newbie server. Also, if two or more parent servers move into a new split server, there will have to be a period adjustment for peculiarities from each of the parent servers as well as for cooperation between old guilds, especially uber guilds. (Who gets to kill Naggy this week?)

I play on Saryrn, a split server from Bristlebane and Veeshan. We went through our own pecularities there. Bristlebain's "bazaar" zone was East Commonlands at the Ro Tunnel, Veeshan's "bazaar" zone (if they had one) seems to have been North Freeport. For a while, we had the larger guilds more or less racing each other to camp the planes and the dragons, even to the point of monopolizing them. Both sides had differing ideas of item prices, though much was similar. There are probably other examples I'm forgetting. Anyhow, months later we're much more smoothed out now.

OK, since I was mentioned, just a couple quick updates for trade skills.

Lets see, so this means you left in April or so.

Blacksmithing = 1100 new recipes!!!! Yes, you saw that right. Dyable Fine Steel plate is not the next step above banded armor. There are (trying to remember) 6 different colors, and 3 shades of each color, plus a LOT of forgeable weapons. We also got Culteral "Defining" armor. Harder to get than planes armor, or Kunark armor, this is the pinicale of the "Status" symbol for armor wearing classes. Dwarves make for the small races, High Elves make for good elves, Humans make for themselves, erudites, and barbarians, Iksar make their own, Ogres make for Fatties, and DE's make their own. Gnomes will get a tinkered version eventually.

Tailoring = MANY new recipes, including a LOT of uses for spiderling silk. Formerly known as useless . Tailord bags (not just packs), tailor'd bandages (lighter than store bought), etc, and the quiver is in game so your skill can go up to 115. This is NOT a complete upgrade. The erudites and halflings will get race defining tailored items, much as dwarves, high elves, humans etc got race defining blacksmithed armor.

Tinkering = MASSIVE update planed, but not yet implimented.

Brewing = LOTS of discovered recipe's, and new content, such as fruit juices that act like water and milk for quenching thirst. Brewers also make the tempers for the new culteral armor.

Etc, etc, etc... You can also now EXCEED 200 in trade skills, but you can choose only 1 to pass it in. All others will cap at 200. This is similar to the spell school specialization casters get. Yes, that means you can now TRIVIAL platinum jewelry, or a lot of it at least . (I've read of someone hitting 250 in Jewelcrafting already)

Anyway, that is a VERY brief run down on some of the new traeskill content. I suggest you hit www.eqtraders.com, or everquest.allakhazam.com, or www.everlore.com, or eq.castersrealm.com (trade stuff here too)
