You know, the more I hear from people, and the more I contemplate the bard class, the more convinced I become that the "eccentric" class of EverQuest needs an "eccentric" balance.

We've got a lot of songs that most classes would consider fairly useless, but we've managed to find ways to use them effectively, for the most part. We have managed to specialize in the short-term charm... (tell another class they can have a charm spell that lasts for 5-15 seconds and see if they're as excited as we are to get it). We dance around like maniacs trying to avoid death, while using area-effect damage songs in places where others insist they are fatal. Nearly every song has a use for some bard amongst our ranks...

Well, Mellifluous Motion is an exception, since I don't know anyone who has found enough use for it to bother to memorize it.

So... perhaps all we really need are a few more unique songs scattered through our list? Maybe even some that rely upon each other to work well.

A few ideas might be:

Stage Death Song - A Pixie Strike mesmerization effect, with a strong AC reduction component. The target would basically be forced into a "feign death" pose.

Dance of Madness - Area Effect hate-list wipe song... Remove the aggro list from everything within range, and force them to dance in a circle for a second or two. This song would essentially shuffle targets in a group combat, since obviously there would be plenty of targets within range after it was played.

Call of Atonal Resonance (i.e. the headache song) - High level damage over time on a single target, with an increase in "fizzled" spells... perhaps through intelligence reduction?

Ballad of the Bacchanal - A group "drunk" song, where each pulse is the same as a sip of drink. Silly and strange... but potentially useful, kind of.

Anthem of Leadership - A song which specifically cancels fear or charm effects on the group. Would by necessity need a huge radius. Would also be very appropriate for a bard.

Temporal Timbres - An extreme unstackable area effect and group haste song. A maxxed out haste which affects everything in an area, grouped or not. Basically, it would speed up a physical combat on both sides. Might be to good effect, might be a mistake, but isn't that how all the best bard songs are?

The Slippery Song - Increases the breakout chance for roots, snares, and entanglements.

-------------
Okay... I could go on, but I just wanted to put this idea out there as an alternative solution, more in keeping with the "flavor" of the bard class, rather than simply bumping up defense or some other bland fix.

Attributes required to play a bard:
1) Introverted player who does not want to lead groups or talk to other players because they'd rather hit keys every 3 seconds for the duration of all combat and critical situations.
2) Fast network connection to avoid lag, because lag cancels any skill you might develop as a player.
3) A fast computer because of the extra processing power needed to display graphical effects generated by any skill you develop.

I think the idea of macroing songs would address at least two of the three issues above.

As for balance... well, I don't have a high level bard so it is hard for me to speak to it. It does seem to me that a high level bard is a "jack of all trades". So while a cleric is a better healer, a warrior a better tank, a mage a better nuker, and an enchanter a better traffic cop a bard should be able to contribute some effectiveness in any of these areas as the need arises. Just because your group has an enchanter shouldn't reduce a bard's contribution to crowd control. Just because you have a cleric shouldn't mean your healing is worthless. Instead healing should be better. Crowd control should be better. Buffing should be better. Everything should be a little better.

Basically, I think a bard should be able to help with whatever role is needed at that moment... A high level bard should be to a well rounded group what spices are to a finely prepared meal. Alone they really don't provide any nourishment, but in combination they accentuate what is already there, making everything a little better. Better crowd control when you're mobbed. Better healing/mana during downtime. Another tank when you need a target to distract a MOB. Another puller when your monk is stuck. Etc. Etc. Etc.

But most of all, fix the ends/starts/ends bug.

At each stage in a bard's life EXCEPT post-50, there is a fair variety for methods of play.

Our damage at lower levels while duel wielding & using our buffs as well as other classes buffs aren't so bad, though our tanking ability dramatically reduces post level 15.

However, this does not ruin our ability to play altogether. Yes, we can melee. But the ability to double attack would overbalance us in the pre-50 game. Giving us double attack post 50 could potentially help, yes, but personally -- and i know from the responses I've read that others agree with me -- I would rather see resist's on our song's lowered so they can be effective against post 50 encounters, and the amount of damage (and for the chant's series, as well as our new velious song for lowering resist's, to increase the amount they drop resist's by) to scale better in the post 50 game to be more compareable to other classes.

I feel this way because I've done the math, I've seen how much potential damage a bard can do if their song's don't resist as much, and it can still

When high level bard's have posted timings and such of the damage they can deal with their melee skills using nice haste items and a high end haste buffs, none of them has topped 1500 damage a minute that I've seen, though I'm sure someone might have enough high end equipment to pull it off.

Now, add in double attack, and let's just assume for a minute that it actually works most of the time, and we'll give them 90% more damage, assuming they can actually deal 1500 damage a minute. This will place the bard at 2850 damage a minute, with just melee skills.

Not so bad ... but then, most bards can't get all of this equipment. Should the resist's on song's be lowered enough that their usefull in a majority of situations post 50, ANY bard has the potential to deal as much as 1500-2000 damage a minute just using chant's and a decent drum (any of the magical drums available in fact ... preferably selo's of course). Add in the fact their dropping resist's for casters to nuke, the damage output their creating is unguessably higher.

Personally I'd like to see a poison and disease based chant's to account for the situations in which something is completely fire, cold, or magic resistant (or a combination of the 3), so we can still provide damage output and not get gimped by the resistance of that creature.

If you take any of the AVERAGE bards, not those equipped with extremely good gear ... I'm close to doubling the amount of damage they deal. I'm still besting post 50 bards with very high end gear (in some cases the best). I think it's pathetic that we're reduced to this, but I do not feel that better melee skills are our answer. If you hate being a mana battery so much, why do you fight to be a mana battery who can swing weapons better?
