I wanted to throw my own two cents in on this, especially since most of the comments here seem to focus on the high end game and less on stuff below 40 to 45. My bard isn't 40 yet and issues for her are different, and fit into a few broad categories. I think one of the core balancing issues is how our songs seem to be valued for balancing.

First at all levels we seem to be evaluated as a partial tank, well depending on who you ask we are a pure caster after some level in the high 20s or 30s doing very little tanking because we have this massive aggro rate and pure caster level hp, and both weak combat defense and offense. At some point our songs must be powerful enough to compete with pure casters for effectiveness because we are effectively casters that stop tanking later than other casters, or we must have our combat skills increased in some way to make it realistic that we are a secondary tank.

Second, we get a huge penalty in our song effectiveness relative to other hybrids because they are mostly manaless (with Dirge costing so much mana that nobody uses it, and I don't intend to buy it when I get to that level if it doesn't change). Although this is nice and it has a side effect that we can cast while moving this seems to be quite overvalued. To balance this our songs are weaker, much more easily resisted and shorter duration. In just about every case except running with our drums our songs are the weakest of their type when compared to even a much lower level caster of the parent class of that type of spell.

One reason that this is the case I think is because the fact that our songs are manaless is seen as a bonus without any downside. Our songs should be evaluated not as "free" but as having a different opportunity cost. That is how much effect can our songs have over time vs. a caster taking into account caster downtime for medding. This should also take into account the level of the song/singer (a little intricate since our songs are better as we level, but at a rate that doesn't keep up with other increases in abilities for other classes) vs. level of spell/caster. I think the major reason that our songs are so much weaker than caster class spells in most cases, is they are overvalued because of being mana-free and not seen as each song being a third to a half of our total casting ability at any time. Because other classes can med back mana for their buffs in much less than the time they last, they are usually worth much less than anything close to a third of their casting balance. Also mana casters can give (or in the case of clarity and SOW) sell their spells to people not in their group, and the effect of the spells continue for some time whether they are still in their group, stunned, far away or dead, this is not true of the Bard. This also means a caster can cast a buff and free up a spell slot to mem another, having more variety of spells available over time. And lastly mana casters can split their spells between only those who need them. Our songs effect everyone in the group who is in range, which is great, except we are rarely in a group where everyone is helped by any given song, so unlike a caster we are effectively casting spells on party members that don't benefit or benefit very little. I think our songs are balanced assuming every member of the party is benefitting from our buffs all the time, and in fact this is rarely true. It is a weak group that is all casters to benefit from our mana songs, and it is rarely the case that all of the members of our party need hp regen.
