I think the most disappointing aspect of this thread is that I think the core issue isn't really being heard, though it has been stated again and again. Semantics have been used as a shield, but the issue remains...

In my opinion (and, I think, the opinion of many), the compelling aspect of illusions is their form (fun factor) and main "effect" (str on earth elem, damage shield on fire elem, etc). The faction aspect is secondary... and is generally only a factor in specific circumstances. For example, once you're over level 34, there are very few reasons for a non-DE enchanter to visit Neriak and use DE form... while using DE form for the ultravision is MUCH more likely. Remember that the faction aspect is capped... I'm barely scowls to Troll Guards... yet in illusion form, I can't improve beyond threatening. It isn't as if I can overcome my faction with an illusion. However, illusions DO get used frequently for fun... for show... and for their abilities (I use air elemental form frequently for free levitate).

Now, Absor has repeatedly stated that the "illusions" for druids and necromancers aren't "illusions" but rather transformations. Well, the same could readily be true of enchanter illusions. I get strength because I'm an earth elemental... I fight better because I'm a werewolf... etc. Let's get off the semantic kick and instead focus on the issue.

The issue, again, is simple. Enchanters have repeatedly been ignored with regards to gaining new uses for their illusion abilities. No one (hopefully) is asking for more powerful illusions. What is being asked for is some additional variety when CLEARLY that space could be so applied... but isn't being. The issue is FUN.

As for the complaint of Monster Summoning... I agree that it is a cool new ability. However, the ability could EASILY have been mimiced. If I could cast Illusion: Monster and become a random monster (KoS to pretty much everything), that would be wonderfully fun! Would it be powerful? Nope. Unbalancing? Nope. Costly in resources? Nope. If you wanted to spend actual work time on it, you could let each form have some trivial effect (like our other illusions do)... tiny poison dot attack in spider form... +STR but -AGI in giant form... etc... but this would be a bonus. The core is just the desire for the fun factor.

So let's address the issue... not the semantics. Please give the fun to enchanters in a similar fashion as you did mages, druids, and shamans. You can do it... easily. The only question is "will you?".

Hello all,

I'm an Enchanter of the 38th season, and personally I would have to agree with Alan when he mentioned that the real "magic" is how you play your class.

I've been trotting around Velious for a week or so now, and yes, I've noticed that the new mage spells are quite the eye-opener (ever had a mage running with a "named" snow griffin behind him yelling for help the whole time? LOL). To me, that doesn't mean that Enchanters are all washed up, though.

Keep this in mind: Our function in the game (in a group) is crowd control. Now mages can have all the nifty pets that they want, but they won't be able to have the same sort of crowd control ability as Enchanters.... Sure, the bard can mez now, but we have:

Root to keep mobs at bay
Entrance/Mez to keep them quiet
Charm/Beguile to let one fight for us
Memory Blur/Wipe if they get too overpowering.

Combine these lines of spells, and we're the lifesavers. In my opinion, that creates much more "awe" from other classes than just having a walrus attack a frost giant.

Once I was in HighKeep with a few people, and the pickclaws got out of hand... there must have been 6 or 7 all attacking our group of 4 people. In a normal situation, that would spell death. With an Enchanter in the group, though? ..:

Charm one, have 1 attack 2, entrance 3, 4, 5, and 6, root all of them afterwards. Color Shift the 7th while everyone attacks it... Then move down the line. If one breaks mez, you re-mez before the root breaks. If the charm is broken, then Color Shift and re-charm.

In the end, we didn't suffer a death, and actually we were all pretty stable afterwards. Everything worked out great. In my opinion, THAT'S the "magic" of our class, and people WILL notice, that's a given.
