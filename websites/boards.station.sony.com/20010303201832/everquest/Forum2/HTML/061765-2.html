Tracking, in and of itself, doesn't strike me as the reason for such disillusionment in the overall Ranger community. The source seems to lie a bit deeper than that: what makes a Ranger so special?

As someone noted, a Ranger is a hybrid of the Warrior and Druid class. We are not their equals though.

A Warrior will always be a better tank because of a much higher AC and two, three, sometimes even four times as many hitpoints. As much as I love my Ranger, I know that I cannot soak the damage the way a Warrior can, nor can I avoid it. This last patch gave Rangers the same offensive skill caps as a Warrior, making a Ranger even more powerful in combat... assuming, as has been said, we "don't get hit."

A Druid has a vast array of spells available. A Druid can be the group's healer, the group's buffer, or can work purely as a damage machine with DoT and DD spells. This is why Druids can solo. This is why they can quad kite. This is what makes Druids valuable to a group, especially pre-50. A Ranger has a limited number of spells, and all of them are received long after the Druid has them. You will not see many groups saying, "Group needs Ranger as healer." Since they are from the same spell lines, they are often overwritten by their parent class, or by others who have more powerful spells in the same vein.

Someone noted the spell-related perks Rangers have received... the increased mana pool was given to all hybrids, as they were being doubly penalized (spells cost more AND the mana pool was lower). So I can have the same manapool as a Druid my level... how many Rangers will sacrifice AC for wisdom? Not many. How many Rangers will step back from a fight and do nothing but cast? Not many. Sure, we CAN do it.. that doesn't mean it's a good way to do it though.

And spellcasting has a speed boost for all hybrids. This was more of a hybrid balancing gimmick than anything. A hybrid, wethr it be Ranger, Paladin, or Shadowknight, stands in the thick of the fight, shoulder to shoulder with the Warriors, Monks, and Rogues. If we're getting pounded on, we can't cast. If we step out of combat to cast, we do less damage. So the speed was upped to both make the hybrids more atractive to players, and to make the spells somewhat useful to them as well.

So what makes a Ranger unique from Warriors and Druids? Paladins and Shadowknights each get a unique ability -- Lay of Hands for one, Harm Touch for the other. So what does a Ranger get that is so unique?

Tracking? Archery? These are the two things that pop to mind for most people. It's the image of a "Ranger" that most people have... the stealthy hunter, tracking his quarry mercilessly across any terrain, finally unleasing a torrent of arrows upon them.

The game works to emphasize this image... the symbol of the Ranger class is the bow. The "Official Players Guide," as referenced to buyers in the back of the game manual, describes Rangers as, "Rangers' skill with the bow and arrow are unmatched by any other class in Norrath." The coverart for Kunark displays a Rangerly type standing back from the fight, aiming his bow at the party's foe.

However, reality gives us a different "image." Archery is not unique to the Ranger class. The bow can be used by most melee classes. Rangers are not the only ones to get criticals with archery -- Warriors can as well. The Trueshot discipline is great... if you're 55 or higher, a goal many Rangers will never see (myself included). And even Trueshot has been noted as being disappointing.. acurracy with the discipline is sometimes no better than normal archery accuracy, and damage is often little better.

Verant has made it quite clear, on numerous occassions, that they do not want archery to be a viable form of primary combat. How many people here will step back from a fight and do nothing but pluck arrows at their target, letting the rest of the party go melee with it?
