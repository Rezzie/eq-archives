I DO think our defensive skills (defense, dodge, riposte, block,) should be much higher than a warriors tho. You think a warrior should be able to outmanouver a Monk with all that gear on?

Proposed Solution: Any and all defensive skills for a monk should be higher than that of a warrior. It just makes sense. Someone told me warriors have equal or higher defnese skills than monks. This makes no sense and should be addressed and tweaked to the monk's favor.

I support the post made by Zadkiel in the first thread. It was developed with input from many monks on the Monkly Business web site/message forum, which Zadkiel graciously devotes his time to create and maintain. I think that it addresses many Monk concerns, much of which are about the post-50 game. This seems to be appropriate, based upon what I have read and heard about (my monk is only 35, but I have been playing him since March 1999).

I did have a few items that I wanted to add (some new thoughts, some emphasis on other posts):

1. Flying Kick Minimum Damage. How about making the minimum damage of a successful Flying Kick equal to the player's level (similar to Rogues' backstab)? If not that formula, how about making the min. damage equal to one-fifth the player's skill level in Flying Kick? This would scale well across the levels, not just post-50.

2. Maximum Flying Kick Damage. How about increasing the maximum damage to be equal to the player's skill level in Flying Kick? This would of course increase the overall average damage done by this special attack.

3. Increase the skill caps on all of the Monk special attacks to 250 (post-50).

4. Add the chance (post-50) for Flying Kick to result in a "string" of attacks, as suggested in Zadkiel's post. I really love that idea, but if that can't be implemented, then how about a chance to double-kick from level 50 onwards?

5. Add a chance to stun to Dragon Punch / Tail Rake:

a. The stun would only have a chance to hit if the special attack hits, and then should have a percent chance based on the player's skill level in the special attack. One possible formula: chance to stun = (skill level / 400). This would give the level 25 monk a 32% chance to stun after a successful punch/rake, and the level 60 monk a 63% chance (based on a 250 skill cap). If the special attack misses, there would be no stun.

b. The duration of the stun could be based on the player's level, with a formula like (level - 25)/5 (seconds), rounded down to the nearest whole number of seconds. This would yield a zero-second stun at level 25 (but would still interrupt casters), and a 7 second stun at level 60.

From a roleplaying aspect this makes sense to everyone, I think. Monks are martial arts masters, they should be able to stun and thus temporarily disable their opponent, and this ability should increase with their experience and training.


Summary:

I support the suggestion about offhand damage bonuses made in Zadkiel's post, and think that this would go a long way towards making Monk damage output fall somewhere between warriors and rogues (which I think everyone agrees is where it should be).

I think that the Monk special attacks are one of the things that help make the class unique, and would like to see the suggestions above considered in addition to the offhand damage proposal.

In my opinion, modifying Dragon Punch/Tail Rake as above would increase our benefit to a group, and also force us to choose between the higher damage of Flying Kick (if the first 2 of my points are implemented) or the possiblity of stunning the target. This choice would make the game more interesting for Monks, I think.

It think that the opportunity is appreciated by most who read any EverQuest-related web site or message board and is seen as a very positive thing. I am sure that distilling all of the information and then deciding what to implement has got to be a monumental task for the team, good luck with it. I know that many suggestions will not be implemented in the class balance tuning, but hope that they provide much food for thought for new content and improvements to the EverQuest world in the future.
