What I am saying here is this. Who here has some programming skills? We all know what a linked list is right? When the pet is given the command to follow, every render, push the casters current position onto the linked list. The pet should then go directly to a point between its position A, and the position just pushed onto the list B. This could be say 10 world units distance from point B along ray AB. Each time the pet reaches 10 world units from point B, B is then popped from the linked list and the pet is then given a new point B to go to, which would be the next point on the linked list that was pushed there by a previous render. Basically the concept is, every location B, pushed on the list, is a bread crumb that the pet follows. The caster is "in code" leaving a trail for the pet to follow. An implementation of some kind, with appropriate tweaking for memory requirements and error handling (object between point A and B stop the pet from advancing to B, Caster moving far faster than the pet, etc.) could very well be the solution to pathing for both mobs and pets alike. Okay, here's a good idea that will "fix" necros during raid time, but not give them any more power or be unbalancing.

Why bring a necro when you can bring anoher cleric, or another tank (a "complete heal")?

Since HP = mana for a necro, this will effectively stop the necro from liching / pumping any more after he complete mana dumped.

Whew, I'm done I personally think this is a decent suggestion, which is completely realistic. I'd like to hear your comments about it, as well as Alans.

Alan,

With all due respect, I think this type of thread is genuinely counter-productive to what you/we are trying to encourage people to get out of the game.

EverQuest is designed on a class system. Where you pick a class that has certain abilities, limitations, bonuses, downfalls, and usefullness dependening upon the situation. This is how all class-based games work, which was first made popular in the mainstream consumer market with games such as Dungeons and Dragons (Notice: I said popular).

What this means is, you pick a class that suits your playstyle and needs. You DON'T pick a playstyle that suits your class. It's impossible in a class-based system, and any attempt to change it basically defeats the entire purpose of making it a class-based system. We aren't playing a game like UO, where you DO mold your character into what you want to play.

It has long been stated that Necromancers lack in later levels what they have as extremely generous benefits all through their younger levels to mid high levels. Heck, to make a Necromancer more group/raid oriented would be to completely deny the class it's born heritage of being a loner. Necromancers can chain summon pets, give mana, heal, grant resists to deadly elements, and this is just the things they can do during a battle.

I don't see anyone asking for Magicians to get a mid-battle upgrade. Versus mobs that are highly resistant (ya know, the same ones that Necromancers are having trouble with and feel they are useless because of it), all a magician is good for is modulation rods, and chain summoning pets. Oh yeah, they can also summon tons of bandages and bind wounds during a battle.

Nor do I see anyone begging for Enchanters to get a mid-battle upgrade. What can they do versus a highly magical resistant mob? Why, chain cast Rune 5 on the tanks, and use that uber-taunting mana drain on mobs, which is also resisted easily. Oh yeah, they do have a pet that is completely uncontrollable with less than 1000 hit points which can be chain cast during a fight, only to get killed 4 hits later.

I'm sorry, but if those people out there who are playing a Necromancer, but want to play a Wizard, Cleric, Shaman, or tank, then they should by all means play those classes. There's no such thing as a Necromancer-Wizard, or a Necromancer-Cleric, or a Necromancer-Shaman, or a Necromancer-Warrior in EverQuest. Giving people the idea that they can post here in hopes that one day they CAN play a class like that is only digging a grave (no pun intended) for yourself and EverQuest.
