Ok, here's the situation as I see it:

- The code for handling automatic inventory isn't very good. Two things happen: main inventory slots are filled up first, making it difficult to keep them open; and quest items are forced into the inventory, causing them to disappear if it does not have the right-size slot free.

- This is a fairly well-known fact. See many posts on Wurmslayer trades gone bad and quest containers poofing.

- It is not a difficult matter to have a space free in your main inventory for quest results, but it requires conscious effort on the player's part. However, it is *completely* within the player's control to maintain this. It is Verant's policy not to reimburse for any losses that are either (a) within the player's control or (b) completely out of Verant's control and the player's (i.e. linkdeath).

- This increases the mechanical complexity of the game, and many people find that undesirable.

What it boils down to is nothing more than bad interface design. It's not a bug, it's not bad service, it's just more awkward. It's fine to ask for interface improvements; I have done so more than once, even started a thread on it after losing my tin box the first time. But you're not going to get the issue addressed if you mislabel it and slander (er, libelize?) a particular GM who is doing his job just the way he's supposed to.
