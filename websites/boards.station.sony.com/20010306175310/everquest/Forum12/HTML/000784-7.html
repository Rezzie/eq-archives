Here is how Abashi Described it in January of 2000:

The changes to Wizards mean that for any Wizard above Level 35 they get a lower chance of their Cold, Fire, or Magic spells being resisted. In reality their spells are basically given a built in "debuff" factor. This stacks with any other debuff, such as Tashania, Malosi or Temperate Flux and gives Wizards much better combat effectiveness. It is not a duration debuff. It does not mean when you cast a DD spell the mobs resistance is lowered for other casters in the group. What the tweak does is when a DD spell is cast it looks at the current resistance of the target, and then the new tweak reduces that resistance amount further, then uses that new reduced resistance within the game formula to determine if the spell lands, or is partially or fully resisted. This process is done each and every time you cast a spell which is enchanced by the new code. Wizards should now notice the following with these types of spells now:
·Green Mobs: Rarely Resisted
·Blue Mobs: Rarely Resisted
·White Mobs: Much Lower Resistance
·Yellow Mobs: Much Lower Resistance
·Light Reds: You will now have a much better chance against these mobs.
·Deep Reds: Regular Resistance, with a slight chance of the "debuff factor" assisting.
This tweak for Wizards doesn't change the 6 level cap.

It simply makes general combat much easier for the Wizard Class. They should be able to use more powerful spells with less resists on mobs that would be resisted by most other members of the party. It should help to keep the Wizard the true masters of direct damage spells."

For example, in order to kill VS, you have to prevent him from ever hitting anyone in the raid party. Wizards dealing fast damage is only a part of that strategy. Also some mobs are so hard-hitting that you need to blast them. But in all those encounters, you need lots of melee people contributing.
In raid parties, in general, the low/mid-50's wizard is OFTEN are unable to contribute AT ALL. Most classes can do something, but all we do is keep getting resisted while casting lures. That's very frustrating. Seriously, we know it's going to be resisted but we cast anyway because there's nothing else for us to do.
Making melee-proof mobs is a way to share our frustrations with others, but that's the wrong way to go.

Quad Kiting: Try it. It's not so easy as you might think, and it's nearly impossible to find the right creatures to pull in the first place. It's fun. It's an accomplishment. If you get good at it, you can benefit, but I don't think it rivals powerleveling or any sort of exploit. I won't cry if it is nerfed by reducing the cast range of the snare AE, but I think it should be left alone for wizards. I have saved my party by snaring a whole gang of mobs as we ran away, so I don't want this nerfed because of the affect on non-quad kite activities.

Suppression spells: (Anti-summoning) I like this idea. We should be able to provide unique forms of defense against enemy magics. However, this would allow us to kite 51+ mobs. But we need this protection so we can med during raids. And what happens if the mob tries to summon you and can't? Does it forget you and attack someone else? Run over to you? I just don't think we can make this idea work out. Instead, just lower the agro of sitting to med.

Blink spells: This is exceptionally cool, but you do realize this would probably only be useful for soloing and outdoor kiting, only. I like it, but our balance problem is our low damage output in a group. If we blink to the other side of the same mob, still in melee range, then it would be good for groups and raids.

Concussion: I'd like to make this unresistable. The only time it doesn't work is on mobs we have trouble casting on anyway, and we have enough limitations against those mobs that this would be a reasonable balance. This is a raid-encounter enhancement request.
