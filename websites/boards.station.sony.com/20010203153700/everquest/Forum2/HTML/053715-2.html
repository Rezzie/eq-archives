Muktuk, I have NEVER seen a shout for a group looking for a "nuker." I see LFG for healer, I see LFG for tank, I see LFG for Enchanters because every healer is better with a Breeze or Clarity. I have never, ever, EVER seen a group shouting "Group looking for nuker." Doesn't happen. Know why? Because EVERY CASTING CLASS CAN NUKE.

Even though they rarely need to use them, Enchanters get a few really good nukes. Mages get nukes as good as a Wizard's, just not as often or as many. Necromancers can nuke, and get HP back for doing it, HP they can convert to mana. Clerics can nuke quite well, especially Undead. Shamen are great nukers, especially with a Jaundiced Bone Bracer. Druids nuke great, I group with one every day and she nukes like a Wizard and can use Harmony to pull anything we need from any situation. Bards have manaless nukes, including AoE. Even a Tank class, Shadowknights, can nuke, and Harm Touch is as good or better than anything a Wizard has.

The only classes that can't really cast nukes are considered Tank classes. Paladins and Rangers. They have a tank's AC, can wear good armor, higher hitpoints than a typical caster, these tanks aren't really casters, they are tanks that can toss small heals (and in a Ranger's case, cast SoW at 39 and up).

So, you will not see a group "looking for a nuker," because there is NO DEMAND for nukers except in a few very specific raid situations like VS, hardly the object of casual pick-up groups.

Maybe that's the mistake Verant made when they designed their Wizard class: They designed it entirely around nuking, but did not give them such clear superiority in nuking that they were in demand for nuking. I mean, all classes can melee, but the Warrior/Rogue/Ranger classes can clearly melee FAR FAR BETTER than, say, an Enchanter can. A dozen times better at least.

But the Wizard, of all the classes that can nuke, only has maybe a few percentage points better nukes than our closest rival in nuking, the Magician. Farthest down on the nuking list is probably the Enchanter, and a Wizard's nuke isn't even twice as good as an Enchanter's at the same spell level.

Why would melee superiority be so heavily tilted in the favor of a few classes, but in the realm of Magical Direct Damage, all nuke-capable classes are much more homogenous in capability? How come my Wizard cannot melee one fourth as effectively as a Shaman, yet also cannot nuke at least five times better than a Shaman to balance the abilities?

To make up for this unbalancing shortfall in capability, other INT casters got pets to help bolster their melee capabilities. Wizards did NOT get this helper, yet our nukes and/or melee cpabilities were not boosted to compensate.

In short, Wizards are the most broken of all the classes, because they have not been balanced. Our meleeing capabilities are near to nothing, we have no pet to compensate for that shortcoming like the other INT classes, we have no unique combat abilities whatsoever, only one non-combat ability which we share with Druids, and to top off that list our nukes are not much stronger than any other nuke-capable class.

There are only four ways to fix this, pick one:
1. Give Wizards better nukes (like TWICE as good as now)
2. Make our current nukes cost one-fourth the mana they now do.
3. Boost the Wizard's meleeing capabilities significantly. Traditionally, when a Wizard in fantasy lore gets high enough in stature, he can begin to wield a magic sword if he feels like it. Maybe after we hit level 30 our melee caps should go up to the same as a Rogue's, and we should be able to equip 1H slash weapons as long as they are magical. It creates a pretty picture that, while not unbalancing really (we won't get hide/sneak, lockpick, pickpocket, or anything else of a Rogue's, just have the same potential capabilities in the form of caps), that of a battling Wizard with a magic sword fighting in a group of adventurers overwhelmed by enemies.
