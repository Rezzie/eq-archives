These are just some thoughts on gameplay issues:

Hate list:
Monks go up quickly on the hate list because of the damage that they do. This is reasonable. Don't you get mad when someone hurts you a lot? How does this relate to warriors though? Warriors increase hate by 2 major methods, damage and the taunt skill. The taunt method deals no damage. In my view, the fair and correct way to balance this is to choose the hate generated by taunts to be slightly more than the difference desired between an average monk's damage output and an average warrior's damage output. Trying to tweak the maximum damage output of a class is silly, due to the wide range of weapons available. Part of the *skill* of playing a monk or a rogue is knowing how much damage you can dish out before whatever you're fighting turns on you. It only makes sense that an opponent would attack the person who is dealing the most damage. Say, for example, that the desired monk damage output should be 25% more than that of a warrior. With the warrior's taunt then set to increase hate at a rate of 25% of his damage output, the total hate generated for that warrior would be 125% times his damage. So if the warrior deals 400 dmg/min, the hate towards him increases as if he were dealing 500 dmg/min. The monk can then deal up to 500 dmg/min without causing the opponent to attack him. In this way, the monk outdamages the warrior, but is kept from dealing an unbalanced amount of damage. Monks, along with every other class, DO have a way of controlling hate: their damage. That is part of the skill and fun of playing a fighter class. Charging into combat and doing as much damage as you possibly can is a sure way to get yourself killed, and that's as it should be. The game becomes boring and pointless if all you do in combat is autoattack and push all your special attack buttons as fast as you can. The same thing applies to rogues, except that their evade ability should reduce hate according to their desired damage output, so that they can deal 600 dmg/min while only increasing hate at a rate of 500 dmg/min.

Monk's role as damage dealers:
See above.

Resistances:
As monks hone their bodies and minds with discipline and training, it only seems right that they should receive some natural resistance bonuses. As it is, with their small number of HP's and lack of equipment with resistance bonuses, they are super-susceptible to any casters. This is one aspect that I believe was overlooked in the whole HP vs AC+dodge/block scheme. In an effort to improve the depth of the game (see below), perhaps resistance bonuses based on wisdom could be introduced. This would represent techniques of dealing with the elements through knowledge and experience.

Multi-dimensional gameplay:
My most prominent concern is increasing the dimension of the game. For monks, I believe this means balancing *within the class* the effects of the different character statistics. Check just about any character creation guide and it says to do one thing: put all your points into strength. This is about as boring as it gets. Most monks I know would gladly give up their +7 AGI/+7 DEX choker for a +7 STR +7 INT necklace. There's also stamina. Why are the benefits of STA, DEX, and AGI so meager compared to the benefits of STR? Shouldn't an agile, dexterous monk be able to whoop a strong, clumsy monk anyday? Shouldn't a sturdily-built monk be able to take the extra damage dealt by a strong monk? What's the use of the character creation screen if all you do is put all your points into STR for a fighter, INT for a caster, or WIS for a priest? That just makes for a one-dimensional game. I would like to see more balance between the character stats themselves.

Thanks for the time spent listening and the place provided to voice these opinions.

So what is the vision for monks, i and many monks have posted for months asking this to be clarified so that at least we can be balanced in light of the vision.

If monks are suposed to be warriors who do grossly equivilant damage yet give up heavy armor and hitpoints and taunt for the ability to feign death, then this is not very balanced. In order to balance this, monks by chosing not to wear armor should have skill advantages to defense, which currently they do not. They should have the ability to avoid damage versus absorb damage, we should have parry added to our list of skills since we have to use weapons to compete with warriors on the basis of damage. Also since we pay so heavily for feign death in hp and in armor, it should at least work correctly. Monks should also get the taunt skill if they are suposed to tank.

If monks are suposed to be more damaging than warriors and less damaging than rogues, and the lack of hp and heavy armor to compensate for this factor, as well as grossly equal defensive skills, then we need to in fact do more damage than a warrior, we need our damage tables fixed so that even using the same weapons we outdamage a warrior by a significant degree. If we are outdamaging warriors, then we need some way to lose the mob's attention once it focus's on us (ie fix feign death and perhaps some sort of psudo evade (fd takes us out of combat, this isn't really effective way to moderate damage) or else we need a boost to defensive skills since we will invariably be the focus of the mob's attention.

If monks are suposed to be dungeon pullers (rangers always will be better outdoors), and give up hit points and heavy armor to be able to pull better than a warrior, then we need to have skills that justify this, feign death should be accepted as a tool for this and fixed, we should also get a boost to running speed so that we can actually live long enough to get back to the group, we should be unstunnable from the back and maintain all of our defensive skills even when something is behind us. Too many times i have just plain ran out of health during the middle of a pull, where a warrior would have successfully gotten back to the group and gotten a heal.

Right now, when a monk pulls, he (or she) spends most of the battle laying on his back/side because feign death doesn't work to lose agro on mobs, when he stays with the group and someone else pulls, he does comparable damage to the warrior and doesn't come close to damaging a rogue, yet he lacks the health and defensive ability to allow him to take the roll of a tank. So the monk is 2nd or 3rd rate at whatever he is doing. Give monks a vision, make balance them according to that vision. Verant always falls back onto the vision as being so important, and yet the monk as a class has no vision, does that mean monks are unimportant?
