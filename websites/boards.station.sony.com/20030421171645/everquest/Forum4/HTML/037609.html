I'd recommend asking your question in the technical help board - you'll probably find better advice there. Also, if you suggest a price range that you can afford it might make it easier to make a suggestion.

With that said, any reasonably new video card should do for EQ. Go for the GeForce 4 Ti 4200 if you want decent power for a reasonable price. Avoid the Geforce 4 MX based cards - they are actually closer to the Geforce 2 chipset and only really suppose DirectX 7 functions.

If you can afford it, the ATI Radeon 9700 is pretty much the fastest thing out there. I'd avoid the 9000 though since it's pretty stripped down. The 9000 pro, or 9500 should do the trick nicely though. I'm running on a Radeon 8500, with an athlon XP 1800+ and only ever have to turn down graphics options in the bazaar or in AoE raids.

I've got a Visiontek Geforce4 Ti4600 with video-in/capture and TV-out. All the features work okay and it plays EQ fairly well.

On my second PC, I've got a Radeon 9700 Pro (not the AIW) which has outputs for dual monitors and TV-OUT, but no capture. It plays EQ and all my 3D games *far* superior to my Ti4600. It's just a better all-around 3D card with better drivers, nicer color and extremely improved performance.

The AIW version of the 9700 pro adds a TV-tuner for watching TV in a window as well as the Video-In capture features you are looking for. Unfortunately, it is a very pricey card so you will have to make your decision from there. The Ti4600's video capture has been flawless and on par with older ATI capture cards with video in, so you will only be sacrificing some 3D game speed for the cheaper Ti4600.
