I want to preface this by saying that i am a lvl 45 bard on Tallon zek, so aquiring a great majority of these armor pieces is a long way off, if not entirely out of my reach.
What bothers me about these effects is not so much that they are not as effective or useful as i would have hoped, plenty of other class's kunark armor has effects that are not as great or useful as they would have liked.

What bothers me about the spell effects put on SS is that it shows a total lack of knowledge about the functionality of the bard class. Looking at the effects, there is nothing else that we bards can feel but insulted.

There is a difference between having spell effects that are underpowered("i have to do WHAT to be able to summon food??"), and having spell effects that demonstrate blatent disreguard and ignorance of an entire class.

First the effects:

boots: summon water
greaves: summon food
bracer: shield of flame(~8 point damage shield)
gloves: enduring breath(water breathing)
vambraces: invigor(stamina regen)
breastplate: invigorate(100 point heal)
helm: eye of zomm(stop laughing)

There are two facts to keep in mind here:

Fact 1: Bards sing constantly. This is because they must do so in order for their songs to continue to have effect. If you see a bard not singing, they are a)meming a song, or b)linkdead.

Fact 2: In order to use the effects on Singing Steel armor, a bard must stop singing.

With these two facts in mind, let's evaluate the spell effects.

gloves: Not a bad piece of armor. Nevermind the fact that i can only think of twice that i have ever had EB casted on me(both KK raids), and that we have a song that already does this. This is one of two armor pieces that gets a thumbs up, because it gives something that a bard might want to use sometime.

bracer: The other non-useless piece of armor. Being able to flame shield everyone in the group if there's not a mage handy is a good thing. No doubt our best spell effect. Of course, when the best kunark spell effect you have is the 8 point damage shield on your bracer, well......the problem should be evident.

Enough props, on to the bashing:

boots and greaves: Summoning food and water is nice, but giving them as spell effects on the uberarmor of a class that gets forage is just insulting.

eye of zomm: This would be an insult to just about any class you gave it to. Giving it to a class that can bind sight, as well as invis, sneak, and track is just ludicrous.

These two pieces are insults to be sure, but i can see how someone MIGHT be able to make a case for them...doubt it though. The final two pieces are the worst, and best demonstrate the ignorance of the bard class.

vambraces: sure, stamina regen is nice, but consider: Since level THREE, we've had a song that does a BETTER job of this, with LESS casting time, to the WHOLE group. The argument that this saves us from memming that song is invalid as well. I can mem and sing jig faster than the item's casting time, as well as the fact that i can always find a slot for jig if the tanks are needing it.

breastplate: A 100 point heal sounds useful, until you consider the casting time. Even if we suspend belief enough so that we could concieve of a bard obtaining this item at the minimum lvl of 45 to use it, we see that she will have no use for it. Twisting hymn of restoration and psalm of shielding at 45 with a lowly mandolin, a bard will be able to heal every member of her party faster than she could heal even 1 person with the breastplate. Not to mention the little bit of mana she will be able to throw in as well. At lvl 47, with niv's/hymn/psalm, and at 55(a more reasonable level for obtaining this item) with cantana of replenishment, the breastplate gets completely blown away.

Is Verant THAT unfamiliar with the abilities and song lineup of bards that it thought these effects would be useful?

Or did they want to insult an already much-maligned class?

Or is there a change in the works for SS armor?
