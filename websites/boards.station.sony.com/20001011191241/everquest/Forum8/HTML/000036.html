Fletching leaves a lot to be desired. Nobody buys arrows -- you need a minimal skill to make the most effective arrows (1 damage, 150 range) and nobody is interested in anything else.

(Yes, you can make 8 damage, 150 range arrows, but on a darkwood bow this is a damage increase of about 15% and a cost increase of literally 10,000% per arrow.)

If there is a way to imbue magic into arrows, that opens a lot of possibilities. e.g., is it possible to make a c-ockatrice beak work like an arrowhead, and when you combine it with the other materials you get an arrow bundle with effect:stun?

If that works, we can come up with endless possibilities. Arrows that proc snare, or a tentacle terror style knockback effect, etc. Short of making them proc ice comet, I don't think there's anything you can do to unbalance archery, because the damage is really pathetic, so there should be plenty of room to add some effect arrows without adding significant power to an archer. (And, for example, those beaks are sufficiently rare drops that you wouldn't be able to flood the market with stun arrows.)

I also think it would be interesting if there could be some items in the game that required a minimum stat to create -- e.g., it costs a troll a LOT more money to raise fletching than, say, a high elf. It would be interesting, though, if there were some special items that could only be made by someone with a 180 strength -- or a 180 int -- etc. Perhaps the strongest bows could only be crafted by someone with a 200 fletching and a 250 strength.

Fletching exhibits an imbalance between risk (cost) and reward (payoff). One way to fix this would be to make Archery a more powerful combat skill (i.e., increase the payoff), but Verant seems not to want to do this for Vision(tm) reasons.

So, we are left with either lowering the risk by reducing the cost or tinkering with the results.

Lower cost: make all bow/arrow components forageable, make tools never be expended, lower merchant prices for components (or raise what they'll pay). If all components aren't forageable, then make them available via a quest of some sort like the components of the Trueshot Bow.

Tinkering: make bows/arrows more interesting and complex. Different additional components for different resulting arrows and bows. I wonder if a successful hit with a special arrow could have an interesting effect on the archer or the bow itself, not just the mob? (Like those intelligent swords in D&D) You could get berserker effect, or haste (lowers delays for a few rounds), or bufs up dex or agi.

One problem with this is that you might end up with little stacks of different effect arrows to sort through in a fight. Not good.

Make quivers do something interesting. I like the idea of decreasing delay.

Add quests where you turn in a cool bow and get something interesting (since players don't want to buy them, alas). Perhaps there are elven archers who want nicely made bows and will give a reward for them, even if it doesn't involve killing a_drooling_monstrosity00?

Critical: You probably have to coordinate with Rowyl on this one, but make it possible to buy a stack of 20 items with one or two mouse-clicks! (How about Ctrl-Click on a merchant item brings up the "how many?" box with 20 preselected?)

Consider raising the skill-caps on trade skills to 250. Then there would be room for more interesting high-skill items to make. (Olympic 20-cam fiberglass bows, Chateau d'Yquem for brewers, etc.)

Consider giving experience for reaching various preset levels of skill in any trade skill, or even for any increase. (When I think of all the time and money I've put into trade skills vs. reward, and the same for fighting MOBs vs. reward, I'm sometimes amazed that I bothered).

The way magical arrows were implemented was a bit of a cop out. All the exciting possibilities for arrow combinations with spells thrown out for what? A silver tipped arrowhead without even a greater chance 'to hit' than the standard arrow.

There were great possibilities of class interactions here - the ideas of different caster classes being able to enchant different types of magic arrow... and from Verant's perspective the happy prospect of yet another platinum sink to soak up those high level bank accounts... I'm surprised Verant didn't jump on the chance.

We know Verant don't want to make archery too powerful and I understand that it could cause an imbalance in the game... it would be bad for the game as a whole to get into the Ultima Online situation... but the arguments that it is a form of 'manaless spell casting' do not really hold up very well.

In order to fletch arrows I have to sink both time and money into the skill - time that I could have spent adventuring - the time spent fletching is similar to meditating for a spell and paying for the arrow components is no different to paying for spell components. I fail to make arrows and casters 'fizzle' spells. I can't shoot arrows at point blank range and casters get interrupted if they get hit. There is a significant delay (casting time) associated with each bow shot. Spells are often partially resisted and arrows often do less than maximum (or even 'average') damage.

Here's another suggestion for a minor improvement to archery for rangers and to one to improve arrows for all.

The Trueshot bow can be made by anyone with a decent skill in fletching... it can only be wielded by a ranger though. What I have in mind is that a ranger who makes his own Trueshot should gain a 'to hit' bonus with that bow because he made it for himself therefore it is tuned to his style of shooting (stance, draw weight etc).

This bonus is not transferable to another ranger (i.e. if the bow is sold or even made for another ranger then the bonus is lost) - this means to get the full benefit of a Trueshot then the ranger must make his own bow. I'm not suggesting a massive ATK bonus, but something that makes a Trueshot (or a Rain Caller constructed from a Trueshot) slightly more effective.

Another bonus that might be applied to these bows, or maybe to all bows across the board, is that the *minimum* damage inflicted is that of the arrow. I'm so sick of seeing my 8 damage arrows, fired from a dam 20 bow (with an archery skill of 201 and a dex of 124) hitting for 1 point of damage. This would give some (limited) encouragement to use the higher damage arrows. To see a 'double damage' hit do a mere 2 points of damage is almost insulting.

The following idea applies to all the classes able to fire an arrow from a bow and / or fletch a magic arrow.

To enhance arrows all casters should be able to enchant arrows. This will prevent the casters griping that one class has been given an extra spell - maybe give them each one or 2 spells that enchant arrows with a spell say 2 circles lower than their current level of power.

Rather than say 'casters can enchant arrows' why not give each caster class a different 'enchant arrow' spell... for example:

Enchanters maybe get a straight + accuracy / damage effect that is stackable with any of the following effects:

Necromancers get a poison arrow effect (a mild DoT)... maybe limited to rogue only when firing the arrows.

Mage gets a fire arrow spell (DoT again) or alternatively a lightning effect (DD).

Wizards get a DD explosive arrow effect. - Possibly a 'fire based' one and an 'ice based' one for flexibility.

Clerics get an effect that enhances arrows against undead.... maybe paladin only?

Druids get an ensnaring root arrow.... ranger only?

Shamans.. maybe a spell that reduces the delay (wolf spirit arrows )

Drawbacks
=========
1) The arrows must be silver tipped (therefore expensive).

2) Can only enchant stacks of 10 at a time and quite expensive on mana.

3) A 'fizzle' while trying to cast the enchantment ruins the arrows.

4) Although this effect should benefit all classes it should be set up like a weapon proc - an archer of too low a level for a given spell will not get the effect - this will reduce the farming of 1 shot kill arrows to low level players.

5) Also each spell requires a gem component of some small value.... but you require one per arrow. For example, to enchant a basic 10 arrow stack you need 10 silver tipped arrows, 10 topaz to enchant them to improve accuracy, then to get the lightning effect spell you need 10 pieces of amber.

6) And of course the aggro effect of the equivalent spell is transferred directly to the archer.... nothing new there.

7) There is still a chance that the arrow will miss its target and the spell could still be resisted. I would suggest that it shouldn't be able to 'fizzle' again though.

I don't think that everyone is going to put down their druids, necromancers etc and start playing rangers and warriors - for one thing we will need these casters to make our arrows - but it will help to make archery a more viable skill although whilst keeping it as an expensive luxury.
