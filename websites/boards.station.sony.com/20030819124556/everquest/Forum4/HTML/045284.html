Hmmm... Freeport is probably the WORST place for spire access. Your best bet would be to find a wiz or druid who could port you to Nexus.

But the closest spires are likely in Greater Faydark. You need to cross Ocean of Tears to Butcherblock Mountains, and then cross those and enter Greater Faydark. About halfway along south border of the zone, you'll come to the spires.

There are also spires in North Karana, but for those you'd have to cross both Commons, Kithicor, HighPass, and East Karana.

Once you are in the bazaar, first MOVE OUT OF THE DOORWAY.

If you are like most of us, you will have to turn down your clip plane. I use 5 percent, others use zero. Using third person view (hitting the f9 key) also can help.

Once you are out of the way and can move somewhat easily, you can bring up the /bazaar window. Using this you can put in various qualifiers and search for items. If there is a specific item you want and you know the name, you can type it in the box on the top. Otherwise, if you are just looking, is a good idea to do a slot by slot search using all the qualifiers you can so... you want sleeves with magic resistance for a wood elf ranger for between 0 and 200 pp. The various items that fit that will come up.

If you see something you want (and you can click on the item name to see the stats) then you can click on the name of the seller and be turned in that direction. Move a little, click again on the name, move into that direction, click again and so forth. Many people will put a location description in their AFK message, for example I am usually "at the end of the row behind the shadowhaven bank" but for your first time that won't help you much. When you find your seller, you can treat them just like an npc. Just run up and click on them, buy your goodies and scoot.

Oh yes, there are two banks in the bazaar. While you are in the bazaar you can move even though you would otherwise be seriously encumbered. Remember to check your weight before you leave so you aren't stuck on the other side of the zone.
