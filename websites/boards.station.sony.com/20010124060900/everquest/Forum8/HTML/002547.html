As one of the few Dark Elves ever to take up the Smithing hammer, and as one of the TRULY rare Dark Elven Warriors ever to choose the profession, I officially inform you, Yakatizma, that you have suffered the final indignity upon the smiths of my race.

When I learned that only followers of Innoruk could ever hope to see magically enhanced Tier'Dal racial armor, I was saddened (seeing my fellow followers of Rallos Zek left behind), but I grudgingly accepted that Rallos would ignore his stepchildren.

When I learned that the Plate Mail we forged could POSSIBLY be Enchanted (and perhaps even be Imbued, maybe, MAYBE even for followers of Rallos), but that the Gods had not put the final piece of the puzzle into the chain (enchanted chain jointing now works, but making of folded sheets fails), I had still held out some infintesmally small hope that someday we might wear our armor with pride.

As of today, the final speck of those hopes has been dashed.

The rarest and most key ingredient to the art of enchanting Tier'Dal Platemail, the Blue Diamond, long expensive yet obtainable (with the vendors of Norrath offering only 250 for them, players would, on occasion, grant us poor smiths their excess diamonds for a mere 250-300), has now been added to the long-standing trade of Jewelcraft.

Not only added, but it can be made into what is arguably some of the absolutely best, and most versatile gear, in existence in Norrath. I logged in this day to discover that my Guild (long the only possible source for Blue Diamonds for the craft) was now hoarding them to make resistance jewelry for Guild Raids.

This final insult is intolerable. You have made it clear to the few Dark Elven master-smiths that we were never meant for the forge, and that our attempts are futile at best. Never again shall I take the hammer to the mystical metal of Adamantite. Were it not for the fact that the Adamantite I now wear will not burn in the cultural forge, I would cast it into the fires forever...ashamed to even admit I was stupid enough to go down that road in the first place.

Perhaps, if you someday come to your senses and give Ornate Chainmail a unique look of its own, then I will take the hammer once again. If Velium Platemail becomes available and is even remotely worth its price, that also may lure me. If you have removed or changed the spell component for Enchant Adamantite so that Blue Diamond is no longer its key, then I will pull the smithy hammer from the mantle.

Until then, consider that you have failed me Yakatizma, and all the Tier'Dal, with this latest change...

Where's the balance?

Jewellery: a skill you can raise without moving from a single room, if you have enough plat, all the way up to 250. Or you can do it wherever you are, and make a profit on many many items.
Then, with a single blue diamond, and a single bar of metal, you can make an item which gives five resistances and a substantial amount of mana, which is ALL/ALL and will be in high demand. Oh, and only one chance of failure per item too.

Smithing: A skill which you can raise to 175 with ingredients purchased in several different zones, which you can raise above that by travelling around the world hunting either sub-10 or 45+ monsters, with a chance at making magical items restricted severely by race, class, and often deity, with stats below the level of armor dropped from the same level monsters you are getting your components from. Then, if you are really good and really lucky, you can combine your gems (up to 10 blue diamonds or jacinths) and your metal, and your tailored silk swatches, and your brewed tempers (made from rare drop ingredients) into some enchanted armor, at which you have chances of failure at these points: brewing temper, making block, making ring, making armor.

Somehow, I don't think there is a risk/reward balance between the two.

Field Plate is an extremely versatile armor product, in that you can create Imbued-Only versions for a wide variety of deities, and these are usable by most any of the "Good" races that worship those deities. Verant has once again created a marketplace where there is only ONE tradeskill that is viable for the pursuit of platinum.... JewelCraft

Jewelcraft had already attained its perch upon the top of the Pantheon of Mt. Olympus. For some reason, unknown to myself, Verant has decided to raise their perch yet another notch while at the same time managing to crush any hope that blacksmiths might have had.

Lets take a moment to consider a few comparisons between Jewelcraft and Blacksmithing:

Jewelcraft:
1. Jewelcraft is primarily DOMINATED by one class - Enchanters. Yes, there are others but really...90% of all jewelers are Enchanters

2. Prior to the introduction of Blue Diamonds... 90 outta 105 jewelcrafting pieces could be made by sitting right next to a merchant. The only exceptions were Jacinth, Black Sapphire, and Diamond jewelry (5 items each with Velium items included). This makes Jewelcrafting the EASIEST, ingredient wise, skill to pursue.

3. Jewelcraft has the HIGHEST cost-to-resale value of any trade. Even items that are trivial to jewelry crafters when their skill is under 80 can be resold for a profit, or worst case for the cost of the ingredients.

4. Every item made with Jewelcraft has significantly greater resale potential than any item set that can be made by ANY and ALL other tradeskills.. go ahead... try to sell a file that you had to make as a blacksmith, or a muffin by a baker for that matter.

5. The Jewelcraft skill can market ALL of their items to EVERY person in the game. Every other skill has items that are only needed by a rare and select few.

6. Regardless of what some may say, a person could reach a skill of 150 in jewelcraft with 400pp or less. I have an enchanter friend whose skill is 153, and thru his own meticulously kept records, he has spent 417pp that he has not yet been able to recover costs for.


Blacksmithing:

1. Blacksmithing is a skill with NO dominant class. From Bards to Wizzards, everybody can be a blacksmith.

2. Blacksmithing requires the use of a stationary forge. And there are NO merchants located exactly next to a forge that stock ANY or ALL of the items required for blacksmithing. Therefore, EVERY person who engages in blacksmithing MUST travel in order to pursue their skill... whether it be a couple stores down, or 13 zones away.

3. Tens, if not hundreds, of items required for blacksmithing are rare or uncommon mob drops. Compared to 3 total items for Jewelcrafting.

4. Blacksmithing has one of the LOWEST cost-to-resale values AND every item created with a trivial of 80 or less either only has 1 specific use to a person in another trade skill or has to be resold to a merchant for a heavy loss.

5. Until a blacksmith can create banded mail, which trivials at 102 and up, every item he/she can create has an extremely crippled resale value or in the best case, has a singular unique (but NOT in demand) potential market.

6. Even items at the Pinnacle of blacksmithing have a limited market to classes who can wear the goods.

7. With unique use items and a Verant crippled, not to mention limited, blacksmithing market.. the costs to obtain a proficiency in blacksmithing are much more formidible than those of the jewelcrafting skill.

8. Considering the enormous amount of time, exhorbanent ingredient costs, rarity of components, and monatory drain blacksmithing entails it is simply amazing that so many blacksmiths have decided to leave their chosen love.

Yet considering all of this... Verant has decided to take the "Holy Grail" of sought after High Elf, Dark Elf, and Ogre ingredients... the extremely rare Blue Diamond... and give it a use to the Jewelcrafters????

Like they didn't already have enough profit potential. Verant decided to throw the last bucket of water on the ever-so-slightly smoldering embers of a viable blacksmithing trade.

What were you thinking Verant? With all the well thought out updates and patches recently that were not only appreciated, but value added to the EQ community you have taken another step backwards in the balancing of the tradeskills.

Unfortunately I realize that this message will probably never be read or responded to by the staff of Verant... I feel what you have done to cultural blacksmithing is a grave injustice to the game, and I needed to express my opinions.

Fare thee well fellow smiths! For I now hang my hammer among the other honored retired smiths.

Several options, in fact, before many smiths walk away in disgust.

1. Reduce the number of blue diamonds needed to make enchanted plate. 10 Blue Diamonds per attempt at a breastplate or leggings is a lot, especially with four separate possible points of failure compared to Jewellery's single point of failure.

2. Review the stats on Enchanted Adamantite and Mithril Plate, as well as (enchanted) Imbued Adamantite Plate. 3. Allow Enchant Mithril and Enchant Adamantite to enchant (purchasable?) BLOCKS of ore instead of (or as an alternative to) LARGE BRICKS of ore with a single Blue Diamond as is currently the case.

4. Replace Blue Diamonds as a component for Enchant Mithril and Adamantite spells.
