I guess what casters want is:

1. A caster haste-like spell to reduce casting times (Absor already said this was out of the question).

2. Better items that actually give us better performance in battle and not 1 more point of INT or 10 more mana.

3. An increase of Mana thoughout the levels, moreso after level 50 than before. In otherwords, we need more mana per level. Something like 15-20% more.

4. A decrease in casting time per level on spells. Spells increase in damage per level now, but there is no reduction in mana usage or casting time. It would make sense for spells to either decrease the casting time or use less mana once a caster has mastered the spell.

5. Fizzle Rates. The fizzle rates after 50 are insane. Last night I was goofing around in Thurgadin with Abscond. I am a level 52 wizard and it's a level 52 spell. Granted, I had not "mastered" casting this spell yet, but I fizzled 8 times in a row, sucking all of my mana right out of me. So I harvested and tried again. Fizzled 5 times and finally just gave up. This is a problem. Fizzles should happen no more that 20% on a new spell. Hell, I fizzle alot on gate and thats a level 4 spell.

6. Focus items. Mages got some in the earlier levels, but there are NO UPGRADES, and there is nothing similar for other casters at all.

7. A lure type spell for each class. This makes alot of sense. If you want to balance classes, there should be no reason to choose a wizard for their lures over a Necro for their lifetaps.

8. More Group spells. The most useful spells most classes have are not Group casting. If you want to make casters more group friendly, give them spells that will lower downtime and are group friendly.

9. Caster disciplines? I've seen lots of players wondering why melee and Hybrids got these and casters didn't. Every time VI adds a new ability to a caster, it is almost always a spell. This makes no sense. Why don't casters get abilites? Oh, we get dodge, but we get nothing that says "Hey, this ability was designed with a caster in mind". It would make sense to let they Hybrids share some of these caster abilities eventually as well.

10. Another specialization after the first. I don't know how many times I wished that my evacs and gates were as mana efficent as my nukes. Perhaps letting us specialize in our secondary form of magic would help a bit.

Well, thats about it so far. I have a few other ideas for each class, but I won't bore you with things that wouldn't be looked at by a blue.

Well, we all saw this coming didn't we? I am actually quite surprised given Verant's actually quite good math background so far. By allowing percentage based advancements to one side, the other, that grows linearly, is now suffering.

I said a while back (on the topic of caster two-handers actually) that we would need to see items that actually increased caster effectiveness. Guess what? Even if the int/wis cap was dropped it would mean almost nothing. Mana pool is not the trouble. Actually, mana regen is a key issue in an experience grouping, but even that is not the real issue.

As melee ramps up in terms of actual power, both output and absorption, the encounters must be made harder to compensate. So far, casters have received *nothing* to offset this. Ok, perhaps nothing is strong. Some of the high-end caster gear does have free casting effects but these are few and far between.

Another way to look at this is through the economy. A level 30 melee these days can be reliably expected to have gear that used to be reserved for the 40+ crowd. This is trickle down and it makes them far more effective. Level 30 casters may have old level 40+ caster gear now too but it doesn't have the same effect. A 30 Wizard with 190 int is not that much better than a level 30 Wizard with 150 int. It is a bigger pool but unless you are OOMing and remedding each fight, it is not useful. The 30th Warrior with a Wurmslayer and Yak combo *is* doing a heck of a lot more damage than the fellow with a BIBS or something. Perhaps that is just twinks but I do see much better equipment at the lower levels over all these days.

Hmm, I ramble and shouldn't. The point is this: I am very happy to see the tanks contributing damage. I always thought they should. I think they are now balanced in respect to pets at least. I do think that upper 50s offensive casters (even Wizards, though less so) are getting a raw deal. Their damage output has fallen sharply in relation to MOB power. The resistance issues only compound this to inane proportions. I do not support inflation of powers in general but feel that casters need either skills or equipment (or spells that are obtainable) to offset the present situation.

I would like to introduce a very simple concept to those of you who are whining that the game was never balanced to begin with, and how now shifted to the other side. Specifically, melee's were outclassed pre-kunark, and now casters are out classed.
In an effort to explain this succintly I will use a big word that I know many of you 7th graders will not understand. That is "inversly proportionate" This means if one side of something is low, you make the other side high. In this way creating a balance amongst the classes.
Damage vs Ability to take damage is an inversly proportionate relationship. Pre-Kunark and SoV melees were upset that casters could out damage them. Casters have fewer HP (take less damage) so could deal more damage than the melee. Melee's have more HP than casters and could deal less damage. Balance. Melee may not like, it but I could care less. Very simple theory.
Now, take the warrior with haste items, and 4k hp who is now doing 4x the damage of a caster. ON ANY MOBS!!! This is a directly proportionate relationship. In boo-boo bear speak (lol I loved that btw... soo funny) if one side of something is high, then so is the other side. Whereas the caster, still having few hp, is regaled to doing as much, if not a little less damage (in hopes of conserving mana and participating in all kills, and not mana burning himself into med time).
This is where the class balance was lost. This is not rocket science people. Completely independent of the fact that some mobs are MR the imbalance exists. This is basic fundamental math that can be taught to all boo boo bears. The MR of mobs only further skews the equation.
And the relationship between mobs to players, should be directly proportionate. Which it is. But instead of it being a mob at lvl 45 is 1.2 times stronger than a player of the same level, we now how have mobs that are hundreds of times more powerful than players of equivalent level. Directly proportionate, but not 1 to 1.2 instead 1 to 100+. Rediculous. Melees were scaled to this much better than were casters. And the way to overcome is to simply attack en mass. In melee mass. Let the casters buff, and help with the CR.
A wonderful thread. I am glad to see it brought up and hope that it is repeated until the powers that be have completed their basic junior high math studies. Verant, proportionate increases. Two words, please wrap your minds around this simple simple theory. If a melee is now able to do 4x more damage than the casters, than you need to make casters do 5 or 6x more damage. Or give us equivalent HP and damage. Which isn't at all what any of us want. So your choice is clear. To almost any 7th grader.
