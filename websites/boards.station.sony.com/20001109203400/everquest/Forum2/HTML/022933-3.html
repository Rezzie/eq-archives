Kynara, I'm sorry but you don't fully understand the formulas that the melee system uses. The damage bonus only applies to the main hand, and is solely based on level. (the nerf 2 patches ago only applied to extremely low *delay* weapons, and has nothing to do with the damage rating of those weapons).

The to-hit odds are player level and skill level based, so the percentage of hits remains constant no matter the speed of the weapon. So the level based damage bonus helps fast weapons more than slow weapons. So yes, a fast weapon with a worse ratio can *easily* outdamage a slow weapon in the hands of a high level player (why in heck do you think the twig was considered so uber? it's ratio was pretty attrocious).

Yes a fast weapon will miss more times in a given fight, it will also hit more times. And each time it hits the level based damage bonus will be added.

The wurmslayer is main hand only, meaning the wielder of this weapon will get far less effect from the main hand damage bonus than he would by wielding a fast weapon in his main hand. I would be in complete agreement with the sentiment that this weapon was 'too good' if it could be equipped in the off hand slot, where the damage bonus isn't applied and all that matters is the ratio.

Pure ratio can't be used to determine weapon strength, but neither can delay + level bonus.

Here are some numbers to help people see straight (from copeland's weapon analyzer v1.81):

For a level 51 warrior with 143 strength and a common haste item (FBSS), assuming a 60% hit rate (which is accurate, based on my own log parsing), wurmslayer can be expected to do 676 damage per minute in an average minute of fighting.

A short sword of ykesha would do 540, so wurmslayer outdamages it by 25%. That, to anyone who understands these things, is a major upgrade.

Clearly there are better weapons, such as a lamentation, which could be expected to do 688 damage per minute. Note however, that the average dmg/min for a lamentation is only 12 more than that of wurmslayer's, so for our level 51 warrior with 143 str, she could reasonably expect a wurmslayer and a lamentation to perform virtually identically.

Those numbers don't, however, take into account ripostes. Since riposting does not care what weapon you are holding or how fast it is, we can reasonably expect to do twice as much damage (or more) by riposting with wurmslayer than with lamentation. However, ripostes don't happen often enough to really make an appreciable difference in overall numbers.

Now let's increase our warrior's level to 60 and give her 200 strength and a cloak of flames and see how the numbers work out:

Wurmslayer: 907

Lamenation: 975

Lamentation has increased its lead to 7%, but a level 60 warrior ought to have even better weapons, such as a blade of the black dragon eye, which clocks in at a whopping 1167 dmg/min.

But what about the level 35 warrior with wurmslayer? What kind of super advantage is he getting? Let's say a level 35 warrior with wurmslayer, 130 strength, and no haste item:

Wurmslayer: 448

Ykesha: 281 (proc not included, since yaks don't proc at 35)

Now HERE'S who gets the real benefit of wurmslayer. When I was level 35 I didn't even have ykeshas due to them still being uber items way back then, but I'd say they are far more common today. But upgrading to wurmslayer at level 35 from a ykesha yields nearly 60% more damage.

Damage over time is really the only way to fairly compare weapons. It seems pretty clear to me that from 50-60 a lamentation really isn't that big of an improvement from a wurmslayer, and if, by chance, you happen to have one of each, you'd be best served by equipping wurmslayer in the primary hand and lamentation in the offhand.

However, lower level (specifically pre-level bonus) people really benefit from wurmslayer's terrific ratio. Draw your own conclusions as to why people want it for their lower level guildmates/twinks/friends.
