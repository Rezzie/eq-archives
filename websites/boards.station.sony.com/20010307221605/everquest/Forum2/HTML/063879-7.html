My name is Morniel, a level 60 cleric on the Tunare server. I have a PoG bp, have had one since they first started dropping (when they had a 6 second cast time).

When I heard last night that my bp is to be the next target of the nerf stick (for the second time), I was pretty upset. It's my favorite item... and this nerf hurts. A one minute casting time basically removes the use of the bp from combat.

Over the past 24 hours, I've thought about it. I've paid attention to when I use it, thinking about whether/how that use will need to change/end. I've thought about why some of those uses aren't in Verant's 'vision' and what use they intended originally. Here are my thoughts.

First - how do I use it? In a bunch of ways.

~ For mana regen when I am in the old world. I can 'crunch' mana by using a mana stone and then using the bp on myself. I can probably go from oom to fm in about 3-4 minutes using this method.

While this is great for recovering from buffs (a harmless use I think), I can also use it during combat to regen mana. I no longer hunt in Solb, but when I was level 57 I spent a fair amount of time duo'ing the Efreeti Lord with a 57 monk. The bp was a key factor in this ability, partly because of my ability to regen mana with it. I suspect that this is part of what Verant wants to stop.


~ Chain casting it on a tank during combat. With that same monk, I also hunted dwarves in Icewell. We were able to duo them quite efficiently - including Royals and Watchers, which the monk was able to pull singly. Granted, I had to supplement heal with real mana - bp ch bp bp ch - but it was a major factor in our ability to kill them as a duo. Adding in a bard and a warrior, the four of us were able to kill the Chancellor - a mob that I really don't think Verant intended to be killed by less than a full group of players.


~ Supplemental healing in a group. Use of the bp in a full group is a little limited. I can use it as long as I don't have to worry about my enchanter getting pounded, for example. I don't use it in pressed combat unless I am forced to, too risky... casting a bp when your group is in combat in Siren's Grotto, for example, is just asking for one of your casters to get pounded and die while you are waiting for the cast time to finish.


~ CR clean up. Any time I res someone, I heal them up a cast from the bp - as long as they are of a level to get it. It's also an incredible aid to putting a wiped out raid back on their feet. Clerics with epics res people back in, bp clerics can heal the newly res'd. I loved this, it was a great elimination of downtime. This use won't really be taken away, although getting people back up in a rush situation (ie before a room spawns) will be a little more difficult.


The increase in casting time won't affect some of these uses very much. I'll still be able to crunch mana in the 'old world', just not as fast and not in a combat situation. I'll still be able to cast it on someone who was just res'd, it (again) just slows things down a little.

What I *won't* be able to do is chain cast it on a tank during combat (unless what we are fighting is dealing out less than 120 damage a tick, which is pretty much never at high levels). Nor will I be able to use it at all to do supplemental healing.

It looks to me like what Verant basically intended with this bp is that it only be cast during downtime (with the exception of casting on the puller right before a pull). I can respect that (as much as I don't like it). However, it occurs to me that I could spend that one minute cast time medding instead of using my bp, and gain back significantly enough more mana than I would need to cast Celestial Elixir... So I'm not really going to use it unless I am full mana to begin with. This means it's not restricted just to downtime, but also to times when I am not grouped/hunting (pretty much the only times I'm going to be full mana).

The bp will be nice for when I res someone on the fly, and potentially for crunching mana in the old world... but that's about it. /sigh. I'm going to miss the other uses a lot. I am somewhat resigned to it at this point, though - because I think that this decision has been made and just isn't subject to change, no matter how much I don't like it.

What I am really curious to know is what the replacement bp will be. I assume that the stats will be identical or at least comparable, but that the effect will be different. What I would really like to see as the effect on the new bp is a buff - ideally Aegolism, or an entirely new buff that is similar but gives fewer hp/ac. This would be an incredible mana and time saver, while not affecting combat at all - it will simply reduce downtime, which I think was the idea behind this bp to begin with. If you think that Aegolism is too much, then Aegis would be another great affect. I absolutely love my Donal's legs because they let me get a wiped out group/raid back up on their feet fast - getting a mana free Naltron on everyone can make a big difference.

Anyway, I don't want to ramble on too much. I'm not happy, but I guess I can see where Verant is coming from. I just hope that the replacement bp will have a useful effect.


~Morniel Everhate, Teir'dal healer of the 60th circle
Tunare Server
