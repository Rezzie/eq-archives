My role: I keep everyone regenerating mana and swinging swords quickly. I keep extra monsters sleeping so they don't demolish my friends. I explore the world as a friend to all people, masking myself as a denizen of each city. I craft lovely magical jewelry for friends and acquaintances.

Here are some changes I would very much like to see:


1. Give us something to do when faced with monsters that are immune or nearly immune to magic-based spells.

Give our high level mez, blur and charm spells a lure resist or change the resist to a different element. For the extremely high cost, I would like to see them stick better than the normal ones, at least in some situations.


2. Remove tedium in buffing.

Change the Gift of Magic/Insight/Brilliance to a group spell at the same cost and increase the mana regeneration component enough so that it is noticeable (+1/2/4 instead of +0/1/2).

Where possible, increase buff durations (with a proportional change in casting cost). In particular, make Boon of the Garou less tedious to cast.


3. Make illusions more fun and useful.

Make illusions last through linkdeath.

Give us group elemental illusions for water breathing and levitation. Illusions are a great way of granting controlled buffs, since a character can only be under the effects of one illusion at a time. (I don't think group racial illusions are balanced because of faction considerations.)


4. Fill out the enchantment aspect of the class.

Put in the rest of the mana vial spells and make some recipes using them.

Put a gem vendor in Freeport.


5. Remove the bottleneck on the enchanter epic quest.

Make that Shissar Spirit spawn more often than once a week!


6. Fix our single target stuns.

The Whirl line was effective. It was too effective and needed to be brought into balance. Instead it was gutted. We went from having single target stuns that were better than anything any other class could provide to basically having none at all. To interrupt spell casting when AE is not an option, I have to use my level 8 DD spell, Chaotic Feedback.

Perhaps the Whirl line could be modified to interrupt spellcasting for its duration but allow melee.

*******New Solutions********

Ok, I've read all 7 pages of this thread. I'm going to attempt to propose some new solutions to problems I've seen repeatedly mentioned.

---

Problem #1: Enchanters take as much if not more damage than Warriors in many fights. Why? Two reasons: Tashani line taunts better than anything else in the game and Enchanters lack AC.

Solution to #1: A spell that lowers magic resistance (Tashani) as well as reduces aggro (Mem Blur). Combine the two in one spell.

Solution to #1: Change Umbra to be a self only buff, and seriously increase it's AC. Say 100 AC self only. Following the Shadow theme, the Enchanter would be twisting the reality around him to appear as if not there, therefore increasing his AC by such dramatic numbers.

Problem #1a: Whoever designed the 55+ spells is high on something. The Tashani series is the biggest taunt in the game. I can easily pull any mob off any party member by casting Tashania on it. So why would I want an AE version of this (Wind of Tashani). So that every mob in an area would jump on me? I don't think so.

Solution #1a: Reduce taunt/aggro of Tashani line. (mentioned before)

Problem #2: The clickable spells on Enchanter items are useless. I've seen druids, shamans, and clerics all loot items which seriously improved their gaming experience. Example - Elder Spiritist Bracer - It casts snare. Snare of all things. But if you've played a druid, you know that one of the major things you add to a group is snaring mobs. Having this item, just freed up a spell slot, and Enchanters are in my honest opinion, hurting for spell slots as much if not worse than any other class.

Solution to #2: Give us a clickable item with Tashania, Mem Blur, Color Skew, Clarity 2, or Group Mana Resist on it. These are the spells we always have loaded. These are some of the spells we have to cast repeatedly in a group. Every other class seems to get items which make their gaming experience more enjoyable. We get Rune 4, and Invis. These are not spells I'm casting 20 times in a 4 hour dungeon romp.

Problem #3: A mob is mezzed, the enchanter knows that mez is going to break in 2 seconds, so he casts an 8 second AE stun(Color Skew). When mez breaks the Color Skew timer also expires. i.e. Color Skew only lasts 2 seconds instead of 8. Or realistically it does nothing, cause mez was still in effect for those 2 seconds.

Solution to #3: Make Enchanter AE stuns stack with mez. It was mentioned previously that this was probably a bug, yet it has not been fixed. Considering the long recast time on these AE stuns, it can be devestating to the Enchanter and the whole party if the timing is missed.

Solution to #3: Make single target mez overwrite AE mez. (Yes this was suggested before, but this needs to be addressed and it can be adddressed either way.)

Other than these suggestions, I recommend you go back to page 1 and read the post by Mezzmo. He summed up the rest of my feelings.

Not only that, but the duration is very short, about half that of Dazzle. There are three ways this can be improved. (one, two, or all three options enabled)
1) Lower mana cost
2) Lengthen duration
3) Make the line interchangeable. For instance, allow me to get the mob mezzed with GoK but then refresh with Dazzle (I know, probably not possible)

Thirdly, I think it's a shame that high level enchanters have no new illusions. Let me start off by asking: what is the major unique stat that enchanters are concerned with? Charisma. Looks are everything. Our class is all about manipulating/affecting others in many different ways. Every single aspect of our class is based on this, even our planar armor is named after a related idea (Insidious). We all understand that illusions are hardly a part of class balance, but this game is about role-playing too. Enchanters are "Illusionists" but recieve no new illusions when they get that title. Phantasmists aren't really masters of the "phantom". Illusions shouldn't stop at 44, there are too many other possibilities: Sarnak, Froglok, Goblin, Orc, Skorpikis, Ghosts, Drolvargs, etc, etc, etc. The Epic Enchanter Quest involves 5 masters. One is in a Sarnak illusion, another in a Skorpikis, and another is a Clockwork Guard! I think it would be so cool to be able to do a flip every time I cast (froglok).

Fourth, Rune. You have no idea how many times the raid leaders say "Enchanters chain cast rune on Main Tank" and after the first cast, I'm dead. Or I tash the mob, and I'm dead. Tash I guess will always be a big taunt, but Rune, like Shrade mentioned, shouldn't be that angering. After all, Enchanters have very limited abilities to affect big encounters DURING the battle except throwing in a few runes. It already costs an arm and a leg (mana and plat), at least it could be a bit more useful.

Lastly, I always wondered why lowering a mob’s stats is totally useless. Cripple seems to be pointless in all but PvP situations. Our DoT’s lower stats too, but are only useful for the damage. Not likely this will be addressed, but thought it was worth mentioning.

There are a few other issues, but I don’t think they’re very important: Lull/Wake of Tranquility, Spinning Spells, etc. Hope some things are changed.

Senegar Imakikitsaz
Level 56 Beguiler of Bertoxxulous
