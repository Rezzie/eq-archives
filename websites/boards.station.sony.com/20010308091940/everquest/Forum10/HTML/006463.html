"Ok, how about apostrophes or double capitals in my surname?

Apostrophes and double capitals are allowable in a surname, particularly if they lend themselves to role-playing. Guides have absolutely NO impact on surnames. No as in nothing. Only Senior Guides+ can change surnames.

But the problem is not even Senior Guides or GM's. The problem are the players. As long as you have players naming themself "Idowank Alot" (seen myself) you have to have a policy. The good thing of a policy is, that it clearly cut's off the real bad names. Unfortunately there is a fine line somewhere that separates the ok ones from the bad ones. Defining that line in a way that everyone agrees on is simply not possible.

In addition, people tend to be greedy and jealous. Yes, Arcane is a word that is used, therefore D'Arcane is not allowed. Now one can say, "but D'Arcana" is not the same and therefore should be allowed. But then you have the "Iwaankh Ahlot" running around saying: "it's the same like D'Arcana, it's different therefore legal"...

It's sad that there even has to be a policy. But it's even sader that people try to break it all the time (not Morndark, don't get me wrong). But the sadest thing is, that a lot of people can't see that the POLICY has nothing to do with the ones enforcing it. It's not the cop's fault that there is a speed limit, it's the fault of the community.
