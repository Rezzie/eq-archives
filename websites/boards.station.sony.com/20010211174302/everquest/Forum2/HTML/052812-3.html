even though it has been posted, all i have to saY is i agree whole haeartedly, and i would like to thank BOdi for his list here, and the entire monk community for making it happen!

This post has been compiled from a very large thread from Zadkiel Hopeseeker's Monkly-Business website. The majority of this post was originally written by Kaiele Katarn, of the Innoruuk Server.

The main issue that Monks currently face is dealing damage in the end game. Prior to Kunark, and the changes in the melee skill caps, we where ahead of all other classes in dealing melee damage with the exception of a backstabbing rogue (which is as it should be).

Since our damage was based on our higher skill caps, and those skill caps were raised for all other melee classes with the Kunark expansion, the gap has closed dramatically. Damage dealing is the only potential a monk adds to a group, especially due to changes in the way the Feign Death skill works, and mobs' reactions to Feign Death.

Currently, a monk offers very little to a group due to the changes in damage. A warrior has higher hitpoints, higher AC, and higher magic resistance -- and thanks to the loss of a skill cap advantage for monks and new, extremely high ratio Velious and Kunark weapons for warriors, monks no longer have a clear advantage in dealing damage over a warrior.

Because the skill caps cannot be raised for monks to re-establish damage potential from that side due to programming issues, we would like to propose the following changes for the monk class.

DAMAGE INCREASE

1) Some type of upgrade to damage output. Rogues are incredibly powerful when they are behind a mob. Warriors are incredible tanks. Monk damage -- either with an "uber" staff or with epic fists -- should be halfway between a rogue's potential when the rogue is behind a mob using a Ragebringer and a warrior who is attacking with his/her best weapons. Keep in mind 10/16 and 13/20 weapons for warriors when rebalancing our damage. There are many ways to tune this damage, but PLEASE do not resort to another UBER-rare staff that will only balance the 1% of the monk population lucky enough to get one of these drops.

2) Raise fist damage post-50 for those without epics. While the delay continues to drop, the damage never increases once a monk hits level 50. Very few monks use fists anymore, but this would make the RP'ers and casual players a tad more viable near the end-game, if they ever get there.

3a) Encourage usage and training of lesser monk special attacks. Either allow us to use one type of strike and one type of kick at the same time by unhooking the special attack re-use timers post-50, or give the possibility of attack "strings". (This is the preferred solution, but would be harder to implement.) If a monk successfully uses Flying Kick on an enemy -- connects for damage -- then give a chance (based on Double Attack skill, preferably) for the monk to automatically use Dragon Punch/Tail Rake. If Dragon Punch/Tail Rake connects for damage, give a slightly lesser chance of doing an Eagle Strike. This is not only an interesting solution, it would make raising all of the monk special attack skills more important. This would be a post-50 enchancement, or when a monk reaches 200 skill in Flying Kick.

--- If this is technically not reasonable from a coding standpoint, then a secondary option would be:

3b) Minimum damage on Flying Kick. It won't make a huge difference in our damage output, but it will at least make us FEEL better. There is nothing worse than doing a flying kick for 3 damage as a post-50 monk. Even making this minimum damage cap post-50 only would be an improvement, and would not unbalance the pre-50 game where monks did have an advantage.

Damage Increase Suggestions:

To recap, a level 50's fist is a 14/30 weapon, which reduces (in delay only) to 14/26 at level 60. One suggestion is to have the damage increase (as it does for the rest of the levels) post 50 as well, so that a Monks fist at 60 is 16/26.

One of the best suggestions that has come from the many Monks is too have the main hand damage bonus applied to the off hand.

This is becasue Monks are the masters of Duel-Wield. We get it as a level 1 skill, and gain 7 points per level, capping faster and higher than any other class. This indicates a natural affinity for the monk to use his off-hand, a kind of natural/trained ambidexterity.

Allowing us to strike just as hard with the off-hand as the primary will solve the damage-dealing problem and bring us up to the correct damage level of being between warriors and rogues.

Other suggestions on ways to increase damage include having a minimum damage of level/2 for special attacks such as Flying Kick, or add some kind of level damage bonus to the special attacks, but the off hand damage bonus is the most favoured solution.

4) Improve Feign Death. Leave it as it currently functions in all but two areas -- If the monk is not removed from the mob's haste list and does not get a "has fallen to the ground" message, give the monk an automatic "Evade"-type effect. In other words, allow a monk to sacrifice a couple of combat rounds to lower him- or herself on the hate list. This would DRAMATICALLY increase monk survival rates on uber-mobs, when we're causing intense aggro with epic fists or other high end weapons. It also provides a check to ensure that monk damage is not overly high, if Verant is still worried about that. Finally, since this is linked to a SKILL, it still has a chance to fail, just like a rogue's evade skill. Also, fix the "no failure message, but the mob just keeps beating on me after the eighteenth feign attempt in a row" problem that I and many other monks have encountered in the post-50 world, especially while grouped in Velious.

One possible suggestion was to have something like "%T has seen through your ruse" when a Mob attacks after a succesful feign, simalar to the fact that Rogues know when they have sneaked succesfully, and another for resisted Spells not breaking Feign.

5) Fix the Celestial Fist effect. Celestial Tranquility is a great effect on our epic, but it stacks with almost NOTHING. Most monks rarely solo post-50, and most common group buffs will keep the effect from working. (Call of Earth, enchanter haste spells, most bard songs, Speed of the Shissar, etc.) At least alter it so that even if the haste is not usable due to other, better buffs then the additional effects -- the extra +AC and +ATK -- will stack anyway, similar to the way the Robe of the Whistling Fists worked. It's pretty bad when a well-meaning ranger tosses me Call of Earth and costs me 40ATK and 12AC. The ranger buff especially does not make sense because Call of Earth is NOT a Haste Buff, and the Celestial Tranquility Effect will indeed stack with the upgrade, Strength of Earth.

Also the Celestial Fists wearers, felt that a graphic for the hands would be better than the hand wraps.

6) Increase Warrior, Paladin, and ShadowKnight taunting skills. Yes, I know this isn't an enhancement to MONKS, but it would DEFINITELY help us out. WE ARE NOT TANKS. Unfortunately, it's very hard to taunt off of us due to our extreme aggro, ESPECIALLY with our low-damage, low-delay epic fists. PLEASE, PLEASE, PLEASE implement this one change if you don't make any other!!

7) At least consider spreading the weight limit increase out over the post-50 levels. Few monks will make 55. Even fewer will make 60. Therefore, weight increases at those two points are not terribly useful. A 2lb increase every other level (i.e., 52, 54, 56, etc) would result in a weight limit of 24 at 60. A 1lb increase every post-50 level would be another way to implement the changes, and would also result in a weight limit of 24 at level 60. If these are "too much" of a weight increase (the current max weight limit at 60 is 22lbs, from four-pound increases at 55 and 60) then skip levels 55 and 60, when we get our passive enhancements of +1 HP per tick while feigned.

The following are additional information gathered from other Monks posts, covering Skills, Disciplines, Armour and Quests.

SKILLS

Mend Perhaps Mend at a skill of 201+ can have a small chance of curing disease/poison. The increase chance to cure will rise as the skill goes above 201+.

Skill Caps Kick caps at 250. So why does Flying kick, eagle strike, and dragon punch all cap at 230?

Also, it was recently announced that they upped the caps for the Hand to Hand skill to equal 1hb/2hb, but all that was raised was the 60 cap, the 50 cap is still only 225 compared with 1hb/2hb cap of 240, so the 50 cap needs to be increased to 240 as well.

Higher Dodge, Riposte & Block. This is a concern for high level Monks in regards to the Big Game Hunts.

With higher skills in these areas, this would give Monks a unique way to tank better - Plate classes absorb damage with their higher hps, Rogues use stealth (evade) to not get hit, and monks just flat out use speed (block/dodge/riposte) to make the mob miss them.

Either increase the 3 skills from there current caps of 225/230 to 252, or have higher percentage chances of working (if that is not taken into consideration with a higher skill)

DISCIPLINES

Thunderkick This needs to be worked on, as currently it is being reported that 24 damage comes up the majority of the time. Possible suggestions include it becoming an innate or a timed discipline where every kick within the disciplines duration can be a thunderkick, {b} the minimum damage on TK to be 1 to 1.5 * level, (so a 60th level Monk will not kick for less than 60-90 damage with it), {c} add a short stun to Thunderkick, or {d} make TK a special attack after flying kick, gained at 50+th level or so.

These suggestions assume that the damage is not based from the FK damage table, and flying kick is not improved in any of the above afore mentioned ways.

We would like all the disciplines to receive an increase in length, and looking at the new hybrid disciplines, no one else has every discipline last for short periods of time.

ARMOUR

Monk armour needs some natural AC progression. It would be nice to see all cresent pieces (obtainable at around level 30) being 1 point more AC then cured silk (obtained at level 15) shiverback (obtained at 46+) being 1-2 AC greater than cresent, and Velious quest armour (obtained at level 50+) being 1-2 greater than shiverback. Each piece should have a higher AC than the same piece of the previous type.

Also, why does our equipment seem to have less (overall) in stats, resists, and clickable effects then other melee classes?

If Shiverback AC was raised to the AC level of the Black Panther armour, and the Velious armour AC rasied to that beyond Black Panther, I think that the vast majority of Monks would be very pleased. You will not increase a Monks AC, only make Monks want to wear their own armour.

To clarify, not one Monk is asking for more AC on pieces than we are already wearing, simply the AC of Monk only armour to be brought in line. If Velious sleeves had an AC of 10, they would be worn by many Monks over the Azure Sleeves they have worn for the past 30+ levels.

MONK QUESTS

Treant Fists. Make them have a slight effect (whether attack raised by 1 to 10, a small self only heal or even an effect like summoning bandages), or alter the stats enough to make the Monks of that level want to do the (35hr average long) quest, rather than the all to easy Gem Inlaid Guantlets, which provides a very simalar quest result in a fraction of the time.

The Collar of Neshika. The Collar itself is to heavy, and the weight needs to be greatly reduced from 1.5, perhaps down to 0.2, and adding one AC, would make the quest worth completing (and wearing over a Undead Collar)

Iksar Quests I belive their final quest (whistling Fist?) is still unfinished, and needs to be looked into.

This post does not cover everything, and as mentioned is compiled from many to help you decide what we think is required. Monks upto level 50, we believe are balanced, and it really is the end game (50 to 60th) that things need to be addressed.

Deficiencies of the Monk class:

1. Our damage output is only marginally higher than warriors and rangers (and in many cases lower if the warrior is well equipped), and is still vastly lower than a rogue. I do not think this is consistent with the monk's role in Everquest. Supposedly, we sacrifice a lot of AC/HP for an increased attack ability, and I don't think we're supposed to be sacrificing 30% of our HP and some of our AC for a 5% increase in damage over warriors. This problem could be solved in many ways.

A. Increase our skill caps
B. Give us new and improved weapons that are not so uber that only 1% of level 60 monks in Uber Uber guilds can obtain. If you do release more weapons, please make them "Monk only".
C. Un-bind the Strike and Kick attacks. A monk could use Flying Kick and a Tiger Claw independently of one another.
D. Give us a minimum damage bonus (a.k.a strength bonus a.k.a. level bonus) on our off-hand.
E. There absolutely must be a minimum damage to Flying Kick. It is humiliating for me (level 53) to do a Flying Kick on a fire beetle and only do 3 damage. The damage formula for Flying Kick must be tweaked.

2. The AC on our armor is extremely poor. I realize that a Monk is meant to have lower AC than other tank classes, and I agree with that notion. However, at level 53 I do not have a single piece of "Class specific" armor except for one Shackle. Why would a monk wear a Silver Moon Headband (6ac 1svAll) when he could be wearing a Froglok Crown (12ac 20svFire/Cold)? Why would a monk be wearing Shiverback arms when they have half the AC of Azure sleeves? I want to wear my monkly armor, and wear it with pride. But by doing so, I would be giving myself an AC handicap. 90% of what level 50+ monks wear they've been wearing for the last 20 levels, and only a tiny percentage of it is "Monk" armor. Give us a reason to wear our own armor. Now, please do not confuse what I'm asking for.

**(I am NOT asking for a monk's total AC to increase, I just want to be able to wear my Monk Planes armor and have the same amount of AC I have now.)

3. A slightly improved FD would be nice. Probably the most appealing part of having a monk in the group is the ability to Feign Pull, but that is extremely awkward and dangerous now. By improving Feign you would be helping to restore the monk's ability to pull and you would also be decreasing a group's REQUIREMENT of having an Enchanter (Improved FD = Less trains = Mez is not a necessity).

4. These are just some nice 'extras' I'd like to see thrown into the game for monks.
A. Mend having some innate special ability at higher levels. As it is now, there is no difference between 100 skill in mend and 200 skill. Maybe a chance to cure poison/disease or restore more than 25% of your hp.
B. Warriors get critical hits with Melee weapons. Rogues get critical hits with Ranged weapons. Rangers get critical hits with Bows. Monks are the only "damage dealing class" that do not get Critical hits. I'd like to see a Monk's Special attacks (Flying kick, tiger claw, tail rake, etc) have a 5% chance of scoring a critical hit which would add 5% to the total damage. A 5% increase in damage to 5% of our special attacks (which is a small percentage of our total damage) is nothing. This is just for flavor.

P.S. I love my class, I just sometimes get the feeling I'm becoming uncompetitive.

I've never played a monk, but this is my oppinion on what role they should fulfill in the game.

1a) A monk should not be able to beat a warrior in toe-to-toe duel.
Tanking is all the warrior does, while the monks have the advantage of FD.
1b) A monk should outdamage a warrior by 20-30% (against the same opponent, given the same buffs, equipment of equal 'value' and obtainability etc.)
2a) A monk should be able to beat a rogue in a toe-to-toe duel.
A rogue should do slightly less damage than a warrior when standing in front of the enemy, but a whole lot more when standing behind, plus a monk should be a better tank than a rogue - Obviously since rogues have evade and monks have taunt.
2b) A monk should NOT be able to outdamage a rogue who is standing behind the mob.

Equal to half the monks level, this would make the minimum damage on Thunderkick equal to the monks level, as is the rogues BS only this requires a discipline. The minimum damage on Thunderkick is very depressing for high-level monks...

Instead of punch punch punch punch FK punch punch punch FK,

Hitpoints: Are fine. Monks don't have warrior hitpoints, nor should they.

I play a monk I have leveled all the way to 60, and the only real concern I have with the class is DAMAGE !! Pre- Kunark the class was well balaced and did what it should. Now, we are on equal footing with warriors offensively and still vastly inferior in tanking.

Monks and Warriors have a damage output that is with in 5% of each other, all other things being equal. That needs to improve DRAMATICALLY.

In general a equally equiped monk should do AT LEAST 35% to 45% more damage than a warrior. Monks should be able to do about 75% to 85% of the damage a rogue can do from the back of a mob. Monks are a melee damage based class, and our damage output should be on par with that.

SUGGESTIONS to improve monks:
1. INCREASE MONK ATK. From level 51 to 60 give monks an automatic 15pt ATK bonus per level. So at level 60 a monk would have an attack 150pts higher than before. This would help improve damage output for all monks regardless of their weapon choice. Pre-Kunark monks already did have this advantage as our offense skill caped well higher than any other class including rogues and warriors. With Kunark, all classes had skill caps increased, (warriors and rogues cap within 2 pts of us offensively and defensively now). Obviously we got the short end of this balance.

2. IMPROVE FIST DAMAGE. (Including celestial fists). Pre-Kunark a monks fist damage increased every 5 levels from 4 to 14 (level 50 monk had 14 damage fist). Fist delay fell from 36 at low levels to 30 at level 50. A level 60 monk STILL has a 14 damage fist. Fist delay at 50 is 30, and at 60 is 26. Clearly monks fist damage isnt keeping up in terms of the natural progression. I would suggest 18/24 (for human at least) as a good target for level 60 monk fists. Of course the celestial fists (9/16 now) would need improvment also then.

3. WEAPON WEIGHTS. The Tranquil Staff is wt 10.0. Many other kunark monk 2hb's are the same wt. These weapons were added most likely with out changing the wt. from a generic 2hb weapon template by a designer who didn't have full knowledge of the game. Being that a level 50 monk has a wt. limit of 14, it is rediculus to ask him to devote over two-thirds of his carrying capacity to a tranquil staff or other overly heavy 2hb weapon. Clearly these weapon wt.'s are bugged, and they need to be fixed asap. Forcing a monk to choose between weilding a tranquil staff in order to do the damage he should as a monk, or carrying extra food and water is a little overboard.

4. FEIGN DEATH. The FD skill was designed to save a monk from death. In the level 50 to 60 game it doesnt really work like this anymore. In kunark to an extent, and almost always in Velious, if you try to FD on a mob after you have inflicted some amount of damage on it, the mobs "sees through it" and continues to pummel you sensless. Hence, in a fight gone bad, or when a mob is "too mad at you" FD is generally useless.

Another issue here that was likely a bug but is now played off as a "design feature" is not being able to FD on casting mobs. If you FD on a mob AFTER it starts to cast a spell on you, but before it hits you, when the spell hits you your succesful feign breaks. Thus it is impossible to save your self from chain casting mobs or multiple casting mobs with FD. I dont think FD was just supposed to work against melee mobs, I think this is just a bug where a spell hitting you after you FD breaks FD for some "buggy " reason. Fix this and you will make a lot of monks happy.

5. GRAPHIC FOR CELESTIAL FISTS. The gloves have no "skin" coded for them and when worn show up as if you are not wearing any gloves at all. If you turn particle effects to medium or higher (which most computers won't allow), you can see the stars coming out of the gloves, but the gloves them selves still have no graphic. Im sure it wouldnt be to hard to code the celestial fists to have the apperance of shiverback gloves or something like that just for completeness of the visual effect.

SUMMARY: Monks were a great class before kunark. Increase our fist damage and general damage dealing capacity somehow to put it back to the same realative place as it was before and monks will be a great class again.

Thanks for your time and starting this helpful discussion.
