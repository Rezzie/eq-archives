In response to zerogravity's post about shadowmen, from what I can tell, the shadowmen don't necessarily resist harmony, but there are a few spawn points that are so close together that even with the reduced aggro radius provided by harmony, you'll still get two if they're right next to each other. Harmony only reduces the radius where the mobs will notice an attack on an ally and come running..for instance if you harmony a group of critters and pull one from the far side, they'll likely bring others with them as they travel through the group of them and set them off.
FD pulling and harmony pulling each take a degree of skill to be successful, and tricks that need to be known to be good at it. FD is more of a "damage control" angle on pulling, while harmony is more of a "preparation" pull.
Another think I've found difficult with harmony is that some mobs seem to have such a large aggro radius, that you have a very narrow "safe" distance you can cast harmony from and not draw them in the process.
I myself have deep respect for monks that are skilled with this form of mob control. It takes nerves and talent to be able to split some of the nasty groups and come back alive. Once a monk friend of mine got completely squished by several harm touches in Kithicor..just nasty.

The reason FD is "against the vision" is because it was not intended to be used to pull mobs. Harmony on the other hand was planned to do just that...pull single mobs. That is why FD is frowned on and harmony isn't.

Now...to address some of the points that have been made. I have tried both "Numb the Dead" with my SK and the soothe line of spells (that are aggro reducing spells like harmony) for my enchanter. These spells prolly have a 30% resist rate for chanters (I quit using it on my SK after like the third time I cast it due to high resists). A resist with these spells is considered an attack spell and will bring the entire camp on a fail. On a success, no monsters attack (but I want to say there is about a 30 second duration on the enchanter line...so you have to cast and pull quick). An enchanter casting this spell will quickly be annihilated under the barrage of pissed off mobs...probably faster than your tanks can pull them off. A ranger can take the hits for a while. Harmony also has about a 1 min or so duration (when I was a newb, I thought someone cast clarity on me...it can be cast on PC's on non-pvp servers but it doesn't do anything). It is also highly non-resistable by mobs under 30 (I think the save may be poison based...most non-undead monsters have fairly low poison resist)...and I believe a fail doesn't bring the camp on you (tho, I may just not have seen a fail =).

Verant does want Rangers, Shadowknights, Necromancers, and Enchanters to be able to pull singles from a group of mobs via an aggro reducing spell. They just made the resist rates so high on all but Rangers that it is not a safe way to pull. This was the vision. Verant has RP problems FD...where a mob thinks you are alive...then dead...then alive...then dead...then alive...then dead...ad nauseum. Even the dumbest creatures prolly wouldn't fall for this more than once.
