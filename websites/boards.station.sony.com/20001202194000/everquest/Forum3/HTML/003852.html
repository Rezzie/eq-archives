Just another thing to bring up. In the rare instances where I can use my rain spells to full use, I have noticed that they have an abismal resist rate, even vs green and very low blue creatures. For instance, was hunting in the Hole with a group at the entrance, fighting the elementals for smithing componets. Almost all of them were green to me, with about 1 in 10 being blue.

However, with both my rain spells, Tears of Druzzel (magic based) and tears of Solusek (fire based) I was seeing about a 30-40% resist rate. Out of about 2 hours playing, I only had a handful of casts where all three rains would hit for full damage, and I easily had as many times where all three rains were resisted. Also it is not a case of the elementals being more resistant then other creatures, as I have had similar results using this spell in Karnors and Sebalis when hunting for exp, which is why this spell is currently shelved for the time being. As comparison, my stun spells, the highest resisted spells to date, are maybe 1 in 10 full resist.


The practical uses of this spell are limited already, to use this spell requires:

1. Group with no pet casters (the big one right here)
2. Good taunters, or lots of stun casters
3. a large enough area to work so you don't catch yourself in the rains
4. People who don't ping pong such that the mob is right next to you when the spell goes off.

But, with the current resist rates for these spells, even when these conditions are met, using these spells is still not feasable due to the resist rates. Please look into this, thanks
