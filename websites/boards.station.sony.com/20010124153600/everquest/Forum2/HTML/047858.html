ok......gonna try to clear this up

XP tables are not changing in the patch, but here is the math. Let's use a human druid vs a human shadow knight.

Druid needs 1000 total xp points to go from start to finish of X level. Shadow Knight needs 1400 total xp points from start to finish of same X level. This is the 40% xp penalty that the Shadow Knight gets. This still will be in effect after the patch.

Here is the math:

Prepatch:

Druid kills X mob for 100 xp points a kill. He has to kill this mob 10 times to get to next level.
100 x 10 = 1000


Shadow Knight kills same X mob for 100 xp points per kill. He has to kill it 14 times to get to next level.
100 x 14 = 1400

Post Patch:

Druid kills same X mob for 100 xp points per kill.
Since druid has no xp penalty prepatch, he gets no xp multiplier postpatch. Still takes 10 kills to get his level.
100 x 1 x 10 = 1000

Shadow Knight kills same X mob for 100 xp points per kill. Shadow Knight has to have 40% more xp prepatch, Shadow knight gets a 40% xp bonus per kill. So his total xp from X mob is 140. It now takes the shadow knight 10 kills to level.
100 x 1.4 x 10 = 1400

Both classes will level at the same rate killing the same mob post patch. Verent has taken into account the extra xp required for hybrids to level and given yall a bonus xp mutiplier to compensate.

OK...on the death part...

A druid dies and looses 500 xp points (ok...everyone cheer here ). He kills same x mob for 100 xp points a kill 5 times to get lost xp back.

100 x 1 x 5 = 500

Now same level Shadow Knight dies and he will loose 750 xp points.
500 (base xp) x 1.4(Multiplier) = 750

Now Shadow Knight kills same X mob at 100 xp points per kill. With the xp multiplier, it will still take the Shadow Knight 5 kills to get his full xp back.

100 x 1.4 x 5 = 750

Yes, Shadow Knights will loose more xp on deaths post patch than druids, but Shadow Knights will be able to get it back in the same number of kills as the Druid.

ok I used the druid because they have no "xp penalty or bonus" pre-patch and the SK has the same "xp penalty" as the other hybrids pre-patch.

I hope this clears up some confusion on the subject.
