Ok, let me explain something.

I'm a lvl 60 warrior. I've played with the same cleric since level 1. She's 60 also, we dinged on the same kill. We're in a great guild, and we consider ourselves lucky. We both have our epics, and she passed her Donal's bp down the line when she got her PoG bp.

We created these characters for many reasons, one of which was that a warrior and a cleric are a great duo. We love to group, and raid, but we also really enjoy hunting quietly for experience on the road to level 60. Warrior-Cleric is a great duo for this. Neither of us want to solo, and there're always some low blues that we can take with just the two of us. As long as we fight them one at a time, of course

So we've done this for 60 levels: Hunt with friends a few nights, guild raids a few nights, and then we we need to get away it's off to Lfay to kill brownies for brownie parts and very little xp. But that's ok. Cleric-Warrior duo is a good duo for single mob kills, but hardly the most efficient. It takes longer, and you're limited to low blues.

Now she's had her PoG BP for a few levels, and it's been very nice. But I can honestly say, there's never been a time when she's chain casted it on me in the manner you suggested, Abashi. It just doesn't make sense.

1) When it's just the two of us fighting, say at the brownie camp, we're fighting stuff that we can kill easily. Sure, she could chain cast the bp on me for that fight, but what's the point? We did fine before the bp, it's not a determining factor.

2) A cleric-warrior duo, post 50, cannot take large risks when hunting for xp. Why? Because if you cut it too close, the warrior dies, everytime. The warrior has no escape route when the mana is gone or if a timed heal fizzles. So in general, all war-cleric combos fight the easy blues, like we do. If we want fast xp, we find a HS or Chardok group.

So let's add the PoG bp into the picture. It can be chain casted, and by your calculations 300hps per tick. What does this do to our low-blue encounters? A bit less downtime, same outcome, we win the fight. Could we use this effect on a high blue mob? Not a chance. Anything mid to high blue in the endgame can easily do more than 300hps of damage in 6 seconds. So if we take the risk, and find this out the hard way, the warrior stays a live through one, maybe two chain casts, then the damage finally catches up and he dies (or flees) midway through the last 20-second cast time. End of fight. Hopefully the cleric can gate or zone or die in a convienent place for CR.

Point: The PoG bp does not allow a war-cleric combo to hunt something they could not otherwise hunt. It simply lessens downtime. And is that a bad thing? An equipment upgrade should result in better efficiency for the team. If I get a better sword, then I will kill a monster faster, which saves mana and downtime. The PoG bp is not overpowering or unbalancing in the war-cleric hunting combo.

3) XP groups: For this example, let's visualize a standard balanced dungeon group. War, Cle, Wiz, Enc, Ran, Dru. The classes really don't matter to explain my point. For a cleric to dedicate, in battle, 20 seconds to cast a Celestial Elixer, then the fight is not challenging enough for the group. 20 seconds is an eternity. What if aggro shifts? The chanter and the ranger both can die in that 20 seconds while the cleric is LOCKED into a CE on the warrior. It's just too risky in any encounter except the safe, boring xp hunts. The same safe, boring xp hunts that are just as easy without the PoG bp. Again, less downtime with better equipment. Hardly overpowering. When we hunt for xp, and we get a fast paced, adrenaline flowing group together, the PoG bp is useful for one thing, and one thing only. After the fight, my cleric casts CE on me and I heal as I head out to pull. Again, it saves downtime but does not allow us succeed where we would not otherwise.

4) Uber encounters: As noted in many posts before this, the effect on the PoG bp can not be used in any manner that would effect the outcome of a high end raid or encounter. The mobs just hit too hard, and too fast for a cleric to even consider a 20 second cast for 300 hps a tick. It's only used after a fight to heal the wounded, and after rez. Again, it saves downtime.

I've given examples of the majority of encounters a 55+ cleric will face. In each of these, Ibelieve I've shown clearly that the current effect on the bp does not affect the outcome of any encounter, it merely saves downtime. Is that really overpowering? Hell, is it bad in any way? If I change my armor, I'd expect an improvement over my old armor, or there'd be no reason to switch. Risk vs Reward: A cleric works hard to get this bp, and is rewarded with less downtime.

Like I said before, I'm a lvl 60 warrior that's spent every one of those levels and over 90 days of played time with the same cleric. She's had the bp for quite some time, and the points I've outlined above are directly from our firsthand knowledge and experience. Trust us on this one, we do know what we're talking about. There is no need to make a change to the PoG cleric breastplate. It is not overpowering in any way.

It's late (early?), tempers have flared, including mine, we've all said quite a bit. Please just read my arguments above, pass them on to the Dev team and let logic prevail. I'm going to get some sleep and hope this was all a bad dream.
