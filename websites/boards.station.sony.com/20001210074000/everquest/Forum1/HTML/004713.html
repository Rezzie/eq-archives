Ok, here is the details.

First off, it seems that many people didn't notice that when I posted the link to the Nvidia website, the message boards made it into two lines. Some people have only been copying the link minus the bit at the end that went to the second line. Make sure you copy and paste the whole thing.

OK here goes the directions now.

When you download the drivers from Nvidia you will need to have some kind of unziping utility. I recommend WinZip, it seems easiest for most users to use. You can download that at www.winzip.com.

After you download and unzip the Riva 128 drivers, you will need to follow these steps:

1. Right click on your desktop, and click on Properties.
2. In the Display Properties window, select the Settings tab.
3. In the settings tap page, turn your color setting down to 16 colors (or the lowest you can go) make sure it isn't set to 16 bit- 16 colors. Turn your Screen area down to 640 X 480 resolution.
4. Press Apply. Depending on your system you may have to reboot for the changes to take effect.
4a. If you had to reboot your computer, please follow steps 1 and 2 again.
5. Back at the settings page, select the "Advanced" button.
6. In the Advanced window, click the Adaptor tab.
7. On the Adaptor page click on the Change button. This will open up the Update Device Driver Wizard.
8. In the device wizard, you will want to tell it to display a list of all the drivers. (I'm trying to remember the specifics for Win98, I should have left it on one of my systems...)
9. Basically you will want to tell your computer to install the Riva 128 drivers. You will need to select it out of a list, and click the Have Disk button. When the Open window pops up, you will need to click on browse. If you let WinZip unzip it to its default directory you will need to point browse to C:\unziped\Riva128 folder. Once you have this location (or if you told WinZip to put it else ware the folder you sent it to) selected you can tell it to install the drivers. Just follow the prompts and you should do good.
10. Reboot your computer. After it reboots, you can then change your color and resolution settings back to what you had before.

I hope this will clarify things better for anyone confused by the origional posting.
