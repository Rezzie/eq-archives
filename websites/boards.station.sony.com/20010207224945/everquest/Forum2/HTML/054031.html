One of the problems with PvP in games like UO, EQ and AC. Is that PvP was an AFTERTHOUGHT of the game production. They designed the game with PvE in mind and not PvP.

Then, OSI realized "damn, people are killing people" and was forced to try and adapt to the situation. As bad as the PvP is in UO now, I think they did a good job trying to adapt the game around it.. Not perfect, but not bad considering all things.

Then EQ/AC saw the failure of PvP in UO and thought "hey! lets have a totatly non-PvP option"

While many players like the idea, many players will also agree (even die hard "PK suck people") will agree - PvP can be fun within context.

Hell, I suck at PvP, and I hate PKs. But PvP within context is alot of fun. There is only so much content and high-level areas you can add to the game before it just becomes stagnant and victem of mudflation (SOV anyone?)

ALOT of games (UWO:Origns, ShadowBane, Anarchy Online (I think), Dark Ages of Camelot) all have PvP oriented sections of the game.

Some games focused around PvP and the war of factions/guilds (DAoC/ShadowBane), and some are just PvP enabled areas to hunt in which ahve better items/reward and faster leveling (UO)

The game designers are realzing the need for contextual PvP in a game, and I think if more players looked beyond the rampant dread days of UO, and thought more about consentual contextual PvP, they would not be so turned off of it.

I think this is a good trend and hope it continues.
