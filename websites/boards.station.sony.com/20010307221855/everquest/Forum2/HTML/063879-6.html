And yes, we've tried to work around the aggro. Nothing much helps. How is it "fun" to have to resurrect half your healers, wait for them to get mana, get them rebuffed, and have an entire raid sit on it's collective rear end for 20 minutes after each fight? Frankly, it's not.

That's just one example, not even the most extreme. Lets take something else, like, say, the Statue of Rallos Zek. Here is a mob that duals for 1250, and hits once every 2-3 seconds. (never timed it specifically, but he hits fast). The primary tank, at best, with absolute max buffs, is going to be at 6khp, if that. With Defensive, this Warrior can barely take 2 rounds of hits from the Statue. This means that 6 seconds into the fight, if you want this tank to live, he must get complete-healed. Thus we must start a CHeal on this tank before we even charge the mob. Given the new way hate works, it is IMPOSSIBLE to build up enough hate to save that cleric. Yes, it might be possible to COH them, but that's not a guaranteed aggro clear, and timing all that is iffy at best. Now, given the power of this mob, the only way to keep the primary tank alive is rolling complete heals, hitting every 4-5 seconds or so. However, any clerics doing this, are of course building up far more aggro than that tank can generate, especially with the changes to proc aggro, and the damage drop with defensive. So what you have, is a situation that by it's very nature is set up for massive cleric death. Combine that with the fact that this mob has oh, 200k-300k hp, and you have a mob that has become functionally impossible to do.

Velious was put out with high end mobs balanced based on multiple donals usage, original heal aggro, etc. Healing has thus been drastically nerfed. Yet none of these mobs have been adjusted one iota to make them easier. Indeed, as posted by someone else, several have been made HARDER, including at the very least dragons in Sleepers, and now Yelinak. If these encounters were "balanced" before, and now you have drastically nerfed the ability to heal, by what standards can they be considered "balanced" now? What has actually happened is that several of these mobs have moved into the ranks of the "totally unkillable", and no one bothers any more. You've thus completely removed a large percentage of the high level game from the very people you intended it for.

Yes, some of the changes on test might help. They shouldn't be necessary though, as the changes never should've been made in the first place. The Donals nerf I can kind of see, but not the aggro nerf nor the CE-casting BP.
