What I find extremely disturbing is this whole attitude that an improvment to one class, *any* improvement, is somehow a "nerf" to every other class. Druids and Shamans get better healing spells at highter levels, this is a cleric nerf. Raising Ranger, Paladin, and SK skill caps is a nerf to warriors. Hell, the Epic Quest weapons for every class, that clearly was a nerf to Paladins.

My frank and honest opinion is that all the changes they've put through over the last few months have been overwhelmingly positive. They've addressed a lot of issues that have been neglected since the start of the game, and redressed a lot of assumptions that turned out to be drastically wrong (e.g. that Hybrids would be inherently better than pure classes.) Yes, some class have gotten more improvement than others (a general opinion by me would be that the hybrids got the most out of things, but I'll be the first to admit that druids got some sweet improvements as well.) But how does that detract from *your* gameplay? Paladins can really, truly tank at higher levels, does this make your warrior useless? You still have quite a lot more hitpoints and do quite a bit more damage. A shaman or druid can heal better, does this make the Cleric superflous? The Clerics are still hands-down the best and most efficient at healing, the new enhancements don't even threaten to make druids or shamans better than clerics in that area. So is an improvement in Druid tracking a death blow to the rangers? Does it affect a Cleric at all?

That being said, let me make it clear that I am wholeheartedly in favor of Rangers being much better trackers than anyone else. But I look at it this way: making another class better at something doesn't make you any worse, particularly if you're still better than them at it.

Yes, I play a druid as my primary character. Would I be happy with a higher track cap than 50? Definitely. I'd be ecstatic. Do I think that Druids should have a higher cap than Bards? Yes, probably. Is it an insult to me as a druid that they don't on the live servers? Absolutely not. The fact that a Bard has a cap of 100 doesn't alter the usefulness of mine at 50. Its a highly useful skill.

To everyone, I say this: show a little faith. Maybe they have more ranger-only improvements in the works. Maybe, in light that Rowl said the skill isn't linear, there are distinct "break points" in the skill level where the skill is drasticaly wider in range. Perhaps a 200 isn't twice as good as a 100, maybe its ten times as good, you don't know. Or maybe they just think that since Track has absolutley nothing to do with combat, improving another class at it slightly won't change the balance of the classes significantly. Or maybe the changes on Test won't go live at all, which would be a shame, but I won't lose any sleep over it. Or perhaps the bard skill cap will be lowered, rather than the druid cap raised, which I also think would be a shame.

But at any rate, enough with the negative attitude toward other people's classes. A positive change to another class will help you, in the end, this is a cooperative game. Think about how a change will *really* effect things, and then take into account that you haven't actually seen the effect on the servers, live. Then reserve judgement. Wait and see. Who knows, maybe your class will be the next to get an enhancement, and everyone else will be crying foul at you?

The Druid tracking cap has been capped pretty low, and until they implemented the scroll bar, that's been an actual advantage. Since the Druids have enjoyed that advantage up until now, they don't deserve to reap the benefits that the scroll bar gives? What about a new druid? Kione, it affects me because when my advantages are given to other classes, they are devalued.

The ability to locate choice targets is a selling point for Rangers looking for groups. If Druid ability in this area is increased, our ability to leverage our Tracking for group spots is diminished.

Rangers are supposed to be the best trackers in the game. If Druids have their skill increased, particularly as much as the current plan calls for, that will not be true in any meaningful sense. Druids will be able to track nearly as well as Rangers, and effectively just as well when you consider that targets at the bottom of the range list are almost certainly engaged before you can get to them anyway.

We were told that 'going forward' (yecch I hate marketroid phrases), Tracking improvements would be limited to Rangers. Well, that wasn't exactly true, was it now. Everyone got the scrollbar -- which was specifically described as a Ranger-only improvement -- and now Druids are getting a huge skill increase. After two years of suffering being the third best of three classes with what is supposed to be our specialty skill we are finally elevated to our rightful place, and no sooner does this happen then it is stripped from us again.

This is the second time in recent history that Rangers have gotten something good only to have it snatched away again, the last time being Trueshot. (When Trueshot was fixed -- the first time -- it did a respectable amount of damage and a 55 Ranger could finally get an invite to a raid...for about a week. Now Trueshot barely has any effect at all.) So yeah, we're getting a little bitter.
