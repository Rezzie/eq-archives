How about lowering the casting difficulty of divine light, or as all the clerics I know refer to it: divine fizzle. I've fizzled 3 times in a row on numerous occasions. That's a bit much for a master of healing if you ask me.

As far as giving more healing to druids and shamans, it definitely changes the playing field. Especially with shamans. Once you slow an NPC, superior healing is sufficient in many places. Of course, you still need a cleric because hunting post 50 without a rez capable cleric is madness. One link death and you are set back hours. I don't see this changing my life as a cleric very much: sit, sit, sit, sit, stand, CH, sit....

I know everyone has complained about the tediousness of the cleric's life but, to be honest, it's a hard problem to fix. A cleric must sit to med. The only way that's not going to happen is if his mana recharge is the same (or nearly the same) standing as when sitting. A spell that did that would drastically change our lives and make it much more interesting. The problem is: 1) anything short of this won't make much difference and 2) this will never happen.

Why won't it happen? Because if it did every other class would be screaming for the same thing and I think VI would consider it too radical a change. There are also other problems.

Would it affect balance? I think so. With jboots a cleric could run around and kite stuff with basically endless mana. You don't see that now. And with mana regenning like he was sitting, it would change the pace of kiting, making it much more rewarding experience wise. Making it indoor only would deal with this but there are other issues as well, and many I haven't thought of I'm sure.

Another idea would be a weapon that proc'd something like "mana gift," a spell that would instantly grant X mana with no health loss. So, on average, you could melee and get almost as much mana as medding. Of course, there are lots of issues with this... is the weapon tuned to provide the amount of mana from medding at level 60 or level 30? Can you make a weapon proc scale appropriately with level? Does this overpower the cleric class? Would this weapon be so insanely difficult to get that it made absolutely no difference to 90%+ clerics? Would clerics with this weapon be given preference over those without based simply upon them having this item? This seems to be perfectly acceptable for warriors... i.e. you pick warriors based on equipment routinely. A warrior with good equipment is good, a warrior with great equipment is great, a naked warrior is basically worthless. This seems to be a "no-no" for priests and casters... A cleric with 200+ wisdom, unless you are going on a raid, is pretty much interchangeable with any other cleric with 200+ wisdom. The same goes for pure casters. I don't understand VI's "vision" here but they seem to be very unwilling to change so I'll pick another battle.

In short, I haven't seen any solution to date that doesn't violate balance issues or VI's "vision" of the game. Clerics were tedious and necessary then. They are tedious and necessary now. And they will be tedious and necessary hereafter. You can usually find a group but you'll be almost as bored as if you didn't.
