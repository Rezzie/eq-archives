No, there are currently no rules againt the "invulnarable healer", whether its due to an "out of range" healer or a "cross-teaming" healer.


>>>No one should be invulerable in combat and you shouldn't have to call an enemy to kills guys of your own race. A design flaw to be sure and this should at least be commented on or addressed by Verant.

Brega, you can say that the system is flawed and should be fixed, but none of the solutions that have been presented have been endorsed by plurality, much less a majority of the players. There are certainly some pretty rash and obscure things that we could do to try and "fix" this to your liking, but what of everyone else?

>>>I've never seen this addressed by Abashi either. Most players just want Racewars to be coded as Racewars. Invulerable healing and buffing to help "Racewar enemy teams" defeat your own team should be coded out as impossible.

Sure, that seems simple, but what is the reasoning behind, for instance, a human paladin and a high-elf paladin to "refuse" (cf. be restricted by code) to heal or buff each other? Why *must* there be animosity between them?

>>>1) Make the healer attackable by his own team for say the next 2 minutes everytime he heals (this happens in guild wars, so it should be possible);

Can't do this with the way that attack rights are handle. In EQ, if I can attack you, you can also attack me. There's no way around that mechanism. The exploit that pops out is that all I'd have to do to be able to go kill my own team would be to cast a beneficial spell on someone of another team. EQ also can't handle a persistant agressor flag. We know who is casting the spell and who the recipient is at the time the spell is cast, but not after. The same is true with melee attacks.

These are the options available to us:

1. Do nothing.
2. Disallow beneficial spells across teams. (handles cross-teaming problem)
3. Disallow beneficial spells out-of-level (handles, for the most part, the out-of-level situation)
4. A combination of 2&3.

The difficulty is that while we want to support PvP and the Teams servers, we cannot in that process harm the PvE aspects of the game. It's still going to be EverQuest, but will be EverQuest with PvP, rather than PvP EverQuest.

There's another possible solution (if indeed people feel that there is a problem) that one of the programmers and I were just brainstorming about. Please let me know if you feel that there are any unacceptable problems with this....

How about... If a player takes damage from another PC, they can only receive beneficial spells from someone in their own team for X-minutes or until they zone.

Let's apply this to the 3 scenarios that the original poster mentions:

Case #1: Dwarf is attacking DE1. DE2 is over 8 levels above DE1. Since DE2 is out-of-level with DE1, DE1 cannot take his beneficial spells since he's being attacked by the dwarf. DE2's only option would be to attack the dwarf if he was in range, but in this case, he's not.

Case #2: The level 40 DE cleric would not be able to support he level 20 marauders since he is out of range.

Case #3: Evils are attacking elves. Assuming that the elves have done damage to the evils, evils could not be healed/buffed by the cross-teaming elf.

Other scenarios:

Human is grouped with 3 elves, they can buff each other all day long. However, a troll attacks the human. The human can now only be healed by other people on the human team.

Human is grouped with 3 elves, and can buff, etc... The troll now attacks one of the elves. That elf cannot receive direct support from the human, but can from the other elves. The human could, of course, attack the troll if he was in range, just couldnt' heal the elf.

Any problems with this idea that you can think of?

-Gordon

That said, I think your proposal to make it so that people could not be healed by an "invulnerable" healer for a set amount of minutes after being attacked by a PC would work for ther server. I think the time limit should be short, like no more than 1 or 2 minutes. That would solve most invulnerable healer situations, without too seriously limiting players' ability to buff and heal group-mates.
