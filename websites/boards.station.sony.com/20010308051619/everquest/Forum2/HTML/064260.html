First off, I want to let you know that I am not an uber player, or part of an uber guild. I play on a mature server (Rodcet Nife) and am in I guess what you would concider a mid-sized guild... big enough to do raids but small enough to know every player's name in the guild list.

Also, for the sake of civility, if you want to post something negative here, there are plenty of other threads that are just posts away from being deleted or locked, rant there. Please keep replys intelegent, and don't post because you don't like one person's view.

I am a level 52 cleric, who fought his way up the experience chain the hard way, never power leveled, never having uber gear, ect. I have not posted in these posts in the past because it was too much negativity to bear, I just did my thing and played the game. But lately things are getting so skewed, that it is no longer enjoyable for me.

This post is for Verent, not as a rant, or to pin the blame on the donkey, just to give you my point of view as to what went wrong and where. You made mistakes, but you are human, so it's allowed. Unfortunately, your mistakes effected a large population of customers, and things got blown out of the water. But hopefully you will learn from your mistakes and will make better games in the future as a result of what you have learned in the Everquest experiment.

You made a wonderful game, it was unlike anything that has ever been done before. And as a first, you wrote the rules as you went, and made some errors, but tried your best to return to the origanal spirit of the game. You had a Vision(tm) where all players started on a level playing feild, for no other reason than to keep one class from overpopulating the game, and to add flavor to the game by having a variety of viable options open to its players. And for a long time you had great success. I admit, there were some things that were just wrong, like the experience penalty for hybreds. I agreed with it at first, until it became apparent that maybe the penalty was too harsh, making it not enjoyable to play that class.

But it seems like lately you have forgotten something significant about the game as it was when it was released. At first, you could pick a number of variations of class/race/dieties, each with distinct advantages/disadvantages. Everyone who did some research 3 months after the game was released could pick a class/race/diety knowing full well the disadvantages, but liking the advantages. I have played a cleric almost exclusively since I started playing a year ago. I have also played a monk and a druid, but never got them past level 20, I had too much fun with my cleric. When I made my cleric, I knew that for the most part, it was not the most exciting class to play. I have been on 7 dragon runs and have yet to see a dragon. Did I complain? No, because I knew that was gonna happen when I started. See, for me the excitement or fun was not from 'beating the mob', but from being the one who kept the ball rolling. It was me that kept the people alive who won or lost the fight. I loved having to pay close attention to everything that was going on around me, knowing that one mistake could spell doom for the entire group. It was a position of responsibility and garnered trust from my fellow players. I was proud of being able to say that I had escaped certain doom on many occations, only to emerge victorious in the end due to a little luck and the ability to think fast on my feet. I was never bored. I know that a lot of clerics complained about the med/heal cycle being too repetitive to want to level a cleric to high levels. I could write a book on why I think they thought that way and how it would have been better if a few things were different, but that is not why I am writing this post. But my point is, the did not come into the class blind. I'm sure that more than once, if they bothered to look into it, the would have seen warning signs that the high level game for clerics was med/heal/buff. But then again if you break it down like that, a warrior does nothing more then attack/taunt/kick. Do they complain they don't have enough to do?

Your present problems started when you forgot that people knew the consequences of choosing certain class/race/diety combonations. If a certain class has done the same thing since the first day Everquest had been released, how can they come to you 2 years after release and say that they are unhappy with thier current role? Sure the game has to evolve to remain fresh for its long term player base, but at what cost? I know that you had certain expectations of class roles when you developed Everquest, but if an oversight of coding mistake happens to make the class behave differently than you had intended, is it really broken? You have said yourself it was an experement, you didn't really know what to expect. So how can you turn around 2 years later and say that was not your intention? It has been that way since it began, and sure, it was not perfect, but it was not so bad that the entire game dynamics as they had evolved should be changed.
What I am trying to say is that there should be a penalty for playing every class. For me it was not being able to solo effectively, or hit the broad side of a barn with a weapon, and burn huge amounts of mana if I decided to cast a damage spell on a mob. I knew what the draw backs were, but I also knew the advantages of being a cleric. I might not be able to solo, but if I tried hard enough, I could always find a group. You felt the game was too dependant on groups having clerics, but at the same time clerics were dependant on groups. So it evened out. But some classes were saying that they were feeling neglected by groups, and could not find a group that wanted them. But did they have other avenues? Yes. They could solo. If they did not want to solo, they could create a new character that was more group friendly. At one time, I got tired of needing a group to get any amount of experience, so know what I did? Did I make a big noise that it was unfair? No, I knew going in that clerics were one of the worst classes to solo with. So I made a druid, who were famous for thier soloing abilities. But I found that it was not as fun to solo, so I stopped playing the druid, and found new love for my cleric.

Being so group dependant with my cleric, I learned how groups work, how dungeons work, how other classes work. I know every ability and shortcoming of every class/race by fighting by thier side and seeing the effects of these advantages/disadvantages. Now lets change the game so that a class who has gone 50+ levels by themselves and make them more group friendly. They might be unaware of the subtleties of each class/race. No matter what you do, it will not change this fact. You cannot change a classes role as it was in a game for 2 years overnight. No matter how much they want to adapt to the change, it will adversely effect all classes. Which brings us to what is happening now.

You had good intentions. Classes were complaining that they did not have the abilities of other classes, and wanted 'balance'. I think you got hung up on what balance meant. To me, in the Everquest world, balance meant that each class had its own pluses and minuses. But you started to listen to people who focused on what they could not do, and failed to see what they could do. In order to fully satisfy these people, what you would have to do is convert every class into a generic class, where everyone had exactly the same abilities. That totally goes against the spirit of the game as it was envisioned in the beginning.
I am going to say some not so nice things about druids. Now understand I am not saying this is typical of druids, or that all druids are evil, or that I hate/envy druids. The reason I am using druids to make my point is because of a minority of druids that made a lot of noise and did not understand what thier class was being used for. There are clerics who are just as bad. Every class has its members that will never be happy with what they have and as such ruin it for everyone else in the class. I am using druids because thier complaints are the reason for what the clerical community is suffering right now. Now, druids were never meant to be primary healers, nor were shamen. If they were, you would have given them better healing spells. They were intended to be useful in a pinch. But they had other roles as well. Shamen were known for thier great stat buffs and ability to reduce damage output of mobs. Druids were known for thier great soloing abilities and as secondary buffers/healers. That is how the classes evolved, and that was the status quo. If you made a druid a month ago, you would either know right away or soon after starting, that druids were not the preferred healers at higher levels. It was a fact. If you did not like it, then don 't play a druid. Its like going to university to follow a certain carreer, and then realise that that carreer was not what you really wanted to do with your life. So do you insist that your job description be changed? You would usually, if you were brave enough, just take up another carreer, of suck it up and stay with the carreer you are not totally satisfied with.
I agree that the penalty for casting a complete heal was too low. The game should force you to use some stratagy to be able to cast such a powerful spell. I have seen allusions to the wizard Ice Comet spell, and I am in total agreement. You should have to think a little before casting the spell, to decide if it is really worth the risk to cast it. But if you took the IC spell and caused it to cause so much agro that if it was cast, the wizard would probably die, then know what would happen? The wizard would stop casting the spell and therefor loose its best spell and become obsolete. The woudl no longer be able to find groups (I know, it is hard for wizards as it is). But clerics at high levels HAVE to cast complete heal. When your tank is being whacked for +300 dmg a round, a spell with a 4.5 second cast time that heals 600 hit points does not cut it. Health points drop much faster than you can return them. Causing the death of the tank, and therefor the group. So no matter how much you tweak them, superior heal will never be a spell that will make a druid or shaman a primary healer. The game was designed around the ability to restore all the hitpoints of the tank. To change it would mean a retooling of the entire high level game. I am all for making them more effective healers. But shamen and druids have abilities that clerics don't have, so if they can be primary healers, where does that leave the cleric? Useless. We will become useful only for our ability to restore experience upon death. But you don't need a group to do that. Just sit at the entrance of a zone and you will have all the oppertunity you need to cast that spell. Without a group, you stop getting experience, and stop progression. So the cleric gets shelved in order to play a class that you can progress with, or quit the game. And if it was only clerics, I would be dissapointed, but not angry. We are a minority, and if to appease the masses you need to get rid of a minority, then so be it. Make druids the prime healers of Everquest and forget that you once had a class of armored healers called clerics. Make clerics of innoruk drop druid plane armor and everyone will be happy, except for the clerics, but thats ok, sometimes you need to hurt a few people to help a lot of people.
But your actions are not just against clerics. If you ask any class if they are totally happy with the way thier class is set up, you will always get a no. People by nature are never satisfied. If we were, progress would never be made, and we would become stagnant. And as you add more to one class, you take away from another when you change roles of classes. Let us have our role in Everquest, it is what we know and what we do best. It is a shame that players have become dependant on us but they are also depentant on tanks and enchanters. Are you going to tweak the Necro class so that they can tank or be enchanter clones in order to make them more useful? Will you make Warriors and Monks able to cast spells so that they can be as versatile as a hybrid? Will you make hybrids tougher in order to make them equal in fighting abilities to Warriors and Monks? You must draw a line somewhere. You must preserve the face of norrath. The more you try to please everyone, the more you dissapoint everyone. You have lost sight of the big picture, where everyone has a job and are specialized to do that job. You will have a population of jacks of all trades and masters of none.
Most people assume that these changes effect only uber guilds or players of the highest levels. But it doesn't. Doing things like nerfing the donal's chestplate do effect only a very small portion of players. Things like the heal/agro change effect everyone, if not immediately then at some point down the road. Lets face it, someday the majority of players will be able to go on a Trakanon raid. I remember thinking that I would never be able to go on a Naggy Raid as it was Uber-guild only. But as they find new challenges, they make way for us lesser folks to take what they left. To think that you will never hold an epic weapon or slay one of the uber mobs mentioned in other posts is unrealisitic. And also, this does not only effect uber mob raids. Our guild was part of a Fear raid last week and it was a total mess, as clerics were dropping like flys, for doing what they were meant to do. To say to a cleric don't heal is like telling a warrior not to attack. If we don't do what we are made to do, what do we do? Might as well save our mana and just rez the fallen heroes after the battle. Or be a hero ourselves and heal the tank that will last an extra minute because you sacrified your self.

To sum up what the topic is about, people are leaving not because they are spoiled, or can't adapt to change, or are just having a hissy fit. They are leaving because the very basic aspects of the game they enjoyed are being removed. And clerics are not the only class to feel the bite of class balancing. But if you took a pole as to what class is getting the shaft in this issue, clerics would win. We loose all and gain nothing in return. And when the clerics leave, so do the people who have depended on them. If they can't go on an adventure where winning is a possibility, then they won't go.

I don't know if this will spell the end of Everquest. I do know that the game has changed more in the past month than ever before, and that is not nessesarily a good thing. I am sure that a few of you will continue on, playing the game you love. But a lot will not unless Verant, you change things quickly. I hope you see what is happening and don't think it is just the usual 'nerf flack' you get every change. I logged on today, and for the first time ever, I just sat there, trying to decide if I wanted to play. I told some good friends in my guild that I might not be there much longer. I am just tired. I will play my monk and maybe powerlevel a few twinks of friends but my career as a cleric is over I am afraid. I am sad that I now know that I will never see 60. I am saddened by the fact that something that I enjoyed is gone. I knew the day would come when I would hang up Sonard's spell book, but I never thought it would end like this. Maybe I am over-reacting. Maybe in 2 weeks Sonard will be back at Seb putting the fear of Tunare into frogloks. But unless some changes are made, I don't see it happening. I spent 49 levels finding death at every corner. I don't want to spend my last 8 levels that way. For now, I will play my monk and pray for better days. I just hope there will be someone to heal me when I need it.

Peace

Sonard, Level 52 Cleric
Officer of the wonderful guild Crew
