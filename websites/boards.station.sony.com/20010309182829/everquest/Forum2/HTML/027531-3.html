I find it remarkable that so few people are playing the classes of most historical interest in the fantasy genre.

I notice more than a few people are of the 'it won't happen' and 'its too hard to do' faction. Or the "it isn't vision(tm)."

Um hello? What are you guys, sheep?

I GMd RPGs for 12 years. Like VI my philosophy was "you are in my world now"

But I put it to you all that the following points are so self evident even a spotty teenager opening up TSR AD&D could work it through:

- combat classes should not have weapon restrictions unless they are religious. IE any melee class can dual wield, use blunt, halberds, their little finger.
- some classes will be better than others at executing those skills (monks unarmed, rangers on bow, rogues on assasin weaps etc) - and that skill ability is on a per level basis, not a skill cap basis (SKs have 75 for bow, uhuh...)
- if the game must have 'levels' it should be fair. If a class has inate powers that give it substantial advantages - xp penalty.
(=> necros, enchanters, and clerics not hybrids...)
- if there is an xp penalty apply it to the right people, not everyone else.
- if you can cast spells, either the mana costs more, the xp costs more, or you cast considerably weaker level spells - but to apply the whole lot as well as a limited selection??
- if you force people to have to group then ensure everyone has something to offer, not make them bad substitutes
- does anyone else find it remarkable that the only way to kill dragons in this game is to have huge raid teams? What happened to the ideal of a small party of hardy (albeit stupid) adventurers?
- how do you rationalise pet classes who have pets who are nastier than the melee classes?
- how do you rationalise that the mobs heal, recast, self buff, at rates no PC can match?
- how do you rationalise that the only characters in the game able to cast bind on a player is another player and not an NPC?
- pet foible, how do you rationalise that one of the SKs nastiest weapons of choice is a small blunt thing?
- hell, how do you rationalise a real time game where the way to get xp is to set up camp, and proceed to steadliy slaughter 100+ mobs over four hours, not complete an epic adventure?
- the rot for hybrid problems starts at 40, not 50 - its here that they skill cap out in key skills; weaps, taunt, dodge, offense, defense

Upshot: VI can take a bow for having developed the best RPG game ever, at least in the online sense - but the ride will come to a pretty sudden halt unless they are prepared to have a pretty solid stab at re establishing the basics.

Heres hoping that if they really can't deliver on the stuff above (hey, its their funeral, we'll all just find another game) they do make an EQ2 which actually offers xp for activity, not just mindless killing.

I love the game - but the hybrid situation, ergh.

I sort of doubt this is feasable for a few reasons one of which is technical. Anyway, this would be the ideal, although most difficult, method.
