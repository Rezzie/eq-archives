Well, as the topic says, I'm rather impressed. It's been awhile since I've hunted in the Qeynos newbie zone and since I had started a newly untwinked Barbarian Shaman I figured I'd give it a try. So here we go:

The first thing I did was turn in my newbie note in Halas, train in 1HB, mem my 2 spells, hail Jinkis (whatever his name is) in the Shaman spell. You have to go talk to Cindl and Greta and get 2 items from them and head back to Jinkis and hand him those 2 items. He gives you a poster and tells you to take it to Qeynos to hand to any bank guard. This is an easy trip with Planes of Power. I handed in the poster to the one bank guard and got a scroll in return. From there I went back to Halas and got my Initiate Symbol which summons Hammer of Wrath which is 6/32 Magic summoned 1-Handed Blunt Hammer with a +3 Damage to undead. Nice weapon for the untwinked newbie and a great start.

Back to Qeynos I go for hunting. Between Plague Rat Tails, and all the other miscellaneous items I had enough at level 2 to purchase 4 backpacks with money left-over. I went back to Halas to purchase all of my level 1 spells from there. Back to Qeynos until 5th level. Long story cut somewhat short I came out with a whopping 52pp. Not bad for a level 5 untwinked newbie. Enough money to buy all of my level 5 spells and possibly enough to buy all of my level 9 spells. WOOT. I don't think I was ever that rich as a level 5 character before. hehe

Oh, and the best money makers in North Qeynos are gopher snakes. Avoid the grass snakes at all costs! They tend to poison more and drop less worthy loot.

Grass Snake: Snake Scales (a few silver/copper)
Snake Egg (a few silver/copper)

Gopher Snake: Snake Scales (as described above), Snake Egg (see above), King Snake Jawbone (couple gold and change), King Snake Scales (couple gold and change), King Snake Scales (Couple gold and change), King Snake Skin (a few silver/copper), King Snake Poison Sac (couple gold and change)

As you can see, snakes are really unbalanced out there, just kill the money makers.

Other loot of interest:
Bone Chips: No good to merchants, but take them to the Bazaar.
Skeleton Tibia: Worthless to players, but sell for a couple gold to merchants.
Bat Fur: Couple gold to merchants
Field Rat Skulls: Couple gold to merchants
Plague Rat Tails: Just over a platinum to merchants

All these items, except the Plague Rat Tails, are stackable.

Good luck and good hunting.
