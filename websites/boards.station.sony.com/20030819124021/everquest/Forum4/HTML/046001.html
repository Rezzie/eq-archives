Each city has many quests, and has from the begining.

Halas had a decent cape quest and one for a kick butt warrior weapon.

Qeynos (original '99) had 63 plus/minus quests in the city and catacombs alone.

Neriak had many quests, and like the other cities has more now.

All starting cities have some pretty nice newbie armor quests. The necromancer robes are great.

The Stein of Mogguk was a great quest in the good old days when it took some time and intelligence to figure out how to get from one zone to another and collect the items needed and then live to tell about it. And it yielded a very good item indeed.

Luclin has dozens of quests: featuring some nice newbie armor for beastlords, monks, warriors in SWT along with decent shields, and weapons. And escalating on up to the Twilight Sea Armor, which is good for all classes, and can be worked through the mid 30s and up.

I was in Twilight Sea, and the adjoining city several times last week. One time and one time only, I saw other players in TS. I never saw one in the city.

There are many quests in PoP. The ring and girdle are decent for mid level 20s.

There are, and always have been solo and group quests (dungeons)in EQ. But, too many do not want to take the time to 'do' them. Why bother, what with the Bazaar and multi-questing and the ever naseating cry of 'time sink' ringing in one's ears.

I am well aware that many of the quests are too 'dated' now, the end results when compared to the items available now not worth working to get.
However, some of these are good for the experience, or just plain fun.

The fun seems to be missing lately from questing-to many hard working levelers, time sinkers, multi-questers, etc.

And, there is the new cry I hear a lot of lately, 'hey, man it don't take a rocket scientist to play eq'...of course not, not when you take the short cuts and out-right cheats that abound now.

Just my two cents, but the quests that are in the game are pretty much ignored now. Why release more?
