Breeze,

The problem that you suggested was considered, but it *really* isn't the case. Basically, everyone is back to losing the same percentage of the experience required for the level at which it was computed. In other words, if a cleric loses 20% of the experience for the previous level (a bit extreme), so will the paladin, but the paladin's is a larger number (Since he required more experience).

It evens out when everyone makes their experience back since the paladin gets more experience per kill than the cleric, but that additional experience doesn't come out of the cleric's share, but is dynamically created.

Now, even if the paladin does lose a level, level loss is of course something that happens in EverQuest, but the percentage difference we are talking about is not that extreme. In a group with 5 40s (six person group total), the a 40 paladin would get an equal share (prior to the addition to make up for the penalty). In a group of 5 40s, a level 39 paladin would get only 0.3% (.003) less experience than his counterparts (prior to the addition to offset the penalty).

The math here gets a bit hairy to explain, I hope I didn't confuse the issue more. I went with full-disclosure in the letter above, so be assured that there isn't some dynamic to the formulae that will result in anyone taking longer to level or recover from death than they do now. It is across the board faster for everyone, unless perhaps you don't have an experience penalty and NEVER group with anyone who does, in which case it would be the same.

-Gordon
