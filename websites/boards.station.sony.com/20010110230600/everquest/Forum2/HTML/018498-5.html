Hi all,

I was thinking about a few comments I read here, saying that a memorial is out of place in a game like Everquest. The thing is, I too was saddened by this death of a person I never met, either in or out of game. I don't know why, but i guess it is because of the kind words spoken by people that knew her. And then because someone that was part of my community died, even though I never met him.

In either case, when people are sad in someone's death, I believe that this is the end of the matter. Feelings are difficult to explain. If a person here feels sad about this death, then another person who doesn't will try in vain to convince him that his feelings are unreasonable. Usually they are.

People keep whispering to themselves on these boards that 'this is only a game'. It is a game, but it isn't _only_ a game, it is also a community. There is no difference between a game and real life, because the game is played by real people. It involves real human beings that interact and communicate, make friendships and break them, develop feelings towards each other exactly like the players in a basketball team, or the players in an orchestra, or any other leisure community that belongs to the 'real word'.

Communities develop their own code of communication, and pay their respect to members of the community using this code. If a member of the basketball team is lost, his team-mates will dedicate him the next victory. A victory in a game? Is this their way to mourn for him? They might even lock his shirt away, so that noone will play again with his number. When teams keep a minute of silence before the match begins, they're bringing their mourning into the game, because the game was a small part of the real life of the deceased and the avenue through which his team-mates got to meet him. It is their communication code in their special community. In another community with a different code, the same message could be conveyed by having a computer animation carry an animated flare between two co-ordinates on a grid. For some people this code gets translated to something meaningful. For others it doesn't.

All if this is called symbolism, and it makes no sense other than making us feel that our goodbye is exactly that, more 'meaningful'. We didn't know you as a person, but we know you to be a part of our community, and through this community we're saying goodbye.

Nothing in this life is 'not real'. Everything is real, and we never judge the reality of things, only their subjective importance to us.
It is said that J.R.Tolkien's tombstone says 'Beren' and his wife's next to him, says 'Luthien'.

Elendar
