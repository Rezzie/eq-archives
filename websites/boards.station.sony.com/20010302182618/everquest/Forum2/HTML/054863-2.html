Just wanted to drop in and say a few things.

First on the Managirl thing, I know this is very "political" issue with people having strong feelings either way. I didn't write this story for her, I wrote this story cause I like to write. I just felt that the space for my usual second part of the story would be better used to spread awareness of it. I don't ask for you to agree with me, in fact I don't ask anything. I just want to say my piece and give my opinion which I hope everyone here is entitled to agree. I came back today and found 20 different threads on the issue, so I'm happy :P Maybe something will happen and maybe it won't, but I've done what I can.

In any case, there's no need to attack anyone here. I know not everyone agrees with me and probably everyone who comes and posts to this board should know that.

As for the people who don't feel that the players own the game...I'm sorry. There is a big difference between something like the Final Fantasy series (my favorite one, which I played back when the first american version came out for the original Nintendo system) which tells an amazing story and does a great job of immersing you in a world, and Everquest.

In Final Fantasy, as in most RPGs, you can be brought into a world, but it's not your world, it's theirs. And just like Zindane you do a stand in for a while, act on a stage, play a roll...and when the story is good enough and the graphics amazing it feels like you're part of a movie, and that's great. I've loved that since I was eight.

That's not Everquest.

The programmers in Everquest don't make a world, they paint a backdrop upon which a world evolves. If you want some logical reason I can think of one...Go to Mith Marr and play there, then play the exact same character on Bertox. Those are two different worlds. The backdrop is the same, same pixels and polygons, but the worlds are different. Logically that would tell you that the people must make the worlds.

But if you need logic to guide you, you've already missed the point entirely.

Verant might own the hardware and copyrights and all, but at the very most you can't argue it's theirs. Just as in any other product in a capitalist democratic society, Verant must cater to the will of the people. Brad is not unlike the President of the US. He might run the show, he can kick people off for writing or saying things, or finding exploits, he could buff Tunare until she was invincible or make her as difficult as a fire beetle. But he's still a person at a company. He has to make money like everyone else. He has to ensure the future profitability of Everquest, so he has to listen to consumers.

He's a servant of the people.

Right now, at this very instant Alan is probably reading discussion of class threads, dozens of programmers are working to execute OUR ideas. The entire concept of Class Talk Threads shows it's our game.

It's our world. I tip my hat to those who made the backdrop, but without us Everquest would be as methodic as a cuckoo clock and as transparent as Velium.

But outside that, we really have no right to Verant's world. We do not buy the copyright to Everquest by paying to play it. That's the point I'm trying to get across, and the point many players are forgetting in their hysteria over how the Managirl situation was dealt with.

Yes, it was the GM's fault for doing bad customer service, but outside petitions and quitting our accounts, we have got nothing on Verant. Players who criticize Verant as a whole forget that it is Verant that brought the game to us. We have every right to complain, but we must never forget that it is their game, they have labored hard and long to craft it, and in the end this is their creation and they are lending it to us for a price. How can we sit here and tell Verant that it's now our game and they should appease us when we had no stake in its creation outside populating it(which is, in the end, only a part of EQ)?
