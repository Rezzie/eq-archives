All right, due to the impression that you all have given me, it seems that I need to cover wizards next…
I’ve already made several notes to myself about wizards, and I may as well start out this discussion with those notes. Keep in mind that these ‘talks’ are geared toward making sure that the classes are ‘balanced’. Please work toward that goal when posting (yes, even if that means you suggest a ‘nerf’). But I’m also interested in hearing about bugs and such too, I’m just not going to focus on them.

Here are the notes that I already have. Keep in mind that these are only my notes, and just because I’ve listed something does NOT mean that I like the idea or that the designers are working on making changes based on these notes. This is just what it is, a list of ideas. I’ve also added my notes to you all under the header of Note:

AoE spells: Mentioned elsewhere… (earlier on my list, probably under mages: basically the idea here is that maybe pets should be removed from the equation).

Gaze/Glimpse: Due to the clipping plane, these spells don’t really allow further seeing, just zooming in (which makes your resolution worse anyhow).

Note: this may not be ‘fixable’ at all due to the way the game works. It’s not like we can force your PC into a higher resolution when you use this spell…

Fade/Shadowstep/Yonder: There seems to be a problem with these spells. Since they will not teleport you up, they don’t work too well in hilly terrain; because the spell only sends you a few feet (stopping when it reaches a hill or building).

Note: Again, this may also not be something we can change, but it’s on the list.

Bind Sight: Seems to cause viewer to see the target as running into walls or spinning in circles if target goes up an incline.

Enstill: It seems that this spell is not a more useful or more powerful root, it’s just more expensive.

Cazic Gate: At level spell is attained, using it will most often cause death because the caster appears amidst many aggressive monsters that /con white to them.

Note: We’ve actually discussed this before. If I remember correctly, the general feeling was that having a dangerous gate like this wasn’t necessarily a bad thing. But it is also still on my list.

Tishan’s Relocation: This spell evacuates you to a very dangerous location, a monster crossroads in the Skyfire mountains. Could the location be moved, or was it intended that this be a very dangerous evacuation spell?

Note: See note about Cazic Gate.

Concussion: A level 39 spell dropped by a creature in a location that requires a party of level 50s to handle. Should it be in such a location?

Icestrike effects: Graphical effect looks like fire.

Lightning Bolt effects: looks exactly like Fire Bolt.

Other lightning and cold effects: It seems that there might be other spells that have the look of fire when they should not.

Having just re-read this list, I see nothing at all related to actual balancing stuff. This is all nit-picky stuff about minor effects and bugs and such (with the possible exception of Area of Effect spells, changing those would certainly have an effect on class balances). So, please, try to focus on balance issues…

Alan

Brief statement of some concerns about the wizard class.
The biggest negative concerns about the wizard class.

1) Many wizards do not feel that they are adding much if any value to their groups.
2) Wizards are not ‘pulling there load’ vs. velious uber mobs, 100k+ hit point mobs make the damage a wizard can deal, very mute.
3) A wizard has a very hard time staying in a battle due to very long med times.
4) Due to many of the enhancement in the various melee classes, wizards can no longer keep up with the damage being dealt by some of the melee classes, both in speed and damage.
5) Wizards lose a lot of power due to not having a pet, though giving us one is not a solution many wizards want, or what I am going to ask for. Pet classes at least can offer their pet as a source of value to a group even when they are oom.
6) Downtime due to medding is the single biggest drawback, and a down fall to this class. I can agree to some of this to some degree.

I would like to point out some of the positive points of the class. Which I don’t feel any more change is necessary in to obtain or maintain balance. A change in any of these areas I feel would create over powering in one area or another.

1) Equally fast Evocation spells to other classes, the 3 second cast of DD spells, and 2 second cast of PB AE spells, is enough to fill the wizards role as masters of quick damage, in any group AE or single pull situations.
2) Spell Damage compared to other classes is slightly higher then most other classes spells or has a lower cast time and mana usage factor, therefore this maintains are title of masters of evocation.
3) AE spell Damage or delay is exceptional nice, so for the extremely rare groups where these spell work we fit nicely.
4) Overall efficiency of the class is good, compared to battles against single mobs with DD spells or multiple mobs with AE spells.

What can be done to the wizard class?

Make a change to the way Harvest works. Bare with me please this is slightly different then most ideas you have previously read.

Cut the mana regained from Harvest in half to 150 maximum, but then also cut the recast time on the spell to 4 ½ minutes. This is not really any different then the spell currently works, however the real change needed is below.

Have Harvest take the number of members in the group and multiply it times the amount of mana normally gained if not grouped. So for example, if I normally gain 150 points of mana from Harvest (solo), if I am grouped with 6 other people I would Harvest 900 points of mana.
This is 4 nukes, or 2,652 extra points of Damage every 4 ½ minutes at level 55, which is about the same amount of damage a mage or necro with there pet is going to out damage me by in this time frame.

Before I go any farther someone other then me came up with this idea and the good reasons why, but I liked it so much I decided to make sure someone reads this. I edited the whole thing but only slightly, so this may or may not reflect the orginial authors ideas.

1) This will help us to keep up with the tanks and pets in damage output.
2) From a role-play sense, we gather mana from are surrounding. So this should be the people and the magical items around us, the best and easiest measurement would be how many people are in are group.
3) Doing so would not allow us to solo something we couldn’t before.
4) Encourage us to find a group.
5) It doesn’t become a PvP issue.
6) Its more fun casting nukes then sitting on are butts doing nothing, holding just enough back for evacs.
7) Would help us stay in the long battles with Velious Uber Mobs, battles lasting up to a hour.
8) Would not be over powering on previous mobs, as I feel a gap is becoming larger, between melee efficency and caster.

56 Wizard of the Nameless

Hopefully this thread leads to some meaningful changes for the wizard class. Eight months ago I parked the wizard that was my first character, and I have refused to go back ever since. The issues that drove me to stop playing my wizard were these:

1) I couldn’t cast on a mob that was red to me, meaning two or more levels higher. When that happened, my role in the group was relegated to sitting and watching the rest of my group fight while I collected free experience. I can tell you from experience that after three or four pulls where you don’t contribute anything, you WILL be disbanded from a group.
1) My mana was good for about five or six casts of my best damage spell. Since this was the case, my normal routine was to cast my biggest spell, followed by my midrange spell, then sit and meditate until close to the end of the combat, at which point I again stood up to cast my highest damage spell. Three spells were hardly contributing that greatly to the groups damage output, but it was all I could afford mana and aggro wise.
1) On the occasion where my group overpulled and I needed to cast my big damage spells, without care for what my mana supply was, I still couldn’t contribute as greatly as I would have liked to. Chain casting of big damage spells would aggro whatever I was casting on, and my life span would be all of about five seconds.

There are a few things that I think should be changed in order to restore balance and worth to the wizard class, and none of them involve infringing upon any other classes abilities. We don’t need increased defense skill, we don’t need to backstab or wear heavier armor, and we don’t need Uberspell_00 that class X has in order to be playable. What we do need is for the powers that be to recognize that our role is to deal the highest magic-caused damage of any class, nothing else. We can’t buff, heal, debuff, mez or charm. Our job is to make the mobs hurt like no one else can, and if we get 100% resists on things that are two or three levels above us, we can’t do the one and only thing that our class is able to contribute to the group.

With all that in mind, here are the things that I propose in order to help the wizards out.

1) We should be able to effect any mob within seven or so levels of our own. Before anyone objects, let me say that there are other classes that can do exactly that with their DD’s. There is no reason that we shouldn’t be as good or better than any other class at effecting higher level mobs than ourselves. The excuse that post 50 we get lure spells isn’t one that holds any water. We have to first get to 50th level, a task which is made harder by how difficult it is for us to find a group, and by the increased resists that we get at pre-Kunark levels.
1) We need some sort of quick cast, low mana spell that lowers our aggro to the mobs. That way if we choose to cast multiple DD’s without concern for our mana, we can do so without needing a cleric to Res us for the next battle.
3) This third item is more a suggestion than a need, but I still think that it has real merit. The mana cost should be reduced for all of our direct damage spells by twenty-five to thirty-three percent. At first the percentages sound high, but consider this; Most wizards can cast about six of their highest damage spells before being out of mana. Decreasing the cost by a third would allow us to cast eight spells, which is certainly not a game unbalancing amount of damage. It is well accepted that as things stand in EQ today, wizards are not the highest damage dealers of the caster classes. All that we can lay title to is the ability to cause the most damage in the least amount of time, assuming that the mobs would actually let us live long enough to let us cast all of our spells on them.

I hope this post reaches the right ears, I know that with the hundreds of others, some things can get lost in the confusion.

There is nothing that we can do that another caster can't do almost as well. Yes, we have slightly better damage to mana ratios, slightly better casting times, and portals/evacs. Magicians can deal 580 to 600, (which is direct, not a bolt), and they have a combination DD and DOT spell that deals 600. Where in this do you perceive an advantage to Wizards that compensates them for not having a pet, or a debuff, or party friendly buffs.

The worst thing about this situation is that I doubt you can do anything about it. The Wizard class requires major revamping, and EQ is far too entrenched in its current setup for that to occur. We should have been MUCH better at DD spells than the other classes. Take the Cleric for example (since they are up for discussion as well). They receive Superior Healing a full 19 levels ahead of the other priest classes, and that defines the Cleric as a master of healing. All the original defining abilities of the Wizard class have proven to be false. Therein lies the biggest problem of class balance that Wizards face...we aren't the masters of anything.

You can't increase our mana pool, neither can you increase the damage we deal without changing the balance of player vs. monster encounters. I hope you find a solution, I really do. Maybe allowing criticals is the best solution, who knows. I will continue to play EQ for the months to come, because there are a lot of things you have done very well, but I am disappointed with what my chosen class has become...or rather never actually became.

There is NO COMPELLING REASON to play a wizard.

I just lost an extremely detailed and lengthy post due to last thread being closed. Don't have the energy to repeat it entirely.

Just wanted to state the biggest balance issue has to do with DD equalization among all classes, particularly at 44th and 54th, as Alavair mentions above (for 44th). If you do the comparisons with all casting classes at 54th you will see the same thing. All casting classes doing extremely similar damage, for virtually the same mana, and just with various casting times and a few classes having some slight hold delays. This is not adequate balance given the two huge things wizards give up to be masters of DD. Pets & Healing.

We do not want pets or healing, but we do want our DDs balanced with our narrow focus in mind. Our DDs MUST do more damage across the board, and particularly on our 51+ draught line in order for the classes to be balanced. We want to be able to do subsantially more damage to foes than other casters. Having a cleric DD do the same damage is unacceptable even with twice the cast time and a few second hold time. This does NOT balance having a heal spell which can heal 3000+ damage for 400 mana. Having a spell which casts 1 second faster than a druid for a miniscule mana/damage ratio improvement does NOT compensate for their being able to heal their comrades for 600 damage at the same level, in addition to all the support buffs, evacs, regeneration, damage shields, etc.

Our DDs are the fundamental issue of class balance. They are inadequate. Yes, I can revert back to Ice Comet for a bigger single blast, this is not acceptable given it goes back to a poorer ratio of mana/damage. In the 51+ game the draughts are our bread and butter DD and they should be substantially more powerful than other casters, not virtually the same. In many long dungeon fights I am casting only 1 or 2 DDs per foe in order to maintain mana for an emergency burndown. In those situations a cleric or druid or shaman or enchanter can do exactly as much damage as I can, and virtually as efficienty. And being able to burn up all your mana very quickly to hurt a single foe (in Kunark) and barely scratch some foes (in Velious) is also unacceptable.

Rains are a good potential group-friendly alternative to doing more damage to a single target for less mana. These are really only useful in groups, so I love these spells. But for rains, the resist rates MUST be looked into. These spells definitely have a resist bonus vs. DDs or at the very least we don't get our wizard bonus to foe's saving against our damage spells with them. Whereas I almost never have more than a single DD resist on regular foes, it is the norm to have 1 wave of a rain resisted, often 2 waves, and sometimes 3. This makes them almost identical mana/damage ratio as DD, but with longer cast time and a hold time.

We want to be damage dealers above other classes, and particularly above healing classes. That's the most important balance issue. There are clerics at my level who have 930 AC to my 567 AC, they can use their pet hammer and chain-stuns to kill a blue-con foe with half the mana it would take me. I do not ask that you nerf any other classes, you guys put out these ridiculously powerful DD spells to all casting classes already and it's too late to take them away. But ours need to be made more powerful accordingly. DD damage has become far too cheap a commodity.

Well, I have played a wizard for well over a year now and recently quit playing him. Part of my quitting the wizard was because of the high level politics on my server and the other and biggest part was because in comparison to most classes I felt obsolete. I am not going to say how much better so and so class is than a wizard, I am just going to suggest a few changes for the class that might help bring fun and effectiveness back to the class.

1) Mana is the biggest problem with a wizard. When you are out of mana, you have 0 use to the group. This running out of mana problem should not happen unless you are soloing or fighting one of the bigger mobs. The solution I propose to this is that you give wizards a spell which is like a dot, but rather than having a fixed amount of damage to the spell, it does damage over time as long as the wizard has mana. What I am saying here is rather than casting a dot, like splurt for example, which takes mana up front and does damage until it has done the amount it is allowed, this spell would rather cost 0 mana to cast and over time it would drain the wizard's mana pool as it does damage to a mob. There are many possibilities with this spell, like having it do more damage as the wizard runs low on mana, or vice versa or even giving a fixed amount of damage per mana point taken. The downfall to casting this spell would have to be something drastic like making the spell wear off if the wizard moves in any way whatsoever for balancing reasons. But adding a spell like this for wizards would fix the problem with running out of mana on the big mobs in the game because they would slowly be doing damage to the mob as the entire fight goes on, rather than just dumping mana into him within the first 3-5 minutes. I'm not going to go into what I think the numbers for mana/damage on this spell should be because I do not know what would be balancing, all I will suggest is that my wizard can dish out roughly 6-8k of damage when full med depending on resists.

2) Druid roots are now better than wizard roots since you put in the patch to fix them. This may not be all in truth, but my entire career as a wizard I have been next to a druid and his roots will outlast mine in any circumstance almost 99% of the time. The second problem with root is that my level 4 root works better than any of the roots from there to 49. To fix this, simply add a minimum amount of time a mob can be rooted based on the level of the spell.

3) A lot of people complain about Cazic gate, but I don't think the real problem they have with it is that it is unsafe, more along the lines that you gate in there at say 50+ and you can get anywhere from 1 to 10 green mobs agro on you that should be running in fear when they see you.

4) Rain spells, take pets out of the equation and you have them fixed.

5) As for the pet spell that you gave us, in my opinion it is completely useless. Although I am not high enough level to cast the spell, I have friends who are high enough and seen it used and honestly it is a waste of 700 mana, not to mention the only conjuration spell "worth" using as a wizard.

6) Wizards with a single target snare? Leave that to the druids.

7) As for wizards taking damage and their agro, concussion was a good idea, but remove the line of spells that deal with sight or whatever they are and add in a line of concussion spells that get better as they go up in levels.

Anyway, these are just things off the top of my head.
