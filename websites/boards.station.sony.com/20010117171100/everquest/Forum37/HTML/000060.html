I play a necro, a mage, and an enchanter (not really a pet class). What I can say about PVP with pets :

Necros: dont get root before level 34, Fear doesnt work. Dont try to kill the pet, you will take a while to kill him, and then all the while the necro drains/dots/etc... Head for the necro: low HP (even lower with mana conversion line spells), low armor, low defense. They die quickly.

Mages: no root, they dont DOT, so just jump them before they DD you, hard to cast when a warrior dualwields / double attacks.

Melee classes have more HP, with good resist gear a caster and his pet alone are no match to a warrior.

The enchanter can be tricky, he has mez and such spells that block you, but if you attacked him and he has a pet, his pet will break the mez so ...

Pets are dangerous if you try to take them down, but they die when the caster dies, the caster dies quickly: in the end, the fight doesnt last long but you lose as much HP as you would meleeing a warrior class.

The pet is really only useful when you are grouped in PVP, in the confusion of battle he is another shield.

What I would add about Necros, is that if they can DOT you, and you have no pumice/way to remove the DOTs, he doesnt need a pet, he just shadow steps away then goes invis to track you down, reapplying DOTs if needed.

For Soloing, sure the pet helps. In dungeons, well, sometimes. I have been killed in Highkeep because my pet decided to go kill the NPC three floors above me (they dont seem to care for the Z axis). In the middle of the fight it just went away, and came back with a train of guards. Or once when I put it on /pet sit down he just woke up and went to kill the guard...

So, Pets on PVP are not much a balance problem. But well, thats my opinion...

What I would care about is that Wizard sniper: invisible somewhere at safe distance, targetting you, and shooting you from far away with nasty LOS DD's: if you run to him you are dead, better run the other way.
