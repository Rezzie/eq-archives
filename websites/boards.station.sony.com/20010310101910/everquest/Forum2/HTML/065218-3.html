Well, two basic ideas have been stated here. One is the idea that there should be a 51-60 board that should only be accessible by people of that level. I don't think this is appropriate at all. After all, if people are giving *specific* examples (i.e. wizard X has Y mana, casts Z spell that does A damage against monster B that has C hit points), why shouldn't a warrior be able to comment effectively on that? If the wizard is level 40 or 60, I can still make valid commentary on the situation since the FACTS were given (whether I am level 20 or 60 and whether I'm a warrior or wizard).

The problem lies on both sides (the 1-50 people and the 51-60) and with most people who post here, in that when someone complains they rarely BOTHER to give all the salient facts. Most of the w***e posts just say "I don't like <X> because it [is unbalanced/is unfair/is too hard/sucks]." How can ANYONE, level 1 or level 60, comment effectively on this kind of post? Even THIS post is an example. It suggests that we should have a 1-50 board and a 51-60 board that are segregated so that low-level people cannot comment on 51-60 "issues." Yet, it does not give examples of ideas that were rejected because of 1-50 comment, nor does it cite any evidence that 1-50 input has ruined the game for 51-60 people. Despite being many levels from 51, I can still comment on things like taunt, be it level 1 taunt or level 60 taunt, because I have the capability to understand the problems being discussed. Further, my input IS important because I *will* be level 51 at some point, and why should changes be made to that part of the game be made without my input? If I get to level 51 and find out that things were changed that it turns out I *don't* like, which were discussed on this "high-level" board, why didn't *I* have a shot at offering input?

This idea is simply yet another idea from a person who wants to stifle dissent about their issues. "Anyone who disagrees with me is a newbie and they shouldn't have input about MY issues." This is the same argument that individual classes have about people NOT of their classes commenting on their issues. "You don't play a [insert any class here], so you don't know what you're talking about." I could have played with that class every day for six months, but there are STILL people who would say that I know nothing about the class. I could have 20 friends (guildmates, perhaps?) who I talk with a lot about the 51-60 game (and who are there now), yet it would be *assumed* by my level that I don't really *know* anything about it. All of these factors militate towards NOT splitting people off according to level. Just because you don't like their opinion doesn't mean the "newbie" doesn't have a valid idea or argument, and the fact that I have never been there doesn't mean that I am unable to comment intelligently on a specific situation (which is what we ALL should be discussing here, instead of all the over-generalized w***ing.

The second idea posted here was to post everyone's characters and levels as a "signature" on their posts. I would never return to the boards given that kind of change (you can stop applauding now ). I do not NEED to be tracked down in-game and harassed because I am one of those who agrees with the naming policy (to use one of the more controversial examples) and will report names that bother me. I do not NEED to be tracked down in-game and harassed by that 15-year-old kid that I categorized as a troll because he won't stop posting a new "Verant Sucks" post every other day. I do not NEED to be ostracized from groups because of *whatever* opinion I posted here. Those results would be a big hassle and I don't want to have to start all new characters in-game.

The OTHER reason that this idea is wrong is because is leads to similar problems as segregating the boards. If everyone sees that X player's highest character is 40, then there are many people who will just sit back and assume that this person is just a newbie who knows nothing about the game. While it's true that the person might not know AS MUCH as the higher level person, that alone does not detract from the quality and/or validity of their particular argument. They *could* be making a very valid point that would be dismissed (and likely flamed) out of hand just bsaed on the player's level. Most *reasonable* discussions require that you listen (read) and think about everyone's point before you agree or disagree. Unfortunately, this isn't a place for *reasonable* discussion. It is too often a place for rationalization, ego stroking, whining and flaming Verant.

This isn't because under-51 people post here and it isn't because the board is "integrated." It's because the collective IQ (at least in terms of what is written) rarely rises above the bare minimum required to feed oneself with a spoon.
