There are EQ contenders coming soon after Scars of Velious (Wich I have ordered already, because I do play this game, though its hard at times to say I enjoy it anymore...

But competition aside, EQ is headed for a downfall if Verant maintains and develops EQ by current standards.

They game, nearing 2 years old, and patched monthly or so, is riddled with bugs, incomplete quests, and crowding issues.

The pace of the game is on a down trend from lvl one, excruciating even for untwinked characters. Every aspect of play and system seems purposefully tweaked in illogical imbalance between PC and NPC. There is no platform for roleplaying in the game, except as a humongous hurdle to progress. One cannot explore until one has achieved leves, and in some cases equipment. Most of the quests are a waste of time, a lot are broken, and many are down right insulting (Sap Sheen staff comes to mind). The NPC's come in 3 versions, all being over powered monks. Some of the over powered monks can also cast spells. Then there are the merchants, who can also be either of the other types of overpowered monks. I find the official response to this issue detestable. If advancing characters and aquiring items isnt the main point of fullfillment in the game, and Role-playing is near impossible, what is the point of the game? Entertainment? It dwindles severely, mostly held up by interaction with friends at later levels. Fantasy is severely drowned in drudgery, and ultimately assassinated when the hero, you, can never slay a dragon without a huge raid lag fest, much less alone (define Hero here now), and all but the most seasoned and well equipped can be quickly throttled by a non-equipped vendor of baking goods, and any of the zillion other members of the coalition of trades folk. Most of this tweaking is to make up for poor programming (pathing and AI are programming issues, plain and simple).

Customer Service in and out of game leaves much to be desired. For not one issue I have had, has a GM or guide been of assistance, and like these boards, the frequent response to player issues is that of generalized and matter-of-fact condescension to the tune of "if its good enough for me...".

The server economies are completely backwards. Maybe 1000 merchant NPC's, and most commerce takes place in East Commons. Vendor equipment is substandard and ferociously over priced. Trade skill products are mostly worthless, and those of value are still substandard and overpriced (not to mention excruciating to develop). The extreme end of these skills that do produce signifigant products (when they do, havent seen any race armor yet) is garnered by the highest level'd pc's in the game.

The game removes nearly any measure of player skill from progress in the game. Especially in tactics is this venomous, where past 30, there are very few and very set ways to make progress. Acquiring items is always opposite of acquiring experience.

The game story is set in stone, none of the dynamic events (beyond there other problems) alter it in a year or so, and there is no GM to player interaction. Beyond that, its not original, and I think JRR Tolkien, TSR, and WW should snub them openly for their plagiarism abnd adulteration of the genre.

On top of that, if SoV is by proportion as laggy and connection trouble as Kunark is compared to nonKunark, they will need to limit zone size to Misty Thicket and zone population to 25.

From top down Verant has allowed their time as a virtual monopoly to atrophy whatever creativity, and novelty was left at their studios when EQ was released.

I mean really, the main interface with npc's is combat, consideration (near useless aspect as far as measuring an npc), and pidgeonholed nonsensical conversation which most of the NPC's are incapable of. Since Combat takes on 3 forms after 25-35, then basically, all three facets of interface are mostly cosmetic.

Someone should start a players union. 300,000 people would probably get some questions answered and bugs fixed.

If they dont progressively deal with at least some of these issues, there will be a great deal of customer base for the next MMORPG that comes out. It is sad that MS could not have done better with AC, but it seems that AC was more of a test of the MMORPG waters done by some insignificant segment of Microprose. UOL is even sadder, they betrayed their customer base by relaesing a 1980's game in 1990's graphics and calling it new. Hopefully future companies that want into this arena will look at these three and seriously consider implementing a very large amount of variation, dynamics, and Player-skill-based interface.

Yeah semper, but Verant has shown that when they enhance the game a la kunark they don't make it MORE playable with less downtime, they make it LESS playable with TONS more downtime.

Everything that verant adds is virtually always negative. Players know this.

I'd like to see you go from 51 - 52 in 24 hours solo ; )

I don't think 60 percent of the people playing NOW will be on in six months even. I also don't think that the critical "new people trying it" will be coming to a game that they hear is high downtime when serveral other choices are availible. It comes down to what choice fits you the best. EQ fits me and a lot of other people horribly.

Do I expect constant pulling and zero downtime all the time? No. The thing is that in EQ that is not the only downtime, everything else in the game is downtime ALSO. Traveling time is one of the BIGGEST downtime enhancers, especially in kunark. The BEST choice you have is a port to kunark from a druid or wiz friend, one port, to a far off area from nearly everywhere you'd be going at 51 - 60 EXCEPT karnors. Your worst choice is the ONLY other way to get there, a usually half hour to up to 45 minutes or MORE boat ride.

Dying without a cleric on hand (which is not always availible ) equals more downtime. It can equal QUITE a bit more downtime in kunark to get one out there if you even can within 3 hours. Without a 90 percent res 51 - 60 playing gets pretty impossible. If you work it out so you ALWAYS have a cleric friend on the same time as you who ALWAYS groups with you thats good, you are one of the lucky ones.

Trades, which should be an alternative to constant downtime adventuring, actually INCREASE downtime a lot since the trade skills patch. Now if my char wants to be able to make a cultural armor, I have to kill something not worth xp for hours and hours since it hardly drops the item anyway.

Quests are one of the biggest downtime complaints in eq since they are often patience and downtime based rather than difficulty based. You go to a spawn and wait, and wait, and wait if you want the item. Wander by? No, that doesn't work since someone else would be there waiting OR someone else would have wandered by.

Verant's track record is bad. It shows undeniably that to them patience + downtime = difficulty rather than just the levels of creatures itself. They have shown this with quests, trades and item drops. If they mixed some of the stuff for patience and some not, it would have been better but their design seems to be when in doubt make it patience and downtime based. Put in new trade stuff, make it patience and downtime based rather than just doing the logical, fair and player requested skill based.

A new line of armor inbetween what drops on low stuff and high stuff which a player can make is a good idea to fill the hole. Making such armor need items so incredibly rare that the value of the armor shoots HIGHER THAN items which are BETTER and often sold is bad. It is a design flaw.

The cultural armor problems show me that Verant is continuing this method of downtime + patience design rather than getting away with it. Other games sound like they are going the opposite way, since they too have seen the mistakes in EQ. Meanwhile Verant is an old stuck in her ways figure standing on a hill lecturing us why we can't have it easier and how we would not really want less downtime and more fun. Well i guess Verant will find out.

Players will not play a stuck in downtime dinosaur when they have better choices. Watch.
