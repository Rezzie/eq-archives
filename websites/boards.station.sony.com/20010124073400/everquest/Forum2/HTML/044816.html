Hear me out on this, it's not meant to insult or support the statement you guys are making, but it is an interesting theory:

I think the "vision" is that the epic items don't go to everyone, it doesn't even go to those who put efforts into it. It goes to those who are fortunate or lucky enough to be somehow blessed with seeing the beast and slaying him.

If they made Faydedar a triggered spawn then everyone would be able to get the epic item. What is the point of being epic or special then? Everybody in an uber guild could get one by the end of the week. It'll just be another matter reserved only for the uber elites.

Afterall, fortune and fate plays a huge enough role in stories compared to actual conscious diligence. The hero who saves the world is often blessed with some form of luck one time or another in his career. For example, a warrior might find a magical sword, or he might escape some dreadful traps through pure luck, or meet some powerful sage who teaches him how to fight, etc. Those things don't just happen to anyone who wanted it to happen or even had the heart to make it happen.

If the logic that "with enough people and resources, we should be able to do anything" holds true in this case, then the hero would never defeat the dark lord, especially in those novels where the dark lord is always stronger than the hero in some way or another.

Verant had proved this again and again with their once-in-a-life-time rare spawns such as Bilge or Pyzyin or Quillmane, who, while easy to kill and carrying loot that surpasses those gained by slaying monsters ten times their strength, are so rare that only a few people could ever attain those items. Heck, some of these spawns are so rare that even with 100+ days played I have yet to see any of them. I've never seen Bilge, never seen Quillmane, never seen Faydedar, never even seen the Guano Harvester even though I've spent alot of time in Solusek B.

Don't think Verant designed those just to spite the players who want those items. They had a purpose in mind, and for players to camp 3+ monthes in a zone and return empty handed is part of it. You ever read about those treasure hunters who spend their entire life looking for something, but then some novice to the trade comes in and steals their lifetime of work by having the luck to discover it? Maybe somethings are simply luck, and just matter of being at the right place in the right time.

I am probably wrong, but I remembered when I used to spend 20+ hours in SRO waiting for the AC to spawn, in a period of almost a week. Thing was, even though I spent 20+ hours and haven't killed him once, some level 30ish newbie came in one day and got the AC after only 30 mins of waiting. Yet all the same, I died one day in Permafrost and was bound exactly at the skeletal ruins in Queynos Hills. By some grace, Pyzyin was up and I nuked her: boom, GBS and I never even really had to camp for it.

Is it fair? No, but then life is often unfair, and it is the element of chance that makes life exciting. It is this element of chance that could, perhaps one day, fall upon you and grace you with its touch. One day, an item might drop from one of the uber dragons, and somehow you might have the fortune to be the only person there to claim it.
