I think this is something that everyone needs to be aware of and get involved in. There should be time for everything of importance in your life, job/work, family and relationships. EQ should only be part of that mix in terms of being entertainment or hobby. Before EQ, how much time did you spend on both entertainment and hobbies for an entire week combined? I'll wager its far less that what the average EQ addict spends in one day playing. I know, I used to be super addicted myself playing anywhere from 12-18 hours per day sometimes. In the process, I lost a good paying job and a few friendships.

This is also something Verant should consider addressing with how they are handling in-game issues. EQ would require less time if they had a better system for binding and travel. If camp-time for items was lowered people would suddenly free up 50% of the time spent on EQ. There are tons of other things that could reduce the amount of time one spends in game. Hobbiest painter's don't like to see a picture half-finish. EQer's probably feel the same way about the next level or item they want or need.

In order to get over the addiction you need to do one thing, QUIT playing completely for 2 weeks. Take note of all the things you accomplished while not playing. Judge for yourself which is more worthwhile, the next level or having a good life outside the game.

I think the problem with some addictive online games like UO and EQ, is that it does not give you the total satisfication you want on the usual non-online games. Online games does not have a goal, and you cannot "beat" an online game, so some people tends to not believe that and continues to find that "goal" of the game, and that's when they start the addiction.

Some off-line games had goals, the end, and many gamers find the satisfication of beating the game, when they see the ending.

In EQ or UO, everything's repetitive(sorry for the spelling). In UO, you either kill or get killed, there's a little tiny bit of a joy and fun when you kill someone in UO, but it does not satisfy the gamer, so they want more, they want to kill more players until they're killed.

In EQ, there's a false goal people believe in, it's level, level, and level. It's true that in order to do more in EQ, you will have to level so you are strong enough to do the thing you always wanted to do (plane raid, running around in crushbone, etc..), but that is not totally true.
