Here are what I consider good solutions to the problems that currently plague Rallos Zek. Probably, being on the 4th page of the 3rd repost, noone will in the public will read it, but I hope someone at Verant does. Oh, and I hope HTML tags work, if not please disregard the garbledey****.

<b>To address the risk/reward imbalance:</b>

<i>Faction</i>. Only the person who INITIATES the pvp encounter (the aggressor) will have faction affected. You've all heard this one, I don't need to rehash it. It's merits are obvious.

<i>Looting</i>. Assign a point value to each equippable item based on stats and item effects (procs, clickables, etc). Assign a point value modifier for each class to each stat. In other words, a +STR item would be worth more points on a warrior than on a wizard. At the time of the death of a player through PVP, the total point value of all items worn by the victorious group or individual is divided by the amount of items worn, coming to an average point value that was risked. Exclude no drop items from this calculation. Anything on the victim that has a point value less than that average can be looted, not including handheld items, but including items in bags. In this way there is a direct, simple correlation between items risked and the rewards gained thereby, and bagging, which makes for a rather boring pvp experience, becomes a very stupid thing to do.

This reintroduces to rallos the problem of heavily twinked newbies. To resolve this, assign a point value cap based on level. For instance, say a cloth tunic, ostensibly one of the best items a level 1 newbie could get on his or her own merits, has a point value of 50. Based on this theory, assign a point value cap to a level 1 newbie of 50. Now, say a rubicite breastplate has a point value of 50000. If it's worn by a level 1 newbie, it's point value drops down to that level's point cap, so the rubi now has a point value of 50 and can be looted by anyone who is wearing at least a cloth tunic.

<i>Looting rights.</i> Only allow a player's corpse to be looted by another player if that player (or the group the player is a part of) did at least 60% group aggregate damage. In other words, You or your group have to do at least 3 bubs of damage on a victim before you can loot the corpse. This should eliminate the opportunistic item ganking that is so common right now.

<b>To address the melee equipment dependency issue:</b>

<i>"Soulbinds".</i> Introduce a few new quests that are a mechanism whereby a player can exchange one of their items for another item that is identical, but no-drop. Link the value of the item (the point value described above) to the difficulty of the quest. In other words, make it impossible for a level 1 to soulbind a rubicite breastplate. That would be bad. Make these quests time consuming enough that it would be unlikely that people would want to do it over and over again to achieve a full set of no-drop. Make them one way, as well, ie no quests to change an item back to it's droppable form. This way the overproliferation of many items will be resolved on a continuing basis, which would do wonders for the in-game economy.

<b>To address delevellers:</b>

<i>Experience debt.</i> You've all heard this one, so I'll just summarize. Make it impossible to lose a level. If you need X experience to be level 10, and you die at exactly level 10, you don't lose level 10, you simply have farther to go to reach level 11.

<b>To address newbie killers:</b>

<i>Sliding Scale pvp range limitations.</i> Newbie killing is fine, just fine, until the combat between one newbie and another becomes totally lopsided. There are 3 things that make this happen. 1 is delevelling, which is addressed above. Another is the fact that even though 4 levels above may not be such a big deal at level 50, it is a VERY big deal at level 1. A level 1 newbie has absolutely no chance standing toe to toe with a level 5 newbie, pure and simple. Make the level limit 1 level above or below from 1-10, 2 from 11-20, 3 from 21-30, etc etc. Not only will this be a big step in making the newbie experience less the hellish trial it is and more a decent introduction to PvP combat, but it will make things more evenly matched across the board, level-wise.

<i>Eliminate no-drops at low levels.</i> The last thing that makes for lopsided combat between newbies are non-lootable items, notably weapons and no drop armor such as shin armor from Guk. Easily fixed. Make weapons lootable using the same point system as described above, but only through level 10. Allow no drop items on players level 10 or below to be looted as if they were not no drop.

<b>To address the issue of exploits:</b>

<i>Empowered, knowledgable guides.</i> All of the exploits that matter to people on Rallos Zek are illegal already. The play nice policies are well written, concise, and logical. They are also widely regarded as a joke. Noone who is impowered to enforce them is willing to do so, nor do they have the time. The GMs are scarce at best, and virtually impossible to get ahold of even when they are online. The guides are typically ignorant of the issues that really need to be looked at, and instead spend their time changing people's last names, enforcing naming policies, and doing corpse retrievals.

There needs to be a pool of guides whose specific mandate is to find and punish exploiters. A new classification of guides, PvP enforcers, the police force of Norrath. They would, acting on tips recieved through /petition, investigate people's behavior, invisibly and undetectably. You see, it's impossible to monitor every zone line, every port, all day every day. The guides currently don't even bother trying to enforce the play nice and anti-exploit policies because they are already kept busy enough by other forms of petitions, for name changes and lost corpses and such. Enforcement needs to be a priority of some of your GM/guide staff. Once this happens, exploitation will go the way of the dodo bird very, very fast, without necessity for code changes.

I'm certain there's something that I've missed, and I'd love to hear it. Please, try and keep flames to a minimum, I've thought long and hard about these points, and though I don't expect everyone to agree, I do hope that people will at least respect me for an honest attempt to describe the path to balanced and enjoyable pvp. Note that none of these changes would in any way affect the PvE game experience.

Absor, I do truly hope you read this far. Anyways, although this post with an SK-only concern, I have posted it here because it really only is a concern on PvP servers. I have been playing an SK from the moment EQ went into Beta 4, and on TZ since the day that server went live... just so as you read the following you can know its an informed opinion.

HARM TOUCH

First, I am the first to agree that the reduction in PvP damage via HT is a GOOD thing in general. HT had become too powerful, expecially at higher levels. Circumstances had flooded in of Lvl 60 SKs being able to "auto-kill" casters. Harm touch in essence became Death touch. The 12% decrease (from prior to the patch) in HT damage in PvP still allows HT to be as viable in PvP as it was before the damaged was upped, while allowing it to be more useful still in PvE.

However, the change I do feel needs to be looked at it the fact that HT will no longer respawn upon death. In my (almost) 2-years of playing EQ, and since TZ has opened, I perhaps have heard, let alone seen, of maybe only 2-3 incidents where SKs bind-rushing another and HTing them was a problem. However, I am willing to accept that some (albeit it a huge minority) may feel it is a problem. Below I will make some points as to why I feel it is LESS of a problem than it is made to be on PvP-servers, and finally, a simple compromise that could be integrated with no large coding changes (I am assuming here, but once you see the solution I think you can agree its not a major change).

1. A typical Lvl 50 SK HT will do approximately 500 pts damage (rough estimation) in PvP. If they use it, are killed in that battle, they return to their bind-point and must wait 72 mins (I belive this is the refresh time... never actually had the patience to check it in-game) before it comes back. However, a Lvl 50 wizard, killed in PvP returns to his bind-point with no mana. How long does it take for that same wizard to meditate enough to be able to cast a 500 pt DD? 5-10 mins? And that is being generous I would assume.

2. Seeing as how SKs, being melees, cannot currently bind anywhere that does not have a "city" flag (i.e. Neriak, CoM, GF), while casters can bind almost anywhere, doesn't this make caster bind-rushing still more viable currently than SK bind-rushing? This leads to point 3...

3. When an SK uses his HT in PvP, and then dies during that encounter, he now must wait upwards of 72 mins before he is at full fighting capability. HT IS a very important part of an SKs arsenal in PvP. A caster on the other hand is at full strength againonce all their mana is back... say, approximately 15 mins. So, for over an hour after a PvP death in which the SK used his HT, he is penealized for that encounter. In all subsequent PvP encounters during this time he is far from able to function at full capability. No other class, not even paladins, have this restriction placed on them

4. Previously an SK could know that in any PvP battle he could use his HT (provided it was up), even if the encounter was in doubt, because on death it would refresh, and therefore in any other PvP encounter following this death he would not be at a severe disadvantage, even though he had died in the first battle. Now, the situation is essentially that you MUST be sure your HT will kill your opponent, or change the outcome of the battle. Why? To use it and still die means you have essentially wasted your HT, since it will not refresh for 72 mins, even after dying. Therefore, in all subsequent PvP encounters during the period the SK is at a severe disadvantage.

SOLUTION:

Upon death, if the SK has previously used his HT, and it has not yet refreshed, instead of having HT refresh immediately (as prior to the latest patch), have a 10 or 15 min timer start, at the end of which the HT will refresh.

This means that no SK can use his HT for bind-rushing, since those who killed him have at least a 10-15 min repreve. This seems consistent with the fact that a caster who returns to his bind-point with no mana after a PvP death need only med for 10-15 mins to be at full functionality, and cause considerably more damage than an SKs HT.

This solution also means that an SK may use his HT in PvP without being concerned that if he were to die in that encounter, he would be at a disdvantage for quite some time even following retrieving his corpse.

Its a good compromise. Ask any questions you may have about the reasoning Absor, I know I kind of began to ramble.
