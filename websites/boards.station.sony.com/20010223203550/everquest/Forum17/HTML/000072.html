Interesting can of worms, really. People have been illegally e-baying EQ stuff for rather a long time, but what one person without a business license is allowed to sell to another is highly unregulated.

Now, if Verant, with their business license started selling virtual items for real money, it might open up all kinds of interesting ethical and eventually legal issues.

Personally, I would have a problem with the ethics of the whole thing, and it would create some serious questions for me about Verant's integrity.

Bottom line: I think it should be illegal.

Perhaps a viable option would be that Verant could offer several types of "account packages" from newbie account with no added features to say, "Level 20 Account" that allowed you to start all your characters at level 20, each with 200pp, all spells to that level, and a limited menu of other items to choose to start with (same monthly price, but higher starting price), then "Level 40 account" same idea, more starting pp, bigger item options.

Of course, that would put a lot more idiots in the game at higher levels who wouldn't know how to play their characters, but at least it wouldn't be such an ethical morass of problems. Mostly because it would all take place at character/account creation, but wouldn't allow you to continuously sink your cash into buying individual virtual items.

I'm not saying I like that idea, either, though. I really just think it's a bad idea all around.

My impression is that this would be a bad business move for Verant. The real attraction to this game is the challenge -- there is always some new place with tougher critters and better loot, or there is always that trade skill item that's just out of reach.

How long would most people remain interested in this game if they could just walk in and blast everything in sight, especially if they didn't have the experience of working up to it. Everything would either be too easy or much too hard, neither of which is likely to keep people interested. This is about continuing revenue, not one-time sales.

Even if it is only limited to selling items in-game for RL money as opposed to complete characters, how would you feel if you spent a week working a spawn to get some ultrarare item only to run into some 3rd level character who just dumped some cash on Verant? I for one would be demoralized to the point of leaving, there are other ways I could be spending the time.
