Station Member 		posted 01-18-2001 01:27 PM           
This was actually a player-requested change. When EQ first came out, stuff that was green to you wouldn't attack you all the way up to level 50. But folks complained about high level players camping low-level dungeons and farming named mobs without having to fight any of the "trash" mobs that the lower level folks had to clear to get to the named mob (only to find it camped by the farmer). So Verant changed it so anything over level 20 will attack you regardless of your level.

You can't compare the levels of a mob with a player directly. The mobs are much tougher that an equivalently levelled player.

Which, of course, is nothing new to anyone here. But just cause you are 20 levels higher than the mob doesn't mean he knows you are that much higher. If he /cons you, it'll just say Threatening, what would you like your tombstone to say as long as you were 3 levels or higher than him. And being a level 20 gnoll, he's pretty sure that maybe you are level 23, and he can wipe the floor with you despite the level differences. In his mind, high risk, but very high reward. Whoops, so you're actually level 42. Eh, no biggie, he is still matching up his strengths with your weaknesses (silly necro, put on some armor!), and has a fair shot at killing you.

The /con system should be taken with a grain of salt. It doesn't mean you should always be able to beat something if they con green or blue, but it just provides a rough measure of how the mob stacks up to you. I wouldn't mind too much if they just took it away.
