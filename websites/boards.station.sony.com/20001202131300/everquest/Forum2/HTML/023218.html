The last piece of analysis I did was with my firewall logs. When I play I use the Torv server. The Firewall logs UDP to the CerfNet address space, therefore Torv is located in CerfNet's Colocation Center (educated guess based on mutual clients use of that address space). The use of multiple upstream ISPs in the 64.37.144.0/20 block is a good sign, UUNet, Level 3, AT&T, etc. What is of concern is the complete lack of redundancy for the CerfNet-located machines. A Ping test against the enumerated world DNS names returns 14 servers on the 64 space and 24 on CerfNet. I do not have historical information, therefore I do not know if this number has changed
in proportion, either for the good, or bad.

By having CerfNet (AT&T) SWIP, or RWhois Sony (or whoever the IP space allocated to them, and provisioning additional circuits to redundant ISPs (like has been done with the 64 block) the connectivity issues of recent memory can (almost) be avoided. Clever announcements, use of route-maps, and community strings can further utilize these links in a backup-only or partial-load-balance manner.

The analysis complete, I am back at my 2 basic questions:
1. To understand why Verant is having these "Connectivity Problems".
2. To ascertain what Verant is doing to avoid them in the future, and to assist in that task.

Speaking to question 1: I can plainly see the technical reasons why these problems are occurring. The question is more of a political (?) nature, not a technical one. Given enough pressure/time/incentive this can easily be fixed. Abashi has commented to me publicly (last week, I believe) in the chat server during another connectivity incident that Verant does not state 'who' has the root problem.

Perhaps Verant can not provide the incentive, but 50,000+ users might. If the root cause is a decision made by Verant, admit your fault (we are all human after all), and tell us what you will do to fix this, and when. The
outcry will likely be severe at first, but less severe then the outcry if this continues. As for my assistance - I hope I am bringing this issue to light in such a way as to provide _constructive_ criticism, unlike the recent barrage of posts on the message board.

Greetings all,
I have spent much time in Chat for months now (and here), I have observed the following:

* There was a dramatic increase in connection problems when the LinkDeath nerf was introduced on Wed 19th July.

* Ever since then I estimate a few hundred players cannot play each day.

* Each patch since then seems to CHANGE the subset of players who cannot connect - there are many players unable to connect for various periods of time over the past few months - way before the last patch.

* Tragically, the trend is upwards, Chat during busy time is packed with connection problem reports, each month it gets worse.

* EQ Connection problems are much more prevalent during peak EQ time.

* During EQ peak time, CERFnet is often overloaded as measured by repeated traces (i.e. high packet loss).

* Connection problems, however, STILL OCCUR during quiet time (but MUCH less frequently).

* EQ connection problems ARE often ISPs, and routers - but there is clearly a remainder of problems at EQs end.

* Many people erroneously think that because it IS sometimes routers/ISPs, then it MUST be always routers/ISPs whenever ANY connect error occurs - this is the frustratingly wrong line pushed by the guides and GMs in Chat that gets everyone so upset.

* Many people erroneously think that if it was an EQ problem then everyone would be affected, not just a few hundred - that is completely FALSE, that is not how bugs work, bugs usually strike a minority, the smaller the minority, the harder to find. It is blatantly false advice like this from Verant reps which make them lack credibility on tech issues.

* Many people erroneously think that a Connection Problem MUST be the Internet. Not so - the connection problem could be at Verant's end of the connection.

* Verant representatives have firmly and clearly stated repeatedly, over many weeks of connection problems by hundreds of players, that Verant does NOT have a problem at their end. I haven't seen a Verant rep even answer a connect problem post for weeks now...

* Oddly, for some people the EQ connection problems SOMETIMES seem to come and go for no obvious reason (i.e. everything is fine, ISP, modem, etc, even checked by experts) - sometimes it just goes bad one day, then goes good again later, various things may contribute to the improvement (re-dial, re-install Dx7.0a, re-install TCP/IP stack, Win98 Critical Updates) but the real problem seems to be at EQ's end.

* Connection problems sometimes strike ONLY one character, while other chars are fine.

* Connection problems sometimes strike ONLY one account, while other accounts from that very PC are fine.

* Connection problems sometimes strike just one
PC, while another PC right next to it is fine.

* It is known that the 1018 Active Char/Synch problem occurs after /q or LinkDeath (GMs warn not to use /q specifically).

* 1018 Active Char error can last for days (which it clearly should not if acting as intended).

* 1017 Error can last for days (unlikely for internet to be bad for days, particularly if all other signs show the Internet is fine, just EQ is bad)

* Verant admitted a connection problem several patches back when they said the 1018 Problem was fixed in the patch message - no improvement was observed.

* One or more servers has come down for one reason or another almost every day for the last couple weeks - usually during peak time.

Posts like Daroz' should help to isolate the problem (at least technically) - the really issue is that Verant has done nothing after months of service denial to hundreds.

Findel
