Now, a few points,
1. Clarity (et.al.) is comparable to regen for a melee types and both are already implemented in the game.
2. Gryndel-Rathe's idea of mana "healing" is equal to healing HPs for melee types and only hp healing is implemented in the game.
3. We can see that the coding makes it possible to "haste" casting times based on spell type and class. Melee types already have haste spells and items to help them output more damage/round. Only melee type of haste is implemented in the game.

Here are the questions,
Why hasn't VI implemented any type of mana "healing"? If it is acceptable for Clerics to be the masters of HP healing, it should be acceptable for A pure caster type to be the masters of mana "healing".
Why hasn't VI implemented any type of casting "haste"? If it is acceptable for melee types to get haste buffs, procs and effects, it should be acceptable for casting types to get casting "haste" buffs, procs and effects.

I do not think that there is a defensible position on this from VI's point of view. I have tried to reason that mana "healing" would make caster types way over powered, but I also see that if the mana healing were implemented in the same way as HP healing, it would no more overpower a caster than warriors are now. Also, there is no reason for there to not be casting "haste" items and buffs in the game.

One final question,
How is it reasoned that the hybrids, who do magic as a sideline along with their melee ability, can get better and faster at casting spells, while the pure casters, who have virtually no melee skill and concentrate solely on magic ability, can not get better and faster at casting spells?

While obviously the spell costs/mana gained need tweaked, the concept is sound.
