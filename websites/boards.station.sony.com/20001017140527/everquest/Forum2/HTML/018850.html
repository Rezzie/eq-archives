First, there are of course, 2 separate teams:
Verant -aka- "MOBs" and The Players -aka- "PCs"

General rules governing the game are as follows:

1) Upon starting the game, MOBs get to start 10 seconds ahead of any PC action. This is just standard game lag that can not be accounted for.

2) MOBs are allowed to use any combination of current, available paintguns, (pump, semi-auto, full auto) paintgrandes, smoke bombs and whatever else they see fit to use on the field to achieve their Vision, err...*cough* excuse me - their goal.

3) The PCs are limited in their use of applied force. Each PC must pick one of 3 defined classes. Gunner, Ammo Carrier, Flag Runner. Once you pick your class - YOU MAY NOT CHANGE. You are set in this class for the rest of the day. Players caught trading classes will be banned! This is to promote group unitity and inter-class depedency.

4) MOBs are allowed to attack in any way they see fit. They are allowed to run in, thru and over trees, walls, barricades, and any other obstacle they may encounter to get to the PCs. If the PC is too fast, the Referee has the ability to HALT play and advance the MOB to the PCs location before allowing play to continue. As always, MOBs get to move 10 seconds before PC on game restart.

5) PC Gunners can only use old Nel-spot single load, pump action paint marker guns. They can carry ONLY 1 ball of paint on them at any time. After firing that round, Gunners must get another round from an Ammo Carrier. Gunners may not touch the flag at any time.

6) PC Ammo Carriers start with 10 paint balls. They can ONLY give ONE paitball at a time to any single Gunner. Once they give out one paitball, they must sit and close their eyes for 10 seconds. For each game they survive, Ammo Carriers can carry one additional paintball at the start of the game. After the Ammo Carrier has played 5 games, they no longer have to close their eyes while sitting. Ammo Carriers may not touch the flag at any time.

7) PC Flag Runners get one gun with one shot in it. Beyond that, they can only touch the flag. They can not reload or get another gun.

8)The opponent's flag must be captured, and returned to your home base.

9) MOB flag is allowed to be booby trapped in anyway the MOBs can think of to make it difficult for the PCs

10) PC flag must be readily accessible, and hard to defend. It must be in the open and easy to find and see.

11) MOB players take 10 shots to kill. MOBs get what is known as a CH. MOBs are allowed to wipe shots off them as if they didn't get hit. Only when they get a full 10 shoots on them before they can wipe once are they considered out.

11a) Special MOB rule - 20% of the MOB force gets the ability to "respawn" after death. Once they are out, they get to fully reload and stand where they once laid dead. When they are ready, play resumes. (10 second MOB headstart in full effect)

12) PCs take one shot - they are out. PCs that take multiple shoots take out additional PCs that are near them. The Referee will remove players closest to said player in relation to the number of shots taken by said player.

13) MOBs may once a game shout a PCs name. That PCs is considered out. It is suggested to keep your name secret, lest the MOBs learn it.

14) ALL PCs must wear name tags

15) At the end of the game, MOBs will be declared the winner, but PCs that played the game well will be awarded items they will never use.

**Note**AMMENDMENT TO THE RULES:
It has been reviewed and observed that Flag Runners are too powerful. Flag Runners will no longer be able to use any gun in any form what so ever. Rule 7 will now read:

7) PC Flag Runners get NO gun and may not touch a gun or ammo. Beyond that, they can only touch the flag. PC Flag runners, after getting the flag, may not run either. Once they have captured the flag, they may only walk.

Between games a lounge will be set up for the mobs and pc's to socialize in. All PC's must give the mobs $10 to pay for the rent on the lounge. Since the mobs are the ones that then hand the lounge owner the money, they get to set the lounge rules.

Discussion of the rules of the game is allowed, but must remain polite. If you treat the game with any amount of passion, then you can be barred for both the lounge, AND the game.

Mobs get to decide what is polite, and do not have to justify their rules.

If a PC says somehting that a Mob doesn't like, they can ban the PC from the lounge and the game, and then make up a retroactive rule that the PC broke.

Discussion of anything that has been discussed before can (at a mobs discretion) be cause for a gag and "locks" to be placed on the PC. This is necessary in so that the Mobs can listen to only new things, and not be bothered with things they have been told before.

Saying something a Mob "is tired of hearing" is grounds for explusion of the PC from both the lounge and the game. The $100 game entry fee, and $10 lounge fee are non-refundable in the event of expulsion.

PC's are encouraged to speak openly as long as they never say anything that a MOB doesn't like. There will be no advanced warning or guidlines about what the Mobs like or don't like.

The lounge is thereby considered a place for free and open exchange about the game. Just stay polite.

And don't say anything that has been said before.

And don't say anything that a MOB is "tired of hearing".

And don't say anything a MOB doesn't like.

But please, speak freely!
