Ay, if you zone with less hp than the "+hp" bonuses from your gear (both direct and indirect), the game will first think you are dead. Thus, stripping all your buffs. But then, it will "remember" your +hp gear and decide you are alive. Thus you zone without dying. But you still don't get your buffs back.

Because buffs and equipment are applied to the character after zoning, there is a certain amount of time that happens between when you finish zoning and when the buffs are applied. If you are at a condition where you have fewer hit points than you would naked and unbuffed then there is a moment that you are, in fact, at negative hit points. At this point you would ordinarily die, but what normally happens is your buffs get stripped and you end up at the "safe point" of the zone. Why they had to put in this hack is unknown.

I don't think they ever came out and said that it was working as intended. I agree with the above:

If you have 1000hps unbuffed and naked. You put on your gear and you've got 1500 hps. If you zone out with your hitpoints less than 501, you'll lose your buffs. The server sees this as you 'died' and strips buffs on zone out. It then sees your gear and adds the hps to equal the amount of hps that your gear added (from zero) and pops you out where you were zoning to. So, using the above example, if you're fighting and get down to 200hps and gate out, you'll pop up at your bind point with 500hps and no buffs. Try it out, I have.
