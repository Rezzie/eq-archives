This isn't scientific by any means - but when I first started playing EQ, it was on an AMD-K6-3D-400 with a Voodoo 3-2000 and cable modem with 128MB RAM. Zoning took forever and a trip to Kunark is like running in molasses.

Got a P-III 600 with Voodoo 3-3500 and 512KB RAM and same cable modem, and I zone in 15-18 seconds, Kunark is no different than any of the older zones and life is a breeze.

All of this with my data rate still set at 5.

Changed it to 9.0 last night - and noticed no appreciable difference in zoning.

There seems to be a major difference in how people define "lag". To me - "Lag" is when my ping rate indicates my packets aren't being passed around fast enough. If I have a decent ping rate, but my character is moving in molasses - that is not lag - that's most likely my CPU and video card going nuts at being fed more than it can handle.

To conclude - I think that a satisfactory playing experience is made of a number of different items, with data rate being only 1 small part.
