Situation:
Dragon raid on Sev in EJ.

Problem Number One:
In a party of 30 people, about 15 people experienced spell lock up. Their HUDS locked up. They could not cast, sit or camp. They could move using arrow keys only but could take no other action.

Cause of Problem One:
If a caster is halfway through casting a spell (i.e. the casting bar is halfway done) and the caster is feared at that point, they will experience spell/HUD lockup. After the fear wears off, the caster will be unable to cast or camp or do anything except run around.

Reproducibility of problem one:
This has happened at least five times in the previous two months on dragon raids to members of my guild, most recently on Saturday 23rd of August, 2000. It has happened to me personally on three ocassions in the last two months.

Effect of Problem One:
Two out of three clerics were locked up and couldnt cast. Our tanks died while the clerics were on full of mana. The dragon got away with a sliver of health (again).

Please note: I am not referring to the poison stun caused by the poison AoE. I am not referring to being unable to cast during being feared. I am talking about the HUD still being locked up 15 minutes later after the dragon was way out of range.

Problem Two:
Because the casters HUD was locked up they were forced to physically pull their modems because they could not camp. For some reason that is not clear to me, this resulted in some of the casters remaining in the world, necessitating a world kick We had 8 casters requiring GM world kicks.

Reproducibility of Problem Two:
This has happened to me twice in the last four weeks, resulting in me needing a world kick.

GM Verifiability:
This is a known bug on Tribunal as GMs have confirmed that this is a bug and repeatedly given GM resses for this bug.

Contact information:
I would be pleased to discuss this matter further. Please contact Sashanna Bluueheeler ingame on the Tribunal server between 7pm and 11pm EST weekdays or email me at simplepeasant@hotmail.com

Yours in gaming
Sasha

If I were a tester I would be swamped with "fix my bug plz" requests...

So I would have to develop an objective criteria to determine what bugs were worth fixing and what bugs should be put off to another day. I would want to focus my efforts on:

(1) Issues that are clearly bugs and not balance issues.

(2) Bugs that are easily reproduceable

(3) Bugs that affect a large number of players

(4) Bugs that have a material adverse impact on the quality of gameplay.

If I was considering a bug that was easily reproduceable and was clearly not a balance issue, but didnt really negatively affect gameplay, I would put it to the bottom of the list. Likewise if the bug materially negatively affected gameplay, but only for a very small group of customers, I would also put it at the bottom of the list.

However if I had a bug that met all of the four above criteria, I think it would be worthy of some attention.

(1) Balance: Caster HUD lockup is clearly not a balance issue, there is no question in anyone's mind that this was put in deliberately with the intention of making the dragons more difficult.

(2) Reproduceability: This is not a one in a hundred bug. Take any caster, cast a long casting spell, dragon fear in the middle and the caster is locked up. Please do not test this with non-consumer software because it has been acknowledged time after time that the version of the client used by Verant staff gives quite different results in testing from the retail version of the client.

(3) Affects a large number of players: Any player that is high enough to do dragons i.e. 45 plus has the potential to be affected by this bug, whether its the tank dying because the cleric is locked up or the wizard that can't lure. Dragons are supposed to be the ultimate goal in this game - and we can't win because of this bug. This bug takes away our end goal, or makes it far more difficult to achieve.

(4) Material adverse impact: This is not a situation where a graphic is messed up, or the wrong race is displayed on a bracer who cant use it anyway. This has a clear and substancial negative affect on the gameplay of all players affected by it.

For these reasons I would respectfully submit that this bug deserves attention.

Thank you for your time.
