I might as well post my support, and my desire to see this thread exceed 400 posts.

Most of what I will say has been said before, but I will restate it for the record. I am trying very hard to comprehend the statements made by both Ester and Abashi.

Ester said that the song does not increase in effectiveness and is not intended for us above level 30.

That is total and complete baloney. Nearly every song that bards get increase in effectiveness as the bard increases in level. Exceptions to this would be the level 4 song, you can't point yourself at a corpse any better, the level 21 song, you can't do a short range tp any better, the level 31 song, levitate is levitate and maybe one other that I am not thinking of. That is the point of bards getting so few songs, the fact that they do get better (increase effect) as the bard increases. We don't get new versions of songs (no upgrades). A song can and should remain useful for the bard's entire career. The exception to this is the post 50 songs, several of those seem to have a combination of effects that remove a bard's need to twist certain songs. The combination is weaker than twisting the two original songs, but hey, we can do more at once with them, it's a trade off.

Abashi said that the primary resist lowers as the bard increases level, but the secondary resist remains constant, as it should.

Excuse me, did you say as it should? Exactly what form of skewed logic are you using? The first part of your statement is correct, the resistance rate should go down as the gap between levels increases. It is the secondary resist that is the issue, with a constant 35% resist rate on all creatures, the song is effectively made useless. As any PC gets up higher and higher level, there should be a lower and lower chance of monsters resisting (assuming that you have a constant monster level to the PCs increase in level). Absolutely no class would even be considered balanced, or even well thought out, if a level one creature had the same chance to resist them at level 15 as they did at level 60.

Abashi also said that a song that uses no mana could never be as powerful as a spell that uses mana.

Again, this has been refuted in so many ways, but I'l like to make sure that you were aware of a couple things when you made that statement. I'm sure that you knew that the most creatures a bard can affect with an AE is 4. This is true for the mez and true for the level 43 song (not sure on other AE songs). Is there a limit of 4 on the enchanter spell? The duration on the bard mez is already 1/3 that of the weakest enchanter version. An enchanter's level 16 mez lasts about 18 seconds, a bard's mez will last at most 6. We are already not as strong as the mana version. Each tick we are refreshing the song, which means that each tick they get new saves against the song anyway. That is 3 times the chance of the monster making a save than against the enchanter version. Okay, enchanter casts mez, he isn't in the middle of the group of monsters, he sits and meds for 2 ticks (assuming he doesn't want to do anythint else), he recovers (some, all or more) of his mana. Bard has to be in the middle of the group of monsters, has to refresh the song at every tick and has no choice but to do nothing else. Then you add the secondary resist? Result: Bards buy the spell and then never use it after the first couple of attempts because it gets them dead fast.

Abashi's comment on the drum animation convinces me that it isn't a single person posting under that username. I am certain that Abashi knows of the drum animation issue, it has been raised before, and he has commented on it before, yet now he doesn't know anything? Evidence of my theory being the weird hours that he posts, his knowledge of a subject then later his ignorance on the same exact subject, his ability to directly contradict his own statements. An alternative would be a frazzled person with no ability to retain long term memory. I like the first theory better, the second implies that he doesn't keep track of issues raised over the long term.

If I may make a suggestion, it would be easy to keep notes. A simple binder with sections marked for class concerns. A subsection for each class.
