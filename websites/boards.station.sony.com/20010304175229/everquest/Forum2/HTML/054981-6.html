Why the perception of clerics are imbalanced. I think I've figured this out (I hope)

I've done my share of ranting and raving, mostly in support, but some objection to the whole druid/cleric/shaman debate.

I think I know why clerics are deemed by many to be too powerful, needed on raids, and any number is ok. We are the only class whos primary role is casting spells on group members that will never get resisted, always tack on for full, always work. I'm by no means implying a resistable heal, that would destroy EQ for every class. I'm simply saying when we spend 400 mana on a CH, we know its going to work if the mana is spent. And since healing is something you can never have too much of, thats why many clerics are desired at once. Necros for instance, their dots stack only once (when they hit of course). So, having 5 necros in a group would be a pointless thing. Shaman kind of go the same way in the respects to DOTs done by one shaman dont need to be casted from another. They won't stack . Clerics, well, you can heal someone 92138473 times in a row, and it'll hit everytime.

Is it clerics fault that so many mobs are so magic resistant that other classes (Druid, Shaman, etc) feel the effects of it, but clerics do not, since we (for the most part) do nothing to the mob itself. Is it clerics fault that mobs in Velious are pretty much unmezzable? No, thats not a "get a group free" card for a cleric, thats a stupid decision made by Verant. Ask any cleric, they love their enchanter first and foremost, or at least they should . We dont want *ANY* class to be hampered, but because Verant has made this decision to make mobs so resistant to magic, etc, clerics get a bad name because - besides melee, we're not affected by it. That make sense?

If anything, Verant needs to drop the MR on some of these mobs. When you make a mob thats 100% MR, you are *BEGGING* for a uber melee/uber healer combo. THAT needs to change for the betterment of all classes, imo.

I am sad to see how this whole thing has turned into a whining contest.

I play a cleric, druid, and shaman they are the three highest level characters I have. My favorite is playing the cleric, with this class I can help others by healing and buffing. I also go out of my way to res people if I am in zone and it will not interfer with my adventures.

At 34th with FS armor I was sitting at 649 AC....that is pretty good when most other clerics at that level were lower than I. What that means is when something agro's on me I can stand there and take a beating for a bit longer to allow time for the warrior to taunt the thing off, or finish off the creature he is killing before taking this one on.

While playing my druid I found that you can't get beat on, it is a diferent playing style....same as the shaman. They each have a different playing style.

I have yet to suffer with any of these 3 classes like everyone says.
