Stating that increasing ranger's defense and offense will make them more desirable than a warrior in any group shows an incomplete understanding of how a ranger is different than a warrior. Several things make warriors the most desirable damage takers in any post-50 group, regardless of Defense.

Armor AC: Ranger's Kunark drop armor is 6-11ac less per piece than warriors.
Item Tolan's -- Cobalt
Helm 18 25
Gauntlets 14 25
Boots 18 25
Breastplate 35 45
Vambraces 18 25
Bracers 14 20
Greaves 24 35

Totals: 141 190

A difference of 49 raw AC, or approximately 76 actual AC. Right-click pells designed to be used during don't really come into balance discussions for melee characters because of long cast times, though I would argue giving pure melee characters spells somewhat degrades hybrid casting abilities. Invis on warrior greaves, shrink on bracer, are useful and somewhat needed, but contribute far more to a warrior than getting mana-free versions of spells we already have does for a hybrid.
Obviously this armor set will not be available to most The same differences are apparent in lower level armor, though to a lesser degree.

Hitpoints: A level 60 Ranger can expect to have under 2k hp naked. A level 60 Ogre warrior can expect to have just under 3k hp naked. This disparity is what makes a CH on a warrior vastly more efficient than a CH on a ranger. Other hybrids have more HP than rangers, and I've seen casters with higher self-buffed HP (and yes, my ranger focuses on HP gear). Hybrids gave up something to get spells: for Paladins and SK, it was the DW ability. For Rangers, it was plate armor. Now in addition to those things, we have:
Lower offense: pal/sk
Lower defense: ranger
Lower hitpoints: all three
Lower armor AC: Pal/SK (also ranger, but we should have this penalty)
Which makes hybrids, overall, worse at both taking and dealing damage.

Not that all this really bothers me...I've retired my ranger and started an Iksar monk, who has more AC naked at 35 (569, I think) than my ranger did at 52 (550 exactly...I die enough to know that number by heart =[).

Hybrids in general need major changes to be desireable in the post-50 world. The versatility they provide is great for a player who never intends to play the very high level game, but in the end-game specialization (warrior, rogue, cleric, enchanter, wizard, monk), not versatility (shaman, druid, hybrids, magician [though CoH (summoned booze...er, nm =p) makes them very desireable]), is what makes things happen.

sorry for the length, but I think I've made it worth reading.
