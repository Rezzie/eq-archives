In answer to the original question, you are not in the wrong to sell the belts at any price.

The belts are purchased by people with high level characters who would rather buy experience for their lower level characters than earn it. They are so lazy, they don't even want to camp the belts themselves.

On any given night in the City of mist, I will usually get at least one item valued around 5000pp. If I were to trade this for belts at 4pp each, that's 1250 belts. How many hours does it take to collect and sell that many belts? The self-twinking people are just whining.

As far as carrying on about price-gouging in ooc or shout, that is just immature. If they want to alert the zone about bad pricing, one shout is enough. They are just starved for attention.

As far as what is the right price to charge? This is certainly dependent on the server you are on, and market conditions. If nobody else is trying to sell the belts, you can charge more. If someone is buying, as mentioned before, you can get more. On Tunare, I have seen belts offered at 1 to 4pp, but I expect that 40-50pp for a backpack full is not outrageous for someone who is looking for the easy way out. We're not talking about widows and orphans needing DF belts to survive the winter. We're talking unethical players who should be spending time procreating instead of playing EQ.

The only thing I want to add here is that it would be unethical of you to camp the belts if the orcs are green to you and another group of lower level desires to hunt them. In that case, you should defer the camp.
