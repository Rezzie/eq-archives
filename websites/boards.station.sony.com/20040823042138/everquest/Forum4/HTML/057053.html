There's really only a few kinds of kiting. These definitions mainly apply to soloers, but can be applied to groups of players, although it might be a little harder to pull off.

I do not claim to be well versed in all the classes, but I will try to be thorough when I mention the most and least likely classes to do this solo.

Fear-kiting : Killing a MoB while it is feared (either through spell or skill) and running away by using spells, pets, DoTs, or melee/ranged attacks. Most likely - Clerics, Necros, Enchanters. Least likely - Rogues, Monks, Bards.

Agro-kiting : Snares and/or speed buffs are highly reccommended. Usually done by pet classes. You order your pet NOT to taunt and you make the MoB good and angry at you through DDs, DoTs, ranged attacks, or melee (if you're insane ). After the MoB is quite p'oed at you and will chase you no matter what, you send in your pet to beat it up while your DoTs continue to injure it, or you continue to hit it with DD spells. All the while, the MoB continues to chase you and your pet is free to fight. Most likely - Nearly every caster can do this, even the ones without pets. Some are better than others and I won't go into it THAT deeply.

Quad-kiting : Named because if the type of spell needed to pull it off. Targeted Area of Effect (AE) spells. These spells can only hit a max of 4 targets. The concept is to get 4 MoBs to chase you around being very close to each other while you keep your distance and cast your AEs and drop them all at about the same time. Always (personal opinion) requires a movement enhancement. Always (personal opinion) requires a snare type spell due to the long casting time on almost all of the AEs (unless you have casting time enhancement items). This method is VERY mana intensive unless your specialization skill (usually evocation in this case) is very high. Or unless you're a bard, then you don't use mana. Most likely - Wizard, Druid. Least Likely - I honestly don't know (how's that for thorough).

Zone-kiting a.k.a. Swarm-kiting : Like quad kiting, except it's done by bards because their AE DoTs hit unlimited targets

Oh come on! I know you've all seen at least 1 bard at some point in your EQ careers with 20+ mobs chasing them!

I think there's more, but I'm not familiar with others.

Swarm kiting sounds like a nifty way to get easy exp. It's not. The difficulty mainly lies in the fact that bards must be pretty close to the mobs in order for the dot to hit em BUT far enough away not to get hit by the mob. Running away from a mob is easy but running just far enough away to swarm kite is pretty tough.

Usually the bard runs in an elipse, using Selos to increase speed. Last time I did this, I used brass, not percussion. Brass increases the damage but, of course, does nothing for speed.

Another problem is a clear kiting area. Since the dot (usually Denon's Dischordant) is an area effect, you may aggro something you don't really want.

I haven't done this for quite awhile. I usually single kite because both Selos (speed) and Tuyans (single target dot) are percussion songs. I get 50 dmg a tic with Tuyans so they go down pretty fast. Still, I suppose I really should practice swarming in EW. It should be about as safe as anywhere.

The definition of swarm kiting given above is different from the definition that most bards use.

AE DoT kiting is when bards pull tens of mobs and run them in a circle killing them with their AE DoT spells. It is dangerous, because those spells have ranges just barely longer than melee range.

Swarm kiting is different. It involves the bard pulling a group of social mobs (ones that assist each other). The bard then charms one of them as a pet and sends it against the swarm of other mobs. When the bard's pet is nearly dead, he breaks charm with an invis song/item and kills the former pet with a DoT or DD. Rinse/repeat. It is good experience because a swarm of enemies is killing your target (your pet) very fast.

===========

As far as aggro kiting, I agree with the above definition but as a wizard I would never consider my single kites "aggro kites" even though the mob is aggroed on me. To me it is only aggro kiting if there is a pet or group member attacking the mob. The mob's aggro on me is the only thing keeping the melee safe. If it is just me with snares and nukes, I guess I would just call that plain kiting.
