Good question. With my new toons, I pickup a sewing kit as soon as possible (I could afford one at lv 3), and put 1 point into tailoring at my guild. I also buy a stack of cap patterns at this time. When I loot
2 spiderling silks = 1 silk thread (allways succeedes)
2 spider silks = 1 silk swatch (allways succeeds), then you make a raw silk cap. 2 skill checks for the price of 1 =)
3 silk threads = 1 silk cord (I think)
The silk cords can be used to make tailored bags. But I don't know ther recipie.
1 Ruined Wold Pelt + 1 Cap Pattern = cap

Etc, you get the picture. As you loot items, make something with them immidiately, as this will save you space in your inventory. SO my inventory has in it a large sewing kit, a stack of patterns, and a stack of silk cord (and a skinning knife when I can get one). Keeps your inventory pretty clear of tradeskill clutter, while giving you the ability to turn a lot of the junk loot you find into skill checks on tailoring.

Using this method, I was able to get my skill into the 30s pretty quickly while going about my leveling business. Then I skipped the leather armor steps and went straight to cured silk, as the spider silk is much easier to obtain than making studded leather, or banded. But as you are also planning on becoming a smith, and metal studs are now stackable, go ahead and make the leather armor.

Depending on my financial situation at the time, I either sold the armor I'd made to a vendor, or gave it to a newbie. When I couldn't do that, I gave it to a bat in a newbie zone (about half the time, the mob will drop it when killed).

Hope this answers your question.

I personally think that anything beyond 26 skill you should wait until you can hunt Crag Spiders in EK (around 18th lev min in a group). Otherwise you will take absolutely FOREVER to get enough silks to advance rapidly. Second choice is carrion spiders in EK, then dune tarantulas in Ro.

Not sure if any of the new ruined pelt bag recipes will increase you beyond 26, but use them as far as you can.

Raw silk isn't too bad, but cured takes a TON of silk to get through, and the heady kiola cost can add up quick with the failures.

Of course there's studded, but I think MQ pelts are far too rare to make these viable, and studs are a pain in the rump.

AnthonyHobbs method is the fastest way to progress that I have found, and a variation can be used in any trade skill. (With smithing,. make metal bits and attempt a toolbox with each success to skill up faster).

Practically speaking,. you will level your character alot faster NOT concentrating on the trade skills, but rather using them to maximize your return on low value looted items like ruined pelts and spider(ling) silk as you gain experience. You can spend more time on trade skills after experience slows down at mid level.
Don't count on ever getting rich off the trades,. the game is not designed for that. The trades are fun at first and you get some satisfaction from making your own armour, but you can loot much better stuff than you can make. Trades are good for twinking your secondary characters tho.

Now.

If you are not twinked, the best thing you could possibly buy with your first few coins is a tailoring kit. Everfrost is the best place for a newbie who can wear armor. Tailoring became a lot easier since they included spiderling silk in the recipies, and Everfrost is a great place for silks and ruined pelts from polar bear cubs. I would suggest to concentrate mainly on the spiderling silk to build your skill. Making your own armor is great (all my characters start with Tailoring just to get the patchwork), but pelts don't stack, and to a newbie, 5sp is a lot of money for a pattern. Besides, Cindi gives patchwork in exchange for polar bear pelts - a nice low level quest. When you become more financially stable switch to pelts and patterns.

Later on (around level 20 or so), get yourself a skinning knife and a file(easy blacksmithing task...FP has the molds and ore for them), then head to Highpass and E.Karana. Now that studs stack, make a couple stacks of them in Highpass...no mold necessary, just metal bits, water, and the file. Buy the same number of mask patterns and head down the ramp. E.K. is a tailor's paradise! The lions there drop HQ cat pelts with surprising regularity, and the carrion and crag spiders drop spider silk usually 2 - 3 at a time. Use the skinning knife to lower the HQ pelts you get to MQ, then make studded masks. Sell them to the vendors in the zone to keep your inventory clear. HighPass also has everything you need to make heady kiolas, and with all the spiders you're killing, you'll have plenty of swatches for cured silk. In a couple nights (about 6 hours total) of intensive carrion spider killing, I acquired over 40 swatches for tailoring. (Plus...carrion spiders drop halfling parts for those of us who like to prepare the more "unusual" dishes in the cookbooks!)
