Ok,
As any high level player knows, raids are almost always communicated through the public channels /ooc or /shout. I have a suggestion.

Create an /alliance command. For instance there are 4 groups in Hate. One of the group leaders targets another group leader and types, say: /formalliance. A box pops up saying, "Soygen wants to creat an Alliance. Do you agree?" Click yes. Then I do the same to the other 2 group leaders.

Once this "Alliance" is formed, all 4 groups can communicate with the /alliance(or /a for short) command. This would save the spam all over the zone(for Vox/Naggy in particular) and would create just an overall easier channel of communication for highly organized raids. Once the groups disband, the channel is gone, just like /group.

What do you think?

Station Member 		posted 12-18-2000 03:45 PM           
Heh. I posted an idea similar to this one (along with the idea of increasing the amount of hotkey pads in the button banks) a while back. The added hotkey pads got put in, but nothing ever happened with /alliance.

I'm not saying that this idea may never be implimented. All I'm saying that it has been suggested in the past, and it hasn't been implimented.

I believe that one of the iffy parts of this is besides being able to talk to each other, would the groups be able to share items/XP amongst themselves? For example: if you form an alliance with another party(ies), do you share the XP as well? Do the parties share whatever coin loot from the MOB? If you form an alliance with another party (and XP/coin sharing is enabled), what's to stop your group from controlling an entire area and power-leveling everybody in the alliance?

My suggestion(s) for the interim is to use a voice chat program (like the Microsoft GameVoice, Roger Wilco, BattleCom, TeamSound, or Alienware's First Contact) to communicate with everybody else. You wouldn't need to include ALL the participants. Rather, you'd just need to get all the group leaders in on it.

The other suggestion is to create hot buttons with /tell "leader1" (replace leader1 with the persons' name) so you can communicate with them directly. Raids are usually pretty well organized, so you WILL know who will be the team leaders for all the groups beforehand. The downside to this is that you can only see the messages directly sent to you/sent from you. BUT!! It'll stop the "I-can't*click*on*that*guy*because*there's*too*many*people*in*here" problem. The other downside is that I think that there's an automatic <return> pressed after the hotkey goes off, so you might not be able to enter in any text after you press the hotkey button. Worst case, get a voice recognition program (like GameCommander2), or use the built-in voice recognition & speech to text capability (along with a program like Naturally Speaking) so you just have to say the commands to talk to each other. I know this is a kludge, but it's better than standing around for a feature that may never come.


-Az
