Want to increase damage?
Do it with how DEX/STR affect the hit/miss percentage and/or the way STR affects damage.

Want to decrease the damage taken?
Do it with the way AGI affects defensive skills.

Want to put in minimum damage?
Do it based on STR or DEX or a combination of the two.

Want to increase HP's?
Do it with the way STA affects HP's.

Want to increase AC?
Do it with the way AGI affects AC.

Want to make ID or FD more effective?
Do it with the way stats effect these skills.

To me this is much more in-line with peoples ideas of a monk (although I am unsure about VI's "Vision" of a monk. Absor (Alan),

I'll first admit that I haven't taken the time to fully sift through the hundreds of rather lengthy posts. But, your comments were posted on eqvault.com and there's a few points I wanted to bring forward.

Verant's Vision of the monk, and their 'role'.
I struggled with this one a bit, as the role I've always known for monks is the puller. Yet according to past statements by Gordon, pulling (especially FD pulling) was not in the Vision. So what's left? To me, the vision was an altnernative to warrior, but more reliant on skills rather than equipment. Yet, in doing so, having a skill based clone of a warrior would be rather dull, so there needs to be a difference between the two. This is where the monk's 'niche' among the pure melee classes comes in. In my opinion, if you were to take rogues, warriors, and monks, then rate them in both offense and defense, monks would be #2 in each category, going 'half way' in the difference between warriors and rogues in each category. As I understand it now, monks are marginally better than warriors in offense, yet fall far behind rogues w/ backstab. On defense, monks are about on the same levels as rogues. I'll go further into both offense and defense.

Offense - pre-kunark, monks had it good. They did a good deal of damage with their fists, leaving warriors behind and almost crept on rogue damage w/backstab (so maybe we did slightly too much damage). We also enjoyed a nice skill advantage in offense. Hand to Hand, 1h blunt,and 2h blunt all capped higher as well, but they were a moot point pre-kunark.

But, the advent of kunark drastically changed things. First, weapons for all 3 classes came out with damage potential unheard of before, with them overall being of the same caliber. The only exception were a few ultra rare monk staves that were a small notch above everything else, also monk fists only went down 4 delay through 10 hell levels. Also, the skill advantage in offense shrinked dramatically. And on top of that, warriors received triple attack at 60, and rogues received double backstab and assassinate, those three abilities clearly augmenting their damage output. Monks received no abilities along the same lines. So whereas before the monk balance in damage was *slightly* in their favor, it's now more out of balance comparatively the opposite way than it was before. Even with Velious, it's still the same and even worse. Warriors and Rogues are getting better weapons than what they could in Kunark. This time though, I have yet to see any monk weapons which clear 'upgrades' to the kunark weapons.

While I'd like to leave it up to you guys to make a proper solution, I'll at least throw in a simple idea: instead of triple attack, double flying kick, special attack combos, or what have you, introduce an 'advanced offense' skill obtainable in the latter levels somewhere. Since the dilemma with the skill advantage is that the code won't let the value exceed 252, why not just make a 'higher level' skill exclusively for monks that'd maybe add more atk/skill point? To me this serves well because it's a) not flashy, b) not a clone of another class'ability, c) adds more damage, and d) doesn't drastically change how the class will be played. As for weapons, the bonus in advanced offense could offset the weapon 'problem', since I realize there isn't much room to upgrade without being deemed as 'too damaging'. Anyhoo, onward...

defense - Again, in the pre-kunark world, life was good for a monk in the defense department. Yeah, we couldnt tank or take the hits like the warrior, but we could still take a pounding, unlike a rogue. The biggest reason for this was, again, our skill advantage(s). We capped significantly higher in defense, and also capped higher, or just as high in dodge, block (parry), and riposte. And while we did ***** about our sub par planar armor, monks were still decent defensive wise.

But again, that has changed after Kunark. The skill advantage has dwindled, rogues and warriors got really nice ac upgrades on their class specific gear, and monk did not even get a high level class specific set. Instead, we received crescent armor that had the AC (and in some cases lower) of cured silk. Also, the mobs in Kunark hit harder and have more hp than their antonican counterparts. In summary, our ac and evasion abilities did not increase much at all while both rogues and warriors received good skill increases and received good armor upgrades.

If I were to present a solution, it'd be almost the same as above with offense, with the difference of also upping the evasion skills as well. The same reasons as above for why this would be good.

I hope this has been somewhat enlightening on the monk as a whole. I understand there is no 'hard' evidence here (although I'm sure there's some from the monk community who have it), but it's more the idea I am trying to portray. I think the solutions fall in line with my interpretation of Verant's Vision for a monk by maintaining a skill advantage rather than relying heavily on equipment, etc.

Good luck to you

51 Monk on Karana.

This topic is about balance, and as such only small changes need to be made. While many people have some interesting suggestions, there is in reality only a few things that need to be adjusted so that higher level Monks feel useful in an average group.

1. Feign Death should not be seen through if a spell is resisted. Not much reasoning is needed to support this. Furthermore, FD past level 50 should lower aggression much more. The change to FD many months ago did not seem to take into consideration Kunark and Velious.

2. Monks should get a spell interrupt attack, not necessarily a stun. This will allow them to be effective against casters.

3. Monk innate damage should be increased significantly. Personally I like using some weapons and am thankful for the opportunity to have options. However, a Monk's hand to hand damage should be increased. Perhaps some changes in disciplines could allow this. Minimum damage on flying kicks and thunder kick would help but not enough. I agree with the idea of giving Monks an off-hand bonus when using fists. After reading all these posts, you should probably be clear that the vision for Monks is to be the supreme master of toe to toe damage (not by just a little)

4. AC is fine, but please do us the favor of increasing the stats of Shiverback-Hide armor. It really is an embarassment. Every other class drools over their Planar armor. Monks destroy it.

Those are the things that would bring Monks in balance in my opinion. Reading below is my list of things we "should" have, according to me.

1. Weight limit scaled past 50 instead of tiered
2. Robes and Celestial Fists quests should be no drop, as discussed here at length. They don't need to be harder, they need to be short-cut proof.
3. Panther armor should be much lighter.
4. Shield of Rainbow Hues should look like a shimmering hue shield instead of a round wooden shield with a spike in the middle.

Overall, I believe Monks to be a wonderfully enjoyable class. We have many great abilities that make us distinctive. Please consider this post as a support for Bodhi's and Zadkiels and Kages previously posted ideas.
