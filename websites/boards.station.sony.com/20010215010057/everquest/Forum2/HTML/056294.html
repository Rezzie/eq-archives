The problem with Druids/Shamans getting the current proposed upgrades is because they are comparable to Remedy/Divine, which are CORE to the cleric.

It would be similar to a cleric getting an updated MR based DD from a wizzie which is about the same in power and most useful to a wizzie; and we know that doesn't make any sense to do.

Clerics are TELLING people that these spells are at the very CORE to them healing and are the most used healing spells for clerics in gameplay. People who don't even play clerics have been brushing it off, twisting words, and are TELLING clerics what their core spells are.

1.) People need to understand Remedy and Divine light are core cleric healing spells. If you cannot understand this, you cannot understand it from the perspective of a cleric.

Now you may ask, why should a non-specialized class receive core spells from another class that will make them almost as effective as a specialized class ? They shouldn't. They should not be almost as effective as a specialized class. These spells in the current state will allow Shammies/Druids to be as almost as effective as clerics as healers.

The 3 main changes made are:

1.) Moving of SH down in levels to address fizzle rates (why they choose to only address fizzle rates for one class is rather strange)
2.) Addition of 2 new spells on par with Remedy and Divine light (level 55 and 60 I believe, respectively).

Clearly when looking at this in the grand scheme of the healing classes, this is imbalanced --the level of power of the new spells infringe too much on what is core to the cleric.

How do you balance this ?

They either reduce the effectiveness on the druid/shaman version or increase the effectiveness of the cleric versions. We already know they aren't going to do anything for clerics as Absor has already stated this; Abashi indicated similar when months back he said, and I quote "Clerics can heal therefore they are balanced".

The only choice is to reduce the effectiveness of the proposed healing changes.

1.) The moving SH down in levels helps on fizzle rates, but I believe this should be addressed for everyone and not just druids --everyone has this problem.

2.) The equivalents of Remedy/DL need to be changed. In their current form they are too powerful in a non-specialized class. Some suggestions:

A.) Leave them in their current state manawise, healing wise, and level wise. But add large recast times (5 seconds for remedy version, 10-20 seconds for DL version) as well as component costs. Bloodstones for the remedy version and jaspers for the DL version. (If they require such great healing power, let them form it by harvesting the power of gems, much like how a cleric has to do it for buffs)

OR

B.) Totally remove both of them, and replace it with a new spell at level 55 or so which is in between remedy and divine light in power and would act like any other heal spell.

-----

What does this accomplish ?

It gives them the healing power needed to be effective enough to get by healing in a group, but not as efficiently or nearly as well as a pure healing class (cleric). (I'd argue that they previously always had enough versatility to fully compensate for this but I won't here). At the same time is doesn't infringe enough on clerics to replace them.
