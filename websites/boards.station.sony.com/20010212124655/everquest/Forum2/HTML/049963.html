Well let me just start off by saying that I've played this class multiple times past level 55.

Once, to level 55, and once, to level 58.

Both monks have been in capable raiding guilds, and have seen many great things in being a monk, but often times, feel - why not just roll up a warrior or a rogue?

-- Why are monks so imbalanced? --

Well lets begin by taking a look at all of the other classes at hand.

Rogues - They hands down, do the most damage out of any class in the game. Circumstantial or not, they sit behind mobs and often get backstabs for over 700+ damage, quite commonly, a double backstab. Against all of the uberspawns, they have the most dependable form of kicking their aggro - evade. This skill allows them never to dodge out of combat to lose potential damage, and they stay in battle until the mob is dead, or the warrior dies. Rogues are now needed to pick locks, sense traps, and make corpse retreival look easy in many zones. So what else? Rogues, can debateably tank as well as a monk can, while dishing out two times as much damage in the same period of time. Their utility? - damage, corpse retreival, picking locks, and sensing traps.

Warriors - Well, they've come a long way from pre-kunark days, up until now. This class is capable of obtaining the most AC out of any class, the most hitpoints, and with their discpline defensive, which allows them to absorb only 75% of the damage taken, for a period of 2-3 minutes. While this discipline recycles every 10 minutes, warriors are in defensive, say 30% of the time. This allows them to be the most efficient utilizers of complete heal. In addition, warriors are also able to withstand the most damage, and still live. They do not need a means of avoiding aggro, because that is their sole intention - to keep aggro'd on to a mob. Well, what else do this 1300 AC, 4000 hitpoint meat shields do other than absorb damage? Lets talk about dealing damage. A warrior, persay using some average weapons - willsapper, and a scimmy of lifestealing. If you want logged combat parses, I can show you. But heres my beef, warriors can do just as much damage, if not more, than a monk. I know this is intentional to keep the aggro on to a warrior, however, how is this supposed to allow monks to hold thier own as a class?

--- Monks, as opposed to rogues and warriors ---

Monks were deemed "the masters of melee combat." As mentioned, I felt this was quite a nice occupation to hold, and that doing some harsh melee damage would make this a viable class to choose.

PREKUNARK MONKS

We were the coolest class. Hands down, we did the most melee damage, took damage well, and had our occupations cut out for us. Hasted, dual wielding whitened treant fists, we were beasts. We were monsters, and we withought any doubt in anyone's mind, did more damage than a warrior. Rogues did comparable damage, which is how I felt it always should have been.

KUNARK

With the implementation of kunark, monks got the shaft as warriors received a higher defense cap (245) and armor that knocked the socks off of anything a monk could wear. (i.e. cobalt vs.. ?). Not only did monks get no new pieces of class based armour, but we received only a few pieces of all/all armor that just doesn't exactly cut it in the utility / AC department. Rogues received mrylokars armor, which allowed them to obtain a higher AC than a monk, and well warriors receiving cobalt made much sense, because they take the damage as opposed to dish it. How did warriors get so close to monks in damage?

Two things :

Lamentation
Jade mace

With these two items, a warrior's damage output became close, if not equal to a monk's.

FEIGN DEATH

There was an obvious implementation of a new aggregation for mobs. Monsters would react differently to feign death, and feign death no longer became a means of killing your aggro mid-combat.

When I fought a mob known as treah greenroot, in the plane of growth, I noticed this new aggro implementation ten-fold. Obviously there is a certain point of aggro in which feign death will become useless. About two minutes into combat, while the warriors, rogues, and magician pets were still going strong, pounding away, I was frantically trying to get distance from the mob (almost out of view) and trying to feign. Shortly afterwards, I was summoned, and kapow, hit for two rounds of 700 damage. My cleric heals me, and I try and feign again. This only seemingly increases my aggro.

Level pulling became the new hype, and monks became useless for splitting mobs in any given situation. So if feign death becomes completely useless, what are monks, besides tanks that deal as much damage as a warrior, and tank worse than a warrior?

Weren't monks supposed to be the masters of dual wield? So we were given only 7 more points of dual wield skill and equal weapon skill to that of a rogue?

VELIOUS

There is no stopping anything with this expansion. While not every piece in the game has been found yet, a good portion of velious has been unveiled. The loot, was obviously impressive. Revealing items that outdo the epic items (obviously, thank you for this...) and have statistics that blow them away (gauntlets of dragonslaying vs. celestial fists to name one). With this expansion out, the arsenal of weapons warriors are allowed to select only allows them to deal MORE damage than a monk (willsapper, for instance).

So to sum up all of the points i've made in my post...

Rogues - Best damage dealers, tank like monks.

Warriors - good damage dealers, tank like tanks.

Monks - good damage dealers, tank like rogues. feign death does not work in many given situations.

So while our damage is only better than warriors by a SLIGHT noticeable margin, and while our damage is pathetic in comparison to a rogues...

So while our tanking ability is only better than rogues by a SLIGHT noticeable margin, and while our tanking ability is quite pathetic in comparison to a warrior...

So while our feign death ability is completely useless, and only good for crawling through a zone and feigning off green mobs, while the warriors just plow through with their massive hitpoints, and rogues just plow through with their sneak / hide ability that runs at regular speed 51+...

So while we try and dish out our damage, as the rogues plow away evading and backstabbing their hearts out, and while warriors stand their taking the blows with their defensive discipline and dishing out the damage like champs...

the MONK is sitting back, over-aggro'd to hell, trying to feign off the mob and kick him/herself down the aggro list down a few notches, LOSING MANY, MANY rounds of combat (1000's of damage, potentially)...

PLEASE tell me there is some sort of fix in the works?

For instance :

-More hitpoints for monks
-Changing the aggro of mobs, so feign works
-Giving monks an "evade" type of skill
-Possibly raising the 1HB, dual wield, H2H, and innate ATK of monks
-Possibly raising defense for monks past 252 (i know this is a game flaw, and it isn't possible... however, mobs with more than 32,000 hitpoints weren't possible, either... right?)

Whatever it is, I really feel, that monks deserve more than to be a class that is :

you guessed it...

able to tank 10% better than a rogue
able to deal damage 10% better than a tank.

We're not just in the middle, we're towards the bottom.

PLEASE, consider my pleas, and give this class something to make them once again - the masters of melee combat.

Ashek
