An EXACT parrallel came up with a guild member whose surname was Healsalot. If Giantslayer (your example) is acceptable, why is Healsalot not? The person in question is a cleric, BTW.

In the above case the person was allowed to choose another surname that he was satisfied with. But, according to the posted changes, this is no longer the case. I would be horrified to find any of my charactors' names changed to say Darren Greenman and have no way to correct the situation.

Also, more horrifying, would be to die, have a corpse that has fallen into a wall, petition for a GM only to have a Name Nazi answer and say my name must be changed and no I can not recover anything that I have worked for 40 odd levels to aquire. Giving more respect for compensation to those that purposely make vulgar names than to those that make an effort to be creative but are deemed to be outside of the naming policy restrictions is way out of line.

Also, the ambaguity of what constitutes a "compound word" that is or is not accaptable gives way to much power of the arbitrary to the Name Police. I have several Charactors that have names that could fall either way to this ambiguity, but still have very appropriate names for their race and/or class. I will mention one here: a woodelf ranger named Truepath. I have two of these on different servers. I will not get into the name of a hungry Ogre here, but it too is appropriate for true hard-core RP'ing.

My rangers hunt exclusively by tracking, for instance. The level acceleration since the con-track patch is beyond belief. The name Truepath *is who he is*, it is his identity. No other name would fit the charactor properly. But according to the changes in the naming policy it could fall either way depending on the GM's descretion.

I do not feel that this kind of ambiguity coupled with this amount of GM discretion is good for coustomer relations and should be rethought and rethought quickly.

firebizz:

Nowhere in the naming policy does it say that if one person petitions your name because they are offended then your character will instantly be deleted. If you are petitioned and it is determined that your name does in fact violate the naming policy (under rules 3-13) then you will be given the opportunity to change your name to something else. If you've ever read the rules for this game, you would know that at the point you are told to change your name, you are instructed to inform the GM/Guide if you have corpses in the world that you have not looted. They are then REQUIRED to give you a fair amount of time to loot the corpse before changing your name.

Now, on the other hand, if your name is something like upyoursbich ( and I apologize if my example offends anyone, but I had to give an example ), well, just expect to get your character deleted as soon as a GM sees it. But it's your fault, not Verant's.
