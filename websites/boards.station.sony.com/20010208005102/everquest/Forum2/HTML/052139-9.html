It was commented that debuffs seem to have little or no effect, this is not completely true when they are stacked. HOWEVER it takes too long to stack them. This is true throughout the entire level range. To be usefull you need to stack the Cripple line with the Weakness line and the Suffocation line. Debuffs are effective if they act over time, however it takes so long to cast them all that they are almost never used, the amount of time they have to act on the mob after they are finally all cast is not enough to justify the cost. The mana cost of debuffs should be the limiting factor on their use in play, not the fact that you can't finish casting them before your target is mostly dead.
Assuming your unlucky enough not to have a shaman in the group the typical debuff casting goes
Tash 4 seconds
Slow 9 seconds
Suffocate 9 seconds
Incapacitate 6 seconds
Weakness 6 seconds

so by the time you've finished casting you are 34 seconds into the fight already. It's no wonder that you end up forgetting the debuffs at the end and just throw a DD in to shorten the fight because it's more efficient.
I would really like there to be some way for debuffs to be usefull in gameplay as an alternative to just nuking. This may entail reducing the casting time on the slows,DoT, and debuffs. Maybe also reducing the mana cost on the debuffs to make them more attractive.

Dyn's Dizzying Draught - this spell is currently useless, my only guess is that the very long recast time was put on the spell because the 2 tick spin was supposed to be unbreakable. At the level this is attained the rate of incoming blows on the mob is so high that it breaks within one or two seconds. Because of the long recast time the mana drain aspect is essentially useless, you only get to cast this once on a mob. The brief debuff can be had with Listless power for only 60% the mana cost and it will last the entire fight, not 2 ticks. Whirl Till You Hurl is more usefull than this spell because although it breaks as quickly it's 1/3 the mana and can be recast 4 times in the same time as DDD can be cast once. If you want to make DDD an upgrade just make it the same as WTYH in effect, time, and cost and then reduce the resist rate so it's tougher to break out. Or make it an unbreakable stun and keep the recast delay (you can drop the mana drain and debuff, it's pointless). Or just get rid of the spell all together, useless spells are just a source of disappointment and frustration. Note that at lvl 56 I'd never be using this spell now, but at the appropriate level I was really disappointed with DDD and the supposed fix when it came along.

PLEASE fix the fact that you can't cast some buffs such as clarity when a bard is singing their songs. These normally stack but you have to cast them before the bard starts singing. I can't stress enough how annoying this is to the point where I get to hate having a bard in the group. Bards have enough problems without this.

In a raid situation we cannot use either our DoT's or Debuffs because of the shaman and necro's present. The concept of a mana expensive one shot targetted pet for boss mob situations would let us feel like we could at least do something. This also can address the inability to do anything with the high MR of velious mobs, at least we'd be able to cast a damage spell.

It was mentioned the taunt on the Tashani spell line was too high, this isn't quite true as both Tashan and Tashani seem appropriate, Tashania however is a huge step up in agro that seems way out of proportion. Tashania needs to be closer to Tashani in taunt. I am not sure about Tashanian as I haven't used it yet.

Okay, first off, some things where I think enchanters have a bit too much power:

Charm in mid levels is a very, very powerful tool. At the low levels it's pretty useless, at the high levels it gets you killed a lot, but in the mid level range it's quite powerful (at level 32 I was capable of soloing about 4 spectres at once).

Enchanters also shine with their AE stuns at around levels 44-50. Past level 50, you head to Kunark, where resist rates increase greatly...But from 44-50 the ability to keep almost an unlimited number of mobs permanently stunned for a great length of time is also very powerful. I'm not sure if it's deserving of a nerf, just thought I'd mention it.

Mesmerize spells below level 46 are also quite powerful. Past level 46, once an enchanter begins to head off to Kunark/Velious/Planes, they change dramatically (for the worse, with increased resist rates), and are more balanced.

That's pretty much my take on where the enchanter has too much power. Now for some fixes:

Argument FOR changing Gift of Brilliance:

2 points of mana per tick is NOTHING. Really. For a cost of 450 mana, that means every 10 minutes (which is 100 ticks) you get 200 mana. That's ALMOST enough to cast ONE extra slow spell every 10 minutes. I'm pretty sure any percieved increase in mana regen from the spell is just because enchanters have been told that the spell increases their mana regen.

I would say the way to go with this spell is either making it a mana transfer over time, or to make it a larger +mana buff and simply remove the mana regen (and I mean a large +mana buff....Absor, maybe you could get the live team to make sure they're really aware of how much mana high level players have). Alternatively, make it a larger mana regen buff and remove the +mana component. As it is, both of the bonuses are too insignificant to make casting this spell valid.

Also, please take a look at Dictate. 750 mana for 48 seconds of charm is in-line; a 5 minute recast is not.

Also, I thought I'd clarify something - the reasons enchanters want a cleric/wizard type pet is because it ATTACKS without you being hit. Our current pet doesn't do this. That means that in an epic battle, we have to get hit by the mob for our pet to attack...I think you can see how highly unsafe that is.

Please take a look at Forlorn Deeds. The casting time on this (6.0 seconds) could really use some lowering...Perhaps just to 4.0 seconds (so that shamans are still superior to us, both in casting time and slow effectiveness).

Please change Wind of Tishani/Wind of Tishanian to more useful spells. Currently no level 55 or 60 enchanter who values his life will cast these spells in any situation where they'd be useful. Maybe you could keep their current resist debuff values (-23 and -40) but make them single target and add a mem blur component to them (applied hopefully AFTER the resist debuff)?

Another clarification: We want monsters that are above the level limit on charm and mez to be flagged that way. High resist/immune to magic monsters don't need to be flagged - just ones that are specifically exempt from our mez/charm spells (for instance, vendors cannot be charmed...neither can most guards...And lots of rock golems in the Hole are immune to mesmerize). I understand your desire to keep enchanters a 'challenging' class to play, but, well...Inane death because I can't tell whether something is mezzable or not is...Stupid.

In regards to your suggestion of a single target, able to hit just about anything, mez spell influencing the old world (IE, trivializing a Vox fight because you could hold her mezzed while the clerics ressed tanks back in): It won't work. There is a level cap on the old world at which mesmerize/stun spells work. I BELIEVE the level is 52, but don't quote me on that...Either way, past ~level 52, NO mesmerize/stun spells work - not even our higher level ones (glamour of kintaz, rapture), that are specifically designed to work. The only zone this is really problematic for us in is the Hole. So I fully back your suggestion of a new mez spell...It would only really change our power in the old world in the Hole, and it'd be a great boon in Velious. If you are THAT worried about it changing the balance of things, flag it as only useable in Velious zones.

Okay, I'm winding down, but I feel this is important...Enchanter effectiveness in Velious. I think anyone who plays a 53+ enchanter or who has any insight into the class whatsoever will agree that they are, in many parts of Velious, completely ineffective. However, saying that 'But they have buffs!' is NOT a good counter argument. This is like making a zone where clerics can't heal and telling them that they're still useful because they have buffs. However useful those buffs may be, the zones where that's all we do are boring as hell. I don't want to count for 10 people on a raid, but I WOULD like to be able to influence the combat once it's begun.../shrug, I leave the solution to this problem for wiser men than I to ponder

Lastly, our suffocate>gasping embrace>asphyxiate line does not stack with the necromancer splurt spell. Could this be looked at?

All race/deity combinations should have some benefit over agnosticism to justify the increased risk.
