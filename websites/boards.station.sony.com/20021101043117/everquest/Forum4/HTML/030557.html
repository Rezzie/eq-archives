OK, so I don't play a warrior. I have a druid, SK, ranger and shaman. I see folks asking for this, please change that, please fix me/them, etc. And, as a general rule, most classes do go through life cycle changes or alterations via VI's hand. All but the warriors. Yeah, the get slightly modified from time to time, but nothing huge. No huge nerfs or enhancements. Yeah, from a warriors point of view there may be huge implications of another class being altered substaintially, but direct change to thier class is not common. Why?

Personally, I think VI has to have a "base" class. A class that all other classes have to be "tuned" or "balanced" to. Sometimes VI does well, sometimes not. But, I think warriors are that "base" class. If you change the "standard" by which all others are measured (yes, I mean casters, too.) then you change all the classes inherently. So, warriors see little or no change over their life cycle and most likely never will. Why else would VI say "We Can't do this or that for warriors" because it will severly impact game mechanics. Not "won't", but "can't". Yeah, maybe I'm off base, but:
- SK's consistantly have their agro "tinkered" with by VI. Why? Because they can't/won't change the warriors agro. So, they tinkered with the other classes to try to make it work.
- Clerics had the agro on heals decreased from a 1/1 ratio to a 2/3's ratio not long ago. Why? Maybe the same reason.
- Mages just had their rods changed. Why? Hmmm, maybe too much mana influx too quickly (KEI, FT items, Rods) reduces the need for tanks to a degree that VI doesn't like.

My point is this. I think warriors are constantly changed by the changes going on with all the other classes in EQ and therefore never see or get any changes that directly effect them. Warriors are the yardstick, so to speak. Yeah, their dodge skill just increased. Great, even though it was in contradiction to their statements about damage mitigation/avoidance, I'm glad they got something. Why would VI improve their avoidance when they say that there job is mitigation? Well, you figure it out this time. Then again, maybe I'm wrong.

Just an observation.

Warriors are constantly being changed, everytime a new warrior weapon or piece of armor goes in that's the same as a caster getting new spells.

When EQ was first released warriors and all tank classes were for absorbing damage, then Kunark came, warriors got a MASSIVE upgrade, disciplines, the Ykesha, former preimer melee weapon was reduced to mid level usefulness, and armor, the armor that came with kunark was comparable or better than planar.

Warriors went from being damage absorbers to damage doers. Casters were obsolete, the Kunark Cycle was born, tank heavy groups with few casters, now instead of having one or two tanks, groups had 3 or 4.

Casters got new spells, Wizards the former primeir damage dealer got jack, some low resist high mana cost ones, a single moderate upgrade from 1120 damage to 1615 at level 60.

Warriors and all tanks get constant upgrades and tweaks, even though they're not usually class specific.
