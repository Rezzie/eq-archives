I parsed up a set of /log data taken from a couple of hours fighting in and around the Sebilis jail. Our party consisted of:
56 ranger (me): Lupine/CSS/noose
55 cleric
55 shaman
56 ogre warrior: Jade Mace/Lamentation/haste belt

I would rank the warrior and ranger weapons as nearly equivalent in damage output. With buffs, the warrior had a 255 strength, I had about 195.

As we didn't have an enchanter, and we fought with no clarity. Hence, we all had to conserve mana.

Both the warrior and I had alacitry on us at all times. The shaman slowed MOBs always, and handled most of the root-parking. The cleric and I also heleped with parking as needed (this decreased my melee damage output).

We fought:
1 pox scarab, 16 iksar necros, 3 bok knights, 11 bok shaman, 1 bok wizard, 7 dar knights, 8 jin shaman, 1 zol knight, 4 leprous scarabs

Ranger combat performance was:
melee 141686 (94227 from main, 42276 from off-hand, 5183 from kick)
successful spells cast:
- 40 drones of doom (32 completed, 8 about 75%): 12920
- 17 call of flames: 3502
- 41 damage shields on warrior: 3240 (estimated)
melee damage/second was 29.36
melee damage taken/second was 1.84
I was hit 123 times. I parried 13 times, riposted 4 times, dodged 4 times, for a total of 18.7% hits avoided..also taunted twice (hehe).

Total melee+spell damage output, ranger: 161408
Total melee damage output, warrior: 151462

I do not believe either the warrior or myself had been Togored that evening (we both had a MR buff, and the warrior has a high MR anyway).

Spells cast by ranger:
225 attempts, 33 fizzles, 3 interrupts, 9.4% resisted
- Drones of doom: 43 (3 resisted)
- Call of Flames: 19 (2 resisted)
- Damage shield: 41
- Ensnare: 60
- Jolt: 26
- Root: 18
- other: 18 (mostly chloroplast)

For the purpose of analysis, I kept a checkoff list on spells (parsers don't handle spells properly).

Conclusion: A ranger, even without clarity, can outdamage a warrior. In addition, we have useful spells like root, Snare and chloroplast which greatly helped group efficiency. I was able to avoid being over-aggroed the entire evening, as a result of Jolt, the warrior's effective taunting capability, and that the warrior was pulling.

As a class, I still see rangers as a useful addition to a group. We are an offensive powerhouse with group-friendly spells. I think we need to spread the positive message!
