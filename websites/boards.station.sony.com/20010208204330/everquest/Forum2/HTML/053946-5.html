The naming policy is inherently subjective. Verant wants players to choose nice fantasy names for their characters. They don't want people to choose offensive names, names that evoke anything non-Norrathian, or names that don't look like names. In principle, I love the naming policy and I think EverQuest benefits tremendously from it. If you are not convinced, try playing a game with no naming policy and see what names people choose.

There are some names that are clearly bad. Nobody would protest having a name changed that contained profanity, for example. Other names are clearly beyond reproach, such as original names created from the player's imagination or the random name generator that don't contain words or phrases.

Then there is a huge gray area. People choose names from other sources, sometimes mangling the spelling to try to make them acceptable or to avoid collisions with all the other people who want the same name. People choose descriptive words and phrases, again often changing spelling. If you are in that grey area, you should probably try to have a backup name ready in case a GM decides your name is unacceptable.

A name does not have to violate any specific point in the naming policy to be determined unacceptable. Those are merely examples of types of names that will be changed.

However, in this case I would characterize a name like Managirl as containing a title in violation of rule 10. X-girl, X-boy, X-man, X-woman all read like superhero names, not fantasy names.

Lisset, I completely disagree.

Managirl doesn't look at it as "just a game" in the sense of Gin Rummy or Parcheesi. It's more like a private club in which games are one of the activites, but simple socializing is also a large part.

In this case, her identity in her chosen group (her friends and acquaintances on her server) was removed from her. She became upset no doubt at the many implications this had. She was probably also not prepared for an assault like this upon her as a player.

On a side note, I think people are far more lenient of anger than they are of sorrow. A sorrowful person is self-indulgent and unstable. An angry person is just considred angry unless they are nearly hysterical.

Suppose you had been having a used car restored and detailed, and someone came along and scratched it with their keys. Hey, it's just a paint job, why get emotional, the car still runs fine, right? Wrong! The car's owner gets very angry, and if they witness the vandalism they might even respond with physical violence. Emotionally unstable? No, just a human being who perceives themselves as having some property rights.

In Managirl's case, she worked on her character for a long time, got to a fairly high level for such a casual player, got fairly friendly with a lot of folks in-game, and then someone who thought her name (which does NOT violate the naming policy, it specifially states that descriptive compound words like Treeclimber were okay, especially (but not exclusively) for surnames.

Mana is not a word in common usage outside of fantasy literature. It is definitely not anachronistic. Girl is simply descriptive of the character's gender. Managirl is a descriptive compund that describes her main in-game preoccupation.

Here is the line allowing her name from Verant's naming policy:
"Descriptive compound words are allowed, especially in surnames (e.g. Treehugger, Giantslayer)"

Notice it does not say "only in surnames," it says "especially in surnames." The main part of the sentence is "Descriptive compound words are allowed," which sounds to me like her name.

Another thing: Verant won't let us change our first names if we ask them to, they have repeatedly told us that the immutability of our names fosters a sense of server community and a personal reputation is important. Why then was a name not in violation changed, simply because it offended the esthetics of what apparently was a Role Playing Dictator?

Role Playing Dictators like to tell other people how to play the game. I ported my Wizard to North Karana to meet a guild member friend who said he "had a present for me." I arrived at the Spires to meet him, and he was wearing a new Shiny Metal Robe. I complimented him on his appearance, and he handed me his old Oracle Robe. Some Role Playing Dictator sent us an emote basically /rude-ing us because "my guild doesn't accept twinks." Never mind the fact that a level 25 Wizard was wearing a Senior Apprentice Robe when she gated in, giving a valuable item to a friend instead of selling it is "twinking," and Role Playing Dictators don't like "twinks."

It is this Role Playing Dictator attitude that prompts people to sic GM's on nice people like Managirl. From some of the responses on this board saying "good, she deserved it," I feel that one Managirl is far more worthy to play alongside me than any dozen of the people who put her down.
