This belongs on bristlebanes message board... and I have to say... openly advertising for a guild is dangerous at best, and foolish at worst. Find a guild that you like the people, that you have grouped with and know something about. Anyone can claim to be a 'raiding' guild and you may find yourself absolutely miserable in it.

Talk to members of guilds that you are friends with or enjoy grouping with. Find out from them... and, if you get in a raiding guild at 51, prepare to be sorely dissapointed. Any guild that is going to accept you is likely a fairly relaxed guild, so they probably have members in the 40-65th range. If they lean more to the higher end, the guild wont be able to handle big targets, but will still be ganking the snot out of the kunark-type stuff they can handle. Also, if you dont know much about raiding, these can be good for learning but wear your patience very soon because of the lack of experienced folks.

Find a guild you enjoy and can learn something from, worry about a solid raiding guild when youre 60+.

Some of the higher end guilds that I have seen have feeder guilds for a low level like you. You could find one of the higher end raiding guilds and ask what the name of their feeder guild is.

I would think they would not want to bother with you if you have not done some hunting with members of their guild. All the guilds I have ever looked at are invitation only based on a members recommendation. You really need to break out of your circle of friends and meet other people to group with.

Or you could start ogranizing raids with the guild you already have. Find another guild to team up with. Many guilds have alliances with other guilds. You really do not have to leave your friends behind.

But from what I have seen once someone starts wanting that higher end content more than they want their old friendships they are going to move on anyway. Might be best to just get to it.
