Since I announced the large scale events without giving the knowledge of the others involved, it appeared that we were ignoring the other types of events.

Below is a complete listing of events that are coming up, but there are some non-combat events that are being worked on, but still in the testing phase.

The event being held on all servers with the warriors is an example of a Tournament Event. We are taking it to the next level with the multi-server compeition.

Tournament Series Events
 The Tournament Series Events are designed to be held in addition to the original Test series events.
 These events are class specific for those not wishing to engage other classes that are of similar kind to their own class, or compete in a smaller sized event (2 hours is target length).
 These events are designed to have a lower entry fee as the target audience is much more specific.
 Anti-Twinking rules are currently under review, and maybe acted upon at anytime by the Event Master of Verant Interactive without warning.
 All of these events are designed as single elimination, standard bracket tournaments.
 All of these events are designed as player vs player events.
 Tournament Series Events are being designed as the standard, and most run event on the servers.
 Tournament Series Events are designed for all levels in mind.

Test Series Events
 These are the Original Series of events that were designed to put similar classes up against similar classes for a more broad range of player involvement.
 These are designed to be high entry fee (Lx10 plat), long lasting (4 hours or greater in length), and high competition events for those wishing the Big Event atmosphere.
 All of these events are designed as single elimination, standard bracket tournaments.
 All of these events are designed as player vs player events.
 Test Series Events require and will always require a Game Master to be present and run the event. Test of Tactics requires an Event Master to be present and run the event.
 Test Series Events are designed for high level players.


Challenge Series Events
 These are single player vs. environment (True NPCs) events designed to test game knowledge and Challenge even the greatest of players to their limits.
 These are designed to be very class specific and level specific, with a low entry fee (LxL in Gold) for short timed (2 hours or less) events that many can enjoy.
 These are designed as an achievement level event, meaning that players will have to overcome different levels of creatures and continue to overcome creatures to the point of death or request by the player to cease the Challenge to achieve the final score. The character who defeats the most amount of mobs does the best in these events.
 Challenge series Events are designed for all levels in mind.

Competition Series Events
 These are single group or multi-grouped events that pit players against predetermined NPCs in a race to defeat these NPCs to achieve a time to be judged not only server wide, but game-wide as well
 Competition Series Events are designed with no entry fee and no prizes, simply for Bragging Rights to other groups, other guilds, and other servers.
 These Events are designed to be very short in length (60 minutes) at the request of specific guilds to achieve a time to be competitive against other times that have been scored.
 The Competition Series Events are still in final testing, more will be released about these events in the future.

I hope this clears up a lot of the questions.

*sigh*

Well it's really good that Verant is doing an event like this, but the event itself just sucks. The idea behind a special event is (well, should be) to involve as many people as possible, with as few resources as possible. Trust me, I've run enough 150+ person LARPs to how how to do that. -- This ain't it. Tournaments are fun for the participants and usually pretty boring for thre rest of us who just get to sit around and watch. Only we don't even get to sit around and watch. How much do I care? None.

Let's see an event that would let anyone on a server participate, regardless of class, level, guild, etc. It can be done if you're willing to put some thought into it. Actually it doesn't require that much thought. Let's take someone like Mayong Mistmoore:

MM has decided, "screw this vampire crap, I'm going to be a god." To achieve godhood he needs to complete the ritual of godhood-ness at the hour of yadda in the place of blah blah. Unfortunately the ritual will be taking place in a heavily guarded area and MM has conjured up additional forces to see to his victory. His fortress has been made almost impenetrable by magical shielding and there is no way anyone can get in to stop him.

However (insert name of person here) has discovered that Mayong is calling upon ancient magics developed by the Combine Empire. These powers can be defeated by expending massive amounts of magical energy and channeling it through a device. Unfortunately that device was split up into pieces that have been lost for thousands of years.

Thus begins the "Dance of the Seven Pinecones" (term used when a bunch of people have to collect a bunch of things to solve a quest). Scattered throughout the game are various chunks of the artifact that must be found, recovered, and reassembled in time to defeat Mayong. Chunks of the artifact can be scattered across damn near every zone so people of all levels can participate in the "scavenger hunt" part of the event. Maybe the device will work if the entire thing is not built, maybe there are parts to make more than one.

Now, I did say that this device requires massive amounts of magical energy, didn't I? Well there are fortunately several dozen sites across norrath that provide focus points for magical energy. Devices could be constructed (or perhaps there are existing artifacts) at these locations that will channel the energy into the central artifact. Now, unfortunately, Mayong knows this as well. As the PCs start to activate the items to call forth the magic, his minions will assault them and attempt to distroy the various artifacts and disrupt the attempt to thwart their master.

Of course Mayong can't afford to hire hordes of incredibly powerful bad-guys. Perhaps a war-band of goblins ( < lvl 10) would attack in one location, some orcs ( lvl 15 to 20) in another, and all the way up to creatures you'd normally see on the planes in other locations. Naturally the most difficult fight of all will come when Mayong's magical protections are stripped away and his keep must be assaulted...

What you have there is a real event people. Something that could entertain the entire population of a server for a whole weekend. Each segment of the event is broken up into appropriate levels so that EVERYBODY can contribute its success (or failure). Natrually, the final assault is going to be the most glory-grabbing, but that final assault can depend on something joe newbie found out in Crushbone.

Can you imagine hacking down hordes of Gnolls out in South Karana, while you wonder what is going on in Mistmoore? If you fail then the party assaulting Mistmoore has no hope, but the Gnolls won't stop coming until Mayong is defeated. Oh dear...
