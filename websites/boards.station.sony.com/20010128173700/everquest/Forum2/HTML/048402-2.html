It makes no sense for melees to be able to cast a bind spell, because they are not casters?
True, but the bind spell itself MAKES NO SENSE.

Gate - character teleports to a fixed location - Makes sense, we can imagine it in any fantasy world with magic in the first place.
Bind - as spell that changes the one location a character can teleport to - Makes sense, given teleportation in the first place.
Bind - as spell that sets your respawn location - Makes NO sense. Characters in fantasy literature do not have a 'respawn point'

The second use of bind affinity - to set respawn location - is entirely a game mechanic. You can imagine a character from a fantasy story seeking healing, seeking a teleport, seeking buffs. But you cannot picture a fantasy character seeking someone to change their respawn point. So while it makes no sense for a melee to 'cast bind', it makes no more sense for a wizard to 'cast bind on the warrior'.

/bind really ought to be a command for all characters, and the spell restricted to its first use. Or your bind point could be set automatically to the last location you /camped at (preferably you could still /quit to get out - with a little more risk - without rebinding) Or you could 'rent a room' from an innkeeper - setting your bind location to that inn. (for that matter, innkeepers would be the ideal NPCs to add the 'buy a bind' utility to. This would also continue to limit the ability of KOS PCs to bind in an area, or in those with no Inns - Kunark Outposts?)

As far as the 'interdependancy' and 'source of income' arguments:
Its completely obvious to everyone except VI (well, almost everyone, a few others are being intentionally dense about it) that there is no INTER-dependancy outside of grouping situations going on. There is a one way dependancy of melees and hybrids on the casters in non-combat situations. Grouping there is interdependance, although the people with all the non-combat convinience abilities also happen to be the better soloes, go figure that one out.

As for the income angle, I'm not sure whether I'm an exceptionally good caster player, or an exceptionally bad melee player, but when I start a non-twinked character, the int caster ones are easilly able to afford their spells and acceptable equipment for their level, the melees have a much harder time affording 'acceptable' equipment. Clerics and Shaman really have it the worst at the start, needing to afford real armour as well as spells, although it doesn't take long to get to where the next weapon upgrade will cost a melee far more than the whole next circle or two of spells. Druids in between in the beginning, but eventually they get teleports with the potential for insane income (or enormous waste of time if they're not mercenary enough about charging) not to mention potential SoW income. Noone whos played the higher level game will begrudge a cleric a few plat for a bind, knowing how much they can lose on symbols, but why my fighter should line the pockets of a robed character, who in my experience can earn enough to keep himself in spells and decent equipment far more easilly than the fighter is beyond me.

As for the 'more downtime spent shouting and auctioning to buy a bind might squeeze another month out of our customers' argument:
Can't refute that one. In fact, as far as I can tell, this is the only valid reason for VI to want to maintain the caster monopoly on the game mechanic of binding. I just wish it weren't enough reason for them.

So how does one avoid death (and the subsequent issues of long corpse recovery)?

Death happens in many ways...

Soloing Deaths
1. You go LD or suffer sudden and extreme packetloss which makes your character unable to cast spells/run/FD.
2. You have one of 'those' fights in which spells are either interrupted/resisted/fizzled and you seem to miss when you try to hit, or when you hit you barely scratch the mob, and when you try to FD the mob sees though it. The mob however hits you consistantly with spells and pounds you into the ground.
3. You get caught up in someone else's train.
4. Invisibility wears off in a city zone and you get jumped by a million guards.
5. Invisibility wears off whilst travelling through a dungeon and you immediately get aggroed by a million mobs.
6. You get a tp with a druid/wizard into CazicThule/Iceclad and immediately get aggroed by the nearby waiting mobs.
7. You inadvertently get involved in a GM event.

Grouping Deaths
1. The healer and other casters go OOM and as a tank your job is to keep the mob/s onto you long enough for the casters to save their own skins.
2. The group gets run over by another's train or bad pull.
3. The group suffers packet loss, or loses vital members (cleric/chanter) to LD.
4. The group gets inadvertently caught up in a GM event.
5. Bad group (poor pulling/inappropriate spellcasting etc.)

Other Death Senarios
1. You go to the planes.
2. You fight in Velious.
3. You die to a recognised bug (like logging/zoning and falling for insane amounts of damage)
4. You duel.

To say 'avoid death' is all very well, and most of us do try to avoid death - but sometimes the best laid plans do go awry and the end result is that our character dies.
