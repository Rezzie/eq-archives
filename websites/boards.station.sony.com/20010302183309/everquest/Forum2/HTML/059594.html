First, balancing the classes is next to impossible. There are just WAY too many varialbes in this game.

Second, every expansion TOTALLY destroys any semblance of balance. New abilities, new levels, new spells, new mobs, new encounters.... they create an ever increasing amount of complexity.

Third, you can't possibly balance classes even remotely with 80 changes at once, being tested on a server w/ 100 high lvl players. It's ridiculous to think that those 100 players can in any way mimic or test what over 300k players do on all of the servers combined.

People on test RARELY kill dragons, they RARELY do high lvl encounters of any sort. So basically there was ALMOST NO TESTING of these changes. Verant has basically rolled the dice and made a best guess. In the past these best guesses have been very far off.

If I was Verant I'd get a dozen players together from FoH, Afterlife, LoS, Da'Kor and other guilds that breakdown their own system and see what they think. Some guy that has a lvl 34 ranger on Bertox doesn't know crap about the game. I dont' care if he designed the entire system himself. He can't possibly understand what the real game implications are unless you play that game.

Basically, the changes go WAY too far in some cases (Bard defense skill = to warriors???, change in combat agro system) and not nearly far enough in other casses (zero defense upgrade to Rangers).

Changes like these should be made in small steps and given time to see what happens.
