The death penalty, healing and med times are all controversial subjects that have been hotly debated on this board. Regardless of one's views about the fairness of these things, the necessity of their existance can be explained within the context of the game. Now we come to the spellbook. How does the world of EverQuest explain why a caster must stare at his spellbook before level 35 and not after? I've never seen it justified to my satisfaction. Staring at a spellbook for 35 levels seems to be a purely punitive requirement with no logical function within the framework of the game.

It makes one wonder. What are casters being punished for? For being casters? The attributes which seperate the casters from the fighters are enough already. Casters are punished sufficiently for being casters by their almost total lack of melee abilities. Is the reason casters must gaze into their spellbook endlessly supposed to promote the idea that learning to use spells is such an arduous undertaking that a caster must study the spellbook for over half his life to become a proficient caster? If so, then by the same token why aren't fighters required to stare at their weapons for 35 levels? I'm not suggesting for a moment that warriors be made to do this, but you see my point.

It seems to me that having to look at your spellbook for the first 35 levels is pointless. It serves no useful purpose, adds nothing but negetive things to the EQ experience and I think it ought to be changed.
