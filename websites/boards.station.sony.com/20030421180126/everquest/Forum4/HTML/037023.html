I'm a roleplayer as well. I only require two things for someone to get something from me. Type full words (typos are ok, odd ways of speaking are ok, intentional dude speak is not) and say please. That's all.

Such as "Could I get a sow please?" My druid would respond to that with "I don't carry piggies around cause they're too heavy, but if you like aminals so much I got wolfie feet to cast on you" So while I make a comment about the sow reference, I do give them what they asked for because they meet my two personal requirements. Note my druid intentionally uses bad grammar, I roleplay her as a 4-5 year old.

I would even respond the same way if they said something like "ug der, culd me get sow pleaz?" It's all misspelled (probably an ogre or troll), but the fundamentals are there (ug der, for those not fluent in ogre/troll means Hi there)

If, though, they say "sow plz" my druid responds with "Sorry, don't carry piggies" and runs off. First because they used plz and second because they obviously couldn't be bothered to type "could I get a sow plz" so that makes it not exactly a question and more of a demand. My character is a child, she doesn't like to be demanded.

I'm not anal about it. People misspell, that's fine. People roleplay bad grammar and spelling, that's fine (or are dyslexic, or 10 years old, or just can't type well) I will not, however, respond to a sentence that looks like "h3y dude, can u give m3 sow plz. tnx m8~" You may think you're saying thanks but you're not, and you may think you sound uber cool, but you just sound like an idiot to me.

Yes it's subjective personal judgement but that's my right. If my druid asks someone else for a buff (say kei) even though I roleplay her as a 4-5 year old, she asks well enough that it's obvious I'm not being a dude, but roleplaying or perhaps a child in real life. For instance, she might say "Heya mister chanter guy. If you gots time and mojo and stuff could I get that koadics mind mojo? I'll gived you 50 platinum shinies and hugged your legs and say thank you lots and lots" Yes it's a lot to type but I'm asking for their time, the least I could do is spend a few seconds typing my request instead of walking up "Hail Chanter" then "KEI plz"
