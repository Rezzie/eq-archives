This is a midlevel (38-41) perspective on Velious.
I'm 44th now but haven't been back since 41st.

ICECLAD:

Seemed like a nice zone, I didn't hunt here much. I loved the humor in the gnomes. A few too many dervishes by the portal. Garou killed me, but it was a fun fight. My only concern in this zone is binding. Is it possible to bind at the gnome igloos and/or gnoll camp? If not, please add that ability so we don't have to run to Thurgadin just to get started adventuring. I thought Coludoth was a bit silly--perhaps something to eat druids who see a named up on tracking and say "hey, a green, snare... SNARE please... um, snare?? ow ow ow ow ow".

Still, this is a general problem with NPCs -- there are a lot that "aren't meant to be killed" but the players have no way to know that an NPC isn't meant to be killed without trying to kill it.

Also, I've almost given up asking NPCs for quests. It seems that you expect people to look up stuff on spoiler sites now. Otherwise, I don't even know what kind of difficulty I am undertaking or what kind of reward to expect when I start a quest. Part of the problem is that there are just too many quests in the game without any in-game mechanism for keeping track of them or pointing me to one that would be appropriate for me to attempt. I keep a notepad by my computer, but it's too cluttered with quests I've tried to start and never got to finish (too difficult, couldn't find what I needed, couldn't get the next quest guy to respond to me talking). This applies to Kunark and the Old World, too.

TOWER OF FROZEN SHADOW

This zone seemed to look interesting, but it bored me quickly. I got tired of sitting on the second floor fighting difficult encounters and getting no interesting loot and no key to the third floor. Also, requiring a rogue to sense/disarm traps was just frustrating, can we please have LESS "required" classes instead of more? I don't want to have to spend more of my time organizing an adventure than actually adventuring. As it is our small group (ranger/ranger/monk) just asked a high level wizard (who was also stuck on the 2nd floor with us, looking for a key) to evac us out after 4 hours of unproductive hunting.

Also, the nonstop whispering was starting to drive me crazy.

EASTERN WASTES

Had fun hunting here, but things seemed to be very difficult for their level. Didn't have too many problems with MR. Ry`Gorr armor is very pretty and very nice stats, so the difficulty is probably appropriate, especially if the experience modifier in this zone makes up for it.

CRYSTAL CAVERNS

This is the best zone in the game, bar none. It's very pretty, and very well designed. I -loved- the mine maze area, it made me feel like I was running around in an M.C.Escher painting. Encounters are difficult but spaced enough to allow you to adventure without getting overwhelmed. Excellent loot placement, with nice items that aren't overpowered for their difficulty.

It may be too late but I wish you would add robe models to all races; there's no real reason why the only druids that can wear the Crystal Covered Shroud are humans... it's just they are the only druid race that also has robe-wearers.

GREAT DIVIDE

I didn't really hunt here much, just used it to get to Thurgadin so I could bank/get bound and go back to hunting in Crystal Caverns

THURGADIN

The most fun city zone in the game. Neat scenes evolving at all times (although to quote a previous poster: Can they change?) Interesting plots. However, I have a few complaints.

Quest text: There are a lot of holes in what people say, and trying to get people to talk to you is incredibly frustrating. For example, there's the carpenter who talks about Argash putting him out of business by bribing the Council, but I couldn't get him to ask me for help, and when I tried to confront Argash I was just met with stony silence. Please make it easier to figure out what I need to say to people. I know you want to remove the brackets from quest text now, but I'd rather play an adventure game than a "guess the phrase you need to say" game.

Exploring: I went exploring off the entrance bridge in Thurgadin, and was very upset to find the only way out was to the bottom of Icewell keep, where a red KOS cube was staring me in the face. I eventually begged a wizard to port me back to Iceclad after being stuck under the bridge for an hour. Please design your zones to ENCOURAGE exploration, not discourage it, or else I'm just going to be forced to go to a spoiler site before I go anywhere, and I'd really prefer to enjoy the game without that.

--
Kiatha Kittenpaw

I have only done a bit of Velious so far, and this is what I see:

Aesthetics:

Zones I've seen (iceclad, EW, GD, Thurg a & b)seem to indicate that Verant has learned quite a bit from Kunark, in some ways. Zones in Velious seem to be better designed, less ghosting, more theme (i.e. snow cougars roaming the iceclad tundra = makes sense; voracious brutes in dreadlands = makes no sense). None of these homogenously-dispersed random wandering mobs type of areas, which is good. Graphics and topography are also a good step up. All in all Velious zones have a good deal more character than Kunark zones.

Gameplay:

Here there are some serious problems. Why would any enchanter or shaman go to Velious? 100% MR = no fun for these classes. They can't do what they do best, what fun is that to play? The absence of these classes on Velious narrows the scope of the game considerably.

I'm not sure about other dungeons in Velious, but the only one I have been to, Icewell Keep, has some of the worst pathing I have seen, even worse than Karnor's Castle. Stuff will be aggro yet run back and forth over the entrance bridge for minutes before getting to you.

I also have to say that it was perfectly true to form for the Plane of Mischief to be unitemized and broken.

I can't express how much Verant needs some experienced play testers and a detailed and systematic quality control system. This is the #1 failure of Verant since EQ came out and the problem that is most persistently ignored. Having one or two people to check out bugs as they are reported simply doesn't compare to running simulations of actual play.

With more and more average players reaching the 55-60 level range, Velious is nice in that it provides more of the end-game environment. Much more places to raid, more difficult encounters. Once you reach 60, doing Seb and Charasis over and over gets dull. It would have been nice, however, to provivde a new exp dungeon for the 55+ crowd.

Issues I see:

1) Make the three cities equal. It's obvious a lot of time was put in to making Thurgadin. It has all the merchants you need, story content, etc - it's a lively, fun city. Kael/Skyshrine on the otherhand are bland and lack most merchants as well as the fun, festive feel. They need animation. I'd be happy, however, if those that decided to side against dwarfs had the same access to merchants, class guilds, etc. All three cities should have equal access to all the trade skills vendors, guilds to train, supplies, etc.

2) Make Kunark 55+ spells drop on SoV 55+ mobs. Level 55-58 Kunark spells are very rare, even after 10 months. I've not even seen two of my spells drop despite multiple trips deep in Sebilis, Charasis, Chardok, killing Trak, VS, and clearing VP. I'd like to explore new lands and not worry about camping the old stuff for spells.

Zone impressions:

Icewell Keep: The lower part of this zone (cubes, tents, golems) can be a fun and challenging place for a small group. Loot and experience is nothing great, but it can be entertaining. The upper part (dwarfs) is just a boring means to get giant faction. MR on the dwarfs is insane. As a 60 enchanter, I can't even land my level 60 dictate spell on them. The green plate armor that drops here is way too common for how nice it is. Exp is ok in a mele/cleric group.

Velkators: This zone was a blast the first couple times. The layout was nice and exploring with one group was fun. Due to exp/loot, however, this zone has almost no replay value. We went, we explored, we tried camping different areas. There's just no reason to go back once you've done it. Despite what many have said, this zone is easily done with one group 55-60.

Kael: As a city, this place blows. You really punish people for taking sides with giants. Yet, you provide no reward for siding with dragons/dwarfs (my guild switched after realizing you're limited to killing in Kael/ToV when siding with CoV). Please make this equal to Thurgadin as a city for us giant lovers. As a dungeon, it's average. There are some fun high end enounters such as Vindicator, Statue, and Tormax. The temple area can be fun for one to two groups with decent loot/quest items.

SkyShrine: This is the same as Kael. Sucks as a city, average as a dungeon. Please fix Yel so that the GMs stop /killing him.

Sirens' Grotto: This zone is a joke. Highly mr mobs that can not be snared, rooted, stunned, mezed, etc make this a huge challenge. That could be fine. However, those same mobs have horrible pathing, run at half health, charm, complete heal, etc. If you have multiple complete healing mobs in concentrated areas. Also, being a mixed land/water zone, you have to deal with only being able to attack a land mob from land and only being able to attack a water mob from water. When a mob runs in and out of water, it's very difficult to deal with. I suggest this whole zone gets revamped. This could be a perfect alternative to Seb/Charasis for the 55-60 crowd if done right. Make it hard, but make it doable by one group with just rewards.

Western Wastes: This zone bores me, others love it. Guess it depends on your class. Being an enchanter forced in to a do nothing but buff role, having most mobs cast aoe dispell is a real pain. My only role is to buff, yet the mobs make buffs almost a waste.

Necropolis: This is a great place for one to two groups. Decent loot, fun to explore. Only problem is its remoteness. You must pass through that hole called Sirens' Grotto to get here. That limits its usability by one group. With no dragon ring in Western Wastes, and no mele bind point beyond Siren's Grotto, doing Necropolis can be a major pain if something goes wrong (ie if you take risks).

Temple of Veshan: The test area was fun, but the quest rewards for the increased difficulty made keeping CoV faction silly. Also, the faction bug made recovering from wipeout in this zone a real nusiance.

Plane of Growth: This place is just boring. The zone is way too easy and spread out. I normally have problems staying awake when my guild goes here. I really don't know how you could make such a large and open zone more challenging without making it impossible, especially considering you have to clear the zone to attack Tunare. I'd rather see it smaller and more concentrated. Sort of a Plane of Fear for level 60s.
