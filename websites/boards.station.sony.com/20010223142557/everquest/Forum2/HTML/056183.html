soloing != power

Let's look at the most POWERFUL classes in the game:

#1 Cleric

As the game currently stands, if you want to group at level 55+, you need a cleric. If you don't have a cleric, you don't have a group. I can't think of anything that defines power more than the ability to end the gameplay of 5 other people when you log off at the end of the night.

#2 Enchanter

There are several exp and loot camps that simply cannot be broken without an enchanter. If you don't have an enchanter, you don't break that camp for exp or you don't get that drop. That, my friends, is power.

#3 Rogue

There are a few exp and/or loot camps that are much more difficult to get to without a rogue. Without the rogue you must spend much more time getting to these areas which leaves open the possiblity of someone getting the camp first, or simply not having as much time to camp the exp or item.

#4 Warrior

It is excedingly difficult to do any epic event without a warrior. Yes, this isn't as important as the above three, yet it is still powerful non-the-less.

Now balance that against the ability to gain exp while waiting for a group. Sorry, soloing just dosn't stack up under the definition of power.

Frankly, people gripe about druids as druids chose a class that arn't as relyant on other players to be able to advance during a gaming session. What they don't understand is the following:

If you want POWER, play a cleric/enchanter/rogue/warrior, if you want to solo, play a druid/necro/shaman. Remember that the bassis of Everquest is grouping, and that all classes should be capable of grouping and bringing something meaningful to the group. If a class honestly wasn't intended to be in a group, Verant wouldn't of added the class to the game.

Anyone who complains about soloing is just letting the kid inside of them whine, "I have to wait for a group, you should have to also!"
