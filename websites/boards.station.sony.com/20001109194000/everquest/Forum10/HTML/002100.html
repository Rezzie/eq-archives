The market for handmade goods has always been weak. This is simply because there are so many alternatives in items and because armor is a one time purchase. That said, banded is still sold on a lot, along with other low end goods on druzzil Ro, which as the oldest split server in the game has some very advanced characters and twinks on (and has people with mana stones!). With trading between players, a lot revolves around knowing the prices and being at the right place at the right time. Good spots are places where trade skills can be practiced easily because of availability of resources, where a multitude of races can safely meet, or near city zones. Don't expect however to fetch more than 1pp for banded, because there are too many upgrades wedged in between it and fine plate by now. Also don't expect to find a hot market for reinforced leather. Only druids would be interested in it since they are the only class that is for a long time stuck on leather only (monks go for cured silk, all other classes for banded). Fletching isn't very profitable because bows are mainly useful for pulling or a stand off weapon for rangers when they have to pull out of the fray for a while, and almost everyone who does use a bow makes his own arrows, pottery just doesn't have any aplications that can't be bought from stores or have better variations dropped from monsters (with the exception of cooking gear, which isnt that much in demand). Cooking and brewing don't bestow any benefits which cant be gotten from store bought items either, so are also just fun skills. And of course jewelcraft is something only enchanters can do well.

In short, don't depend on tradeskills as your main source of income. it can yield a modest profit and is fun to do (ask balkin ), but time efficiently speaking you are better off killing monsters and selling off loot. Trade skills are mainly there to add flavour to the game, and to give players something to do beside hunting.

Good luck finding your niche in the market.

Actually, the present of twinks tends to bring down the prices of items not raise them. It does not matter if the twink has a lot of money or not. It is because of the supply and demand. When there are a lot of twinks, there is a large supply of higher value items at the lower level. Since these twinks do not need the items, their demand for them goes down. A high supply with a low demand makes prices lower in an attempt to get rid of the item.

You can see this economic pattern with new to old servers. When servers are new, the prices are high. For example, a banded tunic on a new server is not unheard of for selling at 60-80pp. While on an old server, it would sell for about 15-20pp. In fact, you can usually get a full set of banded on an old for the price that you get a tunic on a new server. As new server progress to becoming old, and new and better items are descovered and filter to the community, the prices of older lower items drop. Remember, it is the rarity of the item that tends to make it cost a lot. Not the fact that every twink has one.
