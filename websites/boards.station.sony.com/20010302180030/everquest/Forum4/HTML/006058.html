Station Member 		posted 02-16-2001 09:36 AM           
An interesting thread in another forum has been examining a number of ways to repair the "player driven economy" that is basically crippled on every server more than a couple of months old. The essence of the dicussion follows: (And yes, I'm sure that this has been discussed before, but it's certainly worthy of keeping up where Verant can read about it. Who knows what might be possible?)
First the problem. The economy is designed in such a way that on any new server, before very long, the market is glutted with platinum, and middle to high end items. Things that could be created by players advancing their trade skills quickly become meaningless, and almost all the trades fail to be practiced because it's a waste of time, loss of money, and doesn't do anything to advance you. The steep rate of inflation quickly drives the price of most worthwhile items into the stratosphere so that the actual *new* players will not be able to afford most items until they are far beyond the level of needing them. Add to this the immediate habit of the high level players "twinking" their next characters with gluts of platinum and powerful items, and the situation becomes dismal quickly.
How could it be repaired? With some methods that nobody with a bunch of nice stuff would like. Example: Armor and weapons should have a "wear out factor". They should decay over time, and without required maintenance, eventually break, requiring a complete (expensive) overhaul, or disappear entirely. Warnings could be given as items approach end of life, and perhpas their effectiveness could go down. The repairs could become various trade skills that tradesment could do to earn good money, and by NPC's as well, to flush some of the limitless plat out of the system. We already have fishing poles that break. Why not Ghoulbanes?
Next, have NPC's actually offer a fair price to sell things in the game. This would also provide a money sink to get some of those trillions of inflated PP's out of the game.
And for fun, how about some NPC's that could actually pickpocket *YOU* during a battle? Not a lot, mind you, but enough to be annoying. Or who can loot some of your change from the corpse when you die. Not a huge impact, but every little bit would help.
There were more ideas, but this is too long already. But in sumamry, couldn't *something* be done to try to stabilize the economy more like what it would be for the first few months on a new server?
