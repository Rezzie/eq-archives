Station Member 		posted 10-04-2001 10:14 AM           
Essentially you can train at a guild trainer up to 20 in any trade skill. After that, no matter what your level is, you can raise it by practicing it.

You don't HAVE to train 20 points in, actually--you can just start practicing right away and it will still raise. You do not have to put an initial point into any trade skill in order to practice it, but it helps.

Ordieth117
Station Member 		posted 10-04-2001 11:49 AM           
The money part comes from buying the components so you can practice. So, it should read time and money.

Take jewelerycraft for an example. You buy the jewelery bag, the silver bars, and the malachite. You combine these things until they trivial out, then you move up. Eventually you're doing platinum plus malachite. After that, you're doing things like silver plus peridots.

You've spent a LOT of platinum (money) and an even larger amount of time, but finally, your level one (or two, or three, or whatever) character has raised their skill to 100+ (or even 200+) in Jewelerycraft.

I can state, on no uncertain terms...


NON Class/Racial tradeskills can reach any skill level at any exp level. Restricted only by time and cash.

Only 1 trade skill can go above 200 (upto 250), after that all others can reach a Max of 200.

Class/Racial trades (this includes Tinkering, Alchemy and Make Poison), are seperate from the 200+ restrictions. I do not know about a level based restriction however.

So, in theory, a Gnomish Rogue could have a 250 Tinkering Skill, a 250 Make Poison skill, AND another trade skill at 250.

All times are PT (US)
