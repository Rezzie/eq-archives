For this to be fixed they will have to change the networking prodicols from UDP to TCP/IP or something of the equivalent. Using the UDP protocols enables the rest of the world who still dial up to be able to play. Until Dial up modems and low speed connections become a thing of the past, it will never change.

Because of the UDP protocol, users can still play the game with a minimum connection of 24.4kbps. Think of all the people out there who couldn't play the game because they can't afford, or broadband connections aren't available in their area.

Your warping is caused by packet loss. Using UDP vs. TCP/IP is the reason you see the warping. UDP has NO error correcting where as TCP/IP has an error checking capability. When you lose the information packet that contains the placement info for that NPC, the next packet with the info will cause it to apear to warp. Now your problem with your broadband connection is that when you lose packets, you lose a lot more than the people on a slower connection.

as with every connection problem i allways suggest below..

Remove and then reinstall the Transmission Control
Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP). To do so, follow these steps:

1. Click Start, point to Settings, click Control Panel, and then double- click
Network.

2. On the Configuration tab, click TCP/IP, and then click Remove. Repeat this
step for each instance of TCP/IP before continuing to step 3.

3. Click OK, and then click Yes when you are prompted to restart your computer.
(You might also get a warning that your network is not complete choose yes to continue)

4.(After Reboot) Click Start, point to Settings, click Control Panel, and then double-click
Network.

5. Click Protocol, click Add, click Microsoft in the Manufacturers box, click
TCP/IP, and then click OK.

NOTE: If receive version conflict error messages during this process, click No
when you are prompted to keep a newer version of the file.

6. Restart your computer.

7. Verify and enter again any information provided to you by your internet service provider.

9. Restart your computer.

10. If these steps do not resolve this behavior, repeat the steps, but remove and
reinstall all installed network components.

11. If the problem still occurs, check with your Internet service provider to
verify your Internet settings.
