Specifically mentioned in the past were high quality silks made by Erudites, bags by halflings (presumeably weight reducing), and leather armor by wood elves and (I think) halflings. If there was anything else I can't remember.

At the moment 115 is the max, and my suggested progression is:

Using spiderling/spider silks to get to 20-ish, then raw silk to 36. Switch to cured silk (masks), since silk is far easier to find than mq pelts. This will take you into the 80's, but you'll fail a lot at first. You of course need to get brewing up to 60-ish to not fail at heady kiolas.

After cured silk, do quivers until 115. I did reinforced leather, but that requires smithing & lots of running. I'm at 104 now, haven't bothered recently, since I've been using all my HQ cat for leather padding

Spider silk - best place I know of is Crag Spiders in EK. They can drop as many as 6 at a time, and if you're 30+ you can mow through them quickly. Carrion spiders in the area can also drop as many as 4.

For the lower level tailor, the dune tarantulas in NRo are probably your best bet. The ones in WK are few & far between, and they and EC/WC spiders don't seem to drop many silks.

EK also seems to be one of the best places to auction for HQ pelts or silks.

NK is pretty good to hunt for pelts too.

I'm beginning to think I should write the Dark Race Guide to Tailoring here. Sheesh.

Anyhow,modern Tailoring for the Newbie:

First: Bash things. Get up a level or two.
Second: Once you can flatten spiderlings safely,start doing so. Get a sewing kit(large is best). Make threads from spiderling silk,and threads into bandages. Scavenge any kills people leave for pelts or silk. Get a skinning knife and reduce the non-HQ ones(or any wolf pelt) to LQ and combine with silk threads for backings. Take some patterns along and make patchwork from ruined ones,or raw silk armors from swatches of spider silk if you're lucky to nail some. This'll get you into the low 30's max,31 is max for padding.
Third: Learn to smith. Whack out toolboxes until you hit smithing 51(I believe),and start making yourself studs. These stack now,BTW. Now when you find MQ pelts,make studded instead of patchwork. This will get you up to tailoring 51.
Fourth: Learn to brew a little. Heady Kiolas,namely. Use em to make cured silk masks out the wazoo. This'll get you to tailoring 82.
Fifth: This is where every HQ pelt you scrounged up and saved come in. Make reinforced masks out of the HQ wolf,quivers from HQ cat,and backpacks from HQ bear. Packs sell for money to keep you skilling up. Reinforced takes you over 100,and quivers can max you out at 115+.

I have more fun tailoring than I do with any other tradeskill. Any twink with a bucket of money can up the other stats until they get to the point where tailors are every day-having to forage for components.

I hope more and more trade recipies require foraging,hunting for parts,and all the rest of the things that make players have to TRADE with each other for stuff that then gets made into things useful for all. I don't have to camp things to trade.

Do I think some things are a little too tough? Yep. Maybe temper components shouldn't spawn so rarely-more like HQ pelts do. Jacinths and blue diamonds are insane mad tough to get one's hands on. But I don't think high end or even many middle end items should be off-the-vendor completely.
