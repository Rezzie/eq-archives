The simpliest way to find out without being attacked by them is to target them and then conning them (to con them, simply hit your "C" key when you have them targeted or right clicking on them). If they show Threatening or Ready to Attack, beware as they will attack you as long as they are dark blue, white (black), yellow, and red. Greens will only attack you if your very low on hit points (but not always) or sitting down.

Otherwise positive non aggro'ing factions are as follows:

Dubiously
Apprehensively (at this level you can purchase from a merchant)
Indifferently
Amiably
Kindly
Warmly
Ally (The highest faction you can get)

Hope this helps.

You will also need to pay close attention to the "faction hit" messages you get when you kill a creature with an associated faction.

Often times, creatures with a faction associated will give multiple faction hits-- your faction standing with their group will decrease, and if they have opposing factions your standing with that group or groups will increase.

So if killing something results in a negative faction hit with a group that you will need to interact with, you know not to kill any more of those. These interactions can be very non-intuitive. For example, doing the Bard "postal" quests results in a negative faction hit with the Ring of Scale. This is the Kunark dragon faction, who are apparently enemies of Bards throughout Norrath. Conversely, killing most anything in Mistmoore Castle results in positive faction hits with Ring of Scale and League of Antonican Bards.

You can /con pretty much anything from far enough away that it won't attack you. If you can select it, you can /con it.

quote:
Im not saying tag characters with a color(/con already does that), im saying at least a summary of where you are to date with the list of factions.

Con color only tells you the creature's level relative to yours. It tells you nothing about faction. The con message tells you how they feel about you at the time. This may be faction-related, but isn't necessarily. Some monsters are always "scowling" but killing them doesn't result in any faction hits.

The point I'm trying to make is that faction is a "hidden" attribute; you are supposed to discover it for yourself. A list of factions would be providing you information that you are not necessarily entitled to. For example if you've never encountered a creature on faction "X", do you deserve to know if you are KOS to faction "X"? For that matter should you even know there is a faction "X" until you kill something on that faction (and get the resulting faction hit message)?

It's part of the intrigue and mystery of the game that your actions have long-term consequences, and that those consequences aren't necessarily obvious.
