I think it's great that some guilds have been able to beat some uber mobs.

The trouble is that I doubt that the average guild is going to be able to come close the their achivements, ever. And to top it off, Verant is changing the rules of the game to make the high level encounters even harder? Give me a break.

Now, I don't know this for a fact, but isn't it true that even the most uber guilds haven't been everywhere, or been able to kill everything?

I want to be able to feel uber someday. I want to be able to look forward to staring into the gaping maw of some uber mob, and coming out victorious, but I don't think that I ever will because I'm not a politition. I'm not persuasive enough to get enough people together, to take on the high level encounters. Nor do I have the time to devote to such an endevour.

So what this means is that more and more content is being added that I will never be able to see, and I would venture to guess that this is also true for 95% of all the people who play EQ.

What I think is probably going to happen is that another gaming company is going to realise all of this too. They're looking right now at these boards, and very very closely at EQ as a whole. They're going to make a game for the 95% of us, and eventually we're all going to move over to them, and EQ is going to die.

I commend the artists, coders, and all those that had, and continue to have a hand in the creation of this beutiful game. I would hate to see such effort be wasted, but I'm thinking that some of their best work (the high level encounters, and areas) are going to be mostly unseen, and eventually untouched when less and less people play until the entire game is just a dustbowl of emptiness because the management insisted on makeing it harder and harder, until it just wasn't fun enough to keep us. Especially when there is huge competition looming on the horizon.

The risk reward equation is a simple one when you're the only game in town. No risk is too great, and no reward is too small.

What I'm really saying is please tune the vast majority of the game for the small guild that doesn't have all the time in the world to devote to it.

I'm sure that the people at Verant have studied statistics. Wouldn't it be more prudent to design the game around the center of the bell curve? Shouldn't the uber stuff be for the 3/4 mark on that curve instead of the bleeding edge?

Also shouldn't it be made with replayability in mind? If I'm looking at 60 as my goal, and it took me two years to get there, am I going to want to devote that much time and effort again with a different character?

So I lift a 50lb weight and get a cracker. Mmm cracker. That being the beginning game.

Then you entice me to lift that 5000lb weight to get cookie. Hmm.. That's a lot of weight.

Maybe I'll go talk to these other folks who are willing to give me an entire cake for lifting 500lbs..

Hey, don't get me wrong, I think this is a great game, truely. I'm just saying that it's already too difficult. Too time consuming. Requires too much effort to achive that virtual goal whatever it is.

Making it even harder, and more time consuming for the 5% of the playerbase that are fanatics just doesn't make any sense.
