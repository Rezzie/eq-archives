This is the way we slay monsters. We do not have the weapon damage nor ac/hp to fight any blue mob toe to toe. Hence we are masters of kiting.

What is most interesting is the misconceptions formed in the game. Just like in real life, people see what they wanna see, believe what they wanna believe.

One of the first class to kite is actually the Shaman, who get sow 5 levels before druids. But Shamans weren't that popular of a class in the early days of eq, so few saw/knew how effective the Shaman class really are.
Several other classes fear kite very early on.

The latest misconception giving Druids a bad rep, is the multi-kiting. A difficult and challenging technique. It has been a viable strategy when verrant fixed a necro spell bug, that also solved the Ensnare problem.
I first saw this strategy used by, believe it or not, Wizards! Many months ago.
For Druids, this is an extremely mana extensive technique, requiring high wis ~170 or lots of mana items.

We survive because of our Outdoor versitility. Whether it is Snare-Kite, Root-Dot, Fear-Kite(animals only), we have these options available. Bring us into dungeons and our powers are greatly diminished. Which of course is why most of our equipment is in dungeons.
What makes me somewhat annoyed is the class-envy post, declaring our method of fighting in the world of Norrath is unfare, void of danger, and inbalancing towards the more group oriented classes. (sorry some people only get to play an one or two hr session. Not much time for group then)

Everytime I read a nerf post about kiting of some manner, I get a chuckle when Necro's, Shamans, Wizards, Rangers, etc. chime in and voice their protest to the nerf.

btw.
I've "kited" with three different classes.
Druid, Enchanter, Paladin.

As a Druid I pride myself on playing my char the way she was obviously intended. Soloing with the exception of the occasional dungeon trip. Our power calls on the forces of nature and thus we are almost defenseless in most dungeons. If you are not prepared for this lifestyle in game then by all means do not play a druid. We were meant to be outside, we rule the outdoors, we do not have to wait or beg for sow, heal or ports. . We were never meant to be primary healers in dungeons, any druid who does this is only looking to die. Simply put druids must not get hit, period. Everything we have is designed for kiting so thats what a good druid does, learns to kite without getting touched. Most druids are careful about where they kite, they usually drag thier kill off to a quiet area,if you find yourself in an area a druid has marked for kiting, move, it's simple. Another thing druids were designed for and most seem to over look is aiding and assisting people, but sadly few druids realize this. We are one of the few classes whos good buffs are not self casting only. hint. I also highly doubt that u saw one druid kiting 3 or 4 mobs at once without help from the side somewhere..we simply do not have the mana to do this, unless it's a pack of greenies.
BTW a druid can begin kiting at lvl 14 once they get sow..snare..and blast..that easy. Another misconception is that druids can kite most yellow and red mobs...wrong..once in awhile we can do this but more often then not we get resisted so much we oom half way thru the fight. Hell at my lvl I still get resisted by low green mobs sometimes. So to all of you who have druid envy all I can say is...try it.
