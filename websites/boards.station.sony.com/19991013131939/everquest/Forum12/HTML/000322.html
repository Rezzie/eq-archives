In another post I came up with a bunch of "flavor" summonings that I thought would make the Magician a lot of fun to play and more valuable to a group. I am completely in favor of this, I think it adds a great deal to the game.

That said, I have played a Magician and a Wizard to the 12 lvl (Wiz is finally 13 - Yeah!) and there was NO time when the Wizard could consider taking on a yellow creature. exp. gain aside (which, it could be argued, is offset by the pet), this has a much larger impact then you might imagine. Because the Wizard is not able to take on yellows, he's not making the money that the Magician character is making. With less money he has no resources to buy armor... armor that the Wizard would have gotten much more use out of.

"You don't know a character till you play him to the 20 lvl" - I agree with this, however... The impact of a lower-level character not being usefull to a group is a bigger handicap than just having to solo.

As a 1-11 Wizard I had a very hard time finding groups to take me - no food/water/bandages, no buffs, no tanking ability, and my DD was/is matched by almost every other spell caster (and Enchanters have such good buff/debuffs who wouldn't want them). So? Well, look at it this way, while you were (or should have been) forming good relationships with other characters, relationships that are essential for continuing your characters developmnet, I was the wall flower.

This is why I was doing all that bleeting (plus, some prankster changed me into a sheep for awhile). That, and the fact that I want my gun to be bigger then yours =P

We all need more non-combat ("flavor") spells for this to start to feel more like a real world. To think that the only spell research that would ever get done is offense/defense is foolish. Military applications may come fist, but the private sector is always going to turn firepower into fireworks.
