First off no matter what weapon you get you should practice 1HB, 2HB, and 1HS at ever level.

Before level 10 you are capped to something like a maximum of 20 damage no matter what weapon you use. After 10 it goes up a little, after 20 it goes up again, and after 30 I think there is no longer a cap of any kind.

An Ogre war maul will be capped until you are level 20. So it effectively becomes like a 10/50 weapon... that is not very good.

The RTS is a much better choice. The best choice at your level however is probably the Glowing Wooden Crook. They are a little harder to find than an RTS, but if you ask around enough someone will sell you one. I don't know what they go for, but I would pay about 20 or 25 for one and probably no more.

If you can't afford a GWC then go with the RTS. Definitly a nice weapon for the first 15 to 20 level of your career.

Station Member 		posted 03-01-2000 01:15 PM           
Let me clarify what I was saying earlier.

1.) Maxing skills. What I am saying is not that you should keep all 3 skills maxed, just that you should TRY to keep them as high as possible. It is impossible to cap all 3 by 5th level, but it is possible starting at 5th level to cap one of them by 6th and then practice with the others so that by level 15 they are all capped.

2.) Damage. Yes below level 26 the individual weapon damage cap is DMG*2+1. However there is also a hard cap based on your level and class. If you are a level 1-9 warrior wielding a Executioners axe (25/50) you will never do more than 20 points of damage because that is your cap. At level 10 I think it goes up some. Then at level 20 it goes away completely. Shamans have a different cap. At 20 their cap is 50. At 30 it appears to go away completely. The reason you have heard of Shamans hitting for 122 is because they were much stronger at the time than you are now, and probably higher level. These things all raise your damage cap. The end result is that useing a weapon that has a base damage higher than 10 at when you are below level 10 limits the weapon.

3.) The Bloodforge is a decent 2hander, but not exceptional. It is a little slow for good training and if you have a few misses in a row it is a lot harder on you and can cause you to die where with a faster weapon that would be less of an issue. Also as you can see it's damage cap should be 27 but yours is 19 because you are not yet level 10. All in all the best choice before level 10 is the RTS followed closely by the GWC and finally by the Bloodforge. The Ogre War Maul is a 20+ weapon.

4.) The real damage formula:

(DMG x 2 + (level-25)/3)

This is the reason that the limit before level 26 is always Dmg*2+1. This limit is also raised by your STR after level 20. I am not sure what the formula for that is however.

Regardless, I would say you got yourself a good weapon for a decent price. I would suggest looking into getting a magical scimitar and 1HB weapon. Until you find those keep practicing all 3 skills.
