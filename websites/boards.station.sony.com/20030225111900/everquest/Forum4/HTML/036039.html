"The best class to group with is a person you enjoy grouping with."

Absolutely, Ice. But only if having fun is your goal. Like in Real Life, the best place to get a job is somewhere cool, where you love what you do and the people are great - only sometimes what you need more than a great job is a job that pays the bills.

Getting xp in EQ can be like paying the bills, and if that's your aim then the company you keep doesn't matter too much so long as the bar is rising fast.

Now, I am with Ice, personally, and would always choose good company over good xp. But if fast xp in a duo is what you want, then as a cleric I'd go for either a high-damage pet class like a mage or beastlord, or fear-kite with a shadow-knight or ranger. They snare, you use your longer-duration Invoke Fear, then you stun to keep it from moving about too much. Should be able to avoid downtime altogether that way.

You can duo with almost anyone. Necros are better than mages, since all you have to do is buff and heal the pet, and will have very little downtime.

Mages are good as well, but they don't have a means of mana regen, so you'll have more downtime, but their earth pets do make excellant tanks, so that would work out.

Enchanters that used charmed pets are good as well, but a little tricky. You need to be quick with a root if charm breaks. With the clarity line, you're downtime should be mininized.

Beastlords are also good to work with, they can solo quite well, but your healing and buffing should allow for less downtime.

When you get to 56, you get that nice summoned hammer that procs for up to 151 damage. This is where it gets fun. Grab a tank (war, pally, or sk), and go someplace where you can single pull. Give the tank a couple of seconds to grab aggro, and start hitting the mob in the back. You can do a lot of damage yourself, but you need the tank to keep from getting creamed. I've done this in Keal, ME, and Velks. The xp is ok, not as good as in PoP, but if you're having fun, that's all that matters.

Any class that can snare.

Keep that cleric Fear line up. You have both regular Fear and a line devoted to Fearing undead. Stay with the regular line, as it works on undead as well.

It will be extremely repetitive... but if xp is your goal, rapid pulls with no damage to heal can be very fast xp. This also allows you to increase your melee skills as well, as you will be adding to the damage as it creeps away.. snared & feared.

If you are an evil cleric (Marr forbid), you can easily earn a clicky-snare necklace. If that is the case... any class under the sun that can do damage in any fashion would work for your plan to duo.
