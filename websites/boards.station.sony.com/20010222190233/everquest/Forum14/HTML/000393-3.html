Healing:

Druids (and Shaman) want to be better at healing. Uh, AnakinJay, "summoning a player" (as in, to whisk a player from its current location to one's feet, ala 'Call of the Hero') is not the same as an unnatural, ala "summoned" creature. Not even close.

One's a Conjuration, a single spell that pulls somebody from there to here. The reverse of a Wizard's port and a Druid's ring, if you will.

The other is an unnatural, magical force. Being completely and totally alien to the natural balance of nature (ie, a "summoned creature" in terms of what can be affected by 'ward summoned' is a creature not of Tunare - a construct of magic or unholy force) and thus, afflicted by 'ward summoned' because of its disruption of the natural balance of Life.

You want an ability to resist summoning? I grant that is a fine power, and one that at least one class ought to have, whether resist-based or immunity-based. I also like the idea of Druids doing it. 'Bond of the Land' would be my perception of this spell. You cast this on a group-mate (not just anyone) and they receive the Bond, providing them a resist to any attempt to summon them while under its affect. The power of the resist would be equal to that of their magic resistance at the time of the check, so high magic resist will help this spell, but the tashan line can hinder it. Etc.

This includes Gate, Portal/Circle, Creature 'Summoning', and Evacs. You resist these as if they were all an offensive spell, even if you cast them yourself (ever tried to 'fear' yourself?) and they are only successful given a failed resist check. Which means that your 'Bond of the Land' can prevent you from gating, or cause you to be left behind in an Evac.

An upgrade to the spell might be 'Pact of the Land' that provides IMMUNITY to summoning, but also immunity to the aforementioned gate, portal/circle, creature 'summoning' and evacs.

Just because you can be left behind in an evac or port, or have to resist your own gate spell, doesn't make it useless. It will have its uses. Divine Aura (Harmshield, etc.) prevent all spells, beneficial and otherwise, from affecting the recipient including ports and evacs, so why not this?

I've read a LOT about Druid healing, healing rate vs. speed of casting, fizzle rate on high level spells, etc... and all in all, I'm overwhelmed. There are so many conflicting voices on this from so many different people that without personal experience in playing a Druid at high levels, I have no idea where the Druid is coming from.

Nonetheless: I've recently grouped as an enchanter with a shaman, druid, paladin, magician, and necromancer. We pulled single targets in an open area and would debuff them, send in pets to hit them a bit, and sometimes snare/darkness and fear them. Rarely was my ability to crowd control even used. Choke was useless, as it was overwritten by necromancy. One of my debuffs was useless as the shaman did it regularly, the other was overwritten by the shaman. I performed a tashan, a single debuff... and looked for opportunities to get my pet to join the fray.

Not exactly my cup of tea, but I didn't brew it and I wasn't going to go looking for a mug of joe instead.

The Druid was our primary healer, with the shaman his backup. That Druid could barely keep up with the damage our Paladin received from a mob completely debuffed by my and the shaman's spells, even with two pets hitting it at once (their bash is SUPPOSED to stun it a little, right?) and mine thrown in for good measure when possible. Generally, the Druid was able to get a DoT off right after my tashan and either melee or meditate as needed until he had to start healing, and then that was all he did. If we feared the mobs, we won easily. If we couldn't...
well, if we couldn't fear them (too high) then we did a LOT of letting one guy build aggro and run the mob in circles so the pets and Paladin could hit it. Otherwise, the Druid would OOM and just not get enough to heal without pulling the Shaman into the equation too.

Other than nuking like a wizard, giving us all SoW (which is tactically a wonderful thing, but only when it's needed) and skin buffs, and thorns when the fight started, the druid was kinda bored. A few times we complained that he would melee when he should have been meditating, as he didn't have enough mana to heal in the first place.

If I could have asked for one more ability for that Druid to have instead of his single-cast healing spells to enhance the fight, it would have been a form of fast regeneration. Ideally, the spell would be a 20 second healing spell. You tag someone with it and in 20 seconds, they've "grown back" quite a bit of health. During that time, their combat stats and AC also 'grow' proportionate to the amount of time the buff is on the person. When their health maxes out or the duration expires, the spell ends - but until then, this is a regen that would stack with "regeneration" (it's primarily a healing spell, after all) that would both allow the druid to cast it once on a target and then continue using standard heals to supplement in an emergency, or tag one person with this and jump back up to melee again.

It seems like this could be something that would be added to make the Druid more group friendly. It seems like this wouldn't interfere with Clerics, if stacking were not an issue. Of course, two Druids in a group couldn't stack this spell on top of the same person twice to heal them at a doubled rate, but that's its limitation - it's healing over time.

Just a few minor thoughts. Here I'm wishing I knew more about Druids and had less bad experiences with "dr00ds", as I've got this huge bias sitting here keeping me from WANTING to care much about 'em.
