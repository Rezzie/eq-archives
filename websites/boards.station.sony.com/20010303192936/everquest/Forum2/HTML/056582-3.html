Alright, before I get started with my really serious bit here, allow me to say a few things:

The Rogue, a widely debated class in EQ. No spells, where most other-game Rogues had them in some form. No chance of heavy AC armor (save so-called "uber" items), unlike many other games. These are 2 things EQ has much different from other games w/ Rogues (Thieves) in them. References to the Final Fantasy line, Diablo, and upcoming expansion to Diablo II come to mind. But you know what? Those things don't really bother me that much...

The EQ rogue was a whole new take on the Rogue class. Light armor so we can be sneaky and speedy, low HP because we're not built physically strong, we're limber. Double Attack and Dual Wield skills nearly that of a Warrior, with added abilities to steal money / items, find and disarm traps, and poison our weapons (gving us a psuedo spell-system of sorts), along with doing the most damaging melee attack in the game, the Backstab. But above all else, they're a FUN class, and overall, tie with the Bard as my favorite.

I enjoy the fast-paced action of a Rogue. Hit 4 times, backstab, "HOLY CRAP the mob thinks I'm the girl he was with last Friday when he was drunk and wants a piece of me! Taunt, taunt, heal, backing outta battle a second... Ok, I'm back in... WHOA, whaddya mean outta mana? Crap, I'm running for it!" This is how my Rogue was fighting when Evade was implemented, at the time against lvl 30-35 mobs in the Cazic-Thule zone. With TWO Warriors in the group. Then it was nerfed to be slightly less effective, so I was forced to wait 10 seconds of Aggro time before I attacked, lest I be dubbed "Sir Hadanelith Mana-Sink Raswrolski of the Veeshan Server..."

Now you are suggesting another tweak to Evade, to make it even less effective. As it is, I have to time my Backstabs to come just before Evade is ready or I have a serious problem on my hands. Aside from my lvl 35 Rogue I also play a 51 Druid, Trewaldian, both on Veeshan server. I've worked with many rogues, most who don't meddle in the ways of poison near as much as I do. ("It's useless" they say, unaware of the wonders 5 bags of Lixt Wing Dust can do in Lower Guk, dead-side.)

These Rogues are the ones I am healing while the Cleric takes care of the main tank, these are the Rogues I am sometimes forced to watch DIE because it is one Rogue or half the group, save the Rogue or save the Tank and Casters, that is the choice that must be made in some dire circumstances. Evade, force a mob to attack someone on the other side of the zone? Hardly. More like Evade, give the Rogue a fighting chance at making it to the Xp/Loot portion of the battle. A rogue has roughly 1/2 - 2/3 the HP of a warrior, depending on stats and equipment, and roughly 2/3 - 3/4 the AC depending again on Stats/Equipment. If you were to put a value on this, say the Warrior has an 2500 combined score of HP/AC (1750 HP, 750 AC) at a given level, a rogue of the same level would have approximately a score of 1375-1730 (875-1050 HP (very extreme circumstance), 500 - 680AC). Then you suggest that a Rogue should have nearly no chance of Evading more than 4 hits worth, thus forth being able to take the damage a Warrior might? There's a huge difference between 1750 HP and 900 HP in case you haven't noticed.

I admit that my numbers are rough, but according to things I have observed with my Wood-Elf Rogue and other Rogues (Of all races, including Barbarian), and most Human / Barbarian / Troll warriors, this seems to be pretty close to on par. Now take a mob such as a Drolvarg Sentry in Karnor's Castle, which can hit for roughly 100 hp... the rogue has 2/3, BEST CASE SCENARIO, the chance of surviving multiple blows compared to a Warrior of the same level. (the numbers are a bit low in comparison to a Drolvarg Sentry, but even regular Drolvargs in Firiona Vie hit for up to approx 87). This is, of course, assuming that a Cleric is ready to heal your chain-mailed, dagger-wielding, low-HP'd, hard-hitting schnazz before you see the fabled Key Phrase: LOADING, PLEASE WAIT.

My point in case? I will always love my rogue, they will always be fun for me. No matter how much you nerf, degrade, humiliate, torture, and utterly DESTROY my favorite character, I will keep playing him. It will be hard, and I'd best find a way to make more money for Corpse Summons and Resurrections, because it will be one heck of a lot harder to stay alive, but I will do it.
