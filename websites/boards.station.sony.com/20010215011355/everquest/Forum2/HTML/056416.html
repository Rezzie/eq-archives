I just have to add my two pp to this one-sided debate.

The reason warriors are upset about the changes to the hybrids, as I see it, is not that hybrids are getting new spells, discipline, skills and other advancements. I think most warriors, especially those +50, would agree that this is good for all melee types. The issue is that these changes, though not affecting warriors directly, change the FUNDAMENTAL way the warrior community views itself.

We made a decision to choose a class that has no magical abilities. None. No spells, no easy escapes like Gate (An over-balancing spell if there ever was one but that is another discussion) and no methods for healing ourselves save grouping or binding wounds like every other character in Norrath. We chose that sort of character. In exchange for no spells we would have the ability to use all weapons skills at some level or another and have the highest offensive and defensive ratings. This was a fair trade-off. We were dependent on the group and our much-loved clerics and in exchange we were the best at what we did.

By giving Paladins and Shadowknights the same defensive skills as warriors it changes that fundamental agreement. By making Rangers our equals in offensive skill it changes our long held belief that it was okay that we can’t heal ourselves; that it is okay magic users 20 levels below us can safely traverse areas we fear travel alone – because we were the best at what we did. No attributes were taken from warriors. We have the same skills as before. But our fundamental sense of that which we are as a class has changed.

Increase the defensive skills of Paladins and Shadowknights – but keep it below the warrior by even an insignificant amount. Increase the offensive skill of the Ranger but keep it below the warrior.

It is easy to quantify skill levels because they are numerical. Less easy to quantify are the values given a class by their skills (LOH, HT, Track and Sow) and spells (Healing, buffs and DOTs). They may not be perfect and they may not be the equals of the parent class spells but they certainly represent some level of significant gain by those Hybrid classes. Are they equal in value to the combat abilities gained by warriors through critical hits, crippling blows, higher skill levels and disciplines? I speak for myself but I truly thought it was a fair exchange. Spells for skills.

If warriors are a staple class then you must realize that changes in the basic skills DO affect warriors as a class. If there is no benefit to not using magic they why bother? I don’t want to play a magic user class. I want to be a warrior. I made a decision to forgo using magic because it let me do best what I liked in EQ – pure melee. An insignificant Hit Point difference and equal Armor Class do not equal the abilities inherent in the various hybrid classes.

Warriors are not upset that hybrids are getting improvements. Most warriors will agree that something needed to be done. Warriors are upset that by making such drastic changes, especially in the skill levels, you are changing the fundamental and defining elements of the warrior class.

Now, don’t even get me started on Disarm and the Cobalt drop rates………
