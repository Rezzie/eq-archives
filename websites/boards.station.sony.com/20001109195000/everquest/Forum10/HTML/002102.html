I just had one of those moments. I've always stayed away from caster classes because I'm just no good with spells...I'll blast a few times and then want to rush in and melee. About the only caster class I ever got very far at all (meaning beyond 5th level) was the shaman. And even then it seemed like alot of hard work to me.

Well, a good friend in EQ just recently started playing again, on Emarr where I first started playing EQ. For those of you who don't know me, all of my characters are on Luclin now...I only have one character on Emarr, a poor naked 18th level barbarian warrior (naked because, well, just always retrieve your corpse when you die, period).

So now I was stuck choosing between druid and necro, and I hate playing casters. Well, I have fond memories my dark elven rogues that I've played, so I made the typical dark elf necro.

Oh my word. I can't believe. I just sit there! That's all I do! Cast a DoT, /pet attack, and med. Geez! And it's fun! I can't believe it but it's fun! I was by the 'newbie log' last night, and I dot'ed a fire beetle. Well, invariable, about 4 more beetles materialized over the course of the battle, and a wolf besides. I'm only level 3, when I see that second beetle roaring up I'm like "Well crap, I'm dead." But oh no, who should come to my rescue? Good ol' Goner! Yeah! By the time the fight was over, I was low on health and oom, but Goner was still walking around with that stupid grin and there were ALOT of dead bodies around. My warrior could've never done something like that at his level. WoW!!


So now I'm going to beg for advice on playing my lil' inky necro...

- What kind of equipment should I be looking into buying the next few levels (keep in mind I seem to be the worst at acquiring wealth)? With warriors or rogues I always had a good idea of what kind of armor to look for, but I have no clue when it comes to dresses...

/giggles at the silly ogre err.. umm.. inky

Cloth is good for awhile, after ya start killing the larger spiders go for raw silk armor.

get yerself a sewing kit, combine 2 spider silks together and make a silk swatch.
save the silk swatches, yes they are stackable.

after ya get yer skill up to about 20 or so, then ya can start to make silk armor. Get a pattern from a vender (cost is only a couple sp) then combine the pattern with 1 silk swatch.

2 silk swatches for tunic and (legs?)

dont remember fer sure, check out eqtraders.com to make sure.

raw silk is basicly free armor since yer useing loot you've collected, this comes in handy for those who don't make money easily.

when ya hit lvl 12-13, go to Sister Island, that's when the money starts rolling in.
