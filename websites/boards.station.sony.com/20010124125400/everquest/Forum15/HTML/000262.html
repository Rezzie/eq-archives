I know I am going to open myself up to some flames here, but I am at the end of my bardish rope. I have a 53rd level bard, this is my primary character with over 130 days played, I have no other character close to 50th level. A few days ago I decided to retire my beloved alter ego due to the lack of usefullness of the class.

Ours songs, already weak versions of the real spells, all other hybrids get full versions just later in levels, have been chipped away at till they are just overall unreliable. The mana song is useful, but playing mana and being asked NOT to melee by clerics is not exactly playing a game, its watching other people play.

A well balanced group has no reason to invite a bard except to sing mana. A poorly balanced group may invite the bard to do groups right at the zone of maybe a karnors, but not harder stuff. The reason I decided at this time to stop is the haste fix, that was my finally straw, it no longer even makes sense to mem VoV with its poor, non stacking haste.

Bards were originally supposed to be a tri - class character warrior/wizard/rogue that has moved up to a new type of adventurer. That combo is 2/3rds melee (warrior/rogue) and 1/3 caster, (wizard the super damage spell class ), that all screams high damage output. There are many ways to fix the class, make songs effects = spell effect like the rest of the hybrids(remember we get a lot less songs then other classes get spells ), give us the rest of the suite of warrior/rogue skills ( would DA with a cap of 100 unbalance the game ?, would a backstab capped at 100 take anything away from rogues if first given at 40th level ), with lower caps of course, some stronger direct damage songs, would 100 points of damage be so unbalancing? I could give a hundred suggestions but i will stop here.

Non bards ask yourself, when you invite a bard into a well balanced group, what do you expect him to do for the group. Jack of ALL trades, master of none. Being almost good enough does not cut it in highlevel game play. Vision or no vision the class should be playable at ALL levels. I like this game and I want to play it, please please rethink the vision a little, it would be okay if high level bards got to play too, I promise it wont unbalance the game. Funny thing is the bard is my favorite class, and with a couple of tweeks I would love to play him again regularly.
