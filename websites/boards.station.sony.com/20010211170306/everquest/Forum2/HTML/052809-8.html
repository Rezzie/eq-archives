On the issue of CLASS BALANCE

There has been much talk lately concerning class balance. I am not going to tell you that the class I play (warrior) should be made more powerful than every other class. Nor am I going to tell you that other classes should be made weaker to balance them out. I give you a lot of credit for attempting to satisfy the masses without ruining a great game. However, there are some issues that need to be addressed.
I play on Rallos Zek, which as you know, is pure PvP. I have been reading the news postings and updates that have recently gone live with the latest patch as of 1/9/01. Many of the issues in the update deal with class balance- most specifically, hybrids. By reading the comments/ changes that have been implemented as of yet, I think Verant has done a great job in terms of dealing with balance issues on NON-PvP servers.
What Verant must understand is that a purely PvP environment requires some additional consideration. I will explain. On a NON-PvP server, classes are considered “balanced” if everyone in the group has a comparable contribution to the group. This means that every class has their specific job to do. (not just damage)
On a PvP server, however, classes are only balanced if there is no “sure thing” when characters of differing classes fight each other. By this I mean that there is no balance in a game where a Necromancer will ALWAYS beat a Wizard of the same level. Therefore, each class must have its own ability not only to defend its self, but also to inflict damage. Which is why hybrids have received the latest enhancements.

HOW CLASSES COMPARE IN PvP SETTINGS
(Classes are ranked from best PvP class to worst)

1) Bard-they can’t kill others fast, but they are good due to high mobility, charm, regen, and resists. The only reason they can sometimes beat a pet class is because they could at one point charm them against the owner. Can simply kite pure melee characters to death, given the room to do so.
2) Necromancer- has a pet for distraction/ spell interrupt, MASSIVE damage at higher levels, and their primary spells are poison and disease which most players don’t have high resists in. They also have nearly unresistable lifetaps and dots (splurt). They can simply cast Shadowstep to get away from melee chars while DOTS and pet kill the melee char. Can simply kite pure melee characters to death.
3) Shaman- Has high mobility with SOW, and has a weak pet for distraction/ spell interrupt, and SOME damage, and their primary spells are poison and disease which most players don’t have high resists in. They have regen, and heal to use in combat, and can cure ALL dots. They can also boost their resistances quite high. Can simply kite pure melee characters to death.
4) Druid- Has high mobility, good direct damage spells ( 2nd best in the game behind wizard) good resistance buffs like the shaman, and nearly unresistable DOTS (swarm line of spells) Can simply kite pure melee characters to death.
5) Mage- Direct damage spells almost as good as a wizard/druid, but their real power lies in the pet. The pet can inflict 2-3 times that which certain melee classes are capable of. And versus all pure melee classes, there is nothing they can do to avoid being subjected to this damage besides zone out. The mage simply kites the pure melee class till they are dead. If the pure melee tries to fight the pet, even if its blue con, the pet will most likely kill them in straight on melee. Not only that, but if the pet has a damage shield…its even worse. Casters against mages will most likely just root the pet and go for the mage.
6) Wizard-this class makes the best “gankers”. This means that they wait for a character to pull a mob, then attack the player while they are busy with a mob. They have the best direct damage spells in the game, but can be highly resisted with the right gear.
7) Enchanter-decent damage on DOTS, and direct damage spells, but if opponent has high magic resist, there is NOTHING enchanters can do against an opponent.
8) Shadow knight- Good melee skills, almost unresistable lifetaps, low resist DOTS (poison/ disease) and Harmtouch, which can do a sizeable amount of damage (970 damage at level 60)
9) Ranger- Good mobility with sow, they can heal, they have all the melee skills of the warrior, good melee buffs, nearly unresistable dots (swarm line) some direct damage spells, and a damage shield that could spell disaster for melee classes that use low delay weapons. They can simply kite pure melee classes to near death, and then finish them off with melee/ damage shield.
10) Paladin- Good melee ability, can blind, stun, cast minor damage, heal, and most importantly, Lay on hands. While warriors are supposed to have the most hitpoints of any class, this is not true. A paladin with LoH has more by far. Especially when you consider that they get superior heal.
11) Monk- the best of the pure melee’s in duel because they can inflict massive damage with regular melee as well as kicks in the 100’s. They also get some of the best damage/delay/ effect weapons in the game. They also get mend, which can be a great help when dueling.
12) Rogue- they can take advantage of poisons. Some high level poisons can inflict serious damage as well as debuffs. They can also backstab for serious damage.
13) Warrior-Even with massive a hitpoint base, warriors can be defeated by all pure melee classes, and every single caster class except cleric, and maybe enchanter if the warrior has high magic resist. Virtually helpless against any class that has a higher base mobility then they do, warriors are especially at the mercy of pet classes, whose pets can kill them 1 on 1, even without the help of the caster.
14) Cleric- While tuff to kill due to resistances and their ability to heal most damage inflicted to them, they will RARELY ever defeat an opponent by killing them. They simply don’t have the spells to do enough damage. Most, if not all of their damage spells are magic based, which tend to be highly resisted. Most casters will simply run out of mana before the cleric dies. Melee classes could win against a cleric, but it would take a long time if the cleric was able to cast heals.

* As you noticed, mobility is often the deciding factor in the fate of pure melee characters. (Monk, warrior, rogue) While the high level disciplines surely help when dueling other melee classes, they do NOTHING to help them against caster classes.

SOME SUGGESTIONS TO IMPROVE PvP (changes ONLY the way PvP works)

 Give normal resist rates to “unresistable” spells only to player characters-much like the patch that made direct damage spells do 2/3 of the damage to player characters. Melee classes only defense against spell casters is resistance gear, and that gear does nothing to prevent us from taking A LOT of damage because the spell is nearly unresistable. Examples: lures, splurt, winged death, Harm touch, life taps.
 Add disciplines to melee chars such as:
 REFLECTION-bounces back a % of the spell damage to the caster.
 SUMMONING-much like level 50+ melee npc’s (ice giants) can summon players to their side. (“you will not evade me”)
 IMMUNE-gives short duration (~10 seconds) of immunity to all types of spells. Long reuse time.
 Make critical hits occur MUCH more often, and crippling blows occur at 60% health rather than at 40%. The amount of time it takes to go from 40% health to death is too short a time to have a decent change at scoring a crippling blow. I am level 45, and have only scored 2 crippling blows in my entire career. And neither time did they turn the balance of power in the fight. Make them more useful. Also, what is the point of scoring a critical hit for less damage than the max normal damage of the weapon without a critical hit? (If I can make a regular hit for 97, why would a critical hit of 64 be useful?)
 Make a crippling blow do just that- cripple a target. For example, a crippling blow would result in a condition similar to “Snare”, or “Disempower”. ( lower armor class, agility, strength, etc) The way they stand now, crippling blows are so infrequent, that it would not be overpowering to enhance them.
 Make a critical hit stun the target for a short time.
 Give more melee classes the ability to mend, or a variation of it. (less healing power than monks get, etc)
 Increase the damage causing abilities of clerics in PvP situations. The way they are now, they usually don’t have enough resources to kill another player. All their damage spells are magic based, and therefore are resisted a lot. Increase damage output on existing spells for PvP only. Or remove the 2/3 cap on PvP damage for clerics. Add some variety in the types of damage spells clerics and enchanters can cast (poison, disease, etc)
 Increase the attack speed, and damage on range attacks in PvP ONLY. Give them double attack and reasonable delays, as well as a spell interrupt component. Maybe increase the range to be comparable to that of shock type spells.

I think you understand the gist of what I’m saying. What could a pure melee class do to win a duel against a class with higher mobility and the capability to inflict damage from afar? Answer: Nothing. They WILL LOSE. This is NOT a balance of the classes.
The only options are to zone, or die. And death has a stiff penalty on Rallos Zek if you have expensive gear.
