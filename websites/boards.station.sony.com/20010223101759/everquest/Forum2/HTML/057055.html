A handy summary of my post: five reasons why and how a bard is not balanced after level 50 and what can be done about it.

The chief imbalance that bards have comes after level 50. Kunark and Velious were designed to require "the perfect group" of a cleric, enchanter, warrior, plus three other of any class. Dungeon crawling is nearly impossible without these 3 classes, yet I find myself less useful than a druid or a shaman would be in a group like this. In addition, on multigroup raids (which nearly always have plenty of enchanters, clerics, and shamen), the only benefit I provide is clarity song and very, very pathetic melee damage.

Why is it that bards are so unuseful in these cases?

1. Haste songs do not stack with haste spells. We get 11 different haste songs, but the 2 that supposedly do stack require an instrument (meaning our only melee damage is from punching) and do not provide any noticably increased attack speed with enchanter hastes (most likely people are reaching max haste with just items + enchanter). In addition, a level 60 (SIXTY!) bard cannot haste a group to a greater extent than a level 16 (SIXTEEN!) enchanter can! (And the same is true of a shaman's first haste buff.) If haste songs are supposed to be a staple of our class, they should be enhanced a bit, don't you think? Suggestion: make all haste songs stack with haste spells, but not other haste songs or increase the amount of haste given by our songs.

2. Healing songs do not keep up with the amount of damage and amount of hitpoints of Velious and Kunark mobs after level 50. When a cube hits my party member for doubles of 200 points, 20 points of healing just doesn't make the grade. My suggestion: raise the amount of healing done by Niv's (level 47 healing song) and possibly also increase the amount of healing on Cantana (level 55 song).

3. Pixie strike (level 28 mesmerize song) is still too resistable. As an example, I dueled a ranger who is the same level as I. He took off *all* of his magic resist items (base magic resist of 24, I believe) and I put on all of my charisma items (bumping my charisma to 185.) I was resisted 9 out of 10 times when trying to pixie strike him! I cannot dream of replacing or helping an enchanter by doing crowd control with this amount of resists. Please reconsider the resist rate for this and song of twilight (level 53 mez song.) Making any enchanter mez overwrite our songs would also be a kind gesture.

4. The mesmerize resistant creatures in Velious have changed the dynamics of crowd control immensely. Charm is the prefered method of crowd control in Kael and other such places. However, bards cannot charm anything of significant level (it is possibly capped at level 55, but I am not positive about that) and charming takes huge amounts of mana. As a bard, I could be incredibly useful for crowd control if I could charm giants deep in Kael Drakkel. But I can't because of the level limit. I would suggest that bards get a charm song at level 50 or 51 that did not require mana (so that crowd control doesn't require the bard to sit for mana regen!) and could be used on anything blue or green (so bard soloing is not a significant issue). And while the programmers are at it, *pleeeeease* add no-charm tags to gods, major dragons, and any other powerful beings which are just like the no-mez tags on giants, some golems, cyclops, etc. Charming a god is funny, but it isn't balanced at all.

5. Here is a little known secret outside the bard community: in the course of battle, bards rarely get hit and raising defensive skill is nearly impossible. But when we do get hit (almost always because of more than one unmezed mob present), we die nearly instantly. On test server, you raised defensive skill on bards. We are not a defensive hybrid even with this change! Why? A ranger is a *better tank than a bard* because
1. a ranger can get nearly the same AC as us or even more because our armor at higher levels is chain class AC,
2. rangers have far more hit points, and
3. rangers dodge, parry, riposte, and have agility buffs along with a naturally high agility.
My suggestion: make us the other hybrid that is good at *offense*. Paladins and shadowknights make great tanks, but bards don't. Below level 50, bards are quite effective at damage dealing and poor tanks, why not the same past 50? Dual wield should be a clue we are geared for damage dealing.

Thank you for reading this and I look forward to good things to come!

This is a little nerdy, but I think it shows why I don't think Bards are well-balanced in the 50+ game, and I hope someone will pay attention:

At low levels, Casters have power which you can roughly calculate as:

(M * D * E) / C

where:

M = mana per time unit
D = duration of effect
E = apparent power of effect
C = mana cost per effect

Assume that Bards are roughly balanced at those levels.

At higher levels, ALL of these scale up for Casters. Average mana regen is higher, spell duration is longer, strength of effect is greater, casting cost is less, so total power may look something more like:

2M * 2D * 2E / (C/2) = 8 ((M * D * E) / C),

or 8 times the power they had earlier.

M never increases for Bards. Their limit is the amount of time it takes to cast a song, which never changes.

D never increases for Bards. Thier songs all have the same duration, and it never changes.

C never decreases for Bards. No song becomes cheaper.

That means that, in order to keep Bards balanced at high levels, the following equality has to hold:

2M * 2D * 2E / C/2 (caster) = M * D * e / C (bard)

where e = effectiveness of Bard song, which reduces to:

e = 8E, e.g. the Bard song needs to multiply in apparent effectiveness not by the factor of TWO that casters get, but by a factor of EIGHT.

They do not. They scale up linearly by level, if that. Some have additional effects added to them. That roughly balanced things prior to perhaps level 45, although Verant watered down the balance by making dual mistakes: 1) making each component effect weak (because the song had multiple components) and 2) making the resists higher (because the song did more).

In fact, multiple effect songs have a lot of drawbacks, and are virtually never as good as they appear on paper. The two biggest are: 1) they don't stack, quite often not even with other Bard songs that overlap in only one component, and 2) it is rare that all of the components of a song are used at one time.

This does not take into account two other severe limitations that Bard songs have:

1) Bard songs don't work if the Bard dies, camps, goes LD, leaves, or just wanders a little bit out of range
2) Bard songs only work on the group the Bard is in, and can never be used to help more than 5 other people.

Bards can melee, but meleeing often reduces singing effectiveness and vice-versa, because many songs need instruments, while other songs require too much attention to be paid to targetting to allow the Bard to melee. Further, the melee contribution of a Bard lesses in comparison to pure melee classes as they both increase in level. This implies that, in order to keep Bards balanced overall, their total casting effectiveness should increase FASTER than the casting effectiveness of pure casters.

Bards have a number of advantages that need to be considered when counter-balancing things:

1. They can sing on the move.
2. They are only interrupted if they get stunned.
3. They can sing indefinitely.

The first of these doesn't get any better as they level. The second gets worse comparatively, because Casters get channeling and become better able to cast through combat as they get higher in level, while Bards never do. The third is always a factor.

This is why I said, quite seriously, that Bard Bard party buffs should be BETTER in APPARENT effect than the equivalent buffs others can cast.

Verant seems to have a number of "rules" stuck in their heads when it comes to balancing Bards:

1. Bard songs are manaless and hence free. This is wrong, please read the previous posts in here explaining why.

2. Bards are "child" classes and as such, the apparent effectiveness of a song should never be greater than, and should usually be worse than, the "parent" class. Bards are NOT hybrid casters, they work MUCH differently, and this way of balancing them does NOT work at high levels.

3. Bards have a wider variety of things they can do than other classes. But things don't work out that way. Why? Because: a) we can only have the same 8 things memmed as anyone else; b) we are LESS able to modify our songlist in battle than others because it take us MUCH longer to mem AND we have to sacrifice doing things while doing so; c) as we level up, more and more classes have abilities which overlap ours, and theirs are better; and d) many of ours don't stack.

Absent a couple of ridiculous suggestions in here, Verant could make ALL of the changes on this list and Bards would STILL not be balanced at high levels. Verant really needs to think VERY CAREFULLY about how Bards ACTUALLY WORK if they want to pull this balance thing off.

Howdy Bards and non-Bards alike,

I've skimmed over the last few bard-talk threads, and read something that caught my eye. I'm not sure who stated it (It's late and I'm not about to go wade through another hundred or so messages), but it said that levels 20-25 are the weakest time for a bard...

I'd have to say that this is COMPLETELY and UTTERLY untrue. In fact, this is our strongest time! After all, if you do what most of the smarter bards would do at this level (I.E., twist Hymn of Restoriation, Chords of Discordance, and Denon's Disruptive Discord all while switching instruments), you wouldn't need a group--You could just solo your way to 30.

About ten days ago my bard, Ensis Arament, hit the big 20. Got my nice lil' surname, picked up my AoE Slow song, and ran on over to Lake Rathetear.

Now, a week an a half later, I'm level 29, and should be 30 by Wendsday.

It isn't a great accomplishment, but I'd just like to prove that I averaged nearly a level a day. This was while balancing out school and work in my schedule, so I had a maximum of 4 or 5 hours to play per day.

Did I group? No, not really. I grouped once, when I went to the Lake of Ill Omen for the first time. I vowed never to group at that place again... it was a complete waste of time, I could solo the monsters we were fighting, and wouldn't have to wait for the warrior to heal up, or the casters to meditate back to full.

Bards that don't seem to understand their true capability, and twink bards, have the strange notion that they have to group at these levels, and all other levels past 10 or so. They think that because most of our songs affect the whole group, we are a grouping class, and can't do anything without a meat shield in front of us and a cleric behind us. I'm just out to disprove that, since levels 18 - 40 or so is our peak times. We can solo those levels... and probably higher, up to 50, through charm kiting.

So, adding a level 20 mana song will appease those who don't know how to use a bard to his fullest, but it isn't required, nor is it a balance issue. I'd rather you take the enchanter's breeze away before giving the bard it, but that wouldn't be balancing either.
