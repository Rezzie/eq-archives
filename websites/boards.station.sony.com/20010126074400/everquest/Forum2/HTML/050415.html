What I find very strange is that Clerics currently have been very replaceable in Kunark. We do cast Complete Healing, but rarely do we depend on it for all fights in a normal group. (pardon me if I get the ratios a little off)

Pre-Velious mana efficiency :
Greater Healing - 1.9 hit points per mana
Superior Healing - 2.4 hit points per mana
Remedy - 2.5 hit points per mana
Divine Light - 2.6 hit points per mana
Celestial Elixir - 5 hit points per mana
Complete Healing - "Feeling lucky punk?"

A Druid/Shaman pair or combination can outdo a Cleric very easily, IF they were willing to try. Before we receive Celestial Elixir, we get 2.6 hit points per mana at best? Two Druids/Shamans get 3.6 hit points per mana with Greater Healing and can outheal a pre-CE Cleric for everything except Complete Healing. This does not even consider the regeneration spells they receive, nor does it consider the faster mana regen that Shamans are capable of beyond Clarity even, nor does it consider the advantages of spreading the buffing between the two, nor do I consider the group heals that are very rarely used by Clerics.

Post-Velious
Same deal except :
Celestial Healing - 3.3 hit points per mana

Woot! I can personally heal almost as efficiently as that Druid/Shaman combo with Greater Healing can! With Complete Healing, I can take them hands down.

Post-Druid/Shaman healing boost :
Druids and Shamans will gain the ability to heal at 2.4 at 49 (or 44) to 2.6 at 59. They will now be able to equal a Celestial Elixir Cleric who is not using Complete Healing.

The trend seems to be to allow a Druid/Shaman combination of two to heal as well as a similarly leveled Cleric. Complete Healing skews this, and the possiblity of a nerf to Complete Healing has too much of a ring of truth to it, particularly in light of the healing upgrades being discussed for Druids and Shamans. The ideal seems to be to force groups to have two healers rather than one as is currently prefered. Based on the assumption that Complete Healing is going to be nerfed, we have to ask ourselves. "What do we want the other healer in our group to have to work with?" My feeling is that I want the best healing possible. I think that is what is happening at Verant. Clerics will still be primary healers due to getting the spells at lower levels, being able to cast while being beaten on, being able to stop the beating with invulnerabilities and simply because our instinct says "Heal dammit!" rather than "Run!" or "Evac!" (I mean no offense to Druids. I pay more attenion to the health bars than to whether it is time to run or not. Evacs often pleasantly surprise me due to this.)

On the plus side, we may get to group with other Clerics. I believe I can tell an old school Cleric from a powerleveled one relatively easily. It might be nice to get to group with another Cleric to compare tactics. I have no clue how good I actually am compared to other Clerics because I don't get a chance to group with them.

On the down side, classes that have a difficulty grouping that aren't healers will have a harder time finding groups with a Complete Healing nerf. There will be one less spot in the group for them.

The new groups will ideally consist of :
Cleric/Druid/Shaman
Cleric/Druid/Shaman
Melee
Melee
Melee/Magician/Wizard/Bard/Necromancer/Druid/Shaman
Enchanter/Bard/Necromancer

A post Complete Healing nerf EQ will be a good time to be a Druid or a Shaman, it will be a bad time to be a Wizard, Magician or Necromancer. Basically, the same as it has been.

I haven't gotten to group with many of my fellow Clerics. I often meet you at raids and have seen you all around the game. I look forward to grouping with you and discussing tactics and showing off to you.

Complete Healing being nerfed will be the worst thing that could happen to the casters since highly resistant mobs. Clerics will NOT be the losers (except when that Cleric only item drops and we have to lotto against each other.) Besides, it will be nice to have an experience Resurrection back to the camp rather than a Paladin Revive from time to time.

If Verant wants to nerf Complete Healing. I would suggest increasing maximum group size first.

The mana efficiency of the healing is only one part of the equation. You also must consider the healing over time.

Clerics (with CHEAL) heal 10K of damage in 12.5 seconds (counting recharge time)

Druids with SHEAL (at 53) (assuming a 20% fizzle rate) heal 10k of damage in 151 seconds

If you assume you've somewhat less than 10k to heal, say 4k, the cleric STILL takes 12.5 seconds. the Druid takes 60 seconds.

The cleric is capable of healing 800 damage per second. On a 4K target, it's only 320 damage per second.

The Druid is capable of healing about 66 damage per second. If you thow in the Regen the druid can add, you add 2 or 3 damage per second healing.


If your mob is doing significantly more than 66 DPS, or you have more than one of them, then the druid can't keep up.

I assume the numbers for Shamans are similar.

However, (IMO) if you give groups the opportunity to replace the cleric with a druid or shaman on a one-for-one basis, they WILL, for all the OTHER things the druid or shaman bring to the group. The cleric, IMO, should EASILY outheal any single other healer, or they will have no place.
