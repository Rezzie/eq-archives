Zekta, maybe I can give you a response closer to your position. I played EQ for a little over four years, from release until just a few months ago. See, I hated the game post 50. It reminded me way too much of something we used to call Monty Haul gaming and raiding ( in my view ) was akin to a description used by Gunny Highway in "Heartbreak Ridge". So, each time I leveled a character to 50+, I would start again with a brand new character. Over the years I noticed that there were fewer and fewer true newbs entering the game and the lower levels were increasingly populated by know it all twinks, usually with more time on their hands than manners. After a while, even they began to disappear until eventually you couldn't find more than 20 folks around under level 30 that weren't bazaar mules. Simply put, you've come late to the dance.

This is not to say there is no hope. Social networking is the key to the game. Start asking around and talking to folks in game. Get your foot in the door, so to speak. Sure, you're gonna get blown off sometimes, but you will find some friendly folks who can and will help you out. Lots of older, established players delight in seeing the gosh/wow reaction they remember from their early days in the game.

For your how to get there problem, I recommend EQatlas, a website with a collection of zone maps and their connections. It's not entirely current with every expansion, but it should be sufficent. Search around, there are many, many websites devoted to EQ with a wealth of useful information.
That's the only upside to arriving late; all your questions have been answered somewhere.

As for SW:G, after reading the features I decided it just wasn't my cup of tea, so I never bothered with it. I know a few folks that really liked it, as it was less time consuming than EQ. Most of those folks grew bored with the game though and ended up back in EQ. Their reasoning was they enjoyed the challenge presented in EQ rather than the ease of play presented in SW:G. /shrug Go figure.

Finally, I suspect that within the next few months SOE will announce the opening of another newbie server. So, if you decide to cancel, keep your eyes open and reactivate the account when another newbie server opens.

I feel for you Zekta. I tend to always have a few low level alts that arn't twinked to the hilt because due to time zone differences it's not always easy finding a group for my wizard and necro.

And to be honest, I enjoy the 10-40 game - not everyone does.

Ignore the rude people who say "so what, don't play then!". The people who say "build a social network" are correct, but it's not always that easy.

Since you've got a fairly "safe" toon factionwise, use PoP and jump between Crushbone, Blackburrow, North Ro, East and West Commons, Everfrost and the Warrens. Let people know in /ooc that you are a real newbie, that you dont want to be luggage to an uber-twink and you want to have FUN. Eventually you'll find the right people.

Whether you establish longer friendships with those people (whether they are real newbies like you or alts with a clue) is really up to you. Comments like "/g this game sux bad" will probably not help.

There are heaps of friendly people in this game, with all sorts of backgrounds and attitudes. My experience (4 yrs) suggests that those who make the most noise about how great and god-like they are, and about how many 100,000 pp they have are generally not the nicest people in the game. What do they say "An empty vessel makes the most sound"?

Of course, it could be that EQ is simply not your game! My wife loves pen'n'paper roleplaying, yet dislikes EQ with venom. But do give it a fair chance.

I believe it's not the game, gear or technology that makes EQ a great game for me, but the people who play it. YMMV.
