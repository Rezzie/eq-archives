This has been bothering me for a long time. I just cannot understand for one second all this talk about balancing. Why should classes be balanced?

This has become a rule now. Every class should always be balanced resulting in every class getting upgraded.

I can't see the point. Why do we want to be balanced? If I want to be THE best at dealing damage I should choose to make a warrior (IMO). IF I choose to make another character and discover it was not what I wanted after playing 50 levels, well then either I keep playing my class the way it is supposed to, or I make a new character of the class that have all those abilities I always wanted.

Oh, now people will say "If the classes is not balanced everyone will play the same class".

Is that true? Nope! No way!

Equipment is very important in EQ, too important IMO. If everyone would play Warriors I would be happy to be a paladin who could swim in gear noone else wanted.....

The game is too dependent on fighting, classes should be more different by all having their own special abilities. Verant was going the right way when they made rogues more wanted by upgrading picklock. I applauded this move even tho it meant I never could get all the way down in Sebilis for months...

But what happened then? All of a sudden there was epic quests implemented. Every class got their own of course, the game has to be fair and balanced *cough*. Rogues went from experts at picklock, sneaking, hiding, corpse retrieval to being the best damage dealers in the game! Is this right? Is this the picture you all get in your head when you imagine a rogue character?

Too much steps has already been taken, I wish only rangers had tracking, only clerics could ressurect, only necromancers and mages had pets, only shamans could slow, only enchanters could haste etc..

I understand this can not be undone now but PLEASE stop balancing NOW! Right away! There are a LOT of players or each and every class on every server today. That means every class is playable in their current form. So just stop! This whole thing is getting out of hand, really!

And for the record I am currently playing Paladin (52), Warrior (51), Druid (56), Necromancer (31), Rogue (25), Wizard (12) so I have actually tried a few classes and really enjoyed most of them.. and when I got mad at my lack of damage output playing my paladin, I didn't go whine somewhere, I made a warrior instead and I am as happy as ever!

Here's one very good reason for class balance:

It takes months, many months, to rise to levels where imbalance is drastic.

If I played a game for a week and said, aww, crud, my class sucks, I'd make a new character.

After putting 4 months, or 6 months, into a character to find out it sucks, that's when I say it's too much trouble to make a new character. I'll plod along with my mediocre character and miss out on much of the high end content.

Here's another very good reason:

Even if it's reasonable to simply make a new character of one of the few good classes, that means that the majority of classes are hardly ever played.

I for one like the variety. It makes the game less boring, more unique. Take away the reason for playing the variety, and everyone becomes carbon copies of each other.

Erudite necro, halfling druid, troll warrior, high elf cleric, maybe a few barbarian shaman, iksar monks, and dark elf rogues. That's about all you'd need. Throw away the hundred other class/race combos, they're imbalanced and you wouldn't want to play them.

Hardly a good game concept. Especially since most new players would have sunk months into finding out that they picked the wrong character.

Balance is good. It's a game. We should all have fun, all be able to play, all have access to all the content.

Not inviting druids to a group in Velketor's Lab because (in there) they can't do anything but snare and DS and mostly just contribute to LD is plain wrong.

Not inviting SKs/paladins to Skyshrine groups because they die too fast without those cool warrior HPs and disciplines is just wrong.

The examples continue.

It's not the players that are wrong for doing this. It's the game that is wrong for creating this imbalance (simple mistake, we're all human, even game designers) and letting the imbalance continue unmitigated (no longer a mistake; inexcusable and irresponsible mismanagement of real player concerns).

Kudos to Verant for addressing these issues in a timely fashion.

Timely fashion? This imbalance has existed for years, you say.

Yes, but the game content didn't really make the imbalance so drastically play-limiting until SoV came out and tilted the palying field in favor of so few classes. And now, only a couple months later, Verant is fixing it. Kudos again.
