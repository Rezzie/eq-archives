After two years of playing EQ, I really think that class-based MMORPGs are not for me. It gives too much power to the game developers and generally leaves the players contantly feeling nerfed or cheated over time.

In a class-based system the only abilities given to the players come from the "immortals" through skills, spells or items. You cannot transform your toon outside of the strict definition of the class set by the devs. This leads to contant nerfs, rebalancing, class envy, and general GM bitterness toward the players and player bitterness toward te GMs.

If someone interested in EQ perused these boards why would they ever want to play?

Skill-based RPGs give more control to the player who does not have a class per se but a collection of skills. Obviously if a skill allowed a level 1 to kill the top boss mob in the game, it would be unbalanced and nerfed, but in general the devs can adjust balance through content rather than directly changing the players' characters. This is a much more subtle approach, and it allows allows players modify their characters by training newly important skills if they have the credits to do so.

IMO, this leads to a much more civil atmosphere in general and is more challanging to the players. Asheron's Call is a skill based game, and if you look at the two games' message boards, the difference is like night and day. The AC boards have nowhere near the animosity seen on the EQ boards.

This is not meant to be a plug for AC or a rant against EQ, but rather an observation I have made after looking at both message boards and thinking about the design differences of the two games.
