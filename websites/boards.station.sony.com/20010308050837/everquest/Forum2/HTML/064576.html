A FUNDAMENTAL FLAW: WHY THE EQ SYSTEM IS BREAKING DOWN AT HIGH LEVELS
There is a serious flaw in the game mechanics of EverQuest. As Verant attempts to scale up higher level encounters to give its best players a challenge, this flaw creates a giant divide in the desirability of classes and of people. This flaw throws the balance of the entire game to one side, forcing the entire tuning of areas and monsters, both big and small, around the new equilibrium.

The flaw, simply stated, is this.

Melee effectiveness increases with new equipment. Caster effectiveness does not.

Chew on this for a bit. You will slowly come to realize that the implications of this are staggering.

Under the current model of item acquisition in EverQuest, all casters can do is increase their mana pool. The mana pool is a reservoir. It has no bearing on often you can deal damage, or how quickly you do it. Melee, meanwhile, are increasing their damage every day by real and significant percentages simply by obtaining better weapons.

An example? Back in the first days when Fear opened, ykesha's were the hottest swords around. These were 8/28 ratio weapons.

Now take a look at the situation 1.5 years later. Almost every melee has something equivalent to a jade mace, a 9/18 ratio weapon.

Essentially, every melee class has been able to increase their rate of damage output by nearly 100%.

Now take a look at the caster situation.

1.5 years ago, a caster had 200 int.

Today, a caster has 200 int, and some extra mana items, and perhaps some extra ac or resist items on since the soft cap on wis/int prevents any benefit from going past the limit. Their mana pool grows bigger and they acquire slightly more efficient spells as they level up, but keep in mind melee classes also receive their bonuses to damage and defense, as well as
new disciplines, when they level as well.

When all is said and done, casters are essentially at the same place, casting the same spells, that they were 1.5 years ago.

So here you are, with melee class effectiveness having been nearly doubled, while a caster is still casting the same spells and doing the same damage that he always has. And you wonder why casters can't get a group? Furthermore, all any melee has to do to improve his damage or efficiency even more is to get new weapons. What are you, as a caster, going to do? Get that +15 int eyepatch you've been lusting after? Oh wait, you're already at the soft cap. And besides that, all it could possibly do would be increase your mana reserves...which has absolutely nothing to do with how fast or how often you can do damage.

The downward spiral continues from here. As melee increase in effectiveness, the best groups, naturally, are formed from melee classes. The difficulty of most situations comes to be balanced around these classes, shutting out casters from groups because their efficiency is terrible relative to melee classes and has no chance of ever increasing. Furthermore, as Verant tries to create more challenging encounters, the only possible means of doing so is to provide melee classes with better weapons and armor and balance encounters around these factors, since these are the only factors which are capable of scaling under the current system. Thus, classes which support the melee classes such as the enchanter, cleric, and shaman rise to prominence.

Of course, by now, you're asking the obvious question. Where is the magical wristband that increases the damage output of all your fire spells by 20%? Where is the tome which reduces the wizard's casting time by 15%? Where is the staff that reduces the mana cost of your spell by 30%?

I don't have that answer for you. How such a fundamental omission could have been made in this game is beyond me. I have constantly suggested the addition of more innovative and creative caster items that were essentially the caster equivalent of a melee's haste item or new weapon. And truthfully, these items really wouldn't be innovative at all...they would have been the natural result of any logical thinking on a designer's part.

If EverQuest is to survive, casters must be able to significantly increase their damage output, speed, and effectiveness at the same rate as melee classes. The fundamental gap between melee and casting classes, where casters are left unable to progress while melee classes are rewarded constantly with newer and better ratio weapons, will destroy the game. Under the current system, each new expansion will bring melee classes new and better items, while caster classes stagnate with their virtually useless wis/int items.

If you play at the high level game, you will be realizing that we have already travelled down this path to some extent. Your casters are complaining how useless they are in experience groups as well as uber mob fights. Your guild is shifting its emphasis to require more healers and more warriors and rogues. Maybe you been observing these things in action, and agreed with them, but didn't really understand the underlying cause.

Now you do. Melee classes and the healing and buffing classes which support them will grow to become more dominant. Kunark made melee classes 100% more effective in dealing damage. With SoV we may see that number grow to 150%. Your casters, meanwhile, have improved by virtually nothing. Each expansion will widen the gulf between melee and caster classes until caster classes are rendered completely irrelevant. That is, unless the ability for casters to improve themselves constantly through the acquisition of unique dropped spells or items is addressed.

The way to fix this is clear. Yes, it will require a major overhaul of the way caster incentives and progressions are designed in EverQuest. But it is absolutely critical to the survivability of this game. Can Verant save EverQuest in time?

end paste
