Ok, I'm still gathering info (RL kept me from getting it all) but here's some.

Average buyback offer from a vendor is targetted for chars with approx 100 CHA, though some vary, so your prices may too. The average should be reasonably approximate though.

Blacksmithing: 80% of component cost at 83 CHA

Pottery: 79% of cost at 101 CHA

Baking: With judicious use of fishing skill or foraged donations from another character, buyback exceeds 100%, meaning you can actually make money, sometimes an almost appreciable amount! You won't get rich, but an hour's worth of making fish fillets netted me a handful of plat.

Tinkering: When asking about costs and buyback info from tinkerers, each and every one responded with something rude, crude, or disgruntled. Apparently there *IS* a tradeskill worse than Alchemy!

Alchemy: Average buyback appears to be around 10% of cost. This ranged from a high of 14% for SoW potions, and a low of 8.1% for lesser rejuvenation. Sadly, I only had my own potions to go by for this, so nothing higher than Invis to Undead, Shrink, etc were used for this test. I only made two of the stat potions for this, as 20pp is as far as I was willing to go, even for an experiment.

I still lack info on jewelcraft, fletching, higher-level potion costs, and a few other bits and pieces. A recap, to make it plain...

Pottery: 79%
Smithing: 80%
Baking: 100+%
Tinkering: Rude noises and gestures.
Alchemy: 10%

Might want to have a look at both Tinkering *AND* Alchemy!

As far as balance with the potions; really now. Even if I could make Healing Potions (And it **STILL** chaps my heiney that vendors sell it but I don't! Does it exist already and maybe we just haven't discovered it?), the balance effect still is present. If a warrior REALLY wants to solo by using healing potions, they'd still solo without my healing potions. It simply leaves me poorer and him more possibly dead.

About the costs... Who would ever pay more for something than they have to? Make a teleport potion for us. Peg it for around 20pp to make, so we sell it for 30-40. Target them for mid-to-high level Alchemy, with maybe one or two of them low-to-mid. Nobody will drink a 30pp potion when they can get a port for 10pp at most usually. I can see this being unbalancing if they can port from anywhere maybe, so possibly make them only work from druid rings and wizzy spires. They go to one, gulp a potion of Faydark, POOF! Off to the GFay wizzy spires!

I could see a 'gate back to bind point' potion being reasonably expensive. 50pp or so to make maybe.

And for the stat potions, at my level, I can't think of even ONE spell I cast that has negative side effects! Why the heck should my potions have them?! Did I not clean my medicine bag properly or something?

All I can do is hope for the best. Maybe we'll even see some changes! :P
