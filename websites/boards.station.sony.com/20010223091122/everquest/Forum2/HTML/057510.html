Hello again.
This will be the thread for discussion about the rogue class. The idea is to look at the things that need to be changed to make the class 'balanced'. With that as the primary goal, I am still interested in hearing about bugs and other such things. But, primarily, I want to talk about balance.

My take on the rogue is pretty similar to that of the warrior. They are pretty well balanced. Obviously with EverQuest as a primarily player vs. environment game, some of the things that thieves traditionally do are not really part of the package. That's why, in EverQuest, they are titled rogues.

Again, I have had little time (actually, no time at all) to do research on rogue concerns before starting this thread. So please be as detailed as you need to be to get your point across. But, at the same time, please remember that this thread will get long. So being concise and readable is important too.

I do want to report one thing that we have already discovered internally about rogues that is broken and will be fixed when we make these changes. Yes, it is a nerf, but not in the true sense of the word.

In the old days, NPCs had a limited size to their hate-list (basically the way that they track who they are angry at). In those days, evade would just drop you to the bottom of that list. Recently we increased the size of that list dramatically. This allows us to do a lot more with NPCs. But evade was never changed to reflect this, so it still drops you to the bottom of the hate-list. Essentially, this allows a rogue to do significant damage to an opponent, and then with the use of evade, become less 'hated' than someone that has done no damage to the NPC at all, but just has a somewhat negative faction with that NPC and is standing someplace on the other side of the zone. The rogue can then beat the NPC up with impunity. Basically, evade is far too useful for avoiding aggro from an NPC, and we're going to change it to be more in line with its original function.

I wanted to let you know that at the outset. Please don't make it the focus of this thread. It’s certainly something we should talk about. But there are certainly other things that are worth discussion as well.

So let's hear what you think.

Alan

I've only got a few issues to touch on. Some things I agree with have already been mentioned innumberable times, so I'll be brief with them.

1. Evade: This appears to be a main issue of conern for many rogues. Their primary argument lies in the current flaws of the evade system. These rogues are afraid that with changes made to the evade skill, they will become primary targets for creatures which will result in more death. I think that the skill can be changed as has been proposed by the dev team, but also think that it needs one small modification: the uninterruption of evade. This could either be in the form of being able to hide while being hit, or actually incorporating an evade button of some sort that works on a thread separate from hide. Many rogues would call me a traitor to my class for advocating this change, but let me make a point on this. If you have been a rogue for a long time, then you played before evade was even implemented. Knowing how to deal without an evade skill even in the game, I can tell you that it is perfectly possible to play the game without dying repeatedly, it requires you simply to exert a bit of effort in playing your class. Many newer rogues do not realize how pampered they are to even have the evade skill, which has allowed an influx of talentless and brainless rogues to succeed. Another problem with the current evade system is that rogues are able to develop themselves rapidly with substandard equipment. This change would require that rogues actually use the upper-end AC gear over the easily -obtained STR gear. Heaven forbid, you may actually have to learn how to really play and use some strategy and timing!

2. Poisons: Keep these as rogue exclusive skills. However, I do feel that some change needs to be made in poison storage. This could either be in the form of stacking poisons or multi-charge poisons, akin to potions made by shamans.

3. Safe Fall: I'm actually amazed I didn't really see anyone address this; perhaps I wasn't looking closely enough. I think that safe fall, as it currently exists for rogues, is all but useless. The skill cap for it simply MUST exceed its current substandard 94. If this comes in the form of a 50+ skill cap increase, so be it, as long as it happens. Rogues' safe fall skill should be at least on par with that of monks.

4. Corpse Retrieval: Corpse retrieval has always been an important job for rogues, even moreso when the change was made to allow rogues to hide and sneak at the same time, and further yet when rogues were made invisible to a vast array of different creatures (living, dead, etc.) at one time through the use of a single type of invisibility. However, a problem exists with corpse retrieval now; it is practically monopolized by necromancers in upper-level dungeons. A rogue SHOULD be able to sneak into a place like Sebilis and reach a corpse in places like the crypt, disco room, or chef without being seen. The problem here is inconsistency in creatures' vision. For instance, some shamans/wizards can see rogues invis, but some cannot. This would not provide too large a problem were it not for the fact that there are some places a rogue cannot sneak behind said creatures. Some creatures block key passageways and always face the same direction. What can be done? I really have no idea for certain and I would like some input. One idea I had tossed around with friends was implementation of a distract skill that when successful, would cause nearby creatures to face away from the rogue. Many games which focus on stealth as a primary aspect have a similar implementation in the form of throwing rocks, banging on a wall, etc.

5. Kunark Armor: Specifically, the Mrylokar's Greaves and Gauntlets. Though many servers have several Mrylokar's breastplates (enough on some even for alternate cahracters to have them!), nearly every server is lacking in these two pieces. Nearly every class has a problem with greaves as far as I am aware, so though it does not relate specifically to rogues, something should be done with Venril Sathir to alter his drop rates or spawn time to balance this issue. The problem with the Gauntlets is that they scarcely ever drop, their carrier scarcely ever spawns, and said carrier is in a place that is difficult to pull. I have no problem with good items being difficult to obtain, but these are ridiculous. Where I come from there are possibly four or so pairs on the entire server, despite how often people conduct raids on Karnor's Castle. The Gauntlets should either have their drop specifics altered, or should have their casting time significantly reduced to make them more worth the ridiculous amount effort required to get them. Better armor can more easily be obtained under the current system almost by simply purchasing a relatively inexpensive expansion. The Kunark armor should not simply die as a result.

6. Defensive Capabilities: Though I know rogues were not intended to be a defensive class, I have a terrible problem with rogues' defensive capabilities. A fully-armored rogue in plate class armor and wearing various other items geared toward AC can sometimes not even match a monk of lower level. Keep in mind that a monk is nothing more than a man or iksar running around in his underwear or a dress. I do not know what can be done to address this, if anything, but it is hurtful to see that monks can easily surpass a rogue's AC while maintaining their ATK at a good value, whereas a rogue will struggle to obtain his high-end AC items, only to fall short of the monk's AC while severely crippling his ATK power. Perhaps the answer to this solution lies not in the defensive capabilities, but in the offense:defense ratio. I would love some feedback on this as well.

Conclusively, rogues as they exist are a strong, fairly balanced class. However, they, like any other, have their flaws. Some previously unmentioned issues need to be considered in the rogue equation. Overall, power needs to be given back to the more skilled rogues, and a degree of strategy never hurt anyone.
