Typically what I've found is that, even in some of the tier one planes, a druid can do the job just as well as a cleric (well, better considering the additional utility they bring to a group). This is in a normal xp grind kind of group. People (probably more out of habit than anything else) tend to prefer clerics.

Since the mobs hit so hard in the planes (even in tier one planes), someone to slow the mobs is basically a requirement. After the mob is slowed, a druid can keep a MT alive without much of a problem. Shaman as a primary healer I'm not as sure about, their complete heal caps out lower than a druids which may be a problem. But they can generally group easily because of their buffs/slows.

For raid situations, clerics are still the prefered healer. There's no reason that a group of druids couldn't do a psuedo-CH rotation, but I haven't seen it done yet. The fact that clerics can also rez helps out a lot in those situations. In planar groups the need to have a rezzer in the group isn't as great as in some of your more normal deep dungeon crawls, just because the body will eventually make the graveyard and by that time you should at least have a cleric friend that can rez you (or at least log in their cleric from the alt they're playing).

I play a cleric and a druid, both of whom are in their low 50's. I definitely have a much easier time finding a group with my cleric (although as an aside, I think that enchanters have the easiest time finding groups of all classes). However, I find that in many situations, my druid would be able to handle the healing.

Note I didn't say that my druid could do the job as easily -- there are some groups where he'd have to work harder to do the same job. But I think too many people dismiss the idea of a druid as the primary healer. I think a lot of this has to do with the large number of druids who do not like the healing role, and prefer nuking. And druids who have spent their careers soloing, and thus do not know how to act as group healer. I've always enjoyed being a healer with my druid (I specialized in alteration for a reason), and I've always been willing to be the primary healer if no cleric was present. The biggest problem I face is groups that don't feel comfortable with a druid as a healer. I know my limits as a healer, and I won't volunteer to be primary healer with my druid if I know a cleric is needed to do the job, but that doesn't reassure everyone.

Okay, well, I've rambled on enough. To answer the original question, clerics don't have very much trouble finding groups, but they don't automatically get them. If all of the groups in the zone already have a cleric, they're probably not going to want two.
