Without a doubt, the Bard. It's not even a close contest.

Defensively, Bards are the strongest PvP class, bar none. They have the fastest ground movement in the game, coupled with the ability to Shadowstep. They have the best resist buffs, the fastest healing rate, and a virtual immunity to stamina loss. They also get melee-level skill caps and the ability to wear plate mail. Bards also have a virtual immunity to cancel magic due to the recycle rate on their songs.

Offensively, Bards receive the most damaging spell effects of any of the hybrids, as well as the non-damaging effects of Fear, Mesmerize, Mana Drain and Charm. Also, when you add in the effects of their combat buff/debuff songs, they can simply stand toe-to-toe with the other hybrids and beat them senseless with weapons.

Utility-wise, Bards receive virtually everything. Paladins and Rangers can't see invis. Paladins can't go invis. Shadowknights and Paladins can't cast Enduring Breath. Paladins and Rangers can't Locate Corpse. Paladins and Shadowknights can't Bind Sight. Paladins and Shadowknights can't Track. Bards can do all of this.

Lastly, no other hybrid can win a battle without equipment (not an inconsequential problem on a PvP server). They simply don't have the mana to deal enough damage to an opponent. A Bard can be naked and still kill an opponent.

Since I posted the original reply, I noticed two other questions by the original poster on other threads- both directed at being a PvP Bard.

So a word of warning:

While I do believe that the Bard, *played well*, is the best of the hybrids for PvP, this in and of itself is not necessarily a recommendation for playing the class.

A corollary to this would be the issue of Shadowknights vs. Monks as pullers. The Shadowknight is actually the best puller in the game. The slightly inferior Feign Death is compensated by for a vast array of utility spells that make pulling much easier. And the Shadowknight has both taunt and superior access to gear.

Why then do Monks pull, almost to exclusion, on planar events? Simple. Because in order for a Shadowknight to beat a Monk at pulling, the player behind the Shadowknight has to be a true master of the class. The Monk merely needs to capable of hitting a hotkey.

The same can be said here. The Bard, in the hands of a master, has one of the most potent sets of abilities available for PvP. But the Enchanter, played by your average joe, can give the master Bard a serious run for it.

PvP is also very different from fighting vs. mobs. Melee damage, while useful, is much less effective due to the fact that players don't stand still (unless they are casting). And despite this, a skilled Warrior will beat a bad Necromancer any day.

Lastly, you shouldn't let your decision on what class to play rest on the opinion of any one person. I'm sure the Shadowknights are eventually going to chime in with their PvP suitability, and they may present some convincing counterarguments.

Having played a Bard to 19th level on Tallonzek, I can offer some insights on them in PvP. Bards are one of the most survivable classes in PvP, as you can get out of most every situation (Though a Magicians root pet is nasty).

Bards can adapt and present a worthy PvP opponent against every other class. The ability to Bellow is an unusal advantage that startles many of my opponents. However, it is very difficult to kill people solo in PvP, because Bards don't dish out damage particularly fast. This fact may change at the level when a Bard gets Chains (Kinda like ensnare).

In group PvP, the Bard rocks. Bards improve their entire party. Most of my group PvP experiences have ended with the other side having losses and the rest fleeing. This is because, 2 minutes into the fight my team is losing, but not dead, due to the extra 19 points of MR, FR, CR I have added to my group. Then the other side runs out of mana, and I start healing my team and the tide quickly turns.

Group PvP is unpredictable, fast and exciting, and is the best part of Everquest for me.

Tarawyn the Bard
