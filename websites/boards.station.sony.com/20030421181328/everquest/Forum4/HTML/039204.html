Here's a bit of advice when dealing with npc's. They'll typically say something that is in brackets []. To continue a conversation with them, usually you can type "what [bracketed word(s)], such as

MegaJP: Hail, Joe
Joe: Hail MegaJP...have you come here to give us [help]?
MegaJP: what help
Joe: We need someone to deliver mail to Felwithe. Could you deliver the mail?
MegaJP: I will deliver the mail

Remember though, sometimes the key phrase is tricky, so you can also try "who", "what", "where", etc. It takes a bit of creativity sometimes, but you'll get used to it.

To help find your guild, try typing, "/ooc I'm new to the game and am an enchanter, could someone help me find my guild?" or something similiar...chances are, someone will help you or tell you an approximate location of your guild.

Hope this helps, and welcome to EQ

Mega, head here/[url] or [url=http://www.tawest.net/DonS/EverQuest/maps.asp]here to find a map for the city that you started in. I have binders full of maps (some of them dated from early 2000 haha) and I still use them.

If you're not sure of what the name of the zone is that you are in, you can find out by hitting enter (which opens the "chat" bar) and then typing / and hitting enter again. This will display a list of everyone who is in a zone with you and the last line of text will tell you which zone you're in.

Each class (e.g. warrior, ranger, enchanter, cleric, etc.) has a guild hall in their starting town. Some classes have their own guild hall and other classes share a building. If you read your note (which you do by right-clicking and holding) it will tell you the name of the NPC whom you need to give it to.

And come back with any more questions you may have; it's fun to see Norrath through fresh eyes again.
