About these lists:

First and foremost, understand that this is only a list of concerns to be brought to the Live Team. This is not a list of promises, and I'm certainly not telling you that any or all of these items will be changed or implemented.

Also, there are three types of items that I don't have listed here. Epic Quests, Magic Resistances in Velious and Mid-level quests. Those three items were mentioned by enough classes that I've pulled them out for separate examination.

All items are currently marked with NONE for status. Obviously we've already started working on some items, you've seen the lists from the patch messages on the Test Server. I will be updating the status for items early next week to reflect those potential changes and with any responses from the team. I just wanted to get these lists out to you fresh.

I want these lists to be final as you see them now, but I know there will need to be changes made. So this list is not final. But if you want something added to or removed from the list, please be convincing about the change. At this point I don't want to tack on too many more items, since the team is already reviewing the original list.

Thanks for the input so far.

Alan


Shamans:


ITEM: Healing.

DESCRIPTION: There is a general consensus that clerics are the only useful healers past 50th level. Shamans and Druids are supposed to be secondary healers. But post 50 their spells are too inferior and fizzle too often to be effective as healers at all. Some suggestions are to move Superior Heal to level 51 (and reduce its fizzle rate) and/or add in a new one at 56th. Perhaps Torpor can fill that role (either leaving it where it is, or moving it down a few levels). But we also need to be careful not to make them too good at healing. That’s the cleric’s turf. Whatever is done here should also be done with Druids, in some similar fashion (though probably not the same way).

ITEM: Torpor.

DESCRIPTION: Torpor does not stack with the regen line. At the level they obtain this spell, a Shaman almost always has Pack Chloroplast, Regrowth of the Grove, or Regrowth on. Torpor will overwrite all of these spells, causing them to have to recast them. This reduces its overall usefulness because, although Torpor is only 200 mana, if you have to then re-cast Regrowth for an additional 300 mana, you just cost yourself 500 mana for a 1200 point heal. They might as well have cast two Superior Heals, it would have saved a memorization slot and casting time. Torpor lasts 4 ticks (24 seconds). During those 4 ticks, the most they can regain is 60 health with Regrowth. There doesn’t seem to be an exploit in regenerating 60 health while under the effects of Torpor. There seems to be no reason not to make it stack with the Regen line. This would allow them to keep Torpor memorized and use it at will, instead of just when Regrowth wears off.

One idea presented was that Torpor also act as a feign death for its duration. The value of this addition could be far too great. But if this spell can't be clicked off until the duration runs out…

Q: But can't the shaman can cast Torpor?
A: No. Look at the casting time of torpor and its quite serious negative effects.

Q: But can't the shaman slow the mob?
A: No. And here's why...
1) Many mobs are spellcasters. Slow isn't going to help against them.
2) Many melee-based mobs have such a high magic resistance, most notably in Velious, that they simply cannot be affected in any way by magic-based spells.
3) Slowing is not an options while the shaman is performing a primary healer role for a group. Shaman slow spells have a MASSIVE amount of aggro attached and are highly resisted, often requiring multiple casts. A shaman attempting to slow 55+ mobs requires a cleric in the group to keep the shaman alive.

Q: What about cripple?
A: PvP spell only. No noticable effect on mobs.

Q: Can't the shaman cannibalize to have a larger mana pool?
A: No. When a shaman is actively healing a tank they are chain casting Superior Healing in an attempt to keep up with mob damage.

Q: So what if we give shamans another healing spell to fizzle at 55th level, similar to Remedy?
A: You've upped the shaman healing ratio from 2.3 to 2.5. Nothing really has changed. The imbalance still exists.
