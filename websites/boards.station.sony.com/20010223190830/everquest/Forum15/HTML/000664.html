AC, Armor Class, basically reduces damage. How much does the addition of 1AC mean to a character, it's hard to tell. An example ( I hate using death examples but its the best way to illustrate this point): I died 2 days ago in Crystal Caverns (I'm lvl 41 SK) I died to several blue MOB's. My normal AC unbuffed is 805 with all my Armor. When I fight this was I can kill a green Orc kill it without loosing a full bubble of health. After my death I was wearing 2 pieces of armor I had in the bank (waiting to sell them) and my AC was about 500. I had my alternate weapon with the skills maxed out too and a green Orc Agro'd on me and I was able to kill it but lost 2 full bubbles of health. Thats what AC does.

One note, listed AC on a piece of equipment does not equal the AC added to you when you wear it. For some reason you always get more than the shown AC added to your AC per piece of equipment.

The listed AC on an item is known as the raw AC. You will always get something different added to your total AC then that which is listed because there will be an AC adjustment based on your defense skill and agility stat. That is how agility factors into your AC total. By adjusting the raw AC of items.

There are also two parts to AC. These parts are what is called damage advoidance and damage midigation. In Beta, you stat sheet showed you both. When they released EverQuest they changed it because some people said it was to confusing to understand.

Damage advoidance is the part of AC that makes a mob miss you. This is not the same as the part that allows you to parry/dodge/riposte since inorder for those skills to occur, you had to have a hit occur against you. Damage advoidance is only the part that makes "Mob_01 swings and misses you".

Damage midigation is the part of AC that makes a mob do less damage when they do hit you. This is the part that makes the hit occur at 50 points instead of 150 points.

It is presently unclear as to how much a given person may have in each part of AC at any given time.
