Although the following post is about hybrids, it addresses the inherent balance issues in general for EverQuest.

The problem with warriors -vs- hybrids (not counting bards) is this:

If not a single hybrid showed up for an "uber" mob raid in Kunark, or even a non-uber raid in some of the high level Velious zones (and especially the ubers in Velious), the raid can and would still go on, the hybrids would not be missed (if the raid had the needed numbers) and in some encounters (Venril Sathir for example) the hybrids presence would actually HURT the raid, not help it.

If not a single warrior showed up for an "uber" mob raid in Kunark, or even a non-uber raid in some of the high level Velious zones (and expecially the ubers in Velious), the raid would not and COULD not continue (even IF the raid had the needed numbers), warriors of the appropriate levels would be solicited from other places by any means possible. No warriors, no raids.

This to me is unacceptable. HOW can this be considered 'balanced?' I'm not asking warriors' skills to be reduced. I'm asking hybrids skills, abilities and spells (our spells are difficult at BEST to "stick" to Kunark "boss" mobs or the 56+ mobs in Velious) to be increased so we can be a significant contribution to the raid. Yes, we DO want to be needed. Isn't that what we, as the PEOPLE behind the characters want in game and in real life? Knowing that our presence made a difference, that we helped the group and not hurt it is not only fun, it is very gratifying.

For one class of a particular type (fighter/tank/melee or whatever you wanna call us) to be needed EXCLUSIVELY over ALL of the others is very imbalanced.

As an analogy, take Diablo II (granted, that is MUCH more of a single player game, but the analogy still applies). Each character class in that game adds their own unique skills and abilities to the game, each would be able to defeat the final "boss" mob on their own, but as a group they would be much more effective, each adding skills the others don't have.

Now I'm not asking for each class to have the power and ability to solo boss mobs (or even solo for that matter). For EQ to be a "grouping" game, class interdependency is needed. However, reading the "class talk" boards and hearing the complaints of other classes, and especially with the advent of Velious (mob level and hitpoint cap gone), the only classes that are truly NEEDED are clerics and warriors. And although the other classes do add utility and needed skills, if either one or both of those two "core" classes do not show up for a "raid," the raid wouldn't happen. If one of any other class didn't show up, the raid could still continue. THAT is the fundamental problem with EQ and why many people are losing interest with a particular class or with EverQuest itself.

No one wants to be a burden. (well, maybe some people do . . .) But I know I don't. But until I'm given the proper tools, I'll continue to be a burden. This, Verant, I request be changed. How? I don't know. It's your game, you designed it, you control it, you need to decide and implement this change. Otherwise, what are we paying you for?

Absor said "Actually, even if knights had exactly the same armor class and defensive skills as a warrior, they would be worse tanks, since they have noticeably fewer hit points. As I said earlier, improving warriors would almost by default mean an improvement in those other classes, so that the gap would not close. If we choose to close the gap, then warriors will have to remain where they are. I'm certainly not going to advocate an increase in warrior power _just_ so we can give more power to the hybrids. That seems sorta backward to me."

I wanted to look at some hard numbers, so I went to Caster's Realm and sampled from their player's database. I recorded the hitpoints of ten random (legitimate looking characters as opposed to made up sounding ones like Thisserversux) players from each of the plate wearing melee classes. All characters were level 60 and the hps listed included equipment bonuses. Here are the average hps of each class from the samples:

Paladin - 2343
Shadowknight - 2412
Warrior - 2909

Since Warriors have been named the base class, I'll use their hps as the standard here. Unbuffed, the percentages are as follows:

Paladins - 80 percent of warrior
Shadowknight - 83 percent of warrior

These percentages seem proper to me. However, I think it would be a mistake to a judgement on these numbers. We need to factor in buffs still. At the endgame, level 60, the following buffs are standard on tanks:

Symbol of Marzin - 700hps
Heroism - 400hps
Talisman of Kragg - 380hps
Riotous Health - 300hps (from +50 stamina)
Divine Strength - 200hps

Now let's buff our tanks and look at the numbers again:

Paladin - 4323 hps
Shadowknight - 4392 hps
Warrior - 4889 hps

Percentages:

Paladin - 88 percent of warrior
Shadowknight - 90 percent of a warrior

Note how the warrior still has the same number of hps more than the others before and after buffs, yet the effective balance has changed.

For starters, 500hps is not a terribly large advantage in the first place. That's less than one hit from Ixiblat Fer, two third of a hit from Treah Greenroot and two hits from Prince Thirneg. So a warrior's claim to fame, that of more hitpoints, can be negated in a split second by any number of mob encuonters. But this is nothing new, this situation has existed since the last time either of the classes had hp modifications.

I wanted to point these numbers to help discourage hp increases for paladins and shadowknights. The gap between us really isn't much at all, and closing that gap would be a major blow to the warrior class.

I am especially concerned by Absor's comment "If we choose to close the gap, then warriors will have to remain where they are." If I read this right, then a hybrid hp increase would not be followed by a warrior increase. Suppose that increase were 150hps. The new totals would be:

Paladin - 2493 unbuffed (86 percent warrior), 4473 buffed (92 percent)

Shadowknights - 2562 unbuffed (88 percent warrior), 4542 buffed (93 percent warrior)

The primary advantage a warrior has in melee combat is hitpoints. AC is rumored to have a softcap of 1100, which is easily obtained and exceeded by all three classes, so the warrior will not exceed in that area. Damage output would still be somewhat better for a warrior, but I don't know of a way to bring this data to light.

So where does this leave the warrior?

HPs only slightly better than other plate classes.
AC equal.
Damage slightly better.

Now the questions arise. Are these melee benefits worth passing up on the myriad of spells and skills a hybrid has to offer? I don't think they would be. There would be no underlying reason to play a warrior, and that would be a violation of class balance.

I apologize to drag this out, and I will admit the averages were not from a proper statistical sampling, but I think they're accurate enough for this purpose.

I hope these numbers and opinions help.
