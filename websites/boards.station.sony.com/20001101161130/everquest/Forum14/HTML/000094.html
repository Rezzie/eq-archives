EVOC EVOC EVOC

You are not a shaman. Alteration skill is used for only a few spells. Healing, Str buff, SOW, snare, and ports.

Specialization helps 2 things, mana cost, and fizzles. I find that as much as like grouping, clerics always outheal me, sow and str are cheap, and ports are mana intensive but rarely used in emergencies.

Why evoc? Well here's a typical group situation. Snare incoming mobs, maybe put one dot or nuke once, cast regen on the puller now and then. Med through a lot of fights until--ADD ADD (group shorthand for additional mobs that popped or trained). Then all of a sudden it becomes real important to drop a caster or two quickly before they annihilate the group. All your nukes plus the flame lick/immolate/breath of ro line use evocation, and that's where you need your mana to stretch the farthest.

Koolkat has a good point about the healing, at level 39 a cleric can completely heal a tank for 250 mana. Gheal for 150 might get them a bub of life.

I would only consider alteration specialty if: I regularly group with the same people, and I was the only one who could port, and there was a lack of shamans/druids to help with backup healing duties, and there were plenty of nukers in your group already.

What's interesting is that specialization in a particular field is easy to determine. Whichever skill you use the most goes up the fastest, which makes it logical to allow that skill to be the one you specialize in. Most of the time I bet it will be evocation, but if you group a lot, alteration will be the one. Very infrequently will conjuration move up fast enough, as you don't use it hardly at all. The point is, whatever your style is will determine your path. This part of the game was actually designed properly (imagine that) so relax and let your fighting and grouping habits guide you.
