Ok.

The _only_ part of the naming policy that could concievibly apply is:

11. Names that contain sentences, phrases, or more than two words. (Ikillorcs, Ontop, and Petcarbob, Diediedie) Descriptive compound words are allowed, especially in surnames (e.g. Treehugger, Giantslayer)

and

13. Names where the combination of the first and last name form a phrase or violate a previous rule (e.g. Crymea River, Bran Muffin, Story Thyme)

where the 'previous rule' is

12. Popular and easily recognized names from existing media (e.g. Merlin, Gandalf, Belgerath, Tanis).

First, 13. Ok, managirl beachbum is _not_ a phrase, nor is it from the popular media. So, we are left with rule 11:

11. Names that contain sentences, phrases, or more than two words. (Ikillorcs, Ontop, and Petcarbob, Diediedie) Descriptive compound words are allowed, especially in surnames (e.g. Treehugger, Giantslayer)

This is 3 rules in one:
A> Names that contain sentences.
B> Names that contain phrases.
C> Names that contain more than two words.

Only two words there, so that leaves A or B.

It isn't a sentence. Not in english at least. So, that leaves B.

Is Mana Girl a phrase? Maybe. But, there is the compound word exception:

D> Descriptive compound words are allowed, especially in surnames (e.g. Treehugger, Giantslayer)

'especially' doesn't equate to 'exclusively'. Managirl is definately a descriptive compound word.

The same arguements apply to 'BeachBum', even moreso.

Hence, that GM was in fact _not_ enforcing the nameing policy, but was in reality a GM in violation of Verant's policy.

He really should be disciplined, and the damage undone.

Remember, before expressing your opinion, read the policy.
