Welcome to EQ! It is a very complicated game with a long learning time.

At first:

1. Read the manual.
2. Make a character on the Stromm sever (full of new players)
3. learn to use the interface
4. hit 'h' to 'hail' your guild leaders and others to find more info and tings to do
5. At first, you kill things and sell the 'drops' to NPC merchants for coin.
6. Everything is very expensive to new players, so don't expect to buy new gear real soon.
7. Talk with people in-game.
8. Join a hunting group and lern to play as part of a group.

most importantly:

* Have fun!

Visit on-line resources as needed. For right now, just get familiar with the interface.

The trouble with trying to help a new player is knowing where to begin!

Anyway, here's a few pointers in addition to the help you've had so far.

1) Learn how to re-map your auto-attack key. By default, pressing "A" launches you into automatically attacking your selected opponent. If you are on a "blue" server - and by this I mean a Player versus Environment one - chances are you'll attack a town guard and die very quickly.

2) Learn how to /con. "Con" is "consider" and gives a basic overview on what your selected MoB (mobile object - critter or monster or indeed another player) would react to you. The colour of the text is also an indication. One that says "Glowers at you, ready to attack - what would you like your tombstone to say" should be avoided at all costs.

3) Don't gro too fond of your first character. He/she will die alot until you learn the basics and by then, you may well have found a different character/class combination that better suits you. Experiment!

4) Until level 10 you will not lose experience by dying nor lose your food and drink.

5) Avoid Kithicor Forest at night.

6) Learn your "chat channels". By pressing /ooc <insert question here> you are asking a question Out of Character - this is especially helpful when asking for help and people will normally be glad to help out.

7) If someone sends you a "tell", you can hit "r" to immediately respond back to them.

8) Pressing <enter> will toggle you between speach and actions. For instance, selecting a target and pressing "A" will attacj said target. Press <enter> first, then press "A" and you will say "A". Hope that makes sense!

9) Everybody else looks like they know what they're doing all the time. This is not strictly true

10) Look into joining a Guild but select one that fits your needs. And by Guild, I don't mean the "Bards Guild" or the "Druids Guild" as these are game-driven ones. Other players set up player-run guilds that have a specific raison d'etre in mind. A Guild that is there for general friendship maybe or maybe one of the high-end raiding ones. Make sure you join one - if you join one at all - that makes sense to you. And remember, if you don't like it, you can leave.

11) Um... no, I've rattled on too much

Have fun. Welcome to EQ and welcome to TNZ! Don't forget, we're here to help if we can; I know I'm still the eternal newbie!

It can be difficult to provide useful advice without knowing what areas you are confused on.

Always start with the crucial information: what class are you, what race are you, and what server are you on? With these 3 things, people can try to provide more specific advice tailored to your situation.

Ask about anything that confuses you. Odds are someone here has been in exactly the same situation and will know how to fix it.

Keep in mind that there is no right way to play or wrong way to play. If you are looking to do quests, that is perfectly fine. If you just want to go out and fight, that is OK too. Stick to the things you find yourself enjoying.

Like Iceblossom said "Welcome to Norrath and welcome to TNZ"

There are some key things to remember also.

1. You will die. Death happens but you will get better a avoiding it and you will get better at recovering from it.

2. You are new. There are players out there that have played for years. Don't feel bad that you don't have the best gear, and that everyone seems to know more than you. At some point in the future you will be there.

3. Begging for money and gear is looked down apon. This is not to say that someone won't give you money or gear, but just don't ask for it.

4. Money and gear are simple to get if you have time. There are armor and weapons quests for the all races and classes that can be started at level 1. You might not finish them untill you are 12 or 15 but most of it is good untill your 20's Lots of the low level creatures drop stuff the higher level players want, but do not have time to hunt for. (Spiderling silk, Spider silk, and High, Med. & Low Quality pelts off of Bears, Wolves, & Cats)

5. Don't get frustrated. There are many different aspects to the game that make it like no other. If you run into bad luck, bad players, or any host of other things and don't know what to do and you can't find an answer on a website someplace, then come here and ask. Make sure you give as much information as possible and people here can help you. Chances are someone has already asked the question and a quick search will find the answer.
