JC is one of the easiest and cheapest skills to raise (no offense to JCers):
- you can buy all the components you need from the same store
- components stack, so you can buy a lot at once
- only two components per combine, both vendor bought - to really appreciate this one, I suggest you try Misty Thicket Picnics. Just the final combine for MTPs requires eight components, all of which require subcombines.
- buyback prices on final products cover components cost (minus vendor margin, of course). This is rarely the case with newer recipes in other tradeskills.
- success rates will be high, because you can always choose recipy with trivial very close to your current skill level.
- requires no other tradeskills to do subcombines

quote:
...pottery was nerfed to oblivian...

I can verify this statement first hand.

I was right at skill 200 when the pottery nerf patch came through. I watched as my learning rate went from 1 skillup every 5-10 combines to roughly 1 skillup every 70-120 combines. Albeit, being higher than skill 200 will slow down the learning rate, but an examination of the cost involved shows that 70-120 attempts to get ONE skillup is well beyond even vaguely reasonable.

Opal Encrusted Stein (Trivial 222): 20pp average cost

70-120 attempts to gain 1 skill up = roughly 2200 attempts to gain 22 skill points.

2200 attempts at 20pp each = 44,000pp

Star Ruby Encrusted Stein (Trivial > 250): 70pp average cost

250-222 = 28 skill points to gain = 2800 attempts = 2800 x 70pp = 196,000pp

196,000 + 44,000 = 240,000pp to get from 200 to 250 pottery skill.

I know, I know, all you blacksmithers out there are about to say, "THAT'S CHEAP!!" But remember that pottery doesn't produce +51AC steins or +40AC greaves. The best thing a potter can produce is a range slot item.


Btw, all of the above numbers are based on a character with 186 wis. Yeah, I know that getting wis over 200 will help with the learning rate, but the nerf that took place harmed the little guy far more than people realized. It's like all the testing that took place used a test character with 250 wis or 250 int, but failed to realize that people below level 50 often don't reach that peak. Result = little guy screwed.

If you are an enchanter, I would recommend Jewelcraft as your tradeskill of choice. You don't need to hunt for any dropped components, and there are lots of good cities where the gem merchant and the metal merchant are side by side. If you're high enough level for KEI, you can probably invest 5k or so, which you won't really even lose, it's just vastly easier to be able to buy full stacks of gems and metals at a time. You can also enchant the metals yourself, and with KEI the mana will come back quickly; this makes the resulting item sell back to the merchant at a better price, and you might even sell some of the things you make to other characters if you just /auc now and then what you haven't sold back to the merchant yet. With JC you just park yourself in Thurgadin or Shadowhaven (not sure yet if there's a jewelshop in PoK) and click click click your way to 250, moving up the gems as they get trivial, and up the metals when the gems get too expensive. Any failures to combine will be about covered by the slight profit you make for selling back enchanted jewellry. If you have money to throw away, it would go even faster if you don't bother enchanting. And at the end of your efforts, you have a skill that is still of some vague use, because you never know. JC also has the advantage that it covers one of the steps in doing the trophies. Enough pottery skill for the other step can be learned incredibly cheaply and quickly if you feel independent.

And remember, hold down control to pick up one thing out of a stack, you are going to love that shortcut!
