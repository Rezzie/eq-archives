Mientrixx probably hit the nail on the head the best, but I'd like to expand a little more on it.

The fact that Enchanters don't get any post 44 illsuions isn't the real problem, it's one of the symptoms of a larger problem.

Since I've played the game, back in May of '99, there has been a slow, but steady, stream of requests for improvements to the caster classes. A number of these requests were cosmetic and "fun" changes.

People asked for a way for casters to get away from the "robe look". We were told that the resources weren't available to allow that, and they didn't want casters to look like the melee classes.

Enchanters asked for post 44 illusions. Again, we were told that the resources weren't available.

When there was an uproar, when the Enchanter's Insideous Robe was cloned into the Robe of Ishiva, we were told, to some effect, that was an accident and it wouldn't happen again. Months later, the Magician's Robe of the Elements, and recently the Necormancer's Blighted Robe, fell into the cloning vat. Now, the only class specific, unique looking caster items, that are still unique (as far as I know) are the Necormancer's Shadowbound Robe and Flayed Skin Armor.

When people complained about the scarcety and general unimpressive quality of caster items in Kunark, Gordon told us, if I may paraphrase, that you can only do so much with Intellegnce and Mana.

When RoK was released, the Dev team found the resources to put Spectres in the global file, and give Necormancers a new pet. When SoV was released, resources became available to allow the bear to be put in the global file, giving Shaman a new pet, and Druids a new form. Also, "Six new types of visible armor for each playable race." (As a side note, I wonder how many of those 6 sets of armor can be worn by casters.)

Returning to the illusion issue, to illustrate the problem shared with the rest of the issues. When Enchanters asked why these newly found/made available resources couldn't be used to add some more illusions, we were told that the polygon count was to high, the illusions would harm class distinction, faction issues would be overbalancing, etc., etc.

Now, like the other issues, players offered suggestions. Some of the suggestions are good, some are bad, and almost all of them, like in times before, are met with stone dead silence.

This is what's frustrating. This is what leads to accusations of lying. The preception here is that the people of Verant have deemed that these issues do not warrant attention or address, yet feel they need to be polite and not tell us we're out of line and to go get bent.
