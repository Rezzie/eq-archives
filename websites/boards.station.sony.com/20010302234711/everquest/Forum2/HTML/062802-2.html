Short answer:

A single game environment cannot fairly incorporate a balanced PvP game with a balanced PvE game. The reason is simple: 60 characters ganged up on a single NPC overwhelms the ability of any AI to respond in a 'fair' fashion--so some form of AI cheating MUST be incorporated. The chosen form of cheating--outlandish hit points and outlandish melee damage--turns EQ into a game that would cause instant death if the same abilities were applied in PvP combat.

The chosen fixes are weak because they don't address the central issue: mudflation in the high level game because there is no logical game design that states how much damage per second each class SHOULD be able to do. Melees rule because of this and Wizards are pretty much useless, especially as compared with D&D wizards. So a new spell that does %age damage but only to the RIGHT KIND of creature (boy that is explainable in a fantasy story: Merlin, the prince of wizards, ran up to the dragon and cast a spell that withdrew 10% of a dragon's blood, but the dragon saved and only lost 5%.)

The game needs to be re-designed from the ground up based on the defined framework. EVERY spell MUST matter in the PvE game. None of this 'a-list' hogwash. If it is a spell, it should affect gameplay in some specific and distinct fashion. (Utility spells affect gameplay by providing the advertised utility...which is why Spirit of the Wolf works and the supposed upgrade, Scale of Wolf, sucks--Spirit of the Wolf is like color tv...scale of the wolf is like color tv with enhanced resolution: you only notice if you look REALLY closely, and it makes the image muddy when you do.)

Shaman stat buffs and shaman/enchanter stat debuffs are really pretty much meaningless. Shaman protect spell (ac 12-15?? v. the turtle skin 9-11) is almost meaningless in a game where a shaman by 24th level can easily have 510 ac without the buff. Huh? A 3% buff? Huh? It only really matters if your AC is close to the damage cliff--that inflection point in the damage curve where the damage you take goes WAY down because the monster rapidly becomes ineffective in hitting you.


Is it fixable? Not without imagination on Verant's part. So far they have shown an unwillingness to admit there is a problem or to imagine a good fix. There is one out there, but it MUST be imagined...it is going to come out of SOMEONE's cranium...not out of thin air.
