Fishee- Kiting is a strategy in which a player casts a damage over time spell on a creature and then runs away (the player usually has a Spirit of the Wolf (SoW) buff). After about a minute the player stops and casts the Dot spell again and runs away. This strategy is continued until the creature is dead.

Actually, you don;t need ot cast a Damage over time spell to Kite. You can cast Direct Damage spells also.

snare - run - DD - run - DD - run - DD - run DD

or you can

snare - run - DoT - panic animal - DD - DD - DD - Panic animal. repeat DD til dead

Those are 2 examples.

After the DoT nerf, it is sometimes more Mana efficient to use DD then DoTs. If you can have the creature rooted, feared or in Melee, then DoTs are the way to go, otherwise DD will do the job.

From what I have gathered here, on other boards, and in game discussions this will sum up the damage that a DoT will do and answering Pezz's questions:

1) Full damage. The mob can be rooted or engaged in melee with the caster or anything else.

2) 66% damage. Applies to inital pull, chasing the caster, chasing anything else, returning after fear, etc.

3) Full damage. For druids it works great to DoT then Fear any animals.

Caster moving does not affect the damage unless it causes the mobs to chase you. If in a group and you DoT the mob, then your tank takes off running and the mob chases the tank, then it drops to 66% damage.

Hope this helps.

1) Anything that the caster does has no effect on the damage of the DoT.

2) As long the MOB is moving and chasing anyone (no matter if the caster, a groupmember or someone else) the dmg of the DoT is reduced to 66%

3) As long the MOB is moving away (feared) or standing still (rooted or tanked by you, your tank or someone else) the DoT is doing 100% dmg.
