By the way, while we're on the subject... yes, to people who can't see beyond their own wants and desires, it looks like Verant is just out to screw you over. But believe it or not, you can't have everything your way. Otherwise, my enchanter would be able to charm as many things as he wanted and wipe out Veeshan's Peak (because, you know, there wouldn't be any level cap on charm either) all by himself, with the help of his newfound dragon army, of course.

It is essential in a game like this not to unbalance the choices. Every class has cool things they can do, as well as drawbacks. For instance, rogues are by far the most damaging melee class. However, they have to be behind the target to do so. If they are in front and getting hit on, they become rangers with worse weapons, less hps, and no spells.

With warriors, they have lots of hps, more Armor absorbtion AC than any other class, and the second best weapons of any class. The downside, of course, is that they have no method of escape if they're in trouble. No evade, no gate, they can't even jump off of cliffs to get away like I can with my rogue because they don't have safe fall.

Necros are the ultimate escape artists.... they have fear, short duration mes, root, and Feign Death. They have a pet that is almost equal to a warrior (well, a warrior with crappy weapons anyway), the ability to increase the strength, attack speed, AC, and hps of that pet, and the ability to heal their pet and themselves. If their pet doesn't kill it first, or if the mob doesn't kill them first, they can stack over 8000 dmg worth of DoT's on a mob for less than half of their mana (going by an erudite necro with 200 int and 400 in mana items). They have self only versions of clarity that are better than anything an enchanter can cast, yet take health... but since you can heal up from 2 bubbles of health with a single lifetap at higher levels, I don't see that as a problem. You have a snare DoT which lasts a pretty respectable amount of time, damage sheilds, damage absorbtion sheilds, anti undead nukes, undead charms. You take the strengths of most caster classes and use them for your own benefit. Of course, the problem is that you ONLY use them for your own benefit. There are very few spells in a necro's book which can be used on others. But, truth be told, I like having a necro in a normal group (as opposed to a raid group) more than I like having a wizard. Sure wizards are nice, but Necros don't have the downtime that wizards do.

See, everyone has pluses and minuses. And everyones pluses get less noticable and minuses more noticable after 50. Wizards deal with more resists, so they have to resort to less efficient Lure spells in order to get full damage. Enchanters have to deal with shorter duration mes spells because the others are all resisted. Not only that, but the cost of their buffs past 50 reduces them completely to buffing and crowd control... many times buffing OR crowd control. Rogues find that evade works less and less, to the point that a double backstab is the kiss of death for a rogue at really high levels, unless they have a really alert cleric. Tanks have to deal with their taunts being less effective, which is bad news for clerics and pure casters, since a complete heal or a nuke could be the last thing coming from their hands until the corpse recovery. Magicians and necros both have to rely more and more on their pets due to the resistance problems that EVERY caster faces... even wizards. Shamen get some truly powerful spells, unfortunately they too are resisted around 80% of the time. Some of their spells are just plain silly for most classes. Some of everyones spells are just plain silly in fact.

Basically, 51-60 is just like 1-50. As you level up, you get more powerful, but the creatures you are fighting get powerful at a faster rate than you. Remember when you could solo 2 or 3 evens before stopping to rest? Or yellows maybe? Then by the time you got to 50, you were stuck with low blues and exp greens if you were lucky? And if you made any mistakes, that low blue or high green could flatten you really fast. 51-60 is just continuing that trend, only it's all 50 levels of the 1-50 packed into 10 levels, so the difference of power increase is MUCH more noticable. What makes it worse is that when kunark came out, people were having visions of being able to become powerful enough to solo again, or maybe some of em were even thinking about killing Vox or Nagafen by themselves, hoping to get a white dragon scale cloak or a Cloak of flames for their twink. Little did they know... little do they still understand, that 51-60 is just picking up where 1-50 left off, and compressing 50 levels worth of overpowering to take only 10 levels.

I'm not disputing that necros are worse off against the mobs they're fighting at 60 than they were against the ones they were fighting at 50. What I am disputing is that they are NOT worse off than the other classes.
