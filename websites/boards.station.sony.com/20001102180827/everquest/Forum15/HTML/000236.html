Back - Get ahold of Lizzy Scale cloak from Cazic. Or even cloak of the ice bear from Karg / Martar in everfrost.

Legs: Take the time and get the ivy. It is an extremely satisfying quest to achieve. I know I've given away 2 full quivers myself.

Feet: You gots to have magic kick. Kobald is not magical as I recall. Get Black iron for the cheap for now, and after doing the above, head to unrest and camp the ghost. Dwarven is the best you'll see until Thorny.

Hands: I'm unfamiliar with iksar gloves, but check out the stats on dark mail from lower guk and plan accordingly.

Shouder: I recommend crested spaulders from avenging caitliff in MM. YOu are a nice level to camp him and the shoulders are Common.

I'm not up on midrange stuff in Kunark so there may be better stuff there for your level for some of things things, but Get the Ivy. Every ranger REJOICED the day that patch gave us a rubicite alternative, its your birthrite to achieve all you can

Now, on to suggestion for you, Jesseb. Here goes.

Ears: Forest loops are very good. If you do get a chance, pick up a Sarnak Earring of Station. But those items are very rare and hard to obtain.

Head: Ivy helm is very good for you level. This item will last you for a long while. Later, you can look into getting a Crown of Frogluk Kings, Kedge Helm, Throny Vine Helm, or even a Tolan's Darkwood Helm. But for now, ivy is fine.

Neck: Black Pearl Choker (BPC) is lousy. Get ride of it. In fact, check out your AC difference with it on versus nothing on this slot. I believe you will find that your AC is the same if you have your Kitty Feet buff up. As Zaron suggested, look for a Lava Rune Pendet or a etched ivy charm. You may also be able to find a Collar of Undead Protection from the Frogluk Squire in Upper Guk while camping for a Rune Bone Fork.

Face: Get rid of it. Same reasons as the BPC. Get a Dented Goblin Mask or go kill a iksar priah in Warsilk Woods or something. A Forsaken Priah Mask (AC 5, Cha -25) drops off of them every time and can just be removed prior to buying/selling things.

Wrist: Your wrist are good, although you can easily get a Bracer of Scaveringing in Warsilk Woods while you get the priah mask. It has the same AC, but better stats. A Hero Bracer is also possible if you have a good group to kill the Minotaur Hero. It has the same AC as ivy but better stats.

Arms: Quest for Ivy or head to Lower Guk and get some Azure sleeves. Both are better then Barbed if I remember corectly. Lower Guk may be just a bit tough, but you can do a zone group on live side at that level. In a couple level (around 35) you will do fine down there.

Hands: Iksar gloves are great gloves. Keep them until you get Throny Vine or Tolans.

Rings: Jagged Band is a great ring. Save up some money though and eventually replace these with a Platium Ruby ring.

Waist: The straw belt is great. A little heavy but still a great belt. You may decide down the road to replace it with an FBSS for your haste item.

Chest: Ivy tunic is good. I do not know if you high enough to go to Burning Woods and kill giants there. When you are, replace the ivy tunic with the Tree Weave tunic (AC 25, Str 10).

Back: Get rid of banded, but do not be to upset. I did not get rid of my banded cloak until I was level 43. As Zaron stated, get the Lizardscale Cloak or the Cloak of the Ice Bear.

Legs: Long camp I know, but the ivy leggings are good (although recent rumors of brownie scouts in LF may make getting these a bit more dangerous). Another option is the Silver Plated Leggings. You can most likely hunt these in about 5-8 more levels or so.

Feet: I do not know the Kobald Hide Boots. Are they as good as the Ivy boots. If not, consider buying some Ronin from a paladin and go to MM and get some granite to get some Ivy boots.

Shoulders: Ok, this is an interesting one. Place the Bloodstain Mantle in a backpack for when you need magic resist items. Go get some Crested Spaulders from MM. They are AC 11 Sta 6 compaired to the BSM's AC 5. Big difference in AC.

Weapons: Your weapons are fine at that level. In fact, the SSoY will not proc for you for another 6 levels for so. Trueshot is fine, but you may want to get a Runed Bone Fork for its magic resistance. It is another item that you can swap in when resisting magic is more important then bow damage.

As for weapon skills, Tsk Tsk Tsk. You should attempt to keep up all of your skills as much as possible. There are some high level weapons that rangers can use that are very nice. Woodsman staff will beat out most DW combinations. It is a 2HB weapon. Nature's Wrath, Silver Swiftblade, and Swarmcaller are all 2handed weapons that are very nice then many 1handed weapons. Lupine Dagger is a nice piercer. Jade Mace (9/18) is a 1handed blunt weapon that is consided one of the best weapons. There are many fine options available in all catagories in the upper levels for rangers. It would be very said if you would not be able to use one that you are lucky enough to come across just because you never trained in them at lower levels.

Gloves:
Iksar (and Dark Mail gaunlets) are better then Ivy gaunlets, IMO. So those are fine.

Belts:
Straw Spun Belt is good if you do not have a haste item. TBB is better but is extremely difficult to obtain. So having the SSP, you can currently focus on other items to replace.

Boots:
If the Kolbald boots are AC 5, you may want to look into getting the Dwarven Work Boots (AC 8, Str 3, Dex 5) or the Ivy Boots (AC 10, Sta 5).

The Ivy boots require a piece of Ronin plus some other items. In order to get Ronin, you have to get Fairy Dust from killing fairies in LF. This is not necessarily a wise thing to do if you plan to adventially complete your Rain Caller Longbow quest. That was why I bought my piece of Ronin from a paladin who has to collect it for most of their armor. It cost me 1k pp, so you may have to save up for it. But the 5 raw AC increase is well worth the cost of the item.

Or you can go camp the ghost in Unrest basement for the Dwarven Workboots. At level 31, this area is excelent XP for a good group. If you have experience with Harmony, this area become very easy for you to safely pull mobs for a group. My guild and I had a lot of fun camping this area from levels 31-38. These boots cost about 1-1.5k pp.

Range Slot:
The Rune Bone Fork is a piercing weapon with the stats of Dmg 5, Delay 29, Fire 10, Cold 10, Magic 10. It is not a great weapon for the level you can get it and most people do not use it as one. It is desired mainly for the Fire, Cold, and Magic resists and the fact that it can go in the range slot. It is the rare drop off the Froglok Squire in Upper Guk. He spawn very close to the zone in point of Guk so the camp is extremely easy. He is approximately a level 23 mob. His common drop is the Collar of Undead Protection (AC 5, Poison 7, Disease 7). He was green to me at level 35 and I soloed him then for my fork. The maps at Eqlizer show where the Squire spawns in Upper Guk.
