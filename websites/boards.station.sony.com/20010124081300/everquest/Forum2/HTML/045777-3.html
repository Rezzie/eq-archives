BTW Nice post Sern!

(1) Group DMF

I agree with Sern's #5 point regarding group DMF. This is a nice-to-have but this in no way, shape, or form affects the ability of a necromancer to be useful on raids. DMF is the only buff a necro really needs to worry about casting and even a single necromancer can easily buff 40 people with DMF for a Trakanon without breaking a sweat. (Get regrowth, turn on lich, and then start buffing ... it's easy.) In short, group DMF is not going to solve any necromancer problems. Furthermore, the necro robe in VP's only redeeming characteristic is that it is free single-target DMF. The stats on the robe are horrid compared to the relatively-easy-to-get necro velious robe. If you do add group DMF, please do something with this robe to make it useful. (I don't have this robe, but the only other necro item in VP is the broken staff so it's pathetic, yeah, but that DMF robe is the best thing in VP for necro-specific items.) Even disregarding the necro robe VP comment, I'm afraid that Verant will add group DMF and think that somehow they have improved the necro raid situation. Group DMF would do almost NOTHING for necros in raids. Sure, if we were a buffing class and had 4 buffs we needed to cast, sure. But we are not, we just need to DMF.

(2) Pet Resistances

Fear is not the only pet resistance problem. Getting ripped apart by AEs is as much if not a hell of a lot more important. Most ultraspawns don't even fear. Why don't you make it so that bardsongs can effect pets? This would help alleviate pet resistance problems while at the same time not adding any solo and not adding much grouping power. It also makes a hell of a lot of sense. Most players are surprised when they first learn that group effects don't effect pets -- it's not intuitive. Bard speed songs should affect pets too. You guys DID say you wanted pets to behave more like PCs instead of NPCs...

(3) Improving Mana Dumping Efficiency and Infinite Mana

Absor: I know we're not supposed to talk about this issue, but please read this paragraph.

I am not 100% convinced that our best mana conversion efficiency should remain at 37.5% on the grounds that a necromancer to cleric loop should not result in the gain of mana. The problem is that this argument needs to take the amount of time required to make this happen into account. It takes a significant amount of time to fully dump my mana bar on a cleric and even more so to convert mana using demilich. A 60th level necromancer with demilich can still only convert 310 mana per minute. At 37.5% efficiency, that's currently only 116.5 mana! One could argue that all casters have infinite mana because they can sit and meditate indefinitely. I fully agree that 100% efficiency of mana transfer is too powerful, but I still think 37.5% is too low. Maybe 60%? or 50%? <b>Even at 60% efficiency, we're still talking over two minutes to get enough mana for just one complete heal using demilich.</b> Only a handful of necromancers can even cast demilich, the rest are stuck with lich and there the numbers are even worse. Please Absor, please reconsider the stance on this particular issue.

(4) DoT Poofing

Our guild also has not seen this problem in the past half year either. I have never been told to not DoT something in recent memory. Although I have been encouraged to cast DoTs. Like Xerexs stated, the real issue with DoTs at raids is that they aren't that effective. Half our DoTs are automatically 100% resisted by just about all ultraspawns (lifetap DoT, splurt (shamans cast this one anyway with their epic), darkness, and heart flutter line) while of the remaining ones that do have a chance in hell of sticking on a fair number of ultraspawns (disease, poison, fire line) two of those are cast by the shamans that are present. So this more or less means that if the mob is immune to fire DoTs, necros are SOL and even if not, the necros have to fight over who can cast it. And even THEN, that's still only one spell (two, if you stack ignite and pyro). Other damage casters can cast their damage spells multiple times whereas a necro can only cast one spell. Those fire DoTs take almost 2 minutes to do their damage...

(5) Conjure Corpse

This has to be one of the most overrated spells there is. I almost NEVER need to cast this at raids. Most of the time where I need to cast this spell is in small grouping situations. Let's review all the relevant raiding encounters:

(a) Trakanon. Yes, if your guild is weak enough to get killed by Trakanon then yes, conjure corpse is nice here.
(b) Plane of Hate. Monks pull corpses.
(c) Plane of Fear. Monks pull.
(d) Outdoor dragons. Anyone pulls.
(e) Venril Sathir. Anyone pulls.
(f) Velkator/Djinni. Camp a cleric at a nearby semisafe spot.
(g) Vox/Nagafen. Monks pull and camp a cleric.
(h) Kael. Monk pull or camp a cleric. (Not sure about SS or Thurg as our guild is allied with these, but I'm guessing it's the same as Kael.)
(i) Sirens. Maaaaaybe useful here. Siren's is currently kinda fubar, haven't figured out tactics here.
(j) Necropolis. Monk pull or camp a cleric.
(k) The Hole. Monk pull or camp a cleric.
(m) Veeshan's Peak. Monk pull or camp a cleric.
(n) Plane of Growth. Invis non-KOS/monk pull corpses.
(o) Random Outdoor Epic Mob Fight. Not needed.
(p) Sleeper's Tomb, Plane of Mischief, Veeshan's Temple. Not really sure here, but given the track record I'm sure it's more of the same.
(q) Plane of Sky. Okay, conjure corpse is used heavily here. But in a cheesy, almost exploitish way.

I'm not saying I never cast conjure corpse, I'm just saying it's very, very rare. (I go through maybe 2-3 coffins in an entire week of raiding on average -- not including plane of sky.)

The fundamental problem with conjure corpse is that the player needs to be in the same zone with the necromancer. Achieving this requirement is usually far more tedious than simply pulling it using a monk or camping a lowbie cleric or paladin ahead of the encounter. (And a lot cheaper.) If you changed it so that all a necromancer needed was the corpse or player targetted (but not the player in zone) then this spell would be a lot more useful. To prevent unethical necromancers from doing nasty things with other people's corpses, simply require that a necromancer can only summon someone's corpse if they've been consented using the /consent command. This would make necromancers a lot more useful on raids by making one of their spells actually worth casting on a semifrequent basis. This would, however, also have the unwanted side effect of limiting downtime a little so I'm guessing that Verant will not implement this change. (It would increase cash burn rate though.) Maybe this idea can be tied into Aradune's downtime thread. I have not read that huge thread so I am not sure what issues have been covered there.

Absor, again, thanks so much for creating this thread and for listening.
