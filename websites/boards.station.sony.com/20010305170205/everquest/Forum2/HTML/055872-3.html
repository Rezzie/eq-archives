Wow, Absor, I commend you... Reading all of this took a *very* long time... And I have an interest in the subject =P Sadly, I think this ran a little long, too... After reading your first post, Absor, I cut out as much as I could that looked like it was repeating old stuff, even though it appears you missed a lot, too... I felt there were some valid points made. Hope cutting it down helps...

Plainsight: One thing that I've had an issue with since before the release of Kunark was the removal of Plainsight. Granted, it was removed before the release, but it was the spell I was most looking forward to. Kunark is naturally darker than the original lands, and the Ultravision would have been nice... But what I really wanted was that see invisible. Plainsight lasts twice as long as Chill Sight, costs twice as much, adds see invisible, and I feel it makes more sense from a role-playing perspective. Also, Rangers get vision spells such as Eyes like Cat, Bind Sight, Glimpse, Dance of the Fireflies, and Chill Sight, showing that Rangers do and should have vision spells, yet we're still lacking see invisible. As the only class that can cast invisible and not see invisible, it almost makes invisible self-only, since lacking see invisible all but prevents you from using it to travel with friends.

Riposte/Dodge: I feel that an increase of 25 points in Riposte, like the Knights/Warriors got in RoK, and several more points into Dodge would make sense. Rangers already have 220 Parry, and half of Riposte is a parry, while the other half is a return thrust (which could be performed with a second weapon, ala off hand). It makes little sense that Riposte wasn't raised along with the other defense skills. While Riposte may raise our damage, it raises the damage of the Knights even more, since they use 2 handed weapons rather often, and their skill rating is higher. With the bonus damage from Riposte, Knights are doing damage even closer to Rangers, while Rangers are still far behind Knights in their ability to take/avoid damage. As far as Dodge is concerned... Rangers are a chain-based class, like Rogues, but they have much lower skill levels in defense abilities. Rangers should be dodging with the best of them. They should at least be able to do it as well or better than plate-wearing Warriors.

Kick: Originally, Rangers were supposed to get kick, so they could use an extra skill while dual wielding, and Knights were supposed to get bash, which promoted a shield/sword combination for them. However, at higher levels, Paladins (and SKs?) are allowed to bash even while using certain two handed weapons, making the advantage of kick negligible. So, in light of that, I feel it would be fair to add damage to kick, since it was supposed to be more damaging, but lacking of a stun, or to add a stun to it, perhaps at level 60, since by then many Paladins would have their Epic, allowing them to bash while not using a shield.

Track Screen: This is something I really want... A see-through track screen! Something a lot like the new see-through inventory screen. My current text color set-ups blind me whenever I have to hit the track button, and it makes it hard to talk to my group if I can't see what they're saying.

Natural Resists: Rangers should gain a little natural fire/cold resistance as they level. I was excited when we first got the little fire/cold boost, since I was hoping it would be continued as we leveled. It wasn't. Even a point every 5 levels or something would make us that much better in raids where fire/cold resistance is important.

Calefaction: In it's current form, it's about twice the recast for twice the damage of Call of Flame. That means that it's essentially casting Call of Flame twice, which means that Calefaction is hardly an improvement over Call of Flame, and Calefaction is a level 59 spell. Improved damage or reduced recast would go a long way towards improving this spell.

Concern: Don't get me wrong, I love new spells, but while you may think that adding spells like SNL (Or low level spells, since it's the high levels that need help) helps our defense/AC problem (and it does in solo/in some groups), on raids we lose that bonus, while the other melee classes don't lose it, since their bonuses are innate. Even with the spells increasing AC/HPs, in a raid, or in a decent exp group, we lose the enhanced AC/HPs to improved buffs from other classes, where the other melee-classes natural AC/HPs aren't lost.

Offense: It's great that Rangers have Dual Wield equal to Warriors, but it would be nice if their double attack rose the 10 points needed to equal Warriors, since Double Attack applies to both hands, and it goes off more frequently than Dual Wield. Or, you could always raise Dual Wield over Warriors, since Rangers have always been good at that as well. Other ways to possibly raise Ranger offense, if that's the course you choose to take... You could give us Triple Attack, which I find highly unlikely, and probably too damaging, even if with a substantially reduced timetable. Warriors also get Critical attacks and Crippling blows, and while I don't feel that Rangers should get these (they aren't strong fighters), Warriors are often the third best damage dealers in the game. It's an advantage they have, but it's not their specialty. Having Triple Attack or enhanced Dual Wield/Double Attack would raise us to a better level, and would fit the class well, as we're fast, and skilled with the use of weapons. If we're having no changes to defense, shouldn't our offense be raised just that much more?

Anyways, thanks for taking the time reading this, assuming you did so. Wouldn't blame you if you didn't.

I am not going to try to flame the decision to add Panic Animal into our spell lists, but rather to ask the question, why? I also would like to touch on some topics (please forgive any reposted ideas).

My vision of a ranger is one that IS at one with nature. Note the word nature. Nature is descriptive of life that has not been twisted or belched out from the bowels of some great unknown. For that simple reason alone I see adding panic animal as a method for "fear kiting" the very beasts that rangers are to defend as against the spirit of the ranger. To further support this, if one would look into our Ivy Etched Armor quests, they would find that we are supposed to hunt down someone who, as the story line describes, killed animals for purposes of pleasure. Also leading toward this point is our epic quest. We are to find out what is "twisting" the animals of Norrath. I see panic animal as the opposite of what we are suppost to stand for, and also as a beginning to the end of the role-playing part of EverQuest (at least for rangers). Perhaps a better spell would affect the minions brought about by one of the less nature oriented gods/goddesses. We see this with clerics and paladins with their undead line of spells, and I believe, in the interest of roleplaying, the same "mirror entity" affecting spells should be thought about. Not one that is in-line with our story.

On another topic, again as to what a ranger is supposed to be. Our abilities to be the light, fast, and MOBILE pseudo-tank class are severely hampered by our ability to travel from place to place quickly until higher levels. One example is the level requirement for sow (I know there have been debates about this, but it still remains an issue with many rangers). Another example is the lack of teleportation (please read on before flaming). I have thought about these issues for a while and in a manner to help us attain the mobility we should have and not to totally unbalance the class and make others less useful. A couple possible solutions are: A) A version of sow at a lower level that would be 1) SELF-ONLY, 2) Shorter duration, and 3)Slightly slower, but still enable us to move about quicker without having to spam every zone we run through. (I have worked my ranger through to level 40 so this is not for my benefit. B) Teleportation ability. While I do not believe rangers should be able to teleport to every zone that a druid can, I do feel that there should be a couple of zones that we could teleport to. Maybe one per continent (two for Antonica due to sheer size) should be open for rangers to port to. C) The binding issue (I also know this was discussed). I find it illogical that a ranger can be bound in a city, but not in the wilderness. In fact, I believe that a ranger SHOULD NOT be allowed to bind in a city, with the exception of Kelethin due to its own nature. Again this could be a self-only bind spell only useable in a forest or plane based zone (or a centralized zone in a wilderness area)ie. no dungeons, mountain based regions or deserts. This does little damage to balance issues because is would not open up every zone to binding. Also this should be a mid to high level spell.

As far as the issues of defence it has been covered by many, but it still is important for every one of us rangers who get the lovely "snare beatings" and are nearly dead before a healer can get off a spell. In most other fantasy role-playing games that I have played, there has been a class of armor between the chain class and plate class. For instance a scale class or something of that nature. I do not feel we should be given the equipment to raise our AC as high as a warrior, but the limits of the chain class armor does protect a ranger sufficiently in the higher levels during melee combat.

On the topic of pets. Again, being one with nature would lend itself toward a ranger having a animal companion of some sort. I am not going to go too in depth on this topic because I have not put too much thought into it other than the occaisional mental picture of the typical woodsman caring for the animals he is supposed to be protecting.

I would like to thank the developers for taking the time to listen to us, and show enough patients to sort through all of our ideas, wished and concerns.
