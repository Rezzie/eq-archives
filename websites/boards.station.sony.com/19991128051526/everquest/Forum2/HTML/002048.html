I would skid the AMD CPU on the 'cost effective' option. Not AMD's fault but the motherboards and motherboard chipsets for the AMD CPUs are below that of the Intel ZX/BX boards. Certainly if you intend to use high power video cards and almost all the good new ones are those (TNT2 and later, for eg) are high power cards.

Typically the 'budget' Gaming Rig I build incoporates a Celeron 400 or faster. The most important part of the PC would be your motherboard though, problem is, this is something you can only specify if you or someone else is building the PC from scratch, forget Best Buy. 64mb of PC100 SDRAM, while more is better, with prices being what they are.... Some sort of Vortex 2 sound card, I don't like the Live!s, just a bit too quirky, lacking most of the advanced 3D sound features of the Aureal cards. As for video cards, for the budget system, V3 2000. Or a TNT2/V2 SLI with is a bit more expensive but it a bit more flexible. A fast hard drive, reliable modem and a 250watt computer case .

The best stuff?
P!!!
Abit BX6-2, rev 2.03
TNT2 or GeForce256 video card(I'd wait and settle for the TNT2)
Vortex 2 sound card, ie Diamond MX300
IBM 7200 ATA66 hard drive. SCSI drives are much faster... also much more expensive
96mb of PC133 SDRAM but PC100 will do. No loss in performance.
Cheap PCI software modem. Heck with any Celeron, let alone a P!!!, the loss of CPU power is insignificant. Save the dough so you can buy a second copy of EQ and give it away
