I'm sure DAoC, Horizons, and UO: Origin will be fine games, and will even be successful. But they all share a fundamental flaw that will ensure they never do any serious damage to the EQ client base, nor will they ever truly take off the way EQ has.

The reason? Forced PvP. In order to experience most of those games, you HAVE to subject yourself to PvP.

There are many grand ideas about MMORPGs that support the idea of PvP, even calling it intrinsic to the ultimate success of an MMORPG. However, EQ has completely disproven the idea that people require PvP in their MMORPG experience in order for the game to be a wild success.

Also ... look around on these boards, and on the boards about those other games, and just check out the overall mentality. Not to be disparaging, but face it ... there is a small but persistent minority of players that are intent on doing nothing BUT harassing, intimidating, and ruining the fun of other players.

There is also a small, but persistent number of players that seek to "control" the game, or profit from it (as in "uberguild ebay farmers"). While the competition between these players will probably be very fun and intense, the very nature of how they enjoy the game will pretty much ruin it for your "average", casual player.

I think that the old-time "MUDs" of the past basically were ENTIRELY populated by a certain kind of person, simply because of their exclusive nature. You didn't have the average, casual Joe & Mary Smith blundering in on those old skool text MUDs because they were very eclectic and exclusive.

Now, however, EQ is turning the MMORPG market into a mass-market entertainment field, where people go to have a good time after work and on the weekends, instead of watching TV or heading out to the movies. They have absolutely NO interest in PvP (by and large), and aren't prepared to deal with the antics of Uberguild Farmers hoarding areas & loot for RL profit or just in-game notoriety .. or to deal with PKers bent on developing an in-game infamy for their character.

For these players, which in my opinion represent the vast bulk of EQ players, UO: Origin, Dark Age of Camelot, and Horizon will be seen as interesting games, pretty games, even cool games ... but they will never move from the safety and security of "blue" servers and subject themselves to the anarchy and mischief that is displayed by a small yet persistent percentage of the gaming community.

I mean, it's apparent on BLUE servers and even on the message boards .. . can you imagine what these guys would be like in a PvP/Ebay profiteering free-for-all?

Few will leave the safety of the EQ Blue-Server playstyle to dive into the gaming chaos of a PvP realm, regardless of whatever assurances are made about in-game "systems" to deal with rampant PKing.
