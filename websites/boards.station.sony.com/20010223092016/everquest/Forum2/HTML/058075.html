All right, due to the impression that you all have given me, it seems that I need to cover wizards next…
I’ve already made several notes to myself about wizards, and I may as well start out this discussion with those notes. Keep in mind that these ‘talks’ are geared toward making sure that the classes are ‘balanced’. Please work toward that goal when posting (yes, even if that means you suggest a ‘nerf’). But I’m also interested in hearing about bugs and such too, I’m just not going to focus on them.

Here are the notes that I already have. Keep in mind that these are only my notes, and just because I’ve listed something does NOT mean that I like the idea or that the designers are working on making changes based on these notes. This is just what it is, a list of ideas. I’ve also added my notes to you all under the header of Note:

AoE spells: Mentioned elsewhere… (earlier on my list, probably under mages: basically the idea here is that maybe pets should be removed from the equation).

Gaze/Glimpse: Due to the clipping plane, these spells don’t really allow further seeing, just zooming in (which makes your resolution worse anyhow).

Note: this may not be ‘fixable’ at all due to the way the game works. It’s not like we can force your PC into a higher resolution when you use this spell…

Fade/Shadowstep/Yonder: There seems to be a problem with these spells. Since they will not teleport you up, they don’t work too well in hilly terrain; because the spell only sends you a few feet (stopping when it reaches a hill or building).

Note: Again, this may also not be something we can change, but it’s on the list.

Bind Sight: Seems to cause viewer to see the target as running into walls or spinning in circles if target goes up an incline.

Enstill: It seems that this spell is not a more useful or more powerful root, it’s just more expensive.

Cazic Gate: At level spell is attained, using it will most often cause death because the caster appears amidst many aggressive monsters that /con white to them.

Note: We’ve actually discussed this before. If I remember correctly, the general feeling was that having a dangerous gate like this wasn’t necessarily a bad thing. But it is also still on my list.

Tishan’s Relocation: This spell evacuates you to a very dangerous location, a monster crossroads in the Skyfire mountains. Could the location be moved, or was it intended that this be a very dangerous evacuation spell?

Note: See note about Cazic Gate.

Concussion: A level 39 spell dropped by a creature in a location that requires a party of level 50s to handle. Should it be in such a location?

Icestrike effects: Graphical effect looks like fire.

Lightning Bolt effects: looks exactly like Fire Bolt.

Other lightning and cold effects: It seems that there might be other spells that have the look of fire when they should not.

Having just re-read this list, I see nothing at all related to actual balancing stuff. This is all nit-picky stuff about minor effects and bugs and such (with the possible exception of Area of Effect spells, changing those would certainly have an effect on class balances). So, please, try to focus on balance issues…

Alan

The balance problem is simple.

Wizards, pre-Velious, could do a significant amount of damage in a rather short period of time to a monster with 32k HP. Ice Comet -- with no resists -- is 3.5% of a monster's life.

That same nuke -- again, with no resists -- is 0.11% of a Thrifling Focuser's (sp? The trees in PoG with 1,000,000 HP) HP total.

This is not even mentioning the exceedingly high resists that all casters face in Velious, that is driving wizards to use FAR less damaging lure spells just to do any damage AT ALL.

Either wizards are broken, and need HUGE upgrades in mana, damage, and efficiency for Velious (and ONLY Velious) -- or Velious itself is broken. It's up to you to figure out which one you prefer to fix.

Absor made a comment to the effect that "one class should have the hardest epic quest" and that it is NOT the wizard's.

No, by "uberness" of the mobs we have to kill it is not the most difficult. I believe the cleric and enchanter ones to be "more difficult" -- HOWEVER, considering the overall game impact that their epics have, they should be more difficult.

This begs the question, "if the cleric and enchanter epics are so difficult, why do so many have their epics already? Why do only 20 or so wizards have them?"

Simple, the clerics and enchanters aren't competing, for the most part with 60 other guilds for very rare spawns with very rare drops.

THAT, Absor, is the problem. Take the drops OFF of these named and EverCamped<tm> mobs. There is NOTHING unbalancing about putting ALL epic drops on triggered spawn, is there?

Wizards have to kill:
Venril Sathir (1 week spawnrate, or after patch)
Phinigel (2 day spawnrate, or after patch)
Cazic Thule (1 week spawnrate, or after patch)

Venril Sathir, on 8 raids now, has NOT dropped the staff.

Cazic Thule on 13 raids, has NOT dropped the skin.

Phinigel on 5 raids has NOT dropped the staff.

Tolapumj (this is a 6 hour raid in itself) has only dropped the mistletoe once in about 10 kills.

So, fine, ours isn't the most difficult. But our epic is VERY low impact to the overall game, period. It offers NOTHING to groups, and offers very little to the individual wizard -- except for freeing up some slots for resist gear because of the INT bonus on it.

The epics SHOULD be hard to get, I don't argue that. But should they be hard to get because the quest is difficult? Or should they be hard to get because it's nothing more than a waiting game?

I think ALL epics should be doable if a person can organize the appropriate raid. As it is, we're dependent on game-timers, and pure chance. Sorry, that's not a "quest", that's just more "EverCamp<tm>" at work.

I know you were getting "snippy" about this. But I think it's not much to ask for you to be objective about this. You could stick all level 70 mobs in our way to finish the Wizard epic quest, and I wouldn't criticize you for that. Unless, you stuck half of them on one week timers.

Triggered spawns were a great idea, so use them. The wizard quest is not "hard", it's just poorly planned in light of the massive populations and other issues like timezones...

Some will never see their epic not because they don't have the "power" and "connections" to complete their quests, but because they're in a timezone that pretty much disqualifies them.

The Epics are a reward for completing a very "difficult" set of quests. But that difficulty shouldn't be measured by how many months you have to wait for that uber-mob to spawn.

We ALL pay to play. We should ALL have equal opportunity to complete our quests regardless of where we live, and the hours we keep. I play enough hours to clear either Plane, so I'm not a casual player. I >should< be able to do my quest based on the forces I can assemble...
