Here's some stuff that you might see people in groups do or at least talk about. These are things that are just good to know....

Pulling: This is the act of the group sitting in one spot (often called "the camp"), and a single player (the puller) going to grab a critter to kill, and bringing that critter back to the camp for the group to kill.

Now, sometimes the puller doesn't get just one critter, so....

All On One: Everyone should attack the same critter at once. The point is that it's better to all attack one critter until it's dead, than it is to all spread out and fight multiple critters at the same time.

Now, if you have a bunch of critters gathered together, how to you know which one your groupmate is attacking? Like this....

Assist: This is a little tricky, but very imporant. The "/assist" command is typically used as follows: target a player who's already beating on a critter, do /assist, you will now target the critter that the player is beating on. This is great for everyone organizing to beat on the same critter.

So, which critter should everyone attack first? Well, given a choice....

Kill Casters First: No, not your fellow player casters, but critter casters. Why? Because they cast mean spells and still hit as hard as a regular melee critter, so they're very, very dangerous. This is especially important for healer critters, they need to be taken down as quickly as possible, or they will heal themselves and the other critters, making the fight a lot harder.

Crowd Control: There are certain ways to make life easier when you end up having to fight more than one critter at once. "Mez" refers to Mesmerization, a line of spells enchanters get, which make a critter just stand there and not attack -- as long as no one attacks the critter. Other classes have similar spells too at later levels.

Another way to do this is with Root spells. Root the extra critter, and just everyone move away from it. I called this Ghetto Mez. It's a great trick for Druids, Rangers, or anyone with a Root spell.

Roles: There are some common roles that people will often end up playing in a group. Some examples are:
[u]Tank[/u]: A class that can take the critters' beating fairly well. You should try to make sure that you always take the beatings, not your groupmates. Warriors, Paladins, and Shadow Knights are typically the best choices.
[u]Healer:[/u] A class that can heal other classes. Obviously, you heal your groupmates (and yourself). Clerics, Shaman, and Druids often fill this role.
[u]Damage Dealer:[/u] Sometimes called DPS (damage per second) or Nuker (referring to casters). You do a lot of damage and you do it quickly when needed. Includes Rogues, Wizards, Mages, Beastlords, Necromancers...anyone attack-oriented.
[u]Snarer:[/u] Snare-type spells slow down how fast a critter can run, which is very helpful in a lot of situations, especially when the critter tries to run away. You should snare every critter before it runs. Rangers and Druids often play this role, but other classes can too after certain levels.
[u]Main Assist:[/u] Also called Main Tank, this is the person that everyone agrees they will /assist. Keep the same target so everyone can beat on it. Note that this is often the puller, but doesn't have to be.

There are others of course, depending on the situation, but those are kind of the basic ones.

Note also that every situation is different, and who knows what you'll have to do to make it all work out. So be flexible too.

If anyone has anything to add, please do so. But try to remember that I'm trying to keep things basic, and for low level folks.

-Mori

One thing I forgot:

Loot Rules: Most groups will (or at least, should) set up some rules for looting the critters. It sucks to have one fools looting everything while no one else gets anything. Common loot deals are:
1) FFA (Free For All): Don't keep track of anything, just loot what you want.
2) FFA No Greedy: Don't keep track of anything, just loot whatever, but try to only take your fair share and let others enough.
3) Alpha (Alphabetic): Loot in alphabetic order. So Iceblossom loots one, then Kharlist, then Mori, then Venomax, etc.
4) Alpha on [Whatever]: Where [whatever] is some more valuable item that sometimes drops, i.e., Bronze or Fine Steel weapons. So Iceblossom gets the first weapon, then Kharlist gets the next, etc.
5) Loot to Xpp: Each person (usually in alphabetic order) loots until they've gotten X plat worth of coin and stuff.

Note that with all of these, when rare drop items show up, the group should roll on them, regardless of how they're looting the common drops.

So if you're hunting gnolls in Highpass Hold, and the Polished Granite Tomohawk drops, the group should roll on it, regardless of whose turn it is to loot.

Note two things that are VERY RUDE:
1) Being a loot hog. Don't loot every kill, even if you're group is doing FFA. Have /some/ common sense and let others loot some too.
2) Ninja looting. This is worthy of a beat-down. This often refers to looting a rare drop item and then running off with it. Note that your group will know you looted it, and they will hopefully never group with you again.

Good post. A couple of extra points if I may:-

Groups work best with a designated leader. He's the one who decides on looting policy, mob levels to go after, resolves any disagreements, keeps the members in role, handles any requests from players wanting to join/members needing to leave, etc. Channel all such issues through him/her, using tells rather than groupchat (and never use guildchat for group messages, that really hacks off the rest of the guild who aren't there and aren't interested!

Groups also work well with a designated rotation guy - he's the one who keeps the alphabetical list of members for looting and at the end of each fight says something like "Apollo loot, Bravo next then Deadeye". Do NOT allow just the tanks to loot because the casters don't have the encumbrance to carry it, give them equal looting rights or some plat but make sure they aren't sitting there increasingly annoyed that they're being left out.

Healers - don't waste mana healing the tanks until they are down to 50% health. Sit down when you're not healing and don't be afraid to do nothing in a fight if you're not needed - your job is to be there with enough mana when you ARE needed! It doesn't matter how often or how rarely that is, if the group is still alive at the end of the session then you've done your job. If there's more than one healer in the group have a command set up that tells the group who you're healing, that way you don't both waste your time and perhaps another member's life by both healing the same member at the same time. Agree who is primary healer, who is secondary healer (primary keeps all mana for healing) while secondary does some buffing and may join in the fight).

Casters - at the end of every fight and throughout downtime give a mana report, typically saying something like "/g oom", "/g 50m", "/g fm" to cover from being out of mana , through half mana to full mana. If you're out of mana in a fight say so.

Pullers - don't ask for a mana report, get told "20m" and pull! Unless told otherwise, start scouting around when the casters are up to 80m. Use a set hot key message such as "/g Incoming %t" which tells the group you've pulled AND what they can expect (the %t part automatically inserts the name of the targeted mob).

Buffers - don't waste your mana buffing everyone in the party. Similarly, if you're not in the front line don't expect the buffers to give you every buff. It's useful if buffers cast on themselves first, then they know when the buffs need renewing because they can see their own going. Don't forget that some buffs cost components that are expensive to buy, don't ask for those buffs unless you really need them and let the buffer loot enough to cover the cost or pay him something. Regular recipients of such buffs know what components are used and keep a stock to hand to the buffer.

Everyone - remember, it's a team effort. Work together but concentrate more on doing your own job well than telling others how to do theirs ! If you have a gripe with the way amother group member is working then send a tell to the leader, don't have it out in groupchat.

Lastly, if a group isn't working out then just make your excuses and go. Don't wait for the inevitable death and exp loss. Over time you will get to know other people, even join a guild with some of them maybe, and you'll have a nucleus of friends you like to group with. Pickup groups can be worthwhile but they can also be an instant disaster. Learn from experience!

A few more tricks for /assist.

You can also add a person's name to your /assist hotkey. If Morisenshi is the MA or Tank, I can create a hotkey (I would label it Morisenshi) that contains "/assist Morisenshi". That way I don't have to target him all the time. In a large group, I might have an /assist button specifically for the MA, and then another general /assist button in case I need to join someone else in the group.

In addition to using your function keys, you can target a person by clicking on their name int he group window.

You can also use /assist to find out who a mob is beating on. Target your Tank and click /assist to target the mob he is whacking. Thne click /assist again to see who the mob is whacking. If everything is going well, the mob is attacking the tank and the tank is attacking the mob, and you can use the /assist key to alternate between the two, which is useful for casters who might be buffing/healing the tank and nuking the mob. If things are not going well and the mob is whacking on your robed caster, you can target your tank and click /assist twice to target the player the mob is whacking, for healing and buffing.

Actually, the only time you want to kill the higher lvl mob first is if the enchanter is of equal or lower lvl, or it is otherwise unnaturally resistant to mez, or the enchanter has known difficulty mezzing such a creature. The proper kill order, almost universally, is to kill healers first, then kill from the lowest lvl mob up to the highest lvl mob. The reasons being that if you kill the highest lvl mob first, that means it will take longer to kill then all the other mobs, hence more time spent mezzing the other mobs, and more mana used. The reason you kill the healers first is because if, god forbid, the enchanter should die and one of the healers comes unmezzed, you just went from a bad situation to a nearly impossible situation. If another mob comes unmezzed you will be ok root parking it, but if it's the healer rooting it will just inspire it to heal more, slow if it's a shaman. I know that as an enchanter I often would take advantage of the necessity of my class and go to zones like velketors lab at lvl 45 /smirk. In those cases we definitely had to pick off the higher lvl mobs first, also there are some mobs that are not mezzable, and given the option of killing an unmezzable mob first, or a mezzable mob first, obviously you want to kill the unmezzable one. Regardless if the mezzable one is a healer.

A newb note, since this is for the newb forum, IF the unthinkable happens and you break mez, /gasp. If you're not the main tank, hit your assist key again to make sure you're on the right mob, generally speaking you will probably not have to even move cause about 75% of the time you are on the right mob, just the enchanter has had trouble picking the other mobs out to mez, or is using an aoe mez. If you're the main tank, look to see if you were told to switch mobs, if not hack away, the enchanter will figure it out. Another newb note, if a mob agros to a chanter from a resisted mez, you will NOT be helping that enchanter by attacking the mob, I dunno what gives melees the impression that smacking a mob with a sword will out agro a mez spell and a tash, but news flash, it does break mez if the ench should be lucky enough to channel one. We have stuns, fast cast roots, and fast cast mez, leave us to handle our own mobs plz. What would be appropriate if your enchanter needs help is turning off attack, targeting the mob and casting a non damaging root, or hitting a taunt key, if you're a druid or ranger, and your root does dmg, cast snare. Make a hotkey that lets the group know if a mob is snared or rooted, because snare and root have specially unique effects on agro. This is also true of blind, but blind becomes less and less frequently used as you ascend in lvls.

A proper assist key consists of /assist "Insert name here". The trouble with assist keys is you constantly have to remember to change the name there, one hint that you have the wrong name is if you hit your assist key and nothing becomes targeted. You should make an assist key and use it constantly, I have mine hotkeyed to the number 1, everytime I tap 1 I instantly know what the main tank is attacking. This is useful even as an enchanter, because once the main tank chooses a mob, I know that all the rest are fair game for me to play with. If you have multiple mobs that have the same graphic, you can tell which the tank chose by the name of the mob flashing once you have it targeted, if the mobs name is flashing and you need to mez another mob, just click any mob that is attacking your group who's name is not flashing, easy breezy. There are some situations where the mobs do not have visible names, and therefor no visible flashy names either. These are rare and unfortunate, /grumble sony, and in such instances, if you get two mobs of the same graphic/appearance, my method is to click on them until one is at full health or relatively full health, then mez that one. The reason I say relatively full is that he could be at 98-99% health because he is hitting someone who has a damage shield buff cast on them, that dmg's anything that hits that person. But if the mob doesn't attack, he can't be dmg'd by it, therefor it is mez safe, Yay!

Some, enchanters do it another way, and will tell the tank to not attack anything until he (the enchanter) has mezzed all but one mob, and he (the enchanter) will just click things and mez away. Well that's up to the enchanter really, but I have received many many compliments on my system. Currently, using it in the planes of water and fire.

If you use assist you will have mastered a tactic needed from lvl 1-65 in every single group or raid you partake in. It is probably the single most important skill in the game. Some of the others are agro management, which is difficult to describe because it changes by so many factors, your lvl, class, zone, mob, spells, specific spells on the mob, sitting/standing. Etc.. consider how long this post is just from assisting, and mob order, then consider what it would be like if I talked about agro management.
