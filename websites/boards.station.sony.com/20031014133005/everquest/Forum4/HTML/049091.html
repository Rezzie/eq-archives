It's unlikely to affect performance very much, but decent name-brand RAM tends to be somewhat more reliable than generic RAM. Generic RAM is often made by bodging up chips that failed the RAM tests and were therefore turned down by the brand-name manufacturers: in theory that's fine because they don't use the parts of the chips that failed, but who knows what other random failures you might see on a chip that doesn't work 100%?

Kingston is a known company rather than a mom & pop, or a fly-by-night shop with cruddy parts.
Are they better than others? Couldn't tell you.
Just as a tip, you might want to check out Crucial.com and check they're prices. They're the outlet for another of the big names.
Also while you're at the site look for something called "Belarc Advisor". This is a free download that will scan your system and tell you every piece of hardware thats on it. It also tells you what memory you have, what slot it's in and what your systems max upgrade is. All information STAYS ON YOUR SYSTEM. None is sent anywhere else.

I like this for a reference tool for upgrading.
