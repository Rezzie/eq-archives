Before the release of RoK, everyone was very happy with the game. Classes were balanced, the economy was stable, and only 6 or 7 people per server had an Electrum-Bladed Wakizashi.

Now, the bombshell, Verant introuduces level 51 to 60. Yes, it would have been great if Verant keep the margin of difference between classes equal into post-50, but no. Warriors became the main class for it. Warriors gained a HUGE increase of HP post-50, with Rogues coming in second. Rogues became the new gods in the park, with Warriors tanking for them. Rogues could Evade and do damage, and even tank, since their defence is higher than Rangers, who happen to be tanks. Plate-using classes also had an increase to defence skills (Dodge, Parry, Riposte), yet the light armor classes got nothing.

Now, tell me...can a bulky, plate-wearing class dodge better than a lightly armored, agile Ranger? This is where we realized Verant did not know what they were doing. Rogues got Safe-Fall yet they were wearing plate armor faster than Rangers.

Why is it, that a Monk or Rogue who is NOT a tank, can actually tank BETTER than some Rangers or Paladins?! Monks use LEATHER for god's sake!

It goes downhill from here...they try to fix this by giving Rangers Jolt. Bad move. Instead of helping, it actually adds agro when it is resisted, which happens quite a bit, and its hard to cast when a mob is hitting you for 200s.

With Evade being nerfed for Rogues, it will put them in danger of being killed as fast as a Ranger, but they still have very high defence and skill caps, so the nerf may not be so bad.

Verant REALLY went wrong when they made Jolt a spell. Rangers are still lacking anything that links them to druids. They also lack anything that any other class doesn't have.

Wizards are also underpowered since they have spells that are resisted so much, that they end up using a ton of mana for nothing. Even a 2k nuke is not enough to stand up again a mob with 200k of hp.

Levels 51 to 60 have really widened the gap between the melee classes and casting classes. Verant should re-think their idea and try to make the gap smaller, so a Warrior is still the main tank in EQ, but not a requirement for survival when a Paladin could fill in the role. Need a damage dealer? Rangers can do more damage than any other class in EQ by using melee, DoTs, nukes, and damage shields. But when the mob taunts on the Ranger, he has no time to cast or be interupted to cast Jolt, which probably will be resisted and, with his low defence and dodge, be killed in 2 seconds.

Rogues on the other hand, have time. Since they have very high defence (which is does not make since Rangers are light tanks and Rogues are NOT tanks) to hit the Evade key, that is automatically used, and ALWAYS works, and is never resisted or needed time to cast, or interupted.

Monks just Feign Death, but they also have more defence than a Light Tanks, yet they are not tanks at all (they wear LEATHER).

RoK really ruined EverQuest. Verant widened the gap between classes so much, that the game is not playable without a Warrior, Rogue, Cleric, or Enchanter. This is their downfall, and they should think about fixing it before they lose their players to about 50 other online RPGs that are coming out soon.
