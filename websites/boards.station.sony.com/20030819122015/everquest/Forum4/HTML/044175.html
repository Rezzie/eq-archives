Don't listen to the naysayers! If you want it for roleplay purposes then go for it!

Downsides:
10k one is not as fast as jboots (it's just barely faster than normal run)
Startup and Slowdown time
Horse head can block arrows (this doesn't happen with a drogmor)
Difficult to melee on a horse (not impossible, but difficult)
Inability to Forage

Upsides:
Med without sitting (great for when you want to just sit back and fire arrows, but watch line of sight)
No encumberance (great when farming for heavy items or when transporting heavy things/coin)
Safe Fall (minor upside)
Roleplay Aspect (How upside that is depends on how roleplay you are)

Drogmors take up a huge deal of space, so I if you have multiple melees attacking a mob, they are gonna hate you. Also, if mob is being pushed/rotated, keeping up while mounted is gonna be real awkward. But I know that high rangers tend to just shoot from a distance, so you might be ok on that. Not sure on drogs, but to sell back a horse, you turn in in to a 'stable hand' NPC in bazaar, get a 'bag of platinum', and that will sell to a vendor. You lose roughly 33% of horse value when you sell one. So if you buy for 9500, you'll get back only about 6300 if you decide to sell it back.

Why did I get a horse when I'm a Bard and can move faster than anyone else in the game? Because I like them. 'nuff said. I have no shortage of money, and I think they're cute, so I got one.

I think my guild clerics and wizards would kill me if I got one of the ornate chain bridles, though. Would be neat to get one eventually...

Anyway. Actual gameplay upside/downside? Upside: sitting med while being able to cast. That's kinda cool. No sit aggro, either. That can be a lifesaver. They -are- a little faster than run3, but take a few seconds to speed up and slow down, really, if you're getting a horse for moving, get an expensive one. ;p

Downsides: no ducking to interupt casting. The ears get in the way and can make targeting difficult, and the horses can be bulky and get in the way of people meleeing around you, which is really annoying to them. (trust me on this. I melee a lot. Leezards are worse, but horses still get in the way.)

Anyway, kinda losing track of my thoughts now, so. All in all: horses rock. Yay!
