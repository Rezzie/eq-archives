Actually, that's just random -- and in a way pretty lucky, since in a lot of places they're likely to run to some friends when they flee, and get you killed.

There are a couple main ways to keep something from running. The two most common are Root and Snare. Snare is better for that, because a rooted mob will fight to the death, whereas a snared mob will simply flee at zero speed when it gets too low on health. If you're playing a class that can't root or snare and you're soloing, you'll just have to turn autoattack off while it's in the tree, and wait for it be on the ground again before dealing the finishing blow.

There are ways and means to loot these annoying mobs.

Firstly, move around the tree left-clicking furiously on various bits of the trunk and nearby floor - you may well find yourself targetting the corpse. Then you either right-click in the same spot to loot it OR (more reliable) type the /loot command which causes you to loot whatever corpse you've got targetted.

That's particularly useful for those mobs that "float" in the trunk off the ground - sometimes the right-click loot just won't work there even though you can target the mob. I suspect the /loot command doesn't respect the Z-axis (just like mobs fighting) so provided you can target it and you are within the x/y loot range, you'll succeed.

The other option for targetting the corpse won't necessarily work - it's just something that I seem to recall doing... try hitting F8 (target nearest NPC). Like I said, I can't guarantee that that works, but I seem to recall accidentally targetting a corpse with it before now. Once you have the corpse targetted, the /loot command is your friend
