So I read through looking for posts by Absor, and I see them, much to my surprise. So, first I must say, to those of you whining about how Absor isn't listening, that's a bunch of garbage.

I didn't see if there was a follow up on Absor's statement that the Voceration and Greater Voceration fire pets were suggested to him as being not quite as good as the 29 pets, but I will check this later, since I am 32, and I know someone with those spells.

What I'd like to see, is people really working towards important core issues. All the fluff is good, but it takes away resources from more important issues.

I was just in Cazic Thule, and we had a group of casters, sitting and nuking some alligators and lizards, that were just sitting on a bridge, doing nothing, due to pathing. They'd just wait until their mana came back due to the enchanter, and nuke some more. Hey, I want free experience too. Pathing is more than a problem for mages, it's a problem everywhere.

On that note however, I was solo'ing to get from 30-31, because the experience was so slow, and I found something interesting. At similar levels, most caster classes, have to rest if they solo upwards of 5 separate pulls. A lot of people said simply, they could only manage 2. Without any buffs, other than my own, with full mana, I could take 10. With two bubbles of mana, I took upwards of 5. That's with little danger or difficulty. Yes, we're talking blue mob pulls, but the comparison remains the same in regards to other caster classes having to rest.

While I saw a VERY convincing argument that magicians have a low role in the high level game, and that this isn't a big deal, because so few players actually get there....it's still important. If the class distinctions don't carry over, what's the point of having them? After doing some personal research, I've come to the following conclusions, which may or may not be agreed with.

I'll start with a question, what's the point of having a spell to lower magic resistances, if it's resisted?

I don't think pets should be compared to players, I think we're using the wrong basis of comparison. I think that pets should be compared to mobs, of similar levels to the pet. After all, the pet is more like an NPC than a player by far. However, the pet NPC's ability and strength should be taken into account when comparing the mage PC to the other PC classes. I agree that resistances are probably a problem, because I see areas where they're problems now, and shouldn't be, logically speaking.

The biggest problem, is hunting in general at higher levels is supposed to be incredibly difficult for all classes, increasingly difficult as your levels raise. So my suggestion, which will make me VERY unpopular is a combination of nerfing and empowering of classes. Perhaps nerfing the tanks should be looked into, at post 50 levels, as well as empowering the int casters. To a point of balance, not to create another rift where the casters become more powerful....this would defeat the purpose.

The biggest problem is that we can't please everyone. It's impossible. Right now, the post 50 game is suffering more, as far as magicians are concerned, than the lower levels. There are some things that could be changed\added at lower levels, but, the problems become increasingly evident as you increase level.

I only got to about the middle of page 3 in the string of posts, so when I have time later, I intend to read the rest. If I've addressed things others have already suggested, I apologize for wasting additional time.

While others probably won't say this, my appreciation to Absor for looking into the issue, no matter the list he comes up with, so that at least some problems are addressed. To those whining, I'd like to add, at least something here is better than nothing.
