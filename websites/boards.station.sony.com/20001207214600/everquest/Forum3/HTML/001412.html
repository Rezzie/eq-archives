The following quest has been unsolved since about the middle of May, and it is causing some distress for those of us who play Iksar Shamans and actually enjoy the quest aspect of the game.

At the conclusion of the fourth skull quest (fifth overall) the Iksar Shaman is told to turn in his items to Mystic Dovan in the Swamp of No Hope for the next quest. Mystic Dovan gives the Iron Cudgel of the Prophet and sends the shaman out to collect the "Skulls of DiNozok," which must then be returned to Hierophant Zand in the Temple of Terror.

After much searching and identifying, it was finally discovered that the "Iksar Skull Helm" dropped by a Sarnak Adherant in the Sarnak Fortress area of the Lake of Ill Omen zone identifies with the magic spell as a "Skull of Die Nozok." When this is returned to Hierophant Zand, he says, "We are Dai and Die and we await our skulls and your Iron Cudgel of the Prophet. Become a channeler." The problem is that every level 20-30 MOB the length and breadth of Kunark has been slain and nothing drops a skull that identifies as the "Skull of Dai Nozok." My gut hunch, based on the previous quests in the series, is that the Dai skull is supposed to be a rare drop item in the loot table for the Sarnak Adherant, but that it was inadvertantly missed when the loot tables were populated, or if the claim of one player can be believed, that it was inadvertantly scrubbed from the loot table in an update to the game.

A thread detailing the search has reached 110 posts over at the EQ Forge/Forums/Shaman/Quest and Special Items board, and it is rather distressful for those of us trying to complete the quest because we have faith that the ultimate Iron Cudgel upgrade will be a truly special weapon instead of the ho-hum 5/23 magic-hitting toy that we have in the Iron Cudgel of the Prophet.

If Hobart or Esther could please look into this quest and give us some acknowledgement that they have determined that
(1) the quest is indeed bugged, or
(2) that they have found the Dai skull in the place it is supposed to be and we all need to keep searching, it would make a lot of players very happy.

Thank you, Ester, for giving the Iksar quests a priority status.

A note on the stats of the Cudgels: the Iron Cudgel of the Mystic is (if memory serves) 4/20 non magic. To get it you have camp the Undead Crusader (which has more than one spawn point, but is still ridiculously rare - spent 40+ hours getting the six skulls, and I am mightily sick of the inside of Kurn's Tower now). The Undead Crusader drops, besides the skull, a Fine Steel Mace which has better stats than the Cudgel.

The shadowknight Khukris and the warrior Pikes have much better damage/delay ratios, the Pikes have Armor Class, and the necromancer Skullcaps have decent AC and have stat bonuses at the same point in the quest series we're currently at with the Cudgels. All of these quest items, except the Cudgel, are items the class in question would likely use at the level gained. The Cudgels need a slight improvement, either in damage/delay or a stat buff, to be brought up to par with the other class quest items.

Iksar Shaman Cudgel quests in General...

1.) At lower than say level 24, a successful shaman MUST melee to be successful. Therefore a series of weapon upgrades is logical and necessary!

2.) The first quests are just right.

3.) The requirement to go to burning wood to get Mendglow clay for a weapon usable (and otherwise attainable) at around level 9 (or below) is quite harsh.

4.) The Skeletal Crusader in Kurns Tower who drops ALL of the needed components for a quest is such an infrequent spawn that getting these items (Appropriate around level 15-18) would take a minimum of ten hours, and very likely 30 or more hours.

4a.) The quest after the skeletal Crusader/ Ckurns tower can probably be completed in 20% or less of the time required of the previous quest -- indicating to me that it is bugged.

5.) Easily attainable items below 10 PP (Fine Steel Morning Star, Forged Morning Star) are superior to all but the last known Iksar Shaman cudgel reward (having a similar ratio, but magical).

Of the above, #4 is the most severe problem. I would also say that a *marginal* upgrade to the efficiency of the Cudgel gained via Skeletal Crusader and Glowing Skull quest would be quite welcome -- and you might actually see Iksar shaman using their quest weapons after level 10.
