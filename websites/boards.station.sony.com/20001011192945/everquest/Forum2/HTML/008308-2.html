Let me add my voice to the growing numbers of those opposed to this idea. I cannot believe that such a suggestion was even made. The only purpose this could possibly serve is to punish a large number of players who, in my opinion, have done nothing wrong.

I have several objections to this.

First, there is no "reality" or RP sense to such a change. Say I purchase, in real life, a toolbox designed to hold hammers, nails, etc. If I decide to store my wallet in it overnight, my wallet will be there the next day. Sure, the toolbox wasn't designed for overnight wallet storage, but it will work. These containers are not magical in any way.

Second, I see no practical impact on game balance in allowing items to stay in combinable containers. I only see a couple of minor issues. Some combinable containers are slightly less weight than their equivalent non-combinable counterpart. I can save a couple of weight units by using a sewing kit instead of a backpack. There are a couple of containers that can be obtained for free by starting a newbie quest. These are usually lore, so the total impact is that the newbie who figures this out gets to save 5g or so over the cost of a backpack. Big deal!

Third, bank space and inventory space is already very precious to some of us. There are many many items in the world that cannot be used immediately or require additional items in order to properly use them or complete a quest. Other items are not appropriate for my class so I save them to give or sell to others. If one also participates in trade skills, they will have multiple combinable containers. I carry a fletching kit, sewing kit, and a mortar and pestle. If this change gets implemented, I will lose 22 slots of storage space. I carry all three of these, not because I am always practicing all threee skills, but so I can practice these skills as I come across the components. You state that you are opposed to camping and would prefer that people adventure and pick up what items and experience they may. Fewer storage slots are not in line with this vision. Now if I adventure and come across an item, I can hold it while I figure out what to do with it, even if it takes a long time. With reduced storage space, if I come across an item for a quest, I must decide either to destroy the item and not do the quest, or go do the rest of the quest RIGHT NOW.

Think about why people are using combinable containers for storage. I think the main reason is that we must have these containers for trade skills and certain quests, but we are not always working on those trade skills or quests. When not actively working on them, what are we supposed to do with the container that is taking up the valuable bank or inventory slot?

Please reconsider what I believe to be a decision not well thought out. But if you do decide to do this, I would add one suggestion: allow an empty combinable container to be placed inside of another container. If this were done, I could switch between trade skills "on the fly" without worry about losing items if I log out or go link dead.
