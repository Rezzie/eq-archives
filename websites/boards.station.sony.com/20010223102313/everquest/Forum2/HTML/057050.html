I'd like to boil this all down as a sort of recap. Monks are jealous primarily of warriors, because since Kunark's release, Warriors have had an increase in offensive capability that monks haven't gotten. This infringes upon the monk being the master of melee. Problem in a nutshell is that because of a monk's innate lower AC due to armor restrictions, lack of hitpoints due to class restriction, and similar damage output to warriors due to new low damage/delay weapons available to all classes and higher offense caps, monks have lost the clear edge they had in melee. Quite frankly, post-50, Verant has given monks very little reason to keep playing monks instead of warriors. Feign death and mend are good class-defining features, but so are hide-and-sneak and backstab of the rogue, and in a high-level group, backstab can help get the kill, and feign death will keep the monk alive. As a group, I'd rather have the higher damage output of a good rogue over the warrior-like damage output of a good monk.

So what to do about it? Monks would love to have any changes made to fall in line with their characters - more offensive output. How does one balance monks offensively keep the game balanced as a whole? It's hard. But this is a mistake that Verant has fallen into themselves, and they need to make hard decisions. Obviously skill caps cannot be raised, due to coding restrictions. It would seem that this leaves two things left with which to increase damage output of monks: reducing innate weapon delay even further and/or increasing damage bonuses of monks. If Verant is worried about any across-the-board damage bonuses being imbalancing, perhaps damage bonuses against level 55+ mobs would be in order. The main concern is the desirability of the post-50 monk in a group. Perhaps a damage bonus in those critical situations would be in order. Just a thought.

But please don't ignore the imbalance of the monk as a pure melee class. We implore you to balance us to bring us up to par with what was the original Vision of how a monk is supposed to be.

"What we really need is the ability to do a larger scope of damage with our flying kick. To leave the minimum as is would not be a major problem if with 170 STR i could deal damage for 400+ on occasions. This would see us once again fiting the role of damage dealer and our usefulness to any group. "

Actually, this is not the only thing that needs to be done to balance monks.

The problem should not be viewed as "Monks do not do enough damage", although that is true; it should be viewed as "Warriors are doing too much damage."

If you view it this way, you see what the problem is -- with new equipment coming into the game with each new expansion, weaponry has to be upgraded over the old weapons in order to make the expansion attractive, etc. The unfortunate effect of this for Monks, however, is that Warriors are now at the point where equipping a weapon causes them to do more damage than a monk (combined with nearly identical skill caps).

What I'm saying is that if more weapons and expansions are to be added, Verant has to keep an eye on the top tier Warrior (and hybrid/rogue weapons, too) to ensure that there are no weapons combos that will outdamage a Monk with certain equipment. I prefer to use Celestial Fists as the measuring stick. If weapons are to be introduced to the other melee classes, they need to be balanced against the Monk measuring stick (such as the CFs) to ensure that the performance does not exceed the damage output of the CFs from the *front* of the mob. If these types of weapon upgrades are to be put in the game, CFs should be scaled accordingly.

I choose CFs for a few reasons:

a. CFs should be the measuring stick rather than fists because it's unreasonable to hand every 60 monk the best weapons in the game (if fists were to be the balancing factor in other melee weapons).

b. CFs are the 'defining' weapon for monks; some people say a CF/SoS combo or a Tranq Staff will outdamage it, but these weapons don't define the monk -- the CFs do.

The reason all of this needs to be done on a regular basis is simple; All the melee classes are only as effective offensively as their weapons. This worked out extremely well in the Old World, where the best non-planar weapon was an 8/24 slasher with a proc. Monk fists at the time were 14/36 (14/30 after the "haste" ability given to fists), which was pretty darn uber. Combine that with higher skill caps than Warriors, and monks were the damage dealers of the game (along with rogues, once they were fixed ).

Then, with RoK, new weapons with incredible ratios or incredible speeds were introduced. The low-delay, fast weapons became the weapons of choice due to the damage bonus. Warrior skill caps were raised to be nearly identical to Monks, while Monk fist damage received no damage upgrade, only a small upgrade to delay (14/26). Jade maces now outdo monk fist damage. A Warrior dual wielding these maces comes very close to Monk damage output. Rogues shoot into outer space with their damage output, especially once Ragebringer comes along.

Enter Epic Quests. A Warrior dual wielding his Epic blades will do more damage than a Monk wielding his Epic fists. Not a lot more, but enough to cause the Monk to lose his role in EverQuest.

Enter SoV. Amazing items like the Willsapper and exquisite velium stuff futher increase Warrior damage output over the Monk epic fists. Monks have to find even stronger weapons just to keep pace.

I know people will say "Monks get weapons too", but that's part of the problem -- the Monk is supposed to be a hand-to-hand damage dealer. I love the weapons that are in the game; they offer variety, increased damage at lower levels, they look good, and I'm glad they are in the game. However, in the 'end-game' a Monks fists should be the best weapon they can use when combined with their epic weapon (which, incidentally, is a pair of gloves, not a weapon, though for technical reasons it acts like a weapon). When a monk reaches his full potential he should be fighting without a weapon and being a primary damage dealer in the game, second only to the Rogue.

Anyway, that's my thoughts on it -- balancing Monks needs to be done each time a new weapon is added to the game or else Monks will continue to lose their role as new items enter the game.

MONK VISION BY A MONK

Alan once posted in response to a request, or idea, posted by an EQ player, presumably a Monk player, that he “…would have to check to see what the Vision had in mind for monks”. That statement shook the foundry of the monk community. It also raised the question as to what the “Vision” really is for the monk class. As this talk concerns the balancing of this class, perhaps the best way to balance the Monk is the define exactly what the class is and what it does.

As stated above, the Monk is a melee hybrid that SHOULD fall evenly between the Warrior and the Rogue.

Damage Output: Defense Abilities: Armor Class/Hit Points:

Rogue Warrior Warrior
Monk Monk Monk
Warrior Rogue Rogue

The Monk class doesn’t have enough damage dealing potential as the Rogue but gains higher AC, Hit Points, Defensive Abilities, and Feign Death as means to temper this loss. The Monk class also doesn’t have the AC, Hit Points, or Defensive Abilities of the pure Warrior but gains pure damage dealing potential, versatility, and Feign Death as a means to temper this loss.

Outside of pulling, Monks are damage dealers. Many people consider us, and Rogues also, the melee wizards. Though considering the Rogue’s pure abilities, perhaps we’re more of the melee magicians. Monks use a combination of weapons and fists to quickly take small, but noticeable, pieces out of our enemies unlike warriors who slowly take large chunks out of their foes. These small pieces are augmented by large damaging, potentially at least, special attacks. During the course of a battle, the monk will easily surpass, or should at least, the warrior in the group. And should he/she aggro the foe or come to a point where he/she cannot deal with an encounter solo, the use of Feign Death as a ruse is used to escape harm though this is a last ditch tactic that many just assume not have to use.

OFF HAND DAMAGE BONUS: Hand To Hand vs. One Hand Blunt

This suggestion has been brought up before but I think it needs to be explained a bit further. In an attempt to bring the damage dealing capabilities of a monk up to a more reasonable level, several monk players, myself included, have suggested adding an Off Hand Damage Bonus equal to what we have on our main hand now. This suggestion would indeed increase our main ability as a class. But the problem is that the primary goal behind this was to bring the Hand to Hand skill and damage on par with that of One Hand Blunt.

With the release of Kunark came a wide variety of weapons for Monks to choose from. It was stated by Gordon Wrinn (spelling) that the purpose behind this was to 1) give the monk a choice between his/her 3 primary offensive choices and 2) to increase the overall damage of a monk when compared to warriors and rogues with the new Kunark weapons. The problem that came of this was that monks no longer used Hand to Hand because their ability to deal damage with fists was not as adequate or sufficient as when a monk used weapons.

Example 1:
Dual Fighting Batons (attainable at level 30)
vs. Level 30 Fists

8/20
vs. 10/34 Human or 10/35 Iksar

Example 2:
Dual Wu’s Trance Sticks (attainable at level 40)vs. Level 40 Fists
7/18
vs. 12/32 human or 12/33 Iksar

As you can see, there is no possible way that a monk, Human or Iksar, using his/her fists can match the damage as the same monk with weapons. On top of that, with the introduction of faster and more damaging Warrior and Rogue weapons has decreased the margin of the total amount of damage that a monk can dish out when compared to warriors and rogues.

A suggested way of increasing our total damage out put was to introduce an equal damage bonus to the off hand that already exists on the primary hand. But there was a strong out cry from many in the Monk Community that by doing so, it still doesn’t address the problem for damage output for the players that use Hand to Hand only whether it be for role playing purposes or any other reason. To solve this, I suggest the following solutions:

Solution 1) Place an equal Damage Bonus on the off hand that already exists for the main hand for weapons.

Solution 2) Increase the damage bonus on the primary hand for Hand to Hand ONLY. Than, apply an equal damage bonus to the off hand that already exists on the primary hand.

By doing this you not only increase the overall damage that a monk can produce and thereby bring the monk closer to where the class should be, but you also increase FIST damage thus creating a balance between Weapons and Fists and making the use of the Hand to Hand skill versus One Hand Blunt skill just a choice and not one of necessity.

But, in doing these changes, it would of course call for changing several weapons readily available to the Monk class. Some weapons would have to be changed to Primary Hand only. These weapons include:

Wu’s Trance Stick
Fighting Baton
Jade Mace

Some other weapons would have to be deleted entirely from Monk useable. These include:

Moss Covered Twig
Efreeti Battle Standard

DISCIPLINES

With the release of “Ruins of Kunark” came a bonus for the pure melee classes once they passed level 50. These bonuses came in the form of Disciplines which were meant to enhance the power of the pure melee classes in relation to the caster classes and their additional high level spells. Unfortunately for the monk class, several of these Disciplines are unused because they fail to significantly alter our performances.

Level 52: Thunder Kick
Duration: Instant Recycle Time: 7 minutes

>The next successful Flying Kick will do in increased amount of damage.<

This discipline is not as effective in the eyes of the Monk Community as it could be. Granted, with the possible implementation of a Minimum Damage on Flying Kick, the chance of “landing a Thunderous Kick for (6)” will be eliminated. But even then, this is still not what it could be. There are two possible solutions to improving this discipline.

Solution 1) Delete the discipline altogether and add the Thunder Kick as an ability once the Monk achieves Level 51. This would work much like a Warrior’s Critical Hit when using the Flying Kick special attack. Of course the chance of landing a Thunderous Kick would have to be increased due to the long recycle time, but not increased to a point where it would become over powering. If this is done, we suggest that you either move the level 51 discipline, Stone Stance, to level 52 OR just leave it as is and not fill the discipline for that level.

Solution 2) Give the discipline a duration so as to allow the monk using this ability to land several kicks. Of course if this is done, you would have to increase the recycle time to possibly 10 or 12 minutes in order to maintain balance.

Level 59: Ashen Hand
Duration: Instant Recycle Time: 90 minutes

>The next successful Eagle Strike will do an increased amount of damage. Also, the target can be killed outright under certain circumstances.<

This discipline is one that is very rarely used by the Monk class. The main reason is that it is nothing more than a novelty. The Instant-Kill effect only works on MOBs level 32 or below and it rarely ever occurs. Also, the amount of damage that can be attained through the use of Ashen Hand over a 90 minute time period is nothing compared to the amount of damage that can be attained via the use of Innerflame which lasts for 12 seconds and can be recycled and used three consecutive times during the down time of one use of Ashen Hand. Another problem that exists with this discipline is it requires the retraining of a lesser special attack for use and many monks are unwilling to decrease the amount of damage they are already doing using Flying Kick just to increase their skill in Eagle Strike for use in this discipline. The main reason that you included multiple special attacks is because each attack is superior to the last. So, as one levels, he/she increases the damage dealt through use of more highly skilled and more technical maneuvers and no longer continues training in past techniques as they are no longer up to par.

Through the implementation of the monk epic quest reward, the Celestial Fists, Verant Interactive has successfully encouraged monks to go back to the use of the Hand to Hand skill. They have further done so by increasing the skill cap to the same level as One Hand Blunt. They can continue to do so by changing this discipline to meet the basic need and nature of the monk by making a revision in this discipline.

Level 59: Ashen Hand (revised)
Duration: 9 seconds Recycle Time: 45 minutes

>During the time of this discipline, all damage done through the use of the Hand to Hand skill, and this skill only, will be increased. Also, there is a chance that the target can be killed out right under certain circumstances.<

The chance that the target could be killed out right would be the same as the original discipline. Though under this format, a killing blow would “proc” in the form of a 100% unblockable Eagle Strike. The strike would still do an increased amount of damage as before should the user be unable to out right kill the target. But in this revised form, the strike would do less damage than originally planned. There have been reports of these strikes, while using the original attack, exceeding 400 points of damage. I would suggest this be toned down slightly should this change take place. This overall change would not only make this discipline more useful to the monk community but would also reinforce the desirability of the Hand to Hand skill AND would no longer require the training in any of the lesser special attacks.

The amount of damage increased for the Hand to Hand skill can be handled in two separate manners:

1) When using this discipline, the base damage code of the weapon being used (regular fists or Celestial Fists) would be increased by 6. This would change the level 59 fists to 20/28, level 60 fists to 20/26, and the Celestial Fists to 15/16 thus making our hands, for the duration of this discipline, truly deadly and thus capable of instant-killing a target.

2) Multiply all damage dealt by 2 or 3.

It is also recommended that the level of creatures that can be Instant-Killed be raised to all MOBs level 49 and below. This is recommended for the Rogue’s Assassinate and the Wizard’s Instant-Kill spell also. The reasoning behind this is there are no MOBs of epic or raid potential at those levels. Also, as a player progresses in level, the percent of MOBs he/she will encounter that are 49 and below slowly decreases. And by the time a player obtains level 59, the off chance he/she will be facing such mobs if very small in my experience of play.

Level 60: Silent Fist
Duration: Instant Recycle Time: 7 minutes

>The next successful Dragon Punch will do a considerable amount of damage. It will also have a chance to stun the target.<

As with Ashen Hand, this discipline requires the retraining of a lesser special attack in order to achieve the greatest amount of damage that can be obtained through the use of this ability. Again, the main reason that you included multiple special attacks is because each attack is superior to the last. So, as one levels, he/she increases the damage dealt through use of more highly skilled and more technical maneuvers and no longer continues training in past techniques as they are no longer up to par.

Also, the ability to stun an opponent isn’t really something a monk needs. Large race Warriors, Shadow Knights, Shamans, and Rogues get the natural ability to Slam which stuns/interrupts. All other Warrior races, Paladins, and Shadow Knights get the Shield Bash skill at a low level stuns/interrupts. There are several caster classes which getting stun spells such as the Cleric, Wizard, Paladin, and Enchanter and the Magician “magic blades” line of spells interrupts as well. All of these characters can stun and do so long before level 60. Warriors get the Shield Bash skill at level 6 and the Stunning Kick at level 55 because that is part of being a warrior. They are the “meat shields” and are designed to take the punishment that other classes cannot take. That is why they get the high AC, Hit Points, and Defensive skills along with a naturally innate high magic resist so they can take the damage from casters as well as melee MOBs. Their magic resist and ability to stun via shield bash/kick is their way of helping to protect the group they are in. A monk does not need to be able to stun.

Another reason why the stun is not part of the monk line of thinking is because at level 60, the high encounter MOBs in which the monk will be facing cannot be “stunned” and would just resist that portion of the discipline. Also, a monk has several weapons at his/her disposal which do stun/interrupt such as the Tranquil Staff. Though these weapons are rare, so is the likelihood of a character reaching 60.

Finally, this discipline is not really desired by monks as we have the ability to do much more damage with a Flying Kick than we would do with Dragon Punch. And as recycle time of the special plays no part in this, there is really no desire to use this discipline over Thunderous Kick as even the STUN will be of no help in the higher level encounters.

Below is a proposed revision for this discipline. As a pure melee class and one which is capable of a great deal of damage, it seems reasonable to change this discipline to reflect this. Therefore, I propose the following change:

Level 60: Silent Fist (revised)
Duration: 14 seconds Recycle Time: 30 minutes

>During the time this discipline is active, all successful Hand to Hand, Dual Wield, Double Attack, and Special Attack skill checks will land and land for 75% to 100% of the maximum amount of damage possible. These attacks cannot be dodged, blocked, parried, or riposted.<

It could be argued that part of the “…will land for 75% to 100% of the maximum amount of damage possible” portion of this discipline could be considered to be over powering. But in defense of this, the monks main role is that of a damage dealer. Keep in mind that this increase is for Hand to Hand and Special Attacks ONLY. And with this discipline, one that is not unlike that of the Rogue’s Kinesthetics or the Warrior’s Charge disciplines. The only difference is this Monk version is gained at a much later level. And due to this, a small addition is made to substantiate the downfall of gaining this ability at so late in the game.

THE ABILITY TO INTERRUPT CASTERS

Many Monks have been calling for the ability to STUN. This is due to the need for us to interrupt caster MOBs when there is no class present that can. But the sheer imbalance that would be created through giving us STUN would be too much. What Monks want, and need, is the ability to interrupt. Though a monk IS NOT a warrior and cannot do what his heavier brethren can, we are called to fill the role of the tank many times when a Warrior is not available. And as we do not have the hit points or the magic resistance that warriors have, we have a tendency to take A LOT of damage from caster MOBs. Plus, we cannot prevent others in our groups from suffering from caster MOBs either.

A simple solution to this problem is to add the ability to “push back” the casting MOB with our special attacks. It would be similar in nature to the “force modules” on the Sol A Clockwork Guards and the effect that is on the “Force Shock” spell. This allows us to interrupt while not overpowering us with the ability to STUN.

PLAIN OF SKY REWARDS

Please look at changing the stats on these quest items. When Everquest originally came out, these items were to be the pinnacle of gear that a player could obtain. When “The Ruins of Kunark” was released, these items lost a bit of an edge due to the increased availability of almost equal gear. Now, with the release of “The Scars of Vellious”, these items are all but useless when compared to gear already existing in game. This is not only a request for the quest items available for monks, the Golden Sash of Tranquility excluded as it is a GREAT piece of gear, but for gear available for every class.
