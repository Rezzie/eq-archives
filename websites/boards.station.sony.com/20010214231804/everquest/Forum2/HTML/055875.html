Greetings yet again. After spending two hours today reading up on all the new posts in the paladin thread, I thought I would come here and champion the best ideas I saw. Think of this as a revised list for your convenience, Absor.
(I'll make it fast, since I have 10 minutes to get to class....) We're really not asking for much. All we want is the ability to do our part in a 50+ group. Having one emergency heal every 72 minutes is currently not cutting it...

1) Innate Critials and Crippling Blows on Undead
The more I think about this, the more this makes sense. I mean, come on. Duh. Also, this would fit nicely with #2...

2) Holy Forge
Change Holy Forge so that it is a guaranteed crit/cripple upon a successful hit. Fits well with the image of a paladin. Also sort of relates to...

3) Lay Hands alteration
Every RPG I have ever played has treated healing spells as DD spells when they are cast upon undead. Chnage Lay Hands so that it can only be used upon PCs (you have NO idea how frustrating it is to slip with the mouse, click the wrong button, and heal a monster!), and undead NPCs. When cast upon undead, it would act as an equivalently powered DD spell. It makes sense to me that a holy paladin, sending a massive charge of holy energy through his hands, would really put a hurting on the undead. At least consider this, please...

4) Upgraded Ghoulbane
Create a quest in which the Ghoulbane could be upgraded to a better weapon, a "Spectrebane" or a "Lichbane," with stats along the lines of 25/35 + stats, with the proc upgraded to at least Expel Undead.

5) Mark of Karn style spell
Give paladins a reversed Mark of Karn style spell, allowing it to turn a small percentage (10-25%) of the DMG inflicted onto the paladin into healing for the paladin.
A monster hits YOU for 400 DMG!
YOU feel a holy surge.
YOU get back 100 HP!

6) Upgrade the Group Heal spell
I couldn't remember the spell name, and EQ Caster's Realm was having difficulties when I tried, but 200 HP is really not much. Upgrade it to maybe 400 or so for the same mana cost and recast time.

7) Rez Spells
Move Revive (no exp rez) from 49 to 39. At 39, many people would choose getting back to the group without an EXP rez than taking 45 minutes to find a willing cleric for a 50% exp rez.

Add Resuscitate at 49. At this point, clerics can still be annoyingly rare to find when people die, as they are not *absolutely* necessary in a good group. Many people would really prefer a 50% rez to nothing. I have acted as a cleric quite easily in a group with an enchanter.

Keep Ressurection at 59.

8) Group Resist Buffs
It's just annoyingly cumbersome to cast four buffs on each person in the group, one at a time. Allow paladins to cast a group resist buff, for the properly upscaled mana cost and a doubled casting time or something similar. This would be particularly nice to have when some nasty critter AE dispells people...

9) "Sacrifice" discipline/skill
At level 55 or so, allow paladins, when this discipline/skill is activated, to kill themselves and Complete Heal the group. Exp would be lost as if it was an evironmental or monster-inflicted death. This would still CH the group even if the paladin was down to 5 HP when he activated it. Obviously, even with the 96% cleric rez, a death at that level is a nice setback. The exp loss would certainly keep people from using this very often...And yes, the paladin should be rez-able if he uses this.

10) Bash Upgrades
a) Critical bashes. Makes sense.
b) Double-Attack Bash. I like it.
c) Upgrade Bash Damage. I've always wondered why a boot to the shins can cause more damage than a shield to the head.
d) Bash Procs. Been said a lot. Can't be said enough. This would be GREAT.
e) Give paladins the innate ability at 55, when things become unstunnable, to still stun casting at least with a Bash.

11) New Stun Spell
Give paladins at 55 a new stun spell with a high mana cost that CAN INTERRUPT CASTING and can also ACTUALLY STUN level 55+ monsters! The stun series was our distinction until this point. Lose it and we've lost our effectiveness....

12) Disciplines
We want the Parry Discipline. We want the Riposte Discipline. We just want SOMETHING to give us reasons to show up to Velious raids, other than picking up the nodrop paladin-only scraps that drop.

Okay well now I'm about 15 minutes late for class, but I hope this helps, Absor. Like I said, we're not asking for much, we just want to be useful.

Here are some of my ideas on the subject of Paladins:

I agree with the raising of the defense cap but not the associated skills like dodge and parry, thus making us ALMOST as good defensively to warriors, but inferior offensively and overall in melee as it should be.

I am also reasonably happy with the spell situation with the exception of the overall low performance of the proposed 59 healing spell for the level it requires to be able to cast it. We should not be able to replace clerics healing power nor come close to it, but a Paladin of 59 years experience should gain more effective healing ability at that level. I also believe that superior healing should be reduced several levels for Paladins as the closer to 60 we get the more our role becomes healing support (clerical) and less so that of the melee (warrior)half of our class, but this slide begins far earlier in the post 50 levels than 57.

Being a defensive oriented class, you will find no other class that equips a shield as much and as often as the Paladin. Shadowknights do as well, but more of them tend to lean towards 2H overall, and certainly warriors and rogues are seen using shields far less once they dual wield. So why do paladins bother at all with 2H weapons? Almost exclusively because several of our quest holy swords including the FA and FD are 2H and because of the increased damage output. However, again paladins are not the masters of damage dealing, so the 2H choice is situational for when we are a damage dealer, and the 1H is the norm for us in our more typical defensive role.

That being said our choices in these 1H weapons and shields is still fairly limited, and its easy to spot a paladin at most levels from his 1H weapon and shield choice. There were some excellent ideas posted prior about addressing this including implimenting quest 1H swords and shields, having a "turn-in" quest for the FA, FD, Soulfire for equivalent 1H swords.

Another newer area that has been somewhat overlooked is the 2H Piercing. Have a look around any server and see how many Paladins are actively using piercing at this time. The answer is almost none unless they are training it at the time for possible future use. Why is that? Here are some of my thoughts: 2H Piercers known at this time tend to be high delay high damage; this coupled with the fact that this is a brand new skill implimented with velius, there are a great many paladins who would have to start at very low skill and build it over a long time to make it viable. The high damage is the only reason to use it, and most paladins of such level already have this ability in the form of a good 2H slasher, so why train a long and painstaking new skill just to make it almost equal to the damage you already can produce with another weapon. The second major drawback is the lack of a shield. Lances are thought of much as a knight jousting is, but remember a knight jousts with a shield always for protection. Having a shield with the lance is not so outragous a thought, as the knights did it, many many shaman in the game have the benefit of not only having a long spear in one hand and a shield in the other, but even having another spear in their range slot. I realize there would be some issues associated with the possiblity of changing "2H piercing" to become 1H and shield usable instead. The animation is set for 2 hands active, but can easily be changed to the normal piercing animation instead. If the high damage/high delay existing weapons are considered too powerful for 1H which I do not think they are, then they can be rebalanced with higher delay which would make sense for a lance type weapon. The simple fact is, I do not see any way that "2H piercing" will become more used as it exists now. Adding shield usablity to it would give it immediate desireablity, and add variety to the classes viable weapon choices.

Some misc. issues I've not seen mentioned elsewhere:

The paladin trueheart shield from the Plane of Hate off a level 54ish mob looks exactly like a newbie kite shield that costs 2pp in any city. Thats just plain wrong, how about giving it an appearance a little more in line with its difficulty, or even taking the opportunity to upgrade it to more of a unique paladin look like on the box cover as was suggested by one poster earlier which I highly support. Also the Theologan Claymore from the same plane looks exactly like a sawed off blackened iron bastard sword which looks exactly like a rusty short sword, something I would like to see improved in its appearance.

Armor of Ro quests: Quests that I did long ago but had some objections to given the genre. Again this does not make sense for a Paladin quest to me.

Finally, I would like to suggest a quest item for the range slot for both Paladins and our evil mirror image cousins the Shadowknights. The rangers are masters of the bow, and neither Paladins or Shadowknights are competent with its use and in my opinion should never be. However the rangers Raincaller bow makes outstanding use of that slot both as a functunal bow and as an excellent statistic item. A "Symbol of Faith to Karana" (Marr, Brell, Tunare, Prexus, etc) could result with slightly varying statistics or resists depending on diety, but whos primary statistic is is armor class. Based on the many posts here and on the EQ message boards about how to balance Paladins, it has become clear to me that we need Defense as comparable to Warrior Defense as Ranger Offense is comparable to Warrior Offense. Even if our Defense is raised significantly, however, we won't be able to accomplish this goal because of the extreme difference in our armor vs warrior armor (much greater than the difference between warrior weapons and ranger's, the "offensive hybrid class", weapons, btw). That's where my new idea for an Epic Mark 2 comes in:

I propose a new quest for Paladins that, if they choose to do so, allows them to trade in Fiery Defender for a more defense-oriented combination of Shield and Sword, as follows:

Holy Shield, AC 50, 15 STR, 10 STA, 10 WIS, 50 HP, 30 MANA, 10 vs All, effect: Swift Strike (same as Ranger's offhand, adds 40% haste and 30 atk). Nice unique shield graphic.

Fiery Protector, 12/20 1HS, AC 10, 5 STR, 5 WIS, 20 HP, proc: Holy Shock. The delay is low so we have an increased chance of taunting, so we will be more likely to make good use of our shield. Fiery Avenger graphic with Fiery Defender hilt.

The combined stats of these two are the same as Fiery Defender, with 30 ATK, and 45 more AC, plus haste.

EMERGENCY HEALING

Background
Everquest Paladins are often defined by their emergency healing capabilities. Before level 50, paladins have lay-on-hands (LOH) as an emergency healing ability. Post 50 paladins gain divine favour and wave of healing at level 55 to complement their emergency healing capabilities. LOH has been recently enhanced twice but continues to have a 72-minute recast time. Wave of healing heals 200 points of damage for the high mana cost of 450 and a recast time of 60 seconds.

Problem
While divine favour is a limited use but adequate spell, LOH and wave of healing remain flawed in their current implementation. Furthermore, our emergency healing capability is limited to these three spells.

Solution

1. Decrease the recast time on wave of healing to 20 seconds. Given the extremely high mana cost for this spell, the most that a paladin could cast this spell, on full mana, is 3-4 times. The recast time of 60 seconds severely limits the use of this spell. Even if the recast time was 1 second, I would run out of mana after casting this spell a very limited number of times thus limiting its use to a true emergency. Please decrease the recast time on wave of healing to 20 seconds.

2. LOH recast time. The problem is that the cycle time on LOH is currently 72 minutes and this does not change despite your level. While LOH should never be part of the group healing plan, it should be a more useful discipline/ability. I would suggest that paladins should have the cycle time reduced as they gain levels using the following formula: the maximum of (72 - level of paladin) or 30. This provides a very gradual scaling in terms of the effectiveness of LOH so that LOH becomes a more powerful as the paladin gains levels with a cap of 30 minutes on the use of LOH.

3. A new spell/discipline. We need a new spell/discipline that allows us to redirect damage at one party member to ourselves. Being the noble knights we are, we should be able to sacrifice ourselves to save another. There is potential for misuse so I would suggest that this is a discipline and not a spell, and that our ability to sacrifice increases with levels. The ability is gained at 50th level with 50% damage transferal and 2% for each level thereafter. This should have a high taunt factor associated with it.

I'll keep it simple: Two variations of an idea for disciplines that could be balanced as needed.

Key Point: They promote the role of a paladin as someone who takes damage for another in an emergency.

Level 55 or 60 discipline where you can target another player, and for the next X minutes, if a blow to that player would cause that players HP's to go below zero, the damage is done to the paladin instead. Reuse time of Y. X and Y balanced as needed.

Alternatively

Level 55 or 60 discipline where you can target another player, and for the next X minutes, if a blow to that player would cause that players HP's to go below zero, HP's are immediately drawn out of the paladin to heal that player back to full.
