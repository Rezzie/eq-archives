I would just like to clarify few things:

1) Bards are NOT hybrids. They paid the hybrid penalty for a long time, but not even other hybrids consider bards hybrids. Hybrids all have taunt and the ability to inflict damage with both hands (dual wield or two handed weapons) twice (double attack). Bards are a "plate" class like clerics, with a decent armor class. They have cruddy offensive and defensive skills.

2) For balancing purposes, bards should not be treated like weak enchanters and lousy warriors. Since our songs are such short duration, the death of the bard immediately changes the buffs on the party. Only a clerics death has a bigger immediate impact.

3) Bards do NOT cast spells. For game mechanics, there may be some coding that makes songs act like spells, but bards sing, not cast. Songs and spells should not have stacking issues (in most cases they do not), and haste should stack.

4) For the 50+ game, bards should have something to add to even a balanced group. One of the ways to do this is to give songs a longer duration as the bards reach higher levels, such as allowing four song twists at 40th and five song twists at 50th.

5) Bards should probably not have a mana bar. Denon's Desperate Dirge should be on a timer, and the 39th charm should be comnverted to a mez song without level limits (except for mobs flagged as unmezzable, or maybe only mobs of lower level than the bard). This would be totally in line with singing/not casting bard concept.

Not going to repost on the main issues, since they have already been well-covered, repeatedly. However, a few new ideas have been popping up in the threads, so I wanted to add my own thoughts on those.

1) Barbarian bards -- Well, this isn't really a balance issue. Also, the starting CHA would be insanely low. However... it does seem kind of neat. I'd probably start a 2nd bard just to try it. Doubt it'll happen, though, since then it opens a whole big 'why not THIS class with THAT race?' can of worms.

2) Manadrain songs -- These have been covered somewhat before. However, there was a post on the 1st page of this section of the bard talk which had a _wonderful_ suggestion. Specifically, it suggested that our manadrains should be the most powerful in the game & should serve as a way for us to recover our own mana.

Having our manadrains be more powerful than enchanters would serve a positive purpose for balance. Firstly, this would give bards a niche in the post-52 raid, when resistances are (from what I've heard) less important than on the 45-52 raid. Secondly, this would give bards a role to play in groups with enchanters. Thirdly, because enchanters would still have their own manadrain spells (as well as the MANY other things they contribute), it would NOT take anything away from another class.

Using stolen mana to replenish our own would also work well for balance. Because it would (obviously) agro the mob being drained, it would not allow bards to run around casting Denon's Dissension willy-nilly & then grabbing mana back from mobs for free. Draining & battling enough mobs to replenish the bar would still take a fair amount of time, & would entail the risks involved in any fight. Obviously, PC's (on regular servers) & charmed mobs will be immune to this song, as they are to any offensive song/spell. Furthermore, this would fit from an rp perspective -- we don't 'med' for mana like the true casters do, but 'steal' it, in keeping with our roguish nature.

I hope that the idea of bardic manataps will be considered for the balance discussions.

Although I agree with many of the requests here, I think the biggest bard issue is that bards have no defined role or at least a limited role at best. A couple ideas I've had for a role would be:

1. The masters of mana (since we have no need for it)
Perhaps add a song to give all our current mana to a target (and empty our bar), more efficient mana drain songs, and perhaps a drain mana from the enemy into the group. If this was to be the case, I could see ALL our damage songs being converted to mana-drain instead. This would give bards a powerful position for grouping and would remove most of their soloing power.

2. The masters of stacking (since these are songs, not spells)
Move our damage shields to a lower level so we can stack/use them when we are in the melee action. Starting at 27 or so (or when both chanters and shaman have haste) give us haste songs that stack and have a large range so we can stay back a little. Make our chants do no damage but the debuff stacks and each pulse lowers the resist more(ie: 5...10...15...20...ect). Of course, make all these stack with existing spells
3. Jack of all trades (masters of none)
What we are supposed to be the masters at now. This could be improved by allowing a switch that would make our 'buff' songs hit all pc's in range instead of just our group. This will allow us to have a much greater effect in any multi-group event. We would need an agro-reduction for this though
4. All of the above
What can I say? This would completely re-define the class and would really shake things up. Could be a bit much though, but hey, I can dream

As a final, unrelated thought, another possible song would be a targeted song on a groupmate that would taunt the enemies around them towards that groupmate. Would address the tanks taunt problems at higher levels and also help out bards. This would be a level 50 or above song though, imho. Thanks for listening in and I hope you can pick a few good things out of my ramblings.
