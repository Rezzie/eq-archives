Low-level paladin offense

Based on my experiences, a paladin, a warrior, and a monk, all of the same level (in the level range of 20-30) will hit a white-con mob a different percentage of the time. Perhaps this is due to stats, although I doubt it. More likely, each class has a separate "To Hit" table, or perhaps warriors and monks receive a bonus "To Hit." Regardless, the end effect is the same.
Again, based on my experience, a warrior at 17th level, with good equipment (not necessarily twinked, traders usually have excellent equipment) can out-damage a 21st level paladin who has only slightly less good equipment. A cleric healing both will have to watch the warrior’s hp more closely, but the warrior will do MORE damage, and hit MORE often, than the paladin. Even with the skill number differences, this holds true.
Based on further experience, a monk of the same level as a paladin can out-damage said paladin. The monk will hit MORE often, and do MORE damage than the paladin. Again, a cleric healing both will have to watch the monk's hp more closely, but only a little more closely, due to the monk’s impressive damage avoidance skills.
In both cases, the amount of damage the warriors and monks do is not so much the issue as the fact that they seem to have a much higher chance to hit than a paladin does. In the case of the warrior, this is true even with a lower offense skill, and a lower attack value.
In addition, paladins are disadvantaged in offense in several other ways. In groups lower than 45th level, and even as low as 20th level, in order to do better damage a paladin uses a two handed weapon with a higher damage value. This also makes it possible to cast between swings, a nice improvement over the old system. However, doing this requires a paladin to give up his only alternate attack (Bash), while a warrior will still be able to use his alternate attack (Kick). A paladin using a mithril two hander will attack four times (two swings with Double Attack going off) in the time that a warrior gets five attacks (two swings with DA, and Kick or slam). Add to that the fact that the warrior innately hits more often, and you begin to see the problem.
A paladin might use 1h slash and a shield in order to get higher armor class, faster and more attacks, and to be able to stun/interrupt. PURPOSE

The purpose of this post is to address balancing the Paladin class. I propose that the Paladin class is significantly under-balanced as compared to other classes. I will state my argument for how they are under-balanced and propose solutions for fixing the Paladins so that they become balanced and a useful class in Everquest.

SCOPE OF DISCUSSION

I will limit my discussion to Paladin 50+. I believe that paladins are for the most part viable up to level 50. Greater healing at level 39 makes us useful in groups supplementing clerics, druids, and shamans. Our tanking abilities are balanced in relationship to other melee classes (less in every case) and Ghoulsbane provides us with a viable weapon for combating undead. Root allows us to root-park monsters and lay-on-hands can save a party if used appropriately. Our buffing spells are for the most part useless but are, on rare occasions, useful. I will leave it to other commentators to argue otherwise.

ROLE OF THE PALADIN

Within the EQ World, a paladin is a defensive tank. We are a melee class. We have significantly less offensive firepower than other melee classes but compensate for this lack of offense by spell-casting ability and flexibility. We are the masters of fighting undead. We are the best class for interrupting spell casters through our stuns. We can supplement the healing and buffing capability of clerics, druids, and shamans but are inferior to them in this capability. We can root park mobs as necessary to split pulls. We can emergency heal party members through LOH and other post 50 spells to save them.

Given this role, I will now discuss why Paladins 50+ are woefully unbalanced, compared to other classes, to fulfill this role. I suggest that offensively we are working as intended with a minor adjustment needed, defensively we need some adjustments to fulfill the role of defensive tank, and our spell-casting ability post 50 is totally, completely, utterly, unquestionably worthless.

PALADINS AS A DEFENSIVE TANK

Warriors, Rogues, Rangers, Shadow Knights and Monks will dramatically out-damage a paladin. Their offensive skills are higher or in the case of shadow knights, they have offensive skills so that they can out-damage us. Due to the cumulative effects of lower weapon skill, lower offensive skill, lower Double attack skill, lack of Dual Wield, no crippling or critical hits (even though they account for 1-3% additional damage, it adds up) and sub-par weapons, there is a very large difference in offensive output. Verrant needs to reevaluate if this monstrously huge gap in offensive output is working as intended. There is no debate at all that all other melee/hybrid classes should out-damage paladins and that they should significantly out-damage paladins. The focus of the discussion should be on what is a reasonable gap. I would suggest that the gap is currently too large - dual-wielding is the dominant factor in damage output and paladins do not and should not get it. To compensate for the large effect of dual-wielding, I would suggest that paladin weapon and offensive skills be reexamined and the gap closed between paladins and other classes. Raising paladin weapon and offensive skills by 10 would be a step in the right direction while maintaining the clear offensive dominance of every other melee class. Even with this change, our damage output will remain paltry, but the gap will be closer and we will at least be a tank.

Bash as a skill also requires reexamination. Bashes as a skill does minimal damage - what it does do is interrupt spells. Paladins and shadow-knights are the only melee classes that habitually equip a shield. As such, I would suggest that paladins and shadow-knights, the only classes that use a shield, should have their bash skill raised to 252. It makes no sense at all that the classes that uses a shield in combat far more the other classes has a lower skill cap than say a warrior that never uses a shield after they can duel-wield. It makes no sense. This does not increase the offensive capability of paladins significantly (marginally if at all - bash does close to 0 damage post 50). What is does do is change a skill cap to reflect the game. Increase the paladin and shadow-knight bash skill to 252.

Our defensive skills, however, are woefully lacking. We are not a defensive tank because our defensive skills are somewhat low compared to the other melee classes. How in the wild world of gnome-juggling does a rogue (leather armor class) have a greater defense than a paladin (plate class)? I understand he can dodge better but defense? I understand he can parry better but defense? No way - it makes no sense. I would recommend that paladins are the ultimate defense class in the game and they should have the same defense as warriors if not higher given that most of the time they are carrying shields. Raise the defense of paladins to 252. In terms of parrying we are fine - we do not dual-wield and as such should have a lesser ability to parry with our weapon. In terms of dodge we are fine - we don't move so fast in plate armor but I would argue that the gap between warriors and paladins is too large - we should be slightly closer while warriors, the ultimate tanks, retain their superiority. Raise the paladin dodge ability by 10. Think of a human paladin and ogre warrior. Who do you think dodges better? Finally, I would suggest that Paladins should have their taunt ability raised to 200. If you really want to get someone mad, talk about religion or politics. Paladins represent their religion (you cannot be agnostic). To be a defensive tank, we have to have the ability to attract the attention of the monster. If we agree that every other melee class can out-damage paladins and should out-damage paladins, there must be some counter-balancing mechanism for paladins to be defensive tank and absorb some of the monster's fury. I would suggest that taunting, coupled with some timely stunning, rooting and healing, is that mechanism. Increase a paladin's ability to taunt to 200. Remember all those religious zealots in the airport trying to convert you and how mad you get? Imagine one with a big flaming sword in plate armor telling you that your god sucks and you are going to hades while spanking you. Paladins should be able to taunt - increase the skill.

PALADINS SHOULD BE THE MASTER TANKS OF FIGHTING UNDEAD

Paladins hate undead. Undead hate us. We like it that way. Prior to level 50, a paladin with a Ghoulsbane was good at fighting undead. Our undead damage spells did some damage but generally it was better to save the mana for greater heals and Ghoulsbane was an ok weapon prior to getting a Wurmslayer or better weapon. Our ability post 50 is severely lacking for a number of reasons. There are no weapons after Ghoulsbane designed for fighting undead. I would suggest that there should be a paladin only weapon, Lichbane for those paladins that specialize in fighting undead that is an upgrade from Ghoulsbane. Weapon stats would be 16/28 1-handed slash, paladin only, save versus disease +25, effect Expel Undead. This should be a quest no-drop weapon. Turn in your Ghoulsbane and Souldfire to your temple, have warmly faction with the temple, be level 49 or higher, and you receive a Lichbane. Each "good" temple other than your temple also has a related quest associated with fighting undead. These are hard 50+ quests that require saving the surrounding community from some undead threat. For each of these quests that are completed, the delay of the weapon reduces by two. If you complete all the sub-quests (four of them), the process on the weapon is upgraded to Banish Undead. This is an alternate "epic" weapon for paladins that focus on fighting undead. The lowest level Lichbane should be relatively easy to acquire. The highest level Lichbane should be as difficult as a class epic weapon to obtain with faction playing a large role in the quest.

Our Holyforge discipline is almost completely useless and is inappropriate. We should be the masters of fighting undead. After two revisions, Holyforge remains a useless discipline. While warriors get critical hits and crippling blows versus all monsters, we should have critical hits and crippling blows versus undead. Not as a discipline but all the time and at the same time that warriors receive critical hits. We should not be good at fighting undead some of the time - we should be good at fighting undead all of the time. This adds to the flavor of playing a paladin as any warrior will tell you, is not unbalancing at all. Critical hits and crippling blows are eye candy that contribute less than 1-3% to the actual damage output of the character. How can a 1% improvement to a subset of monsters to unbalancing? It is not. Give paladins critical and crippling blows versus undead.

Finally, we should get Improved Invisibility to Undead and Sunskin (group invisibility) at the same time as clerics. As paladins, we live to kill those abominations known as the undead. We should have the ability to bypass the weaker undead and strike at the very heart of the undead strongholds. This is a utility spell. In terms of balance, this is not unbalancing. Any post 50 group will have cleric and especially groups that are focusing on fighting undead. What this does is add some flexibility and flavor to who is casting the spell and it is a role that paladins should be able to fulfill. I am not suggesting that we replace clerics in this role but submit that they have more important things to do (buffing, healing, nuking) and that this is a role where the paladins can supplement clerics in their groups. This makes paladins more useful in groups fighting undead.

STUNNING
Paladins excel at stunning mobs and we have three stuns at our disposal for interrupting casting monsters. From 39-55, this is often THE reason paladins are added to groups as there is no class as good as a paladin at interrupting casting and gating monsters as a paladin. This works quite well pre-55. The problem arises that all monsters post 55 are unstunnable, dragons are unstunnable, and giants are unstunnable. One of our defining characteristics and reasons for having a paladin in the group becomes useless. On the other hand, puny, little creatures like humans should not stun really big creatures like giants and dragons unless we start reciting poetry in the middle of battle.

I propose that stunning should interrupt the spell casting of normal (not named or boss) monsters 55+, and 55- giants and dragons but not actually stun the mob. This retains the principal advantage of stunning (interrupting casters) while accepting the fact that ubber monsters are not going to be stunned or interrupted - they're ubber don't you know? Bashing these mobs should work the same way - it will interrupt their spell casting but will not stun them if we successfully hit. It reinforces one of the principal advantages to having a paladin in the group - the defensive tank that is well suited to combating casting monsters by interrupting them.

HEALING, BUFFING, AND PALADIN ABILITIES

There is no other way to put this but to state the truth - paladin spells are for the most part completely useless. 98% of all groups post 50 have a cleric in the group. Cleric buffs are and should be superior to paladin buffs. Cleric, druid, and shaman healing should be superior to paladin healing. Paladins are a melee class with some spell casting ability. The problem is that our spell-casting ability is almost completely worthless. As it currently stands, a ranger is better at buffing a party than a paladin. This is so wrong and there is no way to fix this without some significant changes to paladin spell-casting. Given this premise, I will now discuss why and recommend solutions for fixing the problem.

HEALING
It is important to define the role of the paladin in healing. Our role is not to be the principal healer in a group. Our role is to supplement the pre and post battle healing capability of the group so as to reduce downtime and enhance group efficiency. Paladins should be able to heal one bubble of health per spell cast on a fully buffed warrior after the battle so the cleric can continue to meditate and heal during battle. Paladin pre-50 are fine as we have greater healing at level 39 and can fulfill this role and from 24-39 we can use center as a heal. Paladins post 50 cannot. Greater heal (300 points of healing) heals about 1/10th of most warriors post 50 and we do not get superior healing until level 57. The problem is a multi-class problem as druids and shamans are equally as useless healing until they get superior heal at 53. This has been discussed in other class discussions and leads to the situation where every group post 50 MUST have a cleric in it to be successful except in the most marginal of cases. I would argue that this is wrong and is so wrong that is it intuitive to the casual observer.

The most often-repeated and best points I've seen in this forum so far (in no particular order):

- Paladins need a Defensive discipline, similar to the one Warriors have. I think we realize that ours won't be as good as Warriors (perhaps it won't refresh as quickly, or will be a *bit* shorter in duration).

- Bash should be a key Paladin/SK skill, as Archery is for Rangers, since we use it more than any other melee class, by far. Everyone seems to feel that we should be at least as good at it as Warriors (much as Rangers excel at Archery along with Warriors).

- The improvements to Defense help a lot, but don't fully make up for the large difference in Armor Class and Hit Points between Warriors and Paladins. This is less of an issue for Rangers Offense vs Warriors, because their weapons are just as good.

- One way to improve our Armor Class is to add better Shields to the game. Currently, the shields available to Paladins aren't much better than those that were available before Ruins of Kunark. Weapons have improved dramatically since then, why haven't shields? I think we're looking for shields with armor class 40 and up, as readily available as the many, many powerful weapons in RoK and SoV.

- Fiery Defender is an adequate Epic Weapon, but is perhaps the worst of all meleer weapons in terms of power, and perhaps the least well suited to it's class' needs. Many people have suggested an optional extension to the Paladin Epic Quest that lets us trade it in for a great Shield and Sword combination (much like the offhand/mainhand sword combination that Rangers have).

- Divine Strength is woefully underpowered for a level 60 spell, especially when compared to the level 60 spell that Rangers got with SoV. Most seem to think it should give more hit points (perhaps 300) AND some Armor Class bonus. The new low-power level 53 version of the spell, Divine Glory, is also underpowered, especially when compared to the level 51 Ranger spell Strength of Nature.

- The new Celestial healing spell is completely useless, and needs to heal at least TWICE as much before any Paladin would use it.

- Paladin spells are generally thought to be very well balanced from levels 1-49, so the changes on Test for spells in this range aren't viewed as being very helpful.

- Yaulp 4 is such an obvious, natural spell for high level Paladins...many of us really feel it should be made available at the highest level.

- The HolyForge discipline is not very helpful, because critical hits in general don't add very much to offense. Many strongly feel that Paladins should simply get critical hits (beginning at level 55) and critical blows (beginning at level 60) all the time, vs all mobs. The logical replacement for this discipline is Defensive.

- an improved version of Divine Might would be greatly appreciated at higher levels, since for many Paladins it's our best spell, addressing the area where we are weakest: melee damage.

- our Offense and Weapon Skills skill caps are quite low, to the point of not being able to hit many high-level mobs with a significant amount of damage. Modest increases in these skills seems appropriate.

- if Ranger weapons are as good as Warrior weapons, why isn't Paladin (and SK) armor as good as Warrior armor? This is one of the main issues confronting Paladins, as it prevents them from equalling a Warrior in defense the way a Ranger can equal a Warrior in offense.

- new versions of the Lay Hands effect need to be made available at higher levels, because this class-defining ability is trivialized by high-level equipment such as Cleric breastplates that cast complete heal and Celestial Elixir. How about LayHands2 at level 51, which heals 3000hp every 60 minutes, LayHands3 at level 55 (4000hp every 45 minutes), and LayHands4 at level 60 (5000hp every 30 minutes)? The same type of change needs to happen to Harm Touch, as well.

- will the laughable graphic of Fiery Defender, our ultimate weapon, ever be fixed?

- many of what are viewed as our very best spells, such as level 60 Resolution and Shield of Words, which look so wonderful on paper, turn out to be useless in-game because we are ALWAYS grouped with clerics, who have far more powerful spells available to them at the same level. We need UNIQUE buffs that stack with Cleric buffs (like Divine Strength), or are self-only (like the Yaulp line).

- there still seems to be a general lack of weapons available to Paladins. This trend started in RoK (where we had 3 class-specific, compared to 10 or more for every other melee class), and continues in SoV (where nobody has found a decent Paladin-only weapon to date).

Thank you for your time. Many patient Paladins (including myself) eagerly await Verant's responses to our concerns.

lvl 55, Diving Rage
