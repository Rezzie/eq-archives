When it comes to LEARNING raid tactics, the criteria you are looking for in zone selection is as follows:

1. Lotta room to operate. Having 12-30 people takes a lot of space and having them cram into the entrance of a zone like City of Mist is just not feasible.

2. Slow mob spawns. If you have to get your tactics down, it's extremely distracting to have half your group constantly fighting wandering mobs or respawns while YOU are trying to instruct them on raid tactics to use in the next fight.

3. Named mobs. Few things pump up a good raid like having a named mob to take down. It would be nice if they had decent loot too, but we're talking about a learning process, not a looting process.

----------

The Vox/Naggy raids utilize levels 46-52 and are the stereotypical raids. Named mob, hard fight, tactics are required, decent amount of room to operate from.

Practicing Raids...
Levels 30-45 - Echo Caverns (High level side). From Shadowhaven, go thru the short folk area all the way to the back. Echo Caverns entrance back there leads to the 'high level' side of the zone.

The corridors are fairly wide, and can accomodate 2 full groups easily. The entrance area is very safe, unless trained, and can easily operate as a staging area. Guards will even jump in as necessary if you pull close to them.

There is a 4-spawn of underbulks on the main corridor that can be single pulled. They are a challenge for a solo level 40-48 character. As a 2-3 group raid of levels 30-40, they can offer a fair challenge without being an overwhelming danger. Farther in, the mobs get a little tougher (appropriate for solo characters level 45-50).

There are even 2 named mobs that can spawn (Trillicor and Fireclaw). Both of these are a *serious* challenge to a solo level 50 character. They cannot be slowed or mezzed. A 2-group raid of 30-40s would find either a very good challenge.

All spawn points are on a 20-minute respawn timer. Plenty of time to work through any tacticl issues and allow for healing, medding, rebuffing, etc.

------ Tatics ------

Pushing a mob into corner is the easiest way for MT to hold agro (he/she doesnt have to constantly move).

General tatic is the MT will back into a corner and agro target (assuming a FD puller brought the target in). When MT has agro in corner, the rest attack from behind to push the mob into the MT.

We've used this tatic on Vindi and Inny (Nadox) effectively. With Nadox also, it stops MT being effected by his knockback proc - holds agro in one corner - much less messy than running around playing agro ping pong!

There are two versions of "cornering" a mob and each is used for different reasons.

Version one is to turn the mobs back to the wall and shove him into the corner. This way he is facing the raid and MA with his back to the corner. This is mostly used simply to keep the mobs from moving out of healer range for a CH cycles and the like. There normally will still be room for your rogues to back stab so no real worries.

Version two is to shrink your MA and stick him in the corner with the MA looking at him and the mobs back to the raid force. This is what you use on mobs such as Vindi and AoW. Both of these mobs enrage and rampage, and if they turn, you are in trouble. Enrage is autoripost and you should turn off autoattack when this happens unless you can stay behind the mob. Rampage is a different hate list then your MA list and means the mob turns for one round of combat and smashes someone besides your MA. Monks are very nice Ramp tanks at the high end because of high defense, block, dodge and perry. They also, with the right gear and buffs, can get extreemly high HPs.

I would really suggest Kael, but be careful of Arena and make sure Vindi and the other named are not up. These would flay you alive at those levels and you would then be expected to vacate arena for the raid that comes to kill them. I will say, that when Vindi is being pulled, you do not want to mess with the puller and get in the way. Yes, it may be annoying to move, but it is safer to do that for all involved and if you handle it well and don't whine and scream and swear, you will make an impression on the other guilds and those rare pickups that happen to get them lol.
