No one but a complete and utter fool would believe that the exp penalties were appropriate in EQ.

1) Traditional - Classes with penalties needed to be more powerful.

Well, my immediate reaction is that the classes that have/had an experience penalty now will have a death penalty. I don't see it any other way. You can't take away 40% more of the exp you have gained upon a death and not call it a death penalty.

I know Brad makes it look like classes are actually gaining exp on the other end. They are, but this is to offset the penalty they will still be using in the calculation. The reality of it is, that unless you get a res you'll incur 40% more exp loss.

It is this way simply because the 1.4 * exp is given to offset the 40% loss of exp. When you cut 40% exp off upon death that was given to offset the penalty, it becomes a death penalty instead of an experience penalty.

Also, it is inaccurate to say that a level 21 contributes more to the group then a level 20 player does. This is a pure fallacy.

Whereas a level 34 necro may be doing more then a level 33 necro (due to the spell circle used by the 34 necro vs the 33), a 21 warrior will in no way contribute more then a level 20 warrior would. Also, a level 21 warrior would not be contributing more then a level 20 wizard would be.

Another example would be the necromancer with a warrior partnership. The necromancer could be 4 levels below the warrior. The necromancer would, in most cases, snare the mob with darkness, fear the mob, and sick their pet. They would then DoT the mob. In the end the necromancer that is 4 levels below the warrior would have contributed in a significant way toward the defeat of the mob. The warrior was only there for the extra damage and may be a good friend of the necromancer. In this case the experience would be disproportionately given to the warrior simply because he is 4 levels higher.


I think Brad threw that out, and hoped that with the hoopla about the seeming removal of the exp penalty, we would not take the time to analyze what is actually happening here.

It will take me a couple more reads to fully understand the impact of what has changed, but it is abundantly clear to me that the exp penalty has now been changed to a death penalty and that the exp distribution between similar levels will be disproportionate for those with only a 1 or 2 level difference.

Verant needs to rethink the exp distribution based on level to be based on level ranges. For instance, those within 2-3 level of one another should get the same exp unless they are in another circle and are actually contributing.

Seriously, something needs to be done to more evenly spread out the experience distribution based actually on the contribution to the defeat instead of purely on the level of the classes involved. Oversimplification of the formula to distribute experience could easily create a divide forcing some people to refuse to group with others because they are x number of levels higher then they are.
