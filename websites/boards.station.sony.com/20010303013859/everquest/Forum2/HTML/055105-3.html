A slightly different take on the 'mana battery thing'

I know you are probably tired of hearing about how Bards don't want to be a mana battery. You may be harboring the idea that this is simply a gameplay choice or peer pressure. However, the mana-battery-syndrome is a result of a number of issues which, if properly balanced out, will remove the problem.

I'll illustrate this through an example. I was playing in Velious hunting the walruses and the walrus-man types in the company of a necromancer. She asked me to not sing the mana song but to snare and pull etc.

She wanted some mana song but not continual - so this isn't a peer pressure situation. However, the difference in effectiveness between my snares and in particular my ability to control extra walruses (low blue to me) that wandered by and HER ability to control them via the necro dot/snare/fear casting was huge. She had resists but if she stuck one in 2 spells it stuck for 30 seconds or more and we had time to finish off the first foe.

Outdoors without camps or line of sight issues with mobs i usually pull with Fufill's for the Tashan effect. Then I'd snare so she could fear. Due to the resist levels I was unable to do anything but snare and try to Fufill when I could. When another mob would wander in I'd try to charm it (if it were a low blue) since barb mez isn't worth the time compared to charm (since it lasts such a short amount of time and is resisted so often).

My charms were resisted well over half the time and lasted an average of 3 seconds ... finally the necro had to save me by fearing one walrus which was eating my lunch as I tried to get it under control.

So I looked at the numbers on the spell lists for mana costs and I realized what most bards have long realized. IT IS VASTLY MORE EFFICIENT FOR ME TO NOT EVEN CAST ON THE MOBS BUT RATHER TO GIVE THE EFFECTIVE CASTER MORE MANA.

It isn't a matter of peer pressure or laziness but rather good tactics. The casters are so much more effective that there is not much point in wasting song cycles on trying to effect the mobs.

That's why there is apush for mana battery use - we can't do enough to make using our other spells as viable as allowing real casters to use more of their spells.

Granted this isn't an absolute rule and it depends on party composition etc. but it is a very hard tactic to deny in many situations. And it's boring .. just thought I'd throw that in.

What we need is a return to the thing that makes a bard - the ability to do meaningful crowd control at ALL levels from 16 to 60.

Not the best but effective enough to be the second choice would seem reasonable.

In addition, the dev team needs to realize that most bard songs DO have a mana cost. In the virtual mana you aren't returning to the casters/healers AND in blood. Bard mana is mostly paid in blood (aggro) - my hits are what race down as I play, the question isn't whether I'll be OOM but OOhitpoints and thus unable to contribute a spell. While I'm chain casting snare 12 times in order to keep it more or less on for 30 seconds i'm being beaten on - draining mana from the healers to keep me alive and running the risk of dying for what is essentially a no-cost spell for a ranger or other snare-enabled class.

So.... my suggestions:

1) lower restists on Snare, Lull, Lullabye, Mez, Charm lines to roughly half what they are now.

I know this seems insanely high but the gap here between use and non-use is HUGE. These things grow increasingly weak as we progress in levels and thus we become harder and harder to justify as doing anything other than mana song.

2) lower resists for other bard songs by a reasonable factor - perhaps 10% - to compensate for the aggro effects which are still extremely punishing.

3) Let bards bind - not gate but bind. Bards are the mid ground between melee and caster and really don't fall under the hybrid umbrella - having one of the useful transport skills seems more appropriate than having none.

4) Check out the many previously mentioned stacking issues - frankly I think that the stacks aren't that serious but enough high-level bards seem concerned that I'm willing to concede it ought to be looked at.

I know this comes late in the thread - probably too late but I hadn't seen the reason behind being a mana battery explained yet and thought it was worthwhile to add.

So much so that they'll beg passing druids, in fact... I'll tell you why, because Songs require that the bard be close, and playing the particular song you want at that moment.

Now... I picked Selo's as an example, because as far as I know it is the ONLY song that is better than equivalent spells in effect! Which makes every other effect we can accomplish both worse statistically, and worse due to the limitations of songs.

So... to BALANCE this problem, either you have to change the limitations of songs... allowing a song to affect as many people as ask within a zone for as long as you play it... which is ridiculous and isn't going to happen, or to make the statistical effects of a song BETTER than the equivalent spells in order to make them more desirable to party members. The final alternative, is to let them enhance the effects of the spells themselves... in other words, let them stack.

Personally, I'm in favor of making songs better than spells. I can only use two or three at a time (using any more exceeds the effects of the first, making sure that every song in a 4 song cycle is dropped for one pulse, in my experience... so that really doesn't count as "successfully twisting 4"... heck, I can twist 8 songs at once, doesn't mean that more than 3 are "up" at any one time.

This means I can pick two or three effects... and have them active. Plus throw in an emergency change up, like a Pixie Strike or something, which will drop all my songs for one pulse in rotation. That's it, that's all I can keep up.
So, unless the casters in the group have only 3 types of enhancements to cast, my having BETTER versions of the effect is not going to keep them from being useful. Plus, this is assuming that I'm not trying to do any damage.

From my observations of the way coding changes take effect after patches, I'm guessing that upping the song effects would be easier and less problematic than actually making them all stack.

Stacking, would be the most elegant solution, allowing us to actually help groups with our songs to the point that they feel we are valuable, without giving us any real extra ability to make another class angry. Of course, it would probably cause a lot of headaches as it created (or recreated, in the case of past-de-enhancements to the class) programming conflicts between different songs/spells.

On the other side: Damage dealing for the bard class should be looked at objectively. With average weapons, we do absolutely pathetic melee damage... which really is fine with me. I don't want to do damage with weapons for my later career as a bard. I view them as a crutch for the young. Yet, we need to be able to dish out some damage somewhere.

I want to do damage with songs... and obviously we are supposed to do that, since we have several songs the cause damage. However, from all I can tell, our damage-output is incredibly low compared to other classes.

Which class should we be comparable to? We can't keep up with the melee classes in terms of rate of constant damage, and we certainly can't compete with the casting classes in terms of single-strike damage. We have a lot of damage-over-time songs, but they're inferior to spell versions, too. Even our last-chance, high level, blow 800 mana song seems to be universally reviled as laughable and useless.

All I ask here is, that you consider finding a point of comparison: Pick another class which you feel we should be equivalent to damage-wise. Then seriously look at the damage they can put forth over time. Then take a good look at the damage bards can put forth: taking into account that if the creature is worth experience to us, and we have maxxed out our requisite musical skills, it will STILL resist at least 1/3 of the time, usually more. Then compare the two, and give us an honest assessment of whether you agree that we need to be adjusted.

Absor..

Since time is comming near for you to end these talks, i would like to do a summary of things that I think should be added to help balance bards in the game post 50+.. here we go!

1) Bard melee is the answer, for the "high Level" Boss mob encounters where the monster is not affected by magic and has to be defeated through melee power.

2) These boss mobs are red to any player! They are 63+, so no player is on equal "footing" with the monster. Due to how level effects spell success, it is not practical for casters to be able to do as much damage to the monsters with massive health over 200K in some cases. So the fights are long, and melee win's the day.

3) Bards are not balanced with other "group buffing" casters. All characters will be buffed prior to the fight and need none of a bards buffs (due to stat, and haste caps). The bards contribution in this situation is his/her melee ability and Mana regen for the casters who are using mana.

4) Bard melee needs to be increased at high level so that they contribute more than a manasong. In such a case, it is more likely that a high leVel raid will look for a "true melee CLASS" to take the spot of the bard.

Solutions..

a) Bard weapons need to do more damage. Review of some of the weapons available to the other melee classes, and add "BRD" to the ones who fit the need (higher damage) and useability of the bard class. I think many "SHD", and "RNG" weapons, may fall in this area.

b) Bards sing and dance.. I see no reason why a bard would not know how to "KICK". This adds a way for bards to do a little more damage, and help bring up their melee contribution at high levels. Give this skill to bards and tweak it as needed (a cap of 155 to start sounds good, have it gain levels the same way Dodge gains levels to 155).

c) Bards have a short coming of Heath. this is simular to how monks don't have as much health as Warriors. Give the "BLOCK" skill to Bards. This will aid in the defense area, and falls in line with what a smart bard would be doing, knowing he/she could not take that heavy hit. it should be capped around 75-100 like our parry skill. It will not help our damage, but it "will" allow bards to melee marginally better against the monsters like the boss mobs in velious who will hit our AC due to their levels alone, and our lack of health makes us a liability to the raid if in melee range. This will help.

d) Low level bards did use Shields.. But many don't actually use them anymore due to the huge loss of melee ability. (and our melee ability at high levels suffers greatly). Allow bards to "BASH" with a shield (note: you cant bash, and kick at same time, which is normal). This is an ability that i think we should have had all along. We should be able to get fairly high in it's use. The trade off of using duel wield is not balanced for a bard who cant use a shield to bash.. nearly all other melee who use a shield get to bash with it.

e) Bards should get regular crits. This shows the skill of a well placed hit and is totally random for warriors and Rogues (one of our parent classes). Since bards are limited to 1 hand weapons, this is one way of adding some melee damage ability, without giving too much. Due to the random nature of regular crits, and the low damage of our hits, it will not add too much damage, but enough to make it worth while.

Summary...

Bard Balance is needed in the "melee" side of our class. Originally our songs were thought to make up for our lack of Melee ability, but with the expansions and the increasing difficulty of the encounters in EverQuest, our songs could not overcome our lack of physical damage in high level encounters, where our songs are not needed and a melee class is. Increasing the "options" and abilities of melee available to a Post 50's bard will balance this out. It's up to verant Dev team to determine how much.

Thanks for listening.
