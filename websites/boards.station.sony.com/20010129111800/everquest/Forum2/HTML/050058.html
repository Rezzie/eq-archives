First, I feel the need to make comments about this whole quad kiting thing.

ALL I was saying was that I, as a player and a person, don’t particularly like it as a tactic. As a Verant employee, my intention is to bring that up in a tuning meeting (as MY opinion, not as something that the players want changed). So, please, can we stop all the threads about how bad my idea is? I’ve got the message already, I know that many of you don’t want anything done to reduce quad kiting. I’ve received several e-mails explaining how it is a valid and balanced (from a risk/reward perspective) tactic. I will bring that with me to the meeting when I mention quad kiting, have no fear. And that meeting isn’t going to be next week, so don’t panic…

I also wanted to talk about a trend that I have been seeing lately. It has probably always been there and I’ve just been slow to see it. But I have been reading more and more posts wherein people are attacking other people. And it bothers me.

I have absolutely no problem with people engaging in spirited and even aggressive arguments over ideas or gameplay or any of that. I encourage it.

But I do not want to see one customer attacking another. I have the responsibility to try to keep this place a forum for talking about EverQuest. For Verant this is one of our main sources for discovering new ideas, areas for improvement, and the general sense of things from our customers. When discussions break down into personal attacks, all that functionality is lost, and bad feelings are generated. Those bad feelings ultimately harm EverQuest and its community.

I would really like to see a dramatic reduction in personal attacks. I hate having to be the bad guy here, but I will if it becomes necessary.

Calling other customers ‘idiots’ is not acceptable here (or any other form of personal attack). And, if I start seeing an excess of personal attacks on Monday or over the weekend, we will be issuing warnings.

I don’t want anyone to feel that we are trying to control topics, or control what you say. But we are willing to control the way you say it. Basically, BE CIVIL TO EACH OTHER.

Or else…

As a final note, I want to get through all of the classes with a ‘talk’ thread for each before February 9th (or thereabouts). This will be… well, a mess. I will be trying to start a new one every other day, starting on Monday (looking at a calendar, I may have to do more than three a week…).

This will take up a lot of my posting time, I suspect. I think it’s a good thing for the game, though, so I’m going to do it.

Don’t be expecting instant results on these issues, though. There are always tons of things going on here. And we really want to look at all of these issues as a whole, so I want to get them all done before we dig deeply into them. You might see some of the easy issues resolved right away, and others might not be addressed for a while (and by ‘addressed’ I mean either changed or checked off as ‘ok as is’).

I’ll do what I can to keep you updated on all that.

Alan

Please promise one thing when considering hybrids and balance: Consider in game real life situations vs. how they look on paper.

Versatility looks great on paper, and allows my Paladin (level 56) to regularly defeat warriors of equal or greater levels in a duel. In truth we give up about 30%- 40% in melee ability for our gains in spell casting ability.

This looks good on paper, and it also allows us to stack up well in a vs. warriors (dueling).

I in fact dueled a level 58 warrior last night to test something. I defeated her, and had 300 hp remaining. So, it would seem that paladins and warriors are balanced. However, when you consider that I expelled 2800 of her hp over the course of the duel, yet she expelled my base of 2025, plus LOH which did about 4 bubbles or 1600, plus I (by miracle) got a root to land and got 4 greater heals off (1200 hp). So in the course of the duel I did 2800 of her hp vs her doing 4500+ hp of mine. The point is that in a real life situation where we are grouped with a cleric, there is no compelling reason to have me grouped over the warrior.

I'm pretty sure you guys have been reading the recent hybrid posts, even if you aren't responding to them. There are (as in all posts) several whines and requests for the ridiculous (such as Paladins getting CH at 49, etc etc). But, I would say that 90% of the posts aside of course from the 'bumps' are provideing well thought out arguements and ideas that I hope verant takes a look at. I will take your lack of response as in fact a good thing, it shows that you guys are testing things and putting thought in yourselves rather than makeing changes that in fact do unbalance the game. With the xp penalty, us hybrids that have attained 55 and higher have put a whole lot of time into our characters. I think I speak for most when I claim that we aren't looking to be the champions of the game, we don't want the classes to be unbalanced. But the high end game is designed with efficiency a priority, and at this level versatility does not equal efficiency. More and more we have to place a greater emphasis on our melee aspect of the hybrid over the caster side because resists are higher, the gap in spell efficiencies grow even larger at the higher levels, and in the case of a paladin, our parent class (which is always there in higher risk <read fun> camps ) has spells that everyone wants over the paladin spell which lags behind up to 2 circles , or 10 - 15 or even more levels. And when compareing this melee that we fall back on, we are running in effect 5-8 levels in ability behind the pure melees.

Just please consider the 'real life situations' that hybrids face rather than considering how things stack up on paper. For we play our characters in these real life situations.
