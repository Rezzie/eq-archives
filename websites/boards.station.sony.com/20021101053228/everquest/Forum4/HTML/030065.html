Taken from the manual...
--snip
Combat Stability: (3 skill levels) - Each skill level gives the character an increased ability to reduce the damage he takes in combat (damage mitigation). The first skill level increases the character's damage mitigation by 2%, the second skill level raises the damage mitigation increase to 5% and the third raises the increase to 10%.
--/snip

I would THINK this would indicate that melee damage would be reduced by 10 percent if the player had Combat Stability 3, but it doesn't appear to work this way.

For about the last month or so I've been doing quite a bit of testing with it and don't notice much of a difference if any. The easiest test has been against level 60 Guardian Wurms in Skyfire Mountains. The characters in question are 1) a level 55 Ogre Shadow Knight with Combat Stability 3, 2) a level 60 Barbarian Shaman, and 3) a level 60 Half Elf Druid. In absolutely all tests, the Guardian Wurms have had the capability of hitting for 530/530 with a bash or kick. The highest the 60 shaman or 60 druid has been hit for is 530. The highest that the Shadow Knight has been hit for is also 530. According to the description, 10 percent damage mitigation on a 530 hit should knock 53 points off of the hit, for a total of 477 damage taken as opposed to 530 damage. Both the Shaman and the Druid have been hit for 530 while sitting, so it is quite apparent that this is the maximum hit capability of the mob. AC/HP on the Shadow Knight, though it shouldn't make a difference I wouldn't think, have been around 1310/4800 and using 2 handed slashing (no shield).

Does anyone know what's happening here? How is Combat Stability actually working? The description of the AA says nothing about "chance to reduce damage"; other AA's such as Combat Fury and Spell Casting Mastery both have "chance to" listed in the descriptions. I would think if Combat Stability was a chance based skill it would say so, like the others do.

So... My newbie zone question is, how does Combat Stability work?

=)

Unfortunately, I've never been much of a log keeper. The moment the Shadow Knight hit 55, 12 AA points were spent in Combat Stability so there have never been any fights with him at level 55 without Combat Stability. Judging solely on the basis of mana required to heal however, on the average it takes roughly the same amount of mana from the Druid to keep the Shadow Knight alive now as it did when the Shadow Knight was 54. Of course there are anomalies - some fights take less healing, others take more, but on average I would say about the same. Reducing damage by avoiding being hit is covered in a seperate AA, Combat Agility.

All of the other AA's seem to be extremely clear as to what they will give you exactly. The closest non-combat AA I can see would probably be:
--snip
Healing Adept: (3 skill levels) - Each skill level improves the character's effectiveness with instant effect healing spells. The first skill level increases the effectiveness of the character's healing spells by 2%, the second skill level raises the healing increase to 5% and the third raises the increase to 10%.
--/snip

This AA increases the base amount of heals - the number of hit points healed, excluding complete heal type spells (new shaman and druid "incomplete heal" count as a complete heal type spell). In the case of this AA it does not work on an average nor on a "chance to" basis. Mitigation is actually a pretty straight foward thing in my mind. If I had 100% damage mitigation, it would mean I alleviate or relieve 100% of the possible damage.

I would sumbit that if it were an average, the verbage on the ability should say "chance to mitigate" rather than just mitigate. If the other AA's were equally as oblique in there descriptions, I would tend to believe that they actually meant average or chance to.

If it is in all actuality only a 10 percent chance to reduce damage taken then I would see this as an exponentially inferior AA to Combat Agility. Rather than having a 10 percent chance to avoid taking ANY damage as with Combat Agility, you now have a 10 percent chance to take LESS damage - still taking some.

I guess I'm just having a hard time explaining myself hehe. =)

If it only succeeds on a mitigation check occassionaly, I would see no reason why it would not say so in the verbage. All other AA's that must pass a check clearly state "chance to". If this is indeed the case this would be the only one out of hundreds that they chose to not be clear on. I would really see no point in that.

If it truly is a chance to reduce damage by 10 percent, I feel rather hosed =\ Had I known this I would have put those points into Combat Agility where at least I have a 10 percent chance of taking 0 damage instead of 530, instead of Combat Stability where I have a 10 percent chance of taking 477 damage instead of 530.
