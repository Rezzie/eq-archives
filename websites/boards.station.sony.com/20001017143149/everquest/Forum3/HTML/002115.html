Once upon a time, rain spells worked very well. They hit everything in the area for 3 waves of damage. This was deemed too powerful. Their maximum allotment of damage was limited to 4 units of damage.

If there is one monster in the area, it will take 3 waves of damage.

If there are 2 monsters in the area, they will each take damage twice.

If there are 3 monsters in the area, they will each take damage once and one will take damage twice.

If there are 4 monsters in the area, they will all take damage once.

If there are 5 or more monsters in the area, 4 will take damage once.

Here is the kicker - the monsters are counted this way:
First monster to take damage is the caster (thanks guys)
Second to take damage are PC pets - NOTE they actually don't take damage, but they count for one wave of the four.
Last to take damage are the Monsters.

So, if a wizard - a mage and pet - a necto and pet - an enchanter and pet are fighting 4 monsters in close combat, and the wizard casts a rain spell:
1) wizard takes damage from their own spell
2) mage pet counts off one damage tick
3) necro pet takes off one damage tick
4) enchanter pet takes off one damage tick
5) NO MONSTER TAKES ANY DAMAGE

I never even bother memorizing rain spells.

Tears of Druzil is a great spell to use when no pets are in the group. Another problem these rain spells have is that they get resisted when the mob is trying to flee and is snared. As a wizard I have gotten into the habit of holding off the nuke till the mob is worn down a bit. If you do this the last wave tends to get resisted more due to the fact the mob may be fleeing at this point. The best thing to do is to cast this spell early in the fight (it has low agro) but learn to not cast it too early that you get agro'd on after first wave. Takes a little practice to get the timing down on this. The timing also differs from group to group pending on how fast the group can kill the mobs you are hunting.
