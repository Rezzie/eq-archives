The Warrior Class, ie:Melee, that evey one say is not balanced, it is balanced. A properly played warrior does the job he/she is ment to do. That job is as simple as it gets. PROTECT THE CASTERS.

He goes in, holds the mobs attention, beats at it, then when the time comes, the Wizards, Mages open up on it.

If you want to look at it modern term.
Infantry=Warrior
Artillery=Casters, all 4 types
Armor(Tanks)=Paladin,Shadow Knights
Bradley=Ranger, Beastlords, Shaman
Medac=Clerics

The Infantry goes into an area, gets the attention of the enemy, once locked on, the Tanks move in unless to much resistance, then the Artillery opens up from the rear. At this point, it is up to the Grunts and Armor to keep the enemy pinned down while the shells turn them into compost. Air support and cover can fall into the nuker and Medac area as well.

I know, not the best comparison, but that is the way it works in reality. Warriors are balanced.

Look at the Old D&D and AD&D. Warriors and heavy melee by late teens main job was to protect the casters. The casters will always do far more damage.

Warriors carry M16s, Casters carry Daisy Cutters and H-Bombs.

I may be off base being a fulltime Ranger, but I was in the U.S. Army for 8 years. I see the Everquest combat system in those term. This my or maynot make sense to some of you, but I believe this comparison is valid.

Just my 2 cents worth.

To at least provide you with a response, I would say some of your reasoning is sound. No one disputes that the damage a warrior can dish out is not THAT important (apart from helping to gain aggro). We know that warriors protect the casters, acting as the meat shield while the rest of the group lays into them. The issue is that while the knight classes may not be able to take a beating quite as well as the warrior (in general) they can reach out and snag aggro at will with their range of spells.

So now the healer has to throw his heals a few seconds earlier than he would with a warrior... but at least he knows the knight has a better chance of keeping the mob from pounding on him.

Thats the gist of it as far as I understand it. My main is a bard, so i am comfortable with knowing only that aggro gets me real dead real fast.
