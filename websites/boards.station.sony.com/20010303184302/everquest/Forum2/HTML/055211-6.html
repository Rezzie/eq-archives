Absor,

After playing a ranger the majority (95%) of my time in Everquest (and in all other forms of RP since the late 70's), I really have very little complaints. By no means would I stop playing my ranger if there was nothing done to improve it...sometimes it not about what you can do or cant do, but how you do it. Theres always been a popularity of rangers mainly due to the image and ideals of the class, rather than the power of it.

But, if there were changes to be made in Everquest, I do think that there are a few improvements that could be made.

The number one complaint I see about rangers (from rangers and non rangers alike) are that we are a mana sink. The most common phrase heard in any raid is "Casting <healing spell> on <Ranger>" (if we are ever invited to one...which reminds me our begging skill should go up each time we attempt to join a group). My healthbar in a massive fight just yo-yos back and forth and I just hold my breath hoping that the little red bar never totally disappears.

Now there has been tons of requests for more hps/AC and solutions on these boards but with more ingame numbers than anything else. If we were to look with some logic, the EQ ranger currently doesn't fit the "mold".

Let's look at a typical ranger image: a hardy weather worn woodsperson, quick and stealthy, living off the land guided by the forces of "Nature". Oh yeah, who own cool bows.

Well, one...we are far from hardy. Paladins have more hps. A class that is defined by excellent hygiene, great teeth, personal grooming and a dazzling smile, should not have more hit points than a weathered traveller of the lands. I guess you can count in the paladin's devotion and training to give them some hp's but please, Ive seen the secret door in the Paladins guild that leads to their pedicurist.

Shadow knights, a class that hangs out in seedy dungeons, torturing small furry cute things and plotting in dark smelly underpits of the earth, also is hardier than the ranger. Yes, count in the unholy devotion to wreak havoc and destruction to all of Norrath and you can justify their hp total also. But arson, torture, murder and basic mayhem just is not as strenuous as planting trees and wrestling bears.

As you can see, the ranger definitely needs more health to live up to its image.

Now on to the defenses. Rangers have always portrayed the quick and agile image. Considering this, we wear armor that allows it. With the exception of warriors, monks and rogues, I think rangers should have the highest dodging skills, and a slight hike in defense to theoretically support this. I dont necessarily support raising of the other skills (parry, riposte), as we do want to keep in balance, but I do feel that rangers should follow the philosophy of "Be quick or be dead". And yes, drop the disarm for the safefall....we do jump off the really big trees we climb without killing ourselves.

Now for the living off the land part. Basically we do that extremely well in Norrath. I would love to see the hide and sneak caps raised heavily in exchange for the fact that we could only use them in outdoors zones. But really minor thing here.

Guided by the forces of Nature part....our spells. Why dont rangers get more shaman buffs? Really about the only one we get that we use alot is feet like cat. I believe that we should get an upgrade to the spell at 49. Dex buffs would be nice but totally unnecessary, but a stamina buff would be a major plus.

As stated before, regeneration would be a welcome addition at 49, as it is silly to have Chloro without regen.

And speaking of shaman, I think barbarians would be a welcome addition to the rangers profession. They would fit in nice, although they wear skirts/kilts/whatever. But please, no dark elf rangers...lets bury the Drizzt Do'Urden complex once and for all and leave it in the Forgotten Realms where it belongs.

Finally archery.....a nice skill. Not class defining by any means and a way for rangers to stay alive and kite for a lil bit while. Almost never used in group situations except to pull. I think an enhancement would do greatly..something like a set shot. There have been many other ideas but a stationary ranger a few feet from the mob and taking a well aimed shot should do great damage to the mob. And please add crippling blows for bows. A crit is nice but 50 points to a Velious mob is like hitting a cow with a thumbtack. Imagine a kneeling ranger away from a warrior tanking a frost giant plunking arrows into its back.

Well...I think Ive just about said enough. I hope many more good things are coming for the rangers, but if not, there will still be rangers around.

I think I speak for many people when I say that rangers are one profession where RL players do take to heart. It is probably the only class in EQ that you can nerf and make it unplayable and there will still be a large group out there who will play it. Its a part of the RP genre and has been and it will remain so and nothing will deter those of us who are truly rangers at heart.

Sincerely,

Dear Absor. First of all I thank you, and all of developers for allowing players such as I be part of the discussion regarding our class' balance.

I have chosen the Ranger class because of the "Light and Agile damage dealer". These types of characters have always appealed to me in all of roleplaying games that I have played. From AD&D trough Vampire the Masquerade, Ultima Online, Aheron's Call...and to Everquest.

At low levels, I must say that we do fit that bill...speed and agility. At the higher end though the Cap skills get skewed to the point that this is not the case. Classes who wear heavy platemail can outdodge and outparry us while we get hit.

I have some opinions as to how Rangers should be balanced. I hope that they are all viable and make sense to you and my fellow players.

Rangers, Paladins and Shadowknights are all hybrid classes (I hesitate to call Bards hybrids as they have unique "spells")
Paladins and Shadowknights are the defensive hybrids, this is shown by their armour type and armour Ac in comparison to our armour. Plate Vs. Chainmail.
At the level of 50 + the defensive hybrids get 230 skill cap of offensive ability and 252 cap of defensive ability.
In Comparison Rangers get 252 offensive, and 200 defensive, this is a 30 point deficiency between the main skills as to where they are capped at.
In order for hybrids to be balanced, our main caps should be mirrors of each other, ie: Rangers: 252 offense and 230 defense, Paladins 252 defense and 230 offense.
Another Issue, we are labeled as an offensive class such as Rogues and Monks. Rogues and Monks both outdamage warriors to a great extent. True that they do not have spells to heal, buff themselves with, but still their damage is superior to that of a warrior with equivalent stats and equipment.
Rangers as an offensive class, do not outdamage warriors by a margin nearly as big as rogues and monks, and while this might seem fair as of us being hybrids, it is not how it seems in other player's eyes. In the end the warrior is a be all middle class. Best tank, good damage dealer. They are not meele offensive class as a definition, but rather a class who can stand its own in the fray and do some damage as well. Damage dealing meeles are meant to outdamage warriors. If we do not, there is NO reason for a group to have us around most of the time, as they can just get another warrior who is a better tank and can do damage just as well as us.

My proposition is as follows:

-Add Triple attack at lv 55
-Increase our Dual Wield and Double Attack skill caps (along with those of monks and rogues) by 30 points (3 more per level 50-60)
-Increase our offhand damage bonus.
-Exchange Firestrike and Calefaction for a line of unique "low resist" "fast cast time" spells so that we do not have to loose meele time in order to cast a spell which might be resisted, interrupted, or plain old fizzle.

Rangers as a damage dealing class have one skill which is out of place in a damage dealer's repertoire: Taunt.
I propose this skill be removed along with riposte, and the points used towards Riposte, Parry, Dodge, and to small extent perhaps Block.
thats 205 points of skills that we do NOT need, and do NOT use.

Thank you for the time and Safe Travels M'lord

I've said this once before and I'll say it again:

Please don't forget the lower levels!

You can add all the 50+ ranger balances you want, but if you don't add some improvements by at the earliest level 20, playing a pre-50 ranger is still going to be just as boring and frustrating. All you'll have created, then, is an environment that promotes twinking and powerleveling.

We see this situation now with rogues. Rogues start being compelling around level 30 or 40, so nobody wants to play a rogue without twinking and/or powerleveling them.

Rangers are the same way. Rangers start to see their disadvantages at level 9, when we realize that our first set of spells - OUR FIRST SET - only contains one single spell (Snare) that's actually useful for something.
This discouragement only continues as we realize that none of our spells are unique, interesting, or effective, because by the time we get any particular offensive or defensive spell, our level is so high that the spell is ineffective. Our healing spells are too wimpy at any given level to be worth the trouble of memorizing them. Our offensive spells get resisted, and our defensive spells aren't dramatic enough to have an impact.

What we need is a Ranger-specific ability, or some better spells, from - at the earliest - level 20.
