The basic problem is that when you put 12 people in close proxemity all beating on a mob lag goes through the roof, with 18+ people start dropping, 24+ lots of people start dropping, 30+ 1/2 the people drop.

The server floods the client with data and all filters are client side. Even hefty lines like DSL get lag, anybody with less sees "You have been disconnected".

Solution: Server side filters. There are a lot of helpful suggestions in the 2 links above.

Since vellious came out the amount of overall system lag has tripled. Vellious zones have a standard 1k-2k packet size. As soon as you enter combat this jumps to 3k-5k with 50% packet loss. And that is with 12 or less people.

The problem was there before, but wasn't as big of a problem because there were fewer encounters that required more than 1-2 groups. Now there are tons of encounters that require 3-6 groups. That is the high level game, what else are you supposed to do at 60? It does no good to take 30 people to kill something if you are only going to have 18 after the first 30 seconds and that is what is happening.

While a person on a 28.8k modem can technically still log in and play the game, they go bye bye with 12 people around, sometimes with 6 in vellious. The system requirements in no way suggest the reality of the game. I have ISDN, I can barely play with 18 people, 24 and there is a good chance of being disconnected. People with DSL and Cable are losing connection, it's very bad.

Its not a random ISP burp or slow traffic in chicago. It happens every time several groups engage in combat with a mob (aka uber mobs, planes raids, high level dungeons) and it happens to everyone present, when combat stops its fine, thats not a random coicidink, thats an easily repeatable problem. Adjusting datarate does nothing for anyone on a slower connection than DSL to help alleviate this problem. We need server side filters.

You have created a portion of the game to require multiple groups to conquer, but the game mechanics does not support this.

Please address this, the game is becoming unplayable. This is easily the largest gameplay issue out there. Class balancing, helm graphic bugs etc mean nothing if you cannot play the game. The players have given tons of suggestions in the threads mentioned. We have no work around, no way to fix the problem from our end, the ball is completely in Verants court.

Well you have to look at both sides of the coin too:

One side is Verants server's being able to handle the traffic and all the calculations, and also their connection the internet.

The next side is your connection to the internet, and also your computers ability to process the information it is recieving. Now most lag is most likely from your internet connection, but remember once you recieve data from the server, your computer has to process that information and send information back. So a Pentium 200 with a 8M video card could have a DS3 going to it, and still have lag because it's unable to keep up with the information being sent to it.

I use a dial-up 56k connection and have minimal lag (no more lag than expected on dial up, rare LD's) even in large gatherings/raids. But I also have a Athlon system with a 64M video card, and plenty of RAM.

I am getting a cable modem in two weeks, perhaps I should post afterwards how much the connection helps.

I agree this is a very bad problem that should be looked into, but I also think we should all clear our end in the mean time.

Another thing I forgot to add, if your on a Cable/DSL/ISDN type connection and still have more lag then you think you should have, try changing (if you have not already) the datarate.txt file to 9.0. This whole talk about "server side filters" COULD IN THEORY INCREASE THE PROBLEM. Verant may already know this, but there is NO WAY for us to tell.

Ever think (for a moment here folks, try it, it's amazing what you can do if you think!) that VI's hardware is dying and people are LD'ing because the software on the server cannot keep up with it?

Now again, this is just a theory, but it could EASILY be either network problems OR (FAR more likely!!) this....
