I was already a master smith and close to master tailor when the last craft skill upgrade came out ... it was not all i wanted but it defently was a step in that direction ( with the bazillion things out their that a bazillion players think need to be dont RIGHT NOW i was happy to see any improvement to the craft skills)

Tailoring got a big bost from the added silks and more patterns (this realy helped tailoring) ... smithing got some weapons finly (thou thay cost too much to make to be profitable) ... pottery got mana vials for the new enchanter spells (the reason i mastered pottery) ... a lot of broken things where fixed for a lot of the skils ...

So what will be next (when is just a matter of time) ...

First of id like to see more broken things fixed , like the meny broken studded armor parts , reinforced mask , and the quivers ...

And talking about tailoring , studded armor is leather armor with studs ADDED , id like to see the studded 1 AC better than leather ,(now it cost about the same to make as buying leather) , and reinforced get a +1Ac too , this would make them sellable items instead of things you have to make to get to backpacks ...

Id also like to see a smith made dagger , and posibly a smith made throwing weapon of some sort ... the smith can make a weapon for any class but the casters ...

Then their would be new things for craft skills ... ALL the craft skill ...

For smiths , magic forged weapons would be the next step , needing mana vials , all the things the normal weapons needs , and a few 'special' componets to make shure its not too easy ... if the production cost was about half that of the buy price of combine (but eeding some work) smiths could then make some cash doing smithing ...

And Smith made plate mail ... it would be hard to make (higher trivial AND more exspensive steel sheets to make as well as tailor made armor straps) this would be above the bronze/banded but beloe the quest armor ACs ... if the production price is right it would be sellable yet not too much so (balance is best)

tailors , how about a 6 slot bag in the 30 to 40 skill range for lower end tailors to make , not a big seller but usefull ... also a higher grade of silks for casters would be nice , Fine Silk armor ... a point or two better AC per location but much harder to make (trivial level and production cost , it might need a new type of brew , like Kiola Tonic or some such , or maybe another componet to add cost)

brewing and Bakeing ... theis skills could use something a player would find usefull , like a brew that restors fatige , a bread that absorbe some poison damage , ect ... nothing on par with alchemesty potions but still something that a player would think worth lugging around and useing , and therefor worth paying for ...

pottery ... a ceramic lamp (light sorce) , and a higher level magic lamp (very good light sorce) ... (not much room here)

Jewlery ... the jewlery skill already reaches 200 skill with a full range of items but dose not iteract with the other crafts skills ... bejewled hilt / pommel for magic blades ? bucles / buttons /clasps for new magic tailored items ?

Fletching could be expanded to cover all wood working weapons , like staves and clubs (and combine a smith made dagger and fletcher made staff to make a spear)


Then their is the posibility for more quests to need crafted items , this is a great way to add legs to the less traved craft skills like bakeing/brewing/pottery ... the quest would have traveling , getting a few hard or semi rare drops , and makeing 1 or more hard craft items ... you could take it further and have a few quests that need several crafted items from diferent skills , like a large jar made with special clays/water (to mix in) , a brew needing hard to find items (for maranading), a baked food needing high level monster meats to make a special kind of food , all given together for a suitable reward ...

just some things id like to see in the craft skills down the road , like some of them ? dont like some of them ? have a few ideas of your own ... lets get the ball rolling and get a good idea of what we al think would make the craft skills better

(please no "super special" items or "it would be great for my class if" things ... id like to see things that help all craftsmen , not a select few)
