you type /l or /loc or maybe even /location but I don't know if that long one works or not. It will tell you where you are in the coordinate system, xyz. X is north south, Y is east west, and Z is up down. In EQ X becomes positive as you go west, very properly so if you as me! Y becomes positive as you go north, just like latitude. Z is kinda self explanatory.

So make a hotkey that types /l for you and hit it a lot to see what is happening. Make sure you hit it when you are fighting. Bad news if you have to chase your corpse all over the zone with no idea where it is. If you have the location in your chatbox along with the battle spam you will know where that silly thing is laying. It may still be inside a wall of some such but you will be way ahead of a lot of problems if you know the location of your corpse.

First coordinate:
Increases towards north
Decreases towards south

Second coordinate:
Increases towards west
Decreases towards east.

Third coordinate is kinda useless, unless in a multilevel dungeon.

Sense heading and compass:
At low Sense Heading, your compass moves chaotically. When you use Sense Heading skill, it either fails or succeeds.
If it fails, the compass continues to move chaotically.
If it succeeds, your compass becomes steady, correctly showing your direction.
After 6 seconds(one tick), Sense Heading buttons pops up, and compass becomes unsteady again.

I have an lvl 27 alt with 140 SH, and he succeeds about 9 times out of 10. Yet unless I click SH button every 6 seconds, my compass remains unsteady. Therefore, I don't think that your compass automatically checks the skill.

However, my main has 200 SH, and compass is always steady. I believe that skill must reach acertain level (might be 200) for this to be so.

Can also create a macro like this...

/loc
/doability # (the number being what ability slot you have assigned sense heading to.. abilities are listed like this..

1 2
3 4
5 6

So if you assigned Sense heading to the top left slot your command is
/doability 1
Then everytime you hit that hot key you get your location and your sense heading check.
