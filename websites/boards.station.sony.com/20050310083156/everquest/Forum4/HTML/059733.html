This whole issue with the tribute nerf has me wondering about something.

Was the tribute problem really a mistake?

Look at the facts - had any sanity checking been done on the tribute values the bug would have been found (something any competent programmer with access to the item database could have done - all they would have had to do was do would be write a script that checked the vendor buy value with the tribute value and report any item with a large % variation).

So either SOE neglected to do any sanity checking whatsoever (And I know I would get fired if I made a large scale change to my work's database without doing sanity checking on the data), or the tribute values were set that way deliberately.

Now why would they do that? The main purpose of the tribute system, like the casino, is to remove items and money from the economy - and a lot of people who just got the expansion are going to go check the new system out, to see if it is worth their time. Now, since the values on items is inflated, people see that they can get a good deal on clearing out the stuff in their bank or on
their trader. So now you have a lot of people with signifigant tribute points. So you make sure that you leave a couple of items with absolutely egregious tribute values, so you have a good excuse to roll back the tribute values retroactively. End result, you have removed a fair amount of items from the system with very little end effect.

If this was truely a mistake, then SOE seriously needs to think about hiring a few people who understand the need for sanity checking and who are capable of making those checks (and after the problems with people macroing tradeskills you would think that they would have already figured this out)

I don't believe any of these 'conspiracy theories' having to do with the tribute system. If you talk to anyone in the beta test they'll let you know that for most of the beta, the tribute system wasn't working.

Strike 1: The tribute system didn't get much chance to be tested before it went live.

Secondly, the people in the beta test were not playing their existing characters. They had new character created for them and artificially bumped up to level 65 with a standard set of gear. They were also largely limited to hunting in GoD zones.

Strike 2: The test characters weren't 'real'. They didn't have old junk sitting in the bank to turn into the tribute masters. They didn't (necessarily) have the tradeskills to make the out-of-whack tradeskill items.

Lastly, the values assigned to items do not (or perhaps did not) appear to be individually assigned. Based on the whacked out tribute values, it appears a formula was used to calcute an item's tribute worth. That formula assigned high values to items with effects, skill mods, or extremely high stats. It apparently did NOT take into account whether the stat was useful (e.g. high tribute value for cheap opal encrusted steins due to +25 cha), nor whether the skill mod was on a cheap item (e.g. +3% fishing skill mod on animated bait).

Strike 3: The formula to calculate tribute values overly weighted +skill mods and high stats.

I hate to dash the conspiracy theorists, but I have to agree with Azirux here:

quote:
Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by stupidity.

They goofed.
