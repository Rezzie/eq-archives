Again, the usual reminder. I am primarily looking for balance concerns here. But I am certainly willing to put bugs on the list. Also, though I really do understand the fact that you want to emphasize and back up statements by others, please, please try to keep the repeat information to a minimum. I'm finding that by page three or four on these threads I'm rereading the same suggestions by people that start their posts with "I didn't read this thread, so I don't know if this had been said…" I don't want to stifle any creativity. But I have to admit that I tend to get bleary eyed reading the same suggestions over and over again, and I know I'm missing good new ideas late in threads when they are buried among things that others have covered several times before.

So, please, let's hear what you think is unbalanced or broken about enchanters. Here are the few things that I had already jotted down:

Gift of Brilliance: When compared to similar buffs by Clerics and Shamans this spell doesn’t seem good enough. Sure, it will see use, but it could be a better spell. Quick comparison:

Aeogolism (60th level Cleric spell): Adds 1,100 to max hit points and 54 to AC for a single target. Lasts 2.5 hours. Costs 750 mana.
Focus of the Spirit (60th level Shaman spell): Adds 405 hit points, 67 strength and 60 dexterity for a single target. Lasts 1.5 hours. Costs 500 mana.
Gift of Brilliance (50th level Enchanter spell): Adds 150 mana and grants a mana regeneration rate increase of 2 points per tick for a single target. Lasts 1.5 hours. Costs 450 mana.

The Shaman and Cleric spells essentially replace other buffs. Gift of Brilliance stacks with Clarity II. It might be nice to have the mana regeneration be 2 points greater than Clarity II and NOT stack with it so that enchanters could use it to save a slot. And perhaps rather than increasing the mana bonus, give it an intelligence bonus. But perhaps at this level enchanters just don't need any more intelligence boosts…

Illusions drop when zoning: Is there a gameplay reason behind the fact that illusions drop when you zone? If not, can we make it so they do not?

More Illusions: I think it’s a good idea to dig through the list of current global characters and see if there are a few more illusions that we can give to enchanters.

I know there are more issues…

Hi everyone.

This thread is so incredibly long that I must admit to not having read it all. If I'm repeating someone forgive me.

I'm posting to ask this simple question: What gives so many people that Enchaters are overpowered? Do those people actually play them or just watch us in action and think we're awesome?

Most players seem to equate power with soloing ability. Do enchanters solo well? No. We have no snare of any kind, so we cannot kite. Our pet is such a wimp (at upper levels) that he can't tank even the lowest blue without major help from us. We have two methods to solo with, root-n-nuke and charm. Both are extremely unreliable and really only amount to emergency techniques.

I think that one of the components of "power" in EQ is survivability. When the situation gets out of control, who is going to die? We have no FD. We have very little armor. We have very few HPs. What we do have in abundance is the attention of large numbers of monsters. Allow me to share a favorite quote from Caster's Realm: "Also Bards, at least at the higher levels, are usually the second to die after the Enchanter."

So is power the ability to kill monsters? If you think so, then enchanters are one of the weakest classes out there. We have a few nukes which we use to help out with when we have extra mana.

Is power about the uber raids? How many enchanters do you need to take down that uber mob? In the final encounter all we are generally good for is buffing. You need a few enchanters to help break into the final encounter in most cases, but you really only ever need a few. How many warriors to you bring with you? Would a ratio of 4 warriors to 1 enchanter be enough?

We have an easier time finding groups than some classes. Really that depends on where you are and your luck. Each group really only needs one enchanter, and if you aren't in a dungeon, they don't need you at all. The other night, I bailed out of Lower Guk because there was already another enchanter LFGing in there. That poor guy had been waiting for an hour before I got there.

We have no SoW and no TP so when it comes to travelling, we are a beggar class just like most. We do have both Invis and Invis to Undead. I'd say considering our lack of ability to defend ourselves, this is a good balance. You need somebody to run in and drag your corpse? No rogue around? Sure, I'll do it, and when the invis breaks at the wrong time and those three blues jump me, I'll die for my effort and not complain.

In truth, enchanters are not powerful at all. What we have instead, is the ability to make others more powerful. This is truly a beautiful thing. We generally don't do any damage ourselves. Instead we haste buff the tanks to increase their damage, and clarity the casters to increase their damage.

Where enchanters really shine is by increasing the group's output through crowd-control. Basically, we can reduce the damage coming in, therefore taking the load off the healers. By doing this, the group is able to kill more monsters per hour than without us. It also can turn a total loss into a winning battle. This is also what gets us killed.

Why do we do this job? Is it for all the "power" we have? No way. It's because it's fun. We run around getting in the monsters' faces, sorting out the bedlam. We balance the control of the situation on the edge of a knife, and yes, the good enchanters make it look easy. Let me tell you, this is not an easy job.

First, Kudos to all the enchanters posting! Most of the threads were well thought out, clearly stated, and offered several viable alternatives. This just supports my contention that those that choose the hard life of an enchanter are truly dedicated to the class!

Second, thank you Verant and all the employees for creating a game that so many people feel so strongly about. It isn't always easy to make people emotional about such a small thing as a computer game.

Now for my comments.

When you are making changes for 'balance' keep in mind the people who actually contribute the most to your bottom line: The part-timers. These are the people who pay the same amount per month, but use the least percentage of resources and time. i.e. Those who pay the highest price/minute of play. (And no, I don't mean ignore the hard-cores)

As a part-timer, I know that it is unlikely I will ever get to level 50. I know I can't spend 8 hours or more camping a spawn for a really cool item, and I can accept that. And being an enchanter, I know that I will spend a great deal of my life trying to find a group that needs me for a few hours. I knew that going in, and so I'm OK with that.

What I'm not OK with is the fact that since the day the game went public, enchanter's have been made harder to play, and less fun. I remember the early days when a pair of enchanter's could go anywhere and do anything. I'm glad that's been toned down, but now it is difficult for an enchanter to do anything but buff.

Here are some things I'd like to see addressed:
- Enchanters are resisted by everything! I group with some necros from time to time and know a couple of them are part-timers. They quickly outpace me -- not due to the time invested, or the risk taken, but due to the fact they can fight white and yellow mobs solo, and red in group. Enchanters get resisted so much that fighting blues solo is difficult, and fighting reds in groups is nearly impossible! Solution: Reduce some of the teribbly high resist rates.

- Enchanters die more than any other class. Every thing we throw has a higher taunt than even clerics doing in battle heals. Once a mob is on us, it is only with great luck that we can get it off us before we are slaughtered. We have no HP and no AC, so we can't withstand punishment for long. Solution: Lower the taunt of the mega-taunt spells.

- Add group faction boost. Many times, I have wanted to aid a newbie or friend in an area where they are KOS, but though I can adjust faction towards myself, I cannot adjust the faction of those in my group. Would that be unbalancing? I don't know, but its always seemed like something the class should be able to do.

- Fix lull series. Everyone knows this is far too dangerous to ever use the way it is.

- Add illusions for fun. Please find a way to add 'illusion copy target'. It doesn't have to have any benefit (including faction), but it would greatly enchance the fun of being an enchanter. If it needs to be restricted to non-undead, that's fine too. And it wouldn't unbalance the game in any way.

If you can't do any of those things, then perhaps you can change some of the outdoor zones to have more single targets at more levels. Then a part time enchanter like me can still play the game, and still solo slowly toward the decent levels. I may never get 'uber' status, but please don't make it so I have to change class in order to enjoy the few precious hours I can get online.

I have a level 59 enchanter with 138 days played. Although a lot of excellent posts have been made, I want to make sure that the key issues that affect me aren't lost amidst the many comments regarding illusions, the uselessness of the lull line, etc.

The greatest joy in playing an enchanter for me is in our crowd control abilities.

Without a good group behind them, the enchanter is arguably one of the weakest classes. With a good group they can make the impossible possible, resulting in misguided calls to nerf enchanters. People see that enchanters are almost mandatory for high level dungeon crawls and infer that as a class we are overpowered. They don't stop to think that that power is offset by many factors. We are nearly superfluous in outdoor encounters. We are often the first to die when things go wrong. We alone among int casters have to focus on two primary stats, not one. Most of our spells are extreme taunts, so we also have to pay attention to AC and use several of our precious spell slots for 'survival' spells such as stuns and rune. We have to manage our mana pool more closely than most, as it's our job to keep everyone alive when the worst possible things happen at the worst possible times.

Disturbingly, Verant apparently sees a group with an enchanter surviving encounters they did not expect and their first reaction is to nerf the enchanter. They artificially tag mobs as unmezzable. They increase a mobs mr to the point where the only thing an enchanter can get to stick is a tash. They set level caps on mezzes without any way for an enchanter to know a mobs level until they have blown mana (and agroed the mob) by casting a lower mez that won't take. They make the same mez spell from different level enchanters not stack to increase the difficulty of raids. At times it seems that Verant regrets having created the enchanter class to begin with.

In my opinion, the 'nerf stick' has already swung too far. For me, the following steps would go a long way to rebalancing the class:

1) If a target cannot be mezzed with a particular spell, have it say so when the cast is attempted. Don't blow the mana. Don't agro the mob. Don't have the failed attempt keep the correct mez spell from sticking. The same could be applied to charm.

2) Don't use the arbitrary application of the giant/unmezzable flags and extremely high mr as a way to increase an encounters difficulty. Surely there must be some other way for Verant to accomplish this goal without singling out the enchanter class. I was chatting in the Eastern Wastes with a lower level guild mate the other day when an orc emissary unexpectedly jumped us. Out of the many spells I cast during the encounter, only tash stuck. My guild mate died and I had to run to the zone with a very very green mob chasing me. Where is the balance in that?

3) Allow the same spell from different level enchanters to overwrite each other. Dazzle freezes a mob for 96 seconds regardless of whether it was cast by a level 49 enchanter or a level 60 one. What reason is there to keep the 49 enchanter from taking over crowd control on a mob initially mezzed by the level 60?

These are the crucial issues for me. A great many other valid points have been clearly and lucidly made by my fellow enchanters, but if only the above three points were addressed I'd feel a good measure of the balance to the enchanter class would be restored.

Like many EQ newbies, I chose a religion for my enchanter when I created him. As a result, most of my illusions are now useless and I have died over 12 times to my religion -- I'm sick and tired of one mouse click consistently ruining my gameplay.

I personally disagree with the way religions work for enchanters (and to some extent, all classes). Currently, NPC's can see through illusions and see the character's religion. This means that if you choose a 'good' religion (such as Tunare or Mithaniel Marr), your character is automatically unable to enter any evil cities and vice versa if you choose an 'evil' religion.

In a strict cost-benefit analysis, the benefits of choosing a religion include:
* Limited faction boosts to one religious facility (ie Temple of Life in Qeynos)
* The ability to use a very limited array (and very useless) of imbued items.

The costs include:
* Global faction declines in evil(or good) cities.
* Unrepairable faction (they hate you too much to be able to easily repair, esp. with evil factions)
* Useless Illusion spells (Ill. DE is almost useless if you chose Tunare or another good aligned god)
*The inability to finish the Epic quest if you choose a religion opposed to the Bloodsabres
* Dying, Dying, Dying... You get the picture.

It doesn't make sense that an entire character can be destroyed because a person clicked on a religion when they were creating their character.

I think one (or several) changes should be implemented to correct this.

* Automatically turn the character's religion to Agnostic after casting an illusion (thus ignoring the religion while the Illusion is up)
* Create an illusion Agnostic spell which switches the character's religion to agnostic and is stackable with other illusions.
* Create a quest which permanently changes your religion to agnostic.
* Create a line of very powerful religious items or spells (other than the lousy tradeskill items) to offset the disadvantages of choosing a religion.
* Or failing all that, change the difficulty level for an enchanter from Moderate difficultly to Very Difficult if a player chooses a religion.
