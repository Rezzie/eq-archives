As druids, before Kunark we believed our purpose was to fulfill the needs of the groups we're in. People laugh and say we have no specialty, but that's just not true. Our true specialty was our powers of balance -we took unbalanced groups and made them balanced enough to work. The role we played most often was group healer, and it's so easy to see why. Wizards, Necromancers, Mages, Monks, Rogues, Rangers, and Warriors all did a lot of damage already, and shamans and clerics were the only other healers. Since healing was in such short supply, and because our fire based nukes were so resisted in 4 out of 5 encounters back then, we played the role of group healer the most.

Our healing was really much too weak, but at least our thorns and dots and chloroplast gave enough of a "nature's heal" and a "nature's defense" that we could still see to our group's needs by putting them all together.

Things changed for the worse in Kunark after 50th level, and Velious went even further. The monsters have so many hp, such strong resists, and do so much damage our powers in every area but our snares and evac spells were completely overshadowed by monster inflation. The only real value we can still offer a group after 50th level is a snare spell (which five other classes already have), and our evacs. Being reduced to basically the group chauffeur is certainly *not* a fulfilling way to play, and the fact that we just aren't effective in our old roles anymore means a lot of the people who would have had groups before Kunark have to spend their time at the zone LFG today and doing without.

Take away our quad-kiting power. It really is overpowered. Take away some of our soloing power if you must, but first and forever a priest should be able to see to the needs of her group, and that's something we lost after 50th level.

The #1 problem with druids is that MOST of the people that play them have absolutely no clue how to. The majority of druids solo up into their 50s, some that haven't even seen groupchat, besides the random port they do. For this reason, in high level dungeons, people are weary to group with druids, as some posters have already mentioned.

Druids aren't needed in groups, and no class should be necissary, but it goes farther than that druids aren't WANTED in groups. At this time, druids don't have anything to offer to a group that other class(es) can.

Snare: This isn't unique to druids, so druids mostlikely won't be invited to a group just because of it.

Heals: Our heals 50+ are a joke. There's definately no way that a druid by themselves can keep up with the heals in a group without significant downtime. The average tank 50+ will have atleast 2500hp.. where warriors at level 60 will have 3000hp with no buffs. Keeping this in mind, a cleric using Complete Heal will heal somewhere between 1800hp to 4000hp, depending on the tank in the group, for 400 mana. So, using Complete Heal, the cleric gets between 4.5hp/mana to 10hp/mana. Another point is, that clerics don't have to use complete heal often in most groups. With a shaman or enchanter slowing the mobs, the cleric would only be using complete heal during bigger fights.

Damage Shields: These are a joke 50+ as well. Mage have better shields than druids, so when grouped with a mage, this is one less thing a druid has to offer to a group. Another point is that 50+(Specifically 55+), every group has either a shaman or an enchanter to slow mobs. Slow totally destroys the efficiency of damage shields.

Regen: This is good, but there is one downfall.. The level 44 cleric spell Celestial Healing used to stack with long duration regen spells, but as of 2 patches ago it now overwrites them. Regen isn't class defining, but it's nice to heal up the little damage left after a battle. Either a cleric has to self the lvl 44 heal, or the druid selfs regen.

Nukes: In their current state, druid nukes are perfect IMO.

Teleports: It would be nice to have Egress points that make transportation easier. Such has having the Egress point in Dreadlands closer to Karnor or Fironia Vie. Druids have no need for anymore teleport spells though.

Succor: This spell is a death trap and doesn't really have much use. This spell should remove the group from the hate list 100% of the time. If you use the spell in it's current state as an evac, mobs that can summon players will 1 by 1 summon the group back to it's death.

DoTs: These are great in their current state, don't change them, we have enough kiting druids as there is.

Solutions: It's very hard to come to a solution to how to make druids more viable in groups without in some way hurting other classes.

I recommend removing the extremely high fizzle rates on all 50+ spells for all classes as this will benefit all. Certain spells, such as Breath of Ro and Superior Healing come to mind for druids, and I've heard complaints from other classes about certain spells being extremely bad for fizzles.

As for damage shields, Maybe they should be scaled to the delay of the mob like Mark of Karn. Instead of doing a fixed damage per hit, they should be tuned to X damage per minute. If we take the level 49 druid single target spell Shield of Thorns, the spell does 25 damage per hit. If a mob has a base delay of 30, it would attack 20 times in a minute. We'll say the mob also double attacks 80% of the time and hits the player 60% of the time. So in 1 minute, the mob would hit a player 22 times(rounded) and the damage shield would do 550 damage in a minute against the average mob.(Somehow this figure seems low, but we'll go with it) If a shaman is using the level 51 slow spell Turgur's Insects, which slows the mob 75%, the mobs new delay would be 53(rounded again). The mob would hit a player 11 times in a minute and the damage shield would do 275 damage. The recommended change would change this spell from a 25 damage per hit spell to a (25*Delay)/30 spell, so in the second case, the spell would do 44 damage per tick. This change is a very small one, and won't significantly change much, but it's a step in the right direction.

As for healing, it would be nice to have a faster casting heal with a ratio around 4.0hp/mana (basically the low end of a clerics complete heal spell used in the low 50s), but the problem with this, is that clerics themselves don't get a heal better than 2.6hp/mana (Divine Light at level 53). A spell similar to Celestial Healing might work, but without giving clerics another heal, it wouldn't be viable to give druids a better heal.

Absolutely no change should be made to druids soloability, they already are extremely good soloers, and this is the main reason that higher level druids have troubles getting into groups. The extremely high number of druids coupled with the extremely high percentage of druids that have little grouping experience makes the druid class not wanted in pickup groups.

It is difficult to be an effective druid in a group, especially groups that rely on you. I can't count the number of times that my guild has invited a druid to hunt with us and watched that druid go OOM from nuking, say OOM and then jump up and start to melee a mob who hits for 100+.

There is a difficult transition for Druids to overcome when facing the reality of grouping dynamics. This should not be removed with greater abilities to make up for a lack of sensitive, group oriented, playstyles IMO.
