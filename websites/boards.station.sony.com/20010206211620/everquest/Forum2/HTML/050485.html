It has to do with the fact that there are very few high level SK's.

The lower levels are rough for SK's and they don't really come into their own until the 30's. The best SK's are the TSK/OSK's... they've got lots of hp's making them the second best tanks in the game. But under the old xp system, they were a massive drain on xp.

At high levels, they suffer the same offensive problems as paladins, pretty much. However, unlike paladins, they don't share spells with the essential cleric, meaning the SK's spells are useful. Groupwise, the SK's spells are very well rounded. Vortex for taunting, FD for pulling, darkness for snare, invis to undead and regular invis.

Anyway, for the most part, those that stick with SK's through the hard years aren't the type to head to the boards and complain. And if they do, like bards, there simply aren't enough of them to get noticed.

Basically, like every other class out there, the SK has it's good and bad points but overall, they've got no glaring problems/issues. Middle of the road, I guess.

First off, SK's are far from 'godly'. We are versatile, not so much as a ranger or a bard, but good enough. Our versatility doesn't really become evident until 30.

Offensively, we are on par with a paladin (same skill caps as us), however, we do get a few spells that allow us to increase our ATK slightly.

Before 50 we are excellent at taunting and can be as good a puller as a monk. After 50, the other melee classes begin to do much more damage than us (rangers, monks, warriors, rogues and sometimes bards) making taunting much harder than it was.

Our quests are few and far between.

Shadow Knights are few and far between.

The one thing that would make most SK's happy is improvements with our abilities on large raids and perhaps a few additional spells. Other than this, most SK's are fairly content.

I for one would like to see hybrids, not just SK's, get the ability to specialize in a school of magic much like our parent classes.
