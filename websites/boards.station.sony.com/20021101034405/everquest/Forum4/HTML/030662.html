Sell Batwings/Spiderling silks/silk threads/bone chips (as already suggested)

Coyote skulls sell to merchants for about 7gp and plague rat tails for about 1pp each, with good charisma.

Depending on your race/class you may be able to cheaply tailor yourself some armor. Its nothing great, but its better than nothing. Also, you may have newbie armor quests available to you--the armor is pretty decent (so far as I've seen, anyway) and you can do most of it just using items you loot from your kills anyway.

Remember: you are supposed to be poor at low levels. I couldn't afford all my spells at each level, and I had to go fishing to buy cloth armor when I started (and I almost lost it while exploring-i.e. lost in-GFay). Money at low levels is a luxury that, fortunately, is getting easier and easier to acquire...you just need patience.

Those large rats you see roaming the newbie zone sometimes drop a "plague rat tail" (nonstackable unfortunately), which sells for over 1PP to NPC merchants each.

Also, many creatures drop things which are specific to them and stackable (mostly items for the newbie armor quests). For example, those red snakes in North Qeynos will drop king snake scales, which is different from the normal "snake scales" that most snakes can drop. Those things can sell to NPC merchants for over 2gp each.

If you keep attacking only creatures of the same name, you can make stacks of such items in no time, and get serious PP when selling those stacks to the NPC merchants.

Good luck!

Also keep in mind where you are in relation to being able to sell those items to other players. For example, if you start in Freeport, you may have a hard time getting to the Bazaar to sell your bounty. In addition, you'll have to stand around as a vendor until you sell them. Say it takes in this case 4 hours to take the boat across OOT, run to GFay, wait on the spires, head up to the bazaar, vendor your gear, and make the return trip. You likely could have gained 3 or 4 levels in that time, and higher levels makes it easier to gain plat.

You didn't mention your class, nor your starting city. In many cities now, there are Newbie Armor quests, which are fairly easy and result in very decent armor. If you are eligable for these quests, by all means they should be your biggest priority, next to leveling.

In addition, many newbie areas (or nearby areas) have some good ways to make money. Without knowing where you are, it makes it hard to recommend a strategy.

A general rule is to be a complete scavenger. Loot and sell everything you can. If a corpse is abandoned by another player, loot it. Many players will just leave items on corpses because they don't want to bother with looting and selling (usually ones with plat already).

Finally, kill any newbie creature carrying a weapon, particularly if it's a staff. You may get lucky and get a cracked staff, that sells for nearly 1pp by itself.

To make money from almost nothing at level 5. Find the oven in your home city. Save any bat wings and fresh fish you catch. Fish and kill bats or shop merchants for wings, or buy wings and fish off merchants. Combine one bat wing and one fish in the oven. Sell in the bazaar the fish rolls for about 5 gold,1pp each. /auction nicely and price competitive so you will sell out in the bazaar fast. Even a level 1 can make some good plats from almost nothing except a little time
