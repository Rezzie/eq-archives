The way I see it - the problem is not so much class balance, but game design.

The fundamental problem is intrinsic in the 6 person group design of EQ.

Post 50+, the worth while mobs have to be killed by multiple groups. If it takes 24 persons (4 full groups) to get to, and kill 1 ubber mob, only 6 out of 24 will get experience. If that ubber mob only drops 1 item, only 1 out of 24 will get a reward. And it is likely that 4 out of 24 will die in the attempt - loosing some XP (even with a XP rez which is not 100%)

So 24 people spend 3 to 8 hours getting to and fighting an ubber mob - perhaps multiple times, hours wasted on CRs, just so 1 person can get an one item?

I miss the days of D&D, when if you killed a dragon there was a chest full of items and everyone got experience and an item or cash.

The solution is to increase post 50 groups to 18 or 24.

Allow group spells to affect all 24 members in the group.

For example, 1 cleric for the same mana cost for buffing 6, now could group buff 24 people. Leaving other clerics use their mana on heals.

What this means is that the more people in the group - the more effecient is the mana use.

** Thus no need to increase an individual's power and upset balance **

Allow other casters to pool their manna to cast "powerful" spells. These spells require several casters linked together because the manna requirements are greater.

** Again no need to increase an individual's power and upset balance **

For example, 10 Wizards linked together to cast a powerful nuke, which would drop that Ubber mob down 2 bubble per blast.

Making this change doesn't reduce the risk or challenge to the game, it only solves the balance problem when confronting the 50+ mobs with out upseting individual balance.

Getting 24 people to show up, stay for 3 to 8 hours, organizing them to play certain roles is still a challenge which no game dynamics can improve upon.

Now these ideas don't eliminate the need to fix certain abilities and spells. A lot of the issues presented in these posts deal with specific spells - which are still issues.

Absor,

After reading through all the posts on Wizard balance issues I have a few thoughts I’d like to share with you that I hope might be of some help.

The balance issues around the Wizard class revolve around one thing. Wizards are a one dimensional class with no defining aspect. This implies that wizards have been unbalanced from day one. Allow me to explain.

A wizard nukes, that is it. An argument can be made that we can also have a second dimension in teleportation, but if you consider the amount of time we spend nuking and the amount of time we spend porting it is obvious that the ports are not a defining aspect of the class, they are just a traveling convenience that we share with Druids. Ports just bring us to where we want to nuke.

Being a one dimensional nuker is not necessarily a bad thing. This can and should be our defining aspect but it is not. The problem is that many other classes can nuke nearly as well as Wizards taking away the unique aspect of the class. Additionally these other classes have other skills and spells that can contribute to a group. At level 29 Clerics get Wrath, a 200 point nuke for 145 mana and Wizards get Inferno Shock a 240 point nuke for 135 mana. The difference between 40 points of damage and 10 points of mana is insignificant when mana points are measured in the hundreds and hit points are measured in the thousands. This is a cleric! They are not even supposed to be an offensive class and yet they can nuke almost as well as a Wizard! Now throw in Mages and Druids. My point is that Wizards may be the “Masters of Direct Damage” but the advantages Wizards have in this area do not outweigh the benefits of a class that can nuke and heal and SOW and cast damage shields and use pets… This is one of the reasons there are so many Druids, and Mages as a class they are very versatile. Wizards are not versatile.

How can this be fixed? It is not easy, I understand why Verant cannot just give us more mana or increase the damage of our spells because that would unbalance the game in the other direction. I believe versatility is the way to go. Add more abilities to make Wizards more attractive, and allow them to contribute passively, like pets and damage shields do. Unfortunately this will require new spells there is no other way. Below are my suggestions…

1. Kill the Al’Kabor line of spells (They are broken and NOBODY uses them) and replace with an Enchant Weapon line of spells. These spells would allow the Wizard to increase the damage of a weapon. This is the flip side of the damage shield coin and I think it blends very well with the Wizards offensive nature. Tanks are a Wizards best friend, about time we returned the favor.
2. Wizards are the “Master of Magic” right? Well how about a magic debuff that wizards can cast on a mob that will prevent it from casting? If cast on a mob all spells cast by that mob will be immediately interrupted. This spell should not last a long time and should have a fairly expensive mana cost. But I think it is a great idea! This spell would not stun the mob it would still be able to melee just not cast for a few seconds. This would increase the demand for Wizards on raids considerably.

These two ideas do not make Wizards over powerful and cannot effectively be used to solo but they add a new dimension to the class that will make Wizards more attractive to groups. The magic debuff does give Wizards a certain advantage over other casters in a duel but they have pets or are much better at melee then us anyway.

I hope you consider these ideas and if not the specific suggestions at least take away the general idea. To balance the Wizard we do not need to add more nukes and more mana, we need to add more versatility.

There are very few times when a wizard is viewed as a good addition to a group. Unlikely other classes which can contribute to the group with some classes being sought almost every time. The fact that the class is not balanced is clear. Just in very rare ocasions a wizard is accepted by a group. Other classes have much less trouble looking for groups.

Not being group friendly, it's true wizards contribute poorly to the group efficiency most of the time, is a consequence of the the unbalance. Wizards were made very mana dependant, to be of use a wizard must have mana. But mana spells even mana drain were given to enchanters which were already insanely group friendly not because of Clarity but because of crowd control. It's terrible to stay most of the encounters just sitting and meditating.

I think it was a flaw in the project giving mana spells to enchanters instead of wizards. Increasing the wizard's mana regen, firepower or mana efficiency will make them more capable of soloing but it will not make them more group friendly. This increase can have nasty consequences as suddenly making the wizard the best soloing class in the game (I doubt it wizards do not have pets). I personally think making them more group friendly would make everyone more happier.

For that reason I can make some suggestions:
-- increased mana regen post certain level as a skill. Perhaps the same effect as not requiring to sit to meditate. Meditating will regain more mana though.

-- a zero cost mana drain spell with very low draining capacity for always making wizards do something in a battle. This will replace medding during battles which is not very fun for the player and will be more acceptable by the group as will show the wizard doing something.

-- increase spell damage by adding wizards level to the damage. This will make wizards able to cast more low level and more mana efficient spells during battles and actually contribute more instead of just being sat and watching the fight, which is very very boring. A 1st level spell will do 67 damage for 7 mana at level 60th. Of course this wouldn't overpower the class and it will perhaps still be more efficient if a 1600 dmg spell is cast and some time is spent medding than casting 25 spells of 1st level. This will just add an option for player to act more dynamically.

-- group harverst.

-- AoE and PB roots which will add limited crowd control ability to wizards. Foes will still be able to attack nearby PCs and cast spells.

-- life cast: a skill used in desperate situations. wizards will be able to use HP instead of mana to cast spells, very nice to evac. (always keeping a bubble of mana to evac is terrible)

-- increased self harvest at higher levels.
