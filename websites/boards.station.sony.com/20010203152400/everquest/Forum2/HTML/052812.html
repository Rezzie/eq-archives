Station Member 		posted 01-29-2001 03:45 PM           
Monks rule. Fun class, I wouldn't change or want changed too much.

One issue is that monk mid-level weapons are almost nonexistant. You have the Fighting Baton, which is a very nice, reasonably common weapon... then almost nothing else. Outside of the rare Trance Stick there's nothing much to upgrade to until you're talking 28/30 Peacebringers and the like. Also, there's an unfortunate lack of nice 1H blunt weapons for monks, outside of the Baton and Trance Stick they don't really exist.

Another problem is that while monks are meant to be better melee damage dealers than other classes, these days the best weapons in the game aren't monk usable. This is an extremely high-level problem mostly, but due to the way EQ's economy works better and better 1H slashing weapons will filter down to the mid levels while monks are still stuck with Fighting Batons. In short, monks need more common upgrades from fighting batons.

Beyond this my only real problem is the completely arbitrary way Feign Death starts working differently if the NPC is above level 32. Is there a purpose to this beyond making me stand up and hit Feign ten times just to be sure?

I wouldn't want the weight limit changed, personally, in fact I'd hate to see it go. The weight limit makes equipment for monks interesting, it means you sometimes have to make tradeoffs and decisions to keep your weight down, which is something lacking from more or less all the other classes. Decisions are a very, very good thing... they're central to any good game and the more meaningful ones that exist, the better.

Absor,

Setting the theme, as I am sure that a very good post from the Monkly-business website will be landing on here shortly (or before me, depending on how quickly I type).

You mentioned that you have attempted to balance a large volume of damage versus a lesser ammount of armor. Overgunning in place of some shields essentially. This makes us define ourselves by our damage content, and is how we try to compare ourselves to other mele classes. We are used to hitting hard and fast, and is what we have come to love.

With recent events, drops, etc things seems to shape up with this scenario with the other mele classes:
Warriors are the damage dealing tanks we all know and love. They can give and take a beating.

Rogues can outdamage a Warrior under the right conditions, but cant take the same abuse.

Monks seem to be about on par with the warrior damage wise, but still lack defensively. This seems to be one of the main concernes we have seen progressing in the game.

Rogues have exceeded us, which is fine. Warriors have also enhanced greatly. However this has placed us without so much of a unique role in the game. We do approximately the same damage, have half the hit points, less armor, and by the way tend to be agro magnets due to our speed. We have all learned to adjust, but we would like our damage capacity to once again offset our armor defficancy.

There are many, tweaks that could conceivabley be done to accomplish this. Some easy, some complex I am sure.

1) Secondary hand bonus

2) Discipline enhancements. Min damage to FK, shorter reset times.

3) Triple hits at half level of our double hits.

4) Questable disciplines for effects like slow, stun, dot, invis. (this dosnt really help our damage but does increace our versatility and reputation as masters of the body)

5) Chain special attacks. A random chance of one attacks spontainiously generating another, lesser one, skill based. (200 skill flying kick has a 25% chance of generating a tail rake, which at 200 skill has a 25% chance of generating a eagle strike which at 150 skill....)

6) Reduction of time between special attacks according to level, similar to disciplines.

7) Increacing our jump height as our safe fall increaces. (Mobility here.. versitality)

Im sure there will be more good thoughts.

Oh, one more.
