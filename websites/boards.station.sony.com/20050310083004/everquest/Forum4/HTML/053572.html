If you are running under load and either dropping or pushing more than .5 volts on any of the power supply outputs, your system can do such things.

If you have the means, check the output from the mlb with a voltage meter, too.

quote:
Weird, I have been having the SAME problem. Checked for MSblaster and it wasn't in "Processes."

I am not an expert on the subject, but you probably wouldn't find many (if any at all) virus' listed in the "Processes" of task manager. After all, if they showed up, they'd be easy to find and disable.

I'd run an AV program if I were you, and preferably one that is on another machine (such as one ran over the net like Norton or Mcaffee have). The reason I say to run one from a remote computer is this...the first thing a virus is going to do is to infect your Anti-virus program. Once the AV Program is infected, it basically won't detect the virus, thus allowing the virus to stick around for a while.
