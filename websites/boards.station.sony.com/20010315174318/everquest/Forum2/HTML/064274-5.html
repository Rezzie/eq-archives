Regarding Healing Breastplates:
I was once told by my father at a young age of the Six P's. I believe it went something like this...

"Poor Prior Planning equals Piss Poor Performance"

Perhaps this is a creed that our friends at Verant should more closely follow. Even since the beginning of Ruins of Kunark Verant KNEW that healing items were unbalancing - Deepwater Helm. Thus I question how they could continually add healing items to the game already knowing they would have to be "nerfed."

After pondering this for a matter of hours, the answer my friends is quite simple: Marketing. People often think of Verant as a place where dreams are made and destroyed. Often times its looked at as a horrible menace that is out to "get" the players. Other times it's praised for its good customer service (sorry not often). However Verant Interactive is, in short, a corporation. A company made to create profits both for Sony and it's employees. Then you must think to yourself... how on earth has it attracted players to this game for so long? Most games people get tired of after a few months.

There are three ways which Verant keeps revenue incoming:
1) Adding Expansions
2) Adding better items
3) Creating a more challenging environment

The first one attracts players because it adds entire new continents full of great items. You notice when RoK first open things like Sarnak Earrings of Station were dropping like crazy. No drop, incredible items that could only be obtained through purchasing the expansion. More obvious is the Plane of Growth. Items better than those that drop off level 70 dragons dropped in this weak, 2 group zone. Now THAT's marketing. How could any player in their right mind not want to spend a mediocre 15 to 20 dollars to get some of the most incredible items in the game.

Well, that covers #1 and #2, how about #3... nerfing these incredible items. Verant knew when they added these items to the game that they would be overpowered, but they saw a chance to increase their revenues so they did it anyway. Now, unfortunately, after several months people have adjusted to this new style of gameplay and exploited it (in the eyes of verant). This is unacceptable, because once people learn to easily defeat monsters they become less and less interested in the game. Just like any game you played as a child, once you beat it, you tossed it to the side. Verant does not want you to toss its game to the side because then their earnings would fall. Therefore they created a more challenging environment by reducing the effectiveness of many items that attracted players to purchased Velious in the first place. This, in the long run, will increase profits and keep more people attracted to the game.

You may ask why I am writing this? Well I am just sick of people saying "Verant Hates druids!" or "Damn you verant, what do you have against necros?!" You must understand that Verant does not hate anyone, they simply love money.

Now, I could sit here all day and break down Verant's motivations, but how exactly would that help with the current breastplate nerfs? It wouldnt. Thus I will offer my own suggestions.

The breastplates that are being nerfed should NOT have had the same effect in the first place. Good god it is 10x as hard to get an Akkirus' breastplate over a Chestplate of Stability. Therefore, there needs to be a hierarchy of spells on these breastplates. Beginning with the most difficult to obtain casting something along the line of a Group Aegolism (if a cleric can obtain a breastplate of such a high level, he deserves to save some peridots here and there) with perhaps a 45second - 1minute cast time which makes it ineffective during battle. The next breastplate down could possible cast a single target aegolism, again with the ineffective cast time. Moving down the line, the breastplates should cast spells that are useful to a cleric but not imbalancing. These include high level stuns, dd's, buffs, and maybe even a new spell only attainable by the breastplate (and no, I dont mean anything like the fantastic new CH on donals bp). In this way, Verant can reward people for attaining high level items, maintain game balance, and reduce the amount of "nerfs" necessary in this game.

In conclusion, I write that I do not resent Verant for their activities. I am sure if I was in their position, and was in pursuit of money I would probably do the same thing. However, I must add that their lack of future planning is disappointing. Verant I encourage you to think ahead when you make the changes on the breastplates, please do not tease us again. Thank you.
