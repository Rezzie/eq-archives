Warriors are, as they now stand, a fairly balanced class. They are not, however, perfect. . . and this is our goal, right? J

The biggest issue I see facing the warrior class at this point is Taunt. The "role" of a warrior is that of a meat-shield. They are supposed to take damage (while dishing out a reasonable supply) and keep the rest of the party alive by focusing a MOB or MOBs attention(s) on themselves. The two tools a warrior has to affect this are damage output and taunt. Since warriors are admittedly not all that high on the melee damage scale (easily out-damaged by monks and rogues, and often by rangers) and since they don't have high agro spells as to the hybrid classes, they are forced to rely on Taunt. Unfortunately Taunt, especially at high levels is woefully inadequate to overcome the aforementioned deficiencies. The recent fix implemented to make MOBs of level 50+ more tauntable helped, but not enough. I'd suggest that whatever change went in needs to be improved some so that warrior taunt is more effective.

Another nice addition would be the addition of an "AE Taunt." In multi-MOB situations it would be nice if the warrior had a skill that would attract the attentions of more than one mob in the vicinity. Of course, this skill would have it's own drawbacks . . . be careful what you wish for.

Another area where Warriors seem to be a little lacking is in the AC and HP department. I am not suggesting that a huge move is needed in either of these areas, but in the high level game, there is precious little difference between a warriors AC and HP versus the hybrids and melee "wizards" (Rogues and Monks) I've even seen clerics with AC numbers within a few percent of a typically equipped warrior. A few percent boost in Warrior HP and AC would really distinguish the Meat Shields in their role. It would also help defer some of the downsides from the Taunt improvements recommended above.

Warrior damage output could also use a boost, again at the higher levels. Logs parsed from large battles (Velious MOBS with 100k+ HP) easily show the huge disparity in damage output between Warriors and Rogues and Monks. I wouldn't ask for a huge increase to match the other melee class damage, but warriors should be able to come closer. This would also help taunt effectiveness.

And finally, there is the Warrior Epic weapon. I know you don't like to read Epic stuff in these threads, but I still think it's relevant to the class as a whole.

The warrior epic is a nice item. In its 2h configuration it is about as good as it gets, even in it's 1h configuration it's very nice, albeit a little slow. The real problems with this item are the relative difficulty in acquiring it and the almost unbelievable fact that it has no haste. . .

Take a look at the other primarily melee epics:

Rogue: Ragebringer - 40% Haste, +ATK, +AC
Monk: Celestial Fists: 40% Song haste, +12 AC and +40ATK - Stacks with Haste Items!! (CoF + Fists is nearly max haste!)
Ranger: 40% Haste, +40ATK, massive Debuff w/slow

As compared to:

Warrior: Jagged blade of war: No haste, No +ATK, No +AC, minor Debuff/DD

This is further compounded by the relative difficulty of the warrior epic as compared to the others. Why the double whammy?

Overall, I think the warrior class is fine. What follows are simply some suggestions, more in line with touching up a few details that have always bugged me.

1) New Skill - In general, the most common complaint I saw from warriors over the life of the game is the absolute lack of anything new to do between levels 25 and 50. Riposte (lvl 25) is a very useful skill, but it's passive, involving no action on the part of the player. Disarm (lvl 35) is indeed an active skill, but one which I would argue is largely useless (more on this skill later). I would propose adding a new skill (sometime between lvl 32 and 40) that would essentially allow a warrior to land a single blow for 4x max. damage (or all attacks landing in a particular round would do 4x max. damage). There should also be a stun component, at the very least a knock-back effect that would disrupt casting. To balance this out, I would suggest one of two things: either the warrior be prevented from attacking for 1-2 rounds after the attack is used, or simply a refresh time on the attack itself (perhaps 6-10 minutes). This is really just an idea to put SOMETHING in that void...by no means should it unbalance things.

2) Disarm - I would argue that this skill is largely useless, more so in the Kunark and Velious expansions. The idea, I assume, was that an enemy with a weapon was more dangerous than one without (generally) and disarming said opponent would be a significant move. I would like to see this examined in detail; to be honest, I never saw a great effect in the original zones unless the NPC was duel-wielding. In Kunark, very few NPC's wield weapons, most of those being magical. The few NPC's who do wield non-magical weapons (let's use the Drolvargs in Karnor's Castle as an example) can be disarmed, but it has no apparent effect on their combat ability. I haven't yet found anything in Velious that uses a weapon that can be disarmed. Surely there is something that could be done to make this skill useful?

3) Bind points - This is more of a general issue, but I think warriors would welcome this. (duh )

4) Equipment - This may not be a balance issue per se, but I think warriors would be interested in the drop rate for Cobalt Bracers. This has been hashed out in many many previous threads; not many exist on any particular server, and the rate (I may be mistaken) seems far less than for the equivalent item for other classes.

5) There is no point 6.

As I said, just a few ideas...overall, I think warriors are fine.

Since so many high level mobs (especially SoV mobs) are very magic resistant, warriors (and meleers in general) become essential for any group. To a certain extent, IMO, this balances out the disparity between casting classes and melee classes in terms of group usefulness/soloing at lower levels. Whether this is perfectly balanced is something that should be looked at and adjusted if need be.

However, the balance between melee classes is something that probably isn't perfect yet. Most melee classes can't solo well, especially when compared to casting classes, so what has to carefully be considered is grouping usefulness. So what is most useful in a group for a melee class?

Damage absorption
Since casters obviously can't absorb much damage, melee classes must do this. HP, AC, resists, disciplines, and block/parry/dodge/riposte skills must be balanced such that all melee classes are not a liability to the group. The efficiency of complete heal also factors into this, since more damage can be absorbed per mana point. Warriors are probably the gold standard for this. Also, due to this primary role of warriors, the taunt cap should be increased to make this more effective, and uncouple the damage dealing ability of other classes from the damage absorption of warriors.

Damage dealing
Since melee damage is very important in high level encounters, this must also be balanced between melee classes. Weapons, weapon skill caps, riposte, and disciplines must be balanced, again so no class is a liability to the group. Rogues are probably the gold standard for this, followed by monks, warriors, rangers, and the other hybrids. However, there is a large disparity (IMO) between the damage dealt by rogues and other melee classes. If damage dealing is part of the role of monks and warriors, I think it should carefully examined whether the drawbacks to rogue damage (i.e., lower damage absorption and defenses) are balanced with the dramatically lower damage dealt by monks and warriors in regular grouping situations.

Also, it must be noticed that every single piece of equipment added to the game can shift the balance dramatically. Ragebringer is a good example of this, since it increased damage of those rogues that have it by a significant amount. If rogues were balanced pre-Ragebringer, then post-Ragebringer they aren't any more. Or monks and fighting batons at mid-levels. Or warriors and wurmslayers. It's something to be very careful about, adding items.

I've been watching the SK talk, and part of the problem there is that an improvement of the SK's defense (and, I'm sure, the Paladin's, when their talk begins) would be an intrusion on the Warrior's role as the ultimate defense. One way to give the SK and Paladin some wiggle room to improve their defense would be to improve the Warrior's defense in tandem. But how to do so without making Warriors completely uber? I came up with two ideas.

Idea #1
As far as I know, all races and classes experience a penalty to AC when attacked from the rear, and as far as I know this penalty is equal (I have not seen anyone suggest otherwise or do any particular study on this). What if we remove or reduce this penalty for the Warrior class? It would only make sense that Warriors would know how to guard their back better than other classes. If removing the penalty entirely is too much, then it could simply be reduced, say by 50 percent, or added as a discipline.

Idea #2
All Ogres are immune to melee stun from the front. What if we extend this ability to all warriors? Right, the Ogres are going to be outraged! To make them happy, give the Ogre Warrior immunity to melee stun from the back as well. Okay, this does seem like a pretty large improvement to the Warrior class, and I can understand if this gets a flat no. But a gift like this to the Warrior class would let you give an equally good gift to the SKs and Paladins.
