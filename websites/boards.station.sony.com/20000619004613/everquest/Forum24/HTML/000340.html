So you want to learn how to play an enchanter eh?
I have not been to the board for awhile so hopefully you will look back upon this note and read the advice I am about to give. An enchanter, if played correctly, is indeed one of the most powerful pvp classes. Just like any class though, it requires you to know what you are doing and excellent timing. Some things to know about enchanter pvp:

1) Deal w/ their pet first by mezmerizing it. They cannot pumice it in a duel.
2) Never cast a dot in a fight. You need to be
able to control the tempo of the fight at all times.
3) Don't use your pet either for fear of breaking mez on your victim or not being able to cast AE spells.

With these 3 things in mind, you only have to remember one other thing...interrupt, interrupt, interrupt. The creators of our class gave us a stun on our DD for a reason. This is exactly the case. If you interrupt them, they can't hit you ( assuming they are a casting class ). At level 20, I would advise you to use chaotic feedback to interrupt and any of the color series spells. To begin a battle, you should either tashani or mezmerize first, followed by the other. If its a melee class you might want to use eye of confusion. Then use your most powerful DD which will break the mez. If they are a melee class, keep switching your DD's. If it is a caster class and you see them begin to cast, run up close to them and cast color flux. This will interrupt them and give you enough time to cast sanity warp before they get unstunned. They will cast again....interrupt them with chaotic feedback...etc. If you are feeling lucky and winning the fight and think you can outblast them, try to cast sanity warp and hope they don't interrupt you. If not, then continue the color flux, sanity warp or whatever other DD you have combo until they die. In most battles I have, I rarely get hit unless it is by a dot or a pet. I simply beat them by not letting them cast on me and capitalizing with stun/dd combos. Just make sure you watch your mana carefully. It's a balancing act and takes some time getting used to it. Btw, might want to pick up a stein of moggok if you don't have one already. Forget the dragoon dirk or any weapon after level 20, you don't need to melee to interrupt. Good Luck.
