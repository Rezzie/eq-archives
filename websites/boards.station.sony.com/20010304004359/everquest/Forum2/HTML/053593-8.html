* Offensive Power (Grouped)

One of the reasons why Rogues are balanced is that their significant damage potential is only realised while in a group: their target must be facing away from them to be able to use backstab. When balancing Monks, Verant will no doubt want to ensure that relatively more offensive power is given to grouped Monks than to solo Monks. Here is one such solution: add a new special attack at level 40, "Critical Strike".

The idea behind Critical Strike is that the Monk takes time to position herself in such a manner that they can strike at a sensitive area of their opponent, thus inflicting significant damage. However, this attack takes time to prepare - the Monk spends 7 seconds ducking and weaving to find just the right opening. If the enemy focuses their attention on the Monk during this time, inflicting even a single hit, the attack opportunity is lost. It is this interruptability that makes the skill useless for soloing.

Note that there is a precedent for a skill that takes time to "kick-in", namely Instill Doubt.

At level 50 it would be nice to see an upgrade to Flying Kick, as many have suggested. I would expect that an opportunistic attack such as Critical Strike would deal more damage under most grouping conditions though.

* Shedding of Aggression

Monks lack an effective means of "shedding aggro". Feign death does not often work as it should in doing this, and it makes *NO* roleplaying sense to use it in this manner (any reasonably intelligent opponent would see through FD if it was used more than once!)

Please, give us a skill that makes a little more sense!

* Hand-to-hand "Weapons"

More gloves, or even quests to raise our inate H2H ability would be very nice. This would enable those who *choose* to do so to be effective martial artists once more.

Isketh
