I put up this post as a reply, but I feel the only way to get my point out is too start a new thread.
Patches have to be done. This game is just too big to not be constantly fixing bugs. Due to the games popularity, it is also expanding. Every single game you have ever played is constently updating and patching their game as well, so this isn't unique to EQ. I remeber that when Diablo II came out, even before it hit the shelves a patch was already up online to fix the bugs that the testers had just discoverd.

Now I live in the PST, but since I am a vampire (well not really, but since I keep virtually the same hours as one, I think I should get some of their cool super powers) I am effect by patches much the same way Eurpean or Asian players are. With that said lets consider this for a moment.

At your job, would you rather A.) Do lots of work or B.) Do less work, get paid the same. Hmmmm, tough choice here. I would choose B, I am sure you would too. Customer Service Reps. for EQ are the same way. Let's look at some numbers, shall we...

During patch times, there are about 400-600 people on per server. These same 400-600 people are constently effected by the patches. Right now they are on their 4th patch in a row. Now those players on at non peak times want at least 1 of these patches done at peak times, so the same people arn't being effected by downtime. The average amount of players per server at peak time is between 1200-1800. So, lets say that 10% of people actually contact customer service and complain about downtime (I know 10% is not acurate, but for this purpose, it is an easy number to work with). So once a day, Customer service has to deal with 40-60 unhappy customers per server. Over a 4 day period that is 1600-2400 complaints per sever. Now lets say they moved one of the patches to peak hours. Now on that patch day they have to deal with 120-180 complaints per server on 1 day (keep in mind there are some 37 servers). That is 3 times the work normally for customer service reps on that one day, PLUS, they still have to deal with the normal complaint flowage on the other 3 days. Seems to me that is more work for them. If I had there job, I would not want that, so I would try to get the patches arranged for the least amount of work for me. Wouldn't you do the same.

Now some of you will argue, that it isn't good customer service to do it that way. The same people are getting disgruntled night after night, eventually they will loose customers right. Well first of all, if all of you are as addictided to the game as I am, you will suffer through this week, maybe gripe a little, and then move on with both my real, an Norrathian life. If you are one of the very few who are turned off by the constent patches, well, keep in mind that EQ is probably the most successful online game to date. Hence people have all made there money already, and the very tiny amount of customers they might loose just really doesn't phase them. It would probably cost them more money in labor to handle the extra work-load if they did a patch during peak hours, then the very few of you who sadly, will quit EQ because of it.

Now the refund issue. Many people say "fine, do a bunch of patches, just give me a refund for my time lost." Well, that refund, prorated would probably be about $1.00 but, the $10.00 a month that we pay is to keep the servers up and running smoothly. So by paying them $10 a month you are saying "I would rather loose playing time so that bugs are fixed and unfair advantages are nulled, and a fun role playing enviorment in maintaned" People, you pay the money for them to do these patches, so don't count on a refund.

Before we get all in a hissy about a situation that isn't too our liking, perhaps we should look at it from the othersides prespective, rather than our own selfish one.
