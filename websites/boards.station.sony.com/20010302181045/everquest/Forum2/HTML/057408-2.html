Here's something interesting. For those of you "in the computer field" you should recognize the Jargon File. Here's its definition of bug:

"An unwanted and unintended property of a program or piece of hardware, esp. one that causes it to malfunction."

The full definition can be found at this link:
http://www.tuxedo.org/~esr/jargon/html/entry/bug.html

Who decides what's unwanted? You might argue that the user does... but since the user didn't "intend" anything as to the program's function, "unintended" definitely refers to the software maker, and therefore in this context so does "unwanted".

Was the pathing issue unwanted by Verant? Definitely. Unintended by Verant? Certainly.

Also, the use of the term dates back to 1896... hardly modern slang. I think it's possible to accept an official definition, from this dictionary or another, without asking the individual user what they think it means.

It is very likely that pathing was used in the game to make host/client interactions much less intense. If collision detection etc were done for every object in the game the network traffic in even the smallest zones would be intense; not to mention the CPU horsepower required to do these checks.

So, I suggest that the problems you see are not a bug, or a design flaw; but rather a design compromise. This compromise has some definate positive points, and some negative. However, the game designers may well have looked over the pluses and minuses and said... if we simply add an in game "rule" that you may not use these minuses as an EXPLOIT, then the design benefits from only the pluses, and does not require the extra network traffic and or CPU horsepower to correct the minuses.

In otherwords... VI is working within a constraint that allows many players on different levels of computers to enjoy a game. In order to do this they required the addition of a simple rule; don't abuse pathing... You are not willing to follow this rule it would appear. This means that you are not willing to work with them to enjoy the game, or to allow the rest of us to do so. Get over it.
