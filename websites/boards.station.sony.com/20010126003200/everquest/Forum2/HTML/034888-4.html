I have a shaman on two servers, one high teens the other 50s. I have read a lot of flack from the shaman community about class nerfing, and I have read a lot of flak from Verant about shaman community whining (displaying rude behavior?). Central to the theme is the vision. My perception is that the shaman community is communicating how the vision is changing, while the verant community is steadfastly defending their changes as retaining vision. What then, is the vision?

MY OPINION - I knew it as a class that was among the few that could solo, though the least effective of the few. I knew it as a class that offered to the group a unique and powerful support role - offering world class slows and dots (offensive) with some healing and buffing ability (supportive). Here's my point - we could solo, but not well, and certainly not against Kunark grade mobs. So our strongest point was in group play where we were considered the best all-around caster class. This is what I thought the vision to be.

These are many of the complaints from the shaman community which they feel have changed the vision of our class:

Took away Charm.
Took away 49 damage shield.
Put level cap on Befriend Animal.
DoT nerf (reduced damage for nothing in return).
Targor's and 49 Strength nerfed for Kunark coding.
Rage line nerfed for Velious coding.
Alchemy was BROKEN for 8 months.
Cannibalize line degrades in efficiency, rather than improves as reward for increasing difficulty in obtaining higher lvl spells (can 2 is difficult kunark quest and can 3 is dungeon drop only). This is particularly disturbing - the spell gets worse as the series progresses, rather than better, but the risks to obtain the series is increasingly more difficult. I have a hard time with this one, personally.
Indirect nerf of shaman quickness line when haste was repaired, not compensated.
Spear of Fate - a fiasco.
Cultural armor - lame.

We could never heal as good as a cleric, could never solo as good as a druid, nor could we ever do something as definitely desired by other classes as resurrection or teleportation. Without a crystal clear class advantage, it was our all around nature, coupled with the best slow, which gave us our identity in groups. What I am trying to communicate to you about the vision of the shaman class is that this identity IS the vision, and that the changes identified above have continued to erode this identity. What is in question now, when Verant mentions the term vision.....is just what is the vision? What is the role intended for this class? It continues to be a very poor solo class, and it has been made abundantly clear verant's global vision does not favor solo gameplay. So no future in that aspect. We are left then with group play, but what do we offer?

We do not tank well.
We do not haste well.
We do not heal well.
We do not snare.
We do not resurrect.
We do not evac.
We do not regenerate mana for any other class, and do not do it very well for self.
We do not offer a viable summoned tank in the form of a pet.
We DO offer buffs which unfortunately have a very minimal effect on combat performance, and are now replaced by weapon procs anyway.
We DO offer a marginally better slow spell.

The core of our outrage, and why we seem to scream so much, is we don't know what we're supposed to be anymore. What is our identity? What is was, is gone now. We are no longer the best "all arounders." Now, we are a class left wondering what our contribution is supposed to be to a group, in a game environment which is decidedly group oriented. Be the group for a second, and try to envision why you might want a shaman....why would a group ever say, "we have to have a shaman!" For what reason would they say that?

I suggest it was once a perfectly made class. It once had a clear vision, and lived up to it. I suspect overconfidence that the Shaman class was "finished" allowed the chipping to begin...how could it hurt such a well balanced class? Enough chips have been taken off to destroy what was once the vision of this class. I believe there is no longer a vision in existence.

I challenge Verant to define the vision of the Shaman class as it exists today. I challenge Verant to tell me why other classes would find a Shaman to be a desireable addition to their group? I challenge Verant to face this issue, which has been the core issue in every stream of vitriol which accompanies every new shaman nerf.....I challenge Verant to not say "lets keep this to the specific issue at hand" and, finally, address the overall issue that it is THE SUM OF ALL THE CHANGES which is the issue, not just this one. I challenge Verant to stop resting on their laurels, stop blindly believing they have some kind of viable vision still intact, and to EVALUATE AT A VISION/GLOBAL LEVEL the Shaman class.
