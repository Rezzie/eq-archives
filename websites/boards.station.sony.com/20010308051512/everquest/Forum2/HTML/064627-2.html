Here's a solution that will make both camps happy:

Leave the current logic in place. But extend it.

When you hit "Give", the items go into the NPC and another player can continue to multiquest if desired. Leave the 'should we allow multiquesting' question for another day.

But here is the idea which would help reduce the pain of exploring quests with rare components:

Add a new button, called "Show". This button will cause the NPC to respond with either "I am interested in <ITEMNAME>" or "I am not interested in <ITEMNAME>" for each item in the trade window.

Finally, if the items in the trade window and the NPC's inventory would result in an item, have the NPC say "I will give you a <REWARD ITEM> if you wish to trade with me". This way, people will know whether they have all the items needed or not.

This way, players can tell in advance if the should give the item to the NPC for questing or potential multiquesting.

This would be a great upgrade to the quest system. If a player has an item and they are trying to give it to an NPC, they deserve to know if the NPC even wants it. If the quest is not described well enough it's not exactly fair to just keep the item, but this lets the player determine for himself if the item is used in the quest.

Thinking back to my early days of EQ, I recall how I eventually became paranoid of doing quests without a full spoiler. After losing just a few rare drops to messed up quests I don't think I ever went back to 'experimenting' on quests of any real value or difficulty.

It would sure be nice to remove that fear.

It's possible to do it all.

When items are turned in, have the NPC hold on to them. So you can give food/weapons/etc. to a NPC and the NPC will keep them.

When items are turned in by other people, those items are thus kept, so multiquesting works.

If you give the wrong items, and get no reward, say a trigger phrase to the NPC (perhaps "I'm done"), and the NPC will tell you that you didn't give him what he wanted, and give you back your items.

Currently when you hand in items, there are 4 fields in the database for that NPC that store what you've given. Just the item ID's are stored, not who turned in the item. What I would do is add a column for the player ID that handed in the item, so the NPC always knows who gave it what item(s). Alternately the current ID field could be used, if there are enough bits to represent both the item and player ID's.

When someone says "I'm done", any items they gave to the NPC are given back. If two people multiquest something, they both have to say "I'm done" to get their items back, if the quest didn't work.

The extra field to identify the player is needed, because otherwise someone could say "I'm done" while two people are trying to multiquest, and get back items that they didn't turn in. Or get weapons/food/whatever off an NPC that someone else gave it, or get a NO DROP item that someone else handed to the NPC, meaning that players could transfer NO DROP items at will. Adding the player ID solves all of these problems.

From what I know about how the current quest system works, using this method not only allows more flexibility in doing quests, but it doesn't require nearly as much code.
