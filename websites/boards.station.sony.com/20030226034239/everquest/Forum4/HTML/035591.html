quote:
The era of tradeskills being a novelty has long passed. There is serious gear and the opportunity for serious money to be made with tradeskills

Most of the examples you cite require grandmastery (and often mastery in a second tradeskill as well, for example brewing to make the required temper). Until and unless you achieve that, most tradeskills are a time and money sink.

For example you can smith banded mail at around a skill of 100, but to whom can you sell it? Banded (and better, like Blackened Alloy) drops like crazy if you know where to go. The price you could get for it from players, if you could even sell it, wouldn't cover the cost of the materials (not even considering failed combines).

There are a couple of tradeskills (like jewelrymaking if you are a 'chanter) that can pay
somewhat at lower levels, but the cost of skilling up rises exponentially as the required materials become more and more expensive (e.g. blue diamonds, enchanted platinum bars).

Frankly the only money I've made via tradeskills has come from farming pelts and silk, making leather padding, and selling it in the bazaar for 20pp each. And that "trivials" somewhere in the 30's.
