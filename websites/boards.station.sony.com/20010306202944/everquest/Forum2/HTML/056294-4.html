Here is the problem.

Scenario 1:
39th+ Cleric grouped with 60th Warrior.
Ratio: 1 mana for 7-12 points using Complete Healing.

Scenario 2:
60th Shaman grouped with 60th Warrior.
Ratio: 1 mana for about 2.3 points using Superior Healing.

Conclusion: In this scenario, a 39th level cleric is a better healer than a 60th shaman. This is due to their best healing spell being up to five times more powerful than the healing spell shamans get at 53rd level.

Q: But can't the shaman can cast Torpor?
A: No. Look at the casting time of torpor and it's serious negative effects.

Q: But can't the shaman slow the mob?
Answers:
1) No. Most 55+ mobs cannot be slowed.
2) Many mobs are spellcasters. Slow isn't going to help any against them.
3) A single resist means the death of the shaman unless there is due to uncontrollable aggro.

Q: What about cripple?
A: PvP spell only. No noticable effect on mobs.

Q: Can't the shaman cannibalize to have a larger mana pool?
A: No. When a shaman is actively healing a tank they are chain casting Superior Healing in an attempt to keep up with mob damage.

Q: So what if we give shamans another healing spell to fizzle at 55th level, similar to Remedy?
A: You've upped the shaman healing ratio from 2.3 to 2.5. Nothing really has changed. The imbalance still exists.
