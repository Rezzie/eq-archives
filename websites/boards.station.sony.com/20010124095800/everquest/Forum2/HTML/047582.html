On Monday January the 9th of 2001 my Clerical Epic Weapon Quest was at the final battle stage where I was to present a shimmering pearl to Zordak Ragefire in Solusek B (Nagafen’s Lair) on the Bertoxxulous server and defeat him in both human and dragon form.

To reach this stage took over 150 man hours of myself and my guild. The hardest point was the assault of the Overking in Chardok which took 6 full groups of level 50+ people to safely complete and obtain the piece I needed there.

So I handed the pearl to Zordak and he attacked me, as other clerics had warned me he would do. We engaged Zordak in his human form and slew him easily. But he then respawned in his dragon form "Zordakalicus Ragefire" and a second battle was upon us. Because we had prepared properly and brought sufficient force we succesfully slew Zordakalicus. But there was one very large problem … the person who landed the killing blow , a warrior named Auratus, was linkdead due to the number of players and lag that had occured and as a result the corpse of the dragon disappeared (or poofed as people call it) without me being able to loot the heart I needed for the last piece of my quest.

This was a great tragedy as I’m sure you can imagine. Everyone was very upset, myself included. In fact I seriously pondered quitting EQ at that point. Its not that this one weapon meant so much to me, but it was that so much TIME was invested by SO MANY people that it was earth shattering. Imagine if you worked for 4 weeks and expected a paycheck at the end of that period but due to an accounting error you did not get paid? This is the feeling .. all the risk, the time, the dedication, the camping and staring at one spot on the ground waiting for that rare monster … was lost in a moment of bad coding.

I /petitioned the problem to the GM and got a response from a guide named Raea. Raea informed me my server GM was not on that night and they themselves could not help me. I should re-petition the issue on Wednesday between the hours of 2pm and 11pm PST to get some assistance.

I logged off very heart heavy but determined to try, I felt sure that the GM would respond in a helpful manner. This was a bug, not something we had done wrong.

I logged on at 2:15 PST on Wednesday and /petitioned my issue. I had to reissue my petition 4 times .. as I did not get a response until close to 7pm PST (I have read on the official message boards that petitions will expire from the queue in 2 hours so I refreshed my petition once every hour and 45 minutes to be safe).

I did not mind waiting 5 hours for my answer because much to my pleasure the GM after telling me they were ‘looking in to the matter’ appeared and put the pearl into my trade window. I clicked "trade" and began to type a thank you but they had dissapeared. I tried to thank them anyways but they were gone and I felt bad that I could not thank them. That pearl represented another chance at redeeming 150 hours of my guilds’ time.

So on Friday the 12th of January 2001 we met again in Solusek B. We buffed and meditated, and gathered about Zordak Ragefire yet again in Nagafen’s Lair. This time he would be mine, this time I would complete my quest!

I wont go into length because the story is simple .. this time a guildmate named Zarux went linkdead and got the killing blow of Zordakalicus Ragefire and the corpse poofed yet again.

Everyone in my guild was shocked. That this could happen twice in a row was utterly unbelievable. I think half the guild petitioned at the moment, every last one of us was highly upset. Every last one of us was intimately effected by the effort that had been destroyed by this bug. I too /petitioned the issue and we waited for a GM to assist us.

We were quite lucky (we thought) to see the same GM who had restored my previous pearl by the name of Rhuyai appear to help us.

However Rhuyai told me that he would not replace my pearl a second time. Rhuyai claimed that people were exploiting the fact that Ragefire was poofing and having other clerics loot the heart, and then asking for a new pearl.

I have no idea whether that is true or not. But I asked Rhuyai to check the server ‘audit trail’ or server ‘log’ as they call it. I have read on the official Everquest message boards that some things can be verified by GM’s in the logs. Rhuyai did not respond to that request, he simply restated that he could not reimburse me a second time.

I was upset, but very polite with him. I asked how I could escalate this issue. He gave me the eqmail@verant.com address, and we parted ways.

I then related this information to my guild, and they were not pleased. Every last one of the 30 people we had there that night had spent many hours helping me do that quest .. and quite a few of them sent additional /petitions.

Rhuyai appeared again to us, and this time my guild took up the argument with him. I think Rhuyai thought I had not explained to my guild what he had explained to me .. but I had. They simply did not like that answer (neither did I to tell the truth.) My guild was very demanding.. and brought up many valid issues ..including why could he not check the log and verify that no one had looted any dragon loot. Why could he not verify that Zarux was linkdead and had gotten the killing blow, and therefore know that there was absolutely no possibility of there being a corpse?

We had not exploited anything and my guild made many strong and heart felt arguments in this regard.

Rhuyai then summoned the assistance of Lead GM Axgoreth. Axgoreth gave us the same answers, but tried to do so more authoritatively. Axgoreth again would not verify any information in the log, and claimed that they were unable to verify anything in the log.

The root of the problem lies in Verants code. And I believe they know about it. Let me explain why this happens.

Have you ever seen a Goblin and a Sarnak fighting in Kunark? Sometimes you just run up and there they are duking it out! Well when one of them wins the corpse of the one who lost ‘poofs’. It dissapears. The reason for this is obvious.. if it did not then you the player would get ‘free loot’. There are also probably a few other reasons, such as reducing server lag by not having NPC corpses on the ground that were not player generated.

Well have you ever had a group mate go linkdead during a fight? I’m sure you have. Well if someone goes linkdead during a fight.. and they are getting hit.. they don’t stop fighting do they? No instead the will keep fighting the monster, and if the monster runs away they chase it.

When a player goes linkdead, the server turns them into an NPC until they are disconnected from the server.

Now this doesn’t happen _that_ often normally. But when you get 5 groups of people together and fight something like a Dragon who is using Area Effect breath weapon spells.. well the fact is its almost certainly going to happen. I cant remember a single dragon fight I’ve been in that someone hasn’t gone linkdead.

Verant put linkdead PC’s on the same code as NPC’s. When Auratus and Zarux got the killing blows vs the Epic Dragon we were killing the same code caused that corpse to dissapear.

This is not my fault. Its not an exploit. This is code done by your company, Verant Inc.

I have followed the steps, I have taken the time. I have spent my hours and hours of time and dedication to show I am ‘worthy’ of the reward. My guild has spent months of time building the proper people and levels and equipment under Verants rules to be capable of doing this.

And finally when we reach the final battle because Verant did not write separate linkdeath code the corpse dissapears?

Its apparent to me that this is a ‘bug’. Now Verant has said in the past they *will* help recover items from known bugs. At the same time they have also said they *will not* help with Epic Quests.

This leaves me in a boggle. You see .. I’m a customer. I pay for several things with my subscription. I pay for a game that works, I pay for customer service. I do not pay Verant to be treated like a liar and scam artist.

When you have knowlege that a problem occured due to your code, why on earth would you make a policy refusing to reimburse it?

You can claim that this problem is due to players internet connectivity problems, However I would beg to differ. I call that negligent coding on your part. It is entirely within Verants powers to give the kill to the group that did the most damage. You have made the coding choice to cause corpses to poof. Players do not make the choice to go linkdead from information overflow during a battle so hard it takes 30 people to complete.

Yes we all turned down every option, turned off every filter we could. But people still go linkdead.

I see there are posts on the offical EQ boards form other servers having the same exact problem. Ragefire’s corpse is ‘poofing’. I see also posts on some of the Cleric related fan sites that his corpse is poofing. I am not alone in this. I am not the only person who has spent hundreds of man hours to complete a quest that you have ‘designed and tested’ (or so you claimed) and have empty hands at its final stages.

Many people were upset tonight and a few of them said things that upset people will say.

Your GM threatened to disband my guild if they had more than 1 person petition a problem in the future. He will claim it is not a threat, however when you tell us "I can have your guild disbanded if you continue to have multiple people petition a problem in the future" we all consider that a threat.

He used the rationalization that this only effected one person. We the customers, who pay you a collective $296 dollars a month who were on that raid tell you that this problem effected every single one of us there on that raid. I as a customer reserve the right to complain to you for customer service if I so desire. If my guildmate feels this problem effected him as well how on earth can you tell him he is not permitted to petition?

I waited 5 hours on Wednesday to get a response from my petition, my guild was very upset and I would guess 15 or so people petitioned on Friday. I would hope that your customer service staff would fully understand the impact a bug of this nature would have on us. I would hope they can click ‘delete’ on a few messages in the queue for the same event and bear some tolerance for a large group of customers who are upset. The time that we as players have I invested in this quest is exponentially larger than the time your customer service team has spent trying to resolve it. The time we as players have spent trying to do this quest is also larger than the time you the developers have spent testing it, or coding it. (And by we as players I mean the player base)

If 30 people bought bus tickets to Reno and the bus didn’t show up I can guarantee you all 30 of those people would complain at the ticket counter. And you can be sure that the ticket salesman wouldn’t threaten to cancel their trip if they didn’t stop complaining. You owe us customer service. You made this game, now please do your duty.

Please treat me like a human being, please respect my feelings. Please also respect the feelings of the 30 people who came on my quest to help me and who also spent many hours of their time on that same quest.

I don’t want to attack you in this request, however I do demand that I be treated properly. Please take what I am saying in the spirit it is intended and that is a firm but polite request for proper customer service and reimbursal of an item that was destroyed due to a bug in your code.

I sincerely hope you address the issue with Ragefire’s corpse poofing. My guild has 2 other clerics who are now ready to perform this step of the quest but they are terrified to even attempt it due to the two failures that I have had.

Yes that’s right.. we are terrified to play your game.

Can you please fix it? Can you please also reimburse me?

Please.

Respectfully,

Badina of Bertox
