Gambit,

Your train of logic with the words "wizard" and such is wrong. I do, however, believe your name was changed in error. As to the copyright issue, Marvel would own the copyright to the look and feel of the character, not to the word or name "Gambit". Also, copyright means nothing, even in a court of law, unless the copyright holder can prove loss of income due to the violation.

While I agree in principle with the naming policy, in practice it has two major problems:

1. Long-time characters have established an "identity" (with all that entails such as reputation, sense of self, etc) with that name; and

2. The rules *cannot* be objectively applied.

Gambit, yours falls into the first category. Changing the name of anyone with more than 20 or so days played is bad juju. At 70+ days played on my main, I would cancel my account immediately if his name was ganked. When I'm playing "Ysgwyrthyr" (my main), I *am* "Ysgwyrthyr". My brain has constructed a set of autonomic reactions around him, his abilities and his relationships with others. As a troll, I dumb down my language to Troll-Speak and this is now automatic. It doesn't happen when I play my alts. If his name was changed, I would find it very hard to adjust to the character due to the separation. The human brain is powerful like that.

To protect the innocent, I'm not going to provide any actual examples of the second point but here are a couple of hypotheticals:

How many people recognise the names in the following list?

"Tezcatlipoca"
"Mictlantecuhtli"
"Paracelsus"
"Temujin"
"Sakhayit"
"Tchort"
"Perun"
"Zorya"
"Glasya"
"Bure"
"Rofocale"
"Baalshebub"
"Furfur"
"Bael"
"Beelzebub"

All of the names above were real people or names from real world myth, half of them from the Christian myth... erm... religion. At what point is the line drawn? Is it just main characters from popular media or does it extend to bit parts? Who determines whether a book, comic, movie, etc is popular enough to be considered as a violation?

Thus, many of the rules are too subjective to be workable.
