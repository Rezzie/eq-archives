Baven,

While I understand your sadness in having friends you have known for 2 years log off that final time, imagine the depth of the sorrow of the person who left and how bad it must have gotten for a person to abandon a "persona" they have lived for 2 years. It is never easy for a dedicated Cleric (or any class I imagine) to hit that "Cancle Account" button and leave behind the friends we make in this virtual world.

You say Verant is systematically eradicating Clerics... I happen to agree with that. I AM a Cleric and in the past few months Verant has done more to damage the playability and enjoyment of being a Cleric than I ever though possible. I can honestly say I never ever thought Verant would so totally destroy a class at the higher levels and certainly never a class that was so excrutiatingly boring to play in the first place. It takes a special person to be a good Cleric... Verant has seen fit to make sure even those individuals can no longer tolerate these massive changes in the name of the "Vision".

My very first toon was a Druid (oh so long ago) and while I found it intersting it simply wasnt my style. I settled on a Cleric soon afterwards and while the class had many restrictions I found the trade-off to be fair. I could no longer port, or SoW, or Snare... but in return I was a better healer at higher levels, better buffer at higher levels and eventually I would be the SOLE class that could return experience. Yes, it was a very narrow place Clerics had... but once again the trade-off was acceptable.

Then came Kunark... I eagerly awaited for the new spell lists to be announced as did most caster classes.... I was slightly disappointed but overall I was happy with the "balance" of the classes additions. While Kunark wasnt terrible for Clerics as a whole it wasnt quite what most of us had hoped for... some of our spells werent working correctly (Death Pact that works 1 out of 300 casts is a good example) but we the Clerics were MORE than willing to deal with it.

Kunark was the beginning of the end of Clerics tho... shame most of us didnt recognize it earlier. Remember Mark of Karn? I dont mean the watered down version we have now but the "original"? It actually gave Clerics something to do besides the sit/heal/med routine and we LOVED it. We could now positively contribute to the group with a spell that wasnt a heal or a buff... Clerics rejoiced !! Then someone decided that MoK was "too powerful" since Hybrids didnt get as much benefit from it and it was nerfed to the point that the ONE spell Clerics were happy to get now sits unused on page 50 of most spellbooks.

The MoK nerf was the beginning of the end for us.... Verant had focused their attention on Clerics as a whole and began re-evaluating the class spells and armor spells as a whole. Had we known than what we know now I'm confident Clerics would have spoken out much louder.

Then came Velious.... while I was mildly dissapointed with the new spells in Kunark I was horrified at Velious.... DRUIDS get a better group buff than Clerics did?!?! Since when were Druids the "Masters of Group Buffs"? Group buffing had always been Clerics and Shamans domain but now not only was it being handed to Druids they were being given BETTER buffs than we had. PotG is a level 60 Druid group buff that adds a hitpoint regen AND a mana regen component.... NO CLERIC HAD THAT. The "best" buff Cleric has is Aegolism... lvl 60 SINGLE target 700 mana 10 plat spell that added no regens... while useful for very high end encounters (provided the mob doesnt dispell it) for the "average" player PotG is far superior to Aeoglism. PotG mana regen stacks with Clarity, Clarity 2, Group Clarity and Bard Song Clarity... who whouldnt want that?

The Donals BP fiacso broke the hearts of many Clerics... an item they had worked so very hard for was destroyed beyond anyones imagination... it is no longer a desirable item for Cleric. Supposedly THE Class defining armor in Kunark is now nothing more than a cruel joke.... those Clerics who have the misfortune of owning one of these are reminded daily of how poorly Verant has treated them. If my guild tries to give me one I'll simply hand it to the first newbie Cleric I find... perhaps THEY can find a use for it because I certainly cant.

The Plane of Growth and quested BPs are much the same as the Donals.... useless beyond anyones comprehension. A 1 minute casting time on a spell that heals 1200 hps over 24 seconds? Whats the purpose? I cant cast this spell to powerlevel a lowbie (spell is too powerful for your intended target).... I cant possibly keep a tank alive with this unless we're fighting orcs in WC... and even without any form of Clarity I can med back the mana faster and just cast it myself. These BPs had NO IMPACT on the high-end game but Verant devastated the Cleric class as a whole in the name of "balance". Once again a lot of Clerics are left with a useless item they get to look at on a daily basis to remind them just how badly Verant has treated them.... and for those people who hunted in PoG they now have the added bonus of ruined factions on top of it.... talk about adding insult to injury.....

Heal Agro .... well... I'm not going to discuss just how bad it is in the high end game... lets just say I lost 2 bubs of exp in lvl 59 from this "balancing"... and yes... I KNOW how to manage agro... it's how I stayed alive BEFORE the change.... after the change it was irrelevant... Healing=Dead Cleric.... perhaps Verant has seen the light and will change it back but somehow I doubt it. I suspect it will be tuned back slightly and called "fixed" and the Dev Team will pat themselves on the back for a job well done.

Class Talk Threads... hmmm... Clerics asked for something to remove the monotony of sit/med/heal we got Hate List Agro Nerf. Clerics asked for "something... anything" to ease the often long and dangerous runs we do to go Res fallen friends.... we got an Undead DoT (laughable at the level obtained and utterly useless 4 levels later). Clerics asked for Group AC Buffs.... Druids got 2 of the Cleric core spells (yeah... this one baffled me too). Clerics asked for some form of self-invis or at the very least for invis potions to be stackable... Wizards got Invis to Undead (another one that baffles me).

I suppose the Cleric Epic will be next to be "balanced". Lets see... a 5 min casting time and just for fun lets give it a set number of charges... or perhaps Verant will see fit to reduce it to Revive. Considering this is the LAST item Cleric have left that hasnt been "balanced" I imagine it wont be long before we see a cute little message from Absor explaining to us WHY this MUST be balanced to save the game.


Verant... if you're listening... just give Druids Complete heal and the lvl 56 Res spell and be done with it. Stop chipping away at us and do it in one fell swoop. It will be far less painful for Clerics as a whole than this ripping us apart a little bit at a time.
