Actually, there are 5 items a druid can wear from the Plane of Fear that gives +wis. Vermiculated Tunic, Vermiculated Leggings, 2 Vermiculated Bracelets, and the Dweomervine Garland. That's not to even mention the Staff of the Earthcafter from Cazic Thule.

Verm Tunic adds 13 wis. So far, there is no better druid tunic for +wis yet discovered.

Verm Bracelets add 9 wis each. So far, there is no better druid wrist item for +wis.

Verm Leggings add +5 wis. So far, the only better leggings are the Eldar Spiritist Leggings, which add +6 wis.

Dweomervine Garland adds +5 wis, the same as the reed belt, and about as rare. The only waist item bette to date is now the Mucilagenous Girdle, which is +6 wis.

Fear gear adds 41 wis. If you are a half elf like me, that adds up to only 136 wis, if you maxxed out your wis during character creation. Wood elves have, what, base wis of 122 or something? That would make 163 with Fear gear. Add in a Testament, +10 wis (173), a Paw, +9 wis (182), a couple plat jasper rings +6 wis, and you have 204 +wis. That leaves all your other slots open for items to enhance other stats, or add mana, or HP, or just for AC. It's REAL easy to hit 200 for a high level wood elf, and then deck yourself out in other stat based or effect based gear.

Currently my half elf is at 197 wis. When I get the Verm Tunic, I will start wearing my Verm armplates, even though they don't add any more wis, they look darn cool. All of my stats, except charisma are over 100 all the time (assuming I'm strength buffing myself, that is). Ony reason I won't wear the Vermiculated Crown if I get one is Half Elves look silly in chain hats.
