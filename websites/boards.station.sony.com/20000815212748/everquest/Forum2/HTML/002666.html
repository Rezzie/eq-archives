It has come to my attention that people are petitioning GM's to complain about invulnerable healers. For those not on the PvP team servers, an invulnerable healer is one who heals people during a PvP combat but cannot be attacked himself because he is either (1) on the same team as the people who wish to attack him, or (2)(more likely), is at least 8+ levels higher than the combatants and therefore cannot be attacked.
I have searched the FAQ and the PNP and could find no mention of this as a proscribed tactic, yet I know of people who have been warned by the guides for doing this.

Case#1
I am running through a zone when I observe a PvP fight. A dwarven palladin is laying the smack down on a poor dark elf. I check the health on the DE and seeing he is about to die, I heal and SoW him. This allows him to run away. Both combatants were in their low teens, whereas I am level 27. Am I going to be warned?

Case#2
My guild runs to Nek forest and starts laying waste. We have 6 members, 5 of whom are level 20, and the sixth is a level 40 cleric. The cleric is only there to heal the other 5 and no one in the zone on the opposing team is high enough to attack him. Is my guild in trouble?

Case#4
A group of Evils is attacking a group of Elves. Everyone in the fight is of equal level. However, the darks have brought with them a cross-teaming Elf, who heals the Evils, and can't be attacked by the other Elves since he is on the same team as them. Is the cross teaming Elf going to be banned?

While I understand the frustration of some players who think they might have had a chance at winning a fight if not for the presence of invulnerable healers, is that really all that much different from being jumped by five friends of the guy you think you are having a one on one fight with?

In my humble opinion, there is no such thing as an INVULNERABLE healer. He might be invulnerable TO YOU or your group, but what is stopping you from sending a tell to a group of high level friends the next zone over to come and fight off the troublesome healer?

Allowing people to petition for this matter will open up a whole can of worms, which will be a burdensome nightmare to everyone. As a mid level shaman, am I supposed to con everyone on the opposing team in a big fight just to make sure SOMEONE can attack me, and am therefore not invulnerable. That is madness.

I do not roleplay 100%, but Cazic-Thule damn it if I'm going to let a fellow lizard die at the hands of some stunted dwarves ONLY because I'm too high level to get attacked. I can see it already:
Drax: You just stood there and watched me die
Carnifex: Sorry old chap, I conned all the dwarves and they were below the PvP level limit.
Drax: I am a fellow broodling. You should have helped me.
Carnifex: Here's a SoW for you to make you feel better on your corpse run.

I would like from you, Abashi, a definite clarification on this matter, and so would many others. I do feel empathy for those on the short end of the stick, but no one said PvP combat would be fair, or easy. It is one thing to camp spawn points, or zone edges, but to insist that everyone involved in a fight be within 8 levels of each other is sheer madness.

I am, and remain,

Carnifex Rex
of the Scaled Mystics

OTOH, it should be possible for the code to identify the source of the damage being healed. If the source of that damage is a PC on the same team as the healer, the code could do one of two things

2) Prevent the healer from healing the damage when it was caused by a PC from his own team.
The first option is preferable, because it allows for more interesting PvP.

Out-of-range healers can be handled the same way, except there it would be better to opt for just blocking the healing in PvP situations; making a level 50 cleric attackable by a level 8 warrior is virtually the same as being Invulnerable .
