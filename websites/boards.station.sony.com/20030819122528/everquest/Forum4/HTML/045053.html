Greetings, all!

Oridnarily I'd give this a pass, since I've started (and replied to) several similar posts in the past, but it looks like this is going from a "there really ARE newbs out there" to a "--and they all SUCK!" subject, and I have a few comments to make.

Sadly, I would tend to agree with the second assessment, just due to my recent experiences over the last few months. The vast majority of people I meet and help (asked or volunteered) seem incapable of expressing any sort of appreciation; it's as though whatever cut-throat games they come from have drained the ability right out of them. Either that, or when they do realize that you are trying to help, they suddenly (and for days afterwards) bombard you with every question they can think of and every request for money and equipment that crosses their minds. As another vetran player recently said to me when I replied to an /ooc asking for help, "You're braver than I am--I never reply to requests for help, it always leads to a ton of parasites following me around for days."

Another form of "newb" I'm seeing more of is the Killer Newb from Hades, who has just enough of a handle on the game to be convinced that he is doing everything right, regardless of results. While running one of my lowbies on Stromm I was grouped with a bunch consisting of 2 clueless newbs and 1 KNFH. After suffering numerous unneeded trains in BB (how in the gods name to you train a horde of patrollers UP from the snake ledge when, presumably, you never left it but were innocently waiting for a pull?) I made a few suggestions. The clueless newbs said nothing, the KNFH instantly jumped on me about my "attitiude" and accused me of thinking they were all newbs and didn't know how to play.

Having had a snootful of this crew, I agreed that they were, they didn't, I did, and then disbanded. The KNFH proceeded to send a slew of obnoxious tells; I made no reply and eventually /ignored him. The only thing impressive about the whole encounter was that at least one of them had figured out how to send /tells.

That attitude seems to be growing, slowly but steadily. Kindly meant advice is responded to instantly and aggressively with mild-to-major abuse, as though this were not just a game but instead a colossal pissing match where you can't for a minute let anyone else think you aren't in complete control.

So, for those who still want to help the newbs, do--many of them are worth the effort and make a fine addition to the friend list. But be aware that there are now cleverly disguised newb-shaped land-mines waiting for you, and don't be surprised when one occasionally blows up in your face.

To those who berate the vetrans for scorning the newbs, keep firmly in mind that (aside from the occasional case of actual rampant egotism) the majority of vets have that scorn because the newbs they encounter inspire it. Polite newbs = happy vetrans. It's a simple equation.

Also, to any newbs who are reading this post, go back and read it again--and notice how many people who replied here don't want your money or your loot, just a polite request followed by a polite thanks.

Do any of you remember when you were a real-live-true-blue newbie?

I started the game with 3 other people and one computer. We got in the game and spent half our time trying to do stuff and the other half trying to read the worthless manual. Not to mention arguing with each other about what we just did. Our native tongue is English (no comments, please). I can't imagine how it would be if we had to have a dictionary handy to be able to able to communicate.

I can just imagine the player to be looking at the manual, looking at a dictionary, discussing with friends, and not being quick responding. Remember that in real life, a large part of communication is nonverbal -- which you don't get in EQ.

Just tonight, I got a /tell from some lvl 17 druid asking about the druid port spells (I was on my druid at the time). I tell him where they are found and say I was hunting with my guild in Umbral Plains and not able to help. He said that was OK, he would wait.

Three hours later, he is lvl 18 and we finally break up. I port him from GFay to WC. Of course, the vendor is missing, so I petition. Eventually, the vendor is put back, he gets his spell, and I buff him up (losing an hour to what should have been 5 minutes, max). Half the time, when I said something, it took about 2 minutes to get a response. I could imagine him looking stuff up, or just overwhelmed.

He did thank me in the end, but that was icing. Even though I have played EQ for years, I still remember my first week. I know how overwhelming it can be for a newbie.
