Chickenlegs has made many valid points. The fact that they are steeped in sarcasm and cynicism is not altogether without reason.

Many longstanding monks have heard the song and dance about class balance for years now. They clung to their class because of loyalty and hope that it would return to the unique class it was before. I think most are beginning to realize, or have already realized, that monks are basically warriors who aren't allowed to wear good armor.

I think that there are people who would say you make a stronger point by avoiding hyperbole and sarcasm, but both those tools are also sometimes very useful and illustrative.

The monk class was never an Uber class. The pre-kunark monk class was somewhat of an enigma to most players. Only those that demonstrated the fortitude to stick through the first 25 or so levels of monk-hell learned that monks were a unique and very special class. They weren't so much better than any other class as unique. The gaming experience for a monk player was very different than that of other classes.

Kunark obliterated that. The differences that used to define a monk were either eliminated or magnified to the point of becoming punitive.

1. What used to be a lack of options in equipment became a deficiency in equipment quality. Monks used to have to work harder to get the equipment they needed, but once they had it, they were happy because it was good...on par with that available to others. Now monks just have to put up with either never having the equipment that is more readily available to their tanking bretheren, or watching it go to someone's twink because it's so rare, and tradeable.

2. What became a different style of fighing that focused on dishing out damage rather than absorbing it, became an obsolete sub-standard form of tanking. Monks used to be balanced, but now warriors are the consummate tank, dealing the most damage because they are the only ones who can stay in the fight for any length of time.

3. Our class-defining skill, Feign Death, is non-essential to groups now. It doesn't perform the function that it was originally used for, because of mob memory and hate list adjustments. What used to make a monk a desireable, and sometimes necessary, part of a group is no longer there.

Now that the word is out that warriors do nearly the same damage as monks, have more hitpoints, and have higher armor class, as well as being able to interrupt casters more readily, why on earth would anyone want a monk?

The answer used to be "Feign Death". Is it the answer anymore? NO!

The answer now is, "Who needs a friggin monk.../ooc group looking for a warrior."
