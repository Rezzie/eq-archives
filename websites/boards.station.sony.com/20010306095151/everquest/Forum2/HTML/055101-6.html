I completely support Zadkiel/Bodi's post made earlier, as well as Xaoshaen's post (top of page two of this thread). I would also like to summarize my own thoughts.

1) Flying Kick -- This move demonstrates absolute mastery of a monk's fighting technique. To connect with a Flying Kick for only 3 damage is a disgrace, and really hurts our ability to deal damage. Once this skill hits 200, it would be nice to give it a minimum damage equal to our level, just like the Rogue's backstab.

2) Spell interrupts -- I've read many suggestions on this subject. Attatching a stun or interrupt to Flying Kick would be a bad idea. The attack is already very powerful, and one interrupt every three rounds is not enough, IMO. Attatching an interrupt to a weaker, faster attack such as Dragon Punch/Tail Rake or Eagle Strike would me more useful, and would give us a reason to keep at least one other attack skill trained. It would also force us to choose between high damage and spell interrupt in the midst of a combat situation.

3) Skills -- All three pure melee classes get a set of disciplines between levels 51 and 60. In addition, Warriors get Tripple Attack post-50, and Rogues get Double Backstab post-50. What skills do monks get post-50? None, as far as I can tell. An increase to our weight limit does not count as a skill. It is entirely useless, considering every monk has had to put up with it for 50+ levels and has gotten used to it. Monks need some new skill beyond 50th level that reflects their mastery of hand-to-hand combat. I like the idea of chain-linked special attacks, suggested several times on this thread. It is original enough that we're not just mimicking warriors with our own tripple attack, and it gives us a reason to keep ALL of our specials maxed.

4) Armor and Weapons -- We can all stop pretending that monks are supposed to be effective with minimal armor. This hasn't been true since the opening of the game. If it were, monks would have a much higher naked AC to make up for the dismal armor selection we have been given. Our naked AC could be increased to make up for this, or we could be given a larger selection of *good* armor sets. And no more pieces like that garbage you call Shiverback. It should be something worth working for. Also, I am a firm believer that a monk's primary fighting method should be his/her fists, and we should be able to stay competitive in the damage-dealing field with hand-to-hand fighting. Our fists' base damage should be increased beyond level 50. If not, then there should be some other benefit to sticking with H2H that late in your character's lifetime. It isn't right that those who abandon the true nature of a monk be rewarded with increased combat effectiveness.

These are my thoughts on the monk class. I would hope that you and the design team will find them reasonable and give them some consideration when the time comes for class balancing.

Thank you

Faeandor
