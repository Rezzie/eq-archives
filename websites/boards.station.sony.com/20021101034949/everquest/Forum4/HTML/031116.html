One note about spell casting and water: If the target is in the water, you tend to have to be in the water as well for a spell to land.

That's why levitate wouldn't stick. You were lucky to get the Lesser Succor to work without jumping in the water yourself. Next time just jump in with her and then succor.

I have never heard of a guard helping anyone out of water. I cannot imagine there is logic in the game to facilitate this action. Way too complex and compasionate for the guards in our fair lands.

Usually there is some way out of water, sometimes an underwater tunnel to a zone such as in ShadowHaven under the bridge to PC. In SH there is the water under the overpass to the monk/intelligence casters guild area that you have to swim through an underwater tunnel to a room where you can drop into a bottemless pit that zones you into PC. In SH there is also the proverbial pool of death around the spires. It is easy to get out of that pool if you are a medium to large race. I have even seen dwarves get out, at one of the 8 magic spots by the corners, sticky wall spots. Each of those spots is about a body length from each of the four outside corners of the pool.

I have no experience with the pool in Sanctus Seru. I know that for lesser succor to work properly you should be in the water with the person. There is some sort of problem if you are on land and the other member of your party is in water. It will almost always leave the groupmember who is in the water behind.

I shall have to go look at that pool and see what is up with it.

As for the SH pool of death around the spires... I made a gnome and could not get out of the pool. I can get out easily with any elf or human I make, but not with a gnome. I did not try a dwarf but I did see a dwarf manage to get out once.

There is a deathtrap in the backside of CrushBone too for small races. There is a pit back there where a dwarf I knew fell in between some rocks and was trapped for many hours while I was trying to find someone to come cast levitate on him. Any larger race cannot get down in that spot but a small race character will fall in and be unable to get out. Amazing.

Now I am a druid and I use lesser succor a lot in these situations. Although I was unable to pull a guy out from under the ground in Dawnshroud Peaks the other day. I think he was about 100 units below the ground level. He could see me and so could invite me to join his group but I could not see him and lesser succor did not pull him out. He had been there for days waiting for a GM to come pull him out. This was on Morrel Thule.
