YOO HOO, ABSOR!!!

Now that I have your attention (I didn't want to get lost on page 7)...

I'd like to point out a few things that weren't covered on the preceding 7 pages, AND suggest a way to implement "the best of all worlds" features, PLUS it's the easiest to code (I'm a programmer, trust me ).

1. Removing multi-questing is guaranteed to break a number of quests, since a lot of quests were implemented with it in mind as a necessary feature. For just one example, it would break the Paladin Soulfire quest (which in turn would break the Paladin Epic Weapon Quest, since it relies on the Fiery Avenger quest which relies on Soulfire). Why? Because to do an early, necessary stage of the quest, you must give four LORE Drom's Champagnes to the drunk Tyklar Renlon in East Freeport. The only way to do this is to give him one, hit Give, then quickly buy another from the nearby bartender, hand that one over, repeat repeat. This only works because of the multi-questing feature.

Remove the multi-quest mechanism and you break the Soulfire quest, not to mention lord knows how many others like it. Do you really want to review ALL quests in the game to see which ones won't work anymore if you remove multi-questing (and then redesign them to work again)?

2. As others have pointed out, a lot of upper-level quests become sane only when able to be multi-quested. If you remove multi-questing, you're going to either have to re-balance a lot of high-level quests, or in effect simply put them out of reach of a lot of people (who aren't going to be happy :mad .

3. People have tailored their actions up to this point using the assumption that multi-questing works (and will continue to work). A lot of people have had friends pick up No Drop quest items for them, or picked up items for friends, relying on the multi-quest mechanism to make this work for them. For one example, the Paladin Fiery Avenger quest requires too VERY hard to get books, from Vox and Nagafen respectively. To complicate matters, the books have to be combined in order to get the next item in the quest chain deep in Neriak, where most Paladins are extremely KoS. So quite a few Paladins have Dark Elf or Enchanter friends loot their books for them, planning on having their non-KoS folks help them by doing the Neriak stage of the quest. Even more, a Paladin that died during the dragon raid may have had a friend on the raid loot the book so it wouldn't poof before the Paladin could get a rez back to the scene, again counting on multi-questing to ensure that this was viable. So right now there are a lot of VERY rare, VERY valuable Paladin quest books resting in the hands of friends of Paladins who had to work a LONG time to get those books at all. Removing multi-questing would suddenly destroy those Paladins' books, in effect, since they'd become almost impossible to use, unless the friend holding the one book so far was dragged along on all raids by the same Paladin, to loot the second book if it happened to drop. Again, people would NOT be happy if that were suddenly made necessary by a game change.

4. The vast majority of the people yelling "yeah, nerf multi-questing!" are doing so because they're unhappy about the one or two quests (*cough*JBoots*cough*) where multi-questing makes camping/farming worse than it would be otherwise. There's a simple solution for that -- fix the poorly designed quests that lead to this sort of behavior. "Fixing" those few quests by destroying multi-questing is using a sledgehammer to swat a fly on your forehead. As others have pointed out, you can fix the JBoots farming by having Hasten Bootstrutter hand out a combinable container that does the "boot construction" (in exchange for the cash he currently wants as part of the quest list), instead of having him accept the quest items himself and then pass out the boots. Nice and easy.

5. The best enhancement actually is the easiest to program, AND addresses everyone's concerns, AND makes the most RP sense anyway. Here it is:

a) If EQ were a real world, offering the wrong quest items to a person wouldn't cause him to him swallow your items and spit in your face, it would cause him to him look at your offerring, laugh, and say "I didn't want *THAT*, stupid..." So make NPCs in Norrath do the same thing (a bit more politely).

b) When you hand ANY items to an NPC that is not a full set of the items he wants for a quest (this INCLUDES things like giving meat to bears and so on -- ANY "not a quest" pile of stuff), a pop-up box appears saying, "<targetname> is not sure what to do with your offerring, are you sure you want to hand over your goodies?" Then the user can hit "Yes" or "No". "Yes" would then result in the items being given, followed by EXACTLY the existing behavior regarding given items (including multi-questing, etc.). Answering "No" would result in exactly the same behavior that "Cancel" does on the current Give window.

c) The only extra coding this requires is a "post-Give-button, pre-acceptance" check to see if the items constitute a full set of quest items for that NPC. The acceptance and quest coding remains unchanged.

d) It also simulates the real reaction you'd get if Norrath were a real place inhabited by real people, who would say "huh, are you sure?" if you offered them stuff they weren't asking for.

e) Why have the bears and such give the same "huh" box? Because otherwise you open the door to people hannding random cheapo items (e.g. bottle of milk) to every NPC in sight, in order to (too easily) figure out which are quest NPCs and which ones aren't. You fix this by making ALL NPCs give the same "don't know what to do with" box when offered less than a full set of expected quest items.

f) It is guaranteed to leave existing working quests unbroken, and guaranteed to leave multi-questing functional.

g) Ideally, it would also give the "huh, are you sure?" message if the quest would have failed due to poor faction. Aagain, this makes RP sense -- if you were not liked well enough to be issued a quest, a quest-giver would just sneer at you or not even look your way when you tried to give him things for a quest.

g) It would also be nice (but not necessary) to add an eqclient.ini setting which would disable the check (thus reverting to the behavior already in place today) for those people who were annoyed by getting a query box every time they tried to dress the banker up with a mandolin.

I think this suggestion addresses everyone's concerns (except those who feel that the JBoots quest needs to be fixed so it's not so heavily camped/farmed, but that can and ought to be handled separately).
