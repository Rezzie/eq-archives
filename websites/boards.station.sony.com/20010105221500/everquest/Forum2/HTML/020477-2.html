In dungeons like Chardok, Howling Stones, and Sebilis, this is a critical issue.

I am a level 54 warrior currently dual wielding a jade mace and dwarven sap, with a FBSS. I often find myself in the same boat as other posters -- chasing mobs around a room vainly trying to get their attention and get them off a caster or cleric. Despite having very speedy weapons, there is often nothing I can do to get a mob onto me.

An example -- in Sebilis Wendesday night we pulled an Illis frog (level 50+ summoning mob). Our Paladin cast one (1) stun on it, and the mob instantly frenzied on him. For the next 3 minutes or so our cleric did nothing but chain cast remedy to keep said Paladin alive. I, with my speedy dual wield combo, my sash, and Anya's Quickening, could do nothing to budge the mob off the two-handed sword wielding Paladin. I even tried /disc furious (10 seconds of all strikes being criticals) with no luck. Four hundred + points damage in 10 seconds, combined with taunt, and the frog would not budge.

Many of the enchanters I group with are well aware of this and often prefer a Shadow Knight or Paladin in our groups for their ability to debuff or stun to pick up a mob's agro. Given that either class lags behind me by 75-100 AC and 300-400 HP at the same level, this is a sad commentary on how effective the taunt cap makes warriors in tanking against level 50+ mobs.

The mention of changes to the damage bonuses awarded to 1-handed weapons has me even more concerned about this problem. Now, in order to taunt consistently, warriors generally choose the speediest primary hand weapon we can find (jade mace, lamentation, etc...) -- the number of times you attempt to hit a mob plays a large role in how much it "hates" you. If the damage bonus awarded to these speedy weapons in the prime hand is lowered, it will make our taunting problems even worse. Warriors will be forced to choose between speedy weapons that have some chance of taunting a mob, and damaging weapons that aid a groups ability to actually kill it.

I very much hope that Verant will examine this issue and make changes. Taunt should be made MUCH more effective against non-god/dragon/boss mobs that are level 50+, and the caps for warriors should be raised. Taunt should be made vastly more effective against all creatures if it is indeed the case that speedy prime hand weapons have their effectiveness lowered.

The following is the only post I know of from Gordon regarding this issue:
_____________________________________________
"Are there any plans to raise the taunt skill of warriors, or any taunters for that matter?"

Not plans to increase the skill, but possibly to increase its effectiveness at higher levels. Taunt purposely reduces in effectiveness on high level MOBs. At that time, all MOBs of that level were special encounters. Now that there is yard trash above 50th level, we've recognized the need to re-evaluate this skill.

- Gordon
----------------------------------------------

So, yes, he said it's working as intended...at least as intended before Kunark. I hope they re-evaluate this soon, I hate this as much as everyone else does.

For those of you under 50 commenting about its ineffectiveness at your level, that's nothing compared to the situation with 50+ mobs. Taunt DOES work as intended (albeit frustrating at times) for all but the 50+ yard trash in high level dungeons. Based on the quote above, it appears they'll be changing how certain (common high level) mobs react to Taunt, rather than raising the Taunt skill. It's probably more work for them than to do this mob by mob, but that way would leave the "working as intended" part unchanged.
