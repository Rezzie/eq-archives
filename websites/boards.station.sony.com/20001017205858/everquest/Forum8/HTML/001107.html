I'm hoping you will read this and possibly take into consideration what I am going to say.

I applaud the new changes to smithing, I really do. However I am seriously dissapionted in the discrepancy in the effort/reward needed to create them?

I have 2 smiths 1 dwarvern, 1 Iksar, and a friend whom is a Barbarian smith. Of these 3 smiths the barbarian and the iksar have already made almost full sets of their Cultural armors. My dwarf has yet to make one peice. Why? B/c unfortunately for Stormgaurd armor a dwarf must not only put forth an imbuded ruby, but at a minimum of 3-4 Janciths per peice of armor. These gems first off are not remotely cheap the rubys run 120 - 180pp and the Janciths rarely drop from extremely high lvl monsters, and usually sell for quite a bit from PC's.

Now it is my understanding that these armors are ment to be rare, and I thought that after camping temper components for well over 15 hours and only reciving enough for 5 tempers that this was how you intended to stem the flow of new cultural armor into the game. However after speaking to all the Dwarven NPC's and figuring out that I needed to add an imbuded ruby to my normal brellium armor recipe to enchant it I found that much to my suprise I needed to enchant the armor as well first and that each peice. Would cost well over 500pp per attempt and thats a bracer!

Now seeing that I wonder is the armor really worth the time and effort a smith puts into it? I estimate for a full suit of Dwarven brellium armor it will cost a smith at least 100+ hours to gather components, and well over 5 - 8k in gemstones and brellium alone.

I am willing to put forth this effort for the status and prestige of creating this armor, however I fear that it is truely unbalanced in cost & effort vs reward. Not many will want to use a 2k (to craft) peice of armor that has the same AC as their easier to obtain quest armor with only a small bonus to stats.

In addition, you need to reasses IMO the cost to create these items... Each race should for the most part balance out in time/cost for their armors. No one race should have to pay significantly more or spend significantly more time to create an armor. If one race has cheaper components, then their temper item drops should be rarer... If their temper drops are rare then the price should be a lil cheaper... If thier imbued gems are extronidnarly expensive then their enchantment component should be cheaper to compinsate. And all races should have to go through all the same steps to create their armor. Honestly it is not fair to the rest of the smiths out there to give 1 or 2 races armor that is extrondianrly easy (cheap) to make in comparison to the others.

These are my concerns and I am sure the concerns of other players as well. I hope that you folks can realize what we go through to play this game, and reward our time and efforts accordingly.

Jasperr
Cleric of Brell
