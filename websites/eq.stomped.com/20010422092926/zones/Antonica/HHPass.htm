    Highpass Hold

    Level Range:      15 - 30
    AllaKhazam Site Resources:

    • Highpass Bestiary
    • Highpass Equipment List
    • Highpass Quest List
    • Highpass Strategy Discussion
    • EQ Atlas Map and Guide to Highpass Hold

    Description:   This zone is treated as a city zone for the purposes of binding, so a non magic user can get bound here.  This is a festive little zone.  It's got a couple of inns and taverns, a weapons shop, a merchant who sells pottery sketches, another merchant who sells pottery supplies, and a number of merchants who sell alcoholic drinks.  If you're a potter, Highpass Hold is one of the most underrated places in Norrath to do pottery.  It's one of only three places where you can do single-zone pottery (i.e. where you can buy all your supplies in the same zone containing the wheel and kiln), and there are three wheels and three kilns in the zone.

    The biggest attraction of the zone for players in the appropriate level range, however, are the gnolls and orcs who spawn at opposite ends of the zone. The orcs spawn near the exit to the Kithicor Forest, in a fairly tight cluster of spawn points.  The orcs have a crazy fast respawn rate, and the orcs that spawn range from the lowly conscripts (around 13th level) to the mighty orc fanatics (22nd level).  A good group can park themselves near the spawn points and rake in the XP and loot. The gnolls spawn near the exit to Eastern Karana, and their spawn points are much more spread out.  Moreover, they don't spawn nearly as quickly as the orcs.  So folks who are hunting gnolls have to do a lot of running around.  The gnolls also don't have as much variety as the orcs, ranging from the basic "a gnoll" (11th-12th level) to the gnoll flamepaws and gnoll champions (both around 19th level).  Both gnolls and orcs occasionally drop ringmail pieces and the occasional bronze weapon.  They also occasionally drop scalps, which can be turned into the captain at the appropriate gate (the Captain near the gnoll spawn points will take gnoll scalps, and the Captain near the orcs will take orc scalps) for up to 5 gold.  This is also one of the few ways known to improve your faction with the Corrupt Qeynos Guards and the Freeport Militia.  The best time to hunt the orcs and gnolls in this zone, especially for melee classes, is during off-peak hours when the competition for kills is scarce.  During peak hours the zone becomes a free-for-all, with everybody attacking the spawns and to the victor go the spoils.  Kill-stealing is rampant during peak hours and whining about kill-stealing is frowned upon. During off-peak hours, things are a little more friendly, with people helping each other out, rotating kills, respecting the kills of others, and so on.

    In the middle of the zone, in a cave across from the keep, are three named smugglers that have been the death of more than one unwary traveller.  The toughest of these is Dalishea, who is 18th level.  Her partners Greth and Brenon are 16th and 13th, respectively.  They sometimes drop bronze weapons.  They spawn slowly, and therefore aren't camped very often.  Also, a little-known part of this zone is the smuggler's cove.  If you dive into the water near the Tiger's Roar Inn, you can find an underwater passageway that leads to a secret cove filled with smugglers and one corrupt Qeynos guard.  The smugglers where either green or red to a 16th level character, so I'm unsure of their level.  The smugglers were, at the time, "glowering dubiously" at me, so I (an Erudite Magician) wasn't KOS to them.  Which is good because they've got a kiln, a pottery wheel, an oven, and a forge in their cove.

    Traveller's Note:  On the older servers, this zone is pretty well camped on both the orc and gnoll sides, which means that low-level characters can survive the trip through the zone without significant difficulty.  On the newer servers, where there aren't enough players high enough to camp the zone, Highpass is a death trap for low level (and even medium level) characters.  If you're travelling through this zone, do a "/who" command and see how many players are there.  If it's 15 or more, you're probably safe.  If it's less than 15, you'll probably either want to take the Runnyeye/Beholder route or become invisible.  (Scouted by Desdinova)
