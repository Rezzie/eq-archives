    Eastern Karana

    Level Range:    12 - 25
    AllaKhazam Site Resources:

    • Eastern Karana Bestiary
    • Eastern Karana Equipment List
    • Eastern Karana Quest List
    • Eastern Karana Strategy Discussion
    • EQ Atlas Map and Guide to Eastern Karana

    Description:   Best known as the pass through on the way from Qeynos to Highhold pass, Eastern Karana is a big zone, about half of which consists of a canyon and a ridge above it.  The canyon leads to a dead end.  The ridge leads to Highpass hold.  There is also a smaller canyon which leads to Beholder.  No monsters spawn on the ridge, and it seems that most of the monsters spawn in the western half of the zone.  The zone is chock-a-block filled with stuff that will kill you to death at early levels and will still be providing a challenge when you're at level 20. There is also a little village with several shops.  For travelling through the zone, there's a Knight (Sir Morgan) and his Squire (Squire Wimbley) who will escort you to the ridge leading to Highpass.  He's usually near the first signpost on the path that leads to the Giant Bridge connecting East and North Karana.  Just hail him and follow the keywords and then follow him when he starts walking.

    In the southwest corner of the zone is a hill with a monument of some kind on top of it.  This hill is where the gnoll reavers and the war wolves spawn.  This is an excellent place to hunt for levels 13-16, but be sure you're in a good group.  If you're coming down the ramp from Highpass, you'll see a signpost at the bottom of the ramp.  Behind the signpost is a small ridge.  If you keep walking until you are just a few steps past this ridge, then turn left and walk a bit more, you will find yourself in the midst of the Mucktail gnolls.  There are quite a few, most of which were green to me at 19th level.  The toughest one is Chief SaNre'Rexsa, a 22nd level gnoll shaman who looks like no other gnoll I've seen in the game.  They have a fast respawn rate, and they're semi-static spawns.  By "semi-static," I mean that they don't wander around like a normal wandering NPC.  Instead, the Mucktails are aggro to damn near everything.  Including other MOBs. Including MOBs that can kick their scrawny gnoll butts.  (Every so often, a Hill Giant will wander through and clean 'em all out.)  If you can catch the Chief just after he's fought off a gorge hound (or if you can catch a crag spider just after it's finished off the Chief), you can usually get a pretty easy kill and some good XP this way.  The upshot of this aggressiveness is that the Mucktails start off in the same location, but then run off when they're chasing after another MOB.  So they may not be near their spawn point.  One final word of warning:  As near as I can tell, you end up with about 7 gnolls, including the Chief, when they're all out.  And they're all usually close enough together (given their aggro-to-other-npc tendencies) that tagging one will bring the whole lot down on you.
