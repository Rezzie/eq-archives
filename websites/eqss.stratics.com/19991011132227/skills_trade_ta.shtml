Guide to Tailoring

 Introduction   Things Needed   A Lesson in Pelts   Crafting Items   Studded and Steel Reinforced   Success Rates   Other Armor Types 

Introduction
Tailoring always requires a pelt of appropriate quality for the make of armor and appropriate animal for the size of the client. The raw materials are always combined in a sewing kit. For advanced tailoring additional raw materials are required to strengthen the armor. More details are found below in easily referenced chapters and charts.
 top


Things Needed for Tailoring
A skilled tradesperson needs only four items to begin their tailoring career.
  1. A Sewing Kit (Small or Large)
  2. The desired Pattern.
  3. The appropriate animal Pelt.
  4. A recommended level of skill in Tailoring; I assert a skill of 15.
Here's a reference chart of materials required to tailor armors of different types:

Armor Type Combined in Sewing Kit
Patchwork 1 Ruined Pelt, 1 Section Pattern (small kit)
Studded 1 Medium Quality Pelt, 1 Section Pattern, # of Studs (large kit)
Steel Reinforced 1 High Quality Pelt, Section Pattern, # of Steel Boning (large kit)

A Lesson in Pelts
Now, to tailor you need to be either rich or relieve yourself of any personal obligation to defend animals. 99% of all tailors spend a lot of time beating on animals like wolves and bears. 1% just have too much money and prefer to buy the pelts so as to avoid the trauma of animal bashing. Your first prey, most likely, will be some sort of weaker wolf, like a Black Wolf--I have yet to yield a pelt from a Coyote. This will allow you to clothe smaller friends, like Gnomes and Elves. There are other examples in other lands, but Freeport lovers will need Black Wolves. By level 5 or 6 you should be able to relieve Black Bears or Brown Bears or Polar Bear Cubs of their coats to tailor for your larger friends. By level 7 or 8, you should be able to get those precious cat pelts from Pumas, or equivelant low level cat, so as to fit your Human and Half-Elven companions. You will notice that small characters are the first to get armor, with humans getting armor last.

Races Size Pelt
Dwarf, Elf, Gnome, Halfling Small Wolf
Barbarian, Elf, Erudian, Half Elf, Human Medium Cat
Barbarian, Ogre, Troll Large Bear

Crafting Tailored Items
Your first goal is to create a Patchwork piece--probably a cap or boots. For all patchwork you will need a form of "ruined pelt." Small characters will need what is called "Ruined Wolf Pelt." Medium characters need "Ruined Cat Pelt" and large characters need "Ruined Bear Pelt." Ruined pelts are far more likely to come from early forms of the appropriate animal. For example, Pumas give "Ruined Cat Pelt"s about 50-60% of the time. Lions give Ruined pelts about 10% of the time, and give "High Quality Cat Pelt" about 50% of the time. For now, if it doesn't say "Ruined" in the description, you don't need it; sell it. With a skill level below 15 you will probably fail too often for Tailoring to be economically feasible, so wait until it gets there.

If you cannot find a Large Kit, then settle for a small one. I can remember finding large kits in two locations: Kelethin and the Inn in Northern Ro. I think I saw another in Halas, but I cannot verify that. They cost just over a platinum. You will save money by waiting for the better model, plus you can use the Large Kit as a backpack as well. While there are several other locations of Large Kits, I cannot remember their location right now.

Once you get a pelt you can use. Put it and any pattern in your Kit. Hit combine. I hope you succeeded. If not, you lost about 6 silver. If you succeeded, you can sell the Patchwork item and make a net profit of about 6 silver, or you can put it on--presuming it fits.

Studded and Steel Reinforced
You will slowly climb to a skill of 26, where you will find that making Patchwork does not increase your skill (the game will prompt you with a "trivial" message). Now you must move beyond petty Patchwork armor and aim for Studded Armor. Here is where you spend all of that money you made the first ten levels. Studs cost quite a bit--most players sell each stud at 1 platinum... ack! I decided to simply work a little on Smithing myself. With 10 skill I succeeded enough to make Studs reasonably often. The book, "Tailoring", can be found on any merchant that also sells Large Sewing Kits. Also, while I cannot verify this, the most common rumor is that players need that book in their inventory to make the higher level armors. Now, since you may need that book anyway, there's no reason for me to repeat what is in that book. If you can't afford to buy that book, then you aren't a very good Tailor.

Success Rates
All three of my my tailor characters approached 40% success rate or better with patchwork armor at skill level 10 and were showing steady but slow improvement. Judging from research, a character shoult achieve a 100% success rate with Patchwork, Studded and Steel Reinforced armor at skill level 100 and have lower success rates on more advanced armor types. It is difficult to make any inferences on the relative effects of DEX and INT on success rate--it seems to be relatively random (e.g., the attributes don't seem to have any bearing at all).

Bottom line is that tailoring can be a fairly lucritive business up to level 10, but beyond that the money is not very good. This may change drastically as we learn more about the other armor types that came in with the trade patch. The only problem with making and selling armor is that invariably you end up doing it at the expense of adventuring and leveling.
