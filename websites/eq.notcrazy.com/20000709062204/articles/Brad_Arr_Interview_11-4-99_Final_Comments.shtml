Brad McQuaid Interview - Arr's Final Comments (11-9-99)

Within the couple hours I spent at Verant, I got a chance to be shown around the office, meet some of the people working there; and of course, give them NotCrazy Pencils. All the folks there are pretty cool, most everyone there is quite friendly, and I throughly enjoyed the experience.

This was the first interview that I have conducted with a game company, and I feel it went off quite well. I was at first afraid that I didnt have enough questions and that the interview would be overly short. But to my surprise, the interview was long enough to be able to spread it out nice and evenly throughout four pages. When I got there, I knew that it was pretty obvious that I wasn't completely sure of how to go about doing everything, funny enough, the first words on my tape recording of the interview somehow managed to be "I don't really know what I'm doing" [chuckle]. But the folks there made everything really easy, and I look forward to the posibility of future interviews.

While I was there, I got a chance to see some of what had been completed in the expansion pack; and while I can't really give too many details, I think everyone will be quite impressed. There are a lot of "little things" being added in the expansion pack that make the whole thing seem even more immersive than it is now. Little things like tassles on the new goblin models (quite nice models) swinging around with the motions of the goblins (as well as their tongues, which seemed to have an intelligence of their own), the shirt on the new giant model moving when he throws a punch (as opposed to being permanantly attached to the body), and other cool, hard to notice things like the trees leaves swaying in the wind. The poly counts are obviously much higher, and it shows, everything is much more rounded, and I had to actually get pretty close to even see some of the polygons.

I must say that the expansion pack is definitly something to be excited about, and I think all of you will be quite impressed when it hits the shelves in March.

This is Arr MiHardies, casting off..
