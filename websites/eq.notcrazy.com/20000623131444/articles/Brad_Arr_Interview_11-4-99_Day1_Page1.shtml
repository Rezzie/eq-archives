Brad McQuaid Interview - Day 1, Page 1 (11-8-99)

Arr: We heard a while back that you were going to be giving a new skill for every level after 50, and to make the level limit 60. Have you decided to implement this?

Brad: Well, The Skill Part is a rumor, and we have not yet committed to whether the level limit is going to be 60, or what. We're still working that out internally. The challenge is to allow people to continue beyond 50 but not to allow a situation such that you’re conning Lady Vox as ‘green yard trash’ -- it just gets silly at that point so we're trying to figure out good way to allow people to keep going, but not let it get silly. It’s going to be probably another couple months before we finally decide on it.

---------------

Arr: Have you determined an official price on the expansion yet, or is that still pretty much up for grabs?

Brad: I assume you're referring to the upgrade process. How its going to work is that the Expansion is going to replace the retail component in stores, very similar to Ultima On-lines' Second Age, such that you'll walk into stores, and you see that box instead of the older box.

Arr: Basically, you buy one, you'll get them both.

Brad: You'll get everything together, right. And that will be just at the regular price point. But we will offer everybody who is already a subscriber to EverQuest an upgrade path where we'll most likely mail out the expansion, C.D. and everything to players who sign up for it. One of the things I was just talking to John [Smedley] about is we were looking into if there is any other way do it like with downloads, which is still up in the air at this point. But, there will be some kind of reduced price and the Expansion will be available to order probably a month or so before we actually release it -- probably some time in February. Those decisions, while I have some influence, 989 [Studios] will make.

Arr: So, basically, currently you're looking at having an on-line ordering thing where they can either choose to have it mailed to them or possibly have them download it?

Brad: Possibly, I want to emphasize the ‘possibly’ because that really has to do with the download situation -- like I was saying, that is a LOT of bandwidth. But, if it would be easier for the customer, it would be easier for us, but its still up in the air at this point.

---------------

Arr: Is EQ still becoming the game you've always wanted to play more and more each day, or has your vision changed a lot because of user demands and needs?

Brad: I think it's 98% what we envisioned and what we've always planned. We're very happy with the way it's turned out. Certainly, as a game designer, I'm looking into the future and thinking to myself, "what else can be accomplished?" I definitely look at it as if it’s the tip of the ice burg. These are the first generation massively multi-player games. The sky is the limit and there is no limit to where we can go from here. But we definitely set out to accomplish EverQuest, and it’s everything we hoped it would be and more. So yeah, definitely very happy with it, but also very excited with where we can go.

---------------

Arr: This is one that I just have to know.

Brad: Sure.

Arr: What do you think of all the little nicknames that notcrazy gives you?

Brad: [Laughs] um.. as long as its all in good fun, it gives me a chuckle. So, I don't mind it.

---------------

Arr: How the expansion going to be tested? Is a select group of dedicated players going to be chosen, or is all the testing being done in house?

Brad: Both, really. We'll start testing in house and at this point, today, it looks like we might have a couple phases of beta testing where we involve testers external to Verant. In some sense it will resemble the testing of EQ 1 in that it will start small then we will bring in as many people as we need. I can’t really get more detailed than that because we still have a lot of details to work out. But, we are definitely going to have a beta, and we'll are definitely involve external people.

---------------

Arr: Is the fifth continent, Velious, going to be added, or is it going to be in a possible future Expansion pack?

Brad: No idea. I'd love to do it, whether it’s in another expansion, or a sequel later on, or what have you. We have a lot of great ideas - a lot of areas, and zones, and cities, and regions, and dungeons, all planned out. I like to use the analogy of the fantasy novel. You open it up and see this map right? And you may only visit 10% of those places in the first book, but you never know. And there are some places you never ever visit -- the author just never gets there. But we really want there to be this in-depth world of Norrath out there that we can slowly but surely flesh out over time. How long that will take and exactly which way we will do it, we don't know.

Arr: Kind of like what they've done with the Star Wars universe with its several books out there.

Brad: Right, yeah. The authors of these things create a lot of depth because there's implications or hints that there's these other areas and places which you may or may not go to, but it makes it makes the world have more depth and seem more real.

---------------
