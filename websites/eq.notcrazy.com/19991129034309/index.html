Brad defines on-topic posts
Sunday, November 28 16:31:6 PST -- Catalyst

Brad "Frisk me" McQuaid made a post to the Ruins of Kunark forum about posting on topic. What generally he would like to see, and what people should be posting. Here are Brad's remarks:

[On what is considered an "On Topic Post"]

New ideas are great and much appreciated.

What I'd prefer not to see are posts about what could change in general with the game -- I'd like the focus to be on the Expansion.

I've posted what the Expansion is focussing on, as well as a list of things that won't be part of the Expansion.

My point is simply that there's plenty of boards and sites to post general ideas about EverQuest, what could be changed/added, etc. So I'd like to see this board stay on topic.

Examples of on-topic suggestions:

1. I think the Iksar should have the following class choices....
2. I think the new level limit should be X because....
3. I think the outdoor zones on Kunark should have...

Examples of off-topic suggestions and/or suggestions pertaining to enhancements we've already stated won't be part of the Expansion:

1. Class X is unbalanced because Class Y has....
2. I don't think characters lose enough experience when they die....
3. In addition to Iksars, you should be able to play the following race...

Anyway, the board is not moderated, but as I said before, the more on-topic posts, questions, and suggestions, the better the chance I or someone else at Verant will be able to respond.

-Brad

Patch message on downtime
Saturday, November 27 14:41:23 PST -- Catalyst

A new EverQuest patch message was just uploaded explaining the downtime:

November 27th, 1:15pm
---------------------

Hi folks,

We sincerely apologize for the downtime today. Here's what happened:

1) At 6:15am PST San Diego Gas & Electric shut the power off in our building. This was a planned outage, except they weren't supposed to turn the power off in our Building. It came as a great surprise for a lot of people in our building.

2) Everything switched as it should to backup power, but it caused many network problems because only major things get backup juice, and some of the minor things became problems. The cutover to the generator isn't as quick as anyone would have hoped (it's supposed to be immediate),

3) As of right now everything is up and running on a backup Gasoline generator (you would have to see this thing to believe it). We are prepared for emergencies, but a few things we weren't ready for bit us where it hurt.

Strangely enough, the biggest problem we had was that our Security system which is supposed to have 12 hours worth of backup battery failed and we were stuck outside of our own building (we went outside temporarily to make sure the Gas Generator was ok). We just got back in (don't ask) and everything is ok now with the exception of Brell which is down for emergency hardware maintenece.

I realize this sounds pretty bizzare, and let me tell you it was.

On the bright side, even with power cut off to our building (actually the EQ servers are located in 2 different facilities both of which have backup juice) everything is back up now.

Again, we apologize Sincerely.

John Smedley
President and CEO
Verant Interactive, Inc.

Brad posts on downtime

Brad "Let's nerf it!" McQuaid stopped by alt.games.everquest today to let everyone know what happened with the EQ servers, unexpected downtime is always fun:

Here's what happened:

SDG&E (the power company in San Diego) had planned on shutting down the power in certain buildings in the complex Verant is in -- but NOT Verant, CerfNET, or 989. But they did by mistake, early this morning, and while the UPS systems kicked in by the time we could get in (security and such), some key systems were still down.

So anyway, it's going to be a rough morning and afternoon as backup power is brought up and then eventually SDG&E gives us regular power again (supposedly around 2:30pm), at which time there could be some further problems when power is rerouted again.

I apologize for the downtime -- certainly wasn't planned.

-Brad

Beta release of EQ ServerScan

Good timing with this, the day EQ is down for a long period of time he releases a server scanner. Duke Elam wrote:

New program to display EverQuest serverstatus - EQ ServerScan

EQ ServerScan is a program that shows you the status of the game servers for the popular online game EverQuest. The program also shows you the number of users playing on each server. Furthermore, you can set the program to signal when a certain number of users are logged in to one or more servers. This will insure a true indication that the server(s) are up if the servers have been down for an extended time. You can also have EQ ServerScan start the EverQuest program.

The program is in beta stage.

Verant Interactive has examined the way this program communicates with their status server and found it most efficient.

Download it at: http://www.dragonorder.net

Rallos Zek: Guardians of the Tome
Saturday, November 27 12:58:49 PST -- Catalyst

Arravis Dru`giir sent this our way, looks like an interesting roleplaying event:

Rallos Zek player-run event: Guardians of the Tome

When and where: Sunday, December 5th, 2:00 p.m. CST, at the Plague Village in East Commons. We'll have some games and give out a few prizes before the event begins; this will allow time for people to gather. At 3:00 p.m. CST the Guardians of the Tome Event will begin.

Who: Any good aligned players, from level 8-24 may participate in the event. The event will be split into 3 groups, each participating in separate parts of the quest. The first group will be level 7-12, the second group will be evel 13-18, and the third group will be 19-24 level characters.

The tale of the Tome of Hatred, as told by Arravis Dru`giir, Wizard and Guardian:

Twenty two hundred years ago the Necromancers, Shadowknights and Priests of Innoruuk created a spell which allowed, their deity, Innoruuk to physically enter the world of mortals. This complex spell was kept within The Tome of Hatred, a singularly evil and vile book. Fortunately the existence of this tome became known to an order of holy monks who stole the book from the Tier`Dal with the objective of destroying it. All but one of the monks were slain in the process of retrieving it, however they were unable to destroy the book. To protect the tome the monk split the pages among seven powerful adventurers. These individuals are to safeguard the pages and avoid contact with each other lest the tome be reconstructed. Unfortunately, the pages themselves still seem to hold a sentient power and can at times exert an influence over the guardian they are bound to. Each page is able to sense the general location of the other pages and their guardian. The guardians are able to bring forth this information themselves, but usually in the form of obscure visions.

The guardians have protected the pages by passing them from generation to generation, however, a few months ago one of the guardians fell into the hands of the Tier`Dal. The dark elves have been using the captured guardian to find and recover the remaining pages. They have captured two additional guardians which will make finding the other four an easy task.

Fellow adventurers, I need the help of the forces of good to avoid this eminent disaster. We must first find the remaining guardians and employ their help to rescue their captured brethren. If enough pages fall into the hands of the Tier`dal, they might have enough information to perform the spell, this must not be allowed to happen. For the future of our world, we must band together and accomplish this task. So gather your spells and take up at arms, the future awaits!

-Arravis Dru`giir
